neither fish nor fowl

1. X

/ ˈniðɚ fɪʃ nɔr faʊl / (AmE), / ˈniːðə fɪʃ nɔː ˈfaʊl / (BrE)
not fitting into any familiar or recognized category; ambiguous or hard to classify.
ambiguous; not easily categorized

Examples

The new law is neither fish nor fowl, combining elements of both civil and criminal penalties.
His latest film is neither fish nor fowl—not quite a drama, but not a comedy either.
Their hybrid vehicle is neither fish nor fowl, appealing to neither traditional car buyers nor motorcycle enthusiasts.

Synset Examples

The book was neither fish nor fowl, hard to categorize.
Her explanation was neither fish nor fowl, confusing us all.
His behavior was neither fish nor fowl at the party.

Etymology

Alluding to something being neither of one group nor another; the phrase dates to Middle English.
