bone in someone's teeth

1. X

/ boʊn ɪn sʌmwʌnz tiθ / (AmE), / bəʊn ɪn sʌmwənz tiːθ / (BrE)
used to describe someone acting with intense focus, determination, or tenacity, as if unwilling to let go or give up
with intense determination or focus

Examples

She tackled the negotiations with a bone in her teeth, refusing to back down.
He worked on the project as if he had a bone in his teeth, relentless and driven.
The reporter pursued the story with a bone in her teeth, determined to uncover the truth.

Synset Examples

She raced down the track with a bone in her teeth.
He approached the challenge with a bone in his teeth.
They tackled the issue with a bone in their teeth.

Etymology

Alludes to a dog tenaciously gripping a bone between its teeth, symbolizing determination.
