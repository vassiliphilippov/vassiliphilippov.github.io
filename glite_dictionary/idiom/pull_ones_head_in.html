to pull one's head in

1. VERB

/ pʊl wʌnz hɛd ɪn / (AmE), / pʊl wʌnz hɛd ɪn / (BrE)
(informal) To become more reserved or cautious and reduce one's outspoken or assertive behavior.
become more reserved or cautious
informal

Examples

After the meeting, the manager advised him to pull his head in until the situation calmed down.
She decided to pull her head in and listen instead of arguing further.
It might be best to pull your head in when everyone else disagrees with you.

Synset Examples

After the criticism, he decided to pull his head in.
You should pull your head in and listen to advice.
It's wise to pull your head in when times are tough.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keep a low profile - to avoid attracting attention to oneself
  • Antonym: to speak out - to express one's opinions openly and forcefully
