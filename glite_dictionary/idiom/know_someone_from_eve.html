to know someone from Eve

1. VERB

/ noʊ ˈsʌmwʌn frəm iv / (AmE), / nəʊ ˈsʌmwən frəm iːv / (BrE)
to have no knowledge or familiarity with someone; to not recognize someone at all
not recognize or know someone at all

Examples

She walked into the meeting and realized she didn't know anyone from Eve.
I can't recommend him; I don't know him from Eve.
We talked for hours, but before tonight, I didn't know her from Eve.

Synset Examples

You can't assume she agrees; you don't know her from Eve.
I don't know her from Eve, so I won't ask her for help.
They don't really know her from Eve, just rumors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to know someone from Adam - to have no knowledge or familiarity with someone

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'not know someone from Adam,' referencing the biblical figures of Adam and Eve.
