aye aye, sir

1. INTJ

/ aɪ aɪ sɝ / (AmE), / aɪ aɪ sə / (BrE)
a phrase used, especially by sailors, to indicate that an order has been received and will be carried out promptly
expression of acknowledgment and readiness (naval)

Examples

When the captain gave his instructions, the sailor responded with a crisp aye aye, sir.
Whenever orders were issued on deck, the crew would promptly shout aye aye, sir.
At the commander's request, she stood at attention and replied, aye aye, sir, before carrying out her duty.

Synset Examples

He replied, 'Aye aye, sir,' before setting off.
The recruits shouted, 'Aye aye, sir,' to their commander.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roger - radio term meaning message received and understood

Etymology

From aye (yes), used in nautical language to indicate compliance; 'sir' addressing a superior officer.
