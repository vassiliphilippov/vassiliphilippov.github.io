at Her Majesty's pleasure

1. ADV

/ ət hər mæˈdʒɛstiz ˈplɛʒər / (AmE), / ət hə ˈmadʒɪstɪz ˈplɛʒə / (BrE)
(in the UK and certain Commonwealth countries) to be kept in prison or in a position for an indefinite period, until authorities decide otherwise.
kept in prison or office for an indefinite period
legal british

Examples

Following his conviction, the judge ruled he would remain at Her Majesty's pleasure.
Some prisoners are detained at Her Majesty's pleasure with no set release date.
Historically, certain officials served at Her Majesty's pleasure rather than for a fixed term.

Synset Examples

He is in prison, living at Her Majesty's pleasure.
The offender must live at Her Majesty's pleasure for now.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indefinite sentence - a punishment where the duration is not fixed

Etymology

From the formal phrase used in British law indicating authority held at the discretion of the monarch.
