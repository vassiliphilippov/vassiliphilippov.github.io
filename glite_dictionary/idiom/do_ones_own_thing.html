to do one's own thing

1. VERB

/ du wʌnz oʊn θɪŋ / (AmE), / duː wʌnz əʊn θɪŋ / (BrE)
to act independently, pursuing one's own interests or preferences, without regard to what others do or expect.
act independently; follow one's own interests

Examples

Ever since moving to the city, she prefers to do her own thing on the weekends.
When you travel alone, you can do your own thing without having to compromise.
He left the group because he wanted to do his own thing musically.

Synset Examples

She loves to do her own thing without anyone's interference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go one's own way - to make independent decisions or take a different path than others

2. VERB

/ duː wʌnz oʊn θɪŋ / (AmE), / duː wʌnz əʊn θɪŋ / (BrE)
to act or behave independently, according to one's personal preferences, beliefs, or inclinations, rather than conforming to others.
act independently or as one prefers

Examples

After college, she decided to do her own thing and start a small business.
He doesn't care about trends; he just likes to do his own thing.
While everyone else joined the tour, I chose to do my own thing for the afternoon.

Synset Examples

He prefers to do his own thing rather than follow the crowd.
During the trip, we all decided to do our own thing for a day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go one's own way - act independently without regard to others' opinions
