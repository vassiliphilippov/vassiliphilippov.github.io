in stride

1. ADV

/ ɪn straɪd / (AmE), / ɪn straɪd / (BrE)
without becoming upset or losing composure, especially when facing challenges or criticism
calmly; without being upset

Examples

Despite the unexpected news, she took it in stride and continued working.
He handles criticism in stride, never letting it bother him.
When plans change suddenly, try to take things in stride instead of worrying.

Synset Examples

she took all his criticism in stride

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calmly - in a tranquil or composed manner
  • Antonym: to lose one's cool - to become angry or upset

2. ADV

/ ɪn straɪd / (AmE), / ɪn straɪd / (BrE)
to deal with something calmly and without being upset or affected
to manage calmly

Examples

Despite the criticism, she took the feedback in stride and continued working hard.
He always handles last-minute changes in stride, never complaining.
The team took the unexpected delay in stride and focused on solutions.

Synset Examples

They handled the delays in stride, staying positive.
He takes setbacks in stride without losing hope.
He faced the unexpected challenge in stride.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take in stride - to deal with something calmly and without difficulty
  • Antonym: panic - to react to difficulty with fear or anxiety
