tell you the truth

1. X

/ tɛl ju ðə truθ / (AmE), / tɛl jʊ ðə truːθ / (BrE)
used to preface a statement that is honest, direct, or frank, sometimes when admitting something difficult or unexpected
to be completely honest; honestly

Examples

Tell you the truth, I wasn't expecting such a quick response from the company.
To tell you the truth, I've never tried sushi before.
I'll tell you the truth: I need some time to think about your offer.

Synset Examples

I'll tell you the truth, it's not easy.
I’ll tell you the truth, I didn’t like the movie.
To tell you the truth, I was nervous about the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to be honest - used to introduce a frank or sincere statement
  • Synonym: frankly - in an honest and direct manner
