to leave the door open

1. VERB

/ liv ðə dɔr ˈoʊpən / (AmE), / liːv ðə dɔː ˈəʊpən / (BrE)
to allow for future opportunities or possibilities to remain available; to not rule something out completely
allow future opportunities or options

Examples

The company decided to leave the door open to potential partnerships in the future.
Even after resigning, he tried to leave the door open for a possible return.
They rejected the initial offer but left the door open for further discussion.

Synset Examples

They left the door open for negotiations later.
She decided to leave the door open for future discussions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keep one's options open - to avoid making a final decision so that you can choose later
