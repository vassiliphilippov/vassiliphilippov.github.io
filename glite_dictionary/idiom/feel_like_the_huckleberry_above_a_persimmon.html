to feel like the huckleberry above a persimmon

1. VERB

/ fil laɪk ðə ˈhʌkəlˌbɛri əˈbʌv ə pərˈsɪmən / (AmE), / fiːl laɪk ðə ˈhʌkəlˌbɛri əˈbʌv ə pəˈsɪmən / (BrE)
to feel particularly special or important compared to others
feel important or special

Examples

After winning the award, she felt like the huckleberry above a persimmon at the ceremony.
Whenever he leads the team to victory, he says he feels like the huckleberry above a persimmon.
With all eyes on me, I couldn't help but feel like the huckleberry above a persimmon for a moment.

Synset Examples

As the leader, I feel like the huckleberry above a persimmon.

Etymology

References to huckleberries and persimmons as Southern U.S. fruits, emphasizing a sense of superiority (the small huckleberry positioned above the larger persimmon).
