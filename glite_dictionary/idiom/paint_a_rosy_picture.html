to paint a rosy picture

1. VERB

/ peɪnt ə ˈroʊzi ˈpɪktʃər / (AmE), / peɪnt ə ˈrəʊzi ˈpɪktʃə / (BrE)
to present an unrealistically optimistic or positive view of a situation
present an overly positive view

Examples

The CEO tried to paint a rosy picture of the company's finances during the meeting.
Politicians often paint a rosy picture of the economy before elections.
Even when things were tough, she would paint a rosy picture to keep everyone hopeful.

Synset Examples

She tends to paint a rosy picture of her travels.
Don't paint a rosy picture; be realistic about the challenges.
The manager painted a rosy picture of the company's future.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sugarcoat - to make something seem more pleasant or acceptable than it really is
  • Antonym: to downplay - to make something appear less important or serious than it really is
