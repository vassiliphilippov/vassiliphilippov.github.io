to get a noseful

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ə ˈnoʊsˌfʊl / (AmE), / ɡɛt ə ˈnəʊzfʊl / (BrE)
to smell something very strongly or intensely, often in an unpleasant way
smell something strongly or intensely

Examples

As soon as I opened the fridge, I got a noseful of spoiled milk.
He got a noseful of perfume when he stepped into the elevator.
Walking past the bakery, she got a noseful of freshly baked bread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smell - to perceive or detect the odor of something
