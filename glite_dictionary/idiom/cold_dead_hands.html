cold, dead hands

1. X

/ koʊld dɛd hændz / (AmE), / kəʊld dɛd hændz / (BrE)
used to emphasize that someone will not relinquish something under any circumstances, implying only death would make them give it up
utter refusal to give up something

Examples

You'll have to pry this trophy from my cold, dead hands.
He clung to his beliefs with cold, dead hands, refusing to compromise.
They will take their secrets to the grave or keep them with cold, dead hands.

Synset Examples

He vowed to keep his rights from my cold, dead hands.
You’ll have to pry this book from my cold, dead hands.
No one will take my tools from my cold, dead hands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: never - not under any circumstances; at no time
  • Antonym: willingly - voluntarily; without needing to be forced
