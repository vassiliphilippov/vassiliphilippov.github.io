at a snail's pace

1. ADV

/ ət ə sneɪlz peɪs / (AmE), / ət ə sneɪlz peɪs / (BrE)
very slowly; at a very slow speed or rate
very slowly

Examples

The construction work is going at a snail's pace and won't be finished on time.
After the accident, traffic moved at a snail's pace through the city.
Negotiations between the two sides are progressing at a snail's pace.

Synset Examples

The renovation is progressing at a snail's pace.
The project is moving at a snail's pace.
Traffic was crawling at a snail's pace this morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slowly - at a slow speed; not quickly
  • Antonym: rapidly - very quickly; at a high speed

Etymology

From the slow movement of snails, used figuratively since the 17th century.
