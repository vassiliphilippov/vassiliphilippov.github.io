like a minefield

1. X

/ laɪk ə ˈmaɪnfiːld / (AmE), / laɪk ə ˈmaɪnfiːld / (BrE)
used to describe a situation or subject that is filled with hidden dangers or difficulties
full of hidden dangers or difficulties

Examples

Navigating office politics can feel like a minefield for newcomers.
Every question from the journalist was like a minefield, waiting to catch the politician off guard.
Trying to fix the old wiring was like a minefield—one wrong move and the power went out.

Synset Examples

Driving through that neighborhood felt like a minefield.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minefield - an area planted with explosive mines; also a situation full of hidden dangers
