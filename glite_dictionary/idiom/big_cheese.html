big cheese

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ tʃiz / (AmE), / bɪɡ tʃiːz / (BrE)
an important and influential person, especially within a particular organization or sphere
an important or influential person
informal

Examples

After her promotion, everyone treated her like the big cheese around the office.
He's not the big cheese he claims to be, but people still listen to him.
At industry events, you can often spot the big cheese from each major company.

Synset Examples

he thinks he's a big shot
she's a big deal in local politics
the Qaeda commander is a very big fish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: big shot - an important or influential person
  • Synonym: bigwig - a powerful or important person
  • Antonym: nobody - an unimportant or insignificant person

Etymology

Early 20th century US slang; 'cheese' possibly from Persian 'chīz' meaning 'thing'.

2. NOUN

/ bɪɡ tʃiz / (AmE), / bɪɡ tʃiːz / (BrE)
an important and influential person in an organization or group
an important person; leader
informal

Examples

After his promotion, Tom became the big cheese in the entire company.
Everyone wanted to impress the big cheese at the annual meeting.
She was surprised to be sitting next to the big cheese at dinner.

Synset Examples

Everyone reports to the big cheese in this office.
As the big cheese, he makes all the final decisions.
He's the big cheese in our department.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: top dog - a person who is in charge or has a dominant position
  • Synonym: big shot - an important or influential person
  • Antonym: nobody - a person of no importance or influence

Etymology

Probably originated in early 20th-century American slang, where 'cheese' meant 'the best thing' or 'an important person', possibly from the Persian 'chiz' (thing).
