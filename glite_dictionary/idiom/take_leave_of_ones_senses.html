to take leave of one's senses

1. VERB

/ teɪk liːv əv wʌnz ˈsɛnsɪz / (AmE), / teɪk liːv əv wʌnz ˈsɛnsɪz / (BrE)
to act in a way that is completely irrational or foolish
to behave irrationally or foolishly

Examples

Some people thought she had taken leave of her senses when she bought a house without seeing it first.
If you believe that story, you must have taken leave of your senses.
Spending so much money on a pair of shoes is like taking leave of one's senses.

Synset Examples

He must have taken leave of his senses to quit his job.
To spend that much money seems like taking leave of one's senses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lose one's mind - to become mentally unstable or act irrationally
  • Antonym: to come to one's senses - to return to a rational state of mind after acting foolishly
