on the back burner

1. ADV

/ ɑn ðə bæk ˈbɝnɚ / (AmE), / ɒn ðə bæk ˈbɜːnə / (BrE)
postponed or given lower priority; temporarily set aside to be dealt with later
temporarily set aside

Examples

With so many urgent tasks, the manager put the training program on the back burner until next quarter.
After her promotion, she decided to keep her travel plans on the back burner for now.
Due to budget cuts, the research project has been placed on the back burner indefinitely.

Related Terms

Etymology

From the practice of moving less urgent pots to the rear burners of a stove.
