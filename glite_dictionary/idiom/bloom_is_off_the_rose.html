bloom is off the rose

1. X

/ bluːm ɪz ɑf ðə roʊz / (AmE), / bluːm ɪz ɒf ðə rəʊz / (BrE)
used to say that the initial excitement, appeal, or joy of something has diminished
initial excitement or appeal has faded

Examples

Ever since the delays began, the bloom is off the rose for the new project.
At first, moving to the city was thrilling, but soon the bloom is off the rose.
When reality set in, they both felt that the bloom is off the rose in their relationship.

Synset Examples

Since the incident, the bloom is off the rose for their partnership.
Once the bloom is off the rose, it's hard to go back.
Now that the bloom is off the rose, they argue constantly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeymoon period - time when something feels new and exciting
