to do one's duty

1. VERB

/ duː wʌnz ˈduːti / (AmE), / dʊ wʌnz ˈdjuːti / (BrE)
to fulfill one's responsibilities or obligations, especially those expected by one's role or position
fulfill one's responsibilities or obligations

Examples

As a firefighter, he always tries to do his duty regardless of personal risk.
Many believe it is essential to do one's duty to the community through volunteering.
She felt proud after she realized she had done her duty as a team leader.

Synset Examples

She felt it was her time to do her duty.
He always tries to do his duty at work.
It's important to do one's duty as a citizen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fulfill one's duty - carry out one's responsibilities
  • Antonym: neglect one's duty - fail to do something one is responsible for
