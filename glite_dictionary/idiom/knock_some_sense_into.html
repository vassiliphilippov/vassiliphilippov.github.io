to knock some sense into

1. VERB

/ nɑk səm sɛns ˈɪntu / (AmE), / nɒk səm sɛns ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
to force someone to start thinking or behaving in a more sensible or reasonable way, often after they have been acting foolishly or unwisely
make someone behave more sensibly

Examples

Sometimes you just need to knock some sense into stubborn kids.
She tried to knock some sense into her friend about the relationship.
It took a serious mistake for his coach to finally knock some sense into him.

Synset Examples
