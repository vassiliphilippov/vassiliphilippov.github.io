to little avail

1. ADV

/ tu ˈlɪtəl əˈveɪl / (AmE), / tə ˈlɪtl əˈveɪl / (BrE)
with little success or result; not achieving much effect
with little success or effect

Examples

She tried to solve the problem, but her efforts were to little avail.
The team appealed for help, to little avail, as no one responded.
They searched for the lost ring to little avail before giving up.

Synset Examples

Her efforts were to little avail in the end.
They fought to little avail against the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in vain - without success or result
