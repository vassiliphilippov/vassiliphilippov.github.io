cake walk

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪk wɑk / (AmE), / ˈkeɪk wɔːk / (BrE)
a task or activity that is very easy to do or accomplish
something very easy to do

Examples

After months of practice, solving the puzzle was a cake walk for him.
Don’t think the interview will be a cake walk; prepare well just in case.
Winning the local election turned out to be a cake walk for the incumbent.

Synset Examples

For him, that task was a complete cake walk.
Winning the quiz was a cake walk for her.
The exam turned out to be a cake walk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walkover - an easy victory or accomplishment
  • Synonym: pushover - a person or thing that is easy to defeat or do
  • Antonym: challenge - a difficult task; something that tests skill or ability

Etymology

From the 19th-century American practice of cakewalk competitions, in which the prize was a cake.
