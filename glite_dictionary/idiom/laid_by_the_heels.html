to laid by the heels

1. VERB

/ leɪd baɪ ðə hilz / (AmE), / leɪd baɪ ðə hiːlz / (BrE)
arrested, captured, or restrained; to be detained by authority or force.
arrested or restrained
archaic

Examples

After months on the run, the fugitive was finally laid by the heels by the police.
Corrupt officials were often laid by the heels when exposed by journalists.
In the old days, debtors might be laid by the heels and thrown into prison.

Synset Examples

Once the evidence surfaced, he was laid by the heels and arrested.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrest - to seize someone by legal authority
  • Synonym: capture - to take someone or something into custody

Etymology

From the archaic practice of restraining a person by holding or binding their heels; documented in English from the 16th century.
