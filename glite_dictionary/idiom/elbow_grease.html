elbow grease

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛlboʊ ˌɡris / (AmE), / ˈɛlbəʊ ˌɡriːs / (BrE)
hard physical effort, especially when cleaning or polishing something; energetic manual work
vigorous manual effort; hard work

Examples

It took a lot of elbow grease to get the old table looking like new.
You need some elbow grease if you want to scrub that stain out.
With plenty of elbow grease, they finished painting the house in one day.

Synset Examples

he got an A for effort
they managed only with great exertion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effort - use of physical or mental energy to do something
  • Synonym: exertion - vigorous physical or mental effort

Etymology

Earliest recorded in the 17th century as a humorous reference to manual labour, especially cleaning.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛlboʊ ɡris / (AmE), / ˈɛlbəʊ ɡriːs / (BrE)
energetic physical effort, especially in cleaning, polishing, or working hard at a manual task
energetic physical effort; hard work

Examples

With a bit of elbow grease, the old table looked as good as new.
Restoring the vintage car took more elbow grease than I expected.
Cleaning these windows properly will require some serious elbow grease.

Synset Examples

It took a lot of elbow grease to clean the garage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effort - physical or mental energy used to do something
  • Synonym: hard work - a lot of effort and diligence in achieving something

Etymology

First recorded in the 17th century; ‘grease’ used figuratively to mean effort, ‘elbow’ as the body part that moves during work.
