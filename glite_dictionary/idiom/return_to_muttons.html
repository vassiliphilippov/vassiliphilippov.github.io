to return to muttons

1. VERB

/ rɪˈtɝn tə ˈmʌtənz / (AmE), / rɪˈtɜːn tə ˈmʌtənz / (BrE)
to resume or return to the main topic, important tasks, or work after a distraction or digression
get back to the main task or topic

Examples

After their lunch break, the team decided to return to muttons and focus on the presentation.
He joked that it was time to return to muttons after their discussion wandered off-topic.
Let's return to muttons and review the agenda, shall we?

Synset Examples

It's time to return to our muttons and finish the project.
After a long chat, they decided to return to their muttons.

Etymology

Humorous calque of the French idiom Retour à nos moutons (literally 'let us return to our sheep'), used to mean resuming the main topic.
