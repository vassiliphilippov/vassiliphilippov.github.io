on the other paw

1. SCONJ

/ ɑn ði ˈʌðɚ pɔ / (AmE), / ɒn ði ˈʌðə pɔː / (BrE)
Used humorously to introduce a contrasting point or alternative perspective, especially in contexts involving animals.
humorous contrastive phrase; animal contexts
informal

Examples

My cat sleeps all day; on the other paw, she is up all night playing with toys.
Dogs are great company; on the other paw, they require a lot of care and attention.
Kittens can be mischievous, but on the other paw, they're very affectionate when they want to be.

Synset Examples

Cats can be aloof; on the other paw, they love cuddling.
Dogs are loyal; on the other paw, they need lots of attention.
My dog loves to chase squirrels; on the other paw, he fears loud noises.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on the other hand - used to introduce a contrasting point of view or statement
