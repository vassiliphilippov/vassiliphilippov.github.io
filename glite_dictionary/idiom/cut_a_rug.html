to cut a rug

1. VERB

/ kʌt ə rʌɡ / (AmE), / kʌt ə rʌɡ / (BrE)
to dance energetically, especially in a lively or enthusiastic way
dance energetically (idiom)
informal american

Examples

Every Saturday night, they cut a rug at the local jazz club.
Grandpa still loves to cut a rug at family gatherings.
Whenever that song comes on, she can't resist getting up to cut a rug.

Synset Examples

He loves to cut a rug whenever his favorite song plays.
She was the first to cut a rug on the dance floor.
They cut a rug at the wedding reception.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dance - to move rhythmically to music

Etymology

American English, originated in the early 20th century dance halls, where vigorous dancing was said to be enough to wear out or 'cut' the rug.
