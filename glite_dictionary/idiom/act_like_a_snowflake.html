to act like a snowflake

1. VERB

/ ækt laɪk ə ˈsnoʊˌfleɪk / (AmE), / ækt laɪk ə ˈsnəʊˌfleɪk / (BrE)
to respond with excessive sensitivity or defensiveness, especially in reaction to criticism
respond overly sensitively or defensively
informal slang

Examples

Whenever someone questions his ideas, he acts like a snowflake and gets offended.
At the meeting, she acted like a snowflake when given constructive feedback.
He tends to act like a snowflake whenever someone points out his mistakes.

Synset Examples

She acted like a snowflake when criticized at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overreact - to respond more emotionally or forcibly than is justified
  • Synonym: take offense - to become upset or annoyed, often unnecessarily
  • Antonym: to shrug off - to ignore criticism or problems without getting upset

Etymology

From 'snowflake', a slang term popularized in the 2010s social and political discourse, referencing the perceived delicacy or fragility of a snowflake.
