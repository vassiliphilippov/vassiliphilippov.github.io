to raise red flag

1. VERB

/ reɪz rɛd flæɡ / (AmE), / reɪz rɛd flæɡ / (BrE)
to signal or indicate a possible problem, danger, or concern
signal a possible problem or concern

Examples

Several unexpected withdrawals from the account started to raise red flags for the bank.
The manager's absence from critical meetings can raise red flags about project leadership.
Any inconsistency in their story should raise red flag for investigators.

Synset Examples

Her behavior has started to raise red flags.
They raised red flags about the safety procedures.
The accountant's mistakes raised red flags for the audit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to warn - to give notice or caution of danger
  • Synonym: alert - to warn someone of a current or impending problem
  • Antonym: all clear - signal that a danger or problem has passed
