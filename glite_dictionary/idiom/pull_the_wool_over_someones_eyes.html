to pull the wool over someone's eyes

1. VERB

/ pʊl ðə wʊl oʊvər sʌmˌwʌnz aɪz / (AmE), / pʊl ðə wʊl əʊvə sʌmˌwʌnz aɪz / (BrE)
to deceive someone by hiding your true intentions, especially by pretending to be honest or well-meaning
deceive or trick someone

Examples

The company tried to pull the wool over investors' eyes by concealing its losses.
You can't pull the wool over my eyes; I know exactly what's going on.
She accused him of pulling the wool over her eyes with his false promises.

Synset Examples

He bamboozled his professors into thinking that he knew the subject well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true
  • Synonym: trick - to deceive someone as a means of achieving a goal
  • Antonym: be honest - to be truthful and not deceive

Etymology

Likely from the 19th-century practice of wearing woolen wigs, suggesting someone's vision is obscured.

2. VERB

/ pʊl ðə wʊl oʊvɚ ˈsʌmwʌnz aɪz / (AmE), / pʊl ðə wʊl ˈəʊvə ˈsʌmwʌnz aɪz / (BrE)
to deceive or trick someone, especially by hiding the truth from them
to deceive or trick someone

Examples

She tried to pull the wool over my eyes, but I quickly saw through her lies.
Politicians sometimes attempt to pull the wool over the public's eyes with misleading statements.
Don't let them pull the wool over your eyes—always double-check the facts.

Synset Examples

Don't try to pull the wool over my eyes; I know the truth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true
  • Synonym: trick - to deceive someone, often in a playful or dishonest way
  • Antonym: be honest - to tell the truth; not to deceive

Etymology

Originates from 19th-century Britain, alluding to the wig-wearing days when pulling a wig (wool) over someone's eyes would obscure their vision.
