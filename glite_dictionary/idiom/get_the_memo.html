to get the memo

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ðə ˈmɛmoʊ / (AmE), / ɡɛt ðə ˈmɛməʊ / (BrE)
to become aware of or be informed about something that others know, especially when it is important or obvious
become informed; be aware of something

Examples

Did you get the memo about the new dress code?
Some people just never get the memo, do they?
He finally got the memo and stopped parking in the manager's spot.

Synset Examples

Make sure everyone gets the memo before we start.
Did you get the memo about the meeting change?
She didn’t get the memo and arrived an hour late.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to catch on - to understand or realize something, especially after a while
  • Synonym: be in the know - to be aware of something known by only a few people

Etymology

From memo, short for memorandum (Latin: ‘thing to be remembered’); idiom based on the idea of receiving a formal notice.
