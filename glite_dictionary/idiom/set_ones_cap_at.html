to set one's cap at

1. VERB

/ sɛt wʌnz kæp æt / (AmE), / sɛt wʌnz kæp æt / (BrE)
To try to attract or pursue someone with the intention of marriage or a romantic relationship.
try to attract romantically

Examples

Rumor has it that Maria has set her cap at the wealthy widower who just moved in next door.
Many believed she would set her cap at the new doctor in town.
It was clear from the start that Emily had set her cap at her sister's charming friend.

Synset Examples

She set her cap at the new manager.
She has set her cap at that handsome artist.
He set his cap at the most eligible bachelor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pursue - to try to attract someone romantically
  • Synonym: court - to seek the affections of someone in a romantic way
  • Antonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to; deliberately disregard romantically

Etymology

From the old custom of women adjusting their cap or head covering to signal romantic interest.
