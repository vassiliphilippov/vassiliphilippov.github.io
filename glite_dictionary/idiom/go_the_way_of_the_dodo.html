to go the way of the dodo

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ðə weɪ əv ðə ˈdoʊdoʊ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ðə weɪ əv ðə ˈdəʊdəʊ / (BrE)
to become extinct or cease to exist; to disappear completely, like the dodo bird
become extinct or disappear

Examples

Many believe that printed newspapers may go the way of the dodo in the digital age.
If we don't upgrade soon, our technology will go the way of the dodo.
Several old traditions have gone the way of the dodo and are rarely practiced today.

Synset Examples

The old software might soon go the way of the dodo.
If not protected, our forests will go the way of the dodo.
Some species are at risk of going the way of the dodo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disappear - cease to be visible or exist
  • Synonym: to vanish - suddenly cease to exist or be present

Etymology

Refers to the dodo, a flightless bird from Mauritius that became extinct in the 17th century.
