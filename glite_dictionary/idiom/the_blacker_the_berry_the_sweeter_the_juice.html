the blacker the berry, the sweeter the juice

1. X

/ ðə ˈblækər ðə ˈbɛri ðə ˈswiːtər ðə dʒus / (AmE), / ðə ˈblækə ðə ˈbɛri ðə ˈswiːtə ðə dʒuːs / (BrE)
Used to suggest that the deeper or more intense something is, the greater its value or reward; often refers to appreciating darker skin tones and their associated beauty or virtues.
the more intense, the greater the reward; esp. with skin color

Examples

Whenever she faced challenges, her mother would say, the blacker the berry, the sweeter the juice, encouraging resilience.
At the family gathering, he repeated, the blacker the berry, the sweeter the juice, to express pride in their heritage.
The film's title echoed the saying, the blacker the berry, the sweeter the juice, highlighting its cultural resonance.

Related Terms

Etymology

Popularized in African American communities; literal reference to fruit, used metaphorically by the early 20th century.
