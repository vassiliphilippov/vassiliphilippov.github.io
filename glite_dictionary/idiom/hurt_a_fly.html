to hurt a fly

1. VERB

/ hɝt ə flaɪ / (AmE), / hɜːt ə flaɪ / (BrE)
(usually in negative constructions) to be so gentle or harmless that one would not harm even the smallest or most innocent creature; to not harm anyone.
to be extremely gentle, not harm anyone

Examples

She is so kind that she wouldn't hurt a fly.
Despite his reputation, Tom wouldn't hurt a fly if given the choice.
Even when provoked, he says he could never hurt a fly.

Synset Examples

He's so gentle; he wouldn't hurt a fly.
Despite his tough appearance, he wouldn’t hurt a fly.
She's the kind of person who wouldn’t hurt a fly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gentle - having or showing a mild, kind, or tender temperament or character
  • Antonym: aggressive - ready or likely to attack or confront; hostile
