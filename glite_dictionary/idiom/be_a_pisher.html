to be a pisher

1. VERB

/ bi ə ˈpɪʃər / (AmE), / biː ə ˈpɪʃə / (BrE)
to behave foolishly or timidly, especially in a way that shows inexperience or lack of confidence
act foolishly or timidly
informal american

Examples

Stop worrying and don't be a pisher when it's your turn to speak.
If you be a pisher every time things get tough, you'll never succeed.
His friends told him not to be a pisher and just jump into the pool.

Synset Examples

Don't be a pisher; just enjoy the moment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be a coward - to behave in a timid or fearful way
  • Antonym: be brave - to act with courage and confidence

Etymology

From Yiddish 'pisher', meaning a young or insignificant person, literally 'one who urinates'.
