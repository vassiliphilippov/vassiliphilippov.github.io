to go by the wayside

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ baɪ ðə ˈweɪsaɪd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ baɪ ðə ˈweɪsaɪd / (BrE)
(of an idea, practice, or thing) to be ignored, neglected, or no longer followed or used.
to be disregarded or neglected

Examples

Many basic manners have gone by the wayside in recent years.
Their earlier promises quickly went by the wayside once the project began.
With the introduction of new technology, typewriters soon went by the wayside.

Synset Examples

Many traditional practices have gone by the wayside.

Etymology

From the literal meaning of 'wayside' meaning the side of a road, suggesting being left on the side and forgotten.
