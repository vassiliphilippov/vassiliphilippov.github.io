neck of the woods

1. NOUN

/ nɛk əv ðə wʊdz / (AmE), / nɛk əv ðə wʊdz / (BrE)
an area or region, especially the local area where someone lives or is familiar with
local area or region

Examples

We haven't seen you around this neck of the woods in a while.
If you're ever in my neck of the woods, stop by for a visit.
There's a new restaurant that just opened up in our neck of the woods.

Synset Examples

the plane crashed in the vicinity of Asheville
it is a rugged locality
he always blames someone else in the immediate neighborhood
I will drop in on you the next time I am in this neck of the woods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vicinity - the area around a particular place
  • Synonym: neighborhood - the area surrounding a particular place or person

Etymology

North American origin; likely from the sense of 'neck' meaning a narrow stretch of land or woods.

2. NOUN

/ nɛk əv ðə wʊdz / (AmE), / nɛk əv ðə wʊdz / (BrE)
a particular area, region, or neighborhood, especially one regarded as familiar or local
a particular area or neighborhood

Examples

There are a lot of great hiking trails in this neck of the woods.
If you’re ever in my neck of the woods, stop by and visit.
She’s new to this neck of the woods and wants to meet people.

Synset Examples

Is there a good diner in your neck of the woods?
She moved to this neck of the woods last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neighborhood - the area or region around a particular place; locality

Etymology

An American English idiom, possibly from regional uses of 'neck' meaning a narrow strip of land or territory.
