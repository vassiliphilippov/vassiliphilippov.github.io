making bricks without straw

1. X

/ ˈmeɪkɪŋ brɪks wɪðaʊt strɔ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪkɪŋ brɪks wɪðaʊt strɔː / (BrE)
Trying to accomplish something without the necessary resources or tools.
trying without needed resources

Examples

Without the right funding, developing the new product was just making bricks without straw.
He felt like he was making bricks without straw when he had to write the report without any data.
Trying to organize the event without support from the team was really making bricks without straw.

Synset Examples

Her efforts were futile, like making bricks without straw.
Without proper tools, we're just making bricks without straw.
Trying to finish the project felt like making bricks without straw.

Etymology

Refers to an ancient practice, cited in the Bible (Exodus), of making bricks using straw as essential reinforcement.
