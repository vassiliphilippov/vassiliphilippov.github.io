to stake my bottom dollar

1. VERB

/ steɪk maɪ ˈbɑːtəm ˈdʌlər / (AmE), / steɪk maɪ ˈbɒtəm ˈdʌlə / (BrE)
to risk all of one's money or resources on the certainty of something
risk everything one has
informal

Examples

You can stake my bottom dollar that she'll win the election.
If he says something, you can stake your bottom dollar it's true.
I wouldn't stake my bottom dollar on that risky investment.

Synset Examples

If you want it, I'm willing to stake my bottom dollar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bet my bottom dollar - to be completely certain about something; to risk everything on it
