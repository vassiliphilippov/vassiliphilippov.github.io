to fire on all cylinders

1. VERB

/ ˈfaɪɚ ɑn ɔl ˈsɪlɪndɚz / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə ɒn ɔːl ˈsɪlɪndəz / (BrE)
to function or operate at the highest possible level of effectiveness or efficiency
operate at maximum efficiency

Examples

The marketing team was firing on all cylinders during the product launch and exceeded their sales goals.
After a slow start, the orchestra began to fire on all cylinders and delivered a breathtaking performance.
If our computers were firing on all cylinders, we could finish this analysis much more quickly.

Synset Examples

The team is firing on all cylinders this quarter.
The engine was running fire on all cylinders today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: run at full throttle - to perform at the maximum possible speed or capacity
  • Synonym: at full tilt - at maximum speed or capacity
  • Antonym: to underperform - to perform below one's capabilities or expectations

Etymology

Metaphor from internal combustion engines, where all cylinders firing indicates peak performance.
