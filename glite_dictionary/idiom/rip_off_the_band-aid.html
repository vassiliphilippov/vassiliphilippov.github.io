to rip off the band-aid

1. VERB

/ rɪp ɔf ðə ˈbænˌdeɪd / (AmE), / rɪp ɒf ðə ˈbæn.deɪd / (BrE)
to confront a difficult, unpleasant, or painful situation quickly and decisively, rather than prolonging it
deal with difficulty quickly

Examples

It's better to rip off the band-aid and get the unpleasant conversation over with.
If you rip off the band-aid, you'll feel relieved sooner than you expect.
Sometimes you just have to rip off the band-aid instead of postponing the inevitable.

Synset Examples

She decided to rip off the band-aid and confront him.
It's best to rip off the band-aid quickly.
Just rip off the band-aid and tell me the truth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get it over with - to finish something unpleasant as soon as possible

Etymology

Refers to the idea that removing a band-aid (adhesive bandage) quickly causes momentary pain but is less prolonged than if done slowly.
