between a rock and a hard place

1. X

/ bɪˈtwin ə ˈrɑk ənd ə ˈhɑrd ˌpleɪs / (AmE), / bɪˈtwiːn ə ˈrɒk ənd ə ˈhɑːd ˌpleɪs / (BrE)
in a difficult situation where there are no good options or easy choices available
in a tough situation with no good choices

Examples

After the merger, employees felt between a rock and a hard place about their future with the company.
Maria was between a rock and a hard place when asked to choose between staying late at work or missing her friend's wedding.
He realized he was between a rock and a hard place when both of his friends needed him at the same time.

Synset Examples

Finding a solution left him between a rock and a hard place.
She felt like she was between a rock and a hard place during the negotiations.
He was between a rock and a hard place with that decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in a bind - in a difficult or awkward situation
  • Synonym: between the devil and the deep blue sea - facing two difficult choices
