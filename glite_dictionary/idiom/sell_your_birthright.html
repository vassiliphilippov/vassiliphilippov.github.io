to sell your birthright

1. VERB

/ sɛl jʊr ˈbɜrθˌraɪt / (AmE), / sɛl jɔː ˈbɜːθˌraɪt / (BrE)
to give up something very valuable or fundamental, especially for something of little worth
give up something valuable for little

Examples

He warned his friend not to sell your birthright for temporary pleasure.
Some fear that, by accepting such a low offer, we might sell your birthright.
In the pursuit of fame, she almost sold her birthright of privacy and peace.

Synset Examples

Don't sell your birthright for a mess of pottage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sacrifice - to give up something valued for the sake of something else
  • Synonym: to betray - to be disloyal or give up something for personal gain
  • Antonym: hold on to - to keep or retain something valuable

Etymology

From the biblical story of Esau selling his birthright to Jacob (Genesis 25:29–34).
