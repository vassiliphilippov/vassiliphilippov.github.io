to sink into quicksand

1. VERB

/ sɪŋk ˈɪntu ˈkwɪksænd / (AmE), / sɪŋk ˈɪntu ˈkwɪksænd / (BrE)
to become trapped in a difficult or worsening situation that is hard to escape
become trapped in a worsening situation

Examples

When the negotiations stalled, the team began to sink into quicksand of bureaucracy and confusion.
He realized too late that he had sunk into quicksand with mounting debts and no escape in sight.
If we keep avoiding the main issue, we'll just sink into quicksand and make things worse.

Synset Examples

She realized the more she panicked, the more she sank into quicksand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get stuck - become unable to move forward or escape a situation
