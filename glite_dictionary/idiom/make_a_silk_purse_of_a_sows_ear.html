to make a silk purse of a sow's ear

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə sɪlk pɝs ʌv ə saʊz ɪr / (AmE), / meɪk ə sɪlk pɜːs ɒv ə saʊz ɪə / (BrE)
to transform something of low quality or value into something attractive or valuable
create value from poor materials

Examples

Despite limited resources, the team managed to make a silk purse of a sow's ear with their project.
The chef tried to make a silk purse of a sow's ear using subpar ingredients, but the result was impressive.
Even the best editor can't always make a silk purse of a sow's ear when the writing needs too much work.

Synset Examples

He really knows how to make a silk purse of a sow's ear.
It's hard to make a silk purse of a sow's ear with these materials.
She managed to make a silk purse of a sow's ear from that old dress.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: you can't make a silk purse out of a sow's ear - it's impossible to create something fine from inferior materials

Etymology

Based on the proverb 'You can't make a silk purse out of a sow's ear,' first recorded in the 16th century.
