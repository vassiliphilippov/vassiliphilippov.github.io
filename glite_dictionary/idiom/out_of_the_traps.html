out of the traps

1. ADV

/ aʊt əv ðə træps / (AmE), / aʊt əv ðə træps / (BrE)
(British) Beginning an activity quickly and energetically, especially at the very start of a competition or event.
to start quickly and energetically
british

Examples

The team was out of the traps and scored an early goal within the first five minutes.
She started out of the traps in her new job, impressing everyone with her enthusiasm.
Politicians were quick out of the traps to comment on the breaking news.

Synset Examples

The team raced out of the traps, eager to win.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: off to a flying start - to start very well or very quickly

Etymology

From greyhound and horse racing, where 'traps' are starting gates; first recorded in 20th-century British sports journalism.
