to touch the hem of someone's garment

1. VERB

/ tʌtʃ ðə hɛm əv ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈɡɑrmənt / (AmE), / tʌtʃ ðə hɛm əv ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈɡɑːm(ə)nt / (BrE)
to seek help, favor, or a blessing from someone important or revered, often by approaching them with humility or reverence
seek favor from someone important

Examples

Many fans traveled great distances just for a chance to touch the hem of her garment during the tour.
The young lawyer hoped to touch the hem of the judge's garment and gain his mentorship.
In their desperate situation, the villagers longed to touch the hem of his garment for support.

Synset Examples

She felt blessed to touch the hem of someone's garment.
He believed that to touch the hem of someone's garment would bring healing.
Many came to touch the hem of someone's garment for hope.

Etymology

From biblical accounts, especially the New Testament, where touching Jesus's garment was believed to bring healing.
