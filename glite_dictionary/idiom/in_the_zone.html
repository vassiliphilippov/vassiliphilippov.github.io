in the zone

1. ADJ

/ ɪn ðə zoʊn / (AmE), / ɪn ðə zəʊn / (BrE)
in a mental state of complete concentration and optimal performance, especially during an activity or task
focused and performing at one's best

Examples

During the final round, the golfer was completely in the zone and didn't miss a single shot.
When I'm coding late at night, I can really get in the zone and solve complex problems.
The team was in the zone throughout the match, dominating their opponents.

Synset Examples

He performed at his best, truly in the zone.
When I'm painting, I feel in the zone.
She was really in the zone during the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in the groove - performing smoothly and effectively, often rhythmically
