in for it

1. ADJ

/ ɪn fər ɪt / (AmE), / ɪn fə ɪt / (BrE)
facing an unpleasant situation or trouble, especially as a result of one's actions
about to face trouble or consequences
informal

Examples

If you break the vase, you'll really be in for it when Mom gets home.
When his boss finds out about the mistake, he's in for it.
They knew they were in for it as soon as the principal walked in.

Synset Examples

If he skips practice, he's really in for it.
They realized they were in for it once the storm hit.
She knew she was in for it after missing the deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in trouble - to be experiencing difficulties or danger
