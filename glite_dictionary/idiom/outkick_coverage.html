to outkick coverage

1. VERB

/ ˌaʊtˈkɪk ˈkʌv.ɚ.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌaʊtˈkɪk ˈkʌv.ər.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
to achieve more than was expected, or attempt something beyond one’s reasonable limits, especially in success, relationships, or opportunities
exceed one’s expected limits or achievements
informal american

Examples

Many people joked that he managed to outkick coverage when he married such a talented partner.
Don’t try to outkick coverage by applying for positions that are well beyond your experience.
After winning that prestigious award, she truly outkicked coverage in her career.

Synset Examples

He managed to outkick his coverage with that promotion.
You shouldn't outkick your coverage when applying for jobs.

Etymology

From American football, where a punter kicks the ball so far that the coverage team cannot catch up, leaving the returner with more space.
