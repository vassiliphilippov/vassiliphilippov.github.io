off the back foot

1. ADV

/ ˌɔf ðə ˌbæk ˈfʊt / (AmE), / ˌɒf ðə ˌbæk ˈfʊt / (BrE)
in a position of disadvantage, difficulty, or on the defensive
at a disadvantage; on the defensive

Examples

After the announcement, the team was immediately put off the back foot by their competitors.
He always seems to be off the back foot when tough questions are asked.
The new regulations left many small businesses off the back foot and struggling to adapt.

Synset Examples

After the setback, we were left off the back foot.
I'm often off the back foot when facing unexpected challenges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on the back foot - in a defensive or disadvantaged position; similar idiom
  • Antonym: on the front foot - in an advantaged, proactive, or aggressive position

Etymology

From cricket terminology, where playing off the back foot involves a defensive or reactive stance.
