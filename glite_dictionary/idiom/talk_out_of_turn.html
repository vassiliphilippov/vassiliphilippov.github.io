to talk out of turn

1. VERB

/ tɔk aʊt əv tɝn / (AmE), / tɔːk aʊt əv tɜːn / (BrE)
to speak when it is inappropriate or when one is not supposed to, especially in a social or formal setting
to speak inappropriately or unwisely

Examples

It's generally considered impolite to talk out of turn during official ceremonies.
She didn't mean to talk out of turn, but the news slipped out.
Children are often reminded not to talk out of turn in classrooms.

Synset Examples

It’s rude to talk out of turn while others are speaking.
Please don’t talk out of turn in the meeting.
He got scolded for talking out of turn in class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interrupt - to stop someone from speaking by speaking yourself
  • Synonym: speak out of turn - to speak when not appropriate or without permission
