Bob's your uncle

1. X

/ bɑbz jər ˈʌŋkəl / (AmE), / bɒbz jə ˈʌŋkl / (BrE)
used to say that something will be easy to achieve or that the result is simple and straightforward
used to indicate something is easy or straightforward
british

Examples

If you want to reset your phone, just follow the menu steps and Bob's your uncle!
Attach the wheels, tighten the bolts, and Bob's your uncle—your bike is ready to ride.
Add the ingredients, mix well, bake for 20 minutes, and Bob's your uncle, you've got delicious muffins.

Synset Examples

Just follow the instructions, and Bob's your uncle!

Etymology

Origin uncertain, but first recorded in the UK in the late 19th century, possibly referring to British politician Robert (Bob) Cecil.
