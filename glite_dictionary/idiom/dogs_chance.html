dog's chance

1. NOUN

/ dɔɡz tʃæns / (AmE), / dɒɡz tʃɑːns / (BrE)
A very slim or nonexistent possibility of success.
very little or no chance

Examples

With better preparation, he might have more than a dog's chance of passing the exam.
They knew they only had a dog's chance of convincing her to change her mind.
Without support from his team, he didn't stand a dog's chance in the competition.

Synset Examples

With that attitude, she has a dog's chance in the interview.
He has a dog's chance of winning without practicing.
You won't get a dog's chance if you don't apply.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long shot - an attempt or effort with little chance of success
  • Synonym: snowball's chance in hell - no chance at all of something happening
