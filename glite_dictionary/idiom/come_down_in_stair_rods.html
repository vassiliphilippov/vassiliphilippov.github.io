to come down in stair rods

1. VERB

/ kʌm daʊn ɪn stɛɚ rɑdz / (AmE), / kʌm daʊn ɪn steə rɒdz / (BrE)
(of rain) to fall very heavily; to rain intensely.
rain very heavily
british

Examples

It started to come down in stair rods just as the match began.
The weather was fine all morning, then it began to come down in stair rods in the afternoon.
If it comes down in stair rods tomorrow, we might have to postpone the wedding.

Synset Examples

We had to cancel the picnic since the rain came down in stair rods.
When it rains, it often comes down in stair rods around here.
The storm caused the rain to come down in stair rods all evening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pour down - to rain heavily

Etymology

British idiom; from the visual image of heavy rain appearing like stair rods (thin metal bars for holding carpets on stairs).
