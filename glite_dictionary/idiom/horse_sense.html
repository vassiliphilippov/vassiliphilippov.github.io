horse sense

1. NOUN

/ hɔrs sɛns / (AmE), / hɔːs sɛns / (BrE)
the ability to make sensible or practical decisions; sound common sense
sound practical judgment

Examples

She may not have a fancy education, but she has plenty of horse sense.
His horse sense told him not to invest in such a risky scheme.
You need a bit of horse sense to solve everyday problems effectively.

Synset Examples

Common sense is not so common
he hasn't got the sense God gave little green apples
fortunately she had the good sense to run away

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common sense - good sense and sound judgment in practical matters

Etymology

Originally American English, drawing a metaphor to horses being considered sensible animals.
