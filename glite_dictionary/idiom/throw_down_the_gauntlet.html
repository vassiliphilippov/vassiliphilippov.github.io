to throw down the gauntlet

1. VERB

/ θroʊ daʊn ðə ˈɡɔːntlət / (AmE), / θrəʊ daʊn ðə ˈɡɔːntlɪt / (BrE)
to publicly issue a challenge, especially in a bold or confrontational manner
issue a challenge boldly

Examples

The new CEO decided to throw down the gauntlet and demanded higher performance from every department.
When their team lost again, the coach threw down the gauntlet with a promise to turn things around.
By openly criticizing her opponent, she effectively threw down the gauntlet before the debate even began.

Synset Examples

He threw down the gauntlet and challenged his rivals.
He threw down the gauntlet for anyone to compete.
They threw down the gauntlet for the next match.
By challenging the champion, she threw down the gauntlet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: challenge - to invite someone to compete or prove something

Etymology

From the medieval practice of throwing a glove (gauntlet) as a challenge to duel, via Old French 'gantlet', from 'gant' (glove).
