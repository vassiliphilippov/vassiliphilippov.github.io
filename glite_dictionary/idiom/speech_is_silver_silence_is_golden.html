speech is silver, silence is golden

1. X

/ spiːtʃ ɪz ˈsɪlvɚ ˈsaɪləns ɪz ˈɡoʊldən / (AmE), / spiːtʃ ɪz ˈsɪlvə ˈsaɪləns ɪz ˈɡəʊldən / (BrE)
used to say that it is often better to remain silent than to speak unnecessarily, as silence is more valuable than unnecessary words
sometimes silence is better than speaking

Examples

Whenever he felt the urge to argue, he reminded himself that speech is silver, silence is golden.
My grandmother often said, "speech is silver, silence is golden," especially when we were gossiping.
At the board meeting, she wisely followed the rule that speech is silver, silence is golden.

Synset Examples

He believed that in conflict, speech is silver, silence is golden.
In arguments, remember, speech is silver, silence is golden.
Sometimes, it's best to embrace that speech is silver, silence is golden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silence is golden - the part of the saying emphasizing the value of silence

Etymology

A proverb of ancient origin, popularized in English in the 19th century, translated from German "Reden ist Silber, Schweigen ist Gold".
