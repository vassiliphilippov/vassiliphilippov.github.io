to show a clean pair of heels

1. VERB

/ ʃoʊ ə klin pɛr əv hilz / (AmE), / ʃəʊ ə kliːn peə əv hiːlz / (BrE)
to run away or escape quickly, especially to avoid danger or trouble
to run away quickly

Examples

As soon as the fire alarm rang, the students decided to show a clean pair of heels.
The thieves showed a clean pair of heels when they heard the police sirens.
Rather than argue, he preferred to show a clean pair of heels and leave quietly.

Synset Examples

He didn't hesitate to show a clean pair of heels when faced with trouble.
She decided to show a clean pair of heels and run.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flee - to run away from a place or situation of danger
  • Antonym: stand one's ground - to refuse to run away or retreat in the face of danger

Etymology

The phrase dates from the early 19th century, alluding to the view of someone's heels as they run swiftly away.
