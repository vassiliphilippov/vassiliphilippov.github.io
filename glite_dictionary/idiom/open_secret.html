open secret

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.pən ˈsiː.krət / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.pən ˈsiː.krɪt / (BrE)
something that is meant to be concealed but is actually widely known
widely known supposed secret

Examples

The fact that he was leaving the company was an open secret among the staff.
Their relationship became an open secret after they were seen together at several events.
It was an open secret in the town that the mayor planned to run for higher office.

Synset Examples
