to open a can of worms

1. VERB

/ ˈoʊpən ə kæn əv wɝmz / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən ə kæn əv wɜːmz / (BrE)
to do or say something that creates a complicated situation with many unexpected problems
create a complicated situation

Examples

Bringing up the old project at the meeting opened a can of worms we weren't prepared for.
When she started investigating the issue, she quickly opened a can of worms.
I didn't want to open a can of worms by mentioning the failed merger.

Synset Examples

He opened a can of worms with his comments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stir up trouble - to cause problems or difficulties
  • Synonym: to open Pandora's box - to cause many unforeseen troubles
