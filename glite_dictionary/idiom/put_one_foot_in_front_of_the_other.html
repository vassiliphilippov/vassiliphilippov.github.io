to put one foot in front of the other

1. VERB

/ pʊt wʌn fʊt ɪn frʌnt ʌv ði ˈʌðɚ / (AmE), / pʊt wʌn fʊt ɪn frʌnt əv ði ˈʌðə / (BrE)
to continue moving forward step by step, especially when facing difficulties or challenges
to keep moving forward, step by step

Examples

When life feels overwhelming, sometimes you just have to put one foot in front of the other and keep going.
Early in her recovery, she focused on simply putting one foot in front of the other each day.
As the marathon grew harder, he told himself to just put one foot in front of the other until the finish line.

Synset Examples

In tough times, just put one foot in front of the other.
When running, remember to put one foot in front of the other.
Just put one foot in front of the other and keep moving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keep going - to persist or continue without stopping
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop trying; to quit
