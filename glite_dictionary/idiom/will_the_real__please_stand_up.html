will the real ... please stand up

1. X

/ wɪl ðə ˈɹiːəl ˈpliːz stænd ʌp / (AmE), / wɪl ðə ˈɹɪəl ˈpliːz stænd ʌp / (BrE)
used to ask which person is genuine or to request someone to reveal their true identity or nature, especially among several similar or false possibilities
request to reveal the genuine person

Examples

After three people claimed responsibility, he said, "Will the real culprit please stand up?"
She joked, "Will the real Sarah please stand up?" when her friends pretended to be her.
In the meeting, he asked, "Will the real leader please stand up?" to address the confusion.

Synset Examples

At the meeting, I wondered, will the real someone please stand up?

Etymology

Popularized by the US television show 'To Tell the Truth' in the 1950s and 1960s, where the phrase was used to identify the genuine person among impostors.
