how the turntables

1. X

/ haʊ ðə ˈtɝːnˌteɪbəlz / (AmE), / haʊ ðə ˈtɜːnˌteɪbəlz / (BrE)
Used humorously to remark on an ironic reversal in circumstances, especially as a playful variation of 'how the tables have turned.'
humorous phrase noting an ironic reversal
informal

Examples

After years of being the underdog, he won the championship, saying, 'how the turntables.'
During the debate, when her opponent stumbled, she smiled and whispered, 'how the turntables.'
The notorious prankster was finally pranked himself, prompting laughter and the phrase 'how the turntables!'

Synset Examples

When she finally got the promotion, she exclaimed, 'How the turntables!'
He laughed and said, 'Oh, how the turntables have turned!'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: how the tables have turned - idiom meaning 'how circumstances have reversed'

Etymology

A playful variation of the idiom 'how the tables have turned,' popularized by its use in the American TV series 'The Office.'
