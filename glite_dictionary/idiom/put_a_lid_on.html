to put a lid on

1. VERB

/ pʊt ə lɪd ɑn / (AmE), / pʊt ə lɪd ɒn / (BrE)
to stop or limit something, especially to prevent it from continuing or becoming public
stop or limit something

Examples

The manager decided to put a lid on rumors by addressing the issue directly in the meeting.
After hours of debate, the chairperson asked everyone to put a lid on the discussion.
The company has tried to put a lid on leaks by tightening security procedures.

Synset Examples

It’s time to put a lid on the project until next year.
We need to put a lid on this gossip.
Let’s put a lid on the discussion for now.
She decided to put a lid on it and stay quiet.
Can you please put a lid on it until we finish?
He told her to put a lid on it during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shut down - to stop something from operating or continuing
  • Synonym: to suppress - to prevent something from being expressed or known
  • Antonym: to encourage - to give support or promote growth of something
