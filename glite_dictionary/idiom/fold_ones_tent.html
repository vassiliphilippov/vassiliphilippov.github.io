to fold one's tent

1. VERB

/ foʊld wʌnz tɛnt / (AmE), / fəʊld wʌnz tɛnt / (BrE)
to withdraw or retreat discreetly from a situation, often in the face of difficulty or defeat
withdraw or retreat discreetly

Examples

After several failed attempts to negotiate, the company decided to fold its tent and abandon the deal.
Rather than admit defeat in public, she chose to fold her tent and step away quietly.
Sometimes it's wiser to recognize a lost cause and simply fold your tent.

Synset Examples

He decided to fold his tent and leave the debate.
After the storm, it was time to fold my tent.
It's best to fold your tent when things get tough.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retreat - to withdraw from a position or situation
  • Antonym: advance - to move forward, especially in position or progress

Etymology

From the literal act of folding a tent to leave a campsite, used figuratively in English since the 19th century; popularized by the phrase "fold their tents" in Henry Wadsworth Longfellow's 1850 poem "The Day is Done".
