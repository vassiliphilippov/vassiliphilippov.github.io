to take a dim view

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə dɪm vju / (AmE), / teɪk ə dɪm vjuː / (BrE)
to disapprove of or have a negative opinion about something
disapprove; have a negative opinion

Examples

Many parents take a dim view of letting their children stay out late at night.
The committee took a dim view of the proposal, citing serious concerns.
Most teachers take a dim view of cheating on exams.

Synset Examples

Experts take a dim view of the proposed changes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: approve of - to think that someone or something is good or acceptable
  • Synonym: disapprove of - to have a negative or unfavorable opinion about something
