to heart bleed

1. VERB

/ hɑrt bliːd / (AmE), / hɑːt bliːd / (BrE)
to feel deep emotional pain or sympathy for someone else's suffering or misfortune
feel deep emotional pain or sympathy

Examples

When she saw the devastation left by the hurricane, her heart bleed for the victims.
His heart bled for the abandoned animals at the shelter.
Every time he heard of injustice, his heart bleeds for those affected.

Synset Examples

He felt his heart bleed for the suffering children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ache - to feel continuous pain or sorrow
  • Synonym: to empathize - to understand and share the feelings of another
