to own the place

1. VERB

/ oʊn ðə pleɪs / (AmE), / əʊn ðə pleɪs / (BrE)
to behave with confidence or arrogance, as if one has complete authority or entitlement in a particular setting
act with confidence as if in charge
informal

Examples

When she walks into the room, she acts like she owns the place.
He strutted through the lobby as though he owned the place, ignoring everyone else.
You can't just walk in here and behave like you own the place!

Synset Examples

She acts like she owns the place.
He walked into the meeting like someone owns the place.
He moves around the office like someone owns the place.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take over - to assume control or possession of something
  • Synonym: to lord it over - to act in a domineering or overbearing way
