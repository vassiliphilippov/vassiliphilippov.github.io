to stay on the ball

1. VERB

/ steɪ ɑn ðə bɔl / (AmE), / steɪ ɒn ðə bɔːl / (BrE)
to remain alert, attentive, and ready to respond to events or changes
remain alert and attentive

Examples

You have to stay on the ball if you want to catch mistakes before they happen.
Teachers need to stay on the ball to manage a lively classroom.
In this fast-paced industry, it's important to stay on the ball at all times.

Synset Examples

If you want to succeed, you need to stay on the ball.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on the ball - alert, attentive, and quick to react
