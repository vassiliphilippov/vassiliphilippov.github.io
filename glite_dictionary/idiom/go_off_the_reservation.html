to go off the reservation

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ɔf ðə ˌrɛzərˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɒf ðə ˌrɛzəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
to act independently or outside accepted boundaries, often in a way that is unexpected or contrary to group expectations
act independently; behave unpredictably

Examples

If you go off the reservation during negotiations, the team may feel frustrated.
She really went off the reservation with that unexpected decision.
He's known to go off the reservation when discussing new ideas.

Synset Examples

If you go off the reservation, expect pushback from the team.
When she suggested that change, I knew she had gone off the reservation.
His comments were completely off the reservation.
That idea seems off the reservation to me.
He tends to go off the reservation during meetings.
She’s acting a bit off the reservation lately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to act out - to behave badly or inappropriately, especially in a disruptive way
  • Antonym: follow the rules - to adhere to established procedures or expectations

Etymology

Originally referred to Native Americans leaving government-assigned reservations, now used colloquially; please note some consider the idiom insensitive.
