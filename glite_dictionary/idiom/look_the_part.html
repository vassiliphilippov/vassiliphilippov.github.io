to look the part

1. VERB

/ lʊk ðə pɑrt / (AmE), / lʊk ðə pɑːt / (BrE)
to have an appearance or style considered appropriate for a particular role, job, or occasion
have a suitable appearance for a role

Examples

She tried to look the part by wearing a sharp suit to the meeting.
If you're auditioning for the hero, you definitely look the part.
He may be inexperienced, but he certainly looks the part of a manager.

Synset Examples

She dressed elegantly to look the part for the event.
To succeed in interviews, you must look the part.
He didn’t look the part of a manager in casual clothes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: look the part of - have an appearance appropriate for a specified role
