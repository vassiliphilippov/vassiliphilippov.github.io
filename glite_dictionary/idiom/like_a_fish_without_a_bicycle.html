like a fish without a bicycle

1. X

/ laɪk ə fɪʃ wɪðaʊt ə ˈbaɪ.sɪ.kəl / (AmE), / laɪk ə fɪʃ wɪðˈaʊt ə ˈbaɪ.sɪ.kl̩ / (BrE)
Used to suggest that something is completely unnecessary or irrelevant, especially in the context of women's independence from men.
something unnecessary or irrelevant

Examples

She declared that needing approval from others was like a fish without a bicycle—completely unnecessary.
For many, learning to cook before moving out is like a fish without a bicycle, as takeout is always an option.
Insisting that all employees wear ties seemed like a fish without a bicycle to the new manager.

Synset Examples

Her motto is that a woman without a man is like a fish without a bicycle.
He believed that a woman without a man is like a fish without a bicycle.
She proudly proclaimed that a woman without a man is like a fish without a bicycle.

Etymology

Popularized in the 1970s feminist movement, reportedly coined by Australian feminist Irina Dunn as a slogan for women's independence.
