to keep a cool head

1. VERB

/ kip ə kul hɛd / (AmE), / kiːp ə kuːl hɛd / (BrE)
to remain calm and not become angry or excited, especially in a difficult situation
stay calm under pressure

Examples

During the heated negotiations, she managed to keep a cool head and find a solution.
It's not always easy to keep a cool head when everyone around you is panicking.
Paramedics are trained to keep a cool head in emergency situations.

Synset Examples

It's important to keep a cool head in stressful situations.
She managed to keep a cool head during the crisis.
He always knows how to keep a cool head in emergencies.
It's important to stay calm and keep a cool head.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to lose one's head - to lose control or become very upset in a difficult situation
  • Synonym: keep one's composure - to stay calm and not show emotion in a stressful situation
