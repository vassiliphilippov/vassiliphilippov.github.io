everything under the sun

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛvriˌθɪŋ ˌʌndɚ ðə sʌn / (AmE), / ˈevriˌθɪŋ ˌʌndə ðə sʌn / (BrE)
a very large or complete range of things; everything imaginable.
a large variety of things

Examples

I feel like I've read everything under the sun about this topic, but I'm still confused.
The hotel offers everything under the sun for its guests, from tennis courts to spa treatments.
They argued about everything under the sun during their long road trip.

Synset Examples

She has tried everything under the sun to lose weight.
They sell everything under the sun at that market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: all - the whole quantity or extent of a particular group or thing
  • Synonym: anything and everything - a wide range of possible things; all sorts of things

Etymology

Based on the phrase 'under the sun', meaning 'on earth' or 'in existence', which dates back to the Bible (Ecclesiastes).
