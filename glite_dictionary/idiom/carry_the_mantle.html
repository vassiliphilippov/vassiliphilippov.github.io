to carry the mantle

1. VERB

/ ˈkæri ðə ˈmæntəl / (AmE), / ˈkæri ðə ˈmæntəl / (BrE)
to take on responsibility or assume a position of leadership, especially as a successor to someone else
assume responsibility or leadership

Examples

After her predecessor retired, she was eager to carry the mantle of team leader.
It's important for the next generation to carry the mantle of environmental stewardship.
When the CEO resigned, Mark was chosen to carry the mantle and guide the company forward.

Synset Examples

He will carry the mantle of responsibility for this project.
It's time for us to carry the mantle of innovation.
She was ready to carry the mantle of leadership after his departure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take up the mantle - to accept responsibility or a role formerly held by someone else

Etymology

From the historical practice of passing a literal cloak or mantle to a successor as a symbol of office or authority; 'mantle' from Latin 'mantellum' (cloak).
