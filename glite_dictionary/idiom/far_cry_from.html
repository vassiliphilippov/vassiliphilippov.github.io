far cry from

1. X

/ fɑr kraɪ frəm / (AmE), / fɑː kraɪ frɒm / (BrE)
used to say that something is very different from something else, especially in a way that is disappointing or surprising
very different from

Examples

The new design is a far cry from the original version everyone loved.
Life in the city is a far cry from the quiet village she grew up in.
The movie was a far cry from what the trailers promised.

Synset Examples

That restaurant is a far cry from what it used to be.
Her new job is a far cry from her previous role.
