clean slate

1. NOUN

/ klin sleɪt / (AmE), / kliːn sleɪt / (BrE)
a situation in which one is given the chance to start anew, free from previous mistakes or prejudices
opportunity to start anew

Examples

After the bankruptcy, she viewed moving to a new city as a clean slate.
The new management promised employees a clean slate and a chance to rebuild trust.
Forgiveness allows both people to start with a clean slate in their relationship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fresh start - a new beginning
  • Synonym: second chance - an opportunity to try again

Etymology

Refers metaphorically to a slate (writing board) wiped clean of marks, symbolizing a fresh start.
