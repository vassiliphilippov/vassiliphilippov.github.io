to have kittens

1. VERB

/ hæv ˈkɪtn̩z / (AmE), / hæv ˈkɪtn̩z / (BrE)
to become extremely upset, angry, or anxious, often in an exaggerated way
become very upset or angry
informal

Examples

When she saw the mess in the kitchen, she nearly had kittens.
My mother always has kittens if I'm even a few minutes late coming home.
He was having kittens over the possibility of losing his passport before the flight.

Synset Examples

The professor combusted when the student didn't know the answer to a very elementary question
Spam makes me go ballistic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freak out - to suddenly become very agitated or upset
  • Antonym: remain calm - to not become agitated or upset
