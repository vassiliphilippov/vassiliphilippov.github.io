to kick to the curb

1. VERB

/ kɪk tə ðə kɝb / (AmE), / kɪk tə ðə kɜːb / (BrE)
To reject or discard someone or something as no longer wanted or useful.
reject or discard; get rid of
informal

Examples

She finally decided to kick to the curb her toxic friendship after years of stress.
If you don't treat your customers well, they'll kick to the curb your business.
Many people choose to kick to the curb unhealthy snacks after New Year's.

Synset Examples

It's time to kick those bad habits to the curb.
The company kicked the outdated policies to the curb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get rid of - to remove or discard
  • Antonym: hold on to - to keep or retain
