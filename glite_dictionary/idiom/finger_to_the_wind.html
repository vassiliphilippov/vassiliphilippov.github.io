finger to the wind

1. X

/ ˈfɪŋɡər tə ðə wɪnd / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡə tə ðə wɪnd / (BrE)
used to refer to the act of assessing the prevailing mood, opinion, or direction of events, especially for making decisions or anticipating change.
to assess the current mood or direction

Examples

With his finger to the wind, he sensed the board’s opinion was changing.
Politicians often keep a finger to the wind to stay aligned with public sentiment.
She always has her finger to the wind when making major business decisions.

Synset Examples

With his finger to the wind, he sensed a change in mood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to read the room - to assess the mood or atmosphere of a group

Etymology

From the literal practice of moistening a finger and holding it up to detect the wind’s direction; used figuratively since the 19th century.
