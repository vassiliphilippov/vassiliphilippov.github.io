to drive (someone) crazy

1. VERB

/ draɪv ˈsʌmwʌn ˈkreɪzi / (AmE), / draɪv ˈsʌmwʌn ˈkreɪzi / (BrE)
to make someone extremely annoyed, upset, or frustrated
make someone very annoyed or frustrated

Examples

The constant construction noise is driving me crazy and I can't concentrate at all.
She loves her little brother, but sometimes his endless questions drive her crazy.
If I hear that song one more time, it will drive me crazy!

Synset Examples

The noise next door is driving me crazy.
This fricking traffic is driving me crazy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drive (someone) mad - to make someone feel extremely annoyed or upset
  • Synonym: drive (someone) up the wall - to make someone very annoyed or irritated
