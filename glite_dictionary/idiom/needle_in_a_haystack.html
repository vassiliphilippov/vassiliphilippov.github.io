needle in a haystack

1. NOUN

/ ˈniːdəl ɪn ə ˈheɪˌstæk / (AmE), / ˈniːdl ɪn ə ˈheɪstak / (BrE)
something that is very difficult or almost impossible to find due to being hidden among many other things
a very hard-to-find thing

Examples

Finding the missing file in these archives is like searching for a needle in a haystack.
Tracking down her birth parents turned out to be a needle in a haystack situation.
He described the hunt for evidence as looking for a needle in a haystack.

Synset Examples

Locating that rare book was like finding a needle in a haystack.
Her lost earring was a needle in a haystack at the beach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: looking for a needle in a haystack - alternate phrasing of the idiom

Etymology

Refers to the difficulty of finding a small object (needle) within a mass of hay; first recorded in early 19th-century English.
