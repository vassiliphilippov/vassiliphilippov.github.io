to fall by the wayside

1. VERB

/ fɔl baɪ ðə ˈweɪsaɪd / (AmE), / fɔːl baɪ ðə ˈweɪsaɪd / (BrE)
to stop participating or being successful, especially because of difficulties or lack of support; to give up or fail to continue
to give up or stop participating

Examples

Many small businesses fall by the wayside during an economic recession.
Several marathon runners fell by the wayside before reaching the halfway point.
Her dreams of becoming a professional musician eventually fell by the wayside after years of struggling.

Synset Examples

In the second round, the challenger gave up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give up - to stop trying or surrender
  • Antonym: to persevere - to keep going despite difficulties

Etymology

From the literal sense of something being left or lost at the side of a road, with figurative use in English since at least the 19th century.

2. VERB

/ fɔl baɪ ðə ˈweɪsaɪd / (AmE), / fɔːl baɪ ðə ˈweɪsaɪd / (BrE)
to become obsolete, neglected, or fail to continue; to stop being active, used, or successful
become neglected or fail to continue

Examples

Many small startups fall by the wayside before reaching profitability.
Old customs often fall by the wayside as technology evolves.
When training for a marathon, some runners fall by the wayside due to injuries or loss of motivation.

Synset Examples

Many traditions fall by the wayside as society changes.
Some businesses fall by the wayside during tough economic times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: become obsolete - to no longer be in use or useful
  • Synonym: go out of fashion - to stop being popular or widely used
  • Antonym: to survive - to continue to exist despite difficulties

Etymology

From the image of something discarded or abandoned at the side of a road ('wayside').

Synset POS: None
