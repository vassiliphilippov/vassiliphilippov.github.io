to mean business

1. VERB

/ miːn ˈbɪznɪs / (AmE), / miːn ˈbɪznɪs / (BrE)
to be serious and determined about what one is doing or saying, not joking or bluffing
to be serious and determined

Examples

When the manager raised her voice, everyone knew she meant business about the new policy.
The look on his face showed he meant business and wouldn't accept excuses.
You can tell by the team's attitude that they mean business this year.

Synset Examples

When she entered the room, everyone knew she meant business.
The coach said he meant business this season.
His stern look made it clear he meant business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be serious - not joking; in earnest
  • Antonym: be joking - not being serious; playful or unserious

2. VERB

/ min ˈbɪznəs / (AmE), / miːn ˈbɪznəs / (BrE)
to be serious and determined to achieve something, not joking or being casual
to be serious and determined

Examples

The coach's stern expression showed he meant business during training.
If you don't finish the assignment by Friday, the boss will know you mean business.
When the police arrived in full gear, it was clear they meant business.

Synset Examples

She means bidness when it comes to closing that deal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be in earnest - to be sincere and serious
  • Antonym: joke - something said or done to make people laugh (contrast with being serious)
