to gather rosebuds

1. VERB

/ ˈɡæðər ˈroʊzbʌdz / (AmE), / ˈɡæðə ˈrəʊzbʌdz / (BrE)
to seize opportunities and enjoy life while you can
seize opportunities while they last

Examples

In her graduation speech, she urged her classmates to gather rosebuds and make the most of new opportunities.
They decided to travel the world now, believing it was the perfect time to gather rosebuds.
Remember to gather rosebuds and cherish every joyful moment you can.

Synset Examples

Life is short; let's gather rosebuds and enjoy it.
We should gather rosebuds while we have the chance.
He always reminded us to gather rosebuds before it's too late.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carpe diem - the urge to seize the day and enjoy the present moment

Etymology

From the poem 'To the Virgins, to Make Much of Time' by Robert Herrick (1648), with the line 'Gather ye rosebuds while ye may.'
