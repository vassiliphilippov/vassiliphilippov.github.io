to air dirty linen

1. VERB

/ ɛr ˈdɜrti ˈlɪnən / (AmE), / eə ˈdɜːti ˈlɪnɪn / (BrE)
to discuss private or embarrassing personal matters in public, especially those that may cause shame or disapproval
discuss private matters publicly

Examples

She refused to air dirty linen in public, preferring to keep family issues private.
It's unwise to air dirty linen at work meetings, where others need not hear personal conflicts.
Despite pressure from the media, the politician avoided airing dirty linen about his colleagues.

Synset Examples

She refused to air her dirty linen in public during the interview.
It's better to resolve issues privately than air your dirty linen in public.
The couple decided not to air their dirty linen in public.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: air one's dirty laundry - to discuss private or embarrassing matters in public (variant idiom)

Etymology

From the metaphor of washing one's dirty laundry—meaning personal or family matters—where 'airing' implies making them public.
