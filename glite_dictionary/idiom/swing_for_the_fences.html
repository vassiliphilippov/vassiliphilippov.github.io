to swing for the fences

1. VERB

/ swɪŋ fər ðə ˈfɛnsɪz / (AmE), / swɪŋ fə ðə ˈfɛnsɪz / (BrE)
to attempt something ambitious or daring, especially by taking a significant risk for a potentially large reward
take a big risk for a large reward

Examples

When the company faced bankruptcy, its CEO decided to swing for the fences and launched an entirely new product line.
If you want to get noticed in this industry, sometimes you just have to swing for the fences.
Realizing it was his last chance, he chose to swing for the fences with his proposal.

Synset Examples

He decided to swing for the fences on his final presentation.
The team knew they had to swing for the fences to win the championship.
In her job interview, she chose to swing for the fences and showcase her bold ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take a chance - to do something that involves risk
  • Antonym: to play it safe - to avoid taking risks

Etymology

From baseball terminology in the United States, where a batter aims to hit the ball over the fence for a home run.
