to throw a tantrum

1. VERB

/ θroʊ ə ˈtæntrəm / (AmE), / θrəʊ ə ˈtæntrəm / (BrE)
to have a sudden, uncontrolled display of anger or frustration, often in a dramatic way
to have an angry outburst

Examples

The toddler began to throw a tantrum when his mother refused to buy him candy.
She threatened to throw a tantrum if she didn’t get her way at the meeting.
Whenever things don’t go his way, he tends to throw a tantrum.

Synset Examples

The toddler threw a tantrum over the toy.
The manbaby threw a tantrum in the store.
The fanbrat threw a tantrum when his team lost.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to have a fit - to become very angry or upset suddenly
  • Antonym: to keep one's cool - to stay calm and controlled in a difficult situation
