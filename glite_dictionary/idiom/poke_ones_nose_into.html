to poke one's nose into

1. VERB

/ poʊk wʌnz noʊz ˈɪntu / (AmE), / pəʊk wʌnz nəʊz ˈɪntu / (BrE)
to interfere in someone else's affairs, usually in an unwelcome or intrusive way
interfere in others' affairs

Examples

She really shouldn't poke her nose into other people's personal matters.
I dislike it when colleagues poke their noses into my work without asking.
Please don't poke your nose into things that aren't your concern.

Synset Examples

He always pokes his nose into my business.
I wish she wouldn’t poke her nose into my affairs.
Don't poke your nose into matters that don't concern you.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to meddle - to interfere in something that is not one's concern
