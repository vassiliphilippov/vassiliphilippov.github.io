to keep ideas fallow

1. VERB

/ kip aɪˈdiəz ˈfæloʊ / (AmE), / kiːp aɪˈdɪəz ˈfæləʊ / (BrE)
to intentionally leave ideas undeveloped or unused for a period, allowing them to mature or be used later
leave ideas undeveloped for later use

Examples

Sometimes it's wise to keep ideas fallow until inspiration strikes again.
The team chose to keep ideas fallow while focusing on urgent tasks.
Creative people often keep ideas fallow before returning with fresh perspective.

Synset Examples

They decided to keep their ideas fallow until the right time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to put on the back burner - to postpone consideration or development of something
  • Synonym: to shelve - to postpone or temporarily discard an idea or project

Etymology

Figurative use based on 'fallow', from agriculture, meaning land left unplanted to restore its fertility.
