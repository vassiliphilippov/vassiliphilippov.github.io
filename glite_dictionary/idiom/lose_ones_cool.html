to lose one's cool

1. VERB

/ luːz wʌnz kuːl / (AmE), / luːz wʌnz kuːl / (BrE)
to suddenly become angry or lose self-control, especially in a stressful situation
become angry; lose self-control

Examples

Even when provoked, Maria rarely loses her cool in front of the team.
Try not to lose your cool when dealing with difficult customers at work.
He lost his cool after hearing the unfair criticism from his boss.

Synset Examples

He managed to lose his cool during the heated discussion.
Don't lose your cool; just breathe and relax.
After waiting for hours, she finally lost her cool.
He lost his cool, but I reminded him, temper temper.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to keep one's cool - to remain calm and not become angry
  • Synonym: to lose one's temper - to become suddenly very angry
