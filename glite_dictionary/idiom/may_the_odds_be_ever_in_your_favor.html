may the odds be ever in your favor

1. X

/ meɪ ði ɑdz bi ˈɛvɚ ɪn jʊr ˈfeɪvɚ / (AmE), / meɪ ði ɒdz bi ˈɛvə ɪn jɔː ˈfeɪvə / (BrE)
used to wish someone good luck, often with an awareness that success depends on chance
wish of good luck, often referencing chance

Examples

Before the interview, she texted, 'may the odds be ever in your favor,' hoping it would help calm his nerves.
The teacher smiled at the class before the big exam and said, 'may the odds be ever in your favor.'
Whenever we play board games, he dramatically proclaims, 'may the odds be ever in your favor,' in a mock-serious tone.

Synset Examples

He raised his glass and toasted, 'May the odds be ever in your favor!'
As she left for the audition, her friends shouted, 'May the odds be ever in your favor!'
Before the competition, they all wished, 'May the odds be ever in your favor!'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: good luck - an expression of wishing someone success
  • Synonym: to break a leg - idiom wishing someone good luck, especially before a performance

Etymology

Popularized by the novel and film series The Hunger Games (2008, Suzanne Collins).
