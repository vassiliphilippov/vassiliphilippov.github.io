son of a gun

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌn əv ə ˈɡʌn / (AmE), / ˌsʌn əv ə ˈɡʌn / (BrE)
(informal) An affectionate or admiring term for a person, often implying toughness, mischievousness, or remarkable qualities; also used as an exclamation of surprise or admiration.
an admirable or tough person (informal)
informal

Examples

That son of a gun fixed the engine in ten minutes.
I'll be a son of a gun—you actually finished the marathon!
She called him a clever son of a gun for solving the puzzle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rascal - a mischievous or cheeky person
  • Synonym: scoundrel - a person who behaves badly, often in a way that is not illegal

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly a euphemistic alteration of "son of a bitch" dating from 17th–18th century British naval slang.
