to pop the question

1. VERB

/ pɑp ðə ˈkwɛs.tʃən / (AmE), / pɒp ðə ˈkwɛs.tʃən / (BrE)
to ask someone to marry you; to propose marriage.
propose marriage

Examples

He finally popped the question after five years of dating.
During their romantic dinner, Jake decided it was the perfect moment to pop the question.
Everyone was surprised when Sarah popped the question instead of waiting for her boyfriend to do it.

Synset Examples

he popped the question on Sunday night
she proposed marriage to the man she had known for only two months
The old bachelor finally declared himself to the young woman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to propose - to ask someone to marry you

Etymology

Originated in the early 19th century, using 'pop' in the sense of 'to bring out suddenly.'
