embarrassment of riches

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈbɛrəsmənt əv ˈrɪtʃɪz / (AmE), / ɪmˈbærəsmənt əv ˈrɪtʃɪz / (BrE)
a situation in which there is such an abundance of valuable options or resources that it becomes difficult to choose or manage
overwhelming abundance of valuable things

Examples

She faced an embarrassment of riches when selecting which college to attend.
After the harvest, the village had an embarrassment of riches and didn't know what to do with the surplus.
The director had an embarrassment of riches with so many talented actors auditioning for the role.

Synset Examples

Her talent led to an embarrassment of riches in opportunities.
With so many job offers, he had an embarrassment of riches.
The charity faced an embarrassment of riches in donations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overabundance - excessive quantity, more than is needed or wanted
  • Antonym: shortage - insufficient amount, lack or scarcity

Etymology

Originally a literal translation of French 'l'embarras des richesses' (18th century), meaning a difficulty arising from having too many good things.
