to step over the line

1. VERB

/ stɛp oʊvɚ ðə laɪn / (AmE), / stɛp əʊvə ðə laɪn / (BrE)
to cross a physical or metaphorical boundary or limit, often going beyond what is considered acceptable
cross a boundary or limit

Examples

If you step over the line at work, you might face serious consequences.
The comedian's joke stepped over the line and offended many people.
He realized he was stepping over the line when he started raising his voice.

Synset Examples

Don’t step over the line marked on the floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cross the line - to go beyond what is acceptable or allowed
  • Synonym: to go too far - to exceed the limits of acceptable behavior
