to rue the day

1. VERB

/ ru ðə deɪ / (AmE), / ruː ðə deɪ / (BrE)
to bitterly regret a particular event, decision, or action in the past
bitterly regret a past event or decision

Examples

If the new law fails, many politicians will rue the day they supported it.
You will rue the day you broke that promise, mark my words.
She knew he would rue the day he walked away from the deal.

Synset Examples

They might rue the day they underestimated her.
He will rue the day he decided to quit.
She'll rue the day she ignored his advice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regret - feel sad or disappointed about something in the past

Etymology

From Middle English 'rue' (to feel sorrow or regret), with 'the day' signifying a specific occasion; used since at least the 16th century.
