below the belt

1. ADV

/ bəˈloʊ ðə bɛlt / (AmE), / bɪˈləʊ ðə bɛlt / (BrE)
in a way that is unfair or unsporting, typically referring to remarks or actions intended to hurt someone unjustly
unfairly or hurtfully

Examples

Her comments about his family were below the belt and hurt him deeply.
Criticizing someone's appearance during a debate is going below the belt.
I thought your joke last night was below the belt; it made everyone uncomfortable.

Synset Examples

they dealt with him unfairly
their accusations hit below the belt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unfair - not just, not right or not reasonable
  • Antonym: above board - open and honest; legal or fair

Etymology

From boxing, where striking below the opponent's belt is illegal; figurative use dates from the late 19th century.

2. ADJ

/ bɪˈloʊ ðə bɛlt / (AmE), / bɪˈləʊ ðə belt / (BrE)
(of a remark or action) unfair or cruel; disregarding the accepted rules of behavior, especially in a way that is considered unacceptable or offensive.
unfair or cruel (remark or action)

Examples

His comment about her appearance was really below the belt.
Making jokes about someone's personal life is below the belt.
That was a below the belt attack during the debate.

Synset Examples

her accusations were below the belt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unfair - not just or equitable
  • Synonym: dirty - morally unprincipled or dishonorable (in this sense, as in 'dirty trick')

Etymology

From boxing, where striking below the belt is against the rules.

3. ADV

/ bəˈloʊ ðə bɛlt / (AmE), / bɪˈləʊ ðə bɛlt / (BrE)
unfair or cruel, especially in a way that is considered inappropriate or personal
unfair or cruel (idiom)

Examples

His criticism of her family was below the belt and completely unnecessary.
Some of the jokes in the debate were below the belt and offended several people.
Accusing him of dishonesty was really below the belt in that situation.

Synset Examples

Making fun of her appearance was below the belt.
His comment was really below the belt.
Criticizing her work was a blow below the belt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unfair - not based on or behaving according to the principles of equality and justice
  • Synonym: cruel - wilfully causing pain or suffering to others
  • Antonym: fair - in accordance with the rules or standards

Etymology

From boxing, where hitting an opponent below the waist is against the rules.
