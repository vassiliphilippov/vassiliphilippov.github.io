to cop yourself on

1. VERB

/ kɑp jərˈsɛlf ɑn / (AmE), / kɒp jəˈsɛlf ɒn / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly Irish English) To start behaving in a more responsible or sensible way; to get your act together.
behave more responsibly; get your act together
informal british

Examples

It's time to cop yourself on and make some real changes in your life.
You need to cop yourself on if you want to keep this job.
After failing his exams, Mark finally decided to cop himself on.

Synset Examples

It's time to cop yourself on and be responsible.
You need to cop yourself on if you want to succeed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get your act together - to become organized and effective; to behave responsibly

Etymology

Possibly from Irish English usage; 'cop' here means 'catch' or 'realize'.
