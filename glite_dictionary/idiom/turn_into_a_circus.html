to turn into a circus

1. VERB

/ tɝn ˈɪntu ə ˈsɝkəs / (AmE), / tɜːn ˈɪntuː ə ˈsɜːkəs / (BrE)
to become disorganized, chaotic, and noisy, often in a way that is entertaining or uncontrollable
become chaotic or disorderly

Examples

The meeting quickly turned into a circus when everyone started shouting their opinions at once.
As soon as the news broke, the quiet office turned into a circus of activity and excitement.
What was supposed to be a simple dinner party turned into a circus thanks to the unexpected guests.

Synset Examples

Every family gathering turns into a circus with the kids playing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bedlam - a scene of uproar and confusion
  • Synonym: to run amok - to behave in a frenzied, uncontrolled way
  • Antonym: to calm down - to become quiet and orderly
