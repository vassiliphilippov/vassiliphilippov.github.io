to dance with the one that brought you

1. VERB

/ dæns wɪð ðə wʌn ðət ˈbrɔt ju / (AmE), / dɑːns wɪð ðə wʌn ðət ˈbrɔːt juː / (BrE)
to remain loyal to or maintain allegiance with those who have supported you, especially in uncertain or changing circumstances
stay loyal to those who helped you

Examples

In tough times, it's important to dance with the one that brought you and support your long-time partners.
He considered switching teams but decided to dance with the one that brought you out of loyalty.
Successful leaders often dance with the one that brought you instead of chasing short-term trends.

Synset Examples

In business, always dance with the one that brought you success.
In relationships, it's wise to dance with the one that brought you.
When in doubt, dance with the one that brought you to the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stick with - to remain with or continue to support someone or something
  • Antonym: to betray - to be disloyal to someone who has helped or trusted you
