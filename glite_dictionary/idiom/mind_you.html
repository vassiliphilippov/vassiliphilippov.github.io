mind you

1. PART

/ maɪnd ju / (AmE), / maɪnd ju / (BrE)
used to emphasize a statement, or to introduce a qualification or contrast to what was previously said
used to emphasize or qualify a statement

Examples

He's very friendly—mind you, he can be quite stubborn at times.
The hike was easy, mind you, the weather wasn't great.
The place is expensive; mind you, the service is excellent.

Synset Examples

He’s quite talented, mind you.
It was a tough race, mind you.
I love her cooking, mind you.
