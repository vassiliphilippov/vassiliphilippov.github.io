to kick the habit

1. VERB

/ kɪk ðə ˈhæbɪt / (AmE), / kɪk ðə ˈhæbɪt / (BrE)
to stop a bad habit or a regular, undesirable behavior, especially one that is difficult to give up
give up a bad habit

Examples

After years of trying, he finally managed to kick the habit of biting his nails.
Many people struggle to kick the habit when it comes to smoking.
She joined a support group to help her kick the habit of overeating.

Synset Examples

Many find it hard to kick the habit of junk food.
It took her years to kick the habit of procrastination.
He finally managed to kick the habit of smoking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to quit - to stop doing something, especially a habit, activity, or job
