point of departure

1. NOUN

/ pɔɪnt əv dɪˈpɑrtʃər / (AmE), / pɔɪnt əv dɪˈpɑːtʃə / (BrE)
the place, time, or idea at which something begins; the starting point for an activity, discussion, or process
starting point for an activity or idea

Examples

The author's childhood served as the point of departure for her memoir.
In our meeting, we used last year's report as a point of departure for discussions.
The theory takes traditional models as its point of departure before proposing new ideas.

Synset Examples

he uses other people's ideas as a springboard for his own
reality provides the jumping-off point for his illusions
the point of departure of international comparison cannot be an institution but must be the function it carries out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: springboard - something that serves as a launching point for an activity
  • Synonym: jumping-off point - a place or idea from which something begins
