to hold it together

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ɪt təˈɡɛðər / (AmE), / həʊld ɪt təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to remain calm and maintain self-control, especially in a difficult situation
maintain composure or control

Examples

After hearing the shocking news, she struggled to hold it together in front of her friends.
He managed to hold it together during the crisis and supported his family.
Sometimes, it's hard to hold it together when everything seems to go wrong.

Synset Examples

He struggled to hold it together after the bad news.
In tough moments, you must hold it together for your team.
She tried to hold it together during the stressful meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keep one's cool - to stay calm in a stressful situation
  • Antonym: to lose it - to lose control of one's emotions
