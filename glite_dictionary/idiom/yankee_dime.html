Yankee dime

1. NOUN

/ ˈjæŋki daɪm / (AmE), / ˈjaŋki daɪm / (BrE)
a small, trivial amount of money or a minor favor, often used jokingly to mean something given as a token rather than real payment
trivial amount; small favor (idiom)
american

Examples

He offered nothing but a Yankee dime for all my trouble.
After hours of help, all she gave me was a Yankee dime.
A Yankee dime doesn’t pay the bills, but it can lift your spirits.

Synset Examples

It was just a Yankee dime, but it meant a lot.
She said a Yankee dime was all it took to help.
He called it a Yankee dime for the small favor he did.

Etymology

American English idiom; 'Yankee' refers to an American, often from the Northern states; 'dime' as a small coin, together suggesting something of little value or significance.
