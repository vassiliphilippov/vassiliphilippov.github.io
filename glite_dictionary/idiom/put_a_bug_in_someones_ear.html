to put a bug in someone's ear

1. VERB

/ pʊt ə bʌɡ ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz ɪr / (AmE), / pʊt ə bʌɡ ɪn ˈsʌmwənz ɪə / (BrE)
to suggest an idea or hint to someone, often to prompt future action or consideration
give someone a suggestion or idea

Examples

I decided to put a bug in his ear about the upcoming job opening.
She put a bug in my ear regarding possible changes at the company.
If you want something done, it helps to put a bug in someone's ear early on.

Synset Examples

She put a bug in his ear about the promotion.
I put a bug in her ear about joining our team.
He put a bug in my ear about changing the project direction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to drop a hint - to give a subtle suggestion or clue
  • Synonym: plant a seed - to initiate an idea or thought in someone's mind
