normal for Norfolk

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɔrməl fə ˈnɔr.fək / (AmE), / ˈnɔː.məl fə ˈnɔː.fək / (BrE)
used, often humorously or disparagingly, to describe something strange, eccentric, or peculiar as being typical in Norfolk, England; often implies that what is odd elsewhere is unsurprising there
peculiar or odd, as is typical in Norfolk
informal british

Examples

Whenever something bizarre happens in that village, people just call it normal for Norfolk.
The mechanic's odd way of fixing things was considered normal for Norfolk by the locals.
They shrugged at the unusual tradition, saying it's simply normal for Norfolk.

Synset Examples

Strange things happen; it's normal for Norfolk.

Etymology

Originating in British English, with reference to perceived eccentricities of people from Norfolk, England.
