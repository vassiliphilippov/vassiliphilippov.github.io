to feel like a wally

1. VERB

/ fil laɪk ə ˈwɑli / (AmE), / fiːl laɪk ə ˈwɒli / (BrE)
to feel foolish or embarrassed, often as a result of one's own actions
feel foolish or embarrassed
informal british

Examples

I really felt like a wally when I realized I'd been talking to the wrong person.
After tripping over the curb in front of everyone, she couldn't help but feel like a wally.
You might feel like a wally if you forget your lines during the performance.

Synset Examples

He felt like a wally for forgetting her birthday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feel foolish - to experience a sense of embarrassment or self-consciousness due to one's actions
  • Antonym: feel confident - to feel sure about oneself and one's actions, the opposite of feeling foolish

Etymology

British slang term 'wally' meaning a fool, possibly from the name Walter used as a generic name.
