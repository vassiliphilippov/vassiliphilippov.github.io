Rome wasn't built in a day

1. X

/ ˈroʊm ˈwɑːzənt bɪlt ɪn ə deɪ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊm ˈwɒznt bɪlt ɪn ə deɪ / (BrE)
used to say that important achievements take time and cannot be accomplished all at once
great things take time to achieve

Examples

Whenever I'm frustrated by slow progress, I remind myself that Rome wasn't built in a day.
She tried to learn piano quickly, but her teacher told her that Rome wasn't built in a day.
They are making steady improvements—after all, Rome wasn't built in a day.

Synset Examples

She reminded him that Rome wasn't built in a day when he felt discouraged.
Remember, Rome wasn't built in a day; we need patience.
He kept repeating that Rome wasn't built in a day to motivate his team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patience - the ability to wait calmly for something without frustration

Etymology

Refers to the extended period over which the city of Rome was established, first recorded use in English in the late 16th century.
