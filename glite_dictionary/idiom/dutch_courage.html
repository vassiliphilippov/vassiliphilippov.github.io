Dutch courage

1. NOUN

/ dʌtʃ ˈkɝːrɪdʒ / (AmE), / dʌtʃ ˈkʌrɪdʒ / (BrE)
confidence or bravery inspired by alcohol, especially when someone acts more boldly after drinking.
bravery from drinking alcohol

Examples

He had a shot of whiskey to give himself some Dutch courage before the interview.
The adventurer relied on Dutch courage before attempting the risky jump.
It's just Dutch courage that's making him act so confidently tonight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: false courage - bravery that is not genuine or lasting

Etymology

Reputedly from the 17th-century practice of English soldiers drinking Dutch gin before battle during the Anglo-Dutch wars.
