on and off

1. ADV

/ ɑn ənd ɔf / (AmE), / ɒn ənd ɒf / (BrE)
from time to time; intermittently, not regularly or continuously
intermittently; not regularly

Examples

I have been seeing the doctor on and off for several years.
It rained on and off throughout the day, so we stayed indoors most of the time.
Our internet connection works on and off, making it hard to finish my assignments.

Synset Examples

they phone each other off and on

Related Terms

  • Synonym: off and on - intermittently; from time to time

2. ADV

/ ɑn ənd ɔf / (AmE), / ɒn ənd ɒf / (BrE)
Occasionally; at intervals; not continuously or regularly.
occasionally; intermittently

Examples

I've been working here on and off for three years now.
The pain in my shoulder comes on and off, but it's usually manageable.
They remained friends on and off despite many disagreements over the years.

Synset Examples

They dated on and off for several years.
It rained on and off throughout the day.
He has been studying French on and off since high school.
She’s been working there on-and-off for a decade.
I’ve been exercising on-and-off since last summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: off and on - used interchangeably, meaning occasionally; not continuously
