no business like show business

1. X

/ noʊ ˈbɪznəs laɪk ʃoʊ ˈbɪznəs / (AmE), / nəʊ ˈbɪznəs laɪk ʃəʊ ˈbɪznəs / (BrE)
used to say that the entertainment industry is exceptionally exciting, rewarding, or challenging compared to other fields
entertainment industry is uniquely exciting

Examples

After years on stage, she truly believes there's no business like show business.
Despite its chaos, he insists there's no business like show business.
Many dreamers flock to Hollywood, lured by the idea that there's no business like show business.

Synset Examples

After all the drama, I realized there's no business like show business.
They always say there's no business like show business for excitement.

Etymology

Popularized by the 1946 Irving Berlin song "There's No Business Like Show Business".
