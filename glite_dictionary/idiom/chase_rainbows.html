to chase rainbows

1. VERB

/ tʃeɪs ˈreɪnˌboʊz / (AmE), / tʃeɪs ˈreɪnbəʊz / (BrE)
to try to achieve things that are very unlikely or impossible to happen
pursue unrealistic dreams or goals

Examples

He spent his twenties trying to chase rainbows instead of building a solid career.
Stop chasing rainbows; focus on practical goals you can actually achieve.
Many people waste time chasing rainbows without realizing the opportunities right in front of them.

Synset Examples

She always chases rainbows instead of settling for reality.
He gave up chasing rainbows and found a stable job.
Chasing rainbows can lead to disappointment and regret.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipe dream - an unrealistic hope or fantasy
  • Synonym: wild goose chase - a foolish and hopeless search for something unattainable
  • Antonym: face reality - accept and deal with the true situation
