just deserts

1. NOUN

/ dʒʌst dɪˈzɝts / (AmE), / dʒʌst dɪˈzɜːts / (BrE)
A punishment or reward that is deserved and appropriate; what someone truly deserves.
deserved outcome, reward or punishment

Examples

After years of cheating his clients, he finally got his just deserts when he was sent to prison.
The villain in the film receives her just deserts in the end, much to the audience’s satisfaction.
He worked hard and earned his promotion—those were his just deserts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comeuppance - a deserved punishment or fate
  • Degree Higher: reward - something given in recognition of service, effort, or achievement
  • Degree Lower: punishment - the infliction of a penalty as retribution for an offense

Etymology

From the noun "desert" meaning 'that which is deserved,' from Old French *deservir* (to deserve). Pronounced as "desserts."
