to plough a lonely furrow

1. VERB

/ plaʊ ə ˈloʊnli ˈfɜroʊ / (AmE), / plaʊ ə ˈləʊnli ˈfɜrəʊ / (BrE)
to follow a course of action alone or with little support, often in the face of difficulty or opposition
to act alone and persistently
british

Examples

Despite criticism, she continued to plough a lonely furrow in her research.
Throughout his career, he has chosen to plough a lonely furrow rather than join popular movements.
The writer ploughed a lonely furrow, resisting commercial trends in literature.

Synset Examples

To plough a lonely furrow is his daily challenge.
He ploughed a lonely furrow in his quest for truth.
She often felt she was ploughing a lonely furrow in her beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go it alone - to do something by oneself without help
  • Synonym: stand alone - to be independent or without support

Etymology

From the literal act of lone ploughing in a field, metaphorically referring to working or persisting alone.
