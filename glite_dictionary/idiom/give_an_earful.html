to give an earful

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ən ˈɪrˌfʊl / (AmE), / ɡɪv ən ˈɪə.fʊl / (BrE)
to express one's anger or annoyance to someone by speaking loudly or at length; to reprimand someone harshly
reprimand or scold someone angrily at length
informal

Examples

My boss gave me an earful when I arrived late to work again.
She gave him an earful for forgetting their anniversary.
If you make that mistake, the coach will definitely give you an earful after the game.

Synset Examples

He really gave me an earful after the meeting.
My dad gave me an earful for missing curfew.
She had to give him an earful about his attitude.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scold - to reprimand or criticize severely or angrily
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration for someone or something
