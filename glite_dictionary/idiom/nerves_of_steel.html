nerves of steel

1. NOUN

/ nɝːvz əv stiːl / (AmE), / nɜːvz əv stiːl / (BrE)
exceptional courage and composure in difficult or dangerous situations
great courage and composure

Examples

It takes nerves of steel to perform on stage in front of thousands of people.
The pilot showed nerves of steel during the emergency landing.
She handled the crisis with nerves of steel, remaining calm throughout.

Synset Examples

I was scared to death, but Fred, who has nerves of steel, faced the thugs bravely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bravery - the quality or state of being brave; courage
  • Antonym: cowardice - lack of bravery; fearfulness
