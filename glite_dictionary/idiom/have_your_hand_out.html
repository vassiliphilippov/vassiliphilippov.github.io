to have your hand out

1. VERB

/ hæv jʊr hænd aʊt / (AmE), / hæv jɔː hænd aʊt / (BrE)
to ask for or expect money or help, especially repeatedly or without making an effort yourself
ask for money or help

Examples

He always seems to have his hand out when it's time to pay for dinner.
The charity is used to people having their hand out for support.
Don't expect a promotion if you constantly have your hand out without contributing.

Synset Examples

It's not polite to have your hand out without offering something in return.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to beg - ask for money or help as charity
