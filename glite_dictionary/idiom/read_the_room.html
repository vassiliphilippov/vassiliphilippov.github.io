to read the room

1. VERB

/ ɹid ðə ɹum / (AmE), / ɹiːd ðə ɹuːm / (BrE)
to understand the mood, feelings, or dynamics of a group or situation and respond appropriately
understand group mood or dynamics

Examples

Before making a proposal, it's wise to read the room to gauge everyone's mood.
She didn't read the room and made a joke at an inappropriate time.
During meetings, managers need to read the room to keep discussions productive.

Synset Examples

It's important to read the room during interviews.
He quickly learned to read the room before speaking.
She knows how to read the room and adjust her approach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pick up on - to notice or become aware of something, especially subtle cues
  • Antonym: misread the room - to fail to understand the mood or situation accurately
