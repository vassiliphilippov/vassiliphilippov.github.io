to fall asleep in the Lord

1. VERB

/ fɔl əˈslip ɪn ðə lɔrd / (AmE), / fɔːl əˈsliːp ɪn ðə lɔːd / (BrE)
(of a Christian) to die, especially with faith in God and hope of resurrection.
Christian euphemism for dying faithfully

Examples

The congregation mourned when their beloved pastor fell asleep in the Lord after decades of faithful service.
She finds comfort in believing her grandmother has fallen asleep in the Lord and is at peace.
He prayed every night, trusting that when he falls asleep in the Lord, he will join the saints in heaven.

Synset Examples

She finds peace when she falls asleep in the Lord.

Etymology

Refers to biblical passages where death is described as 'sleep' for believers (e.g., 1 Thessalonians 4:14).
