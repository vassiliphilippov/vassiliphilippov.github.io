to lose one's temper

1. VERB

/ luːz wʌnz ˈtɛmpər / (AmE), / luːz wʌnz ˈtɛmpə / (BrE)
to suddenly become angry and unable to control one's emotions
suddenly become angry

Examples

He tried to stay calm, but eventually he lost his temper during the meeting.
Children often lose their temper when they are tired or hungry.
Whenever someone cuts him off in traffic, he tends to lose his temper immediately.

Synset Examples

She tends to lose her temper when stressed.
It's easy to lose your temper in traffic.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to keep one's temper - to remain calm and not become angry
  • Synonym: to lose it - to become very angry or lose self-control

2. VERB

/ luːz wʌnz ˈtɛmpɚ / (AmE), / luːz wʌnz ˈtɛmpə / (BrE)
to suddenly become very angry
become suddenly angry

Examples

He lost his temper when the children started shouting during his phone call.
She tries not to lose her temper at work, even under stress.
The coach lost his temper during the heated match.

Synset Examples

He finally lost his temper after waiting too long.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to keep one's temper - to remain calm
  • Synonym: to fly off the handle - to lose one's temper suddenly
