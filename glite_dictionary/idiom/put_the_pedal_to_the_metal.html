to put the pedal to the metal

1. VERB

/ pʊt ðə ˈpɛdl̩ tə ðə ˈmɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / pʊt ðə ˈpɛdl tə ðə ˈmetl / (BrE)
to accelerate or go as fast as possible, especially in an energetic or determined way
go as fast as possible; accelerate fully

Examples

If we're going to make it on time, we need to put the pedal to the metal.
She really put the pedal to the metal to finish the project before the deadline.
As soon as the light turned green, he put the pedal to the metal and sped away.

Synset Examples

They went pedal to the metal to catch the deadline.
When the race started, he put the pedal to the metal.
We need to put the pedal to the metal to meet the deadline.
With the deadline approaching, she put the pedal to the metal.
He told us to put the pedal to the metal in the final stretch.
It's time to put the pedal to the metal and finish this race.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to step on it - to hurry up or go faster
  • Antonym: to slow down - to reduce speed; go slower

Etymology

American English, originating from automobile driving, referring to pushing the gas pedal down to the metal floor of a car.
