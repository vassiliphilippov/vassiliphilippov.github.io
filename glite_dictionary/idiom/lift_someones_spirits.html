to lift someone's spirits

1. VERB

/ lɪft sʌmˈwʌnz ˈspɪrɪts / (AmE), / lɪft sʌmˈwʌnz ˈspɪrɪts / (BrE)
to make someone feel happier or more cheerful, especially after they have felt sad or low
make someone feel happier

Examples

The funny movie really lifted my spirits after a long, hard day.
Kind words from a friend can lift your spirits when you are feeling down.
Her thoughtful message lifted his spirits during a difficult time.

Synset Examples

Her jolly demeanor lifted everyone's spirits.
Her laughter was contagious, lifting everyone's spirits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cheer up - to become or make someone happier or less sad
  • Antonym: bring someone down - to make someone feel worse or less happy
