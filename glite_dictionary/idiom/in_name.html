in name

1. ADV

/ ɪn ˈneɪm / (AmE), / ɪn ˈneɪm / (BrE)
used to indicate that someone or something has a particular title, status, or position, but not the qualities or functions that should go with it.
by title but not in reality

Examples

The company is independent in name, but its operations are controlled by a larger firm.
She is the chairperson in name, but others make the real decisions.
He was awarded the position in name, yet bore none of the responsibilities.

Synset Examples

he's a doctor in name only

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in name only - by title or reputation but not in reality
