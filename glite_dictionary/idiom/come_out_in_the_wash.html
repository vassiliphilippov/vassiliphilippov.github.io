to come out in the wash

1. VERB

/ kʌm aʊt ɪn ðə wɑːʃ / (AmE), / kʌm aʊt ɪn ðə wɒʃ / (BrE)
(of a secret or problem) to be revealed or resolved in the end, often unexpectedly or after some time.
be revealed or resolved eventually
informal

Examples

Don't worry if you made a mistake; it will come out in the wash eventually.
No matter how well you hide the truth, it always comes out in the wash.
All the details of the scandal started coming out in the wash during the investigation.

Synset Examples

Secrets have a way of coming out in the wash.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to come to light - to become known or revealed
  • Antonym: be kept under wraps - to be concealed or kept secret
