all over hell's half acre

1. ADV

/ ɔl ˈoʊvɚ hɛlz hæf ˈeɪkɚ / (AmE), / ɔːl ˈəʊvə hɛlz hɑːf ˈeɪkə / (BrE)
in a disorganized, frantic, or very widespread manner, especially while searching or moving about aimlessly
in a disorganized, frantic way; everywhere
informal american

Examples

We searched all over hell's half acre for that missing report but never found it.
He drove all over hell's half acre looking for an open gas station late at night.
Their kids were running all over hell's half acre during the family picnic.

Synset Examples

He was running all over hell's half acre to find his keys.

Etymology

American English origin, late 19th to early 20th century; 'hell's half acre' was used for remote or desolate places.
