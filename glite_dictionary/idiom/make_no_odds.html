to make no odds

1. VERB

/ meɪk noʊ ɒdz / (AmE), / meɪk nəʊ ɒdz / (BrE)
to not make any difference; to not matter at all
to not make any difference
british informal

Examples

To me, it makes no odds whether we travel by train or bus.
She said it makes no odds what time we start the meeting.
For most people, it makes no odds if it rains on the weekend.

Synset Examples

Whether it's pizza or sushi, it makes no odds.
It makes no odds to me which restaurant we choose.
For him, it makes no odds who wins the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: make no difference - to have no effect on the outcome or situation
  • Antonym: matter - to be important or significant
