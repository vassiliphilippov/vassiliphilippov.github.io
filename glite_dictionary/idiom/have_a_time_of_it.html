to have a time of it

1. VERB

/ hæv ə taɪm əv ɪt / (AmE), / hæv ə taɪm əv ɪt / (BrE)
to experience considerable difficulty or trouble in doing something
experience difficulty or trouble

Examples

We've had a time of it since the storm hit last night.
She had a time of it adjusting to life in the new city.
They really had a time of it getting their car out of the snow.

Synset Examples

They had a time of it trying to find the place.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: struggle - to experience difficulty and make a lot of effort to do something
  • Antonym: to sail through - to complete something with little difficulty
