to make ducks and drakes

1. VERB

/ meɪk dʌks ənd dreɪks / (AmE), / meɪk dʌks ənd dreɪks / (BrE)
to waste or squander resources foolishly or recklessly
waste resources recklessly (archaic idiom)
archaic

Examples

After receiving his inheritance, Tom began to make ducks and drakes of his fortune.
The committee warned the manager against making ducks and drakes of the company’s budget.
She regretted making ducks and drakes of her opportunities in youth.

Synset Examples

He was criticized for making ducks and drakes of the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to squander - to spend money, resources, or opportunities wastefully
  • Synonym: waste - to use something carelessly or without good purpose

Etymology

From the game of skipping flat stones on water (called 'ducks and drakes'), associated with things quickly lost or spent.
