to say less and mean more

1. VERB

/ seɪ lɛs ənd min mɔɹ / (AmE), / seɪ lɛs ənd miːn mɔː / (BrE)
to convey significant meaning with minimal words
convey deep meaning with few words

Examples

She could say less and mean more with just a single look.
Great writers often say less and mean more through subtle details and implications.
In negotiations, it's sometimes better to say less and mean more to make your intentions clear.

Synset Examples

With a glance, she could say less and mean more.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laconic - using very few words; concise
  • Synonym: taciturn - reserved or uncommunicative in speech
  • Antonym: verbose - using more words than necessary
