to give someone his head

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ˈsʌmwʌn hɪz hɛd / (AmE), / ɡɪv ˈsʌmwən hɪz hɛd / (BrE)
to allow someone complete freedom to act or make decisions as they see fit
allow someone full freedom to act

Examples

The manager decided to give John his head and let him run the department as he wished.
The director trusts her team and prefers to give them their head on most projects.
Sometimes, it's best to give someone his head and see what creative solutions emerge.

Synset Examples

The boss decided to give him his head on this project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: give someone free rein - to allow someone complete freedom to act

Etymology

Originally from the practice of letting a horse have rein or 'its head', meaning to let it run freely.
