says who

1. X

/ sɛz hu / (AmE), / sɛz huː / (BrE)
used as a rhetorical question to challenge the authority, credibility, or validity of a statement or rule.
challenges authority or validity of a statement
informal

Examples

"You have to leave now." "Says who?"
When he told her she couldn't enter the club, she replied, "Says who?"
"The meeting is cancelled." "Oh really? Says who?"

Synset Examples

You can't do that? Says who?
You think that's true? Says who?
