on the horns of a dilemma

1. X

/ ɑn ðə hɔrnz əv ə dɪˈlɛmə / (AmE), / ɒn ðə hɔːnz əv ə dɪˈlɛmə / (BrE)
facing a situation in which a difficult choice must be made between two equally undesirable alternatives
facing a difficult choice

Examples

She was on the horns of a dilemma about whether to follow her heart or her career.
When faced with equally risky options, anyone can find themselves on the horns of a dilemma.
He realized he was on the horns of a dilemma when both solutions had serious drawbacks.

Synset Examples

I'm on the horns of a dilemma about which job to take.
She found herself on the horns of a dilemma: stay home or travel.
Being on the horns of a dilemma made his decision very difficult.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dilemma - a situation in which a difficult choice has to be made

Etymology

From the metaphor of being impaled on the two sharp points (horns) of a dilemma, with roots in the use of 'dilemma' from Greek 'di-' (two) + 'lemma' (premise).
