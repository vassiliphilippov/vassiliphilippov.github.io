fair suck of the pineapple

1. NOUN

/ fɛr sʌk əv ðə ˈpaɪnˌæpəl / (AmE), / feə sʌk əv ðə ˈpaɪnˌæpəl / (BrE)
(Australian, informal) Used to express the demand or expectation of a fair chance or equal treatment.
a fair chance or opportunity (Australian idiom)
informal british

Examples

After working so hard, I deserve a fair suck of the pineapple in this project.
It’s time management gave everyone a fair suck of the pineapple instead of favoring a few people.
When promotions come up, all employees want a fair suck of the pineapple.

Synset Examples

You should get a fair suck of the pineapple for your efforts.
It’s only right to give everyone a fair suck of the pineapple.
He always ensures his team gets a fair suck of the pineapple.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fair go - an equal chance, a fair opportunity (Australian idiom)

Etymology

Australian slang phrase from the mid-20th century; likely refers humorously to sharing something equally, with 'pineapple' sometimes used for something valuable or desirable.
