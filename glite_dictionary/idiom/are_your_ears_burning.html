are your ears burning

1. X

/ ɑɹ jʊɹ ɪɹz ˈbɝː.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ɑː jɔːr ɪəz ˈbɜː.nɪŋ / (BrE)
used to ask someone if they are aware, or have guessed, that others have been talking about them, especially when their name has come up unexpectedly
someone being talked about

Examples

Whenever your name comes up, someone always asks, 'Are your ears burning?'
He walked in and joked, 'Are your ears burning? We've just been talking about you.'
When you called, we were actually discussing you—are your ears burning?

Synset Examples

I must be popular; are your ears burning?
She smiled and said, 'Are your ears burning? We were just talking about you!'
