to make a pig of oneself

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə pɪɡ əv wʌnˌsɛlf / (AmE), / meɪk ə pɪɡ ɒv wʌnˌsɛlf / (BrE)
to eat or drink far too much, especially in a greedy or excessive way
to overeat or overindulge

Examples

She tends to make a pig of herself at buffets, piling her plate high with food.
If you make a pig of yourself at dinner, you'll feel sick later.
He regretted making a pig of himself after eating the entire pizza alone.

Synset Examples

Don't make a pig of yourself; save some cake for others.
She tends to make a pig of herself when it comes to chocolate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overeat - to eat more food than your body needs

Etymology

Figurative use of 'pig' as a symbol of greed or gluttony, common in English idioms.
