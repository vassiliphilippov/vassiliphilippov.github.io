to leave for dust

1. VERB

/ liv fər dʌst / (AmE), / liːv fə dʌst / (BrE)
to completely surpass or outpace someone or something, especially in a competition
surpass or outpace completely

Examples

The young chess prodigy left for dust all his seasoned competitors in the tournament.
Their new phone technology leaves for dust anything else on the market.
By mile ten, she had left for dust the rest of the runners.

Synset Examples

They left their rivals for dust in the race.
His skills left everyone else for dust.
Her performance left the competition for dust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outpace - to run or move faster than; to surpass
  • Synonym: to leave behind - to surpass; to move ahead and be no longer close to someone or something
