to keep one's eye on the ball

1. VERB

/ kip wʌnz aɪ ɑn ðə bɑl / (AmE), / kiːp wʌnz aɪ ɒn ðə bɔːl / (BrE)
to remain focused on the main goal or task and not become distracted
stay focused on the main goal or task

Examples

If you want to finish this project successfully, you need to keep your eye on the ball.
It's easy to get distracted, but experienced managers know how to keep their eye on the ball.
During negotiations, she always keeps her eye on the ball and doesn't let side issues interfere.

Synset Examples

She advised him to keep his eye on the ball.
To succeed, always keep your eye on the ball.
During the meeting, keep your eye on the ball.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stay focused - remain attentive to the task or goal
  • Antonym: lose focus - become distracted from the goal

Etymology

Originally from sports, especially baseball and cricket, where watching the ball is key to success.
