to throw a wrench in

1. VERB

/ θroʊ ə rɛntʃ ɪn / (AmE), / θrəʊ ə rɛntʃ ɪn / (BrE)
to cause a disruption or problem in a process, plan, or situation
cause a disruption or problem

Examples

The surprise storm threw a wrench in their outdoor wedding plans.
Her illness threw a wrench in the team's preparations for the final match.
If the supplier doesn't deliver on time, it could throw a wrench in our entire schedule.

Synset Examples

The rain threw a wrench in our picnic plans.
He threw a wrench in the project with his last-minute changes.
The unexpected cost increase threw a wrench in our budget.
Her sudden resignation threw a wrench in the gears of our team.
The software bug threw a wrench in the gears of our launch.
His absence threw a wrench in the gears of our planning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disrupt - to interrupt the normal process or activity
  • Synonym: sabotage - to deliberately destroy or obstruct something, especially for advantage
  • Antonym: to smooth over - to make a problem or difficulty seem less serious or easier to manage

Etymology

From the literal idea of throwing a tool (wrench) into machinery, causing it to malfunction; popularized in American English in the early 20th century.
