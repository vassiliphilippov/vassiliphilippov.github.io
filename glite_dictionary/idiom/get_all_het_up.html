to get all het up

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɔl hɛt ʌp / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɔːl hɛt ʌp / (BrE)
to become very upset or agitated, especially about something relatively unimportant
become upset or agitated
informal

Examples

Don't get all het up about the delay; it's only a few minutes.
She tends to get all het up when plans change unexpectedly.
There's no need to get all het up over such a small mistake.

Synset Examples

She got all het up over the minor mistake in the report.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freak out - to become or cause someone to become very upset or emotional
  • Antonym: to calm down - to become or cause someone to become calm or less upset

Etymology

'Het up' is a dialectal variant of 'heated up,' meaning excited or agitated, used in American English since the early 20th century.
