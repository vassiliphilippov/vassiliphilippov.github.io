a wide berth

1. NOUN

/ ə waɪd bɝːθ / (AmE), / ə waɪd bɜːθ / (BrE)
a safe or cautious distance maintained to avoid danger or trouble
safe distance to avoid danger

Examples

Joggers tend to give unleashed dogs a wide berth when passing by.
It’s wise to give suspicious packages a wide berth at airports.
After the argument, his colleagues gave him a wide berth for the rest of the day.

Synset Examples

The captain gave the rocks a wide berth.
You should give that aggressive dog a wide berth.
We took a wide berth around the construction site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: give a wide berth - to keep a safe distance from something or someone

Etymology

Originally a nautical term referring to giving other ships or hazards enough space when anchoring or passing.
