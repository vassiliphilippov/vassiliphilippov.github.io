to smell fishy

1. VERB

/ smɛl ˈfɪʃi / (AmE), / smɛl ˈfɪʃi / (BrE)
to seem suspicious or dishonest; to give the impression that something is not truthful or genuine
seem suspicious or dishonest

Examples

When he couldn't explain where the money came from, the whole affair began to smell fishy.
Her story about missing the meeting really smelled fishy to everyone involved.
If a business proposal smells fishy, it's best to ask more questions before agreeing.

Synset Examples

The deal seemed to smell fishy from the beginning.
Her excuse for being late smelled fishy.
I think the whole situation smells fishy to me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seem suspicious - to give the impression of being questionable or not entirely honest
  • Antonym: be above board - to be honest, open, and legal
