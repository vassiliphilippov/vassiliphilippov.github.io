to hold sway

1. VERB

/ hoʊld sweɪ / (AmE), / həʊld sweɪ / (BrE)
to have power or influence over people or situations
have power or influence

Examples

Despite public protests, the old policies still hold sway in the company.
For centuries, superstition held sway over the villagers' beliefs.
It is clear that fashion trends hold sway over many young people today.

Synset Examples

His opinions hold sway among the committee members.
In this town, tradition holds sway over innovation.
The CEO holds sway over all major decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dominate - to have a commanding influence over; to control
  • Synonym: reign - to hold royal office; to rule as monarch
  • Antonym: yield - to relinquish influence or control
