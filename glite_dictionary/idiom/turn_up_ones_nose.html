to turn up one's nose

1. VERB

/ tɝn ʌp wʌnz noʊz / (AmE), / tɜːn ʌp wʌnz nəʊz / (BrE)
to show disdain or contempt for something; to reject or dismiss something as inferior or unworthy
show disdain or contempt

Examples

She tends to turn up her nose at anything that isn't designer brand.
The committee turned up its nose at the proposal, calling it unoriginal.
He turned up his nose at the idea of eating leftovers.

Synset Examples

She turned her snoot up at the cheap wine.
They turned up their noses at the new restaurant.
He always turns up his nose at fast food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snub - to reject or ignore disdainfully
  • Synonym: to look down on - to regard with a sense of superiority or disdain
  • Antonym: to admire - to regard with respect or warm approval

2. VERB

/ tɝn ʌp wʌnz noʊz / (AmE), / tɜːn ʌp wʌnz nəʊz / (BrE)
to show disdain or contempt for something; to treat something or someone as unworthy or beneath consideration
show disdain or contempt

Examples

She turned up her nose at the cheap wine, clearly unimpressed by the selection.
Some children turn up their noses at vegetables, even before trying them.
He turned up his nose when offered a job he thought was beneath him.
