to extend an olive branch

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈstɛnd ən ˈɑlɪv bræntʃ / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɛnd ən ˈɒlɪv brɑːntʃ / (BrE)
to make an offer of peace or reconciliation to someone after a disagreement
make an offer of peace or reconciliation

Examples

After months of tension, the president decided to extend an olive branch to the opposition party.
In an effort to rebuild their friendship, Sarah called to extend an olive branch.
Sometimes, it takes courage to extend an olive branch and admit mistakes.

Synset Examples

She extended an olive branch to her estranged friend.
The CEO sent an olive branch to the union leaders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make peace - to become friendly again after a quarrel
  • Synonym: hold out an olive branch - to offer peace or reconciliation (variant form)
  • Antonym: to provoke - to make someone angry or incite conflict (opposite action)

Etymology

From the ancient tradition of using an olive branch as a symbol of peace, dating back to Ancient Greece and Rome.
