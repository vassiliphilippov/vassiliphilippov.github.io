to cover more ground

1. VERB

/ ˈkʌvɚ mɔr ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈkʌvə mɔː ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
to deal with or travel over a greater area, range, or amount of material
deal with a wider area/topic

Examples

By taking notes quickly, the lecturer was able to cover more ground in each session.
We'll need to split into teams if we're going to cover more ground before sunset.
Reading summaries allows students to cover more ground before the exam.

Synset Examples
