to belly up to the bar

1. VERB

/ ˈbɛli ʌp tu ðə bɑɹ / (AmE), / ˈbɛli ʌp tə ðə bɑː / (BrE)
to approach or move up to a bar counter, typically in order to order a drink
approach a bar to order drinks

Examples

After a long day, we like to belly up to the bar and share stories.
He walked into the pub and bellied up to the bar for a pint.
Many locals belly up to the bar as soon as happy hour begins.

Synset Examples

He loves to belly up to the bar and chat.
They decided to belly up to the bar for a drink.
After work, we belly up to the bar to unwind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sidle up - to move closer in a gradual or sneaky way

Etymology

Originates from the imagery of pressing one's stomach ('belly') against the bar counter to get served; recorded in American English since the late 19th century.
