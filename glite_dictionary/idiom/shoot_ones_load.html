to shoot one's load

1. VERB

/ ʃut wʌnz loʊd / (AmE), / ʃuːt wʌnz ləʊd / (BrE)
(informal, vulgar) To expend all one's energy, effort, or enthusiasm at once, often leaving nothing in reserve.
use all one's energy or effort at once (vulgar)
vulgar informal

Examples

He shot his load at the meeting and was too exhausted to contribute to the next project.
The musician shot her load on stage, leaving the crowd in awe of her energy.
Don't shoot your load too early in the competition or you'll run out of steam.

Synset Examples

Once she shot her load of enthusiasm, the team rallied.
After weeks of planning, he finally shot his load on the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go all out - to make the greatest possible effort
  • Antonym: to hold back - to refrain from expending all effort or resources
