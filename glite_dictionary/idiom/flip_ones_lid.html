to flip one's lid

1. VERB

/ flɪp wʌnz lɪd / (AmE), / flɪp wʌnz lɪd / (BrE)
to suddenly become very angry or lose one's temper
become very angry suddenly
informal

Examples

When he saw the mess in the kitchen, he flipped his lid and started yelling.
Don't flip your lid over such a small mistake; it's not worth it.
She almost flipped her lid when she found out her car had been towed.

Synset Examples

The professor combusted when the student didn't know the answer to a very elementary question
Spam makes me go ballistic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lose one's temper - to become suddenly angry
  • Antonym: to keep one's cool - to remain calm and not get angry

2. VERB

/ flɪp wʌns lɪd / (AmE), / flɪp wʌnz lɪd / (BrE)
to suddenly become extremely angry or upset
become extremely angry or upset
informal

Examples

If my parents see the mess in the kitchen, they'll flip their lid.
He almost flipped his lid when he heard the news.
Try not to flip your lid just because the plans changed.

Synset Examples

She almost flipped her lid over the surprise party.
He flipped shit when he found out he was late.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lose one's temper - to suddenly become very angry
  • Antonym: to keep one's cool - to remain calm in a difficult situation
