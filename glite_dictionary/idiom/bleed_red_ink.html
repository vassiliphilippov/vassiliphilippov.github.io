to bleed red ink

1. VERB

/ blid rɛd ɪŋk / (AmE), / bliːd rɛd ɪŋk / (BrE)
to lose money or incur financial losses, especially persistently or severely
to suffer financial losses

Examples

The small startup began to bleed red ink after losing its biggest client.
Several newspapers have continued to bleed red ink in the face of declining subscriptions.
If fuel prices rise much higher, airlines will again bleed red ink throughout the year.

Synset Examples

She worries the project will bleed red ink without more funding.
The company continues to bleed red ink despite cost-cutting efforts.
If we don’t increase sales, we'll bleed red ink this quarter.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: make a profit - to earn financial gain

Etymology

From the traditional use of red ink to indicate losses in accounting ledgers.
