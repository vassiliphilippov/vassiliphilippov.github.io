on all fours

1. ADV

/ ɑn ɔl fɔrz / (AmE), / ɒn ɔːl fɔːz / (BrE)
in a position where the hands and knees are on the ground, resembling the posture of an animal with four legs
on hands and knees

Examples

The toddler crawled on all fours across the living room carpet.
After dropping his keys under the sofa, he got on all fours to retrieve them.
During the yoga class, several poses required participants to be on all fours.

Synset Examples

he got down on all fours to play with his grandson

2. ADV

/ ɑn ɔl fɔrz / (AmE), / ɒn ɔːl fɔːz / (BrE)
with the hands and knees on the ground, moving by using both hands and knees
moving on hands and knees

Examples

The baby crawled on all fours across the carpet.
After dropping his keys under the bed, he had to go on all fours to retrieve them.
When the tunnel got too low, everyone had to move on all fours.

Synset Examples

He had to get on all fours to fix the plumbing.
The toddler crawled on all fours around the living room.
The dog ran around the yard on all fours.
