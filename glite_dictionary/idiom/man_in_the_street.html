man in the street

1. NOUN

/ mæn ɪn ðə stɹit / (AmE), / mæn ɪn ðə stɹiːt / (BrE)
an ordinary or average person, especially one considered as representative of the general public
an average or ordinary person

Examples

The opinion of the man in the street is often overlooked by politicians.
When asked about the policy, the man in the street expressed confusion.
Journalists frequently try to gauge the mood of the man in the street after major events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: everyman - an ordinary or typical person
  • Synonym: woman in the street - an ordinary woman, as representative of the public

2. NOUN

/ mæn ɪn ðə strit / (AmE), / mæn ɪn ðə striːt / (BrE)
an ordinary person, representing the typical views or opinions of the general public
ordinary or average person

Examples

The documentary explores the hopes and fears of the man in the street.
Politicians often claim to understand the concerns of the man in the street.
For the man in the street, economic reports can be difficult to interpret.

Synset Examples

The report reflects the views of the man in the street.
The man in the street has a unique perspective.
She interviewed the man in the street for his opinion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: average Joe - an ordinary person, especially a man
  • Synonym: common man - an ordinary or typical person
  • Synonym: woman in the street - female equivalent for the ordinary person
