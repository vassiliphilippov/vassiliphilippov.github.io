no further ado

1. NOUN

/ noʊ ˈfɝːðər əˈduː / (AmE), / nəʊ ˈfɜːðə əˈduː / (BrE)
without any more delay; without unnecessary or prolonged introduction
without more delay

Examples

With no further ado, the ceremony commenced promptly at noon.
She welcomed everyone and, with no further ado, introduced the guest speaker.
The host started the event with no further ado, eager to begin the proceedings.

Synset Examples

Let’s begin the meeting with no further ado.
He announced the winner with no further ado.
They started the show with no further ado.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: without delay - without hesitation or postponement

Etymology

From Middle English 'ado' (trouble, fuss), meaning activity or fuss; popularized in the phrase 'without further ado'.
