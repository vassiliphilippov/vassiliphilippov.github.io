by the score

1. ADV

/ baɪ ðə skɔr / (AmE), / baɪ ðə skɔː / (BrE)
in large numbers; by the dozens or twenties
in large numbers

Examples

Tourists arrived by the score to witness the festival's opening ceremony.
Birds gathered on the telephone wires by the score at sunset.
During the sale, shoes sold by the score within the first hour.

Synset Examples

He has made friends by the score in his new city.
They received compliments by the score after the performance.
The team won by the score of 4-1.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: by the dozen - in groups or quantities of twelve; in large numbers

Etymology

From 'score' meaning a group of twenty, used since the 14th century for counting.
