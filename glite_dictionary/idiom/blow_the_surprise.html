to blow the surprise

1. VERB

/ bloʊ ðə səˈpraɪz / (AmE), / bləʊ ðə səˈpraɪz / (BrE)
to reveal a secret or spoil a surprise before it is intended to be known
reveal a secret or spoil a surprise

Examples

Please don't blow the surprise for Amy's birthday party.
He almost blew the surprise when he mentioned the trip in front of her.
If you tell him, you'll blow the surprise we've all been planning.

Synset Examples

She didn't want to blow the surprise party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoil the surprise - to ruin a surprise by revealing it too early
  • Synonym: to spill the beans - to reveal secret information unintentionally or prematurely
