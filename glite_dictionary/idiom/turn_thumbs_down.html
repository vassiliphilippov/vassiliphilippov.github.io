to turn thumbs down

1. VERB

/ tɝn θʌmz daʊn / (AmE), / tɜːn θʌmz daʊn / (BrE)
to vote against or express disapproval of something or someone
vote against; express disapproval

Examples

The committee turned thumbs down on the proposed budget increase.
When asked about extending the deadline, the supervisor turned thumbs down without hesitation.
The board members turned thumbs down on the merger after reviewing the financial reports.

Synset Examples

The faculty turned thumbs down on the candidate for the Dean position

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to approve - to accept or agree with something

Etymology

From the ancient Roman gesture of turning the thumb down to indicate disapproval or condemnation, especially in gladiatorial combat.
