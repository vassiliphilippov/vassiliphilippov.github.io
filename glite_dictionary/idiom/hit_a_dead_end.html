to hit a dead end

1. VERB

/ hɪt ə dɛd ɛnd / (AmE), / hɪt ə dɛd ɛnd / (BrE)
to reach a point where no further progress can be made; to be unable to proceed
reach a point of no further progress

Examples

After months of research, the scientists hit a dead end and couldn't find a solution.
Negotiations hit a dead end when neither side would compromise.
We hit a dead end with our project and had to start over.

Synset Examples

The investigation hit a dead end after several weeks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: come to a standstill - to stop moving or making progress
  • Antonym: breakthrough - an important advance or development that removes an obstacle
