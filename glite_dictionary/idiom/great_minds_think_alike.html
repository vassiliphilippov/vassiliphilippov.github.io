great minds think alike

1. X

/ ɡreɪt maɪndz θɪŋk əˈlaɪk / (AmE), / ɡreɪt maɪndz θɪŋk əˈlaɪk / (BrE)
used to say that intelligent people often have the same ideas or think in a similar way, typically as a humorous comment after two people say or do the same thing
used when two people have the same idea

Examples

We both reached for the last cookie at the same time—great minds think alike!
When they proposed the same solution, Emma said with a smile, 'great minds think alike.'
You suggested pizza for dinner, and so did I—great minds think alike.

Synset Examples

Her friend echoed her thoughts, proving that great minds think alike.
After sharing the same opinion, they joked that great minds think alike.
When we both suggested the same idea, I said, 'Great minds think alike!'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: two minds with but a single thought - two people who think identically
