to chew the scenery

1. VERB

/ tʃu ðə ˈsiːnəri / (AmE), / tʃuː ðə ˈsiːnəri / (BrE)
to act in an exaggerated, flamboyant, or overdramatic way, especially on stage or in film
act in an exaggerated or flamboyant way

Examples

The villain couldn't help but chew the scenery in the final act, drawing both laughter and applause.
Whenever she gets a dramatic role, she tends to chew the scenery and outshine her co-stars.
Some critics felt the actor's decision to chew the scenery distracted from the realism of the play.

Synset Examples

His tendency to chew the scenery made him a standout.
She tends to chew the scenery in dramatic roles.
The actor loved to chew the scenery in every performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overact - to act with excessive emotion or exaggeration

Etymology

Likely from theatrical slang, likening an actor's overacting to consuming the stage set.
