to fall in the sack

1. VERB

/ fɔl ɪn ðə sæk / (AmE), / fɔːl ɪn ðə sæk / (BrE)
(informal) To go to bed or go to sleep.
go to bed or sleep (idiom, informal)
informal

Examples

After the movie ended, I was so tired that I decided to fall in the sack right away.
Most nights, he likes to read for a while before he falls in the sack.
It's been a long day, so I'm about ready to fall in the sack.

Synset Examples

After the long day, I fell in the sack early.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hit the hay - go to bed or sleep (idiom, informal)
  • Synonym: to turn in - go to bed, especially at night (phrasal verb, informal)
