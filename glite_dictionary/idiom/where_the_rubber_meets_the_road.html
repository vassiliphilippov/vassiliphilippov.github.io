where the rubber meets the road

1. X

/ wɛr ðə ˈrʌbɚ mits ðə roʊd / (AmE), / weə ðə ˈrʌbə miːts ðə rəʊd / (BrE)
the point at which ideas, plans, or theories are put to a practical test or applied in real situations
point where theory is tested in practice
american

Examples

In business, where the rubber meets the road is when customers start using your product.
The theory sounds impressive, but we'll see where the rubber meets the road during the next phase.
This is the stage where the rubber meets the road—when all our planning is put to the test.

Synset Examples

Now we need to see where the rubber meets the road.
Let's find out where the rubber meets the road in this project.
The plan sounds good, but where the rubber meets the road matters.

Etymology

American idiom originated from the image of car tires (rubber) making contact with the road, meaning the moment when something begins to have a practical effect.
