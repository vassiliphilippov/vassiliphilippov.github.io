sick as a parrot

1. ADJ

/ sɪk əz ə ˈpæɹət / (AmE), / sɪk əz ə ˈpærət / (BrE)
Extremely disappointed or upset, often as a result of an unfavorable outcome.
deeply disappointed or upset (Brit. idiom)
british informal

Examples

He was sick as a parrot when his team lost in the final minutes of the match.
She felt sick as a parrot when she didn't get the promotion she worked so hard for.
After hearing the disappointing results, they were all sick as a parrot.

Synset Examples

After losing the game, he felt sick as a parrot.
She was sick as a parrot when she heard the news.
I was sick as a parrot after eating that spoiled food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: down in the dumps - feeling sad or depressed
  • Antonym: over the moon - extremely happy or thrilled (Brit. idiom)

Etymology

British English idiom, popularized in football (soccer) contexts since the late 20th century.
