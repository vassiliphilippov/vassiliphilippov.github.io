to get one's claws into

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt wʌnz klɔz ˈɪntu / (AmE), / ɡɛt wʌnz klɔːz ˈɪntu / (BrE)
to become involved in something eagerly or to take control or advantage of a situation, often in a determined or aggressive way
eagerly become involved or take control

Examples

The ambitious CEO couldn’t wait to get her claws into the struggling company and turn it around.
He finally got his claws into a role he’d always wanted after years of persistence.
Once she gets her claws into a new project, she doesn’t let go until it’s perfect.

Synset Examples

She couldn’t wait to get her claws into the competition.
He was eager to get his claws into the new assignment.
Once she gets her claws into that project, there's no stopping her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get one's hands on - to obtain or manage to acquire something
