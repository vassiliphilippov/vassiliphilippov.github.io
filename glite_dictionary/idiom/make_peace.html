to make peace

1. VERB

/ meɪk pis / (AmE), / meɪk piːs / (BrE)
to resolve conflicts or disagreements and restore a state of harmony or reconciliation
resolve conflicts; restore harmony

Examples

They decided to make peace after months of bitter arguments.
Both countries finally agreed to make peace and sign a treaty.
She found it difficult to make peace with her past mistakes.

Synset Examples

He made peace with his family on his deathbed.
It's time to make peace and move forward.
They decided to make peace after their long feud.
We should make peace instead of holding grudges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reconcile - restore friendly relations; bring back harmony
  • Antonym: make war - to begin or engage in conflict or hostilities
