bare bones

1. NOUN

/ bɛr boʊnz / (AmE), / bɛː bəʊnz / (BrE)
the most basic or essential facts, elements, or parts, with nothing added or unnecessary
the most basic facts or elements

Examples

He only gave me the bare bones of his plan, leaving out most of the details.
The report contained just the bare bones of what happened during the meeting.
Let's start with the bare bones before adding any extra features to the design.

Synset Examples

he told us only the bare bones of the story

Related Terms

  • Synonym: essentials - basic and necessary elements
