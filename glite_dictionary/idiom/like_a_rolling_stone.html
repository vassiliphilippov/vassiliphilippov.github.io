like a rolling stone

1. X

/ laɪk ə ˈroʊlɪŋ stoʊn / (AmE), / laɪk ə ˈrəʊlɪŋ stəʊn / (BrE)
used to describe someone who is always moving or changing, not settling in one place or situation for long
describes a person who never settles

Examples

She's like a rolling stone, never staying in one job or city for long.
After college, he lived like a rolling stone, traveling across continents for years.
His friends say he moves like a rolling stone, always seeking a new adventure.

Synset Examples

Like a rolling stone, she travels wherever life takes her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drifter - person who moves from place to place with no permanent home
  • Antonym: settler - person who settles in one place, in contrast to a wanderer

Etymology

From the proverb 'a rolling stone gathers no moss', popularized by songs such as Bob Dylan’s 'Like a Rolling Stone'.
