to shake off the dust

1. VERB

/ ʃeɪk ɑf ðə dʌst / (AmE), / ʃeɪk ɒf ðə dʌst / (BrE)
to leave behind past troubles, experiences, or associations and move forward in life
leave past troubles behind

Examples

After the divorce, she was determined to shake off the dust and start a new chapter in her life.
Sometimes you need to shake off the dust of old failures and embrace new opportunities.
He finally shook off the dust from that unfulfilling job and pursued his true passion.

Synset Examples

It's time to shake off the dust from my feet and move on.

Etymology

Biblical origin from the practice of shaking dust from one's feet as a symbolic gesture of leaving a place behind (Matthew 10:14).
