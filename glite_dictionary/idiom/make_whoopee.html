to make whoopee

1. VERB

/ meɪk ˈwʊpi / (AmE), / meɪk ˈwʊpi / (BrE)
to celebrate or enjoy oneself in a lively, noisy way, often involving drinking or partying
celebrate noisily; party exuberantly
informal

Examples

Every New Year's Eve, the whole city comes out to make whoopee in the streets.
After finishing their exams, the students went downtown to make whoopee until dawn.
They're planning to make whoopee at the summer festival this weekend.

Synset Examples

The members of the wedding party made merry all night
Let's whoop it up — the boss is gone!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to celebrate - to observe or honor with festivities
  • Synonym: carouse - to engage in boisterous, drunken merrymaking

Etymology

Popularized in the early 20th century, possibly from the exclamation 'whoopee!' as a cry of excitement.
