to shove it

1. VERB

/ ˈʃʌv ɪt / (AmE), / ˈʃʌv ɪt / (BrE)
(informal) Used to tell someone angrily or rudely that you do not accept their offer, opinion, or behavior and want them to go away.
expression of rejection or dismissal
informal

Examples

If you don't like my idea, you can just shove it.
He told his boss to shove it and walked out of the meeting.
After hearing the proposal, she said they could shove it and left.

Synset Examples

She was fed up and told him where to shove it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get lost - used to rudely tell someone to go away
  • Synonym: stuff it - used to rudely reject or dismiss someone or something (chiefly British)
