to get the sack

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ðə sæk / (AmE), / ɡɛt ðə sæk / (BrE)
to be dismissed or fired from a job; to lose one's employment
be dismissed from a job
informal british

Examples

After arriving late every day for a month, he finally got the sack from the company.
Many employees worry they might get the sack during company restructuring.
She was shocked to get the sack after 20 years of loyal service.

Synset Examples

She didn't expect to get the sack just before the holidays.
He was upset to get the sack after the merger.
Losing his job felt surreal after he thought he was safe from getting the sack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be fired - to lose one's job, especially suddenly or unexpectedly
  • Synonym: be dismissed - to be formally removed from a job or position
  • Antonym: get hired - to be given a job

Etymology

Possibly from the idea of workers having their belongings in a sack when dismissed; British English origin, 19th century.
