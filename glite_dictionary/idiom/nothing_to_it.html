nothing to it

1. X

/ ˈnʌθɪŋ tu ˈɪt / (AmE), / ˈnʌθɪŋ tə ˈɪt / (BrE)
Used to say that something is very easy or simple to do.
very easy; simple to do

Examples

The test was so easy; there was nothing to it.
Once you get the hang of it, there's really nothing to it.
He fixed the car in five minutes, saying there was nothing to it.

Synset Examples

Just follow the instructions; there's nothing to it.
Learning to ride a bike? There’s nothing to it!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piece of cake - something very easy to do
