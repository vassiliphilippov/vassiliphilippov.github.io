to say you swear

1. VERB

/ seɪ ju swɛr / (AmE), / seɪ juː sweə / (BrE)
used to ask someone to make a serious promise or commitment, especially to emphasize sincerity or truthfulness
ask for a serious promise
informal

Examples

Before I tell you, say you swear you won't tell anyone else.
He looked at me and said, 'Just say you swear that you're telling the truth.'
If you want me to trust you, say you swear you'll be careful.

Synset Examples

She made him promise, 'Say you swear to keep it secret.'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to swear - to make a solemn or serious promise
