to man the fort

1. VERB

/ mæn ðə fɔrt / (AmE), / mæn ðə fɔːt / (BrE)
to take responsibility for a situation or place, especially in someone's absence, and ensure that everything continues smoothly or is protected
take charge in someone's absence

Examples

With the manager away, Susan agreed to man the fort until he returned.
Could you man the fort while I step out for a bit?
During the holidays, only a few employees stayed to man the fort.

Synset Examples

We need volunteers to man the fort during the event.
They decided to man the fort while others were away.
It's time to man the fort and defend our interests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hold the fort - to take care of things temporarily while someone is away

Etymology

From the military practice of assigning soldiers to defend a fort, now used figuratively.
