least of all

1. ADV

/ ˈlist əv ɔl / (AmE), / ˈliːst əv ɔːl / (BrE)
used to emphasize that a particular person or thing is the last or least likely or suitable in a group to do something or be described in a certain way.
especially not; least likely or suitable

Examples

She dislikes getting up early, least of all on weekends.
No one was prepared for the storm, least of all the tourists.
None of us wanted to leave, least of all the children.

Synset Examples

nobody, least of all Joe, agreed with me

Related Terms

  • Antonym: most of all - more than anything or anyone else; especially
