to leave the ball in someone's court

1. VERB

/ liv ðə bɔl ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz kɔrt / (AmE), / liːv ðə bɔːl ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz kɔːt / (BrE)
to allow someone else to take responsibility for making a decision or taking action
let someone else decide or act

Examples

I've given him all the information he needs; now I leave the ball in his court.
After making our proposal, we decided to leave the ball in their court.
She apologized and now leaves the ball in your court to respond.

Synset Examples

I've done my part, now I leave the ball in your court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the ball is in someone's court - it is someone else's turn or responsibility to act

Etymology

From tennis, where play continues when the ball is in the opponent's side of the court.

2. VERB

/ liv ðə bɔl ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz kɔrt / (AmE), / liːv ðə bɔːl ɪn ˈsʌmwənz kɔːt / (BrE)
to make someone responsible for the next decision or action
make someone responsible for a decision

Examples

After explaining the options, the doctor left the ball in my court regarding the treatment plan.
He apologized and left the ball in her court to decide whether to forgive him.
We've made our best offer, so the ball is in their court now.

Synset Examples

He made his offer and left the ball in her court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave it up to someone - to allow someone to make the decision or choice

Etymology

From tennis, where control of the ball passes to the opponent's court.
