common touch

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən tʌtʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən tʌtʃ / (BrE)
the ability to connect well and appeal to ordinary people, often by showing relatable qualities or understanding their concerns
an ability to relate to ordinary people

Examples

The politician's common touch made her immensely popular with voters from all backgrounds.
Despite his wealth, he never lost his common touch and treated everyone as equals.
Many leaders aspire to have the common touch but few truly succeed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empathy - the ability to understand and share another person's feelings
