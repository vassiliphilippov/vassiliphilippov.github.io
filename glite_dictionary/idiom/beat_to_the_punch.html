to beat to the punch

1. VERB

/ bit tə ðə pʌntʃ / (AmE), / biːt tə ðə pʌntʃ / (BrE)
to do something before someone else does, especially in a way that gains advantage
act before someone else does

Examples

She woke up early and beat to the punch everyone else to get the tickets.
If you want that promotion, you’ll have to beat to the punch your colleagues.
The company beat to the punch its competitors by launching the new product first.

Synset Examples

He always tries to beat me to the punch with new ideas.
You need to act fast to beat them to the punch.
She managed to beat him to the punch and secure the deal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get in first - to act before someone else does
  • Synonym: preempt - to act in advance to prevent another action

Etymology

Originally from boxing, where landing a punch before one's opponent provided an advantage; figurative use recorded since early 20th century.
