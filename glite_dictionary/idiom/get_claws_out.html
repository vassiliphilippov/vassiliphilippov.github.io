to get claws out

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt klɔz aʊt / (AmE), / ɡɛt klɔːz aʊt / (BrE)
to become aggressive, confrontational, or defensive, often suddenly or in response to provocation
become aggressive or confrontational

Examples

She usually stays calm, but she will get claws out if someone insults her friends.
During the heated debate, the politician started to get claws out and interrupted her opponent repeatedly.
If competitors try to steal her clients, she’ll definitely get claws out and fight back.

Synset Examples

When it comes to business, he gets his claws out.
She might seem friendly, but get her claws out in negotiations.
Don't make her mad; she'll get her claws out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: show one's claws - to act aggressively or reveal hostility
  • Synonym: to bare one's teeth - to show aggression or readiness to fight
