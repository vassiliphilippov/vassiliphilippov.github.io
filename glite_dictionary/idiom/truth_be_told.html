truth be told

1. X

/ truːθ bi toʊld / (AmE), / truːθ bi təʊld / (BrE)
used to indicate that one is being honest, even if the statement may be surprising or awkward
used to admit something honestly

Examples

Truth be told, I find this project quite challenging.
I should say, truth be told, I wasn't expecting to win the award.
Truth be told, I'd rather stay in tonight than go out.

Synset Examples

Truth be told, I forgot about our meeting.
Truth be told, I wasn’t really interested in going.
Truth be told, I enjoy working from home more.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to be honest - used to preface a candid or truthful statement
  • Synonym: frankly - used to introduce a statement that is honest or direct
