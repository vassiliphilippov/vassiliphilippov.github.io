to throw a curve

1. VERB

/ θɹoʊ ə kɜːɹv / (AmE), / θrəʊ ə kɜːv / (BrE)
to surprise or confuse someone by doing something unexpected
surprise or confuse someone unexpectedly
american

Examples

The interviewer threw a curve with an unexpected question.
The teacher threw a curve by moving the test to tomorrow.
His sudden decision to quit really threw a curve at the team.

Synset Examples

He threw her a curve when he announced his resignation.
The sudden change in plans really threw me a curve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throw a curveball - to make a surprising or difficult move; closely related idiom

Etymology

From baseball, where a 'curve' (curveball) is an unexpected pitch; American English, early 20th century.
