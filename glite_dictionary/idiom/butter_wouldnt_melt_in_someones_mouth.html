butter wouldn't melt in someone's mouth

1. X

/ ˈbʌt̬ɚ ˌwʊdnt ˈmɛlt ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz maʊθ / (AmE), / ˈbʌtə ˌwʊdnt ˈmɛlt ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz maʊθ / (BrE)
used to describe someone who appears to be innocent or demure, but may not actually be so
appears innocent or demure

Examples

She acts so sweet, but butter wouldn't melt in her mouth when she wants something.
He gave me a look so innocent—butter wouldn't melt in his mouth.
Don't believe her story; butter wouldn't melt in her mouth, but she's not as nice as she seems.

Synset Examples

He looked so calm, as if butter wouldn't melt in his mouth.
She acts so innocent, like butter wouldn't melt in her mouth.
Don't be fooled; butter wouldn't melt in her mouth.
