to clip someone's wings

1. VERB

/ klɪp ˈsʌmwʌnz wɪŋz / (AmE), / klɪp ˈsʌmwənz wɪŋz / (BrE)
to limit or restrict someone's power, freedom, or ability to achieve their potential
restrict someone's freedom or power

Examples

His parents tried to clip his wings by not letting him travel abroad alone.
The government imposed regulations that would clip the company's wings.
She refused to let criticism clip her wings and pursued her dreams anyway.

Synset Examples

They sought to clip the wings of the opposition party.
Don't clip someone's wings; encourage their growth instead.
The new rules will clip someone's wings in the project.
Her manager's criticism really clipped her wings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hold back - to prevent someone from making progress
  • Synonym: to restrain - to prevent someone or something from doing what they want to do
  • Antonym: to liberate - to set someone free from restrictions

Etymology

From the literal act of trimming a bird's wings to prevent flight, figuratively applied to people since the 17th century.
