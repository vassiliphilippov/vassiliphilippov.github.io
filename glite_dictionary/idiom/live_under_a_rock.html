to live under a rock

1. VERB

/ lɪv ˈʌndɚ ə rɑk / (AmE), / lɪv ˈʌndə ə rɒk / (BrE)
to be unaware of current events or ignorant of things that are widely known
be unaware of current events

Examples

If you've never heard of that movie, you must live under a rock.
She doesn't use social media and seems to live under a rock most of the time.
Unless you live under a rock, you know that the internet is essential these days.

Synset Examples

If you haven't heard the news, you must live under a rock.
Do you live under a rock?
He acts as if he’s been living under a rock for years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: out of touch - not having knowledge about something happening or changing
  • Antonym: aware - having knowledge or perception of a situation or fact (opposite sense)
