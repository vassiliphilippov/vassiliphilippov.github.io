by and large

1. ADV

/ ˌbaɪ ən ˈlɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ ən ˈlɑːdʒ / (BrE)
in general; on the whole; usually
in general; usually

Examples

By and large, people were satisfied with the new policies.
The forecast says it will be sunny, but by and large we get a little rain most months.
By and large, our students perform above average on national exams.

Synset Examples

by and large it doesn't rain much here

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generally - in most cases; usually
  • Synonym: usually - in most situations; most of the time
  • Antonym: specifically - in a detailed and exact way

Etymology

Originally a nautical term referring to sailing both 'by' (close to the wind) and 'large' (with the wind), meaning 'in all ways' or 'on the whole'.

2. ADV

/ baɪ ənd lɑrdʒ / (AmE), / baɪ ənd lɑːdʒ / (BrE)
used to mean 'generally' or 'for the most part'; on the whole.
generally; for the most part

Examples

By and large, the new policy has been effective across all departments.
The weather, by and large, has been excellent this spring.
By and large, students were satisfied with the outcome of the election.

Synset Examples

By and large, the project was successful.
By and large, I agree with your opinion.
The team performed well by and large.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generally - in most cases; usually
  • Synonym: mostly - for the most part; mainly
  • Antonym: rarely - not often; seldom

Etymology

Originated from nautical terminology in the 17th century, referring to sailing both 'by the wind' and 'large' (with the wind).
