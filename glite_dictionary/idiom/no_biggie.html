no biggie

1. X

/ noʊ ˈbɪɡi / (AmE), / nəʊ ˈbɪɡi / (BrE)
(informal) Used to indicate that something is not a serious problem or not worth worrying about.
not a serious problem (informal)
informal

Examples

It’s no biggie if you arrive a few minutes late; we haven’t started yet.
She forgot to call, but honestly, it’s no biggie.
Don’t worry about the broken mug—it’s no biggie at all.

Synset Examples

Missing the deadline? No biggie, we can extend it.
Spilling coffee on my shirt is no biggie.
He forgot my birthday, but it’s no biggie.
It's no biggy; we can fix it later.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: no big deal - not a serious problem
  • Antonym: big deal - an important issue or event
