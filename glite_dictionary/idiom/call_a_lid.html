to call a lid

1. VERB

/ kɔl ə lɪd / (AmE), / kɔːl ə lɪd / (BrE)
to bring something to an end or to stop or restrict an activity
end or stop an activity

Examples

After an intense debate, the chair decided to call a lid on further discussion.
We usually call a lid around 5 p.m., unless there’s urgent business.
The manager called a lid on new proposals until next week.

Synset Examples

The team will call a lid if the discussion turns negative.
They decided to call a lid on the meeting early.
After two hours, they finally called a lid on the negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to call it a day - to stop working for the day; to stop an activity
  • Synonym: to wrap up - to finish or conclude something
