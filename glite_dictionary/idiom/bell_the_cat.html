to bell the cat

1. VERB

/ bɛl ðə kæt / (AmE), / bɛl ðə kæt / (BrE)
to take on a difficult or risky task, especially one that others are reluctant to attempt
take a risky or bold action

Examples

After hours of discussion, no one wanted to bell the cat and confront the boss about the issue.
It's a bold move to bell the cat, but someone has to stand up for the team.
Who is brave enough to bell the cat and report the fraud to management?

Synset Examples

Who is going to bell the cat?

Etymology

From the fable 'The Mice in Council', in which mice suggest putting a bell on the cat to warn of its approach, but none will undertake the dangerous task.

2. VERB

/ bɛl ðə kæt / (AmE), / bɛl ðə kæt / (BrE)
to undertake a risky or dangerous task, especially one for the benefit of a group when others are reluctant to do so
undertake a risky or difficult task

Examples

No one wanted to bell the cat by delivering the bad news to management.
Maria decided she would bell the cat and address the team’s growing concerns.
It takes courage to bell the cat when everyone else is afraid to act.

Synset Examples

Someone had to bell the cat about the budget cuts.
It was time to bell the cat and take action.
They needed to bell the cat and confront the issue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take the plunge - to commit oneself to a challenging or risky course of action
  • Synonym: to stick one's neck out - to take a risk, usually for the benefit of others

Etymology

From the fable ‘The Mice in Council’, where a mouse proposes to hang a bell around a cat’s neck to be warned of its approach, but no mouse is willing to do it.
