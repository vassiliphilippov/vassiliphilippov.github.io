to wallow in the mire

1. VERB

/ ˈwɑloʊ ɪn ðə ˈmaɪər / (AmE), / ˈwɒləʊ ɪn ðə ˈmaɪə / (BrE)
to remain in or become overly absorbed by a difficult, unpleasant, or unhappy situation, especially instead of trying to improve it
indulge in unhappiness or difficulties

Examples

After his business failed, he began to wallow in the mire of self-pity.
Try not to wallow in the mire when things go wrong; focus on moving forward instead.
She refused to wallow in the mire of disappointment and started planning her next steps.

Synset Examples

Stop wallowing in the mire and take action.
He tends to wallow in the mire of his regrets.
Don't wallow in the mire; focus on solutions instead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wallow - to indulge oneself excessively

Etymology

Uses "wallow" (to roll about in mud or water) in a figurative sense; "mire" means muddy ground, symbolizing trouble or difficulty.
