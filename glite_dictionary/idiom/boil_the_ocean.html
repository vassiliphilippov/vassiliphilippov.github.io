to boil the ocean

1. VERB

/ bɔɪl ði ˈoʊʃən / (AmE), / bɔɪl ði ˈəʊʃən / (BrE)
to attempt to do something impractically ambitious or impossible, usually by taking on too much at once
try to do the impossible

Examples

If we try to boil the ocean, we'll never finish this project on time.
The committee was criticized for attempting to boil the ocean with their extensive reform plans.
He warned the team not to boil the ocean and instead to focus on achievable goals.

Synset Examples

She realized she was trying to boil the ocean with her grand plans.
We can't boil the ocean; let's focus on smaller tasks.
Trying to solve every problem at once is like boiling the ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bite off more than you can chew - to try to do more than you are able to handle
  • Synonym: mission impossible - a task that cannot be completed
