to go to your head

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ tə jʊər hɛd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə jɔː hɛd / (BrE)
(of praise, success, or power) to make someone too proud or conceited.
to make someone conceited or arrogant

Examples

Try not to let the promotion go to your head; stay focused and humble.
Fame can easily go to your head if you aren't careful.
She didn't let the compliments go to her head and remained modest.

Synset Examples

Don't let praise go to your head, stay humble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get big-headed - to become conceited or arrogant
