to have one's way with

1. VERB

/ hæv wʌnz weɪ wɪð / (AmE), / hæv wʌnz weɪ wɪð / (BrE)
to skillfully manipulate, control, or use something or someone as desired; to achieve one's intended result with finesse
skillfully manipulate or control

Examples

The conductor had his way with the orchestra, creating a stunning performance.
She had her way with the interview, impressing everyone with her confidence.
The chef skillfully had his way with the local ingredients, crafting an unforgettable meal.

Synset Examples

The artist had her way with the canvas, creating a masterpiece.
He had his way with the negotiations, securing a great deal.
She managed to have her way with the project design.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to manipulate - to handle or control skillfully

2. VERB

/ hæv wʌnz weɪ wɪð / (AmE), / hæv wʌnz weɪ wɪð / (BrE)
to be able to influence, control, or manipulate someone to get what one wants from them
influence or manipulate someone

Examples

Even as a child, she could have her way with most adults by using her charm.
The politician seemed to have his way with the media during the press conference.
Skilled negotiators often have their way with less experienced clients.

Synset Examples

She could have her way with anyone at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to manipulate - to control or influence a person or situation cleverly
