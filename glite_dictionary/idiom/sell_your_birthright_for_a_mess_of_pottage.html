to sell your birthright for a mess of pottage

1. VERB

/ sɛl jʊr ˈbɝːθˌraɪt fɔr ə mɛs əv ˈpɑːtɪdʒ / (AmE), / sɛl jɔː ˈbɜːθˌraɪt fə ə mɛs əv ˈpɒtɪdʒ / (BrE)
to foolishly give up something of great value for something of little or no worth, often for immediate gain
give up something valuable for little gain

Examples

Some believe the nation is about to sell its birthright for a mess of pottage through short-term decisions.
He realized too late that he'd sold his birthright for a mess of pottage just to impress his friends.
Don't sell your birthright for a mess of pottage by abandoning your principles for quick success.

Synset Examples

She regretted selling her birthright for a mess of pottage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to throw the baby out with the bathwater - lose something valuable by mistake while trying to get rid of something unwanted
  • Synonym: short-sighted - lacking foresight or consideration for the future

Etymology

From the biblical story in Genesis of Esau selling his birthright to Jacob for a meal (a 'mess of pottage').
