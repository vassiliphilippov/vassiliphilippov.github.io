a month of Sundays

1. NOUN

/ ə mʌnθ əv ˈsʌndeɪz / (AmE), / ə mʌnθ əv ˈsʌndeɪz / (BrE)
An extremely long period of time; a duration so long that it seems unlikely ever to end.
a very long time

Examples

You won't finish this puzzle in a month of Sundays without some help.
He hasn't called in a month of Sundays, and I doubt he ever will.
It would take me a month of Sundays to clean out that old attic.

Synset Examples

You’d never in a month of Sundays see me do that.
I would wait a month of Sundays for that concert.
He wouldn’t help her never in a month of Sundays.
It'll take a month of Sundays to finish this project.

Etymology

Refers imaginatively to a period of thirty or more Sundays, suggesting an eternity.
