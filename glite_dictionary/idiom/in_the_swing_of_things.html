in the swing of things

1. X

/ ɪn ðə swɪŋ əv θɪŋz / (AmE), / ɪn ðə swɪŋ əv θɪŋz / (BrE)
becoming fully involved in or accustomed to an activity or situation; feeling comfortable or confident doing something new
comfortable or fully involved in something new

Examples

After a few weeks of training, I finally feel in the swing of things at the office.
It can take new students some time to get in the swing of things at university.
Once the festival started, everyone quickly got in the swing of things and enjoyed the party atmosphere.

Synset Examples

It took a while, but I'm finally in the swing of things at my new job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to settle in - to become comfortable in a new situation
