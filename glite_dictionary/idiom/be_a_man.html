to be a man

1. VERB

/ bi ə mæn / (AmE), / biː ə mæn / (BrE)
to act with maturity, courage, or responsibility, especially when facing a difficult situation
act with maturity or courage

Examples

His father told him to be a man and face the truth.
It's time to be a man and admit your mistake.
She encouraged her brother to be a man and apologize for his actions.

Synset Examples

He told his son to be a man and take responsibility.
To succeed, sometimes you just need to be a man.
In tough times, you must learn to be a man.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to man up - to behave in a way considered typically courageous or responsible
  • Antonym: coward - a person who lacks courage
