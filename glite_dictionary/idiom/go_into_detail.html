to go into detail

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈɪntu ˈdiːteɪl / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈɪntuː ˈdiːteɪl / (BrE)
to provide thorough or extensive explanation, covering all the important points
explain thoroughly or at length

Examples

During the interview, she refused to go into detail about the incident.
The technical manual goes into detail about how the machine works.
If you want me to go into detail, please let me know what you need clarified.

Synset Examples

She went into detail about her vacation plans.
They will go into detail during the presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elaborate - to explain or describe something in greater depth
  • Antonym: to summarize - to give a brief statement of main points
