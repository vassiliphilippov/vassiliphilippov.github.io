to snap someone's head off

1. VERB

/ snæp ˈsʌmwʌnz hɛd ɔf / (AmE), / snæp ˈsʌmwənz hɛd ɒf / (BrE)
to respond angrily or speak harshly to someone, especially without good reason
respond angrily; speak harshly

Examples

When I asked a simple question, she snapped my head off in front of everyone.
You don't have to snap his head off just because you're tired.
If you snap someone's head off every time you're upset, people will stop coming to you for help.

Synset Examples

Don't snap my head off, I was just asking!
She tends to snap people's heads off when stressed.
He snapped her head off over a minor mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bite someone's head off - to speak angrily to someone, often without reason
