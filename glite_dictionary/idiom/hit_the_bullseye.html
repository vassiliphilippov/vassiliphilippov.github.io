to hit the bullseye

1. VERB

/ hɪt ðə ˈbʊlˌzaɪ / (AmE), / hɪt ðə ˈbʊlzaɪ / (BrE)
to achieve exactly what was intended or aimed for; to be precisely correct or accurate
to achieve precisely what was intended

Examples

Her analysis hit the bullseye and impressed everyone in the room.
If you want to succeed, your marketing strategy must hit the bullseye.
His joke really hit the bullseye and made the whole audience laugh.

Synset Examples

She threw the dart and hit the bullseye.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nail it - to do something successfully or exactly right
  • Antonym: to miss the mark - fail to achieve what was intended
