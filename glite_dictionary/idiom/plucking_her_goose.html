plucking her goose

1. X

/ ˈplʌkɪŋ hər ɡus / (AmE), / ˈplʌkɪŋ hə ɡuːs / (BrE)
engaging in behavior likely to bring harm or loss, especially through foolish decisions or risky investments
to act in a self-destructive or foolish way

Examples

After betting all her savings on a single horse, it was clear she was plucking her goose.
They warned him he was plucking his goose by ignoring professional advice.
If you think that scheme will make you rich, you're just plucking your goose.

Synset Examples

When she bought that luxury car, I thought the old woman is plucking her goose.
Everyone knows the old woman is plucking her goose with that investment.
He joked that the old woman is plucking her goose if she thinks she'll get rich quick.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shooting oneself in the foot - to do something that causes harm to one's own interests
  • Synonym: throwing good money after bad - continuing to invest in something futile or risky
