to have a cow

1. VERB

/ hæv ə kaʊ / (AmE), / hæv ə kaʊ / (BrE)
to become very upset or angry, often over something minor or trivial
become very upset or angry
informal

Examples

Don't have a cow; it was just a mistake.
My mom will have a cow if she finds out I broke her vase.
If your boss sees you slacking off, he'll probably have a cow.

Synset Examples

Don't have a cow; it was just a mistake.
She tends to have a cow over little things.
You’re going to have a cow when you see this!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lose one's temper - to suddenly become very angry
  • Antonym: to keep one's cool - to remain calm and not become angry
