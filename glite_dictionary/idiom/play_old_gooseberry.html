to play Old Gooseberry

1. VERB

/ pleɪ oʊld ˈɡuːsbɛri / (AmE), / pleɪ əʊld ˈɡuːsbəri / (BrE)
to meddle or interfere in other people's affairs
meddle or interfere in others' affairs
archaic

Examples

Whenever there was an argument, he would play Old Gooseberry and make things worse.
She warned her sister not to play Old Gooseberry in their family's business.
It is unwise to play Old Gooseberry between friends, as it can lead to resentment.

Synset Examples

They didn’t want to play Old Gooseberry at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to meddle - to interfere in something that is not one's concern
  • Synonym: to interfere - to get involved in something that does not concern one; intervene

Etymology

Likely refers to the old term 'gooseberry-picker,' historically used for a chaperone who interferes with a romantic couple.
