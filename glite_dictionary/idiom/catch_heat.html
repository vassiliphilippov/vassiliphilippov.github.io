to catch heat

1. VERB

/ kætʃ hiːt / (AmE), / katʃ hiːt / (BrE)
to receive criticism, blame, or backlash for something, especially from the public or authorities
receive criticism or backlash
informal

Examples

The company began to catch heat after its latest product recall.
He knew he would catch heat from his teammates for missing the winning shot.
Several officials have caught heat over their lack of transparency.

Synset Examples

She might catch heat for that controversial decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: come under fire - to be criticized or attacked
