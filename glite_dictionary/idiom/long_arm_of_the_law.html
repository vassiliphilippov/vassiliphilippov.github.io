long arm of the law

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ ɑrm əv ðə lɔ / (AmE), / lɒŋ ɑːm əv ðə lɔː / (BrE)
used to refer to the far-reaching power and authority of law enforcement to apprehend wrongdoers, no matter where they are.
the extensive power of law enforcement

Examples

After years on the run, the long arm of the law finally caught up with the fugitive.
No matter where he tried to hide, the long arm of the law always seemed one step behind.
He underestimated the long arm of the law when planning his escape.

Synset Examples
