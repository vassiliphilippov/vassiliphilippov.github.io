to rise like Lazarus from the ashes

1. VERB

/ raɪz laɪk ˈlӕzərəs frəm ði ˈæʃɪz / (AmE), / raɪz laɪk ˈlazərəs frəm ði ˈaʃɪz / (BrE)
to be revived or recover successfully after experiencing complete failure or ruin
recover after total failure

Examples

Despite bankruptcy, the company managed to rise like Lazarus from the ashes within just two years.
After years of obscurity, the artist rose like Lazarus from the ashes with a groundbreaking exhibition.
The town, destroyed by the wildfire, is now rising like Lazarus from the ashes thanks to community efforts.

Synset Examples

The project was a total failure, but it rose like Lazarus from the ashes.

Etymology

From the biblical story of Lazarus (John 11), who was raised from the dead, combined with the imagery of rising from ashes, as in the phoenix myth.
