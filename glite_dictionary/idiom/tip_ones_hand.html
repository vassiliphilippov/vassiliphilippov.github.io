to tip one's hand

1. VERB

/ tɪp wʌnz hænd / (AmE), / tɪp wʌnz hænd / (BrE)
to unintentionally or prematurely reveal one's intentions, plans, or resources to others
reveal one's intentions or plans

Examples

The company director refused to tip his hand in the press conference.
If you tip your hand too soon, the competition will react accordingly.
She tried not to tip her hand before the merger was finalized.

Synset Examples

He didn't want to tip his hand during negotiations.
She managed to tip her hand too early in the game.
Don't tip your hand until the final round.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give the game away - to reveal a secret or intention unintentionally
  • Antonym: hold one's cards close to one's chest - to keep one's plans or intentions secret

Etymology

From the practice in card games of accidentally showing one's cards (hand) to other players, making one's position known.
