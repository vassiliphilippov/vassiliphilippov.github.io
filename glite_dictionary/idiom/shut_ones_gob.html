to shut one's gob

1. VERB

/ ʃʌt wʌnz ɡɑb / (AmE), / ʃʌt wʌnz ɡɒb / (BrE)
(informal, British) Used to tell someone to stop talking or to be quiet.
tell someone to stop talking (informal, British)
informal british

Examples

If you’re going to complain all evening, just shut your gob.
He told his friends to shut their gob and listen to the instructions.
You’d better shut your gob before you get yourself in more trouble.

Synset Examples

He was told to shut his gob during the meeting.
She wished he would just shut his gob for once.
If you don't like it, just shut your gob.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shut up - an informal way to tell someone to stop talking

Etymology

‘Gob’ meaning ‘mouth’ comes from Irish Gaelic ‘gob’ (beak, mouth).
