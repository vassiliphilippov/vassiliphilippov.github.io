to start from the drawing board

1. VERB

/ stɑrt frəm ðə ˈdrɔɪŋ ˌbɔrd / (AmE), / stɑːt frəm ðə ˈdrɔːɪŋ ˌbɔːd / (BrE)
to begin a process, plan, or project again from the very beginning, especially after a failure or setback
begin again from the beginning

Examples

After the project was rejected, the team had to start from the drawing board and rethink their approach.
If this prototype doesn't work, we'll just start from the drawing board and try a new design.
The engineers realized they'd made an error and had to start from the drawing board to fix it.

Synset Examples

She had to start from the drawing board after the redesign.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: back to the drawing board - used to indicate starting again after a failure

Etymology

Alludes to returning to architectural drawing boards to create a new design after the previous was rejected.
