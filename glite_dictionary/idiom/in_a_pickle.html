in a pickle

1. X

/ ɪn ə ˈpɪkəl / (AmE), / ɪn ə ˈpɪkəl / (BrE)
in a difficult or troublesome situation
in a difficult situation (idiom)

Examples

I'm really in a pickle now that I’ve lost my wallet and keys.
Sarah found herself in a pickle after missing her flight home.
They were in a pickle when both computers crashed during the presentation.

Synset Examples

He’s really in a pickle over that lost project.
She was in a pickle trying to balance work and family.
I found myself in a pickle when my car broke down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in a jam - in a difficult or awkward situation (idiom)
  • Synonym: in hot water - in trouble or difficulty (idiom)

Etymology

Originally from 16th-century English; 'pickle' used figuratively for a bad situation, possibly influenced by Dutch 'in de pekel zitten.'
