mare's nest

1. NOUN

/ mɛrz nɛst / (AmE), / meəz nɛst / (BrE)
a complex situation involving confusion, misunderstanding, or a tangle of mistaken ideas; a confused jumble of things
a confused or tangled situation

Examples

The new project quickly turned into a mare's nest of conflicting ideas and lost paperwork.
He thought he had discovered something important, but it was just a mare's nest.
Trying to fix the old computer became a mare's nest of tangled wires and missing parts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mess - a dirty or confused state
  • Synonym: tangle - a complicated or confusing situation

Etymology

First recorded in the 16th century; originally implied a mistaken discovery, as in finding a 'mare's nest.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛrz ˌnɛst / (AmE), / ˈmeəz ˌnɛst / (BrE)
a situation that is confusing, chaotic, and full of problems or misunderstandings
confusing or chaotic situation

Examples

The new manager inherited a mare's nest of unresolved complaints when she took over the department.
Sorting through the accounts revealed a mare's nest of conflicting and missing records.
After months of neglect, the software codebase had become a mare's nest no one wanted to deal with.

Synset Examples

This project turned into a mare's nest of issues.
The report was a mare's nest of errors.
He found a mare's nest of confusion in her explanation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mess - a situation that is confused or full of problems
  • Synonym: shambles - a condition of great disorder or confusion

Etymology

Originally from the 16th century, likely referring to a supposed discovery that turns out to be imaginary or mistaken.
