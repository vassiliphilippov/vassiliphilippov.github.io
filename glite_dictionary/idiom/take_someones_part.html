to take someone's part

1. VERB

/ teɪk sʌmˌwʌnz pɑrt / (AmE), / teɪk sʌmwʌnz pɑːt / (BrE)
to support or defend someone, especially in a dispute or argument
support or defend someone

Examples

No matter the situation, she always takes my part when others criticize me.
During the heated argument, I was grateful that John decided to take my part.
If you feel unfairly treated, you can count on me to take your part.

Synset Examples

Even when I’m wrong, he takes my part.
It's nice to know she will take my part no matter what.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stand up for - to defend or support someone or something
  • Synonym: to side with - to support one person or group in an argument

support or defend someone
