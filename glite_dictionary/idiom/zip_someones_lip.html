to zip someone's lip

1. VERB

/ zɪp sʌmwʌnz lɪp / (AmE), / zɪp sʌmwʌnz lɪp / (BrE)
To keep quiet or refrain from speaking, especially deliberately or to avoid revealing information.
remain silent; not speak
informal

Examples

Before the announcement, I had to zip my lip so I wouldn't spoil the surprise.
You'd better zip your lip about what you just saw.
He knew it was wise to zip his lip during the heated argument.

Synset Examples

I had to zip my lip during the meeting.
You should zip your lip about the surprise party.
If you want to stay safe, zip your lip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keep quiet - to not speak or make noise
  • Synonym: to hold one's tongue - to refrain from speaking or expressing an opinion

Etymology

Figurative use of 'zip' (close with a zipper) to mean shutting one's mouth.
