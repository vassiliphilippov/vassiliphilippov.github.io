road to Damascus

1. NOUN

/ roʊd tə dəˈmæskəs / (AmE), / rəʊd tə dəˈmɑːskəs / (BrE)
a sudden and significant turning point or change in beliefs, values, or behavior, especially one that is dramatic or transformative.
a dramatic turning point or conversion

Examples

After years of skepticism, James experienced his road to Damascus moment and became an advocate for climate action.
The CEO's sudden road to Damascus conversion led to sweeping changes in company policy.
Many people reach a road to Damascus point in their lives, fundamentally altering their path.

Etymology

From the biblical account of Saul's (Paul the Apostle's) conversion on the road to Damascus (Acts 9:1–19).

2. NOUN

/ roʊd tə dəˈmæskəs / (AmE), / rəʊd tə dəˈmæskəs / (BrE)
a moment when someone experiences a sudden and significant change in beliefs or perspective
moment of sudden realization or change

Examples

Many politicians have had a road to Damascus experience that altered their views on social issues.
After reading the report, she underwent a road to Damascus transformation concerning environmental policy.
Traveling abroad proved to be his road to Damascus, changing his approach to life completely.

Synset Examples

Her decision to change careers was her road to Damascus.
The therapy session was his road to Damascus for healing.
He had a road to Damascus moment during the lecture.

Etymology

From the biblical account of Saul's (later Paul) conversion on the road to Damascus, described in the New Testament.
