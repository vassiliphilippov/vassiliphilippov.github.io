to shoot from the hip

1. VERB

/ ʃuːt frəm ðə hɪp / (AmE), / ʃuːt frəm ðə hɪp / (BrE)
to act or speak quickly and without careful thought or planning
act or speak impulsively

Examples

When challenged, she tends to shoot from the hip rather than provide a measured response.
It's risky to shoot from the hip during important business negotiations.
He admitted he was shooting from the hip when he criticized the new policy.

Synset Examples

He tends to shoot from the hip when giving advice.
Her comments were blunt; she really shot from the hip.
During the meeting, he shot from the hip without filtering his thoughts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speak off the cuff - to speak without preparation
  • Antonym: to think before you speak - to consider one's words carefully before speaking

Etymology

Originally referring to firing a gun without aiming carefully, likely from the practice of drawing and firing a handgun quickly from the hip.
