to make tracks

1. VERB

/ meɪk træks / (AmE), / meɪk træks / (BrE)
to leave or depart quickly, often in a hurry to avoid something or someone
depart quickly; leave in a hurry
informal

Examples

As soon as the rain started, we decided to make tracks for home.
He realized he was late and hurriedly made tracks out of the office.
Let's make tracks before the traffic gets worse.

Synset Examples

As soon as the meeting ended, he made tracks for the door.
We should make tracks if we want to catch the train.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave - to go away from a place
  • Antonym: to linger - to remain in a place longer than necessary
