to hit like a lightning bolt

1. VERB

/ hɪt laɪk ə ˈlaɪtnɪŋ boʊlt / (AmE), / hɪt laɪk ə ˈlaɪtnɪŋ bəʊlt / (BrE)
to affect someone suddenly and powerfully, causing shock or surprise
affect suddenly; shock or surprise

Examples

The announcement hit like a lightning bolt, and nobody knew how to react.
When Jane heard the diagnosis, the news hit like a lightning bolt and left her speechless.
The truth hit like a lightning bolt when he finally realized what had happened.

Synset Examples

The news hit him like a lightning bolt.
The news hit him like a thunderbolt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hit like a thunderbolt - to be struck by something suddenly and forcefully, causing shock
  • Synonym: to take aback - to surprise or shock someone
