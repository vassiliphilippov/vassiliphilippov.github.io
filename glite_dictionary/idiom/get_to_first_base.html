to get to first base

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt tuː fɜrst beɪs / (AmE), / ɡɛt tə fɜːst beɪs / (BrE)
to make initial progress, especially in a task or relationship; to reach the first stage of success or intimacy
make initial progress; first stage

Examples

Despite his efforts, he couldn’t get to first base with the interviewers.
It takes time to get to first base in a competitive market.
She was relieved when their conversation finally got to first base.

Synset Examples

After months of dating, they finally got to first base.
We just need to get to first base before considering the next steps.
In this game, you need to get to first base fast.
It's hard to get to first base when you're nervous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get off the ground - to begin to be successful, to start progressing
  • Antonym: to strike out - to fail to achieve a goal, especially in romance or business

Etymology

From baseball, where reaching first base marks the first stage of offense.
