salt of the earth

1. NOUN

/ sɔlt əv ði ɝθ / (AmE), / sɒlt əv ðiː ɜːθ / (BrE)
an admirable, honest, and reliable person who is valued in their community or group
admirable, honest, reliable person

Examples

Everyone in the village considers Jane the salt of the earth because of her kindness.
The nurses who worked overtime during the crisis were the true salt of the earth.
Despite his success, he remains the salt of the earth—always humble and helpful.

Synset Examples

He’s a guy who’s truly the salt of the earth.
The volunteers are the salt of the earth in our community.
My grandmother is the salt of the earth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pillar of society - a respected and dependable member of society

Etymology

From the biblical phrase in the Gospel of Matthew, referring to people of great worth.
