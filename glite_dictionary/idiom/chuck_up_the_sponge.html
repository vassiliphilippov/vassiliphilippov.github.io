to chuck up the sponge

1. VERB

/ tʃʌk ʌp ðə spʌndʒ / (AmE), / tʃʌk ʌp ðə spʌndʒ / (BrE)
to admit defeat or stop trying, usually after realizing further effort is futile
admit defeat; give up trying
archaic

Examples

After years of unsuccessful attempts, she finally decided to chuck up the sponge and close her business.
The exhausted team knew it was time to chuck up the sponge when the score became impossible to overcome.
He refused to chuck up the sponge despite facing multiple setbacks throughout the project.

Synset Examples

In the second round, the challenger gave up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give up - to stop trying; to admit defeat
  • Synonym: surrender - to cease resistance and submit
  • Antonym: to persevere - to persist in an activity despite difficulty

Etymology

Based on the practice in boxing of throwing a sponge into the ring to concede defeat.
