to pay the piper

1. VERB

/ peɪ ðə ˈpaɪpər / (AmE), / peɪ ðə ˈpaɪpə / (BrE)
to accept the unpleasant consequences of one's actions or decisions
face the consequences of actions

Examples

After years of overspending, he finally had to pay the piper by selling his house.
She skipped her assignments all semester and had to pay the piper at exam time.
If you break the rules, you will eventually have to pay the piper.

Synset Examples

After months of ignoring his health, he finally paid the piper.
She knew she had to pay the piper for her indulgent lifestyle.
If you spend recklessly, you'll pay the piper later.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to face the music - to accept the unpleasant results of one's actions

Etymology

From the proverb "he who pays the piper calls the tune"; the idiom refers to paying for music after the dance, or more broadly, having to face consequences.
