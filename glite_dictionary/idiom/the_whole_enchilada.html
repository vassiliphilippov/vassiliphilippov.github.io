the whole enchilada

1. NOUN

/ ðə hoʊl ɛn.tʃɪˈlɑː.də / (AmE), / ðə həʊl ɛn.tʃɪˈlɑː.də / (BrE)
everything involved in a situation; the entirety of something or the whole thing
the entire thing; everything involved
informal american

Examples

After months of planning, they finally launched the whole enchilada last week.
If you're investing in this company, you're getting the whole enchilada—all assets and liabilities included.
He didn't want just a small role; he wanted the whole enchilada.

Synset Examples

Completing the assignment means delivering the whole enchilada.
The project was the whole enchilada, including everything we discussed.
I want the whole enchilada, not just the appetizers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the whole shebang - everything involved or the whole thing
  • Synonym: the works - everything available or involved
  • Antonym: part - a portion or segment, as opposed to the whole

Etymology

From Spanish 'enchilada' (a Mexican dish), used idiomatically in American English since the late 20th century to mean 'the whole thing.'
