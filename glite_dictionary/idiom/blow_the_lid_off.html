to blow the lid off

1. VERB

/ bloʊ ðə lɪd ɔf / (AmE), / bləʊ ðə lɪd ɒf / (BrE)
to reveal or expose secret or hidden information, especially something scandalous or shocking
reveal hidden or secret information

Examples

The whistleblower's testimony could blow the lid off the corruption scheme.
The press investigation finally blew the lid off the government's illegal activities.
Her new book promises to blow the lid off the entertainment industry's secrets.

Synset Examples

His revelations are set to blow the lid off the cover-up.
The journalist aimed to blow the lid off the secret project.
The investigation could blow the lid off the scandal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expose - to reveal something hidden or secret
  • Antonym: to cover up - to conceal or hide the truth about something
