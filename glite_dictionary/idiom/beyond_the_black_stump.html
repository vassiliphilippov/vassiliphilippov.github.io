beyond the black stump

1. ADV

/ biˈɑnd ðə blæk stʌmp / (AmE), / biˈɒnd ðə blæk stʌmp / (BrE)
in a place that is very remote, isolated, or far from cities and towns, especially in the Australian outback
in a remote or rural area (Australia)
informal british

Examples

Visiting her farm meant driving beyond the black stump for several hours through dusty roads.
Our campsite was truly beyond the black stump, miles from the nearest town.
You won’t find a signal out there; it’s beyond the black stump.

Synset Examples

They live out in the bush, beyond the black stump.
The town is beyond the black stump and hard to reach.
He travels beyond the black stump for work every week.

Etymology

Australian English; refers to a symbolic or literal charred tree stump marking the edge of settlement in rural Australia.
