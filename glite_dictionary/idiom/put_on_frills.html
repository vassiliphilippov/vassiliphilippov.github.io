to put on frills

1. VERB

/ pʊt ɑn frɪlz / (AmE), / pʊt ɒn frɪlz / (BrE)
to add unnecessary decorations or embellishments, often to make something appear more elaborate or impressive than needed
add unnecessary embellishments

Examples

When it comes to business reports, he prefers not to put on frills and keeps things straightforward.
We decided to put on frills for the wedding and hired a fancy caterer.
There's no need to put on frills with your presentation; just stick to the main points.

Synset Examples

The invitation was simple; they didn’t put on frills.
She likes to put on frills for her birthday parties.
We can’t afford to put on frills for the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embellish - to make something more attractive by the addition of decorative details
