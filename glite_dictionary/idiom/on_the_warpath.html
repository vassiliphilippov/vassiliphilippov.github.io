on the warpath

1. X

/ ɑn ðə ˈwɔrˌpæθ / (AmE), / ɒn ðə ˈwɔːpɑːθ / (BrE)
angry and determined to punish someone or achieve something, especially after being wronged or upset
angrily seeking vengeance or action

Examples

After hearing the news, the director was on the warpath and called an urgent meeting.
You'd better finish your report today—the boss is on the warpath about deadlines.
She came home on the warpath after finding the house a mess.

Synset Examples

After the meeting, she was on the warpath about the budget cuts.
He was on the warpath after hearing the unfair decision.
He’s been on the warpath since he lost the competition.
The manager is on the warpath since sales are down.
After the argument, she was on the warpath for revenge.
The team was on the warpath to win the championship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: up in arms - angry and ready to protest or take action
  • Antonym: calm - not showing anger; peaceful or relaxed

Etymology

Originally referred to Native Americans preparing for battle; now used figuratively for anger or determination.
