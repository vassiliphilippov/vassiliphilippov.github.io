on one's game

1. X

/ ɑn wʌnz ɡeɪm / (AmE), / ɒn wʌnz ɡeɪm / (BrE)
performing at one's best or showing the highest level of skill or effectiveness
performing at one's best

Examples

He really needs to be on his game if he wants to impress the interviewers.
The team was on its game last night, winning by a large margin.
You have to be on your game during final exams to achieve the best results.

Synset Examples

She’s always on her game during important meetings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at the top of one's game - performing at the highest possible level
  • Antonym: off one's game - not performing as well as usual
