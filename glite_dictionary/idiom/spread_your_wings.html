to spread your wings

1. VERB

/ sprɛd jʊr wɪŋz / (AmE), / sprɛd jɔː wɪŋz / (BrE)
to start becoming independent and pursuing new experiences or opportunities, especially for the first time
become independent; explore new possibilities

Examples

After years of living at home, she decided it was time to spread her wings and move to another city.
College gives students a great opportunity to spread their wings and discover their interests.
He finally quit his job to spread his wings and start his own business.

Synset Examples

After graduation, she was ready to spread her wings.
He took a job abroad to spread his wings.
Traveling helps you spread your wings and grow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to branch out - start to do something different or new
  • Synonym: leave the nest - become independent, especially when leaving home for the first time
