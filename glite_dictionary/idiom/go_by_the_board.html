to go by the board

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ baɪ ðə bɔrd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ baɪ ðə bɔːd / (BrE)
(of a plan, proposal, or rule) to be rejected, ignored, or disregarded.
be disregarded or ignored

Examples

All safety precautions went by the board during the emergency.
Her earlier promises go by the board once she gets what she wants.
Many traditional customs have gone by the board in recent years.

Synset Examples

Her suggestion to improve efficiency went by the board.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disregard - to pay no attention to; ignore
  • Synonym: to ignore - to refuse to take notice of

Etymology

Originally a nautical term; from 'the board' meaning the side of a ship, if something 'goes by the board' it falls overboard and is lost.
