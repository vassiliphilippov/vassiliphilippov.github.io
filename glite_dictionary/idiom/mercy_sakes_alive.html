mercy sake's alive

1. INTJ

/ ˈmɜrsi ˌseɪks əˈlaɪv / (AmE), / ˈmɜːsi ˌseɪks əˈlaɪv / (BrE)
an exclamation of surprise or disbelief
expression of surprise or disbelief
archaic

Examples

Mercy sake's alive, what in the world happened to your car?
Mercy sake's alive, I haven't seen you in years!
Mercy sake's alive, this cake is absolutely delicious!

Synset Examples

Mercy sake's alive, what a surprise to see you here!
Mercy sake's alive, I can't believe you did that!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goodness gracious - an exclamation of surprise or shock
  • Synonym: heavens above - used to express surprise or disbelief

Etymology

From 'mercy's sake(s)' (invoking mercy), + 'alive' as an intensifier; common in Southern and Appalachian English.
