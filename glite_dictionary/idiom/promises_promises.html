promises, promises

1. INTJ

/ ˈprɑːmɪsɪz prəˈmɪsɪz / (AmE), / ˈprɒmɪsɪz prəˈmɪsɪz / (BrE)
Used as a sarcastic remark to indicate disbelief or skepticism that promises made will actually be fulfilled.
sarcastic remark about unkept promises

Examples

He keeps saying he'll help with the dishes, but it's just promises, promises.
She laughed and replied, 'promises, promises,' when he swore he'd never be late again.
After hearing it so many times, 'promises, promises' was all I could say.

Synset Examples

He said he would fix it; promises, promises.
She rolled her eyes and said, 'Promises, promises.'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empty promises - promises that are not intended to be kept
