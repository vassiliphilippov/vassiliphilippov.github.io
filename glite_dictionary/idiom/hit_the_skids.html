to hit the skids

1. VERB

/ hɪt ðə skɪdz / (AmE), / hɪt ðə skɪdz / (BrE)
to begin to fail, decline, or come to an abrupt stop, especially after a period of success.
to begin to fail or decline
informal american

Examples

After the economy crashed, many small businesses hit the skids and closed their doors.
Once his irresponsible behavior was exposed, his political campaign hit the skids quickly.
When sales started dropping, it didn't take long for the entire division to hit the skids.

Synset Examples

The project really hit the skids after the funding cut.
His career hit the skids after that scandal.
The company hit the skids when sales dropped.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go downhill - to gradually decline in quality or success
  • Antonym: to bounce back - to recover after a setback or decline

Etymology

Possibly refers to the literal 'skids'—wooden beams used to slow or stop vehicles, with figurative use developing in 20th-century American English.
