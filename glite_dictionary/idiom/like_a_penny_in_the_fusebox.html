like a penny in the fusebox

1. X

/ laɪk ə ˈpɛni ɪn ðə ˈfjuzbɑks / (AmE), / laɪk ə ˈpɛni ɪn ðə ˈfjuːzbɒks / (BrE)
used to describe something that is unexpected or surprising, often appearing out of place or unusual in a particular situation
unexpected or surprising occurrence

Examples

Her sudden laughter was like a penny in the fusebox, taking everyone by surprise.
His bold suggestion landed like a penny in the fusebox during the serious meeting.
The unexpected result felt like a penny in the fusebox to the researchers.

Synset Examples

His ideas were like a penny in the fusebox, unexpected but intriguing.

Etymology

Refers to the old practice of placing a coin in a fusebox as a risky improvisation; the phrase now means something out of place or surprising.
