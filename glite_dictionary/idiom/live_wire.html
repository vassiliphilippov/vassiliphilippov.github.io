live wire

1. NOUN

/ laɪv ˈwaɪɚ / (AmE), / laɪv ˈwaɪə / (BrE)
a person who is very energetic, enthusiastic, and lively
an energetic, lively person

Examples

At every event, Jake is the live wire who keeps the mood energetic.
Everyone loves having a live wire like Carla on the team because she brings so much enthusiasm.
The teacher described his new student as a real live wire who makes class more exciting.

Synset Examples

At the party, she was the live wire, entertaining everyone.
The new employee is a live wire, full of ideas.
That toddler is a live wire, always running around.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firecracker - an energetic and unpredictable person
  • Synonym: dynamo - a person with a lot of energy and enthusiasm
  • Antonym: wet blanket - a person who spoils other people's fun with lack of enthusiasm

Etymology

From the literal 'live wire' (an electrical wire carrying current), metaphorically referring to someone full of energy.
