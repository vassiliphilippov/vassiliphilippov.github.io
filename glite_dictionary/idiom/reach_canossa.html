to reach Canossa

1. VERB

/ riːtʃ kəˈnɑːsə / (AmE), / riːtʃ kəˈnɒsə / (BrE)
to achieve reconciliation after a period of difficulty or humiliation, often after swallowing one's pride
achieve difficult reconciliation

Examples

After years of disagreement, the two finally reached Canossa and resolved their dispute.
It took humility for the CEO to reach Canossa and ask for the board's forgiveness.
Many wondered whether the stubborn leader would ever reach Canossa and make peace with his rivals.

Synset Examples

He felt he would never reach Canossa after the long journey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reconcile - restore friendly relations after a conflict
  • Antonym: to hold a grudge - maintain resentment or anger against someone

Etymology

From the historical event in 1077 at Canossa, Italy, where Holy Roman Emperor Henry IV sought forgiveness from Pope Gregory VII.
