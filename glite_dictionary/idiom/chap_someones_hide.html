to chap someone's hide

1. VERB

/ tʃæp ˈsʌmwʌnz haɪd / (AmE), / tʃæp ˈsʌmwʌnz haɪd / (BrE)
to scold or reprimand someone severely, especially in an angry or irritated manner
scold or reprimand someone harshly
informal american

Examples

If you miss another deadline, the manager will chap your hide in front of the whole team.
My mother really chapped my hide for tracking mud all through the house.
Don't chap my hide about the report—I did the best I could.

Synset Examples

His boss would chap his hide for being late.
Don't chap my hide over a small mistake.
He threatened to chap my hide if I didn't apologize.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chew someone out - to scold or reprimand someone angrily
  • Antonym: let someone off the hook - to refrain from punishing or scolding someone

Etymology

From American slang, where 'hide' refers to a person's skin; 'to chap' means to roughen or irritate.
