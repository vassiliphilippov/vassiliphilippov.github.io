to catch your death

1. VERB

/ kætʃ jər dɛθ / (AmE), / katʃ jɔː dɛθ / (BrE)
(informal) to become seriously ill, especially from exposure to cold weather, often used as a warning or exaggeration.
become ill from cold weather (idiom)
informal

Examples

If you go outside like that, you'll catch your death!
Put on a scarf, or you'll catch your death standing in the snow.
My grandmother always warned us not to play in the rain or we'd catch your death.

Synset Examples

You’ll catch your death if you don’t wear a coat.
Don't sit by the window; you'll catch your death.
