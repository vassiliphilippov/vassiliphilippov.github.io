the living daylights out of

1. X

/ ðə ˈlɪvɪŋ ˈdeɪlaɪts aʊt ʌv / (AmE), / ðə ˈlɪvɪŋ ˈdeɪlaɪts aʊt ɒv / (BrE)
used for emphasis with verbs like 'scare' or 'beat' to indicate doing something to a very great or intense degree, especially in reference to shocking or frightening someone
intensifies expressions of fear or surprise

Examples

The dog jumped out from the bushes and scared the living daylights out of me.
The comedian's prank took the living daylights out of the unsuspecting audience member.
That horror story beat the living daylights out of any other I've heard.

Synset Examples

The horror film scared the living daylights out of him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scare stiff - to frighten someone very much
  • Synonym: scare to death - to frighten someone extremely
