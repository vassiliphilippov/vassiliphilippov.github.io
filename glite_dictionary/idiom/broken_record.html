broken record

1. NOUN

/ ˈbroʊkən ˈrɛkərd / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊkən ˈrɛkɔːd / (BrE)
a person who keeps repeating the same thing over and over, especially to the point of being annoying
someone who repeats the same thing persistently

Examples

I feel like a broken record reminding you to turn off the lights every day.
She sounded like a broken record with her constant reminders about deadlines.
He's been complaining about the same issue for weeks, just like a broken record.

Synset Examples

I feel like a broken record telling you to study.
She keeps repeating herself like a broken record.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nag - to persistently complain or remind someone about something
  • Synonym: harp on - to talk or complain about something repeatedly

Etymology

From the era of vinyl records, where a scratch would cause a record to repeat the same section.
