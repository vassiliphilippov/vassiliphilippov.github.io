in the sack

1. ADV

/ ɪn ðə sæk / (AmE), / ɪn ðə sæk / (BrE)
asleep or resting, especially in bed
asleep or in bed

Examples

I tried calling Lisa, but she was already in the sack by ten o'clock.
He likes to read for a few minutes before getting in the sack.
After a long day of hiking, I was glad to finally be in the sack.

Synset Examples

The little puppy curled up in the sack to nap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asleep - in a state of sleep
  • Antonym: awake - not sleeping
