and your little dog too

1. X

/ ənd jɔr ˈlɪt̬l dɑɡ tu / (AmE), / ənd jɔː ˈlɪtl dɒɡ tuː / (BrE)
Used humorously to indicate that someone or something additional is also included or welcome, often as an exaggerated or playful afterthought.
playful phrase for inclusive invitation

Examples

Feel free to bring your kids to the gathering—and and your little dog too!
The hotel welcomes families, pets, and and your little dog too.
Everyone’s invited to our backyard BBQ, and your little dog too!

Synset Examples

Bring your friends, and your little dog too!
You can come to the party, and your little dog too!

Etymology

Popularized by the 1939 film The Wizard of Oz, in which the Wicked Witch of the West threatens, "...and your little dog, too!"
