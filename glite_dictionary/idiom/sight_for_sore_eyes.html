sight for sore eyes

1. NOUN

/ saɪt fər sɔr aɪz / (AmE), / saɪt fə sɔː aɪz / (BrE)
a person or thing that is very welcome or pleasing to see, especially after a period of absence or difficulty
something very welcome to see

Examples

After two weeks away, his dog was truly a sight for sore eyes.
The hot meal was a sight for sore eyes after the exhausting hike.
Seeing a friendly face in the crowd was a sight for sore eyes.

Synset Examples

Her smile was a sight for sore eyes after the long meeting.
After a long trip, home was a sight for sore eyes.
The sunset was truly a sight for sore eyes.
