to be a chump

1. VERB

/ bi ə tʃʌmp / (AmE), / biː ə tʃʌmp / (BrE)
to act foolishly or to be easily deceived by others
act foolishly; be easily deceived
informal

Examples

He didn't want to be a chump and sign the contract without reading it first.
Don't be a chump—check the facts before you believe what you hear.
She realized she had been a chump to trust them so quickly.

Synset Examples

Don't be a chump and fall for their tricks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be a sucker - to be easily deceived or taken advantage of
  • Antonym: be wise - to act prudently or with good judgment
