to see for dust

1. VERB

/ si fɔɹ dʌst / (AmE), / siː fə dʌst / (BrE)
To never see someone again, especially after they leave quickly or disappear abruptly.
never see again after quick departure

Examples

After the project ended, I didn't see for dust any of my former colleagues.
The thief escaped so quickly that the police didn't see for dust of him.
Once she left the city, we didn't see for dust of her again.

Synset Examples

After their argument, she didn't see him for dust.
