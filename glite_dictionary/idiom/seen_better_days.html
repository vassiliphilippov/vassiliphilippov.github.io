to seen better days

1. VERB

/ sin ˈbɛtər deɪz / (AmE), / siːn ˈbɛtə deɪz / (BrE)
to be in poor condition or worse than it once was, especially due to age or neglect
in poor or worn condition

Examples

The old barn has seen better days, with its roof caving in and paint peeling.
My suitcase has seen better days after years of international travel.
This sofa has definitely seen better days and should probably be replaced.

Synset Examples

The old tenement building had seen better days.
The building has definitely seen better days.
He wore a scratty old jacket that had seen better days.
This old car has seen better days.
The restaurant has seen better days since the new management.
He wore a shabby coat that had seen better days.
She wore a scaggy sweater that had seen better days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: run-down - in a dilapidated or poor condition
