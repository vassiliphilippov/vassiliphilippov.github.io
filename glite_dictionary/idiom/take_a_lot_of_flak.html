to take a lot of flak

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə lɑt əv flæk / (AmE), / teɪk ə lɒt əv flæk / (BrE)
to receive severe criticism or blame
receive strong criticism or blame
informal

Examples

The manager took a lot of flak for the company's declining sales figures.
He takes a lot of flak from the media but handles it with grace.
After announcing the new policy, she took a lot of flak from her teammates.

Synset Examples

She took a lot of flak for her bold fashion choices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take criticism - to receive critical comments or disapproval
  • Synonym: take the heat - to endure pressure or criticism, especially in a challenging situation

Etymology

'Flak' from German Fliegerabwehrkanone ('aircraft defense cannon'), originally referring to anti-aircraft gunfire; later, it became figurative for intense criticism.
