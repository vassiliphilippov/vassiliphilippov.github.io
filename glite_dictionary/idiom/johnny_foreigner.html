Johnny Foreigner

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɑni ˈfɔɹənər / (AmE), / ˌdʒɒni ˈfɒrənə / (BrE)
a foreigner, usually used humorously or pejoratively, especially from a British perspective
humorous or pejorative term for a foreigner
british

Examples

As a Johnny Foreigner, he struggled with the local customs at first.
She joked about feeling like a Johnny Foreigner during her trip to London.
Some tabloids still refer to European players as Johnny Foreigner in sports coverage.

Synset Examples

As a Johnny Foreigner, he struggled with the local customs at first.
He embraced his role as a Johnny Foreigner and learned the language.
Being a Johnny Foreigner, she often received curious stares.

Etymology

Based on the personal name 'Johnny' used generically, plus 'foreigner'.
