it takes one to know one

1. X

/ ɪt teɪks wʌn tə noʊ wʌn / (AmE), / ɪt teɪks wʌn tə nəʊ wʌn / (BrE)
used as a retort to suggest that someone who criticizes another person has the same fault or characteristic themselves
retort implying shared fault

Examples

Whenever someone accuses her of gossiping, she quickly replies, "it takes one to know one."
He laughed off the criticism by saying, "it takes one to know one, buddy."
If you call me stubborn, just remember, it takes one to know one.

Synset Examples

You can't judge me; it takes one to know one.
She pointed out my mistakes, but it takes one to know one.
When he called me lazy, I thought, 'it takes one to know one.'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: look who's talking - used to point out hypocrisy in the speaker
  • Synonym: pot calling the kettle black - idiom used when someone accuses another of a fault they also possess
