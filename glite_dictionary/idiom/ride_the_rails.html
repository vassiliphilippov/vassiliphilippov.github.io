to ride the rails

1. VERB

/ raɪd ðə reɪlz / (AmE), / raɪd ðə reɪlz / (BrE)
to travel by train, often over a long distance or in a leisurely or adventurous manner
travel by train, often long-distance

Examples

He dreamed of quitting his job to ride the rails across the continent.
During the Great Depression, many young men would ride the rails in search of work.
We decided to ride the rails through Europe for our vacation.

Synset Examples

The children were excited to ride the rails at the amusement park.
He loves to ride the rails across the country.
We decided to ride the rails for an adventurous weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: travel by train - to journey from one place to another using trains as transportation
