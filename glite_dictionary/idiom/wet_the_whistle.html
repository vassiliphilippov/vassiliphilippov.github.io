to wet the whistle

1. VERB

/ wɛt ðə ˈwɪsəl / (AmE), / wɛt ðə ˈwɪsəl / (BrE)
to have a drink, especially to quench one's thirst
to have a drink (idiom)

Examples

After the long hike, we stopped at a café to wet the whistle.
He ordered a glass of lemonade to wet the whistle before the meeting.
On a hot day, nothing feels better than to wet the whistle with a cold drink.

Synset Examples

He took a sip of water to wet his whistle.
A cold beer always helps to wet my whistle.
Let's stop for a drink to wet our whistle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: have a drink - to consume a beverage, typically alcohol

Etymology

Possibly from the use of 'whistle' as slang for the throat; first recorded in the 14th century.
