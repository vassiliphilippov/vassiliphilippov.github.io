to play ball

1. VERB

/ pleɪ bɔl / (AmE), / pleɪ bɔːl / (BrE)
to act cooperatively, especially by agreeing to work with others and following their rules or wishes
to cooperate with others

Examples

If you want the deal to go through, you'll need to play ball with the board of directors.
The negotiations won't move forward unless both parties agree to play ball.
She finally decided to play ball and cooperate with the investigation.

Synset Examples

If you want to join the team, you have to play ball.
The manager expects everyone to play ball during the project.
She refused to play ball with the new rules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cooperate - to work together toward a common end
  • Synonym: to collaborate - to work with another person or group to achieve something
  • Antonym: refuse - not to do what someone wants or asks

Etymology

Originally from American baseball, where playing ball refers to cooperating as part of a team.
