mother's little helper

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌðɚz ˌlɪt̬əl ˈhɛlpɚ / (AmE), / ˈmʌðəz ˌlɪtl̩ ˈhɛlpə / (BrE)
A humorous or euphemistic term for something that assists with everyday tasks, especially a tool, device, or a drug taken to ease daily life.
something that helps with daily tasks

Examples

He called his smartphone his mother's little helper because it kept him organized and on schedule.
After a long day, she joked that a cup of coffee was her mother's little helper.
Some parents refer to pain relief tablets as their mother's little helper during stressful times.

Synset Examples

He called his smartphone his mother's little helper for daily tasks.
She's always relied on that ointment as her mother's little helper.

Etymology

Popularized in the 1960s, notably by the Rolling Stones song of the same name; originally referring to tranquilizer drugs used by housewives.
