to win one for the Gipper

1. VERB

/ wɪn wʌn fɔr ðə ˈɡɪpər / (AmE), / wɪn wʌn fə ðə ˈɡɪpə / (BrE)
to achieve victory in honor of someone, especially as an act of tribute or encouragement
achieve victory in someone's honor
american

Examples

The coach urged the players to win one for the Gipper before the championship game.
The team dedicated their playoff victory to their injured captain, determined to win one for the Gipper.
Whenever morale is low, someone suggests they should win one for the Gipper and keep fighting.

Synset Examples

The coach inspired the team to win one for the Gipper.
They chanted to win one for the Gipper before the game.
He reminded them to win one for the Gipper during halftime.

Etymology

From a phrase popularized by American football player and coach Knute Rockne, referencing player George 'Gipper' Gipp.
