to swear blind

1. VERB

/ swɛr blaɪnd / (AmE), / swɛə blaɪnd / (BrE)
to state something very firmly, often insisting it is true, even if others doubt it
insist very firmly that something is true
british informal

Examples

I saw her leave the office, and I swear blind she wasn't carrying anything.
He will swear blind that he never received the email, even though we have proof.
You can swear blind all you like, but I don't believe your story.

Synset Examples

I swear blind that I saw him at the party.
She'll swear blind she never touched your book.
He swore blind he didn't break the vase.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to insist - to demand something forcefully, not accepting refusal
  • Antonym: to deny - to state that something is not true
