if my aunt had balls, she'd be my uncle

1. X

/ ɪf maɪ ænt hæd bɔlz, ʃid bi maɪ ˈʌŋkəl / (AmE), / ɪf maɪ ɑːnt hæd bɔːlz, ʃiːd bi maɪ ˈʌŋkl̩ / (BrE)
used humorously or sarcastically to indicate that discussing hypothetical situations is pointless because they are impossible or irrelevant
dismissal of irrelevant hypotheticals
informal

Examples

People kept saying things would be different if he had more time, but if my aunt had balls, she'd be my uncle.
Whenever someone mentions what could have happened, John just shrugs and says, if my aunt had balls, she'd be my uncle.
Debating all these unlikely scenarios is pointless—if my aunt had balls, she'd be my uncle!

Synset Examples

She said she would quit; if my aunt had balls, she'd be my uncle.
He wished he could play guitar; if my aunt had balls, she'd be my uncle.
She made excuses; if my aunt had balls, she'd be my uncle.
