to drop through the floor

1. VERB

/ drɑp θru ðə flɔr / (AmE), / drɒp θruː ðə flɔː / (BrE)
to decrease or collapse suddenly and dramatically, especially to a very low level
collapse or decrease suddenly

Examples

After the announcement, their sales dropped through the floor overnight.
When interest rates rose, the housing market dropped through the floor.
His motivation dropped through the floor after he failed the exam.

Synset Examples

Her excitement dropped through the floor when she heard the news.
His confidence fell through the floor after the criticism.
The prices went through the floor after the sale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fall through the floor - to decrease or collapse dramatically
