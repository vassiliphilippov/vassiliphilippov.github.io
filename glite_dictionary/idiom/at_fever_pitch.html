at fever pitch

1. ADV

/ ət ˈfiːvɚ pɪtʃ / (AmE), / ət ˈfiːvə pɪtʃ / (BrE)
at a very high level of excitement, intensity, or activity
at a high level of excitement or intensity

Examples

Tension in the stadium was at fever pitch as the final goal was scored.
By the end of the concert, the crowd's excitement was at fever pitch.
Rumors in the office reached at fever pitch just before the big announcement.

Synset Examples

Her enthusiasm for the project was at fever pitch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at a high pitch - at a high level of excitement or intensity

Etymology

From 'fever' meaning high temperature, and 'pitch' meaning a particular point or level, originally from sports and music contexts.
