to budge an inch

1. VERB

/ bʌdʒ ən ɪntʃ / (AmE), / bʌdʒ ən ɪntʃ / (BrE)
refuse to change one's opinion or position, even slightly
refuse to change position or opinion

Examples

No matter how much we argued, she wouldn't budge an inch.
Even after hours of negotiation, he refused to budge an inch.
The committee did not budge an inch from its original proposal.

Synset Examples

I tried to persuade him, but he wouldn’t budge an inch.
He wouldn’t budge an inch on his decision.
She didn’t budge an inch despite the pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give ground - to yield in an argument or position
  • Antonym: to stand firm - to refuse to change one's decision or opinion
