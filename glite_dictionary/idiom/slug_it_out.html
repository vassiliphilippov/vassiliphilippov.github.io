to slug it out

1. VERB

/ slʌɡ ɪt aʊt / (AmE), / slʌɡ ɪt aʊt / (BrE)
to fight or compete with great determination until a winner is decided
fight or compete intensely until the end

Examples

The two candidates will slug it out during the televised debate next week.
Rivals slugged it out on the court until the final whistle.
Despite being exhausted, they kept slugging it out until a winner emerged.

Synset Examples

They had to slug it out to see who would win.
We decided to slug it out until the last point.
The two teams will slug it out in the finals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fight it out - to fight until a result is reached
  • Synonym: battle - to compete or fight with great effort

Etymology

From 'slug' (to hit hard or fight) + 'it out' (to resolve by contest), first recorded in the early 20th century.
