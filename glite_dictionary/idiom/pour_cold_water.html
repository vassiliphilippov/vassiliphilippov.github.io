to pour cold water

1. VERB

/ pɔr koʊld ˈwɔtər / (AmE), / pɔː kəʊld ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
to discourage or dampen someone's enthusiasm or excitement about a plan or idea
discourage enthusiasm or excitement

Examples

His skeptical remarks really poured cold water on our plans for the trip.
I hate to pour cold water on your idea, but we don't have enough money.
Their criticism poured cold water over the team's excitement about the project.

Synset Examples

Her negative comments really poured cold water on my enthusiasm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to discourage - to cause someone to lose confidence or enthusiasm
  • Synonym: dampen enthusiasm - to make someone less excited or enthusiastic
