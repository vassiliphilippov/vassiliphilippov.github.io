to drag through the mud

1. VERB

/ dræɡ θru ðə mʌd / (AmE), / dræɡ θruː ðə mʌd / (BrE)
to publicly criticize someone and damage their reputation
publicly criticize; ruin reputation

Examples

Several newspapers tried to drag through the mud the politician after the scandal.
The company refused to drag through the mud its former CEO during the press conference.
He felt betrayed when his friends dragged through the mud his name in public.

Synset Examples

She badmouths her husband everywhere

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to badmouth - to speak unfavorably about someone
  • Synonym: slander - make false and damaging statements about someone
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00849849-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dræɡ θru ðə mʌd / (AmE), / dræɡ θruː ðə mʌd / (BrE)
to harm someone's reputation by subjecting them to severe criticism or making damaging accusations publicly
publicly damage someone's reputation

Examples

The opposition tried to drag through the mud the minister's reputation during the election campaign.
She was angry that the press would drag through the mud her personal life without any proof.
False accusations can drag through the mud even the most respected individuals.

Synset Examples

They tried to drag her reputation through the mud.
She felt like they were trying to drag her career through the mud.
The scandal dragged his name through the mud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slander - make false spoken statements damaging to a person's reputation
  • Synonym: to defame - damage the good reputation of someone; slander or libel
  • Antonym: praise - express warm approval or admiration, the opposite of criticize harshly
