to make a pig's ear of

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə pɪɡz ɪɹ ʌv / (AmE), / meɪk ə pɪɡz ɪə ɒv / (BrE)
to do something very badly or mess it up completely
do something very badly; make a mess
british informal

Examples

If you don't follow the instructions, you might make a pig's ear of the entire process.
Unfortunately, she made a pig's ear of the report and had to redo it from scratch.
He was nervous and made a pig's ear of his speech at the meeting.

Synset Examples

He made a pig's ear of the project by ignoring the guidelines.
She made a pig's ear of her attempt to cook dinner.
I really made a pig's ear of that presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botch - to carry out a task badly or carelessly
  • Synonym: bungle - to do something clumsily or badly
