to take a powder

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə ˈpaʊdɚ / (AmE), / teɪk ə ˈpaʊdə / (BrE)
to leave or disappear suddenly and without telling anyone, often to avoid something unpleasant
leave suddenly without telling anyone
informal

Examples

After being questioned by the police, he decided to take a powder before things got worse.
Whenever things got tough at work, Mark would just take a powder and not come back for hours.
His partner took a powder with all their money, leaving him to face the consequences alone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disappear - to cease to be visible or present
  • Synonym: to vanish - to disappear suddenly and completely

2. VERB

/ teɪk ə ˈpaʊdɚ / (AmE), / teɪk ə ˈpaʊdə / (BrE)
to leave quickly or discreetly, often to avoid trouble
leave quickly or discreetly
informal american

Examples

When the police showed up, he decided to take a powder before they started asking questions.
Realizing his mistake, she chose to take a powder rather than face everyone.
As soon as things got uncomfortable, Tom was the first to take a powder.

Synset Examples

After the argument, she chose to take a powder.
He decided to take a powder when things got tough.
They always take a powder when the work gets hard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: make a quick exit - to leave quickly
  • Synonym: to slip away - to leave unnoticed or quietly

Etymology

Originally American slang from the early 20th century; possibly from the idea of taking medicinal powder and leaving to recover.
