to go off the deep end

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ɑf ðə dip ɛnd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɒf ðə diːp end / (BrE)
to suddenly behave in an extremely emotional, irrational, or uncontrolled way
behave irrationally or extremely

Examples

When she heard the news, she went off the deep end and started shouting at everyone.
After months of stress at work, Mark finally went off the deep end and quit without notice.
Sometimes, when things don't go his way, he tends to go off the deep end.

Synset Examples

After that last incident, he really went off the deep end.
She seemed to go off the deep end during the argument.
He went off the deep end after losing his job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lose your temper - to suddenly become very angry
  • Antonym: keep your cool - to remain calm in a stressful situation
