large and in charge

1. ADJ

/ lɑrdʒ ənd ɪn tʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / lɑːdʒ ənd ɪn tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
having power, authority, and responsibility over others, especially in an assertive or commanding manner
having authority and responsibility
informal

Examples

Now that she's the manager, she's large and in charge of the entire department.
After winning the election, the mayor felt large and in charge at city hall.
He walked into the meeting looking large and in charge, ready to make important changes.

Synset Examples

With her new promotion, she's large and in charge now.
