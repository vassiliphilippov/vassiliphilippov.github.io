teach grandma how to suck eggs

1. X

/ titʃ ˈɡrændmɑ haʊ tʊ sʌk ɛɡz / (AmE), / tiːtʃ ˈɡrændmɑː haʊ tə sʌk ɛɡz / (BrE)
to give advice or instruction to someone who is already more experienced or knowledgeable about the subject than you are
advise someone on something they already know
british

Examples

There's no need to teach grandma how to suck eggs; she's been cooking for decades.
He felt that explaining basic math to the professor was like trying to teach grandma how to suck eggs.
By telling the master carpenter how to use his tools, you're just teaching grandma how to suck eggs.

Synset Examples

Don't teach grandma how to suck eggs; she knows best.
Explaining that to her is like teaching grandma how to suck eggs.
Telling him how to do his job is like teaching grandma how to suck eggs.

Etymology

From British English; refers to an old, practical skill associated with the elderly, used metaphorically for pointless instruction.
