money talks

1. X

/ ˈmʌni tɑks / (AmE), / ˈmʌni tɔːks / (BrE)
used to say that people with money have power and influence, or that money can persuade people to do things
idiom: money has power and influence

Examples

In business negotiations, money talks and often secures the best deals.
They didn't want to change the rules, but money talks—so the sponsor got what they wanted.
When it comes to politics, money talks louder than good intentions.

Synset Examples

In a dollar-speaking world, money talks louder than words.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cash is king - importance of cash over other forms of wealth
  • Synonym: to put your money where your mouth is - back up words with action or money
