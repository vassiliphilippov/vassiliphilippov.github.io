to lay eyes on

1. VERB

/ leɪ aɪz ɑn / (AmE), / leɪ aɪz ɒn / (BrE)
to see or notice someone or something, often for the first time or after a period of absence
to see or notice someone or something

Examples

I knew he loved the car the moment he laid eyes on it.
She had never laid eyes on such a beautiful painting before.
You can't take anything for granted until you lay eyes on it yourself.

Synset Examples

behold Christ!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to behold - to see or observe, especially something remarkable or impressive
  • Synonym: to catch sight of - to notice or see something briefly or unexpectedly
  • Antonym: overlook - to fail to notice or see something

2. VERB

/ leɪ aɪz ɑn / (AmE), / leɪ aɪz ɒn / (BrE)
to see someone or something for the first time or after a long time
to see for the first time or after a long time

Examples

The moment I laid eyes on the puppy, I knew I wanted to adopt it.
He hasn't laid eyes on his cousin since they were children.
She was thrilled when she finally laid eyes on the Grand Canyon.
