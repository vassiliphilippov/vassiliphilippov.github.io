to brought to nought

1. VERB

/ brɔt tə nɔt / (AmE), / brɔːt tə nɔːt / (BrE)
Rendered completely ineffective or nullified; reduced to nothing.
rendered ineffective or nullified
archaic

Examples

After months of negotiations, the merger was brought to nought by last-minute disagreements.
All their investments were brought to nought by the economic collapse.
Despite his determination, his efforts were brought to nought by unforeseen circumstances.

Synset Examples

Their efforts were ultimately brought to nought by the storm.
The team's hard work was brought to nought by poor planning.
All their plans were brought to nought by the sudden changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to come to nothing - to fail; to amount to nothing
  • Synonym: to nullify - to make legally null and void; to invalidate

Etymology

From archaic 'nought' meaning 'nothing'; 'brought to' indicates being reduced to that state.
