to take time by the forelock

1. VERB

/ teɪk taɪm baɪ ðə ˈfɔrlɑk / (AmE), / teɪk taɪm baɪ ðə ˈfɔːlɒk / (BrE)
to act quickly and decisively; to seize an opportunity before it is lost
act quickly; seize opportunity

Examples

If you want to succeed, you must take time by the forelock whenever an opportunity arises.
She knew this was her chance, so she took time by the forelock and applied immediately.
Instead of hesitating, they wisely took time by the forelock and launched the project before anyone else.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seize the day - to make the most of the present moment
  • Antonym: miss the boat - to fail to seize an opportunity

Etymology

From the classical allegory of Time as a character with a forelock, representing a chance that can be seized when approaching but not once it has passed.
