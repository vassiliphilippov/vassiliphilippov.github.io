no dice

1. X

/ noʊ daɪs / (AmE), / nəʊ daɪs / (BrE)
used to say that there is no chance of success or that something is not allowed or possible
no chance; not possible
informal american

Examples

I asked to borrow his car, but he said no dice.
They tried to get tickets for the concert, but no dice: it was sold out.
She hoped to leave work early, but the boss said no dice.

Synset Examples

I tried to negotiate a better deal, but it was no dice.
I asked if we could postpone; it was no dice.
He applied for the job, but there was no dice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: no way - used to say that something is not possible or not allowed
  • Synonym: not a chance - used to say that there is no possibility of something happening

Etymology

Originally American English, early 20th century, possibly from the use of dice in gambling where 'no dice' meant 'no win, no go'.
