to hold the phone

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ðə foʊn / (AmE), / həʊld ðə fəʊn / (BrE)
used to tell someone to wait or pause, especially to reconsider or allow something to be checked
tell someone to wait or pause
informal

Examples

Hold the phone, I think there's been a misunderstanding.
Before you make that decision, hold the phone and let me explain.
If you're leaving now, hold the phone—I have something important to tell you.

Synset Examples

Can you hold the phone while I find my notes?
Hold the phone, I need to check that information.
Just hold the phone; I have an idea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hang on - wait a short time
