to follow off a cliff

1. VERB

/ ˈfɑloʊ ɑf ə klɪf / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊ ɒf ə klɪf / (BrE)
to blindly follow someone or something without critical thought, often to one's detriment
blindly follow without thinking

Examples

Many investors were criticized for choosing to follow off a cliff during the market crash.
He tends to follow off a cliff whenever his friends make hasty decisions.
Teachers encourage students to question rather than follow off a cliff when it comes to trends.

Synset Examples

She warned him not to follow his friends off a cliff.
Don't just follow someone off a cliff; think for yourself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: follow blindly - to do what someone else does without question or thought
