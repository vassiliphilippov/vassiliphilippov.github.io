to dig one's own grave

1. VERB

/ dɪɡ wʌnz oʊn ɡreɪv / (AmE), / dɪɡ wʌnz əʊn ɡreɪv / (BrE)
to do something that causes one's own failure or downfall
cause one's own downfall

Examples

By ignoring the warning signs, she was digging her own grave at work.
He dug his own grave by repeatedly arriving late to important meetings.
If you keep making those mistakes, you'll end up digging your own grave.

Synset Examples

He didn't realize he was digging his own grave with that decision.
By lying to his boss, he really dug his own grave.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shoot oneself in the foot - to do something that unintentionally harms oneself
  • Synonym: seal one's fate - to take an action that determines an unavoidable bad outcome for oneself
  • Antonym: save one's own skin - to avoid harm to oneself, often by acting selfishly
