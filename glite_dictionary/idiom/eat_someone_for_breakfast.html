to eat (someone) for breakfast

1. VERB

/ it ʌs fɔr ˈbrɛkfəst / (AmE), / iːt ʌs fə ˈbrɛkfəst / (BrE)
to defeat or dominate someone very easily, often with little effort
defeat or dominate easily
informal

Examples

If we don't prepare for the debate, our opponents will eat us for breakfast.
They're a top law firm—they'd eat us for breakfast in court.
He said the new software would eat the competition for breakfast if it launches on time.

Synset Examples

That competitor will eat us for breakfast if we don’t innovate.
The coach warned that the rival team would eat us for breakfast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wipe the floor with - to defeat someone very easily
  • Synonym: to trounce - to defeat someone heavily or thoroughly
  • Antonym: struggle - to have difficulty in contest, the opposite of dominating easily
