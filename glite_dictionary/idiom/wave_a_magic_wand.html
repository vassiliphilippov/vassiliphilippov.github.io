to wave a magic wand

1. VERB

/ weɪv ə ˈmædʒɪk wɑnd / (AmE), / weɪv ə ˈmædʒɪk wɒnd / (BrE)
to make something happen as if by magic, effortlessly and instantly
make something happen magically or easily

Examples

If I could just wave a magic wand, I’d solve all our problems tomorrow.
Unfortunately, no one can wave a magic wand and fix the economy overnight.
She wanted to wave a magic wand to make her worries disappear.

Synset Examples

She wishes she could wave a magic wand for instant success.
