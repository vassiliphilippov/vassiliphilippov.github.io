to rest on one's laurels

1. VERB

/ rɛst ɑn wʌnz ˈlɔrəlz / (AmE), / rɛst ɒn wʌnz ˈlɔːrəlz / (BrE)
to be satisfied with what one has already achieved and stop putting in effort towards further success
stop striving after past achievements

Examples

After winning several awards, she refused to rest on her laurels and kept working hard.
If you rest on your laurels, you may soon be overtaken by more ambitious competitors.
He started resting on his laurels after getting promoted, which affected his performance.

Synset Examples

She refuses to rest on her laurels and continues to strive for excellence.
You can't just rest on your laurels; keep pushing forward.
Winning last year doesn't mean you can rest on your laurels.
He rested on his laurels after the big promotion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coast - to do something with little effort after previous success

Etymology

From the ancient practice of crowning victors with laurel wreaths in Greece and Rome, symbolizing achievement.
