home and dry

1. ADJ

/ hoʊm ənd draɪ / (AmE), / həʊm ənd draɪ / (BrE)
having safely achieved success after a challenge or difficulty; no longer at risk of failure
safe after a challenge; successful

Examples

After securing the contract, the team felt they were home and dry at last.
It took hours to fix the engine, but once it started, we were home and dry.
Once the judge announced the verdict in their favor, they were home and dry.

Synset Examples

Once we finished the project, we were home and dry.
With the last goal scored, they were home and dry.
After a long journey, we were finally home and dry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: safe and sound - free from danger or injury; unharmed
  • Synonym: out of the woods - no longer in danger or difficulty

Etymology

Originally a British idiom, based on the idea of reaching one's home safely and out of danger, especially after a hazardous journey.
