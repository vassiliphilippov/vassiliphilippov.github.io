to look as if she had been dragged through a hedge backwards

1. VERB

/ lʊk æz ɪf ʃi hæd bɪn dræɡd θru ə hɛdʒ ˈbækwɚdz / (AmE), / lʊk æz ɪf ʃi hæd bɪn drɑːɡd θruː ə hɛdʒ ˈbækwədz / (BrE)
(of a person) to appear extremely disheveled, messy, or untidy.
look very disheveled or untidy
british

Examples

After the storm, he looked as if he had been dragged through a hedge backwards in his rumpled clothes.
She looked as if she had been dragged through a hedge backwards after her all-night study session.
When the kids came in from playing outside, they looked as if they had been dragged through a hedge backwards.

Synset Examples
