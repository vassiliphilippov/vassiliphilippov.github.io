out of the hat

1. ADV

/ aʊt əv ðə hæt / (AmE), / aʊt əv ðə hæt / (BrE)
used to describe something that appears or happens suddenly or unexpectedly, often as a surprising solution or idea
suddenly or unexpectedly; as if by magic

Examples

He produced the missing document out of the hat just before the meeting ended.
The new proposal seemed to come out of the hat and took everyone by surprise.
Whenever there's a problem, she pulls a solution out of the hat at the last moment.

Synset Examples

He surprised everyone by pulling a great idea out of the hat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pull a rabbit out of the hat - suddenly produce a surprising solution or result

Etymology

From the conjuring trick of magically producing something (often a rabbit) from an apparently empty hat.
