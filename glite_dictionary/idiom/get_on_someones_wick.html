to get on someone's wick

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɑn ˈsʌmwʌnz wɪk / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒn ˈsʌmwənz wɪk / (BrE)
(informal, British) to annoy or irritate someone.
annoy or irritate (British informal)
informal british

Examples

His constant humming is beginning to get on my wick at work.
The kids' loud games really get on her wick when she's trying to read.
If you keep interrupting me, you're going to get on my wick fast.

Synset Examples

Her sarcasm is starting to get on my wick.
His attitude really gets on my wick.
That noise is getting on my wick.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to annoy - to make someone a little angry
  • Synonym: to irritate - to make someone slightly angry or impatient

Etymology

Rhyming slang: 'wick' from 'candlewick', rhyming with 'pick' (as in 'pick on', i.e., to annoy).
