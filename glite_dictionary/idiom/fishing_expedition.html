fishing expedition

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪʃɪŋ ˌɛkspəˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃɪŋ ˌɛkspɪˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
an investigation carried out in the hope of finding incriminating or useful information, often without specific evidence or scope
search for information without clear target

Examples

The lawyer accused the opposition of going on a fishing expedition during the investigation.
His questions seemed more like a fishing expedition than a genuine attempt to understand what happened.
The audit was criticized as nothing more than a costly fishing expedition.

Etymology

Formed from 'fishing', referring to angling or casting widely, and 'expedition', meaning a journey or undertaking.
