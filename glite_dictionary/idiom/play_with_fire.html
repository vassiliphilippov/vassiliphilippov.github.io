to play with fire

1. VERB

/ pleɪ wɪð ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / pleɪ wɪð ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
to take foolish risks; to engage in actions that are likely to cause harm or trouble
take foolish risks; act dangerously

Examples

If you cheat on your taxes, you're playing with fire and could get caught.
By ignoring the doctor's advice, she was playing with fire with her health.
Investing all your savings in one company is really playing with fire.

Synset Examples

Don’t play with fire by ignoring the warning signs.
If you skip safety precautions, you’re playing with fire.
He’s playing with fire by investing in such risky stocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take a risk - to do something that might have a bad result
  • Antonym: to play it safe - to avoid risks; to act cautiously
