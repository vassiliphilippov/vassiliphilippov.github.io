to act one's age

1. VERB

/ ækt wʌnz eɪdʒ / (AmE), / ækt wʌnz eɪdʒ / (BrE)
to behave in a way that is appropriate or typical for someone of your age, especially more maturely than you currently are
behave suitably for one's age

Examples

If you want people to take you seriously, you should act your age at work.
Her parents told her to act her age when she started arguing over toys.
He finally started to act his age and take responsibility for his decisions.

Synset Examples

She told him to act his age during the meeting.
He needs to act his age, not his shoe size.
You should really act your age at this party.
It's time to act your age, not your shoe size in public.
It's time to act your age and settle down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to grow up - to stop behaving in a childish way and become more mature
