to sow the seeds of change

1. VERB

/ soʊ ðə siːdz əv tʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / səʊ ðə siːdz əv tʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
to do something that begins a process or leads to change in the future
begin a process that leads to change

Examples

The new education program aims to sow the seeds of change in rural communities.
Her speech helped sow the seeds of change within the organization.
By supporting local leaders, the NGO hopes to sow the seeds of change for future generations.

Synset Examples

They hope to sow the seeds of change in the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pave the way - to make progress or development possible
  • Synonym: spark change - to cause change to start happening

Etymology

Figurative use of 'sow' (to plant seeds), drawing on agricultural language.
