birds of a feather flock together

1. X

/ ˈbɝdz əv ə ˈfɛðɚ ˈflɑk təˈɡɛðɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdz əv ə ˈfeðə ˈflɒk təˈɡeðə / (BrE)
People with similar interests, ideas, or characteristics tend to associate with one another.
people with similar traits associate together (idiom)

Examples

Whenever there's a group project, you'll see the same students working together—birds of a feather flock together.
She instantly became friends with the other artists, proving that birds of a feather flock together.
It's no surprise all the tech enthusiasts sit together at lunch; birds of a feather flock together.

Synset Examples

I guess it's true; birds of a feather flock together after all.
They always stick together; birds of a feather flock together.
It's true that birds of a feather flock together in this industry.

Etymology

Proverb recorded in English since the mid-16th century; likely based on observed bird behavior and earlier Latin proverbs.
