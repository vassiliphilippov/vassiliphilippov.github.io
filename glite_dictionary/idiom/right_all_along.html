right all along

1. X

/ raɪt ɔl əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / raɪt ɔːl əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to have been correct from the beginning, especially when others doubted or disagreed
correct from the beginning

Examples

Despite the doubts of his colleagues, John was right all along about the company's financial outlook.
She realized that her intuition had been right all along after the truth came out.
It turned out the scientists were right all along about the link between the two diseases.

Synset Examples

He was right all along about the project timeline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: right - correct or true
  • Antonym: wrong all along - incorrect from the beginning
