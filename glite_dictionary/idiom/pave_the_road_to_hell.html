to pave the road to hell

1. VERB

/ peɪv ðə roʊd tuː hɛl / (AmE), / peɪv ðə rəʊd tuː hɛl / (BrE)
to take actions that inadvertently lead to negative or disastrous outcomes, often as a result of negligence or misguided intentions
cause negative outcomes through misguided actions

Examples

Trying to cover up the mistake will only pave the road to hell for the company.
Some argue that strict regulations can pave the road to hell by creating even bigger problems.
Ignoring environmental concerns now may pave the road to hell for future generations.

Synset Examples

These shortcuts will pave the road to hell.
His negligence might pave the road to hell for the team.
Ignoring warnings will only pave the road to hell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the road to hell is paved with good intentions - proverb meaning well-intended actions can lead to bad outcomes

Etymology

Based on the proverb 'the road to hell is paved with good intentions,' from 17th-century English proverbial usage.
