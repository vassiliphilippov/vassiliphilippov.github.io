cobbler, keep to your last

1. X

/ ˈkɑblɚ kip tu jɚ læst / (AmE), / ˈkɒblə kiːp tuː jɔː lɑːst / (BrE)
used to advise someone to stick to what they know or not to meddle in matters outside their expertise
stick to what you know best

Examples

Whenever I am tempted to give legal advice, I remember the saying: cobbler, keep to your last.
Some politicians could benefit from the rule: cobbler, keep to your last.
He ignored the principle of cobbler, keep to your last and soon found himself out of his depth.

Synset Examples

As a writer, I remind myself to follow the adage: cobbler, keep to your last.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stick to your knitting - focus on your own area of knowledge or competence
  • Synonym: to mind your own business - not interfere in matters that do not concern you

Etymology

From the traditional proverb; a cobbler's 'last' is the tool for shaping shoes. The phrase warns against venturing beyond one's area of expertise.
