to fall between the cracks

1. VERB

/ fɔl bɪˈtwin ðə kræks / (AmE), / fɔːl bɪˈtwiːn ðə kræks / (BrE)
to be overlooked or neglected; to escape attention or not be properly dealt with
to be overlooked or neglected

Examples

Many children with mild learning disabilities fall between the cracks in the school system.
Some emails fell between the cracks during the transition to the new software.
He worries that his concerns will fall between the cracks and never be addressed.

Synset Examples

Her request fell between the cracks of the busy office.
Some important tasks fell through the cracks during the busy season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fall through the cracks - to be overlooked or forgotten
