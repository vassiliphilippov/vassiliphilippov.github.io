to flutter the dovecote

1. VERB

/ ˈflʌtər ðə ˈdoʊvˌkoʊt / (AmE), / ˈflʌtə ðə ˈdʌvkəʊt / (BrE)
to cause excitement, commotion, or disturbance, especially in a settled or peaceful environment
cause excitement or commotion

Examples

The new policy is sure to flutter the dovecote in the office.
Her candid remarks at the meeting managed to flutter the dovecote.
The surprise guest appearance really fluttered the dovecote at the convention.

Synset Examples

The announcement will flutter the dovecote among the employees.
Changes in management can flutter the dovecote significantly.
Her unexpected performance was enough to flutter the dovecote.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cause a stir - to cause excitement or controversy
  • Synonym: ruffle feathers - to cause annoyance or disturbance

Etymology

From the image of doves in a dovecote being disturbed and flying about in confusion.
