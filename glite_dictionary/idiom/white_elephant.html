white elephant

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈɛl.ə.fənt / (AmE), / waɪt ˈɛl.ɪ.fənt / (BrE)
A valuable possession that is difficult or expensive to maintain and is more trouble than it is worth.
costly, troublesome possession

Examples

The massive mansion turned out to be a white elephant that cost more to maintain than it was worth.
Owning a yacht may seem glamorous, but it can easily become a white elephant if maintenance costs spiral out of control.
The city's new sports arena proved to be a white elephant, rarely used yet extremely expensive to upkeep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: albatross - a burdensome or problematic responsibility

Etymology

From the legendary practice of Southeast Asian monarchs giving rare white elephants as royal gifts that became costly burdens to the recipient.

2. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈɛlɪfənt / (AmE), / waɪt ˈɛlɪfənt / (BrE)
an expensive and burdensome possession that is more trouble than it is worth
burdensome, costly possession

Examples

When the company built the factory in that location, it soon became a white elephant they couldn't sell.
The resort is a white elephant—beautiful, but it loses money every year.
Owning the classic car turned out to be a white elephant because of the constant maintenance costs.

Synset Examples

They finally sold the white elephant that was costing them money.
He inherited a white elephant from his grandmother.
The old building became a white elephant for the town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: albatross - a psychological burden; a source of persistent worry or concern
  • Antonym: asset - something useful or valuable

Etymology

From the tradition in Southeast Asia of rare white elephants being considered sacred but costly to maintain, and sometimes given as burdensome gifts.
