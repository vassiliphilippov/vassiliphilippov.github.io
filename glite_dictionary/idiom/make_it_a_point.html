to make it a point

1. VERB

/ meɪk ɪt ə pɔɪnt / (AmE), / meɪk ɪt ə pɔɪnt / (BrE)
to make a deliberate effort to do something; to ensure that something is done, especially as a matter of principle or habit
deliberately ensure something is done

Examples

I always make it a point to greet new team members on their first day.
She made it a point to call her grandmother every Sunday.
Despite his busy schedule, he makes it a point to exercise every morning.

Synset Examples

She made it a point to shake hands with the unemployed at the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make a point of - to deliberately do something regularly or purposefully
