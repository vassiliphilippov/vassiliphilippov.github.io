to bat for the other team

1. VERB

/ bæt fər ði ˈʌðɚ tim / (AmE), / bæt fə ði ˈʌðə tiːm / (BrE)
to support a rival group or, informally, to be gay or supportive of LGBTQ+ interests
support a rival group or be gay (informal)
informal slang

Examples

Many assumed he bat for the other team after he attended the parade with his boyfriend.
In the office politics, it was clear Sarah had decided to bat for the other team.
Some use the phrase 'to bat for the other team' when talking about someone’s sexuality.

Synset Examples

By supporting them, she seems to bat for the other team.
He feels like he’s batting for the other team at work.
Stop batting for the other team and focus on us.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: switch sides - to join the opposing group or team

Etymology

From baseball terminology; 'bat for' denotes playing on a team, extended to figurative use.
