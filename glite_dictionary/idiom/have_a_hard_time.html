to have a hard time

1. VERB

/ hæv ə hɑrd taɪm / (AmE), / hæv ə hɑːd taɪm / (BrE)
to experience difficulty or struggle in dealing with something
struggle to do something

Examples

Many students have a hard time concentrating during long lectures.
Children might have a hard time adjusting to a new school environment.
I used to have a hard time understanding this concept, but now it's much clearer.

Synset Examples

I always have a hard time waking up early.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: struggle - to try hard to do something difficult
  • Antonym: find easy - to not have difficulty with something
