no horse in this race

1. X

/ noʊ hɔrs ɪn ðɪs reɪs / (AmE), / nəʊ hɔːs ɪn ðɪs reɪs / (BrE)
Having no personal interest, involvement, or stake in the outcome of a particular situation or contest.
no personal stake in an outcome

Examples

I don't care which team wins the championship; I have no horse in this race.
Although the debate was heated, the moderator had no horse in this race and remained impartial.
It's easy for him to be objective, since he really has no horse in this race.

Synset Examples

I don’t care who wins; I have no horse in this race.
She expressed her opinion, but she has no horse in this race.
Since I don’t work for either company, I have no horse in this race.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: no skin in the game - to have no personal investment or stake in the outcome

Etymology

Metaphorical idiom based on horse racing, where having a horse in a race means direct investment or involvement.
