to see to it

1. VERB

/ siː tuː ɪt / (AmE), / siː tʊ ɪt / (BrE)
to make sure that something is done or happens; to ensure a particular result
make sure something is done

Examples

Please see to it that all the lights are turned off before you leave the building.
He promised to see to it that everyone was informed about the changes.
She always sees to it that her reports are submitted on time.

Synset Examples

He verified that the valves were closed
See that the curtains are closed
control the quality of the product

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ensure - to make certain that something will happen
  • Synonym: guarantee - to promise to do or ensure something
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to do something required

2. VERB

/ si tu ɪt / (AmE), / siː tʊ ɪt / (BrE)
to make sure that something is done or takes place as expected or required.
ensure something is done

Examples

Please see to it that all the lights are turned off before you leave.
The supervisor will see to it that the project is completed on time.
I'll see to it that your message gets delivered to the manager.

Synset Examples

Please see to it that the report is finished.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make sure - to ensure something is done or certain
  • Synonym: to ensure - to make certain that something will happen
  • Antonym: neglect - fail to give proper attention to something
