ten-dollar word

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛn ˌdɑlər wɝd / (AmE), / ˈtɛn ˌdɒlə wɜːd / (BrE)
a long or complex word used unnecessarily, often to sound impressive or pretentious
an unnecessarily complex word

Examples

He tried to impress everyone with a ten-dollar word, but it only made his point unclear.
The professor warned students against using ten-dollar words instead of plain language.
I wish people would stop throwing around ten-dollar words in casual conversations.

Synset Examples

He always uses a ten-dollar word when a simple one will do.
Her essay was filled with ten-dollar words that confused the reader.
Avoiding ten-dollar words keeps your writing clear and engaging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fancy word - a word that is elaborate or sophisticated, often unnecessarily so
  • Antonym: simple word - a word that is short and straightforward

Etymology

From the notion that using big words makes speech sound more valuable, as if the word itself is worth ten dollars.
