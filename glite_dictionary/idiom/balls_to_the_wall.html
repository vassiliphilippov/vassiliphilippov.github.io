balls to the wall

1. ADV

/ bɔlz tə ðə wɔl / (AmE), / bɔːlz tə ðə wɔːl / (BrE)
with maximum effort, speed, or intensity; without restraint
with maximum effort or intensity
informal slang american

Examples

During the last mile of the race, she ran balls to the wall to catch up with the leader.
We're going balls to the wall on this project to meet our launch date.
If you want to win the championship, you have to play balls to the wall from start to finish.

Synset Examples

We went balls to the wall in the final sprint.
He worked balls to the wall to meet the deadline.
She always gives her all, going balls to the wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flat out - as fast or as hard as possible

Etymology

Originally U.S. Air Force slang, referring to pushing throttle levers (with ball-shaped grips) fully forward in aircraft.
