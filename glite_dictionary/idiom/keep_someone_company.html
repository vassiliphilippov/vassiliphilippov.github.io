to keep someone company

1. VERB

/ kip ˈsʌmwʌn ˈkʌmpəni / (AmE), / kiːp ˈsʌmwʌn ˈkʌmpəni / (BrE)
to stay with someone so that they are not alone, especially to make them feel more comfortable or less lonely
stay with someone so they are not alone

Examples

Would you like me to keep you company while you wait for your appointment?
He brought his dog along to keep him company during the long, quiet evenings.
After her surgery, her friends took turns coming over to keep her company.

Synset Examples

She offered to keep him company during lunch.
