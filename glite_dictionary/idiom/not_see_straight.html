to not see straight

1. VERB

/ nɑt siː streɪt / (AmE), / nɒt siː streɪt / (BrE)
to be unable to think clearly, often because of exhaustion, anger, or confusion
be unable to think clearly

Examples

After staying up all night to finish the project, I could not see straight by morning.
He was so furious that he could not see straight during the meeting.
By the end of the marathon, she was so exhausted she could hardly not see straight.

Synset Examples

After the long drive, I could hardly not see straight.
