in point of fact

1. ADV

/ ɪn pɔɪnt əv fækt / (AmE), / ɪn pɔɪnt əv fakt / (BrE)
used to emphasize the truth or reality of a statement, often when it contrasts with what was previously said or assumed
actually; in reality

Examples

In point of fact, the problem was more complicated than we initially thought.
She seemed angry, but in point of fact she was just tired after a long day.
While many assumed the project was a failure, in point of fact it exceeded expectations.

Synset Examples

in fact, it was a wonder anyone survived
painters who are in fact anything but unsophisticated
as a matter of fact, he is several inches taller than his father

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in fact - actually; in reality
  • Synonym: as a matter of fact - used to emphasize truth, especially when correcting or adding information
