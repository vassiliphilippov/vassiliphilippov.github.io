to keep it in the family

1. VERB

/ kip ɪt ɪn ðə ˈfæməli / (AmE), / kiːp ɪt ɪn ðə ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
to keep important matters, problems, or information private within a family or a closely related group, rather than sharing them with outsiders
keep matters private among relatives

Examples

They wanted to keep it in the family, so they didn't tell anyone about their financial troubles.
The family recipe has always been kept in the family for generations.
When disputes arise, her parents prefer keeping it in the family rather than involving outsiders.

Synset Examples

We prefer to keep it in the family for privacy.
They decided to keep it in the family business.
It's best to keep it in the family when sharing secrets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keep under wraps - to keep something secret or hidden
  • Antonym: air one's dirty laundry - to discuss private or embarrassing family matters in public
