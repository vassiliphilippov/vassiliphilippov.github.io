baby needs new shoes

1. X

/ ˈbeɪbi nidz nu ˈʃuz / (AmE), / ˈbeɪbi niːdz njuː ˈʃuːz / (BrE)
a humorous expression used to justify spending money or expressing the need for new things, especially as an excuse to buy something
humorous excuse for spending money
informal

Examples

Before buying a laptop he joked, 'baby needs new shoes!' to justify the purchase.
She playfully whispered 'baby needs new shoes' while shopping for handbags with her friends.
Whenever he wins a little money at poker, he yells, 'baby needs new shoes!'

Synset Examples

Whenever I want to splurge, I remind myself, 'baby needs new shoes'.
Every time he complains about expenses, I say, 'baby needs new shoes'.
She laughed and said, 'Yes, I know, baby needs new shoes!' when buying new clothes.
