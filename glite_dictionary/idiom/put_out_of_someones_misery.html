to put out of someone's misery

1. VERB

/ pʊt aʊt əv sʌmwʌnz ˈmɪzəri / (AmE), / pʊt aʊt ɒv sʌmwʌnz ˈmɪzəri / (BrE)
to end someone's suffering or distress, either by providing relief or, sometimes, by euthanasia
end someone's suffering or distress

Examples

The vet decided to put out of its misery the dog that had been suffering for days.
Just put me out of my misery and tell me the results already.
After months of uncertainty, his boss finally put him out of his misery and gave him an answer.

Synset Examples

He finally put her out of her misery with the honest truth.
The long wait was unbearable, so we put him out of his misery.
We decided to put the sick pet out of its misery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ease someone's pain - to lessen or relieve someone's pain
  • Antonym: prolong someone's suffering - to cause someone's suffering to continue
