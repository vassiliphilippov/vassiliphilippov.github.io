to give (someone) the sack

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ðə sæk / (AmE), / ɡɪv ðə sæk / (BrE)
to dismiss someone from their job; to fire someone
dismiss from a job
british

Examples

If you keep coming in late, they might give you the sack.
After the scandal, the board decided to give her the sack.
Many workers were worried management would give them the sack during the cuts.

Synset Examples

After the poor performance, the manager decided to give him the sack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire - to dismiss from a job
  • Synonym: to give (someone) the boot - idiom: to dismiss someone from a job
  • Antonym: hire - to employ or take on staff

Etymology

From the practice of giving dismissed workers a sack to carry their belongings.
