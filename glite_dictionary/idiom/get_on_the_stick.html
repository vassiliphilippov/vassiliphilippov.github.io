to get on the stick

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɑn ðə stɪk / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒn ðə stɪk / (BrE)
to begin taking action or start working diligently, especially after a period of delay or inactivity
start working hard; take action quickly
informal american

Examples

If you want to pass your exams, you need to get on the stick and start studying seriously.
We should get on the stick if we want to finish the project before the holidays.
After weeks of procrastination, she finally decided to get on the stick and clean her apartment.

Synset Examples

If we want to meet the deadline, we must get on the stick now.
You need to get on the stick with that project.
He finally decided to get on the stick and finish his report.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get cracking - start doing something quickly and energetically
  • Synonym: to get going - start moving or working; take action

Etymology

American English idiom, early 20th century; possibly from baseball, where 'getting on the stick' meant picking up the bat to hit.
