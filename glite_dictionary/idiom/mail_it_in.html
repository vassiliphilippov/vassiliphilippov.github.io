to mail it in

1. VERB

/ meɪl ɪt ɪn / (AmE), / meɪl ɪt ɪn / (BrE)
to do something with minimal effort or enthusiasm, especially when more is expected
do something with minimal effort
informal american

Examples

During the last week of school, many students just mail it in rather than study for exams.
Critics said the actor mailed it in during the sequel, lacking his usual passion.
It's clear from his attitude that he's mailing it in at his job these days.

Synset Examples

He decided to mail it in instead of giving his best effort.
She felt tempted to mail it in on the last project.
Don't just mail it in; put in some real effort!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phone it in - to perform a duty or task in a perfunctory or indifferent manner

Etymology

Based on the literal act of sending in work by mail, implying lack of personal involvement or effort.
