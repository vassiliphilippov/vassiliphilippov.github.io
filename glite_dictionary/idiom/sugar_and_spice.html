sugar and spice

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃʊɡɚ ənd spaɪs / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡə ənd spaɪs / (BrE)
used to describe someone or something that is very pleasant, charming, or delightful, often in a sweet or endearing way
pleasant and charming qualities

Examples

Her new puppy is all sugar and spice, always playful and affectionate.
The teacher's sugar and spice demeanor puts everyone at ease in the classroom.
With her sugar and spice attitude, she quickly becomes the favorite among her friends.

Synset Examples

She's filled with sugar and spice, making her fun to be around.
That dress is sugar and spice, perfect for the party.
Her personality is sugar and spice, always cheerful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet - having a pleasant or charming quality (personality)

Etymology

From the nursery rhyme "What are little girls made of? Sugar and spice and everything nice."
