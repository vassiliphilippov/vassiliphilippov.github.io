to keep the home fires burning

1. VERB

/ kip ðə hoʊm faɪɚz ˈbɝːnɪŋ / (AmE), / kiːp ðə həʊm faɪəz ˈbɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
to maintain a warm, caring, and welcoming atmosphere at home, especially during someone's absence
maintain a welcoming home atmosphere

Examples

While her partner was deployed overseas, she did her best to keep the home fires burning.
He cooked, cleaned, and kept the kids happy to keep the home fires burning during her long business trip.
Even in tough times, families try to keep the home fires burning for each other.

Synset Examples

He takes care of the kids to keep the home fires burning.
They need to keep the home fires burning while he’s away.
She works from home to keep the home fires burning.

Etymology

Popularized during World War I by the British song 'Keep the Home Fires Burning' (1914).
