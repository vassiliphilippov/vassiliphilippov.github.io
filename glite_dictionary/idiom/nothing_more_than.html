nothing more than

1. X

/ ˈnʌθɪŋ mɔr ðæn / (AmE), / ˈnʌθɪŋ mɔː ðæn / (BrE)
used to emphasize that something is only what is stated and not better, more important, or different
only; just; merely

Examples

The rumors turned out to be nothing more than exaggerated stories.
His offer was nothing more than a polite gesture, not a genuine invitation.
Their disagreement was nothing more than a minor misunderstanding.

Synset Examples

It was nothing more than a misunderstanding between friends.
The error was nothing more than a typo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: only - no more than; nothing except
  • Antonym: more than - to a greater extent than; exceeding
