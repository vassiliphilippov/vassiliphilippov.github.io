to get one's panties in a bunch

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt wʌnz ˈpæntiz ɪn ə bʌntʃ / (AmE), / ɡɛt wʌnz ˈpæntiːz ɪn ə bʌntʃ / (BrE)
to become overly upset, agitated, or annoyed, especially about something trivial or unimportant
become overly upset or agitated
informal

Examples

Don’t get your panties in a bunch; it’s only a minor mistake.
She often gets her panties in a bunch when things don’t go as planned.
There’s no reason to get your panties in a bunch over a simple question.

Synset Examples

He tends to get his panties in a bunch over trivial matters.
Just relax, no need to get your panties in a bunch.
Don’t get your panties in a bunch over this.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get worked up - to become upset or excited, often unnecessarily
  • Synonym: to overreact - respond more emotionally or forcibly than is justified
