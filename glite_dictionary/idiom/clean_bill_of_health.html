clean bill of health

1. NOUN

/ klin bɪl əv hɛlθ / (AmE), / kliːn bɪl əv hɛlθ / (BrE)
an official statement that someone is healthy or that something is in satisfactory condition
statement confirming health or good condition

Examples

After the annual inspection, the restaurant received a clean bill of health from the health department.
The doctor gave her a clean bill of health following her surgery.
The engineer examined the bridge and issued a clean bill of health for continued use.

Synset Examples

the doctor gave him a clean bill of health

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bill of health - official certificate stating health status, especially of a ship

Etymology

Originally from the Nautical and medical practice in the 18th century of providing a written statement that a ship’s crew were free from contagious disease.

2. NOUN

/ klin bɪl əv hɛlθ / (AmE), / kliːn bɪl əv hɛlθ / (BrE)
an official statement that a person, organization, or thing is in good health or free from faults or defects
confirmation of good health or condition

Examples

After months of worry, he was relieved to receive a clean bill of health from his doctor.
The inspector gave the restaurant a clean bill of health after the hygiene check.
The audit concluded with a clean bill of health for the organization's financial records.

Synset Examples

After the tests, he received a clean bill of health.
The doctor gave her a clean bill of health.
Her clean bill of health was a relief to the family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clean bill - a statement confirming everything is in good order, often used in inspection contexts

Etymology

From maritime practice where a 'bill of health' certified that a ship's crew was free from disease.
