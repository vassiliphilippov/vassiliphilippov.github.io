full of tripe

1. ADJ

/ fʊl əv traɪp / (AmE), / fʊl əv traɪp / (BrE)
containing mostly nonsense or worthless material; lacking real value or meaning
filled with nonsense or rubbish

Examples

His explanation was full of tripe and convinced no one.
The article was criticized for being full of tripe and lacking substance.
People were annoyed because the report was simply full of tripe from start to finish.

Synset Examples

The movie was full of tripe that bored the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - language or behavior that is silly or has no meaning
  • Synonym: rubbish - things that are untrue or worthless, especially statements or ideas

Etymology

The word 'tripe' has long been used metaphorically in British English to mean 'nonsense', originally denoting the edible lining of a cow's stomach.
