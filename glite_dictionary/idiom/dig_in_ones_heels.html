to dig in one's heels

1. VERB

/ dɪɡ ɪn wʌnz hilz / (AmE), / dɪɡ ɪn wʌnz hiːlz / (BrE)
to refuse to change one's opinion or decision, especially by showing increased determination
refuse to change position or opinion

Examples

No matter how much they pressured her, she continued to dig in her heels and refused to cooperate.
When negotiations stalled, both sides began to dig in their heels instead of seeking compromise.
He will probably dig in his heels if we try to make him change his plans.

Synset Examples

She decided to dig in her heels against the new policy.
She will dig her heels in if they try to change her mind.
They dug in their heels during the negotiations.
He tends to dig his heels in during discussions.
When challenged, he dug in his heels and refused to budge.
They dug their heels in, refusing to accept the compromise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stand one's ground - to refuse to change one's position or opinion despite opposition
  • Antonym: to give in - to stop resisting; to yield to pressure
