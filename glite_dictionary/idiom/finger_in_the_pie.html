finger in the pie

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪŋɡɚ ɪn ðə paɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡə ɪn ðə paɪ / (BrE)
to be involved in or have influence over something, often one of several people participating in an activity or project
to have involvement or influence in something

Examples

When it comes to major decisions, he always wants to have a finger in the pie.
Several investors had a finger in the pie during the company's early days.
She tried to keep her finger in the pie despite her official retirement.

Synset Examples

He always wants a finger in the pie when it comes to decisions.
With his finger in the pie, he influenced the project direction.
She had her finger in the pie of every major project.
