to cry uncle

1. VERB

/ kraɪ ˈʌŋkəl / (AmE), / kraɪ ˈʌŋkəl / (BrE)
to admit defeat or surrender, especially when pressured or after a struggle
admit defeat; surrender (idiom)
american informal

Examples

He held his opponent in a headlock until he finally cried uncle.
Despite the mounting pressure, she refused to cry uncle.
After three rounds of chess, I had to cry uncle and admit defeat.

Synset Examples

The kids had to cry uncle during the game.
He refused to cry uncle, even when he was losing.
After hours of negotiation, he finally cried uncle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give in - to admit defeat; yield under pressure
  • Synonym: surrender - to stop resisting and admit defeat
  • Antonym: to stand firm - to refuse to give in or surrender
