in the arms of Murphy

1. X

/ ɪn ðə ɑrmz əv ˈmɜrfi / (AmE), / ɪn ði ˈɑːmz əv ˈmɜːfi / (BrE)
in a situation where a series of things go wrong, often due to bad luck, as described by Murphy's Law
experiencing a string of bad luck

Examples

After missing my train and spilling coffee on my shirt, I knew I was in the arms of Murphy.
No matter how much I planned, the event was a disaster; I truly was in the arms of Murphy.
She joked that she must be in the arms of Murphy every Monday morning.

Synset Examples

Everything went wrong; I was definitely in the arms of Murphy today.
When the project failed, I felt like I was in the arms of Murphy.
He realized he was in the arms of Murphy when his car broke down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Murphy's Law - the adage that anything that can go wrong will go wrong

Etymology

Reference to Murphy's Law ('Anything that can go wrong, will go wrong'), popularized in the mid-20th century.
