to make a killing

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə ˈkɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / meɪk ə ˈkɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
to earn a large amount of money or profit very quickly and easily
earn a large profit quickly

Examples

He managed to make a killing selling rare sneakers online.
Many investors hoped to make a killing during the tech boom.
If the business goes well, we could make a killing this year.

Synset Examples

The new restaurant made a killing during the holidays.
He made a killing in the stock market last year.
Investors hope to make a killing on this trend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strike it rich - to suddenly become very wealthy
