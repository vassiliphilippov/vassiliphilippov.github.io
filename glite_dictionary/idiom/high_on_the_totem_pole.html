high on the totem pole

1. ADJ

/ haɪ ɑn ðə ˈtoʊtəm poʊl / (AmE), / haɪ ɒn ðə ˈtəʊtəm pəʊl / (BrE)
having a position of power, status, or importance, especially within an organization or hierarchy
in a position of power or status
informal

Examples

Even though he just started, he already feels high on the totem pole at his new job.
As project leader, Maria is high on the totem pole and makes important decisions.
Those high on the totem pole often receive extra perks and recognition.

Synset Examples

She’s the one high on the totem pole in that company.
Being high on the totem pole has its perks and responsibilities.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: low on the totem pole - in a position of low status or importance

Etymology

From the notion of positions on Native American totem poles, though the phrase's metaphorical use reflects status rather than literal arrangement.
