to poke the bear

1. VERB

/ poʊk ðə bɛɚ / (AmE), / pəʊk ðə beə / (BrE)
to deliberately provoke someone or something dangerous or volatile, especially when wiser to leave it alone
to provoke a dangerous or unstable person or situation

Examples

It's wise not to poke the bear when the boss is already upset.
He loves to poke the bear by making controversial comments online.
If you keep asking those questions, you're just going to poke the bear.

Synset Examples

Sometimes it's best not to poke the bear at work.
Don't poke the bear; he’s already in a bad mood.
She knew better than to poke the bear with her comments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to provoke - to stimulate or incite someone to do or feel something
  • Synonym: to antagonize - to cause someone to become hostile
