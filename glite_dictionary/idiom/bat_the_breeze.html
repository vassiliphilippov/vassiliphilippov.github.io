to bat the breeze

1. VERB

/ bæt ðə briːz / (AmE), / bæt ðə briːz / (BrE)
to have a casual conversation; to chat informally
chat informally; have a casual conversation
informal

Examples

We sat on the porch and bat the breeze for hours.
Whenever Sarah visits, we end up batting the breeze over tea.
After the meeting, they batted the breeze outside the office.

Synset Examples

Let's bat the breeze about last night's game.
They love to bat the breeze over coffee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shoot the breeze - to chat or talk idly; to engage in casual conversation
