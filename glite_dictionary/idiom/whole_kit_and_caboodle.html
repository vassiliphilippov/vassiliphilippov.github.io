whole kit and caboodle

1. NOUN

/ hoʊl kɪt ənd kəˈbudl̩ / (AmE), / həʊl kɪt ənd kəˈbuːdəl / (BrE)
the whole of something; everything involved or available
everything; the entire lot
informal american

Examples

She packed up the whole kit and caboodle and moved to the country.
If you're interested, you can buy the whole kit and caboodle for a discounted price.
After the party, we had to clean the whole kit and caboodle ourselves.

Synset Examples

we saw the whole shebang
a hotdog with the works
we took on the whole caboodle
for $10 you get the full treatment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whole shebang - the whole thing; everything
  • Synonym: the works - everything included; all items or options available

Etymology

Formed from 'kit' (a collection of items) and 'caboodle' (possibly from 'boodle', meaning a collection or lot); commonly used in American English since the 19th century.
