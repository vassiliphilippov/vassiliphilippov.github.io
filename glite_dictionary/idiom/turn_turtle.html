to turn turtle

1. VERB

/ tɝn ˈtɝtəl / (AmE), / tɜːn ˈtɜːtəl / (BrE)
(of a boat, vehicle, etc.) to overturn accidentally so that the bottom is uppermost.
overturn accidentally (of a boat or vehicle)

Examples

During the storm, the small sailboat turned turtle and everyone was thrown into the water.
The car skidded on the icy road and turned turtle in the ditch.
If you don't maintain your balance, the kayak might turn turtle unexpectedly.

Synset Examples

Don't rock the boat or it will capsize!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to capsize - to overturn, especially a boat

2. VERB

/ tɝn ˈtɝtəl / (AmE), / tɜːn ˈtɜːtəl / (BrE)
to capsize or turn upside down, especially said of boats, vehicles, or other objects
to capsize or flip over

Examples

The small sailboat began to turn turtle when the wind suddenly changed direction.
The old truck skidded on the ice and turned turtle in the ditch.
The raft was overloaded and threatened to turn turtle during the storm.

Synset Examples

When it hit the waves, it started to turn turtle.
The boat began to turn turtle in the rough seas.
The car turned turtle after hitting the barrier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to capsize - to overturn in the water, especially of a boat

Etymology

From the image of a turtle turning upside down onto its shell, rendering it immobile.
