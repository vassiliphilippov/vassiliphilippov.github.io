to shut one's mouth

1. VERB

/ ʃʌt wʌnz maʊθ / (AmE), / ʃʌt wʌnz maʊθ / (BrE)
to refrain from talking, especially to keep quiet about something confidential or secret
keep quiet, especially about secrets

Examples

He knew it was best to shut his mouth about the surprise party.
If you can't shut your mouth, you'll ruin the whole plan.
She made sure to shut her mouth when her boss asked about the confidential deal.

Synset Examples

Don't tell him any secrets — he cannot keep his mouth shut!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keep one's mouth shut - to remain silent or not reveal information
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00939649-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ʃʌt wʌnz maʊθ / (AmE), / ʃʌt wʌnz maʊθ / (BrE)
to stop talking or to remain silent, often used as an imperative to tell someone to be quiet
stop talking or remain silent

Examples

He managed to shut his mouth even when the others accused him.
If you don’t have anything nice to say, just shut your mouth.
She had to remind herself to shut her mouth during the heated meeting.

Synset Examples

He did well to shut his mouth during the argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to be quiet - to stop talking or making noise
  • Synonym: to keep one's mouth shut - to deliberately remain silent
