to lit up a durry

1. VERB

/ lɪt ʌp ə ˈdɜːri / (AmE), / lɪt ʌp ə ˈdʌri / (BrE)
smoked a cigarette; to light and begin smoking a cigarette (informal, chiefly Australian English)
smoked a cigarette (Australian slang)
informal slang

Examples

After the meeting, he lit up a durry and leaned against the wall.
She lit up a durry outside the café, trying to relax.
They always lit up a durry together after work to unwind.

Synset Examples

He lit up a durry after a long day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smoke a cigarette - to inhale and exhale the smoke of a cigarette

Etymology

‘Durry’ is Australian slang for cigarette, possibly from British dialect 'durry' (short coarse tobacco).
