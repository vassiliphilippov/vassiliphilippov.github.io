to throw away the key

1. VERB

/ θroʊ əˈweɪ ðə ki / (AmE), / θrəʊ əˈweɪ ðə kiː / (BrE)
to imprison someone for life or an indefinite period, with no hope of release or rehabilitation
imprison indefinitely, no hope of release

Examples

Many believe that dangerous criminals should be locked up and throw away the key.
The judge decided to throw away the key after hearing the details of the case.
When it comes to repeat offenders, some argue we should just throw away the key.

Synset Examples

When he lied, I wanted to throw away the key on trust.
After his crime, they wanted to throw away the key.
She felt like they should throw away the key on his behavior.
Many thought he deserved to be locked up and thrown away the key.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lock up and throw away the key - to imprison and make escape impossible
