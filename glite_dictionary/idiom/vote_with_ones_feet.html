to vote with one's feet

1. VERB

/ voʊt wɪð wʌnz fit / (AmE), / vəʊt wɪð wʌnz fiːt / (BrE)
to show disapproval or preference for something by leaving or choosing to go elsewhere, rather than by expressing an opinion formally
show approval/disapproval by leaving

Examples

If the new policy is unpopular, employees may vote with their feet and seek jobs elsewhere.
When rent went up, many tenants decided to vote with their feet by moving out.
If students are unhappy with the cafeteria, they can vote with their feet and eat elsewhere.

Synset Examples

If the service doesn't improve, we'll vote with our feet.
People will vote with their feet if prices keep rising.
Many chose to vote with their feet and leave the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boycott - to refuse to take part in something as a way of protesting
  • Antonym: support - to agree with or approve of someone or something
