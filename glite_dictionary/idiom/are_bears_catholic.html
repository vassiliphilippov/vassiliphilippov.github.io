Are bears Catholic?

1. X

/ ɑɹ bɛɹz kəˈθolɪk / (AmE), / ɑː bɛəz kəˈθɒlɪk / (BrE)
A sarcastic rhetorical question used to imply that the answer to a previous question is obviously 'yes'.
sarcastic rhetorical question (obviously yes)
informal

Examples

Whenever someone asks a painfully obvious question, she replies, 'Are bears Catholic?' and rolls her eyes.
He asked if she wanted coffee in the morning, so she shot back, 'Are bears Catholic?'
When Tom wondered if the meeting would take long, Mark retorted, 'Are bears Catholic?' to make his point.

Synset Examples

His question about dessert was met with a sarcastic, 'Are bears Catholic?'
When he asked if she liked the new dress, she replied, 'Are bears Catholic?'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Does a bear shit in the woods? - sarcastic rhetorical question implying the answer is obviously yes
