to get you anywhere

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ju ˈɛniˌwɛr / (AmE), / ɡɛt juː ˈeniweə / (BrE)
to achieve any success or make any progress, especially when implying that effort or behavior is ineffective or futile
achieve success or progress

Examples

Hard work alone may not get you anywhere without the right opportunities.
Bragging about your achievements won't get you anywhere with the team.
She realized that arguing with her boss wouldn't get her anywhere.

Synset Examples

Being a dym won't get you anywhere in this job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get ahead - to achieve success or progress, especially in one's career
  • Antonym: get nowhere - fail to make progress or achieve success
