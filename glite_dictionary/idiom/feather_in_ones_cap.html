feather in one's cap

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛðər ɪn wʌnz kæp / (AmE), / ˈfɛðə ɪn wʌnz kap / (BrE)
an achievement or honour that one can be proud of
a proud achievement or honour

Examples

Winning the national science competition was a real feather in her cap.
Publishing his first novel was definitely a feather in his cap.
Securing that big client was another feather in the company's cap.

Synset Examples

Completing the project on time was a feather in her cap.
Getting published is a significant feather in his cap.
Winning the award was a real feather in his cap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: achievement - something accomplished successfully
  • Synonym: distinction - an honour or special recognition

Etymology

Based on the old custom of adding a feather to one's cap to signify a special achievement or honour.
