you're the boss

1. X

/ jʊr ðə bɑs / (AmE), / jɔː ðə bɒs / (BrE)
used to acknowledge that someone has the authority to make decisions or give orders
used to indicate someone is in charge
informal

Examples

If you want to leave early, you're the boss.
"No onions on my burger," he said. "You're the boss," replied the cook.
We can try your suggestion—you're the boss after all.

Synset Examples

If you think this is the best approach, you're the boss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in charge - having control or responsibility for something
