to storm out of the blocks

1. VERB

/ stɔrm aʊt əv ðə blɑks / (AmE), / stɔːm aʊt əv ðə blɒks / (BrE)
to start a race or activity very quickly or energetically
start very quickly or energetically

Examples

The young sprinter stormed out of the blocks and quickly distanced himself from the rest of the field.
Don't be surprised if they storm out of the blocks in the first quarter to set the tempo.
Our marketing team plans to storm out of the blocks with an aggressive new campaign in January.

Synset Examples

He always storms out of the blocks in the marathon.
She stormed out of the blocks and took the lead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burst out of the blocks - to start very quickly at the beginning of a race or activity
