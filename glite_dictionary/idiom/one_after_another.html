one after another

1. ADV

/ wʌn ˈæftər əˈnʌðər / (AmE), / wʌn ˈɑːftə əˈnʌðə / (BrE)
happening or appearing in succession, with each following the previous one immediately or soon after
in succession; consecutively

Examples

The fire alarms went off one after another during the drill.
He answered the questions one after another without hesitation.
Books toppled from the shelf one after another as the earthquake hit.

Synset Examples

The kids came in one after another for snacks.
She knocked on the doors one after another.
The cars passed by one after another on the highway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in succession - happening one after the other; consecutively
  • Synonym: consecutively - following one after another without interruption
  • Antonym: simultaneously - happening at the same time
