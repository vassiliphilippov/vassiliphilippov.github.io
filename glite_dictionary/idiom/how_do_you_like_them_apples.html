how do you like them apples

1. X

/ haʊ duː juː laɪk ðɛm ˈæp.əlz / (AmE), / haʊ djuː juː laɪk ðəm ˈap.lz / (BrE)
(informal) Used to draw attention to one's own or someone else's achievement, often to express triumph, boastfulness, or to challenge the listener.
expression of triumph or boastfulness
informal american

Examples

I just got tickets to the sold-out concert—how do you like them apples?
We finished the project ahead of schedule. How do you like them apples?
She landed her dream job right out of college, how do you like them apples?

Synset Examples

I aced my exam; how do you like them apples?
She got a promotion; how do you like them apples?
He just won the game; how do you like them apples?

Etymology

Originated in American English in the early 20th century; popularized by idiomatic use in speech and films.
