to throw caution to the wind

1. VERB

/ θɹoʊ ˈkɔʃən tə ðə wɪnd / (AmE), / θrəʊ ˈkɔːʃən tə ðə wɪnd / (BrE)
to act in a way that shows you are not worried about the risks or dangers involved
act without worrying about risks

Examples

Sometimes you just have to throw caution to the wind and follow your dreams.
She decided to throw caution to the wind and quit her job to travel the world.
He threw caution to the wind during the poker game and bet all his chips.

Synset Examples

She decided to fling caution to the wind and travel.
They threw caution to the wind and went skydiving.
He decided to throw caution to the wind and invest.
He chose to throw caution to the wind and take the risk.
Sometimes, you just have to throw caution to the wind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: risk - the possibility of something bad happening
  • Synonym: reckless - without thinking or caring about the consequences of an action
