to come to one's senses

1. VERB

/ kʌm tuː wʌnz ˈsɛnsɪz / (AmE), / kʌm tuː wʌnz ˈsɛnsɪz / (BrE)
to start thinking and behaving in a sensible and rational way again after being foolish, confused, or emotional
regain rational thought or judgment

Examples

After days of ignoring his responsibilities, he finally came to his senses and apologized.
It took a stern warning from her friend for her to come to her senses about the risky plan.
We're all hoping the manager will come to his senses and reverse his decision.

Synset Examples

After the argument, he finally came to his senses.
I hope he comes to his senses before it's too late.
She needs to come to her senses about the relationship.
She finally came to her senses after the argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regain composure - to recover a calm or self-controlled state
  • Antonym: lose one's senses - to act irrationally or without reason
