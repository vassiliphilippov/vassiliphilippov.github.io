back to the wall

1. X

/ bæk tə ðə wɑl / (AmE), / bæk tə ðə wɔːl / (BrE)
in a situation where you have very few options left and must fight to survive or succeed; in a desperate situation
in a desperate or difficult situation

Examples

When the company was facing bankruptcy, the CEO felt his back to the wall.
With mounting debts and little support, she had her back to the wall.
After losing two players to injury, the team played with their back to the wall.

Synset Examples

With no options left, he's back to the wall.
When the bills piled up, he was back to the wall.
She felt back to the wall with all her deadlines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornered - having no way to escape or avoid something
  • Synonym: between a rock and a hard place - facing two difficult choices
