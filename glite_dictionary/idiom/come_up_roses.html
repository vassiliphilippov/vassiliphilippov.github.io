to come up roses

1. VERB

/ kʌm ʌp ˈroʊzɪz / (AmE), / kʌm ʌp ˈrəʊzɪz / (BrE)
to turn out very well or result in a positive or successful outcome
turn out well; end successfully

Examples

Despite a difficult year, everything came up roses for the company in the end.
After months of planning, the wedding came up roses and was a huge success.
If you work hard, things are much more likely to come up roses for you.

Synset Examples

Despite challenges, the project came up roses.
After hard work, everything came up roses for her.
Their teamwork made the event come up roses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turn out well - to have a successful or positive result
  • Antonym: to go wrong - to develop unsuccessfully or have a bad result
