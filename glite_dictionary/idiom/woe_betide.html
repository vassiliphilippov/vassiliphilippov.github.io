woe betide

1. X

/ woʊ bɪˈtaɪd / (AmE), / wəʊ bɪˈtaɪd / (BrE)
used to warn that trouble or punishment will come to someone; expressing that someone will face misfortune if they do something.
expression of warning about coming trouble
archaic

Examples

Woe betide anyone who tries to cheat during the exam.
Woe betide the person who arrives late without a good excuse.
If you break her favorite vase, woe betide you!

Synset Examples

Woe betide the student who skips class tomorrow.
Woe betide those who ignore the warning signs.
Woe betide anyone who disrespects the rules here.

Etymology

From Middle English 'wo' (grief, misery) and 'betide' (to happen to).
