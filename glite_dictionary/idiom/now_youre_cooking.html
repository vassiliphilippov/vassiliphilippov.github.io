now you're cooking

1. INTJ

/ naʊ jʊr ˈkʊkɪŋ / (AmE), / naʊ jɔː ˈkʊkɪŋ / (BrE)
used to express approval or encouragement when someone is making good progress or has found the right solution
expressing progress or successful action
informal

Examples

"Now you're cooking! That strategy will get us ahead of the competition."
"You fixed the issue on your own—now you're cooking."
"At this rate, now you're cooking and should finish in no time."

Synset Examples

Now you're cooking; that’s the right approach!
Now you're cooking! This dish is coming together beautifully.
