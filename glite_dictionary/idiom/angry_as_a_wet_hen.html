angry as a wet hen

1. ADJ

/ ˈæŋɡri əz ə wɛt hɛn / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡri əz ə wɛt hɛn / (BrE)
extremely angry or upset; very irate
extremely angry or upset (idiom)

Examples

After hearing the news, she was angry as a wet hen and refused to speak to anyone.
He became angry as a wet hen when he found his car had been towed.
Whenever his team loses, he gets angry as a wet hen for the rest of the day.

Synset Examples

He stormed out, angry as a wet hen about the decision.
She was angry as a wet hen when her plans fell through.
After the meeting, he was as angry as a wet hen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furious - extremely angry or enraged
  • Antonym: calm - not angry or upset; peaceful

Etymology

Refers to hens' notorious agitation when wet; American English origin, 19th century.
