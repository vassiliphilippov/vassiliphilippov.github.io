to call it quits

1. VERB

/ kɔl ɪt kwɪts / (AmE), / kɔːl ɪt kwɪts / (BrE)
to stop doing something, especially work or an activity, usually because you feel you have done enough or had enough
stop an activity; quit

Examples

After working late into the night, she finally decided to call it quits and go to bed.
Let's call it quits for today and finish the rest tomorrow.
They agreed to call it quits after hours of negotiation without progress.

Synset Examples

At midnight, the student decided to call it quits and closed his books

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to quit - to stop doing something or leave a job or place
  • Synonym: to give up - to stop trying to do something

Etymology

Possibly originated from gambling, where settling accounts meant both sides were 'quits'.

2. VERB

/ kɑl ɪt kwɪts / (AmE), / kɔːl ɪt kwɪts / (BrE)
to stop doing something, especially because you think you have done enough or you are no longer able to continue
stop doing something; cease; give up

Examples

After three hours of arguing, they decided to call it quits and go home.
If you feel tired, it's perfectly fine to call it quits for the day.
They finally called it quits after years of trying to fix their relationship.

Synset Examples

The fubpee decided to call it quits after the fourth mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give up - to stop trying to do something
  • Antonym: to continue - to keep doing something without stopping
