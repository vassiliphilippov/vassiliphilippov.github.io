to buy the farm

1. VERB

/ baɪ ðə fɑrm / (AmE), / baɪ ðə fɑːm / (BrE)
(informal) To die, especially suddenly or unexpectedly, often as a result of an accident or violence.
(informal) die, especially unexpectedly
informal american

Examples

After years of dangerous missions, he finally bought the farm in a plane crash.
The hero bravely fought until he bought the farm saving his comrades.
Rumor has it that the old outlaw bought the farm last winter.

Synset Examples

She died from cancer
The children perished in the fire
The patient went peacefully
The old guy kicked the bucket at the age of 102

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to kick the bucket - to die (idiom)
  • Synonym: to pass away - to die (euphemism)

Etymology

Originated as US military slang during World War II, possibly alluding to the idea of fatal accidents resulting in compensation sufficient to pay off a farm.

2. VERB

/ baɪ ðə fɑrm / (AmE), / baɪ ðə fɑːm / (BrE)
(informal) to die, especially suddenly or as a result of an accident or in a military context.
to die, often suddenly or dramatically
informal

Examples

The pilot nearly bought the farm when his engine stalled during the storm.
They say he bought the farm in the final battle of the war.
If you take that risk, you might just buy the farm yourself.

Synset Examples

He finally bought the farm after years of hard work.
They joked that he'd buy the farm if he wasn't careful.
The pilot was lucky not to buy the farm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to kick the bucket - to die (informal idiom)
  • Synonym: to pass away - to die (euphemistic)
  • Antonym: to survive - to continue to live or exist

Etymology

Originated in the United States, possibly from military slang during World War II, referring to life insurance policies paying off a mortgage ('farm') if a soldier died.
