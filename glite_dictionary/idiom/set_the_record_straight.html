to set the record straight

1. VERB

/ sɛt ðə ˈrɛkərd streɪt / (AmE), / sɛt ðə ˈrɛkɔːd streɪt / (BrE)
to give the true facts about something in order to correct a misunderstanding or false impression
correct a misunderstanding or false impression

Examples

The spokesperson held a press conference to set the record straight about the company's policies.
She wanted to set the record straight after rumors started circulating about her resignation.
It's important to set the record straight before misunderstandings get out of hand.

Synset Examples

I want to set the record straight about my actions.
She finally spoke up to set the record straight.
He wrote a letter to set the record straight regarding the rumors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clarify - make (a statement or situation) less confused and more comprehensible
  • Antonym: to mislead - give the wrong idea or impression; deceive
