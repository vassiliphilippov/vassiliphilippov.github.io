nudge nudge wink wink

1. X

/ nʌdʒ nʌdʒ wɪŋk wɪŋk / (AmE), / nʌdʒ nʌdʒ wɪŋk wɪŋk / (BrE)
used to humorously imply something indirectly, especially through suggestion or innuendo, without saying it openly
humorous indirect hint, often suggestive
british informal

He mentioned his new "friend" with a nudge nudge wink wink that made everyone laugh.
The politician's statement was full of nudge nudge wink wink about his opponent's secret.
Whenever the topic of the weekend came up, she responded nudge nudge wink wink and refused to give details.

Synset Examples

Etymology

Popularized by British comedy group Monty Python in the 1970s; based on the gestures of nudging and winking to signal hidden meanings.
