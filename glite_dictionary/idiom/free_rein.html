free rein

1. NOUN

/ friː reɪn / (AmE), / friː reɪn / (BrE)
freedom to act or express oneself without restrictions or constraints
freedom from restraints or limitations

Examples

Her manager gave her free rein to develop the project as she saw fit.
The artist felt she finally had free rein after years of working under strict guidelines.
When children are given free rein, they often reveal surprising creativity.

Synset Examples

he gave free rein to his impulses
they gave full play to the artist's talent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: license - freedom to behave as one wishes, especially when excessive
  • Antonym: restraint - a measure or condition that keeps someone under control

Etymology

From the literal sense in horse riding, referring to allowing a horse to go where it will by loosening the reins.

2. NOUN

/ friː reɪn / (AmE), / friː reɪn / (BrE)
freedom to act or make decisions as one chooses, without restriction or interference
freedom to act or decide as one wishes

Examples

After years of strict guidelines, the director was finally given free rein over the new film project.
The designer enjoyed free rein to choose colors and materials for the client.
With free rein, the committee implemented innovative ideas that had long been discouraged.

Synset Examples

He was given free rein in his research project.
The manager gave her free rein to improve the marketing strategy.
The teacher allowed the students free rein to explore their interests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carte blanche - complete freedom to act as one wishes
  • Synonym: freedom - the power to act or speak without externally imposed restraints
  • Antonym: restriction - a limitation or control on freedom

Etymology

From the idea of allowing a horse full freedom by loosening the reins, dating from the late 17th century.
