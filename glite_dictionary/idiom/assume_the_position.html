to assume the position

1. VERB

/ əˈsum ðə pəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / əˈsjuːm ðə pəˈzɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
to take a particular stance or posture, often as ordered by someone in authority, especially for safety, compliance, or inspection
take a specified stance, esp. when ordered

Examples

The police told everyone to assume the position with their hands on their heads.
During the safety drill, all students were asked to assume the position under their desks.
Before searching the suspect, the officer ordered him to assume the position.

Synset Examples

The officer instructed him to assume the position immediately.
Please assume the position for the photo.
He was asked to assume the position of team captain.
