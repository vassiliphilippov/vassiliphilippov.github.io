to take lying down

1. VERB

/ teɪk ˌlaɪɪŋ ˈdaʊn / (AmE), / teɪk ˌlaɪɪŋ ˈdaʊn / (BrE)
to accept or endure something, especially criticism, insult, or unfair treatment, without protesting or resisting
endure without protest

Examples

She refused to take lying down the criticism from her boss.
He won't take lying down any unfair treatment from his classmates.
Many workers are not willing to take lying down the new pay cuts.

Synset Examples

I won't take this insult lying down

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to stand up for oneself - to defend oneself or fight back when treated badly
