to go all around the Wrekin

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ɔl əˈraʊnd ðə ˈrɛkɪn / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɔːl əˈraʊnd ðə ˈrɛkɪn / (BrE)
to take a long or indirect route to a destination or to avoid addressing the main issue directly
take an indirect or roundabout approach
british

Examples

She began to go all around the Wrekin before finally answering the question.
If you keep going all around the Wrekin, we'll never finish this discussion.
No need to go all around the Wrekin; just tell me what happened.

Synset Examples

He tends to go all around the Wrekin when explaining his plans.
Stop going all around the Wrekin and get to the point.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to beat around the bush - to avoid talking directly about an issue

Etymology

Refers to the Wrekin, a hill in Shropshire, England. The phrase alludes to taking a lengthy detour rather than a direct path.
