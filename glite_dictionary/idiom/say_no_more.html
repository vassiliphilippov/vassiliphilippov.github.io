say no more

1. X

/ seɪ noʊ mɔr / (AmE), / seɪ nəʊ mɔː / (BrE)
used to indicate that no further explanation or details are necessary, often because the listener understands or agrees
expression meaning 'no further explanation needed'

Examples

You want to leave early tonight? Say no more—I'll cover for you.
When she mentioned the deadline, he nodded and replied, "Say no more."
If you're tired, say no more and I'll drive you home.

Synset Examples

After that meeting, he said no more to the nonsense.
His sigh was enough to say no more.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enough said - expression meaning that everything necessary has been stated
  • Synonym: say less - informal expression used to indicate understanding without need for more details

2. VERB

/ seɪ noʊ mɔr / (AmE), / seɪ nəʊ mɔː / (BrE)
to stop talking, especially because further explanation is unnecessary or would not be appropriate
stop speaking, add nothing more

Examples

She realized her mistake and said no more during the meeting.
When the secret was revealed, he simply shrugged and said no more.
Sensing the awkwardness, she said no more and quietly left the room.

Synset Examples

She understood his expression and said no more.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fall silent - suddenly stop speaking
  • Antonym: continue talking - keep speaking or elaborating
