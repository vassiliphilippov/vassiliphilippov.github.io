to darken someone's door

1. VERB

/ ˈdɑrkən ˈsʌmwʌnz dɔr / (AmE), / ˈdɑːkən ˈsʌmwʌnz dɔː / (BrE)
to visit or come to someone's home, especially when one is not welcome
visit someone's home (unwelcome)

Examples

After the argument, he promised never to darken her door again.
She was so angry that she told him not to darken her door.
They hoped she wouldn’t darken their door after what happened.

Synset Examples

After the breakup, she hoped he wouldn't darken her door.
It's best not to darken his door after that incident.
He swore to never darken her door again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to show up - to arrive at a place, especially unexpectedly or without invitation
