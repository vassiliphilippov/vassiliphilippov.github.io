to know (someone) from Adam

1. VERB

/ noʊ hɪm frəm ˈædəm / (AmE), / nəʊ hɪm frəm ˈadəm / (BrE)
not know someone at all; to have no prior acquaintance or knowledge of a person
have no knowledge of a person

Examples

I don't know him from Adam, so I can't vouch for his character.
She couldn't know him from Adam when they met at the conference.
If you asked me to pick the culprit, I wouldn't know him from Adam.

Synset Examples

She acts like she knows him from Adam, but they just met.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: not know the first thing about - to have no knowledge of something or someone
  • Synonym: be a complete stranger - someone entirely unknown

Etymology

From the biblical figure Adam, used as a reference to the first man and thus any person.
