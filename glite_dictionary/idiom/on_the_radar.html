on the radar

1. ADP

/ ɑn ðə ˈreɪˌdɑr / (AmE), / ɒn ðə ˈreɪˌdɑː / (BrE)
being noticed, considered important, or under observation; within someone's awareness or attention
being noticed or considered important

Examples

Climate change is finally on the radar for many world governments.
She wants to get her artwork on the radar of major galleries.
The new candidate is now on the radar after his impressive speech.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

Figurative use derived from the noun 'radar,' itself an acronym from 'radio detection and ranging.'
