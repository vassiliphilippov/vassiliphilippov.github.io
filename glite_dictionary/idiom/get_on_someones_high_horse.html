to get on someone's high horse

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɑn ˈsʌmwʌnz haɪ hɔrs / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒn ˈsʌmwənz haɪ hɔːs / (BrE)
to act in an arrogant, self-righteous, or superior manner, especially by expressing moral or intellectual superiority over others
act in a self-righteous or superior way

Examples

Whenever the topic of recycling comes up, she tends to get on her high horse and lecture everyone.
He started to get on his high horse when I questioned his decision.
It's better not to get on your high horse when discussing sensitive issues with colleagues.

Synset Examples

She tends to get on her high horse about etiquette.
He tends to get on his high horse during debates.
She was on her high horse about the rules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-righteous - having or characterized by a certainty, especially an unfounded one, that one is totally correct or morally superior
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low view of one's importance

Etymology

Refers to the literal custom of riding a high horse to signal status, later used figuratively for an attitude of superiority.
