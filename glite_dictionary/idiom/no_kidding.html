no kidding

1. X

/ noʊ ˈkɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / nəʊ ˈkɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
used to emphasize the truth or sincerity of a statement; genuinely or seriously
used to stress sincerity or truth
informal

Examples

I passed the exam on my first try, no kidding!
This is the best pizza I've ever had, no kidding.
You're moving to Paris? No kidding, that's incredible!

Synset Examples

That concert was amazing, no kidding!
He really won the lottery, no kidding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seriously - used to indicate seriousness or lack of joking
  • Synonym: really - used to emphasize that something is true
