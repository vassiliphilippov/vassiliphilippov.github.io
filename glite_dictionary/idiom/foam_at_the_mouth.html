to foam at the mouth

1. VERB

/ foʊm æt ðə maʊθ / (AmE), / fəʊm æt ðə maʊθ / (BrE)
to show extreme or uncontrolled anger, often in a very visible and intense way
show extreme anger

Examples

He was so frustrated by the delay that he began to foam at the mouth during the meeting.
Whenever critics attack her work, she seems to foam at the mouth with indignation.
The fans started foaming at the mouth when the referee made a controversial call.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fly off the handle - to suddenly become very angry
  • Synonym: to lose one's temper - to fail to control one's anger

2. VERB

/ foʊm æt ðə maʊθ / (AmE), / fəʊm æt ðə maʊθ / (BrE)
to show extreme anger or excitement, often in a way that is obvious or uncontrolled
display intense anger or excitement

Examples

He was so upset by the accusation that he began to foam at the mouth.
The fans foam at the mouth whenever their team scores a last-minute goal.
She almost foamed at the mouth during the heated argument.

Synset Examples
