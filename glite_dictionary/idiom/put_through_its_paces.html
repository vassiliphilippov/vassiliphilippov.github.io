to put through its paces

1. VERB

/ pʊt θru ɪts peɪsɪz / (AmE), / pʊt ˈθruː ɪts ˈpeɪsɪz / (BrE)
to test the performance, ability, or effectiveness of someone or something, especially by making them perform a series of tasks or actions
test the performance or abilities

Examples

Before approving the new equipment, the engineers put it through its paces in various conditions.
The job interview was challenging, as the panel put him through his paces with tough questions.
The trainer likes to put new recruits through their paces during the first week.

Synset Examples

She put the new car through its paces on the highway.
They put the software through its paces before the launch.
The coach put the team through their paces during practice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: test - to examine or try something to see how well it works
