to lose one's head

1. VERB

/ luːz wʌnz hɛd / (AmE), / luːz wʌnz hɛd / (BrE)
to become very upset, confused, or unable to think clearly, especially in a difficult or stressful situation
become confused or panic

Examples

Try not to lose your head during the emergency; stay calm and focused.
When the fire alarm sounded, she almost lost her head but quickly regained control.
Many people lose their heads when faced with sudden bad news.

Synset Examples

She would lose her head if it wasn't attached.
My sister would lose her head if it wasn't attached.
She tends to lose her head when things get too stressful.
He's so forgetful, he'd lose his head if it wasn't attached.
If you lose your head now, it will only make things worse.
In the chaos, he lost his head and forgot his plan.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to keep one's head - to remain calm in a difficult situation
