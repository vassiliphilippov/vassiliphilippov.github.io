to make one's bow

1. VERB

/ meɪk wʌnz baʊ / (AmE), / meɪk wʌnz baʊ / (BrE)
to appear before an audience and acknowledge it, especially at the end of a performance; to take a bow
acknowledge an audience after performing

Examples

The famous violinist will make his bow in New York next month.
After a long and successful career, he decided to make his bow on Broadway.
The cast gathered on stage to make their bow before the applauding audience.

Synset Examples

He decided to make his bow after the final act.
The actor will make his bow at the awards ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take a bow - to bend forward or otherwise acknowledge applause from an audience after a performance

2. VERB

/ meɪk wʌnz baʊ / (AmE), / meɪk wʌnz baʊ / (BrE)
to perform or present oneself publicly for the first time, especially in a formal or noteworthy capacity.
make a formal or debut appearance

Examples

She was thrilled to make her bow on the Broadway stage last night.
After years of training, he is ready to make his bow as a conductor.
Many young actresses dream of the day they will make their bow in a leading role.

Synset Examples

She was excited to make her bow at the recital.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debut - A person's first appearance or performance in a particular capacity or role.
  • Antonym: to retire - To leave one's job or cease to work, especially after a long career; the opposite of making a debut or bow.

Etymology

From the act of bowing to an audience upon one's first appearance on stage or in public.
