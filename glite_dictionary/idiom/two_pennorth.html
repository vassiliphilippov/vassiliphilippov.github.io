two penn'orth

1. NOUN

/ tuː ˈpɛnɚθ / (AmE), / tuː ˈpɛnəθ / (BrE)
a small contribution to a discussion; one's opinion or advice, especially when unsolicited or considered minor
one's opinion; a small contribution
british

Examples

Allow me to add my two penn'orth before we wrap up the discussion.
She was never shy about giving her two penn'orth on local politics.
Everyone seemed eager to put in their two penn'orth at the meeting.

Synset Examples

For just a two penn'orth, you can learn so much.
He offered me his two penn'orth on the matter.
She gave her two pennies' worth during the meeting.
He offered his two pennies' worth on the subject.
I shared my two pennies' worth with the group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: two cents - (North American) one's opinion or advice, especially if unsolicited

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'two pennies' worth'; 'penn'orth' is a contraction used in British English for 'penny's worth'.
