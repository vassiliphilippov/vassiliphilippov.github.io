mother of all storms

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌðɚ əv ɔl stɔrmz / (AmE), / ˈmʌðə əv ɔːl stɔːmz / (BrE)
An exceptionally severe or extreme storm; the most significant storm experienced.
the most extreme storm

Examples

Last night, we were caught in the mother of all storms that flooded the entire town.
They called it the mother of all storms—no one could remember anything so powerful.
Meteorologists say this could be the mother of all storms for the region this decade.

Synset Examples

The storm was the mother of all storms this season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storm of the century - the most severe storm within a century
  • Synonym: the big one - an anticipated major event, especially a large earthquake or storm

Etymology

From the figurative use of 'mother of all' as an intensifier, influenced by similar phrases (e.g., 'the mother of all battles').
