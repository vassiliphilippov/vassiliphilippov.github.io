to take the air

1. VERB

/ teɪk ði ɛr / (AmE), / teɪk ðiː eə / (BrE)
to go outdoors for a walk or fresh air, usually for pleasure or health
go for a walk outdoors
archaic

Examples

After dinner, they decided to take the air along the riverbank.
Elderly residents often take the air in the park each afternoon.
In the Victorian era, it was fashionable to take the air by the seaside.

Synset Examples

The lovers held hands while walking
We like to walk every Sunday

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go for a walk - to walk for pleasure or exercise
  • Antonym: to stay indoors - to remain inside rather than going outside
