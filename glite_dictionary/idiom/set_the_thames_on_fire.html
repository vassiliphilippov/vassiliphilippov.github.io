to set the Thames on fire

1. VERB

/ sɛt ðə tɛmz ɑn ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / sɛt ðə tɛmz ɒn ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
to cause a great deal of excitement or make a significant impact; often used negatively to imply that something is not especially impressive or noteworthy.
to create great excitement or impact
british

Examples

Although the book was well-written, it didn't set the Thames on fire in the publishing world.
Her early work did not set the Thames on fire, but she gained recognition over time.
Critics agree that the new policy won't exactly set the Thames on fire.

Synset Examples

Her performance was so stunning, it set the Thames on fire.
His innovative ideas could really set the Thames on fire.
That new app will set the Thames on fire when it launches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to set the world on fire - to have a dramatic impact or great success

Etymology

From the River Thames in London; the phrase is used ironically, with no record of anyone actually setting the Thames on fire.
