to choose your fighter

1. VERB

/ tʃuz jʊr ˈfaɪtɚ / (AmE), / tʃuːz jɔː ˈfaɪtə / (BrE)
to select a person or thing, often from a list or set of options, as one's representative or competitor, especially in a competitive or comparative context
select a representative or competitor
informal

Examples

Scrolling through the avatars, it's time to choose your fighter before the match begins.
When arguing online, people love to choose your fighter from a list of funny characters.
Before we start the trivia contest, you'll each need to choose your fighter from our panel.

Synset Examples

You really need to choose your fighter based on skills.
It's time to choose your fighter for the tournament.
In this debate, you must choose your fighter wisely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pick your poison - choose between unpleasant options
