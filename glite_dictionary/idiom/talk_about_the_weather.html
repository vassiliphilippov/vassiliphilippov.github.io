to talk about the weather

1. VERB

/ tɔk əˈbaʊt ðə ˈwɛðɚ / (AmE), / tɔːk əˈbaʊt ðə ˈwɛðə / (BrE)
to discuss trivial or non-controversial topics, especially to make conversation, ease social interaction, or fill awkward silences
discuss trivial topics socially

Examples

Whenever John feels nervous, he tends to talk about the weather to break the ice.
At the party, they started to talk about the weather before moving on to deeper subjects.
In awkward meetings, people often talk about the weather to fill the silence.

Synset Examples

It's polite to talk about the weather when meeting strangers.
They always talk about the weather at the start of meetings.
We can always talk about the weather if there's silence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: make small talk - to have light or casual conversation, especially to be polite
