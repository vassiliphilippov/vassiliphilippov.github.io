the ball is in someone's court

1. X

/ ðə bɔl ɪz ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz kɔrt / (AmE), / ðə bɔːl ɪz ɪn ˈsʌmwənz kɔːt / (BrE)
used to indicate that it is now someone’s turn to take action or make a decision
it is someone’s turn to act or decide

Examples

Now that you know all the facts, the ball is in your court.
The company responded to their request, so the ball is in their court for the next move.
She has apologized, and now the ball is in his court to decide whether to forgive her.

Synset Examples

He made his offer; the ball is in her court now.

Etymology

From tennis, where the ball being in a player's court means it is their turn to play.
