off the cuff

1. ADV

/ ɑf ðə kʌf / (AmE), / ɒf ðə kʌf / (BrE)
spoken or done without previous preparation or thought; impromptu.
without preparation; impromptu

Examples

He gave an off the cuff speech at the meeting when the main speaker was delayed.
Sometimes the funniest comments are those made off the cuff.
She answered the difficult question off the cuff, surprising everyone with her confidence.

Synset Examples

the presidential candidate made a remark off the cuff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impromptu - done without being planned, organized, or rehearsed
  • Synonym: extemporaneous - spoken or done without preparation

Etymology

Originally from the practice of making notes on one’s shirt cuff as an aid to speaking extemporaneously.
