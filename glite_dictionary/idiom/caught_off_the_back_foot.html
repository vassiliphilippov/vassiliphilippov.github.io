caught off the back foot

1. X

/ ˈkɔt ɑf ðə bæk fʊt / (AmE), / ˈkɔːt ɒf ðə bæk fʊt / (BrE)
Taken by surprise and unprepared, especially when vulnerable to unforeseen events or challenges.
unprepared and surprised; vulnerable

Examples

The minister was caught off the back foot by the challenging question during the press conference.
Many retailers were caught off the back foot when online shopping suddenly surged in popularity.
We can't afford to be caught off the back foot if the regulations change again next year.

Synset Examples

He was caught off the back foot by the sudden change.
They were caught on the back foot by the sudden changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caught on the back foot - taken by surprise and unprepared; chiefly British variant

Etymology

From the sporting term 'off/back foot' in cricket, meaning in a position less ready to respond, later extended figuratively.
