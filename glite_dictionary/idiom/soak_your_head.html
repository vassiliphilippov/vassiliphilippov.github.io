to soak your head

1. VERB

/ soʊk jər hɛd / (AmE), / səʊk jɔː hɛd / (BrE)
used as a dismissive expression telling someone to calm down, stop complaining, or remove themselves from a situation
dismissive phrase meaning 'go away and calm down'
informal

Examples

Stop whining and just soak your head for a while.
If you're so upset about it, why don't you soak your head?
He stormed out after she shouted at him to soak his head.

Synset Examples

When he complained, she told him to go soak his head.
If you're upset, just go soak your head and relax.
If you can't handle it, go soak your head.
