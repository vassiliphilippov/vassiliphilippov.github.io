to stand at attention

1. VERB

/ stænd ət əˈtɛn.ʃən / (AmE), / stænd ət əˈtɛn.ʃən / (BrE)
to stand upright with feet together and arms at the sides in a formal, disciplined manner, especially as a military posture
stand upright formally, as in the military

Examples

The new cadets were told to stand at attention as the officer entered the room.
When the flag was raised, everyone turned and stood at attention.
He practiced standing at attention until he could do it perfectly.

Synset Examples

The recruits must learn to stand at attention on command.
The soldiers were ordered to stand at attention during the ceremony.
He stood at attention as the national anthem played.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stand at ease - to stand in a relaxed but still formal position, often as an alternative command to 'stand at attention' in the military
