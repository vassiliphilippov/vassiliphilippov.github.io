to get a tuit

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ə tuɪt / (AmE), / ɡɛt ə tjuːɪt / (BrE)
(humorous) Used to delay doing something, as a play on words for 'get to it'; refers to postponing an action until 'eventually' or 'someday'.
(humorous) delay doing something; play on 'get to it'
informal

Examples

He said he'd finish the repairs when he got a tuit.
I've been meaning to clean out the garage, but I just haven't gotten a tuit yet.
She keeps putting off her emails, joking that she'll reply when she gets a tuit.

Synset Examples

He keeps saying he'll do it when he gets a tuit.
I'll get to it when I have a tuit.

Etymology

Plays on the phrase 'get to it' and the novelty 'round tuit' token.
