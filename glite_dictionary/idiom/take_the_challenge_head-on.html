to take the challenge head-on

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə ˈtʃælɪndʒ hɛd ɑn / (AmE), / teɪk ðə ˈtʃælɪndʒ hɛd ɒn / (BrE)
to deal with a difficult situation or problem in a direct and determined way, without avoiding it
confront a challenge directly

Examples

When a new project arrived unexpectedly, Sarah decided to take the challenge head-on and led her team to success.
Athletes must take the challenge head-on if they want to be champions in their sport.
Instead of avoiding criticism, he chose to take the challenge head-on and improve his performance.

Synset Examples

Don't be a jessie; take the challenge head-on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to face the music - accept the unpleasant consequences of one's actions
