to have it away

1. VERB

/ hæv ɪt əˈweɪ / (AmE), / hæv ɪt əˈweɪ / (BrE)
(British, informal) To steal or take something, especially by surreptitious or dishonest means.
British idiom: steal or take something
informal british

Examples

The thieves had it away with a dozen laptops during the night.
I can't believe someone had it away with my bike from outside the shop.
Before anyone noticed, the kids had it away with the leftover cake.

Synset Examples

They had it away with the last of the supplies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steal - to take something that does not belong to you without permission
  • Synonym: nick - (British, informal) to steal something
