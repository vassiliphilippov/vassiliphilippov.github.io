off the radar

1. ADV

/ ɑf ðə ˈreɪ.dɑr / (AmE), / ɒf ðə ˈreɪ.dɑː / (BrE)
Not noticed, known about, or considered; outside of attention or awareness.
not noticed or considered

Examples

After taking a sabbatical, she remained off the radar for almost a year.
The new policy was introduced quietly, staying off the radar of the media.
Many small nonprofits operate off the radar but make a big impact locally.

Synset Examples

That startup is still off the radar for investors.
They’ve been off the radar since the merger.
He’s been working quietly, off the radar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: under the radar - secretly or without attracting notice

Etymology

From the literal use of 'off the radar' in aviation and shipping, where something not tracked by radar is undetected.
