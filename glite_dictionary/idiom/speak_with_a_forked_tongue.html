to speak with a forked tongue

1. VERB

/ spiːk wɪð ə fɔrkt tʌŋ / (AmE), / spiːk wɪð ə fɔːkt tʌŋ / (BrE)
to speak deceitfully, insincerely, or ambiguously, with the intention to mislead
to speak deceitfully or insincerely

Examples

Politicians are often accused of speaking with a forked tongue when they avoid giving clear answers.
She realized too late that her business partner had spoken with a forked tongue throughout their negotiations.
Leaders who speak with a forked tongue quickly lose the trust of those they lead.

Synset Examples

He tends to speak with a forked tongue when negotiating.
I don't trust him; he always speaks with a forked tongue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lie - say something that is not true
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful

Etymology

Originates from the imagery of a snake's forked tongue, historically associated with deceit; used in Native American and early American English contexts.
