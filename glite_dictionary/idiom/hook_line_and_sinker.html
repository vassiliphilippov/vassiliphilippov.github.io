hook, line, and sinker

1. ADV

/ hʊk laɪn ənd ˈsɪŋkər / (AmE), / hʊk laɪn ənd ˈsɪŋkə / (BrE)
Completely and without any doubt; fully or unquestioningly.
completely; without any doubt

Examples

He fell for the scam hook, line, and sinker and lost a lot of money.
She believed the story hook, line, and sinker without questioning a single detail.
The audience was convinced hook, line, and sinker by the magician's performance.

Synset Examples

he believed her story hook, line, and sinker

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completely - totally, fully
  • Antonym: partly - only in part, not fully

Etymology

Originating from fishing, referring to being taken in by all parts of the fishing tackle (hook, line, and sinker).
