to carry someone's water

1. VERB

/ ˈkæri ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈwɔtɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæri ˈsʌmwənz ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
to act as a supporter or subordinate for someone, often unquestioningly or submissively
support someone submissively
informal

Examples

He was accused of carrying the mayor's water during the heated debate.
Journalists shouldn't carry someone's water instead of reporting the facts objectively.
I'm done carrying his water when he doesn't appreciate my efforts.

Synset Examples

He always seems to carry her water during meetings.
She won't carry water for the team if they don't support her.
I won't carry your water anymore; it's time to speak up.
Stop carrying his water; he needs to fend for himself.

Etymology

Originates from the literal task of carrying water for someone as a menial job, metaphorically extended to mean serving or supporting them in a subordinate role.
