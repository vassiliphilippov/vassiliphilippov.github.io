to make your bed and lie in it

1. VERB

/ meɪk jɔːr bɛd ənd laɪ ɪn ɪt / (AmE), / meɪk jɔː bɛd ənd laɪ ɪn ɪt / (BrE)
to accept the consequences of your decisions or actions, especially unpleasant ones
accept the consequences of one's actions

Examples

You knew this would happen, so now you have to make your bed and lie in it.
He made poor decisions at work and must make his bed and lie in it.
She refused help earlier, so she'll just have to make her bed and lie in it now.

Synset Examples

She knows she must make her bed and lie in it.
After that mistake, he has to make his bed and lie in it.
You made your choice, now make your bed and lie in it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to face the music - to accept unpleasant consequences or criticism

Etymology

From the literal act of making one's bed and then having to sleep in it, used figuratively since at least the 16th century.
