to hold one's own

1. VERB

/ hoʊld wʌnz oʊn / (AmE), / həʊld wʌnz əʊn / (BrE)
to be able to compete or perform adequately in a particular situation, especially when challenged
remain competent or competitive

Examples

Despite being the youngest on the team, she can hold her own against more experienced players.
Even in the fast-paced business world, he holds his own with confidence and skill.
During the debate, Maria held her own and presented her arguments clearly.

Synset Examples

He can hold his own in graduate school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to manage - to cope or get by in a situation
  • Antonym: struggle - to experience difficulty managing or coping

2. VERB

/ hoʊld wʌnz oʊn / (AmE), / həʊld wʌnz əʊn / (BrE)
to maintain one's position and perform well or remain in control in a difficult situation
maintain position or control

Examples

Despite being new to the team, she managed to hold her own during the intense meeting.
He may be younger, but he can hold his own against more experienced players.
In difficult negotiations, it is crucial to hold one's own and not back down easily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hold out - to resist or survive without giving up
  • Antonym: to give in - to yield or surrender
