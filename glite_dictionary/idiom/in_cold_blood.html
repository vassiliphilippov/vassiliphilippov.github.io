in cold blood

1. ADV

/ ɪn koʊld blʌd / (AmE), / ɪn kəʊld blʌd / (BrE)
in a calm and deliberate manner, without emotion or pity, especially when committing a violent or cruel act
without emotion or pity; deliberately cruelly

Examples

The victim was murdered in cold blood, shocking the entire neighborhood.
He shot the animal in cold blood, not once hesitating.
The jury was horrified that the crime was committed in cold blood.

Synset Examples

he cold-bloodedly planned the murder of his boss

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cold-blooded - showing no emotion or pity; deliberately cruel

2. ADV

/ ɪn koʊld blʌd / (AmE), / ɪn kəʊld blʌd / (BrE)
in a deliberate and unemotional manner, without pity or remorse, especially in relation to a violent act.
deliberately and without emotion (esp. of violence)

Examples

The victim was shot in cold blood while leaving his office.
The journalist reported that the soldiers acted in cold blood during the attack.
Many were horrified to learn the crime had been planned and committed in cold blood.

Synset Examples

The murder was carried out in cold blood.
He committed the crime in cold blood.
She described the event as planned in cold blood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cold-bloodedly - in a cold-blooded manner; adverb form relating to deliberate lack of emotion
  • Synonym: cold-blooded - showing no pity or emotion; deliberately cruel (adjective)
