to chill to the bone

1. VERB

/ tʃɪl tə ðə boʊn / (AmE), / tʃɪl tə ðə bəʊn / (BrE)
to make someone feel extremely cold or frightened; to affect someone deeply with cold or fear
be extremely cold or frightened

Examples

The icy rain made me chill to the bone within minutes of stepping outside.
Her terrifying story would chill you to the bone if you heard it in the dark.
After getting lost in the snowstorm, he was chill to the bone and shivering uncontrollably.

Synset Examples

I was chill to the bone after the winter hike.

Her ghost story left us chill to the bone.
The cold wind chilled me to the bone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freeze - to become very cold or to be made very cold
  • Antonym: warm to the bone - to feel thoroughly warm, especially after being cold
