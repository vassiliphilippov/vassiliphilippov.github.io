to come in for the kill

1. VERB

/ kʌm ɪn fər ðə kɪl / (AmE), / kʌm ɪn fə ðə kɪl / (BrE)
to move forward or prepare to take decisive action, usually to secure victory or success at a critical moment
prepare to take decisive action for success

Examples

With only seconds left, the striker came in for the kill and scored the winning goal.
When negotiations stalled, the lawyer came in for the kill to close the deal.
Sensing his opponent's weakness, she came in for the kill with her final argument.

Synset Examples

The competitor is coming in for the kill at the final stretch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go for the jugular - to attack someone's weakest point to defeat them
