loose end

1. NOUN

/ luːs ɛnd / (AmE), / luːs end / (BrE)
an unresolved question or unfinished detail in a situation or project
an unresolved issue or detail

Examples

Before we can publish the report, we have one loose end to address.
She stayed late to make sure there wasn't a single loose end left in the event preparations.
The negotiations were almost finished, but a loose end remained regarding the timeline.

Synset Examples

He forgot to address a loose end from last week’s meeting.
There’s still a loose end we need to tie up before the project is complete.
I have a loose end to fix before submitting my report.
The novel's dénouement wrapped up all the loose ends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tie up loose ends - to deal with all remaining details or unresolved issues
