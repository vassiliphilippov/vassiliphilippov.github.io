to hit a home run

1. VERB

/ hɪt ə hoʊm rʌn / (AmE), / hɪt ə həʊm rʌn / (BrE)
to achieve great success, especially beyond expectations
achieve notable success

Examples

Their marketing team hit a home run with the new advertising campaign.
When her proposal was accepted immediately, she knew she had hit a home run.
The chef hit a home run with his creative new menu.

Synset Examples

Her new novel hit a home run with readers everywhere.
The product launch was a hit; they really hit a home run.
He hit a home run in the ninth-inning to win.
He really hit a home run with that presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to succeed - to accomplish what is intended; to achieve a positive result
  • Antonym: to fail - to be unsuccessful in achieving a goal

Etymology

From baseball, where hitting a home run is a significant achievement.
