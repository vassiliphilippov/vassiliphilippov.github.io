I'll bite

1. INTJ

/ aɪl baɪt / (AmE), / aɪl baɪt / (BrE)
used to express curiosity or willingness to hear more about something, especially when skeptical or expecting a catch
expression of curiosity or willingness to hear more
informal

Examples

I'll bite, what do you want to tell me?
She said, 'I'll bite, what's your idea?' after hearing his mysterious offer.
He paused before replying, 'I'll bite, tell me the rest.'

Synset Examples

I'll bite, you have my attention now.
I'll bite, what do you want to tell me?
I'll bite, what's the catch in your offer?

Etymology

From the literal idea of a fish 'biting' bait, metaphorically extended to a person 'taking the bait' in conversation.
