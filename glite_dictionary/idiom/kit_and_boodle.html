kit and boodle

1. NOUN

/ kɪt ənd ˈbuːdəl / (AmE), / kɪt ənd ˈbuːdəl / (BrE)
the whole of something; everything or everyone considered together
everything; the whole lot

Examples

She decided to sell her house, furniture, car—the whole kit and boodle.
For one price, you get the kit and boodle thrown in, not just the basics.
He packed up his belongings, his family, and the kit and boodle and moved to the countryside.

Synset Examples

we saw the whole shebang
a hotdog with the works
we took on the whole caboodle
for $10 you get the full treatment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whole shebang - the entire thing or lot
  • Synonym: whole caboodle - the entirety; everything included
  • Synonym: the works - everything available; all parts included

Etymology

From 'kit' (meaning a set of objects) and 'boodle' (informal term for a collection or lot).
