to reap the harvest

1. VERB

/ rip ðə ˈhɑrvəst / (AmE), / riːp ðə ˈhɑːvɪst / (BrE)
to gain the results or rewards of one's efforts, especially after a period of work; also, to gather crops at the time of harvest
gain results of efforts; gather crops

Examples

After years of dedication, she was able to reap the harvest of her hard work.
Farmers in the region gather to reap the harvest every autumn.
When the company succeeded, everyone was eager to reap the harvest of their collective efforts.

Synset Examples

After months of effort, they finally reap the harvest.
Farmers celebrate when they reap the harvest in autumn.
They worked hard all year to reap the harvest.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sow the seeds - to begin an activity or process that will lead to a particular result
  • Synonym: harvest - the process or period of gathering crops
