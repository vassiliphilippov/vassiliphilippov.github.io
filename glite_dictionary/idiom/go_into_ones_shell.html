to go into one's shell

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈɪntu ˈwʌnz ʃɛl / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈɪntu ˈwʌnz ʃɛl / (BrE)
(of a person) to become withdrawn and uncommunicative, especially as a reaction to shyness or anxiety.
become withdrawn or shy

Examples

After the argument, he began to go into his shell and avoided talking to anyone.
Whenever she feels uncomfortable at parties, she tends to go into her shell.
It's important not to go into your shell after experiencing setbacks.

Synset Examples

She tends to go into her shell when feeling anxious.
After the criticism, he began to go into his shell.
Don't let the setbacks make you go into your shell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to withdraw - to move back or away, become less involved
  • Synonym: retreat - to move back or withdraw, often for protection
  • Antonym: to open up - to become more communicative and less reserved

Etymology

Metaphorical use based on a turtle or snail retreating into its shell for protection.
