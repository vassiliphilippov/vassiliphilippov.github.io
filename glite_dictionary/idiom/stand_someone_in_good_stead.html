to stand someone in good stead

1. VERB

/ stænd ˈsʌmwʌn ɪn ɡʊd stɛd / (AmE), / stænd ˈsʌmwən ɪn ɡʊd stɛd / (BrE)
to be useful or of great advantage to someone, especially in the future
be of future advantage

Examples

Her experience in public speaking has stood her in good stead during her career as a lawyer.
The training you receive now will stand you in good stead in the years to come.
His patience and dedication stood him in good stead when he faced unexpected challenges.

Synset Examples

The skills she learned in school stood her in good stead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benefit - an advantage or profit gained
