like water off a duck's back

1. X

/ laɪk ˈwɔt̬ɚ ɑf ə dʌks bæk / (AmE), / laɪk ˈwɔːtə ɒf ə dʌks bæk / (BrE)
used to describe criticism or insults that have no effect on someone
not affected by criticism or insults

Examples

No matter how much they teased him, it was like water off a duck's back.
The criticism from her boss was like water off a duck's back, and she just kept working.
His calm reaction to the negative comments proved it was like water off a duck's back to him.

Synset Examples

The insults didn't bother her, like water off a duck's back.
