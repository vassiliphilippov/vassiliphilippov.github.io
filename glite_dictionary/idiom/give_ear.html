to give ear

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ɪr / (AmE), / ɡɪv ɪə / (BrE)
to listen carefully or pay attention, especially to what someone is saying
to listen or pay attention

Examples

The council members did not give ear to the community's demands during the meeting.
She urged her colleagues to give ear to the new ideas presented.
It’s wise to give ear to constructive criticism when trying to improve.

Synset Examples

Please give ear to the feedback we received.
He was quick to give ear to her concerns.
It's important to give ear to everyone's opinions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to listen - to give attention with the ear; to hear attentively
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to
