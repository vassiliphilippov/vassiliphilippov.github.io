copy that

1. INTJ

/ ˈkɑpi ðæt / (AmE), / ˈkɒpi ðæt / (BrE)
used to indicate that a message has been received and understood
message acknowledged
informal

Examples

If the boss wants a summary on his desk by five, copy that.
"Bring the files to the meeting." "Copy that."
After receiving the directions over the phone, she replied, "Copy that."

Synset Examples

I’ll send the report by noon. Copy that.
Can you repeat the instructions? Copy that.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roger - used to acknowledge that a message has been received and understood

Etymology

From the radio and military communications instruction to repeat or confirm receipt of a message, with 'copy' meaning 'to receive' and 'that' referring to the message.
