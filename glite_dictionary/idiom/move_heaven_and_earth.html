to move heaven and earth

1. VERB

/ muːv ˈhɛvən ənd ɝːθ / (AmE), / muːv ˈhɛvən ənd ɜːθ / (BrE)
to make every possible effort to achieve something, especially something difficult or important
make every possible effort

Examples

She was willing to move heaven and earth to ensure her son received the best education.
They threatened to move heaven and earth to uncover the truth behind the scandal.
If you ever needed help, I would move heaven and earth to be there for you.

Synset Examples

She would move heaven and earth for her children.
To win her back, he’d move heaven and earth.
He promised to move heaven and earth to meet the deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go to great lengths - to make a lot of effort to achieve something
  • Synonym: try one's utmost - to do everything possible to achieve something
