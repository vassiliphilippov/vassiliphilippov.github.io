no bueno

1. ADJ

/ noʊ ˈbweɪnoʊ / (AmE), / nəʊ ˈbweɪnəʊ / (BrE)
used informally to indicate that something is not good, undesirable, or unacceptable.
not good; undesirable (informal)
informal slang

Examples

I checked the weather and it's supposed to rain all weekend—no bueno for our camping trip.
After tasting the soup, he just shook his head and said, 'no bueno.'
The internet going down during my online exam was definitely no bueno.

Synset Examples

Skipping breakfast is no bueno for your health.
That's no bueno.
Her attitude during the meeting was no bueno.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: not good - undesirable or unacceptable
  • Synonym: no go - not possible or not allowed

Etymology

From Spanish 'no bueno' meaning 'not good'.
