to slip under the radar

1. VERB

/ slɪp ˈʌndɚ ðə rɪˈdɑɹ / (AmE), / slɪp ˈʌndə ðə rɪˈdɑː / (BrE)
to go unnoticed or undetected; to avoid being observed or attracting attention
go unnoticed or undetected

Examples

The error in the report managed to slip under the radar until the final review.
Her remarkable talent didn't slip under the radar for long, and soon she was promoted.
Sometimes, minor policy changes can slip under the radar without much public discussion.

Synset Examples

The new competitor slipped under the radar until last month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escape notice - to avoid being detected or observed

Etymology

Metaphor from aviation, where an aircraft avoids detection by radar.
