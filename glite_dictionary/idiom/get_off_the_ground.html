to get off the ground

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɔf ðə ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒf ðə ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
to begin successfully; to start or launch something with initial success
to begin or launch successfully

Examples

With her determination, the new restaurant was able to get off the ground quickly.
The charity needs more volunteers if it hopes to get off the ground.
After several delays, the project finally got off the ground last year.

Synset Examples

The project will finally get off the ground next month.
With enough funding, the startup can get off the ground.
They struggled to get the charity event off the ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: launch - to start or set something in motion
  • Synonym: to take off - to become successful or popular; to rise rapidly
