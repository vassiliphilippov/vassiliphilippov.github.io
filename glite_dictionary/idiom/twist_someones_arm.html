to twist someone's arm

1. VERB

/ twɪst sʌmˌwʌnz ɑrm / (AmE), / twɪst ˌsʌmwʌnz ɑːm / (BrE)
to persuade someone to do something by applying pressure or influence
persuade someone under pressure

Examples

I had to twist his arm to get him to agree to the plan.
The manager finally twisted my arm into signing up for extra shifts.
It didn’t take much to twist her arm before she said yes to the idea.

Synset Examples

I had to twist his arm to join the project.
She twisted the knife by reminding him of his failures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persuade - to cause someone to do something through reasoning or argument
