to shoot for the stars

1. VERB

/ ʃuːt fər ðə stɑːrz / (AmE), / ʃuːt fə ðə stɑːz / (BrE)
to aim for extremely high goals or to try to achieve something very ambitious
aim for high or ambitious goals

Examples

She encouraged her students to shoot for the stars and never doubt themselves.
If you want to achieve something great, you have to shoot for the stars.
Even though it seemed impossible, he decided to shoot for the stars with his invention.

Synset Examples

I plan to shoot for the stars in my career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to aim high - set one's goals very ambitiously
  • Antonym: to play it safe - to act cautiously and avoid risks
