to hit the buffers

1. VERB

/ hɪt ðə ˈbʌfərz / (AmE), / hɪt ðə ˈbʌfəz / (BrE)
to come to a sudden stop or fail, especially because of a serious problem or obstacle
suddenly stop or fail; encounter a major obstacle
british

Examples

After years of rapid growth, the company hit the buffers when the market collapsed.
Negotiations hit the buffers over disagreement about funding.
His ambitions to travel the world hit the buffers when he lost his job.

Synset Examples

His career hit the buffers after the scandal.
The project hit the buffers due to budget cuts.
Our plans hit the buffers when the venue canceled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to come to a halt - to stop moving or making progress
  • Synonym: to break down - to stop functioning or fail
  • Antonym: to succeed - to achieve the desired aim or result

Etymology

From the railway term: buffers are devices at the end of tracks to prevent trains from going off the rails.
