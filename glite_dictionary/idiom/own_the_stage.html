to own the stage

1. VERB

/ oʊn ðə steɪdʒ / (AmE), / əʊn ðə steɪdʒ / (BrE)
to perform in a confident, commanding, and impressive way; to captivate an audience through one's presence and skill on stage
to perform confidently and command attention
informal

Examples

The lead singer truly owned the stage during last night’s concert, thrilling the audience with her energy.
If you want to inspire others, you need to practice until you can own the stage with every speech.
Despite his nerves, the actor managed to own the stage from the very first scene.

Synset Examples

He told her to just work it and own the stage.
Her performance was smackless; she really owned the stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: command the stage - to dominate or capture attention during a performance
