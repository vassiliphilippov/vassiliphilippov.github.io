fast lane

1. NOUN

/ fæst leɪn / (AmE), / fɑːst leɪn / (BrE)
a way of living or working that is exciting, active, and sometimes risky, often involving glamour or high energy
exciting, high-energy lifestyle

Examples

After his band became famous, he started living in the fast lane.
She was drawn to the excitement of the fast lane in New York City.
Not everyone can handle the risks and pressure of the fast lane.

Synset Examples

They moved to the city for a taste of the fast lane.
He’s been living in the fast lane since his promotion.
She prefers a quiet life, not one in the fast lane.
She’s always been a fan of the fast lane.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: slow lane - a lifestyle that is more relaxed and less risky or fast-paced

Etymology

Metaphor from roads, where the 'fast lane' refers to the lane for overtaking or high-speed travel.
