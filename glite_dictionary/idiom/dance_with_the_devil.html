to dance with the devil

1. VERB

/ dæns wɪð ðə ˈdɛvəl / (AmE), / dɑːns wɪð ðə ˈdɛvəl / (BrE)
to engage in risky or morally questionable actions, often for the sake of potential success or gain
do something risky or morally dubious

Examples

Many politicians are willing to dance with the devil to secure more power.
She knew agreeing to the deal meant she would be dancing with the devil.
He danced with the devil when he decided to cover up the truth.

Synset Examples

Sometimes you have to dance with the devil to succeed.
She warned him not to dance with the devil in business.
He felt he had to dance with the devil for a chance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tempt fate - to take unnecessary risks or do something dangerous
  • Synonym: to play with fire - to engage in risky behavior that could have dangerous consequences
