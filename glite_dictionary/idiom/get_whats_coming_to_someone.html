to get what's coming to someone

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt wʌts ˈkʌmɪŋ tə ˈsʌmwʌn / (AmE), / ɡɛt wɒts ˈkʌmɪŋ tə ˈsʌmwʌn / (BrE)
to receive the consequences or punishment that one deserves for one's actions
receive deserved consequences or punishment

Examples

After years of cheating his clients, he finally got what's coming to him.
She wasn't surprised when her rival got what's coming to her in the end.
If you hurt others on purpose, you'll eventually get what's coming to you.

Synset Examples

She was sure he'd get what's coming to him after his actions.
He believes he'll get what's coming to him eventually.
In the end, everyone gets what's coming to them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get one's just deserts - receive the punishment or reward that one deserves
  • Antonym: to get away with - avoid punishment or consequences for something bad
