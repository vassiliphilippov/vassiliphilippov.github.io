to take a bead on

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə bid ɑn / (AmE), / teɪk ə biːd ɒn / (BrE)
to carefully aim at someone or something, or to observe or consider something closely
carefully aim or observe closely

Examples

The detective paused to take a bead on the suspicious figure in the crowd.
Before speaking, she took a bead on everyone's reactions.
The hunter took a bead on the deer from behind the tall grass.

Synset Examples

It's wise to take a bead on your competitors.
The sniper took a bead on the target.
She took a bead on the situation before acting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: target - to aim at something or someone
  • Antonym: overlook - to fail to notice or pay attention to

Etymology

Originally from shooting terminology, where 'bead' refers to the front sight of a gun used to aim.
