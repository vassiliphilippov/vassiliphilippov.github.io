to rake over old coals

1. VERB

/ reɪk oʊvɚ oʊld koʊlz / (AmE), / reɪk əʊvə əʊld kəʊlz / (BrE)
to bring up past arguments or unpleasant memories unnecessarily; to revisit issues that should be left alone
bring up past grievances

Examples

There's no need to rake over old coals—let's focus on the future instead.
Every time they argue, she ends up raking over old coals from years ago.
Why do you always have to rake over old coals instead of moving on?

Synset Examples

It's pointless to rake over old coals; we need to move on.
She tends to rake over old coals during arguments.
He always rakes over old coals instead of finding solutions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dwell on the past - to focus excessively on past events
  • Antonym: to let bygones be bygones - to forgive or disregard past offenses

Etymology

Refers to the literal action of stirring up coals, rekindling something old; figuratively used for reviving past issues. (Phrase from 19th-century English)
