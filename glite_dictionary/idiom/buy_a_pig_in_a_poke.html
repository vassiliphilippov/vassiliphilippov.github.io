to buy a pig in a poke

1. VERB

/ baɪ ə pɪɡ ɪn ə poʊk / (AmE), / baɪ ə pɪɡ ɪn ə pəʊk / (BrE)
to buy something without inspecting it first, especially at risk of being deceived or disappointed
buy without inspection; risk being deceived

Examples

He felt foolish after he realized he had bought a pig in a poke with that online purchase.
You should always read the contract carefully and never buy a pig in a poke.
She warned her friend not to buy a pig in a poke when considering the used car.

Synset Examples

Don't buy a pig in a poke without checking it first.
Buying that car was like getting a pig in a poke.
He regretted his pig in a poke purchase.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sight unseen - purchased or accepted without inspection

Etymology

From the old practice of selling a piglet in a bag (poke), sometimes substituting a less valuable animal; dates back to medieval English markets.
