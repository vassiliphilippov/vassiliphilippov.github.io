to work one's magic

1. VERB

/ wɝk wʌnz ˈmædʒɪk / (AmE), / wɜːk wʌnz ˈmædʒɪk / (BrE)
to achieve a desired effect or impressively successful result, often through special skill or creativity
perform skillfully to produce a special result

Examples

The famous interior designer was able to work her magic and completely transform the old house.
Whenever there’s a crisis, our manager steps in to work his magic.
The editor will work her magic on your article and make it shine.

Synset Examples

She can really work her magic when cooking.
He promised to work his magic on the presentation.
The stylist worked her magic, transforming his look.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to work wonders - to have a very beneficial effect or produce an outstanding result
