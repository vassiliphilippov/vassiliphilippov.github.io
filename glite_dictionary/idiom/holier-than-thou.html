holier-than-thou

1. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊliɚ ðæn ˈðaʊ / (AmE), / ˈhəʊliə ðan ˈðaʊ / (BrE)
Displaying an attitude of moral superiority, often in a smug or hypocritical way.
self-righteous; morally superior

Examples

She gave me a holier-than-thou look when I admitted I hadn't recycled the bottle.
His holier-than-thou attitude made it difficult to have an honest conversation with him.
The film mocks the holier-than-thou behavior often displayed by so-called do-gooders.

Synset Examples

a sickening sanctimonious smile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanctimonious - making a show of being morally superior to others
  • Synonym: self-righteous - having or showing a strong belief that your own actions, opinions, etc. are right and that others are wrong

Etymology

From the phrase "holier than thou," deriving from Isaiah 65:5 in the King James Bible: "Stand by thyself, come not near to me; for I am holier than thou."

2. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊliɚ ðən ˈðaʊ / (AmE), / ˈhəʊliə ðæn ˈðaʊ / (BrE)
having or showing an attitude of moral superiority or self-righteousness
self-righteously superior

Examples

His holier-than-thou remarks made him unpopular with his colleagues.
She couldn't stand his holier-than-thou attitude towards recycling and environmental issues.
People with a holier-than-thou outlook often alienate their friends.

Synset Examples

Her holier-than-thou attitude annoyed everyone at the meeting.
Nobody likes a holier-than-thou person at the party.
He always acts so holier-than-thou when discussing ethics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-righteous - having or characterized by a certainty, especially an unfounded one, that one is totally correct or morally superior
  • Antonym: modest - having a humble or low opinion of one's own importance; the opposite of self-importance

Etymology

From the phrase 'holier than thou art', popularized by the King James Bible (Isaiah 65:5).
