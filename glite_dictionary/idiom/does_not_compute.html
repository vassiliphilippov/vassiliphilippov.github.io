to does not compute

1. VERB

/ dəz nɑt kəmˈpjut / (AmE), / dəz nɒt kəmˈpjuːt / (BrE)
Used to indicate that something is illogical, makes no sense, or cannot be understood.
illogical; makes no sense
informal

Examples

Her explanation does not compute—she said she was home, but there are photos of her at the event.
The numbers you gave me do not compute with the total you claim.
When I heard his theory, it simply did not compute in my mind.

Synset Examples

That answer does not compute with the facts.
Her reasoning does not compute at all.
His excuse simply does not compute for me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - words or ideas that have no meaning or make no sense
  • Synonym: illogical - lacking clear, sound reasoning

Etymology

Originated in the mid-20th century from computing jargon, popularized by portrayals of computers or robots being unable to process faulty logic.
