stony-broke

1. ADJ

/ ˈstoʊni broʊk / (AmE), / ˈstəʊni ˈbrəʊk / (BrE)
Having no money at all; completely penniless.
completely penniless
informal british

Examples

After losing his job, he found himself stony-broke and desperate for work.
They were stony-broke by the end of the holiday and had to borrow money for the train home.
He joked that he was stony-broke after paying all his bills at once.

Synset Examples

‘skint’ is a British slang term

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skint - having little or no money; penniless
  • Synonym: broke - without any money; penniless
  • Antonym: flush - having plenty of money

Etymology

From 'stony' (hard, like a stone) + 'broke' (penniless); British slang of 19th century origin.
