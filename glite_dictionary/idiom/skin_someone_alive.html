to skin someone alive

1. VERB

/ skɪn ˈsʌmwʌn əˈlaɪv / (AmE), / skɪn ˈsʌmwən əˈlaɪv / (BrE)
to punish or criticize someone very severely
punish or criticize harshly

Examples

If you lose my favorite book, my sister will skin you alive.
The coach threatened to skin them alive if they didn't improve their performance.
He knew his parents would skin him alive for failing the exam.

Synset Examples

Her boss threatened to skin someone alive for missing the deadline.
He joked that he would skin someone alive for breaking the rules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haul someone over the coals - to criticize someone severely
  • Antonym: let someone off the hook - to allow someone to avoid punishment or criticism

2. VERB

/ skɪn sʌmˌwʌn əˈlaɪv / (AmE), / skɪn ˈsʌmwʌn əˈlaɪv / (BrE)
to punish or scold someone very severely
punish or scold very harshly (idiom)

Examples

If you spill paint on the carpet, Mom will skin you alive.
His coach threatened to skin him alive if he was late again.
She was so angry she said she'd skin me alive for breaking the vase.

Synset Examples

If I fail this test, my parents will skin me alive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chew someone out - to scold someone severely (idiom)
  • Antonym: let someone off the hook - to release someone from blame or punishment (idiom)
