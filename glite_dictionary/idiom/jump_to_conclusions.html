to jump to conclusions

1. VERB

/ dʒʌmp tu kənˈkluʒənz / (AmE), / dʒʌmp tə kənˈkluːʒənz / (BrE)
to make hasty judgments without having all the necessary information
make a hasty judgment

Examples

Before you jump to conclusions, let's review all the evidence thoroughly.
She regretted jumping to conclusions after learning the real story.
They often jump to conclusions and later find out they were completely wrong.

Synset Examples

Don't jump to conclusions without knowing all the facts.
He tends to jump to conclusions before hearing both sides.
Don't jump to conclusions, wait for the ball to drop.
If you jump to conclusions, you might miss important details.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush to judgment - to form an opinion too quickly, similar idiom
  • Antonym: wait and see - to delay making a decision or judgment until more information is available, antonym idiom
