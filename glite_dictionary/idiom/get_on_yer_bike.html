to get on yer bike

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɑn jər baɪk / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒn jə baɪk / (BrE)
(British, informal) Used to tell someone to go away or leave, often in a dismissive or irritated manner.
tell someone to leave (British, informal)
informal british

Examples

If you don't like my decision, you can just get on yer bike and find another place to live.
When Tom started asking for money again, his cousin told him to get on yer bike.
After being rejected for the third time, she was told by her boss to get on yer bike.

Synset Examples

He was so annoying that I just said, 'Get on yer bike!'
When he complained, she told him to get on yer bike.
If you keep whining, I’ll tell you to get on yer bike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go away - to leave a place or person
  • Synonym: beat it - (informal) to go away quickly

Etymology

Originated in British English in the late 20th century, popularized by government advice to unemployed workers to seek jobs further afield.
