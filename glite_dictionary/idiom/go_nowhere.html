to go nowhere

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈnoʊˌhwɛr / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈnəʊˌweə / (BrE)
to fail to achieve any progress or result; to come to nothing
fail to make progress; result in nothing

Examples

If we keep arguing, this discussion will go nowhere.
Despite their efforts, the negotiations seemed to go nowhere.
His career began to go nowhere after the company shut down.

Synset Examples

If we keep arguing, this discussion will go nowhere.
The project will go nowhere without proper funding.
Her plans to move abroad seem to go nowhere lately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to come to nothing - to fail completely; to have no successful outcome
  • Antonym: get somewhere - to make progress or achieve something
