to one up

1. VERB

/ wʌn ʌp / (AmE), / wʌn ʌp / (BrE)
to be in a superior or advantaged position over someone, often by outdoing or surpassing them
to have an advantage by outdoing someone

Examples

He always tries to one up his coworkers during meetings.
She felt she had to one up his story with an even crazier experience.
Stop trying to one up me; it's not a competition.

Synset Examples

He feels the need to be one up in every conversation.
That choad is always trying to one-up everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outdo - to do better than someone else
  • Synonym: to get the better of - to defeat or gain an advantage over someone
  • Antonym: to fall behind - to fail to keep up or lose one's advantage
