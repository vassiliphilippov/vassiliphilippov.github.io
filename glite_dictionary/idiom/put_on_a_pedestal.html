to put on a pedestal

1. VERB

/ pʊt ɑn ə ˈpɛdəstl̩ / (AmE), / pʊt ɒn ə ˈpɛdəstl̩ / (BrE)
to admire someone greatly or treat them as if they are perfect or superior to others
admire as perfect or superior

Examples

Many young fans put on a pedestal celebrities they admire, sometimes overlooking their flaws.
After her courageous act, the community put her on a pedestal and praised her endlessly.
We shouldn't put on a pedestal people just because they're successful; everyone makes mistakes.

Synset Examples

He was put on a pedestal by the media after his big win.
He tends to put his favorite authors on a pedestal.
Don't put her on a pedestal; she's just human.
We shouldn't put anyone on a pedestal; everyone has flaws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to idolize - to admire or love someone very much
  • Synonym: to look up to - to admire and respect someone
  • Antonym: to disparage - to criticize or speak disrespectfully about someone
