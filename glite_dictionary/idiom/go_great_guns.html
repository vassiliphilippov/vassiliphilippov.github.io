to go great guns

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ɡreɪt ɡʌnz / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɡreɪt ɡʌnz / (BrE)
to proceed with great speed, success, or enthusiasm; to perform exceptionally well or rapidly
perform with great speed or success

Examples

The marketing team is going great guns this quarter, exceeding all their targets.
After a slow start, the construction project began to go great guns in July.
She's going great guns with her new business, attracting lots of customers.

Synset Examples

Once they launch, they will go great guns.
The team is going great guns with the new project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: make rapid progress - to advance quickly or efficiently
  • Antonym: go slowly - to proceed at a slow pace

Etymology

Originally a naval term, referring to a ship firing all guns with full force.
