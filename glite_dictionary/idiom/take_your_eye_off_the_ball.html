to take your eye off the ball

1. VERB

/ teɪk jʊr aɪ ɒf ðə bɔl / (AmE), / teɪk jɔːr aɪ ɒf ðə bɔːl / (BrE)
to lose focus or stop paying attention to what is important, especially in a critical situation
lose focus or attention

Examples

If you take your eye off the ball, you might make a costly mistake at work.
She warned her team not to take their eye off the ball during the final stages of the project.
It's crucial to stay focused and not take your eye off the ball when managing finances.

Synset Examples

Don't take your eye off the ball during the presentation.
It's easy to take your eye off the ball when distractions arise.
He took his eye off the ball and missed the opportunity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: keep your eye on the ball - to remain focused and attentive

Etymology

From sports, particularly ball games, where players must keep their eyes on the ball to perform well.
