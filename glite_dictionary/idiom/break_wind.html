to break wind

1. VERB

/ breɪk wɪnd / (AmE), / breɪk wɪnd / (BrE)
to release gas from the bowels through the anus, often audibly; to pass gas
to pass gas; flatulence

Examples

He laughed so hard that he broke wind in front of everyone.
It's impolite to break wind in public places.
She quickly left the room after she accidentally broke wind during the meeting.

Synset Examples

He tried to hold it in but accidentally broke wind.
She laughed when he broke wind during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fart - to release gas from the bowels audibly

2. VERB

/ breɪk wɪnd / (AmE), / breɪk wɪnd / (BrE)
to release gas from the bowels through the anus; to pass gas or flatulence
to pass gas from the bowels

Examples

Some people feel embarrassed when they break wind in public.
Whenever she eats beans, she tends to break wind more often.
