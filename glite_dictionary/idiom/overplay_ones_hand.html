to overplay one's hand

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊ.vɚˈpleɪ wʌnz hænd / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.vəˈpleɪ wʌnz hænd / (BrE)
to act with excessive confidence or aggressiveness, taking too many risks, and thus undermining one's own position or advantage
act too boldly and lose advantage

Examples

She overplayed her hand in the negotiations and ended up with a worse deal than she started.
When he demanded a higher salary, he overplayed his hand and lost the job offer entirely.
Investors overplayed their hand by expecting too much, and the opportunity slipped away.

Synset Examples

By demanding too much, she overplayed her hand.
He overplayed his hand and lost the deal.
They overplayed their hand and ended up with nothing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to push one's luck - to take more risks than is wise, risking loss of gains
  • Synonym: to overreach - to fail by trying to do or achieve too much

Etymology

From card games, where 'overplay' means to play a card unwisely and 'hand' refers to the cards dealt to a player.
