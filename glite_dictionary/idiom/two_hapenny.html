two ha'penny

1. NOUN

/ tuː heɪˈpɛni / (AmE), / tuː ˈheɪpni / (BrE)
A phrase used to describe something that is a bargain or exceptionally inexpensive.
describes a bargain or very low price
british archaic

Examples

That market stall offers two ha'penny vegetables compared to the supermarket.
He bought the coat for a two ha'penny price during the clearance sale.
Old timers still talk about the days when tea was two ha'penny in the corner café.

Synset Examples

In that shop, you can get two ha'pennies for a penny.

Etymology

From 'ha'penny,' a British colloquial contraction of 'half-penny,' historically a small denomination of UK currency.
