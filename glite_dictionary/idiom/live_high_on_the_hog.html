to live high on the hog

1. VERB

/ lɪv haɪ ɑn ðə hɑɡ / (AmE), / lɪv haɪ ɒn ðə hɒɡ / (BrE)
to live in great comfort, luxury, or extravagance, often with plenty of money or resources
to live in luxury or extravagance

Examples

After landing that big contract, the whole family began living high on the hog with lavish vacations and gourmet meals.
They were living high on the hog for years, until the recession forced them to cut back.
Some celebrities are known for living high on the hog, spending millions on designer clothes and luxury cars.

Synset Examples

They've been living high on the hog since winning the lottery.
After the promotion, he started eating out and living high on the hog.
They were used to living high on the hog during the holidays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: live in luxury - to live in expensive and comfortable conditions
  • Antonym: live hand to mouth - to have only just enough money to live on

Etymology

From the practice of eating the choicest cuts of pork, which come from the upper parts of a hog; American origin, late 19th century.
