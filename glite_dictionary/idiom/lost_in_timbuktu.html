lost in Timbuktu

1. X

/ lɔst ɪn tɪmˈbʌktu / (AmE), / lɒst ɪn tɪmˈbʌktuː / (BrE)
used to describe something or someone that is lost in a faraway, unknown, or imaginary place
lost in a remote or unknown place

Examples

After hours of searching, she felt as if her keys were lost in Timbuktu.
He said that my letter must have gotten lost in Timbuktu because it never arrived.
Whenever my files go missing on my computer, it's like they're lost in Timbuktu.

Synset Examples

He joked that his missing book was lost in Timbuktu.

Etymology

Refers to Timbuktu, a city in Mali, historically seen as a distant and mysterious place in Western imagination.
