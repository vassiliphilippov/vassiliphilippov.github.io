to get back on track

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt bæk ɑn tɹæk / (AmE), / ɡɛt bæk ɒn tɹæk / (BrE)
to return to the correct course of action or to a normal state after a problem or distraction
return to normal or correct course

Examples

After the power outage, the team managed to get back on track and finish the project on time.
I've been distracted lately, but I'm determined to get back on track with my studies.
The company made several changes in order to get back on track financially.

Synset Examples

After a bad trot, the horse finally got back on track.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: get off track - to lose focus or go off the correct path
