to line up one's ducks

1. VERB

/ laɪn ʌp wʌnz dʌks / (AmE), / laɪn ʌp wʌnz dʌks / (BrE)
to organize or arrange things carefully in preparation for something
make thorough preparations

Examples

Before launching the new project, she made sure to line up her ducks.
If you want to apply for the loan, you need to line up your ducks first.
He's been lining up his ducks to ensure the event runs smoothly.

Synset Examples

Before the meeting, I need to line up my ducks.
To succeed, you must line up your ducks before taking action.
He spent the morning lining up his ducks for the presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get one's ducks in a row - to organize things properly; an alternative idiom
