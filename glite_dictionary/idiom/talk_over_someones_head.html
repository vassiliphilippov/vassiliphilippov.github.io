to talk over someone's head

1. VERB

/ tɑk ˈoʊvɚ sʌmˌwʌnz hɛd / (AmE), / tɔːk ˈəʊvə ˈsʌmwʌnz hed / (BrE)
to speak in a way that is too complicated or technical for someone to understand
speak in a way that's too complex to understand

Examples

The engineer started to talk over my head when she explained the advanced technical details.
Sometimes experts unintentionally talk over people's heads at conferences, leaving the audience confused.
Don't talk over her head; she just started learning about this subject.

Synset Examples

The professor often talks over our heads during lectures.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: baffle - to confuse completely; to bewilder
  • Antonym: to simplify - to make less complicated; to make easier to understand
