to ride on someone's coattail

1. VERB

/ raɪd ɑn ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈkoʊt.teɪl / (AmE), / raɪd ɒn ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈkəʊt.teɪl / (BrE)
to achieve success or gain advantages by benefiting from the success or influence of another person, rather than through one's own efforts
gain success by benefiting from another's achievements

Examples

Many people believed the politician had simply ridden on his predecessor's coattails to win the election.
Rather than working hard himself, he tried to ride on his teammate's coattails to get a promotion.
The band enjoyed fame for years by riding on the coattails of their popular lead singer.

Synset Examples

He rode on her coattail to achieve success.
She was happy to ride the coattail of the popular trend.
It’s not fair to ride the coattails of someone else’s hard work.
He managed to ride the coattails of his successful friend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piggyback - to use something that someone else has done in order to get an advantage
  • Antonym: to stand on one's own two feet - to be independent and not rely on others for success

Etymology

From the practice of following closely behind a successful or prominent person, figuratively clutching at the back of their coat for personal gain.
