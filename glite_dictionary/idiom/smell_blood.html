to smell blood

1. VERB

/ smɛl blʌd / (AmE), / smɛl blʌd / (BrE)
to sense a weakness or opportunity for success, often prompting aggressive or determined action
sense weakness or opportunity for success

Examples

The opposition party could smell blood after the government made a major mistake.
Once the lawyers smelled blood in the case, they pursued it relentlessly.
The team smelled blood when they saw their rivals struggling and quickly took the lead.

Synset Examples

The hunter could smell blood after the chase.
He sensed weakness and could smell blood in the competition.
Once they smelled blood, they went after the target.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: move in for the kill - to take decisive action after sensing weakness
