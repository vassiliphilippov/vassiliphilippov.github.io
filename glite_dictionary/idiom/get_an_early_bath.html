to get an early bath

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ən ˈɜrli bæθ / (AmE), / ɡɛt ən ˈɜːli bɑːθ / (BrE)
to be forced to leave or resign early as a result of failure, mistake, or punishment
leave or resign early due to failure or punishment
british

Examples

After his mistake during the match, the coach told him to get an early bath.
If you keep breaking the rules, you'll soon get an early bath.
The manager had to get an early bath following the failed project.

Synset Examples

She knew she’d get an early bath for that mistake.
He took an early bath after his poor performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be sent off - to be dismissed from play in a sport for breaking the rules

Etymology

From British sports, where a player sent off the field would shower (have a bath) before the game ended.
