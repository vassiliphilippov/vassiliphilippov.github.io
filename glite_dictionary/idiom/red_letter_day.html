red letter day

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛd ˈlɛt̬ɚ ˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈrɛd ˈlɛtə ˌdeɪ / (BrE)
A day that is especially significant, memorable, or joyful.
an especially memorable or significant day

Examples

Winning the award made it a red letter day for her and her family.
Their team’s victory in the championship was truly a red letter day at the school.
The launch of the new product was a red letter day in the company’s history.

Synset Examples

Her promotion was a red letter day for the family.
Graduation day was a red letter day in their lives.
His wedding was a red letter day filled with joy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milestone - a significant event or stage in life, progress, or development

Etymology

From the practice of marking special days in red on calendars, originating from medieval church calendars where feast days were written in red ink.
