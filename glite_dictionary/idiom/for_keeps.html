for keeps

1. ADV

/ fər kiːps / (AmE), / fə kiːps / (BrE)
(informal) In a way that means the winner will keep what is won permanently; not for practice or temporarily.
winner keeps what is won permanently
informal

Examples

When we play marbles for keeps, you get to keep the ones you win.
Be careful—this card game is for keeps and not just for fun.
Once the decision is made, it’s for keeps; there’s no going back.

Synset Examples

they played for keeps

2. ADV

/ fər kiːps / (AmE), / fə kiːps / (BrE)
permanently; for good; in a way that is meant to last or not be reversed
permanently; for good

Examples

The necklace was a gift, so she got to keep it for keeps.
They planned to move into the new house for keeps this time.
After years of renting, they finally bought a car for keeps.

Synset Examples
