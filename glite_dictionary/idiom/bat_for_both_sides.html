to bat for both sides

1. VERB

/ bæt fər boʊθ saɪdz / (AmE), / bæt fə bəʊθ saɪdz / (BrE)
to support, sympathize with, or act in favor of both parties or opposing sides in a discussion or conflict, often to avoid commitment or maintain neutrality.
support both opposing parties or sides

Examples

In business negotiations, she tries to bat for both sides to avoid offending anyone.
If you keep batting for both sides, people might start to question your loyalty.
During the heated debate, Mark was accused of batting for both sides to keep his options open.

Synset Examples

She’s known to bat for both sides to keep peace.
Don't bat for both sides or you'll lose trust.
He tends to bat for both sides in the argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sit on the fence - to avoid making a decision or choosing a side
