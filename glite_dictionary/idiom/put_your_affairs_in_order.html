to put your affairs in order

1. VERB

/ pʊt jʊr əˈfɛrz ɪn ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / pʊt jɔː əˈfeəz ɪn ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
to organize your personal, financial, and legal matters in preparation for a change, such as illness or death
organize personal matters, esp. before a crisis

Examples

Before her surgery, she decided to put her affairs in order just in case.
It is important to put your affairs in order when planning for retirement.
The lawyer advised him to put his affairs in order after receiving the diagnosis.

Synset Examples

It's wise to put your affairs in order as you age.
