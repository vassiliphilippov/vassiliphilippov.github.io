to take the front seat

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə frʌnt siːt / (AmE), / teɪk ðə frʌnt siːt / (BrE)
to assume a leading or prominent role in an activity or situation
assume a leading role

Examples

As the new team leader, she decided to take the front seat and set the agenda.
When urgent issues arise, it’s important for someone to take the front seat and direct efforts.
Michael rarely likes to take the front seat, but he volunteered to lead the committee this year.

Synset Examples

In this project, we need to take the front seat and drive change.
As the project manager, she will take the front seat in this meeting.
He prefers to let others take the front seat during discussions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: take the back seat - to allow others to assume control or prominence
