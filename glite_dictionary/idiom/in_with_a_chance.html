in with a chance

1. ADJ

/ ɪn wɪð ə tʃæns / (AmE), / ɪn wɪð ə tʃɑːns / (BrE)
having a genuine possibility of succeeding or achieving something
having a possibility of success
british

Examples

If we score one more goal, we're in with a chance of winning the match.
She knew she was in with a chance after the shortlist was announced.
Anyone who enters the contest is in with a chance of taking home the prize.

Synset Examples

He felt he was in with a chance for the promotion.
She thinks she's in with a chance of winning.
You're in with a chance if you apply early.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: have a shot - to have an opportunity to try or succeed at something
