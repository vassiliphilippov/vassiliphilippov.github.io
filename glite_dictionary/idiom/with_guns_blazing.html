with guns blazing

1. ADV

/ wɪð ɡʌnz ˈbleɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / wɪð ɡʌnz ˈbleɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
in a very aggressive, determined, or enthusiastic way, often in an effort to achieve something or confront someone
aggressively or enthusiastically

Examples

She defended her proposal with guns blazing during the meeting.
The new coach started the season with guns blazing, making drastic changes to the team.
Whenever he faces a tough challenge, he goes in with guns blazing.

Synset Examples

They entered the meeting room with guns blazing, ready for a fight.
He tackled the project with guns blazing, eager to impress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: full throttle - at maximum speed or intensity
  • Antonym: to hold back - to restrain or act cautiously

Etymology

Refers to the imagery of shooting firearms aggressively; originated from Western films and stories.
