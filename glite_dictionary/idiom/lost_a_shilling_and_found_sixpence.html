lost a shilling and found sixpence

1. X

/ lɔst ə ˈʃɪlɪŋ ənd faʊnd ˈsɪkspəns / (AmE), / lɒst ə ˈʃɪlɪŋ ənd faʊnd ˈsɪkspəns / (BrE)
to look or feel unexpectedly pleased, but not entirely happy, after an initial disappointment; to seem modestly satisfied after a small gain compared to a bigger loss
appear mildly pleased after disappointment
british

Examples

After being reassigned to a smaller office, he looked as if he had lost a shilling and found sixpence.
When she received a lower prize than expected, her expression was like she had lost a shilling and found sixpence.
He smiled, but it was the smile of someone who'd lost a shilling and found sixpence.

Synset Examples

After receiving the award, she looked as if she had lost a shilling and found sixpence.

He looked as if he had lost a shilling and found sixpence after winning the lottery.
He arrived at the party looking as if he had lost a shilling and found sixpence.
When he heard the good news, he looked as if he had lost a shilling and found sixpence.

Etymology

Refers to British pre-decimal currency, where a shilling was worth more than a sixpence; losing one and finding the other results in a net loss but with some consolation.
