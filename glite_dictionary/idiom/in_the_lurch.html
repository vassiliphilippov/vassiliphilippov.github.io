in the lurch

1. ADV

/ ɪn ðə lɝtʃ / (AmE), / ɪn ðə lɜːtʃ / (BrE)
in a difficult or vulnerable situation, especially when abandoned by others at a crucial time
abandoned in a difficult situation

Examples

After the project failed, many employees felt they had been left in the lurch.
She promised to help but disappeared, leaving me in the lurch at the last minute.
When the manager quit unexpectedly, the staff was suddenly in the lurch.

Synset Examples

he resigned and left me in the lurch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to let down - to fail to support or help someone as expected
  • Synonym: leave high and dry - to abandon someone in a difficult situation

Etymology

Likely from the old game 'lurch' in cribbage or backgammon, meaning to leave far behind.

2. X

/ ɪn ðə lɜrtʃ / (AmE), / ɪn ðə lɜːtʃ / (BrE)
left in an awkward or difficult situation without help or support, especially unexpectedly or unfairly.
left in a difficult or awkward situation

Examples

When her ride didn’t show up, she was left in the lurch with no way to get home.
Many employees felt they were in the lurch after the sudden layoffs.
His partner quit at the last minute, leaving him in the lurch for the big presentation.

Synset Examples

She had to cancel, leaving him in the lurch for dinner.
They forgot to notify him, leaving him in the lurch.
He promised to help but left me in the lurch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abandon - to leave someone or something behind, often in a difficult situation
  • Synonym: to let down - to fail to support or help someone as expected

Etymology

Possibly from the French "lourche," a 16th-century game where a loser was said to be "in the lurch" if left far behind.
