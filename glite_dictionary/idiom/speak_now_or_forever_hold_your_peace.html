speak now or forever hold your peace

1. X

/ spiːk naʊ ɔr fəˈrɛvɚ hoʊld jʊr piːs / (AmE), / spiːk naʊ ɔː fəˈrɛvə həʊld jɔː piːs / (BrE)
used to tell people that if they have any objections, they should state them now or remain silent about the matter forever
express objections before it is too late

Examples

At the end of the meeting, the chairperson said, 'speak now or forever hold your peace.'
This is your opportunity to bring up any concerns—speak now or forever hold your peace.
If no one objects, I'll proceed; so speak now or forever hold your peace.

Synset Examples

At the wedding, the officiant said, 'Speak now or forever hold your peace.'
This is your chance: speak now or forever hold your peace.
You can voice your concerns: speak now or forever hold your peace.
