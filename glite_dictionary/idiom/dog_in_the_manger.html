dog in the manger

1. NOUN

/ dɔɡ ɪn ðə ˈmeɪndʒɚ / (AmE), / dɒɡ ɪn ðə ˈmeɪndʒə / (BrE)
a person who prevents others from using or enjoying something even though they themselves have no use for it
someone who spitefully denies others enjoyment

Examples

His refusal to sell the unused equipment is a classic case of a dog in the manger attitude.
She acts like a dog in the manger, refusing to let anyone else use the space she never needs.
Don't be a dog in the manger; let others enjoy what you don't want.

Etymology

From the fable of the dog in the manger, in which a dog occupies a straw-filled manger and refuses to allow the oxen to eat, though it cannot eat straw itself.

2. NOUN

/ dɔg ɪn ðə ˈmeɪndʒɚ / (AmE), / dɒɡ ɪn ðə ˈmeɪndʒə / (BrE)
a person who selfishly prevents others from enjoying or using something they do not need or cannot use themselves
someone who denies others enjoyment out of spite

Examples

Don't be a dog in the manger—if you can't use the tickets, let someone else have them.
She acted like a dog in the manger by refusing to lend the books she never reads.
His dog in the manger attitude ruined the team's chance to use the new equipment.

Synset Examples

Don't be a dog in the manger; let others share the resources.
He’s a dog in the manger, blocking others from using the equipment.
Her behavior at the meeting was very dog in the manger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoilsport - a person who spoils other people's enjoyment

Etymology

From Aesop’s fable of the dog in the manger, in which a dog stops oxen from eating hay it does not want.
