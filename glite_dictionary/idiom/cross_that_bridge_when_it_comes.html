to cross that bridge when it comes

1. VERB

/ krɔs ðæt brɪdʒ wɛn ɪt kʌmz / (AmE), / krɒs ðæt brɪdʒ wɛn ɪt kʌmz / (BrE)
to deal with a problem or situation only when it happens, rather than worrying about it in advance
deal with a problem when it arises

Examples

She prefers to cross that bridge when it comes, rather than stress now.
There's no point in worrying—let's just cross that bridge when it comes.
Whenever faced with uncertainty, he likes to cross that bridge when it comes.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
