deep water

1. NOUN

/ dip ˈwɔt̬ɚ / (AmE), / diːp ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
A situation involving serious trouble or difficulty.
serious trouble; difficulty

Examples

After missing so many deadlines, the company found itself in deep water with its clients.
He realized he was in deep water when the police started asking questions.
If we don’t finish this project on time, we’ll be in deep water for sure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hot water - trouble or difficulty, especially involving punishment or criticism
  • Antonym: out of the woods - out of danger or difficulty
