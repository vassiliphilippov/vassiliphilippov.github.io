to make hay

1. VERB

/ meɪk heɪ / (AmE), / meɪk heɪ / (BrE)
to take advantage of an opportunity, especially for one's own benefit
take advantage of an opportunity

Examples

They tried to make hay of the CEO's misstatement to promote their own agenda.
Analysts are expected to make hay out of any market fluctuations.
Politicians often make hay with any issue that grabs public attention.

Synset Examples

The environmentalist lobby made hay of the nuclear plant accident

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to capitalize - to take advantage of; to turn something to one's gain
  • Synonym: exploit - to make full use of and derive benefit from

Etymology

From the proverb 'make hay while the sun shines', meaning to act while conditions are favorable.

2. VERB

/ meɪk heɪ / (AmE), / meɪk heɪ / (BrE)
to take advantage of an opportunity while conditions are favorable
take advantage of an opportunity

Examples

Entrepreneurs should make hay when market conditions are in their favor.
With everyone distracted, he decided to make hay and secure the best deal.
The team plans to make hay while their competitors are still recovering from setbacks.

Synset Examples

They need to make hay before the opportunity passes.
She plans to make hay during the summer sales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to seize the opportunity - to act quickly to take advantage of an opportunity

Etymology

From the full proverb 'make hay while the sun shines,' emphasizing acting when conditions are right.
