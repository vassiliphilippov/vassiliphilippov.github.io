to quench one's thirst

1. VERB

/ kwɛntʃ wʌnz θɝst / (AmE), / kwentʃ wʌnz θɜːst / (BrE)
to drink until one is no longer thirsty; to satisfy one's need for liquid refreshment.
satisfy one's need to drink

Examples

After hours of hiking in the sun, he finally quenched his thirst at a mountain stream.
A glass of icy water can quickly quench one's thirst on a hot day.
She reached for a bottle of juice to quench her thirst after her workout.

Synset Examples

He quenched his thirst with a cold lemonade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slake one's thirst - to satisfy thirst by drinking
