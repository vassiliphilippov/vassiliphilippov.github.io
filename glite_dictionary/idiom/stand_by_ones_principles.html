to stand by one's principles

1. VERB

/ stænd baɪ wʌnz ˈprɪnsəpəlz / (AmE), / stænd baɪ wʌnz ˈprɪnsɪplz / (BrE)
to continue to act according to one's moral or ethical beliefs, even when it is difficult or unpopular
remain loyal to one's beliefs

Examples

Even under intense criticism, she continued to stand by her principles.
It's not always easy to stand by your principles in the workplace.
They admire him because he has always stood by his principles no matter what.

Synset Examples

He chose to stand by his principles, despite the pressure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compromise - to make concessions that go against one's beliefs
