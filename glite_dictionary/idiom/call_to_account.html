to call to account

1. VERB

/ kɔl tə əˈkaʊnt / (AmE), / kɔːl tuː əˈkaʊnt / (BrE)
to require someone to explain or accept responsibility for their actions or decisions
demand explanation or responsibility

Examples

The board decided to call to account those responsible for the financial losses.
After repeated complaints, the council was finally called to account by the community.
If mistakes are made, it's the supervisor's job to call to account the people involved.

Synset Examples

The manager will call him to account for his mistakes.
It's time to call the company to account for their actions.
They must call the leaders to account for their decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hold accountable - to consider someone responsible for their actions
  • Antonym: to let off the hook - to allow someone to avoid responsibility or blame
