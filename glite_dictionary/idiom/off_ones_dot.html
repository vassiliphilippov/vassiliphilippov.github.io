off one's dot

1. ADJ

/ ɑf wʌnz dɑt / (AmE), / ɒf wʌnz dɒt / (BrE)
behaving abnormally, as if slightly crazy or not in full control of oneself
slightly crazy; not acting normally
informal british

Examples

After hearing his bizarre plan, I thought he must be off his dot.
She’s been acting off her dot ever since she returned from vacation.
Some people claim he’s always off his dot, but I find him charmingly eccentric.

Synset Examples

He’s been acting off his dot since the project began.
I think he’s a bit off his dot today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: off one's rocker - mentally unstable; slightly crazy
  • Synonym: off one's head - not thinking or behaving normally

2. ADJ

/ ɑf wʌnz dɑt / (AmE), / ɒf wʌnz dɒt / (BrE)
behaving oddly or not entirely rational; slightly crazy or eccentric
slightly crazy or eccentric (British idiom)
informal british

Examples

After the news broke, everyone thought he was off his dot.
She started wearing pajamas to work, and people said she was off her dot.
Don't listen to him, he's just off his dot today.

Synset Examples

After that last meeting, she seemed off her dot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mad - insane or crazy
  • Synonym: crazy - mentally deranged or extremely odd
  • Antonym: sane - mentally healthy and rational
