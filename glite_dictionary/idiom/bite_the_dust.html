to bite the dust

1. VERB

/ baɪt ðə dʌst / (AmE), / baɪt ðə dʌst / (BrE)
to fail, break down, or stop working completely; to cease to exist or function
fail, break down, or cease to exist

Examples

After years of faithful service, my washing machine finally bit the dust last night.
Several startups bit the dust during the financial crisis.
His dream of becoming an actor bit the dust when the show was cancelled.

Synset Examples

Many businesses bit the dust during the pandemic.
Unfortunately, our old car finally bit the dust.
The laptop bit the dust after a year of heavy use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fail - to be unsuccessful or not work as intended
  • Synonym: to break down - to stop functioning, especially of machines or devices

Etymology

Originally from the literal imagery of falling to the ground in defeat, as in battle.
