legend in one's own lunchtime

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛdʒənd ɪn wʌnz oʊn ˈlʌntʃˌtaɪm / (AmE), / ˈlɛdʒənd ɪn wʌnz əʊn ˈlʌntʃtaɪm / (BrE)
Someone who believes they are important or admired, but whose reputation is limited or not recognized by others.
person important in their own eyes only
british informal

Examples

She thinks she's a legend in her own lunchtime because everyone in the office knows her name.
Don't be a legend in your own lunchtime; try to stay humble about your achievements.
He walks around like a legend in his own lunchtime but barely anyone outside this village recognizes him.

Synset Examples

She thinks she's a legend in her own lunchtime with her cooking skills.
He acts like a legend in his own lunchtime, but he's just average.
Being a legend in your own lunchtime doesn't impress anyone else.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legend in one's own lifetime - someone famous or admired during their lifetime

Etymology

British English humorous formation (1960s); a play on 'legend in one's own lifetime'.
