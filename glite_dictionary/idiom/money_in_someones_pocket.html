money in someone's pocket

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌni ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈpɑkɪt / (AmE), / ˈmʌni ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈpɒkɪt / (BrE)
having financial resources or disposable income; available money to spend
having disposable income

Examples

After getting her bonus, she finally had some money in her pocket to treat herself.
Lower utility bills mean more money in your pocket every month.
Winning the competition put extra money in his pocket before the holidays.

Synset Examples

Having a good job means money in my pocket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disposable income - money remaining for personal use after taxes and necessities
