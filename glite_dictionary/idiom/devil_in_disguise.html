devil in disguise

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛv(ə)l ɪn dɪsˈɡaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdɛv(ə)l ɪn dɪsˈɡaɪz / (BrE)
A person or thing that appears friendly or harmless but is actually dangerous or evil.
person or thing that seems good but is actually evil

Examples

He seemed helpful at first, but he turned out to be a devil in disguise with selfish motives.
Sometimes, a scammer can act like your friend but is actually a devil in disguise.
That politician pretends to care about the people, but many see him as a devil in disguise.

Synset Examples

Beware of the devil in disguise lurking in the alley.
That charming salesman is a devil in disguise.
She thought he was kind, but he was a devil in disguise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wolf in sheep's clothing - person who hides malicious intent under a benign appearance
