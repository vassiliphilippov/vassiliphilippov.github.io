worst of both worlds

1. NOUN

/ wɝst əv boʊθ wɝldz / (AmE), / wɜːst əv bəʊθ wɜːldz / (BrE)
A situation in which a person experiences the disadvantages or negative aspects of two different choices or situations, without the benefits of either.
disadvantages of two alternatives

Examples

Working part-time and commuting long distances gave her the worst of both worlds.
This compromise leaves us with the worst of both worlds: high costs and poor results.
Renting in the suburbs can mean the worst of both worlds—neither city excitement nor rural peace.

Synset Examples

Living in the city gives you the worst of both worlds.
This job offers the worst of both worlds: stress and low pay.
Choosing that option will give you the worst of both worlds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: best of both worlds - A situation where someone enjoys the advantages of two alternatives
