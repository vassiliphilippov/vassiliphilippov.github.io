glass house

1. NOUN

/ ɡlæs haʊs / (AmE), / ɡlɑːs haʊs / (BrE)
a situation in which someone is vulnerable to criticism because of their own faults or shortcomings
state of being open to criticism

Examples

If you constantly criticize others, remember you may be living in a glass house.
As a public figure, she knows she's in a glass house and must be careful about her actions.
The CEO realized he was in a glass house after the media investigated his own past mistakes.

Synset Examples

Don't throw stones if you live in a glass house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to throw stones - to criticize others, especially when one is also at fault

Etymology

From the literal image of a house made of glass, easily shattered, used metaphorically since the 17th century.
