to left the building

1. VERB

/ lɛft ðə ˈbɪldɪŋ / (AmE), / lɛft ðə ˈbɪldɪŋ / (BrE)
used to indicate that someone has departed or exited a place, often unexpectedly or finally
departed a place; has gone

Examples

Once the star has left the building, the fans start to disperse.
By the time security arrived, the suspect had already left the building.
The boss left the building without saying goodbye to anyone.

Synset Examples

The speaker has left the building, but questions remain.
After the presentation, he has left the building quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depart - to leave, especially to go on a journey
  • Antonym: to arrive - to reach a place, especially at the end of a journey

Etymology

Popularized by the phrase 'Elvis has left the building', originally used by announcers at Elvis Presley concerts in the 1950s–60s.
