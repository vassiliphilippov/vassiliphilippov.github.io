sub rosa

1. ADV

/ sʌb ˈroʊzə / (AmE), / sʌb ˈrəʊzə / (BrE)
In secret; in a confidential or covert manner.
in secret; covertly

Examples

The committee met sub rosa to discuss sensitive issues before the public meeting.
All negotiations between the two companies were conducted sub rosa to protect their interests.
She passed the information sub rosa to avoid drawing any attention.

Synset Examples

held a meeting sub rosa to avoid general criticism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secretly - in a secret manner; covertly
  • Antonym: openly - in a manner that is not secret; publicly

Etymology

From Latin 'sub rosa' (under the rose), where the rose was a symbol of secrecy.
