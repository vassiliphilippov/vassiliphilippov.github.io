to swallow a bug

1. VERB

/ ˈswɑːloʊ ə bʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈswɒləʊ ə bʌɡ / (BrE)
to appear surprised or shocked, often with displeasure or discomfort, as if reacting to something unpleasant
look surprised or displeased

Examples

When the manager announced pay cuts, everyone in the room suddenly swallowed a bug.
She looked like she had swallowed a bug when she saw her exam results.
He swallowed a bug after hearing the unexpected news.

Synset Examples

When he got the ticket, he looked like he swallowed a bug.
She always looks like she swallowed a bug when she’s upset.
He walked in looking like he swallowed a bug after hearing the news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: look taken aback - to look surprised or startled
  • Antonym: look delighted - to look very pleased or happy
