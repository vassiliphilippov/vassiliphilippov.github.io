as bleak as Mordor

1. ADJ

/ əz blik əz ˈmɔrdɔr / (AmE), / əz bliːk əz ˈmɔːdɔː / (BrE)
Extremely desolate or dreary, evoking the barren and forbidding imagery of Mordor in Tolkien's works.
extremely desolate or dreary (Tolkien reference)

Examples

After the storm, the field looked as bleak as Mordor under the gray sky.
His expression was as bleak as Mordor when he heard the bad news.
On winter mornings, the empty playground felt as bleak as Mordor to the children.

Synset Examples

The landscape was as bleak as Mordor itself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleak - cold, bare, and often depressing or hopeless

Etymology

Reference to Mordor, the fictional land of darkness in J.R.R. Tolkien's 'The Lord of the Rings'.
