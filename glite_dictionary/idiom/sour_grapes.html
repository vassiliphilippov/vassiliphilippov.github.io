sour grapes

1. NOUN

/ saʊɚ ɡreɪps / (AmE), / saʊə ɡreɪps / (BrE)
criticism or dismissal of something that one cannot have or achieve, especially as a way of justifying one's disappointment
dismissing what one can't have

Examples

After she didn't get the promotion, her comments about the job were just sour grapes.
His criticism of the winner sounds like sour grapes to me.
She said she never wanted the award anyway, but that's probably just sour grapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rationalize - to attempt to justify or make excuses for something
  • Synonym: envy - a feeling of discontent or resentment for what someone else has or achieves

Etymology

From Aesop's fable "The Fox and the Grapes", in which a fox, unable to reach some grapes, claims they are probably sour anyway.
