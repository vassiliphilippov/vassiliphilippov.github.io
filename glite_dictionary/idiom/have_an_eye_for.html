to have an eye for

1. VERB

/ hæv ən aɪ fər / (AmE), / hæv ən aɪ fə / (BrE)
to have a natural ability to notice, recognize, or appreciate something, especially subtle details or qualities
to notice or appreciate details well

Examples

She really has an eye for interior decoration and always chooses beautiful colors.
Recruiters must have an eye for potential in young athletes.
He has an eye for detail, which makes him an excellent editor.

Synset Examples

He has an eye for fashion and design.
You need to have an eye for talent in this industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talent - natural aptitude or skill
  • Synonym: knack - an acquired or natural skill at performing a task
