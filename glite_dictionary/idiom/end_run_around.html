end run around

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛnd ˈrʌn əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈɛnd ˈrʌn əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
an indirect or evasive action designed to avoid a problem, rule, or obstacle
an evasive action to avoid a problem

Examples

The company tried to make an end run around the new regulations by exploiting a loophole.
He made an end run around his manager to get approval from the director.
By coordinating with another department, she pulled off an end run around the budget restrictions.

Synset Examples

She executed an end run around the problem flawlessly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to circumvent - to find a way around an obstacle or rule
  • Antonym: to confront - to face a problem directly rather than avoid it

Etymology

Originally from American football, where an "end run" involves running around the end of the defensive line.
