to tighten the purse strings

1. VERB

/ ˈtaɪtn ðə ˈpɜrs ˌstrɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtn ðə ˈpɜːs ˌstrɪŋz / (BrE)
to reduce spending or impose stricter control over financial resources
impose stricter control on spending

Examples

With the economic downturn, the government had to tighten the purse strings across all departments.
After overspending last year, the family decided to tighten the purse strings and save more.
When donations dropped, the charity was forced to tighten the purse strings on new projects.

Synset Examples

It's time to tighten the purse strings for the project.
The board voted to tighten the purse strings on spending.
They decided to tighten the purse strings this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cut back - to reduce expenses or spending
  • Antonym: to loosen the purse strings - to allow more spending; reduce financial restrictions

Etymology

From the literal sense of 'purse strings,' the drawstrings that close a traditional purse, metaphorically indicating financial control.
