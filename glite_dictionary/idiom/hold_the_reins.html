to hold the reins

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ðə reɪnz / (AmE), / həʊld ðə reɪnz / (BrE)
to be in control of or to manage a situation, organization, or activity
to be in control or manage

Examples

After the CEO retired, Maria was chosen to hold the reins of the company.
When they moved abroad, Grandpa continued to hold the reins of the family business from afar.
If you want to succeed, you must be ready to hold the reins during difficult times.

Synset Examples

She needs to hold the reins of the project.
It's time for you to hold the reins and lead.
He likes to hold the reins in group activities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take the reins - to take control or assume management
  • Synonym: be in charge - to have control or responsibility

Etymology

From the literal use of 'reins' to guide or control a horse, extended figuratively to mean managing anything.
