old hand

1. NOUN

/ oʊld hænd / (AmE), / əʊld hænd / (BrE)
a person who has a lot of experience in a particular activity or job
experienced person, expert

Examples

As an old hand at negotiations, she quickly found a solution that satisfied everyone.
The company trusts him because he is an old hand in the industry.
It takes an old hand to manage such a difficult project smoothly.

Synset Examples

As an old hand at the game, he knew all the tricks.
Being an old hand, he helped the newcomers settle in quickly.
She’s an old hand in the kitchen, always creating delicious meals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: veteran - a person with long experience in a particular field
  • Antonym: novice - a person who is new and inexperienced in a field or activity
