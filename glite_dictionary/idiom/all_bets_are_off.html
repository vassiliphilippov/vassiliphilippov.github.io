all bets are off

1. X

/ ɔl bɛts ɑr ɑf / (AmE), / ɔːl bets ɑːr ɒf / (BrE)
used to indicate that a situation has become unpredictable or that previous agreements, assumptions, or expectations no longer apply
situation now unpredictable

Examples

If the merger falls through, all bets are off regarding the company's future.
As soon as the main competitor entered the market, all bets are off for who will win.
If they change the schedule, then all bets are off for our travel plans.

Synset Examples

If it rains tomorrow, all bets are off for the picnic.
If the rules change, then all bets are off.
Once he found out the truth, all bets were off.

Etymology

From the world of gambling, where all bets are declared invalid when conditions change.
