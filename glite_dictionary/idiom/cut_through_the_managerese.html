to cut through the managerese

1. VERB

/ kʌt θru ðə ˌmænɪdʒəˈriz / (AmE), / kʌt θruː ðə ˌmænɪdʒəˈriːz / (BrE)
to make something clearer by removing management jargon or unnecessarily complex language
simplify language full of management jargon

Examples

When presenting to the team, try to cut through the managerese and use straightforward terms.
We need someone who can cut through the managerese to explain what this policy actually means.
The consultant helped us cut through the managerese in the report so everyone could understand it.
