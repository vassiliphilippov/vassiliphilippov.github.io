to rise like a phoenix

1. VERB

/ raɪz laɪk ə ˈfiː.nɪks / (AmE), / raɪz laɪk ə ˈfiː.nɪks / (BrE)
to recover or emerge renewed from a difficult situation, like the mythical phoenix bird that is reborn from its ashes
emerge renewed after difficulty

Examples

After the fire destroyed their home, the family managed to rise like a phoenix and rebuild their lives.
The company struggled for years but finally began to rise like a phoenix from bankruptcy.
Despite the criticism, she continued to rise like a phoenix, proving her doubters wrong.

Synset Examples

She rose like a phoenix from the ashes after her setback.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bounce back - to quickly recover from a setback or defeat

Etymology

From the myth of the phoenix, a legendary bird in ancient mythology that rises from its own ashes after death.
