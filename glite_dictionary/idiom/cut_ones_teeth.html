to cut one's teeth

1. VERB

/ kʌt wʌnz tiθ / (AmE), / kʌt wʌnz tiːθ / (BrE)
to gain one's first experience or develop initial skills in a particular activity or field, often early in one's career
gain initial experience or skills

Examples

She cut her teeth in journalism at a small local newspaper.
Many great chefs cut their teeth in busy restaurant kitchens before becoming famous.
He cut his teeth as a graphic designer by doing freelance work for startups.

Synset Examples

She cut her teeth in the industry by working at a startup.
He cut his teeth as a writer by blogging regularly.
Many entrepreneurs cut their teeth on small projects before launching big ones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get started - to begin doing something, especially a new activity or job
  • Synonym: to break in - to gain initial experience in a particular job or activity

Etymology

Originally alluding to the literal process of a baby's teeth emerging, which marks an important stage of early experience.
