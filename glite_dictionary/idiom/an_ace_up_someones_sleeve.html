an ace up someone's sleeve

1. NOUN

/ ən eɪs ʌp ˈsʌmwʌnz sliːv / (AmE), / ən eɪs ʌp ˈsʌmwənz sliːv / (BrE)
a secret advantage, resource, or strategy kept in reserve until needed, especially to gain an advantage over others
secret or hidden advantage

Examples

The negotiator always seemed confident, as if he had an ace up his sleeve.
Despite being behind in the debate, she revealed an ace up her sleeve at the perfect moment.
Investors suspected the company had an ace up its sleeve regarding the new product launch.

Synset Examples

He had an ace up his sleeve for the final exam.
I knew he had a trick up his sleeve for this challenge.
She had a trick up her sleeve to win the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: a trick up one's sleeve - another idiom meaning to have a secret plan or resource

Etymology

Alludes to cheating at cards, where a card is secretly hidden up one’s sleeve.
