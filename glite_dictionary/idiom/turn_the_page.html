to turn the page

1. VERB

/ tɝn ðə peɪdʒ / (AmE), / tɜːn ðə peɪdʒ / (BrE)
to move on to the next stage of something, either literally (such as reading a book) or figuratively (to leave a past event or experience behind and begin anew)
to move on from something or start anew

Examples

After the scandal, the company worked hard to turn the page and restore its reputation.
When you are ready, simply turn the page to start the next chapter.
He realized he needed to turn the page and focus on the future instead of the past.

Synset Examples

She had to turn the page to continue reading.
After the breakup, he needed to turn the page quickly.
She decided to turn the page and start anew.
It's time to turn the page on this chapter.
Please turn the page to continue reading the story.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to move on - to progress to a new situation or activity
  • Synonym: to let go - to stop worrying about or being upset by something
