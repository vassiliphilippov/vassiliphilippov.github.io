to wait for one's ship to come in

1. VERB

/ weɪt fər wʌnz ʃɪp tə kʌm ɪn / (AmE), / weɪt fə wʌnz ʃɪp tə kʌm ɪn / (BrE)
to wait patiently for a significant opportunity, success, or financial gain to arrive
anticipate a future windfall or success

Examples

After years of hard work, she was still waiting for her ship to come in.
He spends every day at the racetrack, waiting for his ship to come in.
Most people never get rich just by waiting for their ship to come in; they make their own luck.

Synset Examples

She’s been patient, waiting for her ship to come in.

Etymology

Alludes to investors or merchants awaiting the arrival of a ship carrying profitable cargo in the 18th–19th centuries.
