living high cotton

1. X

/ ˈlɪvɪŋ haɪ ˈkɑtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈlɪvɪŋ haɪ ˈkɒtn̩ / (BrE)
Enjoying a life of great comfort, luxury, or prosperity, often after a period of hardship.
living in luxury or comfort
informal american

Examples

After landing the new job, they've been living high cotton and enjoying every moment.
Back when the business was booming, the family was really living high cotton.
He likes to remember the days when they were living high cotton before the market crashed.

Synset Examples

Now that they’ve succeeded, they’re living high cotton.
They've been living high cotton since the merger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: living high on the hog - living in luxury or having plenty of money

Etymology

From Southern American English, relating to years when the cotton crop was tall and bountiful, indicating prosperity.
