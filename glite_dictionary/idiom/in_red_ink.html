in red ink

1. ADV

/ ɪn rɛd ɪŋk / (AmE), / ɪn rɛd ɪŋk / (BrE)
(of a business or account) operating at a loss or in debt; losing money.
operating at a financial loss

Examples

After the costly repairs, our family budget was in red ink for several months.
If the company stays in red ink, it may have to close some stores.
Despite good sales, new investments left their accounts in red ink this quarter.

Synset Examples

The company ended the year in red ink again.
After the holidays, our accounts were in red ink.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: in the black - operating at a profit; making money

Etymology

From the practice of using red ink to record financial losses in accounting.
