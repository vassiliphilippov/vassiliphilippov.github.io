to take at face value

1. VERB

/ teɪk ət feɪs ˈvælju / (AmE), / teɪk ət feɪs ˈvæljuː / (BrE)
to accept something as true or genuine without questioning or doubting it
accept without questioning

Examples

You can't always take at face value what you read on the internet.
She tends to take at face value what her boss tells her, even when others are skeptical.
When he made that promise, I decided not to take it at face value.

Synset Examples

It's wise to verify facts rather than take them at face value.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: question - to doubt or challenge the truth of something
  • Synonym: to believe - to accept something as true
