to lose one's shirt

1. VERB

/ luːz wʌnz ʃɝːt / (AmE), / luːz wʌnz ʃɜːt / (BrE)
to lose all or almost all of one's money, especially as a result of a bad investment or risky venture
lose all one's money

Examples

If you invest recklessly in the stock market, you might lose your shirt.
Many gamblers lose their shirts after a night at the casino.
He lost his shirt when his business collapsed.

Synset Examples

She warned him not to lose his shirt at the casino.
If you gamble too much, you might lose your shirt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go bankrupt - to become unable to pay debts; insolvency

2. VERB

/ luːz wʌnz ʃɜrt / (AmE), / luːz wʌnz ʃɜːt / (BrE)
to suffer a large financial loss, often by making a bad investment or risky decision
suffer heavy financial loss

Examples

He invested everything in the company and lost his shirt when it went bankrupt.
Many people lose their shirts at the casino hoping for big wins.
She almost lost her shirt on that risky property deal.

Synset Examples

He really lost his shirt on that bad investment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go broke - to lose all one's money or become bankrupt
  • Antonym: to make a killing - to make a large amount of money quickly
