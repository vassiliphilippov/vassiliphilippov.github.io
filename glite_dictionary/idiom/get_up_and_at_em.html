to get up and at 'em

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ʌp ənd æt əm / (AmE), / ɡɛt ʌp ənd æt əm / (BrE)
to rise from bed and start being active or energetic; to begin the day's activities with energy
rise and become active
informal

Examples

It's Monday morning, so let's get up and at 'em before the day gets away from us.
After hitting the snooze button twice, he finally managed to get up and at 'em.
The coach shouted at the team to get up and at 'em after halftime.

Synset Examples

It's 7 AM, time to get up and at 'em!
She was finally up and at 'em after a long week.
After a good breakfast, they were up and at 'em.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wake up - to stop sleeping and become awake
  • Synonym: to get going - to begin moving or becoming active

Etymology

Contraction of 'get up and at them', with 'at 'em' meaning 'at them' in informal speech.
