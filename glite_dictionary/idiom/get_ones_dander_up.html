to get one's dander up

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt wʌnz ˈdændɚ ʌp / (AmE), / ɡɛt wʌnz ˈdændə ʌp / (BrE)
to make someone angry or irritated; to provoke annoyance or resentment
become angry or irritated

Examples

The rude comments really got my dander up during the meeting.
It doesn’t take much criticism to get his dander up.
Don’t tease her, or you’ll get her dander up again.

Synset Examples

Don’t say that; it will get her dander up.
He has a way of getting people's dander up.
The unfair treatment really got his dander up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to annoy - to cause slight anger or irritation
  • Synonym: to provoke - to stimulate a strong reaction, especially anger
  • Antonym: to calm down - to become less angry or excited

Etymology

‘Dander’ is a dialectal variant of ‘dandruff’; its use in this idiom to mean ‘anger’ likely derives from a figurative sense of one’s hackles rising.
