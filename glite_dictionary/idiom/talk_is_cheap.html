talk is cheap

1. X

/ tɑk ɪz tʃip / (AmE), / tɔːk ɪz tʃiːp / (BrE)
used to say that it is easy to say you will do something, but it is more important to actually do it
saying actions matter more than words

Examples

You keep making promises, but talk is cheap if you don't follow through.
When it comes to helping others, talk is cheap—it's your actions that count.
She always says she'll change, but we all know talk is cheap.

Synset Examples

Remember, talk is cheap; actions matter more.
She believes that talk is cheap without commitment.
He makes promises, but we know talk is cheap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: actions speak louder than words - idiom meaning what you do is more important than what you say
