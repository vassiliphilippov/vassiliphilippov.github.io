gimme a five

1. X

/ ˈɡɪmi ə faɪv / (AmE), / ˈɡɪmi ə faɪv / (BrE)
used to ask someone to give you a high-five, a gesture of slapping hands together in celebration or greeting
request for a high-five gesture
informal

Examples

After she won the race, she turned to her friend and said, 'gimme a five!'
When the deal closed successfully, everyone in the office went around saying 'gimme a five!'
He held out his palm and exclaimed, 'gimme a five!' to celebrate the good news.

Synset Examples

She held out her hand and said, 'gimme a five!'
After the game, we shouted, 'gimme a five!'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: high-five - the gesture of slapping palms with another person

Etymology

From 'give me a five', with 'gimme' as a colloquial contraction of 'give me'.
