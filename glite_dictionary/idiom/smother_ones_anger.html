to smother one's anger

1. VERB

/ ˈsmʌðɚ wʌnz ˈæŋɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈsmʌðə wʌnz ˈæŋɡə / (BrE)
to suppress or conceal one's feelings of anger, often deliberately or with effort
deliberately conceal anger

Examples

Despite his frustration, he managed to smother his anger during the meeting.
She clenched her fists, desperately trying to smother her anger in front of the children.
It's not healthy to always smother one's anger instead of expressing it calmly.

Synset Examples

She tried to smother her anger but failed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suppress - to put an end to an activity, emotion, or expression
  • Synonym: to conceal - to hide or keep something secret
