in the throes of

1. ADP

/ ɪn ðə θroʊz ʌv / (AmE), / ɪn ðə θrəʊz ɒv / (BrE)
experiencing a very intense or painful emotion, situation, or struggle, often especially during a serious or difficult time
experiencing intense emotion or struggle

Examples

The country was in the throes of a deep economic crisis when he took office.
She is in the throes of writing her novel and barely has time to eat.
They were in the throes of a heated argument when I arrived.

Synset Examples

She was in the throes of despair after the loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in the grip of - being firmly affected or controlled by something unpleasant

Etymology

From 'throes', an old word for violent pain or struggle, especially related to childbirth, recorded since Middle English.
