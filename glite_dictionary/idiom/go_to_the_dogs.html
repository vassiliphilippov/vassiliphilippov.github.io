to go to the dogs

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ tə ðə dɔɡz / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə ðə dɒɡz / (BrE)
to become ruined, deteriorated, or in a very poor state
become ruined or very poor

Examples

Many believe that the neighborhood has gone to the dogs since the factory closed.
If the company keeps ignoring its problems, it will go to the dogs sooner or later.
Some say the film industry is going to the dogs these days.

Synset Examples

His business went to pot when the economy soured

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go to pot - to become ruined or spoiled

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ tə ðə dɔɡz / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə ðə dɒɡz / (BrE)
(of a place, organization, or situation) to deteriorate in quality, success, or condition; to decline severely or fail.
deteriorate or decline badly

Examples

Many people worry that the economy will go to the dogs if nothing changes soon.
After the new management took over, the company started to go to the dogs.
Some residents believe the old theater has gone to the dogs since it closed.

Synset Examples

Without proper care, the project will go to the dogs.
The neighborhood has really gone to the dogs lately.
His business went to the dogs after the poor decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deteriorate - become progressively worse
  • Antonym: to improve - become better, increase in quality
