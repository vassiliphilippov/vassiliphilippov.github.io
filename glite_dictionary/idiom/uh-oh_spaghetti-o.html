uh-oh, Spaghetti-O

1. INTJ

/ ˈə ˌoʊ spəˈɡɛti ˌoʊ / (AmE), / ˈʌh ˌəʊ spəˈɡɛti ˌəʊ / (BrE)
used to express mild surprise, concern, or dismay, often in a playful or humorous way
expression of mild surprise or concern
informal american

Examples

When the paint spilled on the carpet, all I could say was uh-oh, Spaghetti-O.
He opened his exam paper and thought, uh-oh, Spaghetti-O, I didn’t study this chapter!
Uh-oh, Spaghetti-O, the power just went out right before dinner.

Synset Examples

She dropped the cake and said, uh-oh, Spaghetti-O!
When I saw the mess, I thought, uh-oh, Spaghetti-O!
Uh-oh, Spaghetti-O, we forgot to buy groceries!

Etymology

Popularized by a jingle for SpaghettiOs canned pasta in the United States in the 1960s.
