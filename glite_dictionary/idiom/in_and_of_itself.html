in and of itself

1. ADV

/ ɪn ənd ʌv ɪtˈsɛlf / (AmE), / ɪn ənd ɒv ɪtˈsɛlf / (BrE)
used to emphasize that something is considered alone, without taking other factors into account; by itself or intrinsically.
by itself; intrinsically

Examples

The law is not unjust in and of itself, but its application has caused problems.
Her art, in and of itself, was not controversial, but her statements sparked debate.
A high salary, in and of itself, does not guarantee job satisfaction.

Synset Examples

this statement is interesting per se

Related Terms

  • Synonym: per se - by itself; intrinsically

2. ADV

/ ɪn ənd ʌv ɪtˈsɛlf / (AmE), / ɪn ənd ɒv ɪtˈsɛlf / (BrE)
used to indicate that something is considered on its own, independently of other factors
considered alone; by itself

Examples

The policy, in and of itself, is not harmful, but it could have unintended effects.
Success, in and of itself, does not guarantee happiness.
This solution, in and of itself, does not address the root of the problem.

Synset Examples

The movie is enjoyable in and of itself.
The idea is simple in and of itself.
The task is challenging in and of itself.
