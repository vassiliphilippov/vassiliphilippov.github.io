piece of someone's mind

1. NOUN

/ pis əv ˈsʌmwʌnz maɪnd / (AmE), / piːs əv ˈsʌmwʌnz maɪnd / (BrE)
to speak angrily or bluntly to someone, especially to express disapproval or criticism
express anger or blunt criticism

Examples

After waiting for hours, she finally gave the clerk a piece of her mind.
If you don't finish your homework, Dad will give you a piece of his mind.
He was so frustrated that he decided to give the team a piece of his mind during the meeting.

Synset Examples

She gave her boss a piece of her mind after the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tell off - to speak angrily to someone because they have done something wrong
  • Synonym: to chew out - to scold or reprimand harshly
  • Antonym: compliment - to praise or express approval
