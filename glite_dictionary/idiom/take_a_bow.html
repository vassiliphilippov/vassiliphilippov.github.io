to take a bow

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə baʊ / (AmE), / teɪk ə baʊ / (BrE)
to acknowledge applause or appreciation, typically by bending forward at the waist after a performance
acknowledge applause after a performance

Examples

After finishing his solo, the violinist took a bow and smiled at the audience.
The whole team lined up to take a bow after their stunning victory.
She worked hard on the project, and now it's time for her to take a bow.
