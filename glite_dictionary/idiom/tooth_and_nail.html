tooth and nail

1. ADV

/ tuːθ ənd neɪl / (AmE), / tuːθ ənd neɪl / (BrE)
with all one's strength and determination; fiercely or ferociously
with great strength and determination

Examples

The two companies competed tooth and nail for control of the market.
She fought tooth and nail to defend her reputation against the accusations.
Residents battled tooth and nail to prevent the construction of the new shopping mall.

Synset Examples

she fought tooth and nail

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiercely - in a fierce or violent way
  • Antonym: reluctantly - in an unwilling or hesitant way

2. ADV

/ tuːθ ənd neɪl / (AmE), / tuːθ ənd neɪl / (BrE)
using all one's strength or resources; with fierce determination or effort.
with fierce determination or effort

Examples

She defended her position tooth and nail during the negotiations.
They competed tooth and nail for the last available scholarship spot.
Activists fought tooth and nail to protect the historic building.

Synset Examples

She defended her project tooth and nail at the meeting.
He battled tooth and nail against the unfair decision.
He defended his beliefs tooth-and-nail during the debate.
They fought tooth and nail to win the championship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fight - to engage in a physical or verbal struggle or effort
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop trying to achieve something; to surrender

Etymology

From Middle English, used since the 16th century; suggests fighting with all possible means, literally teeth and nails.
