to come clean

1. VERB

/ kʌm klin / (AmE), / kʌm kliːn / (BrE)
to confess or tell the truth about something previously hidden or kept secret
confess or admit the truth

Examples

After weeks of denial, he finally decided to come clean about the incident.
It's always better to come clean than to keep lying and get into trouble.
She knew she had to come clean with her parents about breaking the vase.

Synset Examples

She decided to come clean about the missing funds.
He had to come clean about his involvement in the project.
It's time for him to come clean about his mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confess - to admit to something, especially a wrongdoing or secret
  • Synonym: to come out - to reveal the truth or make something known
  • Antonym: to cover up - to hide the truth or keep something secret
