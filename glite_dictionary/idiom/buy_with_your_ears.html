to buy with your ears

1. VERB

/ baɪ wɪð jʊr ɪrz / (AmE), / baɪ wɪð jɔː ɪəz / (BrE)
To make a purchase decision based solely on the sound of a product, rather than on other information such as reviews or specifications.
purchase based only on sound

Examples

Before you buy with your ears, you should compare technical features of the speakers.
Many audiophiles prefer to buy with their ears instead of trusting advertisements.
He regretted his decision to buy with his ears after reading negative reviews online.

Synset Examples

Don't buy with your ears; check the reviews first.
Buying with your ears can lead to regret later.
