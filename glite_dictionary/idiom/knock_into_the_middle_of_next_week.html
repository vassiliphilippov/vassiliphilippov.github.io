to knock into the middle of next week

1. VERB

/ nɑk ˈɪntu ðə ˈmɪdəl əv nɛkst wik / (AmE), / nɒk ˈɪntu ðə ˈmɪdl əv nɛkst wiːk / (BrE)
to hit, shock, or surprise someone so forcefully that they are left dazed or overwhelmed, often used hyperbolically
hit or shock someone extremely forcefully

Examples

If you keep bothering me, I’ll knock you into the middle of next week.
That spicy food was so strong it nearly knocked me into the middle of next week.
The news was so unexpected it knocked her into the middle of next week.

Synset Examples

If you keep talking back, I'll knock you into the middle of next week!
That punch could knock you into the middle of next week!
His comments were so shocking they knocked her into the middle of next week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to knock out - to hit someone so hard they lose consciousness
