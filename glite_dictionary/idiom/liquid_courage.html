liquid courage

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪkwɪd ˈkɝːɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪd ˈkʌrɪdʒ / (BrE)
confidence or bravery gained through the consumption of alcohol
confidence from drinking alcohol
informal

Examples

A glass of wine gave him the liquid courage to sing at karaoke night.
Sometimes, people rely on liquid courage before making a big speech.
She laughed at herself for needing liquid courage to join the conversation.

Synset Examples

A shot of whiskey gave her the liquid courage she needed.
He needed liquid courage to ask her out.
He drank a pint for some liquid courage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dutch courage - confidence or boldness gained from drinking alcohol
