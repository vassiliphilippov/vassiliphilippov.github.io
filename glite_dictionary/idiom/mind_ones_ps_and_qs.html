to mind one's Ps and Qs

1. VERB

/ maɪnd wʌnz piːz ənd kjuːz / (AmE), / maɪnd wʌnz piːz ənd kjuːz / (BrE)
to be careful about how you behave and what you say, especially to be polite and well-mannered
be careful about behavior and manners

Examples

At formal events, it's important to mind your Ps and Qs to make a good impression.
The teacher told the students to mind their Ps and Qs during the field trip.
Whenever he visits his in-laws, he makes sure to mind his Ps and Qs.

Synset Examples

At the job interview, he needed to mind his ps and qs.
She always reminds her kids to mind their ps and qs.
You should mind your ps and qs at the formal dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be on one's best behavior - to behave as well as possible in a particular situation

Etymology

Possibly from the early tavern or printing trade, referring to confusing the letters 'p' and 'q', or an abbreviation for 'please' and 'thank you'. Exact origin uncertain.
