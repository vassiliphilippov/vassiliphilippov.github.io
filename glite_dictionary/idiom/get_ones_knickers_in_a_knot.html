to get one's knickers in a knot

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt wʌnz ˈnɪkɚz ɪn ə nɑt / (AmE), / ɡɛt wʌnz ˈnɪkəz ɪn ə nɒt / (BrE)
To become excessively upset, worried, or agitated about something that may not be very important.
become overly upset or agitated
informal british

Examples

There's no need to get your knickers in a knot; everything will work out fine.
He tends to get his knickers in a knot whenever plans change unexpectedly.
Don't get your knickers in a knot over a minor mistake; just fix it and move on.

Synset Examples

She always gets her knickers in a knot over small details.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get one's knickers in a twist - become overly upset or agitated (variant idiom)

Etymology

Based on the British English garment 'knickers' (women's underwear), metaphorically suggesting agitation.
