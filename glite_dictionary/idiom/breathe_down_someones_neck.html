to breathe down someone's neck

1. VERB

/ briːð daʊn ˈsʌmwʌnz nɛk / (AmE), / briːð daʊn ˈsʌmwənz nɛk / (BrE)
to closely watch, monitor, or pressure someone, especially in a way that feels oppressive or makes them nervous
monitor or pressure someone closely

Examples

I can't get any work done when my manager is breathe down my neck every five minutes.
She felt her supervisor was always breathing down her neck, making it hard to relax at work.
Could you please stop breathing down my neck while I finish this report?

Synset Examples

Stop breathing down my neck; I need space!
The boss was breathing down my neck all day.
She felt like her parents were breathing down her neck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hover over - to keep watch over someone too closely
  • Antonym: give someone space - to allow someone freedom from close supervision
