in the doghouse

1. ADJ

/ ɪn ðə ˈdɔɡˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ɪn ðə ˈdɒɡˌhaʊs / (BrE)
out of favor or in trouble with someone, especially because of one's actions
out of favor; in trouble

Examples

After forgetting their anniversary, he found himself in the doghouse for several days.
If you don't apologize, you'll be in the doghouse with your boss.
She's always in the doghouse after missing deadlines.

Synset Examples

After the argument, he was definitely in the doghouse.
I’m in the doghouse for not doing the dishes again.
He’s in the doghouse after forgetting her birthday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: out of favor - not liked or approved of by someone in authority or someone important
  • Synonym: in hot water - in trouble; likely to be punished or criticized

Etymology

Alludes to the idea of being sent to sleep in the dog's house as a sign of disfavor.
