no holds barred

1. X

/ noʊ hoʊldz bɑrd / (AmE), / nəʊ həʊldz bɑːd / (BrE)
without any restrictions, limits, or rules; in a completely unrestricted way
without restrictions or limits

Examples

The candidates engaged in a no holds barred debate about the policy changes.
It's a no holds barred competition, so expect some wild strategies from the teams.
She gave a no holds barred interview, speaking candidly about her experiences.

Synset Examples

The fighters went at it with no holds barred.
Their debate was intense and no holds barred.
In this competition, it's no holds barred; anything goes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unrestricted - not limited or controlled in any way
  • Antonym: with reservations - having some restrictions or limitations

Etymology

Originally from wrestling, where a 'hold' refers to a grappling move, and 'no holds barred' meant all moves were permitted.
