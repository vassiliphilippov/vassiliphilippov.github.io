to make light work of

1. VERB

/ meɪk laɪt wɝːk ʌv / (AmE), / meɪk laɪt wɜːk əv / (BrE)
to complete or deal with something quickly and easily, with little effort
do something easily and quickly

Examples

She made light work of the complex calculations, finishing them in half the expected time.
The soccer team made light work of their opponents, winning by five goals.
He makes light work of preparing dinner, even for a large group.

Synset Examples

She made light work of the challenging project.
With his experience, he made light work of the repairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to breeze through - to do something easily or quickly
  • Antonym: struggle with - to find something difficult to do or complete
