to sweep the board

1. VERB

/ swip ðə bɔrd / (AmE), / swiːp ðə bɔːd / (BrE)
to win all the prizes, games, or contests that are available in a particular competition or event.
win everything in a competition

Examples

The underdog team managed to sweep the board at the championships, surprising everyone.
If she keeps playing this well, she could sweep the board at the casino tonight.
They were so talented that they swept the board in every category at the awards show.

Synset Examples

He won every game and swept the board effortlessly.
Our team swept the board at the awards ceremony.
The new player managed to sweep the board last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: win hands down - to win very easily
  • Synonym: carry off the prize - to win the main prize or award
