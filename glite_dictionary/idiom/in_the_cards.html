in the cards

1. X

/ ɪn ðə kɑrdz / (AmE), / ɪn ðə kɑːdz / (BrE)
likely to happen or expected to occur.
likely to happen; expected

Examples

A big move to a new city may be in the cards for me next year.
Many experts believe further interest rate increases are in the cards.
If the weather stays nice, a picnic this weekend could be in the cards.

Synset Examples

It looks like a vacation is in the cards this summer.
A promotion seems to be in the cards for her.
An unexpected reunion might be in the cards.
I think a trip abroad is on the cards soon.
A big change is on the cards this year.
It seems a promotion is on the cards for her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on the cards - used in British English to mean likely or expected to happen

Etymology

Alludes to fortune-telling with playing cards.
