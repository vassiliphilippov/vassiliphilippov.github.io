the whole bakery

1. X

/ ðə hoʊl ˈbeɪkəri / (AmE), / ðə həʊl ˈbeɪkəri / (BrE)
an expression meaning to have everything one desires or all the available advantages
have everything one desires

Examples

After landing the big contract, she felt like she had the whole bakery.
He didn't just win the competition—he got the whole bakery.
With these benefits, employees feel they have the whole bakery at work.
