to have another think coming

1. VERB

/ hæv əˈnʌðɚ ˈθɪŋ ˈkʌmɪŋ / (AmE), / hæv əˈnʌðə ˈθɪŋ ˈkʌmɪŋ / (BrE)
to be mistaken about something and be likely to be proven wrong or forced to reconsider
be mistaken and likely to be proven wrong

Examples

If you think you can turn up late every day and not get noticed, you have another think coming.
Anyone who expects this project to finish ahead of schedule has another think coming.
She believed there would be no consequences for her actions, but she had another think coming.

Synset Examples

If you think that’s acceptable, you have another think coming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be mistaken - to be incorrect or wrong about something

Etymology

From the phrase 'if you think (x), you have another think coming', first recorded in early 20th-century American English.
