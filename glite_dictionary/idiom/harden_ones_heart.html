to harden one's heart

1. VERB

/ ˈhɑrdn wʌnz hɑrt / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdn wʌnz hɑːt / (BrE)
to become emotionally unresponsive, indifferent, or less compassionate, often after being hurt or disappointed
become emotionally unresponsive or indifferent

Examples

After the accident, he had to harden his heart just to get through each day.
Despite feeling guilt, she decided to harden her heart and reject his apology.
Years of hardship can harden a person's heart against the world.

Synset Examples

He didn’t want to harden his heart, but the pain was too much.
The betrayal seemed to harden her heart against love.
She tried to harden her heart after the breakup.
Years of disappointment can harden someone's heart towards trust.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: soften one's heart - to become more compassionate or sympathetic
