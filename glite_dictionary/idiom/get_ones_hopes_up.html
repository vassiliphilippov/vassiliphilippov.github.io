to get one's hopes up

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt wʌnz hoʊps ʌp / (AmE), / ɡɛt wʌnz həʊps ʌp / (BrE)
to become overly optimistic or to expect something positive to happen, often when it may not occur
become overly optimistic or expectant

Examples

Try not to get your hopes up before you receive the official results.
She didn't want to get her hopes up about the new job opportunity.
It's easy to get your hopes up when things seem to be going well.

Synset Examples

I tried not to get my hopes up for the concert tickets.
Don't get your hopes up about the promotion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hope - a feeling of expectation and desire for a certain thing to happen
  • Antonym: give up hope - to stop believing that something you want will happen

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt wʌnz hoʊps ʌp / (AmE), / ɡɛt wʌnz həʊps ʌp / (BrE)
to become optimistic or excited about something that may not happen
become optimistic or excited

Examples

Try not to get your hopes up about the promotion until it's official.
He didn't want to get his hopes up only to be disappointed again.
It's easy to get our hopes up when things seem to be going well.

Synset Examples

She got her hopes up for a sunny day but it rained.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: give up hope - to stop believing that something good will happen
