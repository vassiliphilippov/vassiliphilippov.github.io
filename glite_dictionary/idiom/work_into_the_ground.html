to work into the ground

1. VERB

/ wɜrk ˌɪntu ðə ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / wɜːk ˌɪntuː ðə ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
to make someone extremely tired or exhausted through too much work or effort
exhaust someone with too much work

Examples

The company will work into the ground anyone who doesn't set boundaries.
He worries they might work him into the ground if he keeps agreeing to extra hours.
She was worked into the ground by her demanding supervisor last year.

Synset Examples

She felt they would work her into the ground if she let them.
Don't let them work you into the ground; take breaks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wear out - to make someone tired or exhausted through continuous use or effort
