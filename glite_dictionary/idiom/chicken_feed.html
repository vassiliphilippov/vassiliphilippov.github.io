chicken feed

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪk.ɪn fiːd / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪk.ɪn fiːd / (BrE)
an amount of money that is so small as to be insignificant or trivial
a very small amount of money

Examples

For a company of that size, the fine was chicken feed.
He shrugged off the expense, saying it was just chicken feed.
Compared to their profits, the bonus was chicken feed.

Synset Examples

For her, a hundred dollars is just chicken feed.
The prize money was chicken feed compared to what I expected.
He won’t pay that much; it’s just chicken feed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pittance - a very small or inadequate amount of money
  • Antonym: fortune - a large amount of money

Etymology

Originally referred to the actual food given to chickens (which is relatively cheap and insignificant). Now used metaphorically.
