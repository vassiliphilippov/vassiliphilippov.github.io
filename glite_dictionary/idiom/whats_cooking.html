what's cooking

1. X

/ wʌts ˈkʊkɪŋ / (AmE), / wɒts ˈkʊkɪŋ / (BrE)
used to ask what is happening, being planned, or being prepared, especially in an informal way
asking what is happening or being planned (informal)
informal

Examples

Hey, what's cooking with the new project at work?
She walked in and asked, 'what's cooking?' when she saw everyone gathered.
If you want to know what's cooking, just ask the manager.

Synset Examples

Hey, what's cooking for dinner tonight?
She asked with a smile, 'What's cooking in the kitchen?'
I can smell something delicious; what's cooking?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: what's up - used to ask what is happening or wrong (informal)
