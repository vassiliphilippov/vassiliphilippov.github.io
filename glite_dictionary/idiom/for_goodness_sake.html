for goodness' sake

1. X

/ fər ˈɡʊdnəs seɪk / (AmE), / fə ˈɡʊdnɪs seɪk / (BrE)
used to express annoyance, frustration, or emphasis, often when urging someone to do or stop doing something
expression of frustration or urgency

Examples

For goodness' sake, close the window—it's freezing in here!
Could you, for goodness' sake, turn down that music?
For goodness' sake, don't forget to lock the door when you leave.

Synset Examples

He said, 'For goodness' sake, can we get started?'
She exclaimed, 'For goodness' sake, that's enough!'
For goodness' sake, please stop shouting!
He forgot his keys again, for goodness' sakes!
Please stop arguing, for goodness' sakes!
Don't eat that, for goodness' sakes!
