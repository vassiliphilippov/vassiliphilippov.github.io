new kid in town

1. NOUN

/ nu kɪd ɪn taʊn / (AmE), / njuː kɪd ɪn taʊn / (BrE)
a newcomer, especially one who has just arrived in a place or field and is attracting attention
a new or recently popular person

Examples

When Sarah joined the company, she quickly became the new kid in town with her innovative approach.
There's always a new kid in town when it comes to trendy restaurants.
He felt like the new kid in town after moving to the city and starting school.

Synset Examples

Everyone noticed the new kid in town at the party.
The startup quickly became the new kid in town.
With his fresh ideas, he's the new kid in town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newcomer - a person who has recently arrived or started in a place
