to flesh a sword

1. VERB

/ flɛʃ ə sɔrd / (AmE), / flɛʃ ə sɔːd / (BrE)
(archaic) To use or test a sword in battle for the first time.
use a sword in battle (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The young knight longed for the chance to flesh a sword on the battlefield.
Many nobles saw tournaments as opportunities to flesh a sword without risking their lives.
Eager for glory, he was determined to flesh a sword in his first campaign.

Synset Examples

He felt proud to flesh his maiden sword in the tournament.
The knight was eager to flesh his maiden sword in battle.

Etymology

From the verb "flesh," meaning to plunge a weapon into flesh, originating from Old English *flæsc* (flesh).
