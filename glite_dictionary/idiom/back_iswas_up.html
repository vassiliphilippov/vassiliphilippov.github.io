to back is/was up

1. VERB

/ bæk ɪz/ˈwəz ʌp / (AmE), / bæk ɪz/ˈwɒz ʌp / (BrE)
to become defensive or ready to argue, often due to feeling threatened or criticized
to become defensive or angry

Examples

Whenever she felt unfairly treated, her back was up in an instant.
His back is up whenever someone criticizes his work.
You could tell their backs were up as soon as the discussion turned critical.

Synset Examples

When she heard the criticism, her back was up immediately.
As soon as the manager questioned him, his back was up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on edge - feeling tense or irritable
  • Antonym: relaxed - not feeling defensive or threatened

Etymology

From the observed behavior of animals, such as cats, which arch their backs when threatened.
