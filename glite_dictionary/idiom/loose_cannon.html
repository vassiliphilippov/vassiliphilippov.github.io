loose cannon

1. NOUN

/ lus ˈkæn.ən / (AmE), / luːs ˈkæn.ən / (BrE)
a person who behaves in an unpredictable or uncontrollable way and may cause unintentional trouble or damage
unpredictable, potentially damaging person

Examples

He can be a loose cannon in meetings, so be careful what you say around him.
Nobody wants a loose cannon on their team when the stakes are high.
After his unpredictable outburst, the coach was labeled a loose cannon by the press.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild card - a person or thing whose behavior is unpredictable

Etymology

Originally from the literal image of an unsecured cannon on a ship, potentially causing harm; now used figuratively since the late 19th century.
