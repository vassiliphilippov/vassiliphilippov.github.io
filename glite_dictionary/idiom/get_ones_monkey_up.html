to get one's monkey up

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt wʌnz ˈmʌŋki ʌp / (AmE), / ɡɛt wʌnz ˈmʌŋki ʌp / (BrE)
to become angry or upset; to be provoked or roused to indignation
become angry or upset

Examples

He tends to get his monkey up when people criticize his work.
Don't get your monkey up over something so trivial.
She quickly got her monkey up after hearing the unfair accusation.

Synset Examples

She got her monkey up when they changed the rules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get one's back up - to become annoyed or angry
  • Antonym: to calm down - to become less angry or upset
