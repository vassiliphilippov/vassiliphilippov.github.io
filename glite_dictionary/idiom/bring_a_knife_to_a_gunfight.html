to bring a knife to a gunfight

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ ə naɪf tə ə ˈɡʌn.faɪt / (AmE), / brɪŋ ə naɪf tə ə ˈɡʌn.faɪt / (BrE)
to be inadequately prepared or equipped for a serious or competitive situation
be unprepared for a serious challenge

Examples

If you try to debate her without doing your research, you'll bring a knife to a gunfight.
Launching a startup without a solid business plan is like trying to bring a knife to a gunfight.
He quickly realized he had brought a knife to a gunfight when he saw the competition's resources.

Synset Examples

She knew better than to bring a knife to a gunfight in negotiations.
He realized he can't bring a knife to a gunfight in this competition.
Going unprepared was like bringing a knife to a gunfight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unprepared - not ready or equipped
  • Synonym: outmatched - overpowered by a stronger opponent
