to kick the tires

1. VERB

/ kɪk ðə taɪɚz / (AmE), / kɪk ðə taɪəz / (BrE)
to inspect, test, or evaluate something, especially before making a decision or purchase
inspect or test before deciding

Examples

It's wise to kick the tires before committing to any major software purchase.
Before joining the new gym, she wanted to kick the tires and see what facilities they offered.
The investors arrived to kick the tires on the start-up before making a decision.

Synset Examples

Make sure to kick some tires to check the car’s condition.
He likes to kick the tires on every used car he considers.
Before buying the car, make sure to kick the tires.
I plan to kick some tires before I buy a car.
Don't forget to kick the tires when you're test driving.
He likes to kick some tires at the dealership on weekends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inspect - to look at something carefully to assess its condition
  • Synonym: to evaluate - to assess or judge the value or quality of something

Etymology

Originates from the literal action of kicking a car's tires during inspection, later used figuratively for evaluating anything.
