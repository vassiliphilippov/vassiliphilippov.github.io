into thin air

1. ADV

/ ˌɪntu ˌθɪn ˈɛr / (AmE), / ˌɪntuː ˌθɪn ˈeə / (BrE)
used to indicate that someone or something has disappeared completely and without a trace
disappear without a trace

Examples

The documents seemed to vanish into thin air during the investigation.
As soon as he turned the corner, she was gone into thin air.
The ancient statue disappeared into thin air without explanation.

Synset Examples

She felt her worries vanish into thin air.
The magician made the rabbit disappear into thin air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: out of nowhere - appearing or happening suddenly and unexpectedly
