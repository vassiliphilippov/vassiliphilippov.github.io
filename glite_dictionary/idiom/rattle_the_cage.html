to rattle the cage

1. VERB

/ ˈrætl ðə keɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈrætl ðə keɪdʒ / (BrE)
to deliberately provoke, disturb, or agitate someone, especially to see their reaction or cause discomfort
provoke or disturb someone

Examples

He only said that to rattle the cage and see how you’d respond.
The press likes to rattle the cage when politicians get too comfortable.
Sometimes a little criticism is needed to rattle the cage and inspire change.

Synset Examples

Don't rattle the cage; the bird is already scared.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to provoke - to stimulate or incite someone to react
  • Synonym: to annoy - to irritate or bother someone
  • Antonym: to reassure - to make someone feel less anxious
