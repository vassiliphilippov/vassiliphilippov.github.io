beating a dead horse

1. X

/ ˈbiː.tɪŋ ə dɛd hɔɹs / (AmE), / ˈbiː.tɪŋ ə dɛd hɔːs / (BrE)
To continue pursuing or discussing a matter that is already settled or pointless, especially when any further effort is futile.
to pursue something pointless or futile

Examples

There's no sense in beating a dead horse; we've already made our decision.
He kept beating a dead horse by bringing up the same complaint over and over.
Discussing that topic again would just be beating a dead horse.

Synset Examples

At this stage, discussing that issue feels like beating a dead horse.
He realized he was just beating a dead horse by repeating the same arguments.
There's no point in beating a dead horse; we need a new strategy.
Stop trying to convince him; you're just flogging a dead horse.
She realized she was flogging a dead horse by arguing further.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flogging a dead horse - to persist in a futile effort (British variant)

Etymology

From the image of flogging an animal that can no longer move, first recorded in 19th-century English.
