to leave someone in stitches

1. VERB

/ liv ˈsʌmwʌn ɪn ˈstɪtʃɪz / (AmE), / liːv ˈsʌmwən ɪn ˈstɪtʃɪz / (BrE)
to make someone laugh a great deal, often uncontrollably
make someone laugh a lot

Examples

Her jokes always leave me in stitches during family gatherings.
The comedian left the audience in stitches with his hilarious routine.
We were all left in stitches after hearing about their vacation mishaps.

Synset Examples

He told a sleep funny story that left everyone in stitches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crack someone up - to make someone laugh suddenly or uncontrollably
  • Synonym: have someone in hysterics - to make someone laugh uncontrollably
