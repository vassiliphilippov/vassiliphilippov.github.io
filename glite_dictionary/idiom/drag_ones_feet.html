to drag one's feet

1. VERB

/ dræɡ wʌnz fit / (AmE), / dræɡ wʌnz fiːt / (BrE)
to delay or be deliberately slow in doing something, especially because you do not want to do it
delay or procrastinate intentionally

Examples

Whenever it's time to start his homework, he always drags his feet.
Management accused the team of dragging their feet on the project.
She tends to drag her feet when asked to make difficult decisions.

Synset Examples

He did not want to write the letter and procrastinated for days

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to procrastinate - to delay or postpone taking action
  • Antonym: to hasten - to do something quickly or speed up an action

2. VERB

/ dræɡ wʌnz fit / (AmE), / dræɡ wʌnz fiːt / (BrE)
to delay taking action or to be deliberately slow in doing something, especially because you do not want to do it
be slow to act; procrastinate

Examples

He tends to drag his feet when faced with difficult choices.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to procrastinate - to delay doing something; to put off
  • Antonym: to hasten - to move or act quickly; to hurry
