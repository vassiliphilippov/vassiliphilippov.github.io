to tell tales out of school

1. VERB

/ tɛl teɪlz aʊt əv skuːl / (AmE), / tɛl teɪlz aʊt ɒv skuːl / (BrE)
to reveal private, confidential, or sensitive information, especially inappropriately or indiscreetly
reveal confidential or private information

Examples

It's considered rude to tell tales out of school about your coworkers.
She regretted telling tales out of school after her comment spread through the office.
You can trust me; I would never tell tales out of school about our conversation.

Synset Examples

He shouldn't tell tales out of school about her personal life.
She warned him not to tell tales out of school.
It's unprofessional to tell tales out of school at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gossip - to talk about other people's private affairs
  • Synonym: betray a confidence - to reveal something told in confidence
  • Antonym: to keep a secret - to not reveal confidential information

Etymology

From the older phrase 'out of school', meaning 'inappropriately or imprudently', dating to the 16th century.
