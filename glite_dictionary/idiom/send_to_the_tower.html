to send to the Tower

1. VERB

/ sɛnd tu ðə ˈtaʊər / (AmE), / sɛnd tə ðə ˈtaʊə / (BrE)
to imprison or punish someone severely, often in a figurative or historical sense, referencing the Tower of London as a place of punishment
be severely punished or imprisoned
british

Examples

If you break the rules again, you'll be sent to the Tower.
She feared the boss would send her to the Tower after the costly mistake.
He laughed and said he deserved to be sent to the Tower for losing the file.

Synset Examples

He joked he would be sent to the Tower for his mistakes.
After the scandal, he felt like he was sent to the Tower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to imprison - put someone in prison
  • Synonym: punish severely - subject to harsh punishment

Etymology

Reference to the Tower of London, historically used for imprisoning important or disgraced persons.
