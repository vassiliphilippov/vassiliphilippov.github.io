friend of Dorothy

1. NOUN

/ frɛnd əv ˈdɔrəθi / (AmE), / frɛnd əv ˈdɒrəti / (BrE)
a gay man; historically used as a discreet term for a man who is homosexual, especially within the LGBT community.
discreet term for a gay man

Examples

He always felt comfortable at events for friends of Dorothy.
During the cruise, they discreetly asked if there were any friends of Dorothy on board.
In the past, being a friend of Dorothy was a way to signal one's identity without fear of discrimination.

Synset Examples

He proudly identified as a friend of Dorothy at the rally.
Many friends of Dorothy attended the pride parade together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gay - homosexual man or person
  • Antonym: straight - heterosexual person

Etymology

Alludes to Dorothy, the central character in the film 'The Wizard of Oz' (1939), played by Judy Garland, a gay icon.
