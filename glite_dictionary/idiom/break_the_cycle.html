to break the cycle

1. VERB

/ breɪk ðə ˈsaɪ.kəl / (AmE), / breɪk ðə ˈsaɪ.kəl / (BrE)
to put an end to a recurring and often harmful or unwanted pattern of behavior, events, or circumstances, especially through deliberate change or intervention
end a recurring harmful pattern

Examples

She knew she had to break the cycle of addiction for the sake of her children.
Government policies must aim to break the cycle of poverty in disadvantaged areas.
After years of conflict, both communities agreed to break the cycle and strive for peace.

Synset Examples

She enrolled in therapy to break the cycle of anxiety.
She sought help to break the cycle of self-defilement.
Education is key to breaking the cycle of ignorance.
