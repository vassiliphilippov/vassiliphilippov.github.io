a camel through the eye of a needle

1. NOUN

/ ə ˈkæməl θru ði aɪ əv ə ˈniːdl̩ / (AmE), / ə ˈkæməl θruː ði aɪ əv ə ˈniːdl / (BrE)
an extremely difficult, if not impossible, task or achievement
something extremely difficult or impossible

Examples

Getting an approval for the project is a camel through the eye of a needle in this company.
For many, buying a house in this market seems like a camel through the eye of a needle.
Convincing him to change his mind was a camel through the eye of a needle.

Synset Examples

Getting him to apologize is like a camel through the eye of a needle.
Finding a job without experience feels like a camel through the eye of a needle.
Her chance of winning the lottery is a camel through the eye of a needle.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: impossible - not able to be done or occur
  • Degree Lower: difficult - needing much effort or skill to accomplish

Etymology

Allusion to a biblical saying in the New Testament (Matthew 19:24): 'It is easier for a camel to go through the eye of a needle...'
