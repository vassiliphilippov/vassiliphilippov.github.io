to take to heart

1. VERB

/ teɪk tə hɑrt / (AmE), / teɪk tə hɑːt / (BrE)
To consider something very seriously, often in a way that affects one's feelings or behavior.
consider something seriously or personally

Examples

She took the feedback to heart and worked hard to improve her performance.
Try not to take his comments to heart—he didn't mean to upset you.
After the meeting, he really took their concerns to heart and changed his approach.

Synset Examples

Don't take it to heart; they meant no harm.
You should take her advice to heart.
He took the criticism to heart and improved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take personally - to feel offended or affected by something said or done
