the apple doesn't fall far from the tree

1. X

/ ði ˈæpəl ˈdʌzənt fɔl fɑr frəm ðə tri / (AmE), / ði ˈæpəl ˈdʌznt fɔːl fɑː frəm ðə triː / (BrE)
used to say that children often resemble their parents in character, appearance, or abilities
children resemble their parents

Examples

Everyone says the apple doesn't fall far from the tree after seeing how similar they act.
Her daughter has the same stubbornness—the apple doesn't fall far from the tree.
His quick wit proves that the apple doesn't fall far from the tree.

Synset Examples

He’s just like his father; the apple doesn't fall far from the tree.
She’s a talented artist; the apple doesn't fall far from the tree.
Her son is a great musician; the apple doesn't fall far from the tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: like father, like son - a phrase expressing the similarity between father and son
