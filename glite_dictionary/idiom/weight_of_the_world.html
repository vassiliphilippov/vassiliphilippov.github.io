weight of the world

1. NOUN

/ weɪt əv ðə wɝːld / (AmE), / weɪt əv ðə wɜːld / (BrE)
a feeling of overwhelming responsibility or great emotional burden, as if carrying the world's troubles on oneself
overwhelming emotional burden

Examples

She looked exhausted, as if the weight of the world rested on her shoulders.
During the crisis, it felt like he carried the weight of the world alone.
Sometimes, talking to a friend helps ease the weight of the world from your mind.

Synset Examples

He often felt the weight of the world on his shoulders.
He realized he couldn't bear the weight of the world alone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burden - something that is carried; a heavy load or responsibility
  • Antonym: lighten the load - to make a difficult situation easier or reduce someone's burden
