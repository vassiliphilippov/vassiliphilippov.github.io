to slide back down like snakes and ladders

1. VERB

/ slaɪd bæk daʊn laɪk sneɪks ənd ˈlædərz / (AmE), / slaɪd bæk daʊn laɪk sneɪks ənd ˈlædəz / (BrE)
to experience a sudden reversal of fortune or progress, similar to losing ground after making gains
suffer a setback, lose progress suddenly

Examples

After months of hard work, the company slid back down like snakes and ladders when the market crashed.
She was promoted last year, but a mistake caused her to slide back down like snakes and ladders.
Just as they were gaining momentum, unforeseen problems made them slide back down like snakes and ladders.

Synset Examples

He rose to success only to slide back down like snakes and ladders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: setback - a reversal or hindrance to progress
  • Antonym: progress - forward or onward movement toward a destination

Etymology

Alludes to the board game 'Snakes and Ladders' where players can advance rapidly or be suddenly sent backwards.
