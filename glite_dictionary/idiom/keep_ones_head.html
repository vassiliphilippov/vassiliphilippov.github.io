to keep one's head

1. VERB

/ kip wʌnz hɛd / (AmE), / kiːp wʌnz hɛd / (BrE)
to remain calm and think clearly in a difficult or dangerous situation
remain calm in difficulty

Examples

When everyone else was shouting, she managed to keep her head and resolve the issue.
It's important to keep your head when things start to go wrong in business.
He always tries to keep his head during stressful negotiations.

Synset Examples

She managed to keep her head while others panicked.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to lose one's head - to panic or lose control in a difficult situation

2. VERB

/ kip wʌnz hɛd / (AmE), / kiːp wʌnz hɛd / (BrE)
to remain calm and sensible in a difficult or dangerous situation
remain calm under stress

Examples

She managed to keep her head even when everyone else was panicking.
It's hard to keep your head when things are going wrong at work.
Great leaders are able to keep their heads in a crisis.

Synset Examples

It's important to keep your head during emergencies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to lose one's head - to panic or become irrational in a stressful situation
