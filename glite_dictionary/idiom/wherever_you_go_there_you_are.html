wherever you go, there you are

1. X

/ wɛrˈɛvɚ ju ɡoʊ ðɛr ju ɑr / (AmE), / wɛˈrɛvə juː ɡəʊ ðeə juː ɑː / (BrE)
used to express that a person cannot escape themselves or their problems by changing locations; one's personal issues or self always follow, regardless of circumstance or place.
you can't escape yourself or your problems

Examples

No matter how many times she moved cities, she eventually accepted that wherever you go, there you are.
He tried to leave his troubled past behind, but soon realized wherever you go, there you are.
The novel explores the theme that wherever you go, there you are, reminding us that internal struggles persist despite external change.
