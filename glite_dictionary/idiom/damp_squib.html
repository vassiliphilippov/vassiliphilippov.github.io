damp squib

1. NOUN

/ dæmp skwɪb / (AmE), / damp skwɪb / (BrE)
An event or situation that is much less impressive or exciting than expected; a disappointment.
disappointing or anticlimactic event
british

Examples

The highly advertised concert ended up being a damp squib due to technical problems.
Despite the buildup, the supposed scandal was a complete damp squib in the media.
Many hoped for a dramatic finale, but the show fizzled out as a damp squib.

Synset Examples

His presentation was a damp squib, lacking excitement.
The anticipated event was a damp squib with few attendees.
The fireworks turned out to be a damp squib.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: letdown - something that disappoints
  • Synonym: anticlimax - a disappointing end to an exciting or impressive series of events

Etymology

From 'squib' (a small firework) and 'damp' (wet), referring to a firework that fails to ignite, first recorded in the early 19th century.
