okey dokey, artichokey

1. INTJ

/ ˌoʊki ˈdoʊki ˌɑrtɪˈtʃoʊki / (AmE), / ˌəʊki ˈdəʊki ˌɑːtɪˈtʃəʊki / (BrE)
(informal) Used playfully to express agreement or acceptance, similar to 'okay'.
playful way to say okay
informal american

Examples

When the teacher asked if everyone was ready to begin, someone shouted okey dokey, artichokey from the back of the room.
"Time to go?" "Okey dokey, artichokey," she replied with a smile.
"Can you finish that by noon?" "Okey dokey, artichokey—I'll get it done!"

Synset Examples

She agreed to the plan with a cheerful, 'okey dokey, artichokey!'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: okey-dokey - informal way to say okay or all right
  • Synonym: okay - used to express agreement or acceptance

Etymology

Playful extension of 'okey-dokey', which itself is a reduplicative, informal variant of 'okay'. The 'artichokey' portion is added for rhyme and humor.
