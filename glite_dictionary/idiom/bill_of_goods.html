bill of goods

1. NOUN

/ bɪl əv ɡʊdz / (AmE), / bɪl əv ɡʊdz / (BrE)
A set of false or deceptive claims intended to persuade someone of something untrue or undesirable.
deceptive story or claim
informal american

Examples

He was angry when he realized he’d been sold a bill of goods by the contractor.
Don’t let them talk you into buying that car; they’re offering you a bill of goods.
Marketing often tries to sell consumers a bill of goods to boost sales.

Synset Examples

they tried to sell me a bill of goods about a secondhand car

Related Terms

  • Synonym: con - an act of deceiving or tricking
  • Synonym: scam - a dishonest scheme or fraud

Etymology

Originally from a literal bill of goods meaning a list of items delivered or owed; figurative sense from early 20th century US English.

2. NOUN

/ bɪl əv ɡʊdz / (AmE), / bɪl əv ɡʊdz / (BrE)
A deceptive or misleading offer, especially an attempt to trick someone into accepting something false or undesirable.
deceptive or misleading offer

Examples

He realized too late that he'd bought a bill of goods from a dishonest contractor.
We were promised advanced technology, but what we received was just a bill of goods.
The politician tried to sell voters a bill of goods with empty promises.

Synset Examples

He sold me a bill of goods with that story.
Don't let them sell you a bill of goods; it's a scam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scam - a fraudulent scheme
  • Synonym: swindle - a fraudulent act or scheme
