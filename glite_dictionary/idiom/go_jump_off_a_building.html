go jump off a building

1. X

/ ɡoʊ dʒʌmp ɒf ə ˈbɪldɪŋ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ dʒʌmp ɒf ə ˈbɪldɪŋ / (BrE)
(informal) Used sarcastically or jokingly to express frustration or dismiss someone, not meant to be taken literally.
expresses sarcastic frustration or dismissal
informal

Examples

After I made a mistake, my brother jokingly told me to go jump off a building.
She was so annoyed that she muttered, 'Why don't you go jump off a building?' under her breath.
He didn't mean it and later apologized for telling his friend to go jump off a building during the argument.

Synset Examples

She was frustrated and said she might as well go jump off a building.
In jest, he told her to go jump off a building after her mistake.
When he failed, I jokingly told him to go jump off a building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go jump in a lake - an idiom used to tell someone to go away or to express annoyance
