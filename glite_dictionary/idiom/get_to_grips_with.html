to get to grips with

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt tə ɡrɪps wɪð / (AmE), / ɡɛt tə ɡrɪps wɪð / (BrE)
to begin to understand or deal effectively with a difficult situation, problem, or subject
to start to understand or manage

Examples

After several weeks of training, she finally got to grips with the new accounting system.
It can be hard to get to grips with abstract concepts in physics.
Many students struggle at first but eventually get to grips with university life.

Synset Examples

I need to get to grips with this new software.
It took her a while to get to grips with parenting.
He finally got to grips with his finances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: come to grips with - to confront or deal with a problem or difficulty
