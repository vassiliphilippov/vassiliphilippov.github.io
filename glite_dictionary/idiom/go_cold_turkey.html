to go cold turkey

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ koʊld ˈtɝki / (AmE), / ɡəʊ kəʊld ˈtɜːki / (BrE)
to stop engaging in an addictive or habitual activity suddenly and completely, rather than gradually
quit suddenly and completely

Examples

After years of smoking, he decided to go cold turkey and hasn't touched a cigarette since.
It's not easy to go cold turkey on caffeine when you're used to drinking coffee every day.
Many people find it difficult to go cold turkey without support from friends or family.

Synset Examples

She decided to go cold turkey on junk food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quit cold turkey - to quit abruptly (variant phrase)

Etymology

Possibly from American slang; 'cold turkey' refers to abrupt withdrawal and the physical symptoms often associated with it.
