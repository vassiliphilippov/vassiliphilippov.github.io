to keep one's options open

1. VERB

/ kip wʌnz ˈɑpʃənz ˈoʊpən / (AmE), / kiːp wʌnz ˈɒpʃənz ˈəʊpən / (BrE)
to avoid committing to a particular course of action or decision so that other choices remain possible
not commit to one choice

Examples

She chose to keep her options open by applying to several universities.
It's best to keep your options open until all the offers are on the table.
By not signing the contract immediately, they managed to keep their options open.

Synset Examples

She decided to keep her options open for future job offers.
It's wise to keep your options open until you decide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hedge one's bets - to protect oneself from loss by supporting more than one possible result or both sides of an argument
