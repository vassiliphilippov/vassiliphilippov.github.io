to pull strings

1. VERB

/ pʊl strɪŋz / (AmE), / pʊl strɪŋz / (BrE)
to use one's influence or personal connections to control or affect a situation, often in a secretive or indirect way
use influence or connections secretly

Examples

She managed to get her son admitted to the school by pulling strings at the education board.
He has a reputation for pulling strings whenever he wants things done his way.
They suspect someone in management pulled strings to secure the contract for their friend.

Synset Examples

He manipulated public opinion in his favor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pull wires - to use influence or connections to achieve something (chiefly US)
  • Synonym: to manipulate - to control or influence cleverly or unscrupulously

Etymology

Alludes to controlling a puppet by pulling its strings.

2. VERB

/ pʊl strɪŋz / (AmE), / pʊl strɪŋz / (BrE)
to use one's influence or connections to achieve a desired result, often secretly or unofficially
use influence to achieve something

Examples

He managed to get the promotion by pulling strings with his superiors.
If you want to get backstage passes, you’ll have to pull strings.
She admitted that she had pulled strings to secure her son's university place.

Synset Examples

She can pull strings to get us into the concert.
He used his connections to pull strings for the event.
It's not what you know, but who you know to pull strings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pull wires - to use influence to achieve a goal; an alternative form, chiefly in American English

Etymology

From the imagery of controlling a puppet's movements with strings, implying manipulation from behind the scenes.
