dragged through a hedge backwards

1. X

/ dræɡd θru ə hɛdʒ ˈbækwɚdz / (AmE), / dræɡd θruː ə hɛdʒ ˈbækwədz / (BrE)
(Of a person) looking extremely messy, untidy, or disheveled, as if having been through a rough experience.
messy or disheveled appearance (idiom)
british

Examples

She arrived at the meeting looking as if she'd been dragged through a hedge backwards.
After the long flight, I felt like I had been dragged through a hedge backwards.
His suit was so wrinkled, he appeared to have been dragged through a hedge backwards.

Synset Examples

You’ve got to fix your hair; you look as if you've been dragged through a hedge backwards.
He showed up at the event looking as if he had been dragged through a hedge backwards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unkempt - messy or not neatly arranged (especially of hair or appearance)
