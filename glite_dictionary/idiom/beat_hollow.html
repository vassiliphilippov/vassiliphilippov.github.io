to beat hollow

1. VERB

/ bit ˈhɑloʊ / (AmE), / biːt ˈhɒləʊ / (BrE)
to appear clearly inferior or fail to seem convincing compared to something else
be clearly inferior; fail to convince

Examples

Her arguments beat hollow when faced with real evidence.
The imitation product beat hollow next to the original.
His apology beat hollow and failed to sway anyone in the audience.

Synset Examples

The team's performance beat hollow compared to last year.
His excuses beat hollow when I checked the facts.
Her promises started to sound beat hollow after several broken commitments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fall flat - to fail completely in making people laugh or be impressed
  • Antonym: triumph - achieve a great victory or success
