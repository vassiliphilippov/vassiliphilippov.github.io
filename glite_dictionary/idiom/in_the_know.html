in the know

1. ADJ

/ ɪn ðə noʊ / (AmE), / ɪn ðə nəʊ / (BrE)
having access to special or secret information that most people do not have.
having inside or privileged information

Examples

Only a few close advisers are in the know about the company's future plans.
If you want to be in the know, you should attend all the meetings.
Journalists who are in the know often get exclusive stories before anyone else.

Synset Examples

he is always in the know

Related Terms

  • Antonym: in the dark - not having knowledge about something; unaware
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05813020-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ɪn ðə noʊ / (AmE), / ɪn ðə nəʊ / (BrE)
having access to special or insider information that most people do not have; aware of the most recent developments or facts
aware of insider or latest information

Examples

He always seems to be in the know about the latest business trends.
If you want to be in the know, you should subscribe to this newsletter.
Only a few staff members are in the know about the merger plans.

Synset Examples

Her friends are part of the hipoisie, always in the know.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: out of the loop - not informed or aware of recent developments

Synset POS: None
