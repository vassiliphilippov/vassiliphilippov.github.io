at each other's throats

1. X

/ ət itʃ ˈʌðɚz θroʊts / (AmE), / ət iːtʃ ˈʌðəz θrəʊts / (BrE)
(of two or more people) engaged in fierce argument or conflict; fighting angrily with each other.
in fierce argument or conflict

Examples

The two rival politicians have been at each other's throats throughout the entire campaign.
After the decision was announced, the departments were at each other's throats over the new responsibilities.
Whenever there's a big project, the designers and engineers seem to be at each other's throats.

Synset Examples

The co-workers were at each other's throats during the deadline.
They've been at each other's throats since the argument.
The siblings were at each other's throats over the inheritance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to argue - to speak angrily to someone, disagreeing with them
  • Synonym: quarrel - to have an angry argument or disagreement
