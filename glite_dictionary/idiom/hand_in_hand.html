hand in hand

1. ADV

/ hænd ɪn hænd / (AmE), / hænd ɪn hænd / (BrE)
with two people holding each other's hands, especially while walking together
holding hands (two people)

Examples

The couple walked hand in hand along the beach at sunset.
Children skipped hand in hand around the playground.
He saw his grandparents sitting hand in hand on the park bench.

Synset Examples

they walked hand in hand

2. ADV

/ hænd ɪn hænd / (AmE), / hænd ɪn hænd / (BrE)
closely connected or working together; in cooperation or partnership
together; in cooperation

Examples

The two companies worked hand in hand to develop the new product.
Progress and change often go hand in hand in a rapidly evolving society.
In disaster relief operations, various agencies often act hand in hand to provide aid.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: side by side - next to each other; together
