on an even keel

1. ADV

/ ɑn ən ˈivən kil / (AmE), / ɒn ən ˈiːvən kiːl / (BrE)
in a stable or balanced state; functioning smoothly without major problems or disruptions
in a stable or balanced state

Examples

After months of uncertainty, the company is finally on an even keel.
Daily exercise helps keep my mood on an even keel.
It's important to keep family life on an even keel during stressful times.

Synset Examples

He managed to keep his business on an even keel during the crisis.
Keeping the team on an even keel is crucial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stable - firmly fixed or not likely to change or move
  • Synonym: balanced - having different elements in the correct proportions
  • Antonym: in turmoil - in a state of great disturbance or confusion

Etymology

From nautical terminology, where 'keel' refers to the central structure of a ship; a ship is 'on an even keel' when it is balanced in the water.
