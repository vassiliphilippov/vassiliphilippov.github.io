to feel like a potato

1. VERB

/ fil laɪk ə pəˈteɪtoʊ / (AmE), / fiːl laɪk ə pəˈteɪtəʊ / (BrE)
to feel extremely lazy or unproductive, as if lacking energy or motivation to do anything
to feel lazy or unproductive
informal

Examples

After three hours watching TV, I really felt like a potato.
When it rains all weekend, I just feel like a potato and don’t get anything done.
She admitted she feels like a potato whenever she skips her morning workout.

Synset Examples

After that long meeting, I felt like a potato.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lazy - unwilling to work or use energy
  • Antonym: energetic - having or showing a lot of energy
