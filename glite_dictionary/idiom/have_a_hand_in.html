to have a hand in

1. VERB

/ hæv ə hænd ɪn / (AmE), / hæv ə hænd ɪn / (BrE)
to be involved in or take part in something, especially when influencing an outcome
be involved in something

Examples

She was proud to have a hand in organizing the festival this year.
Many experts had a hand in developing the new vaccine.
It's important for employees to have a hand in shaping company policies.

Synset Examples

He had a hand in designing the new logo.
They want to have a hand in the decision-making process.
She wants to have a hand in the charity event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take part in - to participate in something
  • Synonym: be responsible for - to have responsibility for causing or managing something
