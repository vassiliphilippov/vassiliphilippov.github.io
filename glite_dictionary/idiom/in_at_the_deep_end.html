in at the deep end

1. ADV

/ ɪn æt ðə diːp ɛnd / (AmE), / ɪn æt ðə diːp end / (BrE)
(of a person) placed in a difficult or challenging situation without preparation or experience.
in a difficult situation without preparation

Examples

When I moved to the new city, I was in at the deep end from the start.
She felt in at the deep end on her first day teaching the class alone.
They put him in at the deep end by asking him to lead the meeting minutes after joining.

Synset Examples

They threw him in at the deep end with the project.
Starting a new job can feel like being in at the deep end.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrown in at the deep end - put into a difficult situation suddenly
