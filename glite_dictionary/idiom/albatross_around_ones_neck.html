albatross around one's neck

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.bəˌtrɑs əˈraʊnd wʌnz nɛk / (AmE), / ˈæl.bə.trɒs əˈraʊnd wʌnz nɛk / (BrE)
A constant and burdensome problem, guilt, or responsibility that is difficult to get rid of.
burdensome responsibility or problem

Examples

For years, the company's debt was an albatross around his neck, affecting every decision he made.
She considered the old house an albatross around her neck, as its maintenance drained her finances.
His reputation as unreliable became an albatross around his neck in the job market.

Synset Examples

She viewed her student loans as an albatross around her neck.
The failed business was an albatross around his neck.
His past mistakes became an albatross around his neck.
The ongoing lawsuit felt like an albatross around her neck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: millstone - a heavy and inescapable burden

Etymology

From Samuel Taylor Coleridge's poem 'The Rime of the Ancient Mariner,' where an albatross hung around the mariner's neck symbolizes guilt and burden.
