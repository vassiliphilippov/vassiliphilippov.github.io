to lose the bubble

1. VERB

/ luːz ðə ˈbʌb.əl / (AmE), / luːz ðə ˈbʌb.əl / (BrE)
to become confused or lose track of what is happening; to lose focus or understanding
become confused or lose focus

Examples

During the lengthy briefing, he started to lose the bubble and missed important details.
If you lose the bubble in this discussion, just ask for clarification.
She realized she had lost the bubble after several people began talking at once.

Synset Examples

He often loses the bubble during long discussions.
When she started multitasking, she lost the bubble.
After the meeting, I felt like I lost the bubble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lose track - to fail to remain aware of something
  • Synonym: get confused - to become unable to think clearly

Etymology

US military slang, originally referring to losing reference to a directional bubble in navigation instruments.
