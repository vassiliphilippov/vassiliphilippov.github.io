as it were

1. ADV

/ ˌæz ɪt ˈwɜr / (AmE), / ˌæz ɪt ˈwɜː / (BrE)
used to indicate that a word or phrase is not exactly accurate but is a close approximation or metaphorical; so to speak.
used to say 'so to speak' or 'in a manner of speaking'

Examples

He became, as it were, the father of modern art criticism.
The room was, as it were, the heart of the entire house.
She was their leader, as it were, guiding the team through every challenge.

Synset Examples

she lives here, as it were

Related Terms

  • Synonym: so to speak - used to indicate a metaphor or non-literal meaning
  • Antonym: literally - in a literal, exact sense; not metaphorically
