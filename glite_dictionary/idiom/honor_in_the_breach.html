honor in the breach

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːnər ɪn ðə briːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈɒnə ɪn ðə briːtʃ / (BrE)
the belief that breaking a rule or law can be more honorable than following it, especially when the rule is unjust or inappropriate
honor found by breaking a rule or law

Examples

The activists believed there was honor in the breach when protesting against unjust laws.
Sometimes, there is honor in the breach rather than blindly following every order.
She argued that true integrity may require finding honor in the breach of tradition.

Synset Examples

They found honor in the breach, standing up for justice.
There’s honor in the breach, especially in tough times.

Etymology

Adapted from Shakespeare's 'more honour’d in the breach than the observance' (Hamlet, c.1600).
