to beat the daylights out of

1. VERB

/ biːt ðə ˈdeɪˌlaɪts ˈaʊt əv / (AmE), / biːt ðə ˈdeɪˌlaɪts ˈaʊt ɒv / (BrE)
to hit someone or something very hard; to severely damage or destroy
hit or damage severely (idiom)
informal

Examples

If you touch his car, he'll beat the daylights out of you.
The boxer beat the daylights out of his opponent in the first round.
That hailstorm really beat the daylights out of our roof.

Synset Examples

He was so angry that he wanted to beat the daylights out of the wall.
The coach told the players to beat the daylights out of their opponents.
The storm beat the daylights out of our garden last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrash - to beat someone repeatedly and violently
  • Synonym: to pummel - to hit someone or something repeatedly, especially with fists
  • Antonym: go easy on - to treat someone gently or not punish harshly
