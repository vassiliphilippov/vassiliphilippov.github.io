to kick butt

1. VERB

/ kɪk bʌt / (AmE), / kɪk bʌt / (BrE)
to perform exceptionally well or achieve great success, especially in a competitive situation
perform very well; achieve great success
informal

Examples

If our team works together, we can really kick butt in the finals.
She kicked butt in her new job and quickly got promoted.
They're determined to kick butt in the upcoming tournament.

Synset Examples

He really knows how to kick butt in the competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to succeed - to achieve the desired result or aim
  • Antonym: to fail - to be unsuccessful in achieving something expected or attempted
