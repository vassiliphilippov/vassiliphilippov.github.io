short end of the stick

1. NOUN

/ ʃɔrt ɛnd əv ðə stɪk / (AmE), / ʃɔːt end əv ðə stɪk / (BrE)
to receive the worse outcome in a situation or to be treated unfairly compared to others
to receive the unfair or worse outcome

Examples

Despite all his hard work, Mark ended up with the short end of the stick when bonuses were handed out.
Many workers feel like they're getting the short end of the stick compared to management.
She always complains that she gets the short end of the stick in group projects.

Synset Examples

He must have drawn the short end of the shitty stick.
He was given the short end of the stick during negotiations.
She always feels like she gets the short end of the stick.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raw deal - an instance of unfair or bad treatment
