to deliver the goods

1. VERB

/ dɪˈlɪvɚ ðə ɡʊdz / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪvə ðə ɡʊdz / (BrE)
to achieve the desired result or meet expectations, especially by fulfilling a promise or obligation
successfully meet expectations or achieve results

Examples

The new coach promised a championship and he certainly delivered the goods.
It took some time, but the research team finally delivered the goods with their breakthrough.
If this project goes well, we'll really prove we can deliver the goods.

Synset Examples

The enterprise succeeded
We succeeded in getting tickets to the show
she struggled to overcome her handicap and won

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to come through - to do what is needed or expected successfully
  • Antonym: to fall short - to fail to reach the required or expected standard

2. VERB

/ dɪˈlɪvər ðə ɡʊdz / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪvə ðə ɡʊdz / (BrE)
to do what is expected or required, especially by meeting promises or achieving success
fulfill expectations; achieve required results

Examples

When challenged, she always delivers the goods and exceeds expectations.
Fans hoped the new smartphone would deliver the goods, and it certainly impressed them.
We trust our suppliers to consistently deliver the goods, even under pressure.

Synset Examples

If you want to succeed, you must consistently deliver the goods.
The team managed to deliver the goods despite the challenges.
He always finds a way to deliver the goods on time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to come through - to succeed in doing what is needed or expected
  • Antonym: to fall short - to fail to achieve what is expected or needed
