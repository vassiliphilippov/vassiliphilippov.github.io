break of day

1. NOUN

/ breɪk əv deɪ / (AmE), / breɪk əv deɪ / (BrE)
the time when the first light appears in the morning; dawn
dawn; first light of morning

Examples

The fishermen set out to sea at the break of day to catch the freshest fish.
We began hiking at the break of day to avoid the afternoon heat.
She finished her novel just as the break of day crept through her window.

Synset Examples

we got up before dawn
they talked until morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dawn - the time each morning when daylight first appears
  • Synonym: sunrise - the time when the sun appears over the horizon in the morning
  • Antonym: nightfall - the time when it becomes dark in the evening
