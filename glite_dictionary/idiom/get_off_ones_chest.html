to get off one's chest

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɔf wʌnz tʃɛst / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒf wʌnz tʃɛst / (BrE)
to express something you have been worrying about or feeling guilty about so that you feel relieved
express something worrying or troubling

Examples

She finally decided to get off her chest everything that had been bothering her at work.
It's sometimes hard to get things off your chest, but it makes you feel much better.
After he got it off his chest, he felt a great sense of relief.

Synset Examples

I need to get this off my chest before it drives me crazy.
It's good to get the dirty water off your chest sometimes.
Talking to my friend helped me get my worries off my chest.
He felt relieved after getting the dirty water off his chest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confess - to admit something, especially a crime or fault, to someone
  • Antonym: to bottle up - to keep feelings or emotions inside without expressing them

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɔf wʌnz tʃɛst / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒf wʌnz tʃɛst / (BrE)
to talk about something that has been worrying or bothering you, often by making a confession or revealing a secret
express a worry or secret to relieve stress

Examples

I'm glad I finally got it off my chest—I've been worrying about it for weeks.
She needed to get something off her chest before she could relax.
He felt much better after getting the truth off his chest at the meeting.

Synset Examples

She finally got the dirty water off her chest during the meeting.
He finally got his feelings off his chest at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confess - to admit a feeling or reveal something private or uncomfortable
  • Antonym: to bottle up - to keep emotions or worries inside without expressing them
