to bust my chops

1. VERB

/ bʌst maɪ tʃɑps / (AmE), / bʌst maɪ tʃɒps / (BrE)
to tease, criticize, or annoy someone persistently, often in a playful or lighthearted way
tease or criticize persistently
informal american

Examples

He gets defensive whenever his coworkers bust his chops about being late.
I'm trying my best, so please don't bust my chops over one small error.
She likes to bust my chops about my choice of music, but I know it's all in good fun.

Synset Examples

He loves to bust my chops during meetings.
Don’t bust my chops over a small mistake.
They love to bust my chops about my cooking skills.
Stop busting my chops about the project!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rib - to tease or make fun of someone in a friendly way
  • Synonym: to give someone a hard time - to criticize or tease someone persistently
