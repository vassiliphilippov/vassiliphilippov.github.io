the circle gets the square

1. X

/ ðə ˈsɝkəl ɡɛts ðə skwɛr / (AmE), / ðə ˈsɜːkl̩ ɡɛts ðə skweə / (BrE)
(in allusion to the game show 'Hollywood Squares') an announcement made when a player achieves a correct answer or scores a success, sometimes used more generally to indicate victory or accomplishment.
game-show catchphrase for success or correct answer
american

Examples

After answering the final trivia question correctly, she announced, 'the circle gets the square!'
With a confident grin, he declared 'the circle gets the square' when his team won the round.
Whenever they win an argument, they like to say 'the circle gets the square.'

Synset Examples

During the game, he triumphantly declared, 'The circle gets the square!'
She scored the final point and exclaimed, 'The circle gets the square!'
In this debate, the winner said, 'The circle gets the square.'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: success - the achievement of an aim or goal
  • Synonym: victory - winning a competition or game

Etymology

Originated from the American TV game show 'Hollywood Squares', where contestants selected squares marked X or O (circle).
