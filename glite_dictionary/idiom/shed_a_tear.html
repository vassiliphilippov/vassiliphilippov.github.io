to shed a tear

1. VERB

/ ʃɛd ə tɪr / (AmE), / ʃɛd ə tɪə / (BrE)
to cry or express sadness, often with a single tear or in a restrained manner
to cry or show sadness

Examples

It was such a touching story that I couldn't help but shed a tear.
She quietly shed a tear while saying goodbye to her friend.
Even the toughest critics may shed a tear during that emotional performance.

Synset Examples

I couldn’t help but shed a tear during the movie.
She shed a tear at her daughter's wedding.
He didn’t shed a tear, even at the sad news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cry - to produce tears as an expression of sadness or emotion
  • Synonym: to weep - to shed tears, typically as an expression of sorrow
