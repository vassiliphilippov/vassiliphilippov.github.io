to chase poontang

1. VERB

/ tʃeɪs ˈpuːn.tæŋ / (AmE), / tʃeɪs ˈpuːn.tæŋ / (BrE)
to actively seek or pursue casual sexual encounters, especially with women (informal, slang)
pursue casual sexual encounters (slang)
informal slang vulgar american

Examples

During spring break, he would often chase poontang at the beach parties.
Some people grow out of the urge to chase poontang as they get older.
She criticized him for spending all his time trying to chase poontang instead of focusing on his career.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

'Poontang' is American slang dating from the early 20th century, likely from French 'putain' (prostitute).
