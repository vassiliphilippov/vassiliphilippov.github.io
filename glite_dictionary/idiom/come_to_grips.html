to come to grips

1. VERB

/ kʌm tə ɡrɪps / (AmE), / kʌm tə ɡrɪps / (BrE)
to begin to deal with or understand a difficult problem, subject, or situation
begin to deal with a problem or issue

Examples

It took her a long time to come to grips with her new responsibilities at work.
The community is finally coming to grips with the consequences of climate change.
After the initial shock, he began to come to grips with his diagnosis.

Synset Examples

I still have not come to grips with the death of my parents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: face up to - to confront or deal directly with something difficult
