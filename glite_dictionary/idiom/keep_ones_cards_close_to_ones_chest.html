to keep one's cards close to one's chest

1. VERB

/ kip wʌnz kɑrdz kloʊs tu wʌnz tʃɛst / (AmE), / kiːp wʌnz kɑːdz kləʊs tuː wʌnz tʃɛst / (BrE)
to not reveal your plans or intentions; to act cautiously and keep information secret, especially in order to gain an advantage
be secretive about your plans

Examples

It's often wise to keep one's cards close to one's chest during sensitive negotiations.
She tends to keep her cards close to her chest when discussing her future plans.
In a competitive market, executives usually keep their cards close to their chest to protect business secrets.

Synset Examples

He prefers to keep his cards close to his chest until the right moment.
In negotiations, it's wise to keep one's cards close to one's chest.
She always keeps her cards close to her chest in business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: play one's cards close to one's chest - to keep one's plans or intentions secret

Etymology

From the practice in card games of holding your cards close to your chest to prevent others from seeing them.
