to fall on one's face

1. VERB

/ fɔl ɑn wʌnz feɪs / (AmE), / fɔːl ɒn wʌnz feɪs / (BrE)
to fail badly or embarrass oneself, especially in a public or obvious way
fail badly; embarrass oneself

Examples

Despite his best efforts, Tom fell on his face during the presentation.
She tried to impress everyone, but she just fell on her face instead.
When the new policy failed, the manager admitted he had fallen on his face.

Synset Examples

After that mistake, I really fell on my face.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to succeed - to achieve a desired result; to do well
  • Synonym: to mess up - to make a mistake or do something badly
