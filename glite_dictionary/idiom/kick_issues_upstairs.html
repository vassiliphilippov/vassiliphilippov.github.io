to kick issues upstairs

1. VERB

/ kɪk ˈɪʃuːz ʌpˈstɛɹz / (AmE), / kɪk ˈɪʃuːz ʌpˈstɛəz / (BrE)
to pass responsibility for dealing with a problem or decision to a higher level of authority
defer responsibility to higher authority

Examples

Whenever difficult problems arise, the manager tends to kick issues upstairs rather than handle them directly.
Instead of making a decision, the committee chose to kick issues upstairs.
Many employees feel frustrated when leaders continually kick issues upstairs instead of providing clear solutions.

Synset Examples

It's common to kick issues upstairs when they're too complex.
Management often kicks problems upstairs instead of solving them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pass the buck - shift responsibility onto someone else
  • Synonym: to escalate - raise an issue to a higher authority
