laboring oar

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.bɚ.ɪŋ ɔɚ / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.bər.ɪŋ ɔː / (BrE)
the person who does the most work or bears the heaviest burden in a group or partnership
one who does the most work in a group

Examples

After the team leader left, Tom became the laboring oar on the project.
She often finds herself as the laboring oar in her volunteer group.
If you take a break, I'll have to be the laboring oar for a while.

Synset Examples

She’s always been the laboring oar, doing most of the work.
Without her, I’m the laboring oar in this partnership.
He felt like the laboring oar in every group project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mainstay - a person or thing on which something else is based or depends
  • Synonym: workhorse - a person or thing that does most of the hard work

Etymology

From nautical imagery; the oar pulled by the rower doing the hardest work.
