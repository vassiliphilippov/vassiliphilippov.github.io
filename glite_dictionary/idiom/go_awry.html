to go awry

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ əˈraɪ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈraɪ / (BrE)
(of a plan or situation) to go wrong or not as expected.
(of plans) to go wrong

Examples

Their plans for the picnic went awry when it started to rain unexpectedly.
If anything goes awry during the experiment, inform your supervisor at once.
Her carefully organized wedding day went awry when the cake was delivered late.

Synset Examples

The whole plan went akilter when the main speaker canceled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go wrong - to develop in an unexpected or unsuccessful way
