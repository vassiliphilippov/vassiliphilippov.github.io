to get out of someone's face

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt aʊt əv ˈsʌmwʌnz feɪs / (AmE), / ɡɛt aʊt əv ˈsʌmwʌnz feɪs / (BrE)
(informal) to stop bothering or annoying someone; to leave someone alone, especially when they feel crowded or harassed.
stop bothering or annoying someone
informal

Examples

If you don't want my advice, just get out of my face and do it your way.
She told her brother to get out of her face after he kept teasing her.
He was being so pushy that I had to tell him to get out of my face.

Synset Examples

I told him to get out of my face during the meeting.
He was bothering her, so she told him to get out of her face.
