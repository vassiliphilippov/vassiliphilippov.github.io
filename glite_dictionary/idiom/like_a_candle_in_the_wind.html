like a candle in the wind

1. X

/ laɪk ə ˈkændl ɪn ðə wɪnd / (AmE), / laɪk ə ˈkændl ɪn ðə wɪnd / (BrE)
used to describe something that is fragile, unstable, or easily destroyed, often by external forces or circumstances
fragile or easily destroyed

Examples

After her illness, his optimism was like a candle in the wind, barely holding on.
Small businesses in the crisis were like a candle in the wind, struggling to survive.
Their hope for peace remained like a candle in the wind, flickering in difficult times.

Synset Examples

His dreams faded like a candle in the wind.
