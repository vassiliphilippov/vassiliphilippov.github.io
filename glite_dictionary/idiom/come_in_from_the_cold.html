to come in from the cold

1. VERB

/ kʌm ɪn frəm ðə koʊld / (AmE), / kʌm ɪn frəm ðə kəʊld / (BrE)
to return to a safe, familiar, or more accepted situation after being isolated, excluded, or in a difficult position
return to safety or acceptance

Examples

After years of working alone, she decided to come in from the cold and join a new team.
The government invited political dissidents to come in from the cold and participate in reforms.
He finally felt it was safe to come in from the cold after a long period of estrangement.

Synset Examples

It's time to come in from the cold and reconnect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to come back - to return to a place, position, or state

Etymology

From espionage jargon, popularized by the John le Carré novel 'The Spy Who Came in from the Cold' (1963).
