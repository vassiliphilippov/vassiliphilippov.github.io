to kick start my day

1. VERB

/ kɪk stɑrt maɪ deɪ / (AmE), / kɪk stɑːt maɪ deɪ / (BrE)
to do something that gives you energy or motivation at the start of the day
begin the day with energy or motivation
informal

Examples

A brisk walk helps me kick start my day and feel refreshed.
Many people rely on coffee to kick start their day.
I listen to upbeat music in the morning to kick start my day.

Synset Examples

I need coffee to kick start my day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to energize - to give energy to; invigorate
  • Synonym: to motivate - to provide someone with a reason to act; stimulate enthusiasm

Etymology

From 'kick-start', originating from starting a motorcycle engine by forcefully depressing a pedal; here used figuratively for energizing the start of one's day.
