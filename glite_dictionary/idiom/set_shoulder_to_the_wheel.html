to set shoulder to the wheel

1. VERB

/ sɛt ˈʃoʊldər tə ðə wil / (AmE), / sɛt ˈʃəʊldə tə ðə wiːl / (BrE)
to begin working very hard and with determination at a difficult task
start to work very hard; make a determined effort

Examples

When the team faced a tough challenge, everyone set shoulder to the wheel and worked overtime.
It's time to set shoulder to the wheel if we want to finish this project by Friday.
She set shoulder to the wheel after receiving her assignment, refusing to give up until it was done.

Synset Examples

She set her shoulder to the wheel to meet the deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: put your back into it - work hard at something; make a great physical or mental effort
  • Synonym: go the extra mile - to do more than is expected or required

Etymology

From the imagery of pushing a wheel (such as a heavy cart) by putting one's shoulder to it, symbolizing effort and hard work.
