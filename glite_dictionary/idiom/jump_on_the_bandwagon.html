to jump on the bandwagon

1. VERB

/ dʒʌmp ɑn ðə ˈbændˌwæɡən / (AmE), / dʒʌmp ɒn ðə ˈbændˌwæɡən / (BrE)
to join others in doing something that is currently popular or fashionable, especially in order to gain an advantage
join a popular trend or activity

Examples

After the product became a bestseller, many companies tried to jump on the bandwagon and release similar items.
It's easy to jump on the bandwagon when a new trend is sweeping through social media.
She resisted the urge to jump on the bandwagon just because all her friends were trying the latest diet.

Synset Examples

The team’s success caused many fans to hop on the bandwagon.
It's easy to jump on the bandwagon during election season.
Everyone seems to jump on the bandwagon for that new trend.
Everyone jumped on the bandwagon when he won.
You shouldn’t just jump on the bandwagon for popularity.
He tends to jump on the bandwagon when a new diet is popular.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hop on the bandwagon - to join a popular activity or cause

Etymology

From the phrase "bandwagon," originally referring to a wagon carrying a musical band in a parade; used metaphorically since the late 19th century for following a popular movement.
