drop in the ocean

1. NOUN

/ drɑp ɪn ði ˈoʊʃən / (AmE), / drɒp ɪn ði ˈəʊʃən / (BrE)
a very small amount or effort compared to what is needed or expected, especially in relation to a much larger problem or quantity
very small, insignificant amount

Examples

Our company’s reduction in emissions was just a drop in the ocean compared to the global scale of the problem.
Donating one can of food might feel like a drop in the ocean, but every little bit helps.
Her efforts to clean the beach seemed like a drop in the ocean, but she continued undeterred.

Synset Examples

A $100 donation from an individual is generous, but it is a drop in the bucket compared to the $500,000 fundraising goal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drop in the bucket - a very small part of something big or whole
