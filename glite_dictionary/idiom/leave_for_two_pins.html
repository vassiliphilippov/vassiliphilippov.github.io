to leave for two pins

1. VERB

/ liv fɔr tu pɪnz / (AmE), / liːv fə tuː pɪnz / (BrE)
To be very willing or ready to leave a place or situation, often at the slightest provocation or incentive.
depart at the slightest incentive
archaic

Examples

After the argument, I’d leave for two pins if I had somewhere else to go.
She said she would leave for two pins if the conditions didn't improve.
Sometimes, I feel like I could leave for two pins when things get tough.

Synset Examples

I’d leave for two pins if I could.

Etymology

Refers to the small value of 'two pins', suggesting leaving even for a trivial reason.
