to blow one's stack

1. VERB

/ bloʊ wʌnz stæk / (AmE), / bləʊ wʌnz stak / (BrE)
to suddenly become extremely angry or lose one's temper
suddenly become very angry

Examples

If you keep interrupting him, he’ll blow his stack sooner or later.
She tends to blow her stack when things don’t go as planned.
The referee almost blew his stack after the players kept arguing.

Synset Examples

The professor combusted when the student didn't know the answer to a very elementary question
Spam makes me go ballistic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lose one's temper - to suddenly become angry and unable to control oneself
  • Antonym: to keep one's cool - to remain calm and not get angry
