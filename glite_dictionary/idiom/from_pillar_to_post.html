from pillar to post

1. ADV

/ frəm ˈpɪlɚ tə ˈpoʊst / (AmE), / frəm ˈpɪlə tə ˈpəʊst / (BrE)
moving repeatedly from one place or situation to another, usually in a confused or difficult way.
repeatedly moved from place to place

Examples

After the accident, she was sent from pillar to post seeking help.
When the company restructured, many employees were moved from pillar to post.
His complaints were dismissed and he was bounced from pillar to post by different departments.

Synset Examples

we were driven from pillar to post

Etymology

Possibly from the old practice of moving prisoners from one pillar (whipping post) to another for punishment.

2. ADV

/ frəm ˈpɪlər tə poʊst / (AmE), / frəm ˈpɪlə tə pəʊst / (BrE)
from one place to another in a hurried, disorganized, or aimless way, typically with inconvenience or confusion
in a disorganized or aimless manner, from place to place

Examples

She was sent from pillar to post while searching for the right department.
After losing his job, he wandered from pillar to post with no real plan.
During the merger, employees felt as if they were being moved from pillar to post.

Synset Examples

They chased me from pillar to post during the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hither and thither - moving from place to place in a disorganized way
