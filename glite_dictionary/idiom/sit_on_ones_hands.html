to sit on one's hands

1. VERB

/ sɪt ɑn wʌnz hændz / (AmE), / sɪt ɒn wʌnz hændz / (BrE)
to deliberately remain inactive or refrain from taking action, especially when intervention is expected or needed
to remain inactive or refrain from acting

Examples

Despite the problems in the community, the council continued to sit on their hands and offered no support.
He sat on his hands during the meeting, refusing to share any of his ideas.
The team can't afford to sit on its hands while competitors innovate.

Synset Examples

She sat on her hands instead of voicing her opinion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to do nothing - to take no action or refrain from activity
  • Antonym: to take action - to intervene or do something
