to scratch beneath the surface

1. VERB

/ skrætʃ bəˈniːθ ðə ˈsɜrfɪs / (AmE), / skrætʃ bɪˈniːθ ðə ˈsɜːfɪs / (BrE)
to look beyond outward appearances and investigate more deeply or thoroughly into something
investigate deeply; look beyond appearances

Examples

If you scratch beneath the surface, you'll discover the team's real motivations.
Most documentaries scratch beneath the surface of important social issues.
She likes to scratch beneath the surface and uncover hidden stories when reporting.

Synset Examples

We need to scratch beneath the surface to understand the issue.
It's essential to scratch beneath the surface in research.
If you scratch beneath the surface, you'll find deeper problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go deeper - to investigate something in more depth
  • Antonym: to take at face value - to accept something as it appears without deeper investigation
