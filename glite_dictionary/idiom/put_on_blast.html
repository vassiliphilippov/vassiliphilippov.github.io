to put on blast

1. VERB

/ pʊt ɑn blæst / (AmE), / pʊt ɒn blɑːst / (BrE)
to publicly criticize, shame, or expose someone, often in a harsh or attention-grabbing way
publicly criticize or expose
informal slang

Examples

The journalist put on blast several companies for their unethical practices.
She was put on blast on social media for plagiarizing her work.
He regretted putting on blast his colleague during the meeting.

Synset Examples

They put her on blast for her controversial opinions.
She put him on blast for his late arrival.
He put the team's mistakes on blast during the presentation.
The politician was put on blast for his controversial comments.
Her embarrassing photos were put on blast by a friend.
He was put on blast during the live stream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to call out - to criticize someone publicly for wrongdoing
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration publicly
