to pile on the pressure

1. VERB

/ paɪl ɑn ðə ˈprɛʃər / (AmE), / paɪl ɒn ðə ˈprɛʃə / (BrE)
to increase the amount of stress or demands on someone, especially to influence their actions or decisions
increase demands or stress on someone

Examples

The coach continued to pile on the pressure as the team struggled to keep up.
If you pile on the pressure, she might make mistakes.
After they piled on the pressure, the negotiations moved quickly.

Synset Examples

Don’t pile on the pressure; let her relax.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: put pressure on - to try to make someone do something by influencing them
  • Antonym: to ease off - to reduce the pressure or demands on someone
