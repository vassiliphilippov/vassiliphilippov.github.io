to walk away without a scratch

1. VERB

/ wɑk əˈweɪ wɪˈðaʊt ə skrætʃ / (AmE), / wɔːk əˈweɪ wɪˈðaʊt ə skrætʃ / (BrE)
to leave an accident or dangerous situation without suffering any injury or harm
escape unharmed from danger or accident

Examples

Despite the severity of the accident, she managed to walk away without a scratch.
The fire destroyed most of the building, but he walked away without a scratch.
After falling off his bicycle, he was relieved to walk away without a scratch.

Synset Examples

He crashed the bike but walked away without a scratch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: come out unscathed - to emerge from a dangerous situation without injury
