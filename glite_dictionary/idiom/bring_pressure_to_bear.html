to bring pressure to bear

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ ˈprɛʃər tə bɛr / (AmE), / brɪŋ ˈprɛʃə tə beə / (BrE)
to use influence or force to persuade someone or achieve a particular result
apply influence or force

Examples

The union threatened to bring pressure to bear on the company during negotiations.
She hoped public opinion would bring pressure to bear on the council to change its decision.
Activists are bringing pressure to bear on world leaders to address climate change.

Synset Examples

The organization sought to bring pressure to bear on the government.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exert pressure - to use influence to try to achieve something
