to clear the decks

1. VERB

/ klɪr ðə dɛks / (AmE), / klɪə ðə dɛks / (BrE)
to remove obstacles or finish current tasks in order to prepare for something new or important
remove obstacles to prepare for a new task

Examples

Before launching the new campaign, we should clear the decks to focus our efforts.
She spent the weekend clearing the decks so she could devote time to her studies.
It's wise to clear the decks before taking on such a big responsibility.

Synset Examples

We need to clear the decks before starting the new project.
It's time to clear the decks for a fresh start.
He decided to clear the decks and simplify his life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prepare - to make ready for use or consideration
  • Synonym: start afresh - to begin again with a clean state

Etymology

Originally a nautical term referring to preparing a ship for action by clearing everything from the decks.
