to keep one's end up

1. VERB

/ kip wʌnz ɛnd ʌp / (AmE), / kiːp wʌnz ɛnd ʌp / (BrE)
to continue to do one's share of work or fulfill one's responsibilities, especially in difficult circumstances
fulfill responsibilities; maintain standards under difficulty

Examples

You really have to keep your end up if you want the team to succeed.
Despite the challenges, she managed to keep her end up throughout the project.
It's important that everyone keeps their end up so that the plan goes smoothly.

Synset Examples

Everyone needs to keep their end up for success.
He struggled but managed to keep his end up.
She promised to keep her end up in the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pull one's weight - to do one's fair share of work
  • Antonym: let the side down - to fail to do one's part or disappoint a group
