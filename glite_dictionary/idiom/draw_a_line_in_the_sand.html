to draw a line in the sand

1. VERB

/ drɔ ə laɪn ɪn ðə sænd / (AmE), / drɔː ə laɪn ɪn ðə sand / (BrE)
to set a clear limit or boundary, especially one that, once crossed, signals a new situation or consequences
set a definite limit or boundary

Examples

After years of giving in, she finally decided to draw a line in the sand and say no.
The teacher had to draw a line in the sand to maintain discipline in the classroom.
We need to draw a line in the sand when it comes to unacceptable behavior.

Synset Examples

He knew he needed to draw a line in the sand regarding work-life balance.
The company drew a line in the sand about unethical practices.
They set a line in the sand, refusing to compromise any further.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: set boundaries - to establish limits for acceptable behavior or actions
  • Antonym: to cross the line - to go beyond acceptable limits
