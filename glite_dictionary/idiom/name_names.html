to name names

1. VERB

/ neɪm neɪmz / (AmE), / neɪm neɪmz / (BrE)
to reveal or identify specific individuals, especially in a context involving secrecy, accusation, or controversy
reveal identities, often controversially

Examples

Under pressure from the authorities, he finally agreed to name names.
She refused to name names, fearing retaliation from those involved.
The report threatens to name names and expose major players in the scandal.

Synset Examples

If you know who did it, you should name names.
The journalist promised to name names in her next article.
In his testimony, he refused to name names despite pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inform - to give information about someone, often to authorities
  • Antonym: conceal identities - to keep the identities of people secret
