to take liberties

1. VERB

/ teɪk ˈlɪbɚtiz / (AmE), / teɪk ˈlɪbətiz / (BrE)
to behave in a way that is overfamiliar, presumptuous, or beyond what is acceptable, especially without permission.
act without permission or beyond acceptable bounds

Examples

He knew better than to take liberties with company policy, fearing repercussions.
I don’t appreciate it when you take liberties with my personal belongings.
Some guests take liberties that make the hosts uncomfortable.

Synset Examples

He tends to take liberties when making decisions.
Don't take liberties with my trust.
You shouldn’t take liberties in a professional setting.
