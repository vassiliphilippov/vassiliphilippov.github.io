to rain buckets

1. VERB

/ reɪn ˈbʌkɪts / (AmE), / reɪn ˈbʌkɪts / (BrE)
(of rain) to fall very heavily; to rain in large amounts
rain very heavily (informal idiom)
informal

Examples

It started to rain buckets just as we left the house for our walk.
When it rained buckets last night, the streets were flooded by morning.
If it keeps raining buckets like this, the match will be canceled.

Synset Examples

Put on your rain coat — it's pouring outside!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pour - to rain very heavily
  • Degree Lower: drizzle - to rain lightly
