to hold the line

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ðə laɪn / (AmE), / həʊld ðə laɪn / (BrE)
to refuse to change a position, stance, or limit, especially in the face of pressure
maintain a position without yielding

Examples

The government pledged to hold the line on spending despite rising costs.
Despite mounting pressure, the negotiators managed to hold the line during all discussions.
During the crisis, leaders asked everyone to hold the line and remain calm.

Synset Examples

We must hold the line against the competition.
She urged everyone to hold the line during the crisis.
The team is determined to hold the line on prices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stand firm - refuse to change one's decision or position
  • Antonym: to give ground - yield or compromise on a position
