to step on it

1. VERB

/ stɛp ɑn ɪt / (AmE), / stɛp ɒn ɪt / (BrE)
to go faster or hurry up, especially when driving or in urgent situations
go faster; hurry up (idiom)
informal

Examples

If you want to catch the train, step on it!
We told the taxi driver to step on it so we wouldn't miss our flight.
When he saw the storm coming, he decided to step on it and get home faster.

Synset Examples

If you want to be on time, step on it!
I told him to step on it or we'd be late.
We need to step on it to reach the airport.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hurry up - move or act more quickly
  • Antonym: to slow down - reduce speed or go more slowly

Etymology

Originates from the action of pressing the accelerator ('step on the gas') in a car.
