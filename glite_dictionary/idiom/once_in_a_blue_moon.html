once in a blue moon

1. ADV

/ ˌwʌns ɪn ə bluː muːn / (AmE), / ˌwʌns ɪn ə bluː muːn / (BrE)
very rarely; not very often
very rarely

Examples

I only see my cousins once in a blue moon because they live so far away.
She treats herself to dessert once in a blue moon, usually on her birthday.
We have staff meetings once in a blue moon, so I never worry about them.

Synset Examples

She goes to the theater once in a blue moon.
They visit their grandparents once in a blue moon.
I eat fast food once in a blue moon.
He gets a day off once in a purple moon.
They travel abroad once in a purple moon.
She tries new recipes once in a purple moon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hardly ever - almost never; very infrequently
  • Antonym: frequently - often; commonly

Etymology

Originates from the rare occurrence of a second full moon in a single calendar month, which is called a 'blue moon.'
