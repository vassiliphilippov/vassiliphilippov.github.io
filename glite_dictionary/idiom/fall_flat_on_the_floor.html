to fall flat on the floor

1. VERB

/ fɔl flæt ɑn ðə flɔr / (AmE), / fɔːl flæt ɒn ðə flɔː / (BrE)
to suddenly and completely fall so that the body is lying face down or outstretched on the ground or floor
suddenly fall down completely

Examples

He slipped on the ice and fell flat on the floor in front of everyone.
Children sometimes fall flat on the floor when playing too roughly.
She laughed so hard she nearly fell flat on the floor.

Synset Examples

He fell flat on the floor after tripping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fall down - to lose balance and drop to a lower position
