to buck the trend

1. VERB

/ bʌk ðə trɛnd / (AmE), / bʌk ðə trɛnd / (BrE)
to act in opposition to the prevailing direction, opinion, or practice; to do something different from what most people are doing
resist or act against a prevailing trend

Examples

Several small businesses managed to buck the trend during the economic downturn and actually grew their profits.
Maria decided to buck the trend by starting a career in farming rather than moving to the city.
The young artist chose to buck the trend of digital art by working exclusively with oils and canvas.

Synset Examples

He aims to buck the trend of rising costs in the industry.
She decided to buck the trend and wear vintage clothing.
Many entrepreneurs buck the trend by focusing on sustainability.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: go with the flow - to accept and follow what most people are doing
