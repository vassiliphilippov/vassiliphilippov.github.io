off keel

1. ADJ

/ ɑf kil / (AmE), / ɒf kiːl / (BrE)
not stable or balanced, either emotionally or physically; unsteady or unsettled.
not stable or balanced

Examples

After the long flight, I felt completely off keel for hours.
The unexpected news left him emotionally off keel.
The old boat was listing badly and seemed off keel on the water.

Synset Examples

His sudden mood swing left everyone feeling off keel.
The boat was off keel, making it hard to steer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unbalanced - not stable or steady, physically or mentally
  • Antonym: steady - firmly fixed, supported, or balanced; not shaking or moving

Etymology

Derived from nautical terminology, where 'keel' refers to the main structural element of a ship that keeps it upright.
