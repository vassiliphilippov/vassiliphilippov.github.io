to ride one's luck

1. VERB

/ raɪd wʌnz lʌk / (AmE), / raɪd wʌnz lʌk / (BrE)
to depend on continued good fortune or chance rather than on one's own efforts
rely on chance or good fortune

Examples

After winning several games in a row, Carla decided not to ride her luck any further.
He managed to pass every test by riding his luck instead of studying.
If you keep riding your luck, it will eventually run out.

Synset Examples

He tends to ride his luck instead of preparing.
Don't ride your luck too far or it might run out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take a chance - to do something risky in the hope of success
  • Synonym: to tempt fate - to take unnecessary risks that could bring bad luck
