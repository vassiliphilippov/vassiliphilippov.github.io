to press the right buttons

1. VERB

/ prɛs ðə raɪt ˈbʌtn̩z / (AmE), / prɛs ðə raɪt ˈbʌtn̩z / (BrE)
to do or say things that produce the desired reaction or emotion in someone
provoke the desired response

Examples

She knew exactly how to press the right buttons to get what she wanted from her boss.
Politicians often try to press the right buttons to win voters’ support.
Whenever I’m upset, my friend always manages to press the right buttons to cheer me up.

Synset Examples

He always knew how to tekan the right buttons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: push the right buttons - to provoke the desired response; alternative phrasing
  • Synonym: press someone’s buttons - to deliberately provoke or annoy someone
