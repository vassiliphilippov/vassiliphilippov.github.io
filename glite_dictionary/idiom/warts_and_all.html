warts and all

1. X

/ wɔrts ənd ɔl / (AmE), / wɔːts ənd ɔːl / (BrE)
including all flaws or imperfections; without trying to hide any faults.
including all faults or imperfections

Examples

The biography recounts the author's life warts and all, never shying away from controversy.
She loved the old house warts and all, appreciating its character despite its worn look.
We should confront the truth warts and all, rather than pretending everything is perfect.

Synset Examples

He presented his story warts and all, without hiding anything.
The documentary portrayed the artist warts and all.
She accepted him, warts and all, despite his flaws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unvarnished - plain and honest; not disguised or embellished

Etymology

Said to derive from a reputed comment by Oliver Cromwell, who supposedly ordered his portrait to be painted 'warts and all'.
