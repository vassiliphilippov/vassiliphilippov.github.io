to sell the family silver

1. VERB

/ sɛl ðə ˈfæməli ˈsɪlvɚ / (AmE), / sɛl ðə ˈfæməli ˈsɪlvə / (BrE)
to sell valuable assets, especially those of sentimental or long-term value, usually in order to overcome a financial crisis or short-term difficulty
sell valuable possessions in a financial crisis

Examples

With debts piling up, the company was forced to sell the family silver to stay afloat.
Some argue that privatizing public utilities is like selling the family silver.
Rather than sell the family silver, she tried to find another way to solve her money problems.

Synset Examples

During the crisis, they had to sell the family silver to make ends meet.
