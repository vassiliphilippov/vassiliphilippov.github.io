to return to form

1. VERB

/ ɹɪˈtɝn tə fɔɹm / (AmE), / ɹɪˈtɜːn tə fɔːm / (BrE)
to regain a previous level of ability, skill, or performance after a decline or absence
regain previous ability or performance

Examples

The singer managed to return to form after several disappointing albums.
After an injury, the player struggled but eventually returned to form in the finals.
Fans were thrilled to see the director return to form with his latest film.

Synset Examples

She hopes to return to form after recovering from injury.
After a rough patch, the athlete finally returned to form.
The band returned to form with their latest album.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bounce back - to recover quickly after a setback or failure
  • Antonym: slump - temporary decline in performance or ability
