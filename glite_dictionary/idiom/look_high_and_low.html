to look high and low

1. VERB

/ lʊk haɪ ənd loʊ / (AmE), / lʊk haɪ ənd ləʊ / (BrE)
to search everywhere thoroughly for something; to make an exhaustive effort to find someone or something
search everywhere thoroughly

Examples

We looked high and low for the missing document, but it was nowhere to be found.
He looked high and low before realizing he'd left his wallet at home.
They've looked high and low for a solution, but nothing seems to work.

Synset Examples

I've looked high and low for my lost keys.
She searched high and low for the perfect gift.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search high and low - to search everywhere thoroughly for something
