the dickens

1. NOUN

/ ðə ˈdɪkənz / (AmE), / ðə ˈdɪkɪnz / (BrE)
Used to express surprise, confusion, or emphasis, often in questions or exclamations.
expression of surprise or confusion
archaic

Examples

The dickens! I completely forgot about the meeting this morning.
What the dickens is making that strange noise upstairs?
He looked at me as if to say, "Who the dickens are you?"

Synset Examples

Where on earth have you been, the dickens?
What the dickens is happening here?
It was as clear as day, but what the dickens did he mean?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the devil - used similarly for surprise or emphasis in questions

Etymology

Probably a euphemism for 'devil', first used in the 16th century.
