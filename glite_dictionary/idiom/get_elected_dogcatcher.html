to get elected dogcatcher

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɪˈlɛktɪd ˈdɔɡˌkætʃɚ / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɪˈlɛktɪd ˈdɒɡˌkætʃə / (BrE)
to fail to be elected to even the most minor or insignificant office, indicating lack of support or popularity
fail to be elected to a minor position

Examples

Despite running several times, she couldn't even get elected dogcatcher in her hometown.
They joked that he was so unpopular he wouldn't get elected dogcatcher if he tried.
Even with endorsements, his campaign was so weak he failed to get elected dogcatcher.

Synset Examples

With his popularity, he could not get elected dogcatcher.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogcatcher - a person hired to capture and manage stray dogs

Etymology

References the position of dogcatcher, a historically low-level and sometimes ridiculed public office in the US.
