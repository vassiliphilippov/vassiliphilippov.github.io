to run wild

1. VERB

/ rʌn waɪld / (AmE), / rʌn waɪld / (BrE)
to behave or develop without restraint, control, or limitation
act or develop without restraint

Examples

During summer vacation, the children run wild around the neighborhood.
Don't let your spending habits run wild or you'll regret it later.
When left alone, her imagination tends to run wild with possibilities.

Synset Examples

He allowed his thoughts to run wild on the topic.
Let your imagination run wild while writing.
The kids let their creativity run wild during art class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to let loose - to allow to behave freely; to set free from restraint
  • Antonym: to restrain - to keep under control; to limit freedom
