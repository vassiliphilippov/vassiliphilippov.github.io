to put a full stop

1. VERB

/ pʊt ə fʊl stɑp / (AmE), / pʊt ə fʊl stɒp / (BrE)
to end or bring something to a complete stop; to cease entirely
end or cease completely

Examples

The company decided to put a full stop to the project after the budget was cut.
She realized it was time to put a full stop to unhealthy habits.
The manager finally put a full stop to the endless arguments in the meeting.

Synset Examples

After the incident, they decided to put a full stop to all operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bring to an end - cause something to stop or finish
  • Synonym: to terminate - to bring to a final end
