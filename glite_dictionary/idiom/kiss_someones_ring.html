to kiss someone's ring

1. VERB

/ kɪs ˈsʌmwʌnz rɪŋ / (AmE), / kɪs ˈsʌmwənz rɪŋ / (BrE)
to show respect, deference, or submission to someone in authority, often in a way that is seen as overly deferential or servile
show deference or submission to authority

Examples

She refused to kiss his ring just to get a promotion at work.
Some politicians are known for kissing the ring of influential donors.
He made it clear he would never kiss anyone's ring to gain favor.

Synset Examples

In politics, you often have to kiss someone's ring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kowtow - to act in an excessively subservient manner
  • Synonym: to grovel - to act in a way that shows too much respect for someone powerful

Etymology

Refers to the traditional gesture of physically kissing a powerful person's ring, such as a monarch's or religious leader's, as a sign of obedience or respect.
