no better than one ought to be

1. X

/ noʊ ˈbɛtər ðæn wʌn ˈɔt tə bi / (AmE), / nəʊ ˈbɛtə ðən wʌn ˈɔːt tə bi / (BrE)
not as well-behaved or moral as expected; having acted improperly or feeling dissatisfied with one's own conduct or situation
not as moral or proper as expected

Examples

Although he apologized, she still thought he was no better than one ought to be.
After missing the meeting, Tom felt no better than one ought to be.
Gossip spread quickly in the village about those deemed no better than one ought to be.

Synset Examples

He was no better than one ought to be last night.
She felt no better than one ought to be after the exam.
