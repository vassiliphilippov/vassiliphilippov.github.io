to watch each other's back

1. VERB

/ wɑtʃ itʃ ˈʌðɚz bæk / (AmE), / wɒtʃ iːtʃ ˈʌðəz bæk / (BrE)
to protect or support each other, especially by being alert to possible dangers or problems
to protect or support one another

Examples

In dangerous situations, police officers always watch each other's back.
Teammates have to watch each other's back to succeed in a tough competition.
True friends will always watch each other's back when things get tough.

Synset Examples

Friends should always watch each other's back.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: look out for one another - to care for or keep watch over each other
