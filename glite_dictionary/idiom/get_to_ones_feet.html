to get to one's feet

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt tə wʌnz fit / (AmE), / ɡɛt tə wʌnz fiːt / (BrE)
to stand up after sitting or lying down, or to recover and regain strength after a setback
stand up; recover after difficulty

Examples

After tripping on the stairs, it took him a moment to get to his feet again.
Despite losing her job, she managed to get to her feet within a few months.
The crowd watched in suspense as the injured player slowly tried to get to his feet.

Synset Examples

He struggled to get to his feet after the fall.
After a long day, she was eager to get to her feet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stand up - to rise to a standing position
  • Antonym: to sit down - to lower oneself into a sitting position

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt tu wʌnz fit / (AmE), / ɡɛt tə wʌnz fiːt / (BrE)
to stand up, usually in response to something happening, such as applause or excitement
stand up (in response)

Examples

The entire audience got to their feet to applaud the speaker's powerful message.
When his name was called, he slowly got to his feet and walked to the stage.
After the national anthem began, the players got to their feet out of respect.

Synset Examples

The crowd rose to get to their feet when the band started playing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stand up - to come to a standing position
  • Antonym: to sit down - to move from standing to sitting position
