to kick up one's heels

1. VERB

/ kɪk ʌp wʌnz hilz / (AmE), / kɪk ʌp wʌnz hiːlz / (BrE)
to have fun, celebrate, or enjoy oneself in a lively way
celebrate; enjoy oneself energetically

Examples

After finishing the big project, the whole team went out to kick up their heels.
Sometimes you just need a night to kick up your heels and forget your worries.
Graduation is the perfect time to kick up your heels and celebrate your achievements.

Synset Examples

It's time to kick up my heels and celebrate!
She decided to kick up her heels after a long week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to celebrate - to observe or mark a happy occasion
  • Synonym: to let one's hair down - to behave in a relaxed or uninhibited way
