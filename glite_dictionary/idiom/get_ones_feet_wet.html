to get one's feet wet

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt wʌnz fit wɛt / (AmE), / ɡɛt wʌnz fiːt wɛt / (BrE)
to begin to participate in something and gain initial experience, often in a cautious or limited way
start gaining experience

Examples

She wanted to get her feet wet in marketing before making a career switch.
It's normal to feel nervous when you get your feet wet in a new field.
Internships are a great way for students to get their feet wet professionally.

Synset Examples

I want to get my feet wet before diving into the project.
She needs to get her feet wet in this new role.
He decided to get his feet wet by volunteering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dip one's toe in - to try something cautiously or for the first time
  • Degree Higher: to dive in - to start doing something in a very enthusiastic way
