like a behenji

1. ADV

/ laɪk ə beɪˈhɛnʤi / (AmE), / laɪk ə beˈhɛnʤi / (BrE)
in a way that is conservative or traditional, often implying old-fashioned manners or conventional behavior (chiefly South Asian English, informal)
acting conservatively or traditionally (informal, South Asian English)
informal

Examples

Even at the party, she behaved like a behenji and avoided dancing.
Whenever my cousin visits, she tells me I'm dressing like a behenji.
He teased his sister for acting like a behenji in front of their relatives.

Synset Examples

She acted like a behenji despite her glamorous outfit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conservatively - acting in a cautious, traditional, or conventional way
  • Antonym: like a hipster - acting in a trendy or unconventional manner

Etymology

From Hindi 'behenji' (elder sister), used informally in Indian English to describe a woman regarded as conventional or unfashionable.
