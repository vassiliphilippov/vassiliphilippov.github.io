to read to filth

1. VERB

/ ɹid tə fɪlθ / (AmE), / ɹiːd tə fɪlθ / (BrE)
to criticize or insult someone harshly, often in a thorough or ruthless manner
criticize harshly; insult thoroughly
informal slang

Examples

After hearing about the mistake, she read to filth the entire team during the meeting.
The comedian read to filth the celebrity guests, sparing no one from his jokes.
He was so upset that he read to filth his opponent in front of the whole class.

Synset Examples

She read him to filth for his rude comments.
He didn't hold back; he read her to filth about her attitude.
The critic read the movie director to filth in his review.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tear into - criticize someone harshly
  • Synonym: roast - criticize someone humorously or harshly
  • Antonym: praise - express admiration or approval

Etymology

Popularized in African-American drag and LGBTQ+ communities; 'read' meaning to call out flaws.
