to carry one's weight

1. VERB

/ ˈkæri wʌnz weɪt / (AmE), / ˈkari wʌnz weɪt / (BrE)
to do one's fair share of work or contribute equally to a group effort
do one's fair share of work in a group

Examples

In our team, everyone is expected to carry their weight if we want to succeed.
She quickly learned to carry her weight on the project after seeing how dedicated everyone was.
If you don't carry your weight, the rest of the group has to make up for it.

Synset Examples

He felt guilty for not carrying his weight in the group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pull one's weight - do one's fair share of work

2. VERB

/ ˈkæri wʌnz weɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæri wʌnz weɪt / (BrE)
to do your fair share of work or to contribute equally and responsibly within a group or team
do a fair share of work

Examples

Everyone in the household needs to carry their weight to keep things running smoothly.
If you join our project, we expect you to carry your weight just like everyone else.
The new employee quickly proved she could carry her weight on the team.

Synset Examples

In a team, everyone must carry their weight to succeed.
