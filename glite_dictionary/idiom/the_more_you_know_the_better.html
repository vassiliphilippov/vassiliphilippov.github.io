the more you know, the better

1. X

/ ðə mɔr ju noʊ ðə ˈbɛtər / (AmE), / ðə mɔː juː nəʊ ðə ˈbɛtə / (BrE)
used to say that gaining more knowledge leads to better decisions and outcomes
having more knowledge is advantageous

Examples

Teachers often remind students that the more you know, the better, encouraging them to keep learning.
When researching a new job, the more you know, the better your chances of making the right choice.
In cooking and many other skills, the more you know, the better your results will be.

Synset Examples

She always says, 'The more you know, the better choices you make.'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knowledge is power - the idea that knowledge gives people control or advantage
