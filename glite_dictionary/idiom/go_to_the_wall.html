to go to the wall

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ tə ðə wɑl / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə ðə wɔːl / (BrE)
to support or stand up for someone in a difficult or challenging situation
support someone in difficulty (idiom)

Examples

When his colleague was unfairly accused, he decided to go to the wall for her.
True friends are the ones who will go to the wall for you when times are tough.
She knew she could rely on her sister to go to the wall whenever she needed support.

Synset Examples

She felt she had to go to the wall for her friend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stand up for - defend or support someone or something
  • Antonym: abandon - to leave or stop supporting someone
