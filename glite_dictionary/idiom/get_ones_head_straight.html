to get one's head straight

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt wʌnz hɛd streɪt / (AmE), / ɡɛt wʌnz hɛd streɪt / (BrE)
to clear one's mind or regain mental focus; to become able to think clearly again after confusion or emotional stress
to clear one's mind; regain mental focus
informal

Examples

After the argument, she went for a long walk to get her head straight.
He canceled his meetings to get his head straight after receiving the shocking news.
Sometimes you just need some quiet time alone to get your head straight.

Synset Examples

After the breakup, he needed time to get his head straight.
She took a day off to get her head straight.
It's important to get your head straight before making decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pull oneself together - to regain control of one's emotions
  • Antonym: to lose one's head - to lose control or act irrationally under stress
