to look on the dark side

1. VERB

/ lʊk ɑn ðə dɑrk saɪd / (AmE), / lʊk ɒn ðə dɑːk saɪd / (BrE)
to focus on the negative or pessimistic aspects of a situation, especially expecting the worst
focus on negative aspects

Examples

When the project encountered problems, Tim tried not to look on the dark side.
Some people naturally look on the dark side of every situation, seeing only what could go wrong.
Though it rained on their wedding day, they refused to look on the dark side and enjoyed every moment.

Synset Examples

It's easy to look on the dark side during tough times.
She tends to look on the dark side when faced with challenges.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to look on the bright side - focus on positive or hopeful aspects
