to give oneself airs

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv wʌnˈsɛlf ɛrz / (AmE), / ɡɪv wʌnˈsɛlf ɛəz / (BrE)
to behave in a way that shows you think you are more important or superior to others.
act superior or pretentious

Examples

She tends to give herself airs whenever she's among new people.
After winning the award, he began to give himself airs and look down on his colleagues.
You shouldn't give yourself airs just because of one small achievement.

Synset Examples

She gives herself airs, acting like she's better than us.
Don't give yourself airs just because you got a promotion.
He tends to give himself airs when he's around rich people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to put on airs - to behave as if you are superior to others
