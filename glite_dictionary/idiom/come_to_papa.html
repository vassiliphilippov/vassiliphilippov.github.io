come to papa

1. X

/ kʌm tə ˈpɑpə / (AmE), / kʌm tə ˈpɑːpə / (BrE)
used as an exclamation to indicate eagerness or anticipation, especially when receiving something desired or when someone is approaching
eager anticipation, especially for receiving something
informal

Examples

When the winning lottery numbers were announced, he shouted, 'Come to papa!'
As the waiter brought the dessert tray over, she grinned and said, 'Come to papa.'
He finally spotted the last piece of pizza and reached for it with a triumphant 'come to papa.'

Synset Examples

The kids shouted, 'Come to papa!' as they ran to their dad.
Finally, the long-awaited package has arrived; come to papa!
When the dog returned the ball, he exclaimed, 'Come to papa!'
