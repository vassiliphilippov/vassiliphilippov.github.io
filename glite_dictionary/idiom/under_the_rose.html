under the rose

1. ADV

/ ˈʌndɚ ðə roʊz / (AmE), / ˈʌndə ðə rəʊz / (BrE)
in secret; confidentially; not to be disclosed to others
in secret; confidentially

Examples

The committee discussed the issue under the rose to avoid public attention.
Sensitive information was shared under the rose among trusted colleagues only.
All negotiations took place under the rose until an agreement was reached.

Synset Examples

Everything discussed in the meeting was under the rose.
Conversations about the merger were held under the rose.
They kept their plans under the rose for confidentiality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sub rosa - in secret or privately

Etymology

From Latin sub rosa (under the rose), as the rose was an ancient symbol of secrecy.
