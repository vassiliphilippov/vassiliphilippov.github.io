to call the tune

1. VERB

/ kɔl ðə tun / (AmE), / kɔːl ðə tjuːn / (BrE)
to be in control or to have the authority to make important decisions; to exercise authority
be in charge; exercise authority

Examples

As the new manager, she calls the tune in the department now.
Whoever pays for the project usually calls the tune.
He likes to believe he calls the tune, but everyone ignores his orders.

Synset Examples

Who is calling the shots in this house?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to call the shots - to be in control or make the important decisions
