to make a move

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə muːv / (AmE), / meɪk ə muːv / (BrE)
to take action or initiative, especially after a period of inaction or hesitation
take action or initiative

Examples

It's important to make a move when you see an opportunity.
They waited to see if the other side would make a move first.
After months of indecision, she finally decided to make a move.

Synset Examples

They need to make a move before the deadline.

Related Terms
