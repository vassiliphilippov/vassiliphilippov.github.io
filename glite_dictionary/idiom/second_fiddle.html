second fiddle

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛk.ənd ˈfɪd.əl / (AmE), / ˈsɛk.ənd ˈfɪd.əl / (BrE)
a subordinate or less important role in relation to someone or something else
subordinate role; secondary position

Examples

She was tired of always playing second fiddle in the company.
In their partnership, he refused to take second fiddle to anyone.
Although he was talented, he often ended up as second fiddle to his more outgoing brother.

Synset Examples

he hated to play second fiddle to anyone

Related Terms

  • Antonym: first fiddle - the leading or most important role
  • Synonym: second banana - a person in a subordinate role; an assistant

Etymology

From the orchestra position of the second violin, which supports the lead violin (first fiddle).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛkənd ˈfɪdəl / (AmE), / ˌsɛkənd ˈfɪdəl / (BrE)
a person who has a subordinate role in an organization or activity, often supporting someone in a leading position
person in a secondary or subordinate role

Examples

Although she was highly skilled, she was tired of always playing second fiddle to her older brother.
He had to accept a second fiddle position on the project, even though he wanted to lead.
I refuse to be second fiddle in this company any longer—it's time for a promotion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: right-hand man - trusted person who assists someone in a position of authority
  • Antonym: leader - person who leads or commands a group or organization

Etymology

From the orchestral practice where the second violin section supports the first; figurative use from late 19th century.
