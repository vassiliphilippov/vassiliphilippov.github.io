to pick on someone your own size

1. VERB

/ pɪk ɑn ˈsʌmwʌn jʊr oʊn saɪz / (AmE), / pɪk ɒn ˈsʌmwʌn jɔː əʊn saɪz / (BrE)
to challenge someone to confront or target a person who is equally strong or capable, instead of someone weaker or less able
challenge to confront an equal instead of a weaker

Examples

If you're looking for a fight, why don't you pick on someone your own size?
Tired of seeing his brother being bullied, she told the bully to pick on someone your own size.
Bullies rarely pick on someone your own size because they prefer to target the vulnerable.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bully - someone who intimidates or mistreats those who are weaker
