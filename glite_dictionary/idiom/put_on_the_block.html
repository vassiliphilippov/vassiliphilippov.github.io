to put on the block

1. VERB

/ pʊt ɑn ðə blɑk / (AmE), / pʊt ɒn ðə blɒk / (BrE)
to offer something for sale, especially at an auction
offer something for sale, often at auction

Examples

The family decided to put on the block several valuable antiques.
When the company went bankrupt, all its assets were put on the block.
She plans to put on the block her rare collection next month.

Synset Examples

The old house was put on the block for sale.
He decided to put his car on the block this week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auction - a public sale in which goods or property are sold to the highest bidder
