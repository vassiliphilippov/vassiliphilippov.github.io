between Scylla and Charybdis

1. X

/ bɪˈtwin ˈsɪlə ənd kəˈrɪbdɪs / (AmE), / bɪˈtwiːn ˈsɪlə ənd kəˈrɪbdɪs / (BrE)
in a situation where one must choose between two equally dangerous or difficult options
caught between two dangers

Examples

After the scandal, the politician was between Scylla and Charybdis, unsure which move would do less damage.
Small businesses are often between Scylla and Charybdis when facing regulatory changes or rising costs.
She felt between Scylla and Charybdis having to choose between her career and her family obligations.

Synset Examples

Choosing between these two options feels like being between Scylla and Charybdis.
The manager found himself between Scylla and Charybdis during the merger.
He was caught between Scylla and Charybdis when faced with budget cuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: between a rock and a hard place - in a difficult situation with two undesirable options

Etymology

From Greek mythology; Scylla and Charybdis were sea monsters on opposite sides of a narrow strait, representing hazards to sailors.
