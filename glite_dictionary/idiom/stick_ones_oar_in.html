to stick one's oar in

1. VERB

/ stɪk wʌnz ɔɹ ɪn / (AmE), / stɪk wʌnz ɔː ɪn / (BrE)
to become involved or interfere in someone else's affairs, usually when it is unwanted or unnecessary
interfere in someone else's affairs

Examples

I wish he wouldn't stick his oar in whenever we're discussing work matters.
She tends to stick her oar in even when she's not invited to give her opinion.
It's best not to stick your oar in unless you're asked for advice.

Synset Examples

I wish he wouldn't stick his oar in our business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to butt in - to interrupt or meddle in someone else's conversation or affairs
  • Antonym: to mind one's own business - to avoid interfering in other people's matters
