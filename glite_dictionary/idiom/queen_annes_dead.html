Queen Anne's dead

1. X

/ kwiːn ænz dɛd / (AmE), / kwiːn ænz dɛd / (BrE)
used to express relief or indifference after a person has left or something is no longer relevant
expression of relief or irrelevance

Examples

When everyone finally stopped talking about the issue, Sarah joked, "Queen Anne's dead."
As soon as the kids left for school, he sighed and muttered, "Queen Anne's dead."
The enthusiasm faded quickly, and before long everyone was saying, "Queen Anne's dead."

Synset Examples

When he finally quit his job, we all said, 'Queen Anne's dead!'
After the meeting, I felt like saying, 'Queen Anne's dead.'

Etymology

Refers to Queen Anne of Great Britain (1665–1714); the phrase became a catchphrase after her death, used when news was old or irrelevant.
