old wine in a new bottle

1. NOUN

/ oʊld waɪn ɪn ə nu ˈbɑt̬l̩ / (AmE), / əʊld waɪn ɪn ə njuː ˈbɒtl̩ / (BrE)
a familiar idea, product, or concept that is presented as if it were new or different, though it essentially remains unchanged
familiar idea presented as new

Examples

Her proposal was just old wine in a new bottle and offered no real innovation.
Many critics dismissed the sequel as old wine in a new bottle.
The marketing campaign turned out to be old wine in a new bottle, repackaging existing products.

Synset Examples

His speech was old wine in a new bottle, lacking originality.
The company's rebranding felt like old wine in a new bottle.
Their latest product is just old wine in a new bottle.
