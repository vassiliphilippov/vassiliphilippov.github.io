over the line

1. X

/ ˈoʊvɚ ðə laɪn / (AmE), / ˈəʊvə ðə laɪn / (BrE)
behaving in a way that is unacceptable or beyond what is considered appropriate or reasonable
beyond acceptable limits

Examples

Many people felt the comedian's jokes were over the line and offensive.
I think you've gone over the line with your criticism this time.
During the heated argument, his remarks were clearly over the line.

Synset Examples

Their behavior was over the line during the meeting.
His comments were definitely over the line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cross the line - to go beyond what is socially or morally acceptable
