to play first fiddle

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ˈfɜrst ˈfɪd.əl / (AmE), / pleɪ ˈfɜːst ˈfɪd.əl / (BrE)
to take the leading role or be the most important person in an activity or group
take the lead; be most important

Examples

After the merger, she continued to play first fiddle in the new management team.
He doesn't like to play first fiddle, preferring to support others behind the scenes.
Whenever there's a group assignment, Mark is eager to play first fiddle and take charge.

Synset Examples

He always wants to play first fiddle in group projects.
To win the contest, you need to play first fiddle.
In our project, she plays first fiddle and leads the team.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: play second fiddle - to take a subordinate role or be less important than someone else

Etymology

Refers to the leading violinist (first violin) in an orchestra, who has a prominent role.
