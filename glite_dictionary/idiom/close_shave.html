close shave

1. NOUN

/ kloʊs ʃeɪv / (AmE), / kləʊs ʃeɪv / (BrE)
a situation in which someone narrowly avoids danger or a bad outcome
narrow escape from danger

Examples

It was a close shave when the car stopped just inches from the edge of the cliff.
She had a close shave with a serious illness but luckily recovered quickly.
That was a close shave—I almost missed my flight!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: near miss - an instance of narrowly avoiding something unpleasant or dangerous

2. NOUN

/ kloʊs ʃeɪv / (AmE), / kləʊs ʃeɪv / (BrE)
a situation in which someone narrowly avoids danger, disaster, or failure
narrow escape from danger or disaster

Examples

That was a close shave; I almost missed my flight!
He had a close shave when the car nearly hit him.
It was a close shave, but she passed the exam.

Synset Examples

That was a close shave; I almost missed my flight!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: narrow escape - an event where one just manages to avoid harm or failure

Etymology

Originally referred to barbering, implying a shave so close it nearly cuts the skin; by extension, means a narrowly avoided accident.
