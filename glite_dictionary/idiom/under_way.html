under way

1. ADV

/ ˌʌndər ˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˌʌndə ˈweɪ / (BrE)
in motion or in progress; having started and actively proceeding
in motion; in progress

Examples

Once the construction was under way, the city grew quickly.
The meeting got under way at ten o'clock sharp.
The ship was finally under way after hours of delay.

Synset Examples

the ship got under way

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in progress - happening or being done now
  • Synonym: underway - variant form (also accepted spelling, esp. American) meaning 'in progress'

Etymology

Nautical origin from the Dutch 'onderweg' (on the way).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌndər ˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˌʌndə ˈweɪ / (BrE)
(of an activity or process) having started and currently in progress.
in progress; ongoing

Examples

The construction of the new highway is under way and expected to finish next year.
Preparations for the annual festival are already under way in the town square.
Once the meeting was under way, the atmosphere became more relaxed.

Synset Examples

The new project is finally under way.
Changes to the policy are now under way.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ongoing - continuing to exist or develop
  • Synonym: pending - awaiting decision or settlement
  • Antonym: concluded - brought to an end; finished

Etymology

From nautical use: originally referring to a ship that has started moving through the water.
