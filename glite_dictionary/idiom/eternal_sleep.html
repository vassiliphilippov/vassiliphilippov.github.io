eternal sleep

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈtɜrnəl sliːp / (AmE), / ɪˈtɜː.nəl sliːp / (BrE)
a poetic or euphemistic term for death, likening it to a never-ending sleep
euphemism for death

Examples

The novel ended with the hero finally finding his eternal sleep after a long struggle.
Many poets describe death as an eternal sleep, bringing comfort to grieving families.
She believed her grandfather had entered an eternal sleep and would no longer suffer.

Synset Examples

she was laid to rest beside her husband
they had to put their family pet to sleep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: death - the end of life
  • Synonym: rest in peace - phrase expressing a wish for peace after death
