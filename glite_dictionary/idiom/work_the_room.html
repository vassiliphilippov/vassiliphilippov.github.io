to work the room

1. VERB

/ wɝːk ðə ruːm / (AmE), / wɜːk ðə ruːm / (BrE)
to actively move around at a social or professional event, engaging with many people to make connections or leave a positive impression
engage with many people at an event

Examples

She knew exactly how to work the room at the conference and introduced herself to everyone.
Politicians often have to work the room during fundraising dinners to gain support.
He spent the evening working the room, making sure to chat briefly with each guest.

Synset Examples

At the networking event, she knew how to work the room.
She always excels at events because she can work the room.
He managed to work the room and make valuable connections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mingle - to move among and engage with others at a social event
  • Synonym: to circulate - to move around within a group or crowd, especially to meet people
