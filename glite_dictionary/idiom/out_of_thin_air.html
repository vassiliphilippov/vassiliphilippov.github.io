out of thin air

1. ADV

/ aʊt əv θɪn ˈɛr / (AmE), / aʊt əv θɪn ˈeə / (BrE)
suddenly and unexpectedly, without apparent cause or warning
suddenly, without warning or cause

Examples

The magician made the rabbit appear out of thin air during his performance.
Suddenly, a solution to her problem came to her out of thin air.
He claimed the idea had come out of thin air, but I suspect he was inspired by something he read.

Synset Examples

your cousin arrived out of thin air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: out of nowhere - suddenly and without warning or explanation

2. ADV

/ aʊt əv θɪn ɛr / (AmE), / aʊt əv θɪn eə / (BrE)
if someone or something appears out of thin air, they appear suddenly and unexpectedly, as if by magic or without any visible cause
suddenly and unexpectedly appearing

Examples

The solution to the problem seemed to come out of thin air just in time.
A strange sound emerged out of thin air during the recording session.
Rumors about their argument appeared out of thin air and spread quickly.

Synset Examples

She seemed to appear out of thin air at the party.
He conjured up an idea out of thin air for the presentation.
The magician made a rabbit appear out of thin air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: out of nowhere - to appear suddenly or unexpectedly, with no prior indication
