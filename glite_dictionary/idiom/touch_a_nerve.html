to touch a nerve

1. VERB

/ tʌtʃ ə nɜrv / (AmE), / tʌtʃ ə nɜːv / (BrE)
to accidentally provoke a strong emotional reaction in someone, often by mentioning a sensitive subject
provoke a strong emotional response

Examples

His comments about her work really touched a nerve and she became defensive.
You seem to have touched a nerve with that question during the interview.
The issue of salary gaps often touches a nerve among employees.

Synset Examples

His comment seemed to touch a nerve with her.
The topic of politics always touches a nerve.
She didn't mean to touch a nerve with her joke.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hit a nerve - to cause an emotional reaction by mentioning something sensitive
  • Synonym: strike a nerve - to provoke a strong feeling or reaction
