to put your money where your mouth is

1. VERB

/ pʊt jʊr ˈmʌni wɛr jʊr ˈmaʊθ ɪz / (AmE), / pʊt jɔː ˈmʌni weə jɔː ˈmaʊθ ɪz / (BrE)
to show by your actions and not just your words that you support or believe in something
support claims with action

Examples

It's time to put your money where your mouth is and volunteer for the project.
He always talks about improving the environment, but he never puts his money where his mouth is.
If the company really cares about safety, they should put their money where their mouth is and invest in better equipment.

Synset Examples

If you believe in the project, put your money where your mouth is.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to back up - to support with evidence or action
