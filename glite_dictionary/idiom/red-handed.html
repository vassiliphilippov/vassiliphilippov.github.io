red-handed

1. ADV

/ ˌrɛd ˈhændɪd / (AmE), / ˌrɛd ˈhændɪd / (BrE)
in the act of doing something wrong or illegal; with clear evidence of wrongdoing
in the act of wrongdoing

Examples

The thief was caught red-handed trying to break into the house.
She denied the accusations, even though she was seen red-handed at the scene of the crime.
Thanks to the security cameras, the employee was discovered red-handed stealing office supplies.

Synset Examples

he was caught red-handed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in flagrante delicto - in the very act of committing an offense

Etymology

From the Scottish phrase 'caught red-handed', originally referring to having blood on one's hands after committing a crime.

3. ADV

/ ˌrɛdˈhændɪd / (AmE), / ˌrɛdˈhændɪd / (BrE)
in the act of committing a crime or doing something wrong; with clear evidence of guilt
caught in the act of wrongdoing

Examples

The burglar was caught red-handed by the homeowner when he tried to break in.
Thanks to the surveillance cameras, the employee was discovered red-handed stealing office supplies.
She walked in and found her children red-handed with the cookies they weren't supposed to eat.

Synset Examples

The local police caught the sheep-biter red-handed.
She caught him red-handed cheating on the test.
The thief was red-handed when the alarm went off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in flagrante delicto - in the very act of wrongdoing; caught in the act
  • Antonym: unobserved - not seen or detected; escaping notice, especially during wrongdoing

Etymology

From Scottish legal usage, originally meaning 'with blood on the hands' (red hand), first used in the 15th century.
