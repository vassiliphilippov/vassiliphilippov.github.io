to catch a case

1. VERB

/ kætʃ ə keɪs / (AmE), / katʃ ə keɪs / (BrE)
to become involved in legal trouble or be charged with a crime
become involved in legal trouble
slang

Examples

He’s worried he might catch a case if he keeps hanging out with that group.
After the fight broke out at the club, several people were afraid they would catch a case.
She didn’t want to catch a case for something she didn’t do.

Synset Examples

He’s worried he might catch a case soon.

She might catch a case for driving without a license.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get in trouble - to experience difficulties, especially with the law or authorities
