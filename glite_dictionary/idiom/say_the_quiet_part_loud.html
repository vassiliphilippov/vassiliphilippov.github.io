to say the quiet part loud

1. VERB

/ seɪ ðə ˈkwaɪət pɑrt laʊd / (AmE), / seɪ ðə ˈkwaɪət pɑːt laʊd / (BrE)
to reveal openly something that is usually kept hidden or unspoken, often about motives or intentions
reveal hidden motives or truths openly

Examples

In his speech, the senator said the quiet part loud, shocking the audience with his honesty.
She didn't mean to say the quiet part loud, but her comment made everyone uncomfortable.
Some politicians accidentally say the quiet part loud when they state their real intentions.

Synset Examples

In the debate, she dared to say the quiet part loud.
He had a habit of saying the quiet part loud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to let the cat out of the bag - to reveal a secret, often unintentionally
