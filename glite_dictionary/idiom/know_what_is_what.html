to know what is what

1. VERB

/ noʊ wət ɪz wʌt / (AmE), / nəʊ wɒt ɪz wɒt / (BrE)
to understand the true situation or the important facts, especially because of experience or knowledge
understand true or important facts

Examples

After years in the business, she really knows what is what when it comes to negotiations.
You have to know what is what to avoid making costly mistakes in this industry.
He might seem inexperienced, but he actually knows what is what about the whole process.

Synset Examples

In this field, you need to know what is what.
He’s been around long enough to know what is what.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to have a clue - to have some knowledge or understanding of something
