swan song

1. NOUN

/ swɑn sɔŋ / (AmE), / swɒn sɒŋ / (BrE)
a final performance, action, or effort, especially the last one before retirement or death
final performance or effort before retirement

Examples

The author's final novel was considered his swan song by many critics.
After forty years of teaching, her swan song was a moving graduation speech.
The popular band's last concert was their emotional swan song before retiring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farewell performance - last public performance before retiring or leaving

Etymology

From the ancient belief that swans sing a beautiful song just before they die; this notion is found in Greek and Roman literature.

2. NOUN

/ swɑn sɔŋ / (AmE), / swɒn sɒŋ / (BrE)
A final performance, work, or act before someone retires or dies.
final act before retirement or death

Examples

The actor's emotional performance in the play was considered his swan song.
Many critics regard the composer's last symphony as his swan song.
She wanted her swan song to be a project that would inspire generations to come.

Synset Examples

He delivered his swan song at the farewell party.
The artist's latest album is her swan song.
The film was the director's swan song, marking his retirement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farewell - a departure or goodbye, especially at the end of a career or life

Etymology

From the ancient belief that swans sing a beautiful song before they die, recorded in Latin as 'cygni cantus'.
