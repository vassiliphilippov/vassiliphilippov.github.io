in the firing line

1. ADP

/ ɪn ðə ˈfaɪ.ɚ ˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ɪn ðə ˈfaɪə ˌlaɪn / (BrE)
in a position where one is likely to be criticized, blamed, or exposed to danger or risk
exposed to criticism or danger

Examples

After the mistake, the manager was in the firing line during the meeting.
Journalists are often in the firing line when they cover controversial topics.
When the project failed, the entire team found themselves in the firing line.

Synset Examples

He felt like he was in the firing line after the decision.
Being in the firing line taught her resilience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: under fire - facing criticism or attack
  • Degree Lower: in the spotlight - under close attention, especially scrutiny or criticism

Etymology

Originally a military term referring to troops positioned at the front, exposed to enemy fire.
