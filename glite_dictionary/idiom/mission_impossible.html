mission impossible

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪʃən ɪmˈpɑsəbəl / (AmE), / ˈmɪʃ.ən ɪmˈpɒs.ɪ.bəl / (BrE)
a task or undertaking that is extremely difficult or almost impossible to achieve
extremely difficult or impossible task

Examples

Finishing the report by tomorrow will be a mission impossible without more help from the team.
They knew climbing the icy mountain would be a mission impossible, but they tried anyway.
Negotiating peace between the two rivals seemed like a mission impossible at the time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impossible - not able to be done or achieved
  • Antonym: piece of cake - something very easy to do (antonym in context of difficulty)

Etymology

Popularized by the 1960s American television series 'Mission: Impossible'.
