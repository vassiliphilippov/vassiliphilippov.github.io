bed of roses

1. NOUN

/ bɛd əv ˈroʊzɪz / (AmE), / bɛd əv ˈrəʊzɪz / (BrE)
a situation or life that is easy, comfortable, or free from difficulties
an easy or pleasant situation

Examples

She realized that running her own company was not a bed of roses.
He warned his son that life in the city wouldn’t be a bed of roses.
Their relationship had never been a bed of roses, but they worked through their problems.

Synset Examples

Life isn’t always a bed of roses, you know.
She thought marriage would be a bed of roses.
His job is not a bed of roses at all.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowl of cherries - a situation that is very pleasant or easy
  • Antonym: bed of thorns - a situation that is difficult or full of troubles

Etymology

Metaphorical use referring to a rose bed as a symbol of luxury or ease, first recorded in 16th-century English literature.
