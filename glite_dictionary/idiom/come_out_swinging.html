to come out swinging

1. VERB

/ kʌm aʊt ˈswɪŋɪŋ / (AmE), / kʌm aʊt ˈswɪŋɪŋ / (BrE)
to begin an activity, event, or confrontation with energy and aggression, showing strong determination from the start
begin aggressively or energetically

Examples

The company came out swinging with a bold marketing campaign to regain its market share.
Knowing they were the underdogs, the team came out swinging from the opening whistle.
During the interview, she came out swinging with confident and challenging questions.

Synset Examples

After the break, she came out swinging with new ideas.
The team came out swinging in the second half.
He came out swinging in the first round of the debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: come out fighting - to start something with strong aggression or determination
