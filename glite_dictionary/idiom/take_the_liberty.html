to take the liberty

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə ˈlɪbɚti / (AmE), / teɪk ðə ˈlɪbəti / (BrE)
to do something without asking for permission or authorization, often implying a presumptuous act
act without permission or authorization

Examples

I took the liberty of making coffee for everyone before the meeting started.
He takes the liberty to adjust our schedules without consulting us first.
She was taking the liberty of using his computer while he was away.

Synset Examples

He took the liberty of inviting his friends to our party.
I hope you don't mind, but I took the liberty of ordering dinner.
She took the liberty to suggest changes to the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to presume - to do something without permission, often assuming it's allowed
