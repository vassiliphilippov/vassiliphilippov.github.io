sticks and stones may break my bones, but words will never hurt me

1. X

/ stɪks ənd stoʊnz meɪ breɪk maɪ boʊnz bət wɜrdz wɪl ˈnɛvɚ hɜrt mi / (AmE), / stɪks ənd stəʊnz meɪ breɪk maɪ bəʊnz bət wɜːdz wɪl ˈnɛvə hɜːt mi / (BrE)
used to say that physical harm caused by others is worse than any pain caused by insults or harsh words
proverb: physical harm is worse than verbal insults

Examples

Whenever someone teased him, he would recall the saying, sticks and stones may break my bones, but words will never hurt me.
She repeated sticks and stones may break my bones, but words will never hurt me to stay strong against taunts at school.
Believing in the phrase sticks and stones may break my bones, but words will never hurt me helped him ignore gossip.

Synset Examples

He lived by the mantra, sticks and stones may break my bones, but words will never hurt me.
When criticized, she thought, sticks and stones may break my bones, but words will never hurt me.
He reminded himself, sticks and stones may break my bones, but words will never hurt me.

Etymology

Popular English proverb in use since at least the 19th century.
