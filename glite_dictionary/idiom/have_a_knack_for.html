to have a knack for

1. VERB

/ hæv ə næk fər / (AmE), / hæv ə næk fə / (BrE)
to have a special talent or natural ability for doing something
to be naturally talented at something

Examples

She has a knack for remembering everyone's name after meeting them once.
He has a knack for fixing anything that's broken around the house.
My friend has a knack for making people laugh in any situation.

Synset Examples

Betty has a knack for making everyone feel welcome.
Josie has a knack for organizing fun events.
Tessie has a knack for telling funny stories.
He has a knack for creating style-ile outfits.
That girl has a knack for solving problems.
He has a knack for hitting my funny bone.
Mossie has a knack for storytelling that captivates everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be good at - to perform well in a certain activity

Etymology

From 'knack' (Middle English 'knak'), meaning a clever or special way of doing something.
