hell to pay

1. NOUN

/ hɛl tə peɪ / (AmE), / hɛl tə peɪ / (BrE)
severe consequences or trouble resulting from someone's actions
severe consequences or trouble

Examples

If you don't finish the project on time, there will be hell to pay with your boss.
He broke the rules and now there's hell to pay at home.
If anyone finds out about this, there'll be hell to pay.

Synset Examples

when the pig ran away there was hell to pay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consequences - results or outcomes of an action, often negative
  • Synonym: repercussions - unintended consequences, often negative, following an event

2. NOUN

/ hɛl tə peɪ / (AmE), / hɛl tə peɪ / (BrE)
used to say that there will be severe trouble or serious consequences as a result of something that has happened or may happen
serious trouble or consequences

Examples

If you lose those files, there will be hell to pay when the manager finds out.
She knew there would be hell to pay if she broke her parents' rules again.
Messing up this report will mean hell to pay from the whole team.

Synset Examples

When the boss finds out, there will be hell to pay.
If you miss the deadline, there'll be hell to pay.
He forgot their anniversary, and now there's hell to pay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punishment - the act of imposing a penalty for an offense
