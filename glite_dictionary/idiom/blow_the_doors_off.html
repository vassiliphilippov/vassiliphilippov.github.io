to blow the doors off

1. VERB

/ bloʊ ðə dɔɹz ɔf / (AmE), / bləʊ ðə dɔːz ɒf / (BrE)
to achieve outstanding success or to greatly surpass expectations, often by a significant margin
achieve outstanding success
informal

Examples

The new smartphone blew the doors off its competitors with innovative features and speed.
His debut novel blew the doors off all expectations, becoming an instant bestseller.
The team’s performance in the finals blew the doors off and surprised even their coach.

Synset Examples

The new sales strategy blew the doors off last quarter's results.
Her performance blew the doors off the competition.
The movie blew the doors off with its box office success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blow away - to impress greatly or outperform completely
  • Antonym: to fall flat - to fail to have an intended or expected effect
