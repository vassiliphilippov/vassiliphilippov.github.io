to work like a horse

1. VERB

/ wɝːk laɪk ə hɔrs / (AmE), / wɜːk laɪk ə hɔːs / (BrE)
to work with great effort and energy; to work very hard
work very hard

Examples

She had to work like a horse to support her large family.
During the harvest season, farmers often work like a horse from dawn till dusk.
If you work like a horse, you'll achieve your goals faster.

Synset Examples

She always works like a horse to achieve her goals.
He had to work like a horse to finish on time.
They work like horses during busy season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: work hard - to put in great effort when working
  • Synonym: slave away - to work extremely hard, often for long periods
