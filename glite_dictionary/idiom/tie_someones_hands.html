to tie someone's hands

1. VERB

/ taɪ sʌmˌwʌnz hændz / (AmE), / taɪ sʌmˌwʌnz hændz / (BrE)
to restrict someone's ability to act freely or make decisions
restrict someone's freedom to act

Examples

Management has tied my hands and won't let me approve any more refunds.
The new law will tie the governor's hands on tax reform.
He wants to help, but his contract ties his hands in this situation.

Synset Examples

They tie my hands by limiting my options.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restrict - to limit the actions or movements of someone
  • Antonym: free rein - unrestricted freedom to act or decide
