to eat one's words

1. VERB

/ it wʌnz wɝdz / (AmE), / iːt wʌnz wɜːdz / (BrE)
to admit that what one said before was wrong; to retract a previous statement after being proved incorrect
admit one was wrong, retract statement

Examples

After being proven wrong, he reluctantly had to eat his words in front of everyone.
She was quick to judge but eventually had to eat her words when the plan succeeded.
He promised it would never rain, only to eat his words the very next day.

Synset Examples

After seeing the results, she was forced to eat her words.
He had to eat his words after losing the bet.
He never thought he'd have to eat his words about the team's chances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take back - to admit that what was said was wrong or inappropriate
  • Synonym: to recant - to withdraw or retract a statement or belief formally
