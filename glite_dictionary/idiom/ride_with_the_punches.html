to ride with the punches

1. VERB

/ raɪd wɪð ðə ˈpʌntʃɪz / (AmE), / raɪd wɪð ðə ˈpʌntʃɪz / (BrE)
to adapt to difficult or challenging situations by being flexible and resilient
adapt to difficulties; stay resilient

Examples

After losing his job, he tried to ride with the punches by staying positive and looking for new opportunities.
The team has learned to ride with the punches through a tough season of injuries and defeats.
To succeed in a rapidly changing industry, you have to ride with the punches and keep adapting.

Synset Examples

He learned to ride with the punches after several setbacks.
She showed resilience by riding with the punches during the crisis.
In business, you have to learn to ride with the punches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roll with the punches - adapt to challenges by being flexible (original idiom)

Etymology

Modeled after the boxing idiom "roll with the punches," where a boxer moves back or to the side to lessen the impact of blows.
