let alone

1. ADV

/ lɛt əˈloʊn / (AmE), / lɛt əˈləʊn / (BrE)
used to emphasize that what has just been mentioned is even more unlikely or impossible than another, more obvious thing; much less.
used to emphasize an unlikely or extreme comparison

Examples

He didn’t have enough money for a coffee, let alone a full meal.
She has never been to Paris, let alone London or Rome.
Most six-year-olds can’t solve algebra problems, let alone calculus equations.

Synset Examples

she can't boil potatoes, let alone cook a meal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: much less - used to emphasize a greater degree of improbability
  • Synonym: still less - even less likely or possible

2. SCONJ

/ ˈlɛt əˈloʊn / (AmE), / ˈlɛt əˈləʊn / (BrE)
Used to emphasize that something mentioned is even less likely or possible than something already stated.
used to emphasize a greater unlikelihood

Examples

He didn’t have time to read a book, let alone write one.
Most children can’t tie their shoes, let alone solve complex math problems.
The tiny boat can barely hold one person, let alone a group of adults.

Synset Examples

He can’t run a mile, let alone a marathon.
She doesn't have time for a vacation, let alone a weekend trip.
I can barely afford lunch, let alone dinner.
