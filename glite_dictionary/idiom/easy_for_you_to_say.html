easy for you to say

1. X

/ ˈizi fər ju tə seɪ / (AmE), / ˈiːzi fə juː tə seɪ / (BrE)
used to indicate that advice or comments are simple to give when one is not facing the difficulty oneself
phrase showing advice is easier to give than follow

Examples

"You think quitting is simple, but that's easy for you to say when you don't have my responsibilities."
"Of course, it's easy for you to say that things will get better — you're not in my position."
"Whenever someone tells me to just relax, I always think, that's easy for you to say."

Synset Examples

It’s easy for you to say that, but I’m struggling.
You’re not the one doing the work; it’s easy for you to say.
You don’t understand my situation; it’s easy for you to say.
