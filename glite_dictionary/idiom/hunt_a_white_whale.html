to hunt a white whale

1. VERB

/ hʌnt ə waɪt weɪl / (AmE), / hʌnt ə waɪt weɪl / (BrE)
to pursue a challenging or elusive goal, often with obsession or persistence
pursue an elusive or difficult goal

Examples

After months of hunting a white whale, he finally completed his research project.
Writing the perfect novel sometimes felt like hunting a white whale.
She realized she was hunting a white whale in trying to change the company's culture.

Synset Examples

Finding that lost item was like hunting a white whale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chase a will-o'-the-wisp - pursue something impossible to reach
  • Synonym: wild-goose chase - a futile or hopeless quest

Etymology

From Herman Melville's novel 'Moby-Dick', in which Captain Ahab obsessively hunts a white whale.
