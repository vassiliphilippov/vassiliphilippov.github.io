to play the Merry Andrew

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ðə ˌmɛri ˈændruː / (AmE), / pleɪ ðə ˌmɛri ˈændruː / (BrE)
to behave in a clownish or comical manner, especially for the amusement of others
act in a clownish or silly way
archaic

Examples

During the party, Tom decided to play the Merry Andrew and had everyone laughing at his jokes.
She often plays the Merry Andrew when the atmosphere gets too serious at work.
Yesterday, Mark played the Merry Andrew just to cheer up the children at the hospital.

Synset Examples

He played the Merry Andrew, entertaining everyone with his antics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clown around - to act in a silly or playful way
  • Synonym: fool - to behave in a foolish way

Etymology

From 'Merry Andrew', a historical term for a clown or buffoon who amused audiences at fairs, first attested in the 17th century.
