to play all one's cards

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ɔl wʌnz kɑrdz / (AmE), / pleɪ ɔːl wʌnz kɑːdz / (BrE)
to reveal all of one's intentions, strategies, or resources, usually in a competitive situation
reveal all plans or resources

Examples

The negotiator refused to play all his cards during the initial round of talks.
She decided to play all her cards before the final vote to gain support.
If you play all your cards too soon, your opponents may take advantage.

Synset Examples

She played all her cards, revealing her strategy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: show one's hand - to reveal one's intentions or plans
  • Antonym: hold one's cards close to one's chest - to keep one's plans or intentions secret

Etymology

From the imagery of card games, where showing all your cards exposes your strategy.
