to put one's shoulder into

1. VERB

/ pʊt wʌnz ˈʃoʊldɚ ˈɪntu / (AmE), / pʊt wʌnz ˈʃəʊldə ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
to work hard or put a lot of effort into a task, especially in a determined or energetic way
make a great effort

Examples

If we all put our shoulders into it, we'll finish before sunset.
She knew she needed to put her shoulder into preparing for the big exam.
The team put their shoulders into clearing the debris after the storm.

Synset Examples

She had to put her shoulder into the project to meet the deadline.
They put their shoulders into the task and finished it quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: make an effort - to try hard to do something
  • Antonym: to slack off - to work less hard or with less effort

Etymology

Alludes to using one's shoulder to apply force, as in pushing something; in use since the 19th century.
