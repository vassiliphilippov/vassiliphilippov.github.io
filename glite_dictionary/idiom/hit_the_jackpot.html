to hit the jackpot

1. VERB

/ hɪt ðə ˈdʒækˌpɑt / (AmE), / hɪt ðə ˈdʒækpɒt / (BrE)
to have great success or good luck, especially unexpectedly or suddenly
have great unexpected success or luck

Examples

After years of searching for the right job, she finally hit the jackpot with her new position.
When the company launched its new product, they hit the jackpot in the market.
He invested in a startup and really hit the jackpot when it became successful.

Synset Examples

They hit the jackpot with their latest invention.
Her investment in stocks hit the jackpot this year.
Winning that contract felt like hitting the jackpot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strike it rich - to suddenly become very wealthy or successful
  • Antonym: miss the boat - to miss an opportunity

Etymology

From the gambling term 'jackpot', which originally referred to a large accumulated prize in poker.
