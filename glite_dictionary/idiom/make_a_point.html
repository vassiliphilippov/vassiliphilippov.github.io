to make a point

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə pɔɪnt / (AmE), / meɪk ə pɔɪnt / (BrE)
to make a deliberate effort to do something, often because it is considered important
deliberately ensure something is done

Examples

She always makes a point to greet every new employee personally.
I made a point of thanking everyone who helped organize the event.
He makes a point of exercising every morning, no matter how busy he is.

Synset Examples

She always makes a point to arrive early for meetings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take pains - make a deliberate effort
