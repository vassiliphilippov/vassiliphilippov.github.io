to let out a darn

1. VERB

/ lɛt aʊt ə dɑrn / (AmE), / lɛt aʊt ə dɑːn / (BrE)
to utter the mild expletive 'darn' as an expression of frustration or annoyance
utter 'darn' to show annoyance

Examples

She accidentally spilled her coffee and let out a darn in frustration.
Whenever he loses at cards, he tends to let out a darn or two.
The child let out a darn after dropping his ice cream on the sidewalk.

Synset Examples

He let out a darn when he stubbed his toe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exclaim - to cry out suddenly, especially in surprise, anger, or pain
