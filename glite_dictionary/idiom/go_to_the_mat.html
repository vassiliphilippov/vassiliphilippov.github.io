to go to the mat

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ tə ðə mæt / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə ðə mæt / (BrE)
to fight vigorously or defend a position with determination, especially in an argument or dispute
defend with great determination

Examples

She was ready to go to the mat for her team's proposal during the meeting.
The senator promised he would go to the mat to protect local jobs.
If you challenge her integrity, she'll go to the mat every time.

Synset Examples

He decided to go to the mat for his beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stand one's ground - refuse to yield or retreat in an argument or confrontation

Etymology

From wrestling, where combatants compete on a mat; figuratively used since the mid-20th century.
