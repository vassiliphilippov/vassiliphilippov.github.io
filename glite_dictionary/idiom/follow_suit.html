to follow suit

1. VERB

/ ˈfɑloʊ ˈsut / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊ ˈsuːt / (BrE)
to do the same thing as someone else, especially after they have taken the lead
do the same as someone else

Examples

When one department adopted the new software, others quickly followed suit.
If you leave early, others might follow suit and do the same.
The company cut prices, and its competitors followed suit within weeks.

Synset Examples

If she donates, I will follow suit with a contribution.
When the first store offered discounts, others quickly followed suit.
After his success, many competitors began to follow suit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to imitate - to copy the actions or appearance of someone
  • Synonym: copycat - a person who copies another's behavior or actions
  • Antonym: to break the mold - to do something differently from what most people do

Etymology

Originally from the card game bridge, where to 'follow suit' means to play a card of the same suit as the one led.
