browned off

1. ADJ

/ braʊnd ɑf / (AmE), / braʊnd ɒf / (BrE)
greatly annoyed or fed up; out of patience, especially after continued irritation or disappointment
fed up; greatly annoyed
informal british

Examples

He was browned off with the endless paperwork and constant delays at the office.
After waiting for over an hour, she was completely browned off with the terrible service.
Many soldiers became browned off by the boredom and discomfort of camp life.

Synset Examples

had an exasperated look on his face
felt exasperated beyond endurance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fed up - very annoyed or tired of something, especially after a long time
  • Synonym: exasperated - intensely irritated and frustrated
  • Antonym: pleased - feeling happy or satisfied

Etymology

Originally British military slang from the early 20th century, possibly relating to the color of army uniforms or the verb 'brown' meaning to scold.

annoyed or fed up (informal, British)
informal british

Related Terms

Originally British military slang from the early 20th century, related to the idea of being discolored or worn out.
