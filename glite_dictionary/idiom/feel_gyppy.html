to feel gyppy

1. VERB

/ fiːl ˈdʒɪpi / (AmE), / fiːl ˈdʒɪpi / (BrE)
to feel nauseous or slightly ill; to experience discomfort or uneasiness, especially in the stomach
feel nauseous or unwell
informal british

Examples

I've been feeling gyppy all day after that seafood lunch.
She warned him not to go swimming if he started to feel gyppy.
Whenever I feel gyppy, I drink ginger tea to settle my stomach.

Synset Examples

Feeling gyppy, she decided to skip dessert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feel sick - to feel nauseous; to feel as if about to vomit
  • Antonym: feel well - to feel healthy; to not be ill

Etymology

Possibly from the informal 'gyppy tummy', originally British, linked to 'Egyptian tummy' (traveler's stomach upset).
