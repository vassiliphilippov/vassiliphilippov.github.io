in the way

1. ADV

/ ɪn ðə weɪ / (AmE), / ɪn ðə weɪ / (BrE)
preventing progress or causing an obstruction; acting as a hindrance or impediment.
acting as a hindrance or obstruction

Examples

I tried to clean the house, but the boxes were in the way.
A lack of funding got in the way of their research project.
Please move your car—it's in the way of the garage entrance.

Synset Examples

she might have succeeded in her ambition, had not circumstances been in her way

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to obstruct - to block something so that movement or progress is prevented
  • Synonym: to impede - to make it difficult for someone or something to move forward or make progress

2. ADV

/ ɪn ðə weɪ / (AmE), / ɪn ðə weɪ / (BrE)
Obstructing or hindering progress, movement, or access; preventing something from proceeding smoothly.
preventing movement or progress

Examples

The large truck parked outside was in the way, blocking the entrance to the shop.
She tried to clean the kitchen, but boxes were always in the way.
If your worries get in the way of your work, it's hard to be productive.

Synset Examples

His attitude is often in the way of progress.
Please move that box; it's in the way.
The furniture is in the way of the door.
His doubts were in the way of progress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: block - to obstruct the movement or progress of something
  • Synonym: to impede - to delay or prevent by obstructing
  • Antonym: out of the way - no longer obstructing or hindering
