at spearpoint

1. ADV

/ æt ˈspɪrˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ət ˈspɪəpɔɪnt / (BrE)
under extreme pressure or threat, especially when forced to act unwillingly
under extreme threat or pressure

Examples

The company agreed to the terms at spearpoint, with little room for negotiation.
She felt as though she was making the decision at spearpoint, pressured by her superiors.
The treaty was signed at spearpoint after days of escalating threats.

Synset Examples

The deal was signed at spearpoint, pressured by urgency.
The negotiations proceeded at spearpoint, with threats looming.
They faced the enemy at spearpoint, ready to fight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at gunpoint - under threat of being shot with a gun

Etymology

Refers figuratively to being forced to act while threatened by a spear point; modeled after 'at gunpoint.'
