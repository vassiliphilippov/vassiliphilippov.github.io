smooth sailing

1. NOUN

/ smuð ˈseɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / smuːð ˈseɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
A situation in which progress is easy and without difficulty or problems.
easy, trouble-free progress

Examples

After the contract was signed, it was smooth sailing for the rest of the project.
Getting to the airport was smooth sailing with no traffic on the roads.
It wasn't always smooth sailing, but the team eventually completed the renovation.

Synset Examples

Once we got past the traffic, it was smooth sailing.
With all preparations done, it was smooth sailing at the event.
After the initial hiccup, it was smooth sailing.
The mechanic recommended the best sailboat fuel for smooth sailing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plain sailing - an alternative expression meaning an easy or trouble-free situation
  • Antonym: rough sailing - a situation that is difficult or has many problems

Etymology

Originally nautical, referring to favorable conditions for sailing a vessel.
