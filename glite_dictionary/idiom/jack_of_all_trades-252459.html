jack of all trades

1. NOUN

/ dʒæk əv ɔl treɪdz / (AmE), / dʒæk əv ɔːl treɪdz / (BrE)
a person who is skilled at many different kinds of tasks or jobs but is not necessarily an expert in any of them
person skilled at various tasks

Examples

He was a true jack of all trades, able to fix cars, paint houses, and even build furniture.
She considered herself a jack of all trades but wanted to specialize in one field.
As a small business owner, he had to be a jack of all trades to keep everything running smoothly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generalist - person with a range of skills or knowledge
  • Antonym: specialist - a person who is an expert in a particular area

Etymology

Originates from the 17th-century English phrase, using 'Jack' as a generic name for a common man.

2. NOUN

/ dʒæk əv ɔl treɪdz / (AmE), / dʒæk əv ɔːl treɪdz / (BrE)
a person who is competent at many different tasks, but not outstanding in any particular one
person skilled at many tasks

Examples

She’s a true jack of all trades, helping with everything from accounting to graphic design.
In a startup environment, being a jack of all trades is often a valuable asset.
Although he’s known as a jack of all trades, he sometimes lacks the depth of a specialist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generalist - a person whose knowledge and skills cover a broad range of subjects or activities
  • Antonym: specialist - a person who concentrates primarily on a particular subject or activity

Etymology

From the use of 'Jack' meaning 'man' or 'common fellow' in 16th-century English; phrase recorded from the early 17th century.
