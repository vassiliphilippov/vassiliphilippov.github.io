to dine below the mahogany

1. VERB

/ daɪn bɪˈloʊ ðə məˈhɑɡəni / (AmE), / daɪn bɪˈləʊ ðə məˈhɒɡəni / (BrE)
to dine in a formal, prestigious, or aristocratic setting, especially at a table made of mahogany
dine in a formal or prestigious setting

Examples

She was thrilled to dine below the mahogany at the ambassador's banquet.
Only the select few were ever invited to dine below the mahogany in that grand hall.
He boasted of having dined below the mahogany with nobility during his travels.

Synset Examples

She was honored to finally dine below the mahogany with her peers.

Etymology

Refers to dining at a table made of mahogany, associated with gentility and affluence in the 18th–19th centuries.
