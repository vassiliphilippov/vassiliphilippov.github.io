God's honest truth

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑdz ˈɑnɪst truθ / (AmE), / ɡɒdz ˈɒnɪst truːθ / (BrE)
used to emphasize that a statement is completely true and sincere; the absolute truth
emphatic phrase meaning absolute truth

Examples

I'm telling you, this is the God's honest truth—I have never met him before.
She swore on the God's honest truth that she had nothing to do with the theft.
He looked her in the eye and promised he was sharing the God's honest truth.

Synset Examples

In the meeting, he revealed God's honest truth about the project.
She spoke God's honest truth when she addressed the issue.
The politician finally shared God's honest truth about the budget.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: truth - that which is true or in accordance with fact or reality
  • Antonym: lie - a false statement made with deliberate intent to deceive
