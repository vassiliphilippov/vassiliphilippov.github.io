to hold up one's end of the bargain

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ʌp wʌnz ɛnd əv ðə ˈbɑrɡən / (AmE), / həʊld ʌp wʌnz ɛnd əv ðə ˈbɑːɡɪn / (BrE)
to do what one has promised as part of an agreement or deal
fulfill one's part of an agreement

Examples

If you don't hold up your end of the bargain, the project will fall apart.
He promised to hold up his end of the bargain and finish the work by Friday.
They always hold up their end of the bargain when it comes to teamwork.

Synset Examples

She always holds up her end of the bargain.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: break one's word - fail to do what one has promised
  • Synonym: keep one's word - do what one has promised
