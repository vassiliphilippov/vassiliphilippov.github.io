to cut his stick

1. VERB

/ kʌt hɪz stɪk / (AmE), / kʌt hɪz stɪk / (BrE)
to depart or withdraw from a place or activity, especially abruptly or unexpectedly
to leave or withdraw
british archaic

Examples

After the meeting got tense, he decided to cut his stick and head home.
As soon as they realized trouble was brewing, Sam chose to cut his stick.
She thought it best to cut her stick before the argument escalated.

Synset Examples

After the argument, he decided to cut his stick and leave.
When things got too heated, he knew it was time to cut his stick.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave - go away from a place
  • Synonym: to depart - go away; leave

Etymology

Originally British slang, with 'stick' possibly referring to a walking stick, meaning to prepare to leave.
