to put one's affairs in order

1. VERB

/ pʊt wʌnz əˈfɛɹz ɪn ˈɔɹdɚ / (AmE), / pʊt wʌnz əˈfɛəz ɪn ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
to organize one’s personal, legal, or financial matters, especially in preparation for a possible illness, death, or major change
organize personal matters, often before major change

Examples

After receiving his diagnosis, he started putting his affairs in order to make things easier for his family.
She spent the afternoon putting her affairs in order before moving abroad.
Many people wait too long before putting their affairs in order.

Synset Examples

He spent the weekend putting his affairs in order.
