to drive a coach and horses through

1. VERB

/ draɪv ə koʊtʃ ənd ˈhɔrsɪz θru / (AmE), / draɪv ə kəʊtʃ ənd ˈhɔːsɪz θruː / (BrE)
to expose or take advantage of major flaws or gaps in a plan, law, argument, or reasoning
find major flaws or gaps (idiom)
british

Examples

His logic was so flawed that you could drive a coach and horses through it.
The opposition claimed they could drive a coach and horses through the new policy proposals.
Legal experts warned that cunning lawyers would easily drive a coach and horses through the draft legislation.

Synset Examples

His logic was so flawed, you could drive a coach and horses through it.
I could drive a coach and horses through the gaps in her story.
You could drive a coach and horses through that argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to poke holes in - to find faults or gaps in an argument
  • Synonym: find fault with - to criticize or point out flaws

Etymology

From the literal image of a coach and horses passing through a gap, used figuratively from the 19th century.
