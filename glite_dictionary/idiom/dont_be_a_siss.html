don't be a siss

1. X

/ doʊnt biː ə sɪs / (AmE), / dəʊnt biː ə sɪs / (BrE)
used informally to encourage someone to act bravely rather than fearfully or timidly
encouragement to be brave, not fearful
informal slang

Examples

Come on, don't be a siss, it's only a rollercoaster!
He told his friend, "don't be a siss, just ask her to dance!"
When the team hesitated before the match, the coach shouted, "don't be a siss!"

Synset Examples

Don't be a siss, jump into the pool!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: don't be a coward - encouragement to act bravely, not with fear
  • Synonym: don't be a chicken - informal phrase telling someone not to be afraid

Etymology

Variant form of 'sissy', likely from 'sister'.
