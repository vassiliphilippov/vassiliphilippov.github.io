to walk like an Egyptian

1. VERB

/ wɔk laɪk ən ɪˈdʒɪp.ʃən / (AmE), / wɔːk laɪk ən ɪˈdʒɪp.ʃən / (BrE)
to move in a stylized, exaggerated manner, typically with bent arms and rigid posture, imitating the manner depicted in ancient Egyptian art or popularized by the song and its associated dance
imitate stylized Egyptian walk

Examples

At the party, everyone started to walk like an Egyptian to the catchy music.
He made the students walk like an Egyptian for the school talent show.
Whenever the song came on, she couldn't resist trying to walk like an Egyptian.

Synset Examples

At the party, everyone started to walk like an Egyptian.
The kids laughed as they tried to walk like an Egyptian.
During the dance class, we learned to walk like an Egyptian.

Etymology

Popularized by the 1986 hit song 'Walk Like an Egyptian' by The Bangles, referencing depictions of figures in ancient Egyptian art.
