someone's goose is cooked

1. X

/ ˈsʌmwʌnz ɡus ɪz kʊkt / (AmE), / ˈsʌmwʌnz ɡuːs ɪz kʊkt / (BrE)
said to mean that someone is in serious trouble or is about to be punished or ruined, especially after making a mistake or being caught
to be in serious trouble

Examples

If the boss finds out that I lost the client, my goose is cooked.
After missing three exams in a row, her goose is cooked for this semester.
When the evidence was revealed in court, his goose was cooked.

Synset Examples

If she misses the deadline, someone's goose is cooked.
When the truth came out, someone's goose was cooked.
He realized that if he was late again, someone's goose was cooked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in hot water - in trouble or difficulty
  • Antonym: get off scot-free - to escape without punishment

Etymology

Possibly from an old folk saying; exact origin unknown, but recorded in English since at least the 19th century.
