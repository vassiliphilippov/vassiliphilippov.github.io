to come full circle

1. VERB

/ kʌm fʊl ˈsɝːkəl / (AmE), / kʌm fʊl ˈsɜːkl / (BrE)
to return to an original position, situation, or state after going through a series of changes or developments
return to original state

Examples

After years of traveling, she felt her life had come full circle when she moved back home.
Our business came full circle by returning to the original values we started with.
The debate on climate change has come full circle, echoing the arguments made decades ago.

Synset Examples

The story came full circle in the final chapter.
He realized his career had come full circle.
Our friendship has come full circle after all these years.
Their friendship has come full circle after years apart.
