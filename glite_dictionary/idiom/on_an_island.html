on an island

1. X

/ ɔn ən ˈaɪ.lənd / (AmE), / ɒn ən ˈaɪ.lənd / (BrE)
feeling isolated, alone, or unsupported, especially within a group or situation
feeling isolated or unsupported

Examples

During her first weeks at college, Maria often felt on an island in her large classes.
He realized he was on an island when no one backed his proposal at work.
Despite the lively party around her, she felt completely on an island.

Synset Examples

She felt like she was on an island during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isolated - feeling separated from others
  • Synonym: alone - without other people; solitary
  • Antonym: supported - feeling helped or assisted by others
