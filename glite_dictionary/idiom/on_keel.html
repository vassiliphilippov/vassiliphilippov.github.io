on keel

1. ADV

/ ɑn kil / (AmE), / ɒn kiːl / (BrE)
in a stable or balanced state, especially after being unsettled or disorganized
in a stable or balanced state

Examples

After several hectic months, operations are finally on keel.
The manager worked hard to keep the team on keel despite the changes.
Now that the system is on keel, we can start planning for expansion.

Synset Examples

After a rough start, the project is finally on keel.
We need to keep everything on keel to meet the deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on an even keel - in a steady or stable condition

Etymology

Based on nautical use of 'keel' (the central structural base of a ship) to imply stability.
