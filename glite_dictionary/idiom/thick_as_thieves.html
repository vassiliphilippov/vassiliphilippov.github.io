thick as thieves

1. ADJ

/ θɪk əz θivz / (AmE), / θɪk əz θiːvz / (BrE)
(of people) very close or friendly with each other, often sharing secrets or collaborating closely.
very close or secretive friends

Examples

Those two have been thick as thieves ever since they met at college.
Despite their differences, the sisters are thick as thieves and tell each other everything.
The old teammates remain thick as thieves long after their playing days ended.

Synset Examples

In that secret club, the members are thick as thieves.
They've been thick as thieves since childhood.
The two partners were thick as thieves in business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bosom buddies - very close friends
  • Synonym: inseparable - unable to be separated due to strong closeness or friendship
  • Antonym: estranged - no longer close or affectionate to someone

Etymology

Probably from the idea that thieves work closely and secretly with one another; in use since the 19th century.
