first of never

1. X

/ ˈfɜrst əv ˈnɛvɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst əv ˈnɛvə / (BrE)
an expression used to indicate that something is very unlikely or impossible to happen, or that an opportunity will not occur again
something very unlikely or impossible

Examples

It looks like our vacation plans are scheduled for the first of never.
If you expect him to apologize, you'll be waiting until the first of never.
That policy change will happen on the first of never if the current management has its way.

Synset Examples

This opportunity is the first of never for us.
That project is the first of never if we don’t get funding.
Her ambitions seemed like the first of never to him.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: never - at no time; not ever
  • Synonym: never in a million years - another idiom meaning something will definitely not happen
