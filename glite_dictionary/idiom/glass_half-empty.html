glass half-empty

1. ADJ

/ ɡlæs hæf ˈɛmpti / (AmE), / ɡlɑːs hɑːf ˈɛmpti / (BrE)
used to describe a pessimistic attitude or tendency to see the negative side of a situation, often contrasted with 'glass half-full'.
pessimistic attitude or viewpoint

Examples

Even when we achieve great results, she still sees the glass half-empty.
His glass half-empty outlook often discourages others around him.
Don't approach challenges with a glass half-empty mindset.

Synset Examples

Don't always see the glass half-empty; look for the positives!
His pessimism makes him see the glass half-empty.
She tends to see the glass half-empty in every situation.
He always sees the glass-half-empty in every situation.
We need more optimists, not glass-half-empty thinkers.
Her glass-half-empty attitude dampens the team's spirit.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: glass half-full - used to describe an optimistic attitude

Etymology

From the metaphorical phrase comparing a glass filled halfway with liquid, where the description reflects one's outlook.
