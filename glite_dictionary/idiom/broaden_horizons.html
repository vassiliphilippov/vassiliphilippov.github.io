to broaden horizons

1. VERB

/ ˈbrɔd.ən həˈraɪ.zənz / (AmE), / ˈbrɔː.dən həˈraɪ.zənz / (BrE)
to increase the range of one's knowledge, experience, or interests, often by trying new things or encountering new ideas
expand one's experience or knowledge

Examples

Traveling to different cultures can really broaden horizons and change one's perspective on life.
Taking up a new hobby is a great way to broaden horizons and develop new skills.
Attending international conferences has helped her broaden horizons in her career.

Synset Examples

Traveling abroad can really broaden someone's horizons.
Reading diverse books can broaden someone's horizons significantly.
Learning a new language helps to broaden someone's horizons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to open one's mind - become more receptive to new ideas
  • Antonym: narrow-minded - having a limited outlook or scope
