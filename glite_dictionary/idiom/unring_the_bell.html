to unring the bell

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈrɪŋ ðə bɛl / (AmE), / ʌnˈrɪŋ ðə bɛl / (BrE)
to try to undo something that has already happened and cannot be reversed
attempt to undo the irreversible

Examples

After submitting the email to his boss, he realized he couldn't unring the bell.
Once the gossip has spread, it's impossible to unring the bell.
The company tried to recall the announcement, but they couldn't unring the bell.

Synset Examples

He wished he could unring the bell of their last argument.
Once you say it, you can't unring the bell.
I can't unring the bell of that embarrassing moment.

Etymology

Refers to the impossibility of reversing the act of ringing a bell; once rung, a bell cannot be 'unrung'.
