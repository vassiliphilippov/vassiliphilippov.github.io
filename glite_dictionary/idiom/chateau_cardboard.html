Chateau Cardboard

1. NOUN

/ ʃæˈtoʊ ˈkɑrdˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ʃaːˈtoː ˈkɑːdbɔːd / (BrE)
A humorous term for a cheap, shabby, or low-quality place to live, often used ironically to refer to modest or rundown housing.
humorous term for cheap housing
informal

Examples

After he lost his job, Mark had to move into a Chateau Cardboard on the edge of town.
They threw a party at their new place, jokingly calling it Chateau Cardboard.
Even though their Chateau Cardboard was tiny and old, they made it feel like home.

Synset Examples

Living in that rundown place felt like Chateau Cardboard.
We jokingly called it Chateau Cardboard due to its low rent.
Their new apartment feels like a Chateau Cardboard.

Etymology

Modelled on French 'château' (castle, manor house). The phrase mimics the style of wine labels and refers to cardboard used in cheap housing or wine boxes.
