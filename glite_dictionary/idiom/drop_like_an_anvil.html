to drop like an anvil

1. VERB

/ drɑp laɪk ən ˈænvəl / (AmE), / drɒp laɪk ən ˈanvɪl / (BrE)
to abandon or reject something suddenly and completely, often without hesitation
abandon something suddenly and completely

Examples

After the scandal broke, the sponsors dropped like an anvil from the project.
When the plan faced criticism, the committee dropped like an anvil without further discussion.
He dropped like an anvil all his investments once the market took a downturn.

Synset Examples

He dropped the project like an anvil when it lost funding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drop like a hot potato - abandon something very quickly and suddenly
