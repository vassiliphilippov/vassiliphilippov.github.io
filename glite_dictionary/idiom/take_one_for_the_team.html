to take one for the team

1. VERB

/ teɪk wʌn fər ðə tim / (AmE), / teɪk wʌn fə ðə tiːm / (BrE)
to willingly sacrifice personal interest or endure something unpleasant for the benefit of a group
sacrifice for the group
informal

Examples

When no one wanted to speak up, he decided to take one for the team and voiced their concerns.
She volunteered to work the holiday shift, willing to take one for the team so others could spend time with family.
Realizing the deadline was tight, Lana stayed late to take one for the team.

Synset Examples

Sometimes you have to take one for the team to succeed.
She took one for the team and covered for her colleague.
He took one for the team by working late.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sacrifice - to give up something valued for others
