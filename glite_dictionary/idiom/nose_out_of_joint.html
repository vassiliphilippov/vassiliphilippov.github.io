nose out of joint

1. X

/ noʊz aʊt ʌv dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / nəʊz aʊt əv dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
annoyed or upset, especially because of a perceived insult or slight
annoyed or offended (idiom)

Examples

He got his nose out of joint when he wasn't invited to the meeting.
Try not to get your nose out of joint over minor issues at work.
After her suggestion was ignored, she had her nose out of joint for the rest of the day.

Synset Examples

She was upset, with her nose out of joint over the criticism.
Don't let a small mistake put your nose out of joint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offended - feeling resentful or upset by a perceived insult
  • Antonym: unbothered - not annoyed, upset, or offended
