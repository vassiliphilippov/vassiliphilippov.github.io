to see for oneself

1. VERB

/ si fɔr wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / siː fə wʌnˈself / (BrE)
to verify or experience something by personally checking or observing it
verify or experience personally

Examples

She wanted to see for herself whether the new product really worked as advertised.
If you don't believe me, you should see for yourself what a beautiful place it is.
He had to visit the factory to see for himself how the machines operated.

Synset Examples

She wanted to see for herself if the rumors were true.
I decided to visit the site to see for myself.
