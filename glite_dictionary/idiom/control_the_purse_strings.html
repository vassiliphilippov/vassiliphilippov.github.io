to control the purse strings

1. VERB

/ kənˈtroʊl ðə ˈpɜr ˌstrɪŋz / (AmE), / kənˈtrəʊl ðə ˈpɜː ˌstrɪŋz / (BrE)
to have responsibility for managing the money of a household, organization, or group
manage finances or spending

Examples

In most families, it is one partner who tends to control the purse strings.
She refused to let anyone else control the purse strings after paying off their debts.
The committee chairperson effectively controls the purse strings for the entire project.

Synset Examples

When it comes to family vacations, she controls the pursestrings.
She held the pursestrings tightly, saving for a new car.
She holds the purse strings in this organization.
He always holds the purse strings at family gatherings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hold the purse strings - to have control over money and spending

Etymology

From the literal control of a money bag's strings; purse strings once referred to a drawstring purse holding money.
