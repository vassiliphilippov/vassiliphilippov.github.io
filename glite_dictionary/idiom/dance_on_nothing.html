to dance on nothing

1. VERB

/ dæns ɑn ˈnʌθɪŋ / (AmE), / dɑːns ɒn ˈnʌθɪŋ / (BrE)
to perform with apparent ease and skill, often displaying confidence or exhilaration
perform with ease and confidence

Examples

After receiving the award, she felt she could dance on nothing.
His effortless presentation style made it seem like he could just dance on nothing.
The team's victory filled them with such joy they could dance on nothing all night.

Synset Examples
