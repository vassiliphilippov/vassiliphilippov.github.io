to hold over one's head

1. VERB

/ hoʊld oʊvɚ wʌnz hɛd / (AmE), / həʊld əʊvə wʌnz hɛd / (BrE)
to continually remind someone of a past mistake or fault, especially to manipulate or gain advantage over them
remind someone of a mistake to manipulate

Examples

She always threatens to hold over my head that time I was late for the meeting.
It's not fair to hold over his head a mistake he made years ago.
If you forgive someone, you shouldn't hold it over their head forever.

Synset Examples

Don't hold that mistake over my head forever.
She tends to hold her achievements over his head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guilt-trip - to make someone feel guilty to manipulate them
