to let the cat out of the bag

1. VERB

/ lɛt ðə kæt aʊt ʌv ðə bæɡ / (AmE), / lɛt ðə kæt aʊt ɒv ðə bæg / (BrE)
to reveal a secret or disclose information that was meant to be kept confidential
reveal a secret

Examples

If you let the cat out of the bag, everyone will know about the surprise party.
She accidentally let the cat out of the bag during lunch, ruining the secret.
Please don't let the cat out of the bag until after the announcement.

Synset Examples

Be careful — his secretary talks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spill the beans - to reveal secret or confidential information

Etymology

Originating in the 18th century from market fraud where a cat was substituted for a pig in a sack.

2. VERB

/ lɛt ðə kæt aʊt əv ðə bæɡ / (AmE), / lɛt ðə kæt aʊt ɒv ðə bӕɡ / (BrE)
to reveal a secret, often unintentionally or by accident
reveal a secret unintentionally

Examples

She wasn't supposed to mention the plans, but she let the cat out of the bag at dinner.
Try not to let the cat out of the bag before the big announcement.
He accidentally let the cat out of the bag about their engagement.

Synset Examples

She couldn’t keep the secret and let the cat out of the bag.
When he blurted it out, he let the cat out of the bag.
I accidentally let the cat out of the bag about the surprise party.
He always let the cat out of the bag.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spill the beans - to reveal secret information unintentionally or prematurely

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from an old market scam involving substituting a cat for a pig in a sack.
