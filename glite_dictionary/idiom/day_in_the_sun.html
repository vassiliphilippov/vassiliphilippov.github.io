day in the sun

1. NOUN

/ deɪ ɪn ðə sʌn / (AmE), / deɪ ɪn ðə sʌn / (BrE)
a period of success, recognition, or attention, especially after previous obscurity or hard work
period of success or recognition

Examples

After years of hard work, the team finally had their day in the sun when they won the championship.
Many artists work for decades before enjoying a day in the sun.
She never forgot her day in the sun when her novel topped the bestseller list.

Synset Examples

The festival was their day in the sun, showcasing local talent.
He dreamed of a day in the sun as a famous author.
After years of hard work, she finally had her day in the sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moment in the sun - a brief time of success or recognition
  • Synonym: fifteen minutes of fame - a short period during which someone is famous
