to pass muster

1. VERB

/ pæs ˈmʌstɚ / (AmE), / pɑːs ˈmʌstə / (BrE)
to be judged as acceptable or satisfactory according to a set standard
be acceptable; meet a required standard

Examples

Only proposals that pass muster will be forwarded to the next stage.
His essay didn't pass muster with the professor, so he had to rewrite it.
The new safety equipment must pass muster before it can be used on site.

Synset Examples

Her presentation did not pass muster, unfortunately.
Only the best candidates will pass muster in this interview.
The new policies must pass muster with the board.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to measure up - to be good enough; to meet the required standard
  • Antonym: to fall short - to fail to reach a required standard

Etymology

From the military practice of 'muster', meaning an inspection or roll call of troops, in which soldiers' appearance and readiness were judged.
