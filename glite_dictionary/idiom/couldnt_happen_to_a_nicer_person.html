couldn't happen to a nicer person

1. X

/ ˈkʊdənt ˈhæpən tə ə ˈnaɪsər ˈpɝːsən / (AmE), / ˈkʊdənt ˈhæpən tə ə ˈnaɪsə ˈpɜːsən / (BrE)
used to express genuine pleasure that something good has happened to someone who deserves it, often to convey that the recipient is very likable or worthy
expressing deserved good fortune for a likable person

Examples

It was wonderful to hear about her scholarship; couldn't happen to a nicer person.
When he got the promotion, everyone agreed it couldn't happen to a nicer person.
After hearing she recovered fully, her friends said it couldn't happen to a nicer person.

Synset Examples

That award couldn't happen to a nicer candidate.
She got the promotion; it couldn't happen to a nicer person.
He won the lottery; it couldn't happen to a nicer guy.
