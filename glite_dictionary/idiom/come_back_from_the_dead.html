to come back from the dead

1. VERB

/ kʌm bæk frəm ðə dɛd / (AmE), / kʌm bæk frəm ðə dɛd / (BrE)
to regain activity, prominence, or success after a period of complete inactivity, decline, or seeming failure
return to activity or prominence after decline

Examples

After years out of the spotlight, the band managed to come back from the dead with a new album.
No one expected the technology to come back from the dead after being obsolete for so long.
The company seemed finished, but it managed to come back from the dead with an innovative product.

Synset Examples

The old website seemed to come back from the dead after the redesign.
The ancient art form has come back from the dead thanks to modern artists.
His career appeared to come back from the dead after that blockbuster film.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revive - to restore to life, consciousness, or full activity
