to pick oneself up off the floor

1. VERB

/ pɪk wʌnˈsɛlf ʌp ɔf ðə flɔr / (AmE), / pɪk wʌnˈsɛlf ʌp ɒf ðə flɔː / (BrE)
to recover from a setback, failure, or difficult situation and resume one's efforts or normal life
recover after a setback or failure

Examples

Even after losing her job, she managed to pick herself up off the floor and start a new career.
Whenever life knocks him down, he always finds the strength to pick himself up off the floor.
It's not easy to pick yourself up off the floor after a big disappointment, but it's possible.

Synset Examples

She didn’t let failure stop her; she picked herself up off the floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bounce back - to return quickly to a normal condition after a setback
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop trying after a setback or failure
