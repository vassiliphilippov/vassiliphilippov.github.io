to go to bed with the chickens

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ tə bɛd wɪð ðə ˈtʃɪkənz / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə bɛd wɪð ðə ˈtʃɪkɪnz / (BrE)
to go to bed very early, especially at or soon after sunset
to go to bed very early

Examples

Because she wakes up at dawn, she usually goes to bed with the chickens every night.
Whenever I'm exhausted, I just go to bed with the chickens and skip my usual evening TV shows.
If you want to join the early morning run, you'll have to go to bed with the chickens tonight.

Synset Examples

It's so quiet here; I might go to bed with the chickens.
After a long day, I usually go to bed with the chickens.
If I keep waking up early, I might have to go to bed with the chickens.

Etymology

Refers to the habit of chickens roosting at sunset, symbolizing an early bedtime.
