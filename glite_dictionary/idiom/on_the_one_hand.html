on the one hand

1. SCONJ

/ ɑn ðə wʌn hænd / (AmE), / ɒn ðə wʌn hænd / (BrE)
used to introduce one aspect of a situation, especially when it is contrasted with another
introducing one side of a contrast

Examples

On the one hand, the job pays well, but on the other, it's very stressful.
On the one hand, moving abroad is exciting; on the other, I'll miss my family.
She is, on the one hand, very talented, but on the other hand, sometimes unreliable.

Synset Examples

On the one hand, I want to save money.
On the one hand, it’s a good deal.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: on the other hand - used to introduce a contrasting point or aspect
