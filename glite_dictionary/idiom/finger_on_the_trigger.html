finger on the trigger

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪŋɡɚ ɑn ðə ˈtrɪɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡə ɒn ðə ˈtrɪɡə / (BrE)
ready to act immediately, especially in response to an opportunity or threat.
ready to act immediately

Examples

In volatile markets, investors must always have their finger on the trigger to react to sudden changes.
She kept her finger on the trigger during the negotiations, ready to seal the deal at a moment's notice.
The emergency team always has its finger on the trigger to respond to crises as they arise.

Synset Examples

To succeed, you need to have your finger on the trigger in this market.
He always seems to have his finger on the trigger for new trends.
She had her finger on the trigger, ready to make the purchase.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poised - completely ready to do something
  • Antonym: hesitant - slow to act or reluctant

Etymology

From the literal sense of having one's finger on a firearm's trigger, indicating readiness to shoot.
