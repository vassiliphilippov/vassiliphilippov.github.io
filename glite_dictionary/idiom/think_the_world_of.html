to think the world of

1. VERB

/ θɪŋk ðə wɝld əv / (AmE), / θɪŋk ðə wɜːld ɒv / (BrE)
to have a very high opinion of someone or something; to admire or respect greatly
admire or esteem highly

Examples

She thinks the world of her adviser and always values his opinion.
Everyone knows that John thinks the world of his younger sister.
I could tell from the way she spoke that she thinks the world of her team.

Synset Examples

She thinks the world of her adviser

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to admire - to regard with respect or warm approval
  • Antonym: to look down on - to regard with a feeling of superiority

2. VERB

/ θɪŋk ðə wɜrld ʌv / (AmE), / θɪŋk ðə wɜːld ɒv / (BrE)
to have a very high opinion of someone; to greatly admire or respect someone
hold someone in high regard

Examples

She thinks the world of her teacher, who encouraged her to pursue her dreams.
His colleagues think the world of him because of his hard work and honesty.
I think the world of my grandparents; they've always supported me.

Synset Examples

He always says he thinks the world of his mentor.
I really think the world of my best friend.
She thinks the world of her children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to look up to - to respect and admire someone
  • Antonym: think little of - to have a low opinion of someone
