to lower the boom

1. VERB

/ ˈloʊər ðə buːm / (AmE), / ˈləʊə ðə buːm / (BrE)
to punish someone severely or enforce strict consequences for wrongdoing or misconduct
impose strict punishment or consequences

Examples

If the employees keep arriving late, the boss will lower the boom.
After several warnings, the school principal finally lowered the boom on cheating.
The government is expected to lower the boom on companies that break the new law.

Synset Examples

She was ready to lower the boom on any further violations.
The manager decided to lower the boom on tardiness.
He felt it was time to lower the boom on the team's performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to crack down - to take severe measures against undesirable activities

Etymology

From the nautical practice of dropping the boom (a spar on a sailboat), metaphorically indicating swift, forceful action.
