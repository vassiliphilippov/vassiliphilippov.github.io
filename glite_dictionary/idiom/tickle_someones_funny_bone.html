to tickle someone's funny bone

1. VERB

/ ˈtɪk.əl ˈsʌm.wʌnz ˈfʌn.i boʊn / (AmE), / ˈtɪk.əl ˈsʌm.wʌnz ˈfʌn.i bəʊn / (BrE)
to amuse or entertain someone, often causing them to laugh
to amuse or cause laughter

Examples

That joke really tickled my funny bone and made me laugh out loud.
No matter how tough my day is, a good comedy show manages to tickle my funny bone.
The children's laughter told her that her silly faces had tickled their funny bone.

Synset Examples

Her stories always tickle my funny bone.
The comedian's jokes really tickled my funny bone.
That movie tickled my funny bone all night.
That comedian always knows how to tickle my funny bone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to amuse - to make someone laugh or smile
  • Synonym: to entertain - to provide enjoyment or amusement
