to prop up the bar

1. VERB

/ prɑp ʌp ðə bɑr / (AmE), / prɒp ʌp ðə bɑː / (BrE)
to spend time socializing or drinking at a bar, often for an extended period
spend time drinking at a bar
informal british

Examples

After a long day, they decided to prop up the bar until closing time.
He loves to prop up the bar with his mates every Friday night.
She was always ready to prop up the bar and share a laugh after work.
