straight and narrow

1. NOUN

/ streɪt ənd ˈnæroʊ / (AmE), / streɪt ənd ˈnærəʊ / (BrE)
the honest and morally correct way of behaving
path of proper, honest behavior

Examples

After his release from prison, he tried hard to stay on the straight and narrow.
It's not always easy to keep to the straight and narrow when faced with temptation.
She credits her mentor for helping her remain on the straight and narrow throughout her career.

Synset Examples

he taught his children to keep strictly to the straight and narrow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honest - morally upright; free of deceit
  • Antonym: corrupt - showing dishonesty or moral decay

Etymology

From the biblical phrase "strait is the gate, and narrow is the way" (Matthew 7:14, King James Version), with "strait" (meaning 'narrow') later confused with "straight".

3. NOUN

/ streɪt ənd ˈnæroʊ / (AmE), / streɪt ənd ˈnærəʊ / (BrE)
used to refer to a way of living that is honest, morally upright, and disciplined
a morally upright and disciplined way of life

Examples

After his release from prison, he vowed to stay on the straight and narrow.
Parents often hope their children will follow the straight and narrow.
Temptations can make it difficult for some people to remain on the straight and narrow.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: righteous - morally right or justifiable
  • Antonym: corrupt - willing to act dishonestly for personal gain

Etymology

From the phrase 'strait and narrow', influenced by biblical reference (Matthew 7:14), where 'strait' means narrow or difficult.
