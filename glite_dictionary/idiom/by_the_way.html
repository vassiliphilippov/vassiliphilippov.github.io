by the way

1. ADV

/ baɪ ðə weɪ / (AmE), / baɪ ðə weɪ / (BrE)
used to introduce a new subject or to add information, often unrelated to the previous topic of conversation
introducing a different topic; adding information

Examples

By the way, did you remember to call your sister?
I finished reading the report. By the way, the meeting has been moved to 3 PM.
She handed him his coffee and said, 'by the way, your friend dropped by earlier.'

Synset Examples

incidentally, I won't go to the party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incidentally - used to introduce a new or separate point

2. ADV

/ baɪ ðə weɪ / (AmE), / baɪ ðə weɪ / (BrE)
used to introduce a new topic or to add information that is not directly related to what was being discussed
introducing a new topic; incidental information

Examples

By the way, did you hear about the new project at work?
I saw Sarah yesterday, by the way.
By the way, your package arrived this morning.

Synset Examples

By the way, have you seen my keys?
By the way, I’ll be late to dinner.
I enjoyed the movie, by the way.
