apoplectic with rage

1. ADJ

/ ˌæpəˈplɛktɪk wɪð reɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌæpəˈplɛktɪk wɪð reɪdʒ / (BrE)
extremely angry; so filled with rage that one appears as if having a stroke or unable to control oneself.
extremely angry or furious

Examples

After hearing the unfair accusation, she became apoplectic with rage and demanded an apology.
The coach was apoplectic with rage when the team ignored his instructions during the match.
Rumors of budget cuts left many employees apoplectic with rage at the morning meeting.

Synset Examples

He was apoplectic with rage over the decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furious - extremely angry
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling anger

Etymology

From 'apoplectic' (relating to stroke or extreme anger), derived from Greek 'apoplēktikos', and 'rage', from Latin 'rabies'.
