to grit one's teeth

1. VERB

/ ɡrɪt wʌnz tiθ / (AmE), / ɡrɪt wʌnz tiːθ / (BrE)
to face pain, difficulty, or unpleasantness with determination and resolve, often by mentally preparing oneself to endure it
endure difficulty with determination

Examples

Despite her fear, she had to grit her teeth and continue the speech.
You just have to grit your teeth until the situation improves.
He gritted his teeth as the dentist worked on his cavity.

Synset Examples

She had to grit her teeth during the tough workout.
Sometimes you just have to grit your teeth and carry on.
He gritted his teeth and pushed through the pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persevere - to keep going despite difficulties
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop trying to do something difficult
