to look a day over

1. VERB

/ lʊk ə deɪ ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / lʊk ə deɪ ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(used with an age) to appear older than the specified age, often used in negative statements to emphasize a youthful appearance.
appear older than a particular age (idiom)

Examples

She doesn't look a day over forty, even though she just turned fifty.
People often say I don't look a day over thirty, which makes me happy.
You wouldn’t look a day over twenty in that new dress.

Synset Examples

At fifty, she does not look a day over thirty.
He claims he does not look a day over twenty-five.
