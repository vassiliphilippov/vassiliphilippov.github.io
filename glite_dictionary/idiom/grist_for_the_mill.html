grist for the mill

1. NOUN

/ ɡrɪst fər ðə mɪl / (AmE), / ɡrɪst fə ðə mɪl / (BrE)
something that can be used to one's advantage, especially information or events that can be turned to practical use or benefit, often in the context of gossip or reporting.
useful material or information

Examples

The scandal gave the journalists plenty of grist for the mill for their next articles.
His odd behavior was just more grist for the mill for the office rumor mill.
Every little detail about the celebrity’s life was seen as grist for the mill by the tabloids.

Synset Examples

His mistakes provided grist for the mill in the office.
Her story became grist for the mill at the gossip column.
The unexpected news was grist for the mill for the reporters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuel for the fire - something that adds to an existing problem or activity, often making it more intense
  • Synonym: ammunition - information or arguments that can be used in a conflict or debate

Etymology

From the literal use of 'grist' (grain to be ground) in a mill; metaphorically extended to mean anything that provides profit or advantage.
