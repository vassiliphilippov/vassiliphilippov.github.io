to hit the deck

1. VERB

/ hɪt ðə dɛk / (AmE), / hɪt ðə dɛk / (BrE)
to fall or drop to the ground suddenly, often to avoid danger or harm
to drop suddenly to avoid harm

Examples

As soon as the alarm sounded, everyone in the building hit the deck for safety.
The players hit the deck when lightning struck near the field.
During the fireworks malfunction, we all hit the deck to avoid flying debris.

Synset Examples

The soldiers hit the dirt when they heard gunfire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duck - to lower your head or body quickly to avoid being hit
