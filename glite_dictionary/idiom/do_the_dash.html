to do the dash

1. VERB

/ du ðə dæʃ / (AmE), / duː ðə dæʃ / (BrE)
to run away from a place or situation quickly, especially in order to escape.
run away quickly; escape
informal

Examples

When the alarm went off, he decided to do the dash before anyone noticed.
She knew she had to do the dash if she wanted to avoid getting caught.
They planned to do the dash as soon as the distraction started.

Synset Examples

I saw him do the dash when he spotted the cops.
