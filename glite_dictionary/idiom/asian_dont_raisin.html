Asian don't raisin

1. X

/ ˈeɪʒən doʊnt ˈreɪzən / (AmE), / ˈeɪʃ(ə)n dəʊnt ˈreɪz(ə)n / (BrE)
a humorous saying referring to the idea that people of Asian descent tend to show signs of aging slowly or maintain a youthful appearance for a long time
humorous saying about Asians aging slowly
informal slang

Examples

After seeing her grandmother's smooth skin, she laughed and said, "Asian don't raisin."
He credits his youthful looks to good genes, joking, "You know what they say, Asian don't raisin."
Whenever people are surprised by her age, she responds with the phrase, "Asian don't raisin."

Synset Examples

Her youthful appearance made her friends say Asian don't raisin.
She joked that her skincare routine proves Asian don't raisin.
Many believe in the saying Asian don't raisin for healthy skin.

Etymology

Wordplay blending 'Asian' and 'raisin', based on the rhyme and the idea that raisins are wrinkled and old-looking, while Asians are humorously said to stay youthful longer.
