to have to do with

1. VERB

/ hæv tə du wɪð / (AmE), / hæv tə duː wɪð / (BrE)
to be related to or connected with something
be related or connected with

Examples

What does climate change have to do with rising sea levels?
This document doesn't have to do with your current project.
The argument they had last night may have to do with their financial troubles.

Synset Examples

What does this have to do with our meeting?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be connected with - to be related to something
  • Synonym: be relevant to - to have importance to something
