to throw a sprat to catch a mackerel

1. VERB

/ θroʊ ə spræt tu kætʃ ə ˈmækərəl / (AmE), / θrəʊ ə spræt tə kætʃ ə ˈmækərəl / (BrE)
to offer or sacrifice a small thing in the hope of gaining something much larger or more valuable
offer little to gain more (idiom)

Examples

Sometimes you have to throw a sprat to catch a mackerel in business negotiations.
The company offered a discount to new clients, hoping to throw a sprat to catch a mackerel.
Donating a small amount to the campaign was his way of throwing a sprat to catch a mackerel.

Synset Examples

He decided to throw a sprat to catch a mackerel with that small investment.

Etymology

From the practice of using a sprat (a small fish) as bait to catch larger fish like mackerel; attested since the 17th century.
