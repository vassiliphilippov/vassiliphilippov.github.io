squirrel cage

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɝːrəl keɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈskwɪrəl keɪdʒ / (BrE)
an environment or situation filled with constant, frantic activity but offering little or no real progress
frantic busyness with no progress

Examples

After months in the squirrel cage of city life, she needed a break in the countryside.
He often compared his hectic schedule to running in a squirrel cage: always moving, but getting nowhere.
The team found themselves trapped in a squirrel cage of endless meetings and paperwork.

Synset Examples

He felt trapped in a squirrel cage at work, always busy.
The kids' room looked like a squirrel cage after playtime.
The office was a squirrel cage, buzzing with activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rat race - a way of life in which people are caught up in a fiercely competitive struggle for wealth or power

Etymology

From the resemblance to an exercise wheel in which a squirrel runs energetically but never advances.
