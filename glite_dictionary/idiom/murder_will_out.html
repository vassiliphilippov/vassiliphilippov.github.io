murder will out

1. X

/ ˈmɝːdɚ wɪl aʊt / (AmE), / ˈmɜːdə wɪl aʊt / (BrE)
used to say that the truth about wrongdoing or a crime will eventually come to light
truth about wrongdoing is eventually revealed

Examples

Despite years of secrecy, murder will out and the truth finally emerged.
She tried to cover up her mistake, but murder will out as the proverb says.
Many believe that no matter how careful you are, murder will out in the end.

Synset Examples

He learned the hard way that murder will out in the end.
Her secret was safe until she realized murder will out.
No matter how hard she tried, murder will out eventually.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to come to light - be revealed or discovered

Etymology

Proverbial phrase from Middle English, appearing in Chaucer's works (The Nun's Priest's Tale).
