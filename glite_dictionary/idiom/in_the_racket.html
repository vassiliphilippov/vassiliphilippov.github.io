in the racket

1. ADJ

/ ɪn ðə ˈrækɪt / (AmE), / ɪn ðə ˈrækɪt / (BrE)
involved in a particular line of business or activity, especially one that is disreputable or dishonest
involved in a disreputable line of work
informal

Examples

He admitted that he used to be in the racket before starting an honest job.
Many people in the city have some connection to those in the racket of gambling.
Lisa warned me not to get in the racket or risk ruining my reputation.

Synset Examples

He’s always been in the racket of selling antiques.
Many artists are in the racket of music distribution.
