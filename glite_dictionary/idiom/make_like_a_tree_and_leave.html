to make like a tree and leave

1. VERB

/ meɪk laɪk ə tri ənd liv / (AmE), / meɪk laɪk ə tri ənd liːv / (BrE)
to leave or depart quickly, often used humorously or as a pun
depart quickly (humorous idiom)
informal

Examples

As soon as the meeting ended, he decided to make like a tree and leave.
When the drama started, she thought it best to make like a tree and leave.
It was getting awkward, so I figured I'd make like a tree and leave.

Synset Examples

It was time to make like a tree and leave the party.
She chose to make like a tree and leave the argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take off - to leave or depart quickly, especially suddenly

Etymology

A pun on the similarity between 'leave' (depart) and 'leaf' (part of a tree).
