hornet's nest

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrnəts nɛst / (AmE), / ˈhɔːnɪts nɛst / (BrE)
a situation full of conflict, trouble, or danger, often provoked by a particular action
contentious or dangerous situation

Examples

By questioning the manager's honesty, he stirred up a real hornet's nest at the meeting.
The new tax proposal has created a hornet's nest of controversy in parliament.
Publishing that report would only open a hornet's nest of criticism.

Synset Examples

talk of invading Iraq stirred up a political hornets' nest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: can of worms - a situation that will cause a lot of trouble or problems if you try to deal with it
