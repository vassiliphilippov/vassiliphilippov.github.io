to rally round the flag

1. VERB

/ ˈræli raʊnd ðə flæɡ / (AmE), / ˈræli raʊnd ðə flaɡ / (BrE)
to come together and show united support for a cause, leader, or country, especially in times of crisis or danger
unite in support, especially in crisis

Examples

When the town was threatened by flooding, everyone began to rally round the flag to help each other.
After the scandal broke, party members tried to rally round the flag and support their leader.
Whenever the country faces external threats, citizens tend to rally round the flag regardless of their political views.

Synset Examples

In times of war, people tend to rally round the flag.
The citizens rally round the flag during the national crisis.
They always rally round the flag when faced with challenges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to band together - to unite for a common purpose
  • Synonym: to fall in line - to conform or show support for a leader or cause

Etymology

From the tradition of soldiers and citizens physically gathering around a flag to show unity and solidarity, particularly during wartime.
