to hand in the till

1. VERB

/ hænd ɪn ðə tɪl / (AmE), / hænd ɪn ðə tɪl / (BrE)
to steal money from the place where one works, especially from a cash register or company funds
to steal from a workplace's cash

Examples

After years of trust, the manager was shocked to discover that his employee had his hand in the till.
Anyone who puts their hand in the till will face immediate dismissal.
Rumors spread quickly when the auditor accused him of having his hand in the till.

Synset Examples

He was fired for having his hand in the till repeatedly.
The accountant was caught with his hand in the till.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embezzle - to steal money one is responsible for, especially from one's employer
  • Synonym: steal - to take something without permission or legal right
