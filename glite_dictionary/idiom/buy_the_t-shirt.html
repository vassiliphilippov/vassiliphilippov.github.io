to buy the T-shirt

1. VERB

/ baɪ ðə ˈti ˌʃɝt / (AmE), / baɪ ðə ˈtiː ˌʃɜːt / (BrE)
(informal) Used to indicate that someone has firsthand experience of something, often suggesting familiarity, boredom, or having moved on from it.
have firsthand experience (idiom)
informal

Examples

I've bought the T-shirt when it comes to difficult jobs—I know exactly how it feels.
He claimed to have handled dozens of big projects, bought the T-shirt, and now wants a new challenge.
She said she's bought the T-shirt with online dating and isn't interested anymore.

Synset Examples

She said she's been there, done that, bought the T-shirt, and moved on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: been there, done that - already experienced or done something
