to have it in for

1. VERB

/ hæv ɪt ɪn fər / (AmE), / hæv ɪt ɪn fə / (BrE)
to be hostile toward (someone) or to persistently hold a grudge against them and expect harm or ill-will
to hold a grudge against someone

Examples

I can't understand why my supervisor has it in for me these days.
Ever since the mistake, it feels like Mark has it in for his coworker.
She believes the principal has it in for her, no matter what she does.

Synset Examples

I think the boss has it in for me.
He feels like the teacher has it in for him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hold a grudge - to continue to feel angry with someone
  • Antonym: to forgive - to stop feeling angry or resentful toward someone
