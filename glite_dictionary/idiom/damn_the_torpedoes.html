to damn the torpedoes

1. VERB

/ ˌdæm ðə tɔrˈpiːdoʊz / (AmE), / ˌdæm ðə tɔːˈpiːdəʊz / (BrE)
to proceed with bold determination despite risks or potential obstacles, often in a reckless or defiant manner
press on despite risks; act boldly

Examples

We knew there were risks, but we decided to damn the torpedoes and move forward with the plan.
She shouted, 'Damn the torpedoes!' and leaped into action despite opposition.
The candidate chose to damn the torpedoes and address the controversial topic directly.

Synset Examples

In business, sometimes you must damn the torpedoes and take risks.
He decided to damn the torpedoes and pursue his dream.
She thought, 'Damn the torpedoes,' as she applied for the job.

Etymology

From a famous order reportedly given by U.S. Admiral David Farragut during the American Civil War: "Damn the torpedoes, full speed ahead!"
