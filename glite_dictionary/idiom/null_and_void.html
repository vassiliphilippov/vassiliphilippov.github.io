null and void

1. ADJ

/ nʌl ənd vɔɪd / (AmE), / nʌl ənd vɔɪd / (BrE)
Having no legal force; not valid or legally binding.
legally invalid or ineffective
legal

Examples

The judge ruled that the entire agreement was null and void due to misrepresentation.
If a signature is missing, the contract becomes null and void.
Their marriage was declared null and void after the truth emerged.

Synset Examples

The contract was declared null and void due to fraud.
They found the agreement to be null and void in court.
Her apology was deemed null and void after the incident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invalid - not legally valid
  • Antonym: binding - having legal force or effect
