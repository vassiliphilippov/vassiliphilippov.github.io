to tie oneself in knots

1. VERB

/ taɪ wʌnˈsɛlf ɪn nɑts / (AmE), / taɪ wʌnˈsɛlf ɪn nɒts / (BrE)
to become very confused, anxious, or upset, especially because of trying too hard to deal with a difficult situation
to become very confused or anxious

Examples

She tends to tie herself in knots over minor decisions she makes at work.
It's easy to tie yourself in knots worrying about things beyond your control.
He began to tie himself in knots explaining why he missed the meeting.

Synset Examples

Don’t tie yourself in knots trying to please everyone.
She tends to tie herself in knots over minor decisions.
He always ties himself in knots when explaining complex ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get worked up - to become very upset or anxious
  • Antonym: to keep one's cool - to remain calm and not become anxious
