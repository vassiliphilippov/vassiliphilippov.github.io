to shake things up

1. VERB

/ ʃeɪk θɪŋz ʌp / (AmE), / ʃeɪk θɪŋz ʌp / (BrE)
to make significant changes in order to create excitement, variety, or improvement
make changes for excitement or variety

Examples

After ten years at the same job, she wanted to shake things up and try something new.
The manager hired a new coach to shake things up and improve the team's performance.
Sometimes it's good to shake things up in your routine to keep life interesting.

Synset Examples

He decided to shake things up at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stir things up - to cause activity, excitement, or trouble; similar in meaning
