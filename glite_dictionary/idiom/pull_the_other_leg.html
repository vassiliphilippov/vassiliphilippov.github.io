to pull the other leg

1. VERB

/ pʊl ði ˈʌðər lɛg / (AmE), / pʊl ði ˈʌðə lɛg / (BrE)
(British, informal) Used to indicate disbelief in what someone is saying, suggesting they are joking or teasing.
used to show disbelief at a joke or tease
informal british

Examples

Are you serious, or are you just pulling the other leg again?
When she said she won the lottery twice, I knew she was pulling the other leg.
If you think I believe that story, you can pull the other leg!

Synset Examples

You really expect me to believe that? Pull the other leg!
You think I'm falling for that? Pull the other one, it's got bells on!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pull someone’s leg - to tease or joke with someone
