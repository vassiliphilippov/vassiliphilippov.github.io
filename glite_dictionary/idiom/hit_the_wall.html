to hit the wall

1. VERB

/ hɪt ðə wɔl / (AmE), / hɪt ðə wɔːl / (BrE)
to reach a point at which one is unable to continue or make progress, especially due to exhaustion or a sudden barrier
reach a state of exhaustion or blockage

Examples

Many runners hit the wall around the twentieth mile of a marathon.
I hit the wall creatively and couldn't complete the final chapter of my book.
After hours of discussing the problem, the team finally hit the wall and took a break.

Synset Examples

The project stalled when we hit the wall with funding.
She hit the wall during her marathon training yesterday.
After running for hours, I finally hit the wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to burn out - to become extremely tired or exhausted, especially from overwork
  • Antonym: to break through - to overcome an obstacle or barrier, often after persistence
