make a rod for one's own back

1. X

/ ˈmeɪk ə rɑd fɔr wʌnz oʊn bæk / (AmE), / ˈmeɪk ə rɒd fə wʌnz əʊn bæk / (BrE)
to do something that is likely to cause future trouble, difficulty, or suffering for oneself
cause trouble for oneself

Examples

By taking on so many extra responsibilities, she was making a rod for her own back.
If you keep lending him money, you'll only be making a rod for your own back.
His refusal to ask for help was making a rod for his own back.

Synset Examples

Ignoring the warning was like making a rod for his back.

Etymology

From the literal sense of making a stick to be used for punishment, used figuratively since at least the 18th century.
