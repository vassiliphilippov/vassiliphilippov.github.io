to maintain the high ground

1. VERB

/ meɪnˈteɪn ðə haɪ ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / meɪnˈteɪn ðə haɪ ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
to keep a position of advantage or moral superiority, especially in argument or competition
keep a position of advantage or superiority

Examples

Despite criticism, she chose to maintain the high ground throughout the exchange.
It can be difficult to maintain the high ground when others are being rude.
In negotiations, he prefers to maintain the high ground rather than resort to underhanded tactics.

Synset Examples

He always tries to maintain the high ground in debates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take the high ground - to assume a position of advantage or moral superiority
  • Antonym: lose the high ground - to lose one's advantage or moral superiority
