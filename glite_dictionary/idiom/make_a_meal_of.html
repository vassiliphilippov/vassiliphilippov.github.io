to make a meal of

1. VERB

/ ˌmeɪk ə ˈmiːl əv / (AmE), / ˌmeɪk ə ˈmiːl əv / (BrE)
to treat something as more complicated, difficult, or important than it really is; to exaggerate the significance of something
exaggerate complexity or importance
british

Examples

She really made a meal of completing the application form—it should have taken five minutes, not an hour.
Don't make a meal of this small problem; just fix it and move on.
Instead of simply apologizing, he made a meal of his explanation and went on for ages.

Synset Examples

She tends to make a meal of everything she cooks.
He can really make a meal of his stories.
Don't make a meal of this; it's just a simple task.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overdo - to do something to an excessive degree; to exaggerate
  • Antonym: to understate - to present something as being smaller or less important than it actually is
