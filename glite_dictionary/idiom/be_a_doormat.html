to be a doormat

1. VERB

/ bi ə ˈdɔːrmæt / (AmE), / biː ə ˈdɔːmæt / (BrE)
to allow others to treat you badly or take advantage of you without standing up for yourself
allow others to treat you badly

Examples

If you be a doormat, people will keep taking advantage of you.
She realized she didn't want to be a doormat in her relationships anymore.
Learning not to be a doormat can help improve your self-respect.

Synset Examples

She refused to be a doormat and stood up for herself.
Being a doormat won't win you any friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pushover - a person who is easily influenced or controlled
  • Antonym: to stand up for oneself - to defend one's rights or opinions
