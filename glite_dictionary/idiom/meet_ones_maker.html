to meet one's maker

1. VERB

/ mit wʌnz ˈmeɪkɚ / (AmE), / miːt wʌnz ˈmeɪkə / (BrE)
(informal) to die; to pass away, often with the suggestion of facing judgment after death.
to die; to pass away
informal

Examples

If you keep teasing that lion, you might meet your maker sooner than you think.
The old outlaw finally met his maker after years on the run.
Many believe that when they die, they will meet their maker and be judged for their actions.

Synset Examples

Many believe we all eventually meet our maker.
If you keep driving like that, you'll meet your maker.
He joked that he might meet his maker soon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: die - to stop living; to cease to exist
  • Synonym: to pass away - (euphemism) to die
  • Antonym: to be born - to come into existence at birth

Etymology

From 'maker' meaning 'creator', referring to God as the one who made people.
