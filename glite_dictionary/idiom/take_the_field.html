to take the field

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə fiːld / (AmE), / teɪk ðə fiːld / (BrE)
to begin military operations; to go off to war or start a campaign.
begin military operations

Examples

After months of preparation, the army will take the field at dawn.
The general's troops took the field with determination and courage.
Many young men left home to take the field during the war.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01096197-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ teɪk ðə fiːld / (AmE), / teɪk ðə fiːld / (BrE)
(of a sports team or player) to go onto the playing field, especially at the start of a game.
go onto the playing field (sports idiom)

Examples

The players will take the field at 7 p.m. for the championship game.
Cheers erupted as the home team took the field in their new uniforms.
Fans watched anxiously, waiting for their favorite athletes to take the field after the long delay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enter the field - to go onto the playing field; begin participation in a game
