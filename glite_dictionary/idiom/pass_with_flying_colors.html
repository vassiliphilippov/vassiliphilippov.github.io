to pass with flying colors

1. VERB

/ pæs wɪð ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈkʌlɚz / (AmE), / pɑːs wɪð ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈkʌləz / (BrE)
to achieve success easily and with a high level of excellence, especially in an examination or test
to succeed brilliantly, especially in a test

Examples

After months of studying, she managed to pass with flying colors and got the highest score in her class.
He was nervous about the interview, but in the end, he passed with flying colors.
If you prepare well, you will pass with flying colors just like your brother did.

Synset Examples

She sailed through her exams
You will pass with flying colors
She nailed her astrophysics course

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to succeed - to achieve an aim or result
  • Antonym: to fail - to be unsuccessful in achieving an aim or result

Etymology

The idiom originates from the practice of ships returning to port with their flags (colors) flying high to show victory or success.
