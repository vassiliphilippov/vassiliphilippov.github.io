to earn crust

1. VERB

/ ɜrn krʌst / (AmE), / ɜːn krʌst / (BrE)
to make a living; to earn enough money to support oneself
make a living (informal, British)
informal british

Examples

After losing his job, he had to take up odd tasks to earn crust.
Many artists find it challenging to earn crust through their work alone.
She moved to the city hoping to earn crust and build a better future.

Synset Examples

He works hard to earn his crust every day.
Freelancers often hustle to earn their crust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: make a living - to earn money to support oneself
  • Synonym: to get by - to manage to survive, especially financially

Etymology

From the British slang 'crust' meaning money or livelihood, deriving from the idea of earning enough to buy bread ('crust' of bread).
