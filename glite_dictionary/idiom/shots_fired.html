shots fired

1. X

/ ʃɑts ˈfaɪɚd / (AmE), / ʃɒts ˈfaɪəd / (BrE)
used to indicate that someone has made a pointed or harsh remark, often as a verbal attack or criticism
verbal attack; pointed remark
informal

Examples

When she mentioned his mistake in front of everyone, it was clear there were shots fired.
The comedian made a joke about the host—shots fired right at the start of the show.
Social media lit up with shots fired after the company's controversial decision.

Synset Examples

The debate got heated with shots fired from both sides.
During the argument, there were metaphorical shots fired.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dig - an indirect remark intended as criticism
  • Synonym: insult - a disrespectful or scornful remark
