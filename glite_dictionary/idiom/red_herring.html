red herring

1. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈhɛr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / rɛd ˈhɛr.ɪŋ / (BrE)
A clue, idea, or piece of information intended to mislead or distract from the real issue or solution.
misleading distraction

Examples

The detective realized the strange clue was just a red herring to divert the investigation.
Politicians often use a red herring to shift focus away from difficult topics.
That subplot turned out to be a red herring and had nothing to do with the mystery's solution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distraction - something that draws attention away from something else
  • Antonym: main issue - the central topic or problem under discussion

Etymology

Originally referred to smoked herring used to distract scent-tracking dogs, attested from the early 19th century in English.

2. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈhɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / rɛd ˈhɛrɪŋ / (BrE)
A clue or piece of information that is intended to be misleading or distracting from the real issue.
misleading or distracting clue

Examples

The police believed the anonymous tip was a red herring designed to delay the investigation.
The subplot served as a red herring, distracting viewers from the film's real twist.
Adding technical jargon was just a red herring to confuse the committee.

Synset Examples

His excuse was neither fish, flesh, nor good red herring.
His excuse turned out to be a red herring.
They used a red herring to distract from the real issue.
The detective discovered the suspect's alibi was a red herring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decoy - something intended to mislead or distract
  • Synonym: distraction - something that diverts attention from the main issue
  • Antonym: hint - useful or suggestive clue (opposite: gives correct direction)

Etymology

Originally from the practice of using smoked (red) herring as a scent to distract hunting dogs from a trail.
