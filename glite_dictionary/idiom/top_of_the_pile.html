top of the pile

1. NOUN

/ tɑp əv ðə paɪl / (AmE), / tɒp əv ðə paɪl / (BrE)
in the most important, prominent, or favored position; having the highest priority or status
most important or prioritized position

Examples

After her outstanding performance, she found herself at the top of the pile for the next promotion.
Urgent requests usually go straight to the top of the pile on his desk.
Only the best candidates will make it to the top of the pile during the interview selection.

Synset Examples

She submitted her report and felt at the top of the pile.
He always keeps his tasks at the top of the pile.
Being at the top of the pile means more responsibilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: top dog - the person or group in the leading position
  • Antonym: bottom of the pile - in the lowest or least important position
