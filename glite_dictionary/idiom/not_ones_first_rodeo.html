not (one's) first rodeo

1. X

/ nɑt ˈfɜrst roʊˈdiːoʊ / (AmE), / nɒt ˈfɜːst rəʊˈdiːəʊ / (BrE)
used to indicate that someone is experienced and not new to a particular activity or situation
to be experienced; not new to something
informal american

Examples

I've managed big projects before; it's not my first rodeo.
Don't worry about her dealing with tough clients—this isn't her first rodeo.
When it comes to emergencies, it's clearly not his first rodeo.

Synset Examples

She navigated the negotiation skillfully; it's not her first rodeo.
He handled the situation well; it's not his first rodeo.
They knew how to manage the crisis; it’s not their first rodeo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: old hand - a person with long experience in a particular field

Etymology

An American idiom deriving from the phrase 'this is not my first rodeo', referencing the experience gained from repeated participation in rodeos.
