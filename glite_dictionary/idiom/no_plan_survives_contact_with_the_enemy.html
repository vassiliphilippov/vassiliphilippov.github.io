no plan survives contact with the enemy

1. X

/ noʊ ˈplæn səˈvaɪvz ˈkɑntækt wɪð ði ˈɛnəmi / (AmE), / nəʊ ˈplæn səˈvaɪvz ˈkɒntækt wɪð ði ˈɛnəmi / (BrE)
used to express that any plan may fail or need to be changed when faced with real-world conditions or unforeseen challenges
plans rarely succeed unchanged in reality

Examples

Throughout the campaign, they remembered that no plan survives contact with the enemy and adapted quickly.
His detailed proposal changed completely, proving that no plan survives contact with the enemy.
She cautioned her team with the reminder that no plan survives contact with the enemy.

Synset Examples

He quickly realized that no plan survives contact with the enemy during the project.
Her strategy fell apart; no plan survives contact with the enemy.
In war, it's clear that no plan survives contact with the enemy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the best laid plans - expression meaning even well-prepared plans may fail

Etymology

Paraphrase of a saying attributed to the Prussian general Helmuth von Moltke: 'No plan survives first contact with the enemy.'
