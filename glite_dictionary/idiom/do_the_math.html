to do the math

1. VERB

/ du ðə mæθ / (AmE), / də ðə mɑːθ / (BrE)
to calculate or analyze the facts or situation in order to reach a conclusion, often about cost, benefits, or feasibility
calculate or analyze a situation

Examples

Before signing the contract, it's important to do the math to ensure you can afford the payments.
When he realized how much he was spending on coffee each month, he decided to do the math.
If you do the math, buying in bulk often saves you money in the long run.

Synset Examples

She decided to do the math before making a purchase.
If you do the math, you'll see it's cheaper.
To save money, just do the math on your expenses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to figure out - to solve or understand something after thinking about it
