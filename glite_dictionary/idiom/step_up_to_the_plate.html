to step up to the plate

1. VERB

/ stɛp ʌp tə ðə pleɪt / (AmE), / stɛp ʌp tə ðə pleɪt / (BrE)
to take responsibility for something or take action when a difficult situation arises, especially when others may not.
take responsibility in tough situations

Examples

In times of crisis, true leaders step up to the plate and guide their teams forward.
When the project started falling behind, Sarah was the one who stepped up to the plate.
With no one else volunteering, he decided to step up to the plate and organize the event.

Synset Examples

It's time for you to step up to the plate and take responsibility.
When the team needed a leader, she stepped up to the plate.
He always steps up to the plate during tough times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take responsibility - assume control or accountability in a situation

Etymology

Originates from baseball, where a batter steps up to the plate to face a challenge.
