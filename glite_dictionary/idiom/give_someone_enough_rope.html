to give someone enough rope

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ˈsʌmwʌn ɪˈnʌf roʊp / (AmE), / ɡɪv ˈsʌmwʌn ɪˈnʌf rəʊp / (BrE)
to allow someone enough freedom or opportunities that they may cause their own failure or downfall
allowing freedom that leads to self-destruction

Examples

The manager decided to give him enough rope and see if he would finally admit his mistakes.
Sometimes it's better to give someone enough rope rather than confront them directly.
She thought it wise to give her enough rope, hoping her rival would make a fatal error.

Synset Examples

He warned her that if you give someone enough rope, they might disappoint you.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: let someone dig their own grave - to let someone continue actions that will cause their own downfall

Etymology

From the longer proverb 'Give someone enough rope and they'll hang themselves', referring metaphorically to causing one's own misfortune through excessive freedom.
