breath of fresh air

1. NOUN

/ brɛθ əv frɛʃ ɛr / (AmE), / brɛθ əv frɛʃ eə / (BrE)
a person or thing that brings a welcome change or relief from what is usual or unpleasant
welcome change or relief

Examples

Working with Carla was a breath of fresh air after the previous stressful projects.
His sense of humor was a breath of fresh air in the tense office environment.
The team's new approach is a breath of fresh air for the company.

Synset Examples

the new management was like a breath of fresh air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relief - a feeling of reassurance and relaxation following release from anxiety or distress

2. NOUN

/ brɛθ əv frɛʃ ɛr / (AmE), / brɛθ əv frɛʃ eə / (BrE)
a person, thing, or idea that is new, different, and refreshing; a welcomed change from what is usual or expected
a welcome change; something refreshing

Examples

Her innovative approach was truly a breath of fresh air in the company.
The film provides a breath of fresh air amid predictable action movies.
After working with old methods, the new software felt like a breath of fresh air.

Synset Examples

The new café offers a breath of fresh air in our neighborhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refreshing - making you feel less tired or restoring energy; invigorating
  • Antonym: stale - no longer new or interesting; dull
