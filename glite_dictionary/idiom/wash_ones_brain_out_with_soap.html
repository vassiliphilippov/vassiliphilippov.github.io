to wash one's brain out with soap

1. VERB

/ wɑʃ wʌnz breɪn aʊt wɪð soʊp / (AmE), / wɒʃ wʌnz breɪn aʊt wɪð səʊp / (BrE)
(informal) to try to rid oneself of unpleasant or disturbing thoughts, as if cleansing the mind of contamination.
try to rid oneself of disturbing thoughts
informal

Examples

After hearing the gruesome details, she wished she could wash her brain out with soap.
Sometimes I want to wash my brain out with soap after reading disturbing news online.
He joked that he needed to wash his brain out with soap after watching that horror movie.

Synset Examples

The shocking news made her wish she could wash her brain out with soap.
He felt like he needed to wash his brain out with soap after that movie.
Sometimes, you just need to wash your brain out with soap and start fresh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wash one's mouth out with soap - scold someone for using bad language

Etymology

Modelled on the idiom 'wash one's mouth out with soap,' referring to cleansing or purifying, with 'brain' replacing 'mouth' to indicate thoughts instead of speech.
