to shake off the dust from one's feet

1. VERB

/ ʃeɪk ɑf ðə dʌst frəm wʌnz fit / (AmE), / ʃeɪk ɒf ðə dʌst frəm wʌnz fiːt / (BrE)
to leave a place or situation, often after a negative experience, with no intention of returning or maintaining ties; to move on resolutely
depart with finality; move on after negativity

Examples

After the bitter dispute, she chose to shake off the dust from her feet and never looked back.
Many travelers shake off the dust from their feet when they leave a disappointing town behind.
He advised them to shake off the dust from their feet and focus on new opportunities.

Synset Examples

She needed to shake off the dust from her feet after the hike.
After a long day, he decided to shake off the dust from his feet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to move on - to proceed to the next stage or activity; to leave something behind
  • Synonym: to let go - to stop holding onto or being affected by something negative

Etymology

Alludes to a biblical practice, notably in the New Testament, symbolizing the act of renouncing responsibility for or ties to those who reject a message.
