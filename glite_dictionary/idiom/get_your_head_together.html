to get your head together

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt jɔːr hɛd təˈɡɛðər / (AmE), / ɡɛt jɔː hɛd təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to organize your thoughts or emotions, especially after being confused or upset
organize one's thoughts or emotions
informal

Examples

After the breakup, he took some time alone to get his head together.
Before making any decisions, she needed to get her head together.
Taking a walk helped me get my head together before the meeting.

Synset Examples

Sometimes, you need to step back and get your head together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collect yourself - to regain self-control, especially of emotions
  • Synonym: pull yourself together - to regain control of one's emotions or actions
