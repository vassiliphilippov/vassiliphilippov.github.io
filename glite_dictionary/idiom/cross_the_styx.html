to cross the Styx

1. VERB

/ krɑs ðə stɪks / (AmE), / krɒs ðə stɪks / (BrE)
to die or approach death, often used metaphorically or allusively, especially in literary contexts
to die; approach death (figurative)

Examples

The old sailor feared he might cross the Styx during the storm at sea.
As his illness worsened, he joked about being ready to cross the Styx.
Many poets have written about what it means to cross the Styx.

Synset Examples

She believed he would cross the Styx if he keeps working so hard.
He felt like he would cross the Styx before finishing his work.
The old man joked about crossing the Styx soon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pass away - to die (polite/euphemistic usage)
  • Synonym: depart this life - to die (formal/literary usage)

Etymology

From Greek mythology, where the Styx is a river separating the world of the living from the realm of the dead.
