to wrap in cotton wool

1. VERB

/ ræp ɪn ˈkɑːtn̩ wʊl / (AmE), / ræp ɪn ˈkɒtn̩ wʊl / (BrE)
to protect someone too much, preventing them from experiencing difficulty, harm, or risk
protect someone excessively
british

Examples

Her parents wrap her in cotton wool and rarely let her go out alone.
We shouldn't wrap our children in cotton wool; a little risk is part of growing up.
If you wrap employees in cotton wool, they'll never learn to solve problems themselves.

Synset Examples

Don’t wrap him in cotton wool; he needs to learn.
She tends to wrap her children in cotton wool.
