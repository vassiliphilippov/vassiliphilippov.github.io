to take for all one has got

1. VERB

/ teɪk fəɹ ɔl wʌn hæz ɡɑt / (AmE), / teɪk fə ɔːl wʌn hæz ɡɒt / (BrE)
to exploit or deceive someone as much as possible, often taking everything they have
exploit or deceive completely

Examples

The fraudster tried to take her for all she's got with his elaborate scheme.
They were almost taken for all they'd got by the dishonest contractor.
Be wary of scammers who want to take you for all you've got.

Synset Examples

The con artist tried to take her for all she's got.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swindle - to cheat someone out of money or possessions
  • Synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly or unfairly to gain advantage; especially financially
