in someone's corner

1. ADP

/ ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈkɔrnər / (AmE), / ɪn ˈsʌmwənz ˈkɔːnə / (BrE)
supporting or backing someone, especially during difficulties or challenges
supportive of someone

Examples

No matter what happens, I know you’ll always be in my corner.
Her parents were firmly in her corner as she started her new business.
It meant a lot to have colleagues in my corner during the dispute.

Synset Examples

The coach was clearly in his player's corner after the loss.
It's great to know you've got someone in your corner.
My friends are always in my corner during tough times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: back someone up - to give support to someone
  • Synonym: stand by someone - to remain loyal to someone and support them

Etymology

Originates from boxing, where a boxer's supporters and trainers stay in the boxer's corner of the ring.
