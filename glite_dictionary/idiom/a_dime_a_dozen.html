a dime a dozen

1. ADJ

/ ə daɪm ə dəˈzʌzən / (AmE), / ə daɪm ə dəˈzʌzən / (BrE)
very common and therefore of little value or easily obtained
very common; easy to find
american

Examples

In big cities, coffee shops are a dime a dozen on every block.
Talented programmers may seem a dime a dozen, but truly exceptional ones are rare.
Excuses for being late are a dime a dozen, but genuine reasons are uncommon.

Synset Examples

Good ideas are a dime a dozen; execution is key.
Those toys are a dime a dozen around here.
In this town, bright students are a dime a dozen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common - occurring frequently, not rare or unique
  • Antonym: rare - not common or frequent; not easily found

Etymology

American English, from the historical price of certain goods being ten cents for twelve (dime = ten cents, dozen = twelve), implying cheapness and abundance.
