to go into a tizzy

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈɪntu ə ˈtɪzi / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈɪntuː ə ˈtɪzi / (BrE)
to become very upset, agitated, or confused, often in a sudden or dramatic way
become upset or agitated

Examples

When the meeting was unexpectedly cancelled, the whole team went into a tizzy.
He went into a tizzy when he realized he had lost his keys before the big interview.
Every time the computer crashes, she goes into a tizzy and starts pacing the room.

Synset Examples

After hearing the news, she went into a tizzy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panic - sudden overwhelming fear or anxiety
  • Antonym: to calm down - to become less upset or agitated

Etymology

Possibly from US slang 'tizzy', meaning a state of confusion or agitation, dating from the 1930s.
