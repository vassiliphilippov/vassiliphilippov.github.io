to choke on one's words

1. VERB

/ tʃoʊk ɑn wʌnz wɜrdz / (AmE), / tʃəʊk ɒn wʌnz wɜːdz / (BrE)
to hesitate or fail to speak because of nervousness, embarrassment, or emotion
be unable to speak due to nerves or emotion

Examples

He tried to apologize, but he began to choke on his words in front of the crowd.
Whenever she has to speak in public, she tends to choke on her words.
Nervousness made her choke on her words during the interview.

Synset Examples

She might choke on her words if she gets nervous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be at a loss for words - be unable to speak, often from emotion or surprise
