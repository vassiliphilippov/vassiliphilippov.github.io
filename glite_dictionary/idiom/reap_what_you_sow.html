to reap what you sow

1. VERB

/ rip wət ju soʊ / (AmE), / riːp wɒt juː səʊ / (BrE)
to experience the consequences of one's actions, especially as a result of one's own behavior or decisions
experience consequences of one's actions

Examples

After years of dishonesty, he had to reap what he sowed when no one trusted him.
If you treat others kindly, you'll eventually reap what you sow.
She warned him that one day he would reap what he sowed if he continued to cheat.

Synset Examples

If you work hard, you’ll reap what you sow.
Remember, you always reap what you sow with others.
He learned you reap what one sows in life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get one's comeuppance - receive deserved punishment or misfortune
  • Synonym: pay the price - suffer the consequences (often negative) of an action
  • Antonym: escape unscathed - to avoid negative consequences

Etymology

Based on imagery from agriculture; derived from the biblical proverb 'as you sow, so shall you reap'.
