no soap

1. X

/ noʊ soʊp / (AmE), / nəʊ səʊp / (BrE)
used to indicate failure or lack of success, especially after an attempt at something
no success; failure
informal

Examples

I tried calling the support line, but no soap—they never answered.
She hoped for a promotion, but in the end it was no soap.
He applied for several jobs, but it was no soap all summer.

Synset Examples

I tried to fix the car, but no soap.
She asked him for help, but no soap.
Despite my efforts, the project got canceled—no soap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: no dice - used to express refusal or failure

Etymology

20th-century American slang, original meaning uncertain, possibly from 'soap' as slang for 'money' or 'bribe'.
