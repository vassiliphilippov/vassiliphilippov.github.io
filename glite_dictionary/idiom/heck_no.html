heck no

1. INTJ

/ hɛk noʊ / (AmE), / hɛk nəʊ / (BrE)
used to express strong refusal or disagreement with a suggestion or request
emphatic informal refusal
informal

Examples

Heck no, I'm not working overtime this weekend!
Did I eat the last piece of cake? Heck no!
Would I ever move to the North Pole? Heck no, it's way too cold.

Synset Examples

When asked if she’d jump out of a plane, she said, 'Heck no!'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: no way - used to refuse emphatically or express disbelief
  • Antonym: yes - used to express affirmation or agreement

Etymology

'Heck' is a mild euphemism for 'hell', used to avoid swearing.
