cash on the barrelhead

1. NOUN

/ kæʃ ɑn ðə ˈbærəlˌhɛd / (AmE), / kæʃ ɒn ðə ˈbærəlˌhɛd / (BrE)
immediate payment in full, especially in cash, at the time of a transaction
immediate cash payment

Examples

If you want to buy this antique desk, it's cash on the barrelhead—no checks or credit cards.
The landlord insisted on cash on the barrelhead for the first month's rent.
We settled the deal cash on the barrelhead to keep things simple and straightforward.

Synset Examples

He expects cash on the barrelhead before starting the work.
The seller demanded cash on the barrelhead for the car.
They agreed to settle cash on the barrelhead to avoid delays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pay upfront - to pay in advance, before receiving goods or services
  • Synonym: cash on delivery - payment for goods when they are delivered

Etymology

American English origin; refers to cash placed on a barrelhead (barrel top) as a counter in 19th-century U.S. saloons and stores.
