to get the hang of

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ðə hæŋ əv / (AmE), / ɡɛt ðə hæŋ ɒv / (BrE)
to learn how to do something, especially after some practice or experience
learn how to do something through practice

Examples

After a few lessons, I finally got the hang of driving a manual car.
Don't worry if it's tricky at first—you'll get the hang of it soon enough!
It took Emma some time, but she eventually got the hang of skiing.

Synset Examples

Once you get the hang of it, it's quite easy.
It took me a while to get the hang of this game.
I still can't get the hang of riding a bike.
The job felt cruisy once I got the hang of it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: master - acquire complete knowledge or skill in something
  • Antonym: struggle - have difficulty learning or doing something
