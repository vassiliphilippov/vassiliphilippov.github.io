mug's game

1. NOUN

/ mʌɡz ɡeɪm / (AmE), / mʌɡz ɡeɪm / (BrE)
a task or pursuit that is pointless, unprofitable, or likely to end in disappointment or failure
pointless or unprofitable pursuit
british informal

Examples

Trying to predict the stock market every day is a mug's game.
He soon realized that arguing with her was a mug's game.
Chasing quick money in casinos is really a mug's game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool's errand - a task with no hope of success

Etymology

British slang "mug" meaning a fool or dupe; thus, a "mug's game" is one suited to fools.
