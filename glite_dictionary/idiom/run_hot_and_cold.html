to run hot and cold

1. VERB

/ rʌn hɑt ənd koʊld / (AmE), / rʌn hɒt ənd kəʊld / (BrE)
to change frequently and unpredictably in feelings, attitudes, or condition, often shifting between extremes
to fluctuate greatly, especially in feelings or attitudes

Examples

Her enthusiasm for learning Spanish tends to run hot and cold depending on her mood.
Political support for the policy continues to run hot and cold with each new development.
Over the years, his relationship with his family has run hot and cold.

Synset Examples

The weather can run hot and cold during spring.
His interest in the project seems to run hot and cold.
She tends to run hot and cold in her relationships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vacillate - to waver between different opinions or actions
  • Antonym: be consistent - to remain steady or uniform in feelings or opinions
