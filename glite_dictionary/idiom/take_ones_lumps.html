to take one's lumps

1. VERB

/ teɪk wʌnz lʌmps / (AmE), / teɪk wʌnz lʌmps / (BrE)
to accept or endure unpleasant results or punishment, especially as a consequence of one's own actions or mistakes
endure consequences or punishment

Examples

After making that mistake at work, he knew he had to take his lumps from the boss.
If you break the rules, you have to take your lumps like everyone else.
She failed the exam, but she decided to take her lumps and try again next time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to face the music - to accept the unpleasant consequences of one's actions
  • Antonym: to get away with - to escape the consequences of one's actions
