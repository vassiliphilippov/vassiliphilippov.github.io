from whole cloth

1. ADV

/ frəm hoʊl klɔθ / (AmE), / frɒm həʊl klɒθ / (BrE)
Completely invented or fabricated, with no basis in fact or reality.
entirely invented; without factual basis

Examples

The report was created from whole cloth and had no supporting evidence.
Political opponents claimed the accusation was invented from whole cloth.
Many urban legends are spun from whole cloth without any factual foundation.

Synset Examples

She weaved her tale from whole cloth, with no basis in fact.
The rumor was fabricated from whole cloth.
His story was made from whole cloth and completely unbelievable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fabricate - to invent or concoct something, typically with deceitful intent
  • Synonym: to make up - to invent a story or explanation, often untrue
  • Antonym: grounded in fact - based on true or actual information

Etymology

Originally referring to cloth sold as a full piece (not cut), the idiom arose in 19th-century American English; the figurative sense refers to making up something from nothing.
