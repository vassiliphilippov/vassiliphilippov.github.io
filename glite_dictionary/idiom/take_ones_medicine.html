to take one's medicine

1. VERB

/ teɪk wʌnz ˈmɛdəsɪn / (AmE), / teɪk wʌnz ˈmɛdəsɪn / (BrE)
to accept punishment or the unpleasant results of one's actions without complaint
accept consequences of one's actions

Examples

After the mistake was discovered, he had no choice but to take his medicine and apologize.
She knew her actions were wrong, so she decided to take her medicine without protest.
Politicians who break the law must be willing to take their medicine just like anyone else.

Synset Examples

He had to take his medicine and accept the outcome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to face the music - accept unpleasant consequences or criticism

Etymology

From the literal idea of taking medicine as a necessary remedy, even if unpleasant.
