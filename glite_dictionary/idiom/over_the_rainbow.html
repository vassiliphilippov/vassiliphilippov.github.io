over the rainbow

1. X

/ ˈoʊvər ðə ˈreɪnˌboʊ / (AmE), / ˈəʊvə ðə ˈreɪnbəʊ / (BrE)
used to refer to a place or state of perfect happiness and hope, often imagined as being far away or unattainable
place/state of happiness or hope

Examples

As a child, she always imagined a life over the rainbow, free from worries and fears.
After all the struggles, he finally felt he had arrived over the rainbow.
Many people search for a happiness that seems to exist only over the rainbow.

Synset Examples

She dreams of a home somewhere over the rainbow.
I believe happiness is waiting somewhere over the rainbow.
They hope to find peace somewhere over the rainbow.

Etymology

Popularized by the 1939 song 'Over the Rainbow' from The Wizard of Oz.
