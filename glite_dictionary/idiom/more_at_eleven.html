more at eleven

1. X

/ mɔːr æt ɪˈlɛvən / (AmE), / mɔːr æt ɪˈlɛvən / (BrE)
Used to indicate that further details or information will be provided later, originally from broadcast news teases.
additional information to follow (orig. TV news)
american informal

Examples

There’s been a major development in the case—more at eleven.
She claimed she had met a celebrity, but wouldn’t tell us who—more at eleven, apparently.
Rumors are swirling about the company’s new project; more at eleven, as they say.

Synset Examples

The story will have more at eleven, they promised.

Etymology

Originated from U.S. television news broadcasts, where anchors would tease upcoming stories with 'more at eleven' (11 PM being a common newscast time).
