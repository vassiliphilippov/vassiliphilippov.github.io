to make a fuss

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə fʌs / (AmE), / meɪk ə fʌs / (BrE)
to become angry or upset about something, often unnecessarily, and show this through noisy or attention-seeking behavior
become visibly upset or agitated

Examples

The child started making a fuss in the supermarket when he couldn't have candy.
Please stop making a fuss over such a small mistake.
He was making a fuss about the food being a little cold.

Synset Examples

She quietly accepted the award without making a fuss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make a scene - to behave in a dramatic or emotional way in public
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something; to disregard
