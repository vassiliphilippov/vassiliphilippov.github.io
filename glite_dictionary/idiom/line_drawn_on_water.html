line drawn on water

1. NOUN

/ laɪn drɔn ɑn ˈwɔtər / (AmE), / laɪn drɔːn ɒn ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
An idiom meaning something insubstantial, unstable, or unreliable; a thing that cannot last or be depended upon.
insubstantial or unreliable thing (idiom)

Examples

The agreement was nothing more than a line drawn on water, impossible to enforce.
To trust his promises was like relying on a line drawn on water.
Their temporary alliance proved to be a line drawn on water, quickly falling apart.

Synset Examples

The agreement felt like a line drawn on water, lacking substance.
Their promises were like a line drawn on water, easily erased.
Trust issues made their relationship feel like a line drawn on water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: castle in the air - a hope or plan that is unlikely or impossible to achieve
  • Synonym: pie in the sky - a promise of wonderful things in the future that are unlikely to be realized
  • Antonym: rock solid - very stable or reliable
