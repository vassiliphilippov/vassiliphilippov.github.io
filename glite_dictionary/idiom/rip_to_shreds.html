to rip to shreds

1. VERB

/ rɪp tə ʃrɛdz / (AmE), / rɪp tə ʃrɛdz / (BrE)
to criticize someone or something very harshly, or to completely destroy something, especially in a figurative sense
criticize harshly or destroy completely (figurative)

Examples

The critics ripped to shreds the new play in their reviews.
She felt as though her proposal had been ripped to shreds during the meeting.
Online commenters frequently rip to shreds movies they dislike.

Synset Examples

He didn't expect the audience to rip his performance to shreds.
The harsh review will rip her work to shreds.
Critics will rip the movie to shreds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tear to pieces - to criticize or attack very harshly
  • Antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration
