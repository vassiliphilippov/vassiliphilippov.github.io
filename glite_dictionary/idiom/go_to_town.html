to go to town

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ tə taʊn / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə taʊn / (BrE)
to do something with a lot of enthusiasm, energy, or thoroughness, often producing impressive or excessive results
do something energetically or thoroughly

Examples

Sarah really went to town on preparing dinner for the guests.
The marketing team went to town with their new advertising campaign.
After receiving their bonus, the couple went to town on shopping for new furniture.

Synset Examples

She really went to town on decorating the house.
He went to town on his new car, adding every feature.
They went to town on the buffet at the wedding.
They really went to town on the renovation project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go all out - to make a great effort; do something wholeheartedly
  • Antonym: to hold back - to restrain oneself; not do something fully
