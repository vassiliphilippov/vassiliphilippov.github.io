to smoke like a chimney

1. VERB

/ smoʊk laɪk ə ˈtʃɪmni / (AmE), / sməʊk laɪk ə ˈtʃɪmni / (BrE)
to smoke tobacco heavily and frequently
smoke heavily and often

Examples

My uncle used to smoke like a chimney, especially during stressful times at work.
If you smoke like a chimney, your health is likely to suffer.
She promised to quit, but she still smokes like a chimney whenever she goes out with friends.

Synset Examples

She smokes like a chimney, it's hard to keep up.
After dinner, he would smoke like a chimney.
My grandfather used to smoke like a chimney.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to chain-smoke - to smoke cigarettes one after another
