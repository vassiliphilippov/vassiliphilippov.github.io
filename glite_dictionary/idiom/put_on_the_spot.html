to put on the spot

1. VERB

/ pʊt ɑn ðə spɑt / (AmE), / pʊt ɒn ðə spɒt / (BrE)
to place someone in a position where they must answer or act uncomfortably or under pressure, especially suddenly or unexpectedly
put someone in an uncomfortable position

Examples

The teacher put on the spot several students by asking them to explain the theory on the spot.
He felt uncomfortable when the interviewer put on the spot with a personal question.
She doesn't like being put on the spot during team meetings.

Synset Examples

They put him on the spot with tough questions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embarrass - to make someone feel awkward or uncomfortable
  • Antonym: to relieve - to remove a burden or pressure from someone
