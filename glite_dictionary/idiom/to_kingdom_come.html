to kingdom come

1. ADV

/ tə ˈkɪŋdəm kʌm / (AmE), / tə ˈkɪŋdəm kʌm / (BrE)
to a place or state far away, or to complete destruction, often used for being blown, sent, or destroyed entirely.
completely destroyed or far away (idiom)

Examples

The explosion blew the old warehouse to kingdom come.
If you say that again, I'll send you to kingdom come.
He kicked the broken television to kingdom come out of frustration.

Synset Examples

The storm could blow the old barn to kingdom come.

Etymology

From the Christian concept of 'kingdom come' meaning the afterlife or the hereafter.
