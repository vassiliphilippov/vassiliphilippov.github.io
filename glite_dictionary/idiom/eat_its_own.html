to eat its own

1. VERB

/ it ɪts oʊn / (AmE), / iːt ɪts əʊn / (BrE)
(of a group or organization) to betray, attack, or harm its own members, often for self-preservation or internal conflict.
betray or harm one's own group

Examples

After months of disagreements, the committee started to eat its own and blame each other for the problems.
Political parties sometimes eat their own when internal conflicts overshadow external threats.
When the company faced financial trouble, it ate its own by laying off loyal employees.

Synset Examples

The scandal made the company eat its own.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn on - to attack or betray someone who was previously supported
