to make a fool of oneself

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə fuːl əv wʌnˌsɛlf / (AmE), / meɪk ə fuːl əv wʌnˌsɛlf / (BrE)
to behave in a way that makes one seem foolish or ridiculous, often resulting in embarrassment
embarrass oneself by acting foolishly

Examples

If you drink too much at the office party, you might make a fool of oneself.
She was so nervous during her speech that she almost made a fool of herself.
Try not to make a fool of oneself when meeting your partner's parents for the first time.

Synset Examples

He made a fool of himself, acting like a real dicknose.
He made a tomfool of himself at the party.
He really made an ass of himself at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embarrass oneself - to make oneself feel or appear embarrassed
  • Antonym: to save face - to avoid embarrassment or preserve dignity
