between the devil and the deep blue sea

1. X

/ bɪˈtwin ðə ˈdɛvəl ənd ðə dip blu si / (AmE), / bɪˈtwiːn ðə ˈdɛvəl ənd ðə diːp bluː siː / (BrE)
in a situation where one has to choose between two equally unpleasant or difficult options
faced with two bad choices

Examples

She found herself between the devil and the deep blue sea when both job offers meant moving away from family.
Negotiators were between the devil and the deep blue sea, with neither option proving acceptable.
He felt between the devil and the deep blue sea trying to pick between disappointing his boss or his team.

Synset Examples

Being between the devil and the deep blue sea made her decision tougher.
She was between the devil and the deep blue sea, unable to choose.
He felt like he was between the devil and the deep blue sea with that dilemma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: between a rock and a hard place - in a situation where both choices are unpleasant

Etymology

Originated in the 17th century; possibly from nautical imagery referring to a dangerous position on a ship, with 'devil' being a seam near the edge of the deck.
