answer on a postcard

1. X

/ ˈæn.sɚ ɑn ə ˈpoʊstˌkɑrd / (AmE), / ˈɑːn.sə ɒn ə ˈpəʊstˌkɑːd / (BrE)
(chiefly British) An invitation for a brief or informal response, traditionally using a postcard; often used humorously to invite suggestions or answers.
invite a brief/informal reply (often humorous)
british

Examples

If you can guess her secret ingredient, answer on a postcard and you might win a prize.
What should we call our new mascot? Suggestions, answer on a postcard.
For our next puzzle, answer on a postcard for your chance to be featured in the newsletter.

Synset Examples

Send your thoughts, answer on a postcard!
For a chance to win, answer on a postcard!
If you know the answer, send it in, answer on a postcard!

Etymology

Originally from British radio and television competitions where viewers were asked to send answers on postcards.
