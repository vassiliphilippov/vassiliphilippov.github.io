yes and amen

1. X

/ jɛs ənd eɪˈmɛn / (AmE), / jɛs ənd ɑːˈmɛn / (BrE)
used to express emphatic agreement or affirmation
strong agreement; emphatic affirmation

Examples

When the chairperson proposed the initiative, the board responded yes and amen without hesitation.
His enthusiastic support was evident in his yes and amen to every suggestion presented.
The congregation replied yes and amen to the pastor's call for unity.

Synset Examples

Her answer was a resounding yes and amen!
When he said he would help, it was a yes and amen.
The team agreed, yes and amen, to the new strategy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amen - an expression of agreement or affirmation, especially at the end of a prayer
  • Synonym: yes - an affirmative reply or agreement
  • Antonym: no - a negative answer or denial

Etymology

'Amen' from Hebrew 'āmēn', meaning 'so be it'; phrase commonly used in Christian contexts to express total agreement.
