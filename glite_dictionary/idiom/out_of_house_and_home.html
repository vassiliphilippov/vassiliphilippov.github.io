out of house and home

1. ADJ

/ aʊt əv haʊs ənd hoʊm / (AmE), / aʊt əv haʊs ənd həʊm / (BrE)
Left without a place to live, especially as a result of disaster, hardship, or misfortune; homeless.
left homeless; without shelter

Examples

After the floods, many families were out of house and home and needed emergency accommodation.
The war left thousands of people out of house and home, forced to find shelter wherever they could.
If he keeps spending recklessly, he'll end up out of house and home sooner than he thinks.

Synset Examples

The storm destroyed our garden and left us out of house and home.
