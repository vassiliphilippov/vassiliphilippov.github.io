to throw good money after bad

1. VERB

/ θroʊ ɡʊd ˈmʌni ˌæftər bæd / (AmE), / θrəʊ ɡʊd ˈmʌni ˌɑːftə bæd / (BrE)
to continue spending money on a failing venture, thereby increasing losses rather than recovering them
keep investing in a lost cause

Examples

Investing more money in that failed business would just be throwing good money after bad.
After years of repairs, they finally stopped throwing good money after bad and sold the car.
If the project keeps losing money, there's no point in throwing good money after bad.

Synset Examples

Don’t throw good money after bad investments.
He realized he was throwing good money after bad decisions.
Investing more is just throwing good money after bad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waste - use something carelessly or to no purpose
  • Antonym: cut your losses - stop an activity to limit loss or damage
