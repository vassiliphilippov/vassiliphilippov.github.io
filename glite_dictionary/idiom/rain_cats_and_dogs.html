to rain cats and dogs

1. VERB

/ reɪn kæts ənd dɔɡz / (AmE), / reɪn kæts ənd dɒɡz / (BrE)
to rain very heavily; to rain intensely
to rain very heavily

Examples

Last night it rained cats and dogs and the streets were flooded by morning.
If it rains cats and dogs tomorrow, the picnic will be canceled.
We got soaked on our way home because it suddenly started to rain cats and dogs.

Synset Examples

Put on your rain coat — it's pouring outside!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pour - to rain heavily
  • Degree Lower: drizzle - to rain lightly

Etymology

The origin is uncertain; used in English since the 17th century as a vivid metaphor for heavy rain.
