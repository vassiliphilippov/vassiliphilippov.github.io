to lead with one's chin

1. VERB

/ liːd wɪð wʌnz tʃɪn / (AmE), / liːd wɪð wʌnz tʃɪn / (BrE)
to behave in a way that makes oneself vulnerable to attack, criticism, or trouble; to take unnecessary risks that expose oneself to harm or disadvantage
expose oneself to criticism or danger

Examples

If you lead with your chin during salary negotiations, you might get a poor offer.
He tends to lead with his chin whenever he voices unpopular opinions at work.
She realized she was leading with her chin by volunteering for a difficult project without enough support.

Synset Examples

He always tends to lead with his chin in arguments.
In negotiations, don't lead with your chin or you'll lose.
If you keep leading with your chin, you'll get hurt.

Etymology

From boxing, where leading with the chin is a risky move that leaves a fighter open to being hit.
