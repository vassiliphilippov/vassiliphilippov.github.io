actions do the talking

1. X

/ ˈækʃənz du ðə ˈtɔkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈækʃənz duː ðə ˈtɔːkɪŋ / (BrE)
used to indicate that what someone does is more important or revealing than what they say; behavior shows true intentions or character.
let behavior show one's true intentions

Examples

She prefers to let her actions do the talking at work rather than making big promises.
When it comes to community service, his actions do the talking every weekend.
Instead of boasting, he allowed his actions do the talking on the basketball court.

Synset Examples

He always lets his actions do the talking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: actions speak louder than words - what someone actually does means more than what they say
