to don't care a button

1. VERB

/ doʊnt kɛr ə ˈbʌtn̩ / (AmE), / dəʊnt keə ə ˈbʌtn̩ / (BrE)
to not care at all; to be completely indifferent
to not care at all

Examples

He didn't care a button about the rumors spreading around town.
She doesn't care a button what people say about her fashion choices.
Frank doesn't care a button for money; happiness matters more to him.

Synset Examples

I don't care a button about his opinions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: not care a whit - to not care at all; be entirely indifferent
  • Synonym: not give a fig - to not care at all; be totally unconcerned

Etymology

The use of 'button' as a symbol for a thing of little value dates to the 19th century. The phrase extends older expressions referring to buttons as worthless objects.
