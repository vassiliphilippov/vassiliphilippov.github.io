to fret the gizzard

1. VERB

/ frɛt ðə ˈɡɪzərd / (AmE), / frɛt ðə ˈɡɪzəd / (BrE)
to worry excessively, especially about something beyond one's control
worry excessively; be anxious

Examples

You shouldn't fret the gizzard over minor mistakes at work.
He knew it was pointless to fret the gizzard about the weather.
There's no need to fret the gizzard just because plans changed.

Synset Examples

Don't fret the gizzard over things you can't control.
He tried not to fret the gizzard about the interview.
She tends to fret the gizzard whenever deadlines approach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worry - to feel or cause to feel anxious or upset about actual or potential problems
  • Synonym: fret - to be constantly or visibly worried or anxious
