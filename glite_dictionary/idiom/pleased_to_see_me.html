pleased to see me

1. X

/ plizd tə si mi / (AmE), / pliːzd tə siː miː / (BrE)
used humorously to suggest that someone's physical reaction (such as a bulge in the pocket) is due to excitement or sexual attraction upon seeing the speaker
humorous phrase implying attraction
informal

Examples

When she noticed his flustered reaction, she teased, 'Are you just pleased to see me?'
The comedian delivered the classic line, 'Is that a phone in your pocket or are you just pleased to see me?'
He laughed off the awkward moment by claiming he was just pleased to see me.

Synset Examples

He joked, 'Is that a gun in your pocket or are you just pleased to see me?'
