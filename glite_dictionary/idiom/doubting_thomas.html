doubting Thomas

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊtɪŋ ˈtɑməs / (AmE), / ˈdaʊtɪŋ ˈtɒməs / (BrE)
a person who refuses to believe something without direct, physical evidence; originally referring to the Apostle Thomas, who doubted Jesus's resurrection until he saw him.
person who insists on proof before believing, skeptic

Examples

He’s a real doubting Thomas and won’t believe the project will succeed until he sees the results himself.
Don’t be such a doubting Thomas; sometimes you have to trust people’s word.
The team grew frustrated with the doubting Thomas in the group who questioned every solution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skeptic - a person inclined to doubt or question accepted opinions
  • Antonym: believer - a person who accepts something as true without proof

Etymology

From the biblical account of the Apostle Thomas in the Gospel of John, who doubted Jesus's resurrection.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊtɪŋ ˈtɑməs / (AmE), / ˈdaʊtɪŋ ˈtɒməs / (BrE)
a person who refuses to believe something without direct evidence; a skeptic who demands proof before being convinced
skeptical person who demands proof

Examples

My dad is such a doubting Thomas; he won't believe the story until he sees the pictures.
Don't be a doubting Thomas—the results will speak for themselves.
Despite all the evidence, the committee chair remained a doubting Thomas about the new technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skeptic - a person inclined to doubt or question accepted opinions
  • Antonym: believer - a person who accepts something as true or real without proof

Etymology

Refers to the apostle Thomas in the New Testament, who doubted Jesus' resurrection until he saw physical evidence.
