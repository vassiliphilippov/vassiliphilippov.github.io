to put one's name in the hat

1. VERB

/ pʊt wʌnz neɪm ɪn ðə hæt / (AmE), / pʊt wʌnz neɪm ɪn ðə hæt / (BrE)
to formally enter oneself as a candidate or express one's intention to participate or be considered for an opportunity, selection, or competition
enter oneself for selection or competition

Examples

After years of hesitation, she finally put her name in the hat for the board position.
Several employees decided to put their names in the hat for the leadership program.
He wasn't sure at first, but he chose to put his name in the hat for the upcoming election.

Synset Examples

After much thought, he finally put his name in the hat for the committee.
I’m going to put my name in the hat for that scholarship.
She decided to put her name in the hat for the promotion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to throw one's hat in the ring - to announce one's candidacy or intention to compete

Etymology

From the traditional practice of drawing names or lots from a hat to make a selection.
