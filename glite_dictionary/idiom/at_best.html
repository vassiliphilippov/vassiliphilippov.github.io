at best

1. ADV

/ æt ˈbɛst / (AmE), / ət ˈbɛst / (BrE)
used to indicate the most favorable outcome or estimate in a situation, often implying that actual results are likely to be less good
indicating the most favorable outcome

Examples

At best, we can hope for a partial recovery this year.
This solution is temporary at best and may not last long.
His explanation was unclear at best, if not completely misleading.

Synset Examples

at best we'll lose only the money

Related Terms

  • Antonym: at most - indicating the largest possible amount or degree

Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ æt bɛst / (AmE), / æt bɛst / (BrE)
used to indicate the most favorable or optimistic assessment of a situation; only in the best or most hopeful scenario.
in the most favorable view

Examples

His explanation was unconvincing at best, and completely misleading at worst.
The weather will be uncomfortable at best during the hike this weekend.
Her chances of winning the competition are slim at best.

Synset Examples

His excuses were slimsy at best, lacking any real substance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: at worst - in the least favorable or most pessimistic assessment
