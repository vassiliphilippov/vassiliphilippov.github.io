none the wiser

1. ADJ

/ nʌn ðə ˈwaɪzɚ / (AmE), / nʌn ðə ˈwaɪzə / (BrE)
not having gained any additional knowledge or understanding, especially after something has been explained or revealed
lacking increased understanding

Examples

He explained the entire process, but I was none the wiser when he finished.
After three hours of research, she was still none the wiser about the cause of the error.
They told him their version of the story, yet he remained none the wiser.

Synset Examples

He explained everything, but I was none the wiser.
After the meeting, I left none the wiser about the project.
They kept their secrets well, leaving us none the wiser.
He explained it twice, but I'm still not any the wiser.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: no wiser - not having more knowledge or understanding

Etymology

From the comparative adjective 'wiser' meaning 'more wise', in use since the 19th century.
