in spades

1. ADV

/ ɪn speɪdz / (AmE), / ɪn speɪdz / (BrE)
to a very great degree; without doubt or question
to a great degree; without doubt

Examples

Her talent for painting shows in spades in her latest exhibition.
If you want excitement, this city offers it in spades.
Critics praised the novel for its wit and charm in spades.

Synset Examples

it was decidedly too expensive
she told him off in spades
by all odds they should win

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decidedly - certainly; without doubt
  • Synonym: absolutely - completely; totally
  • Antonym: barely - only just; by a small margin

Etymology

Originally a reference to the suit of spades (the highest in some card games), used metaphorically for emphasis.
