to take leave

1. VERB

/ teɪk liv / (AmE), / teɪk liːv / (BrE)
to formally depart or bid farewell, usually in a polite or ceremonious manner
to formally say goodbye

Examples

He politely took leave of his colleagues at the end of the dinner.
Before departing for his new job abroad, she took leave of her family and friends.
After the ceremony, the ambassador took leave of the assembled guests with a warm handshake.

Synset Examples

He politely took his leave after the meeting.
She took leave of her senses when she agreed to that plan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depart - to leave, especially in a formal way
  • Synonym: to say goodbye - to express farewell
