to turn out perfecto

1. VERB

/ tɝn aʊt pərˈfɛktoʊ / (AmE), / tɜːn aʊt pəˈfɛktəʊ / (BrE)
to end or develop in a way that is very successful or perfect
end or develop perfectly
informal

Examples

Despite the rain, the picnic turned out perfecto and everyone had a wonderful time.
The cake I baked for the wedding turned out perfecto—moist, delicious, and beautifully decorated.
We were nervous about the big presentation, but in the end, everything turned out perfecto.

Synset Examples

Everything turned out perfecto at the party!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turn out well - to end or develop satisfactorily or successfully
  • Antonym: turn out badly - to end or develop in an unsuccessful or undesirable way

Etymology

From English phrase 'turn out' + borrow from Spanish 'perfecto' (perfect).
