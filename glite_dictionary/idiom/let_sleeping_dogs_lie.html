to let sleeping dogs lie

1. VERB

/ lɛt ˈslipɪŋ dɔɡz laɪ / (AmE), / lɛt ˈsliːpɪŋ dɒɡz laɪ / (BrE)
to deliberately avoid restarting a conflict or stirring up trouble by leaving a situation as it is
avoid restarting trouble or conflict

Examples

It's sometimes wiser to let sleeping dogs lie than to revisit past conflicts.
After the argument, they agreed to let sleeping dogs lie and move on.
You should let sleeping dogs lie rather than bring up her old mistakes.

Synset Examples

It's best to let sleeping dogs lie and avoid conflict.
She decided to let sleeping dogs lie and not mention the issue.
He chose to let sleeping dogs lie rather than revisit old arguments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave well enough alone - to avoid changing or interfering with something that is working satisfactorily

Etymology

From the proverb “let sleeping dogs lie,” recorded in English since at least the 14th century; uses the image of dogs not to be disturbed lest they cause trouble.
