to cut to the quick

1. VERB

/ kʌt tə ðə kwɪk / (AmE), / kʌt tʊ ðə kwɪk / (BrE)
to deeply hurt or offend someone emotionally; to wound someone's feelings sharply
deeply hurt or offend emotionally

Examples

She was cut to the quick by her friend's harsh remarks.
His unfair accusation cut to the quick, leaving him speechless.
Hearing the criticism from her mentor cut to the quick more than she expected.

Synset Examples

She felt cut to the quick by his comments.
The criticism really cut to the quick this time.
His harsh words cut to the quick and left her hurt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wound (someone's) feelings - to hurt someone emotionally
  • Synonym: to offend - to cause someone to feel hurt or upset

Etymology

'Quick' here refers to the living tissue under the skin or nail, from Old English 'cwic' meaning 'alive.'
