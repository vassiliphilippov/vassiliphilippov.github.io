to seize the opportunity

1. VERB

/ siz ði ˌɑːpərˈtunəti / (AmE), / siːz ði ˌɒpəˈtjuːnəti / (BrE)
to take quick action to make use of or benefit from a chance when it arises
take advantage of a chance

Examples

You have to seize the opportunity when it comes your way, or you might regret it later.
When tickets became available, she immediately seized the opportunity to see her favorite band live.
It's important to seize the opportunity to learn new skills while you're still in school.

Synset Examples

She was quick to seize the opportunity for a promotion.
Don't hesitate; seize the opportunity while it's available.
He encouraged her to seize the opportunity to study abroad.
Let's seize the opportunity to explore the new city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grab the chance - to take the opportunity to do something
  • Antonym: miss the boat - to fail to take advantage of an opportunity
