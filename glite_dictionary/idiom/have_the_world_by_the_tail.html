to have the world by the tail

1. VERB

/ hæv ðə wɜːrld baɪ ðə teɪl / (AmE), / hæv ðə wɜːld baɪ ðə teɪl / (BrE)
to be in a very successful or fortunate position; to feel in control of circumstances
be very successful or in control

Examples

After winning the lottery, she truly had the world by the tail.
With his new degree and job offer, he feels like he has the world by the tail.
They thought they had the world by the tail until unforeseen problems arose.

Synset Examples

After her recent success, she has the world by the tail.
With this promotion, I feel like I have the world by the tail.
He thinks he has the world by the tail now.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be on top of the world - to feel extremely happy or successful
  • Antonym: down and out - lacking money or prospects; destitute
