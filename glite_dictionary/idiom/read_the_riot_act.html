to read the riot act

1. VERB

/ ɹid ðə ˈraɪət ˌækt / (AmE), / ɹiːd ðə ˈraɪət ˌækt / (BrE)
to reprimand or warn someone very sternly, especially with the threat of consequences if behavior is not corrected
warn or scold sternly

Examples

After seeing the mess in the living room, she read the riot act to her children.
The manager read the riot act to the team for missing the deadline.
If you keep coming in late, the boss will read the riot act to you.

Synset Examples

The coach read the players the riot act after their poor performance.
The teacher read the students the riot act about cheating.
She read her son the riot act for coming home late.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scold - to reprimand or criticize angrily
  • Synonym: reprimand - to express sharp disapproval or criticism

Etymology

Refers to the historical Riot Act (1715) in Britain, read aloud before dispersing an unruly crowd.
