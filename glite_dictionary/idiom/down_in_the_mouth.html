down in the mouth

1. ADJ

/ daʊn ɪn ðə maʊθ / (AmE), / daʊn ɪn ðə maʊθ / (BrE)
feeling sad, dejected, or discouraged; unhappy
unhappy or dejected

Examples

Whenever she receives bad news, she looks down in the mouth for days.
He tried to encourage his friend, who had been down in the mouth since losing his job.
Don't be down in the mouth; things will surely get better soon.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depressed - feeling very sad and without hope
  • Antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

Possibly from the image of a person's mouth turned downwards in sadness, first recorded in the 18th century.

2. ADJ

/ daʊn ɪn ðə maʊθ / (AmE), / daʊn ɪn ðə maʊθ / (BrE)
Feeling sad, discouraged, or depressed.
feeling sad or depressed (idiom)

Examples

Whenever he loses a game, he gets down in the mouth for the rest of the day.
She looked down in the mouth after hearing the disappointing news.
Don't be down in the mouth; things will get better soon.

Synset Examples

She was down in the mouth about her recent failures.
No need to be down in the mouth; cheer up!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depressed - feeling very unhappy or without hope
  • Antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

Possibly from the idea of a drooping mouth as a sign of sadness; recorded since the 1600s.
