to pour one's heart out

1. VERB

/ pɔr wʌnz hɑrt aʊt / (AmE), / pɔː wʌnz hɑːt aʊt / (BrE)
to express one's deepest feelings or emotions openly and honestly, often in a moment of vulnerability
express deep feelings openly

Examples

After failing the exam, she needed to pour her heart out to a close friend.
He would occasionally pour his heart out in his diary when he felt overwhelmed.
Sometimes it's easier to pour your heart out to a stranger than to someone you know.

Synset Examples

He poured his heart out in that letter.
After the breakup, he needed to pour his heart out to someone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to open up - to talk freely about one's feelings or private thoughts
  • Antonym: to bottle up - to keep one's emotions or feelings inside without expressing them

2. VERB

/ pɔr wʌnz hɑrt aʊt / (AmE), / pɔː wʌnz hɑːt aʊt / (BrE)
to talk about one's deepest feelings or troubles in an open and emotional way
express one's deepest emotions openly

Examples

After the accident, he finally poured his heart out to his closest friend.
She poured her heart out in the letter, explaining everything she felt.
Sometimes you just need to pour your heart out to someone you trust.

Synset Examples

She poured her heart into her art.
She poured her heart out in her journal every night.
She poured her heart out during the counseling session.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to open up - to talk more freely about one's feelings
  • Antonym: to bottle up - to keep one's feelings or emotions hidden
