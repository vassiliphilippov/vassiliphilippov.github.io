black sheep

1. NOUN

/ blæk ʃip / (AmE), / blæk ʃiːp / (BrE)
a member of a group, especially a family, who is regarded as a disgrace or an outcast because of their behavior or beliefs
outcast or disgrace in a group or family

Examples

He has always been the black sheep of his family, choosing an unconventional career path.
Growing up, she felt like the black sheep among her siblings because of her different interests.
In their small town, being a black sheep meant going against tradition and standing out.

Synset Examples

In a field of black sheep, he felt like the white sheep.
She always felt like the black sheep among her peers.
He's considered the black sheep of the family.
The black sheep often has the most interesting stories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outcast - a person who is rejected by a group or society
  • Synonym: scapegoat - a person blamed for the wrongdoings or faults of others

Etymology

From the rare occurrence and negative value of black-fleeced sheep among white ones; metaphorical use since the 18th century.
