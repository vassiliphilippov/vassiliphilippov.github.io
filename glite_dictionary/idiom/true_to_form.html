true to form

1. ADJ

/ truː tuː fɔrm / (AmE), / truː tə fɔːm / (BrE)
behaving or happening in the way that is usual or expected, based on past experience
behaving as expected; typical pattern

Examples

True to form, he forgot about the meeting again.
The movie was, true to form, full of unexpected twists and turns.
She complained about the weather, true to form.

Synset Examples

He arrived late, true to form.
The weather turned rainy, true to form.
She always forgets her keys, true to form.
