onwards and upwards

1. ADV

/ ˈɑnwərdz ənd ˈʌpwərdz / (AmE), / ˈɒnwədz ənd ˈʌpwədz / (BrE)
used to express progress towards a better or more successful situation after overcoming difficulties.
continuing to progress or improve

Examples

After the successful launch, the company moved onwards and upwards with new projects.
She believes that, despite past failures, it's onwards and upwards from here.
With each award, his career has gone onwards and upwards.

Synset Examples

With each success, it's onwards and upwards for the team.
His motto was always onwards and upwards, no matter the challenges.
After the setback, she was determined to go onwards and upwards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forward - toward a position ahead or improvement
  • Antonym: regress - to return to a worse or less developed state
