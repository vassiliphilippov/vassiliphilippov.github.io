to hit the hay

1. VERB

/ hɪt ðə heɪ / (AmE), / hɪt ðə heɪ / (BrE)
to go to bed or prepare to sleep, especially after a long day
go to bed, prepare for sleep
informal

Examples

After a long day at work, I'm ready to hit the hay early tonight.
She yawned and told everyone she was going to hit the hay.
If you keep studying this late, you really should hit the hay soon!

Synset Examples

I usually turn in at midnight
He goes to bed at the crack of dawn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go to bed - to get into bed with the intention of sleeping
  • Synonym: to turn in - to go to bed, often at a particular time

Etymology

Originally from the practice of sleeping on hay-filled mattresses.
