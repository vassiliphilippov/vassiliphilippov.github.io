if you say so

1. X

/ ɪf juː seɪ soʊ / (AmE), / ɪf juː seɪ səʊ / (BrE)
used to indicate acceptance of what someone has said, sometimes with doubt, reluctance, or lack of enthusiasm
acceptance of someone's statement, often with doubt

Examples

"I finished all my work on time." "If you say so, I believe you."
She shrugged and replied, "If you say so," not fully convinced by his explanation.
He claimed he could do it alone, and I just said, "If you say so."

Synset Examples

It’s true, I guess; if you say so.
