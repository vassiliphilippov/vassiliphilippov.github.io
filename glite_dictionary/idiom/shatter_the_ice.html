to shatter the ice

1. VERB

/ ˈʃæt̬.ɚ ðə aɪs / (AmE), / ˈʃæt.ə ðɪ aɪs / (BrE)
to do or say something that relieves initial social awkwardness or tension in a group or between people
relieve social awkwardness or tension

Examples

A funny joke at the start of the meeting helped to shatter the ice among the team members.
Her warm greeting was enough to shatter the ice and get everyone talking.
He tried using humor to shatter the ice on the first day of class.

Synset Examples

A funny joke helped to shatter the ice at the party.
Her smile was enough to shatter the ice between them.
He told a story to shatter the ice during dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break the ice - to initiate conversation in a socially awkward situation
  • Antonym: freeze up - to become unable to speak or act due to nervousness

Etymology

Variant of the idiom 'break the ice,' which traces back to nautical and social customs.
