to paint the town red

1. VERB

/ peɪnt ðə taʊn rɛd / (AmE), / peɪnt ðə taʊn rɛd / (BrE)
to go out and celebrate in a lively and exuberant way, usually involving visiting bars, restaurants, or clubs
celebrate wildly; go out partying
informal

Examples

After submitting her thesis, Sarah decided to paint the town red with her friends.
If we win the championship, we'll paint the town red tonight!
They all agreed to paint the town red to celebrate their promotions.

Synset Examples

They went out to paint the town red after the exam.
After winning the game, they were ready to paint the town red.
Let's paint the town red and celebrate your birthday!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: party - to enjoy oneself at a gathering with others
  • Synonym: to celebrate - to acknowledge an event with festivities

Etymology

Possibly originating from 19th-century English slang, referring to rowdy celebrations likened to painting the town with red color.
