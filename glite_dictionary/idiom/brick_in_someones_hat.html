brick in someone's hat

1. X

/ brɪk ɪn səmˈwʌnz hæt / (AmE), / brɪk ɪn səmˈwʌnz hæt / (BrE)
used to describe someone who is foolish, confused, or out of touch with reality
to be foolish or confused

Examples

You must have a brick in your hat to believe such a wild story.
After that mistake, everyone thought he had a brick in his hat.
Suggesting we drive across the country in one day—do you have a brick in your hat?

Synset Examples

He must have a brick in his hat to think that.
You really must have a brick in your hat today!
When she suggested that, I thought she had a brick in her hat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: out of one's mind - to be crazy or very foolish
  • Synonym: crazy - mentally deranged; extremely foolish
  • Antonym: sensible - showing good sense or judgement
