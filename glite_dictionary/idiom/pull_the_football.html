to pull the football

1. VERB

/ pʊl ðə ˈfʊtbɔl / (AmE), / pʊl ðə ˈfʊtbɔːl / (BrE)
to repeatedly deceive or disappoint someone by withdrawing promised support or help at the last moment
deceive by withdrawing support repeatedly

Examples

After being promised a raise, I felt my boss would pull the football again and not deliver.
Every year management pulls the football with our bonus, saying it will come but never does.
She warned me not to trust him because he tends to pull the football when it matters most.

Synset Examples

I feel like she’s going to pull the football again.
He always pulls the football before I can kick it.
Every time I tried, she pulled the football away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to let down - to fail someone or disappoint their expectations

Etymology

From the recurring Peanuts comic strip gag where Lucy pulls the football away as Charlie Brown tries to kick it.
