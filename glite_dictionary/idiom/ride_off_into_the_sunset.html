to ride off into the sunset

1. VERB

/ raɪd ˈɔf ˌɪntu ðə ˈsʌnsɛt / (AmE), / raɪd ˈɒf ˌɪntuː ðə ˈsʌnsɛt / (BrE)
to leave a situation in a happy or satisfying way, often at the end of an event or chapter in life
leave happily or satisfyingly

Examples

After finishing the project, she hoped to ride off into the sunset with a sense of accomplishment.
The movie ends with the hero riding off into the sunset, leaving the audience with hope.
Once they solved the final mystery, the team rode off into the sunset to start a new adventure.

Synset Examples

He dreams of riding off into the sunset after retirement.
After the big victory, they rode off into the sunset.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: make a graceful exit - to leave a situation or place smoothly and without causing trouble

Etymology

From imagery in Western films, where heroes depart on horseback at sunset.
