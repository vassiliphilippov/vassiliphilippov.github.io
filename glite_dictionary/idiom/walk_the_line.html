to walk the line

1. VERB

/ wɑk ðə laɪn / (AmE), / wɔːk ðə laɪn / (BrE)
to maintain a careful balance or avoid going to extremes between conflicting values, demands, or expectations
maintain balance between conflicting values

Examples

As a manager, she often has to walk the line between being strict and being supportive.
Politicians must walk the line to appeal to both conservative and liberal voters.
It's hard to walk the line between staying informed and becoming overwhelmed by the news.

Synset Examples

To succeed, you must learn to walk the line carefully.
He had to walk the line between honesty and kindness.
She’s learned to walk the line between work and personal life.
