gravy train

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪvi treɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪvi treɪn / (BrE)
A situation in which someone can earn a lot of money with little effort or difficulty.
easy profit with little effort

Examples

Many people see politics as a gravy train for those already in power.
He stayed at the company for years, riding the gravy train and doing very little work.
Some executives resigned when the gravy train of bonuses finally ended.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goldmine - something that produces wealth or profit easily
  • Synonym: sinecure - a position requiring little or no work but giving financial benefit
  • Antonym: rat race - a way of life in which people compete fiercely for wealth or power

Etymology

Originated in early 20th-century American English, possibly referring to 'gravy' as easy profit and 'train' as a series or flow.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪvi treɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪvi treɪn / (BrE)
a situation that provides easy money or benefits with little effort or risk
situation with easy money or benefits

Examples

After his invention took off, he found himself riding the gravy train for years.
Many politicians have been accused of hopping onto the gravy train at taxpayers' expense.
When the contract was renewed, the whole department jumped aboard the gravy train.

Synset Examples

Her new job feels like a gravy train with all the bonuses.
Investing in that startup turned into a gravy train for early backers.
After getting that promotion, he's on a gravy train now.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: easy street - a situation of comfort and financial security

Etymology

Originated in early 20th-century American English, combining 'gravy' (slang for easy money) and 'train' (suggesting a continuous supply).
