long row to hoe

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ ɹoʊ tə hoʊ / (AmE), / lɒŋ rəʊ tə həʊ / (BrE)
a difficult or challenging task or undertaking, often one that requires persistent effort over time
difficult or challenging task

Examples

Convincing the board to approve her proposal was a long row to hoe.
After his injury, recovering in time for the race proved to be a long row to hoe.
Building a successful business from scratch is often a long row to hoe.

Synset Examples

She realized that raising three kids alone was a long row to hoe.
After the layoffs, the remaining team had a long row to hoe.
Getting this project done is a long row to hoe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uphill battle - a task that is very difficult to achieve
  • Antonym: piece of cake - something very easy to do

Etymology

Alludes to the arduous farm work of hoeing a long row, requiring persistence.
