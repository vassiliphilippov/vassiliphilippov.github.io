to kill the goose that lays the golden eggs

1. VERB

/ kɪl ðə ɡus ðət leɪz ðə ˈɡoʊldən ɛɡz / (AmE), / kɪl ðə ɡuːs ðət leɪz ðə ˈɡəʊldən ɛɡz / (BrE)
to ruin or destroy a reliable source of ongoing income or benefit through foolish or greedy actions
destroy a reliable source of benefit

Examples

If you keep raising taxes on small businesses, you might kill the goose that lays the golden eggs.
Their decision to overfish the lake could kill the goose that lays the golden eggs.
Don't kill the goose that lays the golden eggs by pushing your workers too hard.

Synset Examples

Don't kill the goose that lays the golden eggs with greed.
Investing wisely prevents killing the goose that lays the golden eggs.
They risked killing the goose that lays the golden eggs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shoot oneself in the foot - to harm one's own interests through a foolish action

Etymology

From Aesop's fable 'The Goose That Laid the Golden Eggs', in which greed leads to the loss of ongoing wealth.
