god that failed

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑd ðæt feɪld / (AmE), / ɡɒd ðət feɪld / (BrE)
a person who once had great ambitions or influence but ultimately did not succeed or live up to expectations
person of high ambition who failed

Examples

Many saw the politician as a god that failed after his dramatic fall from power.
The documentary portrays the tech visionary as a god that failed in the fast-paced industry.
She was once hailed as a prodigy, but ended up remembered as a god that failed.

Synset Examples

In the end, he realized his ambitions made him a god that failed.

Etymology

Popularized by the title of the 1949 book 'The God That Failed', referring to disillusionment with an ideal or leader.
