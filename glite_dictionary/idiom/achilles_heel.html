Achilles' heel

1. NOUN

/ əˈkɪliz hil / (AmE), / əˈkɪliːz hiːl / (BrE)
a small but crucial weakness in someone or something that can lead to failure or defeat
critical weakness

Examples

Procrastination has always been his Achilles' heel when it comes to meeting project deadlines.
The team's impressive offense is let down by a defense that remains their Achilles' heel.
While her academic performance is excellent, mathematics is her Achilles' heel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weakness - a point where someone or something is vulnerable
  • Antonym: strength - a strong or advantageous quality

Etymology

From the Greek myth of Achilles, whose only vulnerable spot was his heel.
