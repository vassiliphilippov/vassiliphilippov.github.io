to burn rubber

1. VERB

/ bɝn ˈrʌbɚ / (AmE), / bɜːn ˈrʌbə / (BrE)
to accelerate a vehicle very quickly, causing the tires to skid or leave tire marks on the road
accelerate quickly in a vehicle
informal

Examples

The convertible can burn rubber from zero to sixty in just five seconds.
Teenagers sometimes burn rubber in the school parking lot to impress their friends.
He loves to burn rubber whenever the road is empty.

Synset Examples

He loves to burn rubber on the racetrack.
She stepped on the gas to burn rubber away from the light.
The sports car can burn rubber in seconds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speed off - to drive away very quickly
