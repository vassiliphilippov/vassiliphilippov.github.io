how goes the enemy

1. X

/ haʊ ɡoʊz ði ˈɛnəmi / (AmE), / haʊ ɡəʊz ði ˈɛnəmi / (BrE)
used to ask about the progress of a situation, sometimes humorously referring to the passage of time
set phrase asking about progress or time
british informal

Examples

When she entered the room, she smiled and asked, "How goes the enemy?"
He checked his watch and muttered, "How goes the enemy, I wonder?"
After a long meeting, someone broke the silence with, "How goes the enemy, anyone know?"

Synset Examples

I wondered how goes the enemy in their negotiations.
He always asks, 'So, how goes the enemy these days?'

Etymology

Originally British slang; 'enemy' as a play on 'enemy time' from rhyming slang for 'time.'
