dead of winter

1. NOUN

/ dɛd əv ˈwɪn.tɚ / (AmE), / dɛd əv ˈwɪn.tə / (BrE)
the coldest and most severe period of winter
coldest part of winter

Examples

Even in the dead of winter, the streets were busy with people hurrying to work.
They moved to Florida to escape the dead of winter up north.
Hot soup is especially comforting during the dead of winter.

Synset Examples

The cabin was cozy, even in the dead of winter.
They planned their trip for the dead of winter to enjoy the snow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: midwinter - the middle of the winter season

Etymology

From 'dead' meaning the middle or most intense part (as in 'dead of night') combined with 'winter.'
