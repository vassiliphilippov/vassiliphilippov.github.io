in one's cups

1. ADJ

/ ɪn wʌnz kʌps / (AmE), / ɪn wʌnz kʌps / (BrE)
under the influence of alcohol; intoxicated
intoxicated; drunk

Examples

It's best not to take him seriously when he's in his cups.
She confessed her feelings late one night when she was in her cups.
Many regrettable words have been spoken while people were in their cups.

Synset Examples

She tends to speak her mind when in her cups.
He was often found in his cups after work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drunk - intoxicated by alcohol
  • Antonym: sober - not affected by alcohol; not drunk

Etymology

From the literal meaning of having drunk much from one's drinking cup(s), attested since the 17th century.

2. ADJ

/ ɪn wʌnz kʌps / (AmE), / ɪn wʌnz kʌps / (BrE)
Intoxicated with alcohol; drunk.
drunk, intoxicated

Examples

After the wedding feast, several guests were clearly in their cups and singing loudly.
He tends to let secrets slip when in his cups.
Though usually reserved, she became outspoken in her cups at the party.

Synset Examples

They were all in their cups by the end of the night.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sober - not affected by alcohol; not drunk

Etymology

Used since the 17th century, referring to drinking alcoholic beverages (cups).
