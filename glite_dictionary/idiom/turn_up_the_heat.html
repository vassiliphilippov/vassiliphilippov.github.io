to turn up the heat

1. VERB

/ tɝn ʌp ðə hit / (AmE), / tɜːn ʌp ðə hiːt / (BrE)
to increase pressure, intensity, or urgency in a situation
increase pressure or intensity

Examples

The coach decided to turn up the heat during practice to motivate the team.
They turned up the heat on their competitors with an aggressive marketing campaign.
As the deadline approached, she knew she had to turn up the heat on her work.

Synset Examples

He turned up the heat to make the house warmer.
She decided to turn up the heat during the negotiations.
It's time to turn up the heat on this project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ratchet up - to increase something in amount or intensity
  • Antonym: to ease off - to make less intense or severe
