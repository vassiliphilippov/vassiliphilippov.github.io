to take a crack at

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə kræk æt / (AmE), / teɪk ə kræk ət / (BrE)
to attempt or try to do something, often for the first time or to see if one can succeed
attempt or try (informal idiom)
informal american

Examples

If you don't mind, I'd like to take a crack at fixing the computer myself.
She decided to take a crack at writing a novel during the summer.
Why not take a crack at the crossword if you're feeling bored?

Synset Examples

If you want, you can take a crack at solving the puzzle.
I decided to take a crack at painting.
He wanted to take a crack at the cooking competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: try - to make an attempt or effort
  • Synonym: give it a shot - to attempt something (informal idiom)
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop trying or abandon an attempt

Etymology

Informal American English; 'crack' meaning 'attempt' dates from 19th century slang.
