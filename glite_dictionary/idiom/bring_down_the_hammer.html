to bring down the hammer

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ daʊn ðə ˈhæmɚ / (AmE), / brɪŋ daʊn ðə ˈhamə / (BrE)
to take firm and decisive action, especially by enforcing strict punishment or measures
take strict, decisive action

Examples

The principal warned that she would bring down the hammer on cheating students.
The government decided to bring down the hammer on illegal activities.
After repeated violations, the league finally brought down the hammer on the team.

Synset Examples

He promised to bring down the hammer on any wrongdoing.
Management decided to bring down the hammer on absenteeism.
The judge will bring down the hammer tomorrow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to crack down - to take severe measures against wrongdoing
  • Antonym: to let off the hook - to allow someone to avoid punishment or responsibility
