to fill one's boots

1. VERB

/ fɪl wʌnz buːts / (AmE), / fɪl wʌnz buːts / (BrE)
to indulge oneself excessively or enjoy something to the fullest
indulge oneself excessively; enjoy fully

Examples

After the promotion, he decided to fill his boots with the luxuries he'd always dreamed of.
Whenever there's free food at a party, people tend to fill their boots.
She really filled her boots with excitement after hearing the good news.

Synset Examples

After winning the lottery, she really filled her boots with luxury.
He filled his boots with excitement after landing the new job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enjoy oneself - to take pleasure in an activity or experience
  • Synonym: to overindulge - to indulge oneself to excess

2. VERB

/ fɪl wʌnz buːts / (AmE), / fɪl wʌnz buːts / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly British) To take full advantage of an opportunity or to indulge oneself as much as desired.
take full advantage or indulge oneself
informal british

Examples

Go ahead at the buffet and fill your boots—there's plenty of food for everyone.
When given unlimited overtime, the workers really filled their boots with extra pay.
Whenever there's free pizza in the office, he makes sure to fill his boots.

Synset Examples

His promotion allowed him to fill his boots with a nicer car.
