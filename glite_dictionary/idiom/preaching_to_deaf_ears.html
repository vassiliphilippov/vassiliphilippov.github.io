preaching to deaf ears

1. X

/ ˈpritʃɪŋ tə dɛf ɪrz / (AmE), / ˈpriːtʃɪŋ tə dɛf ɪəz / (BrE)
trying to persuade people who are unwilling or unable to listen or respond
trying to convince unwilling listeners

Examples

Whenever she mentioned the risks, it was like preaching to deaf ears—no one wanted to listen.
His concerns about safety were preaching to deaf ears at the meeting.
She realized that bringing up environmental issues to the group was just preaching to deaf ears.

Synset Examples

Trying to change their minds was like preaching to deaf ears.
He felt he was preaching to deaf ears during the discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: falling on deaf ears - not being listened to or ignored
