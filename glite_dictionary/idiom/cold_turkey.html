cold turkey

1. NOUN

/ koʊld ˈtɝki / (AmE), / kəʊld ˈtɜːki / (BrE)
The abrupt and complete cessation of an addictive substance, habit, or activity, often without preparation or gradual reduction.
sudden, complete withdrawal from a dependency

Examples

After years of drinking coffee, she decided to quit cold turkey.
He stopped taking painkillers cold turkey and faced severe withdrawal symptoms.
Some people find it easier to quit smoking gradually rather than going cold turkey.

Synset Examples

he quit smoking cold turkey
she quit her job cold turkey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: withdrawal - the process of ceasing to use an addictive substance

2. NOUN

/ koʊld ˈtɝr.ki / (AmE), / kəʊld ˈtɜː.ki / (BrE)
the abrupt and complete cessation of a habit, especially the use of drugs, alcohol, or cigarettes, often without gradual reduction or support
quitting a habit abruptly

Examples

After years of smoking, she decided to quit cold turkey and never looked back.
Going cold turkey can cause withdrawal symptoms for people with serious addictions.
He gave up caffeine cold turkey, which made the first week very difficult.

Synset Examples

He quit smoking cold turkey last year.
Going cold turkey was tough, but he felt better.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gradually - to do something slowly over time (antonym for abrupt quitting)

Etymology

Possibly from American slang (early 20th century), related to the plainness and shock associated with cold turkey as food.
