elephant in a china shop

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛlɪfənt ɪn ə ˈtʃaɪnə ʃɑp / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪfənt ɪn ə ˈtʃaɪnə ʃɒp / (BrE)
a person who is very clumsy or insensitive in a situation that requires care and tact
clumsy or insensitive person in delicate situations

Examples

When asked to handle the negotiation, he acted like an elephant in a china shop and upset everyone involved.
As the new intern, I felt like an elephant in a china shop on my first day at the art gallery.
Her comments at the dinner party were like an elephant in a china shop, leaving everyone uncomfortable.

Synset Examples

He felt like an elephant in a china shop during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bull in a china shop - a person who is clumsy, especially in delicate situations
