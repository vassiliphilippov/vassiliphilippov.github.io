all sixes and nines

1. ADJ

/ ɔl sɪksɪz ənd naɪnz / (AmE), / ɔːl sɪksɪz ənd naɪnz / (BrE)
in a state of confusion or disorder; disorganized or muddled
in confusion or disorder

Examples

Since the new manager arrived, the office has been all sixes and nines.
We were all sixes and nines trying to figure out the new schedule.
After the surprise announcement, the team was left all sixes and nines.

Synset Examples

After the storm, everything was all sixes and nines.
Her plans for the event went all sixes and nines.
His explanation left us all sixes and nines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in a muddle - in a confused or disorganized state
  • Synonym: disorganized - lacking order or organization

Etymology

Refers to the visual similarity and potential for confusion between the numbers six and nine; idiom in use since the 18th century.
