to give enough rope

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ɪˈnʌf roʊp / (AmE), / ɡɪv ɪˈnʌf rəʊp / (BrE)
to allow someone enough freedom or opportunities so that they may either demonstrate their ability or bring about their own downfall through their actions
allow enough freedom for success or self-destruction

Examples

The boss decided to give enough rope and see if the new hire could handle the project alone.
Sometimes, it's wise to give enough rope so people reveal their true intentions.
They chose to give enough rope rather than intervene, hoping mistakes would become clear.

Synset Examples

If you give him enough rope, he’ll hang himself.
The manager decided to give her enough rope to prove her skills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: give someone enough rope and they will hang themselves - if people are given enough freedom, they may bring about their own downfall

Etymology

From the proverb 'give a man enough rope and he'll hang himself', originating in the 17th century.
