to beat the bushes

1. VERB

/ bit ðə ˈbʊʃɪz / (AmE), / biːt ðə ˈbʊʃɪz / (BrE)
to search thoroughly and energetically for something or someone, often by exploring all possible sources or avenues.
search thoroughly for something or someone

Examples

After hours spent beating the bushes, the detective finally found a key witness.
We're really going to have to beat the bushes if we want to find the best candidate for the job.
He spent weeks beating the bushes for rare vintage records.

Synset Examples

He decided to beat the bushes for potential clients.
We need to beat the bushes for any information on the project.
They spent the afternoon beating the bushes for volunteers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search high and low - to look everywhere for something

Etymology

Probably derived from an old hunting practice of beating bushes to flush out game.
