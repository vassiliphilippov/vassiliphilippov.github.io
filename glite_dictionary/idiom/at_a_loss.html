at a loss

1. ADV

/ æt ə lɑs / (AmE), / ət ə lɒs / (BrE)
For less money than the original cost or value; making a financial loss.
for less than cost; making a loss

Examples

The retailer was forced to sell winter coats at a loss to clear out old inventory.
They decided to sell their house at a loss after the market downturn.
Many investors panicked and sold their shares at a loss during the economic crisis.

Synset Examples

sold the car at a loss

Related Terms

  • Antonym: profit - financial gain, especially the difference between amount earned and amount spent

2. ADJ

/ æt ə lɔs / (AmE), / ət ə lɒs / (BrE)
Unable to know what to do, say, or think; feeling confused or bewildered.
confused; unsure what to do

Examples

She was at a loss for words when she heard the surprising news.
During the difficult meeting, he felt completely at a loss.
I was at a loss to explain the sudden change in her attitude.

Synset Examples

at a loss to understand those remarks
puzzled that she left without saying goodbye

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bewildered - feeling extremely confused or puzzled
  • Synonym: confused - unable to think clearly or to understand something
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ət ə lɔs / (AmE), / ət ə lɒs / (BrE)
unable to know what to say or do because you are surprised or confused
uncertain or confused; unsure what to do

Examples

When the teacher asked an unexpected question, I was at a loss for words.
She seemed at a loss after receiving the strange letter.
He stood at a loss in the middle of the busy street, unsure where to go.

Synset Examples

I'm at a loss for what to say.
He was at a loss after losing his job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confused - unable to think clearly or understand
  • Antonym: certain - sure and confident
