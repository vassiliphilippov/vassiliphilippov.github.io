lion's share

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪənz ʃɛr / (AmE), / ˈlaɪənz ʃɛː / (BrE)
the largest part or portion of something, especially when distributed among several people or groups
largest part or portion

Examples

The company secured the lion's share of the government contract.
She handled the lion's share of the household chores after her husband fell ill.
During the budget meeting, education received the lion's share of the funding.

Synset Examples

The lion's share of the credit goes to the team.
He put in the lion's share of effort on the project.
She received the lion's share of the inheritance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: majority - the greater part or most of something
  • Antonym: minority - the smaller part or number

Etymology

From the fable in which a lion takes the largest part of a shared catch.
