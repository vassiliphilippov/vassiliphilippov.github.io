to put one's foot in one's mouth

1. VERB

/ pʊt wʌnz fʊt ɪn wʌnz maʊθ / (AmE), / pʊt wʌnz fʊt ɪn wʌnz maʊθ / (BrE)
to say something embarrassing, inappropriate, or tactless, especially without intending to
say something embarrassing or inappropriate

Examples

I really put my foot in my mouth when I mentioned her recent breakup.
Whenever he’s nervous, he tends to put his foot in his mouth and say the wrong thing.
She put her foot in her mouth by joking about the boss’s new haircut.

Synset Examples

He tends to put his foot in his mouth during interviews.
I really put my foot in my mouth at the meeting.
She always puts her foot in her mouth when nervous.
She stuck her foot in her mouth when she mentioned his ex.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunder - to make a careless or embarrassing mistake
  • Synonym: gaffe - an unintentional act or remark causing embarrassment
