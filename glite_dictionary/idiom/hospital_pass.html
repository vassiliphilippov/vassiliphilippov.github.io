hospital pass

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːspɪtl pæs / (AmE), / ˈhɒspɪtl pɑːs / (BrE)
a risky or dangerous pass or action that puts someone in a difficult, vulnerable, or dangerous position, especially one that may expose them to harm or criticism
risky pass putting someone in danger or difficulty

Examples

He gave me a hospital pass during the game, and I was tackled immediately.
In the meeting, she handed her colleague a real hospital pass by assigning him the toughest questions.
Taking over the failing project felt like accepting a hospital pass from management.

Synset Examples

He gave me a hospital pass during the game.
Receiving a hospital pass can be frustrating in teamwork.
That was a hospital pass; I almost got hurt!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poisoned chalice - something apparently desirable but likely to cause harm
  • Antonym: safe haven - a place or situation offering security and safety

Etymology

From sports slang, originally used in rugby and football referring to a pass likely to cause injury, as if sending the receiver to the hospital.
