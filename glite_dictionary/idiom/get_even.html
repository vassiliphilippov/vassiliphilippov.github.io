to get even

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈivən / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈiːvən / (BrE)
to take action to achieve equality in a situation, often by retaliating or evening the score
retaliate; make things equal

Examples

After losing the first match, the team was determined to get even in the next game.
She planned a clever way to get even with her rival.
He didn't like being cheated, so he found a way to get even.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to retaliate - to make an attack in return for a similar attack
  • Antonym: to forgive - to stop feeling angry or resentful towards someone
