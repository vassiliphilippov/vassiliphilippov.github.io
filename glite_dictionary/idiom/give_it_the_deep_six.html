to give it the deep six

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ɪt ðə dip sɪks / (AmE), / ɡɪv ɪt ðə diːp sɪks / (BrE)
to throw something away or get rid of it completely
get rid of or discard something
informal

Examples

After years of collecting dust, she finally decided to give it the deep six and throw it away.
If that old computer still doesn't work, it's time to give it the deep six.
The committee chose to give it the deep six when the proposal proved unworkable.

Synset Examples

deep-six these old souvenirs!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dispose of - to get rid of something; throw away
  • Antonym: keep - to retain or not get rid of something

Etymology

From nautical slang ‘deep six’, meaning a depth of six fathoms (36 feet), traditionally used as a burial at sea.
