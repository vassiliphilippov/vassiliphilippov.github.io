almighty dollar

1. NOUN

/ ɑlˈmaɪti ˈdɑlər / (AmE), / ɔːlˈmaɪti ˈdɒlə / (BrE)
used to refer to money, especially when emphasizing its powerful influence on people's actions or decisions
the powerful influence of money

Examples

In politics, many decisions seem to be shaped by the almighty dollar.
She refused to sacrifice her values for the almighty dollar.
The film critiques how society worships the almighty dollar above all else.

Synset Examples

The almighty dollar often drives corporate decisions.
She joked that the almighty dollar rules the world.
He chased the almighty dollar his whole career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: money - general term for currency or wealth

Etymology

From 'almighty' (all-powerful) + 'dollar', used humorously to liken money to a god; popularized in the 19th century.
