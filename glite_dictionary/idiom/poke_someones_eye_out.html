to poke someone's eye out

1. VERB

/ poʊk sʌmˌwʌnz aɪ aʊt / (AmE), / pəʊk sʌmˌwʌnz aɪ aʊt / (BrE)
to accidentally injure or damage someone's eye, typically by striking it with a pointed object
accidentally injure someone's eye

Examples

Be careful with that umbrella or you'll poke someone's eye out.
She waved the stick around wildly, almost poking someone's eye out.
He cautioned the children not to run with scissors so they wouldn't poke someone's eye out.

Synset Examples

Be careful with that stick; you might poke someone's eye out.
He warned the kids not to play roughly, or they might poke someone's eye out.
Watch out for that sharp object; you could poke someone's eye out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poke - to jab or prod with a finger or a sharp object
