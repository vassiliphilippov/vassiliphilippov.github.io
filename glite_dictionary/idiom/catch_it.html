to catch it

1. VERB

/ kætʃ ɪt / (AmE), / katʃ ɪt / (BrE)
to be reprimanded or punished for something, especially by someone in authority
be reprimanded or punished
informal

Examples

If I miss my curfew again, I’ll catch it from my parents.
He knew he would catch it at work for being late again.
The students will catch it if they break the rules.

Synset Examples

You’re going to catch it if you don’t apologize.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get it - be reprimanded or punished for something
