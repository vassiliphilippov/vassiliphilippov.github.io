to cramp someone's style

1. VERB

/ kræmp sʌmwʌnz staɪl / (AmE), / kramp sʌmwʌnz staɪl / (BrE)
to restrict or hinder someone's ability to act, express themselves, or behave freely
restrict a person's freedom or expression

Examples

My parents' constant supervision really cramps my style when I have friends over.
She didn't want her little brother to cramp her style at the party.
The company's strict dress code can cramp your style if you like to dress creatively.

Synset Examples

Don't cramp someone's style by criticizing their outfit.
He felt that the new policies cramp someone's style creatively.
The strict rules really cramp someone's style at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hold back - to prevent someone from making progress or expressing themselves freely
  • Antonym: free rein - freedom to act and express oneself without restriction
