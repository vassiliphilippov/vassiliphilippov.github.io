to be a Caesar

1. VERB

/ bi ə ˈsiːzɚ / (AmE), / biː ə ˈsiːzə / (BrE)
to achieve great power or status, especially in a manner reminiscent of a powerful ruler
achieve great power or status

Examples

Ever since he entered politics, he has wanted to be a Caesar and influence the nation's destiny.
Some leaders long to be a Caesar, regardless of the cost to their people.
In the corporate world, many executives dream of the chance to be a Caesar.

Synset Examples

He was ambitious, always wanting to be a Caesar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be a king - to be a person of the highest authority
  • Antonym: be a nobody - to have no status or power

Etymology

From the name of Julius Caesar, the Roman general and dictator, whose name became a symbol of imperial power.
