to go nowhere fast

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈnoʊwɛr ˈfæst / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈnəʊweə ˈfɑːst / (BrE)
to make no progress or achieve nothing, often despite putting in effort or acting quickly
fail to make progress

Examples

Despite working overtime on the project, we seemed to go nowhere fast.
If you keep arguing like this, you'll just go nowhere fast.
The team's proposal is going nowhere fast without better leadership.

Synset Examples

If we don't change strategies, we'll go nowhere fast.
Their endless meetings seem to go nowhere fast.
The debate turned into a shouting match and went nowhere fast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get nowhere - to fail to make progress
