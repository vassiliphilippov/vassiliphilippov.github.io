eagle eye

1. NOUN

/ ˈiɡəl aɪ / (AmE), / ˈiːɡl aɪ / (BrE)
the ability to notice details very well, especially to spot mistakes or find things that are hard to see
keen or sharp power of observation

Examples

The editor's eagle eye caught every typo in the manuscript.
She relies on her eagle eye to find antiques at flea markets.
With his eagle eye for detail, he spotted the out-of-place file immediately.

Synset Examples

He has an eagle eye for detail in his work.
With her eagle eye, she spotted the error immediately.
The director has an eagle eye for talent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharp eye - an ability to notice details quickly
  • Antonym: blind spot - an area or aspect that is not noticed or understood
