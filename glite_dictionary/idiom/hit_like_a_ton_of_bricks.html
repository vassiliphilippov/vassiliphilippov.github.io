to hit like a ton of bricks

1. VERB

/ hɪt laɪk ə tʌn əv brɪks / (AmE), / hɪt laɪk ə tʌn ɒv brɪks / (BrE)
to affect someone suddenly and powerfully, especially with shock, surprise, or realization
to shock or overwhelm suddenly

Examples

When she heard the news of her promotion, it hit like a ton of bricks—she could hardly believe it.
The seriousness of the accident hit like a ton of bricks once he saw the damage.
It hit like a ton of bricks when I realized I’d forgotten my passport at home.

Synset Examples

The realization hit him like a ton of bricks.
The news hit me like a ton of bricks.
His betrayal hit her like a ton of bricks.
Her words struck him like a ton of bricks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hit hard - to affect someone strongly
  • Synonym: take by surprise - to shock or surprise someone suddenly
