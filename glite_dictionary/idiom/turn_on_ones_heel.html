to turn on one's heel

1. VERB

/ tɝn ɑn wʌnz hil / (AmE), / tɜːn ɒn wʌnz hiːl / (BrE)
to suddenly change direction or leave abruptly, often in response to something unexpected or unwelcome
to change direction or leave abruptly

Examples

She was so offended that she turned on her heel and marched out of the room.
Without waiting for a reply, he turned on his heel and disappeared into the crowd.
The manager turned on her heel after hearing the bad news, leaving everyone in stunned silence.

Synset Examples

Without a word, she turned on her heel and left.

Etymology

Refers to quickly pivoting by turning on the heel of one's foot; figurative since the 18th century.
