to keep it between the ditches

1. VERB

/ kip ɪt bɪˈtwin ðə ˈdɪtʃɪz / (AmE), / kiːp ɪt bɪˈtwiːn ðə ˈdɪtʃɪz / (BrE)
to remain within safe or acceptable boundaries; to avoid making mistakes or getting into trouble
stay within safe limits

Examples

When you start a new job, it’s best to keep it between the ditches until you learn the ropes.
His financial adviser warned him to keep it between the ditches with his investments.
Whenever I feel tempted to take risks, I remember to keep it between the ditches and play it safe.

Synset Examples

He reminded her to keep it between the ditches on the track.

Etymology

Metaphorical; refers to driving safely on a road bordered by ditches.
