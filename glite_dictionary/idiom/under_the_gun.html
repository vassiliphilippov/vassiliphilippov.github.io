under the gun

1. ADV

/ ˈʌn.dɚ ðə ɡʌn / (AmE), / ˈʌn.də ðə ɡʌn / (BrE)
in a state of intense pressure or urgency, often due to a looming deadline or expectation
in a pressured or urgent situation

Examples

With the project due tomorrow, everyone at the office was under the gun.
Journalists often work under the gun to meet strict publication deadlines.
The chef was under the gun during the dinner rush, but managed to stay calm.

Synset Examples

With the deadline approaching, he felt under the gun.
The team worked late, all under the gun for delivery.
She was under the gun to finish the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: under pressure - feeling stressed because of demanding circumstances
