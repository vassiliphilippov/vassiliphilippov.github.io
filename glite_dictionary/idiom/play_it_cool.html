to play it cool

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ɪt kuːl / (AmE), / pleɪ ɪt kuːl / (BrE)
to remain calm and composed, especially in a stressful or important situation
remain calm and composed

Examples

Try to play it cool when meeting your favorite celebrity for the first time.
Even though he was nervous, he managed to play it cool throughout the presentation.
If you want to impress them, just play it cool and don’t act desperate.

Synset Examples

He decided to play it cool during the interview.
She tried to play it cool, but the cat was out.
Just play it cool and wait for your turn.
She always knows how to play it cool in tough situations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keep one's cool - to remain calm under pressure or in a stressful situation
  • Antonym: to lose one's cool - to lose one's calm; to become upset or angry
