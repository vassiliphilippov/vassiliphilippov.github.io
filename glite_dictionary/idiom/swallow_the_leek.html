to swallow the leek

1. VERB

/ ˈswɑloʊ ðə lik / (AmE), / ˈswɒləʊ ðə liːk / (BrE)
to accept something unpleasant or humiliating, especially during negotiations or a difficult situation
accept something unpleasant or humiliating

Examples

In tough negotiations, sometimes you have to swallow the leek and accept the other party's demands.
After making a mistake at work, she decided to swallow the leek and apologize to her boss.
He found it hard, but he knew he had to swallow the leek and move forward with the plan.

Synset Examples

In negotiations, sometimes you just have to swallow the leek.
After the argument, he felt he had to swallow the leek and move on.
She knew she had to swallow the leek for the team's sake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bite the bullet - accept something difficult or unpleasant
  • Synonym: to grin and bear it - endure a painful or difficult situation with good grace
