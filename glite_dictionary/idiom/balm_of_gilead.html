balm of Gilead

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑm əv ˈɡɪliæd / (AmE), / ˈbɑːm əv ˈɡɪliæd / (BrE)
something that brings comfort or relief in times of trouble or distress
something comforting or soothing

Examples

After a stressful week, her gentle words were a true balm of Gilead to him.
The music served as a balm of Gilead for the troubled crowd.
In times of loss, friendship can be the balm of Gilead that helps us heal.

Synset Examples

Her laughter was the balm of Gilead I needed.
He found the balm of Gilead in her kind words.
The herbal tea was like a balm of Gilead for his soul.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solace - comfort or consolation in a time of distress
  • Synonym: succor - assistance and support in times of hardship
  • Antonym: anguish - severe mental or physical pain (opposite of relief)

Etymology

From the biblical reference in the Old Testament to a healing ointment from Gilead, a region east of the Jordan River.
