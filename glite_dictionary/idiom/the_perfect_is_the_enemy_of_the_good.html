the perfect is the enemy of the good

1. X

/ ðə ˈpɝː.fɪkt ɪz ði ˈɛn.ə.mi əv ðə ɡʊd / (AmE), / ðə ˈpɜː.fɪkt ɪz ði ˈɛn.ə.mi əv ðə ɡʊd / (BrE)
used to express the idea that insisting on perfection can prevent progress or the achievement of satisfactory results
insisting on perfection hinders progress

Examples

When working on the project, remember that the perfect is the enemy of the good.
She wanted to revise her essay again, but her teacher warned her that the perfect is the enemy of the good.
Don’t let the perfect be the enemy of the good—sometimes good enough is the best way forward.

Synset Examples

He was reminded not to let the perfect be the enemy of the good.
Sometimes we must not let the perfect be the enemy of the good.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perfectionism - refusal to accept any standard short of perfection

Etymology

Adapted from the French proverb 'Le mieux est l’ennemi du bien.' Popularized in English in the 18th–20th centuries.
