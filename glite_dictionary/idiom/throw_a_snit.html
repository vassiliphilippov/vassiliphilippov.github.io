to throw a snit

1. VERB

/ ˈθroʊ ə snɪt / (AmE), / ˈθrəʊ ə snɪt / (BrE)
(informal) To suddenly become very angry or upset, behaving in a childish or exaggerated way.
have an outburst of anger or annoyance
informal american

Examples

After being told he couldn't have dessert, the boy threw a snit in the middle of the restaurant.
Whenever plans change unexpectedly, she tends to throw a snit and refuse to cooperate.
He threw a snit when he found out his favorite team had lost.

Synset Examples

He threw a snit when he lost the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to throw a fit - to have a sudden outburst of anger or frustration
  • Synonym: tantrum - a sudden, uncontrolled burst of anger, especially in a child
