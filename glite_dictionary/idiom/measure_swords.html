to measure swords

1. VERB

/ ˈmɛʒɚ ˈsɔrdz / (AmE), / ˈmɛʒə ˈsɔːdz / (BrE)
to engage in competition or confrontation, especially in a contest of skill or strategy
engage in competition or confrontation

Examples

During the debate, the two candidates were eager to measure swords on the big issues.
The rivals will measure swords again in next week's final match.
She was nervous about measuring swords with the most experienced players in the team.

Synset Examples

In negotiations, we must measure swords with caution.
We should measure swords with expertise, not haste.
It's wise to measure swords with your competitors carefully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cross swords - to engage in conflict or dispute with someone

Etymology

From the practice of combatants comparing the length of their swords before a duel.
