by one's own lights

1. ADV

/ baɪ wʌnz oʊn laɪts / (AmE), / baɪ wʌnz əʊn laɪts / (BrE)
according to one's own judgment or understanding; based on one's own beliefs or principles
according to one's own judgment

Examples

Despite advice from friends, he chose to act by his own lights.
She always judges situations by her own lights rather than following the crowd.
By their own lights, the team's tactics made perfect sense, even if others disagreed.

Synset Examples

He makes decisions by his own lights, regardless of others' opinions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in one's own way - according to one's personal style or wishes

Etymology

The word 'lights' here refers to inner light, meaning personal judgment or understanding, a usage dating from the 17th century.
