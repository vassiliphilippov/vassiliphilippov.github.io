to steal the show

1. VERB

/ stil ðə ʃoʊ / (AmE), / stiːl ðə ʃəʊ / (BrE)
to attract the most attention or praise, especially by being the most impressive at an event or in a group
attract more attention than anyone else

Examples

Her vibrant painting managed to steal the show at the art exhibition.
Although he was a supporting actor, his comedic timing let him steal the show.
The young dancer stole the show with her charming performance.
