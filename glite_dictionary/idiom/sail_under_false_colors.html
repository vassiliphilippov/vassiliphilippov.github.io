to sail under false colors

1. VERB

/ seɪl ˈʌndɚ fɔls ˈkʌlɚz / (AmE), / seɪl ˈʌndə fɔːls ˈkʌləz / (BrE)
to deceive others by pretending to be someone or something else, often to gain an advantage
deceive by pretending to be something else

Examples

In business, some companies have been known to sail under false colors to win contracts.
Realizing too late that she had been sailing under false colors, they regretted trusting her.
Politicians who sail under false colors often lose the public's trust when the truth emerges.

Synset Examples

They decided to sail under false colors to gain the enemy's trust.
He was accused of sailing under false colors during the negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true
  • Synonym: to betray - to be disloyal or unfaithful
  • Antonym: deal honestly - to act in a truthful or straightforward way

Etymology

Originates from the 17th-century naval practice of ships flying false flags to deceive enemies.
