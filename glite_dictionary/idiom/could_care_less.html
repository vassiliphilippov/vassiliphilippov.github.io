to could care less

1. VERB

/ kʊd kɛr lɛs / (AmE), / kʊd keə lɛs / (BrE)
used to indicate that one does not care at all about something; often used sarcastically to mean the opposite of the literal words
idiom meaning not to care at all
american informal

Examples

I could care less what they think about my decision.
She acts like she could care less about the gossip, but I think otherwise.
Frank said he could care less about the outcome of the match.

Synset Examples

He could care a button less about the job offer.
I could care fewer about the gossip going around.
She said she couldn't care less if he shows up.
She couldn't care for beans about the outcome.
I couldn't care less about the eff of this situation.
They couldn't care less about the team's performance.
I couldn't care less about that; it's a flying fig to me.
I couldn't care less about the latest celebrity gossip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: couldn't care less - to not care at all about something, original form of the idiom

Etymology

Variant of the phrase "couldn't care less," commonly used in American English with sarcastic intent.
