to break one's back

1. VERB

/ breɪk wʌnz bæk / (AmE), / breɪk wʌnz bæk / (BrE)
to work extremely hard at something, often to the point of exhaustion
work extremely hard

Examples

She broke her back getting the project finished on time.
Don't break your back over this—it isn't worth the stress.
He had been breaking his back to provide for his family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slave away - to work very hard for a long time
  • Antonym: to take it easy - to relax, not work hard
