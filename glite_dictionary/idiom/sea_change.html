sea change

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiː tʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈsiː tʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
a profound or notable transformation; a marked change in the character or nature of something
a significant transformation

Examples

The election marked a sea change in the country's political landscape.
Over the past decade, there has been a sea change in attitudes toward climate change.
Her promotion brought about a sea change in the company's working culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transformation - a thorough or dramatic change in form or appearance
  • Degree Lower: shift - a slight change in position, direction, or tendency

Etymology

First used in Shakespeare's The Tempest (c. 1610), from 'sea' + 'change', referring to a transformation caused by the sea.

2. NOUN

/ siː tʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / siː tʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
a profound or notable transformation in character, appearance, or condition
a significant transformation or shift

Examples

There has been a sea change in attitudes toward renewable energy in the last decade.
The introduction of the internet caused a sea change in the way we communicate.
His experience abroad triggered a sea change in his worldview.

Synset Examples

There was a sea change in public opinion after the scandal.
The new CEO brought a sea change to the company.
Her promotion marked a sea change in her career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transformation - a marked change in form, nature, or appearance
  • Synonym: revolution - a dramatic and wide-reaching change in conditions or attitudes
  • Antonym: gradual change - a change that occurs slowly or by degrees

Etymology

From Shakespeare’s 'The Tempest', where it referred to a transformation by the sea.
