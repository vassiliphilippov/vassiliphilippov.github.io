to go back to the drawing board

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ bæk tə ðə ˈdrɔɪŋ bɔrd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ bæk tə ðə ˈdrɔːɪŋ bɔːd / (BrE)
to start again from the beginning, especially after a plan or idea has failed
start over after failure

Examples

After our proposal was rejected, we had to go back to the drawing board and create a new plan.
If the test results aren't promising, we'll simply go back to the drawing board.
Despite their best efforts, the team had to go back to the drawing board several times before succeeding.

Synset Examples

It's time to go back to the drawing board and rethink our strategy.
The prototype failed, so it’s back to the drawing board.
After the failure, we went back to the drawing board.
The initial design didn't work, so we must go back to the drawing board.
Our initial ideas didn’t work, time to go back to the drawing board.
After the project failed, we had to go back to the drawing board.
After the feedback, we must go back to the drawing board.
Let's return to the drawing board and rethink our strategy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to start over - to begin something again from the beginning
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop trying due to failure or difficulty

Etymology

From the practice of designers and engineers returning to their original plans (drawing boards) when a project or idea fails, dating from the mid-20th century.
