ten to one

1. ADV

/ tɛn tə wʌn / (AmE), / tɛn tə wʌn / (BrE)
Used to say that something is very likely or almost certain to happen.
very likely, almost certain

Examples

Ten to one he’ll forget to call you back before the end of the day.
Ten to one, the traffic will make us late again.
It’s ten to one that this new restaurant will be fully booked tonight.

Synset Examples

It's ten to one that it will rain tomorrow.
Ten to one, she’ll forget our meeting again.
I’d say ten to one that he’ll show up late.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: likely - probable; having a good chance of happening
  • Antonym: unlikely - not probable; not likely to happen

Etymology

From gambling odds, where 'ten to one' means high probability of winning.
