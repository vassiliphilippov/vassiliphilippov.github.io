to wither on the vine

1. VERB

/ ˈwɪðər ɑn ðə vaɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɪðə ɒn ðə vaɪn / (BrE)
(of a plan, project, or goal) to fail to thrive, develop, or succeed, often because of neglect or lack of support.
fail to develop or succeed

Examples

The committee's recommendations were allowed to wither on the vine after the leadership changed.
Many promising startups wither on the vine without adequate investment.
If you ignore your talents, they may simply wither on the vine over time.

Synset Examples

The project will die on the vine without proper funding.
If not acted upon, her dreams will wither on the vine.
His ambitions seemed to wither on the vine after the setback.
Many great ideas wither on the vine without proper support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: die on the vine - fail to develop or succeed, especially due to neglect

Etymology

From the literal image of fruit withering on a vine when not harvested, used figuratively since the 20th century.
