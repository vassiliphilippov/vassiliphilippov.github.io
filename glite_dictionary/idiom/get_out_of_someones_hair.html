to get out of someone's hair

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈaʊt əv ˈsʌmwʌnz hɛr / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈaʊt əv ˈsʌmwənz heə / (BrE)
to stop bothering or disturbing someone; to leave someone alone so they can work or think
stop disturbing someone
informal

Examples

I'll get out of your hair now so you can finish your report.
Could you please get out of my hair while I'm making dinner?
He finally got out of her hair and let her focus on her studies.

Synset Examples

I need to get out of her hair while she works.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave someone alone - to refrain from disturbing or bothering someone
