home and hosed

1. ADJ

/ hoʊm ənd hoʊzd / (AmE), / həʊm ənd həʊzd / (BrE)
in a secure or successful position after completing a difficult task or overcoming a challenge; assured of success or relief
secure or successful after a challenge
british

Examples

Once the final payment clears, we'll be home and hosed.
After months of preparation, the team felt home and hosed as the project concluded successfully.
She won't relax until the exams are over and she's truly home and hosed.

Synset Examples

With the presentation done, we were home and hosed.
Once they signed the contract, they were home and hosed.
After finishing the report, I felt home and hosed.

Etymology

Originating in Australian English, likely from horse racing, where a horse is 'home' (at the finish) and 'hosed down' (cooled off) after the race.
