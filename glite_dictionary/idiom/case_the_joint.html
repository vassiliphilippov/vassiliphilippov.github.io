to case the joint

1. VERB

/ keɪs ðə dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / keɪs ðə dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
to inspect or survey a place, usually with the intention of planning a robbery or other criminal activity
inspect a place for planning a crime
informal slang

Examples

The thieves decided to case the joint before attempting the break-in.
Detectives suspected someone had cased the joint after noticing unusual activity outside the bank.
He was caught casing the joint when the owner confronted him about his suspicious behavior.

Synset Examples

He was told to case the joint for security flaws.
We need to case the joint before the heist.
They spent an hour to case the joint thoroughly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rob - to take property unlawfully from a person or place by force or threat

Etymology

From criminal slang, where 'case' means to observe or study and 'joint' refers to a place or establishment.
