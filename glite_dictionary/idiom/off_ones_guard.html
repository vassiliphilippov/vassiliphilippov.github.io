off one's guard

1. ADJ

/ ɔf wʌnz ɡɑrd / (AmE), / ɒf wʌnz ɡɑːd / (BrE)
not giving attention or being prepared for possible danger or trouble; not alert or vigilant
not alert or prepared; unguarded

Examples

The question caught him off his guard, and he struggled to answer quickly.
She was momentarily off her guard, which allowed her opponent to score a point.
Don’t let yourself be off your guard when walking alone at night.

Synset Examples

the blow caught him napping
caught in an off-guard moment
found him off his guard

Related Terms

  • Antonym: on one's guard - watchful and prepared for danger or trouble
  • Synonym: off-guard - unprepared or unprotected, especially for trouble or danger

2. ADJ

/ ɑf wʌnz ɡɑrd / (AmE), / ɒf wʌnz ɡɑːd / (BrE)
not paying attention or not prepared for something unexpected; not being watchful or alert
not vigilant or prepared

Examples

The thief struck while Tom was off his guard and made off with his wallet.
She was completely off her guard when the interviewer asked about her past.
The news caught everyone off their guard, leaving the team in shock.

Synset Examples

the blow caught him napping
caught in an off-guard moment
found him off his guard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: off guard - not alert or wary; unprepared
  • Antonym: on one's guard - being watchful or alert
