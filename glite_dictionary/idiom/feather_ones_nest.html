to feather one's nest

1. VERB

/ ˈfɛðɚ wʌnz nɛst / (AmE), / ˈfɛðə wʌnz nɛst / (BrE)
to use one's position or opportunities to acquire wealth or personal advantage, especially in a self-serving or unethical manner
enrich oneself by exploiting one's position

Examples

Some officials have been accused of trying to feather their nest instead of serving the public.
He spent years in office simply feathering his nest at the taxpayer's expense.
It was discovered that she had feathered her nest by awarding contracts to her own company.

Synset Examples

The congressmen feathered his nest through his connection with big business

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to line one's pockets - to make money for oneself, especially dishonestly or greedily

Etymology

From the imagery of a bird lining its nest comfortably, used figuratively since the 17th century for personal gain.

2. VERB

/ ˈfɛðɚ wʌnz nɛst / (AmE), / ˈfɛðə wʌnz nɛst / (BrE)
to make oneself rich, often in a way that is considered selfish or dishonest, especially by taking advantage of a position or opportunity
enrich oneself, often selfishly

Examples

The corrupt official used his position to feather his nest at the taxpayers' expense.
Instead of focusing on the team's needs, she tried to feather her nest with personal perks.
Some executives will always look for ways to feather their nest before thinking of the employees.

Synset Examples

She worked hard but never tried to feather her nest.
He was criticized for trying to feather his nest at the company's expense.
Politicians often feather their nests while in office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to line one's pockets - to acquire money, especially by dishonest means

Etymology

From the literal practice of birds lining their nests with feathers; figuratively used in English since the 16th century.
