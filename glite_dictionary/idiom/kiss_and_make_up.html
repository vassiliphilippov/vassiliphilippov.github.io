to kiss and make up

1. VERB

/ kɪs ənd meɪk ʌp / (AmE), / kɪs ənd meɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to become friendly again after a disagreement or argument
reconcile after a quarrel

Examples

They argued for hours but finally decided to kiss and make up before bed.
It's important for friends to kiss and make up after a disagreement rather than hold a grudge.
After the heated debate, the two politicians managed to kiss and make up for the sake of unity.

Synset Examples

They had a big fight but always kiss and make up.
After the argument, they decided to kiss and make up.
Friends often kiss and make up after misunderstandings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reconcile - to restore friendly relations after a dispute
  • Antonym: to fall out - to have a quarrel and stop being friendly
