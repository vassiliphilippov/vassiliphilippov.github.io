all retch and no vomit

1. X

/ ɔl rɛtʃ ənd noʊ ˈvɑmɪt / (AmE), / ɔːl rɛtʃ ənd nəʊ ˈvɒmɪt / (BrE)
used to describe a situation where there is a lot of fuss, complaint, or commotion but no real results, action, or substance
making a fuss without real results

Examples

Her dramatic threats ended up being all retch and no vomit, as nothing ever changed.
The debate was all retch and no vomit, with loud arguments but no meaningful discussion.
He always complains about his job, but it's just all retch and no vomit because he never acts on it.

Synset Examples

She just whined, it was all retch and no vomit.
His complaints were all retch and no vomit, no solutions offered.
Their arguments were all retch and no vomit, lacking substance.
