end of the world

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛnd əv ðə ˈwɝld / (AmE), / ˈɛnd əv ðə ˈwɜːld / (BrE)
a disastrous or extremely bad event or situation, especially when used to suggest that something is worse than it actually is
catastrophic event or outcome (idiomatic)

Examples

Losing your keys isn't the end of the world, so try not to panic.
She acted as if failing the test was the end of the world.
It's disappointing, but missing one train isn't the end of the world.

Synset Examples

everyone was aware of the approaching doom but was helpless to avoid it
that's unfortunate but it isn't the end of the world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catastrophe - an event causing great and usually sudden damage or suffering
  • Synonym: disaster - a sudden event causing great damage, loss, or destruction
