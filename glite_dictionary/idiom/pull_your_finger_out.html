to pull your finger out

1. VERB

/ pʊl jɔr ˈfɪŋɡɚ aʊt / (AmE), / pʊl jɔː ˈfɪŋɡə aʊt / (BrE)
(informal, British) to start making a greater effort, especially after being lazy or inactive.
begin working harder or making more effort
informal british

Examples

You really need to pull your finger out if you want to pass these exams.
She told her team to pull their finger out and meet the deadline.
If we don't pull our finger out soon, we'll miss the opportunity.

Synset Examples

It's time to pull your finger out and help us.
You need to pull your finger out and start working.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get your act together - to start to organize yourself and behave more effectively
  • Synonym: to buckle down - to begin to work seriously
