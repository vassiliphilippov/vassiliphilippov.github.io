cry me a river

1. X

/ kraɪ mi ə ˈrɪvɚ / (AmE), / kraɪ mi ə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
used to express mock sympathy for someone's complaints or to indicate that their grievances are exaggerated or unworthy of concern
expression of mock sympathy

Examples

When he started complaining about losing his wallet again, she just said, 'cry me a river.'
After hearing his endless excuses, I told him to 'cry me a river' and move on.
No one wanted to hear more complaints, so Mark sarcastically replied, 'cry me a river.'

Synset Examples

He always has a reason to cry a Crimea river.
She kept complaining, and I told her to cry me a river.
She joked, 'Don't cry me a Crimea river over that!'
He wanted sympathy, but I just told him to cry me a river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get over it - used to tell someone to stop being upset about something
