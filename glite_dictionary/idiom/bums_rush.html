bum's rush

1. NOUN

/ bʌmz rʌʃ / (AmE), / bʌmz rʌʃ / (BrE)
a hasty or forceful dismissal, especially in a rude or abrupt manner
a hasty or forceful dismissal
informal american

Examples

The security guards gave the protester the bum's rush when he tried to enter the private event.
After the meeting ran overtime, the staff received the bum's rush from the conference room.
He feared he would get the bum's rush if he complained about his manager again.

Synset Examples

The manager showed him the bum's rush after the mishap.
She gave him the bum's rush when he overstayed his welcome.
After the argument, he felt the bum's rush out the door.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to eject - to force someone to leave a place
  • Synonym: to expel - to force someone out of a place or organization

Etymology

Originally American slang, from 'bum' (tramp, vagrant) and 'rush,' referring to forcibly ejecting someone.
