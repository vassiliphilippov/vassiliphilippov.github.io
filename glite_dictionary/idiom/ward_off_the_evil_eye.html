to ward off the evil eye

1. VERB

/ wɔrd ɑf ðə ˈivəl aɪ / (AmE), / wɔːd ɒf ði ˈiːvəl aɪ / (BrE)
to protect oneself or others from misfortune or harm that is believed to be caused by a look of envy or ill will, often through charms or rituals
protect against misfortune from envy or ill will

Examples

She always wears a blue bead to ward off the evil eye when traveling.
Many cultures have rituals to ward off the evil eye and protect children from harm.
The villagers hung symbols on their doors to ward off the evil eye.

Synset Examples

Wearing amulets can ward off the evil eye.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evil eye - a supposed power of some people to inflict harm by a glance

Etymology

From the traditional belief in the 'evil eye' found in Mediterranean, Middle Eastern, and other cultures, where certain glances are thought to cause harm.
