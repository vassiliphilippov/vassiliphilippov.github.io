ugly duckling

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌɡli ˈdʌklɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʌɡli ˈdʌklɪŋ / (BrE)
a person or thing initially considered unattractive or unpromising but who later becomes beautiful, successful, or admired
initially unattractive but later admired

Examples

Growing up, Anna felt like an ugly duckling until she blossomed in college.
The company's early models were dismissed as ugly ducklings, but now they're highly sought after.
Despite being the ugly duckling at school, he became a famous actor.

Synset Examples

She transformed from an ugly duckling into a stunning swan.
Everyone saw him as the ugly duckling of the group.
The movie's theme was about the ugly duckling finding acceptance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: swan - someone who is now beautiful or successful, in contrast to former awkwardness
  • Synonym: wallflower - a person who is shy and unpopular, especially at social events

Etymology

From Hans Christian Andersen's 1843 fairy tale 'The Ugly Duckling'.
