to tan someone's hide

1. VERB

/ tæn sʌmwʌnz haɪd / (AmE), / tæn sʌmwʌnz haɪd / (BrE)
to punish or scold someone severely
punish or scold severely (idiom)
informal

Examples

If you don't do your homework, your teacher will tan your hide for sure.
After his prank at school, his dad threatened to tan his hide.
My grandmother used to say she'd tan my hide if I disobeyed her rules.

Synset Examples

He was warned that his parents would tan his hide.
If you break the rules, I'll tan your hide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrash - to beat someone severely as a punishment
  • Synonym: reprimand - to scold someone severely

Etymology

Refers originally to tanning animal hides into leather, metaphorically extended to beating someone so their skin would be 'tanned.'

2. VERB

/ tæn ˌsʌmwʌnz haɪd / (AmE), / tæn ˌsʌmwənz haɪd / (BrE)
to punish or scold someone severely
punish or scold severely (idiom)
informal

Examples

If you come home past midnight again, your mother will tan your hide for sure.
The boss threatened to tan someone's hide after the major mistake in the report.
He knew his father would tan his hide if he broke the window.

Synset Examples

The coach threatened to tan someone's hide after the loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scold - to speak angrily to someone because they have done something wrong
  • Synonym: to punish - to inflict a penalty or sanction as retribution for an offense

Etymology

From the imagery of tanning animal hides, meaning to beat the skin; figurative use since the 19th century.
