to jump the gun

1. VERB

/ dʒʌmp ðə ɡʌn / (AmE), / dʒʌmp ðə ɡʌn / (BrE)
to act before the proper or expected time, especially by doing something too soon
act prematurely or too soon

Examples

If we send the invitations now, we might jump the gun since the date isn't confirmed.
Try not to jump the gun during the negotiations, or we could ruin the deal.
They jumped the gun by releasing the product before it passed all safety tests.

Synset Examples

Don't jump the gun; we haven't received official confirmation yet.
He tends to jump the gun and make decisions without consulting anyone.
She jumped the gun by announcing the project before it was approved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: act prematurely - to do something before the appropriate or proper time
  • Antonym: to hold back - to wait or delay taking action

Etymology

Originally from athletics, where a runner starts before the starting gun sounds.
