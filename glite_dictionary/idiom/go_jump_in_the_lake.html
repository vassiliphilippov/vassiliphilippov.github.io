go jump in the lake

1. X

/ ɡoʊ dʒʌmp ɪn ðə leɪk / (AmE), / ɡəʊ dʒʌmp ɪn ðə leɪk / (BrE)
(informal) a rude or dismissive way to tell someone to go away or stop bothering you.
dismissive phrase telling someone to go away
informal

Examples

When he kept pestering me, I finally told him to go jump in the lake.
She rolled her eyes and said, 'If you don't like it, then go jump in the lake.'
Fed up with the demands, he told his boss to go jump in the lake.

Synset Examples

When he complained, I told him to go jump in the lake.
He suggested a silly idea, and I told him to go jump in the lake.
She was so annoyed, she told him to go jump in the lake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get lost - informal way to tell someone to leave you alone
  • Synonym: to buzz off - informal phrase meaning 'go away'
