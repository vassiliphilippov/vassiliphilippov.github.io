glue factory

1. NOUN

/ ɡlu ˈfæktəri / (AmE), / ɡluː ˈfæktəri / (BrE)
a place, especially in a metaphorical sense, where people or things that are no longer useful or wanted are sent to be discarded or removed from use
place for discarding the useless (metaphorical)

Examples

After he was fired, he joked that he was headed straight for the glue factory.
The company sent its obsolete computers to the glue factory of technology.
She worried that advancing age would send her career to the glue factory.

Synset Examples

After years of bad behavior, he joked he'd be sent to the glue factory.
They decided it was time to send the outdated machines to the glue factory.
The old horse was finally sent to the glue factory.

Etymology

Refers to factories where old horses were once processed to make glue; now used metaphorically.
