to become a leper

1. VERB

/ bɪˈkʌm ə ˈlɛpər / (AmE), / bɪˈkʌm ə ˈlɛpə / (BrE)
to be shunned or ostracized by society, often as a result of scandal or controversy
become socially ostracized

Examples

After the scandal, he became a leper in the community, with former friends refusing to speak to him.
She feared she would become a leper if the truth came out about her mistake.
Following his controversial statement, the politician became a leper in his own party.

Synset Examples

She became a leper in her town after the scandal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outcast - a person who is rejected by society
  • Synonym: pariah - a person who is despised or avoided by others

Etymology

Leper from Old French *lepre*, from Latin *lepra* (leprosy); originated from the social exclusion of people with leprosy.
