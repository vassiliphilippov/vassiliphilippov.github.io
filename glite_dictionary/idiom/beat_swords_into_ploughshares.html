to beat swords into ploughshares

1. VERB

/ bit sɔrdz ˈɪntu ˈplaʊˌʃɛrz / (AmE), / biːt sɔːdz ˈɪntuː ˈplaʊʃeəz / (BrE)
to convert weapons or military resources into peaceful uses or tools for productive, non-violent purposes
turn weapons into tools for peace

Examples

After years of conflict, the country pledged to beat swords into ploughshares and focus on rebuilding.
The organization encourages former soldiers to beat swords into ploughshares by teaching them new skills.
Many activists hope that future generations will choose to beat swords into ploughshares rather than perpetuate violence.

Synset Examples

After the war, he decided to beat his swords into ploughshares.
They worked hard to beat their swords into ploughshares through community service.
She hoped to help others by beating her swords into ploughshares.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make peace - to end conflict and promote harmony
  • Antonym: beat ploughshares into swords - to convert peaceful tools into weapons for war

Etymology

From a biblical phrase in Isaiah, urging nations to turn from war to peace.
