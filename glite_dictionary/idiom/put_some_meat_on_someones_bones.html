to put some meat on someone's bones

1. VERB

/ pʊt səm miːt ɑn ˈsʌmwʌnz boʊnz / (AmE), / pʊt səm miːt ɒn ˈsʌmwʌnz bəʊnz / (BrE)
to gain weight or become healthier, especially after being thin or ill
to gain weight or become healthier

Examples

After her illness, the doctor told her to put some meat on her bones.
You need to put some meat on your bones before winter comes.
He started eating more to put some meat on his bones for the football season.

Synset Examples

After the winter, she finally put some meat on her bones.
