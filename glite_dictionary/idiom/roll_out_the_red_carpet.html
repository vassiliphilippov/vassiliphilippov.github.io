to roll out the red carpet

1. VERB

/ roʊl aʊt ðə rɛd ˈkɑːrpɪt / (AmE), / rəʊl aʊt ðə rɛd ˈkɑːpɪt / (BrE)
to give someone a very special welcome or treatment, especially as an honored guest
give someone special treatment or welcome

Examples

Whenever a head of state visits, the city rolls out the red carpet to impress them.
For her birthday, her friends decided to roll out the red carpet and make her feel like a star.
The company rolled out the red carpet for new investors at the launch event.

Synset Examples

We should roll out the red carpet for our new clients.
The hotel rolled out the red carpet for their VIP guests.
The award show rolled out the red carpet for its stars.
They rolled out the red carpet for the celebrity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: give someone the royal treatment - to treat someone as if they are very important or special

Etymology

Refers to the literal use of a red carpet for important guests, especially in theaters and for royalty.
