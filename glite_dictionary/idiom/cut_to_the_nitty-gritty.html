to cut to the nitty-gritty

1. VERB

/ kʌt tu ðə ˌnɪtiˈɡrɪti / (AmE), / kʌt tuː ðə ˌnɪtiˈɡrɪti / (BrE)
to start discussing the most important or fundamental details of a subject
get to the most essential details
informal

Examples

Let's cut to the nitty-gritty and discuss the budget for the project.
After a long introduction, the speaker finally cut to the nitty-gritty of the proposal.
If everyone agrees, we can cut to the nitty-gritty and make a decision.

Synset Examples

Let’s cut to the nitty-gritty of our discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get to the point - focus on the main or crucial issue
  • Antonym: to beat around the bush - avoid talking about what is important
