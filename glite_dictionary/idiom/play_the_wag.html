to play the wag

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ðə wæɡ / (AmE), / pleɪ ðə wæɡ / (BrE)
to pretend to be ill or otherwise absent in order to avoid work or duties, especially school
pretend to be absent to avoid duties
british

Examples

She was caught trying to play the wag instead of going to her morning lecture.
Whenever there was a test, Tom would play the wag and not show up to class.
It's tempting to play the wag when the weather is beautiful outside.

Synset Examples

He decided to play the wag and skip the meeting.
Instead of working, he chose to play the wag all day.
She likes to play the wag when she has too much work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to skive - to avoid work or duty by staying away or pretending to be ill
  • Synonym: to play hooky - to stay away from school without permission
  • Antonym: to attend - to be present at a place or event
