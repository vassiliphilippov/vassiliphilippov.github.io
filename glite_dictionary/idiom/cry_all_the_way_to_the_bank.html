to cry all the way to the bank

1. VERB

/ kraɪ ɔl ðə weɪ tə ðə bæŋk / (AmE), / kraɪ ɔːl ðə weɪ tə ðə bæŋk / (BrE)
to make a lot of money from something, especially while appearing to complain or despite criticism or doubt from others
make money despite complaints or criticism

Examples

After critics panned his movie, the director cried all the way to the bank when it became a box office hit.
She might pretend she's unhappy about her contract, but she'll cry all the way to the bank.
Even after the bad reviews, the author cried all the way to the bank as her book topped the sales charts.

Synset Examples

You can cry all the way to the bank after selling that stock.
They’ll cry all the way to the bank with those sales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laugh all the way to the bank - to make a lot of money from something, often when not expected to
