to hit the rocks

1. VERB

/ hɪt ðə ɹɑks / (AmE), / hɪt ðə rɒks / (BrE)
To experience serious problems or difficulties, often leading to failure or collapse, especially in relationships or enterprises.
encounter serious trouble or failure

Examples

Their friendship hit the rocks after several heated disagreements.
Many businesses hit the rocks during the financial crisis of 2008.
If the negotiations hit the rocks, the merger will be called off.

Synset Examples

Their relationship really hit the rocks after that argument.
The business started to hit the rocks during the recession.
We need to turn the boat before we hit the rocks.
After the scandal, the project hit the rocks completely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fall apart - to break into pieces; to fail completely
  • Synonym: to break down - to stop working or functioning; to collapse
