to take the backseat

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə ˈbækˌsiːt / (AmE), / teɪk ðə ˈbækˌsiːt / (BrE)
to assume a less important or subordinate role in an activity or situation
assume a subordinate role

Examples

After Jane became the team leader, Tom chose to take the backseat during meetings.
The seasoned actor had to take the backseat as the younger cast members took center stage.
Sometimes it's wise to take the backseat and let others make the decisions.

Synset Examples

As a sideman, he often took the backseat during performances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take a back seat - play a less prominent or important role
