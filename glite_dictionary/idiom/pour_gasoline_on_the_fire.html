to pour gasoline on the fire

1. VERB

/ pɔr ˈɡæsəliːn ɑn ðə ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / pɔː ˈɡæsəliːn ɒn ðə ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
to do or say something that makes a bad situation worse or intensifies a conflict or problem
make a bad situation worse

Examples

Bringing up his past mistakes would only pour gasoline on the fire during their argument.
If you criticize him now, it will just pour gasoline on the fire.
Her angry response poured gasoline on the fire and made things much worse.

Synset Examples

She knew discussing politics would pour gasoline on the fire.
Bringing up old issues will just pour gasoline on the fire.
His reckless comments poured gasoline on the fire of their conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to add fuel to the fire - to make a bad situation worse
