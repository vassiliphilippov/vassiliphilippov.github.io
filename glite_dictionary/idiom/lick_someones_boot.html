to lick someone's boot

1. VERB

/ lɪk sʌmwʌnz buːt / (AmE), / lɪk ˈsʌmwənz buːt / (BrE)
to flatter or behave in a servile way toward someone in authority, usually to gain personal advantage
flatter or be servile to someone for gain

Examples

He would do anything to get a promotion, even lick someone's boot if necessary.
After the new manager arrived, some employees started licking his boot to impress him.
I'm tired of seeing people lick someone's boot instead of standing up for themselves.

Synset Examples

He would literally lick her boots to gain favor.
She’s always licking the boots of her superiors.
Stop licking the boots of those in power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kowtow - act in an excessively subservient manner
  • Synonym: to suck up - flatter someone for advantage
