what is more

1. ADV

/ wʌt ɪz mɔr / (AmE), / wɒt ɪz mɔː / (BrE)
used to introduce additional information, especially one that is more important or surprising
introduces additional, often stronger, information

Examples

The hotel provided free breakfast and, what is more, offered complimentary transport to the airport.
She managed to finish the project on time; what is more, she exceeded everyone's expectations.
The course is affordable and, what is more, it covers advanced topics rarely taught elsewhere.

Synset Examples

computer chess games are getting cheaper all the time; furthermore, their quality is improving
the cellar was dark; moreover, mice nested there
what is more, there's no sign of a change

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moreover - used to add information
  • Synonym: furthermore - used to add additional point or idea

2. ADV

/ wʌt ɪz mɔr / (AmE), / wɒt ɪz mɔː / (BrE)
used to introduce additional information that makes something even more impressive, surprising, or important; furthermore.
used to add further, stronger information

Examples

She received a promotion; what is more, she was offered a higher salary.
The weather was perfect, and what is more, everyone enjoyed themselves.
He passed the exam on his first try; what is more, he got the highest score in the class.

Synset Examples

She is a talented musician; what is more, she's an excellent teacher.
The event was fun, and what is more, it raised a lot of money.
He finished the project early; what is more, he did it under budget.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furthermore - used to introduce additional information, similar meaning
  • Synonym: moreover - used to add information that supports or emphasizes a previous statement
