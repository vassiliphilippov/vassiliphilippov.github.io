up someone's strasse

1. ADJ

/ ʌp ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈstræs / (AmE), / ʌp ˈsʌmwənz ˈstrɑːs / (BrE)
to be well-suited to someone's interests, tastes, or preferences; to be exactly the sort of thing that appeals to someone
well-suited to someone's tastes/interests
informal british

Examples

That book about vintage cars is really up his strasse.
If you like art films, this festival will be up your strasse.
Cooking shows are completely up her strasse.

Synset Examples

That movie is definitely up his strasse.
This new project is really up my strasse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: up someone's alley - well-suited to someone's tastes or interests

Etymology

From British English slang, with 'strasse' meaning 'street' (borrowed from German 'Straße').
