to write itself

1. VERB

/ raɪt ɪtˈsɛlf / (AmE), / raɪt ɪtˈsɛlf / (BrE)
(of a story, speech, etc.) to develop or compose easily and naturally, without requiring much effort.
develop or compose effortlessly

Examples

The novel's characters were so vivid that the story seemed to write itself.
Once the main idea was clear, the speech almost wrote itself.
With so many interesting facts, the article practically wrote itself.

Synset Examples

The plot was so compelling, it seemed to write itself.
This presentation will write itself if we follow the outline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: come naturally - to happen easily or without effort
