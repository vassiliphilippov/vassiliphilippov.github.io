paper tiger

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪpər ˈtaɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpə ˈtaɪɡə / (BrE)
a person, organization, or country that appears powerful or threatening but is actually ineffectual or powerless
person or thing that appears threatening but isn't

Examples

Many believed the dictator was a threat, but in reality he was just a paper tiger.
Despite its fearsome reputation, the army turned out to be a paper tiger in actual combat.
Analysts now refer to the once-mighty company as a paper tiger after its recent failures.

Synset Examples

he reminded Mao that the paper tiger had nuclear teeth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toothless - lacking real power or effectiveness
  • Antonym: powerhouse - a person, organization, or country with great power or influence

Etymology

Calque of Chinese zhǐ lǎohǔ (纸老虎), popularized in English by Mao Zedong in the mid-20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪpər ˈtaɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpə ˈtaɪɡə / (BrE)
a person or thing that seems threatening but is actually powerless or ineffectual
one who appears threatening but is ineffectual

Examples

Many believed the dictator was formidable, but he proved to be a paper tiger when challenged.
Environmental regulations without enforcement are seen as a paper tiger by activists.
Her threats sounded serious, but everyone knew she was just a paper tiger.

Synset Examples

The company's threats turned out to be a paper tiger.
His bravado was just a paper tiger; he was afraid.
Critics labeled the new policy a paper tiger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toothless - having no real power or ability to act
  • Antonym: powerful - having great power or strength

Etymology

Translation of Chinese zhǐ lǎohǔ (纸老虎), first popularized in English by Mao Zedong in the 1950s.
