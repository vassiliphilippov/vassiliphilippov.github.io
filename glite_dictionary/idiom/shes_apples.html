she's apples

1. X

/ ʃiz ˈæpəlz / (AmE), / ʃiːz ˈæpəlz / (BrE)
(Australian, informal) Used to indicate that everything is fine or satisfactory; there are no problems.
everything is fine; no problems (Aus. idiom)
informal british

Examples

Don't worry about the mistake; she's apples and no harm done.
I checked the engine and she's apples, so we're good to go.
Even though it rained, the picnic was still great—she's apples!

Synset Examples

He said the project is behind, but she's apples.
No need to stress; everything's she's apples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: no worries - (Australian) used to indicate there is no problem
  • Synonym: all good - everything is fine or okay

Etymology

Australian slang, origin uncertain; possibly a playful alteration of "she's apple-pie" (meaning "perfect").
