to cast the first stone

1. VERB

/ kæst ðə fɝst stoʊn / (AmE), / kɑːst ðə fɜːst stəʊn / (BrE)
to be the first to criticize or accuse someone, especially of a fault that the critic may also have
to be the first to criticize or accuse

Examples

Before you cast the first stone, remember that you have made similar mistakes yourself.
He hesitated to cast the first stone, fearing he was not blameless either.
No one in the meeting was willing to cast the first stone against the manager.

Synset Examples

In this situation, no one should cast the first stone.
Let he who is without sin cast the first stone.
She refused to cast the first stone at her friend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to criticize - to express disapproval or find fault with someone or something
  • Synonym: to accuse - to say that someone has done something wrong or illegal

Etymology

From the Biblical story in John 8:7, "Let him who is without sin cast the first stone."
