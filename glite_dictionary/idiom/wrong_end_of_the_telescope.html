wrong end of the telescope

1. NOUN

/ rɔŋ ɛnd əv ðə tɛlɪˌskoʊp / (AmE), / rɒŋ end əv ðə ˈtelɪskəʊp / (BrE)
used to describe a way of looking at a situation that is distorted or misguided, as if seeing things in a reversed or incorrect way
distorted or misguided perspective

Examples

When you analyze only the problems without considering the progress, you're looking at things through the wrong end of the telescope.
The company's strategy seemed like it was devised through the wrong end of the telescope, focusing on minor issues while ignoring the big picture.
If you judge his entire career by one mistake, you're seeing it through the wrong end of the telescope.

Synset Examples

He viewed the situation through the wrong end of the telescope.
They were looking at the problem through the wrong end of the telescope.
Her perspective was skewed, seeing it through the wrong end of the telescope.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: see through rose-colored glasses - to see things in an overly positive or unrealistic way
