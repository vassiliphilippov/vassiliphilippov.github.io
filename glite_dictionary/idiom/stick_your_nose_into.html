to stick your nose into

1. VERB

/ stɪk jʊr noʊz ˈɪntu / (AmE), / stɪk jə nəʊz ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
to interfere in someone else's affairs, especially when it is unwanted or inappropriate.
interfere in others' affairs

Examples

I wish you wouldn't stick your nose into things that don't concern you.
Neighbors who stick their nose into other people's problems often become unpopular.
She tends to stick her nose into office politics, even when it's not her place.

Synset Examples

Please don’t stick your nose into my personal life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to meddle - to interfere in something that is not one's concern
  • Antonym: to mind your own business - to not interfere in someone else's affairs
