to one's trumps

1. X

/ tə wʌnz trʌmps / (AmE), / tə wʌnz trʌmps / (BrE)
to be forced to use one's best resources or strongest means in a difficult situation
be forced to use best resources

Examples

The sudden change in plans put the entire team to their trumps.
Finding herself low on funds, she was finally driven to her trumps.
When negotiations stalled, the company was put to its trumps to secure a deal.

Synset Examples

The crisis put the leader to her trumps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resourceful - able to deal effectively with difficult situations

Etymology

From the card game 'trumps', where a trump card is the highest-ranking card. Used figuratively from the 17th century.
