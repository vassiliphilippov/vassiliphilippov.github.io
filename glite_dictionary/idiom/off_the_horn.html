off the horn

1. ADV

/ ɑf ðə hɔrn / (AmE), / ɒf ðə hɔːn / (BrE)
to communicate informally or casually, especially by telephone
communicate informally by phone
informal

Examples

I just spoke to him off the horn about the schedule changes.
Can we touch base off the horn later this afternoon?
They made most of the arrangements off the horn so nothing was in writing.

Synset Examples

He called me off the horn about our plans.
I’ll let you know my thoughts off the horn.
She prefers to discuss matters off the horn.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: on the horn - to be using the telephone (informal)
