to walk down a yellow brick road

1. VERB

/ wɔk daʊn ə ˈjɛloʊ brɪk roʊd / (AmE), / wɔːk daʊn ə ˈjɛləʊ brɪk rəʊd / (BrE)
to embark on a journey or life experience filled with adventure, challenges, and often self-discovery, especially one with an uncertain or fantastical outcome.
begin a challenging, adventurous journey

Examples

After losing his job, he decided to walk down a yellow brick road in search of a new passion.
Starting a business can feel like you are walking down a yellow brick road, not knowing what lies ahead.
Their move to the city marked the moment they walked down a yellow brick road together into an unknown future.

Synset Examples

Their journey felt like walking down a yellow brick road.

Etymology

Alludes to the road Dorothy follows in L. Frank Baum's 'The Wonderful Wizard of Oz' (1900).
