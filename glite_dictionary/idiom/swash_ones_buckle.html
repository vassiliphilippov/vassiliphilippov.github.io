to swash one's buckle

1. VERB

/ swɑʃ wʌnz ˈbʌkəl / (AmE), / swɒʃ wʌnz ˈbʌkəl / (BrE)
to boast, show off, or use charm or flamboyance to impress someone
boast, show off, or charm someone

Examples

Every time he enters the room, he tries to swash his buckle to impress everyone.
She has a way of swashing her buckle when meeting new people, making them feel special.
You don't need to swash your buckle; just be yourself and they'll like you.

Synset Examples

He loves to swash my buckle with compliments every day.
It's nice when he swashes my buckle with sweet gestures.
She knows how to swash his buckle and make him smile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swashbuckle - behave in a daring, adventurous way; act like a swashbuckler

Etymology

Humorous variation of 'swashbuckle,' modeled on the words 'swash' and 'buckle.'
