armchair quarterback

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrmˌtʃɛr ˈkwɔrtɚˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈɑːmtʃɛə ˈkwɔːtəbæk / (BrE)
a person who offers advice or criticism about situations, especially sports or politics, without having actual experience or direct involvement
person giving uninformed advice or criticism
american

Examples

During the playoffs, every fan seemed to become an armchair quarterback with strong opinions about the coach's strategy.
It's easy to be an armchair quarterback and criticize, but much harder to make decisions on the field.
She grew tired of listening to armchair quarterbacks second-guess her every move at work.

Synset Examples

Everyone became an armchair quarterback during the game.
Critics often sound like armchair quarterbacks on social media.
He always acted like an armchair quarterback after a loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Monday-morning quarterback - person who criticizes with the benefit of hindsight

Etymology

From 'armchair' (suggesting comfort and lack of involvement) + 'quarterback' (the leader on an American football team); popularized in 20th-century America.
