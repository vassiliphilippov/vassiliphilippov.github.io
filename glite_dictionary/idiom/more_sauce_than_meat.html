more sauce than meat

1. X

/ mɔr sɔs ðən mit / (AmE), / mɔː sɔːs ðən miːt / (BrE)
used to describe something that lacks real substance or importance, or a situation where there is more show or presentation than actual content
lacking substance or real importance

Examples

Her speech had more sauce than meat, impressing the crowd but offering few real solutions.
The movie was entertaining, but honestly, it was more sauce than meat.
When reviewing his proposal, the board felt it was more sauce than meat and asked for more concrete details.

Synset Examples

I prefer my pasta with more sauce than meat.
His argument was more sauce than meat and lacked substance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: all sizzle and no steak - more style or talk than actual substance
