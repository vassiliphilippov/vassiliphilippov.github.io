to fall off one's chair

1. VERB

/ fɔl ɔf wʌnz tʃɛr / (AmE), / fɔːl ɒf wʌnz tʃeə / (BrE)
to be extremely surprised or amused
to be very surprised or amused

Examples

When I heard the announcement, I almost fell off my chair in disbelief.
His impression of the boss was so accurate that I fell off my chair laughing.
You could have fallen off your chair when you saw the surprise party.

Synset Examples

The news made me fall off my chair in surprise.
When he told the joke, I nearly fell off my chair laughing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be floored - to be greatly surprised or shocked
  • Synonym: be taken aback - to be very surprised by something unexpected

2. VERB

/ fɔl ɔf wʌnz tʃɛr / (AmE), / fɔːl ɒf wʌnz tʃɛə / (BrE)

be extremely surprised

Examples

You’ll fall off your chair when you hear how much she won in the lottery.
We nearly fell off our chairs laughing at his hilarious joke.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
