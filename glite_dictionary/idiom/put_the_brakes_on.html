to put the brakes on

1. VERB

/ pʊt ðə breɪks ɑn / (AmE), / pʊt ðə breɪks ɒn / (BrE)
to slow down, limit, or put a stop to an activity, process, or development
to slow or stop an activity

Examples

After noticing declining sales, the company decided to put the brakes on hiring new staff.
You should put the brakes on your sugar intake if you want to improve your health.
The government announced plans to put the brakes on further tax cuts.

Synset Examples

He decided to put the brakes on his spending.
We need to put the brakes on this project.
She put the brakes on their plans for a trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hold back - to prevent development or progress
  • Antonym: to speed up - to increase the rate or progress of something

Etymology

Figurative use of 'brakes' as a metaphor for stopping or slowing a vehicle, first recorded in early 20th century English.
