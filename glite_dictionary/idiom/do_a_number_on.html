to do a number on

1. VERB

/ du ə ˈnʌmbɚ ɑn / (AmE), / duː ə ˈnʌmbə ɒn / (BrE)
to cause significant harm or damage to someone or something, often mentally, physically, or emotionally
cause harm or damage
informal

Examples

The hailstorm really did a number on their car, leaving dozens of dents and scratches.
Years of stress have done a number on his health.
Social media can do a number on teenagers' self-esteem if used without caution.

Synset Examples

The storm did a number on our roof.
That criticism really did a number on his confidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damage - physical harm that impairs value or usefulness
  • Synonym: harm - physical or mental injury; damage

2. VERB

/ duː ə ˈnʌmbər ɑn / (AmE), / duː ə ˈnʌmbə ɒn / (BrE)
to have a damaging or harmful effect on someone or something
have a harmful effect
informal

Examples

The recession really did a number on small businesses in the area.
Stress can do a number on your immune system over time.
Years of poor maintenance have done a number on the old bridge.

Synset Examples

The long hours did a number on her health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harm - physical or mental damage or injury
  • Antonym: benefit - an advantage or profit gained from something
