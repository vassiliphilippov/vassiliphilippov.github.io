to risk it for the biscuit

1. VERB

/ ˈrɪsk ɪt fər ðə bəˈskɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɪsk ɪt fə ðə bɪˈskɪt / (BrE)
(informal) To take a chance or risk something in order to achieve a possible reward.
take a risk for a reward (informal)
informal

Examples

She knew it was a long shot, but decided to risk it for the biscuit and apply for the top job.
Sometimes in life you just have to risk it for the biscuit if you want to succeed.
They laughed and said, "Let's risk it for the biscuit and try the new business venture."

Synset Examples

If you want success, you must risk it for the biscuit.
I decided to risk it for the biscuit and invest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take a chance - to do something risky in hopes of success
  • Antonym: to play it safe - to avoid risk or danger
