to raise the bar

1. VERB

/ ˈreɪz ðə bɑr / (AmE), / ˈreɪz ðə bɑː / (BrE)
to set a higher standard or level of expectation, particularly in comparison to previous standards
set a higher standard or expectation

Examples

Her outstanding performance will raise the bar for all future employees.
The company hopes its new phone will raise the bar in terms of design and functionality.
By incorporating sustainability, the architects managed to raise the bar for future building projects.

Synset Examples

Their innovative ideas will raise the bar for competitors.
The new project aims to raise the bar for quality.
We need to raise the bar if we want to succeed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: set the bar - to establish a standard of quality or achievement

Etymology

From the literal use in sports such as high jump or pole vault, where raising the bar increases the challenge.
