to cash in one's chips

1. VERB

/ kæʃ ɪn wʌnz tʃɪps / (AmE), / kæʃ ɪn wʌnz tʃɪps / (BrE)
(informal, humorous) to die; to cease living.
to die (humorous, informal)
informal

Examples

After a long illness, he finally cashed in his chips last night.
Some people say you should travel as much as possible before you cash in your chips.
The detective learned that the suspect had cashed in his chips a week ago.

Synset Examples

She died from cancer
The children perished in the fire
The patient went peacefully
The old guy kicked the bucket at the age of 102

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to kick the bucket - to die (informal idiom)
  • Synonym: to pass away - to die (euphemistic)
  • Synonym: die - to stop living; to cease life

Etymology

From gambling, where 'cashing in one's chips' means exchanging chips for money when leaving the game; figuratively, leaving life.

2. VERB

/ kæʃ ɪn wʌnz tʃɪps / (AmE), / kæʃ ɪn wʌnz tʃɪps / (BrE)
to use one's resources, assets, or advantages, often to gain a benefit or exit from an activity; also, to give up or quit, sometimes with the implication of taking whatever gains are available at the time.
to use resources or quit for benefit

Examples

After years of dedication, she decided to cash in her chips and retire early.
When the company was sold, many long-time employees chose to cash in their chips.
Realizing the opportunity, he finally cashed in his chips and moved abroad.

Synset Examples

After years of hard work, she cashed in her chips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bow out - to leave a job or position; to withdraw or quit

Etymology

From gambling, where 'cashing in one's chips' means exchanging chips for money; figurative use dates from the 20th century.
