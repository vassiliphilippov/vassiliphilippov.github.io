to take to one's heels

1. VERB

/ teɪk tu wʌnz hilz / (AmE), / teɪk tʊ wʌnz hiːlz / (BrE)
to run away quickly, especially to escape from danger or trouble
run away quickly; flee

Examples

The moment the police arrived, he took to his heels and disappeared into the crowd.
When the dog started barking fiercely, the children took to their heels.
Frightened by the sudden noise, she took to her heels without looking back.

Synset Examples

Seeing the bear, he took to his heels immediately.
She took to her heels when the alarm sounded.
When the storm hit, we took to our heels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flee - to run away from danger or threat quickly
  • Synonym: escape - to get away from a place or situation of danger

Etymology

Refers metaphorically to using one's heels (feet) to run from danger; 'heels' for feet dates to early English idioms.
