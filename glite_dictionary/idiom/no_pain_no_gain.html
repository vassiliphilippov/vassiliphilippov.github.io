no pain, no gain

1. X

/ noʊ peɪn, noʊ ɡeɪn / (AmE), / nəʊ peɪn, nəʊ ɡeɪn / (BrE)
used to express the idea that achieving something worthwhile often requires hard work or suffering
success requires effort or hardship

Examples

As my coach always says, no pain, no gain—so I push myself at every practice.
He muttered no pain, no gain as he started his third attempt at the difficult puzzle.
Dieting can be tough, but no pain, no gain if you want results.

Synset Examples

If you want to get fit, remember: no pain, no gain.
In business, you often find that no pain, no gain applies to success.
She reminded him that no pain, no gain when he complained about the workout.

Etymology

Popularized in the 1980s as an exercise motto, but uses older proverbial structure.
