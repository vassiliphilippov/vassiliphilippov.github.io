hoist by one's own petard

1. X

/ hɔɪst baɪ wʌnz oʊn pəˈtɑrd / (AmE), / hɔɪst baɪ wʌnz əʊn pəˈtɑːd / (BrE)
to be harmed or caught by one's own scheme or device intended to harm others
to be hurt by one's own scheme
archaic

Examples

She was hoist by her own petard when her deception was discovered.
The corrupt politician was hoist by his own petard when evidence surfaced against him.
Trying to sabotage the project, he ended up hoist by his own petard.

Synset Examples

She was hoist by her own petard after lying about her credentials.
He was hoist by his own petard when his plan backfired.
They were hoist by their own petard when their scheme failed.

Etymology

From Shakespeare's Hamlet. 'Petard' is from French pétard, an explosive device, ultimately from Old French peter (to break wind).
