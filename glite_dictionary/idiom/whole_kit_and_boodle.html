whole kit and boodle

1. NOUN

/ hoʊl kɪt ənd ˈbudl̩ / (AmE), / həʊl kɪt ənd ˈbuːdl̩ / (BrE)
everything; the entirety of a group or collection, usually preceded by 'the'.
the entire group or collection
informal

Examples

We packed up the whole kit and boodle and moved to the countryside.
If you buy the deluxe edition, you get the whole kit and boodle.
At the garage sale, she sold the whole kit and boodle for just fifty dollars.

Synset Examples

we saw the whole shebang
a hotdog with the works
we took on the whole caboodle
for $10 you get the full treatment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whole shebang - the entire thing; everything involved
  • Synonym: the works - everything that can be included
  • Synonym: kit and caboodle - the whole lot; everything

Etymology

Formed from the blend of 'kit and caboodle', where 'kit' refers to a collection and 'boodle' derives from Dutch 'boedel' meaning 'property'.
