to tear hair out

1. VERB

/ tɛr hɛr aʊt / (AmE), / tɪə heə aʊt / (BrE)
to become extremely frustrated or to express intense frustration, often because of anxiety or helplessness
to be extremely frustrated

Examples

By the end of the project, I was ready to tear my hair out from stress.
She nearly tore her hair out over the children's constant fighting.
Trying to fix the computer had him tearing his hair out for hours.

Synset Examples

I was about to tear my hair out trying to find my keys.
He always tears his hair out when deadlines approach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lose one's temper - to become very angry or upset suddenly
