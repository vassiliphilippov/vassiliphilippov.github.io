to come out of the closet

1. VERB

/ kʌm aʊt əv ðə ˈklɑːzət / (AmE), / kʌm aʊt əv ðə ˈklɒzɪt / (BrE)
to state openly and publicly that one is homosexual, often after having kept it secret
declare one's homosexuality publicly

Examples

After years of hiding his true self, Mark decided to come out of the closet to his family.
She finally felt ready to come out of the closet at the school assembly.
Many celebrities have chosen to come out of the closet and share their stories publicly.

Synset Examples

This actor outed last year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to come out - to openly acknowledge one's homosexuality

Etymology

From 'closet' meaning a private room; later metaphorically representing secrecy, especially in 'skeleton in the closet.'

2. VERB

/ kʌm aʊt əv ðə ˈklɑzɪt / (AmE), / kʌm aʊt əv ðə ˈklɒzɪt / (BrE)
to publicly reveal one's sexual orientation, especially when previously kept private
reveal one's sexual orientation publicly

Examples

After years of hiding his true self, he finally decided to come out of the closet.
Many celebrities have chosen to come out of the closet to inspire others.
She felt liberated once she had come out of the closet to her family.

Synset Examples

He was nervous to come out of the closet.
It's important to support those who come out of the closet.
She encouraged him to come out of the closet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to come out - to reveal or admit to something previously hidden, especially one's sexual orientation

Etymology

From the metaphorical use of 'closet' to mean secrecy or hiding, originating in the late 20th century in the context of LGBTQ+ identity.
