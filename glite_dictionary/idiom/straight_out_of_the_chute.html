straight out of the chute

1. ADV

/ streɪt aʊt əv ðə tʃuːt / (AmE), / streɪt aʊt əv ðə tʃuːt / (BrE)
immediately or directly from the very beginning of an activity or process
immediately from the start

Examples

He made it clear straight out of the chute that he intended to lead the team.
The book captures your attention straight out of the chute with a shocking first chapter.
They identified the major issues straight out of the chute rather than waiting until later meetings.

Synset Examples

The project started straight out of the chute.
They came straight out of the chute with new ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: right off the bat - immediately from the beginning
  • Synonym: from the get-go - from the very start

Etymology

Originally from rodeo terminology, where a 'chute' is a stall holding an animal before a ride or race.
