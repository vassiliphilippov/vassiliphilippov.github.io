to raise to the purple

1. VERB

/ ˈreɪz tə ðə ˈpɝːpəl / (AmE), / ˈreɪz tə ðə ˈpɜːpl̩ / (BrE)
to attain a high-ranking or prestigious position, especially one of royalty or great power
attain a position of great prestige or power

Examples

After years of dedication and ambition, she was finally raised to the purple within the company.
Many aspired to raise to the purple, but only a select few achieved such distinction.
He dreamed of one day being raised to the purple, just like his ancestors before him.

Synset Examples

She worked hard to raise to the purple among her peers.
Only a few are chosen to raise to the purple in this organization.
He was promoted and raised to the purple last week.

Etymology

From the association of the color purple with royalty and high office in ancient Rome.
