to be on one's toes

1. VERB

/ bi ɑn wʌnz toʊz / (AmE), / bi ɒn wʌnz təʊz / (BrE)
to be alert and ready to deal with anything that might happen
be alert and ready

Examples

Working in a busy restaurant keeps me on my toes every night.
As a parent of young children, you have to be on your toes at all times.
The new manager always challenges us, so we remain on our toes.

Synset Examples

To succeed, you need to be on your toes at all times.
Teachers often keep students on their toes with surprise quizzes.
Her minxship kept him on his toes all night.
In this job, you must stay on your toes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be alert - to be watchful and ready for possible danger or opportunity
  • Antonym: be off guard - to be unprepared or not paying attention
