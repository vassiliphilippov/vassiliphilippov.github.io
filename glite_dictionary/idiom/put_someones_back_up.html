to put someone's back up

1. VERB

/ pʊt sʌmˌwʌnz bæk ʌp / (AmE), / pʊt sʌmwənz bæk ʌp / (BrE)
to make someone angry or defensive
make someone angry or defensive

Examples

Her criticism really put my back up during the meeting.
The way he spoke to me put my back up immediately.
If you keep interrupting, you'll just put her back up again.

Synset Examples

His rude comments really put my back up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to annoy - to make someone a little angry or irritated
  • Synonym: to provoke - to cause a strong reaction, typically anger

Etymology

From the image of a cat arching its back when threatened.
