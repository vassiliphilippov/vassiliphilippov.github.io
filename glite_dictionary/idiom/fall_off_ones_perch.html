to fall off one's perch

1. VERB

/ fɑl ɑf wʌnz pɝtʃ / (AmE), / fɔːl ɒf wʌnz pɜːtʃ / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly British) To die or to lose one's position or status, often in a sudden or unexpected way.
to die or lose status (informal, UK)
informal british

Examples

Many believe the manager will fall off his perch if the team loses again.
When the old judge finally fell off his perch, there was a huge media response.
After losing the championship, the team fell off their perch in the rankings.

Synset Examples

The old actor fell off his perch last year.
After years in the industry, he finally fell off his perch.
When the company went under, many fell off their perch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to kick the bucket - to die (informal idiom)
  • Synonym: lose one's grip - to lose control or one's authoritative position

Etymology

From the humorous image of a caged bird falling off its perch when it dies; first half of the 20th century, British slang.
