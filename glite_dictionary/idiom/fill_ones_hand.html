to fill one's hand

1. VERB

/ fɪl wʌnz hænd / (AmE), / fɪl wʌnz hænd / (BrE)
to gather or hold as much of something as can fit in one's hand
gather or hold as much as a hand can hold

Examples

She managed to fill her hand with wildflowers during her morning walk.
He bent down to fill his hand with small pebbles from the shore.
The child tried to fill his hand with candy from the jar.

Synset Examples

She filled her hand with the ripe apples from the orchard.
He filled his hand with cash after selling his old bike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grasp - to seize and hold firmly
  • Synonym: clutch - to hold tightly, often in the hand
