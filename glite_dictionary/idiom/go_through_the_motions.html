to go through the motions

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˌθru ðə ˈmoʊʃənz / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˌθruː ðə ˈməʊʃənz / (BrE)
to do something without genuine effort or enthusiasm, merely for the sake of appearance or routine
do something mechanically or without sincere effort

Examples

After years in the job, he was just going through the motions at work.
She smiled and nodded during the meeting, but it was clear she was only going through the motions.
The team lost motivation and simply went through the motions during practice.

Synset Examples

She isn't really working — she's just going through the motions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fake it - pretend to have a certain feeling or ability
  • Synonym: phoning it in - perform a task with little effort or enthusiasm

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˌθru ðə ˈmoʊʃənz / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˌθruː ðə ˈməʊʃənz / (BrE)
to do something in a mechanical or unenthusiastic way, without genuine effort or feeling
do something without enthusiasm

Examples

Despite his promotion, he continued to go through the motions at work, rarely showing any enthusiasm.
During rehearsals, the actors seemed tired and only went through the motions rather than truly performing.
After years in the same routine, she often found herself going through the motions in her daily life.

Synset Examples

He’s like a zombie banker, just going through the motions.
They were only going through the motions of a relationship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretend - to behave as if something is the case when it is not
  • Synonym: fake - to simulate or feign something
