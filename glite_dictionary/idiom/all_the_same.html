all the same

1. ADV

/ ɔl ðə seɪm / (AmE), / ɔːl ðə seɪm / (BrE)
used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or seems to contradict something that has just been said; nevertheless; in spite of that
nevertheless; despite that

Examples

I know you don’t like horror movies, but all the same you should try this one.
She was tired after work; all the same, she went to the gym.
It might rain tomorrow, but all the same, we’re going hiking.

Synset Examples

although I'm a little afraid, however I'd like to try it
while we disliked each other, nevertheless we agreed
he was a stern yet fair master
granted that it is dangerous, all the same I still want to go

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nevertheless - in spite of that; nonetheless
  • Synonym: nonetheless - despite what has just been said; however

2. ADV

/ ɔl ðə seɪm / (AmE), / ɔːl ðə seɪm / (BrE)
used to indicate that something is true or relevant despite what has just been said; nevertheless.
nevertheless; in spite of that

Examples

She had been warned about the risks, but she went ahead all the same.
It's expensive, but I want to buy it all the same.
He apologized for being late, and I forgave him all the same.

Synset Examples

I didn't like the movie, but it's a classic all the same.
She was late, but I appreciated her effort all the same.
Let's skip the party; if it's all the same, I'm tired.
He failed the test, but I respect him all the same.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nevertheless - in spite of that; however
  • Synonym: nonetheless - in spite of that; nevertheless
  • Synonym: regardless - despite the prevailing circumstances
