on the contrary

1. ADV

/ ɑn ðə ˈkɑnˌtrɛri / (AmE), / ɒn ðə ˈkɒntrəri / (BrE)
used to introduce a statement that says the opposite of what has just been said or that corrects a mistaken idea
introducing a statement with the opposite meaning

Examples

I don't dislike classical music; on the contrary, I listen to it every day.
Many people think the test is easy; on the contrary, it's quite challenging.
He wasn't offended by the comment; on the contrary, he laughed about it.

Synset Examples

he didn't stay home; on the contrary, he went out with his friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in contrast - used to indicate a difference between two things or situations
