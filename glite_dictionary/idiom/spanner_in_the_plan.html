spanner in the plan

1. NOUN

/ ˈspænər ɪn ðə plæn / (AmE), / ˈspænə ɪn ðə plæn / (BrE)
an unexpected problem or obstacle that disrupts or hinders progress or plans
unexpected problem disrupting plans
british

Examples

The sudden weather change threw a real spanner in the plan for our outdoor event.
His illness put a spanner in the plan for the team's project launch.
Budget cuts have become a major spanner in the plan for development.

Synset Examples

Her unexpected resignation was a real spanner in the plan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spanner in the works - unexpected obstacle causing disruption
  • Synonym: monkey wrench in the works - American equivalent phrase for an unexpected problem

Etymology

From British English 'spanner' (wrench), by analogy to machinery being stopped by a spanner dropped into it. Variant of 'spanner in the works'.
