to wash someone's mouth out with soap

1. VERB

/ wɑʃ sʌmˌwʌnz maʊθ aʊt wɪð soʊp / (AmE), / wɒʃ sʌmˌwʌnz maʊθ aʊt wɪð səʊp / (BrE)
to punish someone, especially a child, by cleaning their mouth with soap as a response to swearing or using bad language
punish for swearing by cleaning mouth with soap

Examples

If you keep using that language, I'll wash your mouth out with soap.
Her parents threatened to wash her mouth out with soap after she swore at the dinner table.
He joked that someone should wash his mouth out with soap for his rude comment.

Synset Examples

Her mom threatened to wash her mouth out with soap for swearing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scold - to angrily criticize someone, especially for bad behavior
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration for someone
