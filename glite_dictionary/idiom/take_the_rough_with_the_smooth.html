to take the rough with the smooth

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə rʌf wɪð ðə smuð / (AmE), / teɪk ðə rʌf wɪð ðə smuːð / (BrE)
to accept both the unpleasant and pleasant aspects of a situation or life
accept good and bad experiences

Examples

Learning to take the rough with the smooth helped her cope with the ups and downs of parenting.
Successful athletes know they must take the rough with the smooth throughout their careers.
If we want this partnership to work, we'll have to take the rough with the smooth together.

Synset Examples

He learned to take the rough with the smooth in his new job.
To succeed, sometimes you have to take the rough with the smooth.
In life, you must take the rough with the smooth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accept - agree to receive or deal with something
  • Synonym: to grin and bear it - put up with something unpleasant without complaining
