to take one day at a time

1. VERB

/ teɪk wʌn deɪ ət ə taɪm / (AmE), / teɪk wʌn deɪ ət ə taɪm / (BrE)
to approach challenges or uncertainties by focusing only on each day as it comes, rather than worrying about the future
focus on today, not the future

Examples

After losing his job, he decided to take one day at a time and not stress about what comes next.
During her illness, she learned it was best to take one day at a time instead of getting overwhelmed.
When life feels too much, remember to take one day at a time and focus on small steps.

Synset Examples

I’m trying to take one day at a time during this tough period.
She learned to take one day at a time in her recovery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: live for the moment - focus on the present rather than worrying about the future
