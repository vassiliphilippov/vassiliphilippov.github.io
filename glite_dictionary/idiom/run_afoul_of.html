to run afoul of

1. VERB

/ rʌn əˈfaʊl ʌv / (AmE), / rʌn əˈfaʊl ɒv / (BrE)
to come into conflict with or get into trouble with rules, laws, or someone in authority
come into conflict with rules or the law

Examples

The company ran afoul of environmental regulations and was fined heavily.
He ran afoul of local customs while traveling in the remote village.
Several politicians have run afoul of ethics rules in recent years.

Synset Examples

Her comments ran afoul of company policy.
They ran afoul of the regulations and faced penalties.
He ran afoul of the law during the protest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fall foul of - to get into trouble or conflict with someone or something

Etymology

From 'afoul', meaning entangled or in conflict, from nautical use in the 17th century.
