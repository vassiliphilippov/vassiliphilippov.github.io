hi pot, meet kettle

1. X

/ haɪ pɑt, mit ˈkɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / haɪ pɒt, miːt ˈkɛtl̩ / (BrE)
used to highlight hypocrisy, especially when someone criticizes another for a fault they themselves possess
highlighting hypocrisy in criticism

Examples

When Jane called Mark forgetful, it was a case of hi pot, meet kettle.
He laughed at her disorganization, but she shot back, 'hi pot, meet kettle!'
Whenever people criticize each other's flaws, someone inevitably says, 'hi pot, meet kettle.'

Synset Examples

He complained about her messy room; oh, 'hi pot, meet kettle.'
She called him lazy; I couldn't help but think 'hi pot, meet kettle.'
When he criticized her for being late, I thought, 'hi pot, meet kettle.'
When he criticized her lateness, she replied, 'Pot, meet kettle!'
It's funny how he complains about spending; pot, meet kettle.
He called her messy, but pot, meet kettle!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the pot calling the kettle black - an idiom meaning someone criticizing another for a fault they also have

Etymology

Based on the expression 'the pot calling the kettle black', referencing old-fashioned cookware that would blacken with soot, symbolizing shared faults.
