to feel like hell

1. VERB

/ fil laɪk hɛl / (AmE), / fiːl laɪk hɛl / (BrE)
to experience severe discomfort, pain, or distress; to feel extremely unwell or miserable
feel extremely unwell or distressed
informal

Examples

After running the marathon in the heat, I felt like hell for the next two days.
She always feels like hell when she has the flu.
He admitted he was feeling like hell after staying up all night working.

Synset Examples

It felt like hell waiting for the storm to pass.
The hail felt like hell from above during the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feel awful - to feel very bad or unwell
  • Antonym: feel great - to feel extremely well
