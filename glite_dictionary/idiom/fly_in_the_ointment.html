fly in the ointment

1. NOUN

/ flaɪ ɪn ði ˈɔɪntmənt / (AmE), / flaɪ ɪn ði ˈɔɪntmənt / (BrE)
a small problem or flaw that spoils an otherwise satisfactory situation
minor flaw that mars something good

Examples

The only fly in the ointment was the lack of parking near the venue.
Everything about the vacation was perfect, but the delayed flight was a fly in the ointment.
Her new job was ideal; the long commute was the fly in the ointment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drawback - a disadvantage or problem
  • Antonym: benefit - an advantage or something good

Etymology

Refers to the literal idea of a fly contaminating ointment, first recorded in English in the 17th century; from the Biblical phrase in Ecclesiastes 10:1.

2. NOUN

/ flaɪ ɪn ði ˈɔɪntmənt / (AmE), / flaɪ ɪn ði ˈɔɪntmənt / (BrE)
a small problem or flaw that lessens the overall quality or enjoyment of something
minor flaw that spoils something good

Examples

Although the conference went smoothly, the late cancellation of one speaker was a fly in the ointment.
Her kind remarks were a fly in the ointment during an otherwise tense meeting.
We loved the new house, but a leaking roof was the fly in the ointment.

Synset Examples

Everything was perfect, except for one fly in the ointment.
The delay was just a small fly in the ointment of our plans.
The only fly in the ointment was the unexpected rain.

Etymology

From a biblical reference in Ecclesiastes 10:1 about dead flies causing ointment to stink, adopted into English as an idiom in the 17th century.
