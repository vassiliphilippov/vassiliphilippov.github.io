to get up with the chickens

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ʌp wɪð ðə ˈtʃɪkɪnz / (AmE), / ɡɛt ʌp wɪð ðə ˈtʃɪkɪnz / (BrE)
to wake up very early in the morning, especially at or before dawn
wake up very early in the morning

Examples

I have to get up with the chickens for my morning shift at the bakery.
If you get up with the chickens, you might enjoy a quiet hour before everyone else wakes up.
On weekends, I refuse to get up with the chickens and prefer to sleep in.

Synset Examples

If you get up with the chickens, you can beat the rush.
She likes to get up with the chickens to enjoy the sunrise.
He’s always up with the chickens to catch the sunrise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rise with the lark - wake up very early in the morning

Etymology

From the habit of chickens rising at dawn, used figuratively since at least the 19th century.
