in tatters

1. X

/ ɪn ˈtætɚz / (AmE), / ɪn ˈtætəz / (BrE)
in a very bad condition because something has been damaged, destroyed, or is in a state of severe disarray
in a ruined or damaged state

Examples

After the storm, the old barn was in tatters and barely standing.
When the company went bankrupt, employees' hopes for the future were in tatters.
Her dress was so worn out that it was practically in tatters.

Synset Examples

His reputation was in tatters after the scandal.
The plan fell apart, and now it's all in tatters.
After the argument, their friendship was left in tatters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in ruins - in a severely damaged or destroyed state
  • Synonym: wrecked - thoroughly destroyed or severely damaged
