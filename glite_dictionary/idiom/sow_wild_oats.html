to sow wild oats

1. VERB

/ soʊ waɪld oʊts / (AmE), / səʊ waɪld əʊts / (BrE)
to behave in a reckless or carefree way, especially when young, often by having many romantic or sexual adventures before settling down
behave recklessly or adventurously when young

Examples

Many people believe it is healthy to sow wild oats before starting a family.
He spent several years traveling and sowing wild oats before focusing on his career.
After college, she wanted a chance to sow wild oats abroad before returning home.

Synset Examples

She felt it was time to sow her wild oats in Europe.
College is a great time to sow one's wild oats.
He decided to travel and sow his wild oats before settling down.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to settle down - to begin to live a quieter, more stable life

Etymology

Comes from the literal phrase of planting wild oats, which are unproductive crops, traditionally referring to youthful, unproductive actions.
