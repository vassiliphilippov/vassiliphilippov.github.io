fueled by hopium

1. ADJ

/ ˈfjuːəld baɪ ˈhoʊpiəm / (AmE), / ˈfjʊəld baɪ ˈhəʊpiəm / (BrE)
driven by unrealistic hope or excessive optimism, especially in the face of evidence to the contrary
motivated by unrealistic optimism
informal

Examples

Many retail traders remained fueled by hopium despite the mounting losses in the market.
The startup's projections seemed fueled by hopium rather than supported by data.
His campaign promises were dismissed as fueled by hopium by many political analysts.

Synset Examples

Investors were fueled by hopium during the rally.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wishful thinking - thinking that something will happen or be true even though it is unlikely
  • Synonym: delusional - believing things that are not grounded in reality
  • Antonym: realistic - accepting things as they are; practical

Etymology

Blend of 'hope' and 'opium', popularized in online discourse in the early 21st century.
