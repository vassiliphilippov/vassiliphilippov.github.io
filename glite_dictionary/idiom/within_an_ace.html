within an ace

1. ADV

/ wɪˈðɪn ən eɪs / (AmE), / wɪˈðɪn ən eɪs / (BrE)
very close to achieving or experiencing something, especially something undesirable or critical
very nearly; almost at the point of

Examples

She was within an ace of accepting the job offer, but changed her mind at the last minute.
The team came within an ace of winning the championship.
He was within an ace of being late for the meeting, but he made it just in time.

Synset Examples

He was within an ace of losing everything in the fire.
The climber was within an ace of his life during the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on the verge of - very close to doing or experiencing something
  • Synonym: by a hair's breadth - narrowly; by a very small margin

Etymology

From the noun 'ace' (a single point or unit in cards or dice), indicating a very small margin.
