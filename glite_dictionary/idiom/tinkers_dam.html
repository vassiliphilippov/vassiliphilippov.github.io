tinker's dam

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪŋkərz dæm / (AmE), / ˈtɪŋkəz dam / (BrE)
A thing regarded as worthless; something of no value.
something of no value

Examples

His explanation isn't worth a tinker's dam to anyone who knows the facts.
She didn't care a tinker's dam about what others thought of her decision.
Most of these rumors aren't worth a tinker's dam—they come and go so quickly.

Synset Examples

his promise is not worth a damn
not worth one red cent
not worth shucks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damn - a thing of no value (in the same idiomatic expression)

Etymology

From the practice of tinkers using a temporary dam of dough or clay to hold solder, which was discarded after use.
