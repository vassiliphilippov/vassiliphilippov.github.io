over the transom

1. ADV

/ ˈoʊvər ðə ˈtræn.səm / (AmE), / ˈəʊvə ðə ˈtræn.səm / (BrE)
(of something, especially a manuscript or idea) submitted or received unexpectedly and without prior arrangement, typically unsolicited.
unsolicited or unexpected submission

Examples

The magazine often discovers excellent short stories that come in over the transom.
We weren't expecting the proposal; it just arrived over the transom one morning.
New artists sometimes get their first break when their work lands over the transom.

Synset Examples

The publisher found new talent over the transom.
Submissions often come in over the transom.
They received the manuscript over the transom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unsolicited - not requested; given or done voluntarily

Etymology

Originates from the practice of slipping manuscripts through a door's transom window, especially in publishing offices, in early 20th-century America.
