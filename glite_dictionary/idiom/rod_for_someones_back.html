rod for someone's back

1. NOUN

/ rɑd fər ˈsʌmwʌnz bæk / (AmE), / rɒd fə ˈsʌmwʌnz bæk / (BrE)
something that causes future trouble or punishment for someone, resulting from their own actions or decisions
cause of self-inflicted trouble

Examples

By ignoring the warnings, she made a rod for her own back that would haunt her later.
His continual procrastination was a rod for his back once deadlines arrived.
Failing to address the problem early created a rod for their back down the line.

Synset Examples

Her reckless choices became a rod for her back.
He knew his lies would be a rod for his back.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bring it on oneself - to be the cause of one's own problems or trouble

Etymology

Metaphorically based on a physical rod used for punishment.
