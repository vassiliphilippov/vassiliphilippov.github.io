to burn the candle at both ends

1. VERB

/ bɝn ðə ˈkændl ət boʊθ ɛndz / (AmE), / bɜːn ðə ˈkændl ət bəʊθ ɛndz / (BrE)
to exhaust oneself by working or doing other activities late into the night and starting again early in the morning, often resulting in tiredness or stress
overwork by keeping long hours

Examples

If you burn the candle at both ends, you'll eventually become exhausted.
Jane has been burning the candle at both ends to finish the project on time.
He realized he couldn't burn the candle at both ends forever without consequences.

Synset Examples

He tends to burn the candle at both ends during finals.
If you keep burning the candle at both ends, you’ll burn out.
She’s been burning the candle at both ends all week.

Etymology

Refers to using both ends of a candle, implying faster consumption; first recorded in the 18th century.
