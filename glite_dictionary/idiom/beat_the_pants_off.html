to beat the pants off

1. VERB

/ bit ðə pænts ɔf / (AmE), / biːt ðə pænts ɒf / (BrE)
to defeat someone very decisively or easily
defeat decisively (idiom)
informal

Examples

If we work together, we can beat the pants off our rivals in the finals.
She beat the pants off every other candidate in the spelling bee.
They expected a close match, but the home team beat the pants off their opponents.

Synset Examples

Our team beat the pants off the competition this year.
He was so confident, he knew he would beat the pants off his opponent.
She beat the pants off everyone in the trivia game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to trounce - to defeat someone thoroughly
  • Synonym: thrash - to defeat someone decisively, often in a sports context
  • Antonym: lose to - to be defeated by someone
