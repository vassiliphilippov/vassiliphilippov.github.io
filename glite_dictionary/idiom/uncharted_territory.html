uncharted territory

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈtʃɑːrtɪd ˈtɛrəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʃɑːtɪd ˈtɛrɪtəri / (BrE)
a situation or subject that is new, unfamiliar, or involves unknown risks
new or unfamiliar situation

Examples

When the company launched its first product overseas, it was entering uncharted territory.
For many students, starting university is uncharted territory.
The pandemic forced scientists into uncharted territory as they searched for solutions.

Synset Examples

Starting my own business feels like uncharted territory.
Navigating this new software is like uncharted water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uncharted waters - an unfamiliar or risky situation

Etymology

Based on 'uncharted' (not mapped) and 'territory,' used figuratively since the early 20th century.
