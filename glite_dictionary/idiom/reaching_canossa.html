Reaching Canossa

1. X

/ ˈriː.tʃɪŋ kəˈnɑː.sə / (AmE), / ˈriː.tʃɪŋ kəˈnɒ.sə / (BrE)
Seeking forgiveness, often by making a humiliating or public display of contrition.
appealing for forgiveness in a humbling way

Examples

After months of conflict, the politician ended up Reaching Canossa in front of the media.
The CEO had no choice but to make a Reaching Canossa apology to the board.
Her public statement was a clear case of Reaching Canossa after the scandal.

Synset Examples

Reaching Canossa symbolized his quest for forgiveness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to eat humble pie - to make an apology and accept humiliation

Etymology

From the historical event in 1077 when Holy Roman Emperor Henry IV traveled to Canossa to seek forgiveness from Pope Gregory VII.
