to go downhill

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ daʊnˈhɪl / (AmE), / ɡəʊ daʊnˈhɪl / (BrE)
To gradually become worse in quality, condition, or performance.
to get worse in quality or condition

Examples

After retiring, his fitness began to go downhill rapidly.
The service at the restaurant started to go downhill after the new management took over.
When her partner moved away, her mood seemed to go downhill.

Synset Examples

After the merger, the company began to go downhill.
His health started to go downhill after the accident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deteriorate - to become progressively worse
  • Synonym: decline - to decrease in quality or strength
  • Antonym: to improve - to become better

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ daʊnˈhɪl / (AmE), / ɡəʊ daʊnˈhɪl / (BrE)
to decline or deteriorate in quality, condition, or performance
deteriorate in quality or condition

Examples

Ever since the new management took over, the service has gone downhill.
Their friendship really went downhill after the argument.
The once-popular show has gone downhill in recent seasons.

Synset Examples

The restaurant ain't what it used to was; the food's gone downhill.
The quality of service has gone downhill lately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deteriorate - to become worse in quality or value
  • Antonym: to improve - to become better or make something better
