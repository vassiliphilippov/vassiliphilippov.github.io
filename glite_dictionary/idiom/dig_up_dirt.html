to dig up dirt

1. VERB

/ dɪɡ ʌp dɜrt / (AmE), / dɪɡ ʌp dɜːt / (BrE)
to search for and reveal scandalous, damaging, or embarrassing information about someone, often with the intent of harming their reputation
reveal damaging or scandalous information

Examples

Journalists sometimes dig up dirt on celebrities to boost their own ratings.
Her rivals tried to dig up dirt to ruin her chances in the election.
It's wrong to dig up dirt about people just to create gossip.

Synset Examples

It's unethical to dig up dirt on private citizens.
They managed to dig up dirt that changed the whole case.
The journalist tried to dig up dirt on the politician.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dish the dirt - to gossip or reveal scandalous information
  • Antonym: to cover up - to hide or keep secret information, especially scandalous or damaging
