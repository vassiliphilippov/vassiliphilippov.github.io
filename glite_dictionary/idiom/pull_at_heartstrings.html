to pull at heartstrings

1. VERB

/ pʊl æt ˈhɑrtˌstrɪŋz / (AmE), / pʊl ət ˈhɑːtˌstrɪŋz / (BrE)
to cause strong feelings of sadness, sympathy, or compassion; to deeply move someone's emotions
evoke strong emotions

Examples

Every year, the charity commercial pulls at heartstrings and inspires people to donate.
The writer's touching story really pulled at heartstrings across the nation.
Her speech pulled at heartstrings and brought many in the audience to tears.

Synset Examples

The movie pulled at my heartstring and made me cry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: move - to affect someone's emotions deeply

Etymology

Refers to the 'heartstrings', figuratively meaning the deepest emotions, and the metaphorical act of tugging them.
