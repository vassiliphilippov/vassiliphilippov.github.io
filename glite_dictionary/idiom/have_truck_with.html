to have truck with

1. VERB

/ hæv trʌk wɪð / (AmE), / hæv trʌk wɪð / (BrE)
to have dealings or associations with someone or something, especially with disapproval.
have dealings or association (usu. disapproving)

Examples

He made it clear that he would not have truck with anyone involved in corruption.
She has never had truck with gossip or idle chatter.
As a rule, they refused to have truck with outsiders.

Synset Examples

I don't have truck with those kinds of games.
She refuses to have truck with dishonest people.
I won’t have truck with such nonsense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: associate with - to spend time with or have a connection to someone or something
  • Antonym: to shun - to deliberately avoid or keep away from

Etymology

Truck in this sense comes from Middle French 'troque' meaning 'exchange' or 'barter'.
