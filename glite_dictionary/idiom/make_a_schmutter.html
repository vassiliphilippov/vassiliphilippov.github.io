to make a schmutter

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə ˈʃmʌtər / (AmE), / meɪk ə ˈʃmʌtə / (BrE)
to mess up or perform poorly at a task; to botch something
perform poorly; botch
informal

Examples

He completely made a schmutter of the presentation and forgot his lines.
She was nervous and made a schmutter of the cake for her guests.
They made a schmutter out of a simple job by working too quickly.

Synset Examples

He made a schmutter of his lines during the play.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mess up - to make mistakes or do something badly
  • Synonym: botch - to carry out a task badly or carelessly

Etymology

From Yiddish 'schmutter', meaning 'rag' or 'scrap cloth', used figuratively for something that's been spoiled.
