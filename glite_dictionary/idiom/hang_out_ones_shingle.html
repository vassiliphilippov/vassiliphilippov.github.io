to hang out one's shingle

1. VERB

/ hæŋ aʊt wʌnz ˈʃɪŋɡəl / (AmE), / hæŋ aʊt wʌnz ˈʃɪŋɡəl / (BrE)
to start or establish a business or professional practice, especially by oneself
start a business or professional practice

Examples

After years of working for a large firm, Maria finally decided to hang out her shingle as an architect.
When the clinic closed, Dr. Evans chose to hang out his shingle and open a private practice.
Many young lawyers dream of hanging out their shingle once they've gained enough experience.

Synset Examples

He decided to hang out his shingle as a consultant.
She's ready to hang out her shingle and start her own practice.
After years in corporate, she hung out her shingle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: set up shop - to start a business or begin working in a new place
  • Synonym: go into business - to start a business venture

Etymology

Refers to the historical practice of professionals, especially lawyers or doctors, displaying a sign ('shingle') to indicate their office.
