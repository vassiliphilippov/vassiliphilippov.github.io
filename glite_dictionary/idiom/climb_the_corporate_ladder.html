to climb the corporate ladder

1. VERB

/ klaɪm ðə ˈkɔrpərət ˈlædər / (AmE), / klaɪm ðə ˈkɔːpərət ˈlædə / (BrE)
to advance through the hierarchy of a business or organization, typically by gaining promotions and increased responsibility
advance in business hierarchy

Examples

She worked overtime every week to climb the corporate ladder at her firm.
Many young professionals dream of climbing the corporate ladder to achieve executive positions.
After years of dedication, he finally climbed the corporate ladder to become vice president.

Synset Examples

She climbed the corporate ladder quickly through hard work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get ahead - to make progress in one's career or life
  • Antonym: hit a glass ceiling - reach a point where further advancement is blocked, usually for discriminatory reasons
