to take for granted

1. VERB

/ teɪk fər ˈɡræntɪd / (AmE), / teɪk fə ˈɡrɑːntɪd / (BrE)
to accept or assume that something is true without questioning or verifying it
accept as true without proof

Examples

Many people take for granted that their health will always be good.
I took for granted that the meeting was cancelled, so I didn't show up.
She has been taking for granted the support of her friends without thanking them.

Synset Examples

I assume his train was late

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assume - to suppose something to be true without proof
  • Synonym: to presume - to suppose that something is the case based on probability
