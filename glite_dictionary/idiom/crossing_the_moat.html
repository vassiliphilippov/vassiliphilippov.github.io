crossing the moat

1. X

/ ˈkrɔsɪŋ ðə moʊt / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsɪŋ ðə məʊt / (BrE)
facing a significant challenge or overcoming a major obstacle
facing a major challenge

Examples

Starting my own business felt like crossing the moat, but I was determined to succeed.
Every applicant knows that passing the final interview is like crossing the moat to get the job.
Whenever I face a big challenge, I remind myself that others have managed crossing the moat before me.

Synset Examples

He felt like a knight in armor crossing the moat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overcome an obstacle - to successfully deal with a problem or challenge
  • Synonym: face a hurdle - to encounter a difficulty or barrier

Etymology

Refers to the historical practice of knights crossing a moat, a water-filled defensive barrier around castles, symbolizing overcoming a difficult obstacle.
