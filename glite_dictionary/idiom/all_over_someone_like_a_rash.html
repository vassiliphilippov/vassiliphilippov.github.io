all over (someone) like a rash

1. X

/ ɔl ˈoʊvɚ ˈsʌmwʌn laɪk ə ræʃ / (AmE), / ɔːl ˈəʊvə ˈsʌmwʌn laɪk ə ræʃ / (BrE)
extremely attentive or clingy towards someone, often in an annoying or unwelcome way
excessively attentive or clingy
informal

Examples

Ever since he got famous, the press have been all over him like a rash.
She hates it when people are all over her like a rash at parties.
The moment he entered the office, the interns were all over him like a rash asking questions.

Synset Examples

The fans were all over him like a rash after the concert.
The puppy was all over her like a rash.
He was all over her like a rash after they met.
