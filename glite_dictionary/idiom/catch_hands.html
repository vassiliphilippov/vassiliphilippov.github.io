to catch hands

1. VERB

/ kætʃ hændz / (AmE), / kætʃ hændz / (BrE)
(slang) To become involved in a physical fight or confrontation.
become involved in a fight (slang)
slang informal american

Examples

If you disrespect her like that, you might catch hands.
After the argument escalated, both men were ready to catch hands.
He warned everyone at the party that they would catch hands if they started trouble.

Synset Examples

If you keep talking back, you'll catch hands.
Don't mess with him; he knows how to catch hands.
He was ready to catch hands if they pushed him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throw hands - (slang) to start fighting physically
  • Synonym: fight - to engage in a physical fight
  • Antonym: to make peace - to restore or maintain harmony instead of fighting
