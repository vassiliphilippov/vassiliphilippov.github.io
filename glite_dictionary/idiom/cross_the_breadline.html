to cross the breadline

1. VERB

/ krɔs ðə ˈbrɛd.laɪn / (AmE), / krɒs ðə ˈbrɛd.laɪn / (BrE)
to fall below the minimum income level required for basic living needs
fall below the minimum income level

Examples

During the recession, thousands of families crossed the breadline despite government support.
If food prices keep rising, more elderly people may cross the breadline this year.
She worried her part-time wages would soon have her crossing the breadline.

Synset Examples

In tough times, more people find themselves crossing the breadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: below the poverty line - to have an income lower than what is considered necessary for basic living standards

Etymology

From 'breadline', a term dating from the early 20th century referring to lines for free food during times of poverty.
