on thin ice

1. ADP

/ ɑn ˈθɪn aɪs / (AmE), / ɒn ˈθɪn aɪs / (BrE)
in a situation where one is at risk of trouble, danger, or negative consequences
in a risky or dangerous situation

Examples

After missing the last meeting, Mark knew he was on thin ice with his manager.
You’re on thin ice if you keep ignoring the company’s rules.
She realized she was on thin ice when the teacher gave her a warning.

Synset Examples

She felt like she was on thin ice after the incident.
You’re on thin ice with your boss right now.
Driving without a license puts you on thin ice.
He knows he's skating on thin ice with his boss.
She felt like she was skating on thin ice at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skating on thin ice - being in a risky or precarious position
  • Antonym: safe - not at risk; not in danger

Etymology

From the literal risk of walking on ice too thin to support weight, leading to danger of falling through.
