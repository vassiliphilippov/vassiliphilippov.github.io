around the traps

1. ADV

/ əˈraʊnd ðə træps / (AmE), / əˈraʊnd ðə træps / (BrE)
(informal) circulating as gossip or news within a particular community or social scene; heard through informal channels.
informal gossip or news in a community
informal british

Examples

Rumors about the new manager quickly spread around the traps at the office.
I've heard around the traps that the band is getting back together.
News of the promotion was already around the traps before it was officially announced.

Synset Examples

He knows all the gossip around the traps after the party.
She heard the news around the traps at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the grapevine - the informal transmission of information or gossip

Etymology

Australian English, originally referring to traveling entertainers or sportspeople making regular stops ('the traps').
