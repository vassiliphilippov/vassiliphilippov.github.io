to make heavy going of

1. VERB

/ meɪk ˈhɛvi ˈgoʊɪŋ ʌv / (AmE), / meɪk ˈhɛvi ˈgəʊɪŋ ɒv / (BrE)
to find something more difficult or tedious than it really is; to progress with difficulty with a task or activity
find something unnecessarily difficult
british

Examples

He always makes heavy going of the simplest chores around the house.
She seemed to make heavy going of her homework last night.
Try not to make heavy going of this task; it's really quite straightforward.

Synset Examples

Don't make heavy going of this project; just get it done.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: struggle - to experience difficulty or make little progress with something
