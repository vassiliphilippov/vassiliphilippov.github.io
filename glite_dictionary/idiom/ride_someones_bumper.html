to ride someone's bumper

1. VERB

/ raɪd ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈbʌmpɚ / (AmE), / raɪd ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈbʌmpə / (BrE)
to drive very closely behind another vehicle, especially in a way considered dangerous or aggressive
drive too close behind another car
informal

Examples

She gets nervous when someone starts to ride her bumper on the highway.
It's dangerous to ride someone's bumper, especially in heavy traffic.
The driver behind me was riding my bumper even though I was already at the speed limit.

Synset Examples

She hates it when drivers ride her bumper in the rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tailgate - to drive dangerously close behind another vehicle
