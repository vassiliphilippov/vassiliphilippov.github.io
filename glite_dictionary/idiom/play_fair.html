to play fair

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ˈfɛr / (AmE), / pleɪ ˈfeə / (BrE)
to act honestly, justly, or according to the accepted rules in a situation or competition
act honestly and justly

Examples

In all his business dealings, he always tries to play fair with his partners.
The coach reminded the team to play fair and respect the referees' decisions.
If you want others to trust you, it's important to play fair no matter the situation.

Synset Examples

Don't be a cuntmuncher; play fair and be respectful.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cheat - to act dishonestly or unfairly in order to gain an advantage
