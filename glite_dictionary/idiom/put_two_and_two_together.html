to put two and two together

1. VERB

/ pʊt tuː ənd tuː təˈɡɛðər / (AmE), / pʊt tuː ənd tuː təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to draw a logical conclusion from the available evidence or obvious facts
draw a logical conclusion

Examples

It took her a while to put two and two together and realize who had called.
When he saw the empty jar and crumbs, he quickly put two and two together.
Can you put two and two together to figure out what happened?

Synset Examples

It took her a while to put two and two together.
After seeing the clues, she put two and two together.
He finally put two and two together about the surprise party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deduce - to reach a conclusion based on available information
  • Degree Lower: guess - to estimate or suppose without sufficient information
