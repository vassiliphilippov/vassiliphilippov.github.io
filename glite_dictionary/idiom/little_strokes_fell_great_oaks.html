little strokes fell great oaks

1. X

/ ˈlɪɾl̩ stroʊks fɛl ɡreɪt oʊks / (AmE), / ˈlɪtl̩ strəʊks fɛl ɡreɪt əʊks / (BrE)
Even small and consistent efforts can achieve great results over time.
persistent small efforts achieve big results

Examples

She reminded her team that little strokes fell great oaks when they felt overwhelmed by the huge project.
His success is proof that little strokes fell great oaks, as he made steady progress every day.
Whenever I'm discouraged, I remember that little strokes fell great oaks and keep working bit by bit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perseverance - continued effort to do or achieve something despite difficulties
  • Synonym: persistent effort - continuous or repeated effort over time
  • Synonym: Rome wasn't built in a day - proverb indicating that great things take time

Etymology

A proverb first recorded in the 18th century, popularized by Benjamin Franklin.
