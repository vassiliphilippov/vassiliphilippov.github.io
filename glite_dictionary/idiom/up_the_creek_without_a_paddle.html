up the creek without a paddle

1. X

/ ʌp ðə krik wɪˈðaʊt ə ˈpædl̩ / (AmE), / ʌp ðə ˈkriːk wɪˈðaʊt ə ˈpædl / (BrE)
in a very difficult or troublesome situation with no apparent solution or means of escape
in a hopeless, tough situation
informal

Examples

If we don't find the keys soon, we'll be up the creek without a paddle.
When the funding was cut, the entire project was up the creek without a paddle.
She realized she was up the creek without a paddle when her phone died in the middle of nowhere.

Synset Examples

Without a map, we're up the creek without a paddle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in a bind - in a difficult or awkward situation
  • Synonym: in hot water - in trouble or facing difficulty
  • Antonym: out of trouble - free from difficulty or danger
