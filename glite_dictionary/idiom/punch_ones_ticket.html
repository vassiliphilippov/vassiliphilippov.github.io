to punch one's ticket

1. VERB

/ pʌntʃ wʌnz ˈtɪk.ɪt / (AmE), / pʌntʃ wʌnz ˈtɪk.ɪt / (BrE)
to secure a guaranteed opportunity, reward, or path to success
secure an opportunity or guarantee of success

Examples

Winning the championship really punched his ticket to the national team.
By acing her medical school exams, she punched her ticket to a top residency.
Landing that publishing deal finally punched his ticket to literary fame.

Synset Examples

Winning that award really punched her ticket to success.
By finishing the project early, he punched his ticket for promotion.
He finally punched his ticket to the big leagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make it - to achieve success or reach a goal
  • Synonym: secure - to obtain something, especially after much effort

Etymology

From the literal act of railway/train or event tickets being punched as proof of validity; figuratively extended to signifying entry or qualification.
