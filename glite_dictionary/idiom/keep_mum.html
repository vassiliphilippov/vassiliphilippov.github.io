to keep mum

1. VERB

/ kip mʌm / (AmE), / kiːp mʌm / (BrE)
to remain silent or not reveal a secret or information
stay silent; not reveal a secret

Examples

He decided to keep mum about the deal until everything was finalized.
The children managed to keep mum when questioned by their parents.
It's best to keep mum if you don't want to get into trouble.

Synset Examples

The children shut up when their father approached

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keep quiet - to not speak or reveal information
  • Antonym: to spill the beans - to reveal secret information

Etymology

The term "mum" meaning 'silent' comes from Middle English, probably imitative of the sound made with closed lips.
