to open a hornets' nest

1. VERB

/ ˈoʊpən ə ˈhɔrnɪts nɛst / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən ə ˈhɔːnɪts nɛst / (BrE)
to do something that causes trouble or brings about a lot of controversy
cause trouble or controversy

Examples

His comments at the meeting opened a hornets' nest of criticism from the staff.
The article opened a hornets' nest in the local community, sparking heated debates.
By bringing up that scandal, she opened a hornets' nest that was best left alone.

Synset Examples

Avoid that topic; it’ll open a hornets' nest of drama.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stir up a hornets' nest - to cause anger or trouble
  • Synonym: to open a can of worms - to create a complicated or troublesome situation

Etymology

From the literal image of disturbing a nest of hornets, provoking angry or chaotic reactions.
