gall and wormwood

1. NOUN

/ ɡɔl ənd ˈwɜrmwʊd / (AmE), / ɡɔːl ənd ˈwɜːmwʊd / (BrE)
a circumstance or experience that is deeply unpleasant or bitter
bitter or unpleasant experience

Examples

The sudden loss of the championship was gall and wormwood to the team.
It was gall and wormwood for her to watch her rival succeed.
The harsh criticism from his mentor was pure gall and wormwood to him.

Synset Examples

The news was gall and wormwood after such high hopes.
Her criticism felt like gall and wormwood to his ego.
Facing the truth was gall and wormwood for him.

Etymology

From 'gall' (bitterness) and 'wormwood' (a bitter herb), both symbols of bitterness in literature and the Bible.
