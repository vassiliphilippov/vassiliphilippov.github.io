the fat of the land

1. NOUN

/ ðə fæt əv ðə lænd / (AmE), / ðə fæt ɒv ðə lænd / (BrE)
the best and most abundant things that a place or situation has to offer, often enjoyed as a reward for hard work or good fortune
the best resources or comforts available

Examples

After the harvest, the family feasted on the fat of the land.
They worked hard for decades before finally enjoying the fat of the land.
As a reward, he was given the right to live off the fat of the land.

Synset Examples

We moved to the countryside to eat from the fat of the land.
After years of hard work, they finally enjoyed the fat of the land.
Living in luxury, they savored the fat of the land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luxury - great comfort or elegance, especially when involving great expense

Etymology

From the phrase in the King James Bible (Genesis 45:18), meaning the richest part of the produce of the land.
