to run from pillar to post

1. VERB

/ rʌn frəm ˈpɪlər tə poʊst / (AmE), / rʌn frəm ˈpɪlə tə pəʊst / (BrE)
to move hurriedly or aimlessly from one place or task to another, often feeling confused or harried
move hurriedly from place to place in confusion

Examples

After the meeting was canceled, she ran from pillar to post trying to reorganize her schedule.
During the crisis, the team ran from pillar to post as they tried to put out fires everywhere.
He's been running from pillar to post since the project began, but nothing seems to get done.

Synset Examples

He felt like he was running from pillar to post all day.

Etymology

Probably from an earlier form 'from post to pillar,' possibly referring to persons being shuttled between two points, dating from the 16th century.
