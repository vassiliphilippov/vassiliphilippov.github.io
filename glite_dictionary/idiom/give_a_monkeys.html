to give a monkey's

1. VERB

/ ˈɡɪv ə ˈmʌŋkiz / (AmE), / ˈɡɪv ə ˈmʌŋkɪz / (BrE)
(usually in negative constructions) To care at all; to be concerned (about something).
not care at all (informal idiom)
informal british

Examples

I really don't give a monkey's what he thinks about my haircut.
She acts like she doesn't give a monkey's about the upcoming test.
They clearly give a monkey's about the environment, judging by all their initiatives.

Synset Examples

I don't give a monkey's about their opinions.
He doesn’t give a monkey’s whether it rains or shines.
She gives a monkey’s about her friends' happiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: care - to feel concern or interest
  • Synonym: to give a damn - to care at all (usually negative)
  • Synonym: to give a hoot - to care in the slightest (usually negative)

Etymology

British slang, possibly from the earlier phrase 'I don't give a monkey's toss', with 'monkey's' as a euphemism for something of little value.
