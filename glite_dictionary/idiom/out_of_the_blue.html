out of the blue

1. ADV

/ aʊt əv ðə bluː / (AmE), / aʊt əv ðə bluː / (BrE)
suddenly and unexpectedly; without warning or prior indication.
unexpectedly; without warning

Examples

I hadn't heard from Jane in years, and then she called me out of the blue last night.
The offer arrived out of the blue, and I wasn't sure if it was real at first.
His resignation came out of the blue and surprised everyone at the company.

Synset Examples

her brother showed up at the wedding out of the blue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unexpectedly - in a way that was not expected
  • Synonym: out of nowhere - very suddenly and without warning or apparent reason

2. ADV

/ aʊt əv ðə bluː / (AmE), / aʊt əv ðə bluː / (BrE)
If something happens out of the blue, it happens unexpectedly or without warning.
unexpectedly; without warning

Examples

Her email arrived out of the blue after years of silence.
He proposed, completely out of the blue, and it shocked everyone.
The job offer came out of the blue just when he least expected it.

Synset Examples

unanticipated and disconcerting lines of development
unforeseen circumstances
a virtue unlooked-for in people so full of energy
like a bolt out of the blue
unseen problems

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unexpectedly - not expected; without warning
  • Synonym: suddenly - quickly and without warning
  • Antonym: predictably - in a way that can be foreseen or expected

Etymology

Likely from the idea of a clear blue sky, with something suddenly appearing or occurring.

3. ADV

/ aʊt əv ðə blu / (AmE), / aʊt əv ðə bluː / (BrE)
suddenly and unexpectedly; without any warning or indication
suddenly, unexpectedly

Examples

The news of his resignation came out of the blue and surprised everyone in the office.
She showed up out of the blue after months of no contact.
A job offer arrived out of the blue just when he needed it most.

Synset Examples

He called me out of the blue after years of silence.
My ex called me out of the blue yesterday.
He called me out of the blue the other day.
The promotion came out of the blue, catching everyone off guard.
She received a job offer out of the blue last week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unexpectedly - in a way that is not expected; suddenly
  • Synonym: out of nowhere - suddenly and without warning, as if appearing from nowhere
  • Antonym: predictably - in a manner that can be foreseen; as expected

Etymology

Said to originate from the phrase 'a bolt out of the blue', referring to a sudden lightning strike from a clear sky.
