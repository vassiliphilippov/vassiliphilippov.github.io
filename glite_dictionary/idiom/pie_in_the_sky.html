pie in the sky

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪ ɪn ðə ˈskaɪ / (AmE), / ˌpaɪ ɪn ðə ˈskaɪ / (BrE)
an idea, plan, or hope that is unlikely to happen or be achieved; something impractical or unrealistic
unrealistic or impractical idea

Examples

His promise of free cars for everyone is just a pie in the sky.
Many believe world peace is a pie in the sky, but others remain hopeful.
Don’t be fooled by pie-in-the-sky dreams when planning your future.

Synset Examples

They always chase pie in the sky ideas without any feasibility.
His plans to travel the world are just a pie in the sky.
The proposal seemed like a pie in the sky to us.
Investing in that startup seemed like a pie-in-the-sky gamble.
That job offer was a pie-in-the-sky dream for him.
Her expectations for success felt like a pie-in-the-sky fantasy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipe dream - an unrealistic hope or fantasy

Etymology

Originated from a lyric in a 1911 song by Joe Hill, popularized in labor movements.
