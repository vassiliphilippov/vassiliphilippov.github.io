to come with the territory

1. VERB

/ kʌm wɪð ðə ˈtɛrəˌtɔri / (AmE), / kʌm wɪð ðə ˈtɛrɪtəri / (BrE)
to be a natural or unavoidable part of a particular situation, task, or job
to be an inevitable part of a situation

Examples

As a doctor, dealing with emergencies comes with the territory.
Criticism comes with the territory when you're in the public eye.
Long hours often come with the territory in the restaurant business.

Synset Examples

Challenges will come with the territory of being a leader.
Long hours come with the territory in this job.
Dealing with complaints comes with the territory of customer service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go with the territory - to be an expected part of a particular situation
