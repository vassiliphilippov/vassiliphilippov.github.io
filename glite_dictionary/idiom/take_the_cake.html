to take the cake

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə keɪk / (AmE), / teɪk ðə keɪk / (BrE)
to be the most remarkable or outstanding in a negative or surprising way; to surpass all others in some undesirable quality
be the most extreme (often negative) example

Examples

I've seen a lot of strange outfits, but that pink suit really takes the cake.
The excuses he made for being late just take the cake.
You think this mess is bad? Wait until you see the kitchen—it takes the cake!

Synset Examples

He takes the cake for chutzpah!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take the biscuit - be the most extreme, often negative, example (Chiefly British equivalent)

Etymology

Refers to the cake awarded as a prize for a contest winner; North American origin in the 19th century.

2. VERB

/ teɪk ðə keɪk / (AmE), / teɪk ðə keɪk / (BrE)
to be the most outstanding or extreme example, often in a negative or surprising way
to be the most remarkable or extreme example

Examples

Of all the strange things that happened that day, John's reaction takes the cake.
Your late arrival without any explanation really takes the cake!
This year's snowstorm takes the cake for being the worst in a decade.

Synset Examples

This dessert certainly takes the cake in our competition.
Her performance in the play takes the cake this season.
That excuse really takes the cake for being ridiculous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take the biscuit - British idiom with similar meaning: to be the most remarkable or extreme example

Etymology

From the 19th-century practice of awarding cakes as prizes in competitions, especially in the southern United States.
