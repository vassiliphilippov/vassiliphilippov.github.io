a pretty penny

1. NOUN

/ ə ˈprɪti ˈpɛni / (AmE), / ə ˈprɪti ˈpɛni / (BrE)
a large amount of money, especially a sum that is surprisingly high or more than expected
a large or significant amount of money

Examples

He spent a pretty penny renovating his old farmhouse.
That designer handbag must have cost a pretty penny.
Selling his vintage car earned him a pretty penny.

Synset Examples

She saved a pretty penny for her dream vacation.
That antique vase cost a pretty penny at the auction.
His collection of coins is worth a pretty penny.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: a fortune - a very large amount of money
  • Degree Lower: a small fortune - a large sum of money, though less than a fortune
