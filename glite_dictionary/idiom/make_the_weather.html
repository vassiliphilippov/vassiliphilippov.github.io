to make the weather

1. VERB

/ meɪk ðə ˈwɛðɚ / (AmE), / meɪk ðə ˈwɛðə / (BrE)
to have a strong influence on the mood, atmosphere, or direction of a group or situation
strongly influence mood or atmosphere

Examples

As the editor-in-chief, she can make the weather in the newsroom.
Some leaders naturally make the weather for their whole department.
His inspiring speech really made the weather at the conference.

Synset Examples

The CEO can make the weather in our company culture.
The manager's attitude can really make the weather for everyone.
His decisions often make the weather for the entire team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to set the tone - establish the mood or character of something
  • Synonym: to call the shots - take control of a situation and make important decisions
