to give the sack

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ðə sæk / (AmE), / ɡɪv ðə sæk / (BrE)
to dismiss someone from their job; to fire or discharge from employment
to dismiss someone from a job

Examples

After making several mistakes, the manager finally gave the sack to the careless employee.
Rumors spread quickly when the company gave the sack to five of its workers last week.
If you keep arriving late, they might give the sack to you.

Synset Examples

The boss fired his secretary today
The company terminated 25% of its workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire - to dismiss someone from a job
  • Synonym: to lay off - to dismiss employees, especially temporarily
  • Antonym: hire - to give someone a job

Etymology

From the 19th-century British slang, with 'sack' referring to dismissal (possibly from the idea of giving someone a bag for their belongings).

2. VERB

/ ɡɪv ðə sæk / (AmE), / ɡɪv ðə sæk / (BrE)
to dismiss someone from their job; to fire someone
to dismiss (someone) from a job

Examples

After repeated warnings, the manager finally decided to give the sack to the careless employee.
Rumors spread quickly that John might be given the sack after the recent budget cuts.
If you keep coming in late, they might give the sack to you sooner than you think.

Synset Examples

They had no choice but to give the sack to the troublemaker.
He was shocked to be given the sack unexpectedly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire - to dismiss someone from a job
  • Synonym: sack - to dismiss someone from employment
  • Antonym: hire - to employ someone for a job

Etymology

Refers to the literal act of giving a worker a sack for their belongings upon dismissal, originating in 19th-century British English.
