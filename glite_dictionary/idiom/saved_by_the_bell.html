saved by the bell

1. X

/ seɪvd baɪ ðə bɛl / (AmE), / seɪvd baɪ ðə bɛl / (BrE)
rescued from difficulty or trouble at the last possible moment, often by an interruption or timely event
rescued at the last moment

Examples

Just as I was about to answer a difficult question, the fire alarm went off and I was saved by the bell.
He was saved by the bell when the meeting ended before he had to present his report.
The teacher dismissed the class before I could be asked for homework, so I was saved by the bell.

Synset Examples

He was saved by the bell just in time.
I thought I’d fail, but I was saved by the bell.
I was saved by the bell from a boring lecture.
He was saved by the bell just before the meeting started.
I thought I was going to be late, but I was saved by the bell.
The teacher called time, and I was saved by the bell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: close call - a situation where disaster or trouble is narrowly avoided

Etymology

Originally from boxing, where the bell ends a round and can save a boxer from defeat.
