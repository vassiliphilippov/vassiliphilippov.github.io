to all intents and purposes

1. ADV

/ tu ɔl ɪnˈtɛnts ənd ˈpɝpəsɪz / (AmE), / tə ɔːl ɪnˈtɛnts ənd ˈpɜːpəsɪz / (BrE)
in every practical sense; virtually; for all practical purposes.
in every practical sense

Examples

To all intents and purposes, the deal is already done.
The building is, to all intents and purposes, abandoned.
After the decision, her career was, to all intents and purposes, over.

Synset Examples

to all intents and purposes the case is closed
the rest are for all practical purposes useless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: for all practical purposes - in every way that matters; practically

Etymology

First recorded in late Middle English as 'to all intents, constructions, and purposes.'

2. ADV

/ tə ɔl ɪnˈtɛnts ənd ˈpɜr.pə.sɪz / (AmE), / tə ɔːl ɪnˈtɛnts ənd ˈpɜː.pə.sɪz / (BrE)
used to indicate that something is true in every practical sense, even if not formally so; virtually or effectively.
practically; in every practical sense

Examples

To all intents and purposes, online shopping has replaced traditional retail for many people.
The document is, to all intents and purposes, a contract.
To all intents and purposes, the bridge is finished, even though a few small tasks remain.

Synset Examples

To all intents and purposes, they are now a married couple.
The project, to all intents and purposes, is a success.
The software is, to all intents and purposes, finished.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: for all practical purposes - serving the same function as 'to all intents and purposes'
  • Synonym: virtually - meaning 'practically; almost entirely'

Etymology

Originated in English legal language in the 16th century as 'to all intents, constructions, and purposes'.
