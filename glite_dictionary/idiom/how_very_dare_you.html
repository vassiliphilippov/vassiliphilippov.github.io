how very dare you

1. INTJ

/ haʊ ˈvɛri dɛr ju / (AmE), / haʊ ˈvɛri dɛə ju / (BrE)
used to express strong outrage, indignation, or disbelief at someone's actions or words
expression of strong outrage or disbelief

Examples

When he accused her of lying, she exclaimed, 'how very dare you!'
'How very dare you come in here and make such accusations?' she shouted.
The teacher was shocked and said, 'how very dare you talk to me like that!'

Synset Examples

She gasped, 'How very dare you speak to me like that!'
I couldn't believe it; I said, 'How very dare you accuse me!'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: how dare you - expression of indignation or disapproval
