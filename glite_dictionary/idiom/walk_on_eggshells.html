to walk on eggshells

1. VERB

/ wɑk ɑn ˈɛɡˌʃɛlz / (AmE), / wɔːk ɒn ˈɛɡʃɛlz / (BrE)
to act extremely carefully so as not to upset or offend someone
act very cautiously to avoid offense

Examples

Since the announcement, everyone has been careful to walk on eggshells around the boss.
Whenever there's tension in the house, I feel like I have to walk on eggshells to keep the peace.
He admitted he was tired of having to walk on eggshells in their relationship.

Synset Examples

You can’t be too careful; she’s been so sensitive lately that we all walk on eggshells.
After the argument, I had to walk on eggshells around her.
She felt like she was walking on egg shells.
He felt like he was walking on eggshells during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiptoe around - to act cautiously to avoid offense
