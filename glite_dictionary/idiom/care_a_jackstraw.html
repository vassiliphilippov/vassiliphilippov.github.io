to care a jackstraw

1. VERB

/ kɛɹ ə ˈdʒækˌstrɔ / (AmE), / kɛə ə ˈdʒækˌstrɔː / (BrE)
To care very little or not at all; to be indifferent.
care very little; be indifferent
archaic

Examples

She doesn't care a jackstraw about office gossip and rumors.
Do you think he cares a jackstraw whether they approve?
I cared a jackstraw for those rules when I was younger.

Synset Examples

I care a jackstraw about what they think of me.
He cares a jackstraw about the latest trends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: care a fig - to care very little or not at all
  • Synonym: not give a damn - to not care at all; to be completely indifferent

Etymology

From 'jackstraw', meaning a trivial thing, originally referring to the game pickup sticks.
