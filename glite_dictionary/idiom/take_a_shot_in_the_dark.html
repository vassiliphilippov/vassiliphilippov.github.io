to take a shot in the dark

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə ʃɑt ɪn ðə dɑrk / (AmE), / teɪk ə ʃɒt ɪn ðə dɑːk / (BrE)
to attempt something or make a guess without any prior knowledge or certainty
attempt or guess without knowledge

Examples

With no idea what the answer was, I decided to take a shot in the dark.
He didn't know her size, so he took a shot in the dark and bought a medium.
She took a shot in the dark by investing in the start-up, hoping it would succeed.

Synset Examples

I decided to take a shot in the dark and apply for that job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guess - an act of estimating or supposing
