to have someone's way

1. VERB

/ hæv ˈsʌmwʌnz weɪ / (AmE), / hæv ˈsʌmwənz weɪ / (BrE)
to get or do what one wants, especially when facing opposition or contrary advice
get or do what one wants

Examples

Despite the objections, he managed to have his way in the end.
If she can't have her way, she becomes quite upset.
Children are not always allowed to have their way at school.

Synset Examples

If he can’t have his way, he pouts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get one's way - to do what one wants despite opposition
