on edge

1. ADJ

/ ɑn ˈɛdʒ / (AmE), / ɒn ˈɛdʒ / (BrE)
feeling tense, anxious, or nervous; easily upset or agitated
anxious or tense

Examples

She had been on edge all week, waiting for the interview call.
The tense music kept the audience on edge throughout the movie.
After hearing the rumors of layoffs, everyone in the office was on edge.

Synset Examples

His gorry tales kept the campers on edge.
His sudden outburst left everyone on edge.
The combustious debate kept everyone on edge.
Everyone was on edge due to the groomzilla's last-minute requests.
The campfire scare story kept us all on edge.
She was on edge waiting for the exam results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxious - experiencing worry or nervousness
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness or agitation
