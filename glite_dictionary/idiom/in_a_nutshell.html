in a nutshell

1. ADV

/ ɪn ə ˈnʌtʃˌɛl / (AmE), / ɪn ə ˈnʌtʃɛl / (BrE)
used to indicate that something has been stated very concisely or briefly
briefly; in summary

Examples

To put it in a nutshell, the plan simply didn’t work as intended.
The report, in a nutshell, is a call for urgent action.
So, in a nutshell, we need more time and more resources.

Synset Examples

gave the facts in a nutshell
just tell me the story in a nutshell
explained the situation in a nutshell

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in summary - used to state something briefly or to sum up
  • Antonym: at length - in great detail; fully

Etymology

Refers to the idea of compressing significant information into something very small, like a nutshell; recorded in English since the 16th century.

2. ADV

/ ɪn ə ˈnʌtʃˌɛl / (AmE), / ɪn ə ˈnʌtʃɛl / (BrE)
used to indicate that something has been explained or summarized in a very brief and clear way
very briefly; in summary

Examples

In a nutshell, the project was a complete success.
She described the whole situation in a nutshell at the meeting.
If you want my opinion in a nutshell, I think we should postpone it.

Synset Examples

In a nutshell, we need more time.
The report summarized everything in a nutshell.
Her explanation was clear, in a nutshell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: briefly - using few words; short in time or length
  • Synonym: in summary - to give a brief statement of the main points

Etymology

From the idea of fitting something large into a nutshell (as a metaphor for brevity), dating back to the 16th century.
