to throw spaghetti at the wall

1. VERB

/ θroʊ spəˈɡeti æt ðə wɔl / (AmE), / θrəʊ spəˈɡɛti æt ðə wɔːl / (BrE)
to try different methods or ideas to see which will succeed or be effective
try various methods to see what works
informal

Examples

We had to throw spaghetti at the wall until one of our proposals was finally accepted.
The producer kept throwing spaghetti at the wall hoping for a breakout hit.
When facing tough problems, sometimes you just throw spaghetti at the wall and hope something works.

Synset Examples

Sometimes you have to throw spaghetti at the wall to find a solution.
The marketing team decided to throw spaghetti at the wall with their new ideas.
We're just throwing spaghetti at the wall to see what sticks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: try everything - make an attempt with all available options
  • Synonym: to see what sticks - try various ideas and use the successful ones

Etymology

Refers to the literal action of throwing spaghetti at a wall to test if it's cooked (sticks).
