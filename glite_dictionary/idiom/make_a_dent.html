to make a dent

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə dɛnt / (AmE), / meɪk ə dɛnt / (BrE)
to have a noticeable impact or effect on something, especially by reducing its size, amount, or intensity
have a noticeable impact or effect

Examples

Her efforts finally made a dent in the pile of paperwork on her desk.
Although we worked all day, we barely made a dent in the renovations.
A large donation would really make a dent in the cost of the new building.

Synset Examples

He wants to make a dent in the homelessness issue.
We need to make a dent in our budget deficit.
Our donations will make a dent in their medical bills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: make an impression - to have a strong or lasting effect on someone or something
