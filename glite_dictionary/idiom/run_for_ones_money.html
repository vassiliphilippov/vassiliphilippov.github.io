run for one's money

1. NOUN

/ rʌn fər wʌnz ˈmʌni / (AmE), / rʌn fə wʌnz ˈmʌni / (BrE)
a situation in which someone provides strong competition or a serious challenge to someone else, making it difficult for them to succeed easily
strong competition or challenge

Examples

The underdog team gave the champions a run for their money throughout the entire game.
Her innovative design gave the established brands a real run for their money.
The new restaurant is giving its rivals a run for their money in terms of quality and service.

Synset Examples

Her performance really gave the veterans a run for their money.
This new phone gives the competition a run for its money.
The local team put up a fight, giving them a run for their money.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: challenge - an act of competing or making things difficult for someone
  • Antonym: easy win - a victory achieved without difficulty or real competition

Etymology

Originally from horse racing (19th century), referring to keeping up with or challenging the favorite in a race.
