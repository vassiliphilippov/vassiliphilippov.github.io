to pay the freight

1. VERB

/ peɪ ðə freɪt / (AmE), / peɪ ðə freɪt / (BrE)
to accept the consequences of one's actions or to bear the costs or expenses involved in something
accept consequences or bear costs

Examples

If you want the upgraded package, you'll have to pay the freight yourself.
After the accident, the company was forced to pay the freight for its poor safety record.
He knew he'd have to pay the freight for his risky investment.

Synset Examples

Sometimes you just have to pay the freight for your mistakes.
He realized he had to pay the freight for his choices.
If you want this service, you must pay the freight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to face the music - to accept the unpleasant consequences of one's actions
  • Synonym: to foot the bill - to pay the entire cost of something

Etymology

Originally from shipping, where 'freight' refers to charges for transporting goods.
