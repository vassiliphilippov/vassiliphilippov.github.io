to chase away the gloom

1. VERB

/ tʃeɪs əˈweɪ ði ɡluːm / (AmE), / tʃeɪs əˈweɪ ðə ɡluːm / (BrE)
to make feelings of sadness or negativity disappear; to lift a downcast mood
dispel sadness or negativity

Examples

Listening to cheerful music always helps to chase away the gloom on rainy days.
A fresh coat of paint and new curtains did much to chase away the gloom from the old room.
Her kind words were enough to chase away the gloom that had settled after the bad news.

Synset Examples

His jokes did little to chase away the gloom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lift one's spirits - to make someone feel happier or more cheerful
