to shuffle off this mortal coil

1. VERB

/ ˈʃʌf.əl ɑf ðɪs ˈmɔr.tl kɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈʃʌf.əl ɒf ðɪs ˈmɔː.tl kɔɪl / (BrE)
to die or depart from life, often used in a poetic or literary context
to die (literary idiom)

Examples

Many poets have written about what it means to shuffle off this mortal coil.
When his illness worsened, he prepared himself to shuffle off this mortal coil with dignity.
The old professor often mused about what comes after we shuffle off this mortal coil.

Synset Examples

He often joked about shuffling off this mortal coil.
We all must eventually shuffle off this mortal coil.
She wrote a poem about shuffling off this mortal coil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: die - to stop living; to cease to exist
  • Synonym: to pass away - to die (euphemistic)

Etymology

From Shakespeare's Hamlet (1600), where 'mortal coil' refers to the troubles of mortal life.
