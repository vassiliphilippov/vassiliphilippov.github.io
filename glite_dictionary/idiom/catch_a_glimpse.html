to catch a glimpse

1. VERB

/ kætʃ ə ɡlɪmps / (AmE), / katʃ ə ɡlɪmps / (BrE)
to see something or someone for a very short moment, often only partially or briefly
see briefly or partially

Examples

As the train sped by, I caught a glimpse of her waving from the platform.
He caught a glimpse of the secret document before it was whisked away.
Through the crowd, she caught a glimpse of the famous actor signing autographs.

Synset Examples

She caught a glimpse of the didapper flying overhead.
I caught a glimpse of the sunset through the trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to catch sight of - to suddenly notice or become aware of something or someone
