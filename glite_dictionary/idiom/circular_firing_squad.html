circular firing squad

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.kjə.lɚ ˈfaɪ.ɹɪŋ skwɑd / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.kjʊ.lə ˈfaɪə.rɪŋ skwɒd / (BrE)
a situation in which members of a group harm or undermine each other, often through mutual criticism or blame, instead of working together against a common challenge
group undermining itself through infighting

Examples

During the meeting, the committee turned into a circular firing squad, with members blaming each other for the failure.
Political parties sometimes form a circular firing squad when facing a scandal, damaging their own chances.
We can't afford a circular firing squad if we want this project to succeed; we need to cooperate.

Synset Examples

Avoiding a circular firing squad is key to teamwork.
The team became a circular firing squad during the crisis.
It's a circular firing squad when everyone blames each other.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutual recrimination - accusations traded within a group
  • Synonym: backstabbing - betraying allies within a group
  • Antonym: unity - working together as a cohesive group

Etymology

Alludes to the image of people in a circle accidentally firing at one another, used metaphorically since the late 20th century.
