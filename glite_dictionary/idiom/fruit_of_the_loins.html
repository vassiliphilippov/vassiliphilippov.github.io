fruit of the loins

1. NOUN

/ fruːt əv ðə lɔɪnz / (AmE), / fruːt əv ðə lɔɪnz / (BrE)
a person's children or descendants
children or descendants (idiom)

Examples

She took great pride in the fruit of her loins as they achieved their dreams.
At the family gathering, he introduced the fruit of his loins to the extended relatives.
Many parents hope the fruit of their loins will carry on the family name.

Synset Examples

The fruit of her loins always made her smile.
He proudly spoke about the fruit of his loins at the family reunion.
Parents often cherish the fruit of their loins above all else.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offspring - a person's child or children
  • Synonym: descendant - a person descended from a particular ancestor

Etymology

From the figurative use of 'fruit' (offspring) and 'loins' (the part of the body regarded as the seat of reproductive power).
