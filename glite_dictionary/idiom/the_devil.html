the devil

1. NOUN

/ ðə ˈdɛvəl / (AmE), / ðə ˈdɛvəl / (BrE)
used to refer to something very difficult or troublesome to do or deal with.
something very difficult or troublesome

Examples

Fixing the old car was the devil after so many parts had rusted away.
This math problem is the devil to solve, even for experts.
Getting everyone to agree on the new policy will be the devil for management.

Synset Examples

it will be the devil to solve

Related Terms

  • Synonym: a tough nut to crack - something that is difficult to deal with or understand
  • Antonym: a walk in the park - something very easy to do
