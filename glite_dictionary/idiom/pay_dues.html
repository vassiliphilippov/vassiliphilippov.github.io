to pay dues

1. VERB

/ peɪ 'duz / (AmE), / peɪ 'djuːz / (BrE)
to endure hardships or make necessary sacrifices, especially as a prerequisite for achieving success or recognition
endure hardships before success

Examples

As a journalist, you have to pay dues before covering high-profile stories.
She knew that to succeed in the industry, she would have to pay dues.
Many athletes spend years paying dues before they reach the major leagues.

Synset Examples

Every artist must pay their dues before achieving success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: put in the hours - to work long and hard, often before seeing results
  • Antonym: coast - to achieve something without effort or hard work
