to roll off the tongue

1. VERB

/ roʊl ɔf ðə tʌŋ / (AmE), / rəʊl ɒf ðə tʌŋ / (BrE)
to be easy, smooth, or pleasant to say or pronounce
be easy or pleasant to say

Examples

The name 'Samantha' really rolls off the tongue nicely.
She chose a brand name that would roll off the tongue easily.
Many popular slogans roll off the tongue and are easy to remember.

Synset Examples

Some phrases really roll off the tongue easily.
Her name just rolls off the tongue beautifully.
The title of the book rolls off the tongue.
