by a landslide

1. ADV

/ baɪ ə ˈlændˌslaɪd / (AmE), / baɪ ə ˈlændslaɪd / (BrE)
by a very large margin, especially in an election or competition
by a very large margin

Examples

The mayoral candidate won by a landslide, leaving her opponents far behind.
They beat their rivals by a landslide in the championship final.
He was elected president by a landslide, with more than seventy percent of the vote.

Synset Examples

They defeated their rivals by a landslide in the game.
The candidate won the election by a landslide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overwhelmingly - to a very great degree or with a great majority
  • Antonym: narrowly - by a small margin, just barely

Etymology

Metaphor from landslide (a mass of earth sliding down), suggesting an overwhelming force or majority.
