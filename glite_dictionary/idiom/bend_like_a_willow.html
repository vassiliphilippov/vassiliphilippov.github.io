to bend like a willow

1. VERB

/ bɛnd laɪk ə ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / bɛnd laɪk ə ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
to be flexible and adaptable, especially in challenging or adverse situations
to be flexible and adaptable

Examples

She always tries to bend like a willow during tough negotiations rather than resist change.
Good leaders know when to bend like a willow and when to stand firm.
In the face of adversity, he chose to bend like a willow rather than break.

Synset Examples

She bent like a willow in the wind during the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adapt - to adjust to new conditions
  • Synonym: yield - to give way or be flexible under pressure
