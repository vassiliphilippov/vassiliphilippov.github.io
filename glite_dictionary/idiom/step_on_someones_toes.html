to step on someone's toes

1. VERB

/ stɛp ɑn ˈsʌmwʌnz toʊz / (AmE), / stɛp ɒn ˈsʌmwʌnz təʊz / (BrE)
to offend or interfere with someone, especially by encroaching on their responsibilities or territory
offend/interfere with someone

Examples

Try not to step on anyone's toes when suggesting new ideas.
He refused to step on his boss's toes by making decisions without approval.
She was worried about stepping on her colleague's toes during the team meeting.

Synset Examples

Be careful not to step on anyone's toes at work.
Be careful not to tread on his toes during the project.
Don't tread on her toes; she values her independence.
He tends to tread on people's toes at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tread on someone's toes - to offend or interfere with someone
