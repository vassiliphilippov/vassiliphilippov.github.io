low road

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ ˌroʊd / (AmE), / ˈləʊ ˌrəʊd / (BrE)
A course of action considered unethical, dishonest, or lacking in moral integrity.
unethical or dishonorable course of action

Examples

He decided to take the low road by spreading rumors about his opponent.
When faced with a tough choice, some people unfortunately choose the low road.
Her willingness to take the low road damaged her professional reputation.

Synset Examples

Choosing the low road only hurt his reputation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: high road - an honorable or ethical course of action
