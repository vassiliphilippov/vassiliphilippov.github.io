to get to the bottom of

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt tə ðə ˈbɑːtəm əv / (AmE), / ɡɛt tə ðə ˈbɒtəm əv / (BrE)
to investigate thoroughly in order to discover the real cause or truth about something
investigate and find the truth

Examples

The manager promised to get to the bottom of the accounting error as soon as possible.
After several interviews, the journalist finally got to the bottom of the corruption scandal.
It took weeks, but the team is now getting to the bottom of the system failure.

Synset Examples

She was determined to get to the bottom of the issue.
Let's work together to get to the bottom of this problem.
We need to get to the bottom of this mystery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to investigate - to examine or study something in detail
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something
