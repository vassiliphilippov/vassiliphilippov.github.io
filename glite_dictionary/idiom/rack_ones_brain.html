to rack one's brain

1. VERB

/ ræk wʌnz breɪn / (AmE), / ræk wʌnz breɪn / (BrE)
to think very hard in order to remember something or to solve a problem
to think very hard

Examples

I've been racking my brain all day trying to remember where I put my keys.
She racked her brain for ideas to make the party special.
He racks his brain every time he's faced with a tricky crossword puzzle.

Synset Examples

She racked her brain trying to remember his name.
He racked his brain but couldn't find the answer.
I racked my brain for a solution to the problem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: think hard - to use a lot of mental effort

Etymology

From the verb 'rack' meaning to stretch or torture, originally referring to the torture device known as the rack.
