battle of wits

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæɾl əv wɪts / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv wɪts / (BrE)
a situation or contest in which two or more people compete by using their intelligence and cleverness rather than force
competition of mental skill or cleverness

Examples

The debate turned into a battle of wits between the two experienced lawyers.
Chess is often described as a battle of wits rather than just a game.
Their negotiations became a fierce battle of wits, with neither side willing to back down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: war of nerves - a struggle involving psychological tactics
