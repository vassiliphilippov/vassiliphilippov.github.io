to leave at the door

1. VERB

/ liv æt ðə dɔr / (AmE), / liːv æt ðə dɔː / (BrE)
to choose not to bring one's worries, issues, or emotions into a particular situation or environment
not bring worries/issues into a situation

Examples

Before you enter the group session, please leave at the door any distractions or negative thoughts.
She tries to leave at the door all her work problems when she comes home to her family.
We encourage students to leave at the door their personal conflicts and focus on learning.

Synset Examples

Please leave your worries at the door.
It's best to leave personal issues at the door.
