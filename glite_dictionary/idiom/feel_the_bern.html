to feel the Bern

1. VERB

/ fil ðə bɝn / (AmE), / fiːl ðə bɜːn / (BrE)
To express or experience strong enthusiasm or support for Bernie Sanders, especially his political ideas or campaign.
show enthusiasm for Bernie Sanders
informal american

Examples

Many young activists began to feel the Bern as the campaign gained momentum.
You could really feel the Bern in the packed auditorium last night.
Whenever Bernie takes the stage, his supporters feel the Bern and cheer loudly.

Synset Examples

Many young voters feel the Bern this election.
Supporters really feel the Bern at rallies.
When he speaks, you can feel the Bern in the crowd.

Etymology

Popularized during Bernie Sanders's 2016 US presidential campaign, with 'Bern' referring to his name.
