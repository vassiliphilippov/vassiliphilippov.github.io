to judge a book by its cover

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒʌdʒ ə bʊk baɪ ɪts ˈkʌvɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌdʒ ə bʊk baɪ ɪts ˈkʌvə / (BrE)
to form an opinion about someone or something based only on their outward appearance
form opinion by outward appearance

Examples

You shouldn't judge a book by its cover; people often have hidden depths.
I realized I'd made a mistake when I tried to judge a book by its cover.
She learned not to judge a book by its cover after meeting her new neighbor.

Synset Examples

Her appearance is deceiving; you can't judge a book by its cover.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: don't judge a book by its cover - used to warn against assessing things on appearance only
