computer says no

1. X

/ kəmˈpjuː.t̬ɚ seɪz noʊ / (AmE), / kəmˈpjuː.tə sɛz nəʊ / (BrE)
used to indicate an unhelpful or inflexible refusal, especially one attributed to a computer system or bureaucracy rather than a person
automated or bureaucratic refusal
british informal

Examples

Every time I ask for something slightly unusual, the computer says no and they can't help me.
The bank worker shrugged and simply said, 'computer says no,' ending the conversation.
Sometimes it feels like the whole system is just built around a computer says no attitude.

Synset Examples

Her application was denied with a simple 'computer says no'.
When I asked for a refund, the customer service replied, 'computer says no'.

Etymology

Popularized by the British television show 'Little Britain' (2003), where it was used as a catchphrase.
