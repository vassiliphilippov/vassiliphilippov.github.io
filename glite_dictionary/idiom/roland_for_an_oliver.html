Roland for an Oliver

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊlənd fər ən ˈɑlɪvər / (AmE), / ˈrəʊlənd fər ən ˈɒlɪvə / (BrE)
a situation in which two people or groups are evenly matched, giving as good as they get
an evenly matched contest or exchange
archaic

Examples

Whenever they argue, it's always a Roland for an Oliver; neither side ever backs down.
The chess match was truly a Roland for an Oliver, with both players displaying equal skill.
In business, competition between those companies has become a Roland for an Oliver.

Synset Examples

Finding solutions felt like a Roland for an Oliver.
In this debate, it's a Roland for an Oliver.
They always seem like a Roland for an Oliver.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tit for tat - an equivalent retaliation or response

Etymology

From the names of two legendary French knights, Roland and Oliver, who were said to be equals in bravery and strength.
