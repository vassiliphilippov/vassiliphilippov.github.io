to have (something) in one's sights

1. VERB

/ hæv ... ɪn wʌnz saɪts / (AmE), / hæv ... ɪn wʌnz saɪts / (BrE)
to be aiming for or planning to achieve something, especially as a goal or objective.
focused on aiming for a goal

Examples

She worked hard all year, keeping the promotion in her sights the entire time.
With the gold medal in his sights, the runner trained harder than ever before.
They have expansion to Europe in their sights for the next quarter.

Synset Examples

She has her promotion in her sights.
He had a new car in his sights for next year.
The athlete has the championship in his sights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: target - to aim at something that you want to achieve or obtain

Etymology

From the literal sense of having a target within the 'sights' of a firearm or optical device.
