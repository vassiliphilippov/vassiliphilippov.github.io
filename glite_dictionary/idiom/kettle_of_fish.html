kettle of fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛtəl əv fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɛtəl əv fɪʃ / (BrE)
an awkward, difficult, or problematic situation
difficult or problematic situation (idiom)
informal

Examples

When we lost the keys, it really created a kettle of fish for the whole group.
This legal dispute has turned into quite a kettle of fish.
If he misses the meeting, he'll be in a real kettle of fish with the boss.

Synset Examples

he got into a terrible fix
he made a muddle of his marriage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mess - a confused or untidy state of affairs
  • Synonym: predicament - a difficult, unpleasant, or embarrassing situation
  • Antonym: easy ride - a situation that is simple or without difficulties

Etymology

From the phrase "a pretty kettle of fish" (18th century), originally referring to a mess or awkward situation.
