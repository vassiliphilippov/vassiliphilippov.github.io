to give the devil his due

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ðə ˈdɛvəl hɪz du / (AmE), / ɡɪv ðə ˈdɛvəl hɪz djuː / (BrE)
to acknowledge or praise someone's good qualities or achievements, even if you dislike or disapprove of them otherwise
acknowledge merits despite faults

Examples

She may be tough, but I’ll give the devil his due—her work ethic is impressive.
Although I disagree with his methods, I must give the devil his due for getting results.
We argue a lot, but I’ll give the devil his due; he's completely reliable in a crisis.

Synset Examples

She may be tough, but I’ll give the devil his due.
Despite our differences, I have to give the devil his due.
He’s a tough competitor, so I’ll give the devil his due.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: give credit where credit is due - to acknowledge someone’s merit or achievements fairly

Etymology

Alludes to the proverb meaning even someone bad deserves fair treatment or recognition.
