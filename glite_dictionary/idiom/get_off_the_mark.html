to get off the mark

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɔf ðə mɑːrk / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒf ðə mɑːk / (BrE)
to start or begin performing or making progress, especially after a period of inactivity
to begin or start making progress

Examples

The company finally got off the mark with its first product launch last year.
It took a while for the conversation to get off the mark, but soon everyone was engaged.
The striker failed to get off the mark in the first half of the season.

Synset Examples

He was slow to get off the mark in the race.
It took her a while to get off the mark with her new job.
The team didn't get off the mark until the second half.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get started - to begin to do something
  • Synonym: to make headway - to make progress
