the old soldier

1. NOUN

/ ði oʊld ˈsoʊldʒər / (AmE), / ðɪ əʊld ˈsəʊldʒə / (BrE)
an experienced person, especially one who makes use of their experience or cunning, often to gain advantage
an experienced or cunning person

Examples

You have to watch out for the old soldier when he starts telling his war stories; he always exaggerates.
She's a bit of the old soldier—she knows how to navigate office politics.
Whenever negotiations get tough, he plays the old soldier and uses his experience to win others over.

Synset Examples

He doesn't just brag; he comes the old soldier effortlessly.
He always comes the old soldier when discussing past battles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: veteran - a person with long experience in a particular area

Etymology

Refers originally to a veteran of military service; later broadened to mean any experienced or worldly-wise person.
