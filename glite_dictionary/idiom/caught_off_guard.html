to caught off guard

1. VERB

/ kɔt ɑf ɡɑrd / (AmE), / kɔːt ɒf ɡɑːd / (BrE)
surprised or unprepared for something unexpected
unexpectedly surprised or unprepared

Examples

The sudden announcement caught off guard many employees, who weren't expecting any changes.
I'm sorry—I was caught off guard by your question and didn't know how to answer.
The opponent's quick move caught off guard the entire team.

Synset Examples

His lolrandom comments always caught everyone off guard.
The skiffy plot twist caught everyone off guard.
She caught me off guard with a trick question.
Doughfaceism is a common reaction when people are caught off guard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taken aback - suddenly surprised or startled
  • Antonym: prepared - ready for something; not surprised
