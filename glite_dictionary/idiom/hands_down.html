hands down

1. ADV

/ hændz daʊn / (AmE), / hændz daʊn / (BrE)
Easily and without any difficulty; by far.
easily; without difficulty

Examples

She won the competition hands down, impressing all the judges.
This is hands down the best restaurant in town.
He solved the puzzle hands down, finishing before anyone else.

Synset Examples

she beat him handily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: easily - without difficulty; with ease
  • Synonym: effortlessly - without effort; easily

Etymology

Originated from horse racing, where jockeys could relax their grip on the reins ('put their hands down') when certain of winning.
