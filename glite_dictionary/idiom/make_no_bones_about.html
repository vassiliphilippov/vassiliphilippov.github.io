to make no bones about

1. VERB

/ meɪk noʊ boʊnz əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / meɪk nəʊ bəʊnz əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to state something openly and without hesitation or to be frank and direct about something, even if it is unpleasant
state something frankly and openly

Examples

She made no bones about her disappointment with the decision.
The company makes no bones about its desire to expand internationally.
He made no bones about telling them that their plan would not work.

Synset Examples

He makes no bones about the fact that he is gay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be frank - to speak honestly and openly
  • Antonym: to conceal - to hide or not reveal something

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from 15th-century English expressions referring to bones as obstacles in soup, later generalized to openness or lack of hesitation.

2. VERB

/ meɪk noʊ boʊnz əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / meɪk nəʊ bəʊnz əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to express something openly and directly, without hesitation or evasion
speak openly and directly

Examples

She made no bones about her disappointment with the project's outcome.
The coach made no bones about expecting more effort from the players.
They made no bones about their desire to change company policy.

Synset Examples

He made no bones about his dislike for the policy.
She made no bones about wanting a promotion.
They made no bones about their intentions to expand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speak frankly - to express oneself honestly and directly
  • Synonym: to speak openly - to talk in a direct and honest way
  • Antonym: to beat around the bush - to avoid getting to the point of an issue

Etymology

Possibly from 15th-century English, where 'bones' referred to difficulties, with 'make bones' meaning 'hesitate'.
