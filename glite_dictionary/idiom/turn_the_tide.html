to turn the tide

1. VERB

/ tɝn ðə taɪd / (AmE), / tɜːn ðə taɪd / (BrE)
to cause a complete reversal in the state or outcome of a situation, especially making an unfavorable situation favorable
cause a reversal of circumstances

Examples

The new evidence helped turn the tide of the trial in his favor.
After months of struggling, the team's victory finally turned the tide for their season.
It will take a bold decision to turn the tide against rising unemployment.

Synset Examples

The tables are turned now that the Republicans are in power!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reverse - to change something to its opposite direction, order, or result
  • Synonym: change the course - to change the direction or progress of something

Etymology

Metaphor from the movement of ocean tides, signifying change.

2. VERB

/ tɝn ðə taɪd / (AmE), / tɜːn ðə taɪd / (BrE)
to cause a reversal or significant change in the course of events, especially from a negative to a positive situation (or vice versa)
cause a major change in circumstances

Examples

Her speech helped turn the tide in favor of the new reforms.
A single brilliant move can sometimes turn the tide during a close chess match.
The arrival of reinforcements finally turned the tide of the battle.

Synset Examples

The new policy could turn the tide for the company.
Community efforts helped turn the tide against pollution.
They worked hard to turn the tide of public opinion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reverse the trend - to cause an ongoing pattern to change direction
  • Antonym: to make matters worse - to worsen a situation, the opposite of improving it

Etymology

Based on the imagery of the sea tide changing direction, first recorded in the 17th century.
