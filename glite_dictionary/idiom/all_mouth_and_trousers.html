all mouth and trousers

1. X

/ ɑl maʊθ ənd ˈtraʊzɚz / (AmE), / ɔːl maʊθ ənd ˈtraʊzəz / (BrE)
(British, informal) talking boastfully or confidently without backing it up with action; someone who talks a lot but does not deliver.
boastful but lacking action (Brit.)
informal british

Examples

Everyone thought he would help with the project, but he was all mouth and trousers.
When it came to actually doing the work, she proved to be all mouth and trousers.
His promises turned out to be all mouth and trousers when no results followed.

Synset Examples

He's all mouth and trousers, but never delivers.
She realized he was all mouth and trousers when he backed out.
Don't listen to him; he's just all mouth and trousers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: all bark and no bite - talks threateningly but does not take action
  • Synonym: empty talk - speech without substance or intention

Etymology

British informal idiom, possibly from 20th century reference to brash young men (showy in words and appearance).
