to pick up the thread

1. VERB

/ pɪk ʌp ðə θrɛd / (AmE), / pɪk ʌp ðə θred / (BrE)
to resume or continue a previous activity, conversation, or discussion after an interruption
resume after an interruption

Examples

When the meeting restarted, Sarah was able to pick up the thread of the discussion effortlessly.
After several months away, he struggled to pick up the thread of his research project.
Let’s pick up the thread after lunch and finish planning the event.

Synset Examples

After the break, we need to pick up the threads of our discussion.
It's time to pick up the threads of our project.
Let's pick up the threads and continue from where we left off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resume - to begin again or continue after a pause
  • Antonym: interrupt - to stop the continuous progress of something
