right under someone's very eyes

1. ADV

/ raɪt ˈʌndɚ sʌmˌwʌnz ˈvɛri aɪz / (AmE), / raɪt ˈʌndə sʌmˌwʌnz ˈveri aɪz / (BrE)
used to describe something happening openly and obviously, especially without being noticed or prevented by those present
in plain sight; very obvious

Examples

The keys disappeared right under my very eyes, and I still don’t know how.
The painting was stolen right under the guards' very eyes during the crowded exhibition.
They managed to switch the files right under his very eyes, and he didn’t notice a thing.

Synset Examples

The theft happened right under our very eyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in broad daylight - in a public and open way, easy to see
  • Synonym: out in the open - visible to everyone; not hidden
  • Synonym: under someone's nose - happening close to someone without them noticing
