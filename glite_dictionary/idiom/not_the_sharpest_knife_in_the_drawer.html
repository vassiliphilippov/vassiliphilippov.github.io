not the sharpest knife in the drawer

1. ADJ

/ nɑt ðə ˈʃɑrpəst naɪf ɪn ðə drɔr / (AmE), / nɒt ðə ˈʃɑːpɪst naɪf ɪn ðə drɔː / (BrE)
(informal, humorous) used to say that someone is not very intelligent or quick-witted.
not very intelligent (idiom)
informal

Examples

He's kind and hardworking, but he's not the sharpest knife in the drawer.
Sometimes I get confused by simple instructions—I'm not the sharpest knife in the drawer when it comes to technology.
Her teachers realized early on that she was not the sharpest knife in the drawer.

Synset Examples

He can be funny, but he’s not the sharpest knife in the drawer.
When it comes to math, he’s not the sharpest knife in the drawer.
She’s not the sharpest knife in the drawer, but she means well.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: not the brightest bulb in the box - not very intelligent (idiom)
  • Synonym: dull - lacking intelligence, slow to understand
  • Antonym: sharp - intelligent, quick-witted (colloquial, antonym)
