in due course

1. ADV

/ ɪn du ˈkɔrs / (AmE), / ɪn djuː ˈkɔːs / (BrE)
at the appropriate or expected time; eventually, after a suitable period has passed.
at the appropriate future time

Examples

You will receive further information in due course once all the documents are reviewed.
The company said updates would be provided in due course regarding the merger.
All necessary repairs will be made in due course as part of the regular maintenance schedule.

Synset Examples

In due course, you'll receive your test results.
She assured him that justice would be served in due course.
The project will be completed in due course.
