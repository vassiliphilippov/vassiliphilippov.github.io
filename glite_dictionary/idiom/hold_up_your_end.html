to hold up your end

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ʌp jʊr ɛnd / (AmE), / həʊld ʌp jɔːr end / (BrE)
to fulfill your share of the responsibilities or obligations in a joint task or agreement
fulfill your share of responsibilities

Examples

Everyone on this team needs to hold up your end if we want to meet the deadline.
She always holds up her end of the bargain, no matter how difficult the task.
If you don't hold up your end, the whole project could fall apart.

Synset Examples

It's crucial to hold up your end of the contract.
If you don’t hold up your end, we can’t continue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pull your weight - to do your fair share of work
  • Antonym: let someone down - to fail to support or help someone as expected
