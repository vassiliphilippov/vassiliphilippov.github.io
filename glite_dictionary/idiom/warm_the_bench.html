to warm the bench

1. VERB

/ wɔrm ðə bɛntʃ / (AmE), / wɔːm ðə bɛntʃ / (BrE)
(of an athlete) to remain on the sidelines as a substitute, not taking part in a game or match.
remain a substitute, not participate in a game

Examples

After being injured last season, he had to warm the bench for most of the year.
Some talented players are frustrated when they warm the bench instead of getting field time.
She didn't want to warm the bench so she worked hard to prove herself at practice.

Synset Examples

Miller was riding the bench in Saturday's game

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ride the bench - to remain on the sidelines as a substitute
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02423129-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ wɔrm ðə bɛntʃ / (AmE), / wɔːm ðə bentʃ / (BrE)
to be a non-playing member of a sports team, sitting out of play; to not participate in the active part of an event or activity
sit out as a non-playing team member

Examples

Despite practicing every day, he had to warm the bench for most of the season.
After returning from injury, she was disappointed to warm the bench during the finals.
You won't improve your skills if you just warm the bench instead of playing the game.

Synset Examples

I hate to warm the bench when I could be playing.
He had to warm the bench during the playoffs.
The star player was injured, leaving others to warm the bench.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sit on the sidelines - to not actively participate; to observe without involvement
