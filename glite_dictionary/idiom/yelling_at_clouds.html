to yelling at clouds

1. VERB

/ ˈjɛlɪŋ æt klaʊdz / (AmE), / ˈjɛlɪŋ ət klaʊdz / (BrE)
to complain angrily about something that cannot be changed or is irrelevant, often in a way that seems old-fashioned or out of touch
futile, outdated complaining
informal

Examples

Whenever my uncle starts yelling at clouds about new music, we just smile and nod.
Stop yelling at clouds and focus on solutions you can control.
Online forums are full of people yelling at clouds over every little update.

Synset Examples

Don’t be an old man yelling at clouds; embrace change.
Her complaints about social media sounded like an old man yelling at clouds.
He’s just an old man yelling at clouds about modern technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: futile complaint - complaint that serves no effective purpose
  • Antonym: to embrace change - to accept and adapt to change or new things

Etymology

Popularized by a cultural meme referencing an episode of the TV show The Simpsons.
