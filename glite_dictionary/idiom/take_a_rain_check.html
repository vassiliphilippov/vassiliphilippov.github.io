to take a rain check

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə ɹeɪn tʃɛk / (AmE), / teɪk ə ɹeɪn tʃɛk / (BrE)
to politely decline an invitation or offer, suggesting that you would like to accept it at another time
postpone acceptance of an invitation

Examples

I’m sorry, but I’ll have to take a rain check on lunch today; something urgent came up.
She asked if I wanted to join them for drinks, but I had to take a rain check.
Thanks for the invitation, but can I take a rain check and join you next time?

Synset Examples

Let’s take a rain check for next week instead.
I’ll take a rain check on that dinner invitation.
I’d love to join you, but I’ll take a rain check.
Can we take a rain check on our dinner plans?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to postpone - delay something to a later time
  • Synonym: to reschedule - arrange for something to occur at a different time

Etymology

Originally from the practice in 19th-century American baseball of issuing 'rain checks'—tickets for a rescheduled game when rain postponed the original event.
