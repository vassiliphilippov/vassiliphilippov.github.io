to teach someone a lesson

1. VERB

/ titʃ ˈsʌmwʌn ə ˈlɛsən / (AmE), / tiːtʃ ˈsʌmwən ə ˈlɛsən / (BrE)
to punish or reprimand someone so that they learn from their mistake or wrongdoing
punish to make someone learn a lesson

Examples

After he lied to her, she decided to teach him a lesson by not speaking to him for a week.
Vandalizing the neighbor’s fence earned him community service, which really taught him a lesson.
If you keep being late, your boss might teach you a lesson by docking your pay.

Synset Examples

She decided to teach him a lesson by ignoring him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to punish - to cause someone to suffer for a wrongdoing
