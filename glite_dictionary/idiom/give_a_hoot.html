to give a hoot

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ə huːt / (AmE), / ɡɪv ə huːt / (BrE)
(usually in the negative) to care at all; to show any interest or concern about something.
show any concern or interest (usually negative)
informal

Examples

He really doesn't give a hoot what people think of him.
Most teenagers don't give a hoot about traditional customs.
Why should I give a hoot if they disagree with me?

Synset Examples

I don't give a hoot
She doesn't give a damn about her job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give a damn - to care at all; to show any interest (used in the negative)

2. VERB

/ ɡɪv ə hut / (AmE), / ɡɪv ə huːt / (BrE)
to care about or be concerned about something
to care or be concerned
informal

Examples

I really don't give a hoot about what people say behind my back.
She always gives a hoot about animal welfare and helps at the shelter.
Most politicians don't seem to give a hoot about the average citizen.

Synset Examples

He doesn't give a hoot about the politics at work.
She gives a hoot about the environment and volunteers often.
I don't give a hoot about what they say.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: care - to feel concern or interest
  • Synonym: to give a damn - to care; often used in the negative
