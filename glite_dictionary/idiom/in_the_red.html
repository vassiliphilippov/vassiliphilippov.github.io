in the red

1. ADJ

/ ɪn ðə rɛd / (AmE), / ɪn ðə rɛd / (BrE)
having a negative financial balance; operating at a loss
in debt or at a financial loss

Examples

After several unexpected expenses, our savings account was in the red by the end of the month.
If the business stays in the red for much longer, it may have to close.
Their household budget slipped in the red following a drop in income.

Synset Examples

She realized her account was in the red after checking.
Our budget is currently in the red this month.
The company has been in the red for two quarters.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: in the black - having a positive financial balance or making a profit

Etymology

Refers to red ink traditionally used to indicate losses in financial statements.
