to pour oil on troubled waters

1. VERB

/ pɔr ɔɪl ɑn ˈtrʌbəld ˈwɔtɚz / (AmE), / pɔːr ɔɪl ɒn ˈtrʌbəld ˈwɔːtəz / (BrE)
to do or say something to calm a difficult or tense situation
calm a tense or difficult situation

Examples

After the argument, Maria tried to pour oil on troubled waters by suggesting a group dinner.
The company leader stepped in to pour oil on troubled waters during the heated debate.
Whenever there are family disputes, my father knows how to pour oil on troubled waters.

Synset Examples

His calm words were meant to pour oil on troubled waters.
To pour oil on troubled waters, he offered a sincere apology.
Let’s try to pour oil on troubled waters before tensions rise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to soothe - to calm or ease distress or agitation
  • Antonym: to agitate - to make a situation more tense or disturbed

Etymology

From the old practice of pouring oil onto rough sea waters to calm them, first recorded in English in the 18th century.
