to burn the roof down

1. VERB

/ bɝn ðə ɹuf daʊn / (AmE), / bɜːn ðə ruːf daʊn / (BrE)
to generate an extremely lively, exciting, or intense atmosphere, especially at a party or event
create intense excitement or energy
informal

Examples

The band was so good last night, they really burned the roof down with their performance.
If you want to burn the roof down at your next event, hire a top DJ.
Everyone was cheering and dancing, ready to burn the roof down before the night was over.

Synset Examples

If we keep dancing like this, we’ll burn the roof down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bring the house down - to receive loud applause or enthusiastic approval from an audience
