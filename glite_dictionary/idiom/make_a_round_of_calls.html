to make a round of calls

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə raʊnd əv kɔlz / (AmE), / meɪk ə raʊnd əv kɔːlz / (BrE)
to make a series of telephone calls, often to several people in succession
make several phone calls in succession

Examples

I'll need to make a round of calls to confirm the meeting times.
Every morning, she makes a round of calls to check on her clients.
The manager asked his assistant to make a round of calls to all the suppliers.

Synset Examples

We need to make a round of calls this afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: call around - to phone several people to get information or help
