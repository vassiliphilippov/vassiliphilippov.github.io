to lose one's bottle

1. VERB

/ luːz wʌnz ˈbɑtəl / (AmE), / luːz wʌnz ˈbɒtəl / (BrE)
(British, informal) To lose one's courage or confidence, especially suddenly before taking action.
lose courage or confidence (British, informal)
informal british

Examples

She planned to speak up at the meeting but lost her bottle at the last minute.
Just before my turn on stage, I completely lost my bottle and couldn't go on.
He wanted to ask her out but lost his bottle and walked away.

Synset Examples

She planned to confront her boss but lost her bottle at the last minute.
He was about to jump off the diving board but lost his bottle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lose one's nerve - To lose the courage to do something
  • Synonym: to chicken out - To decide not to do something because you are afraid

Etymology

British slang; 'bottle' meaning 'courage' is thought to come from 'bottle and glass' (rhyming slang for 'arse').

2. VERB

/ luːz wʌnz ˈbɑːtl̩ / (AmE), / luːz wʌnz ˈbɒtl̩ / (BrE)
(British, informal) To lose confidence or courage, especially at a crucial moment.
lose confidence or courage; become afraid
informal british

Examples

He nearly asked her out, but at the last moment he lost his bottle.
She wanted to confront her boss, but she lost her bottle when the time came.
Don't lose your bottle—it's just a friendly competition.

Synset Examples

I thought I could speak in public, but I lost my bottle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lose one's nerve - to become afraid or anxious and fail to do something
  • Antonym: pluck up courage - to force oneself to be brave enough to do something

Etymology

British slang, derived from 'bottle' meaning courage or nerve, perhaps from Cockney rhyming slang 'bottle and glass' (arse).
