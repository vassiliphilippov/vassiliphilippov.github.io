to save the day

1. VERB

/ seɪv ðə deɪ / (AmE), / seɪv ðə deɪ / (BrE)
to prevent disaster or failure, especially at the last moment
prevent disaster or failure

Examples

Just when the presentation was about to fail, Linda stepped in to save the day.
The goalkeeper's incredible save in the final seconds really saved the day.
A power outage threatened the wedding, but a portable generator saved the day.

Synset Examples

In the movie, the hero had to rearrange the villain's face to save the day.
A last-minute goal saved the day for our team.
The actor's performance saved the day for the film.
The story featured a brave wench who saved the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rescue - to save someone or something from a dangerous or difficult situation
  • Antonym: ruin - to cause great and usually irreparable damage or harm to something
