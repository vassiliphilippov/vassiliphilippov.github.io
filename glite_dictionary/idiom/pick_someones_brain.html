to pick someone's brain

1. VERB

/ pɪk sʌmwʌnz breɪn / (AmE), / pɪk sʌmwʌnz breɪn / (BrE)
to ask someone for ideas, advice, or detailed information about something they know well
ask for ideas or information

Examples

I was hoping to pick your brain about the new software updates.
Before I make my decision, can I pick your brain for some guidance?
She loves when her students pick her brain during office hours.

Synset Examples

She asked to pick his brain regarding his travel experiences.
Can I pick your brain for some advice on this project?
I’d love to pick your brain about marketing strategies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to consult - to seek information or advice from someone
  • Degree Higher: to interrogate - to ask someone questions, sometimes aggressively or formally
