inside baseball

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.saɪd ˈbeɪs.bɔl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.saɪd ˈbeɪs.bɔːl / (BrE)
specialized knowledge, jargon, or details that are understood mainly by insiders or experts in a particular field
specialized insider knowledge or jargon
informal american

Examples

The book reveals a lot of inside baseball about how tech companies really operate.
Unless you're an investor, some of that financial talk is pure inside baseball.
She enjoys discussing the inside baseball of professional cooking with fellow chefs.

Synset Examples

She loves the inside baseball of the film industry.
His comments were a bit too inside baseball for the audience.
They discussed the inside baseball of the company's finances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jargon - specialist language used by a particular group
  • Synonym: arcana - mysterious or specialized knowledge known only to a few
  • Antonym: common knowledge - information known by most people

Etymology

Originally from American baseball, where 'inside baseball' referred to intricate strategies within the game; now applies to any area with insider knowledge.
