at daggers drawn

1. ADV

/ ət ˈdæɡɚz drɔn / (AmE), / ət ˈdæɡəz drɔːn / (BrE)
in a state of open hostility or sharp conflict with someone
in open hostility or conflict

Examples

The two family members have been at daggers drawn since the argument last year.
During the negotiations, the unions and management were at daggers drawn over pay increases.
Ever since the merger, the departments have been at daggers drawn about their roles.

Synset Examples

They have been at daggers drawn for years.
The two rivals were at daggers drawn before the match.
Management and staff were at daggers drawn over the new policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at loggerheads - in strong disagreement or dispute

Etymology

From the image of opponents ready to attack each other with daggers, first recorded in the 16th century.
