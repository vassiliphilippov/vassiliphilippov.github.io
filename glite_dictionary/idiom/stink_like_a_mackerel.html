to stink like a mackerel

1. VERB

/ stɪŋk laɪk ə ˈmækərəl / (AmE), / stɪŋk laɪk ə ˈmækərəl / (BrE)
to have a very strong and unpleasant smell, or to be very obvious or suspicious in a negative way
emit a strong unpleasant odor; seem very bad

Examples

By the end of the day, my shoes stink like a mackerel.
The deal stinks like a mackerel—there's something not right about it.
These old towels have been left too long and now they stink like a mackerel.

Synset Examples

The leftovers in the fridge stink like a mackerel.
His excuses stink like a mackerel.
After the workout, I stink like a mackerel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reek - to smell strongly and unpleasantly
  • Synonym: to stink to high heaven - to smell extremely bad
