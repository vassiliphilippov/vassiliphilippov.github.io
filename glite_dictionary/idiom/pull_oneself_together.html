to pull oneself together

1. VERB

/ pʊl wʌnˈsɛlf təˈɡɛðɚ / (AmE), / pʊl wʌnˈsɛlf təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to regain control of one's emotions or composure, especially after being upset or distressed
regain emotional composure

Examples

After hearing the bad news, he had to pull himself together before speaking to his family.
You need to pull yourself together if you want to finish this project on time.
She managed to pull herself together and continue performing despite the mistake.

Synset Examples

After the breakup, she had to pull herself together quickly.
It's hard, but you need to pull yourself together now.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to calm down - to become less angry, upset, or excited
  • Antonym: to fall apart - to lose control of one's emotions

regain one's composure or self-control after being upset or disorganized
regain composure or self-control

Examples
