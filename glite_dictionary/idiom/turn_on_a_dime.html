to turn on a dime

1. VERB

/ tɝːn ɑn ə daɪm / (AmE), / tɜːn ɒn ə daɪm / (BrE)
to turn in a very small space or with great agility
turn in a very small space
american

Examples

The new sports car can turn on a dime, making it ideal for city driving.
With his agility, the dancer turned on a dime during the performance.
This bike turns on a dime, which is perfect for tight mountain trails.

Synset Examples

My little subcompact car turns on a dime!

Etymology

Originally American English, referencing the small size of a dime (10-cent coin), suggesting minimal space required for turning.
