to bear away the bell

1. VERB

/ bɛr əˈweɪ ðə bɛl / (AmE), / bɛə əˈweɪ ðə bɛl / (BrE)
to win first place or achieve victory in a competition or contest
win or achieve victory
archaic

Examples

To everyone's surprise, the underdog managed to bear away the bell in the final race.
Despite the odds, they were determined to bear away the bell and claim the trophy.
Only the team with the most points will bear away the bell at the end of the tournament.

Synset Examples

In the competition, she managed to bear away the bell.
Only one team can bear away the bell at the end.
Their hard work allowed them to bear away the bell this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: win - to achieve victory in a competition
  • Antonym: to lose - to fail to win a contest or competition

Etymology

From the custom at English fairs where a prize bell was awarded to the winner; 'bear away' meaning to carry off as a prize.
