to turn one's coat

1. VERB

/ tɝn wʌnz koʊt / (AmE), / tɜːn wʌnz kəʊt / (BrE)
to change one's allegiance or opinion opportunistically, especially for personal advantage
change allegiance for advantage

Examples

Many voters distrusted the candidate after he was accused of turning his coat during the campaign.
She decided not to turn her coat even though the new management offered her a promotion.
Throughout history, leaders who turn their coat are often viewed with suspicion by their supporters.

Synset Examples

Politicians often turn their coat to stay in power.
He turned his coat when the party changed leadership.
She accused him of turning his coat for personal gain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: switch sides - to join the opposing group or team
  • Synonym: defect - to abandon a cause or party often in favor of an opposing one
  • Antonym: to stand fast - to remain loyal or unchanged in opinion

Etymology

From the 16th-century practice of literally turning a coat inside out to show a different color, indicating a shift in allegiance.
