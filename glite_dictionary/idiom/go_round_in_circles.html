to go round in circles

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ raʊnd ɪn ˈsɜr.kəlz / (AmE), / ɡəʊ raʊnd ɪn ˈsɜː.kəlz / (BrE)
to engage in a pointless or repetitive discussion or activity that does not lead to any progress or resolution
repeatedly make no progress

Examples

We argued for hours but just went round in circles without finding a solution.
If you keep going round in circles, you'll never finish the project.
The negotiations went round in circles and failed to produce any agreement.

Synset Examples

His explanation just made us go round in circles.
We keep going round in circles without a clear plan.
Stop going round in circles and make a decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tread water - to make no progress despite effort
  • Synonym: spin one's wheels - to put in effort without getting results
