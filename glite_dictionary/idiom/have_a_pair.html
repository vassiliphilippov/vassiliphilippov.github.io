to have a pair

1. VERB

/ hæv ə pɛr / (AmE), / hav ə peə / (BrE)
to be courageous or bold, especially in a challenging or confrontational situation (informal)
be courageous; show boldness (idiom)
informal

Examples

It takes someone who really has a pair to challenge the boss in public.
She finally decided to have a pair and told her roommate the truth.
You're never going to succeed unless you have a pair and take some risks.

Synset Examples

You really need to have a pair to stand up for yourself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: have guts - to be courageous or brave (idiom)
  • Synonym: to have the nerve - to have the courage to do something difficult (idiom)
