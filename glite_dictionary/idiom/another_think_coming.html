another think coming

1. NOUN

/ əˈnʌðɚ ˈθɪŋ ˈkʌmɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈnʌðə ˈθɪŋ ˈkʌmɪŋ / (BrE)
used to indicate that someone is wrong in their belief or assumption and will soon realize their error
realization that a belief is mistaken

Examples

If you think you can cheat on your taxes without getting caught, you have another think coming.
She thought her plan would work flawlessly, but she had another think coming.
Anyone who believes the rules don't apply to them has another think coming.

Synset Examples

If you believe he’ll help, you have another think coming.
She thinks she can skip practice; she has another think coming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: another thing coming - variant of the idiom, sometimes considered a mishearing

Etymology

From the older phrase 'have another think', meaning 'reconsider'.
