choir invisible

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwaɪ.ɚ ɪnˈvɪz.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪ.ə ɪnˈvɪz.ɪ.bəl / (BrE)
a euphemism referring to those who have died; the dead collectively
the dead (euphemism, idiom)

Examples

Few among us have not lost a loved one to the choir invisible.
After his passing, friends spoke as if he had joined the choir invisible.
The poet dreamed of joining the choir invisible, leaving a lasting legacy behind.

Synset Examples

Sadly, he has joined the choir invisible after a long illness.
Many have joined the choir invisible, leaving behind cherished memories.
She believes her late husband has joined the choir invisible.

Etymology

Popularized by 19th-century poet George Eliot in 'O May I Join the Choir Invisible.'
