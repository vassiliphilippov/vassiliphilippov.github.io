to go pear-shaped

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈpɛr ʃeɪpt / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈpeə ʃeɪpt / (BrE)
(of a situation or plan) to become unsuccessful or go badly wrong.
to go badly wrong or fail
british informal

Examples

Despite months of careful planning, everything began to go pear-shaped at the last minute.
Our holiday plans went pear-shaped when our flight was cancelled due to bad weather.
If we ignore the warning signs, the company could go pear-shaped very quickly.

Synset Examples

If we don't act now, the situation may go pear-shaped.
The project went pear-shaped when the budget was cut.
Things went pear-shaped during the final stages of the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go awry - to not go as planned or expected
  • Antonym: go smoothly - to proceed as planned without problems

Etymology

British slang, origin uncertain, first recorded in the late 20th century.
