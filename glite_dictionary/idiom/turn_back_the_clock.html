to turn back the clock

1. VERB

/ tɝn bæk ðə klɑk / (AmE), / tɜːn bæk ðə klɒk / (BrE)
to return, or wish to return, to a previous time or situation, especially one perceived as better or simpler
wish to return to an earlier time

Examples

Sometimes I wish I could turn back the clock and undo my mistakes.
No one can turn back the clock, so we have to accept what happened.
Many people dream of a way to turn back the clock to happier times.

Synset Examples

Some days, I just want to turn back the clock and relive my childhood.

If only we could turn back the clock to better days.
If only we could turn back time.
I wish I could wind back the clock to my youth.
Let's wind back the clock to when we first met.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wind back the clock - to make time go backwards; to return to an earlier period
