to steady the ship

1. VERB

/ ˈstɛdi ðə ʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈstɛdi ðə ʃɪp / (BrE)
to take action to restore order or stability, especially during a period of difficulty or uncertainty
restore stability in a tough situation

Examples

After the CEO resigned, the board quickly appointed a new leader to steady the ship.
During the economic downturn, the finance minister took bold measures to steady the ship.
Her reassuring words helped steady the ship when the team faced unexpected challenges.

Synset Examples

We need to steady the ship before making any big decisions.
He worked hard to steady the ship after the storm.
The manager's calm demeanor helped steady the ship during the crisis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calm the waters - to make a tense or difficult situation calmer
  • Antonym: to rock the boat - to cause disturbance in a stable situation (opposite)
