on (one's) guard

1. ADJ

/ ɑn wʌnz ɡɑrd / (AmE), / ɒn wʌnz ɡɑːd / (BrE)
alert and ready to deal with possible danger or problems; vigilant
vigilant; alert to danger or trouble

Examples

After hearing about recent thefts, I stayed on my guard while walking home at night.
You need to be on your guard when dealing with unfamiliar websites.
Security was on guard throughout the entire event to prevent any incidents.

Synset Examples

on guard against con artists
must remain on your guard in such a situation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alert - quick to notice and respond to danger
  • Antonym: off guard - not prepared for a surprise or danger
