to draw a blank

1. VERB

/ drɔ ə blæŋk / (AmE), / drɔː ə blæŋk / (BrE)
to fail to recall or remember something; to be unable to think of an answer or information, especially when trying
be unable to remember

Examples

I tried to think of his name, but I drew a blank during the meeting.
She often draws a blank when asked to recall childhood events.
Whenever I'm put on the spot with tough questions, I sometimes just draw a blank.

Synset Examples

I'm drawing a blank
You are blocking the name of your first wife!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to forget - fail to remember
  • Antonym: to remember - recall to mind

Etymology

Originally an allusion to drawing a losing ticket in a lottery, where a blank meant no prize.
