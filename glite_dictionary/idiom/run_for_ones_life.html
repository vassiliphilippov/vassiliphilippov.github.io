to run for one's life

1. VERB

/ rʌn fər wʌnz laɪf / (AmE), / rʌn fə wʌnz laɪf / (BrE)
to flee as quickly as possible to escape imminent danger or harm
to flee to escape danger

Examples

The villagers had to run for their lives when the volcano erupted.
As soon as the alarm sounded, everyone began to run for their lives.
During the fire, he had to run for his life to reach safety.

Synset Examples

She ran for her life when the bear appeared.
He ran for his life when he saw the bear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flee - to run away from a place or situation of danger
  • Synonym: escape - to get away from danger or confinement

2. VERB

/ rʌn fər wʌnz laɪf / (AmE), / rʌn fə wʌnz laɪf / (BrE)
to flee quickly in order to escape danger or to save oneself from harm or death
escape from danger by running

Examples

As the wildfire spread rapidly, villagers had to run for their lives.
When the alarm sounded, everyone in the building began to run for their lives.
The explorers realized they had to run for their lives as soon as the avalanche started.

Synset Examples

In the horror movie, characters always run for their lives.
They had to run for their lives from the approaching fire.
