to smell the barn

1. VERB

/ smɛl ðə bɑrn / (AmE), / smɛl ðə bɑːn / (BrE)
to sense or anticipate the end of a journey or situation, especially when close to home or a familiar goal
anticipate a journey's end or familiar situation

Examples

As the days grew shorter, the team could smell the barn and worked even harder to finish.
With the end of the semester in sight, students began to smell the barn.
The runners started to smell the barn as the finish line came into view.

Synset Examples

As a farmer, he always knows when to smell the barn.
After weeks away, he could finally smell the barn.
She could smell the barn from miles away after the rain.
