wind at someone's back

1. NOUN

/ wɪnd æt ˈsʌmwʌnz bæk / (AmE), / wɪnd æt ˈsʌmwənz bæk / (BrE)
enjoying support or favorable conditions that help to achieve success or make progress easier
having support or favorable conditions

Examples

She succeeded in her new job, clearly having the wind at her back all year.
With the wind at his back, the candidate won the election easily.
They launched the product with the wind at their back, thanks to the booming market.

Synset Examples

He felt confident with the wind at his back during the race.
Having the wind at her back helped her sail faster.
With the wind at his back, he sprinted to the finish.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: against the wind - facing opposition or unfavorable conditions
