nickel tour

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪk.l̩ tʊr / (AmE), / ˈnɪk.əl tʊə / (BrE)
a brief or superficial tour, usually given quickly or with minimal detail
quick, superficial tour
american informal

Examples

He gave us a nickel tour of his new apartment before heading out for dinner.
During orientation, we received only a nickel tour of the campus.
The celebrity was too busy for anything more than a nickel tour of the studio.

Synset Examples

The nickel tour was just a quick overview, not in-depth.
We got a nickel tour of the factory during the open house.
I only had time for a nickel tour before the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whistle-stop tour - a rapid tour with brief visits at each place

Etymology

Refers to the historical cost of inexpensive tours in the U.S., when a nickel (five-cent coin) was charged.
