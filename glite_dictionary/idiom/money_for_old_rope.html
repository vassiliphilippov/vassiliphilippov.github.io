money for old rope

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌni fər oʊld roʊp / (AmE), / ˈmʌni fə əʊld rəʊp / (BrE)
money earned with little or no effort; easy profit
easy money; effortless profit

Examples

For a few hours of work, he made money for old rope.
Many considered the consulting job to be money for old rope.
Selling those unused tickets turned out to be money for old rope.

Synset Examples

The artist made money for old rope with her prints.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: easy money - money earned with little effort

Etymology

Originates from the 19th-century practice where old rope was sold for reuse, requiring little work.
