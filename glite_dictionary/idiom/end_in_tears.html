to end in tears

1. VERB

/ ɛnd ɪn tɪrz / (AmE), / ɛnd ɪn tɪəz / (BrE)
to result in unhappiness, disappointment, or distress, especially after a hopeful or positive beginning
result in unhappiness or disappointment

Examples

If you keep arguing, the whole evening will end in tears.
Their business partnership looked promising but eventually ended in tears.
The teacher warned that cheating could end in tears for those involved.

Synset Examples

His harsh words could end in tears for her.
If we don’t resolve this, it will end in tears.
The argument was heated and could easily end in tears.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: end badly - to result in a negative or unfortunate outcome
  • Antonym: end well - to result in a positive or satisfactory outcome
