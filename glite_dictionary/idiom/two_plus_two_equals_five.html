two plus two equals five

1. X

/ tuː plʌs tuː ˈikwəlz faɪv / (AmE), / tuː plʌs tuː ˈiːkwəlz faɪv / (BrE)
used to describe an obviously false or absurd statement, or a situation where the accepted truth is manipulated or denied
obviously false or absurd statement

Examples

Calling that outcome fair is like saying two plus two equals five.
Their explanation made about as much sense as two plus two equals five.
Whenever facts are ignored, it feels like two plus two equals five all over again.

Synset Examples

Sometimes, it feels like two plus two equals five in this project.
In this case, two plus two equals five for sure!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absurdity - quality of being ridiculously unreasonable or illogical
  • Synonym: nonsense - absurd or meaningless statement
  • Antonym: truth - that which is true or in accordance with fact

Etymology

Popularized by George Orwell in 'Nineteen Eighty-Four' (1949) to illustrate the control of truth under totalitarian regimes.
