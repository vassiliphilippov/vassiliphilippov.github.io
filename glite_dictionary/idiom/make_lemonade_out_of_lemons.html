to make lemonade out of lemons

1. VERB

/ meɪk ˌlɛm.əˈneɪd aʊt əv ˈlɛm.ənz / (AmE), / meɪk ˌlɛm.əˈneɪd aʊt ɒv ˈlɛm.ənz / (BrE)
to turn a negative situation into a positive one by making the best of what you have
turn a negative into a positive

Examples

Despite losing her job, she decided to make lemonade out of lemons and started her own business.
He has a knack for making lemonade out of lemons whenever things don't go as planned.
After their vacation was canceled, the family tried to make lemonade out of lemons by exploring local sights instead.

Synset Examples

He always says to make lemonade out of lemons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turn a negative into a positive - to change a bad situation into something good

Etymology

Based on the proverb 'When life gives you lemons, make lemonade,' using 'lemons' to symbolize misfortune.
