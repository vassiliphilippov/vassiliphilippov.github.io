to show her his etchings

1. VERB

/ ʃoʊ hɚ hɚ ɪz ˈɛtʃɪŋz / (AmE), / ʃəʊ hə hɪz ˈɛtʃɪŋz / (BrE)
to invite someone (often a woman) to view one's artistic work, used euphemistically or humorously to imply a romantic or sexual advance
invite someone under pretense of art, implying romance

Examples

He coyly asked if she wanted to show her his etchings, making her laugh at the obvious innuendo.
The phrase 'Would you like to show her his etchings?' became a classic joke about awkward romantic overtures.
He joked at the party that he was going to show her his etchings, causing everyone to roll their eyes.

Synset Examples

He thought it was time to show her his etchings.
She laughed when he offered to show her his etchings.
He invited her to dinner to show her his etchings.

Etymology

Humorous allusion dating from early 20th century, based on using art as a pretext for intimacy.
