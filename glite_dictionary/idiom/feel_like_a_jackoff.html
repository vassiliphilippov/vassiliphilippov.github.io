to feel like a jackoff

1. VERB

/ fil laɪk ə ˈdʒækˌɔf / (AmE), / fiːl laɪk ə ˈdʒækɒf / (BrE)
to feel foolish, irresponsible, or embarrassed, typically after making a mistake or behaving carelessly
feel foolish or irresponsible
vulgar informal

Examples

After spilling coffee all over the documents, I couldn't help but feel like a jackoff.
She forgot to attach the file to the email and really felt like a jackoff afterward.
When he wore two different shoes to work, he started to feel like a jackoff.
