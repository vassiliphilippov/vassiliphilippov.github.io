to raise the line

1. VERB

/ reɪz ðə laɪn / (AmE), / reɪz ðə laɪn / (BrE)
to increase standards, expectations, or requirements beyond their previous level
increase standards or expectations

Examples

The management decided to raise the line for quality control across all departments.
Our coach told us we need to raise the line if we want to compete with the best teams.
New regulations will raise the line for energy efficiency in new buildings.

Synset Examples

The committee agreed to raise the line for environmental regulations.
They decided to raise the line to improve safety standards.
To enhance productivity, we must raise the line on our expectations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to raise the bar - to increase the standard or level of difficulty required
