with knobs on

1. ADV

/ wɪð nɑbz ɑn / (AmE), / wɪð nɒbz ɒn / (BrE)
(informal, British) Used to indicate that something is present or done to an exaggerated or enhanced extent.
to an exaggerated or enhanced degree
informal british

Examples

The kids' excitement was contagious with knobs on when the circus came to town.
If you think this party is wild, wait for New Year's Eve—it's the same with knobs on.
She didn't just finish the project; she finished it with knobs on.

Synset Examples

The presentation was impressive with knobs on!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: and then some - to an even greater extent

Etymology

British slang, from the literal sense of adding decorative knobs to make something more ornate or impressive.
