on the other hand

1. ADV

/ ɑn ði ˈʌðɚ hænd / (AmE), / ɒn ði ˈʌðə hænd / (BrE)
used to introduce a contrasting point of view or situation, especially when comparing two different possibilities
introducing contrast or alternative viewpoint

Examples

I love living in the city; on the other hand, it can be quite noisy at night.
The job pays well; on the other hand, the hours are very long.
She wants to accept the promotion; on the other hand, she's worried about the extra stress.

Synset Examples

on the other hand, she is too ambitious for her own good
then again, she might not go

Related Terms

  • Synonym: however - used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or seems to contradict something that has been said previously
  • Synonym: on the contrary - used to intensify a denial of what has just been implied or stated

2. ADV

/ ɑn ðə ˈʌðɚ hænd / (AmE), / ɒn ði ˈʌðə hænd / (BrE)
used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or differs from something that has just been said
used to show contrast

Examples

He’s very talented; on the other hand, he doesn’t work very hard.
She wanted to go to the party; on the other hand, she was feeling tired.
My job pays well. On the other hand, it can be very stressful.

Synset Examples

I love hiking; on the other hand, I hate camping.
It's expensive; on the other hand, it's worth it.
She can be strict; on the other hand, she cares deeply.
