what's eating at you

1. X

/ wʌts ˈiːtɪŋ æt juː / (AmE), / wɒts ˈiːtɪŋ ət juː / (BrE)
used to ask what is bothering or troubling someone
ask what is troubling someone
informal

Examples

You've been very quiet all evening—what's eating at you?
She asked me, 'what's eating at you?' when she saw my worried expression.
If something's wrong, just tell me what's eating at you.

Synset Examples

You seem off today; what's eating at you?
I can tell something's wrong; what's eating at you?
You look upset; what's eating you?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: what's bothering you - used to ask what is causing concern or distress
  • Synonym: what's on your mind - used to ask what someone is thinking about or worried about
