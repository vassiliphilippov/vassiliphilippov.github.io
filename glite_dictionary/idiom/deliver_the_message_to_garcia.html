to deliver the message to Garcia

1. VERB

/ dɪˈlɪvɚ ðə ˈmesɪdʒ tu ɡɑrˈsiə / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪvə ðə ˈmesɪdʒ tʊ ɡɑːˈsiːə / (BrE)
To take initiative and responsibility to complete a task reliably, without needing detailed instructions or supervision.
take initiative to fulfill a task reliably

Examples

She trusted him to deliver the message to Garcia and finish the project on time.
The company needs employees who will deliver the message to Garcia instead of waiting for instructions.
Whenever a challenge arises, he’s always the one to deliver the message to Garcia without complaint.

Synset Examples

In business, sometimes you need to deliver the message to Garcia directly.
She was determined to deliver the message to Garcia without fail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take initiative - begin a task or undertaking independently
  • Antonym: to procrastinate - delay or postpone action

Etymology

From Elbert Hubbard's 1899 essay about delivering a message to General García during the Spanish–American War.
