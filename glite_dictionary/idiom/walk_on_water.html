to walk on water

1. VERB

/ wɑk ɑn ˈwɔtər / (AmE), / wɔːk ɒn ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
to appear to have extraordinary abilities, success, or fortune, often to the point of being considered almost miraculous
seemingly have extraordinary abilities or luck

Examples

Many fans believe the new coach can walk on water after winning the championship in his first year.
She doesn't walk on water, but her dedication makes her a standout employee.
He likes to pretend he can walk on water, but even he faces challenges sometimes.

Synset Examples

He acts like he can walk on water, but he’s just lucky.
That athlete can’t walk on water, no matter how great he is.
To her fans, she seemed to walk on water with her talent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miracle worker - someone who performs remarkable feats
  • Antonym: ordinary - not exceptional, just usual or average

Etymology

Alludes to the biblical story of Jesus walking on water (Matthew 14:22–33).
