the jaws of defeat

1. NOUN

/ ðə dʒɔz əv dɪˈfit / (AmE), / ðə dʒɔːz əv dɪˈfiːt / (BrE)
a point at which defeat seems certain or imminent, often used to describe a situation narrowly avoided or overcome
at the point where defeat seems certain

Examples

The team managed to escape the jaws of defeat with a last-second comeback.
He pulled his company out of the jaws of defeat by securing a crucial contract.
She was on the verge of losing but snatched success from the jaws of defeat.

Synset Examples

With a last-minute goal, they snatched victory from the jaws of defeat.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: the jaws of victory - point at which victory seems certain
