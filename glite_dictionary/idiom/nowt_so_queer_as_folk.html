nowt so queer as folk

1. X

/ naʊt soʊ kwɪr æz foʊk / (AmE), / naʊt səʊ kwɪə æz fəʊk / (BrE)
used to express the idea that people can be very strange or unpredictable
people are strange or unpredictable (British idiom)
british

Examples

After everything I've seen in this job, I really believe there's nowt so queer as folk.
She shook her head at their behavior; well, nowt so queer as folk, she thought.
Every time I think I've seen it all, something new happens—nowt so queer as folk.

Synset Examples

Whenever I travel, I remember there's nowt so queer as folk.
I've seen some strange things; there's nowt so queer as folk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: there's no accounting for taste - people's preferences can be surprising

Etymology

From English dialect 'nowt' meaning 'nothing'; popular in Northern England.
