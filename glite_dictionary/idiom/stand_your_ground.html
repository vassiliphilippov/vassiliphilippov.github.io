to stand your ground

1. VERB

/ stænd jər ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / stænd jɔː ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
to refuse to change your opinion or position when faced with opposition or pressure
refuse to change position or opinion

Examples

When challenged by the committee, she chose to stand your ground and defend her proposal.
It's important to stand your ground when negotiating your salary.
Even under intense criticism, he stood your ground and did not back down.

Synset Examples

It's vital to stand your ground in negotiations.
Don't buckle to demands; stand your ground.
Don't be such a softy; stand your ground!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: yield - to give way or surrender under pressure
  • Synonym: to hold firm - to remain steadfast in one's views or position
