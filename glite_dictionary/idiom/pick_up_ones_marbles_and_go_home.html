to pick up one's marbles and go home

1. VERB

/ pɪk ʌp wʌnz ˈmɑrbəlz ənd ɡoʊ hoʊm / (AmE), / pɪk ʌp wʌnz ˈmɑːbəlz ənd ɡəʊ həʊm / (BrE)
to withdraw from a situation, organization, or activity in response to frustration or not getting one's way
withdraw in frustration or protest

Examples

After losing the debate, he decided to pick up his marbles and go home instead of continuing the conversation.
Sometimes, when negotiations stall, one side will pick up their marbles and go home.
Rather than compromise, she chose to pick up her marbles and go home, leaving the team in a difficult position.

Synset Examples

She felt frustrated and wanted to pick up her marbles and go home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to quit - to stop or leave a job, activity, or place
  • Synonym: to storm off - to leave angrily, especially in protest or frustration

Etymology

From the practice of children taking their marbles and leaving a game when upset; first recorded in American English in the mid-20th century.
