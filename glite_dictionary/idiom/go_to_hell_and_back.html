to go to hell and back

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ tə hɛl ənd bæk / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə hɛl ənd bæk / (BrE)
to experience very severe difficulties, suffering, or challenges and survive or recover from them
endure extreme difficulties and survive

Examples

After recovering from his illness, his friends said he had gone to hell and back.
She felt like she had gone to hell and back after losing her job and her house.
He truly understands struggle—he’s gone to hell and back for his family.

Synset Examples

After going to hell and back, she finally succeeded.
He’s been to hell and back in his career.
She's been to hell and back during this project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be through the mill - to have experienced a lot of difficulties or hardships
  • Synonym: have a rough ride - to have a difficult or challenging experience
