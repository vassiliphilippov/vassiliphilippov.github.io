at square one

1. ADV

/ ət skwɛr wʌn / (AmE), / ət skwɛə wʌn / (BrE)
At the original starting point, especially after a failure or setback; having to begin again from the beginning.
back to the starting point

Examples

After months of work, the project collapsed and we were at square one again.
Her idea didn't work out, so now she's at square one with her research.
Every time we think we've solved the problem, something unexpected happens and we're back at square one.

Synset Examples

The negotiations fell apart, leaving us at square one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: back to the drawing board - to start again after a failure

Etymology

Possibly from board games or televised instructional guides in the 20th century, indicating the first step or starting position.
