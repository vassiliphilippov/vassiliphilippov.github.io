what you see is what you get

1. X

/ wʌt ju si ɪz wʌt ju ɡɛt / (AmE), / wɒt juː siː ɪz wɒt juː ɡɛt / (BrE)
used to say that someone or something is exactly as they appear to be, with no hidden qualities or deceptive features.
things are as they appear, nothing hidden

Examples

Her personality is refreshing; what you see is what you get.
With this product, what you see is what you get—no gimmicks or surprises.
The candidate's approach is simple: what you see is what you get.

Synset Examples

Her personality is refreshing; what you see is what you get.
I love this place; truly, what you see is what you get.
In this job, what you see is what you get.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: straightforward - direct and honest, with nothing hidden
  • Antonym: deceptive - having the power to deceive or mislead
