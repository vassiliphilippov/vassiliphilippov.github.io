to stall for time

1. VERB

/ stɔl fər taɪm / (AmE), / stɔːl fə taɪm / (BrE)
to deliberately delay taking action or making a decision in order to gain more time
to delay action to gain more time

Examples

I needed to stall for time until my friend arrived to help me.
She kept asking irrelevant questions to stall for time during the interview.
The negotiator tried to stall for time while a solution was being worked out.

Synset Examples

They had to stall for time until the results were ready.
He called a break to stall for time during the debate.
She tried to stall for time while thinking of an answer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to buy time - to make more time available, often by employing delaying tactics
  • Antonym: to waste time - to spend time doing something unnecessary or unproductive
