to talk someone under the table

1. VERB

/ tɑk ˈsʌmwʌn ˌʌndɚ ðə ˈteɪbəl / (AmE), / tɔːk ˈsʌmwʌn ˌʌndə ðə ˈteɪbl̩ / (BrE)
to talk more skillfully, persuasively, or at greater length than someone else in conversation, often overwhelming or outsmarting them
outtalk or outsmart someone in conversation

Examples

She could talk someone under the table when it came to politics.
No matter how hard he tried, he couldn't talk his boss under the table during the meeting.
They both enjoy debating, but only Maria can talk people under the table every time.

Synset Examples

He can talk me under the table any day.
He’s so persuasive, he could talk anyone under the table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outtalk - to talk more than or better than someone else
  • Synonym: to outsmart - to defeat someone by being more clever
