other fish to fry

1. X

/ ˈʌðɚ fɪʃ tu ˈfraɪ / (AmE), / ˈʌðə fɪʃ tʊ ˈfraɪ / (BrE)
to have more important or more urgent matters to deal with than what is currently being discussed
have more important matters to do

Examples

I would help, but I have other fish to fry at the moment.
She declined the invitation because she had other fish to fry.
Let's move on; we all have other fish to fry today.

Synset Examples

They have other fish to fry besides this minor issue.
I have other fish to fry today, so I can't help.
She’s busy; she has other fish to fry today.
I can't help you now; I have other fish to fry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bigger fish to fry - to have more important matters to attend to
