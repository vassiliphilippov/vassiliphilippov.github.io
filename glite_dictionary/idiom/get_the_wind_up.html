to get the wind up

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ðə wɪnd ʌp / (AmE), / ɡɛt ðə wɪnd ʌp / (BrE)
to become anxious or nervous, often suddenly or unexpectedly
become anxious or nervous
british informal

Examples

She always gets the wind up before public speaking.
The unexpected news really got the wind up in the office.
You could tell he got the wind up when he saw the exam paper.

Synset Examples

She always gets the wind up before public speaking.
The unexpected news really got the wind up in the office.
His sudden change of plans got the wind up among his friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get nervous - to become worried or anxious
  • Antonym: to relax - to become less tense or anxious

Etymology

British slang dating from early 20th century, possibly from the military phrase 'to get the wind up', meaning to become alarmed.
