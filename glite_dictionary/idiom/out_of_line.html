out of line

1. ADJ

/ aʊt əv laɪn / (AmE), / aʊt əv laɪn / (BrE)
behaving in a way that is inappropriate, unacceptable, or not in accordance with expected standards
inappropriate or unacceptable behavior

Examples

Your comments about the manager were out of line and could cause trouble.
It's out of line to interrupt someone while they're speaking.
The coach warned him that his actions were out of line with team rules.

Synset Examples

The employee was reprimanded for being out of line.
Her behavior was out of line for someone in her position.
His remarks were completely out of line at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: out of order - not acceptable or not behaving appropriately
  • Synonym: out of bounds - beyond the limits of acceptable behavior
  • Antonym: acceptable - meeting certain standards of appropriate behavior
