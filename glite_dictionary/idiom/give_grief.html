to give grief

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ɡriːf / (AmE), / ɡɪv ɡriːf / (BrE)
to criticize or complain about someone persistently, often causing annoyance or distress
criticize or complain about someone, often persistently
informal

Examples

My boss tends to give grief to anyone who makes a small mistake.
Why do you always give grief to your friend for being late?
If you give grief to your siblings all the time, they'll start avoiding you.

Synset Examples

She always seems to give me grief about my decisions.
Stop giving your sister grief over her choices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give someone a hard time - to criticize, tease, or harass someone persistently
