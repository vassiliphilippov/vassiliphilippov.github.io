to hold someone's hand

1. VERB

/ hoʊld səmˌwʌnz hænd / (AmE), / həʊld səmˌwʌnz hænd / (BrE)
to provide support, comfort, or guidance to someone, especially during a difficult or unfamiliar situation
give support or guidance

Examples

Don't worry, I'll hold your hand throughout the registration process.
She's very nervous about starting her new job and needs someone to hold her hand at first.
The teacher didn't want to hold the students' hands for every step, encouraging independence instead.

Synset Examples

He offered to hold her hand while she spoke.
She needs someone to hold her hand through this process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - to give help or encouragement to someone
  • Synonym: guide - to show someone the way; to lead or direct

2. VERB

/ hoʊld ˈsʌmwʌnˌz hænd / (AmE), / həʊld ˈsʌmwʌnz hænd / (BrE)
to guide or support someone carefully, especially when they are inexperienced or anxious
give careful guidance or support

Examples

I know this process is new, but I can't hold your hand through every step.
She no longer needed anyone to hold her hand at meetings.
During my first week, my manager really held my hand and made me feel comfortable.

Synset Examples

The teacher will hold your hand during the test.

Related Terms
