to cut bait

1. VERB

/ kʌt ˈbeɪt / (AmE), / kʌt ˈbeɪt / (BrE)
to stop wasting time and either take decisive action or abandon an unproductive effort
stop wasting time and act or give up

Examples

After months of delays, the boss told his team to cut bait and move forward with another plan.
If this partnership isn’t working, maybe it’s time to cut bait and focus elsewhere.
He realized the project was going nowhere and decided to cut bait before wasting further resources.

Synset Examples

It's time to cut bait and find a new strategy.
If you're not committed, it's best to cut bait now.
After months of no progress, he decided to cut bait.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give up - to stop trying to do something difficult
  • Synonym: to take action - to do something to achieve a result rather than hesitate or delay

Etymology

From the practice of fishing, where a person either continues to fish or cuts bait to prepare for a different action.
