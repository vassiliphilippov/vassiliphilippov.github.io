an eye for an eye, a tooth for a tooth

1. X

/ ən aɪ fər ən aɪ ə tuθ fər ə tuθ / (AmE), / ən aɪ fə ən aɪ ə tuːθ fə ə tuːθ / (BrE)
the principle that a person who has injured another should receive a punishment or retribution equal to the injury inflicted
exact retribution; equal retaliation

Examples

The principle of an eye for an eye, a tooth for a tooth was applied in the old law code.
Their feud became a cycle of an eye for an eye, a tooth for a tooth, with each side seeking revenge.
Some argue that justice should not be based on an eye for an eye, a tooth for a tooth.

Synset Examples

The conflict escalated into an eye for an eye, a tooth for a tooth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tit for tat - retaliation in kind
  • Antonym: to forgive and forget - to let go of grievances without seeking retribution

Etymology

From ancient legal codes, notably the Biblical Hebrew phrase 'ayin tachat ayin, shen tachat shen' (Exodus 21:24).
