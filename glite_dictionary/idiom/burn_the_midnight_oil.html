to burn the midnight oil

1. VERB

/ bɜrn ðə ˈmɪdnaɪt ɔɪl / (AmE), / bɜːn ðə ˈmɪdnaɪt ɔɪl / (BrE)
to work or study late into the night, especially to meet a deadline or finish a task
work or study late at night

Examples

During finals week, many students burn the midnight oil to prepare for their exams.
She had to burn the midnight oil to get her report finished on time.
Mark often burns the midnight oil when working on important projects.

Synset Examples

She often burns the midnight oil studying for exams.
He burned the midnight oil to meet the deadline.
I had to burn the midnight oil to finish my project.
He worked Chinese overtime last week, burning the midnight oil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pull an all-nighter - to stay awake all night working or studying

Etymology

Refers to studying or working by lamplight before the advent of electric lighting.
