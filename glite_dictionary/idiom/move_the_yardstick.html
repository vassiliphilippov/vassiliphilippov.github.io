to move the yardstick

1. VERB

/ muv ðə ˈjɑrdˌstɪk / (AmE), / muːv ðə ˈjɑːdˌstɪk / (BrE)
to change the standards or criteria used to judge success, often making achievement more difficult or different than before
change the standards or criteria

Examples

Whenever the team met their targets, management would move the yardstick to keep them striving for more.
It's frustrating when they move the yardstick after we've already started the evaluation process.
The school board decided to move the yardstick by raising the academic standards this year.

Synset Examples

It's time to move the yardsticks on our sales targets.
The new policy will move the yardsticks for employee performance.
They decided to move the yardsticks on project deadlines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: move the goalposts - change the rules or standards, making them more difficult

Etymology

From the use of 'yardstick' as a standard unit of measurement in English-speaking countries.
