blot on the escutcheon

1. NOUN

/ blɑt ɑn ði ɪˈskʌtʃən / (AmE), / blɒt ɒn ði ɪˈskʌtʃɪən / (BrE)
an event or fact that damages someone's reputation or record; a stain of disgrace
damage to one's reputation

Examples

The scandal proved to be a lasting blot on the escutcheon of the entire organization.
For years, the incident remained the only blot on the escutcheon of his otherwise stellar career.
No amount of good deeds could remove that blot on the escutcheon from her public image.

Synset Examples

The mistake was a minor blot on the escutcheon of an otherwise perfect report.
Her DUI was a serious blot on the escutcheon of her reputation.
His scandal was a blot on the escutcheon of his career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stigma - a mark of disgrace or infamy
  • Synonym: blemish - a small mark or flaw that spoils the appearance or reputation

Etymology

From heraldry, where an 'escutcheon' is a shield; a 'blot' would mar its honor.
