to fend for oneself

1. VERB

/ fɛnd fər wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / fɛnd fə wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to look after oneself without help from others; to be self-reliant
look after oneself without help

Examples

After losing his job, he had to fend for himself for the first time.
Wild animals learn quickly how to fend for themselves in the forest.
When her parents traveled abroad, she was left to fend for herself at home.

Synset Examples

She had to fend and prove herself in a male-dominated field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: look after oneself - to care for oneself independently
  • Antonym: depend on others - to rely on help from other people
