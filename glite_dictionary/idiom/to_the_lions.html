to the lions

1. X

/ tə ðə ˈlaɪ.ənz / (AmE), / tə ðə ˈlaɪ.ənz / (BrE)
to put someone in a situation where they are likely to face severe criticism, difficulty, or danger, often without adequate preparation or support
expose to a harsh situation

Examples

The manager sent the intern to the lions during the board meeting without any warning.
On her first day, she was thrown to the lions by being assigned the toughest client.
When negotiations fell apart, the spokesman found himself to the lions at the press conference.

Synset Examples

They threw the new employee to the lions in that meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to throw to the wolves - to abandon someone to criticism or attack without support
  • Synonym: in at the deep end - to place someone in a difficult situation without preparation

Etymology

Alludes to the practice in ancient Rome of throwing people to lions as punishment or public spectacle.
