on it like a car bonnet

1. X

/ ˈɑn ɪt laɪk ə kɑr ˈbɑnɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒn ɪt laɪk ə kɑː ˈbɒnɪt / (BrE)
Extremely focused, attentive, or quick to act on something.
very quick and focused to act
informal british

Examples

Whenever there's a problem, she's on it like a car bonnet before anyone else even notices.
As soon as the news broke, the press were on it like a car bonnet.
If you need that report finished tonight, I'll be on it like a car bonnet.

Synset Examples

When the project deadline approached, she was on it like a car bonnet.
The team was on it like a car bonnet during the final push.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on the ball - alert, attentive, and quick to react
  • Antonym: asleep at the wheel - not paying attention or failing to act when needed

Etymology

Slang formation in British English; 'car bonnet' rhymes with 'on it' and is used for emphasis.
