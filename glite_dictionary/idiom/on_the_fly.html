on the fly

1. ADV

/ ɑn ðə flaɪ / (AmE), / ɒn ðə flaɪ / (BrE)
while in motion or without stopping; during the course of an activity.
while in motion; without stopping

Examples

He fixed the report on the fly as new information came in.
The coach made a decision on the fly during the game.
She grabbed a sandwich on the fly on her way to the meeting.

Synset Examples

he caught the punt on the fly

2. ADV

/ ɑn ðə flaɪ / (AmE), / ɒn ðə flaɪ / (BrE)
while doing something quickly or without stopping; in a hurry or without preparation
while in a hurry or without preparation

Examples

She made the decision on the fly, without stopping to think it through.
The chef whipped up a new dish on the fly for the customer.
He fixed the bug on the fly during the presentation.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in a rush - in a hurry, without much time

/ ɑn ðə flaɪ / (AmE), / ɒn ðə flaɪ / (BrE)

quickly, without preparation

Examples

The chef can prepare meals on the fly during busy service.
