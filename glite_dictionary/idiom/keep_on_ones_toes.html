to keep on (one's) toes

1. VERB

/ kip ɑn ðɛr toʊz / (AmE), / kiːp ɒn ðeə təʊz / (BrE)
to cause someone to remain alert, attentive, and ready for unexpected situations
cause to stay alert and attentive

Examples

Working in a busy newsroom really keeps me on my toes every day.
The coach's tough training methods kept the players on their toes throughout the season.
Unpredictable clients often keep us on our toes at work.

Synset Examples

As a dribbler, he always kept the defense on their toes.
Her unpredictable schedule keeps him on his toes.
The new manager's style really keeps everyone on their toes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keep alert - to remain watchful and attentive
  • Antonym: to catch off guard - to surprise someone who is unprepared
