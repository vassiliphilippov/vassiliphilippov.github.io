half an eye

1. NOUN

/ hæf ən aɪ / (AmE), / hɑːf ən aɪ / (BrE)
used to indicate that someone notices or understands something with minimal attention or effort
with minimal attention or effort

Examples

She noticed the change in his mood with half an eye.
Even with half an eye, he could tell things were not going well.
You could see with half an eye that the answer was wrong.

Synset Examples

I can see with half an eye that you’re not paying attention.
You can see with half an eye that they’re lying.
He can see with half an eye that something’s wrong.

  • Degree Lower: glimpse - a brief or quick view or look
