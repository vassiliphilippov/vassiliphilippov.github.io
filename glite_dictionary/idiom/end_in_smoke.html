to end in smoke

1. VERB

/ ɛnd ɪn smoʊk / (AmE), / end ɪn sməʊk / (BrE)
to result in complete failure or to produce no significant result
to result in failure or nothing

Examples

Despite their high hopes, the project would only end in smoke if they ignored the experts.
Countless negotiations have ended in smoke over the years due to stubborn disagreements.
Her plans to start a new business may end in smoke without proper funding.

Synset Examples

Those negotiations seem to end in smoke every time.
Their plans to go hiking ended in smoke due to the rain.
All our efforts to save the project ended in smoke.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to come to nothing - to fail to achieve a desired result
  • Antonym: to succeed - to achieve the desired outcome
