to howl at the moon

1. VERB

/ haʊl æt ðə muːn / (AmE), / haʊl æt ðə muːn / (BrE)
to express frustration, longing, or uncontrollable emotion, often in a futile or dramatic way
express strong emotion or longing

Examples

After losing the game, he wanted nothing more than to howl at the moon in frustration.
Some people howl at the moon when faced with challenges they cannot change.
She spent the night alone, feeling the urge to howl at the moon over her lost love.

Synset Examples

They gathered outside to howl at the moon together.
Sometimes, you just need to go howl at the moon.
The kids love to howl at the moon during camp.
Sometimes, I just want to howl at the moon.

2. VERB

/ haʊl æt ðə mun / (AmE), / haʊl ət ðə muːn / (BrE)
to make a loud, prolonged cry, especially at night, often expressing strong emotion or in reference to a wild animal such as a wolf or jackal; sometimes used figuratively for expressing frustration or longing.
make a loud cry at night like a wolf or jackal

Examples

The wolves howled at the moon all night, sending chills down our spines.
Sometimes people just need to howl at the moon to let out their frustration.
She stood in the empty field and howled at the moon, feeling free and wild.

Synset Examples

The jackal howled at the moon, echoing through the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: howl - to make a long, loud, wailing cry
