crab mentality

1. NOUN

/ kræb mɛnˈtæləti / (AmE), / kræb mɛnˈtalɪti / (BrE)
the tendency to undermine or pull down others who are succeeding, often out of jealousy or competitiveness
undermining others' success out of jealousy

Examples

If we want to grow as a team, we must avoid the crab mentality and support each other's achievements.
The prevalence of crab mentality in the workplace can hinder progress and innovation.
Her lecture addressed the effects of crab mentality on community development.

Synset Examples

She warned against the crab mentality in our group.
The crab mentality is toxic to team spirit.
His crab mentality prevents others from succeeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tall poppy syndrome - resentment or criticism of people who are successful or distinguished
  • Antonym: supportive - providing encouragement or emotional help to others

Etymology

Derived from the metaphor of crabs pulling each other back in a bucket, preventing any from escaping.
