to soften the blow

1. VERB

/ ˈsɔfən ðə bloʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɒfən ðə bləʊ / (BrE)
to make an unpleasant or painful situation less severe or easier to accept
make bad news or events easier to accept

Examples

She tried to soften the blow by being gentle when giving me the bad news.
The company offered extra benefits to soften the blow of the layoffs.
A sincere apology can sometimes soften the blow of harsh criticism.

Synset Examples

She used humor to soften the blow of rejection.
He tried to soften the blow of the bad news.
A kind approach can help soften the blow of criticism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cushion the blow - to make something unpleasant less severe
