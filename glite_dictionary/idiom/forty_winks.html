forty winks

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔr.ti ˈwɪŋks / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.ti ˈwɪŋks / (BrE)
a short sleep or nap, especially not in bed
a brief nap; short sleep

Examples

After lunch, he closed his eyes and had forty winks before returning to work.
She likes to catch forty winks on the sofa during the afternoon.
Travelers often try to get forty winks at the airport between flights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nap - a short sleep, especially during the day
  • Synonym: doze - to sleep lightly for a short period

Etymology

From the phrase 'forty winks', first recorded in the early 19th century, with 'wink' meaning 'a brief sleep'.
