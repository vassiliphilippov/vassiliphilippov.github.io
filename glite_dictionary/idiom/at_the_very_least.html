at the very least

1. ADV

/ ət ðə ˈvɛri list / (AmE), / ət ðə ˈvɛri liːst / (BrE)
used to emphasize that the stated amount or action is the minimum necessary, expected, or likely.
minimum requirement or expectation

Examples

You should bring a coat; at the very least, take an umbrella in case it rains.
We should try to finish the report today, or at the very least, complete the most important sections.
He should apologize at the very least for his mistake, if not make things right.

Synset Examples

You should call them at the very least.
At the very least, we need to finish this today.
You should apologize, at the very least.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at least - used to indicate the minimum amount or degree
