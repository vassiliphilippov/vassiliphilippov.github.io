to play possum

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ˈpɑsəm / (AmE), / pleɪ ˈpɒsəm / (BrE)
to pretend to be dead or unconscious, especially to avoid danger or attention
pretend to be dead/unconscious

Examples

The child decided to play possum when it was time for chores, hoping her mother would leave her alone.
When the intruder entered the cabin, he played possum until the danger had passed.
Some animals play possum as a survival strategy to escape predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feign death - to fake being dead
  • Antonym: to come to - to regain consciousness

Etymology

From the behavior of opossums, which feign death as a defense mechanism.

2. VERB

/ pleɪ ˈpɑsəm / (AmE), / pleɪ ˈpɒsəm / (BrE)
to pretend to be dead, asleep, or inactive, especially to avoid attention or confrontation
pretend to be dead or inactive

Examples

When the children heard their parents coming, they decided to play possum and pretended to be asleep.
Some animals play possum to avoid predators in the wild.
Instead of answering the difficult question, he tried to play possum during the meeting.

Synset Examples

When confronted, he chose to play possum and avoid conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feign death - to pretend to be dead, often as a protective strategy
  • Antonym: face up to - to confront or deal directly with a problem or situation
