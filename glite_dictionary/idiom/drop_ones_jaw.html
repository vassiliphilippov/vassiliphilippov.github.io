to drop one's jaw

1. VERB

/ drɑp wʌnz dʒɑ / (AmE), / drɒp wʌnz dʒɑː / (BrE)
to open one's mouth wide in surprise or amazement
open mouth in surprise

Examples

His jaw dropped when he saw the final exam questions.
When she entered in that dress, everyone's jaw dropped in astonishment.
My jaw dropped at the breathtaking view from the top of the mountain.

Synset Examples

She dropped her jaw in disbelief at the news.
The news of his promotion made her jaw drop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jaw drop - to become very surprised or shocked
