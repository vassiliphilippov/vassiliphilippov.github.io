to wring every bit of information

1. VERB

/ rɪŋ ˈɛvri bɪt əv ˌɪnfərˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪŋ ˈɛvri bɪt əv ˌɪnfəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
to extract all possible information from a source or situation, often with persistence or effort
extract all possible information

Examples

The journalist tried to wring every bit of information out of the witness during the interview.
She managed to wring every bit of information from the old documents before they deteriorated.
Investigators worked late into the night to wring every bit of information from the computer data.

Synset Examples

They wring every bit of information from the interview.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squeeze information out of - to obtain information through persistent questioning or effort

Etymology

From the verb 'wring', meaning to forcefully extract or squeeze, figuratively applied to obtaining information.
