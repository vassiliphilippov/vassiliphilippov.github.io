to go their separate ways

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ðɛr ˈsɛpɚət weɪz / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ðeə ˈsɛpərət weɪz / (BrE)
to part company and pursue different paths, especially after a relationship or collaboration ends
to part ways or split up

Examples

After graduation, most friends go their separate ways and start new chapters in their lives.
Once the project was completed, the team members decided to go their separate ways.
After their long journey together, the travelers chose to go their separate ways.

Synset Examples

The band members agreed to go their separate ways after the tour.
Eventually, all the students will go their separate ways after graduation.
After years of friendship, they decided to go their separate ways.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: split up - to end a relationship or partnership
  • Synonym: part company - to stop associating together; separate
