done and dusted

1. ADJ

/ dʌn ən ˈdʌstɪd / (AmE), / dʌn ənd ˈdʌstɪd / (BrE)
completely finished or brought to a satisfactory conclusion; settled
completely finished, settled

Examples

The deal is done and dusted, so you can relax now.
Once the repairs were done and dusted, everyone was relieved.
Let’s get this paperwork done and dusted before lunchtime.

Synset Examples

Once the paperwork is signed, we’re done and dusted.
The event was done and dusted by noon.
With the presentation finished, we’re all done and dusted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finished - completed, brought to an end
  • Antonym: ongoing - still in progress, not completed

Etymology

British English, likely originated in the mid-20th century from the practice of cleaning up after a task; 'dusted' meaning 'completed'.
