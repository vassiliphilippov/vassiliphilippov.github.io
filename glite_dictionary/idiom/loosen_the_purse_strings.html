to loosen the purse strings

1. VERB

/ ˈlusən ðə pɝs strɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈluːsən ðə pɜːs strɪŋz / (BrE)
to become less strict about spending money; to allow more money to be spent
spend money more freely

Examples

The board decided to loosen the purse strings to fund new research and development.
Parents may loosen the purse strings during the holiday season to buy gifts for their children.
If the city wants to improve public transport, it must loosen the purse strings.

Synset Examples

The new project required the manager to loosen the purse strings a bit.
If we want better resources, we must loosen the purse strings.
The charity event encouraged people to loosen the purse strings for a good cause.
He was reluctant to loosen the pursestring for unnecessary expenses.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to tighten the purse strings - to become more strict about spending money; to reduce spending

Etymology

Alludes to opening one's purse to allow for easier removal of money.
