to do it tough

1. VERB

/ du ɪt tʌf / (AmE), / duː ɪt tʌf / (BrE)
to experience or endure difficult circumstances, especially with perseverance
endure hardship
informal

Examples

Many families have to do it tough during economic downturns.
She really did it tough after losing her job but never gave up.
If you're willing to do it tough, you will appreciate your success even more.

Synset Examples

Sometimes, you just have to do it tough and keep going.
He had to do it tough while adjusting to college.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tough it out - to endure a difficult or painful situation
  • Synonym: struggle - to experience difficulty or make strenuous effort
