over-the-hill

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvər ðə ˈhɪl / (AmE), / ˌəʊvə ðə ˈhɪl / (BrE)
no longer as effective, capable, or attractive as one once was; considered too old to be useful or desirable
too old to be useful or effective

Examples

Many people think turning forty means you're over-the-hill, but that's just a stereotype.
The actor refuses to accept that he is over-the-hill and continues to seek leading roles.
She joked about being over-the-hill when she got her first pair of reading glasses.

Synset Examples

He left the house … for the support of twelve superannuated wool carders

Related Terms

  • Synonym: past it - no longer able to do something well due to age
  • Antonym: in one's prime - at the peak of physical or mental ability
