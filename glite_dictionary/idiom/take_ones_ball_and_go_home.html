to take one's ball and go home

1. VERB

/ teɪk wʌnz bɔl ænd ɡoʊ hoʊm / (AmE), / teɪk wʌnz bɔːl ənd ɡəʊ həʊm / (BrE)
to withdraw from a situation out of displeasure, disappointment, or not getting one's way, often abruptly or childishly
withdraw due to disappointment

Examples

When the committee rejected his proposal, he chose to take his ball and go home instead of negotiating further.
She tends to take her ball and go home when things don't go exactly as she planned.
Rather than work through the disagreement, Mark decided to take his ball and go home.

Synset Examples

When he lost the game, he decided to take his ball and go home.
She always takes her ball and goes home when she loses.

Etymology

From the literal image of a child taking their ball away to end a game when upset.
