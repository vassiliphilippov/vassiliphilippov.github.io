to keep someone's head in the game

1. VERB

/ kip ˈsʌmwʌn(z) hɛd ɪn ðə ɡeɪm / (AmE), / kiːp ˈsʌmwən(z) hɛd ɪn ðə ɡeɪm / (BrE)
to remain focused and attentive, especially in a challenging situation
stay focused and attentive

Examples

If you want to finish this project on time, you need to keep your head in the game.
Despite the noisy crowd, Rachel managed to keep her head in the game and scored the winning goal.
It's important to keep your head in the game when things start to get tough.

Synset Examples

To win, you must keep your head in the game.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lose your head - to lose self-control or panic in a stressful situation
