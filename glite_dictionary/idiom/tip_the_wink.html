to tip the wink

1. VERB

/ tɪp ðə wɪŋk / (AmE), / tɪp ðə wɪŋk / (BrE)
to give someone a secret or discreet signal or hint
give a discreet signal or hint
british

Examples

During the meeting, Sarah tipped the wink to let me know it was time to speak.
He quietly tipped the wink to his colleague about the upcoming announcement.
Before the inspector arrived, someone had tipped the wink to the factory workers.

Synset Examples

He gave me a tip the wink to start the surprise.
He tipped the wink, and the plan was set in motion.
She tipped the wink to her friend during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: give a hint - impart a subtle suggestion or clue
  • Synonym: signal - to convey information subtly or indirectly

Etymology

From British English slang of the 18th century, combining 'tip' (to give, inform) and 'wink' (a silent signal with the eye).
