lost in the shuffle

1. ADJ

/ lɔst ɪn ðə ˈʃʌfəl / (AmE), / lɒst ɪn ðə ˈʃʌfəl / (BrE)
not noticed, overlooked, or forgotten because of confusion or disorganization
overlooked or forgotten amid confusion

Examples

Several patients' records got lost in the shuffle during the hospital's move.
Important tasks can easily become lost in the shuffle when deadlines pile up.
Her request for support was lost in the shuffle and never received a response.

Synset Examples

A few details were lost in the shuffle during the meeting.
Her important email was lost in the shuffle of messages.
My application got lost in the shuffle at the office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overlooked - not noticed or missed
  • Antonym: noticed - to be seen or observed

Etymology

Originally from the image of playing cards being shuffled, where individual cards can easily be missed or misplaced.
