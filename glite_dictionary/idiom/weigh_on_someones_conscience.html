to weigh on someone's conscience

1. VERB

/ weɪ ɑn ˈsʌmwʌnz kɑnʃəns / (AmE), / weɪ ɒn ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈkɒnʃəns / (BrE)
to make someone feel guilty or troubled over something they have done or failed to do
cause someone to feel guilty or troubled

Examples

His mistake continued to weigh on his conscience long after the incident.
She apologized because the lie had started to weigh on her conscience.
The secret didn't weigh on his conscience until he saw the consequences.

Synset Examples

The decision could weigh on his conscience for years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to weigh on someone's mind - to make someone feel worried or anxious
