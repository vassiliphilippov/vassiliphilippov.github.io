to keep one's distance

1. VERB

/ kip wʌnz ˈdɪstəns / (AmE), / kiːp wʌnz ˈdɪstəns / (BrE)
to deliberately stay away from someone or something; to avoid close contact or involvement
deliberately stay away or avoid

Examples

The police advised us to keep our distance from the crime scene.
Since their argument, Daniel has tried to keep his distance from Maria.
When encountering wild animals, it's safest to keep your distance.

Synset Examples

Keep your hands off my wife!
Keep your distance from this man — he is dangerous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to avoid - to stay away from someone or something
  • Antonym: approach - to come nearer to someone or something
