to render unto Caesar

1. VERB

/ ˈrɛndər ˌʌnˌtu ˈsiːzər / (AmE), / ˈrɛndə ˌʌnˌtuː ˈsiːzə / (BrE)
to give what is due or owed to authorities or the government, especially in the context of taxes or civic obligations
give the government its due

Examples

During audit season, it's best to render unto Caesar and file your taxes honestly.
While she disagreed with the new law, she chose to render unto Caesar and comply.
You may protest in other ways, but you must still render unto Caesar when it comes to civic duties.

Synset Examples

In tax season, it's wise to render unto Caesar without delay.
He often reminded us to render unto Caesar while helping the less fortunate.
We must render unto Caesar what is Caesar's and focus on our duties.

Etymology

From the biblical phrase in the Gospel of Matthew (22:21), 'Render therefore unto Caesar the things which are Caesar's.'
