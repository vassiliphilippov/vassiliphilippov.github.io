close call

1. NOUN

/ kloʊs kɔl / (AmE), / kləʊs kɔːl / (BrE)
a situation in which something bad nearly happens or is only narrowly avoided
a narrow escape or near miss

Examples

That was a close call; the car almost hit us at the intersection.
The team won the game, but it was a close call in the final minutes.
I made my flight, but it was a close call after the heavy traffic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: near miss - a situation in which something almost happened, especially something dangerous
