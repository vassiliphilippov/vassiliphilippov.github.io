to change one's tune

1. VERB

/ tʃeɪndʒ wʌnz tun / (AmE), / tʃeɪndʒ wʌnz tjuːn / (BrE)
to alter one's opinion, attitude, or manner, especially suddenly or after being persuaded or proved wrong
alter one's opinion or attitude

Examples

After the budget cuts, the director changed her tune and supported the new proposal.
He was against the plan at first, but he changed his tune after the latest report.
Once she realized the benefits, she quickly changed her tune about the project.

Synset Examples

After the meeting, she changed her tune about the project.
He changed his tune once he saw the evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: change one's mind - to decide to do something different from what was originally decided

Etymology

From the idea of playing or singing a different melody, figuratively referring to a shift in attitude or stance.
