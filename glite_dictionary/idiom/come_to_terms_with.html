to come to terms with

1. VERB

/ kʌm tə tɜrmz wɪθ / (AmE), / kʌm tə təːmz wɪð / (BrE)
to accept or learn to deal with an unpleasant or difficult situation
accept a difficult situation

Examples

After many months, she finally came to terms with her illness and started her treatment.
It's not easy to come to terms with the death of a loved one.
They are still trying to come to terms with the changes at work.

Synset Examples

It took him years to come to terms with his loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accept - to agree to or come to recognize a situation
  • Antonym: to deny - to refuse to accept or acknowledge
