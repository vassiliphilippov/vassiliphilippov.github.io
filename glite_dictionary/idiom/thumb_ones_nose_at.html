to thumb one's nose at

1. VERB

/ θʌm wʌnz noʊz æt / (AmE), / θʌm wʌnz nəʊz æt / (BrE)
to show open contempt or disrespect, especially by deliberately ignoring or defying authority or expectation
show open contempt or disrespect

Examples

The rebellious student thumbed his nose at the school's strict dress code.
She thumbs her nose at convention by wearing wild, colorful outfits to work.
Some companies thumb their nose at environmental regulations, putting profit before the planet.

Synset Examples

She thumbed her nose at the critics with her success.
They thumbed their noses at the rules and had fun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to defy - to openly resist or refuse to obey
  • Antonym: show respect for - to act in a way that respects someone or something

Etymology

Refers to the gesture of placing the thumb on the nose and wiggling the fingers, historically used as a sign of derision.

2. VERB

/ θʌm wʌnz noʊz æt / (AmE), / θʌm wʌnz nəʊz ət / (BrE)
to openly show disdain or disrespect for someone or something, especially by making a rude gesture
show open disrespect or defiance

Examples

Many drivers thumb their noses at the new speed limits.
He deliberately thumbed his nose at the committee's decision.
The rebellious artist thumbs her nose at traditional conventions in her work.

Synset Examples

He thumbed his nose at authority during the protest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to defy - to openly resist or refuse to obey
  • Antonym: respect - to show regard or esteem for; to honor
