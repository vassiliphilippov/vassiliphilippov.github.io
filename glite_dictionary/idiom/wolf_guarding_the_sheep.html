wolf guarding the sheep

1. X

/ wʊlf ɡɑrdɪŋ ðə ʃip / (AmE), / wʊlf ɡɑːdɪŋ ðə ʃiːp / (BrE)
a person who is responsible for protecting others but may themselves be a potential threat or have questionable motives
protector who may pose a danger

Examples

As the new compliance officer, she felt like a wolf guarding the sheep in her department.
Some critics argue that giving the company unchecked power is like a wolf guarding the sheep.
With his history of deception, many saw him as a wolf guarding the sheep in the negotiation process.

Synset Examples

He felt like a wolf guarding the sheep at the meeting.
In this project, he's the wolf guarding the sheep from distractions.
The manager is a wolf guarding the sheep, protecting his team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wolf in sheep's clothing - someone who hides malicious intent under a harmless appearance

Etymology

Alludes to traditional stories and proverbs about the danger of having a wolf as a guardian for sheep.
