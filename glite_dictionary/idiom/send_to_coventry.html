to send to Coventry

1. VERB

/ sɛnd tu ˈkʌvəntri / (AmE), / sɛnd tu ˈkɒvəntri / (BrE)
to deliberately ignore or ostracize someone, usually as a form of social punishment
deliberately ignore or ostracize someone
british

Examples

After the argument, the entire class decided to send him to Coventry for a week.
She was so upset that she chose to send her colleague to Coventry and stopped speaking to him.
Being sent to Coventry at work made him feel completely isolated from his coworkers.

Synset Examples

After their argument, she decided to send him to Coventry.
They ignored him completely, sending him to Coventry.
He felt like he was sent to Coventry after the fight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ostracize - to deliberately exclude from a group or society
  • Synonym: to shun - to avoid deliberately; to keep away from

Etymology

British idiom dating from the 18th century, origin possibly relating to the city of Coventry's association with social exclusion.
