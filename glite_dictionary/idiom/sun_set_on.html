to sun set on

1. VERB

/ sʌn sɛt ɑn / (AmE), / sʌn sɛt ɒn / (BrE)
to mark the end of a period, era, or relationship; to signify that something has finished or concluded
mark the end of a period or relationship

Examples

After years of success, the sun set on the company’s dominance in the market.
It felt bittersweet when the sun set on their long-standing partnership.
As the sun set on the dynasty, a new era began.

Synset Examples

Once the sun sets on their friendship, they drift apart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: draw to a close - to come to an end
  • Synonym: come to an end - to finish or stop

Etymology

From the natural phenomenon of the sun setting, symbolizing the end of the day, used metaphorically for endings.
