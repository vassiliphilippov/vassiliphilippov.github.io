to bite the bullet

1. VERB

/ baɪt ðə ˈbʊlɪt / (AmE), / baɪt ðə ˈbʊlɪt / (BrE)
to face a painful or difficult situation with courage and determination, even though it is unpleasant or challenging
face a difficult situation bravely

Examples

After months of procrastination, she finally decided to bite the bullet and start her thesis.
Sometimes you have to bite the bullet and tell your friend the truth, no matter how hard it is.
He knew the project would be tough, but he was ready to bite the bullet and give it his best effort.

Synset Examples

He had to bite the bullet and admit his mistake.
It's time to bite the bullet and start saving money.
Sometimes you just need to bite the bullet and do it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to face the music - to accept the unpleasant consequences of one's actions
  • Synonym: to grit one's teeth - to prepare oneself to endure something painful or unpleasant

Etymology

Originally from the practice of having soldiers bite on a bullet during surgery before anesthesia, to help them endure pain.
