to choke a horse

1. VERB

/ tʃoʊk ə hɔrs / (AmE), / tʃəʊk ə hɔːs / (BrE)
(of an amount) extremely large or excessive, especially of food or something being more than enough.
extremely excessive amount

Examples

He piled spaghetti on his plate—enough to choke a horse.
At Thanksgiving, there’s always food to choke a horse on the table.
She won a basket of chocolate bars—enough to choke a horse!

Synset Examples

He ate enough to choke a horse at dinner.
They prepared a feast enough to choke a horse.
She bought cookies enough to choke a horse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enough to choke a horse - so much of something (often food) that it is excessive
