pity pot

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪti pɑt / (AmE), / ˈpɪti pɒt / (BrE)
a state of self-pity, especially when someone dwells excessively on their own troubles or misfortunes
a state of self-pity
informal american

Examples

Whenever bad things happen to him, he sits on his pity pot and complains all day.
She refused to join me on the pity pot after my job loss, encouraging me to move on instead.
Don't stay on your pity pot too long—it's better to try doing something positive.

Synset Examples

It's easy to get stuck in the pity pot.
He was wallowing in the pity pot instead of moving forward.
She keeps her feelings in the pity pot too long.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-pity - excessive unhappiness over your own troubles
