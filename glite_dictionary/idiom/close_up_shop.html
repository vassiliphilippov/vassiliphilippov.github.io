to close up shop

1. VERB

/ kloʊz ʌp ʃɑp / (AmE), / kləʊz ʌp ʃɒp / (BrE)
to stop doing business or cease operations, either temporarily or permanently
to stop doing business

Examples

The bakery had to close up shop after fifty years in the neighborhood.
During the holidays, many small businesses close up shop for a week or two.
We should close up shop now before the storm gets worse.

Synset Examples

The store will close up shop for renovations next week.
They had to close up shop due to declining sales.
After a long day, we decided to close up shop early.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shut down - to stop operating a business, factory, or system
  • Antonym: open for business - to be actively trading or doing business
