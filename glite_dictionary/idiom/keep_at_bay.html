to keep at bay

1. VERB

/ kip ət beɪ / (AmE), / kiːp ət beɪ / (BrE)
to prevent something or someone from coming too close or causing trouble or harm
to prevent approach or harm

Examples

She tried to keep at bay the persistent worries that plagued her at night.
In the winter, villagers burned fires to keep at bay the wolves roaming nearby.
Regular exercise can help keep at bay many health problems.

Synset Examples

They used a fence to keep the wildlife at bay.
The dog barked to keep the intruders at bay.
She managed to keep her fears at bay yesterday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hold off - to delay or prevent from approaching
  • Synonym: to ward off - to protect or defend against something

Etymology

Likely from naval or hunting terminology, where 'bay' refers to the position of being held off by barking dogs or at a distance.
