the check is in the mail

1. X

/ ðə tʃɛk ɪz ɪn ðə meɪl / (AmE), / ðə tʃɛk ɪz ɪn ðə meɪl / (BrE)
used to indicate that payment has supposedly been sent, often used to delay or avoid payment or to make a promise that may not be fulfilled.
said when promising (often insincerely) that payment has been sent

Examples

Whenever I remind him about the rent, he just says the check is in the mail.
She assured the landlord that the check is in the mail, but he remained skeptical.
If your client tells you the check is in the mail, you should follow up until payment arrives.

Synset Examples

She said the check is in the mail, so hold tight.
Don't worry about the payment; the check is in the mail.
He reassured her with, 'The check is in the mail, trust me.'
