to grin and bear it

1. VERB

/ ɡrɪn ænd ˈbɛr ɪt / (AmE), / ɡrɪn ənd ˈbeə r ɪt / (BrE)
to accept an unpleasant situation without complaining or showing how upset you are
endure without complaint

Examples

Even though the service was slow, she chose to grin and bear it rather than make a fuss.
Sometimes we just have to grin and bear it when faced with unavoidable problems.
With a broken air conditioner on a hot day, he decided to grin and bear it until help arrived.

Synset Examples

She decided to grin and bear it during the long meeting.
Sometimes you just have to grin and bear it.
When life gets tough, I choose to grin and bear it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to put up with - to tolerate or endure something unpleasant
  • Antonym: to complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance
