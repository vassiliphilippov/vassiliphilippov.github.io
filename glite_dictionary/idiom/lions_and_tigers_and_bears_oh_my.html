lions and tigers and bears, oh my

1. X

/ ˈlaɪ.ənz ənd ˈtaɪ.ɡɚz ənd ˈbɛɹz oʊ ˈmaɪ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ.ənz ənd ˈtaɪ.ɡəz ənd ˈbeəz əʊ ˈmaɪ / (BrE)
an exclamation used humorously or theatrically to express fear, surprise, or apprehension, often in response to a daunting or overwhelming situation
used to express fear or surprise

Examples

The forest was dark and mysterious, so she whispered, 'lions and tigers and bears, oh my,' to break the tension.
When faced with endless paperwork, he sighed, 'lions and tigers and bears, oh my,' dreading the work ahead.
Before starting their first day at the new school, the twins jokingly chanted, 'lions and tigers and bears, oh my.'

Synset Examples

As we hiked through the forest, I joked, 'Lions and tigers and bears, oh my!'
The movie had a scene where they shouted, 'Lions and tigers and bears, oh my!'
When the kids saw the zoo, they exclaimed, 'Lions and tigers and bears, oh my!'

Etymology

Popularized by the 1939 film The Wizard of Oz, where it was chanted by characters entering a forest.
