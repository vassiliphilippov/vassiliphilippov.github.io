to boldly go where no one has gone before

1. VERB

/ ˈboʊldli ɡoʊ wɛr noʊ wʌn hæz ɡɔn bɪˈfɔr / (AmE), / ˈbəʊldli ɡəʊ weə nəʊ wʌn hæz ɡɒn bɪˈfɔː / (BrE)
to venture into unexplored, unprecedented, or unknown areas or situations; to do something completely new or different
venture into the unknown; pioneer

Examples

Scientists continue to boldly go where no one has gone before in the search for cures to rare diseases.
The company's new project aims to boldly go where no one has gone before in renewable energy technology.
Inspired by explorers, she wanted to boldly go where no one has gone before and travel to remote parts of the world.

Synset Examples

Their mission was to boldly go where no man has gone before.
In her career, she aims to boldly go where no man has gone before.
He dreamed of boldly going where no man has gone before.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explore new ground - to investigate or experience something novel or unknown
  • Synonym: chart new territory - to do something innovative or unprecedented

Etymology

Popularized by the opening narration of the TV series Star Trek (first aired 1966).
