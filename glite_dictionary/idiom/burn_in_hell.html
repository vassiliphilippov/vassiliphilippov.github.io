to burn in hell

1. VERB

/ bɝn ɪn hɛl / (AmE), / bɜːn ɪn hɛl / (BrE)
(of a person) to suffer eternal punishment or damnation, often expressed as a curse or wish for someone to endure severe suffering after death.
wish for someone to suffer eternal punishment
offensive

Examples

She was so angry at his betrayal that she screamed, "I hope you burn in hell!"
The villain in the movie tells the hero he will burn in hell for his actions.
After the argument, he muttered under his breath that his rival could burn in hell.

Synset Examples

After that betrayal, she wished he would burn in hell.
He said his rival can burn in hell for all he cares.
He jokingly told his enemies to burn in hell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go to hell - an expression wishing someone to suffer or be punished, often angrily
