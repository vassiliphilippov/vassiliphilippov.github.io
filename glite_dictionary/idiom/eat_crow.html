to eat crow

1. VERB

/ it kroʊ / (AmE), / iːt krəʊ / (BrE)
to admit that one was wrong or accept humiliation, often after being proved wrong in a dispute
admit being wrong; accept humiliation

Examples

After insisting he was right, he had to eat crow when the truth came out.
The coach was forced to eat crow after his team lost to the underdogs.
She didn’t want to eat crow, but an apology was necessary after her mistake.

Synset Examples

He had to eat crow after losing the bet.
You’ll eat crow once you see I was right.
She had to eat crow when she apologized.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to eat humble pie - to admit one is wrong and apologize
  • Antonym: to save face - to avoid humiliation or preserve dignity

Etymology

American origin (mid-19th century); from the unappetizing idea of being forced to eat a crow as a punishment for error.
