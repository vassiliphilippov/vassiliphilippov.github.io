to make the cut

1. VERB

/ meɪk ðə kʌt / (AmE), / meɪk ðə kʌt / (BrE)
to be chosen or selected for a team, group, or opportunity, often after a selection process
be selected or chosen

Examples

After months of auditions, only ten singers will make the cut for the final performance.
Despite his efforts, Tom didn't make the cut for the basketball team this year.
If you want to make the cut, you need to score above 90 on the test.

Synset Examples

He was thrilled to make the cut for the team.
She worked hard to ensure she would make the cut.
Only the top students will make the cut for the scholarship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be selected - to be chosen as part of a group
  • Antonym: be rejected - to not be chosen as part of a group
