far cry

1. NOUN

/ fɑr kraɪ / (AmE), / fɑː kraɪ / (BrE)
a long distance away, originally referring to the distance over which a shout or cry can be heard
a long distance away

Examples

The village is a far cry from the nearest town.
Her cottage is a far cry from the city center.
It's a far cry from here to the next gas station.

Synset Examples

it's a far cry from here

Etymology

Originally referred to the distance over which a human cry could be heard; recorded since the 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ fɑr kraɪ / (AmE), / fɑː kraɪ / (BrE)
a very noticeable difference between two things; something that is very different from something else
a significant difference; very different thing

Examples

The new policy is a far cry from the old one.
Her cheerful attitude is a far cry from the sadness she once felt.
This quiet village is a far cry from the bustling city where I grew up.

Synset Examples

This new phone is a far cry from my old model.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: world apart - something that is completely different
  • Antonym: similar - having a resemblance or likeness
