to put one's feet up

1. VERB

/ pʊt wʌnz fit ʌp / (AmE), / pʊt wʌnz fiːt ʌp / (BrE)
to relax or rest, especially by sitting or lying down with your feet raised
relax; rest with feet raised

Examples

After a long day at work, she loves to put her feet up and read a book.
You deserve to put your feet up and enjoy some peace and quiet this evening.
He finally got the chance to put his feet up after finishing the garden work.

Synset Examples

She likes to put her feet up and relax on weekends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to relax - to make or become less tense or anxious
  • Synonym: to take it easy - to relax and not do anything strenuous
