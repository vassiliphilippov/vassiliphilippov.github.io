to turn the other cheek

1. VERB

/ tɝn ði ˈʌðɚ tʃik / (AmE), / tɜːn ði ˈʌðə tʃiːk / (BrE)
to refuse to retaliate or seek revenge after being insulted or harmed; to respond with patience and forgiveness.
refuse to retaliate when insulted

Examples

Even after being unfairly criticized at work, she decided to turn the other cheek.
He taught his children to turn the other cheek when someone tries to provoke them.
Instead of reacting angrily online, I chose to turn the other cheek and ignore the comment.

Synset Examples

She decided to turn the other cheek after the insult.
He chose to turn the other cheek instead of retaliating.
In tough times, it's best to turn the other cheek.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to retaliate - to make an attack in return for a similar attack
  • Synonym: to forgive - to stop feeling angry or resentful towards someone

Etymology

From the Bible (Matthew 5:39), where Jesus advises to 'turn the other cheek' as a response to being struck.
