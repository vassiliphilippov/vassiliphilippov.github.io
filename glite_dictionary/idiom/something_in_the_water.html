something in the water

1. X

/ ˈsʌmθɪŋ ɪn ðə ˈwɔːtɚ / (AmE), / ˈsʌmθɪŋ ɪn ðə ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
used to suggest that a mysterious or unknown factor is causing a group of people to behave unusually or experience the same condition
mysterious influence affecting a group

Examples

Everyone in the office has been unusually energetic; there must be something in the water.
So many students aced the test this week—maybe there's something in the water.
All our plants are thriving lately; there has to be something in the water!

Synset Examples

Everyone seems happy lately; there must be something in the water.
The kids are all behaving well; there must be something in the water.
The neighborhood has become friendlier; there must be something in the water.
