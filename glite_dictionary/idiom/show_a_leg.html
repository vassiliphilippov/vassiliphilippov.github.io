to show a leg

1. VERB

/ ʃoʊ ə leɡ / (AmE), / ʃəʊ ə leɡ / (BrE)
to make an appearance or reveal oneself, often in a playful or inviting manner; to participate or become visible
make an appearance; reveal oneself playfully

Examples

After much hesitation, he finally decided to show a leg and join the festivities.
If you want breakfast before ten, you'd better show a leg early.
The team captain told everyone to show a leg and get on the field.

Synset Examples

He thought it was time to show a leg at the party.
The waiter asked her to show a leg for the special.
She playfully decided to show a leg at the beach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to show up - to appear or arrive, especially for an event or appointment

Etymology

Originally a nautical phrase, traditionally used as a call for sailors to get out of bed and reveal themselves by showing a leg.
