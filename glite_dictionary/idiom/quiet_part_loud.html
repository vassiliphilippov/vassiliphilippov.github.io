quiet part loud

1. ADV

/ ˈkwaɪət pɑrt laʊd / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪət pɑːt laʊd / (BrE)
To state openly what is usually left unspoken or implied, especially something normally considered inappropriate or sensitive to say aloud.
to say something that's usually left unsaid
informal

Examples

When the politician admitted to favoring only wealthy donors, he really said the quiet part loud.
She accidentally said the quiet part loud by admitting the policy was meant to deter voters.
It was awkward when he said the quiet part loud during the meeting, revealing what everyone was thinking.

Synset Examples

His comment was brave, as it said the quiet part loud.
