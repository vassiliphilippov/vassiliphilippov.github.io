to enter the lists

1. VERB

/ ˈɛn.tɚ ðə lɪsts / (AmE), / ˈɛn.tə ðə lɪsts / (BrE)
to decide to take part in a contest or competition, especially one with rivals already involved
decide to compete or take part
archaic

Examples

When the mayor announced the new open position, several candidates chose to enter the lists.
Determined to win the scholarship, Sophie decided to enter the lists despite tough competition.
He long hesitated, but finally resolved to enter the lists for the national poetry prize.

Synset Examples

He decided to enter the lists for the competition.
She was excited to enter the lists for the tournament.
Only the bravest knights would enter the lists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to compete - to take part in a contest or competition

Etymology

From medieval jousting arenas known as 'lists', where knights competed in tournaments.
