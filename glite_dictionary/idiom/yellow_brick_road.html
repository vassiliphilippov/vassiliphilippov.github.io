yellow brick road

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛl.oʊ ˈbrɪk roʊd / (AmE), / ˈjɛl.əʊ ˈbrɪk rəʊd / (BrE)
a course of action or path toward one's goal or dreams, often implying challenges and unexpected adventures along the way
path toward a dream or goal, with challenges

Examples

She hopes her new business venture is the start of her own yellow brick road.
After graduation, he set off down the yellow brick road toward a fulfilling career.
They warned her that the yellow brick road to success was not without its obstacles.

Synset Examples

He followed his dreams as if on a yellow brick road.
Life's challenges are often like a yellow brick road.

Etymology

From the yellow brick road featured in L. Frank Baum's 'The Wizard of Oz' (1900), symbolizing a journey toward a desired destination.
