to spark a firestorm

1. VERB

/ spɑrk ə ˈfaɪərˌstɔrm / (AmE), / spɑːk ə ˈfaɪəˌstɔːm / (BrE)
to cause a sudden and intense outburst of public emotion, controversy, or criticism
cause intense controversy or reaction

Examples

The senator's comments sparked a firestorm of criticism on social media.
The company's decision to lay off workers sparked a firestorm among its employees.
Publishing the report sparked a firestorm in the international community.

Synset Examples

The proposal sparked a firestorm among environmental activists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provoke an outcry - cause widespread public protest or criticism
  • Synonym: to spark outrage - cause intense anger or shock in public
