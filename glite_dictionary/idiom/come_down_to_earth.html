to come down to earth

1. VERB

/ kʌm daʊn tu ɝθ / (AmE), / kʌm daʊn tʊ ɜːθ / (BrE)
to start behaving in a realistic or practical way after being overly idealistic or excited
behave in a realistic or practical way

Examples

After the excitement of winning the lottery, it took her weeks to come down to earth.
Once the project started, everyone had to come down to earth and focus on practical details.
He realized he needed to come down to earth and set achievable goals.

Synset Examples

She had to come down to earth after dreaming big.
After the promotion, he needed to come down to earth.
His sudden fame made him forget to come down to earth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get real - to be realistic or practical
  • Antonym: dream - to imagine or fantasize, often unrealistically
