to run a tight ship

1. VERB

/ ɹʌn ə taɪt ʃɪp / (AmE), / ɹʌn ə taɪt ʃɪp / (BrE)
to manage an organization or group in a very efficient and disciplined way
manage efficiently and strictly

Examples

Our department head really runs a tight ship, making sure every detail is handled.
The new principal runs a tight ship and the school has never been more organized.
If you want success here, you need to run a tight ship from day one.

Synset Examples

She runs a tight ship at work.
The manager ensures everything runs a tight ship.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: run a loose ship - to manage with little discipline or order

Etymology

Originally a nautical phrase referring to the strict management required on ships.
