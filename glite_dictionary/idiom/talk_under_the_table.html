to talk under the table

1. VERB

/ tɑk ˌʌndɚ ðə ˈteɪbəl / (AmE), / tɔːk ˌʌndə ðə ˈteɪbəl / (BrE)
to outtalk someone or dominate a conversation, especially by being more eloquent or persistent
dominate a conversation

Examples

She's so charismatic that she can talk under the table even the most experienced debaters.
Don't try to argue with Mark—he'll just talk under the table you every time.
During the meeting, Linda managed to talk under the table all her colleagues.

Synset Examples

At dinner, she talked him under the table effortlessly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outtalk - to talk more and dominate a conversation
