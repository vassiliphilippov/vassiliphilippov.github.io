to get off to a flying start

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɔf tʊ ə ˈflaɪɪŋ stɑrt / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒf tʊ ə ˈflaɪɪŋ stɑːt / (BrE)
to begin an activity or endeavor very successfully, achieving early progress or success
begin very successfully

Examples

The marketing campaign got off to a flying start with record sales in the first week.
Our team got off to a flying start in the tournament, winning all of its opening matches.
If you get off to a flying start in a new job, it can boost your confidence for the rest of your career.

Synset Examples

She got off to a flying start with her new job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hit the ground running - to begin something energetically and successfully
