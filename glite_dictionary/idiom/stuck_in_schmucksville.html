stuck in Schmucksville

1. ADJ

/ stʌk ɪn ˈʃmʌksˌvɪl / (AmE), / stʌk ɪn ˈʃmʌksvɪl / (BrE)
feeling trapped in a foolish, embarrassing, or awkward situation, often as a result of one's own actions
trapped in an embarrassing situation
informal slang

Examples

After his mistake at work, he felt completely stuck in Schmucksville during the meeting.
She realized she was stuck in Schmucksville when everyone started laughing at her joke gone wrong.
Every time he embarrasses himself in public, he says he's stuck in Schmucksville for the week.

Synset Examples

After his latest blunder, he felt stuck in Schmucksville.

Etymology

From 'schmuck' (Yiddish origin, meaning a foolish person) and '-sville' (slang for a metaphorical place or state).
