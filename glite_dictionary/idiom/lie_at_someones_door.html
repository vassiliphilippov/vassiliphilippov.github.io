to lie at someone's door

1. VERB

/ laɪ æt ˈsʌmwʌnz dɔr / (AmE), / laɪ æt ˈsʌmwənz dɔː / (BrE)
(of responsibility, blame, or fault) to be ascribed to someone; to be their responsibility or fault.
to be someone's responsibility or fault

Examples

The committee decided the error should lie at John's door rather than the team's.
We can't let all the blame lie at her door when others were involved.
Responsibility for the failure must lie at their door as they made the key decisions.

Synset Examples

The failure lies at her door, unfortunately.
All the consequences lie at his door now.
The blame lies at your door for this mess.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be someone's fault - to be the responsibility or error of someone
  • Synonym: be to blame - to be responsible for a fault or wrong
