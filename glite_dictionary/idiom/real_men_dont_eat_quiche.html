real men don't eat quiche

1. X

/ ˌɹiːəl mɛn doʊnt iːt kiːʃ / (AmE), / ˌɹiːəl mɛn dəʊnt iːt kiːʃ / (BrE)
used humorously or ironically to refer to traditional or stereotypical ideas of masculinity, especially those that reject things considered unmanly or effeminate
stereotypical saying about masculinity

Examples

At the party, someone laughed and quoted 'real men don't eat quiche' when he chose the vegetarian option.
The book humorously explores the notion that 'real men don't eat quiche'.
She rolled her eyes after hearing her uncle repeat 'real men don't eat quiche' during the family brunch.

Synset Examples

His father believed that real men don't eat quiche.
That saying about real men don't eat quiche is outdated.
He joked that real men don't eat quiche at brunch.

Etymology

Popularized by the title of the 1982 satirical book 'Real Men Don’t Eat Quiche' by Bruce Feirstein.
