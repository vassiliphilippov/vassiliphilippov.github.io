to jump off a bridge

1. VERB

/ dʒʌmp ɑf ə brɪdʒ / (AmE), / dʒʌmp ɒf ə brɪdʒ / (BrE)
(usually in the phrase 'If someone told you to jump off a bridge, would you do it?') Used to question whether a person would do something obviously foolish or dangerous just because others do it.
(idiom) do something foolish or dangerous due to peer pressure

Examples

If your friends told you to jump off a bridge, would you do it?
Asking me to jump off a bridge makes as much sense as following every silly trend.
My parents always said, 'Just because others do it, don't jump off a bridge yourself.'

Synset Examples

Her friends warned her, 'If someone told you to jump off a bridge, would you do it?'
He questioned her decisions, asking, 'If someone told you to jump off a bridge, would you do it?'
You can't just follow trends; if someone told you to jump off a bridge, would you do it?
