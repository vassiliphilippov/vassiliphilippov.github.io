fever pitch

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːvɚ pɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈfiːvə pɪtʃ / (BrE)
a state of extreme excitement or intensity
a state of intense excitement

Examples

The excitement in the arena had reached a fever pitch by the start of the concert.
Rumors about the new product launch sent speculation to a fever pitch.
As the final votes were counted, tension hit fever pitch among the crowd.

Synset Examples

The excitement in the room reached a fever pitch.
The crowd was at a fever pitch by the end of the game.
The negotiations reached a fever pitch last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crescendo - the highest point of something, especially in music or excitement

Etymology

Originally a musical term; 'fever' (illness) and 'pitch' (high point or degree).
