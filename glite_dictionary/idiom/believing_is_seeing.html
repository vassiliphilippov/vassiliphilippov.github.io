believing is seeing

1. X

/ bɪˈlivɪŋ ɪz ˈsiːɪŋ / (AmE), / bɪˈliːvɪŋ ɪz ˈsiːɪŋ / (BrE)
used to express the idea that one's beliefs or expectations can shape or determine what one perceives to be true or real.
belief shapes perception

Examples

For some people, believing is seeing, and their faith makes them notice signs everywhere.
He approached the project with an open mind, proving that believing is seeing.
In discussions about optimism, the phrase believing is seeing often comes up to highlight the power of positive thinking.

Synset Examples

Remember, believing is seeing when it comes to miracles.
They say believing is seeing; I’ll wait for proof.
For some, believing is seeing, especially in love.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seeing is believing - the idea that only physical or concrete evidence is convincing

Etymology

A reversal of the common phrase 'seeing is believing', popular in English sources from the 20th century onwards.
