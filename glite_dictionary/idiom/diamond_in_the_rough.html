diamond in the rough

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪəmənd ɪn ðə rʌf / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəmənd ɪn ðə rʌf / (BrE)
a person or thing with exceptional potential or talent but lacking polish or refinement
person or thing with undeveloped potential

Examples

The coach saw that the new player was a diamond in the rough and began to mentor him.
Many investors hope to find a diamond in the rough in rising start-up companies.
The novel was a diamond in the rough, needing some editing but full of brilliant ideas.

Synset Examples

She’s a diamond in the rough, full of potential.
He discovered a diamond in the rough at the auction.
That old house is a diamond in the rough.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rough diamond - someone who is good-natured but lacks polish or manners

Etymology

Alludes to an uncut diamond, which looks rough but is valuable when polished.
