flash in the pan

1. NOUN

/ flæʃ ɪn ðə pæn / (AmE), / flæʃ ɪn ðə pæn / (BrE)
a person or thing that briefly attracts attention or enjoys sudden success but fails to achieve lasting results
someone with brief, fleeting success

Examples

His first novel was a bestseller, but it turned out to be just a flash in the pan.
Many thought her sudden fame would make her a star, but she became a flash in the pan.
The band's only hit single marked them as a flash in the pan in music history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: one-hit wonder - person or thing successful only once

Etymology

Originates from the flintlock muskets of the 17th century, where gunpowder flaring in the pan sometimes failed to fire the main charge.

2. NOUN

/ flæʃ ɪn ðə pæn / (AmE), / flæʃ ɪn ðə pæn / (BrE)
a sudden, short-lived success that is not repeated or sustained
short-lived or fleeting success

Examples

His first novel got a lot of attention, but it turned out to be just a flash in the pan.
Everyone thought their technology would change the world, but it ended up being a flash in the pan.
That viral dance trend was a flash in the pan, quickly fading from memory.

Synset Examples

People still reference Scaramucci as a flash in the pan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: one-hit wonder - a performer or act that achieves brief popular success and then fades away

Etymology

Originally from the misfire of an old flintlock firearm, where the 'flash' would occur in the gun's pan without firing a shot.
