to zig when someone should zag

1. VERB

/ zɪɡ wɛn ˈsʌmwʌn ʃəd zæɡ / (AmE), / zɪɡ wɛn ˈsʌmwən ʃəd zæɡ / (BrE)
to make a mistake by taking the wrong action or making a poor choice, especially by acting contrary to what the situation requires
make a wrong move or choice

Examples

During the interview, he zigged when he should have zagged and gave the wrong answer.
You can't afford to zig when you should zag in a high-pressure situation like this.
She always seems to zig when she should zag whenever there's a big decision to make.

Synset Examples

She zigged when she should have zagged, causing confusion.
He always zigs when one should zag in negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make a blunder - to make a serious mistake
  • Degree Lower: zigzag - to move in sharp turns or changes in direction, which the idiom draws from
