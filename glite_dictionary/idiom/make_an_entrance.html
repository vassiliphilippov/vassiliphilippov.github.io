to make an entrance

1. VERB

/ meɪk ən ˈɛntrəns / (AmE), / meɪk ən ˈɛntrəns / (BrE)
to arrive at a place in a way that is noticeable or attracts a lot of attention, often in a dramatic or stylish manner
arrive in a noticeable or dramatic way

Examples

She rehearsed for hours, determined to make an entrance at her debut party.
The actor managed to make an entrance even more dramatic than his last performance.
With all eyes on him, he certainly knew how to make an entrance.

Synset Examples

He always knows how to make an entrance at events.
The celebrity made an entrance just as the show started.
She made an entrance that turned every head in the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make a splash - to do something in a way that attracts a lot of attention
