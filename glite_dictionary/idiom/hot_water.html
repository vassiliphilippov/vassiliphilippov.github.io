hot water

1. NOUN

/ hɑt ˈwɔːtɚ / (AmE), / hɒt ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
a situation in which someone is in trouble or facing difficulties, especially as a result of their own actions
trouble; difficult situation

Examples

If you keep lying to your boss, you'll end up in hot water.
The company found itself in hot water after the scandal was exposed.
She got into hot water at school for skipping classes.

Synset Examples

his views on race got him into political hot water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trouble - a situation in which you are likely to be punished or face difficulties
  • Synonym: jeopardy - danger of harm or loss
  • Antonym: out of hot water - no longer in trouble or difficulty
