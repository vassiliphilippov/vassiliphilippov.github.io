to throw a wobbly

1. VERB

/ θroʊ ə ˈwɑbli / (AmE), / θrəʊ ə ˈwɒbli / (BrE)
to suddenly become very angry or upset in a dramatic or uncontrolled way
suddenly lose one's temper
informal british

Examples

If you tell him the meeting is cancelled, he might throw a wobbly.
Julia threw a wobbly when she saw her birthday cake was ruined.
Teenagers sometimes throw a wobbly over the smallest things.

Synset Examples

Whenever he doesn't get his way, he throws wobblies.
She tends to throw a wobbly over minor mistakes.
The toddler will throw a wobbly if bedtime is delayed.
He threw a wobbly when he lost his phone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: have a tantrum - to behave in a very angry or unreasonable way
  • Synonym: to lose one's temper - to suddenly become very angry
  • Antonym: to keep one's cool - to remain calm instead of getting angry
