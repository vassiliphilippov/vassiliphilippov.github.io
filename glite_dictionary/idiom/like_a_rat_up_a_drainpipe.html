like a rat up a drainpipe

1. ADV

/ laɪk ə ræt ʌp ə ˈdreɪnˌpaɪp / (AmE), / laɪk ə ræt ʌp ə ˈdreɪn.pʌɪp / (BrE)
very quickly or in a great hurry, especially when leaving or escaping
very quickly; in a hurry
british

Examples

When the bell rang, the students left the classroom like a rat up a drainpipe.
As soon as the police arrived, the thief disappeared like a rat up a drainpipe.
He grabbed his coat and was out of the house like a rat up a drainpipe.

Synset Examples

He ran out of the meeting like a rat up a drainpipe.
She left the party like a rat up a drainpipe when she saw her ex.
