behind the bit

1. ADJ

/ bɪˈhaɪnd ðə bɪt / (AmE), / bɪˈhaɪnd ðə bɪt / (BrE)
(of a horse) not responsive to the rider's commands or restricted in performance, especially by evading contact with the bit.
unresponsive or restricted horse (equestrian idiom)

Examples

The trainer explained that the horse was behind the bit during yesterday's lesson.
A horse can become behind the bit if the rider's hands are too restrictive.
She recognized immediately that the gelding was behind the bit and needed encouragement to move forward.

Synset Examples

He struggled to control the horse that was behind the bit.
The horse was slow since it was behind the bit.
The jockey worked hard to get the horse out from behind the bit.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: on the bit - (of a horse) alert, responsive, and accepting contact with the bit
