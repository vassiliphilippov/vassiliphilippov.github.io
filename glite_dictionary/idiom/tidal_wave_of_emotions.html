tidal wave of emotions

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪdəl weɪv əv ɪˈmoʊʃənz / (AmE), / ˈtaɪdəl weɪv əv ɪˈməʊʃənz / (BrE)
a sudden and overwhelming surge of strong feelings
sudden surge of emotions

Examples

When she heard the news, a tidal wave of emotions crashed over her, leaving her speechless.
Standing on stage, he was hit by a tidal wave of emotions that nearly brought him to tears.
The old photographs triggered a tidal wave of emotions that she found hard to control.

Synset Examples

A tidal wave of emotions washed over her.
A tsunami of emotions hit her during the speech.
She felt a crimson wave of emotion as she watched the film.
She felt a wave of emotions as she saw the gravid cat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wave of emotion - a sudden, strong feeling or rush of emotion

Etymology

Derived from the metaphor of a tidal wave, a large and powerful ocean wave, to describe the overpowering nature of strong emotions.
