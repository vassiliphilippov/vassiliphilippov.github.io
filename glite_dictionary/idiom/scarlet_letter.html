scarlet letter

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɑːr.lət ˈlɛt.ər / (AmE), / ˈskɑː.lɪt ˈlɛt.ə / (BrE)
a mark or symbol that publicly singles out someone as being guilty of wrongdoing, especially in a way that brings shame or social disapproval
symbol of public shame or guilt

Examples

After the scandal, she felt like she was wearing a scarlet letter everywhere she went.
His bankruptcy became a scarlet letter that followed him for years.
In some communities, divorce can still be seen as a scarlet letter.

Synset Examples

She felt like she wore a scarlet letter after that mistake.
The rumor left him with a scarlet letter in the office.
He carried the scarlet letter of failure for months.

Etymology

From Nathaniel Hawthorne's novel 'The Scarlet Letter,' in which an adulterous woman is forced to wear a red letter 'A.'
