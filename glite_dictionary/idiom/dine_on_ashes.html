to dine on ashes

1. VERB

/ daɪn ɑn ˈæʃɪz / (AmE), / daɪn ɒn ˈæʃɪz / (BrE)
to experience deep disappointment or sorrow, often following a loss or hardship
feel deep disappointment or sorrow

Examples

After her dreams were shattered, she felt she could only dine on ashes for years.
He tried to celebrate, but the memory of his failure made him dine on ashes.
Following the tragic news, the entire family seemed to dine on ashes for months.

Synset Examples

Feeling lost, she felt she could only dine on ashes.
After the fire, they had to dine on ashes.
They had to dine on ashes after losing everything.
