Roma locuta est, causa finita est

1. X

/ ˈroʊmə loʊˈkuːtə ɛst ˈkaʊsə fɪˈniːtə ɛst / (AmE), / ˈrəʊmə ləʊˈkuːtə est ˈkaʊzə fɪˈniːtə est / (BrE)
(Latin) Used to indicate that once an authoritative decision has been made, the matter is considered closed or settled.
decision is final (Latin phrase)

When the judge pronounced her verdict, it was a clear case of Roma locuta est, causa finita est.
After the CEO made his decision, the team accepted it as Roma locuta est, causa finita est.
In theological debates, he often invokes Roma locuta est, causa finita est to end discussion.

Etymology

Latin for 'Rome has spoken, the case is closed.' Originally stated by St. Augustine, referring to the finality of a decision by church authority in Rome.
