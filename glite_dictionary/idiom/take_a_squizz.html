to take a squizz

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə skwɪz / (AmE), / teɪk ə skwɪz / (BrE)
to have a quick look at something, often out of curiosity or briefly
have a quick look
informal british

Examples

She stopped to take a squizz at the headlines before leaving the shop.
He leaned over to take a squizz at her notes during the lecture.
I always take a squizz at the dessert menu, even if I’m not hungry.

Synset Examples

I took a squizz at the menu before ordering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glance - to take a brief or hurried look
  • Synonym: peek - to look quickly or furtively

Etymology

Probably from British and Australian slang 'squizz,' meaning 'look,' of uncertain origin, early 20th century.
