to sell one's soul

1. VERB

/ sɛl wʌnz soʊl / (AmE), / sɛl wʌnz səʊl / (BrE)
to betray one's principles or integrity for personal advantage, especially for money, fame, or success
compromise integrity for gain

Examples

Many believe that he had to sell his soul to reach that level of success.
She would never sell her soul just to become famous.
Some politicians are accused of selling their soul for power.

Synset Examples

He felt he had to sell his soul for success.
She won't sell her soul for fame.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compromise - to accept standards that are lower than desirable
  • Antonym: keep one's integrity - to maintain one's moral principles

2. VERB

/ sɛl wʌnz soʊl / (AmE), / sɛl wʌnz səʊl / (BrE)
to betray one's principles or morals in exchange for personal gain, often at a significant cost to one's integrity
betray morals for personal gain

Examples

She felt like she was selling her soul by accepting the bribe.
Many artists worry about selling their soul to achieve commercial success.
He accused his rival of selling his soul for power and influence.

Synset Examples

He believed that selling his soul was worth it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compromise - to make concessions that undermine one's principles
  • Antonym: keep one's integrity - to remain true to one's principles or morals despite temptation

Etymology

From the traditional idea of making a pact with the devil in exchange for something desirable.
