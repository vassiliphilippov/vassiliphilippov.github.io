to change the playing field

1. VERB

/ tʃeɪndʒ ðə ˈpleɪɪŋ fiːld / (AmE), / tʃeɪndʒ ðə ˈpleɪɪŋ fiːld / (BrE)
to alter the conditions or circumstances, especially in a way that affects the level of competition between people or organizations
alter competitive circumstances

Examples

New technology could change the playing field for small businesses.
Government subsidies are expected to change the playing field in the energy sector.
A major new competitor would dramatically change the playing field in the market.

Synset Examples

The regulations will change the playing field in the industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: level the playing field - make conditions fair so everyone has an equal chance
  • Antonym: tilt the playing field - make conditions unfairly advantageous for some
