new lease of life

1. NOUN

/ nuː lis əv laɪf / (AmE), / njuː liːs əv laɪf / (BrE)
a renewed period of vitality, success, or usefulness; a fresh start or improvement in condition
renewed vitality or usefulness

Examples

After the major repairs, the old steam train was given a new lease of life.
Winning the award brought a new lease of life to his acting career.
The struggling business found a new lease of life after its rebranding.

Synset Examples

The renovation gave the old building a new lease of life.
The community project provided a new lease of life for the park.
After the surgery, she felt like she had a new lease of life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revival - an improvement in the condition or strength of something
  • Synonym: resurgence - an increase or revival after a period of little activity

Etymology

From the legal term ‘lease of life’, referring to a renewed lease on property, figuratively extended to vitality in the 19th century.
