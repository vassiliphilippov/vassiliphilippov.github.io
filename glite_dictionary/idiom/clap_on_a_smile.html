to clap on a smile

1. VERB

/ klæp ɑn ə smaɪl / (AmE), / klap ɒn ə smaɪl / (BrE)
to put on a smile quickly, often in a way that appears artificial or forced
put on a smile quickly, often artificially

Examples

Despite her nervousness, she clapped on a smile and greeted the audience.
He clapped on a smile before joining the festive crowd, trying to hide his exhaustion.
As soon as the cameras started rolling, she clapped on a smile for the viewers.

Synset Examples

She clapped on a smile before entering the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: force a smile - to make oneself smile, often despite not feeling like it
  • Antonym: beam - to smile broadly and genuinely
