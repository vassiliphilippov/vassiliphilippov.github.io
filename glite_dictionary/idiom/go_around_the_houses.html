to go around the houses

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ əˈraʊnd ðə ˈhaʊzɪz / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈraʊnd ðə ˈhaʊzɪz / (BrE)
to avoid getting to the point or giving a straight answer; to be unnecessarily indirect or circuitous
be indirect or avoid the point
british

Examples

Every time I ask her a simple question, she goes around the houses before answering.
Stop going around the houses and tell us what you really think.
He tends to go around the houses rather than address the issue directly.

Synset Examples

She tends to go around the houses instead of getting straight to the facts.
It's frustrating when he goes around the houses instead of answering directly.
Stop going around the houses and tell me your opinion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to beat around the bush - avoid talking about what is important or directly addressing a topic
  • Antonym: to get to the point - to say something directly and without unnecessary details
