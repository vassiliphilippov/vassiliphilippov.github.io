to make a scene

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə siːn / (AmE), / meɪk ə siːn / (BrE)
to behave in a loud or noticeable way in public, often to attract attention or show strong emotion, especially anger or distress
create a public commotion

Examples

Please don't make a scene at the dinner party; let's discuss it later at home.
She accidentally dropped the cake but managed not to make a scene about it.
He always threatens to make a scene if things don't go his way.

Synset Examples

The snipper-snapper made a scene over trivial matters.
He made a scene because he was stupid drunk.
That cooze at the party was really making a scene.
Please don't make a scene in the restaurant.
She spilled the drink on purpose to make a scene.
Let's not make a scene over a small misunderstanding.
She tends to make a scene whenever she's upset.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cause a stir - to provoke public excitement or argument
  • Antonym: to keep a low profile - to avoid attracting attention to oneself
