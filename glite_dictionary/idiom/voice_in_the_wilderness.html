voice in the wilderness

1. NOUN

/ vɔɪs ɪn ðə ˈwɪldərnəs / (AmE), / vɔɪs ɪn ðə ˈwɪldənəs / (BrE)
a person, group, or message whose suggestion or warning is ignored or unheeded by others.
an unheeded suggestion or warning

Examples

In the early stages of the debate, his proposal was dismissed as a voice in the wilderness.
She felt like a voice in the wilderness when no one took her environmental concerns seriously.
The scientist became a voice in the wilderness warning of the potential dangers of the new technology.

Synset Examples

Churchill's early warning of the danger of Nazism was a voice in the wilderness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cry in the wilderness - a warning or suggestion that is ignored

Etymology

Biblical allusion to John the Baptist, who preached in the wilderness (Matthew 3:3).

2. NOUN

/ vɔɪs ɪn ðə ˈwɪldərnəs / (AmE), / vɔɪs ɪn ðə ˈwɪldəˌnɪs / (BrE)
a person who expresses an unpopular opinion or warning that is ignored or unheeded by others
person whose warning/idea is ignored

Examples

She was a voice in the wilderness when she spoke out against pollution at the time.
For years, his warnings about financial risk made him a voice in the wilderness.
Many innovators have been a voice in the wilderness before their ideas gained acceptance.

Synset Examples

As a voice in the wilderness, she fought for environmental issues.
His warnings were often dismissed, making him a voice in the wilderness.
He felt like a voice in the wilderness with his unpopular opinions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lone voice - person expressing an unpopular or solitary opinion

Etymology

From the biblical phrase in Isaiah, referring to 'a voice crying in the wilderness.'
