hard put

1. ADJ

/ hɑrd pʊt / (AmE), / hɑːd pʊt / (BrE)
having difficulty or being in a challenging situation, especially with limited resources or ability to cope
having difficulty doing something

Examples

Many families were hard put to pay their bills during the recession.
She was hard put to finish the project on time with so few resources.
The small business was hard put to survive after losing its main client.

Synset Examples

distressed companies need loans and technical advice
financially hard-pressed Mexican hotels are lowering their prices
we were hard put to meet the mortgage payment
found themselves in a bad way financially

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard-pressed - facing difficulties, especially financial ones
  • Synonym: at a loss - uncertain or unsure what to do
