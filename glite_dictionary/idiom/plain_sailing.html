plain sailing

1. NOUN

/ pleɪn ˈseɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / pleɪn ˈseɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
used to say that something is easy to do and free from difficulties once started
easy progress; no difficulties

Examples

Once the new software was installed, setting up the system was plain sailing.
After months of preparation, the actual event turned out to be plain sailing.
The exam was difficult, but once I finished the first section, the rest was plain sailing.

Synset Examples

after we solved that problem the rest was plain sailing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smooth sailing - easy progress without obstacles; synonym for 'plain sailing'

Etymology

Originally nautical, referring to straightforward navigation using plain (basic) sailing methods compared to more complex methods.
