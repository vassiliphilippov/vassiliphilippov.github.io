to rub salt in the wound

1. VERB

/ rʌb sɔlt ɪn ðə ˈwund / (AmE), / rʌb sɒlt ɪn ðə ˈwuːnd / (BrE)
to make a bad situation or someone's feelings even worse, especially by reminding them of their disappointment or failure
make a painful situation worse

Examples

It really rubbed salt in the wound when he laughed at my mistake.
Her criticism rubbed salt in the wound on a day that was already going badly.
Bringing up my failed project only rubbed salt in the wound for me.

Synset Examples

The criticism just rubbed salt in the wound after my failure.
Winning the award rubbed salt in the wound of those who lost.
Hearing his ex's new song really rubbed salt in the wound.
Don't rub salt in her wounds by mentioning the breakup.
He always finds a way to rub salt in my wounds.
She didn't mean to rub salt in his wounds during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to add insult to injury - to make a bad situation even worse; a similar idiom

Etymology

From the old practice of applying salt to wounds to cause additional pain, used figuratively since the 19th century.
