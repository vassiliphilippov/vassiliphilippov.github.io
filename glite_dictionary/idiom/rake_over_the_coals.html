to rake over the coals

1. VERB

/ reɪk oʊvɚ ðə koʊlz / (AmE), / reɪk əʊvə ðə kəʊlz / (BrE)
to criticize or reprimand someone very severely
reprimand or criticize severely

Examples

The employee was raked over the coals for mishandling the client's order.
After the meeting, she felt like she'd been raked over the coals by her supervisors.
The journalist was raked over the coals in the press for the inaccurate report.

Synset Examples

She felt raked over the coals after the harsh criticism.
The manager raked him over the coals for missing the deadline.
He was raked over the coals for his poor performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scold - to reprimand angrily or harshly
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

Refers to an old practice of punishment, figuratively using burning coals as an emblem of harsh treatment.
