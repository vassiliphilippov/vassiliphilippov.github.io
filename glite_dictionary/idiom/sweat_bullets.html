to sweat bullets

1. VERB

/ swɛt ˈbʊlɪts / (AmE), / swɛt ˈbʊlɪts / (BrE)
to be extremely nervous or anxious, especially in anticipation of something difficult or frightening
be extremely nervous or anxious
informal

Examples

I always sweat bullets before a big presentation at work.
She was sweating bullets while waiting for her exam results.
He sweated bullets when the police officer waved him over.

Synset Examples

I always sweat bullets before a big presentation.
He sweated bullets when he couldn't find his wallet.
I was sweating bullets during the scary movie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panic - to feel sudden uncontrollable fear or anxiety
  • Antonym: to relax - to become calm and less worried
