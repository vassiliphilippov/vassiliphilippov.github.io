to shoot holes in

1. VERB

/ ʃuːt hoʊlz ɪn / (AmE), / ʃuːt həʊlz ɪn / (BrE)
to find and point out serious faults or flaws in; to criticize effectively by revealing weaknesses, especially in an argument or proposal
to expose flaws or criticize

Examples

The lawyer tried to shoot holes in the witness's testimony during cross-examination.
Analysts quickly shot holes in the proposal, highlighting its many weaknesses.
Whenever I suggest a new idea, someone is ready to shoot holes in it.

Synset Examples

Let’s not shoot holes in each other's ideas too quickly.
Critics are quick to shoot holes in his argument.
He managed to shoot holes in the theory effortlessly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to criticize - to express disapproval of or point out the faults of someone or something
  • Synonym: to refute - to prove (a statement or theory) to be wrong or false
