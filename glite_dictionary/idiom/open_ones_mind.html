to open one's mind

1. VERB

/ ˈoʊpən wʌnz maɪnd / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən wʌnz maɪnd / (BrE)
to become receptive to new ideas or viewpoints; to be willing to consider different perspectives
be receptive to new ideas

Examples

Traveling to different countries can really help you open your mind to new experiences.
It's important to open your mind before making judgments about others.
She tried to open her mind during the discussion and listened to all viewpoints.

Synset Examples

He urged her to open her mind to different perspectives.
Traveling can help you open your mind to new cultures.
Reading diverse authors can really help you open your mind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas; unprejudiced
