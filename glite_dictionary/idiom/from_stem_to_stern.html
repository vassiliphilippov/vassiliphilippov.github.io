from stem to stern

1. ADV

/ frəm stɛm tə stɝn / (AmE), / frəm stɛm tə stɜːn / (BrE)
completely or thoroughly throughout the whole of something, originally referring to the entire length of a ship
completely throughout; from one end to the other

Examples

The entire mansion was redecorated from stem to stern in just three weeks.
She checked her outfit from stem to stern before leaving the house.
The team cleaned the office from stem to stern for the big inspection.

Synset Examples

The renovation was done from stem to stern.
The ship was inspected from stem to stern.
They cleaned the house from stem to stern for the guests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: from head to toe - from the top of the head to the feet; completely

Etymology

Refers to the length of a ship, from the stem (front) to the stern (back); now used figuratively.
