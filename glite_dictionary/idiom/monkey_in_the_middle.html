monkey in the middle

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌŋki ɪn ðə ˈmɪdəl / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋki ɪn ðə ˈmɪdəl / (BrE)
a person who is caught between two parties or opposing sides, often unable to influence the outcome
person caught between opposing sides

Examples

In family disputes, she often found herself as the monkey in the middle torn between her parents.
Negotiating between feuding coworkers made him feel like the monkey in the middle.
During the merger talks, the manager became the monkey in the middle between the two companies.

Synset Examples

Being the monkey in the middle is never fun.
She was the monkey in the middle of the debate.
He felt like the monkey in the middle during the argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: between a rock and a hard place - in a difficult situation with no easy solution
  • Synonym: caught in the crossfire - involved in a situation where two parties are in conflict and suffering as a result

Etymology

Originates from the name of a children's game in which one child tries to catch a ball being thrown between two others.
