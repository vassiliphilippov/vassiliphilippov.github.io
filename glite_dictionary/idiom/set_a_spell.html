to set a spell

1. VERB

/ sɛt ə spɛl / (AmE), / sɛt ə spɛl / (BrE)
to take a short break, usually by sitting down and relaxing, often to enjoy one's time or have a chat
take a short break and relax
informal

Examples

Why don't you set a spell and tell me about your day?
After walking for hours, they decided to set a spell on the porch.
We like to set a spell by the fire after dinner and talk.

Synset Examples

They decided to set a spell and relax.
Let’s set a spell and enjoy the evening.
It’s nice to set a spell and catch up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rest - to stop working or moving in order to relax or recover strength
  • Synonym: to take it easy - to relax and avoid stress or hard work
