to kiss the gunner's daughter

1. VERB

/ kɪs ðə ˈɡʌnɚz ˈdɔtər / (AmE), / kɪs ðə ˈɡʌnəz ˈdɔːtə / (BrE)
(historical) To undergo corporal punishment, especially in the navy, by being bent over a cannon and flogged.
naval euphemism for punishment by flogging
archaic

Examples

If a sailor broke the rules, he might be forced to kiss the gunner's daughter as punishment.
Many recruits dreaded the day they would have to kiss the gunner's daughter for the first time.
The captain ordered the misbehaving midshipman to kiss the gunner's daughter before the assembled crew.

Synset Examples

In the old sailor's tale, he had to kiss the gunner's daughter.
The phrase 'kiss the gunner's daughter' amused the crew.

Etymology

From British naval slang: 'gunner's daughter' refers to the cannon over which the punished sailor was bent for flogging.
