to can't stand the heat

1. VERB

/ kænt stænd ðə hit / (AmE), / kɑːnt stænd ðə hiːt / (BrE)
(informal) Unable to tolerate pressure, criticism, or stressful conditions.
unable to handle pressure or stress
informal

Examples

If you can't stand the heat, maybe this high-stress job isn't for you.
Some politicians resign because they can't stand the heat of public scrutiny.
He admitted he can't stand the heat when the competition gets tough.

Synset Examples

I can't stand the heat in this room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: can't take the pressure - unable to cope with stressful situations
  • Antonym: to keep a cool head - remain calm under pressure

Etymology

Popularized from the saying 'If you can't stand the heat, get out of the kitchen', attributed to U.S. President Harry S. Truman.
