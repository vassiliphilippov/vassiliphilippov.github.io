to give line

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv laɪn / (AmE), / ɡɪv laɪn / (BrE)
to provide false information or a distraction, often to mislead someone or divert their attention
mislead or distract someone

Examples

She kept giving line to the reporters, hoping to keep them away from the real issue.
He realized someone was giving line just to buy some time.
Whenever she's late, she starts giving line about traffic or broken elevators.

Synset Examples

Stop giving him line; he knows the truth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deceive - to mislead or trick someone
  • Synonym: to distract - to divert someone's attention
