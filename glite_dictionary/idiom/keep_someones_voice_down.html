to keep (someone's) voice down

1. VERB

/ kip ˈsʌmwʌnz vɔɪs daʊn / (AmE), / kiːp ˈsʌmwʌnz vɔɪs daʊn / (BrE)
to speak quietly or at a lower volume, especially so as not to disturb others
speak quietly; lower your voice

Examples

Please keep your voice down while others are working.
The librarian reminded us to keep our voices down in the reading room.
She whispered, asking him to keep his voice down so as not to wake the baby.

Synset Examples

She asked him to keep his voice down while on the phone.
I had to remind him to keep his voice down.
He prefers to keep his voice low during meetings.
Please keep your voice down in the library.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speak softly - to talk in a quiet voice
  • Antonym: shout - to speak very loudly
