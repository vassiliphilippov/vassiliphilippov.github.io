to get the greenlight

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ðə ˈɡriːnˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ɡɛt ðə ˈɡriːnˌlaɪt / (BrE)
to receive official approval to begin or continue a project or activity
receive approval to proceed

Examples

The team can't start construction until they get the greenlight from the city council.
After months of waiting, the filmmakers finally got the greenlight for the new movie.
Before we can launch the product, we need to get the greenlight from upper management.

Synset Examples

The project finally got the greenlight after months of planning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to approve - to officially agree to or accept as satisfactory
  • Antonym: veto - to refuse to approve; forbid

Etymology

Derived from the green light in traffic signals, which indicates permission to go.
