on the eve of

1. ADP

/ ɑn ði iv ʌv / (AmE), / ɒn ðiː iːv ɒv / (BrE)
immediately before a significant event or occasion, especially the day before it
immediately before an important event

Examples

The president gave a speech on the eve of the election.
We finished preparing the house on the eve of our guests' arrival.
Tensions rose on the eve of the important summit.

Synset Examples

On the eve of her birthday, she felt excited.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prior to - before a specific event or time

Etymology

From 'eve' meaning evening or day before, from Old English 'ǣfen' (evening).
