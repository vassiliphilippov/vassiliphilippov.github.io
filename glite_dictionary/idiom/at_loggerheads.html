at loggerheads

1. ADJ

/ ət ˈlɑɡərˌhɛdz / (AmE), / ət ˈlɒɡəhɛdz / (BrE)
in strong disagreement or dispute with someone, especially over a particular issue.
in strong disagreement or dispute

Examples

The two companies have been at loggerheads over the contract terms for months.
Ever since the policy change, the teachers and administration were at loggerheads.
They found themselves at loggerheads about how to spend the inheritance.

Synset Examples

Sam and his parents were at loggerheads over the question of car privileges

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in conflict - in opposition or quarrel with someone
  • Antonym: in agreement - not in dispute or opposition

Etymology

Possibly from the use of "loggerhead" (a type of iron tool) to mean a blockhead or stubborn person.

2. ADV

/ ət ˈlɑːɡərˌhɛdz / (AmE), / ət ˈlɒɡəhɛdz / (BrE)
(of people or groups) in strong disagreement or conflict with each other.
in conflict or strong disagreement

Examples

The committee and the board are at loggerheads over the budget proposal.
She found herself at loggerheads with her colleague on several key issues.
The two countries have long been at loggerheads about their border.

Synset Examples

The two teams are at loggerheads over the project direction.
They were at loggerheads during the negotiations.
Management is at loggerheads with the employees' demands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in disagreement - not agreeing; having opposing opinions
  • Antonym: in accord - in agreement or harmony

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from 'loggerhead', a blockhead or stubborn person in 17th-century English.
