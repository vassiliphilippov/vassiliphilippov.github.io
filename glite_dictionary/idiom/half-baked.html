half-baked

1. ADJ

/ ˌhæfˈbeɪkt / (AmE), / ˌhɑːfˈbeɪkt / (BrE)
(of ideas or plans) not fully planned, considered, or sensible; foolish or impractical.
not fully thought out; impractical

Examples

His half-baked plan to start a business with no experience quickly fell apart.
We shouldn’t implement any half-baked ideas without proper discussion and research.
The board rejected the half-baked proposal because it lacked important details.

Synset Examples

a crazy scheme
half-baked ideas
a screwball proposal without a prayer of working

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crazy - extremely foolish or impractical (of ideas/plans)
  • Antonym: well-thought-out - carefully considered and planned

Etymology

Originally referring to bread or food not baked thoroughly; figurative use from the 17th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˌhæfˈbeɪkt / (AmE), / ˌhɑːfˈbeɪkt / (BrE)
(of an idea, plan, or proposal) not fully thought through or lacking sufficient planning; impractical or incomplete.
not fully thought out; impractical

Examples

Her half-baked solution did not solve any of the underlying issues.
The committee rejected the half-baked proposal for lack of detail and research.
He regretted making a half-baked promise he couldn't keep.

Synset Examples

The proposal was criticized as being half-baked and impractical.
His half-baked idea lacked any real planning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ill-conceived - badly planned or thought out
  • Antonym: well-thought-out - carefully considered and planned

Etymology

From 'half' meaning 'partially' and 'baked', drawing analogy to something not properly cooked, hence not ready or finished.
