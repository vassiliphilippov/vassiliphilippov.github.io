pigs might fly

1. X

/ pɪɡz maɪt flaɪ / (AmE), / pɪɡz maɪt flaɪ / (BrE)
used to indicate that something is extremely unlikely or impossible to happen
expression of disbelief or impossibility

Examples

He says he'll clean his room without being told—pigs might fly!
They promise this will be finished tomorrow? Pigs might fly before that happens.
She’ll pay you back? Pigs might fly.

Synset Examples

If pigs had wings they would fly.
She'll apologize first? Pigs might fly!
He'll finish that project on time? Pigs might fly!
They’ll change their minds? Pigs might fly!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: when hell freezes over - an expression meaning something will never happen

Etymology

Based on the absurd idea of pigs being able to fly; recorded in English since the 17th century.
