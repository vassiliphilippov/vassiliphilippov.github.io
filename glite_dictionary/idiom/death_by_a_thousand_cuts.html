death by a thousand cuts

1. NOUN

/ dɛθ baɪ ə ˈθaʊzənd kʌts / (AmE), / dɛθ baɪ ə ˈθaʊzənd kʌts / (BrE)
a gradual and cumulative worsening or harm caused by many small, seemingly insignificant problems or actions
harm from many small problems

Examples

The constant changes to the project plan felt like death by a thousand cuts to the team.
Over years, rising fees and declining benefits caused employees to experience death by a thousand cuts.
For small businesses, new regulations often result in death by a thousand cuts rather than a single catastrophic failure.

Synset Examples

The constant criticism was like death by a thousand cuts.
She described her workload as death by a thousand cuts.
He felt the stress of his job was death by a thousand cuts.
The project’s endless revisions felt like death by a thousand paper cuts.
He likened the slow decline of the business to death by a thousand paper cuts.
Her anxiety felt like death by a thousand paper cuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slow death - gradual weakening or demise
  • Antonym: sudden collapse - a rapid or abrupt failure or end

Etymology

Metaphorical use based on the historical method of execution known as lingchi ('death by a thousand cuts') from Imperial China.
