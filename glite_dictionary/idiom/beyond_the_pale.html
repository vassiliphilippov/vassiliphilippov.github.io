beyond the pale

1. ADJ

/ biˈjɑnd ðə peɪl / (AmE), / bɪˈjɒnd ðə peɪl / (BrE)
considered morally or socially unacceptable; outside the bounds of proper behavior
unacceptable; improper behavior

Examples

Most people agreed that his remarks were beyond the pale and deeply offensive.
The company's decision to ignore safety regulations is simply beyond the pale.
Such actions are beyond the pale in any civilized society.

Synset Examples

The treatment of those prisoners was truly beyond the pale.
His behavior at the party was beyond the pale.
Cheating in the game was considered beyond the pale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: out of line - behaving in an unacceptable or improper way
  • Synonym: over the line - having crossed the threshold of acceptability

Etymology

From the historical use of 'pale' meaning a boundary or enclosed area, especially the English-controlled region around Dublin, Ireland; behavior 'beyond the pale' was outside the accepted limits.
