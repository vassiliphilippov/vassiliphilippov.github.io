look at the mantelpiece when poking the fire

1. X

/ lʊk ət ðə ˈmæntəlˌpis wɛn ˈpoʊkɪŋ ðə ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / lʊk ət ðə ˈmæntəlˌpiːs wɛn ˈpəʊ.kɪŋ ðə ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
to distract oneself by focusing on something irrelevant instead of the main task at hand
distract oneself from a task

Examples

If you want to finish this work quickly, don't look at the mantelpiece when poking the fire.
She tends to look at the mantelpiece when poking the fire whenever she tries to study.
Instead of solving the problem, he kept looking at the mantelpiece when poking the fire.

Synset Examples

Focus on your tasks; you don't look at the mantelpiece when you're poking the fire.
