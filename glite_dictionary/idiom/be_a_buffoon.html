to be a buffoon

1. VERB

/ bi ə bəˈfuːn / (AmE), / bi ə bəˈfuːn / (BrE)
to act in a foolish or ridiculous way
act foolishly or ridiculously

Examples

Please don't be a buffoon during the meeting; it's an important discussion.
Whenever he tries to impress his friends, he ends up being a buffoon.
She realized she was being a buffoon and quickly apologized for her behavior.

Synset Examples

Don't be a buffoon; take this seriously.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fool around - to behave in a silly or playful way
  • Antonym: act sensibly - to behave in a reasonable or serious manner

Etymology

From French *bouffon* (jester), from Italian *buffone*, from *buffare* (to puff, act with jest).
