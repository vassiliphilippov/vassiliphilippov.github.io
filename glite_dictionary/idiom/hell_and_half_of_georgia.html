hell and half of Georgia

1. X

/ hɛl ənd hæf ʌv ˈdʒɔrdʒə / (AmE), / hɛl ənd hɑːf ɒv ˈdʒɔːdʒə / (BrE)
for a very long time or with a great deal of effort, often more than expected or reasonable
with much effort or for a long time
american informal

Examples

I spent hell and half of Georgia finding the right parts for the old car.
It seemed to take hell and half of Georgia before we finally got the paperwork approved.
She went through hell and half of Georgia just to get here on time.

Synset Examples

He talked about the project for hell and half of Georgia.
She spent hell and half of Georgia explaining her ideas.
It took hell and half of Georgia to convince him.

Etymology

An American idiom, probably originating in the southern United States in the 20th century, combining strong imagery to emphasize duration or effort.
