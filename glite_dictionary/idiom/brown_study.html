brown study

1. NOUN

/ ˌbraʊn ˈstʌdi / (AmE), / ˌbraʊn ˈstʌdi / (BrE)
A state of deep or dreamy absorption in thought; a mood of abstraction or pensiveness.
state of deep thought or abstraction
archaic

Examples

He sat in a brown study, staring out of the window and ignoring everyone around him.
The teacher's question jolted Emily out of her brown study.
Lost in a brown study, he didn't notice the time passing by.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reverie - a state of being pleasantly lost in one's thoughts
  • Synonym: daydream - a series of pleasant thoughts that distract one from the present

Etymology

First recorded in the 16th century; origin of 'brown' in this context unclear, possibly meaning 'gloomy' or 'somber'.
