to lay down the marker

1. VERB

/ leɪ daʊn ðə ˈmɑrkər / (AmE), / leɪ daʊn ðə ˈmɑːkə / (BrE)
to set a clear standard, challenge, or expectation against which others are judged or measured
set a clear standard or challenge

Examples

By winning the first race, she laid down the marker for her rivals.
Their ambitious project lays down the marker for future innovation in the field.
He laid down the marker by demanding transparency in all negotiations.

Synset Examples

She laid down the marker for her competitors by winning the championship.
He laid down the marker by refusing to compromise on quality.
The new policy lays down the marker for company expectations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: set the standard - to establish a level of quality or achievement
  • Synonym: to raise the bar - to increase standards or expectations
