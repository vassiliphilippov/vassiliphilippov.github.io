to rub shoulders

1. VERB

/ rʌb ˈʃoʊldɚz / (AmE), / rʌb ˈʃəʊldəz / (BrE)
to associate or mix socially with other people, especially those who are important or famous
mingle socially, especially with important people

Examples

At the exclusive party, she hoped to rub shoulders with movie stars and musicians.
Young entrepreneurs often attend these conferences to rub shoulders with industry leaders.
During his internship, he had the chance to rub shoulders with top executives.

Synset Examples

She loves to rub shoulders with celebrities at charity events.
She rubbed shoulders with muckety mucks at the event.
You never know who you'll rub shoulders with at the gala.
At the conference, I got to rub shoulders with industry leaders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hobnob - to mix socially, especially with people of higher status
