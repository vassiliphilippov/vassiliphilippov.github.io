does Macy's tell Gimbel's?

1. X

/ ˈmeɪsiz tɛl ˈɡɪmbəlz / (AmE), / ˈmeɪsiz tɛl ˈɡɪmbəlz / (BrE)
(informal, rhetorical) Used to indicate that one party would not reveal information to a rival or that something will not happen as expected.
used to imply sharing secrets with a rival is unlikely
informal american

Examples

"Will she reveal her secret recipe? Does Macy's tell Gimbel's?"
Whenever someone asks if I know the gossip, I just reply, "Does Macy's tell Gimbel's?"
He wanted to know our company's plans, but come on—does Macy's tell Gimbel's?

Synset Examples

Will he apologize? Does Macy's tell Gimbel's?
If he says no, does Macy's tell Gimbel's?

Etymology

From the names of two famous competing American department stores, Macy's and Gimbel's, used in a rhetorical question since the mid-20th century.
