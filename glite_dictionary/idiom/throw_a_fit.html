to throw a fit

1. VERB

/ θroʊ ə fɪt / (AmE), / θrəʊ ə fɪt / (BrE)
to become very angry or lose one's temper suddenly and visibly
suddenly lose temper

Examples

If you criticize him in front of others, he might throw a fit.
The child started to throw a fit when he didn't get dessert.
She'll throw a fit if she finds out you broke her vase.

Synset Examples

The professor combusted when the student didn't know the answer to a very elementary question
Spam makes me go ballistic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: have a tantrum - become very upset or angry in an uncontrolled way
  • Antonym: to keep one's cool - remain calm; not get angry

2. VERB

/ θɹoʊ ə fɪt / (AmE), / θɹəʊ ə fɪt / (BrE)
to suddenly become very angry or upset, often in a dramatic way
suddenly become very angry

Examples

My son threw a fit when I told him he couldn't go to the party.
If you change her schedule, she often throws a fit for no reason at all.
He threw a fit after hearing the disappointing news.

Synset Examples

She threw a fit when she lost her keys.
The shitgoblin threw a fit over a minor issue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lose one's temper - to suddenly become angry
  • Antonym: to keep one's cool - to remain calm and not become angry
