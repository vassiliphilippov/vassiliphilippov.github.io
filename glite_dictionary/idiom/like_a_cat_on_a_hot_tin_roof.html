like a cat on a hot tin roof

1. X

/ laɪk ə kæt ɑn ə hɑt tɪn ruːf / (AmE), / laɪk ə kæt ɒn ə hɒt tɪn ruːf / (BrE)
extremely restless, agitated, or anxious; unable to stay still due to nervousness or excitement
restless or anxious; very agitated

Examples

Before the exam, she was like a cat on a hot tin roof, unable to concentrate on anything.
He’s been like a cat on a hot tin roof ever since he heard the news about the layoffs.
The children were like a cat on a hot tin roof waiting for their turn at the amusement park.

Synset Examples

She was pacing around the office like a cat on a hot tin roof.
The kids were jumping around like a cat on a hot tin roof.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on edge - nervous, tense, or anxious
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness or agitation

Etymology

Popularized by Tennessee Williams' 1955 play 'Cat on a Hot Tin Roof', though the phrase existed in the US South before then.
