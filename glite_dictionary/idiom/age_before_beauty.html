age before beauty

1. X

/ eɪdʒ bɪˈfɔr ˈbjuːti / (AmE), / eɪdʒ bɪˈfɔː ˈbjuːti / (BrE)
used humorously to suggest that older people should go before younger, more attractive people, usually as a playful gesture of courtesy
humorous phrase prioritizing age before attractiveness

Examples

As the door opened, she smiled and said, 'age before beauty,' letting her grandfather enter first.
He stepped aside with a grin, joking, 'age before beauty,' as he invited his colleague ahead.
Whenever we line up for cake, Dad insists, 'age before beauty,' to get the first slice.

Synset Examples

She joked, 'Age before beauty, so I’ll go first!'
The old man chuckled, 'Age before beauty!' as he opened the door.
He always says age before beauty when letting her in first.
