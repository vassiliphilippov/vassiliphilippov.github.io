to strike a chord

1. VERB

/ straɪk ə kɔrd / (AmE), / straɪk ə kɔːd / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel an emotion or relate to something, often because it is familiar or meaningful
evoke a strong emotional response

Examples

The speaker's powerful words struck a chord with the audience, leaving many moved to tears.
Her story of overcoming adversity really struck a chord with me.
The film strikes a chord with anyone who has ever faced a difficult decision.

Synset Examples

this writer strikes a chord with young women
The storyteller touched a chord

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to touch a chord - provoke an emotional response
  • Synonym: move - to cause strong feelings or emotions

Etymology

Metaphor from music, referring to the emotional effect of a musical chord.
