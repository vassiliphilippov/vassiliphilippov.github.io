up in arms

1. ADJ

/ ʌp ɪn ɑrmz / (AmE), / ʌp ɪn ɑːmz / (BrE)
angry and actively protesting against something, especially a decision or situation perceived as unfair
angry and protesting

Examples

Local business owners were up in arms about the proposed tax increase.
Fans were up in arms when their favorite show was cancelled.
Many parents are up in arms over the new school policies.

Synset Examples

The community was up in arms about the new law.
Residents are up in arms regarding the construction plans.
They were up in arms over the proposed cuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angry - feeling or showing strong annoyance or displeasure
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling anger or protest
