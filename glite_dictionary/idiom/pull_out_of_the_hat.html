to pull out of the hat

1. VERB

/ pʊl aʊt əv ðə hæt / (AmE), / pʊl aʊt ɒv ðə hæt / (BrE)
to produce something surprising or unexpected, especially in a difficult situation
produce something surprising or unexpected

Examples

At the last minute, the coach managed to pull out of the hat a new strategy that surprised everyone.
Just when the project seemed doomed, Emily pulled out of the hat an innovative idea to save it.
Can you really pull out of the hat another solution, or are we out of options?

Synset Examples

She always knows how to pull something amazing out of the hat.
The manager pulled a solution out of the hat at the last moment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pull a rabbit out of the hat - to suddenly produce a surprising solution or result
  • Synonym: to conjure up - to produce or make something appear unexpectedly

Etymology

Refers to the magician's trick of pulling a rabbit or object out of a seemingly empty hat.
