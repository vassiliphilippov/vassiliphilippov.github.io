to nerves jangle

1. VERB

/ nɜrvz ˈdʒæŋɡəl / (AmE), / nɜːvz ˈdʒæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
(of a person) to feel anxious, tense, or on edge, especially as a result of stress or anticipation.
feel anxious or tense

Examples

Her nerves jangled every time the phone rang late at night.
Waiting for the exam results made his nerves jangle with worry.
You could see her nerves jangling before she stepped onto the stage.

Synset Examples

His nerves started to jangle before the big presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nervous - feeling or showing anxiety
  • Antonym: calm - not feeling nervous or anxious
