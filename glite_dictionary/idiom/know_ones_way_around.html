to know one's way around

1. VERB

/ noʊ wʌnz weɪ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / nəʊ wʌnz weɪ əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to be familiar with the details of a particular place or subject and able to deal with it effectively
be familiar with a place or subject

Examples

After a few months at the company, she really knew her way around the office procedures.
If you know your way around a computer, this task should be easy.
Tourists often get lost, but locals know their way around the city.

Synset Examples

She knows her way around the kitchen effortlessly.
You really need to know your way around the software.
He knows his way around the city like a local.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be familiar with - to have knowledge or experience of something
