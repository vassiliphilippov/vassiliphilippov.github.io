to buy time

1. VERB

/ baɪ taɪm / (AmE), / baɪ taɪm / (BrE)
to deliberately delay an event or action in order to gain an advantage or prepare for something
delay to gain advantage

Examples

The lawyer tried to buy time by requesting a last-minute extension.
She asked unnecessary questions during the meeting to buy time for her team to finish the report.
The negotiators bought time by promising to consider the offer overnight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stall - to delay taking action in order to gain time
  • Antonym: to hasten - to move or act quickly; opposite of delaying

2. VERB

/ baɪ taɪm / (AmE), / baɪ taɪm / (BrE)
to deliberately delay something in order to gain extra time to prepare or decide what to do
delay action to gain extra time

Examples

The company tried to buy time by requesting a review of the contract.
She asked irrelevant questions to buy time before giving her presentation.
He made small talk with the client to buy time until his manager arrived.

Synset Examples

They used excuses to buy time during negotiations.
She tried to buy time by asking more questions.
He needed to buy time before making a decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stall - to delay taking action, often intentionally
  • Antonym: to hasten - to make something happen sooner or more quickly
