to hide your light under a bushel

1. VERB

/ haɪd jʊr laɪt ˈʌndɚ ə ˈbʊʃəl / (AmE), / haɪd jɔː laɪt ˈʌndə ə ˈbʊʃəl / (BrE)
to conceal your talents, abilities, or achievements instead of making them known to others
conceal your talents or abilities

Examples

It's important not to hide your light under a bushel when applying for a new job.
She tends to hide her light under a bushel and rarely talks about her accomplishments.
Many gifted people hide their light under a bushel out of modesty or shyness.

Synset Examples

Don't hide your light under a bushel; share your talents!
He always hides his light under a bushel, never speaking up.
She felt she was hiding her light under a bushel at work.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to show off - to display one's talents or abilities in a way that attracts attention

Etymology

From a Biblical parable (Matthew 5:15) about not hiding a lamp under a bushel basket.
