to make a bolt

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə boʊlt / (AmE), / meɪk ə bəʊlt / (BrE)
to leave or run away quickly, usually suddenly or unexpectedly
leave or escape quickly

Examples

When the fire alarm rang, several students made a bolt for the exit.
He panicked at the sight of the police and made a bolt down the alley.
As soon as she saw her ex enter the party, she made a bolt for the door.

Synset Examples

She made a bolt to the bran to catch the bus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bolt - to run away suddenly
  • Synonym: to make a run for it - to attempt to escape quickly
  • Antonym: to linger - to stay in a place longer than necessary
