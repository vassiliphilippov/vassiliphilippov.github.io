at odds

1. ADJ

/ æt ɑdz / (AmE), / æt ɒdz / (BrE)
in disagreement or in conflict with someone or something
in conflict or disagreement

Examples

The two scientists were at odds over the interpretation of the results.
Her views are often at odds with those of her colleagues.
Management found itself at odds with the union during the negotiations.

Synset Examples

the figures are at odds with our findings
contradictory attributes of unjust justice and loving vindictiveness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: in agreement - in harmony; not in conflict or disagreement
  • Synonym: at variance - differing; not agreeing; also means in disagreement
