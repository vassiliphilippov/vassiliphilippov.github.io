for toffee

1. ADV

/ fər ˈtɑfi / (AmE), / fə ˈtɒfi / (BrE)
(British, informal) Used in negative statements to emphasize a complete lack of ability or success at doing something.
not at all; lacking any ability (Brit.)
informal british

Examples

He can't dance for toffee, but he always tries at parties.
I wouldn't trust him to fix a bike for toffee.
She can't cook for toffee, yet insists on hosting dinner every month.

Synset Examples

He can’t keep a secret for toffee.
She wouldn’t understand for toffee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at all - in any way or to any extent
