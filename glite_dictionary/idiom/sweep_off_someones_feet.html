to sweep off someone's feet

1. VERB

/ swip ɑf sʌmwʌnz fit / (AmE), / swiːp ɒf sʌmwʌnz fiːt / (BrE)
to impress or enchant someone so strongly that they become completely captivated or infatuated
impress or enchant greatly

Examples

She was completely swept off her feet by his heartfelt proposal.
The rock star's performance swept the crowd off their feet.
He hopes to sweep her off her feet with a romantic getaway.

Synset Examples

The surprise party swept him off his feet.
His charm helped sweep her off her feet.
She wants to sweep him off his feet with romance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to captivate - to attract and hold the interest of someone
  • Synonym: to enchant - to delight someone completely, often by charm
