to let your freak flag fly

1. VERB

/ lɛt jɔr friːk flæɡ flaɪ / (AmE), / lɛt jɔː friːk flæɡ flaɪ / (BrE)
to express your unique personality or unconventional traits openly and proudly
express your individuality openly
informal

Examples

At the festival, she decided to let her freak flag fly by wearing a wild, colorful outfit.
You should never be afraid to let your freak flag fly—that's what makes you interesting.
Sometimes, it's liberating to just let your freak flag fly around close friends.

Synset Examples

It's important to let your freak flag fly sometimes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stand out - to be noticeably different or distinctive
  • Antonym: to blend in - to not attract attention, to conform

Etymology

Popularized in the 1960s, especially after the phrase appeared in music, with 'freak' meaning an unconventional person and 'flag' symbolizing pride.
