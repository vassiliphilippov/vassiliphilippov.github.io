to mark someone's card

1. VERB

/ mɑrk ˈsʌmwʌnz kɑrd / (AmE), / mɑːk ˈsʌmwʌnz kɑːd / (BrE)
to give someone important information or a warning, especially to prepare them for what is about to happen
inform or warn someone
british

Examples

Before starting the new job, her friend marked her card about the company's strict dress code.
If you see him heading this way, mark my card so I'm not caught by surprise.
It's wise to have someone mark your card before negotiating with them.

Synset Examples

She knew how to mark his card in the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to warn - to tell someone about possible danger or problems
