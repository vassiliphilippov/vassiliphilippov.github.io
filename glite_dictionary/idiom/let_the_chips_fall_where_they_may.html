to let the chips fall where they may

1. VERB

/ lɛt ðə tʃɪps fɑl wɛr ðeɪ meɪ / (AmE), / lɛt ðə tʃɪps fɔːl weə ðeɪ meɪ / (BrE)
to allow events to happen naturally, accepting whatever consequences occur without trying to influence the outcome
accept outcome without interference

Examples

She decided to tell the truth and let the chips fall where they may, regardless of the consequences.
Sometimes you just have to take a risk and let the chips fall where they may.
He submitted his application and chose to let the chips fall where they may, trusting that things would work out.

Synset Examples

In the end, I’ll let the chips fall where they may.
I’ll speak my mind and let the chips fall where they may.
He made his decision and let the chips fall where they may.

Etymology

Originates from gambling or woodworking, where 'chips' fall as they may when an action is taken; attested since mid-20th century.
