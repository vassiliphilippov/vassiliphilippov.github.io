to bat an eyelid

1. VERB

/ bæt ən ˈaɪˌlɪd / (AmE), / bæt ən ˈaɪlɪd / (BrE)
to show no surprise, fear, or emotion in response to something unexpected or shocking
show no surprise or emotion

Examples

She didn't bat an eyelid when she heard the expensive vase had been broken.
Even after hearing the outrageous proposal, he didn't bat an eyelid.
When asked to work overtime again, she didn't bat an eyelid and simply agreed.

Synset Examples

She won't bat an eyelid when faced with criticism.
He didn't bat an eyelid at the shocking news.
I couldn't believe he didn’t bat an eyelid at the idea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bat an eye - (US) to show no surprise or reaction
