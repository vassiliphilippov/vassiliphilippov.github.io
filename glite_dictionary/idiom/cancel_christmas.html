to cancel Christmas

1. VERB

/ ˈkænsl ˈkrɪs·məs / (AmE), / ˈkænsl ˈkrɪs.məs / (BrE)
to ruin or spoil the enjoyment, excitement, or spirit of a festive occasion, especially Christmas; to put an end to any hope or celebration
spoil holiday enjoyment or festive spirit

Examples

The boss's announcement about extra work really canceled Christmas for everyone in the office.
Their argument on Christmas Eve almost canceled Christmas for the whole family.
When the snowstorm hit and flights were grounded, it pretty much canceled Christmas for us.

Synset Examples

His harsh words really canceled my Christmas this year.
Missing the concert canceled my Christmas completely.
