to stick one's nose into

1. VERB

/ stɪk wʌnz noʊz ˈɪntu / (AmE), / stɪk wʌnz nəʊz ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
to interfere in someone else's business or affairs, often without being asked or wanted
interfere in others' affairs (idiom)

Examples

I wish you wouldn't stick your nose into things that don't concern you.
She tends to stick her nose into other people's problems, even when she's not invited.
He regretted sticking his nose into the neighbors' argument after things got heated.

Synset Examples

He always stuck his nose into everyone’s business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to meddle - to interfere in something that is not one's concern
  • Antonym: to mind one's own business - to not interfere in others' affairs
