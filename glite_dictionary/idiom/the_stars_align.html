to the stars align

1. VERB

/ ðə stɑɹz əˈlaɪn / (AmE), / ðə stɑːz əˈlaɪn / (BrE)
used to say that circumstances come together in a fortunate or favorable way
favorable circumstances converge

Examples

Sometimes, the stars align and everything works out better than expected.
It’s rare that the stars align for a project to succeed so quickly.
Whenever the stars align, she knows it’s her chance to shine.

Synset Examples

Sometimes, the stars align for perfect opportunities.
She believed the stars align for those who dare to dream.
When the stars align, magic happens in life.

Etymology

From the ancient belief in astrology that the positions of stars affect earthly events.
