to come into one's own

1. VERB

/ kʌm ˈɪntu wʌnz oʊn / (AmE), / kʌm ˈɪntuː wʌnz əʊn / (BrE)
to become fully confident, independent, or successful; to achieve recognition or realize one's full potential
achieve potential or recognition

Examples

After switching careers, she really came into her own and found happiness.
It took a while, but his business finally came into its own after years of hard work.
During college, he came into his own as an artist and gained widespread acclaim.

Synset Examples

The young artist has truly come into her own.
After years of practice, she has come into her own.
He finally came into his own as a leader.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flourish - to develop rapidly and successfully; thrive
  • Synonym: blossom - to develop or come to a promising stage
  • Antonym: fade - to gradually lose strength or importance
