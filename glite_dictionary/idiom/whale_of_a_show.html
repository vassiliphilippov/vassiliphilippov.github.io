whale of a show

1. NOUN

/ weɪl əv ə ʃoʊ / (AmE), / weɪl əv ə ʃəʊ / (BrE)
an event or performance that is exceptionally good or impressive
outstanding or impressive event

Examples

They put on a whale of a show last night at the festival.
The circus was a whale of a show that kept the audience entertained for hours.
Everyone agreed that the school play was a whale of a show this year.

Synset Examples

The concert was a whale of a show, truly unforgettable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whale of a time - a very enjoyable experience

Etymology

From the idiom 'a whale of a,' using 'whale' to mean 'very large or impressive' (19th century American English).
