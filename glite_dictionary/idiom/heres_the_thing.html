here's the thing

1. X

/ hɪɹz ðə ˈθɪŋ / (AmE), / hɪəz ðə ˈθɪŋ / (BrE)
used to introduce the most important point or a key issue in a discussion, especially when explaining or justifying something
introducing a key point in discussion
informal

Examples

Here's the thing, we don't have enough evidence to proceed.
Here's the thing: you need to decide by tomorrow if you want the job.
When asked about the plan, she replied, "here's the thing, it might not work as expected."

Synset Examples

Here's the thing, I don't think that's a good idea.
Here's the thing, we can't afford to lose time.
Here's the thing: we need to act fast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the point is - used to highlight the main point or issue in discussion
