sleeping giant

1. NOUN

/ ˈsliːpɪŋ ˈdʒaɪənt / (AmE), / ˈsliːpɪŋ ˈdʒaɪənt / (BrE)
a person, organization, or thing with great but unrealized or inactive power or potential
something with dormant power or potential

Examples

Many analysts believe that India is a sleeping giant in the global economy.
The new technology could awaken a sleeping giant in the industry.
For years, the old political party has been a sleeping giant, but now it's regaining influence.

Synset Examples

The company is a sleeping giant, ready to dominate the market.
They ignored the sleeping giant until it woke up.
Climate change is a sleeping giant that needs urgent action.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powerhouse - an entity with great power or energy
  • Antonym: underdog - a competitor thought to have little chance of winning
