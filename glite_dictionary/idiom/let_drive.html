to let drive

1. VERB

/ lɛt draɪv / (AmE), / lɛt draɪv / (BrE)
to shoot or launch something forcefully, typically from a weapon such as a gun or bow
fire or launch forcefully (weapon)
archaic

Examples

At the signal, the archers let drive a volley of arrows toward the enemy lines.
Startled by the noise, he let drive his pistol at the shadow in the doorway.
The rebel leader let drive a stone from his sling, striking the guard.

Synset Examples

The soldiers let drive their bullets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire - to shoot a gun or other weapon
