to break someone's chops

1. VERB

/ breɪk ˈsʌmwʌnz tʃɑps / (AmE), / breɪk ˈsʌmwʌnz tʃɒps / (BrE)
to tease, criticize playfully, or nag someone, often in a persistent way
tease or nag someone playfully
informal

Examples

Stop trying to break my chops—I'm working as fast as I can.
She likes to break his chops about his messy desk every morning.
He was only breaking your chops; don't take it so seriously.

Synset Examples

She loves to break his chops during the meetings.
Don’t break my chops, I’m doing my best here!
He always likes to break my chops about my cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bust someone's chops - to criticize or tease someone persistently or harshly
