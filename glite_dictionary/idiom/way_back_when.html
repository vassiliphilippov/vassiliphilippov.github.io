way back when

1. ADV

/ weɪ bæk wɛn / (AmE), / weɪ bæk wen / (BrE)
used to refer to a time in the distant past; a long time ago
a long time ago (idiom)
informal

Examples

Way back when, people sent letters instead of emails.
He remembers how things were different way back when.
Way back when, my grandparents first met in this city.

Synset Examples

Way back when, I used to play outside all day.
She reminisced about wayback when they were inseparable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long ago - a long time in the past
  • Antonym: recently - not long ago, in the near past
