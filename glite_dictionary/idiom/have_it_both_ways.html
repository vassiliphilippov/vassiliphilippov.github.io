to have it both ways

1. VERB

/ ˈhæv ɪt boʊθ weɪz / (AmE), / ˈhav ɪt bəʊθ weɪz / (BrE)
to try to benefit from two opposing or incompatible options at the same time
benefit from two incompatible options

Examples

He wants to have it both ways by spending freely and still keeping his savings untouched.
You can't have it both ways; you must decide which side you're on.
She tried to have it both ways by working part-time but expecting a full salary.

Synset Examples

You can't have it both ways, choose one.
If you want to save money, you can't have it both ways.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sit on the fence - to avoid making a decision or choice
