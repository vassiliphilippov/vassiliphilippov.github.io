to pull the plug

1. VERB

/ pʊl ðə plʌɡ / (AmE), / pʊl ðə plʌɡ / (BrE)
to stop something from happening or continuing, especially by withdrawing support or resources
end or stop something; withdraw support

Examples

After months of financial losses, the board decided to pull the plug on the project.
They threatened to pull the plug if the results didn't improve soon.
When the sponsor withdrew funding, it effectively pulled the plug on the festival.

Synset Examples

The government pulled the plug on spending

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cancel - to decide that something will not take place
  • Antonym: to continue - to keep happening, existing, or doing something

Etymology

Originally from the literal action of removing the plug from a device or drain to stop it functioning; figuratively adopted in the 20th century.
