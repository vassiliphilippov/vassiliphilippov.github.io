to rise from the ashes

1. VERB

/ raɪz frəm ði ˈæʃɪz / (AmE), / raɪz frəm ði ˈæʃɪz / (BrE)
to recover successfully after experiencing defeat, destruction, or great difficulties
recover after disaster or failure

Examples

After the devastating fire, the community worked together to rise from the ashes and rebuild their homes.
Many admired her determination to rise from the ashes following her personal losses.
The company managed to rise from the ashes after facing bankruptcy and heavy criticism.

Synset Examples

The business hopes to rise from the ashes after bankruptcy.
She managed to rise from the ashes of her failures.
The city will rise from the ashes of the disaster.

Etymology

From the myth of the phoenix, a bird in Greek mythology that is reborn from its own ashes.
