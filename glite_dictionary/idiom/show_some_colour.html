to show some colour

1. VERB

/ ʃoʊ səm ˈkʌlər / (AmE), / ʃəʊ səm ˈkʌlə / (BrE)
to add vibrancy, liveliness, or brightness, either literally by introducing color or figuratively by expressing liveliness or personality.
add vibrancy or liveliness

Examples

Let's show some colour in our marketing materials to attract more attention.
Her designs always show some colour, making each piece unique and lively.
You should show some colour in your speeches to engage the audience.

Synset Examples

It’s time to show some colour in this dull room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to brighten up - make or become more cheerful or lively
