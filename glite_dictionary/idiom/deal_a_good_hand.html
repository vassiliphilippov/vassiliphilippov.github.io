to deal a good hand

1. VERB

/ diːl ə ɡʊd hænd / (AmE), / diːl ə ɡʊd hænd / (BrE)
to manage or handle a situation effectively, especially by making the best use of available circumstances or resources
manage a situation effectively

Examples

She always manages to deal a good hand, even when faced with difficult challenges.
If you want to succeed in business, you must learn how to deal a good hand.
Although his resources were limited, he was able to deal a good hand and finish the project successfully.

Synset Examples

In life, it’s not about the cards you get, but how you deal a good hand.
He managed to deal a good hand at the negotiation table.
She always knows how to deal a good hand in poker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: play your cards right - to make good decisions to achieve a favorable outcome

Etymology

Alludes to card games, where being 'dealt a good hand' means being given favorable cards, extended figuratively to life situations.
