to bend your ear

1. VERB

/ bɛnd jɚ ɪr / (AmE), / bɛnd jɔː ɪə / (BrE)
to talk to someone for a long time, often in a way that may be tiresome or persistent
talk to someone at length

Examples

Whenever I visit, he loves to bend my ear about his travels.
Don't bend my ear with your complaints all afternoon.
She was eager to bend his ear about her new ideas for the company.

Synset Examples

I need to bend your ear about my new project.
Stop bending my ear; I get your point.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to chew the fat - have a long and informal conversation
  • Synonym: to pester - to bother or annoy with persistent requests or talking
