to walk a tightrope

1. VERB

/ wɔk ə ˈtaɪtˌroʊp / (AmE), / wɔːk ə ˈtaɪtrəʊp / (BrE)
to be in a difficult or dangerous situation where careful and precise action is needed to avoid failure or trouble
be in a risky or delicate situation

Examples

As the company's new CEO, she felt like she was walking a tightrope every day.
Negotiating the deal involved walking a tightrope between both sides' demands.
He realized he was walking a tightrope in trying to keep both friends happy at once.

Synset Examples

She was on a tightrope trying to please everyone.
Managing her schedule felt like walking a tightrope.
Managing a team requires walking a tightrope of personalities.
The negotiations felt like walking a tightrope without a safety net.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be between a rock and a hard place - in a situation with no easy or safe option
  • Synonym: to play with fire - to do something risky or dangerous

Etymology

From the literal act of performing on a tightrope, extended metaphorically to mean a precarious or risky situation.
