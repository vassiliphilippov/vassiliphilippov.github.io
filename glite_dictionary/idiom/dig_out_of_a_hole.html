to dig out of a hole

1. VERB

/ dɪɡ ˈaʊt əv ə hoʊl / (AmE), / dɪɡ ˈaʊt əv ə həʊl / (BrE)
to recover or escape from a difficult or problematic situation, especially one caused by earlier mistakes or misfortune
recover from a difficult situation

Examples

After losing his job, Tom managed to dig out of a hole by starting his own business.
It took months of hard work to dig out of a hole financially after the unexpected expenses.
Every time she makes a mistake, she somehow manages to dig out of a hole and improve her situation.

Synset Examples

He had to work hard to dig out of that financial hole.
She found a way to dig out of her emotional hole.
He struggled to dig out of the hole he created.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get out of a hole - to escape a difficult or problematic situation
  • Antonym: to fall into a hole - to get into a difficult or problematic situation
