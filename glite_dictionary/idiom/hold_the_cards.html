to hold the cards

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ðə kɑrdz / (AmE), / həʊld ðə kɑːdz / (BrE)
to have control or an advantage in a particular situation
to have control or an advantage

Examples

In this negotiation, the company clearly holds the cards and can dictate the terms.
During the merger talks, investors realized they no longer held the cards.
She likes situations where she holds the cards and others rely on her decisions.

Synset Examples

They think they hold the cards, but we have a few tricks.
He always knows how to hold the cards in negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to call the shots - to be in control and make the important decisions
  • Synonym: hold all the aces - to be in a very strong or advantageous position
  • Antonym: be at a disadvantage - to be in a less favorable position
