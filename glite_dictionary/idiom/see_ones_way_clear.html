to see one's way clear

1. VERB

/ si wʌnz weɪ klɪr / (AmE), / si wʌnz weɪ klɪə / (BrE)
to understand how to proceed or find it possible to take a particular action, especially after overcoming difficulty or hesitation
find it possible to proceed

Examples

After the budget was approved, I saw my way clear to hiring two new employees.
Once the obstacles were removed, she saw her way clear to investing in the project.
Despite his doubts earlier, Mark now sees his way clear to signing the contract.

Synset Examples

Once we resolve this issue, I see my way clear to moving forward.
After considering all options, I see my way clear to accept the offer.
I see my way clear to finishing the project this week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to find a way - discover a method or possibility to do something
