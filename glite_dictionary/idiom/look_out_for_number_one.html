to look out for number one

1. VERB

/ lʊk aʊt fər ˌnʌm.bɚ ˈwʌn / (AmE), / lʊk aʊt fə ˌnʌm.bə ˈwʌn / (BrE)
to prioritize one's own interests or well-being above those of others
prioritize oneself or one's own interests

Examples

When resources were scarce, she had to look out for number one to survive.
He always seems to look out for number one, regardless of the team's needs.
Sometimes it's necessary to look out for number one in a competitive environment.

Synset Examples

To succeed, sometimes you have to look out for number one.
In business, you must look out for number one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: put oneself first - to give priority to one's own needs or desires
  • Degree Higher: selfish - lacking consideration for others and caring only about oneself
