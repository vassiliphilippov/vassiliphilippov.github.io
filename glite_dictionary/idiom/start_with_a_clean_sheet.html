to start with a clean sheet

1. VERB

/ stɑrt wɪð ə klin ʃit / (AmE), / stɑːt wɪð ə kliːn ʃiːt / (BrE)
to begin a new undertaking or phase without being influenced by previous events, mistakes, or issues
begin anew without past burdens

Examples

After the merger, the two companies agreed to start with a clean sheet and build a new culture together.
Sometimes it's best to start with a clean sheet rather than trying to fix old problems.
Moving to a new city gave me the chance to start with a clean sheet.

Synset Examples

After the meeting, we decided to start with a clean sheet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: start from scratch - to begin from nothing, without prior advantage
  • Synonym: turn over a new leaf - to start behaving in a better or different way
