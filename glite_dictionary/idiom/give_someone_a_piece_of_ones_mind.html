to give (someone) a piece of one's mind

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ə ˈpis əv wʌnz maɪnd / (AmE), / ɡɪv ə ˈpiːs əv wʌnz maɪnd / (BrE)
to express one's disapproval or anger to someone, often in a direct or forceful way
speak angrily or frankly to someone

Examples

After the poor service, she decided to give the manager a piece of her mind.
If you keep ignoring my calls, I'll give you a piece of my mind next time I see you.
He marched into the office to give his boss a piece of his mind about the unfair rule.

Synset Examples

He was upset and wanted to give her a piece of his mind.
I plan to give him a piece of my mind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tell off - to speak angrily to someone for doing something wrong
  • Synonym: scold - to criticize angrily, especially a child
