opposite sides of the barricades

1. X

/ ˈɑːpəzɪt saɪdz əv ðə ˌbærəˈkeɪdz / (AmE), / ˈɒpəzɪt saɪdz ɒv ðə ˌbærɪˈkeɪdz / (BrE)
in opposing groups or positions in a debate, dispute, or conflict
in conflict or opposition; on rival sides

Examples

After the election, they found themselves on opposite sides of the barricades in every meeting.
Historians often take opposite sides of the barricades when interpreting controversial events.
During the negotiation, both leaders came from opposite sides of the barricades, making compromise difficult.

Synset Examples

They argued passionately from opposite sides of the barricades.
They found themselves on opposite sides of the barricades during the protest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on opposite sides - in opposing groups or positions
  • Antonym: in agreement - not in conflict or opposition; sharing the same view

Etymology

From the French 'barricade' (a defensive barrier), used metaphorically to refer to divisions in conflicts.
