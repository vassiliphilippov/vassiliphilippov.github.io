to extract the Michael

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈstrækt ði ˈmaɪkəl / (AmE), / ɪkˈstrakt ði ˈmaɪkəl / (BrE)
to mock or tease someone, often in a light-hearted or playful manner
tease or mock playfully
informal british

Examples

He loves to extract the Michael when things get too serious at work.
Please don't extract the Michael during this important meeting.
She was just extracting the Michael, so don't take it personally.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take the mickey - to tease or mock someone (British informal)
  • Synonym: take the piss - to make fun of someone (British slang)

Etymology

Alteration of 'take the mickey,' itself a euphemism for 'take the piss.'
