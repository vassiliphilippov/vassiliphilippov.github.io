to shake the pagoda tree

1. VERB

/ ʃeɪk ðə pəˈɡoʊdə ˌtri / (AmE), / ʃeɪk ðə pəˈɡəʊdə ˌtriː / (BrE)
to provoke a reaction or reveal something hidden, often by challenging the status quo
provoke a reaction or reveal secrets

Examples

When the manager started asking tough questions, he was clearly trying to shake the pagoda tree.
Sometimes it's necessary to shake the pagoda tree if you want to discover the real problems in an organization.
She didn't want to shake the pagoda tree, fearing it might upset the unofficial balance in the office.

Synset Examples

He decided to shake the pagoda tree to see what falls.

Etymology

Alludes to pagoda trees (from East Asia), sometimes metaphorically linked to opportunities or hidden riches.
