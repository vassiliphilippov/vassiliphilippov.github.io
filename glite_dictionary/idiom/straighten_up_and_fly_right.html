to straighten up and fly right

1. VERB

/ ˈstreɪtən ʌp ənd flaɪ raɪt / (AmE), / ˈstreɪt(ə)n ʌp ənd flaɪ raɪt / (BrE)
to start behaving in a proper or disciplined way, especially after a period of misconduct
behave properly; act correctly

Examples

If you don't straighten up and fly right, you might get expelled from school.
Her manager warned her to straighten up and fly right or face disciplinary action.
After the warning, he tried to straighten up and fly right at work.

Synset Examples

If you want to succeed, you need to straighten up and fly right.
He decided it was time to straighten up and fly right after his last mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shape up - to improve one's behaviour or performance
  • Antonym: to misbehave - to behave badly or improperly

Etymology

Originally popularized by the 1944 song "Straighten Up and Fly Right" by Nat King Cole, using aviation as a metaphor for good behavior.
