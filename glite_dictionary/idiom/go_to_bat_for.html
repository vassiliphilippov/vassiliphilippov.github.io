to go to bat for

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ tə bæt fɔr / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə bæt fə / (BrE)
to support or defend someone, especially in a difficult situation
support or defend someone
informal american

Examples

Whenever her coworkers face criticism, she always goes to bat for them.
I'm grateful that my manager went to bat for me during the negotiations.
He promised he would go to bat for us if the proposal is challenged.

Synset Examples

She always goes to bat for her team.
I will go to bat for you if needed.
He went to bat for his friend during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stand up for - to defend or protect someone or something
  • Synonym: stick up for - to support or defend someone or something, especially when others criticize them
  • Antonym: to turn against - to stop supporting or defending someone and oppose them instead

Etymology

From baseball terminology (American English), where 'to go to bat' means to take one's turn batting for a team.
