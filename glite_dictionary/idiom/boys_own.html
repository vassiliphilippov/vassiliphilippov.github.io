Boy's Own

1. ADJ

/ bɔɪz oʊn / (AmE), / bɔɪz əʊn / (BrE)
Used to describe an adventurous or exciting lifestyle, often reminiscent of traditional adventure stories for boys.
adventurous, exciting way of life
british

Examples

He spent his twenties living a Boy's Own adventure, traveling through remote jungles and mountains.
The rescue mission had a Boy's Own feel, full of risk and heroics.
Journalists described his daring journey as straight out of a Boy's Own tale.

Synset Examples

They lived a Boy's Own existence, full of thrills.

Etymology

From the British children's magazine 'The Boy's Own Paper' (published 1879–1967), which featured adventure stories aimed at boys.
