life and death

1. NOUN

/ laɪf ənd dɛθ / (AmE), / laɪf ənd dɛθ / (BrE)
used to describe a situation of extreme importance or serious risk, often involving the possibility of survival or disaster
situation of extreme seriousness or risk

Examples

The doctor stressed that the patient's condition was a life and death matter.
During the natural disaster, he faced life and death decisions every day.
They treated the argument as if it were a life and death issue.

Synset Examples

In emergencies, it's often a matter of life and death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grave - very serious or important
  • Antonym: trivial - not serious or important
