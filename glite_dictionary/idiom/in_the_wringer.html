in the wringer

1. ADJ

/ ɪn ðə ˈrɪŋər / (AmE), / ɪn ðə ˈrɪŋə / (BrE)
in a difficult or stressful situation; undergoing hardship or intense pressure
in a stressful or difficult situation

Examples

After a day full of urgent meetings, I felt completely in the wringer.
She's really in the wringer since her project fell behind schedule.
Any athlete would feel in the wringer after such an intense competition.

Synset Examples

After that workout, I'm really in the wringer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: under the gun - under pressure or in a difficult situation
  • Synonym: in a bind - in a difficult position or predicament
  • Antonym: out of the woods - out of difficulty or danger; no longer in trouble

Etymology

From the image of a wringer (a device that squeezes water out of clothes), figuratively suggesting being squeezed or pressured.
