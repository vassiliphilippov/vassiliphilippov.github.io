to know where the bodies are buried

1. VERB

/ noʊ wɛr ðə ˈbɑdiz ɑr ˈbɛrid / (AmE), / nəʊ weə ðə ˈbɒdiz ə ˈbɛrid / (BrE)
to possess secret or insider knowledge about the hidden, usually unpleasant, details of an organization or situation, often because of long association
have insider or secret knowledge

Examples

Everyone on the team turns to Sarah because she knows where the bodies are buried around here.
After working for the company for two decades, Martin knows where the bodies are buried.
You should keep her on your good side—she really knows where the bodies are buried.

Synset Examples

He’s the one who knows where the bodies are buried in this office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insider - a person within an organization with special knowledge
  • Synonym: secret - something kept hidden or unknown from others
