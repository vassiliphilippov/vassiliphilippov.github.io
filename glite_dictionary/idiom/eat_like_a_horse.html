to eat like a horse

1. VERB

/ it laɪk ə hɔrs / (AmE), / iːt laɪk ə hɔːs / (BrE)
to eat a very large quantity of food, especially habitually or at a particular time
eat a large amount of food

Examples

After hiking all day, I could eat like a horse.
He always eats like a horse during the holidays.
Their teenage son started eating like a horse since he joined the football team.

Synset Examples

She works out a lot, so she eats like a horse.
After the marathon, he could eat like a horse.
Teenagers often eat like a horse at dinner.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to eat like a bird - to eat only a very small amount of food
