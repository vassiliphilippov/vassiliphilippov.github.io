to rise with the lark

1. VERB

/ raɪz wɪð ðə lɑrk / (AmE), / raɪz wɪð ðə lɑːk / (BrE)
to wake up very early in the morning, often at or just after sunrise
wake up very early

Examples

Every Monday, he likes to rise with the lark and watch the sunrise with a cup of coffee.
If you rise with the lark, you can get a lot done before most people are awake.
She made a habit of rising with the lark to enjoy a peaceful morning walk.

Synset Examples

They rise up with the lark to farm before the heat.
To be productive, I try to rise with the lark daily.
I prefer to rise with the lark and start my day early.
She always rises with the lark, ready to jog.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: early bird - someone who wakes up or arrives early
  • Antonym: night owl - someone who stays up late and prefers evenings

Etymology

Refers to the lark, a bird noted for singing early at dawn.
