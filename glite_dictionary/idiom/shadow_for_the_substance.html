shadow for the substance

1. X

/ ˈʃædoʊ fər ðə ˈsʌbstəns / (AmE), / ˈʃadəʊ fə ðə ˈsʌbstəns / (BrE)
To mistake appearance for reality or confuse something insubstantial with something real.
confuse appearance with reality

Examples

The philosopher cautioned his students not to take the shadow for the substance when studying abstract ideas.
It's easy in politics to take the shadow for the substance and be swayed by appearances.
In business negotiations, one should never take the shadow for the substance or rely solely on first impressions.

Synset Examples

Don't take the shadow for the substance; look deeper.
He warned her not to take the shadow for the substance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: appearance - the way that someone or something looks; the outward form
  • Degree Higher: reality - the state of things as they actually exist
  • Synonym: illusion - a false idea or belief; something that is not what it seems to be
