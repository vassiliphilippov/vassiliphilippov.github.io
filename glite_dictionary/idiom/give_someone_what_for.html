to give someone what for

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ˈsʌmwʌn ˌwʌt ˈfɔr / (AmE), / ɡɪv ˈsʌmwən ˌwɒt ˈfɔː / (BrE)
to scold or reprimand someone angrily or severely
scold or reprimand someone harshly
informal

Examples

If you come home late again, your mother will give you what for.
The manager gave the entire staff what for after the poor sales numbers.
He was afraid his teacher would give him what for when she saw his messy homework.

Synset Examples

She really gave him what for after his rude comment.
She gave him what-for when he broke her vase.
The coach gave the team what for after their loss.
He really gave her what for after the mistake.
I’ll give him what for if he keeps pushing me.
He was ready to give her what for for being late.
She threatened to give him what for if he was late again.
I will give him what-for if he doesn't apologize.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tell someone off - to reprimand someone harshly
  • Synonym: reprimand - to formally express disapproval
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
