four sheets to the wind

1. ADJ

/ fɔr ʃits tə ðə wɪnd / (AmE), / fɔː ʃiːts tə ðə wɪnd / (BrE)
extremely drunk or intoxicated
very drunk

Examples

After a long night at the pub, he was four sheets to the wind before midnight.
She arrived at the party already four sheets to the wind.
By the end of the wedding, most of the guests were four sheets to the wind.

Synset Examples

He was four sheets to the wind by midnight.
After three drinks, he was four sheets to the wind.
She stumbled home, four sheets to the wind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: three sheets to the wind - very drunk; the original form of the idiom

Etymology

An extension of the nautical phrase 'three sheets to the wind,' where 'sheet' refers to a rope controlling a sail; if multiple sheets are loose, a ship reels drunkenly.
