to take one's sweet time

1. VERB

/ teɪk wʌnz swiːt taɪm / (AmE), / teɪk wʌnz swiːt taɪm / (BrE)
to do something deliberately slowly, taking much longer than necessary, often implying a lack of haste or urgency
do something very slowly; with no hurry

Examples

You really take your sweet time getting ready in the morning.
He decided to take his sweet time reading the letter, savoring every word.
She always takes her sweet time when choosing what to wear.

Synset Examples

He likes to take his sweet time while cooking.
She'll take her sweet little time finishing that project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dawdle - to waste time; to move or act slowly
  • Antonym: hurry - to do something quickly or with speed
