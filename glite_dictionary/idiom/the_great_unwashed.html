the great unwashed

1. NOUN

/ ðə ɡreɪt ʌnˈwɑʃt / (AmE), / ðə ɡreɪt ʌnˈwɒʃt / (BrE)
(humorous, sometimes derogatory) Ordinary people as a group, especially when contrasted with the privileged or elite.
ordinary people (often humorously or ironically)

Examples

The movie star tried to hide from the great unwashed at the airport.
Sometimes politicians forget the needs of the great unwashed when crafting policies.
He joked that he was forced to mingle with the great unwashed at the crowded pub.

Synset Examples

separate the warriors from the mass
power to the people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the masses - the ordinary people in society
  • Antonym: the elite - a group of people considered to be superior

Etymology

First used in early 19th century; likely popularized by writer Edward Bulwer-Lytton.
