last chance saloon

1. NOUN

/ læst tʃæns səˈlun / (AmE), / lɑːst tʃɑːns səˈluːn / (BrE)
a final opportunity to succeed or avoid failure, often in a challenging or critical situation
final opportunity to succeed or avoid failure

Examples

After losing the first two games, the third match was the last chance saloon for the team.
Many believed the summit was the last chance saloon for reaching a compromise on climate change.
When the company faced bankruptcy, the emergency meeting became the last chance saloon for its survival.

Synset Examples

This job interview feels like my last chance saloon.
With the deadline approaching, we were in the last chance saloon.
This meeting is the last chance saloon to save the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: last ditch - final, desperate attempt or effort

Etymology

Alludes to saloons in the American West, with the phrase likely originating from 19th-century bars offering a final drink before leaving town or facing consequences.
