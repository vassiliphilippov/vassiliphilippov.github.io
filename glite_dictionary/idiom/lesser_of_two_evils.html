lesser of two evils

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛsɚ əv tu ˈivəlz / (AmE), / ˈlɛsə əv tuː ˈiːvɪlz / (BrE)
the less harmful or undesirable of two unpleasant choices
the less harmful of two bad options

Examples

When faced with two corrupt candidates, voters felt compelled to choose the lesser of two evils.
She didn't like either job offer, but she picked the lesser of two evils.
Sometimes you have to settle for the lesser of two evils rather than holding out for something perfect.

Synset Examples

Choosing the greater of two evils is never easy.
Choosing the lesser of two evils was a tough decision.
In this election, many voters felt they had to choose the lesser of two evils.
Sometimes, the lesser evil is just the greater of two evils.
We had to decide on the greater of two evils in the election.
He chose the lesser of two evils to avoid a bigger problem.
