to pack heat

1. VERB

/ pæk hit / (AmE), / pæk hiːt / (BrE)
to carry a firearm, especially a handgun, usually for personal protection or as a display of readiness for violence.
carry a firearm (slang/idiom)
informal american

Examples

You shouldn't pack heat unless you really know how to use a gun safely.
Rumor had it that the new boss always packs heat wherever he goes.
After the robbery uptown, more people started packing heat for self-defense.
