to hold all the cards

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ɔl ðə kɑrdz / (AmE), / həʊld ɔːl ðə kɑːdz / (BrE)
to have complete control or an overwhelming advantage in a situation
have total control or advantage

Examples

In this negotiation, she holds all the cards and can dictate the terms.
It's difficult to win when your opponent holds all the cards.
After the merger, the new company held all the cards in the industry.

Synset Examples

In this deal, she holds all the cards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hold the upper hand - to be in a position of advantage or control
  • Synonym: be in the driver's seat - to be in control of a situation
  • Antonym: be at someone's mercy - to be in a position of complete dependence or disadvantage
