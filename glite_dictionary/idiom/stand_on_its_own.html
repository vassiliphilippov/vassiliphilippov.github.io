to stand on its own

1. VERB

/ stænd ɑn ɪts oʊn / (AmE), / stænd ɒn ɪts əʊn / (BrE)
to be independent or self-sufficient, judged by one's own qualities or merits rather than relying on support from others
be independent, self-sufficient

Examples

The new software is impressive enough to stand on its own in the market.
His conclusions must stand on their own without external validation.
Our small business can finally stand on its own after years of support.

Synset Examples

The project can stand on its own without additional funding.
Her argument can stand on its own without support.
This report must stand on its own merits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stand alone - be able to function independently
  • Antonym: to depend on - to require help or support from others
