to keep one's mouth shut

1. VERB

/ kip wʌnz maʊθ ʃʌt / (AmE), / kiːp wʌnz maʊθ ʃʌt / (BrE)
to refrain from revealing information or speaking about something, especially something confidential or sensitive
remain silent; not reveal information

Examples

You'd better keep your mouth shut if you want to avoid trouble.
I promised to keep my mouth shut about the surprise party.
They were warned to keep their mouths shut during the investigation.

Synset Examples

Don't tell him any secrets — he cannot keep his mouth shut!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keep quiet - refrain from speaking or revealing information
  • Antonym: to spill the beans - reveal secret or confidential information

2. VERB

/ kip wʌnz maʊθ ʃʌt / (AmE), / kiːp wʌnz maʊθ ʃʌt / (BrE)
to remain silent or to purposely not reveal information, often to avoid trouble or maintain secrecy
remain silent; not disclose information
informal

Examples

He decided it was best to keep his mouth shut and let others do the talking.
If you hear a rumor at work, sometimes it's wiser to keep your mouth shut.
She promised to keep her mouth shut about the surprise party.

Synset Examples

He knew to keep his mouth shut during the meeting.
She learned to keep her mouth shut about sensitive topics.
If you want to avoid trouble, just keep your mouth shut.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keep quiet - to refrain from speaking or making noise, especially to avoid revealing something
  • Antonym: to spill the beans - to reveal secret or confidential information
