to box the compass

1. VERB

/ bɑks ðə ˈkʌmpəs / (AmE), / bɒks ðə ˈkʌmpəs / (BrE)
to recite all the points of the compass in their order, or to know and indicate all directions precisely
recite all compass points in order

Examples

In his exam, the cadet had to box the compass without making a single mistake.
Old sailors were expected to box the compass as part of their training.
She surprised her friends by being able to box the compass flawlessly.

Synset Examples

The sailor could box the compass without any hesitation.
He learned to box the compass during his sailing course.

Etymology

'Box' in this sense means 'to name' or 'enumerate'; the expression dates from early use in nautical contexts in the 17th century.
