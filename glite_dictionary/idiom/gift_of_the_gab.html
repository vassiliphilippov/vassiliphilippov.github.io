gift of the gab

1. NOUN

/ ɡɪft əv ðə ɡæb / (AmE), / ɡɪft ɒv ðə ɡæb / (BrE)
the ability to speak easily, confidently, and persuasively, especially in a way that is likely to impress or influence others
ability to speak persuasively and confidently

Examples

She owes her success to her gift of the gab and quick thinking.
People with the gift of the gab often excel in sales and public speaking.
His gift of the gab made him popular at every party.

Synset Examples

Her gift of the gab won her many friends.
He has the gift of the gab, charming everyone.
In sales, having the gift of the gab is essential.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silver tongue - the ability to speak in a very persuasive way

Etymology

From the slang sense of 'gab' meaning 'talk' or 'chatter', dating back to the 18th century.
