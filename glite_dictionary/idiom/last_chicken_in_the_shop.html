last chicken in the shop

1. NOUN

/ læst ˈtʃɪkən ɪn ðə ʃɑp / (AmE), / lɑːst ˈtʃɪkɪn ɪn ðə ʃɒp / (BrE)
someone who considers themselves to be highly valued, unique, or special, sometimes also feeling unwanted or overlooked.
person who thinks they are unique or special

Examples

He strutted into the room acting like the last chicken in the shop, expecting everyone’s admiration.
Sometimes she feels like the last chicken in the shop, both wanted and a bit out of place.
Don’t act like you’re the last chicken in the shop; we’re all important here.

Synset Examples

He strutted around, thinking he was the last chicken in the shop.
At the party, she felt like the last chicken in the shop, everyone wanted her company.
He's acting like the last chicken in the shop with all that attention.
