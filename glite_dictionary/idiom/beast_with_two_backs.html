beast with two backs

1. NOUN

/ bist wɪð tuː bæks / (AmE), / biːst wɪð tuː bæks / (BrE)
a literary euphemism for sexual intercourse, describing two people engaged in the act
euphemism for sexual intercourse
archaic

Examples

In Shakespearean drama, the beast with two backs is a notorious euphemism.
The poet alluded to the beast with two backs in his bawdy verses.
That passage blushes with its reference to the beast with two backs.

Synset Examples

The phrase 'beast with two backs' often refers to passionate intimacy.
In the romantic scene, they were described as a beast with two backs.
The Shakespearean reference to the beast with two backs made everyone blush.

Etymology

Popularized by Shakespeare in 'Othello' (1603); literal metaphor for two people joined in intercourse as one animal with two backs.
