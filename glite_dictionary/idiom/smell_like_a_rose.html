to smell like a rose

1. VERB

/ smɛl laɪk ə roʊz / (AmE), / smɛl laɪk ə rəʊz / (BrE)
to appear or emerge in a favorable or unimpaired state, especially after a difficult or problematic situation
emerge favorably after trouble

Examples

Despite the controversy, he managed to smell like a rose in the end.
No matter the problem at work, she always seems to smell like a rose.
After handling the crisis, the company came out smelling like a rose.

Synset Examples

No matter the chaos, she makes it smell like a rose.
He always manages to make things smell like a rose.
After the cleanup, everything will smell like a rose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: come up smelling of roses - emerge from a situation with one's reputation untarnished or improved
