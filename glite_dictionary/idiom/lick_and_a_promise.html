lick and a promise

1. NOUN

/ lɪk ənd ə ˈprɑːmɪs / (AmE), / lɪk ənd ə ˈprɒmɪs / (BrE)
a quick and superficial job, done hastily with the intention of coming back to do it properly later
quick, superficial job

Examples

She gave the floor just lick and a promise before the guests arrived.
He ran a cloth over the shelves, but it was only lick and a promise.
You're supposed to clean the car thoroughly, not just give it lick and a promise!

Synset Examples

He cleaned the kitchen with a lick and a promise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: once-over - a quick or cursory look or cleaning

Etymology

Originally used in the 19th century, referring to a hasty wash (a 'lick') with the intention ('a promise') of cleaning properly later.
