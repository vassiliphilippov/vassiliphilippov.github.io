to put the toothpaste back in the tube

1. VERB

/ pʊt ðə ˈtuːθˌpeɪst bæk ɪn ðə tuːb / (AmE), / pʊt ðə ˈtuːθpeɪst bæk ɪn ðə tjuːb / (BrE)
to attempt to undo something that cannot be reversed; to try to return to a previous situation after an irreversible action has been taken
try to undo an irreversible action

Examples

Once private information is leaked online, it's nearly impossible to put the toothpaste back in the tube.
After publishing the report, the company couldn't put the toothpaste back in the tube even if it tried.
Now that they've made their decision public, it's too late to put the toothpaste back in the tube.

Synset Examples

After the scandal, it was too late to put the toothpaste back in the tube.
You can’t put the toothpaste back in the tube once it’s out.
After the announcement, there was no way to put the toothpaste back in the tube.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to close the stable door after the horse has bolted - try to prevent something after it has already happened
  • Synonym: irreversible - not able to be undone or changed
