few cards shy of a full deck

1. ADJ

/ fju kɑrdz ʃaɪ əv ə fʊl dɛk / (AmE), / fjuː kɑːdz ʃaɪ ɒv ə fʊl dɛk / (BrE)
used to say that someone is not very intelligent or is slightly mentally unstable
not very intelligent; slightly crazy (idiom)
informal american

Examples

Some days I wonder if my boss is few cards shy of a full deck.
After he tried to start his car with a banana, it was clear he was few cards shy of a full deck.
People say her brother's few cards shy of a full deck, but he's actually just very eccentric.

Synset Examples

He might be a few cards shy of a full deck.
He’s a few cards shy of a full deck sometimes.
She’s a bit few cards shy of a full deck when stressed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: not the sharpest tool in the shed - not very intelligent (idiom)
  • Synonym: crazy - mad, mentally unstable

Etymology

Alludes to the idea of a deck of playing cards being incomplete, metaphorically suggesting something lacking or not quite right; American English origin (mid-20th c.)
