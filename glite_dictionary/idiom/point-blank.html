point-blank

1. ADV

/ pɔɪntˈblæŋk / (AmE), / pɔɪntˈblaŋk / (BrE)
In a direct and straightforward manner, without trying to hide anything or avoid offense.
direct and straightforward manner

Examples

She asked him point-blank whether he had cheated on the exam.
The journalist point-blank demanded to know if the company planned any layoffs.
I refused to lie for him, telling him point-blank that I wouldn't help.

Synset Examples

I asked him point-blank whether he wanted the job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: directly - in a straightforward or immediate way
  • Antonym: indirectly - in a roundabout or not straightforward way

Etymology

Originally referring to a gun fired at close range (from French 'point blanc', literally 'white mark').
