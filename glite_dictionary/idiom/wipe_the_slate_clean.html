to wipe the slate clean

1. VERB

/ waɪp ðə sleɪt klin / (AmE), / waɪp ðə sleɪt kliːn / (BrE)
to start fresh by forgetting past mistakes or problems and making a new beginning
start fresh; forget past mistakes

Examples

After the bankruptcy, the company tried to wipe the slate clean and rebuild its reputation.
They agreed to wipe the slate clean and put their old arguments behind them.
Sometimes, moving to a new city can help you wipe the slate clean and start over.

Synset Examples

After the argument, they decided to wipe the slate clean.
Let's wipe the slate clean and start fresh.
It's time to wipe the slate clean and move on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turn over a new leaf - start behaving in a better or new way
  • Synonym: to let bygones be bygones - forget past disagreements or mistakes

Etymology

Refers to erasable writing slates once used in schools, where cleaning the slate allowed for a new start.
