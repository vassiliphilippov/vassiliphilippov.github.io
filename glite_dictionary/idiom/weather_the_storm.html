to weather the storm

1. VERB

/ ˈwɛð.ɚ ðə stɔrm / (AmE), / ˈwɛð.ə ðə stɔːm / (BrE)
to successfully deal with and survive a difficult or challenging period or situation
endure a difficult situation

Examples

Despite financial troubles, the company managed to weather the storm and remain open.
Families often come together to weather the storm during times of crisis.
She knew it wouldn't be easy, but she was determined to weather the storm until things improved.

Synset Examples

They decided to ride out the storm at home.
We decided to wait out the storm indoors.

They plan to weather the storm together as a family.
Together, they can weather the storm and emerge stronger.
She was determined to weather the storm and keep going.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ride out the storm - to endure a period of trouble or danger without much loss or harm
