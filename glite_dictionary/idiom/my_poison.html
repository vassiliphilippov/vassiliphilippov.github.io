my poison

1. NOUN

/ maɪ ˈpɔɪzən / (AmE), / maɪ ˈpɔɪzən / (BrE)
used to refer to one's preferred indulgence, especially an alcoholic drink, food, or habitual vice
someone's preferred indulgence or vice
informal

Examples

When the waiter asked for my drink order, I replied, 'Whiskey's my poison.'
For some, sweets are harmless, but chocolate is definitely my poison.
After a long week, a cold beer is always my poison.

Synset Examples

When it comes to desserts, chocolate cake is my poison.
He asked me what my poison was, and I said coffee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vice - bad habit or immoral practice
  • Synonym: indulgence - something one allows oneself for pleasure

Etymology

Refers originally to the phrase 'What's your poison?', slang for asking what someone would like to drink, especially alcohol.
