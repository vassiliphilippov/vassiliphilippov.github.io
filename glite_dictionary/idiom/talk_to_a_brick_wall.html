to talk to a brick wall

1. VERB

/ tɔk tə ə brɪk wɔl / (AmE), / tɔːk tə ə brɪk wɔːl / (BrE)
to try to communicate with someone who refuses to listen or respond
try to communicate with an unresponsive person

Examples

No matter how many times I explained the rules, it felt like I was talking to a brick wall.
Whenever I mention chores to my teenagers, I might as well talk to a brick wall.
Trying to change his mind was like talking to a brick wall.

Synset Examples

She felt as if she was talking to a brick wall during the meeting.
Trying to explain it to him was like talking to a brick wall.
No matter how hard I tried, it was like talking to a brick wall.
