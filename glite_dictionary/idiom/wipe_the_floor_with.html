to wipe the floor with

1. VERB

/ waɪp ðə flɔr wɪð / (AmE), / waɪp ðə flɔː wɪð / (BrE)
to defeat someone decisively, especially in a competition, argument, or contest
defeat decisively

Examples

The underdog team wiped the floor with last year's champions in a stunning upset.
She absolutely wiped the floor with her opponent during the chess tournament final.
I didn't expect them to wipe the floor with us so completely in the final match.

Synset Examples

The team wiped the floor with their opponents.
She wiped the floor with him in the debate.
He promised to wipe the floor with his competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to trounce - to defeat decisively
  • Synonym: beat - to win against someone in a contest
  • Antonym: lose to - to be defeated by someone
