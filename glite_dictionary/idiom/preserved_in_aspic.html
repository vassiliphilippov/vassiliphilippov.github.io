preserved in aspic

1. ADJ

/ pɚˈzɝvd ɪn ˈæspɪk / (AmE), / pəˈzɜːvd ɪn ˈæspɪk / (BrE)
(of something) kept unchanged or stagnant, often with a sense of being frozen in time or resistant to change.
unchanged or stagnant; frozen in time

Examples

Many rural customs seem preserved in aspic, hardly altered over the decades.
The company's policies are so old-fashioned, they're practically preserved in aspic.
In her writing, Victorian manners are preserved in aspic for modern readers to observe.

Synset Examples

Her childhood memories are preserved in aspic, untouched.
Those traditions seem preserved in aspic, never changing.
The old stories felt like they were preserved in aspic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unchanged - remaining the same, not altered
  • Synonym: frozen in time - stopped or preserved without change, as if time has paused

Etymology

Refers to the practice of preserving food in aspic (a gelatin made from meat stock), used metaphorically from the late 20th century.
