wrong side of history

1. NOUN

/ rɔŋ saɪd əv ˈhɪstəri / (AmE), / rɒŋ saɪd ɒv ˈhɪstəri / (BrE)
to support or hold views that will be judged as morally or ethically mistaken by future generations
supporting a position seen as morally mistaken in hindsight

Examples

Many politicians now fear being on the wrong side of history as public opinion shifts.
Those who opposed civil rights were ultimately found on the wrong side of history.
He doesn’t want his legacy to be associated with the wrong side of history.

Synset Examples

They’ll realize they’re on the wrong side of history.
Some believe he is on the wrong side of history.
His actions put him on the wrong side of history.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: right side of history - to support a position that will be viewed as morally correct by later generations
