to get a round tuit

1. VERB

/ ˈɡɛt ə raʊnd tʊ.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡɛt ə raʊnd tjuː.ɪt / (BrE)
(humorous) To finally do something that one has been postponing or putting off for a long time.
finally do something long delayed (humorous idiom)
informal

Examples

I swear I'll fix the leaky tap when I get a round tuit.
Maybe this weekend I'll finally get a round tuit and wash my car.
She keeps saying she'll clean her room when she gets a round tuit, but it never happens.

Synset Examples

Once I get a round tuit, I’ll organize my closet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get around to - to finally do something after delaying it

Etymology

A pun on the phrase 'get around to it,' with 'tuit' as an invented object representing motivation to complete procrastinated tasks.
