to go the way of all flesh

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ðə weɪ əv ɔl fleʃ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ðə weɪ əv ɔːl fleʃ / (BrE)
to die; to cease to live, especially as part of the natural course of life
to die; pass away

Examples

After a long, fulfilling life, he finally went the way of all flesh.
Many ancient civilizations have gone the way of all flesh, leaving only ruins behind.
She knew that even her beloved pet would eventually go the way of all flesh.

Synset Examples

He understood that all living things go the way of all flesh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: die - to stop living
  • Synonym: to pass away - to die (euphemism)

Etymology

From biblical usage, meaning all humans (flesh) are mortal and must die.
