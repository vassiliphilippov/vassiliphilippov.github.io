to make one's way

1. VERB

/ meɪk wʌnz weɪ / (AmE), / meɪk wʌnz weɪ / (BrE)
to move forward or progress, especially by overcoming obstacles or difficulties
move forward or progress

Examples

After the meeting, she hurried to make her way to the train station.
They managed to make their way across the river despite the strong current.
It took us hours to make our way through the winding forest paths.

Synset Examples

They decided to make their way through the crowded streets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to proceed - to move forward, especially after stopping
  • Synonym: advance - to move forward, often with effort or purpose
  • Antonym: retreat - to move back or withdraw
