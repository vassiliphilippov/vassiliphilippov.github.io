to get your shorts in a knot

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt jɔɹ ʃɔɹts ɪn ə nɑt / (AmE), / ɡɛt jɔː ʃɔːts ɪn ə nɒt / (BrE)
to become overly upset or anxious, often over something unimportant
become unnecessarily upset or anxious
informal

Examples

Don't get your shorts in a knot over a simple misunderstanding.
He tends to get his shorts in a knot whenever plans change unexpectedly.
You shouldn't get your shorts in a knot about things you can't control.

Synset Examples

No need to get your shorts in a knot about the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get your knickers in a twist - to become needlessly upset or anxious
  • Antonym: to calm down - to become less upset or anxious
