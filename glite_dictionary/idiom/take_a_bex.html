to take a Bex

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə bɛks / (AmE), / teɪk ə bɛks / (BrE)
(Australian) To take a dose of Bex (a brand of analgesic powder), typically for relief from pain or stress; now sometimes used figuratively to suggest someone should relax or calm down.
(Australian) take a dose of Bex medicine
british

Examples

Whenever I have a headache, I take a Bex and rest for a while.
She told him to take a Bex and calm down after his outburst.
Back in the 1960s, many Australians would take a Bex for minor aches and pains.

Synset Examples

He suggested I take a Bex for the pain.
Whenever I feel unwell, I just take a Bex.
After dinner, I need to take a Bex for my headache.

Etymology

From 'Bex', a popular Australian brand of analgesic powder used in the mid-20th century.
