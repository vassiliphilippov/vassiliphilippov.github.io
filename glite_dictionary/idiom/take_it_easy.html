to take it easy

1. VERB

/ teɪk ɪt ˈiːzi / (AmE), / teɪk ɪt ˈiːzi / (BrE)
to relax and avoid making much effort or becoming stressed; to be calm and not worry
relax; avoid stress or effort

Examples

After the surgery, the doctor advised me to take it easy for a few weeks.
Why don’t you take it easy this evening and watch a movie?
When work gets overwhelming, remember to take it easy and breathe.

Synset Examples

After a long week, it's time to take easy.
He told me to take it easy and not stress out.
After the stressful week, I decided to take it easy.
You should take it easy this weekend and relax.
He decided to take it easy and skip the gym today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to relax - to become less tense or anxious; to rest
  • Synonym: to unwind - to relax after stress or tension
  • Antonym: stress - a state of mental or emotional strain or tension
