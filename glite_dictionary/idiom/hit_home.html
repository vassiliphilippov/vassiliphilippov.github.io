to hit home

1. VERB

/ hɪt hoʊm / (AmE), / hɪt həʊm / (BrE)
(of a comment, event, or situation) to have a strong emotional impact or to be fully understood, often causing someone to realize the significance or seriousness of something.
have a strong emotional impact; become fully understood

Examples

Her criticism about my work ethic really hit home and made me reflect on my habits.
The reality of the situation hit home when I saw the empty apartment.
Hearing about the accident hit home for the entire community.

Synset Examples

His words hit home, reminding me of my own experiences.
When she shared her story, it hit home for everyone.
The documentary really hit home for many viewers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to strike home - to have the intended effect or impact; to be fully understood
