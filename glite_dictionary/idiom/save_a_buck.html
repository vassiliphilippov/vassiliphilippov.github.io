to save a buck

1. VERB

/ seɪv ə bʌk / (AmE), / seɪv ə bʌk / (BrE)
to spend less money than usual on something; to economize
to spend less; to economize
informal american

Examples

He always tries to save a buck by using coupons at the grocery store.
They decided to renovate the kitchen themselves to save a buck.
If you want to save a buck on transportation, consider taking the bus instead of a taxi.

Synset Examples

She saved a buck by shopping during the sale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to save money - to avoid spending money, to economize
