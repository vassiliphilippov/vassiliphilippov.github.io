to make one's mark

1. VERB

/ meɪk wʌnz mɑrk / (AmE), / meɪk wʌnz mɑːk / (BrE)
to achieve recognition, success, or a notable reputation in a particular field or area
achieve recognition or success

Examples

She worked hard for years before she finally made her mark in the art world.
The young scientist made his mark with a groundbreaking new theory.
Many athletes dream of making their mark in the Olympics.

Synset Examples

He finally made his mark as a bestselling author.
Even as a bit player, he made his mark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stand out - to be easily noticed or recognized as different
  • Synonym: leave a lasting impression - to have a strong effect or influence that is remembered
