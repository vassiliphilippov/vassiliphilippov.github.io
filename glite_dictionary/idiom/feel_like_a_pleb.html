to feel like a pleb

1. VERB

/ ˈfil laɪk ə plɛb / (AmE), / ˈfiːl laɪk ə plɛb / (BrE)
to experience a sense of being common, ordinary, or inferior compared to others
to feel ordinary or inferior
informal

Examples

Whenever I wear cheap clothes to the party, I feel like a pleb.
Watching those luxury cars, she suddenly felt like a pleb walking down the street.
After hearing them talk about their vacations, he couldn't help but feel like a pleb in comparison.

Synset Examples

In that group, he felt like a complete pleb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pleb - an ordinary person, especially one regarded as lower-class

Etymology

From 'pleb', informal abbreviation of 'plebeian', originally from Latin 'plebeius' (of the common people).
