to put on the line

1. VERB

/ pʊt ɑn ðə laɪn / (AmE), / pʊt ɒn ðə laɪn / (BrE)
to expose someone or something to risk, danger, or possible loss
expose to risk or danger

Examples

He was willing to put on the line everything he had for his family.
She put her reputation on the line when she spoke out against the policy.
The company put millions on the line to develop the new product.

Synset Examples

We risked losing a lot of money in this venture
Why risk your life?
She laid her job on the line when she told the boss that he was wrong

Related Terms

  • Synonym: risk - to expose to the possibility of loss or injury
  • Antonym: to protect - to keep safe from harm or injury

2. VERB

/ pʊt ɑn ðə laɪn / (AmE), / pʊt ɒn ðə laɪn / (BrE)
to risk something important, such as money, reputation, or safety
to risk something important

Examples

She decided to put on the line her career to stand up for what she believed in.
They put on the line their savings to start the new business.
Every firefighter puts on the line their life when responding to an emergency.

Synset Examples

He put everything on the line for this opportunity.
