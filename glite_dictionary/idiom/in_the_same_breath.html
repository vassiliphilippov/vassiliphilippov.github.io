in the same breath

1. ADV

/ ɪn ðə seɪm brɛθ / (AmE), / ɪn ðə seɪm brɛθ / (BrE)
used to indicate that two things are said or done almost at the same moment, often showing contradiction or surprise that both occur together.
almost simultaneously; unexpectedly together

Examples

He apologized and made another complaint in the same breath.
She admired his intelligence but doubted his honesty in the same breath.
The manager promised improvements and warned about possible layoffs in the same breath.

Synset Examples

she praised and criticized him in the same breath

2. ADV

/ ɪn ðə seɪm brɛθ / (AmE), / ɪn ðə seɪm brɛθ / (BrE)
used to indicate that two things are said or done at almost exactly the same time, often with a sense of contradiction or surprise.
simultaneously; almost at the same time (idiomatic)

Examples

He managed to encourage her and criticize her in the same breath.
The politician denied the accusation and in the same breath admitted his mistake.
You can’t say you’re too busy and in the same breath offer to help.

Synset Examples

She praised his work and, in the same breath, offered constructive criticism.
They celebrated their success and, in the same breath, acknowledged the challenges ahead.
He can complain and apologize in the same breath.
