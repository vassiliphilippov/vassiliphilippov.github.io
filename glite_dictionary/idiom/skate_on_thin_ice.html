to skate on thin ice

1. VERB

/ skeɪt ɑn θɪn aɪs / (AmE), / skeɪt ɒn θɪn aɪs / (BrE)
to take a risk or behave in a way that could cause trouble or danger
take a risky or dangerous action

Examples

After breaking the rules twice, he's really skating on thin ice with his boss.
You know you're skating on thin ice if you keep ignoring the warnings.
She realized she was skating on thin ice by arriving late every day.

Synset Examples

Don't skate on thin ice; follow the rules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to play with fire - to take a dangerous risk
  • Synonym: to walk a tightrope - to be in a risky or precarious situation
