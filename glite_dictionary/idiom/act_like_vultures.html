to act like vultures

1. VERB

/ ækt laɪk ˈvʌltʃɚz / (AmE), / ækt laɪk ˈvʌltʃəz / (BrE)
to take advantage of someone else's difficulties or misfortune for one's own benefit, especially in a predatory or opportunistic way
exploit others' misfortune for gain

Examples

As soon as the company announced layoffs, a few recruiters began to act like vultures around the building.
When the housing bubble burst, some buyers started to act like vultures, looking for cheap properties.
After the landlord died, his distant relatives acted like vultures, fighting over his estate.

Synset Examples

The investors acted like vultures, circling the failing company.
They swooped in like vultures after the business went bankrupt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exploit - make full use of and derive benefit from (a resource or situation)
  • Synonym: prey on - to take advantage of or harm someone weaker

Etymology

Metaphorical use, comparing opportunistic people to vultures that feed on the weak or dead.
