to earn a seat at the table

1. VERB

/ ɝn ə sit æt ðə ˈteɪbəl / (AmE), / ɜːn ə siːt æt ðə ˈteɪbəl / (BrE)
to gain a rightful or respected position within a group or organization, typically through effort or merit
gain a respected position in a group

Examples

After years of dedication, she finally earned a seat at the table in the executive team.
Many young professionals work hard to earn a seat at the table in their industry.
He felt proud to have earned a seat at the table among the decision makers.

Synset Examples

She finally earned her seat at the table after years of hard work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get a foot in the door - to achieve initial access or entry into a group or organization
