to rule the roost

1. VERB

/ ruːl ðə ruːst / (AmE), / ruːl ðə ruːst / (BrE)
to be in control; to be the person who makes all the important decisions in a group or situation
to be in control or dominant

Examples

Ever since he got promoted, he likes to rule the roost in every meeting.
Jenny may be young, but she sure knows how to rule the roost at home.
It's clear that management wants to rule the roost without input from staff.

Synset Examples

The oldest sibling tends to rule the roost during family gatherings.
She definitely knows how to rule the roost at work.
In that household, he rules the roost.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be in charge - to have control over others
  • Synonym: to dominate - to have a commanding influence over; to control

Etymology

Likely from the notion of a dominant rooster ruling over hens in a chicken roost.
