are you deaf

1. X

/ ɑr ju dɛf / (AmE), / ɑː juː dɛf / (BrE)
used rhetorically to indicate that someone is not listening or paying attention, rather than literally questioning their hearing ability
rhetorical question about not paying attention
informal offensive

Examples

She waved her hand in front of his face and said, 'Are you deaf?' when he ignored her instructions.
After repeating himself several times, he finally snapped, 'Are you deaf or something?'
It's rude to shout 'are you deaf' at someone just because they didn't hear you the first time.

Synset Examples

When he ignored her, she shouted, 'Are you deaf?'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: are you blind - rhetorical question implying someone is ignoring something obvious
