trouble at mill

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌb.əl ət mɪl / (AmE), / ˈtrʌb.əl ət mɪl / (BrE)
Used to indicate problems or difficulties arising in a situation, especially involving conflict or crisis, often with a hint of impending disruption.
problems or conflict arising in a situation
british

Examples

After the manager announced layoffs, there was obvious trouble at mill among the staff.
The supervisor warned that if demands were not met, there would soon be trouble at mill.
When the rumor spread about new policies, people sensed trouble at mill in the department.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trouble brewing - problems or conflict developing
  • Synonym: all hell breaks loose - sudden outbreak of chaos or trouble

Etymology

Originally British, from northern English dialect, referencing labor unrest in textile mills; popularized by comedic sketches in the early 20th century.
