to hit the books

1. VERB

/ hɪt ðə bʊks / (AmE), / hɪt ðə bʊks / (BrE)
to begin studying hard, especially by reading textbooks or notes
start studying intensely

Examples

I have a big exam tomorrow, so I need to hit the books tonight.
She decided to hit the books after realizing she was behind in her studies.
After his vacation, Mark immediately hit the books to catch up on his classes.

Synset Examples

He is studying geology in his room
I have an exam next week: I must hit the books now

Related Terms

  • Synonym: study - to apply oneself to acquiring knowledge, especially from books
  • Synonym: to cram - to study intensively over a short period of time

2. VERB

/ hɪt ðə bʊks / (AmE), / hɪt ðə bʊks / (BrE)
to begin studying intensely, especially for an exam or test
study intensely

Examples

I have a big test tomorrow, so I really need to hit the books tonight.
After a long summer, it was time to hit the books again when school started.
She prefers to hit the books early in the morning when it’s quiet.

Synset Examples

If I want to pass, I must hit the books now.
She decided to hit the books all weekend.
I need to hit the books before the exam.
