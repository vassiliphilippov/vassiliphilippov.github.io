call to arms

1. NOUN

/ kɔl tə ɑrmz / (AmE), / kɔːl tʊ ɑːmz / (BrE)
a strong appeal or demand for people to take action, especially in support of a cause or in preparation for conflict
strong appeal to take action or mobilize

Examples

The politician's speech was a call to arms for voters to demand change.
After the disaster, the charity issued a call to arms for donations and support.
His article acted as a call to arms for people to join the environmental movement.

Synset Examples

Her speech served as a call to arms for the community.
The organization made a call to arms for volunteers.
The president issued a call to arms for national unity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rallying cry - a phrase or slogan used to encourage people to unite for a cause

Etymology

Originally referred to a summons to take up weapons for military service.
