to make like a baby

1. VERB

/ meɪk laɪk ə ˈbeɪbi / (AmE), / meɪk laɪk ə ˈbeɪbi / (BrE)
to leave or depart quickly, often used humorously or playfully
leave or depart quickly (humorous idiom)
informal

Examples

It's getting late; let's make like a baby and head out of here.
After the meeting dragged on, he decided to make like a baby and leave early.
As soon as the movie ended, the crowd started to make like a baby and head for the exits.

Synset Examples

Let’s make like a baby and head out for ice cream.
It's time to make like a baby and head out!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave - to go away from a place or person
  • Synonym: to depart - to go away; to leave

2. VERB

/ meɪk laɪk ə ˈbeɪ.bi / (AmE), / meɪk laɪk ə ˈbeɪ.bi / (BrE)
(informal, humorous) To leave quickly or without notice, typically used in the phrase 'make like a baby and head out.'
leave quickly; depart suddenly (humorous idiom)
informal slang

Examples

As soon as the clock struck five, she made like a baby and headed for the door.
Not wanting to get caught, Tom made like a baby and slipped out unnoticed.
The guests made like a baby when the party ended without saying goodbye.

Synset Examples

After the meeting, he made like a baby and headed out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave - to go away from a place or person
  • Synonym: to depart - to go away; to leave a place, especially to start a journey
