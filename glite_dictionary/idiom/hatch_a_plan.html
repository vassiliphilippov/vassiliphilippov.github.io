to hatch a plan

1. VERB

/ hætʃ ə plæn / (AmE), / hætʃ ə plæn / (BrE)
to devise or develop a plan, usually in a secret or careful way
devise a plan, often secretly

Examples

The committee decided to hatch a plan to address the growing parking problem in the city.
He spent all night hatching a plan to surprise his friend for her birthday.
Detectives believed the thieves hatched a plan months before the daring robbery.

Synset Examples

We need to hatch a plan before the deadline.
The team will hatch a strategy to improve sales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devise a plan - to invent or formulate a plan
  • Synonym: scheme - a secret or devious plan

Etymology

The verb 'hatch' in this sense stems from the idea of bringing something forth, as when an egg hatches.
