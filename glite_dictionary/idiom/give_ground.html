to give ground

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡɪv ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
to yield, concede, or make a compromise, especially during a debate or negotiation
to concede or compromise

Examples

Despite pressure from the committee, the manager refused to give ground on her decision.
The union eventually had to give ground on some demands to reach an agreement.
When both parties give ground, compromise becomes possible.

Synset Examples

In negotiations, it's important to give ground occasionally.
After much debate, she decided to give ground to her colleagues.
He refused to give ground on his principles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to concede - to admit or yield something, especially unwillingly
  • Antonym: to hold firm - to refuse to yield or compromise
