to do you wrong

1. VERB

/ duː juː rɔŋ / (AmE), / duː juː rɒŋ / (BrE)
to treat someone unfairly or betray their trust
betray or harm someone

Examples

I swear I would never do you wrong—I value our friendship too much.
He felt hurt and angry that she would do him wrong after all they had been through together.
She apologized sincerely, insisting it was never her intention to do you wrong.

Synset Examples

I would never do you wrong; you're my best friend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to betray - to hurt someone by not being loyal
  • Antonym: do you right - to treat you well or fairly
