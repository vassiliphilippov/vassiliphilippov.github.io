to nourish a viper in one's bosom

1. VERB

/ ˈnɜrɪʃ ə ˈvaɪpər ɪn wʌnz ˈboʊsəm / (AmE), / ˈnʌrɪʃ ə ˈvaɪpə ɪn wʌnz ˈbəʊsəm / (BrE)
to support or trust someone who turns out to be dangerous, treacherous, or ungrateful
support someone harmful or treacherous

Examples

She later regretted that she had nourished a viper in her bosom, as he betrayed her trust.
The board felt they had nourished a viper in their bosom by promoting him to manager.
Trusting Mark turned out to be like nourishing a viper in one's bosom; he eventually undermined the whole project.

Synset Examples

You can't nourish a viper in one's bosom and expect loyalty.
She realized too late that she had nourished a viper in her bosom.
Trusting him was like nourishing a viper in one's bosom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bite the hand that feeds you - to act against or betray someone who has helped you

Etymology

From the ancient fable of a person who cares for a viper, only to be bitten. The imagery originates in classical literature and Aesop's fables.
