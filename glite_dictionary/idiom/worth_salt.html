worth salt

1. ADJ

/ wɝθ sɔlt / (AmE), / wɜːθ sɒlt / (BrE)
having value or deserving respect; worthwhile or competent
valuable or competent; deserving respect

Examples

No doctor worth salt would ignore a patient's symptoms.
His advice isn't worth salt after lying so many times.
A politician worth salt must listen to the needs of the people.

Synset Examples

Her complaints are not worth salt at this point.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worthy - having worth or value; deserving
  • Antonym: worthless - having no value or use

Etymology

Refers to the historical value of salt, which was once highly prized and often used as currency.
