bang for the buck

1. NOUN

/ bæŋ fəɹ ðə bʌk / (AmE), / bæŋ fə ðə bʌk / (BrE)
a good value for the money spent; a favorable return on investment
good value for money
informal american

Examples

I bought a used car and got great bang for the buck compared to buying new.
If you want the most bang for the buck, this computer is the best choice.
Traveling off-season gives you more bang for the buck than at other times.

Synset Examples

You really get bang for the buck with this deal.
This new phone is the best bang for the buck.
Finding a good restaurant here is great bang for the buck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: value for money - the worth received compared to cost

Etymology

Originally American English, deriving from the idea of getting more effect ('bang') for the money ('buck'), popularized in the mid-20th century.
