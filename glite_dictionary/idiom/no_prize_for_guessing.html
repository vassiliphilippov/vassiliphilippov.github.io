no prize for guessing

1. X

/ noʊ praɪz fər ˈɡɛsɪŋ / (AmE), / nəʊ praɪz fə ˈɡɛsɪŋ / (BrE)
used to indicate that something is very obvious or easily predicted
something obvious or predictable

Examples

With her skills, there's no prize for guessing who will get the promotion.
The store was empty—no prize for guessing why, after all those bad reviews.
He looks exhausted; no prize for guessing that he's been working late.

Synset Examples

When the lights dimmed, there was no prize for guessing who would win.
He wore a red shirt; no prize for guessing his favorite team.
With her talent, there’s no prize for guessing she’ll win.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obvious - easily perceived or understood; clear, self-evident
  • Synonym: predictable - able to be foretold or anticipated
