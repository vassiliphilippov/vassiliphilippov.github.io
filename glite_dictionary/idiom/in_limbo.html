in limbo

1. ADV

/ ɪn ˈlɪmˌboʊ / (AmE), / ɪn ˈlɪm.bəʊ / (BrE)
in an uncertain or undecided state, often awaiting a decision or outcome
in an uncertain or undecided state

Examples

After graduation, many students feel in limbo as they look for their first job.
The negotiations have stalled, leaving the agreement in limbo.
His visa status is in limbo until the government processes his application.

Synset Examples

The project is still in limbo due to budget cuts.
Her application is in limbo until the committee meets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uncertain - not able to be relied on; not known or definite
  • Antonym: resolved - firmly determined or having a clear outcome

Etymology

From Latin 'limbus' meaning 'edge' or 'border'; originally referred in theological use to a place on the border of hell.
