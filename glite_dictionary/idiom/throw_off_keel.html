to throw off keel

1. VERB

/ θroʊ ɔf kil / (AmE), / θrəʊ ɒf kiːl / (BrE)
to cause someone to become unbalanced, disoriented, or unsettled, especially emotionally or mentally
make someone feel unsettled or unbalanced

Examples

The abrupt cancellation of the project threw everyone off keel and led to confusion.
Her harsh criticism threw him off keel during his presentation.
The sudden snowstorm threw the travel plans off keel for the entire team.

Synset Examples

The unexpected news threw her off keel during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unsettle - to make someone feel anxious or disturbed
  • Antonym: to stabilize - to make or become steady and balanced again

Etymology

Originally nautical, where a vessel is said to be 'off keel' when not upright or steady.
