to put in a box

1. VERB

/ pʊt ɪn ə bɑks / (AmE), / pʊt ɪn ə bɒks / (BrE)
to categorize or limit someone, especially in a way that restricts their identity or abilities
restrict or categorize someone

Examples

People often try to put artists in a box, but creativity can’t be contained.
She resented when colleagues would put her in a box based on her background.
Don't put yourself in a box—you are capable of so many things.

Synset Examples

Don’t put me in a box; I’m more than my job.
She felt they were trying to put her in a box.
He refused to let anyone put him in a box.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stereotype - to view or represent someone as a stereotype
  • Antonym: free-spirited - not easily restricted or categorized
