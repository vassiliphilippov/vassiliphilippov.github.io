to build a fiefdom

1. VERB

/ bɪld ə ˈfiːf.dəm / (AmE), / bɪld ə ˈfiːf.dəm / (BrE)
to establish or create a personal area of power or control, often within an organization, independent from others' influence
establish personal domain of control

Examples

The manager spent years trying to build a fiefdom within the company.
Ruth used her influence to build a fiefdom that operated independently from upper management.
It's risky to let one department head build a fiefdom unchecked.

Synset Examples

She built her fiefdom within the organization, gaining loyal followers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to carve out a niche - to create a specialized but influential position for oneself
  • Synonym: consolidate power - to strengthen and secure one's control or influence

Etymology

From 'fiefdom,' meaning an area controlled by a feudal lord, metaphorically used for personal domains of power.
