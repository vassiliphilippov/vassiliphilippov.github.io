fruit of the poisonous tree

1. NOUN

/ fruːt əv ðə ˈpɔɪzənəs triː / (AmE), / fruːt əv ðə ˈpɔɪzənəs triː / (BrE)
evidence that has been obtained through illegal or improper means and is therefore inadmissible in court
inadmissible evidence obtained illegally
legal american

Examples

The judge ruled that the photographs were fruit of the poisonous tree and could not be used in the trial.
Any witness statement obtained after an unlawful search may be considered fruit of the poisonous tree.
The defense attorney argued that all subsequent evidence was fruit of the poisonous tree due to the illegal wiretap.

Synset Examples

The evidence was dismissed as fruit of the poisonous tree.
The confession was considered fruit of the poisonous tree.
Any evidence obtained illegally is fruit of the poisonous tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inadmissible evidence - evidence not allowed in court

Etymology

Legal metaphor originating in the United States, first used in the early 20th century.
