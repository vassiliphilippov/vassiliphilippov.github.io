off with someone's head

1. X

/ ɑf wɪð sʌmwʌnz hɛd / (AmE), / ɒf wɪð sʌmwʌnz hɛd / (BrE)
a phrase ordering that someone be executed by beheading, especially as a form of punishment in historical or literary contexts
order for execution by beheading

Examples

In the classic story, the queen shouted, 'Off with his head!' when she was angered.
During the play, the king declared 'off with her head' as punishment for treason.
The phrase 'off with someone's head' is often used to evoke harsh royal justice in fiction.

Synset Examples

In medieval tales, treason often ended with 'off with someone's head'.
