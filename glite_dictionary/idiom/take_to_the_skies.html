to take to the skies

1. VERB

/ teɪk tu ðə skaɪz / (AmE), / teɪk tʊ ðə skaɪz / (BrE)
to begin flying or to become airborne, especially of aircraft or birds
to begin flying; become airborne

Examples

Thousands of geese take to the skies each autumn during migration.
After months of testing, the new aircraft will finally take to the skies next week.
As soon as the weather clears, helicopters can take to the skies for rescue operations.

Synset Examples

As the sun set, the planes took to the skies.
After weeks of preparation, they finally took to the skies.
The birds took to the skies at dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take off - to leave the ground and begin to fly
  • Antonym: land - to return to the ground after flying
