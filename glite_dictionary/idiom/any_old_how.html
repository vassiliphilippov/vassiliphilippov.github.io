any old how

1. ADV

/ ˈɛni oʊld haʊ / (AmE), / ˈeni əʊld haʊ / (BrE)
in a careless or disorganized manner; without care or particular method
in a careless or haphazard manner

Examples

Don't just stack the books any old how; organize them by category.
She threw her clothes into the suitcase any old how before rushing to catch her train.
If you answer the questions any old how, you'll lose marks for careless mistakes.

Synset Examples

She did her homework any old how, without much thought.
Don't approach the task any old how; be careful.
He finished the project any old how, just to get it done.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haphazardly - in a random or careless way
  • Antonym: methodically - in an orderly or systematic manner
