to take the count

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə kaʊnt / (AmE), / teɪk ðə kaʊnt / (BrE)
(of a boxer) to remain down on the floor during the referee's ten count, resulting in a knockout defeat.
be counted out in boxing

Examples

The champion was knocked to the mat and had to take the count in the final round.
After a powerful uppercut, the boxer decided to take the count rather than get up.
He fought bravely, but in the end he took the count and lost the match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be counted out - to be declared defeated after a ten count in boxing

2. VERB

/ teɪk ðə kaʊnt / (AmE), / teɪk ðə kaʊnt / (BrE)
to count votes, ballots, or items; to calculate or tally a total
to count or tally votes or items

Examples

After the speeches ended, they began to take the count to determine the winner.
Election officials were instructed to take the count with extra care this year.
Can you take the count of the ballots before we close the polls?

Synset Examples

It’s important to take the count accurately in elections.
Please take the count before announcing the results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: count - to say numbers in order or find the total number of things
