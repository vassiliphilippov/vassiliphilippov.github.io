mark of Cain

1. NOUN

/ mɑrk əv keɪn / (AmE), / mɑːk əv keɪn / (BrE)
A symbol or sign of guilt, shame, or infamy, often marking someone as an outcast.
symbol of guilt or shame; infamy

Examples

After the scandal, he carried the mark of Cain in the community.
She feared her past would always be a mark of Cain she couldn't escape.
The incident left him with a mark of Cain in the eyes of his peers.

Synset Examples

He felt like he bore the mark of Cain after his mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stigma - a mark of disgrace associated with a particular circumstance or person
  • Antonym: badge of honor - a mark indicating honor or achievement, opposite in implication to 'mark of Cain'

Etymology

From the biblical story in Genesis, where God places a mark on Cain after he kills Abel.
