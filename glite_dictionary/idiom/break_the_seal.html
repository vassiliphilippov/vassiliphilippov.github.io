to break the seal

1. VERB

/ breɪk ðə siːl / (AmE), / breɪk ðə siːl / (BrE)
to open something that has been sealed, especially for the first time, or to use something for the first time
open something sealed for the first time

Examples

She carefully broke the seal on the jar to avoid making a mess.
You'll need to break the seal before using the new bottle of medicine.
Once you break the seal on the package, it can't be returned.

Synset Examples

Make sure to break the seal carefully to avoid spills.
He hesitated to break the seal on the package.
Once you break the seal, the warranty is voided.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: open - to move or remove something so that it is no longer closed or covered
