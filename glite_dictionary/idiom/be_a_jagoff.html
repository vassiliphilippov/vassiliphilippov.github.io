to be a jagoff

1. VERB

/ bi ə ˈdʒæɡˌɑf / (AmE), / biː ə ˈdʒæɡɒf / (BrE)
to act in a rude or contemptible manner, especially in a way that annoys others (slang, chiefly Pittsburgh)
act rudely or contemptibly
slang american

Examples

If you keep interrupting people like that, you're really being a jagoff.
Nobody wants to work with someone who is a jagoff all the time.
She's not being a jagoff, she's just having a tough day.

Synset Examples

Don't be a jagoff; help your friends when they need it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be a jerk - to behave in an unpleasant or annoying way
  • Antonym: be considerate - to act in a thoughtful or respectful manner

Etymology

Pittsburgh regional slang; 'jagoff' is believed to derive from 'jag', a term for annoyance, with the suffix '-off' as a mild pejorative.
