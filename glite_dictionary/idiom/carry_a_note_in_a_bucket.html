to carry a note in a bucket

1. VERB

/ ˈkæri ə ˈnoʊt ɪn ə ˈbʌkət / (AmE), / ˈkæri ə ˈnəʊt ɪn ə ˈbʌkɪt / (BrE)
to be completely unable to sing in tune or hold a melody
be unable to sing in tune

Examples

Despite his enthusiasm, Jake really can't carry a note in a bucket when he sings.
She was so cheerful at parties, even though she couldn't carry a note in a bucket.
Even with voice lessons, he admitted he would never carry a note in a bucket.

Synset Examples

He sings off-key; he couldn't carry a note in a bucket.
She loves karaoke, but she couldn't carry a note in a bucket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sing off-key - to sing out of tune; not matching the intended pitch
