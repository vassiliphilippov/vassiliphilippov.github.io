to rap on the knuckles

1. VERB

/ ræp ɑn ðə ˈnʌkəlz / (AmE), / ræp ɒn ðə ˈnʌkəlz / (BrE)
to criticize or punish someone mildly for a mistake or minor offense
mild reprimand or punishment

Examples

The manager gave him a rap on the knuckles for being late to the meeting.
Journalists received a rap on the knuckles after publishing inaccurate details in the article.
She was gently rapped on the knuckles by her supervisor for missing an important deadline.

Synset Examples

He didn’t mean to rap her knuckles, but it was necessary.
The teacher had to rap his knuckles for talking back.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scold - to criticize someone angrily for doing something wrong
  • Synonym: rebuke - to criticize strongly or reprimand
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration of someone or something
