to make a comeback

1. VERB

/ ˈmeɪk ə ˈkʌmbæk / (AmE), / ˈmeɪk ə ˈkʌmbæk / (BrE)
to become popular or successful again after a period of being less popular or absent
to return to popularity or success

Examples

After years out of the spotlight, the singer is making a comeback with a new album.
Vinyl records are making a comeback among music enthusiasts.
Classic board games are making a comeback in many households.

Synset Examples

The sidebang trend is making a comeback this season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bounce back - to recover quickly after a setback
  • Antonym: fall out of favor - to become unpopular or unfashionable
