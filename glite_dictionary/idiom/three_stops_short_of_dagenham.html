three stops short of Dagenham

1. X

/ θri stɑps ʃɔrt əv ˈdeɪgənəm / (AmE), / θri stɒps ʃɔːt əv ˈdæɡənəm / (BrE)
(British, informal) Lacking common sense or awareness; not fully sensible or developed in thought or action.
not fully sensible; lacking sense
informal british

Examples

Some say the new manager is three stops short of Dagenham with his latest changes.
That theory is three stops short of Dagenham if you ask me.
People who believe those rumors are three stops short of Dagenham.

Synset Examples

His ideas are three stops short of Dagenham.
She's three stops short of Dagenham with those ridiculous plans.

Etymology

A humorous British idiom referencing the London Underground, where Dagenham is three stops before Barking; 'barking' is UK slang for 'mad'.
