the natives are restless

1. X

/ ðə ˈneɪ.t̬ɪvz ɑr ˈrɛs.tl̩s / (AmE), / ðə ˈneɪ.tɪvz ɑː ˈrɛs.tləs / (BrE)
used to describe a situation in which people are becoming agitated, impatient, or dissatisfied, especially when unrest or discontent is growing within a group
people are becoming agitated or restless

Examples

As the meeting dragged on without answers, the natives are restless became the unspoken mood in the room.
After weeks without an update from management, the natives are restless and demanding action.
With prices rising and no sign of relief, it's no wonder the natives are restless in the neighborhood.

Synset Examples

The lack of communication has made the natives restless in our community.
When deadlines are missed, you can be sure the natives are restless.
With the recent changes, it's clear the natives are restless.

Etymology

Originated as a stereotypical phrase in early 20th-century films and literature, referencing colonial attitudes; now used metaphorically.
