to have one's head read

1. VERB

/ hæv wʌnz hɛd rɛd / (AmE), / hæv wʌnz hɛd rɛd / (BrE)
to be considered as acting irrationally or strangely, often used to suggest that one may need to have their sanity or judgment examined
behave irrationally; need sanity checked
informal

Examples

You must be mad to quit your job now; you should have your head read.
After spending all that money, he began to wonder if he needed to have his head read.
When she agreed to the dare, her friends joked she ought to have her head read.

Synset Examples

She thought he should have his head read for taking that risk.
Sometimes, I think I need to have my head read for my choices.
He joked that he needed to have his head read after that decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crazy - mentally deranged, acting irrationally
  • Antonym: sane - in a healthy state of mind
