to close ranks

1. VERB

/ kloʊz ræŋks / (AmE), / kləʊz ræŋks / (BrE)
to unite or come together in order to support each other, especially during times of difficulty or challenge
unite for support or a common purpose

Examples

In the face of criticism, the organization decided to close ranks and present a united front.
After the scandal, party members were urged to close ranks to protect the party's reputation.
When team morale was low, the coach encouraged everyone to close ranks and work together.

Synset Examples

The community rallied to close ranks against the proposed changes.
In tough times, it's important to close ranks and support each other.
The team decided to close ranks after the sudden layoffs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stand together - to support each other as a group
  • Antonym: to fall apart - to become disorganized or lose unity

Etymology

From a military command meaning to move soldiers into a tighter formation for defense.
