to lock up and toss the key

1. VERB

/ lɑk ʌp ənd tɔs ðə ki / (AmE), / lɒk ʌp ənd tɒs ðə kiː / (BrE)
to imprison someone or something permanently and without hope of release, often used figuratively or humorously.
permanently imprison (often humorously)

Examples

If he keeps causing trouble, they’ll have to lock him up and toss the key.
Some people think corrupt politicians should be locked up and tossed the key.
She joked that her rowdy teenagers should be locked up and the key tossed until they calm down.

Synset Examples

She joked that her brother should be locked up and the key tossed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to throw away the key - to imprison someone forever, never to release them
