play stupid games, win stupid prizes

1. X

/ pleɪ ˈstuːpɪd ɡeɪmz, wɪn ˈstuːpɪd ˈpraɪzɪz / (AmE), / pleɪ ˈstjuːpɪd ɡeɪmz, wɪn ˈstjuːpɪd ˈpraɪzɪz / (BrE)
used to express the idea that taking foolish risks or making poor decisions will likely result in negative or undesirable consequences.
foolish actions lead to bad outcomes
informal

Examples

He ignored all the warnings and crashed his bike—play stupid games, win stupid prizes.
If you taunt the dog, don’t be surprised if you get bitten; play stupid games, win stupid prizes.
You keep cheating on your exams, and now you've been suspended. Well, play stupid games, win stupid prizes.

Synset Examples

If you keep taking risks, remember: play stupid games, win stupid prizes.
