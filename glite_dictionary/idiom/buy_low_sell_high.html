to buy low, sell high

1. VERB

/ baɪ loʊ, sɛl haɪ / (AmE), / baɪ ləʊ, sɛl haɪ / (BrE)
to purchase assets when their prices are low and sell them when prices are high, with the aim of making a profit
investing strategy: buy cheaply and sell at a higher price

Examples

Successful investors often follow the buy low, sell high principle to grow their wealth.
Although buy low, sell high sounds simple in theory, it can be difficult to execute in practice.
She explained her investment approach: always try to buy low, sell high for better returns.

Synset Examples

Investors aim to buy low, sell high for profits.
It's essential to buy low, sell high in stocks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: buy high, sell low - the opposite investment practice—buying at high prices and selling at low prices
