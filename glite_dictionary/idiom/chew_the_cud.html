to chew the cud

1. VERB

/ tʃu ðə kʌd / (AmE), / tʃuː ðə kʌd / (BrE)
to spend time in relaxed conversation or reminiscing about the past
engage in relaxed conversation or reminisce

Examples

After dinner, the neighbors gathered on the porch to chew the cud and watch the sunset.
We love to chew the cud over old family photos whenever we get together.
She met her friend at the café to chew the cud and catch up on life.

Synset Examples

It’s nice to chew the cud over a cup of coffee.
He enjoys chewing the cud with friends after work.
They sat by the fire, chewing the cud about old times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chat - to talk in a friendly and informal way
  • Synonym: converse - to engage in conversation

Etymology

Originally refers to ruminant animals chewing partly digested food, later used figuratively for thoughtful or leisurely conversation.
