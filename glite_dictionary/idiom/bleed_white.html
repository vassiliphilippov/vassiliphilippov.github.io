to bleed white

1. VERB

/ bliːd waɪt / (AmE), / bliːd waɪt / (BrE)
to become extremely exhausted or drained of energy, resources, or vitality
become extremely exhausted or drained

Examples

If we keep working overtime like this, we'll all bleed white before the project ends.
The endless demands of the job made her feel she would eventually bleed white.
After organizing the festival by herself, she was completely bled white.
