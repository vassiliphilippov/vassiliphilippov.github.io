to throw the bull around

1. VERB

/ θɹoʊ ðə bʊl əˈɹaʊnd / (AmE), / θrəʊ ðə bʊl əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to engage in informal and often exaggerated conversation or storytelling, especially in a relaxed social setting
chat informally, tell exaggerated stories
informal slang

Examples

On Friday nights, they love to throw the bull around after work.
We sat by the fire, just throwing the bull around and reminiscing about old times.
Whenever the family gathers, there's always someone ready to throw the bull around and make everyone laugh.

Synset Examples

He enjoys throwing the bull around with friends during poker night.
They were just throwing the bull around at the bar.
We spent the afternoon throwing the bull around by the lake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shoot the breeze - to chat casually or informally
  • Synonym: bullshit - to talk nonsense or lies, often exaggerated stories

Etymology

Extension of 'bull' as slang for nonsense or exaggerated talk, originating from 'bullshit'.
