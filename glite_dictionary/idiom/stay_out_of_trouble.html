to stay out of trouble

1. VERB

/ steɪ aʊt əv ˈtrʌbəl / (AmE), / steɪ aʊt ɒv ˈtrʌbəl / (BrE)
to avoid getting involved in situations that could cause problems or difficulties, especially with authority or the law
avoid problematic situations or trouble

Examples

After his release from prison, he promised his mother he would stay out of trouble this time.
Teenagers are often told to stay out of trouble and focus on their studies.
If you want to keep your job, it's best to stay out of trouble at work.

Synset Examples

If you want to stay out of trouble, keep your nose clean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keep your nose clean - to behave well and avoid trouble
