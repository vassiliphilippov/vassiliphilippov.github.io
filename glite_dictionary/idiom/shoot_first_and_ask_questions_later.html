to shoot first and ask questions later

1. VERB

/ ʃuːt fɝst ənd æsk ˈkwɛs.tʃənz ˈleɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ʃuːt fɜːst ənd ɑːsk ˈkwɛs.tʃənz ˈleɪ.tə / (BrE)
to act quickly and impulsively without waiting to understand a situation fully, often leading to rash or uninformed decisions
to act impulsively without understanding a situation

Examples

When faced with unexpected challenges, it's often dangerous to shoot first and ask questions later.
Leaders who shoot first and ask questions later are prone to making serious mistakes.
Their company's policy is not to shoot first and ask questions later, but to analyze all available information.

Synset Examples

Sometimes, it’s better to shoot first and ask questions later.
In a crisis, he tends to shoot first and ask questions later.
She has a habit to shoot first and ask questions later in debates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: act on impulse - to do something without thinking carefully
  • Antonym: to think before you act - to consider carefully before making a decision or taking action
