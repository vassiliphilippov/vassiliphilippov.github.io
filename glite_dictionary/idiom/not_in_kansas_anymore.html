not in Kansas anymore

1. X

/ nɑt ɪn ˈkænzəs ˌeniˌmɔr / (AmE), / nɒt ɪn ˈkɑːnzəs ˌeniˈmɔː / (BrE)
used to express that one is in a situation that is unfamiliar, unexpected, or very different from what is normal or accustomed
in an unfamiliar or surprising situation

Examples

When I started my new job in the big city, I knew I was not in Kansas anymore.
The advanced technology in the company made her feel like she was not in Kansas anymore.
After moving abroad, it was clear to him that he was not in Kansas anymore.

Synset Examples

This city is wild; we're not in Kansas anymore.
After the storm, I realized we're not in Kansas anymore.
Once they moved to New York, they felt not in Kansas anymore.

Etymology

Popularized by the line 'Toto, I've a feeling we're not in Kansas anymore' from the 1939 film The Wizard of Oz.
