Billy-be-damned

1. X

/ ˈbɪli-bi-ˈdæmd / (AmE), / ˈbɪli-bi-ˈdamd / (BrE)
An exclamatory phrase used to express disregard for potential consequences, rules, or others' opinions.
expression showing disregard for consequences

Examples

He invested all his savings, Billy-be-damned, because he believed in his idea.
She left the board meeting early, Billy-be-damned, to pick up her daughter.
They drove through the storm, Billy-be-damned, determined to reach home by midnight.

Synset Examples

They took the risk, Billy-be-damned, and it paid off.
She went out in that dress, Billy-be-damned!

Etymology

Origin uncertain; likely a colloquial formation referencing the personal name Billy and the phrase 'be damned' to intensify defiance.
