to keep one's eyes peeled

1. VERB

/ kip wʌnz aɪz pild / (AmE), / kiːp wʌnz aɪz piːld / (BrE)
to stay alert and watchful for something; to pay close attention
be watchful; stay alert

Examples

While hiking in the woods, you should keep your eyes peeled for snakes.
The detective told his partner to keep his eyes peeled for any suspicious activity.
If you want to find bargains at the market, keep your eyes peeled for unadvertised sales.

Synset Examples

Keep your eyes peeled for any policemen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keep an eye out - to be watchful for something or someone
  • Synonym: to watch out - to be vigilant or careful for possible danger

Etymology

From the verb 'peel' meaning to open or uncover, suggesting having one's eyes wide and open.
