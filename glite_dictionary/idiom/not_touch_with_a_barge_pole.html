to not touch with a barge pole

1. VERB

/ nɑt tʌtʃ wɪð ə bɑrdʒ poʊl / (AmE), / nɒt tʌtʃ wɪð ə bɑːdʒ pəʊl / (BrE)
to refuse to become involved with someone or something, often because it is considered undesirable or problematic
avoid involvement entirely (esp. British)
british

Examples

The company’s finances are such a mess that I’d not touch it with a barge pole.
Although they offered me a job, I would not touch that place with a barge pole.
He’s so unreliable that most managers would not touch him with a barge pole.

Synset Examples

She said she'd not touch him with a barge pole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to steer clear of - to deliberately avoid someone or something
  • Synonym: to shun - to deliberately avoid someone or something

Etymology

Refers to the long pole used to push a barge; figuratively indicates avoiding any contact.
