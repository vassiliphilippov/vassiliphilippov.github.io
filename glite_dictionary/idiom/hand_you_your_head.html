to hand you your head

1. VERB

/ hænd ju jɚ hɛd / (AmE), / hænd juː jɔː hɛd / (BrE)
to punish or reprimand someone severely
punish or reprimand severely

Examples

If you keep arriving late, the boss will hand you your head in front of everyone.
The coach threatened to hand you your head if you missed another practice.
Messing up the project will make your manager hand you your head during the meeting.

Synset Examples

If you don’t improve, I’ll hand you your head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chew someone out - to scold or reprimand someone harshly
  • Antonym: let someone off easy - to punish or reprimand someone leniently
