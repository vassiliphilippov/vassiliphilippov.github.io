all roads lead to Rome

1. X

/ ɔl roʊdz lid tə roʊm / (AmE), / ɔːl rəʊdz liːd tə rəʊm / (BrE)
used to say that there are many different ways of achieving the same result or reaching the same goal
many methods to the same outcome (idiom)

Examples

Although their strategies differed, in business all roads lead to Rome.
You might take several approaches to solving the puzzle, but all roads lead to Rome.
In learning a language, remember that all roads lead to Rome—find the method that works for you.

Synset Examples

Despite the different paths, all roads lead to Rome.
Her research showed that all roads lead to Rome eventually.
In the end, all roads lead to Rome for a solution.

Etymology

From the ancient Roman saying reflecting that all routes in the Roman Empire led to the capital, Rome.
