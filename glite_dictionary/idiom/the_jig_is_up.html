the jig is up

1. X

/ ðə dʒɪɡ ɪz ʌp / (AmE), / ðə dʒɪɡ ɪz ʌp / (BrE)
used to indicate that someone's secret or deception has been uncovered and they can no longer continue their scheme
the deception has been discovered (idiom)

Examples

When the evidence was presented in court, Sarah knew the jig is up.
After months of pretending to be someone else, he realized the jig is up.
Once the lights came on and everyone saw him, the jig is up.

Synset Examples

They thought they could hide, but the jig is up.
When the police arrived, the jig was up.
He realized the jig was up as soon as they questioned him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caught red-handed - caught in the act of wrongdoing

Etymology

Possibly from 18th-century slang where 'jig' referred to a trick or practical joke.
