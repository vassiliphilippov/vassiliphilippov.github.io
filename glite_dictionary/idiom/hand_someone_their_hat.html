to hand someone their hat

1. VERB

/ ˈhændəd sʌmˌwʌn ðɛr hæt / (AmE), / ˈhændɪd sʌmwʌn ðeə hæt / (BrE)
(informal) To dismiss someone from a job or position, often signaling that it is time for them to leave.
dismiss or fire someone (informal idiom)
informal

Examples

After missing several deadlines, the manager finally handed him his hat.
When the scandal broke, the company quietly handed her her hat.
It was no surprise when the board handed him his hat after the poor results.

Synset Examples

Disappointed, she handed him his hat and said goodbye.
After that performance, the director handed him his hat.
The coach handed the player his hat after the loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire - to dismiss someone from a job
  • Synonym: let someone go - to dismiss or terminate an employee
  • Antonym: hire - to employ someone for a job

Etymology

Alludes to the gesture of handing an employee their hat as a signal to leave; attested from the late 19th century.
