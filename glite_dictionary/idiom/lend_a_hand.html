to lend a hand

1. VERB

/ lɛnd ə hænd / (AmE), / lɛnd ə hænd / (BrE)
to offer help or assistance to someone
offer help; assist someone

Examples

Whenever there's extra work, she's always willing to lend a hand.
Could you lend a hand moving these boxes upstairs?
The community came together to lend a hand after the storm.

Synset Examples

She was happy to lend a hand to her neighbor.
Can you lend me a hand with this project?
Can you lend a hand with my project this weekend?
He volunteered to lend a hand at the food bank.
Don't be such a nicka; just lend him a hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to help out - to assist someone, often temporarily
  • Synonym: to pitch in - to join in and help with work or effort
  • Antonym: to hold back - to not offer help or support
