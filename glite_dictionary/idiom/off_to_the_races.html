off to the races

1. X

/ ˈɔf tə ðə ˈreɪsɪz / (AmE), / ˈɒf tə ðə ˈreɪsɪz / (BrE)
used to indicate that something has started energetically or enthusiastically, especially a process, project, or competition
to start enthusiastically or energetically

Examples

As soon as the bell rang, the students were off to the races to complete their projects.
With the new manager on board, the company is off to the races in expanding its market share.
The product launch went smoothly, and now the whole team is off to the races with marketing efforts.

Synset Examples

The project is off to the races after our discussion.
As soon as the plan was approved, it was off to the races.
Once the meeting ended, we were off to the races.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get going - to start moving or taking action
  • Synonym: to kick off - to begin an event, action, or period of time
  • Antonym: to wrap up - to finish or complete an activity

Etymology

Originally from the context of horse racing, where the race begins with excitement and energy.
