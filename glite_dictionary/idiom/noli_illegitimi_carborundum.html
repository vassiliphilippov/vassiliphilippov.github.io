noli illegitimi carborundum

1. X

/ ˈnoʊli ɪˌlɛdʒɪˈtaɪmi kɑːrbəˈrʌndəm / (AmE), / ˈnəʊli ɪˌlɛdʒɪˈtiːmiː kɑːbɒˈrʌndəm / (BrE)
A humorous pseudo-Latin phrase meaning 'don't let the bastards grind you down', used to encourage resilience in the face of adversity.
humorous phrase meaning 'don't let the bastards grind you down'

Examples

Whenever her colleagues criticized her work, she would silently repeat noli illegitimi carborundum to herself.
He ended his speech with the phrase noli illegitimi carborundum to inspire the team.
The old sign above his desk read noli illegitimi carborundum as a daily reminder to stay positive.

Synset Examples

She lives by the motto noli illegitimi carborundum to stay motivated.

Whenever faced with challenges, I remember: noli illegitimi carborundum.

Etymology

A mock-Latin expression invented as a humorous pseudo-motto, combining Latin-sounding words and Carborundum, a trademark for a type of abrasive.
