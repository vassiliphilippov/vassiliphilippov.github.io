to know one's own mind

1. VERB

/ noʊ wʌnz oʊn maɪnd / (AmE), / nəʊ wʌnz əʊn maɪnd / (BrE)
to have a clear understanding of one's beliefs, desires, or intentions, and to act accordingly
be sure of one's own beliefs or feelings

Examples

Even as a teenager, she knew her own mind and followed her passions.
Before accepting the job offer, he made sure to know his own mind.
It is easier to deal with people who know their own mind.

Synset Examples

She always knows her own mind and stands by it.
It's important to know one's own mind before making choices.
He knows his own mind and makes decisions confidently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decisive - having the ability to decide quickly and firmly
  • Antonym: indecisive - not able to make decisions quickly and effectively
