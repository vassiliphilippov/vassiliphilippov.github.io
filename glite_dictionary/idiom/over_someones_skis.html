over someone's skis

1. ADJ

/ ˈoʊvɚ ˈsʌmwʌnz skis / (AmE), / ˈəʊvə ˈsʌmwʌnz skis / (BrE)
in a situation where one is out of one's depth or overwhelmed by circumstances
out of one's depth; overwhelmed
informal american

Examples

After accepting the promotion, he quickly realized he was over his skis with all the new responsibilities.
I'm definitely over my skis trying to manage this complex project alone.
They found themselves over their skis when the funding ran out sooner than expected.

Synset Examples

She felt she was over her skis in that advanced course.
He was over his skis when he tried to negotiate the deal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: out of one's depth - in a situation that is too difficult to handle
  • Synonym: in over one's head - facing a task that is too challenging

Etymology

Derived from the image of a skier leaning too far forward and losing control, commonly used in American English business slang.
