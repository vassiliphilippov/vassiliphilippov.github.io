to shake a leg

1. VERB

/ ʃeɪk ə lɛɡ / (AmE), / ʃeɪk ə lɛɡ / (BrE)
to hurry up or move quickly, especially when prompted to act faster
hurry up; move quickly (idiom)
informal

Examples

If you don’t shake a leg, we’ll miss the train.
You’d better shake a leg or we’ll be late for the meeting.
The coach told the players to shake a leg and get back on the field.

Synset Examples

He needs to shake a leg if he wants to finish on time.
Come on, it's time to shake a leg and leave!
If you want to catch the bus, shake a leg!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hurry up - to do something more quickly
  • Antonym: to dawdle - to waste time or move slowly
