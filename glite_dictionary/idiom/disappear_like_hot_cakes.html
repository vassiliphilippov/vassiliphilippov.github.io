to disappear like hot cakes

1. VERB

/ dɪsəˈpɪr laɪk hɑt keɪks / (AmE), / dɪsəˈpɪə laɪk hɒt keɪks / (BrE)
to be sold or taken very quickly and in large quantities
be sold or taken very quickly

Examples

As soon as the concert tickets went on sale, they disappeared like hot cakes.
The limited-edition sneakers disappeared like hot cakes as soon as they were released.
All the freshly baked bread disappeared like hot cakes from the bakery shelves.

Synset Examples

Her cookies disappeared like hot cakes at the bake sale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sell like hot cakes - to sell very quickly and in large quantities

Etymology

From the popularity of hot cakes (pancakes), which were quickly bought at fairs and markets in 19th-century America.
