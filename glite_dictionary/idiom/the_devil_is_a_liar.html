the devil is a liar

1. X

/ ðə ˈdɛvəl ɪz ə ˈlaɪɚ / (AmE), / ðə ˈdɛvəl ɪz ə ˈlaɪə / (BrE)
used to assert that negative thoughts or temptations are deceptive and should not be believed, often in a religious or motivational context
statement about resisting deception or negativity

Examples

Whenever doubt enters your mind, remember that the devil is a liar.
She trusted her faith and told herself that the devil is a liar.
In difficult moments, he would assure his friends, "the devil is a liar."

Synset Examples

Whenever I feel down, I remember the devil is a liar.
She reminded me that the devil is a liar in tough times.
He always said, the devil is a liar, don't believe his tricks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lie - a false statement made with deliberate intent to deceive
  • Antonym: truth - the quality or state of being true

Etymology

Refers to the biblical association of the devil with deception and lies.
