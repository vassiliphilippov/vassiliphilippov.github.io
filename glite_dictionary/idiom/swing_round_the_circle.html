to swing round the circle

1. VERB

/ swɪŋ raʊnd ðə ˈsɝː.kəl / (AmE), / swɪŋ raʊnd ðə ˈsɜː.kəl / (BrE)
to go around a group to collect final opinions or comments from each person
gather final opinions from a group

Examples

Before making a decision, the manager suggested we swing round the circle for input from everyone.
At the end of the workshop, we swing round the circle to hear each participant's thoughts.
Let's swing round the circle to ensure all perspectives are considered before concluding.

Synset Examples

They decided to swing round the circle for final thoughts before concluding.
During the meeting, we swung round the circle to gather opinions.
Let’s swing round the circle and discuss everyone's ideas.
