to hold court

1. VERB

/ hoʊld kɔrt / (AmE), / həʊld kɔːt / (BrE)
to attract and entertain a group of people, often by talking in a way that draws their attention
entertain or engage a group of people

Examples

Every Friday evening, Uncle Joe would hold court at the pub, surrounded by laughing friends.
During the book signing, the celebrity author held court with journalists and fans alike.
Whenever she visits, Grandma holds court in the kitchen with stories from her childhood.

Synset Examples

The manager held court in the break room, sharing stories.
At the party, she held court, entertaining everyone with her tales.
He loves to hold court with his friends after work.

Etymology

Figurative use derived from the legal phrase 'to hold court,' referring to a judge presiding over a courtroom.
