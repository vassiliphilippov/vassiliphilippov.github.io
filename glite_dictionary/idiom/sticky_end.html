sticky end

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪki ɛnd / (AmE), / ˈstɪki end / (BrE)
an unpleasant, troublesome, or disastrous conclusion or outcome, especially one involving suffering or punishment
unpleasant or disastrous outcome

Examples

The criminal met a sticky end after years of evading the law.
Many superstitious people believe that cheaters are destined for a sticky end.
In old tales, the villains often come to a sticky end for their misdeeds.

Synset Examples

Most villains in stories come to a sticky end.
His reckless behavior is bound to come to a sticky end.
The villain always meets a sticky end in the movies.
She feared her career would come to a sticky end.
If you keep lying, you might meet a sticky end.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gruesome end - a violent or horrifying death or conclusion
  • Antonym: happy ending - a positive or fortunate outcome
