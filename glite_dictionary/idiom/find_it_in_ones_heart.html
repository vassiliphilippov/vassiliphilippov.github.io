to find it in one's heart

1. VERB

/ faɪnd ɪt ɪn wʌnz hɑrt / (AmE), / faɪnd ɪt ɪn wʌnz hɑːt / (BrE)
to be able to summon the courage or compassion needed to do something, especially something difficult or compassionate
have courage or compassion to do something

Examples

Even though he was angry, he couldn't find it in his heart to blame her.
After years of silence, she finally found it in her heart to call her brother.
Do you think she'll find it in her heart to accept my apology?

Synset Examples

I couldn't find it in my heart to say no.
Can you find it in your heart to forgive him?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bring oneself to do something - to be able to force oneself to do something
