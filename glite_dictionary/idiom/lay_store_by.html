to lay store by

1. VERB

/ leɪ stɔr baɪ / (AmE), / leɪ stɔː baɪ / (BrE)
to regard something as important, valuable, or worthy of trust; to count on or value highly
consider important or valuable

Examples

Many investors lay store by expert opinions when making decisions about their portfolios.
She has always laid store by honesty and hard work in her team.
Some people lay store by old superstitions, while others dismiss them entirely.

Synset Examples

I lay store by my instincts when making decisions.
She doesn’t lay store by gossip and hearsay.
They lay store by the quality of their products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: value - to consider something important or useful
  • Antonym: disregard - to pay no attention to; to treat as unimportant
