to come to a close

1. VERB

/ kʌm tu ə kloʊz / (AmE), / kʌm tʊ ə kləʊz / (BrE)
to reach an end or conclusion; to finish
reach an end; finish

Examples

The semester will come to a close next week after final exams.
As the negotiations came to a close, both sides seemed satisfied with the results.
The awards ceremony was coming to a close when the surprise guest appeared.

Synset Examples

The event will come to a close by midnight.
As the concert came to a close, the crowd cheered.
The year is quickly coming to a close.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: end - to come to an end; finish
  • Antonym: to begin - to start or commence
