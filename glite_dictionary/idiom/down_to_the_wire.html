down to the wire

1. ADV

/ daʊn tə ðə ˈwaɪɚ / (AmE), / daʊn tə ðə ˈwaɪə / (BrE)
used to describe a situation where something is not decided or finished until the very last moment before a deadline
at the very last moment

Examples

The negotiations went down to the wire, but an agreement was finally reached.
Her essay wasn't finished until down to the wire, but she managed to submit it on time.
Our team worked down to the wire to complete the project before the deadline.

Synset Examples

We worked hard to finish the project down to the wire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: last minute - at the latest possible time before something happens
  • Synonym: in the nick of time - just in time; at the last possible moment

Etymology

Probably from racing, where a wire marked the finish line.
