fox in the henhouse

1. NOUN

/ fɑks ɪn ðə ˈhɛnˌhaʊs / (AmE), / fɒks ɪn ðə ˈhenˌhaʊs / (BrE)
a person who is a danger or threat in a place where others are vulnerable, especially in a position of trust or responsibility
person posing threat in a vulnerable situation

Examples

Giving him access to company finances is putting a fox in the henhouse.
Appointing a former competitor as advisor was like letting a fox in the henhouse.
If you hire someone with a history of fraud, it’s like having a fox in the henhouse.

Synset Examples

The new manager is a fox in the henhouse.
You don’t want a fox in the henhouse in this project.
Hiring him is like a fox in the henhouse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wolf in sheep's clothing - a person who appears harmless but is actually dangerous
  • Synonym: infiltrator - someone who secretly enters an organization to cause damage

Etymology

Alludes to a fox being a predator in a henhouse, implying danger in a vulnerable place.
