to spoil the market

1. VERB

/ spɔɪl ðə ˈmɑrkɪt / (AmE), / spɔɪl ðə ˈmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
to harm normal market conditions or lower the credibility or value of a market, often by unfair competition or an oversupply of poor-quality goods
harm normal market conditions

Examples

Heavy discounting by online retailers can spoil the market for local shops.
If counterfeit products flood the shelves, it may spoil the market for authentic brands.
Price wars among manufacturers often spoil the market and hurt long-term profits.

Synset Examples

Discounts can sometimes spoil the market for premium brands.
Excess supply could spoil the market for new sellers.
Poor quality products can spoil the market's reputation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undercut - to sell goods at a lower price than a competitor, often harming the market
