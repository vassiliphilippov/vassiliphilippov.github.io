new sheriff in town

1. NOUN

/ nu ˈʃɛɹɪf ɪn taʊn / (AmE), / njuː ˈʃɛɹɪf ɪn taʊn / (BrE)
a new person in a position of authority, often one expected to enforce change or stricter control
new authority figure; person in charge

Examples

With her promotion, there is a new sheriff in town and things will change quickly.
Since the restructuring, employees know there's a new sheriff in town who means business.
After the old manager retired, a new sheriff in town started running the office.

Synset Examples
