trick in the book

1. NOUN

/ trɪk ɪn ðə bʊk / (AmE), / trɪk ɪn ðə bʊk / (BrE)
every possible method or tactic, especially a clever or deceptive one, that someone might use to achieve a goal
every possible method or tactic

Examples

He tried every trick in the book to get out of doing his homework, but none worked.
Our competitors have used every trick in the book to win over customers.
As a seasoned negotiator, she is familiar with every trick in the book.

Synset Examples

As a Luber, he knows every trick in the book.
As a TDSer, she knows every trick in the book.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ploy - a cunning plan or action designed to turn a situation to one's own advantage
  • Synonym: ruse - a trick or act used to deceive someone
