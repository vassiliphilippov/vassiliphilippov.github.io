last hurrah

1. NOUN

/ læst həˈrɑ / (AmE), / lɑːst həˈrɑː / (BrE)
a final effort, performance, or achievement, especially before retirement or the end of a career or period in one's life
a final effort or performance

Examples

After working in the industry for 40 years, his retirement party was truly his last hurrah.
The band reunited for one last hurrah before disbanding for good.
She decided to run one more marathon as a last hurrah before turning fifty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finale - the last part of a performance or event
  • Synonym: swansong - a final act or performance before ending one's career

Etymology

Popularized in the U.S. around the mid-20th century, possibly from the 1956 novel 'The Last Hurrah' by Edwin O'Connor.

2. NOUN

/ læst həˈrɑ / (AmE), / lɑːst həˈrɑː / (BrE)
a final activity, event, or celebration that happens before a significant change or ending, often done with special enthusiasm
final celebration/event before a change

Examples

Graduating seniors organized a last hurrah before everyone moved away for college.
The concert was the band's last hurrah before announcing their split.
We all wanted one last hurrah together before the end of summer.

Synset Examples

The party was his last hurrah before retirement.
They planned a last hurrah trip before college.
His birthday celebration felt like a last hurrah.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swan song - a final performance or effort before retirement or death
  • Synonym: farewell - an occasion or expression of parting

Etymology

From the word 'hurrah' (an exclamation of joy or triumph), popularized in the 20th century; notably by Edwin O'Connor's 1956 novel 'The Last Hurrah'.
