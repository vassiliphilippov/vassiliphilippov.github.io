to take someone's arm

1. VERB

/ teɪk ˈsʌmwʌnz ɑrm / (AmE), / teɪk ˈsʌmwənz ɑːm / (BrE)
to hold someone's arm to assist, guide, or support them while walking or moving
hold someone's arm to assist or guide

Examples

He politely took her arm as they crossed the busy street together.
When I felt dizzy, my friend took my arm and led me to a seat.
The nurse took his arm to help him stand up from the wheelchair.

Synset Examples

She gently took his arm and guided him away.
