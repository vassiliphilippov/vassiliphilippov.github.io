under the radar

1. ADV

/ ˈʌndɚ ðə rəˌdɑr / (AmE), / ˈʌndə ðə ˈreɪdɑː / (BrE)
If someone or something is under the radar, they are not noticed by other people, often deliberately.
not noticed; unnoticed

Examples

The new company stayed under the radar until its groundbreaking product was revealed.
Many artists work under the radar before achieving fame.
She prefers to keep her achievements under the radar, avoiding attention from the media.

Synset Examples

He managed to operate under the radar for years.
The project flew under the radar until it was finished.
She likes to keep her projects under the radar.
The new technology helps us stay under the radar.
He decided to fly low under the radar to avoid attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unnoticed - not seen or observed
  • Antonym: prominent - very noticeable or important

Etymology

From aviation, where staying below radar detection means remaining unseen; figuratively used since the late 20th century.
