to hit pay dirt

1. VERB

/ hɪt peɪ dɜrt / (AmE), / hɪt peɪ dɜːt / (BrE)
to achieve significant success or profit, especially after effort or searching
achieve significant success or profit

Examples

After years of hard work, the scientists finally hit pay dirt with their latest experiment.
When she landed that big contract, she knew she had hit pay dirt.
The prospector hit pay dirt after months of futile searching.

Synset Examples

After months of research, we finally hit pay dirt.
The investors hit pay dirt with their latest startup.
He felt he hit pay dirt when the stock soared.
After months of digging, they finally struck pay dirt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strike it rich - to suddenly acquire great wealth
  • Antonym: come up empty - to fail to find success or profit

Etymology

Originally from American English mining jargon, where "pay dirt" meant earth containing enough precious metal to be profitable.
