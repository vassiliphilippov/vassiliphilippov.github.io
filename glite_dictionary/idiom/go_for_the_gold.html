to go for the gold

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ fər ðə ɡoʊld / (AmE), / ɡəʊ fə ðə ɡəʊld / (BrE)
to strive wholeheartedly to win first place or achieve the best possible outcome, especially in a competition or challenging endeavor
strive for the best or first place

Examples

Despite the challenges, she decided to go for the gold in her final exam preparations.
Our startup is ready to go for the gold and become an industry leader.
If you want to succeed, don't hold back—just go for the gold!

Synset Examples

She trained hard to go for the gold in the Olympics.
The team is ready to go for the gold this season.
He always aims to go for the gold in every competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go all out - make one's maximum effort to achieve success
  • Synonym: give it your all - put all energy and effort into something
  • Antonym: settle for second best - accept something less than the best outcome

Etymology

From the practice of awarding gold medals to first-place winners, especially in the Olympic Games.
