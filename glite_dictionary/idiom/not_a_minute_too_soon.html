not a minute too soon

1. ADV

/ nɑt ə ˈmɪnɪt tu sun / (AmE), / nɒt ə ˈmɪnɪt tuː suːn / (BrE)
used to say that something happened just in time, or at the last possible moment before it would have been too late
just in time; at the last possible moment

Examples

The doctor arrived not a minute too soon to save the patient.
The firefighters reached the burning house not a minute too soon.
She submitted her assignment not a minute too soon before the deadline.

Synset Examples

I finished the report, not a minute too soon.
He arrived not a minute too soon for the meeting.
She woke up, and it was not a minute too soon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: just in time - arriving or happening at the last possible moment
