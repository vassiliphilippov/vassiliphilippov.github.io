ambulance at the bottom of a cliff

1. NOUN

/ ˈæm.bjə.ləns ət ðə ˈbɑː.təm əv ə klɪf / (AmE), / ˈæm.bjʊ.ləns ət ðə ˈbɒt.əm əv ə klɪf / (BrE)
an approach that addresses problems only after they have occurred, rather than preventing them in advance
fixing problems after the damage is done

Examples

The company’s response was just an ambulance at the bottom of a cliff, not a long-term fix.
Relying on emergency repairs is like putting an ambulance at the bottom of a cliff.
Instead of investing in prevention, they waited for disaster and sent an ambulance at the bottom of a cliff.

Synset Examples

We need preventive measures; it’s not an ambulance at the bottom of a cliff situation.
Planning ahead avoids an ambulance at the bottom of a cliff scenario.
Investing without research is like putting an ambulance at the bottom of a cliff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: band-aid solution - a temporary or superficial solution to a problem
  • Antonym: preventive - intended to stop something undesirable before it happens
