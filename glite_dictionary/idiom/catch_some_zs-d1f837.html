to catch some z's

1. VERB

/ kætʃ səm ziːz / (AmE), / kætʃ səm ziːz / (BrE)
to sleep or take a nap, especially for a short period
sleep; take a nap (informal)
informal american

Examples

After a long flight, I just wanted to catch some z's before dinner.
He sneaked away during lunch to catch some z's in his car.
You'll feel better if you catch some z's before your night shift starts.

Synset Examples

She always tries to catch some z's on weekends.
I need to catch some z's before the big meeting.
After working late, I finally caught some z's.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleep - be in a state of rest in which the eyes are closed and the mind and body are inactive
  • Synonym: take a nap - to sleep for a short period of time, especially during the day

Etymology

From 'Z', representing the sound of snoring in comics and cartoons, dating to mid-20th century American English.
