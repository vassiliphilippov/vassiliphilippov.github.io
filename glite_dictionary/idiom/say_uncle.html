to say uncle

1. VERB

/ seɪ ˈʌŋkəl / (AmE), / seɪ ˈʌŋkl̩ / (BrE)
to admit defeat or surrender, especially in response to persistent pressure or physical challenge
admit defeat or surrender

Examples

After several minutes of tickling, she finally had to say uncle.
He was so stubborn that he refused to say uncle, no matter what happened.
The coach pushed the team hard, but they wouldn’t say uncle and kept going.

Synset Examples

After a long struggle, he finally had to say uncle.
When he couldn't take it anymore, he shouted, 'say uncle!'
Reluctantly, the kid had to say uncle in the wrestling match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give in - to stop resisting; to surrender
  • Synonym: surrender - to stop resisting and accept defeat
  • Antonym: to stand firm - to refuse to give up; to resist
