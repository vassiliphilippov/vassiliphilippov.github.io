to keep tabs on

1. VERB

/ kip tæbz ɑn / (AmE), / kiːp tæbz ɒn / (BrE)
to watch or monitor someone or something carefully, often to keep informed of progress or changes
monitor or watch carefully

Examples

She likes to keep tabs on her children's schoolwork to ensure they don't fall behind.
The manager regularly keeps tabs on sales figures to spot any downward trends.
Reporters tried to keep tabs on the mayor's activities during the election.

Synset Examples

The government keeps tabs on the dissidents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monitor - to observe and check the progress or quality of something
  • Synonym: to oversee - to supervise or watch over something or someone
