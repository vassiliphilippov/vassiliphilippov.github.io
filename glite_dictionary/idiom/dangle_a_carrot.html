to dangle a carrot

1. VERB

/ ˈdæŋɡl ə ˈkærət / (AmE), / ˈdaŋɡl ə ˈkærət / (BrE)
to entice or motivate someone by promising a reward or incentive, especially to encourage a desired behavior
entice with a promised reward

Examples

The manager tried to dangle a carrot to convince the team to meet the deadline.
Parents often dangle a carrot to get their children to do chores.
He thought he could dangle a carrot and make me accept the deal.

Synset Examples

He dangled a carrot in front of the kids to get them to clean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carrot and stick - a policy of offering a reward and a punishment to induce behavior

Etymology

Refers metaphorically to the practice of motivating a donkey or horse by holding a carrot on a stick in front of it.
