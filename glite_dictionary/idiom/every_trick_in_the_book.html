every trick in the book

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛvɹi tɹɪk ɪn ðə bʊk / (AmE), / ˈɛvɹi tɹɪk ɪn ðə bʊk / (BrE)
every possible method or strategy that could be used to achieve something, often implying resourcefulness or cunning
all possible methods or strategies

Examples

The experienced lawyer used every trick in the book to win the case.
She tried every trick in the book to get her kids to eat vegetables.
Mark used every trick in the book to secure the best deal for his company.

Synset Examples

She knows every trick in the book when it comes to sales.
He thinks he knows every trick in the book.
As a veteran, he knows every trick in the book.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trick - a clever or cunning action or scheme
