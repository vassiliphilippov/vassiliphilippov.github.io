to fix someone's wagon

1. VERB

/ fɪks ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈwæɡən / (AmE), / fɪks ˈsʌmwənz ˈwæɡən / (BrE)
to punish, get revenge on, or teach someone a lesson
get revenge or teach a lesson
informal

Examples

If he tries to cheat us again, we'll fix his wagon and make sure he regrets it.
She's planning to fix his wagon after what he did to her last week.
The coach warned, 'If you don't follow the rules, I'll fix your wagon.'

Synset Examples

He was late again, so I decided to fix his wagon.
She schemed to fix his wagon after he betrayed her.
They planned to fix his wagon for always skipping out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get back at - to take revenge on someone
  • Antonym: to forgive - to stop feeling anger or resentment toward someone
