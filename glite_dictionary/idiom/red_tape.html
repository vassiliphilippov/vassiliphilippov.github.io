red tape

1. NOUN

/ rɛd teɪp / (AmE), / rɛd teɪp / (BrE)
Excessive bureaucracy or adherence to official rules and formalities, especially when causing delay or obstruction.
excessive bureaucracy; obstructive procedures

Examples

Getting a building permit involved so much red tape that we almost gave up.
She was frustrated by the endless red tape delaying her visa application.
The new law was designed to cut through red tape and speed up the process.

Synset Examples

Dealing with all the red tape was frustrating.
We can't proceed due to excessive red tape.
Living in quangoland means dealing with endless red tape.
He spent hours dealing with the red tape of bureaucracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bureaucracy - system of government or management with complex rules and procedures

Etymology

From the 18th-century practice of binding official documents with red tape.
