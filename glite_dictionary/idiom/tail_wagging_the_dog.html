tail wagging the dog

1. NOUN

/ teɪl ˈwæɡɪŋ ðə dɔɡ / (AmE), / teɪl ˈwaɡɪŋ ðə dɒɡ / (BrE)
A situation where a minor or less important part exerts control over the main or more important aspect.
minor part controls the whole

Examples

In the new policy debate, social media outrage became the tail wagging the dog.
Some fear that a small committee is really the tail wagging the dog in this organization.
If we let marketing dictate product design, we'll have the tail wagging the dog.

Synset Examples

The budget issue was just the tail wagging the dog.
This project is the tail wagging the dog of our strategy.
Don't let the details become the tail wagging the dog.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to put the cart before the horse - to do things in the wrong order or confuse priorities
  • Antonym: in charge - to be in control or have authority
