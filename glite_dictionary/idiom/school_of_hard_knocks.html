school of hard knocks

1. NOUN

/ skuːl əv hɑrd nɑks / (AmE), / skuːl əv hɑːd nɒks / (BrE)
used to refer to the experience gained from difficult or challenging situations in life, rather than from formal education
learning through life's tough experiences

Examples

Many successful entrepreneurs attribute their achievements to the school of hard knocks rather than traditional education.
After losing his job and starting over, he graduated from the school of hard knocks.
She didn't go to college, but the school of hard knocks taught her more than any classroom could.

Synset Examples

Her experience in the school of hard knocks shaped her character.
The school of hard knocks taught him resilience.
He learned life lessons in the school of hard knocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: life experience - knowledge or skill acquired through living
  • Degree Higher: streetwise - having the knowledge and experience needed to deal with the difficulties of life in urban environments
