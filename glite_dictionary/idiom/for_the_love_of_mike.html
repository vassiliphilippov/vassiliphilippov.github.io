for the love of Mike

1. X

/ fər ðə lʌv əv maɪk / (AmE), / fə ðə lʌv əv mʌɪk / (BrE)
used to express feelings such as exasperation, urgency, or annoyance, in place of stronger language
expression of exasperation or urgency
informal

Examples

For the love of Mike, turn the music down before the neighbors complain.
I wish you would, for the love of Mike, just pay attention for once.
For the love of Mike, don’t leave the door unlocked when you go out.

Synset Examples

I can’t understand, for the love of Mike, why he’s upset.
For the love of Mike, can you please stop interrupting?
She said, for the love of Mike, just give it a try.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: for the love of Pete - expression of exasperation or urgency; similar euphemism
  • Synonym: for heaven's sake - expression used to show frustration or urgency
  • Degree Higher: for God's sake - stronger expression of exasperation or pleading

Etymology

Mild euphemism for 'for the love of God', using 'Mike' to avoid blasphemy; 'Mike' possibly refers to 'Michael'.
