to flex one's muscle

1. VERB

/ flɛks wʌnz ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / flɛks wʌnz ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
to show power or strength, especially as a warning or in order to impress others
demonstrate power or strength

Examples

The government began to flex its muscle in response to international criticism.
Major corporations often flex their muscle during contract negotiations.
The new manager wasted no time in flexing her muscle to implement changes.

Synset Examples

The company flexed its muscles by launching a new product.
He flexed his muscle to show off his strength.
She flexed her muscles in negotiations to secure a better deal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to show off - to display something, especially one's abilities, to impress others
  • Synonym: to assert oneself - to behave confidently to make people recognize your authority or rights
