to make quick work of

1. VERB

/ meɪk kwɪk wɝːk ʌv / (AmE), / meɪk kwɪk wɜːk ɒv / (BrE)
to do or finish something quickly and efficiently
finish something quickly and efficiently

Examples

The chef made quick work of preparing the large dinner order.
With the right tools, the workers made quick work of fixing the broken pipe.
She makes quick work of her homework every night before watching TV.

Synset Examples

The long-grasser made quick work of the tall weeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to finish off - to complete something, often quickly
  • Antonym: to take one's time - to do something slowly or carefully, opposite in approach
