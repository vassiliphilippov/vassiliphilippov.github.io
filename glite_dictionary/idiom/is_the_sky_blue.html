is the sky blue?

1. X

/ ɪz ðə skaɪ blu / (AmE), / ɪz ðə skaɪ bluː / (BrE)
A rhetorical question used to indicate that the answer to a previous question is obviously or unquestionably 'yes'.
rhetorical question for obvious affirmatives

Examples

When asked if she loves chocolate, she replied, 'Is the sky blue?'
Do you think he'll be late again? Is the sky blue?
'Will the team try their best?' 'Is the sky blue?'

Synset Examples

If he thinks he can succeed without effort, is the sky blue?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: does a bear shit in the woods? - rhetorical question expressing that the answer is obviously yes
  • Synonym: is water wet? - rhetorical question meaning the answer is obviously yes
