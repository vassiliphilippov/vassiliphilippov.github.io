up to one's neck

1. ADJ

/ ʌp tə wʌnz nɛk / (AmE), / ʌp tʊ wʌnz nɛk / (BrE)
deeply involved or entangled in a difficult or unpleasant situation
deeply involved in a situation

Examples

After the merger, he was up to his neck in paperwork.
She found herself up to her neck in debt after buying the house.
They were up to their necks in trouble with the tax authorities.

Synset Examples

neck-deep in work
up to their necks in debt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: up to one's ears - deeply involved or implicated

2. ADJ

/ ʌp tə wʌnz nɛk / (AmE), / ʌp tʊ wʌnz nɛk / (BrE)
deeply or heavily involved or entangled (in something), often more than is desirable or comfortable
deeply involved; heavily entangled

Examples

We're up to our necks in paperwork after last week's audit.
By summer, the farmers were up to their necks in work during harvest season.
She realized she was up to her neck in trouble when she missed another payment.

Synset Examples

neck-deep in work
up to their necks in debt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in over one's head - involved in something too difficult to handle
  • Synonym: up to one's ears - heavily involved or entangled (in something)
  • Antonym: free - not involved or entangled in something
Source: WordNet
