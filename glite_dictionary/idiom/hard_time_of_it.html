hard time of it

1. NOUN

/ hɑrd taɪm əv ɪt / (AmE), / hɑːd taɪm əv ɪt / (BrE)
a period of difficulty or adversity, often involving hardship or struggle
a difficult or challenging experience

Examples

After losing his job, he really had a hard time of it for several months.
Many families had a hard time of it during the economic crisis.
She gave her friend support when she was having a hard time of it at home.

Synset Examples

They had a hard time of it in the storm.
She had a hard time of it during the exam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard time - a period of hardship or trouble
