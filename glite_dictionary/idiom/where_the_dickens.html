where the dickens

1. X

/ wɛr ðə ˈdɪkənz / (AmE), / wɛə ðə ˈdɪkɪnz / (BrE)
used to express surprise, confusion, or emphasis when asking about the location of something or someone
expression of surprise or confusion about whereabouts
archaic

Examples

Where the dickens did you find that old photograph?
I can't imagine where the dickens she has gone to this time.
He wondered where the dickens everyone had disappeared to.

Synset Examples

Where the dickens did you put my keys?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: where on earth - used for emphasis when asking about location, expressing surprise
  • Synonym: where the hell - informal, emphatic expression of surprise about location

Etymology

'Dickens' is a mild old euphemism for 'devil', dating from the 16th century.
