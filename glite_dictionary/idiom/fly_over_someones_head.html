to fly over someone's head

1. VERB

/ flaɪ ˈoʊvɚ ˈsʌmwʌnz hɛd / (AmE), / flaɪ ˈəʊvə ˈsʌmwʌnz hɛd / (BrE)
(of an idea, remark, etc.) to not be understood by someone, typically because it is too complicated or advanced.
to not be understood by someone

Examples

The professor's explanation completely flew over my head during the lecture.
Sometimes complex jokes just fly over her head and she doesn't laugh.
If you use too many technical terms, your point may fly over their heads.

Synset Examples

He spoke in geekspeak that flew over my head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go over someone's head - to not be understood by someone
