to bet the house

1. VERB

/ bɛt ðə haʊs / (AmE), / bɛt ðə haʊs / (BrE)
to risk everything you own on a particular outcome or decision
risk everything on one outcome

Examples

I wouldn't bet the house on that risky investment, no matter how promising it seems.
If she wins the next match, she will have effectively bet the house on her strategy.
Despite the odds, they decided to bet the house on the underdog team.

Synset Examples

I wouldn't bet the house on that stock pick.
He chose to bet the house on the final game.
They are ready to bet the house that it will rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go all in - to commit all resources or efforts to one course of action
