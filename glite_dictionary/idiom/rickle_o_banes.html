rickle o' banes

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪkəl ə ˈbeɪnz / (AmE), / ˈrɪkəl ə ˈbeɪnz / (BrE)
A series of misfortunes, troubles, or a chaotic situation.
series of misfortunes, chaos

Examples

After losing her job and her car breaking down, her month turned into a rickle o' banes.
He laughed off the rickle o' banes that the wedding planning had become.
Every project this year seems to end up as a rickle o' banes for our team.

Synset Examples

She described her week as a rickle o' banes due to constant setbacks.
The party was a rickle o' banes with all the drama.
His life feels like a rickle o' banes these days.

Etymology

Scottish dialect; 'rickle' meaning 'heap' or 'pile' and 'banes' (bones), metaphorically used for troubles.
