to bend someone's will

1. VERB

/ bɛnd ˈsʌmwʌn(z) wɪl / (AmE), / bɛnd ˈsʌmwən(z) wɪl / (BrE)
to use influence or persuasion to make someone change their mind or comply with your wishes
persuade or control someone

Examples

Despite intense negotiations, he refused to let them bend his will.
The leader sought to bend the committee's will to his own agenda.
They used every tactic, but they could not bend her will.

Synset Examples

It took a lot to bend his will on the matter.
She tried to bend his will, but he stood firm.
They couldn't bend her will despite their efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persuade - to induce someone to do or believe something by reasoning or pleading
  • Degree Higher: to coerce - to force someone to do something against their will
  • Synonym: to convince - to cause someone to believe firmly in the truth of something
