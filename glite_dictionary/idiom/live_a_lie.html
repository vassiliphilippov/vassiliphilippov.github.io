to live a lie

1. VERB

/ lɪv ə laɪ / (AmE), / lɪv ə laɪ / (BrE)
to act in a way that deliberately conceals the truth about oneself or one's situation, often by pretending something is true when it is not
pretend or hide the truth about oneself

Examples

For years, she continued to live a lie because she was afraid of what others would think.
He didn't want to live a lie any longer and finally told everyone the truth.
They realized they had been living a lie throughout their relationship.

Synset Examples

She chose to live a lie rather than face reality.
He realized he couldn't live a lie anymore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceive oneself - to deliberately make oneself believe something that is not true
