jewel of the first water

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːəl əv ðə ˈfɝst ˈwɔtɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːəl əv ðə ˈfɜːst ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
a gemstone of the highest quality or purity; something or someone considered outstanding or excellent
a gemstone of the highest quality

Examples

This diamond is indeed a jewel of the first water.
The violinist's performance was a jewel of the first water, dazzling the audience with her skill.
Their new store design is truly a jewel of the first water among modern retail spaces.

Synset Examples

This diamond is indeed a jewel of the first water.

Etymology

Refers to the use of 'water' to describe the clarity and quality of gemstones, particularly diamonds, in 17th–18th century English.
