off the hook

1. ADJ

/ ɑf ðə hʊk / (AmE), / ɒf ðə hʊk / (BrE)
No longer in difficulty, trouble, or under obligation; freed from blame or responsibility.
freed from blame or obligation

Examples

After hours of questioning, the suspect was finally off the hook when new evidence appeared.
Thanks to her alibi, she was off the hook for the missing money.
The manager let him off the hook with just a warning this time.

Synset Examples

I let him off the hook with a mild reprimand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in the clear - free from suspicion or difficulty
  • Antonym: on the hook - responsible or obliged for something

2. ADJ

/ ɑf ðə hʊk / (AmE), / ɒf ðə hʊk / (BrE)
No longer responsible or liable for an obligation or punishment; freed from difficulty or blame.
no longer responsible or in trouble

Examples

After the witness confessed, she was finally off the hook for the crime.
The manager let me off the hook and said I didn't have to work late tonight.
If you can provide a receipt, you’ll be off the hook for the missing funds.

Synset Examples

You're off the hook this time, but don't let it happen again.
Once he finished the project, he was off the hook from extra work.
She was off the hook after proving her innocence.
The clothes are off the hooks; please put them back.
The decorations are all off the hooks for the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: off the hook - no longer physically attached to a hook (literal sense)
  • Antonym: on the hook - responsible or liable for something
