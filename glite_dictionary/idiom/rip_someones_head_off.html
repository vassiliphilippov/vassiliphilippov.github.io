to rip someone's head off

1. VERB

/ rɪp sʌmwʌnz hɛd ɑf / (AmE), / rɪp sʌmwʌnz hɛd ɒf / (BrE)
to react to someone with extreme anger, especially by shouting or threatening them
react with great anger

Examples

If my boss finds out about the mistake, she’ll rip my head off.
You don’t need to rip his head off just because he was late.
When the children broke the window, their mother nearly ripped their heads off.

Synset Examples

He warned his colleague not to rip his head off for a small mistake.
Don't yell at me or I'll rip your head off!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to blow up - to suddenly lose one's temper
  • Antonym: to calm down - to become less angry or upset
