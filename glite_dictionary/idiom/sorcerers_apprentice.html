sorcerer's apprentice

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔrsərərz əˈprɛntɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɔːsərəz əˈprɛntɪs / (BrE)
a person who loses control of a situation they have created, often due to inexperience or incompetence, leading to chaos or unintended consequences
person overwhelmed by their own actions

Examples

When the computer started crashing, I felt like a sorcerer's apprentice overwhelmed by my own system changes.
The new manager became a sorcerer's apprentice when his reforms caused confusion among the staff.
Her attempts to fix the plumbing turned her into a sorcerer's apprentice, making the problem much worse.

Synset Examples

She joked about being a sorcerer's apprentice in the kitchen.
He felt like a sorcerer's apprentice with all his gadgets.
The project turned chaotic, like a sorcerer's apprentice gone wrong.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novice - a person new to a situation or job
  • Antonym: master - a person who has control or authority over something

Etymology

From 'The Sorcerer's Apprentice,' a story popularized by Goethe in 1797 and later by Disney's 1940 film 'Fantasia.'
