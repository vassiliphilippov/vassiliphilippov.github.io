wrong turn at Albuquerque

1. NOUN

/ rɔŋ tɝn æt ˌæl.bɚˈkɝː.ki / (AmE), / rɒŋ tɜːn ət ˌæl.bəˈkɜː.ki / (BrE)
a significant mistake or misstep, especially one that causes a major change in direction or circumstances
significant mistake or misstep
informal american

Examples

Many people feel they've taken a wrong turn at Albuquerque when their career doesn't go as planned.
After the bad investment, Tim joked that he must have made a wrong turn at Albuquerque.
She blamed her failed road trip on that wrong turn at Albuquerque along the interstate.

Synset Examples

She realized she had taken a wrong turn at Albuquerque when the scenery changed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunder - a careless or serious mistake
  • Synonym: mistake - an action or judgment that is wrong

Etymology

Popularized by Bugs Bunny cartoons (Warner Bros., mid-20th century), where Bugs says he "should have taken a left turn at Albuquerque."
