double Dutch

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʌbəl ˈdʌtʃ / (AmE), / ˌdʌbəl ˈdʌtʃ / (BrE)
speech or writing that is impossible to understand; gibberish.
unintelligible speech or writing

Examples

His explanation of the new policy was just double Dutch to me.
The instructions were double Dutch, and I couldn't follow a word.
She listened politely, though the technical jargon sounded like double Dutch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gibberish - unintelligible or meaningless speech or writing

Etymology

Probably from the pejorative use of 'Dutch' in English slang, reflecting historical rivalry.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʌbəl ˈdʌtʃ / (AmE), / ˌdʌbəl ˈdʌtʃ / (BrE)
language or speech that is impossible to understand; gibberish.
unintelligible or confusing language

Examples

The technical jargon in the manual was pure double Dutch to me.
Whenever he tries to explain quantum physics, it all sounds like double Dutch.
She handed me the report, but most of it looked like double Dutch and made no sense.

Synset Examples

The instructions were so complicated, it felt like double Dutch.
I can't understand his explanation; it sounds like double Dutch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gibberish - meaningless or unintelligible speech or writing
  • Synonym: nonsense - speech or writing that has no meaning or makes no sense

Etymology

Originally a pejorative British term (17th-18th century) referring to Dutch language or customs as confusing or incomprehensible.
