where it's been

1. X

/ wɛɚ ɪts bɪn / (AmE), / wɛə ɪts bɪn / (BrE)
used to refer to the unknown or possibly dubious history or condition of something, often implying it may have been in unsanitary or undesirable circumstances
unknown history or condition (idiomatic expression)

Examples

I wouldn't touch that sandwich; you don't know where it's been.
She won't buy used phones because she's unsure where it's been.
When he handed me the old book, I wondered where it's been all these years.

Synset Examples

Be careful with that money; you don't know where it's been.
