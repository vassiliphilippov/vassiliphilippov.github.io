to move through the gears

1. VERB

/ muːv θruː ðə ɡɪrz / (AmE), / muːv θruː ðə ɡɪəz / (BrE)
(of a person) to progress smoothly through different stages or tasks, often becoming more effective or increasing the pace as one gains confidence or experience.
progress smoothly through stages or tasks

Examples

The team began to move through the gears as they adjusted to the new project.
Once she found her confidence, she could move through the gears with ease.
With each milestone, he managed to move through the gears more quickly.

Synset Examples

As she gained confidence, she began to move through the gears faster.
With practice, he learned to move through the gears smoothly.
He can move through the gears of this project effortlessly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get into gear - to start working effectively and with energy
  • Synonym: hit your stride - to reach your optimal working pace

Etymology

Metaphor from driving vehicles, where shifting gears increases speed or changes performance.
