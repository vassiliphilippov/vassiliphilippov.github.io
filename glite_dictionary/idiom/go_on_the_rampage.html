to go on the rampage

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ɑn ðə ˈræmˌpeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɒn ðə ˈræmpeɪdʒ / (BrE)
to behave in a violent, uncontrolled, or destructive way
behave violently or uncontrollably

Examples

Rioting fans went on the rampage after their team lost the final match.
The angry protesters went on the rampage, smashing windows and overturning cars.
After discovering the broken vase, her little brother went on the rampage through the whole house.

Synset Examples

He went on the rampage after losing his job.
The lion was on the rampage, frightening the villagers.
The kids were on the rampage after their snacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to run amok - to behave uncontrollably and disruptively
