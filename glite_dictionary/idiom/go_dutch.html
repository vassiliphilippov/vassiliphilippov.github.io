to go Dutch

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ dʌtʃ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ dʌtʃ / (BrE)
to agree that each person pays for themselves, especially when eating out
share costs equally (esp. at a meal)

Examples

Whenever we eat out, we go Dutch to keep things simple.
On their first date, Emily suggested they go Dutch rather than one person paying.
It's fairer to go Dutch when no one wants to impose.

Synset Examples

At dinner, we decided to go Dutch on the bill.
It’s common to go Dutch when you’re just friends.
They agreed to go Dutch for their first date.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: split the bill - to divide the cost of something equally between people

Etymology

Possibly from the stereotype of Dutch frugality; attested in English since the late 19th century.
