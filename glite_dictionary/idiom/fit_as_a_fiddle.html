fit as a fiddle

1. ADJ

/ fɪt əz ə ˈfɪd.əl / (AmE), / fɪt əz ə ˈfɪd.əl / (BrE)
in very good or excellent health; physically well and energetic
in excellent health

Examples

After recovering from his surgery, he was fit as a fiddle and ready to travel again.
My grandmother is ninety but still fit as a fiddle; she walks every morning.
Regular exercise helps me stay fit as a fiddle throughout the year.

Synset Examples

He exercises daily and feels fit as a fiddle.
Despite his age, he's still fit as a fiddle.
After her vacation, she returned fit as a fiddle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hale and hearty - strong and healthy, especially for an old person
  • Synonym: in fine fettle - in very good health or condition

Etymology

Originates from 17th-century English, where "fiddle" referred to something in good condition; "fit" meaning suitable or healthy.
