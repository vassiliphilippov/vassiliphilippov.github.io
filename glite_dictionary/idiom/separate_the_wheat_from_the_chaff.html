to separate the wheat from the chaff

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛpəˌreɪt ðə wiːt frəm ðə tʃæf / (AmE), / ˈsɛpəˌreɪt ðiː wiːt frəm ðə tʃɑːf / (BrE)
to distinguish valuable or useful things or people from those that are of no value or use
distinguish valuable from worthless

Examples

At the audition, the director must separate the wheat from the chaff to find the best actors.
Investors try to separate the wheat from the chaff when choosing companies to support.
Before publishing the report, she needs to separate the wheat from the chaff among all the collected data.

Synset Examples

It's time to separate the wheat from the chaff in our hiring process.
He quickly separated the wheat from the chaff in his project.
We need to separate the wheat from the chaff in this project.
The teacher helped us separate the wheat from the chaff in our research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sort the sheep from the goats - to separate people or things into two groups, those of high quality and those of low quality
  • Synonym: to distinguish - to recognize or treat as different; perceive clearly

Etymology

From Biblical imagery referencing separating edible wheat grain from inedible chaff after harvest.
