bad apple

1. NOUN

/ bæd ˈæpəl / (AmE), / bad ˈæpəl / (BrE)
a person who has a harmful or negative influence on others within a group
troublemaker in a group

Examples

The coach warned the team not to let a bad apple ruin their season.
She was worried that one bad apple in the class would disrupt the whole year.
If you don't address a bad apple quickly, their behavior could spread.

Synset Examples

Don't listen to him; he's a bad apple.
That bad apple was always causing trouble.
One bad apple can spoil the whole bunch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: troublemaker - a person who habitually causes trouble

Etymology

From the proverb "one bad apple spoils the whole bunch," reflecting the idea that one unruly person can influence others.
