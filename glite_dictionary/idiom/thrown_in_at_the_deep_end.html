thrown in at the deep end

1. X

/ ˈθroʊn ɪn æt ðə ˈdiːp ɛnd / (AmE), / ˈθrəʊn ɪn æt ðə ˈdiːp end / (BrE)
placed in a difficult or challenging situation suddenly, without preparation or support
suddenly put in a difficult situation

Examples

When the manager left unexpectedly, I was thrown in at the deep end and had to take over the project.
Many new teachers feel thrown in at the deep end during their first week in the classroom.
He was thrown in at the deep end when asked to present to the entire company on his first day.

Synset Examples

I was thrown in at the deep end at my new job.
She learned quickly after being thrown in at the deep end.
She felt thrown in at the deep end during training.
He was thrown in at the deep end on his first day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: out of one's depth - in a situation too difficult to handle
