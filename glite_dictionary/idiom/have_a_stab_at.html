to have a stab at

1. VERB

/ hæv ə stæb æt / (AmE), / hæv ə stæb æt / (BrE)
to try or attempt to do something, especially when not certain of success
attempt something
informal

Examples

After hearing about the contest, she decided to have a stab at painting a landscape.
I’ll have a stab at answering your question, though I’m not sure of the answer.
Anyone interested can have a stab at solving the riddle displayed on the board.

Synset Examples

She decided to have a stab at writing a novel.
I'll have a stab at fixing the car myself.
Let's have a stab at solving this puzzle together.
I’ll take a stab at solving this puzzle.
Can you take a stab at answering this question?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: try - make an attempt or effort to do something
  • Synonym: to have a go at - attempt to do something, especially something new or difficult
