to run with scissors

1. VERB

/ rʌn wɪð ˈsɪsərz / (AmE), / rʌn wɪð ˈsɪzəz / (BrE)
to engage in reckless or dangerous behavior, especially without regard for the possible consequences
act recklessly or dangerously

Examples

Ignoring the expert’s advice, he decided to run with scissors by investing all his money in one risky stock.
I know it sounds exciting, but starting a business without a plan is just running with scissors.
When you drive that fast in the rain, you're basically running with scissors.

Synset Examples

Don't run with scissors; it's dangerous!
My mom always warned me not to run with scissors.
Children should never be allowed to run with scissors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: act rashly - to act without careful consideration
  • Antonym: to play it safe - to act cautiously
