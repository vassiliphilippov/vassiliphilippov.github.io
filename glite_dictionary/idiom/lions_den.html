lion's den

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪənz ˌdɛn / (AmE), / ˈlaɪənz ˌdɛn / (BrE)
a situation or place where someone faces great danger, hostility, or discomfort
dangerous or hostile situation

Examples

She knew giving her honest opinion would be like walking into a lion's den.
The journalist entered the political lion's den to ask tough questions.
Facing the panel of examiners felt like being thrown into the lion's den.

Synset Examples

Entering the lion's den was a risky move.
He felt like he was in the lion's den during the meeting.
Surviving the lion's den of critics was challenging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minefield - a situation presenting many dangers or difficulties
  • Antonym: safe haven - a safe or peaceful place, opposite of a dangerous environment

Etymology

From the biblical story of Daniel in the lion's den; the phrase has come to symbolize facing danger or adversity.
