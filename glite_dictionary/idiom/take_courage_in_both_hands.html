to take courage in both hands

1. VERB

/ teɪk ˈkɝː.ɪdʒ ɪn boʊθ hændz / (AmE), / teɪk ˈkʌr.ɪdʒ ɪn bəʊθ hændz / (BrE)
to deliberately gather all one's courage in order to do something difficult or face a challenge
summon one's courage to act

Examples

She took courage in both hands and told her family the truth.
He took courage in both hands before stepping onto the stage for the first time.
After hesitating, she finally took courage in both hands and applied for the job.

Synset Examples

She took her courage in both hands and spoke up.
He needed to take his courage in both hands to confront his boss.
Taking his courage in both hands, he faced the crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pluck up courage - to force oneself to be brave enough to do something
  • Antonym: lose one's nerve - to suddenly become too afraid to do something
