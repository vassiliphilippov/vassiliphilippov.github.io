to play fast and loose

1. VERB

/ pleɪ fæst ənd luːs / (AmE), / pleɪ fɑːst ənd luːs / (BrE)
to behave in a careless or irresponsible way, especially by not considering the rules or others' feelings
to act irresponsibly or carelessly

Examples

You can't expect people to trust you if you play fast and loose with the truth.
The politician was accused of playing fast and loose with the public's trust.
They lost their jobs after playing fast and loose with company funds.

Synset Examples

He tends to play fast and loose with the rules.
You can't play fast and loose if you want to succeed.
She has been playing fast and loose with her finances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: act recklessly - to behave in a reckless manner
  • Antonym: act responsibly - to behave in a responsible way

Etymology

From an old cheating game called 'fast and loose' played at fairs, giving rise to the figurative use.
