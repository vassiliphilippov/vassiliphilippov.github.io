to go into business for oneself

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈɪntuː ˈbɪznəs fəɹ wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈɪntuː ˈbɪznɪs fə wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to start and run one's own business instead of working for someone else
start one's own business

Examples

After years of working for others, Sarah decided to go into business for herself and opened a bakery.
Many people dream of going into business for themselves to have more independence and control over their work.
He saved enough money to finally go into business for himself last year, launching a consulting firm.

Synset Examples

He decided to go into business for himself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: start a business - begin a commercial enterprise
  • Antonym: be employed - work for someone else or a company
