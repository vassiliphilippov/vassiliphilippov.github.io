to give the heave-ho

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ðə ˌhiv ˈhoʊ / (AmE), / ɡɪv ðə ˌhiːv ˈhəʊ / (BrE)
to dismiss or get rid of someone or something, often abruptly or unceremoniously.
dismiss or get rid of (someone or something)
informal

Examples

After missing several deadlines, the manager decided to give the heave-ho to the unreliable employee.
When their old car broke down again, they finally gave the heave-ho to it and bought a new one.
The voters gave the heave-ho to the incumbent mayor in the latest election.

Synset Examples

The company decided to give him the heave-ho after his poor performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire - to dismiss someone from employment
  • Synonym: sack - to remove someone from a job
  • Antonym: hire - to employ someone (opposite of dismiss)

Etymology

From 'heave-ho', a nautical cry used when sailors worked together to haul or throw something, later used figuratively for discarding.
