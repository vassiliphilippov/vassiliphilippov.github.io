on a collision course

1. X

/ ɑn ə kəˈlɪʒən kɔɹs / (AmE), / ɒn ə kəˈlɪʒən kɔːs / (BrE)
likely to result in conflict, disaster, or failure if current actions continue
heading toward inevitable conflict or disaster

Examples

His aggressive tactics put the company on a collision course with regulators.
She realized the project was on a collision course with failure if changes were not made.
Their stubborn attitudes placed them on a collision course with management.

Synset Examples

Their plans were on a collision course with reality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: headed for disaster - likely to end in disaster
  • Antonym: on track - proceeding as planned or toward a positive outcome
