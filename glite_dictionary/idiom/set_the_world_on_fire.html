to set the world on fire

1. VERB

/ sɛt ðə wɝld ɑn ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / sɛt ðə wɜːld ɒn ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
to achieve outstanding success or have a major, noticeable impact
achieve great success or impact

Examples

The young scientist's discovery could set the world on fire if proven true.
While his first book was good, it didn't set the world on fire.
She hopes her new technology will set the world on fire within the next decade.

Synset Examples

The new artist is ready to set the world on fire.
His ideas are ambitious enough to set the world on fire.
Her talent will set the world on fire one day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make a splash - to attract a lot of attention or cause a sensation
  • Synonym: to leave a mark - to have a lasting effect or influence
  • Antonym: fade into obscurity - to become unnoticeable or forgotten
