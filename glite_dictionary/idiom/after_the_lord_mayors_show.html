after the Lord Mayor's show

1. X

/ ˈæf.tɚ ðə lɔrd ˈmeɪ.ɚz ʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɑːf.tə ðə lɔːd ˈmeə.z ˌʃəʊ / (BrE)
used to describe a reduction in excitement or activity that follows a major event or celebration.
lull after excitement or event
british

Examples

The atmosphere at work this week has been quiet, almost like after the Lord Mayor's show.
The excitement of graduation faded, and it felt very much after the Lord Mayor's show.
The festival was followed by a dull Monday, as always happens after the Lord Mayor's show.

Synset Examples

The excitement faded quickly, as it always does after the Lord Mayor's show.
The crowd dispersed, like after the Lord Mayor's show.
The event was quiet, just like after the Lord Mayor's show.

Etymology

From the Lord Mayor's Show in London, a major public event followed by a noticeable decline in public excitement.
