to pull out all the stops

1. VERB

/ pʊl aʊt ɔl ðə stɑps / (AmE), / pʊl aʊt ɔːl ðə stɒps / (BrE)
to make every possible effort; to use all available resources in order to achieve something
make every possible effort

Examples

They pulled out all the stops to ensure the concert was a huge success.
For her birthday party, we pulled out all the stops and hired a live band.
When it came to fundraising, the committee really pulled out all the stops.

Synset Examples

The organizers pulled out all the stops for the centennial meeting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go all out - to make a maximum effort

Etymology

From the controls (stops) of a pipe organ, where pulling out all stops allows the instrument to play at full volume.

2. VERB

/ pʊl aʊt ɔl ðə stɑps / (AmE), / pʊl aʊt ɔːl ðə stɒps / (BrE)
to do everything possible to achieve a desired result or make something as successful as possible
do everything possible for success

Examples

They decided to pull out all the stops for their wedding, leaving nothing to chance.
When it came to the final match, the coach told the players to pull out all the stops.
The company pulled out all the stops to ensure the product launch was a resounding success.

Synset Examples

For her birthday, they pulled out all the stops for the party.
He pulled out all the stops to impress the clients.
The team pulled out all the stops to win the championship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go all out - to make the maximum possible effort

Etymology

Originally from the practice of pulling out all the stops on a pipe organ to make it as loud as possible.
