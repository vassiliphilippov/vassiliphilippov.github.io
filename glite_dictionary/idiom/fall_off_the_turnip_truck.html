to fall off the turnip truck

1. VERB

/ fɔl ɑf ðə ˈtɝnəp ˌtrʌk / (AmE), / fɔːl ɒf ðə ˈtɜːnɪp ˌtrʌk / (BrE)
to be naive or inexperienced, especially in a way that makes someone easy to fool; often used to suggest that a person is not as gullible as someone might think
to be naive or easily fooled
informal american

Examples

He may act clueless, but he didn't just fall off the turnip truck.
Don't treat me as if I fell off the turnip truck—I've been around.
She might seem shy, but she certainly didn't fall off the turnip truck yesterday.

Synset Examples

He thinks he's clever, but he didn't just fall off the turnip truck.
She may seem naive, but she didn’t just fall off the turnip truck.
Don't treat me like I just fell off the turnip truck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gullible - easily fooled or deceived
  • Antonym: streetwise - experienced and aware of how things work in the real world

Etymology

Refers to rural imagery where turnip trucks would bring inexperienced workers, implying naivety.
