a little bird

1. NOUN

/ ə ˈlɪɾl̩ bɝd / (AmE), / ə ˈlɪtl̩ bɜːd / (BrE)
used to indicate that the speaker has received information from a secret or unidentified source
secret or unidentified source of information

Examples

A little bird told me you have some exciting news to share.
I'm not saying who, but a little bird mentioned your surprise party.
A little bird informed me that the meeting has been canceled.

Synset Examples

I heard from a little bird that you got promoted.
