old salt

1. NOUN

/ oʊld sɔlt / (AmE), / əʊld sɒlt / (BrE)
an experienced and often elderly sailor, typically one with many stories of the sea
experienced or elderly sailor

Examples

The old salt entertained us with tales of storms and distant ports.
Every crew has at least one old salt who knows every trick in the book.
That old salt could navigate by the stars long before GPS existed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mariner - a sailor; a person who navigates or assists in navigating a ship
  • Antonym: landlubber - a person unfamiliar with the sea or sailing

Etymology

From 'salt,' an informal term for sailor, combined with 'old' to suggest experience and age.

2. NOUN

/ oʊld sɔlt / (AmE), / əʊld sɒlt / (BrE)
an experienced and seasoned sailor, especially one who has spent many years at sea
experienced sailor

Examples

Growing up in a coastal town, he always admired the old salt who could predict the weather at a glance.
The old salt entertained the young crew with incredible stories of his adventures on the high seas.
Everyone respected the old salt for his wisdom and unmatched knowledge of the ocean.

Synset Examples

He’s an old salt, having spent decades at sea.
The old salt shared tales of storms and shipwrecks.
As an old salt, he knew every trick of the trade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea dog - an experienced sailor
