why not both

1. X

/ waɪ nɑt boʊθ / (AmE), / waɪ nɒt bəʊθ / (BrE)
used to suggest that more than one option or possibility should be considered or chosen instead of just one
suggesting choosing more than one option

Examples

When asked whether to travel by train or plane, she replied, 'Why not both?'
Faced with two appealing desserts, he smiled and said, 'Why not both?' before ordering them.
The committee debated investing in marketing or research, but someone suggested, 'Why not both?'

Synset Examples

In a debate over two options, he said, 'Why not both?'
