a few sandwiches short of a picnic

1. ADJ

/ ə fjuː ˈsændwɪtʃɪz ʃɔrt əv ə ˈpɪknɪk / (AmE), / ə fjuː ˈsænwɪʤɪz ʃɔːt əv ə ˈpɪknɪk / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly British) Used to describe someone who is not very intelligent or sensible.
not very bright or sensible (idiom)
informal british

Examples

Everyone knows that Mark is a few sandwiches short of a picnic, but he means well.
She came up with an idea that was a few sandwiches short of a picnic.
Sometimes I wonder if he's a few sandwiches short of a picnic after hearing his theories.

Synset Examples

That idea seems a few sandwiches short of a picnic.
She’s a few sandwiches short of a picnic today.
He’s definitely a few sandwiches short of a picnic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: not the sharpest tool in the shed - not very intelligent (idiom)
  • Antonym: bright - intelligent or clever
