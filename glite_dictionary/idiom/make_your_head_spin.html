to make your head spin

1. VERB

/ meɪk jɔr hɛd spɪn / (AmE), / meɪk jɔː hɛd spɪn / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel overwhelmed or confused, often due to excessive information or rapid activity
overwhelm or confuse someone

Examples

The complexity of the new tax laws can make your head spin.
Trying to keep up with all the latest technology developments will make your head spin.
All of the sudden changes at work might make your head spin if you're not prepared.

Synset Examples

The number of choices can make your head spin.
All the information available can make your head spin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bewilder - to confuse or puzzle completely
  • Antonym: to clarify - to make clear or less confused
