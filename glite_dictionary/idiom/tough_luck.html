tough luck

1. NOUN

/ tʌf lʌk / (AmE), / tʌf lʌk / (BrE)
used to express sympathy or, sometimes, lack of sympathy for someone who has experienced misfortune or an unlucky situation
expression of sympathy for bad luck

Examples

When he missed the train by just two minutes, all she could say was, "Tough luck."
If you forgot your ticket, that's tough luck—no exceptions allowed.
They tried their best, but a sudden storm cost them the game. Tough luck!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bad luck - unfortunate circumstance or result
  • Antonym: good luck - fortunate or favorable circumstance or result
