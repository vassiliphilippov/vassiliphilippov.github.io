by the book

1. ADV

/ baɪ ðə bʊk / (AmE), / baɪ ðə bʊk / (BrE)
strictly according to established rules, guidelines, or procedures; adhering exactly to regulations or instructions
strictly following rules or procedures

Examples

The inspector insisted that every procedure be done by the book to ensure fairness.
When it comes to tax returns, she prefers to do everything by the book.
If you handle this by the book, there won't be any legal issues.

Synset Examples

He always follows procedures by the book.
We have to do this by the book to avoid penalties.
To ensure safety, we operate by the book.
The lawyer approached the case by-the-book to avoid any complications.
She always does her work by-the-book, ensuring everything is perfect.
He prefers to play by-the-book rather than take risks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: by-the-book - following rules strictly (hyphenated, used as an adjective)
