out of one's own pocket

1. ADV

/ aʊt əv wʌnz oʊn ˈpɑkɪt / (AmE), / aʊt əv wʌnz əʊn ˈpɒkɪt / (BrE)
using one's own money to pay for something rather than receiving reimbursement or funding from another source
using one's own money

Examples

Despite the lack of funding, she paid for all the materials out of her own pocket.
The teacher often buys supplies for her classroom out of his own pocket.
Some volunteers travel to remote locations out of their own pockets to help those in need.

Synset Examples

She funded the project out of her own pocket.
He paid for the repairs out of his own pocket.
They covered the costs out of their own pockets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pay out of pocket - to pay for something oneself
