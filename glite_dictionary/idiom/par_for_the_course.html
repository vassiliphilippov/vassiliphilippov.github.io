par for the course

1. NOUN

/ pɑɹ fəɹ ðə kɔɹs / (AmE), / pɑː fə ðə kɔːs / (BrE)
typical or to be expected in a particular situation; nothing unusual
typical or expected (idiom)

Examples

Delays in receiving official approval are par for the course when dealing with bureaucracy.
For a new parent, sleepless nights are par for the course.
Criticism from competitors is par for the course in this industry.

Synset Examples

The delays were par for the course on this project.
Unexpected challenges are par for the course in startups.
Missing deadlines is just par for the course here.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: normal - conforming to the usual, typical, or expected
  • Antonym: unusual - not usual or expected

Etymology

From golf, where 'par' is the number of strokes a good player is expected to take for a hole or course.
