to make a big thing out of

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə bɪɡ θɪŋ aʊt əv / (AmE), / meɪk ə bɪɡ θɪŋ aʊt ɒv / (BrE)
to treat something as more important or serious than it really is; to exaggerate the importance of something
exaggerate the importance of something

Examples

There's no need to make a big thing out of a simple mistake—everyone makes errors.
She tends to make a big thing out of minor inconveniences at work.
Let's not make a big thing out of his late arrival; it happens to everyone.

Synset Examples

I wish he wouldn't make a big thing out of my birthday.
She tends to make a big thing out of every little issue.
Don't make a big thing out of this mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exaggerate - to make something seem larger, better, or worse than it really is
  • Antonym: to minimize - to make something seem less important or serious than it is
