all hat and no cattle

1. X

/ ɑl hæt ənd noʊ ˈkæt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ɔːl hæt ənd nəʊ ˈkætl̩ / (BrE)
Pretending to be important or capable while lacking real ability, achievement, or substance.
boastful without substance
american

Examples

He's always bragging about his business, but he's all hat and no cattle.
Many politicians seem all hat and no cattle when it comes to delivering on promises.
She sounds impressive, but when it comes time to act, she's all hat and no cattle.

Synset Examples

His claims are just all hat and no cattle.
That proposal was all hat and no cattle.
She talks a big game, but it’s all hat and no cattle.
His tough talk is all hat and no cowboy.
He's all hat and no cowboy when it comes to action.
Don’t believe him; he’s just all hat and no cowboy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: all bark and no bite - someone who makes threats but never carries them out
  • Synonym: empty suit - a person regarded as lacking substance or ability

Etymology

American English; from the image of someone wearing a cowboy hat but not actually owning cattle, symbolizing a lack of real credentials.
