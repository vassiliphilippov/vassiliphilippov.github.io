straight from the horse's mouth

1. ADV

/ streɪt frəm ðə hɔrs maʊθ / (AmE), / streɪt frəm ðə hɔːs maʊθ / (BrE)
Directly from the original or most reliable source.
directly from the original source

Examples

I received the news straight from the horse's mouth, so I know it's accurate.
If you want the truth, you'll have to hear it straight from the horse's mouth.
She told everyone the story straight from the horse's mouth after speaking with the manager.

Synset Examples

I heard it straight from the horse's mouth.
He confirmed the rumor straight from the horse's mouth.
She got the news straight from the horse's mouth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firsthand - (of information) coming directly from personal experience or the original source

Etymology

Refers to information coming directly from a racehorse's mouth, implying the most authoritative source.
