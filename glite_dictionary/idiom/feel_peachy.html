to feel peachy

1. VERB

/ fil ˈpiːtʃi / (AmE), / fiːl ˈpiːtʃi / (BrE)
to feel very happy, content, or well
feel very happy or content
informal

Examples

After finishing her final exams, she began to feel peachy about her summer plans.
Despite the rain, he woke up and somehow managed to feel peachy all day.
You can't help but feel peachy after receiving such wonderful compliments.

Synset Examples

He felt peachy after receiving good news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feel great - to feel very good or excellent
  • Antonym: feel miserable - to feel very unhappy or uncomfortable
