to get through one's head

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt θruː wʌnz hɛd / (AmE), / ɡɛt θruː wʌnz hɛd / (BrE)
(informal) To finally accept or understand something, especially when it is difficult or unwelcome.
understand or accept something (idiom)
informal

Examples

No matter how many times I explain, he just can't get through his head that the deadline is tomorrow.
It finally got through her head that smoking was harming her health.
The coach shouted, hoping the new tactics would get through their heads before the game started.

Synset Examples

She couldn't get it through her skull that he was serious.
He struggles to get the concept through his head.
It took time for her to get through her head that she needed help.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sink in - to become understood or accepted
