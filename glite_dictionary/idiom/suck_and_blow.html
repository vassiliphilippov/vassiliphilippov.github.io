to suck and blow

1. VERB

/ sʌk ənd bloʊ / (AmE), / sʌk ənd bləʊ / (BrE)
to attempt to do two opposite or contradictory things at the same time
try contradictory actions simultaneously

Examples

You can't always suck and blow; eventually, you need to make a decision.
The company tried to suck and blow by supporting both sides of the debate.
It's impossible to suck and blow instead of choosing a clear course of action.

Synset Examples

Life isn't all about suck and blow; sometimes you have to choose.
You can't just suck and blow; you need to commit to one.
He was told to stop trying to suck and blow on both sides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to have it both ways - to try to get the benefits of two opposing things
  • Synonym: sit on the fence - to avoid making a decision or commitment
