over a barrel

1. X

/ ˌoʊvər ə ˈbærəl / (AmE), / ˌəʊvə ə ˈbærəl / (BrE)
in a situation where you are helpless or at someone's mercy, unable to act effectively or escape difficulty
in a helpless or difficult situation

Examples

After missing the deadline, he was over a barrel with his boss.
The mechanic had her over a barrel because she needed her car fixed immediately.
The negotiations left the company over a barrel and with few options.

Synset Examples

He felt over a barrel when they raised the price unexpectedly.
I couldn’t argue back; I was completely over a barrel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at someone's mercy - unable to act independently, under someone else's control
  • Synonym: in a bind - in a difficult situation
