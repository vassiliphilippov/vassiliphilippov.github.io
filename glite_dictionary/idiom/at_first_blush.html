at first blush

1. ADV

/ ət ˈfɝst blʌʃ / (AmE), / ət ˈfɜːst blʌʃ / (BrE)
used to indicate that something seems a certain way based on an initial impression, before closer examination
on first impression

Examples

At first blush, her explanation seemed perfectly reasonable, but later I noticed some inconsistencies.
The proposal looked promising at first blush, but the details raised several concerns.
He dismissed the idea at first blush, not realizing its potential benefits upon further review.

Synset Examples

at first blush the offer seemed attractive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at first glance - as a first impression or upon first looking

Etymology

From the use of 'blush' in the sense of a glance or quick look; dates to the 16th century.

2. ADV

/ ət ˈfɝst blʌʃ / (AmE), / ət ˈfɜːst blʌʃ / (BrE)
when something is considered for the first time or superficially, without deeper analysis
upon initial consideration

Examples

At first blush, the data seemed to support our hypothesis, but later analysis revealed flaws.
His explanation sounded reasonable at first blush, but the facts told a different story.
At first blush, the painting appeared simple, yet it held many hidden details.

Synset Examples

Her idea, at first blush, appeared impractical.
At first blush, things looked promising for the project.
At first blush, the proposal seems beneficial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at first glance - upon first consideration or visual impression

Etymology

Refers to the first glance or appearance of something; 'blush' in this sense means a glance or look, from Middle English.
