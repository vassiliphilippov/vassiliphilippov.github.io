to maintain the upper hand

1. VERB

/ meɪnˈteɪn ði ˈʌpɚ hænd / (AmE), / meɪnˈteɪn ði ˈʌpə hænd / (BrE)
to continue to have an advantage or control over someone or something
keep an advantage or control

Examples

Despite their efforts, the team could not maintain the upper hand against their strong opponents.
The company struggled to maintain the upper hand in a rapidly changing industry.
It's important to adapt quickly in negotiations in order to maintain the upper hand.

Synset Examples

They worked hard to maintain the upper hand in the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: have the upper hand - to be in a position of advantage or control
