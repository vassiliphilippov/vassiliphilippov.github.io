full circle

1. NOUN

/ fʊl ˈsɝkəl / (AmE), / fʊl ˈsɜːkl / (BrE)
having returned to the original point or situation, especially after a series of changes or developments
returning to the original point

Examples

After years abroad, he came full circle and moved back to his hometown.
The company has gone full circle, returning to its original business model.
Their conversation went full circle, ending where it had begun.

Synset Examples

And so, this is where we came in, full circle.
