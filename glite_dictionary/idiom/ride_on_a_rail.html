to ride on a rail

1. VERB

/ raɪd ɑn ə reɪl / (AmE), / raɪd ɒn ə reɪl / (BrE)
to be publicly punished or ridiculed, especially in a humiliating or ceremonial manner
to be publicly punished or ridiculed
archaic american

Examples

After the scandal broke, the mayor felt he might ride on a rail out of town.
If the employees found out about the decision, the boss could ride on a rail at the company meeting.
He joked that if he forgot their anniversary again, he would probably ride on a rail at home.

Synset Examples

He felt like he was going to ride on a rail after his mistakes.
If he keeps this up, he'll be the one to ride on a rail.
She knew she would ride on a rail if she didn't apologize.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tar and feather - to punish or humiliate publicly by covering with tar and feathers
  • Synonym: public disgrace - shame or humiliation before others

Etymology

From the 18th–19th century American punishment of forcing offenders to straddle a fence rail and parading them through town.
