you know nothing, Jon Snow

1. X

/ juː noʊ ˈnʌθɪŋ dʒɑn snoʊ / (AmE), / juː nəʊ ˈnʌθɪŋ dʒɒn snəʊ / (BrE)
used to indicate that someone is ignorant or unaware of an important fact or situation, often in a teasing or emphatic way
expression indicating ignorance

Examples

Whenever Mark tries to explain advanced physics, his friends tease him by saying, you know nothing, Jon Snow.
After failing to use the coffee machine, Linda laughed and said to herself, 'you know nothing, Jon Snow.'
When he guessed the plot twist incorrectly, his cousin jokingly told him, 'you know nothing, Jon Snow.''

Synset Examples

About that secret, you know nothing, Jon Snow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness in general or about something specific

Etymology

Popularized by the TV series Game of Thrones, where the character Ygritte frequently says this to Jon Snow.
