as stubborn as all get-out

1. ADJ

/ əz ˈstʌbɚn əz ɔl ˈɡɛt aʊt / (AmE), / əz ˈstʌbən əz ɔːl ˈɡɛt aʊt / (BrE)
extremely stubborn; very unwilling to change one’s opinion or attitude.
extremely stubborn (idiomatic)
informal american

Examples

My grandpa can be as stubborn as all get-out when it comes to his favorite routines.
Don’t bother arguing with her; she’s as stubborn as all get-out once she’s made up her mind.
The old mule was as stubborn as all get-out and refused to budge.

Synset Examples

He was as stubborn as all get-out about his decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stubborn - unwilling to change one's mind or attitude

Etymology

Originates from American colloquial expression 'all get-out', meaning to the greatest degree.
