to hold the bag

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ðə bæɡ / (AmE), / həʊld ðə bæɡ / (BrE)
to be left with the blame or responsibility for something, especially after others have avoided it or escaped involvement
be left with the blame or responsibility

Examples

After the rest of the team left early, I was the one holding the bag during the audit.
He denied any involvement, but she was still left holding the bag for the missing funds.
Whenever a project goes wrong, it seems like I'm always holding the bag at the end.

Synset Examples

She abandoned her responsibilities, leaving him holding the bag.
They all quit, leaving me holding the bag alone.
When the project failed, he left me holding the bag.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take the fall - to accept blame or punishment, especially in place of others
  • Antonym: pass the buck - to shift responsibility to someone else

Etymology

Possibly from 18th-century British criminal slang, referring to being left literally holding stolen goods while others fled.
