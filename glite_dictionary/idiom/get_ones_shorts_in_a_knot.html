to get one's shorts in a knot

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt wʌnz ʃɔrts ɪn ə nɑt / (AmE), / ɡɛt wʌnz ʃɔːts ɪn ə nɒt / (BrE)
to become upset, angry, or agitated, especially over something minor or trivial
become upset or agitated
informal american

Examples

Try not to get your shorts in a knot about the delay; everything will work out.
Every time something goes wrong, he tends to get his shorts in a knot.
Don't get your shorts in a knot—it's just a simple mistake.

Synset Examples
