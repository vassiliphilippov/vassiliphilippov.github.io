order of the boot

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər əv ðə buːt / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə əv ðə buːt / (BrE)
an act of being dismissed or expelled from a job or position; an order to leave or be fired
dismissal or expulsion from a position

Examples

After the audit uncovered fraud, the entire team got the order of the boot.
Anyone caught breaking the rules risks receiving the order of the boot.
Facing public outrage, the CEO was quietly given the order of the boot.

Synset Examples

After the scandal, he faced the order of the boot.
She received the order of the boot for her poor performance.
The manager issued an order of the boot to the lazy employee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the sack - Dismissal from employment
  • Synonym: dismissal - The act of removing someone from their job
  • Antonym: promotion - A move to a higher position at work
