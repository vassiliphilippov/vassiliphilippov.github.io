to be an ostrich

1. VERB

/ bi ən ˈɑstrɪtʃ / (AmE), / bi ən ˈɒstrɪtʃ / (BrE)
To deliberately ignore or refuse to acknowledge obvious problems or difficulties, especially by pretending they do not exist.
ignore problems deliberately

Examples

You can't just be an ostrich whenever challenges arise at work.
As much as I'd like to be an ostrich, I know I have to handle this debt.
If management continues to be an ostrich, the company's problems will only get worse.

Synset Examples

Don't be an ostrich; acknowledge the issues at hand.
Stop being an ostrich and face your problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bury one's head in the sand - To avoid or ignore obvious danger or problems

Etymology

From the misconception that ostriches bury their heads in the sand to avoid danger.
