to blow something out of the water

1. VERB

/ bloʊ ˈsʌmˌθɪŋ ˌaʊt əv ðə ˈwɔtər / (AmE), / bləʊ ˈsʌmθɪŋ ˌaʊt ɒv ðə ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
to completely surpass, outperform, or defeat something or someone by a large margin.
dramatically surpass or outperform

Examples

The new product blew everything else out of the water when it launched last year.
Her singing blew everything else out of the water at the competition.
He blew everything else out of the water with his innovative approach.

Synset Examples

Her performance blew everything else out of the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outshine - to be much better than someone or something
  • Synonym: to outclass - to be much better than; to be superior
  • Antonym: to fall behind - to not keep up with; to be overtaken by
