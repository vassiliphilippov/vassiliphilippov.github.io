to have a seat

1. VERB

/ hæv ə siːt / (AmE), / hæv ə siːt / (BrE)
used as a polite invitation for someone to sit down
polite invitation to sit down

Examples

When the interviewer arrived, she smiled and said, "Have a seat and we’ll begin shortly."
Guests are welcome to have a seat in the lounge until dinner is served.
He motioned to the empty chair and invited me to have a seat.

Synset Examples

Please, have a seat while you wait for your appointment.
Can you please have a seat and relax?
Feel free to take a seat while you wait.
Please take a seat, Your Highnessness.
Please take a seat while you wait.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take a seat - polite way to ask someone to sit down
  • Synonym: to sit down - to move from standing to sitting position
