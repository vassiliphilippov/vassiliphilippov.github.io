to bring it weak

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ ɪt wik / (AmE), / brɪŋ ɪt wiːk / (BrE)
to perform poorly or lack energy, effort, or strength, especially in a competitive or challenging situation
to perform inadequately or without effort
informal slang

Examples

If you bring it weak, you're not going to impress the judges.
They lost the game because they brought it weak in the second half.
Don't bring it weak to the meeting—show them what you can do.

Synset Examples

She told him not to bring it weak during the performance.
If you bring it weak, you'll lose every time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bring it - to give your best effort or performance
