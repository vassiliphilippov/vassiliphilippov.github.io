Mackem through and through

1. ADJ

/ ˈmækəm θru ənd θru / (AmE), / ˈmækəm θruː ənd θruː / (BrE)
Completely and thoroughly embodying the culture, identity, or characteristics of a Mackem (a person from Sunderland, UK).
fully embodying Mackem identity/culture
british

Examples

She’s a Mackem through and through, proud of her Sunderland heritage.
Everyone could tell he was Mackem through and through from his accent and football loyalties.
Despite living abroad, she remains Mackem through and through in her attitude and values.

Synset Examples

She’s a Mackem through and through, always representing her roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Geordie through and through - completely embodying the culture of a Geordie (Newcastle native)

Etymology

From "Mackem" (a nickname for a person from Sunderland, UK) + "through and through" (entirely, completely).
