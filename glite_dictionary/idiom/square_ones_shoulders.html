to square one's shoulders

1. VERB

/ skwɛr wʌnz ˈʃoʊldɚz / (AmE), / skwɛə wʌnz ˈʃəʊldəz / (BrE)
to stand or sit with shoulders pulled back, showing confidence or readiness to face something difficult
adopt a confident or determined posture

Examples

She took a deep breath, squared her shoulders, and walked into the interview room.
He squared his shoulders to face the difficult conversation with his boss.
Before giving their speeches, the students squared their shoulders in a show of confidence.

Synset Examples

It's time to square your shoulders and take responsibility.
He squared his shoulders before delivering the bad news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brace oneself - to prepare mentally or physically for something difficult
  • Antonym: lose one's nerve - to become too afraid to do something
