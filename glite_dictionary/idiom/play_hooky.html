to play hooky

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ˈhʊki / (AmE), / pleɪ ˈhʊki / (BrE)
to stay away from school or work without permission or a valid reason
skip school or work without permission
informal american

Examples

Tom decided to play hooky and spent the afternoon at the arcade instead of going to class.
She was caught playing hooky from work and faced disciplinary action from her boss.
Some students would rather play hooky than take a difficult test.

Synset Examples

The boy often plays hooky

Related Terms

  • Synonym: play truant - to stay away from school without permission

Etymology

American English, first recorded in the late 19th century; 'hooky' is probably an alteration of 'hook it', meaning to make off or run away.

2. VERB

/ pleɪ ˈhʊki / (AmE), / pleɪ ˈhʊki / (BrE)
to stay away from school or work without permission, usually pretending to be ill or otherwise excused
skip school or work without permission
informal

Examples

She decided to play hooky on Friday and spend the day at the amusement park instead.
When I was a teenager, I used to play hooky whenever there was a big math test.
A group of employees tried to play hooky during the hot summer afternoon.

Synset Examples

They often play hooky to enjoy the beach.
I used to play hooky on sunny Fridays.
He decided to play hooky from school today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skip school - to be absent from school without permission
  • Synonym: truancy - the act of staying away from school without good reason

Etymology

Possibly from the Dutch word "hoekje" (hide-and-seek).
