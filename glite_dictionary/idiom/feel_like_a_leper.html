to feel like a leper

1. VERB

/ fil laɪk ə ˈlɛpər / (AmE), / fiːl laɪk ə ˈlɛpə / (BrE)
to feel as though one is avoided, excluded, or rejected by others, as a leper would be
to feel excluded or rejected

Examples

After the embarrassing incident, she felt like a leper whenever she entered the room.
Ever since his colleagues found out about his mistake, he has felt like a leper at work.
It's hard not to feel like a leper when everyone ignores you at a party.

Synset Examples

He felt like a leper after being shunned by his friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostracized - excluded from a group or society
  • Synonym: alienated - made to feel isolated or estranged

Etymology

Refers to the historical isolation of people with leprosy, who were often shunned by society.
