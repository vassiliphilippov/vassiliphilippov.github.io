to ride the wave

1. VERB

/ raɪd ðə weɪv / (AmE), / raɪd ðə weɪv / (BrE)
to take advantage of a growing trend, movement, or opportunity while it lasts
take advantage of a rising trend or opportunity

Examples

The startup hopes to ride the wave of interest in renewable energy.
Many artists are trying to ride the wave of social media trends to gain exposure.
She managed to ride the wave of public support to push her project forward.

Synset Examples

The team is excited to ride the wave of innovation.
He decided to ride the wave of success from his last project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go with the flow - to accept things as they happen and not try to change or control them
  • Synonym: to seize the opportunity - to act quickly to take advantage of a chance
