to bring to heel

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ tə hiːl / (AmE), / brɪŋ tə hiːl / (BrE)
to force someone or something to obey or submit, especially after resistance or unruliness
force obedience or submission

Examples

The government introduced strict regulations to bring to heel the financial sector.
After several warnings, the manager finally brought to heel the disruptive employees.
It took months of training to bring to heel the energetic puppy.

Synset Examples

The trainer was able to bring the unruly dog to heel.
The new policies aim to bring the industry to heel.
The coach worked to bring the team to heel after their poor performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to subdue - to bring under control, especially by force
  • Synonym: control - to exercise restraint or direction over
  • Antonym: allow free rein - to allow someone to act freely, the opposite of bringing to heel

Etymology

From the practice in dog training of commanding a dog to walk beside the handler's heel.
