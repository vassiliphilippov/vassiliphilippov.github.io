to put the genie back in the bottle

1. VERB

/ pʊt ðə ˈdʒini bæk ɪn ðə ˈbɑɾl̩ / (AmE), / pʊt ðə ˈdʒiːni bæk ɪn ðə ˈbɒtl̩ / (BrE)
to try to reverse an irreversible action or situation, especially after revealing a secret or information that cannot be concealed again
attempt to reverse the irreversible

Examples

After the data was leaked, it was impossible to put the genie back in the bottle.
Now that the legislation is in place, politicians can't put the genie back in the bottle.
She realized too late that sending the email meant she could not put the genie back in the bottle.

Synset Examples

We can't put the genie back in the bottle after that announcement.
Once you tell a secret, you can't put the genie back in the bottle.
Trying to fix the mistake is like putting the genie back in the bottle.

Etymology

Based on the folk tale of a genie released from a bottle who cannot easily be put back in; popularized in modern English from Middle Eastern folklore (notably in 'One Thousand and One Nights').
