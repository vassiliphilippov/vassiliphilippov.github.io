knowledge is power

1. X

/ ˈnɑlɪdʒ ɪz ˈpaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈnɒlɪdʒ ɪz ˈpaʊə / (BrE)
knowing things gives a person the ability or advantage to succeed or influence others
being informed gives you an advantage

Examples

She always reminds her students that knowledge is power when they doubt the value of studying.
In the business world, knowledge is power; staying informed can give companies a competitive edge.
He learned the hard way that knowledge is power and ignorance can be dangerous.

Synset Examples

He believes that knowledge is power; the more you know, the more confident you are.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ignorance is bliss - not knowing about something can make you happier

Etymology

The phrase has been attributed to Sir Francis Bacon (1597).
