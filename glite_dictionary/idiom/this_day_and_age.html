this day and age

1. NOUN

/ ðɪs deɪ ənd eɪdʒ / (AmE), / ðɪs deɪ ənd eɪdʒ / (BrE)
used to refer to the present time or the current era, especially when making a comparison with the past
the present time or era

Examples

It's surprising that people still use typewriters in this day and age.
You'd think in this day and age that internet access would be universal.
In this day and age, information travels faster than ever before.

Synset Examples

This day and age demands quick adaptability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nowadays - at the present time; in these days
  • Synonym: these days - currently; at the present time
  • Antonym: olden days - a period in the past
