which way the wind is blowing

1. X

/ wɪtʃ weɪ ðə wɪnd ɪz ˈbloʊɪŋ / (AmE), / wɪtʃ weɪ ðə wɪnd ɪz ˈbləʊɪŋ / (BrE)
to assess or understand the current state of affairs or the prevailing opinion before acting
assess current situation/opinion

Examples

Before making any major changes at work, it's wise to find out which way the wind is blowing.
Politicians always want to see which way the wind is blowing before taking a stand on controversial issues.
She waited to determine which way the wind was blowing before voicing her opinion in the meeting.

Synset Examples

Before making decisions, check which way the wind is blowing.
I need to see which way the wind is blowing first.
It’s wise to know which way the wind is blowing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sense the atmosphere - to perceive the mood of a situation
  • Synonym: stick one's finger in the wind - to try to estimate opinion or the way things are going
