to break the back of

1. VERB

/ breɪk ðə bæk ʌv / (AmE), / breɪk ðə bæk ɒv / (BrE)
to complete the most difficult or important part of a task, making the rest much easier
do the hardest part of a task

Examples

After months of effort, the team finally broke the back of the renovation project.
Once we break the back of the paperwork, the process will become much smoother.
She stayed late at work to break the back of her assignments before the weekend.

Synset Examples

We need to break the back of this project by Friday.
They worked late to break the back of the backlog.
To succeed, we must break the back of our toughest challenges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overcome - to succeed in dealing with a problem or difficulty
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop trying to do something difficult
