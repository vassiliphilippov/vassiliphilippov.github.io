to return the favor

1. VERB

/ rɪˈtɝn ðə ˈfeɪvɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈtɜːn ðə ˈfeɪvə / (BrE)
to do something nice or helpful for someone who has previously done something for you
repay a kindness or favor

Examples

I'll be happy to return the favor if you ever need a ride somewhere.
After years of mentoring, she was glad to have the chance to return the favor.
He offered to fix my computer, so I'll return the favor by treating him to lunch.

Synset Examples

He agreed to help me on a promise to return the favor.
If she cooks dinner, I'll return the favor with dessert.
He promised to return the favor after I lent him money.
I helped her move, so she'll return the favor soon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reciprocate - to respond to a gesture or action by making a corresponding one
