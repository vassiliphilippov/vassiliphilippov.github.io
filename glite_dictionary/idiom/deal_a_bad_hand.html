to deal a bad hand

1. VERB

/ diːl ə bæd hænd / (AmE), / diːl ə bæd hænd / (BrE)
to cause someone to be in an unfavorable or difficult situation, often due to chance or circumstances beyond their control
to put in an unfavorable situation

Examples

Many people feel that society has dealt them a bad hand through no fault of their own.
When economic downturn hit, the town was dealt a bad hand and struggled to recover.
Even though he was dealt a bad hand early in life, he persevered and achieved success.

Synset Examples

Life can sometimes deal a bad hand unexpectedly.
They learned to cope when life dealt a bad hand.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to deal a good hand - to put someone in a favorable situation

Etymology

From the metaphor of card games, where being dealt a bad hand means receiving poor cards.
