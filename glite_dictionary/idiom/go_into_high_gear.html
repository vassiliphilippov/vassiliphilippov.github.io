to go into high gear

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈɪntu haɪ ɡɪr / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈɪntuː haɪ ɡɪə / (BrE)
to start working faster, more intensely, or more effectively; to increase speed or effort
increase speed or intensity of activity

Examples

As soon as the company received the large order, production went into high gear.
When the storm warning was issued, emergency crews went into high gear to prepare the city.
Final exams are next week, so my studies have gone into high gear.

Synset Examples

The team went into high gear as the deadline approached.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to step up - to increase the effort, speed, or intensity of something
  • Antonym: to slow down - to decrease speed or effort

Etymology

From the mechanical metaphor of shifting a vehicle into a higher gear to go faster.
