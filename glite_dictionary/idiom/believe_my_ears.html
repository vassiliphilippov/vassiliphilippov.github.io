to believe my ears

1. VERB

/ bɪˈliv maɪ ɪrz / (AmE), / bɪˈliːv maɪ ɪəz / (BrE)
to be very surprised or unable to accept what one has just heard
to be surprised by what one hears

Examples

When he told me he was moving abroad, I couldn't believe my ears.
She was so shocked by the news that she could hardly believe her ears.
After hearing the results, we all stood in silence, unable to believe our ears.

Synset Examples

I couldn't believe my ears when she said she won the lottery.
When he claimed he finished the project early, I could hardly believe my ears.
Hearing that the concert was canceled made me believe my ears.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: not believe my eyes - to be very surprised or incredulous about what one sees
