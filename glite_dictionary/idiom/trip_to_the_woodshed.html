trip to the woodshed

1. NOUN

/ trɪp tə ðə ˈwʊdʃɛd / (AmE), / trɪp tə ðə ˈwʊdʃɛd / (BrE)
an occasion when someone is punished or strongly reprimanded, often privately
punishment or strong reprimand
informal american

Examples

After missing several deadlines, he knew a trip to the woodshed was coming.
Her careless mistake led to an unexpected trip to the woodshed from her supervisor.
When the team lost the game, the coach gave them a real trip to the woodshed in the locker room.

Synset Examples

Her trip to the woodshed taught her a valuable lesson.
After the argument, he knew a trip to the woodshed was inevitable.
He got a trip to the woodshed after his poor performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reprimand - an expression of disapproval or criticism
  • Synonym: scolding - an angry rebuke or criticism

Etymology

Refers to the old practice of taking someone to a woodshed (a shed for storing wood) for private punishment or discipline, particularly in rural America.
