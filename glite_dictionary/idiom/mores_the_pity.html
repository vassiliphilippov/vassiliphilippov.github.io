more's the pity

1. INTJ

/ mɔrz ðə ˈpɪti / (AmE), / mɔːz ðə ˈpɪti / (BrE)
used to express regret or disappointment that something unfortunate has happened or is the case
expression of regret or disappointment

Examples

They couldn't attend the wedding, more's the pity.
We have no more tickets left—more's the pity.
He didn't get the job, more's the pity.

Synset Examples

They missed the concert; more's the pity.
He lost the match; more's the pity.
She didn't attend the party; more's the pity.

Etymology

Contraction of 'the more is the pity', dating from early modern English.
