to feel like a ghost

1. VERB

/ fiːl laɪk ə ɡoʊst / (AmE), / fiːl laɪk ə ɡəʊst / (BrE)
to feel as though one is invisible or ignored by others, as if one's presence goes unnoticed
feel invisible or ignored

Examples

After moving to the new city, she often felt like a ghost among her colleagues.
He felt like a ghost in his own family, rarely included in conversations.
Standing in the crowded room, I felt like a ghost as no one acknowledged my presence.

Synset Examples

He felt like a ghost at the feast, unnoticed and alone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feel invisible - to feel unnoticed or ignored
  • Antonym: be in the spotlight - to attract or receive a lot of attention
