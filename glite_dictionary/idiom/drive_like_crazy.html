to drive like crazy

1. VERB

/ draɪv laɪk ˈkreɪzi / (AmE), / draɪv laɪk ˈkreɪzi / (BrE)
to drive a vehicle at a very high speed or in a reckless manner
drive very fast or recklessly
informal

Examples

After hearing the news, he started to drive like crazy to reach the hospital as fast as possible.
You shouldn't drive like crazy just because you're late for work.
When the rain began to pour, she continued to drive like crazy despite the slippery roads.

Synset Examples

She was driving like crazy to catch the train.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speed - to move quickly; travel fast
  • Antonym: drive carefully - to drive with caution and attention
