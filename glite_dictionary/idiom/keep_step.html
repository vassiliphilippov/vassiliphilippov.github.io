to keep step

1. VERB

/ kip stɛp / (AmE), / kiːp stɛp / (BrE)
to move at the same pace as someone else, especially in walking or marching together
move at the same pace as another

Examples

She tried hard to keep step with the taller hikers on the trail.
The soldiers practiced marching to keep step with the drumbeat.
It's difficult for a small child to keep step with energetic adults.

Synset Examples

The child cannot keep step with his big brother

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to fall behind - to move more slowly than someone and not keep up
