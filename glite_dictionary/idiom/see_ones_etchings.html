to see one's etchings

1. VERB

/ si wʌnz ˈɛtʃɪŋz / (AmE), / siː wʌnz ˈetʃɪŋz / (BrE)
used humorously as a euphemism for inviting someone to one's home with the intention of making a sexual advance, under the pretense of showing artwork
euphemism for making a sexual advance
informal

Examples

He asked if I wanted to see his etchings, but I knew what he really meant.
She laughed when I suggested we see my etchings after dinner.
The phrase 'would you like to see my etchings?' became a classic humorous innuendo.

Synset Examples

I've been asked to see her etchings at the gallery.
When we met, he offered to let me see his etchings.

Etymology

Alludes to the pretext of inviting someone to view etchings as a socially acceptable reason for a private encounter.
