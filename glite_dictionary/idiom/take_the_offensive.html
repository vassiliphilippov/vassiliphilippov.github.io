to take the offensive

1. VERB

/ teɪk ði əˈfɛnsɪv / (AmE), / teɪk ði əˈfɛnsɪv / (BrE)
to begin attacking or adopt an aggressive approach in a conflict or competition
to begin attacking; take aggressive action

Examples

After a cautious start, the general decided to take the offensive and launch a surprise attack.
If you want to gain control in a debate, sometimes you have to take the offensive.
The company chose to take the offensive by releasing a series of bold new products.

Synset Examples

The team decided to take the offensive after the opponent's first goal.
In negotiations, it's best to take the offensive to gain ground.
He chose to take the offensive rather than wait for a response.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go on the offensive - to begin attacking or take aggressive action
  • Antonym: take the defensive - to adopt a defensive or non-aggressive position
