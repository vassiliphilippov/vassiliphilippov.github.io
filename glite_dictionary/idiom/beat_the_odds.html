to beat the odds

1. VERB

/ biːt ði ɑdz / (AmE), / biːt ði ɒdz / (BrE)
to succeed in spite of great difficulty or against expectations
succeed despite difficulties

Examples

Despite his illness, he managed to beat the odds and graduate with honors.
Many small businesses struggle, but a few beat the odds and thrive.
The team was not favored to win, yet they beat the odds in the final match.

Synset Examples

She managed to beat the odds and finish the marathon.
They worked hard to beat the odds and win the championship.
They managed to beat the odds and win the competition.
Against all expectations, he beat the odds and succeeded.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overcome the odds - to achieve something despite being unlikely to succeed
  • Synonym: against the odds - despite being very unlikely
