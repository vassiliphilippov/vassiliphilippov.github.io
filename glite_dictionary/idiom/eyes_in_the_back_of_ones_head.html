eyes in the back of one's head

1. X

/ aɪz ɪn ðə bæk əv wʌnz hɛd / (AmE), / aɪz ɪn ðə bæk əv wʌnz hɛd / (BrE)
to have an uncanny ability to notice everything happening around you, especially things that are behind you or not directly visible
ability to notice things happening out of sight

Examples

Even though I tried to sneak past her quietly, it was as if my mother had eyes in the back of her head.
Teachers seem to have eyes in the back of their heads, always catching students misbehaving.
To keep track of three energetic kids, a parent needs eyes in the back of their head.

Synset Examples

She managed to catch the mischief; she has eyes in the back of her head.
