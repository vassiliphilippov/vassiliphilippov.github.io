to get over your skis

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈoʊvɚ jɚr skiːz / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈəʊvə jɔː skiːz / (BrE)
to take on more responsibility, risk, or activity than one can manage effectively; to become overextended or overconfident
take on more than you can handle
american informal

Examples

If you try to run two projects at once, you might get over your skis.
She realized she was getting over her skis when she agreed to chair three committees at work.
Investors often get over their skis during a booming market and take unnecessary risks.

Synset Examples

Don't get over your skis with that risky investment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bite off more than you can chew - to attempt more than one is capable of

Etymology

From the imagery of skiing, where leaning too far forward ('over your skis') can cause loss of control.
