to go to the bow-wows

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ tə ðə ˈbaʊ waʊz / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə ðə ˈbaʊ waʊz / (BrE)
(of a situation, business, etc.) to decline badly, be ruined, or fail completely.
be ruined or fail utterly (idiom)
informal

Examples

After the new manager was hired, the company began to go to the bow-wows.
If you keep neglecting your responsibilities, your career will go to the bow-wows.
Many believed the old hotel had already gone to the bow-wows long before it closed.

Synset Examples

If we don’t act soon, our plans will go to the bow-wows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fall apart - to break into pieces; to fail completely
  • Synonym: to go downhill - to decline in quality, health, or condition
  • Antonym: to prosper - to succeed or thrive, especially financially
