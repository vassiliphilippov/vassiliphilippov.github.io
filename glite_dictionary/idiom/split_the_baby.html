to split the baby

1. VERB

/ splɪt ðə ˈbeɪ.bi / (AmE), / splɪt ðə ˈbeɪ.bi / (BrE)
to reach a compromise by dividing the outcome between opposing parties, especially in a situation where a clear win for either side is not possible
reach a compromise in a tough dispute

Examples

Rather than favor one team, the coach decided to split the baby and let both play equal minutes.
During the merger talks, the executives agreed to split the baby to satisfy both companies’ interests.
Faced with a deadlock, the mediator proposed they split the baby so no party would leave empty-handed.

Synset Examples

They tried to split the baby fairly during negotiations.
The judge had to split the baby in the custody case.
In the argument, he suggested splitting the baby to appease both sides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compromise - settlement in which each side gives up something
  • Synonym: to split the difference - agree to settle a dispute for an amount halfway between both demands

Etymology

Alludes to the biblical story of King Solomon, who suggested dividing a disputed baby to reveal its true mother.
