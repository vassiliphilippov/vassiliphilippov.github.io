kit and caboodle

1. NOUN

/ kɪt ənd kəˈbudl̩ / (AmE), / kɪt ən kəˈbuːdl̩ / (BrE)
the whole of something; everything considered together; the entire lot
everything; the whole lot
informal

Examples

She packed up the kit and caboodle and moved to a new city.
If you want to buy the kit and caboodle, it will cost extra.
They sold the house and the kit and caboodle that went with it.

Synset Examples

we saw the whole shebang
a hotdog with the works
we took on the whole caboodle
for $10 you get the full treatment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the whole shebang - everything; the entire lot
  • Synonym: the works - everything included; all available extras

Etymology

From 'kit' (collection of personal items) and 'caboodle,' a humorous alteration of 'boodle' (a lot, collection), American slang from Dutch 'boedel' (property).
