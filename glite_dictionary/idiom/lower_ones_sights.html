to lower one's sights

1. VERB

/ ˈloʊ.ɚ wʌnz saɪts / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.ə wʌnz saɪts / (BrE)
to reduce one's ambitions or expectations, often in response to reality or difficulties
reduce ambitions or expectations

Examples

After several failed auditions, he decided to lower his sights and apply for smaller roles in local theater.
When the company faced budget cuts, management had to lower their sights regarding expansion plans.
Realizing her exam scores were not high enough, she chose to lower her sights and apply to less competitive universities.

Synset Examples

She decided to lower her sights and focus on achievable goals.
He had to lower his sights after realizing the competition.
Sometimes it's wise to lower one's sights for better outcomes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to aim high - set one's goals or ambitions at a high level

Etymology

From the literal act of adjusting the sight on a firearm or aiming device to a lower target, used figuratively since the 20th century.
