to mark time

1. VERB

/ mɑrk taɪm / (AmE), / mɑːk taɪm / (BrE)
to spend time waiting without making progress, especially by doing something unproductive
pass time without progress

Examples

While waiting for the results, she decided to mark time by reading magazines.
The project was delayed, so the team continued to mark time until approval came through.
He hates to mark time and always looks for something productive to do.

Synset Examples

We decided to mark time until the storm passed.
He likes to mark time by playing games during long waits.
They marked time while waiting for the meeting to start.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idle - to spend time doing nothing
  • Antonym: to make headway - to make progress

Etymology

Originally a military term for marching in place rather than advancing.
