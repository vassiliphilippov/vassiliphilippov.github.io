to beat someone's brains out

1. VERB

/ biːt sʌmwʌnz breɪnz aʊt / (AmE), / biːt sʌmwʌnz breɪnz aʊt / (BrE)
to work or study very hard, often to the point of exhaustion; or to hit someone violently, often as a threat or expression of anger
work intensely hard or hit violently

Examples

I've been beating my brains out trying to solve this problem all night.
She threatened to beat his brains out if he cheated again.
You don't have to beat your brains out over this assignment; take a break and relax.

Synset Examples

He felt like he would beat his brains out studying for finals.
He threatened to beat his brains out if he lost.
She joked about beating his brains out during the competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to work one's fingers to the bone - to work extremely hard
  • Synonym: hammer away - to persistently work at something
  • Antonym: to take it easy - to relax and avoid hard work
