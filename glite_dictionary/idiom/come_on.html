come on

1. INTJ

/ kʌm ɑn / (AmE), / kʌm ɒn / (BrE)
used to express disbelief, irritation, or to urge someone to do something
expression of disbelief or encouragement
informal

Examples

Come on, you don't really believe that story, do you?
Come on, let's get going before it starts to rain.
Oh, come on! You know that's not true.

Synset Examples

Oh, come on! That’s not fair!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: give me a break - expression of disbelief or annoyance
  • Synonym: to hurry up - urging someone to act faster
