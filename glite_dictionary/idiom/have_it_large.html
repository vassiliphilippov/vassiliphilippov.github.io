to have it large

1. VERB

/ hæv ɪt lɑrdʒ / (AmE), / hæv ɪt lɑːdʒ / (BrE)
to celebrate or enjoy oneself in an extravagant or uninhibited way, especially at a party or social event
celebrate or party extravagantly
informal british

Examples

They went out to have it large on New Year's Eve, staying out until dawn.
Whenever the team wins, the coach loves to have it large with his staff.
She invited all her friends because she wanted to have it large for her graduation party.

Synset Examples

For her birthday, she wanted to have it large at the venue.
When celebrating, we always have it large with friends.
He likes to have it large when hosting parties at his place.
