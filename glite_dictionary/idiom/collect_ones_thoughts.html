to collect one's thoughts

1. VERB

/ kəˈlɛkt wʌnz θɑts / (AmE), / kəˈlɛkt wʌnz θɒts / (BrE)
to take time to organize one's ideas or emotions, especially in order to think or speak clearly
gather and compose one's thoughts

Examples

Please give me a minute to collect my thoughts before I respond to your question.
He stepped outside after the argument to collect his thoughts and calm down.
She closed her eyes for a moment, trying to collect her thoughts amid the chaos.

Synset Examples

I need a moment to collect my thoughts before speaking.
He paused to collect his thoughts before answering the question.
After the meeting, she stepped outside to collect her thoughts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to gather one's thoughts - arrange one's ideas or emotions; closely related idiom
  • Synonym: compose oneself - bring one's emotions or thoughts under control
