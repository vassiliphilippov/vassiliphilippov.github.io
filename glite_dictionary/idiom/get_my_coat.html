to get my coat

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt maɪ koʊt / (AmE), / ɡɛt maɪ kəʊt / (BrE)
(informal) Used to indicate readiness to leave a place, often as a discreet or humorous signal for departure.
prepare to leave (idiomatic)
informal

Examples

After hearing the announcement, I thought it was time to get my coat and head home.
She made a joke so awful that I wanted to get my coat and leave immediately.
As soon as the meeting dragged on, he whispered that he would get my coat.

Synset Examples

I'll get my coat, this party is dull.
