to put up or shut up

1. VERB

/ pʊt ʌp ɔr ʃʌt ʌp / (AmE), / pʊt ʌp ɔː ʃʌt ʌp / (BrE)
used to tell someone to take action on what they are saying or stop talking about it
take action or stop talking

Examples

If you really think you can do better, it's time to put up or shut up.
The manager told the team to put up or shut up before the big match.
After months of complaining, he was finally told to put up or shut up by his colleagues.

Synset Examples

She challenged him to put up or shut up regarding his promises.
It's time for him to put up or shut up about his claims.
In business, you often have to put up or shut up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: act or get off the pot - informal idiom: act or stop hesitating
