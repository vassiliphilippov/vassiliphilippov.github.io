to hunt where the ducks are

1. VERB

/ hʌnt wɛr ðə dʌks ɑr / (AmE), / hʌnt weə ðə dʌks ɑː / (BrE)
to seek opportunities or direct efforts where success is most likely; to go where the potential or demand exists
pursue opportunities where they exist

Examples

In business, it's important to hunt where the ducks are rather than waste resources elsewhere.
The company decided to hunt where the ducks are by targeting a growing market segment.
If you want the best results, you need to hunt where the ducks are instead of taking unnecessary risks.

Synset Examples
