to act like a jessie

1. VERB

/ ækt laɪk ə ˈdʒɛsi / (AmE), / ækt laɪk ə ˈdʒɛsi / (BrE)
To behave in a cowardly or weak manner, especially by showing timidity or fear.
behave cowardly or weakly
informal british

Examples

Stop acting like a jessie and just dive in.
He refused to speak up for himself, always acting like a jessie.
If you keep acting like a jessie, you'll never get anywhere in life.

Synset Examples

Stop acting like a jessie and just dive in.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to chicken out - to withdraw from something because of fear
  • Synonym: coward - a person who lacks courage
  • Antonym: brave - showing courage

Etymology

Jessie is used as a slang term for a coward or weak person, particularly in British English; origin uncertain, possibly from the given name.
