run-of-the-mill

1. ADJ

/ ˌrʌn əv ðə ˈmɪl / (AmE), / ˌrʌn əv ðə ˈmɪl / (BrE)
Ordinary; not special or remarkable in any way.
ordinary; unremarkable

Examples

Most of the suggestions were run-of-the-mill and did not inspire any excitement in the team.
He wore a run-of-the-mill grey suit to the meeting, blending in with everyone else.
The film was entertaining but ultimately run-of-the-mill, with a predictable plot and characters.

Synset Examples

Her performance was just run-of-the-mill, lacking excitement.
He prefers unique art over run-of-the-mill pieces.
This restaurant serves run-of-the-mill food, nothing special.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: average - typical; not outstanding
  • Antonym: exceptional - unusually good; outstanding

Etymology

Originates from the early 20th century, referring to goods coming directly from the mill, before any selection for quality.
