to knock off one's perch

1. VERB

/ nɑk ɔf wʌnz pɜrtʃ / (AmE), / nɒk ɒf wʌnz pɜːtʃ / (BrE)
to remove someone from a position of success or superiority, especially by defeating or outdoing them
remove from a position of advantage

Examples

The new technology startup managed to knock the established company off its perch in just a few months.
After years as the league champions, they were finally knocked off their perch by a rival team.
It will take a lot of work to knock him off his perch as the top salesperson.

Synset Examples

The loss of the championship really knocked the team off their perch.
His unexpected promotion knocked her off her perch.
Her success in the competition knocked him off his perch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to topple - to remove from a position of power or prominence
  • Synonym: to unseat - to remove someone from a position of authority or importance
