to know what's what

1. VERB

/ noʊ wʌts wʌt / (AmE), / nəʊ wɒts wɒt / (BrE)
to be well-informed or to have a good understanding of a situation or subject
be well-informed; understand clearly

Examples

When it comes to investing, Sarah really knows what's what.
You have to know what's what to succeed in this business.
After years in the industry, he finally felt like he knew what's what.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be in the know - to have special or inside information
  • Antonym: be out of the loop - to be uninformed or unaware
