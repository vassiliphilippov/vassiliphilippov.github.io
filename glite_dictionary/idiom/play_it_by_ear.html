to play it by ear

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ɪt baɪ ɪr / (AmE), / pleɪ ɪt baɪ ɪə / (BrE)
to decide how to deal with a situation as it develops rather than planning in advance
improvise; act without a set plan

Examples

We don't have a reservation, so let's just play it by ear when we get to the restaurant.
I'm not sure how the meeting will go; I'll play it by ear and see what happens.
He prefers to play it by ear rather than making detailed plans for everything.

Synset Examples

She didn't know what to expect from her new job, so she played it by ear

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to improvise - to create or perform spontaneously, without preparation

Etymology

Originally from musical terminology, meaning to play music without written notes, relying on listening and improvisation.
