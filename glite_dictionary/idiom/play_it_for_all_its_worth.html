to play it for all it's worth

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ɪt fər ɔl ɪts wɝθ / (AmE), / pleɪ ɪt fə ɔːl ɪts wɜːθ / (BrE)
to use a situation or opportunity as much as possible to gain benefit from it
make the most of an opportunity

Examples

Whenever she finds a good deal, she plays it for all it's worth.
He had the chance to lead the project and played it for all it's worth.
When given a little praise, he tends to play it for all it's worth and seek more attention.

Synset Examples

She decided to play it for all it's worth at the competition.
He knew he had to play it for all it's worth to win.
