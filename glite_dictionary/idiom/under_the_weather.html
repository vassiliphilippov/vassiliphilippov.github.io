under the weather

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌndɚ ðə ˈwɛðɚ / (AmE), / ˌʌndə ðə ˈwɛðə / (BrE)
Feeling slightly ill or unwell.
slightly ill; unwell

Examples

I'm feeling under the weather today, so I think I'll stay home from work.
She seemed a bit under the weather during the meeting and left early.
If you're under the weather, it's best to rest and drink plenty of fluids.

Synset Examples

my poor ailing grandmother
feeling a bit indisposed today
you look a little peaked
feeling poorly
a sickly child
is unwell and can't come to work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ill - feeling unwell
  • Synonym: unwell - not feeling well
  • Antonym: well - healthy; not feeling sick

Etymology

Said to originate from nautical slang, where being ill was attributed to adverse weather conditions at sea.
