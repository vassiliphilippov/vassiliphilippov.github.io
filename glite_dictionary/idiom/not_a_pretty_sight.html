not a pretty sight

1. X

/ nɑt ə ˈprɪti saɪt / (AmE), / nɒt ə ˈprɪti saɪt / (BrE)
used to describe an unpleasant, shocking, or distressing scene or situation
an unpleasant or distressing scene

Examples

The aftermath of the accident was not a pretty sight for anyone passing by.
His dirty, mud-splattered clothes were not a pretty sight at the dinner table.
After the long night, the kitchen was definitely not a pretty sight in the morning.

Synset Examples

His reaction to the news was not a pretty sight.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: grotesque - comically or repulsively ugly or distorted
  • Degree Higher: disturbing - causing anxiety or discomfort
