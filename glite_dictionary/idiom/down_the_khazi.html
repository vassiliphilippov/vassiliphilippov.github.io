down the khazi

1. X

/ daʊn ði ˈkɑzi / (AmE), / daʊn ðə ˈkɑːzi / (BrE)
(informal, British slang) Used to describe something that is lost, wasted, or ruined; also, to refer to using the toilet.
wasted, ruined, or toilet (Brit. slang)
informal slang british

Examples

After his company went bankrupt, all his investments went down the khazi.
She felt her hard work had gone down the khazi after failing the exam.
I really need to go down the khazi before the meeting starts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: down the drain - wasted or lost (idiom)
  • Synonym: down the tubes - wasted or ruined (idiom)

Etymology

British slang; 'khazi' is thought to derive from Italian 'casa' (house), adopted as slang for 'toilet' in the UK.
