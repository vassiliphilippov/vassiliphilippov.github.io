to cry a river

1. VERB

/ kraɪ ə ˈrɪvɚ / (AmE), / kraɪ ə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
to complain or express unhappiness in an excessive or exaggerated way
complain excessively or dramatically
informal

Examples

Every time something doesn't go her way, she starts to cry a river about it.
You can cry a river, but that won't change my decision.
He tends to cry a river whenever he faces criticism at work.

Synset Examples

He always has a reason to cry a Crimea river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whine - to complain in a feeble or petulant way
  • Synonym: to complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance
