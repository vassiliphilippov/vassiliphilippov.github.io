to pile Pelion on Ossa

1. VERB

/ paɪl ˈpiːliən ɑn ˈɑsə / (AmE), / paɪl ˈpiːliən ɒn ˈɒsə / (BrE)
to attempt something impossible or excessive, especially by needlessly adding to a difficult situation
take on or add excessive extra burdens
archaic

Examples

Adding another complex requirement now would be to pile Pelion on Ossa.
He keeps piling Pelion on Ossa instead of solving the basic issue.
The committee's suggestions only piled Pelion on Ossa, making the project even harder.

Synset Examples

The new project feels like they’re trying to pile Pelion on Ossa.
To ask for more funding now would be like piling Pelion on Ossa.
They’re just piling Pelion on Ossa with these added requests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make a mountain out of a molehill - to exaggerate a minor problem

Etymology

From Greek mythology, where the giants attempted to reach Olympus by piling Mount Pelion on top of Mount Ossa.
