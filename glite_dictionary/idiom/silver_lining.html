silver lining

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvɚ ˈlaɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə ˈlaɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
an advantage or positive aspect that emerges from a difficult or unpleasant situation
positive aspect in a bad situation

Examples

Although losing her job was hard, the silver lining was that she finally pursued her passion.
Even in the midst of tragedy, he tries to find the silver lining.
Remote work has its challenges, but a silver lining is spending more time with family.

Synset Examples

Despite the setbacks, there was a silver lining to the experience.
He always finds a silver lining in every setback.
The rain canceled our picnic, but the silver lining was a cozy day indoors.
Every cloud has a silver lining, they say.
She always finds a silver lining in every tough situation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bright side - an optimistic view or aspect of a situation

Etymology

From the proverbial phrase 'every cloud has a silver lining' (19th century), referring to the bright edge of a cloud backlit by the sun.
