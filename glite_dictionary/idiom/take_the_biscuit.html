to take the biscuit

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə ˈbɪskɪt / (AmE), / teɪk ðə ˈbɪskɪt / (BrE)
(British, informal) to be the most extreme, surprising, or outrageous among other bad or remarkable things, often in a negative sense.
be the most outrageous or surprising, often negatively
british informal

Examples

I thought I'd heard every excuse, but this one really takes the biscuit.
Her rude remark at dinner took the biscuit and left everyone speechless.
Just when I thought things couldn't get worse, his latest mistake took the biscuit.

Synset Examples

This mess takes the biscuit for worst customer service.
That excuse really takes the biscuit!
His behavior at the meeting took the biscuit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take the cake - to be the most remarkable or outrageous, esp. in a negative sense (American English idiom)

Etymology

British English idiom; 'biscuit' here refers to a prize or reward, similar to the American 'take the cake.'
