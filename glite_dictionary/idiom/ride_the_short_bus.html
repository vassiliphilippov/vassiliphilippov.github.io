to ride the short bus

1. VERB

/ raɪd ðə ʃɔrt bʌs / (AmE), / raɪd ðə ʃɔːt bʌs / (BrE)
(Offensive, slang) To be considered of lower ability or intelligence, often used to mock someone as less competent or capable.
be of lower ability/intelligence (offensive, slang)
offensive slang american

Examples

After his mistake at work, he joked that he must ride the short bus.
Some people use the phrase ride the short bus to mock others, but it's considered offensive.
Whenever she struggled with basic math, her brother teased her by saying she might ride the short bus.

Synset Examples

After his comments, he felt like he might as well ride the short bus.
She joked about needing to ride the short bus to understand their plans.
He often felt like he was riding the short bus during those meetings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dumb - lacking intelligence or common sense
  • Synonym: slow - informal; not quick to understand or learn

Etymology

From the use of shorter school buses in North America for special education students, which became a pejorative expression.
