in the line of duty

1. ADV

/ ɪn ðə laɪn əv ˈduːti / (AmE), / ɪn ðə laɪn əv ˈdjuːti / (BrE)
while performing one's job or responsibilities, especially in contexts such as military, police, or other official service, often referring to actions or events occurring as part of one's professional obligations
while performing one's official duties

Examples

The firefighter received a medal for saving three children in the line of duty.
Many officers face unexpected dangers in the line of duty.
He was tragically injured in the line of duty while helping civilians.

Synset Examples

Many soldiers lose their lives in the line of duty.
Police officers often face danger in the line of duty.
Many soldiers sacrifice their lives in the line of duty.
He was honored for his bravery in the line of duty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on duty - currently working or responsible for professional tasks
