Gordian knot

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɔrdiən nɑt / (AmE), / ˈɡɔːdiən nɒt / (BrE)
a very difficult or seemingly unsolvable problem
an extremely difficult problem

Examples

The merger negotiations turned into a Gordian knot that no one seemed able to unravel.
The issue of climate change policy is often described as a political Gordian knot.
Her proposal finally cut through the Gordian knot facing the committee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conundrum - a confusing and difficult problem or question
  • Synonym: enigma - a person or thing that is mysterious or difficult to understand
  • Antonym: solution - an act of solving a problem or difficult situation

Etymology

From the legendary knot tied by Gordius, king of Phrygia, said to be untied only by the future ruler of Asia; Alexander the Great 'solved' it by cutting through it with his sword.
