one-trick pony

1. NOUN

/ ˌwʌn trɪk ˈpoʊni / (AmE), / ˌwʌn trɪk ˈpəʊni / (BrE)
a person or thing with only one talent, skill, or area of expertise
person/thing with only one skill

Examples

Many critics dismissed the band as a one-trick pony after their second album sounded just like their first.
If you want to advance in your career, try not to become a one-trick pony.
People often call him a one-trick pony, but he takes pride in perfecting his one specialty.

Synset Examples

Being a one-trick pony limits your opportunities for growth.
She felt like a one-trick pony in her current job.
The magician was a one-trick pony with his signature act.
He's a one-trick pony, always using the same strategy.
She realized he was a one-trick pony, doing the same thing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: jack of all trades - a person who can do many different types of work but is not necessarily very competent at any of them

Etymology

From the idea of a circus pony trained to perform only one trick; first recorded in the US in the late 20th century.
