to keep a tight ship

1. VERB

/ kip ə taɪt ʃɪp / (AmE), / kiːp ə taɪt ʃɪp / (BrE)
to manage an organization, department, or group very efficiently and with strict discipline
manage efficiently and strictly

Examples

As a manager, she keeps a tight ship to ensure deadlines are met and goals achieved.
The new principal is determined to keep a tight ship at the school this year.
If you want your team to succeed, you must keep a tight ship at all times.

Synset Examples

He keeps a tight ship, avoiding any distractions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to run a tight ship - to manage operations efficiently and strictly

Etymology

Based on the metaphor of a ship run with strict discipline for safety and efficiency.
