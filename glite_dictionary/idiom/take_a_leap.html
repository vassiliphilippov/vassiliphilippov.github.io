to take a leap

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə liːp / (AmE), / teɪk ə liːp / (BrE)
to make a bold or risky decision; to take a chance or decisive action, often involving uncertainty or risk
make a bold or risky decision

Examples

If you want to start your own business, you have to take a leap at some point.
After years of doubting herself, Jenna finally decided to take a leap and move abroad for work.
Sometimes you need to take a leap to discover what you are truly capable of.

Synset Examples

She made a leap toward her dreams last year.
He took a leap and decided to jump into freelancing.
Taking that leap was the best decision he ever made.
He decided to take the leap, Billy-be-damned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take the plunge - to decide to do something important or risky, especially after hesitation
  • Antonym: to play it safe - to avoid risks
