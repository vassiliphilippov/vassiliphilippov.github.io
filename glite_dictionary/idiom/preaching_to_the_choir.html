preaching to the choir

1. X

/ ˈprit͡ʃɪŋ tə ðə ˈkwaɪ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈpriːtʃɪŋ tə ðə ˈkwaɪ.ə / (BrE)
expressing an opinion or argument to people who already agree with or support what you are saying
expressing views to those who already agree

Examples

Arguing about climate change with these scientists felt like preaching to the choir.
When you tell me to avoid fast food, you're preaching to the choir—I already eat healthy.
By repeating those safety rules to experienced pilots, the instructor was just preaching to the choir.

Synset Examples

He was just preaching to the choir at the meeting.
Discussing health tips with her felt like preaching to the choir.
Telling her to save money felt like preaching to the choir.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preach to the converted - to try to persuade people who already agree with you

Etymology

From the Christian practice of delivering sermons (preaching) to the church choir, who are expected to already share the same beliefs.
