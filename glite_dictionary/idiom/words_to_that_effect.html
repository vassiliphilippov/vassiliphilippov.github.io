words to that effect

1. X

/ wɝːdz tə ðæt ɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / wɜːdz tə ðæt ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
used to indicate that something was said or written using similar wording or meaning, though not the exact words quoted
using similar wording or meaning

Examples

She assured us the meeting was postponed, or words to that effect.
The manager criticized his performance—at least, he used words to that effect.
The email said we had missed the deadline, or words to that effect.

Synset Examples

She said she’d help, or words to that effect.
The instructions were vague, or words to that effect.
He mentioned a raise, or words to that effect.
