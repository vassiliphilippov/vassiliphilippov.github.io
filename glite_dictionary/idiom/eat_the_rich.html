eat the rich

1. X

/ it ðə rɪtʃ / (AmE), / iːt ðə rɪtʃ / (BrE)
(informal, often humorous or provocative) Used to criticize the wealthy or to express the idea that extreme wealth inequality should be opposed, sometimes suggesting radical redistribution of wealth.
slogan mocking or criticizing the wealthy
informal

Examples

The protestors marched through the city chanting eat the rich as their rallying cry.
Some satirical cartoons use eat the rich to highlight growing economic inequality.
The phrase eat the rich appeared on banners at the demonstration against government tax breaks for the wealthy.

Synset Examples

Her song's theme was about the need to eat the rich.
The protestors held signs that read 'Eat the rich!'
Many believe we should eat the rich to reduce inequality.

Etymology

From a phrase attributed to Jean-Jacques Rousseau: 'When the people shall have nothing more to eat, they will eat the rich.'
