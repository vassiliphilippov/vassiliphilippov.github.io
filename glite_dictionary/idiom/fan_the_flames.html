to fan the flames

1. VERB

/ fæn ðə fleɪmz / (AmE), / fan ðə fleɪmz / (BrE)
to make a situation, emotion, or conflict worse or more intense by encouraging or provoking it
intensify a conflict or situation

Examples

His criticism only served to fan the flames of the argument.
It's best not to fan the flames when tempers are already high.
Social media can fan the flames of misinformation very quickly.

Synset Examples

Media coverage can sometimes fan the flames of public outrage.
His comments only served to fan the flames of the controversy.
We should not fan the flames of anger with harsh words.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exacerbate - to make a problem or situation worse
  • Antonym: to calm down - to make a situation less intense or emotional

Etymology

Refers metaphorically to fanning a fire to make the flames grow; first recorded in figurative use in the late 19th century.
