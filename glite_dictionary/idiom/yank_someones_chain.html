to yank someone's chain

1. VERB

/ jæŋk sʌmwʌnz tʃeɪn / (AmE), / jæŋk sʌmwʌnz tʃeɪn / (BrE)
to tease or annoy someone, often in order to provoke a reaction
tease or provoke someone
informal american

Examples

He always tries to yank my chain when he knows I'm stressed.
Are you yanking my chain or is there really good news?
She didn't realize he was just yanking her chain with that story.

Synset Examples

He loves to yank my chain just to see my reaction.
Don't yank my chain; I'm serious about this.
Stop yanking my chain and tell me the truth.
Don’t yank her chain; she’s serious about this project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pull someone's leg - to tease or joke with someone in a playful way
  • Synonym: tease - to make fun of or provoke someone playfully

Etymology

American slang, from the literal idea of pulling someone's chain as a way to get their attention or disturb them.
