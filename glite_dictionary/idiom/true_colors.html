true colors

1. NOUN

/ tru ˈkʌlərz / (AmE), / truː ˈkʌləz / (BrE)
A person's real character or intentions, especially when these differ from how they appear at first.
real character or intentions

Examples

After months of pretending to be helpful, she revealed her true colors when she betrayed the team.
It's only when facing adversity that many people show their true colors.
He seemed charming, but his true colors came out during the argument.

Synset Examples

She seemed nice, but soon showed her true colors.
You’ll see their true colors when the chips are down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: real self - a person's genuine personality, as opposed to a facade
  • Antonym: mask - something that conceals real character or intentions

Etymology

Originally from the practice of warships flying false flags; 'colors' refers to flags denoting allegiance.
