to sweat the details

1. VERB

/ swɛt ðə dɪˈteɪlz / (AmE), / swɛt ðə dɪˈtiːlz / (BrE)
to worry excessively about small or minor aspects of something
worry about minor aspects

Examples

My manager told me not to sweat the details on this project and just finish it by Friday.
If you sweat the details too much, you might miss the bigger picture.
She tends to sweat the details, even when they aren't very important.

Synset Examples

Don't sweat the details; just get it done.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sweat the small stuff - worry about minor or insignificant matters
