to cut out the middleman

1. VERB

/ kʌt aʊt ðə ˈmɪdəlˌmæn / (AmE), / kʌt aʊt ði ˈmɪd(ə)lmæn / (BrE)
to deal directly with the producer or supplier, thereby eliminating any intermediaries and often reducing costs
eliminate intermediaries to reduce costs

Examples

Many small farmers try to cut out the middleman by selling their produce directly to consumers.
By booking flights yourself online, you can often cut out the middleman and find cheaper deals.
They decided to cut out the middleman and negotiated directly with the manufacturer.

Synset Examples

They cut out the middleman to save money on their purchase.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go direct - to deal without intermediaries
