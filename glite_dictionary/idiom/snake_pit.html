snake pit

1. NOUN

/ ˈsneɪk ˌpɪt / (AmE), / ˈsneɪk pɪt / (BrE)
a place or situation characterized by confusion, hostility, or intense rivalry, often creating a stressful or threatening environment
hostile or chaotic environment

Examples

The office had become a real snake pit since the new manager arrived.
Journalists described the political debate as a snake pit of backstabbing and rivalry.
Trying to solve the problem in that snake pit of a meeting was nearly impossible.

Synset Examples

Working in that office feels like a snake pit.
The negotiations turned into a snake pit quickly.
The meeting turned into a snake pit of accusations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minefield - a situation presenting many unpredictable dangers or difficulties
  • Synonym: war zone - an area or situation where there is intense conflict or hostility

Etymology

Originally from the literal sense of a pit containing snakes, used figuratively to describe hostile or dangerous situations.
