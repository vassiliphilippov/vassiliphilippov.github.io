to lead by the nose

1. VERB

/ lid baɪ ðə noʊz / (AmE), / liːd baɪ ðə nəʊz / (BrE)
to control or manipulate someone by deceiving them or hiding one's true intentions
manipulate or control (someone) by deceit

Examples

The clever salesman managed to lead by the nose everyone in the meeting.
She was so trusting that her colleagues could easily lead her by the nose.
Politicians often try to lead the public by the nose with false promises.

Synset Examples

He bamboozled his professors into thinking that he knew the subject well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to manipulate - to control or influence someone cleverly or unscrupulously
  • Synonym: to bamboozle - to deceive or trick someone
  • Antonym: to liberate - to set someone free from control or influence

Etymology

From the practice of leading animals with a ring through their nose, implying control and obedience.

2. VERB

/ lid baɪ ðə noʊz / (AmE), / liːd baɪ ðə nəʊz / (BrE)
to control someone completely and make them do whatever you want, often without them realizing it
to control completely; dominate

Examples

The politician managed to lead by the nose the entire board into supporting his plan.
She was so infatuated that he could lead her by the nose without resistance.
They claimed the CEO was led by the nose by his advisors on every important decision.

Synset Examples

He tends to lead his team by the nose, never trusting them.
She felt he would lead her by the nose if she let him.
The manager leads by the nose, making all decisions himself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dominate - to have control or power over someone
  • Synonym: to manipulate - to control or influence someone cleverly or unscrupulously
