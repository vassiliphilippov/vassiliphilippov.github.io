to put up one's dukes

1. VERB

/ pʊt ʌp wʌnz duks / (AmE), / pʊt ʌp wʌnz djuːks / (BrE)
to raise one's fists in preparation for a fight or to defend oneself
prepare to fight (raise fists)
informal

Examples

If you keep taunting me, I might have to put up my dukes.
He shouted, 'Come on, put up your dukes!' to challenge his opponent.
Whenever someone insulted her friend, she was ready to put up her dukes.

Synset Examples

She told him to put up his dukes if he wanted to spar.
When the argument escalated, he put up his dukes.
He was ready to put up his dukes in defense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fight - to engage in a physical altercation
  • Antonym: to stand down - to cease fighting or withdraw from confrontation

Etymology

Likely from "dukes" as slang for fists or hands, with uncertain origin—possibly rhyming slang from "Duke of York" meaning fork (slang for hand).
