all the way

1. ADV

/ ɒl ðə weɪ / (AmE), / ɔːl ðə weɪ / (BrE)
completely; as far as possible or as much as possible.
completely; to the fullest extent

Examples

She stayed with the project all the way to the end, never giving up.
To win this competition, you need to focus all the way.
The road stretches all the way from the city to the coast.

Synset Examples

slept clear through the night
there were open fields clear to the horizon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completely - entirely; to the full extent
  • Antonym: partially - only to some extent; not completely

for the entire distance or to the full extent; from start to finish.
