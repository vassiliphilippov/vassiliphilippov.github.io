down at heel

1. ADJ

/ daʊn ət ˈhiːl / (AmE), / daʊn ət ˈhiːl / (BrE)
(of a person or place) having a shabby, neglected, or worn appearance, especially due to poverty.
shabby or neglected in appearance

Examples

After losing his job, Tom became quite down at heel and struggled with his finances.
The down at heel hotel had peeling paint and broken furniture everywhere.
She felt embarrassed by her down at heel coat at the elegant restaurant.

Synset Examples

They lived in a down at heel part of town.
His shoes looked down at heel after years of wear.
She felt down at heel in her old clothes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shabby - worn out or scruffy in appearance
  • Synonym: dilapidated - in a state of disrepair or ruin

Etymology

Originally referred to shoes with worn-down heels, later generalized to shabby appearance.
