to put oneself in someone's shoes

1. VERB

/ pʊt ˈwʌnˌsɛlf ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz ʃuz / (AmE), / pʊt ˈwʌnsɛlf ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz ʃuːz / (BrE)
to imagine what it would be like to be in another person's situation or to understand someone else's feelings, perspective, or experience
imagine being in another's situation

Examples

It's important to put oneself in someone else's shoes before making a judgment.
Teachers are encouraged to put themselves in their students' shoes to better understand their needs.
If you put yourself in his shoes, you'll see why he made that decision.

Synset Examples

To empathize, we must put ourselves in others' shoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to empathize - to understand and share the feelings of another
  • Synonym: see through someone else's eyes - to look at something from another person's perspective
