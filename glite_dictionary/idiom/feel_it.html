to feel it

1. VERB

/ ˈfiːl ɪt / (AmE), / ˈfiːl ɪt / (BrE)
to be engaged, enthusiastic, or motivated about something; to enjoy or connect with an activity or situation.
to be enthusiastic, engaged, or motivated
informal

Examples

I tried to start the project, but I just wasn't feeling it today.
During the concert, the crowd was definitely feeling it and singing along.
If you're not feeling it, maybe we should take a break and come back later.

Synset Examples

I'm just not feeling it today, sorry.
She wasn't feeling it during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be into - to be interested or enthusiastic about something
  • Antonym: not in the mood - not feeling motivated or interested at the moment
