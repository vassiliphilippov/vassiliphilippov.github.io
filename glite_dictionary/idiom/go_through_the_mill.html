to go through the mill

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ θru ðə mɪl / (AmE), / ɡəʊ θruː ðə mɪl / (BrE)
to experience a series of difficult or testing experiences; to be subjected to hardship or scrutiny
to undergo hardship or difficulty

Examples

After months of going through the mill, the team finally launched their product.
She really went through the mill during her divorce, but came out stronger on the other side.
His ideas had to go through the mill before being accepted by the board.

Synset Examples

After going through the mill, she emerged stronger than ever.
He felt like he was going through the mill at work.
This lawsuit has gone through the mill for years.
She was put through the mill during her first week.
The project was put through the mill by the critics.
He felt like he was put through the mill after that meeting.
He's been through the mill lately with all his challenges.
The project went through the mill before it was approved.
Her latest project really went through the mill before completion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: put through the wringer - to subject someone to a difficult or stressful situation
  • Synonym: to have a hard time - to experience difficulty or trouble
  • Antonym: to breeze through - to complete something easily with little difficulty

Etymology

Metaphorical use of 'mill' as a place where things are crushed or ground, likening hardship to being put through a grinding process.
