to come to the scratch

1. VERB

/ kʌm tə ðə skrætʃ / (AmE), / kʌm tə ðə skrætʃ / (BrE)
to respond or assist when required, especially in difficult or pressured situations
respond or assist under pressure

Examples

Even when the project seemed hopeless, she came to the scratch with new ideas.
You can always count on him to come to the scratch when the company faces a crisis.
The team needed someone to volunteer, and Mark came to the scratch without hesitation.

Synset Examples

When the team needed him, he came to the scratch.
In tough times, he knows how to come to the scratch.
She always comes to the scratch when her friends need help.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rise to the occasion - to perform well in a difficult or important situation

Etymology

Originally from boxing, where 'the scratch' was the line fighters came up to at the start of a round.
