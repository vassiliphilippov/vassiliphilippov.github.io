worth one's salt

1. ADJ

/ wɜrθ wʌnz sɔlt / (AmE), / wɜːθ wʌnz sɒlt / (BrE)
Deserving of one's pay, status, or respect; competent or valuable.
deserving pay or respect; competent

Examples

Any manager worth one's salt would know how to handle this situation calmly.
She quickly showed she was worth her salt by solving the team's toughest problem.
Do you think he's worth his salt in this demanding new role?

Synset Examples

He proved he was worth his salt during the project.
Anyone worth their salt will agree with this decision.
A good chef is worth their salt in any kitchen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: competent - having the necessary ability or skill to do something successfully
  • Antonym: ineffective - not producing the intended effect or lacking competence

Etymology

Originating from the ancient practice of paying workers or soldiers with salt, which was a valuable commodity.
