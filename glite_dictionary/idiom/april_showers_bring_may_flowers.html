April showers bring May flowers

1. X

/ ˈeɪprəl ˈʃaʊərz brɪŋ ˈmeɪ ˈflaʊərz / (AmE), / ˈeɪprɪl ˈʃaʊəz brɪŋ ˈmeɪ ˈflaʊəz / (BrE)
(saying) Difficult or unpleasant situations can lead to better or more positive outcomes.
better results follow difficult times (idiom)

Examples

When things aren't going your way, just remember that April showers bring May flowers.
She comforted her friend by saying, 'April showers bring May flowers,' encouraging her to stay patient through difficulties.
Although his project failed, he believed that April showers bring May flowers and tried again.

Synset Examples

Remember, April showers bring May flowers, so stay hopeful.
His optimism reminded me that April showers bring May flowers.
The garden looks dull now, but April showers bring May flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: every cloud has a silver lining - there is something good even in unpleasant situations
  • Synonym: after a storm comes a calm - peace follows trouble or crisis

Etymology

Refers to the seasonal pattern of rain in April promoting flowers in May in temperate regions; used metaphorically since at least the 16th century.
