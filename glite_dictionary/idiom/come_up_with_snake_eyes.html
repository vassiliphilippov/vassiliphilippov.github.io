to come up with snake eyes

1. VERB

/ kʌm ʌp wɪð sneɪk aɪz / (AmE), / kʌm ʌp wɪð sneɪk aɪz / (BrE)
to experience a very poor or unlucky result; to fail or have bad luck, especially in a crucial situation
to get a very bad or unlucky result

Examples

After hours of preparation, he still came up with snake eyes in the final interview.
Whenever she plays poker, she seems to come up with snake eyes at the worst times.
We invested so much, but unfortunately, we came up with snake eyes on this deal.

Synset Examples

She always seemed to come up with snake eyes when it mattered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to strike out - to fail completely
  • Antonym: to come up roses - to have a successful or favorable outcome

Etymology

From gambling, where 'snake eyes' refers to rolling two ones with dice, the lowest possible roll and considered unlucky.
