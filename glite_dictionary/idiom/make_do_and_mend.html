to make do and mend

1. VERB

/ meɪk duː ənd mend / (AmE), / meɪk duː ənd mend / (BrE)
to manage with limited resources by repairing what you have instead of buying new replacements
manage by repairing instead of replacing
british

Examples

During tough economic times, families had to make do and mend to get by.
My grandmother taught me to make do and mend rather than throwing things away.
Many people now try to make do and mend as a way to live sustainably.

Synset Examples

She always encouraged us to make do and mend our clothes.
It's important to make do and mend instead of wasting resources.
During the war, families had to make do and mend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make do - to manage with what is available

Etymology

Popularized in Britain during World War II as a government campaign encouraging people to save resources by repairing clothes and household items.
