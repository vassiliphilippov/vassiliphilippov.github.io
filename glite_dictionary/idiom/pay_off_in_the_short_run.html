to pay off in the short run

1. VERB

/ peɪ ɑf ɪn ðə ʃɔrt rʌn / (AmE), / peɪ ɒf ɪn ðə ʃɔːt rʌn / (BrE)
to result in benefits or advantages within a relatively short period of time
bring benefits quickly

Examples

Implementing stricter safety measures will pay off in the short run with reduced accidents.
The new advertising campaign may pay off in the short run, but its long-term effects are uncertain.
Studying intensively just before exams might pay off in the short run, but lasting understanding requires consistent effort.

Synset Examples

Investing now will pay off in the short run.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pay off - to yield a profitable or advantageous result
