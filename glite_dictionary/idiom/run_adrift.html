to run adrift

1. VERB

/ rʌn əˈdrɪft / (AmE), / rʌn əˈdrɪft / (BrE)
to float without control or direction at sea; also, to lack guidance or purpose in a situation or activity
to float uncontrolled or lack direction

Examples

After the storm, the boat was left to run adrift in the open ocean.
The company's strategy seems to run adrift without a clear leader.
Without a strong plan, our efforts began to run adrift halfway through the project.

Synset Examples

After the storm, the boat was left to run adrift.
The committee’s decisions seemed to run adrift without leadership.
Without clear guidance, the project began to run adrift.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drift - to be carried slowly by a current of air or water
