to have it your way

1. VERB

/ hæv ɪt jʊr weɪ / (AmE), / hæv ɪt jɔː weɪ / (BrE)
to do, choose, or receive something according to your own preferences or wishes, especially after disagreement or discussion
do or choose as you prefer

Examples

Fine, have it your way—I'll stop arguing and let you decide.
If you want to skip dessert, have it your way and go straight home.
The company slogan invites customers to have it your way when ordering meals.

Synset Examples

She always tells her kids to have it your way when ordering.
At this restaurant, you can have it your way, no problem.
You can customize the sandwich; just have it your way.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get your own way - to achieve what you want, even if others disagree
