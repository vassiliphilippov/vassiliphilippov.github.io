on the loose

1. ADJ

/ ɑn ðə lus / (AmE), / ɒn ðə luːs / (BrE)
having escaped from confinement; free and possibly dangerous or uncontrolled
escaped or free, possibly dangerous

Examples

The police warned that the dangerous animal was on the loose in the city.
With the convict still on the loose, residents were advised to lock their doors.
Several pets got on the loose after the storm damaged their enclosures.

Synset Examples

a convict still at large
searching for two escaped prisoners
dogs loose on the streets
criminals on the loose in the neighborhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at large - escaped, especially from confinement; free

2. ADJ

/ ɑn ðə lus / (AmE), / ɒn ðə luːs / (BrE)
having escaped from a place of confinement or control and therefore potentially dangerous or difficult to find; not captured or restrained.
escaped and not restrained

Examples

The police are searching for a criminal who is on the loose in the city.
Don't leave the doors open; there's a dangerous animal on the loose in the neighborhood.
Parents were worried because a convicted felon was reportedly on the loose nearby.

Synset Examples

The dog is on the loose, so keep your pets inside.
They warned us that a bear was on the loose nearby.
The escaped prisoner is still on the loose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at large - (of a criminal or dangerous animal) having escaped; not captured
