to scratch one's own itch

1. VERB

/ skrætʃ wʌnz oʊn ɪtʃ / (AmE), / skrætʃ wʌnz əʊn ɪtʃ / (BrE)
To address or fulfill a personal need or desire, especially by creating or doing something that solves a problem you yourself have.
address a personal need or desire

Examples

Many entrepreneurs found businesses to scratch their own itch when they can’t find a solution elsewhere.
The software was developed because the creator wanted to scratch his own itch.
She often starts projects that scratch her own itch rather than following market trends.

Synset Examples

She started the project to scratch her own itch for innovation.
He decided to scratch his own itch by creating an app.
By writing that book, he scratched his own itch for storytelling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fulfill a need - to satisfy a personal necessity or requirement
