to come up with the goods

1. VERB

/ kʌm ʌp wɪð ðə ɡʊdz / (AmE), / kʌm ʌp wɪð ðə ɡʊdz / (BrE)
to do what is expected, promised, or required; to deliver results
fulfill expectations or promises

Examples

Whenever there's a challenge, she comes up with the goods and impresses everyone.
The team was under pressure to win, but they came up with the goods in the final match.
If you want that promotion, you'll have to come up with the goods on this project.

Synset Examples

The designer truly came up with the goods for the event.
After weeks of work, he finally came up with the goods.
She always manages to come up with the goods on time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deliver - to provide or produce what is expected or promised
  • Antonym: to fail - to not do what is expected or required
