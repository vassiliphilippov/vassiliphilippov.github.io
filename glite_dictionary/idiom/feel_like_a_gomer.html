to feel like a gomer

1. VERB

/ fil laɪk ə ˈɡoʊmər / (AmE), / fiːl laɪk ə ˈɡəʊmə / (BrE)
to feel foolish, awkward, or incompetent in a situation
to feel foolish or incompetent
informal slang american

Examples

I felt like a gomer when I tripped in front of the whole class.
He always feels like a gomer when trying to assemble furniture.
Trying to cook a fancy meal for the first time, she felt like a gomer.

Synset Examples

She felt like a gomer trying to fix her computer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feel foolish - to experience embarrassment or a lack of competence
  • Antonym: feel confident - to feel self-assured and capable

Etymology

From 'gomer,' US slang for a foolish or inept person, possibly of unknown origin; popularized in medical slang.
