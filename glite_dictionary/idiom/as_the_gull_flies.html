as the gull flies

1. ADV

/ æz ðə ɡʌl flaɪz / (AmE), / az ðə ɡʌl flaɪz / (BrE)
measured in a straight line between two places, rather than by the route taken on roads or paths
in a straight line (direct distance)

Examples

The lighthouse is just three miles away as the gull flies, but much farther if you follow the winding roads.
We could see the island from the shore, less than a mile as the gull flies.
His house is only a short distance from mine as the gull flies.

Synset Examples

It's a short distance to the lake, as the gull flies.
The beach is about two miles away, as the gull flies.
The park is close, as the gull flies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: as the crow flies - in a straight line; direct distance

Etymology

Modelled on the earlier idiom 'as the crow flies'; 'gull' substituted for 'crow', especially in coastal contexts.
