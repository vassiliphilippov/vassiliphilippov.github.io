to cudgel my brain

1. VERB

/ ˈkʌdʒəl maɪ ˈbreɪn / (AmE), / ˈkʌdʒəl maɪ ˈbreɪn / (BrE)
to think very hard or make a great mental effort, especially when trying to remember something or solve a difficult problem.
think very hard; struggle to remember

Examples

I had to cudgel my brain to recall where I had seen him before.
She cudgelled her brain for hours, but still couldn't solve the puzzle.
Whenever I'm asked a tricky question, I cudgel my brain for the answer.

Synset Examples

I had to cudgel my brain to remember her name.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rack my brain - to think very hard; to make a great effort to remember or solve something

Etymology

From the verb 'cudgel', meaning to beat with a cudgel (a short, thick stick), figuratively applied to the brain from the early 17th century.
