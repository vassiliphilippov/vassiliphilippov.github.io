on solid ground

1. ADP

/ ɑn ˈsɑlɪd ˈɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ɒn ˈsɒlɪd ˈɡraʊnd / (BrE)
in a situation where one feels secure, confident, or stable, especially after a period of uncertainty
in a secure or stable position

Examples

After switching jobs, Maria finally felt on solid ground in her new career.
The team's finances are now on solid ground thanks to a successful fundraising campaign.
He needs more evidence to feel on solid ground with his theory.

Synset Examples

She felt on solid ground after passing the test.
After months of work, we are finally on solid ground.
His arguments are based on solid ground in research.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: on thin ice - in a risky or dangerous situation
