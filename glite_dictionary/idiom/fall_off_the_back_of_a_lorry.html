to fall off the back of a lorry

1. VERB

/ fɔl ɑf ðə bæk əv ə ˈtrʌk / (AmE), / fɔːl ɒf ðə bæk əv ə ˈlɒri / (BrE)
(of goods) to be acquired under suspicious circumstances, especially at an unusually low price, typically implying that they were stolen.
acquired goods, probably stolen
informal british

Examples

He claims that his designer watch fell off the back of a lorry, but I don't believe him.
Those smartphones are so cheap, they must have fallen off the back of a lorry.
She joked that her new handbag fell off the back of a lorry when asked about the price.

Synset Examples

He got a great deal; it must have fallen off the back of a lorry.
She found a fancy dress that seemed to fall off the back of a truck.
I can't believe you bought that; it looks like it fell off the back of a lorry.
That new TV looks like it fell off the back of a lorry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hot - (of goods) stolen or obtained illegally

Etymology

British English idiom originating from the idea of stolen goods being explained as having 'fallen off' a vehicle during transportation.
