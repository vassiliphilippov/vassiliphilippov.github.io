sweet summer child

1. NOUN

/ swit ˈsʌmɚ tʃaɪld / (AmE), / swiːt ˈsʌmə tʃaɪld / (BrE)
a person who is innocent or naive, often displaying a trusting optimism about the world
innocent, naive, or trusting person

Examples

You really are a sweet summer child if you believe he’ll finish the project on time.
As a sweet summer child, she never imagined her friends could betray her trust.
He looked at me, shook his head, and said, "Oh, you sweet summer child."

Synset Examples

He’s such a sweet summer child, always seeing the good in everyone.
Her optimism makes her the sweet summer child of our group.
Being a sweet summer child, she enjoys life to the fullest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naive - having or showing a lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment
  • Synonym: innocent - free from guilt or wrongdoing; also, lacking experience or sophistication

Etymology

Popularized by the television series Game of Thrones, originally based on George R. R. Martin’s novels.
