to chew the meat and spit out the bones

1. VERB

/ tʃu ðə mit ənd spɪt aʊt ðə boʊnz / (AmE), / tʃuː ðə miːt ənd spɪt aʊt ðə bəʊnz / (BrE)
to accept the useful or valuable parts of something and discard what is not useful or relevant
accept the valuable and discard the rest

Examples

In any self-help book, it's wise to chew the meat and spit out the bones.
When it comes to feedback, remember to chew the meat and spit out the bones.
We should chew the meat and spit out the bones from the many opinions offered.

Synset Examples

You should chew the meat and spit out the bones of that criticism.
We can chew the meat and spit out the bones of the proposal later.
In this meeting, let’s chew the meat and spit out the bones of the feedback.
