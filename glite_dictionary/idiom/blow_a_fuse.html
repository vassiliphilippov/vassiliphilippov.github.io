to blow a fuse

1. VERB

/ bloʊ ə fjuz / (AmE), / bləʊ ə fjuːz / (BrE)
to suddenly become very angry or enraged
become very angry suddenly

Examples

When I saw the mess in the kitchen, I couldn't help but blow a fuse.
Don't blow a fuse just because someone made a mistake at work.
She almost blew a fuse when her computer crashed before she could save her document.

Synset Examples

The professor combusted when the student didn't know the answer to a very elementary question
Spam makes me go ballistic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lose one's temper - to suddenly become angry
  • Antonym: to keep one's cool - to remain calm and not become angry

Etymology

Originally referring to an electrical fuse burning out and abruptly stopping a circuit.
