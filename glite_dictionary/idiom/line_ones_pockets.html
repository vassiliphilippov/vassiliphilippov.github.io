to line one's pocket(s)

1. VERB

/ laɪn wʌnz ˈpɑkɪts / (AmE), / laɪn wʌnz ˈpɒkɪts / (BrE)
to make money, often dishonestly or by using one's position or influence unethically
make money dishonestly or unethically

Examples

Many believed the officials used their power only to line their pockets.
She exposed the executives who had been lining their pockets through fraudulent schemes.
He denied ever attempting to line his pocket with company funds.

Synset Examples

He's just trying to line his pockets with that shady deal.
She was accused of trying to line her pockets at the charity's expense.
It's unethical to line someone's pockets in exchange for favors.
Politicians often line their pockets with donations from lobbyists.
The corrupt contractor lined his pockets with taxpayer money.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embezzle - steal or misappropriate money placed in one's trust
  • Synonym: corrupt - acting dishonestly for personal gain

Etymology

From the literal sense of 'lining' (filling) one’s pockets with money; used figuratively since the 17th century.
