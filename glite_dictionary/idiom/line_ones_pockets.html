to line one's pockets

1. VERB

/ laɪn wʌnz ˈpɑkɪts / (AmE), / laɪn wʌnz ˈpɒkɪts / (BrE)
to make a lot of money, especially in a dishonest or selfish way
make a lot of money (often dishonestly)

Examples

The corrupt official used his position to line his pockets with public funds.
Some contractors were found to be lining their pockets at the taxpayers' expense.
He entered politics not to serve the people but to line his pockets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profit - gain money, especially as the result of business activity

2. VERB

/ laɪn wʌnz ˈpɑːkɪts / (AmE), / laɪn wʌnz ˈpɒkɪts / (BrE)
to make money, especially by dishonest, unfair, or unethical means
make money dishonestly

Examples

The corrupt official used his position to line his pockets with public funds.
Critics claimed the company was only interested in lining its pockets rather than helping customers.
There were allegations that the contractors had been lining their pockets at the taxpayers' expense.

Synset Examples

They accused the company of lining its pockets with unfair pricing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embezzle - to steal money that you are responsible for or that belongs to your employer
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere

Etymology

Metaphoric use of 'line' meaning to fill and 'pockets' as a container for money, first recorded in the 17th century.
