in the ballpark

1. ADJ

/ ɪn ðə ˈbɔlˌpɑrk / (AmE), / ɪn ðə ˈbɔːlpɑːk / (BrE)
approximately correct or within a reasonable or expected range
approximately correct; within a reasonable range

Examples

Your numbers are in the ballpark, but we will need to check the details.
I'm not sure of the exact price, but $500 should be in the ballpark.
Her guess was in the ballpark of what I expected.

Synset Examples

His performance was in the ballpark of what we wanted.
Her estimate was in the ballpark of what I expected.
The final cost was in the ballpark of our budget.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: close - near in amount or degree
  • Synonym: estimate - an approximate calculation or judgment
  • Antonym: exact - completely correct or precise

Etymology

From American baseball, where the 'ballpark' denotes the stadium, metaphorically extended to mean 'within the correct area.'
