by all means

1. ADV

/ baɪ ɔl minz / (AmE), / baɪ ɔːl miːnz / (BrE)
used to give someone permission or to strongly encourage them to do something; certainly; of course
certainly; giving permission

Examples

If you have any questions, by all means ask them now.
You can borrow my car by all means if you need it for the weekend.
He said I could use his office by all means whenever he's away.

Synset Examples

Visit us by all means

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certainly - without any doubt
  • Antonym: by no means - not at all; in no way
