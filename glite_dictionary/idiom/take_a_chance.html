to take a chance

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə tʃæns / (AmE), / teɪk ə tʃɑːns / (BrE)
to do something that involves risk in order to gain an advantage or achieve something
do something risky for potential gain

Examples

You sometimes have to take a chance if you want to succeed in life.
She decided to take a chance and move to a new city for her career.
Despite his doubts, he took a chance on the investment and it paid off.

Synset Examples

Sometimes, you must take a chance to achieve your dreams.
You should take a chance and apply for that job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: risk - act or process of doing something involving danger or loss
