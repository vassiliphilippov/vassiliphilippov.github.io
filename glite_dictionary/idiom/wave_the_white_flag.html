to wave the white flag

1. VERB

/ weɪv ðə waɪt flæɡ / (AmE), / weɪv ðə waɪt flæɡ / (BrE)
to admit defeat or surrender, especially after a struggle or conflict
to surrender or admit defeat

Examples

Eventually, the exhausted team decided to wave the white flag and accept defeat.
Rather than argue any further, she chose to wave the white flag and apologise.
When their efforts failed to solve the problem, they all waved the white flag and moved on.

Synset Examples

It’s time to wave the white flag and seek help.
After hours of negotiation, they decided to wave the white flag.
He finally waved the white flag and admitted defeat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surrender - to cease resisting and submit
  • Synonym: to give up - to stop making an effort; admit defeat
  • Antonym: to resist - to withstand the action or effect of something

Etymology

From the practice of raising a white flag to signal surrender in battle, dating back to the 17th century.
