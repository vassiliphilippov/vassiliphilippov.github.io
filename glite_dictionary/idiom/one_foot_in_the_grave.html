one foot in the grave

1. X

/ wʌn fʊt ɪn ðə ɡreɪv / (AmE), / wʌn fʊt ɪn ðə ɡreɪv / (BrE)
very close to dying, or in a state of serious decline or trouble, usually because of old age or poor health
nearly dead or in serious trouble

Examples

My grandfather jokes that he has one foot in the grave, but he still enjoys gardening every day.
After the serious accident, doctors feared she had one foot in the grave.
He works so hard he says he feels like he has one foot in the grave already.

Synset Examples

She joked that her lack of exercise means she has one foot in the grave.
His bad habits make it seem like he has one foot in the grave.
After that scare, I feel like I have one foot in the grave.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at death's door - close to death or very ill
