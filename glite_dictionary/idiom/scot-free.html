scot-free

1. ADV

/ skɑt fri / (AmE), / skɒt friː / (BrE)
without suffering any punishment or harm for something wrong that has been done
without punishment or penalty

Examples

Despite overwhelming evidence, he walked scot-free from the courtroom.
Many believed the company got away scot-free after the scandal.
She caused the problem but managed to remain scot-free while others took the blame.

Synset Examples

he went scot-free

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unpunished - not punished for wrongdoing or crime
  • Antonym: culpable - deserving blame; guilty

Etymology

From Old English *scot* (a tax or payment) and *free*; originally referred to someone escaping payment.
