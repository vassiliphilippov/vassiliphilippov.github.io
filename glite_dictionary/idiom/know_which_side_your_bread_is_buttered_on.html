to know which side your bread is buttered on

1. VERB

/ noʊ wɪtʃ saɪd jʊr brɛd ɪz ˈbʌtərd ɑn / (AmE), / nəʊ wɪtʃ saɪd jɔː brɛd ɪz ˈbʌtəd ɒn / (BrE)
to be aware of where your advantage or best interests lie and act accordingly
know where your best interests lie

Examples

He always supports the winning team because he knows which side his bread is buttered on.
In the office, Maria stays neutral in conflicts since she knows which side her bread is buttered on.
Politicians often change their stance when they know which side their bread is buttered on.

Synset Examples

In negotiations, he always plays nice; he knows which side his bread is buttered on.
He always sides with the boss; he knows which side his bread is buttered on.
