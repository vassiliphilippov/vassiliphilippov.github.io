hell mend him

1. X

/ hɛl mɛnd hɪm / (AmE), / hɛl mɛnd hɪm / (BrE)
used to express that someone should suffer the consequences of their actions, often with indifference or lack of sympathy
let someone suffer their own consequences
british

Examples

He was warned about the risks, but hell mend him if he ignores them.
If she spends all her money again, hell mend her.
If they want to ignore advice, then hell mend them.

Synset Examples

If he keeps lying, hell mend him.
She said, 'If he doesn't change, hell mend him.'
I told him to be careful; hell mend him if he falls.

Etymology

Probably of Scottish origin; 'hell mend' meaning 'may hell repair or punish'.
