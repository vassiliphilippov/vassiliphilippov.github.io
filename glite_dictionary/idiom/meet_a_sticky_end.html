to meet a sticky end

1. VERB

/ mit ə ˈstɪki ɛnd / (AmE), / miːt ə ˈstɪki end / (BrE)
to come to an unpleasant, dangerous, or fatal conclusion or situation
come to an unpleasant end

Examples

Many believe that the corrupt politician will meet a sticky end if he keeps taking bribes.
In the movie, the villain finally meets a sticky end after years of wrongdoing.
If you continue driving so recklessly, you might meet a sticky end one day.

Synset Examples

He warned his friends they could meet a sticky end.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: come to a bad end - to end badly or come to ruin
  • Antonym: escape unscathed - to avoid harm or trouble
