to fall off someone's radar

1. VERB

/ fɔl ɔf ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈreɪˌdɑr / (AmE), / fɔːl ɒf ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈreɪˌdɑː / (BrE)
to become less noticeable or important to someone; to cease being the focus of attention
become less important or noticeable

Examples

After the project ended, Steven fell off her radar entirely and they lost contact.
New artists often fall off someone's radar after a few years if they don't release new work.
She was surprised at how quickly the issue fell off his radar once something else came up.

Synset Examples

She quickly fell off his radar after moving away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slip someone's mind - to be forgotten by someone
  • Synonym: be off the map - to become obscure or unknown

Etymology

Metaphor from radar technology: when something is no longer detected, it is no longer observed or tracked.
