bone of contention

1. NOUN

/ boʊn əv kənˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / bəʊn əv kənˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
an issue or point of disagreement that causes dispute between people
subject of dispute or disagreement

Examples

The inheritance quickly became a bone of contention among family members.
The new policy is likely to be a bone of contention between management and staff.
Parking spaces in the building have long been a bone of contention for residents.

Synset Examples

the real bone of contention, as you know, is money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sticking point - an obstacle causing disagreement in negotiations
  • Antonym: agreement - a state of harmony or accord

Etymology

From the metaphor of a bone fought over by dogs, used since the 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ boʊn əv kənˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / bəʊn əv kənˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
a subject or issue over which people disagree or argue
subject of disagreement

Examples

The new company policy quickly became a bone of contention among the employees.
Parking spaces have always been a bone of contention in this neighborhood.
Their father's will was a bone of contention between the siblings for years.

Synset Examples

Their differing opinions on the project were a bone of contention.
The budget has become a bone of contention among team members.
The election date was a bone of contention for many voters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sticking point - an obstacle or issue causing disagreement or delay
  • Synonym: matter of dispute - a subject that people are arguing about

Etymology

From the literal sense of a bone that animals fight over, figuratively used in English since at least the 1700s.
