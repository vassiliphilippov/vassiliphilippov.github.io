to feel like a pinball

1. VERB

/ fil laɪk ə ˈpɪnˌbɔl / (AmE), / fiːl laɪk ə ˈpɪnbɔːl / (BrE)
to feel disoriented and directionless, as if being pushed from one thing to another without control
feel disoriented and directionless

Examples

After changing jobs three times in one year, she felt like a pinball moving through her career.
With constant interruptions and shifting priorities, the manager often feels like a pinball in the office.
He was so overwhelmed by unexpected events that he felt like a pinball bouncing around all day.

Synset Examples

He felt like a pinball, bouncing from task to task.

Etymology

From 'pinball', a game where the ball bounces erratically; used figuratively since the late 20th century.
