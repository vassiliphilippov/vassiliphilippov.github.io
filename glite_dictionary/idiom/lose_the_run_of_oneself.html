to lose the run of oneself

1. VERB

/ luːz ðə rʌn əv wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / luːz ðə rʌn əv wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to become confused, disoriented, or behave in an irrational or uncontrolled way, especially due to emotion or excitement
become confused or lose self-control
british

Examples

You shouldn't lose the run of yourself just because things aren't going your way.
After winning the award, he lost the run of himself and started acting arrogant.
Try not to lose the run of yourself in front of the crowd.

Synset Examples

He tends to lose the run of himself when excited.
Don't lose the run of yourself during the competition.
After the party, I completely lost the run of myself.

Etymology

Primarily used in Irish English; 'run' in this context refers to the direction or course of oneself.
