to show one's true colors

1. VERB

/ ʃoʊ wʌnz tru ˈkʌlərz / (AmE), / ʃəʊ wʌnz truː ˈkʌləz / (BrE)
to reveal one's real character, intentions, or feelings, especially when they are previously hidden or different from what was pretended
reveal true character or intentions

Examples

During the crisis, the mayor really showed his true colors by prioritizing personal gain over public welfare.
You only show your true colors when faced with difficult decisions.
It wasn't until we worked together under stress that she showed her true colors.

Synset Examples

After the argument, he really showed his true colors.
Under pressure, she showed her true stripes as a leader.
In tough times, people often show their true colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unmask - to reveal true character or identity
  • Antonym: disguise - to hide one's true identity or character

Etymology

Naval origin; refers to a ship revealing its true flag (colours) in the 18th century.
