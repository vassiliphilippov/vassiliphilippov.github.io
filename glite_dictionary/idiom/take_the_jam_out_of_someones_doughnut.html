to take the jam out of someone's doughnut

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə dʒæm aʊt əv ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈdoʊˌnʌt / (AmE), / teɪk ðə dʒæm aʊt ɒv ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈdəʊnʌt / (BrE)
to make someone feel disappointed, unhappy, or irritable, often by spoiling their mood or enjoyment
spoil someone's happiness or mood
informal

Examples

Hearing the bad news really took the jam out of her doughnut this morning.
The sudden cancellation took the jam out of my doughnut, and I couldn't hide my disappointment.
Don't let criticism take the jam out of your doughnut; keep smiling!

Synset Examples

You seem grumpy; who took the jam out of your doughnut?
