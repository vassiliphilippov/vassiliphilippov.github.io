to have legs

1. VERB

/ hæv lɛɡz / (AmE), / hæv lɛɡz / (BrE)
(of an idea, project, or product) to have potential to be successful or to continue for a long time.
to have potential for lasting success

Examples

Many investors believe the startup could have legs in the competitive tech market.
If the television show continues to attract viewers, it will definitely have legs.
That trend doesn't seem to have legs—it will probably disappear in a few months.

Synset Examples

The project has legs and should succeed soon.
That new idea will definitely have legs in the market.
If the campaign resonates, it will have legs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take off - to quickly become successful or popular
