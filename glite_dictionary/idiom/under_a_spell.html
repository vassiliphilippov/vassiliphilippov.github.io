under a spell

1. ADJ

/ ˈʌndɚ ə spɛl / (AmE), / ˈʌndə ə spɛl / (BrE)
so fascinated or influenced by someone or something that you seem to have no control over your actions or feelings
entranced; fascinated; captivated

Examples

She listened to the storyteller under a spell, unable to look away.
Ever since he started his new job, he’s been under a spell and talks of nothing else.
The audience sat under a spell throughout the magical performance.

Synset Examples

He felt like he was under a spell when he met her.
The children were mesmerized, as if under a spell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spellbound - unable to move because of being so amazed or interested
  • Synonym: mesmerized - captivated or hypnotized by someone or something
