to take a mile

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə maɪl / (AmE), / teɪk ə maɪl / (BrE)
to exploit a small concession or favor, often by demanding much more than was initially offered
exploit a small favor or concession

Examples

You need to set boundaries, or they'll take a mile every time you compromise.
If you let your employees leave early once, some may try to take a mile in the future.
She agreed to one small request, but he tried to take a mile and asked for much more.

Synset Examples

Be careful; give her an inch and she will take a mile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: give an inch - make a small concession or offer a little help
