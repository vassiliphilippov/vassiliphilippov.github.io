over and out

1. X

/ ˈoʊvɚ ənd aʊt / (AmE), / ˈəʊvə ənd aʊt / (BrE)
used to signal the end of a conversation, especially in radio communication; said when finishing speaking or signing off
phrase to end a communication

Examples

At the end of the transmission, the operator said, 'over and out.'
She finished the call with a lighthearted 'over and out.'
With that, he ended his speech: 'over and out.'

Synset Examples

He signed off the call with a cheerful, 'over and out!'
He finished his report with a simple, 'over and out.'
The captain concluded the mission briefing with, 'over and out.'
After the meeting, she said, 'Alright, over and out!'

Etymology

Originated from radio communication procedures.
