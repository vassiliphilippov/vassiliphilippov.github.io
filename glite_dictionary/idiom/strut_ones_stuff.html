to strut one's stuff

1. VERB

/ strʌt wʌnz stʌf / (AmE), / strʌt wʌnz stʌf / (BrE)
to confidently display one's abilities, achievements, or physical appearance in order to impress others
show off one's abilities or talents

Examples

She loves to strut her stuff on the stage during open mic nights.
After his promotion, he couldn't wait to strut his stuff at the staff meeting.
Contestants took turns to strut their stuff on the runway in front of the judges.

Synset Examples

He walked in to strut his new outfit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to show off - to display something ostentatiously or boastfully
