in the dock

1. ADV

/ ɪn ðə dɑk / (AmE), / ɪn ðə dɒk / (BrE)
in a situation where one is accused of wrongdoing or is on trial, especially in a court of law
accused or on trial
british

Examples

The politician found himself in the dock after the allegations came to light.
Several executives were in the dock for fraud charges.
The company's practices have been in the dock in recent weeks.

Synset Examples

The CEO found himself in the dock over the company's financial scandal.
The defendant was in the dock, awaiting the jury's verdict.
After the investigation, several board members were in the dock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on trial - facing judgment in court
  • Synonym: under suspicion - being suspected of wrongdoing

Etymology

From the physical dock in British courts where defendants stand or sit during trial.
