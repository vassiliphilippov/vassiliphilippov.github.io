to force someone's hand

1. VERB

/ fɔrs sʌmwʌnz hænd / (AmE), / fɔːs sʌmwʌnz hænd / (BrE)
to make someone do something or make a decision sooner than they intended or before they are ready
compel someone to act or decide

Examples

The committee's decision to announce early forced her hand before she was ready.
They tried to stay silent, but the growing crisis forced his hand.
Rising costs eventually forced their hand to raise prices.

Synset Examples

The deadline forced her hand to make a decision.
The circumstances forced my hand to act quickly.
They had to force his hand to reveal the truth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to compel - to force someone to do something
  • Synonym: pressure - to apply influence or force to get someone to act
