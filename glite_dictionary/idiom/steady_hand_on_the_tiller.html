steady hand on the tiller

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛdi hænd ɑn ðə ˈtɪlər / (AmE), / ˈstɛdi hand ɒn ðə ˈtɪlə / (BrE)
calm and skillful control or management, especially in difficult situations
calm, skillful control in tough times

Examples

The new CEO brought a steady hand on the tiller during the company's financial crisis.
In times of political uncertainty, voters seek leaders with a steady hand on the tiller.
With a steady hand on the tiller, she guided her team through the challenging project.

Synset Examples

A steady hand on the tiller is crucial in storms.
She has a steady hand on the tiller while sailing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steady hand - consistent and calm control

Etymology

Based on the nautical metaphor of steering a boat with a tiller (a steering lever).
