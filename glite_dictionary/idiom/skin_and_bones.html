skin and bones

1. NOUN

/ skɪn ənd boʊnz / (AmE), / skɪn ənd bəʊnz / (BrE)
Extremely thin or emaciated, with little flesh on one's body.
extremely thin; emaciated

Examples

After weeks in the hospital, he was nothing but skin and bones.
The rescued cat was so skin and bones that you could see her ribs.
She became worried when her friend started looking like skin and bones.

Synset Examples

After his illness, he looked like skin and bones.
The stray dog was nothing but skin and bones.
She felt weak and frail, just skin and bones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emaciated - abnormally thin or weak, often due to illness or lack of food
