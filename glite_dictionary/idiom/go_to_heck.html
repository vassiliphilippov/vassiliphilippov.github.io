to go to heck

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ tə hɛk / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə hɛk / (BrE)
(informal) Used as a mild or euphemistic way to tell someone to go away, expressing anger or dismissal.
tell someone to go away (milder form)
informal

Examples

When Jim refused to cooperate, his boss finally told him to go to heck.
She laughed off the criticism and said everyone could just go to heck.
After hearing the rude remark, Mark told his friend to go to heck and walked away.

Synset Examples

If you don’t like my decision, go to heck.
When facing criticism, he simply said to go to heck.
I told him to go to heck after his comments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go to hell - a strong way of telling someone to go away or expressing anger

Etymology

Euphemistic alteration of 'go to hell,' with 'heck' as a milder substitute for 'hell.'
