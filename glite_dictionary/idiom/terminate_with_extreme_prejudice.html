to terminate with extreme prejudice

1. VERB

/ ˈtɜrmɪˌneɪt wɪð ɪkˈstrim ˈprɛdʒədɪs / (AmE), / ˈtɜːmɪneɪt wɪð ɪkˈstriːm ˈprɛdʒʊdɪs / (BrE)
to kill or eliminate someone in a decisive or ruthless manner, especially as part of a covert operation
to eliminate ruthlessly (often euphemistic)

Examples

The agency decided to terminate with extreme prejudice those involved in the plot.
His orders were to terminate with extreme prejudice if the target could not be captured.
Rumors spread that the government would terminate with extreme prejudice anyone threatening national security.

Synset Examples

The mission was to terminate with extreme prejudice.
He was ordered to terminate with extreme prejudice to protect the operation.
They had to terminate with extreme prejudice due to the threat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assassinate - to murder (an important person) for political or religious reasons
  • Synonym: to eliminate - to completely remove or get rid of

Etymology

Popularized during the Vietnam War era, the phrase was used as military jargon in US intelligence circles.
