to cut through the noise

1. VERB

/ kʌt θru ðə nɔɪz / (AmE), / kʌt θruː ðə nɔɪz / (BrE)
to communicate a message clearly and effectively despite distractions or competing information
communicate effectively despite distractions

Examples

The CEO's simple explanation helped the company cut through the noise during the crisis.
In a crowded market, strong branding can cut through the noise and reach customers.
Her insightful comments always cut through the noise in meetings and get everyone focused.

Synset Examples

He managed to cut through the noise with his speech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get the message across - to successfully communicate an idea or information
  • Synonym: be heard above the din - to be noticed or listened to despite a lot of noise or distractions
