to follow to the grave

1. VERB

/ ˈfɑːloʊ tu ðə ɡreɪv / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊ tuː ðə ɡreɪv / (BrE)
to remain loyal or committed to someone or something until death
stay loyal until death

Examples

No matter what happened between them, she promised she would follow him to the grave.
The knight vowed to follow his king to the grave, demonstrating absolute loyalty.
They shared a bond so deep that they would follow each other to the grave if necessary.

Synset Examples

She felt he would follow her to the grave if needed.
Their love was so strong, it seemed they would follow to the grave.
