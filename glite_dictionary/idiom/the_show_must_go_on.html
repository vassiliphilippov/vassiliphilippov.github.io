the show must go on

1. X

/ ðə ʃoʊ məs(t) ɡoʊ ɑn / (AmE), / ðə ʃəʊ məst ɡəʊ ɒn / (BrE)
used to say that an event or activity must continue even if there are problems or difficulties
activities continue despite problems

Examples

Even though the lead actor was ill, the show must go on, so the understudy took his place.
Financial troubles threatened the festival, but organizers believed the show must go on.
After the technical difficulties, the director reminded everyone that the show must go on.

Synset Examples

Despite the rain, the show must go on.
No matter what happens, the show must go on.
Even with a few hiccups, the show must go on.

Etymology

Originated in the 19th-century circus and theatrical world, reflecting the tradition that shows should continue despite mishaps.
