Davy Jones's locker

1. NOUN

/ ˌdeɪvi ˈdʒoʊnz ɪz ˈlɑkər / (AmE), / ˌdeɪvi ˈdʒəʊnz ɪz ˈlɒkə / (BrE)
the bottom of the sea, especially regarded as the final resting place of drowned sailors
bottom of the sea; sailors' resting place

Examples

The ship was lost in the storm and now lies in Davy Jones's locker.
Many a sailor has feared meeting Davy Jones's locker during rough seas.
The treasure hunters hoped to recover artifacts from Davy Jones's locker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ocean floor - the bottom surface of the ocean

Etymology

From nautical folklore; 'Davy Jones' is the spirit of the sea, first recorded in the 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪvi ˈdʒoʊnzəz ˈlɑkər / (AmE), / ˈdeɪvi ˈdʒəʊnzɪz ˈlɒkə / (BrE)
the bottom of the sea, especially regarded as a grave for drowned sailors; used metaphorically for the final resting place of lost things or people
the bottom of the sea; sailor's grave

Examples

Sailors feared their ship might go down to Davy Jones's locker during the storm.
He tossed the broken compass overboard, consigning it to Davy Jones's locker.
Many treasures are rumored to lie hidden in Davy Jones's locker at the sea's bottom.

Synset Examples

She joked that her lost keys were now in Davy Jones's locker.
The old shipwreck sank straight into Davy Jones's locker.
The treasure map led to Davy Jones's locker, full of secrets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the deep - the sea, especially regarded as mysterious or dangerous
  • Synonym: grave - a place where a dead body is buried

Etymology

Originating from nautical folklore of the 1700s, where 'Davy Jones' referred to the spirit of the sea or the devil of the deep.
