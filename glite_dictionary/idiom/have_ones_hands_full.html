to have one's hands full

1. VERB

/ hæv wʌnz hændz fʊl / (AmE), / hæv wʌnz hændz fʊl / (BrE)
to be very busy or occupied with multiple responsibilities or tasks
be very busy or occupied

Examples

With three young children, she really has her hands full these days.
I had my hands full at work and couldn't take a break until late afternoon.
During the busy season, the staff always have their hands full with orders.

Synset Examples

He's got his hands full managing multiple clients.
I can’t help right now; I have my hands full.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be busy - to be actively engaged in activities or work
  • Antonym: be free - to be unoccupied or not busy
