to chew the fat

1. VERB

/ tʃu ðə fæt / (AmE), / tʃuː ðə fæt / (BrE)
to engage in casual conversation or chat informally, often at length
chat informally; have a casual conversation
informal

Examples

We sat around the kitchen table and chewed the fat about old times.
After work, they often meet at the pub to chew the fat before heading home.
The old friends spent hours chewing the fat without ever getting bored.

Synset Examples

the men were sitting in the cafe and shooting the breeze

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shoot the breeze - to talk casually or chat informally
  • Synonym: make small talk - to engage in casual or trivial conversation

Etymology

Origins uncertain; possibly refers to sailors passing time by chewing on salt pork or fat.
