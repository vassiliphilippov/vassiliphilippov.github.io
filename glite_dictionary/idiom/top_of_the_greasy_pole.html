top of the greasy pole

1. NOUN

/ tɑp əv ðə ˈɡrisi poʊl / (AmE), / tɒp əv ðə ˈɡriːsi pəʊl / (BrE)
the highest and most difficult-to-attain position in a highly competitive environment, especially politics or business
highest position in a competitive field

Examples

After years of hard work, she finally reached the top of the greasy pole in her company.
Many politicians are willing to do almost anything to climb to the top of the greasy pole.
It's a challenge to stay at the top of the greasy pole once you've made it there.

Synset Examples

He finally reached the top of the greasy pole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summit - the highest attainable level of achievement
  • Synonym: pinnacle - the most successful point; the culmination
  • Antonym: bottom of the heap - lowest position in a competitive hierarchy

Etymology

Possibly from 19th-century fairground and climbing competitions, referring to the difficulty of climbing a greased pole.
