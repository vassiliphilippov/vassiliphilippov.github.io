to put facts on the ground

1. VERB

/ pʊt fæks ɑn ðə ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / pʊt fækts ɒn ðə ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
to establish or make facts clear and concrete, especially in a practical or observable way; to confirm the truth of something by making it real and evident
establish or confirm facts concretely

Examples

The manager insisted that we put facts on the ground before moving forward with the proposal.
Investigators must put facts on the ground to support their claims in court.
It is important to put facts on the ground so that everyone understands the true situation.

Synset Examples

The team needs to put facts on the ground before making decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to establish - to set up or confirm something on a firm basis
