in a tight spot

1. ADV

/ ɪn ə taɪt spɑt / (AmE), / ɪn ə taɪt spɒt / (BrE)
in a difficult or challenging situation, especially one that is hard to get out of
in a difficult situation

Examples

After forgetting her speech, Maria realized she was in a tight spot during the presentation.
If the company doesn't secure more funding soon, they'll be in a tight spot.
I was in a tight spot when my car broke down late at night.

Synset Examples

I'm in a tight spot with this deadline.
She found herself in a tight spot during the meeting.
He was in a tight spot after losing his wallet.
He found himself in a tough spot, like a pussyhole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in a bind - in a difficult situation
  • Synonym: in a jam - in trouble or difficulty
  • Antonym: out of the woods - no longer in difficulty or danger
