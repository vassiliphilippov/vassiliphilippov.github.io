to toe the line

1. VERB

/ toʊ ðə laɪn / (AmE), / təʊ ðə laɪn / (BrE)
to do what is expected or required, or to conform strictly to rules or standards
conform to expectations or rules

Examples

Employees are expected to toe the line if they want to keep their jobs.
If you don't toe the line, you might get into trouble with the coach.
She refused to toe the line and challenged the company's outdated policies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to conform - to comply with rules, standards, or laws
  • Antonym: rebel - to resist authority or control

Etymology

Originally from the practice of standing with one's toes touching a line, especially in the military or sports contexts, to show compliance.
