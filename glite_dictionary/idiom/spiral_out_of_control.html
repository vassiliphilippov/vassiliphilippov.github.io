to spiral out of control

1. VERB

/ ˈspaɪrəl aʊt əv kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ˈspaɪərəl aʊt əv kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
to become impossible to manage or contain, often worsening rapidly and uncontrollably
become unmanageable or chaotic

Examples

After the initial disagreement, the debate began to spiral out of control.
If we don't address these issues soon, the situation could spiral out of control.
Her anxiety would sometimes spiral out of control, making it hard for her to focus.

Synset Examples

The conversation began to spiral out of control quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get out of hand - become difficult or impossible to control
