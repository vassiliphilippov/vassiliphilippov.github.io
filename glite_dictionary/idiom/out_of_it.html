out of it

1. ADJ

/ aʊt əv ɪt / (AmE), / aʊt əv ɪt / (BrE)
Not included in an activity or social group; feeling or being excluded or detached from what is happening.
excluded from a group or activity

Examples

Ever since I changed schools, I’ve felt so out of it at parties.
She sat in the corner, looking out of it while everyone else danced.
After being away for months, he returned and felt completely out of it among his old friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: left out - not included or excluded from a group or activity

2. ADJ

/ aʊt əv ɪt / (AmE), / aʊt ɒv ɪt / (BrE)
not responsive or aware of what is happening, especially due to tiredness, drugs, illness, or shock; in a dazed or stupefied state.
in a dazed or unresponsive state
informal

Examples

After working a double shift, she was so out of it she barely noticed the bus had arrived.
He was completely out of it after his surgery and didn't recognize anyone for hours.
During the meeting, Mark seemed out of it and didn't contribute to the discussion at all.

Synset Examples

he lay insensible where he had fallen
drugged and senseless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insensible - unable to respond to stimulation; unconscious
  • Antonym: alert - fully aware and attentive
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00574798-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ aʊt ʌv ɪt / (AmE), / aʊt əv ɪt / (BrE)
not aware of what is happening, often because of lack of information or attention; disconnected from current events or surroundings.
unaware or disconnected; uninformed
informal

Examples

After coming back from her vacation, she felt completely out of it during the meeting.
When the conversation turned to politics, I realized I was out of it and didn't understand the issues.
He looked tired and out of it after staying up all night studying.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: with it - aware and up to date with events or trends
  • Synonym: in the loop - aware or informed about something, especially news or information
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
