dead in the water

1. ADJ

/ dɛd ɪn ðə ˈwɔtər / (AmE), / dɛd ɪn ðə ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
completely unable to succeed or make progress; having no chance of continuing effectively
unable to succeed or progress

Examples

After the budget was rejected, the project was dead in the water.
His campaign was dead in the water before it even began.
The company's expansion plans are dead in the water due to lack of funding.

Synset Examples

Without support, the plan was dead in the water.
The proposal was dead in the water after the meeting.
After the scandal, their reputation was dead in the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doomed - certain to fail; having no chance of success
  • Synonym: hopeless - having no hope; impossible to succeed

Etymology

Originally a nautical phrase referring to a ship unable to move due to lack of wind or engine power.
