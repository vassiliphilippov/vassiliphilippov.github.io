under fire

1. ADJ

/ ˈʌndɚ ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈʌndə ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
being attacked with weapons or strongly criticized; under attack or scrutiny.
being attacked or criticized

Examples

The company's policies have come under fire from environmental groups.
After the scandal broke, the minister was under fire from the media.
Rescue workers came under fire while trying to reach the injured soldier.

Synset Examples

an official under fire for mismanagement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: under attack - being attacked, especially physically or verbally
  • Synonym: in the line of fire - in a position where you are likely to be shot or criticized

2. ADJ

/ ˈʌndɚ ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈʌndə ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
being subjected to criticism or attack, especially in public
being criticized or attacked

Examples

The company came under fire for its handling of customer complaints.
The coach has been under fire after several disappointing losses this season.
Journalists are under fire for spreading misinformation online.

Synset Examples

The team was under fire after their poor performance.
He was under fire from critics for his latest film.
The politician was under fire for her controversial statements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: under attack - being attacked or criticized; a similar phrase
  • Degree Lower: in the spotlight - receiving a lot of public attention, often critical
