to scare the bejesus

1. VERB

/ skɛr ðə bəˈdʒizəs / (AmE), / skeə ðə bəˈdʒiːzəs / (BrE)
to frighten someone very much; to cause extreme surprise or shock
frighten someone greatly
informal

Examples

That horror movie scared the bejesus out of me last night.
The sudden lightning strike scared the bejesus out of the campers.
Don't sneak up on me like that—you nearly scared the bejesus out of me!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scare the daylights - frighten someone intensely
  • Degree Lower: to startle - to surprise someone suddenly and slightly
  • Degree Higher: to terrify - to frighten someone extremely

Etymology

From 'bejesus', an exclamation derived from 'by Jesus', used as a mild oath.
