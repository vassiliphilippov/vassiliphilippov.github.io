to stand up and be counted

1. VERB

/ stænd ʌp ənd bi ˈkaʊntɪd / (AmE), / stænd ʌp ənd bi ˈkaʊntɪd / (BrE)
to publicly show your support for something, especially when it involves taking a risk or showing courage
declare support publicly; show courage

Examples

When the company was in trouble, he decided to stand up and be counted despite the risk.
It's important for citizens to stand up and be counted in times of crisis.
Many activists chose to stand up and be counted by joining the march for equal rights.

Synset Examples

It's time to stand up and be counted.
If you believe in this cause, stand up and be counted.
It's time to stand up and be counted for our rights.
Don't be afraid; stand up and be counted among supporters.
