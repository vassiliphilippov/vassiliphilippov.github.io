to cut red tape

1. VERB

/ kʌt rɛd teɪp / (AmE), / kʌt rɛd teɪp / (BrE)
to eliminate bureaucratic obstacles or unnecessary administrative procedures
eliminate bureaucratic obstacles

Examples

The mayor vowed to cut red tape to encourage new business investment in the city.
If we cut red tape, we can launch the project much sooner than expected.
Her main priority was to cut red tape and simplify the permit process for homeowners.

Synset Examples

We need to cut red tape to speed up the approval process.
The government promised to cut red tape for entrepreneurs.
The new policy aims to cut red tape for small businesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remove red tape - to remove bureaucratic processes or restrictions
  • Antonym: impose red tape - to add bureaucratic processes or restrictions

Etymology

The phrase 'red tape' refers to the red or pink tape formerly used to bind legal documents in Britain; used figuratively for excessive bureaucracy since the 19th century.
