to take up the gauntlet

1. VERB

/ teɪk ʌp ðə ˈɡɔntlət / (AmE), / teɪk ʌp ðə ˈɡɔːntlət / (BrE)
to accept a challenge, especially one presented publicly or in a confrontational way
accept a challenge (idiom)

Examples

Though she was hesitant at first, Maria chose to take up the gauntlet and debate her opponent.
When the company challenged its rivals, the CEO was quick to take up the gauntlet.
After the controversial remarks, he promised to take up the gauntlet and prove his critics wrong.

Synset Examples

She decided to take up the gauntlet and lead the project.
He took up the gauntlet to challenge the reigning champion.
They took up the gauntlet to improve their community.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to throw down the gauntlet - to issue a challenge to someone, esp. in a public or forceful way

Etymology

From the medieval practice of issuing a challenge by throwing down a gauntlet (armored glove); picking it up meant accepting the challenge.
