orh hor

1. INTJ

/ ɔ hɔr / (AmE), / ɔː hɔː / (BrE)
used to express playful admission, surprise, or mild shock, especially in informal conversation
expression of playful admission or surprise
informal

Examples

Orh hor, you finally admitted it!
He spilled the drink and someone immediately went, 'orh hor!'
When the teacher caught us sneaking snacks, my friend whispered, 'orh hor!'

Synset Examples

She laughed and said, 'Orh hor, you caught me!'
He exclaimed, 'Orh hor, that was a surprise!'
When he saw the gift, he couldn't help but say, 'Orh hor!'

Etymology

From Hokkien, used in Singaporean and Malaysian English as an exclamation.
