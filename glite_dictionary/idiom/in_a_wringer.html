in a wringer

1. ADP

/ ɪn ə ˈrɪŋər / (AmE), / ɪn ə ˈrɪŋə / (BrE)
in a difficult or stressful situation; under pressure or distress
in a stressful or difficult situation

Examples

After his boss found out about the mistake, he really felt in a wringer at work.
Whenever deadlines pile up, I always end up in a wringer trying to get everything done.
She was in a wringer after being questioned by the police for hours.

Synset Examples

Don’t get your tits in a wringer; it’s just a game.
She always gets her tits in a wringer over minor issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: under the gun - under pressure or in a stressful situation
  • Synonym: in hot water - in trouble or a difficult situation

Etymology

From the noun 'wringer', a device for squeezing water out of clothes, metaphorically extended to mean a distressing or squeezing situation.
