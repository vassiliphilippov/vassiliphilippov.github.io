to make game of

1. VERB

/ meɪk ɡeɪm ʌv / (AmE), / meɪk ɡeɪm ɒv / (BrE)
to mock or ridicule someone, or to treat something serious in a frivolous way
mock or make fun of someone or something

Examples

It's not kind to make game of your classmates if they make a mistake.
Reporters should never make game of people dealing with tragedies.
She was hurt when they made game of her accent during the meeting.

Synset Examples

It's not right to make game of people's struggles.
He tends to make game of serious situations.
They love to make game of every little mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ridicule - to mock or subject to contemptuous laughter
  • Synonym: mock - to make fun of someone or something
  • Antonym: respect - to regard with honor

Etymology

From the use of 'game' meaning 'mockery' or 'jest' in Middle English.
