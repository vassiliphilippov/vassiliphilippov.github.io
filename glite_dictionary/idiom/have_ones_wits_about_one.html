to have (one's) wits about (one)

1. VERB

/ hæv wɪts əˈbaʊt wʌn / (AmE), / hæv wɪts əˈbaʊt wʌn / (BrE)
to remain alert, aware, and able to think quickly, especially in difficult or unexpected situations
remain alert and aware

Examples

When crossing a busy street, it's wise to have your wits about you.
She managed to escape the scam because she had her wits about her.
During the intense debate, he always had his wits about him and responded quickly.

Synset Examples

She always has her wits about her when traveling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alert - quick to notice and respond to dangers or problems
  • Antonym: off one's guard - not paying attention or not being cautious

Etymology

From 'wit' meaning mental sharpness or intelligence; phrase dates back to the 17th century.
