to catch the light

1. VERB

/ kætʃ ði laɪt / (AmE), / katʃ ðiː laɪt / (BrE)
to reflect light in a way that is visually striking or attractive, often creating a sparkling or shimmering effect
reflect light attractively

Examples

The diamond ring caught the light and sparkled brilliantly on her finger.
As he moved, the polished shoes caught the light with every step.
The chandelier crystals catch the light and fill the room with rainbows.

Synset Examples

Her dress had a shimmery finish that caught the light beautifully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sparkle - to shine with bright flashes of light
  • Synonym: gleam - to shine with a soft or reflected light
