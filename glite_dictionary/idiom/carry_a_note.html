to carry a note

1. VERB

/ ˈkæri ə noʊt / (AmE), / ˈkæri ə nəʊt / (BrE)
to be able to sing or produce music accurately
sing or produce music accurately

Examples

She can carry a note beautifully and often performs as the lead singer in the choir.
He tried to sing along but clearly couldn't carry a note.
Many people are shy to sing in public, fearing they might not carry a note well.

Synset Examples

Despite his love for music, he couldn't carry a note in a bucket.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: be tone-deaf - unable to distinguish musical notes accurately; lacking musical ability
