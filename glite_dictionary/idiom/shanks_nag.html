shanks' nag

1. NOUN

/ ʃæŋks næɡ / (AmE), / ʃæŋks næɡ / (BrE)
one's legs used for walking, especially when no other form of transport is available; walking on foot (humorous, chiefly British idiom)
humorous idiom for walking on foot
british informal

Examples

After the car broke down, we finished the last mile on shanks' nag.
He joked that he'd have to rely on shanks' nag to get home until payday.
Rather than waiting for the bus, they decided to use shanks' nag and enjoy a long walk.

Synset Examples

We took the shanks' nag instead of the bus this time.
My shanks' nag is tired after that long walk.
The kids rode their shanks' nag to the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shanks's pony - one's legs used for walking (main alternative form)

Etymology

From 'shank' (the lower part of the leg) and 'nag' (an old horse), suggesting using one's legs as a horse.
