het up

1. ADJ

/ hɛt ʌp / (AmE), / hɛt ʌp / (BrE)
Emotionally agitated, especially due to anger or excitement.
emotionally agitated or upset
informal british

Examples

She tends to get het up about small arguments at work.
Try not to become het up over things you can't control.
He was visibly het up after reading the angry review.

Synset Examples

was terribly het up over the killing of the eagle
got really het up over the new taxes
he was suddenly het up about racing cars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worked up - emotionally aroused or agitated
  • Antonym: calm - free from agitation or excitement

Etymology

From dialectal 'het', a variant of 'heated', with 'up' intensifying the state.

2. ADJ

/ hɛt ʌp / (AmE), / hɛt ʌp / (BrE)
Feeling agitated, upset, or excited, especially in a tense or emotional way.
agitated or upset
informal

Examples

She was het up about the argument and couldn't sit still.
Try not to get het up over things you can't control.
He sounded really het up when he called last night.

Synset Examples

He was het up after hearing the bad news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worked up - feeling upset or excited
  • Antonym: calm - not agitated or upset

Etymology

Dialectal variant of 'heated up', influenced by Scots and Northern English usage.
