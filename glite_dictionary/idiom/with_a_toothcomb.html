with a toothcomb

1. ADV

/ wɪð ə ˈtuθˌkoʊm / (AmE), / wɪð ə ˈtuːθkəʊm / (BrE)
In a very thorough or meticulous way, paying close attention to every detail.
very thoroughly; meticulously

Examples

The accountant reviewed the financial statements with a toothcomb to catch any errors.
The editor went over the manuscript with a toothcomb before publication.
They searched the old attic with a toothcomb for lost family heirlooms.

Synset Examples

We went through the report with a toothcomb to find discrepancies.
The detective searched the crime scene with a toothcomb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: painstakingly - showing great care and thoroughness

Etymology

From the image of using a fine-toothed comb for careful inspection or search.
