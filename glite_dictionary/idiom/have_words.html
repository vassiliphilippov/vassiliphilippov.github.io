to have words

1. VERB

/ hæv wɜrdz / (AmE), / hæv wɜːdz / (BrE)
to argue or discuss something angrily or seriously, often because of a disagreement
to have an argument or angry discussion

Examples

If you keep arriving late, we will have words about it.
They had words after the meeting over who should lead the project.
She stormed out, saying she would be having words with her team about the mistake.

Synset Examples

They will have words if this continues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to argue - to give reasons for or against something; to have a verbal fight
