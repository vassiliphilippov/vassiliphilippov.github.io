to make an example of

1. VERB

/ meɪk ən ɪɡˈzæmpəl ʌv / (AmE), / meɪk ən ɪɡˈzɑːmpəl ɒv / (BrE)
to punish someone in order to serve as a warning or deterrent to others
punish as a warning to others

Examples

The company hoped to make an example of the employee who broke the rules.
The coach threatened to make an example of anyone who skipped practice.
To discourage cheating, the teacher decided to make an example of the students involved.

Synset Examples

They decided to make an example of the violators.
The school aims to make an example of anyone caught cheating.
Management will make an example of those who are late.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to punish - to inflict a penalty for an offense
