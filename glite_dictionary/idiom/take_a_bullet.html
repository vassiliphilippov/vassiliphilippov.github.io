to take a bullet

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə ˈbʊlɪt / (AmE), / teɪk ə ˈbʊlɪt / (BrE)
to endure pain, hardship, or risk, especially for the sake of protecting or helping someone else
endure risk or hardship for another

Examples

She would take a bullet for her little brother without hesitation.
During tough negotiations, a good manager might take a bullet for the team.
He didn’t hesitate to take a bullet when his friend needed him most.

Synset Examples

In a crisis, he would take a bullet to protect his family.
He was willing to take a bullet for his friend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sacrifice oneself - to give up one's own interests or well-being for others
  • Synonym: throw oneself on the grenade - to make a sacrifice to save others, similar idiom
  • Antonym: look out for oneself - to act only in one's own interest

Etymology

From the literal act of being shot on someone’s behalf, now used figuratively.
