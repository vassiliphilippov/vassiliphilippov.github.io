to stir the embers

1. VERB

/ stɝ ði ˈɛmbɚz / (AmE), / stə ði ˈɛmbəz / (BrE)
to rekindle feelings, memories, or situations that have faded or diminished; to revive something that was nearly forgotten
revive something faded or forgotten

Examples

We tried to stir the embers of our childhood friendship during the reunion.
Sometimes a song can stir the embers of sadness you thought was gone.
She hoped to stir the embers of creativity by revisiting her old studio.

Synset Examples

He knows how to stir the embers of old friendships.
Let's stir the embers to keep the fire going.
They gathered to stir the embers after the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rekindle - to revive something that has been lost or diminished
  • Degree Higher: to ignite - to set something on fire or to cause an emotion to become active
