hitch one's wagon to

1.

/ hɪtʃ wʌnz ˈwæɡən tu / (AmE), / hɪtʃ wʌnz ˈwæɡən tʊ / (BrE)
to align oneself with a successful person, group, or trend in order to achieve success or benefit
align with success for advantage

Examples

After seeing the company's rapid growth, she decided to hitch her wagon to their success.
Politicians often hitch their wagon to popular causes to gain voter support.
It's sometimes risky to hitch your wagon to a trend that may fade quickly.

Synset Examples

He always hitches his wagon to the latest trends.
She decided to hitch her wagon to the successful startup.
It's wise to hitch your wagon to a reliable partner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ride (someone's) coattails - to achieve success by associating with someone successful
  • Antonym: to go it alone - to act independently, without relying on others

Etymology

From the literal act of attaching one's wagon to something for movement, popularized figuratively by 19th-century American writer Ralph Waldo Emerson.
