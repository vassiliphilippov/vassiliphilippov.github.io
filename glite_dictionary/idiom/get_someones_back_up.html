to get someone's back up

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈsʌmwʌnz bæk ʌp / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈsʌmwʌnz bæk ʌp / (BrE)
to make someone annoyed or irritated
to annoy or irritate someone

Examples

Try not to get his back up if you want his help with the project.
Her criticism really got my back up during the meeting.
If you keep making those comments, you'll only get their back up.

Synset Examples

Don’t get his back up with that comment.
Her attitude can easily get my back up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to annoy - to make someone feel slightly angry
  • Synonym: to irritate - to make someone feel annoyed or impatient
  • Antonym: please - to make someone happy or satisfied

Etymology

From the image of a cat arching its back when angry or threatened.

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt sʌmwʌnz bæk ʌp / (AmE), / ɡɛt sʌmwʌnz bæk ʌp / (BrE)
to make someone annoyed, angry, or defensive, often as a result of criticism or provocation.
make someone annoyed or defensive

Examples

Her constant criticism would always get my back up when I was younger.
Try not to get his back up with your remarks during the meeting.
It really gets my back up when people ignore my emails.

Synset Examples

Her constant criticism can get my back up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to annoy - to make someone slightly angry
  • Synonym: to provoke - to deliberately make someone angry
