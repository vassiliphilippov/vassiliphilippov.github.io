poor time of it

1. NOUN

/ pɔr taɪm əv ɪt / (AmE), / pɔː taɪm əv ɪt / (BrE)
to have a difficult or unpleasant experience; to go through hardship or trouble
to have a difficult experience

Examples

After losing her job and getting sick, she really had a poor time of it last year.
The hikers had a poor time of it when the weather turned unexpectedly harsh.
Many students have a poor time of it adjusting to college in their first semester.

Synset Examples

They really have a poor time of it during the winter.
She seems to have a poor time of it lately.
He always seems to have a poor time of it at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard time - a period marked by difficulty or adversity
  • Antonym: easy time - a period or experience with little or no difficulty
