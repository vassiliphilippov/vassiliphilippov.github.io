to get your priorities straight

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt jʊr praɪˈɔrɪtiz streɪt / (AmE), / ɡɛt jɔː praɪˈɒrɪtiz streɪt / (BrE)
to decide or recognize what is most important to focus on or address
decide what is most important

Examples

If you want to balance work and family, you need to get your priorities straight.
She finally realized she had to get her priorities straight after missing several deadlines.
Before taking on more tasks, try to get your priorities straight to avoid feeling overwhelmed.

Synset Examples

To succeed, you must get your priorities straight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get your act together - to become better organized or more effective
  • Antonym: to lose sight of - to forget what is most important
