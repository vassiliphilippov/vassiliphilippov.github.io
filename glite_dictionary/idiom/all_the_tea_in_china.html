all the tea in China

1. X

/ ɔl ðə ti ɪn ˈtʃaɪnə / (AmE), / ɔːl ðə tiː ɪn ˈtʃaɪnə / (BrE)
used to emphasize that no amount of money or other incentive would persuade someone to do something, because it is extremely valuable or important to them.
no incentive is enough to persuade someone

Examples

I wouldn't sell this painting for all the tea in China.
She wouldn't betray her friend for all the tea in China.
He refused to give up his dream for all the tea in China.

Synset Examples

I wouldn't sell my car for all the tea in China.
She won't move for all the tea in China.
He wouldn't trade his friendship for all the tea in China.
