to look like a frump

1. VERB

/ lʊk laɪk ə frʌmp / (AmE), / lʊk laɪk ə frʌmp / (BrE)
to appear unfashionable, dowdy, or sloppy, especially in dress or style
appear unfashionable or dowdy
informal

Examples

She didn't want to look like a frump at the party, so she chose a stylish dress.
Even with expensive clothes, he managed to look like a frump because of his careless grooming.
After a long flight, I always look like a frump when I arrive at my destination.

Synset Examples

He looked like a frump in that oversized sweater.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frumpy - unfashionable and dowdy in appearance

Etymology

From 'frump', meaning a dowdy or drab person, likely of 17th-century origin, possibly imitative.
