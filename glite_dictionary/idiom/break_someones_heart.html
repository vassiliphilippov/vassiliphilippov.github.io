to break someone's heart

1. VERB

/ breɪk sʌmwʌnz hɑrt / (AmE), / breɪk sʌmwʌnz hɑːt / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel great emotional pain or sadness, especially after a romantic disappointment
cause deep emotional pain or sadness

Examples

It would break my heart to see her leave the country.
He knew his decision would break her heart, but he felt he had no choice.
She didn't want to break his heart, so she tried to let him down gently.

Synset Examples

The young man broke the girl's heart when he told her was going to marry her best friend

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to devastate - to cause someone severe shock or distress
  • Antonym: to cheer up - to make someone feel happier

2. VERB

/ breɪk sʌmwʌnz hɑrt / (AmE), / breɪk sʌmwʌnz hɑːt / (BrE)
to cause someone great emotional pain or distress, especially over love or disappointment
cause deep emotional pain

Examples

She knew the news would break his heart, but she had to tell him the truth.
It always breaks her heart to see abandoned animals on the street.
He never wanted to break anyone's heart, so he ended things gently.

Synset Examples

He feared he would break her heart with his decision.
She didn’t want to break his heart with bad news.
To break someone's heart is never an easy choice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to break up - to end a romantic relationship
  • Antonym: console - to comfort someone who is sad or disappointed
