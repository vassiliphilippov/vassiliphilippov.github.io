to have tickets on oneself

1. VERB

/ hæv ˈtɪkɪts ɑn ˌwʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / hæv ˈtɪkɪts ɒn ˌwʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to consider oneself to be more important, talented, or attractive than is actually the case; to be conceited or arrogant
to be conceited or self-important
informal

Examples

After getting a promotion, he started acting like he had tickets on himself.
Don’t have tickets on yourself just because you won one match.
Some people say she has tickets on herself, but I think she's just confident.

Synset Examples

I don't want to sound like I have tickets on myself.
She really has tickets on herself after winning that award.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conceited - having an excessively high opinion of oneself
  • Antonym: modest - having a humble view of one's talents or abilities

Etymology

Originally Australian slang, probably from the idea of 'carrying tickets' as a metaphor for advertising oneself.
