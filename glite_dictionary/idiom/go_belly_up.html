to go belly up

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˌbɛli ˈʌp / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˌbɛli ˈʌp / (BrE)
(of a business or project) to fail completely or go bankrupt.
fail or go bankrupt (idiom)
informal

Examples

The company went belly up after several bad investments.
Many startups go belly up within the first five years.
Her small bookstore had gone belly up by the end of the season.

Synset Examples

The restaurant went belly up after just one year.
Her investment went belly up during the recession.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fail - to be unsuccessful in achieving a goal
  • Antonym: to succeed - to achieve a desired aim or result

Etymology

Originally an allusion to dead fish floating upside down, first attested in American English in the 20th century.
