to get up to speed

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ʌp tu spiːd / (AmE), / ɡɛt ʌp tə spiːd / (BrE)
to become fully informed or familiar with the latest information or developments in a particular area.
become fully informed or up-to-date

Examples

It didn't take her long to get up to speed after joining the project.
The manager will help new hires get up to speed on company policy.
Before we start, let's get up to speed with the latest updates.

Synset Examples

He quickly got up to speed with the new software.
I need to get you up to speed on the latest changes.
The training session will bring everyone up to speed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to catch up - to reach the same standard or level as others
  • Antonym: to fall behind - to fail to keep pace with others or with progress
