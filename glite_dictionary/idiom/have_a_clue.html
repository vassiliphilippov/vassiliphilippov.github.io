to have a clue

1. VERB

/ hæv ə kluː / (AmE), / hæv ə kluː / (BrE)
to have any understanding or knowledge about something, especially when such understanding is uncertain or minimal
have any understanding or knowledge

Examples

Despite searching for hours, I didn't have a clue where my keys could be.
He acts confident, but he really doesn't have a clue about the new technology.
None of the students had a clue how to solve the difficult math problem.

Synset Examples

She doesn't have a clue where he went.
They don't have a clue what to do next.
I don't have a clue about the project.
He didn't have a clue what to do next.
Do you have a clue about the surprise party?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to know - to have information or awareness
  • Antonym: be clueless - to have no knowledge or understanding
