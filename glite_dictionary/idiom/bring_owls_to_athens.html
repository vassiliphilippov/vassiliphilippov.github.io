to bring owls to Athens

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ aʊlz tu ˈæθənz / (AmE), / brɪŋ aʊlz tə ˈæθənz / (BrE)
to do something superfluous or offer unnecessary advice, especially when it is already well provided for
to do something unnecessary or redundant

Examples

Giving grammar tips to a linguistics professor is like bringing owls to Athens.
She realized she was bringing owls to Athens by explaining basic computer skills to the IT team.
You're bringing owls to Athens if you bring more dessert; we already have plenty.

Synset Examples

Don’t bring owls to Athens with your redundant advice.
Stop trying to sell me on that; it’s bringing owls to Athens.
That idea is like bringing owls to Athens; unnecessary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to carry coals to Newcastle - to do something unnecessary by providing what is already abundant
  • Synonym: to gild the lily - to unnecessarily embellish something already beautiful or sufficient

Etymology

From the ancient Greek saying; Athens was known for its abundance of owls (symbolizing wisdom) and thus bringing more was pointless.
