to feel like a plonker

1. VERB

/ fil laɪk ə ˈplɑŋkər / (AmE), / fiːl laɪk ə ˈplɒŋkə / (BrE)
to feel foolish or silly, often after making a mistake or behaving awkwardly
to feel foolish or embarrassed
informal british

Examples

After tripping over my own feet in front of everyone, I really felt like a plonker.
She felt like a plonker when she realized she’d been talking to the wrong person all along.
Missing the meeting because I forgot the time made me feel like a plonker.

Synset Examples

I felt like a plonker for forgetting my keys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make a fool of oneself - to behave in a way that makes you look silly or foolish
  • Synonym: feel like an idiot - to feel very stupid or foolish about something you have done

Etymology

From British slang 'plonker,' meaning a foolish person; 'feel like a' + plonker idiomatically combines the sense of personal embarrassment.
