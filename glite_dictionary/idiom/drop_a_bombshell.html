to drop a bombshell

1. VERB

/ drɑp ə ˈbɑmˌʃɛl / (AmE), / drɒp ə ˈbɒmʃɛl / (BrE)
to suddenly reveal surprising or shocking information
reveal shocking information

Examples

At the end of the meeting, she dropped a bombshell that left everyone speechless.
He dropped a bombshell by announcing his resignation effective immediately.
The CEO dropped a bombshell in the press conference, revealing the company would be sold.

Synset Examples

He dropped a bombshell during the meeting yesterday.
The report dropped a bombshell on the entire team.
She dropped a bombshell about her surprise engagement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break the news - to inform someone of important or startling news

Etymology

The phrase refers metaphorically to the shock or impact of an exploding bomb; first recorded early 20th century.
