go jump off a cliff

1. X

/ ɡoʊ dʒʌmp ɑf ə klɪf / (AmE), / ɡəʊ dʒʌmp ɒf ə klɪf / (BrE)
(informal, often hyperbolic) Used to tell someone you strongly reject, disapprove of, or are annoyed by what they have said or done.
expression of strong rejection or disapproval
informal

Examples

If you don't like my decision, you can just go jump off a cliff.
He told his critics to go jump off a cliff when they doubted his success.
She felt so irritated, she nearly shouted, 'Why don't you go jump off a cliff!'

Synset Examples

When they suggested that plan, I felt like saying go jump off a cliff.
If you think that's a good idea, you can go jump off a cliff.
Feeling overwhelmed, I joked I should just go jump off a cliff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go jump in a lake - similar hyperbolic phrase expressing annoyance or telling someone to go away
