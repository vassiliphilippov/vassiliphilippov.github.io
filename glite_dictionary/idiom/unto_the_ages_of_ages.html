unto the ages of ages

1. X

/ ˈʌn.tu ði ˈeɪ.dʒɪz əv ˈeɪ.dʒɪz / (AmE), / ˈʌn.tuː ðɪ ˈeɪ.dʒɪz əv ˈeɪ.dʒɪz / (BrE)
For an indefinitely long or eternal period, especially to express everlasting commitment or duration.
for an indefinitely long or eternal period

Examples

The church bells will ring unto the ages of ages as a symbol of hope.
She vowed her loyalty would extend unto the ages of ages and never fade.
Their legacy is said to endure unto the ages of ages in the village.

Synset Examples

They promised to keep this tradition unto the ages of ages.
Her love for him will last unto the ages of ages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forever - for all time; eternally
  • Synonym: eternal - lasting forever or without end
  • Antonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period

Etymology

From biblical translations where similar phrases render Greek 'εἰς τοὺς αἰῶνας τῶν αἰώνων' (eis tous aiōnas tōn aiōnōn), meaning 'to the ages of ages', used to denote eternity.
