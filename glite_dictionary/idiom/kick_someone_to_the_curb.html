to kick someone to the curb

1. VERB

/ kɪk ˌsʌmwʌn tə ðə kɝb / (AmE), / kɪk ˌsʌmwʌn tə ðə kɜːb / (BrE)
to reject or abruptly dismiss someone from one's life or a relationship
abruptly reject or dismiss someone
informal

Examples

After years of putting up with his disrespect, she finally decided to kick him to the curb.
If you feel unappreciated in a friendship, maybe it's time to kick them to the curb.
He cheated twice, so I had no choice but to kick him to the curb.

Synset Examples

She decided to kick him to the curb after his lies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dump - to suddenly end a romantic relationship with someone
  • Antonym: take someone back - to allow someone to return after rejecting them
