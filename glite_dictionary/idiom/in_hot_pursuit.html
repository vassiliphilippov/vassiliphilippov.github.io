in hot pursuit

1. ADV

/ ɪn hɑt pərˈsuːt / (AmE), / ɪn hɒt pəˈsjuːt / (BrE)
chasing someone or something eagerly or with great urgency, often immediately after the other has fled or escaped
chasing eagerly or urgently

Examples

The police sped down the highway in hot pursuit of the stolen car.
She dashed out the door in hot pursuit of her escaping dog.
The detective was always in hot pursuit of the truth.

Synset Examples

She ran in hot pursuit of her bus before it left.
The dog was in hot pursuit of the squirrel.
The police were in hot pursuit of the fleeing suspect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pursue - to follow someone or something in order to catch them
  • Synonym: to give chase - to begin chasing someone or something
