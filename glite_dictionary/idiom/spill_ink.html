to spill ink

1. VERB

/ spɪl ɪŋk / (AmE), / spɪl ɪŋk / (BrE)
to write extensively or create art, especially using ink, often evoking the image of ink being spent or spilled
write extensively or create art with ink

Examples

The journalist would often spill ink late into the night to meet her deadlines.
Many poets have spilled ink trying to capture the beauty of nature.
He loves to spill ink on sketches before moving to digital art.

Synset Examples

The author would spill ink for hours on her novel.
He began to spill ink on the page with his quill.
She watched as the artist would spill ink across the canvas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to write - to form letters, words, or symbols on a surface, especially with a pen or pencil
