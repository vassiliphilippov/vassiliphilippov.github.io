to take the fall

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə fɔl / (AmE), / teɪk ðə fɔːl / (BrE)
to accept blame or punishment for something, especially in place of someone else
accept blame or punishment for someone

Examples

He refused to take the fall for a crime he didn't commit.
Maria decided she would take the fall so the rest of the team wouldn't get in trouble.
It's unfair to expect one person to take the fall for everyone else's mistakes.

Synset Examples

Sometimes, you have to take the fall to protect others.
She decided to take the fall instead of pointing fingers.
He was willing to take the fall for his friend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take the rap - accept punishment or blame, especially instead of someone else
