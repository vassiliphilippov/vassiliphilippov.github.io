down on one's uppers

1. ADJ

/ daʊn ɑn wʌnz ˈʌpɚz / (AmE), / daʊn ɒn wʌnz ˈʌpəz / (BrE)
in a difficult financial situation; experiencing poverty or hardship
in financial difficulty or hardship

Examples

After losing his job, Tom was down on his uppers and struggling to make ends meet.
She found herself down on her uppers and had to borrow money from friends.
The family was down on their uppers until they received help from the community.

Synset Examples

She felt down on her uppers after paying the bills.
After the expenses, she's down on her uppers this month.
He's been living down on his uppers since the layoffs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard up - in need of money; short of cash
  • Synonym: broke - having no money; penniless

Etymology

Originates from the notion of having worn out the soles of one's shoes (uppers) due to poverty.
