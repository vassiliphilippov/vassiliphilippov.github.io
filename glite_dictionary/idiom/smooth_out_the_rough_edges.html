to smooth out the rough edges

1. VERB

/ smuð aʊt ðə rʌf ˈɛdʒɪz / (AmE), / smuːð aʊt ðə rʌf ˈɛdʒɪz / (BrE)
to make something less harsh, difficult, or problematic by addressing minor faults or imperfections
make less harsh or problematic

Examples

We held several meetings to smooth out the rough edges before launching the project.
The new editor helped smooth out the rough edges in the manuscript, making it much clearer.
Regular practice can smooth out the rough edges in your presentation skills.

Synset Examples

They used a buffer to smooth out the rough edges.
