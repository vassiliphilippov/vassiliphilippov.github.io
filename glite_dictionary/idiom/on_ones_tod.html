on one's tod

1. ADV

/ ɑn wʌnz tɑd / (AmE), / ɒn wʌnz tɒd / (BrE)
(British, informal) Alone; by oneself, without company or assistance.
alone; by oneself (British, informal)
british informal

Examples

After his friends left, he finished the meal on his tod.
She went to the cinema on her tod because she didn't mind being alone.
Sometimes I enjoy walking through the park on my tod.

Synset Examples

Feeling adventurous, he decided to travel on his tod.
He likes to go to the pub on his tod sometimes.
She often spends evenings on her tod, enjoying quiet time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alone - without anyone else
  • Synonym: by oneself - without the company or help of others
  • Antonym: with company - together with other people

Etymology

Rhyming slang from "Tod Sloan," an American jockey; "Tod" rhymes with "own" in "on one's own."
