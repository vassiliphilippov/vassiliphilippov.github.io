to rant and rave

1. VERB

/ rænt ənd reɪv / (AmE), / rænt ənd reɪv / (BrE)
to shout, complain, or speak loudly and angrily, often for a long time, especially in a way that is unreasonable or excessive
complain or shout loudly and angrily

Examples

My boss tends to rant and rave whenever a project is delayed.
After the referee's decision, the coach began to rant and rave on the sidelines.
She would rant and rave about the traffic every morning while driving to work.

Synset Examples

He would rant and rave when he lost his favorite team.
She tends to rant and rave about politics during dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rant - to speak or shout in an angry, uncontrolled way
