to put the shoe on the other foot

1. VERB

/ pʊt ðə ʃu ɑn ði ˈʌðɚ fʊt / (AmE), / pʊt ðə ʃuː ɒn ði ˈʌðə fʊt / (BrE)
to switch roles or perspectives, especially to imagine oneself in another person's situation
to reverse roles or viewpoints

Examples

It's helpful to put the shoe on the other foot before making a judgment about someone's actions.
When she complained about being criticized, I suggested she put the shoe on the other foot for a moment.
If you put the shoe on the other foot, you might better understand how difficult his situation is.

Synset Examples

Now that you’re in charge, let’s put the shoe on the other foot.
It’s time to put the shoe on the other foot and see how he feels.
If I were you, I’d consider putting the shoe on the other foot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: put oneself in someone else's shoes - to imagine oneself in another person's position
