vale of tears

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪl əv ˈtɪrz / (AmE), / ˈveɪl əv ˈtɪəz / (BrE)
a life or world regarded as full of suffering or sorrow; a state marked by grief
a condition or world of sorrow or suffering

Examples

After losing his job, he felt trapped in a vale of tears for months.
Religious texts often refer to life as a vale of tears that must be endured.
Despite living in a vale of tears, she managed to find hope and joy.

Synset Examples

Life can feel like a vale of tears sometimes.
In the vale of tears, friends brought comfort.
She described her struggles as a vale of tears.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valley of sorrow - another idiomatic expression describing a state of deep sadness

Etymology

From Latin 'vallis lacrimarum', used in Christian liturgy and biblical texts to describe earthly existence as sorrowful.
