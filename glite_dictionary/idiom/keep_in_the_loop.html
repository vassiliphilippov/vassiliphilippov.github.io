to keep in the loop

1. VERB

/ kip ɪn ðə lup / (AmE), / kiːp ɪn ðə luːp / (BrE)
to make sure that someone is regularly given all the latest information about a situation
keep someone informed

Examples

Before making any decisions, please keep me in the loop about changes to the schedule.
She promised to keep him in the loop as the negotiations progressed.
It's important to keep the team in the loop so everyone knows what's going on.

Synset Examples

Please keep me in the loop regarding the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keep someone posted - to keep someone informed about recent developments
