finders keepers; losers weepers

1. X

/ ˈfaɪndərz ˈkipərz ˈluːzərz ˈwiːpərz / (AmE), / ˈfaɪndəz ˈkiːpəz ˈluːzəz ˈwiːpəz / (BrE)
used to say that whoever finds something is entitled to keep it, while the person who lost it cannot complain
whoever finds something keeps it; loser cannot complain

Examples

When her brother found the dollar bill on the floor, he shouted, "finders keepers; losers weepers!"
In the playground, the children argued over a toy until one invoked the old rule: finders keepers; losers weepers.
After spotting an abandoned umbrella at the café, Sarah joked, "Well, finders keepers; losers weepers."

Synset Examples

At the park, it was clear: finders, keepers; losers, weepers.
When she found the lost necklace, it was finders, keepers; losers, weepers.
