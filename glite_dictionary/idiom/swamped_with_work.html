swamped with work

1. ADJ

/ swɒmpt wɪð wɜrk / (AmE), / swɒmpt wɪð wɜːk / (BrE)
overwhelmed or inundated by a large amount of work or tasks
overwhelmed by too much work

Examples

After taking on three new clients, she was swamped with work and barely had time to rest.
He apologized for the late reply, explaining that he was swamped with work this week.
Many employees feel swamped with work during the end-of-year rush.

Synset Examples

He felt swamped with work after the holiday break.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overwhelmed - feeling completely overcome by workload or emotion
  • Degree Lower: busy - having a lot to do or occupied with tasks

Etymology

From the verb 'swamp', meaning to overwhelm or flood, originally referring to waterlogged land.
