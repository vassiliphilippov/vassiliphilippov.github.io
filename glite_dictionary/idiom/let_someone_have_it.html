to let someone have it

1. VERB

/ lɛt ˈsʌmwʌn ˈhæv ɪt / (AmE), / lɛt ˈsʌmwən ˈhæv ɪt / (BrE)
to scold, criticize, or punish someone severely
scold or punish severely
informal

Examples

When she found out about the broken window, she let him have it in front of everyone.
If you come home late again, Dad will let you have it.
The coach really let the team have it after their poor performance.

Synset Examples

When he saw the mess, he let her have it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tell someone off - to reprimand someone severely
