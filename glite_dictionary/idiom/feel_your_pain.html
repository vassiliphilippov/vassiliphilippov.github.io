to feel your pain

1. VERB

/ fil jʊər peɪn / (AmE), / fiːl jɔː peɪn / (BrE)
to empathize with someone by recognizing and understanding their suffering or hardship
express empathy for someone's suffering

Examples

Whenever you're going through a tough time, just know that I feel your pain.
As a parent, I truly feel your pain about sending your child away to college.
Many people feel your pain when they hear about losing a beloved pet.

Synset Examples

I can feel your pain, I went through something similar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sympathize - to share or understand someone's feelings
  • Synonym: to empathize - to understand and share another person's feelings
