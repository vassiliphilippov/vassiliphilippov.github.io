to see the back of

1. VERB

/ si ðə bæk əv / (AmE), / siː ðə bæk ɒv / (BrE)
to be glad when someone leaves or when something ends or disappears, usually because it was unwelcome or unpleasant
be glad to get rid of (someone or something)

Examples

After months of renovations, we were happy to see the back of the construction crew.
She couldn't wait to see the back of her cold after being sick for a week.
Many employees were relieved to see the back of the strict manager.

Synset Examples

Once the storm passed, we finally saw the back of the rain.
He was relieved to see the back of his old job.
I can't wait to see the back of this stressful project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get rid of - to remove or eliminate someone or something unwanted
  • Antonym: welcome - to be pleased about the arrival or presence of something or someone
