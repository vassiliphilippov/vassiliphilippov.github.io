what was someone thinking

1. X

/ wʌt wəz ˈsʌmwʌn ˈθɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / wɒt wəz ˈsʌmwən ˈθɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
used as a rhetorical question to express disbelief, surprise, or criticism about someone's decision or action
used to express disbelief or criticism about a decision

Examples

She wore sandals in the snow—what was she thinking?
He quit his job without another offer lined up; what was he thinking?
You spent all your money on lottery tickets? What were you thinking?

Synset Examples

He just bought that car? What was someone thinking?
