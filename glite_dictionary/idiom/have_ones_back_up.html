to have one's back up

1. VERB

/ hæv wʌnz bæk ʌp / (AmE), / hæv wʌnz bæk ʌp / (BrE)
to become defensive or angry in response to something, often showing resistance or hostility
become defensive or angry

Examples

Whenever she feels criticized, she tends to have her back up immediately.
He had his back up during the entire meeting, barely listening to suggestions.
Don't have your back up—we're just trying to help.

Synset Examples

She had her back up when he made that comment.
I could tell she had her back up about the criticism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be on the defensive - to react as if under attack, ready to defend oneself
  • Synonym: take offense - to become upset or annoyed, usually due to something said
  • Synonym: get angry - to become angry

Etymology

Probably from the image of an animal, such as a cat, arching its back when threatened.

2. VERB

/ hæv wʌnz bæk ʌp / (AmE), / hæv wʌnz bæk ʌp / (BrE)
to become defensive or hostile, often as a reaction to something perceived as threatening or annoying
become defensive or hostile

Examples

Whenever someone criticizes his work, he tends to have his back up immediately.
She had her back up during the entire meeting, making cooperation tough for everyone.
If you approach him gently, he won't have his back up so quickly.

Synset Examples

He always has his back up when discussing politics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be on the defensive - to be prepared to defend oneself, usually because one feels threatened
  • Antonym: lower one's guard - to stop being defensive or cautious

Etymology

Alludes to the image of a cat arching its back when threatened.
