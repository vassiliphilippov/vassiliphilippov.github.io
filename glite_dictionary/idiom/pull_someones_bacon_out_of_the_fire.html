to pull someone's bacon out of the fire

1. VERB

/ pʊl ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈbeɪkən aʊt əv ðə ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / pʊl ˈsʌmwənz ˈbeɪkən aʊt əv ðə ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
to rescue someone from a difficult or dangerous situation, especially at the last moment
rescue from trouble or danger

Examples

I almost missed the deadline, but my colleague pulled my bacon out of the fire by finishing the report for me.
Her quick thinking during the crisis really pulled my bacon out of the fire.
He forgot his speech at home, but his assistant pulled his bacon out of the fire by bringing a copy.

Synset Examples

His quick thinking pulled my bacon out of the fire.
I was struggling, but her help pulled my bacon out of the fire.
That last-minute solution really pulled my bacon out of the fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: save someone's skin - to protect someone from harm or trouble
  • Antonym: leave someone in the lurch - to abandon someone in a difficult situation

Etymology

Refers to the literal act of saving bacon from a fire, with figurative use attested in the 19th century.
