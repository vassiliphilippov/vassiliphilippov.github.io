to have it made

1. VERB

/ ˌhæv ɪt ˈmeɪd / (AmE), / ˌhæv ɪt ˈmeɪd / (BrE)
to be in a very advantageous or favorable situation, often with little to worry about
be in a very favorable situation
informal

Examples

After graduating from a top university, she knew she had it made.
If you inherit a fortune, people might say you have it made.
Now that he runs his own successful business, he truly has it made.

Synset Examples

After landing that job, she felt she really had it made.
If you can work from home, you definitely have it made.
With his successful career, he knows he has it made.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be set - to be in a good position or secure situation
  • Synonym: be in luck - to experience good fortune
  • Antonym: be in trouble - to be experiencing difficulties or problems
