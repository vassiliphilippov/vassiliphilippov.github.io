daylight robbery

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪˌlaɪt ˈrɑːbəri / (AmE), / ˈdeɪlaɪt ˈrɒbəri / (BrE)
an act of blatant theft or charging someone an outrageously high price; something considered unfairly expensive
blatant theft or excessive charge

Examples

The cost of parking in the city is just daylight robbery.
Charging ten dollars for a cup of coffee is daylight robbery.
He called the new tax on energy bills a form of daylight robbery.

Synset Examples

The prices they charge feel like daylight robbery.
He described the fee as nothing less than daylight robbery.
That ticket price is a daylight robbery for a concert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rip-off - an act of cheating someone by charging too much or by selling a worthless product
  • Antonym: bargain - something bought or offered for sale much more cheaply than is usual or expected
