in my wheelhouse

1. X

/ ɪn maɪ ˈwiːlˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ɪn maɪ ˈwiːlˌhaʊs / (BrE)
Within one's area of expertise, skill, or comfort; something one is particularly familiar with or good at.
within one's area of expertise or comfort
informal american

Examples

Solving complex puzzles is definitely in my wheelhouse, so let me give it a try.
Teaching young children is not in my wheelhouse, but I admire those who can do it well.
The topic of renewable energy is in my wheelhouse, as I've studied it for years.

Synset Examples

Public speaking is not in my wheelhouse; I prefer writing.
That project is right in my wheelhouse, so I’m excited to start.

Etymology

Originally American, from 'wheelhouse' (the pilothouse on a ship), in reference to the area a pilot controls confidently.
