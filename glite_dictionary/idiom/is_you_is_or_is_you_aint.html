is you is or is you ain't

1. X

/ ɪz ju ɪz ɔɹ ɪz ju eɪnt / (AmE), / ɪz ju ɪz ɔː ɪz ju eɪnt / (BrE)
(informal) A colloquial rhetorical phrase used to ask for confirmation or to inquire whether something is true or not.
colloquial confirmation-seeking phrase
informal slang american

Examples

She looked at him and asked, 'is you is or is you ain't going to help me today?'
In the movie, the character teased, 'is you is or is you ain't my friend?'
He laughed and repeated, 'is you is or is you ain't coming to the game?'

Synset Examples

At the party, he asked, 'Is you is or is you ain't coming?'

Etymology

American English colloquialism, popularized by Louis Jordan's 1944 song 'Is You Is or Is You Ain't My Baby?'
