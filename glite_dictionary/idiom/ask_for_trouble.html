to ask for trouble

1. VERB

/ æsk fəɹ ˈtɹʌbəl / (AmE), / ɑːsk fə ˈtrʌb(ə)l / (BrE)
to behave in a way that is likely to cause difficulty or problems; to persist in actions or attitudes despite the likelihood of negative consequences.
behave in a way likely to cause problems

Examples

If you ignore the safety instructions, you are asking for trouble.
Driving on icy roads without snow tires is really asking for trouble.
She knew that challenging the rules was asking for trouble, but she did it anyway.

Synset Examples

He is asking for trouble with his behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courting disaster - acting in a way that makes disaster likely
  • Synonym: to play with fire - to act in a way that risks serious consequences
