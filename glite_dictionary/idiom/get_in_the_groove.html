to get in the groove

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɪn ðə ɡruv / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɪn ðə ɡruːv / (BrE)
to become comfortable and efficient in a task, especially after a period of adjustment
become comfortable and effective at something
informal

Examples

It took a few weeks, but I finally got in the groove at my new job.
Musicians often perform better once they get in the groove together.
After warming up, the team quickly got in the groove and played flawlessly.

Synset Examples

Once she got in the groove, the writing flowed easily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: find one's stride - to reach an effective or productive state
