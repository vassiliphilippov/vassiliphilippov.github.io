to get your wires crossed

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt jɔr waɪɚz krɔst / (AmE), / ɡɛt jɔː waɪəz krɒst / (BrE)
to misunderstand something or become confused, especially due to miscommunication
misunderstand; become confused

Examples

I think we got our wires crossed about the time of the dinner reservation.
They got their wires crossed while planning the event, which led to the confusion.
If you get your wires crossed, just ask for clarification.

Synset Examples

When plans change, it’s easy to get your wires crossed.
I think we got our wires crossed about the meeting time.
They must have gotten their wires crossed on the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to misunderstand - to fail to understand correctly
  • Synonym: be at cross purposes - to work toward different goals without realizing it

Etymology

From the early days of telephony, where crossed wires led to mixed signals.
