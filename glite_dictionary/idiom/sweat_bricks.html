to sweat bricks

1. VERB

/ swɛt brɪks / (AmE), / swɛt brɪks / (BrE)
to feel extremely anxious or nervous, especially in anticipation of something
feel extremely anxious or nervous
informal

Examples

I was sweating bricks before my driving test results came in.
She always sweats bricks before public performances.
We sweated bricks while waiting to hear if we'd made the team.

Synset Examples

He sweated bricks waiting for the job interview results.
She was sweating bricks at the thought of public speaking.
I was sweating bricks during the final exam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sweat bullets - to feel extremely nervous or anxious
