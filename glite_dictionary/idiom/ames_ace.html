ames ace

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪmz eɪs / (AmE), / ˈeɪmz eɪs / (BrE)
very close to succeeding or achieving something, but just falling short
very nearly succeeding
archaic

Examples

The soccer team came within ames ace of winning the championship.
She was an ames ace away from getting the job she wanted.
The explorers were within ames ace of reaching the summit before the storm forced them back.

Synset Examples

The team was within ames ace of achieving their goals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: narrowly miss - to just fail to reach or achieve something
  • Synonym: to fall short - to not quite reach a desired result

Etymology

From the name of a dice game, where 'ames ace' referred to a throw of the lowest possible score (two aces).
