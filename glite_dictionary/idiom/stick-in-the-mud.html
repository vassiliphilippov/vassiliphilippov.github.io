stick-in-the-mud

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪk ɪn ðə mʌd / (AmE), / ˈstɪk ɪn ðə mʌd / (BrE)
a person who is old-fashioned and resistant to change or new ideas
old-fashioned, unadventurous person
informal

Examples

Don't be such a stick-in-the-mud—come join the dance with us!
They called him a stick-in-the-mud because he refused to try the new technology.
Her stick-in-the-mud attitude made it difficult to introduce any changes at work.

Synset Examples

moss-grown ideas about family life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuddy-duddy - a person with old-fashioned ideas and attitudes
  • Synonym: old-fashioned - outdated or not modern in style or thinking
  • Antonym: progressive - favoring or implementing new ideas and methods

Etymology

From the literal image of a stick stuck in the mud, indicating immobility or reluctance to move forward.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɪk ɪn ðə mʌd / (AmE), / ˈstɪk ɪn ðə mʌd / (BrE)
a person who is slow to act, resistant to change, or set in their ways
person resistant to change or slow to act

Examples

Don't be such a stick-in-the-mud—come join us at the party!
My brother can be a real stick-in-the-mud when it comes to trying new foods.
Everyone called him a stick-in-the-mud because he never wanted to travel.

Synset Examples

in England they call a slowpoke a ‘slowcoach’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slowcoach - a person who moves or acts slowly
  • Synonym: slowpoke - someone who moves slowly or lags behind others
