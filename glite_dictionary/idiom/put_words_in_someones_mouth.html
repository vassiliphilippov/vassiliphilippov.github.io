to put words in someone's mouth

1. VERB

/ pʊt wɝdz ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz maʊθ / (AmE), / pʊt wɜːdz ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz maʊθ / (BrE)
to falsely claim that someone has said something they did not actually say
falsely attribute statements to someone

Examples

Please don't put words in my mouth; I never accused anyone of lying.
She was frustrated when the reporter tried to put words in her mouth during the interview.
I wish people would stop putting words in my mouth and listen to what I actually say.

Synset Examples

Don't put words in my mouth; I never said that.
Stop putting words in my mouth; I can speak for myself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to misrepresent - to give a false or misleading account of something
