to clean someone's clock

1. VERB

/ klin sʌmwʌnz klɑk / (AmE), / kliːn sʌmwʌnz klɒk / (BrE)
to defeat someone decisively, especially in a competition or confrontation
defeat someone decisively
informal

Examples

If you underestimate our team, we’ll clean your clock in the final match.
The defending champion had his clock cleaned by the newcomer in just two rounds.
Nobody expected her to clean his clock so thoroughly in the debate.
