sick to the back teeth

1. ADJ

/ sɪk tə ðə bæk tiθ / (AmE), / sɪk tə ðə bæk tiːθ / (BrE)
extremely annoyed, fed up, or tired of something; having no more patience for someone or something
extremely annoyed or fed up

Examples

I’m sick to the back teeth of their constant arguing and lack of cooperation.
She was sick to the back teeth with sorting out everyone else's problems at work.
After months of delays, the team was sick to the back teeth of waiting for a decision.

Synset Examples

She was sick to the back teeth of the drama.
He’s sick to the back teeth of cold weather.
I'm sick to the back teeth of his excuses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fed up - no longer able to tolerate something; annoyed or bored
  • Synonym: sick and tired - completely bored or annoyed with something
