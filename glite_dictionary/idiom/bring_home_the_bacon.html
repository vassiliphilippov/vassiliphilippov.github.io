to bring home the bacon

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ hoʊm ðə ˈbeɪkən / (AmE), / brɪŋ həʊm ðə ˈbeɪkən / (BrE)
to earn money to support a household or to achieve success in a task
to earn a living or succeed

Examples

Ever since he started his new job, he's been able to bring home the bacon for his family.
She worked two jobs to bring home the bacon after her husband was laid off.
After months of hard work, the team finally brought home the bacon with a major contract.

Synset Examples

The enterprise succeeded
We succeeded in getting tickets to the show
she struggled to overcome her handicap and won

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to succeed - to achieve a desired aim or result
  • Antonym: to fail - to be unsuccessful in achieving something

Etymology

Possibly from British fairs where a side of bacon was awarded as a prize, or from the phrase referring to earning a living.

2. VERB

/ brɪŋ hoʊm ðə ˈbeɪkən / (AmE), / brɪŋ həʊm ðə ˈbeɪkən / (BrE)
to earn money to support your household; to provide for a family's needs through work or effort
earn a living; support a household

Examples

After losing his job, he struggled to bring home the bacon for his family.
She works overtime every week to bring home the bacon.
In most households, both parents must bring home the bacon to make ends meet.

Synset Examples

He provides for his large family by working three jobs
Women nowadays not only take care of the household but also bring home the bacon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to provide for - to support someone financially or supply their needs

Etymology

Possibly from the tradition of awarding a side of bacon as a prize in English village contests, later used in American slang for earning money.

3. VERB

/ brɪŋ hoʊm ðə ˈbeɪkən / (AmE), / brɪŋ həʊm ðə ˈbeɪkən / (BrE)
to earn money to support a household or family; to provide for one's dependents
earn a living; provide for family

Examples

He worked two jobs to bring home the bacon after his wife fell ill.
In many families, both parents now bring home the bacon to cover expenses.
After a long search, she finally found a job to bring home the bacon.

Synset Examples

They are determined to bring home the bacon this year.
She juggles two jobs to bring home the bacon.
He works hard to bring home the bacon for his family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to provide - to supply the means of subsistence; synonym for earning a living

Etymology

Early 20th century American English; originally referred to success in sports and later generalized to earnings.
