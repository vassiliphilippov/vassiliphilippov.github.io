to put behind bars

1. VERB

/ pʊt bəˈhaɪnd bɑrz / (AmE), / pʊt bɪˈhaɪnd bɑːz / (BrE)
to send someone to prison; to confine someone in jail
send someone to prison

Examples

After years of investigation, the police finally put behind bars the notorious crime boss.
Many activists have been put behind bars for speaking out against the government.
His actions led to him being put behind bars for more than a decade.

Synset Examples

The suspects were imprisoned without trial
the murderer was incarcerated for the rest of his life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to imprison - to confine someone in prison
  • Antonym: to set free - to release someone from imprisonment or confinement
