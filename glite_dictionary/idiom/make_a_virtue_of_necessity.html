to make a virtue of necessity

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə ˈvɝːtʃu əv nəˈsɛsəti / (AmE), / meɪk ə ˈvɜːtʃuː əv nɪˈsɛsəti / (BrE)
to gain some advantage or find something good in a situation one is obliged to face, especially by adapting positively to it
find advantage in necessity

Examples

Instead of complaining about his workload, he chose to make a virtue of necessity and improved his skills.
The company had to reduce costs, but they managed to make a virtue of necessity by developing more efficient processes.
When her travel plans fell through, she decided to make a virtue of necessity and explored local attractions.

Synset Examples

She decided to make a virtue of necessity and embrace her new role.
They had to make a virtue of necessity during the challenging times.
He made a virtue of necessity by turning his hobby into a career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: necessity is the mother of invention - the idea that difficult situations inspire inventive solutions

Etymology

From a proverbial expression dating to Middle English; popularized by Chaucer and Shakespeare.
