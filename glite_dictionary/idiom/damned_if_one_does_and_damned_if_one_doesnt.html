damned if one does and damned if one doesn't

1. X

/ ˈdæmd ɪf wʌn ˈdʌz ənd ˈdæmd ɪf wʌn ˈdʌzənt / (AmE), / ˈdamd ɪf wʌn ˈdʌz ənd ˈdamd ɪf wʌn ˈdʌzənt / (BrE)
used to describe a situation in which a person will face negative consequences no matter which course of action they take
no-win situation or dilemma

Examples

No matter which decision he makes, he's damned if he does and damned if he doesn't.
Faced with this difficult choice, she knew she was damned if she does and damned if she doesn't.
The politician claimed he was damned if he does and damned if he doesn't during the heated debate.

Synset Examples

It's a classic case of being damned if one does and damned if one doesn't.
She felt damned if she does and damned if she doesn't in this dilemma.
In this situation, you’re damned if you do and damned if you don’t.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catch-22 - a dilemma or difficult circumstance from which there is no escape because of mutually conflicting conditions
