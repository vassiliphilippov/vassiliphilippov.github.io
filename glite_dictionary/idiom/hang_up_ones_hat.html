to hang up one's hat

1. VERB

/ hæŋ ʌp wʌnz hæt / (AmE), / hæŋ ʌp wʌnz hæt / (BrE)
to settle or take up residence in a particular place, making it one's home
settle or take up residence

Examples

After years of traveling, she finally decided to hang up her hat in a quiet coastal town.
Many retirees choose Florida as the place to hang up their hats and enjoy their golden years.
He moved from city to city for work, never staying long enough to hang up his hat anywhere.

Synset Examples

It's hard to find a place to hang up your hat here.

Etymology

From the literal act of hanging one's hat upon arrival to indicate staying somewhere; figurative use dates from the 19th century.

2. VERB

/ hæŋ ʌp wʌnz hæt / (AmE), / hæŋ ʌp wʌnz hæt / (BrE)
to retire from work or cease one’s regular activity, especially after a long period
retire or stop working

Examples

After thirty years at the company, he finally hung up his hat and began traveling the world.
Many athletes struggle to decide when to hang up their hat and leave the sport.
She was proud of her accomplishments when she hung up her hat and retired from teaching.

Synset Examples

After traveling the world, she finally hung up her hat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to retire - cease to work after reaching a certain age or length of service
  • Synonym: to step down - voluntarily leave an important job or position

Etymology

Alludes to no longer needing to hang one’s hat at work, symbolizing the end of employment.
