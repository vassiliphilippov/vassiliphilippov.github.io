to raise the stakes

1. VERB

/ reɪz ðə steɪks / (AmE), / reɪz ðə steɪks / (BrE)
to increase the level of risk or reward involved in a situation, making the consequences more significant
increase risk or consequence

Examples

The company decided to raise the stakes by launching a major advertising campaign.
She raised the stakes in the negotiation by offering an even bigger discount.
Introducing a time limit will raise the stakes and create a sense of urgency.

Synset Examples

To win the game, they need to raise the stakes.
The new evidence will raise the stakes in the trial.
Investing more money will raise the stakes significantly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to up the ante - to increase the demands or level of risk in a situation

Etymology

Originally from gambling, where placing more money increases what can be won or lost.
