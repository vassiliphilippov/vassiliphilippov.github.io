to suffer fools gladly

1. VERB

/ ˈsʌfər fulz ˈɡædli / (AmE), / ˈsʌfə fuːlz ˈɡædli / (BrE)
to have patience or tolerance for people regarded as foolish or incompetent
to tolerate fools or incompetence

Examples

The new manager does not suffer fools gladly and expects competence from everyone.
She is known to suffer fools gladly, always remaining calm with difficult clients.
If you can't suffer fools gladly, this job may test your patience.

Synset Examples

He does not suffer fools gladly in his workplace.
She rarely suffers fools gladly during meetings.
I can't suffer fools gladly when deadlines are tight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tolerate - accept or endure someone or something unpleasant
  • Synonym: to endure - put up with something or someone difficult
  • Antonym: lose patience - stop being patient or tolerant

Etymology

From the biblical phrase in 2 Corinthians 11:19, 'ye suffer fools gladly'.
