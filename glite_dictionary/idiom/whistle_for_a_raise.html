to whistle for a raise

1. VERB

/ ˈwɪsəl fəɹ ə ɹeɪz / (AmE), / ˈwɪsl fə ə ɹeɪz / (BrE)
(informal) To expect something, such as a raise, that is very unlikely to happen or that will not be given, especially despite one's efforts.
expect something unlikely or unattainable
informal

Examples

After the budget cuts, employees can just whistle for a raise this year.
If you don't improve your performance, you may as well whistle for a raise.
He finally understood he'd have to whistle for a raise despite all his extra hours.

Synset Examples

He realized he could whistle for a raise after his hard work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whistle for something - to hope or wait for something that will probably never happen
