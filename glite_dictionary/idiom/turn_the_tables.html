to turn the tables

1. VERB

/ tɝn ðə ˈteɪ.bəlz / (AmE), / tɜːn ðə ˈteɪ.bəlz / (BrE)
to reverse the situation so that you have an advantage over someone who previously had an advantage over you
reverse a situation to gain advantage

Examples

After losing the first match, the team managed to turn the tables and win the tournament.
She turned the tables on her opponent by presenting unexpected evidence in court.
The detective turned the tables on the criminal during the final confrontation.

Synset Examples

The tables are turned now that the Republicans are in power!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reverse the situation - to change a situation so that the previous advantage is lost or switched

Etymology

Originally from the practice of reversing the board in table games to change positions, 17th century.

2. VERB

/ tɝn ðə ˈteɪ.bəlz / (AmE), / tɜːn ðə ˈteɪ.bəlz / (BrE)
to reverse a situation, especially by gaining the advantage over someone who previously had the upper hand
reverse a situation to gain advantage

Examples

After trailing by ten points, the team managed to turn the tables in the second half.
She turned the tables on her opponent with a well-timed counterargument.
With one final move, he was able to turn the tables and win the game.

Synset Examples

She managed to turn the tables on her critics.
He was able to turn the tables during the debate.
He played the Uno reverse card to turn the tables on his opponent.
With a clever strategy, they turned the tables completely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gain the upper hand - to achieve a position of advantage
  • Antonym: be at a disadvantage - to be in a less favorable position

Etymology

From the practice in games such as backgammon, where physically turning the board would reverse players' positions.
