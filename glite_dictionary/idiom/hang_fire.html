to hang fire

1. VERB

/ hæŋ ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / hæŋ ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
to delay or postpone taking action or making a decision
delay or postpone taking action

Examples

We decided to hang fire on the announcement until the legal team reviewed the contract.
It might be better to hang fire until we hear back from all the applicants.
The committee chose to hang fire rather than rush into a hasty decision.

Synset Examples

They decided to hang fire until the meeting next week.
Let's hang fire until we have more information.
We need to hang fire on that decision for now.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to postpone - to put off something to a later time
  • Synonym: to defer - to put off (an action or event) to a later time
  • Antonym: to hasten - to move or act quickly, to speed up something

Etymology

Originally from firearms, where a 'hang fire' refers to a delayed discharge after pulling the trigger.
