fall from grace

1. VERB

/ fɔl frəm ɡreɪs / (AmE), / fɔːl frəm ɡreɪs / (BrE)
to go back to behaving badly after a period of good behavior, or to lose approval or respect after a wrongdoing
lose favor or revert to bad behavior

Examples

After years of sobriety, Mark fell from grace and began drinking again.
The celebrity fell from grace following a scandal that shocked her fans.
The students had been on their best behavior until they fell from grace during the class trip.

Synset Examples

The children fell from grace when they asked for several helpings of dessert

Related Terms

  • Antonym: redeem oneself - to make up for past faults or mistakes

2. NOUN

/ fɔl frəm ɡreɪs / (AmE), / fɔːl frəm ɡreɪs / (BrE)
the loss of status, respect, or prestige, especially after a wrongdoing or error
loss of status or respect

Examples

The politician's fall from grace was swift after the bribery allegations became public.
After years at the top of her field, the athlete suffered a fall from grace following a doping scandal.
Many fans could not believe his sudden fall from grace after such a promising career.

Synset Examples

After the accusations, she experienced a fall from grace.
His fall from grace shocked everyone in the industry.
The celebrity fell from grace after the scandal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgrace - loss of honor or respect as a result of a bad action
  • Antonym: rehabilitation - the restoration of someone's reputation

Etymology

From the biblical idea of losing divine favor or standing through sin or misdeed.
