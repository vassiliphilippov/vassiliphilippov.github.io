wide of the mark

1. ADJ

/ waɪd əv ðə mɑrk / (AmE), / waɪd əv ðə mɑːk / (BrE)
not accurate; incorrect or failing to achieve the intended result or target
not accurate; incorrect; off target

Examples

Her predictions about the election results were wide of the mark.
The reporter's assumptions turned out to be wide of the mark in the final analysis.
If your guess is wide of the mark, you'll have to try again.

Synset Examples

the kick was wide
the arrow was wide of the mark
a claim that was wide of the truth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: off the mark - not correct or accurate
