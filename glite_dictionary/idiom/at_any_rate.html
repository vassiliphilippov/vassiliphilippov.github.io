at any rate

1. ADV

/ æt ˈɛni reɪt / (AmE), / ət ˈɛni reɪt / (BrE)
used to add something to clarify or limit a previous statement, or to emphasize what is most important in a situation.
to clarify or emphasize a point

Examples

It was a difficult meeting, but at any rate we were able to agree on next steps.
She didn't win the prize, but at any rate, her work was highly praised.
He may have made mistakes; at any rate, he tried his best.

Synset Examples

at least he survived
they felt — at any rate Jim felt — relieved though still wary
the influence of economists — or at any rate of economics — is far-reaching

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at least - used to indicate the minimum or to introduce a concession

2. ADV

/ ət ˈɛni ˌreɪt / (AmE), / ət ˈɛni ˌreɪt / (BrE)
used to indicate that a statement clarifies, supports, or qualifies what was said before, or to signal a change in topic or emphasis.
used to clarify or qualify a previous statement

Examples

At any rate, we need to finish the project by next week.
He didn't win the match, but at any rate he played very well.
I'm not sure if she'll arrive on time; at any rate, we should be prepared.

Synset Examples

Anyhow, he is dead now
I think they're asleep; anyhow, they're quiet
I don't know what happened to it; anyway, it's gone
anyway, there is another factor to consider
I don't know how it started; in any case, there was a brief scuffle
in any event, the government faced a serious protest
but at any rate he got a knighthood for it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anyway - used to indicate a return to or emphasis on a previous point
  • Synonym: in any case - used to indicate that what is being said is true regardless of other things
