a little slice of Mayberry

1. NOUN

/ ə ˈlɪɾl slaɪs əv ˈmeɪˌbɛri / (AmE), / ə ˈlɪtəl slaɪs əv ˈmeɪbəri / (BrE)
A nostalgic or idealized small-town experience reminiscent of the simple, friendly life portrayed in the television show 'The Andy Griffith Show.'
an idealized small-town experience
american

Examples

Her new neighborhood feels like a little slice of Mayberry, with friendly neighbors and tree-lined streets.
After retiring, they moved to a town that seemed a little slice of Mayberry—peaceful, quiet, and close-knit.
The community festival had a little slice of Mayberry charm, bringing everyone together in a wholesome way.

Synset Examples

Her hometown felt like a little slice of Mayberry.

Etymology

Reference to Mayberry, the fictional town in 'The Andy Griffith Show' (1960–1968), symbolizing idealized American small-town life.
