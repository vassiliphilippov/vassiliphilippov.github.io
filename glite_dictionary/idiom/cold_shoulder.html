cold shoulder

1. NOUN

/ koʊld ˈʃoʊldər / (AmE), / kəʊld ˈʃəʊldə / (BrE)
a deliberate act of ignoring or snubbing someone, especially by refusing to acknowledge their presence
deliberate act of ignoring someone

Examples

After their argument, Sarah gave Mark the cold shoulder at the office party.
He tried to say hello, but she responded with a frosty cold shoulder and looked away.
Receiving the cold shoulder from his teammates made him feel isolated and unwelcome.

Synset Examples

the snub was clearly intentional

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snub - an act of rebuffing or ignoring someone
  • Antonym: welcome - to greet someone in a friendly way

Etymology

Originated in the early 19th century, possibly from the custom of serving a cold shoulder of meat to an unwelcome guest.

2. NOUN

/ koʊld ˈʃoʊldɚ / (AmE), / kəʊld ˈʃəʊldə / (BrE)
to deliberately ignore or show indifference towards someone
deliberate act of ignoring someone

Examples

Whenever I see her at work, she gives me the cold shoulder.
After the argument, Jane decided to give Mark the cold shoulder for a week.
Despite his apology, his friends still gave him the cold shoulder.

Synset Examples

I tried to talk to her, but got the cold shoulder.
He felt awkward when she gave him the cold shoulder at the party.
He received the cold shoulder from his colleagues today.
After that argument, she gave him the cold shoulder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snub - to ignore or spurn someone disdainfully
  • Antonym: welcome - to greet someone in a friendly way

Etymology

Originated in early 19th-century English, possibly from the phrase "to give the cold shoulder" referring to giving someone a cold slice of meat as an unfriendly gesture.
