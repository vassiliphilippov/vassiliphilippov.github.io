to cut the umbilical cord

1. VERB

/ kʌt ði ʌmˈbɪlɪkəl kɔrd / (AmE), / kʌt ði ʌmˈbɪlɪkəl kɔːd / (BrE)
To become independent from someone, especially parental influence or support.
become independent from parents or family

Examples

After graduating college, he finally cut the umbilical cord and found his own apartment.
It can be hard to cut the umbilical cord when your parents are very supportive.
She encouraged her son to cut the umbilical cord and make decisions on his own.

Synset Examples

It's challenging to cut the umbilical cord with family.
She felt it was time to cut the umbilical cord.
Once he moved out, he finally cut the umbilical cord.
He cut the umbilical cord after the baby was born.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: become independent - to stop depending on someone else for support or guidance

Etymology

From the literal act in childbirth of severing the baby's umbilical cord, metaphorically extended to detaching from parental dependence.
