to make a monkey out of

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə ˈmʌŋki aʊt əv / (AmE), / meɪk ə ˈmʌŋki aʊt ɒv / (BrE)
to make someone appear foolish or silly, often by tricking or embarrassing them
make someone seem foolish

Examples

He tried to make a monkey out of me in front of my friends, but I didn't fall for it.
She was embarrassed when her coworkers made a monkey out of her with a practical joke.
Don't let anyone make a monkey out of you during the meeting.

Synset Examples

Don't make a monkey out of me with your jokes.
She felt they were trying to make a monkey out of her.
He vowed never to let them make a monkey out of him again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: make a fool of - to cause someone to appear foolish
