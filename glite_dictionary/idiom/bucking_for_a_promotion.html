to bucking for a promotion

1. VERB

/ ˈbʌkɪŋ fər ə prəˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / ˈbʌkɪŋ fə ə prəˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
trying hard or showing great effort to get a promotion at work
actively trying to get a promotion
informal american

Examples

He was bucking for a promotion long before the new management took over.
It's obvious that she's bucking for a promotion by volunteering for every challenging project.
Some employees start bucking for a promotion as soon as they join a new company.

Synset Examples

He's always bucking for a promotion at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seek a promotion - to try to get a higher position at work
  • Antonym: settle - to accept one's current position without striving for advancement

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'buck for' (to strive for), which originated in American English in the early 20th century.
