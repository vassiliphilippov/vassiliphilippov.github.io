to make mincemeat out of

1. VERB

/ meɪk ˈmɪnsˌmit ˈaʊt əv / (AmE), / meɪk ˈmɪnsmiːt ˈaʊt əv / (BrE)
to defeat thoroughly, criticize harshly, or destroy the arguments of someone or something
defeat or demolish completely

Examples

The champion easily made mincemeat out of his inexperienced rival in the first round.
Her sharp arguments made mincemeat out of the opposition's case in court.
The new software made mincemeat out of the complex data analysis that used to take hours.

Synset Examples

The champion made mincemeat out of his opponent.
The critics made mincemeat out of the poorly written play.
She made mincemeat out of the tough math problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to trounce - to defeat convincingly
  • Synonym: to annihilate - to destroy completely
  • Antonym: go easy on - to treat gently or leniently

Etymology

From the process of turning something into mincemeat, figuratively meaning to reduce to fragments or render powerless. The phrase dates back to the 19th century.
