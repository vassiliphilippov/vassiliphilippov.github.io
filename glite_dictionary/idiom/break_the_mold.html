to break the mold

1. VERB

/ breɪk ðə moʊld / (AmE), / breɪk ðə məʊld / (BrE)
to do something in a completely new, original, or innovative way; to depart from tradition or standard practice
do something completely new or different

Examples

The company hopes to break the mold with its revolutionary approach to sustainability.
Her new novel truly breaks the mold of traditional storytelling.
By starting his own business at 18, he decided to break the mold rather than follow a conventional path.

Synset Examples

This product breaks the mold with its unique features.
She aims to break the mold with her innovative approach.
The new design really breaks the mold of traditional architecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to innovate - to introduce new ideas or methods
  • Antonym: to follow the crowd - to do what most other people do, without originality

Etymology

Originally referring to casting molds used in manufacturing; breaking the mold prevents exact duplication, symbolizing uniqueness.
