to face brickbats

1. VERB

/ feɪs ˈbrɪkˌbæts / (AmE), / feɪs ˈbrɪkˌbæts / (BrE)
to receive criticism or harsh remarks, especially in a public setting
receive criticism or harsh remarks

Examples

After leaking the report, the manager had to face brickbats from both staff and media.
The new policy announcement made the CEO face brickbats at the press conference.
Even talented artists sometimes face brickbats for their controversial work.

Synset Examples

The politician faced brickbats during the debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: face criticism - to be subjected to critical comments or disapproval
  • Antonym: receive praise - to be given positive feedback or approval

Etymology

From 'brickbat', meaning a piece of brick used as a missile, metaphorically extended to mean verbal attacks.
