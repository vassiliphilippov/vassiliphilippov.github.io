easy as pie

1. ADJ

/ ˈiːzi æz paɪ / (AmE), / ˈiːzi æz paɪ / (BrE)
extremely easy to do; requiring little effort or skill
very easy; requiring little effort

Examples

Finishing that assignment was easy as pie, and I did it in under an hour.
Don't worry about fixing the printer—it's easy as pie once you know how.
She found learning to ride a bicycle easy as pie compared to driving a car.

Synset Examples

Solving that riddle was easy as pie.
Getting there by bus is easy as pie.
Cooking this dish is easy as pie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: a piece of cake - something that is very easy to do

Etymology

Probably from American English slang of the 19th–20th century, likening something pleasurable and simple to eating pie.
