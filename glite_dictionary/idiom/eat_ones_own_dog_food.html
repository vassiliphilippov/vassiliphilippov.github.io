to eat one's own dog food

1. VERB

/ it wʌnz oʊn dɔɡ fuːd / (AmE), / iːt wʌnz əʊn dɒɡ fuːd / (BrE)
(of a company or its employees) to use its own products or services to demonstrate their quality and gain better insight into them.
use one's own product or service

Examples

At our startup, we always eat our own dog food before releasing features to the public.
The CEO urged his team to eat their own dog food and test the new app thoroughly.
If you truly believe in your solution, you should eat your own dog food every day.

Synset Examples

The company decided to eat its own dog food by using its software daily.
If we want customers to trust us, we must eat our own dog food.
He believes in eating his own dog food, so he uses his products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: practice what you preach - to act according to the advice you give others

Etymology

Originated in the US technology industry in the 1980s, evoking the imagery of a company confident enough to use its own product.
