nook and cranny

1. NOUN

/ nʊk ənd ˈkræni / (AmE), / nʊk ənd ˈkræni / (BrE)
every small or hidden place in a location, especially when searching or examining thoroughly
every small or hidden place

Examples

She searched every nook and cranny of the old house for her missing necklace.
The cleaning crew made sure to reach every nook and cranny in the building.
Tourists love exploring the city's nook and cranny for hidden cafes and shops.

Synset Examples

He knows every nook and cranny of the old library.
He can find a deal in any nook or cranny.
Don't overlook a single nook or cranny while cleaning.
The cat hides in every nook or cranny of the house.
The cat hid in any nook or cranny available.
They searched every nook or cranny for the missing keys.
You can find treasures in every nook or cranny here.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: every corner - each possible place; all parts of a location

Etymology

From "nook" (a corner or recess) and "cranny" (a small, narrow opening or crevice); both words date to Middle English.
