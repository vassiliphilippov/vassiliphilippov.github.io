to get one's freak on

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt wʌnz friːk ɑn / (AmE), / ɡɛt wʌnz friːk ɒn / (BrE)
to enjoy oneself uninhibitedly, often by dancing or behaving in an uninhibited or energetic manner
let loose; behave freely and energetically
informal

Examples

Whenever the music starts, she can't wait to get her freak on and dance.
He really knows how to get his freak on at weddings.
The festival is the perfect place to get your freak on and express yourself.

Synset Examples

He decided to get his freak on and let loose at the party.
She loves to get her freak on at the club.
