cap in hand

1. ADV

/ kæp ɪn hænd / (AmE), / kæp ɪn hand / (BrE)
in a humble or submissive manner when asking for help or forgiveness
humbly asking for help or forgiveness

Examples

He went cap in hand to his boss to ask for his job back.
The company approached the government cap in hand requesting financial assistance.
After the mistake, she came cap in hand to apologize to the team.

Synset Examples

She went cap in hand to her parents for help.
The charity worker came cap in hand, seeking donations for the event.
He approached his boss with cap in hand, asking for a raise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humble - showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance
  • Antonym: arrogant - having or revealing an exaggerated sense of one's own importance

Etymology

From the historical practice of removing one’s cap as a sign of respect or humility.
