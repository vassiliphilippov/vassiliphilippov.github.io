every bit of

1. X

/ ˈɛvri bɪt ʌv / (AmE), / ˈɛvri bɪt ɒv / (BrE)
used to emphasize that something includes all parts or amounts of something; the whole of.
the whole of; all of something

Examples

She enjoyed every bit of her vacation in Italy.
The children ate every bit of their dinner without complaining.
You deserve every bit of the praise you’re receiving.

Synset Examples

I enjoyed every bit of the chocolate cake.
She appreciated every bit of help he offered.
He saved every bit of money for his trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: all of - the whole amount or quantity of something
