to pull one's finger out

1. VERB

/ pʊl wʌnz ˈfɪŋɡɚ aʊt / (AmE), / pʊl wʌnz ˈfɪŋɡər aʊt / (BrE)
(British, informal) to start working harder or taking action, especially after a period of inactivity or procrastination.
start taking action; stop procrastinating
informal british

Examples

If we want the event to be a success, everyone needs to pull their finger out and contribute.
She pulled her finger out just in time to meet the deadline.
You'll have to pull your finger out if you want to get promoted.

Synset Examples

He finally pulled his finger out and finished the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get a move on - start doing something quickly
  • Synonym: to get one's act together - become organized and efficient
