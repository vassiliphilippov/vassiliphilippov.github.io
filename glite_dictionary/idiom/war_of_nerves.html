war of nerves

1. NOUN

/ wɔr əv nɜrvz / (AmE), / wɔːr əv nɜːvz / (BrE)
a contest or confrontation in which opponents try to wear each other down or gain advantage through psychological means, tension, and pressure rather than physical force
psychological contest involving tension

Examples

Both teams were locked in a war of nerves before the crucial match began.
The negotiations became a war of nerves as each side refused to back down.
Enduring the constant uncertainty felt like being in a war of nerves.

Synset Examples

In a war of nerves, patience often wins over aggression.
She played a war of nerves with her opponent until the last moment.
The negotiation turned into a war of nerves between both parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychological warfare - the use of psychological tactics to destroy the enemy's morale
