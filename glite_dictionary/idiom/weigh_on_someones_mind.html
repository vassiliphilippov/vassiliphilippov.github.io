to weigh on someone's mind

1. VERB

/ weɪ ɑn ˈsʌmwʌnz maɪnd / (AmE), / weɪ ɒn ˈsʌmwʌnz maɪnd / (BrE)
to make someone feel anxious, worried, or troubled over a period of time
trouble or worry someone persistently

Examples

The possibility of losing his job continued to weigh on his mind for weeks.
Her harsh words still weigh on my mind even after all this time.
Financial worries can weigh on your mind and keep you up at night.

Synset Examples

Her words continued to weigh on my mind long after.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bother - to cause someone to feel worried or upset
  • Antonym: to reassure - to remove someone's doubts or fears
