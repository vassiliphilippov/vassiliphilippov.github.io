to gag for it

1. VERB

/ ɡæɡ fɔr ɪt / (AmE), / ɡæɡ fə ɪt / (BrE)
to want something very strongly or eagerly, often used informally and particularly in British English.
eagerly want something, informal idiom
informal british

Examples

After the long meeting, everyone was gagging for it—a cup of fresh coffee.
By the end of the match, the players were gagging for it: a chance to finally relax.
He's been gagging for it ever since he heard about the promotion.

Synset Examples

After that presentation, he was gagging for it.

I'm gagging for it after that long workout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long for - to want something very much
  • Synonym: to crave - to feel a powerful desire for something
