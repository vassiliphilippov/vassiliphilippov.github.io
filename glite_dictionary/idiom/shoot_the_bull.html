to shoot the bull

1. VERB

/ ʃut ðə bʊl / (AmE), / ʃuːt ðə bʊl / (BrE)
to engage in informal or casual conversation; to chat idly
to chat informally
informal american

Examples

After the meeting, we went to a café to shoot the bull for a while.
Sometimes my uncle and I just shoot the bull on the porch in the evenings.
They would often shoot the bull about politics and local news over coffee.

Synset Examples

The guys gathered to shoot the bull after work.
He loves to shoot the bull about his travels.
We shot the bull about sports and movies last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shoot the breeze - to converse casually or idly
