to work like a dog

1. VERB

/ wɝk laɪk ə dɔɡ / (AmE), / wɜːk laɪk ə dɒɡ / (BrE)
to work very hard and tirelessly, often with little rest or reward
to work very hard

Examples

She had to work like a dog to finish the project before the deadline.
Even after years of working like a dog, he barely made enough to support his family.
If you work like a dog all your life, you deserve a comfortable retirement.

Synset Examples

He had to work like a dog to pay the bills.
During harvest season, farmers work like dogs.
She works like a dog to keep her family comfortable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toil - to work extremely hard
  • Antonym: to take it easy - to relax and avoid hard work
