to scare the hoes

1. VERB

/ skɛr ðə hoʊz / (AmE), / skɛə ðə həʊz / (BrE)
To act in a way that frightens, intimidates, or makes others uncomfortable, especially in a social or humorous context; often used to describe behavior that disrupts enjoyment or vibe.
to ruin the mood or intimidate others (slang)
slang informal

Examples

If you keep talking about politics at the party, you'll scare the hoes.
Playing that creepy music all night might scare the hoes away.
She warned him not to start his rant, saying it would scare the hoes.

Synset Examples

Don't tell that story; it might scare the hoes!
He was trying to scare the hoes with his spooky tales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kill the vibe - to ruin the mood or atmosphere (slang)
