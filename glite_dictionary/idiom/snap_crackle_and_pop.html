snap, crackle and pop

1. NOUN

/ snæp ˈkrækəl ənd pɑp / (AmE), / snæp ˈkrækəl ənd pɒp / (BrE)
a series of sharp, crackling sounds like those made by crisp cereals when eaten or by joints during movement
sharp crackling sounds, as of cereal or joints

Examples

When she poured milk over her cereal, it began to snap, crackle and pop in the bowl.
He laughed as his knees would snap, crackle and pop every time he stood up.
The fire in the grate gave a satisfying snap, crackle and pop as it burned.

Synset Examples

The cereal was fresh, snap, crackle and pop in every bite.
His joints snap, crackle and pop as he stretches.
The campfire made a snap, crackle and pop sound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crackle - to make a series of short, sharp noises

Etymology

Popularized by the advertising slogan for Rice Krispies cereal, first used in the 1930s.
