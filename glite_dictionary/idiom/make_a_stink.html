to make a stink

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə stɪŋk / (AmE), / meɪk ə stɪŋk / (BrE)
to complain or protest loudly and forcefully, especially to show dissatisfaction or disapproval.
complain or protest loudly

Examples

If the company cuts benefits, workers are sure to make a stink about it.
She threatened to make a stink if the problem wasn't resolved quickly.
The parents made a stink over the school's new policy.

Synset Examples

She raised hell when she found out that she would not be hired again

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to raise hell - to cause a disturbance by protesting or complaining loudly
  • Synonym: to kick up a fuss - to make a lot of unnecessary noise or complaint

2. VERB

/ meɪk ə stɪŋk / (AmE), / meɪk ə stɪŋk / (BrE)
to complain or protest loudly and publicly, especially in a way that draws attention; to cause a commotion by expressing dissatisfaction
complain or protest loudly
informal

Examples

If the company ignores your complaint, you should make a stink about it.
The parents threatened to make a stink if the school didn't address the issue.
When he was overcharged, he didn't hesitate to make a stink at the store.

Synset Examples

You should make a stink if your rights are violated.
They decided to make a stink about the unfair policy.
The residents made a stink over the proposed construction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raise a fuss - to make a commotion or protest; express annoyance loudly
  • Synonym: kick up a stink - to create a disturbance by complaining; protest noisily
