stone the crows

1. INTJ

/ stoʊn ðə kroʊz / (AmE), / stəʊn ðə krəʊz / (BrE)
An exclamation used to express surprise, disbelief, or astonishment.
exclamation of surprise or disbelief
informal british

Examples

Stone the crows, I never thought I’d see the day he arrived on time!
"Stone the crows!" she said when she heard the news.
Stone the crows, you mean he climbed the whole mountain by himself?

Synset Examples

Stone the crows! I can't believe you won!
He exclaimed, stone the crows, what a surprise!
Stone the crows, that was an unexpected twist!

Etymology

British English idiom, origin uncertain; recorded from the 19th century as a mild oath.
