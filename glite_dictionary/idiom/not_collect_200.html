not collect $200

1. X

/ nɑt kəˈlɛkt tu ˈhʌndrəd ˈdɑlɚz / (AmE), / nɒt kəˈlɛkt tu ˈhʌndrəd ˈdɒləz / (BrE)
Used to indicate that someone is not allowed to receive an expected benefit, especially by evoking a rule from Monopoly where a player cannot collect a reward.
denied an expected benefit (Monopoly reference)

Examples

After being disqualified, the runner had to leave the race and not collect $200.
If you break the rules, you'll be sent home and not collect $200.
The employee was dismissed for misconduct and told to not collect $200 on the way out.

Synset Examples

They told him to leave and not collect $200.

Etymology

Reference to the Monopoly board game instruction, 'Do not pass Go, do not collect $200.'
