to dot the i's and cross the t's

1. VERB

/ dɑt ði aɪz ənd krɔs ðə tiːz / (AmE), / dɒt ði aɪz ənd krɒs ðə tiːz / (BrE)
to pay careful attention to small details, especially when finishing a task
pay attention to details

Examples

Before we send out the contract, let's dot the i's and cross the t's to avoid any mistakes.
She was hired because she always dotted the i's and crossed the t's on every project.
The editor spent hours dotting the i's and crossing the t's to ensure the article was perfect.

Synset Examples

They needed to dot their i's and cross their t's.
We need to dot the i's and cross the t's on this project.
Before submitting the report, make sure to dot the i's and cross the t's.
She always dots the i's and crosses the t's in her presentations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be meticulous - to show great attention to detail; very careful and precise

Etymology

Refers to making documents accurate by ensuring every letter is correctly formed; the idiom arose in the 19th century.
