to set by the ears

1. VERB

/ sɛt baɪ ðə ɪrz / (AmE), / sɛt baɪ ði ɪəz / (BrE)
to cause disagreement, disturbance, or conflict between people
to cause conflict or disturbance
archaic

Examples

The heated rumor set by the ears everyone in the office for days.
She feared that her remarks might set by the ears the entire committee.
It was easy for the troublemaker to set by the ears the rival teams.

Synset Examples

Their disagreement set the entire team by the ears.
The argument set the neighbors by the ears.
The two players were set by the ears after the foul.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarrel - an angry argument or disagreement
  • Synonym: to incite - to stir up or provoke (especially conflict)

Etymology

Refers metaphorically to animals fighting, grabbing each other by the ears; attested from late Middle English.
