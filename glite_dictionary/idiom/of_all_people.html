of all people

1. X

/ əv ɔl ˈpipəl / (AmE), / əv ɔːl ˈpiːpl / (BrE)
used to express surprise or irony that a particular person, more than anyone else, is involved in something unexpected or unusual
used to show surprise at someone's involvement

Examples

I never thought Mary, of all people, would arrive late to the meeting.
Why did you trust Tom, of all people, with that secret?
He forgot my birthday, of all people!

Synset Examples

I can't believe he, of all people, forgot my birthday.
Why did she invite him, of all people?
She chose him, of all people, to confide in.
