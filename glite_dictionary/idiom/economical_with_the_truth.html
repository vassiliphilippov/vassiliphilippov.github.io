economical with the truth

1. ADJ

/ ɪˌkɑːnəˈmɪkəl wɪð ðə truːθ / (AmE), / ɪˌkɒnəˈmɪkəl wɪð ðə truːθ / (BrE)
deliberately withholding the whole truth, being intentionally vague or misleading rather than openly lying
deliberately not fully honest or straightforward
british

Examples

During the interview, the official was economical with the truth about the real reasons for the cuts.
It's clear she was economical with the truth when she described her involvement in the accident.
Some writers are economical with the truth to create suspense or protect privacy.

Synset Examples

The politician was economical with the truth during the debate.
Being economical with the truth rarely ends well in relationships.
She was economical with the truth about her past experiences.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: lie - an intentionally false statement
  • Synonym: to deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true

Etymology

Originally popularized in British political discourse, especially after being used during the 1986 Spycatcher trial.
