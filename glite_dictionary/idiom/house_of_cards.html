house of cards

1. NOUN

/ haʊs əv kɑrdz / (AmE), / haʊs əv kɑːdz / (BrE)
a plan, organization, or situation that appears strong or solid but is actually fragile and likely to fail suddenly
unstable or fragile situation

Examples

After months of expansion, the company's finances turned out to be a house of cards and quickly collapsed.
Their alliance was a house of cards, falling apart at the first sign of disagreement.
His elaborate excuse was a house of cards that crumbled under simple questioning.

Synset Examples

his proposal was nothing but a house of cards
a real estate bubble

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bubble - a speculative economic cycle characterized by rapid escalation of asset prices followed by a contraction
  • Antonym: stable - not likely to change or fail; firmly established

2. NOUN

/ haʊs əv kɑrdz / (AmE), / haʊs əv kɑːdz / (BrE)
a situation, plan, or system that is very fragile and can easily collapse if something goes wrong
fragile or unstable situation

Examples

After the scandal, their entire company was revealed to be a house of cards.
With just one key witness missing, the prosecution's case became a house of cards.
Their marriage, based on secrets, eventually collapsed like a house of cards.

Synset Examples

The project turned into a house of cards after the budget cuts.
Building a business on lies is like making a house of cards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unstable - not firm or fixed, liable to collapse
  • Antonym: secure - firmly fixed and not likely to fall or change

Etymology

Originally referring to a structure made by stacking cards, easily knocked down; figurative use since the 17th century.
