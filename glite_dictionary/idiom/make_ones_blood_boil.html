to make one's blood boil

1. VERB

/ meɪk wʌnz blʌd bɔɪl / (AmE), / meɪk wʌnz blʌd bɔɪl / (BrE)
to make someone feel extremely angry
make someone very angry (idiom)

Examples

The sight of injustice always makes my blood boil.
It made her blood boil to hear such blatant lies.
Their careless attitude toward the environment really makes my blood boil.

Synset Examples

The unfair decision really made his blood boil.
Her rude comments made my blood boil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to infuriate - to make very angry
  • Synonym: to enrage - to make extremely angry
  • Antonym: to calm down - to make less angry or upset
