pardon my French

1. X

/ ˈpɑrdn maɪ ˈfrɛntʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːdn maɪ ˈfrɛntʃ / (BrE)
used to apologize for swearing or using rude or offensive language, often said humorously
apology for offensive language

Examples

He spilled coffee on his shirt and, after a brief outburst, said, "pardon my French."
Pardon my French, but that was an incredibly stupid idea.
She always says 'pardon my French' after she lets a swear word slip.

Synset Examples

She nervously said, 'pardon my French,' after swearing.
He cursed loudly, and said, 'pardon my French!'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excuse my language - used to apologize for using swear words or offensive language

Etymology

Used humorously from the 19th century, originally a reference to the use of actual French words in conversation.
