he who said the rhyme did the crime

1. X

/ hi hu sɛd ðə raɪm dɪd ðə kraɪm / (AmE), / hiː huː sɛd ðə raɪm dɪd ðə kraɪm / (BrE)
used to suggest that the person who utters an incriminating rhyme or accusation is the one actually responsible for the misdeed; the guilty party reveals themselves by speaking first.
the guilty person reveals themself by speaking first
informal

Examples

Whenever something was broken in class, the teacher would say, "he who said the rhyme did the crime."
Kids chanted, "he who said the rhyme did the crime," after someone made a suspicious accusation on the playground.
In the family, repeating he who said the rhyme did the crime was a way to tease the sibling most eager to blame others.

Synset Examples

In the debate, everyone knew he who said the rhyme did the crime.
