to tickle the ear

1. VERB

/ ˈtɪkəl ði ɪr / (AmE), / ˈtɪkəl ðiː ɪə / (BrE)
to please or delight someone with pleasant sounds, especially with music or gentle words
to delight with pleasant sounds

Examples

The gentle rain on the rooftop seemed to tickle the ear and soothe her to sleep.
The violinist's beautiful solo tickled the ear of everyone in the concert hall.
His melodic accent always tickles the ear when he speaks.

Synset Examples

The sweet melody was sure to tickle the ear.
Her soothing words tickled the ear like music.
The singer's voice tickled the ear of the audience.
