to wag the dog

1. VERB

/ wæɡ ðə dɒɡ / (AmE), / wæɡ ðə dɒɡ / (BrE)
to intentionally divert attention from an important matter to something less significant, especially in politics or the media
divert attention from an important issue

Examples

Critics accused the administration of trying to wag the dog with a sudden foreign policy distraction.
Some believe the scandal was created simply to wag the dog and deflect from economic issues.
Whenever bad news breaks, politicians are quick to wag the dog with a new controversy.

Synset Examples

The media often tries to wag the dog with sensational stories.
In politics, it’s easy to wag the dog for distraction.
Sometimes it feels like they’re just trying to wag the dog.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to distract - to prevent someone from giving full attention to something

Etymology

From the idea that a dog should wag its tail, not the tail wag the dog; popularized by the 1997 film "Wag the Dog".
