lay of the land

1. NOUN

/ leɪ əv ðə lænd / (AmE), / leɪ əv ðə lænd / (BrE)
the existing state of affairs or circumstances in a particular situation
the current state of affairs

Examples

Before starting the project, it's wise to get the lay of the land.
The new manager spent her first week learning the lay of the land.
Let's meet tomorrow to discuss the lay of the land before we proceed.

Synset Examples

Knowing the lay of the land helps with planning.
I need to understand the lay of the land first.
Before making decisions, let's assess the lay of the land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: state of affairs - the situation as it is
  • Synonym: situation - a set of circumstances at a particular time

Etymology

Originally referred to the physical features of an area; now used figuratively for situations.
