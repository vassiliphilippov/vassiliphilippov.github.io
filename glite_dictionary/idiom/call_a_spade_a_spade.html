to call a spade a spade

1. VERB

/ kɔl ə speɪd ə speɪd / (AmE), / kɔːl ə speɪd ə speɪd / (BrE)
to speak plainly and directly about something, even if it is unpleasant or controversial
speak plainly and directly

Examples

She always prefers to call a spade a spade, even if the truth is uncomfortable.
Let’s call a spade a spade and say this plan isn’t working.
It’s refreshing to hear someone call a spade a spade in politics.

Synset Examples

To be honest, we need to call a spade a spade here.
It's time to call a spade a spade about their performance.
We should call a spade a spade and address the issue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speak plainly - to express oneself in a clear and direct manner
  • Antonym: to beat around the bush - to avoid getting to the point or speaking directly

Etymology

From the earlier phrase 'to call a spade a spade,' first recorded in English in the 16th century, translating a Greek proverb meaning 'to call a fig a fig and a trough a trough.'
