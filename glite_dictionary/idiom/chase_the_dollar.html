to chase the dollar

1. VERB

/ tʃeɪs ðə ˈdɑlər / (AmE), / tʃeɪs ðə ˈdɒlə / (BrE)
to actively seek or pursue wealth or financial gain, often with great effort or determination
pursue financial gain

Examples

After graduating from college, he decided to chase the dollar instead of following his childhood dream.
Many people move to big cities to chase the dollar and improve their financial situation.
She grew tired of always chasing the dollar and started focusing on her passions instead.

Synset Examples

Investing in stocks is like chasing the dollar of the daddies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pursue wealth - to try to become rich
  • Degree Lower: make a living - earn enough money to support oneself
