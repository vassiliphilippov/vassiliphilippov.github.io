the high road

1. NOUN

/ ðə haɪ roʊd / (AmE), / ðə haɪ rəʊd / (BrE)
A course of action that is morally correct or ethical, especially when a less principled option is available.
morally correct course of action

Examples

Even though he was insulted, Mark decided to take the high road and not respond with anger.
In difficult situations, it's often best to choose the high road rather than retaliate.
She always prefers the high road, maintaining her integrity despite challenges.

Synset Examples

Taking the high road often leads to better outcomes.
She chose the high road, refusing to engage in gossip.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: the low road - a course of action that is morally questionable or unethical
