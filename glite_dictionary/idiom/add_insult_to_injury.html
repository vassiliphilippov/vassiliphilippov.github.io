to add insult to injury

1. VERB

/ æd ˈɪn.sʌlt tu ˈɪn.dʒə.ri / (AmE), / æd ˈɪn.sʌlt tʊ ˈɪn.dʒə.ri / (BrE)
to make a bad situation even worse by doing something that upsets someone further
make a bad situation worse

Examples

Forgetting my birthday added insult to injury after he had already missed our dinner.
The company fired him and then added insult to injury by withholding his last paycheck.
Failing the course was tough, but the teacher added insult to injury with harsh criticism.

Synset Examples

She arrived late and added insult to injury by forgetting my name.
Losing the game was bad enough, but the referee added insult to injury.
He broke my phone and then added insult to injury by laughing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rub salt in the wound - to make someone feel worse about something bad that has already happened

Etymology

From Latin phrase 'addere iniuriam iniuriae', used since the 18th century in English.
