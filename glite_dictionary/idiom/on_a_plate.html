on a plate

1. ADV

/ ɑn ə pleɪt / (AmE), / ɒn ə pleɪt / (BrE)
If something is given to you on a plate, it is offered very easily or with no effort required from you.
offered very easily, without effort

Examples

The opportunity to work abroad was given to her on a plate.
He expected the answers to be handed on a plate without doing any research himself.
She got the contract on a plate, thanks to her father's connections.

Synset Examples

She offered me the job on a plate, no interview needed.
He served the promotion to her on a plate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handed to someone on a silver platter - offered very easily or without effort

2. ADV

/ ɑn ə pleɪt / (AmE), / ɒn ə pleɪt / (BrE)
if something is given or handed to you on a plate, you receive it very easily, without having to work for it.
received easily, without effort

Examples

After all his hard work, victory was finally offered on a plate.
She expected the job to be handed to her on a plate, but she had to compete like everyone else.
Opportunities like this aren't usually given on a plate, so make the most of it.

Synset Examples

They handed me the solution on a plate, which was unexpected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handed to someone on a silver platter - given as a gift or advantage without effort
