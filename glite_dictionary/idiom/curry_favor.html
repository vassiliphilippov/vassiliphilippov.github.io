to curry favor

1. VERB

/ ˌkɜri ˈfeɪvər / (AmE), / ˌkʌri ˈfeɪvə / (BrE)
to try to gain someone's approval or advantage by giving them praise or flattery, often insincerely
seek approval by flattery

Examples

She tried to curry favor with her boss by constantly complimenting him on his ideas.
Politicians often curry favor with wealthy donors during election campaigns.
He was accused of currying favor with the teachers to get better grades.

Synset Examples

This employee is currying favor with his superordinates

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flatter - to praise excessively, especially insincerely
  • Synonym: to ingratiate - to try to win favor with someone by pleasing them

Etymology

From Middle English 'curre favell,' an adaptation of Old French 'cuerre favel' (literally 'to curry the chestnut horse'), referring to deceitful behavior; 'favor' later replaced 'favel' by folk etymology.
