straight from central casting

1. ADJ

/ streɪt frəm ˈsɛntrəl ˈkæstɪŋ / (AmE), / streɪt frəm ˈsɛntrəl ˈkɑːstɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person, thing, or quality) appearing perfectly suited or stereotypical for a role or situation, as if chosen deliberately for authenticity or effect.
perfectly suited or stereotypical for a situation

Examples

The new coach looks straight from central casting with his whistle and stern demeanor.
Her voice is straight from central casting if you’re searching for a classic radio presenter.
That old mansion seems straight from central casting for a haunted house movie.

Synset Examples

Her talent seemed straight from central casting.

Etymology

Refers to actors selected from 'Central Casting', a famous Hollywood agency established in 1925 that supplied extras for films.
