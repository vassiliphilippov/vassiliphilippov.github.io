not so fast

1. X

/ nɑt soʊ fæst / (AmE), / nɒt səʊ fɑːst / (BrE)
used to tell someone to slow down or wait, especially when they are acting too quickly or making assumptions
used to tell someone to slow down or wait

Examples

Not so fast; you need to listen to all the instructions first.
He jumped up to leave, but his teacher said, "Not so fast! We have one more thing to do."
Before you assume everything is settled, not so fast—there are more details to consider.

Synset Examples

She wanted to finish, but I told her not so fast.
Not so fast; we need to check the details first.
He tried to leave, but I said not so fast.
