blue blood

1. NOUN

/ bluː blʌd / (AmE), / bluː blʌd / (BrE)
a person of noble or aristocratic birth or background
person of noble descent

Examples

Many royal families are considered blue bloods with a long history of privilege.
Despite being a blue blood, she chose to work in public service.
He was treated differently at school because he was rumored to be a blue blood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aristocrat - a member of the aristocracy
  • Antonym: commoner - a person without noble rank or title

Etymology

From Spanish 'sangre azul', referring to the visible blue veins of fair-skinned nobility.
