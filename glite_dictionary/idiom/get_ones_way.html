to get one's way

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt wʌnz weɪ / (AmE), / ɡɛt wʌnz weɪ / (BrE)
to achieve what one wants, especially in spite of opposition or difficulty
achieve what one wants

Examples

Despite objections from the team, Julia managed to get her way and launch the project.
Children sometimes throw tantrums when they don't get their way.
He was used to being in charge and always got his way at work.

Synset Examples

He always finds a way to get his way.
Sometimes it's hard to get one's way in a group.
He can be mean when he doesn't get his way.
She always gets her way in negotiations.
He always gets his way in meetings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prevail - to gain the desired outcome, especially through persistence
  • Antonym: lose out - to fail to achieve what one wants
