to cast a pall

1. VERB

/ kæst ə pɔl / (AmE), / kɑːst ə pɔːl / (BrE)
to cause a gloomy or depressing atmosphere to spread over a situation or event
create a gloomy atmosphere

Examples

The tragic news cast a pall over the entire community.
Her absence cast a pall on the usually cheerful meeting.
The failure of the project cast a pall over the company's annual celebration.

Synset Examples

His sudden departure cast a pall on the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cast a shadow - to make something less positive or cause unhappiness

Etymology

From 'pall', meaning a cloth covering a coffin, figuratively extended to mean something that covers with darkness or gloom.
