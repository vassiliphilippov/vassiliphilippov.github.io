not at all

1. ADV

/ nɑt ət ɔl / (AmE), / nɒt ət ɔːl / (BrE)
used to emphasize a complete lack of anything or to strongly deny something
complete negation; strong denial

Examples

He said he was tired, but he didn't look not at all weary to me.
The new policy affected me not at all, so I didn't object.
Are you angry with me? Not at all—I'm just a bit surprised.

Synset Examples

I'm not at all interested in that movie.
I was not at all surprised by the outcome.
