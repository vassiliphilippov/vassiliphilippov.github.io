crack of dawn

1. NOUN

/ kræk əv dɔn / (AmE), / kræk əv dɔːn / (BrE)
the moment when the sun first rises; very early in the morning
very early morning

Examples

We set our alarms and left at the crack of dawn to catch the earliest train.
She prefers to start gardening at the crack of dawn when the air is still cool.
Farmers often begin their work at the crack of dawn to make the most of daylight.

Synset Examples

She enjoys jogging at the crack of dawn.
The birds start chirping at the crack of dawn.
We left at the crack of dawn to avoid traffic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daybreak - the time in the morning when daylight first appears
  • Synonym: dawn - the first appearance of light in the sky before sunrise

Etymology

Refers to the 'crack' or first appearance of daylight at sunrise.
