to lick one's wounds

1. VERB

/ lɪk wʌnz wundz / (AmE), / lɪk wʌnz wuːndz / (BrE)
to take time to recover after emotional pain, defeat, or disappointment
recover after setback or hurt

Examples

After failing the exam, he spent a quiet weekend at home to lick his wounds.
The team retreated to the locker room to lick their wounds after the tough defeat.
She needed some time alone to lick her wounds before returning to work.

Synset Examples

After the breakup, she needed time to lick her wounds.
He took a few days off to lick his wounds after the loss.
It's time to lick your wounds and move on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recover - regain strength or confidence after difficulty
  • Synonym: to move on - to move forward emotionally after a setback or hurt
