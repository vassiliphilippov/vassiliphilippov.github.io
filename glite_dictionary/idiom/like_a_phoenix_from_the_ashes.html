like a phoenix from the ashes

1. X

/ laɪk ə ˈfiː.nɪks frəm ði ˈæʃ.ɪz / (AmE), / laɪk ə ˈfiː.nɪks frəm ði ˈæʃ.ɪz / (BrE)
used to describe someone or something that restores itself or is renewed after experiencing destruction or great difficulty
reborn or renewed after destruction

Examples

After filing for bankruptcy, the company emerged like a phoenix from the ashes, stronger than ever.
She rebuilt her life like a phoenix from the ashes after the tragedy.
The town recovered from the devastating fire like a phoenix from the ashes.

Synset Examples

He returned to the sport like a phoenix from the ashes.
The city rebuilt itself like a phoenix from the ashes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rise from the ashes - to recover or emerge stronger from destruction or defeat

Etymology

From the mythical phoenix, a bird in ancient mythology that is reborn from its own ashes.
