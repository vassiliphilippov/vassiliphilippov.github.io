in jig time

1. ADV

/ ɪn dʒɪɡ taɪm / (AmE), / ɪn dʒɪɡ taɪm / (BrE)
very quickly; in a very short amount of time.
very quickly; promptly

Examples

She completed the assignment in jig time and surprised her teacher.
The chef prepared the meal in jig time for the hungry customers.
He organized the files in jig time, impressing his colleagues with his efficiency.

Synset Examples

The kids cleaned their rooms in jig time before dinner.
She solved the puzzle in jig time, impressing everyone.
We finished the project in jig time, ahead of schedule.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in no time - very quickly; without delay
  • Synonym: immediately - at once; without delay
  • Antonym: slowly - at a slow speed; not quickly

Etymology

From 'jig', a lively dance (originating from French 'gigue'), used figuratively to mean quick movement.
