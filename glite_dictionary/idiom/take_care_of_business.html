to take care of business

1. VERB

/ teɪk kɛr əv ˈbɪznəs / (AmE), / teɪk keə əv ˈbɪznəs / (BrE)
to attend to important matters or handle necessary tasks or responsibilities
attend to important tasks or responsibilities

Examples

Before leaving for vacation, John made sure to take care of business at the office.
Sometimes you have to wake up early to take care of business before the day gets hectic.
If you don't take care of business, your responsibilities will pile up quickly.

Synset Examples

It's time to take care of business and finalize the deal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handle - to deal with or manage a situation or task
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for something properly
