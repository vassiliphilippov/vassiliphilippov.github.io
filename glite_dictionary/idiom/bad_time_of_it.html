bad time of it

1. NOUN

/ bæd taɪm əv ɪt / (AmE), / bæd taɪm əv ɪt / (BrE)
to experience difficulty, discomfort, or suffering; to have a difficult or unpleasant time
to experience difficulty or discomfort

Examples

She had a bad time of it during the job interview and left feeling discouraged.
Many small businesses had a bad time of it during the recession.
Anyone without proper training is going to have a bad time of it in those conditions.

Synset Examples

If you don’t prepare, you’ll have a bad time of it.
They really had a bad time of it during the storm.
He tends to have a bad time of it when traveling alone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard time - a period of difficulty or suffering
