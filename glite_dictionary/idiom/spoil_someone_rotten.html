to spoil someone rotten

1. VERB

/ spɔɪl ˈsʌmwʌn ˈrɑtən / (AmE), / spɔɪl ˈsʌmwʌn ˈrɒtən / (BrE)
to treat someone with such excessive kindness, gifts, or indulgence that they become overindulged or demanding
indulge someone excessively

Examples

Grandparents tend to spoil their grandchildren rotten whenever they visit.
If you spoil your children rotten, they may grow up to be entitled.
He believes in strict discipline and refuses to spoil anyone rotten.

Synset Examples

She loves to spoil her kids rotten with gifts.
She spoiled that brat rotten with gifts.
Grandparents often spoil someone rotten during visits.
He tends to spoil someone rotten when they visit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pamper - to indulge with every attention, comfort, and kindness
  • Synonym: to indulge - to allow (someone) to enjoy a desired pleasure
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for someone properly
