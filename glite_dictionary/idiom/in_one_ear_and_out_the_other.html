in one ear and out the other

1. X

/ ɪn wʌn ɪr ənd aʊt ði ˈʌðɚ / (AmE), / ɪn wʌn ɪə ənd aʊt ði ˈʌðə / (BrE)
(of information) heard but immediately forgotten or disregarded; not retained in memory.
(of information) quickly forgotten or ignored

Examples

Whenever the teacher gives instructions, it goes in one ear and out the other with some students.
I told my brother to lock the door, but it seemed to go in one ear and out the other.
Don't let my suggestions go in one ear and out the other this time!

Synset Examples

His advice went in one ear and out the other.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to forget - to be unable to remember something
  • Antonym: to retain - to keep something in one's memory or possession
