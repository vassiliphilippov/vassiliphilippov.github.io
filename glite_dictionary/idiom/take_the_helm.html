to take the helm

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə hɛlm / (AmE), / teɪk ðə helm / (BrE)
to assume control or leadership of an organization, group, or project
assume control or leadership

Examples

After the founder stepped aside, Sarah was chosen to take the helm of the company.
The new coach is expected to take the helm and guide the team to victory.
When a crisis hits, someone must take the helm and make tough decisions.

Synset Examples

It's time for him to take the helm and lead the team.
After the CEO resigned, she was ready to take the helm.
It's time for someone new to take the helm.
He took the helm of the company last year.
They were thrilled to see her take the helm of the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take charge - assume responsibility or control
  • Antonym: to step down - resign from a position of leadership

Etymology

From nautical terminology, where 'helm' refers to the mechanism used to steer a ship.
