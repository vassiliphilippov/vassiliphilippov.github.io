to mince no words

1. VERB

/ mɪns noʊ wɝdz / (AmE), / mɪns nəʊ wɜːdz / (BrE)
to speak in a very direct and frank manner without softening the truth or being diplomatic
speak directly and frankly

Examples

She minced no words when criticizing the proposal, making her concerns abundantly clear.
The coach minced no words after the loss, telling the team exactly what went wrong.
He minces no words in his reviews, so his opinions are always honest.

Synset Examples

He minced no words about his disappointment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speak plainly - to say something directly or without hiding the truth
  • Antonym: beat about the bush - to avoid talking about what is important

Etymology

From the idiom 'not to mince words', where 'mince' means to moderate or soften one's words; 'mince' from Old French 'mincier', meaning 'to cut into small pieces'.
