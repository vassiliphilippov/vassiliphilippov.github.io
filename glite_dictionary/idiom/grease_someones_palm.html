to grease someone's palm

1. VERB

/ ɡriːs ˈsʌmwʌnz pɑːm / (AmE), / ɡriːs ˈsʌmwʌnz pɑːm / (BrE)
to secretly give money to someone, especially an official, in order to persuade them to do something for you; to bribe
to bribe someone for a favor

Examples

Rumors spread that the construction manager would grease the inspector's palm to pass the project.
Some officials are honest, but others expect you to grease their palm before processing paperwork.
She refused to grease anyone's palm, even though it would have sped up the approval.

Synset Examples

It's illegal to grease someone's palm, but some still do.
They thought greasing her palm would guarantee a better deal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bribe - to give someone money or a gift to persuade them to do something, usually dishonest

Etymology

From the idea of making the 'palm' (hand) slippery with money, first recorded in the 16th century.
