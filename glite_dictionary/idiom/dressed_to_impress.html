dressed to impress

1. ADJ

/ drɛst tə ɪmˈprɛs / (AmE), / drɛst tʊ ɪmˈprɛs / (BrE)
wearing clothing that is intended to attract attention or make a favorable impression on others
wearing attractive, attention-getting clothing

Examples

Whenever she goes out in the evening, she's always dressed to impress.
He arrived at the office party dressed to impress, hoping to make a good impression on his colleagues.
At the awards ceremony, every celebrity was dressed to impress on the red carpet.

Synset Examples

She’s a style-hon, always dressed to impress.
She felt like Cinderella at the ball, dressed to impress.
That Ah Huay always dresses to impress at parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dressed to kill - wearing very stylish or glamorous clothing to attract attention
