to the marrow

1. ADV

/ tə ðə ˈmæroʊ / (AmE), / tə ðə ˈmærəʊ / (BrE)
(of a feeling or quality) to the deepest or most profound degree; extremely or intensely.
very deeply; to the deepest degree

Examples

His loyalty was unwavering to the marrow.
She was chilled to the marrow by the icy wind.
The movie moved him to the marrow.

Synset Examples

The story touched her heart to the marrow.
He believed in his values to the marrow.
She felt the pain to the marrow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to the core - to the deepest or most essential part; thoroughly

Etymology

From the literal marrow inside bones, used figuratively to express depth or extremity.
