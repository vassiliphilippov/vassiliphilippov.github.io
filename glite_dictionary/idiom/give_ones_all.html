to give one's all

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv wʌnz ɔl / (AmE), / ɡɪv wʌnz ɔːl / (BrE)
to put in as much effort, energy, or commitment as possible; to do one's utmost
put in maximum effort

Examples

She always gives her all at work, no matter how difficult the project is.
During the charity marathon, he gave his all and finished in the top ten.
The team gave their all in the finals, impressing everyone with their dedication.

Synset Examples

He always gives his all during soccer matches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to do one's best - to make the greatest possible effort
  • Antonym: to hold back - to not give full effort or energy
