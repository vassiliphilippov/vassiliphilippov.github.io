to head for the hills

1. VERB

/ hɛd fəɹ ðə hɪlz / (AmE), / hɛd fə ðə hɪlz / (BrE)
to run away quickly to escape danger or trouble
run away to escape danger or trouble

Examples

As soon as the alarm sounded, everyone headed for the hills to avoid being caught.
When they saw the approaching storm, people living near the coast headed for the hills.
After the scandal broke, the politician headed for the hills to escape the media.

Synset Examples

If you see this man, run!
The burglars escaped before the police showed up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flee - to run away from danger or unpleasant situation
  • Synonym: escape - to break free from confinement or control
  • Antonym: to stand your ground - refuse to run or back down in the face of danger

2. VERB

/ hɛd fər ðə hɪlz / (AmE), / hɛd fə ðə hɪlz / (BrE)
to leave quickly in order to avoid danger or trouble; to flee or escape suddenly
escape or flee quickly

Examples

When they heard the alarm, everyone decided to head for the hills immediately.
If the business fails, investors may head for the hills to protect themselves.
When the scandal broke, most of the executives tried to head for the hills.

Synset Examples

When the storm hit, we decided to head for the hills.
They saw trouble brewing and chose to head for the hills.
After the argument, she just wanted to head for the hills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to run away - to leave a place or situation quickly to escape danger or trouble
  • Antonym: to stay put - to remain in one place; to not flee or escape

Etymology

Refers to literally escaping danger by running to the hills; figuratively used since the early 20th century in American English.
