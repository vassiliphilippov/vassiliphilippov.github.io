believe you me

1. INTJ

/ bəˈliv ju ˈmi / (AmE), / bɪˈliːv juː ˈmiː / (BrE)
Used to emphasize that what you are saying is true, especially when you know from your own experience.
used for emphasis, meaning 'trust me'
informal

Examples

Believe you me, there’s no better way to solve this problem.
She will get the job done, believe you me.
Believe you me, traveling with kids is never as easy as it sounds.

Synset Examples

Believe you me, she knows what she's talking about.
Believe you me, this recipe will change your life.
If you think it’s easy, believe you me, it’s not.
