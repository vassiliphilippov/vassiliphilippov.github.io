for the ages

1. X

/ fɔr ðə ˈeɪdʒɪz / (AmE), / fɔː ðə ˈeɪdʒɪz / (BrE)
memorable and expected to endure or be celebrated for a long time
memorable; enduring through time

Examples

Many consider Michael Jordan's final game-winning shot to be a moment for the ages.
The novelist hoped to write a book for the ages, one that would outlive her.
Their wedding was a celebration for the ages, with stories still retold years later.

Synset Examples

Her artwork will be remembered for the ages.
This film is a classic, a story for the ages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timeless - not affected by the passage of time; eternal
  • Antonym: ephemeral - lasting for a very short time
