to stem the tide

1. VERB

/ stɛm ðə taɪd / (AmE), / stɛm ðə taɪd / (BrE)
to stop or slow the increase or worsening of something, usually a negative trend or problem
to stop or slow a worsening trend

Examples

The new regulations are intended to stem the tide of illegal fishing in coastal waters.
Innovative policies are needed to stem the tide of homelessness in urban areas.
The campaign was launched to stem the tide of plastic waste in the oceans.

Synset Examples

We need to stem the tide of misinformation online.
Efforts to stem the tide of pollution are ongoing.
They are trying to stem the tide of rising costs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hold back - to prevent the progress or development of something
  • Synonym: curb - to control or limit something, especially something undesirable
