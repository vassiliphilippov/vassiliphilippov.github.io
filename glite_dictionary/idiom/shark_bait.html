shark bait

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑrk beɪt / (AmE), / ʃɑːk beɪt / (BrE)
a person who is easily exploited or mistreated, especially in a competitive or aggressive environment
someone easily exploited

Examples

The new intern felt like shark bait during his first week at the company.
Without experience or strong connections, she became shark bait in the cutthroat industry.
If you don't prepare thoroughly, you'll just be shark bait for your competitors.

Synset Examples

The new recruits were treated like shark bait by the veterans.
He felt like shark bait in the competitive job market.
In the business world, inexperienced employees are often seen as shark bait.
