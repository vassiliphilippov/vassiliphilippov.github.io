jot and tittle

1. NOUN

/ dʒɑt ənd ˈtɪt̬l̩ / (AmE), / dʒɒt ənd ˈtɪt.əl / (BrE)
every small detail or the smallest part of something, typically used to emphasize the importance of being thorough or accurate
every minor detail, smallest part

Examples

The lawyer went over the agreement, making sure not a jot and tittle was overlooked.
She insisted that every jot and tittle of the instructions be followed exactly.
His attention to jot and tittle details made him an excellent accountant.

Synset Examples

The editor reviewed the manuscript for jot and tittle errors.
He checked the contract for every jot and tittle.
Make sure to include every jot and tittle in your report.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detail - a small part of something; a minor aspect

Etymology

From 'jot', meaning a very small amount (from Latin 'iota'), and 'tittle', a small mark or point in writing (from Latin 'titulus', inscription).
