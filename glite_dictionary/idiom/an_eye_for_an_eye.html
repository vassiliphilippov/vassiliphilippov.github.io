an eye for an eye

1. NOUN

/ ən aɪ fər ən aɪ / (AmE), / ən aɪ fə ən aɪ / (BrE)
the idea that retaliation or punishment should be equal in kind and degree to the original offense
equal retaliation; tit-for-tat justice

Examples

Some laws are based on the principle of an eye for an eye.
He took an eye for an eye approach to settling disputes.
Many people argue that an eye for an eye only leads to more conflict.

Synset Examples

Her philosophy is an eye for an eye, no exceptions.
They settled their differences with an eye for an eye.
He believes in an eye for an eye justice.
Their feud turned into an eye for an eye, a tooth for a tooth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tit for tat - equivalent retaliation; a similar response to an action

Etymology

From the Old Testament phrase 'an eye for an eye, a tooth for a tooth' (Exodus 21:24), expressing a principle of retributive justice.
