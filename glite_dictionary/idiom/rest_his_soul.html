rest his soul

1. X

/ rɛst hɪz soʊl / (AmE), / rɛst hɪz səʊl / (BrE)
(expressing a wish) used to show respect for a man who has died, often as a parenthetical remark after mentioning him.
used to show respect for a deceased man

Examples

My father, rest his soul, taught me everything I know about gardening.
She would always mention her late husband, rest his soul, when telling family stories.
He was a kind man, rest his soul, and is still missed by many.

Synset Examples

In difficult times, she finds comfort in knowing he'll rest his soul.
He was a great man, may he rest his soul.
She often spoke of her grandfather, rest his soul.
After all these years, I still miss him, rest his soul.
He often said he would rest his soul when his work was done.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rest her soul - used to show respect for a woman who has died
