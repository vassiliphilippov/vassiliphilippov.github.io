to bite one's tongue

1. VERB

/ baɪt wʌnz tʌŋ / (AmE), / baɪt wʌnz tʌŋ / (BrE)
to stop yourself from saying something that you want to say, usually because it would be unwise or impolite
refrain from speaking one's mind

Examples

During the tense meeting, I had to bite my tongue and keep my opinions to myself.
She desperately wanted to correct him, but decided to bite her tongue instead.
It's often wise to bite your tongue rather than speak out in anger.

Synset Examples

I had to bite my tongue during the argument.
He bit his tongue to avoid starting an argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hold one's tongue - to remain silent or not to speak one's mind
