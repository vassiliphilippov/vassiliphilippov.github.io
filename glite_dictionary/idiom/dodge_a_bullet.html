to dodge a bullet

1. VERB

/ ˈdɑdʒ ə ˈbʊlɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɒdʒ ə ˈbʊlɪt / (BrE)
to narrowly avoid a dangerous, unpleasant, or difficult situation
narrowly avoid danger or trouble

Examples

She felt like she dodged a bullet when the job offer fell through.
I really dodged a bullet by not investing in that company.
We dodged a bullet when the storm changed direction at the last minute.

Synset Examples
