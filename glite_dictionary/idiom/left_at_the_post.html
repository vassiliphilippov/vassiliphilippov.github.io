to left at the post

1. VERB

/ lɛft ət ðə poʊst / (AmE), / lɛft ət ðə pəʊst / (BrE)
to be left behind or neglected, especially at the start of something; to fail to keep up with others from the outset
to be left behind or neglected

Examples

If you don't act quickly in this business, you may be left at the post by competitors.
During the reorganization, several employees felt they had been left at the post.
The new technology advanced so fast that older companies were left at the post.

Synset Examples

If you don’t hurry, you’ll be left at the post.
He felt he would be left at the post in the race.
Don’t let anyone be left at the post during the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fall behind - to fail to keep up with others
  • Antonym: to keep up - to continue at the same level or pace as others

Etymology

From horse racing, where a horse fails to leave the starting post as the race begins.
