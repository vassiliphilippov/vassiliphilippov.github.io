to play hardball

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ˈhɑrdˌbɔl / (AmE), / pleɪ ˈhɑːdˌbɔːl / (BrE)
to act aggressively or ruthlessly, especially in order to achieve one's goals in a competitive context
to act aggressively to achieve goals

Examples

If they refuse to compromise, we may have to play hardball to get what we want.
The company isn't afraid to play hardball during contract negotiations.
Sometimes you need to play hardball with tough competitors to protect your interests.

Synset Examples

She decided to play hardball with the competition.
In negotiations, he is known to play hardball.
We need to play hardball to win this deal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to negotiate - to try to reach an agreement through discussion
  • Antonym: play soft - to act cautiously or without aggression

Etymology

Originally from American baseball, where 'hardball' refers to the standard game played with a hard baseball, as opposed to softball; figuratively extended to mean tough or uncompromising tactics.
