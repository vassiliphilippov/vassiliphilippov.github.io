to pour cold water on

1. VERB

/ pɔr koʊld ˈwɔtɚ ɑn / (AmE), / pɔː kəʊld ˈwɔːtə ɒn / (BrE)
to express doubt or disapproval about an idea, plan, or enthusiasm, thereby discouraging others
discourage or express disapproval

Examples

The manager poured cold water on our proposal, saying it was unrealistic.
Sara was excited about her new idea, but Tom quickly poured cold water on it.
It's discouraging when people pour cold water on your dreams instead of supporting them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to discourage - to make someone less confident or enthusiastic
  • Antonym: support - to give approval or encouragement to something

2. VERB

/ pɔr koʊld ˈwɔt̬ɚ ɑn / (AmE), / pɔː kəʊld ˈwɔːtə ɒn / (BrE)
to discourage or criticize something, especially a plan or idea, making people feel less enthusiastic about it
discourage or criticize (an idea, plan, etc.)

Examples

Every time we mention a new idea, he pours cold water on it.
She didn't want to pour cold water on their excitement, but the plan seemed unworkable.
The critics poured cold water on the film before it was even released.

Synset Examples

It's easy to pour cold water on others' ideas.
Don’t pour cold water on my plans before I even start!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to discourage - to cause someone to lose confidence or enthusiasm
  • Synonym: to criticize - to find fault with, express disapproval of
