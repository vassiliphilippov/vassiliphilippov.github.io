to knock the living daylights out of

1. VERB

/ nɑk ðə ˈlɪvɪŋ ˈdeɪˌlaɪts ˈaʊt əv / (AmE), / nɒk ðə ˈlɪvɪŋ ˈdeɪlaɪts ˈaʊt ɒv / (BrE)
to beat someone very severely or to shock or frighten someone extremely
beat or shock/frighten someone severely

Examples

If you say that again, I'll knock the living daylights out of you.
The sudden explosion knocked the living daylights out of everyone nearby.
She was so scared that it nearly knocked the living daylights out of her.

Synset Examples

He threatened to knock the living daylights out of the bully.
That movie was so scary, it nearly knocked the living daylights out of me.
The storm knocked the living daylights out of the old house.

Etymology

The phrase "living daylights" originally referred to a person's eyes or vital senses; "knock the living daylights out of" evolved in the 19th century as a figurative way to say "beat senseless."
