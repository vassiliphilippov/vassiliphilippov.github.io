to go on one's merry way

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ɑn wʌnz ˈmɛri weɪ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɒn wʌnz ˈmɛri weɪ / (BrE)
to continue on one's path or activity happily and without concern for possible problems or consequences.
continue cheerfully without concern

Examples

After the presentation, she went on her merry way without a second thought.
He fixed the issue, thanked everyone, and went on his merry way.
Ignoring the warnings, they simply went on their merry way through the park.

Synset Examples

After the meeting, she went on her merry way.
He finished his task and went on his merry way.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go on one's way - to continue on one's path or course
