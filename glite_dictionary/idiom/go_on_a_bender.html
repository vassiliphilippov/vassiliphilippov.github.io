to go on a bender

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ɑn ə ˈbɛndɚ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɒn ə ˈbɛndə / (BrE)
to engage in a period of excessive drinking, often lasting several days
engage in heavy drinking over a period

Examples

After losing his job, Tom decided to go on a bender for the entire weekend.
She worried her brother might go on a bender after hearing the bad news.
Many celebrities have been known to go on a bender following public scandals.

Synset Examples

He went on a bender after the breakup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bender - a period of heavy drinking

Etymology

Possibly from 'bender' (drinking spree), 19th-century British slang.
