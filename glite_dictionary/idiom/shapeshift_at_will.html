to shapeshift at will

1. VERB

/ ˈʃeɪpˌʃɪft æt wɪl / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪpʃɪft ət wɪl / (BrE)
to change one's form or appearance freely whenever one wishes
change form freely at any time

Examples

According to legend, the wizard could shapeshift at will into any animal he chose.
In the story, the villain uses her power to shapeshift at will and deceive her enemies.
Many mythical creatures are said to shapeshift at will to escape danger or hunt more effectively.

Synset Examples

Certain cultures believe gods can shapeshift at will.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to transform - to change in form, appearance, or structure
  • Synonym: to morph - to change smoothly from one image to another
