to sleep at night

1. VERB

/ sliːp æt naɪt / (AmE), / sliːp æt naɪt / (BrE)
to be able to live with one's actions or decisions without feeling guilty or troubled in conscience
to feel at peace with one's actions

Examples

If telling the truth helps you sleep at night, then you should do it.
He wondered how his boss could sleep at night after making such a decision.
Many people cheat, but I want to sleep at night knowing I did the right thing.

Synset Examples

She told him to do whatever helps you sleep at night.
If lying helps you sleep at night, do it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: have a clear conscience - to feel free from guilt about one's actions
