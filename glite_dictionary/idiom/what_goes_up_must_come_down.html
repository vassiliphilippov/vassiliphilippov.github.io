what goes up must come down

1. X

/ wʌt ɡoʊz ʌp mʌst kʌm daʊn / (AmE), / wɒt ɡəʊz ʌp mʌst kʌm daʊn / (BrE)
used to express the idea that things which rise or increase, especially prices or fortunes, will eventually fall or decrease
proverb about rises eventually being followed by falls

Examples

When investing, remember that what goes up must come down sooner or later.
After months of booming sales, the CEO reminded everyone that what goes up must come down.
The dramatic rise in housing prices proved that what goes up must come down when the market eventually crashed.

Synset Examples

In this economy, what goes up must come down.
Prices have risen, but remember, what goes up must come down.
The stock market is volatile; what goes up must come down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: easy come, easy go - things gained easily are often lost just as easily
