old as the hills

1. ADJ

/ oʊld æz ðə hɪlz / (AmE), / əʊld æz ðə hɪlz / (BrE)
very old; having existed for a very long time
very old; ancient

Examples

Some of his stories are old as the hills, but he tells them well.
The equipment in that laboratory looks old as the hills and needs to be updated.
That superstition is old as the hills and still influences many people's behavior.

Synset Examples

Her wisdom is old as the hills, passed down through generations.
That joke is old as the hills but still funny.
The tradition is old as the hills in this town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ancient - very old; from a long time ago
  • Antonym: brand new - completely new; just made or acquired
