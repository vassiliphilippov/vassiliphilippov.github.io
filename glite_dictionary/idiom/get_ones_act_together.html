to get one's act together

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt wʌnz ækt təˈɡɛðər / (AmE), / ɡɛt wʌnz ækt təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to organize oneself effectively or improve one's behavior or performance, especially in order to achieve a goal or meet expectations
organize oneself; improve performance
informal

Examples

After missing several deadlines, I knew I had to get my act together if I wanted to keep my job.
It took her a while, but she managed to get her act together before the big presentation.
The team really needs to get its act together if they hope to win the championship.

Synset Examples

He really needs to get his act together for the exam.
I had to get my act together before the deadline.
If she wants to succeed, she must get her act together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pull yourself together - regain control of one's emotions or behavior
