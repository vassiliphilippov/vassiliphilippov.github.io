on the face of it

1. ADV

/ ɑn ðə feɪs əv ɪt / (AmE), / ɒn ðə feɪs əv ɪt / (BrE)
judging by appearances only; based on what is immediately obvious, without further investigation
judging by appearances only

Examples

On the face of it, the proposal looks like a good idea.
On the face of it, he seemed qualified for the job, but his references raised concerns.
The plan appears simple on the face of it, but it's actually quite complex.

Synset Examples

irrigation often produces bumper crops from apparently desert land
the child is seemingly healthy but the doctor is concerned
had been ostensibly frank as to his purpose while really concealing it
on the face of it the problem seems minor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apparently - as far as one knows or can see
  • Synonym: seemingly - so as to appear to be true
