to circle the wagons

1. VERB

/ ˈsɝːkəl ðə ˈwæɡənz / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkl ðə ˈwæɡənz / (BrE)
to unite in defense or protection against outside criticism or threats
unite for defense or protection

Examples

The company tried to circle the wagons when negative media coverage threatened its reputation.
When accusations started flying, the politicians quickly circled the wagons to protect their party.
As criticism mounted, the family decided to circle the wagons and support each other.

Synset Examples

In tough times, it's important to circle the wagons.
When criticism arose, the team decided to circle the wagons.
They circled the wagons to protect their interests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rally round - to come together to support or protect someone or something
  • Synonym: to close ranks - to unite solidly against outside opposition

Etymology

From the practice of arranging wagons in a circle as a defensive position by American pioneers in the 19th century.
