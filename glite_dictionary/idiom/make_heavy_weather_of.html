to make heavy weather of

1. VERB

/ meɪk ˈhɛvi ˈwɛðɚ ʌv / (AmE), / meɪk ˈhɛvi ˈwɛðə ɒv / (BrE)
to overcomplicate or exaggerate the difficulty of a situation, often making it seem more troublesome than it is
exaggerate or complicate a situation

Examples

She tends to make heavy weather of even the simplest tasks at work.
Let’s not make heavy weather of this project—it really won’t take long.
You’re making heavy weather of the presentation; try to relax and keep it simple.

Synset Examples

We don't need to make heavy weather of this issue; it’s straightforward.
He always makes heavy weather out of minor problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overcomplicate - to make something more complicated than needed
  • Synonym: to exaggerate - to represent something as greater than it is

Etymology

Originally a nautical term referring to a ship struggling in bad weather; later extended metaphorically to general difficulties.
