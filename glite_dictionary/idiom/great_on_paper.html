great on paper

1. ADJ

/ ɡreɪt ɑn ˈpeɪpər / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ɒn ˈpeɪpə / (BrE)
appearing excellent in theory or according to plans, but possibly not effective or successful in practice
good in theory, but not necessarily in practice

Examples

The new design seems great on paper, but it might be too expensive to build.
Their proposal looked great on paper, yet it failed during real-world testing.
The contract is great on paper, but the details need to be checked carefully.

Synset Examples

The plan looks great on paper but needs work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in theory - as something is supposed to be, not necessarily as it is in reality
