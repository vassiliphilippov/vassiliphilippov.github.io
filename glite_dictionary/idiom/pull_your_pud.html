to pull your pud

1. VERB

/ pʊl jʊr pʌd / (AmE), / pʊl jɔː pʌd / (BrE)
(slang) To waste time or avoid productivity by being idle or lazy.
be idle or lazy (slang)
informal slang

Examples

Stop pulling your pud and help me with these boxes.
If you keep pulling your pud at work, you'll get in trouble with the boss.
He spent half the meeting just pulling his pud instead of contributing.

Synset Examples

Don't just pull your pud; get something done!
He was caught pulling his pud instead of helping out.
He spent the afternoon pulling his pud instead of working.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to slack off - to be lazy or avoid work
  • Synonym: idle - to spend time doing nothing
