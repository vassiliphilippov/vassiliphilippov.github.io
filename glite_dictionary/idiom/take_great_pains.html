to take great pains

1. VERB

/ teɪk ɡreɪt peɪnz / (AmE), / teɪk ɡreɪt peɪnz / (BrE)
to make a lot of effort or show great care and attention to detail when doing something
make a lot of effort or show great care

Examples

She always takes great pains to ensure her reports are accurate and thorough.
The artist took great pains in selecting the perfect colors for the painting.
He is taking great pains to plan a memorable birthday party for his friend.

Synset Examples

He takes great pains when cooking to impress his guests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go to great lengths - to make a great or strenuous effort to achieve something
  • Antonym: to cut corners - to do something quickly and carelessly to save effort or cost
