stick for your own back

1. X

/ stɪk fər jɔːɹ oʊn bæk / (AmE), / stɪk fə jɔːr əʊn bæk / (BrE)
To do something that causes future problems or difficulties for oneself.
cause trouble for oneself
british

Examples

By taking on too much work, she made a stick for her own back.
If you keep lying, you'll only create a stick for your own back.
He doesn't realize he's making a stick for his own back by not asking for help.

Synset Examples

By procrastinating, she made a stick for her own back.
He made a stick for his own back by ignoring the warnings.
Don't make a stick for your own back with reckless decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dig your own grave - to do something that will cause one’s own failure or downfall

Etymology

Refers to the idea of making a stick with which one could later be beaten—i.e., creating one's own punishment. Common in British English.
