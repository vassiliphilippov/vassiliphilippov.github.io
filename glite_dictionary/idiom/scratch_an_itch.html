to scratch an itch

1. VERB

/ skrætʃ ən ɪtʃ / (AmE), / skrætʃ ən ɪtʃ / (BrE)
to satisfy a desire or craving for something, especially one that persists or is hard to ignore
fulfill a persistent desire or craving

Examples

She finally decided to scratch an itch for adventure by joining a hiking club.
Whenever I feel restless, a new project helps me scratch an itch for creativity.
He took up gardening to scratch an itch he couldn't ignore any longer.

Synset Examples

He decided to scratch an itch for travel by booking a last-minute flight.
Reading helps me scratch an itch for knowledge.
