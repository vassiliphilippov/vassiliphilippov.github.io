to leave competitors in the dust

1. VERB

/ liv kəmˈpɛt̬ɪtɚz ɪn ðə dʌst / (AmE), / liːv kəmˈpɛtɪtəz ɪn ðə dʌst / (BrE)
to greatly surpass or outperform competitors, leaving them far behind
greatly outperform competitors

Examples

The new startup managed to leave competitors in the dust with its innovative platform.
By reducing costs and improving service, the company hopes to leave competitors in the dust.
With their record-breaking sales, they have left competitors in the dust this year.

Synset Examples

The new model is so fast, it leaves competitors in the dust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outperform - to do better than someone else
  • Antonym: lag behind - to fall behind others in progress or achievement
