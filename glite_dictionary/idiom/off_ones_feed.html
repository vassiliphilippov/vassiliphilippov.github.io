off one's feed

1. ADJ

/ ɔf wʌnz fid / (AmE), / ɒf wʌnz fiːd / (BrE)
not feeling well or having lost one's appetite; lacking usual interest or energy, especially due to illness or sadness
lacking appetite due to not feeling well
informal

Examples

Ever since he caught that cold, he's been off his feed and doesn't want dinner.
After hearing the sad news, she was clearly off her feed and quiet all day.
If your dog seems off its feed, you should probably call the vet.

Synset Examples

He looked off his feed after the bad news.
She felt off her feed and skipped the gym.
You seem off your feed; are you feeling okay?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lose one's appetite - to no longer feel hungry or interested in eating

Etymology

Originally from livestock terminology, where animals refusing to eat were said to be 'off their feed.'
