light on the hill

1. NOUN

/ laɪt ɑn ðə hɪl / (AmE), / laɪt ɒn ðə hɪl / (BrE)
a person, organization, or example that serves as a source of hope, inspiration, or moral guidance, often held up as an ideal for others to follow
source of hope or inspiration

Examples

She always thought of her late grandmother as a light on the hill guiding her in tough times.
The school aims to be a light on the hill for the entire community, setting an example for others.
In his speech, the leader called upon his country to become a light on the hill for the world.

Synset Examples

She saw her family as a light on the hill guiding her.
They viewed their charity work as a light on the hill.
The church was a light on the hill for the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beacon - a light or fire that acts as a warning signal or guide
  • Synonym: inspiration - a person or thing that motivates or encourages

Etymology

Biblical allusion from Matthew 5:14, "a city set on a hill cannot be hidden," popularized in political and religious contexts.
