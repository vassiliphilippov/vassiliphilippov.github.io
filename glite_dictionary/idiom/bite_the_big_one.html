to bite the big one

1. VERB

/ baɪt ðə bɪɡ wʌn / (AmE), / baɪt ðə bɪɡ wʌn / (BrE)
to experience a significant failure or suffer a serious setback or consequence
to suffer a big failure or setback
informal

Examples

If we don't deliver this project on time, the whole team might bite the big one.
After missing the deadline, the company really bit the big one and lost a major contract.
Don't let nerves get to you during the interview, or you could bite the big one.

Synset Examples

Failing that exam means I'll really bite the big one.
If we lose this client, our business will bite the big one.
When he crashed his car, he knew he would bite the big one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fail - to be unsuccessful in achieving a goal
  • Antonym: to succeed - to achieve the desired aim or result
