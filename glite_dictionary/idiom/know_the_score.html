to know the score

1. VERB

/ noʊ ðə skɔr / (AmE), / nəʊ ðə skɔː / (BrE)
to be well-informed about the real situation or the essential facts, especially when they are unpleasant or not widely known
be well-informed about the real situation

Examples

Everyone knows the score at this company—if you miss a deadline, you're in trouble.
She pretended she didn't know the score, but I could tell she understood everything.
Before you make any decisions, make sure you know the score about what's really going on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be in the know - to be aware of information that is not generally known

2. VERB

/ noʊ ðə skɔr / (AmE), / nəʊ ðə skɔː / (BrE)
to understand the real situation, especially when it is not immediately obvious or pleasant
understand the real situation

Examples

She may pretend not to notice, but she definitely knows the score.
If you work here long enough, you soon know the score about office politics.
Before you get involved, you should know the score so you're not caught off guard.

Synset Examples

She acts surprised, but we all know the score.
You think I'm naive? Trust me, I know the score.
After all these years, I know the score about his intentions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be in the know - be aware of the facts or have information about something
  • Antonym: be clueless - lack understanding or knowledge about a situation
