to show the door

1. VERB

/ ʃoʊ ðə dɔr / (AmE), / ʃəʊ ðiː dɔː / (BrE)
to tell someone to leave a place, especially in a firm or dismissive manner
ask someone to leave

Examples

After the heated argument, the manager showed the door to the disruptive customer.
He was shown the door after repeatedly breaking the company's rules.
If you don't pay your rent, the landlord will show you the door.

Synset Examples

I was shown the door when I asked for a raise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to kick out - to force someone to leave a place
  • Antonym: invite in - to allow or request someone to enter a place
