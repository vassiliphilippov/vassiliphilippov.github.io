to keep your head on a swivel

1. VERB

/ kip jɚ hɛd ɑn ə ˈswɪvəl / (AmE), / kiːp jɔː hɛd ɒn ə ˈswɪvəl / (BrE)
to remain extremely alert and aware of what is happening around you, especially in situations requiring vigilance
remain highly alert and watchful

Examples

In unfamiliar neighborhoods, it's wise to keep your head on a swivel to avoid potential danger.
Reporters covering protests are taught to keep their heads on a swivel for sudden unrest.
Security guards are trained to keep their head on a swivel during busy events.

Synset Examples

While driving in the city, always keep your head on a swivel.
During the hike, it's important to keep your head on a swivel for wildlife.
In a crowded market, you must keep your head on a swivel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stay alert - to keep attentive and ready
  • Antonym: let your guard down - to become less alert or cautious
