on the outside, looking in

1. X

/ ɑn ði ˈaʊtsaɪd ˈlʊkɪŋ ɪn / (AmE), / ɒn ði ˈaʊtsaɪd ˈlʊkɪŋ ɪn / (BrE)
feeling excluded from a group or situation; not being part of something that others are involved in
feeling excluded; not part of a group

Examples

For years, he felt on the outside, looking in at his colleagues' close friendships.
After moving to a new city, she was on the outside, looking in during social events.
Sometimes, being on the outside, looking in makes you appreciate genuine connections even more.

Synset Examples

Being on the outside, looking in can be lonely.
She was on the outside, looking in at the family gathering.
I felt on the outside, looking in at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outsider - a person who does not belong to a group
