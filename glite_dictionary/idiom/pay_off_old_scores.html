to pay off old scores

1. VERB

/ peɪ ɑf oʊld skɔrz / (AmE), / peɪ ɒf əʊld skɔːz / (BrE)
to settle past disputes or grievances, often by seeking retribution or closure
settle past grievances or disputes

Examples

After years of waiting, he finally got the chance to pay off old scores with his former business partner.
Many believed the government was trying to pay off old scores under the guise of reform.
Instead of dwelling on the past, she decided not to pay off old scores and moved forward with her life.

Synset Examples

It's time to pay off old scores and move on.
He decided to pay off old scores and forgive his enemies.
In the end, she chose to pay off old scores rather than seek revenge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: settle the score - to take revenge or get even for a past wrong
