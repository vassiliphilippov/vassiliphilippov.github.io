to rest my case

1. VERB

/ rɛst maɪ keɪs / (AmE), / rɛst maɪ keɪs / (BrE)
to indicate that one has finished presenting evidence or arguments, especially when they believe their point has been proven
to conclude one's argument or evidence

Examples

After presenting all the evidence to the jury, the attorney said, 'rest my case.'
Having shown the proof to her friends, she simply declared, 'rest my case.'
Once he made his final point in the debate, he concluded, 'rest my case.''

Synset Examples

The lawyer confidently stated, 'I rest my case.'
After showing the evidence, she confidently stated, 'I rest my case.'
With that final point, he concluded, 'I rest my case.'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finish - to bring something to an end
  • Synonym: to withdraw - to cease pursuing an argument or case
