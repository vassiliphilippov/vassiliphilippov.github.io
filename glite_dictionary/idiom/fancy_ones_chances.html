to fancy one's chances

1. VERB

/ ˈfænsi wʌnz ˈtʃænsɪz / (AmE), / ˈfænsi wʌnz ˈtʃɑːnsɪz / (BrE)
to believe that one is likely to be successful in a particular situation
believe one will succeed
british

Examples

I wouldn't fancy my chances against such a strong opponent.
Do you fancy your chances of getting the job this time?
He really fancies his chances in the upcoming election.

Synset Examples

I wouldn't fancy my chances in that competition.
She didn't fancy her chances of winning.
He fancied his chances against the experienced player.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: think one's chances are good - to believe that one is likely to succeed
  • Antonym: doubt one's chances - to believe that one is unlikely to succeed
