to find one's voice

1. VERB

/ faɪnd wʌnz vɔɪs / (AmE), / faɪnd wʌnz vɔɪs / (BrE)
to begin expressing one's own opinions clearly or to discover personal style or confidence, especially in speech, writing, or artistic expression
to start expressing personal opinions or confidence

Examples

After years of self-doubt, Maria finally began to find her voice as a writer.
New editors often struggle before they find their voice in the newsroom.
The artist needed time to find his voice in the competitive art world.

Synset Examples

She needed time to find her voice in the debate.
After practicing, he began to find his voice as a singer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to find oneself - to become aware of one's true character or potential
