to punch above one's weight

1. VERB

/ pʌntʃ əˈbʌv wʌnz weɪt / (AmE), / pʌntʃ əˈbʌv wʌnz weɪt / (BrE)
to achieve success or compete at a level higher than would be expected based on one's resources, ability, or experience
compete or succeed beyond one's expected ability

Examples

The small tech company continued to punch above its weight by winning contracts against much larger rivals.
Despite limited experience, she managed to punch above her weight in the debating competition.
As a new player in the market, they really had to punch above their weight to attract investors.

Synset Examples

She tends to punch above her weight in competitions.
He really knows how to punch above his weight in negotiations.
They are trying to punch above their weight in this project.

Etymology

Originally a term from boxing, where fighters compete within designated weight classes.
