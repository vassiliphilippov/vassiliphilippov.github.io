to make a fist of

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə fɪst əv / (AmE), / meɪk ə fɪst ɒv / (BrE)
to try to do something, especially something difficult, often with some degree of success or effort
attempt or manage something difficult

Examples

Despite his lack of experience, he managed to make a fist of running the department.
She really tried to make a fist of the job, even though it was beyond her training.
If you don’t study, you’ll never make a fist of your exams.

Synset Examples

He tried to make a fist of the project alone.
He couldn’t make a fist of the new software.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make a go of - to attempt something successfully
  • Antonym: make a mess of - to do something badly or unsuccessfully
