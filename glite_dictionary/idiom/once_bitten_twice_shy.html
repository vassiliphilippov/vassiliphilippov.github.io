once bitten, twice shy

1. ADJ

/ wʌns ˈbɪtn̩ ˈtwaɪs ʃaɪ / (AmE), / wʌns ˈbɪtən ˈtwaɪs ʃaɪ / (BrE)
used to say that someone is cautious or wary because of a previous bad experience
cautious after a bad experience

Examples

After losing money in the stock market, he became once bitten, twice shy with his investments.
She refused to adopt another pet so quickly—once bitten, twice shy.
The candidate avoided making promises he couldn't keep, remembering that once bitten, twice shy.

Synset Examples

After that experience, I'm once bitten, twice shy.
She was once bitten, twice shy about dating again.
He's careful now; once bitten, twice shy.
