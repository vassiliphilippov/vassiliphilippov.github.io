to fly in the teeth of

1. VERB

/ flaɪ ɪn ðə tiθ ʌv / (AmE), / flaɪ ɪn ðə tiːθ ɒv / (BrE)
to openly go against or defy something, especially accepted rules, advice, or authority
to go openly against or defy

Examples

Her decision to speak out flew in the teeth of the company's official policy.
He always seems to fly in the teeth of conventional wisdom, regardless of the consequences.
By questioning the tradition, they were flying in the teeth of centuries of established beliefs.

Synset Examples

This action flies in the face of the agreement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fly in the face of - to act in direct defiance of something
