to come to oneself

1. VERB

/ kʌm tə wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / kʌm tʊ wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to recover consciousness or composure after being shocked, confused, or fainting
regain consciousness or composure

Examples

After fainting in the heat, it took several minutes for Jill to come to herself.
He sat quietly, trying to come to himself after hearing the startling news.
She needed a moment to come to herself before she could speak.

Synset Examples

He needed time to come to himself after the news.
She took a moment to come to herself after the shock.
It took a while for her to come to herself after fainting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regain consciousness - become conscious again after fainting or being unconscious
