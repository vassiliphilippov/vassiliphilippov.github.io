by and by

1. ADV

/ ˌbaɪ ən ˈbaɪ / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ ən ˈbaɪ / (BrE)
at some time in the future; eventually
eventually; at a future time

Examples

By and by, she started to understand the reasons behind his actions.
Things will get better for you by and by.
Don't worry, I'll call you by and by.

Synset Examples

By and by he'll understand
I'll see you later

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eventually - in the end, especially after a long delay
  • Degree Lower: soon - in or after a short time

Etymology

From the earlier phrase 'by and by', meaning 'soon' or 'after a while', in use since the 16th century.
