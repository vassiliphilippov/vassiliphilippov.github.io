to the bitter end

1. ADV

/ tə ðə ˈbɪt̬ɚ end / (AmE), / tə ðə ˈbɪtə end / (BrE)
persisting with something until it is completely finished, no matter how difficult or unpleasant it becomes.
persist until the very end despite hardship

Examples

She was determined to finish the marathon to the bitter end, even though her legs hurt.
They fought to the bitter end despite knowing they might lose.
He stood by his decision to the bitter end, refusing to compromise.

Synset Examples

She was determined to see this project through to the bitter end.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to see through - to continue with something until it is completed

Etymology

Popularized in the late 19th century; 'bitter' refers to unpleasantness rather than taste.
