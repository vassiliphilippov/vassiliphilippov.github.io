love you and leave you

1. X

/ lʌv ju ənd liv ju / (AmE), / lʌv ju ənd liːv ju / (BrE)
used as a lighthearted, affectionate way to say goodbye when leaving, often when wishing one could stay longer
affectionate farewell expression
informal

Examples

Sorry, but I have to love you and leave you—work calls early tomorrow.
It was wonderful to see you, but now I'll love you and leave you.
After dinner, she smiled and said, 'Time for me to love you and leave you.'

Synset Examples

It's getting late; I have to love you and leave you.
I wish I could stay longer, but I have to love you and leave you.
We had a great time, but I have to love you and leave you.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goodbye - an expression used to express farewell
