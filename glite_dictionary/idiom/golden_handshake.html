golden handshake

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldən ˈhændʃeɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən ˈhændʃeɪk / (BrE)
a large sum of money given to an employee when they leave a company, often as a result of retirement or redundancy
large severance payment to departing employee

Examples

After thirty years of service, the company offered him a golden handshake to retire early.
The board approved a golden handshake as part of the outgoing director's compensation.
Many executives hope for a golden handshake when they step down from their positions.

Synset Examples

She negotiated a golden handshake after her dismissal.
The CEO's golden handshake raised many eyebrows.
He received a generous golden handshake upon retirement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: severance package - compensation paid to an employee upon leaving a company

Etymology

Metaphorical use of 'golden' to imply value, and 'handshake' to denote agreement or parting, first recorded in the mid-20th century.
