salad days

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæl.əd deɪz / (AmE), / ˈsæl.əd deɪz / (BrE)
a period of youthful inexperience or peak freshness and vitality, often regarded as the best time of one's youth
period of youthful inexperience or prime

Examples

In her salad days, she traveled the world and dreamed big dreams.
He often reminisced about his salad days on the university football team.
Back in my salad days, I could stay up all night without feeling tired.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heyday - the period of greatest success or vigor
  • Synonym: prime - the best or most vigorous period
  • Antonym: decline - the period after one's prime, when strength or success decreases

Etymology

First used in Shakespeare’s Antony and Cleopatra (1606), alluding to the freshness of salad greens as a metaphor for youth.
