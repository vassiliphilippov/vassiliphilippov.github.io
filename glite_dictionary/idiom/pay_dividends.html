to pay dividends

1. VERB

/ peɪ ˈdɪvəˌdɛndz / (AmE), / peɪ ˈdɪvɪˌdɛndz / (BrE)
(of an action, effort, or investment) to yield significant benefits or positive results, especially after some time.
yield positive results or benefits

Examples

Hard work and persistence in your studies will pay dividends in your career.
Taking the time to build strong relationships can pay dividends in both business and personal life.
Her patience with the project began to pay dividends as the results improved.

Synset Examples

Investing in education will pay dividends later in life.
Regular exercise will pay dividends for your health.
The marketing strategy began to pay dividends after six months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bear fruit - to produce successful results
  • Antonym: backfire - to have the opposite effect to what was intended

Etymology

From the financial term 'dividend', meaning a share of profits paid to shareholders; used figuratively since the early 20th century.
