who should not

1. X

/ hu ʃəd nɑt / (AmE), / hu ʃəd nɒt / (BrE)
(used after a statement praising oneself) expressing modesty or self-doubt for speaking well of oneself.
phrased modesty after self-praise
archaic

Examples

Though I say it, who should not, I am proud of my achievements.
She handled the negotiations skillfully, though she said it, who should not.
It was a clever solution, if I may say so, who should not.

Synset Examples

Though I say it who should not, I did well.
