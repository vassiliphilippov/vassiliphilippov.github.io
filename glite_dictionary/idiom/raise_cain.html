to raise Cain

1. VERB

/ reɪz keɪn / (AmE), / reɪz keɪn / (BrE)
to cause a loud disturbance or protest; to create an uproar.
cause a disturbance or uproar

Examples

When the new rules were announced, several employees began to raise Cain during the meeting.
Her parents threatened to raise Cain if the noise didn't stop.
After the team lost, their fans raised Cain outside the stadium.

Synset Examples

Don't raise Cain in public; it’s embarrassing.
He raised Cain over the unfair grading policy.
The kids raised Cain when they lost their game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make a scene - to behave in a disruptive or attention-seeking way in public
  • Synonym: to kick up a fuss - to complain or protest noisily
  • Antonym: to calm down - to become less agitated or upset

Etymology

From the biblical story of Cain in the Book of Genesis, who committed the first murder by killing his brother Abel.
