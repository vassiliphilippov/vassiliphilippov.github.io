dollar to a doughnut

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːlər tə ə ˈdoʊnət / (AmE), / ˈdɒlə tə ə ˈdəʊnət / (BrE)
used to express strong confidence or certainty about an outcome, often in the context of making a bet or prediction
used to express strong confidence or certainty
informal american

Examples

I'd bet a dollar to a doughnut that the meeting will run late.
It's dollars to donuts she'll be the one to get the promotion.
He was willing to put dollar to a doughnut on the outcome of the game.

Synset Examples

I'll bet a dollar to a doughnut that they'll win.
I bet a dollar to a doughnut he won't show up.
He said he would bet a dollar to a doughnut on his team's victory.
Dollars to donuts, she’ll win the competition.
I’d bet dollars to donuts he’ll be late again.
I’d say dollars to donuts that they won’t show up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bet - to risk something, usually money, on the outcome of an event
  • Synonym: wager - to make a bet

Etymology

Originated in the United States in the late 19th century, where betting a dollar (of much greater value) against a doughnut signified high confidence.
