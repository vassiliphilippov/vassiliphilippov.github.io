to make a fortune

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə ˈfɔr.tʃən / (AmE), / meɪk ə ˈfɔː.tʃuːn / (BrE)
to earn a large amount of money, especially within a relatively short time
earn a large amount of money

Examples

He hopes to make a fortune by investing in tech startups.
After years of struggling, she finally made a fortune selling her artwork online.
Many people dream of making a fortune in the stock market.

Synset Examples

The city banker made a fortune in real estate.
The stockjobber made a fortune during the market boom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strike it rich - to suddenly become very wealthy
  • Antonym: lose a fortune - to lose a large amount of money
