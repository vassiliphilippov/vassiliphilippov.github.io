all's well that ends well

1. X

/ ɔːlz wɛl ðət ɛndz wɛl / (AmE), / ɔːlz wɛl ðət ɛndz wɛl / (BrE)
used to say that a difficult situation has ended with a good result, so everything is satisfactory
end result makes everything satisfactory

Examples

The project had many setbacks, but all's well that ends well now that it's completed successfully.
We were late for our flight but managed to board just in time—all's well that ends well.
Although their trip started badly, they arrived safely, so all's well that ends well.

Synset Examples

After the argument, they made up; all's she's apples.

Etymology

Popularized by the title of Shakespeare's play 'All's Well That Ends Well' (1601–1608).
