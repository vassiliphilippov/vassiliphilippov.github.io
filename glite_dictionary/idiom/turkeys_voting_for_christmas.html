turkeys voting for Christmas

1. X

/ ˈtɝːkiz ˈvoʊtɪŋ fər ˈkrɪs·məs / (AmE), / ˈtɜːkiz ˈvəʊtɪŋ fə ˈkrɪs·məs / (BrE)
(British) Used to describe a person or group making a decision that is clearly against their own self-interest, often foolishly or self-destructively.
making a foolish, self-destructive choice
british

Examples

They were like turkeys voting for Christmas when they supported those cuts to their own department.
Approving those changes would be turkeys voting for Christmas for the staff.
He argued that accepting lower wages was just turkeys voting for Christmas.

Synset Examples

It's risky, like turkeys voting for Christmas.
That decision feels like turkeys voting for Christmas.
Choosing that option is like turkeys voting for Christmas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shoot oneself in the foot - to do something that damages one's own interests
  • Synonym: bite the hand that feeds you - to harm or act against someone who is helping you

Etymology

Refers to the fact that turkeys are traditionally eaten at Christmas in the UK, so voting for Christmas would be self-destructive for turkeys.
