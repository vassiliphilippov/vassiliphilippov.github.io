to have words (with)

1. VERB

/ hæv wɝːdz wɪð / (AmE), / hæv wɜːdz wɪð / (BrE)
to have an argument or heated discussion with someone.
to argue or have a heated discussion

Examples

After the meeting, John had words with his colleague about the project.
She had words with her neighbor over the noise last night.
They often have words when money issues come up.

Synset Examples

She had words with her boss over the deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarrel - a heated argument or disagreement
  • Antonym: to make up - to reconcile after an argument
