in the act

1. ADV

/ ɪn ði ækt / (AmE), / ɪn ðiː ækt / (BrE)
while doing something, especially something wrong or forbidden; caught in the process of an action.
caught in the process of an action

Examples

The thief was caught in the act by the police as he tried to escape.
She walked in and found her children in the act of drawing on the walls.
The company was fined after being discovered in the act of dumping waste illegally.

Synset Examples

We caught him in the act of stealing cookies.
She was surprised to find her cat in the act of climbing the curtains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red-handed - caught in the very act of committing an offence
