to hatch a scheme

1. VERB

/ ˈhætʃ ə skim / (AmE), / ˈhætʃ ə skiːm / (BrE)
to secretly devise or plan a strategy, often with cunning or careful thought
devise or plan a strategy

Examples

The two friends hatched a scheme to surprise their colleague on his birthday.
She had secretly hatched a scheme to help her team win the contest.
They spent hours hatching a scheme that would save the community center.

Synset Examples

They hatched a brilliant scheme to boost morale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devise - to invent or plan something
  • Synonym: plot - to make a secret plan, usually to do something illicit
  • Antonym: to improvise - to create or perform without preparation

Etymology

From 'hatch' meaning to bring forth or create, metaphorically extended from the sense of an egg hatching.
