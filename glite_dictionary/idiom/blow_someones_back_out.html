to blow someone's back out

1. VERB

/ bloʊ ˈsʌmwʌnz bæk aʊt / (AmE), / bləʊ ˈsʌmwʌnz bæk aʊt / (BrE)
to cause someone to suffer back pain, especially as a result of lifting something heavy
cause back pain through heavy lifting
informal

Examples

After moving all the furniture, he nearly blew his back out and had to lie down.
That sofa is so heavy, you'll blow your back out if you try to lift it alone.
My friend warned me that shoveling snow all morning could blow my back out.

Synset Examples

He always risks blowing someone's back out with his heavy lifting.
