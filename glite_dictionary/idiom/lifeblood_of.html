lifeblood of

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪfˌblʌd ʌv / (AmE), / ˈlaɪfˌblʌd ɒv / (BrE)
the essential element or main source of vitality necessary for the success or survival of something
essential element or source of vitality

Examples

Tourism is often considered the lifeblood of the local economy.
Innovation is the lifeblood of any successful technology company.
Volunteers are the lifeblood of our organization, keeping everything running smoothly.

Synset Examples

Education is the lifeblood of a thriving society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backbone of - main support or chief foundation of something
  • Synonym: cornerstone - an important quality or feature on which a particular thing depends

Etymology

From the literal use of 'lifeblood' meaning the blood vital to sustain life, used figuratively since the late 16th century.
