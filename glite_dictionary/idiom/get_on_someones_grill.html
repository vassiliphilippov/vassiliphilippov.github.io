to get on someone's grill

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɑn ˈsʌmwʌnz ɡrɪl / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒn ˈsʌmwʌnz ɡrɪl / (BrE)
to confront or criticize someone in a direct or aggressive way
confront or criticize aggressively (informal)
informal slang american

Examples

If you keep messing around, the coach will get on your grill during practice.
My parents always get on my grill when my grades drop.
Please don't get on my grill about the mess—I said I'd clean it up.

Synset Examples

He got on my grill for being late.
Don't get on my grill about my choices.
She tends to get on my grill when I'm stressed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get in someone's face - to confront someone aggressively

Etymology

Grill is used as slang for face or personal space; 'get on someone's grill' draws on this sense.
