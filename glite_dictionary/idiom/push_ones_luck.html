to push one's luck

1. VERB

/ pʊʃ wʌnz lʌk / (AmE), / pʊʃ wʌnz lʌk / (BrE)
to risk the chance of losing success by continuing to take risks or demanding more than is sensible
take unnecessary risks; risk losing success

Examples

I wouldn't push my luck by asking for another extension on the project.
She knows she succeeded once, but she doesn't want to push her luck again.
They were caught sneaking in, proving that sometimes it's better not to push your luck.

Synset Examples

Don’t push your luck with the boss today.
I think you’re pushing your luck by asking for another favor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tempt fate - risk bad luck by taking unnecessary chances
  • Antonym: to play it safe - to avoid unnecessary risks

2. VERB

/ pʊʃ wʌnz lʌk / (AmE), / pʊʃ wʌnz lʌk / (BrE)
to risk a negative outcome by continuing to take chances or ask for more than is reasonable
take excessive risks; risk undeserved success

Examples

If you keep asking for favors, you might be pushing your luck with your boss.
After two victories, he thought entering a third race would be pushing his luck.
She realized she was pushing her luck by staying out so late.

Synset Examples

She felt she was pushing her luck by staying out late.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tempt fate - to take unnecessary risks, especially after a success
  • Antonym: to play it safe - to avoid risks or dangerous situations
