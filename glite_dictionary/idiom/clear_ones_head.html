to clear one's head

1. VERB

/ klɪr wʌnz hɛd / (AmE), / klɪə wʌnz hɛd / (BrE)
to stop thinking about confusing or troubling thoughts in order to think more clearly or feel calmer
become mentally calm or clear

Examples

I went out for a brisk walk to clear my head after the stressful meeting.
She meditated every morning to clear her head before starting work.
He needed some time alone in nature to clear his head and gain perspective.

Synset Examples

He took a little break to clear his head.
She walked a mile to clear her head.
We need a retreat to clear our heads.
Let's take a walk to clear our heads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to collect one's thoughts - to compose oneself to think more clearly
  • Antonym: to lose one's mind - to become mentally unstable or unable to think clearly
