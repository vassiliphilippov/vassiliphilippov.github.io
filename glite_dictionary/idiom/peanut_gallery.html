peanut gallery

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːnʌt ˌɡæləri / (AmE), / ˈpiːnʌt ˌɡæləri / (BrE)
a group of people whose criticisms or comments are considered irrelevant, insignificant, or unwelcome, especially those offered from a less informed or less respected position
group making trivial or unwelcome comments
informal american

Examples

I wish the peanut gallery would keep their opinions to themselves during the meeting.
Ignore the comments from the peanut gallery and focus on your work.
Her suggestion was met with sarcastic laughter from the peanut gallery.

Synset Examples

he ignored complaints from the peanut gallery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hecklers - people who interrupt performances with unwanted remarks
  • Degree Higher: audience - the people watching or listening to a performance

Etymology

Originally from vaudeville theaters in the United States, where the cheapest seats (often occupied by rowdy spectators) were called the 'peanut gallery'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpinət ˌɡæləri / (AmE), / ˈpiːnət ˌɡæləri / (BrE)
a group of critics or commentators, often outspoken and lacking authority or credibility
group of vocal, unimportant critics

Examples

Please ignore the peanut gallery and focus on your work.
She made a joke that drew laughter from the peanut gallery.
Whenever a decision is made, the peanut gallery always has something to say.

Synset Examples

Ignore the peanut gallery; they always criticize.
The comedian took jabs at the peanut gallery during the show.
Don't listen to the peanut gallery; they know nothing.

Etymology

Originally referred to the cheapest seats in a theater, where peanuts were sometimes thrown; now used figuratively for unimportant critics.
