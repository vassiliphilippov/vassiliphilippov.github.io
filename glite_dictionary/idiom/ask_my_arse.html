ask my arse

1. X

/ æsk maɪ ɑrs / (AmE), / ɑːsk maɪ ɑːs / (BrE)
(British, informal, vulgar) A dismissive phrase indicating disbelief or refusal to help, implying the speaker thinks the request or statement is absurd.
dismissive retort of disbelief or refusal
informal vulgar british

Examples

He claims he can fix it in five minutes—ask my arse!
'Can you lend me £50?' – ask my arse, not a chance!
If you want honesty from him, ask my arse.

Synset Examples

She said it's impossible; just ask my arse.
