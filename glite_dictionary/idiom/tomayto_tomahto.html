tomayto, tomahto

1. X

/ təˈmeɪdoʊ təˈmɑːtoʊ / (AmE), / təˈmeɪtəʊ təˈmɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
Used to suggest that the difference between two things is insignificant or unimportant; to express indifference over minor distinctions.
indifference over minor differences

Examples

Whether we call it a résumé or a CV, it's just tomayto, tomahto.
She insists on calling it 'advertisement' while he says 'advert', but really—tomayto, tomahto.
They kept arguing over details, but to everyone else it was tomayto, tomahto.

Synset Examples

To me, it's all the same: tomayto, tomahto.
When it comes to preferences, it's simply tomayto, tomahto.
Whether you like it or not, it's tomayto, tomahto.

Etymology

Popularized by the song "Let's Call the Whole Thing Off" (1937) by George and Ira Gershwin; based on differing pronunciations of "tomato" in American and British English.
