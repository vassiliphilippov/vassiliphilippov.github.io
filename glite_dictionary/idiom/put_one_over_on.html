to put one over on

1. VERB

/ pʊt wʌn ˈoʊvər ɑn / (AmE), / pʊt wʌn ˈəʊvə ɒn / (BrE)
to deceive or trick someone, especially by making them believe something that is not true
deceive or trick someone

Examples

They tried to put one over on the teacher, but she quickly caught their lie.
I can't believe you put one over on such a careful manager.
He's clever, but I doubt he could put one over on her twice.

Synset Examples

She put one over on her boss by finishing early.
It was hard to believe he put one over on everyone.
He managed to put one over on the unsuspecting crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool - to trick or deceive someone
  • Synonym: to outsmart - to defeat or trick by being more clever
