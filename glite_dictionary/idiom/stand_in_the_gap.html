to stand in the gap

1. VERB

/ stænd ɪn ðə gæp / (AmE), / stænd ɪn ðə ɡap / (BrE)
to take action to support others, especially by intervening or providing help when it is needed
to provide needed support or intervention

Examples

During the staffing shortage, she was willing to stand in the gap for her coworkers.
Community leaders must often stand in the gap to ensure everyone has access to basic services.
When disaster struck, many volunteers stood in the gap, providing food and shelter to those affected.

Synset Examples

He chose to stand in the gap for the community.
Volunteers often stand in the gap for those in need.
She stood in the gap for her friends during tough times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intervene - to become involved in a situation to alter or prevent an outcome
  • Synonym: to step in - to become involved, especially to help or prevent something

Etymology

Biblical origin, from Ezekiel 22:30, where 'stand in the gap' referred to someone intervening on behalf of others.
