to go out the window

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ aʊt ðə ˈwɪndoʊ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ aʊt ðə ˈwɪndəʊ / (BrE)
(of a quality, idea, or possibility) to be lost, disregarded, or no longer considered important, especially suddenly or abruptly.
be lost or disregarded abruptly

Examples

All hopes of finishing the project on time went out the window when the power went out.
Once the game started, his diet plans went out the window.
Their friendship went out the window after the heated argument.

Synset Examples

Her chances for the job went out the window after the interview.
His good intentions went out the window during the argument.
All my plans went out the window when it started raining.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disappear - to cease to exist or be visible or accessible
  • Synonym: to vanish - to suddenly and completely go out of sight or existence
