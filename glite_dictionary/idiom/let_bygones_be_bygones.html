to let bygones be bygones

1. VERB

/ lɛt ˈbaɪˌɡɑnz bi ˈbaɪˌɡɑnz / (AmE), / lɛt ˈbaɪˌɡɒnz bi ˈbaɪˌɡɒnz / (BrE)
to forgive past offenses or disagreements and not allow them to affect the present or future
forgive and forget the past

Examples

After years of rivalry, the two companies decided to let bygones be bygones and collaborate on a project.
It's best to let bygones be bygones if you want to maintain a close relationship with your family.
Despite their past conflicts, both leaders agreed to let bygones be bygones for the sake of peace.

Synset Examples

They agreed to let bygones be bygones for the sake of friendship.
It's time to let bygones be bygones and move forward.
After the argument, they decided to let bygones be bygones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to forgive and forget - to stop feeling angry about something and not allow it to affect one's relationship with another
