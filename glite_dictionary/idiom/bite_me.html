bite me

1. X

/ ˈbaɪt mi / (AmE), / ˈbaɪt miː / (BrE)
used to express annoyance, defiance, or dismissal toward someone, often in response to criticism or provocation
expression of annoyance or defiance
informal

Examples

If you don’t like my suggestion, then bite me.
When her coworker criticized her outfit, she just said, 'Bite me.'
He smirked and replied, 'Bite me,' refusing to back down.

Synset Examples

He laughed and replied, 'Oh please, bite me!'
If you don’t like my style, you can just bite me.
She rolled her eyes and said, 'Bite me!'
