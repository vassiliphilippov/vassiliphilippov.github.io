to pull for the underdog

1. VERB

/ pʊl fər ði ˈʌndɚˌdɑɡ / (AmE), / pʊl fə ði ˈʌndədɒɡ / (BrE)
to support or root for the competitor who is expected to lose or is less favored to win
support the less favored competitor

Examples

Growing up, I would always pull for the underdog during championship games.
It is inspiring to see so many fans pull for the underdog in the tournament.
Even though the team was down by ten points, we kept pulling for the underdog until the very end.

Synset Examples

I always pull for the underdog in sports.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: root for the underdog - to cheer or support the less favored competitor
