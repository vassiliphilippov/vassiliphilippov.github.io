out of the frying pan, into the fire

1. X

/ aʊt əv ðə ˈfraɪɪŋ ˌpæn ˌɪntu ðə ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / aʊt əv ðə ˈfraɪɪŋ ˌpæn ˌɪntuː ðə ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
used to describe a situation where someone escapes a bad or difficult situation only to find themselves in an even worse one
from a bad to a worse situation

Examples

It was out of the frying pan, into the fire when the company switched managers during the crisis.
After quitting her stressful job, she found her new one was even worse—truly out of the frying pan, into the fire.
He moved to a new apartment to escape noisy neighbors, but ended up next to a construction site—out of the frying pan, into the fire.

Synset Examples

Leaving that job felt like out of the frying pan, into the fire.
She left one bad relationship, only to find herself out of the frying pan, into the fire.
He thought he was escaping trouble, but it was out of the frying pan, into the fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: from bad to worse - moving from a bad situation to an even worse one
  • Synonym: out of the fire, into the frying pan - reversed variant of the idiom

Etymology

Dates back to at least the 16th century, originally proverbial English.
