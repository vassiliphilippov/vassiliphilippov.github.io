to play the field

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ðə fiːld / (AmE), / pleɪ ðə fiːld / (BrE)
to date several different people without committing to a serious relationship with any of them
date multiple people casually

Examples

Now that he's single, he wants to play the field before committing again.
Many people play the field in their twenties to find the right partner.
She admitted she isn't ready for something serious—she prefers to play the field.

Synset Examples

She’s not ready to settle down; she wants to play the field.
After the breakup, he decided to play the field for a while.
He’s just playing the field before making any serious commitments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to settle down - to begin a steady, committed relationship or lifestyle

Etymology

Originally from sports, meaning to cover or range over the playing area; figuratively applied to dating from the early 20th century.
