where's the beef

1. X

/ wɛɹz ðə bif / (AmE), / wɛəz ðə biːf / (BrE)
Used to express doubt or impatience about the substance or quality of something, especially when it seems lacking or insubstantial.
questioning substance or value

Examples

After the lengthy proposal, investors were left asking, where's the beef?
Her report was impressive on paper, but I still wondered, where's the beef?
Politicians often make promises, but voters want to know, where's the beef?

Synset Examples

In this meeting, I kept thinking, where's the beef?
After all that talk, I wondered, where's the beef?
He promised big changes, but I asked, where's the beef?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: what's the point - Used to question the purpose, meaning, or value of something

Etymology

Popularized by a 1984 American fast food commercial for Wendy's.
