to seal the deal

1. VERB

/ siːl ðə diːl / (AmE), / siːl ðə diːl / (BrE)
to finalize or confirm an agreement, arrangement, or transaction
finalize an agreement or arrangement

Examples

The two companies finally agreed on the terms and sealed the deal with a handshake.
Her impressive sales pitch was enough to seal the deal with the client.
They spent hours negotiating, but a simple nod from the boss sealed the deal.

Synset Examples

They shook hands to seal the deal.
A smile is often enough to seal the deal.
He was determined to seal the deal; he was in for the kill.
The presentation helped seal the deal with investors.
His persuasion skills helped seal the big deal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: close the deal - to successfully complete or finalize an agreement

Etymology

From the figurative sense of 'seal' meaning to make official or binding, as with a physical seal on a document.
