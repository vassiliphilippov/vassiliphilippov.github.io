vicious circle

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʃ.əs ˈsɝː.kəl / (AmE), / ˈvɪʃ.əs ˈsɜː.kəl / (BrE)
A situation in which one problem causes another problem that makes the first problem worse, creating a cycle that is difficult to break.
cycle of problems worsening each other

Examples

The country was trapped in a vicious circle of poverty and debt.
Unemployment and crime form a vicious circle in many urban neighborhoods.
Breaking the vicious circle of mistrust between the groups required patient negotiation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vicious cycle - A situation where problems compound each other in a continuous cycle
  • Antonym: virtuous circle - A situation in which a beneficial effect produces more benefits in a cycle

Etymology

From Latin 'viciosus' (faulty, corrupt) and 'circle', referring to endless repetition.
