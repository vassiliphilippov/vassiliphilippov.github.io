to run for the hills

1. VERB

/ ˈrʌn fər ðə hɪlz / (AmE), / ˈrʌn fə ðə hɪlz / (BrE)
to flee or escape quickly, usually from danger or trouble
escape quickly from danger

Examples

As soon as the fire alarm went off, people ran for the hills in a panic.
When the market crashed, many investors ran for the hills to avoid losses.
The sight of the approaching storm made everyone run for the hills immediately.

Synset Examples

Seeing the angry dog, he ran for the hills.
When the storm approached, they ran for the hills.
The unexpected news made everyone run for the hills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flee - to run away from danger or threat
  • Synonym: retreat - to withdraw or move back, especially from danger
  • Antonym: to stay put - to remain in the same place and not try to escape
