to push up daisies

1. VERB

/ pʊʃ ʌp ˈdeɪziːz / (AmE), / pʊʃ ʌp ˈdeɪziːz / (BrE)
to die or be buried, especially used humorously or as a euphemism
to die (euphemistically or humorously)

Examples

He joked that if he kept driving so fast, he might be pushing up daisies before thirty.
The gangster warned his rival, 'Cross me again and you'll be pushing up daisies.'
Many old buildings have been demolished, and their former owners are now pushing up daisies in the local cemetery.

Synset Examples

If you don’t take care of your health, you'll push up daisies soon.
He joked that he would push up daisies if he didn’t quit smoking.
After years of hard work, he now fears pushing up daisies too soon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to kick the bucket - to die (informal and humorous)
  • Synonym: to pass away - to die (mild euphemism)
  • Synonym: die - to stop living

Etymology

Alludes to burial, with flowers (daisies) growing above a grave.
