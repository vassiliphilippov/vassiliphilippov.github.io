a Jew's eye

1. NOUN

/ ə dʒuz aɪ / (AmE), / ə dʒuːz aɪ / (BrE)
A thing that is extremely valuable or highly prized; sometimes used to refer to something for which a high price is paid or a great sacrifice is made (now considered offensive).
something very valuable (offensive, dated)
offensive archaic

Examples

The ring was worth a Jew's eye to collectors in the antique market.
He said he'd give a Jew's eye to own such a rare manuscript.
In old tales, pirates would fight for treasure that was called a Jew's eye.

Synset Examples

His rare coin collection is worth a Jew's eye.
He would give a Jew's eye for a chance to travel.
He would give a Jew's eye for a good cup of coffee.
She would give a Jew's eye for that vintage dress.

Etymology

The phrase dates to the 16th century, reflecting anti-Semitic stereotypes associating Jews with wealth and avarice.
