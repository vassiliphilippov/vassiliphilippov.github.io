to get in a gaum

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɪn ə ɡɔm / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɪn ə ɡɔːm / (BrE)
to become confused or upset, especially over trivial matters
become confused or upset
informal

Examples

Whenever she faces a tight deadline, she tends to get in a gaum and forgets what to do next.
Try not to get in a gaum if your plans change unexpectedly; just adapt as best you can.
He often gets in a gaum when too many people are talking at once.

Synset Examples

Don't get in a gaum over minor details.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flustered - nervous or confused, especially from agitation
  • Antonym: composed - calm, self-possessed

Etymology

Possibly from dialectal 'gaum' meaning mess or confusion, of Scandinavian origin.
