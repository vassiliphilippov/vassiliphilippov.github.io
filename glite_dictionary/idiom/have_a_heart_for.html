to have a heart for

1. VERB

/ hæv ə hɑrt fər / (AmE), / hæv ə hɑːt fə / (BrE)
to feel compassion for or a strong desire to support or help someone or something
show compassion or care for

Examples

She really has a heart for teaching children and volunteers in her free time.
Our organization is looking for people who have a heart for social justice.
He had a heart for animals and often rescued stray dogs from the streets.

Synset Examples

You really have a heart for helping others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be compassionate - to show sympathy and concern for others
  • Antonym: be indifferent - to lack interest, concern, or sympathy
