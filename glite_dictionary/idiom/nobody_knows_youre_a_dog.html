nobody knows you're a dog

1. X

/ ˈnoʊˌbɑːdi noʊz jʊr ə dɔg / (AmE), / ˈnəʊbədi nəʊz jɔː ə dɒɡ / (BrE)
used to highlight the anonymity and lack of identity verification that is possible online, where people can conceal their true selves
emphasizes online anonymity

Examples

On discussion forums, nobody knows you're a dog, so it's easy to create a new identity.
The phrase nobody knows you're a dog illustrates how people behave differently online than in person.
In virtual worlds, nobody knows you're a dog, making it difficult to trust appearances.

Synset Examples

Remember, on the Internet, nobody knows you're a dog, so be cautious.
That meme reminds us that on the Internet, nobody knows you're a dog.
You can be whoever you want; on the Internet, nobody knows you're a dog.

Etymology

Popularized by a 1993 New Yorker cartoon by Peter Steiner.
