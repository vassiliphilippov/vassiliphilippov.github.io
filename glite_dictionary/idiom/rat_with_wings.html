rat with wings

1. NOUN

/ ɹæt wɪð wɪŋz / (AmE), / ɹat wɪð wɪŋz / (BrE)
(informal, derogatory) An undesirable or annoying person or thing, often one that is persistently active or intrusive.
annoying or undesirable person or thing
informal

Examples

People sometimes refer to city pigeons as rats with wings because of their abundance and mess.
My little brother was being such a rat with wings at the party, never sitting still.
The pop-up ads on this website are true rats with wings, always appearing when I least expect them.

Synset Examples

That new gadget feels like a rat with wings.
In the office, he's like a rat with wings, always buzzing around.
