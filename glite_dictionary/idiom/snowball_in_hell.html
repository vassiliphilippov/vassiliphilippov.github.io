snowball in hell

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnoʊˌbɔl ɪn hɛl / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌbɔːl ɪn hɛl / (BrE)
used to describe something that is extremely unlikely or impossible
extremely unlikely or impossible
informal

Examples

He doesn’t have a snowball in hell of winning that competition.
There’s a snowball in hell chance she’ll agree to our proposal.
You have about as much chance as a snowball in hell of getting a promotion this year.

Synset Examples

There’s a snowball in hell chance this plan will work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long shot - something with little chance of success
  • Synonym: chance - possibility, especially a slim one
  • Antonym: certainty - something sure to happen
