to act like a buffoon

1. VERB

/ ækt laɪk ə bəˈfuːn / (AmE), / ækt laɪk ə bʊˈfuːn / (BrE)
to behave in a foolish, silly, or clumsy manner, especially in a way that attracts attention or ridicule
behave foolishly or clumsily

Examples

He tends to act like a buffoon when he's nervous in front of large crowds.
Please don't act like a buffoon at the company party; it's an important event.
She realized too late that she had acted like a buffoon during the job interview.

Synset Examples

He acted like a buffoon during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fool around - behave in a silly or foolish way
