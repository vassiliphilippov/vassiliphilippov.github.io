to the nth degree

1. ADV

/ tə ði ɛnθ dɪˈɡri / (AmE), / tə ði ɛnθ dɪˈɡriː / (BrE)
to the greatest, most extreme, or most intense extent possible
to the greatest or most extreme extent

Examples

Her attention to detail is impressive to the nth degree.
He pushed his training to the nth degree to prepare for the competition.
Their generosity was obvious to the nth degree during the holidays.

Synset Examples

He studies math to the nth degree.
She loves her job to the nth degree.
They prepared for the presentation to the nth degree.

Etymology

From mathematical use of 'nth' to denote an unspecified but large number or degree.
