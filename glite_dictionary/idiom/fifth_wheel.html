fifth wheel

1. NOUN

/ fɪfθ wil / (AmE), / fɪfθ wiːl / (BrE)
A person or thing that is unnecessary or superfluous in a particular situation, especially one who feels out of place in company.
unnecessary or superfluous person or thing

Examples

I felt like a fifth wheel when I joined my friends on their romantic dinner.
Whenever Mary brings her boyfriend, I end up as the fifth wheel.
Nobody wants to be the fifth wheel at a couples-only event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: third wheel - an unwanted or unnecessary person in a group of three, especially among couples

2. NOUN

/ fɪfθ wil / (AmE), / fɪfθ wiːl / (BrE)
A person who feels unnecessary or out of place in a group, especially among couples.
person who feels out of place

Examples

He felt like a fifth wheel at the couple's dinner, not quite fitting in with the conversation.
Whenever the group went out as couples, she often ended up being the fifth wheel.
Being single at the wedding made him feel somewhat like a fifth wheel.

Synset Examples

He felt like a fifth wheel at the couple's dinner.
She always felt like a fifth wheel during their outings.
Being the only single friend made him feel like a fifth wheel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outsider - a person who does not belong to a particular group
  • Synonym: third wheel - a person who feels superfluous among couples or close friends

Etymology

Originally referred to an extra wheel on carriages or vehicles, which was often unnecessary.
