to go round the bend

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ raʊnd ðə bɛnd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ raʊnd ðə bend / (BrE)
to lose one's sanity or become irrational, usually due to extreme stress or annoyance
become insane or irrational
informal british

Examples

If I have to listen to that song again, I’ll go round the bend.
After working nonstop for days, he felt like he might go round the bend.
She was so frustrated by the delays that she nearly went round the bend.

Synset Examples

Things got crazy once we went round the bend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go mad - to become insane or mentally unstable
  • Synonym: to lose one's mind - to become insane or irrational
  • Antonym: to keep one's cool - to remain calm and composed
