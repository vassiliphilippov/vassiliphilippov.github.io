to get one's head together

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt wʌnz hɛd təˈɡɛðər / (AmE), / ɡɛt wʌnz hɛd təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to organize one's thoughts or feelings, especially after a period of confusion or turmoil
organize one's thoughts or feelings
informal

Examples

After a long walk, she was able to get her head together and make a decision.
He took a few days off work to get his head together after the breakup.
I need some time alone so I can get my head together before the meeting.

Synset Examples

She finally managed to get her head together after months of chaos.
He is trying to get his head together after college.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pull oneself together - to regain control of one's emotions or composure
