new wine in an old bottle

1. NOUN

/ nuː waɪn ɪn ən oʊld ˈbɑːtəl / (AmE), / njuː waɪn ɪn ən əʊld ˈbɒtəl / (BrE)
new ideas or innovations presented within an outdated or traditional framework, often without achieving true progress
modern ideas in a traditional setting

Examples

The company's latest product is just new wine in an old bottle, offering little real innovation.
Many critics accused the reform movement of being new wine in an old bottle.
Although the design seemed fresh, it was actually new wine in an old bottle.

Synset Examples

The policy changes felt like new wine in an old bottle.
His ideas were just new wine in an old bottle.
The project was a case of new wine in an old bottle.

Etymology

An allusion to the biblical phrase 'new wine in old bottles', which refers to incompatibility between new and old systems.
