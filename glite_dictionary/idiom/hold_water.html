to hold water

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ˈwɔtər / (AmE), / həʊld ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
(of an argument, theory, or explanation) to be logical, credible, or able to withstand scrutiny or criticism.
be logical or credible; withstand scrutiny

Examples

Her argument didn't hold water when the facts were examined closely.
Scientists quickly realized the theory wouldn't hold water under peer review.
Do you think his explanation can really hold water?

Synset Examples

Her shoes won't hold up
This theory won't hold water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make sense - to be clear and easy to understand
  • Antonym: to fall apart - to fail or be shown to be unsound, especially of arguments
