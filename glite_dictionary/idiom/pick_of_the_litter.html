pick of the litter

1. NOUN

/ pɪk əv ðə ˈlɪtər / (AmE), / pɪk əv ðə ˈlɪtə / (BrE)
the best or most desirable person or thing among a group
the best choice in a group

Examples

Everyone wanted to hire her because she was clearly the pick of the litter among the applicants.
The chef chose the pick of the litter from the fresh vegetables delivered that morning.
When he bought his car, he made sure to get the pick of the litter from the showroom.

Synset Examples

That puppy is the pick of the litter.
She always selects the pick of the litter at adoption events.
We got the pick of the litter from the breeder.

Etymology

Originally refers to the best animal (usually a puppy) from a group born at the same time.
