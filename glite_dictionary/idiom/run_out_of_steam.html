to run out of steam

1. VERB

/ rʌn ˈaʊt əv stiːm / (AmE), / rʌn ˈaʊt əv stiːm / (BrE)
to lose energy, enthusiasm, or motivation and become unable to continue effectively
lose energy or motivation

Examples

After working nonstop for six hours, I finally ran out of steam and took a break.
The creative team ran out of steam halfway through the campaign.
She often runs out of steam before finishing her workout routine.

Synset Examples

The team ran out of steam halfway through the project.
After an hour, he suddenly ran out of steam.
I always run out of steam during long meetings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tire - to become physically or mentally fatigued
  • Synonym: lose momentum - to slow down or become less active or effective

Etymology

Originally referring to steam-powered engines losing power as the steam diminished.
