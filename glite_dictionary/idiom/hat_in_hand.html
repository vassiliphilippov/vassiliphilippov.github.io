hat in hand

1. ADV

/ hæt ɪn hænd / (AmE), / hæt ɪn hand / (BrE)
in a humble or respectful manner, often when asking for something or admitting a mistake
in a humble or respectful manner

Examples

After making a mistake, he returned hat in hand to apologize to his colleagues.
She went to the director hat in hand to request additional funding for her project.
He approached the committee hat in hand, hoping they would give him a second chance.

Synset Examples

He approached the boss with his hat in hand.
The student asked for help, hat in hand.
She came to the meeting with her hat in hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance
  • Antonym: arrogant - having a sense of superiority and lack of respect for others

Etymology

Refers to the former custom of removing one's hat as a gesture of respect or humility.
