skinful of laughter

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɪnfʊl əv ˈlæftɚ / (AmE), / ˈskɪnfʊl əv ˈlɑːftə / (BrE)
an abundance of laughter or great enjoyment; a lot of laughter
a lot of laughter

Examples

Her jokes gave the whole group a skinful of laughter during dinner.
After the performance, everyone left the theater with a skinful of laughter.
The comedian's stories filled the evening with a skinful of laughter.

Synset Examples

A skinful of laughter made the night unforgettable.

Etymology

From 'skinful' (an amount to fill the skin, often used for drink) extended metaphorically to laughter.
