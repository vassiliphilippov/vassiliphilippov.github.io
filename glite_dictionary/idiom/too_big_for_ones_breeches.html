too big for one's breeches

1. ADJ

/ tu bɪɡ fəɹ wʌnz ˈbrɪtʃɪz / (AmE), / tuː bɪɡ fə wʌnz ˈbrɪtʃɪz / (BrE)
Behaving in an overly self-important or arrogant way; having an exaggerated sense of one's own worth or abilities.
overly arrogant or conceited
informal

Examples

Ever since his promotion, he's been too big for his breeches and treats everyone as if they're beneath him.
Don't get too big for your breeches just because you won one game.
Her parents warned her not to become too big for her breeches after winning the award.

Synset Examples

a snotty little scion of a degenerate family
they're snobs — stuck-up and uppity and persnickety

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrogant - having an exaggerated sense of one's own importance or abilities
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low view of one's importance

Etymology

From 'breeches' (trousers), implying a person acts larger or more important than their position justifies; used in the US since the 19th century.
