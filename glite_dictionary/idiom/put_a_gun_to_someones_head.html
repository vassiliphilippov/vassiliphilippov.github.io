to put a gun to someone's head

1. VERB

/ pʊt ə ɡʌn tu ˈsʌmwʌnz hɛd / (AmE), / pʊt ə ɡʌn tu ˈsʌmwənz hɛd / (BrE)
to pressure someone severely; to force someone to make a decision or take action by threatening negative consequences
to pressure or threaten someone into action

Examples

No one is putting a gun to your head; you can decide for yourself.
When the boss put a gun to his head about the deadline, he had no choice but to agree.
She felt like her parents put a gun to her head to choose a particular college.

Synset Examples

They put a gun to their own heads with that risky decision.
He felt like they put a gun to his head over the budget cuts.
The deadline felt like they put a gun to my head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pressure - to strongly urge someone into action or decision
  • Synonym: to coerce - to force someone to do something by threat or pressure
