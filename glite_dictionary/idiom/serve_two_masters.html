to serve two masters

1. VERB

/ sɝv tu ˈmæstɚz / (AmE), / sɜːv tuː ˈmɑːstəz / (BrE)
to attempt to satisfy two authorities or groups with conflicting interests, making it impossible to please both fully
try to satisfy conflicting authorities

Examples

She realized she couldn't serve two masters when her loyalties were divided between her boss and her family.
Trying to serve two masters left him stressed and ultimately dissatisfied both at work and home.
If you serve two masters, you risk letting down both sides.

Synset Examples

You can't serve two masters without conflict.
It's tough to serve two masters at once.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sit on the fence - to avoid taking sides when two options are in conflict
  • Synonym: to hedge one's bets - to protect oneself against loss by supporting more than one possible outcome

Etymology

From the biblical phrase in Matthew 6:24: 'No man can serve two masters'.
