to pay (someone) back in (their) own coin

1. VERB

/ peɪ hɪm bæk ɪn hɪz oʊn kɔɪn / (AmE), / peɪ hɪm bæk ɪn hɪz əʊn kɔɪn / (BrE)
to retaliate against someone by treating them in the same way they have treated you, especially when that treatment is unfair or unpleasant
retaliate in the same manner

Examples

After being insulted, she decided to pay him back in his own coin by ignoring him at the meeting.
If he keeps spreading rumors, someone will eventually pay him back in his own coin.
He had always been rude to others, so they finally paid him back in his own coin.

Synset Examples

She was determined to pay him back in his own coin for the betrayal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to retaliate - to make an attack or assault in return for a similar attack
  • Synonym: to turn the tables - to reverse one's position relative to someone else, especially by turning a disadvantage into an advantage

Etymology

From the literal notion of repaying with money (coin); metaphorical use dates from at least the 17th century.
