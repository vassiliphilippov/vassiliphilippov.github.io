to run circles around

1. VERB

/ rʌn ˈsɝːkəlz əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / rʌn ˈsɜːkəlz əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to outperform or outpace someone by a wide margin; to be much better or faster than someone else
outperform or outpace easily

Examples

She can run circles around most of her classmates in mathematics.
The new software runs circles around its competitors in both speed and reliability.
In debates, he often runs circles around his opponents with his quick thinking.

Synset Examples

She legiterally ran circles around her competitors.
The experienced team ran rings around the newcomers.
He ran rings around the competition in the marathon.
The new software runs circles around the old version.
He runs circles around his competitors with ease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outperform - to do better than someone else
  • Synonym: run rings around - to greatly outdo or surpass someone
