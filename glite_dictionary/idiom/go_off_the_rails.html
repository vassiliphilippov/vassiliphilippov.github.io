to go off the rails

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ɑf ðə reɪlz / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɒf ðə reɪlz / (BrE)
(of a person, project, or situation) to become chaotic or uncontrolled; to deviate from the expected or correct course.
become chaotic or uncontrolled

Examples

After the manager quit, the entire team seemed to go off the rails and missed all their deadlines.
The movie started strong but really went off the rails in the second half.
If you don't set clear goals, your plans may go off the rails unexpectedly.

Synset Examples

He realized the discussion had gone off the rails.
The project went off the rails last month.
Their plans quickly went off the rails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go off the track - to deviate from the expected or correct course

Etymology

Originally alluding to a train leaving its tracks, hence losing control or direction.
