to find another gear

1. VERB

/ faɪnd əˈnʌðər ɡɪr / (AmE), / faɪnd əˈnʌðə ɡɪə / (BrE)
to increase effort or intensity, especially when faced with a challenge or higher demands
put in greater effort or intensity

Examples

When the competition intensified, she managed to find another gear and outperformed her rivals.
During the final quarter, the basketball team had to find another gear to secure the win.
Despite his fatigue, he somehow found another gear and completed the race ahead of schedule.

Synset Examples

He managed to find another gear when the pressure increased.
The team needs to find another gear to win this match.
She decided to find another gear during the final lap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to step up - to increase effort or action; to do more

Etymology

Metaphor based on changing to a higher gear in a vehicle to go faster or work harder.
