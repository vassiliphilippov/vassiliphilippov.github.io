to go out of one's mind

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ aʊt əv wʌnz maɪnd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ aʊt əv wʌnz maɪnd / (BrE)
to become extremely anxious, distressed, or insane
become extremely distressed or insane

Examples

She was so worried about her missing dog that she nearly went out of her mind.
If I have to listen to that song one more time, I will go out of my mind.
During the long wait for the exam results, he felt like he was going out of his mind.

Synset Examples

She went out of her mind with worry last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lose one's mind - to become insane or extremely distressed
  • Antonym: to keep one's head - to remain calm under pressure
