high time

1. NOUN

/ haɪ taɪm / (AmE), / haɪ taɪm / (BrE)
used to say that something should have happened or been done sooner and must happen now
moment when something is overdue

Examples

It's high time we made a decision about the project.
After waiting for so long, it's high time they fixed the broken elevator.
Don’t you think it’s high time you cleaned your room?

Synset Examples

it is high time you went to work
Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ haɪ taɪm / (AmE), / haɪ taɪm / (BrE)
the appropriate or long overdue moment to do something, often implying it should have been done earlier
long overdue time for action

Examples

It's high time we addressed the issue before it gets worse.
Many people feel it's high time for political reform in the country.
After months of waiting, it's high time she received some recognition for her hard work.

Synset Examples

It's high time we started our project.
She said it was high time to clean the house.
I think it's high time for a vacation.
