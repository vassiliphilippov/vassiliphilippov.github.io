to cock a snook

1. VERB

/ kɑk ə snuk / (AmE), / kɒk ə snuːk / (BrE)
to openly show disrespect or defiance towards someone or something, often by making a gesture or behaving contemptuously
show disrespect in a defiant way
british

Examples

The rebellious artist cocked a snook at the critics who doubted his talent.
Some young people enjoy cocking a snook at authority whenever possible.
She cocked a snook at convention by wearing a bright red dress to the formal event.

Synset Examples

He cocked a snook at authority during the protest.
They cocked a snook at the rules and had fun.
She decided to cock a snook at the critics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to thumb one's nose at - to show contempt or defiance, especially by making a rude gesture

Etymology

Probably from the gesture of putting one's thumb to the nose with fingers extended, symbolizing mockery.
