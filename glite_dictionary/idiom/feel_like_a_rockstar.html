to feel like a rockstar

1. VERB

/ fil laɪk ə ˈrɑːkˌstɑr / (AmE), / fiːl laɪk ə ˈrɒkˌstɑː / (BrE)
to feel highly celebrated, admired, or extremely successful, as if one were a famous rock musician
to feel highly celebrated or successful

Examples

After finishing the marathon, he felt like a rockstar as the crowd cheered him on.
She felt like a rockstar when everyone congratulated her on the successful project.
Every time I solve a tough problem at work, I feel like a rockstar.

Synset Examples

After winning the award, she felt like a rockstar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feel on top of the world - to feel extremely happy or successful
  • Antonym: feel like a failure - to feel unsuccessful or disappointed in oneself

Etymology

Derived from 'rockstar', meaning a star of rock music, associated with fame and celebration.
