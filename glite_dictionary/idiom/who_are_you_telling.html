who are you telling

1. X

/ hu ɑɹ ju ˈtɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / huː ɑː juː ˈtɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
Used as a rhetorical question to express that the speaker already knows the information and does not need to be told.
rhetorical: already know this
informal

Examples

I know the project will be tough—who are you telling?
She complained about the weather, but who are you telling? We’ve all been stuck here all week.
"Exams are stressful," he said, and I just thought, who are you telling.

Synset Examples

I already knew the news; who are you telling?
