to put on ice

1. VERB

/ pʊt ɑn aɪs / (AmE), / pʊt ɒn aɪs / (BrE)
to postpone or temporarily stop something, especially a plan, project, or activity
postpone or delay something

Examples

The company decided to put on ice the expansion plans due to economic uncertainty.
Let's put on ice any further discussions until next week.
After the budget cuts, management had no choice but to put on ice several ongoing projects.

Synset Examples

He decided to put his plans on ice until next year.
Let’s put that idea on ice until next year.
We decided to put the project on ice for now.
He was put on ice until he recovered from his injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to postpone - to delay or arrange for something to take place at a later time
  • Synonym: to shelve - to decide not to continue with a plan, idea, etc., for a period of time
