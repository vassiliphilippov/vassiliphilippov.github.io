to go to pot

1. VERB

/ ˌɡoʊ tə ˈpɑt / (AmE), / ˌɡəʊ tə ˈpɒt / (BrE)
(idiom) To become ruined, deteriorated, or to fall into a state of disrepair or decline.
to deteriorate or be ruined

Examples

During the recession, many small shops went to pot and had to close down.
If you neglect your studies, your grades will quickly go to pot.
After her husband died, the garden went to pot without anyone to care for it.

Synset Examples

His business went to pot when the economy soured

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fall apart - to break into pieces or stop working properly
  • Antonym: to improve - to become better

Etymology

Originally from the idea of scraps being thrown into a cooking pot, meaning something has been reduced to a useless state.

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ tə pɑt / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə pɒt / (BrE)
to deteriorate, decline in quality, or fail, especially after a period of good condition or success
deteriorate or decline

Examples

After the manager left, the entire department began to go to pot within a few months.
If you stop taking care of your garden, it will quickly go to pot.
Many small businesses went to pot during the economic crisis.

Synset Examples

After the layoffs, the morale began to go to pot.
The project really started to go to pot last year.
If we don’t fix this, the whole plan will go to pot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go downhill - to worsen or decline
  • Synonym: to fall apart - to break down or deteriorate badly
  • Antonym: to improve - to become better

Etymology

Possibly from the notion of something being reduced to scraps for the cooking pot, hence coming to ruin. Originates in late 16th century English.
