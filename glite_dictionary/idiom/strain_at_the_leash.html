to strain at the leash

1. VERB

/ streɪn æt ðə liʃ / (AmE), / streɪn ət ðə liːʃ / (BrE)
to be very eager or impatient to begin or do something, often being held back temporarily
be eager or impatient to begin

Examples

The young athletes are straining at the leash to compete in the championship match.
Investors are straining at the leash to get involved in the new technology sector.
After months of preparation, the team was straining at the leash to launch their project.

Synset Examples

He felt ready to strain at the leash for a new adventure.
The dog began to strain at the leash, eager to run.
The children strained at the leash, excited to enter the amusement park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to champing at the bit - to be very eager to start or do something
  • Synonym: impatient - eager or unable to wait for something

Etymology

Refers to the literal action of a dog pulling at its leash, extended metaphorically to people or things showing impatience or eagerness.
