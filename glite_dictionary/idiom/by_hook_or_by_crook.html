by hook or by crook

1. ADV

/ baɪ hʊk ɔɹ baɪ kɹʊk / (AmE), / baɪ hʊk ɔː baɪ kɹʊk / (BrE)
using any method possible, especially when trying to achieve something in a determined way
by any means necessary

Examples

She was determined to finish the project by hook or by crook, even if it meant working all night.
He managed to get the interview by hook or by crook, despite all the obstacles.
They promised to deliver the package today, by hook or by crook.

Synset Examples

I'll pass this course by hook or by crook

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at all costs - doing something no matter what it takes

Etymology

Probably from Middle English, earliest recorded 1380; origin uncertain, possibly referring to wood-gathering rights by 'hook' (billhook) or 'crook' (shepherd's crook).

2. ADV

/ baɪ hʊk ɔɹ baɪ kɹʊk / (AmE), / baɪ hʊk ɔː baɪ krʊk / (BrE)
by any means necessary; using any possible method, even if it is difficult or dishonest.
by any means necessary

Examples

They were determined to win the competition by hook or by crook.
She promised to finish the assignment by hook or by crook, even if it meant staying up all night.
We will rescue the animals from the storm by hook or by crook.

Synset Examples

They planned to finish the project by hook or by crook.
I must find my way home by hook or by crook.
We'll get tickets by hook or by crook.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at all costs - no matter what must be done
  • Synonym: come hell or high water - no matter what difficulties arise

Etymology

Origin uncertain; recorded since the 14th century, possibly referring to hooks and crooks (shepherd’s tools) or as a reference to medieval land access rights.
