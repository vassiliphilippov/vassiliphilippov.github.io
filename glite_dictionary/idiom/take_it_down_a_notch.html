to take it down a notch

1. VERB

/ teɪk ɪt daʊn ə nɑtʃ / (AmE), / teɪk ɪt daʊn ə nɒtʃ / (BrE)
to reduce the intensity, force, or severity of one's behavior or attitude; to become less extreme or aggressive
reduce intensity or severity
informal

Examples

If you keep shouting, you'll need to take it down a notch before the neighbors complain.
During the heated debate, the moderator asked everyone to take it down a notch.
When Sarah started bragging, her friend advised her to take it down a notch.

Synset Examples

She had to take her enthusiasm down a notch to fit in.
After his outburst, I told him to take it down a notch.
You might want to take it down a notch during the presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tone it down - to make something less forceful or intense
  • Synonym: dial it back - to reduce the intensity or force of something
