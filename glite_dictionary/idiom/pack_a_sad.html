to pack a sad

1. VERB

/ pæk ə sæd / (AmE), / pæk ə sæd / (BrE)
(Austral., NZ, informal) To become upset, sulky, or emotional, especially in response to disappointment or frustration.
become upset or sulky (Austral., NZ)
informal

Examples

He started to pack a sad when they canceled the event unexpectedly.
Whenever she loses at chess, she tends to pack a sad for hours.
After hearing the disappointing news, he just sat there and packed a sad.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulk - be moodily silent or withdrawn; express resentment
