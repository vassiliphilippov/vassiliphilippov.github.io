to break the rut

1. VERB

/ breɪk ðə rʌt / (AmE), / breɪk ðə rʌt / (BrE)
to escape from a monotonous or unchanging situation or routine
escape monotony or routine

Examples

After years in the same job, he decided to break the rut and start his own business.
Traveling to a new country helped her break the rut she felt in her daily life.
They found that volunteering was a great way to break the rut and meet new people.

Synset Examples

They tried to break the rut by exploring new hobbies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to break the mold - to do something in a completely new way
  • Antonym: stuck in a rut - unable to change a monotonous situation or routine
