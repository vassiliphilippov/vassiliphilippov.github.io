in one's bones

1. X

/ ɪn wʌnz boʊnz / (AmE), / ɪn wʌnz bəʊnz / (BrE)
used to say that someone has a deep, instinctive feeling or certainty about something, often without clear evidence
deep, instinctive feeling or certainty

Examples

She knew in her bones that something was wrong, even without any proof.
In your bones, you can sometimes sense a storm before it arrives.
He felt in his bones that taking the job was the right decision.

Synset Examples

She knew in her bones it would rain today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gut feeling - an instinctive feeling, as opposed to an opinion based on facts
  • Synonym: sixth sense - a supposed intuitive faculty giving awareness not explicable in terms of normal perception

2. ADV

/ ɪn wʌnz boʊnz / (AmE), / ɪn wʌnz bəʊnz / (BrE)
to have a strong, intuitive feeling or sense about something, often without concrete evidence
to have a strong intuition or feeling

Examples

Even though the weather forecast was clear, she felt a storm coming in her bones.
I just knew in my bones that he was telling the truth.
The players could sense victory in their bones before the final whistle blew.

Synset Examples

He sensed danger in his bones before it happened.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sense - become aware of; perceive
  • Synonym: intuition - the ability to understand something instinctively
