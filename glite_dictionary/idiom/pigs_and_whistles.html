pigs and whistles

1. NOUN

/ pɪɡz ənd ˈwɪsəlz / (AmE), / pɪɡz ənd ˈwɪsəlz / (BrE)
to complicate or exaggerate a simple task, or to make a mess of something
make a mess of something; overcomplicate a task

Examples

He managed to make pigs and whistles of the entire presentation.
Please don't turn this repair job into pigs and whistles again!
Whenever she tries to organize, it ends up as pigs and whistles.

Synset Examples

He tends to make pigs and whistles of simple tasks.
Don't make pigs and whistles of the instructions!
She always makes pigs and whistles of her projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botch - to carry out a task badly or carelessly
  • Synonym: bungle - to do something clumsily or incompetently
  • Antonym: to simplify - to make something simpler or easier
