been to the rodeo

1. X

/ bɪn tə ðə roʊˈdi.oʊ / (AmE), / bɪn tə ðə rəʊˈdi.əʊ / (BrE)
having experience in difficult or challenging situations, especially implying familiarity with hardships or adversity
experienced challenging situations
informal

Examples

You can tell she's been to the rodeo by the way she handles stressful projects.
I've been to the rodeo enough times to know how to deal with setbacks.
After years in this business, they know I've been to the rodeo before.

Synset Examples

I've been to the rodeo; I can handle tough situations.
She’s been to the rodeo and can train the new staff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: been around the block - having experience, especially in challenging or worldly matters

Etymology

From the literal experience of participating in a rodeo, extended figuratively to mean having undergone tough or challenging situations.
