to stand sentinel

1. VERB

/ stænd ˈsɛntənəl / (AmE), / stænd ˈsɛntɪnəl / (BrE)
to keep watch or guard, especially in order to protect someone or something from danger
keep watch or guard

Examples

A lone soldier stood sentinel at the castle gate throughout the night.
Volunteers stand sentinel at the entrance, checking every visitor's ID.
The old tree stood sentinel over the town square for more than a century.

Synset Examples

We must stand sentinel to protect ourselves
The jewels over which they kept guard were stolen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stand guard - to stay in a place and watch so as to protect it
  • Synonym: patrol - to keep watch over an area by regularly walking or traveling around it
