to go south

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ saʊθ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ saʊθ / (BrE)
(informal) To decline, fail, or deteriorate; to get worse.
to decline or worsen (informal)
informal

Examples

After we lost our biggest client, profits began to go south rapidly.
She invested in tech stocks just before the market started to go south.
The project was going well, but things began to go south when funding was cut.

Synset Examples

If sales go south, we'll have to cut costs.
Things started to go south after the merger.
If prices continue to head south, we'll lose money.
The economy is expected to head south next quarter.
It looks like our plans will head south if it rains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deteriorate - to become worse
  • Antonym: to improve - to become better

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ saʊθ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ saʊθ / (BrE)
(of a situation or condition) to fail, decline, or deteriorate suddenly or noticeably.
to fail or deteriorate
informal american

Examples

After the new manager arrived, everything at the factory went south.
Our vacation plans went south when the airline canceled our flights.
Negotiations quickly went south after both sides refused to compromise.

Synset Examples

The deal went south, leaving everyone feeling schmucky.
The meeting went south after the disagreement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fail - to be unsuccessful in achieving a goal
  • Synonym: collapse - to suddenly fall down or fail completely
  • Antonym: go well - to proceed in a positive and successful way

Etymology

Originally American English, possibly from compass directions where 'south' can mean 'downward' on a map.
