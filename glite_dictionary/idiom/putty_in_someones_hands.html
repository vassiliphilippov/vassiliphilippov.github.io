putty in someone's hands

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌti ɪn sʌmˌwʌnz hændz / (AmE), / ˈpʌti ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz hændz / (BrE)
easily influenced or controlled by someone; submissive to another person's wishes or manipulations
easily influenced or controlled by someone

Examples

Since the interview began, the candidate was putty in her hands and agreed to every suggestion.
As soon as he heard her praise, he became putty in their hands.
The clever lawyer made the witness feel like putty in his hands during cross-examination.

Synset Examples

He felt like putty in her hands after that sweet smile.
With her charm, he became putty in her hands instantly.
After his apology, she knew he was putty in her hands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malleable - easily influenced or controlled
  • Antonym: resistant - not easily influenced or controlled

Etymology

Figurative use of 'putty', a soft substance easily shaped, to describe someone's pliability in another's control.
