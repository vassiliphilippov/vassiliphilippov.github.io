at the end of one's rope

1. X

/ æt ði ɛnd əv wʌnz roʊp / (AmE), / æt ði ɛnd əv wʌnz rəʊp / (BrE)
having reached the limit of one's patience, endurance, or resourcefulness; unable to continue or cope any longer
out of patience or endurance

Examples

After months of sleepless nights with the newborn, she was at the end of her rope.
I'm at the end of my rope with this malfunctioning computer—it just won't work.
After juggling three jobs and school, he finally admitted he was at the end of his rope.

Synset Examples

She was at the end of her rope with the constant delays.
She felt at the end of her tether with the constant demands.
After all the stress, I feel at the end of my rope.
When he lost his job, he felt he was at the end of his rope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at the end of one's tether - having reached the limit of one's patience or endurance
