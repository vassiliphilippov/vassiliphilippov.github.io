to break the glass ceiling

1. VERB

/ breɪk ðə ɡlæs ˈsiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / breɪk ðə ɡlɑːs ˈsiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
to overcome social or institutional barriers that prevent minorities or women from rising to higher positions in a profession or organization
overcome a barrier to advancement

Examples

She worked hard for years to finally break the glass ceiling in her industry.
Many hope that her election will inspire others to break the glass ceiling.
Despite facing discrimination, he remained determined to break the glass ceiling.

Synset Examples

She broke the glass ceiling to become CEO.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glass ceiling - an invisible barrier preventing certain groups from rising beyond a certain level in a hierarchy

Etymology

From 'glass ceiling', a metaphor coined in the 1980s for invisible barriers preventing advancement, especially for women and minorities.
