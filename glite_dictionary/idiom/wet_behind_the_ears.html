wet behind the ears

1. ADJ

/ wɛt bəˈhaɪnd ði ɪrz / (AmE), / wɛt bɪˈhaɪnd ðiː ɪəz / (BrE)
having little experience and therefore not fully competent or knowledgeable; naive or inexperienced.
inexperienced or naive (idiom)

Examples

The new employee is still wet behind the ears and needs time to adjust.
Although talented, the team was a bit wet behind the ears in international competitions.
Don’t be too hard on her—she’s just wet behind the ears and eager to learn.

Synset Examples

Being wet behind the ears is normal for new graduates.
He's still wet behind the ears in this job.
Don't worry, you'll learn; everyone is wet behind the ears at first.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: green - inexperienced or new to something
  • Synonym: naive - lacking experience, wisdom, or judgement
  • Antonym: seasoned - having a lot of experience in a particular activity or job

Etymology

Refers to newborn animals, especially calves, whose ears are still wet, suggesting immaturity.
