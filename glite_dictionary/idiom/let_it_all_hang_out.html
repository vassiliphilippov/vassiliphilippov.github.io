to let it all hang out

1. VERB

/ lɛt ɪt ɔl hæŋ aʊt / (AmE), / lɛt ɪt ɔːl hæŋ aʊt / (BrE)
to relax completely and behave or express oneself without inhibition
relax and act uninhibitedly
informal

Examples

After a long week of work, she just wanted to let it all hang out with her friends.
The music festival was a place where people could let it all hang out and be themselves.
He rarely gets a chance to let it all hang out, so vacations are important to him.

Synset Examples

On vacation, he decided to let it all hang out and relax.
At the party, everyone was encouraged to let it all hang out.
She danced like no one was watching, letting it all hang out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unwind - to relax after tension or work
  • Synonym: to let one's hair down - to behave in a relaxed or uninhibited way
