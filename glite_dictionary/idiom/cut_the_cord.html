to cut the cord

1. VERB

/ kʌt ðə kɔrd / (AmE), / kʌt ðə kɔːd / (BrE)
to end a relationship or dependency, typically in order to become independent
end a dependency to gain independence

Examples

After years of relying on her parents, she knew it was time to cut the cord and move out.
Many people are choosing to cut the cord from traditional television services in favor of streaming platforms.
He found it difficult to cut the cord with his former business partner, but he knew it was necessary.

Synset Examples

She finally decided to cut the cord on her toxic friendship.
He felt relieved after he cut the cord with his old job.
It's time to cut the cord and live independently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break free - to escape from control or constraint
  • Synonym: to detach - to separate or remove from something
  • Antonym: to cling to - to remain attached or dependent on something

Etymology

Originally referred to the literal cutting of the umbilical cord at birth, now used figuratively for achieving independence.
