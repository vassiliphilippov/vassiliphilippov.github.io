not being funny

1. X

/ nɑt ˈbiɪŋ ˈfʌni / (AmE), / nɒt ˈbiːɪŋ ˈfʌni / (BrE)
used to emphasize that what one is saying is serious and not meant as a joke, often in British English
used to stress sincerity, not joking
british informal

Examples

Not being funny, but I really think you should see a doctor about that cough.
Honestly, not being funny, but your driving made me nervous.
Not being funny, I just don't think that's a good idea.

Synset Examples

I'm not being funny, but that was really dangerous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seriously - in a sincere or earnest manner
  • Antonym: just kidding - used to indicate that a previous statement was a joke
