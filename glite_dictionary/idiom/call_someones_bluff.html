to call someone's bluff

1. VERB

/ kɔl sʌmwʌnz blʌf / (AmE), / kɔːl sʌmwʌnz blʌf / (BrE)
to challenge someone to prove the truth of what they are claiming, usually by demanding that they follow through on a threat or boast
challenge someone to prove a claim

Examples

The manager decided to call his bluff and asked him to present his findings to the board.
She threatened to quit, but her boss called her bluff and said she was free to leave if she wanted.
During the negotiation, the agent tried to call their bluff by insisting on seeing the competing offer.

Synset Examples

John called Mary's bluff when she claimed she could prove the theorem in under an hour

Related Terms

  • Synonym: challenge - to invite someone to prove or justify something

Etymology

From the poker term 'bluff', meaning to pretend to have a strong hand.

2. VERB

/ kɔl ˈsʌmwʌnz blʌf / (AmE), / kɔːl ˈsʌmwʌnz blʌf / (BrE)
to challenge someone to prove the truth of what they are saying or claiming, expecting that they are deceiving or exaggerating
challenge someone to prove a claim, suspecting deception

Examples

When I threatened to quit, my boss decided to call my bluff and didn't try to stop me.
He likes to boast, but if you call his bluff, he usually backs down.
She called their bluff by demanding proof of their accusations.

Synset Examples

I decided to call his bluff and challenged him.
He hesitated, and I knew I could call his bluff.
She was confident enough to call his bluff during the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluff - pretend to have a stronger position or more resources than is actually the case

Etymology

From poker, where 'bluff' means pretending to have better cards; 'call' means challenging a bet to reveal cards.
