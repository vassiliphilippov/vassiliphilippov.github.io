to skip a beat

1. VERB

/ skɪp ə bit / (AmE), / skɪp ə biːt / (BrE)
(of a heart) to seem to stop briefly because of sudden excitement, fear, or strong emotion.
(of a heart) seem to stop briefly from emotion

Examples

When he called out my name unexpectedly, my heart skipped a beat.
Her heart skipped a beat as she opened the long-awaited letter.
Seeing the car swerve suddenly made my heart skip a beat.

Synset Examples

The news made my heart skip a beat.
My heart skipped a beat when I saw her.
He almost skipped a beat after hearing the surprise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miss a beat - to fail to react or show surprise, often used in similar emotional contexts
