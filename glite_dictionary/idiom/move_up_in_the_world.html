to move up in the world

1. VERB

/ muːv ʌp ɪn ðə ˈwɝːld / (AmE), / muːv ʌp ɪn ðə ˈwɜːld / (BrE)
to become more successful or improve one's social, economic, or professional position
become more successful in life

Examples

After getting promoted, she really started to move up in the world.
Many people dream of buying a bigger house as they move up in the world.
He hoped that earning a degree would help him move up in the world.

Synset Examples

She hopes to move up in the world by networking.
He worked hard to move up in the world.
After years of effort, he finally started to move up in the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get ahead - to become successful, typically by making progress in your work or life
  • Antonym: to fall behind - to become less successful or make less progress than others
