in tune

1. ADJ

/ ɪn tun / (AmE), / ɪn tjuːn / (BrE)
in agreement or harmony with someone or something
in agreement or harmony

Examples

The manager and her team were in tune throughout the meeting, making decisions quickly.
His ideas are not in tune with the current trends in education.
The partners stayed in tune on the goals for the new business.

Synset Examples

They worked in tune to finish the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in sync - in a state where two or more people or things agree or move together harmoniously
  • Antonym: at odds - in conflict or disagreement
