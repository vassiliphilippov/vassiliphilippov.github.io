in vain

1. ADV

/ ɪn veɪn / (AmE), / ɪn veɪn / (BrE)
without success; without producing any result or effect
without success; fruitlessly

Examples

She searched for her lost keys in vain despite hours of effort.
The rescue team worked in vain; there were no survivors found.
He tried in vain to convince his parents to let him stay out late.

Synset Examples

he looked for her in vain
the city fathers tried vainly to find a solution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vainly - in a way that produces no result; fruitlessly
  • Antonym: successfully - in a way that achieves the desired result

Etymology

From the word 'vain', meaning 'having no real value or effect', ultimately from Latin 'vanus' (empty, idle).

2. ADV

/ ɪn veɪn / (AmE), / ɪn veɪn / (BrE)
without achieving the desired result; unsuccessfully
without success; unsuccessfully

Examples

Despite hours of negotiation, the diplomats worked in vain to resolve the crisis.
She tried in vain to start the car on the cold morning.
He searched in vain for his lost keys.

Synset Examples

He searched in vain for his lost keys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unsuccessfully - without achieving success
  • Antonym: successfully - in a way that achieves the desired result
