to move the needle

1. VERB

/ muːv ðə ˈniː.dəl / (AmE), / muːv ðə ˈniː.dl̩ / (BrE)
to have a noticeable or significant effect or impact, especially in a measurable way
make a significant impact or change

Examples

The government is looking for policies that will truly move the needle on economic growth.
Introducing new technology helped move the needle in healthcare efficiency.
His bold decision finally moved the needle for the entire team.

Synset Examples

This initiative is designed to move the needle on productivity.

We need a strategy that will really move the needle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make a difference - to have an important effect on something
  • Synonym: to leave a mark - to have a lasting impact on a situation
