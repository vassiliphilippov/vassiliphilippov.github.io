to fall into place

1. VERB

/ fɔl ˈɪntu pleɪs / (AmE), / fɔːl ˈɪntuː pleɪs / (BrE)
to become clear, understandable, or easy to comprehend; to make sense after a period of confusion or uncertainty
become clear or understandable

Examples

After hours of studying, all the details finally fell into place and she understood the topic.
When I met his family, everything about his behavior fell into place.
The clues seemed random at first, but then they fell into place and the mystery was solved.

Synset Examples

It dawned on him that she had betrayed him
she was penetrated with sorrow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make sense - to be clear, logical, or easy to understand
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00593150-v

2. VERB

/ fɔl ˈɪntu pleɪs / (AmE), / fɔːl ˈɪntuː pleɪs / (BrE)
(of events, information, or ideas) to start to become clear, organized, or happen in a satisfactory way, often after confusion or uncertainty.
become clear or organized; happen as intended

Examples

After weeks of confusion, the solution finally fell into place for the entire team.
Her research notes didn't make sense at first, but eventually they fell into place.
Once the missing piece was found, everything in the puzzle fell into place.

Synset Examples

Her ideas began to fall into place during the discussion.
Once he got the job, all his plans fell into place.
After weeks of planning, everything finally fell into place.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to come together - (of plans or events) to work out successfully or become organized
