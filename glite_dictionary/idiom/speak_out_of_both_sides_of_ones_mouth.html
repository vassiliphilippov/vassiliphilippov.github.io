to speak out of both sides of one's mouth

1. VERB

/ spiːk aʊt əv boʊθ saɪdz əv wʌnz maʊθ / (AmE), / spiːk aʊt əv bəʊθ saɪdz əv wʌnz maʊθ / (BrE)
to make contradictory or hypocritical statements; to act in a deceitful or insincere way
to be hypocritical or deceitful

Examples

Some leaders speak out of both sides of their mouth to appeal to different audiences.
You can't trust people who speak out of both sides of their mouth about important issues.
He was accused of speaking out of both sides of his mouth during the negotiations.

Synset Examples

Politicians often speak out of both sides of their mouth.
She accused him of talking out of both sides of his mouth.
He talks out of both sides of his mouth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypocritical - pretending to have virtues or beliefs one does not possess
  • Synonym: deceitful - dishonest; misleading or untruthful
