to push the envelope

1. VERB

/ pʊʃ ðə ˈɛn.və.loʊp / (AmE), / pʊʃ ði ˈɛn.və.ləʊp / (BrE)
to approach or exceed the limits of what is currently possible, especially by being innovative or bold
go beyond accepted limits; innovate

Examples

Her bold choreography continues to push the envelope in the world of contemporary dance.
This software company strives to push the envelope with every new product they develop.
As a director, she likes to push the envelope by exploring unconventional narratives.

Synset Examples

They aim to push the envelope and redefine what’s possible in tech.
The artist always tries to push the envelope with her innovative designs.
He loves to push the envelope in his scientific research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break new ground - to do something innovative or previously unattempted
  • Degree Higher: to go too far - to exceed reasonable or accepted limits

Etymology

Originally from aviation slang in the late 20th century, referring to tests that go beyond performance limits (envelope) of an aircraft.
