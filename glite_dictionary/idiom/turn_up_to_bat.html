turn up to bat

1. NOUN

/ tɝn ʌp tə bæt / (AmE), / tɜːn ʌp tə bæt / (BrE)
one's chance or opportunity to take action or participate, especially after waiting one's turn.
one's turn to act or participate

Examples

After months of preparation, it's finally my turn up to bat at the big meeting.
When the lead actor got sick, her understudy had her turn up to bat.
Everyone was nervous until it was their turn up to bat in the debate.

Synset Examples

It's my turn up to bat during the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to step up to the plate - to take responsibility or act when needed

Etymology

From baseball, where a player's 'turn at bat' means their chance to hit.
