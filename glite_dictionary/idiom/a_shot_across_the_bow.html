a shot across the bow

1. NOUN

/ ə ʃɑt əˈkrɔs ðə baʊ / (AmE), / ə ʃɒt əˈkrɒs ðə baʊ / (BrE)
an action or remark intended as an early warning or threat to signal that more serious measures may follow
early warning or threat

Examples

The manager's comment was a shot across the bow to staff who were underperforming.
Her sudden move to a rival firm was seen as a shot across the bow by her current employers.
The editorial served as a shot across the bow to politicians planning to cut education funding.

Synset Examples

The manager's warning was a shot across the bow.
Her resignation was a shot across the bow to the board.
The criticism was a shot across the bow for the team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warning shot - an action or statement intended to warn someone

Etymology

Originally a naval term describing a warning shot fired across the front of another ship to signal it to stop or change course.
