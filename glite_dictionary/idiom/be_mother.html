to be mother

1. VERB

/ bi ˈmʌðɚ / (AmE), / bi ˈmʌðə / (BrE)
to act in a nurturing or caring role, especially by serving others (traditionally used when serving tea)
act as caretaker or server (esp. tea)
british

Examples

At tea time, someone always asks, 'Who will be mother and pour the tea?'
She likes to be mother whenever friends come over for a chat.
During the meeting, Tom volunteered to be mother and handed out the coffee.

Synset Examples

In our circle, she tends to be mother when we gather.
To be mother means to care for others deeply.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nurture - to care for and encourage the growth of someone

Etymology

Traditional British usage, based on the role of a mother in serving or caring for others.
