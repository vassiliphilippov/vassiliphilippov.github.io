to take the low road

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə loʊ roʊd / (AmE), / teɪk ðə ləʊ rəʊd / (BrE)
to choose an action or approach that is less moral or less honorable, often for personal gain or advantage
choose a less moral option

Examples

Even under pressure, she refused to take the low road in business negotiations.
Some politicians will take the low road to win votes, resorting to smear campaigns.
He regretted his decision to take the low road instead of acting honestly.

Synset Examples

She refused to take the low road during the argument.
He took the low road by spreading rumors about her.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to take the high road - to act with integrity and honor, especially when others do not
