to give someone the brush-off

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ˈsʌmwʌn ðə ˈbrʌʃ ɒf / (AmE), / ɡɪv ˈsʌmwʌn ðə ˈbrʌʃ ɒf / (BrE)
to dismiss or reject someone in a brusque or unfriendly manner
dismiss or reject someone abruptly

Examples

After months of waiting for his response, the manager finally gave him the brush-off in a curt email.
When I tried to get advice from my professor, she just gave me the brush-off without any explanation.
Many applicants felt the company gave them the brush-off after the first round of interviews.

Synset Examples

He tried to ask her out, but she just gave him the brush-off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - to send someone away or refuse to consider them
  • Synonym: rebuff - to reject someone or something in an abrupt way
  • Antonym: welcome - to greet or receive someone in a friendly way

Etymology

Probably from the literal act of brushing something away to indicate dismissal, attested in early 20th-century American English.
