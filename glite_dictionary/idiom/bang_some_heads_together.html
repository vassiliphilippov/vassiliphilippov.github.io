to bang some heads together

1. VERB

/ bæŋ səm hɛdz təˈɡɛðɚ / (AmE), / bæŋ səm hɛdz təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to force people to cooperate by confronting or reprimanding them firmly
force people to cooperate

Examples

If the team can't reach a decision soon, the manager may have to bang some heads together.
Sometimes a teacher must bang some heads together to get a group project moving.
The director threatened to bang some heads together if the departments couldn't cooperate.

Synset Examples

Sometimes you just have to bang some heads together for results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knock some heads together - to force people to cooperate by strong action
  • Degree Lower: mediate - to intervene to help people agree
