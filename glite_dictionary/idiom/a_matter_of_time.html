a matter of time

1. NOUN

/ ə ˈmætər əv taɪm / (AmE), / ə ˈmætə əv taɪm / (BrE)
used to say that something will definitely happen, although you do not know exactly when
inevitably going to happen

Examples

With their talent and determination, winning is only a matter of time.
It's just a matter of time before he finds out the truth.
Given market trends, a merger is a matter of time.

Synset Examples

He'll apologize; it's just a matter of time.
It's only a matter of time before she succeeds.
Winning the championship is just a matter of time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inevitable - certain to happen; unavoidable
  • Degree Higher: imminent - about to happen very soon
