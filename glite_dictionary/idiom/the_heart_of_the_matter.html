the heart of the matter

1. NOUN

/ ðə hɑrt əv ðə ˈmætɚ / (AmE), / ðə hɑːt əv ðə ˈmætə / (BrE)
the most important or central point of an issue or discussion
the main or central issue

Examples

Let's get to the heart of the matter and address what's really important.
She always has a way of reaching the heart of the matter in any discussion.
After hours of debate, they finally touched on the heart of the matter.

Synset Examples

To get to the heart of the matter, that's the bunny.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the crux of the matter - the decisive or most important point at issue
  • Synonym: the essence - the intrinsic nature or indispensable quality of something, especially something abstract
  • Antonym: periphery - the outer limits or edge of an area or object; opposite of the central point

Etymology

From metaphorical use of 'heart' to mean the central or vital part.
