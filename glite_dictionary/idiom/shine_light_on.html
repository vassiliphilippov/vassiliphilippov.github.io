to shine light on

1. VERB

/ ʃaɪn laɪt ɑn / (AmE), / ʃaɪn laɪt ɒn / (BrE)
to make something clear or reveal information that was previously hidden or unknown
clarify or reveal hidden information

Examples

The journalist's investigation helped shine light on the government’s secret activities.
The documentary aims to shine light on the challenges faced by refugees.
New scientific research could shine light on the causes of the disease.

Synset Examples

The investigation will shine light on the corruption.
They hope to shine light on mental health awareness.
The report will shine light on the company’s practices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to illuminate - to make something clear or easier to understand
  • Antonym: obscure - to make something less clear or harder to understand
