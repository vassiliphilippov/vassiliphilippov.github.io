snuff-and-butter

1. NOUN

/ snʌf ənd ˈbʌtər / (AmE), / snʌf ənd ˈbʌtə / (BrE)
Used to describe something trivial, ordinary, or characterized by superficial conversation.
trivial or superficial matter

Examples

The discussion was just snuff-and-butter, with nothing new or insightful shared.
Most of the articles in the magazine are snuff-and-butter, lacking any real depth.
He dismissed their complaints as mere snuff-and-butter, not worth his attention.

Synset Examples

The project was just snuff-and-butter, nothing special.
He described the meeting as snuff-and-butter fluff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: small talk - polite or trivial conversation
  • Synonym: run of the mill - ordinary or unremarkable; not special
  • Antonym: extraordinary - very unusual or remarkable
