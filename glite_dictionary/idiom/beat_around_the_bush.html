to beat around the bush

1. VERB

/ biːt əˈraʊnd ðə bʊʃ / (AmE), / biːt əˈraʊnd ðə bʊʃ / (BrE)
to avoid talking directly about the main topic, often to delay or evade giving a clear answer
avoid speaking directly

Examples

Stop beating around the bush and answer my question honestly.
Whenever I ask about his plans, he just beats around the bush instead of telling me directly.
Instead of beating around the bush, she finally admitted her mistake.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to get to the point - to address the main topic directly

Etymology

Originally from hunting terminology in medieval England, where beaters would literally beat bushes to flush out game, avoiding moving directly towards the center.

2. VERB

/ biːt əˈraʊnd ðə bʊʃ / (AmE), / biːt əˈraʊnd ðə bʊʃ / (BrE)
to avoid talking about something directly; to delay or avoid stating the main point
avoid getting to the point (idiom)

Examples

Please don't beat around the bush; just tell me the truth.
She kept beating around the bush instead of answering the question.
Whenever he's nervous, he tends to beat around the bush during meetings.

Synset Examples

Let’s not beat around the bush; we need to make a decision.
He tends to beat around the bush instead of giving a straight answer.
Stop beating around the bush and tell me what happened.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to get to the point - to speak directly or state something plainly

Etymology

Possibly from hunting, where beating bushes flushes out game indirectly.
