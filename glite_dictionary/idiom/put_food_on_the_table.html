to put food on the table

1. VERB

/ pʊt fuːd ɑn ðə ˈteɪbəl / (AmE), / pʊt fuːd ɒn ðə ˈteɪbl̩ / (BrE)
to earn enough money to support oneself or one's family and meet basic living needs
earn income to support basic needs

Examples

She worked late every night to put food on the table for her children.
Many people take on extra jobs just to put food on the table during tough times.
His primary motivation is to put food on the table, not to seek fame or fortune.

Synset Examples

The overtime shifts help him put food on the table.
His main goal is to put food on the table for his family.
She works two jobs to put food on the table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make ends meet - to have enough money to cover essential expenses
