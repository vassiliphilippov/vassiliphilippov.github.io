in a tailspin

1. ADP

/ ɪn ə ˈteɪlspɪn / (AmE), / ɪn ə ˈteɪlspɪn / (BrE)
in a state of sudden confusion, panic, or crisis, often resulting in loss of control or stability
in a state of crisis or confusion

Examples

After the sudden resignation of the CEO, the entire department was in a tailspin.
When the stock market crashed, investors found themselves in a tailspin of uncertainty.
The breakup left him in a tailspin, struggling to make sense of everything.

Synset Examples

The company's finances are in a tailspin.
She felt her emotions in a tailspin after the breakup.
After losing the job, he went into a tailspin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in a spin - in a state of agitation or confusion
  • Antonym: in control - having control or stability

Etymology

Originally from aviation, where 'tailspin' describes a steep, spiraling descent of an aircraft out of control.
