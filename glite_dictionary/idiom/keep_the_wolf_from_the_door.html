to keep the wolf from the door

1. VERB

/ kip ðə wʊlf frəm ðə dɔɹ / (AmE), / kiːp ðə wʊlf frəm ðə dɔː / (BrE)
to manage to avoid hunger or extreme poverty, often by making just enough money or resources to survive
avoid extreme poverty or hunger

Examples

During hard times, she took on extra work to keep the wolf from the door.
He sold his paintings just to keep the wolf from the door.
Even small savings can help many families keep the wolf from the door during difficult winters.

Synset Examples

He took on two jobs to keep the wolf from the door.
Her savings helped keep the wolf from the door last winter.
They sold their car to keep the wolf from the door.

Etymology

Refers metaphorically to a wolf representing hunger or poverty lurking at one's door, common in English since the 18th century.
