to make the grade

1. VERB

/ meɪk ðə ɡreɪd / (AmE), / meɪk ðə ɡreɪd / (BrE)
to reach the necessary standard; to be good enough to succeed, especially in a particular activity or field
reach the required standard

Examples

Despite his efforts, Tom didn’t make the grade in the final round of interviews.
It takes years of training to make the grade as a professional athlete.
If your proposal doesn’t make the grade, it will be rejected by the committee.

Synset Examples

If you don’t work harder, you won’t make the grade.
She always manages to make the grade.
He studied all night to make the grade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to measure up - to reach the required standard or expectation
  • Synonym: to cut it - to succeed or be adequate for something required
  • Antonym: to fall short - to fail to reach the standard or expectation
