on the horn

1. ADV

/ ɑn ðə hɔrn / (AmE), / ɒn ðə hɔːn / (BrE)
talking on the telephone; using the phone to speak with someone
talking on the telephone
informal

Examples

She promised to get on the horn with the manager right away.
Let me get on the horn and find out what's happening with your order.
He spent the morning on the horn discussing the new project.

Synset Examples

She was on the horn, negotiating the best deal.
I’ll be on the horn with tech support shortly.
He was on the horn with the client for hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on the phone - speaking on the telephone

Etymology

Refers to early telephones, which were known as 'horns' due to their horn-shaped speaking and listening tubes.
