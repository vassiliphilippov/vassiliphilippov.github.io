to cut one's coat according to one's cloth

1. VERB

/ kʌt wʌnz koʊt əˈkɔr.dɪŋ tu wʌnz klɔθ / (AmE), / kʌt wʌnz kəʊt əˈkɔː.dɪŋ tʊ wʌnz klɒθ / (BrE)
to live within one's means; to act according to the resources available
live within one's means

Examples

Learning to cut one's coat according to one's cloth helped Sarah manage her finances effectively.
We must cut our coat according to our cloth if we want to avoid running into debt.
He advised his children to cut their coat according to their cloth and not overspend.

Synset Examples

He always cuts his coat according to his cloth to avoid debt.
She decided to cut her coat according to her cloth when budgeting for the holiday.
To stay within budget, they cut their coat according to their cloth.
You must cut your cloth to suit your purse when budgeting.
If you want to save, cut your cloth to suit your purse.

Etymology

Proverbial phrase referring to tailoring a coat to fit the available cloth, first recorded in English in the 16th century.
