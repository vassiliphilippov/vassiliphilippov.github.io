the way of the world

1. NOUN

/ ðə weɪ əv ðə wɝːld / (AmE), / ðə weɪ əv ðə wɜːld / (BrE)
the manner in which people usually behave or things generally happen, regarded as a typical or accepted norm
typical manner in which things happen

Examples

She quickly learned the way of the world after moving to a big city.
It's just the way of the world that people come and go from our lives.
Despite his age, he seemed innocent of the way of the world.

Synset Examples

the ordinary reader is endowed with considerable wisdom and knowledge of the way of the world
she was well-versed in the ways of the world before she had taken the veil
he was amazingly innocent of the ways of the world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: custom - an accepted or habitual practice
  • Synonym: normal - conforming to a standard; usual or expected
