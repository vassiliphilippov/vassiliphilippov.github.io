kiss of life

1. NOUN

/ kɪs əv laɪf / (AmE), / kɪs əv laɪf / (BrE)
an action or influence that revitalizes or rescues something from failure or decline
action that revitalizes or saves

Examples

The government grant was the kiss of life the local theater needed to avoid closure.
His passionate speech gave the campaign a much-needed kiss of life.
The new manager's fresh ideas proved to be the kiss of life for the struggling company.

Synset Examples

He gave the project a kiss of life with fresh ideas.
Her encouragement was the kiss of life I needed.
A good mentor can be the kiss of life for students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lifeline - something that provides help or rescue in a difficult situation
  • Antonym: death knell - an action or event signaling the end of something

Etymology

Originally referring to a term for mouth-to-mouth resuscitation, later extended metaphorically to other contexts.
