low blow

1. NOUN

/ loʊ bloʊ / (AmE), / ləʊ bləʊ / (BrE)
an unfair or unethical remark, criticism, or act intended to hurt someone; an unscrupulous abuse
unfair or hurtful remark/action

Examples

His rude comment about her appearance was a low blow during the meeting.
Bringing up his past mistakes was a low blow and completely unnecessary.
Calling her out in front of everyone felt like a low blow to him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheap shot - an unfair or unsporting criticism or attack
  • Synonym: underhanded - acting or done in a secret or dishonest way
  • Antonym: fair play - behavior that is fair, honest, and follows the rules

Etymology

From boxing, where a punch below the belt is illegal and considered unfair.
