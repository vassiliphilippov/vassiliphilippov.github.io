to raise hackles

1. VERB

/ ˈreɪz ˈhækəlz / (AmE), / ˈreɪz ˈhækəlz / (BrE)
to cause someone to become angry or irritated; to provoke annoyance or hostile feelings
provoke anger or irritation

Examples

His dismissive remarks raised hackles among the committee members.
The company's decision to cut benefits has raised hackles throughout the workforce.
Bringing up that subject is sure to raise hackles at the dinner table.

Synset Examples

His rude comments raised her hackles immediately.
The proposal raised his hackles due to its implications.
That topic always raises her hackles during discussions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to provoke - to make someone angry, especially deliberately
  • Antonym: calm - to make someone less angry or upset

Etymology

Derived from the idea of an animal's hackles (erectile hairs on the back of the neck) rising when threatened or angry.
