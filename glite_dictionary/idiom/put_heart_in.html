to put heart in

1. VERB

/ pʊt hɑrt ɪn / (AmE), / pʊt hɑːt ɪn / (BrE)
to invest deep sincere emotion or effort into something
invest deep emotion or effort

Examples

She always put heart in her volunteer work, making a real difference to those around her.
If you put heart in your studies, you'll see much better results.
The chef put heart in every dish, and it showed in the flavors.

Synset Examples

She really put her heart in her music.
He put his heart in every piece he painted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: put one's heart into - to do something with sincere effort or enthusiasm
