on the hook

1. ADJ

/ ɑn ðə hʊk / (AmE), / ɒn ðə hʊk / (BrE)
in a difficult, dangerous, or obligated situation, often one requiring a solution or repayment
in a difficult or obligated situation

Examples

After agreeing to cosign the loan, she found herself on the hook for her friend's debt.
He's on the hook to finish the report by Monday or risk disappointing the entire team.
Once the police discovered the evidence, he realized he was on the hook for the crime.

Synset Examples

there I was back on the hook

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in a bind - in a difficult situation
  • Antonym: off the hook - freed from difficulty or obligation

2. ADJ

/ ɑn ðə hʊk / (AmE), / ɒn ðə hʊk / (BrE)
responsible for something, especially in a way that one is obliged to do or pay something; liable or obligated
responsible or obliged; liable

Examples

After their mistake, the team was on the hook for the cost of repairs.
She realized she was on the hook to finish the project by next week.
If the client cancels last minute, we're still on the hook for the venue fees.

Synset Examples

He felt on the hook after missing the deadline.
I'm on the hook for organizing the event this year.
The company is on the hook for the damages.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: off the hook - no longer responsible or obliged
