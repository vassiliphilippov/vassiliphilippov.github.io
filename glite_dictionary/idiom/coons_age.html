coon's age

1. NOUN

/ kunz eɪdʒ / (AmE), / kuːnz eɪdʒ / (BrE)
a very long time; an extended period since something last happened
a very long time
informal archaic

Examples

I haven't seen Jenny in a coon's age—how has she been?
It's been a coon's age since anyone used that old phone.
He felt like he waited a coon's age for his food to arrive.

Synset Examples

I feel like I've waited a coon's age for this.
I haven't seen him in a coon's age!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ages - a very long time
  • Synonym: forever - a very long or seemingly endless time

Etymology

From 'coon', an abbreviation of 'raccoon', based on the belief that raccoons live a long time.
