how are you diddling

1. X

/ haʊ ɑːr juː ˈdɪdlɪŋ / (AmE), / haʊ ɑː juː ˈdɪdlɪŋ / (BrE)
(informal, British) An informal way of asking someone how they are doing or feeling.
informal greeting; how are you?
informal british

Examples

Whenever he sees me, he greets me with a cheerful 'how are you diddling?'
After months apart, Sarah called and said, 'how are you diddling these days?'
It's a phrase my grandfather always used: 'how are you diddling?'.

Synset Examples

He asked me casually, how are you diddling?
It's been a while, how are you diddling these days?
I haven't seen you in ages, how are you diddling?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: how are you doing - informal greeting; asking about someone's wellbeing
