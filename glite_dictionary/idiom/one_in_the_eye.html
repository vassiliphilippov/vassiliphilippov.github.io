one in the eye

1. NOUN

/ wʌn ɪn ði aɪ / (AmE), / wʌn ɪn ðiː aɪ / (BrE)
A defeat, setback, or source of disappointment to someone, often used when an outcome goes against another's expectations or hopes.
a setback or disappointment (to someone else)

Examples

His promotion was one in the eye for those who underestimated him.
The underdog's victory provided one in the eye for their critics.
Missing the deadline was one in the eye for the project's supporters.

Synset Examples

Losing the match was one in the eye for the rivals.
His success was one in the eye for doubters.
