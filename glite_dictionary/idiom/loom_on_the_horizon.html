to loom on the horizon

1. VERB

/ lum ɑn ðə həˈraɪzən / (AmE), / luːm ɒn ði həˈraɪzən / (BrE)
(of an event or danger) to seem likely to happen soon and cause worry or concern.
be likely to happen soon, causing concern

Examples

Rumors of layoffs began to loom on the horizon, making everyone at the office anxious.
With exams looming on the horizon, the students started studying more seriously.
Economic problems loomed on the horizon, threatening to disrupt the country's recovery.

Synset Examples

A storm began to loom on the horizon.
