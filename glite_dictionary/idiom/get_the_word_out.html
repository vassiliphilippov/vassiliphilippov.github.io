to get the word out

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ðə wɝd aʊt / (AmE), / ɡɛt ðə wɜːd aʊt / (BrE)
to inform a large number of people about something; to make something widely known
make information widely known

Examples

We need to get the word out about the new policy as soon as possible.
The marketing team worked hard to get the word out before the launch.
She asked all her friends to help get the word out about the missing pet.

Synset Examples

She used social media to get the word out quickly.
They held a press conference to get the word out.
We need to get the word out about the charity event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spread the word - to pass information on to others
