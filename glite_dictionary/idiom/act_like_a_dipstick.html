to act like a dipstick

1. VERB

/ ækt laɪk ə ˈdɪpˌstɪk / (AmE), / ækt laɪk ə ˈdɪp.stɪk / (BrE)
to behave in a foolish or clumsy way; to act stupidly
behave foolishly or clumsily
informal

Examples

Don’t act like a dipstick—think before you speak next time.
I really acted like a dipstick when I tripped over nothing in front of everyone.
She was acting like a dipstick all day, making silly mistakes.

Synset Examples

He acted like a dipstick when he forgot his keys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: act like a fool - to behave foolishly or stupidly
  • Antonym: act sensibly - to behave in a reasonable or intelligent way
