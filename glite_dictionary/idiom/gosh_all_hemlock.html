gosh all hemlock

1. INTJ

/ ɡɑʃ ɔl ˈhɛmlɑk / (AmE), / ɡɒʃ ɔːl ˈhɛmlɒk / (BrE)
used to express surprise, disbelief, or frustration.
expression of surprise or frustration
archaic american

Examples

Gosh all hemlock, I can't believe I missed the last bus!
Gosh all hemlock, you startled me when you walked in so quietly.
He dropped the heavy box on his foot and shouted, "gosh all hemlock!"

Synset Examples

Gosh all hemlock, I forgot my wallet again.
Gosh all hemlock, the weather really turned bad.
Gosh all hemlock, that was a close call!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: good grief - an exclamation of surprise or dismay

Etymology

Euphemistic alteration of 'God' to 'gosh'; 'hemlock' added for emphasis, popular in 19th-century American English.
