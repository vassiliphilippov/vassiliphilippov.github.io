name is mud

1. X

/ ˈneɪm ɪz mʌd / (AmE), / ˈneɪm ɪz mʌd / (BrE)
said of someone who has a bad reputation or is in disgrace, typically due to a mistake or wrongdoing
to be in disgrace or disfavor

Examples

Ever since the incident at work, his name is mud with the management.
After being caught cheating, her name is mud among her classmates.
If you forget their anniversary again, your name is mud at home.

Synset Examples

With that error, his name is mud in the office.
After that mistake, his name is mud with the boss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persona non grata - an unwelcome or unacceptable person
  • Antonym: in favor - having approval or high regard

Etymology

Believed to have originated in 19th-century America; may refer to Dr. Samuel Mudd, who treated John Wilkes Booth, Lincoln's assassin, but this connection is debated.
