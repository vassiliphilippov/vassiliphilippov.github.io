by a long shot

1. ADV

/ baɪ ə lɔŋ ʃɑt / (AmE), / baɪ ə lɒŋ ʃɒt / (BrE)
by a large amount; by far
by a large amount

Examples

She finished the race first, by a long shot, ahead of all the other competitors.
This is the best pizza in town, by a long shot.
He didn't win the award, not by a long shot.

Synset Examples

he is the best by a long shot
his labors haven't ended there — not by a long shot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: by far - to a great degree; by a wide margin
  • Antonym: by no means - not at all; not in any way

Etymology

Originally an American English idiom, possibly relating to shooting contests where a 'long shot' is an improbable success.
