can of corn

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæn əv ˈkɔrn / (AmE), / ˌkæn əv ˈkɔːn / (BrE)
an easy task or an easy catch, especially in baseball
something easy or effortless
american informal

Examples

The presentation went smoothly; it was a can of corn.
With the bases loaded, the final out was a can of corn to center field.
“Don’t worry about this exam,” he said. “It’ll be a can of corn for you.”

Synset Examples

The presentation went smoothly; it was a can of corn.
That catch was a can of corn for the outfielder.
Hitting that target was a can of corn for him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piece of cake - something very easy to do
  • Synonym: walk in the park - a task that is very easy or uncomplicated
  • Antonym: challenge - something that is difficult or tests a person's abilities

Etymology

American baseball slang, origin uncertain; possibly from grocery clerks using a stick to tip canned goods from high shelves for easy catching.
