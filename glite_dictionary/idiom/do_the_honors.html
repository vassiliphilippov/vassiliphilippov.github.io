to do the honors

1. VERB

/ du ði ˈɑnɚz / (AmE), / duː ði ˈɒnəz / (BrE)
to act as host by receiving or introducing guests, or to perform a social or ceremonial task on behalf of others
act as host or perform a ceremonial task

Examples

Would you please do the honors and introduce our speaker for the evening?
At the ribbon-cutting ceremony, the mayor was asked to do the honors.
After dinner, John offered to do the honors and pour everyone some coffee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: host - a person who receives or entertains guests

2. VERB

/ du ðə ˈɑnɚz / (AmE), / duː ðə ˈɒnəz / (BrE)
to perform a social or ceremonial duty, especially one that marks a special occasion, such as introducing guests or starting an event
perform a ceremonial or social task

Examples

Would you like to do the honors and cut the cake?
She was asked to do the honors of presenting the award to the winner.
At family gatherings, my uncle always does the honors by carving the turkey.
