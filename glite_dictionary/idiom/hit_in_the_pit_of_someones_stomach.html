hit in the pit of someone's stomach

1. X

/ hɪt ɪn ðə pɪt əv ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈstʌmək / (AmE), / hɪt ɪn ðə pɪt əv ˈsʌmwənz ˈstʌmək / (BrE)
(of news or an event) to cause a sudden strong feeling of fear, anxiety, or shock, felt physically in one's stomach.
sudden shock or anxiety felt physically

Examples

When I heard the accident had happened, it hit in the pit of my stomach and I felt sick.
The announcement of layoffs hit him in the pit of his stomach, and he struggled to process the news.
Every time she sees a police car, it hits her in the pit of her stomach because of that old fear.

Synset Examples

The news hit me in the pit of my stomach.
