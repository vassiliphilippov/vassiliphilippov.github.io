to play hob

1. VERB

/ pleɪ hɑb / (AmE), / pleɪ hɒb / (BrE)
to disrupt or interfere with something, causing trouble or confusion
cause disruption or trouble

Examples

The sudden power outage will play hob with our production schedule.
Too many last-minute changes may play hob with the team's preparations.
Dust and humidity can play hob with delicate electronics.

Synset Examples

The storm will play hob with our travel plans.
His constant distractions play hob with my concentration.
The new software update might play hob with the system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disrupt - to interrupt or disturb an activity or process
  • Synonym: to interfere - to become involved in something so as to hinder or alter it
  • Antonym: to facilitate - to make an action or process easier

Etymology

Hob refers to a mischievous sprite in folklore; the phrase dates from the mid-19th century.
