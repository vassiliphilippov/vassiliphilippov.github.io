to feel one's way

1. VERB

/ fil wʌnz weɪ / (AmE), / fiːl wʌnz weɪ / (BrE)
to proceed carefully or gradually, often without clear guidance, by exploring or testing the situation step by step
proceed cautiously or tentatively

Examples

In the absence of clear instructions, Maria had to feel her way through the entire process.
He felt his way around the pitch-black hallway, searching for a light switch.
As a new manager, she is still feeling her way in the company.

Synset Examples

I had to feel my way through the dark room.
He tried to feel his way around the unfamiliar city.
She decided to feel her way into the new job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grope - to feel about or search blindly or uncertainly
