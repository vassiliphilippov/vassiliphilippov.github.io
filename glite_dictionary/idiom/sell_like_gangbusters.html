to sell like gangbusters

1. VERB

/ sɛl laɪk ˈɡæŋˌbʌstɚz / (AmE), / sɛl laɪk ˈɡæŋˌbʌstəz / (BrE)
to sell extremely quickly or successfully
to sell very fast or successfully
informal american

Examples

The new phone is selling like gangbusters in its first week on the market.
Tickets to the concert sold like gangbusters and were gone within an hour.
Once the advertisement aired, the limited edition sneakers started selling like gangbusters.

Synset Examples

The new product is selling like gangbusters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sell like hotcakes - to sell very quickly and in large quantities

Etymology

American English, from 'gangbusters,' a term evoking great energy or success, originally from a popular radio crime show in the 1930s.
