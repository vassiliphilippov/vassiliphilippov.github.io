to cross the red line

1. VERB

/ krɔs ðə rɛd laɪn / (AmE), / krɒs ðə rɛd laɪn / (BrE)
to go beyond an accepted limit or boundary, especially one that results in serious consequences
go beyond an acceptable limit

Examples

You must not cross the red line in negotiations with your boss.
The politician knew he would cross the red line if he revealed confidential information.
If you cross the red line, there may be serious consequences.

Synset Examples

Don't cross the red line when discussing politics.
They crossed the red line with that last comment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go too far - to exceed acceptable limits; act excessively
  • Synonym: to overstep the mark - to behave in a way that is considered unacceptable
  • Antonym: stay within bounds - to remain inside acceptable limits

Etymology

From the use of 'red line' to mark a point which must not be crossed, metaphorically referring to rules or boundaries.
