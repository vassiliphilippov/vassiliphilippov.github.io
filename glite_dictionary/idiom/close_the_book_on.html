to close the book on

1. VERB

/ kloʊz ðə bʊk ɑn / (AmE), / kləʊz ðə bʊk ɒn / (BrE)
to decide that something has ended and no further action will be taken; to conclude or finish something
conclude or finish something

Examples

After years of investigation, the police were finally able to close the book on the mysterious case.
It's time to close the book on that chapter of our lives and move forward.
Once the contract was signed, the team could close the book on the project.

Synset Examples

After months of negotiations, they finally closed the book on the deal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finish - bring something to an end or completion
  • Synonym: to wrap up - to complete or finish something, especially a project or activity
