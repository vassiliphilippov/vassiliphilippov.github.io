to get the point

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ðə pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ɡɛt ðə pɔɪnt / (BrE)
to understand the main idea or purpose of something that is being said or done
understand the main idea

Examples

It took me a moment to get the point of his story.
Despite her explanations, he just couldn't get the point.
Once you see the bigger picture, you'll finally get the point.

Synset Examples

It took a while, but he started to get the point of the feedback.
After a few minutes, she began to get the point of the lecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to understand - grasp the meaning of something
