to and fro

1. ADV

/ tu ənd froʊ / (AmE), / tuː ən ˈfrəʊ / (BrE)
back and forth; in one direction and then in the opposite, repeatedly
back and forth repeatedly

Examples

The old swing moved to and fro in the gentle breeze.
She watched the dancers glide to and fro across the floor.
After hours of pacing to and fro, he finally made his decision.

Synset Examples

The boat rocked to and fro on the waves.
She paced to and fro, deep in thought.
The kids ran to and fro in the park, laughing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: back and forth - moving alternately in one direction and then in the opposite
