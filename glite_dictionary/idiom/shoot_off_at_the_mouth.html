to shoot off at the mouth

1. VERB

/ ʃuːt ɔf ət ðə maʊθ / (AmE), / ʃuːt ɒf ət ðə maʊθ / (BrE)
to speak impulsively or without thinking, especially in a way that may cause offense or regret
speak impulsively or rashly
informal

Examples

Whenever Mark shoots off at the mouth, he usually ends up in trouble with his boss.
Try not to shoot off at the mouth during the interview, or you might say something you'll regret.
She realized she had shot off at the mouth and apologized for her harsh words.

Synset Examples

When angry, she tends to shoot off at the mouth.
He often shoots off at the mouth without thinking.
You should avoid shooting off at the mouth in meetings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to blurt out - to say something suddenly and impulsively
  • Antonym: to hold one's tongue - to refrain from speaking
