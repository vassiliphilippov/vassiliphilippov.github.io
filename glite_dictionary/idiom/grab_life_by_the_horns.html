to grab life by the horns

1. VERB

/ ɡræb laɪf baɪ ðə hɔrnz / (AmE), / ɡrab laɪf baɪ ði hɔːnz / (BrE)
to deal with life or challenges boldly and proactively; to take control of one's circumstances with determination.
face life boldly; take control

Examples

After years of hesitation, she decided to grab life by the horns and pursue her dream career.
Sometimes you just have to grab life by the horns if you want real change.
He inspired his friends to grab life by the horns and live without regrets.

Synset Examples

She knew it was time to grab life by the horns.
He always believed in grabbing life by the horns.
They decided to grab life by the horns and start their own business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take the bull by the horns - to confront a difficult situation directly and bravely

Etymology

Inspired by the earlier idiom 'take the bull by the horns'.
