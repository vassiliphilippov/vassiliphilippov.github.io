to think on your feet

1. VERB

/ θɪŋk ɑn jər fit / (AmE), / θɪŋk ɒn jə fiːt / (BrE)
to react quickly and effectively to unexpected situations or questions, especially in conversation or under pressure
respond quickly and effectively

Examples

In an interview, you'll often need to think on your feet to answer tough questions.
Emergency responders have to think on their feet in dangerous situations.
When the power went out, she had to think on her feet to keep the event running smoothly.

Synset Examples

Teachers must think on their feet during class.
You need to think on your feet in this fast-paced job.
Being a lawyer requires you to think on your feet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to improvise - to create or perform spontaneously without preparation
