catch-as-catch-can

1. X

/ ˌkætʃ əz ˌkætʃ ˈkæn / (AmE), / ˌkætʃ əz ˌkætʃ ˈkæn / (BrE)
using whatever methods or resources are available; improvised or flexible in approach, often without strict planning.
improvised or flexible method/approach

Examples

During the blackout, cooking meals became a catch-as-catch-can affair for everyone.
With limited supplies, their building process was strictly catch-as-catch-can.
The meeting was scheduled in a catch-as-catch-can fashion as people arrived at different times.

Synset Examples

In this game, it's catch-as-catch-can, so be ready.
Their approach to the project was purely catch-as-catch-can.
We decided to play catch-as-catch-can with the rules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improvised - created or done without preparation; made up as needed
  • Antonym: methodical - done according to a systematic or established procedure

Etymology

Originally used in wrestling in the 19th century to describe a style without fixed rules.
