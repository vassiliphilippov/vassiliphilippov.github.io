screw this

1. INTJ

/ skruː ðɪs / (AmE), / skruː ðɪs / (BrE)
used to express frustration, annoyance, or a sudden decision to stop doing something, often because of dissatisfaction
expression of frustration or quitting
informal

Examples

I'm tired of waiting in this long line—screw this, I'm leaving.
He missed his train again and muttered, "screw this, I'm taking a taxi instead!"
After hours of trying to fix the computer, she finally said, "screw this, I'll deal with it tomorrow."

Synset Examples

After the third delay, I thought, screw this!
Screw this, let’s just go home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forget it - used to indicate that something should be ignored or abandoned
  • Synonym: never mind - used to suggest disregarding something or moving on
