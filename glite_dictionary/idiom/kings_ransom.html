king's ransom

1. NOUN

/ kɪŋz ˈrænsəm / (AmE), / kɪŋz ˈræn.səm / (BrE)
an extremely large amount of money or a very valuable treasure
a very large sum of money

Examples

He sold the painting for a king's ransom at the auction.
It would cost a king's ransom to buy a house in that neighborhood today.
The rare diamond is insured for a king's ransom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortune - a large amount of wealth
  • Antonym: pittance - a very small or inadequate amount of money

Etymology

From the historical practice of demanding large sums as ransom for the release of a captured king.
