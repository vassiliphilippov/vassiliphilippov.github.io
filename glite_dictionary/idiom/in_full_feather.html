in full feather

1. ADV

/ ɪn fʊl ˈfɛðɚ / (AmE), / ɪn fʊl ˈfɛðə / (BrE)
In full plumage or appearance; in high spirits or good condition; dressed very elegantly or stylishly.
in full plumage; in high spirits; dressed elegantly

Examples

The peacocks were in full feather, showing off their brilliant tails.
After a restful holiday, he returned to work in full feather and full of enthusiasm.
All the guests arrived at the gala in full feather, wearing their most glamorous outfits.

Synset Examples

The birds were out in full feather, displaying their vibrant colors.
The team was in full feather, ready to tackle the project.
She arrived at the party in full feather, looking stunning.

Etymology

Draws on the imagery of birds displaying their fullest feathers for display or health.
