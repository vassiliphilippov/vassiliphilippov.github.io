easy come, easy go

1. X

/ ˌiːzi ˈkʌm ˌiːzi ˈɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˌiːzi ˈkʌm ˌiːzi ˈɡəʊ / (BrE)
used to express the idea that something acquired without effort or difficulty can be lost just as easily and should not be a cause for concern.
acquired easily can be lost easily

Examples

She didn't seem upset about losing her job, just shrugged and said, easy come, easy go.
He spent all the lottery money in a month, proving that it's easy come, easy go.
When my favorite mug broke, I tried not to dwell on it—easy come, easy go.

Synset Examples

With her carefree attitude, she lives by easy come, easy go.
He lost his winnings at the casino; easy come, easy go.
The money was nice, but easy come, easy go.
