thinking cap

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɪŋkɪŋ kæp / (AmE), / ˈθɪŋkɪŋ kap / (BrE)
used to refer to a state or frame of mind in which one concentrates and thinks carefully to solve a problem or make a decision
mental state of concentrated thinking

Examples

Before we tackle this puzzle, let's all put on our thinking cap.
She needed her thinking cap to come up with a solution to the tricky problem.
Whenever I'm faced with a tough decision, I put on my thinking cap and consider all the options.

Etymology

Based on the figurative idea of someone putting on a special 'cap' to think better; first recorded in the early 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈθɪŋkɪŋ kæp / (AmE), / ˈθɪŋkɪŋ kæp / (BrE)
used to refer to a state of mind or attitude in which someone focuses their attention and tries to solve a problem
(idiom) a mindset for problem-solving

Examples

Whenever I have a tricky math problem, I put on my thinking cap and focus.
Before we start the meeting, let's all wear our thinking caps and brainstorm some ideas.
She needed her thinking cap to come up with a creative solution to the puzzle.

Synset Examples

I need to wear my thinking cap for this tough exam.
He put on his thinking cap to tackle the complex issue.
Put on your thinking cap and solve this puzzle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concentration - the action of focusing one's attention
  • Antonym: daydreaming - a state of being distracted from the present task
