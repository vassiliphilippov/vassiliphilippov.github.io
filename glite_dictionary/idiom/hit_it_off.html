to hit it off

1. VERB

/ hɪt ɪt ɔf / (AmE), / hɪt ɪt ɒf / (BrE)
(of people) to immediately form a good relationship or become friendly when meeting each other for the first time.
form a good relationship quickly

Examples

Although they had just met, Sarah and Tom hit it off and talked for hours.
Whenever she travels, she seems to hit it off with new people right away.
I'm so glad you and my brother hit it off at the party last night.

Synset Examples

He was just a rando, but we hit it off instantly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get along - to be friendly or compatible with someone
  • Antonym: to fall out - to have a quarrel and stop being friendly
