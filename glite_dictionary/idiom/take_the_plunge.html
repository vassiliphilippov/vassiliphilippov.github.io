to take the plunge

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə plʌndʒ / (AmE), / teɪk ðə plʌndʒ / (BrE)
to decide to do something difficult or risky, especially after thinking about it for a long time
decide to do something risky or difficult

Examples

After saving for years, they decided to take the plunge and move abroad.
If you're unhappy at work, maybe it's time to take the plunge and look for a new job.
She finally took the plunge and told him how she really felt.

Synset Examples

She took a deep breath and made the plunge into the water.
The stock market took a plunge after the bad news.
After years of hesitation, he took the plunge and proposed.
I think it's time to take the plunge and buy a house.
She finally decided to take the plunge and start her own business.
He decided to take the plunge and start his own business.
Stop being a mardarse and take the plunge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dare - to have enough courage to do something risky
  • Antonym: to hesitate - to pause or delay because of uncertainty or fear

Etymology

Originally from the metaphor of plunging into water, suggesting a bold, decisive action.
