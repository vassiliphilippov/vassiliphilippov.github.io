to hold down the fort

1. VERB

/ hoʊld daʊn ðə fɔrt / (AmE), / həʊld daʊn ðə fɔːt / (BrE)
to be responsible for something or take care of a situation in someone else's absence
be responsible during someone's absence
informal american

Examples

Can you hold down the fort while I'm on vacation next week?
She asked her assistant to hold down the fort during her meeting.
Don't worry, I'll hold down the fort until you get back.

Synset Examples

Can you hold down the fort while I'm away?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hold the fort - to take care of things while someone is away

Etymology

Originated in American English, related to military use of 'holding the fort' during an absence.
