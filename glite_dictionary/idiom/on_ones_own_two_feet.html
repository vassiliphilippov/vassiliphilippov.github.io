on one's own two feet

1. X

/ ɑn wʌnz oʊn tu fit / (AmE), / ɒn wʌnz əʊn tuː fiːt / (BrE)
to be independent or self-sufficient, especially by supporting oneself without help from others
independent; self-reliant

Examples

After graduating from college, she was determined to live on her own two feet.
He finally stood on his own two feet after moving into his first apartment.
It's important for children to learn to be on their own two feet as they grow up.

Synset Examples

She confidently stood on her own two feet in the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stand on one's own two feet - be independent or self-reliant
  • Antonym: to depend on - to rely on someone or something
