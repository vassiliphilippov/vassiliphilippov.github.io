the handbags came out

1. X

/ ði ˈhændˌbæɡz keɪm aʊt / (AmE), / ði ˈhændˌbæɡz keɪm aʊt / (BrE)
(informal, humorous) used to indicate that a disagreement or argument has escalated, often in a petty or dramatic way.
a metaphor for the start of a heated argument
informal british

Examples

As soon as the team lost, the handbags came out among the fans.
It was all polite until the handbags came out at the last committee meeting.
The interview was going smoothly, but the handbags came out when they mentioned the budget cuts.

Synset Examples

When the discussion got heated, the handbags came out.
It was a friendly debate until the handbags came out.
