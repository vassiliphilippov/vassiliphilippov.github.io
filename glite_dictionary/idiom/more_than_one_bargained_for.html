more than one bargained for

1. X

/ mɔr ðæn wʌn ˈbɑrɡənd fər / (AmE), / mɔː ðæn wʌn ˈbɑːɡənd fə / (BrE)
Used to describe receiving or experiencing more than was expected or agreed, often in a challenging or unwelcome way.
receiving more than expected or agreed upon

Examples

After accepting the new job, she quickly realized she had more than one bargained for.
The hike turned out to be more than one bargained for when the weather changed suddenly.
They expected a simple renovation, but they got more than one bargained for with hidden repairs.

Synset Examples

They realized they got more than one bargained for with the project.
He faced more than one bargained for during the hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: more than one counted on - experienced more than anticipated or planned for
  • Synonym: bit off more than one can chew - to attempt more than one can handle
  • Antonym: less than one bargained for - received or experienced less than expected or agreed upon
