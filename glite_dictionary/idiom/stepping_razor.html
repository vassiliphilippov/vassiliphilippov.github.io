stepping razor

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛpɪŋ ˈreɪzər / (AmE), / ˈstɛpɪŋ ˈreɪzə / (BrE)
a person who moves with speed and precision, often suggesting danger or formidable qualities
formidable, dangerous person with swift precision

Examples

After the incident, his colleagues started calling him a stepping razor because of his unpredictable intensity.
Known for his quick reflexes, the boxer was considered a stepping razor in the ring.
She navigated the boardroom like a stepping razor, sharp and unafraid to confront challenges.

Synset Examples

He moved like a stepping razor, swift and precise.
In the ring, he was a stepping razor, ready to strike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: force to be reckoned with - a person or thing that is strong, influential, or formidable

Etymology

Popularized by Jamaican reggae, notably through Peter Tosh's song 'Stepping Razor'.
