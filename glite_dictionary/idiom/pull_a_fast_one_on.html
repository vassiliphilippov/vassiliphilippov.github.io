to pull a fast one on

1. VERB

/ pʊl ə fæst wʌn ɑn / (AmE), / pʊl ə fɑːst wʌn ɒn / (BrE)
to deceive or trick someone in order to gain an advantage
deceive or trick someone

Examples

He tried to pull a fast one on the manager by calling in sick when he wasn't actually ill.
Don't think you can pull a fast one on me—I've heard all your excuses before.
The company accountant pulled a fast one on his boss by hiding the missing funds.

Synset Examples

We tricked the teacher into thinking that class would be cancelled next week

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trick - to deceive or cheat someone
  • Synonym: to deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true
  • Antonym: be honest with - to tell the truth to someone
