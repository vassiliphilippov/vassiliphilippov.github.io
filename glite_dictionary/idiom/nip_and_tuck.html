nip and tuck

1. ADJ

/ nɪp ənd tʌk / (AmE), / nɪp ənd tʌk / (BrE)
so close that the outcome could go either way; evenly matched in a race or competition
closely matched or undecided

Examples

The basketball game was nip and tuck until the final seconds when one team finally pulled ahead.
It was a nip and tuck competition between the two top runners throughout the marathon.
The debate remained nip and tuck as both candidates made compelling arguments.

Synset Examples

as they approached the finish line they were neck and neck
the election was a nip and tuck affair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neck and neck - even with each other in a race or contest

Etymology

Origin uncertain, but recorded in 19th-century American English, possibly from sewing or tailoring terms.

2. ADV

/ nɪp ənd tʌk / (AmE), / nɪp ənd tʌk / (BrE)
used to describe a race, competition, or situation where the competitors are evenly matched and the outcome is uncertain until the end
very close or even competition

Examples

The election was nip and tuck all the way until the final votes were counted.
It was nip and tuck between the two teams right up to the last minute of the game.
Their rivalry has always been nip and tuck, making for exciting matches every season.

Synset Examples

the horses ran neck and neck
he won nip and tuck

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neck and neck - extremely close in a race or competition

Etymology

Originated in 19th-century America, possibly from tailoring or surgery, referring to close shaping or cutting.
