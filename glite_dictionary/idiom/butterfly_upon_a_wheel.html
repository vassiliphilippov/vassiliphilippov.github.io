butterfly upon a wheel

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌtərˌflaɪ əˈpɑn ə wil / (AmE), / ˈbʌtəˌflaɪ əˈpɒn ə wiːl / (BrE)
an act of overwhelming something delicate or insignificant with excessive force, or a futile effort against a much greater power or challenge
overwhelming the delicate; futile effort against odds

Examples

Many considered the harsh criticism just a butterfly upon a wheel for a minor mistake.
The government's legal action seemed like a butterfly upon a wheel against a harmless protest.
His dramatic response was described as a butterfly upon a wheel by news commentators.

Synset Examples

The controversy was a butterfly upon a wheel for the celebrity.
Her reaction seemed like a butterfly upon a wheel in the grand scheme.
Spending so much on that lawsuit was just a butterfly upon a wheel.

Etymology

Adapted from the phrase 'to break a butterfly upon a wheel', famously used by Alexander Pope (1735) and meaning to use disproportionate force on something fragile.
