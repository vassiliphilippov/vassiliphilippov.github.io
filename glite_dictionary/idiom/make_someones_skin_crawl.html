to make someone's skin crawl

1. VERB

/ meɪk sʌmˈwʌnz skɪn krɔl / (AmE), / meɪk sʌmˈwʌnz skɪn krɔːl / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel intense discomfort, fear, or disgust
cause intense discomfort or fear

Examples

The story she told about the abandoned house really made my skin crawl.
Even thinking about spiders makes my skin crawl.
His icy stare made my skin crawl in that dark hallway.

Synset Examples

Seeing the horror movie trailer made his skin crawl.
Her creepy laughter made my skin crawl.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creep out - to cause someone to feel uneasy or scared
  • Synonym: unnerving - causing worry or fear, making uncomfortable

/ meɪk sʌmˌwʌnz skɪn krɔl / (AmE), / meɪk ˈsʌmwʌnz skɪn krɔːl / (BrE)

cause strong disgust or fear

Examples

Just thinking about the smell in that room makes my skin crawl.
The sight of so many insects crawling around made her skin crawl.
Hearing that eerie, scraping noise at night makes his skin crawl.
