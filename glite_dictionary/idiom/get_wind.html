to get wind

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt wɪnd / (AmE), / ɡɛt wɪnd / (BrE)
to hear about or become aware of something, especially by chance or not through official means
learn of (usually indirectly or accidentally)

Examples

The company desperately tried to keep the merger secret, but reporters soon got wind of it.
She got wind of the surprise party and pretended to be surprised.
If the boss gets wind of our plan, he might not approve.

Synset Examples

I learned that she has two grown-up children
I see that you have been promoted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to find out - discover information, often intentionally
  • Synonym: to overhear - hear about something unintentionally

Etymology

From the phrase 'get wind of', with 'wind' meaning scent or trace, as used in hunting.
