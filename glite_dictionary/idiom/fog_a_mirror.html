to fog a mirror

1. VERB

/ fɑɡ ə ˈmɪɹɚ / (AmE), / fɒɡ ə ˈmɪɹə / (BrE)
(informal) To be alive or to show the minimal level of competence required, often implying that the standard is very low.
be alive; minimally competent (idiom)
informal

Examples

These days, if you can fog a mirror, you can get a job at that company.
They don't care about qualifications as long as the applicant can fog a mirror.
After the illness, his friends joked that he could barely fog a mirror.

Synset Examples

They only hire people who can fog a mirror these days.
If he can fog a mirror, he's eligible for the job.
You know it's a good sign if she can fog a mirror.
