to shot from the lip

1. VERB

/ ʃɑt frəm ðə lɪp / (AmE), / ʃɒt frəm ðə lɪp / (BrE)
to speak impulsively, without thinking or self-censorship
speak impulsively without thinking

Examples

It's easy to get into trouble when you shot from the lip in meetings.
He regretted having shot from the lip during the interview.
Many politicians have learned not to shot from the lip on sensitive topics.

Synset Examples

She shot from the lip, revealing more than intended.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shoot from the hip - to act or speak quickly without thought or preparation
