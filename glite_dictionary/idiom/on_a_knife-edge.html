on a knife-edge

1. ADV

/ ɑn ə ˈnaɪf ɛdʒ / (AmE), / ɒn ə ˈnaɪf ɛdʒ / (BrE)
in a precarious or dangerously unstable situation, where the outcome is uncertain and could go either way
in a precarious or risky situation

Examples

The company's finances are on a knife-edge, and one small loss could mean bankruptcy.
Tensions were on a knife-edge as the two leaders met for crucial talks.
His career was on a knife-edge after a series of poor performances.

Synset Examples

He balanced the discussion on a knife-edge, avoiding controversy.
The discussion was on a knife-edge, ready to explode.
Their relationship was on a knife-edge after the argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precarious - in a dangerous or unstable situation
