where one's interests lie

1. X

/ wɛr wʌnz ˈɪntrəsts laɪ / (AmE), / weə wʌnz ˈɪntrəsts laɪ / (BrE)
the specific area, activity, or matters that are most important or advantageous to someone
the matters most important to someone

Examples

She never forgets where her interests lie when making business decisions.
Politicians must always be aware of where their interests lie.
He joined the committee because that's where his interests lie.

Synset Examples

She never argues with her clients; she knows where her interests lie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: priority - something regarded as more important than others
