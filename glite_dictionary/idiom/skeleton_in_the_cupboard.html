skeleton in the cupboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɛlətən ɪn ðə ˈkʌbərd / (AmE), / ˈskɛlɪtən ɪn ðə ˈkʌbəd / (BrE)
an embarrassing or shameful secret about someone's past that is kept hidden
hidden shameful secret

Examples

Every family has a skeleton in the cupboard they're afraid to reveal.
The politician's career ended when a skeleton in the cupboard from his past came to light.
She was always nervous at reunions, worried that someone might discover her skeleton in the cupboard.

Synset Examples

there must be a skeleton somewhere in that family's closet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skeleton in the closet - a scandal that is kept secret (American variant)

Etymology

Refers to the idea of hiding something (like a skeleton) in a cupboard to avoid discovery; first recorded in early 19th-century Britain.
