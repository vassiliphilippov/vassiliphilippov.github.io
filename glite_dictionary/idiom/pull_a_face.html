to pull a face

1. VERB

/ pʊl ə feɪs / (AmE), / pʊl ə feɪs / (BrE)
to make a facial expression, especially to show dislike, annoyance, or another emotion
make a facial expression showing emotion

Examples

The child pulled a face when asked to eat his vegetables.
She couldn't help but pull a face at the sour taste of the lemon.
Whenever he hears bad news, he pulls a face without realizing it.

Synset Examples

He grimaced when he saw the amount of homework he had to do

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grimace - to make a twisted expression showing disgust, pain, or wry amusement

2. VERB

/ pʊl ə feɪs / (AmE), / pʊl ə feɪs / (BrE)
to make a facial expression showing disapproval, dislike, or discomfort
make a disapproving or annoyed facial expression

Examples

Whenever she tastes something sour, she pulls a face without realizing it.
The children pulled a face when the teacher mentioned more homework.
Try not to pull a face during the photo shoot, please.

Synset Examples

Don't pull a face; it's just a joke!
He always pulls a face when he eats broccoli.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make a face - to contort one's face, usually to show dislike or irritate someone
