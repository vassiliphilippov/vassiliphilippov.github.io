to tighten one's belt

1. VERB

/ ˈtaɪtən wʌnz bɛlt / (AmE), / ˈtaɪt(ə)n wʌnz bɛlt / (BrE)
to reduce spending and live more frugally, usually because of decreased resources or income
live more frugally; reduce spending

Examples

With rising prices, many families have had to tighten their belts to make ends meet.
After losing his job, he knew he would need to tighten his belt and stick to a strict budget.
The company decided to tighten its belt by cutting unnecessary expenses this quarter.

Synset Examples

In the new economy, we all have to learn to tighten our belts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to economize - to use resources carefully and avoid waste
  • Antonym: splurge - to spend money freely or extravagantly
