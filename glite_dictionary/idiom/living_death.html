living death

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪvɪŋ dɛθ / (AmE), / ˈlɪvɪŋ dɛθ / (BrE)
a condition or existence characterized by unrelenting misery and suffering, as if one were alive but deprived of all joy or vitality.
state of constant misery

Examples

For years after the accident, his life was a living death from which he could not escape.
Prisoners in solitary confinement often describe their existence as a living death.
The chronic pain made every day feel like a living death to her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hell on earth - a place or condition of extreme suffering
  • Antonym: bliss - perfect happiness; great joy
