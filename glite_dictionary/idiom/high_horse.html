high horse

1. NOUN

/ haɪ hɔrs / (AmE), / haɪ hɔːs / (BrE)
an attitude or manner showing arrogant superiority or self-righteousness
arrogant or superior attitude

Examples

She needs to get off her high horse and listen to other people's opinions.
After the argument, he remained on his high horse, refusing to apologize.
People accused the politician of being on a high horse during the debate.

Synset Examples

get off your high horse and admit you are wrong

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrogant - having or revealing an exaggerated sense of one's own importance
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's own importance

Etymology

Originally refers to the literal high horses ridden by people of rank, later used figuratively for superiority.
