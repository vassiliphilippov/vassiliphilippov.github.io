good head on one's shoulders

1. X

/ ɡʊd hɛd ɑn wʌnz ˈʃoʊldɚz / (AmE), / ɡʊd hɛd ɒn wʌnz ˈʃəʊldəz / (BrE)
to have sound judgment or practical common sense
to have good judgment or sense

Examples

Even as a teenager, Maya had a good head on her shoulders and avoided reckless decisions.
Employers value candidates who show they have a good head on their shoulders when faced with pressure.
If you want financial success, you'll need a good head on your shoulders to make wise investments.

Synset Examples

He's got a good head on his shoulders for business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: level-headed - having a calm and sensible attitude
  • Antonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgment
