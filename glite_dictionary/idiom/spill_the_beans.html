to spill the beans

1. VERB

/ spɪl ðə binz / (AmE), / spɪl ðə biːnz / (BrE)
to reveal secret or confidential information, often unintentionally
reveal secret information

Examples

Don't spill the beans about the surprise party to anyone.
She accidentally spilled the beans during the meeting and revealed the new project.
He tried not to spill the beans, but his excitement made it difficult.

Synset Examples

Be careful — his secretary talks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to let the cat out of the bag - to reveal a secret, especially by accident
  • Antonym: to keep a secret - to not reveal secret or confidential information

2. VERB

/ spɪl ðə binz / (AmE), / spɪl ðə biːnz / (BrE)
to reveal secret information by mistake or before it is intended to be known.
reveal a secret

Examples

Try not to spill the beans about the surprise party to anyone.
When she got nervous, she almost spilled the beans during dinner.
He accidentally spilled the beans about the new store opening.

Synset Examples

Don't spill the beans until the project is finished.
Be careful not to spill the beans about the surprise.
He promised not to spill the beans about the plan.
She accidentally spilled the beans about the surprise party.
She spilled the proverbial beans about the surprise party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to let the cat out of the bag - to disclose a secret carelessly or by mistake
  • Antonym: to keep a secret - to not reveal secret information

Etymology

Possibly from an ancient Greek voting system using beans, where spilling them revealed the vote early.
