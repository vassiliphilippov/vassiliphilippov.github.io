to slip back to old ways

1. VERB

/ slɪp bæk tu oʊld weɪz / (AmE), / slɪp bæk tuː əʊld weɪz / (BrE)
to gradually return to one's previous habits or behaviors, especially those considered undesirable
revert to previous habits

Examples

After a stressful week, it's easy to slip back to old ways and neglect new routines.
She worked hard to change, but occasionally she would slip back to old ways.
Therapists encourage patients not to slip back to old ways after treatment.

Synset Examples

After a few months, he began to slip back to old ways.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relapse - to fall back into a former state, especially a bad habit
