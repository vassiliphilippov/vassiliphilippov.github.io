in the trenches

1. ADV

/ ɪn ðə ˈtrɛntʃɪz / (AmE), / ɪn ðə ˈtrɛntʃɪz / (BrE)
directly involved in the most difficult or demanding aspects of a job, situation, or activity
actively involved in difficult situations

Examples

As a nurse, she was in the trenches during the peak of the crisis.
Managers need to understand what it's like to be in the trenches with their staff.
The startup's founder spent years in the trenches before achieving success.

Synset Examples

She feels like she’s in the trenches with her team.
The soldiers were in the trenches fighting bravely.
The volunteers are in the trenches helping the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on the front lines - directly involved where most action or difficulty occurs

Etymology

Metaphorical use of 'trenches,' originally referring to soldiers' positions in warfare (especially World War I).
