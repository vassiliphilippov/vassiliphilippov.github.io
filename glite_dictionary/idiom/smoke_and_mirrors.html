smoke and mirrors

1. NOUN

/ smoʊk ənd ˈmɪrərz / (AmE), / sməʊk ənd ˈmɪərəz / (BrE)
actions or explanations intended to deceive or distract from the truth.
deceptive actions or explanations

Examples

The company’s so-called new strategy turned out to be just smoke and mirrors to cover up their losses.
Most of the flashy advertisements are pure smoke and mirrors with little real value behind them.
Her impressive résumé seemed to be smoke and mirrors once her skills were tested.

Synset Examples

His explanation was all smoke and mirrors, hiding the truth.
The politician's promises were nothing but smoke and mirrors.
The sales pitch was just smoke and mirrors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deception - the act of deceiving or misleading
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful

Etymology

Originally from magic acts and illusion shows using smoke and mirrors to create deceptive effects.
