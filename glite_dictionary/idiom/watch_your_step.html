to watch your step

1. VERB

/ wɑtʃ jɚ stɛp / (AmE), / wɒtʃ jɔː stɛp / (BrE)
Be careful or cautious in your actions, especially when walking or moving.
be careful, especially when walking

Examples

The sign at the entrance warned everyone to watch your step on the icy sidewalk.
When climbing the old staircase, please watch your step as some boards are loose.
You'd better watch your step if you want to avoid trouble with the new boss.

Synset Examples

Watch your step; the ground is slippery.
You need to watch your step on these uneven paths.
He was careful to watch his step around the construction site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to be careful - to take care to avoid danger or mistakes
