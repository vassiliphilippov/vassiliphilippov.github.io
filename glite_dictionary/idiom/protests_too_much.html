to protests too much

1. VERB

/ proʊˈtɛsts tu ˈmʌtʃ / (AmE), / prəˈtɛsts tʃuː ˈmʌtʃ / (BrE)
to deny or defend against an accusation so forcefully or insistently that it suggests the opposite may be true
denies so much it suggests guilt

Examples

Whenever questioned about the missing money, he protests too much, making everyone suspicious.
She protests too much whenever someone suggests she had help with the project.
If you protests too much, people might start believing you have something to hide.

Synset Examples

She must be guilty; she protests too much.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deny - to state that something is not true
  • Antonym: to admit - to confess or acknowledge something is true

Etymology

From the famous line 'The lady doth protest too much, methinks' in Shakespeare's Hamlet.
