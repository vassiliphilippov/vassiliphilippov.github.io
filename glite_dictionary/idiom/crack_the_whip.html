to crack the whip

1. VERB

/ kræk ðə wɪp / (AmE), / kræk ðə wɪp / (BrE)
to use authority or pressure to make others work harder or obey rules strictly
enforce discipline or prompt action

Examples

The new coach wasn’t afraid to crack the whip when players skipped practice.
Sometimes a supervisor must crack the whip to meet tough deadlines.
If sales keep falling, management will likely crack the whip and demand better results.

Synset Examples

The manager had to crack the whip to meet the deadline.
She decided to crack the whip to motivate her team.
It's time to crack the whip and get this project finished.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lay down the law - to assert authority by making rules or giving orders
  • Antonym: go easy on - to be less strict or demanding

Etymology

Refers to the literal act of cracking a whip to control animals, transferred metaphorically to people since the 19th century.
