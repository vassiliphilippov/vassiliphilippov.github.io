to make hay while the sun shines

1. VERB

/ meɪk heɪ waɪl ðə sʌn ʃaɪnz / (AmE), / meɪk heɪ waɪl ðə sʌn ʃaɪnz / (BrE)
to take advantage of an opportunity while it lasts; to act while conditions are favorable
take advantage of favorable conditions

Examples

If we want to finish painting before it rains, we should make hay while the sun shines.
She knows it's smart to make hay while the sun shines and save money during the busy season.
The construction crew worked overtime to make hay while the sun shines and finish the project early.

Synset Examples

He advised them to make hay while the sun shines.
You should make hay while the sun shines and invest now.
We should make hay while the weather's nice.
Let's make hay while the sun shines this weekend.

Etymology

Metaphor from farming, when hay must be made during sunny weather before rain comes.
