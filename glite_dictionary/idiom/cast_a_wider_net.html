to cast a wider net

1. VERB

/ kæst ə ˈwaɪdɚ nɛt / (AmE), / kɑːst ə ˈwaɪdə nɛt / (BrE)
to broaden one's search or efforts in order to include more possibilities or options
broaden the scope of a search

Examples

In order to find the right candidate, we should cast a wider net during our recruitment process.
After several failed attempts, the researchers chose to cast a wider net in their search for evidence.
If you're not getting enough responses, you might need to cast a wider net with your advertisements.

Synset Examples

The team worked hard to cast a wider net for clients.
He decided to cast his net wide in the job market.
To find the best candidate, we should cast our net wide.
They cast their net wide to attract more clients.

Etymology

Derived from the literal act of casting a fishing net to cover a larger area, used figuratively since the late 19th century.
