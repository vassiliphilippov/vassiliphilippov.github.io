in infancy

1. X

/ ɪn ˈɪnfənsi / (AmE), / ɪn ˈɪnfənsi / (BrE)
at an early stage in the development or existence of something
at an early stage of development

Examples

Solar power technology was still in infancy just a few decades ago.
Her business was in infancy when she won her first major client.
Discussions about online privacy were only in infancy at the start of the internet era.
