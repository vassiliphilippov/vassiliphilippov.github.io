no matter how thin you slice it

1. X

/ noʊ ˈmætər haʊ ˈθɪn ju ˈslaɪs ɪt / (AmE), / nəʊ ˈmætə haʊ ˈθɪn juː ˈslaɪs ɪt / (BrE)
used to emphasize that something remains true or unchanged regardless of how it is examined, analyzed, or approached
regardless of how it is examined

Examples

No matter how thin you slice it, his explanation still doesn't make sense.
They tried to spin the results positively, but no matter how thin you slice it, the outcome was a failure.
No matter how thin you slice it, cheating on the test is still wrong.

Synset Examples

He tried to justify his decision, but no matter how thin you slice it, it's still baloney.
No matter how thin you slice it, it's still baloney to claim that.

2. X

/ noʊ ˈmæt̬ɚ haʊ θɪn ju slaɪs ɪt / (AmE), / nəʊ ˈmætə haʊ θɪn juː slaɪs ɪt / (BrE)
Used to indicate that something remains fundamentally the same regardless of how it is presented, explained, or interpreted.
fundamentally unchanged despite interpretation

Examples

Her explanation doesn't add up; no matter how thin you slice it, it's still not convincing.
No matter how thin you slice it, lying is still dishonest.
You can dress up the proposal, but no matter how thin you slice it, it's still a bad deal.

Synset Examples

Her excuses were weak; no matter how thin you slice it, it's still baloney.
