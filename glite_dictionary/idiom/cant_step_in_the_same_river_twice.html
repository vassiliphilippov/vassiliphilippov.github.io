can't step in the same river twice

1. X

/ kænt stɛp ɪn ðə seɪm ˈrɪvər twaɪs / (AmE), / kɑːnt stɛp ɪn ðə seɪm ˈrɪvə twaɪs / (BrE)
used to convey that each situation or experience is unique and cannot be exactly duplicated; things are always changing
things always change; nothing stays the same

Examples

After moving away for several years, returning home made her realize you can't step in the same river twice.
He wanted to relive his childhood holidays, but soon saw that you can't step in the same river twice.
No matter how hard they tried, the reunion proved you can't step in the same river twice—the feeling was different.

Synset Examples

Life keeps changing; you can't step in the same river twice.
Remember, you can't step in the same river twice.
Each moment is unique; you can't step in the same river twice.

Etymology

Derived from the philosophy of Heraclitus, who stated that you cannot step into the same river twice, as both you and the river have changed.
