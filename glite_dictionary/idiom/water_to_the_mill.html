water to the mill

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔː.tɚ tə ðə mɪl / (AmE), / ˈwɔː.tə tə ðə mɪl / (BrE)
something that contributes to one's benefit or advantage
something beneficial or advantageous

Examples

The positive reviews were water to the mill for the new restaurant.
The sudden surge in demand was water to the mill for their small business.
Any new information is water to the mill of investigative journalists.

Synset Examples

Every new client brings water to his mill.
Her success in the competition was water to her mill.
The publicity from the event was water to their mill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grist to the mill - something that can be turned to one's advantage (more common variant)

Etymology

From the literal action of water driving a mill, providing benefit to the miller; compare 'grist to the mill.'
