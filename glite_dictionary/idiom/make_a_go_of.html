to make a go of

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə ɡoʊ ʌv / (AmE), / meɪk ə ɡəʊ ɒv / (BrE)
to attempt to succeed in or make something work, especially a business, relationship, or endeavor
try to succeed in something

Examples

Sarah is determined to make a go of her new restaurant, despite the competition.
After several failed attempts, they finally managed to make a go of their partnership.
He moved to the city to make a go of his acting career.

Synset Examples

Despite challenges, he will make a go of his dream.
They are trying to make a go of their startup.
She decided to make a go of her new bakery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to succeed - achieve the desired aim or result
  • Antonym: to fail - be unsuccessful in achieving one's goal
