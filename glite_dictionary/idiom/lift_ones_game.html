to lift one's game

1. VERB

/ lɪft wʌnz ɡeɪm / (AmE), / lɪft wʌnz ɡeɪm / (BrE)
to improve one's performance, effort, or standard, especially in order to meet competition or expectations
improve one's performance or effort

Examples

If the team wants to reach the finals, they will need to lift their game.
She realized her presentation skills needed work, so she decided to lift her game.
After seeing his rival's progress, he knew he had to lift his game to keep up.

Synset Examples

To succeed, she must lift her game significantly.
He needs to lift his game if he wants to win.
The competition pushed him to lift his game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raise one's game - to increase the level of one's performance or effort
