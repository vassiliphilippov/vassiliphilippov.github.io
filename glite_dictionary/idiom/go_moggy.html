to go moggy

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈmɑɡi / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈmɒɡi / (BrE)
to become very upset or emotional, often leading to feelings of sadness or depression
become upset or emotional
informal

Examples

He started to go moggy after hearing the bad news from work.
Try not to go moggy over little setbacks; things will get better.
Every time exams approach, she tends to go moggy from the stress.

Synset Examples

After the breakup, he seemed to go moggy for weeks.
