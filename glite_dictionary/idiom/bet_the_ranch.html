to bet the ranch

1. VERB

/ bɛt ðə ræntʃ / (AmE), / bɛt ðə rɑːntʃ / (BrE)
to risk everything one has on a single opportunity or decision
risk everything on a gamble or decision

Examples

Even though the proposal sounded promising, I wouldn't bet the ranch on its success.
She was so confident in her team's victory that she was willing to bet the ranch.
If you really believe that's the winning ticket, are you ready to bet the ranch?

Synset Examples

If you're confident, then go ahead and bet the ranch.
He wouldn't bet the ranch without some solid evidence.
She felt lucky enough to bet the ranch on that last deal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go for broke - to risk everything in an all-out effort
  • Antonym: to play it safe - to avoid risks and take a cautious approach
