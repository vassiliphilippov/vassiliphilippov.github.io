dog in the hunt

1. NOUN

/ dɔg ɪn ðə hʌnt / (AmE), / dɒg ɪn ðə hʌnt / (BrE)
Someone who is actively involved in or has a stake in a situation, or is pursuing a particular goal.
person actively involved or pursuing a goal

Examples

Even if you don't have a dog in the hunt, it's hard not to get caught up in the excitement.
He finally felt like a dog in the hunt after years of being sidelined at work.
Without real responsibility, she didn't have a dog in the hunt for the decision.

Synset Examples

The team felt like a dog in the hunt during the project.
She was a dog in the hunt for her promotion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dog in the fight - person with a strong interest or stake in an outcome

Etymology

Metaphorical use comparing personal involvement to a dog participating in a hunt.
