to spin your wheels

1. VERB

/ spɪn jɔr wilz / (AmE), / spɪn jɔː wɪəlz / (BrE)
to waste time or effort without making any progress
waste time without progress

Examples

If you don't set clear goals, you'll just spin your wheels without achieving anything.
She realized after months of job searching that she was simply spinning her wheels.
Stop spinning your wheels and start working on a solution.

Synset Examples

Don't spin your wheels; focus on what's important.
She realized she was spinning her wheels instead of progressing.
He felt like he was just spinning his wheels at work.
Stop spinning my wheels and give me a straight answer.
You’re just spinning my wheels with all this indecision.
He's just spinning your wheels with those empty promises.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tread water - to make no progress while expending effort

Etymology

From the literal action of a vehicle's wheels rotating without gaining traction, especially on slippery surfaces.
