to spend a penny

1. VERB

/ spɛnd ə ˈpɛni / (AmE), / spɛnd ə ˈpɛni / (BrE)
(British, informal) To urinate; to go to the toilet to pass urine.
(Brit.) to urinate; go to the toilet
informal british

Examples

Excuse me, I need to spend a penny before we leave.
At the motorway services, he stopped to spend a penny after the long drive.
Grandma discreetly said she was off to spend a penny before dessert was served.

Synset Examples

Again, the cat had made on the expensive rug

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to urinate - to pass urine

Etymology

Refers to the fee of one penny to use public toilets in Britain, especially after the introduction of coin-operated locks in the 19th and early 20th centuries.

2. VERB

/ spɛnd ə ˈpɛni / (AmE), / spɛnd ə ˈpɛni / (BrE)
(British, informal) To use the restroom, especially to urinate; a polite or euphemistic expression for going to the toilet.
go to the restroom (British euphemism)
informal british

Examples

I'm just going to spend a penny before we leave the house.
When she excused herself to spend a penny, everyone understood she needed the restroom.
In the old days, you literally had to spend a penny to use some public toilets.

Synset Examples

Excuse me, I need to spend a penny.
The café has a sign indicating where to spend a penny.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: use the restroom - to go to the toilet; another euphemism
  • Synonym: go to the toilet - to use the toilet

Etymology

British origin; derives from pay toilets in the UK which cost one penny to use, especially in the early 20th century.
