to pay one's own freight

1. VERB

/ peɪ wʌnz oʊn freɪt / (AmE), / peɪ wʌnz əʊn freɪt / (BrE)
to take responsibility for one's own expenses or costs, without relying on others
cover one's own expenses

Examples

After leaving home, he was determined to pay his own freight through university.
She admired her friend's determination to pay her own freight instead of accepting help.
In this club, everyone is expected to pay their own freight for events and meals.

Synset Examples

He prefers to pay his own freight rather than rely on others.
She was proud to pay her own freight and not borrow money.
If you want the job done right, you must pay your own freight.

Etymology

From freight, meaning the cost of transporting goods; figuratively used to mean covering one's own costs.
