dead to rights

1. X

/ dɛd tə raɪts / (AmE), / dɛd tə raɪts / (BrE)
caught in a situation where one is clearly and undeniably guilty or at fault
undeniably caught in wrongdoing

Examples

The lawyer knew his client was dead to rights after seeing the security footage.
When the examiners found the cheat sheet, the student was dead to rights.
The auditors had the company dead to rights on financial misreporting.

Synset Examples

She had him dead to rights with the incriminating evidence.
The police found him dead to rights during the raid.
The thief was caught dead to rights on camera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red-handed - in the act of committing a crime or doing something wrong
  • Antonym: off the hook - free from blame or responsibility
