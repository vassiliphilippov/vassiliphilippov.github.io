five will get you ten

1. X

/ faɪv wɪl ɡɛt ju tɛn / (AmE), / faɪv wɪl ɡɛt juː tɛn / (BrE)
used to indicate strong confidence that a particular event will happen, as in a bet or wager
expressing strong confidence (like a bet)

Examples

Five will get you ten it rains before the parade starts.
Five will get you ten that she calls you back within an hour.
Five will get you ten they're going to cancel the game due to the storm.

Synset Examples

Five will get you ten it rains tomorrow.
Five will get you ten that he forgets again.
Five will get you ten he won't show up.

Etymology

From betting terminology, where odds of five to ten indicate expectation of a likely outcome.
