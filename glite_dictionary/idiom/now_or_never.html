now or never

1. X

/ naʊ ər ˈnɛvɚ / (AmE), / naʊ ɔː ˈnɛvə / (BrE)
used to indicate that immediate action is required because there may not be another chance
urgent moment for immediate action

Examples

It's now or never if we want to catch the last train home.
He realized it was now or never and finally confessed his feelings.
You have to apply today; it's now or never for this job opportunity.

Synset Examples

It's now or never to make your dreams come true.
If you want to start a business, it's now or never.
You must decide now or never if you want to join.
