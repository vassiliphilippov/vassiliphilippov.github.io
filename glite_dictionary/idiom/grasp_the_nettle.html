to grasp the nettle

1. VERB

/ ɡræsp ðə ˈnɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ɡrɑːsp ðə ˈnɛtl̩ / (BrE)
to confront a difficult or unpleasant situation directly and with determination
confront something difficult directly

Examples

He knew he had to grasp the nettle and tell his team about the changes.
The government must grasp the nettle and implement necessary reforms.
After months of delay, she finally decided to grasp the nettle and start the project.

Synset Examples

You need to grasp the nettle if you want to succeed.
It's time to grasp the nettle and make a decision.
She decided to grasp the nettle and confront her fears.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to face the music - to accept the unpleasant consequences of one's actions
  • Antonym: avoid the issue - to evade dealing with something difficult

Etymology

From the idea that the stinging nettle must be seized firmly to avoid being stung, used metaphorically since the 18th century.
