water over the dam

1. X

/ ˈwɔt̬ɚ oʊvɚ ðə dæm / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə əʊvə ðə dæm / (BrE)
used to say that something, usually an unpleasant event or mistake, has already happened and cannot be changed, so it should no longer be a concern
past event that cannot be changed

Examples

He apologized for his mistake, but it's just water over the dam now.
There's no point worrying about what happened—it's water over the dam.
All those arguments are water over the dam and we need to move forward.

Synset Examples

What happened last year is just water over the dam.
We can’t change it now; it’s all water over the dam.
Let’s not dwell on it; it’s water over the dam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water under the bridge - past events that are forgiven or no longer important
