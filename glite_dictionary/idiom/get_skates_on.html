to get skates on

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt skeɪts ɑn / (AmE), / ɡɛt skeɪts ɒn / (BrE)
to hurry up or start acting quickly
hurry up; act quickly (British idiom)
british informal

Examples

We'd better get skates on if we want to arrive on time.
If you don't get skates on, you'll miss the start of the match.
She told him to get skates on because dinner was getting cold.

Synset Examples

I need to get my skates on before the movie starts.
Let's get our skates on; we’re running late!
You better get your skates on if you want to catch the bus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hurry up - to do something more quickly
  • Antonym: to dawdle - to move or act too slowly
