to give someone hell

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ˌsʌmwʌn hɛl / (AmE), / ɡɪv ˌsʌmwʌn hɛl / (BrE)
to reprimand, punish, or treat someone very harshly, especially by scolding or criticizing them strongly
scold or punish harshly
informal

Examples

My dad will give me hell if I fail this test.
The coach gave the team hell after their poor performance.
She knew her boss would give her hell for missing the deadline.

Synset Examples

He promised to give her hell if she was late again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chew someone out - to scold someone harshly
  • Synonym: dress someone down - to reprimand someone severely
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration for someone

2. VERB

/ ɡɪv ˈsʌmwʌn hɛl / (AmE), / ɡɪv ˈsʌmwʌn hɛl / (BrE)
to scold or criticize someone very harshly; to treat someone severely, usually because of their actions or behavior.
scold or criticize strongly
informal

Examples

If you don't turn in your assignment on time, the teacher will give you hell.
Her parents gave her hell for staying out so late last night.
Jake knew his boss would give him hell if he missed the morning meeting.

Synset Examples

She’s going to give him hell for breaking the rules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tell someone off - to reprimand someone severely
