to fall on someone's neck

1. VERB

/ fɔl ɑn ˈsʌmwʌnz nɛk / (AmE), / fɔːl ɒn ˈsʌmwənz nɛk / (BrE)
to embrace someone enthusiastically, often as a sign of strong emotion
embrace enthusiastically

Examples

As soon as he saw his sister at the airport, he fell on her neck and began to cry.
Overcome with relief, she fell on his neck when he returned safely.
The children fell on their mother's neck after being apart for a year.

Synset Examples

When she won the award, her friends fell on her neck.
