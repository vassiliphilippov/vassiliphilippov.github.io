to punch someone's lights out

1. VERB

/ pʌntʃ sʌmwʌnz laɪts aʊt / (AmE), / pʌntʃ sʌmwʌnz laɪts aʊt / (BrE)
to hit someone very hard and knock them unconscious, usually in anger or retaliation
knock someone unconscious with a punch
informal

Examples

If he insults me again, I'll punch his lights out.
She warned her brother not to tease her or she would punch his lights out.
He's always bragging that he could punch anyone's lights out in a fight.

Synset Examples

If he keeps bothering me, I'll punch his lights out.
He threatened to punch my lights out if I didn't back off.
He's got a reputation for punching someone's lights out in the ring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to knock out - to make someone unconscious with a blow
