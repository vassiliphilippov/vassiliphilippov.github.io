to turn a phrase

1. VERB

/ tɝn ə freɪz / (AmE), / tɜːn ə freɪz / (BrE)
to express something in a particularly skillful, clever, or eloquent manner
to express skillfully or eloquently

Examples

She always knows how to turn a phrase and captivate her listeners.
The author's ability to turn a phrase makes his writing truly memorable.
Politicians often try to turn a phrase to make their statements more persuasive.

Synset Examples

He tried to turn a phrase that would impress the audience.
She can always turn a phrase to make it sound elegant.
He knows how to turn a phrase beautifully in his writing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wordsmith - a person skilled in the use of words
  • Antonym: mangle a phrase - to express something awkwardly or inelegantly
