to put up someone's back

1. VERB

/ pʊt ʌp ˈsʌmwʌnz bæk / (AmE), / pʊt ʌp ˈsʌmwənz bæk / (BrE)
to make someone annoyed or defensive; to irritate or provoke someone
to irritate or provoke someone
british

Examples

His constant criticism really puts up my back during meetings.
Try not to put up her back with unnecessary comments.
He didn't mean to put up your back, but his joke was misunderstood.

Synset Examples

Don't put my back up with those accusations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to annoy - to make someone feel irritated or angry
  • Synonym: to provoke - to cause a person to become angry or annoyed
  • Antonym: to calm down - to become less angry or upset
