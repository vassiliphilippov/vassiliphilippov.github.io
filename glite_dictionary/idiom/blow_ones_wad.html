to blow one's wad

1. VERB

/ bloʊ wʌnz wɑd / (AmE), / bləʊ wʌnz wɒd / (BrE)
to spend all of one's money at once, often impulsively or on a single purchase or activity
spend all one's money at once
informal vulgar american

Examples

After getting paid, he blew his wad at the casino in just one night.
She blew her wad on a luxury vacation, leaving nothing for emergencies.
It's easy to blow your wad during the holidays if you don't budget carefully.

Synset Examples

He blew his wad on the latest gadgets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to splash out - spend a lot of money freely
  • Antonym: to save up - accumulate money by not spending

Etymology

Originally an American slang expression; 'wad' refers to a bundle of money.
