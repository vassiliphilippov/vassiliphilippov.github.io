thorn in the flesh

1. NOUN

/ θɔrn ɪn ðə fleʃ / (AmE), / θɔːn ɪn ðə fleʃ / (BrE)
a persistent or recurring source of annoyance, difficulty, or trouble
persistent source of annoyance or trouble

Examples

The rising maintenance costs have become a real thorn in the flesh for the company.
For years, his noisy neighbors were a thorn in the flesh that he simply couldn't ignore.
Her unfinished dissertation was a thorn in the flesh throughout her academic career.

Synset Examples

The unresolved issue remained a thorn in the flesh.
His chronic illness was a thorn in the flesh.
That debt is a constant thorn in the flesh for him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thorn in one's side - a persistent or recurring source of annoyance, difficulty, or trouble
  • Synonym: nuisance - a person or thing causing inconvenience or annoyance

Etymology

From the biblical phrase in 2 Corinthians 12:7, where the apostle Paul refers to a 'thorn in the flesh.'
