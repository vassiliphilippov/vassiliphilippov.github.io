end of the line

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛnd əv ðə ˈlaɪn / (AmE), / ˌɛnd əv ðə ˈlaɪn / (BrE)
the point at which something stops or is finished; the conclusion or termination of a process or situation
conclusion or termination point

Examples

After twenty years, the company finally reached the end of the line.
When the old bus broke down again, it was clearly the end of the line for it.
If negotiations fail today, that could be the end of the line for the peace process.

Synset Examples

This is the end of the line for our project.
After years of service, he reached the end of the line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finish - an end or final part of something
  • Antonym: beginning - the point at which something starts
