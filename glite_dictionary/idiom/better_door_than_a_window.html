better door than a window

1. X

/ ˈbɛtər dɔr ðæn ə ˈwɪndoʊ / (AmE), / ˈbɛtə dɔː ðən ə ˈwɪndəʊ / (BrE)
(informal) Used humorously to tell someone they are blocking the view or standing in the way.
humorous phrase for someone blocking the view
informal

Examples

Could you please move? You make a better door than a window.
Whenever he stands in the middle of the room, someone remarks that he makes a better door than a window.
I had to laugh when my friend said I was a better door than a window while blocking the TV.

Synset Examples

When he stepped in front of the screen, I told him he was making a better door than a window.
He stood there, and I jokingly said he made a better door than a window.
You're blocking the TV; you make a better door than a window.
