off one's trolley

1. ADJ

/ ɑf wʌnz ˈtrɑli / (AmE), / ɒf wʌnz ˈtrɒli / (BrE)
behaving in a strange or crazy way; not thinking or acting normally
acting irrationally or crazy
informal british

Examples

After staying up for three nights in a row, he seemed completely off his trolley.
She must be off her trolley to buy such an expensive coat for the dog.
Are you off your trolley? That plan will never work!

Synset Examples

When she started yelling, we knew she was off her trolley.
He's been off his trolley since the accident.
He was completely off his trolley during the debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crazy - mentally deranged or behaving very strangely
  • Antonym: sane - having a normal, healthy mind; not crazy

Etymology

British slang from the late 19th century, probably related to trams running 'off their trolley' (electric contact wire).
