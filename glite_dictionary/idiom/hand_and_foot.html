hand and foot

1. ADV

/ hænd ənd fʊt / (AmE), / hænd ənd fʊt / (BrE)
in every possible way; as completely as possible, especially in serving or attending to someone
in all possible ways, completely

Examples

The hotel staff waited on us hand and foot during our stay.
She likes to spoil her grandchildren, attending to them hand and foot.
They served the celebrity hand and foot at the private event.

Synset Examples

they served him hand and foot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wait on someone hand and foot - to serve or attend to someone in every possible way
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00148538-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ hænd ənd fʊt / (AmE), / hænd ənd fʊt / (BrE)
in a way that is completely devoted or attentive, often to the point of doing everything for someone; sometimes, to be completely bound or constrained by something
in a completely devoted or bound manner

Examples

Her children expected her to wait on them hand and foot all summer.
The strict rules bound him hand and foot, leaving him no freedom to choose.
He served his employer hand and foot for years, never complaining.

Synset Examples

This contract binds you hand and foot to the agreement.
She waited on him hand and foot during his recovery.
