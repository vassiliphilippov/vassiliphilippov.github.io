to paint the lily

1. VERB

/ peɪnt ðə ˈlɪli / (AmE), / peɪnt ðə ˈlɪli / (BrE)
to add unnecessary ornamentation or improvement to something that is already beautiful or perfect
add unnecessary adornment

Examples

To add gold trim to that stunning vase would be to paint the lily.
He tried to paint the lily by editing an already flawless speech.
In her quest for perfection, she tends to paint the lily with needless embellishments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to gild the lily - to adorn unnecessarily something that is already beautiful

Etymology

From a misquotation of Shakespeare's 'to gild refined gold, to paint the lily.'

2. VERB

/ peɪnt ðə ˈlɪli / (AmE), / peɪnt ðə ˈlɪli / (BrE)
to add unnecessary ornament or improvement to something that is already perfect or complete
make unnecessary improvements to something perfect

Examples

Attempting to paint the lily by adding extra decorations ruined the simplicity of the design.
If you revise the report again, you'll only paint the lily.
Her suggestion to rewrite the already perfect speech is just trying to paint the lily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to gild the lily - to unnecessarily adorn or improve something already beautiful

Etymology

Misquotation from Shakespeare's 'King John': 'To gild refined gold, to paint the lily...'
