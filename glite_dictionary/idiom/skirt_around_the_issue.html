to skirt around the issue

1. VERB

/ skɝt əˈraʊnd ði ˈɪʃu / (AmE), / skɜːt əˈraʊnd ði ˈɪsjuː / (BrE)
to deliberately avoid dealing with or addressing a subject directly, especially a difficult or sensitive one
avoid addressing a topic directly

Examples

During the interview, the politician continued to skirt around the issue instead of giving a direct answer.
It's frustrating when people skirt around the issue rather than confronting problems head-on.
Rather than admit his mistake, he chose to skirt around the issue with vague explanations.

Synset Examples

He tried to skirt around the issue during the meeting.
The artist decided to skirt the controversial topic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dodge the question - to avoid answering a question directly
  • Antonym: confront the issue - to address a subject directly and openly

Etymology

From the verb 'skirt' meaning to go around the edge of something, metaphorically applied to topics or issues.
