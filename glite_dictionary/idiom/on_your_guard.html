on your guard

1. ADJ

/ ɑn jʊr ɡɑrd / (AmE), / ɒn jɔː ɡɑːd / (BrE)
alert and ready to deal with danger, problems, or unexpected situations
alert; prepared for danger or threats

Examples

You should always be on your guard when traveling in an unfamiliar city.
The watchdog kept the house safe by staying on its guard all night.
She was on her guard after hearing about recent burglaries in the neighborhood.

Synset Examples

on guard against con artists
must remain on your guard in such a situation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alert - quick to notice and respond to danger or problems
  • Antonym: off guard - not prepared for something unexpected
