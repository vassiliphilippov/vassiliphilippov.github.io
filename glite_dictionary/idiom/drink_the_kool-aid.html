to drink the Kool-Aid

1. VERB

/ drɪŋk ðə ˈkuːl ˌeɪd / (AmE), / drɪŋk ðə ˈkuːl ˌeɪd / (BrE)
to uncritically accept a belief, idea, or policy, often with unquestioning enthusiasm or loyalty
uncritically accept a belief or idea
informal

Examples

Many employees started to drink the Kool-Aid after the charismatic CEO unveiled his ambitious vision.
It's important to research thoroughly instead of just drinking the Kool-Aid when new health trends emerge.
He had clearly drunk the Kool-Aid and spoke passionately about the company's mission without question.

Synset Examples

She warned us not to drink the Kool-Aid on this new project.
Don’t drink the Kool-Aid; we need to stay critical.
After the meeting, he seemed to have drunk the Kool-Aid completely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swallow the bait - to be easily deceived or tricked; to believe something unquestioningly

Etymology

Refers to the 1978 Jonestown tragedy, where followers drank a flavored beverage laced with poison; Kool-Aid was a known brand.
