in the catbird seat

1. X

/ ɪn ðə ˈkætbɝd ˌsiːt / (AmE), / ɪn ðə ˈkætbɜːd ˌsiːt / (BrE)
In a position of power, dominance, or advantage over others.
in a position of advantage or control
informal american

Examples

With her extensive experience, she was clearly in the catbird seat during the job interview.
After the merger, their company found itself in the catbird seat within the industry.
If this bid succeeds, we'll be in the catbird seat for future contracts.

Synset Examples

With her promotion, she’s now in the catbird seat.
Having all the information put him in the catbird seat.
She always finds herself in the catbird seat during negotiations.

Etymology

American English, popularized by baseball announcer Red Barber in the mid-20th century; possibly referring to the catbird's habit of choosing a high, prominent perch.
