first port of call

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɝst ˈpɔrt əv ˈkɑl / (AmE), / ˌfɜːst ˈpɔːt əv ˈkɔːl / (BrE)
the first place you go to for help, information, or to achieve something
initial place to go for help or information

Examples

The HR department should be your first port of call if you have any issues at work.
When I arrive in a new city, the tourist office is always my first port of call.
If you need IT support, the helpdesk is your first port of call.

Synset Examples

The library is my first port of call for research.
For questions, the help desk is your first port of call.

Etymology

Originally a nautical term, referring to the first harbor visited by a ship on a journey; now used figuratively.
