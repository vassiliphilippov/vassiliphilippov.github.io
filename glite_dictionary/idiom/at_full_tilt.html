at full tilt

1. ADV

/ æt fʊl tɪlt / (AmE), / ət fʊl tɪlt / (BrE)
operating or moving at the greatest possible speed or with the maximum possible effort
at the greatest possible speed or effort

Examples

The assembly line ran at full tilt all night to meet demand.
She studied at full tilt for her finals, barely stopping to rest.
The company is operating at full tilt to roll out the new product.

Synset Examples

The team was working at full tilt to meet the deadline.
The engine was running at full tilt during the race.
They were running at full tilt to catch the bus.
His enthusiasm was at full tilt during the presentation.
She approached the challenge at full tilt and succeeded.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flat out - as fast as possible; at full speed or capacity
  • Antonym: at a crawl - moving extremely slowly

Etymology

Originated in the 16th century, where 'tilt' referred to jousting at full speed.
