Jesum Crow

1. INTJ

/ ˈdʒizəm kroʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒizəm krəʊ / (BrE)
An exclamation used to express surprise or disbelief, chiefly in North American dialects and now archaic.
exclamation of surprise or disbelief (archaic, chiefly North American)
archaic american

Examples

Jesum Crow! I never expected to see you here after all these years.
"Jesum Crow, that was a close call!" he exclaimed as the car skidded.
When the news was announced, Aunt May cried, "Jesum Crow, what a shock!"

Synset Examples

When he saw the surprise party, he shouted, 'Jesum Crow!'

Etymology

Euphemistic alteration of 'Jesus Christ.'
