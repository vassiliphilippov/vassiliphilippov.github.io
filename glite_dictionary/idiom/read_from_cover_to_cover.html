to read from cover to cover

1. VERB

/ ɹid frəm ˈkʌvɚ tə ˈkʌvɚ / (AmE), / riːd frəm ˈkʌvə tə ˈkʌvə / (BrE)
to read an entire book, magazine, or similar item thoroughly from beginning to end
read something thoroughly from beginning to end

Examples

She read from cover to cover every novel in the series over summer vacation.
I was so fascinated by the article that I read from cover to cover without taking any breaks.
He often reads from cover to cover when he gets a new magazine.

Synset Examples

I read the book from cover to cover in one night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: read through - to read the entire length of a text, often quickly or for checking
