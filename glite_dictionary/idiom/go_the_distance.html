to go the distance

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ðə ˈdɪstəns / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ðə ˈdɪstəns / (BrE)
to continue working or striving until something is finished, especially when it is difficult or takes a long time
persevere to the end; persist

Examples

Despite many setbacks, the team was determined to go the distance and finish the project.
She knew it would be tough, but she was ready to go the distance for her dream job.
No one thought he could go the distance, but he proved them all wrong.

Synset Examples

He always believed he could go the distance.
They promise to go the distance for their clients.
She trained hard to go the distance in the marathon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persevere - to continue despite difficulties
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop trying or abandon an effort
