to crawl before you walk

1. VERB

/ krɔl bɪˈfɔɹ ju wɑk / (AmE), / krɔːl bɪˈfɔː juː wɔːk / (BrE)
to start with basic or simpler tasks before attempting more complicated ones
start with basics before advancing

Examples

Before starting with advanced features, it's wise to crawl before you walk.
My manager encouraged me to crawl before I walk for better understanding of the processes.
In learning a new language, you really have to crawl before you walk.

Synset Examples

She reminded him to crawl before he walks in coding.
You need to crawl before you walk in this job.
Learning the basics means you must crawl before you walk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walk before you run - to learn the basics before moving to advanced stages
