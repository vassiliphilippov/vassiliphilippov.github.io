to eat their own

1. VERB

/ it ðɛr oʊn / (AmE), / iːt ðeə əʊn / (BrE)
(of a group or its members) to betray, criticize, or harm people from within one's own group or community.
betray or harm one's own group

Examples

In politics, parties sometimes eat their own when facing internal disagreements.
When the team began to lose, fans started to eat their own by blaming one another.
It's unfortunate to see companies eat their own during times of crisis.

Synset Examples

It's sad when people eat their own in tough times.
