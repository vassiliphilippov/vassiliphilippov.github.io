to wash dirty laundry in public

1. VERB

/ wɑʃ ˈdɜrti ˈlɔndri ɪn ˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / wɒʃ ˈdɜːti ˈlɔːndri ɪn ˈpʌb.lɪk / (BrE)
to discuss or reveal private, personal, or embarrassing matters openly, especially in public or in front of others
reveal private matters publicly

Examples

It’s best not to wash dirty laundry in public if you want to keep family matters private.
She regrets washing dirty laundry in public during that heated interview.
Some employees feel uncomfortable when managers wash dirty laundry in public at team meetings.

Synset Examples

It's unwise to wash one's dirty laundry in public during meetings.
They shouldn’t wash their dirty laundry in public.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to air dirty laundry - to reveal private or embarrassing matters in public
