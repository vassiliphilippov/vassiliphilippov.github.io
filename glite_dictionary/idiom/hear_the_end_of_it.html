to hear the end of it

1. VERB

/ hɪr ði ɛnd əv ɪt / (AmE), / hɪə ði end əv ɪt / (BrE)
(used with 'never') to continue being reminded of or bothered by something, especially complaints or criticism, for a long time.
never stop being reminded of something

Examples

If you break her favorite mug, you'll never hear the end of it.
He lost the match, and now he won't hear the end of it from his teammates.
Whenever I make a mistake at work, I feel like I'll never hear the end of it.

Synset Examples

If he keeps whining, we'll never hear the end of it.
Once she starts talking, you'll never hear the end of it.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to let it go - to stop worrying or talking about something
