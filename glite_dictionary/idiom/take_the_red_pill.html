to take the red pill

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə rɛd pɪl / (AmE), / teɪk ðə rɛd pɪl / (BrE)
to choose to learn or accept an uncomfortable or difficult truth, especially when it challenges one's previous beliefs or realities
choose to face an uncomfortable truth

Examples

Sometimes you have to take the red pill if you really want to understand what’s going on in the world.
After reading the shocking report, she felt as if she had taken the red pill and could never go back.
Choosing to take the red pill means accepting reality, no matter how painful it may be.

Synset Examples

He chose to take the red pill and face reality.
To understand the truth, you have to take the red pill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to face the truth - to confront reality or accept the truth
  • Antonym: bury one’s head in the sand - to avoid or ignore an unpleasant situation

Etymology

From the 1999 film The Matrix, where the 'red pill' reveals hidden truths.
