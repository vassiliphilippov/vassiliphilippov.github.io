to push to the limit

1. VERB

/ pʊʃ tə ðə ˈlɪmɪt / (AmE), / pʊʃ tə ðə ˈlɪmɪt / (BrE)
to exert maximum effort or to test the maximum capacity or endurance of someone or something
exert to the maximum, test limits

Examples

The coach always encouraged the team to push to the limit during practice.
She pushed to the limit to finish the project before the deadline.
Extreme conditions can push to the limit even the most experienced climbers.

Synset Examples

He pushed himself to his maximum during training.
He pushed his limits, going beyond his previous achievements.
When pushed to the limit, I choose violence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go all out - to make one's maximum effort
  • Antonym: to hold back - to refrain from using one's full energy or effort
