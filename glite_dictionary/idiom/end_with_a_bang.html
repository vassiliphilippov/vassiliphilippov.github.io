to end with a bang

1. VERB

/ ɛnd wɪð ə bæŋ / (AmE), / ɛnd wɪð ə bæŋ / (BrE)
to finish in an exciting, dramatic, or memorable way
finish in an exciting way

Examples

The concert ended with a bang, leaving the audience cheering for more.
Their vacation ended with a bang when they unexpectedly won a free trip home.
The campaign ended with a bang thanks to a big rally on the final day.

Synset Examples

The party ended with a bang thanks to the Roman-candle fireworks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: end with a whimper - to finish in an unimpressive or disappointing way
