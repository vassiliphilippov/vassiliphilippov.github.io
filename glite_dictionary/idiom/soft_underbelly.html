soft underbelly

1. NOUN

/ sɔft ˈʌndɚˌbɛli / (AmE), / sɒft ˈʌndəˌbɛli / (BrE)
a vulnerable or weak point in an organization, system, or plan that can be easily attacked or exploited
vulnerable or weak point

Examples

Competitors are always looking for a company's soft underbelly to gain an advantage.
Cybersecurity threats often target the soft underbelly of even the most secure systems.
The city council's plan has a soft underbelly that critics are quick to expose.

Synset Examples

The report highlighted the soft underbelly of the company's strategy.
They found the soft underbelly of the competition's defense.
Ignoring the soft underbelly of your plan can lead to failure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weak spot - a place or aspect that is vulnerable or not strong
  • Synonym: Achilles' heel - a person's or thing's principal weakness

Etymology

From the literal meaning of a soft underside, made famous by Winston Churchill during World War II.
