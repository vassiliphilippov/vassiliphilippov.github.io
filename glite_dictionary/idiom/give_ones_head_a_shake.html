to give one's head a shake

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv wʌnz hɛd ə ʃeɪk / (AmE), / ɡɪv wʌnz hɛd ə ʃeɪk / (BrE)
to reconsider one's thoughts or actions in order to regain clarity or focus; to stop behaving irrationally
reconsider or regain focus

Examples

After getting the wrong answer, I had to give my head a shake and refocus.
Sometimes you just need to give your head a shake when you're overthinking things.
His friends told him to give his head a shake and stop worrying about it.

Synset Examples

She told him to give his head a shake and move on.
She told him to give his head a wobble and reconsider.
After that mistake, he should really give his head a wobble.
You need to give your head a shake and focus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to come to one's senses - to regain one's normal, rational state of mind

2. VERB

/ ɡɪv wʌnz hɛd ə ʃeɪk / (AmE), / ɡɪv wʌnz hɛd ə ʃeɪk / (BrE)
to reconsider or dismiss an idea, often after realizing it is unreasonable or mistaken
reconsider or dismiss an idea
informal

Examples

I almost agreed to the risky plan, but then I gave my head a shake and declined.
Sometimes you need to give your head a shake before making an important decision.
She was tempted to believe the rumor until she gave her head a shake and ignored it.

Synset Examples

After thinking it over, I gave my head a shake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to think twice - to reconsider; to think carefully before acting
  • Synonym: to come to one's senses - to begin to think sensibly; to realize one's mistake
