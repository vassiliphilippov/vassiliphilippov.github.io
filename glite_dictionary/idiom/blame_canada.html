to blame Canada

1. VERB

/ bleɪm ˈkænədə / (AmE), / bleɪm ˈkanədə / (BrE)
(humorous) Used to suggest, often facetiously, that Canada should be held responsible for a problem or mishap, typically when the blame is unreasonable or exaggerated.
jokingly assign blame to Canada
informal

Examples

Whenever something doesn't go as planned, he likes to blame Canada just to be funny.
In the office, if coffee runs out, someone inevitably quips, "Let's just blame Canada."
The comedian made the crowd laugh by repeatedly telling them to blame Canada for their minor woes.

Synset Examples

Whenever things go wrong, he just says blame Canada.
In the debate, they humorously suggested to blame Canada for everything.
They joked that the new policy was just another way to blame Canada.

Etymology

Popularized by the 1999 song 'Blame Canada' from the animated film South Park: Bigger, Longer & Uncut.
