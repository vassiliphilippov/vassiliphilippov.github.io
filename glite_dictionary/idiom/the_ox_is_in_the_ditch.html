the ox is in the ditch

1. X

/ ði ɑks ɪz ɪn ðə dɪtʃ / (AmE), / ði ɒks ɪz ɪn ðə dɪtʃ / (BrE)
a situation that requires urgent attention or immediate action
situation needing urgent action

Examples

Don’t worry about formalities—the ox is in the ditch and we need all hands on deck.
We rarely work Sundays, but this time the ox is in the ditch.
When the ox is in the ditch, everyone pitches in to solve the crisis.

Synset Examples

It’s time to help; the ox is in the ditch now.
We can't delay; the ox is in the ditch today.
When the ox is in the ditch, action is necessary.

Etymology

From a biblical reference in Luke 14:5, where helping an ox fallen into a pit is used to illustrate urgent necessity.
