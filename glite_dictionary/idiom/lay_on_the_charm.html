to lay on the charm

1. VERB

/ leɪ ɑn ðə tʃɑrm / (AmE), / leɪ ɒn ðə tʃɑːm / (BrE)
to behave in a very charming or flattering manner, often to achieve a particular aim
act in a deliberately charming way

Examples

Whenever he wants something, he really lays on the charm to get it.
She decided to lay on the charm at the dinner party to impress her boss.
Politicians often lay on the charm during election campaigns.

Synset Examples

He decided to lay on the charm during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pour on the charm - to behave very charmingly, often unexpectedly or for effect
