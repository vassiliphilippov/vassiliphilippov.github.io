under a cloud

1. ADV

/ ˈʌndɚ ə klaʊd / (AmE), / ˈʌndə ə klaʊd / (BrE)
in a state of suspicion, disfavor, or disgrace; feeling sad or depressed
in a state of suspicion or disgrace

Examples

Ever since the accusations surfaced, the politician has been under a cloud in the public eye.
She left the company under a cloud, her colleagues unsure of what really happened.
After the audit, the organization operated under a cloud of mistrust from investors.

Synset Examples

The company was under a cloud after the scandal.
He felt under a cloud after losing the election.
Their reputation was under a cloud due to rumors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in disgrace - having lost people's respect or approval
  • Antonym: in favor - in a state of approval or popularity
