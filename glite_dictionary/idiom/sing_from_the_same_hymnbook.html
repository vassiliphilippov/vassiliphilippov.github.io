to sing from the same hymnbook

1. VERB

/ sɪŋ frəm ðə seɪm ˈhɪmbʊk / (AmE), / sɪŋ frəm ðə seɪm ˈhɪmˌbʊk / (BrE)
to have or express the same opinions or ideas as others; to act in agreement or unity
act in agreement; be in harmony

Examples

To achieve our goals, it's important that we all sing from the same hymnbook.
After the meeting, the management team began to sing from the same hymnbook about the new strategy.
During the press conference, the politicians made sure to sing from the same hymnbook on the main issues.

Synset Examples

We need to sing from the same hymnbook to succeed.
They finally decided to sing from the same hymnbook.
It’s crucial we all sing from the same hymnbook.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be on the same page - to be in agreement or have the same understanding
  • Antonym: to disagree - to have a different opinion

Etymology

From the practice of singing the same song during religious services, using a hymnbook.
