to be a berk

1. VERB

/ bi ə bɜrk / (AmE), / biː ə bɜːk / (BrE)
to behave in a foolish or stupid manner; to act like an idiot
act foolishly or stupidly (informal, British)
informal british

Examples

Stop being a berk and listen to what I'm saying.
If you are a berk at work, people may stop taking you seriously.
He realised he was being a berk when he forgot her birthday again.

Synset Examples

Don't be a berk and forget your keys again!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fool around - to behave in a silly or irresponsible way
  • Synonym: be an idiot - to act in a way characteristic of an idiot
