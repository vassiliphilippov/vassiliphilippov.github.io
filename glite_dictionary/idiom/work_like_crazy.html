to work like crazy

1. VERB

/ wɝk laɪk ˈkreɪzi / (AmE), / wɜːk laɪk ˈkreɪzi / (BrE)
to work extremely hard or with great effort, often for a sustained period
to work extremely hard
informal

Examples

She had to work like crazy to finish her thesis before the end of the semester.
If we work like crazy for the next few days, we can get the project done on time.
They worked like crazy all week to prepare for the big presentation.

Synset Examples

He worked like crazy to meet the deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: work hard - to put in a great deal of effort while working
  • Antonym: to relax - to rest or take it easy, not working hard
