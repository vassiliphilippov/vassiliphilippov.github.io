where there's smoke, there's fire

1. X

/ wɛr ðɛrz smoʊk ðɛrz faɪr / (AmE), / weə ðəz sməʊk ðəz faɪə / (BrE)
used to say that if there are signs of a problem or wrongdoing, there is likely to be some truth to the rumors or suspicions
rumors are often based on some truth

Examples

Many people believe that where there's smoke, there's fire when it comes to rumors about celebrities.
Despite his denials, some thought that where there's smoke, there's fire and suspected he was involved.
She tries not to gossip, but often where there's smoke, there's fire.

Synset Examples

She believes where there's smoke, there's fire in the gossip.
He cautioned that where there's smoke, there's fire in this case.
They say where there's smoke, there's fire regarding the rumors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: no smoke without fire - British variant of the idiom meaning rumors are often based on reality
