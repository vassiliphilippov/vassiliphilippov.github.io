to float like a butterfly

1. VERB

/ floʊt laɪk ə ˈbʌtɚˌflaɪ / (AmE), / fləʊt laɪk ə ˈbʌtəflaɪ / (BrE)
to move with light, graceful, and agile steps, often with a sense of ease and elegance
move gracefully and lightly

Examples

During the performance, the ballerina seemed to float like a butterfly across the stage.
He tried to float like a butterfly while sparring in boxing practice.
Her footsteps were so light, she appeared to float like a butterfly through the garden.

Synset Examples

His boxing style allowed him to float like a butterfly.
He danced across the stage, floating like a butterfly.
In the ring, she moved gracefully, floating like a butterfly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glide - to move smoothly and continuously as if without effort or resistance

Etymology

Popularized by the phrase 'float like a butterfly, sting like a bee,' associated with the American boxer Muhammad Ali (1942–2016).
