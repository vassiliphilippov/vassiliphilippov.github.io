to kick up a fuss

1. VERB

/ kɪk ʌp ə fʌs / (AmE), / kɪk ʌp ə fʌs / (BrE)
to complain loudly or make a disturbance, often over something small or trivial
complain loudly or cause a disturbance

Examples

If you kick up a fuss every time something goes wrong, people may stop listening to you.
The customer started to kick up a fuss when her order arrived late.
There's no need to kick up a fuss; it was just a small mistake.

Synset Examples

Don't kick up a fuss; just accept the changes.
He always kicks up a fuss when things don't go his way.
She kicked up a fuss over the unfair treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make a scene - to behave in a loud or noticeable way in public because you are angry or upset
  • Synonym: raise a ruckus - to create a commotion or disturbance
  • Antonym: to keep quiet - to remain silent or not complain
