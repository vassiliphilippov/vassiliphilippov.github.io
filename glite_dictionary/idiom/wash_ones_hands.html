to wash one's hands

1. VERB

/ wɑʃ wʌnz hændz / (AmE), / wɒʃ wʌnz hændz / (BrE)
to disclaim or remove responsibility for something; to refuse to be involved with or assume any blame for an action or outcome
disclaim responsibility; refuse further involvement

Examples

After the project failed, the manager decided to wash his hands of the situation.
She made it clear she would wash her hands of the matter if the conflict continued.
The company tried to wash its hands of any responsibility after the incident.

Synset Examples

I wash my hands of this

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disown - to refuse to acknowledge or be associated with something or someone
  • Antonym: assume responsibility - to take responsibility for something

Etymology

Alludes to the biblical account of Pontius Pilate washing his hands to show lack of responsibility for Jesus's fate.
