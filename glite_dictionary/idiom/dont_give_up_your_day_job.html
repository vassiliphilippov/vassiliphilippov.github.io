don't give up your day job

1. X

/ doʊnt ɡɪv ʌp jʊr deɪ dʒɑb / (AmE), / dəʊnt ɡɪv ʌp jə deɪ dʒɒb / (BrE)
used to humorously suggest that someone is not good enough at something to do it professionally, so they should continue doing their regular work.
humorous advice not to pursue an activity professionally

Examples

After hearing his attempt at singing, I told him, don't give up your day job.
She tried her hand at stand-up comedy, but her friends said, don't give up your day job.
When his painting was met with laughter, someone joked, 'don't give up your day job.'

Synset Examples

She was told, after her bad joke, don't give up your day job.
When he sang off-key, I laughed, don't give up your day job.
After his performance, we said, don't give up your day job.
