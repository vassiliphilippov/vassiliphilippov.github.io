to see past the end of one's nose

1. VERB

/ si pæst ði ɛnd əv wʌnz noʊz / (AmE), / siː pɑːst ðiː end əv wʌnz nəʊz / (BrE)
to consider or understand things beyond one's immediate concerns or personal interests
think beyond immediate concerns

Examples

Maria can't see past the end of her nose when making decisions at work.
Visionary leaders must see past the end of their noses to anticipate future challenges.
If you never see past the end of your nose, progress will be difficult.

Synset Examples

She often fails to see past the end of her nose when planning.
He needs to learn to see past the end of his nose.
In business, it's crucial to see past the end of your nose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: short-sighted - not considering the long-term results of actions
  • Synonym: narrow-minded - not willing to accept ideas or ways of behaving that are different from your own
