to have a problem

1. VERB

/ hæv ə ˈprɑːbləm / (AmE), / hæv ə ˈprɒbləm / (BrE)
to experience or encounter a difficulty or issue; to find something troublesome or objectionable
encounter a difficulty or issue

Examples

If you have a problem with the software, please contact technical support for assistance.
She didn't want to admit that she had a problem trusting new people after her experience.
Do you have a problem with the way I handled the project?

Synset Examples

You seem frustrated; what is your problem today?
What is your problem with the new policy?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to face a challenge - confront a difficult situation
  • Antonym: have no problem - not have any difficulty or issue
