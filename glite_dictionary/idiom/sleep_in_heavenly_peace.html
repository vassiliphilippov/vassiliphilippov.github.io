to sleep in heavenly peace

1. VERB

/ sliːp ɪn ˈhɛvənli piːs / (AmE), / sliːp ɪn ˈhɛvənli piːs / (BrE)
to die peacefully, often used as a gentle or comforting expression regarding someone's passing
to die peacefully (euphemistic)

Examples

After a long illness, she finally slept in heavenly peace surrounded by her family.
We pray that all those lost may sleep in heavenly peace.
His obituary said he slept in heavenly peace after a life well lived.

Synset Examples

Her final wish was to sleep in heavenly peace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pass away - to die (polite/formal euphemism)
  • Synonym: die - to stop living

Etymology

From the line 'sleep in heavenly peace' in the 19th-century Christmas carol 'Silent Night', now used figuratively as a euphemism for dying peacefully.
