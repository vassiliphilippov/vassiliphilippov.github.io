to lead the line

1. VERB

/ lid ðə laɪn / (AmE), / liːd ðə laɪn / (BrE)
to be at the forefront of a group and act as its leader or principal performer
be the leader or main performer of a group

Examples

As an experienced player, he was expected to lead the line for his club.
During the opening ceremony, the choreographer asked her to lead the line.
He proudly led the line of dancers onto the stage.

Synset Examples

He was eager to lead the line during the parade.
She was chosen to lead the line in the performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lead - to go at the front or be in charge
  • Antonym: to follow - to go or come after someone or something
