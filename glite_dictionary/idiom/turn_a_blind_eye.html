to turn a blind eye

1. VERB

/ tɝn ə blaɪnd aɪ / (AmE), / tɜːn ə blaɪnd aɪ / (BrE)
to deliberately ignore something that you know is wrong or unacceptable
deliberately ignore wrongdoing

Examples

The manager chose to turn a blind eye to the team's frequent tardiness.
Many governments turn a blind eye to environmental violations.
She couldn't turn a blind eye any longer to the signs of neglect.

Synset Examples

He turns a blind eye to the injustices in his office

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ignore - to refuse to take notice of or acknowledge
  • Synonym: overlook - to fail to notice or consider; to ignore on purpose

Etymology

Reportedly related to Admiral Nelson's alleged use of his blind eye to avoid seeing a signal during battle.

2. VERB

/ tɝn ə blaɪnd aɪ / (AmE), / tɜːn ə blaɪnd aɪ / (BrE)
to deliberately ignore something wrong or illegal, pretending not to notice it
deliberately ignore wrongdoing

Examples

The teacher decided to turn a blind eye to the playful note passing during class.
Many government officials were accused of turning a blind eye to corruption in the city.
She often turns a blind eye to her son's mischief, believing he will learn from experience.

Synset Examples

Sometimes we must turn a blind eye to keep the peace.
He couldn't turn a blind eye to the injustice happening nearby.
The manager decided to turn a blind eye to the minor mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ignore - to refuse to take notice of something or someone
  • Synonym: overlook - to fail to notice or to choose to disregard something

Etymology

Popularly believed to originate from Admiral Nelson, who supposedly put a telescope to his blind eye to ignore a signal.
