to have one's chips

1. VERB

/ hæv wʌnz tʃɪps / (AmE), / hæv wʌnz tʃɪps / (BrE)
(informal, British) To be in serious trouble, defeated, or out of options, often with the implication that there is no way back.
be in serious trouble or finished
informal british

Examples

If he keeps making mistakes like this, he'll have his chips by the end of the month.
When the police arrived, I knew I had my chips and there was no escape.
She lied to the boss, and now she might have her chips at the company.

Synset Examples

If he doesn't apologize, he's had his chips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be done for - to be in serious trouble or defeated
  • Synonym: be out of luck - to have no more chances or options left

Etymology

British idiom, refers figuratively to casino chips—when one has lost all chips, one is out of the game.
