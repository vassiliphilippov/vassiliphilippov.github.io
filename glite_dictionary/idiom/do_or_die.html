do or die

1. X

/ duː ɔr daɪ / (AmE), / duː ɔː daɪ / (BrE)
used to describe a situation in which one must succeed because failure is not an option
critical situation demanding success

Examples

It's a do or die moment for the entire project team.
Knowing it was do or die, she gave the performance everything she had.
With seconds left on the clock, the match became a do or die battle.

Synset Examples

It's do or die for the team in this final match.
He felt it was do or die when facing his biggest challenge.
In this situation, it's really do or die for us.
In a do-or-die battle, every move counts.
It’s a do-or-die situation; we must act now.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sink or swim - to be left to succeed or fail on one's own
