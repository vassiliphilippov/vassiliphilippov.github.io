to stick it to the man

1. VERB

/ stɪk ɪt tu ðə mæn / (AmE), / stɪk ɪt tʊ ðə mæn / (BrE)
to openly resist, defy, or challenge those in power or authority, especially in a rebellious or subversive way
resist or challenge authority
informal

Examples

He tried to stick it to the man by voting against the proposed regulation.
Teenagers often feel the urge to stick it to the man through small acts of rebellion.
By exposing the corporation's wrongdoing, the whistleblower managed to stick it to the man.

Synset Examples

He always finds ways to stick it to the man.
They plan to stick it to the man with their protest.
She believes it's important to stick it to the man at every opportunity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to defy - to openly resist or refuse to obey
  • Antonym: to submit - to yield to the authority or will of another
