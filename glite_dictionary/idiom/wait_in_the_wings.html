to wait in the wings

1. VERB

/ weɪt ɪn ðə wɪŋz / (AmE), / weɪt ɪn ðə wɪŋz / (BrE)
to be ready and prepared to take action or assume a role when the opportunity arises, often while staying out of the spotlight
be ready for an opportunity

Examples

Although he was not the main team leader, Tom continued to wait in the wings for his moment to shine.
Several ambitious employees wait in the wings hoping to step into the manager's position.
She had been waiting in the wings for years before finally taking over as CEO.

Synset Examples

The backup singer waited in the wings during the performance.
She has always been ready to wait in the wings for her opportunity.
Several actors wait in the wings for their turn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bide one's time - to wait patiently for a good opportunity

Etymology

Originally from theater, referring to actors standing offstage (in the wings) ready to perform.
