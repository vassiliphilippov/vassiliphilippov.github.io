to stay out on one's feet

1. VERB

/ steɪ aʊt ɑn wʌnz fit / (AmE), / steɪ aʊt ɒn wʌnz fiːt / (BrE)
to remain active, conscious, or standing despite tiredness, difficulty, or adverse circumstances
remain standing or active despite fatigue

Examples

After running the marathon, she managed to stay out on her feet until the very end.
The boxer was visibly exhausted but continued to stay out on his feet through all the rounds.
Despite feeling ill, he tried to stay out on his feet during the important meeting.

Synset Examples

He managed to stay out on his feet despite the fatigue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keep going - to continue without stopping, especially despite difficulties
