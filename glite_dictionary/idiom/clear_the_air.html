to clear the air

1. VERB

/ klɪr ðə ɛr / (AmE), / klɪə ðiː eə / (BrE)
to resolve misunderstandings or negative feelings, especially by discussing a problem openly
resolve misunderstandings or bad feelings

Examples

After their heated argument, they met to clear the air and restore their friendship.
Sometimes a frank conversation is enough to clear the air in the workplace.
She apologized to clear the air and move forward with her team.

Synset Examples

The group called a meeting to finally clear the air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mend fences - to repair a relationship or restore harmony
  • Synonym: to let bygones be bygones - to forget past disagreements and move on
