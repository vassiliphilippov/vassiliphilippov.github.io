Montezuma's revenge

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑn.təˈzuː.məz rɪˈvɛndʒ / (AmE), / ˌmɒn.tɪˈzjuː.məz rɪˈvɛnʤ / (BrE)
An informal term for traveler's diarrhea, especially that contracted by tourists in Mexico, typically due to consuming contaminated food or water.
traveler's diarrhea (Mexico, informal)
informal

Examples

Many tourists suffer from Montezuma's revenge after eating street food in Central America.
After just two days in Mexico, he was already feeling the effects of Montezuma's revenge.
Doctors advised her to drink only bottled water to avoid getting Montezuma's revenge.

Synset Examples

He spent the night battling Montezuma's revenge after the trip.
Travelers are often warned about Montezuma's revenge in Mexico.
After eating street food, I caught Montezuma's revenge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traveler's diarrhea - mild illness causing diarrhea contracted while traveling

Etymology

Named humorously after Montezuma II, Aztec emperor, as a form of revenge against foreigners.
