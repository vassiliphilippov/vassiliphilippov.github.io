to up the ante

1. VERB

/ ʌp ði ˈænti / (AmE), / ʌp ði ˈænti / (BrE)
to increase the level of risk, demands, or stakes in a situation
increase the stakes or demands

Examples

The team decided to up the ante by hiring a top consultant before the final presentation.
She always finds a way to up the ante when planning her parties, making each one grander than the last.
As negotiations stalled, the company chose to up the ante with a more generous offer.

Synset Examples

He decided to up the ante with a bigger bet.
The competition is fierce, so we must up the ante.
To win the game, you'll need to up the ante.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to raise the stakes - to increase the level of risk or challenge

Etymology

From poker, where 'ante' refers to the minimum stake; 'up the ante' originally meant to increase this stake.
