to knock down for the count

1. VERB

/ nɑk daʊn fər ðə kaʊnt / (AmE), / nɒk daʊn fə ðə kaʊnt / (BrE)
to be knocked down and unable to continue, especially in a boxing match; to be so defeated or overwhelmed that recovery is not possible
unable to recover after being defeated

Examples

After three brutal rounds, the champion was knocked down for the count and lost the match.
The business was knocked down for the count by the economic crisis and never recovered.
He tried to stand after the blow, but was knocked down for the count.

Synset Examples

The boxer was knocked down for the count in the sixth round.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to knock out - to defeat someone so they cannot continue, especially in boxing

Etymology

From boxing terminology, where 'for the count' refers to a referee's count to determine a knockout.
