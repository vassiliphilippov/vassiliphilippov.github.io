to think of England

1. VERB

/ θɪŋk əv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (AmE), / θɪŋk əv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (BrE)
to mentally distance oneself or focus on something else in order to endure an unpleasant or uncomfortable experience.
mentally escape during discomfort
british

Examples

During the tedious meeting, she simply thought of England and waited for it to end.
Whenever the dentist started drilling, he closed his eyes and thought of England.
He endured his neighbor’s relentless stories by sitting quietly and thinking of England.

Synset Examples

He closed his eyes and thought of England during the awkward moment.
He closed his eyes and thought of England when the conversation turned awkward.
She just closed her eyes and thought of England during the dentist visit.

Etymology

Early 20th-century British origin, originally a euphemism advising women to mentally escape to endure marital sex.
