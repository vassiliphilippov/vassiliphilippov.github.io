what do you want, blood?

1. X

/ wʌt du ju wɑnt blʌd / (AmE), / wɒt dʊ jʊ wɒnt blʌd / (BrE)
used as a rhetorical question to accuse someone of making unreasonable or excessive demands
expression for excessive demands

Examples

After all I've given you, what do you want, blood?
She kept adding requests until he finally asked, 'what do you want, blood?'
If a simple apology isn't enough, what do you want, blood?

Synset Examples

Asking for a raise during the meeting felt like what do you want: blood?
He was so demanding, it felt like what do you want: blood?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: what more do you want? - used to challenge further demands as excessive
