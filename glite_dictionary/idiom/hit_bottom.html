to hit bottom

1. VERB

/ hɪt ˈbɑːtəm / (AmE), / hɪt ˈbɒtəm / (BrE)
to reach the lowest point or worst state, especially after a period of decline or difficulty
reach the lowest or worst point

Examples

After his business failed, Tom felt that he had hit bottom emotionally and financially.
Many economic analysts believe the housing market hit bottom last year and is now improving.
Some addicts only seek help after they hit bottom and see no way out.

Synset Examples

After months of struggles, he finally hit bottom financially.
The stock market hit bottom, prompting investors to buy.
She felt she had hit bottom before finding a new job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rock bottom - the lowest possible level or point
  • Antonym: to recover - to return to a normal or improved state after decline
