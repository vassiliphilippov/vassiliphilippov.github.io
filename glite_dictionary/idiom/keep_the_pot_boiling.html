to keep the pot boiling

1. VERB

/ kip ðə pɑt ˈbɔɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / kiːp ðə pɒt ˈbɔɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
to maintain activity, excitement, or interest in a situation so that it continues to progress or attract attention
maintain interest or activity

Examples

The manager made sure to keep the pot boiling by encouraging regular brainstorming sessions.
She introduced exciting rewards to keep the pot boiling among her team members.
The author released teaser chapters to keep the pot boiling for her upcoming novel.
