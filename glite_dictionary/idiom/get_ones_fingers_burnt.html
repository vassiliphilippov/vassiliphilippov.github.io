to get one's fingers burnt

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt wʌnz ˈfɪŋɡɚz bɝːnt / (AmE), / ɡɛt wʌnz ˈfɪŋɡəz bɜːnt / (BrE)
to suffer unpleasant consequences or loss as a result of a risky or unwise action
suffer loss from a risky action

Examples

She invested heavily in real estate last year and got her fingers burnt when the market collapsed.
Many first-time entrepreneurs get their fingers burnt by underestimating the challenges of running a business.
After getting his fingers burnt once, he now avoids risky investments.

Synset Examples

He got his fingers burnt by investing too quickly.
They got their fingers burnt in the stock market crash.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burn oneself - to experience harm or damage oneself, literally or figuratively

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt wʌnz ˈfɪŋɡərz bɜrnt / (AmE), / ɡɛt wʌnz ˈfɪŋɡəz bɜːnt / (BrE)
to experience negative consequences as a result of one's actions, especially after taking a risk
suffer consequences after a risky action

Examples

Many investors get their fingers burnt when the stock market crashes.
He warned me not to try it or I'd get my fingers burnt.
After her last business failed, she became cautious about new ventures, not wanting to get her fingers burnt again.

Synset Examples

After that deal, she learned to be cautious and not get her fingers burnt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to burn one's fingers - to suffer because of one's actions, often by taking risks; same idiom with different form
