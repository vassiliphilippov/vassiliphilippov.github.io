clear sailing

1. NOUN

/ klɪr ˈseɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / klɪə ˈseɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
a situation in which progress is easy and without obstacles or difficulties
easy, unobstructed progress

Examples

Once the negotiations were settled, it was clear sailing to finalize the contract.
After the first mile, the marathon became clear sailing for the experienced runners.
With all the obstacles removed, the project team enjoyed clear sailing toward completion.

Synset Examples

after we solved that problem the rest was plain sailing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plain sailing - easy and uncomplicated progress or task

Etymology

Nautical origin: refers to sailing in waters free of hazards or obstructions, used figuratively since the 19th century.
