to make short work of

1. VERB

/ meɪk ʃɔrt wɝk əv / (AmE), / meɪk ʃɔːt wɜːk ɒv / (BrE)
to deal with or finish something quickly and efficiently
finish something quickly and efficiently

Examples

Despite the size of the project, the team managed to make short work of it.
She always seems to make short work of her homework and still gets top marks.
The chef can make short work of chopping vegetables thanks to his skill.

Synset Examples
