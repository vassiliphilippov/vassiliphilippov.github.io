to put cards on the table

1. VERB

/ pʊt kɑrdz ɑn ðə ˈteɪbəl / (AmE), / pʊt kɑːdz ɒn ðə ˈteɪbl / (BrE)
to be completely open and honest about one's intentions, plans, or feelings
be open and honest about intentions

Examples

It's important to put cards on the table before making any serious business decisions.
During the discussion, she decided to put cards on the table and share her real concerns.
He asked the team to put cards on the table so they could find a solution together.

Synset Examples

She finally put her cards on the table about the project.
He put his cards on the table during the negotiation.
It's time to put your cards on the table and be honest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to come clean - to confess or admit the truth openly
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep something secret or hidden
