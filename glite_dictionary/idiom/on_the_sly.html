on the sly

1. ADV

/ ɑn ðə slaɪ / (AmE), / ɒn ðə slaɪ / (BrE)
secretly or in a furtive, sneaky manner, especially to avoid detection or notice
secretly; furtively

Examples

She had been meeting her friend on the sly because her parents disapproved.
He managed to take some snacks on the sly during the meeting.
The children watched television on the sly after their bedtime.

Synset Examples

the soldiers were furtively crawling through the night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secretly - without others knowing; in secret
  • Antonym: openly - in an open or public way; not secret

2. ADV

/ ɑn ðə slaɪ / (AmE), / ɒn ðə slaɪ / (BrE)
In a secret or stealthy manner; without others knowing.
secretly; without being noticed

Examples

She saved money on the sly for a vacation without telling anyone.
He sometimes eats extra snacks on the sly after dinner.
They met on the sly because their families disapproved.

Synset Examples

She was working on the sly to surprise him.
He tried to sneak out on the sly without anyone noticing.
They arranged the meeting on the sly to avoid detection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secretly - in a way that is hidden from others or not intended to be known
  • Antonym: openly - in a way that is not secret; without hiding
