to have someone's head

1. VERB

/ hæv ˈsʌmwʌnz hɛd / (AmE), / hæv ˈsʌmwʌnz hɛd / (BrE)
(informal) to be very angry with someone or to punish them severely.
to be very angry or punish severely
informal

Examples

If you tell anyone what happened, she'll have your head.
When the boss finds out about this mistake, he'll have my head for sure.
You forgot to lock the door—your mother will have your head when she gets home.

Synset Examples

If you mess this up, you'll have my head!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chew someone out - to scold someone severely
  • Antonym: let someone off - to punish less severely or not at all
