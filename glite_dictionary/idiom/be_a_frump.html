to be a frump

1. VERB

/ bi ə frʌmp / (AmE), / biː ə frʌmp / (BrE)
to behave or dress in an unfashionable, dull, or dowdy manner
act or dress in an unfashionable way

Examples

She refused to be a frump at the party and chose a bright, stylish dress instead.
Just because it's cold outside doesn't mean you have to be a frump.
I don't want to be a frump anymore, so I'm updating my wardrobe.

Synset Examples

Don't be a frump; wear something more colorful!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frumpy - (of a person or clothing) dowdy, unfashionable

Etymology

From 'frump', meaning an unattractive or dowdy person, possibly from 17th-century English.
