in bad odor

1. ADJ

/ ɪn bæd ˈoʊdər / (AmE), / ɪn bad ˈəʊdə / (BrE)
in disfavor or regarded with disapproval; not liked or trusted by others, especially following wrongdoing or scandal
in disfavor or public disapproval

Examples

After the failed merger, the CEO was in bad odor with the board of directors.
Ever since the incident, she has remained in bad odor among her peers.
The policy left the administration in bad odor with the public.

Synset Examples

He found himself in bad odor with his colleagues.
After the scandal, the company was in bad odor.
His comments left him in bad odor with management.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: in good odor - in favor or regarded positively; in public approval or esteem

Etymology

From the use of 'odor' in the sense of reputation or character, influenced by the idea that a bad smell metaphorically indicates something unfavorable.
