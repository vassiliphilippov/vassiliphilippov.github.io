let's be having you

1. X

/ ˌlɛts bi ˈhævɪŋ ju / (AmE), / ˌlɛts bi ˈhavɪŋ juː / (BrE)
used to call or invite someone to join, come forward, or come inside, especially in an encouraging or mildly impatient way
invitation to join or come in
british

Examples

Dinner's ready, so let's be having you at the table.
Come on now, it's time to go—let's be having you.
You're still outside in the cold—let's be having you in here!

Synset Examples

You’re late; let’s be having you inside now.
It's time for dinner, so let’s be having you at the table.
