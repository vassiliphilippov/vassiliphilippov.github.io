to enter the equation

1. VERB

/ ˈɛn.tɚ ði ɪˈkweɪ.ʒən / (AmE), / ˈɛn.tə ði ɪˈkweɪ.ʒən / (BrE)
to become an important factor or consideration in a situation
become a factor in a situation

Examples

When cost efficiency enters the equation, our strategy will need to change.
Her expertise entered the equation during the crisis talks.
New government regulations have recently entered the equation for tech companies.

Synset Examples

His experience will enter the equation in the decision-making process.
We need to enter the equation to solve this problem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: come into play - to begin to have an effect or influence on a situation
