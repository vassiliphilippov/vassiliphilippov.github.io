mess of pottage

1. NOUN

/ mɛs əv ˈpɑtɪdʒ / (AmE), / mɛs əv ˈpɒtɪdʒ / (BrE)
something of little or trivial value accepted in exchange for something much more valuable, often with lasting regret
something trivial accepted for something valuable

Examples

She gave up her promising career for a mess of pottage and regretted it deeply.
Don't exchange your principles for a mess of pottage when you face temptation.
He realized too late that he had sold his dreams for a mess of pottage.

Synset Examples

Don't sacrifice your future for a mess of pottage.
Her decision felt like a mess of pottage in hindsight.
He sold his birthright for a mess of pottage.
He traded his birthright for a mess of pottage.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: birthright - a particular right of possession or privilege one has from birth
  • Synonym: triviality - something of little value or importance

Etymology

From the biblical story of Esau, who traded his birthright for a meal of lentil stew ('pottage').
