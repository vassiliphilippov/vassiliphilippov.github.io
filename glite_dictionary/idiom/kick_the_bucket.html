to kick the bucket

1. VERB

/ kɪk ðə ˈbʌkət / (AmE), / kɪk ðə ˈbʌkɪt / (BrE)
(informal) To die; to pass away.
to die (informal idiom)
informal

Examples

Nobody expected Uncle George to kick the bucket so suddenly.
When the cat kicked the bucket, the whole family was devastated.
Some people like to write a will before they kick the bucket.

Synset Examples

She died from cancer
The children perished in the fire
The patient went peacefully
The old guy kicked the bucket at the age of 102

Related Terms

  • Synonym: die - to stop living; to cease to be alive
  • Synonym: to pass away - to die (a more polite or gentle expression)

Etymology

Origin uncertain; dates back to the 18th century in English slang.

2. VERB

/ kɪk ðə ˈbʌkɪt / (AmE), / kɪk ðə ˈbʌkɪt / (BrE)
to die (used informally or humorously)
to die (informal idiom)
informal

Examples

He has lived a long and interesting life, but last year he finally kicked the bucket.
She wrote a letter to her children to be read in case she kicks the bucket unexpectedly.
People often make jokes about what they'll do before they kick the bucket.

Synset Examples

She left a will in case she kicked the bucket unexpectedly.
He joked about kicking the bucket before his birthday.
After all these years, he finally kicked the bucket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: die - to stop living; to cease to be alive
  • Synonym: to pass away - to die, often used euphemistically
