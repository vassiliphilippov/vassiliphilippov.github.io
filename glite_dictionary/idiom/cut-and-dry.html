cut-and-dry

1. ADJ

/ ˌkʌt ənd ˈdraɪ / (AmE), / ˌkʌt ənd ˈdraɪ / (BrE)
(of a situation or explanation) clear and straightforward, and not likely to be changed; according to what is usual or expected.
clear and straightforward; as expected

Examples

The instructions were not cut-and-dry, which made assembling the furniture difficult.
Their divorce agreement was anything but cut-and-dry, as there were many complications to resolve.
He prefers cut-and-dry solutions that follow standard procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clear-cut - definite and easy to understand
  • Antonym: ambiguous - open to more than one interpretation

Etymology

Originally from the phrase 'cut and dried,' referring to herbs prepared for sale. Applied figuratively since the 18th century.
