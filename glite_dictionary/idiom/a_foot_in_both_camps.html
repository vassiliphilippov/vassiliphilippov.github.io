a foot in both camps

1. X

/ ə fʊt ɪn boʊθ kæmps / (AmE), / ə fʊt ɪn bəʊθ kæmps / (BrE)
to be involved or supporting two opposing sides or groups at the same time
supporting opposing sides simultaneously (idiom)

Examples

Because she advises two competing companies, she effectively has a foot in both camps.
Journalists must avoid having a foot in both camps when covering political issues.
He was accused of keeping a foot in both camps to maximize his influence.

Synset Examples

She has a foot in both camps, working for rivals.
It's tricky when you have a foot in both camps.
He has a foot in both camps with his dual roles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on the fence - undecided between two options or sides
