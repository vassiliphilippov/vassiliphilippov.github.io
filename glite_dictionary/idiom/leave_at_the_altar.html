to leave at the altar

1. VERB

/ liv æt ði ˈɔltɚ / (AmE), / liːv ət ði ˈɔːltə / (BrE)
to abandon someone at the last possible moment before marriage, especially at the wedding ceremony
abandon a partner before marriage, especially at the ceremony

Examples

She was heartbroken when he left at the altar on their wedding day.
Everyone in the church gasped when the bride was left at the altar.
Some people fear commitment so much they might leave at the altar.

Synset Examples
