to give the elbow

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ði ˈɛlˌboʊ / (AmE), / ɡɪv ði ˈɛl.bəʊ / (BrE)
to reject or dismiss someone, especially from a relationship or job
reject or dismiss someone
british informal

Examples

After years of arguments, she finally decided to give the elbow to her boyfriend.
When the team performed poorly, the coach didn't hesitate to give the elbow to several players.
He knew his boss was about to give the elbow because of his repeated mistakes.

Synset Examples

He was quick to give the elbow to any rude behavior.
She decided to give him the elbow after he lied.
The manager had to give the elbow to the underperforming staff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dump - to end a romantic relationship with someone
  • Synonym: to dismiss - to remove someone from their job or position

Etymology

Likely from the image of pushing someone away with one's elbow. Attested in British slang from the 19th century.
