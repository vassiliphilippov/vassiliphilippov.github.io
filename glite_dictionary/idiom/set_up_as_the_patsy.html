to set up as the patsy

1. VERB

/ sɛt ʌp æz ðə ˈpætsi / (AmE), / sɛt ʌp æz ðə ˈpætsi / (BrE)
to make someone appear to be guilty or responsible for something they did not do, usually by deliberate deception
to frame someone to take the blame

Examples

He realized too late that he had been set up as the patsy by his colleagues.
The company executive was set up as the patsy for the financial scandal.
In the movie, an innocent man is set up as the patsy to divert attention from the real criminals.

Synset Examples

The detective realized he was being set up as the patsy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frame - to make someone appear guilty by falsifying evidence
  • Synonym: scapegoat - a person who is blamed for the wrongdoings of others
