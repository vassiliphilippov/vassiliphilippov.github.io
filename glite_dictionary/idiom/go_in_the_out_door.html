to go in the out door

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ɪn ði aʊt dɔr / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɪn ði aʊt dɔː / (BrE)
To act in a contradictory, illogical, or nonsensical manner, especially by doing the opposite of what is expected or advised.
act in a contradictory or illogical way

Examples

Trying to go in the out door will just create confusion and frustration for everyone involved.
He tends to go in the out door when faced with rules he doesn't like.
If you always go in the out door, don't be surprised if things don't go smoothly.

Synset Examples

He always tries to go in the out door during rush hour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go against the grain - act in a way that is contrary to what is expected or normal
