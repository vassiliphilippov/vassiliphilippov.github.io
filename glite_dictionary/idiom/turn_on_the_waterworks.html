to turn on the waterworks

1. VERB

/ tɝn ɑn ðə ˈwɔtɚˌwɜrks / (AmE), / tɜːn ɒn ðə ˈwɔːtəˌwɜːks / (BrE)
to begin crying, often in a way meant to elicit sympathy or manipulate others
start crying (often for effect)

Examples

When he couldn't get his way, he decided to turn on the waterworks in front of everyone.
She tends to turn on the waterworks whenever she wants someone to feel sorry for her.
The little boy tried to turn on the waterworks, but his mother wasn't fooled.

Synset Examples

He always knows how to turn on the waterworks.
Don't turn on the waterworks; it won't work this time.
She turned on the waterworks to gain sympathy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cry - to shed tears, especially as an expression of emotion
  • Synonym: to weep - to shed tears or grieve

Etymology

Alluding to 'waterworks' as a humorous term for tears; figurative use of 'turn on' (to start, as with a tap/faucet).
