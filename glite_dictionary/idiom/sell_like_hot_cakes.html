to sell like hot cakes

1. VERB

/ sɛl laɪk hɑt keɪks / (AmE), / sɛl laɪk hɒt keɪks / (BrE)
to be bought quickly and in large quantities; to be extremely popular or in high demand
be sold very quickly

Examples

The new smartphone is expected to sell like hot cakes once it’s released.
Homemade jam from that shop tends to sell like hot cakes during the holidays.
Tickets for the concert sold like hot cakes and were gone within an hour.

Synset Examples

Her cookies sell like hot cakes at the fair.
These new shoes are selling like hot cakes.
The tickets sold like hot cakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fly off the shelves - to be sold quickly and in large numbers

Etymology

Refers to hot cakes (pancakes) selling quickly at markets or fairs in the 19th century.
