take a long walk on a short pier

1. X

/ teɪk ə lɔŋ wɑk ɑn ə ʃɔrt pɪr / (AmE), / teɪk ə lɒŋ wɔːk ɒn ə ʃɔːt pɪə / (BrE)
(sarcastic) to tell someone to go away or leave, often with an implication of annoyance or dismissal.
sarcastically tell someone to go away

Examples

When he wouldn't stop bothering me, I told him to take a long walk on a short pier.
If my neighbor complains again, I'll just say, 'Why don't you take a long walk on a short pier?'
Tired of the criticism, she snapped and told the reporter to take a long walk on a short pier.

Synset Examples

He told his boss to take a long walk on a short pier.
If she keeps complaining, I might suggest she take a long walk on a short pier.
After their argument, he wanted her to take a long walk on a short pier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get lost - to go away; leave immediately
  • Synonym: to buzz off - (informal) go away; leave someone alone
