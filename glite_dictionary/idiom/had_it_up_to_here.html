to had it up to here

1. VERB

/ hæd ɪt ʌp tu hɪr / (AmE), / hæd ɪt ʌp tə hɪə / (BrE)
used to say that one can no longer tolerate a situation because it has become too annoying or frustrating
reached the limit of patience or tolerance

Examples

I've had it up to here with your constant complaining.
She told her boss she had had it up to here and needed a change.
They've had it up to here with the broken promises from management.

Synset Examples

We've had it up to here with the delays.
She's had it up to here with his excuses.
I've had it up to here with the noise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fed up - annoyed or upset at a situation one can no longer tolerate
  • Synonym: at the end of one's rope - having no more patience or endurance left
