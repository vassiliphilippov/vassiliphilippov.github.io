to curry favour

1. VERB

/ ˈkʌri ˈfeɪvər / (AmE), / ˈkʌri ˈfeɪvə / (BrE)
to try to gain someone's approval or advantage by using insincere praise or flattery
seek approval by flattery

Examples

She tried to curry favour with her boss by complimenting his ideas at every meeting.
It was obvious he was currying favour with the coach to get more playing time.
Politicians often curry favour with voters by making grand promises during elections.

Synset Examples

This employee is currying favor with his superordinates

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flatter - to praise excessively, especially insincerely
  • Synonym: to butter up - to flatter in order to gain favor

Etymology

From Middle English 'curry favell', originally referring to grooming a horse (currying) named Favell, associated with deceit; later reanalyzed as 'favour'.
