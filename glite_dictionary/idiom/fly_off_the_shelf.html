to fly off the shelf

1. VERB

/ flaɪ ɑf ðə ʃɛlf / (AmE), / flaɪ ɒf ðə ʃɛlf / (BrE)
to sell extremely quickly or become suddenly very popular, usually referring to products.
sell very quickly; become popular

Examples

The new video game began to fly off the shelf just hours after its release.
After the celebrity endorsement, her perfume started to fly off the shelf.
Limited edition sneakers always fly off the shelf as soon as they hit stores.

Synset Examples

Tickets for the concert flew off the shelves in minutes.
These shoes are flying off the shelves this season.
The new phone is flying off the shelves like hot cakes.
The new phone model is flying off the shelves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sell like hot cakes - to sell very quickly and in large quantities
