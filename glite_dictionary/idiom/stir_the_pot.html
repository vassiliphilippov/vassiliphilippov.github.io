to stir the pot

1. VERB

/ stɝ ðə pɑt / (AmE), / stɜː ðə pɒt / (BrE)
to deliberately cause controversy, trouble, or provoke reactions, especially to create conflict or excitement within a group
provoke trouble or controversy

Examples

Jake loves to stir the pot by making provocative comments in group chats.
She accused her colleague of stirring the pot just to see how people would react.
If you keep stirring the pot, you'll only create more drama in the office.
