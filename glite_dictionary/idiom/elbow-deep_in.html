elbow-deep in

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛlboʊ ˌdip ɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛlbəʊ ˌdiːp ɪn / (BrE)
deeply involved or fully engaged in a particular activity or task, often suggesting being surrounded by or absorbed in it
deeply involved in a task or activity

Examples

She spent the morning elbow-deep in garden soil, planting new flowers for spring.
By midnight, the team was still elbow-deep in troubleshooting the software issue.
He found himself elbow-deep in dishes after hosting a big family dinner.

Synset Examples

He was elbow-deep in paperwork all afternoon.
She got elbow-deep in the project, making significant progress.
The kids were elbow-deep in clay during art class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immersed in - deeply absorbed or involved in something
  • Synonym: up to one's ears in - having a large amount to deal with; deeply involved
