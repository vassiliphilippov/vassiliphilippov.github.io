on sight

1. ADV

/ ɑn saɪt / (AmE), / ɒn saɪt / (BrE)
immediately or as soon as something is seen or noticed
immediately upon seeing

Examples

He recognized the suspect on sight and called the police.
Some rare birds can be identified on sight by experienced ornithologists.
She promised to accept the offer on sight if it met her requirements.

Synset Examples

I avoid that restaurant on sight after last time.
The flowers bloomed beautifully on sight in the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immediately - at once; without delay
  • Synonym: on the spot - at once; without delay
