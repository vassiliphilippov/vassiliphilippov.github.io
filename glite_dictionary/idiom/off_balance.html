off balance

1. ADJ

/ ɔf ˈbæləns / (AmE), / ɒf ˈbæləns / (BrE)
in a state of physical or emotional instability or disorientation, often as a result of something unexpected
feeling unstable or disoriented

Examples

The sudden turn in conversation left me off balance and unsure how to respond.
After the roller coaster stopped abruptly, I felt a bit off balance.
The rapid changes at work have kept everyone feeling off balance.

Synset Examples

The unexpected news left me feeling off balance.
He was off balance after missing his morning coffee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unsteady - not firm, stable, or balanced
  • Synonym: disoriented - having lost one's sense of direction or position
  • Antonym: steady - firmly fixed, supported, or balanced
