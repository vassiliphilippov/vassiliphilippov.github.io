to the four winds

1. X

/ tə ðə fɔr wɪndz / (AmE), / tə ðə fɔː wɪndz / (BrE)
dispersed or scattered so that something cannot be recovered or gathered again; lost completely or gone in every direction.
scattered or lost completely

Examples

After the company closed, years of hard work were scattered to the four winds.
He threw the old letters to the four winds, never wanting to remember the past.
With no plan in place, their resources quickly vanished to the four winds.

Synset Examples

Her plans for the weekend vanished to the four winds.
The documents seemed to vanish to the four winds overnight.

Etymology

An allusion to the four cardinal directions (north, south, east, and west), symbolizing dispersal in every possible direction.
