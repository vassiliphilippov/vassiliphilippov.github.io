to drink from a firehose

1. VERB

/ drɪŋk frəm ə ˈfaɪərˌhoʊz / (AmE), / drɪŋk frəm ə ˈfaɪəhəʊz / (BrE)
to be overwhelmed by receiving too much information or too many tasks at once, more than can be processed or handled
be overwhelmed by too much information

Examples

During my first week at the new company, I felt like I had to drink from a firehose to keep up.
Graduate school can feel like drinking from a firehose because there is so much to learn so quickly.
She joked that onboarding to the team was like drinking from a firehose due to the sudden influx of information.

Synset Examples

Starting a new job felt like trying to drink from a firehose.
The training session was overwhelming; it was like drinking from a firehose.
As a new parent, you often feel like you’re drinking from a firehose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: information overload - state of being overwhelmed by too much information

Etymology

From the imagery of attempting to drink from a powerful firehose, meaning to be blasted with more water than one can handle; first recorded in the late 20th century in American English.
