to take it on the chin

1. VERB

/ teɪk ɪt ɑn ðə tʃɪn / (AmE), / teɪk ɪt ɒn ðə tʃɪn / (BrE)
to accept failure, criticism, or a setback without complaining or showing it has upset you
accept failure or criticism bravely

Examples

Even after losing the finals, she managed to take it on the chin and congratulated her opponent.
Our team has to take it on the chin after that unexpected defeat last night.
He always takes it on the chin when someone criticizes his work and tries to improve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take a beating - to be badly defeated or harmed
  • Antonym: to bounce back - to recover after a defeat or setback

Etymology

Originally from boxing, where taking a blow on the chin meant receiving a hit without falling.

2. VERB

/ teɪk ɪt ɑn ðə tʃɪn / (AmE), / teɪk ɪt ɒn ðə tʃɪn / (BrE)
to accept difficulty, criticism, or punishment bravely and without complaint
accept adversity or criticism bravely

Examples

Even after losing the championship game, the team decided to take it on the chin and congratulated their opponents.
She knew she would face criticism, but she was prepared to take it on the chin.
When the company announced layoffs, many employees had to take it on the chin without complaint.

Synset Examples

Sometimes you just have to take it on the chin.
He took the criticism on the chin and moved on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roll with the punches - adapt to adversity or criticism with resilience
  • Antonym: to break down - fail to cope with adversity or criticism

Etymology

Originally from boxing, where a blow to the chin is a test of resilience.
