baker's dozen

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪkərz ˈdʌzən / (AmE), / ˈbeɪkəz ˈdʌzən / (BrE)
A group of thirteen items, especially baked goods such as bread, rolls, or bagels.
thirteen (items), especially baked goods

Examples

We picked up a baker's dozen of fresh rolls from the bakery.
Every Saturday, she buys a baker's dozen of muffins for her family.
The shop gives a baker's dozen when you purchase a dozen bagels.

Synset Examples

I bought a baker's dozen of bagels for breakfast.
We received a baker's dozen of cookies from her.
He always orders a baker's dozen of donuts.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dozen - a group of twelve items

Etymology

Refers to the old practice of bakers giving thirteen items for the price of twelve, to avoid punishment for shortchanging customers.
