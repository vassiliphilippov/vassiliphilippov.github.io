to suck rocks

1. VERB

/ sʌk rɑks / (AmE), / sʌk rɒks / (BrE)
(informal) To be very disappointing, bad, or unpleasant.
to be very bad or disappointing (informal)
informal american

This new phone battery sucks rocks; it barely lasts two hours.
Honestly, that restaurant just sucks rocks, and I wouldn't recommend it to anyone.
