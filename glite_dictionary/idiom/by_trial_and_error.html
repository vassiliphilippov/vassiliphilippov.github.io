by trial and error

1. ADV

/ baɪ ˈtraɪəl ənd ˈɛrɚ / (AmE), / baɪ ˈtraɪəl ənd ˈɛrə / (BrE)
by experimenting with various methods until a solution is found, often by learning from mistakes or failures
testing different methods to solve a problem

Examples

She learned how to bake bread by trial and error until she perfected her recipe.
The scientists developed a vaccine by trial and error over many years of research.
He fixed the engine by trial and error, testing different parts until he found the problem.

Synset Examples

this can be empirically tested

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empirically - based on, concerned with, or verifiable by observation or experience rather than theory
