musical chairs

1. NOUN

/ ˌmjuːzɪkəl ˈtʃɛrz / (AmE), / ˌmjuːzɪkəl ˈtʃeəz / (BrE)
the activity of frequently changing roles, positions, or arrangements within an organization or situation, usually without any significant effect
frequent rearrangement without effect

Examples

The company had another round of musical chairs in management but nothing really changed.
Shareholders are tired of the musical chairs that lead nowhere.
Despite all the musical chairs among the staff, productivity stayed the same.

Synset Examples

the company is looking for stability after years of musical chairs with directors
shareholders don't want the company playing musical chairs with their investment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reshuffle - an act of reorganizing people or things, especially staff or departments, often implying little real change

Etymology

From the children's game in which players compete for limited chairs when music stops, used figuratively since mid-20th century.
