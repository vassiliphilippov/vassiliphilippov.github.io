just beneath the surface

1. X

/ dʒʌst bəˈniθ ðə ˈsɜrfəs / (AmE), / dʒʌst bɪˈniːθ ðə ˈsɜːfɪs / (BrE)
used to describe something, such as a feeling or quality, that is hidden or not immediately obvious but is close to being revealed or easily brought out
hidden but nearly visible or revealed

Examples

A sense of excitement was just beneath the surface throughout the meeting.
His true feelings are always just beneath the surface, even when he acts calm.
Tension simmered just beneath the surface during the debate.

Synset Examples

He could see her anger smoldering just beneath the surface.
