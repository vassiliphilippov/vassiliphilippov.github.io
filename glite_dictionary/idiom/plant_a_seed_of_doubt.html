to plant a seed of doubt

1. VERB

/ plænt ə siːd ʌv daʊt / (AmE), / plɑːnt ə siːd ɒv daʊt / (BrE)
to introduce uncertainty or suspicion in someone's mind
introduce uncertainty or suspicion

Examples

The detective tried to plant a seed of doubt in the jury's minds.
Rumors can plant a seed of doubt that damages trust in an organization.
Her comments were intended to plant a seed of doubt about the project’s success.

Synset Examples

She decided to plant a seed of doubt in his mind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sow doubt - to cause someone to begin to doubt
