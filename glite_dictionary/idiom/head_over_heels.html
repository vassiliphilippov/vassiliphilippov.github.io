head over heels

1. ADV

/ hɛd oʊvɚ hilz / (AmE), / hɛd əʊvə hiːlz / (BrE)
In a state of disorderly haste; moving quickly and often clumsily or without control.
in disorderly haste

Examples

The children ran head over heels down the hill to catch the ice cream truck.
In his hurry to board the train, he rushed head over heels through the crowd.
Papers flew everywhere as the wind sent her notes head over heels across the room.

Synset Examples

we ran head over heels toward the shelter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helter-skelter - in disorderly haste or confusion

Etymology

Originally from the notion of tumbling or falling in such a way that one's head passes over one's heels; the phrase dates back to the late 18th century.

2. ADV

/ ˌhɛd oʊvər ˈhilz / (AmE), / ˌhɛd əʊvə ˈhiːlz / (BrE)
very much in love or completely infatuated; experiencing intense romantic feelings for someone.
deeply or completely in love

Examples

She fell head over heels for her college sweetheart the moment they met.
I was head over heels in love and couldn't stop thinking about him all day.
He is head over heels for his new partner and tells everyone about her.

Synset Examples

She fell headless over heels for that puppy.
They were headless over heels in love at first sight.
He was headless over heels for her charm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smitten - strongly and suddenly attracted to someone or something
  • Antonym: indifferent - lacking interest or affection in someone or something

Etymology

First used in the 18th century as 'heels over head,' later inverted to 'head over heels' for emphasis on tumbling or being thrown upside down with emotion.
