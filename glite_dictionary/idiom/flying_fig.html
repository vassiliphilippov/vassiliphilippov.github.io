flying fig

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪɪŋ fɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈflaɪɪŋ fɪɡ / (BrE)
something completely unimportant or irrelevant; a matter of no consequence
something unimportant or irrelevant

Examples

He didn't give a flying fig about the criticism he received at work.
Whether it rains or shines tomorrow is a flying fig to me.
The manager made it clear that their complaints weren't worth a flying fig.

Synset Examples

When he mentioned the issue, I thought it was a flying fig.
I couldn't care less about that; it's a flying fig to me.
To him, her complaints were just a flying fig.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fig - something of little value or importance (as in the phrase 'not give a fig')
