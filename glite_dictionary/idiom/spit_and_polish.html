spit and polish

1. NOUN

/ spɪt ənd ˈpɑlɪʃ / (AmE), / spɪt ənd ˈpɒlɪʃ / (BrE)
Careful attention to cleanliness, order, and appearance, especially in a military context.
meticulous attention to appearance/order

Examples

The general insisted on spit and polish in the barracks at all times.
Despite the fancy spit and polish, the job itself was monotonous and dull.
His obsession with spit and polish made his home spotless but hardly welcoming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neatness - the quality of being clean and orderly
  • Antonym: sloppiness - lack of care or order; untidiness

Etymology

Originally military slang from the literal act of polishing boots and equipment with spit; in use since the late 19th century.
