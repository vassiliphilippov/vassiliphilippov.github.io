fuzzy end of the lollipop

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌzi ɛnd əv ðə lɑːliˌpɑp / (AmE), / ˈfʌzi end əv ðə ˈlɒli.pɒp / (BrE)
to receive the worst or least desirable outcome in a situation; to be in an unfavorable position
to get the worst outcome

Examples

After working overtime all week, Jim still got the fuzzy end of the lollipop when he wasn't thanked.
Whenever the company makes budget cuts, employees feel like they're getting the fuzzy end of the lollipop.
Moving into the noisy apartment was really the fuzzy end of the lollipop in that housing swap.

Synset Examples

After losing the game, she said she got the fuzzy end of the lollipop.
He felt like he got the fuzzy end of the lollipop.
Getting stuck in traffic felt like the fuzzy end of the lollipop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: short end of the stick - to receive the least favorable outcome
  • Synonym: raw deal - an unfair or unfavorable outcome

Etymology

Popularized by Marilyn Monroe in the 1959 film 'Some Like It Hot.'
