dead hand of the past

1. NOUN

/ dɛd hænd əv ðə pæst / (AmE), / dɛd hand ɒv ðə pɑːst / (BrE)
the oppressive or controlling influence of previous events, decisions, or traditions that hinder change or progress
oppressive influence of previous events

Examples

The dead hand of the past still shapes our laws and institutions today.
Efforts at reform have often stumbled under the dead hand of the past.
She fought hard to break free from the dead hand of the past that restricted her ambitions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dead hand - an oppressive or controlling influence from the past
