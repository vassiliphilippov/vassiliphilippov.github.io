to come to blows

1. VERB

/ kʌm tu bloʊz / (AmE), / kʌm tuː bləʊz / (BrE)
to begin fighting physically with someone, usually after an argument or disagreement
engage in a physical fight

Examples

The two players nearly came to blows after the controversial call.
During the stressful meeting, colleagues came to blows over the budget cuts.
Tensions rose so high that they eventually came to blows in the hallway.

Synset Examples

The two rivals came to blows during the heated debate.
The argument escalated, and they almost came to blows.
If they keep arguing like this, they'll come to blows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fight - to take part in a physical struggle
  • Degree Lower: to fall out - to have a serious disagreement resulting in the end of a relationship
