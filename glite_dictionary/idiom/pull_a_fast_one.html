to pull a fast one

1. VERB

/ pʊl ə fæst wʌn / (AmE), / pʊl ə fɑːst wʌn / (BrE)
to successfully deceive or trick someone, especially in a clever or sly way
deceive or trick someone

Examples

He tried to pull a fast one by switching the documents before the meeting.
Don’t think you can pull a fast one on me; I’m paying attention.
She managed to pull a fast one during the card game without anyone noticing.

Synset Examples

He tried to pull a fast one on his boss.
Don't think you can pull a fast one with me.
She managed to pull a fast one during the game.
That motherhumper really pulled a fast one on us.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true
  • Synonym: trick - to deceive someone, especially as a prank or in order to gain an advantage
