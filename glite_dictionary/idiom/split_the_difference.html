to split the difference

1. VERB

/ splɪt ðə ˈdɪf.ɚ.əns / (AmE), / splɪt ðə ˈdɪf.ərəns / (BrE)
to compromise by accepting a middle value or position, especially in negotiations when two parties disagree on an amount
compromise by choosing a middle value

Examples

If you want $100 and I want $50, let's split the difference and settle for $75.
After some debate over the timeline, they agreed to split the difference and start next month.
Neither side would budge, so they decided to split the difference on the cost.

Synset Examples

Let's split the difference and meet halfway.
To resolve the conflict, they decided to split the difference.
They agreed to split the difference on the price.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to meet halfway - to compromise by yielding partially to each other
