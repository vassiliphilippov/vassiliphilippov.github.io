in line

1. ADJ

/ ɪn laɪn / (AmE), / ɪn laɪn / (BrE)
waiting or standing in a queue for something; awaiting one's turn or opportunity, especially for something due or expected.
waiting in a queue or for something expected

Examples

We had to wait in line for thirty minutes before entering the theater.
She stood in line at the post office to collect her package.
Several candidates are in line for the promotion this year.

Synset Examples

people were in line at the checkout counter
she was in line for promotion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in queue - standing or waiting in a line, especially for service
  • Antonym: out of line - not in accordance with expected or acceptable behavior
