to stick the knife in

1. VERB

/ stɪk ðə naɪf ɪn / (AmE), / stɪk ðə naɪf ɪn / (BrE)
to deliberately say or do something to deeply hurt or betray someone, especially when they are already in a difficult situation
to deeply hurt or betray someone

Examples

Even after winning, she couldn’t resist sticking the knife in by criticizing her opponent’s efforts.
His old friend stuck the knife in when he revealed her secrets to the press.
When I failed the test, my brother stuck the knife in with a sarcastic remark.

Synset Examples

He really stuck the knife in with that harsh comment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twist the knife - to make someone's suffering worse, especially after already hurting them
