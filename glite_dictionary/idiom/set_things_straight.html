to set things straight

1. VERB

/ sɛt θɪŋz streɪt / (AmE), / sɛt θɪŋz streɪt / (BrE)
to clarify a situation or resolve confusion; to correct a misunderstanding or misperception
clarify a situation; resolve confusion

Examples

After the rumors spread, he decided to set things straight with his colleagues.
She wrote an email to set things straight about what really happened at the meeting.
It's important to set things straight before the misunderstanding grows worse.

Synset Examples

He called to set things straight after the misunderstanding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clear up - to make a situation or matter less confused or more comprehensible
