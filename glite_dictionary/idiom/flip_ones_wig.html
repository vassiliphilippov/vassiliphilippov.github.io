to flip one's wig

1. VERB

/ flɪp wʌnz wɪɡ / (AmE), / flɪp wʌnz wɪɡ / (BrE)
to suddenly become extremely angry or lose control of one's temper
suddenly become very angry
informal

Examples

Don't flip your wig, but there's been a small accident with the car.
She nearly flipped her wig when she saw the mess in the kitchen.
If my boss finds out about the mistake, he'll flip his wig for sure.

Synset Examples

The professor combusted when the student didn't know the answer to a very elementary question
Spam makes me go ballistic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lose one's temper - to suddenly become angry
  • Synonym: to blow a fuse - to suddenly lose one's temper
  • Antonym: to keep one's cool - to remain calm under stress or provocation
