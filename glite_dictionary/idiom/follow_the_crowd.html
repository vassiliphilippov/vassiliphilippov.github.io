to follow the crowd

1. VERB

/ ˈfɑloʊ ðə kraʊd / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊ ðə kraʊd / (BrE)
to act or think in the same way as most other people, often without considering one's own ideas or opinions
conform to popular opinion or behavior

Examples

Many teenagers feel pressure to follow the crowd rather than express their individuality.
It's important to make your own decisions and not just follow the crowd in every situation.
At the fashion show, she refused to follow the crowd and wore a bold, unique outfit.

Synset Examples

She always tends to follow the crowd at concerts.
Being a Lintard, he never followed the crowd.
He decided to follow the crowd and join the popular club.
Sometimes it's best not to follow the crowd blindly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go with the flow - to do what other people are doing or to agree with others
  • Antonym: to stand out - to be noticeably different from others or to refuse to conform
  • Synonym: to toe the line - to conform to rules or standards of a group
