to save face

1. VERB

/ seɪv feɪs / (AmE), / seɪv feɪs / (BrE)
to avoid embarrassment or maintain dignity or respect, especially after a mistake or failure
preserve dignity and avoid embarrassment

Examples

After forgetting his lines during the speech, he tried to save face by making a joke.
The company issued a public apology to save face after their product recall.
She offered to take the blame so her colleague could save face in front of the clients.

Synset Examples

They needed to save face after the public relations disaster.
She made excuses to save face in front of her peers.
He wanted to save her face in front of the boss.
We need to find a way to save his face.
She tried to save his face after the blunder.
He tried to save face after the embarrassing mistake.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to lose face - to suffer embarrassment or loss of respect

Etymology

From Chinese 面子 (miànzi, 'face; reputation'), introduced into English via translated texts and cultural contact in the 19th century.
