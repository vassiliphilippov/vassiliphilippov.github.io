on cruise control

1. ADV

/ ɑn kruːz kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ɒn kruːz kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
operating or continuing with minimal effort or oversight, often after achieving a certain level of progress or stability
continuing with minimal effort or attention

Examples

After completing most of the major tasks, he set the rest of the work on cruise control.
Since the company became profitable, things have been running on cruise control.
Once her team was trained, she managed the department on cruise control for the rest of the year.

Synset Examples

She put the project on cruise control after the initial phase.

Etymology

From the literal use of 'cruise control' in automobiles (a system that maintains a steady speed); figurative use extended to other contexts in the late 20th century.
