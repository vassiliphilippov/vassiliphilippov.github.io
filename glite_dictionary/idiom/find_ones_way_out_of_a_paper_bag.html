to find one's way out of a paper bag

1. VERB

/ faɪnd wʌnz weɪ aʊt əv ə ˈpeɪpɚ bæɡ / (AmE), / faɪnd wʌnz weɪ aʊt əv ə ˈpeɪpə bæɡ / (BrE)
(usually used negatively, often with 'can't' or 'couldn't') To be able (or unable) to deal with even the simplest situations or tasks, especially as a metaphor for being inept or lacking basic competence.
to be inept at handling simple situations
informal

Examples

Despite years of training, he still can't find his way out of a paper bag when faced with a new problem.
My navigation skills are so bad, I probably couldn't find my way out of a paper bag.
She laughed and said her brother couldn't find his way out of a paper bag if his life depended on it.

Synset Examples

She joked that he wouldn't find his way out of a paper bag.
It's hard to believe he can't find his way out of a paper bag.
He can't find his way out of a paper bag.
He joked that the dumbocrats couldn't find their way out of a paper bag.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incompetent - lacking ability or skill
  • Antonym: resourceful - able to handle difficult situations well
