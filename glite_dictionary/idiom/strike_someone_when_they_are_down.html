to strike someone when they are down

1. VERB

/ straɪk ˈsʌmwʌn wɛn ðeɪ ɑr daʊn / (AmE), / straɪk ˈsʌmwən wɛn ðeɪ ɑː daʊn / (BrE)
to attack, criticize, or take advantage of someone when they are already in a weak or vulnerable position
attack or criticize someone in a vulnerable state

Examples

Even his rival refused to strike someone when they are down, choosing instead to offer support.
The media loves to strike someone when they are down, piling on criticism after a public mistake.
She scolded him for trying to strike someone when they are down during the meeting.

Synset Examples

It’s cruel to strike someone when they are down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to kick someone when they are down - to make things worse for someone already in trouble
