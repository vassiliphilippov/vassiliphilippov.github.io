flat out

1. ADV

/ flæt aʊt / (AmE), / flæt aʊt / (BrE)
in a completely direct or blunt manner, without trying to hide one's feelings or intentions
bluntly, directly, without pretense

Examples

She told him flat out that she disagreed with his proposal.
If you don't want the job, just say so flat out rather than making excuses.
He asked her flat out if she was lying to him.

Synset Examples

he spoke bluntly
he stated his opinion flat-out
he was criticized roundly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluntly - in a very direct and honest way, sometimes rudely so
  • Synonym: roundly - in a vehement or outspoken manner

2. ADV

/ flæt aʊt / (AmE), / flæt aʊt / (BrE)
as fast or as hard as possible; at top speed or with maximum effort
at top speed; with maximum effort

Examples

The team worked flat out to finish the project before the deadline.
She drove flat out for two hours to reach the hospital in time.
He ran flat out and barely made it to the train before it left.

Synset Examples

he ran flat out to catch the bus
he was off down the road like blue murder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: full tilt - at full speed or with maximum energy
  • Antonym: at a crawl - moving very slowly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00086743-r
Synset POS: r
