tall in the saddle

1. ADJ

/ tɔl ɪn ðə ˈsædəl / (AmE), / tɔːl ɪn ðə ˈsædəl / (BrE)
Confident, self-assured, and in control of a situation.
confident and in control

Examples

Even in the face of criticism, she remained tall in the saddle and handled every question with ease.
After months of training, he finally felt tall in the saddle during team meetings.
The new CEO was tall in the saddle, taking decisive action from day one.

Synset Examples

He's always tall in the saddle during negotiations.
Being tall in the saddle boosts his confidence.
She felt tall in the saddle after winning the award.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confident - feeling or showing certainty and self-assurance
  • Antonym: insecure - lacking confidence or self-assurance

Etymology

Originally from horseback riding, where sitting 'tall in the saddle' signifies confidence and authority.
