to talk turkey

1. VERB

/ tɑk ˈtɜr.ki / (AmE), / tɔːk ˈtɜː.ki / (BrE)
to discuss something honestly and directly, especially to negotiate seriously about business or important matters
discuss or negotiate seriously and frankly

Examples

Let's skip the small talk and talk turkey about the terms of the contract.
When both sides decided to talk turkey, they finally reached an agreement.
We need to talk turkey if we're going to solve this budget problem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to negotiate - to try to reach an agreement by discussion
  • Antonym: to beat around the bush - to avoid talking directly about an issue

Etymology

Early 19th-century American English, origin uncertain, possibly relating to frank bartering with Native Americans.

2. VERB

/ tɔk ˈtɝː.ki / (AmE), / tɔːk ˈtɜː.ki / (BrE)
To discuss serious matters in a direct and straightforward manner, often with the intention of reaching an agreement or solution.
discuss matters directly and seriously
informal american

Examples

If you're serious about buying the car, let's talk turkey and settle on a price.
The two CEOs finally sat down to talk turkey after weeks of back-and-forth emails.
It's no use making small talk—we need to talk turkey about how to fix the problem.

Synset Examples

Let's talk turkey about the budget for this project.
We need to sit down and talk turkey before making decisions.
It's time to talk turkey and finalize the deal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to negotiate - discuss to reach an agreement
  • Antonym: to beat around the bush - avoid discussing serious matters directly

Etymology

American English; origin uncertain, early 19th century, possibly related to frank negotiations between settlers and Native Americans.
