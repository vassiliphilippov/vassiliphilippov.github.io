to fall victim to

1. VERB

/ fɔl ˈvɪktɪm tu / (AmE), / fɔːl ˈvɪktɪm tuː / (BrE)
to be harmed, deceived, or affected by something bad, especially when unable to prevent it
be harmed or affected by something bad

Examples

After years of success, the restaurant fell victim to changing customer tastes.
Tourists often fall victim to pickpockets in busy city centers.
She fell victim to a phishing email and lost access to her account.

Synset Examples

The company fell victim to a ransomware attack last week.
Many people fall victim to online scams each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to succumb - to give in or yield to something
  • Antonym: to withstand - to resist or not be harmed by something negative
