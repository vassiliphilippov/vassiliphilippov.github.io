to make a splash

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə splæʃ / (AmE), / meɪk ə splæʃ / (BrE)
to attract a great deal of attention or excitement, especially in a dramatic or noteworthy way
attract much attention or excitement

Examples

The new smartphone is expected to make a splash in the tech market this year.
With her bold artwork, the young artist managed to make a splash at the gallery opening.
Their unique advertising campaign helped them make a splash among younger consumers.

Synset Examples

The kids made a big splash in the pool.
The kids cheered as he made a big splash with his cannonball.
She made a splash at the party with her dress.
The new restaurant aims to make a splash in town.
They hope to make a splash with their innovative product launch.
Her performance was designed to make a splash at the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cause a stir - to provoke excitement or controversy
  • Synonym: to steal the show - to attract the most attention or praise
