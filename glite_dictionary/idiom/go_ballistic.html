to go ballistic

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ bəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ɡəʊ bəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
to become extremely angry or enraged
become very angry

Examples

My father will go ballistic if he finds out I broke his computer.
The coach went ballistic after the team lost the match.
She goes ballistic whenever someone parks in her spot.

Synset Examples

The professor combusted when the student didn't know the answer to a very elementary question
Spam makes me go ballistic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lose one's temper - to suddenly become very angry
  • Synonym: to blow a fuse - to lose one's temper suddenly

Etymology

Originally from 'ballistic,' relating to the flight of projectiles; figuratively, suggesting explosive anger.

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ bəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ɡəʊ bəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
to suddenly become extremely angry or upset
become extremely angry

Examples

When her son confessed to breaking the window, she went ballistic and started yelling.
The fans went ballistic when their team scored the winning goal in the last minute.
If my parents find out I failed the exam, they'll probably go ballistic.

Synset Examples

He went ballistic when he found out his car was towed.
My boss went ballistic over the missed deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lose your temper - to become very angry suddenly
  • Synonym: to blow a fuse - to suddenly become very angry

Etymology

Originally referencing the uncontrolled flight of ballistic missiles, later applied figuratively to explosive anger.
