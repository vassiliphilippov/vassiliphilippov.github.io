to hang tight

1. VERB

/ hæŋ taɪt / (AmE), / hæŋ taɪt / (BrE)
to wait patiently and calmly, often during a delay or period of uncertainty
wait patiently
informal

Examples

Just hang tight while I find out what's happening.
The doctor told her to hang tight until the test results came in.
If you hang tight, the next train should be here any minute.

Synset Examples

Just hang tight while I grab my jacket.
Hang tight, the bus will be here soon.
You need to hang tight until the storm passes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wait - to stay in a place or delay action until a particular time or event
