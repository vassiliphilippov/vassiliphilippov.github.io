to tempt fate

1. VERB

/ tɛmpt feɪt / (AmE), / tɛmpt feɪt / (BrE)
to take unnecessary risks or actions that may result in bad luck or disaster
take risky actions that may bring bad luck

Examples

He refused to tempt fate by crossing the rickety old bridge during the storm.
Some people believe it’s bad luck to talk about success too early, as if you’re tempting fate.
I try not to tempt fate by making careless choices when it comes to my health.

Synset Examples

Don’t tempt fate by driving in bad weather.
He decided not to tempt fate with another risky bet.
She knew she shouldn’t tempt fate by skipping practice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: court disaster - risk something bad happening through reckless action
  • Synonym: to play with fire - engage in risky or dangerous behavior
