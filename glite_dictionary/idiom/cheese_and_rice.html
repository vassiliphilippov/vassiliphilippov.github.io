cheese and rice

1. INTJ

/ tʃiz ənd raɪs / (AmE), / tʃiːz ənd raɪs / (BrE)
an exclamation used to express surprise or frustration, often as a mild euphemism for 'Jesus Christ'
exclamation of surprise or frustration
informal

Examples

Cheese and rice, I can't believe how much this car repair will cost!
Cheese and rice, that was a close call on the freeway.
When he dropped his phone, he yelled, "cheese and rice!"

Synset Examples

When he tripped, I couldn't help but say, 'Cheese and rice!'
He exclaimed, 'Cheese and rice, I forgot my keys!'

Etymology

Euphemistic alteration of 'Jesus Christ', used to avoid blasphemy.
