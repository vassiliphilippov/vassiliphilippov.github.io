water under the bridge

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔt̬ɚ ˌʌndɚ ðə ˈbrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌʌndə ðə ˈbrɪdʒ / (BrE)
used to refer to events or situations in the past that are no longer considered important or worth discussing
past events no longer important

Examples

They argued years ago, but that's just water under the bridge now.
All the mistakes I made at my old job are water under the bridge for me.
There's no point dwelling on what happened; it's all water under the bridge.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to let bygones be bygones - to forgive or ignore past disagreements
