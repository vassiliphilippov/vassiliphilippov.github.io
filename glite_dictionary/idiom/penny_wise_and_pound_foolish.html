penny wise and pound foolish

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɛni ˈwaɪz ənd ˌpaʊnd ˈfuːlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˌpɛni ˈwaɪz ənd ˌpaʊnd ˈfuːlɪʃ / (BrE)
careful and prudent with small amounts of money but extravagant or wasteful with larger sums
careful with small sums, wasteful with large sums
british

Examples

By skimping on regular car servicing, he proved to be penny wise and pound foolish.
The company was penny wise and pound foolish, cutting minor expenses but losing money on big mistakes.
She acted penny wise and pound foolish by spending hours to save a few dollars but ignoring major bills.

Synset Examples

They were penny wise and pound foolish, ignoring maintenance costs.
He was penny wise and pound foolish by skipping insurance.
Being penny wise and pound foolish cost her dearly.

Etymology

From British currency units: 'penny' (small coin) and 'pound' (large sum).
