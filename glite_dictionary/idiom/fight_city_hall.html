to fight City Hall

1. VERB

/ faɪt ˌsɪti ˈhɔl / (AmE), / faɪt ˌsɪti ˈhɔːl / (BrE)
to try to challenge or resist the established authorities, especially when such efforts are seen as futile or extremely difficult
challenge or resist authority

Examples

You might want to improve the regulations, but it's never easy to fight City Hall.
After years of trying to change the law, she realized how hard it is to fight City Hall.
Some people refuse to fight City Hall, thinking that challenging government rules is pointless.

Synset Examples

Trying to change that policy is futile; you can't fight City Hall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rail against - to criticize severely or protest strongly
  • Antonym: to submit - to accept or yield to authority

Etymology

Refers metaphorically to the American institution of 'City Hall' representing local government, authority, or bureaucracy.
