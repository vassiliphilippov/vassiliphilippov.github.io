to fight shy of

1. VERB

/ faɪt ʃaɪ əv / (AmE), / faɪt ʃaɪ ɒv / (BrE)
to avoid or be unwilling to do something; to be reluctant or hesitant to face or get involved with something
avoid or hesitate to do something

Examples

Many politicians fight shy of discussing controversial issues in public.
He tends to fight shy of making big decisions without consulting others first.
Some people fight shy of commitment in relationships.

Synset Examples

They often fight shy of taking risks.
He tends to fight shy of confrontation.
She didn’t fight shy of asking for help.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shy away from - avoid doing something due to fear or reluctance
