to beat (someone) at (their) own game

1. VERB

/ biːt æt ˈsʌmwʌnz oʊn ɡeɪm / (AmE), / biːt æt ˈsʌmwʌnz əʊn ɡeɪm / (BrE)
to succeed in competing with someone in an activity at which they are usually superior, by using their own methods or skills against them
outsmart someone in their own area of expertise

Examples

After years of losing to her rival, Mary finally beat her at her own game.
The hacker was confident until a newcomer managed to beat him at his own game.
You have to study their strategies if you want to beat them at their own game.

Synset Examples

He was surprised when she beat him at his own game.
She finally managed to beat him at his own game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outsmart - to gain an advantage over someone by being more intelligent or clever
  • Antonym: lose to someone at their own game - to be defeated in your own area of expertise, the opposite of winning
