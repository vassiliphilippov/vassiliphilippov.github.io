deep pockets

1. NOUN

/ diːp ˈpɑːkəts / (AmE), / diːp ˈpɒkɪts / (BrE)
a large amount of money or great financial resources; the possession or availability of substantial wealth, especially as an advantage
substantial wealth or financial resources

Examples

To take on the lawsuit, you'll need deep pockets to cover the legal fees.
The company survived the crisis thanks to investors with deep pockets.
Art museums often rely on donors with deep pockets to fund exhibitions.

Synset Examples

a patron of the arts should have deep pockets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wealth - an abundance of valuable possessions or money
