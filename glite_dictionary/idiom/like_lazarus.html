like Lazarus

1. ADV

/ laɪk ˈlæzərəs / (AmE), / laɪk ˈlæzərəs / (BrE)
to return suddenly or unexpectedly, especially after having disappeared or seemed gone for good
return dramatically after being gone

Examples

After months of silence, she returned to social media like Lazarus.
The faded band made a comeback tour and was welcomed like Lazarus by their fans.
The company, once bankrupt, was revived like Lazarus and thrived against all odds.

Synset Examples

After months of silence, she returned to social media like Lazarus.

Etymology

Allusion to the biblical story of Lazarus, who was raised from the dead by Jesus (John 11).
