to play with a full deck

1. VERB

/ pleɪ wɪð ə fʊl dɛk / (AmE), / pleɪ wɪð ə fʊl dɛk / (BrE)
to be mentally sound, sane, or fully aware; not mentally deficient or irrational
to be mentally sound or sane

Examples

Despite his unusual sense of humor, he definitely plays with a full deck.
Some doubted her, but she proved she could play with a full deck under pressure.
If you think he doesn't play with a full deck, you're mistaken.

Synset Examples

He doesn't always seem to play with a full deck.
It's hard to trust someone who doesn't play with a full deck.
She assured me that he plays with a full deck despite his quirks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: play with half a deck - to be mentally unsound or lacking awareness; not all there

Etymology

Originally from playing card terminology, referring to someone not missing any cards (mentally complete).
