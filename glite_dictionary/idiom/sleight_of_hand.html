sleight of hand

1. NOUN

/ slaɪt əv hænd / (AmE), / slaɪt əv hand / (BrE)
skillful and quick use of one's hands, especially to perform tricks or deceive others, as in magic
manual dexterity for tricks or deception

Examples

The magician's sleight of hand left the audience baffled and amazed.
With remarkable sleight of hand, she made the coin disappear before our eyes.
Card sharps often rely on sleight of hand to win at poker.

Synset Examples

The magician amazed everyone with his sleight of hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legerdemain - skillful use of one's hands for deception, especially in magic
  • Synonym: trickery - the use of tricks to deceive someone

Etymology

From Middle English 'sleight' (cunning, skill), originally from Old Norse 'slœgð' (cleverness) + of hand.
