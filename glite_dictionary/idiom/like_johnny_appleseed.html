like Johnny Appleseed

1. X

/ laɪk ˌdʒɑni ˈæpəlˌsid / (AmE), / laɪk ˌdʒɒni ˈæp(ə)lˌsiːd / (BrE)
used to describe someone who plants trees or does good deeds, referencing the American folk figure known for planting apple trees
like someone who plants trees or does good deeds
american

Examples

He traveled from town to town, sowing wildflower seeds like Johnny Appleseed.
She volunteers at every event, always helping out like Johnny Appleseed.
Their organization donates books to rural schools, acting like Johnny Appleseed in the world of education.

Synset Examples

He wandered the countryside, planting trees like Johnny Appleseed.

Etymology

From the nickname of John Chapman (1774–1845), known as 'Johnny Appleseed,' an American pioneer famous for planting apple trees across the Midwest.
