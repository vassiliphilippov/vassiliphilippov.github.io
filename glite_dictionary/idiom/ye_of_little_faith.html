ye of little faith

1. X

/ ji əv ˈlɪt̬l ˈfeɪθ / (AmE), / ji əv ˈlɪtl ˈfeɪθ / (BrE)
used to address someone who is doubting or lacking confidence, especially when they should be more trusting or hopeful
address to doubters or those lacking confidence
archaic

Examples

Whenever you hesitate to join us, I always say, ye of little faith.
The coach laughed at his team's worries, calling them ye of little faith.
When she doubted their plan would work, he replied, "ye of little faith."

Synset Examples

O ye of little faith, don't doubt your abilities.
O ye of little faith, trust the process!
O ye of little faith, have hope in tomorrow's outcome.

Etymology

Biblical phrase, found in early English translations such as the King James Bible.
