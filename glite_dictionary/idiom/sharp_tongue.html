sharp tongue

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑrp tʌŋ / (AmE), / ʃɑːp tʌŋ / (BrE)
a tendency to speak in a harsh, critical, or biting manner
tendency to speak harshly or critically

Examples

Despite her sharp tongue, everyone knew she cared deeply about her friends.
His sharp tongue often made people wary of disagreeing with him in meetings.
She tried to control her sharp tongue but sometimes her criticisms slipped out anyway.

Synset Examples

His tongue was so sharp, it cut deep.
Be cautious; her sharp tongue doesn’t hold back.
He has a sharp tongue that can cut deep.
Her sharp tongue often gets her in trouble.
Everyone jokingly called her Xanthippe for her sharp tongue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caustic - speaking in a way that is severely critical or sarcastic
  • Synonym: biting - delivering hurtful or sharply critical remarks
  • Antonym: tactful - having or showing sensitivity in dealing with others
