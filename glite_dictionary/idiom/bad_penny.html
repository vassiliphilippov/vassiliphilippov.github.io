bad penny

1. NOUN

/ bæd ˈpɛni / (AmE), / bad ˈpɛni / (BrE)
something or someone unwanted that repeatedly appears or returns, especially when not welcome
unwelcome thing or person that keeps returning

Examples

He always turns up like a bad penny whenever there's food around.
Just when we thought the issue was settled, it came back like a bad penny.
Despite her efforts to avoid him, he reappeared in her life like a bad penny again.

Synset Examples

That rumor reappeared, just like a bad penny.

Etymology

From the idea that counterfeit or damaged coins ('bad pennies') would circulate back into use despite efforts to get rid of them.
