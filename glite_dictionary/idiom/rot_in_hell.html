to rot in hell

1. VERB

/ rɑt ɪn hɛl / (AmE), / rɒt ɪn hɛl / (BrE)
(of a person) used to curse someone or express a strong wish that they suffer greatly, especially as punishment or after death.
wish severe punishment or suffering on someone
offensive

Examples

After hearing what he had done, she screamed at him to rot in hell.
Some villains in history are so reviled that people say they should rot in hell.
He angrily told his betrayer to rot in hell and never come back.

Synset Examples

After the argument, she wished he would rot in hell.
Some people just deserve to rot in hell for their actions.
He told his enemies to rot in hell for their betrayal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go to hell - an expression used to angrily tell someone to leave or expressing strong animosity
  • Synonym: damn you - an expression wishing misfortune or punishment upon someone
