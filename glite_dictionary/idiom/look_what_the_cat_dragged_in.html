look what the cat dragged in

1. X

/ lʊk wət ðə kæt drægd ɪn / (AmE), / lʊk wɒt ðə kæt drægd ɪn / (BrE)
used humorously to acknowledge someone's arrival, especially when it is unexpected or the person appears disheveled or untidy
humorous greeting for an unexpected or messy arrival

Examples

Well, look what the cat dragged in—I haven't seen you in years!
She opened the door and said, 'look what the cat dragged in,' when she saw her brother covered in mud.
After being gone all weekend, I came home to my roommate saying, 'look what the cat dragged in.'

Synset Examples

Everyone laughed as she entered the party, 'Look what the cat's dragged in!'
Well, look what the cat's dragged in, our long-lost friend!
He stumbled in late, and I said, 'Look what the cat's dragged in!'
