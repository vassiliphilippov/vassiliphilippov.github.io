all in the family

1. X

/ ɔl ɪn ðə ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ɔːl ɪn ðə ˈfæməli / (BrE)
restricted to or involving only family members or close relatives, often referring to a business or activity kept within a single family
involving only family members or relatives

Examples

The new manager is his cousin; they like to keep it all in the family.
Ownership of the company has stayed all in the family for over a century.
They handled the dispute quietly, preferring to keep things all in the family.

Synset Examples

The bakery is all in the family, passed down through generations.
They keep the business all in the family to maintain trust.
The reunion was all in the family, full of laughter and memories.
