to go tutti-frutti

1. VERB

/ goʊ tʌti ˈfruti / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tʌti ˈfruːti / (BrE)
to use or incorporate colorful, varied, or flamboyant elements, especially in style or decoration
become colorful or flamboyant
informal

Examples

The designers really went tutti-frutti with the costumes for the musical.
For her birthday, she went tutti-frutti and decorated everything in bright, clashing colors.
Whenever he hosts a party, he goes tutti-frutti with wild themes and decorations.

Synset Examples

At the party, they went tutti-frutti with the decorations.

Etymology

From Italian 'tutti frutti' meaning 'all fruits', referring to a mixed variety, often used for colorful or eclectic mixtures.
