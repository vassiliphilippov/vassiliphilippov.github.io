woe is me

1. INTJ

/ woʊ ɪz miː / (AmE), / wəʊ ɪz miː / (BrE)
used to express sorrow, distress, or lamentation, often in a humorous or exaggerated way
expression of sorrow or distress
archaic

Examples

Woe is me, for my plans have come to nothing.
After hearing the bad news, she sighed and exclaimed, "woe is me."
Forgotten again—woe is me!

Synset Examples

Woe is me, for I have lost my favorite book.
Woe is me, I forgot my umbrella in the rain.
Woe is me, how will I finish this project on time?

Etymology

From archaic English; 'woe' meaning sorrow or misery and 'is me' meaning upon myself. Common in biblical and poetic usage.
