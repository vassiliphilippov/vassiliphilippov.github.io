to get under someone's skin

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈʌndɚ sʌmˌwʌnz skɪn / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈʌndə sʌmˌwʌnz skɪn / (BrE)
to irritate or annoy someone to a significant degree
to annoy or irritate someone

Examples

His constant complaining really gets under my skin at work.
No matter how hard I try, her sarcastic comments always get under my skin.
Don't let their rude remarks get under your skin; just ignore them.

Synset Examples

I try to ignore her, but she always gets under my skin.
His attitude really gets under my skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to irritate - to make someone annoyed or impatient
  • Synonym: to annoy - to make someone slightly angry
