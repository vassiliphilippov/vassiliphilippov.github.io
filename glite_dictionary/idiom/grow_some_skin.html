to grow some skin

1. VERB

/ ɡroʊ səm skɪn / (AmE), / ɡrəʊ səm skɪn / (BrE)
to develop resilience or emotional toughness, especially in response to criticism or adversity
develop emotional resilience

Examples

After receiving harsh feedback, she realized she had to grow some skin if she wanted to succeed.
In politics, it pays to grow some skin and not take every criticism to heart.
You can't please everyone, so you'll need to grow some skin sooner or later.

Synset Examples

He realized he needed to grow some skin to cope.
You must grow some skin if you want to handle criticism.
To survive in this industry, you need to grow some skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grow a thick skin - develop emotional toughness or resilience
