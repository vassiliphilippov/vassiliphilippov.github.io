to sink faster than the Titanic

1. VERB

/ sɪŋk ˈfæstər ðən ðə taɪˈtænɪk / (AmE), / sɪŋk ˈfɑːstə ðən ðə taɪˈtænɪk / (BrE)
to fail very quickly and dramatically, often with little chance of recovery
fail quickly and dramatically
informal

Examples

After just a week, his new business idea began to sink faster than the Titanic.
The movie's reputation sank faster than the Titanic when critics saw the poor reviews.
Plans to reform the system sank faster than the Titanic once the funding was withdrawn.

Synset Examples

Their plans sank faster than the Itanic when faced with reality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fail spectacularly - to fail in a dramatic or notable way

Etymology

Reference to the 1912 sinking of the ocean liner Titanic, widely noted for its speed and scale.
