to warm the cockles of one's heart

1. VERB

/ wɔrm ðə ˈkɑkəlz əv wʌnz hɑrt / (AmE), / wɔːm ðə ˈkɒkəlz əv wʌnz hɑːt / (BrE)
to make someone feel deeply pleased or emotionally uplifted
make someone feel happy or touched

Examples

The generous donation truly warmed the cockles of my heart.
Seeing old friends gather at the reunion warmed the cockles of her heart.
The teacher's praise for his efforts warmed the cockles of his heart.

Synset Examples

Seeing the puppies play can warm the cockles of anyone's heart.
His surprise gift warmed the cockles of her heart.
Her kind words really warmed the cockles of my heart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to gladden - to make someone glad or happy

Etymology

Possibly from Latin 'cochlea' (snail shell, ventricle of the heart), referring to the heart's chambers.
