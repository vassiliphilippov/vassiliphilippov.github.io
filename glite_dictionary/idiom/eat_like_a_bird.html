to eat like a bird

1. VERB

/ it laɪk ə bɝd / (AmE), / iːt laɪk ə bɜːd / (BrE)
to eat very little; to have a very small appetite
eat very little

Examples

My grandmother eats like a bird and never finishes her meal.
Whenever I'm nervous, I tend to eat like a bird.
You must be hungry—don’t eat like a bird tonight!

Synset Examples

She tends to eat like a bird during lunch.
He only nibbles, always eats like a bird.
My sister eats like a bird, barely touches her food.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to eat like a horse - to eat a lot; to have a large appetite
