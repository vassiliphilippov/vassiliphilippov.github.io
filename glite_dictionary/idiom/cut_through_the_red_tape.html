to cut through the red tape

1. VERB

/ kʌt θru ðə rɛd teɪp / (AmE), / kʌt θruː ðə rɛd teɪp / (BrE)
to remove bureaucratic obstacles or simplify procedures, especially by overcoming complicated rules and regulations
remove bureaucratic obstacles

Examples

The new regulations helped the company cut through the red tape and launch their product faster.
It took him weeks of persistence to finally cut through the red tape and get his permit approved.
Government officials promised to cut through the red tape to speed up disaster relief efforts.

Synset Examples

She spent hours cutting through the red tape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red tape - excessive bureaucracy or adherence to official rules and formalities

Etymology

Refers to the red tape that was historically used to bind official documents, now symbolic of excessive bureaucracy.
