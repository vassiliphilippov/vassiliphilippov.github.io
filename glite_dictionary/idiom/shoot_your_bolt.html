to shoot your bolt

1. VERB

/ ʃuːt jʊr boʊlt / (AmE), / ʃuːt jɔː bəʊlt / (BrE)
to use up all your resources, energy, or opportunities, leaving nothing in reserve
to use up all resources or options

Examples

He knew he had shot his bolt after delivering his best argument early in the debate.
Don't shoot your bolt before the project is halfway done; pace your efforts.
By the end of the first round, the boxer had shot his bolt and was too tired to continue.

Synset Examples

Once you shoot your bolt, there's no turning back.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expend - to use up or spend completely
  • Antonym: to hold back - to retain or keep in reserve

Etymology

Originally from archery, referring to using up one's last bolt (arrow or missile).
