floodgate opened

1. X

/ ˈflʌdˌɡeɪt ˈoʊpənd / (AmE), / ˈflʌdɡeɪt ˈəʊpənd / (BrE)
used to describe a situation where a sudden, uncontrollable inflow or outpouring of something occurs, often after a barrier or restraint is removed
sudden, uncontrollable influx or outpouring

Examples

When she finally spoke about her experiences, it was as if the floodgate opened and all her emotions poured out.
After the policy changed, the floodgate opened and hundreds of applications arrived within a week.
Once the news broke, the floodgate opened to a wave of public criticism.

Synset Examples

Once the floodgate opened, the crowd rushed in.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: floodgate - barrier controlling water flow

Etymology

From 'floodgate', a gate for regulating water flow, and 'opened'; refers metaphorically to water rushing in when the gate is opened.
