fire in the hole

1. INTJ

/ ˈfaɪɚ ɪn ðə hoʊl / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə ɪn ðə həʊl / (BrE)
used as a warning that an explosion or sudden danger is about to occur, especially in military or mining contexts
warning of impending explosion or danger

Examples

As soon as the fuse was lit, he shouted, fire in the hole to warn everyone.
Hearing fire in the hole, the crew quickly took cover behind the barricades.
You'd better duck when someone yells fire in the hole on a construction site.

Synset Examples

He shouted 'fire in the hole' before the blast.
They all ducked when he yelled 'fire in the hole!'
With a loud bang, he warned them, 'fire in the hole!'

Etymology

Originated in mining and military contexts in the 19th–20th centuries, referring to warning before igniting explosives in a hole.
