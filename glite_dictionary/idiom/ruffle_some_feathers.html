to ruffle some feathers

1. VERB

/ ˈrʌfəl səm ˈfɛðərz / (AmE), / ˈrʌf(ə)l səm ˈfɛðəz / (BrE)
to upset or annoy people, especially by causing disturbance or challenging the status quo
to upset or annoy people

Examples

The politician's comments on tax reform are bound to ruffle some feathers in the community.
She knew her suggestion at the staff meeting might ruffle some feathers, but she spoke up anyway.
Bringing up that sensitive topic could ruffle some feathers, so let's handle it carefully.

Synset Examples

His outspoken comments are sure to ruffle some feathers.
They ruffled some feathers with their bold proposal.
She didn't mean to ruffle some feathers at the meeting.
His loud laughter tends to ruffle everyone's feathers.
He loves to ruffle his sister's feathers for fun.
Don't ruffle her feathers right before the presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to annoy - to make someone slightly angry; to irritate
  • Synonym: upset - to disturb or trouble emotionally

Etymology

From the literal sense of 'ruffle' (to disarrange or disturb, especially feathers of a bird), used figuratively since the 19th century.
