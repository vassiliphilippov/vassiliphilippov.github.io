next thing one knows

1. X

/ nɛkst θɪŋ wʌn noʊz / (AmE), / nɛkst θɪŋ wʌn nəʊz / (BrE)
used to indicate that something happens suddenly or unexpectedly, often without the speaker noticing a transition
something happens suddenly or unexpectedly

Examples

I was cleaning the kitchen, and next thing one knows, hours had passed.
She started chatting with a friend, and next thing one knows, they had missed the train.
You begin scrolling through your phone, and next thing one knows, it's already midnight.

Synset Examples

He started reading, and next thing one knows, the book was finished.
We were chatting, and next thing one knows, it was midnight.
I was just resting, and next thing one knows, I fell asleep.
