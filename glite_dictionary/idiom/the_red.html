the red

1. NOUN

/ ðə rɛd / (AmE), / ðə rɛd / (BrE)
a situation in which a business or person is operating at a financial loss; the state of having debts or expenditures exceeding income.
financial loss, debts exceeding income

Examples

After the holiday season, many retailers found themselves in the red.
If we don't cut costs soon, we'll stay in the red for another quarter.
The firm's new product finally pulled it out of the red and into profitability.

Synset Examples

the company operated at a loss last year
the company operated in the red last year

Related Terms

  • Antonym: the black - a situation in which a business or person is making a profit

Etymology

From the historical practice of recording losses in red ink in financial accounts.
