to act like a tsar

1. VERB

/ ækt laɪk ə tsɑr / (AmE), / ækt laɪk ə zɑː / (BrE)
to behave in an authoritarian or autocratic manner, exercising power in a domineering way
behave in an authoritarian way

Examples

Despite leading a small team, he would often act like a tsar and refuse to listen to feedback.
People accused the mayor of acting like a tsar when she made decisions without consulting the council.
After his promotion, Mark sometimes acts like a tsar and ignores the suggestions of others.

Synset Examples

The CEO acted like a tsar in the boardroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: act like a dictator - to behave in a highly authoritarian way
  • Antonym: democratic - relating to equality and participation in decision-making

Etymology

From 'tsar', title of Russian emperors, associated with autocratic rule.
