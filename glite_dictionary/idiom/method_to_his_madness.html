method to his madness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛθəd tə hɪz ˈmædnəs / (AmE), / ˈmɛθəd tə hɪz ˈmadnəs / (BrE)
a logical plan or reason behind apparently irrational or bizarre actions or behavior
reason behind strange behavior

Examples

Although his desk looks chaotic, there is a method to his madness and he can find everything quickly.
Her unorthodox teaching style may seem confusing, but there's a method to her madness.
It looks disorganized, but trust me, I have a method to my madness.

Synset Examples

His late-night coding sessions are his method to his madness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhyme or reason - observable order or logic to something

Etymology

From the phrase 'Though this be madness, yet there is method in't' in Shakespeare's Hamlet (1603).
