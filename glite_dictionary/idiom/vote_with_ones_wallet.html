to vote with one's wallet

1. VERB

/ voʊt wɪð wʌnz ˈwɑːlɪt / (AmE), / vəʊt wɪð wʌnz ˈwɒlɪt / (BrE)
to express one's preferences or opinions by choosing where or how to spend money
express preference by spending money

Examples

Many environmentally conscious shoppers vote with their wallets by purchasing green products.
After the company raised prices, customers voted with their wallets and stopped buying its goods.
More and more people are voting with their wallets to support fair trade producers.

Synset Examples

He decided to vote with his wallet by choosing local products.
By not shopping there anymore, she voted with her wallet.
Consumers tend to vote with their wallets, supporting ethical brands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boycott - to refuse to buy or use a product or service as a way of expressing disapproval
