to swing both ways

1. VERB

/ swɪŋ boʊθ weɪz / (AmE), / swɪŋ bəʊθ weɪz / (BrE)
to be adaptable or versatile in preferences or interests; often refers to being sexually attracted to both men and women.
be adaptable or bisexual in preferences
informal

Examples

Some people in the arts are comfortable enough to swing both ways in their creative expressions.
She said she's always been able to swing both ways when it comes to romantic relationships.
In business, leaders who swing both ways can adapt to changing markets.

Synset Examples

In her career, she can swing both ways with ease.
Her interests seem to swing both ways, male and female.
He claimed his humor swings both ways.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bisexual - sexually attracted to both men and women
  • Antonym: straight - heterosexual; sexually attracted only to the opposite sex
  • Antonym: gay - homosexual; sexually attracted only to the same sex
