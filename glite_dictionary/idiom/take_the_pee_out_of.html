to take the pee out of

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə piː aʊt ʌv / (AmE), / teɪk ðə piː aʊt ɒv / (BrE)
(chiefly British, informal) To mock or tease someone; to make fun of someone in a playful or derisive way.
mock or tease someone (British, informal)
informal british

Examples

Tom always likes to take the pee out of his friends whenever they make a mistake.
She's constantly taking the pee out of her brother for his bad jokes.
You shouldn't take the pee out of people just because they dress differently.

Synset Examples

They love to take the pee out of each other.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take the mickey - mock or tease someone (British English idiom)
  • Synonym: mock - to make fun of someone
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

A euphemistic variant of 'take the piss (out of)', with 'pee' standing in for 'piss'.
