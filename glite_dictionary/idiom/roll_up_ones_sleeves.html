to roll up one's sleeves

1. VERB

/ roʊl ʌp wʌnz sliːvz / (AmE), / rəʊl ʌp wʌnz sliːvz / (BrE)
to prepare to work hard or tackle a difficult task
prepare to work hard

Examples

With the project deadline approaching, we need to roll up our sleeves and start working overtime.
She decided to roll up her sleeves and clean the entire house by herself.
Before opening the restaurant, the owner would roll up his sleeves and prep food alongside his staff.

Synset Examples

He always rolls up his sleeves when there's heavy lifting.
It's time to roll up our sleeves and get to work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get down to business - start to focus on work seriously
  • Antonym: to take it easy - to relax and not work hard

2. VERB

/ roʊl ʌp wʌnz sliːvz / (AmE), / rəʊl ʌp wʌnz sliːvz / (BrE)
to prepare to begin working hard or tackling a difficult task
prepare to work hard

Examples

Before starting the renovation, they all rolled up their sleeves and set to work.
It's time to roll up our sleeves and fix this problem together.
She rolled up her sleeves before tackling the messy project.

Synset Examples

She rolled up her sleeves before tackling the messy project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get down to business - to begin serious work or discussion
  • Synonym: to pull one's weight - to do one's fair share of work

Etymology

Refers to the physical action of rolling up shirt sleeves before working, used figuratively since the 19th century.
