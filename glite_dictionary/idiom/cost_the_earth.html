to cost the earth

1. VERB

/ kɑst ði ɝθ / (AmE), / kɒst ði ɜːθ / (BrE)
to be extremely expensive; to require a lot of money to buy or do
be very expensive

Examples

Renovating our kitchen didn't cost the earth because we chose budget materials.
Some designer bags can cost the earth if you buy them new.
He wanted the best phone, but thankfully it didn't cost the earth.

Synset Examples

Renovating the house will cost the earth if we're not careful.
That luxury vacation is going to cost the earth.
Buying a new car can cost the earth these days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cost a fortune - to be very expensive
  • Antonym: cost next to nothing - to be very cheap
