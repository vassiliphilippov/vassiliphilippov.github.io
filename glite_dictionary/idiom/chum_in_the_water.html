chum in the water

1. NOUN

/ tʃʌm ɪn ðə ˈwɑtər / (AmE), / tʃʌm ɪn ðə ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
someone or something that is exposed and vulnerable to attack or criticism, often attracting hostile or negative attention
person or thing vulnerable to attack

Examples

After her mistake became public, she was chum in the water for her rivals.
The new regulation was chum in the water for critics of the administration.
His outspoken comments made him chum in the water at the press conference.

Synset Examples

With those rumors, he felt like chum in the water.
He made himself chum in the water by oversharing.
The bad news was chum in the water for gossip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sitting duck - someone or something easily targeted or attacked
  • Synonym: easy target - a person or thing that is easy to attack or criticize

Etymology

From 'chum', bait consisting of chopped fish, spread in the water to attract predators.
