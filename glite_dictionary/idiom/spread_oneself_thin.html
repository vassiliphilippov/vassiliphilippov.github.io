to spread oneself thin

1. VERB

/ sprɛd wʌnˈsɛlf θɪn / (AmE), / sprɛd wʌnˈsɛlf θɪn / (BrE)
to try to do too many things at once, so that you do not have enough time or energy to do any of them well
try to do too many things at once

Examples

If you spread yourself thin, you might end up not finishing anything at all.
He found himself spreading himself thin between work, family, and volunteering.
Don't spread yourself thin—it's better to prioritize a few important tasks.

Synset Examples

She spread herself thin by joining too many clubs.
Don't spread yourself thin; focus on one project.
He realized he was spreading himself too thin at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overextend oneself - to take on more work, responsibilities, or activities than can be managed
