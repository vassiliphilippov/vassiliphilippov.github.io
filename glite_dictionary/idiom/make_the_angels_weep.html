to make the angels weep

1. VERB

/ ˈmeɪk ði ˈeɪn.dʒəlz wiːp / (AmE), / ˈmeɪk ði ˈeɪn.dʒəlz wiːp / (BrE)
to evoke such deep emotion, sadness, or exquisite beauty that even angels would be moved to tears
evoke extreme emotion or beauty

Examples

Her tragic story could make the angels weep with sorrow.
The sorrow in his voice would make the angels weep.
The beauty of the sunrise was enough to make the angels weep.

Synset Examples

His heartfelt speech was enough to make the angels weep.
That painting is so beautiful, it’s enough to make the angels weep.
