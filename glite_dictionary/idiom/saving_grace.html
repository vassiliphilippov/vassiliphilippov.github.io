saving grace

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪvɪŋ ˌɡreɪs / (AmE), / ˈseɪvɪŋ ˌɡreɪs / (BrE)
a quality or feature that makes a person or thing acceptable despite its faults, failings, or disadvantages
redeeming quality or feature

Examples

The delicious sauce was the dish's only saving grace.
His wit and charm proved to be his saving grace during the awkward meeting.
The team's saving grace was their strong defense, even though their offense struggled.

Synset Examples

her love of music remains her one saving grace
her sense of humor has to be a saving grace
the saving grace for both developments is that they are creating jobs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redeeming feature - a positive attribute that compensates for faults
