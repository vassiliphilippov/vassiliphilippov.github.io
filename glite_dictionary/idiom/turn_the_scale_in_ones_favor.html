to turn the scale in one's favor

1. VERB

/ tɝːn ðə skeɪl ɪn wʌnz ˈfeɪ.vɚ / (AmE), / tɜːn ðə skeɪl ɪn wʌnz ˈfeɪ.vər / (BrE)
to influence a situation or outcome to benefit oneself
influence a situation to one's advantage

Examples

She presented new evidence to turn the scale in her favor during the legal dispute.
The coach made a strategic substitution to turn the scale in his favor late in the game.
Careful preparation can turn the scale in your favor when facing tough competition.

Synset Examples

He tried to turn the scale in his favor during negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tip the balance - to influence the outcome of a situation
  • Antonym: work against - to influence a situation to one's disadvantage
