to hold one's breath

1. VERB

/ hoʊld wʌnz brɛθ / (AmE), / həʊld wʌnz brɛθ / (BrE)
To stop breathing for a short period, or to wait anxiously or without expectation.
stop breathing briefly; wait anxiously or doubtfully

Examples

You should hold your breath while swimming under water.
The team promised big changes, but fans shouldn't hold their breath.
Everyone in the room held their breath as the winner was announced.

Synset Examples

I had to hold my breath to avoid the smoke.
They say it’ll rain tomorrow, but I wouldn't hold my breath.
She advised him to hold his breath and britches tight.
Hold your breath while you dive underwater.
He promised to change, but don't hold your breath.
You might want to hold your breath for this surprise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wait with bated breath - wait anxiously or eagerly for something to happen
  • Antonym: to breathe - to take air into the lungs and then expel it
