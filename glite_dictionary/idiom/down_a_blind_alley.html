down a blind alley

1. ADV

/ daʊn ə blaɪnd ˈæli / (AmE), / daʊn ə blaɪnd ˈæli / (BrE)
following a course of action that leads nowhere or is unproductive
pursuing an unproductive course

Examples

After months of research, the scientists realized they were going down a blind alley.
I tried fixing the computer myself, but soon saw I was heading down a blind alley.
The investigation went down a blind alley until new evidence emerged.

Synset Examples

His idea led us down a blind alley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dead end - a situation offering no progress or advancement
