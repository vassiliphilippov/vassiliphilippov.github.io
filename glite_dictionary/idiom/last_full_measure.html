last full measure

1. NOUN

/ læst fʊl ˈmɛʒər / (AmE), / lɑːst fʊl ˈmɛʒə / (BrE)
the utmost effort or ultimate sacrifice, especially in the face of great difficulty or danger
utmost effort or ultimate sacrifice

Examples

She put in her last full measure during the final exam and passed with honors.
The soldiers gave their last full measure in defense of their country.
He devoted his last full measure to completing the project before the deadline.

Synset Examples

She put in her last full measure during the final exam.
They fought to the last full measure in the championship.
He gave his last full measure for the team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sacrifice - giving up something valued for the sake of others
  • Synonym: ultimate effort - the greatest or final effort possible

Etymology

Popularized by Abraham Lincoln in the Gettysburg Address (1863): "They gave the last full measure of devotion."
