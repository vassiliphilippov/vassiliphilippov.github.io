to trip the light fantastic

1. VERB

/ trɪp ðə laɪt fænˈtæstɪk / (AmE), / trɪp ðə laɪt fænˈtæstɪk / (BrE)
to dance or move in a lively, graceful way, especially to music
dance in a lively way

Examples

At the party, everyone couldn’t wait to trip the light fantastic once the band started playing.
Back in their youth, they would often trip the light fantastic at the ballroom downtown.
He decided it was time to trip the light fantastic and impress everyone with his dance moves.

Synset Examples

My husband and I like to dance at home to the radio

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dance - to move rhythmically to music, typically following a set sequence of steps

Etymology

Phrase popularized from John Milton's poem 'L'Allegro' (1645): 'Come, and trip it as ye go, on the light fantastick toe.'
