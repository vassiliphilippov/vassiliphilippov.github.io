Chinaman's chance

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪnəmənz tʃæns / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪnəmənz tʃɑːns / (BrE)
Almost no possibility of success; a very slim or improbable chance (now considered offensive and archaic).
almost no chance of success (offensive, archaic)
offensive archaic

Examples

With limited funds, he knew he had a Chinaman's chance of winning the contract.
They entered the tournament with only a Chinaman's chance of beating the champions.
She realized she didn't have a Chinaman's chance of getting the promotion over her experienced colleagues.

Synset Examples

Without proper experience, he thought he had a Chinaman's chance at success.
He knew he had a Chinaman's chance of winning the lottery.
In this competition, she felt she had a Chinaman's chance against the experts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long shot - something having a very small chance of succeeding
  • Degree Higher: chance - possibility of something happening

Etymology

From 19th-century American usage, reflecting discriminatory attitudes toward Chinese immigrants.
