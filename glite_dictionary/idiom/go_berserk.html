to go berserk

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ bərˈzɜrk / (AmE), / ɡəʊ bəˈzɜːk / (BrE)
to become violently angry or out of control; to act in a wild, frenzied manner
lose control and become wild or angry

Examples

If I spill coffee on his papers, he will go berserk for sure.
The crowd began to go berserk after the team scored the winning goal.
She almost went berserk when she realized she had lost her passport.

Synset Examples

He'll go berserk if you touch his computer.
The dog went berserk at the sight of the squirrel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lose one's temper - to suddenly become very angry
  • Antonym: to keep one's cool - to remain calm under stress or provocation

Etymology

From 'berserk', originally referring to Norse warriors known for frenzied rage; 'go' + 'berserk' as in 'to become berserk'.
