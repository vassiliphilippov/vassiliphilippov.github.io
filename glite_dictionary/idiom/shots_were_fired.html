shots were fired

1. X

/ ʃɑts wɚ ˈfaɪɚd / (AmE), / ʃɒts wə ˈfaɪəd / (BrE)
(informal) Criticisms or attacks were made, especially in a figurative or conversational context.
criticisms or verbal attacks made
informal

Examples

During the heated debate, shots were fired from both sides of the room.
When the news broke, shots were fired at the company's leadership for their poor decisions.
On social media, shots were fired after the politician made a controversial statement.

Synset Examples

In the meeting, several shots were fired at the proposal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take a shot at - to criticize or attack someone or something verbally

Etymology

From the literal phrase used to describe gunfire, now commonly used metaphorically for verbal conflict.
