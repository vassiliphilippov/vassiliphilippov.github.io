in high cotton

1. ADJ

/ ɪn haɪ ˈkɑtn̩ / (AmE), / ɪn haɪ ˈkɒtn̩ / (BrE)
in a position of comfort, wealth, or prosperity, especially after a period of lesser fortune
in a prosperous or comfortable situation
informal american

Examples

Ever since she got that new job, she's been in high cotton.
After winning the lottery, they found themselves in high cotton for the first time.
He's in high cotton now, but he remembers when money was tight.

Synset Examples

After the promotion, she felt like she was in high cotton.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: well off - in a good financial situation
  • Antonym: hard up - in a difficult or poor financial situation

Etymology

U.S. Southern origin, referencing times when a high cotton crop meant prosperity for farmers.
