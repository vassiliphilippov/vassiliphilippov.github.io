to stand on someone's shoulders

1. VERB

/ stænd ɑn ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈʃoʊldɚz / (AmE), / stænd ɒn ˈsʌmwənz ˈʃəʊldəz / (BrE)
to use the knowledge, discoveries, or achievements of people who came before you in order to make further progress
build upon predecessors' achievements

Examples

Many scientific breakthroughs happen when researchers stand on someone's shoulders rather than start from scratch.
As young artists, we often stand on someone's shoulders to develop our own unique styles.
Technological advancements move quickly because each generation stands on someone's shoulders.

Synset Examples

He was able to stand on his mentor's shoulders to succeed.
To achieve this, we all stand on our predecessors' shoulders.
She stands on her father's shoulders as a successful artist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to build on - make use of others' work to pursue further development

Etymology

Based on the metaphor 'standing on the shoulders of giants', popularized by Isaac Newton in the 17th century.
