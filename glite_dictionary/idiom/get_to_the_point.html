to get to the point

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt tə ðə pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ɡɛt tə ðə pɔɪnt / (BrE)
to begin talking about the most important or relevant part of a subject, avoiding unnecessary detail or digression
speak directly about the main issue

Examples

Please get to the point instead of telling the whole backstory.
He urged the speaker to get to the point because time was running short.
It's important to get to the point during a business meeting.

Synset Examples

She always knows how to get to the point quickly.
Stop playing games and get to the point.
Stop talking moeshit and get to the point.
Let’s skip the small talk and get to the point.
Just get to the point and tell me the news.
Stop blathering and get to the point.
Could you please get to the point of your argument?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: come to the point - to begin talking about what is important or relevant
