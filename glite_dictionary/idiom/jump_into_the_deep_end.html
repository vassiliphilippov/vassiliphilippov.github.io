to jump into the deep end

1. VERB

/ dʒʌmp ˈɪntu ðə dip ɛnd / (AmE), / dʒʌmp ˈɪntuː ðə diːp end / (BrE)
to begin something that is difficult or challenging without any preparation or experience
start something risky or challenging unprepared

Examples

Starting a new job overseas, she really jumped into the deep end.
When he moved out on his own, he jumped into the deep end and learned a lot.
Sometimes you have to jump into the deep end to discover what you're capable of.

Synset Examples

He jumped into the deep end without knowing how to swim.
Starting a business can feel like jumping into the deep end.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take the plunge - to decide to do something important or risky, especially after thinking about it for a long time
  • Antonym: to play it safe - to act cautiously and avoid risks
