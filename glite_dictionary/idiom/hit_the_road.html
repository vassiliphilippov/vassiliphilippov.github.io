to hit the road

1. VERB

/ hɪt ðə roʊd / (AmE), / hɪt ðə rəʊd / (BrE)
to leave a place or begin a journey, especially by starting to travel by road
to start a journey or leave a place
informal

Examples

We decided to hit the road at dawn to make the most of our trip.
After saying goodbye to her friends, she got in the car and hit the road.
I think it's getting late; we should hit the road if we want to arrive before dark.

Synset Examples

Let's hit the road before traffic gets heavy.
Let's rev up the engine and hit the road.
He said it was time to roll and hit the road.
It's time to hit the road and start our journey.
After breakfast, we hit the road for the beach.
We hit the road early to avoid traffic.
He had to pump gas before hitting the road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to set off - to begin a journey
  • Synonym: to depart - to leave, especially to start a journey
