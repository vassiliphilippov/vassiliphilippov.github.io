to got to run

1. VERB

/ ɡɑt tə rʌn / (AmE), / ɡɒt tə rʌn / (BrE)
used to indicate that one needs to leave immediately or very soon, often abruptly or in haste
need to leave quickly
informal

Examples

Sorry, I’ve got to run, or I’ll miss my train.
It was great catching up, but I’ve got to run to another meeting.
He checked his watch and said, 'I’ve got to run, let’s talk soon.'

Synset Examples

I’ve got to run; see you later!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: have to go - need to leave
