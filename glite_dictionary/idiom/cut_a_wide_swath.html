to cut a wide swath

1. VERB

/ kʌt ə waɪd swɑθ / (AmE), / kʌt ə waɪd swɒθ / (BrE)
to have a significant effect or influence, often attracting attention through one's actions or achievements
have a significant influence or impact

Examples

With her innovative ideas, she cut a wide swath in the tech industry.
His bold speeches cut a wide swath through the political landscape.
The company's aggressive expansion strategy cut a wide swath across the market.

Synset Examples

Her charity work has cut a wide swath in the community.
He cut a wide swath with his bold business strategies.
He cut a wide swath through the competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make a splash - to attract a lot of attention, especially suddenly or dramatically
  • Synonym: to leave a mark - to have a lasting effect or influence

Etymology

From the literal image of a scythe cutting a wide path through crops, used figuratively since the 19th century.
