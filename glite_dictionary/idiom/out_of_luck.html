out of luck

1. ADJ

/ aʊt əv lʌk / (AmE), / aʊt əv lʌk / (BrE)
having no success or experiencing misfortune, especially when unable to get something desired or needed
experiencing bad luck; unsuccessful

Examples

I'm afraid you're out of luck; the tickets are all sold out.
She was out of luck and missed her train by just two minutes.
We tried to get a table at the restaurant, but we were out of luck.

Synset Examples

They were out of luck when the store closed early.
If you missed the bus, you’re out of luck.
He realized he was out of luck without a ticket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unlucky - not having good fortune; experiencing bad luck
  • Antonym: fortunate - having good fortune; experiencing luck
