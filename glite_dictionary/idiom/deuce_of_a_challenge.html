deuce of a challenge

1. NOUN

/ dus əv ə ˈtʃælɪndʒ / (AmE), / djuːs əv ə ˈtʃælɪndʒ / (BrE)
A very difficult or frustrating task.
very difficult or frustrating task

Examples

The final level of the game was a deuce of a challenge to complete.
Repairing the old car without manuals proved a deuce of a challenge.
Getting his approval for the project was a deuce of a challenge.

Synset Examples

Finding parking here is a deuce of a challenge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tough nut to crack - something very difficult to solve or deal with
  • Antonym: piece of cake - something very easy to do

Etymology

From 'deuce' as a mild expletive meaning 'devil' or 'something severe', used since the 17th century.
