to put foot in it

1. VERB

/ pʊt fʊt ɪn ɪt / (AmE), / pʊt fʊt ɪn ɪt / (BrE)
to say or do something embarrassing or tactless, usually accidentally
say or do something embarrassing

Examples

She didn't mean to put foot in it, but her comment made everyone uncomfortable.
I really put foot in it when I asked about his job—he'd just been fired.
Whenever he tries to be funny, he tends to put foot in it instead.

Synset Examples

She always seems to put her foot in it during conversations.
He really put his foot in it at the party.
I put my foot in it by mentioning her ex.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to put one's foot in one's mouth - to say something embarrassing or inappropriate
