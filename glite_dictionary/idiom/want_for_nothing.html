to want for nothing

1. VERB

/ wɑnt fər ˈnʌθɪŋ / (AmE), / wɒnt fə ˈnʌθɪŋ / (BrE)
to have everything one needs or desires; to lack nothing essential or desired
have everything one needs or desires

Examples

With their wealth, the children want for nothing.
After moving to the city, she wanted for nothing.
As long as his family is happy, he wants for nothing.

Synset Examples

With her new job, she doesn't want for nothing anymore.
They live in luxury and want for nothing at all.
After his promotion, he wanted for nothing in life.
