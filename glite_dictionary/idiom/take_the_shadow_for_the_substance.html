to take the shadow for the substance

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə ˈʃædoʊ fɔr ðə ˈsʌbstəns / (AmE), / teɪk ðə ˈʃædəʊ fə ðə ˈsʌbstəns / (BrE)
to mistake something insignificant or insubstantial for something real or important
confuse appearance with reality

Examples

It is easy to take the shadow for the substance in a world full of clever advertising.
Many people take the shadow for the substance when judging others by appearances alone.
Careful analysis helps us avoid taking the shadow for the substance in complex situations.

Synset Examples

It's easy to take the shadow for the substance in this debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistake appearance for reality - to confuse something's outward look with its true nature
