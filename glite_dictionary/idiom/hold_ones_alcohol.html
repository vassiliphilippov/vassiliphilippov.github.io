to hold one's alcohol

1. VERB

/ hoʊld wʌnz ˈæl.kə.hɑl / (AmE), / həʊld wʌnz ˈæl.kə.hɒl / (BrE)
to be able to drink alcoholic beverages without becoming drunk or showing signs of intoxication
tolerate alcohol without getting drunk

Examples

Despite drinking several glasses of wine, she managed to hold her alcohol all night.
Some people can't hold their alcohol no matter how little they drink.
He prides himself on being able to hold his alcohol better than his friends.

Synset Examples

He can really hold his alcohol during parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handle one's liquor - to tolerate alcoholic drinks without getting drunk
  • Antonym: lightweight - someone who becomes drunk easily
