wild blue yonder

1. NOUN

/ waɪld blu ˈjɑndɚ / (AmE), / waɪld blu ˈjɒndə / (BrE)
the sky, especially as seen during the day; the open sky
the open sky during daylight

Examples

The fighter jets soared into the wild blue yonder, leaving vapor trails behind them.
After graduation, he set off into the wild blue yonder to seek new adventures.
She gazed up at the wild blue yonder, dreaming of what lay beyond.

Synset Examples

he shot an arrow into the blue

Etymology

Popularized by the 1939 song 'Into the Wild Blue Yonder', originally U.S. Air Force anthem.
