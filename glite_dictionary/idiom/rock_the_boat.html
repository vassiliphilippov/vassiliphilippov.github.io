to rock the boat

1. VERB

/ rɑk ðə boʊt / (AmE), / rɒk ðə bəʊt / (BrE)
to do or say something that causes trouble or disturbs the balance of a situation
cause disruption or trouble

Examples

She didn't want to rock the boat by raising concerns during the meeting.
His refusal to follow the new rules really rocked the boat at the company.
Try not to rock the boat while negotiations are still delicate.

Synset Examples

He tends to rock the boat during team meetings.
Please don’t rock the boat; everything is going smoothly.
We need to avoid rocking the boat with our complaints.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make waves - to cause trouble or disturb the existing situation
  • Antonym: to keep the peace - to avoid causing trouble or disruption
