old hat

1. ADJ

/ oʊld hæt / (AmE), / əʊld hæt / (BrE)
Something that is no longer interesting, original, or fashionable because it has been seen or done before.
no longer interesting or fashionable

Examples

All of these design ideas are old hat to experienced architects.
The movie's plot seemed like old hat to most viewers.
Don't tell me that story again—it's old hat by now.

Synset Examples

Her jokes are getting old hat; we’ve heard them all.
The idea felt old hat, lacking any originality.
He prefers new challenges rather than the old hat routines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outdated - no longer in use or fashionable
  • Antonym: fresh - new or original

Etymology

Originally referred to an old, worn hat as something familiar and unremarkable; metaphorical sense from early 20th century.
