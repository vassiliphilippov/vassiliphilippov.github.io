out of one's depth

1. ADJ

/ aʊt əv wʌnz dɛpθ / (AmE), / aʊt əv wʌnz dɛpθ / (BrE)
unable to deal with a situation because it is too difficult or unfamiliar; feeling overwhelmed or lacking the necessary knowledge or skill
feeling overwhelmed or unqualified

Examples

After the promotion, Mark soon realized he was out of his depth managing a large team.
She found herself out of her depth when the legal issues grew more complicated.
Many students feel out of their depth at the beginning of university.

Synset Examples

He quickly became out of his depth in the technical meeting.
I felt out of my depth during the advanced seminar.
She realized she was out of her depth in that discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overwhelmed - feeling unable to cope; synonym in some contexts
  • Synonym: in over one's head - another idiom meaning out of one's depth
  • Antonym: competent - able to do something successfully; opposite ability to out of one's depth
