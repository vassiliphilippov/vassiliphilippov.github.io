turkeys voting for an early Christmas

1. X

/ ˈtɝːkiz ˈvoʊtɪŋ fər ən ˈɝːli ˈkrɪsməs / (AmE), / ˈtɜːkiz ˈvəʊtɪŋ fə ən ˈɜːli ˈkrɪsməs / (BrE)
to make a decision or support something that is clearly against one's own interests or likely to result in one's harm or downfall
act against one's own interests
british

Examples

Implementing these new policies would be like turkeys voting for an early Christmas.
If the workers accept those terms, it's turkeys voting for an early Christmas.
Backing that candidate would be turkeys voting for an early Christmas for the party.

Synset Examples

Giving them power is like turkeys voting for an early Christmas.
Trusting them with money is like turkeys voting for an early Christmas.
Their plan is doomed, like turkeys voting for an early Christmas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutting off one's nose to spite one's face - to harm oneself in an effort to harm someone else, resulting in self-detriment
  • Synonym: self-sabotage - the act of causing harm to one's own interests or success

Etymology

Originated in British political discourse, referencing the fate of turkeys at Christmas.
