tomorrow's chip paper

1. NOUN

/ təˈmɑːroʊz tʃɪp ˈpeɪpər / (AmE), / təˈmɒrəʊz tʃɪp ˈpeɪpə / (BrE)
news or information that is unimportant and will quickly be forgotten
news soon forgotten; insignificant
british

Examples

The rumor was everywhere yesterday, but it'll be tomorrow's chip paper soon enough.
Politicians know that negative headlines are just tomorrow's chip paper.
He dismissed their complaints as tomorrow's chip paper, not worth worrying about.

Synset Examples

This news is just tomorrow's chip paper, who cares?
That scandal will be tomorrow's chip paper soon enough.
His promises are as useful as tomorrow's chip paper.

Etymology

Refers to the practice in the UK of wrapping fish and chips in old newspapers, suggesting today's news is worthless by tomorrow.
