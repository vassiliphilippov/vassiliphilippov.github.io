all or nothing

1. X

/ ˌɔl ər ˈnʌθɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɔːl ɔː ˈnʌθɪŋ / (BrE)
involving complete commitment to a course of action with no compromise or middle ground; either the maximum effort or none at all
approach with complete commitment, no compromise

Examples

Her approach to studying is all or nothing; she either spends hours revising or doesn't open her books at all.
They adopted an all or nothing strategy during negotiations, refusing to compromise on any points.
You can't always succeed with an all or nothing attitude; sometimes small steps are more effective.

Synset Examples

He never does things by halves; it’s all or nothing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: all in - fully committed or invested
  • Antonym: halfhearted - without full effort or enthusiasm
