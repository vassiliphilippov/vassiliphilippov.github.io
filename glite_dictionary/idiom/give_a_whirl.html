to give a whirl

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ə wɜrl / (AmE), / ɡɪv ə wɜːl / (BrE)
to try something, especially for the first time, to see if you like it or are good at it
try something out
informal

Examples

If you’re unsure about yoga, you could give it a whirl at the community class.
She’d never tried mountain biking before, but decided to give it a whirl last Saturday.
You might like chess—why not give it a whirl?

Synset Examples

I decided to give surfing a whirl this summer.
Why not give cooking a whirl this weekend?
She will give painting a whirl at the art class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to try out - to test or attempt something
  • Synonym: to have a go - make an attempt at something
