she'll be right

1. X

/ ʃil bi ˈraɪt / (AmE), / ʃiːl bi ˈraɪt / (BrE)
used, especially in Australia, to express reassurance that everything will turn out fine and there is no need to worry
Australian idiom for 'everything will be fine'
informal british

Examples

Forgot my keys at home, but she'll be right—I'll manage without them today.
The tent's a bit small, but she'll be right for the night.
We might be late to the party, but she'll be right.

Synset Examples

I forgot my umbrella, but she'll be right.
The dinner is a mess, but she'll be right.
Don't worry about the car; she'll be right.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: no worries - expression meaning 'don't worry; everything is fine'

Etymology

Popularized in Australian English; 'she' refers colloquially to a situation or thing.
