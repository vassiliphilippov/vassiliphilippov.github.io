time on someone's side

1. X

/ taɪm ɑn ˈsʌmwʌnz saɪd / (AmE), / taɪm ɒn ˈsʌmwʌnz saɪd / (BrE)
having an advantage because one has plenty of time to achieve something or for things to improve
having an advantage due to having enough time

Examples

Don't worry about making a quick decision—you have time on your side.
She feels optimistic because, at her age, time on her side allows her to try again.
With time on their side, the team could carefully plan the next steps.

Synset Examples

With this project, we definitely have time on our side.
We have time on our side for this decision.
When you're young, you have time on your side.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: against the clock - having little time to accomplish something
  • Antonym: to run out of time - having no more time left to complete something
