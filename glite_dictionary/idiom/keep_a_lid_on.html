to keep a lid on

1. VERB

/ kip ə lɪd ɑn / (AmE), / kiːp ə lɪd ɒn / (BrE)
to control or suppress something, especially to prevent it from becoming known or out of control
control or suppress (something)

Examples

The government worked hard to keep a lid on the story to avoid public panic.
She struggled to keep a lid on her frustration during the meeting.
It's important to keep a lid on costs if we want the project to succeed.

Synset Examples

She tried to keep a lid on her excitement.
We need to keep a lid on this project until it's ready.
Make sure to keep a lid on the surprise party details.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suppress - to put an end to the activities of (a person, body of persons, etc.)
  • Synonym: to conceal - to keep something secret or hidden
