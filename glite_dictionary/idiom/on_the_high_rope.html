on the high rope

1. ADV

/ ɑn ðə haɪ roʊp / (AmE), / ɒn ðə haɪ rəʊp / (BrE)
in a risky or precarious situation where failure is possible or consequences are severe
in a risky or precarious situation

Examples

She felt on the high rope when making critical decisions without support.
Negotiating the deal placed him on the high rope professionally.
Handling two jobs at once left her on the high rope for months.

Synset Examples

Managing the project alone felt like being on the high rope.
He felt like he was on the high rope during the presentation.
The team was on the high ropes, tackling complex challenges.
I felt like I was on the high ropes during the interview.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on thin ice - in a risky or dangerous situation
  • Synonym: in hot water - in trouble or difficulty

Etymology

Metaphorically derived from circus or acrobatic acts performed on a high rope, suggesting danger and risk.
