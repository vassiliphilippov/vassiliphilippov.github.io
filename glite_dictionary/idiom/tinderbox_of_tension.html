tinderbox of tension

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪndɚˌbɑks əv ˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˈtɪndəbɒks əv ˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
a situation that is highly charged and likely to erupt into conflict or violence at any moment
situation likely to erupt in conflict

Examples

The negotiations were a tinderbox of tension, with both sides barely containing their anger.
After months of disputes, the workplace became a tinderbox of tension.
The match was played in a tinderbox of tension as fans anxiously awaited the result.

Synset Examples

Their arguments turned the room into a tinderbox of tension.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powder keg - a situation that could erupt into violence

Etymology

From 'tinderbox', a container for tinder, figuratively used since the 19th century for a place or situation easily set alight or provoked.
