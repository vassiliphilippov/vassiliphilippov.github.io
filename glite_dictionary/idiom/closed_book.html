closed book

1. NOUN

/ kloʊzd bʊk / (AmE), / kləʊzd bʊk / (BrE)
a subject, person, or thing that is difficult to understand or not known about
something difficult to understand or unknown

Examples

Advanced quantum physics is a closed book to most people.
To me, her past will always be a closed book.
Astrology remains a closed book for many scientists.

Synset Examples

how it got out is a mystery
it remains one of nature's secrets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mystery - something difficult or impossible to understand or explain
  • Antonym: open book - something or someone easy to understand or explain

2. NOUN

/ kloʊzd bʊk / (AmE), / kləʊzd bʊk / (BrE)
a subject that is finished and no longer considered, or a person or thing that is difficult to understand or know about
something finished or difficult to understand

Examples

To everyone at the company, the controversy was now a closed book.
Ever since his childhood, his emotions have been a closed book even to his closest friends.
The history of that ancient language is a closed book to most modern scholars.

Synset Examples

To him, that chapter in her life was a closed book.
After the scandal, he's become a closed book.
She regarded her past as a closed book she wouldn't reopen.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open book - someone or something easy to understand or know about
