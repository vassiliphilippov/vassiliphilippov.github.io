to keep one's eyes open

1. VERB

/ kip wʌnz aɪz oʊpən / (AmE), / kiːp wʌnz aɪz əʊpən / (BrE)
to be alert and watchful; to pay close attention to possible danger or opportunity
be alert and attentive

Examples

You should keep your eyes open when walking alone at night.
While traveling, keep your eyes open for pickpockets.
If you keep your eyes open, you might notice something interesting.

Synset Examples

Keep your eyes peeled for any policemen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keep one's eyes peeled - be very watchful or attentive

2. VERB

/ kip wʌnz aɪz ˈoʊpən / (AmE), / kiːp wʌnz aɪz ˈəʊpən / (BrE)
to remain alert and watchful, especially to notice potential dangers or opportunities
stay alert; be watchful

Examples

When traveling in a new city, it's wise to keep your eyes open for pickpockets.
She always tries to keep her eyes open for new job opportunities.
It's important to keep your eyes open during meetings to spot any problems early.

Synset Examples

He approached the new job with his eyes open.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be on the lookout - to be watching for something or someone
  • Synonym: be vigilant - to be alert to possible danger
