to think with one's little head

1. VERB

/ θɪŋk wɪð wʌnz ˈlɪtl hɛd / (AmE), / θɪŋk wɪð wʌnz ˈlɪtl hɛd / (BrE)
To make decisions or act based on emotion rather than logical reasoning.
act from emotion, not logic

Examples

Whenever he made decisions about relationships, he tended to think with his little head.
In stressful situations, it's easy to think with your little head and let emotions drive your actions.
She admitted that she was thinking with her little head when she quit her job on impulse.

Synset Examples

She realized she was thinking with her little head during the argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: think with one's heart - to act according to emotion or feeling, not logic
  • Antonym: think with one's head - to act rationally or logically
