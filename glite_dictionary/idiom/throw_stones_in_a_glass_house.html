to throw stones in a glass house

1. VERB

/ θroʊ stoʊnz ɪn ə ɡlæs haʊs / (AmE), / θrəʊ stəʊnz ɪn ə ɡlɑːs haʊs / (BrE)
to criticize others when you yourself are vulnerable to similar criticism or have similar faults
criticize while vulnerable to criticism

Examples

He accused his colleague of incompetence, but he shouldn't throw stones in a glass house given his own mistakes.
Before you throw stones in a glass house, consider your own actions and flaws.
Politicians often throw stones in a glass house without addressing their own shortcomings.

Synset Examples

If you throw stones in a glass house, expect retaliation.
He learned not to throw stones in a glass house after the incident.
She shouldn’t throw stones in a glass house after her own failures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypocrisy - the practice of claiming to have moral standards to which one's own behavior does not conform
  • Degree Higher: censorious - tending to criticize others harshly or frequently

Etymology

From the proverb 'People who live in glass houses shouldn't throw stones', meaning one should not criticize others for faults one has oneself.
