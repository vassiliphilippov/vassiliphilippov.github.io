to dig a hole for oneself

1. VERB

/ dɪɡ ə hoʊl fər wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / dɪɡ ə həʊl fə wʌnˈself / (BrE)
to act in a way that causes oneself difficulty or puts oneself in a problematic situation, usually as a result of one's own actions or decisions
create problems for oneself

Examples

By telling so many lies, he dug a hole for himself that he couldn't get out of.
She dug a hole for herself by not preparing for the interview.
If you blame your colleague, you might just dig a hole for yourself at work.

Synset Examples

She dug a hole for herself by skipping work.
He really dug a hole for himself with that comment.
She dug her own grave by ignoring the warnings.
She dug herself in a hole by not studying for the exam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dig one's own grave - to do something that will cause one's own failure or downfall

create problems for oneself
