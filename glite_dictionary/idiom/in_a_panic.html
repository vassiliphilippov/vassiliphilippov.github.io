in a panic

1. ADV

/ ɪn ə ˈpænɪk / (AmE), / ɪn ə ˈpænɪk / (BrE)
experiencing sudden or intense anxiety or fear, often leading to uncontrolled or irrational behavior
experiencing extreme anxiety or fear

Examples

The children were in a panic when the fire alarm suddenly rang.
Don't get in a panic if things don't go as planned; stay focused.
He was in a panic after realizing he had forgotten his passport at home.

Synset Examples

They were in a panic as the deadline approached.
She called me, clearly in a state of panic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panicked - suddenly overcome with fear or anxiety
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness or anxiety
