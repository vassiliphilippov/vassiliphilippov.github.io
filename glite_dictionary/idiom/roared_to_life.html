to roared to life

1. VERB

/ rɔrd tə laɪf / (AmE), / rɔːd tə laɪf / (BrE)
(of a machine or engine) started suddenly and noisily, often after being turned on or activated.
start operating suddenly and noisily

Examples

The old tractor roared to life after months in the barn.
As soon as she turned the key, the car roared to life and startled everyone nearby.
The stadium roared to life as the band began to play.

Synset Examples

The chainsaw roared to life as he started working.
The motor roared to life with a simple turn of the key.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spring to life - to become suddenly active or lively
