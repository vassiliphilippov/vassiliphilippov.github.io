to show who's boss

1. VERB

/ ʃoʊ huːz bɑs / (AmE), / ʃəʊ huːz bɒs / (BrE)
to demonstrate one's authority over others or make it clear who is in charge
assert authority; establish control

Examples

The new manager wanted to show who's boss by making several important decisions on her first day.
He raised his voice just to show who's boss during the heated debate.
Sometimes parents feel the need to show who's boss when their children challenge their authority.

Synset Examples

It's time to show who's boss in this project.
She decided to show who's boss during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assert oneself - to behave confidently and forcefully
  • Synonym: to take charge - to assume control or responsibility
