to make a dash for

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə dæʃ fər / (AmE), / meɪk ə daʃ fə / (BrE)
to run quickly towards a particular place or object, usually in order to reach it before something happens or before someone else
run quickly towards something

Examples

As soon as the doors opened, the shoppers made a dash for the sale items.
When the bell rang, the students made a dash for the cafeteria.
She made a dash for her umbrella when it started to rain.

Synset Examples

He made a dash for the bus before it left.
The kids made a dash for the playground when school ended.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - to move quickly with urgency
  • Synonym: race - to move very fast towards something
