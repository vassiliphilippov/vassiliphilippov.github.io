to turn the screw

1. VERB

/ tɝn ðə skru / (AmE), / tɜːn ðə skruː / (BrE)
to apply pressure or increase demands on someone in order to achieve a desired result
apply additional pressure or demands

Examples

The coach knew he had to turn the screw to get the best from his players.
When the negotiations stalled, the company decided to turn the screw on its suppliers.
Her parents began to turn the screw about her grades as exams approached.

Synset Examples

The coach had to turn the screw to motivate the team.
The manager decided to turn the screw on deadlines.
He felt they would turn the screw until he complied.
