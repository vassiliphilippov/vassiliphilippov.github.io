to know where it's been

1. VERB

/ noʊ wɛr ɪts bɪn / (AmE), / nəʊ weə ɪts bɪn / (BrE)
to understand or have knowledge about the previous history or cleanliness of something, especially as a reason for caution or avoidance
be aware of previous history or cleanliness
informal

Examples

I never borrow someone else's toothbrush because I don't know where it's been.
She refused to eat the cake left out on the counter; you never know where it's been.
Before you sit on that bench, make sure you know where it's been.

Synset Examples

I won't drink from that cup; you don't know where it's been.
