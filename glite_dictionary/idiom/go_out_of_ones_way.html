to go out of one's way

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ aʊt əv wʌnz weɪ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ aʊt əv wʌnz weɪ / (BrE)
to make a special or extra effort to do something, especially to help someone or achieve a goal
make a special or extra effort

Examples

She always goes out of her way to make new employees feel welcome.
He really went out of his way to help me move last weekend.
You don't have to go out of your way—just do what you can.

Synset Examples

I would go out of my way for a good friend.
He went out of his way to make everyone feel welcome.
She went out of her way to help him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: make an effort - to try hard to do something, especially something that is not easy
