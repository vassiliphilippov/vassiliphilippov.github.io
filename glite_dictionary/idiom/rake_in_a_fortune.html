to rake in a fortune

1. VERB

/ reɪk ɪn ə ˈfɔrtʃən / (AmE), / reɪk ɪn ə ˈfɔːtʃuːn / (BrE)
to earn a large amount of money, especially with little effort or in a short time
earn a large amount of money

Examples

During the tech boom, many startups managed to rake in a fortune overnight.
He hopes to rake in a fortune from his new invention.
Tour companies can rake in a fortune during the tourist season.

Synset Examples

They managed to rake in a fortune from the sale.
She continues to rake in profits from her online business.
The concert tickets raked in millions for charity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make a killing - earn a lot of money quickly
  • Synonym: strike it rich - suddenly become very wealthy
  • Antonym: lose a fortune - to lose a large amount of money

Etymology

From the verb 'rake in,' figuratively meaning to gather or accumulate, combined with 'fortune' for wealth.
