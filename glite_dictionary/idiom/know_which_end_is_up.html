to know which end is up

1. VERB

/ noʊ wɪt͡ʃ ɛnd ɪz ʌp / (AmE), / nəʊ wɪtʃ end ɪz ʌp / (BrE)
to understand what is going on or how things work, especially in a particular situation or field
be aware of what is happening; be knowledgeable
informal

Examples

After a week at her new job, she finally knew which end was up.
Don't try to trick him—he knows which end is up in this business.
It took me a while to know which end is up at college.

Synset Examples

He’s been in the business long enough to know which end is up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to have a clue - to have knowledge or understanding of something
  • Synonym: be in the know - to be well-informed
