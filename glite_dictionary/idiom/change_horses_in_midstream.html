to change horses in midstream

1. VERB

/ tʃeɪndʒ ˈhɔrsɪz ɪn ˈmɪdˌstrim / (AmE), / tʃeɪndʒ ˈhɔːsɪz ɪn ˈmɪdstriːm / (BrE)
to change plans, strategies, or leaders during an ongoing process, usually when it is risky or inadvisable
change plans or leaders during a process

Examples

It's often unwise to change horses in midstream when a project is already underway.
During a negotiation, they realized it was too late to change horses in midstream.
She warned the team not to change horses in midstream while revising the proposal.

Synset Examples

He decided not to change horses in midstream with the election coming.
It's risky to change horses in midstream during the project.
Changing horses in midstream can lead to disastrous results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to change course - to alter one's plan or direction
  • Antonym: to stick to one's guns - to maintain one's position or plan

Etymology

Alludes to the risk of switching horses while crossing a stream; attested in 19th-century American English.
