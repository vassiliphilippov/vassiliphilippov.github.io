to handle with kid gloves

1. VERB

/ ˈhændl wɪð kɪd ɡlʌvz / (AmE), / ˈhændl wɪð kɪd ɡlʌvz / (BrE)
to treat someone or something with great care and sensitivity, especially to avoid upsetting them
treat with great care and sensitivity

Examples

The new employee is sensitive, so the manager tries to handle with kid gloves when giving feedback.
Some clients must be handled with kid gloves to avoid causing offense or upset.
Due to the political tensions, the diplomats handled with kid gloves during negotiations.

Synset Examples

You have to handle the students with kid gloves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treat with care - to show careful or gentle treatment

Etymology

Refers to 'kid gloves' made from soft young goat leather, symbolizing delicacy; figurative use from the 19th century.
