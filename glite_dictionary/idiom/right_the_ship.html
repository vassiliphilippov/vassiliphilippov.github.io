to right the ship

1. VERB

/ raɪt ðə ʃɪp / (AmE), / raɪt ðə ʃɪp / (BrE)
to correct a problematic situation or organization; to restore order or proper functioning
restore order; fix a problematic situation

Examples

The company was struggling, but the CEO stepped in to right the ship.
After a series of setbacks, the coach managed to right the ship and lead the team to victory.
It took strong leadership and careful planning to right the ship after the crisis.

Synset Examples

The new manager was brought in to right the ship.
We need to right the ship before it's too late.
It took time, but they managed to right the ship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to steady the ship - to bring stability to a situation or organization experiencing trouble

Etymology

From the maritime practice of returning a capsized or listing ship to an upright position; figuratively extended to troubled organizations or situations.
