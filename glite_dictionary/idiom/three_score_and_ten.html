three score and ten

1. NOUN

/ θri ˈskɔr ənd tɛn / (AmE), / θri ˈskɔːr ənd tɛn / (BrE)
seventy years; specifically used to refer to a lifespan of seventy years
seventy years (age, lifespan)
archaic

Examples

Her grandmother lived to the age of three score and ten before passing peacefully.
He often quoted the saying that man's years are three score and ten in his speeches.
Upon reaching three score and ten, she decided to travel the world.

Synset Examples

She lived a full life, reaching three score and ten.
He celebrated his three score and ten birthday with family.
Most people consider three score and ten a ripe old age.

Etymology

From archaic counting system where 'score' means twenty; found in the Bible (Psalm 90:10).
