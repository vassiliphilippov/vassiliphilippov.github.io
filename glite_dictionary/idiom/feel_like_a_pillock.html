to feel like a pillock

1. VERB

/ fil laɪk ə ˈpɪlək / (AmE), / fiːl laɪk ə ˈpɪlək / (BrE)
to feel foolish or stupid, often as a result of one's own actions
feel foolish or stupid
informal british

Examples

After tripping in front of everyone at the meeting, he felt like a pillock for the rest of the day.
She felt like a pillock when she realized she’d been talking to the wrong person the entire time.
Forgetting his mother’s birthday made him feel like a pillock in front of his family.

Synset Examples

He felt like a pillock after forgetting his wallet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feel like an idiot - to feel foolish or embarrassed by one's own actions

Etymology

From British slang 'pillock' (fool), of uncertain origin, possibly from Norwegian 'pullok' (fool).
