to jump out of one's skin

1. VERB

/ dʒʌmp aʊt əv wʌnz skɪn / (AmE), / dʒʌmp aʊt ɒv wʌnz skɪn / (BrE)
to be extremely startled or surprised, often suddenly; to react with a strong jolt when frightened
to be extremely startled or surprised

Examples

The loud bang made me jump in my skin and spill my coffee.
She tends to jump in her skin whenever the phone rings unexpectedly.
When the fire alarm went off, everyone seemed to jump in their skin.

Synset Examples

The cat's sudden movement made me jump in my skin.
He always jumps in his skin at unexpected surprises.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to startle - to cause someone to feel sudden shock or surprise
  • Synonym: flinch - to make a quick, nervous movement as a reaction to fear or surprise
