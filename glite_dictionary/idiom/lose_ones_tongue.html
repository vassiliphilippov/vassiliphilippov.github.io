to lose one's tongue

1. VERB

/ luːz wʌnz tʌŋ / (AmE), / luːz wʌnz tʌŋ / (BrE)
Become suddenly unable to speak or express oneself, typically due to nervousness, fear, or embarrassment.
become unable to speak; struck silent

Examples

Whenever he faces the camera, he seems to lose his tongue and can't say a word.
Don't lose your tongue when it's your turn to speak to the manager.
She completely lost her tongue during the interview and forgot what she wanted to say.

Synset Examples

I often lose my tongue when speaking in public.
During the presentation, I lost my tongue completely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be tongue-tied - unable to speak due to nervousness or embarrassment

2. VERB

/ luːz wʌnz tʌŋ / (AmE), / luːz wʌnz tʌŋ / (BrE)
to become suddenly unable to speak, typically as a result of shock, surprise, or embarrassment
become unable to speak

Examples

He was so nervous during the interview that he lost his tongue for several seconds.
When asked about the accident, she seemed to lose her tongue and couldn't reply.
Don't tell me you've lost your tongue—say something!

Synset Examples

He was so surprised that he lost his tongue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be speechless - temporarily unable to speak due to shock or surprise
