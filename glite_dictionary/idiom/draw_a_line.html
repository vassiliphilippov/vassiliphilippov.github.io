to draw a line

1. VERB

/ drɔ ə laɪn / (AmE), / drɔː ə laɪn / (BrE)
To set a limit beyond which one will not go or tolerate; to establish a boundary for acceptable behavior or actions.
set a limit or boundary

Examples

When it comes to personal privacy, I draw a line that nobody is allowed to cross.
It's important for teachers to draw a line between being friendly and being too informal with students.
She had to draw a line somewhere, so she refused to work overtime on weekends.

Synset Examples

I draw the line when it comes to lending money to friends!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to draw the line - set a limit on what one will accept or allow
