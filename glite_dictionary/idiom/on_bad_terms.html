on bad terms

1. ADV

/ ɑn bæd tɜrmz / (AmE), / ɒn bæd tɜːmz / (BrE)
not having a friendly relationship; in a state of disagreement or hostility with someone
in a state of hostility or disagreement

Examples

After the argument, they were on bad terms for weeks.
She doesn’t want to be on bad terms with her colleagues.
It’s unfortunate that the two brothers are still on bad terms after all these years.

Synset Examples

The neighbors have been on bad terms since last summer.
After the fallout, they remained on bad terms for years.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: on good terms - having a friendly relationship; being amicable
