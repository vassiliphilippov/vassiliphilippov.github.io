to walk and chew gum at the same time

1. VERB

/ wɔk ənd tʃu ɡʌm æt ðə seɪm taɪm / (AmE), / wɔːk ənd tʃuː ɡʌm æt ðə seɪm taɪm / (BrE)
to be able to do two simple things simultaneously; used to suggest basic competence at multitasking
manage two simple tasks simultaneously
informal

Examples

People joked that the new intern couldn’t even walk and chew gum at the same time.
Despite the distractions, she can easily walk and chew gum at the same time.
Critics say he can't walk and chew gum at the same time, but he handled both issues smoothly.

Synset Examples

She proved she can walk and chew gum at the same time under pressure.
Sometimes I wonder if he can walk and chew gum at the same time.
He can't even walk and chew gum at the same time!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multitask - to perform more than one task at the same time
