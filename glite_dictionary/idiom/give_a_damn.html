to give a damn

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ə dæm / (AmE), / ɡɪv ə dam / (BrE)
to care at all about someone or something; to show any interest or concern (usually in the negative)
to care at all; to be concerned (usu. negative)
informal

Examples

He doesn't give a damn about what other people think of him.
I honestly don't give a damn if we arrive late tonight.
She acts like she gives a damn, but really she doesn't care at all.

Synset Examples

I don't give a hoot
She doesn't give a damn about her job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give a hoot - to care at all (usually in the negative)
  • Synonym: care - to feel concern or interest

2. VERB

/ ɡɪv ə dæm / (AmE), / ɡɪv ə dæm / (BrE)
to care at all about something or someone, usually used in negative statements to express indifference
care about something, usually in negatives
informal

Examples

She doesn't give a damn what people think about her unusual style.
If you give a damn about your grades, you should study harder for the exam.
I honestly don't give a damn whether we go out or stay home tonight.

Synset Examples

I don't give a damn about the rumors anymore.
Why should I give a damn about their opinions?
He clearly doesn’t give a damn about his responsibilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: care - to feel concern or interest
  • Synonym: to give a hoot - to care about something (informal, similar meaning)
