all bedlam broke loose

1. X

/ ɔl ˈbɛd.ləm broʊk lus / (AmE), / ɔːl ˈbɛd.ləm brəʊk luːs / (BrE)
used to describe a situation in which chaos or wild disorder suddenly erupts
chaos or disorder erupted

Examples

As soon as the substitute teacher turned her back, all bedlam broke loose in the classroom.
When the doors to the sale opened, all bedlam broke loose as shoppers rushed inside.
Moments after the referee blew the whistle, all bedlam broke loose on the football field.

Synset Examples

As soon as the concert started, all bedlam broke loose.
The kids went wild, and then all bedlam broke loose.
When the fire alarm rang, all bedlam broke loose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: all hell broke loose - complete chaos or disorder erupted

Etymology

Bedlam derives from 'Bethlehem', the name of a famous London mental hospital, and became a word for chaos or madness.
