to miss the memo

1. VERB

/ mɪs ðə ˈmɛmoʊ / (AmE), / mɪs ðə ˈmeməʊ / (BrE)
to be unaware of important information, especially because one was not informed or failed to notice something significant
be unaware of key information

Examples

Several employees missed the memo about the mandatory training session and showed up late.
If you miss the memo on company policy changes, you might make mistakes.
She acted like she missed the memo when everyone else dressed up for the party.

Synset Examples

Did you miss the memo regarding the team meeting?
He seemed to miss the memo on the new policy.
I must have missed the memo about the schedule change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be out of the loop - to not be informed or not be part of a group receiving information
  • Antonym: in the know - to be well-informed about something
