hell's half acre

1. NOUN

/ hɛlz hæf ˈeɪkɚ / (AmE), / hɛlz hɑːf ˈeɪkə / (BrE)
an extensive area, often chaotic, disorderly, or difficult to search or manage
large, chaotic or dispersed area

Examples

The missing package could have been delivered anywhere on hell's half acre.
After the party, the house looked like hell's half acre.
She chased the dog all over hell's half acre before she finally caught it.

Synset Examples

The kids are scattered all over hell's half acre.
She searched all over hell's half acre for her lost dog.

Etymology

From the use of 'hell' as a metaphor for chaos or confusion, combined with 'half acre' (a unit of land), forming a vivid idiom.
