fire in the furnace

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪər ɪn ðə ˈfɝːnɪs / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə ɪn ðə ˈfɜːnɪs / (BrE)
continued vitality or passion, especially despite age; strong energy or desire that remains even as one gets older
vitality or passion despite age

Examples

Even though he's in his eighties, there's still fire in the furnace when he talks about his work.
She impressed everyone by showing there was plenty of fire in the furnace during her comeback performance.
People joked that there may be snow on the rooftop, but there was certainly fire in the furnace.

Synset Examples

At 70, he still runs marathons; there may be snow on the rooftop but there is fire in the furnace.
He still plays soccer every weekend; there may be snow on the rooftop but there is fire in the furnace.
