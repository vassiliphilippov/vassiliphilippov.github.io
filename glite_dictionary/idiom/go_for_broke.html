to go for broke

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ fəɹ broʊk / (AmE), / ɡəʊ fə brəʊk / (BrE)
to risk everything in a single effort to achieve success
risk everything in one attempt

Examples

Knowing they had little chance of winning, the team decided to go for broke in the final quarter.
She chose to go for broke and invest all her savings in her startup.
Sometimes, you have to go for broke if you really want to stand out.

Synset Examples

the cyclist went for broke at the end of the race

Related Terms

  • Synonym: risk it all - to put everything at stake
  • Antonym: to play it safe - to avoid risks or danger

Etymology

Popularized in American English during World War II, originally a gambling term meaning to wager everything.

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ fər broʊk / (AmE), / ɡəʊ fə brəʊk / (BrE)
to risk everything in order to achieve the best possible result, even if this means potentially losing everything
risk everything to succeed

Examples

In the final round, he chose to go for broke and risked everything on a single answer.
After years of hesitation, she decided to go for broke and start her own restaurant.
The team knew they had to go for broke if they wanted a chance at the championship.

Synset Examples

In the final round, he chose to go for broke.
She decided to go for broke and invest in her dream.
They went for broke with their new business idea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: risk it all - to take a great risk, risking everything for a possible gain
  • Antonym: to play it safe - to avoid risks and act cautiously

Etymology

Possibly from gambling slang and popularized in American English during World War II, especially among Hawaiian soldiers.
