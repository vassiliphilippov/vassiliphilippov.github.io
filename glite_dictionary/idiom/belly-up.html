belly-up

1. ADJ

/ ˌbɛli ˈʌp / (AmE), / ˌbɛli ˈʌp / (BrE)
(of a business or organization) having failed financially; bankrupt or out of business.
financially ruined; bankrupt (of businesses)
informal

Examples

After years of declining sales, the company finally went belly-up last fall.
Many small restaurants went belly-up during the economic downturn.
If the investment goes belly-up, we could lose everything.

Synset Examples

a bankrupt company
the company went belly-up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bankrupt - unable to pay debts; financially ruined
  • Synonym: insolvent - unable to pay debts; bankrupt

Etymology

Alludes to dead fish floating with their bellies upward, signifying failure or ruin.
