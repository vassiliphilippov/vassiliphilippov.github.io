to settle someone's hash

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛt̬l ˈsʌmwʌnz hæʃ / (AmE), / ˈsɛt.əl ˈsʌmwʌnz hæʃ / (BrE)
to deal with someone decisively, often by punishing, defeating, or putting an end to their actions
deal with or punish someone decisively

Examples

If you don't stop causing trouble, I'll settle your hash for good.
She warned him that someone would settle his hash if he kept lying to his friends.
The coach finally settled their hash after months of quarrelling on the team.

Synset Examples

If he keeps lying, I will settle his hash.
He threatened to settle someone's hash if they crossed him again.
The boss decided to settle their hash once and for all.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deal with - to take action to solve a problem or manage a person
  • Synonym: have it out (with someone) - to resolve a dispute by confrontation

Etymology

Originally 19th-century American English; 'hash' in slang meaning a situation or person to be dealt with.
