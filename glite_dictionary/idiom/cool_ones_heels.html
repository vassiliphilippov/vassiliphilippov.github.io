to cool one's heels

1. VERB

/ kul wʌnz hilz / (AmE), / kuːl wʌnz hiːlz / (BrE)
to be kept waiting, often for a long time or impatiently
be kept waiting (idiom)

Examples

We were left to cool our heels in the lobby for nearly an hour.
The applicants cooled their heels outside the interview room, growing increasingly impatient.
She found herself cooling her heels while the manager finished a long phone call.

Synset Examples

She kicked her heels for hours at the gate of the Embassy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to kick one's heels - to wait or spend time aimlessly, often while being made to wait
