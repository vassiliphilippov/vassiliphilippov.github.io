on the brink of

1. ADP

/ ɑn ðə brɪŋk əv / (AmE), / ɒn ðə brɪŋk ɒv / (BrE)
very close to experiencing or achieving something, usually a significant or decisive event, often involving risk or change
very close to a major event or change

Examples

After years of research, the scientists were on the brink of a breakthrough.
Many feared the country was on the brink of war.
She felt she was on the brink of giving up, but decided to keep going.

Synset Examples

The team was on the brink of victory last night.
They stood on the brink of a major discovery.
The company was on the precipice of bankruptcy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on the verge of - very close to doing or experiencing something
  • Synonym: on the edge of - almost in a particular state or situation
