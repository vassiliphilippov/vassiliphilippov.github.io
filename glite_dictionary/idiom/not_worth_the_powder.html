not worth the powder

1. ADJ

/ nɑt wɝθ ðə ˈpaʊdɚ / (AmE), / nɒt wəːθ ðə ˈpaʊdə / (BrE)
not worth the effort, trouble, or resources expended
not worth the effort or trouble

Examples

Their disagreement is not worth the powder and should be ignored.
Fixing that old chair is not worth the powder—it's better to get a new one.
The small amount owed is not worth the powder to pursue in court.

Synset Examples

Their disagreement is not worth the powder, really.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: not worth it - not deserving the effort or cost involved
  • Antonym: worthwhile - worth the time, money, or effort spent

Etymology

Refers to gunpowder, meaning not worth the cost of firing a shot; dates from 17th-century English usage.
