rolling in it

1. ADJ

/ ˈroʊlɪŋ ɪn ɪt / (AmE), / ˈrəʊlɪŋ ɪn ɪt / (BrE)
having a lot of money; extremely wealthy
very wealthy; having a lot of money
informal

Examples

After winning the lottery, she was suddenly rolling in it and could buy anything she wanted.
Despite what he says, you can tell from his expensive car that he's rolling in it.
They must be rolling in it to afford a house like that in the city.

Synset Examples

With all that overtime, he's really rolling in it.
After the big sale, she was rolling in it.
He's been rolling in it ever since he got promoted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loaded - very rich; having a lot of money
  • Antonym: broke - having no money; financially destitute
