by a nose

1. ADV

/ baɪ ə noʊz / (AmE), / baɪ ə nəʊz / (BrE)
by a very small margin; used to describe winning or succeeding with only the slightest lead over others.
winning or succeeding by a narrow margin

Examples

The underdog team clinched victory by a nose in the final seconds of the game.
She finished the race by a nose, just ahead of her closest competitor.
Our proposal was approved by a nose, with only one more vote than the runner-up.

Synset Examples

The horse won by a nose in the final stretch.
The gee-gee won the race by a nose.
The team won by a nose in a thrilling match.
She managed to win by a nose in the election.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: by a hair - by a very small margin
  • Synonym: by a whisker - by an extremely small amount

Etymology

From horse racing, where a horse may win by the length of its nose.
