to overstep the mark

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvɚˈstɛp ðə mɑrk / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈstɛp ðə mɑːk / (BrE)
to go beyond what is considered acceptable or appropriate in a particular situation
go beyond acceptable limits

Examples

If you criticize your boss in public, you might overstep the mark and face consequences.
The comedian apologized after realizing his jokes had overstepped the mark for some audience members.
She urged her team to be bold but not to overstep the mark in negotiations.

Synset Examples

She was careful not to overstep the mark with her suggestions.
His comments really overstepped the mark during the meeting.
They felt he overstepped the mark with his criticism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go too far - to exceed a reasonable or acceptable limit
  • Synonym: to cross the line - to do something unacceptable or improper
