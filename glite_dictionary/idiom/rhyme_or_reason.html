rhyme or reason

1. NOUN

/ raɪm ɔr ˈriːzən / (AmE), / raɪm ɔː ˈriːzn̩ / (BrE)
used to emphasize that something has no logical explanation or clear rationale
no logical explanation or rationale

Examples

There was no rhyme or reason to the way the items were arranged.
He spends money without any rhyme or reason.
Some decisions in this company seem to lack rhyme or reason.

Synset Examples

There’s no rhyme or reason to his strange behavior.
She tried to find rhyme or reason in the chaotic schedule.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: logic - reasoning conducted according to strict principles

Etymology

From Middle English 'rime' (verse) and 'reason' (logical thinking); the pairing dates to at least the 15th century in English.
