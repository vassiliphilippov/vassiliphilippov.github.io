to make out like a bandit

1. VERB

/ meɪk aʊt laɪk ə ˈbændɪt / (AmE), / meɪk aʊt laɪk ə ˈbændɪt / (BrE)
to gain or profit significantly, especially in an unexpected or advantageous way
profit or gain greatly (idiom)
informal

Examples

After the lucky investment, she made out like a bandit and retired early.
With those discounts, he really made out like a bandit at the store.
They make out like bandits every year during the sales.

Synset Examples

After the deal, she felt she made out like a bandit.
He made out like a bandit selling old electronics.
They made out like bandits at the garage sale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cash in - to take advantage or profit from a situation
  • Synonym: strike it rich - to suddenly become very wealthy

Etymology

From the word 'bandit', meaning a robber, suggesting gaining as much as a bandit might through theft.
