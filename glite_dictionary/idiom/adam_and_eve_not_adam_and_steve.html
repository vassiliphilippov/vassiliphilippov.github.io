Adam and Eve, not Adam and Steve

1. X

/ ˈædəm ənd ˈiv nɑt ˈædəm ənd ˈstiv / (AmE), / ˈædəm ənd ˈiːv nɒt ˈædəm ənd ˈstiːv / (BrE)
(often disapproving) A slogan used to assert that heterosexual relationships are the only natural or acceptable kind, typically in opposition to same-sex relationships or marriage.
expression opposing same-sex relationships
offensive

Examples

The politician repeated Adam and Eve, not Adam and Steve during the heated debate on marriage laws.
Some protest signs read Adam and Eve, not Adam and Steve, sparking controversy at the event.
He used the phrase Adam and Eve, not Adam and Steve to argue against changes in traditional marriage legislation.

Synset Examples

The debate sparked discussions about Adam and Eve not Adam and Steve.

The phrase Adam and Eve not Adam and Steve often arises in discussions.

Etymology

Plays on the biblical couple 'Adam and Eve' contrasted with a rhyming invented pair; popularized in late 20th century debates.
