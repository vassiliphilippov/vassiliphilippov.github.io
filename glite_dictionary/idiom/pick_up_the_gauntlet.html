to pick up the gauntlet

1. VERB

/ pɪk ʌp ðə ˈɡɔːn(t)lət / (AmE), / pɪk ʌp ðə ˈɡɔːntlət / (BrE)
to accept a challenge or dare and attempt to meet it
accept a challenge

Examples

When the critics doubted her abilities, she decided to pick up the gauntlet and prove them wrong.
No one expected him to pick up the gauntlet, but he accepted the risky assignment.
Challenged by his opponents, the mayor was quick to pick up the gauntlet and defend his policies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to throw down the gauntlet - to issue a challenge to someone

Etymology

From the medieval custom of throwing down a gauntlet (an armored glove) to issue a challenge; picking it up signified acceptance.
