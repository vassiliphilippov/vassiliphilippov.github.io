to feel like a louse

1. VERB

/ fil laɪk ə laʊs / (AmE), / fiːl laɪk ə laʊs / (BrE)
to feel guilty or ashamed, especially after behaving badly toward someone
to feel guilty or ashamed

Examples

After forgetting his anniversary, he felt like a louse for days.
She felt like a louse after lying to her friend about the incident.
Whenever I disappoint my team, I feel like a louse and want to make things right.

Synset Examples

He felt like a louse for forgetting her birthday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feel guilty - to experience a sense of guilt
  • Antonym: feel proud - to experience a sense of pride

Etymology

From 'louse', a contemptible or unpleasant person, used derogatorily since the mid-20th century.
