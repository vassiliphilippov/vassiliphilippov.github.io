to live and breathe

1. VERB

/ lɪv ənd brið / (AmE), / lɪv ənd briːð / (BrE)
to be extremely enthusiastic and passionate about something, often making it a central part of one's life
be very passionate about something

Examples

She lives and breathes classical music, spending hours practicing every day.
You can tell he lives and breathes football by the way he talks about it.
The chef lived and breathed his restaurant, rarely taking a day off.

Synset Examples

Being a Narutard means you live and breathe the series.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be obsessed with - to have an excessive preoccupation with something
  • Antonym: be indifferent to - to lack interest or passion for something
