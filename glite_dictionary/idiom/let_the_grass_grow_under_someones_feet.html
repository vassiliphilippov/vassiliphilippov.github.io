to let the grass grow under someone's feet

1. VERB

/ lɛt ðə ɡræs ɡroʊ ˈʌndɚ ˈsʌmwʌnz fit / (AmE), / lɛt ðə ɡrɑːs ɡrəʊ ˈʌndə ˈsʌmwʌnz fiːt / (BrE)
to delay taking action or to be slow in responding to opportunities
delay action; be slow to act

Examples

She refuses to let the grass grow under her feet when it comes to advancing her career.
If you let the grass grow under your feet, someone else will take the opportunity instead.
Successful people never let the grass grow under their feet; they move quickly and decisively.

Synset Examples

She never lets the grass grow under her feet when opportunities arise.
If you wait too long, you’ll let the grass grow under your feet.
Don't let the grass grow under your feet; take action now.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to procrastinate - delay taking action
  • Antonym: act promptly - to take action without delay
