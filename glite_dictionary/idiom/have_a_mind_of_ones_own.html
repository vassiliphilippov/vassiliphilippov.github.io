to have a mind of one's own

1. VERB

/ hæv ə maɪnd əv wʌnz oʊn / (AmE), / hæv ə maɪnd əv wʌnz əʊn / (BrE)
to think independently and form one's own opinions or decisions, rather than being influenced by others
think and decide independently

Examples

Even as a child, Lucy always seemed to have a mind of her own and challenged the rules.
The robot was so advanced it appeared to have a mind of its own.
It's important for leaders to have a mind of their own rather than just following the crowd.

Synset Examples

My daughter has a mind of her own about her career.
He may be young, but he has a mind of his own.
Teachers appreciate students who have a mind of their own.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: independent - not influenced or controlled by others
