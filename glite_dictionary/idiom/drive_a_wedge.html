to drive a wedge

1. VERB

/ draɪv ə wɛdʒ / (AmE), / draɪv ə wɛdʒ / (BrE)
to cause disagreement or division between people, often intentionally
cause division or conflict

Examples

He tried to drive a wedge between me and my best friend with his lies.
Political opponents are attempting to drive a wedge between different communities.
The manager's harsh criticism drove a wedge between the team members.

Synset Examples

Don't let misunderstandings drive a wedge in your relationship.
The new policy might drive a wedge between management and employees.
His comments drove a wedge between the two friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divide - to separate into parts or groups, especially causing opposition
  • Antonym: to unite - to bring together as one group or whole
