to come a cropper

1. VERB

/ ˈkʌm ə ˈkrɑːpər / (AmE), / ˈkʌm ə ˈkrɒpə / (BrE)
to fail badly or suffer a sudden mishap, often unexpectedly
fail or suffer a sudden mishap
informal british

Examples

Several new companies came a cropper during the economic downturn.
Janet came a cropper when she tried skiing for the first time.
He worked hard but still came a cropper in the final exam.

Synset Examples

He came a cropper in the finals yesterday.
The project came a cropper due to poor planning.
She always feared she’d come a cropper at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fail - to be unsuccessful in achieving a goal

Etymology

Originally British slang, possibly from horse-riding accidents in the 19th century.
