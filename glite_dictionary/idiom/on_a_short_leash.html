on a short leash

1. X

/ ɑn ə ʃɔrt liʃ / (AmE), / ɒn ə ʃɔːt liːʃ / (BrE)
under strict control or supervision; having limited freedom or autonomy
under strict control; limited freedom

Examples

The new employee was kept on a short leash until he proved he could be trusted.
After the scandal, the politician found himself on a short leash with the media.
Many parents keep their teenagers on a short leash during exam season.

Synset Examples

He kept the puppy on a short leash during training.
He felt he was on a short leash with his boss.
Parents often keep their kids on a short leash.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: under someone's thumb - completely controlled or dominated by someone
  • Antonym: give someone free rein - allow someone complete freedom to act
