devil's luck

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛvəlz lʌk / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəlz lʌk / (BrE)
unexpectedly good fortune or extremely good luck, especially when it seems undeserved or improbable
unexpectedly extreme good fortune

Examples

He always seems to have devil's luck when it comes to risky investments.
With sheer devil's luck, she missed the storm by just a few minutes.
Somehow, by devil's luck, the team managed to score in the final seconds.

Synset Examples

She managed to escape the accident with devil's luck.
With devil's luck, he won the lottery twice.
He found a $100 bill on the ground; pure devil's luck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: good luck - fortune or success, especially that which comes by chance
  • Antonym: bad luck - misfortune; unfortunate events or outcomes

Etymology

Alludes to the idea that the devil sometimes brings undeserved good fortune; in use since at least the 17th century.
