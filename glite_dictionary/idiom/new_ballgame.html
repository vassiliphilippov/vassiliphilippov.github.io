new ballgame

1. NOUN

/ nu ˈbɔlˌɡeɪm / (AmE), / njuː ˈbɔːlˌɡeɪm / (BrE)
a situation that is completely different from the one before, especially requiring a different approach or understanding
a radically changed situation

Examples

After the merger, the industry became a new ballgame for all its competitors.
Remote work is a new ballgame for many traditional companies.
When technology shifts, adapting to the new ballgame is essential for survival.

Synset Examples

HDTV looks the same but it's really a whole new ballgame

Related Terms

  • Synonym: game changer - an event or idea that changes the situation significantly

Etymology

From American English, drawing an analogy to restarting a sporting match with new conditions.
