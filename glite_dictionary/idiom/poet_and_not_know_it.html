poet and not know it

1. X

/ ˈpoʊ.ɪt ænd nɑt ˈnoʊ ɪt / (AmE), / ˈpəʊ.ɪt ənd nɒt ˈnəʊ ɪt / (BrE)
used humorously to indicate that someone has unwittingly said or written something poetic, usually in rhyme or rhythm, without realizing it
humorous phrase for unwitting poetic skill

Examples

You made that rhyme without trying—you’re a poet and not know it!
He ended his speech with a couplet and we all laughed, saying he’s a poet and not know it.
She wrote a catchy verse for the party invitation; she’s truly a poet and not know it.

Synset Examples

Her spontaneous lyrics suggest she could be a poet and not know it.
After reciting his lines, we joked he might be a poet and not know it.
His rhymes were clever; he must be a poet and not know it.
