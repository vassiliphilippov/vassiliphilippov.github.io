to come to life

1. VERB

/ kʌm tə laɪf / (AmE), / kʌm tə laɪf / (BrE)
to become lively, animated, or full of energy; to appear vivid or real
become lively or animated

Examples

The old town came to life during the festival, buzzing with activity and laughter.
As the sun rose, the forest came to life with the sound of birds singing.
When the actors started performing, the story truly came to life on stage.

Synset Examples

The party really came to life once the music started.
Her artwork seemed to come to life under the spotlight.
The story will come to life on stage next month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spring to life - to suddenly become energetic or active
