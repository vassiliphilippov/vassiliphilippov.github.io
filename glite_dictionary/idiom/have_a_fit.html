to have a fit

1. VERB

/ hæv ə fɪt / (AmE), / hæv ə fɪt / (BrE)
to become very angry or upset suddenly and visibly
become very angry or upset

Examples

My dad will have a fit if he finds out I broke his laptop.
She nearly had a fit when she saw the price of the dress.
Try not to have a fit over such a small mistake.

Synset Examples

Don't have a fit; it's just a minor issue.
He'll have a fit if he misses the train.
She had a fit when she saw the mess.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lose one's temper - to suddenly become angry
  • Antonym: remain calm - to stay calm and not become angry
