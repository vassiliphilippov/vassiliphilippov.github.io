to put one's foot in someone's ass

1. VERB

/ pʊt wʌnz fʊt ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz æs / (AmE), / pʊt wʌnz fʊt ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz ɑːs / (BrE)
to criticize or motivate someone very harshly or forcefully, often to prompt improvement or action
harshly motivate or criticize someone
informal vulgar

Examples

My father threatened to put his foot in my ass if I kept slacking off in school.
Sometimes a coach needs to put his foot in someone's ass to get results.
After the poor performance, the manager put his foot in my ass during the meeting.

Synset Examples

My coach really put his foot in my ass to improve my game.
The boss put his foot in my ass for missing deadlines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chew someone out - scold or criticize someone forcefully
  • Synonym: kick someone into gear - to motivate someone strongly to take action
  • Antonym: let someone off the hook - to not punish or criticize someone

2. VERB

/ pʊt wʌnz fʊt ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz æs / (AmE), / pʊt wʌnz fʊt ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz ɑːs / (BrE)
(slang, vulgar) To reprimand or physically punish someone severely, often as a result of their misbehavior or incompetence.
reprimand or punish severely (slang, vulgar)
slang vulgar

Examples

If you miss another deadline, the boss is going to put his foot in your ass.
The coach threatened to put his foot in their ass if they didn't start playing better.
After repeated warnings, she finally put her foot in his ass and he straightened up.

Synset Examples

He put his foot in my ass after I slacked off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chew someone out - to angrily reprimand someone
  • Antonym: let someone off - to not punish or reprimand as expected
