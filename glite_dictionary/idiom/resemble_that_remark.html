resemble that remark

1. X

/ rɪˈzɛm.bəl ðæt rɪˈmɑrk / (AmE), / rɪˈzɛm.bəl ðæt rɪˈmɑːk / (BrE)
used humorously to indicate that a comment or insult applies to oneself, often as a playful or self-deprecating reply
humorous reply agreeing a comment applies to oneself
informal

Examples

When her brother joked about procrastinators, she grinned and said, 'resemble that remark.'
After being teased for always being late, he replied, 'resemble that remark!'
Hearing the joke about chocolate lovers, I couldn't help but say, 'I resemble that remark.'

Synset Examples

After hearing the critique, she laughed, saying, 'I resemble that remark!'
When he called me lazy, I replied, 'I resemble that remark!'
