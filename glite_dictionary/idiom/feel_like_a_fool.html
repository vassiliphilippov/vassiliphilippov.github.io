to feel like a fool

1. VERB

/ fil laɪk ə ful / (AmE), / fiːl laɪk ə fuːl / (BrE)
to feel embarrassed, awkward, or foolish, especially as a result of one's own actions or mistakes
to feel embarrassed or foolish

Examples

After tripping in front of everyone at the meeting, he couldn't help but feel like a fool.
She always feels like a fool when she forgets someone's name.
They felt like a fool when they realized they had been tricked by the obvious prank.

Synset Examples

She felt like a fool for trusting him.
She felt like an idiot for forgetting the meeting.
He felt like a moron for forgetting the meeting.
She felt like an imbecile after making that mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feel embarrassed - to experience a feeling of shame or awkwardness
