asking for a friend

1. X

/ ˈæskɪŋ fɔɹ ə frɛnd / (AmE), / ˈɑːskɪŋ fə ə frɛnd / (BrE)
(humorous) used to suggest that a question is being asked on behalf of someone else, when in fact it is for oneself.
humorous way to imply a personal question
informal

Examples

Is it weird to have pizza for breakfast, or am I just asking for a friend?
What’s the maximum number of cats one should own—just asking for a friend.
Do you know any tips for getting wine out of carpet? Asking for a friend.

Synset Examples

Do you think he likes me, or just asking for a friend?
Are you dating anyone, or just asking for a friend?
Is this restaurant any good, or just asking for a friend?
