without rhyme or reason

1. ADV

/ wɪðˈaʊt raɪm ɔr ˈriːzən / (AmE), / wɪðˈaʊt raɪm ɔː ˈriːzən / (BrE)
with no logical or obvious explanation; in a random or irrational manner
with no logical explanation

Examples

The rules seemed to change without rhyme or reason every day.
Sometimes people behave without rhyme or reason, leaving others confused.
He threw away the papers without rhyme or reason, making it hard to find anything later.

Synset Examples

Without rhyme or reason, he decided to quit his job.

Etymology

From the literary use of 'rhyme' and 'reason' to mean order and logic, dating back to Middle English and found in works by Chaucer and Shakespeare.
