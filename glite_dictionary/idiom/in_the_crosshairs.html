in the crosshairs

1. X

/ ɪn ðə ˈkrɔsˌhɛrz / (AmE), / ɪn ðə ˈkrɒsˌheəz / (BrE)
under scrutiny or attack; being the focus of criticism or targeting
target of scrutiny or criticism

Examples

The company found itself in the crosshairs after the environmental scandal broke.
After making the controversial statement, the actor was in the crosshairs of social media critics.
With budget cuts looming, many public programs are in the crosshairs for elimination.

Synset Examples

With the new regulations, companies are now in the crosshairs of regulators.
The politician was clearly in the crosshairs of the media this week.
He felt like he was in the crosshairs of his boss after missing the deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: under fire - being criticized or attacked
  • Synonym: in the spotlight - being the center of attention, especially scrutiny

Etymology

From the crosshairs found in firearm or optical sights, symbolizing being directly targeted.
