to change my mind

1. VERB

/ tʃeɪndʒ maɪ maɪnd / (AmE), / tʃeɪndʒ maɪ maɪnd / (BrE)
to alter one's decision or opinion after previously deciding something
alter one's opinion or decision

Examples

I was planning to go out, but I changed my mind at the last minute.
You can't change my mind about this, no matter what you say.
If I change my mind later, I'll let you know.

Synset Examples

She managed to change my mind about the project.
What of it if I change my mind later?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reconsider - to think again about a decision or opinion
  • Antonym: stick to my guns - to refuse to change one's opinion or decision
