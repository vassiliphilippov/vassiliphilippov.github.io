to take the veil

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə veɪl / (AmE), / teɪk ðə veɪl / (BrE)
to become a nun by entering a religious order
become a nun

Examples

After years of uncertainty, she decided to take the veil and devote her life to service.
Many women in the past would take the veil after experiencing personal loss or seeking spiritual fulfillment.
She surprised her family when she announced she would take the veil at the local convent.

Synset Examples

The young woman took the veil after her fiance died

Related Terms

  • Synonym: become a nun - to join a convent as a nun

Etymology

From the custom of new nuns adopting a veil as part of their religious habit.

2. VERB

/ teɪk ðə veɪl / (AmE), / teɪk ðə veɪl / (BrE)
to become a nun; to enter a religious order as a woman
become a nun; enter religious life

Examples

After years of contemplation, she decided to take the veil and devote her life to service.
Many women in her family had chosen to take the veil and join the convent over the centuries.
During the solemn ceremony, the young woman prepared to take the veil before the gathered congregation.

Synset Examples

She chose to take the veil and embrace her faith.
They will take the veil during the ceremony tomorrow.
He was surprised when she decided to take the veil.

Etymology

Refers to the ceremonial act of donning a veil, symbolizing entrance into a monastic order.
