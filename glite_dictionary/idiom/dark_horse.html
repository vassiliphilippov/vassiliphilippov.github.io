dark horse

1. NOUN

/ dɑrk hɔrs / (AmE), / dɑːk hɔːs / (BrE)
a candidate or competitor about whom little is known but who unexpectedly wins or succeeds
an unexpected winner or unknown competitor

Examples

She was considered a dark horse in the election but ended up winning by a large margin.
The team's dark horse status made their victory in the tournament all the more surprising.
He's the dark horse of the competition—nobody expected him to get this far.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: underdog - a competitor thought to have little chance of winning

Etymology

Originally from horse racing, where an unknown horse could unexpectedly win a race.

2. NOUN

/ dɑrk hɔrs / (AmE), / dɑːk hɔːs / (BrE)
a person or team whose abilities, plans, or likelihood of success are unknown or unexpected, and who surprises others by succeeding
an unexpected or little-known winner

Examples

No one expected the dark horse candidate to win, but she claimed a surprising victory.
The musician was a dark horse in the competition, impressing the judges with his talent.
In the tournament, their team proved to be a dark horse, advancing further than anyone predicted.

Synset Examples

She was a dark horse who surprised everyone.
He was a dark horse in the election race.
The team’s dark horse player scored the winning goal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: underdog - a competitor thought to have little chance of winning

Etymology

Originally from horse racing, referring to a horse whose abilities are little known, and so surprise by winning.
