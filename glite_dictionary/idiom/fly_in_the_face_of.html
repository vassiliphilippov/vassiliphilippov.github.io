to fly in the face of

1. VERB

/ flaɪ ɪn ðə feɪs əv / (AmE), / flaɪ ɪn ðə feɪs ɒv / (BrE)
to openly oppose or act in direct contradiction to something, especially rules, tradition, or expectations.
openly contradict or oppose

Examples

His decision to ignore the advice flies in the face of common sense.
Introducing this policy flies in the face of years of tradition.
Their refusal to accept the offer fly in the face of our agreement.

Synset Examples

This action flies in the face of the agreement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to defy - to openly resist or refuse to obey
  • Antonym: to comply - to act in accordance with a wish or command

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ flaɪ ɪn ðə feɪs ʌv / (AmE), / flaɪ ɪn ðə feɪs əv / (BrE)
to openly oppose or disregard something, especially accepted rules, beliefs, or evidence
openly oppose or disregard

Examples

Choosing to ignore expert advice would fly in the face of reason.
Their new policy flies in the face of decades of scientific research.
Supporting the plan would fly in the face of public opinion.

Synset Examples

This decision flies in the face of common sense.
Her actions fly in the face of company policy.
Their claims fly in the face of the evidence presented.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to contradict - to assert the opposite of a statement or belief
  • Synonym: to defy - to openly resist or refuse to obey
