autumn years

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔtəm jɪrz / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəm jɪəz / (BrE)
the later period of a person's life, especially after retirement or the main part of one's career
later years of one's life
archaic

Examples

Many people choose to travel extensively during their autumn years.
He hoped to spend his autumn years in a quiet village by the sea.
The couple enjoyed gardening together throughout their autumn years.

Synset Examples

He spent his autumn years surrounded by family and friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden years - a period of life after retirement, often considered peaceful or prosperous

Etymology

From 'autumn,' metaphorically referring to the later stage of the year or life, following the metaphor of the seasons.
