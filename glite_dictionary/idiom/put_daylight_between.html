to put daylight between

1. VERB

/ pʊt ˈdeɪˌlaɪt bɪˈtwin / (AmE), / pʊt ˈdeɪˌlaɪt bɪˈtwiːn / (BrE)
to create distance or separation from someone or something, often to avoid involvement or reduce a connection
create distance or separation

Examples

After the argument, she decided to put daylight between herself and the group for a while.
He realized it was healthiest to put daylight between himself and his old habits.
Sometimes you need to put daylight between your work and your personal life to maintain balance.

Synset Examples

She chose to put daylight between herself and that toxic friend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distance oneself - deliberately become less involved or connected
  • Synonym: get away from - move away to achieve separation
