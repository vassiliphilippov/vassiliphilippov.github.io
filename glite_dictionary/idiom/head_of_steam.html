head of steam

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛd əv ˈstiːm / (AmE), / ˌhɛd əv ˈstiːm / (BrE)
a large amount of energy, enthusiasm, or drive, especially just before taking action or as motivation builds up
a lot of energy or enthusiasm

Examples

After a few cups of coffee, she built up a head of steam and finished the project quickly.
The campaign gained a head of steam as more volunteers joined the effort.
He came into the meeting with a head of steam, ready to tackle every issue on the agenda.

Synset Examples

He came into the meeting with a head of steam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: momentum - strength or energy that keeps increasing

Etymology

Originally from steam engines, where building a 'head of steam' referred to accumulating enough steam pressure to move the engine.
