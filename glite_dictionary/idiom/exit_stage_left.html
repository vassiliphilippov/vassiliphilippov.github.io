to exit stage left

1. VERB

/ ˈɛksɪt steɪdʒ lɛft / (AmE), / ˈɛksɪt steɪdʒ lɛft / (BrE)
to leave a situation quietly, discreetly, or unobtrusively, often to avoid attention or trouble
leave discreetly or quietly

Examples

When the meeting grew tense, Marcus decided to exit stage left rather than argue further.
Sensing his mistake, she tried to exit stage left before anyone noticed.
After the controversial announcement, the politician chose to exit stage left and avoid questions.

Synset Examples

After the awkward silence, she chose to exit stage left.
He made a quick exit stage left when things got tense.
He decided to exit stage left after the argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to slip away - to leave quietly or without attracting attention
  • Synonym: make an exit - to leave a place or situation, especially one that is uncomfortable

Etymology

From stage directions in theater, where an actor exits from the left side of the stage (from the audience's perspective).
