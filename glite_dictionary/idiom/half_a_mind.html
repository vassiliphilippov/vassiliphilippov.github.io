half a mind

1. NOUN

/ hæf ə maɪnd / (AmE), / hɑːf ə maɪnd / (BrE)
used to indicate that one is inclined or tempted to do something but is not fully committed to doing it
inclined or tempted to do something

Examples

I had half a mind to walk out of the meeting after hearing his remarks.
She has half a mind to quit her job and travel the world.
He had half a mind to confront them about what he saw.

Synset Examples

With half a mind, he decided to join the project.
She had half a mind to skip the meeting altogether.
I had half a mind to call her back.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inclined - having a tendency or disposition to do something
  • Antonym: reluctant - not willing or eager to do something
