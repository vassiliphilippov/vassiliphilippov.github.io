to gild the lily

1. VERB

/ ɡɪld ðə ˈlɪli / (AmE), / ɡɪld ðə ˈlɪli / (BrE)
to unnecessarily add ornament or embellishment to something that is already beautiful or perfect
unnecessarily embellish something already beautiful

Examples

Painting the already stunning mural felt like trying to gild the lily.
Adding diamonds to that elegant ring would just gild the lily.
When you rewrite that perfect sentence, you risk gilding the lily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embellish - to make something more attractive by adding decoration

Etymology

From a misquotation of Shakespeare's 'to gild refined gold, to paint the lily' (King John, 4:2).

2. VERB

/ ɡɪld ðə ˈlɪli / (AmE), / ɡɪld ðə ˈlɪli / (BrE)
to unnecessarily add decoration, embellishment, or improvement to something that is already beautiful or complete, thus risking excess or redundancy
to over-embellish something already perfect

Examples

To add more decorations to this room would be to gild the lily.
She decided not to gild the lily by editing the already perfect speech.
Sometimes, trying to gild the lily only makes things worse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overdo - to do something to an excessive degree
  • Synonym: to embellish - to make something more attractive by adding details or decorations

Etymology

From a misquotation of Shakespeare's 'to gild refined gold, to paint the lily' in King John (1595).

3. VERB

/ ɡɪld ðə ˈlɪli / (AmE), / ɡɪld ðə ˈlɪli / (BrE)
To add unnecessary ornaments or improvements to something that is already beautiful or perfect.
add unnecessary embellishment

Examples

There is no need to gild the lily; the design is already stunning.
She tried to gild the lily by adding extra details to her painting, but it only distracted from the original beauty.
Sometimes advertisers gild the lily when describing their products, making them seem more impressive than they are.

Synset Examples
