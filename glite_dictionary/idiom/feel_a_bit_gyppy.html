to feel a bit gyppy

1. VERB

/ fiːl ə bɪt ˈdʒɪpi / (AmE), / fiːl ə bɪt ˈdʒɪpi / (BrE)
to feel slightly unwell or queasy, especially in the stomach
to feel slightly unwell or nauseous
informal british

Examples

I felt a bit gyppy after eating the seafood at that restaurant.
If you feel a bit gyppy this morning, you should probably skip breakfast.
She started feeling a bit gyppy soon after the drive, perhaps from motion sickness.

Synset Examples

After the meal, I felt a bit gyppy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: queasy - feeling slightly sick or nauseated
  • Synonym: nauseous - feeling as if you might vomit

Etymology

Possibly from 'gyppy tummy', originally British slang referring to digestive upset experienced in Egypt ('Gyppy' from 'Egyptian').
