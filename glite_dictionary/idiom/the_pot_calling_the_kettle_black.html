the pot calling the kettle black

1. X

/ ðə pɑt ˈkɔlɪŋ ðə ˈkɛtəl blæk / (AmE), / ðə pɒt ˈkɒlɪŋ ðə ˈkɛtl blæk / (BrE)
used to accuse someone of hypocrisy, especially when they criticize another person for a fault they also have
accusing someone of hypocrisy

Examples

Accusing me of gossiping is a case of the pot calling the kettle black.
When politicians argue about honesty, it's often the pot calling the kettle black.
Saying I'm disorganized is just the pot calling the kettle black.

Synset Examples

He criticized her work ethic; it's the pot calling the kettle black.
It's the pot calling the kettle black, accusing me of lying.
When he complained about being late, it was the pot calling the kettle black.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypocrisy - practice of claiming to have moral standards to which one's own behavior does not conform

Etymology

Refers to old iron cookware that would blacken with soot, suggesting hypocrisy in criticizing someone's faults that one shares.
