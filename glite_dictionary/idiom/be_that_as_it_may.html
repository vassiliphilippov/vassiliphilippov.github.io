be that as it may

1. X

/ bi ðæt æz ɪt meɪ / (AmE), / bi ðat az ɪt meɪ / (BrE)
used to indicate that something may be true, but it does not affect what is being said or decided next; nevertheless.
nonetheless; regardless of what has just been mentioned

Examples

I'm not sure we have enough money; be that as it may, we need to finish the project.
You may not agree with my decision; be that as it may, I have made up my mind.
He apologized for being late; be that as it may, he missed the important part of the meeting.

Synset Examples

It's raining outside; be that as it may, we’re still going.
I disagree with your choice; be that as it may, it's yours.
He wasn't qualified; be that as it may, he got the job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nevertheless - in spite of what has just been said or referred to
  • Synonym: nonetheless - in spite of what has just been said; nevertheless
