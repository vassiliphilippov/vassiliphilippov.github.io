to cross the line

1. VERB

/ krɔs ðə laɪn / (AmE), / krɒs ðə laɪn / (BrE)
to behave in a way that is not considered acceptable or that goes beyond an established limit of acceptable conduct
go beyond what is acceptable

Examples

Many thought the comedian crossed the line with his controversial joke.
I don't mind criticism, but you crossed the line when you insulted my family.
Her comments about her boss clearly crossed the line and got her into trouble.

Synset Examples

She felt he crossed the line with that joke.
He crossed the line; that does it!
His comments were inappropriate and crossed the line.
The prank was funny until it crossed the line.
She felt he crossed the line with his rude remarks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go too far - to exceed acceptable limits, especially in behavior or remarks
  • Synonym: to overstep the mark - to go beyond what is acceptable or allowed
