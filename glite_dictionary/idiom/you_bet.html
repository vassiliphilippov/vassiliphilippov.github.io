you bet

1. ADV

/ ju ˈbɛt / (AmE), / juː ˈbɛt / (BrE)
used to express strong agreement or to affirm that something is certain or assured
expression of strong agreement
informal

Examples

"Are you coming to the party?" "You bet! I wouldn't miss it for the world."
If you need help moving, you bet I'll be there.
"Will it be finished by tomorrow?" "You bet, I'll have it done before noon."

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absolutely - used to emphasize complete agreement
  • Antonym: no way - expression indicating strong disagreement or refusal

2. INTJ

/ juː ˈbɛt / (AmE), / juː ˈbɛt / (BrE)
used to emphasize that something is certainly true or will definitely happen; of course.
certainly; of course (informal)
informal

Examples

"Are you coming to the party tonight?" "You bet! I wouldn’t miss it for the world."
If you ask if I enjoy skiing, you bet—I go every winter.
"Can I borrow your notes from class?" "You bet, I'll email them to you this afternoon."

Synset Examples

If you need help, you bet I'm here for you.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certainly - without doubt; surely
  • Antonym: no way - absolutely not, under no circumstances
