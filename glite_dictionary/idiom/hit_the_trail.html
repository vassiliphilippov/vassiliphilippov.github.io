to hit the trail

1. VERB

/ hɪt ðə treɪl / (AmE), / hɪt ðə treɪl / (BrE)
to begin a journey or start hiking, especially in the countryside or wilderness
begin a journey or hike

Examples

We need to hit the trail early if we want to reach the summit by noon.
After saying their goodbyes, they packed their gear and hit the trail.
Let's hit the trail before the weather changes for the worse.

Synset Examples

It's time to hit the trail and start our adventure.
After breakfast, they decided to hit the trail.
We will hit the trail early to avoid the heat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to set out - to begin a journey; to start traveling
  • Synonym: to depart - to leave, especially to begin a journey
  • Antonym: to arrive - to reach a destination

Etymology

Originates from 19th-century American English, referring to starting out on a physical trail or journey, especially in the context of the American frontier.
