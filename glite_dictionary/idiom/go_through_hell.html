to go through hell

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ θru hɛl / (AmE), / ɡəʊ θruː hɛl / (BrE)
to experience extremely difficult, unpleasant, or painful situations or periods in life
suffer greatly; endure extreme difficulty

Examples

After losing his job and his home, he went through hell before things got better.
She went through hell during the divorce and struggled to stay positive.
Many families go through hell when their loved ones fall seriously ill.

Synset Examples

They went through hell during the storm but survived.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go through the mill - to experience a difficult or painful time
  • Synonym: to have a hard time - to experience trouble, problems, or suffering
