a hundred years too early

1. X

/ ə ˈhʌndrəd jɪrz tu ˈɜrli / (AmE), / ə ˈhʌndrəd jɪəz tuː ˈɜːli / (BrE)
far ahead of current time or trends; ahead of its time or premature for the present era
far ahead of its time; premature

Examples

Some inventions are considered a hundred years too early to be accepted by society.
He felt his research was a hundred years too early for anyone to appreciate.
The artist's unique style was seen as a hundred years too early for the art world.

Synset Examples

His ideas may be brilliant, but they’re a hundred years too early.
Her vision for space travel seems to be a hundred years too early.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ahead of its time - having ideas or qualities that are too advanced for the period
