to buy a dog and bark

1. VERB

/ baɪ ə dɔg ənd bɑrk / (AmE), / baɪ ə dɒg ənd bɑːk / (BrE)
to take responsibility for one's own actions or do a task oneself rather than relying on others
do something yourself rather than having others do it

Examples

If you want full control over the outcome, you should buy a dog and bark yourself.
She decided to buy a dog and bark rather than depend on someone else's work.
Don't expect others to solve your problems; sometimes you have to buy a dog and bark.

Synset Examples

She felt it was time to buy a dog and bark herself.
If you want it done right, buy a dog and bark yourself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: do it yourself - to do something on your own rather than relying on others
  • Antonym: pass the buck - to shift responsibility to someone else
