in for the kill

1. X

/ ɪn fər ðə kɪl / (AmE), / ɪn fə ðə kɪl / (BrE)
ready to take decisive action to secure success, especially when an opportunity arises
ready to achieve decisive success

Examples

As soon as her opponent showed weakness, she was in for the kill.
The sales team was in for the kill after months of preparation.
He saw the perfect opening and went in for the kill during negotiations.

Synset Examples

The predator was poised, ready in for the kill.
He was determined to seal the deal; he was in for the kill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go for the jugular - to attack in a way that is intended to be decisive or to overwhelm
