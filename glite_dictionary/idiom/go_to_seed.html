to go to seed

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ tə sid / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə siːd / (BrE)
to become neglected, overgrown, or to deteriorate in quality or appearance
become neglected or deteriorate

Examples

If left unattended, the old building will go to seed in just a few years.
After her retirement, the immaculate garden began to go to seed.
Without regular maintenance, the machinery quickly started to go to seed.

Synset Examples

The garden began to go to seed after the summer ended.
If you don’t maintain it, the project will go to seed.
Without care, the once beautiful lawn started to go to seed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deteriorate - to become progressively worse
  • Antonym: to improve - to become or make better
