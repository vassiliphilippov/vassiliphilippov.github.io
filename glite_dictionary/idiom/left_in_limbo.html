left in limbo

1. ADJ

/ lɛft ɪn ˈlɪmboʊ / (AmE), / lɛft ɪn ˈlɪmbəʊ / (BrE)
in a state of uncertainty or unresolved situation, often abandoned without guidance or clear outcome
in a state of uncertainty

Examples

After the court case was delayed, the project was left in limbo for months.
Many students felt left in limbo when the university failed to announce new guidelines.
Negotiations broke down, and the contract was left in limbo indefinitely.

Synset Examples

After the merger, many employees were left in limbo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in limbo - in a state of neglect or uncertainty

Etymology

From 'limbo' (medieval Latin *limbus*, edge), originally meaning a place between heaven and hell; 'left in limbo' came to mean left in an unresolved or uncertain state.
