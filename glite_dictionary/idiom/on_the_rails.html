on the rails

1. ADV

/ ɑn ðə reɪlz / (AmE), / ɒn ðə reɪlz / (BrE)
functioning as intended; back on course and proceeding smoothly after difficulties
proceeding smoothly; back on course

Examples

The project is finally on the rails after months of delay.
After a shaky start, the team got the campaign on the rails.
Once the new manager arrived, everything was back on the rails.

Synset Examples

The event planning is now on the rails again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on track - proceeding as planned or intended
  • Antonym: off the rails - not functioning properly; out of control
