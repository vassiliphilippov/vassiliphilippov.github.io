what are you smoking

1. X

/ wʌt ɑr ju ˈsmoʊkɪŋ / (AmE), / wɒt ɑː ju ˈsməʊkɪŋ / (BrE)
used as a rhetorical question to express strong disbelief or to question the reasoning behind someone's statement or action
expression of disbelief or incredulity
informal

Examples

You seriously think that plan will work? What are you smoking?
If you believe the boss will approve that budget, what are you smoking?
She wants to move to Mars next year—what are you smoking?

Synset Examples

Seriously, what are you smoking to think that's a good plan?
If you believe that, what are you smoking?
That idea is wild—what are you smoking?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: are you out of your mind - expression used to question someone's sanity or judgment
  • Synonym: have you lost your mind - expression to challenge the rationality of someone's statement or idea
