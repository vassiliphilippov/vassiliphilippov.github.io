Nantucket sleigh ride

1. NOUN

/ nænˈtʌkɪt ˈsleɪ raɪd / (AmE), / nænˈtʌkɪt ˈsleɪ raɪd / (BrE)
an exciting or perilous ride, especially one resembling a wild chase at sea, originally referring to the fast, dangerous tow a whaleboat received from a harpooned whale
exciting or dangerous wild ride, esp. at sea

Examples

The storm turned their return journey into a Nantucket sleigh ride they would never forget.
After the whale was harpooned, the crew braced themselves for the Nantucket sleigh ride.
The entrepreneur described his first year in business as a Nantucket sleigh ride of ups and downs.

Synset Examples

The adventure felt like a Nantucket sleigh ride on the waves.
The whale gave us a thrilling Nantucket sleigh ride.
They experienced a Nantucket sleigh ride during their fishing trip.

Etymology

Named after Nantucket, a whaling hub; refers to a harpooned whale pulling a boat at high speed.
