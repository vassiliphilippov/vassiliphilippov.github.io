off and on

1. ADV

/ ˌɔf ənd ˈɑn / (AmE), / ˌɒf ənd ˈɒn / (BrE)
happening occasionally but not regularly; from time to time.
occasionally; at intervals

Examples

I've been taking painting classes off and on for the last five years.
It rained off and on throughout the weekend, making our plans uncertain.
They kept in touch off and on after leaving college.

Synset Examples

they phone each other off and on

Related Terms

  • Synonym: occasionally - at infrequent or irregular intervals; sometimes
  • Antonym: regularly - at fixed intervals; consistently
