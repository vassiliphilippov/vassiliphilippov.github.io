in vacuo

1. ADV

/ ɪn ˈvæk.ioʊ / (AmE), / ɪn ˈvæk.ju.əʊ / (BrE)
in a vacuum; in an environment where air or other gases are removed, especially used in scientific contexts.
in a vacuum (scientific idiom)

Examples

The substance was heated in vacuo to prevent unwanted reactions with air.
Some crystals form only when they are grown in vacuo.
The experiment must be conducted in vacuo for accurate results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in a vacuum - in an environment from which air is removed

Etymology

Latin, meaning 'in a vacuum'.
