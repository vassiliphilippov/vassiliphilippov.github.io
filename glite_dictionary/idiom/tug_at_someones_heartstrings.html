to tug at someone's heartstrings

1. VERB

/ tʌɡ æt ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈhɑːrtˌstɹɪŋz / (AmE), / tʌɡ ət ˈsʌmwənz ˈhɑːtˌstɹɪŋz / (BrE)
to cause strong feelings of sadness or sympathy in someone
evoke deep emotion

Examples

The movie tugged at my heartstrings and left me in tears by the end.
Her letter tugs at the heartstrings of anyone who reads it.
The sight of the abandoned puppy tugged at their heartstrings immediately.

Synset Examples

His story tugged at my heartstring deeply.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: move - to cause strong emotion, especially sadness or sympathy
  • Synonym: touch - to affect someone's emotions deeply

Etymology

From the metaphor of 'heartstrings,' referring to the deepest emotions, with 'tug at' indicating pulling on these feelings.
