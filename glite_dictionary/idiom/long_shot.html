long shot

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ ʃɑt / (AmE), / lɒŋ ʃɒt / (BrE)
an attempt or chance that is unlikely to succeed but is worth trying
unlikely chance of success

Examples

Getting a promotion this year is a long shot, but I'm still going to try.
They know it's a long shot, but they're hoping to raise enough money for the project.
She thought applying for the scholarship was a long shot, yet she was accepted.

Synset Examples

It's a long shot, but I’ll apply for that job.
She took a long shot and entered the contest.
Winning the lottery is a long shot for most people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outside chance - a small possibility, similar to 'long shot'
  • Antonym: certainty - something that is sure to happen

Etymology

Originally from marksmanship, referring to a shot made from a great distance, hence a low probability of success.
