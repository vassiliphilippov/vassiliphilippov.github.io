to ring true

1. VERB

/ rɪŋ truː / (AmE), / rɪŋ truː / (BrE)
to appear to be genuine, believable, or authentic
seem genuine or believable

Examples

Her apology just didn't ring true to anyone in the room.
The witness's testimony rang true compared to the evidence.
While the story was dramatic, parts of it didn't ring true for the audience.

Synset Examples

Her story didn't ring true to me.
His excuses always seem to ring true.
The facts presented in his argument ring true.
That saying rings true; she looks youthful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sound plausible - to seem reasonable or believable
  • Antonym: to ring false - to seem not genuine or suspicious
