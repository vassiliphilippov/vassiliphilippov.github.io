two shakes of a lamb's tail

1. NOUN

/ tu ʃeɪks əv ə læmz teɪl / (AmE), / tuː ʃeɪks əv ə læmz teɪl / (BrE)
in a very short time; very quickly or soon
very quickly; very soon

Examples

I’ll have the report ready in two shakes of a lamb's tail, so please wait.
She promised to call me back in two shakes of a lamb's tail, and she did.
If you follow these instructions, you’ll finish the job in two shakes of a lamb's tail.

Synset Examples

The train will arrive in two shakes of a lamb's tail.
Dinner will be ready in two shakes of a lamb's tail.
I'll finish this task in two shakes of a lamb's tail.
I’ll be back in two shakes of a lamb's tail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in no time - very quickly; immediately
  • Synonym: right away - at once; without delay
  • Antonym: slowly - in a slow manner; not quickly

Etymology

Refers humorously to the very brief time it takes for a lamb to shake its tail; first recorded in the 19th century.
