see the other guy

1. X

/ si ði ˈʌðɚ ɡaɪ / (AmE), / siː ði ˈʌðə ɡaɪ / (BrE)
(used humorously, especially after a fight or conflict) said to suggest that although one may appear hurt, the other person involved is even worse off.
humorous retort after a fight; the other person is worse off
informal

Examples

When people asked about his black eye, he just said, "You should see the other guy."
After her rough soccer match, she joked to her friends, "Yeah, but you should see the other guy."
He returned from the brawl bruised, but with a grin, said, "Just wait till you see the other guy."

Synset Examples

After the fight, he said I should see the other guy.
She laughed and said, you should see the other guy.
