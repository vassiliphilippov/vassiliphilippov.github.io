to couldn't help but

1. VERB

/ ˈkʊd(ə)nt hɛlp bʌt / (AmE), / ˈkʊd(ə)nt hɛlp bʌt / (BrE)
used to say that someone is unable to stop themselves from doing something
unable to avoid doing something

Examples

She couldn't help but laugh out loud at the comedian's joke.
I couldn't help but notice the beautiful paintings in the hallway.
They couldn't help but feel excited as the concert began.

Synset Examples

He couldn't help but smile at their playful mischief.
She couldn't help but frown at the gloomy weather.
When he heard the news, he just couldn't help but boff.
He couldn't help but sniggle at the silly joke.
He couldn't help but chuckle at his friend's beanbreath comment.
When she walked by, I couldn't help but holla.
He couldn't help but bop along to the beat.
He couldn't help but beam with pride at the ceremony.
She couldn't help but smile when he arrived.
He couldn't help but bibble during the presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: can't help but - informal variant with 'can't' instead of 'couldn't'
  • Synonym: can't help (doing something) - used when someone is unable to stop themselves from doing something (using gerund)
