loose lips sink ships

1. X

/ luːs lɪps sɪŋk ʃɪps / (AmE), / luːs lɪps sɪŋk ʃɪps / (BrE)
used to warn that careless talk can cause trouble or danger, especially by revealing sensitive information
warns against careless talk

Examples

Remember, loose lips sink ships, so don't share those details with anyone outside the team.
The manager reminded everyone that loose lips sink ships before the product launch.
They operate quietly in that industry—loose lips sink ships when it comes to trade secrets.

Synset Examples

A loose lip can sink ships, as the saying goes.

Etymology

Originated as a World War II slogan used in American propaganda to encourage discretion about military secrets.
