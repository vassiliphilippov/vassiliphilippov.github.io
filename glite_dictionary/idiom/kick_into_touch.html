to kick into touch

1. VERB

/ kɪk ˌɪntu ˈtʌtʃ / (AmE), / kɪk ˌɪntə ˈtʌtʃ / (BrE)
to postpone or abandon a plan, idea, or proposal, often indefinitely
postpone or abandon a plan
british

Examples

The committee chose to kick into touch the expensive renovation until next year.
Many politicians simply kick into touch any controversial reforms instead of facing them.
After much debate, the council kicked into touch the proposal to build a new sports center.

Synset Examples

They decided to kick the proposal into touch for now.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to put on the back burner - postpone or delay consideration

Etymology

From rugby, where to kick the ball into touch means putting it out of play.
