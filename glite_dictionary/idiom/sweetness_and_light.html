sweetness and light

1. NOUN

/ ˈswiːtnəs ənd laɪt / (AmE), / ˈswiːtnəs ənd laɪt / (BrE)
a state or attitude of pleasantness, friendliness, or mild reasonableness
pleasant and reasonable demeanor

Examples

Whenever the new manager visits, the whole office is sweetness and light.
He was all sweetness and light at the meeting, but his emails were quite critical.
Their conversation started with sweetness and light, but soon turned into an argument.

Synset Examples

when he learned who I was he became all sweetness and light

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amicable - showing friendliness or goodwill
  • Antonym: hostility - unfriendliness or opposition

Etymology

Popularized by 19th-century essayist Matthew Arnold; metaphor comparing agreeable qualities to sweetness (pleasantness) and light (enlightenment).

2. NOUN

/ ˈswit.nəs ənd laɪt / (AmE), / ˈswiːt.nəs ənd laɪt / (BrE)
a cheerful, pleasant, and agreeable attitude or peaceful state of affairs
cheerful, agreeable attitude or conditions

Examples

Though they seemed to be all sweetness and light in public, disagreements simmered behind the scenes.
After the argument ended, the household returned to sweetness and light.
Whenever there is a problem, she manages to bring sweetness and light to the situation.

Synset Examples

They promised sweetness and light, but delivered tension instead.
Her demeanor was all sweetness and light during the presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmony - agreement or pleasant relations
  • Antonym: discord - disagreement or lack of harmony

Etymology

Popularized by English writer Matthew Arnold in the 19th century, originally from Jonathan Swift's writings.
