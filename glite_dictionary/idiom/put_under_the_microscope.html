to put under the microscope

1. VERB

/ pʊt ˌʌndɚ ðə ˈmaɪ.krəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / pʊt ˌʌndə ðə ˈmaɪ.krə.skəʊp / (BrE)
to subject something to close and careful examination or scrutiny
subject to close scrutiny

Examples

The committee decided to put the proposal under the microscope before approving any funding.
His report was put under the microscope by industry experts looking for errors.
Whenever a scandal breaks, the media tends to put every detail under the microscope.

Synset Examples

The project was put under the microscope for review.
Their spending habits are now under the microscope.
The scientist placed the specimen under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scrutinize - to examine something very closely or carefully
  • Antonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to something
