to cut down the tall poppy

1. VERB

/ kʌt daʊn ðə tɔl ˈpɑpi / (AmE), / kʌt daʊn ðə tɔːl ˈpɒpi / (BrE)
to criticize or undermine someone who stands out for their success or achievements, often out of envy or desire to promote equality
criticize or undermine someone successful

Examples

Some people love to cut down the tall poppy rather than celebrate their achievements.
The media sometimes cut down the tall poppy when covering stories about successful celebrities.
In this environment, anyone who excels risks being cut down the tall poppy by their peers.

Synset Examples

The manager tends to cut down the tall poppy in meetings.
They often cut down the tall poppy to maintain harmony.
It's unfair to cut down the tall poppy in our team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tall poppy syndrome - the tendency to criticize or resent people who are successful or distinguished

Etymology

Refers to the Australian cultural phrase 'tall poppy syndrome', where 'tall poppies' symbolize people distinguished by success.
