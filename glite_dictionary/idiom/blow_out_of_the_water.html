to blow out of the water

1. VERB

/ bloʊ aʊt əv ðə ˈwɔtɚ / (AmE), / bləʊ aʊt əv ðə ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
to surprise greatly, overwhelm or defeat someone or something decisively
to surprise or defeat decisively

Examples

The new smartphone blows the competition out of the water with its advanced features.
Her performance in the finals blew everyone out of the water and left the judges speechless.
They were blown out of the water by the opposing team's remarkable strategy.

Synset Examples

I was floored when I heard that I was promoted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: floor - to astonish or surprise someone completely
  • Synonym: knock someone's socks off - to greatly surprise or impress someone
