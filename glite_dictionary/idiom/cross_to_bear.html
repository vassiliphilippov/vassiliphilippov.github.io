cross to bear

1. NOUN

/ krɒs tu bɛr / (AmE), / krɒs tə beə / (BrE)
a burden or difficult situation that one must endure or accept, often over a long period
personal burden or ongoing difficulty

Examples

Losing her job became a cross to bear, but she tried to stay positive.
Everyone has their own cross to bear at some point in life.
For him, the responsibility of caring for his parents was a heavy cross to bear.

Synset Examples

He felt that his past was a cross to bear.
Each of us has our own cross to bear in life.
Her broken leg was a heavy cross to bear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burden - a heavy load or something difficult to deal with

Etymology

Refers to the biblical story of Jesus carrying his cross before crucifixion, symbolizing a heavy burden to endure.
