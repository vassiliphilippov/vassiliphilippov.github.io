to watch your language

1. VERB

/ wɑtʃ jʊr ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / wɒtʃ jɔː ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
be careful to avoid using offensive or inappropriate words when speaking
be careful not to use bad language

Examples

You should watch your language when speaking in front of children.
The teacher reminded the students to watch their language during the debate.
If you don't watch your language, you'll be asked to leave the room.

Synset Examples

He was told to watch his language during the meeting.
You need to watch your language in formal settings.
Please watch your language around the children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mind your language - be careful about the words you use; avoid offensive words
