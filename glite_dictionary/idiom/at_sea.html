at sea

1. ADJ

/ æt si / (AmE), / ət siː / (BrE)
feeling perplexed, confused, or unsure what to do, especially when faced with a difficult situation
bewildered; confused or uncertain

Examples

After the sudden change in plans, I felt completely at sea and didn't know what to do.
Many students are at sea during their first week at university.
Without clear instructions, the team was left at sea about how to complete the project.

Synset Examples

obviously bemused by his questions
bewildered and confused
a cloudy and confounded philosopher
just a mixed-up kid
she felt lost on the first day of school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confused - lacking clarity or understanding, bewildered
  • Synonym: disoriented - having lost one's sense of direction or bearings, especially mentally

Etymology

Figurative use of being literally 'at sea'—adrift or lost on the ocean—dating from the 18th century.

2. ADJ

/ æt si / (AmE), / ət siː / (BrE)
confused or unable to understand what is happening or what to do
confused or uncertain

Examples

After the instructor gave complicated instructions, I felt completely at sea.
She was at sea in her new job, unsure of the procedures.
Many students are at sea with the new curriculum changes.

Synset Examples

I'm at sea when it comes to advanced math.
The sailors were at sea for weeks without sighting land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lost - not knowing where you are or what to do; confused
  • Synonym: bewildered - very confused and not sure what to do
