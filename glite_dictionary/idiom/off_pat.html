off pat

1. ADV

/ ɔf pæt / (AmE), / ɒf pæt / (BrE)
(of knowledge or a performance) learned or memorized perfectly, so that it can be repeated or performed without hesitation.
memorized or learned perfectly
british

Examples

She answered every question off pat in the spelling bee final.
He can recite all the Shakespeare sonnets off pat after weeks of practice.
Make sure you know the evacuation procedure off pat before the drill.

Synset Examples

She knows her lines off pat for the play.
You should have the presentation off pat by now.
He recited the speech off pat without any prompts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: by heart - learned so well that it can be repeated from memory

Etymology

Likely from the word 'pat' meaning 'readily or promptly'.
