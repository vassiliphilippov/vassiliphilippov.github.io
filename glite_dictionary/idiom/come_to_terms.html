to come to terms

1. VERB

/ kʌm tə tɜrmz / (AmE), / kʌm tə tɜːmz / (BrE)
To accept or reconcile oneself to a difficult or unpleasant situation; to reach an agreement or understanding, especially after a period of struggle or reflection.
accept or reach understanding

Examples

After the accident, it took her months to come to terms with her new reality.
They are slowly coming to terms with their financial loss.
He has finally come to terms with the fact that he needs help.

Synset Examples

She finally came to terms with her decision.
She couldn't come to terms with the breakup.
It's hard to come to terms with such a tragedy.
They need to come to terms with the new policy.
He is trying to come to terms with the changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accept - to receive or agree to something, often reluctantly
  • Antonym: to resist - to oppose something, especially emotionally or mentally
