on the qui vive

1. NOUN

/ ɑn ðə kiː viːv / (AmE), / ɒn ðə kiː viːv / (BrE)
alert and watchful; in a state of readiness or vigilance.
watchful; alert and ready

Examples

The guards were on the qui vive after hearing rumors of a possible attack.
You have to stay on the qui vive when driving in heavy traffic.
The staff was on the qui vive during the important event to ensure everything went smoothly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alert - quick to notice and respond to danger or problems
  • Synonym: vigilant - keeping careful watch for possible danger or difficulties

Etymology

From French 'qui vive?' meaning 'who goes there?,' a traditional challenge from sentries.
