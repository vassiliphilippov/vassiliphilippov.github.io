castle in the air

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæs.əl ɪn ði ɛr / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.sl̩ ɪn ðiː eə / (BrE)
a hope, plan, or idea that is impossible or very unlikely to be achieved; a daydream
an unrealistic hope or dream

Examples

He spent hours thinking up castles in the air instead of focusing on his work.
All her plans for a career in Hollywood turned out to be just castles in the air.
Don't build castles in the air; try to set realistic goals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipe dream - an unrealistic hope or plan
  • Synonym: daydream - a pleasant but unlikely fantasy or wish

Etymology

From the image of building imaginary castles in the sky, noted in English since the 16th century.

2. NOUN

Examples
