to hit the roof

1. VERB

/ hɪt ðə ruːf / (AmE), / hɪt ðə ruːf / (BrE)
to become extremely angry or furious suddenly
become extremely angry

Examples

When he found out his car had been scratched, he hit the roof.
My parents will hit the roof if I come home late again.
She hit the roof after hearing about the mistake in the report.

Synset Examples

The professor combusted when the student didn't know the answer to a very elementary question
Spam makes me go ballistic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to blow a fuse - to become very angry or lose one's temper
  • Synonym: to go ballistic - to become extremely angry, show sudden rage
  • Antonym: keep calm - to stay calm and avoid getting angry

2. VERB

/ hɪt ðə ɹuːf / (AmE), / hɪt ðə ruːf / (BrE)
To suddenly become very angry.
become extremely angry

Examples

When he saw the extra charges on his credit card, he hit the roof.
She hit the roof when her son arrived home past midnight.
If my boss hears about this mistake, he'll probably hit the roof.

Synset Examples

When he saw the bill, he hit the roof.
The manager hit the roof over the missed deadline.
Her parents hit the roof when they found out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to blow one's top - to suddenly become very angry
  • Synonym: to lose one's temper - to become very angry quickly
  • Antonym: to keep one's cool - to remain calm and not get angry
