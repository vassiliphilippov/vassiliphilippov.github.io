to leave no stone unturned

1. VERB

/ liv noʊ stoʊn ʌnˈtɜrnd / (AmE), / liːv nəʊ stəʊn ʌnˈtɜːnd / (BrE)
to search or investigate every possible place or means in order to achieve a goal or find something
search thoroughly and exhaustively

Examples

The detectives left no stone unturned in their quest to solve the mystery.
We will leave no stone unturned to ensure every detail is perfect for the event.
She promised to leave no stone unturned in her search for the missing documents.

Synset Examples

The police left no stone unturned in looking for the President's murderer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search high and low - to search everywhere thoroughly

Etymology

From a Greek proverb attributed to Euripides, popularized in English since the 16th century.

2. VERB

/ liv noʊ stoʊn ʌnˈtɝnd / (AmE), / liːv nəʊ stəʊn ʌnˈtɜːnd / (BrE)
to search or investigate thoroughly and exhaustively, making every possible effort to achieve something
make every effort; search thoroughly

Examples

The police promised to leave no stone unturned in their search for the missing child.
Determined to win the contract, the team left no stone unturned in their proposal.
She assured her client that she would leave no stone unturned in gathering evidence.

Synset Examples

We will leave no stone unturned to find the truth.
He left no stone unturned in preparing for the exam.
She vowed to leave no stone unturned in her research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search - to look for something carefully and thoroughly
  • Antonym: overlook - to fail to notice or search completely

Etymology

First recorded in English in the 16th century; based on an ancient Greek legend about searching for a hidden treasure.
