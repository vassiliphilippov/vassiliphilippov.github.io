to keep company

1. VERB

/ kip ˈkʌmpəni / (AmE), / kiːp ˈkʌmpəni / (BrE)
to spend time with someone regularly, especially as a companion or friend
spend time with someone

Examples

I often keep company with my neighbors after work to relax and chat.
He preferred to keep company with artists and musicians rather than businessmen.
During her illness, many friends came to keep company with her to lift her spirits.

Synset Examples

They decided to keep company during the long wait.
He prefers to keep company with his close friends.
She likes to keep company with positive people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hang out - to spend time relaxing or socializing
  • Antonym: to avoid - to stay away from someone or something
