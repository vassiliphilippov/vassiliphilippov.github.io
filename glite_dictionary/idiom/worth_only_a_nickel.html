worth only a nickel

1. ADJ

/ wɝːθ ˈoʊnli ə ˈnɪkəl / (AmE), / wɜːθ ˈəʊnli ə ˈnɪkl̩ / (BrE)
of very little value; almost worthless
of very little value

Examples

The souvenirs looked cheap and were worth only a nickel to most tourists.
After years of neglect, the bike was worth only a nickel.
He joked that his advice was worth only a nickel if you asked his boss.

Synset Examples

The old car was worth only a nickel at the junkyard.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: worth only a dime - of very little value (slightly higher than a nickel)
  • Synonym: worthless - having no value at all
