a thousand and one reasons

1. NOUN

/ ə ˈθaʊzənd ənd wʌn ˈɹiːzənz / (AmE), / ə ˈθaʊzənd ənd wʌn ˈɹiːznz / (BrE)
very many different reasons or motives for something; countless justifications
very many different reasons

Examples

She listed a thousand and one reasons why she deserved a vacation.
There are a thousand and one reasons to learn a new language.
He could think of a thousand and one reasons not to attend the meeting.

Synset Examples

She counted a thousand one reasons to move away.

Etymology

From the literary tradition of 'A Thousand and One Nights,' implying a very large number.
