quick on one's feet

1. ADJ

/ kwɪk ɑn wʌnz fit / (AmE), / kwɪk ɒn wʌnz fiːt / (BrE)
able to think and respond quickly, especially in unexpected or difficult situations
able to think and act quickly

Examples

In high-pressure interviews, it's important to be quick on your feet when unexpected questions arise.
The lawyer was quick on her feet during cross-examination, adapting rapidly to each twist.
He’s always quick on his feet when solving problems at work.

Synset Examples

She’s always quick on her feet during tough situations.
The comedian was quick on his feet with witty responses.
In negotiations, you need to be quick on your feet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nimble - able to move or think quickly and easily
  • Antonym: slow-witted - not quick to understand or respond
