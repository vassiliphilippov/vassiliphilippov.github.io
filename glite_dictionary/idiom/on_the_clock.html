on the clock

1. ADV

/ ɑn ðə klɑk / (AmE), / ɒn ðə klɒk / (BrE)
within paid working hours; subject to a set time limit or deadline
during paid work hours; within a time limit

Examples

You are not allowed to use your phone while you are on the clock.
She completed all the paperwork on the clock before leaving for lunch.
The repairmen weren't rushing because they were still on the clock.

Synset Examples

You need to be punctual; it’s all on the clock.
We have to finish this project on the clock.
He worked overtime, staying late on the clock.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: off the clock - outside of paid working hours
