to keep in touch

1. VERB

/ kip ɪn tʌtʃ / (AmE), / kiːp ɪn tʌtʃ / (BrE)
to continue to communicate with someone, especially after leaving or moving apart
maintain communication

Examples

After graduating, we promised to keep in touch no matter where life took us.
Please keep in touch and let me know how you're doing at your new job.
She moved abroad last year, but we still keep in touch by email and video calls.

Synset Examples

He promised to keep in touch while traveling.
Let’s stay in touch after you move away.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lose touch - to stop communicating with someone
  • Synonym: stay in touch - to continue communicating with someone
