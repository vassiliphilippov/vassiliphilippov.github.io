to skate your lane

1. VERB

/ skeɪt jʊr leɪn / (AmE), / skeɪt jɔː leɪn / (BrE)
to focus on your own tasks or responsibilities without interfering in others' affairs
focus on your own responsibilities

Examples

It's important to skate your lane if you want the team to function smoothly.
My supervisor reminded me to skate my lane and not get involved in other departments' issues.
She tries hard to skate her lane and avoid office drama.

Synset Examples

He needs to learn to skate his lane and not interfere.
You should skate your lane and focus on your tasks.
It's best to skate your lane to avoid conflicts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mind your own business - to not interfere with other people's affairs
