to shuck and jive

1. VERB

/ ʃʌk ənd dʒaɪv / (AmE), / ʃʌk ənd dʒaɪv / (BrE)
to talk or behave evasively or insincerely, especially in order to avoid answering directly or to deceive
to speak evasively or insincerely
informal american

Examples

The politician began to shuck and jive when asked about the controversial policy.
Instead of answering directly, he just tried to shuck and jive his way through the interview.
Tired of all his shuck and jive, the manager insisted on a straightforward explanation.

Synset Examples

Don't shuck and jive; just tell me the truth.
He tried to shuck and jive his way out of trouble.
The salesman attempted to shuck and jive his pitch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prevaricate - to speak or act in an evasive way
  • Synonym: to evade - to avoid giving a direct answer
  • Antonym: to come clean - to admit the truth without evasion or deceit

Etymology

From African American Vernacular English; 'shuck' and 'jive' both refer to deceptive or evasive behaviors. Originally associated with insincere talk or trickery.
