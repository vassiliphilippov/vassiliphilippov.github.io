to tell it to the marines

1. VERB

/ ˈtɛl ɪt tu ðə məˈrinz / (AmE), / ˈtɛl ɪt tə ðə məˈriːnz / (BrE)
used to express disbelief or dismiss someone's story or complaint as unimportant or unlikely to be true.
expression dismissing someone's account as untrue or trivial

Examples

You expect me to believe that? Tell it to the marines!
When he gave yet another excuse for being late, she said, 'Tell it to the marines.'
If you think anyone will believe that story, tell it to the marines.

Synset Examples

He's not interested in your sob story; tell it to the marines.
When she whined, he told her to tell it to the marines.
If you don't believe me, tell it to the marines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pull the other one - used to show disbelief in what someone has said
  • Antonym: to believe - to accept as true

Etymology

Originating in early 19th-century Britain, possibly from the idea that only gullible marines would believe tall tales.
