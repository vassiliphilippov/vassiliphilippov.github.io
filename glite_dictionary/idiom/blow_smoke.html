to blow smoke

1. VERB

/ bloʊ smoʊk / (AmE), / bləʊ sməʊk / (BrE)
to deliberately deceive or mislead someone, especially by making exaggerated or false statements
deceive or mislead with false claims

Examples

Please don't blow smoke; I want honest feedback about my work.
Politicians sometimes blow smoke to avoid giving direct answers to tough questions.
She realized he was just blowing smoke when his promises never came true.

Synset Examples

Don't blow smoke; I need the facts.
He tends to blow smoke to impress others.
Stop blowing smoke and get to the point.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lie - to say something that is not true in order to deceive
  • Synonym: to mislead - to cause someone to have a wrong idea or impression
  • Antonym: to tell the truth - to state what is true
