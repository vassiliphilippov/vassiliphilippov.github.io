to give the axe

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ði æks / (AmE), / ɡɪv ðiː æks / (BrE)
to dismiss someone from their job; to terminate someone's employment
dismiss someone from a job

Examples

The company had to give the axe to several employees during the merger.
Rumors are spreading that the manager will give the axe to anyone who misses another deadline.
After years of service, she was shocked when they gave the axe to her without warning.

Synset Examples

The boss fired his secretary today
The company terminated 25% of its workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire - to remove someone from a job
  • Synonym: to dismiss - to remove someone from employment
  • Antonym: hire - to employ someone for a job

Etymology

From the figurative use of 'axe' as a symbol of dismissal or execution, based on the literal instrument.

2. VERB

/ ɡɪv ði æks / (AmE), / ɡɪv ði æks / (BrE)
to end a relationship or dismiss someone suddenly and decisively
end a relationship or dismiss suddenly

Examples

His boss gave him the axe after only two months on the job.
She decided to give the axe to their friendship after the argument.
The TV network gave the axe to several underperforming shows this season.

Synset Examples

Mary gave John the axe after she saw him with another woman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to break up - end a romantic relationship
  • Synonym: fire - dismiss someone from a job
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00354293-v
Synset POS: v
