no rose without a thorn

1. X

/ noʊ ɹoʊz wɪðˈaʊt ə θɔɹn / (AmE), / nəʊ ɹəʊz wɪðˈaʊt ə θɔːn / (BrE)
used to express the idea that every good or desirable thing has its difficulties or drawbacks
every good thing has its troubles

Examples

She knew their new business would face setbacks; after all, no rose without a thorn.
He found the job rewarding, but stressful at times, proving that no rose without a thorn.
Although her vacation was wonderful, a few problems reminded her that no rose without a thorn.

Synset Examples

He reminded her that no rose without a thorn in life.
Their relationship had ups and downs; no rose without a thorn.
She smiled, knowing that there’s no rose without a thorn in love.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: every cloud has a silver lining - even bad situations have a positive side

Etymology

From a traditional proverb dating back to the late Middle Ages, reflecting the metaphor of a beautiful rose accompanied by thorns.
