to climb down from one's high horse

1. VERB

/ klaɪm daʊn frəm wʌnz haɪ hɔrs / (AmE), / klaɪm daʊn frəm wʌnz haɪ hɔːs / (BrE)
to become less arrogant, stop acting superior, and behave with more humility or realism
become less arrogant or more humble

Examples

After the criticism, she finally climbed down from her high horse and listened to our ideas.
It's time for you to climb down from your high horse and admit you made a mistake.
The manager had to climb down from his high horse when his proposal was rejected.

Synset Examples

She decided to climb down from her high horse.
He got off his high horse and apologized.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get off one's high horse - to stop acting superior or arrogant

Etymology

From the image of someone on a high horse acting superior; to 'climb down' is to adopt a more humble attitude.
