to bet one's bottom dollar

1. VERB

/ bɛt wʌnz ˈbɑtəm ˈdɑlər / (AmE), / bɛt wʌnz ˈbɒtəm ˈdɒlə / (BrE)
to be absolutely sure of something; to feel certain about a particular outcome
be completely certain

Examples

You can bet your bottom dollar that they'll make an appearance at the event.
She would bet her bottom dollar that the test was tomorrow, not today.
I bet my bottom dollar he's already heard the news.

Synset Examples

I bet my bottom dollar she'll pass the exam.
I will bet my bottom dollar on that team's victory.
I'll bet my bottom dollar that they'll win the championship.
You can bet your bottom dollar he's coming to the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be sure - to have no doubt; to feel certain
  • Antonym: doubt - to feel uncertain or lack belief

Etymology

Refers to staking one's last dollar, emphasizing complete confidence.
