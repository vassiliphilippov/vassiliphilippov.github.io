to put a spanner in

1. VERB

/ pʊt ə ˈspænər ɪn / (AmE), / pʊt ə ˈspænə ɪn / (BrE)
To cause disruption or complications in a plan, process, or situation.
cause disruption or complications
british

Examples

The last-minute change in requirements put a spanner in the development process.
If you cancel now, it will really put a spanner in everyone's plans.
The sudden rainstorm put a spanner in our outdoor event preparations.

Synset Examples

The new regulations put a spanner in our project timeline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to throw a wrench in - To cause trouble or disruption (American equivalent idiom)

Etymology

British English idiom, from the literal use of 'spanner' (wrench) as a tool that can halt machinery when inserted.
