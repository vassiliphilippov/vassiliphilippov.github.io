every nook and cranny

1. X

/ ˈɛvri nʊk ənd ˈkræni / (AmE), / ˈɛvri nʊk ənd ˈkræni / (BrE)
every part of a place or thing, including all the small or hidden areas
every part, including small hidden spaces

Examples

The inspectors checked every nook and cranny of the building for safety hazards.
She cleaned every nook and cranny of her apartment before the guests arrived.
He explored every nook and cranny of the old castle, searching for hidden treasures.

Synset Examples

They searched every nook and cranny for the missing keys.
She cleaned every nook and cranny of the house.

Etymology

From 'nook' (a corner or recess) and 'cranny' (a small crack or opening), both of Middle English origin.
