to fly under the radar

1. VERB

/ flaɪ ˈʌndɚ ðə ˈreɪˌdɑr / (AmE), / flaɪ ˈʌndə ðə ˈreɪˌdɑː / (BrE)
to avoid being noticed or attracting attention, especially by staying inconspicuous
avoid being noticed; stay inconspicuous

Examples

The company had been flying under the radar for years before gaining national recognition.
Sometimes it's best to fly under the radar when making big changes at work.
She prefers to fly under the radar and let her achievements speak for themselves.

Synset Examples

He managed to fly under the radar during the audit.
She likes to fly under the radar at work.
The startup has been flying under the radar until now.
He managed to slip under the radar during the audit.
Some talented artists often slip under the radar unnoticed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keep a low profile - to avoid attracting attention to oneself

Etymology

From the aviation term 'under the radar', where aircraft fly at a low altitude to evade detection by radar systems.
