to drop a line

1. VERB

/ drɑp ə laɪn / (AmE), / drɒp ə laɪn / (BrE)
to write a short letter or message to someone, especially as a way of keeping in touch
write a brief message to someone

Examples

If you have time, please drop a line to your old friends from college.
She promised to drop a line when she arrived in London.
Whenever I travel, I make sure to drop a line to my family back home.

Synset Examples

Write to her soon, please!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: write to - to send a letter or message to someone

2. VERB

/ drɑp ə laɪn / (AmE), / drɒp ə laɪn / (BrE)
to send someone a brief message or note, especially to keep in touch
send a brief message or note

Examples

If you have any questions about your reservation, just drop a line and I'll get back to you.
She promised to drop a line during her travels through Europe.
Whenever you change your address, remember to drop a line to your old friends.

Synset Examples

I'll drop her a line to check in.
Make sure to drop me a line when you arrive.
Don't forget to drop me a line about the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to write - compose and send a written message
  • Synonym: contact - communicate with someone
