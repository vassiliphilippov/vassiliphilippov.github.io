to give someone the business

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ˈsʌmwʌn ðə ˈbɪznəs / (AmE), / ɡɪv ˈsʌmwʌn ðə ˈbɪznɪs / (BrE)
to reprimand or scold someone severely, often in a critical or harsh manner
reprimand or scold someone
informal

Examples

The manager gave him the business after he arrived late three days in a row.
If you forget her birthday again, she's really going to give you the business.
My coach gave us the business for not playing hard enough in the first half.

Synset Examples

The boss really gave him the business for missing deadlines.
When I returned the faulty product, they gave me the business.
She always gives him the business about his messy office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tell off - to reprimand someone, especially harshly
  • Synonym: scold - to speak angrily or severely to someone because they have done something wrong
