to throw a dummy spit

1. VERB

/ θɹoʊ ə ˈdʌmi spɪt / (AmE), / θɹəʊ ə ˈdʌmi spɪt / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly Australian and British) To have a tantrum or sudden outburst of anger or frustration, typically regarded as childish or excessive.
have a childish tantrum or outburst
informal british

Examples

When the referee made a bad call, he threw a dummy spit and stormed off the field.
She threw a dummy spit after realising someone else got her promotion at work.
Whenever things don't go his way, he tends to throw a dummy spit instead of discussing calmly.

Synset Examples

He threw a dummy spit when he didn’t get his way.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to throw a tantrum - to lose one's temper in a childish way
  • Synonym: to lose one's temper - to suddenly become very angry

Etymology

Australian English, from 'dummy' (a baby's pacifier) and 'spit' (to eject), referring to a baby's act of spitting out a pacifier in anger.
