to sign on the dotted line

1. VERB

/ saɪn ɑn ðə ˈdɑt̬ɪd laɪn / (AmE), / saɪn ɒn ðə ˈdɒtɪd laɪn / (BrE)
to formally agree to something, typically by signing a document, making the agreement official and binding
to agree formally by signing

Examples

After weeks of negotiations, she was finally ready to sign on the dotted line.
It's important to read the contract carefully before you sign on the dotted line.
He couldn't believe he was about to sign on the dotted line for his first home.

Synset Examples

He was excited to finally sign on the dotted line.
She hesitated to sign on the dotted line for the loan.
Before you leave, please sign on the dotted line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sign - to write one's name on a document to indicate agreement
