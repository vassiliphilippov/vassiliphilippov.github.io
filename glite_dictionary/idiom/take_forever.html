to take forever

1. VERB

/ teɪk fəˈrɛvɚ / (AmE), / teɪk fəˈrɛvə / (BrE)
to take an extremely long or unnecessarily long amount of time
require a very long time

Examples

It always seems to take forever for the bus to arrive on rainy days.
The meeting took forever because everyone wanted to share their opinion.
Waiting for the website to load is taking forever this morning.

Synset Examples

This mothertrucking project is taking forever to finish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to drag on - to last or continue tediously for a long time
