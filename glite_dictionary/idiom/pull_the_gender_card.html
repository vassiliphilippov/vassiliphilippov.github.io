to pull the gender card

1. VERB

/ pʊl ðə ˈdʒɛndər kɑrd / (AmE), / pʊl ðə ˈdʒɛndə kɑːd / (BrE)
to mention or emphasize gender, often to gain advantage, sympathy, or to deflect criticism in an argument or situation
invoke gender for advantage or sympathy

Examples

During the meeting, she was accused of pulling the gender card to avoid further scrutiny.
Critics claimed that the politician pulled the gender card when challenged on her record.
He resented the implication that he was pulling the gender card to win sympathy from the judges.

Synset Examples

He accused her of pulling the gender card during the debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to play the race card - to mention one's race to gain advantage or sympathy

Etymology

Modelled on 'play the race card', combining 'gender' with 'card' as a metaphor for a strategic move.
