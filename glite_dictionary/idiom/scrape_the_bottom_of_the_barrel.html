to scrape the bottom of the barrel

1. VERB

/ skreɪp ðə ˈbɑtəm əv ðə ˈbærəl / (AmE), / skreɪp ðə ˈbɒtəm ɒv ðə ˈbærəl / (BrE)
to use the least desirable or only remaining options because nothing better is available
use the least desirable options left

Examples

By the end of the season, the chef had to scrape the bottom of the barrel for fresh ingredients.
We're really scraping the bottom of the barrel when we have to hire people with no experience.
After so many rejections, she finally scraped the bottom of the barrel and accepted the only offer left.

Synset Examples

We had to scrape the bottom of the barrel for ideas.
When the team scraped the bottom of the barrel, they found some gems.
After months of searching, he finally scraped the bottom of the barrel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desperate measures - actions taken when nothing else works
  • Synonym: pickings are slim - very few good options are available
