to play a vital role

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ə ˈvaɪtəl roʊl / (AmE), / pleɪ ə ˈvaɪtəl rəʊl / (BrE)
to have a significant or essential influence on something or to be extremely important in a situation or process
to be extremely important or influential

Examples

Teachers often play a vital role in shaping a child's future.
Public transportation can play a vital role in reducing city pollution.
Volunteers have played a vital role during the disaster recovery process.

Synset Examples

Moslem traditions play a vital role in our culture.
Technology plays a vital role in our daily lives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: play a key role - to be very important in something; synonym for 'play a vital role'
  • Antonym: play a minor role - to have little importance or influence in something
