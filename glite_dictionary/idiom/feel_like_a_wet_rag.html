to feel like a wet rag

1. VERB

/ fil laɪk ə wɛt ræɡ / (AmE), / fiːl laɪk ə wɛt ræɡ / (BrE)
to feel extremely tired, exhausted, or worn out, especially after an effort or setback
to feel exhausted or worn out

Examples

By the end of the marathon, I felt like a wet rag and could barely walk.
After staying up all night to finish the project, she felt like a wet rag at work.
He feels like a wet rag every time he pulls an all-nighter.

Synset Examples

After that defeat, the team felt like a wet rag.
Her negative comments made him feel like a wet rag.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhausted - very tired; worn out, physically or mentally
  • Synonym: defeated - having lost a contest; overcome by adversity
