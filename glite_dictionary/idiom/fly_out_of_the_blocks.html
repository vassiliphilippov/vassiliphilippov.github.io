to fly out of the blocks

1. VERB

/ flaɪ aʊt əv ðə blɑks / (AmE), / flaɪ aʊt əv ðə blɒks / (BrE)
to start an activity very quickly and energetically, especially from the beginning of a race or a new endeavor
start quickly and energetically

Examples

The new product flew out of the blocks and became a bestseller within weeks of its launch.
She tends to fly out of the blocks at work meetings, making her points immediately.
If our team flies out of the blocks in the first quarter, we'll put pressure on the opposition early.

Synset Examples

The athlete flew out of the blocks at the start.
She flew out of the blocks and took the lead.
Once the whistle blew, he flew out of the traps.
They flew out of the traps and secured victory.
The horse flew out of the traps at the start.
He always flies out of the blocks in races.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get off to a flying start - to begin something very successfully or enthusiastically
  • Antonym: get off to a slow start - to begin something sluggishly or without momentum

Etymology

From athletics, where 'blocks' refers to the starting blocks used by sprinters.
