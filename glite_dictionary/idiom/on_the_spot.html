on the spot

1. ADV

/ ɑn ðə spɑt / (AmE), / ɒn ðə spɒt / (BrE)
at the exact place where something is happening or has happened; there and then
at the exact place; there and then

Examples

The police were on the spot within minutes of the accident.
He had to make a decision on the spot without consulting anyone.
Reporters rushed to be on the spot as the story unfolded.

Synset Examples

they were on the spot when it happened
it had to be decided by the man on the spot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immediately - without delay or hesitation; straight away
  • Synonym: on site - at the location of an activity or event
