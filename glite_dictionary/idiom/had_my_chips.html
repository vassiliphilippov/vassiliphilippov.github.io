to had my chips

1. VERB

/ hæd maɪ tʃɪps / (AmE), / hæd maɪ tʃɪps / (BrE)
to be in a hopeless situation or to be out of luck, often implying that one's chances have run out
be out of luck or in a hopeless situation
informal british

Examples

After that embarrassing interview, I really had my chips.
When his plan failed, he knew he'd had his chips.
She felt she had her chips after missing the final exam.

Synset Examples

After that mistake, I've really had my chips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be done for - to be in serious trouble or doomed
  • Synonym: down on one's luck - experiencing a period of misfortune
  • Antonym: in luck - experiencing good fortune

Etymology

British English idiom, possibly from gambling, where losing all one’s chips means losing the game.
