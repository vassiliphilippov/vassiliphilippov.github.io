what, me worry

1. X

/ wʌt mi ˈwɜːri / (AmE), / wɒt mi ˈwʌri / (BrE)
an expression used to indicate a lack of concern or indifference about something, often in a lighthearted or dismissive way
expression of nonchalance or indifference
informal american

Examples

Whenever things go wrong at work, he just shrugs and says, what, me worry?
The team's captain smiled before the tough match and joked, what, me worry?
She missed the deadline but responded to her boss with a playful, what, me worry?

Synset Examples

She smiled and replied, what, me worry about that?
When faced with trouble, he laughed, what, me worry?
He shrugged and said, what, me worry?

Etymology

Popularized as the catchphrase of Alfred E. Neuman, the mascot of Mad magazine (1950s, USA).
