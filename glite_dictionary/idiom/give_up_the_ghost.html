to give up the ghost

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ʌp ðə ɡoʊst / (AmE), / ɡɪv ʌp ðə ɡəʊst / (BrE)
(informal, often humorous) To die; to stop functioning or come to an end, especially after a struggle or long period of decline.
to die; to cease functioning
informal

Examples

After years of loyal service, my old laptop finally gave up the ghost yesterday.
The engine gave up the ghost halfway through our road trip, forcing us to call for help.
She fought bravely against illness for months before eventually giving up the ghost.

Synset Examples

She died from cancer
The children perished in the fire
The patient went peacefully
The old guy kicked the bucket at the age of 102

Related Terms

  • Synonym: die - to cease living
  • Synonym: to kick the bucket - to die (slang idiom)
  • Synonym: to perish - to die, usually in a sudden or untimely way

Etymology

Originally used in the Bible, where "ghost" referred to the soul or spirit; "give up the ghost" meant to expire or die.
