to put a sock in it

1. VERB

/ pʊt ə sɑk ɪn ɪt / (AmE), / pʊt ə sɒk ɪn ɪt / (BrE)
(informal) Used to tell someone to stop talking or be quiet.
tell someone to be quiet
informal british

Examples

If you're going to complain all night, please just put a sock in it.
The teacher told the noisy students to put a sock in it and listen to the instructions.
After his fifth story in a row, Lara asked him to put a sock in it.

Synset Examples

He told his brother to put a sock in it during the movie.
I wished he’d just put a sock in it already.
She finally told him to put a sock in it after hours of complaining.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shut up - to stop talking; to be quiet
  • Synonym: to pipe down - to become quiet or stop talking
  • Antonym: to speak up - to talk louder or express one's opinion; opposite meaning

Etymology

Believed to have originated in early 20th-century Britain, possibly referring to muffling the sound from a gramophone with a sock.
