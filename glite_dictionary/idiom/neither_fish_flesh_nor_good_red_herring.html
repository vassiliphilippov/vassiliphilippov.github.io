neither fish, flesh, nor good red herring

1. X

/ ˈniðər fɪʃ flɛʃ nɔr gʊd rɛd ˈhɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈniːðə fɪʃ fleʃ nɔː ɡʊd rɛd ˈherɪŋ / (BrE)
used to describe something that is ambiguous, indefinite, or does not clearly fall into any category; also meaning irrelevant or insignificant
ambiguous or irrelevant thing

Examples

The new policy was neither fish, flesh, nor good red herring, leaving everyone confused about its purpose.
His explanation was neither fish, flesh, nor good red herring—it didn't help clarify the issue at all.
Critics said the novel was neither fish, flesh, nor good red herring, lacking a clear genre or focus.

Synset Examples

The proposal was neither fish, flesh, nor good red herring.
The argument was neither fish, flesh, nor good red herring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irrelevant - not connected with or relevant to something
  • Synonym: ambiguous - open to more than one interpretation; not having one obvious meaning

Etymology

Based on an old English proverb, referencing various kinds of food to express indeterminacy.
