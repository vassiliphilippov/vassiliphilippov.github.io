to keep one's ears open

1. VERB

/ kip wʌnz ɪrz ˈoʊpən / (AmE), / kiːp wʌnz ɪəz ˈəʊpən / (BrE)
to remain alert and attentive in order to notice or learn something
be attentive for information

Examples

I always keep my ears open for interesting news at work.
Travelers should keep their ears open for any safety announcements at the station.
She made sure to keep her ears open during the meeting for hints about the changes.

Synset Examples

I always keep my ears open for job opportunities.
Make sure to keep your ears open for any news.
Keep your ears open; you might learn something valuable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be on the lookout - to watch carefully for something
  • Antonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to
