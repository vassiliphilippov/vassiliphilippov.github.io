to bend to someone's will

1. VERB

/ bɛnd tu ˈsʌmwʌnz wɪl / (AmE), / bɛnd tʊ ˈsʌmwʌnz wɪl / (BrE)
to submit or yield to the wishes, authority, or influence of another person
submit to another's wishes or authority

Examples

She refused to bend to his will and stood firm in her beliefs.
The government would not bend to the protesters' will despite growing pressure.
He tried to bend to her will, but ultimately made his own decision.

Synset Examples

She learned to bend to his will during negotiations.
They will bend to their boss's will without protest.
She had to bend to his will eventually.
They will bend to her will if pressured.
He refuses to bend to anyone's will.
He didn't want to bend to her will.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to submit - accept or yield to a superior force or to the authority of another
  • Antonym: to resist - refuse to accept or comply with something; try to prevent by action or argument
