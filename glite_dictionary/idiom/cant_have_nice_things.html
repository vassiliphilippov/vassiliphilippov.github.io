can't have nice things

1. X

/ kænt hæv naɪs θɪŋz / (AmE), / kɑːnt hæv naɪs θɪŋz / (BrE)
used to express frustration when good situations or possessions are spoiled, often due to carelessness or misbehavior
phrase expressing frustration when situations are ruined
informal

Examples

The kids spilled juice on the new sofa; this is why we can't have nice things.
Every time a project is going well, someone messes it up, proving we can't have nice things.
She scratched my car again—sometimes I think we just can't have nice things.

Synset Examples

Every time I try, this is why we can't have nice things.
