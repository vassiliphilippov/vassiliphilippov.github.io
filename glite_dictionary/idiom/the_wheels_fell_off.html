the wheels fell off

1. X

/ ðə wilz fɛl ɔf / (AmE), / ðə wiːlz fɛl ɒf / (BrE)
used to say that a situation suddenly went wrong, failed, or became chaotic after going smoothly.
suddenly go wrong or fail

Examples

After their star player was injured, the wheels fell off and the team lost every game.
The meeting started well, but the wheels fell off when the budget figures didn't add up.
It was going smoothly until the power outage—then the wheels fell off entirely.

Synset Examples

Once he quit, the wheels fell off the entire operation.
After the argument, it felt like the wheels fell off.
When the project went over budget, the wheels fell off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fall apart - to break into pieces or become disorganized
  • Synonym: to go belly up - to fail completely
