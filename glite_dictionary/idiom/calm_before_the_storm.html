calm before the storm

1. NOUN

/ kɑm bɪˈfɔr ðə stɔrm / (AmE), / kɑːm bɪˈfɔː ðə stɔːm / (BrE)
a period of quiet or peace before something intense or chaotic happens
peaceful period before chaos

Examples

It was eerily quiet in the house, the calm before the storm of the coming party.
They enjoyed a relaxing lunch together, knowing it was just the calm before the storm at work.
As we waited backstage, the silence felt like the calm before the storm.

Synset Examples

There’s always a calm before the storm in a busy office.
It was the calm before the storm as we prepared for the event.
We enjoyed a quiet dinner, the calm before the storm of guests.
