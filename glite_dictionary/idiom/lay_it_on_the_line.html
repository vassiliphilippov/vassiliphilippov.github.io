to lay it on the line

1. VERB

/ leɪ ɪt ɑn ðə laɪn / (AmE), / leɪ ɪt ɒn ðə laɪn / (BrE)
to speak honestly and directly, often making something clear or risking something important
speak plainly or risk something valuable

Examples

Sometimes you have to lay it on the line if you want people to understand the truth.
She wasn't afraid to lay it on the line during the difficult meeting.
If you want results, lay it on the line and tell them exactly what you expect.

Synset Examples

She laid it on the line during the negotiation.
He laid his reputation on the line for this project.
You should lay it on the line and be clear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speak frankly - to express oneself in a direct and honest way
  • Antonym: to beat around the bush - to avoid speaking directly about something
