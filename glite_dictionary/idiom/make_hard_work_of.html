to make hard work of

1. VERB

/ meɪk hɑrd wɝk ʌv / (AmE), / meɪk hɑːd wɜːk ɒv / (BrE)
to make a task more difficult than it needs to be, often by approaching it inefficiently or overcomplicating it
complicate a task unnecessarily

Examples

She really made hard work of decorating the room, taking hours to finish a simple task.
They make hard work of every meeting by arguing over small details.
You don’t have to make hard work of this; just follow the instructions step by step.

Synset Examples

Don't make hard work of the instructions.
He tends to make hard work of simple tasks.
She always makes hard work of organizing events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overcomplicate - to make something unnecessarily complex
  • Antonym: to simplify - to make something simpler or easier
