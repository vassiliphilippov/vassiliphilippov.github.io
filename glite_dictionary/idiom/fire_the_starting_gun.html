to fire the starting gun

1. VERB

/ ˈfaɪɚ ðə ˈstɑrtɪŋ ɡʌn / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə ðə ˈstɑːtɪŋ ɡʌn / (BrE)
to initiate or begin something, especially a project or process
to start or initiate something

Examples

The manager will fire the starting gun on the new initiative at tomorrow's meeting.
Once the regulations are approved, we can fire the starting gun on construction.
The company hopes to fire the starting gun for this ambitious project by summer.

Synset Examples

Once we finalize the plan, we can fire the starting gun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to kick off - to begin or start something, especially an event or activity
  • Synonym: to begin - to start to happen or exist, or to make something start to happen
  • Antonym: to wrap up - to finish or complete something

Etymology

From the literal act of firing a gun to start a race, used figuratively.
