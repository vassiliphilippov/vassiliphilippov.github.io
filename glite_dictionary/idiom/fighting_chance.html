fighting chance

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪtɪŋ tʃæns / (AmE), / ˈfaɪtɪŋ tʃɑːns / (BrE)
a reasonable possibility of achieving success, especially when success requires effort or is unlikely
reasonable opportunity to succeed

Examples

Even though their team was the underdog, they still had a fighting chance to win.
If we start now, there’s a fighting chance we can finish the project on time.
Early intervention gives the patient a fighting chance at recovery.

Synset Examples

With hard work, they felt they had a fighting chance.
He gave her a fighting chance to win the competition.
This new strategy gives us a fighting chance against the rival.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: outside chance - a very small possibility of success
