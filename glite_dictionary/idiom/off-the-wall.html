off-the-wall

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔf ðə ˈwɔl / (AmE), / ˌɒf ðə ˈwɔːl / (BrE)
very strange, unusual, or unconventional in a way that may seem surprising or bizarre
bizarrely unconventional, eccentric
informal american

Examples

Her off-the-wall ideas always surprise the rest of the team.
The comedian is known for his off-the-wall sense of humor and unpredictable jokes.
That movie was so off-the-wall that I couldn't tell what would happen next.

Synset Examples

restaurants of bizarre design — one like a hat, another like a rabbit
famed for his eccentric spelling
a freakish combination of styles
his off-the-wall antics
the outlandish clothes of teenagers
outre and affected stage antics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outlandish - very strange or unusual; bizarre
  • Synonym: eccentric - unconventional and slightly strange
  • Antonym: conventional - following traditional or accepted standards

Etymology

Originally American slang (mid-20th century), likely from sports or games involving balls rebounding unpredictably from walls.
