to walk on the high rope

1. VERB

/ wɔk ɑn ðə haɪ roʊp / (AmE), / wɔːk ɒn ðə haɪ rəʊp / (BrE)
to be in a situation that is risky, unstable, or requires great care to avoid failure or trouble
be in a risky or precarious situation

Examples

After the scandal, the politician had to walk on the high rope to win back public trust.
In her new management role, she often feels like she's walking on the high rope.
Launching a startup means you must walk on the high rope every day.

Synset Examples

Negotiating with the board can feel like walking on the high rope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to walk a tightrope - to be in a risky or delicate situation
