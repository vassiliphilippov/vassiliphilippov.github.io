to run away from it all

1. VERB

/ rʌn əˈweɪ frəm ɪt ɔl / (AmE), / rʌn əˈweɪ frəm ɪt ɔːl / (BrE)
to escape from one's problems, responsibilities, or the stresses of everyday life, often by going somewhere else temporarily
escape from problems or responsibilities

Examples

After months of stress at work, she decided to run away from it all and booked a trip to the mountains.
Sometimes people dream of running away from it all, but few actually do it.
He packed his bags and ran away from it all for a week of solitude by the sea.

Synset Examples

She felt the urge to run away from it all.
