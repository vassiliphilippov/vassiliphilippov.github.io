to bet the feathers will fly

1. VERB

/ bɛt ðə ˈfɛðərz wɪl flaɪ / (AmE), / bɛt ðə ˈfɛðəz wɪl flaɪ / (BrE)
Used to indicate that a heated argument or conflict is expected.
expect a heated argument or conflict
informal

Examples

If the board tries to cut bonuses, you can bet the feathers will fly.
Whenever rival managers meet, bet the feathers will fly during the negotiations.
If the proposal goes through, bet the feathers will fly at the next meeting.

Synset Examples

Whenever they play poker, you can bet the feathers will fly.
