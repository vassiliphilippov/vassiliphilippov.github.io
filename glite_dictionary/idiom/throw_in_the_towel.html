to throw in the towel

1. VERB

/ θroʊ ɪn ðə ˈtaʊəl / (AmE), / θrəʊ ɪn ðə ˈtaʊəl / (BrE)
to stop trying to do something because you have realized that you cannot succeed; to admit defeat
admit defeat; give up trying

Examples

After several failed attempts to fix the car, he finally decided to throw in the towel.
The company refused to throw in the towel despite their mounting losses.
She was determined not to throw in the towel, no matter how tough things got.

Synset Examples

In the second round, the challenger gave up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give up - to stop trying; to surrender
  • Antonym: to persevere - to continue trying despite difficulty

Etymology

From boxing, where a trainer throws a towel into the ring to signal surrender.

2. VERB

/ θroʊ ɪn ðə ˈtaʊ.əl / (AmE), / θrəʊ ɪn ðə ˈtaʊ.əl / (BrE)
to admit defeat or stop trying, especially after repeated failures
admit defeat; stop trying

Examples

After countless failed experiments, the scientists finally decided to throw in the towel and start over.
Despite losing the first set, she refused to throw in the towel and fought back to win the match.
If we throw in the towel now, all our hard work will have been for nothing.

Synset Examples

She had to throw in the towel after failing repeatedly.
He’s not one to throw in the towel easily.
After many attempts, he decided to throw in the towel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give up - to stop trying; to surrender or admit defeat

Etymology

Originally from the sport of boxing, where throwing a towel into the ring signals surrender.
