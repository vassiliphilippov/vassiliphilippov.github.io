down the pan

1. ADV

/ daʊn ðə pæn / (AmE), / daʊn ðə pan / (BrE)
used to say that something is ruined, wasted, or fails completely
became ruined or wasted
british

Examples

The company’s years of hard work went down the pan after the new regulations were introduced.
If we lose this contract, all our investments could go down the pan.
Her chances of winning the competition went down the pan when she missed the final round.

Synset Examples

The budget cuts sent our expansion plans down the pan.
Their relationship went down the pan after the argument.
His chances of promotion went down the pan with that mistake.
His reputation went down the pan after that scandal.
Their plans for the project went down the pan.
The whole plan went down the pan after the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: down the drain - used to say something is wasted or ruined
