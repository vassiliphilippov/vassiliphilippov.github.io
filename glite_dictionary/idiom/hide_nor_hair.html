hide nor hair

1. NOUN

/ haɪd nɔr hɛr / (AmE), / haɪd nɔː hɛə / (BrE)
no trace or sign of someone or something
no trace or sign (idiom)

Examples

I've not seen hide nor hair of my keys since yesterday.
Police searched for hours without finding hide nor hair of the missing hiker.
She waited all afternoon but there was no hide nor hair of the delivery truck.

Synset Examples

She searched the house but found no hide nor hair of her missing cat.
I haven't seen hide nor hair of him lately.

Etymology

From the literal words 'hide' (skin of an animal) and 'hair,' signifying complete absence, dating from the 19th century.
