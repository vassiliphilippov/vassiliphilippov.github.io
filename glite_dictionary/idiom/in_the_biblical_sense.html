in the biblical sense

1. ADV

/ ɪn ðə ˈbɪblɪkəl sɛns / (AmE), / ɪn ðə ˈbɪblɪkəl sɛns / (BrE)
used euphemistically to refer to sexual intercourse, especially when saying two people have had sex with each other.
euphemism for having sex

Examples

He humorously claimed they knew each other in the biblical sense after only a week of dating.
It was clear they were close, but not in the biblical sense.
She insisted their friendship was not in the biblical sense, just purely platonic.

Synset Examples

He joked that they knew each other in the biblical sense after their first date.
Just because they went out doesn't mean they know each other in the biblical sense.
They joked about their relationship, not serious in the biblical sense.
They were just friends, not together in the biblical sense.
He meant they were close, but not in the biblical sense.
They’ve been dating for months but don’t know each other in the biblical sense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sleep with - to have sexual intercourse with someone
  • Antonym: platonically - in a non-sexual way

Etymology

Refers to the style of wording in the Bible, where 'to know' someone often implied sexual intercourse.
