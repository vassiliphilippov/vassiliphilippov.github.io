stone's throw

1. NOUN

/ stoʊnz θroʊ / (AmE), / stəʊnz θrəʊ / (BrE)
a short distance away; a distance very close to a particular point
a short distance

Examples

The school is just a stone's throw from my house.
They live only a stone's throw away from the park.
The restaurant is a stone's throw from the beach, making it very convenient.

Synset Examples

it's only a step to the drugstore

Related Terms

  • Synonym: step - a short distance
  • Antonym: mile - a long distance

Etymology

From the old practice of describing a short distance as being within the distance a stone could be thrown.
