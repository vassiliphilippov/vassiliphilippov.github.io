buyer beware

1. X

/ ˈbaɪər bɪˈwɛr / (AmE), / ˈbaɪə bɪˈweə / (BrE)
used to warn that a person making a purchase should be cautious and aware that the responsibility for checking the quality or suitability of goods lies with the buyer.
warning to be cautious when buying

Examples

When shopping for antiques, always remember buyer beware.
After a bad experience with the car dealership, he now lives by the phrase buyer beware.
Online marketplaces still operate under the rule of buyer beware.

Synset Examples

He learned the hard way that 'buyer beware' is often true.
The sign read 'buyer beware' due to hidden fees.
With this deal, it's definitely a case of buyer beware.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caveat emptor - the principle that the buyer alone is responsible for checking the quality of goods before a purchase

Etymology

Translation of Latin 'caveat emptor', a legal principle meaning 'let the buyer beware'.
