to fly solo

1. VERB

/ flaɪ ˈsoʊloʊ / (AmE), / flaɪ ˈsəʊləʊ / (BrE)
to work, act, or operate independently without help or support from others
act independently

Examples

After years of working with a partner, she decided to fly solo and start her own business.
He wanted to fly solo for the journey, even though his friends offered to come along.
Many artists choose to fly solo at some point in their careers to explore new styles.

Synset Examples

I decided to fly solo on this project.
She prefers to fly solo rather than work in teams.
He flew solo during the entire trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go it alone - to do something without help from others

Etymology

Originally from aviation, where a pilot flies an aircraft alone.
