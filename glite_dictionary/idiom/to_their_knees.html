to their knees

1. X

/ tə ðɛr niz / (AmE), / tə ðeə niːz / (BrE)
brought into a state where one is weakened, defeated, or forced to submit, often by some hardship or difficulty
reduced to weakness or submission

Examples

The scandal brought the government to their knees and forced new elections.
After months of drought, the farmers were brought to their knees financially.
The relentless pressure from competitors finally brought the company to their knees.

Synset Examples

The economic crisis has brought many businesses to their knees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on its knees - in a severely weakened or ruined state
