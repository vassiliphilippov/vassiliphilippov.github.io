to save for a rainy day

1. VERB

/ seɪv fər ə ˈreɪni deɪ / (AmE), / seɪv fə ə ˈreɪni deɪ / (BrE)
to put aside money or resources for a time when they are needed unexpectedly or in the future
set aside resources for future need

Examples

My grandmother always told me to save for a rainy day in case something unexpected happened.
Even though he has a steady job, he tries to save for a rainy day every month.
It's wise to save for a rainy day rather than spend all your earnings.

Synset Examples

They saved their slopdosh for a rainy day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: put by - to save money for the future
  • Antonym: spendthrift - someone who spends money recklessly; opposite attitude to saving
