limb of Satan

1. NOUN

/ lɪm əv ˈseɪtən / (AmE), / lɪm əv ˈseɪtn̩ / (BrE)
a person, act, or influence regarded as especially wicked or evil
someone or something especially wicked or evil

Examples

Some critics called the villain in the play a true limb of Satan.
He was accused of being a limb of Satan because of his malevolent deeds.
Old stories often depict witches as a limb of Satan corrupting the village.

Synset Examples

She referred to his reckless behavior as the limb of Satan.
He joked that his bad habits were the limb of Satan.
The corrupt politician was seen as a limb of Satan in the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiend - an evil spirit or wicked person
  • Antonym: saint - a person of great holiness or virtue

Etymology

From the biblical notion of Satan as the personification of evil; 'limb' figuratively means an agent or extension of a person or force.
