to tear someone a new one

1. VERB

/ tɛɚ ˈsʌmwʌn ə ˈnu ˌwʌn / (AmE), / tɛə ˈsʌmwʌn ə ˈnjuː ˌwʌn / (BrE)
to scold or criticize someone very harshly, often with strong language or anger
scold or criticize harshly (idiom)
informal

Examples

If you miss another deadline, the boss will tear you a new one.
The coach tore them a new one after their poor performance in the game.
He was torn a new one in front of everyone for forgetting the report.

Synset Examples

She tore him a new one for being late again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chew someone out - to reprimand someone severely
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration for someone
