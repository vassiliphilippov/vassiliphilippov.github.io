to tear (someone) a new one

1. VERB

/ tɛr ə nuː wʌn / (AmE), / tɛə ə njuː wʌn / (BrE)
to reprimand or criticize someone very harshly, often in an angry or severe manner
to scold or criticize severely
informal vulgar

Examples

If you miss another deadline, the boss will tear you a new one.
She tore him a new one after he crashed the car.
If I forget her birthday, my wife will definitely tear me a new one.

Synset Examples

If you miss the meeting, I'll tear you a new one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chew (someone) out - to scold or reprimand someone harshly
  • Synonym: tell (someone) off - to speak angrily to someone for doing something wrong
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
