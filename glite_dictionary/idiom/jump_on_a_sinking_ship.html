to jump on a sinking ship

1. VERB

/ dʒʌmp ɑn ə ˈsɪŋkɪŋ ʃɪp / (AmE), / dʒʌmp ɒn ə ˈsɪŋkɪŋ ʃɪp / (BrE)
to join or become involved with a failing or doomed project or venture, often despite obvious risks
join a failing or doomed venture

Examples

Many cautioned against buying shares, saying it would be like jumping on a sinking ship.
By joining that unpopular startup, he was really jumping on a sinking ship.
Investors who jump on a sinking ship often end up regretting their decisions.

Synset Examples

Investing in that company is like jumping on a sinking ship.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: jump ship - to leave a failing or problematic situation
