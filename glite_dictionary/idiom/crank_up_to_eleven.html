to crank up to eleven

1. VERB

/ kræŋk ʌp tu ɪˈlɛvən / (AmE), / kraŋk ʌp tuː ɪˈlɛvən / (BrE)
to increase something to its maximum or an extreme level; to take something as far as possible
increase to the maximum or extreme
informal

Examples

The crowd's excitement was cranked up to eleven as the band walked on stage.
He always cranks up to eleven when he's talking about his favorite movies.
During the final minutes, the basketball game was cranked up to eleven with non-stop action.

Synset Examples

The party vibe was cranked up to eleven last night.
Her enthusiasm was turned up to eleven during the presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn up the heat - to increase the intensity or pressure
  • Antonym: to dial down - to decrease intensity or force

Etymology

Popularized by the 1984 film 'This Is Spinal Tap', where an amplifier's volume could go beyond the usual maximum setting of ten.
