to lay one's tongue to

1. VERB

/ leɪ wʌnz tʌŋ tuː / (AmE), / leɪ wʌnz tʌŋ tʊ / (BrE)
to be able to pronounce or articulate (a word or phrase), often with difficulty
to pronounce or articulate with effort

Examples

Despite years of study, he still couldn't lay his tongue to the correct French pronunciation.
She was embarrassed when she failed to lay her tongue to the technical jargon.
Even after hearing it several times, I struggled to lay my tongue to the unusual name.

Synset Examples

He couldn't lay his tongue to the unfamiliar word.
She struggled to lay her tongue to the complicated phrase.
