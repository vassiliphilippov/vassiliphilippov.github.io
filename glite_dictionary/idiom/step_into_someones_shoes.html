to step into someone's shoes

1. VERB

/ stɛp ˈɪntu ˈsʌmwʌnz ʃuz / (AmE), / stɛp ˈɪntuː ˈsʌmwənz ʃuːz / (BrE)
to imagine or understand another person's situation or experience by considering things from their perspective
imagine another's situation or experience

Examples

Before criticizing your friend, try to step into someone's shoes and see things from their side.
Leaders must step into someone's shoes to truly empathize with their team members.
It's an important skill to step into someone's shoes when resolving conflicts.

Synset Examples

It's hard to step into someone's shoes without understanding their struggles.
Try to step into her shoes before judging her decisions.
He found it challenging to step into his father's shoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to put oneself in someone's shoes - to imagine oneself in another person's position or situation
