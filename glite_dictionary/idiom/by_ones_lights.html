by one's lights

1. ADP

/ baɪ wʌnz laɪts / (AmE), / baɪ wʌnz laɪts / (BrE)
according to one's own beliefs, understanding, or judgement
according to one's beliefs or judgement

Examples

She acted by her lights and made the best decision she could.
He always does what he thinks is right, by his lights.
They judged the situation by their lights and chose a cautious approach.

Synset Examples

She did the best she could, by her lights.

2. ADV

/ baɪ wʌnz laɪts / (AmE), / baɪ wʌnz laɪts / (BrE)
according to one's own understanding or beliefs; as one sees fit
according to one's beliefs or understanding

Examples

He always acted by his lights, even when others disagreed with his choices.
She judged the decision by her lights, believing it to be the right thing to do.
They made that call by their lights and did what they thought best.

Synset Examples

They acted, by their lights, to help the community.

Etymology

From the word 'lights' in the sense of 'mental faculties' or 'understanding', dating from the 1600s.
