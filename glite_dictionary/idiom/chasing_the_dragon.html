to chasing the dragon

1. VERB

/ ˈtʃeɪsɪŋ ðə ˈdræɡən / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪsɪŋ ðə ˈdræɡən / (BrE)
Using drugs, especially by inhaling the vapor from heated opiates; also, persistently pursuing an unattainable or elusive goal, often to one's detriment.
drug addiction or futile pursuit
informal slang

Examples

After years of chasing the dragon, he finally sought help to overcome his addiction.
For some, trying to relive past glory is like chasing the dragon—it never quite feels the same.
The documentary revealed the dangers of chasing the dragon and its toll on communities.

Synset Examples

Chasing the dragon can lead to serious addiction problems.
She realized that chasing the dragon would ruin her life.
He decided to stop chasing the dragon for good.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pursuing a chimera - Attempting to achieve an impossible or illusory goal
  • Antonym: giving up - Ceasing one's efforts or pursuits

Etymology

Originally referring to the movement of opiate vapor (like a dragon) when heated for inhalation.
