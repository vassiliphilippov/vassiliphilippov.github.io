to air dirty laundry

1. VERB

/ ɛr ˈdɜrti ˈlɔn(d)ri / (AmE), / eə ˈdɜːti ˈlɔːndri / (BrE)
to discuss or reveal private or embarrassing personal matters in public, especially when it would be more appropriate to keep them private
reveal personal or embarrassing matters in public

Examples

It's best not to air dirty laundry during a family gathering.
Their decision to air dirty laundry on social media surprised their friends.
The politician refused to air dirty laundry about his colleagues during the debate.
