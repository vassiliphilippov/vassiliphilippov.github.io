to take the bull by the horns

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə bʊl baɪ ðə hɔrnz / (AmE), / teɪk ðə bʊl baɪ ðə hɔːnz / (BrE)
to confront a difficult or challenging situation boldly and directly, without avoidance
confront a challenge directly

Examples

It's time to take the bull by the horns and address the team's falling morale.
When the budget cuts were announced, she decided to take the bull by the horns and reorganize the department.
Rather than waiting for someone else to fix the problem, he chose to take the bull by the horns and lead the solution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to face the music - accept the consequences of one's actions
  • Antonym: to bury one's head in the sand - ignore or avoid a difficult situation
