to stay out of someone's beeswax

1. VERB

/ steɪ aʊt əv sʌmwʌnz ˈbiːzwæks / (AmE), / steɪ aʊt əv sʌmwʌnz ˈbiːzwæks / (BrE)
(informal) Used to tell someone to mind their own business or not to interfere.
mind your own business (informal idiom)
informal slang american

Examples

She told her brother to stay out of her beeswax when he asked about her phone call.
If you can't stay out of my beeswax, I'll have to leave the room.
Some parents wish their children would just stay out of their beeswax when they're working from home.

Synset Examples

Stay out of my beeswax and let me work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mind your own business - To not interfere in someone else's affairs

Etymology

Playful alteration of 'business' to 'beeswax,' popular in American slang from early 20th century.
