to keep watch

1. VERB

/ kip wɑtʃ / (AmE), / kiːp wɒtʃ / (BrE)
to remain alert and observe for security, safety, or protection; to stand guard or closely supervise
to stand guard or stay alert for safety

Examples

The nurse was asked to keep watch over the patient throughout the night.
They took turns to keep watch while the others slept in the tent.
He stayed by the door to keep watch in case anyone tried to enter.

Synset Examples

The guard was asked to keep watch at the entrance.
He decided to keep watch over his younger siblings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guard - to watch over in order to protect or control
