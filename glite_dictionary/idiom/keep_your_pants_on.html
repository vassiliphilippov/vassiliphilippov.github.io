to keep your pants on

1. VERB

/ kip jɚ pænts ɑn / (AmE), / kiːp jɔː pænts ɒn / (BrE)
used to tell someone to remain calm and not become impatient or excited
remain calm; don't get impatient
informal

Examples

Keep your pants on; I'll be there in a minute.
She told her brother to keep your pants on when he started complaining about the wait.
If you can just keep your pants on, the results will be ready soon.

Synset Examples

Just keep your pants on; we'll get to the fun part soon.
There's no need to rush; keep your pants on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to calm down - become quiet or less agitated
  • Synonym: hold your horses - wait; slow down, don't be impatient
