hot air

1. NOUN

/ hɑt ɛr / (AmE), / hɒt eə / (BrE)
statements that are loud, boastful, or persuasive but lack real substance, meaning, or sincerity; empty talk
boastful or empty talk

Examples

Politicians are often accused of speaking a lot of hot air during election campaigns.
His promises sounded convincing, but they turned out to be nothing but hot air.
We need real solutions, not just more hot air from management.

Synset Examples

mere rhetoric

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empty talk - statements without substance or meaning
  • Synonym: rhetoric - speech or writing intended to be effective but regarded as lacking in sincerity or meaningful content
