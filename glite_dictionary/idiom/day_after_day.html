day after day

1. ADV

/ ˌdeɪ ˈæftɚ ˈdeɪ / (AmE), / ˌdeɪ ˈɑːftə ˈdeɪ / (BrE)
used to express that something happens repeatedly or continuously over many days, often suggesting monotony or persistence.
repeatedly on consecutive days

Examples

He went to the hospital day after day to visit his friend.
Day after day, the rain kept falling without a break.
She practiced the piano day after day until she improved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: day in, day out - happening every day for a long time, especially in a monotonous way

2. ADV

/ ˈdeɪ ˌæftər ˈdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ ˌɑːftə ˈdeɪ / (BrE)
repeatedly or continuously over a period of days, especially in a monotonous or relentless way
repeatedly over many days

Examples

She went to the hospital day after day to visit her friend.
He practices the piano day after day to improve his skills.
The rain fell day after day without stopping.

Synset Examples

She faced challenges day after day with determination.
