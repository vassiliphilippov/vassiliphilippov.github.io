to know someone from Adam

1. VERB

/ noʊ ˈsʌmwʌn frəm ˈædəm / (AmE), / nəʊ ˈsʌmwən frəm ˈædəm / (BrE)
(informal) To not know or recognize someone at all; to have no previous knowledge of someone.
not know someone at all (idiom)
informal

Examples

I've never met him—I don't know him from Adam.
She couldn't know me from Adam, but she still greeted me with a smile.
He asked if I trusted the new guy, but I don't know him from Adam.

Synset Examples

I don't know him from Adam, so I can't trust him.
He claimed to be an expert, but I know him from Adam.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to know - to have knowledge or awareness of someone or something

Etymology

Reference to Adam, the biblical first man, who would be unknown to people today.
