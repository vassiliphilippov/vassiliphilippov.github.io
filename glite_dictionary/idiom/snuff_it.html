to snuff it

1. VERB

/ snʌf ɪt / (AmE), / snʌf ɪt / (BrE)
(informal, slang) To die; to cease living.
die; cease living (informal)
informal slang british

Examples

He was only forty-five when he snuffed it unexpectedly.
Some people are terrified they’ll snuff it before finishing their bucket list.
The old television finally snuffed it after years of service.

Synset Examples

She died from cancer
The children perished in the fire
The patient went peacefully
The old guy kicked the bucket at the age of 102

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to kick the bucket - to die (informal; idiom)
  • Synonym: to pass away - to die (more gentle/formal)
  • Synonym: die - to cease living

Etymology

Originally British slang, possibly from 'snuff out' (like extinguishing a candle).
