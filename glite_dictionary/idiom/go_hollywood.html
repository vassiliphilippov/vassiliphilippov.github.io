to go Hollywood

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈhɑlɪˌwʊd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈhɒlɪˌwʊd / (BrE)
to pursue fame and fortune, especially by adopting the behaviors or lifestyle associated with the Hollywood entertainment industry
pursue fame and fortune, adopt Hollywood lifestyle

Examples

After releasing their first hit song, the band decided to go Hollywood and moved to Los Angeles.
Some critics say she changed after she started to go Hollywood and chase bigger roles.
He used to be so down-to-earth, but lately he seems to have gone Hollywood.

Synset Examples

They decided to go Hollywood after their big success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go for broke - to risk everything to achieve success
  • Synonym: to sell out - to compromise one’s values for personal gain

Etymology

Named after Hollywood, a district in Los Angeles renowned as the center of the American film industry.
