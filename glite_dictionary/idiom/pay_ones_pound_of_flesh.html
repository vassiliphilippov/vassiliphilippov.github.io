to pay one's pound of flesh

1. VERB

/ peɪ wʌnz paʊnd əv flɛʃ / (AmE), / peɪ wʌnz paʊnd əv fleʃ / (BrE)
to endure or fulfil a severe demand, sacrifice, or penalty, especially one that is harsh or unreasonable
endure a harsh or excessive demand or sacrifice

Examples

After months of overtime, she felt she had paid her pound of flesh at the new job.
Despite his success, he knew he had paid his pound of flesh in stress and lost time.
Sometimes, it seems every promotion comes only after you've paid your pound of flesh.

Synset Examples

He felt like he had paid his pound of flesh already.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pay the price - to suffer consequences or costs for something

Etymology

From Shakespeare's 'The Merchant of Venice,' where a 'pound of flesh' refers to a legal but harsh demand.
