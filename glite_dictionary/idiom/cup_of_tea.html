cup of tea

1. NOUN

/ kʌp əv tiː / (AmE), / kʌp əv tiː / (BrE)
something that you like or are good at; something that suits your tastes or interests.
something one likes or is good at

Examples

Traveling alone is not really my cup of tea, but I’ll do it if I have to.
Classical music has always been her cup of tea.
Mathematics was never his cup of tea at school.

Synset Examples

chemistry is not my cup of tea
his bag now is learning to play golf
marriage was scarcely his dish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bag - an activity or subject that someone is interested in or good at (slang)
  • Synonym: dish - something that someone likes or excels at (chiefly in negative contexts, dated)

Etymology

From the literal beverage tea, possibly British in origin as tea is a common everyday drink; transferred figurative sense attested from the early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ kʌp əv ti / (AmE), / kʌp əv tiː / (BrE)
something that a person enjoys or prefers; a subject or activity that suits someone's taste
something enjoyed or preferred

Examples

Reading science fiction novels has always been my cup of tea.
Jogging at sunrise isn't really my cup of tea.
If adventure sports are your cup of tea, you'll love bungee jumping.

Synset Examples

Classical music is someone's cup of tea, for sure.
Cooking is not really someone's cup of tea.
Hiking isn't everyone's cup of tea, but I love it.
She took a cup of tea to relax.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: not one's cup of tea - something a person does not enjoy or prefer
