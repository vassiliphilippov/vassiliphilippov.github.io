cross my heart and hope to die

1. X

/ krɔs maɪ hɑrt ənd hoʊp tə daɪ / (AmE), / krɒs maɪ hɑːt ənd həʊp tə daɪ / (BrE)
used to emphasize that one is telling the truth or will keep a promise
to emphasize sincerity of a promise

Examples

I'll keep your secret, cross my heart and hope to die.
She told her son she'd never leave him, cross my heart and hope to die.
He promised, cross my heart and hope to die, that he would be home by midnight.

Synset Examples

I’ll always be there for you, cross my heart and hope to die.
She swore she'd keep her promise, cross my heart and hope to die.
He promised he wouldn't tell anyone, cross my heart and hope to die.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to swear - to promise or state emphatically that something is true
  • Antonym: to break a promise - to fail to do what one said one would do
