alphabet soup

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.fə.bɛt suːp / (AmE), / ˈæl.fə.bɛt suːp / (BrE)
a confusing mixture of many letters, abbreviations, or acronyms, especially in the names of organizations or systems
confusing mix of abbreviations or acronyms

Examples

After the merger, the company was left with an alphabet soup of different policies and procedures.
The government's financial sector is full of regulatory bodies, forming an alphabet soup of acronyms.
New employees are often confused by the alphabet soup of technical jargon and abbreviations.

Synset Examples

Roosevelt created an alphabet soup of federal agencies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jumble - an untidy or disorganized collection or mixture of things
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand

2. NOUN

/ ˈælfəˌbɛt ˌsuːp / (AmE), / ˈælfəbɛt ˌsuːp / (BrE)
a confusing mixture of abbreviations, acronyms, or jargon that is difficult to understand
confusing mix of acronyms or jargon

Examples

The financial report was full of alphabet soup that made it hard to follow.
Government agencies often communicate in an alphabet soup that outsiders can't easily understand.
I got lost in the alphabet soup of technical terms during the conference.

Synset Examples

Her resume was filled with an alphabet soup of certifications.
The project had an alphabet soup of acronyms that confused everyone.
He loves using an alphabet soup of jargon to impress clients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jargon - special words or expressions used by a profession or group
  • Synonym: gibberish - unintelligible or meaningless speech or writing

Etymology

From the literal 'alphabet soup' (soup with pasta shaped like letters), used figuratively since the 20th century.
