to fit the crime

1. VERB

/ fɪt ðə kraɪm / (AmE), / fɪt ðə kraɪm / (BrE)
to be appropriate in severity or nature to the offense committed, especially referring to punishment
be appropriate to the offense committed

Examples

Many believe that the penalty for theft should fit the crime rather than being overly harsh.
The new sentencing guidelines aim to ensure punishments fit the crime committed by each offender.
She argued in court that justice requires consequences to fit the crime fairly.

Synset Examples

They agreed that punishment should fit the crime.
