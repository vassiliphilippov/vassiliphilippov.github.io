to get one's groove on

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt wʌnz ɡruːv ɑn / (AmE), / ɡɛt wʌnz ɡruːv ɒn / (BrE)
to enjoy oneself, especially by dancing with enthusiasm
enjoy oneself dancing
informal american

Examples

Everyone was ready to get their groove on as soon as the DJ started playing upbeat music.
After a stressful week, she went out with friends to get her groove on at the club.
He told us he can't wait to get his groove on at the wedding reception.

Synset Examples

He loves to get his groove on at every party.
She finally found a way to get her groove on again.
Let’s hit the dance floor and get our groove on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to let loose - to relax and behave freely, especially in a fun way

Etymology

From 'groove' (a rhythmic quality in music or dance), popularized in American English during the late 20th century.
