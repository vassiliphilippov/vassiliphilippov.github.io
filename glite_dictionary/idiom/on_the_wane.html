on the wane

1. ADJ

/ ɑn ðə weɪn / (AmE), / ɒn ðə weɪn / (BrE)
Becoming weaker, less important, or less widespread; in decline.
in decline; decreasing

Examples

Interest in traditional print newspapers is on the wane as more people turn to digital media.
Membership in the club has been on the wane since the pandemic started.
The influence of the old political party is clearly on the wane in recent elections.

Synset Examples

Public interest in the topic is on the wane.
The festival's attendance is on the wane every year.
His popularity is on the wane these days.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: on the rise - increasing; becoming more widespread or important
  • Synonym: declining - becoming weaker or less important

Etymology

From 'wane', meaning to decrease or diminish, originally from Old English 'wanian'.
