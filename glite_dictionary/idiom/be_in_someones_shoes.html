to be in someone's shoes

1. VERB

/ bi ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz ʃuz / (AmE), / biː ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz ʃuːz / (BrE)
to imagine oneself in another person's situation and try to understand their feelings or perspective
imagine oneself in another’s situation

Examples

It's never easy to be in someone's shoes when they're going through a hard time.
Before judging others, try to be in their shoes for a moment.
She asked him how he'd feel if he had to be in her shoes.

Synset Examples

It's tough being in her shoes right now.
He couldn’t put himself in her shoes and understand her pain.
Try to put yourself in her shoes before judging.
It’s hard to stand in someone’s shoes without knowing their story.
You should stand in his shoes to understand his decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: put oneself in someone else's shoes - imagine experiencing another person's situation
  • Synonym: to stand in someone's shoes - to occupy another person's position or circumstances
