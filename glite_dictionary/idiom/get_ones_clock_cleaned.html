to get one's clock cleaned

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt wʌnz klɑk klin̩d / (AmE), / ɡɛt wʌnz klɒk kliːnd / (BrE)
to be defeated or beaten comprehensively, especially in a competition or argument
be soundly defeated
informal

Examples

If you try to play chess against her, you’ll get your clock cleaned.
He got his clock cleaned in the debate last night.
Without proper training, the team will get their clock cleaned by the champions.

Synset Examples

You’ll get your clock cleaned if you’re not careful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be beaten - to be defeated in a contest or fight
  • Synonym: to trounce - to defeat someone easily and completely

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt wʌnz klɑk kliːnd / (AmE), / ɡɛt wʌnz klɒk kliːnd / (BrE)
to be thoroughly defeated or outperformed, especially in a competition or confrontation
to be thoroughly defeated
informal

Examples

After losing every debate so far, Mark really got his clock cleaned in the finals.
The team went into the match confident, but ended up getting their clocks cleaned by the underdogs.
If you don't prepare properly, you're going to get your clock cleaned out there.

Synset Examples

She got her clock cleaned in the last round of trivia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be thrashed - to be beaten soundly or severely
  • Antonym: win - to be victorious
