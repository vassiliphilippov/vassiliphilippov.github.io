to ring a warning bell

1. VERB

/ rɪŋ ə ˈwɔrnɪŋ bɛl / (AmE), / rɪŋ ə ˈwɔːnɪŋ bɛl / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel concern or suspect that there may be a problem
warn of possible trouble

Examples

The sudden drop in profits rang a warning bell for the company's investors.
When I noticed the outdated fire equipment, it rang a warning bell in my mind.
The surgeon's hesitation rang a warning bell for the patient.

Synset Examples

The low sales figures rang a warning bell for management.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raise a red flag - to signal a warning or alert
  • Synonym: set alarm bells ringing - to indicate danger or cause concern
