to pass the torch

1. VERB

/ pæs ðə tɔrtʃ / (AmE), / pɑːs ðə tɔːtʃ / (BrE)
to transfer responsibility, leadership, or a role to another person, especially to a successor
hand over leadership or responsibility

Examples

After twenty years as CEO, he decided to pass the torch to his trusted deputy.
It's important for experienced teachers to pass the torch to the next generation of educators.
With the company's future secure, she felt ready to pass the torch and retire.

Synset Examples

It's time to pass the torch to the next generation.
She was proud to pass the torch after years of service.
Young leaders are ready to pass the torch in our organization.
The team passed the torch to the new leaders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hand over the reins - to give control or responsibility to someone else
  • Synonym: to step down - to resign from a position of leadership

Etymology

From the ancient tradition of the Olympic torch relay, symbolizing the transfer of duty or honor.
