misters before sisters

1. X

/ ˈmɪstərz bɪˈfɔr ˈsɪstərz / (AmE), / ˈmɪstəz bɪˈfɔː ˈsɪstəz / (BrE)
(humorous, informal) The idea that relationships or loyalty to male friends or partners should take precedence over friendships with women.
prioritizing men over female friends (humorous)
informal

Examples

At college, he liked to say misters before sisters whenever his friends asked him to hang out.
She broke the misters before sisters rule and decided to spend the night with her girlfriends instead.
He laughed and quoted misters before sisters when his best friend called during date night.

Synset Examples

He joked about misters before sisters at the wedding.
She followed the misters before sisters rule and skipped the girls' night.
Her friends teased her about misters before sisters.

Etymology

Modeled on the structure of similar colloquial expressions such as "bros before hoes".
