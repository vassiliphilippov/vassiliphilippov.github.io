to write checks you can't cash

1. VERB

/ raɪt tʃɛks ju kænt kæʃ / (AmE), / raɪt tʃɛks juː kɑːnt kæʃ / (BrE)
to make promises or commitments that one is unable or unwilling to fulfill
make unfulfillable promises or commitments
informal american

Examples

If you continue to write checks you can't cash, no one will trust your promises.
She warned him not to write checks he can't cash in front of his clients.
It's easy to write checks you can't cash when you're feeling confident, but much harder to deliver.

Synset Examples

He tends to write checks he can't cash every time.
Don't write checks you can't cash with your promises.
You shouldn't write checks you can't cash during negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overpromise - to promise more than one can deliver
  • Antonym: keep your word - to fulfill a promise or commitment

Etymology

Based on the literal act of writing a bank check without funds to cover it, first recorded in American English slang in the 20th century.
