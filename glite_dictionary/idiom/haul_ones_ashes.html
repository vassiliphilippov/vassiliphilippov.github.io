to haul one's ashes

1. VERB

/ hɔl wʌnz ˈæʃɪz / (AmE), / hɔːl wʌnz ˈæʃɪz / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly US) To go home, especially when tired or after a long or exhausting experience.
go home when tired, chiefly US informal
informal american

Examples

After working a double shift, I couldn't wait to haul my ashes home.
It's getting late—maybe we should haul our ashes before midnight.
By the time the movie ended, I was ready to haul my ashes back to bed.

Synset Examples
