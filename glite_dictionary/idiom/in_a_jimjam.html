in a jimjam

1. X

/ ɪn ə ˈdʒɪmˌdʒæm / (AmE), / ɪn ə ˈdʒɪmˌdʒæm / (BrE)
In a confused or anxious state.
feeling confused or anxious
informal

Examples

He always gets in a jimjam before big presentations at work.
There's no need to be in a jimjam about the upcoming test; you'll do fine.
Whenever she misplaces her keys, she ends up in a jimjam for hours.

Synset Examples

After the argument, she felt quite in a jimjam.
Don’t get yourself in a jimjam over trivial matters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in a tizzy - in a state of nervous excitement or confusion
  • Synonym: in a flap - worried or nervous state
