nose to the grindstone

1. X

/ noʊz tə ðə ˈɡraɪndstoʊn / (AmE), / nəʊz tə ðə ˈɡraɪndstəʊn / (BrE)
to work hard and continuously, often with focused determination and persistence
work hard and steadily

Examples

During tax season, the accountants keep their nose to the grindstone to meet all the deadlines.
If you want to get that promotion, it’s time to put your nose to the grindstone.
She kept her nose to the grindstone throughout medical school and graduated at the top of her class.

Synset Examples

He kept his nose to the grindstone during finals.
Working hard, she had her nose to the grindstone all week.
It's time to put your nose to the grindstone and finish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: work hard - put in a lot of effort and diligence
  • Antonym: to slack off - work less hard or be lazy

Etymology

Refers to the literal act of grinding, with the grindstone used in sharpening blades, and the image of someone working close and attentively.
