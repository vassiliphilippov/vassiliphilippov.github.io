to go down the wrong way

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ daʊn ðə rɔŋ weɪ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ daʊn ðə rɒŋ weɪ / (BrE)
(of words or actions) to be received with displeasure or misunderstanding; to have an unintended negative impact.
be poorly received or misunderstood

Examples

Her remarks about the project went down the wrong way with the team and sparked an argument.
Be careful how you phrase your feedback so it doesn't go down the wrong way.
I think my joke went down the wrong way at dinner last night.

Synset Examples

If you take that route, you might go down the wrong way.
That comment really went down the wrong way with her.
He realized his joke went down the wrong way.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rub someone the wrong way - to irritate or annoy someone unintentionally
