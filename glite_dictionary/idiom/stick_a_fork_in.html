to stick a fork in

1. VERB

/ stɪk ə fɔrk ɪn / (AmE), / stɪk ə fɔːk ɪn / (BrE)
to check whether food is cooked by poking it with a fork; also, to indicate that someone or something is finished or done
test food for doneness or declare something finished
informal

Examples

You can stick a fork in the potatoes to see if they're soft enough to eat.
The team was so far behind that you could stick a fork in them by halftime.
After eight hours of meetings, I told my coworkers to stick a fork in me – I was finished.

Synset Examples
