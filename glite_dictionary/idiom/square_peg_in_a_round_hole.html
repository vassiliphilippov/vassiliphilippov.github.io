square peg in a round hole

1. NOUN

/ skwɛr pɛg ɪn ə raʊnd hoʊl / (AmE), / skwɛə pɛg ɪn ə raʊnd həʊl / (BrE)
a person who does not fit easily into a particular position or situation
someone out of place or unsuitable

Examples

She always felt like a square peg in a round hole at family gatherings.
The company’s rigid culture made him a square peg in a round hole.
Trying to make a creative thinker follow strict rules is like placing a square peg in a round hole.

Synset Examples

Trying to fit her creativity into that structure felt like being a square peg in a round hole.
He felt like a square peg in a round hole at that job.
The new strategy made him feel like a square peg in a round hole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misfit - a person who does not fit easily into a particular group or situation
  • Synonym: fish out of water - someone who feels uncomfortable or out of place

Etymology

Refers to the physical impossibility of fitting a square peg into a round hole, metaphorically describing mismatch or unsuitability. First recorded in the early 19th century.
