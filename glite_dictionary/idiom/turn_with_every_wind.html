to turn with every wind

1. VERB

/ tɝn wɪð ˈɛvri wɪnd / (AmE), / tɜːn wɪð ˈɛvri wɪnd / (BrE)
to change one's opinions or actions frequently according to changing circumstances or influences, often lacking steadfast principles
change opinions or direction frequently

Examples

Politicians who turn with every wind often lose the trust of their voters.
It is hard to respect someone who will turn with every wind just to fit in.
She accused him of being unreliable because he tends to turn with every wind when making decisions.

Synset Examples

She tends to turn with every wind in politics.
Her decisions seem to turn with every wind of change.
He doesn't have a strong opinion and turns with every wind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blow with the wind - adopt the opinions or attitudes that are most popular at a given time
  • Synonym: fickle - changing frequently, especially with regard to loyalties or affections
