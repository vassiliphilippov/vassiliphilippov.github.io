Elvis has left the building

1. X

/ ˈɛlvɪs hæz lɛft ðə ˈbɪldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɛlvɪs hæz lɛft ðə ˈbɪldɪŋ / (BrE)
used to indicate that an event or activity has ended and there is nothing more to expect
used to mean something is over

Examples

After the meeting ended, someone joked, "Elvis has left the building," signaling it was time to go.
The politician finished his speech and the host announced, "Elvis has left the building."
Once the sale ended, customers knew Elvis has left the building—there were no more deals.

Synset Examples

With that announcement, everyone knew Elvis has left the building.
The concert was over, and Elvis has left the building.
After the final act, you could say Elvis has left the building.

Etymology

Originated from announcements at Elvis Presley concerts to disperse crowds, now used figuratively.
