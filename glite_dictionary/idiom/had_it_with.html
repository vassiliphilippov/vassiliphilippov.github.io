to had it with

1. VERB

/ ˈhæd ɪt wɪθ / (AmE), / ˈhæd ɪt wɪð / (BrE)
(informal) To be no longer able or willing to tolerate someone or something; to have reached one's limit of patience or endurance.
lost tolerance or patience
informal

Examples

I've had it with your complaining and negativity.
She finally had it with the broken promises and walked away.
We had it with the constant delays, so we called management.

Synset Examples

She has had it with his constant excuses.
I've had it with the noise from construction.
They've had it with the delays in the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fed up with - no longer able to tolerate something or someone
  • Synonym: sick of - very tired or annoyed by something, cannot tolerate more
