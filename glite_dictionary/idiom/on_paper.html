on paper

1. ADV

/ ɑn ˈpeɪpɚ / (AmE), / ɒn ˈpeɪpə / (BrE)
in theory or according to what is written, but not necessarily in practice or reality
in theory; not always in practice

Examples

On paper, the new policy should increase efficiency, but real-world results may differ.
The plan looked impressive on paper, yet it failed to deliver the expected benefits.
They seem like the ideal team on paper, but their personalities clash in the office.

Synset Examples

On paper, they are a perfect match.
The project sounds feasible on paper, but it’s risky.
