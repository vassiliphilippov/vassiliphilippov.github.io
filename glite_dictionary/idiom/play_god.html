to play God

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ɡɑd / (AmE), / pleɪ ɡɒd / (BrE)
to act as if one has authority or power over life, fate, or important outcomes, especially in a way that is considered presumptuous or morally questionable
assume control over others' fate

Examples

Doctors should not play God when making decisions about their patients' lives.
Some scientists worry that gene editing technology allows humans to play God.
She accused the judge of playing God by deciding who deserved forgiveness.

Synset Examples

He shouldn't play God with the patient's treatment.
Trying to play God can lead to dire consequences.
It's dangerous to play God with people's lives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take matters into one's own hands - to deal with a problem oneself instead of waiting for others to act
