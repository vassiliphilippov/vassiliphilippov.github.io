to make the welkin ring

1. VERB

/ meɪk ðə ˈwɛlkɪn rɪŋ / (AmE), / meɪk ðə ˈwɛlkɪn rɪŋ / (BrE)
to fill the air with loud and joyful sounds, especially of laughter or song
fill the air with joyous noise
archaic

Examples

Their laughter could make the welkin ring on a summer's evening.
The villagers' cheers made the welkin ring during the celebration.
The news was so joyful it seemed to make the welkin ring throughout the town.

Synset Examples

The choir's performance made the welkin ring during the concert.
The children sang to make the welkin ring with joy.
Their laughter could make the welkin ring at the party.

Etymology

Welkin is an archaic word for 'sky' or 'heavens,' from Old English *wolcen* (cloud).
