to kick someone when they are down

1. VERB

/ kɪk ˈsʌmwʌn wɛn ðeɪ ɑr daʊn / (AmE), / kɪk ˈsʌmwən wɛn ðeɪ ə daʊn / (BrE)
to attack, criticize, or treat someone badly when they are already experiencing difficulties or are in a vulnerable position
treat badly when someone is vulnerable

Examples

She lost her job and her so-called friends started to kick her when she was down.
It’s unfair to kick someone when they are down instead of supporting them.
Critics began to kick the company when it was down after its stock plummeted.

Synset Examples

It's unfair to kick a dog when it's down in a competition.
He refused to kick a dog when it's down, offering help instead.
He felt it was wrong to kick someone when they are down.
Don't kick someone when they are down; offer a helping hand.
It’s cruel to kick someone when they are down.
Criticizing her after the loss felt like kicking a dog when it's down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to add insult to injury - to make a bad situation even worse for someone
