to box clever

1. VERB

/ bɑks ˈklɛvɚ / (AmE), / bɒks ˈklɛvə / (BrE)
to behave in a shrewd or resourceful way in order to achieve an advantage, especially by using intelligence or cunning
act strategically to gain an advantage
british

Examples

If you want to get promoted here, you really need to box clever.
They managed to box clever and secure the contract despite fierce competition.
He always finds a way to box clever when facing difficult challenges at work.

Synset Examples

To succeed, you must learn to box clever with resources.
She always knows how to box clever in negotiations.
You need to box clever to outsmart your competitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outsmart - to defeat or trick someone by being more intelligent or clever
  • Synonym: to outwit - to gain an advantage by being more clever
  • Synonym: be cunning - to show skill in achieving one's ends by deceit or evasion

Etymology

Box in British slang means 'to fight', so 'box clever' draws an analogy to being clever in a boxing match.
