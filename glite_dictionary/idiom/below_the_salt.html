below the salt

1. ADV

/ bɪˈloʊ ðə sɔlt / (AmE), / bɪˈləʊ ðə sɒlt / (BrE)
In a position of low status or importance, often regarded or treated as inferior to others, especially within a social or organizational hierarchy.
in a position of low status or importance

Examples

As a junior associate, she often felt below the salt during board meetings.
Interns sometimes sense they are below the salt compared to permanent staff.
Despite his talent, he was seated below the salt at the award banquet.

Synset Examples

In their company, interns often felt below the salt in meetings.
He always felt he was treated as if he were below the salt at family gatherings.
The new hire quickly realized he was below the salt in the office hierarchy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: above the salt - in a position of high status or importance

Etymology

From medieval dining practice, where those seated below the salt cellar at the lord’s table were of lower rank.
