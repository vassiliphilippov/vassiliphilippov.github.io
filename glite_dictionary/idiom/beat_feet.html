to beat feet

1. VERB

/ bit fit / (AmE), / biːt fiːt / (BrE)
to leave or run away quickly; to hurry away, especially to escape or avoid something
leave or run away quickly
informal

Examples

As soon as the police arrived, they decided to beat feet out of the alley.
You'd better beat feet if you don't want to miss the train.
The kids saw the dog and immediately beat feet down the street.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
