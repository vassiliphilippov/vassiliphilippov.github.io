how long is a piece of string

1. X

/ haʊ lɔŋ ɪz ə pis əv strɪŋ / (AmE), / haʊ lɒŋ ɪz ə piːs əv strɪŋ / (BrE)
used to indicate that it is impossible to give a precise answer to a question, often because it depends on unknown or variable factors
impossible to answer; depends on many factors

Examples

Whenever I ask when the repairs will be finished, he just says, 'how long is a piece of string?'
If you want to know how many people will turn up, well, 'how long is a piece of string?'
She replied with 'how long is a piece of string?' when asked about the cost of the project.

Synset Examples

In response to my question, she shrugged, 'How long is a piece of string?'
He chuckled and said, 'Well, how long is a piece of string?'
When I asked about the project timeline, he said, 'How long is a piece of string?'
