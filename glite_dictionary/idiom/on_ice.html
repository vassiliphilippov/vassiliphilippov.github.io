on ice

1. ADV

/ ɑn aɪs / (AmE), / ɒn aɪs / (BrE)
temporarily suspended or postponed; not currently being carried out or continued
temporarily suspended; postponed

Examples

The negotiations have been put on ice until next month.
Many companies placed expansion plans on ice during the recession.
The government kept the policy on ice while reviewing alternatives.

Synset Examples

Their project is on ice until they find investors.
The event is on ice due to bad weather.
