to make your mark

1. VERB

/ meɪk jʊr mɑrk / (AmE), / meɪk jɔː mɑːk / (BrE)
to achieve recognition, distinction, or success in a particular field or activity
achieve recognition or success

Examples

After years of hard work, she finally made her mark in the fashion industry.
He hopes to make his mark as a scientist with his latest discovery.
Young artists try to make their mark by exhibiting at local galleries.

Synset Examples

To succeed, you must make your mark early.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stand out - to be noticeable or to be recognized as different or better
  • Synonym: leave your mark - to have a lasting impact or influence
  • Antonym: fade into obscurity - to become unnoticed or unknown
