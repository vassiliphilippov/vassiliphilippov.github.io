to splice the mainbrace

1. VERB

/ splaɪs ðə ˈmeɪnˌbreɪs / (AmE), / splaɪs ðə ˈmeɪnbrɛɪs / (BrE)
to celebrate by having an alcoholic drink, especially after hard work, traditionally used as a command or expression in nautical contexts
celebrate or drink (nautical idiom)

Examples

After a long day at sea, the captain allowed the crew to splice the mainbrace and relax.
The sailors cheered when they were permitted to splice the mainbrace after the victory.
It's an old tradition to splice the mainbrace whenever a voyage ends safely.

Synset Examples

After the storm, they had to splice the mainbrace quickly.
It's time to splice the mainbrace and celebrate!
The captain ordered the crew to splice the mainbrace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to celebrate - to mark a happy occasion with enjoyable activities
  • Antonym: to commiserate - to express or feel sympathy, usually in sorrow; opposite sentiment to celebrate

Etymology

From the nautical practice of repairing (splicing) the main brace (a rope controlling a ship's main yard), with the crew traditionally rewarded with a drink.
