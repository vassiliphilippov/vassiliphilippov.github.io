to throw down a challenge

1. VERB

/ θroʊ daʊn ə ˈtʃælɪndʒ / (AmE), / θrəʊ daʊn ə ˈtʃælɪndʒ / (BrE)
to issue a challenge to someone, especially in a way that invites competition or confrontation
issue a challenge to someone

Examples

The CEO decided to throw down a challenge to his competitors by launching a new product.
She didn't hesitate to throw down a challenge to the reigning chess champion.
By questioning the status quo, he threw down a challenge to authority.

Synset Examples

He decided to throw down a challenge to his rival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: challenge - a call to take part in a contest or competition
  • Antonym: accept a challenge - to agree to compete or respond to a challenge
