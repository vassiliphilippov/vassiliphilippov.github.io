I'll be a monkey's uncle

1. X

/ aɪl bi ə ˈmʌŋkiz ˈʌŋkəl / (AmE), / aɪl bi ə ˈmʌŋkiz ˈʌŋkl / (BrE)
used to express surprise or disbelief at something unexpected or hard to believe
expression of disbelief or surprise
informal

Examples

I'll be a monkey's uncle if he actually passed the exam without studying.
When she walked through the door after ten years, I thought, I'll be a monkey's uncle!
If they finish the project on time, I'll be a monkey's uncle.

Synset Examples

I'll be a monkey's uncle; I never expected that.
I'll be a monkey's uncle if that actually works!

Etymology

American English, possibly originating in the 1800s as a sarcastic or humorous expression of incredulity.
