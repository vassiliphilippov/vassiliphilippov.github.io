to sing for one's supper

1. VERB

/ sɪŋ fər wʌnz ˈsʌpər / (AmE), / sɪŋ fə wʌnz ˈsʌpə / (BrE)
to work or perform in order to earn a reward, wage, or place, especially when it is not guaranteed
work to earn a reward or keep

Examples

As a freelance writer, you always have to sing for your supper by bringing in new clients.
To keep living rent-free, Mark knew he would have to sing for his supper around the house.
In show business, even successful artists must constantly sing for their supper to stay relevant.

Synset Examples

She knew she'd have to sing for her supper in the competition.
He had to sing for his supper at the open mic.
In this industry, you must sing for your supper daily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to earn one's keep - to work in return for accommodation or sustenance

Etymology

Refers to itinerant performers who would literally sing to earn food or shelter.
