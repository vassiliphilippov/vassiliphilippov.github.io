shanks' pony

1. NOUN

/ ʃæŋks ˈpoʊni / (AmE), / ʃæŋks ˈpəʊni / (BrE)
used humorously to refer to walking rather than traveling by vehicle; going on foot
walking on foot (humorous)
british

Examples

We missed the bus and had to use shanks' pony to get home.
I'm afraid it's shanks' pony for us today, since the train is cancelled.
She often prefers shanks' pony to taking the car when commuting to work.

Synset Examples

I traveled on shank's mare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walk - to move at a regular pace by lifting and setting down each foot in turn

Etymology

From 'shank' (the part of the leg between knee and ankle), suggesting one's own legs as a means of transport, with 'pony' humorously referring to an animal for riding.

walking or travelling on foot rather than using a vehicle
walking on foot (British idiom)
informal british

Examples

When the train was cancelled, I had to rely on shanks' pony to get home.
Every morning, she takes shanks' pony to the office instead of driving.
After missing the last bus, they finished the journey by shanks' pony.

Synset Examples

I always take shanks' pony when I miss the bus.
Using shanks' pony is free, unlike taxi fares.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on foot - by walking rather than by vehicle

Etymology

British English, from 'shank' (the lower part of a leg) and 'pony' (used humorously for one's own legs as transport).
