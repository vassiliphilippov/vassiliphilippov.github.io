shank's pony

1. NOUN

/ ʃæŋks ˈpoʊni / (AmE), / ʃæŋks ˈpəʊni / (BrE)
used humorously to mean traveling by walking rather than by car, bus, etc.
walking instead of using transport
british informal

Examples

With no bus in sight, we set off on shank's pony to the next village.
After his bike broke, he had to rely on shank's pony to get to school.
She often jokes that shank's pony is the cheapest way to travel.

Synset Examples

I traveled on shank's mare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shank's mare - idiom meaning to walk rather than ride or drive

Etymology

From 'shank' meaning the lower part of the leg and 'pony' as a playful reference to riding; recorded from the early 19th century in British English.
