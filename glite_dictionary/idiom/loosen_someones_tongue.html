to loosen someone's tongue

1. VERB

/ ˈluːsən sʌmˌwʌnz tʌŋ / (AmE), / ˈluːsən sʌmˌwʌnz tʌŋ / (BrE)
to make someone willing to talk more freely, especially to reveal information or secrets
make someone talk more freely

Examples

A couple of drinks helped to loosen his tongue at the party.
The detective hoped the promise of immunity would loosen her tongue.
As the evening went on, the relaxed atmosphere began to loosen their tongues.

Synset Examples

They hoped the friendly atmosphere would loosen her tongue.
A little wine might loosen his tongue during the interview.
A good meal can often loosen someone's tongue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: make someone talk - cause a person to speak or reveal information
