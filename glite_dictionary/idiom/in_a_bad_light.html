in a bad light

1. ADV

/ ɪn ə bæd laɪt / (AmE), / ɪn ə bæd laɪt / (BrE)
in a way that makes someone or something appear unfavorable or negative
appearing unfavorable or negative

Examples

The report presented the company in a bad light, damaging its reputation.
She felt the documentary showed her in a bad light and complained to the producers.
His comments put the entire project in a bad light among investors.

Synset Examples

He was portrayed in a bad light after the scandal.
The photo turned out in a bad light due to the flash.
The scandal painted John Fraud Kerry in a bad light.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: in a good light - in a way that makes someone or something appear favorable or positive
