under someone's wing

1. ADV

/ ˈʌndɚ ˈsʌmwʌnz wɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʌndə ˈsʌmwʌnz wɪŋ / (BrE)
protected or guided by someone more experienced; receiving care, support, or mentorship.
under someone's protection or guidance

Examples

She felt much more confident once her manager took her under his wing.
The young athlete thrived after an experienced coach took her under her wing.
He offered to take the new team member under his wing and show him the ropes.

Synset Examples

She was glad to have someone experienced under her wing.
The mentor took the young artist under his wing.
He offered to take the new hire under his wing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take under one's wing - to begin to protect or mentor someone

Etymology

Figurative use deriving from the image of a bird sheltering its young under its wing.
