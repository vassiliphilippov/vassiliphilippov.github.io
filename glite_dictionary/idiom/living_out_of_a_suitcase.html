living out of a suitcase

1. X

/ ˈlɪvɪŋ ˈaʊt əv ə ˈsuːtkeɪs / (AmE), / ˈlɪvɪŋ ˈaʊt əv ə ˈsuːtkeɪs / (BrE)
spending much of your time traveling, especially without a permanent home or stable residence
frequently traveling without a permanent home

Examples

After years of living out of a suitcase, she finally decided to settle down in one city.
He grew tired of living out of a suitcase for his job as a consultant.
Musicians on tour often get used to living out of a suitcase and rarely unpack completely.

Synset Examples

Being a roadwhore means living out of a suitcase.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nomadic - constantly moving rather than settling in one place
