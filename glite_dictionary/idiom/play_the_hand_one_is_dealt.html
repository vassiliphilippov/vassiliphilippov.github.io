to play the hand one is dealt

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ðə hænd wʌn ɪz dɛlt / (AmE), / pleɪ ðə hænd wʌn ɪz dɛlt / (BrE)
to deal with one's circumstances as they are and make the best of the situation, regardless of its challenges or limitations
make the best of one's circumstances

Examples

Even though his company downsized, he resolved to play the hand one is dealt and look for new opportunities.
When faced with unexpected challenges, it's important to play the hand one is dealt rather than giving up.
She had always admired her father's ability to play the hand one is dealt without complaints.

Synset Examples

She learned to play the hand one is dealt with grace.
You can only succeed if you play the hand one is dealt.
In life, you have to play the hand one is dealt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: make the best of it - to accept a bad or difficult situation and do as well as possible

Etymology

From card playing, where players must play with the cards they are given; adopted figuratively in the early 20th century.
