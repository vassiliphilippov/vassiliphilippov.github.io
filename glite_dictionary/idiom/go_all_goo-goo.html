to go all goo-goo

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ɔl ˈɡu ˌɡu / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɔːl ˈɡuː ˌɡuː / (BrE)
to behave in a silly or excessively affectionate way, especially in reaction to something cute or lovable
act in a silly, affectionate way
informal

Examples

Whenever she sees a baby animal, she goes all goo-goo and starts cooing.
He went all goo-goo over his girlfriend's romantic note.
Don’t go all goo-goo just because you got a compliment from your boss.

Synset Examples

She looked at the puppy and went all goo-goo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fawn over - to give exaggerated attention or affection
  • Synonym: swoon - to be overcome with emotion, especially admiration or affection

Etymology

Imitative formation from 'goo-goo', a reduplication used to express baby talk or infatuation.
