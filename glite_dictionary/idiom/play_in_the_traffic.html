to play in the traffic

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ɪn ðə ˈtræfɪk / (AmE), / pleɪ ɪn ðə ˈtræfɪk / (BrE)
used humorously to tell someone to stop complaining or to suggest they should do something silly or dangerous, often to express annoyance
tell someone to stop complaining or do something silly/dangerous (humorous idiom)
informal

Examples

He was whining so much that I almost told him to play in the traffic.
Whenever my brother pesters me, I jokingly say he should play in the traffic.
She laughed when her friend suggested he should just play in the traffic instead of complaining.

Synset Examples

If you keep complaining, I might just tell you to go play in the traffic.
She was so annoying, I wanted to tell her to go play in the traffic.
They were being so reckless, I joked they should go play in the traffic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get lost - to go away; leave someone alone (informal)
  • Synonym: to drop dead - an angry way to tell someone to go away
