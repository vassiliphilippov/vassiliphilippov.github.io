kingdom come

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪŋdəm kʌm / (AmE), / ˈkɪŋdəm kʌm / (BrE)
the next world; the state after death or the afterlife (often used humorously or hyperbolically to refer to being destroyed or killed)
the afterlife; state after death

Examples

The explosion nearly blew the entire lab to kingdom come.
He threatened to send his enemy to kingdom come.
After the crash, the car was wrecked to kingdom come.

Synset Examples

he nearly blew us to kingdom come

Etymology

From the phrase 'Thy kingdom come' in the Lord's Prayer, referring to the coming of God's kingdom or the afterlife.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɪŋdəm ˈkʌm / (AmE), / ˈkɪŋdəm ˈkʌm / (BrE)
the end of time; the next world, or an indefinite future moment (often used to suggest something will never happen or as a reference to death or the afterlife)
the end of time; the next world

Examples

You can keep asking, but he won't change his mind till kingdom come.
The old house will probably stand until kingdom come.
She waited for her lost cat, hoping he would return before kingdom come.

Synset Examples

you can wet the bed till kingdom come, for all I care

Etymology

From the Christian phrase 'thy kingdom come' in the Lord's Prayer, referring to the coming of God's kingdom at the end of time.
