to committing sudoku

1. VERB

/ kəˈmɪtɪŋ suˈdoʊku / (AmE), / kəˈmɪtɪŋ suˈdəʊkuː / (BrE)
(informal, humorous) Making a confusing mistake or serious blunder; a playful misstatement of 'committing seppuku,' often used online to exaggerate embarrassment or failure.
making a serious, often confusing blunder (humorous idiom)
informal slang

Examples

After mispronouncing the boss's name in front of everyone, he joked about committing sudoku out of embarrassment.
She realized she had emailed the wrong client and texted her friend, 'Time for me to commit sudoku.'
Missing such an easy question on the test felt like committing sudoku to her.

Synset Examples

He joked that forgetting the meeting was like committing sudoku.
She said missing the deadline felt like committing sudoku.
He thought he could get away with it, but ended up committing sudoku.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: committing seppuku - ritual suicide by disembowelment (Japanese origin)
  • Synonym: blunder - a careless or serious mistake

Etymology

Blend of 'committing seppuku' (Japanese ritual suicide) and 'sudoku' (number puzzle), used humorously to refer to making a blunder.
