in the money

1. ADJ

/ ɪn ðə ˈmʌni / (AmE), / ɪn ðə ˈmʌni / (BrE)
having a lot of money or wealth; being rich or financially successful
wealthy; financially successful

Examples

After receiving a huge bonus at work, he was suddenly in the money.
She knew she was in the money when her business took off.
If I win this competition, I'll finally be in the money.

Synset Examples

They had a great quarter and are now in the money.
After years of hard work, she's finally in the money.
Winning the lottery means he's in the money now.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rich - having a great deal of money or assets
  • Synonym: wealthy - having a large amount of money, property, or valuable possessions
  • Antonym: broke - having no money, penniless
