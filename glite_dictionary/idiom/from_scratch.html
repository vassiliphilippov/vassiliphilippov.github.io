from scratch

1. ADV

/ frəm skrætʃ / (AmE), / frɒm skrætʃ / (BrE)
starting from the very beginning, without using anything that already exists or any special advantages
from the very beginning, using no pre-existing materials

Examples

He learned to code from scratch and eventually created his own software company.
They decided to renovate the old house from scratch, doing all the work themselves.
Baking a loaf of bread from scratch takes more time, but the result is worth it.

Synset Examples

he baked the torte from scratch
she built her business up from scratch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: from the ground up - completely from nothing; wholly new

Etymology

Possibly from the sporting term 'scratch' meaning a starting line or point in 19th-century English.

2. ADV

/ frəm skrætʃ / (AmE), / frəm skrætʃ / (BrE)
from the very beginning, without using anything that already exists or pre-made parts
starting from the very beginning

Examples

After moving to a new country, they had to build their lives from scratch.
She cooked every dish for the dinner party from scratch rather than using store-bought items.
The entrepreneur started her business from scratch with no outside investment.

Synset Examples

They learned to code from scratch during the boot camp.
She baked the cake from scratch for the party.
He learned how to make meat from scratch.
He built the furniture from scratch using reclaimed wood.

Etymology

Possibly from sporting races where marks on the ground (scratches) indicated starting points.
