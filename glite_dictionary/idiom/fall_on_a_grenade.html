to fall on a grenade

1. VERB

/ fɔl ɑn ə ɡrəˈneɪd / (AmE), / fɔːl ɒn ə ɡrəˈneɪd / (BrE)
to make a significant personal sacrifice, often accepting blame or risk, for the benefit or protection of others
make a personal sacrifice for others

Examples

Sometimes a leader must fall on a grenade to protect the rest of the group from criticism.
Sarah decided to fall on a grenade by volunteering for the toughest assignment.
He didn't hesitate to fall on a grenade so his colleagues wouldn't get in trouble.

Synset Examples

He agreed to fall on a grenade for the team.
To protect his friend, he had to fall on a grenade.
She knew she had to fall on a grenade this time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take one for the team - to accept an unpleasant task or responsibility for a group's benefit

Etymology

Metaphor from the literal act of a soldier throwing themself onto a grenade to protect others.
