all wet

1. ADJ

/ ɔl wɛt / (AmE), / ɔːl wɛt / (BrE)
completely wrong or misguided in opinion, idea, or judgment
completely wrong; misguided (idiom)
informal

Examples

Your analysis of the situation is all wet and fails to consider the main facts.
If you think it'll never rain today, you're all wet.
Many critics were all wet about the film's chances at the box office.

Synset Examples

His theory about the economy is completely all wet.
Your assumptions about the project are all wet.
She was all wet after walking in the rain without an umbrella.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistaken - incorrect or wrong in judgment or belief
  • Antonym: correct - free from error; right
