to sail close to the wind

1. VERB

/ seɪl kloʊs tə ðə wɪnd / (AmE), / seɪl kləʊs tə ðə wɪnd / (BrE)
to act in a way that is risky, borderline unethical, or nearly breaks rules or laws
take risks; act near the limit of legality

Examples

The company has often sail close to the wind to outpace its competitors, skirting regulations.
He knew he was sailing close to the wind by not reporting all his earnings.
You risk getting into trouble if you continue to sail close to the wind with these tax deductions.

Synset Examples

They tend to sail close to the wind with their business practices.
He likes to sail close to the wind with his risky investments.
To succeed, sometimes you have to sail close to the wind.
He sailed close to the wind with that risky investment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to push the envelope - to go beyond accepted limits or boundaries
  • Synonym: to play with fire - to behave in a way that is risky or dangerous
  • Antonym: keep within the law - to act within legal boundaries

Etymology

Originally from sailing, where to sail close to the wind means to steer as close as possible to the direction from which the wind is blowing, risking stalling.
