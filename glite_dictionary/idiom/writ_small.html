writ small

1. X

/ rɪt smɔl / (AmE), / rɪt smɔːl / (BrE)
written in small print, often to conceal details or make them less noticeable, especially in formal or legal documents.
written in small print, especially to hide details

Examples

Be sure to read the information writ small at the bottom of the page.
He missed the penalties writ small in the loan agreement.
Crucial details are sometimes writ small in contracts to avoid drawing attention.

Synset Examples

Etymology

From the archaic past participle 'writ' (written) + 'small' (in small letters or print).
