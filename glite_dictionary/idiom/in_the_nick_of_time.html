in the nick of time

1. ADV

/ ɪn ðə nɪk əv taɪm / (AmE), / ɪn ðə nɪk əv taɪm / (BrE)
at the last possible moment before it is too late; just in time
at the last possible moment

Examples

The ambulance arrived in the nick of time and saved the boy’s life.
She handed in her assignment in the nick of time, just before the deadline.
I caught my train in the nick of time, right as the doors were closing.

Synset Examples

she was saved in the nick of time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: just in time - arriving or happening right when needed

Etymology

Possibly from the use of 'nick' meaning a critical moment, attested in the 16th century.
