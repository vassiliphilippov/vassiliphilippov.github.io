to do justice

1. VERB

/ du ˈdʒʌstɪs / (AmE), / duː ˈdʒʌstɪs / (BrE)
to treat or appreciate something in a way that fully recognizes its value, quality, or significance
treat or value something properly

Examples

The camera just doesn't do justice to the stunning colors of the sunset.
She worked hard to do justice to her grandmother's secret recipe.
His thoughtful review really does justice to the complexity of the novel.

Synset Examples

The diners did the food and wine justice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: do honor to - to show respect or bring credit to something or someone
