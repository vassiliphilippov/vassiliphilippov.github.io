to throw one's hat over the wall

1. VERB

/ θroʊ wʌnz hæt ˈoʊvɚ ðə wɔl / (AmE), / θrəʊ wʌnz hæt ˈəʊvə ðə wɔːl / (BrE)
to commit oneself to a course of action or challenge, making a decisive and irrevocable move
commit fully to a course of action

Examples

After months of indecision, he finally decided to throw his hat over the wall and start his own business.
When she enrolled in the marathon, she really threw her hat over the wall without hesitation.
Sometimes you just need to throw your hat over the wall to move forward in life.

Synset Examples

He threw his hat over the wall by applying for the job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take the plunge - to commit to a significant course of action

Etymology

Possibly from Irish folk tales, where throwing one's hat over a wall signified the commitment to retrieve it, regardless of obstacles.
