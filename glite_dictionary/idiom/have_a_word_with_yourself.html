to have a word with yourself

1. VERB

/ hæv ə wɜrd wɪð jərˈsɛlf / (AmE), / hæv ə wɜːd wɪð jəˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to take time to reflect on one's own actions or decisions, often to reconsider or admonish oneself
reflect on one's actions or decisions

Examples

After missing another deadline, I really had to have a word with yourself about my time management.
Whenever you feel overwhelmed, take a moment to have a word with yourself and set your priorities straight.
If you keep acting this way, you might want to have a word with yourself before things get worse.

Synset Examples

Sometimes you need to have a word with yourself about priorities.
