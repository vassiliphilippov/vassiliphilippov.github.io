to be a skaapie

1. VERB

/ bi ə ˈskɑːpi / (AmE), / biː ə ˈskɑːpiː / (BrE)
to act in a foolish or naive manner
act foolishly or naively

Examples

Don't be a skaapie; always question things that seem too good to be true.
He felt embarrassed after realizing he'd been a skaapie and fallen for the prank.
Stop being a skaapie and double-check the details before signing anything.

Synset Examples

Don't be a skaapie, think before you act.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be gullible - to be easily deceived
  • Antonym: be wise - to show good judgment

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'skaap' (sheep), often used metaphorically for gullibility.
