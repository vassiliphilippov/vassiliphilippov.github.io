to put one's shoulder to the wheel

1. VERB

/ pʊt wʌnz ˈʃoʊldɚ tə ðə wil / (AmE), / pʊt wʌnz ˈʃəʊldə tə ðə wiːl / (BrE)
to begin to work very hard or make a determined effort to achieve something
work very hard; make a determined effort

Examples

If we want to make this event a success, we all need to put our shoulder to the wheel.
She decided to put her shoulder to the wheel and finish the assignment ahead of schedule.
The company turned things around after everyone put their shoulder to the wheel and worked together.

Synset Examples

The team put their shoulder to the wheel to complete the project.
If we want to succeed, we must put our shoulder to the wheel.
Let’s put our shoulder to the wheel and make this happen.
He set his shoulder to the wheel and worked hard.
If we all set our shoulders to the wheel, we can finish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: work hard - to put in great effort; to labor diligently
  • Antonym: to take it easy - to relax and not work hard

Etymology

Alludes to pushing against the wheel of a stuck cart to set it in motion; in figurative use since the 17th century.
