to the hilt

1. ADV

/ tə ðə hɪlt / (AmE), / tə ði hɪlt / (BrE)
to the greatest possible extent; fully or completely
fully; to the utmost degree

Examples

She supported her brother to the hilt during the legal battle.
The company was in debt to the hilt after the failed acquisition.
If you’re going to commit, you should do it to the hilt or not at all.

Synset Examples

you are in this to the hilt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completely - totally; absolutely, without exception
  • Antonym: partially - only in part; not completely

Etymology

Originally from sword terminology, "hilt" meaning the handle of a sword; thrusting a blade 'to the hilt' means to do something as fully as possible.
