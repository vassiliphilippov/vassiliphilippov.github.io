what have you

1. X

/ wʌt hæv ju / (AmE), / wɒt hæv juː / (BrE)
used to refer to other similar things or people that have not been specifically mentioned; and so on; etc.
and other similar things; etc.
informal

Examples

He enjoys hiking, cycling, photography, and what have you in his free time.
You can buy bread, milk, eggs, or what have you at the grocery store.
People arrived with gifts, cards, flowers, and what have you for the celebration.

Synset Examples

He collects coins, stamps, and what have you.
You can choose pizza, burgers, or what have you.
Bring snacks, drinks, or what have you.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: and so on - used to refer to continuing a list in a similar way
  • Synonym: et cetera - and other similar things; abbreviated as etc.
