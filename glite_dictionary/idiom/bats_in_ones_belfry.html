bats in one's belfry

1. X

/ bæts ɪn wʌnz ˈbɛlfri / (AmE), / bæts ɪn wʌnz ˈbɛlfri / (BrE)
to be crazy or eccentric; to act in a way that is strange or insane
to be crazy or eccentric

Examples

Everyone thought the inventor had bats in his belfry after he unveiled his flying machine.
She started talking to plants, and her neighbors suspected she had bats in her belfry.
If you claim to have seen a UFO, skeptics may say you have bats in your belfry.

Synset Examples

She acts like she has bats in her belfry when she talks about aliens.
He must have bats in his belfry to think that's a good idea.
If you believe that rumor, you might have bats in your belfry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mad as a hatter - completely insane or crazy
  • Synonym: crazy - mentally deranged; insane

Etymology

From the idea of bats flying around a church belfry as a metaphor for a mind full of confused thoughts. 'Belfry' originally meant a bell tower.
