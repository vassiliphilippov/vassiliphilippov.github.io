in the limelight

1. ADV

/ ɪn ðə ˈlaɪmˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ɪn ðə ˈlaɪmlaɪt / (BrE)
receiving a great deal of public attention or interest
in a position of public attention

Examples

After the scandal, the politician was suddenly in the limelight for all the wrong reasons.
The young singer found herself in the limelight after her debut performance went viral.
Some people prefer staying out of in the limelight and working behind the scenes.

Synset Examples

She enjoys being in the limelight at every event.
Being in the limelight can be overwhelming for many celebrities.
After the award, he found himself in the limelight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in the spotlight - in a position attracting public attention

Etymology

From the use of limelight (an intense white stage light produced by heating lime) in 19th-century theaters to highlight performers.
