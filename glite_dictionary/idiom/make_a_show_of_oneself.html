to make a show of oneself

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə ʃoʊ əv wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / meɪk ə ʃəʊ əv wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to behave in a way that attracts attention, often causing embarrassment or disapproval
attract attention by behaving conspicuously

Examples

She tried not to make a show of herself during the presentation.
He always manages to make a show of himself when there are guests.
If you make a show of yourself at the meeting, people might not take you seriously.
