to asking for it

1. VERB

/ ˈæskɪŋ fər ɪt / (AmE), / ˈɑːskɪŋ fə ɪt / (BrE)
behaving in a way likely to invite trouble, criticism, or negative consequences
inviting trouble or negative consequences

Examples

If you walk into traffic without looking, you’re really asking for it.
He kept insulting his coach, and honestly, he was asking for it.
When you ignore safety rules, you’re practically asking for it.

Synset Examples

He felt he was asking for it by driving so recklessly.
She knew she was asking for it by challenging the boss.
If you keep ignoring warnings, you’re asking for it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bring it on oneself - to cause trouble or difficulty for oneself by one's own actions
