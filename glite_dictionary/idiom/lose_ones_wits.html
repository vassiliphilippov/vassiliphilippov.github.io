to lose one's wits

1. VERB

/ luːz wʌnz wɪts / (AmE), / luːz wʌnz wɪts / (BrE)
to become very confused or unable to think clearly, typically due to fear, stress, or shock
become confused or unable to think clearly

Examples

If you panic during the exam, you might lose your wits and forget everything you studied.
He was so overwhelmed by the situation that he almost lost his wits.
In the darkness and confusion, she feared she would lose her wits entirely.

Synset Examples

He felt he might lose his wits with all this stress.
She nearly lost her wits during the chaotic event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lose one's mind - to go insane or become extremely irrational
  • Antonym: keep one's wits - to remain calm and able to think clearly, especially in difficult situations
