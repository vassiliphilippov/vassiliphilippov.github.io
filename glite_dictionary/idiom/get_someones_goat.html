to get someone's goat

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈsʌmwʌnz ɡoʊt / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈsʌmwʌnz ɡəʊt / (BrE)
to deliberately annoy or irritate someone
to annoy or irritate someone
informal

Examples

No matter how hard I try, little things like that always get my goat.
She really knows how to get his goat during meetings, and it's quite amusing.
Don't let rude comments from coworkers get your goat; try to stay calm.

Synset Examples

She knows how to get his goat during meetings.
Don't let his comments get under your goat.
Don't let his comments get your goat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to annoy - to make someone a little angry
  • Synonym: to irritate - to make someone feel annoyed or impatient

Etymology

Possibly from early 20th-century American slang; precise origin uncertain.

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈsʌmwʌnz ɡoʊt / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈsʌmwʌnz ɡəʊt / (BrE)
to make someone very annoyed or irritated
to annoy or irritate someone
informal

Examples

People who chew loudly really get my goat, especially in quiet restaurants.
It gets my goat when drivers don't use their turn signals.
Don't let his rude remarks get your goat; just ignore him.

Synset Examples

His constant teasing really gets my goat.
His constant teasing really gets under my goat.
She has a knack for getting under my goat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to annoy - to bother or disturb someone, causing irritation
  • Antonym: to calm down - to make someone less angry or upset

Etymology

Early 20th century American origin; possibly from an old practice of taking a goat from a racehorse's stall to upset it.
