put that in your pipe and smoke it

1. X

/ pʊt ðæt ɪn jʊr paɪp ənd smoʊk ɪt / (AmE), / pʊt ðæt ɪn jɔː paɪp ənd sməʊk ɪt / (BrE)
(informal) Used to tell someone to accept a fact or situation, often when it is unwelcome or proves a point.
used to tell someone to accept an unwelcome fact
informal

Examples

I got the job, so put that in your pipe and smoke it!
If you don't believe I can do it, just watch me—put that in your pipe and smoke it.
We won the debate, so put that in your pipe and smoke it.

Synset Examples
