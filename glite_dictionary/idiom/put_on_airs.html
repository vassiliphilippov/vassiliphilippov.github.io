to put on airs

1. VERB

/ pʊt ɑn ɛrz / (AmE), / pʊt ɒn ɛəz / (BrE)
to behave in a way that shows you think you are better or more important than other people; to act pretentiously or affectedly
act pretentiously; behave superiorly

Examples

After receiving the promotion, he started to put on airs around his colleagues.
She dislikes people who put on airs and pretend to be something they're not.
Even though he came from a humble background, he was often accused of putting on airs at formal events.

Synset Examples

He is lording it over the students

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to act superior - to behave as if one is better than others
  • Antonym: be humble - to behave without pretension or superiority

2. VERB

/ pʊt ɑn ɛrz / (AmE), / pʊt ɒn eəz / (BrE)
to behave in a way that shows you think you are better or more important than other people
act pretentious or superior

Examples

She started to put on airs after she was promoted at work.
There's no need to put on airs when we're all friends here.
He never puts on airs despite his great success.

Synset Examples

Don’t put on airs; we all know you’re just like us.
He tends to put on airs when around his wealthy friends.
She always puts on airs at fancy parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to act superior - to behave as if one is better than others
  • Antonym: be humble - to act with humility and lack of pretension

Etymology

From the French 'air' (appearance, manner), entered English in the 16th century meaning a way of behaving or looking.
