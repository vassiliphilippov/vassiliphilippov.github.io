salt in the wound

1. NOUN

/ sɔlt ɪn ðə wund / (AmE), / sɒlt ɪn ðə wuːnd / (BrE)
something that makes an already bad or painful situation worse
to make a bad situation worse

Examples

Losing the game was hard enough, but his teasing was salt in the wound.
When my boss criticized my work in front of everyone, it was salt in the wound.
Failing the exam was bad, but hearing others talk about their success added salt in the wound.

Synset Examples

Bringing up my past mistakes felt like salt in the wound.
His comments were salt in the wound after the loss.
Her constant reminders were just salt in the wound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to add insult to injury - to make a bad situation even worse by doing something else to upset someone

Etymology

Refers to the old practice of rubbing salt into wounds, which increases pain.
