six and two threes

1. X

/ sɪks ənd tu ˈθriz / (AmE), / sɪks ənd tu ˈθriːz / (BrE)
used to indicate that two options or situations are essentially the same or make no real difference
no real difference between options
informal british

Examples

Honestly, it's six and two threes whether we drive or take the train.
For me, Coke or Pepsi is six and two threes—they taste very similar.
The results will be six and two threes no matter which route we choose.

Synset Examples

It's all six and two threes; nothing changes.
To him, the choices were six and two threes.
Both options are six and two threes in my opinion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: six of one, half a dozen of the other - two choices that are essentially the same
