to outrun the constable

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈrʌn ðə ˈkɑnstəbl̩ / (AmE), / aʊtˈrʌn ðə ˈkɒnstəbl̩ / (BrE)
to escape from or avoid being caught by law enforcement, especially in the context of evading arrest or debt
escape or evade the police
archaic

Examples

After the robbery, the thief hoped to outrun the constable through the dark alleyways.
She tried to outrun the constable but was eventually caught a few streets away.
It's hard to outrun the constable when the police are using fast patrol cars.

Synset Examples

He thought he could outrun the constable after the theft.
They believed they could outrun the constable in that old car.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escape the law - to avoid apprehension or capture by authorities
  • Antonym: turn oneself in - to surrender to the police

Etymology

From the role of a 'constable' as a police officer; 'outrun' meaning to run faster than or escape from.
