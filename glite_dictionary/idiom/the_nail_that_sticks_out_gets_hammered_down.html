the nail that sticks out gets hammered down

1. X

/ ðə neɪl ðət stɪks aʊt ɡɛts ˈhæmɚd daʊn / (AmE), / ðə neɪl ðət stɪks aʊt ɡɛts ˈhæməd daʊn / (BrE)
used to express the idea that individuals who stand out or act differently are often criticized or pressured to conform
those who stand out may be criticized or suppressed

Examples

In strict workplaces, the nail that sticks out gets hammered down, so people avoid taking risks.
She hesitated to express her unusual ideas, recalling that the nail that sticks out gets hammered down.
Many cultures warn that the nail that sticks out gets hammered down, discouraging people from being too different.

Synset Examples

He learned quickly that the nail that sticks out gets hammered down in this office.
In this competitive environment, remember: the nail that sticks out gets hammered down.
She kept a low profile, knowing that the nail that sticks out gets hammered down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stand out - to be easily noticed or different from others
  • Antonym: to blend in - to look or seem the same as those around you; to not stand out

Etymology

Popularized from the Japanese proverb 'Deru kugi wa utareru' (The nail that sticks out gets hammered down).
