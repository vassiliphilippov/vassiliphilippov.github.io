blood on one's hands

1. NOUN

/ blʌd ɑn wʌnz hændz / (AmE), / blʌd ɒn wʌnz hændz / (BrE)
responsibility for causing someone's death or serious harm, especially through one's actions or negligence
responsibility for causing death or harm

Examples

The investigation revealed that the CEO had blood on his hands after the tragic factory fire.
She couldn't sleep at night, haunted by the feeling that she had blood on her hands.
Many protesters argued that the government had blood on its hands for failing to act sooner.

Synset Examples

If you don't report the crime, you'll have blood on your hands.
She feels she may have blood on her hands due to her negligence.
The politician has blood on his hands after the scandal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guilt - a feeling of responsibility for wrongdoing or harm
