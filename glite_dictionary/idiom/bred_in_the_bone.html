bred in the bone

1. ADJ

/ brɛd ɪn ðə boʊn / (AmE), / brɛd ɪn ðə bəʊn / (BrE)
(Of a quality or characteristic) deeply ingrained or inherent; forming an essential and permanent part of someone's nature.
inherently ingrained; innate

Examples

Her generosity is bred in the bone and cannot be taught or learned.
His artistic talent is truly bred in the bone—it's part of who he is.
That family's sense of duty seems to be bred in the bone across generations.

Synset Examples

His love for music was breed in the bone.
His work ethic is bred-in-the-bone from his parents.

She is a bred-in-the-bone artist, always creating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innate - existing naturally or by heredity, not learned
  • Antonym: acquired - learned or developed, not inherent

Etymology

Refers to qualities metaphorically 'bred' (i.e., ingrained by birth or upbringing) as deep as in one's bones.
