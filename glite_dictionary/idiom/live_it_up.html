to live it up

1. VERB

/ ˈlɪv ɪt ʌp / (AmE), / ˈlɪv ɪt ʌp / (BrE)
to enjoy oneself in a carefree or extravagant way, often spending a lot of money and having a good time
enjoy oneself extravagantly; party heavily
informal

Examples

After getting his bonus, he decided to live it up and booked a five-star cruise.
Whenever they're together, the old friends live it up until the early hours of the morning.
They plan to live it up in Paris for their anniversary next month.

Synset Examples

They decided to live it up during their vacation.
Let's live it up this weekend!
He loves to live it up at parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to celebrate - to acknowledge a significant or happy event with festivities
  • Synonym: to let one's hair down - to behave in an uninhibited or relaxed manner
  • Antonym: be frugal - to be economical with resources; the opposite of spending extravagantly
