to be the bigger man

1. VERB

/ bi ðə ˈbɪɡɚ mæn / (AmE), / biː ðə ˈbɪɡə mæn / (BrE)
to act in a more mature or gracious way than others, especially by choosing not to react negatively in a difficult situation
act more maturely or graciously

Examples

Even though he was clearly wronged, Peter decided to be the bigger man and apologized first.
Sometimes you just have to be the bigger man and forgive, even when it's hard.
She told her son that if his friend was rude, he should be the bigger man and not retaliate.

Synset Examples

If they insult you, just be the bigger man and walk away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take the high road - to behave in a morally superior way in a difficult situation
