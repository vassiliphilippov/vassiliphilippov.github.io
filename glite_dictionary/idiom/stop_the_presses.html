to stop the presses

1. VERB

/ stɑp ðə ˈprɛsəz / (AmE), / stɒp ðə ˈprɛsɪz / (BrE)
used to express that something important has happened and immediate attention or action is required, especially in journalism when urgent news interrupts regular publishing.
call to halt publication for urgent news

Examples

When the editor heard about the scandal, she yelled, 'stop the presses!'
They had to stop the presses to include the late-breaking election results.
If this information is true, someone needs to stop the presses immediately.

Synset Examples

I had to shout to stop the presses for the new headline.
They announced they would stop the presses for updates.
The news was so big, they had to stop the presses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breaking news - information about an event that has just happened or is currently happening
  • Synonym: hold the front page - expression to indicate something is so important it should be front-page news

Etymology

Originates from newspaper publishing, when presses were physically stopped to update for urgent news.
