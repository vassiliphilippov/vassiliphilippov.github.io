to wind up in trouble

1. VERB

/ waɪnd ʌp ɪn ˈtrʌbəl / (AmE), / waɪnd ʌp ɪn ˈtrʌbəl / (BrE)
to eventually find oneself in a difficult or problematic situation, often as a result of one's actions or choices
end up in a difficult situation

Examples

If you don't follow the rules, you might wind up in trouble.
She didn't study for the exam and wound up in trouble with her parents.
Many people winding up in trouble never expected things to go so wrong.

Synset Examples

If you keep arguing, you’ll wind up in trouble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get into trouble - to become involved in a problematic or dangerous situation
