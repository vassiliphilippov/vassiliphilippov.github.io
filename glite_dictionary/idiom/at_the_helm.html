at the helm

1. ADV

/ ət ðə hɛlm / (AmE), / ət ðə helm / (BrE)
in a position of leadership or control; in charge of an organization, group, or activity
in control; leading

Examples

With the new CEO at the helm, the company experienced rapid growth.
Fans were relieved to have an experienced manager at the helm during the playoffs.
When she is at the helm, the team always performs at its best.

Synset Examples

She was at the helm of the project during the crisis.
They placed her at the helm of the committee.
With him at the helm, we felt confident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in charge - responsible for or in control of something
  • Synonym: in command - having authority or control
  • Antonym: out of control - not in control; lacking leadership or direction

Etymology

Originally from nautical usage, referring to steering a ship by holding the helm (the steering wheel or tiller).
