lame duck

1. NOUN

/ leɪm dʌk / (AmE), / leɪm dʌk / (BrE)
an elected official or leader who has lost power or influence, especially after losing an election or nearing the end of their term in office
official or leader with diminished power

Examples

After the election loss, the senator became a lame duck with little political influence.
The CEO was considered a lame duck during the transition to new management.
As his final term drew to a close, the governor felt like a lame duck in office.

Synset Examples

After losing the election, he's now a lame duck.
The president is a lame duck during his last term.
The company faced challenges with its lame duck leadership.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caretaker - a person who temporarily carries out a leader's duties; acting replacement
  • Antonym: incumbent - a current officeholder; often with full powers

Etymology

First used in 18th-century Britain to refer to bankrupt businessmen, later adopted for politicians with reduced authority.
