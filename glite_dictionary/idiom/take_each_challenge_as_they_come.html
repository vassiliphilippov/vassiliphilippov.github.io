to take each challenge as they come

1. VERB

/ teɪk iːtʃ ˈtʃælɪndʒ æz ðeɪ kʌm / (AmE), / teɪk iːtʃ ˈtʃælɪndʒ æz ðeɪ kʌm / (BrE)
to deal with problems or difficulties one at a time, as they arise, rather than worrying about them all at once
deal with problems one at a time

Examples

When facing a busy week, I try to take each challenge as they come instead of feeling overwhelmed.
As a new manager, he learned to take each challenge as they come and remain flexible.
It's best to take each challenge as they come rather than worrying about every possible problem at once.
