Houston, we have a problem

1. X

/ ˈhuːstən ˌwiː hæv ə ˈprɑːbləm / (AmE), / ˈhjuːstən ˌwiː hav ə ˈprɒbləm / (BrE)
An expression used to indicate that a significant or unexpected problem has arisen.
phrase signaling a major problem

Examples

Houston, we have a problem; the servers are all down this morning.
Everyone was enjoying the event until someone spilled wine on the mayor—Houston, we have a problem.
When the project manager didn't show up, it was clear: Houston, we have a problem.

Synset Examples

After the storm, Houston, we have a problem with the roof.
Houston, we have a problem with the engine.
Houston, we have a problem; the shipment is delayed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: problem - a matter or situation regarded as unwelcome or harmful and needing to be dealt with

Etymology

Originates from the Apollo 13 mission (1970), when astronaut Jack Swigert reported a critical fault to NASA's Mission Control in Houston.
