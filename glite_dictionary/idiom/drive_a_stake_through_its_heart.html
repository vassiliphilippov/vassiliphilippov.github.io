to drive a stake through its heart

1. VERB

/ draɪv ə steɪk θru ɪts hɑrt / (AmE), / draɪv ə steɪk θruː ɪts hɑːt / (BrE)
to bring about the complete and final end of something, especially a plan, idea, or problem; to eliminate it decisively
eliminate or end something decisively

Examples

The new law will finally drive a stake through its heart and stop the illegal practice.
With that powerful argument, she managed to drive a stake through its heart.
The company hopes this product will drive a stake through its heart and end the competition.

Synset Examples

To end the debate, she decided to drive a stake through its heart with solid evidence.
The activist aimed to drive a stake through its heart by exposing the truth.
They hoped to drive a stake through its heart by cutting funding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to put an end to - to stop something from continuing
  • Synonym: to finish off - to destroy or eliminate completely

Etymology

From the traditional method in folklore of killing a vampire by driving a wooden stake through its heart.
