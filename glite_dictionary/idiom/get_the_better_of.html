to get the better of

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ðə ˈbɛtər əv / (AmE), / ɡɛt ðə ˈbɛtə rɒv / (BrE)
to gain control over someone or something, especially in a way that is difficult to resist or prevent
gain control over someone or something

Examples

She tried to stay calm, but her nerves got the better of her before the interview.
Don’t let your impatience get the better of you in difficult situations.
Sometimes curiosity gets the better of me and I have to ask questions.

Synset Examples

Don’t let anger get the better of you.
Her emotions sometimes get the better of her.
He always lets stress get the better of him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overcome - to succeed in dealing with or controlling
