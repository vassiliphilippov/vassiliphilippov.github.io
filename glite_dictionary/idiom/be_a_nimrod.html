to be a nimrod

1. VERB

/ bi ə ˈnɪmˌrɑd / (AmE), / biː ə ˈnɪmrɒd / (BrE)
to behave in a foolish or inept way; to act as a fool
act foolishly or ineptly
informal american

Examples

I told you not to be a nimrod during the interview, but you spilled coffee everywhere.
Stop being a nimrod and read the instructions carefully this time.
If you be a nimrod at the meeting again, the boss will not be impressed.

Synset Examples

Don't be a nimrod and forget your keys again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fool around - to behave in a silly or irresponsible way
  • Antonym: act sensibly - to behave in a reasonable or wise manner

Etymology

Originally from the biblical name Nimrod (noted as a hunter), later shifting to 'fool' in North American slang, possibly via ironic use in popular culture (e.g., Bugs Bunny cartoons).
