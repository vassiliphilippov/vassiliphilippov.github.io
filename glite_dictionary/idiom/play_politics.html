to play politics

1. VERB

/ pleɪ pəˈlɪtɪks / (AmE), / pleɪ pəˈlɪtɪks / (BrE)
to engage in manipulative or strategic behavior, often for personal or organizational gain, especially within an organization or group
act in a manipulative or strategic way

Examples

If you want to succeed in that company, you'll have to play politics like everyone else.
He was more interested in playing politics than actually solving the problem.
She refused to play politics, preferring to be direct about her intentions.

Synset Examples

She decided to play politics to secure her promotion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to manipulate - to control or influence someone or something, often in a clever or dishonest way
  • Antonym: act straightforwardly - to behave in an open and honest way without manipulation
