to get religion

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt rɪˈlɪdʒən / (AmE), / ɡɛt rɪˈlɪdʒən / (BrE)
to become serious or deeply committed to something, especially after a period of indifference or neglect
become committed or serious about something

Examples

After years of reckless spending, he finally got religion and began saving for retirement.
The company got religion about customer service after losing several major clients.
She never cared about nutrition before, but now she's got religion and eats only healthy food.

Synset Examples

She finally got religion about her health and started exercising.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to see the light - suddenly understand or become convinced of something
  • Synonym: turn over a new leaf - change one's behavior for the better

Etymology

From the literal sense of adopting religious faith, extended figuratively in American English (mid-19th century) to mean becoming serious or committed.
