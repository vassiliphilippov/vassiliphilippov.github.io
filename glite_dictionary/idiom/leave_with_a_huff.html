to leave with a huff

1. VERB

/ liv wɪð ə hʌf / (AmE), / liːv wɪð ə hʌf / (BrE)
to depart suddenly and obviously to show anger or annoyance
depart angrily or in annoyance

Examples

Frustrated by the decision, she left with a huff before anyone could explain further.
When the meeting got heated, Tom left with a huff and slammed the door behind him.
After waiting for hours with no service, the customer finally left with a huff.

Synset Examples

After the argument, he left with a huff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storm out - to leave a place suddenly because you are angry or upset
  • Synonym: leave in a huff - to leave quickly because of being annoyed or angry
