to take one's place on the throne

1. VERB

/ teɪk wʌnz pleɪs ɑn ðə θroʊn / (AmE), / teɪk wʌnz pleɪs ɒn ðə θrəʊn / (BrE)
to assume a position of power or leadership, especially in the context of monarchy or high authority
assume power or leadership, esp. as monarch

Examples

After the death of the king, his daughter was next to take her place on the throne.
Many believed he was destined to take his place on the throne and rule with compassion.
The prince waited for years before he could finally take his place on the throne.

Synset Examples

He aspired to take his place on the throne.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ascend the throne - to become king or queen; to take up royal power
  • Antonym: to abdicate - to step down from the throne or position of power
