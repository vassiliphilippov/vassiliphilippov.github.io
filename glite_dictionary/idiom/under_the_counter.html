under the counter

1. ADV

/ ˈʌn.dɚ ðə ˈkaʊn.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈʌn.də ðə ˈkaʊn.tə / (BrE)
in a secret or illegal way, especially when selling or keeping something that is not allowed or is restricted
secretly or illegally sold or kept

Examples

He managed to buy some imported whiskey under the counter despite the ban.
Rare and expensive medicines are sometimes sold under the counter in some countries.
The shopkeeper kept cigarettes under the counter to avoid getting caught by the authorities.

Synset Examples

He bought the rare book under the counter.
You can find those old records under the counter.
They sold the banned items under the counter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on the sly - in a secretive way, sneakily
  • Antonym: above board - open and legal, not secret or dishonest
