to talk through one's hat

1. VERB

/ tɑk θru wʌnz hæt / (AmE), / tɔːk θruː wʌnz hæt / (BrE)
to speak without knowledge, or to say things that are not true or are not based on facts
speak insincerely or ignorantly

Examples

He tends to talk through his hat whenever the conversation turns to politics.
If you don't know the answer, don't talk through your hat just to sound important.
The expert accused the witness of talking through his hat during the trial.

Synset Examples

The politician was not well prepared for the debate and faked it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talk nonsense - speak in a way that does not make sense or lacks truth
  • Synonym: bluff - to pretend to know or have something in order to deceive

Etymology

Possibly from the late 19th-century American usage meaning to talk nonsense, origin uncertain.

2. VERB

/ tɑk θru wʌnz hæt / (AmE), / tɔːk θruː wʌnz hæt / (BrE)
to speak without knowing the facts; to make unfounded or nonsensical statements
speak without knowledge; make unfounded claims

Examples

When he claimed he could finish the project in a day, he was just talking through his hat.
Experts dismissed the politician's comments, saying he was talking through his hat about the economy.
Emily often talks through her hat when discussing subjects she knows little about.

Synset Examples

Don't believe him; he's just talking through his hat.
If you keep talking through your hat, no one will take you seriously.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talk nonsense - to say things that are foolish or untrue
  • Antonym: talk sense - to speak logically and reasonably

Etymology

Originated in the late 19th century, likely referring to making empty or foolish remarks, as if speaking through a hat.
