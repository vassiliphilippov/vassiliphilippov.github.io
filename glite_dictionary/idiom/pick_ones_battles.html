to pick one's battles

1. VERB

/ pɪk wʌnz ˈbætəlz / (AmE), / pɪk wʌnz ˈbætəlz / (BrE)
to decide which problems or conflicts are worth confronting, rather than trying to address every issue
choose which conflicts to engage with

Examples

In a demanding job, it's important to pick your battles so you don't burn out.
She realized she needed to pick her battles with her teenage son to keep the peace.
Managers often have to pick their battles to focus on what truly matters.

Synset Examples

He learned to pick his battles during family discussions.
Sometimes, you have to pick your battles wisely.
In a relationship, it's best to pick your battles.
