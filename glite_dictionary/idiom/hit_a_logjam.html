to hit a logjam

1. VERB

/ hɪt ə ˈlɔɡˌdʒæm / (AmE), / hɪt ə ˈlɒɡˌdʒæm / (BrE)
to encounter a situation in which progress stops because of a disagreement or obstacle; to reach an impasse or deadlock
reach a deadlock or impasse

Examples

After hours of discussion, the project finally hit a logjam and progress stopped.
Negotiations hit a logjam when neither side was willing to compromise.
Talks over the new contract hit a logjam last week due to unresolved differences.

Synset Examples

The negotiations hit a logjam over budget issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reach a stalemate - to get to a situation where no progress can be made
  • Antonym: to break the deadlock - to resolve a situation in which no progress is possible

Etymology

From the literal sense of 'logjam', meaning a large quantity of logs blocking a river, creating an obstruction.
