to look sharp

1. VERB

/ lʊk ʃɑrp / (AmE), / lʊk ʃɑːp / (BrE)
to act quickly or hurry; to move or do something with speed and alertness
act quickly; hurry

Examples

If you want to catch the train, you'd better look sharp!
The coach told the players to look sharp and get ready for the next drill.
You need to look sharp or you'll be late for your appointment.

Synset Examples

We have to rush!
hurry — it's late!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hurry up - to move or act more quickly
  • Synonym: rush - to move or act with great speed
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00460453-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ lʊk ʃɑrp / (AmE), / lʊk ʃɑːp / (BrE)
to appear stylish, fashionable, or well-dressed
appear stylish or well-dressed
informal

Examples

You really look sharp in that new suit.
She knew she had to look sharp for her interview.
He always looks sharp when he goes out on weekends.

Synset Examples

He looked sharp in his canary fit jacket at the gala.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dress up - to put on formal or elaborate clothing
  • Antonym: look scruffy - to appear untidy or poorly dressed
