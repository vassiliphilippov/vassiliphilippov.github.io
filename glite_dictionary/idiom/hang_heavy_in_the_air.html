to hang heavy in the air

1. VERB

/ hæŋ ˈhɛvi ɪn ði ɛr / (AmE), / hæŋ ˈhɛvi ɪn ðiː eə / (BrE)
(of a feeling, mood, or atmosphere) to be strongly present, creating a sense of tension, sadness, or another emotion that is noticeable and affecting those present.
be strongly felt in an atmosphere

Examples

After the bad news was announced, a sense of despair seemed to hang heavy in the air.
Tension hung heavy in the air as the teams waited for the referee's decision.
Sadness hung heavy in the air throughout the house after their beloved dog passed away.

Synset Examples

His disappointment seemed to hang heavy in the air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to linger - to remain for a long time in a place or situation
  • Antonym: dispell - to make a feeling or atmosphere disappear
