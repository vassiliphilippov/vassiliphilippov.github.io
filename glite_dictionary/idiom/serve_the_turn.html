to serve the turn

1. VERB

/ sɝv ðə tɝn / (AmE), / sɜːv ðə tɜːn / (BrE)
to be adequate or to fulfill a purpose, especially temporarily or when adapting to changing circumstances
to suffice or adapt in a situation

Examples

If you don't have any sugar, honey will serve the turn in this recipe.
Though not ideal, this solution will serve the turn until we find a better one.
In times of crisis, people often have to serve the turn with whatever resources they have.

Synset Examples

We need to serve the turn and adapt quickly.
They will serve the turn by changing the strategy.
It’s time to serve the turn and innovate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make do - to manage with what is available; to adapt or suffice temporarily
  • Synonym: to suffice - to be enough or adequate for a purpose
