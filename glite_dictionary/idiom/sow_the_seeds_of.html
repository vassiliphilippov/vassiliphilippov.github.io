to sow the seeds of

1. VERB

/ soʊ ðə siːdz ʌv / (AmE), / səʊ ðə siːdz ɒv / (BrE)
to do something that will cause a particular result to develop in the future, often something significant or consequential
begin a process leading to a future result

Examples

She hoped her actions would sow the seeds of change within the organization.
Political unrest may sow the seeds of future conflict in the region.
His generosity sowed the seeds of goodwill among the entire community.

Synset Examples

The initiative aims to sow the seeds of innovation.
Her speech will sow the seeds of motivation among the youth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: initiate - to begin something; to start a process
  • Synonym: to bring about - to cause something to happen

Etymology

From the literal act of planting seeds for future growth, used metaphorically since at least the 16th century.
