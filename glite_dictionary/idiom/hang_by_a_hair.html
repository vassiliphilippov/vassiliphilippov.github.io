to hang by a hair

1. VERB

/ hæŋ baɪ ə hɛr / (AmE), / hæŋ baɪ ə heə / (BrE)
to be in a very precarious or risky situation, where failure or danger is imminent
to be in a highly precarious situation

Examples

After the accident, his career seemed to hang by a hair for months.
The peace agreement continues to hang by a hair amidst rising tensions.
Their financial future is hanging by a hair after the company lost its largest client.

Synset Examples

His life now hangs by a thread

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hang by a thread - to be in great danger or at risk of failing
