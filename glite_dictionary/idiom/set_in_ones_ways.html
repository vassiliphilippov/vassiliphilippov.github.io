set in one's ways

1. ADJ

/ sɛt ɪn wʌnz weɪz / (AmE), / sɛt ɪn wʌnz weɪz / (BrE)
unwilling to change one's habits, beliefs, or ways of doing things
unwilling to change habits or opinions

Examples

My grandfather is set in his ways and refuses to try new technologies.
Despite their advice, she remained set in her ways and kept the same routine.
Many managers become set in their ways after years of running the company.

Synset Examples

She is set in her ways and resists change.
He's very set in his ways about work.
It's no use trying to convince him; he's set in his ways.
Older people can often be set in their ways.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stubborn - unwilling to change one's mind or attitude
  • Antonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas
