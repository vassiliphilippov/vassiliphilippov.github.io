matter of course

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæt̬ɚ əv kɔrs / (AmE), / ˈmætə əv kɔːs / (BrE)
something that happens as a natural or expected result, especially without any special attention or effort
something expected or automatic

Examples

Attendance at the weekly meeting was treated as a matter of course by all employees.
In many places, recycling has become a matter of course rather than a special effort.
It was taken as a matter of course that the contract would be renewed.
