to shock the hell out of someone

1. VERB

/ ʃɑk ðə hɛl aʊt əv ˈsʌmwʌn / (AmE), / ʃɒk ðə hɛl aʊt əv ˈsʌmwʌn / (BrE)
to surprise someone extremely or suddenly, causing strong emotional reaction
surprise someone greatly
informal

Examples

The news shocked the hell out of me when I heard it last night.
Her sudden resignation shocked the hell out of everyone at the office.
That movie ending shocked the hell out of the whole audience.

Synset Examples

He showed up and shocked the hell out of everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shock - to surprise or upset someone suddenly
  • Synonym: to stun - to make someone feel shocked, dazed, or unable to react
  • Synonym: to astonish - to surprise someone greatly
