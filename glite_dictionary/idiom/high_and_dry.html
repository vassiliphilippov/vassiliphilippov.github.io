high and dry

1. ADJ

/ haɪ ənd draɪ / (AmE), / haɪ ənd draɪ / (BrE)
left without help or resources in a difficult or helpless situation
abandoned in a difficult situation

Examples

When the company closed, many employees were left high and dry with no severance pay.
Please don't leave your friends high and dry when they need help the most.
The hikers feared they would be high and dry if the guide abandoned them.

Synset Examples

He left her high and dry without any explanation.
After the storm, they were left high and dry.
Don’t leave me high and dry during the project.
She felt high and dry without a ride home.
He left me high and dry without any explanation.
They abandoned the plan, leaving us high and dry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abandon - to leave someone or something completely and finally
  • Antonym: support - help or assistance

Etymology

Originally a nautical term, referring to a boat stranded on land by a receding tide and left unable to float.
