old chestnut

1. NOUN

/ oʊld ˈtʃɛs.nʌt / (AmE), / əʊld ˈtʃɛs.nʌt / (BrE)
a story, joke, or remark that has been repeated so often that it is boring or tiresome
overused story, joke, or remark

Examples

Every Christmas, my uncle tells the same old chestnut about his first job.
At the meeting, someone brought up that old chestnut about teamwork again.
The comedian avoided telling any old chestnuts, preferring new material.

Synset Examples

It's an old chestnut, but it never gets old.
That old chestnut about the dog still makes me laugh.
He told the old chestnut about his childhood adventures.
He told the same old chestnut at the party last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cliché - a phrase or idea that has been used so often it is no longer interesting or effective
  • Synonym: groaner - a tired or old joke, especially one that is no longer funny
  • Antonym: original - something new or not repeated

Etymology

First used in the 19th century; the origin is uncertain, possibly from a popular play where a repeated joke involved a chestnut tree.
