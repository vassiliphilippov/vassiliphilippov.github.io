in the black

1. ADJ

/ ɪn ðə blæk / (AmE), / ɪn ðə blæk / (BrE)
making a profit or being financially solvent; not in debt.
profitable; not in debt

Examples

After a tough few years, the restaurant is finally in the black again.
If the project stays under budget, we’ll end the year in the black.
Her careful spending habit keeps her bank account in the black.

Synset Examples

The investment paid off, and profits are in the black.
After a successful quarter, the company is finally in the black.
Thanks to careful budgeting, I’m now in the black.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: in the red - in debt or operating at a financial loss

Etymology

From traditional bookkeeping, where profits were recorded in black ink and losses in red ink.
