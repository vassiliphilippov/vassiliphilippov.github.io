growing pains

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡroʊɪŋ peɪnz / (AmE), / ˈɡrəʊɪŋ peɪnz / (BrE)
the difficulties or emotional distress experienced during the process of maturing, especially in adolescence
emotional distress of growing up

Examples

Every teenager faces growing pains as they adapt to new responsibilities and social challenges.
The move to a new city caused some growing pains for the whole family, but they adjusted over time.
Even successful companies can experience growing pains as they expand rapidly and take on new staff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adolescence - the period in human development following the onset of puberty

Etymology

Originally referred to physical pains experienced by children as they grow, later extended metaphorically to emotional or developmental challenges.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡroʊɪŋ peɪnz / (AmE), / ˈɡrəʊɪŋ peɪnz / (BrE)
difficulties or troubles encountered during the early stages of developing, expanding, or changing something, especially an organization or enterprise
early-stage problems or difficulties

Examples

Many start-up companies experience growing pains as they try to scale their operations quickly.
The organization had some growing pains after merging with a larger corporation, but things eventually stabilized.
Every new system goes through growing pains before it runs smoothly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teething problems - initial small difficulties, especially in new projects or systems

Etymology

Originally referred to physical pains experienced by children as they grow, later adopted figuratively for difficulties in development.
