to have a quiet word

1. VERB

/ hæv ə ˈkwaɪət wɜrd / (AmE), / hæv ə ˈkwaɪət wɜːd / (BrE)
to speak privately with someone, especially to discuss a sensitive or discreet matter
talk privately about a sensitive issue

Examples

I need to have a quiet word with you about your recent performance.
Before the announcement, the manager decided to have a quiet word with the team leader.
She pulled him aside, hoping to have a quiet word out of earshot of the others.

Synset Examples

She asked to have a quiet word before the meeting started.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speak privately - to talk with someone in a private setting
  • Synonym: air one's concerns - to express one's worries or complaints privately
