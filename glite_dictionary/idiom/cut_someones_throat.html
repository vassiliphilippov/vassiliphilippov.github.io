to cut someone's throat

1. VERB

/ kʌt ˈsʌmwʌnz θroʊt / (AmE), / kʌt ˈsʌmwʌnz θrəʊt / (BrE)
to harm or betray someone, especially in order to gain an advantage for oneself
betray or harm for gain (idiom)

Examples

In the cutthroat business world, some people will cut someone's throat just to climb the corporate ladder.
He was so ambitious that he didn't hesitate to cut someone's throat for a promotion.
Rumors spread that she would cut someone's throat if it meant getting ahead.

Synset Examples

Her competitive nature often led her to cut others' throats.
The movie's villain threatened to slit someone's throat.
In a fit of rage, he claimed he would slit someone's throat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stab someone in the back - to betray someone unexpectedly
  • Antonym: help someone - to assist or support someone
