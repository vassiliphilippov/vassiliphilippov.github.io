to kick out the jams

1. VERB

/ kɪk aʊt ðə dʒæmz / (AmE), / kɪk aʊt ðə dʒæmz / (BrE)
to play music very loudly and energetically, often to energize a group or create a lively atmosphere
play music loudly and energetically
informal slang

Examples

When the band took the stage, they promised to kick out the jams all night long.
At the reunion, everyone wanted to kick out the jams and relive their rock-and-roll days.
It's Friday night—let's put on some music and kick out the jams!

Synset Examples

Let's kick out the jams and enjoy this party!
The band is ready to kick out the jams at the festival.
We always kick out the jams when driving on a road trip.

Etymology

Popularized by the MC5 song 'Kick Out the Jams' (1969); likely originated as slang within rock music culture.
