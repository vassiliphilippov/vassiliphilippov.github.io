to cop one's whack

1. VERB

/ kɑp wʌnz wæk / (AmE), / kɒp wʌnz wæk / (BrE)
(informal) To receive one's fair share or what one is due, especially as a reward or share of something divided.
receive one's fair share
informal british

Examples

After months of effort, he was eager to cop his whack from the business profits.
Everyone on the team wanted to cop their whack after the successful project.
She worked overtime to ensure she would cop her whack come bonus time.

Synset Examples

He felt he deserved to cop his whack after the hard work.
He expects to cop his whack at the end of the project.
She always wants to cop her whack of the credit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get one's due - to receive what one deserves or is owed

Etymology

"Cop" is British slang for "to receive" or "get"; "whack" is British informal for "share, portion".
