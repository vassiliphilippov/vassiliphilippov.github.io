full stop

1. NOUN

/ fʊl stɑp / (AmE), / fʊl stɒp / (BrE)
a definitive end to something; a complete halt
definitive end; complete halt

Examples

The negotiations reached a full stop after the latest disagreement.
His resignation put a full stop to any hopes of reconciliation.
Financial constraints brought the campaign to a sudden full stop.

Synset Examples

He said it was a full stop on any further discussions.
The project has come to a full stop due to funding issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: end - the final point of something
  • Synonym: halt - an act of stopping or being stopped
  • Antonym: continuation - the act of carrying on or proceeding
