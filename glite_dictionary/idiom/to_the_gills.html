to the gills

1. ADV

/ tə ðə ɡɪlz / (AmE), / tə ðə ɡɪlz / (BrE)
completely; as much as possible; to an excessive degree
completely; to excess

Examples

The suitcase was packed to the gills with clothes and souvenirs.
After Thanksgiving dinner, I was stuffed to the gills and couldn't eat another bite.
The tiny venue was crowded to the gills during the band's final show.

Synset Examples

They decorated the house to the gills for the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to the brim - (idiom) completely full; filled to the top
  • Synonym: full to bursting - (idiom) so full that it is almost overflowing

Etymology

An allusion to fish gills, implying being so full as to reach the gills.
