to tie the knot

1. VERB

/ taɪ ðə nɑt / (AmE), / taɪ ðə nɒt / (BrE)
to get married; to enter into marriage
get married (idiom)

Examples

They decided to tie the knot after five years of dating.
Many couples dream of tying the knot in a beautiful outdoor ceremony.
Sarah was nervous and excited as she prepared to tie the knot with her partner.

Synset Examples

She was excited to tie the knot with her best friend.
They plan to tie the knot next summer.
After years of dating, they finally tied the knot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get married - to become joined in marriage
  • Antonym: divorce - to legally end a marriage

Etymology

An idiom that likely refers to ancient wedding customs involving knots to symbolize unity.
