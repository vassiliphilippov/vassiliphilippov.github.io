to skin a flint for a penny

1. VERB

/ skɪn ə flɪnt fɔr ə ˈpɛni / (AmE), / skɪn ə flɪnt fə ə ˈpɛni / (BrE)
to be extremely frugal or miserly, going to great lengths to avoid spending money
to be extremely frugal or miserly

Examples

My uncle is so tight with his money, he'd skin a flint for a penny rather than pay full price for anything.
You have to be careful asking him for help; he'd skin a flint for a penny if it saved him a dime.
The company became notorious for cutting costs at every turn, willing to skin a flint for a penny.

Synset Examples

He's so stingy, he could skin a flint for a penny.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skinflint - a person who is extremely stingy or miserly
  • Synonym: miser - a person who hoards wealth and spends as little as possible

Etymology

From the image of scraping skin from a hard stone (flint), meaning someone who is so miserly they try to extract value even where there is none. See also 'skinflint'.
