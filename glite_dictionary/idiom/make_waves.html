to make waves

1. VERB

/ meɪk weɪvz / (AmE), / meɪk weɪvz / (BrE)
to cause a significant change, disturbance, or controversy, often by challenging the status quo
cause disturbance or controversy

Examples

Her bold fashion choices always make waves at the office.
The CEO's public statement made waves in the financial community.
He isn't afraid to make waves if it means improving the process.

Synset Examples

The new policy is expected to make waves among employees.
She tends to make waves whenever she speaks up.
His bold ideas are sure to make waves in the industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rock the boat - to disturb the status quo or cause trouble

2. VERB

/ meɪk weɪvz / (AmE), / meɪk weɪvz / (BrE)
to cause a significant impact or disturbance, often by doing something innovative, controversial, or attention-getting
cause a significant impact or stir

Examples

The new CEO quickly made waves by implementing radical changes across the company.
Her bold fashion choices always make waves at industry events.
He isn't afraid to make waves if it means standing up for what he believes in.

Synset Examples

In London, Red Ken made waves during his mayoral campaign.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cause a stir - to cause excitement or controversy
