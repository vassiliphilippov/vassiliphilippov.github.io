tar baby

1. NOUN

/ tɑr ˈbeɪbi / (AmE), / tɑː ˈbeɪbi / (BrE)
a problematic situation that becomes more complicated or difficult the more one attempts to handle it
problematic situation that worsens when handled

Examples

He knew that taking sides would turn the dispute into a tar baby he couldn’t escape from.
When she tried to fix the broken software, it quickly became a tar baby full of unexpected issues.
The contract negotiations turned into a tar baby, growing more complex with every meeting.

Synset Examples

He found the issue to be a tar baby that just got messier.
The project turned into a tar baby after several setbacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quagmire - a complicated or difficult situation
  • Synonym: catch-22 - a problematic situation with no clear solution

Etymology

From the character in Joel Chandler Harris’s ‘Uncle Remus’ stories (late 19th century), a doll made of tar used to trap Br’er Rabbit; now used figuratively.
