to avoid tsuris

1. VERB

/ əˈvɔɪd ˈtsʊrɪs / (AmE), / əˈvɔɪd ˈtsʊərɪs / (BrE)
To steer clear of trouble or problems; to avoid getting involved in situations that may cause distress or complications.
avoid trouble or problems
informal

Examples

He tries to avoid tsuris by not getting involved in office politics.
Whenever possible, she likes to avoid tsuris and keep things peaceful.
If you want a quiet life, it's best to avoid tsuris altogether.

Synset Examples

My grandmother always said to avoid tsuris at all costs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keep out of trouble - to stay away from problematic or risky situations

Etymology

Tsuris is from Yiddish tsures, meaning 'troubles', itself from Hebrew tzarot.
