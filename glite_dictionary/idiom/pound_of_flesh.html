pound of flesh

1. NOUN

/ paʊnd əv flɛʃ / (AmE), / paʊnd əv fleʃ / (BrE)
a demand for repayment or punishment that is strict, harsh, or unreasonable
harsh or unreasonable demand for repayment

Examples

The creditor insisted on his pound of flesh despite her difficult circumstances.
After losing the lawsuit, he feared the court would demand a pound of flesh in damages.
She warned that taking a pound of flesh from the team would ruin morale.

Synset Examples

It's unfair to take a pound of flesh for a small mistake.
The loan shark demanded his pound of flesh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retribution - punishment that is considered to be morally right and fully deserved
  • Antonym: leniency - showing mercy or tolerance instead of harshness

Etymology

From Shakespeare's play 'The Merchant of Venice,' where Shylock demands a literal pound of flesh as collateral.
