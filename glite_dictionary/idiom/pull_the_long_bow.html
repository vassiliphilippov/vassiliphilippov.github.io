to pull the long bow

1. VERB

/ pʊl ðə lɔŋ boʊ / (AmE), / pʊl ðə lɒŋ bəʊ / (BrE)
to exaggerate or tell lies, especially in retelling events or stories
to exaggerate or lie

Examples

Whenever he tells fishing stories, he tends to pull the long bow a bit too much.
I wouldn't trust her; she's known to pull the long bow when talking about her achievements.
Stop pulling the long bow and stick to the facts, please.

Synset Examples

Don't pull the long bow; just tell the truth.
She tends to pull the long bow when recounting adventures.
His story was so exaggerated it felt like he was pulling the long bow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exaggerate - to make something seem larger or more important than it really is
  • Synonym: lie - to intentionally make a false statement
  • Antonym: to downplay - to make something seem less important or serious

Etymology

Alludes to the English longbow, suggesting stretching the truth as one would string a long bow.
