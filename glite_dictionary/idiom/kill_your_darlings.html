to kill your darlings

1. VERB

/ kɪl jɔɹ ˈdɑɹlɪŋz / (AmE), / kɪl jɔː ˈdɑːlɪŋz / (BrE)
to remove elements from your writing, even those you are most attached to, to improve clarity or quality
cut cherished parts of writing for improvement

Examples

Aspiring authors often struggle to kill their darlings during the editing process.
It was difficult, but the journalist had to kill his darlings to keep the article concise.
Great writing sometimes requires you to kill your darlings, no matter how much you love them.

Synset Examples

Writers must kill their darlings to improve their work.
To refine the script, he had to kill some darlings.
She learned to kill her darlings during editing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to edit - to revise or correct a text to improve it
  • Synonym: cut - to remove or delete a part of a text or work

Etymology

Popularized by novelist William Faulkner, though similar advice predated him.
