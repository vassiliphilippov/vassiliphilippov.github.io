to shake one's head

1. VERB

/ ʃeɪk wʌnz hɛd / (AmE), / ʃeɪk wʌnz hɛd / (BrE)
to move one's head from side to side as a way of showing disagreement, disapproval, or refusal
indicate disagreement by moving head

Examples

She could only shake her head in disbelief when she heard the news.
He shook his head to indicate he didn't agree with the plan.
The teacher shakes her head whenever students talk during the lesson.

Synset Examples

Every time she suggests a new plan, he just shakes his head, living in Nopeville.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nod - move the head up and down to indicate agreement or approval
