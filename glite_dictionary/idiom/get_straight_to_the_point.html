to get straight to the point

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt streɪt tə ðə pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ɡɛt streɪt tə ðə pɔɪnt / (BrE)
to speak or express oneself directly, without unnecessary details or digression
speak directly without digression

Examples

If we have limited time, let's get straight to the point and address the main issue.
She appreciated it when her boss would get straight to the point in meetings.
He tends to get straight to the point rather than wasting time on small talk.

Synset Examples

Let's not wastetime and get straight to the point.
Let's get straight to the point of this discussion.
Let’s go straight to the point in this meeting.
She avoided pollyspeak and got straight to the point.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get to the point - to begin talking about what is most important or relevant
