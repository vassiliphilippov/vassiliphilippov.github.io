to jump before one is pushed

1. VERB

/ dʒʌmp bɪˈfɔr wʌn ɪz pʊʃt / (AmE), / dʒʌmp bɪˈfɔː wʌn ɪz pʊʃt / (BrE)
to leave a position or situation voluntarily before being forced out
leave voluntarily before being forced out

Examples

Realizing the company was restructuring, he decided to jump before he was pushed.
Some politicians jump before they are pushed to avoid public scandal.
She wisely chose to jump before she was pushed and resigned from her post.

Synset Examples

It's wise to jump before one is pushed out.
She decided to jump before one is pushed out of the company.
You should always jump before one is pushed in business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to resign - to leave a job or position voluntarily
