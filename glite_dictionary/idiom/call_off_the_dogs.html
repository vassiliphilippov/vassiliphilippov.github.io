to call off the dogs

1. VERB

/ kɔl ɑf ðə dɔɡz / (AmE), / kɔːl ɒf ðə dɒɡz / (BrE)
to stop criticizing, pursuing, or attacking someone, especially after being aggressive or persistent
stop criticizing or attacking

Examples

After several harsh comments, the manager decided to call off the dogs.
The media finally agreed to call off the dogs once the truth was revealed.
If you think he's learned his lesson, maybe it's time to call off the dogs.

Synset Examples

Let’s call off the dogs and give him a break.
You should call off the dogs; they’re getting too aggressive.
He decided to call off the dogs after a long investigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to back off - to move away or stop pressing or attacking
  • Synonym: to lay off - to stop bothering or criticizing someone

Etymology

Originates from hunting, where dogs would be called off to end a chase.
