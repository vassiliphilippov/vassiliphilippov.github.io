denial is not a river in Egypt

1. X

/ dɪˈnaɪ.əl ɪz nɑt ə ˈrɪv.ɚ ɪn ɪˈdʒɪpt / (AmE), / dɪˈnaɪ.əl ɪz nɒt ə ˈrɪv.ə ɪn ɪˈdʒɪpt / (BrE)
used humorously to point out that someone is denying reality or refusing to accept the truth
humorous idiom about denying the truth
informal

Examples

Whenever you're tempted to avoid bad news, just remember that denial is not a river in Egypt.
He kept insisting he was fine, but denial is not a river in Egypt.
When confronted with the facts, she just smiled and said, 'denial is not a river in Egypt.'

Synset Examples

Remember, denial is not a river in Egypt; face the truth.
She laughed and said, 'Denial is not a river in Egypt!'
You can't ignore the problem; denial is not a river in Egypt.

Etymology

Plays on the homophone 'denial' and 'the Nile' river in Egypt for comedic effect.
