to fight to the death

1. VERB

/ faɪt tə ðə dɛθ / (AmE), / faɪt tə ðə dɛθ / (BrE)
to engage in a struggle or contest that only ends when one side is completely defeated, often implying willingness to go as far as dying rather than surrendering
engage in a struggle until one side is defeated or killed

Examples

The gladiators were forced to fight to the death in front of the roaring crowd.
Two business rivals continued to fight to the death for control of the company.
They vowed they would fight to the death rather than let their homeland fall.

Synset Examples

The rivals fought to the death in the arena.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fight tooth and nail - to fight very fiercely or with all one's strength
  • Antonym: surrender - to give up or yield, especially in a contest or conflict
