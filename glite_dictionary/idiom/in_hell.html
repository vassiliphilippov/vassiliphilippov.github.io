in hell

1. ADV

/ ɪn hɛl / (AmE), / ɪn hɛl / (BrE)
experiencing extreme discomfort, distress, or suffering, often used figuratively.
in a state of extreme discomfort or suffering

Examples

During the summer without air conditioning, we felt in hell every afternoon.
She described her recovery from the illness as being in hell for weeks.
With deadlines piling up and no help, he said he was in hell at work.

Synset Examples

I thought I was in hell when the meeting dragged on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in agony - experiencing extreme physical or emotional pain
  • Antonym: in heaven - in a state of great happiness or bliss
