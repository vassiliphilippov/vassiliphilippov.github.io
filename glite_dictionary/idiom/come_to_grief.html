to come to grief

1. VERB

/ kʌm tə ɡriːf / (AmE), / kʌm tə ɡriːf / (BrE)
to experience failure, misfortune, or serious trouble, especially suddenly or unexpectedly
suffer failure or misfortune

Examples

Several expeditions have come to grief while trying to cross the desert.
If you ignore the warning signs, you might come to grief sooner than you think.
His risky investments eventually came to grief.

Synset Examples

He came to grief after ignoring the safety warnings.
Her reckless driving caused her to come to grief.
Many adventurers come to grief in those treacherous mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fail - to be unsuccessful in achieving a goal
  • Antonym: to succeed - to achieve desired results or success
