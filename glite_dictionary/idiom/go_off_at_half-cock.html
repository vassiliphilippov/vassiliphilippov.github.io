to go off at half-cock

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ɔf ət hæf ˈkɑk / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɒf ət hɑːf ˈkɒk / (BrE)
to act too hastily, without adequate preparation or thought; to take action prematurely
act hastily or without preparation

Examples

Try not to go off at half-cock before you know all the facts.
Politicians often go off at half-cock when reacting to breaking news.
He realized he had gone off at half-cock by sending the email without proofreading it.

Synset Examples

she wanted to quit her job but her mother told her not to go off half-cocked

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to jump the gun - to start something too soon, especially without preparation
  • Synonym: act rashly - to act without careful consideration or thought

Etymology

Originally from the mechanism of firearms, where a gun at 'half-cock' is not fully prepared or ready to fire.
