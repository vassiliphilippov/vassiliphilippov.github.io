to take heed

1. VERB

/ teɪk hiːd / (AmE), / teɪk hiːd / (BrE)
to pay careful attention to something, especially a warning or advice
pay careful attention

Examples

The teacher warned the students to take heed of the safety rules during experiments.
If you take heed of the advice given, you are less likely to make mistakes.
Travelers should take heed when the weather forecast predicts severe storms.

Synset Examples

Listen to your father
We must hear the expert before we make a decision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heed - to pay attention to; regard
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to

Etymology

From Middle English 'heden' (to heed, to observe) and the phrase 'take heed'—'heed' from Old English 'hēdan' (to take care, protect).
