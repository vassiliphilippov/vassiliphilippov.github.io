to have one's head

1. VERB

/ hæv wʌnz hɛd / (AmE), / hæv wʌnz hɛd / (BrE)
to be allowed to act as one wishes without interference; to have freedom or control over one's actions.
be free to act as one wishes

Examples

The manager decided to have his head and let him handle the project as he saw fit.
If you just have your head, I'm sure the task will be done perfectly.
Once the artist was allowed to have her head, her creativity flourished.

Synset Examples

Let him have his head; he knows what he’s doing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: have free rein - to be allowed complete freedom of action or expression
  • Antonym: be under someone's thumb - to be under another's strict control or authority
