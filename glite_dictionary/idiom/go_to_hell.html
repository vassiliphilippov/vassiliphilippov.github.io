to go to hell

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ tu hɛl / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə hɛl / (BrE)
used to tell someone angrily that you do not care about them or their opinions, or to express strong rejection or disdain
angry expression of rejection or disdain
offensive

Examples

If you don't like my decision, you can go to hell.
She was so frustrated with his rudeness that she finally yelled, "Go to hell!"
Tired of the constant criticism, he told his coworkers to go to hell.

Synset Examples

If they don't understand, they can go to hell.
He laughed and told the naysayers to go to hell.
She told him to go to hell after the argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get lost - an impolite way of telling someone to go away
  • Synonym: to go away - to leave a place or person
  • Antonym: come here - to move toward or approach the speaker
