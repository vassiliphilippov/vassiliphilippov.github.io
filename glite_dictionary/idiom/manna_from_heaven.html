manna from heaven

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænə frəm ˈhɛvən / (AmE), / ˈmænə frəm ˈhɛvən / (BrE)
an unexpected benefit or windfall, especially one that comes at a critical time
unexpected blessing or windfall

Examples

The unexpected inheritance was a real manna from heaven for the struggling artist.
For the small business, the new government grant came as manna from heaven.
After months of drought, the sudden rainstorm was manna from heaven for the farmers.

Synset Examples

the demand for testing has created a boom for those unregulated laboratories where boxes of specimen jars are processed like an assembly line

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windfall - a sudden, unexpected gain or advantage

Etymology

From the biblical manna, miraculously provided food for the Israelites in the Book of Exodus.
