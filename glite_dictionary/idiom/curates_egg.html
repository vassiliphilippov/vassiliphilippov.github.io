curate's egg

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjʊrˌeɪts ɛɡ / (AmE), / ˈkjʊərɪts ɛɡ / (BrE)
something that is partly good and partly bad; having both positive and negative qualities.
something partly good, partly bad

Examples

The team's performance was a curate's egg, with some impressive moments but clear mistakes too.
Critics called the play a curate's egg, noting both its brilliant dialogue and awkward pacing.
Her new novel is a bit of a curate's egg, offering some sharp insights alongside confusing passages.

Synset Examples

Her presentation was a curate's egg of ideas.
It’s a curate's egg, with some great pieces.
The report was a curate's egg of good and bad news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mixed bag - something with both positive and negative qualities
  • Antonym: lemon - something that is unsatisfactory or defective (slang)

Etymology

From a 19th-century British cartoon in 'Punch', where a curate politely claims his bad egg is 'good in parts'.
