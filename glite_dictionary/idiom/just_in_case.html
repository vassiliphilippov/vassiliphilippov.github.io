just in case

1. ADV

/ dʒʌst ɪn keɪs / (AmE), / dʒʌst ɪn keɪs / (BrE)
as a precaution; to be prepared for something that might happen
as a precaution

Examples

I always carry an umbrella, just in case it rains unexpectedly.
She brought her passport, just in case she needed to show identification.
He set two alarms just in case he didn't wake up to the first.

Synset Examples

in case of trouble call 911
I have money, just in case

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in case - if something happens or as a precaution
  • Synonym: in the event - if something should happen; as a precaution

2. ADV

/ dʒʌst ɪn keɪs / (AmE), / dʒʌst ɪn keɪs / (BrE)
as a precaution; to be prepared for the possibility that something might happen
as a precaution

Examples

Take your umbrella just in case it rains later.
I always keep some extra cash in my wallet just in case.
Bring a sweater just in case it gets cold tonight.

Synset Examples

She carries a pill in the pocket just in case.
