to keep one's cool

1. VERB

/ kip wʌnz kul / (AmE), / kiːp wʌnz kuːl / (BrE)
to remain calm and in control of one's emotions, especially in a stressful or difficult situation
remain calm under pressure

Examples

Even during the heated debate, she managed to keep her cool and respond politely.
It's important to keep your cool when dealing with difficult customers.
He struggled to keep his cool after receiving the unexpected news.

Synset Examples

In stressful situations, it's vital to keep one's cool.
She managed to keep her cool during the crisis.
He failed to keep his cool when things went wrong.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to lose one's cool - to suddenly become angry or upset
  • Synonym: to keep one's head - to remain calm in a difficult situation
  • Synonym: stay calm - to remain calm and not get upset
