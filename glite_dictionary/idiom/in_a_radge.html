in a radge

1. X

/ ɪn ə rædʒ / (AmE), / ɪn ə rædʒ / (BrE)
In a state of anger or extreme annoyance.
angry; extremely annoyed
british

Examples

After the argument, he was in a radge all evening.
If you keep bothering her, she'll soon be in a radge.
The manager was in a radge when the project was delayed.

Synset Examples

She was in a radge after hearing the news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in a rage - in a state of intense anger

Etymology

'Radge' is chiefly Scottish, probably an alteration of 'rage', ultimately from Latin 'rabies' (madness).
