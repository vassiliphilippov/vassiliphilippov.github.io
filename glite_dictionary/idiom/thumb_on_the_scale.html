thumb on the scale

1. X

/ θʌm ɑn ðə skeɪl / (AmE), / θʌm ɒn ðə skeɪl / (BrE)
to manipulate a process or situation in a dishonest or unfair way to gain an advantage
act to unfairly influence a result

Examples

The judge was accused of putting his thumb on the scale during the trial.
When regulators have their thumb on the scale, businesses can struggle to compete fairly.
If someone has their thumb on the scale, the results are never trustworthy.

Synset Examples

She had her thumb on the scale during the weigh-in.
If you have your thumb on the scale, it's unfair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tilt the scales - to influence the outcome in one's favor
  • Synonym: to stack the deck - to arrange something in a way that unfairly increases one's chances

Etymology

From the practice of physically pressing a thumb on a weighing scale to artificially increase a measurement, especially by dishonest vendors.
