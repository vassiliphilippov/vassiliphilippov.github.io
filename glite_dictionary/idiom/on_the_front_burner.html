on the front burner

1. ADJ

/ ɑn ðə frʌnt ˈbɝnɚ / (AmE), / ɒn ðə frʌnt ˈbɜːnə / (BrE)
considered to be a priority or urgent issue that needs immediate attention
top priority; urgent issue

Examples

The new marketing plan is on the front burner while older initiatives are on hold.
With the deadline approaching, this project is on the front burner for the whole team.
Her career goals are now on the front burner after years of focusing on family.

Synset Examples

The budget review is on the front burner this week.
Her health is now on the front burner of her priorities.
This issue needs to be on the front burner today.
We have to put this project on the front burner.
Her proposal is now on the front burner for review.
Let’s put this project on the front burner for now.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: on the back burner - considered a low priority or to be delayed until later

Etymology

From the practice in cooking of placing the most important or immediate pot on the stove's front burner for easier access.
