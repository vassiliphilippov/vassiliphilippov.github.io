to roll back the years

1. VERB

/ roʊl bæk ðə jɪrz / (AmE), / rəʊl bak ðə jɪəz / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel as if they are younger or to bring back nostalgic memories of the past
make one feel younger or nostalgic

Examples

Hearing their old hit song can roll back the years for many fans.
The reunion really seemed to roll back the years for the classmates, making them feel young again.
Watching classic movies helps me roll back the years and remember my childhood.

Synset Examples

That song can really roll back the years for me.
Looking at old photos can roll back the years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nostalgic - feeling pleasure and sadness about the past
