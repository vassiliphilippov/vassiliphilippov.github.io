got news for you

1. X

/ ɡɑt nuːz fər ju / (AmE), / ɡɒt njuːz fə juː / (BrE)
used to introduce surprising or important information, often with emphasis or irony.
to introduce surprising or important news

Examples

Got news for you, the meeting has been postponed until next week.
Listen, got news for you: we're moving to a new city next month.
If you thought he was retiring, got news for you—he just signed a new contract.

Synset Examples

Have I got news for you, the event is canceled!
Have I got news for you, we won the lottery!
Have I got news for you, she’s moving abroad!
