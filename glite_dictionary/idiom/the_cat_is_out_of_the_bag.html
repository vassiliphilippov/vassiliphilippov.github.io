the cat is out of the bag

1. X

/ ðə ˈkæt ɪz aʊt əv ðə bæɡ / (AmE), / ðə ˈkat ɪz aʊt əv ðə bæɡ / (BrE)
used to say that a secret has been revealed or is no longer hidden
a secret has been revealed

Examples

I was planning a surprise, but now the cat is out of the bag.
Now that the cat is out of the bag, we can talk openly about the new project.
Once you told James, the cat is out of the bag and everyone will soon know.

Synset Examples

The cat was out of the bag before the surprise party.
Now that the cat's out of the bag, we can celebrate.
Once she told him, the cat's out of the bag.
He tried to keep it a secret, but the cat's out of the bag.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spill the beans - reveal a secret
  • Antonym: keep under wraps - keep something secret

Etymology

Probably from the old practice of substituting a less valuable cat for a pig in a sack at markets; once the cat was out, the trick was revealed.
