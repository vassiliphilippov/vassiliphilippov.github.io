to hit the bottle

1. VERB

/ hɪt ðə ˈbɑt̬.əl / (AmE), / hɪt ðə ˈbɒt.əl / (BrE)
to start drinking alcoholic beverages, often heavily or habitually
drink alcohol, often heavily

Examples

After losing his job, he began to hit the bottle more frequently.
She was known to hit the bottle when life got stressful.
It's never a good solution to hit the bottle after an argument.

Synset Examples

We were up drinking all night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drink - to consume liquid, especially alcohol

2. VERB

/ hɪt ðə ˈbɑt̬l̩ / (AmE), / hɪt ðə ˈbɒtl̩ / (BrE)
to start drinking alcohol heavily, often as a way of coping with problems or stress
drink alcohol heavily

Examples

After losing his job, he began to hit the bottle almost every night.
She promised her family she wouldn’t hit the bottle again.
Many people turn to hit the bottle when faced with personal difficulties.

Synset Examples

She vowed not to hit the bottle during the week.
He hit the bottle hard after losing his job.
After the breakup, he started to hit the bottle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drink to excess - to consume alcohol in large amounts
  • Antonym: to abstain - to deliberately not consume alcohol or other intoxicants
