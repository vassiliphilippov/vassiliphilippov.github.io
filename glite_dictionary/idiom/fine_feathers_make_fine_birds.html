fine feathers make fine birds

1. X

/ faɪn ˈfɛðɚz meɪk faɪn bɝdz / (AmE), / faɪn ˈfɛðəz meɪk faɪn bɜːdz / (BrE)
People are often judged by their outward appearance; attractive or stylish clothing can make someone appear more impressive.
appearance creates good first impressions (proverb)

Examples

My grandmother always said that fine feathers make fine birds when I dressed up for special occasions.
In the world of fashion, it's often true that fine feathers make fine birds.
He bought a new suit for the interview because he knows fine feathers make fine birds.

Synset Examples

He believes fine feathers make fine birds, so he dresses well.
Remember, fine feathers make fine birds in the business world.
Her outfit proved that fine feathers make fine birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clothes make the man - the way a person dresses influences how they are judged

Etymology

Refers to the idea that birds with beautiful feathers appear more attractive, used figuratively in English since at least the 16th century.
