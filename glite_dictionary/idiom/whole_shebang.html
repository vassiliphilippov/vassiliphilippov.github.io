whole shebang

1. NOUN

/ hoʊl ʃəˈbæŋ / (AmE), / həʊl ʃəˈbæŋ / (BrE)
everything involved or available; the entirety of something, usually referred to as a single unit or collection.
everything; the entire lot
informal

Examples

After buying the deluxe package, we got the whole shebang included in our vacation deal.
He decided to sell the whole shebang rather than just a few items from his collection.
When we moved, we packed up the whole shebang and started fresh in a new city.

Synset Examples

we saw the whole shebang
a hotdog with the works
we took on the whole caboodle
for $10 you get the full treatment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the works - everything included; all available items or options
  • Synonym: the whole caboodle - the entire lot; everything

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from 19th-century American slang, originally referring to a hut or dwelling and later generalized.
