like a leech

1. ADV

/ laɪk ə liːtʃ / (AmE), / laɪk ə liːtʃ / (BrE)
Behaving in a way that is excessively dependent on or exploitative of others, especially for support or resources.
overly dependent or exploitative (simile)

Examples

Ever since he lost his job, Tom has been living like a leech off his parents.
I don't want to seem like a leech, so let me pay for my share.
She worries that her brother acts like a leech, always asking her for money without offering anything in return.

Synset Examples

He felt like a leech, always borrowing money.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parasite - a person who habitually relies on or exploits others

Etymology

From the noun 'leech', an old term for a bloodsucking worm, used figuratively for people who exploit others.
