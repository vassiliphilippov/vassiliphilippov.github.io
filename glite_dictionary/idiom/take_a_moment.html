to take a moment

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə ˈmoʊmənt / (AmE), / teɪk ə ˈməʊmənt / (BrE)
to pause briefly in order to think, rest, or acknowledge something
pause briefly for reflection or rest

Examples

Before making a decision, please take a moment to consider all the options.
He likes to take a moment each morning to meditate before starting his day.
Let's take a moment to remember those who helped us succeed.

Synset Examples

Let's take a moment to appreciate our achievements.
Take a moment to rest before tackling the next task.
She took a moment to breathe before speaking.
She took a moment to dry her eyes after the movie.
The performer took a moment to bow after the applause.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pause - to temporarily stop an activity
  • Synonym: to take a break - to rest for a short time from work or activity
