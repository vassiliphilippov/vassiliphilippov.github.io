to make a clean breast of

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə klin brɛst ʌv / (AmE), / meɪk ə kliːn brɛst ɒv / (BrE)
to confess or fully disclose something, especially a wrongdoing or mistake
confess or fully disclose (idiom)

Examples

After months of denial, he finally made a clean breast of the fraud scheme to the authorities.
She decided to make a clean breast of her involvement before things got worse.
It's always better to make a clean breast of your mistakes rather than let them haunt you.

Synset Examples

the writer of the anonymous letter owned up after they identified his handwriting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confess - to admit to wrongdoing or a crime
  • Antonym: to conceal - to hide or keep something secret

Etymology

From the metaphor of uncovering the breast to reveal everything; recorded in English since the 18th century.

2. VERB

/ meɪk ə klin brɛst ʌv / (AmE), / meɪk ə kliːn brɛst ɒv / (BrE)
to confess fully or reveal the truth about something, especially something wrong or embarrassing
to confess or admit openly

Examples

After days of anxiety, he decided to make a clean breast of his role in the incident.
She wanted to make a clean breast of her mistakes before anyone else found out.
The manager urged the employee to make a clean breast of what happened to avoid worse consequences.

Synset Examples

It's time to make a clean breast of all the secrets.
He decided to make a clean breast of his mistakes.
She wanted to make a clean breast of her past.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confess - to admit to something, especially wrongdoing or a crime
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep something secret or hidden

Etymology

Refers metaphorically to revealing one's heart, dating from the 18th century, where 'breast' stood for feelings or conscience.
