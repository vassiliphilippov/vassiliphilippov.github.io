to queer someone's pitch

1. VERB

/ kwɪr ˈsʌmwʌnz pɪtʃ / (AmE), / kwɪə ˈsʌmwəns pɪtʃ / (BrE)
to spoil or disrupt someone's plans, making it more difficult for them to succeed
spoil or disrupt someone's plans
british

Examples

He didn't mean to queer someone's pitch, but his comments ruined the negotiations.
If you arrive late and take her seat, you'll queer someone's pitch for sure.
The last thing I want is to queer someone's pitch just before the big presentation.

Synset Examples

It's easy to queer someone's pitch if you're not careful.
Don't queer someone's pitch by interrupting them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoil someone's plans - to affect someone's arrangements or intentions so they cannot succeed

Etymology

Originally British English; 'queer' meaning 'spoil' dates to the late 19th century; 'pitch' refers to a performance spot or opportunity.

2. VERB

/ kwɪr ˈsʌmwʌnz pɪtʃ / (AmE), / kwɪə ˈsʌmwənz pɪtʃ / (BrE)
to spoil, disrupt, or undermine someone's plans or proposals, making success more difficult or impossible
to disrupt someone's plans
british

Examples

His careless remark queered my pitch during the meeting, and my proposal was rejected.
The unexpected announcement queered her pitch for a promotion at work.
You risk queering your own pitch if you ignore their advice.

Synset Examples

She accidentally queered his pitch with her comments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sabotage - deliberately destroy or obstruct
  • Synonym: thwart - prevent from accomplishing something

Etymology

The phrase originates from 19th-century British slang, where 'queer' meant 'to spoil' and 'pitch' referred to a place of business or opportunity.
