to hold one's nose

1. VERB

/ hoʊld wʌnz noʊz / (AmE), / həʊld wʌnz nəʊz / (BrE)
To tolerate or accept something unpleasant, often while trying to avoid noticing its negative aspects.
endure something unpleasant

Examples

Sometimes you just have to hold your nose and do what needs to be done.
He decided to hold his nose and accept the job promotion, despite the extra stress.
Many voters felt they had to hold their noses and choose the lesser of two evils.

Synset Examples

He had to hold his nose while cleaning the fish.
You might need to hold your nose during that meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to grin and bear it - to accept something unpleasant without complaining
  • Synonym: to put up with - to tolerate or endure

2. VERB

/ hoʊld wʌnz noʊz / (AmE), / həʊld wʌnz nəʊz / (BrE)
to do something unpleasant or distasteful, usually because it is necessary
do something unpleasant despite misgivings

Examples

Sometimes you just have to hold your nose and finish the task, no matter how much you dislike it.
He didn't want to lie, but he decided to hold his nose and go along with the plan.
We may have to hold our noses and accept the proposal for the greater good.

Synset Examples

She decided to hold her nose and dive into the cold water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bite the bullet - to do something difficult or unpleasant that one has been putting off or hesitating over
  • Synonym: swallow the bitter pill - to accept or do something unpleasant or difficult
