shape up or ship out

1. X

/ ʃeɪp ʌp ɔr ʃɪp aʊt / (AmE), / ʃeɪp ʌp ɔː ʃɪp aʊt / (BrE)
used to tell someone that they must improve their performance, behavior, or attitude, or else leave their position or situation
improve or leave (idiom)

Examples

The manager warned the team to shape up or ship out if they wanted to keep their jobs.
It's shape up or ship out in this company when it comes to meeting targets.
Parents sometimes tell their children to shape up or ship out when behavior gets out of control.

Synset Examples

If you can't meet deadlines, it's shape up or ship out.
The boss said it was shape up or ship out for the team.
The coach told the players to shape up or ship out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shape up - to improve performance or behavior
