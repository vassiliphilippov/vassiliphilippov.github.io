to scare the bejesus out of

1. VERB

/ skɛr ðə bɪˈdʒizəs ˈaʊt ʌv / (AmE), / skeə ðə bɪˈdʒiːzəs ˈaʊt ɒv / (BrE)
to frighten someone very much; to cause someone a sudden and intense fear or shock
frighten someone intensely (idiom)
informal

Examples

The dog jumped out and scared the bejesus out of me as I walked by the gate.
That thunderstorm last night scared the bejesus out of everyone in the neighborhood.
When the balloon popped behind her, it scared the bejesus out of my little sister.

Synset Examples

That movie scared the bejesus out of the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to frighten - to make someone afraid or scared
  • Synonym: to startle - to cause someone to feel sudden surprise or alarm
  • Antonym: to reassure - to make someone feel less afraid or anxious

Etymology

Alteration of 'Jesus' as an exclamation, forming 'bejesus' for emphasis.
