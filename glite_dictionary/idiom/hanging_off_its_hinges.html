hanging off its hinges

1. ADJ

/ ˈhæŋ.ɪŋ ɑf ɪts ˈhɪndʒɪz / (AmE), / ˈhæŋ.ɪŋ ɒf ɪts ˈhɪndʒɪz / (BrE)
(of a door, gate, or similar object) not properly attached or functional, often because it is damaged or loose from its supports.
not properly attached; loose from its hinges

Examples

The old shed's door was hanging off its hinges after years of neglect.
After the accident, the mailbox was hanging off its hinges and barely standing.
You could tell the building was abandoned because every window was hanging off its hinges.

Synset Examples

The storm left the gate hanging off its hinges.
The door swung open and fell off its hinges.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: falling off its hinges - coming completely away from the hinges; detached
