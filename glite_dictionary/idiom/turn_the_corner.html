to turn the corner

1. VERB

/ tɝn ðə ˈkɔrnɚ / (AmE), / tɜːn ðə ˈkɔːnə / (BrE)
to begin to improve after a difficult period or to recover from a setback or illness
begin to improve after difficulty

Examples

After months of slow sales, the company finally turned the corner and started making a profit.
Following surgery, her doctor said she had turned the corner and would recover quickly.
The team turned the corner after a string of losses, winning several matches in a row.

Synset Examples

I think we finally turned the corner in our project.
After months of hard work, we've turned the corner.
She felt she had turned the corner in her recovery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recover - to return to a normal state after a setback or illness
  • Antonym: to deteriorate - to become worse in condition or quality
