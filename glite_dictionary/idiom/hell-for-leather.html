hell-for-leather

1. ADV

/ ˌhɛl fə ˈlɛðɚ / (AmE), / ˌhɛl fə ˈlɛðə / (BrE)
At full speed; as fast as possible.
at full speed, as fast as possible

Examples

The car raced hell-for-leather down the empty highway, trying to beat the approaching storm.
She ran hell-for-leather to catch the last train home that evening.
They cycled hell-for-leather to make it to the picnic before the rain started.

Synset Examples

they were travelling hell-for-leather

Related Terms

  • Synonym: full tilt - at top speed or with maximum energy
  • Synonym: flat out - as fast or as hard as possible

Etymology

Originated in the 19th century, referring to riding horses at full gallop, risking leather saddles and tack.
