from way back

1. ADV

/ frəm weɪ bæk / (AmE), / frəm weɪ bak / (BrE)
used to indicate that something or someone has existed, been known, or in a particular state since long ago
since long ago; from a distant time

Examples

She and I have been friends from way back.
The building has been a landmark from way back.
They've known each other from way back, long before moving here.

Synset Examples

she knows him from way back
