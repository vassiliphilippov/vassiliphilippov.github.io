to make a mountain out of a molehill

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə ˈmaʊn.tən ˈaʊt əv ə ˈmoʊl.hɪl / (AmE), / meɪk ə ˈmaʊn.tɪn ˈaʊt əv ə ˈməʊl.hɪl / (BrE)
to exaggerate the importance or seriousness of a minor problem or issue
exaggerate a minor problem

Examples

You shouldn't make a mountain out of a molehill every time something minor happens at work.
Whenever mistakes are made, he tends to make a mountain out of a molehill and overreact.
Let's not make a mountain out of a molehill; the issue is easily fixed.

Synset Examples

He always makes a mountain out of a molehill when stressed.
She tends to make a mountain out of a molehill during meetings.
Stop making a mountain out of a molehill, it's not that serious.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exaggerate - to represent a situation as greater than it really is
  • Antonym: to understate - to present something as being smaller or less important than it really is
