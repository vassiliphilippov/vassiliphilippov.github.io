to get on someone's nerves

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɑn ˈsʌmwʌnz nɝvz / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒn ˈsʌmwʌnz nɜːvz / (BrE)
to annoy or irritate someone, especially in a way that is persistent or particularly bothersome
annoy or irritate someone

Examples

Her loud chewing really gets on my nerves when we're eating dinner together.
It gets on my nerves when people are late without letting me know.
His constant whistling at work gets on my nerves after a while.

Synset Examples

His constant humming is starting to get on my nerves.
She really knows how to get on my nerves sometimes.
It gets on my nerves when people interrupt.
His stupidish behavior is getting on my nerves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to annoy - to make someone slightly angry
  • Synonym: to irritate - to make someone feel annoyed or impatient
  • Antonym: please - to make someone feel happy or satisfied
