at liberty

1. ADJ

/ æt ˈlɪbɚti / (AmE), / ət ˈlɪbəti / (BrE)
free to do as one wishes; not under restraint or restriction.
free to do as one wishes

Examples

You are at liberty to leave whenever you wish.
The witness was finally at liberty to speak after the court's decision.
Now that her contract has ended, she is at liberty to work elsewhere.

Synset Examples

He felt at liberty to take a break whenever he wanted.
You are at liberty to choose your own path.
She was finally at liberty to express her opinions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: free - not under the control or in the power of another; able to act or be as one wishes
  • Antonym: restricted - limited in freedom or movement; kept within bounds

Etymology

From the noun 'liberty,' from Old French 'liberté,' from Latin 'libertas' (freedom).
