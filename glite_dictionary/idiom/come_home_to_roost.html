to come home to roost

1. VERB

/ kʌm hoʊm tu ruːst / (AmE), / kʌm həʊm tuː ruːst / (BrE)
(of one's actions or words) to have unpleasant consequences for the person who performed or said them; to face the results of one's behavior, usually after some time.
to face consequences of one's actions

Examples

After years of neglecting his health, the effects finally came home to roost in his forties.
All the lies she told at work are about to come home to roost.
His dishonesty came home to roost when no one trusted him anymore.

Synset Examples

His bad decisions finally came home to roost.
It's true that the chickens come home to roost after a while.
When the chickens come home to roost, he'll regret his actions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reap what you sow - to experience the consequences of one's actions

Etymology

Originates from the proverb 'curses, like chickens, come home to roost,' referring to the idea that bad deeds return to cause trouble.
