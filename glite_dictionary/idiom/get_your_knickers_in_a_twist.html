to get your knickers in a twist

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt jɔr ˈnɪkərz ɪn ə twɪst / (AmE), / ɡɛt jɔː ˈnɪkəz ɪn ə twɪst / (BrE)
to become upset, agitated, or overly worried, especially about something minor or trivial
become upset or agitated
british informal

Examples

There's no need to get your knickers in a twist; the deadline has been extended.
She tends to get her knickers in a twist over the smallest things at work.
He always gets his knickers in a twist whenever the plans change unexpectedly.

Synset Examples

She gets her knickers in a twist over every little mistake.

Try not to get your knickers in a twist over minor issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make a mountain out of a molehill - to exaggerate a minor problem
  • Antonym: keep your cool - to remain calm under stress

Etymology

British slang, originating mid-20th century, using 'knickers' (women's underwear) humorously to suggest agitation.
