all that jazz

1. X

/ ɔl ðæt dʒæz / (AmE), / ɔːl ðæt dʒæz / (BrE)
used to refer to similar things or actions, or to indicate that more of the same could be mentioned.
and other similar things

Examples

She bought decorations, invitations, snacks, and all that jazz for the party.
He keeps talking about yoga, meditation, mindfulness, and all that jazz.
I'm tired of the meetings, calls, emails, and all that jazz every day.

Synset Examples

We went to the concert and all that jazz.
He packed his guitar and all that jazz.
I love jazz music and all that jazz.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: and so on - used to indicate continuation of a list in a similar way
  • Synonym: et cetera - and other similar things; etc.

Etymology

Originally from 1920s American slang, with 'jazz' representing lively things or activities.
