to wash one's mouth out

1. VERB

/ wɑʃ wʌnz maʊθ aʊt / (AmE), / wɒʃ wʌnz maʊθ aʊt / (BrE)
to punish or reprimand oneself or someone else for using inappropriate or offensive language, often used humorously or as a mild rebuke
punish for inappropriate language

Examples

After accidentally using a rude word, she said she should wash her mouth out with soap.
You'd better wash your mouth out if you keep talking like that in front of Grandma.
He laughed and admitted he might need to wash his mouth out after telling that joke.

Synset Examples

He joked that he needed to wash his mouth out after that comment.
If you keep cursing, I'll have to wash your mouth out!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clean one's mouth - to remove inappropriate content from one's speech
