away with someone

1. X

/ əˈweɪ wɪð ˈsʌmwʌn / (AmE), / əˈweɪ wɪð ˈsʌmwʌn / (BrE)
used to tell someone forcefully and dismissively to go away
expression to tell someone to go away

Examples

Away with you! I have no time for complaints this morning.
When the children started making noise, she shouted, "Away with you!"
He waved his hand and said, "Away with you!" in a tone that brooked no argument.

Synset Examples

When he complained, she just said, 'Away with you!'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go away - to leave or depart from a place or person
