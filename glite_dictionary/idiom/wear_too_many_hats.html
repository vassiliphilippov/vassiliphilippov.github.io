to wear too many hats

1. VERB

/ wɛr tu ˈmɛni hæts / (AmE), / wɛə tu ˈmɛni hæts / (BrE)
to take on more roles or responsibilities than one can effectively manage, often at the same time
take on too many roles or duties

Examples

Since the company downsized, Maria has been wearing too many hats and rarely takes a break.
If you try wearing too many hats, some tasks are bound to be neglected.
Frank realized he was wearing too many hats when he started falling behind in his work.

Synset Examples

He realized he was wearing too many hats and needed help.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spread oneself too thin - to try to do too many things at the same time, causing a lack of effectiveness

Etymology

From the practice of wearing different hats to indicate different professional or social roles.
