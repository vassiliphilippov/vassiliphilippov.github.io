tit for tat

1. NOUN

/ tɪt fər tæt / (AmE), / tɪt fə tæt / (BrE)
an equivalent action given in return for something done, especially as retaliation or revenge
retaliatory action in return

Examples

Her actions were simply tit for tat after the way he'd treated her.
The two countries imposed tit for tat tariffs, escalating the trade war.
Responding with tit for tat seldom solves the underlying problem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revenge - the act of hurting someone in return for harm
  • Synonym: reprisal - an act of retaliation
  • Antonym: forgiveness - the act of stopping anger or resentment toward someone

Etymology

Possibly from an old alteration of 'tip for tap,' both imitating the sound of light blows.

2. NOUN

/ tɪt fər tæt / (AmE), / tɪt fə tæt / (BrE)
an equivalent retaliation; an action or insult given in return for one received, often as part of a repeated exchange
equivalent retaliation; reciprocal action

Examples

The two companies engaged in tit for tat by raising their prices every week.
Political debates often devolve into tit for tat arguments rather than constructive discussion.
Their friendly competition became a petty tit for tat over minor issues.

Synset Examples

The rivalry was purely tit for tat, with no resolution in sight.
Their argument quickly escalated into a tit for tat situation.
In negotiations, it often turns into tit for tat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retaliation - the act of returning an attack; revenge
  • Synonym: revenge - the action of inflicting hurt or harm on someone for an injury or wrong

Etymology

Origin uncertain; attested from the mid-16th century, possibly from dialectal variants or as a reduplication expressing reciprocal action.
