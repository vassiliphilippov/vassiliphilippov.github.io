to touch grass

1. VERB

/ tʌtʃ ɡræs / (AmE), / tʌtʃ ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
to take a break from online or virtual activities and spend time in the real world, especially outdoors or in nature
take a break from the online world; go outside
informal slang

Examples

You've been on your computer for hours—maybe it's time to touch grass.
Sometimes all you need is to touch grass and clear your mind.
After a week of working from home, she decided to touch grass and visit the park.

Synset Examples
