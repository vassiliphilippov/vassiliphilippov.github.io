chickens come home to roost

1. X

/ ˈtʃɪk.ɪnz kʌm hoʊm tu roʊst / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪk.ɪnz kʌm həʊm tuː rəʊst / (BrE)
used to say that the bad consequences of one's past actions have returned to cause problems for them
bad consequences returning to someone

Examples

After years of cheating his business partners, the chickens came home to roost and he lost his company.
She ignored her health for years, but now the chickens have come home to roost.
His reckless spending meant the chickens came home to roost when he went bankrupt.

Synset Examples

Eventually, the chickens come home to roost for his reckless decisions.

Etymology

Proverbial use dating from the 14th century; originally from the idea that a bird returns to its home to sleep.
