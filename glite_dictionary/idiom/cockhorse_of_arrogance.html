cockhorse of arrogance

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑkˌhɔrs əv əˈrɛɡəns / (AmE), / ˈkɒkˌhɔːs əv əˈræɡəns / (BrE)
A metaphorical expression describing overt and boastful self-assurance or arrogance.
metaphor for excessive arrogance

Examples

Many politicians find themselves astride the cockhorse of arrogance after a landslide victory.
She warned him not to mount the cockhorse of arrogance just because of one success.
Critics accused the CEO of riding the cockhorse of arrogance during the press conference.

Synset Examples

He rides that cockhorse of arrogance daily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrogance - the quality of being overly self-important or proud
  • Antonym: humility - the quality of being humble and modest

Etymology

From 'cockhorse', meaning a high-spirited or proud horse, combined with 'arrogance' to create a metaphor for overblown self-confidence.
