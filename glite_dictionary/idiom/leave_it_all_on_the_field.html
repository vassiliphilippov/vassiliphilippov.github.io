to leave it all on the field

1. VERB

/ liv ɪt ɔl ɑn ðə fild / (AmE), / liːv ɪt ɔːl ɒn ðə fiːld / (BrE)
to give the maximum possible effort or commitment, especially during a competition or activity
give maximum effort (esp. in competition)

Examples

The coach urged the team to leave it all on the field in the championship match.
No matter the outcome, she always tries to leave it all on the field.
They may have lost the game, but they definitely left it all on the field.

Synset Examples

She promised to leave it all on the field during the finals.
He always leaves it all on the field, giving 100%.
The players were determined to leave it all on the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: give it your all - to make the greatest effort possible
  • Antonym: to hold back - to not give full effort
