to see eye to eye

1. VERB

/ si aɪ tə aɪ / (AmE), / siː aɪ tʊ aɪ / (BrE)
to agree with someone; to have the same opinion or point of view
be in agreement

Examples

Although we don't always see eye to eye, we respect each other's opinions.
My sister and I rarely saw eye to eye when we were teenagers.
They finally started seeing eye to eye after a long discussion.

Synset Examples

We never saw eye to eye on this question

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to agree - to have the same opinion as someone else
  • Antonym: to disagree - to have a different opinion; not be in agreement

2. VERB

/ si ˈaɪ tə ˈaɪ / (AmE), / siː ˈaɪ tə ˈaɪ / (BrE)
to agree with someone or have the same opinion about something
to agree or have the same view

Examples

Despite their differences, the two leaders finally see eye to eye on the new policy.
My sister and I rarely see eye to eye about what movie to watch.
It's hard for a team to succeed if its members don't see eye to eye on their goals.

Synset Examples

It's important for partners to see eye to eye.
They finally learned to see eye to eye on the issue.
We don't always see eye to eye, but we respect each other.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to agree - to have the same opinion
  • Antonym: to disagree - to have a different opinion
