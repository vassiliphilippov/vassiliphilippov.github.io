skin in the game

1. NOUN

/ skɪn ɪn ðə ɡeɪm / (AmE), / skɪn ɪn ðə ɡeɪm / (BrE)
having a personal investment or involvement that makes one risk something valuable, such as money, reputation, or effort, in a project or undertaking.
personal risk or investment in something

Examples

He refused to invest unless he had skin in the game and a say in the decisions.
Politicians often lack skin in the game when making policies that affect others.
She put her own money into the company, finally giving her real skin in the game.

Synset Examples

He has skin in the game, so he cares about the project.
Investors need skin in the game to take risks.
Without skin in the game, it's hard to trust their advice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stake - a share or interest in a business, situation, or undertaking
