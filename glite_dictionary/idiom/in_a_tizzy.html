in a tizzy

1. ADV

/ ɪn ə ˈtɪzi / (AmE), / ɪn ə ˈtɪzi / (BrE)
in a state of nervous excitement, confusion, or agitation
in a state of nervous excitement or confusion

Examples

She was in a tizzy when she realized she was late for the meeting.
The whole office was in a tizzy after the sudden announcement of changes.
Don't get in a tizzy over a little mistake; everything will be fine.

Synset Examples

She was in a tizzy about the upcoming presentation.
He always gets in a tizzy before big events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agitated - feeling or appearing troubled or nervous
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness or agitation
