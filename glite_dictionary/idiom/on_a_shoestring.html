on a shoestring

1. ADV

/ ɑn ə ˈʃuˌstrɪŋ / (AmE), / ɒn ə ˈʃuːstrɪŋ / (BrE)
using very little money or with only a very small budget.
with very little money

Examples

Despite launching the company on a shoestring, they quickly found loyal customers.
She traveled across Europe on a shoestring by staying in hostels and eating street food.
The independent film was made on a shoestring but received critical acclaim.

Synset Examples

Traveling the world on a shoestring can be quite an adventure.
He organized the event on a shoestring, but it was successful.
Starting a business on a shoestring requires creativity.
He lived on a shoestring while studying abroad.
They managed to travel on a shoestring budget.

Etymology

From the idea of shoestrings as something inexpensive and minimal, used since the early 20th century.
