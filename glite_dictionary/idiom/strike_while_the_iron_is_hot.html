to strike while the iron is hot

1. VERB

/ straɪk waɪl ði ˈaɪɚn ɪz hɑt / (AmE), / straɪk waɪl ði ˈaɪən ɪz hɒt / (BrE)
to act quickly in order to take advantage of an opportunity while it lasts
act quickly while opportunity exists

Examples

I decided to strike while the iron is hot and started my own business as soon as I graduated.
Investors should strike while the iron is hot before property prices rise further.
If you want that promotion, you need to strike while the iron is hot and put your application in now.

Synset Examples

You should strike while the iron is hot and accept the job offer.
He plans to strike while the iron is hot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seize the day - make the most of the present moment; take advantage of opportunities
  • Antonym: miss the boat - fail to take advantage of an opportunity

Etymology

From blacksmithing, referring to shaping iron when it is hot and malleable.
