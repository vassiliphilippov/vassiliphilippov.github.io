to rain on someone's parade

1. VERB

/ reɪn ɑn ˈsʌmwʌnz pəˈreɪd / (AmE), / reɪn ɒn ˈsʌmwʌnz pəˈreɪd / (BrE)
to spoil someone's plans, happiness, or enthusiasm, especially by criticizing or pointing out problems
spoil someone's plans or enthusiasm

Examples

I don't want to rain on your parade, but I don't think your plan will work.
She was so excited about her promotion; I didn't have the heart to rain on her parade.
Even though he tried to rain on my parade, I stayed positive and continued celebrating.

Synset Examples

Don’t rain on my parade; I’m excited about the trip!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoil the fun - to ruin someone's enjoyment or amusement
  • Antonym: to lift someone's spirits - to make someone happier or more enthusiastic

Etymology

Metaphor from literal parades being disrupted by rain; originated in American English in the mid-20th century.
