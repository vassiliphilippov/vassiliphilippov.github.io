to come unhinged

1. VERB

/ kʌm ʌnˈhɪndʒd / (AmE), / kʌm ʌnˈhɪndʒd / (BrE)
To lose control of one's emotions or actions; to become mentally unstable or extremely upset.
lose emotional or mental control

Examples

After weeks of constant stress, he finally came unhinged during the meeting.
Some people feared that she might come unhinged after the unexpected loss.
When the project failed, the usually calm director came unhinged in front of everyone.

Synset Examples

The manager came unhinged after the team missed the target.
He really came unhinged when he lost his job.
She might come unhinged if pushed too far.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lose it - to lose control of one’s emotions or composure
  • Synonym: to fall apart - to break down emotionally or mentally
  • Antonym: to keep it together - to remain composed and calm

Etymology

Metaphorically derived from the idea of a door or object coming off its hinges, thus no longer stable.
