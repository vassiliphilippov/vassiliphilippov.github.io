to knock down a peg

1. VERB

/ nɑk daʊn ə pɛɡ / (AmE), / nɒk daʊn ə pɛɡ / (BrE)
to reduce someone's pride or self-importance, often by criticizing or humiliating them
reduce someone's pride or self-importance

Examples

After boasting about his achievements, his boss quickly knocked him down a peg in front of the team.
The defeat in the final match really knocked the champion down a peg.
She managed to knock him down a peg with a single clever remark.

Synset Examples

She knocked him down a peg with her witty comeback.

His arrogance was knocked down a peg after the failure.
Her loss in the game took her down a peg.
It's time to take him down a peg or two.
He needed to be taken down a peg after bragging all day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take down a peg - to make someone less proud or confident

Etymology

Peg refers to a notched stake used for measuring, historically used in expressions about lowering rank or importance.
