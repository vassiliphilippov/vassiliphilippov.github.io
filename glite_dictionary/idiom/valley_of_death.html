valley of death

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæli əv ˈdɛθ / (AmE), / ˈvæli əv ˈdɛθ / (BrE)
a very dangerous or perilous situation, often one where survival or success is uncertain
extremely dangerous situation

Examples

Crossing the desert without supplies can quickly become a valley of death for the unprepared.
For start-ups, the first two years are often called the valley of death because so many companies fail.
The rescue team entered the valley of death, risking their own lives to save the stranded hikers.

Synset Examples

The soldiers advanced courageously through the valley of death.
They trekked through the valley of death on their expedition.
He felt trapped in the valley of death, surrounded by danger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: danger zone - an area or situation that is very dangerous
  • Synonym: predicament - a difficult or dangerous situation
  • Antonym: safe haven - a place of safety or refuge, opposite in meaning

Etymology

From the biblical phrase 'the valley of the shadow of death' (Psalm 23); popularized in English by Tennyson's 'The Charge of the Light Brigade'.
