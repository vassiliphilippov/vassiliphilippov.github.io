to angle for farthings

1. VERB

/ ˈæŋɡəl fər ˈfɑrðɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡl fə ˈfɑːðɪŋz / (BrE)
to try to gain small advantages or benefits, often in a petty or calculating way
try to gain minor benefits
archaic british

Examples

He was angling for farthings during the negotiations, fussing over every tiny detail.
She criticized him for always angling for farthings rather than seeing the bigger picture.
In business, some people waste energy angling for farthings while missing real opportunities.

Synset Examples

He always angles for farthings when making a deal.

Etymology

From 'angle' meaning to fish or try to obtain (from Old English 'angel', a fishhook), and 'farthing', an old British coin of very small value.
