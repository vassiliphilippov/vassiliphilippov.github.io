live by the sword, die by the sword

1. X

/ lɪv baɪ ðə sɔrd daɪ baɪ ðə sɔrd / (AmE), / lɪv baɪ ðə sɔːd daɪ baɪ ðə sɔːd / (BrE)
used to warn that those who lead a violent or ruthless life are likely to suffer violent consequences themselves
one suffers consequences of a violent life

Examples

He always warned his friends that if you live by the sword, die by the sword, so they should be careful.
The mafia boss's end was a classic case of live by the sword, die by the sword.
It's a harsh truth in that world: those who live by the sword, die by the sword.

Synset Examples

The criminal learned that if you live by the sword, die by the sword.
In his dangerous profession, it was truly live by the sword, die by the sword.
He always said, live by the sword, die by the sword.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: what goes around comes around - people will eventually face the consequences of their actions
  • Synonym: to reap what you sow - you experience the consequences of your actions

Etymology

From the biblical saying in Matthew 26:52, 'all who draw the sword will die by the sword.'
