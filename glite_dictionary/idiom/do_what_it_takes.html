to do what it takes

1. VERB

/ du ˌwʌt ɪt teɪks / (AmE), / duː ˌwɒt ɪt teɪks / (BrE)
to take all necessary actions in order to achieve a desired goal, no matter how difficult
take all necessary actions to achieve a goal

Examples

She was determined to do what it takes to secure the promotion.
If you want to succeed in this business, you have to do what it takes.
They were willing to do what it takes to finish the project on time.

Synset Examples

I’ll do what it takes to finish this.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go the extra mile - to make a special effort to achieve something
  • Synonym: do whatever it takes - to use any means necessary to achieve a goal
