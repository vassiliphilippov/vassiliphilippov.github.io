the bottom of the barrel

1. NOUN

/ ðə ˈbɑtəm əv ðə ˈbɛrəl / (AmE), / ðə ˈbɒtəm əv ðə ˈbærəl / (BrE)
the least desirable or worst among a group of options, especially when choices have been depleted
the least desirable option

Examples

All the good cars were sold, so we're left with the bottom of the barrel.
Hiring from the bottom of the barrel is never a good business strategy.
By the end of the sale, the shelves were stocked with the bottom of the barrel.

Synset Examples

After much searching, I found the bottom of the barrel.
This is the bottom of the barrel for quality.
When it comes to choices, this is the bottom of the barrel.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cream of the crop - the best or most desirable among a group

Etymology

From the practice of scraping the bottom of a barrel for the remnants, indicating poor quality.
