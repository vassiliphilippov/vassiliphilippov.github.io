to take by storm

1. VERB

/ teɪk baɪ stɔrm / (AmE), / teɪk baɪ stɔːm / (BrE)
to suddenly become extremely popular or successful, attracting widespread attention and admiration
become suddenly popular or successful

Examples

The young singer's debut album took by storm and topped the charts within a week.
The new smartphone design is taking the market by storm with its innovative features.
That viral dance challenge took social media by storm last month.

Synset Examples

Chipzilla has taken the tech world by storm.
Her performance took the audience by storm.
The festival took the town by storm last weekend.
The Barbenheimer phenomenon took social media by storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be a hit - to be very successful or popular
  • Antonym: flop - to fail completely, especially in performance or popularity
