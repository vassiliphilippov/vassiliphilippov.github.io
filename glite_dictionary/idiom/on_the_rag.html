on the rag

1. ADJ

/ ɑn ðə ræɡ / (AmE), / ɒn ðə ræɡ / (BrE)
(of a woman) menstruating; having a period (used rudely or disparagingly, often considered vulgar).
menstruating (informal, vulgar)
informal vulgar

Examples

She gets quite moody when she's on the rag.
I realized she was on the rag when she mentioned needing painkillers.
Some find the phrase on the rag offensive, so it's best to use it cautiously.

Synset Examples

He noticed she was on the rag and gave her space.
She's been on the rag this week, feeling quite irritable.
Whenever she's on the rag, she prefers to stay home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to menstruate - to discharge blood as part of the menstrual cycle

Etymology

Originates from 20th-century slang, alluding to absorbent rags used during menstruation before commercial products were common.
