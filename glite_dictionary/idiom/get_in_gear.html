to get in gear

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɪn ɡɪr / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɪn ɡɪə / (BrE)
to begin to work effectively or with more energy; to start becoming productive or active
start working effectively
informal

Examples

You need to get in gear if you want to finish this project on time.
After a slow start, the team finally got in gear and scored three goals.
It's already noon; let's get in gear and clean the house.

Synset Examples

He finally got his ass in gear and started training.
Get your arse in gear and start working!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get going - to begin to move or act
  • Synonym: to get started - to commence action or activity
  • Synonym: to get moving - to begin to act with energy or speed
