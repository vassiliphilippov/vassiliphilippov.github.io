to give it the gun

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ɪt ðə ɡʌn / (AmE), / ɡɪv ɪt ðə ɡʌn / (BrE)
to apply maximum effort or proceed with great enthusiasm and energy, especially to complete something quickly or successfully
put in maximum effort; proceed with enthusiasm
informal

Examples

Before the final lap, the coach told us to give it the gun and not hold back.
If you really want that promotion, you have to give it the gun at work this month.
The students decided to give it the gun and finish the group project ahead of schedule.

Synset Examples

He urged everyone to give it the gun and finish strong.
They decided to give it the gun and start the project.
The team was ready to give it the gun and meet the deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go all out - to make the greatest possible effort
  • Antonym: to take it easy - to proceed without hurry or exertion

Etymology

Originally referencing accelerating quickly when driving, using 'the gun' as slang for the accelerator pedal or trigger.
