to boot

1. ADV

/ tə buːt / (AmE), / tə buːt / (BrE)
Used to indicate that something is added to what has already been mentioned; in addition; furthermore.
in addition; furthermore

Examples

The job comes with a generous salary to boot.
She is a talented musician and a skilled painter to boot.
The weather was perfect, and we got free tickets to boot.

Synset Examples

he serves additionally as the CEO

Related Terms

  • Synonym: additionally - as something added; in addition
  • Synonym: moreover - as a further matter; besides

Etymology

From Old English 'bōt' meaning 'advantage, remedy, compensation'.
