to get straight to the facts

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt streɪt tə ðə fækts / (AmE), / ɡɛt streɪt tʊ ðə fækts / (BrE)
to focus immediately and directly on the most important information, avoiding unnecessary details
focus directly on the important information

Examples

The detective asked her to get straight to the facts instead of describing every tiny detail.
In job interviews, it's best to get straight to the facts and highlight your main achievements.
We don't have much time, so could you please get straight to the facts?

Synset Examples

Can you get straight to the facts, please?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get to the point - to say the most important thing without delay
