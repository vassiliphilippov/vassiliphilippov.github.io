to play (someone) for a fool

1. VERB

/ pleɪ (ˈsʌmwʌn) fɚ ə ful / (AmE), / pleɪ (ˈsʌmwʌn) fə ə fuːl / (BrE)
to try to deceive or take advantage of someone by assuming they are naive or unintelligent
attempt to deceive someone as if they are foolish

Examples

He was angry when he realized his friends tried to play him for a fool.
Don’t play me for a fool; I know exactly what’s going on.
She refused to let her colleagues play her for a fool during the negotiations.

Synset Examples

Don't play for a fool; I know the truth.
He realized she was trying to play him for a fool.
She won’t let anyone play her for a fool again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deceive - to intentionally make someone believe something that is not true
  • Synonym: to outsmart - to defeat or trick someone by being more clever
