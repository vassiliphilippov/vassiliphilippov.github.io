to fall between two stools

1. VERB

/ fɔl bɪˈtwin tu ˈstulz / (AmE), / fɔːl bɪˈtwiːn tuː ˈstuːlz / (BrE)
to fail to achieve either of two aims because of trying to satisfy both at once
fail by trying to satisfy two aims at once

Examples

By trying to combine modern and traditional designs, the architect fell between two stools.
The proposal falls between two stools and satisfies neither side fully.
If you keep changing your approach, you might end up falling between two stools.

Synset Examples

He tried to please everyone and fell between two stools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fall short - to fail to reach a desired standard or goal

Etymology

From an old proverb about missing both of two chairs, originating in medieval England.
