to run, don't walk

1. VERB

/ rʌn doʊnt wɑk / (AmE), / rʌn dəʊnt wɔːk / (BrE)
used to urge someone to act immediately or with great urgency, especially to take advantage of an opportunity
urge to act with urgency

Examples

If you hear about a job opening at that company, run, don't walk to apply.
When they announced free tickets, everyone rushed to run, don't walk to the counter.
She told me if that book is still in stock, run, don't walk and buy it.

Synset Examples

The concert tickets sold out fast; it's run, don't walk time!
If you see that dress, run, don't walk to buy it!
When the sale started, customers began to run, don't walk to the store.
