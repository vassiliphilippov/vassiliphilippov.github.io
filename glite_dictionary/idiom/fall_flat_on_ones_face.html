to fall flat on one's face

1. VERB

/ fɔl flæt ɑn wʌnz feɪs / (AmE), / fɔːl flæt ɒn wʌnz feɪs / (BrE)
to fail completely or in an embarrassing way
fail in an embarrassing way

Examples

I tried to impress my boss with my idea, but I fell flat on my face during the meeting.
The new comedy show fell flat on its face after the first episode received terrible reviews.
She was so nervous about her speech that she almost fell flat on her face in front of the entire class.

Synset Examples

He thought he could cook but fell flat on his face.
He fell on his face during the presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fail - to not succeed in achieving a goal
