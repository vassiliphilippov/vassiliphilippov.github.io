to break a leg

1. VERB

/ breɪk ə lɛɡ / (AmE), / breɪk ə lɛɡ / (BrE)
used to wish someone good luck, especially before a performance
wish of good luck (esp. before a performance)

Examples

You’ve got this audition—break a leg!
Before the curtain rose, the cast whispered, 'break a leg' to one another.
Her friend smiled and told her to break a leg before she went on stage.

Synset Examples

She told her friend to break a leg before the audition.
Good luck on your performance; go break a leg!
The director always says to break a leg before every show.

Etymology

Possibly from a superstition among actors that wishing 'good luck' directly would bring bad luck, so a seemingly negative phrase was used instead.
