to cop oneself on

1. VERB

/ kɑp wʌnˈsɛlf ɑn / (AmE), / kɒp wʌnˈsɛlf ɒn / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly Irish English) To realize one's mistake or begin to behave more sensibly or responsibly.
realize one's mistake, become sensible (Irish idiom)
informal british

Examples

After the meeting, she finally copped herself on and apologized to her team.
It took failing the exam for him to cop himself on and start studying seriously.
You really need to cop yourself on if you want to keep your job.

Synset Examples

After the mistake, he finally copped himself on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wise up - to become aware or informed
  • Synonym: to realize - to become aware of something
  • Antonym: blunder on - to continue making mistakes without realizing

Etymology

From Irish English slang 'cop on', meaning to wise up or become aware.
