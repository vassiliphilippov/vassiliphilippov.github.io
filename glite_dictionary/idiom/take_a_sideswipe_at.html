to take a sideswipe at

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə ˈsaɪdˌswaɪp æt / (AmE), / teɪk ə ˈsaɪdswaɪp æt / (BrE)
to make a critical or disparaging remark about someone or something, often indirectly or in passing
make a critical comment

Examples

During the meeting, he took a sideswipe at the new project manager's decision.
Her article takes a sideswipe at government policies without naming anyone directly.
Without warning, James took a sideswipe at his rival in his speech.

Synset Examples

He took a sideswipe at his colleague during the speech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to criticize - to express disapproval of someone or something
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

From the notion of a 'sideswipe' (a glancing blow), used figuratively for indirect criticism.
