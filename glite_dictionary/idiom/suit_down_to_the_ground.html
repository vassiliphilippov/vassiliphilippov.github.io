to suit down to the ground

1. VERB

/ suːt daʊn tu ðə ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / suːt daʊn tə ðə ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
to be perfectly suitable or appropriate for someone or something
to be perfectly suitable
british

Examples

A remote working arrangement would suit me down to the ground as I dislike commuting.
The style of the house suits her down to the ground—it's exactly what she's always wanted.
