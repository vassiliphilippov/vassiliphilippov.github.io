to take one's time

1. VERB

/ teɪk wʌnz taɪm / (AmE), / teɪk wʌnz taɪm / (BrE)
to do something slowly and carefully, without hurrying or feeling pressured to finish quickly
do something without rushing

Examples

You don't have to rush—just take your time and finish the work carefully.
She likes to take her time in the mornings and enjoy a cup of coffee.
He took his time answering the questions so he wouldn't make mistakes.

Synset Examples

She always takes her sweet time at the coffee shop.
She always takes her time to enjoy her meals.
He decided to take his time and not rush the project.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hurry - to do something quickly or with urgency
