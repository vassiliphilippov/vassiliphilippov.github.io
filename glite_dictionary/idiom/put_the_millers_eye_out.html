to put the miller's eye out

1. VERB

/ pʊt ðə ˈmɪlɚz aɪ aʊt / (AmE), / pʊt ðə ˈmɪləz aɪ aʊt / (BrE)
to make a sharp or cutting remark, often intended to criticize or wound
make a sharp, critical remark
archaic

Examples

She didn't mean to put the miller's eye out, but her words stung nonetheless.
During the debate, he put the miller's eye out with his witty retort.
It's best not to put the miller's eye out when giving feedback to colleagues.

Synset Examples

He really put the miller's eye out with that comment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutting remark - an unkind or hurtful comment
  • Antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration
