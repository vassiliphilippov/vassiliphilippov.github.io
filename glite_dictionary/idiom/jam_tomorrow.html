jam tomorrow

1. NOUN

/ dʒæm təˈmɑroʊ / (AmE), / dʒam təˈmɒrəʊ / (BrE)
Used to refer to a promise of future benefits that are unlikely to be fulfilled or are always postponed.
unrealistic promise of future benefits
british

Examples

The company's commitment to invest in better facilities feels like jam tomorrow to the employees.
He knew the politician's pledge was just jam tomorrow, not something he could rely on.
We've heard enough promises of jam tomorrow; we want real action now.

Synset Examples

We’ll deal with the issues jam tomorrow, not today.
Don't worry about the deadline; it’s jam tomorrow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pie in the sky - a promise of wonderful things in the future that are unlikely to be achieved

Etymology

From Lewis Carroll's 'Through the Looking-Glass' (1871), referencing 'jam tomorrow' as something promised but never delivered.
