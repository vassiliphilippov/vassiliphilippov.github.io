the early bird gets the worm

1. X

/ ði ˈɜrli bɝd ɡɛts ðə wɜrm / (AmE), / ði ˈɜːli bɜːd ɡɛts ðə wɜːm / (BrE)
people who act or arrive early are more likely to succeed or gain an advantage
acting early brings success or advantage

Examples

I always try to arrive early at interviews because the early bird gets the worm.
She wakes up at dawn to start her studies, believing that the early bird gets the worm.
You should submit your applications sooner—remember, the early bird gets the worm.

Synset Examples

The early bird gets the worm, they say.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to strike while the iron is hot - act quickly while the opportunity exists
