to roll about in laughter

1. VERB

/ roʊl əˈbaʊt ɪn ˈlæftɚ / (AmE), / rəʊl əˈbaʊt ɪn ˈlɑːftə / (BrE)
to laugh uncontrollably and with great amusement, often physically writhing or moving around
laugh uncontrollably

Examples

The children would roll about in laughter every time the clown performed a silly trick.
She told such a funny story that everyone began to roll about in laughter.
At the comedy show, the audience started to roll about in laughter after the punchline.

Synset Examples

They would roll about in laughter during the picnic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to crack up - laugh loudly or uncontrollably
  • Synonym: to burst out laughing - suddenly start laughing
