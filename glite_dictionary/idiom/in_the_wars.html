in the wars

1. ADJ

/ ɪn ðə wɔrz / (AmE), / ɪn ðə wɔːz / (BrE)
having experienced many difficulties or troubles, especially showing signs of hardship
having faced many challenges or troubles

Examples

She looked in the wars after a stressful week at work.
After his recent illness, he felt very much in the wars.
You seem a bit in the wars today—did something happen?

Synset Examples

Her tough experiences show she has been in the wars.
He acts like he has been in the wars, always sharing stories.
