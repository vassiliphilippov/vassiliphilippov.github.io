no problem

1. X

/ noʊ ˈprɑbləm / (AmE), / nəʊ ˈprɒbləm / (BrE)
used to indicate that something can be done easily or that there is no difficulty involved; also used to reassure someone that everything is fine or not to worry about a request or apology.
expression meaning 'without difficulty' or 'that's fine'
informal

Examples

"Can you help me move these boxes?" — "No problem, Ill be right there."
When I asked for a later deadline, my supervisor said it was no problem.
"Thanks for the ride." — "No problem, happy to help!

Synset Examples

She can finish the report by tomorrow, no problem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: you're welcome - response to thanks, acknowledging gratitude
