cut from the same cloth

1. X

/ kʌt frəm ðə seɪm klɔθ / (AmE), / kʌt frəm ðə seɪm klɒθ / (BrE)
(of two or more people) very similar in character, background, or behavior.
very similar in character or nature

Examples

Both leaders are cut from the same cloth, displaying integrity and determination in their roles.
You and your sister are cut from the same cloth—smart, resourceful, and driven.
They realized they were cut from the same cloth after discovering their shared values and backgrounds.

Synset Examples

The siblings are cut from the same cloth, both artists.
Those two are cut from the same cloth, always sharing ideas.
They're cut from the same cloth, both stubborn and determined.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: like two peas in a pod - used to describe people who are very similar
  • Antonym: diametrically opposed - completely different from each other

Etymology

Originally from tailoring, where pieces cut from the same cloth are identical; figuratively used since at least the mid-1800s.
