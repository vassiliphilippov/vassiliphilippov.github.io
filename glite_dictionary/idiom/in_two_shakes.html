in two shakes

1. ADV

/ ɪn tu ʃeɪks / (AmE), / ɪn tuː ʃeɪks / (BrE)
very soon or very quickly; in a short time
very soon; quickly

Examples

I'll be finished with this report in two shakes, so just wait a moment.
Dinner will be ready in two shakes, so please set the table.
If you give me your address, I'll send you the information in two shakes.

Synset Examples

Just wait here; I’ll be back in two shakes.
He can solve that problem in two shakes!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in a jiffy - very quickly; in a moment
  • Synonym: in no time - without any delay or almost immediately

Etymology

Originally a shortening of the phrase 'in two shakes of a lamb's tail'.
