mover and shaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈmuːvər ənd ˈʃeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈmuːvə ənd ˈʃeɪkə / (BrE)
an influential person with power to initiate change or make things happen, especially in a particular field
influential and powerful person

Examples

She quickly became a mover and shaker in the tech industry, launching several successful startups.
Many politicians want to be seen as movers and shakers who can implement real change.
The annual conference attracted movers and shakers from all corners of the business world.

Synset Examples

a shaker of traditional beliefs
movers and shakers in the business world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: influencer - a person who has the power to affect others' decisions, especially online
  • Synonym: leader - one who leads or commands a group, organization, or country

Etymology

Originated in early 20th-century American English, popularized by the 1874 poem 'Ode' by Arthur O'Shaughnessy: 'We are the music makers, and we are the dreamers of dreams... Yet we are the movers and shakers of the world forever, it seems.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈmuːvɚ ənd ˈʃeɪkɚ / (AmE), / ˈmuːvə ənd ˈʃeɪkə / (BrE)
an influential and energetic person who plays an important role in shaping events or developments in a particular field
influential and active person

Examples

As a mover and shaker in the finance world, she is always in demand at major events.
The new director quickly established himself as a mover and shaker within the company.
Many movers and shakers attended the gala to support the charity's cause.

Synset Examples

She’s a real mover and shaker in the tech industry.
As a mover and shaker, he influences many decisions.
The conference is full of movers and shakers from all sectors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: power broker - a person who has influence over the decisions of others
  • Synonym: influencer - a person who has the power to influence many people, especially on social media
  • Antonym: outsider - a person who does not have influence or is not a central figure in a field

Etymology

Popularized in the early 20th century, from the phrase 'movers and shakers' in Arthur O’Shaughnessy’s 1874 poem.
