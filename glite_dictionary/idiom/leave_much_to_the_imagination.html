to leave much to the imagination

1. VERB

/ liv mʌtʃ tu ði ɪˌmædʒəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / liːv mʌtʃ tuː ði ɪˌmædʒɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
to allow room for interpretation or speculation; not revealing all details or information.
allow room for interpretation

Examples

The minimalist set design leaves much to the imagination, encouraging the audience to envision the missing elements.
Her writing style left much to the imagination, hinting at romance but never describing it explicitly.
The old photograph leaves much to the imagination, with its faded figures and indistinct background.

Synset Examples

The artist's work left much to the imagination.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to spell out - to explain in detail, leaving no ambiguity
