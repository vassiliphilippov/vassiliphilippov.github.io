to go begging

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈbɛɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈbɛɡɪŋ / (BrE)
to remain unfulfilled, unwanted, unused, or unclaimed; to be available but not taken or used
remain unfulfilled or unwanted

Examples

Several positions in the company go begging despite high unemployment.
The surplus food often went begging at the end of the event.
If their advice goes begging, it may never help those in need.

Synset Examples

He might go begging if he doesn't find a job soon.
Without proper funding, the project will go begging.
Their talents shouldn't go begging in this economy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remain unclaimed - not taken or accepted by anyone
  • Antonym: find a taker - to be accepted, bought, or used by someone
