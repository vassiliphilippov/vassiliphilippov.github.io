to shake like a leaf

1. VERB

/ ʃeɪk laɪk ə liːf / (AmE), / ʃeɪk laɪk ə liːf / (BrE)
to tremble or shiver noticeably from fear, anxiety, or cold
tremble from fear or anxiety

Examples

She was so nervous before the interview that she began to shake like a leaf.
After hearing the thunder, the little boy started to shake like a leaf.
Standing alone in the dark, he could feel himself shake like a leaf.

Synset Examples

He was shaking like a leaf during the presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tremble - to shake involuntarily, typically from fear or cold
  • Synonym: quiver - to shake with a slight, rapid motion

2. VERB

/ ʃeɪk laɪk ə liːf / (AmE), / ʃeɪk laɪk ə liːf / (BrE)
to tremble or shake uncontrollably, typically due to fear, anxiety, or cold
tremble or shake uncontrollably

Examples

After hearing the news, he shook like a leaf and could barely stand.
She was shaking like a leaf before her big speech on stage.
The child came inside, still shaking like a leaf from the cold.

Synset Examples

The dog shook like a leaf during the thunderstorm.
She shook like a leaf while waiting for her test results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tremble - to shake involuntarily, especially from fear or cold
  • Synonym: quiver - to shake slightly, often because of emotion
