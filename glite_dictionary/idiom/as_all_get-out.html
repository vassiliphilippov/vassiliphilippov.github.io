as all get-out

1. ADV

/ æz ɔl ˈɡɛt aʊt / (AmE), / az ɔːl ˈɡɛt aʊt / (BrE)
(informal) Used to emphasize an adjective or adverb, meaning extremely or very much.
very much; extremely (informal emphasis)
informal american

Examples

That dog was friendly as all get-out, wagging its tail at everyone.
I'm sorry, but this cake is sweet as all get-out and I can't eat any more.
She was nervous as all get-out before her big presentation.

Synset Examples

It was hot as all get-out at the beach yesterday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: very - to a high degree
  • Synonym: extremely - to an extreme degree

Etymology

American English, likely expanded from expressions using 'get out' as an intensive since the 19th century.
