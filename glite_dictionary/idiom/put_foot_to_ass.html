to put foot to ass

1. VERB

/ pʊt fʊt tə æs / (AmE), / pʊt fʊt tə as / (BrE)
to take strong, forceful action, often to discipline or motivate someone to act
take strong disciplinary action
informal vulgar

Examples

If the team doesn't improve, the manager will put foot to ass to get results.
Sometimes you need to put foot to ass when people aren't doing their jobs.
The coach wasn't afraid to put foot to ass after the disappointing loss.

Synset Examples

After the team's poor performance, the coach put foot to ass.
The boss put foot to ass when deadlines were missed.
It’s time to put foot to ass and finish this project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to kick butt - take decisive or forceful action, often to discipline or motivate
