to put to death

1. VERB

/ pʊt tu dɛθ / (AmE), / pʊt tuː dɛθ / (BrE)
to kill someone, especially as a legal or official punishment
execute as legal punishment

Examples

After being found guilty of the crime, the prisoner was put to death by lethal injection.
Centuries ago, heretics were often put to death for their beliefs.
The law allowed people to be put to death for a wide range of offenses.

Synset Examples

In some states, criminals are executed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to execute - to carry out a death sentence on someone
