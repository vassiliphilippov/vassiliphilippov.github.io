to draw a straight furrow

1. VERB

/ drɔː ə streɪt ˈfɜːroʊ / (AmE), / drɔː ə streɪt ˈfʌrəʊ / (BrE)
to follow a steady and unwavering course of action, or to perform a task carefully and consistently
to act steadily and consistently

Examples

If we want to finish this project successfully, we'll need to draw a straight furrow and not get distracted.
She believes in drawing a straight furrow in both her personal and professional life.
Farmers must draw a straight furrow when planting, but the principle applies just as well to our ambitions.

Synset Examples

To ensure success, we must draw a straight furrow in our goals.
He always tries to draw a straight furrow while plowing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plow a straight furrow - to follow a steady and consistent course

Etymology

Metaphor from agriculture, where plowing a straight furrow shows skill and dedication.
