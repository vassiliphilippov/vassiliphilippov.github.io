to mind one's language

1. VERB

/ maɪnd wʌnz ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / maɪnd wʌnz ˈlӕŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
to be careful about what one says, especially to avoid using rude or offensive words
be careful about what one says

Examples

You should mind your language when speaking to your teacher.
Please mind your language in the meeting; there are children present.
He was told to mind his language after he swore at work.

Synset Examples

He was reminded to mind his language at work.
Please mind your language in front of the children.

2. VERB

/ maɪnd wʌnz ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / maɪnd wʌnz ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
be careful about what one says, especially to avoid using language that may be rude or offensive
be cautious with one's words

Examples

You should mind your language when speaking in front of children.
If he doesn't mind his language, he'll get into trouble at work.
The teacher warned the student to mind his language during the discussion.

Synset Examples

She had to mind her language during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watch one's language - to be careful not to use offensive or inappropriate language
