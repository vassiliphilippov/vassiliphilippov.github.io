fool's paradise

1. NOUN

/ fʊlz ˈpɛr.ə.daɪs / (AmE), / fuːlz ˈpær.ə.daɪs / (BrE)
a state of happiness based on false beliefs or illusions, especially one that is likely to end badly
illusory happiness or wellbeing

Examples

After hearing the positive rumors, she lived in a fool's paradise, believing everything would work out perfectly.
He thought his job was secure, but he was in a fool's paradise until the layoffs began.
Don't let temporary success lead you into a fool's paradise—the challenges are not over yet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illusion - something that deceives by producing a false impression
  • Antonym: reality check - an instance of facing the true facts instead of illusion

Etymology

From Middle English, found in literature as early as the 15th century, using 'paradise' figuratively for a blissful state.

2. NOUN

/ fʊlz ˈpɛrəˌdaɪs / (AmE), / fuːlz ˈpærədaɪs / (BrE)
a state of happiness based on false hopes or beliefs, especially one that is likely to end badly
happiness based on illusion or false hope

Examples

After winning the lottery, he lived in a fool's paradise, ignoring the risks of overspending.
Believing the project would succeed without effort was a fool's paradise.
Many people choose to stay in a fool's paradise rather than face difficult truths.

Synset Examples

His optimism felt like a fool's paradise to me.
Living in a fool's paradise won't solve your problems.
She created a fool's paradise in her imagination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delusion - a belief or impression that is not in accordance with reality
  • Antonym: reality check - an occasion that causes one to recognize the truth about a situation

Etymology

First recorded in the 15th century, combining "fool" with "paradise" to describe deceptive happiness.
