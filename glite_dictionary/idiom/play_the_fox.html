to play the fox

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ðə fɑks / (AmE), / pleɪ ðə fɒks / (BrE)
to behave in a cunning or deceitful manner, especially to outsmart others
act cunningly or deceitfully

Examples

She knew when to play the fox to get what she wanted.
They tried to play the fox during negotiations, but were eventually caught.
He often plays the fox in business to outmaneuver his rivals.

Synset Examples

She played the fox, outsmarting her competitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outsmart - to gain an advantage over someone by being more clever or cunning
  • Synonym: be cunning - to use clever but often dishonest means to achieve something
  • Antonym: to play fair - to act honestly and straightforwardly without deceit

Etymology

From the fox's longstanding reputation in Western folklore as a symbol of cunning and trickery.
