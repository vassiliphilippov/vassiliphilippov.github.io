to return to dust

1. VERB

/ rɪˈtɜrn tə dʌst / (AmE), / rɪˈtɜːn tə dʌst / (BrE)
to perish and decompose, or to be reduced to nothing over time
perish and decompose; reduce to nothing

Examples

After the last member of the dynasty died, their legacy seemed to return to dust.
If the building is not properly maintained, it will eventually return to dust.
Legends tell of empires that rise and return to dust over the ages.

Synset Examples

All living things eventually return to dust.
The ancient ruins will return to dust if left unattended.
After the fire, the remains slowly returned to dust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to perish - to die, especially in a sudden or violent way
  • Synonym: to disintegrate - to break into small parts or cease to exist
