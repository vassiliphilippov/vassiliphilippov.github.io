to stick to one's last

1. VERB

/ stɪk tu wʌnz læst / (AmE), / stɪk tʊ wʌnz lɑːst / (BrE)
to restrict oneself to one's area of expertise and not attempt tasks beyond one's skill or knowledge
restrict oneself to one's expertise
archaic

Examples

Although many projects tempt him, he prefers to stick to his last and work within his expertise.
Critics argued that the author should stick to her last rather than attempt a novel in a new genre.
In business, it's sometimes best to stick to your last and not invest in unfamiliar markets.

Synset Examples

As a specialist, she chooses to stick to her last in her field.
It's wise to stick to your last and not overreach.
He has learned to stick to his last to ensure quality work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stay in your lane - to focus on your own responsibilities or area
  • Antonym: to overreach - to try to do more than is possible or reasonable

Etymology

From the proverb 'let the cobbler stick to his last,' where 'last' refers to a shoemaker's form for shaping shoes.
