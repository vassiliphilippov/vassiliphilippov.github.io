to put lipstick on a pig

1. VERB

/ pʊt ˈlɪpstɪk ɑn ə pɪɡ / (AmE), / pʊt ˈlɪpstɪk ɒn ə pɪɡ / (BrE)
to make superficial or cosmetic changes to something undesirable in an attempt to make it seem more attractive, without addressing the underlying problems
superficially improve something undesirable

Examples

No matter how much you put lipstick on a pig, it’s still the same flawed policy.
The company tried to put lipstick on a pig by redesigning the logo, but customers noticed the underlying issues.
You can’t put lipstick on a pig and expect investors to ignore the problems.

Synset Examples

Her attempt to put lipstick on a pig failed miserably.
You can’t just put lipstick on a pig and expect change.
They tried to put lipstick on a pig with that new marketing campaign.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: window dressing - superficial or misleading improvement
  • Synonym: polish a turd - to try to improve something fundamentally bad
