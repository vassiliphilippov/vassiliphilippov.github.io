to cast one's mind back

1. VERB

/ kæst wʌnz maɪnd bæk / (AmE), / kɑːst wʌnz maɪnd bæk / (BrE)
to deliberately think about or remember something that happened in the past
recall or remember a past event

Examples

When I cast my mind back to childhood, I remember summers spent at my grandparents' house.
He tried to cast his mind back to the events of that evening, searching for details he might have missed.
If you cast your mind back to last year, you'll see how much progress we've made since then.

Synset Examples

When I cast my mind back, I remember that day vividly.
He cast his mind back to their last meeting.
She cast her mind back to her childhood memories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reminisce - to talk or think about past experiences
  • Antonym: to forget - to fail to remember a past event or fact
