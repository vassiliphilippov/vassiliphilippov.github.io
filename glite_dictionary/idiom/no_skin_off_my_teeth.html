no skin off my teeth

1. X

/ noʊ skɪn ɑf maɪ tiθ / (AmE), / nəʊ skɪn ɒf maɪ tiːθ / (BrE)
used to say that something does not affect or trouble one at all; not a problem or inconvenience
not a problem for me; doesn't affect me

Examples

You can change the date if you want; it's no skin off my teeth.
If they don't listen to my advice, it's no skin off my teeth.
It's no skin off my teeth if they decide to go somewhere else.

Synset Examples

Missing the meeting is no skin off my teeth.
Not getting the promotion is no skin off my teeth.
If I miss the bus, it’s no skin off my teeth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: no skin off my nose - not a problem or concern for me

Etymology

Variant of the older phrase 'skin of my teeth', possibly influenced by phrases like 'no skin off my nose'.
