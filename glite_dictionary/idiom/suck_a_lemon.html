to suck a lemon

1. VERB

/ sʌk ə ˈlɛmən / (AmE), / sʌk ə ˈlɛmən / (BrE)
to endure or deal with a difficult or unpleasant situation, often with resignation
endure a difficult situation

Examples

Whenever things go wrong at work, Mark just says he'll suck a lemon and move on.
You can't always avoid hardship; sometimes you just have to suck a lemon.
Facing rejection again, she knew she'd have to suck a lemon and keep trying.

Synset Examples

She told him to stop complaining and just suck a lemon.
When life gets tough, sometimes you just have to suck a lemon.
After hearing the bad news, he felt like he could only suck a lemon.
