to snatch defeat from the jaws of victory

1. VERB

/ snætʃ dɪˈfit frəm ðə dʒɔz əv ˈvɪktəri / (AmE), / snætʃ dɪˈfiːt frəm ðə dʒɔːz əv ˈvɪktəri / (BrE)
to lose a seemingly certain victory, often because of an unexpected mistake or reversal
unexpectedly lose when about to win

Examples

Once again, the team managed to snatch defeat from the jaws of victory with a careless mistake in the final minute.
After leading the entire game, they somehow snatched defeat from the jaws of victory in overtime.
The company's poor decision at the last moment made them snatch defeat from the jaws of victory.

Synset Examples

It’s frustrating to watch them snatch defeat from the jaws of victory.
He always finds a way to snatch defeat out of the jaws of victory.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: snatch victory from the jaws of defeat - to win when loss seemed certain
