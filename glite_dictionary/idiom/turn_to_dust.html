to turn to dust

1. VERB

/ tɜrn tə dʌst / (AmE), / tɜːn tə dʌst / (BrE)
to come to complete ruin or destruction; to fail completely or become worthless
to become ruined or destroyed

Examples

After the storm, the farmer’s hopes for a good harvest turned to dust.
The company’s ambitious expansion plan turned to dust during the recession.
She feared all their years of hard work would turn to dust if the deal fell through.

Synset Examples

They watched their plans turn to dust after the setback.
The old building is destined to turn to dust.
All her dreams seemed to turn to dust overnight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fall apart - to break into pieces; to collapse or fail completely
  • Synonym: to come to nothing - to end in failure or not produce a result
