to keep one's ear to the ground

1. VERB

/ kip wʌnz ɪr tə ðə ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / kiːp wʌnz ɪə tə ðə ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
to pay close attention to what is happening or being said, so as to be well-informed about current events, trends, or rumors
stay alert to news or rumors

Examples

It's important to keep one's ear to the ground if you want to succeed in a fast-changing industry.
She tries to keep her ear to the ground to hear about any upcoming job openings.
Journalists often keep their ears to the ground for the latest news and trends.

Synset Examples

She keeps her ear to the ground for gossip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stay tuned - to remain attentive for more information
  • Synonym: be in the know - to be well-informed about something
  • Antonym: to turn a deaf ear - to deliberately ignore what is happening or being said

Etymology

Alludes to listening closely for distant sounds, as if literally placing an ear to the ground.
