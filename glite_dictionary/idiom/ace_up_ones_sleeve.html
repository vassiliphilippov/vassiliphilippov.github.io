ace up one's sleeve

1. NOUN

/ eɪs ʌp wʌnz sliːv / (AmE), / eɪs ʌp wʌnz sliːv / (BrE)
a secret advantage or resource kept in reserve until needed
a hidden advantage or resource

Examples

Despite their setbacks, the negotiators still had an ace up their sleeve to use at the crucial moment.
Whenever things get tough, she seems to pull an ace up her sleeve and surprise everyone.
His brilliant comeback in the final round was the ace up his sleeve that won him the trophy.

Synset Examples

She always keeps an ace up her sleeve during competitions.
The team had an ace up their sleeve for the championship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secret weapon - a person or thing kept in reserve to provide an advantage when needed

Etymology

From card games where secretly holding an ace in one's sleeve gave an unfair advantage. The idiom has been in use since the late 19th century.
