pebble in someone's shoe

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛb.əl ɪn ˈsʌm.wʌnz ʃu / (AmE), / ˈpɛb.əl ɪn ˈsʌm.wənz ʃuː / (BrE)
a minor but persistent annoyance or irritation
persistent minor annoyance

Examples

His micromanagement style was a real pebble in my shoe at work.
That recurring software glitch is a pebble in your shoe you just can't ignore.
Having to rewrite the same document became a pebble in her shoe every day.

Synset Examples

His constant complaints were just a pebble in my shoe.
Feeling like a pebble in my shoe, I couldn't focus.
Every little error felt like a pebble in my shoe today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thorn in someone's side - a persistent source of irritation or trouble
