to reopen old wounds

1. VERB

/ ˌrioʊpən oʊld wundz / (AmE), / ˌriːəʊpən əʊld wuːndz / (BrE)
to revive painful memories or issues from the past, especially those best left forgotten or healed
bring up past painful memories

Examples

Bringing up the argument from years ago would only reopen old wounds.
They tried to move on, not wanting to reopen old wounds from the past.
Discussing the incident would reopen old wounds for everyone involved.

Synset Examples

His comments were likely to reopen old wounds from their breakup.
They chose to avoid the topic to not reopen old wounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rub salt in the wound - to make a bad experience even more painful for someone
