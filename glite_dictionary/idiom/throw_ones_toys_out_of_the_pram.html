to throw one's toys out of the pram

1. VERB

/ θɹoʊ wʌnz tɔɪz aʊt ʌv ðə præm / (AmE), / θrəʊ wʌnz tɔɪz aʊt ɒv ðə præm / (BrE)
(British, informal) To behave in a childish, sulky, or angry way, especially in response to disappointment or frustration.
act childishly after disappointment
informal british

Examples

When the manager criticized his work, he threw his toys out of the pram and refused to speak to anyone.
There’s no need to throw your toys out of the pram just because you didn’t get your way.
They threw their toys out of the pram after the decision went against them.

Synset Examples

After losing the game, he threw his toys out of the pram in frustration.
She tends to throw her toys out of the pram when things don't go her way.
Stop throwing your toys out of the pram and be mature about it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: have a tantrum - to lose one's temper in a childish way

Etymology

British English expression based on the literal action of a child in a pram (baby carriage).
