to act like a clodpole

1. VERB

/ ækt laɪk ə ˈklɑdˌpoʊl / (AmE), / ækt laɪk ə ˈklɒd.pəʊl / (BrE)
to behave in a foolish or clumsy way
behave foolishly or clumsily
archaic

Examples

If you act like a clodpole in meetings, people may not take you seriously.
He tends to act like a clodpole when he's nervous or unsure of himself.
Stop acting like a clodpole and pay attention to what you're doing.

Synset Examples

Stop acting like a clodpole and think before you speak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fool around - to behave in a silly or playful way
  • Antonym: act sensibly - to behave with reason and care

Etymology

From 'clodpole,' an archaic term for a stupid or foolish person, originally meaning 'clod' (a lump of earth) + 'pole' (head).
