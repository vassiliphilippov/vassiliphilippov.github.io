to see things from someone's perspective

1. VERB

/ si ˈθɪŋz frəm ˈsʌmwʌnz pərˈspɛktɪv / (AmE), / siː ˈθɪŋz frəm ˈsʌmwʌnz pəˈspɛktɪv / (BrE)
to understand or consider a situation from another person's point of view.
understand another's viewpoint

Examples

To resolve conflicts, it's important to see things from someone's perspective rather than just defending your own.
She tried to see things from her colleague's perspective to better understand his frustration.
If you see things from your parent's perspective, their concerns might make more sense.

Synset Examples

You should see things from his perspective for better understanding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to empathize - to understand and share another person's feelings
  • Synonym: to see eye to eye - to agree with someone
  • Antonym: to ignore - to refuse to acknowledge or consider
