full to the gills

1. ADJ

/ fʊl tə ðə ɡɪlz / (AmE), / fʊl tə ðə ɡɪlz / (BrE)
completely full or filled to capacity; extremely crowded or packed
completely full; filled to capacity

Examples

By the end of the harvest, the storage barns were full to the gills with grain.
At the concert, the stadium was full to the gills with fans from all over the country.
After Thanksgiving dinner, I always feel full to the gills.

Synset Examples

Her schedule is full to the gills this week.
The restaurant was full to the gills on Friday night.
The suitcase was full to the gills with clothes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: packed - very full or crowded
  • Synonym: stuffed - filled up completely; full, usually with food

Etymology

From the literal reference to fish gills, suggesting being stuffed up to the mouth.
