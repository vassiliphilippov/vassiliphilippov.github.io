to set foot on terra firma

1. VERB

/ sɛt fʊt ɑn ˌtɛrə ˈfɜrmə / (AmE), / sɛt fʊt ɒn ˌtɛrə ˈfɜːmə / (BrE)
to arrive or land on solid ground, especially after being on water or in the air
arrive or land on solid ground

Examples

After the turbulent flight, the passengers were relieved to set foot on terra firma again.
Sailors longed to set foot on terra firma after months at sea.
It felt wonderful to finally set foot on terra firma following the exhausting hiking expedition.

Synset Examples

After weeks at sea, we finally set foot on terra firma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: land - to come down onto ground after flight or travel by water

Etymology

'Terra firma' is Latin for 'solid earth'.
