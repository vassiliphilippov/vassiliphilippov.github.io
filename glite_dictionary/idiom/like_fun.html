like fun

1. ADV

/ laɪk fʌn / (AmE), / laɪk fʌn / (BrE)
(used ironically) as an expression of disbelief or emphatic denial; certainly not.
used ironically to express denial or disbelief
informal

Examples

He claimed he could finish the project in one day—like fun he could!
You think she'll forgive you that easily? Like fun she will!
They said the repairs would be cheap, but like fun they were.

Synset Examples

says he'll help me? Like hell he will!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: like hell - used for emphatic denial or disbelief
  • Degree Lower: like mad - with great intensity or speed (literal meaning, not ironic denial)
