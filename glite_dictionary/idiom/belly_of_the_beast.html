belly of the beast

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛli əv ðə ˈbiːst / (AmE), / ˈbɛli əv ðə ˈbiːst / (BrE)
the most dangerous or challenging part of a situation, especially being at the center of difficulty or danger
center of danger or difficulty

Examples

The protest leader found himself in the belly of the beast when he entered the parliament building.
During the trial, the lawyer stood in the belly of the beast facing a hostile crowd.
Many activists willingly go into the belly of the beast to fight for their causes.

Synset Examples

He ventured into the belly of the beast to gather information.
The journalist went straight to the belly of the beast for the story.
Living in the city felt like being in the belly of the beast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lion's den - a place of great danger or difficulty
  • Antonym: safe haven - a place of safety or refuge

Etymology

From the Biblical story of Jonah in the belly of a great fish (the 'beast'), denoting a place of great danger or hardship.
