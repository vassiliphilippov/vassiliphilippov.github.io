out of the game

1. ADJ

/ aʊt əv ðə ɡeɪm / (AmE), / aʊt əv ðə ɡeɪm / (BrE)
not participating or involved in an activity, situation, or competition
not participating or involved

Examples

After his injury, Mark was out of the game for the rest of the season.
Since she transferred departments, Lisa feels completely out of the game at work meetings.
Don’t worry if you’re out of the game for now—you can always join in later.

Synset Examples

You're out of the game, na-na na-na boo-boo!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: in the game - actively participating in an activity or competition
