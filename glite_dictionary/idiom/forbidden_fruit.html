forbidden fruit

1. NOUN

/ fərˈbɪdən frut / (AmE), / fəˈbɪdn fruːt / (BrE)
a thing that is very attractive or tempting but not allowed, often because it is considered immoral or dangerous
tempting but prohibited thing

Examples

The allure of forbidden fruit often makes risks more appealing than they should be.
To teenagers, staying out past curfew has the charm of forbidden fruit.
He knew the investment was illegal, but the promise of quick wealth was like forbidden fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temptation - the desire to do something, especially something wrong or unwise

Etymology

From the biblical story of the Garden of Eden, where Adam and Eve ate the fruit forbidden by God.

2. NOUN

/ fərˈbɪdən frut / (AmE), / fəˈbɪdn fruːt / (BrE)
something that is very tempting but not allowed or is considered wrong
something tempting but prohibited

Examples

She knew dating her coworker was forbidden fruit, but the attraction was undeniable.
To teenagers, breaking curfew often seems like forbidden fruit.
The mysterious document represented forbidden fruit that he couldn't resist reading.

Synset Examples

She knew dating him was forbidden fruit, yet she couldn't resist.
Eating candy before dinner is the forbidden fruit.
He found the job offer tempting, but it felt like forbidden fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temptation - a desire to do something, especially something wrong or unwise

Etymology

From the biblical story of the Tree of Knowledge, whose fruit was forbidden to Adam and Eve.
