snootful of

1. X

/ ˈsnut.fʊl əv / (AmE), / ˈsnuːt.fʊl əv / (BrE)
a large quantity of something, especially a pleasant smell or, informally, alcohol, that is taken in through the nose or experienced at once
large amount, usually a smell or alcohol, taken in
informal

Examples

After opening the spice jar, she got a snootful of cinnamon.
He leaned over the grill and received a snootful of smoke.
The dog stuck its nose in the bag and got a snootful of treats.

Synset Examples

She had a snootful of the delicious aroma from the bakery.
He took a quick whiff and had a snootful of fresh flowers.

Etymology

From 'snoot' (slang for 'nose'), combined with 'full', meaning the nose is full of something.
