to pull one's weight

1. VERB

/ pʊl wʌnz weɪt / (AmE), / pʊl wʌnz weɪt / (BrE)
to do one's fair share of work or duties, especially as part of a group or team
do one's fair share of work

Examples

Everyone in the team needs to pull their weight if we want to finish the project on time.
She quickly became respected in the office for always pulling her weight.
If you don't pull your weight, the rest of us will have to pick up the slack.

Synset Examples

Bob has never pulled his weight, and we all have to work harder to make up for his laziness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: do one's bit - to make a useful contribution to a group effort
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ pʊl wʌnz weɪt / (AmE), / pʊl wʌnz weɪt / (BrE)
to do one's fair share of work or contribute equally to a group effort
do one's fair share of work

Examples

If you want to be part of this team, you have to pull your weight.
She always pulls her weight around the house and helps with the chores.
The project failed because too many people didn't pull their weight.

Synset Examples

He was warned to start pulling his weight or face consequences.
He's such a bludger; he never pulls his weight.
Everyone needs to pull their weight in this project.
If you want to succeed, you must pull your weight.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to slack off - to avoid work or not do one's share
  • Synonym: do your part - to contribute one's share of effort or responsibility
