avant la lettre

1. ADV

/ aˌvɑ̃ la ˈlɛtər / (AmE), / aˌvɑ̃ lɑː ˈlɛtə / (BrE)
Describing someone or something that existed or acted in a particular way before the concept was officially recognized or named; ahead of its time.
ahead of its time; before officially named

Examples

His research was considered avant la lettre, anticipating concepts decades before they became mainstream.
She was a feminist avant la lettre, championing women's rights long before the term became popular.
The novel was postmodern avant la lettre, using techniques that later defined the genre.

Synset Examples

The artist's work was avant la lettre, inspiring future generations.
He was an environmentalist avant la lettre, advocating for nature long ago.
Her innovative ideas were avant la lettre, ahead of their time.

Etymology

French, literally 'before the letter' (before the term/label was coined).
