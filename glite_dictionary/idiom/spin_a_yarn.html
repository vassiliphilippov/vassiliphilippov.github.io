to spin a yarn

1. VERB

/ spɪn ə jɑrn / (AmE), / spɪn ə jɑːn / (BrE)
to tell a long or imaginative story, often one that is exaggerated or not entirely true
tell an exaggerated or imaginative story

Examples

My uncle loves to spin a yarn whenever we visit during the holidays.
She began to spin a yarn about her supposed encounter with a celebrity.
After dinner, they would sit by the fire and spin a yarn to pass the time.

Synset Examples

She can really spin a yarn that keeps everyone entertained.
Grandpa loves to spin a yarn about his adventures.
He spun a yarn so captivating that no one left.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tell a tale - to tell a story, often with imagination or exaggeration

Etymology

Originally nautical slang, referring to sailors spinning yarn while telling stories.
