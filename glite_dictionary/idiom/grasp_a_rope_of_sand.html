to grasp a rope of sand

1. VERB

/ ɡræsp ə roʊp əv sænd / (AmE), / ɡrɑːsp ə rəʊp əv sand / (BrE)
To attempt something that is impossible or hopelessly futile.
attempt the impossible; futile task

Examples

Trying to fix the broken vase without glue was like grasping a rope of sand.
He soon realized that searching for his lost notes was grasping a rope of sand.
Negotiating peace between those two rivals is often compared to grasping a rope of sand.

Synset Examples

Investing in that venture was like grasping a rope of sand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: a wild goose chase - a hopeless or pointless pursuit
  • Synonym: a fool's errand - a task with no hope of success
