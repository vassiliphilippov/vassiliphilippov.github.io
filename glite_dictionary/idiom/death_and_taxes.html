death and taxes

1. NOUN

/ dɛθ ənd ˈtæksɪz / (AmE), / dɛθ ənd ˈtæksɪz / (BrE)
used to refer to the two things in life that are certain and inescapable—death and paying taxes
life's unavoidable certainties

Examples

No matter how hard you try to avoid them, death and taxes always catch up with you.
In discussions about life's inevitabilities, death and taxes are usually at the top of the list.
He joked that only death and taxes are truly unavoidable in this world.

Synset Examples

In life, nothing is certain but death and taxes, they say.
As the saying goes, nothing is certain but death and taxes.
It’s a harsh reality that nothing is certain but death and taxes.

Etymology

Popularized by Benjamin Franklin in an 1789 letter: "nothing is certain except death and taxes."
