to raise the flag

1. VERB

/ ˈreɪz ðə flæɡ / (AmE), / ˈreɪz ðə flaɡ / (BrE)
to announce or signal something openly, often to test reactions or gauge support
announce or signal something openly

Examples

At the meeting, she decided to raise the flag about possible issues with the plan.
When we raise the flag, we'll find out who supports the new policy.
Sometimes it's risky to raise the flag before you have all the facts.

Synset Examples

It's time to raise the flag and see who salutes in this project.
When we announce the changes, we'll raise the flag and see who salutes.
Let's raise the flag and see who salutes during the meeting.
They raised the flag on the tall pole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signal - to make a gesture, action, or sound that gives information
  • Synonym: call attention to - to bring something to people's notice
