ground ball with eyes

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd bɑl wɪð aɪz / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd bɔːl wɪð aɪz / (BrE)
(baseball, informal) A ground ball that moves in an unexpected or unpredictable way, making it difficult for fielders to play.
unpredictable ground ball in baseball
informal

Examples

The shortstop lunged for the ground ball with eyes, but it slipped into the outfield.
That inning changed after a ground ball with eyes snuck past the diving third baseman.
He was lucky when his slow ground ball with eyes brought home the tying run.

Synset Examples

It was a ground ball with eyes that led to a run.
The batter sent a ground ball with eyes right past the shortstop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seeing-eye single - a softly-hit ball that gets past infielders for a hit
