to send packing

1. VERB

/ sɛnd ˈpækɪŋ / (AmE), / sɛnd ˈpækɪŋ / (BrE)
to dismiss someone abruptly or force them to leave, especially in a way that makes it clear they are no longer wanted
dismiss or force to leave

Examples

After his repeated mistakes, the manager finally sent packing the unreliable employee.
When the tenants refused to pay rent, the landlord sent them packing without hesitation.
The club found out about his cheating and sent him packing the next day.

Synset Examples

They dropped her after she had a child out of wedlock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - to remove someone from a position or job
  • Synonym: to kick out - to force someone to leave a place or group

/ sɛnd ˈpækɪŋ / (AmE), / sɛnd ˈpækɪŋ / (BrE)
to dismiss someone abruptly, often in a way that makes them leave immediately
dismiss someone abruptly

Examples

If you disrespect the coach, he won’t hesitate to send you packing.
After failing three times to deliver results, the company finally sent him packing.
