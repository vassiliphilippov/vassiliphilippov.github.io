to give it a rest

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ɪt ə rɛst / (AmE), / ɡɪv ɪt ə rɛst / (BrE)
to stop doing or talking about something, especially because it is becoming annoying or excessive
stop doing or talking about something
informal

Examples

You've made your point, so why don't you give it a rest now?
He kept complaining all day until I finally told him to give it a rest.
After hours of practicing, she decided to give it a rest and relax.

Synset Examples

She finally decided to give it a rest and go to bed.
You've been talking for hours; give it a rest!
He needs to give it a rest and stop complaining.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to let up - to become less intense or to stop
  • Antonym: to carry on - to continue something, especially when asked to stop
