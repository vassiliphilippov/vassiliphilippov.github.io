hot on someone's heels

1. ADV

/ hɑt ɑn sʌmwʌnz hilz / (AmE), / hɒt ɒn sʌmwʌnz hiːlz / (BrE)
following very closely or pursuing someone with little distance separating the two.
following closely or pursuing

Examples

Despite his head start, his rival was hot on his heels by the halfway mark.
Reporters were hot on their heels as the scandal unfolded.
The leading cyclist knew the others were hot on her heels as she neared the finish line.

Synset Examples

She was hot on his heels during the race.
The competitor is hot on our heels this quarter.
The police were hot on the thief's heels.
The competition is right at his heels this season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on someone's heels - right behind someone; directly following
