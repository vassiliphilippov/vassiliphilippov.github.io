to tie up loose ends

1. VERB

/ taɪ ʌp lus ɛndz / (AmE), / taɪ ʌp luːs endz / (BrE)
to complete the final details of something or deal with unresolved issues before finishing a task or project
to finish final details or unresolved tasks

Examples

Before launching the new software, we must tie up loose ends with the current release.
He stayed late at the office to tie up loose ends on the project.
During her last week at work, she worked hard to tie up loose ends before leaving.

Synset Examples

Let's tie up loose ends before the meeting.
We need to tie up loose ends on this project.
She plans to tie up loose ends this weekend.
I need to tie up the loose ends before leaving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to finish up - to bring something to an end; complete
  • Synonym: to wrap up - to complete or finish something
