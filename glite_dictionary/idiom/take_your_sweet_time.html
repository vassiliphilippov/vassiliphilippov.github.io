to take your sweet time

1. VERB

/ teɪk jɚ swit taɪm / (AmE), / teɪk jɔː swiːt taɪm / (BrE)
to do something slowly and without hurrying, often more slowly than others might expect or wish
act slowly and without hurry

Examples

He always takes his sweet time getting ready in the morning, no matter how late it is.
You can take your sweet time with the assignment; there's no need to rush.
She took her sweet time answering the email, even though it was marked urgent.

Synset Examples

You can take your sweet little time; there’s no rush.
Don't worry, just take your sweet time with the presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dawdle - to waste time and move slowly
  • Antonym: hurry - to move or act with haste
