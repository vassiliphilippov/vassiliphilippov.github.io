to keep one's chin up

1. VERB

/ kip wʌnz tʃɪn ʌp / (AmE), / kiːp wʌnz tʃɪn ʌp / (BrE)
to remain cheerful and optimistic in the face of adversity or difficulty
remain cheerful in adversity

Examples

Even during months of unemployment, she tried to keep her chin up and not lose hope.
The team lost by a wide margin, but the coach urged them to keep their chins up.
It's hard to keep one's chin up when things go wrong, but it helps to stay positive.

Synset Examples

He told her to keep her chin up after the breakup.
He told her to keep her chin up despite the loss.
It's tough, but you have to keep your chin up.
Even in hard times, remember to keep your chin up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stay positive - to remain optimistic
  • Antonym: give up hope - to stop feeling hope when facing difficulties
