the shoe is on the other foot

1. X

/ ðə ʃu ɪz ɑn ði ˈʌðɚ fʊt / (AmE), / ðə ʃuː ɪz ɒn ði ˈʌðə fʊt / (BrE)
used to say that a situation has reversed, so that the person who was at an advantage is now at a disadvantage, or vice versa
the situation has reversed

Examples

Now the shoe is on the other foot, and he must answer to his former assistant.
She used to criticize me, but now the shoe is on the other foot.
After the merger, the shoe is on the other foot and they must adapt to new management.

Synset Examples

When he was laid off, the shoe was on the other foot.
Now that she's in charge, the shoe is on the other foot.
After the promotion, the shoe is now on the other foot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tables have turned - used when the circumstances have completely changed, especially reversing power or position
