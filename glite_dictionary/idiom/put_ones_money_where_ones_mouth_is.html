to put one's money where one's mouth is

1. VERB

/ pʊt wʌnz ˈmʌni wɛr wʌnz ˈmaʊθ ɪz / (AmE), / pʊt wʌnz ˈmʌni wɛə wʌnz ˈmaʊθ ɪz / (BrE)
to take action in support of one's statements or claims, especially by providing financial support or practical effort
back up words with actions or money

Examples

It's time for the company to put its money where its mouth is and invest in renewable energy.
If you truly care about education, you should put your money where your mouth is by donating to local schools.
Politicians often promise change, but few actually put their money where their mouth is.

Synset Examples

She always talks about charity; now she needs to put her money where her mouth is.
He challenged his critics to put their money where their mouth is.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walk the talk - to act according to what one preaches or claims
