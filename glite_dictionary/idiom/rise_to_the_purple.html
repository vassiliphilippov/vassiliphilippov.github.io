to rise to the purple

1. VERB

/ raɪz tə ði ˈpɝːpəl / (AmE), / raɪz tə ði ˈpɜːpl̩ / (BrE)
to achieve a high rank or position, especially rapidly or unexpectedly
achieve a high position quickly

Examples

After years of hard work, she managed to rise to the purple within the company.
Many young politicians dream of one day being able to rise to the purple.
It is rare to rise to the purple so quickly in such a traditional organization.

Synset Examples

Her efforts helped her rise to the purple in no time.
The new leader truly knows how to rise to the purple.
To succeed here, you must rise to the purple.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: climb the ladder - to advance progressively to higher positions
  • Antonym: fall from grace - to lose status or favor

Etymology

Alluding to the color purple historically associated with royalty and high office, particularly in ancient Rome.
