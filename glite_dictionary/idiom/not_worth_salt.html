not worth salt

1. ADJ

/ nɑt wɜrθ sɔlt / (AmE), / nɒt wɜːθ sɒlt / (BrE)
Considered to have no value or importance.
of no value or importance

Examples

After reviewing the report, the manager said it was not worth salt.
The old machinery we found in the warehouse is not worth salt anymore.
She dismissed his excuses as not worth salt and insisted on a better explanation.

Synset Examples

His contribution to the project was not worth salt.
The advice he gave was not worth salt.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: worth one's salt - deserving respect or pay; competent or valuable

Etymology

Refers to the historic value of salt, once a precious commodity; something not worth one's salt is not deserving of pay or regard.
