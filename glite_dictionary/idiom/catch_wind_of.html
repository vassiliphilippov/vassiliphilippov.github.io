to catch wind of

1. VERB

/ kætʃ wɪnd ʌv / (AmE), / kæʧ wɪnd ɒv / (BrE)
to hear about or become aware of something, especially in an indirect or informal way
learn of something informally

Examples

The manager was upset when he caught wind of the rumor circulating among employees.
She caught wind of the changes before the official announcement was made.
If the press catches wind of this scandal, it could be disastrous.

Synset Examples

With his ear to the ground, he caught wind of the new policy.
She always gets wind of the latest gossip in town.
He didn't want anyone to get wind of his secret.
I got wind of the promotion before it was officially announced.
He got wind of the sale and rushed to the store.
We got wind of the changes before they were announced.
I got wind of the surprise party through a friend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hear of - to be told about something or someone
  • Synonym: to find out - to discover information
