to have your act together

1. VERB

/ hæv jʊr ækt təˈɡɛðər / (AmE), / hæv jɔːr ækt təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to be well organized, efficient, and prepared for something
be organized and prepared

Examples

You really need to have your act together if you want to succeed in this company.
After months of disorganization, she finally managed to have her act together.
The coach only selects players who have their act together on and off the field.

Synset Examples

You need to have your act together before applying for that job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: have it together - be organized and efficient
  • Antonym: to fall apart - become disorganized or unable to function effectively
