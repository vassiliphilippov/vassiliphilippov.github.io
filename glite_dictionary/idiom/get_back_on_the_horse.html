to get back on the horse

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈbæk ɑn ðə hɔrs / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈbak ɒn ðə hɔːs / (BrE)
to try again or resume an activity after a failure or setback, especially after losing confidence
resume after a setback; try again

Examples

After her business failed, she decided it was time to get back on the horse and start fresh.
It's not easy after a tough loss, but you need to get back on the horse and keep working.
He was nervous about dating again, but his friends encouraged him to get back on the horse.

Synset Examples

After falling, he decided to get back on the horse.
He realized he must get back on the horse that bucked one.
To succeed, I must get back on the horse that bucked one.
It's time to get back on the horse after my breakup.
She encouraged him to get back on the horse.
She wasn't afraid to get back on the horse that bucked one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to try again - make another attempt after failing

Etymology

From the literal advice to remount a horse after falling, used figuratively since the 19th century.
