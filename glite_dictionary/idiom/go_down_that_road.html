to go down that road

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ daʊn ðæt roʊd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ daʊn ðət rəʊd / (BrE)
to pursue or begin a particular course of action, especially one that may have significant or undesirable consequences
pursue a particular course of action

Examples

If you decide to go down that road, be sure you know what you're getting into.
She warned him not to go down that road if he wanted to avoid trouble.
We don't have to go down that road again; let's try another solution.

Synset Examples

If you go down that road, you might regret it.
If we go down this road, we might find the perfect spot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take that path - to choose or follow a particular course of action
