out of the gate

1. ADV

/ aʊt əv ðə ɡeɪt / (AmE), / aʊt əv ðə ɡeɪt / (BrE)
immediately at the start of an activity or event, often with strong performance or energy
immediately at the start; from the outset

Examples

The new product sold incredibly well out of the gate and exceeded expectations.
Our team struggled out of the gate but quickly recovered.
Her proposal was met with enthusiasm out of the gate by the board members.

Synset Examples

She was out of the gate quickly with her new business.
The team performed well out of the gate in the season.
They went out of the gate strong with their marketing strategy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: from the get-go - from the very beginning

Etymology

From horse racing, where horses burst 'out of the gate' (starting gate) at the beginning of a race.
