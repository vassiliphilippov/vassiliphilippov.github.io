to haul ashes

1. VERB

/ hɔl ˈæʃɪz / (AmE), / hɔːl ˈæʃɪz / (BrE)
To feel extremely tired or worn out, especially after exertion.
feel very tired or exhausted
informal

Examples

After running the marathon, I really hauled ashes all afternoon.
She was hauling ashes by the end of her double shift at the hospital.
They all hauled ashes after moving furniture for hours.

Synset Examples

He felt like hauling his ashes after that workout.
