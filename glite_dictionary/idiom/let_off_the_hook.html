to let off the hook

1. VERB

/ lɛt ɑf ðə hʊk / (AmE), / lɛt ɒf ðə hʊk / (BrE)
To free someone from blame or from a difficult situation; to excuse or pardon someone for a mistake or wrongdoing.
excuse from blame or trouble

Examples

The teacher let him off the hook for not doing his homework because he was sick.
If you keep making excuses, they might not let you off the hook next time.
She was relieved to be let off the hook after she explained her mistake.

Synset Examples

She was let off the hook for missing the deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to forgive - to stop feeling angry or resentful toward someone for an offense or mistake
  • Antonym: to punish - to subject someone to a penalty for an offense
