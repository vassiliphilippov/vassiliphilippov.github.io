to get into one's stride

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈɪntuː wʌnz straɪd / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈɪntuː wʌnz straɪd / (BrE)
to become comfortable, confident, and effective in a situation after an initial period of adjustment
become effective after starting

Examples

It took me a few days to get into my stride at university, but now I feel at home.
The team struggled at first but quickly got into their stride after halftime.
Once she got into her stride, her performance was outstanding.

Synset Examples

It took me a week to get into my stride at the new job.
After a slow start, she finally got into her stride.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to find one's feet - to become confident or comfortable in a new situation
