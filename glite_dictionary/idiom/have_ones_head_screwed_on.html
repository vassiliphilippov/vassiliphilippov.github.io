to have one's head screwed on

1. VERB

/ hæv wʌnz hɛd skruːd ɑn / (AmE), / hæv wʌnz hɛd skruːd ɒn / (BrE)
to be sensible, practical, and able to make good decisions
be sensible and practical

Examples

Everyone trusts Lisa because she has her head screwed on and gives great advice.
If you want to succeed in stressful situations, you must have your head screwed on.
Unlike his absent-minded colleagues, Raj had his head screwed on during the busy season.

Synset Examples

He always seems to have his head screwed on when it counts.
You need to have your head screwed on to excel in this job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: level-headed - having a sensible and practical attitude
  • Antonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgment
