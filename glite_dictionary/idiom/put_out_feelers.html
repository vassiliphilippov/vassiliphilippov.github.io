to put out feelers

1. VERB

/ pʊt aʊt ˈfilɚz / (AmE), / pʊt aʊt ˈfiːləz / (BrE)
to make informal or preliminary inquiries or investigations to discover what people think or what is possible
make preliminary inquiries or investigations

Examples

Before approaching the board, she decided to put out feelers about the new proposal.
The company is putting out feelers to see if any partners are interested in collaboration.
He put out feelers to his contacts to learn more about job opportunities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sound out - to try to find out someone's opinion or intentions by talking to them

2. VERB

/ pʊt aʊt ˈfiːlɚz / (AmE), / pʊt aʊt ˈfiːləs / (BrE)
to make informal inquiries or test opinions to discover someone's thoughts or interest, especially discreetly or in advance of action
make informal inquiries or test interest

Examples

Before committing to the project, she decided to put out feelers to see if anyone was interested.
We should put out feelers to potential investors before launching the product.
He quietly put out feelers about switching departments, but didn't make any official moves yet.

Synset Examples

She decided to put out feelers for a new job.
He put out feelers about the upcoming conference.
They put out feelers to gauge interest in the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sound out - to try to find out what someone thinks or intends by asking questions
