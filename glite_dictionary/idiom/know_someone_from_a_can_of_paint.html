to know someone from a can of paint

1. VERB

/ noʊ ˈsʌmwʌn frəm ə kæn əv peɪnt / (AmE), / nəʊ ˈsʌmwən frəm ə kæn əv peɪnt / (BrE)
used to say that one does not know a person at all; to indicate a total lack of familiarity with someone
expressing total lack of familiarity
informal

Examples

I don't know her from a can of paint, but she seems nice.
He claimed he didn't know me from a can of paint when questioned.
She wouldn't know him from a can of paint if they passed on the street.

Synset Examples

I don't know her from a can of paint, but she seems nice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to know someone from Adam - to have any knowledge or familiarity with a person
