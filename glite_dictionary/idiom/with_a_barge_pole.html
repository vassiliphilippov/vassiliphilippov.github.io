with a barge pole

1. X

/ wɪð ə bɑrdʒ poʊl / (AmE), / wɪð ə bɑːdʒ pəʊl / (BrE)
used to emphasize that you would not want to be involved with or go near someone or something at all.
not want to go near (something) at all
british

Examples

I wouldn't touch that house with a barge pole after hearing its history.
She said she wouldn't go out with him with a barge pole.
Most investors won't invest in the company with a barge pole now.

Synset Examples

He wouldn't touch that old car with a barge pole.
