to come out of one's shell

1. VERB

/ kʌm aʊt əv wʌnz ʃɛl / (AmE), / kʌm aʊt əv wʌnz ʃɛl / (BrE)
to become more confident, outgoing, or willing to interact with others than before
become more outgoing or sociable

Examples

After joining the drama club, she really began to come out of her shell.
It was wonderful to see Mark come out of his shell during summer camp.
Teachers hope their students will come out of their shell and participate more in class.

Synset Examples

After a few weeks, she began to come out of her shell.
He will really come out of his shell at the party.
It's great to see him come out of his shell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to open up - to become more willing to talk and share feelings
  • Antonym: withdraw into one's shell - to become more shy or less communicative
