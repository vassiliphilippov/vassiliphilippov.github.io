to see daylight

1. VERB

/ si ˈdeɪˌlaɪt / (AmE), / siː ˈdeɪlaɪt / (BrE)
to perceive hope or clarity, especially after a period of difficulty or confusion
perceive hope or clarity after difficulty

Examples

After months of searching for a job, I finally see daylight in my prospects.
When the negotiations made progress, the team began to see daylight at last.
It's been a tough year, but now we can see daylight ahead.

Synset Examples

With the project nearly finished, we can see daylight now.

Once the storm passed, we could see daylight again.
They could see the daylight streaming through the windows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to see the light - to suddenly understand or realize something clearly
