to cut the apron strings

1. VERB

/ kʌt ði ˈeɪprən strɪŋz / (AmE), / kʌt ði ˈeɪprən strɪŋz / (BrE)
to become independent and stop relying on one's parents, especially to make one's own decisions or live separately
become independent from parental control

Examples

After graduating college, he decided to cut the apron strings and find his own apartment.
It's time for you to cut the apron strings and make decisions for yourself.
Many young adults struggle to cut the apron strings and gain independence from their families.

Synset Examples

She finally cut the apron strings and moved out.

Etymology

From the literal apron strings once used to attach young children to their mothers, symbolizing parental dependence.
