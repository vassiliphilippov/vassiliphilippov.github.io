to fishing in troubled waters

1. VERB

/ ˈfɪʃɪŋ ɪn ˈtrʌbəld ˈwɑtɚz / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃɪŋ ɪn ˈtrʌbəld ˈwɔːtəz / (BrE)
to try to gain an advantage or benefit from a difficult, chaotic, or risky situation, often by exploiting others' confusion or difficulty
seeking advantage in a risky or chaotic situation

Examples

After the merger was announced, many opportunists began fishing in troubled waters to secure better positions.
Journalists accused the politician of fishing in troubled waters during the crisis for personal gain.
With the company in turmoil, some employees tried fishing in troubled waters by lobbying for promotions.

Synset Examples

Navigating the politics felt like fishing in troubled waters.
Investing now feels like fishing in troubled waters.
She was accused of fishing in troubled waters by stirring up drama.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make hay while the sun shines - to take advantage of favorable conditions while they last
