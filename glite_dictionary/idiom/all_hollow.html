all hollow

1. ADJ

/ ɔl ˈhɑloʊ / (AmE), / ɔːl ˈhɒləʊ / (BrE)
Completely insincere or lacking genuine substance or real value.
completely insincere; without real substance

Examples

Her promises sounded all hollow after she failed to keep them.
The congratulations felt all hollow since nobody truly believed in his achievement.
Their laughter was all hollow and did not reach their eyes.

Synset Examples

His apologies felt all hollow after the argument.
The praise seemed all hollow without any real effort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hollow - insincere; without real value
