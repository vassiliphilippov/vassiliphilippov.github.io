one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind

1. X

/ wʌn smɔl stɛp fər mæn, wʌn ˈdʒaɪənt lip fər ˈmænˌkaɪnd / (AmE), / wʌn smɔːl stɛp fə mæn, wʌn ˈdʒaɪənt liːp fə ˈmænkaɪnd / (BrE)
used to describe a small action or event that leads to a significant achievement for humanity as a whole
a small act leading to great progress

Examples

The invention of the internet was one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind in the world of technology.
Her research represents one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind in the fight against disease.
Passing the new law was one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind in advancing equality.

Synset Examples

The first vaccine was one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind.
Her promotion felt like one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind.
This breakthrough is one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milestone - an important event marking a significant stage or progress

Etymology

First spoken by American astronaut Neil Armstrong in 1969 as he stepped onto the Moon.
