putting a hat on a hat

1. X

/ ˈpʊtɪŋ ə ˈhæt ɑn ə ˈhæt / (AmE), / ˈpʊtɪŋ ə ˈhæt ɒn ə ˈhæt / (BrE)
To add something unnecessary or redundant to something that is already sufficient.
adding something unnecessary or redundant

Examples

Adding more features to the already complicated app is just putting a hat on a hat.
He described the extra paperwork as putting a hat on a hat.
We all agreed that installing two alarms is putting a hat on a hat.

Synset Examples

Adding another layer of frosting is just putting a hat on a hat.
Her suggestion felt like putting a hat on a hat.
We don’t need more decorations; it’s just putting a hat on a hat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to gild the lily - to add unnecessary ornamentation to something already beautiful
  • Synonym: overkill - more of something than is necessary or appropriate
