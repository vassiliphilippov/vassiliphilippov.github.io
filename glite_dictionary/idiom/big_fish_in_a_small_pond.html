big fish in a small pond

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ fɪʃ ɪn ə smɑl pɑnd / (AmE), / bɪɡ fɪʃ ɪn ə smɔːl pɒnd / (BrE)
an important or influential person within a small group, organization, or sphere, often with limited wider significance
important person in a small group

Examples

After moving back to her hometown, she became a big fish in a small pond.
He enjoyed being the big fish in a small pond rather than competing in a larger company.
Some people prefer the status of a big fish in a small pond over the challenges of a bigger market.

Synset Examples

He feels like a big fish in a small pond here.
As a big fish in a small pond, she stands out.
Being a big fish in a small pond has its perks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: small fish in a big pond - a person with little influence in a much larger or more competitive environment
