to go all the way

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ɔl ðə weɪ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɔːl ðə weɪ / (BrE)
to fully commit to something or carry an action through to its completion
fully commit or finish something

Examples

If you want to win this competition, you have to go all the way.
She decided to go all the way and finish what she started.
They knew it would be hard, but they were determined to go all the way to success.

Synset Examples

She decided to go all the way with her education and pursue a doctorate.
You should go all the way to achieve your dreams.
If you want to succeed, you have to go all the way.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: see it through - to continue something until it is finished
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop trying to do something before it is finished
