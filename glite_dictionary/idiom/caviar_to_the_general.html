caviar to the general

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæv.i.ɑr tə ðə ˈdʒɛn.ə.rəl / (AmE), / ˈkæv.i.ɑː tə ðə ˈdʒɛn.ər.əl / (BrE)
Something of refined or high quality that is not appreciated or understood by most people.
something too refined for the majority

Examples

The professor's complex theories were really caviar to the general in that small rural town.
His poetry was caviar to the general, admired by few but misunderstood by many.
Much of avant-garde art can seem like caviar to the general and is often underappreciated.

Synset Examples

The expert's insights felt like caviar to the general audience.
Her thoughtful suggestions were caviar to the general crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acquired taste - something appreciated only by few people with special knowledge or interest

Etymology

From Shakespeare's Hamlet, 'caviare to the general', where 'general' means the general public.
