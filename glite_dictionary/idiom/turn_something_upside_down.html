to turn (something) upside down

1. VERB

/ tɝn ˈʌpsaɪd daʊn / (AmE), / tɜːn ˈʌpsaɪd daʊn / (BrE)
to cause a dramatic change or disruption, resulting in confusion or a significant alteration of circumstances
to disrupt or radically change a situation

Examples

The sudden news completely turned his world upside down.
New regulations turned the industry upside down within a few months.
Her unexpected arrival turned our plans upside down for the weekend.
