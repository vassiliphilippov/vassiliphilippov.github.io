to make someone's day

1. VERB

/ meɪk sʌmwʌnz deɪ / (AmE), / meɪk sʌmwənz deɪ / (BrE)
to make someone feel very happy or pleased; to bring great joy to someone
to make someone feel very happy

Examples

Her friend's thoughtful gift made her day after a difficult week.
The kind words from her teacher really made her day.
A simple smile from a stranger can sometimes make your day.

Synset Examples

Winning the award made her day.
Winning the door prize made her day at the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cheer up - to make someone feel happier
  • Antonym: upset - to make someone feel unhappy or distressed

2. VERB

/ meɪk ˈsʌmwʌnz deɪ / (AmE), / meɪk ˈsʌmwʌnz deɪ / (BrE)
to make someone feel very happy or pleased, often because of something unexpected or special
cause someone to feel very happy

Examples

Your thoughtful gift really made my day after a tough morning.
When I heard the good news, it absolutely made my day.
A simple compliment from a stranger made my day today.

Synset Examples

Your surprise visit really made my day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cheer up - to make someone feel happier or less sad
