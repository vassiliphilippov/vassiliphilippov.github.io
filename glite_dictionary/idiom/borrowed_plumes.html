borrowed plumes

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːɹoʊd pluːmz / (AmE), / ˈbɒɹəʊd pluːmz / (BrE)
items, qualities, or achievements taken from others to enhance one's own appearance or reputation, especially without rightful claim
claiming others' things as one's own

Examples

He was quick to criticize others who wore borrowed plumes instead of cultivating their own talents.
The artist's latest work seemed like it wore borrowed plumes, drawing heavily from earlier masters.
She warned her students not to rely on borrowed plumes when presenting their projects.

Synset Examples

It's easy to adorn oneself with borrowed plumes in the fashion world.
She doesn't truly shine, she just adorns herself with borrowed plumes.
He tends to adorn himself with borrowed plumes at parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretense - an attempt to make something that is not the case appear true
  • Synonym: affectation - behavior, speech, or writing that is artificial and designed to impress

Etymology

From the fable of the crow that adorned itself with feathers from other birds; metaphorical use since the 16th century.
