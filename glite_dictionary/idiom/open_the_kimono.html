to open the kimono

1. VERB

/ ˈoʊ.pən ðə kɪˈmoʊ.noʊ / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.pən ðə kɪˈməʊ.nəʊ / (BrE)
to reveal confidential or proprietary information; to be transparent, especially in business or negotiation contexts
reveal confidential information; be transparent

Examples

The company refused to open the kimono during the early negotiations with investors.
After months of secrecy, the startup finally decided to open the kimono and explain its technology.
The lawyer warned them not to open the kimono too soon in the process.

Synset Examples

The CEO will open the kimono to share financial insights.
In the meeting, they decided to open the kimono about their strategy.
It's time to open the kimono and reveal our plans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reveal - to make known something previously secret
  • Synonym: to disclose - to make secret information known
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep something secret or hidden

Etymology

Figurative use originating in American business jargon; 'kimono' is a Japanese garment, used here as metaphor for revealing something hidden.
