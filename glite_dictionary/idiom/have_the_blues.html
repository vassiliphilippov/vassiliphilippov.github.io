to have the blues

1. VERB

/ hæv ðə bluːz / (AmE), / hæv ðə bluːz / (BrE)
to feel sad or depressed
feel sad or depressed

Examples

Whenever the weather is gloomy, I often have the blues for no particular reason.
After his best friend moved away, Sam seemed to have the blues all week.
Listening to upbeat music sometimes helps when I have the blues.

Synset Examples

After losing the game, he really had the blues.
She tends to have the blues during rainy days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to feel down - to feel unhappy or depressed
  • Antonym: be cheerful - to feel happy and positive

Etymology

From 'the blues', a term for sadness or depression, influenced by blues music, which traditionally expresses melancholic themes.
