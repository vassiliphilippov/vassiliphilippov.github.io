to draw fire

1. VERB

/ drɔː ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / drɔː ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
to attract criticism, hostility, or adverse attention, especially as a result of one's actions or statements
attract criticism or hostility

Examples

The politician's controversial statement quickly drew fire from media outlets across the country.
Her decision to implement the new policy is likely to draw fire from skeptical employees.
He was prepared to draw fire for expressing an unpopular opinion during the debate.

Synset Examples

The bold statement was sure to draw fire from opponents.
His comments were meant to draw fire from the critics.
She knew her article would draw fire but published it anyway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attract criticism - receive negative comments or disapproval
  • Antonym: evade criticism - manage to avoid receiving negative attention

Etymology

Originally from military usage meaning 'to attract hostile gunfire.'
