to take a step back

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə stɛp bæk / (AmE), / teɪk ə stɛp bæk / (BrE)
to pause and reconsider a situation or to reevaluate one's position, often to gain perspective
pause and reconsider a situation

Examples

When faced with a challenge, it's wise to take a step back and assess the situation.
Sometimes you need to take a step back to see the bigger picture.
She decided to take a step back from her work to focus on her family.

Synset Examples

Sometimes you need to take a step back to reflect.
Take a step back and reassess your goals.
Let's take a step back and evaluate our options.
He took a step back to reassess his priorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reconsider - to think about again, especially to change a decision
  • Synonym: to reflect - to think deeply or carefully about something
