to carve out a niche

1. VERB

/ kɑrv aʊt ə niːʃ / (AmE), / kɑːv aʊt ə niːʃ / (BrE)
to successfully create and establish a specialized role, position, or market for oneself or an organization
create a specialized position or role

Examples

After years of hard work, she finally carved out a niche as a leading designer in sustainable fashion.
The new tech startup aims to carve out a niche in the competitive app market.
Writers often try to carve out a niche by focusing on a particular genre or style.

Synset Examples

He managed to carve out a niche in the market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: find a niche - to discover and occupy a particular, suitable position
  • Antonym: lose one's niche - to lose a specialized position or advantage
