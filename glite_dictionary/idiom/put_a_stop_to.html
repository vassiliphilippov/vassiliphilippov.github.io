to put a stop to

1. VERB

/ pʊt ə stɑp tu / (AmE), / pʊt ə stɒp tʊ / (BrE)
to bring an end to something; to cause something to cease or be discontinued
to bring something to an end

Examples

The principal decided to put a stop to bullying at the school.
After months of late-night parties, the landlord finally put a stop to the noise.
Environmentalists are urging the government to put a stop to illegal logging.

Synset Examples

We need to put a stop to the noise complaints.
The boss put a stop to the gibship in the office.
The manager decided to put a stop to the delays.
She finally put a stop to his constant interruptions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stop - to bring something to an end or cause it to cease
  • Synonym: to put an end to - to bring something to a close; to terminate
