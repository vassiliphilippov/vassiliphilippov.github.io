to be in the wars

1. VERB

/ bi ɪn ðə wɔrz / (AmE), / bi ɪn ðə wɔːz / (BrE)
to have suffered many difficulties, injuries, or problems, especially physically or emotionally
to have had many difficulties or injuries
british

Examples

After his bike accident, Tom was in the wars with cuts and bruises all over.
She looked like she had been in the wars after moving all weekend.
You’ve really been in the wars lately; I hope things improve soon.

Synset Examples

After all those surgeries, he has definitely been in the wars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be battered - to be badly injured or damaged
  • Antonym: be unscathed - to be unharmed; not injured or damaged
