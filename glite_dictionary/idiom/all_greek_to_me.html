all Greek to me

1. X

/ ɔl griːk tə miː / (AmE), / ɔːl ɡriːk tuː miː / (BrE)
used to say that something is completely incomprehensible or impossible to understand
completely incomprehensible; impossible to understand

Examples

When the professor started talking about quantum mechanics, it was all Greek to me.
I tried to read the contract, but the legal language was all Greek to me.
Computer programming looked all Greek to me until I took an introductory course.

Synset Examples

When he explained the math, it was all Greek to me.
The technical jargon in the meeting was all Greek to me.
Despite his enthusiasm, the presentation was all Greek to me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gibberish - unintelligible or nonsensical speech or writing
  • Synonym: clear as mud - equally unclear or difficult to understand

Etymology

From the phrase 'it's Greek to me', popularized in English by Shakespeare's play Julius Caesar (1599).
