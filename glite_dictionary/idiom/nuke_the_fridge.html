to nuke the fridge

1. VERB

/ nuːk ðə frɪdʒ / (AmE), / njuːk ðə frɪdʒ / (BrE)
(informal) To completely abandon or discard something, or to start over from scratch.
completely abandon or restart
informal

Examples

After several failed attempts to fix the code, the team decided to nuke the fridge and rewrite the program.
The new product launch went so poorly that management wanted to nuke the fridge and start fresh with a new concept.
When the storyline got too complicated, the writers chose to nuke the fridge and reboot the entire series.

Synset Examples

They decided to nuke the fridge and start over completely.
The project was so bad, we had to nuke the fridge.
After that last meeting, I felt like we should nuke the fridge.

Etymology

Based on a scene in the 2008 film Indiana Jones and the Kingdom of the Crystal Skull; patterned after the idiom "jump the shark."
