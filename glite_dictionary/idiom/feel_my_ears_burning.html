to feel my ears burning

1. VERB

/ fil maɪ ɪrz ˈbɜrnɪŋ / (AmE), / fiːl maɪ ɪəz ˈbɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
to have the sensation that someone is talking about you, often used humorously when one's name is mentioned in conversation
sense that someone is talking about you

Examples

Whenever people talk about my past mistakes, I can feel my ears burning.
Did you feel your ears burning yesterday? We were all talking about your achievements.
I always feel my ears burning when my family discusses me at family gatherings.

Synset Examples

When you mention her name, I can almost feel my ears burning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talk about someone behind their back - to discuss someone without their knowledge, especially in a negative or secretive way
