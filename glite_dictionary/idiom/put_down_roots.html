to put down roots

1. VERB

/ pʊt daʊn ruːts / (AmE), / pʊt daʊn ruːts / (BrE)
to settle permanently or for a long time in a particular place, especially by making it one's home
settle permanently in a place

Examples

After moving from city to city for years, they wanted to put down roots in a quiet suburb.
She never truly felt at home until she managed to put down roots in the local community.
Many people put down roots after finding stable jobs and starting families.

Synset Examples

After years of traveling, they finally put down roots in Seattle.
He decided to put down roots and start a family.
They are looking to buy a house and put down roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to settle down - to adopt a more stable or permanent way of life, especially in one place

Etymology

From the metaphor of a plant growing roots and becoming established in the soil.
