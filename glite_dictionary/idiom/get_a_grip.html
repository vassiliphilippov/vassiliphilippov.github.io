to get a grip

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ə ɡrɪp / (AmE), / ɡɛt ə ɡrɪp / (BrE)
to regain control of one's emotions or actions; to compose oneself after becoming upset or disoriented
regain composure or self-control

Examples

You need to get a grip before making any important decisions.
After hearing the bad news, she took a moment to get a grip on herself.
Whenever he feels overwhelmed, he reminds himself to get a grip and carry on.

Synset Examples

After the breakdown, he finally learned to get a grip.
It's time to get a grip and focus on your goals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pull oneself together - to recover control of one's emotions or actions
  • Antonym: to fall apart - to lose control emotionally or mentally
