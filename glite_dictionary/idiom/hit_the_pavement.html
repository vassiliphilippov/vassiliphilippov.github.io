to hit the pavement

1. VERB

/ hɪt ðə ˈpeɪvmənt / (AmE), / hɪt ðə ˈpeɪvmənt / (BrE)
to start actively looking for work, begin running or jogging, or go out onto the streets to promote or sell something
go out to look for work or take action

Examples

After college, he hit the pavement to find his first job.
Every morning at six, she hits the pavement for her daily run.
The sales team hit the pavement to hand out flyers to local businesses.

Synset Examples

After college, he hit the pavement looking for jobs.
She hit the pavement to start her morning run.
They hit the pavement early to distribute flyers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pound the pavement - to walk the streets actively seeking work
