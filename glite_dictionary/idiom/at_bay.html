at bay

1. ADJ

/ ət ˈbeɪ / (AmE), / ət ˈbeɪ / (BrE)
forced to confront or face attackers or danger, usually in a defensive position; unable to escape or retreat.
forced to face attackers or danger

Examples

The suspect was finally brought at bay by the police after a long chase.
The wounded animal stood at bay, ready to defend itself against the hunters.
His persistent critics kept him at bay with constant questions during the interview.

Synset Examples

a stag at bay
she had me cornered between the porch and her car
like a trapped animal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornered - forced into a position where escape is difficult or impossible
  • Synonym: trapped - being in a situation from which escape is difficult or impossible

Etymology

From hunting terminology where prey, especially stags, are 'at bay' when cornered and compelled to turn and face hounds.
