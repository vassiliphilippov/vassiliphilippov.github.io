out of wedlock

1. ADV

/ aʊt əv ˈwɛdlɑk / (AmE), / aʊt əv ˈwɛdlɒk / (BrE)
(of a child) born to parents who are not married to each other.
born to unmarried parents

Examples

He was born out of wedlock in a small rural town.
The law once discriminated against children born out of wedlock.
She revealed that her oldest son was born out of wedlock.

Synset Examples

2. ADV

/ aʊt əv ˈwɛdlɑk / (AmE), / aʊt əv ˈwɛdlɒk / (BrE)
(of a child) born to parents who are not married to each other.
born to unmarried parents

Examples

The couple had their first child out of wedlock and later got married.
Children born out of wedlock used to face social stigma in many societies.
She gave birth out of wedlock, which was uncommon in her community at the time.

Synset Examples

They had a child out of wedlock but are happy.
Having a child out of wedlock was a scandal in those days.
She was raised by a single mother out of wedlock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illegitimate - born to parents who are not married to each other

Etymology

From 'wedlock' meaning the state of being married; 'wedlock' from Old English 'weddlac' (state of being married).
