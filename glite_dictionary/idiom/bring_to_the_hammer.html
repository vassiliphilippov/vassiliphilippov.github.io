to bring to the hammer

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ tu ðə ˈhæmɚ / (AmE), / brɪŋ tʊ ðə ˈhamə / (BrE)
to offer something for sale at an auction
to sell at auction

Examples

The company decided to bring to the hammer several rare antiques to raise funds.
After her death, the entire estate was brought to the hammer by her heirs.
They plan to bring to the hammer their old collection of paintings next month.

Synset Examples

They decided to bring the artwork to the hammer next month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auction - a public sale in which goods or property are sold to the highest bidder
