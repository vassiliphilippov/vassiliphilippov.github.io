to get a crowd going

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ə kraʊd ˈɡoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ɡɛt ə kraʊd ˈɡəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
to make an audience lively, excited, or enthusiastic
to energize or excite an audience

Examples

The comedian really knows how to get a crowd going with his lively performances.
The band managed to get a crowd going despite the rainy weather.
Her energetic speech helped get a crowd going before the main event.

Synset Examples

As a jokesmith, he knows how to get a crowd going.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hype up - to stimulate or excite intensely
  • Antonym: to calm down - to make or become less excited or agitated
