to stand out in a crowd

1. VERB

/ stænd aʊt ɪn ə kraʊd / (AmE), / stænd aʊt ɪn ə kraʊd / (BrE)
to be easily noticed or distinct among many people
be noticeable among many people

Examples

Her unique sense of style makes her stand out in a crowd.
If you want to stand out in a crowd, wear something bright and colorful.
His confidence allowed him to stand out in a crowd during the interview.

Synset Examples

Her personality makes her stand out in a crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stand out - to be outstanding or prominent
  • Antonym: to blend in - not to be noticed; to look or act like those around you
