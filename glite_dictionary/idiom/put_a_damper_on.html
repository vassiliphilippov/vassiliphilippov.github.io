to put a damper on

1. VERB

/ pʊt ə ˈdæmpər ɑn / (AmE), / pʊt ə ˈdæmpə ɒn / (BrE)
to make something less enjoyable, successful, or exciting; to reduce enthusiasm or joy about something
reduce enthusiasm or enjoyment

Examples

The unexpected announcement put a damper on the entire conference.
His harsh criticism put a damper on what should have been a happy occasion.
Heavy traffic put a damper on our plans to arrive early at the event.

Synset Examples

Her negative comments put a damper on the mood.
The rain really put a damper on our picnic plans.
The news of the accident put a damper on the celebration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dampen - to make feelings or enthusiasm weaker; to lessen excitement
  • Antonym: to lift one's spirits - to make someone feel happier or more enthusiastic

Etymology

From the noun 'damper' (a device for reducing vibration or controlling air flow), used figuratively in the idiom.
