straight-from-the-shoulder

1. ADJ

/ streɪt frəm ðə ˈʃoʊldər / (AmE), / streɪt frəm ðə ˈʃəʊldə / (BrE)
(of speech or manner) direct and frank, without subtlety or evasion.
direct and frank; blunt

Examples

Her straight-from-the-shoulder comments made it clear how she felt about the proposal.
I appreciate his straight-from-the-shoulder advice, even if it's sometimes hard to hear.
The journalist was known for her straight-from-the-shoulder interviewing style.

Synset Examples

blunt talking and straight shooting
a blunt New England farmer
I gave them my candid opinion
forthright criticism
a forthright approach to the problem
tell me what you think — and you may just as well be frank
it is possible to be outspoken without being rude
plainspoken and to the point
a point-blank accusation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunt - speaking in a very direct, often abrupt way
  • Synonym: candid - truthful and straightforward; frank
  • Antonym: tactful - showing sensitivity and skill in dealing with others or difficult issues

Etymology

Refers to the straightforward motion of a punch delivered directly from the shoulder, likening direct speech to a direct physical movement.
