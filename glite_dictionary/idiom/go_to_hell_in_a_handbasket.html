to go to hell in a handbasket

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ tə hɛl ɪn ə ˈhændˌbæskɪt / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə hɛl ɪn ə ˈhændˌbɑːskɪt / (BrE)
(informal) To decline or deteriorate rapidly, especially in an uncontrollable or disastrous way.
deteriorate rapidly; go badly wrong
informal

Examples

When the economy crashed, the business went to hell in a handbasket within months.
After the new policy was introduced, everything seemed to go to hell in a handbasket.
Their vacation plans went to hell in a handbasket after their flight was cancelled.

Synset Examples

Their relationship fell to hell in a handbasket.
The project went to hell in a handbasket quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fall apart - to break down or collapse, especially suddenly; lose effectiveness or organization

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ tu hɛl ɪn ə ˈhændˌbæskət / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tuː hɛl ɪn ə ˈhændˌbɑːskɪt / (BrE)
to become worse very quickly, often in a way that is out of control or seems hopeless
rapidly deteriorate or fail

Examples

Many believe the country's education system is going to hell in a handbasket due to budget cuts.
If we don't fix the leaks now, the whole house will be going to hell in a handbasket soon.
After the new management took over, the company started going to hell in a handbasket.

Synset Examples

Without proper management, the business is going to hell in a handbasket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deteriorate - become progressively worse
  • Antonym: to improve - become better or make something better
