to play false

1. VERB

/ pleɪ fɔls / (AmE), / pleɪ fɔːls / (BrE)
to deceive someone by concealing true motives, often by pretending to have good intentions while seeking a personal advantage
deceive by pretending good intentions

Examples

It soon became clear that he was trying to play false with his business partners.
You should be careful not to play false when dealing with people who trust you.
She regretted playing false and causing so much disappointment.

Synset Examples

He bamboozled his professors into thinking that he knew the subject well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deceive - to mislead or trick someone
  • Synonym: to betray - to be disloyal to someone who trusts you
