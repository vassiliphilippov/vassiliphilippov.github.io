as far away as Timbuktu

1. X

/ əz fɑɹ əˈweɪ əz tɪmˈbʌktu / (AmE), / əz fɑːr əˈweɪ əz tɪmˈbʊktuː / (BrE)
used to describe something that seems extremely distant, remote, or difficult to reach or obtain
extremely distant or unreachable

Examples

In high school, college felt as far away as Timbuktu to many students in the village.
Financial stability sometimes seems as far away as Timbuktu for those struggling with debt.
The technology we wanted was as far away as Timbuktu when we started the project.

Synset Examples

She felt like her dreams were as far away as Timbuktu.

Etymology

Refers figuratively to Timbuktu, a city in Mali long regarded in the West as a remote and distant place.
