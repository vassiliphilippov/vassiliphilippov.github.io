string to one's bow

1. NOUN

/ strɪŋ tə wʌnz boʊ / (AmE), / strɪŋ tə wʌnz bəʊ / (BrE)
an additional skill or resource that someone has, especially one that may be useful in the future
an extra skill or resource

Examples

Learning public speaking has given me another string to my bow in my professional life.
She sees her language skills as an important string to her bow.
He decided to study graphic design to add a new string to his bow.

Synset Examples

She added cooking as a string to her bow.
He added coding as another string to his bow.
Learning Spanish is another string to my bow.
Having coding skills is a great string to her bow.
Having photography skills is another string to her bow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ace up one's sleeve - a secret advantage or resource kept in reserve
  • Synonym: feather in one's cap - an achievement or honor to be proud of

Etymology

From the idea of a bow (as in archery) being more versatile with extra strings; attested since at least the 17th century.
