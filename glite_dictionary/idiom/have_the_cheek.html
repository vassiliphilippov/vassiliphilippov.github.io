to have the cheek

1. VERB

/ hæv ðə tʃik / (AmE), / hæv ðə tʃiːk / (BrE)
to have the audacity or impudence to do something, often in a way that is surprising or disrespectful
to act with audacity or impudence
british

Examples

I can't believe she had the cheek to borrow my dress without asking.
You have the cheek to criticize my work after missing every deadline.
He had the cheek to complain about the service after causing so much trouble himself.

Synset Examples

He had the cheek to ask for a raise after being late.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to have the nerve - to have the audacity or boldness
  • Synonym: to have the gall - to have the impudence or rudeness to do something

Etymology

From the British English slang sense of 'cheek' meaning impudence or rudeness, originally referring to having the nerve to 'show one's face' in a bold way.
