for all they were worth

1. ADV

/ fɚ ɔl ðeɪ wɚ wɝθ / (AmE), / fə ɔːl ðeɪ wə wɜːθ / (BrE)
using all the effort, strength, or resources they have; as much as possible
using all available effort or resources

Examples

The cyclists pedaled for all they were worth to reach the finish line.
She argued for all she was worth to convince the board to fund her project.
The children tried for all they were worth to push the heavy cart up the hill.

Synset Examples

They campaigned for all they were worth to win the election.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to the utmost - to the greatest possible extent or degree
  • Antonym: half-heartedly - without enthusiasm or effort
