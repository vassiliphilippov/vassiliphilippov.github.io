to pull that stunt

1. VERB

/ pʊl ðæt stʌnt / (AmE), / pʊl ðæt stʌnt / (BrE)
to perform a reckless, foolish, or unexpected act, often causing surprise or disapproval
perform a reckless or foolish act
informal

Examples

If you pull that stunt again at work, you could get fired.
Everyone was shocked when he actually pulled that stunt despite all the warnings.
Her parents were furious after she pulled that stunt at the party.

Synset Examples

I can't believe he pulled that stunt; what a doucheburger!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pull a stunt - to do something foolish or risky
