on toast

1. X

/ ɑn toʊst / (AmE), / ɒn təʊst / (BrE)
in a position of difficulty, trouble, or disadvantage, often as a result of someone's actions
in trouble or a difficult situation
informal

Examples

If he misses another deadline, he'll be on toast with his boss.
After that disastrous meeting, the whole team was on toast.
She knew she was on toast when the error was discovered.

Synset Examples

With his latest mistake, he really has the manager on toast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in hot water - in trouble or a difficult situation
  • Synonym: on the ropes - in a situation close to defeat or serious trouble
