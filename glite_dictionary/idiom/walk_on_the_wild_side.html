to walk on the wild side

1. VERB

/ wɑk ɑn ðə waɪld saɪd / (AmE), / wɔːk ɒn ðə waɪld saɪd / (BrE)
to take risks or engage in adventurous or unconventional activities, often outside one's usual experience
engage in risky or adventurous activities

Examples

Sometimes you need to walk on the wild side to break out of your routine.
She decided to walk on the wild side by traveling alone around South America.
He encouraged his friends to walk on the wild side and try something new.

Synset Examples

Sometimes, you have to walk on the wild side to feel alive.
She decided to walk on the wild side by trying skydiving.
He always encourages his friends to walk on the wild side and explore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to play with fire - to take dangerous risks
  • Antonym: to play it safe - to avoid risks or stick to safe options
