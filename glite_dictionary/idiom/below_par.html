below par

1. ADJ

/ bɪˈloʊ pɑr / (AmE), / bɪˈləʊ pɑː / (BrE)
not as good as usual or expected; feeling less healthy or energetic than normal.
not as good as usual; feeling unwell

Examples

After the long flight, I felt below par for most of the next day.
The restaurant's service was below par compared to our last visit.
If your work continues to be below par, we might need to discuss your performance.

Synset Examples

His performance was below par this time.
The team's effort was below par in the last game.
She felt below par after missing sleep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substandard - of lower quality than expected or required
  • Antonym: fit as a fiddle - in very good health (idiom, antonym)

Etymology

From 'par' meaning a standard or expected level, especially in golf terminology.
