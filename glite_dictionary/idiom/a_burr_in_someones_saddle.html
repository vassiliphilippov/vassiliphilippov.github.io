a burr in someone's saddle

1. NOUN

/ ə bɝ ɪn ˈsædəl / (AmE), / ə bɜː ɪn ˈsædəl / (BrE)
a persistent cause of annoyance or irritation for someone
persistent cause of annoyance

Examples

The new tax laws have become a burr in his saddle for months now.
Dealing with unreliable suppliers was a burr in her saddle throughout the project.
The loud construction next door proved to be a burr in their saddle during exam season.

Synset Examples

The constant delays were a burr in his saddle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thorn in someone's side - a continual or persistent source of annoyance
  • Synonym: pet peeve - something that someone finds particularly annoying

Etymology

Alludes to the irritation caused to a rider if a burr—a prickly seed—gets stuck in a horse's saddle.
