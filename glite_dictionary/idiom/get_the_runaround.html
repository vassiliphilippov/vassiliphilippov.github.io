to get the runaround

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ðə ˈrʌnəˌraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡɛt ðə ˈrʌnəraʊnd / (BrE)
to be given evasive or misleading answers instead of direct help or information, especially by an authority or organization.
receive evasive or misleading answers

Examples

After calling three departments, I still just got the runaround about my refund.
Every time she asks about her missing parcel, she gets the runaround from customer service.
He was exhausted from getting the runaround at each government office.

Synset Examples

He felt frustrated after getting the runaround all day.
They always give me the runaround when I ask for help.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stonewall - refuse to answer questions or be cooperative
  • Synonym: give the runaround - to provide evasive or misleading information

Etymology

From the noun 'runaround', meaning evasive treatment, which developed in early 20th century American English.
