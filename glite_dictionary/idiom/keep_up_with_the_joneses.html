to keep up with the Joneses

1. VERB

/ kip ʌp wɪð ðə ˈdʒoʊnzɪz / (AmE), / kiːp ʌp wɪð ðə ˈdʒəʊnzɪz / (BrE)
to try to match one's neighbors or social peers in status, possessions, or lifestyle, often for appearance's sake
try to match others' status or lifestyle

Examples

Many families feel pressure to keep up with the Joneses by buying expensive gadgets.
She went on an extravagant vacation just to keep up with the Joneses.
Trying to keep up with the Joneses can lead to unnecessary debt.

Synset Examples

She feels pressured to keep up with the Joneses in her neighborhood.
He regrets trying to keep up with the Joneses financially.
They bought a new car just to keep up with the Joneses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to compete - strive to achieve superiority over others
  • Synonym: to emulate - to imitate someone, often with the intent to equal or surpass

Etymology

From a comic strip titled 'Keeping Up with the Joneses' (1913) by Arthur R. 'Pop' Momand.
