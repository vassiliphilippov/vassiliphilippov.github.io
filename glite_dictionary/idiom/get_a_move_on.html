to get a move on

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ə muv ɑn / (AmE), / ɡɛt ə muːv ɒn / (BrE)
to hurry up or start moving; to act more quickly
hurry up; start moving quickly
informal

Examples

We'd better get a move on or we'll miss the beginning of the movie.
If you don't get a move on, you'll be late for school again.
Come on, let's get a move on and finish this before lunch.

Synset Examples

You better get a move on or you'll be late!
It's getting late, so we need to get a move on.
We should get a move on if we want to catch the train.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hurry up - to do something more quickly
  • Antonym: to dawdle - to waste time or move too slowly
