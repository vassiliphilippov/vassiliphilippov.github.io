to peg the needle

1. VERB

/ pɛɡ ðə ˈniː.dl̩ / (AmE), / pɛɡ ðə ˈniː.dl̩ / (BrE)
to reach or maintain the highest possible level or limit, as measured by a gauge or indicator
reach or stay at the maximum limit
informal

Examples

With sales pegging the needle this quarter, the team celebrated outstanding results.
During the race, his engine was pegging the needle for almost the entire lap.
If your stress levels peg the needle for too long, it's time to take a break.

Synset Examples

He had to peg the needle to stay on track.
It's important to peg the needle while driving.
Make sure to peg the needle during the presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to max out - to reach the maximum limit, especially of a resource or value
  • Synonym: to redline - to operate at or push to the maximum safe limit, especially for engine speed

Etymology

From the literal action of a gauge needle reaching its maximum position on a dial.
