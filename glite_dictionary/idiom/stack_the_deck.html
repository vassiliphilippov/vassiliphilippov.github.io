to stack the deck

1. VERB

/ stæk ðə dɛk / (AmE), / stak ðə dɛk / (BrE)
to arrange a situation unfairly so as to achieve a desired outcome or advantage
arrange something unfairly to one's advantage

Examples

The company tried to stack the deck by hiring only supporters to serve on the committee.
She knew someone was trying to stack the deck against her during the job interview.
It's unfair to stack the deck so that nobody else has a chance to win.

Synset Examples

He always finds a way to stack the cards in his favor.
You shouldn't stack the deck if you want honest results.
To win, you can't stack the deck; play fair.
They tried to stack the deck for their own advantage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rig - to manipulate something in a dishonest way
  • Antonym: to play fair - to act in an honest and fair manner

Etymology

From the literal practice of stacking a deck of cards to predetermine the outcome, used figuratively since the 20th century.
