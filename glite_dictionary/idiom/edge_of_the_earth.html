edge of the earth

1. NOUN

/ ɛdʒ əv ði ɝθ / (AmE), / ɛdʒ əv ði ɜːθ / (BrE)
the most distant or remote place imaginable; the extreme limit or boundary of the world, often used metaphorically for a faraway location
the furthest or most remote place; farthest limit

Examples

Some people believe adventure begins at the edge of the earth, where maps end and mystery begins.
She felt like she had traveled to the edge of the earth to find peace and solitude.
Legends and myths often describe monsters lurking at the edge of the earth.

Synset Examples

The explorers stood at the edge of the earth, mesmerized.
They traveled to the edge of the earth to explore.
Legends speak of treasures hidden at the edge of the earth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ends of the earth - the most distant or remote places; everywhere on earth
