in any case

1. ADV

/ ɪn ˈɛni keɪs / (AmE), / ɪn ˈeni keɪs / (BrE)
used to introduce a statement that supports or emphasizes what was just said, or to change the topic slightly; anyway, regardless of what has been mentioned
regardless; anyway; to emphasize or shift topic

Examples

We might not have enough tickets, but in any case, we’ll figure something out.
I’m not sure if he’s coming; in any case, I’ll save him a seat.
The plan didn’t work; in any case, we learned something valuable.

Synset Examples

Anyhow, he is dead now
I think they're asleep; anyhow, they're quiet
I don't know what happened to it; anyway, it's gone
anyway, there is another factor to consider
I don't know how it started; in any case, there was a brief scuffle
in any event, the government faced a serious protest
but at any rate he got a knighthood for it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anyway - used to confirm or support a previous statement, or to change the subject
  • Synonym: at any rate - used to indicate that something is true or certain whatever happens
