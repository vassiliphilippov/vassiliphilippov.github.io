broth of a boy

1. NOUN

/ brɔθ əv ə bɔɪ / (AmE), / brɒθ əv ə bɔɪ / (BrE)
an exceptional or outstanding young man; a lively or remarkable boy (chiefly Irish).
an outstanding or lively young man

Examples

They all agreed Seamus was a broth of a boy, full of wit and good cheer.
At the festival, he was called a broth of a boy for his generous spirit.
If ever there was a broth of a boy, it's young Patrick with his clever tricks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broth of a girl - an outstanding or lively young woman (parallel idiom)

Etymology

From Irish usage, where 'broth of a boy' refers to someone as choice or excellent, likening the person to a delicious broth concentrated to essence.
