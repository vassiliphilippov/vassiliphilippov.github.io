to get the chop

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ðə tʃɑp / (AmE), / ɡɛt ðə tʃɒp / (BrE)
to be dismissed or removed from a job or position, especially suddenly or unexpectedly
be dismissed or removed from a position

Examples

Rumors are swirling that three team members may get the chop after the merger.
He was shocked to get the chop just before the holidays.
New management often means some employees will get the chop as part of restructuring.

Synset Examples

Rumors say the manager might get the chop soon.
They decided to cut costs, so someone had to get the chop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get the sack - be dismissed from a job
  • Synonym: get fired - be dismissed from employment
