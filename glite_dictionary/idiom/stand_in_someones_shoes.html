to stand in someone's shoes

1. VERB

/ stænd ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz ʃuz / (AmE), / stænd ɪn ˈsʌmwənz ʃuːz / (BrE)
to imagine oneself in another person's situation, especially in order to understand their feelings or perspective
empathize with another's situation

Examples

Before making a decision, try to stand in her shoes for a moment.
It can be difficult to stand in someone else's shoes and see things from their perspective.
He was finally able to resolve the conflict after he tried to stand in his brother's shoes.

Synset Examples

Before judging, try to stand in her shoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to put oneself in someone's shoes - to imagine being in another person's position or circumstances
  • Synonym: to empathize - to understand and share the feelings of another
