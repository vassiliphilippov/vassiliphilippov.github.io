snake in the grass

1. NOUN

/ sneɪk ɪn ðə ɡræs / (AmE), / sneɪk ɪn ðə ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A person who pretends to be friendly but is actually deceitful or treacherous.
deceitful or treacherous person

Examples

I thought I could trust him, but he turned out to be a real snake in the grass.
Beware of that snake in the grass who pretends to help but always causes trouble.
They discovered a snake in the grass among their close friends and felt deeply betrayed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traitor - a person who betrays someone or something
  • Antonym: friend - a person whom one knows and with whom one has a bond of mutual affection

Etymology

From the ancient Roman poet Virgil: 'latet anguis in herba' (a snake lies hidden in the grass); adopted in English in the 17th century.
