to come out of the woodwork

1. VERB

/ kʌm aʊt əv ðə ˈwʊdwɝk / (AmE), / kʌm aʊt əv ðə ˈwʊdwɜːk / (BrE)
to appear suddenly or unexpectedly, especially after having been hidden, inactive, or unnoticed
suddenly appear after being hidden or inactive

Examples

After she won the lottery, relatives she had never met came out of the woodwork to ask for money.
Whenever there's a big controversy, critics come out of the woodwork to give their opinions.
During the reunion, people I hadn't seen in years came out of the woodwork to say hello.

Synset Examples

Once the news broke, critics came out of the woodwork.
Old friends come out of the woodwork during reunions.
People always come out of the woodwork for charity events.

Etymology

Alludes to insects or creatures emerging from hidden places in woodwork, first recorded in the 20th century.
