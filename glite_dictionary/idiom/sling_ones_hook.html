to sling one's hook

1. VERB

/ slɪŋ wʌnz hʊk / (AmE), / slɪŋ wʌnz hʊk / (BrE)
to leave or depart, especially abruptly or when not wanted
depart; leave, often abruptly
informal british

Examples

After the disagreement, he decided to sling his hook and didn't come back.
If you don't like it here, you can just sling your hook.
She finally slung her hook and moved to a new city.

Synset Examples

After the argument, he decided to sling his hook and leave.
It's best to sling your hook if you’re not welcome.
She finally slung her hook from the toxic friendship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clear off - to go away or leave quickly
  • Synonym: leave - to go away from a place
  • Antonym: to arrive - to reach a place after traveling

Etymology

Probably British slang from early 19th century seafaring, where 'hook' refers to an anchor or tool, with 'sling' meaning to throw or remove.
