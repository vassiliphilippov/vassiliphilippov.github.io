quick off the mark

1. ADJ

/ kwɪk ɑf ðə mɑrk / (AmE), / kwɪk ɒf ðə mɑːk / (BrE)
prompt or fast to respond or take action, especially at the very start of an event or situation
prompt or fast to respond or act

Examples

She was quick off the mark when it came to solving the problem.
Politicians have to be quick off the mark in responding to breaking news.
You need to be quick off the mark if you want to get the best deals.

Synset Examples

He was quick off the mark to seize the opportunity.
She was quick off the mark in answering the question.
The athlete was quick off the mark at the start of the race.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: slow off the mark - slow to respond or take action

Etymology

Originates from athletics, where 'off the mark' refers to starting quickly from a starting line.
