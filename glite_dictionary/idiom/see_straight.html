to see straight

1. VERB

/ si ˈstreɪt / (AmE), / siː ˈstreɪt / (BrE)
to be able to think clearly or rationally, especially under stress or fatigue
think clearly or rationally
informal

Examples

After working all night, I was so exhausted that I couldn't see straight.
He's so overwhelmed by emotions that he can't see straight right now.
You need a break if you want to see straight in this situation.

Synset Examples

With all the stress, I cannot see straight anymore.
She was so tired that she could not see straight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to think straight - to think in a clear or logical way
