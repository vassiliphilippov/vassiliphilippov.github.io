time will tell

1. X

/ taɪm wɪl tɛl / (AmE), / taɪm wɪl tɛl / (BrE)
used to say that the outcome or result of something will become clear in the future
the future will reveal the outcome

Examples

Only time will tell if this approach is really effective.
She trusted her judgment, and knew that time will tell who was right.
People are excited about the new technology, but time will tell if it lives up to the hype.

Synset Examples

Only time will tell if this strategy works.
I believe in her talent; time will tell how far she goes.
He invested in stocks, but time will tell if it pays off.
