all over with

1. ADJ

/ ɔl ˈoʊvɚ wɪð / (AmE), / ɔːl ˈəʊvə wɪð / (BrE)
finished or completed; having come to an end.
finished, completed

Examples

The exam is all over with now, so I can finally relax.
Once the meeting was all over with, everyone left the room quickly.
She couldn't wait for the tedious job to be all over with.

Synset Examples

The party is all over with, time to clean up.
Her homework is all over with for the week.
The exam is all over with now, finally!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finished - having been completed
  • Synonym: over - at an end or finished
  • Antonym: beginning - the point in time or space at which something starts
