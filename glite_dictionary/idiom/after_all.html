after all

1. ADV

/ ˌæf.tɚ ˈɔl / (AmE), / ˌɑːf.tə ˈɔːl / (BrE)
used to indicate that something is true or happens despite previous doubts or reasons to the contrary; in the end or ultimately
despite doubts; in the end

Examples

She decided to attend the meeting after all, even though she was hesitant at first.
We thought the store was closed, but it was open after all.
He apologized after all, which surprised everyone in the office.

Synset Examples

She decided to go to the concert after all.
He might be late after all, so let's wait.
I decided to join the team after all.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ultimately - in the end, after everything else has been considered
  • Synonym: nevertheless - in spite of that; notwithstanding
