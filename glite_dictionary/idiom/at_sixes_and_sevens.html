at sixes and sevens

1. X

/ ət ˈsɪksɪz ənd ˈsɛvənz / (AmE), / ət ˈsɪksɪz ənd ˈsɛvənz / (BrE)
in a state of confusion or disorder; in a disorganized or uncertain situation
in confusion or disorder

Examples

After the renovation, the house was at sixes and sevens for weeks.
When the new manager arrived, the team was at sixes and sevens about their roles.
We were at sixes and sevens until the instructions finally arrived.

Synset Examples

After the move, everything was at sixes and sevens.
The office was at sixes and sevens before the deadline.
The plans were at sixes and sevens due to miscommunication.

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly from an old dice game; first recorded in the late Middle Ages in England.
