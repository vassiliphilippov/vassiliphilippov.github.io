to tear one's hair out

1. VERB

/ tɛɹ wʌnz hɛɹ aʊt / (AmE), / tɛə wʌnz heə aʊt / (BrE)
to feel or show extreme frustration or anxiety, often because of a difficult or stressful situation
be extremely frustrated or anxious

Examples

I've been tearing my hair out trying to fix this computer all day.
She nearly tore her hair out over the complicated instructions.
Parents everywhere are tearing their hair out during exam season.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be at one's wits' end - to be so worried, confused, or annoyed that you do not know what to do next
