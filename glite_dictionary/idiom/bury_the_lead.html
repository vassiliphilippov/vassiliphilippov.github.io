to bury the lead

1. VERB

/ ˈbɛri ðə liːd / (AmE), / ˈbɛri ðə liːd / (BrE)
to fail to emphasize or to delay stating the most important or interesting part of a news story, report, or discussion
fail to highlight the main point

Examples

If you bury the lead in your essay, readers might miss your main message.
Journalists are trained not to bury the lead when writing news reports.
The editor accused the reporter of trying to bury the lead by focusing on minor details first.

Synset Examples

The article buried the lead, making it hard to follow.
She often buries the lede in her articles, losing readers' interest.
He tended to bury the lead, causing confusion among readers.
Don't bury the lead in your report; highlight the key facts.
Make sure not to bury the lede in your presentation.
He tends to bury the lede, which frustrates his editor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: highlight - to draw attention to the main or most important part

Etymology

From journalistic terminology, where the 'lead' refers to the opening summary of a news story.
