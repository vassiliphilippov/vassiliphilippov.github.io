much ado about nothing

1. NOUN

/ mʌtʃ əˈdu əˌbaʊt ˈnʌθɪŋ / (AmE), / mʌtʃ əˈduː əˌbaʊt ˈnʌθɪŋ / (BrE)
a lot of fuss or unnecessary concern over something that is not important
excessive fuss over trivial matters

Examples

The board's debate over the expense was simply much ado about nothing.
Her complaints were much ado about nothing, as everything was resolved quickly.
After all the gossip, it proved to be much ado about nothing.

Synset Examples

They made much ado about nothing during the meeting.
It turned out to be much ado about nothing in the end.
The argument was really much ado about nothing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storm in a teacup - a lot of unnecessary anger or worry about a minor matter (British idiom)
  • Synonym: tempest in a teapot - a small event blown out of proportion (American idiom)

Etymology

Popularized by the title of Shakespeare's play 'Much Ado About Nothing' (1598–99).
