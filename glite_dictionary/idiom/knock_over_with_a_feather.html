to knock over with a feather

1. VERB

/ nɑk ˈoʊvɚ wɪð ə ˈfɛðɚ / (AmE), / nɒk ˈəʊvə wɪð ə ˈfɛðə / (BrE)
to surprise or shock someone so much that they are almost unable to react
to be extremely surprised or shocked

Examples

When I heard the news, you could have knocked me over with a feather.
The results of the test knocked him over with a feather.
She was so surprised by the offer that it nearly knocked her over with a feather.

Synset Examples

I was knocked over with a feather by the surprise party.
The announcement knocked her over with a feather.
Hearing the unexpected news knocked him over with a feather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to astonish - to surprise greatly
  • Synonym: to amaze - to fill with great surprise or wonder
  • Synonym: to take aback - to surprise or shock someone
