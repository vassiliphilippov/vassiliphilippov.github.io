to go the way of the dinosaurs

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ðə weɪ əv ðə ˈdaɪnəˌsɔrz / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ðə weɪ əv ðə ˈdaɪnəˌsɔːz / (BrE)
to become extinct or obsolete, especially as a result of failing to adapt to change
to become extinct or obsolete

Examples

Many traditional newspapers may soon go the way of the dinosaurs if they fail to embrace digital media.
Typewriters have largely gone the way of the dinosaurs in the age of computers.
Experts warn that failing to upgrade legacy software will cause it to go the way of the dinosaurs.

Synset Examples

Many small businesses go the way of the dinosaurs.
Innovations that fail to adapt may go the way of the dinosaurs.
If we ignore climate change, we might go the way of the dinosaurs.
Many outdated technologies have gone the way of the dinosaurs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: become obsolete - to no longer be used or useful
  • Synonym: go out of fashion - to stop being popular or current
  • Antonym: to endure - to continue to exist for a long time

Etymology

Refers to dinosaurs, an extinct group of reptiles, as a metaphor for things that disappear or become obsolete.
