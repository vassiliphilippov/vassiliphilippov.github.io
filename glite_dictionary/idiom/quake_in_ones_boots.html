to quake in one's boots

1. VERB

/ kweɪk ɪn wʌnz buːts / (AmE), / kweɪk ɪn wʌnz buːts / (BrE)
to feel extremely frightened or nervous
be extremely frightened

Examples

As the verdict was about to be read, he began to quake in his boots.
The soldiers made the villagers quake in their boots just by appearing at the gate.
Despite the tough exam, Mia refused to quake in her boots and faced it bravely.

Synset Examples

He began to quake in his boots when he heard the thunder.
The horror story made him quake in his boots.
She didn't quake in her boots despite the approaching storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tremble - to shake involuntarily, typically as a result of anxiety, excitement, or frailty
  • Synonym: to shake like a leaf - to tremble or shake with fear or nervousness
