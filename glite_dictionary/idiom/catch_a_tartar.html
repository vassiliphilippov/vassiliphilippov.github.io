to catch a Tartar

1. VERB

/ kætʃ ə ˈtɑrtər / (AmE), / kætʃ ə ˈtɑːtə / (BrE)
to unexpectedly meet or be faced with a difficult or formidable opponent or challenge
meet a formidable opponent or challenge

Examples

He thought the interview would be easy, but he soon realized he'd catch a Tartar in the panel chair.
The defending champion finally caught a Tartar in the quarter-finals and was eliminated.
Negotiators knew they would catch a Tartar if they tried to push for more concessions.

Synset Examples

It’s rare to catch a Tartar in these waters.
They didn't expect to catch a Tartar so early in the season.
He was surprised to catch a Tartar during the fishing trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meet your match - encounter an equal opponent; find someone as strong or skilled as you
  • Synonym: bite off more than you can chew - to take on a task that is too big or difficult

Etymology

From the historical reputation of the Tatars (often spelled 'Tartars'), a Central Asian people perceived as fierce opponents.
