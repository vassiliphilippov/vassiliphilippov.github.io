to button one's lip

1. VERB

/ ˈbʌtn wʌnz lɪp / (AmE), / ˈbʌtn wʌnz lɪp / (BrE)
to refrain from speaking or revealing information; to keep silent or quiet, especially intentionally
remain silent; keep quiet

Examples

When I heard the bad news, I knew I had to button my lip and not upset anyone.
She wanted to complain, but decided to button her lip just this once.
Sometimes it's better to button your lip instead of saying something you'll regret.

Synset Examples

She learned to button her lip in difficult situations.
He decided to button his lip about the secret.
I had to button my lip during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keep quiet - to avoid making noise or speaking
  • Antonym: to spill the beans - to reveal secret information
