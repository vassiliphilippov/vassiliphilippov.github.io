full sail

1. NOUN

/ fʊl seɪl / (AmE), / fʊl seɪl / (BrE)
operating or moving with maximum speed, energy, or effort; at the peak of one's capacity or momentum
at maximum speed or effort

Examples

The company is going full sail on its latest innovation strategy.
With full sail, the team completed the project ahead of schedule.
After months of indecision, she finally set out with full sail toward her dreams.

Synset Examples

The team is moving forward at full sail with the new project.
We set out on our journey with full sail and excitement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: full steam ahead - moving forward with maximum speed and determination
  • Antonym: half-hearted - without enthusiasm or commitment

Etymology

From nautical language, where a ship with all sails unfurled moves at top speed.
