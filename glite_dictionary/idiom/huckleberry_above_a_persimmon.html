huckleberry above a persimmon

1. X

/ ˈhʌkəlˌbɛri əˈbʌv ə pərˈsɪmən / (AmE), / ˈhʌkl̩ˌbɛri əˈbʌv ə pəˈsɪmən / (BrE)
To be the best or most favored in a group; someone who takes charge or feels superior.
someone who is the best or leader
american

Examples

In her small hometown, she always considered herself a huckleberry above a persimmon in every competition.
He loves being called the huckleberry above a persimmon at work because he's the top performer.
Don't go around acting like a huckleberry above a persimmon—everyone deserves respect.

Synset Examples

He claimed to be the huckleberry above a persimmon in our team.
In this project, I'm the huckleberry above a persimmon.
She's always been a huckleberry above her persimmon in this project.
His ego makes him feel like a huckleberry above his persimmon in the office.
Don't act like a huckleberry above your persimmon; stay humble.

Etymology

A rare American idiom comparing huckleberries and persimmons to signify superiority.
