for good measure

1. ADV

/ fər ɡʊd ˈmeɪʒər / (AmE), / fə ɡʊd ˈmeʒə / (BrE)
in addition to what has already been done or given, especially to ensure enough or as a bonus
as an added extra; in addition

Examples

She added a pinch of salt for good measure to the recipe.
They gave him an extra day off for good measure after his hard work.
The manager thanked everyone and handed out some gift cards for good measure.

Synset Examples

the car salesman threw in the radio, for good measure

Etymology

From the practice of adding a little extra to a measured amount to ensure it is sufficient or generous.

2. ADV

/ fər ɡʊd ˈmɛʒər / (AmE), / fə ɡʊd ˈmɛʒə / (BrE)
in addition to what is necessary, to ensure a sufficient or generous amount
in addition; to make sure there's enough

Examples

She decided to add a few more decorations for good measure before the guests arrived.
He double-checked the numbers for good measure to avoid any mistakes.
The chef sprinkled some extra herbs on the dish for good measure.

Synset Examples
