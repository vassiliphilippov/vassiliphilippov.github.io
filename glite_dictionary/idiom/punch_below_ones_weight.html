to punch below one's weight

1. VERB

/ pʌntʃ bɪˈloʊ wʌnz weɪt / (AmE), / pʌntʃ bɪˈləʊ wʌnz weɪt / (BrE)
to perform less effectively or achieve less than one's true potential, especially compared to one's perceived ability or capacity
perform below one's potential

Examples

Despite having talented players, the team continued to punch below its weight in the tournament.
The new marketing department is punching below its weight, considering its innovative ideas.
If you lack confidence, you might end up punching below your weight at work.

Synset Examples

The company is punching below its weight due to poor management.
He often punches below his weight when he doubts himself.
The team is known to punch below their weight in the league.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to punch above one's weight - to perform better than one's abilities would suggest

Etymology

From the sport of boxing, where fighting below one's designated weight class implies facing weaker opponents.
