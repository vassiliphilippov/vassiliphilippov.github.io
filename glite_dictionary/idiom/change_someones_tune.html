to change someone's tune

1. VERB

/ tʃeɪndʒ ˈsʌmwʌnz tuːn / (AmE), / tʃeɪndʒ ˈsʌmwənz tjuːn / (BrE)
to change one's opinion or attitude, especially in response to new evidence or circumstances
change one's opinion or attitude

Examples

He was critical of the plan at first, but after hearing the details, he changed his tune.
If they offer her a promotion, she might change her tune about leaving the company.
You'll change your tune when you realize how much this decision will benefit you.

Synset Examples

You’ll change your tune when you see the results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: change one's mind - to decide to do something different after previously deciding otherwise
  • Antonym: to stick to one's guns - to refuse to change one's opinion or position

Etymology

Alludes to changing the melody or key in music, figuratively representing a shift in opinion or stance.
