to get a rise out of

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ə raɪz aʊt ʌv / (AmE), / ɡɛt ə raɪz aʊt ɒv / (BrE)
to deliberately provoke a reaction, especially annoyance or anger, in someone
provoke a reaction (annoyance, etc.) in someone

Examples

My friends love to get a rise out of me by teasing my favorite sports team.
The comedian kept trying to get a rise out of the audience with his controversial jokes.
She grinned mischievously, knowing just how to get a rise out of her little brother.

Synset Examples

It's easy to get a rise out of my brother during family dinners.
She knows how to get a rise out of him with her jokes.
He always tries to get a rise out of his friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to provoke - to cause a reaction, especially an emotional one
