wait for it

1. X

/ weɪt fər ɪt / (AmE), / weɪt fə ɪt / (BrE)
used to draw attention to something impressive, surprising, or exciting that is about to happen or be revealed
phrase signaling something exciting to come
informal

Examples

The magician paused dramatically and said, wait for it, before revealing the trick.
He’s about to announce the winner, wait for it—you won!
The suspense was killing me as she kept saying, wait for it, before showing the surprise.

Synset Examples

You’ll see the results soon, just wait for it.
The best part of the show is next, wait for it.
