on a kick

1. ADJ

/ ɑn ə kɪk / (AmE), / ɒn ə kɪk / (BrE)
Enthusiastically involved in a particular activity or interest for a period of time, often to the exclusion of other things.
being absorbed in an activity for a time
informal

Examples

Lately, I've been on a kick of watching old detective movies every weekend.
He's on a kick with meditation and rarely misses a session these days.
Jessica went on a kick of painting every evening after work for a month.

Synset Examples

I’m currently on a kick of trying new recipes.
She’s been on a kick of reading classic novels this month.
He's been on a kick for healthy eating lately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on a spree - engaged in an activity with enthusiasm for a short period
