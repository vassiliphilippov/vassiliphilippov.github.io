to call an audible

1. VERB

/ kɔl ən ˈɑː.də.bəl / (AmE), / kɔːl ən ˈɔː.dɪ.bəl / (BrE)
to make a change in plans or strategy in response to unexpected circumstances, especially at the last moment
change plans at the last moment
informal american

Examples

When the restaurant was closed, we had to call an audible and find a new place for dinner.
The boss decided to call an audible after hearing the client's feedback at the last minute.
During the meeting, she quickly called an audible to address an unexpected problem.

Synset Examples

I had to call an audible to adjust to the sudden changes.
He had to call an audible when the plan fell through.
The coach decided to call an audible during the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to improvise - to create or perform without preparation
  • Antonym: to stick to the plan - to continue following the original instructions or strategy

Etymology

From American football, where a quarterback changes a play at the line of scrimmage by calling out a new instruction ('audible').
