to ride on the back of

1. VERB

/ raɪd ɑn ðə bæk əv / (AmE), / raɪd ɒn ðə bæk ɒv / (BrE)
to depend on or benefit from something that has previously happened or been achieved, especially past success
benefit from past achievements

Examples

The politician tried to ride on the back of his predecessor's popularity.
Their new app managed to ride on the back of earlier technology breakthroughs.
Many companies ride on the back of global trends to achieve quick growth.

Synset Examples

He decided to ride on the back of his reputation to get the job.
The new product rides on the back of last year's innovations.
Their success rides on the back of strong teamwork.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capitalize on - to gain an advantage from something
  • Synonym: to take advantage of - to make use of an opportunity or resource
