to save someone's ass

1. VERB

/ seɪv ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈæs / (AmE), / seɪv ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈas / (BrE)
to rescue someone from a difficult or dangerous situation, often at the last moment
rescue someone from trouble (informal, vulgar)
informal vulgar

Examples

Thanks for calling the police—you really saved my ass last night.
She always knows how to save someone's ass when things go wrong at work.
The lawyer saved his ass by finding a loophole in the contract.

Synset Examples

He really saved my ass during that presentation.
Without his help, I would have saved no one’s ass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: save someone's skin - rescue someone from difficulty or danger
  • Antonym: let someone down - fail to help or support someone, especially in a difficult situation
