to save (someone's) ass

1. VERB

/ seɪv ˈæs / (AmE), / seɪv ˈas / (BrE)
to rescue someone from a difficult or dangerous situation, often at the last moment; to save someone from trouble or embarrassment
rescue from trouble or danger (vulgar, idiom)
informal vulgar

Examples

He really saved my ass when he warned me about the deadline.
She tried to save his ass by covering up his mistake during the meeting.
If you’re ever in trouble, don’t expect me to save your ass again.

Synset Examples

I had to step in and save her ass from embarrassment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bail (someone) out - rescue someone from trouble, especially financial
  • Synonym: save (someone's) skin - rescue someone from trouble or danger (less vulgar)
