to fire away

1. VERB

/ ˌfaɪɚ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˌfaɪə əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to begin speaking or asking questions freely, especially when given permission to do so
begin speaking or asking questions freely

Examples

If you're ready to begin the meeting, please fire away with your questions.
The interviewer said, 'You can fire away whenever you're ready.'
She nodded and told him to fire away with his suggestions.

Synset Examples

If you have questions, feel free to fire away.
She told him to fire away with his ideas.
You can fire away when you're ready to start.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to speak up - to speak more loudly or express one's opinions openly
  • Antonym: to hold back - to hesitate or refrain from speaking or acting

Etymology

Originally a military command to begin shooting, later used figuratively in English.
