on the coattails

1. ADV

/ ɑn ðə ˈkoʊtˌteɪlz / (AmE), / ɒn ðə ˈkəʊtˌteɪlz / (BrE)
immediately benefiting from or following another person's success, achievements, or actions, often undeservedly
benefiting from another's success

Examples

Many politicians were elected on the coattails of a popular leader.
The new manager rose to power on the coattails of her predecessor's accomplishments.
He gained his reputation on the coattails of his older brother's fame.

Synset Examples

the CEO resigned on the coattails of the scandal
he was elected on his predecessor's coattails

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ride someone's coattails - to benefit from another's success

Etymology

From the literal image of following closely behind someone, often by holding onto their coat’s tails, suggesting benefit from their forward movement or position.
