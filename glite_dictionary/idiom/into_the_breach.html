into the breach

1. ADV

/ ˈɪn.tu ðə briːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tə ðə briːtʃ / (BrE)
to step forward and take action in a difficult or challenging situation, especially when others are unwilling or unable to do so
take action in a difficult situation

Examples

When the team leader fell ill, Sarah bravely stepped into the breach to keep the project on track.
As volunteers quit during the busy season, more seniors moved into the breach to help out.
When the power failed, it was Mark who went into the breach and fixed the generator.

Synset Examples

Once more into the breach, we face the challenge.
She was ready to step into the breach during the crisis.
When the manager left, I had to step into the breach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to step up - to take responsibility or action, especially in a crisis
  • Antonym: to stand back - to not take action or remain uninvolved in a situation

Etymology

From Shakespeare's play Henry V: 'Once more unto the breach, dear friends...' referring to entering a gap in a wall under attack.
