dry behind the ears

1. ADJ

/ draɪ bɪˈhaɪnd ði ɪrz / (AmE), / draɪ bɪˈhaɪnd ði ɪəz / (BrE)
Inexperienced or naive; lacking the maturity or experience expected in a certain context.
inexperienced or naive

Examples

Though he's not dry behind the ears yet, he's eager to prove himself on the job.
You can tell she's still dry behind the ears when it comes to negotiations.
He may not be dry behind the ears, but he learns quickly and asks good questions.

Synset Examples

She may be dry behind the ears, but she's eager to learn.
He's still dry behind the ears in this business.
Don't worry, you're still dry behind the ears.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wet behind the ears - naive or inexperienced; the more common variant

Etymology

Refers to young animals, such as calves, whose ears remain moist after birth until they grow older.
