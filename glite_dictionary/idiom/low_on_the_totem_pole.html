low on the totem pole

1. ADJ

/ loʊ ɑn ðə ˈtoʊtəm poʊl / (AmE), / ləʊ ɒn ðə ˈtəʊtəm pəʊl / (BrE)
having a low status or rank within an organization, group, or social structure
of low rank or status

Examples

As the youngest employee, I was definitely low on the totem pole and got all the simple tasks.
New students usually feel low on the totem pole until they find their place in the group.
Being low on the totem pole, she was rarely included in important meetings.

Synset Examples

As an intern, he felt low on the totem pole.
She was new and low on the totem pole at the company.
Being low on the totem pole means less responsibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at the bottom of the ladder - of lowest status; lowest position in an organization
  • Antonym: high on the totem pole - having a high rank or status in an organization

Etymology

From the perceived hierarchy on a Native American totem pole, though the original meaning in Native traditions is unrelated to rank.
