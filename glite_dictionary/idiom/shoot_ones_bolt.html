to shoot one's bolt

1. VERB

/ ʃuːt wʌnz boʊlt / (AmE), / ʃuːt wʌnz bəʊlt / (BrE)
to use up all one's energy, resources, or opportunities; to make one's final or only effort
to exhaust one's efforts or options

Examples

After working on the project for hours, he realized he had shot his bolt and couldn't continue.
Negotiators warned that the company had shot its bolt in earlier talks and had little left to offer.
By the final round, the athlete had shot his bolt and simply couldn't keep up with the competition.

Synset Examples

After the first few attempts, he felt he had shot his bolt.
He realized he had shot his bolt too early in the negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to run out of steam - to lose energy or enthusiasm; to tire out
  • Synonym: to give up - to stop trying; to admit defeat

Etymology

From the use of 'bolt' meaning a short, heavy arrow used in a crossbow; originally referred to firing one's only shot.
