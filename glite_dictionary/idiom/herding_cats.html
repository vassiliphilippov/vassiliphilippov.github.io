herding cats

1. X

/ ˈhɜrdɪŋ kæts / (AmE), / ˈhɜːdɪŋ kæts / (BrE)
used to describe the difficulty of managing or coordinating a situation that is chaotic or people who are difficult to control
trying to manage chaos or unruly people

Examples

Trying to coordinate everyone for the group photo was like herding cats.
She compared leading the committee to herding cats—everyone had their own ideas and agenda.
Getting the whole family out the door on time each morning is basically herding cats.

Synset Examples

Trying to organize this event is just herding cats.
She joked that her job was like herding cats.
Managing this project feels like herding cats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: like nailing jelly to a wall - attempting something nearly impossible to organize
