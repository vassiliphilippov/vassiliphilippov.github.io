packed to the rafters

1. ADJ

/ pækt tə ðə ˈræftɚz / (AmE), / pækt tə ðə ˈrɑːftəz / (BrE)
Completely full; filled to maximum capacity with people or things.
completely full; at maximum capacity

Examples

When the sale started, the store was packed to the rafters with eager customers.
His birthday party was packed to the rafters despite the rainy weather.
By the time the event began, the hall was packed to the rafters and there was standing room only.

Synset Examples

The concert hall was packed to the rafters with fans.
Their house was packed to the rafters after the family reunion.
The market was packed to the rafters with holiday shoppers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crammed - filled completely or squeezed tightly
  • Synonym: overflowing - so full that contents spill out or almost spill out

Etymology

Refers to buildings being so full that people or things reach up to the rafters (beams supporting the roof).
