all told

1. ADV

/ ɔl toʊld / (AmE), / ɔːl təʊld / (BrE)
used to indicate the total amount when everything is included or counted
with everything included or counted

Examples

All told, the project took over two years to complete.
There were fifteen people in the room, all told.
All told, they raised more than $10,000 for charity.

Synset Examples

altogether he earns close to a million dollars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: altogether - completely; with everything included

2. ADV

/ ɔl toʊld / (AmE), / ɔːl təʊld / (BrE)
when everything is considered; in total; altogether
in total; altogether

Examples

All told, more than fifty guests attended the wedding.
They raised over $10,000 for charity, all told.
All told, the renovations took nearly six months to complete.
