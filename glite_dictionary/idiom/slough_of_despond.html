slough of despond

1. NOUN

/ slaʊ əv dɪˈspɑnd / (AmE), / slaʊ əv dɪˈspɒnd / (BrE)
a state of deep sadness or despair, often one that is difficult to escape
state of deep despair
archaic

Examples

After the breakup, Mark found himself in a slough of despond and struggled to stay motivated.
History shows how entire communities can be trapped in a slough of despond during prolonged hardship.
The novel's protagonist fights to escape the slough of despond that engulfs him after failure.

Synset Examples

She felt trapped in a slough of despond after losing her job.
The movie depicted a character in a slough of despond.
He struggled to get out of his slough of despond.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: despair - the complete loss of hope
  • Synonym: melancholy - a deep and long-lasting sadness
  • Antonym: hope - a feeling of expectation and desire for a certain thing to happen

Etymology

Popularized by John Bunyan in "The Pilgrim's Progress" (1678), referring to a deep bog representing spiritual despondency; 'slough' means a muddy pit or swamp.
