to keep a dog and bark yourself

1. VERB

/ kip ə dɔːɡ ənd bɑrk jərˈsɛlf / (AmE), / kiːp ə dɒɡ ənd bɑːk jəˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to do work oneself that one has employed someone else to do; to perform unnecessary tasks when delegation is possible
do work oneself instead of delegating
british

Examples

There's no point in hiring an assistant if you keep a dog and bark yourself.
He insists on checking every detail; it's like he wants to keep a dog and bark himself.
If you trust your staff, you shouldn't keep a dog and bark yourself.

Synset Examples

She micromanages; why keep a dog and bark yourself?
You’re doing all the work; why keep a dog and bark yourself?

Etymology

Refers to the idea of hiring a watchdog but still barking oneself; proverbial idiom dating back to at least the 19th century.
