to let this cup pass from me

1. VERB

/ lɛt ðɪs kʌp pæs frəm mi / (AmE), / lɛt ðɪs kʌp pɑːs frəm mi / (BrE)
(idiomatic, often literary or religious) To wish to be spared from a difficult or unpleasant situation or responsibility.
wish to avoid a difficult situation

Examples

Though the decision was painful, she prayed to let this cup pass from me.
In moments of hardship, I sometimes wish life would let this cup pass from me.
He stood before the court, silently hoping fate would let this cup pass from me.

Synset Examples

He prayed to let this cup pass from me during his trials.
I wish this stress would let this cup pass from me.
In difficult times, I often wish to let this cup pass from me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to avoid - to keep away from or prevent (something) from happening
  • Antonym: to face the music - to confront unpleasant consequences or reality

Etymology

Biblical allusion to Jesus's prayer in the Garden of Gethsemane (Matthew 26:39) asking for suffering to be taken away.
