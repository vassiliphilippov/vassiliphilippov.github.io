to live on the edge

1. VERB

/ lɪv ɑn ði ɛdʒ / (AmE), / lɪv ɒn ði ɛdʒ / (BrE)
to lead a life full of risk, danger, or excitement by engaging in risky or adventurous activities
lead a risky or adventurous life

Examples

Some people choose to live on the edge by traveling to remote and dangerous places.
He prefers to live on the edge rather than settle for a quiet, predictable life.
Extreme athletes often live on the edge, seeking thrills that others might find terrifying.

Synset Examples

They enjoy adventures that make them live on the edge.
He loves to live on the edge with extreme sports.
Living on the edge keeps life exciting for her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take risks - to engage in actions involving uncertainty or danger
  • Antonym: to play it safe - to avoid risk and choose caution
