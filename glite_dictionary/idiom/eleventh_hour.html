eleventh hour

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛvənθ ˈaʊər / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛv(ə)nθ ˈaʊə / (BrE)
the latest possible time before something happens, especially before a deadline or a decisive event
the latest possible moment

Examples

The company submitted its proposal at the eleventh hour, just before the deadline.
She finished her assignment at the eleventh hour after procrastinating for weeks.
Negotiations between the two countries succeeded at the eleventh hour, avoiding a major crisis.

Synset Examples

money became available at the eleventh hour
at the last minute the government changed the rules

Related Terms

  • Synonym: last minute - the latest possible moment to do something

Etymology

From the Biblical parable in Matthew 20:1–16, where workers hired at the eleventh hour received the same wages as those hired earlier.
