to beat one's breast

1. VERB

/ biːt wʌnz brɛst / (AmE), / biːt wʌnz brɛst / (BrE)
to express deep regret, sorrow, or remorse, often by striking oneself on the chest as a traditional sign of contrition
express deep regret or sorrow

Examples

Amidst his guilt, he beat his breast and begged for forgiveness.
She beat her breast in grief after hearing the tragic news.
They beat their breast and wept during the solemn ceremony.

Synset Examples

He beat his breast in despair after losing the match.
In frustration, he beat his breast and shouted for help.
She beat her breast, lamenting her missed opportunities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lament - to express passionate grief or sorrow
  • Antonym: to rejoice - to feel or show great joy or delight

Etymology

Derived from the ancient custom of striking the chest as an outward expression of agony or penitence, seen in religious and literary texts.
