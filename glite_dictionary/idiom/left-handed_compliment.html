left-handed compliment

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛft ˈhændəd ˈkɑmpləmənt / (AmE), / ˌlɛft ˈhændɪd ˈkɒmplɪmənt / (BrE)
A remark that appears to be a compliment but is actually insincere or carries an implied insult.
insincere or subtly insulting compliment

Examples

When he said she was smarter than she looked, it was clearly a left-handed compliment.
Her comment that my speech was better than usual felt like a left-handed compliment.
He received a left-handed compliment when a colleague praised his work ethic but criticized his methods.

Synset Examples

His remark was a left-handed compliment disguised as praise.
That was a left-handed compliment, not very flattering.
She gave him a left-handed compliment about his new haircut.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backhanded compliment - an insincere or ambiguous compliment, often implying criticism
  • Degree Lower: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration
  • Antonym: insult - a disrespectful or scornful remark or act

Etymology

Refers to the traditional association of the left hand with awkwardness or untrustworthiness in European cultures.
