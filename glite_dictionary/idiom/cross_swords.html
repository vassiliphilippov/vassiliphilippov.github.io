to cross swords

1. VERB

/ krɑs sɔrdz / (AmE), / krɒs sɔːdz / (BrE)
to engage in a conflict or argument, especially a verbal one
to argue or have a conflict

Examples

The two lawyers crossed swords during the heated trial.
She never hesitated to cross swords with her critics in public.
We often cross swords at meetings, but we always find a compromise.

Synset Examples

The two rivals finally crossed swords in the debate.
The boxers crossed swords in an intense championship match.
They crossed swords over their differing opinions on the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to argue - to discuss or disagree, often strongly
  • Antonym: to agree - to have the same opinion; not in conflict
