pickin' and grinnin'

1. X

/ ˈpɪkɪn ən ˈɡrɪnɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɪkɪn ən ˈɡrɪnɪn / (BrE)
enjoying oneself, especially while playing music, often with a joyful or playful spirit
having fun, esp. playing music
informal american

Examples

After dinner, everyone gathered on the porch and started pickin' and grinnin' until midnight.
The bluegrass band loves pickin' and grinnin' at local festivals every summer.
There was plenty of pickin' and grinnin' going on during the family reunion.

Synset Examples

At the festival, they were pickin' and grinnin' all evening.
He loves pickin' and grinnin' with his friends on weekends.
The band was pickin' and grinnin' while the crowd danced.

Etymology

From American folk and country music slang: 'pickin'' (playing an instrument, esp. guitar or banjo) and 'grinnin'' (smiling).
