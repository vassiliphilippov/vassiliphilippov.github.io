infinite wisdom

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.fə.nət ˈwɪz.dəm / (AmE), / ˌɪn.fɪ.nət ˈwɪz.dəm / (BrE)
used, often ironically, to refer to someone's supposed supreme knowledge or judgment, especially when it leads to questionable decisions
(often ironic) supposed supreme knowledge or judgment

Examples

In his infinite wisdom, he decided to cancel the trip despite perfect weather.
The committee, in its infinite wisdom, approved the confusing new policy.
Parents sometimes act in their infinite wisdom, but children see things differently.

Synset Examples

In his infinite wisdom, he decided to ignore the advice.
She made a mistake in her infinite wisdom, as usual.
He chose to invest in stocks, in his infinite wisdom.
