Jesus, Mary and Joseph

1. INTJ

/ ˈdʒiːzəs ˌmɛri ənd dʒəˈsoʊf / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːzəs ˌmeəri ənd dʒəˈsəʊf / (BrE)
used, especially in Irish English, to express surprise, astonishment, or shock
expression of surprise or shock

Examples

Jesus, Mary and Joseph, what happened to your car?
When I opened the door, I gasped, 'Jesus, Mary and Joseph!'
'Jesus, Mary and Joseph, are you all right?' she exclaimed.

Synset Examples

When she saw the mess, she exclaimed, Jesus, Mary and Joseph!
He couldn't believe his eyes and said, Jesus, Mary and Joseph!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goodness gracious - expression of surprise or shock
  • Synonym: my God - interjection for surprise or emphasis

Etymology

Refers to Jesus Christ, Mary (his mother), and Joseph (her husband), commonly invoked in Irish Catholic speech.
