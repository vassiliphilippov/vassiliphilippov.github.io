to get the boogaloo going

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ðə ˈbuːɡəˌluː ˈɡoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ɡɛt ðə ˈbuːɡəluː ˈɡəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
to start or encourage a lively dancing or party atmosphere
start a lively dance or party
informal

Examples

As soon as the band started playing, it really got the boogaloo going on the dance floor.
She knew how to get the boogaloo going with her amazing playlist.
A few energetic guests managed to get the boogaloo going despite the slow start to the night.

Synset Examples

They played some funky music to get the boogaloo going.

Etymology

From 'boogaloo', a style of dance music that originated in the 1960s in the U.S.; 'boogaloo' used here to mean lively dancing.
