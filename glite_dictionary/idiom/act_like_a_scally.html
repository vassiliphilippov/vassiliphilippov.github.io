to act like a scally

1. VERB

/ ækt laɪk ə ˈskæli / (AmE), / ækt laɪk ə ˈskæli / (BrE)
to behave in a foolish, irresponsible, or loutish manner, especially in a way typical of a 'scally' (a mischievous or unruly person, especially in British usage)
behave in a foolish or irresponsible way
informal british

Examples

Stop telling him to act like a scally when we're at the interview.
If you act like a scally at school, you'll get into trouble sooner or later.
She couldn't believe he would act like a scally in front of her parents.

Synset Examples

They warned him not to act like a scally during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to misbehave - to behave badly or improperly
  • Antonym: to behave - to act in a proper or desired way

Etymology

From British slang 'scally', a shortening of 'scallywag' (a mischievous or unruly person).
