to fly by the seat of your pants

1. VERB

/ flaɪ baɪ ðə sit əv jʊr pænts / (AmE), / flaɪ baɪ ðə siːt əv jɔː pænts / (BrE)
to act or make decisions by instinct, improvising rather than planning ahead or following rules
improvise or act without preparation

Examples

When the power went out during the show, the actors had to fly by the seat of their pants.
He didn't have a script, so he just flew by the seat of his pants and hoped for the best.
We can't afford to fly by the seat of our pants with such an important client.

Synset Examples

He often flies by the seat of his pants when making decisions.
I had to fly by the seat of my pants during the presentation.
We can't just fly by the seat of our pants on this project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to improvise - to create or perform spontaneously without preparation
  • Antonym: plan - to decide and arrange in advance

Etymology

Originally from early aviation, when pilots flew airplanes without instruments, relying on instinct and sensation.
