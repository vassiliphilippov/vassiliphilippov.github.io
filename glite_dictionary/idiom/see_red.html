to see red

1. VERB

/ si rɛd / (AmE), / siː rɛd / (BrE)
to become suddenly very angry
become very angry

Examples

Every time someone parks in his space, he sees red.
She began to see red when accused unfairly in the meeting.
If you insult his friends, he'll instantly see red.

Synset Examples

He angers easily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lose one's temper - suddenly become very angry
  • Antonym: to calm down - become less angry or agitated

2. VERB

/ si ˈrɛd / (AmE), / siː ˈrɛd / (BrE)
to become very angry or furious; to be filled with intense irritation or rage
to become very angry

Examples

Whenever he thinks someone has lied to him, he starts seeing red.
She tried to stay calm, but the constant interruptions made her see red.
It takes a lot to make her see red, but this time she really lost her temper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to blow a fuse - to suddenly lose one's temper; become very angry
  • Synonym: to lose one's temper - to fail to control one's anger; become angry

Etymology

From the association of red with anger, possibly referring to the redness of the face or to the image of a bull reacting to a red cape.
