to give full measure

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv fʊl ˈmɛʒɚ / (AmE), / ɡɪv fʊl ˈmɛʒə / (BrE)
to do something with the greatest possible effort or commitment; to perform a task as well as possible
perform with maximum effort

Examples

Despite his illness, he continued to give full measure at work every day.
The team always strives to give full measure in every performance.
She expects her students to give full measure on every assignment.

Synset Examples

The cast gives full measure every night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give one's all - to put all one's effort into something
  • Synonym: to do one's best - to perform as well as one is able

Etymology

Originates from the practice of measuring commodities honestly and completely, extended metaphorically to mean giving the utmost effort.
