from the rooter to the tooter

1. X

/ frəm ðə ˈruːtər tə ðə ˈtuːtər / (AmE), / frəm ðə ˈruːtə tə ðə ˈtuːtə / (BrE)
Completely or thoroughly, from beginning to end; covering every part or aspect.
completely; from beginning to end
informal

Examples

After the storm, volunteers cleaned the park from the rooter to the tooter.
She checked the manuscript from the rooter to the tooter before submitting it to the publisher.
They renovated the house from the rooter to the tooter, leaving no corner untouched.

Synset Examples

The cleaning crew scrubbed the office from the rooter to the tooter.
He refurbished the car from the rooter to the tooter.
The renovation changed the house from the rooter to the tooter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: from start to finish - from the beginning to the end
  • Synonym: from soup to nuts - from beginning to end (American idiom)

Etymology

Originating in African-American Vernacular English, based on the anatomy of a pig from snout ('rooter') to tail ('tooter').
