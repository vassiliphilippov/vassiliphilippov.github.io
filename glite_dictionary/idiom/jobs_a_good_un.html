job's a good 'un

1. X

/ dʒɑbz ə ɡʊd ən / (AmE), / dʒɒbz ə ɡʊd ən / (BrE)
(British) Used to express satisfaction after successfully completing a task or job; all done and well done.
expression of satisfaction after a job is done
british informal

Examples

We finally finished painting the fence; job's a good 'un!
Once the software installed without errors, she smiled and said, job's a good 'un.
He put the last screw in place and declared, job's a good 'un.

Synset Examples

After fixing the car, he said, 'job's a good 'un.'
The presentation went well; job's a good 'un, indeed!
He finished the project; job's a good 'un!

Etymology

Colloquial British English; 'good 'un' is a contracted form of 'good one.'
