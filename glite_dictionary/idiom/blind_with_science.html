to blind with science

1. VERB

/ blaɪnd wɪð ˈsaɪəns / (AmE), / blaɪnd wɪð ˈsaɪəns / (BrE)
to overwhelm or confuse (someone) with technical or scientific information that is difficult to understand
overwhelm with complex scientific information

Examples

Whenever I listen to her explain quantum mechanics, I feel blind with science.
The salesperson tried to make me blind with science by using lots of technical jargon.
He likes to blind with science when talking to people unfamiliar with computers.

Synset Examples

She became blind with science when discussing quantum physics.
He was blind with science during the debate.
The professor's complex theories left us blind with science.
