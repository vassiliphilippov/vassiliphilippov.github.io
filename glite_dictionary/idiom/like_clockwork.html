like clockwork

1. ADV

/ laɪk ˈklɑːkˌwɝk / (AmE), / laɪk ˈklɒkˌwɜːk / (BrE)
in a regular and precise manner; happening smoothly and without problems or delays
with regularity and precision

Examples

Every morning, the train arrives like clockwork at precisely 8 a.m.
Her monthly reports are always submitted like clockwork, never late or missing details.
The automatic sprinklers turn on like clockwork every evening at six.

Synset Examples

the rocket launch went off like clockwork

Etymology

Alludes to the reliable and precise movement of a well-made clock.
