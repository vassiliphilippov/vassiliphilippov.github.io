to put the clock back

1. VERB

/ pʊt ðə klɑk bæk / (AmE), / pʊt ðə klɒk bæk / (BrE)
to imagine or wish for a return to an earlier time, especially in the hope of changing events or reliving the past
wish to return to an earlier time

Examples

If I could put the clock back, I would make a different decision.
Sometimes she wishes she could put the clock back and relive her childhood summers.
It's impossible to put the clock back, but we can learn from our mistakes.

Synset Examples

If only we could put the clock back and start again.
I wish I could put the clock back to yesterday.
She wants to put the clock back to her childhood.
