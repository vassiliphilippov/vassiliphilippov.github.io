to hold in contempt

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ɪn kənˈtɛmpt / (AmE), / həʊld ɪn kənˈtɛmpt / (BrE)
to regard someone or something with strong disdain or disrespect
regard with strong disdain or disrespect

Examples

Many critics hold in contempt the film's superficial message.
If you hold in contempt those around you, you may find yourself isolated.
The professor seemed to hold in contempt any work he considered intellectually shallow.

Synset Examples

She felt he was trying to hold her in contempt for her opinions.
His actions caused the committee to hold him in contempt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to despise - to feel a strong dislike or lack of respect for someone or something
  • Antonym: to admire - to regard someone or something with respect or warm approval
