to haul over the coals

1. VERB

/ hɔl ˈoʊvɚ ðə koʊlz / (AmE), / hɔːl ˈəʊvə ðə kəʊlz / (BrE)
to reprimand someone severely or criticize them harshly for a mistake or wrongdoing
reprimand or criticize harshly

Examples

If you arrive late again, the director will haul you over the coals in front of everyone.
He was hauled over the coals by the board for mishandling the project budget.
After the error was discovered, she knew she would soon be hauled over the coals by her supervisor.

Synset Examples

The manager will haul you over the coals for missing the deadline.
Don't let him haul you over the coals for a small mistake.
She was ready to haul him over the coals for his poor performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebuke - to express sharp disapproval or criticism
  • Synonym: scold - to angrily criticize someone for their actions
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

Refers to a historical method of punishment, vividly describing harsh criticism; first recorded in English in the 17th century.
