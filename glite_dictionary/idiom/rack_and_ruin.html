rack and ruin

1. NOUN

/ ræk ənd ˈruː.ɪn / (AmE), / ræk ənd ˈruː.ɪn / (BrE)
A state of complete decay or destruction; total collapse or deterioration.
total decay or destruction

Examples

After years of neglect, the old mansion fell into rack and ruin.
The orchard has gone to rack and ruin since the owners moved away.
If the building isn't restored soon, it will end up in rack and ruin.

Synset Examples

The abandoned house fell into rack and ruin over the years.
Without care, the garden descended into rack and ruin.
The once vibrant community was left in rack and ruin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruin - the process or state of complete decay or destruction
  • Synonym: decay - the gradual destruction or decline of something

Etymology

From Middle English "wrack" (wreck, destruction) and "ruin" (collapse, decay), used together since at least the 16th century.
