testicular fortitude

1. NOUN

/ tɛˈstɪkjələr ˈfɔrtəˌtud / (AmE), / tɛˈstɪkjʊlə ˈfɔːtɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
courage or bravery, especially when facing difficult or challenging situations
courage or bravery in tough situations
informal

Examples

He finally showed testicular fortitude and stood up to his boss.
It takes a lot of testicular fortitude to start your own business from scratch.
The politician's testicular fortitude impressed voters during the heated debate.

Synset Examples

He showed testicular fortitude by speaking out against injustice.
It takes testicular fortitude to confront your fears.
She admired his testicular fortitude in tackling tough issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courage - the ability to do something that frightens one; bravery
  • Synonym: bravery - courageous behavior or character
  • Antonym: cowardice - lack of courage; timid behavior

Etymology

Formed from 'testicular' (relating to testicles, from Latin 'testiculus') and 'fortitude' (from Latin 'fortitudo' meaning courage or strength). Used metaphorically to suggest boldness.
