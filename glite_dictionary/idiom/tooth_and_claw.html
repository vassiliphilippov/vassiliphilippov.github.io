tooth and claw

1. X

/ tuθ ənd klɔ / (AmE), / tuːθ ənd klɔː / (BrE)
with great effort, determination, or aggression; using any means necessary, especially in a fierce or ruthless manner
with fierce effort or aggression

Examples

She fought tooth and claw to get the promotion she deserved.
The two companies competed tooth and claw for market dominance.
Election campaigns are often battled tooth and claw until the very end.

Synset Examples

The team fought tooth, fang, and claw for victory.
They worked tooth, fang, and claw to secure the deal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fight tooth and nail - to fight with great determination or ferocity

Etymology

From the phrase 'nature red in tooth and claw,' popularized by Tennyson, referring to the brutal struggle for survival in nature.
