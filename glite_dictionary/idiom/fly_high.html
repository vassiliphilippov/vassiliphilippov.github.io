to fly high

1. VERB

/ flaɪ haɪ / (AmE), / flaɪ haɪ / (BrE)
to be very successful or at the peak of achievement, especially in a career or activity
achieve great success or prominence

Examples

After winning the championship, the team continued to fly high for several seasons.
Her business is really flying high after the recent product launch.
Many artists fly high early in their careers but struggle later on.

Synset Examples

The new student is thriving

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to thrive - to grow or develop vigorously; to be successful
  • Synonym: flourish - to develop in a healthy or vigorous way, especially as the result of a favorable environment
  • Antonym: struggle - to experience difficulty and make a great effort to achieve something

2. VERB

/ flaɪ haɪ / (AmE), / flaɪ haɪ / (BrE)
to achieve great success or to be extremely happy
achieve great success or happiness

Examples

After years of hard work, she finally began to fly high in her chosen field.
With their latest album topping the charts, the band continued to fly high.
When you feel confident and motivated, you can really fly high.

Synset Examples

She always wanted to fly high in her career.
His dreams seemed to fly high after the promotion.
It's amazing to watch the eagle fly high above the mountains.
When you succeed, it's like you can fly high above the rest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to succeed - to achieve a desired aim or result
  • Synonym: flourish - to develop in a healthy, vigorous way; to thrive
  • Antonym: to fail - to not succeed in achieving something
