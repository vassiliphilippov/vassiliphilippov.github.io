to have a bite

1. VERB

/ hæv ə baɪt / (AmE), / hæv ə baɪt / (BrE)
to eat a small amount of food, typically a snack or light meal
eat a small amount of food

Examples

Why don't we have a bite before the movie starts?
She suggested they have a bite at the nearby bakery.
After the meeting, everyone wanted to have a bite to eat.

Synset Examples

Do you want to have a bite before we leave?
Let’s have a bite at that new café down the street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to grab a bite - to have a quick meal or snack
