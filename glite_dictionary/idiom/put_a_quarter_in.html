to put a quarter in

1. VERB

/ pʊt ə ˈkwɔrtɚ ɪn / (AmE), / pʊt ə ˈkwɔːtə ɪn / (BrE)
(of a person) to become suddenly energetic, enthusiastic, or talkative, as if activated or switched on.
to suddenly become energetic or talkative
informal american

Examples

Ever since you got that promotion, it's like someone put a quarter in you at work.
Wow, who put a quarter in Sarah? She’s been giving ideas non-stop all morning.
As soon as the music started, he acted like someone had put a quarter in him.

Synset Examples

You seem energetic today; who put a quarter in you?

Etymology

Refers humorously to coin-operated machines that are activated by inserting a quarter (25-cent coin).
