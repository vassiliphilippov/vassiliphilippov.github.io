to put one's hand in one's pocket

1. VERB

/ pʊt wʌnz hænd ɪn wʌnz ˈpɑːkɪt / (AmE), / pʊt wʌnz hænd ɪn wʌnz ˈpɒkɪt / (BrE)
to spend or contribute money, especially willingly or when it was not strictly necessary
to spend or contribute money

Examples

When the charity needed funds, she didn't hesitate to put her hand in her pocket.
You'll have to put your hand in your pocket if you want to buy better quality.
For the team dinner, each member was expected to put their hand in their pocket.

Synset Examples

I had to put my hand in my pocket to pay the bill.
Sometimes you just have to put your hand in your pocket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pay - to give money in exchange for something
  • Synonym: to chip in - to contribute one's share of money
