to go off on tangents

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ɔf ɑn ˈtændʒənts / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɒf ɒn ˈtændʒənts / (BrE)
to start talking about or discussing topics that are only slightly related or completely unrelated to the main subject
deviate from the main subject

Examples

During the lecture, the professor would often go off on tangents and share unrelated personal stories.
We wasted a lot of time when the team started to go off on tangents instead of focusing on the agenda.
She tends to go off on tangents, making meetings longer than necessary.

Synset Examples

He tends to go off on tangents during meetings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to digress - to leave the main subject temporarily in speech or writing

Etymology

From mathematical 'tangent', meaning a line that touches a curve at one point but does not cross it; figurative use for diverging from a main line of thought.
