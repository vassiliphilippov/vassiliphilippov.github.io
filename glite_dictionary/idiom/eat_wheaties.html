to eat Wheaties

1. VERB

/ it ˈwiːtiːz / (AmE), / iːt ˈwiːtiːz / (BrE)
to be energized or highly motivated, as if exceptionally prepared for a challenge
feel energized and ready for a challenge
informal american

Examples

Before the big presentation, Mark acted like he had truly eaten his Wheaties.
Our quarterback must have eaten his Wheaties this morning—he played incredibly well!
She was so focused in class that her friends joked she'd clearly eaten her Wheaties.

Synset Examples

After a good night's sleep, she really felt like she had eaten her Wheaties.
He tackled the project with energy, as if he had eaten his Wheaties.
She showed up to the game ready, clearly having eaten her Wheaties.

Etymology

From a long-running advertising slogan for Wheaties breakfast cereal promoting strength and energy.
