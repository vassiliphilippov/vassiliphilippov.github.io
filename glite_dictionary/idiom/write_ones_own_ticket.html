to write one's own ticket

1. VERB

/ raɪt wʌnz oʊn ˈtɪkət / (AmE), / raɪt wʌnz əʊn ˈtɪkɪt / (BrE)
to be in a position to choose or determine one's own future or terms of success, especially due to one's abilities or achievements
be in control of one's own future

Examples

With her impressive portfolio, she can write her own ticket in the art world.
Talented programmers often write their own ticket in the tech industry.
After years of dedication, he finally wrote his own ticket to success.

Synset Examples

With her skills, she can write her own ticket in the industry.

Graduating with honors allows you to write your own ticket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to call the shots - to be in control and make important decisions
