kiss of death

1. NOUN

/ kɪs əv dɛθ / (AmE), / kɪs ɒv dɛθ / (BrE)
an event or action that ultimately causes failure or ruin to someone or something
something that leads to inevitable failure

Examples

The scandal was the kiss of death for his celebrity career.
Failing to meet the deadline could be the kiss of death for the project.
Poor customer service has been the kiss of death for many businesses.

Synset Examples

if this were known, it would be the kiss of death for my political career

Related Terms

  • Synonym: death blow - a decisive, finishing, or destructive event

Etymology

From the biblical story of Judas Iscariot betraying Jesus with a kiss.

2. NOUN

/ kɪs əv dɛθ / (AmE), / kɪs əv dɛθ / (BrE)
An act, event, or circumstance that inevitably causes failure, ruin, or destruction.
thing that guarantees failure or ruin

Examples

For many companies, ignoring new technology can be the kiss of death.
Her lack of preparation was the kiss of death in the final interview.
Delays in responding to customer complaints proved to be the kiss of death for the restaurant.

Synset Examples

For a politician, a scandal is the kiss of death.
Not adapting to change is the kiss of death for businesses.
That mistake was the kiss of death for his career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: death blow - an action or event that brings about ruin or destruction
  • Antonym: saving grace - a redeeming quality or factor that prevents failure

Etymology

Originally from the act of a Mafia boss kissing someone to mark them for death, later extended to mean any event leading to inevitable ruin.
