crux of the matter

1. NOUN

/ krʌks əv ðə ˈmætər / (AmE), / krʌks əv ðə ˈmætə / (BrE)
the most important or central point of an issue or discussion
the central or main point of an issue

Examples

We talked for hours, but only now have we reached the crux of the matter.
The crux of the matter is whether we have enough resources to finish the project.
Let's not get distracted and focus on the crux of the matter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heart of the matter - the central or most important part of an issue
  • Synonym: essence - the intrinsic nature or most important quality of something

Etymology

From Latin 'crux' meaning 'cross' or 'critical point'.
