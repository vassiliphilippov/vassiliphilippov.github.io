on your high horse

1. ADV

/ ɑn jɔr haɪ hɔrs / (AmE), / ɒn jɔː haɪ hɔːs / (BrE)
acting in a superior, self-righteous, or arrogant manner, often by judging others harshly
behaving in a self-righteous or arrogant way

Examples

You don't have to get on your high horse just because I made a mistake.
Whenever he talks about politics, she climbs on her high horse and lectures everyone.
Stop getting on your high horse; we're all just trying our best.

Synset Examples

Don't get on your high horse; we all make mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrogant - having an exaggerated sense of one's own importance or abilities
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low view of one's importance

Etymology

From the historical association of riding a high horse with superiority or arrogance.
