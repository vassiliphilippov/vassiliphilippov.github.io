bolt from the blue

1. NOUN

/ boʊlt frəm ðə blu / (AmE), / bəʊlt frəm ðə bluː / (BrE)
a sudden and unexpected event or shock
sudden, unexpected event

Examples

The company’s sudden closure came as a bolt from the blue to its employees.
Winning the lottery was a real bolt from the blue for her family.
His decision to move abroad struck everyone like a bolt from the blue.

Synset Examples

The sudden storm felt like a bolt from the blue.
The news of their engagement was a bolt from the blue.
Her resignation was a bolt from the blue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: out of the blue - completely unexpectedly; without warning
