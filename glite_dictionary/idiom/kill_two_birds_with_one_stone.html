kill two birds with one stone

1. X

/ kɪl tu bɝdz wɪð wʌn stoʊn / (AmE), / kɪl tuː bɜːdz wɪð wʌn stəʊn / (BrE)
to achieve two objectives with a single action or effort
achieve two objectives at once (idiom)

Examples

We realized we could kill two birds with one stone by doing our grocery shopping on the way to visit my parents.
By organizing a joint event, the club managed to kill two birds with one stone.
She killed two birds with one stone by finishing her assignment while waiting at the airport.

Synset Examples

We can kill two birds with one stone by combining the meetings.
She managed to kill two birds with one stone by studying while commuting.
He killed two birds with one stone by exercising and socializing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get two things done at once - accomplish two tasks simultaneously

Etymology

Literal meaning first recorded in 17th-century English; possibly adapted from earlier proverbs in various languages.
