down the line

1. ADV

/ daʊn ðə laɪn / (AmE), / daʊn ðə laɪn / (BrE)
at some point in the future; later on
at a future time

Examples

We expect to see significant changes down the line as technology advances.
If you keep saving now, it will benefit you down the line.
Problems ignored now may become much bigger down the line.

Synset Examples

This decision will affect us down the line.
He plans to invest more money down the line.
We will see the results down the line.
