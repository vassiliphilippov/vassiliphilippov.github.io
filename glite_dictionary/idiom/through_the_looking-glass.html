through the looking-glass

1. X

/ θru ðə ˈlʊkɪŋˌɡlæs / (AmE), / θruː ðə ˈlʊkɪŋˌɡlɑːs / (BrE)
used to describe entering a strange, surreal, or alternate reality, often with distorted logic or unexpected events
in a surreal or alternate reality

Examples

After the merger, the office atmosphere felt like we had gone through the looking-glass overnight.
In that courtroom, logic was inverted, as if we’d slipped through the looking-glass.
Her theories take readers through the looking-glass into a world of possibility.

Synset Examples

Life feels like we're living through the looking-glass sometimes.
Her story was like stepping through the looking-glass into another world.
I entered a realm of fantasy, like through the looking-glass.

Etymology

From Lewis Carroll's book 'Through the Looking-Glass' (1871), where a character enters a bizarre world through a mirror.
