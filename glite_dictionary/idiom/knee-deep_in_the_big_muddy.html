knee-deep in the Big Muddy

1. ADJ

/ niː ˈdiːp ɪn ðə bɪɡ ˈmʌdi / (AmE), / niː ˈdiːp ɪn ðə bɪɡ ˈmʌdi / (BrE)
Deeply involved in a difficult or worsening situation, especially one where withdrawing is challenging or risky.
deeply involved in a difficult situation
american

Examples

After taking on so many risky investments, the company found itself knee-deep in the Big Muddy.
He hesitated, realizing they were already knee-deep in the Big Muddy with the failed marketing campaign.
By ignoring the warning signs, the team became knee-deep in the Big Muddy before they even noticed the financial losses.

Synset Examples

The project left us knee-deep in the Big Muddy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in hot water - in trouble or difficulty
  • Antonym: out of the woods - no longer in difficulty or danger

Etymology

From Pete Seeger's 1960s antiwar song 'Waist Deep in the Big Muddy,' evoking imagery of being stuck in a worsening situation.
