to make it up as one goes along

1. VERB

/ ˈmeɪk ɪt ʌp æz wʌn ɡoʊz əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪk ɪt ʌp əz wʌn ɡəʊz əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to improvise or invent something as the situation develops, without a pre-planned approach
improvise without a plan

Examples

Sometimes, in new situations, you just have to make it up as you go along.
He didn't have a script for the presentation and had to make it up as he went along.
Experienced teachers know how to make it up as they go along when plans change unexpectedly.

Synset Examples

When teaching, she prefers to make it up as she goes along.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to improvise - to create or perform without preparation
