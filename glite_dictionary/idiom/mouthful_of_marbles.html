mouthful of marbles

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊθ.fəl əv ˈmɑr.bəlz / (AmE), / ˈmaʊθ.fəl əv ˈmɑː.bəlz / (BrE)
(of speech) sounding indistinct, unclear, or difficult to understand, as if speaking with something obstructing the mouth.
speaking unclearly or indistinctly

Examples

After his dental surgery, he sounded like he had a mouthful of marbles when he spoke.
The phone connection was so bad, her words came across like a mouthful of marbles.
Whenever he gets nervous, he talks as if he has a mouthful of marbles.

Synset Examples

She spoke like she had a mouthful of marbles.
Trying to understand him felt like listening to a mouthful of marbles.
His accent made him sound like he had a mouthful of marbles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mumble - to speak quietly and not clearly enough for the words to be understood easily
  • Antonym: to enunciate - to pronounce words clearly (opposite meaning in this context)
