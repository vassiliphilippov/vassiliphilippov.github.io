down Queer Street

1. X

/ daʊn kwɪr strit / (AmE), / daʊn kwɪə striːt / (BrE)
in trouble or facing serious financial or personal difficulties, especially as a result of risky or questionable decisions.
in difficulty; facing trouble, esp. financially
british

Examples

If you keep spending like that, you'll end up down Queer Street.
After failing to pay back the loan, Mark realized he was down Queer Street.
She warned him that accepting the shady deal could land him down Queer Street.

Synset Examples

She joked that he was heading down Queer Street with his choices.
After the scandal, he felt like he was on Queer Street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in hot water - in trouble or in a difficult situation
  • Antonym: out of the woods - out of danger or difficulty

Etymology

The phrase dates to the 19th century in Britain, possibly referencing 'Care Street', with 'Queer' here meaning strange or difficult.
