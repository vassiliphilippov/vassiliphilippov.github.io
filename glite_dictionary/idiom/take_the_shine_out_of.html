to take the shine out of

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə ʃaɪn aʊt ʌv / (AmE), / teɪk ðə ʃaɪn aʊt ɒv / (BrE)
to make something seem less enjoyable or impressive; to lessen the pleasure or positive aspect of something
lessen enjoyment or impressiveness

Examples

She didn't let the criticism take the shine out of her performance.
A last-minute mistake could take the shine out of an otherwise perfect presentation.
Their constant complaints really take the shine out of working here.

Synset Examples

Don't let anyone take the shine out of your success.
Their negativity really took the shine out of the celebration.
His criticism took the shine out of her achievement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dampen - to make something less strong or intense, especially feelings
  • Synonym: take the wind out of someone's sails - to make someone less confident or enthusiastic
