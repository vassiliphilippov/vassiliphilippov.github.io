to overegg the pudding

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈɛɡ ðə ˈpʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərˈɛɡ ðə ˈpʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
to add unnecessary detail or embellishment to something, making it excessive or ruined by overdoing
exaggerate or overdo something
british

Examples

I think if you add more decorations, you’ll just overegg the pudding.
The director overegged the pudding by including too many plot twists in the film.
Let's just keep the design simple and not overegg the pudding.

Synset Examples

Don’t overegg the pudding; it’s already perfect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overdo - to do something to an excessive degree
  • Antonym: to understate - to present or express in a restrained or minimal way

Etymology

British idiom, based on the idea that adding too many eggs spoils a pudding.
