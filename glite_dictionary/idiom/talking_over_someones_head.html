to talking over someone's head

1. VERB

/ ˈtɔkɪŋ ˈoʊvɚ sʌmˌwʌnz hɛd / (AmE), / ˈtɔːkɪŋ ˈəʊvə sʌmˌwʌnz hɛd / (BrE)
to discuss something in a way that is too complex or advanced for the listener to understand
speak in too complex terms

Examples

The professor kept talking over my head and I felt lost in the lecture.
I wish you wouldn't talk over her head when explaining technical details.
He realized he was talking over their heads and tried to simplify his language.

Synset Examples

She explained it simply, avoiding talking over my head.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to dumb down - to make something simpler and easier to understand
  • Antonym: to talk down to - to speak to someone as if they are less intelligent or knowledgeable
