to hold with the hare and run with the hounds

1. VERB

/ hoʊld wɪð ðə hɛr ənd rʌn wɪð ðə haʊndz / (AmE), / həʊld wɪð ðə heə ənd rʌn wɪð ðə haʊndz / (BrE)
to try to support or please two opposing sides at the same time, often in a way that is insincere or opportunistic
support two opposing sides at once

Examples

It is impossible to hold with the hare and run with the hounds during a heated political debate.
Her attempt to hold with the hare and run with the hounds left both sides distrustful of her motives.
If you try to hold with the hare and run with the hounds, you risk displeasing everyone in the end.

Synset Examples

To succeed, you can't hold with the hare and run with the hounds.
He tries to hold with the hare and run with the hounds at work.
She learned that holding with the hare and running with the hounds is risky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sit on the fence - to avoid taking sides in a dispute

Etymology

Alludes to hunting, where the hare is chased by the hounds; used since at least the 18th century to describe trying to please both sides.
