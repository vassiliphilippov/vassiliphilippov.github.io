to shoot the breeze

1. VERB

/ ʃuːt ðə briːz / (AmE), / ʃuːt ðə briːz / (BrE)
to engage in light, informal conversation; to chat casually, especially about unimportant matters.
chat informally; make casual conversation
informal

Examples

On Friday nights, we like to shoot the breeze over dinner at the local diner.
They spent hours on the porch just shooting the breeze as the sun went down.
During lunch breaks, coworkers often shoot the breeze about movies and sports.

Synset Examples

the men were sitting in the cafe and shooting the breeze

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chat - to talk in a friendly and informal way
  • Synonym: converse - to engage in spoken exchange of ideas
  • Degree Higher: to discuss - to talk about a subject in detail
  • Degree Higher: gossip - to talk about personal or sensational matters

2. VERB

/ ʃuːt ðə briːz / (AmE), / ʃuːt ðə briːz / (BrE)
to engage in casual or idle conversation, usually about unimportant topics
talk informally; chat casually
informal american

Examples

Every Friday afternoon, my coworkers gather to shoot the breeze before heading home for the weekend.
After dinner, my grandparents would sit on the porch and shoot the breeze for hours.
We ended up shooting the breeze about our favorite old movies all night.

Synset Examples

We often meet at the park to shoot the breeze.
On weekends, I like to shoot the breeze with friends.
They spent the afternoon shooting the breeze over coffee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chat - talk in a friendly and informal way
  • Synonym: make small talk - engage in light, casual conversation

Etymology

Originated in early 20th-century American slang, likely evoking the image of speaking aimlessly as if firing into the air.
