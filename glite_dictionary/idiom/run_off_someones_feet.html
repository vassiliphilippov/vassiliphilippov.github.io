to run off someone's feet

1. VERB

/ rʌn ɔf ˈsʌmwʌnz fit / (AmE), / rʌn ɒf ˈsʌmwʌnz fiːt / (BrE)
to exhaust or overwhelm someone with too much activity or work
exhaust with excessive activity

Examples

The waiters were run off their feet during the lunch rush at the restaurant.
With all the last-minute orders, we were run off our feet yesterday.
She's been run off her feet organizing the conference this week.

Synset Examples

The busy holiday season will run us off our feet.
Her energy level can run everyone off their feet.
The new project has run the team off their feet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhaust - to make someone extremely tired
  • Synonym: to overwhelm - to give someone too much to deal with
