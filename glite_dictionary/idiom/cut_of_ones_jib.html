cut of one's jib

1. NOUN

/ kʌt əv wʌnz dʒɪb / (AmE), / kʌt ɒv wʌnz dʒɪb / (BrE)
the general appearance or character of a person, especially as it suggests something about them
person's appearance or character

Examples

I like the cut of his jib; he seems reliable and straightforward.
The cut of her jib tells me she's determined to succeed.
You can usually tell a person's intentions by the cut of their jib.

Synset Examples

The cut of her jib suggests she's an ambitious person.
You can tell the cut of his jib; he’s quite professional.
I like the cut of his jib; he seems trustworthy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demeanor - outward behavior or bearing
  • Synonym: appearance - the way that someone looks

Etymology

From nautical jargon; originally referred to the shape of a ship's jib sail, which could indicate nationality or intention.
