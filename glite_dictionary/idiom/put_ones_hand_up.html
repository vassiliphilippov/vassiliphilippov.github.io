to put one's hand up

1. VERB

/ pʊt wan(z) hænd ʌp / (AmE), / pʊt wʌnz hænd ʌp / (BrE)
to raise one's hand to volunteer or indicate willingness to participate, especially in a group setting
volunteer or signal by raising hand

Examples

She always puts her hand up first when the teacher asks a question.
If you want to join the team, just put your hand up and let us know.
Several people put their hands up to volunteer for the project.

Synset Examples

I put my hand up when they asked for help.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volunteer - to offer to do something by choice
  • Synonym: to raise one's hand - to lift a hand in the air, often to ask for attention or indicate desire to participate
