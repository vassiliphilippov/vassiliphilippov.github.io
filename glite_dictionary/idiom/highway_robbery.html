highway robbery

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌweɪ ˈrɑːbəri / (AmE), / ˈhaɪweɪ ˈrɒbəri / (BrE)
a situation in which something is sold at an unreasonably high price, often considered unfair or exploitative.
unfairly high price or charge

Examples

The price they charge for bottled water at the stadium is highway robbery.
She said that paying $50 for parking downtown was highway robbery.
It's highway robbery to pay so much for such a simple meal.

Synset Examples

what they are asking for gas these days is highway robbery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rip-off - something that is unreasonably expensive or a swindle

Etymology

From the historical practice of robbing travelers on highways, metaphorically extended to situations of monetary exploitation.
