take it or leave it

1. X

/ ˈteɪk ɪt ər ˈliv ɪt / (AmE), / ˈteɪk ɪt ɔː ˈliːv ɪt / (BrE)
used to say that an offer is final and cannot be changed, so you can either accept it as it is or refuse it
final, non-negotiable offer

Examples

The price for the car is $5,000—take it or leave it.
This is my final offer, so take it or leave it.
She told him it was a take it or leave it situation and refused to negotiate.

Synset Examples

The manager's final offer was take it or leave it.
This is the best price I can offer, take it or leave it.
I’m not negotiating further; it’s take it or leave it.
