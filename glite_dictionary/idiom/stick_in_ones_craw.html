to stick in one's craw

1. VERB

/ stɪk ɪn wʌnz krɔ / (AmE), / stɪk ɪn wʌnz krɔː / (BrE)
to be particularly annoying or hard to accept; to cause ongoing resentment or irritation
to cause annoyance or resentment

Examples

Her constant criticism really sticks in my craw and makes it hard to work with her.
It sticks in my craw that he took credit for something I did.
The unfair decision still sticks in my craw years later.

Synset Examples

That comment really sticks in my craw; it was uncalled for.
His arrogance really sticks in my craw.

Etymology

From the noun 'craw', meaning the crop or gullet of an animal; the phrase refers metaphorically to something that cannot be swallowed easily.

2. VERB

/ stɪk ɪn wʌnz krɔ / (AmE), / stɪk ɪn wʌnz krɔː / (BrE)
(of something) to be difficult or impossible to accept, tolerate, or forget, usually because it feels especially unjust or aggravating.
be hard to accept or tolerate

Examples

The unfair decision really stuck in her craw all week and she couldn't stop thinking about it.
It sticks in my craw that he took credit for our work.
The judge's remarks stuck in their craws long after the trial ended.

Synset Examples

The unfair decision stuck in her craw all week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rankle - (of a bad feeling or memory) to continue to cause annoyance or resentment
  • Antonym: digest - to accept or come to terms with bad news or information

Etymology

Originally alludes to a bird's craw (throat or gullet), with the idea of something being difficult to swallow.
