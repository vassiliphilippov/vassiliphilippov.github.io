off one's box

1. ADJ

/ ɑf wʌnz bɑks / (AmE), / ɒf wʌnz bɒks / (BrE)
behaving in a confused, irrational, or abnormal way; not thinking clearly, often due to intoxication or emotional disturbance
acting irrationally or not thinking clearly
informal british

Examples

After a long night out, Tom was completely off his box and couldn't remember where he lived.
She started shouting at everyone and seemed totally off her box during the meeting.
You must be off your box if you think that's a good idea.

Synset Examples

Lately, he's been off his box and making silly mistakes.
He was acting off his box during the presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: off one's head - acting irrationally or not thinking straight
  • Synonym: out of one's mind - mentally deranged or irrational

2. ADJ

/ ɔf wʌnz bɑks / (AmE), / ɒf wʌnz bɒks / (BrE)
behaving in a way that is irrational, crazy, or out of control, often as a result of excitement, confusion, or intoxication
behaving irrationally or crazily
informal slang british

Examples

After staying up for two days straight, he was completely off his box.
You must be off your box if you think that's a good idea.
She acted as if she was off her box during the whole party.

Synset Examples

She must be off her box to suggest that idea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mad - mentally ill or behaving irrationally (British English)
  • Synonym: crazy - mentally deranged, especially as manifested in a wild or aggressive way
