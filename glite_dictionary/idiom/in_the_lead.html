in the lead

1. ADV

/ ɪn ðə lid / (AmE), / ɪn ðə liːd / (BrE)
ahead of others in a competition, race, or contest; in first place.
ahead in a competition or race

Examples

Despite a slow start, our team is now in the lead with only minutes remaining.
As the election results came in, it was clear she was in the lead.
By the halfway point of the marathon, he found himself in the lead for the first time.

Synset Examples

the horse was three lengths ahead going into the home stretch
ahead by two pawns
our candidate is in the lead in the polls
way out front in the race
the advertising campaign put them out front in sales

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ahead - further forward in position or progress
  • Synonym: out front - in a leading position
  • Antonym: behind - to the rear of others; in a worse position

2. ADP

/ ɪn ðə lid / (AmE), / ɪn ðə liːd / (BrE)
in an advantageous, first, or leading position in a competition, race, or comparison
ahead in a competition or race

Examples

At the halfway point, our team was in the lead by ten points.
She remained in the lead throughout the tournament and claimed the trophy.
Despite a strong start, he lost his position in the lead after making a mistake.

Synset Examples

The candidate is still in the lead according to the latest polls.
Our team is in the lead after winning the last game.
He has been in the lead for most of the race.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in front - ahead of others; leading
  • Antonym: behind - in a position following others; not leading
