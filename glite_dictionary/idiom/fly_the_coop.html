to fly the coop

1. VERB

/ flaɪ ðə kuːp / (AmE), / flaɪ ðə kuːp / (BrE)
to escape or leave suddenly, especially from a place where one is supposed to remain
leave or escape suddenly
informal american

Examples

Once the guards relaxed, the prisoners flew the coop within minutes.
When things got tough at work, Sarah decided to fly the coop and travel the world.
By the time the police arrived, the burglars had already flown the coop.

Synset Examples

If you see this man, run!
The burglars escaped before the police showed up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escape - to get away from a place or situation
  • Synonym: to break out - to escape from a place or situation, often after a struggle

Etymology

Idiom originating from American English in the 19th century, based on 'coop' (chicken coop) as a metaphor for confinement.
