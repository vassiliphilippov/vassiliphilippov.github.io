to close the stable door after the horse has bolted

1. VERB

/ kloʊz ðə ˈsteɪbl dɔr ˈæftɚ ðə hɔrs hæz ˈboʊltɪd / (AmE), / kləʊz ðə ˈsteɪbl dɔːr ˈɑːftə ðə hɔːs hæz ˈbəʊltɪd / (BrE)
to take action that is pointless because the problem has already happened and it is too late to prevent it
act too late to prevent a problem
british

Examples

Trying to improve security now is just closing the stable door after the horse has bolted.
Apologizing now feels like closing the stable door after the horse has bolted.
Making these changes is simply closing the stable door after the horse has bolted.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shut the barn door after the horse has bolted - American variant of the idiom

Etymology

Based on the literal image of securing a stable after a horse has already escaped, British in origin.
