to batten down the hatches

1. VERB

/ ˈbæt̬.ən daʊn ðə ˈhætʃ.ɪz / (AmE), / ˈbæt.ən daʊn ðə ˈhætʃ.ɪz / (BrE)
To prepare for trouble or difficult situations, especially by taking precautions or making necessary arrangements.
prepare for trouble or difficulty

Examples

With the economy worsening, many families decided to batten down the hatches and cut unnecessary expenses.
As the hurricane approached, the townspeople rushed to batten down the hatches and secure their homes.
Before the big product launch, the team worked overtime to batten down the hatches and check every detail.

Synset Examples

She advised him to batten down the hatches during the crisis.
We need to batten down the hatches before the storm hits.
It's time to batten down the hatches and prepare for winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brace yourself - Prepare yourself for something difficult or unpleasant
  • Synonym: to prepare - Make ready for something to happen

Etymology

Originally from nautical language, referring to securing a ship's hatches in anticipation of rough weather.
