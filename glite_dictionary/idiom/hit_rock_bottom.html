to hit rock bottom

1. VERB

/ hɪt rɑk ˈbɑtəm / (AmE), / hɪt rɒk ˈbɒtəm / (BrE)
to reach the lowest point in a situation, especially in one's life or fortunes, often implying a crisis or turning point
reach the lowest point

Examples

After his business failed and his savings ran out, he hit rock bottom and turned to his family for support.
She realized she had hit rock bottom when she lost her home and had nowhere else to go.
Sometimes people need to hit rock bottom before they can truly begin to rebuild their lives.

Synset Examples

They hit rock bottom before seeking help.
After losing his job, he hit rock bottom.
After losing his job, he finally hit rock bottom.
She realized she had hit rock bottom after the breakup.
Her mood hit rock-bottom after the bad news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hit the skids - enter a period of decline or failure
  • Antonym: to bounce back - recover from a difficult situation
