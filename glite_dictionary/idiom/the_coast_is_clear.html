the coast is clear

1. X

/ ðə koʊst ɪz klɪr / (AmE), / ðə kəʊst ɪz klɪə / (BrE)
used to indicate that it is safe to proceed or take action because there is no danger of being caught or observed
safe to proceed; no danger present

Examples

He peeked around the corner and whispered that the coast is clear.
We can leave now—the coast is clear and no one is watching.
Wait until the coast is clear before you open the door.

Synset Examples

He waited until the coast is clear to make his move.
Once the coast is clear, we can head out.
She whispered that the coast is clear, and they dashed outside.

Etymology

First recorded in 16th century English; origin likely nautical, referring to the absence of enemy ships along the coast.
