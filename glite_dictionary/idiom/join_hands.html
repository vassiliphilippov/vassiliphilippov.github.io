to join hands

1. VERB

/ dʒɔɪn hændz / (AmE), / dʒɔɪn hændz / (BrE)
to work together with others or cooperate towards a common goal
work together for a common purpose

Examples

The two companies decided to join hands and launch a new product.
People from all backgrounds must join hands to address climate change.
Volunteers from different organizations will join hands to clean up the park this weekend.

Synset Examples

The community came together to join hands for the fundraiser.
It's time for us to join hands and make a change.
Let's join hands and support this noble cause.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cooperate - to work jointly towards the same end
  • Synonym: to collaborate - to work together with someone to achieve a goal
