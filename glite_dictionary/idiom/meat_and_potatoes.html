meat and potatoes

1. NOUN

/ ˈmit ənd pəˈteɪtoʊz / (AmE), / ˈmiːt ənd pəˈteɪtəʊz / (BrE)
the most important or fundamental part of something; the essentials
the essentials; the main part

Examples

Learning to write clear emails is the meat and potatoes of professional communication.
The meat and potatoes of the course focus on practical problem-solving skills.
During training, we skipped the theory and went straight to the meat and potatoes of the job.

Synset Examples

successful negotiation is the meat and potatoes of arbitration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heart - the central or most important part
  • Synonym: core - the essential or most important part

Etymology

Originally referring to the basic staple foods of a meal, especially in North American cuisine, later used figuratively for essentials.
