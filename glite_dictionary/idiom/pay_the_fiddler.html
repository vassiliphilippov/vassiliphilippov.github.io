to pay the fiddler

1. VERB

/ peɪ ðə ˈfɪdlɚ / (AmE), / peɪ ðə ˈfɪdlə / (BrE)
to accept or face the consequences for one's actions, especially after having enjoyed certain benefits or pleasures
face the consequences for one's actions

Examples

After years of reckless spending, he finally had to pay the fiddler and declare bankruptcy.
She knew she would eventually have to pay the fiddler for skipping so many classes.
They enjoyed the party, but now it's time to pay the fiddler and clean up the mess.

Synset Examples

If you ignore the warnings, you will pay the fiddler later.
You can’t run away; you’ll have to pay the fiddler eventually.
Every decision has consequences; sooner or later, you’ll pay the fiddler.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to face the music - to confront the consequences of one's actions

Etymology

From the proverb 'he who pays the piper calls the tune,' referring to covering the cost of entertainment, which later evolved to mean facing consequences.
