the blind leading the blind

1. NOUN

/ ðə blaɪnd ˈliːdɪŋ ðə blaɪnd / (AmE), / ðə blaɪnd ˈliːdɪŋ ðə blaɪnd / (BrE)
used to describe a situation in which people without knowledge or experience are guiding others who are equally uninformed or unskilled.
uninformed people leading others equally uninformed

Examples

With no experienced manager on the team, it felt like the blind leading the blind.
Trying to fix the software without training was just the blind leading the blind.
If you don't know the answer either, this is the blind leading the blind.

Synset Examples

It's just the blind leading the blind in that group.
Don't follow him; it's the blind leading the blind.
The team was like the blind leading the blind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incompetent - lacking the necessary ability or knowledge

Etymology

From a biblical parable (Matthew 15:14): "If the blind lead the blind, both shall fall into the ditch."
