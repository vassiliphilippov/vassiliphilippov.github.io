stuck in the Stone Age

1. ADJ

/ stʌk ɪn ðə stoʊn eɪdʒ / (AmE), / stʌk ɪn ðə stəʊn eɪdʒ / (BrE)
very old-fashioned or behind current trends or technology; outdated.
very outdated; behind the times

Examples

My parents are so stuck in the Stone Age when it comes to using new technology.
If our website doesn't improve, customers will think we're stuck in the Stone Age.
The school's curriculum was criticized for being stuck in the Stone Age compared to others.

Synset Examples

Her refusal to use smartphones makes her feel stuck in the Stone Age.
His outdated tech ideas make him seem stuck in the Stone Age.
Some argue the company is stuck in the Stone Age with its policies.
Her fashion sense seemed stuck in the Stone Age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outdated - no longer fashionable or useful
  • Synonym: old-fashioned - not modern; belonging to or typical of a previous time
  • Antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times as opposed to the remote past
