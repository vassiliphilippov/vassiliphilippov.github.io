to make sport of

1. VERB

/ meɪk spɔrt əv / (AmE), / meɪk spɔːt ɒv / (BrE)
to mock or tease someone, often in a playful or unkind way
mock or tease someone

Examples

They laughed and made sport of the new student's accent.
It's unkind to make sport of someone's appearance.
She often makes sport of her little brother during family gatherings.

Synset Examples

They love to make sport of his clumsiness.
It's not nice to make sport of people's mistakes.
She often makes sport of her brother's fashion choices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mock - to tease or laugh at in a scornful way
  • Synonym: ridicule - to make fun of; to mock or scorn
