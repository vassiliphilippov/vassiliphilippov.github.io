cock-and-bull story

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑk ənd ˈbʊl ˌstɔri / (AmE), / ˌkɒk ənd ˈbʊl ˌstɔːri / (BrE)
an absurd or implausible story, especially one told to deceive or excuse oneself
absurd, implausible story

Examples

He gave me some cock-and-bull story about missing the bus and losing his wallet.
No one believed her cock-and-bull story about why she was late to work.
That sounds like a cock-and-bull story if you ask me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tall tale - an exaggerated story that is difficult to believe
  • Antonym: truth - true facts or reality, as opposed to imagination or lies

Etymology

Possibly from fanciful tales allegedly exchanged at English inns named Cock and Bull, 17th–18th centuries.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑk ən ˈbʊl ˌstɔri / (AmE), / ˌkɒk ən ˈbʊl ˌstɔːri / (BrE)
a story or explanation that is silly, unlikely, and hard to believe; an absurd excuse or tale.
an implausible, absurd story or excuse

Examples

He told us a cock-and-bull story about being abducted by aliens, but no one believed him.
Whenever he's late, he always comes up with another cock-and-bull story to explain himself.
The official explanation sounded like a cock-and-bull story and raised more questions than it answered.

Synset Examples

She spun a cock-and-bull story about how she lost her keys.
Don't believe his cock-and-bull story about meeting celebrities.
His explanation was just a cock-and-bull story.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tall tale - an exaggerated, untrue story
  • Antonym: truth - the actual facts or reality
  • Synonym: lie - a false statement made with intent to deceive

Etymology

Originating in the early 19th century, possibly from fanciful stories told at English coaching inns named the Cock and the Bull.
