full speed ahead

1. ADV

/ fʊl spiːd əˈhɛd / (AmE), / fʊl spiːd əˈhɛd / (BrE)
used to indicate proceeding with maximum effort, speed, or determination, especially after a decision has been made or an obstacle removed
at maximum effort or speed; without hesitation

Examples

Once the funds were secured, the company went full speed ahead with the development of the new app.
Despite the challenges, the team moved full speed ahead to meet the deadline.
We're going full speed ahead on this renovation project now that we have the permits.

Synset Examples

After the meeting, we decided to proceed full speed ahead.
With the plan approved, we’re going full speed ahead.
Let’s go full speed ahead with the project launch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: all out - using all available resources and effort
  • Antonym: to hold back - to hesitate or not proceed with full effort

Etymology

Originally a nautical term used to instruct engineers to set ship engines to their highest speed.
