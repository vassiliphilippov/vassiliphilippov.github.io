Fanny Adams

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæni ˈædəmz / (AmE), / ˈfæni ˈædəmz / (BrE)
A British nautical slang term for tinned meat, originally used humorously or cynically to refer to food of dubious quality.
British slang for tinned meat
slang british

Examples

The sailors complained about the Fanny Adams served at every meal during the voyage.
After weeks at sea, we grew tired of eating nothing but Fanny Adams from cans.
Stories about the origins of the term Fanny Adams circulated around the ship's mess hall.

Etymology

From the name Fanny Adams, after a murder victim in 1867; sailors jokingly used her name for tinned meat.
