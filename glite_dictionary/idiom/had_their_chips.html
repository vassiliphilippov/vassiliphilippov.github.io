to had their chips

1. VERB

/ hæd ðɛr tʃɪps / (AmE), / had ðə tʃɪps / (BrE)
(British, informal) To be in a hopeless situation, to have run out of options or chances.
be in a hopeless situation (UK, informal)
informal british

Examples

I'm afraid the team had their chips when their best player was sent off.
By the time they realized the danger, they had their chips.
If the money doesn't come through soon, the project will have had their chips.

Synset Examples

Without a backup plan, they've had their chips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be done for - to be in serious trouble or ruined
