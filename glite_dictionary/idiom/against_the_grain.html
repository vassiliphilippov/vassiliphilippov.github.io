against the grain

1. ADV

/ əˈɡɛnst ðə ɡreɪn / (AmE), / əˈɡenst ðə ɡreɪn / (BrE)
in a way that is different from what is normal, usual, or expected, especially by challenging established customs or values
contrary to what is usual or expected

Examples

Speaking out in that meeting really went against the grain for him.
Her unconventional outfit was against the grain at such a formal event.
Sometimes doing what you believe is right means going against the grain.

Synset Examples

He always does things against the grain, challenging norms.
She felt it would go against the grain of her values.
Her decision to quit was against the grain of expectations.
Choosing to speak up can sometimes go against the grain.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to conform - to behave in a way that is expected or accepted by most people

Etymology

Originally referring to cutting wood contrary to its natural direction. Figurative meaning from the 17th century.
