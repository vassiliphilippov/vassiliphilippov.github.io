in the same boat

1. X

/ ɪn ðə seɪm boʊt / (AmE), / ɪn ðə seɪm bəʊt / (BrE)
experiencing the same difficulties or being in the same unfortunate situation as others
facing the same difficulties as others

Examples

After being laid off, I realized I was in the same boat as many of my colleagues.
Don't worry; we're all in the same boat with these new regulations.
During the storm, the travelers knew they were in the same boat and needed to cooperate.

Synset Examples

During the pandemic, everyone felt like they were in the same boat.
During the crisis, it was clear we were row in the same boat.
They realized they were in the same boat regarding their career struggles.
The team feels like they are row in the same boat now.
We're all row in the same boat with this deadline.
We're all in the same boat when it comes to budgeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in the same situation - in a similar situation as someone else
  • Synonym: on the same page - in agreement or having a shared understanding
  • Antonym: in the clear - out of difficulty or danger

Etymology

First recorded in the late 16th century; derived from the idea of sharing a boat and thus sharing fate or circumstances.
