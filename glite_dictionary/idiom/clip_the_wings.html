to clip the wings

1. VERB

/ klɪp ðə wɪŋz / (AmE), / klɪp ðə wɪŋz / (BrE)
to restrict or limit someone's freedom, power, or potential
restrict someone's freedom or potential

Examples

The new regulations are likely to clip the wings of innovative startups.
Her parents tried to clip the wings of her ambitions by discouraging her from moving abroad.
The board decided to clip the wings of the CEO by limiting his spending authority.

Synset Examples

The changes will clip the wings of ambitious employees.
We aim to clip the wings of any potential fraud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hold back - to stop someone or something from making progress
  • Antonym: to set free - to allow someone to be or act freely; to release restrictions

Etymology

From the literal act of clipping a bird's wings to prevent it from flying, used figuratively since at least the 16th century.
