to swallow pride

1. VERB

/ ˈswɑloʊ ˈpraɪd / (AmE), / ˈswɒləʊ ˈpraɪd / (BrE)
to accept humiliation or admit defeat by putting aside one's dignity or ego, usually to achieve a greater good or reconcile with someone
put aside one's dignity or ego

Examples

She had to swallow her pride and ask for help after her plans failed.
He finally decided to swallow his pride and accept the job offer.
They urged him to swallow his pride and make amends with his old friend.

Synset Examples

I’ll swallow my pride if it means saving our friendship.
He decided to swallow his pride and apologize first.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humble oneself - to act in a way that shows you are not proud; to admit you are not perfect
