power behind the throne

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊɚ bɪˈhaɪnd ðə θroʊn / (AmE), / ˈpaʊə bɪˈhaɪnd ðə θrəʊn / (BrE)
a person who exerts significant control or influence over a leader or authority figure while remaining in the background or unrecognized by the public
person secretly holding real power

Examples

Many suspect that the prime minister's advisor is the power behind the throne in the government.
Though the CEO was the public face, her brother was the true power behind the throne.
History is full of examples of a power behind the throne shaping major decisions.

Synset Examples

Many believe he is the power behind the throne.
The advisor acted as the power behind the throne.
She was the power behind the throne, guiding the king.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: figurehead - a person with official leadership title but without real power

Etymology

Alludes to a person who influences the throne (the ruler) from behind the scenes; first used in English in the 19th century.
