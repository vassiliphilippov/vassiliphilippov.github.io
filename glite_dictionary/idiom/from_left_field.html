from left field

1. ADV

/ frəm lɛft fiːld / (AmE), / frəm lɛft fiːld / (BrE)
used to describe something that is unexpected or surprising
unexpected or surprising
american

Examples

His suggestion came from left field and took everyone by surprise.
The criticism about her work was totally from left field.
That question was so from left field that I didn't know how to answer.

Synset Examples

The question he asked was completely from left field.
Her idea for the project came out of left field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: out of the blue - completely unexpected or without warning

Etymology

Originally from American baseball, where balls hit from left field could be unexpected.
