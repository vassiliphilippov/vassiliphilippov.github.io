to pull a Homer

1. VERB

/ pʊl ə ˈhoʊmər / (AmE), / pʊl ə ˈhəʊmə / (BrE)
To make a significant or embarrassing mistake, often in a way that is humorous or unexpected.
make a significant mistake
informal

Examples

I can't believe you pulled a Homer during that important presentation.
After forgetting his passport at home, he realized he'd pulled a Homer.
She pulled a Homer by sending the email to the wrong person.

Synset Examples

I can't believe you pulled a Homer on that exam.
She pulled a Homer when she forgot her lines.
He really pulled a Homer by missing that easy shot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunder - a careless or stupid mistake
  • Synonym: goof up - to make a foolish mistake
  • Antonym: nail it - to do something perfectly or exactly right

Etymology

Popularized by the television show The Simpsons, referencing the character Homer Simpson, who is known for amusing blunders.
