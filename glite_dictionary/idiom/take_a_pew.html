to take a pew

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə pjuː / (AmE), / teɪk ə pjuː / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly British) To sit down, originally referring to sitting in a church seat but also used more generally.
to sit down (informal, chiefly British)
informal british

Examples

You look tired—come in and take a pew for a few minutes.
She gestured to the visitor, saying, 'Please, take a pew.'
After standing for so long, he was relieved to finally take a pew near the back.

Synset Examples

He sat down to take a pew and listen to the sermon.
Feel free to take a pew and relax.
The church was nearly empty, so I could take a pew anywhere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sit down - to take a seat; to sit

Etymology

From the noun 'pew' (church bench), with 'take' meaning 'occupy'; by extension, used more generally to mean 'sit down'.
