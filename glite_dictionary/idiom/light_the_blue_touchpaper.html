to light the blue touchpaper

1. VERB

/ laɪt ðə blu tʌtʃˌpeɪpər / (AmE), / laɪt ðə blu ˈtʌtʃpeɪpə / (BrE)
to cause an event or situation to begin, usually one that is expected to develop rapidly or become exciting or controversial
begin an explosive or exciting process
british

Examples

It's time to light the blue touchpaper and announce the new policy to the team.
Whenever she voices her opinions in that meeting, she lights the blue touchpaper for lively debate.
The CEO's daring proposal lit the blue touchpaper for innovation across the industry.

Synset Examples

Let's light the blue touchpaper and see what happens next.
He knew it was time to light the blue touchpaper and start the project.
They decided to light the blue touchpaper on their new initiative.

Etymology

Alludes to blue touchpaper, a type of fuse used to ignite fireworks, from historical British fireworks terminology.
