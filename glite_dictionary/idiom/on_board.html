on board

1. ADJ

/ ɑn bɔrd / (AmE), / ɒn bɔːd / (BrE)
in agreement with or supportive of a plan, idea, or project
in agreement or supportive of something

Examples

After the presentation, everyone was on board with the new marketing strategy.
We need the finance department on board before we launch the project.
It's important that all team members are on board for this change to succeed.

Synset Examples

They need more members on board to finalize the plan.
When he asked if I was on board, I said, 'Yessir!'
Are you on board with the new project proposal?
Every oiler on board knows the importance of safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in favor - supportive of or agreeing with something
  • Antonym: against - opposed to or not supporting something
