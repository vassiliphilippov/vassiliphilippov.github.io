the way of all flesh

1. NOUN

/ ðə weɪ əv ɔl flɛʃ / (AmE), / ðə weɪ əv ɔːl fleʃ / (BrE)
the inevitable fate of death that comes to all living beings; mortality
the inevitable fate of death; mortality

Examples

In the end, even the mightiest monarch must go the way of all flesh.
The wise accept the way of all flesh as a part of life.
Stories throughout history remind us of the way of all flesh and the importance of living fully.

Synset Examples

In the end, we must all go the way of all flesh.
The story reminds us that we all go the way of all flesh.
He accepted the way of all flesh and moved on.
She reflected on the way of all flesh during her meditation.
The novel explores the way of all flesh and mortality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: death - the end of life; the state of being dead
  • Synonym: mortality - the state of being subject to death

Etymology

Biblical allusion, from the phrase 'go the way of all the earth' in the Old Testament (Joshua 23:14), adapted into English idiom.
