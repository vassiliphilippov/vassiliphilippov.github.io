to stand on the shoulders of

1. VERB

/ stænd ɑn ðə ˈʃoʊldɚz əv / (AmE), / stænd ɒn ðə ˈʃəʊldəz əv / (BrE)
to make progress or achieve success by using the discoveries, understanding, or work of those who came before you
build upon earlier achievements

Examples

Modern scientists stand on the shoulders of generations of earlier thinkers and inventors.
Every innovation in this company stands on the shoulders of many previous breakthroughs.
She acknowledged that her success stood on the shoulders of her mentors and predecessors.

Synset Examples

We stand on the shoulders of previous researchers in this field.
This technology stands on the shoulders of decades of innovation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to build on - to use something as a foundation for further development
  • Antonym: pioneer - to be among the first to develop or use a new method or activity

Etymology

From the metaphor made famous by Isaac Newton's phrase, 'If I have seen further it is by standing on the shoulders of giants.'
