worth a Continental

1. ADJ

/ wɝθ ə ˌkɑn.tɪˈnɛn.tl̩ / (AmE), / wɜːθ ə ˌkɒn.tɪˈnɛn.tl̩ / (BrE)
Of little or no value; worthless.
of little or no value (idiom)
archaic american

Examples

After the scandal, his promises weren't worth a Continental to anyone in the company.
It's an old superstition that's not worth a Continental nowadays.
All those outdated shares are no longer worth a Continental.

Synset Examples

That old book is not worth a Continental now.
The effort was not worth a Continental in the end.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worthless - having no value, use, or importance

Etymology

Refers to the Continental currency issued during the American Revolution, which became nearly worthless by the war’s end.
