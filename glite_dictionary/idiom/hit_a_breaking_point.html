to hit a breaking point

1. VERB

/ hɪt ə ˈbreɪkɪŋ pɔɪnt / (AmE), / hɪt ə ˈbreɪkɪŋ pɔɪnt / (BrE)
to reach a stage where a situation or a person's endurance can no longer cope, leading to a breakdown or change.
reach a critical limit or tolerance

Examples

After working sixteen-hour days, Sarah finally hit a breaking point and took a much-needed vacation.
When the constant arguments wouldn't stop, they hit a breaking point and decided to separate.
The team's stress levels kept rising until someone eventually hit a breaking point during the meeting.

Synset Examples

They finally hit a breaking point after months of conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reach a breaking point - arrive at a critical or unbearable limit
  • Antonym: to hold it together - maintain composure under pressure
