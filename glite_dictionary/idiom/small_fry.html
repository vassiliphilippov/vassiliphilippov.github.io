small fry

1. NOUN

/ smɔl fraɪ / (AmE), / smɔːl fraɪ / (BrE)
a person or group regarded as unimportant or insignificant, especially compared to others
person or group of little importance

Examples

Compared to the company’s main competitors, we’re just small fry in the industry.
The police were not interested in the small fry; they wanted to catch the ringleader.
For years he was small fry in politics, but now he holds a powerful office.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: big shot - an important or influential person
  • Synonym: nobody - a person of no importance or influence

Etymology

Originally referring to small, young fish considered of little value; figurative use dates to the 19th century.
