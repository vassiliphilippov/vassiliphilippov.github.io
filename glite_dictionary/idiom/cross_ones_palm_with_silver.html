to cross one's palm with silver

1. VERB

/ krɔs wʌnz pɑm wɪð ˈsɪlvɚ / (AmE), / krɒs wʌnz pɑːm wɪð ˈsɪlvə / (BrE)
to give someone money for a service, particularly to obtain a favor or ensure cooperation, sometimes suggesting bribery
to pay someone for a service (often secretly or as a bribe)

Examples

The fortune teller refused to speak until I crossed her palm with silver.
If you want the official to speed up the paperwork, you may need to cross his palm with silver.
Some believe that to guarantee good luck, one should cross their palm with silver before an important venture.

Synset Examples

To ensure a safe trip, she crossed his palm with silver.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bribe - money or gift given to persuade someone to act dishonestly
  • Synonym: reward - a thing given in recognition of service, effort, or achievement

Etymology

Refers to an old practice of paying soothsayers or performers by placing silver coins in their hands.
