on the wagon

1. ADJ

/ ɑn ðə ˈwæɡən / (AmE), / ɒn ðə ˈwæɡən / (BrE)
not drinking alcoholic beverages, typically after a period of excessive drinking or as part of a decision to abstain
abstaining from alcohol

Examples

After his doctor's warning, Mark decided to stay on the wagon for good.
She's been on the wagon since her baby was born last year.
Whenever he's on the wagon, he feels more energetic and alert.

Synset Examples

He's been on the wagon since last summer.
After years of drinking, he's finally on the wagon.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: off the wagon - resumed drinking alcohol after a period of abstinence

Etymology

Originates from late 19th-century American English; refers to riding on a water wagon instead of drinking alcohol.
