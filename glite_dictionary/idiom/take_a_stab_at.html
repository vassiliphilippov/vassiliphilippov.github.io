to take a stab at

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə stæb æt / (AmE), / teɪk ə stæb ət / (BrE)
to attempt to do something, especially when uncertain of success
attempt something uncertainly
informal

Examples

He took a stab at cooking dinner for the first time.
I'll take a stab at answering that difficult question.
She took a stab at writing poetry in her spare time.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
