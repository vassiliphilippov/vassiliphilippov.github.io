to go to the mattresses

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ tə ðə ˈmætrəsɪz / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə ðə ˈmætɹəsɪz / (BrE)
to prepare for a serious conflict or confrontation, often by taking defensive or aggressive measures
prepare for serious conflict

Examples

When the company threatened layoffs, the union decided to go to the mattresses in negotiations.
As rumors of hostile takeover emerged, the board prepared to go to the mattresses.
If another team tries to steal our clients, we'll have no choice but to go to the mattresses.

Synset Examples

When the competition heated up, we had to go to the mattresses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brace for battle - to prepare for a fight or challenge
  • Antonym: to make peace - to resolve conflict or reach an agreement

Etymology

Popularized by the film 'The Godfather' (1972), originating from Italian-American slang referring to mobsters sleeping on mattresses during gang wars.
