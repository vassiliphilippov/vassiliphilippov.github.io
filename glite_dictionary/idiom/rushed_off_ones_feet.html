rushed off one's feet

1. ADJ

/ rʌʃt ɔf wʌnz fit / (AmE), / rʌʃt ɒf wʌnz fiːt / (BrE)
extremely busy or overwhelmed with too many tasks
extremely busy; overwhelmed

Examples

I was rushed off my feet all afternoon trying to meet the deadline.
The staff were rushed off their feet during the lunch rush at the café.
She felt rushed off her feet balancing work and family responsibilities.

Synset Examples

She felt rushed off her feet with all the tasks.
I was rushed off my feet at work today.
The holiday season had everyone rushed off their feet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: busy - having a lot to do; occupied
  • Antonym: idle - not busy; unoccupied
