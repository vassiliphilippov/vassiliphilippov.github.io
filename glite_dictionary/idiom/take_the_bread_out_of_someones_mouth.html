to take the bread out of someone's mouth

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə brɛd aʊt ʌv ˈsʌmwʌnz maʊθ / (AmE), / teɪk ðə brɛd aʊt ɒv ˈsʌmwʌnz maʊθ / (BrE)
to deprive someone of their means of earning a living or their livelihood
deprive of livelihood

Examples

Many local businesses fear that the new supermarket will take the bread out of their mouths.
By stealing her idea, he effectively took the bread out of her mouth.
I would never want to take the bread out of someone's mouth by taking away their job.

Synset Examples

His actions could take the bread out of my mouth.
Don't take the bread out of someone's mouth by undercutting them.
Competing unfairly can take the bread out of someone's mouth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deprive - to take something necessary away from someone
