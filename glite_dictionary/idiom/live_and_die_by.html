to live and die by

1. VERB

/ lɪv ənd daɪ baɪ / (AmE), / lɪv ənd daɪ baɪ / (BrE)
to adhere strictly to or depend heavily on particular beliefs, rules, or principles
adhere strictly to rules or beliefs

Examples

Many athletes live and die by their strict training routines and dietary rules.
She has always lived and died by her personal code of honesty, even when it was difficult.
Startups often live and die by their ability to innovate quickly.

Synset Examples

They live and die by their team's performance.
He will live and die by his principles.
She lives and dies by the rules of her job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stand by - to support or remain loyal to someone or something
  • Antonym: to betray - to be disloyal or act against one's principles
