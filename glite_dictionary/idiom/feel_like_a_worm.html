to feel like a worm

1. VERB

/ fil laɪk ə wɝm / (AmE), / fiːl laɪk ə wɜːm / (BrE)
to feel deeply humiliated or ashamed, as if lacking self-worth
to feel humiliated or ashamed

Examples

After forgetting his best friend's birthday, he felt like a worm all day.
She felt like a worm when she realized her mistake had affected the whole team.
He admitted in front of everyone that he had lied, and immediately felt like a worm.

Synset Examples

He felt like a worm after losing the argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feel ashamed - to experience a sense of shame or embarrassment
  • Antonym: feel proud - to feel a sense of self-respect or satisfaction
