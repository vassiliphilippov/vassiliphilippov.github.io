to stand pat

1. VERB

/ stænd pæt / (AmE), / stand pæt / (BrE)
to refuse to change one's decision or position; to remain firm in one's stance
refuse to change decision or position

Examples

Despite criticism from colleagues, she chose to stand pat on her decision.
Some investors prefer to stand pat during market uncertainty rather than risk change.
Even after the negotiations failed, the company continued to stand pat on its offer.

Synset Examples

In a volatile market, many investors choose to stand pat.
He decided to stand pat rather than make any changes.
They plan to stand pat with their current strategy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hold firm - to remain resolute or steadfast
  • Antonym: to back down - to withdraw from a position or change one's mind

Etymology

Originally from the language of poker, where 'stand pat' meant to play one's hand without drawing new cards.
