baptism of fire

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæp.tɪ.zəm əv ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈbæp.tɪ.zəm əv ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
an initial experience of something, typically difficult or challenging, that tests someone's abilities or courage
difficult or challenging initiation

Examples

Her first day as a manager was a true baptism of fire.
The new recruit had a baptism of fire during the busy holiday season.
Moving abroad alone was his baptism of fire into independent living.

Synset Examples

Her first day as a manager was a baptism of fire.
The new employee faced a baptism of fire during the product launch.
Joining the army was his baptism of fire into adulthood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trial by fire - a test of one's abilities under pressure

Etymology

From the Christian religious rite 'baptism', combined with 'fire' symbolizing severe trial; originally referred to a soldier's first experience of battle.
