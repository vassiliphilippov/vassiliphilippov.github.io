to air out the situation

1. VERB

/ ɛr aʊt ðə səˈtʃuː.eɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / eə aʊt ðə sɪˈtjuː.eɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
to discuss an issue or clarify the circumstances, especially to resolve misunderstandings or make a decision
discuss or clarify the circumstances
informal

Examples

Before we move on, let's air out the situation so everyone is on the same page.
We need to air out the situation to avoid any further confusion among the team members.
It would be best to air out the situation instead of letting resentment build up.

Synset Examples

Let’s air out the situation before making a decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to talk over - to discuss something thoroughly
  • Synonym: to clear the air - to resolve misunderstanding or tension

Etymology

Derived from the idiom 'air out' meaning 'to bring into the open for discussion'.
