to feel blue

1. VERB

/ fil bluː / (AmE), / fiːl bluː / (BrE)
to feel sad or depressed; to experience a period of unhappiness
to feel sad or depressed

Examples

After hearing the bad news, she couldn't help but feel blue all afternoon.
Whenever I feel blue, listening to music usually cheers me up.
It's normal to feel blue from time to time, especially during the winter months.

Synset Examples

Her new workout routine has her feeling blue fit.
Feeling blue, she decided to go for a walk.
He was blue after losing the championship game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: down in the dumps - feeling depressed or unhappy
  • Antonym: cheerful - feeling happy and positive

Etymology

The use of 'blue' to mean 'sad' dates from the 18th century, possibly related to associations with blue colors and melancholy.
