to look the other way

1. VERB

/ lʊk ði ˈʌðɚ weɪ / (AmE), / lʊk ði ˈʌðə weɪ / (BrE)
to deliberately ignore something wrong or unpleasant, especially to avoid dealing with it
deliberately ignore wrongdoing or unpleasantness

Examples

Government officials sometimes look the other way when minor laws are broken.
We shouldn't look the other way if we see someone being treated unfairly.
The manager chose to look the other way instead of reporting the safety violation.

Synset Examples

Don't look the other way; confront the issue head-on.
Sometimes, we must look the other way to maintain peace.
He chose to look the other way when the argument started.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn a blind eye - to ignore something that you know is wrong
  • Antonym: to confront - to face up to and deal with a problem or difficult situation
