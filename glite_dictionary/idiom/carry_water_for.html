to carry water for

1. VERB

/ ˈkæri ˈwɔtər fər / (AmE), / ˈkæri ˈwɔːtə fə / (BrE)
to support or defend someone, especially in an organization or political context, often without receiving any benefit in return
support someone without benefit

Examples

Many journalists were accused of carrying water for the administration during the scandal.
She refused to carry water for her boss when she knew he was wrong.
He has spent years carrying water for the company instead of pursuing his own interests.

Synset Examples

Don't carry water for anyone who doesn't appreciate it.
He refused to carry water for the corrupt politicians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: back - to support or approve of someone or something
  • Antonym: to oppose - to act against or resist someone or something

Etymology

Originally from the literal act of carrying water for others, later used figuratively in political slang.
