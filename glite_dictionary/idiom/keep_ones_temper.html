to keep one's temper

1. VERB

/ kip wʌnz ˈtɛmpɚ / (AmE), / kiːp wʌnz ˈtɛmpə / (BrE)
to remain calm and not become angry, especially in a difficult or provoking situation
remain calm and not get angry

Examples

Despite the long delay, he did his best to keep his temper in check.
It's not always easy to keep your temper when dealing with difficult customers.
She tried hard to keep her temper during the heated meeting.

Synset Examples

He managed to keep his temper while waiting in line.
She struggled to keep her temper during the argument.
It's hard to keep your temper with rude customers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to lose one's temper - to become angry suddenly and easily
