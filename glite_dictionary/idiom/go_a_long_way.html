to go a long way

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ə lɔŋ weɪ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ə lɒŋ weɪ / (BrE)
to suffice or be adequate for a period of time or to a certain extent; to have a strong or lasting impact
be sufficient or effective for some time

Examples

A little encouragement can go a long way when someone is feeling down.
Just a small donation will go a long way toward helping those in need.
In this recipe, a pinch of salt really goes a long way.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suffice - to be enough or adequate
  • Antonym: to fall short - to fail to reach the required amount or standard

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ə lɔːŋ weɪ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ə lɒŋ weɪ / (BrE)
to have a significant positive effect or make a substantial contribution towards achieving something
make a significant contribution

Examples

A little patience can go a long way when dealing with customer complaints.
Regular compliments and encouragement go a long way toward boosting team morale.
In negotiations, showing respect often goes a long way to reach a mutual agreement.

Synset Examples

Her kindness will go a long way in building trust.
A smile can go a long way in tough times.
Effort and dedication can go a long way.
A little of something goes a long way in friendships.
Remember, a little of something goes a long way in cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: help - to make it easier or possible for something to happen
  • Synonym: to make a difference - to have a significant effect
