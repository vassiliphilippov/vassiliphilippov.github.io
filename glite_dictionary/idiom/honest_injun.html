honest injun

1. X

/ ˈɑnəst ˈɪndʒən / (AmE), / ˈɒnɪst ˈɪndʒən / (BrE)
(offensive, informal) Used to emphasize that one is speaking truthfully and sincerely.
used to stress sincerity (offensive)
offensive informal

Examples

Honest injun, I didn’t know anything about the surprise party.
I'm telling you the truth—honest injun, that's how it happened.
She said, honest injun, she would return the money by Friday.

Synset Examples

I swear, honest injun, I didn’t take your book.
He said, honest injun, that was the last cookie.
When she promised to help, she meant it, honest injun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honestly - used to assert that one is speaking the truth
  • Synonym: I swear - phrase to emphasize truthfulness

Etymology

Derived from a stereotypical representation of Native Americans; "Injun" is a dated and offensive variant of "Indian".
