not invented here

1. X

/ nɑt ɪnˈvɛntɪd hɪr / (AmE), / nɒt ɪnˈvɛntɪd hɪə / (BrE)
an unwillingness to adopt ideas, products, or solutions developed outside one's own organization or group
bias against external ideas

Examples

Many companies suffer from not invented here and miss out on valuable innovations from outside.
The team's decision to build their own software was driven by not invented here.
Her suggestions were ignored because they fell victim to the department's not invented here mentality.

Synset Examples

He ignored the outside solution, typical of not invented here.
Their policy reflects a classic case of not invented here.
They rejected my idea; it's just not invented here.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insular - uninterested in ideas, cultures, or people outside one's own experience
  • Antonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas; unprejudiced
