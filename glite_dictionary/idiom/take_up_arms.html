to take up arms

1. VERB

/ teɪk ʌp ɑrmz / (AmE), / teɪk ʌp ɑːmz / (BrE)
to begin fighting or to prepare to fight, especially with weapons, against an enemy or oppression.
to begin to fight or resist, often with weapons

Examples

When the invaders crossed the border, the villagers decided to take up arms to defend their homes.
Throughout history, people have taken up arms to fight for their freedom and rights.
If the government fails to listen, many may feel compelled to take up arms against injustice.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: surrender - to stop fighting and admit defeat
  • Synonym: to fight back - to defend oneself or retaliate with force
