to blaze a trail

1. VERB

/ bleɪz ə treɪl / (AmE), / bleɪz ə treɪl / (BrE)
to be the first to do something new or innovative, paving the way for others
pioneer a new path or method

Examples

Marie Curie helped to blaze a trail for women in science.
The designer hopes to blaze a trail with her innovative fashion line.
Their research team blazed a trail in renewable energy technologies.

Synset Examples

She hopes to blaze a trail in environmental activism.
They plan to blaze a trail in the tech industry.
He blazed a trail for others to follow in his career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pioneer - to be among the first to do something

Etymology

Originally from the practice of marking trees to create a visible path through forests.
