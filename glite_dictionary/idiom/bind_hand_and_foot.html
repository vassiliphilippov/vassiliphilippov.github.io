to bind hand and foot

1. VERB

/ baɪnd hænd ənd fʊt / (AmE), / baɪnd hænd ənd fʊt / (BrE)
to restrict someone completely so that they are unable to act freely
completely restrict or constrain

Examples

The new regulations will bind hand and foot those trying to start a small business.
She felt the strict traditions bound her hand and foot, preventing her from pursuing her dreams.
The contract's terms bind him hand and foot, leaving him with no flexibility.

Synset Examples

The new policy will bind employees hand and foot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restrain - to hold back from action; to keep under control
  • Antonym: free - to release from restriction or confinement
