to go through the wringer

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ θru ðə ˈrɪŋər / (AmE), / ɡəʊ θruː ðə ˈrɪŋə / (BrE)
to experience a very difficult, stressful, or testing situation
to endure a difficult ordeal

Examples

After working twelve hours straight, I felt like I had gone through the wringer.
The company went through the wringer during the economic downturn but managed to survive.
She goes through the wringer every time her boss reviews her work.

Synset Examples

The project went through the wringer before final approval.
She felt she was put through the wringer by all the questions.
The project put us through the wringer last month.
I was put through the wringer during the interview process.
I felt like I went through the wringer after that meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be put through the mill - to undergo a difficult or demanding experience
  • Synonym: have a rough time - to experience hardship or distress
  • Antonym: have an easy time - to experience something without difficulty or stress

Etymology

From the literal meaning of a 'wringer', a device used to squeeze water from clothes, metaphorically indicating being subjected to pressure or stress.
