to break the fourth wall

1. VERB

/ breɪk ðə fɔrθ wɑl / (AmE), / breɪk ðə fɔːθ wɔːl / (BrE)
(of an actor or character) to address the audience directly during a performance, thereby acknowledging the fictional nature of the work.
address the audience directly (theatre/film)

Examples

In the middle of the play, the main character suddenly breaks the fourth wall and greets the audience.
When the comedian broke the fourth wall, the crowd erupted with laughter.
Some movies cleverly break the fourth wall to draw viewers into the story.

Synset Examples

The actor decided to break the fourth wall and speak directly to the audience.
In the movie, the protagonist humorously breaks the fourth wall.
Comedians often break the fourth wall to engage the crowd.
In the sitcom, characters often joke about breaking the fourth wall.
The actor broke the fourth wall by addressing the audience directly.

Etymology

From the theatrical concept of the 'fourth wall', referring to the imaginary barrier between the actors and the audience.
