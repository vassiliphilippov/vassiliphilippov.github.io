to feel someone's pain

1. VERB

/ fil ˈsʌmwʌnz peɪn / (AmE), / fiːl ˈsʌmwʌnz peɪn / (BrE)
to empathize deeply with someone's suffering or distress
empathize with someone's suffering

Examples

After losing his job, he could really feel her pain when she was laid off.
As a parent, she could feel his pain during his difficult times at school.
Whenever tragedy strikes, good friends always feel your pain and offer support.

Synset Examples

He truly feels her pain during this difficult time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to empathize - to understand and share the feelings of another
  • Synonym: to sympathize - to share or understand the feelings of another, especially sorrow
