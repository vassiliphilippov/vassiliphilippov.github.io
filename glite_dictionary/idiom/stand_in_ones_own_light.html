to stand in one's own light

1. VERB

/ stænd ɪn wʌnz oʊn laɪt / (AmE), / stænd ɪn wʌnz əʊn laɪt / (BrE)
to act in a way that prevents oneself from succeeding or reaching one's full potential
hinder one's own progress or success

Examples

You shouldn't stand in your own light by refusing to take credit for your achievements.
By doubting her abilities, she often stands in her own light when opportunities arise.
He realized too late that he had stood in his own light during his early career.

Synset Examples

Sometimes, we just stand in our own light and hinder our progress.
He tends to stand in his own light by doubting his abilities.
Don’t stand in your own light; embrace new opportunities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-sabotage - the act of undermining one's own success
  • Antonym: to help oneself - to act to support or improve one's own situation
