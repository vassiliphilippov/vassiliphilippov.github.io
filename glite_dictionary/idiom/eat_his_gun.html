to eat his gun

1. VERB

/ it hɪz ɡʌn / (AmE), / iːt hɪz ɡʌn / (BrE)
(informal, sometimes offensive) To feel or express extreme distress or despair, often used hyperbolically to indicate a strong negative emotional reaction.
feel extreme distress or despair (idiom)
informal offensive american

Examples

After hearing the bad news, he said he wanted to eat his gun.
She joked that if she failed the interview again, she'd eat her gun.
He was so overwhelmed by work that he felt like eating his gun.

Synset Examples

He joked about needing to eat his gun after that loss.
He felt like eating his gun after failing the exam.

Etymology

Alludes to suicide by firearm; figurative usage emerged in 20th-century American English.
