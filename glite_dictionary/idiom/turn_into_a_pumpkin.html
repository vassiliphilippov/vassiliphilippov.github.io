to turn into a pumpkin

1. VERB

/ tɝn ˈɪntu ə ˈpʌmpkɪn / (AmE), / tɜːn ˈɪntuː ə ˈpʌmpkɪn / (BrE)
to humorously say one must leave or go to bed, especially because it is late or one is tired, alluding to the fairy tale where Cinderella's carriage turns back into a pumpkin at midnight
joke about needing to leave because it is late

Examples

I need to go home now before I turn into a pumpkin.
She always says she'll turn into a pumpkin if she stays out past midnight.
He joked that after eleven o'clock, he might turn into a pumpkin from exhaustion.

Synset Examples

I have to leave now before I turn into a pumpkin.
She joked that she would turn into a pumpkin if she stayed out late.
The party ends at midnight, or we’ll all turn into pumpkins!

Etymology

From the fairy tale 'Cinderella,' where the magic carriage becomes a pumpkin at midnight.
