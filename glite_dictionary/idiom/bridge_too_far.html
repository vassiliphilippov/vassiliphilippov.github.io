bridge too far

1. NOUN

/ brɪdʒ tu fɑr / (AmE), / brɪdʒ tuː fɑː / (BrE)
an effort or demand that is excessive and likely to fail because it is too ambitious
an overly ambitious or excessive effort

Examples

Trying to renovate the entire house by next month is a bridge too far.
For many small businesses, complying with the new regulations could be a bridge too far.
He found leading the project alone was a bridge too far for him.

Synset Examples

He realized that asking for a promotion now was a bridge too far.
Planning a vacation during finals felt like a bridge too far.
Trying to finish the project in one day was a bridge too far.

Etymology

Popularized by the 1977 film title 'A Bridge Too Far', based on a British saying referring to overreaching in the World War II Arnhem campaign.
