smoking gun

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmoʊkɪŋ ˌɡʌn / (AmE), / ˈsməʊkɪŋ ˌɡʌn / (BrE)
a piece of evidence that clearly proves someone has committed a crime or done something wrong
conclusive evidence of wrongdoing

Examples

The investigation turned up a smoking gun that linked the company to illegal activity.
Journalists searched for a smoking gun in the leaked emails but found nothing conclusive.
They had suspicions for years, but the video finally provided the smoking gun.

Synset Examples

The detective finally found the smoking gun in the suspect's home.
Without a smoking gun, the case fell apart.
The document was the smoking gun that proved his guilt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proof - evidence establishing a fact or the truth of a statement
  • Antonym: red herring - misleading or distracting clue or information

Etymology

From the literal idea of a gun still smoking after being fired, suggesting guilt is fresh and obvious.
