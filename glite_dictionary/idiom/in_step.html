in step

1. ADV

/ ɪn stɛp / (AmE), / ɪn stɛp / (BrE)
moving in harmony, synchrony, or agreement with someone or something
moving in harmony or agreement

Examples

The dancers moved in step with each other, creating a perfect performance.
Management and staff tried to stay in step on the new policy changes.
The children marched in step, following their teacher down the hall.

Synset Examples

They walked in step during the parade.
The team moved in step with the music.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: out of step - not moving in harmony or agreement
