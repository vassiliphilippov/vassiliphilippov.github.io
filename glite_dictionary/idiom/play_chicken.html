to play chicken

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ˈtʃɪk.ən / (AmE), / pleɪ ˈtʃɪk.ɪn / (BrE)
to take part in a dangerous contest of nerve, usually involving risk or confrontation to see who backs down first
engage in a risky contest of nerve

Examples

The two drivers decided to play chicken on the deserted road late at night.
Investors who play chicken with the stock market risk losing everything.
Politicians often play chicken during budget negotiations, waiting to see who will compromise first.

Synset Examples

They decided to play chicken on the highway.
Don't play chicken with your health; get checked.
The two companies are playing chicken over the contract terms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brinkmanship - the art or practice of pursuing a dangerous policy to the limits of safety before stopping

Etymology

Refers to a dangerous game where participants drive towards each other to see who swerves first, metaphorically extended to confrontations of nerve.
