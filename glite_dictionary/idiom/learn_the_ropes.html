to learn the ropes

1. VERB

/ lɝn ðə roʊps / (AmE), / lɜːn ðə rəʊps / (BrE)
to begin to understand how to do a particular job or task by acquiring basic skills and knowledge
to acquire basic skills for a job or task

Examples

It took a few weeks for the interns to learn the ropes in the busy office.
You'll need patience while you learn the ropes of your new responsibilities.
She shadowed an experienced technician to learn the ropes before working independently.

Synset Examples

The new recruits are eager to learn the ropes.
Everyone was once a nooblet, just learning the ropes.
He started as a foot soldier in the company, learning the ropes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get the hang of - to begin to understand how to do something

Etymology

An allusion to sailors being taught the ropes on a ship.
