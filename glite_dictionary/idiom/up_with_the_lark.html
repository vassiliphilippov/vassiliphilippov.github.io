up with the lark

1. ADJ

/ ʌp wɪð ðə lɑrk / (AmE), / ʌp wɪð ðə lɑːk / (BrE)
to wake up or be active very early in the morning
wake up very early

Examples

Every day he's up with the lark to get a head start on his work.
Even on weekends, she prefers being up with the lark rather than sleeping in.
If you want to catch the sunrise, you'll need to be up with the lark.

Synset Examples

She enjoys being up with the lark and starting fresh.
I’m up with the lark every morning for yoga.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: early bird - a person who wakes up or begins work early each day

Etymology

From the association of larks (a type of bird) with early morning activity, as they are known to sing at dawn.
