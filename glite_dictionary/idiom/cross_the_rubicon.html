to cross the Rubicon

1. VERB

/ krɔs ði ˈruːbɪˌkɑn / (AmE), / krɒs ði ˈruːbɪˌkɒn / (BrE)
to take a decisive and irreversible step, especially one that commits someone to a specific course of action
make an irreversible decision

Examples

After months of hesitation, Anna finally crossed the Rubicon and submitted her resignation letter.
Expanding into international markets was when the company really crossed the Rubicon.
By leaking the confidential files, he knew he had crossed the Rubicon and there was no turning back.

Synset Examples

Once he quit his job, he felt he had crossed the Rubicon.
They knew they had crossed the Rubicon with that decision.
After signing the contract, they crossed the Rubicon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burn one's bridges - to do something that makes it impossible to return to an earlier state; to commit irrevocably

Etymology

From the historical event when Julius Caesar crossed the Rubicon River in 49 BC, committing to war against Rome.
