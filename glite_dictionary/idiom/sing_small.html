to sing small

1. VERB

/ sɪŋ smɔl / (AmE), / sɪŋ smɔːl / (BrE)
to behave modestly, speak quietly about one's abilities, or deliberately understate one's talents or achievements
act or speak modestly about oneself

Examples

She always sing small about her accomplishments, even though she is very talented.
Instead of showing off, he decided to sing small during the meeting.
Don't sing small—take pride in what you've achieved!

Synset Examples

She decided to sing small at the audition.
He tends to sing small when asked to perform.
Don't sing small; show us your real talent!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: boast - to talk with excessive pride about one's achievements or abilities
