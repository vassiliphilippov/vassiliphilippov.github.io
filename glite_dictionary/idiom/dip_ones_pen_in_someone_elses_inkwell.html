to dip one's pen in someone else's inkwell

1. VERB

/ dɪp wʌnz pɛn ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈɪŋkwɛl / (AmE), / dɪp wʌnz pɛn ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈɪŋwɛl / (BrE)
to use or borrow another person's ideas or work, often without proper acknowledgment
to take and use someone else's ideas

Examples

He was accused of dip his pen in someone else's inkwell after submitting a report with nearly identical conclusions.
Many artists have dipped their pens in someone else's inkwell at some point in their careers.
It's important to give credit rather than dip your pen in someone else's inkwell when presenting new ideas.

Synset Examples

He really dipped his pen in her inkwell when he used her ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to plagiarize - to take someone else's work or ideas and present them as your own
