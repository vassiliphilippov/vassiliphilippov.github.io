in lockstep

1. ADV

/ ɪn ˈlɑkˌstɛp / (AmE), / ɪn ˈlɒkˌstɛp / (BrE)
in perfect synchronization or acting in complete agreement with others
in perfect synchronization or agreement

Examples

The two companies moved in lockstep when it came to technological innovation.
Politicians from the same party often vote in lockstep on important issues.
Marching bands train to move in lockstep during parades.

Synset Examples

The team worked in lockstep to meet their deadline.
They marched in lockstep, showcasing their unity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in harmony - in agreement or accord
  • Antonym: out of step - not in agreement or not synchronized

Etymology

Originally from the literal 'lockstep' marching formation used in prisons and military drills.
