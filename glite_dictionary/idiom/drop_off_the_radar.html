to drop off the radar

1. VERB

/ drɑp ɑf ðə ˈreɪˌdɑr / (AmE), / drɒp ɒf ðə ˈreɪˌdɑː / (BrE)
to become unnoticed, out of contact, or forgotten, often after previously being active or visible
become unnoticed or forgotten

Examples

After the company closed its offices, it dropped off the radar for several years.
Once she switched careers, she dropped off the radar and no one heard from her.
Many indie bands drop off the radar after their first album.

Synset Examples

He used to call, but now he's dropped off the radar.
Since moving, they've dropped off the radar completely.
Once he got busy, he dropped off the radar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fall off the map - to become forgotten or no longer important

Etymology

Metaphorical use of 'radar' (radio detection and ranging), alluding to disappearing from monitoring systems.
