back in the groove

1. ADJ

/ bæk ɪn ðə ɡruv / (AmE), / bæk ɪn ðə ɡruːv / (BrE)
functioning again in a familiar or effective way after a break; having returned to one's usual routine or level of performance
returned to a usual, effective routine
informal

Examples

After a few days at my new job, I finally felt back in the groove.
She needed some practice before she was back in the groove on the piano.
It took a while after vacation, but now I’m back in the groove with my studies.

Synset Examples

After a week off, I’m finally back in the groove.
Once I started writing again, I was back in the groove.
He listened to music to get back in the groove.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in the groove - performing in a confident or successful way; in a routine
  • Antonym: off your game - not performing at your usual standard
