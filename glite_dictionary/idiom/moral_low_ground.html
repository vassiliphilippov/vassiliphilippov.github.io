moral low ground

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔɹəl loʊ ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈmɒɹəl ləʊ ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
a position or course of action in an argument or debate that is considered unethical or lacking in moral integrity
unethical stance in an argument

Examples

The politician took the moral low ground by spreading false rumors about his opponent.
Resorting to bribery put the company firmly on the moral low ground in the eyes of the public.
Accusing others without evidence is a way of taking the moral low ground in any discussion.

Synset Examples

Attacking her character was a clear moral low ground move.
Using personal attacks is taking the moral low ground.
He resorted to the moral low ground during the debate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: moral high ground - a position of superior ethical principles in a dispute

Etymology

Contrast to the established phrase 'moral high ground,' formed by analogy.
