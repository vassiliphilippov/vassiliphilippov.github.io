to stretch one's budget

1. VERB

/ strɛtʃ wʌnz ˈbʌdʒət / (AmE), / strɛtʃ wʌnz ˈbʌdʒɪt / (BrE)
to make financial resources last longer by spending carefully or economizing
make money last longer

Examples

With rising prices, many families have to stretch their budget to cover basic needs.
I learned how to stretch my budget by shopping for discounts and avoiding unnecessary purchases.
During college, she had to stretch her budget just to pay rent and buy groceries.

Synset Examples

She likes to stretch her budget for the month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tighten one's belt - reduce one's spending due to limited resources
