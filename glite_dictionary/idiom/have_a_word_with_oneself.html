to have a word with oneself

1. VERB

/ hæv ə wɜrd wɪð wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / hæv ə wɜːd wɪð wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to talk to oneself, especially in order to reflect on or criticize one's own behavior or actions.
talk to oneself about one's behavior

Examples

After making that mistake, he decided to have a word with himself about paying better attention at work.
She often needs to have a word with herself when she feels overwhelmed or stressed.
Before making important decisions, I usually have a word with myself to weigh the pros and cons.

Synset Examples

He had a word with himself to stop procrastinating.
