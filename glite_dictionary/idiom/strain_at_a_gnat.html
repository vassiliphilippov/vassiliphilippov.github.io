to strain at a gnat

1. VERB

/ streɪn ət ə næt / (AmE), / streɪn ət ə næt / (BrE)
to pay excessive attention to trivial details while ignoring more important issues
focus on minor details, ignore bigger issues

Examples

It's easy to strain at a gnat when you're nervous about making mistakes.
We shouldn't strain at a gnat during this project and overlook the major deadline.
The committee tends to strain at a gnat instead of addressing the root problems.

Synset Examples

Let's not strain at a gnat when we have larger problems.
Don't strain at a gnat when there are bigger issues to tackle.
He tends to strain at a gnat, missing the bigger picture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make a mountain out of a molehill - to exaggerate the importance of a minor problem
  • Synonym: to nitpick - to focus overly on small or minor details; to find fault unnecessarily

Etymology

From a biblical phrase in Matthew 23:24: "Ye blind guides, which strain at a gnat, and swallow a camel."
