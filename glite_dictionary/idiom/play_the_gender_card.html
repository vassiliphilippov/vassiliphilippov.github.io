to play the gender card

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ðə ˈdʒɛndɚ kɑrd / (AmE), / pleɪ ðə ˈdʒɛndə kɑːd / (BrE)
to invoke gender issues or bias, particularly to gain an advantage or to influence a situation
invoke gender issues for personal advantage

Examples

Many people criticized the politician for trying to play the gender card during the debate.
She refused to play the gender card, insisting that her achievements spoke for themselves.
When challenged on her policies, she accused her opponent of attempting to play the gender card.

Synset Examples

He accused her of trying to play the gender card unfairly.
It's not right to play the gender card in every argument.
She refused to play the gender card in the workplace.
She felt he played the gender card to avoid criticism.
