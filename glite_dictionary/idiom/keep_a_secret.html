to keep a secret

1. VERB

/ kip ə ˈsiː.krət / (AmE), / kiːp ə ˈsiː.krɪt / (BrE)
to not tell anyone information that is meant to be confidential
not disclose confidential information

Examples

Can you keep a secret, or do you always tell your friends everything?
She was trusted because everyone knew she could keep a secret.
It's hard to keep a secret when you're excited about the news.

Synset Examples

I'll tell you a secret if you promise to keep it.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to spill the beans - to reveal a secret or disclose confidential information
  • Antonym: to let the cat out of the bag - to reveal a secret carelessly or by mistake
