like the sound of one's own voice

1. X

/ laɪk ðə saʊnd əv wʌnz oʊn vɔɪs / (AmE), / laɪk ðə saʊnd əv wʌnz əʊn vɔɪs / (BrE)
to enjoy hearing oneself talk, often because of self-importance or arrogance
enjoy hearing oneself talk; arrogant

Examples

People say he likes the sound of his own voice because he never stops talking about himself.
Whenever she joins a meeting, it seems she likes the sound of her own voice more than listening to others.
Some politicians clearly like the sound of their own voices, often dominating every discussion.

Synset Examples

He always talks loudly, like the sound of his own voice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: egotistical - having an exaggerated sense of self-importance
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest opinion of one's importance

2. VERB

/ laɪk ðə saʊnd əv wʌnz oʊn vɔɪs / (AmE), / laɪk ðə saʊnd əv wʌnz əʊn vɔɪs / (BrE)
to enjoy hearing oneself speak, often implying that someone talks excessively or self-importantly without considering others' interest
enjoy listening to oneself speak (idiom)

Examples

He always dominates meetings because he likes the sound of his own voice.
Don't mind her—she just likes the sound of her own voice and loves to lecture everyone.
Some people talk at length not to contribute but because they like the sound of their own voice.

Synset Examples

He often interrupts, clearly liking the sound of his own voice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talkative - tending to talk a lot
  • Antonym: modest - not talking about oneself excessively
