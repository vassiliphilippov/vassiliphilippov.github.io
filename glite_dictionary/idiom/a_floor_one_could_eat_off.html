a floor one could eat off

1. NOUN

/ ə flɔr wʌn kəd it ɔf / (AmE), / ə flɔː wʌn kəd iːt ɒf / (BrE)
a floor that is extremely clean or spotless, so clean that one could almost eat food directly off it
extremely clean floor

Examples

The kitchen was so spotless it had a floor one could eat off.
After hours of scrubbing, the janitor declared the classroom had a floor one could eat off.
They keep their apartment pristine, with a floor one could eat off in every room.

Synset Examples

She maintained a house with a floor one could eat off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotless - completely clean; without any dirt or stains
  • Antonym: filthy - very dirty; not clean at all
