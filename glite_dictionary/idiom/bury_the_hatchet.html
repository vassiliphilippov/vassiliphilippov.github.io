to bury the hatchet

1. VERB

/ ˈbɛri ðə ˈhætʃɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɛri ðə ˈhætʃɪt / (BrE)
to make peace with someone or reconcile after an argument or conflict
make peace after conflict

Examples

After years of rivalry, the two companies finally decided to bury the hatchet.
Let's bury the hatchet and focus on finding a solution together.
It took a heartfelt apology before they could bury the hatchet and become friends again.

Synset Examples

Let's bury the hatchet and collaborate on this project.
It's time to bury the hatchet and move forward.
After their talk, they agreed to bury the hatchet.
They decided to bury the hatchet after years of fighting.
It’s time to bury the hatchet and move forward.
He decided to bury the hatchet after their argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make up - to reconcile after a disagreement
  • Synonym: to reconcile - to restore friendly relations
  • Antonym: to hold a grudge - to continue feeling angry toward someone

Etymology

From a Native American custom in which a war hatchet was literally buried as a sign of peace.
