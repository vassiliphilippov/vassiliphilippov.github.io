to hit a rough patch

1. VERB

/ hɪt ə rʌf pætʃ / (AmE), / hɪt ə rʌf patʃ / (BrE)
to experience a period of difficulty or trouble, especially after a time of relative success or stability.
experience a difficult period

Examples

After losing his job, John hit a rough patch but eventually got back on his feet.
Their company hit a rough patch last year due to declining sales.
We all hit a rough patch from time to time, but support from friends can make a big difference.

Synset Examples

Their relationship hit a rough patch during the last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go through a rough patch - to go through a difficult period
  • Synonym: to hit a snag - to encounter an unexpected problem or difficulty
