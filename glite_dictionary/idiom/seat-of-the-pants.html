seat-of-the-pants

1. ADJ

/ ˌsit əv ðə ˈpænts / (AmE), / ˌsiːt əv ðə ˈpænts / (BrE)
done using instinct or improvisation rather than careful planning or analysis.
improvised; intuitive; not planned

Examples

The new manager took a seat-of-the-pants approach to the budget meeting.
Their victory was largely due to a seat-of-the-pants strategy rather than careful preparation.
Many startups rely on seat-of-the-pants decision-making in their early days.

Synset Examples

His seat-of-the-pants approach to planning often backfires.
The project was a seat-of-the-pants effort without any strategy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improvised - created or done without preparation
  • Synonym: ad hoc - formed or done for a particular purpose as necessary

Etymology

Originally American English (early 20th century), alluding to pilots flying airplanes by instinct rather than instruments.
