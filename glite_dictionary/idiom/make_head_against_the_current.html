to make head against the current

1. VERB

/ meɪk hɛd əˈgɛnst ðə ˈkɝːrənt / (AmE), / meɪk hɛd əˈɡenst ðə ˈkʌrənt / (BrE)
to move forward or make progress despite facing opposition or difficulties
progress despite difficulties

Examples

Despite the strong opposition, the committee continued to make head against the current.
It's hard to make head against the current when so many obstacles block your path.
She struggled to make head against the current in her challenging new job.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make headway - to make progress, especially when this is slow or difficult
