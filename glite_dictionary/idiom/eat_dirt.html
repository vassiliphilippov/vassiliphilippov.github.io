to eat dirt

1. VERB

/ it dɝt / (AmE), / iːt dɜːt / (BrE)
to accept humiliation or admit defeat, often unwillingly or after being proven wrong
accept humiliation or defeat

Examples

After the debate, he had to eat dirt in front of his colleagues.
Sometimes you have to eat dirt when you make a mistake at work.
She refused to eat dirt even though it was clear she was wrong.

Synset Examples

She said I could eat dirt if I disagreed.
He told me to eat dirt after losing the argument.
I felt like I had to eat dirt for my mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swallow one’s pride - to accept humiliation or to act modestly despite wanting not to
