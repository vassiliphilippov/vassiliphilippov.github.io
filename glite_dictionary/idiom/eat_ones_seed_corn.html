to eat one's seed corn

1. VERB

/ it wʌnz siːd kɔrn / (AmE), / iːt wʌnz siːd kɔːn / (BrE)
to use resources intended for future growth or success, thereby risking long-term prospects for short-term gain
use resources meant for future growth

Examples

By cutting research funding, the company risks eating its seed corn and harming future innovation.
If a nation spends all its savings now, it's essentially eating its seed corn.
The manager warned that using the emergency reserves was like eating one's seed corn.

Synset Examples

Investing all profits now is like eating one's seed corn.

Etymology

From the agricultural practice where eating seed corn—a portion reserved for planting—jeopardizes future harvests.
