to fly off the handle

1. VERB

/ flaɪ ɑf ðə ˈhændl̩ / (AmE), / flaɪ ɒf ðə ˈhændl / (BrE)
to suddenly become very angry and lose self-control
become suddenly very angry

Examples

Whenever the internet goes down, my brother tends to fly off the handle about it.
She flew off the handle when she saw the mess in the kitchen.
Try not to fly off the handle over small mistakes; it's better to stay calm.

Synset Examples

The professor combusted when the student didn't know the answer to a very elementary question
Spam makes me go ballistic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lose one's temper - to suddenly become angry
  • Antonym: to keep one's cool - to remain calm and not become angry

Etymology

Originally an American idiom from the early 19th century, likening sudden anger to an axe head coming loose from its handle.

2. VERB

/ flaɪ ɑf ðə ˈhændl̩ / (AmE), / flaɪ ɒf ðə ˈhændl̩ / (BrE)
(of a person) to suddenly lose one's temper or become very angry without warning.
lose one's temper suddenly

Examples

Whenever traffic gets bad, he can fly off the handle in an instant.
Try not to fly off the handle during the meeting if something upsets you.
Her habit of flying off the handle made her difficult to work with.

Synset Examples
