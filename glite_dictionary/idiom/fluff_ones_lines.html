to fluff one's lines

1. VERB

/ flʌf wʌnz laɪnz / (AmE), / flʌf wʌnz laɪnz / (BrE)
to forget or say one's lines incorrectly during a performance, especially on stage or in a recording
make a mistake when speaking lines in a performance

Examples

The actor was nervous and fluffed his lines in the opening scene.
If you fluff your lines on stage, just keep going and don’t pause too long.
She fluffed her lines, but the audience hardly noticed the mistake.

Synset Examples

He managed to fluff his lines during the performance.
Don't fluff your lines; just remember your cue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miss one's cue - fail to respond or speak at the correct moment in a performance
