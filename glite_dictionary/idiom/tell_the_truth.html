to tell the truth

1. VERB

/ tɛl ðə truθ / (AmE), / tɛl ðə truːθ / (BrE)
to speak honestly and openly without hiding any facts or lying
speak honestly; not lie

Examples

It's always easier to tell the truth than to keep up a lie.
She finally decided to tell the truth about what happened that night.
If you tell the truth, you'll feel better in the long run.

Synset Examples

Let me tell you the truth; it's not what you think.
It's best to tell the truth to your friends.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lie - to say something that is not true
  • Synonym: be honest - to be truthful and open
