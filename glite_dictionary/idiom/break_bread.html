to break bread

1. VERB

/ breɪk brɛd / (AmE), / breɪk brɛd / (BrE)
to share a meal with others, often signifying friendship or community
share a meal with others

Examples

Every Sunday, our family would break bread together in the dining room.
Leaders from both communities sat down to break bread and discuss peace.
After the meeting, the colleagues decided to break bread at a nearby restaurant.

Synset Examples

The early Christian disciples broke bread together

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dine - to eat dinner or the main meal
  • Synonym: feast - to eat and drink sumptuously

Etymology

From the literal act of breaking bread during meals; popularized by early Christian tradition.
