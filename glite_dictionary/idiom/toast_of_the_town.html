toast of the town

1. NOUN

/ toʊst əv ðə taʊn / (AmE), / təʊst ɒv ðə taʊn / (BrE)
a person who is currently very popular or widely admired in a particular place or community
someone who is very popular or admired

Examples

After winning the award, she became the toast of the town overnight.
The actor was the toast of the town following his blockbuster performance.
Their new invention made them the toast of the town among entrepreneurs.

Synset Examples

The restaurant's new chef is the toast of the town.
His charity work made him the toast of the town.
After her performance, she became the toast of the town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celebrity - a famous person, especially in entertainment or sport
  • Antonym: nobody - a person of no importance or influence

Etymology

Refers to the tradition of offering a toast (a drink in someone's honor), suggesting the person is being celebrated by society.
