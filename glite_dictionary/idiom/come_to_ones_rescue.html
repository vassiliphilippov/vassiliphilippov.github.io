to come to one's rescue

1. VERB

/ kʌm tu wʌnz rɛsˈkju / (AmE), / kʌm tʊ wʌnz ˈrɛskjuː / (BrE)
to help someone who is in trouble or needs assistance, especially at a crucial moment
help someone in trouble; assist in need

Examples

Whenever I needed help, my sister would always come to my rescue.
A kind stranger came to her rescue when her car broke down on the highway.
The firefighters came to the rescue of the family trapped inside the burning house.

Synset Examples

Friends always come to my rescue when I'm in trouble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to save the day - to do something that solves a serious problem
  • Antonym: to let down - to fail to help or support someone
