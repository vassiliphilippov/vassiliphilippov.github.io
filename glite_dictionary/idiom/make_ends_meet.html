to make ends meet

1. VERB

/ meɪk ɛndz mit / (AmE), / meɪk endz miːt / (BrE)
to manage to live on the money one has, especially when income is limited
manage financially with limited means

Examples

After losing his job, he found it difficult to make ends meet each month.
They had to cut back on expenses in order to make ends meet.
She took a part-time job just to make ends meet while studying.

Synset Examples

During the crisis, they had to sell the family silver to make ends meet.
It's hard to make ends meet on a single salary.
He works two jobs to make ends meet.
He worked at the slaughterhouse to make ends meet.
They struggle to make ends meet each month.
Many families struggle to make ends meet and are often at the breadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scrape by - to barely manage to survive financially
  • Synonym: to get by - to manage to cope or survive, especially financially
  • Degree Higher: struggle - to have difficulty managing financially
