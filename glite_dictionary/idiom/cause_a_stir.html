to cause a stir

1. VERB

/ kɔz ə stɝ / (AmE), / kɔːz ə stɜː / (BrE)
to create excitement, interest, or controversy that attracts attention
create excitement or controversy

Examples

The artist's controversial new exhibition caused a stir in the local art community.
The announcement of his retirement caused a stir among his colleagues and fans.
Her decision to wear a bold outfit to the meeting caused a stir in the usually conservative office.

Synset Examples

Her unexpected departure caused a stir in the office.
His bold speech at the conference caused a stir among the attendees.
Biscuitgate caused a stir in the political arena.
The new restaurant opening caused quite a stir downtown.
Her scandalous remarks caused quite a stir at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make waves - to cause trouble or attract notice by being different or controversial
  • Synonym: cause a commotion - to create a disturbance or noisy disruption
