to fall into the wrong hands

1. VERB

/ fɔl ˈɪntu ðə rɔŋ hændz / (AmE), / fɔːl ˈɪntuː ðə rɒŋ hændz / (BrE)
to become accessible to people who are not authorized or who may use it for harmful purposes
become controlled by bad or unauthorized people

Examples

We must encrypt the data so it does not fall into the wrong hands.
If the formula falls into the wrong hands, it could be misused for criminal purposes.
She locked away the documents to prevent them from falling into the wrong hands.

Synset Examples

That sensitive document could fall into the wrong hands.
We need to secure the files to prevent them from falling into the wrong hands.
If the technology falls into the wrong hands, it could be dangerous.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fall into the right hands - become controlled by people who are trustworthy or well intentioned
