to bear the brunt

1. VERB

/ bɛr ðə brʌnt / (AmE), / bɛə ðə brʌnt / (BrE)
to suffer or endure the worst or main part of something unpleasant or harmful
suffer the worst part

Examples

Local businesses will bear the brunt of the new tax regulations.
During the recession, young workers often bear the brunt of job cuts.
The emergency services bore the brunt of the initial rescue efforts.

Synset Examples

The residents bore the brunt of the storm's damage.
Teachers often bear the brunt of budget cuts.
He bore the brunt of the criticism after the mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take the brunt - experience the worst impact of something
