jewel in the crown

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːəl ɪn ðə kraʊn / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːəl ɪn ðə kraʊn / (BrE)
The most valuable, impressive, or important part of something, often among a group or collection.
most valuable or prized part

Examples

The new concert hall is the jewel in the crown of the city's cultural attractions.
This necklace is considered the jewel in the crown of his family's estate.
For many fans, the championship win remains the jewel in the crown of the team's history.

Synset Examples

This painting is the jewel in the crown of our collection.
Her promotion was the jewel in the crown of her career.
Winning the championship was the jewel in the crown for the team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centerpiece - the most important or attractive part of something
  • Synonym: highlight - the most outstanding part of an event or period of time

Etymology

A metaphor referring to a precious gem set in a crown, often historically used to describe the most prized colonial possession.
