to eat the onion

1. VERB

/ it ði ˈʌnjən / (AmE), / iːt ði ˈʌnjən / (BrE)
to face a difficult or unpleasant situation directly; to confront an unpleasant task or reality without avoiding it
face an unpleasant reality directly

Examples

It's time for us to eat the onion and admit our mistakes.
Sometimes, management just has to eat the onion and make tough decisions.
If you want things to improve, you need to eat the onion instead of avoiding the issue.

Synset Examples

We need to eat the onion and deal with the consequences.
Sometimes, you just have to eat the onion and move on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bite the bullet - to force oneself to perform a painful or unpleasant task
  • Synonym: to face the music - to accept the unpleasant consequences of one's actions
