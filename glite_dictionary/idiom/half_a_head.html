half a head

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæf ə ˈhɛd / (AmE), / ˌhɑːf ə ˈhɛd / (BrE)
used to describe someone who is foolish or lacking sense
foolish or lacking sense (idiom)
informal

Examples

Everyone thought he had half a head after he lost his wallet for the third time this month.
You must have half a head to believe everything you read online.
She told me I had half a head when I tried to fix the sink myself.

Synset Examples

She looked at him like he had half a head for suggesting that.
I can’t believe he forgot; he’s got half a head.
He must have lost half a head to think that was a good idea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgment
  • Antonym: sensible - acting or showing good sense
