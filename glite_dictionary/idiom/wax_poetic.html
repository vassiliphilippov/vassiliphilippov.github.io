to wax poetic

1. VERB

/ wæks poʊˈɛtɪk / (AmE), / waks pəʊˈɛtɪk / (BrE)
to speak or write in an elaborate, lyrical, or fanciful way, especially with enthusiasm or sentimentality
speak or write with elaborate enthusiasm

Examples

Whenever he talks about the stars, he begins to wax poetic and loses track of time.
The author tends to wax poetic when describing the landscape in her novels.
After a few drinks, she started waxing poetic about her childhood memories.

Synset Examples

She loves to wax poetic about her travels.
It's easy to wax poetic when you're passionate about something.
He began to wax poetic during his speech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rhapsodize - to speak or write with great enthusiasm and delight
  • Antonym: be laconic - to use very few words

Etymology

From the verb 'wax' meaning 'to grow or become', used here in a figurative sense; 'poetic' relating to poetry or poetic style.
