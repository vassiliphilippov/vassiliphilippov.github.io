to catch hell

1. VERB

/ kætʃ hɛl / (AmE), / kætʃ hɛl / (BrE)
(informal) to receive strong criticism or punishment for something wrong or a mistake.
receive severe criticism or punishment
informal

Examples

If you forget to lock the door again, you’ll catch hell from Dad.
The whole team caught hell after they lost the championship game.
She knows she’ll catch hell at work if she’s late again.

Synset Examples

You’ll catch hell if you don’t finish this project.
He’s going to catch hell from his coach for that mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get in trouble - to be in a situation where you are likely to be punished or scolded
  • Synonym: come under fire - to be severely criticized

2. VERB

/ kætʃ hɛl / (AmE), / katʃ hɛl / (BrE)
to receive severe criticism or punishment, especially in an informal context
receive severe criticism or punishment (idiom)
informal

Examples

The students caught hell for not turning in their assignments on time.
We knew we would catch hell if we broke the rules again.

Synset Examples

I caught hell for forgetting my parents' anniversary.

Related Terms
