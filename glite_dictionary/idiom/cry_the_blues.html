to cry the blues

1. VERB

/ kraɪ ðə bluːz / (AmE), / kraɪ ðə bluːz / (BrE)
to feel or express sadness or distress, especially by complaining or lamenting
express sadness or distress

Examples

Every time she has a rough day at work, she starts to cry the blues to her friends.
He's been crying the blues ever since his team lost the match.
They don't want to cry the blues, but things have been tough lately.

Synset Examples

Whenever it rains, I tend to cry the blues.
After the breakup, she started to cry the blues.
He’s been crying the blues since he lost his job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lament - to express grief or sorrow
  • Antonym: to rejoice - to feel or show great joy

Etymology

Associated with 'the blues,' a genre of music expressing melancholy, from the color blue signifying sadness.
