Christmas come early

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪs.məs kʌm ˈɜr.li / (AmE), / ˈkrɪs.məs kʌm ˈɜː.li / (BrE)
an unexpected but very welcome event or piece of good fortune, as if receiving gifts before Christmas
unexpected good fortune

Examples

For fans, the early release of the album was Christmas come early.
Getting an all-expenses-paid trip felt like Christmas come early to him.
Her promotion was Christmas come early after months of hard work.

Synset Examples

Finding that rare book felt like Christmas come early.
Winning the lottery was Christmas come early for her.
The surprise party made it feel like Christmas come early.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windfall - a large or unexpected gain, especially of money
  • Synonym: stroke of luck - a sudden or unexpected event bringing good luck

Etymology

Refers to the excitement and happiness associated with receiving gifts at Christmas, used figuratively for lucky surprises.
