to have one's bread buttered

1. VERB

/ hæv wʌnz brɛd ˈbʌtəd / (AmE), / hav wʌnz brɛd ˈbʌtəd / (BrE)
to be financially secure or well provided for; to have one's material needs comfortably met
to be financially secure

Examples

After years of hard work, Mark finally has his bread buttered and doesn't worry about money.
She knew how to make sure she had her bread buttered by marrying into wealth.
With a great pension plan, retirees often find their bread buttered for the rest of their lives.

Synset Examples

Thanks to her investments, she has her bread buttered for life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: well-off - having plenty of money or possessions
  • Synonym: comfortable - being in a state of financial ease or security
  • Antonym: hard up - having very little money, especially for a short period

Etymology

Based on the idea that bread with butter is more appealing and signifies comfort or provision.
