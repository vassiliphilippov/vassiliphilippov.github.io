in the first place

1. ADV

/ ɪn ðə fɝst pleɪs / (AmE), / ɪn ðə fɜːst pleɪs / (BrE)
used to introduce the first and most important reason, point, or consideration; primarily or to begin with.
first and most important; to begin with

Examples

In the first place, you should have called to let me know you were going to be late.
She wanted to explain why she declined the offer in the first place.
In the first place, our decision was based on financial reasons.

Synset Examples

this is primarily a question of economics
it was in the first place a local matter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firstly - used to introduce the first point in a series
  • Synonym: primarily - mainly; for the most part
  • Antonym: finally - at the end or as the last consideration

3. ADV

/ ɪn ðə fɝst pleɪs / (AmE), / ɪn ðə fɜːst pleɪs / (BrE)
used to indicate the original or initial reason for something, or to ask why something happened at all.
originally; to begin with

Examples

I don't understand why you trusted him in the first place.
She shouldn't have agreed to the proposal in the first place.
If you never wanted it, why did you buy it in the first place?

Synset Examples

It was a mistake to trust him in the first place.
Why did you invite him in the first place?
I shouldn't have gone out in the first place.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to begin with - used to indicate the initial point or reason
