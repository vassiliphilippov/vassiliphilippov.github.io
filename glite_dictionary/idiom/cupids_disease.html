Cupid's disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːpɪdz dɪˈziːz / (AmE), / ˈkjuːpɪdz dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A humorous or metaphorical way to refer to being in love, likening it to catching a contagious illness.
humorous term for being in love

Examples

After meeting her, he claimed he was suffering from Cupid's disease and couldn't think of anything else.
They teased him about his odd behavior, suggesting he had come down with Cupid's disease.
Whenever Valentine's Day arrives, it seems like everyone catches Cupid's disease.

Synset Examples

She laughed, saying Cupid's disease was contagious.
He joked about having Cupid's disease after falling in love.
Many say Cupid's disease makes people act foolishly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lovesick - experiencing strong feelings of love, typically accompanied by longing or sadness

Etymology

From Cupid, the Roman god of love, and 'disease', used metaphorically to describe being lovesick.
