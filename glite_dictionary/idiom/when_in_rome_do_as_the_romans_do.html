when in Rome, do as the Romans do

1. X

/ wɛn ɪn ɹoʊm du æz ðə ˈɹoʊmənz du / (AmE), / wɛn ɪn ˈɹəʊm duː æz ðə ˈɹəʊmənz duː / (BrE)
follow the customs or behavior of the place you are in or the people around you
adapt to local customs or practices

Examples

When you travel to a new country, it's best to remember: when in Rome, do as the Romans do.
As the company culture was very formal, he thought it wise to when in Rome, do as the Romans do and wear a suit.
She always tries to fit in wherever she goes, firmly believing when in Rome, do as the Romans do.

Synset Examples

It's wise to follow local customs; when in Rome, do as the Romans do.

Etymology

From a Latin proverb referring to adapting to local customs, popularized in English since the 16th century.
