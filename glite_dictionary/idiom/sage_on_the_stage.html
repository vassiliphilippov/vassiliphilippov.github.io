sage on the stage

1. NOUN

/ seɪdʒ ɑn ðə steɪdʒ / (AmE), / seɪdʒ ɒn ðə steɪdʒ / (BrE)
a teacher or lecturer who imparts knowledge by speaking to an audience in a traditional, often one-way, manner
traditional lecturing teacher

Examples

Many universities are shifting away from the sage on the stage model to more interactive teaching methods.
Her teaching style was often described as that of a sage on the stage, focusing on delivering lectures.
The panel debated whether the sage on the stage approach was still effective in modern classrooms.

Synset Examples

He delivered his lecture like a sage on the stage.
The professor was seen as a sage on the stage.
In this workshop, we encourage everyone to be a sage on the stage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: guide on the side - a facilitator who supports student learning rather than just lecturing
