to run a risk

1. VERB

/ rʌn ə rɪsk / (AmE), / rʌn ə rɪsk / (BrE)
to do something that could have a bad result; to take a chance of loss or harm
take a chance with possible bad outcome

Examples

He knew he would run a risk by speaking out against the policy.
If you invest in that company, you run a risk of losing your money.
Anyone who ignores the safety instructions is running a risk.

Synset Examples

When you buy these stocks you are gambling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take a risk - to do something despite possible danger or loss
  • Antonym: to play it safe - to avoid taking risks
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02550045-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rʌn ə rɪsk / (AmE), / rʌn ə rɪsk / (BrE)
to do something that may result in danger, harm, or loss; to take a chance that something bad might happen
expose oneself to danger or loss

Examples

If you don't wear a helmet while biking, you run a risk of injury.
They knew they would run a risk by investing in a new business.
He doesn't like to run a risk when making important decisions.

Synset Examples

He might run a risk by skipping safety protocols.
Investing in stocks can run a risk of loss.
She decided to run a risk and start her own business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take a chance - do something involving risk or uncertainty
  • Antonym: to play it safe - to avoid risk
