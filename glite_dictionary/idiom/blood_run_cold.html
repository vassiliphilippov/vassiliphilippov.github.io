to blood run cold

1. VERB

/ blʌd rʌn koʊld / (AmE), / blʌd rʌn kəʊld / (BrE)
to feel sudden and intense fear or shock
feel intense fear or shock

Examples

The sight of the shadowy figure made his blood run cold.
Her blood ran cold when she heard footsteps behind her late at night.
As soon as he opened the eerie letter, his blood ran cold.

Synset Examples

When he heard the news, someone's blood ran cold.
The horror movie made someone's blood run cold.
Her scream made someone's blood run cold instantly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freeze - to become suddenly still with fear
