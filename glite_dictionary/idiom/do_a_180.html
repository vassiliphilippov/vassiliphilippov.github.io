to do a 180

1. VERB

/ du ə wʌn ˈeɪti / (AmE), / duː ə wʌn ˈeɪti / (BrE)
to completely reverse one's opinion, decision, or direction
make a complete reversal
informal

Examples

After hearing both sides of the story, he decided to do a 180 and support the other team.
Last year, the company did a 180 on its environmental policy.
At first she was against the idea, but she quickly did a 180 after learning more details.

Synset Examples

She did a 180 on her opinion about the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: change one's mind - to decide to do something different from what was decided earlier
  • Synonym: to backtrack - to reverse or retract one's previous position or opinion

Etymology

From the notion of turning 180 degrees (a half turn) to go in the opposite direction.
