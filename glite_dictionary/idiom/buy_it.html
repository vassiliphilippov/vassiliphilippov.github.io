to buy it

1. VERB

/ ˈbaɪ ɪt / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ ɪt / (BrE)
(informal) To be killed or to die, especially suddenly or as a result of violence or an accident.
die; be killed (informal idiom)
informal american

Examples

He nearly bought it when his car skidded off the road last night.
Many soldiers bought it in the first wave of the attack.
If we make one wrong move, we could all buy it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to kick the bucket - to die (informal idiom)
  • Synonym: to pass away - to die (euphemistic)

Etymology

Probably from military slang; possibly from ‘buy the farm’ or allusion to paying the ultimate price.
