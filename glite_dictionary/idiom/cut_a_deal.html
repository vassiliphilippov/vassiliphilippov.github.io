to cut a deal

1. VERB

/ kʌt ə dil / (AmE), / kʌt ə diːl / (BrE)
to negotiate and reach an agreement, especially in business or politics
negotiate an agreement

Examples

After hours of negotiation, both companies finally cut a deal that satisfied everyone involved.
She hoped to cut a deal with her landlord to lower the rent for next year.
The politician promised voters that he would not cut a deal with special interests.

Synset Examples

Let's cut a deal that benefits both of us.
He was eager to cut a deal with the investors.
They managed to cut a deal before the deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to strike a deal - to successfully negotiate an agreement
