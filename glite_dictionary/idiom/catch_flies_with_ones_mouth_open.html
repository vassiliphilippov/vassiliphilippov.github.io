to catch flies with one's mouth open

1. VERB

/ kætʃ flaɪz wɪð wʌnz maʊθ oʊpən / (AmE), / katʃ flaɪz wɪð wʌnz maʊθ əʊpən / (BrE)
to be inattentive, unguarded, or careless, usually by being distracted or daydreaming
be inattentive or careless

Examples

She was so distracted during the meeting that she almost caught flies with her mouth open.
If you catch flies with your mouth open in class, you might miss something important.
He warned his son not to catch flies with his mouth open while crossing the street.

Synset Examples

He always warns not to catch flies with your mouth open.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daydream - to be lost in one's thoughts and unaware of the surroundings
  • Antonym: be alert - to be attentive and watchful
