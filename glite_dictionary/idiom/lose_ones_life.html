to lose one's life

1. VERB

/ luːz wʌnz laɪf / (AmE), / luːz wʌnz laɪf / (BrE)
to die, especially as a result of an accident, violence, or disaster
to die; to be killed

Examples

Many soldiers lost their lives in the conflict.
Rescue workers risked everything so that no one would lose their life in the fire.
The explorer nearly lost his life crossing the jungle.

Synset Examples

He was warned that he could lose his life if he didn't stop.
She almost lost her life in the car accident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: die - to stop living
  • Antonym: to survive - to continue to live after a difficult or dangerous situation
