close, but no cigar

1. X

/ kloʊs bət noʊ səˈɡɑr / (AmE), / kləʊs bət nəʊ sɪˈɡɑː / (BrE)
used to say that someone almost succeeded but failed at the last moment or missed the target by a small margin
almost succeeded, but failed

Examples

He came in second place in the race—close, but no cigar.
Her answer in the quiz was very nearly correct—close, but no cigar.
They almost made the playoffs this year; close, but no cigar.

Synset Examples

He almost finished the puzzle, but it was close, but no cigar.
He guessed the answer correctly, but it was close, but no cigar.
She missed the deadline by an hour; close, but no cigar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: almost - very nearly but not quite
  • Synonym: to miss the mark - fail to achieve the intended result

Etymology

Originated in 20th-century America, where fairground games awarded cigars as prizes for winning; near misses received the phrase as consolation.
