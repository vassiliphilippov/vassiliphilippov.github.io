to get some air

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt səm ɛr / (AmE), / ɡɛt səm eə / (BrE)
to go outside, typically to breathe in fresh air, especially after being indoors for a while
go outside for fresh air

Examples

After hours in the office, I just needed to get some air outside.
She stepped onto the balcony to get some air and clear her mind.
We decided to leave the stuffy room to get some air in the park.

Synset Examples

After being cooped up, I needed to get some air.
Let’s go for a walk to get some air.
I stepped outside to get some air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get fresh air - to go outside to breathe fresh air
