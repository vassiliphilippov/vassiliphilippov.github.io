to grind to a halt

1. VERB

/ ɡraɪnd tu ə hɔlt / (AmE), / ɡraɪnd tə ə hɔːlt / (BrE)
To slow down gradually and stop moving, progressing, or operating.
come to a stop; cease functioning

Examples

The city’s public transportation system ground to a halt during the severe snowstorm.
Production in the factory grinds to a halt whenever there is a power failure.
Negotiations ground to a halt after both sides refused to compromise.

Synset Examples

The car bogged down in the sand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: come to a standstill - to stop moving or progressing completely
  • Synonym: to bog down - to become stuck and unable to progress
