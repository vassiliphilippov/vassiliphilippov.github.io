to bring up short

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ ʌp ʃɔrt / (AmE), / brɪŋ ʌp ʃɔːt / (BrE)
to stop or interrupt someone suddenly and unexpectedly, especially in speech or action
to stop or interrupt suddenly

Examples

His sharp question brought up short everyone in the meeting.
I was brought up short by the sight of an old friend from school.
The sudden noise brought her up short as she entered the room.

Synset Examples

Her sudden arrival brought the conversation up short.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interrupt - to stop someone from continuing what they are saying or doing
