to cook someone's goose

1. VERB

/ kʊk ˈsʌmwʌnz ɡus / (AmE), / kʊk ˈsʌmwənz ɡuːs / (BrE)
to ruin someone's plans, chances of success, or future prospects
ruin someone's chances

Examples

If the boss finds out about your mistake, it will definitely cook your goose.
He lied during the interview, and that really cooked his goose.
Spreading those rumors could cook her goose at the company.

Synset Examples

Failing the exam might just cook her goose for college.
If you reveal the secret, you'll really cook your goose.
He thought he could skip work, but that cooked his goose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruin - to destroy or severely damage something
  • Synonym: sabotage - to deliberately damage or destroy something to prevent success
