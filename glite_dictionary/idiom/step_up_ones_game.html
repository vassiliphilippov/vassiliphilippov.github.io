to step up one's game

1. VERB

/ stɛp ʌp wʌnz ɡeɪm / (AmE), / stɛp ʌp wʌnz ɡeɪm / (BrE)
to improve one's performance, effort, or level of skill, especially in response to increased competition or difficulty
improve performance or effort
informal

Examples

If I want to win this competition, I'll have to step up my game.
After our rivals launched a new product, the company knew it needed to step up its game.
When she realized the importance of the project, she really stepped up her game.

Synset Examples

She really stepped up her game in the final round.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raise one's game - to improve one's performance or skill in response to a challenge
