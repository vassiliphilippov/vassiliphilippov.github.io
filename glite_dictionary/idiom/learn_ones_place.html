to learn one's place

1. VERB

/ lɝn wʌnz pleɪs / (AmE), / lɜːn wʌnz pleɪs / (BrE)
to come to understand one's proper role or position within a group, organization, or society, often implying acceptance of hierarchical status or limitations
understand one's role or position

Examples

After missing an important deadline, he was told to learn his place in the company.
Some people need to learn their place before they try to challenge authority.
She had to learn her place when she joined the strict boarding school.

Synset Examples

He needs to learn his place in the team.
It's important to learn your place in the hierarchy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: know one's place - be aware of one's proper position or role, especially in a hierarchy

2. VERB

/ lɝːn wʌnz pleɪs / (AmE), / lɜːn wʌnz pleɪs / (BrE)
to accept or become aware of the appropriate role, rank, or status one holds, especially in a social or organizational hierarchy
accept or understand social position or role

Examples

After being reprimanded by her manager, she finally learned her place in the company.
He tried to speak out, but soon learned his place among the senior staff.
Moving to a new school, she quickly learned her place in the social hierarchy.

Synset Examples

She quickly learned her place among her peers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: know one's place - to recognize and behave according to one's social position or role
