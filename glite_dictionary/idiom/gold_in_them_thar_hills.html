gold in them thar hills

1. X

/ ɡoʊld ɪn ðɛm ðɑr hɪlz / (AmE), / ɡəʊld ɪn ðem ðɑː hɪlz / (BrE)
used to suggest that there is potential wealth, opportunity, or profit to be found in a particular place or situation
potential opportunity or profit in a place

Examples

Investors believe there's gold in them thar hills when it comes to emerging technologies.
The new mayor promised his supporters that there was gold in them thar hills if they revitalized the downtown area.
Even though the factory is abandoned, some still think there's gold in them thar hills.

Synset Examples

He always claimed there was gold in them thar hills.
Rumors spread that there was gold in them thar hills, attracting many.

Etymology

Popularized in 19th-century American English, mimicking the dialect of prospectors during the Gold Rush.
