smell of the lamp

1. NOUN

/ smɛl ʌv ðə læmp / (AmE), / smɛl əv ðə læmp / (BrE)
a phrase referring to signs of hard and late-night study or effort, or literally, the odor produced by an oil or wax lamp
signs of hard, late-night study or literal lamp odor
archaic

Examples

Her thesis, though impressive, bore the smell of the lamp in its detailed footnotes and references.
After hours of work, his report had the smell of the lamp about it.
The old library retained the literal smell of the lamp from countless evenings spent reading.

Synset Examples

His essays often carried the smell of the lamp.
She could sense the smell of the lamp after studying all night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smell of midnight oil - signs of late-night studying or hard work

Etymology

Refers to scholars or writers working late by lamplight, especially before electric lighting.
