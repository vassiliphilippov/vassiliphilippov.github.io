to hurry up and wait

1. VERB

/ ˌhɝː.i ˈʌp ənd ˈweɪt / (AmE), / ˌhʌr.i ˈʌp ənd ˈweɪt / (BrE)
to rush or be made to act quickly only to have to wait for something to happen or become ready
to rush then experience an unnecessary delay

Examples

We were told to hurry up and wait before the meeting started.
The whole process was a classic case of hurry up and wait, with everyone rushing only to stand around.
In the film industry, it's common to hurry up and wait between scenes.

Synset Examples

It's frustrating to hurry up and wait for the bus.
Our travel plans felt like a hurry up and wait situation.
The military often practices hurry up and wait during drills.
