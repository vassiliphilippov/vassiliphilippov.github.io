in high gear

1. ADV

/ ɪn haɪ ɡɪr / (AmE), / ɪn haɪ ɡɪə / (BrE)
operating at maximum efficiency or speed; progressing very rapidly
at maximum efficiency or speed

Examples

With the holiday season approaching, the shopping malls are in high gear all week.
Our preparations for the wedding are now in high gear.
Production at the factory shifted in high gear after the new order came in.

Synset Examples

Her career is in high gear after that promotion.
The project is now in high gear, moving ahead quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in full swing - at the most active or successful stage
  • Antonym: in low gear - moving or operating slowly and less efficiently

Etymology

From the literal sense in automobiles where higher gears allow faster movement.
