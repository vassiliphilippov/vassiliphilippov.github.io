writ large

1. ADJ

/ rɪt lɑrdʒ / (AmE), / rɪt lɑːdʒ / (BrE)
used to indicate that something is made more obvious, prominent, or clear; shown in a more exaggerated or evident way.
more obvious or prominent

Examples

The country's problems are its past mistakes writ large in the present.
The artist's bold style is his personality writ large on canvas.
The tension in the room was writ large on everyone's face.

Synset Examples

the effect of … his irregular life could be seen writ large on his gaunt features

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obvious - easily perceived or understood
  • Synonym: manifest - clear or obvious to the eye or mind

Etymology

Literal past participle of 'write' used figuratively since the 17th century.

2. ADJ

/ rɪt lɑrdʒ / (AmE), / rɪt lɑːdʒ / (BrE)
very obvious or clearly evident; made plain or expressed in a way that is easy to see or understand.
very obvious; made plain or evident

Examples

The effects of climate change are writ large in the rising sea levels.
The company's priorities are writ large in its new advertising campaign.
Her concern for her students was writ large in every lesson she taught.

Synset Examples

The problem was writ large in the report.
Her dedication was writ large in her achievements.
The consequences were writ large for everyone to see.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obvious - easily perceived or understood; clear, self-evident
  • Synonym: manifest - clear or obvious to the eye or mind
  • Antonym: subtle - not obvious; delicate or precise

Etymology

From archaic 'writ' (meaning written) and 'large' meaning 'in large letters'; originally referring to something written more openly or obviously.
