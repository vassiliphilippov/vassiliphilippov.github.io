journey to Canossa

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝːni tu kəˈnɑːsə / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːni tʊ kəˈnɒsə / (BrE)
an act of penance or submission, especially one that is public and humbling
significant act of penance or submission

Examples

After the scandal, the politician's public apology was seen as a journey to Canossa by many observers.
The CEO's humble visit to the union leaders resembled a journey to Canossa in the business world.
Many interpreted the president's televised concession as a symbolic journey to Canossa.

Synset Examples

The king's journey to Canossa was a significant historical event.

Etymology

From the historical journey of Holy Roman Emperor Henry IV to Canossa in 1077 to seek forgiveness from Pope Gregory VII.
