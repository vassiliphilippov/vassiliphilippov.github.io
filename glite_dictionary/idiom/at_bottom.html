at bottom

1. ADV

/ æt ˈbɑtəm / (AmE), / ət ˈbɒtəm / (BrE)
used to say what is most basic or important about someone or something; fundamentally or in reality
fundamentally; in reality

Examples

At bottom, the argument is about free speech, not economics.
He seems strict, but at bottom he cares deeply about his students.
The company faced many challenges, but at bottom its values never changed.

Synset Examples

she is very kind at heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at heart - in one's true nature

2. ADV

/ æt ˈbɑtəm / (AmE), / æt ˈbɒtəm / (BrE)
used to indicate the most basic or fundamental truth about something
essentially; fundamentally

Examples

At bottom, his motives were selfish, despite what he claimed.
The two proposals are, at bottom, quite similar in their principles.
At bottom, the conflict arises from a lack of clear communication.

Synset Examples

At bottom, her decision is about personal happiness.
At bottom, the issue is about trust and honesty.
At bottom, he just wants to be understood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: essentially - used to emphasize the basic, fundamental nature of something
  • Synonym: fundamentally - relating to the most important or basic aspect of something
