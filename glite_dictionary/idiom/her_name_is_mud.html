her name is mud

1. X

/ hɜr neɪm ɪz mʌd / (AmE), / hə neɪm ɪz mʌd / (BrE)
used to say that someone is in disgrace or has a very bad reputation, often after doing something wrong or unpopular
in disgrace or having a bad reputation

Examples

After she told the secret, her name is mud at the club.
If I don't finish this project on time, her name is mud with the boss.
Because of the missed deadline, her name is mud among the team.

Synset Examples

She forgot the meeting, so now her name is mud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgraced - having lost the respect of people, in disgrace
  • Antonym: in good standing - having a good reputation or status

Etymology

Possibly from Dr. Samuel Mudd, a physician who treated John Wilkes Booth after Abraham Lincoln's assassination, but use of 'name is mud' predates this, appearing in the early 19th century.
