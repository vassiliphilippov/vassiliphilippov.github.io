to sweep under the rug

1. VERB

/ swip ˌʌndər ðə rʌɡ / (AmE), / swiːp ˌʌndə ðə rʌɡ / (BrE)
to try to hide a problem or mistake instead of dealing with it openly
conceal a problem or mistake

Examples

Many companies try to sweep under the rug any complaints they receive from customers.
Instead of addressing the issue, management chose to sweep it under the rug and hope people would forget.
She refused to sweep her mistakes under the rug and admitted what she had done.

Synset Examples

The president tried to sweep the embarrassing incident under the rug

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cover up - to hide the truth about something, especially a mistake or crime
  • Antonym: to bring to light - to make something known that was previously hidden or secret

2. VERB

/ swip ˈʌndɚ ðə rʌg / (AmE), / swiːp ˈʌndə ðə rʌg / (BrE)
to try to hide a problem or mistake instead of dealing with it
conceal or ignore a problem

Examples

Every time an issue comes up, the management tries to sweep it under the rug.
Instead of addressing financial irregularities, they swept the problem under the rug for years.
We can't just sweep our differences under the rug and pretend everything is fine.

Synset Examples

They chose to sweep the issue under the rug.
It's easier to sweep mistakes under the rug than fix them.
Don't sweep your problems under the rug; address them.
She ignored the mess and swept it under the rug.
Problems were hidden under the rug for too long.
He hid the letter under the old rug.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cover up - to try to stop people from discovering the truth about something wrong or illegal
  • Antonym: face up to - to confront or deal with a problem
