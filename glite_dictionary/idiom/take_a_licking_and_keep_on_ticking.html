to take a licking and keep on ticking

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə ˈlɪkɪŋ ənd kip ɑn ˈtɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / teɪk ə ˈlɪkɪŋ ənd kiːp ɒn ˈtɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
to withstand hardships, challenges, or adversity and continue to function or persist without giving up
endure trouble and keep going

Examples

No matter how many setbacks he faces, he just takes a licking and keeps on ticking.
Their relationship has taken a licking and kept on ticking through years of ups and downs.
My old phone has taken a licking and kept on ticking despite being dropped so many times.

Synset Examples

The old clock may take a licking and keep on ticking.
He proved he could take a licking and keep on ticking.
Despite setbacks, she takes a licking and keeps on ticking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resilient - able to withstand or recover quickly from difficult conditions
  • Synonym: persistent - continuing firmly despite difficulties
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop trying or surrender

Etymology

Popularized by a 1970s advertising slogan for Timex watches: 'Takes a licking and keeps on ticking.'
