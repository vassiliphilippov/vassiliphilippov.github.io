to tread the boards

1. VERB

/ trɛd ðə bɔrdz / (AmE), / trɛd ðə bɔːdz / (BrE)
to perform as an actor on the stage, especially in the theater
act on stage; perform in theater

Examples

After spending years in drama classes, she finally got the chance to tread the boards at a major theater.
Many aspiring actors hope to tread the boards in the West End one day.
He has been treading the boards since he was a teenager.

Synset Examples

Many actors aspire to tread the boards on Broadway.
After years of practice, he finally got to tread the boards.
She has always dreamed of treading the boards in London.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to perform - to entertain an audience by acting, singing, etc.

Etymology

Refers to the wooden floorboards of a theater stage.
