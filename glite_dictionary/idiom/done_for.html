done for

1. ADJ

/ dʌn fɔr / (AmE), / dʌn fə / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) doomed to failure, extinction, or destruction; unable to be saved or rescued.
doomed to failure or extinction

Examples

After that last mistake, the company was done for and soon went out of business.
If the rescue team doesn't arrive soon, we're all done for.
Everyone knew the old tradition was done for in the modern world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finished - having reached an end; no longer in operation or existence
  • Antonym: safe - not in danger or at risk; protected

2. ADJ

/ dʌn fɔɹ / (AmE), / dʌn fɔː / (BrE)
in a situation so bad that one is doomed to fail, be destroyed, or be killed; finished.
in serious trouble or doomed

Examples

If we don't get help soon, we're done for.
The car broke down in the middle of nowhere, and I thought we were done for.
After that mistake, she knew her chances were done for.
