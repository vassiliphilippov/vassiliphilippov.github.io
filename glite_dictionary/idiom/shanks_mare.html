shank's mare

1. NOUN

/ ʃæŋks mɛr / (AmE), / ʃæŋks mɛə / (BrE)
used humorously to refer to walking as a means of travel; going on foot
travel by walking

Examples

After the bus broke down, we finished the journey on shank's mare.
He couldn't afford a taxi, so he relied on shank's mare to get home.
They explored the city using nothing but shank's mare.

Synset Examples

I traveled on shank's mare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walk - to move along on foot
  • Antonym: ride - to travel on or be carried along on an animal or vehicle

Etymology

'Shank' refers to the lower part of the leg; 'mare' is a female horse, humorously likening one's legs to a horse for travel.
