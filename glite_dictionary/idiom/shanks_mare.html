shanks' mare

1. NOUN

/ ʃæŋks mɛr / (AmE), / ʃæŋks mɛə / (BrE)
used to refer to walking, especially instead of using a vehicle; to travel on foot
to travel on foot

Examples

Since the buses were on strike, we had to rely on shanks' mare to get to work.
They missed the last train and finished their journey by shanks' mare.
Whenever his bicycle breaks down, Tom uses shanks' mare to go to school.

Synset Examples

I traveled on shank's mare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walk - to move at a regular pace by lifting and setting down each foot in turn

Etymology

From 'shank' meaning leg (of a person) and 'mare' meaning horse, thus humorously calling one's legs a horse.

/ ʃæŋks mɛr / (AmE), / ʃæŋks mɛə / (BrE)
used to refer humorously to walking as a means of transportation; travelling on foot.
walking as a means of transport
informal british

Examples

After missing the last bus, I had to rely on shanks' mare to get home.
He said he'd take shanks' mare to the store since his bike was broken.
Tourists often use shanks' mare to explore the old city centers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walk - to move at a regular pace by lifting and setting down each foot in turn

Etymology

Refers to "shank" (the lower part of the leg); 'mare' (a horse), forming an idiom meaning one's own legs used for travel.
