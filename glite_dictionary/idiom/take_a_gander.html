to take a gander

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə ˈɡændɚ / (AmE), / teɪk ə ˈɡændə / (BrE)
to look at or glance at something, especially in a casual or curious way
look at; have a glance
informal

Examples

Why don't you take a gander at the new restaurant down the street?
She stopped to take a gander through the shop window as she walked by.
Before signing the contract, make sure to take a gander at the fine print.

Synset Examples

Let's take a gander at the new exhibit.
He took a gander at the menu before ordering.
You should take a gander at this amazing deal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to look at - to direct one's gaze toward something
  • Synonym: to glance at - to take a brief or hurried look

Etymology

Originally slang; 'gander' meaning 'male goose' came to mean 'look' in 19th-century British and American English, likening the act of stretching one's neck to look to a goose's motion.
