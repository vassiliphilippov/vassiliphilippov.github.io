to have someone's name written all over it

1. VERB

/ hæv ˈsʌmwʌnz neɪm ˈrɪtn ɔl ˈoʊvɚ ɪt / (AmE), / hæv ˈsʌmwʌnz neɪm ˈrɪtn ɔːl ˈəʊvə r ɪt / (BrE)
(of something) to be very typical of someone or show clear signs of being created, caused, or influenced by that person.
be clearly associated with someone

Examples

This prank has your name written all over it—I know how much you love practical jokes.
The creative solution to the problem had Anna's name written all over it.
The messy handwriting on the report has Mark's name written all over it.

Synset Examples

The failure has her name written all over it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typical - having the distinctive qualities of a particular type of person or thing
