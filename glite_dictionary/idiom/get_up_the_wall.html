to get up the wall

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ʌp ðə wɔl / (AmE), / ɡɛt ʌp ðə wɔːl / (BrE)
(informal) To become very annoyed or frustrated.
become very annoyed or frustrated
informal

Examples

His constant whistling really makes me get up the wall.
If I hear that song one more time, I’ll get up the wall.
She tends to get up the wall when things don’t go as planned.

Synset Examples

Her constant questions were getting up the wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drive (someone) up the wall - cause someone to become very annoyed or frustrated
