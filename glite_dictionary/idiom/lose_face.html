to lose face

1. VERB

/ luːz feɪs / (AmE), / luːz feɪs / (BrE)
to suffer a loss of respect or dignity; to be embarrassed or humiliated in the eyes of others
suffer a loss of respect or dignity

Examples

He apologized quickly so he wouldn't lose face in front of his team.
The company tried to hide the error to avoid losing face.
If you admit the mistake, you might lose face with your clients.

Synset Examples

He didn't want to lose face in front of his colleagues.
She was worried she might lose face after the mistake.
To avoid losing face, he quickly apologized for the error.
The public scandal led to a terrible loss of face.
Avoiding a loss of face is crucial in negotiations.
His mistake caused a significant loss of face at work.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to save face - to avoid embarrassment or preserve dignity

Etymology

Calqued from Chinese diū liǎn (to lose face), entering English in the late 19th century.
