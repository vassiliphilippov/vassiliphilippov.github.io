to go west

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ wɛst / (AmE), / ɡəʊ west / (BrE)
to leave for another place, especially the western direction, usually in search of better opportunities or adventure; to venture elsewhere to improve one's prospects
to seek new opportunities elsewhere, often westward

Examples

After losing his job, John decided to go west and start over in California.
When gold was discovered out west, many families chose to go west seeking fortune.
If their town doesn't recover, more people will likely go west for new beginnings.

Synset Examples

If the business fails, he might have to go west for new prospects.
She decided to go west in search of adventure.
When opportunities dry up, it's time to go west.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: head west - to travel or move in a westerly direction
