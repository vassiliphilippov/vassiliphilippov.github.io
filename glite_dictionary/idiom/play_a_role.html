to play a role

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ə ɹoʊl / (AmE), / pleɪ ə rəʊl / (BrE)
to participate in or have a significant influence on a particular situation or outcome
to participate or influence

Examples

Teachers play a role in shaping the values of society.
Her experience will play a role in the committee's decision.
We all play a role in protecting the environment.

Synset Examples

Teachers play a role in shaping students' futures.
She hopes to play a role in the upcoming project.
Each citizen plays a role in building the nation.
Her ethnicity plays a significant role in her identity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to contribute - to help to cause or bring about something
  • Synonym: to play a part - to be involved in or have an effect on a situation
