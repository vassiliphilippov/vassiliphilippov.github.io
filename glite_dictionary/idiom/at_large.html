at large

1. ADJ

/ ət ˈlɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ət ˈlɑːdʒ / (BrE)
having escaped from confinement or not yet captured, especially used of criminals or dangerous animals
escaped; not captured (esp. criminal)

Examples

The suspect remains at large after escaping from police custody last night.
Authorities warned that the wild animal is still at large in the area.
Several dangerous criminals are at large following the prison break.

Synset Examples

a convict still at large
searching for two escaped prisoners
dogs loose on the streets
criminals on the loose in the neighborhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on the loose - having escaped from confinement or not under control
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01065803-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADV

/ ət ˈlɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ət ˈlɑːdʒ / (BrE)
1. As a whole; in general, especially referring to groups or the public. 2. Not captured; remaining free, especially after escaping or avoiding arrest.
in general; not captured

Examples

The community at large has shown support for the new policy.
The escaped prisoner is still at large and considered dangerous.
These issues concern society at large, not just a single group.

Synset Examples

The community is at large discussing the upcoming event.
The festival attracts visitors at large from various regions.
The suspect is still at large after the incident.
The at-large suspect was finally captured by police.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on the loose - not captured; having escaped confinement

Etymology

From Middle English, where 'large' could mean 'free, at liberty.' Used since the 14th century in both senses.
