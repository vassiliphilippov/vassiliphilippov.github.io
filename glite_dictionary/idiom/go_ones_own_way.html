to go one's own way

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ wʌnz oʊn weɪ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ wʌnz əʊn weɪ / (BrE)
to act independently or follow one's own course in life, especially when it differs from what others do or expect
act independently; follow one's own path

Examples

After college, she decided to go her own way and start a business instead of working for someone else.
Despite his parents' advice, Mark chose to go his own way and travel the world.
Sometimes, it's important to go your own way and trust your instincts.

Synset Examples

He chose to go his own way in life.
She decided to go her own way after the disagreement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be independent - to not rely on others; act on one's own
