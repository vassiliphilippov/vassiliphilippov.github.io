best-laid plan

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɛst leɪd ˈplæn / (AmE), / ˌbɛst leɪd ˈplæn / (BrE)
an expression meaning that even the most carefully prepared plans can go wrong
carefully made plans that may fail

Examples

No matter how detailed your best-laid plans are, unexpected problems can still arise.
She was frustrated when her best-laid plans for the trip fell through at the last moment.
It’s a reminder that even the best-laid plans may not work out exactly as expected.

Synset Examples

Her best laid plans fell apart at the last minute.
Even the best laid plans can go awry.
They learned that the best laid plans often change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plan - a set of intended actions to achieve something

Etymology

From the line 'the best-laid schemes o' mice an' men' in the 1786 poem by Robert Burns.
