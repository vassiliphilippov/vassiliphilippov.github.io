to bury one's head in the sand

1. VERB

/ ˈbɛri wʌnz hɛd ɪn ðə sænd / (AmE), / ˈbɛri wʌnz hɛd ɪn ðə sand / (BrE)
to deliberately ignore or avoid facing a difficult or unpleasant situation
ignore or avoid facing problems

Examples

He always buries his head in the sand when it comes to financial problems.
Instead of burying your head in the sand, try to address the issue directly.
Too many people bury their heads in the sand and hope difficulties will go away.

Synset Examples

She acted like an ostrich, burying her head in the sand.
Burying your head in the sand won't solve the problem.
You can't just bury your head in the sand about this issue.
Don't bury your head in the sand; face the reality.
She puts her head in the sand when things get tough.
He tends to put his head in the sand about bills.
Don't put your head in the sand; face the problem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn a blind eye - to deliberately ignore something bad or wrong
  • Antonym: to face the music - to confront the unpleasant consequences of one's actions

Etymology

From the mistaken belief that ostriches hide their heads in the sand when threatened.
