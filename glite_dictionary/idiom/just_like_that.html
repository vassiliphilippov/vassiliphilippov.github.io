just like that

1. ADV

/ dʒʌst laɪk ðæt / (AmE), / dʒʌst laɪk ðat / (BrE)
used to indicate that something happens suddenly, unexpectedly, or with no apparent effort
suddenly or effortlessly

Examples

She quit her job just like that, leaving everyone in shock.
He fixed the car just like that, as if it were the easiest thing in the world.
The rain stopped just like that and the sun came out.

Synset Examples

And just like that, poof! The surprise was revealed.
The deal was closed just like that, no fuss.
She dismissed the idea just like that.
He solved the problem just like that!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suddenly - quickly and without warning
  • Synonym: instantly - immediately, without delay
