to break one's lance

1. VERB

/ breɪk wʌnz læns / (AmE), / breɪk wʌnz lɑːns / (BrE)
to engage in a noble struggle or fight passionately for a cause, often with chivalrous or honorable intentions
fight nobly for a cause
archaic

Examples

Many activists are willing to break their lance for environmental justice.
In the courtroom, she broke her lance to defend the rights of the oppressed.
He was known to break his lance whenever he saw injustice.

Synset Examples

He decided to break his lance for a worthy cause.
They will break their lance for environmental protection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: champion - to support or defend a cause vigorously

Etymology

From the medieval practice of jousting, where knights would break their lance as a sign of combat.
