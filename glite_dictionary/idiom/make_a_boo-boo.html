to make a boo-boo

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə ˈbuː buː / (AmE), / meɪk ə ˈbuː buː / (BrE)
to make a mistake, especially a minor or silly one
make a mistake (informal, childish)
informal

Examples

I accidentally sent the email to the wrong person—I really made a boo-boo this time.
Don't be too hard on yourself; everyone makes a boo-boo now and then.
She realized she made a boo-boo when she spilled the juice on the carpet.

Synset Examples

Don't worry, everyone makes a boo-boo sometimes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: make a mistake - to do something incorrectly or wrong
