to spill one's guts

1. VERB

/ spɪl wʌnz ɡʌts / (AmE), / spɪl wʌnz ɡʌts / (BrE)
to reveal personal thoughts, emotions, or secrets, often in a candid or unrestrained way
reveal secrets or emotions
informal

Examples

After hours of questioning, he finally spilled his guts and told the truth.
Whenever she feels overwhelmed, she calls her best friend to spill her guts.
He didn't want to spill his guts about what happened at work, but eventually he did.

Synset Examples

She finally decided to spill her guts about her feelings.
He felt better after deciding to spill his guts to her.
It took a while for him to spill his guts about his past.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confess - to admit or disclose something private or hidden
  • Antonym: to keep quiet - to not share information; to remain silent
