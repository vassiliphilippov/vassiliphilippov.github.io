to get one's kicks

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt wʌnz kɪks / (AmE), / ɡɛt wʌnz kɪks / (BrE)
to obtain enjoyment, excitement, or pleasure from something
derive enjoyment or excitement
informal

Examples

She gets her kicks from hiking in the mountains every weekend.
Many teenagers get their kicks by watching scary movies together.
He used to get his kicks riding his motorcycle through the desert.

Synset Examples

He gets his kicks from riding his motorcycle.
They get their kicks from extreme sports.
She loves to get her kicks by trying new foods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: have fun - to enjoy oneself or have a good time
  • Synonym: get a thrill - to experience excitement or pleasure

Etymology

From 'kick' as slang for excitement or thrill, popularized in American English in the mid-20th century.
