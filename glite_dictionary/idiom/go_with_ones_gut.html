to go with one's gut

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ wɪð wʌnz ɡʌt / (AmE), / ɡəʊ wɪð wʌnz ɡʌt / (BrE)
to act according to one's instinct or intuition rather than careful thought or analysis
act on instinct or intuition

Examples

Even though the plan sounded risky, she decided to go with her gut and make the investment.
When making tough choices, it's often best to go with your gut instead of overanalyzing.
He usually weighs every option, but this time he chose to go with his gut and it worked out.

Synset Examples

She decided to go with her gut and take the job offer.
Sometimes, you just have to go with your gut and trust yourself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trust one's instincts - rely on one's own intuition or feeling
  • Antonym: to overthink - analyze a situation in excessive detail, often to one's detriment
