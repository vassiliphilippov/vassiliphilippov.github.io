worth the powder

1. ADJ

/ wɜrθ ðə ˈpaʊdər / (AmE), / wɜːθ ðə ˈpaʊdə / (BrE)
Not worth the effort, trouble, or resources required.
not worth the effort or trouble
archaic

Examples

He decided the project was not worth the powder and abandoned it early.
Many believed the repairs were hardly worth the powder needed to fix such an old car.
If the reward is so small, it isn't worth the powder to complain about it.

Synset Examples

The argument was not worth the powder it took.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worth the trouble - not worth making the effort for

Etymology

Refers to gunpowder; originally meant not worth using powder to shoot at.
