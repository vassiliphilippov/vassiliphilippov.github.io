deep down

1. ADV

/ dip daʊn / (AmE), / diːp daʊn / (BrE)
used to say that something is true at a fundamental or underlying level, even if it is not openly admitted or apparent.
at a fundamental or underlying level

Examples

Deep down, I think she still misses her hometown despite loving city life.
He acts tough, but deep down he's really quite sensitive.
You might pretend not to care, but deep down it bothers you.

Synset Examples

Deep down, I believe everything will turn out fine.
He felt insecure, but deep down he was confident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at heart - used to refer to someone's true nature or feelings
  • Antonym: on the surface - appearing in a certain way outwardly; not at a deep level
