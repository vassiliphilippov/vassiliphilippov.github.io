in Dutch

1. ADV

/ ɪn dʌtʃ / (AmE), / ɪn dʌtʃ / (BrE)
in trouble or disfavor, especially with authority or someone in charge
in trouble or disfavor

Examples

After missing the important meeting, John was in Dutch with his supervisor.
If you forget their anniversary, you'll really be in Dutch.
The kids knew they'd be in Dutch once their parents found out about the broken vase.

Synset Examples

She’s been in Dutch ever since her last mistake.
They were in Dutch after the argument about the project.
He found himself in Dutch with his boss after missing the deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in hot water - in trouble or difficulty
  • Synonym: out of favor - not approved of or liked by someone in authority

Etymology

Possible reference to historical Anglo-Dutch rivalry; in early American slang, 'Dutch' often referred to trouble or awkward situations.
