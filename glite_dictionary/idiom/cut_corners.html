to cut corners

1. VERB

/ kʌt ˈkɔrnərz / (AmE), / kʌt ˈkɔːnəz / (BrE)
to do something in the easiest or cheapest way, often by ignoring rules or not following the correct procedure, usually resulting in lower quality or standards
do something quickly or cheaply, lowering standards

Examples

Some companies try to cut corners to reduce costs, but it can lead to poor quality products.
If we cut corners on this project, the final result won't meet our usual standards.
The builder was accused of cutting corners when he rushed to finish the house ahead of schedule.

Synset Examples

Cut corners to make a cheaper product

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to skimp - to use less time, money, or material than is necessary, often producing a poorer result

2. VERB

/ kʌt ˈkɔrnɚz / (AmE), / kʌt ˈkɔːnəz / (BrE)
to do something in a way that saves time, effort, or money, often by compromising quality or ignoring rules
do something badly to save effort or cost

Examples

The builder tried to cut corners and the result was a poorly constructed house.
If you cut corners when preparing the report, important details may be missed.
They were criticized for cutting corners to finish the project on time.

Synset Examples

We can't cut corners on quality for this project.

In this project, we can't afford to pick corners.

Cutting corners is bad business for any company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take shortcuts - do something in a quicker or easier way than usual, often with less care
