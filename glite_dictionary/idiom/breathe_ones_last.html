to breathe one's last

1. VERB

/ briːð wʌnz læst / (AmE), / briːð wʌnz lɑːst / (BrE)
to die; to draw one's final breath
to die (idiom)

Examples

After a long illness, the poet finally breathed his last at the age of eighty.
As the flames consumed the house, the old piano seemed to breathe its last.
Surrounded by her children, she breathed her last in the quiet of the night.

Synset Examples

She knew she would breathe her last in peace.
He breathed his last surrounded by family.
The old tree breathed its last after the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: die - to stop living; to cease to exist
  • Synonym: to pass away - to die (polite or euphemistic)
  • Antonym: to be born - to come into existence at birth
