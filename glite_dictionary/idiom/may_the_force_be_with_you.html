may the Force be with you

1. X

/ meɪ ðə fɔrs bi wɪð ju / (AmE), / meɪ ðə fɔːs bi wɪð juː / (BrE)
used to wish someone good luck or success, often in a playful or supportive way, originally from the Star Wars films
expression wishing luck or support (Star Wars)

Examples

Before his final exam, she smiled and said, 'may the Force be with you.'
After shaking his hand, the coach whispered, 'may the Force be with you out there.'
He ended his email to the team with, 'may the Force be with you.'

Synset Examples

In his speech, he warned rivals, 'May the Force not be with you today!'
He smiled and replied, 'May the Force be with you always.'
With a wink, she said, 'May the Force not be with you during the exam.'
Before the big game, she whispered, 'May the Force be with you.'
As he boarded the ship, his friends said, 'May the Force be with you.'
In a playful mood, he said, 'If you insist, may the Force be without you.'
She laughed and replied, 'Fine, may the Force be without you!'
He smirked and said, 'May the Force not be with you in this challenge!'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: good luck - an expression used to wish success or fortune

Etymology

Popularized by the Star Wars film series (from 1977), where 'the Force' is a mystical energy.
