to darken a church door

1. VERB

/ ˈdɑrkən ə tʃɜrtʃ dɔr / (AmE), / ˈdɑːkən ə tʃɜːtʃ dɔː / (BrE)
to enter a church, often with the implication of reluctance, shame, or wrongdoing.
enter a church (reluctantly or shamefully)

Examples

She never thought she would darken a church door after her childhood experiences.
He promised not to darken a church door unless his daughter was getting married.
Many in the town gossip that he has not darkened a church door in years.

Synset Examples

After their argument, she refused to darken a church door.
He vowed never to darken a church door again.
He felt guilty for having darkened a church door.
