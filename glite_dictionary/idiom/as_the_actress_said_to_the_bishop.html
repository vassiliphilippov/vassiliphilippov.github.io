as the actress said to the bishop

1. X

/ æz ði ˈæktrəs sɛd tə ðə ˈbɪʃəp / (AmE), / əz ði ˈæktrɪs sɛd tʊ ðə ˈbɪʃəp / (BrE)
used humorously to draw attention to a potentially risqué double meaning or innuendo in what was just said
humorous phrase highlighting innuendo
british informal

Examples

"It's quite hard to handle," as the actress said to the bishop.
They all burst out laughing when he casually added, as the actress said to the bishop.
Mind the gap, as the actress said to the bishop.

Synset Examples

This is complicated, as the actress said to the bishop.
Well, that’s a funny story, as the actress said to the bishop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: that's what she said - a similar North American phrase used to point out innuendo

Etymology

British English idiom; emerged in the early 20th century as a play on double entendre.
