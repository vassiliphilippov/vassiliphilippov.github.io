cheers, big ears

1. INTJ

/ tʃɪrz bɪɡ ɪrz / (AmE), / tʃɪəz bɪɡ ɪəz / (BrE)
A playful or teasing way of saying goodbye, often used among friends.
playful teasing farewell
informal british

Examples

As he left the café, Tom called out, 'cheers, big ears!' to his mate across the street.
Whenever we say goodbye after a silly argument, it's always 'cheers, big ears!' between us.
She waved from the car window and shouted, 'cheers, big ears!' before driving off.

Synset Examples

He said, 'Cheers, big ears!' after I caught him eavesdropping.
Whenever I talk loudly, my friend teases me with 'cheers, big ears!'
I can hear you, cheers, big ears!
