to step on a rake

1. VERB

/ stɛp ɑn ə reɪk / (AmE), / stɛp ɒn ə reɪk / (BrE)
to encounter unexpected problems or negative consequences, often as a result of one's own actions or oversight
face unexpected consequences

Examples

If we change suppliers without proper research, we may step on a rake and cause delivery delays.
He didn't review the contract details and ended up stepping on a rake when unexpected fees appeared.
The company stepped on a rake by ignoring early warning signs from the market.

Synset Examples

He stepped on a rake and got hit in the face.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shoot oneself in the foot - to harm one's own interests by mistake
  • Synonym: backfire - to have the opposite result from what was intended, causing damage
