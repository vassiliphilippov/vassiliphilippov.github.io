to bring to justice

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ tə ˈdʒʌstɪs / (AmE), / brɪŋ tə ˈdʒʌstɪs / (BrE)
to make someone face legal proceedings and be punished for a crime or wrongdoing
hold legally accountable; prosecute

Examples

After years on the run, the fugitive was finally brought to justice by the authorities.
Human rights activists are working to bring to justice those responsible for war crimes.
The detective was determined to bring to justice whoever had committed the robbery.

Synset Examples

The community rallied to bring the perpetrator to justice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prosecute - to bring criminal charges against someone in a court of law
  • Antonym: to let off - to free someone from blame or punishment
