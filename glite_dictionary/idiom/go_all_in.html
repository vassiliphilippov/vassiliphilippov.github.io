to go all in

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ɔl ɪn / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɔːl ɪn / (BrE)
to commit oneself completely to a course of action, decision, or investment
commit fully to a course of action

Examples

After much deliberation, she decided to go all in and launch her own business.
If you want to succeed in this field, you really have to go all in.
The company is ready to go all in on renewable energy.

Synset Examples

If we're investing, we might as well go all in for a dime, in for a dollar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commit - to dedicate oneself to a cause or action
  • Antonym: to hedge one's bets - to avoid complete commitment to one option

Etymology

Originally from poker, where 'going all in' means betting all of one's chips.
