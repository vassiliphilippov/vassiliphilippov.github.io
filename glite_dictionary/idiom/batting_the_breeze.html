to batting the breeze

1. VERB

/ ˈbæt̬ɪŋ ðə briːz / (AmE), / ˈbætɪŋ ðə briːz / (BrE)
engaging in casual or aimless conversation
casual conversation; chatting idly
informal

Examples

We sat on the porch batting the breeze long into the evening.
Whenever they meet up, they end up batting the breeze about nothing in particular.
She enjoys batting the breeze with her neighbors on weekend mornings.

Synset Examples

We spent hours just batting the breeze at the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shooting the breeze - engaging in casual, light conversation
  • Synonym: chitchatting - talking informally about unimportant things
