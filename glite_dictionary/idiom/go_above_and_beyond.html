to go above and beyond

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ əˈbʌv ənd bɪˈjɑnd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈbʌv ənd bɪˈjɒnd / (BrE)
to do more than what is required or expected; to make an extra effort
do more than expected

Examples

Our teachers often go above and beyond to help students succeed.
She really went above and beyond in organizing the charity event.
We appreciate employees who go above and beyond what their job descriptions require.

Synset Examples

Good doctors often go above and beyond for patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exceed expectations - to do more than what is expected
  • Antonym: to fall short - to fail to meet expectations or requirements
