to cry in one's beer

1. VERB

/ kraɪ ɪn wʌnz bɪr / (AmE), / kraɪ ɪn wʌnz bɪə / (BrE)
to express sadness, regret, or self-pity in a self-indulgent way, especially by complaining or dwelling on one's troubles
express self-pity or sadness, often by complaining

Examples

He tends to cry in his beer whenever things don't go his way at work.
There's no use crying in your beer—let's figure out a solution instead.
After the breakup, she spent the evening crying in her beer with her friends.

Synset Examples

He always cries in his beer after a tough week.
After losing the game, he sat alone, crying in his beer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feel sorry for oneself - to indulge in self-pity or dwell on one's own misfortunes

2. VERB

/ kraɪ ɪn wʌnz bɪr / (AmE), / kraɪ ɪn wʌnz bɪə / (BrE)
to express sorrow or self-pity, often by complaining about one's troubles, usually in a way that seeks sympathy from others
complain or feel sorry for oneself
informal

Examples

Stop crying in your beer and do something about the situation.
He spent the evening crying in his beer after losing his job.
Nobody likes to hear someone cry in their beer all night.

Synset Examples

She didn't want to cry in her beer, so she left the bar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to complain - to express dissatisfaction or unhappiness about something
  • Synonym: moan - to complain persistently, often with self-pity
