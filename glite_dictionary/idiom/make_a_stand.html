to make a stand

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə stænd / (AmE), / meɪk ə stænd / (BrE)
to adopt or express a firm position in support of or opposition to something, often in a public or decisive way
to take a firm position on an issue

Examples

Many activists decided to make a stand against the new policy.
It's sometimes necessary to make a stand even if it means facing criticism.
The community felt it was time to make a stand and demand better services.

Synset Examples

You need to plant your feet if you want to make a stand.
They will take a stand during the upcoming protests.
It's time to take a stand against bullying.
She decided to take a stand for environmental issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take a stand - to express or adopt a firm position on an issue
