broad in the beam

1. ADJ

/ brɔd ɪn ðə bim / (AmE), / brɔːd ɪn ðə biːm / (BrE)
having a wide width, especially used of a boat for stability, or sometimes referring humorously to a person's hips or body
having a wide width (esp. of a boat or body)

Examples

My uncle's old fishing boat is broad in the beam, making it very stable in rough waters.
Some swimmers are naturally broad in the beam, which can help with buoyancy.
She joked that after the holidays she was getting a bit broad in the beam.

Synset Examples

The boat was stable because it was broad in the beam.
He prefers boats that are broad in the beam for stability.
Her frame is broad in the beam, making her a strong swimmer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wide - having a great width
  • Antonym: narrow in the beam - having a small width, especially of a boat or body
