to pass the smell test

1. VERB

/ pæs ðə smɛl tɛst / (AmE), / pɑːs ðə smɛl tɛst / (BrE)
to be considered acceptable, credible, or satisfactory when judged by common standards or scrutiny
be acceptable or credible

Examples

The politician's explanation didn't pass the smell test for most listeners.
If your business plan can't pass the smell test, investors won't be interested.
The contract looked suspicious and didn't pass the smell test for our legal team.

Synset Examples

Her dish didn’t pass the smell test at the dinner party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stand up to scrutiny - to be able to withstand careful examination without fault being found
  • Synonym: to hold water - to remain logical or sound under examination
  • Antonym: to raise suspicion - to seem questionable or dubious
