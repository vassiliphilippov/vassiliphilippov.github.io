skinful of drink

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɪnfʊl əv drɪŋk / (AmE), / ˈskɪnfʊl əv drɪŋk / (BrE)
an amount of alcohol that is enough to make someone drunk; a large quantity of alcoholic drinks consumed
large amount of alcohol consumed
informal

Examples

After a skinful of drink, he could barely walk straight.
She regretted having a skinful of drink the night before the interview.
They were in high spirits after getting a skinful of drink at the pub.

Synset Examples

After a skinful of drinks, they stumbled home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skinful - an amount of alcohol that is enough to make someone drunk

Etymology

From 'skinful', originally referring to the amount a skin (animal hide flask) could hold.
