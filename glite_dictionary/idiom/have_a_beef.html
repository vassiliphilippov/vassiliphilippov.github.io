to have a beef

1. VERB

/ hæv ə bif / (AmE), / hæv ə biːf / (BrE)
to have a complaint or a grievance about something
have a complaint or grievance
informal american

Examples

If you have a beef with the decision, you should bring it up at the meeting.
She told me she had a beef with the new manager's attitude.
Whenever he's upset, he usually has a beef about something at work.

Synset Examples

He's always complaining; he has a beef with everyone.
I have a beef with the way they handled the situation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance
  • Synonym: have a gripe - to have a complaint
  • Antonym: be satisfied - to have no complaint; to be content

Etymology

American slang, likely derived from 19th-century U.S. English, where 'beef' meant 'complaint'.
