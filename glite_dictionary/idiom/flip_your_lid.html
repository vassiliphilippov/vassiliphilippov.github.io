to flip your lid

1. VERB

/ flɪp jʊr lɪd / (AmE), / flɪp jɔː lɪd / (BrE)
to suddenly become very angry or lose control of your emotions
suddenly become very angry
informal

Examples

If you interrupt him now, he might flip your lid over the unfinished work.
She almost flipped her lid when she found out about the mistake.
Try not to flip your lid if the train is late again.

Synset Examples

Don't flip your lid; it's just a small mistake.
He flipped his lid when he saw the mess.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lose your temper - to become very angry suddenly
  • Antonym: keep your cool - to remain calm when upset or angry
