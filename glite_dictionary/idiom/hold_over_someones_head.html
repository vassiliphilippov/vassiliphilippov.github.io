to hold over someone's head

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ˈoʊvɚ ˈsʌmwʌnz hɛd / (AmE), / həʊld ˈəʊvə ˈsʌmwʌnz hɛd / (BrE)
to use something, such as a past mistake or secret, as leverage or a threat to control or influence someone
to use something as a threat or leverage

Examples

She threatened to hold his secret over his head if he ever left her.
It's not fair to hold that mistake over his head for years.
He realized she had been holding the incident over his head to get what she wanted.

Synset Examples

He felt she was holding the argument over his head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackmail - the action of demanding money or another benefit from someone in return for not revealing compromising information about them
  • Antonym: to forgive - to stop feeling angry or resentful toward someone for an offense or mistake
