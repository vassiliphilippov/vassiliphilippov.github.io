in the sticks

1. ADV

/ ɪn ðə stɪks / (AmE), / ɪn ðə stɪks / (BrE)
in a remote or sparsely populated rural area, far from cities or towns
in a remote rural area
informal american

Examples

They used to live in the sticks before moving to the city.
It's hard to get good internet service when you're in the sticks.
Even though it's peaceful in the sticks, some people miss the hustle of urban life.

Synset Examples

They decided to build their house in the sticks.
Living in the sticks has its own charm and challenges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backwoods - remote, undeveloped rural area
  • Synonym: boondocks - rough or isolated country

Etymology

Originally American slang from the early 20th century, referring to wooded rural areas ('sticks').
