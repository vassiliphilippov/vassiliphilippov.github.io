at will

1. ADV

/ æt wɪl / (AmE), / æt wɪl / (BrE)
whenever or as often as one pleases; without restriction or limitation
whenever or as often as one pleases

Examples

Employees are allowed to leave the building at will during lunch breaks.
He was able to summon the data at will from his computer.
Guests may come and go at will throughout the evening.

Synset Examples

he can roam the neighborhood at will

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freely - without restriction
  • Synonym: at one's discretion - according to one's own judgment

2. ADV

/ æt wɪl / (AmE), / ət wɪl / (BrE)
Whenever one wishes; as one pleases or chooses, without restriction.
whenever one wishes or chooses

Examples

You are free to come and go at will in this building.
She changed the settings at will to suit her preferences.
The door is open; enter at will.

Synset Examples

You can leave the meeting at will.
He can use the facilities at will.
Employees can take vacation days at will.
