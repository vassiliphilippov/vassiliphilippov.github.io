to play small

1. VERB

/ pleɪ smɔl / (AmE), / pleɪ smɔːl / (BrE)
to behave with less ambition or confidence than one is capable of; to limit one's potential or aspirations
to limit one's ambitions or potential
informal

Examples

She often plays small in meetings, even though she has great ideas to share.
Don't play small; you have the talent to lead this project.
They realized they had been playing small and decided to take more risks.

Synset Examples

Don't play small; your ideas deserve recognition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to play it safe - to act cautiously and avoid risk
