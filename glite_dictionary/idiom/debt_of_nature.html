debt of nature

1. NOUN

/ dɛt ʌv ˈneɪtʃɚ / (AmE), / dɛt əv ˈneɪtʃə / (BrE)
the inevitable obligation of death; the unavoidable fate that every living being must die
the inevitability of death

Examples

It is said that kings and beggars alike must pay the debt of nature.
After a long illness, he finally paid the debt of nature.
Writers throughout history have reflected on the debt of nature that all humans owe.

Synset Examples

She viewed helping others as a debt of nature.
She believes in living life fully before paying the debt of nature.
The community paid their debt of nature by planting trees.
Everyone must eventually pay the debt of nature.
He felt a debt of nature to care for his aging parents.
He lived a full life and recently paid the debt of nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: die - to stop living; to cease to exist as a living being

Etymology

From Latin 'debitum naturae' meaning 'debt to nature', historically used to refer to the certainty of death.
