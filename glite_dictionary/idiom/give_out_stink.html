to give out stink

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv aʊt stɪŋk / (AmE), / ɡɪv aʊt stɪŋk / (BrE)
to complain loudly or express dissatisfaction; or to emit a strong, unpleasant odor.
complain noisily or emit a bad smell
informal

Examples

When the new policy was announced, several employees began to give out stink to management.
If you leave those leftovers any longer, they're really going to give out stink.
She didn't hesitate to give out stink when she thought something was unfair.

Synset Examples

He gave out stink about the unfair rules.
The garbage is starting to give out stink.
The spoiled food began to give out stink in the fridge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance
  • Synonym: stink - emit a strong, unpleasant smell
