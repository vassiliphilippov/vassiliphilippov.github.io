hot potato

1. NOUN

/ hɑt pəˈteɪɾoʊ / (AmE), / hɒt pəˈteɪtəʊ / (BrE)
a subject or situation that is difficult or controversial and which people avoid dealing with
controversial or difficult situation

Examples

The issue of tax reform has become a real hot potato in Congress.
She handled the hot potato of staff layoffs with surprising tact.
Whenever funding for the arts comes up, it turns into a political hot potato.

Synset Examples

he dropped the topic like a hot potato

Related Terms

  • Synonym: touchy subject - a topic that is sensitive or likely to cause disagreement
  • Synonym: minefield - a situation presenting many difficulties or dangers
  • Antonym: open-and-shut case - a case or situation that is easy to decide or solve

2. NOUN

/ hɑt pəˈteɪɾoʊ / (AmE), / hɒt pəˈteɪtəʊ / (BrE)
a controversial or awkward issue that is difficult to deal with or pass on to someone else
controversial or awkward issue

Examples

The new tax plan became a hot potato in Parliament, with no one eager to discuss it.
Her controversial comment turned into a hot potato at the press conference.
Management quickly passed the hot potato of budget cuts to the finance department.

Synset Examples

That issue is a hot potato in the board meeting.
The topic became a hot potato among the team.
No one wants to handle that hot potato.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: touchy subject - an issue that is sensitive or likely to cause discomfort
  • Synonym: can of worms - a complex or troublesome issue

Etymology

Refers metaphorically to a literal hot potato, which is difficult to hold and quickly passed on; figurative use from the 19th century.
