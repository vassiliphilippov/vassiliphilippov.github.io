to trip the light fantastic toe

1. VERB

/ trɪp ðə laɪt fænˈtæstɪk toʊ / (AmE), / trɪp ðə laɪt fænˈtæstɪk təʊ / (BrE)
to dance, especially in a lively or elegant manner, often to music
to dance in a lively way
archaic

Examples

At the wedding, the couple decided to trip the light fantastic toe to their favorite song.
She loves nothing more than to trip the light fantastic toe at every party she attends.
In the musical, the entire cast trips the light fantastic toe during the grand finale.

Synset Examples

My husband and I like to dance at home to the radio

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dance - to move rhythmically to music

Etymology

Originally from John Milton's 1645 poem 'L’Allegro': 'Come and trip it as ye go, On the light fantastick toe.'
