to jump down someone's throat

1. VERB

/ dʒʌmp daʊn ˈsʌmwʌnz θroʊt / (AmE), / dʒʌmp daʊn ˈsʌmwənz θrəʊt / (BrE)
to respond to someone with sudden anger or criticism, often over a minor issue
react angrily or defensively

Examples

Whenever I make a small mistake at work, my boss jumps down my throat immediately.
She apologized quickly, not wanting him to jump down her throat again.
There's no reason to jump down my throat just because I asked a question.

Synset Examples

I just suggested a change, and he jumped down my throat.
When I asked for help, he jumped down my throat instead.
There's no need to jump down her throat over a small mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snap at someone - to speak to someone sharply or angrily
