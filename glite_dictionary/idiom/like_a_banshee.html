like a banshee

1. ADV

/ laɪk ə ˈbænʃi / (AmE), / laɪk ə ˈbænʃi / (BrE)
(of a person) in a way that is very loud and uncontrolled, often describing crying or shouting.
very loudly and uncontrollably (cry, shout, etc.)

Examples

When the clock struck midnight, the children ran around the house like a banshee.
The baby was crying like a banshee all night, keeping the neighbors awake.
He cheered like a banshee when his favorite team scored the winning goal.

Synset Examples

She cried like a banshee when her team lost.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screaming - to make a loud, high-pitched cry
  • Synonym: wailing - to make a prolonged, high-pitched cry of pain, grief, or anger

Etymology

From banshee, a female spirit in Irish folklore whose wailing is believed to foretell a death.
