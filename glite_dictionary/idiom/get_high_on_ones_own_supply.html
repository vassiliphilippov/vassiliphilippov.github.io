to get high on one's own supply

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt haɪ ɑn wʌnz oʊn səˈplaɪ / (AmE), / ɡɛt haɪ ɒn wʌnz əʊn səˈplaɪ / (BrE)
to become excessively self-satisfied or deluded by relying too much on one's own products, resources, or ideas, often to one's detriment
to believe too much in one's own work or success
informal

Examples

It's easy for entrepreneurs to get high on their own supply and ignore market realities.
The critic warned the company not to get high on its own supply after a string of successes.
As a writer, try not to get high on your own supply and stay open to feedback.

Synset Examples

Don't get high on your own supply; stay grounded in reality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overconfident - excessively confident; too sure of oneself
  • Synonym: self-deluded - having an unrealistically positive view of oneself or one's work

Etymology

Popularized in the 1990s, originally referencing drug culture and later adopted metaphorically in business and creative fields.
