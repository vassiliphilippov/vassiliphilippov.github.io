to drop one's bundle

1. VERB

/ drɑp wʌnz ˈbʌndəl / (AmE), / drɒp wʌnz ˈbʌndəl / (BrE)
to lose control or composure; to become extremely upset or distressed, often feeling overwhelmed or defeated
lose composure or become very upset

Examples

She really dropped her bundle during the presentation and forgot what she was saying.
After hearing the bad news, he dropped his bundle and started to cry.
I nearly dropped my bundle when I saw the amount of work I had left to do.

Synset Examples

She really dropped her bundle during the presentation.
He dropped his bundle when he heard the news.
After the exam, I felt like I dropped my bundle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lose one's cool - to lose temper or composure suddenly
  • Synonym: to fall apart - to lose emotional or mental stability
  • Antonym: to keep one's cool - to remain calm and in control of oneself

Etymology

Originally Australian English, referring to literally dropping one's bundle of belongings, later figuratively meaning to lose mental composure.
