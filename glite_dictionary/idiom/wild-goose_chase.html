wild-goose chase

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪld ˌɡus ˌtʃeɪs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪld ˌɡuːs ˌtʃeɪs / (BrE)
a search or pursuit that is hopeless or pointless because the goal cannot be attained
hopeless or pointless pursuit

Examples

After hours tracking bad directions, we realized the entire trip was a wild-goose chase.
Chasing the old urban legend turned out to be nothing but a wild-goose chase.
He sent her on a wild-goose chase looking for documents that didn't exist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool's errand - a task that has no hope of success
  • Synonym: dead end - a situation or process that leads nowhere

Etymology

First recorded in the late 16th century, originally describing a type of erratic horse race in which riders followed a lead rider as wild geese fly in formation.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪld ˌɡus ˌtʃeɪs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪld ˌɡuːs ˌtʃeɪs / (BrE)
A search or pursuit that is hopeless, pointless, or impossible to achieve.
futile or hopeless pursuit

Examples

After hours of searching, we realized we had been sent on a wild-goose chase.
Looking for his keys at the crowded beach turned out to be a wild-goose chase.
The detective refused to waste time on what he believed was just a wild-goose chase.

Synset Examples

We went on a wild-goose chase looking for that restaurant.
Trying to find the lost cat was a wild-goose chase.
Searching for that missing file turned into a wild-goose chase.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool's errand - a task or activity that is a waste of time or has no hope of success

Etymology

Originated in the late 16th century, originally referring to a type of horse race in which horses followed a lead rider over a course like a flock of geese.
