to dampen spirits

1. VERB

/ ˈdæmpən ˈspɪrɪts / (AmE), / ˈdæmpən ˈspɪrɪts / (BrE)
to make someone less enthusiastic or less cheerful
reduce enthusiasm or happiness

Examples

The rainy weather did little to dampen spirits at the outdoor concert.
Criticism from her supervisor could not dampen her spirits as she pursued her project.
News of the cancellation failed to completely dampen spirits among the students.

Synset Examples

Sunlessness during the holidays can dampen spirits.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lift spirits - make someone feel happier or more cheerful
