to fish or cut bait

1. VERB

/ fɪʃ ɔr kʌt beɪt / (AmE), / fɪʃ ɔː kʌt beɪt / (BrE)
to make a decision to either take action or withdraw; to choose between acting or stepping aside
decide to act or withdraw

Examples

We've discussed this for hours—it's time to fish or cut bait.
She told the team to fish or cut bait regarding the new proposal.
If you can't commit, you need to fish or cut bait and let someone else take over.

Synset Examples

It's time to fish or cut bait on this project.
You need to decide if you want to commit, fish or cut bait.
When it comes to your career, it's fish or cut bait now.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: make up your mind - decide what to do
  • Antonym: to hesitate - pause before taking action

Etymology

Originally from fishing: 'fish' (try to catch fish) or 'cut bait' (prepare bait for others to fish), meaning to act or let others proceed. Now used generally for making a decision.
