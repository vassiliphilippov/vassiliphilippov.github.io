to run the gauntlet

1. VERB

/ rʌn ðə ˈɡɔnt.lət / (AmE), / rʌn ðə ˈɡɔːnt.lɪt / (BrE)
to be subjected to a series of difficult or unpleasant experiences or criticisms
endure many challenges or criticisms

Examples

After revealing his controversial opinion, he had to run the gauntlet of public criticism.
The new employee was forced to run the gauntlet of tough questions from senior staff.
During the campaign, she bravely ran the gauntlet of negative media coverage.

Synset Examples

He ran the gauntlet of criticism after the project failed.
They must run the gauntlet of obstacles to reach their goal.
Running the gauntlet of questions was exhausting.
She had to run the gauntlet of tough questions during the interview.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to face the music - accept unpleasant consequences or criticism
  • Synonym: to walk on eggshells - act very carefully to avoid upsetting someone or causing problems

Etymology

Originally referred to a military punishment in which someone was forced to run between two lines of people who struck them, from Swedish 'gata' (lane) and 'lopp' (course).
