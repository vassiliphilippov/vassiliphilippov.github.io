bang to rights

1. ADV

/ bæŋ tə raɪts / (AmE), / baŋ tə raɪts / (BrE)
caught in the act of committing a crime or wrongdoing, with definite proof or evidence
caught with clear evidence of wrongdoing
informal british

Examples

The thief was bang to rights when the CCTV footage was shown in court.
He knew he was bang to rights after they found the stolen goods in his bag.
They had her bang to rights with a list of witnesses ready to testify.

Synset Examples

She was bang to rights when they found her fingerprints.
They caught him bang to rights stealing the car.
The police had him bang to rights with the evidence.
