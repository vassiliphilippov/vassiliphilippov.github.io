to keep someone's pecker up

1. VERB

/ kip ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈpɛkər ʌp / (AmE), / kiːp ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈpɛkə ʌp / (BrE)
to remain cheerful and optimistic, especially in difficult situations
remain cheerful; stay optimistic
british informal

Examples

Whenever things get tough, try to keep your pecker up and look on the bright side.
She managed to keep her pecker up despite the disappointing news.
Their coach told them to keep their pecker up after the loss and prepare for the next game.

Synset Examples

He advised her to keep her pecker up and stay positive.
No matter what happens, just keep your pecker up.
Even in tough times, it's important to keep your pecker up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stay positive - to maintain a positive attitude
  • Antonym: lose heart - to feel discouraged

Etymology

British slang; 'pecker' here refers to the nose or head, historically meaning 'spirits' or mood.
