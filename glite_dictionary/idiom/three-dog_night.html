three-dog night

1. NOUN

/ ˌθri dɔɡ ˈnaɪt / (AmE), / ˌθri dɒɡ ˈnaɪt / (BrE)
an extremely cold night; a night so cold that multiple dogs are needed for warmth
an extremely cold night
informal

Examples

Last winter, we had a three-dog night that froze the pipes in the kitchen.
It’s going to be a three-dog night—better pile on the quilts!
Campers know to expect a three-dog night during the harshest months in the mountains.

Synset Examples

It was a three-dog night; we needed all the blankets.
On a three-dog night, I just stay in and read.
It felt like a three-dog night with that chilly wind.

Etymology

From Australian Aboriginal practice of sleeping with dogs for warmth; the phrase refers to needing three dogs to keep warm on a particularly cold night.
