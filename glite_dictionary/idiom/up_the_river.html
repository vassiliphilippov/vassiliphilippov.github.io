up the river

1. ADV

/ ʌp ðə ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ʌp ðə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
in a difficult or problematic situation; in trouble.
in trouble; facing difficulties
informal

Examples

If we don't get more funding soon, the whole project will be up the river.
Without his passport, he found himself up the river at the airport.
She realized they were up the river when their only car broke down in the middle of nowhere.

Synset Examples

If we miss the deadline, we're really up the river.
