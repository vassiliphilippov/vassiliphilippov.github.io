to watch one's mouth

1. VERB

/ wɑtʃ wʌnz maʊθ / (AmE), / wɒtʃ wʌnz maʊθ / (BrE)
to be careful about what one says, especially to avoid offending or angering others
be careful what you say

Examples

You should watch your mouth when talking to your boss.
After his outburst, his parents told him to watch his mouth at the dinner table.
If you don’t watch your mouth, you might get into trouble.

Synset Examples

He learned to watch his mouth after the last argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hold one's tongue - to remain silent or refrain from speaking
  • Antonym: to speak one's mind - to say exactly what one thinks without worrying about repercussions
