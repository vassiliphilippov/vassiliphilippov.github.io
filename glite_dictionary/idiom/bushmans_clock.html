bushman's clock

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊʃmənz klɑk / (AmE), / ˈbʊʃmənz klɒk / (BrE)
a humorous term for a natural or instinctive sense of timing or punctuality, sometimes used when someone is notably prompt or late
humorous term for a natural sense of time
informal british

Examples

He always wakes up at sunrise, relying only on his bushman's clock.
Liam joked that his bushman's clock made him the last to arrive at every meeting.
Without a watch, Sarah trusted her bushman's clock to estimate when it was lunchtime.

Synset Examples

He arrived right on time; guess he has a bushman's clock.
She always seems to be late; her bushman's clock is broken.
Their punctuality is impressive; they must follow a bushman's clock.

Etymology

Refers to the supposed keen time sense of bushmen; used informally in Australian and South African English.
