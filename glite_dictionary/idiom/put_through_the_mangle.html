to put through the mangle

1. VERB

/ pʊt θru ðə ˈmæŋɡəl / (AmE), / pʊt θruː ðə ˈmæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
to subject someone or something to a harsh, rigorous, or damaging process
subjected to a harsh process

Examples

My proposal was put through the mangle during the review meeting, but I finally got approval.
The manuscript was put through the mangle by the editor before publication.
After facing so many tough questions, he felt he had been put through the mangle.

Synset Examples

His report was put through the mangle before approval.
The old documents were put through the mangle for shredding.
After the presentation, my ideas were put through the mangle.

Etymology

Refers to the 'mangle', a machine used to squeeze water out of laundry, metaphorically indicating being pressured or battered.
