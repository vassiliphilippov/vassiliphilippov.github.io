to have its moments

1. VERB

/ hæv ɪts ˈmoʊmənts / (AmE), / hæv ɪts ˈməʊmənts / (BrE)
to have some good or notable parts or times, even if overall something is not outstanding or impressive
to have some notable or good parts

Examples

The concert had its moments, but overall the sound quality was poor.
This old comedy show has its moments of brilliance despite being dated.
The journey had its moments of excitement, though most of it was uneventful.

Synset Examples

The movie had its moments, but overall it was dull.
