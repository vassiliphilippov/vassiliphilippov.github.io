the last straw

1. NOUN

/ ðə læst strɑ / (AmE), / ðə lɑːst strɔː / (BrE)
the final in a series of problems or annoyances that causes someone to take action or lose patience
final problem causing a reaction

Examples

When they canceled my flight for a third time, it was the last straw and I demanded a refund.
Her coworker's constant criticism was irritating, but missing her bonus was the last straw.
For many voters, the tax increase was the last straw that made them switch parties.

Synset Examples

For her, that incident was the last straw.
That rude comment was the last straw.
Losing the game was the last straw for him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the straw that broke the camel's back - the minor or final burden that causes catastrophe or collapse

Etymology

From the proverb 'the last straw breaks the camel's back,' referencing a seemingly minor addition that causes a major reaction.
