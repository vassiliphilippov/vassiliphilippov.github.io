to hang up one's boots

1. VERB

/ hæŋ ʌp wʌnz buːts / (AmE), / hæŋ ʌp wʌnz buːts / (BrE)
to retire from a profession, activity, or career, especially in sports
retire from a profession or activity

Examples

After winning the championship, he decided to hang up his boots and focus on coaching.
Many athletes struggle with the decision to finally hang up their boots.
She knew it was time to hang up her boots and enjoy a quieter life.

Synset Examples

After twenty years in the league, he decided to hang up his boots.
It's time to hang up your boots and enjoy retirement.
She finally hung up her boots after a successful career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to retire - to leave one's job or stop working after reaching a certain age or completing a career

Etymology

From the literal act of hanging up sports boots upon retiring, especially in football and similar sports.
