so long, and thanks for all the fish

1. X

/ soʊ lɔŋ ənd θæŋks fər ɔl ðə fɪʃ / (AmE), / səʊ lɒŋ ənd θæŋks fə ɔːl ðə fɪʃ / (BrE)
Used humorously to say goodbye, especially when departing or leaving a situation, made famous by Douglas Adams' 'The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy'.
humorous farewell phrase

Examples

When he retired, Tom waved and said, 'so long, and thanks for all the fish,' making his coworkers laugh.
As the meeting ended abruptly, I joked, 'so long, and thanks for all the fish,' before leaving the room.
Bidding farewell to her classmates, she signed her yearbook with 'so long, and thanks for all the fish.'

Synset Examples

After the party, she exclaimed, 'So long, and thanks for all the fish!'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goodbye - an act of parting or saying farewell

Etymology

Popularized by Douglas Adams in 'The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy' (1984), where it is used as the dolphins' farewell to humanity.
