flying colours

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈkʌlɚz / (AmE), / ˈflaɪɪŋ ˈkʌləz / (BrE)
with complete success or triumph, often used in the phrase 'with flying colours'.
complete success; triumph

Examples

She passed her exams with flying colours and earned a scholarship.
The proposal was approved by the board with flying colours.
He completed the training course with flying colours, impressing all his instructors.

Synset Examples

they passed inspection with flying colors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triumph - a great victory or achievement
  • Antonym: failure - lack of success

Etymology

From naval tradition, referring to ships returning with their flags (colours) flying to signal victory.
