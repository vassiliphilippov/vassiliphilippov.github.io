to keep one's shoulder to the wheel

1. VERB

/ kip wʌnz ˈʃoʊldɚ tu ðə wil / (AmE), / kiːp wʌnz ˈʃəʊldə tuː ðə wiːl / (BrE)
to work hard and persistently, especially when faced with difficulty
work persistently; make sustained effort

Examples

Despite many setbacks, she continued to keep her shoulder to the wheel and eventually succeeded.
If you want to finish this project on time, you'll need to keep your shoulder to the wheel.
The team managed to keep their shoulder to the wheel throughout the busy season.

Synset Examples

She keeps plugging away at her dissertation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persevere - to persist in a course of action despite difficulty or delay
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop making an effort; to cease trying

Etymology

Allusion to the action of pushing a heavy cart or wagon by putting one's shoulder to the wheel.
