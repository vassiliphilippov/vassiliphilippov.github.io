to hedge one's bets

1. VERB

/ hɛdʒ wʌnz bɛts / (AmE), / hɛdʒ wʌnz bɛts / (BrE)
to protect oneself against loss or failure by supporting more than one possibility or by taking multiple precautions
protect against risk by multiple options

Examples

She decided to hedge her bets by keeping both job offers open until the last minute.
Many investors hedge their bets by spreading their money across various asset classes.
It's wise to hedge your bets when the outcome is so uncertain.

Synset Examples

He decided to hedge his bets by applying to several universities.
To hedge one's bets, she invested in multiple stocks.
It's smart to hedge your bets in a volatile market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to play it safe - to act cautiously to avoid risk
  • Antonym: to take a chance - to take a risk

Etymology

From the verb "hedge" (to limit or qualify), drawing on the literal sense of a hedge as a protective barrier, plus "bet" (a wager).
