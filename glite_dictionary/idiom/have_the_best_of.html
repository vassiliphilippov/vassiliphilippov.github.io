to have the best of

1. VERB

/ hæv ðə bɛst ʌv / (AmE), / hav ðə bɛst ɒv / (BrE)
to defeat or overcome (someone) through circumstances or factors beyond their control
to be defeated or overcome by something

Examples

Sometimes, nerves can have the best of even the most experienced speakers.
Don't let your emotions have the best of you during the negotiation.
Fatigue finally had the best of him after hours of hiking.

Synset Examples

Heart disease can get the best of us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get the better of - to overcome or outdo someone or something
