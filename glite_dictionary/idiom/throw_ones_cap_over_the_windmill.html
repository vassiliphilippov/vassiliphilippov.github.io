to throw one's cap over the windmill

1. VERB

/ θroʊ wʌnz kæp ˈoʊvɚ ðə ˈwɪndˌmɪl / (AmE), / θrəʊ wʌnz kæp ˈəʊvə ðə ˈwɪndmɪl / (BrE)
to make a decisive and irreversible commitment to a course of action, often one involving risk or boldness
make an irrevocable commitment

Examples

After months of hesitation, Maria finally threw her cap over the windmill and started her own company.
Deciding to move abroad was when he threw his cap over the windmill, leaving everything familiar behind.
They threw their cap over the windmill by announcing the project before securing all the necessary funding.

Synset Examples

She threw her cap over the windmill by quitting her job.
After years of saving, he finally threw his cap over the windmill.

Etymology

Attested from at least the 19th century; probably alludes to a symbolic gesture of boldness inspired by stories such as Don Quixote and traditional folk tales.
