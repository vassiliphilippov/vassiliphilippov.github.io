thus and such

1. X

/ ðʌs ənd sʌtʃ / (AmE), / ðʌs ənd sʌtʃ / (BrE)
used to refer vaguely to something previously mentioned or understood by context, often when the specific details are unimportant or forgotten
used to refer vaguely to something previously mentioned

Examples

During the interview, she said thus and such about her experience but didn't provide details.
Whenever I ask for specifics, he just mentions thus and such and quickly changes the subject.
In her report, she referred to thus and such as the causes of the delays.

Synset Examples

I remember you saying thus and such about the project.
We discussed the issue thus and such last week.
He mentioned thus and such during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: such and such - a way to refer vaguely to something or someone unspecified
