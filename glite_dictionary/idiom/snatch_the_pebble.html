to snatch the pebble

1. VERB

/ snætʃ ðə ˈpɛbəl / (AmE), / snætʃ ðə ˈpɛbəl / (BrE)
to demonstrate mastery or exceptional skill, often after intense learning or practice
achieve mastery or exceptional skill

Examples

After years of training, she was finally able to snatch the pebble and earn her teacher's respect.
When you snatch the pebble, you'll know you've reached true expertise.
'Young grasshopper, when you can snatch the pebble from my hand, it will be time for you to leave,' said the master.

Synset Examples

To win, you must learn to snatch the pebble swiftly.
In martial arts, you must snatch the pebble from my hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mastery - complete control or superior skill
  • Antonym: apprenticeship - the state of learning from a master; the period before 'snatching the pebble'

Etymology

Popularized from the 1970s television series 'Kung Fu', where a student becomes a master by snatching a pebble from the master's hand.
