two-a-penny

1. ADJ

/ tu ə ˈpɛni / (AmE), / tuː ə ˈpɛni / (BrE)
Very common and of little value; easily found and not considered special or important.
very common; of little worth
british

Examples

In today's market, smartphone apps are two-a-penny and rarely unique.
Honest politicians seem rare, but promises are two-a-penny during election season.
Critics say that such generic rom-com movies are two-a-penny these days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dime a dozen - very common and of little value (US equivalent)
