fire and brimstone

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚ ənd ˈbrɪmstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə ənd ˈbrɪmstəʊn / (BrE)
used to refer to the threat of hell and divine punishment, especially as preached in stern religious sermons; fierce or passionate warnings of damnation.
threat of hell and divine punishment
archaic

Examples

The preacher spoke of fire and brimstone, warning the sinners of God's wrath.
Her grandfather's fire and brimstone beliefs shaped his strict views on morality.
Many revival meetings were filled with fire and brimstone sermons to stir the congregation.

Synset Examples

his sermons were full of fire and brimstone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hellfire - the fire of hell; torments of the damned

Etymology

From the biblical phrase describing God's punishment involving fire and sulfur ('brimstone'), particularly in the Old Testament.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪər ənd ˈbrɪmstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə ənd ˈbrɪmstəʊn / (BrE)
the threat of severe punishment or hellfire, often used in reference to vivid descriptions of divine wrath or judgment, especially in religious preaching
intense punishment or hellfire (idiom)

Examples

The preacher warned the congregation about fire and brimstone if they strayed from the path of righteousness.
The novel's depiction of fire and brimstone painted a terrifying image of judgment day.
He grew up in a church where fire and brimstone sermons were delivered every Sunday.

Synset Examples

The preacher spoke of rain fire and brimstone in his sermon.

Etymology

From the biblical description of the destruction of Sodom and Gomorrah, where fire and brimstone (sulfur) rained from the sky.
