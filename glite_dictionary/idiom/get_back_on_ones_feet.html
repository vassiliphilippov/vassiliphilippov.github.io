to get back on one's feet

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt bæk ɑn wʌnz fit / (AmE), / ɡɛt bæk ɒn wʌnz fiːt / (BrE)
to recover from a difficult situation, especially after illness, financial trouble, or a setback
recover after difficulties

Examples

It took a few months, but she finally got back on her feet after losing her job.
The organization helps people get back on their feet after financial hardship.
He's determined to get back on his feet and rebuild his life.

Synset Examples

After the injury, he was back on his feet quickly.
He was proud to be back on his feet after struggling.
She finally got back on her feet after the setback.
He was once a rough sleeper before getting back on his feet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recover - to return to a normal state after a setback
