to nip in the bud

1. VERB

/ nɪp ɪn ðə bʌd / (AmE), / nɪp ɪn ðə bʌd / (BrE)
to stop something at an early stage before it develops or becomes a bigger problem
stop something early, before it grows

Examples

The company tried to nip the rumor in the bud before it spread throughout the office.
Parents should nip bad habits in the bud to prevent future problems.
If we nip this conflict in the bud, it won't turn into a crisis.

Synset Examples

We must nip this problem in the bud before it escalates.
She nipped their arguments in the bud immediately.
The teacher decided to nip the bullying in the bud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prevent - to stop something from happening or arising
  • Antonym: to encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone, often allowing growth or continuation

Etymology

From gardening, where removing a bud prevents further growth; attested since the 17th century.
