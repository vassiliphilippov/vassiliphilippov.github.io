to go steady

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈstɛdi / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈstɛdi / (BrE)
(of two people) to date each other exclusively, often with the implication of a committed romantic relationship.
date someone exclusively
informal

Examples

After a few dates, they decided to go steady and see each other exclusively.
In high school, it was common for couples to go steady and exchange class rings.
She asked him if he wanted to go steady, making their relationship official.

Synset Examples

She wants to go steady, but he's not ready.
They decided to go steady after dating for months.
I think they should go steady before moving in.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to break up - to end a romantic relationship
