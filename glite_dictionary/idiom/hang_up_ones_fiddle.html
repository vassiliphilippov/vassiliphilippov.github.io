to hang up one's fiddle

1. VERB

/ hæŋ ʌp wʌnz ˈfɪd.əl / (AmE), / hæŋ ʌp wʌnz ˈfɪd.əl / (BrE)
to quit or retire from an activity, especially from playing music
retire from an activity, especially music

Examples

After the tour, she decided it was finally time to hang up her fiddle and enjoy retirement.
He knew the injury meant he had to hang up his fiddle earlier than planned.
Once the group disbanded, most members chose to hang up their fiddle for good.

Synset Examples

She decided to hang up her fiddle after the concert.
He had to hang up his fiddle after the injury.
After years of playing, he finally hung up his fiddle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to retire - to stop working at one's job, usually due to age or after a long period
  • Synonym: to give up - to stop doing something; to quit
