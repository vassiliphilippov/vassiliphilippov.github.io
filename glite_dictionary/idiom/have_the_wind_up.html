to have the wind up

1. VERB

/ hæv ðə wɪnd ʌp / (AmE), / hæv ðə wɪnd ʌp / (BrE)
to be anxious or nervous, often in anticipation of something
to feel nervous or anxious
informal british

Examples

She always seems to have the wind up before exams.
After watching the horror movie alone, I really had the wind up.
He'll have the wind up until he hears the results of his job interview.

Synset Examples

She always seems to have the wind up before exams.
After that scary movie, I really have the wind up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be nervous - to feel easily agitated or uneasy
  • Antonym: be calm - to feel relaxed and not anxious

Etymology

British slang, originally early 20th century, probably from military slang ‘wind up’ meaning ‘anxiety’.
