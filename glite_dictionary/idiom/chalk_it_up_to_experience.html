to chalk it up to experience

1. VERB

/ tʃɔk ɪt ʌp tə ɪkˈspɪriəns / (AmE), / tʃɔːk ɪt ʌp tə ɪkˈspɪəriəns / (BrE)
to accept a mistake or failure as something from which one can learn, rather than as a defeat
treat a mistake as a learning experience

Examples

After failing the test, he decided to chalk it up to experience and try harder next time.
If you make a bad investment, just chalk it up to experience and move on.
She lost her first client but chose to chalk it up to experience rather than get discouraged.

Synset Examples

Don't worry; just chalk it up to experience.
I chalked my mistakes up to experience.
She chalked her failed project up to experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: learn from one's mistakes - to gain knowledge or wisdom by reflecting on errors
