back in the game

1. ADJ

/ bæk ɪn ðə ɡeɪm / (AmE), / bak ɪn ðə ɡeɪm / (BrE)
having returned to participation or a state of competitiveness, especially after a period of absence or difficulty
resumed participation or competitiveness

Examples

After months of rehab, she finally felt back in the game and ready to compete.
It's been a tough year, but our company is back in the game with new innovations.
After recovering from his illness, Mark is back in the game and working full-time again.

Synset Examples

After a tough loss, the team is back in the game.
She finally feels back in the game after her injury.
After months of recovery, he’s back in the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: back on track - having returned to the right path or course after a setback
