to get to grips

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt tə ɡrɪps / (AmE), / ɡɛt tə ɡrɪps / (BrE)
to begin to deal with or understand a difficult situation or subject
begin dealing with a problem or subject

Examples

After some initial confusion, the new manager quickly got to grips with the company's complex procedures.
It's taken scientists decades to get to grips with the effects of climate change.
She is finally getting to grips with her responsibilities at her new job.

Synset Examples

I still have not come to grips with the death of my parents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to come to grips - to begin to deal with or accept something difficult
