to stare someone in the face

1. VERB

/ stɛr ˈsʌmwʌn ɪn ðə feɪs / (AmE), / stɛə ˈsʌmwən ɪn ðə feɪs / (BrE)
to be very obvious or apparent to someone, often in a way that is impossible to ignore or avoid; to confront someone directly.
be obvious or directly confronting

Examples

The answer was staring him in the face, but he still couldn't see it.
Failure seemed to stare her in the face, yet she refused to give up.
The consequences of his actions stared him in the face, forcing him to make a tough decision.

Synset Examples

She knew challenges would stare her in the face every day.
