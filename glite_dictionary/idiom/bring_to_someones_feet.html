to bring to someone's feet

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ tu ˈsʌmwʌnz fiːt / (AmE), / brɪŋ tʊ ˈsʌmwʌnz fiːt / (BrE)
to cause an audience or group of people to stand up, especially to applaud or show enthusiasm
cause standing ovation or applause

Examples

The powerful final scene brought the audience to their feet in thunderous applause.
Her stirring speech brought everyone to their feet and inspired lasting change.
The underdog's unexpected victory brought the entire stadium to its feet in celebration.

Synset Examples

Winning the game with a Yahtzee brought everyone to their feet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bring down the house - to receive loud cheers or applause from the audience
  • Degree Lower: move - to deeply affect emotionally
