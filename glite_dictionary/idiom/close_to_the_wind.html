close to the wind

1. ADV

/ kloʊs tə ðə wɪnd / (AmE), / kləʊs tə ðə wɪnd / (BrE)
behaving in a way that is risky, controversial, or just within the limits of legality, decency, or acceptability
acting in a risky or borderline way

Examples

His business tactics sometimes sail a little close to the wind.
Journalists occasionally have to get close to the wind to get the full story.
She likes to joke in a way that can be a bit close to the wind.

Synset Examples

Flying a little close to the wind can lead to trouble.
Her comments were close to the wind during the debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to push the envelope - to go beyond the usual limits or boundaries; to innovate at risk
  • Synonym: to play with fire - take dangerous risks
  • Antonym: err on the side of caution - to choose the safer or less risky option

Etymology

From nautical terminology: sailing close to the wind means sailing nearly against the wind, which is difficult and risky.
