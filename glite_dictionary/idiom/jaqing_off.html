to JAQing off

1. VERB

/ dʒækɪŋ ɑf / (AmE), / dʒækɪŋ ɒf / (BrE)
To repeatedly ask disingenuous questions or raise irrelevant issues, often to derail or distract from the main point in a discussion.
asking disingenuous questions to distract
slang

Examples

It's obvious that you're just JAQing off rather than looking for real answers.
The moderator warned the participant to stop JAQing off and stick to the topic.
She accused him of JAQing off when he kept asking loaded questions in the debate.

Synset Examples

If you keep JAQing off, you'll annoy everyone.

Etymology

Blend of the initialism "JAQ" (just asking questions) and the slang term "jacking off".
