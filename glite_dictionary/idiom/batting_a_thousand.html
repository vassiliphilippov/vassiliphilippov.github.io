to batting a thousand

1. VERB

/ ˈbætɪŋ ə ˈθaʊzənd / (AmE), / ˈbætɪŋ ə ˈθaʊzənd / (BrE)
to achieve perfect success or to perform flawlessly, especially over a series of attempts or tasks
to achieve perfect success
american informal

Examples

Ever since her promotion, she's really batting a thousand at work.
Don't expect to be batting a thousand on your first day; mistakes are normal.
The team's new strategy had them batting a thousand during the playoffs.

Synset Examples

In her new job, she’s batting a thousand with clients.
She feels like she’s batting a thousand this season.
He’s been batting a thousand in his recent projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hit it out of the park - to do something exceptionally well
  • Antonym: to strike out - to fail completely at something

Etymology

From baseball, where a batting average of 1.000 ('batting a thousand') means hitting successfully every time at bat.
