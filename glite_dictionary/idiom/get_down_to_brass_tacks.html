to get down to brass tacks

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt daʊn tə bræs tæks / (AmE), / ɡɛt daʊn tə brɑːs tæks / (BrE)
to begin to discuss or deal with the most important or basic details of a situation
focus on the essential details

Examples

Let's get down to brass tacks and figure out how much this project will cost.
After an hour of small talk, they finally got down to brass tacks and discussed the contract.
It's important to get down to brass tacks when planning a large event.

Synset Examples

It's time to get down to brass tacks about our budget.
Once we get down to brass tacks, decisions will be clearer.
Before we proceed, let's get down to brass tacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get to the point - to begin talking about what is most important

Etymology

Origin uncertain; recorded in American English from the late 19th century, possibly referring to upholsterer's brass tacks as the essential component.
