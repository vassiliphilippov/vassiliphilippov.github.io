every which way

1. ADV

/ ˈɛv.ri wɪtʃ weɪ / (AmE), / ˈɛv.ri wɪtʃ weɪ / (BrE)
In all directions; in a haphazard or disorganized manner.
in a haphazard or all-directional way

Examples

After the earthquake, furniture was scattered every which way across the room.
The children threw their toys every which way and made a huge mess.
Papers were stacked every which way on his cluttered desk.

Synset Examples

the books were piled up helter-skelter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helter-skelter - in a disorderly or confused manner
  • Antonym: orderly - arranged or organized in order

2. ADV

/ ˈɛv.ɹi wɪtʃ weɪ / (AmE), / ˈɛv.ɹi wɪtʃ weɪ / (BrE)
In all directions or in a random, disorganized manner.
in all directions; randomly
informal

Examples

After the storm, branches and debris were scattered every which way across the yard.
The children ran every which way as soon as the bell rang for recess.
Papers flew every which way when the wind gusted through the open window.

Synset Examples

the houses were randomly scattered
bullets were fired into the crowd at random

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at random - without a definite plan, order, or aim
  • Synonym: haphazardly - in a random, unplanned manner
