to suck the kumara

1. VERB

/ sʌk ðə kuːˈmɑːrə / (AmE), / sʌk ðə kjuːˈmɑːrə / (BrE)
(New Zealand, informal) To endure hardship or disappointment; sometimes also used ironically to mean enjoy something or to describe something unpleasant in taste.
endure hardship (NZ idiom)
informal british

Examples

When the team lost the finals, the fans really had to suck the kumara.
After getting a pay cut, he told everyone he would just have to suck the kumara for a while.
The meal was so bland that it felt like I had to suck the kumara.

Synset Examples

He joked that he would suck the kumara for dessert.
When things get hard, just remember to suck the kumara.
She said the dish tasted like it was meant to suck the kumara.

Etymology

From New Zealand English, kumara meaning 'sweet potato', from Māori kūmara.
