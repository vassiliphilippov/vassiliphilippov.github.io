to be apples

1. VERB

/ bi ˈæpəlz / (AmE), / bi ˈæp(ə)lz / (BrE)
(Australian, informal) To be fine, okay, or satisfactory.
be fine or okay (Australian)
informal british

Examples

Don't worry—everything will be apples once we've finished the project.
She said her car broke down, but now it seems to be apples.
After a good night's rest, you'll be apples for tomorrow's game.

Synset Examples

Don't worry about the exam; she'll be apples.
After the meeting, she'll be apples again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be fine - to be in a good or satisfactory condition
  • Synonym: be all right - to be acceptable or not problematic
