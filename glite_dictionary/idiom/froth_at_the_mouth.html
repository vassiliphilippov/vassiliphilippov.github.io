to froth at the mouth

1. VERB

/ frɑːθ æt ðə maʊθ / (AmE), / frɒθ æt ðə maʊθ / (BrE)
to be extremely and uncontrollably angry
be extremely and uncontrollably angry

Examples

He began to froth at the mouth when he heard the unfair decision.
The politician frothed at the mouth during the heated debate about the policy.
She was so upset by the rumor that she seemed ready to froth at the mouth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lose one's temper - to become very angry suddenly
  • Antonym: to calm down - to become less angry or excited
