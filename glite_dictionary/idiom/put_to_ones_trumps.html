to put to one's trumps

1. VERB

/ pʊt tu wʌnz trʌmps / (AmE), / pʊt tʊ wʌnz trʌmps / (BrE)
to be confronted with a challenge that requires all of one's skill, ability, or resources
faced with a demanding challenge

Examples

During the negotiations, the team was put to their trumps to secure a favorable deal.
He felt truly put to his trumps when given a project with a tight deadline.
The surprise audit put the company to its trumps to produce the required documents.

Synset Examples

She was put to her trumps during the final exam.
The unexpected challenge really put him to his trumps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to put to the test - to test someone's abilities or qualities, usually in a challenging situation

Etymology

From the card game term 'trump,' meaning a valuable or winning card; figuratively, to use one's best resources.
