mire of one's own making

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪɚ əv wʌnz oʊn ˈmeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪə əv wʌnz əʊn ˈmeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
a difficult or troublesome situation that a person has brought upon themselves through their own actions or decisions
trouble caused by oneself

Examples

After several poor choices, she found herself in a mire of her own making that was difficult to escape.
It is easy to blame others, but sometimes we are stuck in a mire of our own making.
He was left to sort out the mire of his own making after ignoring everyone’s advice.

Synset Examples

He felt trapped in a mire of his own making.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mess of one's own making - a predicament one has caused for oneself
  • Synonym: predicament - a difficult or unpleasant situation

Etymology

From 'mire', meaning a bog or swamp, figuratively used for trouble; combined with 'of one's own making' to indicate self-caused situations.
