to talk someone's ear off

1. VERB

/ tɑk sʌmˌwʌnz ɪr ɔf / (AmE), / tɔːk sʌmˌwʌnz ɪə ɒf / (BrE)
to talk to someone for a very long time, especially in a way that becomes tiresome or excessive
talk to someone excessively
informal

Examples

She will talk your ear off if you ask her about classic movies.
He talked my ear off during the entire car ride home.
Try not to mention sports around Mark, or he'll talk your ear off for hours.

Synset Examples

I had to excuse myself; he was talking my ear off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatter - talk rapidly or incessantly
  • Synonym: to go on - keep talking at length
  • Antonym: to be quiet - not talk; remain silent
