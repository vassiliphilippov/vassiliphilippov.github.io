to drop the ball

1. VERB

/ drɑp ðə bɑl / (AmE), / drɒp ðə bɔːl / (BrE)
to make a mistake or fail to do something that one is responsible for
make a serious mistake

Examples

If you drop the ball on this project, we could lose an important client.
She realized she had dropped the ball when she forgot to send the email.
I can't afford to drop the ball during the final stage of the competition.

Synset Examples

I blundered during the job interview

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunder - make a careless or serious mistake
  • Synonym: fumble - handle something clumsily or make a mess of it
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02572142-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ drɑp ðə bɔl / (AmE), / drɒp ðə bɔːl / (BrE)
to make a mistake by not doing something that you should have done, especially in a way that allows an opportunity to be lost or responsibility to be neglected
fail to fulfill a responsibility
informal

Examples

We can't afford to drop the ball on this project, or we might lose the client.
The team dropped the ball when they forgot to check the final numbers before the meeting.
She apologized for dropping the ball and promised to do better next time.

Synset Examples

If we don't act now, we'll drop the ball.
She dropped the ball by not sending the report.
I really dropped the ball, pardon my French.
He really dropped the ball on that project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mess up - make a mistake or do something badly
  • Antonym: to succeed - to achieve the desired result or do well

Etymology

From the literal action in sports (such as baseball or football) where dropping the ball indicates an error or failure.
