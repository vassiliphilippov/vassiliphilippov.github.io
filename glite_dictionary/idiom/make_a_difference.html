to make a difference

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə ˈdɪfərəns / (AmE), / meɪk ə ˈdɪf(ə)rəns / (BrE)
to have a significant effect or influence on a situation or person
have a significant impact

Examples

She hopes her research will make a difference in the fight against climate change.
Donating even a small amount can make a difference to those in need.
Teachers can truly make a difference in their students' lives.

Synset Examples

Volunteering can really make a difference in the community.
Every little effort can make a difference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: matter - to be important or have an effect
  • Antonym: make no difference - to have no effect on a situation or outcome

2. VERB

/ meɪk ə ˈdɪfəɹəns / (AmE), / meɪk ə ˈdɪfərəns / (BrE)
to have a significant effect or impact, especially in improving a situation or changing the outcome
have a significant effect or impact

Examples

Her encouragement made a difference in his confidence and performance.
Even a small donation can make a difference to families in need.
Their quick response made a difference during the emergency.

Synset Examples

Tampon Tim's kindness made a difference at the party.
