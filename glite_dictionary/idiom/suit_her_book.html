to suit her book

1. VERB

/ sut hɚ bʊk / (AmE), / suːt hə bʊk / (BrE)
To match or be convenient for one's own plans or interests.
match one's plans or interests
british

Examples

She agreed to the schedule changes because they suited her book.
Find an arrangement that will suit your book and let me know.
The new policy didn't suit his book, so he voiced his concerns.

Synset Examples

She always picks options that suit her book perfectly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suit oneself - to do what one prefers
  • Synonym: suit somebody's purpose - to be convenient or useful for someone's aim
