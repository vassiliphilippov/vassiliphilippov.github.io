to face facts

1. VERB

/ feɪs fækts / (AmE), / feɪs fækts / (BrE)
to accept or acknowledge the truth of a situation, especially when it is difficult or unpleasant
accept the truth of a situation

Examples

It's time to face facts—the company can't afford to hire more staff.
Sometimes people find it difficult to face facts about their own mistakes.
We should face facts and admit that the plan isn't working.

Synset Examples

We need to face facts; we're running out of time.
Sometimes you just have to face facts and move on.
It's hard to face facts, but the project failed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accept reality - acknowledge things as they are
  • Antonym: to deny - refuse to accept or admit the truth
