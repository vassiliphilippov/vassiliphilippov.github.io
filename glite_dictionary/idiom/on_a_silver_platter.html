on a silver platter

1. X

/ ɑn ə ˈsɪlvɚ ˈplætɚ / (AmE), / ɒn ə ˈsɪlvə ˈplætə / (BrE)
If something is given to you on a silver platter, you receive it very easily or without having to make any effort.
given easily or without effort

Examples

The inheritance was delivered to him on a silver platter without any struggle.
They expected the answers to be handed on a silver platter instead of researching themselves.
She didn't want opportunities served on a silver platter; she preferred to earn them through hard work.

Synset Examples

He was handed the promotion on a silver platter, thanks to his connections.
The solution was presented to them on a silver platter.
She got the job on a silver platter because of her father.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handed to someone on a plate - provided very easily or without effort
