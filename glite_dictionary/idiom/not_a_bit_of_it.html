not a bit of it

1. X

/ nɑt ə bɪt əv ɪt / (AmE), / nɒt ə bɪt əv ɪt / (BrE)
used to emphasize that something is not at all true or does not happen in the slightest degree
not at all; not in the slightest

Examples

She said she was angry, but not a bit of it showed on her face.
They warned the hike would be tough, but not a bit of it was difficult for him.
He pretended to be interested, but not a bit of it was genuine.

Synset Examples

He said he was tired, but not a bit of it!
She claimed to be scared, but not a bit of it.
When asked if he was worried, he said, 'Not a bit of it.'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: not at all - not in any way; not even slightly
  • Synonym: absolutely - completely; totally
