to hit the ceiling

1. VERB

/ hɪt ðə ˈsiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / hɪt ðə ˈsiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
to suddenly become very angry
suddenly become very angry

Examples

When Dad saw the mess we made in the kitchen, he hit the ceiling.
My boss hit the ceiling when he realized the report wasn't finished.
She tends to hit the ceiling over the smallest mistakes.

Synset Examples

The professor combusted when the student didn't know the answer to a very elementary question
Spam makes me go ballistic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go ballistic - to become extremely angry
  • Synonym: to fly off the handle - to lose one's temper suddenly
  • Antonym: to keep one's cool - to remain calm
