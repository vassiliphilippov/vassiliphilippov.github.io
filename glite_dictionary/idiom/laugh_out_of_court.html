to laugh out of court

1. VERB

/ læf ˌaʊt əv ˈkɔrt / (AmE), / lɑːf ˌaʊt əv ˈkɔːt / (BrE)
to dismiss or reject something as so absurd or unworthy that it is not given serious consideration, especially in a legal or formal context
dismiss as absurd or unworthy

Examples

The committee laughed out of court the proposal to replace all textbooks with comic books.
Her lawsuit was so poorly constructed that it was quickly laughed out of court.
When he claimed he could run a mile in three minutes, his boast was laughed out of court.

Synset Examples

The judge decided to laugh the case out of court.
His ridiculous claim was laughed out of court by everyone.
They laughed the absurd allegations out of court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - to decide that something or someone is not worth considering
  • Antonym: to accept - to take or receive something offered or approve an idea
