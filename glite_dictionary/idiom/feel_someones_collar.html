to feel someone's collar

1. VERB

/ fil sʌmwʌnz ˈkɑlɚ / (AmE), / fiːl sʌmwʌnz ˈkɒlə / (BrE)
to apprehend or restrain someone, especially by seizing them; to arrest or detain someone
to arrest or detain someone
british

Examples

As he tried to sneak out of the building, the guard felt his collar and stopped him.
The police finally felt his collar after weeks of surveillance.
If you don't pay your debts, someone will come and feel your collar.

Synset Examples

He was caught and they felt his collar tightly.
The detective had to feel his collar to get information.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrest - to seize someone by legal authority
  • Antonym: to let go - to release someone from restraint or control
