to settle a score

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛt̬l̩ ə skɔr / (AmE), / ˈsɛt.əl ə skɔː / (BrE)
to take action to resolve a disagreement or get revenge for a past grievance
resolve a conflict or seek revenge

Examples

After years of rivalry, Mark finally decided to settle a score with his old competitor.
Sometimes, athletes use crucial matches to settle a score with their opponents.
She never forgot the insult and waited patiently to settle a score someday.

Synset Examples

It's time to finally settle a score from last year.
He wanted to settle a score with his old rival.
They decided to settle the score during the next match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get even - to take revenge or retaliate
  • Antonym: to forgive - to stop feeling anger or resentment toward someone
