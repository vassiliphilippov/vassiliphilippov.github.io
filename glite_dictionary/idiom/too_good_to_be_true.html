too good to be true

1. ADJ

/ tu ɡʊd tə bi tru / (AmE), / tuː ɡʊd tə biː truː / (BrE)
so good that you cannot believe it is real, often causing suspicion or doubt
unbelievably good; arousing suspicion

Examples

The offer was too good to be true, so I decided to double-check the details.
Sometimes a job advertisement can sound too good to be true, so it's wise to be cautious.
His promises seemed too good to be true, making everyone in the room suspicious.

Synset Examples

The quacktitioner offered cures that sounded too good to be true.
The deal seemed too good to be true, so I hesitated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suspicious - having distrust or doubt about something
  • Synonym: skeptical - having or showing doubt about something's genuineness
