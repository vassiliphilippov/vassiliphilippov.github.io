to loosen the apron strings

1. VERB

/ ˈluːsən ði ˈeɪprən strɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈluːsən ði ˈeɪprən strɪŋz / (BrE)
to allow someone, especially a child or dependent, to become more independent by granting greater freedom or responsibility
allow someone to become independent

Examples

After years of constant supervision, her parents finally decided to loosen the apron strings.
Some mothers find it difficult to loosen the apron strings as their children grow up.
He realized he needed to loosen the apron strings and let his daughter make her own decisions.

Synset Examples

She finally decided to loosen the apron strings and trust her kids.
Parents need to loosen the apron strings to help their children grow.
It's time to loosen the apron strings and let him be independent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cut the apron strings - to end someone's dependence, especially a child's dependence on a parent

Etymology

From the symbolic use of an 'apron string' as a tie between a mother and child or dependent; origin in reference to releasing maternal control.
