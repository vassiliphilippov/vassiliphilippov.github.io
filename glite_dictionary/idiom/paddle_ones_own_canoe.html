to paddle one's own canoe

1. VERB

/ ˈpædəl wʌnz oʊn kəˈnu / (AmE), / ˈpædəl wʌnz əʊn kəˈnuː / (BrE)
to be independent and take care of oneself without relying on others
be independent or self-reliant

Examples

After college, he had to paddle his own canoe and manage his finances.
Starting her new job in a different city taught her to paddle her own canoe.
You won't learn responsibility until you paddle your own canoe.

Synset Examples

He learned to paddle his own canoe after moving out.
She decided to paddle her own canoe and start a business.
It’s important to paddle your own canoe in life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stand on one's own two feet - to be able to support oneself without help
  • Antonym: be dependent - to rely on others for support or help

Etymology

From the literal act of paddling a canoe oneself, used figuratively since the 19th century.
