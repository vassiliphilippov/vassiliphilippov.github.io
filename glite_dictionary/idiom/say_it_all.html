to say it all

1. VERB

/ seɪ ɪt ɔl / (AmE), / seɪ ɪt ɔːl / (BrE)
to express everything necessary or to convey a complete message, often without using words
express everything necessary (often nonverbally)

Examples

Her silence said it all—she didn't approve of the plan.
The look on his face said it all as he saw the surprise.
When she slammed the door, her actions said it all.

Synset Examples

She gave him a look that said it all.
The expression on her face said it all.

2. VERB

/ seɪ ɪt ɔl / (AmE), / seɪ ɪt ɔːl / (BrE)
to express everything necessary, especially without words
express everything necessary (often nonverbally)

Examples

Her expression said it all when she walked into the room.
Sometimes, just a glance between friends can say it all.
His silence after hearing the news said it all.

Synset Examples

That smile can say it all without a single word.
Her letter seemed to say it all without any words.
