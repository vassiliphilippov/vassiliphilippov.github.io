to fly off at a tangent

1. VERB

/ flaɪ ɑf ət ə ˈtændʒənt / (AmE), / flaɪ ɒf ət ə ˈtændʒənt / (BrE)
to suddenly start discussing or considering a completely different subject, often unrelated to the current topic
suddenly change subject

Examples

Whenever John gets excited, he tends to fly off at a tangent during conversations.
Our discussion kept losing focus because people would fly off at a tangent every few minutes.
It's important not to fly off at a tangent when explaining complex ideas.

Synset Examples

During the meeting, he flew off at a tangent about unrelated topics.
Her explanation flew off at a tangent before returning to the main point.
If we keep flying off at a tangent, we'll never finish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to digress - to depart from the main subject temporarily in speech or writing
  • Antonym: stick to the point - to remain focused on the main subject or issue

Etymology

From the mathematical term 'tangent', meaning a line that touches a curve and then moves away; figuratively applied to changing direction in conversation.
