to pass the hat

1. VERB

/ pæs ðə hæt / (AmE), / pɑːs ðə hæt / (BrE)
to ask a group of people to contribute money, typically for a specific cause or person
collect money from a group of people

Examples

After the show, the performers decided to pass the hat to cover their expenses.
When their teammate fell ill, the team manager suggested they pass the hat to help with bills.
Whenever someone retires, we pass the hat and buy them a gift from everyone.

Synset Examples

During the meeting, they will pass the hat for contributions.
Let's pass the hat and help our neighbor.
They decided to pass the hat for charity donations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fundraise - to collect money for a cause or charity

Etymology

From the practice of literally passing a hat around a group to collect monetary contributions.
