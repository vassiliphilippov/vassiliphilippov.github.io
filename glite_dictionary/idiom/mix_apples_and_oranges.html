to mix apples and oranges

1. VERB

/ mɪks ˈæpəlz ənd ˈɔrɪndʒɪz / (AmE), / mɪks ˈæpəlz ənd ˈɒrɪndʒɪz / (BrE)
to compare things that are fundamentally different and should not be compared directly
compare unlike things

Examples

You can't make a fair decision if you mix apples and oranges like that.
When evaluating the two proposals, try not to mix apples and oranges.
It's never helpful to mix apples and oranges when discussing such different topics.

Synset Examples

Don't mix apples and oranges when comparing prices.
They always mix apples and oranges in their comparisons.
It's hard to judge if you mix apples and oranges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compare apples and oranges - to make a comparison between two fundamentally different things

Etymology

Refers to the obvious differences between apples and oranges, used as a metaphorical comparison since the 20th century.
