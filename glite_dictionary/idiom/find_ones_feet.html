to find one's feet

1. VERB

/ faɪnd wʌnz fit / (AmE), / faɪnd wʌnz fiːt / (BrE)
to become confident or comfortable in a new situation or environment after some time
become comfortable or confident

Examples

It can take a few months to find one's feet in a new city.
She struggled in the beginning but soon found her feet at university.
After joining the company, he needed some time to find his feet in the new role.

Synset Examples

Once she started working, she quickly found her feet.
He struggled at first, but eventually found his feet.
After moving, it took her a while to find her feet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to settle in - to become established and comfortable in a new place, job, or activity
  • Synonym: find one's bearings - to become oriented or familiar with a new situation
