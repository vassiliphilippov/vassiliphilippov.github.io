appointment in Samarra

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɔɪntmənt ɪn səˈmɑrə / (AmE), / əˈpɔɪntmənt ɪn səˈmɑːrə / (BrE)
an unavoidable or predestined encounter with one's fate, often especially death
an unavoidable fate or predestined meeting

Examples

Some believe that running from danger is pointless because it only hastens their appointment in Samarra.
The novel's central theme revolves around each character's own appointment in Samarra.
He regarded the challenge as inevitable, his personal appointment in Samarra.

Synset Examples

She felt her decision was an appointment in Samarra.
His trip is just an appointment in Samarra; fate awaits.
Life's unpredictability often feels like an appointment in Samarra.

Etymology

From the title of a story retold by W. Somerset Maugham, referencing the ancient city of Samarra in Iraq, symbolizing inevitable fate.
