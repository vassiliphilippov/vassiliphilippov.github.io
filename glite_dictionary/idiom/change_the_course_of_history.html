to change the course of history

1. VERB

/ tʃeɪndʒ ðə kɔrs əv ˈhɪstəri / (AmE), / tʃeɪndʒ ðə kɔːs əv ˈhɪstəri / (BrE)
to do something that has a significant and lasting effect on the direction of future events
to significantly alter future events

Examples

His invention helped change the course of history by making communication easier for everyone.
It only took one brave decision to change the course of history in that country.
Scientific discoveries can sometimes change the course of history forever.

Synset Examples

An activist judge can change the course of history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make history - to do something significant that will be remembered
