to face the music

1. VERB

/ feɪs ðə ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / feɪs ðə ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
to accept unpleasant consequences or criticism for one's actions; to confront the results of something one has done
accept consequences for actions

Examples

After forgetting her assignment, Maria had to face the music when the teacher asked for it.
It was time for the CEO to face the music after the company's failure.
He knew he'd made a mistake and decided to face the music instead of hiding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take the consequences - accept the outcome, especially unpleasant
  • Synonym: face up to - to confront and deal with (something difficult)

Etymology

Possibly American in origin, dating from the 19th century, with theatrical roots (performers literally facing the orchestra, and thus criticism) or military roots (soldiers facing drums as a form of punishment).

2. VERB

/ feɪs ðə ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / feɪs ðə ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
to accept and deal with the punishment or unpleasant consequences of one's actions.
accept consequences or punishment

Examples

After months of avoiding his boss, he finally had to face the music about his mistakes.
She knew she had to face the music after breaking the window.
It's better to admit the truth now and face the music instead of hiding.

Synset Examples

you have to face the music
take your medicine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take your medicine - accept punishment or consequences without complaint

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from 19th-century American theatrical slang, referring to actors facing the orchestra (the 'music') when appearing on stage.

Examples

Related Terms
