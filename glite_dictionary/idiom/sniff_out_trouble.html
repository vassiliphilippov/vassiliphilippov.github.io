to sniff out trouble

1. VERB

/ snɪf aʊt ˈtrʌb.əl / (AmE), / snɪf aʊt ˈtrʌb.əl / (BrE)
to detect problems or danger easily, often before others notice
detect problems or danger easily

Examples

As a seasoned detective, she could sniff out trouble the moment she entered a room.
Parents often seem able to sniff out trouble before their children even realize it.
The manager relied on his assistant to sniff out trouble in the office early.

Synset Examples

He could sniff out trouble from a mile away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sense trouble - to become aware of possible danger or problems
