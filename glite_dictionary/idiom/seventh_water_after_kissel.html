seventh water after kissel

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛvənθ ˈwɔtər ˈæftər ˈkɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈsɛvənθ ˈwɔːtə ˈɑːftə ˈkɪsl̩ / (BrE)
an idiomatic expression used to describe something extremely convoluted, confusing, or diluted to the point of uselessness
something very convoluted or diluted

Examples

The directions he gave were the seventh water after kissel—impossibly convoluted and hard to follow.
This bureaucratic process is the seventh water after kissel; no one can make sense of it anymore.
By the time the rumor reached me, it was the seventh water after kissel, barely resembling the original story.

Synset Examples

Trying to understand his reasoning was like the seventh water after kissel.
Her explanation was so convoluted, it was like the seventh water after kissel.
When he explained the situation, it felt like the seventh water after kissel.

Etymology

From Russian idiom 'седьмая вода на киселе' (sedmaya voda na kisele), literally meaning 'seventh water on kissel', referring to something excessively diluted or of distant relation.
