Goodnight Irene

1. X

/ ɡʊdˌnaɪt aɪˈrin / (AmE), / ɡʊdˌnaɪt aɪˈriːn / (BrE)
used playfully or affectionately as a way to say farewell or goodnight
playful or affectionate farewell

Examples

After the meeting, Sarah smiled and said, 'Goodnight Irene,' before heading out the door.
He ended every family gathering with a cheerful 'Goodnight Irene!' that made everyone laugh.
Whenever she was tired, she would wave and say, 'Goodnight Irene,' signaling it was time to go to bed.'

Synset Examples

She chuckled, 'Goodnight Irene, see you tomorrow!'
After a long day, he said, 'Goodnight Irene, I’m off to bed.'
As the party ended, he waved and said, 'Goodnight Irene!'

Etymology

Popularized by the 20th-century American folk song 'Goodnight, Irene' by Lead Belly.
