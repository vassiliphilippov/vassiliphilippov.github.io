to run (someone) ragged

1. VERB

/ rʌn ˈsʌmwʌn ˈræɡɪd / (AmE), / rʌn ˈsʌmwən ˈræɡɪd / (BrE)
to make someone extremely tired or exhausted by demanding a lot of effort or attention from them.
make extremely tired

Examples

Her new job ran her ragged after only a week in the office.
Taking care of three children under five runs her ragged most days.
The endless meetings and deadlines ran him ragged by Friday evening.

Synset Examples

The kids' constant demands ran her ragged all weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhaust - to make someone extremely tired
