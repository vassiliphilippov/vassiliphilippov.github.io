to keep options open

1. VERB

/ kip ˈɑpʃənz ˈoʊpən / (AmE), / kiːp ˈɒpʃənz ˈəʊpən / (BrE)
to avoid making a final decision so that you are free to choose among different possibilities later
remain undecided to allow flexibility

Examples

She decided to keep her options open by applying to several universities.
It's wise to keep your options open when considering a new career path.
The company is keeping its options open in case the merger falls through.

Synset Examples

He always keeps his options open when dating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hedge your bets - to protect yourself against loss by supporting more than one possible result or both sides in a competition
