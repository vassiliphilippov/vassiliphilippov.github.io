to lead to the altar

1. VERB

/ lid tə ði ˈɔltər / (AmE), / liːd tə ði ˈɔːltə / (BrE)
to marry someone or arrange for someone to get married, often implying a formal or serious commitment
to marry or arrange a marriage

Examples

Many classic novels end with the hero finally leading his beloved to the altar.
After years of dating, they decided it was time to lead each other to the altar.
Rumors circulated that the celebrity couple would soon be led to the altar.

Synset Examples

He promised to lead her to the altar soon.
They plan to lead her to the altar this summer.
She dreams of being led to the altar one day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tie the knot - get married
  • Synonym: get hitched - become married (informal)
  • Antonym: divorce - legal dissolution of marriage

Etymology

Refers to the wedding tradition of the bride being led to the altar for the marriage ceremony.
