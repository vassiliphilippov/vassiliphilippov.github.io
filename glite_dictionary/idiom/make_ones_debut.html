to make one's debut

1. VERB

/ meɪk wʌnz deɪˈbju / (AmE), / meɪk wʌnz deɪˈbjuː / (BrE)
to perform or appear in public for the first time, especially in a professional context
perform publicly for the first time

Examples

The actress made her debut on Broadway at the age of nineteen.
He made his debut as a solo pianist in a packed concert hall.
After years of training, she finally made her debut as a professional chef.

Synset Examples

She made her debut on the big screen last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debut - a person's first public appearance or performance

Etymology

From French 'début' (a first appearance), from 'débuter' (to begin).
