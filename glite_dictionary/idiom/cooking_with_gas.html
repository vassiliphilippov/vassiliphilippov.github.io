cooking with gas

1. X

/ ˈkʊkɪŋ wɪð ɡæs / (AmE), / ˈkʊkɪŋ wɪð ɡas / (BrE)
To be performing very well or efficiently, especially after a period of lesser success.
performing very well or efficiently

Examples

Now that all our systems are up and running, we're cooking with gas.
After struggling at first, the team is finally cooking with gas on this project.
Once the funding came through, the researchers were really cooking with gas.

Synset Examples

After the first few failures, we're finally cooking with gas.
Once we figured it out, we were cooking with gas.
Now that we have a new stove, it's cooking with gas!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on a roll - experiencing a period of success or good luck

Etymology

Originated in the mid-20th century as an advertising slogan for gas stoves, later adopted idiomatically for effective progress.
