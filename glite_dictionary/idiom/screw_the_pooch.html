to screw the pooch

1. VERB

/ skru ðə puːtʃ / (AmE), / skruː ðə puːtʃ / (BrE)
to make a serious or costly mistake, often due to carelessness or poor judgment
make a serious mistake (informal idiom)
informal slang american

Examples

If you forget the tickets again, you’ll really screw the pooch this time.
The team screwed the pooch by missing that crucial play in the final minutes.
He knew he’d screwed the pooch as soon as he hit send on the wrong email.

Synset Examples

She didn’t mean to screw the pooch, but it happened.
He really screwed the pooch with that presentation.
If we miss the deadline, we’ll have screwed the pooch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mess up - to make a mistake or do something badly
  • Synonym: blunder - a careless or serious mistake

Etymology

US slang, first recorded in the 1960s, possibly derived from earlier expressions meaning to blunder badly.
