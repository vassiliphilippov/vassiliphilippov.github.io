to take up the cudgel

1. VERB

/ teɪk ʌp ðə ˈkʌdʒəl / (AmE), / teɪk ʌp ðə ˈkʌdʒl̩ / (BrE)
to begin to defend or support a person, cause, or idea, especially publicly or vigorously
defend or support a cause

Examples

Many citizens decided to take up the cudgel after the proposed law threatened their local parks.
She was not afraid to take up the cudgel whenever she saw injustice at work.
Journalists often take up the cudgel for those whose voices are ignored.

Synset Examples

It's time to take up the cudgels against injustice.
She took up the cudgels, fighting for her community.
She took up the cudgel for animal rights.
They took up the cudgel for environmental protection.
He took up the cudgel for the underprivileged.
He took up the cudgels after witnessing the unfair treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to defend - to protect from harm or attack; to speak or act in support of
  • Synonym: champion - to support, defend, or fight for a cause or person
  • Antonym: to oppose - to disagree with or fight against a cause or idea

Etymology

From the literal act of picking up a cudgel (a short thick stick used as a weapon) to fight for a cause, dating back to the 16th century.
