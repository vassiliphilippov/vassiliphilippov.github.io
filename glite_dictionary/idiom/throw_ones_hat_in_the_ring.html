to throw one's hat in the ring

1. VERB

/ θroʊ wʌnz hæt ɪn ðə rɪŋ / (AmE), / θrəʊ wʌnz hæt ɪn ðə rɪŋ / (BrE)
to announce one's intention to join a competition or contest
declare intention to compete

Examples

After much deliberation, she decided to throw her hat in the ring for city council.
Several candidates have already thrown their hats in the ring for the upcoming mayoral election.
He is still unsure if he should throw his hat in the ring or support someone else.

Synset Examples

They encouraged him to throw his hat in the ring for the contest.
He plans to throw his hat in the ring for the promotion.
She decided to throw her hat in the ring for the local election.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enter the race - to become a candidate or competitor

Etymology

From the old practice of throwing a hat into a boxing ring to signal a challenge.
