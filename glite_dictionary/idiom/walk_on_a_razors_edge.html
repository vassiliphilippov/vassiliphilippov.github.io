to walk on a razor's edge

1. VERB

/ wɔk ɑn ə ˈreɪzɚz ɛdʒ / (AmE), / wɔːk ɒn ə ˈreɪzəz ɛdʒ / (BrE)
to be in a very dangerous or precarious situation where a small mistake could have serious consequences
be in a very risky situation

Examples

After the scandal, the company was forced to walk on a razor's edge to avoid bankruptcy.
Diplomats often walk on a razor's edge when negotiating peace between hostile nations.
She felt like she was walking on a razor's edge juggling her demanding job and family responsibilities.

Synset Examples

They walked on a razor's edge with that risky investment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to walk a tightrope - to be in a risky or precarious situation requiring care
