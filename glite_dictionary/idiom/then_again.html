then again

1. ADV

/ ðɛn əˈɡɛn / (AmE), / ðen əˈɡen / (BrE)
used to introduce an alternative thought, possibility, or perspective that contrasts with a previous statement
introduces an alternative perspective

Examples

It seemed like a good idea at first; then again, it could be risky.
The restaurant is expensive; then again, the food is excellent.
I thought she was upset; then again, maybe she was just tired.

Synset Examples

I might go to the party; then again, I might stay home.
It could rain tomorrow; then again, the forecast is clear.
He seems nice; then again, I hardly know him.
