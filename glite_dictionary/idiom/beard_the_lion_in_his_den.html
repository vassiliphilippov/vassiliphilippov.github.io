to beard the lion in his den

1. VERB

/ bɪrd ðə ˈlaɪ.ən ɪn hɪz dɛn / (AmE), / bɪəd ðə ˈlaɪ.ən ɪn hɪz dɛn / (BrE)
to confront someone powerful or a difficult situation directly and boldly, especially in their own domain
confront authority or danger directly

Examples

After much hesitation, she chose to beard the lion in his den by reporting her concerns to the CEO.
Sometimes you need to beard the lion in his den rather than avoiding the problem altogether.
He knew the risks, but still decided to beard the lion in his den during the heated debate.

Synset Examples

He decided to beard the lion in his den and confront the boss.
To advocate for change, she had to beard the lion in his den.
He bravely bearded the lion in his den during negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to face the music - to deal with the consequences of one's actions; to confront a difficult situation
  • Synonym: to confront - to face and deal with a challenge or difficult situation

Etymology

From the imagery of daring to approach a lion in its own den; attested from early 19th century English.
