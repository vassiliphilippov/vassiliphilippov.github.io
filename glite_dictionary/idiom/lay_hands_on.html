to lay hands on

1. VERB

/ leɪ hændz ɑn / (AmE), / leɪ hændz ɒn / (BrE)
to touch, handle, or take hold of something with the hands; to physically manage or grasp.
physically touch or handle

Examples

The magician quickly laid hands on the deck of cards to shuffle them.
If you lay hands on that old manuscript, handle it with care.
She managed to lay hands on the keys despite the darkness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: touch - make physical contact with something
  • Synonym: handle - to hold or manipulate with the hands
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01214588-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ leɪ hændz ɑn / (AmE), / leɪ hændz ɒn / (BrE)
to obtain or acquire something, especially with effort or difficulty
acquire or obtain (something)

Examples

She managed to lay hands on a ticket for the sold-out concert.
It's nearly impossible to lay hands on good croissants in this town.
After months of searching, he finally laid hands on the original manuscript.

Synset Examples

I can’t wait to lay hands on the latest smartphone.
He was eager to lay hands on that rare comic book.
If I could just lay hands on the missing files, I'd be set.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get hold of - to obtain or acquire something
  • Synonym: to come by - to acquire or obtain, often with difficulty
