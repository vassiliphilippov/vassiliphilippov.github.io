to have one's ducks in a row

1. VERB

/ hæv wʌnz dʌks ɪn ə roʊ / (AmE), / hæv wʌnz dʌks ɪn ə rəʊ / (BrE)
to be well organized or fully prepared for something, especially before beginning an activity or task.
be organized and prepared

Examples

Before launching the product, the team made sure to have their ducks in a row.
I can't start the presentation until I have my ducks in a row.
It's wise to have your ducks in a row before applying for a loan.

Synset Examples

He always likes to have his ducks in a row before traveling.
Before the meeting, she ensured she had her ducks in a row.
It's important to have your ducks in a row for the project deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get one's act together - to become organized and prepared
  • Synonym: be in order - to be arranged or prepared correctly
