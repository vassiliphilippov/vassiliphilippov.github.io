to earn one's keep

1. VERB

/ ɝn wʌnz kip / (AmE), / ɜːn wʌnz kiːp / (BrE)
to earn enough money or provide sufficient value to support oneself, especially for basic living needs such as food and shelter
support oneself by working or contributing

Examples

After graduating, he took odd jobs to earn his keep.
She felt she had to earn her keep by helping around the house.
Many artists struggle to earn their keep while pursuing their passion.

Synset Examples

She works part-time to earn her keep.
He volunteers at the shelter to earn his keep.
They need to find jobs to earn their keep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support oneself - to provide for one's own needs through work or effort
  • Synonym: make a living - to earn enough income to live on
