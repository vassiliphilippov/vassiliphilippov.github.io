sweet Fanny Adams

1. NOUN

/ swit ˈfæni ˈædəmz / (AmE), / swiːt ˈfæni ˈædəmz / (BrE)
used to emphasize that there is nothing or very little of something; nothing at all.
nothing at all; very little (British idiom)
british

Examples

When I opened the fridge, there was sweet Fanny Adams left to eat.
Despite all his promises, the company delivered sweet Fanny Adams.
I asked for a refund but got sweet Fanny Adams in return.

Synset Examples

I asked for a raise and they gave me bugger-all
I know sweet Fanny Adams about surgery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bugger all - very little or nothing; nothing at all
  • Synonym: zilch - nothing; zero
  • Synonym: nothing - not anything; no single thing

Etymology

British origin; from the name Fanny Adams (1859–1867), a murder victim, later used by Royal Navy sailors to mean tinned meat and humorously extended to mean 'nothing'.
