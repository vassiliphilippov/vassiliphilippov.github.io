to resonate unto the ages of ages

1. VERB

/ ˈrɛzəˌneɪt ˈʌntu ði ˈeɪdʒɪz əv ˈeɪdʒɪz / (AmE), / ˈrɛzəˌneɪt ˈʌntu ði ˈeɪdʒɪz ɒv ˈeɪdʒɪz / (BrE)
to have an enduring impact or significance for all time; to remain meaningful forever
endure with eternal significance
archaic

Examples

The power of her speech will resonate unto the ages of ages, inspiring future generations.
Great works of art often resonate unto the ages of ages, touching people long after their creation.
His heroic deeds will resonate unto the ages of ages in the annals of history.

Synset Examples

The book's wisdom will resonate unto the ages of ages.

Etymology

From the poetic phrase 'ages of ages', a hyperbolic form meaning 'forever', influenced by biblical expressions like the Greek 'εἰς τοὺς αἰῶνας τῶν αἰώνων' (unto the ages of ages).
