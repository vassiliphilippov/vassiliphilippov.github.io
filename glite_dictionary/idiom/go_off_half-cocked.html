to go off half-cocked

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ɔf hæf ˈkɑkt / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɒf hɑːf ˈkɒkt / (BrE)
to act before thinking carefully or being fully prepared, often resulting in mistakes or problems
act prematurely or without proper preparation

Examples

It's best not to go off half-cocked before understanding all the facts.
He often went off half-cocked, leading to unnecessary problems at work.
We shouldn't go off half-cocked and make decisions hastily.

Synset Examples

she wanted to quit her job but her mother told her not to go off half-cocked

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush into - to do something too quickly without thinking
  • Antonym: to hesitate - to pause before doing something, often due to uncertainty

Etymology

Originally from firearms terminology; a gun at 'half-cock' is not fully ready to fire, indicating lack of preparation.

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ɑf hæf ˈkɑkt / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɒf hɑːf ˈkɒkt / (BrE)
to act or speak without sufficient thought or preparation, often resulting in mistakes or embarrassment
act without sufficient preparation

Examples

He tends to go off half-cocked and regrets it later.
Before making any accusations, don't go off half-cocked.
She realized she had gone off half-cocked after hearing the full story.

Synset Examples

Don't go off half-cocked; think before you act.
It's easy to go off half-cocked when emotions run high.
She realized she went off half-cocked with her first response.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: act rashly - to act without thinking carefully
  • Antonym: to think twice - to consider carefully before acting

Etymology

Originally referring to a firearm that would discharge when not properly cocked, from 19th-century American English.
