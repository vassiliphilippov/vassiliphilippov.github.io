to give a kick

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ə kɪk / (AmE), / ɡɪv ə kɪk / (BrE)
to stimulate or initiate action or progress in something
stimulate or initiate action

Examples

We had to give the campaign a kick to reignite public interest.
She gave the meeting a kick by proposing a new idea.
A bonus can give productivity a kick when morale is low.

Synset Examples

He gave the project a kick to get it moving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jump-start - start or stimulate something quickly, especially an activity or process
