to get out of Dodge

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt aʊt əv dɑdʒ / (AmE), / ɡɛt aʊt əv dɒdʒ / (BrE)
to leave a place quickly, especially to avoid trouble or danger
leave a place quickly
informal

Examples

As soon as the fireworks started, we decided to get out of Dodge before things got out of hand.
The company announced layoffs, so many employees wanted to get out of Dodge while they could.
After the report was published, the minister tried to get out of Dodge and avoid the press.

Synset Examples

After the argument, he decided to get out of Dodge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flee - to run away quickly to escape danger
  • Antonym: stay - to remain in a place

Etymology

From reference to Dodge City, Kansas, made famous by Westerns, especially 'Gunsmoke', as a place to escape from.
