to hand someone their cards

1. VERB

/ hænd ˈsʌmwʌn ðɛr kɑrdz / (AmE), / hænd ˈsʌmwən ðɛː kɑːdz / (BrE)
to dismiss someone, especially from a job, or to reveal the truth about someone's situation or intentions
dismiss someone or reveal someone's true situation

Examples

After years of loyal service, the company finally handed him his cards without much explanation.
She realized her boss was about to hand her her cards after the final meeting.
When the truth came out, the board decided it was time to hand the CEO his cards.

Synset Examples

He knew it was time to hand her her cards.
It's time to hand them their cards and show the truth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire - to dismiss someone from a job
  • Synonym: to let go - to end someone's employment
  • Antonym: hire - to employ someone

Etymology

From the practice in card games of being dealt or given cards, metaphorically used for revealing the truth or ending participation.
