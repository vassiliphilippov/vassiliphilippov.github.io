freeze a brass monkey

1. X

/ friz ə bræs ˈmʌŋki / (AmE), / friːz ə brɑːs ˈmʌŋki / (BrE)
(informal) Used to emphasize that the weather is extremely cold.
idiom meaning extremely cold weather
informal british

Examples

It was so cold last night it could freeze a brass monkey outside.
They say January here can freeze a brass monkey, so dress warmly.
The icy wind this morning would freeze a brass monkey off the porch.

Synset Examples

It's cold enough to freeze a brass monkey!

Etymology

Originating in British naval slang, referring humorously to cold weather and possibly alluding to metal shrinking in low temperatures. Actual origin uncertain.
