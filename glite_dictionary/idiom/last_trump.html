last trump

1. NOUN

/ læst trʌmp / (AmE), / lɑːst trʌmp / (BrE)
the final and decisive resource, action, or strategy used when all else has failed
final decisive resource or strategy

Examples

When negotiations stalled, the manager played his last trump to persuade the board.
She kept her last trump hidden until the crucial moment in the trial.
Launching the new product was their last trump in an effort to revive sales.

Synset Examples

The CEO played his last trump to save the company.
He saved his last trump for the final round.
She considered her promotion her last trump in negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trump card - a valuable resource used as a last resort
  • Synonym: ace in the hole - a hidden advantage kept for use when needed

Etymology

Alludes to the biblical 'last trumpet' signaling final judgment, but here refers metaphorically to a last decisive move.
