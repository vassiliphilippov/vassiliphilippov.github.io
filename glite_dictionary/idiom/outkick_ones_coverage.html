to outkick one's coverage

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈkɪk wʌnz ˈkʌvɚɪdʒ / (AmE), / aʊtˈkɪk wʌnz ˈkʌvərɪdʒ / (BrE)
to do better than expected, especially by achieving or obtaining something superior to what one's circumstances, abilities, or status might predict; originally used in sports, now used more broadly
perform better than expected, surpass usual limits
informal american

Examples

Many said he outkicked his coverage by landing such a prestigious job straight out of college.
Everyone agrees that Sarah outkicked her coverage when she married someone so accomplished.
If you get into that university, you'll definitely have outkicked your coverage.

Synset Examples

In the game, he outkicked his coverage, scoring a surprise goal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to punch above one's weight - to achieve more than expected for one's position or ability

Etymology

From American football, where kick coverage teams must not run too far ahead of the kicker; figurative use since late 20th century.
