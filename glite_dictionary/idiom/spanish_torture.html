Spanish torture

1. NOUN

/ ˈspænɪʃ ˈtɔɹ.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈspanɪʃ ˈtɔː.tʃə / (BrE)
an experience or situation that causes great frustration, discomfort, or distress, often used humorously or exaggeratedly
frustrating or painful experience (idiom)
informal

Examples

That three-hour exam felt like Spanish torture to everyone in the class.
He called the tedious bureaucratic process a kind of Spanish torture.
Enduring another one of her boring stories was pure Spanish torture.

Synset Examples

She described the lengthy meeting as a form of Spanish torture.
The movie's plot twist was a real Spanish torture for the audience.
He joked that waiting in line felt like Spanish torture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ordeal - a painful or horrific experience
  • Antonym: delight - a feeling of great pleasure or satisfaction
