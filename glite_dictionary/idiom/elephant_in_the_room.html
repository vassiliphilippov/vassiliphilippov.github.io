elephant in the room

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛlɪfənt ɪn ðə rum / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪfənt ɪn ðə ruːm / (BrE)
an obvious problem or difficult situation that people do not want to talk about or acknowledge
obvious but ignored problem or issue

Examples

During the meeting, no one wanted to mention the elephant in the room: the company's financial troubles.
It's hard to have an honest conversation if we keep pretending the elephant in the room doesn't exist.
She finally addressed the elephant in the room by discussing the unresolved conflict.
