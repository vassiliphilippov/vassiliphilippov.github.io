to walk on broken glass

1. VERB

/ wɑk ɑn ˈbroʊkən ɡlæs / (AmE), / wɔːk ɒn ˈbrəʊkən ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
to endure a situation that is very difficult, painful, or tense, often requiring great caution
endure a very difficult or tense situation

Examples

After the argument, I felt I had to walk on broken glass around him to avoid another conflict.
Working for such a demanding boss is like having to walk on broken glass every day.
During the negotiations, the diplomats were constantly walking on broken glass to keep both sides satisfied.

Synset Examples

It feels like I have to walk on broken glass every day.
Living in that stressful environment makes me feel like I’m walking on broken glass.
She said talking to him was like walking on broken glass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to walk on eggshells - to act very carefully to avoid upsetting someone
