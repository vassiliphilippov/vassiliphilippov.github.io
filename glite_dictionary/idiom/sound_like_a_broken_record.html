to sound like a broken record

1. VERB

/ saʊnd laɪk ə ˈbroʊkən ˈrɛkərd / (AmE), / saʊnd laɪk ə ˈbrəʊkən ˈrɛkɔːd / (BrE)
to keep repeating the same thing, especially a complaint or opinion, to the point of annoyance
repeat oneself annoyingly

Examples

I don't want to sound like a broken record, but could you please turn off the lights when you leave?
She sounded like a broken record complaining about her job every day.
If I sound like a broken record, it's only because this is important.

Synset Examples

His complaints are starting to sound like a broken record.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harp on - to talk or complain about something repeatedly
  • Synonym: to repeat oneself - to say the same thing more than once

Etymology

Alludes to the repeating sound made when a scratched vinyl record gets stuck while playing.
