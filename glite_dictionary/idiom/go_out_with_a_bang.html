to go out with a bang

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ aʊt wɪð ə bæŋ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ aʊt wɪð ə bæŋ / (BrE)
to finish or end something in a very memorable, impressive, or exciting way
end in an exciting or impressive way

Examples

The TV series will go out with a bang in its final episode, featuring a surprise twist.
She decided to go out with a bang at her retirement party, giving a heartfelt speech and a memorable performance.
The band wants to go out with a bang at their last ever concert.

Synset Examples

He wanted his last speech to go out with a bang.
The fireworks display will go out with a bang tonight.
They planned to go out with a bang at the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: end with a flourish - to finish something in an impressive way
