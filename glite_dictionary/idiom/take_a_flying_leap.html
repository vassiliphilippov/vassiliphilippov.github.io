to take a flying leap

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə ˈflaɪɪŋ liːp / (AmE), / teɪk ə ˈflaɪɪŋ liːp / (BrE)
(informal) Used to rudely tell someone to go away or leave, expressing dismissal or rejection.
tell someone to go away rudely
informal

Examples

If you don't like my proposal, you can take a flying leap.
She told the persistent salesman to take a flying leap.
When they criticized his decision, he told them all to take a flying leap.

Synset Examples

You can take a flying leap if you think I’ll help.
When asked for a favor, she said to take a flying leap.
He told his critics to take a flying leap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get lost - to go away; leave someone alone (informal)
  • Synonym: to buzz off - to go away immediately (informal)
  • Synonym: to go away - to leave a place or someone
