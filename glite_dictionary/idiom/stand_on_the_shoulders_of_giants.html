to stand on the shoulders of giants

1. VERB

/ stænd ɑn ðə ˈʃoʊldɚz əv ˈʤaɪənts / (AmE), / stænd ɒn ðə ˈʃəʊldəz əv ˈdʒaɪənts / (BrE)
to make progress or achieve something by using the ideas, discoveries, or work of people who came before you
make progress using earlier achievements

Examples

Modern scientists often stand on the shoulders of giants to advance their research.
She acknowledged that her breakthroughs wouldn't be possible without standing on the shoulders of giants.
New technologies are often developed by standing on the shoulders of giants from previous generations.

Synset Examples

Scientists today stand on the shoulders of giants like Einstein.
We can achieve more when we stand on the shoulders of giants.
Her discoveries stand on the shoulders of giants in mathematics.
They stand on the shoulders of giants in the industry.
His discoveries were a dwarf standing on the shoulders of giants.
The scientist referred to Newton as a dwarf standing on the shoulders of giants.
Every new idea is a dwarf standing on the shoulders of giants.

Etymology

Popularized by Isaac Newton's phrase, adapted from earlier Proverbs in Western literature.
