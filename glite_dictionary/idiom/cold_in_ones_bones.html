cold in one's bones

1. NOUN

/ koʊld ɪn wʌnz boʊnz / (AmE), / kəʊld ɪn wʌnz bəʊnz / (BrE)
a deep, penetrating feeling of cold that seems to reach into the body
deep, penetrating cold felt inside the body

Examples

Even with the heater on, I could still feel the cold in my bones.
The damp weather left a cold in her bones that would not go away.
After waiting outside for hours, he had a cold in his bones that lasted all night.

Synset Examples

I can feel the cold in my bones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bone-chilling - very cold; causing a deep sense of coldness
  • Synonym: to chill to the bone - to make someone feel extremely cold
