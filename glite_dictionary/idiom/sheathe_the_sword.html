to sheathe the sword

1. VERB

/ ʃið ðə sɔrd / (AmE), / ʃiːð ðə sɔːd / (BrE)
to stop fighting or putting an end to conflict; to make peace
stop fighting; make peace

Examples

After years of rivalry, both companies agreed to sheathe the sword and partner on a new project.
It's important for leaders to sheathe the sword when negotiations reach a critical point.
They finally chose to sheathe the sword and rebuild their friendship.

Synset Examples

They decided to sheathe the sword for the sake of their friendship.
It's time to sheathe the sword and make peace.
After the argument, he chose to sheathe the sword.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make peace - bring about reconciliation or end hostilities
  • Antonym: fight - to engage in physical or verbal conflict

Etymology

Alludes to literally putting a sword back into its sheath as a symbol of ceasing to fight.
