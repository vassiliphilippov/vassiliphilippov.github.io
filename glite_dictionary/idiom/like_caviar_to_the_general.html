like caviar to the general

1. X

/ laɪk ˈkæviˌɑr tə ðə ˈdʒɛnərəl / (AmE), / laɪk ˈkævɪɑː tə ðə ˈdʒɛnərəl / (BrE)
(of something) valuable and refined, but not appreciated or understood by most people.
valuable but unappreciated by most

Examples

Her thoughtful presentation was like caviar to the general, going largely unnoticed by her colleagues.
The artist's avant-garde work was like caviar to the general and failed to attract a wide audience.
His philosophical insights were like caviar to the general, too sophisticated for most listeners.

Synset Examples

His advice was like caviar to the general, ignored by most.

Etymology

From a line in Shakespeare's 'Hamlet' (Act 2, Scene 2): "it was caviare to the general."
