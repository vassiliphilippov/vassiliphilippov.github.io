to make a snook

1. VERB

/ meɪk ə snʊk / (AmE), / meɪk ə snuːk / (BrE)
to make a rude gesture by placing one's thumb to the nose and wiggling the fingers, expressing derision or mockery
perform a mocking hand gesture
british

Examples

During the argument, he made a snook at his opponent to show his disdain.
The children made a snook behind the teacher's back when she wasn't looking.
She couldn't resist making a snook after winning the game, much to everyone's amusement.

Synset Examples

She made a snook at her friend during the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cock a snook - to make the same mocking nose-thumb gesture

Etymology

Probably from the British slang 'cock a snook', related to the idea of a taunting gesture.
