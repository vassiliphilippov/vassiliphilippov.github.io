up the boohai

1. X

/ ʌp ðə ˈbuːhaɪ / (AmE), / ʌp ðə ˈbuːhaɪ / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly NZ) Used to describe a situation that has gone wrong or failed completely.
gone wrong; failed (NZ informal)
informal

Examples

After the main investor pulled out, the whole deal went up the boohai.
If we miss today's deadline, this project could go up the boohai.
The plan was well-intentioned, but it soon went up the boohai after a series of mistakes.

Synset Examples

The project went up the boohai due to poor planning.
When the budget was cut, everything went up the boohai.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fall through - to fail to happen as planned
  • Synonym: to go awry - to go wrong or not as intended

Etymology

Probably from New Zealand English, originally referring to a remote or imaginary place (Boohai), meaning 'out of reach; irretrievable.'
