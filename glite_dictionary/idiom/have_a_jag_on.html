to have a jag on

1. VERB

/ hæv ə dʒæg ɑn / (AmE), / hæv ə dʒæg ɒn / (BrE)
To be in a bad mood or become upset, often for a temporary period.
be upset or in a bad mood
informal

Examples

Lately, he's been known to have a jag on for no clear reason.
After hearing the disappointing news, she started to have a jag on at work.
You’d better check on him—he seems to have a jag on this morning.

Synset Examples

After that breakup, he really had a jag on.
He's been known to have a jag on during the holidays.
She tends to have a jag on when she's stressed.
