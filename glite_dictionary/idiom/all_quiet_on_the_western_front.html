all quiet on the Western Front

1. X

/ ɔl ˈkwaɪət ɑn ðə ˈwɛstərn frʌnt / (AmE), / ɔːl ˈkwaɪət ɒn ðə ˈwɛstən frʌnt / (BrE)
used to say that nothing significant, unusual, or noticeable is happening, especially after a period of activity or tension
no significant activity or change

Examples

After a hectic morning, it was all quiet on the Western Front in the office.
Following the heated argument, things settled down and it was all quiet on the Western Front.
The negotiations stalled, so for now, it's all quiet on the Western Front.

Synset Examples

It was all quiet on the Western Front after the storm.
The meeting ended, and it was all quiet on the Western Front.
After the announcement, it was all quiet on the Western Front.

Etymology

From the title of the 1929 novel 'All Quiet on the Western Front' by Erich Maria Remarque, referring to inactivity along a military front.
