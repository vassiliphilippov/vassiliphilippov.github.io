to learn one's lesson

1. VERB

/ lɝn wʌnz ˈlɛsən / (AmE), / lɜːn wʌnz ˈlɛsən / (BrE)
to gain wisdom or understanding from a negative experience, often making one avoid repeating the same mistake
gain wisdom from a mistake

Examples

After failing the exam, he finally learned his lesson and started studying regularly.
She learned her lesson and now double-checks her work before submitting it.
They learned their lesson about leaving food uncovered after ants invaded the kitchen.

Synset Examples

He learned his lesson after the failed project.
After touching the hot stove, he learned his lesson.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wise up - become more aware or knowledgeable
  • Antonym: repeat one's mistake - to make the same error again

2. VERB

/ lɝːn wʌnz ˈlɛs.ən / (AmE), / lɜːn wʌnz ˈlɛs.ən / (BrE)
to understand and remember the consequences of a mistake, so as to avoid repeating it in the future
gain understanding after a mistake

Examples

After forgetting her keys twice, she finally learned her lesson and kept a spare at work.
He didn't lock his bicycle, but after it was stolen he definitely learned his lesson.
If you spend all your money at once, you will quickly learn your lesson.

Synset Examples

After that mistake, she learned her lesson well.
I finally learned my lesson about being punctual.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to learn the hard way - gain understanding through difficult or painful experience
