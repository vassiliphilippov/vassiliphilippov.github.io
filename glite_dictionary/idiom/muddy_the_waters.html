to muddy the waters

1. VERB

/ ˈmʌdi ðə ˈwɔtərz / (AmE), / ˈmʌdi ðə ˈwɔːtəz / (BrE)
to make a situation or issue more confusing or complicated than it was before
make matters more confusing

Examples

The spokesperson tried to muddy the waters during the press conference instead of answering directly.
Introducing unrelated statistics will only muddy the waters of our discussion.
His vague explanation did nothing but muddy the waters for everyone involved.

Synset Examples

Don't muddy the waters with irrelevant comments.
They tried to muddy the waters to avoid responsibility.
Her accusations only served to muddy the waters further.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confuse - to make someone unable to think clearly or understand
  • Antonym: to clarify - to make something easier to understand
