labor of love

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪbər əv lʌv / (AmE), / ˈleɪbə əv lʌv / (BrE)
work that is done for pleasure, not for money or other material reward
work done for pleasure, not money

Examples

Building the community garden was truly a labor of love for the volunteers.
Her memoir was a labor of love, written over many years without any expectation of profit.
Restoring the old house became a labor of love for the entire family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voluntary work - work done by choice without payment

2. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.bɚ əv ˈlʌv / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.bə əv ˈlʌv / (BrE)
work undertaken for pleasure or passion rather than for financial reward
work done for personal satisfaction

Examples

Volunteering at the animal shelter is a true labor of love for many people.
Writing this book was a labor of love that took more than five years to complete.
The annual community festival is organized as a labor of love by local residents.

Synset Examples

Her garden is truly a labor of love.

Restoring the old car was a labor of love for him.

Related Terms
