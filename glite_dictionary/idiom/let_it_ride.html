to let it ride

1. VERB

/ lɛt ɪt raɪd / (AmE), / lɛt ɪt raɪd / (BrE)
to allow a situation or process to continue without interfering or changing it, usually to see how it develops
allow a situation to continue without interference

Examples

Sometimes the best strategy is to just let it ride and see what happens.
When she kept complaining, he decided to let it ride rather than confront her.
Instead of making changes right away, the manager chose to let it ride for another week.

Synset Examples

Let it ride

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ignore - to intentionally pay no attention to something
  • Antonym: to intervene - to get involved in a situation to change what is happening

2. VERB

/ ˌlɛt ɪt ˈraɪd / (AmE), / ˌlɛt ɪt ˈraɪd / (BrE)
to allow a situation or issue to continue without intervening or making changes
allow something to continue without interference

Examples

Even though she felt uneasy, she decided to let it ride and see what happened.
Sometimes the best action is to let it ride rather than make things worse.
If you keep worrying about every detail, you'll never just let it ride and relax.

Synset Examples

He decided to let the argument ride instead of escalating it.
I think we should let this issue ride for now.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something

Etymology

Originally from gambling, where a player allows a bet to remain instead of collecting winnings.
