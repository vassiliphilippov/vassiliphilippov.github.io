to see things clearly

1. VERB

/ si ˈθɪŋz ˈklɪrli / (AmE), / siː ˈθɪŋz ˈklɪəli / (BrE)
to understand a situation well and without confusion or misunderstanding
understand a situation well

Examples

After stepping back from the problem, I could finally see things clearly.
Once the facts were presented, she began to see things clearly.
Talking to a friend helped him see things clearly again.

Synset Examples

I can see things clearly after our discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: have a clear picture - to fully understand a situation
  • Antonym: be in the dark - to not know or understand something
