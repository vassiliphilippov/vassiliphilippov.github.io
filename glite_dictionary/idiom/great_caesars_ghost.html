Great Caesar's ghost

1. INTJ

/ ɡreɪt ˈsiːzɚz ɡoʊst / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ˈsiːzəz ɡəʊst / (BrE)
used to express surprise, shock, or disbelief
exclamation of surprise or disbelief

Examples

Great Caesar's ghost! You scared me half to death sneaking up like that.
When the team won against all odds, the coach shouted, "Great Caesar's ghost!"
Great Caesar's ghost, how on earth did you finish that puzzle so quickly?

Synset Examples

Great Caesar's ghost! I can't believe you did that!
She exclaimed, great Caesar's ghost, that was unexpected!
When I saw the news, I thought, great Caesar's ghost!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: good grief - exclamation expressing surprise or frustration
  • Synonym: heavens above - expression of surprise or amazement

Etymology

Reference to Julius Caesar, Roman general and statesman, used in English exclamations since the late 19th century.
