once more into the breach

1. X

/ wʌns mɔːr ˈɪntu ðə briːtʃ / (AmE), / wʌns mɔː ˈɪntu ðə briːtʃ / (BrE)
An encouragement to confront a challenge again or make another attempt despite previous difficulties.
encouragement to try again despite difficulty

Examples

As the deadline approached, the group decided it was once more into the breach to finish the project.
After several failed attempts, he went once more into the breach to solve the problem.
Inspired by their leader, the soldiers went once more into the breach despite their exhaustion.

Synset Examples

She encouraged everyone, once more into the breach!
The team rallied, once more into the breach for victory.

Etymology

From Shakespeare's play Henry V: 'Once more unto the breach, dear friends, once more.'
