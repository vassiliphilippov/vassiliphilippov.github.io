shrinking violet

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃrɪŋkɪŋ ˈvaɪələt / (AmE), / ˈʃrɪŋkɪŋ ˈvaɪəlɪt / (BrE)
a person who is very shy or timid and avoids drawing attention to themselves
very shy or timid person

Examples

Despite her talent, Maria is a shrinking violet at parties and rarely speaks up.
He may seem like a shrinking violet, but when it counts, he steps up.
The job requires confidence, so a shrinking violet might struggle with the demands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wallflower - a shy person who tends to avoid social activities
  • Antonym: extrovert - an outgoing, sociable person

Etymology

From the notion that violets are small, modest flowers, suggesting timidity or shyness.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃrɪŋkɪŋ ˈvaɪələt / (AmE), / ˈʃrɪŋkɪŋ ˈvaɪələt / (BrE)
a person who is very shy or timid and tends to avoid attention
shy or timid person

Examples

Despite her talent, Anna was a true shrinking violet at parties and never mingled much.
In meetings, don't act like a shrinking violet; your ideas are valuable and should be heard.
Although he appeared confident, deep down he was a shrinking violet who dreaded public speaking.

Synset Examples

She was no shrinking violet when it came to opinions.
Don't be a shrinking violet; speak up for yourself!
He called her a shrinking violet for never expressing her views.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wallflower - a person who tends to remain on the sidelines at social events

Etymology

From the idea that violets (the flower) are small and often hidden, symbolizing shyness; recorded since the early 19th century.
