up someone's street

1. ADV

/ ʌp sʌmˌwʌnz strit / (AmE), / ʌp sʌmˌwʌnz striːt / (BrE)
(British, informal) Suited to someone's tastes, interests, or abilities; exactly the kind of thing someone likes or is interested in.
suited to someone's tastes or interests
informal british

Examples

That new science fiction novel is up your street if you enjoy space adventures.
Cooking shows are really up her street, as she's passionate about food.
If you love puzzles, this board game will be up your street.

Synset Examples

The new video game is really up his street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: right up someone's alley - exactly suited to someone's interests or abilities (chiefly US)
