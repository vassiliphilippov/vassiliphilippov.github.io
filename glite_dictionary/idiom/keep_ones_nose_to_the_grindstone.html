to keep one's nose to the grindstone

1. VERB

/ kip wʌnz noʊz tə ðə ˈɡraɪndˌstoʊn / (AmE), / kiːp wʌnz nəʊz tə ðə ˈɡraɪndstəʊn / (BrE)
to work hard and continuously, especially on something that is tedious or difficult
work persistently and hard

Examples

If you keep your nose to the grindstone, you'll finish the project by the end of the week.
She managed to get promoted by keeping her nose to the grindstone for several years.
Even during tough times, he kept his nose to the grindstone and never complained.

Synset Examples

She keeps plugging away at her dissertation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to plug away - work steadily and persistently at something
  • Antonym: to slack off - to work less hard or be less diligent

Etymology

From the imagery of someone working so closely to a grindstone that their nose almost touches it, emphasizing diligent hard work.
