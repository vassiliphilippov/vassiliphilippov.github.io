from the horse's mouth

1. ADV

/ frʌm ðə hɔrsɪz maʊθ / (AmE), / frɒm ðə hɔːsɪz maʊθ / (BrE)
(of information) obtained directly from the original, most reliable, or authoritative source.
directly from the original or reliable source

Examples

I heard the news from the horse's mouth, so you can trust it.
She got the information from the horse's mouth, not through rumors.
If you want the real story, ask him yourself and hear it from the horse's mouth.

Synset Examples

Don't believe the rumors; I got it from the horse's mouth.
She confirmed it was true from the horse's mouth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firsthand - directly observed or obtained; not from a secondary source
  • Antonym: secondhand - (of information) received from others rather than directly from the original source

Etymology

Originates from horse racing, referring to tips supposedly coming directly from the horse, the ultimate authority on its condition.
