to catch one's breath

1. VERB

/ kætʃ wʌnz brɛθ / (AmE), / katʃ wʌnz brɛθ / (BrE)
to pause to rest or recover normal breathing, especially after physical exertion or stress
pause to rest and recover breathing

Examples

After running up the stairs, he stopped to catch his breath before going into the meeting.
Give me a moment to catch my breath; that was a tough workout!
She paused halfway up the hill to catch her breath and admire the view.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to take a breather - to have a short rest or break

2. VERB

/ kætʃ wʌnz brɛθ / (AmE), / kætʃ wʌnz brɛθ / (BrE)
to pause in order to regain normal breathing, especially after exertion or a shock
pause to recover normal breathing

Examples

After running up the stairs, I had to catch my breath before starting the meeting.
Give me a moment to catch my breath—that was a tough workout!
She paused on the path to catch her breath and admire the view.

Synset Examples

She lay prostrate on the grass to catch her breath.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recover - to return to a normal state after exertion, loss, or shock
