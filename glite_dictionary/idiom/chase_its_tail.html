to chase its tail

1. VERB

/ tʃeɪs ɪts teɪl / (AmE), / tʃeɪs ɪts teɪl / (BrE)
to engage in pointless or repetitive activity without making progress
engage in pointless or repetitive activity

Examples

The company has been chasing its tail with endless meetings but has made no real decisions.
Sometimes trying to keep the house clean with young children feels like chasing its tail.
He's chasing his tail by redoing the same work without finding a solution.

Synset Examples

He watched the 401 playfully chase its tail.
The children laughed as the squirrel chased its tail.
The cat chased its tail playfully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go around in circles - to make no progress despite lots of effort
  • Synonym: run in place - to move without making progress
