to pick up one's marbles

1. VERB

/ pɪk ʌp wʌnz ˈmɑrbəlz / (AmE), / pɪk ʌp wʌnz ˈmɑːbəlz / (BrE)
(informal) To withdraw from a situation, often as a result of disagreement or dissatisfaction.
withdraw from a situation (informal)
informal

Examples

After losing the debate, she decided to pick up her marbles and leave the meeting.
Whenever he doesn't get his way, he tends to pick up his marbles and quit.
Rather than compromise, he just picked up his marbles and walked out of the project.

Synset Examples

After the argument, he decided to pick up his marbles and go home.
He threatened to pick up his marbles and go home if they didn’t cooperate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to withdraw - to pull out or remove oneself from participation
  • Synonym: to give up - to cease making an effort; admit defeat

Etymology

Refers to children's games where a player might literally take their marbles home if upset, leaving the game.
