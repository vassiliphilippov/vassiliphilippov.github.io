mountain to climb

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊn.tn̩ tə klaɪm / (AmE), / ˈmaʊn.tɪn tə klaɪm / (BrE)
a difficult challenge or task that needs to be overcome
a major challenge or obstacle

Examples

After his injury, he knew he had a mountain to climb to regain his former fitness.
The new government faces a mountain to climb in reducing national debt.
We realized there was still a mountain to climb before launching the product.

Synset Examples

She knows she has a mountain to climb to finish the project.
He felt he had a mountain to climb to regain trust.
After the audit, the team realized they have a mountain to climb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hurdle - an obstacle or difficulty to be overcome
  • Antonym: walk in the park - something that is very easy
