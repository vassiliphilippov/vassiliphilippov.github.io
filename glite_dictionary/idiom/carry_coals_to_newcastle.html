to carry coals to Newcastle

1. VERB

/ ˈkæri koʊlz tə ˈnuːkasəl / (AmE), / ˈkæri kəʊlz tə ˈnjuːkɑːsəl / (BrE)
to do something that is superfluous or unnecessary because it is already amply supplied
do something unnecessary or redundant

Examples

Trying to explain basic math to a professor is like carrying coals to Newcastle.
Bringing your own sand to the beach is essentially carrying coals to Newcastle.
Offering more chocolate to a pastry chef would be carrying coals to Newcastle.

Synset Examples

Selling ice in Alaska is just carrying coals to Newcastle.
He thought offering his advice was like carrying coals to Newcastle.
Bringing more umbrellas to Seattle is like carrying coals to Newcastle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redundant - unnecessary, more than needed
  • Synonym: superfluous - more than what is needed or wanted

Etymology

Named after Newcastle, an English city once known for its abundant coal; thus, carrying coals there is pointless.
