like a lemon

1. ADV

/ laɪk ə ˈlɛmən / (AmE), / laɪk ə ˈlɛmən / (BrE)
in a passive, idle, or awkward manner; behaving uselessly or feeling out of place, especially in a group situation
in an idle or awkward manner
informal british

Examples

He just stood there like a lemon when everyone else started dancing.
During the meeting, I felt like a lemon, not knowing what to contribute.
She waited like a lemon while her friends chatted without her.

Synset Examples

Don't sit there like a lemon; participate!
He just sat there like a lemon, not joining in.
He just stood there like a lemon, not knowing what to do.
She sat there like a lemon while everyone else laughed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awkward - feeling embarrassed or out of place
  • Synonym: stand out like a sore thumb - to be conspicuously out of place
