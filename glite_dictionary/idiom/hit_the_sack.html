to hit the sack

1. VERB

/ hɪt ðə sæk / (AmE), / hɪt ðə sæk / (BrE)
to go to bed or prepare to sleep, especially after a long day
go to bed; prepare to sleep (informal)
informal

Examples

I'm exhausted after that hike, so I'm going to hit the sack early tonight.
Whenever she has an early morning, she tries to hit the sack before ten.
After finishing his homework, Mark decided it was time to hit the sack.

Synset Examples

I usually turn in at midnight
He goes to bed at the crack of dawn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn in - to go to bed
  • Synonym: to go to bed - to get into bed in order to sleep

Etymology

Originating in early 20th-century American English; 'sack' refers to a mattress or bed.
