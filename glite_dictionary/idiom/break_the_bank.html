to break the bank

1. VERB

/ breɪk ðə bæŋk / (AmE), / breɪk ðiː bæŋk / (BrE)
To cost a great deal of money or to spend so much that it exhausts one's resources; to be very expensive.
to cost or spend a lot of money

Examples

You can find a stylish dress that won't break the bank.
Upgrading your phone doesn't have to break the bank.
We managed to redecorate the house without breaking the bank.

Synset Examples

Her shopping spree didn't break the bank this time.
The new car didn't break the bank, thankfully.
We can have a great vacation without breaking the bank.
We had a blast in Brokelona without breaking the bank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cost a fortune - to be very expensive
  • Synonym: spend a fortune - to spend a lot of money
  • Antonym: cheap - not costing a lot of money
