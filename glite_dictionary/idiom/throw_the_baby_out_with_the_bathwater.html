to throw the baby out with the bathwater

1. VERB

/ θroʊ ðə ˈbeɪ.bi aʊt wɪð ðə ˈbæθˌwɔt̬.ɚ / (AmE), / θrəʊ ðə ˈbeɪ.bi aʊt wɪð ðə ˈbɑːθˌwɔː.tə / (BrE)
to unintentionally get rid of something valuable or important while trying to remove something undesirable
discard something valuable along with the unwanted

Examples

If we scrap the entire project, we risk throwing the baby out with the bathwater.
During reforms, it's important not to throw the baby out with the bathwater by eliminating good traditions.
Let's fix the mistakes carefully and avoid throwing the baby out with the bathwater.

Synset Examples

Don't throw the baby out with the bathwater during the changes.
We must be careful not to throw the baby out with the bathwater.
In our rush to improve, let’s not throw the baby out with the bathwater.

Etymology

From a German proverb dating to the 16th century: "das Kind mit dem Bade ausschütten" (to throw the child out with the bath).
