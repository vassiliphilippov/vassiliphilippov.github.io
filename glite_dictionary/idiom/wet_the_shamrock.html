to wet the shamrock

1. VERB

/ wɛt ðə ˈʃæmrɑk / (AmE), / wɛt ðə ˈʃæmrɒk / (BrE)
to celebrate St. Patrick's Day by drinking alcohol, especially as part of a customary ritual for good luck
celebrate St. Patrick's Day with a drink for luck
british

Examples

Every year on March 17th, people across Ireland wet the shamrock to honor St. Patrick.
He's planning to wet the shamrock at the local pub with his friends this evening.
Tourists were invited to wet the shamrock and join in the festivities during the parade.

Synset Examples

It's tradition to wet the shamrock and celebrate Irish heritage.
He always remembers to wet the shamrock before the parade.
They decided to wet the shamrock for good luck on St. Patrick's Day.

Etymology

Comes from the Irish tradition of dipping a shamrock in a drink on St. Patrick's Day for good fortune.
