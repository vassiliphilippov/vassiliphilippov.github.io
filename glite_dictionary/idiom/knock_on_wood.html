knock on wood

1. X

/ nɑk ɑn wʊd / (AmE), / nɒk ɒn wʊd / (BrE)
said to avoid bad luck after mentioning good fortune or hope for a favorable outcome
superstitious phrase to avoid bad luck

Examples

We've had a great year, knock on wood!
Everything's going smoothly so far, knock on wood.
She hasn't caught a cold all winter—knock on wood.

Synset Examples

We've had a great year, knock on wood!
Everything's going well, but knock on wood.
I hope to stay healthy; knock on wood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to touch wood - British equivalent phrase for the same superstition

Etymology

Possibly from the ancient practice of touching or knocking on wood to ward off evil spirits.
