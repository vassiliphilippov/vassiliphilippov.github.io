away with the fairies

1. ADJ

/ əˈweɪ wɪð ðə ˈfɛriz / (AmE), / əˈweɪ wɪð ðə ˈfeəriːz / (BrE)
in a state of being distracted or lost in daydreams or fantasies, appearing inattentive to the present situation
lost in daydreams; distracted
british

Examples

She was so away with the fairies in class that she didn’t even hear her name called.
Whenever he’s painting, he looks away with the fairies and barely notices anyone around him.
Sorry, I was away with the fairies for a moment—what did you say?

Synset Examples

Sometimes, I feel like I'm away with the fairies.
She often seems away with the fairies during meetings.
His daydreaming makes him appear away with the fairies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daydreaming - being lost in pleasant thoughts not connected to the present
  • Synonym: absent-minded - frequently forgetting things or not paying attention

Etymology

Originally British; fairy folklore references distractibility or absence of mind.
