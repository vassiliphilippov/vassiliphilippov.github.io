nothing doing

1. X

/ ˈnʌθɪŋ ˈduɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnʌθɪŋ ˈduːɪŋ / (BrE)
used to say that something will not happen or to refuse a request or suggestion.
refusal; not happening
informal

Examples

When I asked if I could borrow the car, my dad just said, 'nothing doing.'
I tried to get tickets at the door, but it was 'nothing doing.'
She wanted to change the rules, but the committee said 'nothing doing.'

Synset Examples

When I proposed a road trip, she replied, 'Nothing doing!'
I asked if he wanted to join, but it was nothing doing.
I tried to convince him, but it was nothing doing.
