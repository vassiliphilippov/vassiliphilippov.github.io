to ride tall in the saddle

1. VERB

/ raɪd tɔl ɪn ðə ˈsædəl / (AmE), / raɪd tɔːl ɪn ðə ˈsæd(ə)l / (BrE)
to feel or act with confidence and self-assurance
feel/act confidently and self-assuredly

Examples

After landing his dream job, he began to ride tall in the saddle at work.
Her recent success on the project made her ride tall in the saddle during meetings.
Despite criticism, he continued to ride tall in the saddle, confident in his decisions.

Synset Examples

She felt confident and ready to ride tall in the saddle at the meeting.
Winning the award made him feel like he was riding tall in the saddle.
After getting promoted, he walked into the office riding tall in the saddle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confident - feeling or showing certainty and self-assurance
  • Antonym: insecure - lacking confidence or assurance; uncertain and anxious

Etymology

Originally from cowboy culture in the American West, where sitting tall in the saddle symbolized pride and confidence.
