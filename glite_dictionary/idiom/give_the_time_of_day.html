to give the time of day

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ðə taɪm əv deɪ / (AmE), / ɡɪv ðə taɪm əv deɪ / (BrE)
to pay someone any attention or show them even minimal respect, usually used negatively to indicate complete disregard
to show someone any attention or respect

Examples

He was so upset that he didn't give the time of day to anyone at the party.
After their disagreement, she refused to give the time of day to her former friend.
Despite his efforts, the manager wouldn't give the time of day to his suggestions.

Synset Examples

It's hard to believe he wouldn't give her the time of day.
She wouldn't even give him the time of day after the argument.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to someone or something
