to not sleep a wink

1. VERB

/ nɑt slip ə wɪŋk / (AmE), / nɒt sliːp ə wɪŋk / (BrE)
to not sleep at all; to get no sleep during a period of time
not sleep at all

Examples

I was so anxious about the exam that I did not sleep a wink last night.
The thunderstorm was so loud that I did not sleep a wink throughout the night.
After drinking too much coffee, she found she could not sleep a wink.

Synset Examples

After the long flight, I didn't sleep a wink.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stay awake - to remain awake and not sleep
  • Antonym: to doze off - to fall lightly asleep
