to call on the carpet

1. VERB

/ kɔl ɑn ðə ˈkɑr.pɪt / (AmE), / kɔːl ɒn ðə ˈkɑː.pɪt / (BrE)
to severely reprimand or censure someone, especially in a formal or authoritative manner.
formally reprimand; censure

Examples

After the error was discovered, the manager called Tom on the carpet during the morning meeting.
She was called on the carpet for missing several deadlines in a row.
The employee knew he would be called on the carpet after the client lodged a complaint.

Synset Examples

The mother scolded the child for entering a stranger's car
The deputy ragged the Prime Minister
The customer dressed down the waiter for bringing cold soup

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reprimand - to scold or censure formally
  • Synonym: to dress down - to scold severely
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

Originated in the late 19th century, possibly referring to being summoned before an authority who would be seated on a carpet in an office.
