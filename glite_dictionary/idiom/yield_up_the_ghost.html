to yield up the ghost

1. VERB

/ jiːld ʌp ðə ɡoʊst / (AmE), / jiːld ʌp ðə ɡəʊst / (BrE)
to die or to stop functioning, especially after a period of decline; to cease to exist
die; stop functioning (idiom)

Examples

After years of faithful service, my laptop finally yielded up the ghost last night.
When the company’s finances dried up, its operations soon yielded up the ghost.
His old watch yielded up the ghost just before the important meeting.

Synset Examples

The old car finally yielded up the ghost yesterday.
His enthusiasm for the project has yielded up the ghost.
After years of neglect, the house has yielded up the ghost.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give up the ghost - to die; to stop working (synonymous idiom)

Etymology

From the biblical phrase 'give up the ghost', meaning to die, where 'ghost' means 'spirit'.
