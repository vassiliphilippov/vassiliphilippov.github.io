news for you

1. X

/ nuːz fər ju / (AmE), / njuːz fə juː / (BrE)
used to announce information, often when it is surprising or likely to affect the listener's expectations
announcement of often unexpected information

Examples

News for you: the class has been canceled today due to the storm.
I've got news for you, and it's not what you were hoping to hear.
Here's some news for you—they decided to promote Mark instead.

Synset Examples

I've got news for you; the meeting is canceled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: I've got news for you - variant: I've got news for you
