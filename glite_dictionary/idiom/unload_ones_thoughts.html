to unload one's thoughts

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈloʊd wʌnz θɔts / (AmE), / ʌnˈləʊd wʌnz θɔːts / (BrE)
to express or share one's thoughts or feelings, often to gain relief or clarity
express or share one's thoughts or feelings

Examples

After a stressful meeting, she needed to unload her thoughts to her best friend.
It's helpful to unload your thoughts by writing them down in a journal.
He felt lighter after he unloaded his thoughts during the group discussion.

Synset Examples

He had to unload his thoughts after a long day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vent - to express feelings, especially complaints or negative emotions
  • Antonym: to bottle up - to keep one's emotions or thoughts inside and not express them

Etymology

From 'unload' (remove a load) and 'thoughts', metaphorically referring to releasing mental burdens.
