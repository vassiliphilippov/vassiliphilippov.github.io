crocodile tears

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɑː.kəˌdaɪl tɪrz / (AmE), / ˈkrɒk.ə.daɪl tɪəz / (BrE)
an insincere expression of sorrow or sympathy; a display of false or exaggerated grief
insincere or fake show of sorrow

Examples

The politician's apology was dismissed as just crocodile tears by the public.
She shed crocodile tears when her rival was fired, though everyone knew she was pleased.
His display of crocodile tears did little to win back the trust of his colleagues.

Synset Examples

the secretaries wept crocodile tears over the manager's dilemma
politicians shed crocodile tears over the plight of the unemployed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insincere grief - false or exaggerated presentation of sadness
  • Antonym: genuine tears - sincere or authentic show of sadness or emotion

Etymology

From the ancient belief that crocodiles weep while devouring their prey; recorded since the 16th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɑː.kəˌdaɪl tɪrz / (AmE), / ˈkrɒk.əˌdaɪl tɪəz / (BrE)
insincere or false expressions of sorrow or sympathy, often intended to deceive others
insincere expressions of sorrow

Examples

The politician's crocodile tears after the scandal were not convincing to the public.
After breaking the vase, she offered her mother crocodile tears rather than a true apology.
His crocodile tears during the interview failed to elicit any sympathy from the viewers.

Synset Examples

She shed crocodile tears during the apology.
They accused him of shedding crocodile tears for attention.
His crocodile tears fooled no one at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insincerity - lack of genuine feeling or honesty
  • Antonym: genuine grief - authentic expressions of sadness or sorrow

Etymology

Alludes to an ancient belief that crocodiles weep while devouring their prey. From a tale found in medieval bestiaries.
