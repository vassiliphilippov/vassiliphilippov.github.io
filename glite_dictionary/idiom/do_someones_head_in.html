to do someone's head in

1. VERB

/ du ˈsʌmwʌnz hɛd ɪn / (AmE), / duː ˈsʌmwʌnz hɛd ɪn / (BrE)
to make someone feel very annoyed, confused, or mentally distressed
cause annoyance or mental distress
informal british

Examples

The constant arguments at home are doing my head in.
All that paperwork really did his head in last week.
If you keep shouting, you're going to do her head in!

Synset Examples

The constant noise from the construction site is doing my head in.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to annoy - to make someone a little angry or irritated
  • Synonym: to disturb - to interrupt the calm or peace of someone, causing mental discomfort
