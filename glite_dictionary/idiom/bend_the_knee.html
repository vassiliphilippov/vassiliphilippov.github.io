to bend the knee

1. VERB

/ bɛnd ðə ni / (AmE), / bɛnd ðə ni / (BrE)
to submit or show loyalty or obedience to someone, often as a symbol of acknowledging their authority
submit or show loyalty

Examples

Despite his pride, he was forced to bend the knee to the new rulers.
The villagers refused to bend the knee and stood firm against the invaders.
She would never bend the knee to anyone, no matter the cost.

Synset Examples

She had to bend the knee to maintain peace.
The knight chose to bend the knee in loyalty.
He refused to bend the knee to anyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to submit - to accept or yield to a superior force or authority
  • Antonym: to resist - to refuse to accept or comply with something

Etymology

Refers to the act of kneeling as a sign of submission or allegiance, historically associated with feudal and royal ceremony.
