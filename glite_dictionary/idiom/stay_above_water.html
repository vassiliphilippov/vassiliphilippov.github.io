to stay above water

1. VERB

/ steɪ əˈbʌv ˈwɔt̬ɚ / (AmE), / steɪ əˈbʌv ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
to manage to survive or cope, especially in difficult situations, often relating to finances or obligations
manage to survive or cope

Examples

Even with two jobs, she's barely able to stay above water these days.
We need to cut expenses if we want to stay above water until next year.
After the unexpected repairs, it became hard for the company to stay above water.

Synset Examples

She took on extra shifts to stay above water financially.
After paying the bills, I barely stayed above water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keep one's head above water - to just manage to survive or avoid failure
  • Antonym: to go under - to fail or be overwhelmed, especially financially
