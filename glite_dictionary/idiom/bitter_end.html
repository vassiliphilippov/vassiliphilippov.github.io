bitter end

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪt̬ɚ ɛnd / (AmE), / ˈbɪtə end / (BrE)
the final and most difficult or unpleasant part of a process or situation, especially when someone continues until it is finished despite challenges
the final, often unpleasant, stage

Examples

Even with little hope left, they defended their home to the bitter end.
She refused to give up and stayed with the project until the bitter end.
The negotiations dragged on to the bitter end, with neither side willing to compromise.

Synset Examples

he was determined to fight to the bitter end

Related Terms

  • Synonym: last gasp - the very last moment or effort before the end
  • Synonym: finish line - the end or goal of a race or process

Etymology

Originally a nautical term referring to the inboard end of a ship's anchoring cable, later used figuratively.
