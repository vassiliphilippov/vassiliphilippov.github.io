to get a fix of

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ə fɪks ʌv / (AmE), / ɡɛt ə fɪks ɒv / (BrE)
to obtain or consume something one craves or needs, often temporarily satisfying a desire or habit
obtain or consume something craved

Examples

Every morning, I need to get a fix of caffeine before I can function properly.
After a week without his favorite show, he was eager to get a fix of entertainment.
During stressful times, she likes to get a fix of chocolate to calm her nerves.

Synset Examples

He went to the store to get a fix of his favorite snack.
She needs to get a fix of coffee to wake up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get a dose of - to receive an amount of something, especially medicine or an experience

Etymology

From the slang use of 'fix' to mean a dose of a drug, extended to cravings for other things.
