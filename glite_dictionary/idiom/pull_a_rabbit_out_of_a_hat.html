to pull a rabbit out of a hat

1. VERB

/ pʊl ə ˈræbɪt aʊt əv ə hæt / (AmE), / pʊl ə ˈræbɪt aʊt ɒv ə hæt / (BrE)
to produce a surprising or ingenious solution to a problem, especially at the last possible moment
produce a surprising solution unexpectedly

Examples

When the company faced bankruptcy, the new CEO managed to pull a rabbit out of a hat and secure funding.
We were struggling with the deadline, but somehow Sarah pulled a rabbit out of a hat and finished the project on time.
If you want to impress the investors, you'll have to pull a rabbit out of a hat during tomorrow's presentation.

Synset Examples

He managed to pull a rabbit out of a hat at the meeting.
Can you pull a rabbit out of a hat for this project?
She always knows how to pull a rabbit out of a hat during presentations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: work magic - to achieve a remarkable or seemingly impossible result
  • Synonym: to save the day - to successfully resolve a difficult situation

Etymology

From the magician's trick of producing a rabbit from an apparently empty hat.
