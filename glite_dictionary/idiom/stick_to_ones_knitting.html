to stick to one's knitting

1. VERB

/ stɪk tu wʌnz ˈnɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / stɪk tʊ wʌnz ˈnɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
to focus on one's own tasks or area of expertise, especially by avoiding distractions or interference in other matters
focus on one's own tasks or responsibilities
informal

Examples

My manager told me to stick to my knitting and let her deal with external clients.
Entrepreneurs often succeed when they stick to their knitting instead of chasing every new trend.
If we stick to our knitting, we'll finish this project more efficiently.

Synset Examples

She prefers to stick to her knitting and not get involved in office politics.
It's best to stick to your knitting and focus on your strengths.
He believes in sticking to his knitting instead of branching out too much.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mind one's own business - to avoid interfering in other people's affairs
  • Antonym: to meddle - to interfere in something that is not one's concern

Etymology

Metaphorically uses 'knitting' (a focused manual task) to suggest paying attention to one's own work. Originated in early 20th-century American English.
