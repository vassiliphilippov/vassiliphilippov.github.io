flea in one's ear

1. NOUN

/ fli ɪn wʌnz ɪr / (AmE), / fliː ɪn wʌnz ɪə / (BrE)
A suspicion, concern, or sometimes a sharp rebuke; a feeling of doubt or unease about something.
a suspicion or concern; a sharp rebuke

Examples

After overhearing their conversation, she left with a flea in her ear about what was really happening.
His boss gave him a flea in his ear for coming in late three times this week.
The detective’s unusual questions left the suspect with a flea in his ear about the investigation.

Synset Examples

She had a flea in her ear about his intentions.

Etymology

From the French idiom 'une puce à l’oreille', literally 'a flea in the ear', meaning a cause for concern or suspicion.
