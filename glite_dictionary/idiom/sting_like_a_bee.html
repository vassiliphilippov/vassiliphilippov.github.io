to sting like a bee

1. VERB

/ stɪŋ laɪk ə bi / (AmE), / stɪŋ laɪk ə biː / (BrE)
to hurt, attack, or criticize sharply and intensely, often in a sudden or forceful way
hurt or criticize sharply and intensely

Examples

Her clever remarks can really sting like a bee if you're not careful.
The comedian's jokes stung like a bee and left the audience speechless.
His criticism stings like a bee, making people think twice before speaking out.

Synset Examples

That bee sting will sting like a bee!
His sharp words can sting like a bee.
The criticism stung like a bee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hurt - to cause physical or emotional pain
  • Antonym: gentle - having or showing a mild, kind, or tender temperament or character

Etymology

Popularized by the boxer Muhammad Ali in the phrase 'float like a butterfly, sting like a bee.'
