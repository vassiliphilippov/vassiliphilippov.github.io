to hang a Louie

1. VERB

/ hæŋ ə ˈluːi / (AmE), / hæŋ ə ˈluːi / (BrE)
(North American informal) to make a left turn, especially while driving or cycling.
make a left turn (informal)
informal american

Examples

When you reach the main street, hang a Louie at the first light.
He realized too late that he needed to hang a Louie to get to the gas station.
At the next intersection, just hang a Louie and the restaurant will be on your right.

Synset Examples

He had to hang a Louie to avoid the traffic.
Don't forget to hang a Louie at the next intersection.
She quickly hung a Louie to get back on track.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hang a right - to make a right turn, especially when driving

Etymology

From "hang," meaning 'make a turn,' and "Louie" (by association with 'left'), paralleling "hang a Ralph" (for 'right').
