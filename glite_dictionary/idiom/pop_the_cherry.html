to pop the cherry

1. VERB

/ pɑp ðə ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / pɒp ðə ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
(informal, sometimes vulgar) To do something for the first time, especially to use or experience something new; originally referring to a person's first sexual experience.
do something for the first time (idiom, informal)
informal vulgar american

Examples

He finally got to pop the cherry by playing his new guitar at the concert.
When you pop the cherry on a new project, there's always a learning curve.
She was excited to pop the cherry at her first art exhibition.

Synset Examples

Finally, I was able to pop the cherry on my first marathon.
He was excited to pop the cherry on his new skateboard.
They let her pop the cherry on the first cake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to break in - to use something for the first time, or make it ready for use
  • Synonym: initiate - to begin or start something for the first time

Etymology

Originally US slang; metaphor from slang use of 'cherry' for virginity.
