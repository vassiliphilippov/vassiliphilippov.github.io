slave of the lamp

1. NOUN

/ sleɪv əv ðə læmp / (AmE), / sleɪv əv ðə læmp / (BrE)
a person who is excessively dedicated to work, often at the expense of rest or leisure
person overly devoted to work

Examples

Since the big project started, I've become a slave of the lamp, rarely leaving my desk before midnight.
He realized one day that being a slave of the lamp was damaging his health and relationships.
Many young professionals turn into slaves of the lamp hoping to impress their bosses.

Synset Examples

He felt like a slave of the lamp, always at his computer.
As a writer, I often become a slave of the lamp.
Working late made her feel like a slave of the lamp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: workaholic - person who compulsively works long hours

Etymology

From the magical lamp in 'Aladdin', where the genie was bound to serve; here, the lamp symbolizes continuous, exhausting work by its light.
