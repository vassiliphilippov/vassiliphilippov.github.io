to have a finger in many pies

1. VERB

/ hæv ə ˈfɪŋɡɚ ɪn ˈmɛni paɪz / (AmE), / hæv ə ˈfɪŋɡə ɪn ˈmeni paɪz / (BrE)
to be involved in several different activities or enterprises, often at the same time
be involved in many activities

Examples

As a manager, she tends to have a finger in many pies at the office.
Despite his busy schedule, John loves to have a finger in many pies and never says no to a new project.
You can tell he likes to have a finger in many pies because he is always part of different committees.

Synset Examples

It's common for entrepreneurs to have their fingers in many pies.
She likes to have her fingers in many pies, exploring various interests.
He admits to having his fingers in many pies at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multi-task - do several things at once
  • Antonym: to specialize - focus on one particular activity or subject
