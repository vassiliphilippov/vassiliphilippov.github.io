out of the blocks

1. ADV

/ aʊt əv ðə blɑks / (AmE), / aʊt əv ðə blɒks / (BrE)
starting very quickly or energetically at the beginning of an activity or event, especially compared to others
starting quickly or energetically

Examples

The new company got out of the blocks with immediate success in its first quarter.
She was quick out of the blocks in responding to the latest challenge.
His campaign was slow out of the blocks, but he soon gained momentum.

Synset Examples

The athlete stormed out of the blocks with determination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: off to a flying start - beginning very well or energetically

Etymology

From athletics, where the starting blocks are used by sprinters to launch at the start of a race.
