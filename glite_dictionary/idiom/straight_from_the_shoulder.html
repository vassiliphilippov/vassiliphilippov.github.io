straight from the shoulder

1. ADV

/ streɪt frəm ðə ˈʃoʊldɚ / (AmE), / streɪt frəm ðə ˈʃəʊldə / (BrE)
in a direct, honest, and straightforward manner, without trying to hide one's true feelings or opinions
in a frank and direct way

Examples

The manager spoke straight from the shoulder about the company's problems.
You always know where you stand with her—she talks straight from the shoulder.
His advice was given straight from the shoulder, with no sugar-coating.

Synset Examples

He spoke straight from the shoulder during the meeting.
The coach talked straight from the shoulder after the game.
She gave her opinion straight from the shoulder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluntly - in a straightforward and direct manner, often without regard for politeness
  • Synonym: frankly - in an open, honest, and direct manner
  • Antonym: indirectly - not in a straight or direct manner, often evasive or tactful

Etymology

Originally referring to a punch delivered directly from the shoulder in boxing, signifying forceful directness.
