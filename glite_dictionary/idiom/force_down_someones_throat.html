to force down someone's throat

1. VERB

/ fɔrs daʊn ˈsʌmwʌnz θroʊt / (AmE), / fɔːs daʊn ˈsʌmwʌnz θrəʊt / (BrE)
to impose ideas, beliefs, or opinions on someone in a way that is considered aggressive or unwanted
impose ideas or opinions aggressively

Examples

The teacher tried to force down her students' throats what she believed was the only correct approach.
It's unhelpful to force your political views down other people's throats during family gatherings.
He resented having rules forced down his throat without any explanation or discussion.

Synset Examples

I don’t want to force my opinions down your throat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ram (something) down someone's throat - to force someone to accept something unwanted
