to play like a violin

1. VERB

/ pleɪ laɪk ə vaɪˈoʊlɪn / (AmE), / pleɪ laɪk ə vaɪˈəʊlɪn / (BrE)
to manipulate someone easily and skillfully, often for personal gain
manipulate someone skillfully

Examples

She knew exactly what to say to play like a violin when she wanted something.
Politicians sometimes play like a violin to gain public support for their policies.
He felt betrayed after realizing she had played him like a violin for years.

Synset Examples

The salesman tried to play me like a violin to close the deal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to manipulate - to control or influence a person cleverly or unscrupulously
  • Synonym: to outwit - to deceive or defeat someone by being more clever
