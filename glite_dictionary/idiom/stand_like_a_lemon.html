to stand like a lemon

1. VERB

/ stænd laɪk ə ˈlɛmən / (AmE), / stænd laɪk ə ˈlɛmən / (BrE)
to appear awkward, embarrassed, or out of place by doing nothing in a situation where action is expected
to act awkwardly by doing nothing
informal british

Examples

When my friends started playing football, I just stood like a lemon on the sidelines.
He felt shy at the party and ended up standing like a lemon most of the time.
If you don't know anyone at the conference, don't just stand like a lemon; go introduce yourself!

Synset Examples

When they asked him to dance, he stood there like a lemon.
Don't just stand there like a lemon; join in!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stand like a muppet - to look foolish or awkward while doing nothing
