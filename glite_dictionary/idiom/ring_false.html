to ring false

1. VERB

/ rɪŋ fɔls / (AmE), / rɪŋ fɔːls / (BrE)
to seem untrue, insincere, or implausible
seem untrue or insincere

Examples

Her apology seemed to ring false despite her earnest expression.
The company's explanations ring false to many customers.
If a statement rings false, people are likely to doubt your honesty.

Synset Examples

His excuses ring false to me.
The information provided seemed to ring false.
Her story rings false; I don’t believe it.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to ring true - to seem genuine, believable, or sincere
