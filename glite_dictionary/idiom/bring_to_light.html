to bring to light

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ tə laɪt / (AmE), / brɪŋ tə laɪt / (BrE)
to reveal or make something known, especially facts that were previously hidden or secret
reveal or make known

Examples

The journalist's investigation helped bring to light several irregularities in the company's finances.
A thorough review will bring to light any mistakes that were overlooked before.
The letter finally brought to light the real reason for her sudden resignation.

Synset Examples

Activists hope to bring these issues to light during the conference.
The investigation aimed to bring the truth to light.
New evidence may bring the matter to light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expose - to make something visible or reveal something hidden
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep something hidden or secret
