in tandem

1. ADV

/ ɪn ˈtændəm / (AmE), / ɪn ˈtændəm / (BrE)
If people or things do something in tandem, they do it together or in coordination with each other.
together; in coordination

Examples

The two departments worked in tandem to improve efficiency.
She prefers to run projects in tandem with a partner.
Economic growth and job creation often happen in tandem.

Synset Examples

They worked in tandem to complete the project.
In tandem, they achieved their goals faster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: together - with or in proximity to another person or people
  • Synonym: cooperation - the process of working together for the same end

Etymology

From the Latin word 'tandem' (at length, at last), used in English to refer to two horses harnessed one behind the other.
