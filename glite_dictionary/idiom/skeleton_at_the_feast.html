skeleton at the feast

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɛlətən ət ðə fiːst / (AmE), / ˈskɛlɪtən ət ðə fiːst / (BrE)
A negative or disturbing presence that overshadows and spoils an otherwise joyful occasion.
issue souring a happy event

Examples

Unfortunately, news of the layoffs became the skeleton at the feast during the company party.
The unresolved argument was the skeleton at the feast in an otherwise cheerful family dinner.
Their financial troubles were a constant skeleton at the feast at every celebration.

Synset Examples

His sudden outburst was the skeleton at the feast during dinner.
The budget cuts were the skeleton at the feast of our celebration.
Her ex's presence was the skeleton at the feast of their reunion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoilsport - a person who spoils the pleasure of others

Etymology

Alludes to ancient Roman practice of displaying a skeleton at banquets to remind guests of mortality.
