to assume room temperature

1. VERB

/ əˈsum ruːm ˈtɛmpɚətʃɚ / (AmE), / əˈsjuːm ruːm ˈtɛmpərətʃə / (BrE)
to reach the same temperature as the surrounding environment, especially after being left out from a cooled or heated state.
reach the ambient temperature

Examples

If the butter is left out, it will assume room temperature within an hour.
Let the eggs assume room temperature before adding them to the batter.
The wine should assume room temperature for best flavor before serving.

Synset Examples

If you leave that out, it will assume room temperature.
Make sure to cook the meat before it assumes room temperature.
