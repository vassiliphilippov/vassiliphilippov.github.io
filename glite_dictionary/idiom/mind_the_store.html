to mind the store

1. VERB

/ maɪnd ðə stɔɹ / (AmE), / maɪnd ðə stɔː / (BrE)
to take care of a shop or business, especially while the owner or usual person is away
look after a shop or business

Examples

Could you mind the store while I step out for lunch?
She trusted him to mind the store during her vacation.
When the manager left early, Tom was asked to mind the store.

Synset Examples

I asked her to mind the store during my absence.
He agreed to mind the store for a few hours.
Can you mind the store while I take my break?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to look after - to take care of someone or something
