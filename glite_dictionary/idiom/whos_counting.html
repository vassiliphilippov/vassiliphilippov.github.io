who's counting

1. X

/ huːz ˈkaʊntɪŋ / (AmE), / huːz ˈkaʊntɪŋ / (BrE)
used to suggest that the exact number of times something has happened is unimportant or not worth mentioning, often humorously or to minimize an issue.
it doesn't matter how many times

Examples

I've been late three times this week, but who's counting?
We’ve ordered takeout maybe ten times this month—who's counting?
He’s made the same joke at least six times, but who's counting?

Synset Examples

I've read this book five times, but who's counting?
We've had a few arguments, but who's counting?
I've made a few mistakes, but who's counting?
