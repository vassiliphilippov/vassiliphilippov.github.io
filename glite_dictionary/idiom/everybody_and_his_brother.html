everybody and his brother

1. X

/ ˈɛvriˌbɑdi ənd hɪz ˈbrʌðər / (AmE), / ˈɛvribɒdi ənd hɪz ˈbrʌðə / (BrE)
a phrase meaning almost everyone; a very large number of people
almost everyone

Examples

Everybody and his brother turned up at the festival this year.
The new restaurant is so popular that everybody and his brother is trying to get a reservation.
You can't keep it a secret—everybody and his brother already knows.

Synset Examples

It seems like everybody and his brother wants to join the club.
Everybody and his brother showed up to the party.
Everybody and his brother was talking about the concert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: everyone and his dog - another idiom meaning almost everyone
