by no means

1. ADV

/ baɪ ˈnoʊ ˌminz / (AmE), / baɪ ˈnəʊ ˌmiːnz / (BrE)
not at all; in no way; definitely not.
not at all; definitely not

Examples

The outcome of the project is by no means guaranteed despite everyone's hard work.
She is by no means convinced that this is the best solution.
The weather is by no means perfect for a picnic today.

Synset Examples

the prize is by no means certain
and that isn't all, not by a long sight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: not at all - not in any way or to any degree
  • Antonym: by all means - certainly; of course (opposite meaning)

2. ADV

/ baɪ noʊ minz / (AmE), / baɪ nəʊ miːnz / (BrE)
used to emphasize that something is not true or not possible at all; not in any way.
not at all; in no way

Examples

The work is by no means finished yet, so please be patient.
By no means should you disclose this information to anyone outside the company.
Success is by no means guaranteed, even with hard work.

Synset Examples

He is by no means the best player on the team.
This challenge is by no means impossible.
This is by no means an easy task.
He's not a bad player, not by any means.
It's not perfect, not by any means.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: by any means - in any way at all; whatsoever

Synset POS: None
