to beat the poop out of

1. VERB

/ biːt ðə puːp aʊt ʌv / (AmE), / biːt ðə puːp aʊt ɒv / (BrE)
(informal) to physically assault or severely punish someone; to beat someone very badly.
to physically beat or punish severely
informal

Examples

If you insult him again, he might beat the poop out of you.
The group threatened to beat the poop out of anyone who tried to cheat them.
He jokingly said his older brother would beat the poop out of him if he lost the game.

Synset Examples

They said they would beat the poop out of the bully.
He threatened to beat the poop out of anyone who crossed him.
The kids play rough, but they don’t actually beat the poop out of each other.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beat the crap out of - to beat someone very badly (vulgar)
  • Synonym: thrash - to beat someone repeatedly and violently

Etymology

Alteration of the more vulgar phrase 'beat the crap/hell out of', substituting 'poop' as a milder term.
