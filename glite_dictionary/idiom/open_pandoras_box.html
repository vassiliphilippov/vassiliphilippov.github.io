to open Pandora's box

1. VERB

/ ˈoʊpən pænˈdɔːrəz bɑks / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən pænˈdɔːrəz bɒks / (BrE)
to do something that leads to many unexpected problems or difficulties
initiate many unforeseen problems

Examples

She was warned that raising that topic would open Pandora's box of controversies.
The scientist feared that the experiment might open Pandora's box and result in unknown dangers.
By allowing exemptions, the committee may have opened Pandora's box of new legal challenges.

Synset Examples

That decision could open Pandora's box of unforeseen consequences.
Once you start questioning authority, you might open Pandora's box.
Revisiting that old debate might open Pandora's box.
Her decision to move away opened a Pandora's box of emotions.
Once we started discussing politics, it opened Pandora's box.
Opening that email felt like opening Pandora's box.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: can of worms - a complex situation likely to cause trouble

Etymology

From the Greek myth of Pandora, who opened a box (actually a jar) releasing all the world's troubles.
