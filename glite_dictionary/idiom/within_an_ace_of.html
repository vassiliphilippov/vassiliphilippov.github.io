within an ace of

1. ADV

/ wɪˈðɪn ən eɪs ʌv / (AmE), / wɪˈðɪn ən eɪs ɒv / (BrE)
very nearly; extremely close to achieving or experiencing something
very close to achieving something

Examples

After months of hard work, he came within an ace of finishing first in the race.
The team was within an ace of winning the championship but lost in the final seconds.
She was within an ace of quitting her job before the promotion offer arrived.

Synset Examples

He's always been within an ace of winning the championship.
She felt she was within an ace of her life after the accident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on the verge of - very close to doing or experiencing something
  • Synonym: on the brink of - very near to achieving or experiencing something, especially something significant or dangerous

Etymology

From the noun 'ace', meaning a single spot on a die or a playing card, signifying a very small margin; first used in this sense in the 18th century.
