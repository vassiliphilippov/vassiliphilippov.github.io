not my circus, not my monkeys

1. X

/ nɑt maɪ ˈsɝːkəs nɑt maɪ ˈmʌŋkiːz / (AmE), / nɒt maɪ ˈsɜːkəs nɒt maɪ ˈmʌŋkiːz / (BrE)
used to express that something is not one's responsibility or concern, especially when declining involvement in a problematic situation
not my responsibility or concern (idiom)

Examples

Whenever office politics arise, I remind myself not my circus, not my monkeys.
When asked to mediate their argument, he replied, "Not my circus, not my monkeys."
She listened to their complaints and thought, 'not my circus, not my monkeys.'

Synset Examples

Their drama is not my circus, not my monkeys.
I don’t want to get involved; it’s not my circus, not my monkeys.
Her issues are not my circus, not my monkeys to handle.
That's their problem; it's not your circus, not your monkeys.
I won't get involved; it's not your circus, not your monkeys.
Remember, it's not your circus, not your monkeys.

Etymology

Calque from Polish 'nie mój cyrk, nie moje małpy', meaning 'not my circus, not my monkeys'.
