to believe my eyes

1. VERB

/ bəˈliv maɪ aɪz / (AmE), / bɪˈliːv maɪ aɪz / (BrE)
to be so surprised or astonished by what one sees that it is difficult to accept it as real
be amazed by what one sees

Examples

I couldn't believe my eyes when I saw the size of the cake.
When she walked into the room, he had to believe his eyes—she looked incredible.
You'll hardly believe your eyes when you see the transformation.

Synset Examples

I couldn't believe my eyes when I saw the sunset.
He claimed he could fly, and I couldn't believe my eyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cannot believe my eyes - be so surprised it seems impossible
  • Synonym: believe one's ears - to be astonished by what one hears
