to sidestep the issue

1. VERB

/ ˈsaɪdˌstɛp ði ˈɪʃu / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌstɛp ði ˈɪsjuː / (BrE)
to deliberately avoid addressing or dealing with a particular problem or difficult topic
avoid addressing a problem

Examples

Whenever asked about his past, he would sidestep the issue by changing the subject.
The spokesperson tried to sidestep the issue rather than give a straightforward answer.
It's frustrating when politicians sidestep the issue instead of offering real solutions.

Synset Examples

He tried to sidestep the issue during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evade - to escape or avoid, especially by cleverness or trickery
  • Antonym: to confront - to face a problem or challenge directly
