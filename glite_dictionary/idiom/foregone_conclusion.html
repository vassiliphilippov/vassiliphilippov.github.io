foregone conclusion

1. NOUN

/ fɔrˈɡɔn kənˈkluʒən / (AmE), / fɔːˈɡɒn kənˈkluːʒən / (BrE)
a result or outcome that is certain to happen and cannot be avoided
an inevitable outcome

Examples

The outcome of the match was a foregone conclusion after the first half.
Many considered the election result to be a foregone conclusion due to the candidate's popularity.
With such a strong lead, their victory became a foregone conclusion long before the final results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certainty - something that is sure to happen
  • Antonym: possibility - something that may or may not happen

Etymology

From the past participle of 'forego' (meaning to go before) and 'conclusion.' Popularized by Shakespeare in 'Othello.'
