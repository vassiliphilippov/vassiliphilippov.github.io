at a pinch

1. ADV

/ æt ə pɪntʃ / (AmE), / æt ə pɪntʃ / (BrE)
if necessary and only in a difficult or emergency situation
if absolutely necessary; in an emergency

Examples

We have only five chairs but, at a pinch, we can bring in a stool from the kitchen.
She could drive the car herself at a pinch if no one else is available.
There are enough sandwiches, but at a pinch we can make some more.

Synset Examples

I can cook dinner at a pinch.
You could use a spoon at a pinch.
We can share a room at a pinch.
