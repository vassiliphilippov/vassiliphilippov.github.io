to spread like wildfire

1. VERB

/ sprɛd laɪk ˈwaɪldfaɪɚ / (AmE), / sprɛd laɪk ˈwaɪldfaɪə / (BrE)
to increase or spread extremely quickly and widely
become widespread very quickly

Examples

Rumors about the CEO's resignation spread like wildfire throughout the entire company.
The news of their engagement spread like wildfire on social media.
False information can spread like wildfire if people aren't careful about what they share online.

Synset Examples

News of the wildfire traveled like wildfire through town.
The quidnunctious gossip spread like wildfire.
The twitteration about the event spread like wildfire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spread rapidly - to increase or distribute with great speed
  • Antonym: spread slowly - to increase or distribute at a slow rate
