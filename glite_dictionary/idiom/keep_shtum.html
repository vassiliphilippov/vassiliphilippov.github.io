to keep shtum

1. VERB

/ kip ʃtʊm / (AmE), / kiːp ʃtʊm / (BrE)
to remain silent or refuse to speak, especially deliberately or in order to avoid trouble
remain silent; say nothing
informal british

Examples

If I were you, I'd keep shtum about what happened last night.
The witness decided to keep shtum when questioned by the police.
They told her to keep shtum until the right moment.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

From Yiddish *shtum* (silent, mute), itself from German *stumm* (silent, mute).
