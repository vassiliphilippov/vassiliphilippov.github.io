to walk a mile in someone's shoes

1. VERB

/ wɑk ə maɪl ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz ʃuz / (AmE), / wɔːk ə maɪl ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz ʃuːz / (BrE)
to try to understand another person's experiences, challenges, or feelings by imagining oneself in their situation
try to understand someone's experience

Examples

It's important to walk a mile in someone's shoes before offering criticism.
She tried to walk a mile in his shoes to better understand his struggles.
Wisdom often comes from the willingness to walk a mile in someone's shoes.

Synset Examples

He learned empathy by walking a mile in her shoes.
It's easy to criticize, but try to walk a mile in their shoes.
Before judging her, you should walk a mile in her shoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to empathize - to understand and share the feelings of another
  • Antonym: judge - to form an opinion or conclusion, often without full understanding
