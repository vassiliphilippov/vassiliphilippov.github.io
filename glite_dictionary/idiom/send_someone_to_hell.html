to send someone to hell

1. VERB

/ sɛnd ˈsʌmˌwʌn tu hɛl / (AmE), / sɛnd ˈsʌmwʌn tuː hɛl / (BrE)
to angrily dismiss or reject someone, expressing extreme anger, contempt, or disdain toward them
angrily dismiss or reject someone
informal

Examples

After the heated debate, she sent him to hell in front of everyone.
Feeling betrayed, Tom finally sent his boss to hell and quit the job.
She couldn't take his behavior anymore and sent him to hell over the phone.

Synset Examples

She sent him to hell after their argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tell someone off - to scold or reprimand someone strongly
  • Synonym: curse someone out - to angrily insult someone using foul language
