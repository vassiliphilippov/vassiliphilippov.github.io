to scare the living daylights out of

1. VERB

/ skɛr ðə ˈlɪvɪŋ ˈdeɪlaɪts ˈaʊt ʌv / (AmE), / skeə ðə ˈlɪvɪŋ ˈdeɪlaɪts ˈaʊt ɒv / (BrE)
to frighten someone extremely; to cause someone to be very scared
frighten someone very much

Examples

That horror movie scared the living daylights out of me.
The thunderstorm scared the living daylights out of my little brother last night.
She jumped out from behind the door and scared the living daylights out of him.

Synset Examples

I didn't mean to scare the living daylights out of you!
That haunted house is sure to scare the living daylights out of visitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to frighten - to make someone feel afraid
  • Degree Higher: to terrify - to fill someone with intense fear
