spot of bother

1. NOUN

/ spɑt əv ˈbɑðər / (AmE), / spɒt əv ˈbɒðə / (BrE)
a minor difficulty or problem; a small amount of trouble
minor difficulty or trouble

Examples

She was in a spot of bother when she lost her wallet at the station.
We had a spot of bother with the computer system this morning but it was fixed quickly.
If you ever find yourself in a spot of bother, just ask for help.

Synset Examples

He got into a spot of bother over the misplaced keys.
I found myself in a spot of bother with my car.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: difficulty - a situation that is hard to deal with; trouble
  • Degree Higher: crisis - a time of intense difficulty or danger
