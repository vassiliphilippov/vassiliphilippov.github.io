to raise someone's spirits

1. VERB

/ reɪz sʌmwʌnz ˈspɪrɪts / (AmE), / reɪz sʌmwʌnz ˈspɪrɪts / (BrE)
to make someone feel happier or more cheerful; to lift someone's mood
make someone feel happier

Examples

A thoughtful message from a friend can raise someone's spirits after a difficult day.
Hearing his favorite song always seemed to raise his spirits.
The team's victory helped raise the spirits of the entire community.

Synset Examples

She brought flowers to raise his spirits after the loss.
A kind word can really raise someone's spirits.
A surprise party is sure to raise her spirits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheer someone up - to make someone feel happier
  • Synonym: to hearten - to encourage or make someone more cheerful
  • Antonym: lower someone's spirits - to make someone feel less happy or more discouraged
