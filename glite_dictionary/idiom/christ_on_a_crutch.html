Christ on a crutch

1. X

/ kraɪst ɑn ə krʌtʃ / (AmE), / kraɪst ɒn ə krʌtʃ / (BrE)
An expression used to describe a weak or inadequate excuse, or to suggest that someone is being excessively dependent.
expression for a weak excuse or excessive dependence

Examples

His explanation was just Christ on a crutch—no one believed it for a second.
Relying on that old argument is like leaning on Christ on a crutch.
Every time something goes wrong, he blames bad luck, but that's just Christ on a crutch again.

Synset Examples

His excuses were just Christ on a crutch.
Using that line is like having Christ on a crutch.
