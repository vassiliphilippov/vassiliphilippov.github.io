the back of one's hand

1. NOUN

/ ðə bæk əv wʌnz hænd / (AmE), / ðə bæk əv wʌnz hænd / (BrE)
used to say that someone knows a place, subject, or situation extremely well or thoroughly
very familiar; extremely well known

Examples

She knows these streets like the back of her hand, so she never gets lost.
After working here for ten years, he understands the building like the back of his hand.
I’ve read this book so often that I know it like the back of my hand.

Synset Examples

He can navigate the area like the back of his hand.
He knows the key of the street like the back of his hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inside out - knowing something extremely thoroughly
  • Synonym: like the palm of one's hand - very familiar with something
