to work with one's hands

1. VERB

/ wɝk wɪð wʌnz hændz / (AmE), / wɜːk wɪð wʌnz hændz / (BrE)
to perform tasks involving physical labor, manual skills, or craftwork rather than mental or administrative work
do physical labor or craft

Examples

My grandfather used to work with his hands as a carpenter all his life.
She prefers to work with her hands rather than sit at a computer all day.
People who work with their hands often develop practical problem-solving skills.

Synset Examples

He enjoys jobs where he can work with his hands daily.
He loves to work with his hands on carpentry projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manual labor - work involving physical effort rather than mental effort
