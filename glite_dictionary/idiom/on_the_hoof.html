on the hoof

1. ADV

/ ɑn ðə huf / (AmE), / ɒn ðə huːf / (BrE)
while in motion or busy; done quickly and without careful planning, often while occupied with other tasks.
done quickly while busy or moving

Examples

We grabbed lunch on the hoof between meetings since our schedule was so packed.
Politicians sometimes make promises on the hoof without fully thinking them through.
She managed to prepare her presentation on the hoof during her commute.

Synset Examples

I prefer to make decisions on the hoof instead of overthinking.
We can discuss it on the hoof as we walk.
She often writes her reports on the hoof while commuting.

Etymology

Originally referring to livestock being alive and moving, later extended figuratively to mean 'while on the move.'
