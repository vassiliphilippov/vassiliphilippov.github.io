to sell down the river

1. VERB

/ sɛl daʊn ðə ˈrɪvɚ / (AmE), / sɛl daʊn ðə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
to betray someone, especially to gain a personal advantage or benefit
betray someone for personal gain

Examples

I can't believe you would sell me down the river just to save your own job.
Many loyal employees felt they had been sold down the river by the new management.
She accused him of selling her down the river during the negotiations.

Synset Examples

Don't sell me down the river for a promotion.
She was devastated when her friend sold her down the river.
He felt betrayed when they sold him down the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to betray - to be disloyal or act treacherously toward someone

Etymology

Originally referred to the sale of slaves to plantations further south along the Mississippi River, where conditions were harsher.
