to come calling

1. VERB

/ kʌm ˈkɔlɪŋ / (AmE), / kʌm ˈkɔːlɪŋ / (BrE)
to arrive or occur unexpectedly, often bringing significant or unwelcome consequences
arrive or occur unexpectedly

Examples

Come calling in the middle of the night, and you might not get a warm welcome.
When trouble comes calling, it's best to be prepared for anything.
Sometimes, opportunity comes calling just when you need it most.

Synset Examples

Disappointment often comes calling when you least expect it.
When success came calling, she was ready to seize it.
The opportunity to collaborate came calling at the perfect time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn up - to appear unexpectedly or without warning
  • Synonym: to show up - to arrive or become apparent, often unexpectedly
