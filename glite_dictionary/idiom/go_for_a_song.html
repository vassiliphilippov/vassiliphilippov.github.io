to go for a song

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ fəɹ ə sɔŋ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ fə ə sɒŋ / (BrE)
to be sold for much less than its value; to be sold very cheaply
to be sold very cheaply

Examples

The old piano went for a song at the auction.
With so many houses on the market, some properties are going for a song.
Her designer handbag went for a song at the secondhand store.

Synset Examples

The car was practically given away, it went for a song.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bargain - something bought or sold at a low price
  • Antonym: cost an arm and a leg - to be very expensive

Etymology

From the idea that a song is something of little value; first recorded in the 19th century.
