dirt on someone

1. NOUN

/ dɚt ɑn ˈsʌmwʌn / (AmE), / dɜːt ɒn ˈsʌmwʌn / (BrE)
information about someone that could cause embarrassment or harm to their reputation if revealed
damaging or embarrassing information
informal

Examples

The journalist claimed to have dirt on several politicians.
If you want a promotion, you better hope your boss doesn't find any dirt on you.
She threatened to go public with the dirt on her former employer.

Synset Examples

She had some dirt on him that could cause trouble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scandal - an action or event regarded as morally or legally wrong and causing public outrage
  • Synonym: secret - something kept hidden or unexplained
