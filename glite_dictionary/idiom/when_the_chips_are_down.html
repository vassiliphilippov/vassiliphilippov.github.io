when the chips are down

1. SCONJ

/ wɛn ðə tʃɪps ɑr daʊn / (AmE), / wɛn ðə tʃɪps ɑː daʊn / (BrE)
when a situation becomes critical or when you are faced with serious difficulties
in a crisis or difficult situation

Examples

You truly learn who you can rely on when the chips are down.
Even when the chips are down, she remains optimistic and determined to succeed.
The team's true character showed when the chips are down during the championship game.

Synset Examples

He always performs well when the chips are down.
True friends show their support when the chips are down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in a pinch - in a difficult situation, if necessary
  • Synonym: in a jam - in a difficult or troublesome situation

Etymology

From gambling, where 'chips' refer to tokens staked in games like poker; popularized in mid-20th century American English.
