to take the gilt off the gingerbread

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə ɡɪlt ɔf ðə ˈdʒɪndʒɚˌbrɛd / (AmE), / teɪk ðə ɡɪlt ɒf ðə ˈdʒɪndʒəˌbrɛd / (BrE)
to diminish or spoil the attractive or appealing aspect of something
spoil or lessen appeal

Examples

The disappointing sequel really took the gilt off the gingerbread for many fans.
Repeated delays have taken the gilt off the gingerbread regarding the new project's launch.
Her careless remarks unfortunately took the gilt off the gingerbread at the celebration.

Synset Examples

Her mistakes took the gilt off the gingerbread during the performance.
The harsh criticism took the gilt off the gingerbread for her presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoil the effect - make something less impressive or enjoyable
  • Synonym: take the shine off - make something less impressive or enjoyable

Etymology

From the literal practice of gilding gingerbread to make it attractive; used figuratively since the 19th century.

2. VERB

/ teɪk ðə ɡɪlt ɔf ðə ˈdʒɪndʒərˌbrɛd / (AmE), / teɪk ðə ɡɪlt ɒf ðə ˈdʒɪndʒəˌbrɛd / (BrE)
to diminish the charm, excitement, or attractiveness of something; to make something seem less appealing or special than it first appeared
diminish the appeal of something
british

Examples

The technical glitches took the gilt off the gingerbread during the opening ceremony.
Her careless remarks took the gilt off the gingerbread of his achievement.
Rain on the wedding day unfortunately took the gilt off the gingerbread for the happy couple.

Synset Examples

His rude comments took the gilt off the gingerbread of the event.

Etymology

Refers to the tradition of decorating gingerbread with gold leaf ('gilt') to make it look special; removing the gold lessens its splendor. Common in British English from the 19th century.
