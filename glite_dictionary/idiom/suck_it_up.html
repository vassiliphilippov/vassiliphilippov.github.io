to suck it up

1. VERB

/ sʌk ɪt ʌp / (AmE), / sʌk ɪt ʌp / (BrE)
to endure a difficult or unpleasant situation without complaining
endure without complaining
informal

Examples

If you want to play on the team, you’ll just have to suck it up and practice harder.
Sometimes, you need to suck it up and deal with harsh criticism in your job.
He didn’t really like the assignment, but he decided to suck it up and do his best.

Synset Examples

If you can't handle it, just suck it up.
Sometimes, you just have to suck it up and move on.
She decided to suck it up and face her fears.
You need to suck it up and finish the job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tough it out - to get through a difficult situation by being tough
  • Synonym: to put up with - to tolerate an unpleasant situation or person
