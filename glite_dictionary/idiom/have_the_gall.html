to have the gall

1. VERB

/ hæv ðə ɡɔl / (AmE), / hæv ðə ɡɔːl / (BrE)
to have the audacity or boldness to do something considered disrespectful or shocking
have the audacity or nerve

Examples

I couldn't believe she had the gall to blame us for her own mistake.
They had the gall to demand a refund after damaging the goods themselves.
He had the gall to interrupt the meeting to talk about unrelated topics.

Synset Examples

She had the gall to ask for a raise after being late.
He had the gall to criticize my work without offering help.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to have the nerve - to have the courage or audacity to do something bold
  • Synonym: to have the cheek - to behave with impudent boldness, especially in British English
  • Antonym: show humility - to act modestly or without boldness

Etymology

From 'gall' meaning impudence or boldness, originally referring to bile, associated with bitterness and daring.
