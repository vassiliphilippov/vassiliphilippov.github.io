to come in hot

1. VERB

/ kʌm ɪn hɑt / (AmE), / kʌm ɪn hɒt / (BrE)
to arrive with enthusiasm or urgency; to perform with high energy or intensity; to arrive suddenly and unexpectedly
arrive or act with energy or urgency
informal

Examples

The new manager came in hot and started making changes on the very first day.
The rescue team came in hot, ready to act at a moment's notice.
At the meeting, she came in hot with passionate arguments for her proposal.

Synset Examples

She came in hot with her new project idea.
The athlete came in hot during the final lap.
The news report came in hot, surprising everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burst onto the scene - arrive suddenly with noticeable energy or impact
  • Synonym: make a dramatic entrance - to arrive with a lot of attention or excitement
