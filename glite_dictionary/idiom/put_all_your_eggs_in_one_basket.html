to put all your eggs in one basket

1. VERB

/ pʊt ɔl jʊr ɛgz ɪn wʌn ˈbæskət / (AmE), / pʊt ɔːl jɔː ɛgz ɪn wʌn ˈbɑːskɪt / (BrE)
to risk all of your resources or opportunities on a single venture or plan, so that if it fails, everything is lost
risk everything on one plan

Examples

She decided not to put all her eggs in one basket by applying to several colleges.
If you put all your eggs in one basket, you could lose everything in a downturn.
It's risky to put all your eggs in one basket when investing your savings.

Synset Examples

He didn't want to put all his eggs in one basket with that deal.
Don't put all your eggs in one basket when planning your career.
Investing in one stock is putting all your eggs in one basket.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hedge your bets - to protect yourself against loss by supporting more than one possible result or option
