to eat humble pie

1. VERB

/ it ˈhʌm.bəl paɪ / (AmE), / iːt ˈhʌm.bəl paɪ / (BrE)
to admit that you were wrong, usually in a way that is embarrassing or humiliating
admit one's mistake with embarrassment

Examples

After the board proved him wrong, he had to eat humble pie in front of his colleagues.
She hated to eat humble pie but knew she owed everyone an apology.
He refused to eat humble pie even after realizing his mistake.

Synset Examples

After the mistake, he had to eat humble pie.
It's time to eat humble pie and admit I was wrong.
She learned to eat humble pie after her blunder.
After his mistake, he had to eat humble pie at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to apologize - to say sorry for a fault or offense
  • Antonym: boast - to speak with excessive pride or self-satisfaction

Etymology

From the 17th-century expression "to eat umble pie" (where "umbles" were deer offal), later associated with "humble."
