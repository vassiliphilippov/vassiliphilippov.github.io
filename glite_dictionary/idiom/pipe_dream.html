pipe dream

1. NOUN

/ paɪp driːm / (AmE), / paɪp driːm / (BrE)
an unrealistic hope or fantasy; a plan or idea that is impossible or very unlikely to happen
unrealistic hope or fantasy

Examples

Owning a mansion by the sea is just a pipe dream for most people.
He realized that his hopes of becoming a movie star might be a pipe dream.
They dismissed her business proposal as a pipe dream, but she refused to give up.

Synset Examples

His plans to travel the world were a pipe dream.
Winning the lottery is just a pipe dream.
She had a pipe dream of traveling the world.
Starting a business without funds is a pipe dream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fantasy - imaginative idea not based in reality
  • Synonym: delusion - false belief or idea
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

Originally referred to the fantasies induced by smoking an opium pipe in the late 19th century.
