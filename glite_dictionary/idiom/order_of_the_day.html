order of the day

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər əv ðə deɪ / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə əv ðə deɪ / (BrE)
the most important or prevailing issue, practice, or activity at a particular time
most important issue or practice at a time

Examples

Innovation is the order of the day in the technology industry.
During the crisis, swift action became the order of the day.
At the annual meeting, transparency was clearly the order of the day.

Synset Examples

Honesty is the order of the day in our meetings.
Safety protocols are the order of the day here.
Teamwork was the order of the day for the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watchword - a word or phrase that expresses a principle or goal to be followed
  • Synonym: priority - something regarded as more important than another
