cloak-and-dagger

1. ADJ

/ ˌkloʊk ənd ˈdæɡər / (AmE), / ˌkləʊk ənd ˈdæɡə / (BrE)
Involving secrecy, intrigue, or espionage, often characterized by hidden motives or methods.
marked by secrecy or intrigue

Examples

The agency was involved in many cloak-and-dagger missions during the Cold War.
He enjoys novels filled with cloak-and-dagger plots and mysterious characters.
Rumors of cloak-and-dagger dealings at the corporation spread quickly through the office.

Synset Examples

clandestine intelligence operations
cloak-and-dagger activities behind enemy lines
hole-and-corner intrigue
secret missions
a secret agent
secret sales of arms
surreptitious mobilization of troops
an undercover investigation
underground resistance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secret - kept hidden or not known by others
  • Synonym: clandestine - kept secret or done secretively, especially for an illicit purpose

Etymology

Refers to the 19th-century imagery of masked or disguised characters in romantic novels who carried a cloak and a dagger, symbolizing secrecy and intrigue.
