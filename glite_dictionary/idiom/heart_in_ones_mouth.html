heart in one's mouth

1. NOUN

/ hɑrt ɪn wʌnz maʊθ / (AmE), / hɑːt ɪn wʌnz maʊθ / (BrE)
feeling extremely anxious, nervous, or fearful, especially in anticipation of something uncertain or frightening
feeling anxious or fearful

Examples

I had my heart in my mouth when I heard footsteps behind me on the dark street.
Her heart was in her mouth as she waited for the interview results to be announced.
Their heart was in their mouth during the tense final moments of the game.

Synset Examples

As she waited for the result, her heart was in her mouth.
When the car skidded, my heart was in my mouth.
His heart was in his mouth during the final moments of the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on edge - nervous and unable to relax
  • Antonym: calm - not feeling anxious or nervous
