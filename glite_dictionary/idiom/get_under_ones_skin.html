to get under one's skin

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈʌndɚ wʌnz skɪn / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈʌndə wʌnz skɪn / (BrE)
to irritate, annoy, or become a persistent source of discomfort or concern to someone
irritate or annoy persistently

Examples

His constant criticism really gets under my skin.
The noises from the construction site started to get under her skin after a few days.
Don't let his rude remarks get under your skin; he's just trying to provoke you.

Synset Examples

Her childish behavior really gets to me
His lying really gets me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to annoy - to irritate or make someone slightly angry
  • Synonym: bother - to disturb or cause concern to someone
  • Antonym: to soothe - to calm or reduce irritation or anxiety
