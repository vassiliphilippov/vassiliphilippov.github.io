to get out of someone's sight

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt aʊt əv ˈsʌmwʌnz saɪt / (AmE), / ɡɛt aʊt ɒv ˈsʌmwənz saɪt / (BrE)
to leave someone's presence, especially because they are angry or upset with you
leave someone's presence in anger

Examples

She was so angry she yelled, 'Get out of my sight!'
After the argument, he told his brother to get out of his sight and never come back.
If you can't behave, just get out of my sight right now.

Synset Examples

She was so angry she screamed, 'Just get out of my sight!'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go away - to leave a place or a person
  • Antonym: stay - to remain in the same place
