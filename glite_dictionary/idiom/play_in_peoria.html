to play in Peoria

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ɪn piˈɔriə / (AmE), / pleɪ ɪn piˈɔːriə / (BrE)
(of an idea, product, or performance) to be accepted or succeed with mainstream or ordinary audiences, especially in small towns or typical communities in the United States.
be accepted by mainstream or average audiences
american

Examples

The new show may be popular in New York, but will it play in Peoria?
Before launching the campaign nationwide, the company wants to see if it will play in Peoria.
The director decided to change the script, worried that the comedy wouldn't play in Peoria.

Synset Examples

Many comedians start by playing in Peoria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: play to the gallery - appeal to popular taste rather than critical judgment

Etymology

Refers historically to testing theatrical productions in Peoria, Illinois, considered a typical American town.
