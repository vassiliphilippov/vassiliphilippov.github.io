to put one's best foot forward

1. VERB

/ pʊt wʌnz bɛst fʊt ˈfɔrwərd / (AmE), / pʊt wʌnz bɛst fʊt ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to make a great effort to make a good impression or to succeed, especially when starting something new
make a strong effort or impression

Examples

You should always put your best foot forward when meeting new people.
The company encouraged employees to put their best foot forward during the big project.
She tried to put her best foot forward at the job interview to impress the panel.

Synset Examples

He decided to put his best foot forward during the presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: make a good impression - to cause people to think well of you
