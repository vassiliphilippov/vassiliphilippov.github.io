no rest for the weary

1. X

/ noʊ rɛst fər ðə ˈwɪri / (AmE), / nəʊ rɛst fə ðə ˈwɪəri / (BrE)
used to express the idea that even tired people must continue working or dealing with problems without a break
continuous work without relief

Examples

Working late into the night, she sighed, "no rest for the weary."
With three children and a full-time job, there's truly no rest for the weary.
After finishing the big project, he realized there was no rest for the weary as another task awaited.

Synset Examples

He found that in this industry, there’s no rest for the weary.
She laughed, saying there’s no rest for the weary in this job.
After the long meeting, there was no rest for the weary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: no peace for the wicked - used to suggest that evildoers will not find relief or escape from trouble
