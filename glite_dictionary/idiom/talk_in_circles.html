to talk in circles

1. VERB

/ tɑk ɪn ˈsɝkəlz / (AmE), / tɔːk ɪn ˈsɜːklz / (BrE)
to discuss a subject repeatedly without making progress or reaching a conclusion
discuss without reaching a conclusion

Examples

Whenever the meeting gets heated, people just talk in circles instead of making decisions.
We spent over an hour talking in circles about budget cuts without agreeing on anything.
If we continue to talk in circles, this project will never move forward.

Synset Examples

I felt frustrated as we talked in circles for hours.
We keep talking in circles without making a decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to beat around the bush - avoid addressing the main point
  • Antonym: to get to the point - address the main subject directly
