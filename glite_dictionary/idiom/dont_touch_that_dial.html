don't touch that dial

1. X

/ doʊnt tʌtʃ ðæt daɪəl / (AmE), / dəʊnt tʌtʃ ðæt daɪəl / (BrE)
used to tell the audience to continue listening or watching and not to change the station or channel, especially during a break in programming.
keep listening or watching; stay tuned (old media idiom)

Examples

The host said, "Don't touch that dial, we'll be back with more news after the break."
After these messages, don't touch that dial—there's an exclusive interview coming up.
Classic TV shows often told viewers, "Don't touch that dial, there's more fun ahead!"

Synset Examples

We’ll be right back after this break, so don’t touch that dial!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stay tuned - remain listening or watching for more

Etymology

Originated in the early days of radio and television, when stations had dials for tuning.
