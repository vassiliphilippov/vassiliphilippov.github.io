who are you and what have you done with [someone]

1. X

/ hu ɑr ju ənd wʌt həv ju dʌn wɪð [ˈsʌmwʌn] / (AmE), / huː ɑː juː ənd wɒt həv juː dʌn wɪð [ˈsʌmwʌn] / (BrE)
a humorous way of expressing surprise at someone behaving very differently from usual, as if they had been replaced by someone else
humorous expression of surprise at changed behavior

Examples

You did the dishes without being asked—who are you and what have you done with my son?
He actually arrived on time today; who are you and what have you done with John?
She volunteered to lead the meeting—who are you and what have you done with Emma?

Synset Examples

When he showed up early, I wondered, who are you and what have you done with someone?
