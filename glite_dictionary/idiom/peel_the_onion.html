to peel the onion

1. VERB

/ pil ði ˈʌnjən / (AmE), / piːl ði ˈʌnjən / (BrE)
to examine or analyze a problem, situation, or subject carefully, especially by uncovering its different layers or aspects one at a time
analyze something in depth, layer by layer

Examples

In our investigation, we had to peel the onion to uncover all the facts.
Therapists often help clients peel the onion of their emotions to reach the core issues.
The journalist promised to peel the onion and reveal the truth behind the scandal.

Synset Examples

Let's peel the onion and see what the real issue is.
During the meeting, we will peel the onion regarding our budget concerns.
We need to peel the onion to understand the problem deeply.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get to the bottom of - to find the real cause of something
  • Antonym: scrape the surface - to deal with only a small part of a subject or problem

Etymology

Figurative use of the literal act of peeling an onion, evoking the idea of uncovering successive layers.
