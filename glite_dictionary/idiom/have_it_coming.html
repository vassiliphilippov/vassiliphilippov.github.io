to have it coming

1. VERB

/ hæv ɪt ˈkʌmɪŋ / (AmE), / hæv ɪt ˈkʌmɪŋ / (BrE)
to deserve something, usually something bad or unpleasant, because of one's actions or behavior.
deserve (usually something unpleasant)

Examples

After lying to so many people, she had it coming when no one believed her story.
Some think the company had it coming after years of ignoring complaints.
Don’t feel sorry for him—he has it coming with the way he treats others.

Synset Examples

It's too bad he got fired, but he sure had it coming

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deserve - to be worthy of something, especially as a result of actions

2. VERB

/ hæv ɪt ˈkʌmɪŋ / (AmE), / hæv ɪt ˈkʌmɪŋ / (BrE)
to deserve the consequences, punishment, or criticism that one receives, especially because of one’s own actions or behavior.
deserve the consequences received

Examples

After years of cheating his customers, he finally got arrested; he had it coming.
She warned him to stop lying, so when people stopped trusting him, he had it coming.
If you treat your friends badly, don't be surprised when you have it coming.

Synset Examples

He treated everyone poorly, so he had it coming.
She knew the criticism was coming; she had it coming.
After that lie, he had it coming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deserve - to be worthy of; to merit a consequence or reward
