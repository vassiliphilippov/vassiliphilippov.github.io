with a fine-tooth comb

1. X

/ wɪð ə faɪn tuθ koʊm / (AmE), / wɪð ə faɪn tuːθ kəʊm / (BrE)
If you go through something with a fine-tooth comb, you examine it very carefully and thoroughly.
to examine extremely carefully

Examples

She examined the documents with a fine-tooth comb before signing them.
Detectives went over the crime scene with a fine-tooth comb to find any evidence.
The editor checked every line of the manuscript with a fine-tooth comb.

Synset Examples

She went through the report with a fine-tooth comb.
He checked the contract with a fine-tooth comb for errors.
We need to examine this project with a fine-tooth comb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scrutinize - to examine closely or minutely
  • Degree Lower: scan - to examine quickly, sometimes carefully

Etymology

Originally referring to using a comb with closely spaced teeth for meticulous searching; figurative use since the 19th century.
