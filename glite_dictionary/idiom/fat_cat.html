fat cat

1. NOUN

/ fæt kæt / (AmE), / fæt kæt / (BrE)
a wealthy and powerful person, especially one who is perceived as greedy or selfish
wealthy or powerful person

Examples

Many voters resent the influence of fat cats in politics.
After the merger, only the fat cats at the top benefited.
Some believe that tax laws favor corporate fat cats over ordinary people.

Synset Examples

The fat cat lounged on the windowsill all day.
Everyone loved that fat cat at the shelter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tycoon - a wealthy and powerful business person
  • Antonym: pauper - an extremely poor person

Etymology

Originally American slang (early 20th century), referring to wealthy political donors; from 'fat' meaning wealthy and 'cat' as a slang term for a person.
