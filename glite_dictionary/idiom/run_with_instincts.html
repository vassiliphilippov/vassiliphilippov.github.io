to run with instincts

1. VERB

/ rʌn wɪð ɪnˈstɪŋkts / (AmE), / rʌn wɪð ɪnˈstɪŋkts / (BrE)
to follow one's intuition or natural feeling when making decisions, rather than relying solely on logic or analysis
follow one's intuition

Examples

She decided to run with her instincts and accepted the job offer.
Sometimes it's best to run with your instincts when facing tough choices.
Great leaders often run with their instincts to make bold decisions.

Synset Examples

He tends to run with his instincts when making decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trust one's gut - to rely on one's intuition
