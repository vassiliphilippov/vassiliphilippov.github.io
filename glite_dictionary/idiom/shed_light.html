to shed light

1. VERB

/ ʃɛd laɪt / (AmE), / ʃɛd laɪt / (BrE)
to make something clear or easier to understand; to provide new information that helps explain something
explain or make clear

Examples

The new evidence may shed light on the cause of the accident.
Her comments helped shed light on the confusing policy.
The historian's research sheds light on what really happened during that period.

Synset Examples

The report will shed light on the recent events.
The documentary sheds light upon the struggles of the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clarify - to make clear or easier to understand
  • Synonym: to elucidate - to make clear; to explain
  • Antonym: obscure - to make unclear or difficult to understand
