to feel like Lazarus

1. VERB

/ fil laɪk ˈlæzərəs / (AmE), / fiːl laɪk ˈlæzərəs / (BrE)
to feel revitalized or revived after a period of illness, hardship, or difficulty
feel revived after difficulty

Examples

After weeks of exhaustion, she felt like Lazarus when her energy finally returned.
When the medication started working, he felt like Lazarus coming back to life.
I felt like Lazarus emerging from my room after being bedridden with the flu.

Synset Examples

He felt like Lazarus after finally recovering from the flu.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revive - to regain energy or strength
  • Synonym: feel reborn - to feel as if given new life or energy

Etymology

Refers to Lazarus, whom Jesus is said to have raised from the dead in the New Testament of the Bible.
