blue wall of silence

1. NOUN

/ blu wɑl əv saɪləns / (AmE), / bluː wɔːl əv saɪləns / (BrE)
An unwritten code among police officers not to report on a colleague's errors or misconduct, resulting in a refusal to cooperate with investigations.
code of police silence around misconduct

Examples

The documentary exposed a blue wall of silence that hindered the investigation of police misconduct.
Despite mounting evidence, officers maintained a blue wall of silence in the trial.
Efforts to break the blue wall of silence have faced resistance within law enforcement.

Synset Examples

The police maintained a blue wall of silence during the investigation.
Witnesses felt intimidated by the blue wall of silence.
Journalists often encounter a blue wall of silence in such cases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: code of silence - an agreement not to talk about something, especially wrongdoing
  • Antonym: whistleblower - a person who exposes secret misconduct

Etymology

Derived from 'blue' (the color of police uniforms), 'wall' (barrier), and 'silence' (refusal to speak); popularized in the late 20th century.
