rotten apple

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɑtən ˈæpəl / (AmE), / ˌrɒtən ˈæpəl / (BrE)
a person whose negative influence is likely to corrupt or harm the rest of a group
person who corrupts a group

Examples

Everyone on the committee is hardworking except for that rotten apple who always causes trouble.
The teacher warned that one rotten apple could disrupt the entire classroom.
If there's a rotten apple in the group, it can ruin the team spirit.

Synset Examples

He’s become the rotten apple of the team.
Don't let that rotten apple influence your decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bad apple - a person who negatively influences others in a group

Etymology

From the proverb 'one rotten apple spoils the whole barrel,' alluding to a single bad element corrupting the rest.
