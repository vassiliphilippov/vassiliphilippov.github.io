to lurk in the shadows

1. VERB

/ lɝk ɪn ðə ˈʃædoʊz / (AmE), / lɜːk ɪn ðə ˈʃædəʊz / (BrE)
To remain hidden, often with the intent to watch or wait stealthily; to stay out of sight, often for a mysterious or suspicious purpose.
hide or wait stealthily out of sight

Examples

Many wild animals lurk in the shadows, waiting for the perfect moment to pounce on their prey.
She always had the feeling that someone was lurking in the shadows as she walked home at night.
Fear seemed to lurk in the shadows of the abandoned building, making everyone uneasy.

Synset Examples

The legend spoke of a ghoul lurking in the shadows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lie in wait - to remain hidden and ready to attack, often used for ambush
  • Synonym: hide - to put or keep out of sight; conceal from view
