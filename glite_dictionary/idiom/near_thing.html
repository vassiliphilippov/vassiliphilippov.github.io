near thing

1. NOUN

/ nɪr θɪŋ / (AmE), / nɪə θɪŋ / (BrE)
a situation in which failure or disaster is narrowly avoided
narrow escape from failure or disaster

Examples

It was a near thing, but the plane landed safely despite the storm.
England almost lost the match; it was a near thing.
I had a near thing this morning when I almost missed my train.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: close call - a situation in which something dangerous or unpleasant almost happens
  • Synonym: narrow escape - a situation where someone just manages to avoid danger or failure
