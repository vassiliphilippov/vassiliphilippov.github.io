to throw one's hat over the fence

1. VERB

/ θroʊ wʌnz hæt oʊvɚ ðə fɛns / (AmE), / θrəʊ wʌnz hæt əʊvə ðə fens / (BrE)
to commit oneself to a course of action or challenge, making it clear that one intends to follow through despite difficulties
commit to a challenge or course of action

Examples

After years of doubt, she finally threw her hat over the fence and applied to medical school.
Deciding to open his own restaurant, Mark has thrown his hat over the fence for good.
Sometimes, the only way to make progress is to throw your hat over the fence and not look back.

Synset Examples

She threw her hat over the fence to show her dedication.
By moving to the city, she threw her hat over the wall.
He decided to throw his hat over the fence and start anew.
Throwing his hat over the fence symbolized his commitment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commit oneself - to dedicate oneself to a task or decision

Etymology

Possibly from Irish folklore, where throwing one's hat over a wall indicated commitment to retrieving it, thus committing to a challenge.
