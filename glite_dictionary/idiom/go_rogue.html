to go rogue

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ roʊɡ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ rəʊɡ / (BrE)
to act independently and unexpectedly, often disregarding rules or authority
act independently, defy authority

Examples

After years of strict compliance, the scientist decided to go rogue and pursue his own research.
Some software developers may go rogue and release unauthorized updates.
If you go rogue on this project, you could risk your job.

Synset Examples

He chose to go rogue after feeling stifled by rules.
Sometimes you have to go rogue to achieve your goals.
The agent decided to go rogue and investigate alone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to defy - to resist or refuse to obey authority or rules

Etymology

From 'rogue' (a dishonest or unprincipled person); 'go rogue' popularized in modern English from late 20th century media.
