trial by fire

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪ.əl baɪ ˈfaɪ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.əl baɪ ˈfaɪ.ə / (BrE)
a test or experience that is very difficult, often used to prove someone's abilities under pressure or in challenging circumstances.
a difficult, proving test or ordeal

Examples

Becoming a manager during the company crisis was a real trial by fire for her.
His first week at the hospital was a trial by fire as he learned to handle emergencies.
The intense boot camp was designed to be a trial by fire for new recruits.

Synset Examples

The chef's trial by fire involved cooking for a large event.
He faced a trial by fire during his first presentation.
Starting a new job can feel like a trial by fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baptism by fire - a person's first experience with a new and difficult task

Etymology

From the medieval practice of 'trial by fire,' in which a person's innocence was tested by subjecting them to painful ordeals.
