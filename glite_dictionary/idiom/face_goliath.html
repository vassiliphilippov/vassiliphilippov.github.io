to face Goliath

1. VERB

/ feɪs ɡəˈlaɪəθ / (AmE), / feɪs ɡəˈlaɪəθ / (BrE)
to confront a very powerful opponent or a formidable challenge; to take on something much stronger or larger than oneself
confront a formidable opponent or challenge

Examples

When the small startup decided to face Goliath, everyone doubted they could succeed.
She knew she would have to face Goliath in court to defend her rights.
The team showed courage by facing Goliath in the final match.

Synset Examples

He faced Goliath when he challenged the company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: David and Goliath - a situation in which a smaller or weaker opponent faces a much larger or stronger adversary

Etymology

From the biblical story of David and Goliath, where Goliath represents a giant enemy.
