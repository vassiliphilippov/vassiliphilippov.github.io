long live the king

1. X

/ lɔŋ lɪv ðə kɪŋ / (AmE), / lɒŋ lɪv ðə kɪŋ / (BrE)
an exclamation expressing loyalty and support for a reigning king, often used at the moment of succession
exclamation of loyalty for a king

Examples

The crowd shouted, 'long live the king!' as the new monarch took the throne.
After the coronation ceremony, supporters raised their voices and declared, 'long live the king!'
The phrase 'long live the king!' echoed throughout the palace halls during the celebration.

Synset Examples

After the old monarch passed, the crowd cheered, 'The king is dead, long live the king!'
The royal family mourned, yet tradition dictated: 'The king is dead, long live the king.'
In a sudden twist of fate, the announcement echoed: 'The king is dead, long live the king!'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long live the queen - exclamation of loyalty for a queen
  • Synonym: God save the king - traditional anthem in praise of the king

Etymology

From the traditional proclamation upon the accession of a new monarch, reflecting the continuity of monarchy.
