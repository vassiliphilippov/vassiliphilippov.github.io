to make merry

1. VERB

/ meɪk ˈmɛri / (AmE), / meɪk ˈmɛri / (BrE)
to celebrate in a lively and noisy way, often involving drinking and cheerful activities
celebrate in a lively, noisy way

Examples

The townspeople gathered in the square to make merry during the annual festival.
They stayed up late, singing songs and making merry with friends.
After the successful project, the team decided to make merry at their favorite bar.

Synset Examples

The members of the wedding party made merry all night
Let's whoop it up — the boss is gone!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revel - to take part in noisy festivities, especially with drinking
  • Synonym: carouse - to drink and enjoy oneself in a noisy way
  • Synonym: to celebrate - to observe or honor with ceremonies and festivities
