to fight one's corner

1. VERB

/ faɪt wʌnz ˈkɔrnər / (AmE), / faɪt wʌnz ˈkɔːnə / (BrE)
to defend one's position, opinions, or interests against opposition or criticism
defend one's position or interests
british

Examples

In meetings, it's essential to fight one's corner if you want your ideas taken seriously.
She always fights her corner, even when others doubt her perspective.
During contract talks, he knew he would need to fight his corner to secure better terms.

Synset Examples

She learned to fight her corner at work.
It’s important to fight your corner in negotiations.
He will always fight his corner in a debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stand one's ground - refuse to change position or yield in an argument
  • Antonym: to give in - to yield or surrender in argument or confrontation
