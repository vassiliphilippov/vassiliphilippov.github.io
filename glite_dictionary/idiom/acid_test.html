acid test

1. NOUN

/ ˈæsɪd ˌtɛst / (AmE), / ˈæsɪd ˌtɛst / (BrE)
a decisive or critical test that proves the value, quality, or truth of something
decisive or crucial appraisal

Examples

The real acid test of her leadership will come during the crisis.
Tonight's game will be an acid test for the new team.
The product's launch served as an acid test for the company's marketing strategy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: litmus test - a test that uses a single indicator to prompt a decision

Etymology

Originally referred to the use of acid to distinguish gold from base metals.
