to take it

1. VERB

/ teɪk ɪt / (AmE), / teɪk ɪt / (BrE)
used to indicate that one is assuming or inferring something from what has been said or seen
assume or infer (something)

Examples

I take it you’ve finished your work already?
So, you take it that they’ve left the building?
I take it you disagree with the decision.

Synset Examples

I take it you’ve heard the news already?
I take it you’re not coming to the party?
So, I take it you enjoyed the concert last night?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assume - to suppose something without proof
  • Synonym: to presume - to suppose something is true based on probability
