to buck up your ideas

1. VERB

/ bʌk ʌp jʊr aɪdiz / (AmE), / bʌk ʌp jə aɪdɪəz / (BrE)
(British) To start making more effort or to improve your behavior, attitude, or performance.
make more effort; improve behavior, attitude, or performance
british

Examples

If you don't buck up your ideas soon, you'll be in trouble with the manager.
You'll have to buck up your ideas if you want to pass the exam this term.
She was told to buck up her ideas or risk being replaced on the team.

Synset Examples

It's time to buck up your ideas for the presentation.
