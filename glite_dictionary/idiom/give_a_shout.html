to give a shout

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ə ʃaʊt / (AmE), / ɡɪv ə ʃaʊt / (BrE)
to contact or call someone, especially informally; to let someone know
contact or notify (someone), informally
informal

Examples

If you have any questions, just give a shout and I'll come help you.
Whenever you're ready to leave, give a shout and I'll meet you at the door.
You can give a shout by phone or email if something urgent comes up.

Synset Examples

I'll give you a shout when I'm free.
Give me a shout if you need anything.
Don’t hesitate to give me a shout anytime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get in touch - to make contact with someone
