on the lam

1. ADJ

/ ɑn ðə læm / (AmE), / ɒn ðə læm / (BrE)
in hiding or on the run, especially from the police or other authorities
fleeing from the law
informal american

Examples

After the robbery, the accomplice was on the lam for several months.
The suspect remained on the lam until his arrest in Chicago.
Rumors spread that she was on the lam following the court verdict.

Synset Examples

They planned to take it on the lam until things cooled down.
The fugitive was finally caught after being on the lam.
He was forced to take it on the lam after the police arrived.
They say he's been on the lam since the trial.
After the heist, he was on the lam for weeks.
After the heist, the gang decided to take it on the lam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on the run - fleeing or hiding to avoid capture or arrest

Etymology

Probably from slang 'lam' meaning 'to run' or 'escape,' origin uncertain, used in US criminal slang since late 19th century.
