to play well with others

1. VERB

/ pleɪ wɛl wɪð ˈʌðɚz / (AmE), / pleɪ wɛl wɪð ˈʌðəz / (BrE)
to cooperate or collaborate effectively and harmoniously with other people, especially in a team or group setting
cooperate or collaborate harmoniously with others

Examples

To succeed in a corporate environment, you need to play well with others.
Her ability to play well with others makes her a valuable member of the team.
If you want to be promoted, you should demonstrate that you play well with others.

Synset Examples

In team projects, it's important to play well with others.
He doesn't always play well with others in group settings.
She was praised for learning to play well with others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cooperate - work together toward the same end
  • Synonym: to collaborate - work jointly with others
  • Antonym: be difficult - not be easy to work with; refuse to cooperate
