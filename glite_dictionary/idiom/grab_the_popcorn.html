to grab the popcorn

1. VERB

/ ɡræb ðə ˈpɑpˌkɔrn / (AmE), / ɡræb ðə ˈpɒpˌkɔːn / (BrE)
To prepare for watching an entertaining or dramatic event, especially as a spectator; to get ready for something likely to be exciting or amusing.
prepare for something entertaining or dramatic
informal

Examples

As the drama unfolded online, everyone told each other to grab the popcorn and watch.
Every awards show brings surprises, so go ahead and grab the popcorn.
Whenever my friends start arguing in the group chat, I just grab the popcorn and enjoy the show.

Synset Examples

Before the big game, we always grab the popcorn.
It's time to grab the popcorn for the season finale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: popcorn moment - a moment of anticipated drama or excitement, as if watching a movie
