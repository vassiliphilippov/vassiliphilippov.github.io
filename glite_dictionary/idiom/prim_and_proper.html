prim and proper

1. ADJ

/ prɪm ənd ˈprɑpər / (AmE), / prɪm ənd ˈprɒpə / (BrE)
very formal and correct in behavior and appearance, often in a way that seems stiff or prudish
very formal and correct in manner

Examples

Her grandmother was always prim and proper, insisting on perfect manners at all times.
The boarding school's rules required students to be prim and proper during every lesson.
He tried to appear prim and proper, but his mischievous smile always gave him away.

Synset Examples

They expect the guests to be prim and proper at the gala.
She always dressed prim and proper for the meetings.
His prim and proper demeanor won him many admirers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prim - very formal, stiffly proper
  • Synonym: starchy - very formal and proper in manner
  • Antonym: improper - not proper or correct in behavior
