to break one's duck

1. VERB

/ breɪk wʌnz dʌk / (AmE), / breɪk wʌnz dʌk / (BrE)
to achieve a first success or score, especially after previous unsuccessful attempts
achieve a first success or score
british

Examples

After several games, she finally broke her duck.
It took a while, but he broke his duck in the tournament.
He finally broke his duck and scored his first goal.

Synset Examples

Etymology

British sports slang; from cricket, where 'duck' refers to a score of zero due to the shape resembling a duck's egg.
