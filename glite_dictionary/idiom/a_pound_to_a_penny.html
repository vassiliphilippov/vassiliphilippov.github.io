a pound to a penny

1. X

/ ə paʊnd tu ə ˈpɛni / (AmE), / ə paʊnd tʊ ə ˈpɛni / (BrE)
used to express that something is almost certain or very likely to happen; a strong belief or certainty.
very likely; almost certain
british

Examples

A pound to a penny he’ll forget her birthday again this year.
It’s a pound to a penny they’ll cancel the match if it rains.
A pound to a penny, she’ll be promoted before the end of the year.

Synset Examples

You’ll find a pound to a penny this deal is a scam.
A pound to a penny he’ll be late again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: odds-on - very likely to happen, almost certain
  • Synonym: a sure thing - something that is certain to happen
  • Antonym: improbable - unlikely to happen

Etymology

From the difference in value between a pound and a penny, emphasizing strong odds or certainty.
