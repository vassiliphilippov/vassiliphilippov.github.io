to raise hell

1. VERB

/ reɪz hɛl / (AmE), / reɪz hɛl / (BrE)
to behave in a rowdy or disruptive way; to cause trouble, commotion, or uproar, often for enjoyment or protest
cause trouble or commotion

Examples

The teenagers were raising hell in the empty park until the police arrived.
Local fans began raising hell outside the stadium after their team lost.
She threatened to raise hell if her complaint wasn’t taken seriously.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make a scene - to behave in a loud or noticeable way in public, especially to attract attention
  • Antonym: to keep the peace - to maintain order and avoid causing trouble
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01225521-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ reɪz hɛl / (AmE), / reɪz hɛl / (BrE)
to behave in a loud, angry, or disruptive way, especially to show protest or discontent
make a loud or angry protest
informal

Examples

The workers threatened to raise hell if their demands were not met.
She promised to raise hell at the next meeting if nothing changed.
Neighbors started to raise hell about the noise from the club.

Synset Examples

She raised hell when she found out that she would not be hired again

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to kick up a fuss - to protest or complain loudly
  • Synonym: to make a scene - to behave in a dramatic and noticeable way, often due to anger
  • Synonym: to cause a stir - to create a commotion or draw a lot of attention
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00912607-v
Synset POS: v
