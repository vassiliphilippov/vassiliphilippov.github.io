topsy-turvy

1. ADV

/ ˌtɑpsi ˈtɜːrvi / (AmE), / ˌtɒpsi ˈtɜːvi / (BrE)
in a state of confusion, disorder, or in a confused or hasty manner
in confusion or disorderly haste

Examples

The children ran topsy-turvy through the playground after the bell rang.
Papers were scattered topsy-turvy all over the desk during the search.
With the sudden news, everyone went topsy-turvy rushing to prepare.

Synset Examples

we ran head over heels toward the shelter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helter-skelter - in confused or disorderly haste

Etymology

Probably of imitative or reduplicative origin, first recorded in the 16th century.
