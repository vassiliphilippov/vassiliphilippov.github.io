to act like a buzzard

1. VERB

/ ækt laɪk ə ˈbʌzɚd / (AmE), / ækt laɪk ə ˈbʌzəd / (BrE)
to wait eagerly for someone else to experience difficulty or misfortune, in order to benefit from it
wait for and exploit others' misfortune

Examples

Some journalists act like a buzzard, waiting for celebrities to have a scandal.
She accused him of acting like a buzzard during the layoffs, eager to get a promotion.
It felt uncomfortable to see my colleagues act like a buzzard after my mistake.

Synset Examples

He was acting like a buzzard, waiting for someone to fail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circle like a vulture - wait for someone to fail or become vulnerable in order to take advantage

Etymology

Metaphor from the scavenging behavior of buzzards, which wait for animals to die before feeding.
