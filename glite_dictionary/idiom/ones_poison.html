one's poison

1. NOUN

/ wʌnz ˈpɔɪzən / (AmE), / wʌnz ˈpɔɪzən / (BrE)
a person's preferred alcoholic drink or, by extension, any chosen indulgence
preferred alcoholic drink or indulgence
informal

Examples

After a long week, she poured herself her poison and settled in for a quiet evening.
When we go to the pub, everyone orders their poison before dinner.
He chose whiskey as his poison while the others stuck with beer.

Synset Examples

She's a wine lover; red is definitely her poison.

Etymology

From the phrase 'pick your poison,' referring to choosing an alcoholic drink, with 'poison' as a slang term for alcohol.
