to do the deed

1. VERB

/ du ðə did / (AmE), / duː ðə diːd / (BrE)
to carry out a significant or decisive action, often used euphemistically for marriage or sexual intercourse
to carry out an important action

Examples

After years of dating, they finally did the deed and got married.
Everyone kept asking when we were going to do the deed and settle down.
They nervously talked about doing the deed before the wedding.

Synset Examples

They finally did the deed and got married last weekend.
