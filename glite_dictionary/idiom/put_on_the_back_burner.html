to put on the back burner

1. VERB

/ pʊt ɑn ðə bæk ˈbɜrnər / (AmE), / pʊt ɒn ðə bæk ˈbɜːnə / (BrE)
to postpone dealing with something so that it is addressed later or considered less urgent
postpone or delay dealing with

Examples

Given our budget, we'll have to put the renovation on the back burner until next year.
She decided to put her hobbies on the back burner while she focused on her studies.
Many companies put hiring on the back burner during periods of economic uncertainty.

Synset Examples

I'll put that project on the back burner for now.
Let's put that idea on the backburner for later.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to postpone - to delay something until a later time
  • Antonym: to prioritize - to deal with something as more important or urgent

Etymology

From the practice of moving less urgent pots to the back burners on a stove, dating from mid-20th century American English.
