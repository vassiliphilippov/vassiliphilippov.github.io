to make haste slowly

1. VERB

/ meɪk heɪst ˈsloʊli / (AmE), / meɪk heɪst ˈsləʊli / (BrE)
to proceed with care and deliberation even when urgency is required
proceed carefully despite urgency

Examples

Sometimes it is better to make haste slowly rather than rush and make mistakes.
The manager told his team to make haste slowly during the product launch.
You should make haste slowly when dealing with complex financial matters.

Synset Examples

It’s wise to make haste slowly when planning.
In this project, we must make haste slowly.
He advised me to make haste slowly with my decisions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to hasten - to do something quickly or speed up
  • Synonym: take your time - to proceed slowly and carefully

Etymology

Translates the Latin proverb 'festina lente,' meaning 'make haste slowly.'
