to blur the line

1. VERB

/ blɝ ðə laɪn / (AmE), / blɜː ðə laɪn / (BrE)
to make the distinction between two things less clear or definite
make the distinction between things less clear

Examples

Advances in technology continue to blur the line between reality and virtual experiences.
Some comedies intentionally blur the line between humor and offense to provoke discussion.
Her creative approach to fashion design blurs the line between art and clothing.

Synset Examples

Her comments blur the line between criticism and support.
The new policy might blur the line between work and personal life.
Social media often blurs the line between public and private.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to distinguish - recognize or treat as different; to see the difference
