to drain the swamp

1. VERB

/ dreɪn ðə swɑmp / (AmE), / dreɪn ðə swɒmp / (BrE)
to eliminate corruption, inefficiency, or unnecessary elements from an organization or system, especially for clarity or reform
eliminate corruption or inefficiency

Examples

The candidate vowed to drain the swamp if elected, promising major governmental reforms.
Corporate leaders must drain the swamp to restore public trust in the company.
After the scandal, the board decided to drain the swamp and introduce new accountability measures.

Synset Examples

They promised to drain the swamp by the end of summer.
We need to drain the swamp for better visibility.
It's time to drain the swamp and clean up the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clean house - to remove unwanted personnel or practices from an organization

Etymology

Originally refers to draining wetlands to remove hazards; used metaphorically since the 20th century, notably in political contexts.
