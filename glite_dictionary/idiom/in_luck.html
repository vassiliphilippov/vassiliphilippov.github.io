in luck

1. ADJ

/ ɪn lʌk / (AmE), / ɪn lʌk / (BrE)
experiencing good fortune or a favorable situation, usually unexpectedly
experiencing good fortune

Examples

If you hurry, you might be in luck and catch the last train.
I was in luck—there was one slice of cake left.
You're in luck; the store is still open.

Synset Examples

If you call now, you’ll be in luck.
If you’re here now, you’re in luck!
You’re in luck; we just got more tickets.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: out of luck - not having good fortune or not being successful
