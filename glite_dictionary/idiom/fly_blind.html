to fly blind

1. VERB

/ flaɪ blaɪnd / (AmE), / flaɪ blaɪnd / (BrE)
to proceed without adequate information, guidance, or preparation
act or proceed without guidance or information

Examples

After the system crashed, the engineers had to fly blind and hope for the best.
If you start a business without research, you're basically flying blind.
She flew blind on her first solo hike and almost got lost.

Synset Examples

He decided to fly blind and trust his instincts.
Without a map, we had to fly blind through the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to improvise - to invent or perform without preparation
  • Synonym: to play it by ear - to proceed without planning, responding as events occur

Etymology

From aviation, where pilots operate without visual reference or instruments, later used figuratively.
