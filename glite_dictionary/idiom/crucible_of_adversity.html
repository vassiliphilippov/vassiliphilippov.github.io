crucible of adversity

1. NOUN

/ ˈkruː.sɪ.bəl əv ədˈvɜr.sə.ti / (AmE), / ˈkruː.sɪ.bəl əv ədˈvɜː.sɪ.ti / (BrE)
a severe test or trial that challenges individuals or relationships, often resulting in growth or change
a severe test or trial

Examples

Their friendship was forged in the crucible of adversity.
Great leaders often emerge from the crucible of adversity.
The team’s unity was strengthened by the crucible of adversity they endured.

Synset Examples

Their friendship was tested in the crucible of adversity.
The crucible of war forged their loyalty.

Etymology

Crucible: from Medieval Latin *crucibulum* (melting pot); adversity: from Latin *adversitas* (opposition, hardship).
