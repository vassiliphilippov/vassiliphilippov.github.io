widow's mite

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪdoʊz ˌmaɪt / (AmE), / ˈwɪdəʊz ˌmaɪt / (BrE)
a small but meaningful contribution made by someone with little to give, especially in the context of charity
small but meaningful contribution by someone with little

Examples

Although he had very little, he offered his widow's mite to support the cause.
The charity valued every widow's mite it received from the community.
Even her widow's mite was appreciated in the fundraising effort.

Synset Examples

He donated his widow's mite to the charity.
The widow's mite symbolizes small but meaningful gifts.
Her contribution was a widow's mite, but it mattered.

Etymology

From the biblical story in the Gospel of Mark, where a poor widow donates two small coins ('mites') to the temple treasury.
