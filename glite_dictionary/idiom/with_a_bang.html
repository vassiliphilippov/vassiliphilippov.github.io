with a bang

1. ADV

/ wɪð ə bæŋ / (AmE), / wɪð ə bæŋ / (BrE)
in a spectacular, exciting, or impressive manner; with great impact or attention
impressively or spectacularly

Examples

The team ended the season with a bang, winning their final game by a landslide.
The new restaurant opened with a bang, attracting hundreds of guests on the first night.
It's always better to start with a bang to grab everyone's attention.

Synset Examples

He finished the project with a bang, impressing everyone.
The fireworks started the celebration with a bang.
She entered the party with a bang, wearing a stunning dress.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: with a whimper - in a quiet, unimpressive, or disappointing way
