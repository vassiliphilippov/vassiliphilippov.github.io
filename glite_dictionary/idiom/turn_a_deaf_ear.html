to turn a deaf ear

1. VERB

/ tɝn ə dɛf ɪɚ / (AmE), / tɜːn ə dɛf ɪə / (BrE)
to deliberately ignore or refuse to listen to someone or something
refuse to listen; ignore

Examples

Despite repeated warnings, the company continued to turn a deaf ear to environmental concerns.
Whenever people tried to give him advice, he would turn a deaf ear.
The government cannot afford to turn a deaf ear to the needs of its citizens.

Synset Examples

She chose to turn a deaf ear to the gossip.
They always turn a deaf ear to the community's concerns.
He tends to turn a deaf ear to criticism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to something or someone
  • Antonym: to pay attention - to listen to or take notice of something
