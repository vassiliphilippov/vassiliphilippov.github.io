to wear one's heart on one's sleeve

1. VERB

/ wɛr wʌnz hɑrt ɑn wʌnz sliːv / (AmE), / wɛə wʌnz hɑːt ɒn wʌnz sliːv / (BrE)
to openly display one's emotions or feelings, rather than hiding them
to show emotions openly

Examples

When it comes to her children, she wears her heart on her sleeve and can't hide her pride.
It's easy to know how he feels because he wears his heart on his sleeve in every situation.
Some people admire those who wear their heart on their sleeve, while others prefer to hide their feelings.

Synset Examples

She always wears her heart on her sleeve at work.
He wears his heart on his sleeve, never hiding his feelings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hide one's feelings - to not show one's emotions openly

Etymology

Possibly from the custom in medieval jousts of knights wearing their lady's colors on their sleeve.
