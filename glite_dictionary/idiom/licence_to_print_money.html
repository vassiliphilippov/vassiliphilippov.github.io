licence to print money

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪsəns tə prɪnt ˈmʌni / (AmE), / ˈlaɪsns tə prɪnt ˈmʌni / (BrE)
an arrangement, activity, or situation that allows someone to make a large amount of money with little effort
situation allowing easy large profits
british

Examples

To many investors, the software subscription model seemed like a licence to print money.
The cable company's monopoly was described as a licence to print money by angry customers.
He believed that owning a popular online platform was essentially a licence to print money.

Synset Examples
