to point fingers

1. VERB

/ pɔɪnt ˈfɪŋɡərz / (AmE), / pɔɪnt ˈfɪŋɡəz / (BrE)
to accuse or blame someone for something, especially in situations where responsibility is uncertain or shared
to accuse or assign blame

Examples

It's not helpful to point fingers when we should be working together to solve the problem.
After the incident, management tried not to point fingers at any particular employee.
Instead of pointing fingers, the team took collective responsibility and moved forward.

Synset Examples

During the car ride, they all pointed fingers, claiming, 'He who smelt it dealt it.'
Let’s not point fingers; we all made mistakes.
Don't point the finger at others without evidence.
It's easy to point the finger at mistakes.
Don't point the finger; take responsibility instead.
It’s easy to point the finger when things go wrong.
She pointed the finger at her coworker during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blame - to assign responsibility for a fault or wrong
  • Synonym: to accuse - to say someone has done something wrong or illegal
