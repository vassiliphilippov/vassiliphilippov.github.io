knight of the spigot

1. NOUN

/ naɪt əv ðə ˈspɪɡət / (AmE), / naɪt əv ðə ˈspɪɡət / (BrE)
a humorous or semi-formal title given to a person skilled in brewing or ale making
ale maker; expert brewer (humorous)
archaic

Examples

The annual fair awarded him the title of knight of the spigot for his superior beer.
Among local brewers, she was affectionately known as the knight of the spigot.
Legend has it, the knight of the spigot kept the city’s finest taverns well supplied.

Synset Examples

The knight of the spigot crafted the best ales in town.
At the festival, he proudly claimed the title knight of the spigot.
He's known as the knight of the spigot for his brewing expertise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brewmaster - a person who makes beer as a profession
  • Degree Lower: bartender - a person who serves drinks, especially alcoholic ones, at a bar

Etymology

From 'knight' (a person honored with a title) and 'spigot' (a tap for drawing liquid), alluding to the craft of beer making.
