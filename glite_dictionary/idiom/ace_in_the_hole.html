ace in the hole

1. NOUN

/ eɪs ɪn ðə hoʊl / (AmE), / eɪs ɪn ðə həʊl / (BrE)
a secret advantage or resource kept in reserve until it is needed
hidden advantage or resource

Examples

Her ability to speak four languages was her ace in the hole when applying for international jobs.
The company's research team proved to be the ace in the hole during fierce competition.
If negotiations fail, we have an ace in the hole that could turn things in our favor.

Synset Examples

They used their connections as an ace in the hole.
His experience was the ace in the hole during negotiations.
She always kept an ace in the hole for tough times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trump card - an advantage or resource kept until needed

Etymology

From the card game stud poker, where an 'ace in the hole' refers to a concealed ace card dealt face down.
