to take the high road

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə haɪ roʊd / (AmE), / teɪk ðə haɪ rəʊd / (BrE)
to act with moral integrity or behave in a principled, ethical way, especially when confronted with dishonesty or provocation
act with moral integrity

Examples

Even though she was falsely accused, she chose to take the high road and did not respond in anger.
It's often difficult to take the high road when others are treating you unfairly.
When faced with criticism, leaders are expected to take the high road rather than stoop to insults.

Synset Examples

He decided to take the high road and not retaliate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to take the low road - to act in a less honorable or unscrupulous way
