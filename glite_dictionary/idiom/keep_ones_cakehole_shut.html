to keep one's cakehole shut

1. VERB

/ kip wʌnz ˈkeɪkˌhoʊl ʃʌt / (AmE), / kiːp wʌnz ˈkeɪkˌhəʊl ʃʌt / (BrE)
(informal) to stop talking; to remain silent or not reveal something
remain silent; not talk
informal slang british

Examples

If you can't keep your cakehole shut, you'll ruin the whole plan.
She had to keep her cakehole shut or risk offending her boss.
They begged him to keep his cakehole shut about the party.

Synset Examples

He couldn’t keep his cakehole shut about the surprise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keep quiet - to refrain from speaking or revealing information
  • Antonym: to spill the beans - to reveal a secret

Etymology

'Cakehole' is slang for 'mouth,' originally British English, combining 'cake' and 'hole.'
