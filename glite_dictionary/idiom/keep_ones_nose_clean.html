to keep one's nose clean

1. VERB

/ kip wʌnz noʊz klin / (AmE), / kiːp wʌnz nəʊz kliːn / (BrE)
to avoid trouble or wrongdoing; to behave in a manner that does not attract attention or cause problems
avoid trouble or wrongdoing

Examples

After his release from prison, he did his best to keep his nose clean and avoid any trouble.
If you want to move up in the company, it’s important to keep your nose clean.
She advised her brother to keep his nose clean during his probation period.

Synset Examples

He always tried to keep his nose clean in the workplace.
If you want to stay out of trouble, keep your nose clean.
To avoid any issues, just keep your nose clean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stay out of trouble - to deliberately avoid situations that could cause problems
