to hide the dirt

1. VERB

/ haɪd ðə dɝt / (AmE), / haɪd ðə dɜːt / (BrE)
to conceal evidence of wrongdoing, faults, or immoral actions from others
conceal wrongdoing or faults

Examples

The company tried to hide the dirt by deleting incriminating emails before the audit.
Politicians often attempt to hide the dirt when scandals threaten their careers.
It's unhealthy for a family to constantly hide the dirt instead of dealing with problems openly.

Synset Examples

He was so upset, he couldn't hide the dirt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sweep under the rug - to hide evidence of something embarrassing or wrong
  • Synonym: to cover up - to hide or disguise the truth about something wrong
