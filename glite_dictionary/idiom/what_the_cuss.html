what the cuss

1. INTJ

/ wʌt ðə kʌs / (AmE), / wɒt ðə kʌs / (BrE)
an exclamation used to express surprise, disbelief, or annoyance, serving as a euphemism for a vulgar phrase
euphemism for surprise or disbelief
informal

Examples

What the cuss is that supposed to mean?
He opened the door and shouted, 'what the cuss is going on here?'
What the cuss! That car nearly hit me.

Synset Examples

I can't believe you did that; what the cuss!
What the cuss was that loud noise?
What the cuss is going on here?

Etymology

Formed as a euphemism, substituting 'cuss' (slang for 'curse') for a stronger expletive; popularized by the film 'Fantastic Mr. Fox'.
