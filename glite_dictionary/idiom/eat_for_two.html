to eat for two

1. VERB

/ it fɔr tu / (AmE), / iːt fə tuː / (BrE)
to eat more than usual because one is pregnant and needs to nourish both themselves and their baby
eat extra when pregnant

Examples

Since becoming pregnant, she often jokes that she has to eat for two.
It's common for mothers-to-be to say they eat for two during pregnancy.
He playfully reminded his partner to eat for two to keep their baby healthy.

Synset Examples

Remember to eat for two during this special time.
During pregnancy, she jokes about eating for two.
Now that she's pregnant, she really eats for two.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: overeating - eating too much food
  • Degree Lower: diet - the kinds of food that a person habitually eats
