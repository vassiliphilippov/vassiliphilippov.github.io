to be an ogre

1. VERB

/ bi æn ˈoʊɡɚ / (AmE), / biː ən ˈəʊɡə / (BrE)
to behave in an unkind or selfish way, especially in a manner that intimidates or frightens others
act unkindly or selfishly

Examples

If you be an ogre to your classmates, they won't want to play with you.
She didn't want to be an ogre, so she listened patiently to everyone's opinion.
Try not to be an ogre about the chores—it's important to be fair.

Synset Examples

Don't be an ogre; share your toys with others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be a monster - to act in a cruel or evil way
  • Antonym: be a sweetheart - to act in a very kind and gentle way

Etymology

From 'ogre', a mythical monster in folklore, used figuratively for a frightening or unpleasant person.
