to sell one's own grandmother

1. VERB

/ sɛl wʌnz oʊn ˈɡrændˌmʌðɚ / (AmE), / sɛl wʌnz əʊn ˈɡrænˌmʌðə / (BrE)
to be willing to betray or exploit anyone, even those close to you, for personal gain
betray anyone for personal gain (idiom)

Examples

He's so unscrupulous he'd sell one's own grandmother to make some extra money.
Many politicians would sell one's own grandmother for a chance at power.
I could never trust someone who would sell one's own grandmother just to get ahead.

Synset Examples

In business, he’d sell his own grandmother if needed.

He'd sell his own grandmother for profit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sell someone down the river - to betray someone for one's own advantage
