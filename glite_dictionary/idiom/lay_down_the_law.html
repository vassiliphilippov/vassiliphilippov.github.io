to lay down the law

1. VERB

/ leɪ daʊn ðə lɔ / (AmE), / leɪ daʊn ðə lɔː / (BrE)
to assert authority or set rules in a firm or forceful manner
to assert authority or set strict rules

Examples

The coach had to lay down the law after the team's repeated lateness to practice.
If these students keep breaking the rules, the principal will have to lay down the law.
When their children got out of hand, the parents didn't hesitate to lay down the law.

Synset Examples

The manager laid down the law about tardiness during the meeting.
It's time to lay down the law and enforce the rules.
He had to lay down the law when the kids misbehaved.
He’s such a hensopper, always laying down the law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enforce - to make sure people obey a law or rule
  • Antonym: be lenient - to be permissive or not strict
