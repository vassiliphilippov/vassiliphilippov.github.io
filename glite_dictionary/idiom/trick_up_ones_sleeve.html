trick up (one's) sleeve

1. NOUN

/ trɪk ʌp wʌnz sliːv / (AmE), / trɪk ʌp wʌnz sliːv / (BrE)
a secret plan, idea, or advantage kept in reserve until needed
hidden plan or advantage

Examples

Even when he looks defeated, he often has a trick up his sleeve to turn things around.
The magician always keeps a trick up his sleeve to surprise the audience.
She wasn't worried because she had a trick up her sleeve for the final round.

Synset Examples

He always has a trick up his sleeve during negotiations.
I have a few tricks up my sleeve for the presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ace up (one's) sleeve - a hidden advantage kept in reserve
  • Synonym: card up (one's) sleeve - a secret resource kept for use when needed

Etymology

From the image of magicians hiding objects in their sleeves as part of a trick.
