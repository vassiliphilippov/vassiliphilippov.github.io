to knock it out of the box office

1. VERB

/ nɑk ɪt aʊt əv ðə bɑks ˈɑfɪs / (AmE), / nɒk ɪt aʊt ɒv ðə bɒks ˈɒfɪs / (BrE)
to perform exceptionally well in movie ticket sales; to achieve great commercial success as a film
perform exceptionally well commercially (movies)
informal

Examples

Everyone thought the new superhero film would knock it out of the box office this summer.
That unexpected indie comedy really knocked it out of the box office last year.
If this sequel delivers, it could knock it out of the box office again.

Synset Examples

This movie is sure to knock it out of the box office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blockbuster - a film that is highly successful commercially
  • Antonym: flop - a film that fails commercially
