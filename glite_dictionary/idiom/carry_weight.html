to carry weight

1. VERB

/ ˈkæri ˈweɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæri ˈweɪt / (BrE)
to have influence or importance in a particular situation or with certain people
have influence; be important

Examples

Her advice always carries weight in the company’s decision-making process.
Such recommendations will only carry weight if they are backed by strong evidence.
In academic circles, his name carries weight far beyond his own university.

Synset Examples

Her opinion carries a lot of weight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: matter - to be important or significant
  • Synonym: count - to have value or importance
