Sir John Lack-Latin

1. NOUN

/ sɚ dʒɑn ˈlæk ˌlætɪn / (AmE), / sə dʒɒn ˈlæk ˌlætɪn / (BrE)
A humorous or mocking term for someone lacking academic knowledge or education, particularly in Latin, formerly considered essential for the educated.
person lacking academic knowledge; unprepared or ignorant
archaic

Examples

He always felt like a Sir John Lack-Latin when surrounded by scholars.
After failing to answer the professor's question, I was called Sir John Lack-Latin by my classmates.
In jest, she claimed her brother deserved the title of Sir John Lack-Latin for his ignorance of classical texts.

Synset Examples

He joked about being a Sir John Lack-Latin in his studies.
Her friend called her Sir John Lack-Latin for missing the class.
He felt like a Sir John Lack-Latin during the foreign film.

Etymology

A playful English formation from "Sir John" (a generic name) plus "Lack-Latin" (lacking Latin knowledge), referencing the historical importance of Latin in education.
