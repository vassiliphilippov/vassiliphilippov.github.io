to climb the social ladder

1. VERB

/ klaɪm ðə ˈsoʊʃəl ˈlædər / (AmE), / klaɪm ðə ˈsəʊʃəl ˈlædə / (BrE)
to improve one's social status, often by acquiring wealth, education, or influential connections
improve one's social status

Examples

She worked hard to climb the social ladder after moving to the city.
Many people dream of ways to climb the social ladder and gain more respect.
By marrying into a wealthy family, he was able to climb the social ladder.

Synset Examples

They discussed strategies to climb the social ladder at the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to move up in the world - to become more successful socially or financially
  • Antonym: sink in society - to lose social status
