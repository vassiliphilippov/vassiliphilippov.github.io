spick-and-span

1. ADJ

/ spɪk ənd spæn / (AmE), / spɪk ən spæn / (BrE)
extremely clean, neat, or fresh in appearance; looking new and well cared for
extremely clean or new-looking

Examples

The kitchen was spick-and-span after she spent the morning cleaning.
He arrived in a spick-and-span suit that looked as if it had just come from the tailor.
They keep their house so spick-and-span that you could eat off the floor.

Synset Examples

shiny brand-new shoes
a spick-and-span novelty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotless - completely clean; without any dirt
  • Antonym: messy - dirty or untidy

Etymology

First recorded in the late 16th century, from 'spick' meaning a spike or nail and 'span' meaning a wooden chip; originally 'spick and span new' referred to something as new as a recently made nail or chip.
