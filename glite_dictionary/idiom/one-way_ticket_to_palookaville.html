one-way ticket to Palookaville

1. NOUN

/ ˌwʌn ˌweɪ ˈtɪkɪt tə pəˈlukəˌvɪl / (AmE), / ˌwʌn ˌweɪ ˈtɪkɪt tə pəˈluːkəˌvɪl / (BrE)
an action or situation that is certain to lead to failure or serious trouble
something certain to cause failure or trouble
informal american

Examples

Quitting school with no backup plan is a one-way ticket to Palookaville.
His refusal to accept change was practically a one-way ticket to Palookaville.
Trusting someone so unreliable is just asking for a one-way ticket to Palookaville.

Synset Examples

His reckless spending was a one-way ticket to Palookaville.
Ignoring her health was a one-way ticket to Palookaville.
After the breakup, it felt like a one-way ticket to Palookaville.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: downfall - a loss of power, prosperity, or status
  • Synonym: ruin - the process of being ruined or destroyed
  • Antonym: success - the accomplishment of an aim or purpose

Etymology

Palookaville is a fictional place, originally from American boxing slang where a 'palooka' means an unsuccessful or mediocre boxer; popularized by the 1954 film 'On the Waterfront'
