to give a sneck posset

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ə snɛk ˈpɑsɪt / (AmE), / ɡɪv ə snɛk ˈpɒsɪt / (BrE)
(informal, archaic) To care at all about something; to be concerned or interested in the slightest.
to care at all (archaic idiom)
informal archaic

Examples

Frank doesn’t give a sneck posset what people think of his clothes.
She claimed not to give a sneck posset about the outcome of the argument.
I wouldn't give a sneck posset for his opinion on the matter.

Synset Examples

She wouldn’t give a sneck posset about the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give a damn - to care at all; be concerned
  • Synonym: care - to feel concern or interest
  • Synonym: not care a whit - to not care in the slightest

Etymology

Possibly from dialect words 'sneck' (latch) and 'posset' (milk curdled with wine), used humorously for something trivial.
