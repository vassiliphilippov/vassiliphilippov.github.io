to keep one's head down

1. VERB

/ kip wʌnz hɛd daʊn / (AmE), / kiːp wʌnz hɛd daʊn / (BrE)
to deliberately avoid drawing attention to oneself, especially to avoid trouble or conflict
avoid attracting attention or trouble

Examples

During the restructuring, he tried to keep his head down and avoid office politics.
It's best to keep your head down until the controversy blows over.
She learned to keep her head down in class to avoid being called on.

Synset Examples

He decided to keep his head down and work hard.
To avoid trouble, just keep your head down.
In tough times, it's best to keep your head down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lie low - to avoid attention or remain unnoticed to escape trouble
  • Antonym: to stand out - to be clearly noticeable or attract attention
