to shift into fourth gear

1. VERB

/ ʃɪft ɪntu fɔrθ ɡɪr / (AmE), / ʃɪft ɪntu fɔːθ ɡɪə / (BrE)
to increase speed or intensity in an activity, often after a period of steady effort; derived from changing to a higher gear in a vehicle to go faster
increase speed or intensity

Examples

As the deadline approached, the team had to shift into fourth gear to finish the project on time.
Once the race started, she quickly shifted into fourth gear and passed several competitors.
After a slow start, the company shifted into fourth gear to meet their ambitious sales targets.

Synset Examples

He shifted into fourth gear to speed up the car.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: step up a gear - to increase effort or speed in an activity

Etymology

From automotive terminology, where changing to fourth gear allows a vehicle to go faster.
