to call it a day

1. VERB

/ kɔl ɪt ə deɪ / (AmE), / kɔːl ɪt ə deɪ / (BrE)
to stop working or doing an activity, especially for the rest of the day
stop working for the day

Examples

After finishing the project, the team decided to call it a day and went home.
It's getting late; maybe we should call it a day and continue tomorrow.
She was exhausted, so she chose to call it a day before sunset.

Synset Examples

At midnight, the student decided to call it quits and closed his books

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to call it quits - to stop doing something and accept that enough has been done

2. VERB

/ kɔl ɪt ə deɪ / (AmE), / kɔːl ɪt ə deɪ / (BrE)
to stop working for the day or to decide that no further work can be done at this time
stop working for the day

Examples

After hours of meetings, we finally decided to call it a day and go home.
Once the storm started, the workers chose to call it a day and continue tomorrow.
It's getting late, so let's call it a day and finish up in the morning.

Synset Examples

It's time to hand in my dinner pail and call it a day.
After eight hours of work, we should call it a day.
He suggested we call it a day and finish tomorrow.
They agreed to call it a day after the meeting.
I'd say it's time to call it a day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pack it in - to stop doing something, especially work
