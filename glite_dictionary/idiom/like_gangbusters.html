like gangbusters

1. ADV

/ laɪk ˈɡæŋˌbʌstərz / (AmE), / laɪk ˈɡæŋˌbʌstəz / (BrE)
with great energy, force, or success; extremely well or vigorously
with great energy or success
informal american

Examples

The new product launch took off like gangbusters and exceeded all our expectations.
Tickets for the concert sold like gangbusters within hours of their release.
Once the campaign went online, it spread like gangbusters on social media.

Synset Examples

The festival was attended like gangbusters this year.

Etymology

Originally from the 1940s US radio show 'Gang Busters', known for its dramatic, energetic opening.
