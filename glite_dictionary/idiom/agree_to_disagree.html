to agree to disagree

1. VERB

/ əˈɡri tə dɪˈsɡri / (AmE), / əˈɡriː tʊ dɪsˈɡriː / (BrE)
to accept that people have different opinions and decide not to argue about them
accept differing opinions without conflict

Examples

After hours of debate, they decided to agree to disagree and move on to another topic.
Sometimes it's healthier for friends to agree to disagree than to continue arguing.
Let's agree to disagree about politics so our dinner can stay peaceful.

Synset Examples

Can we agree, for once, to disagree?
Sometimes it's best to agree to disagree.
They decided to agree to disagree after the argument.
Let's just agree to disagree on this issue.
Let's draw a line and agree to disagree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compromise - to settle a disagreement by mutual concession
