to come out of the broom closet

1. VERB

/ kʌm aʊt əv ðə bruːm ˈklɑːsɪt / (AmE), / kʌm aʊt ɒv ðə bruːm ˈklɒzɪt / (BrE)
to openly declare that one practices or is interested in witchcraft, Paganism, or related spiritual paths, especially after having kept it secret
declare witchcraft or Pagan identity open

Examples

After years of secrecy, he finally decided to come out of the broom closet to his family and friends.
Many people feel anxious before they come out of the broom closet, fearing judgment or misunderstanding.
Writing her blog was a way for her to come out of the broom closet and share her beliefs with others.

Synset Examples

It's time to come out of the broom closet and embrace my craft.
She finally decided to come out of the broom closet.
Coming out of the broom closet felt liberating for her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to come out of the closet - to publicly acknowledge one's homosexuality or hidden identity

Etymology

Formed from the phrase "come out of the closet" (to confess a hidden identity) and "broom" (associated with witches); refers to openness about pagan or witchcraft identity.
