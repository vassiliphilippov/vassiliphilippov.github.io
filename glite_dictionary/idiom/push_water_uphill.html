to push water uphill

1. VERB

/ pʊʃ ˈwɔt̬ɚ ʌpˈhɪl / (AmE), / pʊʃ ˈwɔːtə ʌpˈhɪl / (BrE)
to try to achieve something that is extremely difficult or impossible
try to do something futile or very difficult

Examples

Trying to change the company's policy single-handedly felt like pushing water uphill.
Without enough support, getting the new law passed would be like pushing water uphill.
Convincing her to forgive him was as hard as pushing water uphill.

Synset Examples

It’s exhausting to push water uphill every time we meet.
Trying to convince him to change his mind felt like pushing water uphill.
Getting the team to agree was like pushing water uphill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flog a dead horse - waste effort on something that has no chance of succeeding
  • Synonym: mission impossible - a task that is extremely hard or hopeless
