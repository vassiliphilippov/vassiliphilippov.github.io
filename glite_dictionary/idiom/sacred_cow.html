sacred cow

1. NOUN

/ ˌseɪ.krɪd ˈkaʊ / (AmE), / ˌseɪ.krɪd ˈkaʊ / (BrE)
a person or thing that is considered to be exempt from criticism or questioning, often unreasonably so
person or thing immune to criticism

Examples

The old professor is treated like a sacred cow in the department, and no one dares question his views.
Public broadcasting has long been a sacred cow in this country, protected from budget cuts.
For many years, the policy was a sacred cow that no politician would dare change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: untouchable - a person or thing not subject to criticism or attack

Etymology

From the reverence of cows in Hinduism, where cows are considered sacred and not to be harmed or criticized.

2. NOUN

/ ˈseɪkrəd kaʊ / (AmE), / ˈseɪkrɪd kaʊ / (BrE)
a belief, custom, institution, or practice that is unreasonably immune from criticism or questioning
belief or practice immune from criticism

Examples

In many organizations, the founder's decisions are treated as a sacred cow that no one dares question.
Environmental regulations became a sacred cow in the debate, immune from any criticism.
Challenging the grading system is difficult because it's considered a sacred cow in this school.

Synset Examples

Don't challenge that sacred cow; it's too controversial.
In that company, the old software is a sacred cow.
He treats his old car like a sacred cow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taboo - a social or cultural prohibition or restriction

Etymology

Figurative use from the reverence for cows in Hinduism, regarded as sacred animals.
