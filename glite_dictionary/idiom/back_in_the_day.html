back in the day

1. ADV

/ bæk ɪn ðə deɪ / (AmE), / bak ɪn ðə deɪ / (BrE)
used to refer to a time in the past, often with a sense of nostalgia or reminiscence
in the past, often nostalgically
informal

Examples

Back in the day, children spent hours playing outside without electronic devices.
People say that things were more affordable back in the day.
My grandfather always talks about how different life was back in the day.

Synset Examples

This neighborhood ain't what it used to was back in the day.
I used to play outside all day back in the day.
He was a member of the Taffia back in the day.
Back in the day, music was played on vinyl records.
Back in the day, we rode our bikes everywhere.
