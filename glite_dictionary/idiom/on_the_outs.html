on the outs

1. ADJ

/ ɑn ðə aʊts / (AmE), / ɒn ðə aʊts / (BrE)
In a state of disagreement or estrangement, especially in relation to another person or group.
in disagreement or estrangement
informal

Examples

After the disagreement, she was on the outs with her best friend.
The coach and the player are on the outs following last week's incident.
It's awkward when coworkers are on the outs and don't speak to each other.

Synset Examples

After the argument, they were on the outs for weeks.
They were on the outs, but now they are friends again.
He’s been on the outs with his colleagues lately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in hot water - in trouble or difficulty
  • Antonym: on good terms - in a friendly or harmonious relationship
