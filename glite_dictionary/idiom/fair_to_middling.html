fair to middling

1. ADJ

/ fɛr tə ˈmɪdlɪŋ / (AmE), / feə tə ˈmɪdlɪŋ / (BrE)
of average quality; neither very good nor very bad
of average or moderate quality

Examples

His performance was fair to middling, but nothing extraordinary.
The food at the restaurant was fair to middling—not great, but not terrible either.
She described her job satisfaction as fair to middling over the past year.

Synset Examples

more than adequate as a secretary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mediocre - of only average quality; not very good
  • Antonym: excellent - of outstanding quality or superior merit

Etymology

Originates from the agricultural trade where 'middling' referred to a medium grade, especially of wool or cattle.
