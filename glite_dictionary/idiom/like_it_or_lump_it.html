to like it or lump it

1. VERB

/ ˈlaɪk ɪt ɔr ˈlʌmp ɪt / (AmE), / ˈlaɪk ɪt ɔː ˈlʌmp ɪt / (BrE)
used to say that someone must accept a situation as it is, whether they like it or not, because it cannot be changed
accept or reject without alternatives

Examples

The new policy will be implemented next week, like it or lump it.
You’ll have to share your room with your brother, like it or lump it.
It's the only option available, so like it or lump it, that's what we have to do.

Synset Examples

You have to accept the rules, like it or lump it.
The schedule is final, like it or lump it.
That's my decision, so like it or lump it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take it or leave it - an offer which can be accepted or rejected as is, with no negotiation

Etymology

Originates from British English slang 'lump', meaning 'to accept' or 'tolerate', attested from the 19th century.
