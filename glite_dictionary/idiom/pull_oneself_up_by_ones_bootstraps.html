to pull oneself up by one's bootstraps

1. VERB

/ pʊl wʌnˈsɛlf ʌp baɪ wʌnz ˈbuːtstræps / (AmE), / pʊl wʌnˈsɛlf ʌp baɪ wʌnz ˈbuːtstraps / (BrE)
to improve one's situation or achieve success through one's own effort and hard work, without outside help
improve one's situation by personal effort

Examples

After losing his job, he decided to pull himself up by his bootstraps and start his own business.
Many believe that anyone can pull themselves up by their bootstraps regardless of background.
She pulled herself up by her bootstraps to become a successful lawyer.

Synset Examples

He managed to pull himself up by his bootstraps after losing everything.
It's inspiring to see how she pulled herself up by her bootstraps.
She believes anyone can pull themselves up by their bootstraps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-made - having achieved success by oneself

Etymology

Refers originally to the physical impossibility of lifting oneself by one's own bootstraps; dates from 19th-century American English.
