a thing or two

1. NOUN

/ ə θɪŋ ɔɹ tuː / (AmE), / ə θɪŋ ɔː tuː / (BrE)
some knowledge, skill, or experience about something, often used to imply a capability to teach or demonstrate to others
some knowledge or skill

Examples

I bet she can teach you a thing or two about painting if you ask nicely.
After working here for years, he knows a thing or two about customer service.
The old mechanic showed the young apprentice a thing or two about engine repair.

Synset Examples

She'll show him a thing or two about gardening.
He’s about to show you a thing or two in chess.
I can't wait to show you a thing or two about cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: know-how - practical knowledge or skill
