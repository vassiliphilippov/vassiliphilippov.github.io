new phone, who dis

1. X

/ nuː foʊn huː dɪs / (AmE), / njuː fəʊn huː dɪs / (BrE)
a humorous phrase used to pretend not to recognize someone contacting you, often implying indifference or lack of interest
humorous phrase feigning not recognizing a contact
informal slang

Examples

When my old friend texted out of the blue, I just replied, 'new phone, who dis?'.
I lost all my contacts and kept sending 'new phone, who dis?' to everyone who messaged me.
Whenever she wants to avoid someone, she jokingly says 'new phone, who dis?'.

Synset Examples

When he texted me, I replied, 'New phone, who dis?'
She laughed, saying, 'New phone, who dis?' when I called.
