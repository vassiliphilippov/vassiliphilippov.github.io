on the front foot

1. ADV

/ ɑn ðə frʌnt fʊt / (AmE), / ɒn ðə frʌnt fʊt / (BrE)
In a proactive, assertive, or advantageous position; taking the initiative.
being proactive or in an advantageous position

Examples

The team started the season on the front foot, scoring three goals in the first match.
By asking questions early, she stayed on the front foot throughout the discussion.
Good leaders keep their organizations on the front foot during times of change.

Synset Examples

We're starting the campaign on the front foot this year.
He approached the negotiation on the front foot.
She always tries to stay on the front foot in meetings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: on the back foot - being in a defensive or disadvantageous position

Etymology

Originally from cricket, where batting 'on the front foot' indicates an attacking or positive stance.
