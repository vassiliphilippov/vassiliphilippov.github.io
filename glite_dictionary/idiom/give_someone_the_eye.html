to give (someone) the eye

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ˈsʌmwʌn ði ˈaɪ / (AmE), / ɡɪv ˈsʌmwən ði ˈaɪ / (BrE)
to look at someone in a way that shows you are sexually or romantically interested in them
look at flirtatiously
informal

Examples

Every time I glanced up, I saw her give me the eye from behind her book.
He tried to give me the eye, but I pretended not to notice.
I knew she liked me when she started to give me the eye at the party.

Synset Examples

I noticed her give me the eye from across the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: make eyes at - to look at someone with romantic or flirtatious interest
  • Antonym: to turn a blind eye - to deliberately ignore something (antonym: paying attention through a look)
