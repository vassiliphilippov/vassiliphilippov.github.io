little old me

1. PRON

/ ˈlɪt̬l oʊld mi / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.əl əʊld miː / (BrE)
used humorously to refer to oneself in a modest or self-deprecating way, often expressing surprise or disbelief at receiving attention or credit
self-deprecating reference to oneself
informal

Examples

Who, little old me? I never expected to be the guest of honor.
They gave little old me a standing ovation after the speech.
Could little old me really be the one who saved the day?

Synset Examples

Little old me was surprised to be invited to the gala.
I can’t believe little old me won the contest!
Little old me just got a promotion at work!
