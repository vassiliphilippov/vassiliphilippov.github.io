going to Oz

1. X

/ ˈɡoʊɪŋ tə ɑz / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊɪŋ tə ɒz / (BrE)
Traveling to, or imagining oneself in, a magical, ideal, or fantastical place, often as an escape from reality.
going to a magical or ideal place

Examples

Every time life got tough, she found herself going to Oz in her imagination.
Some people cope with stress by mentally going to Oz for a while.
He admitted that daydreaming about going to Oz helped him get through boring meetings.

Synset Examples

He dreams of going to Oz one day.

Etymology

From L. Frank Baum's 'Oz'—the fictional land in 'The Wonderful Wizard of Oz' (1900); 'Oz' has since become a symbol of fantasy and escapism.
