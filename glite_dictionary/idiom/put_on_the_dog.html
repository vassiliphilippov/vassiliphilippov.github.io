to put on the dog

1. VERB

/ pʊt ɑn ðə dɔɡ / (AmE), / pʊt ɒn ðə dɒɡ / (BrE)
to behave in a showy or ostentatious manner, especially to impress others
behave in a showy manner to impress

Examples

They spared no expense and really put on the dog for the wedding reception.
He tends to put on the dog when he's trying to impress his colleagues.
There's no need to put on the dog; just be yourself at the party.

Synset Examples

They really put on the dog for their anniversary.
Don’t put on the dog; we’re here for a casual dinner.
He likes to put on the dog when meeting clients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to show off - to behave in a way intended to attract attention or admiration
  • Antonym: be modest - to behave in a humble or unpretentious manner

Etymology

Possibly from late 19th-century American slang, suggesting fancy or flashy display, perhaps related to fashionable dog-ownership.
