Faustian bargain

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaʊstiən ˈbɑːrɡən / (AmE), / ˈfaʊstɪən ˈbɑːɡɪn / (BrE)
an agreement in which a person sacrifices their moral principles or values in exchange for power, success, or other benefits
deal sacrificing morals for success

Examples

She made a Faustian bargain when she agreed to cover up the scandal for a promotion.
The athlete's use of performance-enhancing drugs was later described as a Faustian bargain.
Many believe the company's rapid growth was made possible only through a Faustian bargain with questionable investors.

Synset Examples

Many feared the Faustian bargain would haunt them later.
He realized accepting the Faustian bargain was a mistake.
The deal felt like a Faustian bargain for quick success.
She regretted her Faustian deal after losing her integrity.
The Faustian deal left him with empty victories.
They entered a Faustian deal for fame and fortune.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deal with the devil - an agreement that involves sacrificing morals or risking one's soul for personal gain

Etymology

From the legend of Faust, a character who sold his soul to the devil for knowledge and power.
