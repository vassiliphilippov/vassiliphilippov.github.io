to champing at the bit

1. VERB

/ ˈtʃæmpɪŋ ət ðə bɪt / (AmE), / ˈtʃæmpɪŋ ət ðə bɪt / (BrE)
very eager or impatient to begin or do something; showing readiness and an inability to wait patiently
impatiently eager to start

Examples

The new players were champing at the bit to prove themselves on the field.
After months of planning, she was champing at the bit to launch her business.
He’s champing at the bit for his turn to speak in the meeting.

Synset Examples

The kids were champing at the bit to go outside.
He was champing at the bit for the race to start.
They are champing at the bit for the concert tickets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impatient - easily irritated by delay or eager for something to happen
  • Synonym: eager - keen or enthusiastic; impatiently desirous

Etymology

Alludes to horses chewing (champing) on the bit when eager to move; 'bit' refers to the mouthpiece in a horse's bridle.
