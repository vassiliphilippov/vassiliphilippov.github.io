the feathers fly

1. X

/ ðə ˈfɛðɚz flaɪ / (AmE), / ðə ˈfɛðəz flaɪ / (BrE)
Used to describe a situation that has become chaotic or heated, especially one involving intense arguments or conflicts.
situation becomes heated or chaotic

Examples

When management announced the layoffs, the feathers flew during the staff meeting.
Tensions were high, and you could tell the feathers were about to fly.
Every family reunion, someone brings up politics and the feathers fly.

Synset Examples

At the debate, the feathers flew between the candidates.
When they argue, you know the feathers fly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: all hell breaks loose - chaos suddenly erupts
  • Synonym: to come to blows - start fighting physically or violently
