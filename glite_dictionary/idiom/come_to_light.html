to come to light

1. VERB

/ kʌm tə laɪt / (AmE), / kʌm tə laɪt / (BrE)
to be revealed or discovered after being hidden or unknown
become known or revealed

Examples

After years of secrecy, the real reason for his resignation finally came to light.
New evidence came to light during the investigation, changing the course of the trial.
It wasn't until much later that her involvement came to light.

Synset Examples

The truth finally came to light

Related Terms

  • Synonym: become known - to be made known or public
  • Antonym: be concealed - to remain hidden or not revealed

2. VERB

/ kʌm tə laɪt / (AmE), / kʌm tə laɪt / (BrE)
If something comes to light, it becomes known or is revealed, especially something previously hidden or unknown.
become known or revealed

Examples

After years of speculation, the scandal finally came to light in a newspaper article.
Several important facts came to light during the investigation.
The error came to light only after the final results were published.

Synset Examples

Secrets often come to light when least expected.
New evidence has come to light in the investigation.
The truth will eventually come to light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emerge - to become visible or come out into view
  • Synonym: surface - to become visible or known after being hidden
  • Antonym: be concealed - to be kept secret or hidden
