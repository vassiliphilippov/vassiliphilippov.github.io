to read between the lines

1. VERB

/ ɹid bɪˈtwin ðə laɪnz / (AmE), / ɹiːd bɪˈtwiːn ðə laɪnz / (BrE)
to understand or find a meaning in something that is not openly stated but is implied or suggested
look for hidden or implied meanings

Examples

If you read between the lines, you can sense the dissatisfaction in her letter.
He never said he was unhappy, but I could read between the lines.
Sometimes it's important to read between the lines to grasp the true intentions in a report.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to infer - to deduce or conclude from evidence and reasoning
