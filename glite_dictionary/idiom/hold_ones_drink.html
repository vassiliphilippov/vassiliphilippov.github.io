to hold one's drink

1. VERB

/ hoʊld wʌnz drɪŋk / (AmE), / həʊld wʌnz drɪŋk / (BrE)
to be able to drink alcohol without becoming drunk or losing control
tolerate alcohol well

Examples

Unlike his friends, John can really hold his drink and stay sober late into the night.
She prides herself on being able to hold her drink at office parties.
If you can't hold your drink, it's better not to challenge others to drinking games.

Synset Examples

He always knows how to hold his drink at the bar.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: drink someone under the table - to be able to drink more alcohol than someone else without becoming drunk
  • Degree Lower: lightweight - someone who becomes drunk easily or can't tolerate much alcohol
