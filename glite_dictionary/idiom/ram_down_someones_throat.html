to ram down someone's throat

1. VERB

/ ræm daʊn ˈsʌmwʌnz θroʊt / (AmE), / ræm daʊn ˈsʌmwʌnz θrəʊt / (BrE)
to force someone to accept or agree with ideas, beliefs, or opinions, especially in an aggressive or insistent way
force ideas or opinions on someone

Examples

I wish they wouldn't ram down my throat their political views every time we meet.
Every commercial seemed to ram down our throats the idea that happiness comes from buying more.
She hates it when people try to ram their advice down her throat without listening to her first.

Synset Examples

She felt pressured when they tried to ram their beliefs down her throat.
He was frustrated when they rammed their agenda down his throat.
Stop trying to ram your opinions down my throat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to force down someone's throat - to impose something on someone in an aggressive way
