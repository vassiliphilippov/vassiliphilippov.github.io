eradicator

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈrædɪˌkeɪtər / (AmE), / ɪˈrædɪkeɪtə / (BrE)
a person whose occupation is to exterminate or eliminate troublesome pests such as rodents and insects
person who exterminates pests

Examples

The city hired an eradicator to deal with the sudden rat infestation in the subway tunnels.
As a professional eradicator, she was called in to eliminate the termite problem in the old library.
The eradicator used safe and effective chemicals to control the spread of bedbugs in the hotel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exterminator - a person whose job is to kill unwanted creatures such as insects or rodents

Etymology

From Latin 'eradicare' (to root out) plus English agent suffix '-or'.
