sicklepod

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪk.əlˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈsɪk.əlˌpɒd / (BrE)
A tropical herb or subshrub with yellow flowers and slender, curved pods, often considered a weed.
tropical plant with curved pods

Examples

The farmer removed the sicklepod plants from his field to prevent them from spreading.
Sicklepod thrives in tropical climates and is often found growing along roadsides.
The distinctive curved pods make sicklepod easy to identify among other weeds.

Etymology

Named for the sickle-shaped (curved) pods; from 'sickle' and 'pod'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪkəlˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈsɪkəlˌpɒd / (BrE)
A North American rock cress with very long, curved seed pods.
North American plant with long curved pods

Examples

The sicklepod is easily recognized by its distinctive, curved seed pods.
Several botanists studied the habitat preferences of the sicklepod in the wild.
Gardeners were excited to find a cluster of sicklepods growing near the stream.

Etymology

From 'sickle' (a curved cutting tool) + 'pod', describing the shape of the plant's seed pods.
