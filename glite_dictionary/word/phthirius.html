Phthirius

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɪr.i.əs / (AmE), / ˈθɪr.ɪ.əs / (BrE)
A genus of parasitic insects, known as crab lice, that infest humans, especially in the pubic region.
genus of lice (crab lice)
medical

Examples

Phthirius pubis is the species responsible for so-called pubic lice in humans.
Medical professionals often use the term Phthirius when referring to this tiny parasite.
Patients infested with Phthirius may experience intense itching and discomfort.

Etymology

From Greek phthir (louse).
