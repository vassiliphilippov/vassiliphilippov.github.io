to congeal

1. VERB

/ kənˈdʒiəl / (AmE), / kənˈdʒiːl / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to become thick or semi-solid, especially by cooling or setting, often taking on a gelatinous form.
to become thick or gelatinous

Examples

As the soup cooled, it began to congeal and take on a jelly-like texture.
The fat left in the pan will congeal if it is not cleaned promptly.
After several hours in the fridge, the dessert had completely congealed.

Synset Examples

the liquid jelled after we added the enzyme

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to solidify - become solid or more solid
  • Antonym: to liquefy - become liquid or more liquid

Etymology

From Latin 'congelare' (to freeze together), from 'com-' (together) + 'gelare' (to freeze).
