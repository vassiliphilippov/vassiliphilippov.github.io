shaggy

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃæɡi / (AmE), / ˈʃaɡi / (BrE)
covered with or having long, thick, and often untidy hair, fur, or material; having a rough, irregular surface or appearance.
having long, thick, or untidy hair or material

Examples

The old dog had a shaggy coat that needed frequent brushing.
They placed a shaggy rug in front of the fireplace for added comfort.
Her shaggy hair blew wildly in the wind as she ran down the hill.

Synset Examples

junipers with shagged trunks
shaggy rugs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hairy - covered with hair
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface

Etymology

From Middle English 'shagge,' meaning rough hair or wool.

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃæɡi / (AmE), / ˈʃaɡi / (BrE)
(especially of hair or fur) long, thick, and messy, giving an untidy appearance.
long, thick, untidy hair or fur

Examples

The old man's shaggy beard made him look like a hermit.
She wore her shaggy hair loose around her shoulders.
A shaggy dog ran through the muddy yard, its fur sticking out everywhere.

Synset Examples

bushy locks
a shaggy beard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bushy - (of hair or fur) thick and spreading
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even, regular surface; opposite of rough or shaggy (of hair or fur)

Etymology

From Middle English shagge, possibly from Old Norse skegg (beard), related to the appearance of hair.
