barbitone

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːrbɪˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˈbɑːbɪtəʊn / (BrE)
a barbiturate drug used chiefly as a sedative and hypnotic
a sedative and hypnotic barbiturate drug
medical british

Examples

The doctor prescribed barbitone to help the patient sleep during her hospital stay.
Prolonged use of barbitone can lead to dependence and withdrawal symptoms.
Researchers studied the sedative properties of barbitone in clinical trials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phenobarbital - a commonly used barbiturate with longer-acting effects

Etymology

From 'barbituric acid' + '-one', reflecting its chemical structure.
