appraisal

1. NOUN

/ əˈpreɪzəl / (AmE), / əˈpreɪzəl / (BrE)
an expert estimation or assessment of the value, quality, quantity, or condition of someone or something
expert estimation or assessment

Examples

The bank required an independent appraisal of the property's value before approving the loan.
She requested a professional appraisal of her antique jewelry collection.
The annual performance appraisal helps employees understand their strengths and areas for improvement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assessment - the act of evaluating or appraising a person or situation or event

Etymology

From late Middle English, based on 'appraise' (from Old French 'apreisier', from late Latin 'appretiare', 'ad' + 'pretium' meaning 'price').

2. NOUN

/ əˈpreɪzəl / (AmE), / əˈpreɪzəl / (BrE)
A written report estimating the value of something, especially for purposes such as insurance or taxation.
written estimate of value

Examples

The insurance company required an appraisal of the house before issuing the policy.
She handed the bank an appraisal to secure the loan against her jewelry.
A recent appraisal revealed the painting was worth far more than expected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valuation - an estimation of something's worth, often in the form of a document
  • Synonym: assessment - the act of evaluating or estimating the nature, value, or quality of something

Etymology

From appraise + -al, mid 19th century.

3. NOUN

/ əˈpreɪzəl / (AmE), / əˈpreɪzəl / (BrE)
the act of assessing or estimating the value, quality, or significance of someone or something
assessment or evaluation of value

Examples

The company ordered an appraisal of its physical assets before the merger.
She waited anxiously for her annual performance appraisal from her manager.
The painting was sent to an expert for appraisal before the auction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assessment - the evaluation or estimation of the nature, quality, or ability of someone or something
  • Synonym: evaluation - the making of a judgement about the amount, number, or value of something

Etymology

From late Latin appretiare (to value), from Latin pretium (price, value).
