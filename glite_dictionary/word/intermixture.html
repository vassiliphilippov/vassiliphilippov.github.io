intermixture

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈmɪkstʃər / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmɪkstʃə / (BrE)
an additional substance that is mixed into something else; a component added by mixing with the base
added ingredient or component

Examples

The pastry needed a small intermixture of butter to achieve the right texture.
A slight intermixture of herbs made the soup especially flavorful.
The soil contained an intermixture of clay and silt, perfect for pottery.

Synset Examples

the growing medium should be equal parts of sand and loam with an admixture of peat moss and cow manure
a large intermixture of sand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admixture - something added by mixing; a component mixed in

Etymology

From Latin 'intermixtura', from 'inter-' (between) + 'miscere' (to mix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈmɪkstʃər / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmɪkstʃə / (BrE)
a food or substance made by blending different ingredients together
food or substance made by combining ingredients

Examples

The chef presented an unusual intermixture of spices and vegetables in the dish.
She was famous for her signature intermixture of chocolate and chili in her desserts.
His breakfast consisted of an odd intermixture of cereal and fruit juice.

Synset Examples

he volunteered to taste her latest concoction
he drank a mixture of beer and lemonade

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mixture - a combination of different substances or ingredients
  • Synonym: blend - a mixture of different substances or elements

Etymology

From Latin intermixtura, from intermiscere (to mix together).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈmɪkstʃər / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmɪkstʃə / (BrE)
the action or process of mixing different substances or elements together
action or process of mixing things

Examples

The intermixture of oil and vinegar creates a delicious salad dressing.
Ancient cities often show an intermixture of architectural styles.
There was a subtle intermixture of fear and excitement in his expression.

Synset Examples

paste made by a mix of flour and water
the mixing of sound channels in the recording studio

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mixture - a combination of different things that have been mixed together
  • Synonym: blend - the action or result of mixing things together so that they combine well
  • Antonym: separation - the action or state of moving or being moved apart

Etymology

From Latin 'intermixtura', from 'inter' (among) + 'mixtura' (mixture).
