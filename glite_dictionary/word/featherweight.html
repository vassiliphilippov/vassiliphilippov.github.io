featherweight

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛðɚˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɛðəˌweɪt / (BrE)
a boxer or contestant in a sport who weighs between 126 and 139 pounds, especially in boxing or mixed martial arts
boxer in the 126–139 lbs weight class

Examples

The featherweight defeated his opponent in the third round of the championship match.
She is training hard to qualify as a featherweight for the upcoming boxing tournament.
Last year's featherweight champion has moved up to the lightweight division.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: lightweight - a boxer in the next higher weight class, typically above featherweight
  • Degree Lower: bantamweight - a boxer in the next lower weight class, typically below featherweight

Etymology

Formed from 'feather' (referring to lightness) + 'weight'; originally used in boxing in the late 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɛðərˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɛðəweɪt / (BrE)
A weight division in boxing, mixed martial arts, and other combat sports, for competitors heavier than bantamweight but lighter than lightweight.
weight class in combat sports

Examples

He won the featherweight championship after a grueling twelve-round match.
The gym recruited several promising featherweights for their boxing team this year.
At only 125 pounds, she easily qualifies for the featherweight category.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bantamweight - a lighter weight division in combat sports
  • Degree Higher: lightweight - the next heavier weight division in combat sports after featherweight

Etymology

Formed from 'feather' (a very light object) + 'weight', referring to the lightness of the class compared to others.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɛðɚˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɛðəˌweɪt / (BrE)
an amateur boxer whose weight does not exceed 126 pounds (57.15 kilograms), or a professional boxer in a similar weight class
a boxer in the 126-pound weight class

Examples

He won the world featherweight title after a tough 12-round match.
As a featherweight, she must carefully monitor her weight to stay within the class.
The club recently signed a promising young featherweight from Ireland.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: lightweight - a boxer who is heavier than featherweight but lighter than welterweight
  • Degree Lower: bantamweight - a boxer who is lighter than featherweight

Etymology

Named for being lighter than lightweight, combining 'feather' (lightness) and 'weight'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈfɛðərˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɛðəweɪt / (BrE)
a professional boxer or contestant in a weight division between lightweight and bantamweight, typically weighing between 123 and 126 pounds (56–57 kg)
a boxer in the 123–126 lb weight class

Examples

The featherweight defeated his opponent in the final round.
She has been training to compete as a featherweight for the upcoming championships.
Some of the most famous boxers have held the featherweight title during their careers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: lightweight - a boxer in the weight class above featherweight (typically 130–135 lb)
  • Degree Lower: bantamweight - a boxer in the weight class below featherweight (typically 118–122 lb)

Etymology

From 'feather' + 'weight', referring to the lightweight of the competitors.
