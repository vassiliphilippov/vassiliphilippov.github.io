memoriser

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛməˌraɪzər / (AmE), / ˈmɛmərʌɪzə / (BrE)
a person who learns or commits things to memory, especially by rote
person who memorizes things by rote

Examples

The actor was known as a skilled memoriser of long scripts.
As a child, he became a memoriser of poetry due to his mother's encouragement.
Some people regard being a memoriser as less valuable than creative thinking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memorizer - alternative US spelling; one who memorizes

Etymology

From 'memorise' + '-er', based on the verb 'memorise' meaning 'to commit to memory'.
