kimberlite

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪmbɚˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɪmbəˌlaɪt / (BrE)
a rare, volcanic rock (a type of peridotite) that sometimes contains diamonds, found especially in South Africa and Siberia
diamond-bearing igneous rock

Examples

Large quantities of diamonds have been found in kimberlite pipes in South Africa.
Geologists study kimberlite deposits to search for potential diamond sources.
The ancient volcanic eruptions that brought kimberlite to the surface also transported diamonds from deep within the Earth.

Etymology

Named after Kimberley, a city in South Africa where the rock was first discovered.
