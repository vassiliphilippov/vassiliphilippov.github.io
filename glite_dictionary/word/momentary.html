momentary

1. ADJ

/ ˈmoʊ.mənˌteri / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.mən.tər.i / (BrE)
lasting for a very short period of time; brief or fleeting
lasting for a short time

Examples

The lights flickered in a momentary power outage but soon returned to normal.
She felt a momentary pang of regret after making the decision.
There was a momentary pause before the crowd erupted in applause.

Synset Examples

a fleeting glance
fugitive hours
rapid momentaneous association of things that meet and pass
a momentary glimpse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fleeting - lasting for a very short time
  • Synonym: transient - lasting only for a short time; impermanent
  • Antonym: permanent - lasting or meant to last indefinitely

Etymology

From Latin 'momentarius' (lasting for a moment), from 'momentum' (moment).
