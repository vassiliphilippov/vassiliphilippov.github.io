Cordylus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrdɪləs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of spiny lizards native to Africa, noted for their armor-like scales and resemblance to tiny crocodiles.
a genus of spiny African lizards

Examples

The Cordylus lizard defends itself with its tough, spiny armor from predators.
Several species within the genus Cordylus are popular in the pet trade due to their unique appearance.
Scientists observed that Cordylus uses its tail to wedge itself into rocky crevices.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Cordylidae - the family of spiny-tailed lizards to which Cordylus belongs

Etymology

From Greek kordyle (club), referring to the shape or club-like tail of some species.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːrdɪləs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of spiny lizards in the family Cordylidae, known for their resemblance to tiny crocodiles.
a genus of spiny lizards

Examples

The Cordylus genus includes lizards often called girdled lizards for their ringed tails.
Several species in the Cordylus genus have bodies covered in tough, spiky scales.
Researchers found a new species belonging to the Cordylus genus in southern Africa.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Cordylidae - the family that includes Cordylus and related spiny lizards

Etymology

From Greek 'kordyle' meaning 'club', referring to the tail shape of some species.
