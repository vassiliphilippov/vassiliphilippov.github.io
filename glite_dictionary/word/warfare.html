warfare

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrˌfɛr / (AmE), / ˈwɔːˌfeə / (BrE)
the activity of fighting a war, including the methods and tactics used in armed conflict
the activity and practice of waging war

Examples

The country has been involved in brutal warfare for over a decade.
Modern warfare relies heavily on advanced technology and intelligence gathering.
The documentary explores the psychological effects of warfare on soldiers.

Synset Examples

thousands of people were killed in the war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: war - a state of armed conflict between countries or groups

Etymology

From Old French 'warrefare', from 'guerre' (war) + 'faire' (to make or do).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrˌfɛr / (AmE), / ˈwɔːˌfeə / (BrE)
competition or active struggle between opposing forces or entities, not necessarily involving physical conflict.
competition or struggle between opponents

Examples

The companies engaged in intense warfare to dominate the smartphone market.
Political warfare can be just as fierce as any battle on the field.
The rise in warfare between streaming platforms has benefited consumers.

Synset Examples

a price war
a war of wits
diplomatic warfare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: competition - the act of competing or striving for the same objective
  • Antonym: cooperation - the process of working together for a common goal

Etymology

From Old French 'guerre' (war) + 'faire' (to make), meaning the act of making war.

Synset POS: n
