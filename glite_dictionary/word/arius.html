Arius

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛəriəs / (AmE), / ˈɛːrɪəs / (BrE)
A genus of sea catfishes in the family Ariidae, found in coastal and estuarine waters of the tropics.
genus of sea catfishes

Examples

The species Arius seemanni is commonly known as the shark catfish.
Researchers studied several members of the genus Arius in the estuary.
Commercial fisheries sometimes target Arius species for their value as food.

Etymology

From Latin Arius, the scientific name assigned in zoological nomenclature.

2. PROPN

/ ˈɛəriəs / (AmE), / ˈɛːrɪəs / (BrE)
(c. 256–336) A Greek Christian theologian from Alexandria, known for his teachings on the nature of God, which led to the development of Arianism and his later condemnation as a heretic.
Greek theologian and founder of Arianism

Examples

Many early church councils gathered to debate the teachings of Arius.
The controversy surrounding Arius led to the convening of the First Council of Nicaea.
Historians study Arius to understand the origins of early Christian heresies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arianism - the Christian doctrine developed from the teachings of Arius

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛəriəs / (AmE), / ˈɑːriəs / (BrE)
A genus of sea catfishes, which is the type genus of the family Ariidae.
genus of sea catfishes

Examples

The genus Arius includes several species of sea catfishes found in tropical waters.
Biologists recently discovered a new member of the Arius genus along the coastline.
Most Arius species are known for their distinctive barbels and forked tails.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Ariidae - the family of sea catfishes to which Arius belongs

Etymology

From Latin Arius, used in zoological nomenclature.
