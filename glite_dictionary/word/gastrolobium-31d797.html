Gastrolobium

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæstroʊˈloʊbiəm / (AmE), / ˌɡæstrəˈləʊbiəm / (BrE)
A genus of Australian evergreen shrubs that are poisonous to livestock, commonly known as poison bush.
Australian poison bush genus

Examples

Some species of Gastrolobium are highly toxic and have caused livestock deaths in Australia.
Botanists studied the adaptation of Gastrolobium to the harsh Australian climate.
Farmers avoided grazing cattle near areas where Gastrolobium was abundant.

Etymology

From Greek 'gastēr' (stomach) and 'lobos' (pod, capsule), referring to the plant’s podded fruit.
