receipt

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsit / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːt / (BrE)
a written or printed statement that shows that something has been paid for or received
proof of payment or receiving goods

Examples

Please keep your receipt if you need to return the item.
The cashier handed him a receipt after he paid for his groceries.
She asked for a receipt as proof of purchase for the warranty.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: invoice - a list of goods sent or services provided, with a statement of the amount due

Etymology

From Old French *recete*, from Latin *recepta* (something received, a receipt).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06532213-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈsit / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːt / (BrE)
the act of receiving something; the fact of having received something
the act of receiving

Examples

Formal receipt of the documents will be acknowledged within five business days.
We are expecting the receipt of your payment by the end of this week.
Access to the funds is available upon receipt of the signed contract.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to receive - to be given or presented with something

Etymology

From Old French *receite*, from Latin *recepta* (things received), from *recipere* (to receive).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00091073-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ rɪˈsit / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːt / (BrE)
to mark a bill or invoice as paid, typically by stamping or signing it
mark as paid (bill/invoice)

Examples

Please receipt the invoice after you process the payment.
Once the customer has paid, the accountant will receipt the bill to confirm it.
The clerk forgot to receipt the document, so the records were incomplete.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to acknowledge - to accept or admit the existence or truth of something, often in writing

Etymology

From Old French *recete* and Latin *recepta* (something received).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00664403-v
Synset POS: v
