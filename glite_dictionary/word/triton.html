triton

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪtən / (AmE), / ˈtraɪtən / (BrE)
a large tropical marine gastropod mollusk with a beautifully colored spiral shell, found chiefly in warm seas
tropical sea snail with a colorful spiral shell

Examples

The diver found a stunning triton shell while exploring the coral reef.
Many collectors prize the rare and vibrant patterns on triton shells.
A large triton can often be seen hunting starfish on the ocean floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea snail - any marine gastropod mollusk

Etymology

From New Latin *Triton*, named after the Greek sea god Triton.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪtən / (AmE), / ˈtraɪtən / (BrE)
a small, usually brightly colored, semiaquatic salamander found in North America, Europe, and northern Asia; especially referring to species of newts
a small semiaquatic salamander (newt)

Examples

The European triton is often found in garden ponds during spring and summer.
She studied the bright colors of the triton in her biology class.
The triton swims gracefully among aquatic plants in the stream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newt - a small semiaquatic salamander, often used as a synonym for triton

Etymology

From French 'triton', originally from Latin 'Triton', a sea god in Greek mythology, later applied to these amphibians due to their aquatic nature.
