anuria

1. NOUN

/ əˈnʊriə / (AmE), / əˈnjʊəriə / (BrE)
the inability to urinate; a condition in which the kidneys stop producing urine
inability to urinate (medical)
medical

Examples

Severe dehydration can sometimes lead to anuria, making urination impossible.
The patient was admitted with symptoms of anuria and acute kidney failure.
Early detection of anuria is important for preventing further kidney damage.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: oliguria - reduced urine production

Etymology

From Greek 'an-' (without) + 'ouron' (urine).
