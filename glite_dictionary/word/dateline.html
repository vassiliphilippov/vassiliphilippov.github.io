dateline

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪtˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈdeɪt.laɪn / (BrE)
A line at the beginning of a news article stating the date and place from which the report was sent.
news article heading with date and place

Examples

The reporter's story had a dateline reading 'Beijing, March 15'.
Without a dateline, readers might not know where the news originated.
The editor asked her to correct the dateline before sending the article to print.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: byline - line in a newspaper article naming the writer

Etymology

Formed from 'date' and 'line', originally from journalistic use.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06360400-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪtˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈdeɪtˌlaɪn / (BrE)
an imaginary line, roughly along the 180th meridian, on the Earth's surface where each calendar day begins; also known as the International Date Line
imaginary line where each calendar day begins

Examples

When a ship crosses the dateline, it must adjust its calendar by one day.
Flights crossing the dateline can cause travelers to experience a change in date.
The dateline is important for keeping international time and dates consistent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: International Date Line - the line on the Earth's surface where the date changes by one day

Etymology

From 'date' + 'line', referring to the line marking the change of calendar date.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08617348-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈdeɪtˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈdeɪtlaɪn / (BrE)
to mark (a document, article, etc.) with the date and place of writing or publication
mark with a date and place

Examples

The editor asked her to dateline the article before submitting it for publication.
All press releases must be properly datelined to indicate their source.
He always datelines his correspondence when traveling abroad.

Synset Examples

dateline a newspaper article

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timestamp - to record the time something happens

Etymology

Formed from 'date' + 'line', originally relating to the line at the beginning of news reports giving the place and date.
