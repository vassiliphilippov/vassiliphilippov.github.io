swinging

1. ADJ

/ ˈswɪŋɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈswɪŋɪŋ / (BrE)
having a lively and rhythmic movement or beat, as in music or walking.
having a lively rhythm or movement

Examples

The band played a swinging tune that got everyone dancing.
She walked with a swinging stride down the boulevard.
The dancer's swinging movements added energy to the performance.

Synset Examples

an easy lilting stride
the flute broke into a light lilting air
a swinging pace
a graceful swingy walk
a tripping singing measure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lilting - having a cheerful, rhythmic flow
  • Synonym: swingy - moving or sounding with a swinging rhythm

2. NOUN

/ ˈswɪŋɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈswɪŋɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of moving back and forth with a regular rhythm, especially as on a swing or suspended object
act of moving back and forth

Examples

The children spent hours swinging at the playground before lunchtime.
There’s a sense of freedom in the simple joy of swinging back and forth.
She loves the feeling of swinging under the old oak tree in her backyard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oscillation - repeated movement back and forth at regular intervals

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'swing', from Old English 'swingan' (to beat, whip, fling oneself).
