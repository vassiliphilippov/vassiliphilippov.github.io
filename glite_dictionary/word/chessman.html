chessman

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛs.mæn / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛs.mæn / (BrE)
any of the individual pieces used by each player during a game of chess, totaling sixteen for each side (white or black).
a piece used in chess

Examples

He carefully moved his chessman across the board, planning his next attack.
One chessman was missing, so they couldn't play a full game.
The queen is the most powerful chessman in the set.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chess piece - an alternative term for a piece used in chess

Etymology

From 'chess' + 'man', indicating a figure or piece in chess.
