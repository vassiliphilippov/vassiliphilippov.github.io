Echinochloa

1. NOUN

/ ɛˌkaɪnoʊˈkloʊə / (AmE), / ɛˌkɪnəˈkləʊə / (BrE)
a genus of annual or perennial succulent grasses found in warm regions, some species of which are weeds or cultivated for food
genus of succulent grasses

Examples

Echinochloa is commonly found in rice paddies where it may compete with the crop for nutrients.
Some farmers cultivate certain species of Echinochloa as a source of fodder for livestock.
The botanist collected samples of Echinochloa to study its growth patterns.

Etymology

From Greek ekhinos (hedgehog) + chloē (green shoot, grass), referring to the bristly seed heads.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛkɪˈnoʊkloʊə / (AmE), / ˌɛkɪˈnəʊkləʊə / (BrE)
A genus of annual or perennial succulent grasses found in warm regions, commonly known as barnyard grasses.
a genus of grasses found in warm regions

Examples

Many rice farmers struggle to control the spread of Echinochloa species in their fields.
The genus Echinochloa includes several weeds that are resistant to common herbicides.
Researchers are studying the growth habits of Echinochloa to improve crop management.

Etymology

From Greek 'echinos' (hedgehog, sea urchin) + 'chloë' (grass), referring to the bristly flower heads of some species.
