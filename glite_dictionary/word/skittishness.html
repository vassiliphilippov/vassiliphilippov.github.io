skittishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɪtɪʃnɪs / (AmE), / ˈskɪtɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
Nervous or easily frightened behavior; the quality of being easily startled or timid.
nervous or easily frightened behavior

Examples

The horse's skittishness made it difficult for the rider to keep control during the parade.
Her skittishness in unfamiliar situations often led her to avoid large gatherings.
Early signs of skittishness in the puppy indicated it would need extra socialization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nervousness - state of being nervous
  • Antonym: confidence - state of being certain and self-assured

Etymology

Formed from 'skittish' + -ness.
