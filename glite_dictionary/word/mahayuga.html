mahayuga

1. NOUN

/ mɑːˈhɑːˌjuːɡə / (AmE), / mɑːˈhɑːˌjuːɡə / (BrE)
In Hindu cosmology, a great age consisting of a cycle of four yugas, totaling 4.32 million years.
Hindu unit of time (great cycle of four yugas)

Examples

According to Hindu cosmology, a mahayuga is made up of four ages: Satya, Treta, Dvapara, and Kali.
A complete mahayuga spans 4.32 million years, according to ancient Hindu scriptures.
Many texts discuss the significance of each mahayuga in the cycle of creation and destruction.

Synset Examples

One full cycle of four Yugas is one Mahā-Yuga (4.32 million solar years).

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: yuga - an epoch or era within Hindu chronology

Etymology

From Sanskrit mahā (great) + yuga (age, era).
