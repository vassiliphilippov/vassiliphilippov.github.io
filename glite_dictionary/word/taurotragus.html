Taurotragus

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɔrəˈtræɡəs / (AmE), / ˌtɔːrəˈtræɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of large African antelopes comprising the elands.
genus of large African antelopes

Examples

The common eland belongs to the genus Taurotragus.
Several species within Taurotragus are native to eastern and southern Africa.
Researchers studied the social behavior of Taurotragus in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eland - a large African antelope, member of Taurotragus

Etymology

From Greek 'tauros' (bull) and 'tragos' (goat), referring to the animal's appearance.
