Bassariscidae

1. NOUN

/ bæsəˈrɪsɪˌdi / (AmE), / bæsəˈrɪsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small carnivorous mammals, sometimes considered separate from Procyonidae, including ringtails and cacomistles.
family of small carnivorous mammals (ringtails, cacomistles)

Examples

The Bassariscidae family includes species like the ringtail and the cacomistle.
Taxonomists sometimes classify Bassariscidae as distinct from the raccoon family, Procyonidae.
Members of the Bassariscidae are agile climbers native to North and Central America.

Etymology

From New Latin, from genus name Bassariscus + -idae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ bæsəˈrɪsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / basəˈrɪsɪdiː / (BrE)
a family or subfamily of mammals in the order Carnivora, including species such as the ringtail and cacomistle, sometimes considered a separate family from Procyonidae.
a family of mammals including the ringtail and cacomistle

Examples

The Bassariscidae family includes the well-known ringtail, native to North America.
Some taxonomists argue that Bassariscidae should be distinguished from raccoons and classified separately.
The nocturnal habits of the Bassariscidae make them difficult to observe in the wild.

Etymology

From modern Latin Bassariscidae, based on the genus Bassariscus.
