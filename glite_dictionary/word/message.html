message

1. VERB

/ ˈmɛsɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɛsɪdʒ / (BrE)
to send a message to someone, especially by electronic means such as email, text, or instant messaging
send a message (especially electronically)

Examples

Please message me if you have any questions about the assignment.
She messaged the doctor to confirm her appointment for tomorrow.
We have been messaging each other all evening about the project.

Synset Examples

She messaged the committee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: text - to send a written message via mobile phone
  • Synonym: email - to send a message electronically via email
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01073654-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˈmɛsɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɛsɪdʒ / (BrE)
the main idea or information that someone is trying to communicate in speech, writing, art, or actions
main idea or information conveyed

Examples

The film's message is that kindness can change the world.
I think the author is trying to send a powerful message about equality.
Sometimes the message of an artwork isn't immediately obvious.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meaning - the thing one intends to convey, especially by language
  • Synonym: theme - the central topic or idea in a work or communication
  • Synonym: subtext - an underlying and often distinct theme in a piece of writing or conversation

Etymology

From Old French 'message', from Latin 'missus' (a sending), from 'mittere' (to send).

Source: WordNet

5. NOUN

/ ˈmɛsɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmesɪdʒ / (BrE)
a communication, usually short, that is written, spoken, or signaled to convey information to someone
brief written, spoken, or signaled communication

Examples

I received a short message from my friend this morning.
Please leave a message after the tone and I'll get back to you.
She wrote a quick message on a sticky note and stuck it to my desk.

Synset Examples

he sent a three-word message

Related Terms

  • Synonym: note - a brief written message
  • Degree Higher: announcement - a formal public statement or message
  • Degree Higher: letter - a written, longer form of communication sent to someone

Etymology

From Old French 'message', from Latin 'missaticum', from 'missus', past participle of 'mittere' (to send).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06263820-n
Synset POS: n
