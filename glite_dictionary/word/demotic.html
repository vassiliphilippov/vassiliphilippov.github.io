Demotic

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈmɑːtɪk / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒtɪk / (BrE)
a simplified cursive script used in ancient Egypt, derived from hieratic and later replaced by Greek; mainly for daily and administrative writing
ancient Egyptian cursive script

Examples

Scholars deciphered the Demotic inscriptions alongside Greek and hieroglyphic texts on the Rosetta Stone.
The Demotic script became widely used for administrative documents in the later periods of ancient Egypt.
Few modern people can read Demotic without specialized training in ancient Egyptian scripts.

Synset Examples

Demotic script was eventually replaced by Greek

Etymology

From Greek *dēmotikos* (popular, of the people), from *dēmos* (people).

2. NOUN

/ dəˈmɑːtɪk / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒtɪk / (BrE)
the modern vernacular form of the Greek language, as distinguished from Classical Greek and Katharevousa.
the modern vernacular Greek language

Examples

Linguists study Demotic to understand how modern Greek evolved from ancient times.
He is fluent in both Demotic and Katharevousa.
Demotic is widely spoken in everyday life across Greece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Greek - the language of the Greek people

Etymology

From Greek demōtikós (popular, of the people).
