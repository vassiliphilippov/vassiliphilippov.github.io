neutral

1. ADJ

/ ˈnutʃrəl / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtrəl / (BrE)
(of a color) having no strong hue; not belonging to any particular color family, such as black, white, or gray.
having no strong color or hue

Examples

She prefers neutral tones like gray and beige when decorating her home.
The walls were painted in a neutral color to match any furniture.
Wearing neutral shades can create a classic and timeless look.

Synset Examples

neutral colors like black or white

Related Terms

  • Synonym: achromatic - without color; not chromatic
  • Antonym: colorful - having much or varied color; bright or distinctive in color

Etymology

From Latin 'neutralis', from 'neuter' (neither).

5. ADJ

/ ˈnuːtrəl / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtrəl / (BrE)

Examples

In a salt solution, the sodium ion is positive and the chloride ion is negative, but water molecules are generally neutral.
A neutral atom has an equal number of protons and electrons, resulting in no overall charge.
Neutrons are neutral particles found in the nucleus of an atom.
