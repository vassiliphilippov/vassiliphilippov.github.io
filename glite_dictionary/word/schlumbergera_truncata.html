Schlumbergera truncata

1. NOUN

/ ʃlʌmˈbɜːrɡərə truŋˈkeɪtə / (AmE), / ʃlʌmˈbɜːɡərə trʌŋˈkɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of South American cactus with segmented stems and usually red or pink flowers, often grown as a houseplant and commonly known as the Thanksgiving cactus.
a flowering cactus houseplant; Thanksgiving cactus

Examples

My Schlumbergera truncata blooms with bright pink flowers every November.
Many gardeners choose Schlumbergera truncata as a holiday houseplant for its striking appearance.
The Schlumbergera truncata thrives in indirect sunlight and well-draining soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Christmas cactus - a closely related plant, often confused with Schlumbergera truncata, with similar appearance

Etymology

Named for French botanist Frédéric Schlumberger; Latin truncatus 'cut off, shortened'.
