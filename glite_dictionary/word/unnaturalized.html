unnaturalized

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈnætʃrəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ʌnˈnatʃrəlʌɪzd / (BrE)
(of a person) not having acquired the citizenship of a country; not naturalized.
not having acquired citizenship

Examples

Many unnaturalized immigrants face restrictions on voting rights and access to government benefits.
As an unnaturalized resident, he needed a special permit to work in the country.
The census included data on both naturalized and unnaturalized foreign-born individuals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: naturalized - having acquired citizenship

Etymology

From un- (not) + naturalized, from Latin naturalis (by birth, native), used in the sense of 'to grant citizenship'.
