kinetics

1. NOUN

/ kɪˈnɛtɪks / (AmE), / kɪˈnɛtɪks / (BrE)
the branch of mechanics that deals with the forces producing or changing the motion of bodies
branch of mechanics dealing with motion-causing forces

Examples

In physics class, we studied kinetics to understand how different forces cause objects to move.
The kinetics of the falling ball were explained using Newton's second law.
Understanding kinetics is essential for engineers designing safe vehicles and machinery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dynamics - the branch of mechanics concerned with the motion of bodies under the action of forces

Etymology

From Greek 'kinētikos' (putting in motion), from 'kinein' (to move).
