scythe

1. VERB

/ saɪð / (AmE), / saɪð / (BrE)
to cut (grass, grain, etc.) with a scythe
cut with a scythe

Examples

Every morning, the farmer would scythe the tall grass in the meadow.
She scythed effortlessly through the fields of wheat, clearing a wide path.
Teams of workers scythe the overgrown roadside verges each summer.

Synset Examples

scythe grass or grain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mow - to cut down (grass or grain) with a machine or tool

Etymology

From Old English *sigði* (sickle), ultimately from Proto-Germanic.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01322545-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ saɪð / (AmE), / saɪð / (BrE)
A tool with a long handle and a curved blade used for cutting grass or crops by hand.
agricultural cutting tool with curved blade

Examples

He swung the scythe through the tall grass, clearing a wide path.
The old farmer sharpened his scythe before beginning the harvest.
Using a scythe requires skill and strength to cut evenly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sickle - a short-handled farming tool with a curved blade for cutting grain or grass

Etymology

From Old English *siðe*, related to Old High German *segansa* (to cut).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04165311-n
Synset POS: n
