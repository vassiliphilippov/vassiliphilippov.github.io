deceptive

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɛptɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛptɪv / (BrE)
Giving an appearance or impression different from the true one; likely to mislead or trick.
likely to mislead or trick

Examples

There was a deceptive calm before the storm hit the coast.
The brochure gave a deceptive impression of the hotel's size and facilities.
Her deceptive smile made it hard to know what she was really thinking.

Synset Examples

deceptive calm
a delusory pleasure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misleading - giving the wrong idea or impression
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere

Etymology

From Latin 'deceptivus', from 'decipere' meaning 'to deceive'.

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɛptɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛptɪv / (BrE)
intended or having the effect of making someone believe something that is not true; misleading.
intended to mislead or give a false impression

Examples

The label gave a deceptive impression that the juice contained no sugar.
Her deceptive smile made it hard to know what she was really thinking.
Appearances can be deceptive, so don't judge by looks alone.

Synset Examples

the deceptive calm in the eye of the storm
deliberately deceptive packaging
a misleading similarity
statistics can be presented in ways that are misleading

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misleading - giving the wrong idea or impression
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere

Etymology

From Latin decipere (to deceive) via Medieval Latin deceptivus.
