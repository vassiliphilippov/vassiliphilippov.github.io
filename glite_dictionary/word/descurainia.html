Descurainia

1. NOUN

/ dɛskjʊˈreɪniə / (AmE), / dɛskjʊˈreɪnɪə / (BrE)
A genus of annual or biennial herbs native to America and Europe, closely related to genera like Sisymbrium or Hugueninia.
plant genus of annual or biennial herbs

Examples

Several species of Descurainia are commonly known as tansymustard.
Botanists sometimes include Descurainia within the genus Sisymbrium.
You can find Descurainia plants in disturbed soils across western North America.

Etymology

Named after French botanist François Descurain (1658–1740).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛskjʊˈreɪniə / (AmE), / ˌdɛskjʊˈreɪnɪə / (BrE)
a genus of annual or biennial herbs native to America and Europe, closely related to Sisymbrium and sometimes included in that genus; part of the mustard family
plant genus of herbs (mustard family)

Examples

Several species within the Descurainia genus are known as tansy mustard.
The presence of Descurainia in the region indicates disturbed soil conditions.
Botanists sometimes reclassify Descurainia under different genera, depending on the system used.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sisymbrium - a related genus of plants in the mustard family

Etymology

Named after French botanist François Descurain (1658–1740).
