Volvariella

1. NOUN

/ vɑlˌvɛriˈɛlə / (AmE), / vɒlˌvɛəriˈɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of edible mushrooms, some species of which are cultivated and consumed, particularly in Asian cuisine.
genus of edible mushrooms

Examples

The straw mushroom, a species of Volvariella, is commonly used in Asian stir-fries.
Researchers collected several samples of Volvariella from the rice fields.
Cultivation of Volvariella requires a moist, warm environment to thrive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mushroom - a fungus with a domed cap, many edible or cultivated

Etymology

From modern Latin, diminutive of Latin 'volva' (sheath), referring to the mushroom's sheath-like structure.

2. NOUN

/ voʊlˌvɛriˈɛlə / (AmE), / vɒlˌvɛəriˈɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of mushrooms, including several edible species, found chiefly in Asia.
a genus of edible mushrooms

Examples

The straw mushroom, a species of Volvariella, is widely used in Asian cuisine.
Scientists recently discovered a new Volvariella species growing in tropical forests.
Members of the genus Volvariella are recognized by their distinctive cup-like volva.

Etymology

From modern Latin, diminutive of Latin 'volva' (sheath, wrapper), referring to the volva structure at the base of the mushroom.
