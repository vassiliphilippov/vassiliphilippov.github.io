drowsy

1. ADJ

/ ˈdraʊzi / (AmE), / ˈdraʊzi / (BrE)
feeling sleepy and only half awake; likely to fall asleep soon.
feeling sleepy or half awake

Examples

The warm afternoon sun made me feel drowsy during the meeting.
He grew drowsy while reading and nearly fell asleep with the book in his hands.
Driving late at night can make even the most alert driver dangerously drowsy.

Synset Examples

made drowsy by the long ride
it seemed a pity to disturb the drowsing (or dozing) professor
a tired dozy child
the nodding (or napping) grandmother in her rocking chair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleepy - ready or needing to sleep; feeling inclined to sleep
  • Antonym: alert - fully awake and attentive

Etymology

From Old English drūsian (to be sluggish or languid).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdraʊzi / (AmE), / ˈdraʊzi / (BrE)
feeling or appearing to be sleepy, inattentive, or bored, especially as a result of tiredness or boredom
sleepy or inattentive

Examples

The room felt warm and everyone looked drowsy during the lecture.
She became drowsy after listening to the professor's monotone voice.
After lunch, I often feel drowsy and find it hard to focus on work.

Synset Examples

the yawning congregation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleepy - feeling ready for or needing sleep
  • Antonym: alert - fully awake and attentive

Etymology

From Old English drūsian (to become sluggish or fall asleep).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00167028-s
Synset POS: s
