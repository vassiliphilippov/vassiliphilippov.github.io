run-through

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌnˌθru / (AmE), / ˈrʌnˌθruː / (BrE)
an uninterrupted rehearsal of a performance or event, typically to practice all parts in sequence without stopping
a continuous rehearsal of a performance

Examples

The cast gave a flawless run-through of the play before opening night.
We'll do a final run-through of the presentation tomorrow morning.
The director asked for a complete run-through without any interruptions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rehearsal - a practice or trial performance
