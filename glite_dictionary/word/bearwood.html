bearwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈber.wʊd / (AmE), / ˈbeə.wʊd / (BrE)
A shrubby tree native to the Pacific coast of the United States, known for yielding cascara sagrada.
Pacific U.S. tree; yields cascara sagrada

Examples

The medicinal bark of the bearwood is harvested in early summer.
Bearwood is often found along riverbanks and in forest clearings in Oregon and Washington.
The bearwoods growing along the coast provide important habitat for local wildlife.
