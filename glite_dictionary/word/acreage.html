acreage

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪkɚɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈeɪkərɪdʒ / (BrE)
an area of land, typically measured in acres, especially when used for a particular purpose such as farming or building
area of land, measured in acres

Examples

The ranch includes over 500 acres of acreage for raising cattle and growing crops.
They purchased additional acreage last year to expand their vineyard.
Much of the acreage near the city has been rezoned for commercial development.

Synset Examples

he wanted some acreage to build on

Related Terms

  • Synonym: land - the solid part of the surface of the earth; ground

Etymology

Formed from 'acre' (unit of land area) + '-age' (denoting collection or measure).
