Micrococcaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkroʊˌkɑːkˈkeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊkɒkˈkeɪsɪiː / (BrE)
A family of spherical or elliptical, usually aerobic bacteria that often produce yellow, orange, or red pigments; includes both toxin-producing and harmless species.
family of pigmented, spherical bacteria
medical

Examples

The genus Micrococcus, belonging to the Micrococcaceae family, is commonly found on human skin.
Some members of the Micrococcaceae are used in microbiology as indicators of air quality.
Researchers identified a novel strain within the Micrococcaceae that produces an unusual red pigment.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'micrococcus' (a bacterial genus) and the suffix '-aceae' indicating families in taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkroʊˌkɑːkəˈsiːiː / (AmE), / ˌmʌɪkrəʊˌkɒkəˈsiːiː / (BrE)
A family of spherical or elliptical, usually aerobic eubacteria that often produce yellow, orange, or red pigments; includes some species that produce toxins as well as harmless commensal and saprophytic forms.
family of pigmented spherical bacteria
medical

Examples

Several species within the Micrococcaceae family produce brightly colored pigments.
Bacteria from the Micrococcaceae group are found on human skin as harmless commensals.
Scientists studied the Micrococcaceae to understand their role in soil ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Staphylococcus - genus of bacteria within the Micrococcaceae family
  • Synonym: Micrococcus - another genus of bacteria within the Micrococcaceae family

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'mikros' (small) + 'kokkos' (berry/grain), referencing shape.
