cellulosic

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛljəˈloʊsɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɛljʊˈləʊsɪk / (BrE)
a plastic material made from cellulose or its derivatives, often used in film, sheets, or coatings
plastic made from cellulose

Examples

The packaging was made of cellulosic instead of conventional plastic.
Researchers developed a biodegradable cellulosic as an alternative to petroleum-based films.
He specializes in designing cellulosics for use in photographic film.

Etymology

From cellulose + -ic, denoting relation to cellulose.

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɛl.jəˈloʊ.sɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɛl.jʊˈləʊ.sɪk / (BrE)
relating to, containing, or made from cellulose, especially in reference to fibers, materials, or products derived from cellulose.
made from or containing cellulose

Examples

Many fabrics are made from cellulosic fibers such as rayon and acetate.
The company developed a new cellulosic biofuel using agricultural waste products.
Scientists are researching cellulosic materials as alternatives to plastics.

Synset Examples

cellulosic fibers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cellulose - a complex carbohydrate forming the main constituent of plant cell walls

Etymology

From cellulose + -ic, indicating 'relating to cellulose'.
