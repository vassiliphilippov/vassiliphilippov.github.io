Pholis gunnellus

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊlɪs ɡʌˈnɛləs / (AmE), / ˈfəʊlɪs ɡʌˈnɛləs / (BrE)
A slippery, scaleless food fish of the northern Atlantic coastal waters, also known as the butterfish or rock gunnel.
species of slippery Atlantic fish

Examples

The Pholis gunnellus is often found hiding among rocks on the seashore.
Fishermen sometimes refer to the Pholis gunnellus as the butterfish because of its slippery body.
The biology class studied the unique characteristics of Pholis gunnellus during their field trip to the coast.

Etymology

Latin scientific name: Pholis (genus) and gunnellus (species).
