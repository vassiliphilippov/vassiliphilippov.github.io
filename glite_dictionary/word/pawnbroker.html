pawnbroker

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔnˌbroʊkər / (AmE), / ˈpɔːnˌbrəʊkə / (BrE)
a person whose business is lending money at interest in exchange for valuable items left as security, which may be sold if the loan is not repaid
a person who lends money against pledged valuables

Examples

The pawnbroker examined the gold ring carefully before offering a loan.
Many people visited the pawnbroker during hard times to pawn their valuables.
The old watch had been sitting in the pawnbroker's window for weeks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pawnshop - a shop where people can pawn items for cash

Etymology

From 'pawn' (a pledge, from Middle French *pan*) + 'broker' (one who arranges transactions).
