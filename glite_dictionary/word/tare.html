tare

1. NOUN

/ ter / (AmE), / teə / (BrE)
any of several weedy vetch plants, especially Vicia species, sometimes cultivated as forage but often regarded as weeds
a weedy vetch plant, sometimes grown for forage

Examples

Farmers sometimes plant tare as a cover crop to improve soil fertility.
The field was overrun with wild tare, making it difficult to grow wheat.
Some varieties of tare can be used for livestock forage in spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vetch - a climbing plant of the pea family, often grown as fodder

Etymology

From Old English *tare*, of uncertain origin, used for vetch plants.

2. NOUN

/ tɛr / (AmE), / tɛə / (BrE)
the weight of the packaging or container that is deducted from the gross weight to obtain the net weight of goods
weight of packaging subtracted from total

Examples

The tare must be subtracted from the gross weight to find the net weight of the product.
Before weighing the fruit, the clerk entered the tare for the empty basket.
Digital scales often have a tare function to automatically account for containers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: net weight - actual weight of the goods excluding packaging
  • Antonym: gross weight - total weight including goods and packaging

Etymology

From French *tare* (from Italian *tara*), likely from Arabic *ṭarḥa* (something deducted).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ tɛr / (AmE), / teə / (BrE)
the weight of a vehicle, container, or aircraft when empty, that is, without its load, fuel, or cargo
weight of an empty vehicle or container

Examples

The driver checked the tare of the truck before loading the cargo.
To calculate the net weight, subtract the tare from the gross weight.
The railway company's records list both the tare and loaded weight of each wagon.

Etymology

From French *tare* (deficiency), from Italian *tara*, from Arabic *ṭarḥa* (something deducted).

4. NOUN

/ tɛr / (AmE), / tɛː / (BrE)
a weedy annual grass, especially Vicia sativa, that often grows in grainfields and other cultivated land, whose seeds are sometimes considered poisonous
a weedy annual grass often found in grainfields

Examples

The farmer was concerned about tare growing among his wheat crop.
Traditionally, tare seeds have been considered mildly poisonous to livestock.
She pulled up handfuls of tare from the field before planting new seeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vetch - a plant of the genus Vicia, related to tares

Etymology

From Old English 'taru', meaning a weed or darnel, especially Vicia species.

5. NOUN

/ tɛr / (AmE), / tɛə / (BrE)
In chemical analysis, an empty container or counterweight used to balance the container holding chemicals when measuring weight.
empty counterweight used in chemical analysis

Examples

Before adding the reagent, place a tare on the opposite pan to balance the scale.
The laboratory technician used a tare to ensure the measurement was accurate.
A tare is essential when weighing volatile chemicals to exclude the container's weight.

Etymology

From Latin *tara* (deduction, allowance), via Italian *tara* and Arabic *ṭarḥ* (what is removed).
