gracilariid

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈsɪləriɪd / (AmE), / ɡrəˈsɪləriɪd / (BrE)
A small, dull or metallic-colored moth of the family Gracilariidae, whose larvae feed by mining in plant leaves.
a type of leaf-mining moth

Examples

The larvae of the gracilariid create distinctive tunnels within the leaves of many plants.
Entomologists discovered a new species of gracilariid in the rainforest ecosystem.
Some gardeners are concerned about the damage gracilariids can cause to their ornamental shrubs.

Etymology

From the family name Gracilariidae, based on type genus Gracilaria.
