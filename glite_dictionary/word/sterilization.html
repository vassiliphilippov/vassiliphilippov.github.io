sterilization

1. NOUN

/ ˌstɛrələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstɛrɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making an object completely free from live bacteria or other microorganisms, usually by heat or chemicals
removal of microorganisms from an object
medical

Examples

All surgical instruments undergo sterilization before use to prevent infections.
The laboratory invested in new equipment to improve the sterilization process.
Proper sterilization of medical devices is crucial in any hospital setting.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: disinfection - the process of cleaning something to destroy bacteria

Etymology

From Latin 'sterilis' (barren) + English suffix '-ization'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌstɛrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstɛrɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making an organism unable to reproduce, often by medical or surgical means
process of making infertile
medical

Examples

The clinic offers sterilization services to help control the pet population.
After the sterilization procedure, the animal was no longer capable of reproducing.
Some countries have laws regulating the sterilization of domestic animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: castration - removal of the testes or ovaries to make infertile
  • Antonym: fertilization - the process of making fertile; the opposite of sterilization

Etymology

From Latin 'sterilis' (barren) + English suffix '-ization'.
