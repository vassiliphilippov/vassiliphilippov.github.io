Presbytes entellus

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɛz.baɪ.tiːz ɛnˈtɛl.əs / (AmE), / ˌprɛz.baɪ.tiːz ɛnˈtɛl.əs / (BrE)
A species of langur native to southern Asia, revered as sacred in India, commonly known as the Hanuman langur.
a sacred langur monkey of southern Asia

Examples

The Presbytes entellus is often seen in temples, where it is considered sacred by locals.
Researchers observed a group of Presbytes entellus foraging in the forest canopy.
In parts of India, harming a Presbytes entellus is believed to bring bad luck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: langur - a long-tailed arboreal Asian monkey

Etymology

From New Latin: Presbytes (genus name from Greek 'presbys', old man) + entellus (species name).
