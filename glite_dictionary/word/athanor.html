athanor

1. NOUN

/ ˈæθəˌnɔr / (AmE), / ˈæθənɔː / (BrE)
a self-feeding furnace designed to maintain a steady temperature, especially one used by alchemists
self-feeding alchemical furnace

Examples

The ancient alchemist carefully monitored the athanor to ensure the temperature remained constant.
Scholars learned about the operation of an athanor from medieval alchemical treatises.
In the corner of the laboratory stood an old, brick athanor used for long experiments.

Etymology

From Arabic al-tannūr (the oven).
