hectolitre

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛktəˌliːtər / (AmE), / ˈhɛktəliːtə / (BrE)
a metric unit of volume equal to 100 liters, used especially for measuring liquids
metric unit of volume (100 liters)

Examples

A hectolitre of water weighs about 100 kilograms.
The brewery produced over 500 hectolitres of beer last month.
Wine is often sold in bulk by the hectolitre in European countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liter - basic metric unit of volume
  • Degree Higher: kiloliter - a metric unit of volume equal to 1,000 liters

Etymology

From French 'hectolitre', from Greek 'hekaton' (hundred) and French 'litre'.
