spy

1. NOUN

/ spaɪ / (AmE), / spaɪ / (BrE)
a person who secretly observes others, especially to obtain information without their knowledge, often for a government or organization
person who secretly watches others

Examples

The government arrested a spy who had been leaking classified information to another country.
He felt as if a spy was following him through the crowded streets.
Corporate spies sometimes try to steal new product designs from rivals.

Synset Examples

my spies tell me that you had a good time last night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: informant - person who provides secret information, usually to authorities
  • Synonym: agent - a person who works on behalf of an organization, often secretly
  • Synonym: detective - person who investigates and gathers information, typically for the police
  • Antonym: counterspy - a spy working against another espionage organization

Etymology

From Old French 'espier' (to watch stealthily), from Germanic origin.

3. VERB

/ spaɪ / (AmE), / spaɪ / (BrE)
to watch, observe, or inquire about someone or something secretly, often to gather information
watch or observe secretly

Examples

  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to; disregard

4. NOUN

/ spaɪ / (AmE), / spaɪ / (BrE)
a person who secretly collects information about enemies or competitors for a government or organization
person who secretly gathers information

Examples

The government sent a spy to infiltrate the enemy camp.
She felt as if a spy was watching her every move at work.
The notorious spy was finally captured after years of evading authorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agent - a person who acts on behalf of another, especially in secret or official capacity
  • Synonym: informant - someone who provides secret or inside information
  • Synonym: detective - a person, especially a police officer, who investigates crimes
  • Antonym: counterspy - a spy who detects and thwarts spying by an enemy

Etymology

From Old French *espier* (to watch), of Germanic origin.
