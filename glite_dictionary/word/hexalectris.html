Hexalectris

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛksəˈlɛktrɪs / (AmE), / ˌhɛksəˈlɛktrɪs / (BrE)
A genus of orchids consisting of fully myco-heterotrophic species, native to North America.
a genus of myco-heterotrophic orchids

Examples

The rare Hexalectris orchid is sought after by botanists for its unique appearance.
Several species within the Hexalectris genus are native to arid regions of North America.
Conservationists are concerned about habitat loss affecting populations of Hexalectris in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - any plant of the large family Orchidaceae

Etymology

From Greek 'hexa' (six) and 'lektris' (one who lies), referring to floral structure.

2. NOUN

/ hɛksəˈlɛktrɪs / (AmE), / hɛksəˈlɛktrɪs / (BrE)
a genus of orchids in the family Orchidaceae, consisting of about ten known species that are fully myco-heterotrophic, lacking chlorophyll and depending on fungi for nutrition
a genus of myco-heterotrophic orchids

Examples

The rare Hexalectris orchids can be found growing in symbiosis with underground fungi.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Hexalectris genus.
Because Hexalectris orchids lack chlorophyll, they obtain nutrients entirely through myco-heterotrophy.

Etymology

From Greek hex- (six) and lektris (couch or bed), referring to the flower structure.
