venae labiales posteriores

1. NOUN

/ ˈviːnaɪ læˈbaɪəliːz pɒsˈtɪəriˌɔːriz / (AmE), / ˈviːnaɪ læˈbeɪliːz pɒsˈtɪəriˌɔːriːz / (BrE)
Veins that pass posteriorly from the labia majora to the internal pudendal vein.
posterior veins from the labia majora
medical

Examples

The venae labiales posteriores drain blood from the labia majora into the internal pudendal vein.
During surgery, care must be taken to avoid damaging the venae labiales posteriores.
An obstruction in the venae labiales posteriores can lead to localized swelling.

Etymology

Latin: venae (veins), labiales (of the lips), posteriores (posterior, toward the back).
