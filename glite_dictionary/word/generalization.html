generalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛn(ə)rələˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn(ə)rəlʌɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
(Psychology) The process by which a learned response to one stimulus is elicited by similar, but different, stimuli.
transfer of a learned response to similar stimuli
medical

Examples

In classical conditioning, generalization can cause a dog to salivate at the sound of similar tones.
The child's fear showed generalization when she became anxious around all dogs, not just the one that bit her.
Therapists monitor generalization to ensure positive behaviors apply to new settings or stimuli.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛn(ə)rəlɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn(ə)rəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of forming a general concept by identifying and abstracting common features from specific instances
formulating general concepts from specific cases

Examples

Scientists use generalization to draw broad conclusions from specific data gathered in experiments.
A key skill in mathematics is the generalization of patterns to solve a wide range of problems.
The child's generalization that all four-legged animals are dogs was corrected by his parents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abstraction - the process of extracting the underlying essence or most important aspects

Etymology

From generalize + -ation; ultimately from Latin generalis (of a whole class).

3. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛn(ə)rələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn(ə)rəlʌɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a statement or conclusion that applies broadly rather than to a specific case or individual.
a broad statement or conclusion

Examples

People often make sweeping generalizations about cultures they know little about.
The study warns against drawing generalizations from a small sample size.
He tends to rely on generalizations rather than looking at specific details.

Synset Examples

he spoke in broad generalities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stereotype - a widely held but oversimplified idea of a particular type of person or thing
  • Antonym: particularization - the process of making something specific

Etymology

From French généralisation and late Latin generalisatio, from generalis (general).

4. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛn(ə)rələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn(ə)rələˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the process of forming general concepts or principles from a set of specific instances or facts
reasoning from specifics to general principles

Examples

His generalization from a few experiments led to a widely accepted scientific principle.
It is important to avoid making a generalization based on limited evidence.
She explained how a generalization is formed by spotting patterns among individual cases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: induction - reasoning from specific cases to general rules
  • Antonym: deduction - reasoning from general principles to specific instances

Etymology

From French 'généralisation', from 'généraliser' (to generalize).
