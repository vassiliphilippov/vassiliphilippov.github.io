mole

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊleɪ / (AmE), / ˈməʊleɪ / (BrE)
A rich, spicy Mexican sauce, often containing chocolate, served with meat or poultry.
spicy Mexican sauce with chocolate

Examples

The chicken was topped with a rich mole that balanced sweet and savory flavors.
At the festival, they served homemade mole poured over roasted pork.
She spent hours preparing a traditional Oaxacan mole for the family dinner.

Etymology

From Mexican Spanish 'mole', from Nahuatl 'mōlli' (sauce).

3. NOUN

/ moʊl / (AmE), / məʊl / (BrE)
a small, burrowing mammal with velvety fur, tiny eyes, and strong front feet adapted for digging
small burrowing mammal

Examples

A mole dug a network of tunnels under the vegetable garden.
The soft fur of a mole helps it move easily through the soil.
We rarely see a mole above ground during the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrew - small insectivorous mammal similar in size to a mole

Etymology

From Middle English 'mol', from Old English 'moldwyrt' (literally 'earth thrower').

4. NOUN

/ moʊl / (AmE), / məʊl / (BrE)
The SI base unit of amount of substance, equal to the quantity containing as many elementary entities as there are atoms in 12 grams of carbon-12 (approximately 6.022 × 10²³ entities).
SI unit of amount of substance (chemistry)

Examples

One mole of water contains approximately 6.022 × 10²³ water molecules.
To accurately measure reagents in chemistry, quantities are often expressed in moles.
The mole is a fundamental unit used in scientific calculations involving chemical substances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: molecule - A group of atoms bonded together

Etymology

From German 'Mol', coined in the late 19th century from the word 'Molekül' (molecule).

5. NOUN

/ moʊl / (AmE), / məʊl / (BrE)
a spy who secretly works within an organization to pass information to another, often enemy, group
spy secretly working within an organization

Examples

The government discovered that a mole had been leaking sensitive information for years.
They suspected there was a mole inside the organization feeding their rivals.
After months of investigation, the police finally identified the mole responsible for the security breach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spy - a person who secretly collects and reports information about others

Etymology

Popularized in the espionage sense from British intelligence slang in the 20th century, possibly influenced by the animal's tendency to live underground and act unseen.
