Notechis

1. NOUN

/ noʊˈtiːkɪs / (AmE), / nəʊˈtiːkɪs / (BrE)
A genus of highly venomous snakes known as tiger snakes, found chiefly in Australia and Tasmania.
genus of Australian tiger snakes

Examples

The genus Notechis includes several species of highly venomous snakes found throughout southern Australia.
Researchers studied the behavior of Notechis to understand their ecological role in wetland environments.
The bite of a Notechis snake can be life-threatening and requires immediate medical attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiger snake - a venomous Australian snake of the genus Notechis

Etymology

From Greek 'nótēchis', from 'nótos' (back) + 'ékhis' (viper).

2. NOUN

/ noʊˈtiːkɪs / (AmE), / nəʊˈtiːkɪs / (BrE)
a genus of highly venomous snakes known as tiger snakes, found in southern regions of Australia and Tasmania
genus of venomous snakes (tiger snakes)

Examples

The Notechis genus includes some of the most dangerous snakes in Australia.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Notechis genus.
The venom of a Notechis can be fatal if not treated promptly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiger snake - a highly venomous Australian snake belonging to the genus Notechis

Etymology

From Greek 'notekhis', meaning 'having a keeled back', referring to the snake's dorsal scales.
