Tisha B'Av

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɪʃə bɑːˈɑːv / (AmE), / ˌtɪʃə bɒˈav / (BrE)
In Judaism, a major fast day observed on the Jewish calendar to commemorate the destruction of the First and Second Temples in Jerusalem.
Jewish fast day commemorating the destruction of the Temples

Examples

Many Jews observe Tisha B'Av each year by fasting and mourning the destruction of the temples.
On Tisha B'Av, it is traditional to read the Book of Lamentations in synagogue.
The atmosphere during Tisha B'Av services is solemn and reflective.

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Tish'ah be-Av', meaning 'ninth of Av', referring to the ninth day of the Hebrew month Av.
