to memorialise

1. VERB

/ məˈmɔːriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / mɪˈmɔːrɪəlaɪz / (BrE)
To preserve the memory of a person or event, especially by creating a memorial or holding a commemoration.
commemorate with a memorial

Examples

The town decided to memorialise its founders with a new statue in the park.
A plaque was installed to memorialise all those who contributed to the community center.
Historically significant events are often memorialised through annual public ceremonies.

Synset Examples

This sculpture commemorates the victims of the concentration camps
We memorialized the Dead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commemorate - to honor the memory of someone or something, especially with a ceremony or memorial

Etymology

From 'memorial' + '-ise', modeled after 'memorialize'.

2. VERB

/ məˈmɔːriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / mɪˈmɔːriəlaɪz / (BrE)
to commemorate or honor someone or something in a formal speech, writing, or ceremony
commemorate formally; honor in speech or writing

Examples

Every year, the community memorialises those lost in the disaster with a public ceremony.
She memorialised her grandfather in a touching speech at the dedication.
The plaque memorialises the achievements of the institution's founders.

Synset Examples

The President memorialized the heroes of the battle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commemorate - to honor the memory of someone or something

Etymology

From memorial + -ise, influenced by French mémorialiser.
