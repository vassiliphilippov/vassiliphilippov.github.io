Ceterach officinarum

1. NOUN

/ ˌsiː.təˈræk əf.ɪ.sɪˈneə.rəm / (AmE), / ˌsiː.təˈræk ɒf.ɪ.sɪˈneə.rəm / (BrE)
A small European fern with chaffy, leathery fronds, commonly found on rocky habitats.
a small European fern

Examples

The rocky walls were covered with Ceterach officinarum, thriving in the crevices.
Botanists often study Ceterach officinarum due to its resilience to drought.
Gardeners value Ceterach officinarum for its attractive, leathery fronds.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Ceterach', a traditional fern name, and 'officinarum', meaning 'of the shops' (i.e., used in medicine).
