Laricariidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛrɪˈkɛərɪˌɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌlarɪˌkɛərɪˈɪdɪiː / (BrE)
a family of freshwater catfish known as armored catfish, native chiefly to South America, with bony plates covering their bodies
family of armored freshwater catfish

Examples

The Laricariidae are well-known for their bony armored plates and sucker mouths.
Aquarists often keep species from the Laricariidae family due to their algae-eating habits.
Researchers discovered several new Laricariidae species in the Amazon River basin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armored catfish - common name for members of Laricariidae, freshwater fish with bony plates

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus name Loricaria and suffix -idae indicating a zoological family.
