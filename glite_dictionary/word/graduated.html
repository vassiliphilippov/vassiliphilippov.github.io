graduated

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡrædʒuˌeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈɡrædjuːeɪtɪd / (BrE)
marked with lines or divisions showing different levels or measurements, especially on instruments or containers
marked with measurement divisions

Examples

The laboratory uses graduated cylinders to measure precise volumes of liquid.
Make sure the beaker is graduated so you can read the amount accurately.
A graduated scale on the ruler helps users make exact measurements.

Synset Examples

a calibrated thermometer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calibrated - marked or adjusted with measurements for accuracy

Etymology

From Latin 'gradus' (step, degree), via Medieval Latin 'graduatus' (graduated).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03159654-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡrædʒuˌeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈɡrædʒuːeɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of something) arranged or divided into steps, stages, or degrees; marked by successive levels or increments.
arranged in steps or degrees

Examples

The test uses a graduated scale to measure improvement over time.
We implemented a graduated system of rewards based on performance.
Their response to the problem was graduated, becoming stricter with each violation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incremental - increasing gradually, especially by regular degrees or additions

Etymology

From Latin 'gradus' (step), via Medieval Latin 'graduatus' (having steps).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01146623-s
Synset POS: s
