renovator

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛnəˌveɪtər / (AmE), / ˈrɛnəʊveɪtə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to restore or refurbish buildings, rooms, or antique furniture
person who restores buildings or furniture

Examples

The renovator transformed the old house into a beautiful modern home.
As a professional renovator, she specialized in restoring Victorian-era furniture.
The museum hired a renovator to refinish several antique cabinets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restorer - a person who restores works of art, buildings, or artifacts
  • Degree Lower: builder - a person in the business of constructing buildings

Etymology

From renovate + -or, indicating an agent noun.
