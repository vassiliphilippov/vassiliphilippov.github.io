shanghaier

1. NOUN

/ ʃæŋˈhaɪər / (AmE), / ʃæŋˈhaɪə / (BrE)
a person who kidnaps men, often by drugging or trickery, to force them into service on a ship
one who forces men onto ships

Examples

The notorious shanghaier prowled the docks, searching for unsuspecting victims.
In the nineteenth century, sailors feared falling prey to a shanghaier in port cities.
After awakening on a foreign vessel, he realized he'd been taken by a shanghaier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kidnapper - a person who seizes and detains someone by force

Etymology

From the city name Shanghai, after the practice of coercing sailors who then often ended up in the Chinese port of Shanghai.
