planting

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈplɑːn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of placing seeds or young plants in the ground so that they can grow
placing seeds or plants in soil to grow

Examples

Spring is the best time for planting vegetables in your garden.
The planting of trees along the highway helps reduce pollution.
Careful planting ensures the crops have enough space to grow.

Synset Examples

the planting of corn is hard work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sowing - the act of scattering seeds on or in the earth

Etymology

From the verb 'plant' plus the noun-forming suffix '-ing'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈplæn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈplɑːn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
an area where trees, shrubs, or flowers have been planted, typically as part of a garden or landscape design
area of planted trees, shrubs, or flowers

Examples

The landscaper added a new planting of roses near the entrance to the building.
A narrow planting of shrubs separates the driveway from the lawn.
They admired the colorful planting of tulips along the garden path.

Synset Examples

the landscape architect suggested a small planting in the northwest corner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garden bed - a section of ground where plants are grown together
  • Synonym: grove - a small group of trees planted together

Etymology

From the verb 'plant' + -ing; noun use dates to the 17th century.

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ˈplæn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈplɑːn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of placing something or someone firmly in position, especially secretly or with intent to cause an effect
act of fixing or placing firmly in place

Examples

The planting of microphones in the room was discovered by investigators.
There was evidence of the planting of security guards at all entrances before the event.
She was accused of the planting of false information to mislead the public.

Synset Examples

he ordered the planting of policemen outside every doorway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: placement - the act of putting something in a specific place
  • Antonym: removal - the act of taking something away from a place

Etymology

From the verb 'plant', ultimately from Old English 'plante', from Latin 'planta'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
