Tsuga caroliniana

1. NOUN

/ ˈtsuːɡə ˌkæroʊlɪˈaːnə / (AmE), / ˈtsuːɡə ˌkærəlɪˈɑːnɪə / (BrE)
A medium-sized evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States, known for its spreading branches and widely diverging cone scales; commonly called Carolina hemlock.
species of hemlock tree native to southeastern U.S.

Examples

Botanists identified the old tree as a Tsuga caroliniana thriving on the rocky hillside.
The Tsuga caroliniana is highly valued for its shade and ornamental qualities in southern gardens.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect native stands of Tsuga caroliniana from invasive pests.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Tsuga (of Japanese origin), species epithet caroliniana referring to the Carolinas (southeastern U.S.).
