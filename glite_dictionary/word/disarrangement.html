disarrangement

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.əˈreɪndʒ.mənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈreɪndʒ.mənt / (BrE)
A state in which the usual or proper order of something has been disrupted.
disturbed or disrupted order

Examples

The sudden disarrangement of her desk made it hard to find anything quickly.
Scientists studied the disarrangement of particles in the experiment after the explosion.
There was a clear disarrangement in the files, making the archive difficult to navigate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disorder - lack of order or organization
  • Antonym: order - a condition of regular or proper arrangement

Etymology

Formed from 'dis-' (a prefix meaning 'apart' or 'away') and 'arrangement'.
