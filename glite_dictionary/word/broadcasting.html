broadcasting

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɔːdˌkæstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdˌkɑːstɪŋ / (BrE)
the distribution of audio or video content to a large audience via television, radio, or other telecommunications media
transmitting programs by radio, television, or telecommunications

Examples

Modern broadcasting allows millions of people to watch live events from their homes.
The rapid evolution of broadcasting has transformed how society receives news and entertainment.
She pursued a career in broadcasting after graduating from journalism school.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: narrowcasting - the transmission of information to a specific, limited audience
  • Synonym: television - the transmission of moving images and sound to viewers
  • Synonym: radio - the transmission of audio programs via electromagnetic waves

Etymology

From 'broadcast' + '-ing'; 'broadcast' originally meant 'scattering seeds widely', later applied to radio/TV transmission.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrɔdˌkæstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdkɑːstɪŋ / (BrE)
the occupation or activity of taking part in radio or television programs, especially as a host, presenter, or contributor
taking part in radio or TV programs

Examples

She began her career in broadcasting as a newsreader for the local radio station.
Many people dream of working in broadcasting but only a few succeed.
Live broadcasting requires quick thinking and excellent communication skills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: presenting - hosting or introducing radio or TV programs
  • Antonym: narrowcasting - transmitting a programme to a specialized audience

Etymology

From 'broadcast' (to transmit by radio or television) + '-ing', originally from agriculture, meaning to scatter seed widely.
