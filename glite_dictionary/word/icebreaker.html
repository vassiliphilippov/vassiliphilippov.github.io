icebreaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪsˌbreɪkər / (AmE), / ˈaɪsˌbreɪkə / (BrE)
an action, remark, or activity intended to relieve tension or get conversation going in a formal or awkward situation
an action to relax tension or start conversation

Examples

The team played a silly game as an icebreaker before starting the meeting.
Her witty story served as the perfect icebreaker at the dinner party.
A simple introduction exercise can act as an effective icebreaker in a group of strangers.

Synset Examples

he told jokes as an icebreaker

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conversation starter - something said or done to initiate conversation

Etymology

From 'ice' + 'breaker', originally referring to ships that break ice; figurative use dates from the late 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪsˌbreɪkər / (AmE), / ˈaɪsˌbreɪkə / (BrE)
a ship designed with a reinforced bow to break up ice and keep shipping channels open in ice-covered waters
ship that breaks ice for navigation

Examples

An icebreaker plowed through the thick Arctic ice to lead the way for cargo ships.
The research team relied on an icebreaker to access the remote polar station during winter.
New icebreakers are being built to withstand even harsher frozen sea conditions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ice - frozen water; solid state of water

Etymology

From 'ice' + 'breaker', describing its function to break ice.
