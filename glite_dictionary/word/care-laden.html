care-laden

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɛrˌleɪdən / (AmE), / ˈkeəˌleɪdən / (BrE)
Burdened with worries or anxieties; weighed down by cares.
burdened with worries or cares

Examples

She walked into the room, her face care-laden after a long day.
The novel portrays a care-laden protagonist struggling with personal losses.
His care-laden expression revealed the stress he tried to hide from others.

Synset Examples

all ye that labor and are heavy-laden

Related Terms

  • Antonym: carefree - free from worries or responsibilities

Etymology

Formed from 'care' (worry) and the suffix '-laden' (carrying a load), from Old English 'hlæden'.
