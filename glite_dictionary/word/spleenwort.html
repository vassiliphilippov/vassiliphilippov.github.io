spleenwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈspliːnwɜːrt / (AmE), / ˈspliːnwɜːt / (BrE)
any of a group of ferns, chiefly growing on rocks or walls, of the genus Asplenium
a type of rock-dwelling fern (Asplenium)

Examples

The old stone wall was covered in spleenwort and moss.
Gardeners often seek out spleenwort for its delicate, feathery fronds.
Several species of spleenwort thrive in shady, rocky crevices.

Etymology

From Middle English 'splenewort', from Old English 'splēonwyrt' (plant supposedly useful in treating disorders of the spleen, from 'splēon' (spleen) + 'wyrt' (wort, plant)).
