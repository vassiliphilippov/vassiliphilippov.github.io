SbW

1. NOUN

/ ɛs biː ˈdʌbəl.juː / (AmE), / ɛs biː ˈdʌbəl.juː / (BrE)
The compass point that is one point west of due south, corresponding to south by west (191.25° on a compass).
compass point one point west of due south

Examples

The ship is heading SbW to avoid the storm.
Set your course to SbW and you'll reach the harbor by noon.
According to the chart, the lighthouse lies SbW from our current position.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: south - the compass point at 180°
  • Degree Higher: south-southwest - the compass point one point west of SbW (202.5°)

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'south by west,' used in navigation.
