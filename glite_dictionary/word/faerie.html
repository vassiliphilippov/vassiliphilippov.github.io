faerie

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛri / (AmE), / ˈfeəri / (BrE)
A small, magical being in folklore, typically human in form and often depicted as playful or mischievous.
mythical small magical being

Examples

In the story, a mischievous faerie led the children through the mysterious forest.
The delicate wings of the faerie sparkled in the moonlight.
Legends say that faeries dance in circles on midsummer’s eve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fairy - a small mythical being of folklore with magical powers

Etymology

From Old French 'faerie' (enchantment, fairyland), from 'fae' (fairy).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪ.əri / (AmE), / ˈfeəri / (BrE)
the magical realm inhabited by fairies; an enchanted supernatural world, often found in folklore and literature
the enchanted world of fairies

Examples

Many ancient tales are set in the mysterious land of faerie.
The poet described his journey through faerie as a dreamlike adventure.
Legends say that mortals rarely return unchanged from the realm of faerie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fairyland - the mythical land or realm where fairies live
  • Synonym: fairy - a small imaginary being of human form with magical powers

Etymology

From Old French 'faerie' (enchantment, fairyland), from 'fae' (fairy).
