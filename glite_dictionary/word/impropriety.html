impropriety

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˌprɑːˈpraɪ.ə.ti / (AmE), / ɪmˌprɒˈpraɪ.ə.ti / (BrE)
an act or behavior that is improper, indecent, or not in accordance with accepted standards
improper or indecent act

Examples

The senator resigned after allegations of financial impropriety emerged.
Any impropriety at work could lead to disciplinary action.
She was shocked by the impropriety of his remarks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: propriety - conformity to accepted standards, proper behavior
  • Synonym: misconduct - improper or unlawful behavior

Etymology

From Late Latin improprietas (impropriety), from Latin improprius (improper).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪm.prəˈpraɪ.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪm.prəˈprʌɪ.ə.ti / (BrE)
behavior that is improper or not in accordance with accepted standards; an act that is socially or morally incorrect
improper behavior or act

Examples

His comments during the meeting were considered a serious impropriety by his colleagues.
Any impropriety in her public behavior could damage her reputation.
He was dismissed from the company due to financial improprieties uncovered during an audit.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: propriety - behavior that conforms to accepted standards; appropriateness
  • Synonym: misconduct - improper or unprofessional behavior

Etymology

From Latin improprietas, from improprius (not proper).

3. NOUN

/ ɪmˌprɑːˈpraɪəti / (AmE), / ɪmˌprɒˈpraɪəti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being improper; behavior or actions that are not socially acceptable or do not conform to standards
state of being improper; improper behavior

Examples

The manager was dismissed for financial impropriety during his tenure.
Any suggestion of impropriety can damage a politician's career.
She apologized for her social impropriety at the dinner party.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: propriety - the state of conforming to conventional standards of behavior
  • Synonym: impropriety - an improper act or behavior

Etymology

From Latin improprietas (want of propriety), from improprius (improper).

4. NOUN

/ ˌɪm.prəˈpraɪ.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪm.prəˈprʌɪ.ə.ti / (BrE)
an act or instance of improper or indecent behavior, especially undue intimacy or conduct considered socially unacceptable
improper or indecent behavior

Examples

There was talk of impropriety after the two colleagues were seen leaving the party together.
The politician resigned following allegations of impropriety with a staff member.
Any hint of impropriety at the workplace must be taken seriously by management.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indecency - behavior or language that is morally offensive, especially in a sexual way
  • Antonym: propriety - behavior that is considered correct or appropriate

Etymology

From Late Latin improprietas (impropriety), from Latin improprius (not proper).
