sanctified

1. ADJ

/ ˈsæŋk.tɪ.faɪd / (AmE), / ˈsaŋk.tɪ.faɪd / (BrE)
made holy through a religious rite; declared holy or set apart for sacred use.
made or declared holy

Examples

The old chapel was sanctified by a bishop over a century ago.
The ceremony included sanctified water sprinkled on the congregation.
Some traditions regard marriage as a sanctified bond.

Synset Examples

a consecrated church
the sacred mosque
sacred elephants
sacred bread and wine
sanctified wine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consecrated - declared sacred, especially through a religious ceremony
  • Synonym: sacred - regarded with reverence or religious respect
  • Antonym: profane - not holy or not relating to religion; secular

Etymology

From Latin sanctificare (to make holy), from sanctus (holy) + facere (to make).
