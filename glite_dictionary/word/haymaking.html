haymaking

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪˌmeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈheɪˌmeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
The activity of cutting grass and drying it to make hay, typically as animal feed.
the process of making hay

Examples

Every summer, the farmers spend weeks engaged in haymaking to prepare for winter.
Traditional haymaking requires both good weather and careful timing to ensure the best quality hay.
With advances in machinery, haymaking has become faster and less labor-intensive than in the past.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harvesting - the process of gathering mature crops from the fields

Etymology

Formed from 'hay' (grass cut and dried) + 'making'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈheɪˌmeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈheɪˌmeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of taking full advantage of a favorable opportunity while it lasts
taking advantage of an opportunity

Examples

The festival only comes to town once a year, so vendors are busy haymaking during the busy weekend.
With the stock market booming, investors are haymaking while conditions are right.
Many companies are haymaking as consumer demand for their products temporarily spikes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opportunism - the practice of taking advantage of opportunities regardless of morals

Etymology

From the literal 'making of hay' in agriculture, used metaphorically from the proverb 'make hay while the sun shines.'
