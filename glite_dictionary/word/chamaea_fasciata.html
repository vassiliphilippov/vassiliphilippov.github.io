Chamaea fasciata

1. NOUN

/ kəˌmiːə fæˈsiːətə / (AmE), / kəˌmiːə fæsɪˈɑːtə / (BrE)
A small brown bird native to California, resembling a wren; also known as the wrentit.
a small Californian bird (wrentit)

Examples

While hiking, we spotted a Chamaea fasciata darting through the underbrush.
The Chamaea fasciata is known for its distinctive song and elusive nature.
In California, the Chamaea fasciata is often found in coastal scrub habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrentit - common name for Chamaea fasciata

Etymology

From Greek khamaí (on the ground) and Latin fasciata (banded, striped), referring to its coloring and habits.
