proto-

1. X

/ ˈproʊtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈprəʊtəʊ / (BrE)
a prefix meaning 'first', 'earliest', or 'original', used to form words that refer to the earliest or original version of something
prefix meaning earliest or original

Examples

The term proto-hominid refers to the earliest members of the human lineage.
Linguists study protolanguages to trace the origins of modern languages.
Archaeologists discovered proto-cities dating back to the Neolithic period.

Synset Examples

‘proto’ is a combining form in a word like ‘protolanguage’ that refers to the hypothetical ancestor of another language or group of languages

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pre- - prefix meaning before in time or order

Etymology

From Greek prōto-, from prōtos (first).
