dobson

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑb.sən / (AmE), / ˈdɒb.sən / (BrE)
a large brown aquatic larva of the dobsonfly, used as fishing bait
aquatic larva of the dobsonfly

Examples

Fishermen often use dobson as bait because of its effectiveness in attracting fish.
The riverbank was teeming with rocks hiding the elusive dobson.
He carefully hooked a wriggling dobson before casting his line into the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hellgrammite - the aquatic larva of the dobsonfly, used as bait

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɑb.sən / (AmE), / ˈdɒb.sən / (BrE)
A large soft-bodied insect; the male has long slender mandibles, and its aquatic larvae are often used as bait.
large insect; aquatic larvae used as bait

Examples

Fishermen often use dobson larvae as live bait for catching bass and trout.
The male dobson is known for its impressively long jaws.
As children, we would search streams for dobson larvae under rocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dobsonfly - a large insect, especially the male with long mandibles
