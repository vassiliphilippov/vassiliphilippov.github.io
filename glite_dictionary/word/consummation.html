consummation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnsəˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsjʊˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of bringing something to completion or fulfilment
the act of completion or fulfilment

Examples

The consummation of the long-awaited peace treaty was celebrated by both nations.
She waited patiently for the consummation of her years of hard work and dedication.
The project reached its consummation after many months of planning and effort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completion - the act of completing something
  • Antonym: beginning - the act of starting something

Etymology

From Latin consummatio (completion), from consummare (to complete).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnsəˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsjʊˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making a marriage or relationship complete by having sexual intercourse, especially on the wedding night
completion of marriage by sexual intercourse
legal

Examples

They celebrated the consummation of their marriage with a traditional wedding night ceremony.
In some cultures, the consummation of marriage is an important legal and social milestone.
The annulment was granted on the grounds of non-consummation of the union.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completion - the act of finishing something

Etymology

From Latin consummatio (completion, finishing), from consummare (to complete).
