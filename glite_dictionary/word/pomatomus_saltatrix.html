Pomatomus saltatrix

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈmætəməs sælˈtætrɪks / (AmE), / pəˈmatəməs sælˈtatrɪks / (BrE)
A species of bluish, warm-water marine fish found in many parts of the world, valued both as food and game fish and known for following schools of smaller fish into shallow waters; commonly called bluefish.
species of bluefish; marine game fish

Examples

Fishermen often seek out Pomatomus saltatrix during the summer months for its spirited fight and flavor.
The diet of Pomatomus saltatrix includes sardines, anchovies, and other small fish.
Researchers study the migration patterns of Pomatomus saltatrix along the Atlantic coast each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluefish - common name for Pomatomus saltatrix, a marine predatory fish

Etymology

From Greek 'poma' (cover, lid) + 'tome' (cut) + Latinized as a specific epithet.
