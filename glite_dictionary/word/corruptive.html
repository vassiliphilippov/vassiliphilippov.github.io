corruptive

1. ADJ

/ kəˈrʌptɪv / (AmE), / kəˈrʌptɪv / (BrE)
causing or tending to cause corruption or moral deterioration
causing corruption

Examples

Some argue that unchecked power has a corruptive influence on even the most principled individuals.
The corruptive nature of the bribe undermined the integrity of the entire organization.
Children are especially vulnerable to the corruptive effects of violent media content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrupt - having or showing a willingness to act dishonestly for personal gain
  • Antonym: reformative - serving to reform or improve morally

Etymology

From Latin 'corruptus', past participle of 'corrumpere' (to destroy, corrupt), with the suffix '-ive' forming adjectives meaning 'having the nature of'.
