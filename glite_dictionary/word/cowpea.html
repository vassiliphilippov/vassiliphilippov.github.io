cowpea

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊˌpi / (AmE), / ˈkaʊpiː / (BrE)
A plant of the species Vigna unguiculata, grown for its edible beans, which are eaten fresh or dried.
edible bean of the cowpea plant

Examples

The farmer harvested fresh cowpea pods from the field early in the morning.
Dried cowpea is a staple ingredient in many West African dishes.
She added chopped cowpea leaves to the soup for extra nutrition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black-eyed pea - a variety of cowpea with a distinctive black spot

Etymology

From 'cow' + 'pea', originally referring to use as fodder for cows.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊˌpi / (AmE), / ˈkaʊˌpiː / (BrE)
An annual leguminous plant (Vigna unguiculata), cultivated for its edible seeds, pods, and as fodder or green manure, especially in warm regions.
an edible legume plant; Vigna unguiculata

Examples

Farmers often plant cowpea as a cover crop to enrich the soil with nitrogen.
The southern United States is known for traditional dishes that use cowpea as a main ingredient.
This variety of cowpea is highly resistant to drought and suitable for hot climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black-eyed pea - a variety of cowpea with a distinctive black spot

Etymology

Formed from 'cow' + 'pea', referring to its use as fodder and its resemblance to other peas.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊ.pi / (AmE), / ˈkaʊ.piː / (BrE)
the edible seed or pod of a tropical leguminous plant (Vigna unguiculata), widely cultivated as food and forage
edible seed or pod of the cowpea plant

Examples

The farmer grew cowpeas in his field to improve the soil and provide food for his family.
Dishes made with cowpea are popular in West African cuisine.
After harvesting, the cowpea seeds were dried and stored for the winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black-eyed pea - a variety of cowpea with a distinctive black spot
  • Degree Lower: chickpea - another edible legume seed, often confused with cowpea

Etymology

From 'cow' + 'pea', named for its use as fodder and resemblance to other peas.
