longanimity

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɔːŋ.əˈnɪm.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌlɒŋ.əˈnɪm.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
patient endurance of hardship, provocation, or delay without complaint; good-natured tolerance, especially in the face of adversity or incompetence.
patient endurance or forbearance

Examples

Despite numerous setbacks, her longanimity inspired those around her to remain calm and hopeful.
The manager handled the repeated mistakes with remarkable longanimity instead of anger.
In times of crisis, longanimity can be the key to maintaining unity and purpose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forbearance - patient self-control; restraint and tolerance
  • Synonym: patience - the capacity to accept or tolerate delay, problems, or suffering
  • Antonym: impatience - lack of patience; unwillingness to tolerate delay

Etymology

From Latin longanimitas, from longus (long) + animus (mind, spirit).
