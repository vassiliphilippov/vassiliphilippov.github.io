waybill

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪˌbɪl / (AmE), / ˈweɪˌbɪl / (BrE)
an official document that lists details of goods being transported and serves as a receipt issued by the carrier to the shipper, specifying terms of delivery.
document listing shipped goods and terms

Examples

The shipping company attached the waybill to the package to ensure it reached the correct recipient.
Before loading the cargo, the driver checked the waybill for delivery instructions and items listed.
Every consignment must be accompanied by a valid waybill as proof of shipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bill of lading - a detailed receipt for shipped goods, used as a contract for carriage

Etymology

From 'way' (meaning 'route') + 'bill' (meaning 'written statement or list').
