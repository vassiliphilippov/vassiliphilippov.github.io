chapterhouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæp.tɚˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈtʃæp.təˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A house used as a residence by a chapter of a fraternity or similar organization.
fraternity residence house

Examples

The university's oldest chapterhouse stands at the corner of Fifth and Maple.
Many events and fundraisers are held at the chapterhouse throughout the academic year.
After joining the fraternity, he moved into the chapterhouse with his new brothers.

Etymology

From 'chapter', referring to an organized group, and 'house'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæp.tɚˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈtʃæp.təˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A building attached to a monastery or cathedral, used as a meeting place for the chapter.
monastery or cathedral meeting building

Examples

The monks gathered in the chapterhouse every morning to discuss abbey matters.
The cathedral's chapterhouse is famous for its intricate stained glass windows.
Restoration work was recently completed on the medieval chapterhouse adjacent to the cloister.

Etymology

From 'chapter' (referring to the clerical body) + 'house.'
