chime

1. NOUN

/ tʃaɪm / (AmE), / tʃaɪm / (BrE)
a percussion instrument consisting of a set of tuned metal bells that are struck with a hammer to produce musical notes, used in orchestras and ensembles
musical instrument of tuned bells

Examples

The orchestra featured a chime solo during the dramatic climax of the piece.
She carefully played each chime in the set to create a delicate melody.
The composer wrote a part for chime that added brightness to the music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glockenspiel - a percussion instrument with metal bars arranged like a keyboard

Etymology

From Old French *chier* (to ring), from Late Latin *clamare* (to call, cry).

2. VERB

/ tʃaɪm / (AmE), / tʃaɪm / (BrE)
(of a bell or similar object) to make a clear, ringing sound; to emit a series of musical notes.
make a ringing sound (as a bell)

Examples

At noon, the church bells chime across the entire valley, marking the hour.
Every morning, the ancient clock chimes six times to wake the village.
They all paused to listen as the temple bells chimed gently in the breeze.

Synset Examples

bells and gongs chimed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ring - to make a resonant or bell-like sound

Etymology

From Old French *chimer* (to resound), probably from Late Latin *camminare* (to sing responsively).
