to forefend

1. VERB

/ ˌfɔrəˈfɛnd / (AmE), / ˌfɔːrɪˈfɛnd / (BrE)
To prevent something from happening; to protect against or ward off danger or harm.
to prevent or ward off
archaic

Examples

In ancient times, people hung charms to forefend evil spirits from their homes.
The medical staff took every precaution to forefend infection during the operation.
He hoped his caution would forefend any misunderstandings during the negotiation.

Synset Examples

Let's avoid a confrontation
head off a confrontation
avert a strike

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prevent - to stop something from happening
  • Synonym: to avert - to turn away or prevent something undesirable
  • Antonym: invite - to cause or welcome something, especially something undesirable

Etymology

From Old English 'forfendan', influenced by Latin 'defendere' (to defend).
