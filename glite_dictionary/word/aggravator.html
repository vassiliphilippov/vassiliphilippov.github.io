aggravator

1. NOUN

/ ˈæɡrəˌveɪtər / (AmE), / ˈæɡrəˌveɪtə / (BrE)
a person who causes annoyance or exasperation; someone who irritates others
person who irritates or annoys

Examples

No one wanted to sit next to the aggravator who kept tapping his pen during the meeting.
She discovered her little brother was quite the aggravator when he hid her phone for the third time that day.
The relentless aggravator in the apartment upstairs made it impossible to get a good night’s sleep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annoyer - a person who annoys others
  • Synonym: irritator - a person or thing that irritates
  • Antonym: comforter - a person who consoles or soothes

Etymology

From Latin 'aggravare' (to make heavier), via the verb 'aggravate'.
