individualist

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.dəˈvɪdʒ.u.ə.lɪst / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɪˈvɪdʒ.u.ə.lɪst / (BrE)
a person who thinks or acts independently rather than following others or conforming to a group
person valuing independence

Examples

As an individualist, she prefers to make her own decisions rather than follow the crowd.
The company values individualist thinkers who bring fresh perspectives to their projects.
He has always been an individualist, unafraid to challenge prevailing opinions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonconformist - a person who does not conform to established customs or attitudes
  • Antonym: conformist - a person who conforms to accepted behavior or established practices

Etymology

From French *individualiste*, from *individu* (individual) + -iste (-ist).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪndəˈvɪdʒuəlɪst / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈvɪdjʊəlɪst / (BrE)
characterized by or showing a strong sense of individuality and independence
showing individuality; independent

Examples

Her individualist approach to fashion made her stand out from the crowd.
The company encourages an individualist attitude among its employees.
He has always been an individualist thinker, preferring his own path over popular trends.

Synset Examples

an individualistic way of dressing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: individualistic - showing a marked individuality; not conforming
  • Antonym: conformist - tending to conform to accepted standards or customs

Etymology

From individual + -ist, first used in the early 19th century.
