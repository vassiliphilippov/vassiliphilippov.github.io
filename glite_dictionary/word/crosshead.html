crosshead

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌhɛd / (BrE)
A heading for a subsection of text, printed within the main body of an article or document, often to break up or organize content.
heading within the body of text

Examples

Each chapter featured a crosshead to introduce its key themes.
The editor added several crossheads to make the article easier to navigate.
A bold crosshead separated the section on safety precautions from the main instructions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subheading - a secondary heading dividing a section of text

Etymology

From 'cross' + 'head', referring to a heading across the page or section.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌhɛd / (BrE)
A metal block in a piston engine that connects to a piston and slides on guides to move a connecting rod back and forth.
metal block connecting piston and rod in engines

Examples

The engine's crosshead needs regular lubrication to ensure smooth movement on the guides.
A worn crosshead can lead to serious damage within the steam engine assembly.
The mechanic replaced the damaged crosshead with a new component designed for higher efficiency.

Etymology

Formed from 'cross' and 'head', referring to the shape and function in machinery.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03141695-n
Synset POS: n
