meager

1. ADJ

/ ˈmiɡər / (AmE), / ˈmiːɡə / (BrE)
lacking in quantity or quality; insufficient or scanty
lacking in quantity or quality

Examples

They struggled to survive on their meager wages.
The portions at the restaurant were surprisingly meager.
After the drought, only a meager harvest was gathered.

Synset Examples

meager resources
meager fare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scant - barely sufficient in amount or quantity
  • Antonym: abundant - existing or available in large quantities
  • Synonym: scarce - insufficient for the demand

Etymology

From Latin 'mēger', meaning 'lean, meager'.
