Ranunculus

1. NOUN

/ rəˈnʌŋkjələs / (AmE), / rəˈnʌŋkjʊləs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants commonly known as buttercups or crowfoots, which includes annual, biennial, and perennial herbs with usually yellow or white flowers.
genus of plants: buttercup or crowfoot

Examples

Many Ranunculus species are known for their bright yellow flowers and are often called buttercups.
The garden was full of blooming Ranunculus, bringing a splash of color to the landscape.
Botanists studied several specimens of Ranunculus growing in the marshy meadow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttercup - a common name for any plant of the Ranunculus genus

Etymology

From Latin *ranunculus* (little frog), originally referring to the plant's habitat near water.

2. NOUN

/ rəˈnʌŋkjələs / (AmE), / rəˈnʌŋkjʊləs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants including buttercups and crowfoots, often with bright yellow flowers, found worldwide.
genus of flowering plants including buttercups

Examples

The bright yellow flowers of the Ranunculus caught our attention during the spring walk.
Gardeners often plant Ranunculus for their vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
The Ranunculus species are popular in bouquets for their delicate, layered petals.

Etymology

From Latin 'ranunculus', diminutive of 'rana' (frog), referring to the plant’s wet habitat.
