Zonotrichia

1. NOUN

/ zoʊ.nəˈtrɪ.ki.ə / (AmE), / zəʊ.nɒˈtrɪ.ki.ə / (BrE)
A genus of large New World sparrows, native to the Americas, including species such as the white-crowned sparrow and the golden-crowned sparrow.
genus of large New World sparrows

Examples

The Zonotrichia genus includes several well-known sparrows found across North and Central America.
Researchers studied how Zonotrichia species adapt to different climates in their habitats.
Bird watchers often identify members of Zonotrichia by their distinctive head stripes.

Etymology

From Greek zōnē (belt) and thrix, trichos (hair), referring to the birds' head markings.
