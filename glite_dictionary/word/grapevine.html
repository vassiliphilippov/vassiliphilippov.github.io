grapevine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪpˌvaɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪpvaɪn / (BrE)
a climbing woody plant of the genus Vitis, which bears clusters of edible berries called grapes
woody vine that bears grape clusters

Examples

The grapevine in the backyard produced a bumper crop of grapes this year.
Many wineries cultivate several varieties of grapevine to make their wines.
The old grapevine wrapped itself around the wooden trellis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vine - a climbing or trailing woody-stemmed plant

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪpˌvaɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪpˌvaɪn / (BrE)
rumor or unofficial information spread from person to person, especially through casual conversation rather than formal means
rumor passed by word of mouth

Examples

I heard through the grapevine that the company is planning layoffs next month.
Rumors about their relationship have been circulating on the grapevine for weeks.
The news soon spread along the grapevine, reaching people all over town.

Synset Examples

the news of their affair was spread by word of mouth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rumor - unofficial information passed from person to person
  • Synonym: gossip - casual conversation about others, often involving unconfirmed information

Etymology

Originally referring to literal grapevines; figurative sense from 'heard it through the grapevine,' 19th-century American English.

3. VERB

/ ˈɡreɪpˌvaɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪpvaɪn / (BrE)
to hear or learn information informally through rumors or unofficial sources.
learn through rumors or informal sources

Examples

I grapevined the news about the company's merger before it was officially announced.
She usually finds out what's happening in the office by grapevining.
We grapevine most of our information before HR confirms anything to us.

Synset Examples

They say they heard through the grapevine about the promotion.
You didn't hear it from me, but I heard through the grapevine.
I heard through the grapevine that she's moving away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rumor - unverified information spread informally

Etymology

From the noun 'grapevine', originally referring to the plant, later used for informal communication thought to resemble the sprawling vines.
