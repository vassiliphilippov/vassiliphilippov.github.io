sandlot

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændˌlɑt / (AmE), / ˈsændˌlɒt / (BrE)
an empty, often unpaved lot in a city or town, used by children to play informal games, especially baseball
urban vacant lot for informal games

Examples

The boys spent every afternoon playing baseball on the sandlot near their school.
Growing up, Lisa would join neighborhood games on the old sandlot behind her apartment building.
Many famous athletes got their start playing in local sandlots before joining formal teams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: playground - an outdoor area provided for children to play in, especially at a school or public park
