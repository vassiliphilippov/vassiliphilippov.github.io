pockmarked

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɑkˌmɑrkt / (AmE), / ˈpɒkˌmɑːkt / (BrE)
having small pits or scars, especially as a result of an eruptive skin disease such as smallpox or acne
scarred with pits or marks

Examples

The old man's face was pockmarked after surviving a severe case of smallpox.
Many buildings in the war zone were pockmarked by bullet holes and shrapnel.
Her cheeks became pockmarked with scars after years of severe acne.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarred - having lasting marks from wounds or injury

Etymology

Formed from 'pock' (a pustule in smallpox) + 'marked'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɑkˌmɑrkt / (AmE), / ˈpɒkˌmɑːkt / (BrE)
Covered with small holes or pits; having many marks or indentations on the surface, especially as a result of damage or wear.
marked with pits or holes

Examples

The old road was pockmarked with potholes that made for a very bumpy ride.
After years of heavy use, the parking lot became pockmarked and uneven.
A pockmarked runway can cause significant problems for landing airplanes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitted - having small depressions or holes

Etymology

From 'pock', an old term for a pustule or pit, especially from smallpox, and 'marked', meaning 'having marks'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02249458-s
Synset POS: s
