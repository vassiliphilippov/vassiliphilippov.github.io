adrenocorticotropic

1. ADJ

/ əˌdrɛn.oʊ.kɔːrˌtɪ.koʊˈtrɑː.pɪk / (AmE), / əˌdriː.nəʊ.kɔː.tɪ.kəʊˈtrɒ.pɪk / (BrE)
stimulating or acting on the adrenal cortex, especially in reference to hormones or drugs.
stimulating the adrenal cortex
medical

Examples

Adrenocorticotropic hormone increases the production of cortisol by acting on the adrenal cortex.
The adrenocorticotropic effect of the drug was evident in patients with adrenal insufficiency.
Researchers are studying the adrenocorticotropic properties of several synthetic compounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adrenocorticotrophic - having a stimulating effect on the adrenal cortex; variant spelling

Etymology

From adreno- (relating to the adrenal glands) + cortico- (cortex) + -tropic (turning toward, affecting).
