emended

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈmɛndɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈmɛndɪd / (BrE)
(of a text) improved or corrected by critical editing.
corrected by editing

Examples

The editor published the emended version of the manuscript.
Scholars compared the emended text with the original.
After careful review, an emended edition was released to correct earlier errors.

Synset Examples

the emended text

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrected - having mistakes or errors removed
  • Antonym: original - existing in or belonging to the beginning; not altered

Etymology

From Latin emendare (to correct, improve).
