to apple-polish

1. VERB

/ ˈæpəlˌpɑlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈæpəlˌpɒlɪʃ / (BrE)
to flatter someone in an insincere way, typically to gain favor or advantage
to flatter for personal gain
informal american

Examples

He tried to apple-polish his way into a promotion by constantly complimenting his boss.
It's obvious when someone is apple-polishing rather than giving genuine praise.
She apple-polished the teacher so often that her classmates noticed.

Synset Examples

He tried to apple-polish his way into a promotion.
Don’t apple-polish; just do your job honestly.
Her tendency to apple-polish annoyed her coworkers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flatter - to praise excessively in order to please someone
  • Synonym: to brown-nose - to try to win favor with someone in authority by flattering them
  • Synonym: to ingratiate - to try to make oneself liked by someone, often through flattery

Etymology

American English, from the metaphor of giving an apple to a teacher to seek favor, combined with "polish" (to make something shine).
