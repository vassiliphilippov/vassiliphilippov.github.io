curassow

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɑːsoʊ / (AmE), / kjʊəˈræsəʊ / (BrE)
a large crested arboreal bird of the family Cracidae, native to the Americas, with long legs and tails, valued as game and for food
large crested American game bird

Examples

The remote rainforest is home to the rare curassow, which is admired by birdwatchers.
Local hunters prize the meat of the curassow for its flavor and tenderness.
During our expedition, we spotted several curassows perched high in the trees.

Etymology

From French *couroucou* or *curassou*, of South American origin; possibly from Cariban languages.
