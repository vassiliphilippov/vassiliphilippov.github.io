piercingly

1. ADV

/ ˈpɪrsɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈpɪəsɪŋli / (BrE)
In an extremely intense, sharp, or penetrating manner.
in an intensely sharp manner

Examples

A piercingly cold wind blew through the city streets.
Her gaze was piercingly direct, making him uncomfortable.
The alarm rang piercingly in the quiet building, startling everyone.

Synset Examples

it was bitterly cold
bitter cold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharply - in a sharp manner; intensely
  • Antonym: mildly - in a mild or gentle manner

2. ADV

/ ˈpɪrsɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈpɪəsɪŋli / (BrE)
In a way that is very sharp, intense, or shrill, especially referring to sound or appearance.
in a very sharp or shrill way

Examples

The baby cried piercingly in the quiet hospital ward.
She whistled piercingly, turning every head in the park.
The alarm rang piercingly in the early hours of the morning.

Synset Examples

she sang rather shrilly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrilly - in a very high-pitched and piercing manner

Etymology

From 'piercing' + adverbial suffix '-ly'.
