laser

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪzər / (AmE), / ˈleɪzə / (BrE)
a device that produces an intense, narrow beam of coherent light through the process of light amplification by stimulated emission of radiation
device producing intense coherent light

Examples

The surgeon used a laser to perform the delicate eye operation.
Modern barcode scanners often use a laser to read information on products.
She pointed a laser at the wall to create a pattern of light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maser - a device operating like a laser but produces microwaves

Etymology

Acronym from 'light amplification by stimulated emission of radiation.'
