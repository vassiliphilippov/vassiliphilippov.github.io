disquietude

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈkwaɪətˌud / (AmE), / dɪsˈkwaɪ.ɪ.tjuːd / (BrE)
A feeling of anxiety or uneasiness; a state of restlessness and nervousness.
state of anxiety or uneasiness

Examples

A sense of disquietude settled over the crowd as they waited for the announcement.
She tried to hide her disquietude behind a polite smile during the tense meeting.
News of the sudden changes caused widespread disquietude among the employees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uneasiness - a feeling of anxiety or discomfort
  • Synonym: anxiety - a feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease
  • Antonym: calm - the absence of nervousness or agitation

Etymology

From Latin 'disquietudo', from 'dis-' (lack of) + 'quietus' (quiet, calm).
