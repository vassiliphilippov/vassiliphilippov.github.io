twinkle

1. NOUN

/ ˈtwɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈtwɪŋkəl / (BrE)
A bright or lively expression in the eyes or face that suggests merriment or animation.
lively brightness in the eyes or face

Examples

There was a mischievous twinkle in her grandfather's eyes as he told the story.
He spoke with a twinkle that made everyone in the room smile.
As she accepted the award, a proud twinkle lit up her face.

Synset Examples

he had a sparkle in his eye
there's a perpetual twinkle in his eyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sparkle - a lively or bright expression seen in someone's eyes or face

Etymology

From Middle English 'twynclen', frequentative of 'twinken' (to wink), of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04960557-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈtwɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈtwɪŋkəl / (BrE)
a brief or rapid flash of light; a sudden sparkle or glimmer
brief flash of light

Examples

A twinkle of light caught her eye as she opened the box.
He noticed a faint twinkle in the distance, coming from the lighthouse.
The twinkle of the stars was visible through the clear night sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sparkle - a series of small flashes of light
  • Synonym: glimmer - a faint or brief light
  • Degree Higher: glow - steady light, stronger and longer than a twinkle
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07426245-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈtwɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈtwɪŋkəl / (BrE)
to shine with a light that flashes on and off rapidly or appears to move; to gleam or glow intermittently
shine intermittently; flash or glitter

Examples

The stars twinkle brightly in the night sky.
The Christmas lights twinkled all along the street.
She watched as his eyes twinkled with amusement.

Synset Examples

The lights were flashing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glitter - shine with bright, shimmering, reflected light
  • Synonym: flash - shine with a sudden, brief light

Etymology

From Middle English *twinklen*, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch *twinkelen*, German *zwinkern* (to blink).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02164320-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈtwɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈtwɪŋkəl / (BrE)
to emit or reflect light with a flickering or sparkling effect
flicker or sparkle with light

Examples

The stars twinkle brightly on a clear winter night.
She noticed how the sunlight twinkled on the surface of the lake.
Holiday lights twinkling in the trees made the street look magical.

Synset Examples

Does a constellation twinkle more brightly than a single star?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glimmer - to shine with a weak or unsteady light
  • Synonym: sparkle - to shine with flashes of light
  • Degree Higher: shine - to produce or reflect light

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin; related to Middle Dutch twinkelen.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02770723-v
Synset POS: v
