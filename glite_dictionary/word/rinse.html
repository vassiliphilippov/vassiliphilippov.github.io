rinse

1. NOUN

/ rɪns / (AmE), / rɪns / (BrE)
a liquid preparation applied to wet hair to add a tint or color
hair coloring liquid

Examples

She applied a rinse to her hair to achieve a subtle copper tone.
After washing, he used a blue rinse to brighten his greying hair.
The salon offers a variety of rinses for different hair colors.

2. NOUN

/ rɪns / (AmE), / rɪns / (BrE)
an act of removing soap or detergent from something by washing it with clean water, usually at the end of cleaning
removal of soap with water after washing

Examples

After washing your hair, give it a thorough rinse with warm water.
The dishes need one more rinse to remove the last bit of soap.
He finished the car wash with a final rinse to make it shine.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: wash - the act of cleaning something with water or another liquid

Etymology

From Old French *rincer*, from Vulgar Latin *recentiare* (to refresh), from Latin *recens* (fresh).

/ rɪns / (AmE), / rɪns / (BrE)

act of washing lightly with water

Examples

He gave the dishes a rinse before putting them in the dishwasher.
A gentle rinse under the tap was enough to clean the strawberries.

5. VERB

/ rɪns / (AmE), / rɪns / (BrE)
to clean something, especially using water or a chemical solution, to remove dirt, detergent, or impurities
clean with water or chemicals

Examples

After you apply the shampoo, thoroughly rinse your hair with warm water.
Lab equipment must be rinsed with distilled water before use.
He rinsed the vegetables in a solution to remove any pesticide residues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wash - to clean something using water or another liquid, usually with soap

Etymology

From Middle English 'rins(en)', probably from Old French 'rincier', based on Latin 'recensare' (to wash out).

6. VERB

/ rɪns / (AmE), / rɪns / (BrE)
to wash something quickly, especially with clean water, in order to remove soap, dirt, or residue
wash to remove soap or dirt

Examples

After you soap up the dishes, make sure to rinse them thoroughly.
He quickly rinsed his hands under the tap before dinner.
You should always rinse your hair after using shampoo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wash - to clean using water or another liquid, usually with soap
  • Antonym: dirty - to make or become unclean

Etymology

From Old French 'rincer', from Vulgar Latin *recentiare, based on Latin 'recentare' (to make recent), with a sense shift to cleaning.
