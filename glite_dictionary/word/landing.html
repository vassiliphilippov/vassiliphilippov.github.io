landing

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlændɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of coming down onto the ground or another surface, especially from the air
coming down onto a surface

Examples

The airplane's landing was delayed due to fog.
The astronauts prepared for a safe landing on the lunar surface.
Her perfect landing impressed the judges at the gymnastics competition.

Synset Examples

the plane made a smooth landing
his landing on his feet was catlike

Related Terms

  • Antonym: takeoff - the act of becoming airborne

2. NOUN

/ ˈlændɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlændɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of coming to land, especially after a journey by sea or air
arrival at land after a journey

Examples

The plane's landing was delayed due to heavy fog.
After a long voyage across the Atlantic, their landing was met with cheers from the crowd.
The captain carefully guided the ship to a smooth landing on the island shore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrival - the act of arriving at a place
  • Antonym: departure - the act of leaving a place

Etymology

From land + -ing, dating from Middle English.

3. NOUN

/ ˈlændɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlændɪŋ / (BrE)
a platform or structure at the edge of water where boats can load or unload people or goods
structure for boarding or unloading from boats

Examples

The boat docked at the landing to let passengers off.
Fresh supplies were carried from the ship to the shore via the landing.
We waited by the landing until the ferry arrived.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dock - a structure where vessels may be moored, loaded, or unloaded
  • Synonym: pier - a platform extending from the shore into the water, used as a landing place for boats
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈlændɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlændɪŋ / (BrE)
an intermediate flat platform between sections of a staircase, usually where stairs change direction or provide a resting spot
platform between parts of a staircase

Examples

She paused on the landing between the first and second floors to catch her breath.
The children played quietly on the wide landing at the top of the stairs.
A large window on the landing lets natural light into the staircase.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: staircase - a set of stairs inside a building
