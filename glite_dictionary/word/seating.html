seating

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiːtɪŋ / (BrE)
an area arranged with seats for several people
area with seats for people

Examples

The stadium's seating was upgraded to provide more comfort to spectators.
There isn't enough seating for all the guests at the reception.
We reserved a table in the outdoor seating area of the restaurant.

Synset Examples

there is seating for 40 students in this classroom

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: seats - individual objects for sitting on

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiːtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiːtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or service of showing people to their seats, especially in a theater, restaurant, or other venue
ushering people to their seats

Examples

The seating for tonight's performance will begin at 7:00 pm.
We had to wait by the entrance until seating staff showed us to our table.
A polite usher managed the orderly seating of over five hundred guests.
