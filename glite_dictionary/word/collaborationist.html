collaborationist

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlæbəˈreɪʃənɪst / (AmE), / kəˌlæbəˈreɪʃənɪst / (BrE)
A person who willingly cooperates with an enemy occupying force, especially during a war.
person who collaborates with an occupying enemy

Examples

The new regime treated all known collaborationists with suspicion and contempt.
During the occupation, he was accused of being a collaborationist for aiding the enemy.
Historians debated the motivations of French collaborationists during World War II.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collaborator - a person who cooperates traitorously with an enemy
  • Antonym: resister - a person who actively resists an enemy occupier

Etymology

From French 'collaborationniste', from 'collaborer' (to collaborate) + '-iste' (denoting a person).
