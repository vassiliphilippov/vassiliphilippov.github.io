stink

1. NOUN

/ stɪŋk / (AmE), / stɪŋk / (BrE)
a very unpleasant or offensive smell
an unpleasant or offensive smell

Examples

There was a terrible stink coming from the garbage bin in the alley.
The stink of rotten eggs filled the laboratory and made everyone leave.
After forgetting to clean the refrigerator, a horrible stink developed inside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stench - a strong and very unpleasant smell
  • Synonym: reek - a strong, unpleasant smell
  • Antonym: fragrance - a pleasant, sweet smell

Etymology

Old English *stincan* (to emit a smell), related to German *stinken* (to stink).

2. VERB

/ stɪŋk / (AmE), / stɪŋk / (BrE)
(informal) To be very bad, inadequate, or objectionable.
be inadequate or objectionable
informal

Examples

The service at that restaurant really stinks these days.
Her attitude toward her coworkers absolutely stinks.
I think that new policy just stinks—it makes everything harder.

Synset Examples

this sucks!
this blows!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suck - to be bad or objectionable (slang, informal)
  • Antonym: rock - to be excellent (slang, informal)

3. VERB

/ stɪŋk / (AmE), / stɪŋk / (BrE)
to have a strong and unpleasant smell
have a bad smell

Examples

If you leave the trash outside, it will soon stink in the summer heat.
Her shoes stank after she wore them in the rain all day.
The locker room stinks of sweat and old gym clothes.

Synset Examples

The building reeks of smoke

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reek - to smell strongly and unpleasantly
  • Synonym: smell - to emit a scent, especially a distinctive one
  • Antonym: fragrant - having a pleasant smell

Etymology

Old English *stincan* (emit a smell), from Proto-Germanic *stinkaną*.
