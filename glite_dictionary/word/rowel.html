rowel

1. NOUN

/ ˈraʊ.əl / (AmE), / ˈraʊ.əl / (BrE)
a small spiked wheel at the end of a spur, used to urge a horse forward
spiked wheel on a spur

Examples

The cowboy touched his rowel to the horse's side, prompting it to move faster.
She carefully checked the rowel on her spur to make sure it wasn’t too sharp.
Modern riding spurs have a rowel that rotates smoothly to prevent harming the animal.

Etymology

From Old French roel (small wheel), from Latin rotella, diminutive of rota (wheel).
