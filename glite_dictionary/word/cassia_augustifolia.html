Cassia augustifolia

1. PROPN

/ ˌkæsiə ɔːˌgʌstəˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˌkæsɪə ɔːˌgʌstɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
an erect shrub with tawny yellow flowers, whose dried leaves are used medicinally as a cathartic; also known as senna
plant used medicinally; senna

Examples

Cassia augustifolia is often used in herbal medicine as a natural laxative.
The dried leaves of Cassia augustifolia are sold in health food stores worldwide.
Researchers studied the effects of extracts from Cassia augustifolia on digestive health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: senna - a plant whose leaves are used as a laxative, especially Senna alexandrina

Etymology

From Latin; 'Cassia' is a genus of tropical plants, 'augustifolia' means 'narrow-leaved' in Latin.
