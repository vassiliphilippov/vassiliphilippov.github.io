Euonymus radicans vegetus

1. NOUN

/ juˈɑnɪməs ˈrædɪˌkænz vəˈdʒiːtəs / (AmE), / juˈɒnɪməs ˈrædɪˌkænz vɪˈdʒiːtəs / (BrE)
A broad, bushy, twining shrub from Asia, noted for its pinkish fruit, with many subspecies or varieties.
an Asiatic twining shrub with pinkish fruit

Examples

The Euonymus radicans vegetus is cultivated in many gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
Botanists are studying the different varieties within the Euonymus radicans vegetus group.
Pinkish fruit appear in clusters on the Euonymus radicans vegetus during the autumn season.

Etymology

Genus name 'Euonymus' from Greek *euōnymos*, meaning 'of good name'; species name from Latin.
