to debar

1. VERB

/ dɪˈbɑr / (AmE), / dɪˈbɑː / (BrE)
to officially exclude someone from entering a place, taking part in an activity, or holding a position, especially for a period of time
officially exclude temporarily

Examples

The school decided to debar the student for repeated violations of its code of conduct.
Employees who cheat during the examination will be debarred from future promotions.
The committee has the authority to debar members found guilty of misconduct.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exclude - to prevent someone from entering a place or taking part in something
  • Antonym: to admit - to allow someone to enter a place or participate

Etymology

From Old French 'desbarer', from 'des-' (dis-) + 'barre' (bar).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈbɑr / (AmE), / dɪˈbɑː / (BrE)
to officially prevent someone from doing something or from entering a place
officially prevent or exclude

Examples

The committee decided to debar the company from future contracts due to misconduct.
Individuals under the age of eighteen are debarred from entering the club.
Her criminal record could debar her from holding public office.

3. VERB

/ dɪˈbɑr / (AmE), / dɪˈbɑː / (BrE)
to officially prevent someone from entering a place, taking part in an activity, or joining an organization
to officially prevent entry or participation

Examples

The rules may debar you from future competitions if you are found cheating.
Some countries debar convicted criminals from voting in elections.
The committee decided to debar him from attending the conference due to his misconduct.

Synset Examples

He was barred from membership in the club

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bar - to prohibit or prevent someone from entering or participating
  • Antonym: to admit - to allow someone to enter or take part

Etymology

From Old French 'debarer', from 'des-' (from Latin 'dis-') + 'barre' (bar).
