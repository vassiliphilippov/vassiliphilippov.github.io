clapboard

1. VERB

/ ˈklæbˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈklæb.bɔːd / (BrE)
to cover a building with long, thin, overlapping wooden boards
to cover with overlapping wooden boards

Examples

The builders will clapboard the exterior of the house next week.
They clapboarded the cottage to protect it from harsh weather.
We plan to clapboard our new shed for a traditional look.

2. NOUN

/ ˈklæpˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈklæpˌbɔːd / (BrE)
A long, thin board with one edge thicker than the other, used as siding on buildings by overlapping each board over the one below.
overlapping board for building siding

Examples

The old house was covered in white clapboard siding.
Carpenters replaced several rotting clapboards on the exterior wall.
Many historic American homes feature painted wooden clapboard exteriors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weatherboard - a board used for weatherproof siding, especially in British English

Etymology

From Middle English *clapbord*, possibly related to Dutch *klaphout* (splitting wood).
