urn

1. NOUN

/ ɝn / (AmE), / ɜːn / (BrE)
A large decorative vase, typically with a pedestal or feet, used for ornamental or ceremonial purposes.
large decorative vase with pedestal or feet

Examples

The museum displayed an ancient Greek urn in the center of the hall.
She placed a bouquet of flowers in the marble urn on the terrace.
The ornate urn stood atop a pedestal in the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vase - an open container used for holding flowers or for decoration

Etymology

From Latin 'urna' (vessel, jar, urn).

2. NOUN

/ ɝːn / (AmE), / ɜːn / (BrE)
a large metal container with a tap, used for making and serving coffee or tea, especially in large quantities
large pot for making coffee or tea

Examples

The hotel staff filled the urn with fresh coffee for the breakfast buffet.
She poured hot tea from the urn into waiting cups during the conference break.
We rented an urn to provide enough hot water for all the guests.

Etymology

From Latin urna (a jar, vessel, urn).
