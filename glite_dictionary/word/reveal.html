to reveal

1. VERB

/ rɪˈvil / (AmE), / rɪˈviːl / (BrE)
to make something known or allow it to be seen that was previously hidden or unknown
make something visible or known

Examples

The investigation will reveal the truth behind the mysterious disappearance.
His smile revealed his excitement at the news.
The artist used bright colors to reveal the beauty of the landscape.

Synset Examples

Summer brings out bright clothes
He brings out the best in her

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep something hidden; not make it known
  • Synonym: to disclose - to make secret or new information known

Etymology

From Latin revelare 'unveil, uncover'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rɪˈvil / (AmE), / rɪˈviːl / (BrE)
to make information known to the public that was previously secret or known only to a few people
make known something secret

Examples

The documents reveal the true extent of the company's financial problems.
He refused to reveal the source of his information to the reporters.
Her smile revealed how happy she truly was.

Synset Examples

The auction house would not disclose the price at which the van Gogh had sold
The actress won't reveal how old she is
bring out the truth
he broke the news to her
unwrap the evidence in the murder case
The newspaper uncovered the President's illegal dealings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disclose - to make secret or new information known
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep something secret or hidden

Etymology

From Latin 'revelare' (to uncover), from 're-' (again) + 'velare' (to cover).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00935783-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ rɪˈvil / (AmE), / rɪˈviːl / (BrE)
(of a deity or higher power) to make known or disclose information, often through divine or prophetic means.
(of deity) make known through prophets

Examples

In many traditions, it is believed that God may reveal hidden truths to prophets.
The ancient scripture describes how the divine will was revealed through visions.
She hoped the oracle would reveal the meaning of her dreams.

Synset Examples

God rarely reveals his plans for mankind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disclose - to make something known; to reveal
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep something secret; to hide

Etymology

From Latin 'revelare' (to unveil, uncover).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
