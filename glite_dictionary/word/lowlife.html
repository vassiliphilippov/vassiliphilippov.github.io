lowlife

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊˌlaɪf / (AmE), / ˈləʊˌlaɪf / (BrE)
a person considered to be morally reprehensible, disreputable, or contemptible
despicable or contemptible person
informal

Examples

The police arrested several lowlifes in connection with the recent thefts.
She refused to associate with anyone she considered a lowlife.
In crime novels, the detective often questions the lowlife characters in shady bars.

Synset Examples

only a rotter would do that

throw the bum out
you cowardly little pukes!
the British call a contemptible person a ‘git’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scoundrel - a dishonest or unscrupulous person
  • Synonym: villain - a wicked or evil person
  • Antonym: gentleman - a man of good, courteous, or noble character

Etymology

Formed from 'low' (of inferior status) + 'life' (person), originally in American English (early 20th century).

2. NOUN

/ ˈloʊˌlaɪf / (AmE), / ˈləʊˌlaɪf / (BrE)
a person who is considered morally unacceptable or engages in criminal or disreputable behavior
morally reprehensible or criminal person

Examples

The police arrested the lowlife who had been breaking into cars in the neighborhood.
She refused to associate with any lowlife involved in illegal activities.
Many consider him a lowlife because of the way he cheats and manipulates others.

Synset Examples

She warned him about that lowlife who steals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criminal - person who has committed a crime
  • Synonym: scoundrel - dishonest or unscrupulous person
  • Antonym: upstanding - morally upright and respectable person

Etymology

Formed from 'low' (of little social status) + 'life' (person); American English, early 20th century.
