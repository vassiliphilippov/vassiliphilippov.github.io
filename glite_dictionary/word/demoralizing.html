demoralizing

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈmɔrəˌlaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒr.ə.laɪ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing someone to lose confidence, hope, or morale.
causing loss of morale or confidence

Examples

The team's third straight loss was particularly demoralizing for the players.
Facing constant rejection can be incredibly demoralizing when looking for a job.
It was demoralizing to see all their hard work go unrecognized.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disheartening - causing loss of hope or confidence; discouraging
  • Antonym: encouraging - giving someone support or confidence; supportive

Etymology

From verb 'demoralize', from French 'démoraliser', based on Latin 'moris' (morale).
