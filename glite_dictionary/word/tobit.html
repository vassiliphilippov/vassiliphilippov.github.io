Tobit

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊbɪt / (AmE), / ˈtəʊbɪt / (BrE)
An Apocryphal book of the Bible, considered a popular novel for several centuries, telling the story of Tobit and his family.
book of the Apocrypha; biblical story

Examples

The story of Tobit is often cited as an example of faith and perseverance in biblical literature.
Many biblical scholars analyze the literary themes found in Tobit.
She referenced the book of Tobit in her research on religious texts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Book of Judith - another Apocryphal biblical book often grouped with Tobit

Etymology

From Latin *Tobias*, from Hebrew *Toviyyah* meaning 'the Lord is my good'.
