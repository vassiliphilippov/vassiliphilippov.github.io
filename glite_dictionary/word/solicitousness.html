solicitousness

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɪs.ɪ.təs.nəs / (AmE), / səˈlɪs.ɪ.təs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of showing anxious concern or care for someone or something
anxious concern or care

Examples

Her solicitousness for her elderly neighbor was appreciated by everyone in the community.
He noticed her solicitousness when she constantly checked if everyone was comfortable.
The hostess’s solicitousness made the guests feel especially welcome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concern - a feeling of worry about something important

Etymology

From solicitous + -ness, ultimately from Latin 'sollicitus' (anxious, concerned).
