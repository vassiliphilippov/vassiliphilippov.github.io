Leycesteria formosa

1. NOUN

/ leɪˈsɛstəriə fɔrˈmoʊsə / (AmE), / lɪˈsɛstiəriə fɔːˈməʊsə / (BrE)
A perennial shrub native to the Himalayas, with drooping spikes of purplish flowers and edible dark berries; also called Himalayan honeysuckle.
shrub with drooping purplish flowers; Himalayan honeysuckle

Examples

The gardener planted a row of Leycesteria formosa along the fence for its attractive flowers.
Leycesteria formosa is often grown in ornamental gardens for its showy blooms.
Birds are attracted to the berries of Leycesteria formosa in late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Himalayan honeysuckle - common name for Leycesteria formosa

Etymology

From the genus name Leycesteria, named after William Leycester, a British colonial botanist, and Latin formosa meaning 'beautiful'.
