mesolithic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɛzəˈlɪθɪk, ˌmɛsəˈlɪθɪk / (AmE), / ˌmiːzəˈlɪθɪk, ˌmɛsəˈlɪθɪk / (BrE)
relating to the Middle Stone Age, the period between the Paleolithic and Neolithic eras.
of the Middle Stone Age

Examples

Archaeologists have discovered tools from the Mesolithic era in this region.
The mesolithic period marked a transition in human lifestyles from hunting and gathering to early farming.
There were significant technological advancements during the Mesolithic age compared to the Paleolithic.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: paleolithic - relating to the early Stone Age
  • Degree Higher: neolithic - relating to the late Stone Age, following the Mesolithic

Etymology

From Greek mesos (middle) + lithos (stone), referring to the middle part of the Stone Age.
