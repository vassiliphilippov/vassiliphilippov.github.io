extropy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛkstroʊpi / (AmE), / ˈɛkstrəʊpi / (BrE)
The belief or prediction that human intelligence and technology will allow life to expand and evolve in an orderly and progressive way throughout the universe.
belief in technological and orderly universal expansion of life

Examples

Many futurists support extropy as an optimistic vision for humanity's technological future.
Her essay discussed how extropy could shape ethical thinking in a universe governed by intelligence.
Critics argue that extropy may overestimate both technology's potential and humanity's foresight.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: entropy - a measure of disorder or randomness, the opposite tendency

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'entropy', from Latin 'ex-' (out of) and Greek 'tropos' (turning, change).
