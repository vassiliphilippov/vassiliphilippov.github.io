folk

1. NOUN

/ foʊk / (AmE), / fəʊk / (BrE)
traditional music, often passed down orally, that expresses the culture and life of a community or group of people
traditional community music

Examples

The festival featured live folk from around the world.
She enjoys listening to Irish folk in the evenings.
Many folk songs tell stories of hardship and hope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folk music - traditional music characteristic of a community or people
  • Antonym: pop - modern popular music, especially with mass appeal

Etymology

From Old English folc (people, nation).

2. NOUN

/ foʊk / (AmE), / fəʊk / (BrE)
People who share a common ancestry, especially members of a family or ethnic group.
people of common ancestry or family

Examples

Many of my folk still live in the old country.
She always enjoys learning about her Irish folk and their traditions.
He traced his folk back to early settlers in Virginia.

Synset Examples

his family has lived in Massachusetts since the Mayflower

Related Terms

  • Synonym: family - a group consisting of parents and children or ancestors and descendants

Etymology

From Old English folc (people, tribe); related to German Volk.

3. NOUN

/ foʊk / (AmE), / fəʊk / (BrE)
people in general, especially those of a particular group, country, or community; common people
people in general; common people

Examples

The folk of this village have celebrated the harvest festival for centuries.
She enjoys traveling and meeting new folk wherever she goes.
Country folk often have strong traditions and close-knit communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: people - human beings in general or as a group

Etymology

4. NOUN

/ foʊk / (AmE), / fəʊk / (BrE)
a group of people sharing the same culture, especially one forming a traditional or social subdivision, sometimes with reference to preliterate societies
a cultural or social group of people

Examples

Anthropologists have studied the customs of this mountain folk for decades.
Storytellers help preserve the traditions of indigenous folk around the world.
The folk of the village gathered to celebrate the harvest festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: people - human beings considered as a group or community
  • Synonym: tribe - a social group consisting of people of the same race or having the same language, culture, and history

Etymology

From Old English 'folc' (people, nation), related to German 'Volk'.
