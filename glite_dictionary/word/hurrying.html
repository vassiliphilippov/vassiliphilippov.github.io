hurrying

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɜːriɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhʌriɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of moving or acting quickly, often because of urgency
the act of moving quickly

Examples

She was hurrying to catch the last train home.
Their hurrying down the hallway caused quite a commotion.
After a morning of hurrying, he finally sat down to rest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - a sudden quick movement towards something
  • Antonym: delay - a period of waiting or being slow

2. ADJ

/ ˈhɜriɪŋ, ˈhʌriɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhʌriɪŋ, ˈhɜːriɪŋ / (BrE)
moving quickly or with great haste
moving quickly; in a rush

Examples

She walked past the group of hurrying commuters, barely noticing the rush.
A hurrying waiter nearly dropped his tray in his haste to serve all the tables.
The hurrying footsteps in the hallway signaled that the meeting was about to start.
