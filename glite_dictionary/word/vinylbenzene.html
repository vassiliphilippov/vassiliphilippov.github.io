vinylbenzene

1. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪnəlˈbɛnziːn / (AmE), / ˌvaɪnəlˈbɛnziːn / (BrE)
a colorless oily liquid used chiefly as the monomer for polystyrene and other plastics; also known as styrene
monomer used in making polystyrene

Examples

Vinylbenzene is used industrially to produce polystyrene plastics and resins.
Exposure to vinylbenzene should be minimized due to potential health risks.
Researchers studied how vinylbenzene interacts with other chemicals during polymerization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: styrene - another name for vinylbenzene

Etymology

From 'vinyl' (denoting the presence of an ethenyl group) and 'benzene' (the aromatic ring).
