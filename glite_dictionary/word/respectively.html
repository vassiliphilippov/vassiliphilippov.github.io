respectively

1. ADV

/ rɪˈspɛktɪvli / (AmE), / rɪˈspɛktɪvli / (BrE)
in the order given; relating or referring to each in the sequence mentioned
in the order given

Examples

The gold, silver, and bronze medals were awarded to Anna, Brian, and Cara, respectively.
The children are aged five, seven, and ten, respectively.
The committee members voted yes, no, and abstained, respectively.

Synset Examples

the brothers were called Felix and Max, respectively

Etymology

From Latin 'respectivus' (having regard for).
