reliable

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈlaɪ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / rɪˈlaɪ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
Consistently good in quality or performance; able to be trusted.
able to be trusted; dependable

Examples

You need a reliable car if you plan to drive across the country.
She is a reliable babysitter who always arrives on time.
The company is known for its reliable customer service.

Synset Examples

a reliable source of information
a dependable worker

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dependable - able to be relied on; trustworthy
  • Antonym: unreliable - not able to be relied on; untrustworthy

Etymology

From rely + -able, patterned after liable.

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈlaɪ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / rɪˈlaɪ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
Consistently good in quality or performance; able to be trusted or believed because it is true or accurate.
trustworthy; consistently dependable or accurate

Examples

We need reliable information before making any major decisions.
She is a reliable source of advice whenever I need it.
The weather forecast proved to be reliable for our weekend plans.

Synset Examples

an authentic account by an eyewitness
reliable information

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trustworthy - able to be relied on as honest or truthful
  • Antonym: unreliable - not able to be trusted or depended on

Etymology

From rely + -able, patterned after French 'reliable' meaning 'that may be relied upon'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ rɪˈlaɪ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / rɪˈlaɪ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
Consistently good in quality or performance; able to be trusted or depended on.
able to be trusted or depended on

Examples

Jess is a reliable friend who always keeps her promises.
We need a reliable source of information for our research project.
His car is very reliable and rarely breaks down.

Synset Examples

a dependable worker
an honest working stiff
a reliable source of information
he was true to his word
I would be true for there are those who trust me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dependable - capable of being relied on; trustworthy
  • Antonym: unreliable - not able to be relied on or trusted

Etymology

From rely + -able, modeled after the earlier French fiable (from fier, 'to trust').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02474747-s
Synset POS: s
