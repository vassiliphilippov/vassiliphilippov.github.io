Attalea

1. NOUN

/ əˈteɪliə / (AmE), / əˈteɪliə / (BrE)
A genus of unarmed feather palms native to Central and northern South America.
genus of South American feather palms

Examples

The towering Attalea palms are a prominent feature of the Amazonian landscape.
Certain species within the Attalea genus are prized for their edible seeds.
Researchers studied the ecological role of Attalea in supporting local wildlife diversity.

Etymology

From New Latin Attalea, the scientific name for the genus.

2. NOUN

/ əˈteɪliə / (AmE), / əˈteɪliə / (BrE)
A genus of unarmed feather palms native to central and northern South America.
genus of South American feather palms

Examples

The Attalea genus includes several economically important palm species in South America.
Unlike many other palms, the Attalea species lack armature such as spines or thorns.
Researchers discovered a new species belonging to the Attalea genus during their expedition.

Etymology

Named after Charles VI of Naples and Sicily (Carlo Attilio); applied in botanical Latin.
