ascertained

1. ADJ

/ ˌæs.ɚˈteɪnd / (AmE), / ˌæs.əˈteɪnd / (BrE)
discovered or determined by careful observation, especially in scientific or formal contexts.
determined by careful observation

Examples

The total cost was ascertained after a thorough review of all expenses.
Researchers have ascertained the species' population trends using satellite tracking data.
The ascertained facts did not support the initial theory.

Synset Examples

variation in the ascertained flux depends on a number of factors
the discovered behavior norms
discovered differences in achievement
no explanation for the observed phenomena

Related Terms

  • Synonym: determined - conclusively established or decided
  • Synonym: discovered - found out or revealed for the first time

Etymology

From the verb 'ascertain', which comes from Old French 'acertener', based on 'certain'.
