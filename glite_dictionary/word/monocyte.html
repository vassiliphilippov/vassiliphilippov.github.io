monocyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.nəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.ə.saɪt / (BrE)
a large white blood cell with a single nucleus that helps defend the body by ingesting bacteria and other foreign substances
type of white blood cell
medical

Examples

A monocyte can transform into a macrophage to engulf and destroy pathogens.
Doctors measured the patient's monocyte levels to assess her immune response.
The blood test revealed an increase in monocyte count after the infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leukocyte - a general term for a white blood cell
  • Degree Lower: lymphocyte - a type of white blood cell involved in immune response

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) and 'kytos' (cell).
