Nitrosomonas

1. NOUN

/ ˌnaɪtroʊsəˈmoʊnəs / (AmE), / ˌnɪtrəʊsəˈməʊnəs / (BrE)
A genus of ellipsoidal soil bacteria that oxidize ammonia into nitrites as part of the nitrogen cycle.
genus of soil bacteria
medical

Examples

Nitrosomonas play an essential role in the nitrogen cycle by converting ammonia to nitrites in soil.
Researchers studied the growth rate of Nitrosomonas in different agricultural environments.
The presence of Nitrosomonas can greatly influence soil fertility and plant health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nitrobacter - a genus of bacteria that oxidizes nitrites to nitrates

Etymology

From Greek 'nitron' (nitre) + 'soma' (body), referring to its role in nitrogen processes.

2. NOUN

/ ˌnaɪtrəˈsoʊmənəs / (AmE), / ˌnɪtrəʊˈsəʊmənəs / (BrE)
A genus of ellipsoidal soil bacteria that oxidize ammonia into nitrites, important in the nitrogen cycle.
genus of soil bacteria involved in nitrification
medical

Examples

Nitrosomonas play a vital role in converting ammonia to nitrite in soil ecosystems.
Scientists studied the abundance of Nitrosomonas in agricultural fields to improve fertilizer efficiency.
Many wastewater treatment plants rely on Nitrosomonas to help reduce harmful ammonia levels.

Etymology

From Latin *nitrum* (natron, soda) + Greek *sōma* (body), referring to their chemical activity.
