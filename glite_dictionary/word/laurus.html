Laurus

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔːrəs / (AmE), / ˈlɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of small evergreen trees or shrubs with aromatic leaves, including the bay tree.
genus of evergreen aromatic trees/shrubs

Examples

The leaves of Laurus nobilis are commonly used as bay leaves in cooking.
Some species in the Laurus genus are valued for their fragrant foliage.
Botanists studied the distribution of Laurus trees in Mediterranean climates.

Etymology

From Latin *laurus* (bay tree, laurel).
