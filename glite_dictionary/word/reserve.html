reserve

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɝv / (AmE), / rɪˈzɜːv / (BrE)
an athlete who is not a regular starter but is available to play if needed, especially as a substitute for another player
substitute player in sports

Examples

The coach decided to put a reserve on the field after the main striker was injured.
She spent most of the season as a reserve but finally got a chance to start.
Every team needs a strong reserve in case of unexpected injuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitute - a player who replaces another during a game

Etymology

From Old French 'reserver', from Latin 'reservare' (to keep back).

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɝːv / (AmE), / rɪˈzɜːv / (BrE)
(Medicine) The potential capacity of an organism or organ to respond to stress or increased demand in order to maintain vital functions.
medical: capacity to respond to stress
medical

Examples

The patient's cardiac reserve allowed her to withstand the surgery with few complications.
Doctors assess cognitive reserve to predict the onset of symptoms in neurodegenerative diseases.
Aging can reduce the body's overall physiological reserve, making recovery from illness more challenging.

Etymology

From Latin reservare (to keep back, save).
