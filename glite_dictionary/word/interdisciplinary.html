interdisciplinary

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪntərˈdɪsəˌplɪˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈdɪsɪplɪnəri / (BrE)
involving two or more academic, professional, or artistic disciplines or fields of study
involving multiple fields of study

Examples

The university encourages interdisciplinary research projects that involve both scientists and artists.
She attended an interdisciplinary conference about sustainability and urban development.
The new curriculum promotes interdisciplinary studies across the sciences and humanities.

Synset Examples

interdisciplinary studies
an interdisciplinary conference

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multidisciplinary - involving several different fields of study at once
  • Synonym: transdisciplinary - integrating knowledge from different disciplines to create a unity of intellectual frameworks

Etymology

From inter- (between) + discipline, modeled after German interdisziplinär.
