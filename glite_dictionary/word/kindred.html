kindred

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɪndrəd / (AmE), / ˈkɪndrɪd / (BrE)
(of people or groups) related by blood or marriage; of the same family or ancestry.
related by blood or marriage

Examples

The museum displays artifacts from several kindred tribes that once lived in the valley.
The families, though kindred, had very different traditions and customs.
These kindred branches of the royal house often allied through marriage.

Synset Examples

kindred clans

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unrelated - not connected by blood or marriage

Etymology

From Old English cynrǣden (relationship), from cyn (kin, family).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɪndrəd / (AmE), / ˈkɪndrɪd / (BrE)
a group of people related by blood or marriage; relatives or kin
group of relatives; kin

Examples

After the wedding, the two families discovered they were distant kindred.
He sought comfort among his kindred during times of trouble.
Researching her ancestry, she felt a deep connection to her forgotten kindred.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kin - one's family and relations
  • Synonym: family - people related by blood or marriage

Etymology

From Old English 'gecyndra' (of the same nature or kind), related to 'kin'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07986142-n

3. ADJ

/ ˈkɪndrəd / (AmE), / ˈkɪndrɪd / (BrE)
similar in character or quality; sharing the same nature or origin
similar in character; related by nature

Examples

They found themselves to be kindred spirits, sharing many values and beliefs.
There was a kindred feeling between the musicians as they played together.
Her passion for literature made her feel kindred to other avid readers.

Synset Examples

a feeling akin to terror
kindred souls

Related Terms

  • Synonym: akin - of similar character; related by nature
  • Antonym: unrelated - not connected by character or quality

Etymology

From Old English cynþry, related to 'kin' (family or race) and 'red' (condition, kind).
