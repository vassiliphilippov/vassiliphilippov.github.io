leoncita

1. NOUN

/ leɪˈɒnsɪtə / (AmE), / leɪˈɒnsɪtə / (BrE)
A small South American marmoset with silky fur and a long nonprehensile tail.
South American marmoset species

Examples

The leoncita is known for its silky fur and playful behavior in the rainforest.
Researchers observed a family of leoncita feeding on insects and fruit.
Unlike larger monkeys, the leoncita uses its long tail for balance rather than for grasping branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marmoset - small New World monkey of the family Callitrichidae

Etymology

From Spanish 'leoncita', diminutive of 'león' (lion), referring to its lion-like mane.
