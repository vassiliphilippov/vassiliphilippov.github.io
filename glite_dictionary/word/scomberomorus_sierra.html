Scomberomorus sierra

1. NOUN

/ skoʊmˌbɛrəˈmɔrəs siˈɛrə / (AmE), / skɒmˌbɛrəˈmɔːrəs sɪˈɛrə / (BrE)
A species of Spanish mackerel native to the Pacific coast of western North America.
a species of Spanish mackerel

Examples

The Scomberomorus sierra is often caught by local fishermen along the Pacific coast of Mexico.
Many recipes in Baja California use freshly grilled Scomberomorus sierra as the main ingredient.
Marine biologists studied the migration patterns of Scomberomorus sierra for conservation efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spanish mackerel - species of fish closely related to Scomberomorus sierra

Etymology

From New Latin; Scomberomorus from Greek skombros (mackerel) + moros (form), sierra from Spanish (saw, for the fish's appearance).
