assessee

1. NOUN

/ əˌsɛˈsi / (AmE), / əˈsɛːsi / (BrE)
a person or entity whose income, property, or assets are assessed, especially for tax purposes
person or entity being assessed (tax)
legal

Examples

The government sent a notice to the assessee regarding unpaid taxes.
Every assessee must file an income tax return by the due date.
If an assessee disagrees with the valuation, they may appeal the decision.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: assessor - a person who evaluates or estimates the nature, ability, or quality of something, especially for tax purposes

Etymology

From English 'assess' + '-ee' (person who is the object of an action).
