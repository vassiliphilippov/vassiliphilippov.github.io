catachrestical

1. ADJ

/ ˌkætəkˈrɛstɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈkrɛstɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to or characterized by catachresis; involving the improper or strained use of words.
relating to catachresis (misuse of words)

Examples

The critic described the novelist's frequent use of catachrestical metaphors as distracting rather than innovative.
Her explanation was so catachrestical that it left the audience confused about her actual meaning.
Scholars debated whether the poem's catachrestical expressions enriched or undermined its impact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catachrestic - relating to or characterized by catachresis
  • Antonym: literal - using words in their usual or most basic sense

Etymology

Formed from 'catachresis' (from Greek 'katakhrēsis', misuse) + '-ical'.
