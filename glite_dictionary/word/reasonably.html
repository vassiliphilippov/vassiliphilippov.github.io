reasonably

1. ADV

/ ˈriːzənəbli / (AmE), / ˈriːzənəbli / (BrE)
in a sensible or rational manner; with good judgment or intelligence
in a sensible or rational way

Examples

He handled the emergency reasonably and kept everyone calm.
If you explain your concerns reasonably, people are more likely to listen.
The committee members discussed the proposal reasonably before reaching a decision.

Synset Examples

he acted sensibly in the crisis
speak more sanely about these affairs
acted quite reasonably

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensibly - in a way that shows good sense or judgment
  • Antonym: irrationally - in an unreasonable or illogical manner

Etymology

From 'reasonable' + -ly; ultimately from Old French 'raisonnable'.

2. ADV

/ ˈriːzənəbli / (AmE), / ˈriːzənəbli / (BrE)
to a fair or moderate degree; fairly, moderately, or to some extent.
to a moderate or fair degree

Examples

The house is reasonably priced for the area.
The weather was reasonably good during our trip.
She managed to finish the project reasonably quickly.

Synset Examples

pretty big
pretty bad
jolly decent of him
the shoes are priced reasonably
he is fairly clever with computers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fairly - to some extent, moderately
  • Degree Lower: slightly - to a small extent
  • Degree Higher: very - to a great extent or degree

Etymology

From 'reasonable' + '-ly', based on Latin 'ratio' (reason).

Source: WordNet
