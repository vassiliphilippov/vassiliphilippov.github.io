Calyptridium umbellatum

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlɪptrɪdiəm ʌmˈbɛlətəm / (AmE), / kəˌlɪptrɪdɪəm ʌmˈbɛlətəm / (BrE)
A plant species in western North America with pink clusters of densely packed flowers on prostrate stems, resembling upturned pads of cats' feet, typically found in coniferous forests.
a western North American flowering plant species

Examples

Calyptridium umbellatum is found in high-altitude coniferous forests throughout western North America.
The fleshy pink flowers of Calyptridium umbellatum are often mistaken for the paws of a small animal.
Botanists conducted a study on the ecological role of Calyptridium umbellatum in its native habitats.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Calyptridium from Greek 'kalyptos' (covered), species name from Latin 'umbellatus' (having umbels).
