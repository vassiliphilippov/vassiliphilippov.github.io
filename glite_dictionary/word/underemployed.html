underemployed

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌndərɪmˈplɔɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəɪmˈplɔɪd / (BrE)
working in a job that does not use your skills or abilities fully, or working fewer hours than you want or need
not fully employed or underused in a job

Examples

Many recent graduates are underemployed in jobs that do not require a college degree.
She felt underemployed because her administrative position did not allow her to use her engineering training.
Due to the economic downturn, several skilled workers have become underemployed or forced into part-time roles.

Synset Examples

migrants are likely to be poor and underemployed
able people are kept underemployed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unemployed - without a job; not working
  • Synonym: overqualified - having more skills or education than a job requires

Etymology

From prefix 'under-' (not enough) + 'employed' (having a job).
