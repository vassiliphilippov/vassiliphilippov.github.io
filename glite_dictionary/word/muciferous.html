muciferous

1. ADJ

/ mjuˈsɪfərəs / (AmE), / mjuˈsɪfərəs / (BrE)
containing or producing mucus
containing or secreting mucus
medical

Examples

Certain glands in the body are described as muciferous because they secrete mucus.
The muciferous lining helps protect the respiratory tract from dust and pathogens.
Pathologists identified several muciferous structures within the tissue sample.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mucous - producing or secreting mucus

Etymology

From Latin 'mucus' (slime, mucus) + '-ferous' (bearing, producing).
