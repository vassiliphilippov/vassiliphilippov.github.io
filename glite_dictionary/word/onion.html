onion

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌn.jən / (AmE), / ˈʌn.jən / (BrE)
A round vegetable with thin, papery skin and many layers, having a strong flavor and aroma, used in cooking.
a strong-flavored, layered vegetable

Examples

Chop the onion finely before adding it to the soup.
The smell of fried onion filled the kitchen.
She planted onions in her backyard garden this spring.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: garlic - a plant with a strong taste and smell, used for flavouring food
  • Degree Lower: shallot - a mild-flavored onion-like vegetable

Etymology

From Latin 'unio', meaning a kind of onion.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌn.jən / (AmE), / ˈʌn.jən / (BrE)
A round vegetable with a white, yellow, or red skin and many layers, the bulb of the onion plant, used in cooking for its strong flavor and aroma.
edible bulb vegetable

Examples

She sliced the onion thinly before adding it to the salad.
The smell of fried onion filled the kitchen.
He cried while chopping the onion for the soup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garlic - a plant with a strong-smelling bulb used as a flavoring

Etymology

From Old French 'oignon', from Latin 'unionem'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈʌnjən / (AmE), / ˈʌnjən / (BrE)
A round edible bulb with pungent, flavorful layers, grown as a vegetable and used in cooking worldwide.
edible bulb used as a vegetable

Examples

She diced an onion before adding it to the soup.
The sharp smell of onion made his eyes water.
Red onion is often used in salads for its mild flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shallot - a type of small onion used in cooking
  • Synonym: garlic - a strongly flavored bulbous plant related to the onion

Etymology

From Old French oignon, from Latin unio(n-), meaning ‘single (bulb)’.
