jotting

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒtɪŋ / (BrE)
a brief note written quickly by hand, often as a reminder or record
quick handwritten note

Examples

She filled her notebook with quick jottings during the meeting.
I found an old jotting in the margin of the book, reminding me to call John.
The detective made a jotting of the license plate number before it disappeared.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: note - a short piece of writing to help remember something

Etymology

From the verb 'jot', of uncertain origin, possibly from Middle English 'jotten' or associated with 'jot' (small amount).
