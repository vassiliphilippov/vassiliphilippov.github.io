vapourousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪpɚəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈveɪpərəsnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being cloudy or hazy due to the presence of mist, haze, or vapor.
cloudiness from mist or vapor

Examples

The vapourousness of the early morning air made it difficult to see the lake clearly.
She admired the vapourousness of the valley as the sun rose and burned away the mist.
The artist captured the vapourousness of the atmosphere in his landscape painting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloudiness - the quality of being cloudy
  • Antonym: clearness - the quality of being clear or transparent

Etymology

From 'vapour' + '-ous' + '-ness'; 'vapour' from Latin 'vapor' (steam, exhalation).
