to devitalise

1. VERB

/ dɪˈvaɪtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diːˈvaɪtəlaɪz / (BrE)
To make something or someone less lively or energetic; to sap of vitality or life.
make less lively or energetic

Examples

The constant criticism began to devitalise her enthusiasm for the project.
Years of neglect had devitalised the old building, leaving it gloomy and unwelcoming.
Harsh economic policies can quickly devitalise a thriving community.

Synset Examples

The recession devitalized the economy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to devitalize - American English spelling of devitalise
  • Synonym: to enervate - to weaken or drain energy from
  • Antonym: to invigorate - to give strength or energy to

Etymology

From de- (removal) + vitalise, ultimately from Latin 'vita' (life).
