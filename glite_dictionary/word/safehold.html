safehold

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪfˌhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈseɪfhəʊld / (BrE)
a place of safety or refuge; a stronghold offering protection from attack
a place of safety or refuge

Examples

The villagers retreated to their safehold in the mountains when the invaders approached.
The ancient castle was long regarded as an impenetrable safehold by the local people.
They spent the night hidden in a remote safehold, waiting for the danger to pass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stronghold - a place that has been fortified to protect it against attack
  • Synonym: refuge - a place providing shelter or protection from danger

Etymology

Formed from 'safe' + 'hold', inspired by the sense of a stronghold or place of safety.
