tittering

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɪtərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɪtərɪŋ / (BrE)
characterized by nervous or suppressed laughter; giggling quietly, often in a self-conscious or timid way.
nervous or suppressed laughter (adj.)

Examples

The tittering group of students tried to keep quiet during the important lecture.
She gave a tittering response, unable to hold back her nervous amusement.
A tittering audience filled the theater after the comedian's unexpected joke.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giggling - laughing lightly in a silly or nervous way

Etymology

From the verb 'titter' (to give a short, half-suppressed laugh), origin uncertain, possibly imitative.
