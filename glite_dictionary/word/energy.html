energy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛnɚdʒi / (AmE), / ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
a source of power that can be used to do work, such as electricity, fuel, or heat
source of usable power

Examples

Wind turbines are used to generate energy for homes and businesses.
The country is investing in renewable energy sources like solar and wind.
Many scientists are searching for cleaner forms of energy to combat climate change.

Synset Examples

the DOE is responsible for maintaining the energy policy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: power - ability to do work or produce energy

Etymology

From French 'énergie', from Latin 'energia', from Greek 'energeia' (activity, operation).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛnɚdʒi / (AmE), / ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
(Physics) The capacity of a physical system to do work, measured in units such as joules or ergs.
capacity to do work (physics)

Examples

The battery stores chemical energy that can power a flashlight.
During photosynthesis, plants convert light energy into chemical energy.
Thermal energy is transferred from the hot coffee to the surrounding air.

Synset Examples

energy can take a wide variety of forms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: power - the rate of doing work or transferring energy (physics)
  • Synonym: work - the energy transferred by a force (physics)

Etymology

From Greek 'energeia' (activity, operation) via French 'énergie' and Latin 'energia'.
