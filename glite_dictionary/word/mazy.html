mazy

1. ADJ

/ ˈmeɪ.zi / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.zi / (BrE)
Full of winding passages or intricate patterns; resembling a maze or labyrinth in complexity or form.
resembling a maze; intricate or winding

Examples

The hikers lost their way in the mazy trails of the old forest.
Her thoughts became mazy as she tried to solve the puzzle.
Rivers carved a mazy pattern across the valley floor.

Synset Examples

a labyrinthine network of tortuous footpaths

Related Terms

  • Synonym: labyrinthine - like a labyrinth; extremely intricate or complex

Etymology

From maze + -y, dating from late Middle English.
