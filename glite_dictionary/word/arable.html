arable

1. ADJ

/ ˈærəbəl / (AmE), / ˈærəbəl / (BrE)
(of land) suitable for growing crops.
suitable for growing crops

Examples

Farmers in the region rely on arable fields to produce enough food for their communities.
Only a small portion of the country consists of arable land suitable for agriculture.
After years of drought, the once arable soil had become dry and unproductive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fertile - land that is rich in nutrients and supports abundant plant growth

Etymology

From Latin arabilis, from arare (to plow).
