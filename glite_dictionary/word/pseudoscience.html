pseudoscience

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːdoʊˌsaɪəns / (AmE), / ˈsjuːdəʊˌsaɪəns / (BrE)
A system of theories, beliefs, or methods that claims to be scientific but is not based on valid scientific evidence or principles.
Claimed science lacking evidence

Examples

Many people mistakenly believe that astrology is a pseudoscience despite the lack of scientific evidence supporting it.
The book debunks several popular pseudoscience theories and explains why they don't meet scientific standards.
Homeopathy has often been criticized as a pseudoscience by medical professionals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: science - the systematic study of the structure and behavior of the physical and natural world
  • Synonym: quackery - dishonest practices and claims to have special knowledge and skill in some field, typically medicine

Etymology

From pseudo- (false) + science; pseudo- from Greek pseudo- (false).
