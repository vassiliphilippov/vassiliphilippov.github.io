aldotetrose

1. NOUN

/ ˌældoʊˈtɛtˌroʊs / (AmE), / ˌaldəʊˈtɛtrəʊz / (BrE)
A monosaccharide (simple sugar) containing four carbon atoms and an aldehyde group, such as threose or erythrose.
four-carbon aldehyde sugar

Examples

Threose is an example of an aldotetrose found in nature.
Scientists studied the structure of the aldotetrose to understand its chemical properties.
Both erythrose and threose belong to the class of aldotetrose sugars.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tetrose - a monosaccharide with four carbon atoms
  • Degree Higher: aldopentose - a five-carbon aldose (aldose with five carbon atoms)
  • Degree Higher: aldose - a monosaccharide containing an aldehyde group

Etymology

From 'aldo-' (referring to an aldehyde group) and 'tetrose' (a sugar with four carbon atoms).
