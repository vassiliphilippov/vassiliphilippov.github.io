coda

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ.də / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.də / (BrE)
the concluding passage of a piece of music, typically bringing it to an end after the main part has been completed
final section of a musical piece

Examples

The symphony ended with a powerful coda that left the audience breathless.
Each movement of the sonata features a distinct coda to conclude the theme.
The composer added a new coda to the overture for its anniversary performance.

Etymology

From Italian 'coda' (tail), from Latin 'cauda' (tail).
