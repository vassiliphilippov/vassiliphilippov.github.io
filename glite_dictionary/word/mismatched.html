mismatched

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɪsˈmætʃt / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈmætʃt / (BrE)
not matching or not suitably paired; not corresponding appropriately with something else
not matching or suitably paired

Examples

He wore mismatched socks to the interview, which made him feel self-conscious.
The designer intentionally created a room with mismatched furniture for a quirky look.
Their skills were mismatched, making teamwork difficult.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: matched - suitably matched or paired
  • Synonym: incompatible - not able to exist or work together

Etymology

From prefix 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'matched'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌmɪsˈmætʃt / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈmætʃt / (BrE)
(of a contest or opponents) not fairly matched in terms of strength, skill, or suitability; unevenly paired.
unevenly paired (contest/opponents)

Examples

The boxing match was clearly mismatched, with one fighter much stronger than the other.
It was a mismatched contest from the start, as the experienced team faced complete novices.
Some critics argued that the final was disappointingly mismatched, with victory inevitable.

Synset Examples

vaudeville … waged an uneven battle against the church

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uneven - not equal or level in quality or strength
  • Antonym: well-matched - evenly matched or equally suited opponents

Etymology

From 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'matched', past participle of 'match'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00896066-s
Synset POS: s
