Rheum australe

1. NOUN

/ ˈɹuːəm ɔːˈstreɪli / (AmE), / ˈrɪəm ɔːˈstreɪli / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to the Himalayas, valued for its medicinal rhizomes; also known as Himalayan rhubarb.
Himalayan medicinal herb
medical

Examples

Many traditional remedies in the region utilize Rheum australe for its healing properties.
Researchers are studying the chemical compounds found in Rheum australe.
The roots of Rheum australe are harvested in the Himalayas for medicinal use.

Etymology

From Latin 'rheum' (rhubarb) and 'australe' (southern).
