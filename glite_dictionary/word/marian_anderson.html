Marian Anderson

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɛriən ˈændɚsən / (AmE), / ˈmɛːrɪən ˈandəsən / (BrE)
(1902–1993) an American contralto and renowned singer known for her performances of spirituals and classical music, and a prominent figure in the civil rights movement.
American contralto and civil rights figure

Examples

Marian Anderson broke barriers as the first African American to perform at the Metropolitan Opera in New York.
The 1939 concert by Marian Anderson at the Lincoln Memorial became a landmark event in civil rights history.
Music students still study recordings of Marian Anderson for her rich, expressive vocal talent.
