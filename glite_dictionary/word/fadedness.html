fadedness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪdɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈfeɪdɪdnɪs / (BrE)
The state or quality of having lost some of its original color or intensity.
the state of being faded

Examples

The fadedness of the old photograph made the details hard to see.
She preferred the fadedness of vintage clothing to brand-new items.
Over time, the fadedness of the curtains became more noticeable in the sunlight.

Synset Examples

In my youth, I was obsessed with the fadedness of my jeans, which took some doing because I only ever wore original shrink-to-fit Levi's 501s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faded - having lost color or brightness
  • Antonym: brightness - the quality of being bright or vivid in color
