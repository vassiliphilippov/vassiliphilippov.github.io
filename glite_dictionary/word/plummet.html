plummet

1. NOUN

/ ˈplʌmət / (AmE), / ˈplʌmɪt / (BrE)
A small, heavy metal weight, especially one used at the end of a plumb line to determine vertical alignment.
metal bob on a plumb line

Examples

The carpenter attached a plummet to the end of the string to ensure perfect vertical alignment.
Surveyors often use a plummet when establishing true vertical lines on construction sites.
She held the plummet steady until it stopped swaying, confirming the wall's upright position.

Etymology

From Old French *plommet* (small lead), diminutive of *plom* (lead), from Latin *plumbum* (lead).

2. VERB

/ ˈplʌmət / (AmE), / ˈplʌmɪt / (BrE)
to fall or drop suddenly and steeply in amount, value, or position
fall or drop sharply

Examples

House prices continued to plummet after the financial crisis hit.
Her mood plummeted when she heard the disappointing news.
Shares in the company plummeted by more than 30% in a single day.

Synset Examples

The stock market plummeted

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to soar - to rise very quickly to a high level
  • Synonym: drop - to decrease or fall to a lower level

Etymology

From Middle English *plommet* (lead weight), from Old French *plommet* (little lead; plumb-bob), from Latin *plumbum* (lead).
