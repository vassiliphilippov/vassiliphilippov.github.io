pneumogastric

1. NOUN

/ ˌnuːmoʊˈɡæstrɪk / (AmE), / ˌnjuːməˈɡæstrɪk / (BrE)
the vagus nerve; a major nerve that supplies the pharynx, larynx, lungs, heart, esophagus, stomach, and most abdominal organs
the vagus nerve in anatomy
medical

Examples

The pneumogastric is crucial for regulating heart rate and digestion.
Damage to the pneumogastric can lead to difficulties in swallowing and breathing.
Stimulation of the pneumogastric has been explored as a treatment for certain medical conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vagus nerve - the tenth cranial nerve, supplying many organs; another name for pneumogastric

Etymology

From Greek pneuma (breath) + gastēr (stomach), referring to its distribution.
