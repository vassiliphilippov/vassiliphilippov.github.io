Gastropoda

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæstrəˈpoʊdə / (AmE), / ˌɡæstrəˈpəʊdə / (BrE)
a large class of molluscs that includes snails, slugs, and their relatives, characterized by a muscular foot and a head with tentacles
class of molluscs: snails and slugs

Examples

The class Gastropoda includes thousands of species, from garden snails to marine conchs.
Students studied Gastropoda to better understand the diversity of molluscs.
Fossils of Gastropoda reveal much about prehistoric aquatic environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mollusc - invertebrate animal with soft, unsegmented body

Etymology

From Greek gastēr (stomach) + pous, podos (foot), referring to 'stomach-footed' animals.

2. PROPN

/ ˌɡæstrəˈpoʊdə / (AmE), / ˌɡastrəˈpəʊdə / (BrE)
a large class of mollusks that includes snails, slugs, and their relatives, typically having a single shell and a muscular foot
class of mollusks: snails and slugs

Examples

Many marine creatures in the class Gastropoda are vital to aquatic ecosystems.
The shell of most Gastropoda species is spirally coiled.
Biologists study Gastropoda to understand the evolution of mollusks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gastropod - a member of the class Gastropoda; a snail or slug

Etymology

From Greek gaster (stomach) + pous, pod- (foot), referring to the position of the foot.
