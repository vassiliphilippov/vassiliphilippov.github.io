aigret

1. NOUN

/ eɪˈɡrɛt / (AmE), / eɪˈɡrɛt / (BrE)
A long decorative plume, especially one made of egret feathers, worn on a hat or used as a piece of jewelry fashioned to resemble a plume.
decorative plume or plume-shaped ornament

Examples

The queen's tiara was adorned with a sparkling aigret shaped like a feather.
She chose a delicate aigret to complement her vintage hat at the garden party.
In the 18th century, it was fashionable to wear an aigret on formal occasions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plume - a feather or arrangement of feathers worn as an ornament

Etymology

From French 'aigrette', diminutive of 'aigre' (egret, a type of bird).
