cicatrix

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈkeɪtrɪks / (AmE), / sɪˈkætrɪks / (BrE)
a scar left on the skin or tissue after the healing of a wound or injury
scar left after wound heals
medical

Examples

The surgeon explained that the cicatrix on her arm would gradually fade over time.
A pale cicatrix marked the spot where he had once been badly burned.
After the operation, a visible cicatrix formed along the incision site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scar - a mark left on the skin after a wound heals

Etymology

From Latin cicatrix (scar).
