atonic

1. ADJ

/ əˈtɑnɪk / (AmE), / əˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of a muscle or tissue) lacking normal muscular tone or strength; having decreased or absent tonus.
lacking muscular tone
medical

Examples

The patient's arm muscles became noticeably atonic after the stroke.
In atonic bladder conditions, the organ fails to contract efficiently.
The newborn exhibited atonic limbs, indicating possible neurological issues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tonic - having normal muscle tone or tension

Etymology

From Greek atonos (without tone), from a- (without) + tonos (tone).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02658063-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ əˈtɑnɪk / (AmE), / əˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of a syllable) not carrying stress; unstressed.
unstressed (in linguistics)

Examples

In the word 'banana', the middle syllable is atonic.
The linguist noted that the final syllable of the word was atonic.
Unstressed, or atonic, syllables are common in English polysyllabic words.

Synset Examples

an atonic syllable carries no stress

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stressed - (of a syllable) carrying stress or emphasis

Etymology

From Greek atonos, from a- (not) + tonos (tone, stress).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02328637-a
Synset POS: a
