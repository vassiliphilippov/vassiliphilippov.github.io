Lepismatidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛpɪˈsmætɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌlɛpɪˈsmætɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, wingless insects including silverfish and firebrats, characterized by elongated bodies covered with shiny scales.
family of insects including silverfish and firebrats

Examples

The family Lepismatidae includes both the common silverfish and the firebrat.
Many Lepismatidae species are considered household pests due to their tendency to feed on starchy materials.
Entomologists study Lepismatidae to understand their morphology and evolutionary history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silverfish - a small, wingless insect in the Lepismatidae family
  • Synonym: firebrat - a heat-loving insect in the Lepismatidae family

Etymology

From New Latin Lepismatidae, from genus Lepismata + -idae (taxonomic suffix for families).

2. NOUN

/ lɛp.ɪˈmæ.tɪ.di / (AmE), / lɛp.ɪˈmæ.tɪ.diː / (BrE)
A family of small, wingless insects including firebrats and silverfish, known for their silvery appearance and swift movements.
insect family (firebrats, silverfish)

Examples

Many households encounter Lepismatidae in the form of silverfish scurrying in dark, damp places.
Scientists study Lepismatidae to understand how these insects have adapted to survive in homes.
The family Lepismatidae includes several species that can damage paper and fabrics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firebrat - a small, fast-moving, heat-loving insect
  • Synonym: silverfish - a small, silvery, wingless insect

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Lepismata (from Greek lepis, lepid- 'scale') + -idae (denoting family).
