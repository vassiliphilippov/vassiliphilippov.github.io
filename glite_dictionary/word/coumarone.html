coumarone

1. NOUN

/ ˈkuːməˌroʊn / (AmE), / ˈkuːmərəʊn / (BrE)
A colorless, oily chemical compound obtained from coal tar, used chiefly in the manufacture of synthetic resins.
chemical compound from coal tar

Examples

The factory produces coumarone for use in making durable synthetic resins.
Research on coumarone has led to stronger adhesives in the automotive industry.
Exposure to coumarone fumes should be limited in industrial workplaces.

Etymology

From coumarin, with alteration influenced by chemical nomenclature.
