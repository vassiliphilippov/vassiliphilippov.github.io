Pholadidae

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈlædəˌdiː / (AmE), / fəʊˈlædɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of burrowing marine bivalve mollusks, including the piddocks, characterized by their ability to bore into wood, clay, and rock
family of burrowing marine bivalve mollusks

Examples

Many species within the Pholadidae family are known for boring into hard substrates like wood and rock.
Scientists have studied the unique shell structure of Pholadidae to understand their burrowing mechanism.
The coastal ecosystem provides an ideal habitat for several members of Pholadidae.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'pholas' (a kind of shellfish) + '-idae' (family suffix in zoology).

2. NOUN

/ foʊˈlædɪˌdi / (AmE), / fəʊˈlædɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of bivalve mollusks known for boring into wood, clay, or stone, commonly called piddocks.
family of boring bivalve mollusks (piddocks)

Examples

The Pholadidae are known for their ability to bore into hard substrates such as wood and rock.
Several coastal habitats are home to species from the Pholadidae family.
Researchers studied the unique boring technique of Pholadidae in marine environments.

Etymology

From New Latin Pholadidae, based on the genus Pholas.
