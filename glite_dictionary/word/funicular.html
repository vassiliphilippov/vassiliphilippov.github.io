funicular

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnɪkjələr / (AmE), / fjʊˈnɪkjʊlə / (BrE)
a railway on a steep slope, where cars are pulled up and lowered by a cable, typically with ascending and descending cars balancing each other
cable railway on a steep slope

Examples

We took the funicular to the top of the hill for a scenic view of the city.
The oldest funicular in Europe is still in operation today.
During winter, the funicular helps skiers reach the mountain slopes easily.

Etymology

From French 'funiculaire', from Latin 'funiculus' (small rope), diminutive of 'funis' (rope).

2. ADJ

/ fəˈnɪkjələr / (AmE), / fjʊˈnɪkjʊlə / (BrE)
(of a railway or system) relating to or operated by a cable, typically one used to pull vehicles up a steep slope.
operated by a cable

Examples

The funicular railway climbed the mountainside with ease thanks to its cable system.
A funicular system is often the best solution for transporting people up steep hills.
The city invested in a funicular cable lift to connect the lower and upper districts.

Synset Examples

funicular railway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cable car - a vehicle moved by cables, often up inclines

Etymology

From Latin 'funiculus' (a small rope), diminutive of 'funis' (rope).
