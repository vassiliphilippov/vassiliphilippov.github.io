cliché

1. NOUN

/ kliˈʃeɪ / (AmE), / kliːˈʃeɪ / (BrE)
A phrase or idea that has been overused to the point of losing its original impact or meaning; a trite or obvious remark.
an overused remark or idea

Examples

Her speech was full of clichés that failed to inspire the audience.
Calling it 'a blessing in disguise' is just another tired cliché.
Writers are often warned to avoid using clichés in their work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: platitude - a remark or statement, especially one with a moral content, that has been used too often to be interesting or thoughtful
  • Antonym: originality - the quality of being novel or unusual

Etymology

From French 'cliché', meaning 'stereotype' or 'printing plate', from past participle of 'clicher' (to click, to make a stereotype plate).
