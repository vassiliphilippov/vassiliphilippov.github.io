wingback

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪŋˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈwɪŋ.bæk / (BrE)
a player position in football, usually located on the flank, responsible for both defense and attack along the wing
football position on the wing

Examples

The coach praised the wingback for his outstanding defensive work and attacking runs.
She played as a wingback throughout the tournament and provided several crucial assists.
Modern football formations often rely on a wingback to stretch the opposition's defense.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: fullback - a defensive position in football behind the main line of defense
  • Degree Higher: winger - an attacking football player who plays near the sideline

Etymology

Formed from 'wing' (side or flank) + 'back' (defensive position in football).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪŋˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈwɪŋˌbæk / (BrE)
(American football) an offensive back who lines up just behind or outside the end.
offensive football position
american

Examples

The coach decided to use a wingback for more options on the outside.
He played as a wingback during his college football career.
The wingback motioned to the left before the snap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fullback - an offensive back positioned deep behind the line; usually a lead blocker
  • Synonym: halfback - an offensive back who usually lines up behind the quarterback

Etymology

Formed from 'wing' + 'back', referring to the player's position on the formation.
