plateful

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪtfəl / (AmE), / ˈpleɪtfʊl / (BrE)
An amount of food or other substance that fills a plate.
amount that fills a plate

Examples

She served me a generous plateful of pasta for dinner.
After skipping lunch, he ate a whole plateful of cookies.
She piled her plateful high at the buffet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowlful - the quantity contained in a bowl

Etymology

Formed from 'plate' + 'ful', following the pattern of 'handful', 'cupful', etc.
