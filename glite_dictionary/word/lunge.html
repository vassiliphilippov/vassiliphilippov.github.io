lunge

1. NOUN

/ lʌndʒ / (AmE), / lʌndʒ / (BrE)
a sudden forward movement, especially to attack someone or reach for something
sudden forward movement

Examples

He made a quick lunge to catch the falling glass.
With a sudden lunge, the fencer attacked his opponent.
The cat's lunge caught the mouse by surprise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrust - a sudden forward movement; to push or drive with force
  • Antonym: retreat - the act of withdrawing or moving back

Etymology

From French *allonger* (to lengthen), from Latin *longus* (long).

2. NOUN

/ lʌndʒ / (AmE), / lʌndʒ / (BrE)
(in fencing) a sudden forward thrust made by extending the front leg, keeping the back leg straight, and reaching forward with the sword arm to attack an opponent.
attacking thrust in fencing

Examples

The fencer executed a perfect lunge to score the winning point.
During practice, she worked on the speed and accuracy of her lunge.
A quick, well-timed lunge can catch your opponent off guard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrust - a forceful movement forward, especially with a weapon

Etymology

From French *lancer* (to throw, to launch) and Italian *lanciare* (to throw), related to the idea of a thrust or attack.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01176741-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ lʌndʒ / (AmE), / lʌndʒ / (BrE)
to make a sudden forward movement, especially to attack, reach, or seize something
make a sudden forward movement

Examples

The fencer lunged forward, aiming directly at her opponent's torso.
He suddenly lunged at the door when he heard someone screaming outside.
During the workout, she lunged across the gym floor to build up her leg strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrust - to push suddenly or violently in a specified direction

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; perhaps from obsolete French *allonger* (to lengthen) or from Italian *lungare* (to lengthen), from Latin *longus* (long).
