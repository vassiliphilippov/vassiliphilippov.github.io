to overbear

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈbɛr / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbɛə / (BrE)
to overcome or suppress (objections, criticism, or opposition) by force, authority, or influence
overcome or suppress (by force or authority)

Examples

The leader's authority was enough to overbear any objections from the committee.
She tried to overbear the protests with her firm decision.
During the heated debate, his arguments managed to overbear all criticism.

Synset Examples

overbear criticism, protest, or arguments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overcome - to succeed in dealing with or controlling a problem or feeling
  • Antonym: to succumb - to give in to something; fail to resist

Etymology

From Old English oferberan, with 'ofer' (over) + 'beran' (to bear).
