stacte

1. NOUN

/ ˈstækˌti / (AmE), / ˈstækˌti / (BrE)
a sweet-smelling resin or gum mentioned in the Old Testament as one of the spices used in incense
aromatic resin used in biblical incense
archaic

Examples

The ancient priests burned stacte as part of their incense offerings in the temple.
Some historians believe stacte refers to the resin from certain species of myrrh trees.
Biblical instructions for sacred incense included stacte among its ingredients.

Etymology

From Greek staktē (dripping), referring to the way the resin drips from the tree.
