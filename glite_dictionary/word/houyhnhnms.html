Houyhnhnms

1. NOUN

/ huːˈɪnəmz / (AmE), / huːˈɪnəmz / (BrE)
An imaginary race of intelligent horses who rule over the Yahoos in Jonathan Swift's 'Gulliver's Travels.'
race of intelligent horses in Swift's 'Gulliver's Travels'

Examples

The Houyhnhnms exemplify reason and rationality in Swift's satirical narrative.
Gulliver admires the society of the Houyhnhnms for their order and lack of vice.
In literature classes, we discussed the symbolic significance of the Houyhnhnms in 'Gulliver's Travels.'

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Yahoo - brutish, uncivilized humans in 'Gulliver's Travels'

Etymology

Coined by Jonathan Swift in 'Gulliver's Travels' (1726).
