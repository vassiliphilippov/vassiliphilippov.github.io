pinnacle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪn.ə.kəl / (AmE), / ˈpɪn.ə.kəl / (BrE)
(architecture) A slender, upright spire, typically pointed, placed at the top of a buttress or tower as an ornamental feature or to add weight for stability.
slender spire atop a buttress or tower

Examples

The Gothic cathedral was adorned with a pinnacle on each flying buttress.
Restorers carefully repaired the damaged pinnacle atop the ancient tower.
Ornate stone carvings decorated the pinnacle, drawing the eye upward toward the sky.

Etymology

From Old French *pinacle*, from Latin *pinnaculum* (small wing, gable), a diminutive of *pinna* (wing, battlement).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɪn.ə.kəl / (AmE), / ˈpɪn.ə.kəl / (BrE)
a high, pointed piece of rock; a lofty peak
a high, pointed peak

Examples

The explorers finally reached the pinnacle of the mountain after days of climbing.
A solitary pinnacle rose above the surrounding forest, visible for miles around.
From the pinnacle, the hikers could see the entire valley spread out below them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peak - the pointed top of a mountain
  • Synonym: summit - the highest point of a hill or mountain
  • Antonym: base - the lowest part of something, often used as the antonym for 'pinnacle' in context

Etymology

From Latin *pinnaculum*, diminutive of *pinna* (wing, battlement).

4. VERB

/ ˈpɪnəkəl / (AmE), / ˈpɪnəkəl / (BrE)
to place someone or something on a high point or exalted position, as if on a pinnacle
to raise to a high or exalted position

Examples

The artist was pinnacled by critics who hailed her as the greatest of her generation.
He feared being pinnacled as a genius, preferring to work quietly behind the scenes.
Their victory in the championship pinnacled the team's remarkable season.

Synset Examples

He did not want to be pinnacled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exalt - to raise to a higher rank or position
  • Antonym: to debase - to lower in value or dignity; degrade

Etymology

From the noun 'pinnacle', ultimately from Latin 'pinnaculum' (small wing, gable).
