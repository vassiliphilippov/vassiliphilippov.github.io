parcelling

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrsəlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsəlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of dividing or distributing something into parts or portions, especially according to a plan
the act of distributing or dividing

Examples

The parcelling of the estate was carried out carefully among the heirs.
Government officials oversaw the parcelling of relief supplies to the affected regions.
His job involved the parcelling of company assets during the merger.

Synset Examples

the apportionment of seats in the House of Representatives is based on the relative population of each state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distribution - the process of giving or allocating shares or portions
  • Synonym: apportionment - the act of dividing and assigning in portions

Etymology

From the verb 'parcel,' whose root is from Old French *parcelle*, from Medieval Latin *particella*, a diminutive of Latin *particula* (small part).
