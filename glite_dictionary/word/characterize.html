to characterize

1. VERB

/ ˈkærɪktəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈkærɪktəraɪz / (BrE)
to be a typical feature or quality of someone or something; to distinguish as a particular type
be a typical feature of

Examples

A strong sense of community characterizes life in the small town.
The 19th century characterized itself by major technological innovations.
Openness and honesty characterize her approach to teaching.

Synset Examples

What characterizes a Venetian painting?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to distinguish - to be a feature that makes someone or something different
  • Synonym: to define - to be the feature that most clearly marks the nature of something

Etymology

From French 'caractériser', from caractère 'character', ultimately from Greek 'kharaktēr'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02703792-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈkærɪktəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈkærəktəraɪz / (BrE)
to describe or define the distinctive qualities or features of someone or something
describe the qualities of

Examples

It is unfair to characterize her as lazy based on one incident.
The city's architecture can be characterized by its ornate facades and narrow streets.
Historians often characterize the period as one of rapid social change.

Synset Examples

You can characterize his behavior as that of an egotist
This poem can be characterized as a lament for a dead lover

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to describe - to give an account of something's characteristics
  • Synonym: to define - to state exactly the nature or qualities of something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin characterizare, from Greek kharaktērizein (to engrave or describe a character).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00958719-v
Synset POS: v
