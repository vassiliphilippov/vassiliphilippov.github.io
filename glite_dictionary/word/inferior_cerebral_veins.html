inferior cerebral veins

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɪriɚ sərˈibrəl veɪnz / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪəriə səˈriːbrəl veɪnz / (BrE)
veins that drain the underside of the cerebral hemispheres and empty into the cavernous and transverse sinuses
veins on underside of the brain
medical

Examples

The inferior cerebral veins are important in draining blood from the underside of the brain.
Damage to the inferior cerebral veins can lead to neurological complications.
During surgery, the location of inferior cerebral veins must be carefully considered.

Etymology

From Latin: 'vena' (vein), 'cerebrum' (brain), 'inferior' (lower).
