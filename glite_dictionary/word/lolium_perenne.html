Lolium perenne

1. NOUN

/ ˌloʊliəm pəˈrɛni / (AmE), / ˌləʊliəm pəˈrɛni / (BrE)
A European perennial grass species widely cultivated for pasture, hay, and as a lawn grass.
a grass species used for lawns and pasture

Examples

Lolium perenne is often sown to create durable and attractive lawns in public parks.
Farmers value Lolium perenne for its excellent forage qualities and rapid growth.
Golf courses frequently use Lolium perenne for its fine texture and resistance to wear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perennial ryegrass - common name for Lolium perenne, a grass species used for lawns and pasture

Etymology

From Latin: 'Lolium' (the genus name), and 'perenne' meaning perennial.
