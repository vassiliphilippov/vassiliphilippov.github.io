Crecy

1. PROPN

/ ˈkrɛsi / (AmE), / ˈkresi / (BrE)
the site of a decisive battle in 1346 during the Hundred Years' War, where English forces under Edward III defeated the French army.
battle site in the Hundred Years' War (1346)

Examples

The Battle of Crecy marked a turning point in the Hundred Years' War.
Students visited Crecy to learn about medieval European history.
The English archers played a crucial role at Crecy.

Etymology

From the French place name Crécy, specifically Crécy-en-Ponthieu in northern France.
