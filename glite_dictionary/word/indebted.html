indebted

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdɛtɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛtɪd / (BrE)
feeling or showing gratitude because of a favor or help received from someone
grateful for help or favors received

Examples

She felt deeply indebted to her mentor for all his support over the years.
I am indebted to you for your kindness during my illness.
He left the company feeling indebted to his colleagues for their friendship and guidance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grateful - feeling or showing thanks because someone has done something for you
  • Synonym: thankful - pleased and relieved; showing gratitude

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'indebitatus', from Latin 'debitum' (something owed).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdɛtɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛtɪd / (BrE)
owing gratitude or a favor to someone; under an obligation, often financial or moral, to another person or entity
owing gratitude or obligation to someone

Examples

She felt deeply indebted to her mentor for all his support and guidance.
The company is heavily indebted to several major banks after recent investments.
I am forever indebted to you for saving my life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obliged - feeling that one must do something for someone; in someone's debt
  • Synonym: grateful - feeling or showing thanks to someone
  • Antonym: free - not under any obligation or debt

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *indebitatus*, past participle of Latin *indebitare* (to make a loan).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01621296-s
Synset POS: s
