heartland

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrtˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtˌlænd / (BrE)
the central region of a country or continent, especially one considered vital to its identity, economy, or culture
vital central region of a country or continent

Examples

The Midwest is often called the heartland of America.
Many traditions in the heartland reflect the nation's agricultural roots.
After decades abroad, she returned to her country's heartland to help her family.

Etymology

From 'heart' + 'land', indicating the core area of a country or region.
