Miguel de Cervantes Saavedra

1. PROPN

/ miˈɡɛl deɪ sərˈvɑːntɛs səˈviːdrə / (AmE), / mɪˈɡɛl deɪ sərˈvɑːntɛs səˈveɪdrə / (BrE)
(1547–1616) A Spanish writer best known for 'Don Quixote', whose works profoundly influenced the development of the modern novel.
Spanish author of 'Don Quixote'

Examples

Miguel de Cervantes Saavedra is widely regarded as one of the greatest writers in the Spanish language.
Many scholars consider Miguel de Cervantes Saavedra's 'Don Quixote' to be the first modern European novel.
The legacy of Miguel de Cervantes Saavedra continues to influence writers and artists around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Don Quixote - Cervantes's most famous novel, satirizing chivalry

Etymology

Spanish personal name, with 'de Cervantes' indicating his father's family and 'Saavedra' his mother's.
