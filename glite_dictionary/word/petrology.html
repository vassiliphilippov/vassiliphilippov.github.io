petrology

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtrɑlədʒi / (AmE), / pəˈtrɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The branch of geology that deals with the study of rocks, including their origin, formation, composition, and classification.
the scientific study of rocks

Examples

She decided to specialize in petrology during her graduate studies in geology.
Advances in petrology have helped scientists better understand how the Earth's crust was formed.
The university offers an advanced course on petrology and mineral analysis.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: geology - the science that deals with the earth's physical structure and substance
  • Synonym: mineralogy - the scientific study of minerals

Etymology

From Greek petra (rock) + -logia (study).
