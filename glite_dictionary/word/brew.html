brew

1. NOUN

/ bruː / (AmE), / bruː / (BrE)
a drink made by steeping, boiling, and fermenting ingredients, especially alcoholic beverages like beer, rather than by distilling
fermented drink (especially beer)

Examples

The local pub serves its own brew that has a rich and hoppy flavor.
She tasted the homemade brew and smiled at its unique taste.
Many people enjoy a cold brew after a long day at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beer - an alcoholic drink made from yeast-fermented malt flavored with hops
  • Antonym: distillate - a liquid product condensed from vapor in a distillation process

Etymology

Old English *breowan* (to brew, ferment).

2. VERB

/ bruː / (AmE), / bruː / (BrE)
to make a beverage, such as tea or coffee, by mixing it with hot or boiling water and letting it stand to extract the flavor
make tea or coffee by infusing in hot water

Examples

I like to let my tea brew for five minutes before drinking it.
The coffee was brewing in the kitchen when I arrived.
She set the teapot aside to allow the herbs to brew properly.

Synset Examples

the tea is brewing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steep - to soak food or tea in water to extract flavor

Etymology

Old English *breowan* (to brew, ferment), related to German *brauen*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00328623-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ bruː / (AmE), / bruː / (BrE)
to make a beverage such as beer or tea by mixing, boiling, and fermenting or infusing ingredients
make (beer or tea) by brewing

Examples

Every morning, she likes to brew a fresh pot of coffee before work.
Local farmers brew their own cider using apples from their orchards.
He learned how to brew craft beer at home as a hobby.

Synset Examples

people have been brewing beer for thousands of years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ferment - to cause a substance to undergo fermentation

Etymology

Old English *breowan* (to brew, ferment); related to Dutch *brouwen* and German *brauen*.
