monologist

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɑːlədʒɪst / (AmE), / məˈnɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a person who performs monologues, especially as an entertainer on stage
performer of monologues

Examples

The acclaimed monologist captivated the audience with her powerful stories about family and identity.
He started his career as a monologist before moving on to television and film.
Many monologists write their own material, tailoring each performance to their unique style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monologue - a long speech by one person in a play or performance

Etymology

From 'monologue' + '-ist', patterned after analogous terms in French ('monologuiste').
