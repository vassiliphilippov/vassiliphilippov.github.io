mebibit

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛbɪˌbɪt / (AmE), / ˈmiːbɪˌbɪt / (BrE)
A unit of information equal to 1,048,576 (2^20) bits, primarily used in computing to measure data size or transmission speed.
unit of data, 1,048,576 bits

Examples

File sizes are often measured in mebibits in certain technical contexts.
The network speed was advertised as 100 mebibits per second.
One mebibit is equal to 1,048,576 bits, providing a precise measurement for digital data.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: kibibit - A unit of information equal to 1024 bits
  • Degree Higher: gigabit - A unit of information equal to 1,000,000,000 bits

Etymology

Formed from "mega binary bit"; modeled on SI and IEC naming conventions.
