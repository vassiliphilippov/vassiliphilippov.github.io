toitoi

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔɪˌtɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈtɔɪ.tɔɪ / (BrE)
A tall grass native to New Zealand, often used by the Maori people for thatching roofs.
tall New Zealand grass used for thatching

Examples

The roof of the traditional hut was made from carefully woven toitoi.
We saw large clumps of toitoi swaying in the New Zealand breeze.
Maori people have long used toitoi as a material for thatching and weaving.

Etymology

From Maori 'toetoe', the name of the plant.
