secpar

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkˌpɑr / (AmE), / ˈsɛkˌpɑː / (BrE)
A unit of astronomical distance equal to the distance at which a star would have a parallax of one second of arc, approximately 3.262 light-years.
astronomical distance unit (1 sec of parallax)

Examples

The star is almost two secpars away from our solar system.
Distances in deep space are often measured in secpars rather than kilometers.
Astronomers use the secpar to describe vast lengths between stars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parsec - a unit of astronomical length equivalent to about 3.26 light years
  • Degree Lower: light year - unit of astronomical distance covering the distance light travels in one year

Etymology

Shortened form from 'second of parallax'.
