Euterpe

1. NOUN

/ juːˈtɜrpi / (AmE), / juːˈtɜːpiː / (BrE)
A genus of graceful monocotyledonous palm trees native to tropical America, some species of which are cultivated for their edible fruit and ornamental value.
genus of South American palms

Examples

The Euterpe genus includes the popular açaí palm, known for its nutritious berries.
Many Euterpe species grow along the banks of rivers in the Amazon rainforest.
Botanists collected several specimens of Euterpe during their expedition in South America.

Etymology

From Greek Euterpē, name of the Muse of music and joy, reflecting the graceful appearance of the palms.

2. PROPN

/ juːˈtɜːrpi / (AmE), / juːˈtɜːˌpiː / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the Muse of music, especially of the flute.
the Muse of music in Greek mythology

Examples

In ancient poetry, Euterpe was often invoked as the inspiration for musical compositions.
A statue of Euterpe, the Muse of music, stands in the town square.
The composer dedicated his symphony to Euterpe, seeking her mythical blessing.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Εὐτέρπη (Euterpē), meaning 'giver of much delight', from eu- 'well' + terpō 'to delight'.

3. NOUN

/ juːˈtɝːpi / (AmE), / juːˈtɜːpiː / (BrE)
A genus of graceful palm trees native to tropical America, including several species cultivated for their fruit and ornamental value.
genus of tropical American palm trees

Examples

Several species within the Euterpe genus are valued for their edible berries known as açaí.
Botanists discussed the conservation of Euterpe palms in the Amazon rainforest.
The slender trunks of Euterpe trees contribute to their elegant appearance in tropical landscapes.

Etymology

From Greek Euterpē, the name of one of the Muses in Greek mythology.
