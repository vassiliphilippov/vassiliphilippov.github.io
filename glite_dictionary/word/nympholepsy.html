nympholepsy

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪm.foʊˌlɛp.si / (AmE), / ˈnɪmfəˌlɛpsi / (BrE)
an intense emotional frenzy or yearning, especially for something unattainable or idealized
frenzy or yearning for the unattainable

Examples

Caught in the throes of nympholepsy, the poet spent sleepless nights yearning for his ideal muse.
Critics described her obsession with perfect beauty as a kind of nympholepsy.
His nympholepsy made him forever restless, seeking after something he could never attain.

Etymology

From Greek nympholēpsia, from nymphē (nymph) + lēpsis (seizure).
