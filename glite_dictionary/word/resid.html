resid

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛzɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɛzɪd / (BrE)
the heavy oil products that remain after crude petroleum has been distilled, especially as fuel or further processed material
residual petroleum distillation product

Examples

The refinery sold the resid for use as bunker fuel.
After distillation, only the thick resid remained at the bottom of the tower.
Some plants further process the resid into lighter, more valuable products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: residue - what is left after a process or removal of other parts

Etymology

Shortened from 'residue', meaning that which remains after a process.
