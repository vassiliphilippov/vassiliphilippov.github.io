trogon

1. NOUN

/ ˈtroʊɡɒn / (AmE), / ˈtrəʊɡɒn / (BrE)
A brightly colored forest bird of tropical regions of the Americas, known for its lustrous plumage and long tail.
a brightly colored New World forest bird

Examples

The resplendent quetzal is a stunning species of trogon found in Central America.
Birdwatchers visit the rainforest hoping to catch a glimpse of a rare trogon.
With its brilliant feathers, the trogon stood out among the dense, green foliage.

Etymology

From New Latin *Trogon*, from Greek *trōgōn* ('gnawing'), referring to the birds' habit of gnawing holes in trees for nests.
