trilateral

1. ADJ

/ traɪˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / traɪˈlæt.ər.əl / (BrE)
having three sides or involving three parties, groups, or countries
having three sides; involving three parts

Examples

The architect designed a trilateral structure for the new plaza.
A trilateral agreement was reached between the three nations.
The artist drew a trilateral figure on the board.

Synset Examples

a trilateral figure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triangular - having three angles and three sides

Etymology

From Latin 'tri-' (three) + 'lateralis' (sided).

2. NOUN

/ traɪˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / traɪˈlæt.ər.əl / (BrE)
A three-sided polygon; a geometric figure with three straight sides and three angles.
a three-sided polygon

Examples

The geometry lesson focused on the properties of a trilateral.
A trilateral is another term for a triangle in mathematical contexts.
Each trilateral was colored differently to highlight its unique shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triangle - a three-sided polygon; shape with three angles

Etymology

From Latin 'tri-' (three) + 'lateralis' (of sides).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13901977-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ traɪˈlætərəl / (AmE), / traɪˈlætərəl / (BrE)
involving or carried out by three parties, especially governments, organizations, or groups
involving three parties

Examples

The countries held trilateral talks to address regional security concerns.
A trilateral agreement was signed between the three companies to collaborate on the project.
The trilateral commission focuses on relations among North America, Europe, and Asia.

Synset Examples

trilateral talks

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bilateral - involving two parties

Etymology

From Latin 'tri-' (three) + 'lateralis' (of side), meaning 'three-sided'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01952784-s
Synset POS: s
