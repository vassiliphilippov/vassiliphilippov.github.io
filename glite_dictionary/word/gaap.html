GAAP

1. NOUN

/ dʒi æp / (AmE), / dʒiː æp / (BrE)
Generally Accepted Accounting Principles; a set of rules and standards for financial accounting and reporting.
accounting standards and rules
legal

Examples

All companies listed on the exchange must prepare their reports according to GAAP.
The accountant explained the importance of following GAAP when compiling financial statements.
The new regulations aim to align local laws more closely with GAAP standards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: IFRS - International Financial Reporting Standards; global accounting guidelines

Etymology

Acronym from 'Generally Accepted Accounting Principles'.
