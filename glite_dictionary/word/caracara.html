caracara

1. NOUN

/ ˌkærəˈkɑrə / (AmE), / ˌkærəˈkɑːrə / (BrE)
a long-legged, carrion-eating hawk of South and Central America, known for its scavenging habits
a South/Central American carrion-eating hawk

Examples

We spotted a caracara scavenging by the roadside in Costa Rica.
The distinctive crest of the caracara makes it easy to identify among other raptors.
Unlike most hawks, the caracara often walks on the ground searching for food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawk - a bird of prey with broad rounded wings and a long tail

Etymology

From Spanish 'caracara', probably of indigenous South American origin.
