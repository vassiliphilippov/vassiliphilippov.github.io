Amsonia

1. NOUN

/ æmˈsoʊniə / (AmE), / æmˈsəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous plants and subshrubs with milky sap and showy bluish flowers, native to regions from Europe and Asia Minor to Japan and North America.
genus of flowering plants (Amsonia)

Examples

Several species of Amsonia are prized for their attractive blue flowers in spring gardens.
The botanical garden features a rare Amsonia native to North America.
Gardeners appreciate Amsonia for its hardiness and unique milky sap.

Etymology

Named after the American physician John Amson (active 18th century).

2. NOUN

/ æmˈsoʊniə / (AmE), / amˈsəʊnɪə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs and subshrubs with milky juice and showy bluish flowers, native to Europe, Asia Minor, Japan, and North America.
genus of flowering herbs and subshrubs

Examples

The garden's newest addition is an Amsonia with striking pale blue flowers.
She identified the plant as Amsonia, commonly known as bluestar.
Several Amsonia species are popular choices for perennial borders due to their hardiness.

Etymology

Named after John Amson (c. 1698–1765), an English-born American physician.
