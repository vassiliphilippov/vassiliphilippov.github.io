cole

1. NOUN

/ koʊl / (AmE), / kəʊl / (BrE)
A kind of coarse, curly-leafed cabbage, often grown for animal feed or as a hardy vegetable.
type of curly-leafed cabbage

Examples

Some farmers grow cole as a winter crop for their livestock.
You can substitute cole in recipes that call for leafy greens.
The garden was filled with rows of hardy cole plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kale - a hardy cabbage with curled leaves

Etymology

From Latin caulis (stem, cabbage), via Old English cāl.

2. NOUN

/ koʊl / (AmE), / kəʊl / (BrE)
A hardy cabbage with coarse, curly leaves that do not form a compact head, often grown as a vegetable.
hardy cabbage with curly leaves

Examples

The farmer planted rows of cole for a hearty winter crop.
Cooked cole makes a nutritious and flavorful side dish.
Many gardeners choose cole because it tolerates cold temperatures well.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kale - a type of hardy cabbage with curled leaves

Etymology

From Old English 'cawl', from Latin 'caulis' (stem, cabbage).
