wry

1. ADJ

/ raɪ / (AmE), / raɪ / (BrE)
showing or expressing dry, mocking, or sarcastic humor, often with a touch of irony
dryly humorous; mocking or sarcastic

Examples

She told the story with a wry smile, clearly amused by its irony.
His wry sense of humor made even the most awkward situations funny.
The author’s wry comments add a layer of subtle mockery to the novel.

Synset Examples

dry humor
an ironic remark often conveys an intended meaning obliquely
an ironic novel
an ironical smile
with a wry Scottish wit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ironic - using words to convey a meaning opposite to their literal meaning, often for humorous effect
  • Synonym: mocking - making fun of or ridiculing someone or something
  • Antonym: earnest - showing sincere and serious intention

Etymology

From Old English *wrygan* (to turn or twist).

2. ADJ

/ raɪ / (AmE), / raɪ / (BrE)
bent or twisted to one side; distorted in shape or position.
bent or twisted to one side

Examples

The tree’s trunk grew in a wry fashion after the storm.
She noticed the wry angle of his smile, hinting at past injury.
He suffered from a wry neck, making it painful to turn his head.

Synset Examples

a wry neck

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twisted - contorted or bent out of shape

Etymology

From Old English *wrīgian* (to turn or bend), related to Old Norse *ruga* (to bend, twist).
