ruination

1. NOUN

/ ˌruːnəˈʃən / (AmE), / ˌruːɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or state of complete destruction or severe damage; the loss of all value or hope
complete destruction; devastating loss

Examples

The scandal led to the ruination of his political career.
Years of neglect resulted in the ruination of the historic building.
Gambling addiction can bring financial ruination to families.

Synset Examples

you have brought ruin on this entire family

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruin - the physical, moral, or financial collapse or destruction of something
  • Synonym: devastation - great destruction or damage

Etymology

From Latin 'ruina' (a collapse, downfall) via Old French 'ruination'.

4. NOUN

/ ˌruːnˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌruːɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of losing one's position, reputation, or success; the state of being ruined
the state of being ruined or destroyed

Examples

The company's risky investments led to its financial ruination within just a few years.
His involvement in the scandal brought about the complete ruination of his reputation.
The sudden strike almost caused the ruination of the small family business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destruction - the act of destroying something completely
  • Synonym: downfall - the loss of power, prosperity, or status
  • Antonym: restoration - the process of returning something to a former condition

Etymology

From Middle English ruine, from Latin ruina (collapse, downfall) with suffix -ation forming the noun.
