friendliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛndlɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈfrɛndlɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being friendly; showing kindness and pleasant behavior toward others
the quality of being friendly

Examples

Her friendliness made everyone feel welcome at the party.
The friendliness of the staff is one reason I return to that café.
Despite being strangers, his friendliness quickly broke the ice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amiability - the quality of being pleasant and easy to like
  • Antonym: hostility - unfriendly or antagonistic behavior or attitude

Etymology

Formed from 'friendly' + '-ness', both from Old English 'freond' (friend).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛndlinɪs / (AmE), / ˈfrɛndlɪnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being kind, pleasant, and supportive toward others
quality of being friendly

Examples

Her friendliness made everyone feel welcome at the party.
Many customers appreciate the friendliness of the staff at this store.
Despite being nervous, his friendliness helped him make new friends quickly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hostility - a hostile or unfriendly state, attitude, or action
  • Synonym: amiability - the quality of being friendly and pleasant

Etymology

From 'friendly' + '-ness', with 'friendly' from Old English 'freondlic' (like a friend).
