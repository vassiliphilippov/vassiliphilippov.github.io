to reface

1. VERB

/ riˈfeɪs / (AmE), / riˈfeɪs / (BrE)
to put a new facing on a garment
put new facing on a garment

Examples

The tailor agreed to reface the jacket to give it a newer look.
After years of wear, she decided to reface her favorite coat.
Carefully refacing the collar can extend the life of your garment.

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 're-' (again) and 'face' (the front or surface of something).

WordNet ID: oewn-01274703-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌriˈfeɪs / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfeɪs / (BrE)
to cover the surface of something, especially a building, with new material to give it a fresh appearance
cover with a new surface or facing

Examples

The city decided to reface the historic clock tower to preserve its character.
Many homeowners choose to reface their kitchen cabinets instead of replacing them.
After years of weather damage, the company refaced the office building with modern panels.

Synset Examples

The building was refaced with beautiful stones

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to renovate - to restore something to a good condition by repairing or improving it

Etymology

From re- (again) + face (surface); modeled after the sense of 'face' as a surface appearance.
