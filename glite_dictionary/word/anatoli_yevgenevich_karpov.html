Anatoli Yevgenevich Karpov

1. PROPN

/ əˈnɑːtoʊli jɛvˈɡɛnyəvɪtʃ ˈkɑːrpɔv / (AmE), / əˈnatəli jɛvˈɡɛnjəvɪtʃ ˈkɑːpɒf / (BrE)
A Russian chess grandmaster who was World Chess Champion from 1975 to 1985.
Russian world chess champion, 1975–1985

Examples

Anatoli Yevgenevich Karpov dominated the world of chess during his decade as champion.
Many experts consider Anatoli Yevgenevich Karpov to be one of the greatest positional players in history.
In 1985, Anatoli Yevgenevich Karpov lost his world title to Garry Kasparov.
