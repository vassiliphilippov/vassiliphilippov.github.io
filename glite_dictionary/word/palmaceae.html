Palmaceae

1. NOUN

/ pɑːlˈmeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / pɑːlˈmeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of chiefly tropical trees, shrubs, and vines, commonly known as the palm family, with tall trunks and large leaves; coextensive with the order Palmales.
the palm family of tropical plants

Examples

The Palmaceae family includes the coconut and date palm among its many members.
Researchers are studying the ecological impact of Palmaceae in tropical rainforests.
Many ornamental plants sold in nurseries belong to the Palmaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arecaceae - the alternative modern family name for palms
  • Synonym: Palmales - the order of flowering plants including palms

Etymology

From Latin palma (palm tree) + -aceae (a standard suffix for plant family names, from Greek -ακεια).

2. NOUN

/ pælˈmeɪsiː / (AmE), / pɑːlˈmeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly tropical trees, shrubs, and vines with tall, columnar trunks and large leaves, including the palms; now usually called Arecaceae
the palm family of plants

Examples

The coconut tree is a member of the Palmaceae family, thriving in tropical climates.
Many ornamental species in gardens belong to the Palmaceae.
Researchers study the Palmaceae for their ecological and economic importance in the tropics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arecaceae - the current botanical name for the palm family

Etymology

From Modern Latin Palmaceae, from 'palma' (palm tree), the type genus.
