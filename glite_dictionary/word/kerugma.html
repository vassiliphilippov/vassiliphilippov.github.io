kerugma

1. NOUN

/ kəˈruːɡmə / (AmE), / kəˈruːɡmə / (BrE)
the preaching of the gospel of Christ in the style characteristic of the early Christian church
preaching gospel in early church style

Examples

Early church leaders emphasized the importance of kerugma in spreading the message of Christianity.
The theologian wrote extensively about the role of kerugma in forming Christian doctrine.
Understanding the kerugma helps scholars trace the development of early Christian beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homily - a sermon or religious discourse
  • Synonym: sermon - a talk on a religious or moral subject given during a church service

Etymology

From Greek *kērygma* (proclamation; preaching), from *kērussein* (to proclaim).
