expansible

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈspænsɪbəl / (AmE), / ɪkˈspansɪbəl / (BrE)
capable of expanding or being expanded in size, volume, or extent.
able to expand or be expanded

Examples

The balloon is made of an expansible material that can stretch without tearing.
Certain metals are expansible when heated, allowing for flexible machine design.
The expansible chamber adjusted its volume to maintain the desired pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expandable - able to be made larger or increased in size

Etymology

From French 'expansible' or Medieval Latin 'expansibilis', from Latin 'expansus' (spread out).

2. ADJ

/ ɪkˈspænsəbl / (AmE), / ɪkˈspansɪb(ə)l / (BrE)
capable of being expanded or enlarged; able to increase in size or volume.
able to be expanded or enlarged

Examples

The balloon is made from an expansible material that stretches as it fills with air.
Gases are expansible and will fill any available space inside a container.
The device relies on an expansible chamber that increases its volume when heated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expandable - able to be expanded; capable of expansion
  • Antonym: contractible - capable of contracting or being contracted

Etymology

From French expansible, from Latin expandere (to spread out).

Synset POS: s
