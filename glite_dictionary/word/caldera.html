caldera

1. NOUN

/ kælˈdɛr.ə / (AmE), / kælˈdɛə.rə / (BrE)
A large, bowl-shaped volcanic crater formed by a major eruption leading to the collapse of a volcano's mouth.
large volcanic crater

Examples

The massive caldera at Yellowstone National Park attracts geologists from around the world.
After the eruption, a deep caldera formed where the mountain peak once stood.
Many volcanic islands have a large central caldera filled with water, forming a lake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crater - a bowl-shaped geological formation at the top of a volcano or caused by an impact

Etymology

From Spanish 'caldera', literally 'cauldron', from Latin 'caldaria' (cooking pot).
