cerussite

1. NOUN

/ səˈrʌsaɪt / (AmE), / səˈrʌsaɪt / (BrE)
A mineral consisting of lead carbonate, serving as an important source of lead.
a lead carbonate mineral

Examples

The geologist identified the sparkling white mineral as cerussite.
Large deposits of cerussite were historically important for the extraction of lead.
Collectors value cerussite crystals for their unique twinned structures.

Etymology

From German 'Zerussit', derived from Latin 'cerussa' (white lead).
