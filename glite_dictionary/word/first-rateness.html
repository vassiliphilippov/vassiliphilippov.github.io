first-rateness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɝstˌreɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɜːstˌreɪtnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being exceptionally good or excellent.
the state of being excellent

Examples

The first-rateness of the performance earned the actors a standing ovation.
Many travelers are drawn to the first-rateness of the city's restaurants and hotels.
Her first-rateness as a leader was clear to everyone in the organization.

Synset Examples

York City and Chicago don't have many mountains or (usable) beaches either, and the first-rateness of their pro sports is currently debatable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excellence - the quality of being outstanding or extremely good
  • Antonym: mediocrity - the quality of being average or not very good

Etymology

Formed from 'first-rate' (excellent) + '-ness' (state, quality).
