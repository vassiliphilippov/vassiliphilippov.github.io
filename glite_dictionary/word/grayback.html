grayback

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪˌbæk / (BrE)
A type of dowitcher bird with a grey back, especially during its non-breeding plumage.
dowitcher with a grey back

Examples

We spotted a grayback feeding along the muddy shoreline at dawn.
Many birdwatchers travel to the coast in winter to see the migrating graybacks.
In its non-breeding season, the grayback is often mistaken for other shorebirds due to its coloration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dowitcher - a type of long-billed wading bird

Etymology

From 'gray' (colour) + 'back', referring to the coloration of the bird's back.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪˌbak / (BrE)
a small sandpiper that breeds in the Arctic and migrates to the Southern Hemisphere for winter
type of migratory sandpiper

Examples

Flocks of grayback sandpipers stop to feed along the shore during migration.
We spotted a grayback in its striking breeding plumage near the tundra lakes.
The annual journey of the grayback covers thousands of miles between continents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandpiper - a type of wading bird often found near water
