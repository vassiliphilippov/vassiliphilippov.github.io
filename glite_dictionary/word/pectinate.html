pectinate

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛk.tɪ.neɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɛk.tɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
having narrow parallel projections or divisions arranged like the teeth of a comb.
having divisions like a comb

Examples

The fern leaves are pectinate, resembling the teeth of a comb.
Many bird species have pectinate claws on their middle toes for grooming.
Microscope analysis revealed the feather had a distinctly pectinate edge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comb-like - resembling or shaped like a comb

Etymology

From Latin 'pectinatus', from 'pecten' (comb).
