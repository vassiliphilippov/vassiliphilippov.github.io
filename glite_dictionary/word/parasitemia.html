parasitemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəsaɪˈtiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌpærəsaɪˈtiːmiə / (BrE)
The presence of parasites in the blood.
presence of parasites in the blood
medical

Examples

Doctors confirmed that the patient had parasitemia after detecting malaria parasites in his blood.
High levels of parasitemia can indicate a severe infection and require urgent treatment.
The research study measured parasitemia to evaluate the effectiveness of the new antimalarial drug.

Etymology

From Greek 'parasitos' (parasite) + 'haima' (blood), referring to organisms present in the bloodstream.
