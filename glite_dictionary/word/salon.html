salon

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɑn / (AmE), / ˈsælɒn / (BrE)
a gallery or exhibition, especially for the display of works of art
art gallery or exhibition

Examples

The annual art salon attracted painters and critics from all over Europe.
Her sculpture was chosen for display in the prestigious Paris salon.
He visited a salon last weekend to see new works by emerging artists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gallery - a room or building for the display or sale of works of art

Etymology

French, from Italian *salone* (large hall), from *sala* (hall).

2. NOUN

/ səˈlɑn / (AmE), / səˈlɒn / (BrE)
an elegant sitting room in a large house, used for receiving guests
elegant sitting room for receiving guests

Examples

The guests gathered in the salon for afternoon tea before dinner was served.
Her Parisian salon was renowned for lively discussions among artists and writers.
The mansion's elegant salon overlooked the gardens through tall french windows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drawing room - a room in a large house where guests are received and entertained

Etymology

From French 'salon', from Italian 'salone' (large hall), augmentative of 'sala' (room, hall).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04138098-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ səˈlɑn / (AmE), / sæˈlɒn / (BrE)
A shop where hairdressers or beauticians provide hair and beauty treatments.
hair or beauty treatment shop

Examples

She visits the salon every few weeks to have her hair styled.
The salon offers a range of services, from haircuts to manicures.
He opened a new salon downtown specializing in organic beauty treatments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barbershop - a place where men have their hair cut and beards trimmed
  • Synonym: spa - a place where people go for health and beauty treatments
  • Synonym: parlour - another word for a shop, especially an old-fashioned one

Etymology

From French 'salon' (reception room), from Italian 'salone', augmentative of 'sala' (hall).
