acidosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæsɪˈdoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌæsɪˈdəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition in which there is an abnormally high level of acidity in the blood and other body tissues.
abnormal acidity of blood or tissues
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with diabetic acidosis after lab results revealed elevated blood acid levels.
Untreated acidosis can disrupt normal organ function and may become life-threatening.
Doctors monitored her acidosis closely to ensure it did not worsen during treatment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: alkalosis - abnormally low acidity (or high alkalinity) of the blood and other body tissues

Etymology

From New Latin, from acid + -osis (denoting a medical condition).
