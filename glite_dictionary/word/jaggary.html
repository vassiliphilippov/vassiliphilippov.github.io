jaggary

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæɡəri / (AmE), / ˈdʒæɡəri / (BrE)
an unrefined brown sugar made chiefly from the sap of palm trees or sugarcane, commonly used in South Asian cooking
unrefined brown sugar from palm or cane

Examples

She sprinkled some jaggary into her tea instead of white sugar for a richer flavor.
Traditional Indian desserts often use jaggary as a sweetener.
The market sold blocks of fresh jaggary wrapped in banana leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jaggery - unrefined brown sugar from palm sap or sugarcane

Etymology

From Hindi 'gur' via Portuguese 'jagara', from Malayalam 'śarkkara' (sugar)
