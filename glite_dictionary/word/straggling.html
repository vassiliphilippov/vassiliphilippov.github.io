straggling

1. ADJ

/ ˈstræɡlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstræɡlɪŋ / (BrE)
growing, spreading, or arranged in an irregular, untidy, or scattered way; not compact or orderly
spreading or arranged in an untidy, irregular way

Examples

The straggling vines crept across the old garden wall in every direction.
His straggling handwriting made the letter difficult to read.
A few straggling houses stood apart from the main cluster of the village.

Synset Examples

sprawling handwriting
straggling branches
straggly hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sprawling - spreading out over a large area in an untidy or irregular way
  • Antonym: neat - arranged in a tidy and orderly way

Etymology

From the verb 'straggle,' likely from Middle English 'straglen,' of uncertain origin.
