penetrator

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛn.əˌtreɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɛn.ɪˌtreɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person or thing that enters or forces a way into or through something, often overcoming obstacles or resistance
one who enters or forces way through

Examples

The security team quickly detected the penetrator before any damage was done.
The missile is designed to act as a deep bunker penetrator.
In espionage, a skilled penetrator can gather information without being noticed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intruder - a person who enters a place without permission
  • Synonym: invader - someone who enters by force to conquer

Etymology

From Latin 'penetrator', from 'penetrare' (to enter, to pierce).
