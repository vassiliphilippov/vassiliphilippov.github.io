spiritless

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɪrɪtləs / (AmE), / ˈspɪrɪtlɪs / (BrE)
lacking energy, enthusiasm, or liveliness; showing little spirit or vigor.
lacking energy or enthusiasm

Examples

After several defeats, the team's performance became noticeably spiritless on the field.
She gave a spiritless answer to the interviewer's challenging question.
The meeting was long and spiritless, with few participants showing any enthusiasm.

Synset Examples

a spiritless reply to criticism

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lively - full of life and energy
  • Antonym: energetic - having or showing energy and enthusiasm
  • Synonym: listless - lacking energy or enthusiasm

Etymology

From English 'spirit' + '-less' (without).

2. ADJ

/ ˈspɪrɪtləs / (AmE), / ˈspɪrɪtləs / (BrE)
Lacking energy, enthusiasm, or courage; showing little liveliness or determination.
lacking energy or courage

Examples

The team's spiritless performance disappointed the fans and coaches alike.
She offered only a spiritless response to the exciting news, barely managing a smile.
His spiritless attitude made it difficult for him to stand up for himself at work.

Synset Examples

compliant and anxious to suit his opinions of those of others
a fine fiery blast against meek conformity
she looked meek but had the heart of a lion
was submissive and subservient

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lively - full of life and energy
  • Synonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence

Etymology

Formed from 'spirit' + '-less,' meaning 'without spirit'.
