Erithacus svecicus

1. NOUN

/ ɛˌrɪθəkəs swɪˈsiːkəs / (AmE), / ɛˌrɪθəkəs swɪˈsiːkəs / (BrE)
A species of small, brightly colored songbird found in northern Europe and Asia, commonly known as the bluethroat.
a species of Eurasian songbird (bluethroat)

Examples

The Erithacus svecicus displays a vivid blue patch on its throat during the breeding season.
Birdwatchers in Scandinavia are often excited to spot the elusive Erithacus svecicus.
The migratory patterns of Erithacus svecicus take it from Asia to parts of Europe each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluethroat - a Eurasian songbird with a blue throat; common name for Erithacus svecicus

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name Erithacus with the species epithet svecicus, meaning 'Swedish'.
