Galium odoratum

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪliəm oʊˈdɔrətəm / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪliəm əʊˈdɒrətəm / (BrE)
A perennial herb of the bedstraw family with small white flowers and narrow leaves, grown for its fragrance and use in flavorings and sachets; also known as sweet woodruff.
fragrant perennial herb; sweet woodruff

Examples

The shaded garden bed was carpeted with Galium odoratum, filling the air with a sweet scent.
Some European liqueurs use Galium odoratum to impart a distinctive aroma and flavor.
We planted Galium odoratum as a decorative ground cover beneath the oak trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet woodruff - common name for Galium odoratum, a fragrant perennial herb

Etymology

Galium from Latin, meaning 'milk', referring to the use of some species to curdle milk; odoratum from Latin 'odorus' meaning 'fragrant'.
