Nymphaea stellata

1. NOUN

/ nɪmˈfiːə stəˈlɑːtə / (AmE), / nɪmˈfiːə stɛˈlɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of blue lotus native to India and southeastern Asia, known for its attractive blue flowers and aquatic habitat.
blue lotus native to India and SE Asia

Examples

The Nymphaea stellata blooms in shallow ponds during the summer in India.
Many people admire the vibrant colors of Nymphaea stellata in botanical gardens.
In traditional paintings from Southeast Asia, the Nymphaea stellata often appears as a symbol of beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue lotus - A common name for aquatic plants with blue flowers, especially Nymphaea species
  • Synonym: Nymphaea nouchali - A closely related water lily species

Etymology

From Latin: *Nymphaea* (water lily genus) + Latin *stellata* (star-like), referring to the star-shaped flowers.
