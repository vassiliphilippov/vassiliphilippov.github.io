Muharrum

1. NOUN

/ muːˈhɑːrəm / (AmE), / mʊˈharəm / (BrE)
the first month of the Islamic calendar, considered sacred by Muslims and marked by important religious observances
first month of the Islamic calendar

Examples

Muslims observe several important rituals during Muharrum, including the Day of Ashura.
The new year in the Islamic calendar begins with Muharrum.
Many communities hold commemorative gatherings throughout Muharrum.

Etymology

From Arabic مُحَرَّم‎ (muḥarram), meaning 'forbidden' or 'sacred'.
