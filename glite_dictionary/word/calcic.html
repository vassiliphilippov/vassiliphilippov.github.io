calcic

1. ADJ

/ ˈkælsɪk / (AmE), / ˈkalsɪk / (BrE)
containing calcium or lime; relating to or derived from calcium
containing calcium or lime

Examples

The region is known for its calcic soils, which are ideal for certain crops.
Geologists identified a layer of calcic material beneath the volcanic ash.
This fertilizer is specifically designed for plants that require calcic nutrients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calcium - a chemical element, Ca, essential to living organisms
  • Synonym: lime - a white caustic alkaline substance consisting of calcium oxide

Etymology

From Latin 'calx, calcis' (lime), with '-ic' forming an adjective.
