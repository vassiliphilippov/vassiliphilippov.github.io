Wyrd

1. NOUN

/ wɪrd / (AmE), / wɪəd / (BrE)
Fate or destiny, especially as personified in Old English or Norse mythology; sometimes used for any of the three Weird Sisters in literature.
personified fate; Weird Sister
archaic

Examples

The heroes of ancient stories often believed in the power of Wyrd to guide their actions.
In some interpretations, each Wyrd sister represents a different aspect of fate.
The concept of Wyrd was central to Anglo-Saxon beliefs about destiny.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fate - the development of events beyond a person's control
  • Synonym: destiny - the events that will necessarily happen to a particular person or thing in the future

Etymology

Old English *wyrd* (fate, destiny), related to Old Norse *urðr* (fate), Proto-Germanic *wurđiz* (turning, event).
