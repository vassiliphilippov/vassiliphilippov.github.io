Liquidambar styraciflua

1. NOUN

/ lɪˌkwɪdˌæmbɑr ˌstaɪrəˈkɪflʊə / (AmE), / lɪˌkwɪdˌæmbɑː ˌstɪrəsɪˈfluːə / (BrE)
A North American deciduous tree with prickly spherical fruit clusters and fragrant sap, also known as the American sweetgum.
North American sweetgum tree species

Examples

The distinctive leaves of the Liquidambar styraciflua turn brilliant colors in the autumn.
Many city streets are lined with Liquidambar styraciflua trees for their ornamental value.
The spiky fruit of the Liquidambar styraciflua can be found scattered on the ground in late fall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweetgum - North American deciduous tree with star-shaped leaves and spiky fruit, another name for Liquidambar styraciflua

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Liquidambar' and species 'styraciflua', meaning 'flowing storax'.
