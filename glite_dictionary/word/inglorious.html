inglorious

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈɡlɔriəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡlɔːriəs / (BrE)
Deserving or causing shame or a loss of honor; not bringing praise or glory.
shameful or bringing disgrace

Examples

The team's inglorious performance in the championship left the fans disappointed.
His career ended in an inglorious scandal that ruined his reputation.
They made an inglorious retreat from the battlefield after the defeat.

Synset Examples

Man … has written one of his blackest records as a destroyer on the oceanic islands
an ignominious retreat
inglorious defeat
an opprobrious monument to human greed
a shameful display of cowardice

Related Terms

  • Antonym: glorious - deserving great admiration, praise, or honor
  • Synonym: ignominious - deserving or causing public disgrace or shame
  • Synonym: shameful - deserving shame; disgraceful

Etymology

From Latin 'ingloriosus' (without glory), from 'in-' (not) + 'gloriosus' (glorious).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈɡlɔriəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡlɔːriəs / (BrE)
not bringing honor or glory; shameful or lacking distinction
not bringing honor or glory

Examples

His defeat in the competition was particularly inglorious after years of hard work.
The soldiers returned home after an inglorious campaign that achieved nothing.
He slipped away in inglorious silence, unwilling to face the crowd.

Synset Examples

some mute inglorious Milton here may rest

Related Terms

  • Antonym: glorious - bringing honor and glory
  • Synonym: shameful - deserving or causing shame

Etymology

From Latin 'ingloriosus', from 'in-' (not) + 'gloria' (glory).
