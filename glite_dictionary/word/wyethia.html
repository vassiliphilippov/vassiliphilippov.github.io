Wyethia

1. NOUN

/ waɪˈiːθiə / (AmE), / waɪˈiːθiə / (BrE)
a genus of coarse, leafy perennial plants resembling sunflowers, native to western North America
a genus of sunflower-like plants

Examples

The slopes were covered in bright yellow Wyethia blossoms during the spring.
Botanists identified the plant as a member of the Wyethia genus.
Gardeners in the western United States often cultivate Wyethia for its sunflower-like flowers.

Etymology

Named after the American explorer Nathaniel J. Wyeth.
