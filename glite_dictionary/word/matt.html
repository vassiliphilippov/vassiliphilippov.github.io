matt

1. NOUN

/ mæt / (AmE), / mæt / (BrE)
A finish or surface lacking shine, gloss, or highlights; dull and non-reflective.
dull, non-glossy finish

Examples

The walls were painted in a matt to prevent reflections from the lights.
She preferred a matt finish on her photographs rather than a glossy one.
The makeup provides a matt look that lasts all day.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gloss - A surface shine or luster that reflects light
  • Degree Higher: satin - A smooth, slightly glossy finish; between matt and gloss

Etymology

From French mat (dull, not shiny).

2. ADJ

/ mæt / (AmE), / mæt / (BrE)
(of a surface or finish) dull and not shiny; not reflecting light.
dull, non-shiny finish

Examples

She chose a matt paint for the bedroom walls to create a warm, cozy atmosphere.
The artist preferred working with matt paper because it prevented glare and reflections.
After printing, the photographs had a beautiful matt finish rather than a glossy shine.

Synset Examples

flat wall paint
a photograph with a matte finish

Related Terms

  • Antonym: glossy - shiny and lustrous in appearance
  • Synonym: matte - variant spelling meaning dull and not shiny

Etymology

From French 'mat' meaning 'dull, not shiny'.
