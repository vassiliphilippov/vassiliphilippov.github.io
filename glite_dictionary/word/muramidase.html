muramidase

1. NOUN

/ mjʊˈræməˌdeɪs / (AmE), / mjʊˈræmɪdeɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme present in saliva, sweat, and tears that breaks down the cell walls of certain bacteria
enzyme that destroys some bacterial cell walls
medical

Examples

Saliva contains muramidase, which helps protect the mouth from bacterial infections.
Scientists studied the activity of muramidase against harmful bacteria in human tears.
A deficiency of muramidase may reduce the body's first line of defense against pathogens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lysozyme - an enzyme that breaks down bacterial cell walls; synonym for muramidase

Etymology

From Latin 'murus' (wall) and the suffix '-idase', after its action on bacterial cell walls.
