lawless

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɔləs / (AmE), / ˈlɔːlɪs / (BrE)
not controlled by or obedient to laws; lacking order or legal restraint
not controlled by law

Examples

The remote valley was known for its lawless inhabitants and lack of police presence.
During the civil unrest, several neighborhoods became lawless zones overnight.
Stories of the lawless Wild West have fascinated generations of readers.

Synset Examples

lawless bands roaming the plains

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orderly - following or obeying rules and laws
  • Synonym: anarchic - lacking law or order

Etymology

From Old English 'laglēas', from 'lagu' (law) + 'lēas' (less).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɔləs / (AmE), / ˈlɔːləs / (BrE)
(of a place or situation) not governed by or obedient to laws; lacking control or order.
not governed by laws or control

Examples

The movie portrays a lawless frontier town in the Wild West.
Some criticized the internet as a lawless environment where anything goes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anarchic - without government or law; lacking order
  • Antonym: orderly - following laws or rules; controlled

3. ADJ

/ ˈlɔləs / (AmE), / ˈlɔːləs / (BrE)
not controlled by laws or lacking effective enforcement of laws; disregarding rules or laws.
not controlled by laws

Examples

The remote valley became a lawless region where bandits roamed freely.
During the rebellion, the city descended into lawless chaos for several weeks.
Outlaws once ruled the lawless frontier towns of the Old West.

Synset Examples

a wide-open town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anarchic - not governed by rules or order
  • Antonym: orderly - well organized or controlled by rules

Etymology

From Old English 'lagless' (without law), from 'law' + 'less'.
