agora

1. NOUN

/ ˈæɡərə / (AmE), / ˈaɡərə / (BrE)
an open area in ancient Greek cities that served as a gathering place for assemblies and markets
public assembly place in ancient Greece

Examples

The ruins of the agora in Athens attract many tourists each year.
In ancient Greece, citizens met at the agora to discuss important issues.
Archaeologists uncovered pottery and coins in the old agora.

Etymology

From Greek ἀγορά (agorá), meaning 'assembly' or 'gathering place'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæɡərə / (AmE), / ˈæɡərə / (BrE)
An open public space used for assemblies and markets in ancient Greek cities.
marketplace in ancient Greece

Examples

The ancient Athenians gathered in the agora to discuss politics and philosophy.
Vendors sold their goods at stalls arranged throughout the agora.
Archaeologists uncovered artifacts from the old agora in Athens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forum - the marketplace or public square of an ancient Roman city

Etymology

From Ancient Greek ἀγορά (agorá), meaning 'assembly, marketplace'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɑːɡərə / (AmE), / ˈæɡərə / (BrE)
A monetary unit of Israel, equal to one hundredth of a shekel.
Israeli monetary unit (1/100 of shekel)

Examples

The price was listed as five agorot on the packaging.
He paid the bus fare using several agorot coins.
Today, one agora is worth less than one US cent.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: shekel - the main currency unit of Israel

Etymology

From Modern Hebrew אֲגוֹרָה ('agorá), meaning 'coin' or 'money'.
