to de-aerate

1. VERB

/ diˈɛˌreɪt / (AmE), / diːˈɛəreɪt / (BrE)
to remove air or gas from a substance, often to prevent spoilage or improve quality
remove air or gas from

Examples

Before bottling, the wine must be de-aerated to preserve its quality.
They used a vacuum pump to de-aerate the solution effectively.
Manufacturers de-aerate packaged foods to extend their shelf life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to aerate - to introduce air into a material or substance

Etymology

From Latin prefix de- (removal) + aerate (to supply or introduce air).
