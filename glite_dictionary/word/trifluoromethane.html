trifluoromethane

1. NOUN

/ traɪˌflʊəroʊˈmiːθeɪn / (AmE), / traɪˌflʊərəˈmiːθeɪn / (BrE)
A colorless, odorless gaseous haloform (CHF3) similar to chloroform, used in refrigeration and as a specialty solvent.
a colorless gaseous compound, CHF3
medical

Examples

Trifluoromethane is commonly used in the electronics industry for plasma etching.
Scientists studied the environmental impact of trifluoromethane as a greenhouse gas.
The laboratory ordered a new cylinder of trifluoromethane for their experiments.

Etymology

From tri- (three), fluoro- (fluorine), and methane, indicating three fluorine atoms attached to methane.
