tailed

1. ADJ

/ teɪld / (AmE), / teɪld / (BrE)
having a tail of a particular kind or shape, often used in combination with other words (e.g., long-tailed, short-tailed).
having a tail of a certain kind

Examples

The red-tailed hawk soared above the field.
Some lizards are long-tailed, while others have very short tails.
She photographed a white-tailed deer in the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tail - the hindmost part of an animal, especially when prolonged beyond the rest of the body
