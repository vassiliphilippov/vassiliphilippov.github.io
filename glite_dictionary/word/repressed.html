repressed

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈprɛst / (AmE), / rɪˈprɛst / (BrE)
having emotions or impulses that are kept under control or not allowed to be expressed
having suppressed emotions or impulses

Examples

Her repressed anger eventually came out during the meeting.
He grew up in a family where emotions were repressed rather than discussed.
The repressed trauma from his childhood affected his relationships as an adult.

Synset Examples

their reactions were partly the product of pent-up emotions
repressed rage turned his face scarlet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppressed - kept from being revealed or expressed
  • Synonym: pent-up - not released; kept inside

Etymology

From Latin 'repressus', past participle of 'reprimere' (to press back).
