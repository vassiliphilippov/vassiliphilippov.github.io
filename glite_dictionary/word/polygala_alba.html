Polygala alba

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːlɪˈɡeɪlə ˈælbə / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈɡeɪlə ˈalbə / (BrE)
A perennial bushy herb native to central and southern United States, with white flowers that have green centers and often a purple crest, related to Seneca snakeroot.
a North American perennial herb with white flowers

Examples

The prairie was dotted with Polygala alba blooming among the tall grasses.
Botanists studied Polygala alba to compare its properties with other species in the Polygala genus.
Polygala alba is sometimes called white milkwort due to its distinctive pale blossoms.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Polygala' meaning 'much milk' and 'alba' meaning 'white', referencing its genus and the color of its flowers.
