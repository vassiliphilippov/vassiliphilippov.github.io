Inca

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋkə / (AmE), / ˈɪŋkə / (BrE)
a ruler of the Inca civilization, or a member of its royal family
ruler or royal of the Inca Empire

Examples

The Inca presided over ceremonies in the Temple of the Sun.
As an Inca, he was expected to lead both in war and in peace.
Legends say the Inca descended from the sun god.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Inca Empire - the empire founded by the Inca civilization in South America

Etymology

From Spanish 'Inca', from Quechua 'Inka' (lord, ruler).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋkə / (AmE), / ˈɪŋkə / (BrE)
a member of the indigenous Quechuan people of Peru, especially associated with the Cuzco valley and the historic Inca Empire
a Quechuan person from Peru, former Inca Empire

Examples

Many modern Peruvians are descendants of the ancient Inca people.
The Inca were skilled builders and engineers, famous for Machu Picchu.
She studied the customs and traditions of the Inca during her trip to Cuzco.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Incan - relating to the Inca people or their civilization

Etymology

From Spanish 'inca', from Quechua 'inka' (ruler, lord).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋkə / (AmE), / ˈɪŋkə / (BrE)
a member of the Quechua-speaking people of Peru who established a powerful empire centered in Cuzco from about 1100 to the early 1530s
member of the Inca Empire in Peru

Examples

Archaeologists have discovered several Inca sites high in the Andes Mountains.
The Inca were renowned for their advanced agriculture and engineering.
Many legends about the lost city of the Inca still capture people's imagination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Incan - relating to the Inca people or their civilization

Etymology

From Spanish 'Inca', from Quechua 'Inka' (lord, ruler, Inca emperor).
