to overstate

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈsteɪt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈsteɪt / (BrE)
to describe something as being larger, better, or more important than it really is; to exaggerate
exaggerate, make something seem more important

Examples

The politician tended to overstate the impact of his policies during the debate.
It would be hard to overstate the importance of regular exercise for your health.
The salesperson overstated the benefits of the product, making it sound better than it was.

Synset Examples

tended to romanticize and exaggerate this ‘gracious Old South’ imagery

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to understate - to describe something as less important or serious than it really is
  • Synonym: to exaggerate - to represent something as being larger, better, or worse than it really is

Etymology

From the prefix 'over-' (excessively) + 'state' (to express in words).
