manky

1. ADJ

/ ˈmæŋki / (AmE), / ˈmæŋki / (BrE)
dirty, unpleasant, or of poor quality; inferior or worthless
dirty or inferior in quality
informal british

Examples

The floor was manky after the party and needed a good clean.
He threw out his manky old trainers that were falling apart.
Don’t give me that manky cake—get a fresh one, please.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shabby - in poor condition through long use or lack of care
  • Degree Higher: filthy - very dirty

Etymology

Probably from Scots or northern English dialect, perhaps from French 'manqué' (lacking).
