saurel

1. NOUN

/ səˈrɛl / (AmE), / sɔːˈrɛl / (BrE)
A large, elongated, and laterally compressed food fish found in the Atlantic waters of Europe, related to the jack mackerels.
Atlantic European food fish

Examples

Fishermen along the coast often catch saurel for local markets.
The texture of saurel is prized in many Mediterranean dishes.
We grilled a fresh saurel with herbs and olive oil last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horse mackerel - another name for similar food fish from the same family

Etymology

From French 'saurel', referring to certain kinds of jack or horse mackerel species.

2. NOUN

/ sɔːˈrɛl / (AmE), / sɔːˈrɛl / (BrE)
A type of fish found off the coast of California, also known as the jack mackerel.
California food fish (jack mackerel)

Examples

Fishermen reported large catches of saurel near the Monterey coast this season.
The restaurant special featured grilled saurel served with lemon and herbs.
As a child, she often saw saurel displayed at the local seafood market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jack mackerel - A common name for the saurel; a species of mackerel found in the Pacific Ocean

Etymology

From French 'saurel', referring to a kind of fish.
