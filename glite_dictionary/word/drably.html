drably

1. ADV

/ ˈdræbli / (AmE), / ˈdræbli / (BrE)
In a dull, unimaginative, or lifeless manner; lacking brightness or interest.
in a dull or uninspired way

Examples

The room was furnished drably, with gray walls and worn-out curtains.
She spoke drably, her voice devoid of excitement or enthusiasm.
The actors were dressed drably for their roles as factory workers.

Synset Examples

she dresses drably

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drab - dull, lacking brightness or interest

Etymology

From 'drab' (dull, lacking brightness) + -ly (adverbial suffix).
