unofficially

1. ADV

/ ˌʌnəˈfɪʃəli / (AmE), / ˌʌnəˈfɪʃəli / (BrE)
in a manner that is not authorized or approved by an official body; without official approval.
without official approval

Examples

He was unofficially appointed to manage the team before the formal announcement.
Unofficially, she runs the office despite her junior title.
The event has unofficially started, even though the organizers are still setting up.

Synset Examples

he had made some money on the side

Related Terms

  • Antonym: officially - in a manner authorized or recognized by authority

Etymology

From 'unofficial' + adverbial suffix '-ly'; 'unofficial' from 'un-' (not) + 'official'.

2. ADV

/ ˌʌnəˈfɪʃəli / (AmE), / ˌʌnəˈfɪʃəli / (BrE)
In a way that is not formal or authorized by an official body.
not in an official capacity

Examples

Unofficially, he serves as the treasurer for the committee.
The decision was made unofficially before the meeting even started.
She acted unofficially as the team leader during the project.

Synset Examples

unofficially, he serves as the treasurer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: officially - in an official manner; with formal authorization

Etymology

From 'unofficial' + '-ly'; 'unofficial' from un- (not) + official.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
