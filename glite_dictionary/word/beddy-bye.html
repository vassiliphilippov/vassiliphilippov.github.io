beddy-bye

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛdi ˌbaɪ / (AmE), / ˈbɛdi ˌbaɪ / (BrE)
A childish or affectionate term for bedtime or going to sleep.
child's term for bedtime
informal

Examples

The twins brushed their teeth and got ready for beddy-bye.
After a long day at the zoo, Jimmy was fast asleep at beddy-bye.
She read her daughter a story before beddy-bye.

Synset Examples

He kissed her goodnight before beddy-bye.
She tucked her son in for his beddy-bye.
It's time for little ones to say their beddy-byes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bedtime - the usual time for going to bed or sleeping

Etymology

Formed by reduplication and playful alteration of 'bed', with 'bye' as in 'byebye' (goodbye, sleep).
