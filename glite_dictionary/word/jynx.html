Jynx

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪŋks / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪŋks / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the woodpecker family, known as wrynecks, found in Europe and Asia.
genus of wryneck birds

Examples

The Eurasian Jynx is known for its ability to twist its neck in a snake-like manner.
Several species within the genus Jynx are found throughout Europe and Asia.
Ornithologists studied the migration patterns of Jynx during the spring season.

Etymology

From Latin 'Jynx', from Ancient Greek 'iunx' (wryneck).
