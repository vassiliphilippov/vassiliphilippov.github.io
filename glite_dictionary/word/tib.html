TiB

1. NOUN

/ ˌtiːˈaɪ ˈbiː / (AmE), / ˌtiːˈaɪ ˈbiː / (BrE)
A unit of digital information equal to 1,024 gibibytes or 2^40 bytes (1,099,511,627,776 bytes).
unit of digital information (1,024 GiB)

Examples

The new server has a storage capacity of 8 TiB for its databases.
Backup files totaling one TiB took several hours to transfer over the network.
The engineer recommended upgrading to a 16 TiB hard drive for future expansion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tebibyte - the full form of TiB; unit of digital information equal to 2^40 bytes
  • Degree Lower: gigabyte - unit of digital information equal to 10^9 bytes (1,000,000,000 bytes)
  • Degree Higher: PiB - unit of digital information equal to 1,024 TiB

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'tebibyte', formed from 'tera' (trillion) and 'binary byte'.
