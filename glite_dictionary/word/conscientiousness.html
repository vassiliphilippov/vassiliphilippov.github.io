conscientiousness

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.ʃiˈen.ʃəs.nəs / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.ʃiˈen.ʃəs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being careful to do things correctly and thoroughly, especially with attention to detail and a strong sense of responsibility
quality of being thorough and responsible

Examples

Her conscientiousness made her an excellent employee, as she always double-checked her work.
The surgeon's conscientiousness is evident in every careful movement during operations.
Parents praised the teacher for her conscientiousness in preparing detailed lesson plans.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: carelessness - lack of attention or responsibility
  • Synonym: diligence - careful and persistent work or effort

Etymology

From Latin 'conscientia' (knowledge within oneself) + English suffix '-ousness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.ʃiˈen.ʃəs.nəs / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.ʃiˈen.ʃəs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being governed by one's conscience, especially in doing one's work or duties thoroughly and with attention to detail
carefulness and moral responsibility

Examples

Her conscientiousness made her an exemplary student who always submitted perfect assignments.
Employers value conscientiousness in workers who reliably complete their tasks with great care.
Because of his conscientiousness, he was trusted with handling sensitive company information.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diligence - careful and persistent work or effort
  • Antonym: carelessness - lack of attention or thought to avoid harm or errors

Etymology

Formed from 'conscientious' + '-ness', from Latin *conscientia* (knowledge within oneself).
