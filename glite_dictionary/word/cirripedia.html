Cirripedia

1. NOUN

/ sɪrəˈpiːdiə / (AmE), / sɪrɪˈpiːdɪə / (BrE)
A subclass of marine crustaceans comprising barnacles, which attach themselves to underwater surfaces.
subclass of marine crustaceans (barnacles)
medical

Examples

Many marine biologists study Cirripedia to understand their unique reproductive strategies.
The hull was covered in Cirripedia after months at sea.
Charles Darwin did extensive research on Cirripedia during his early scientific career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barnacle - a type of marine crustacean that attaches to surfaces

Etymology

From Latin 'cirripedium', from 'cirrus' (curl, tuft) and 'pes' (foot), relating to their feathery appendages.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɪr.ɪˈpiː.di.ə / (AmE), / ˌsɪr.ɪˈpiː.di.ə / (BrE)
a subclass of crustaceans consisting of barnacles, which are marine animals that attach themselves permanently to surfaces such as rocks, ships, or piers
a subclass of crustaceans: barnacles

Examples

Many marine biologists study Cirripedia to better understand how barnacles adapt to intertidal environments.
The shells of old ships are often covered in Cirripedia colonies.
Researchers collected specimens of Cirripedia from the rocky shores for classification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barnacle - a marine crustacean of the subclass Cirripedia that attaches itself to surfaces

Etymology

From Latin 'cirriped', from 'cirrus' (curl, tendril) and 'pes, pedis' (foot), referring to their curled feeding appendages.
