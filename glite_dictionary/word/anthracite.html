anthracite

1. NOUN

/ ˈænθrəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈanθrəˌsʌɪt / (BrE)
a hard, natural, high-grade coal that burns slowly with little smoke and produces intense heat
hard, high-grade coal

Examples

The fireplace was designed to burn anthracite and keep the whole house warm during winter.
Mining companies extracted large quantities of anthracite from the Appalachian mountains.
Unlike bituminous coal, anthracite produces much less smoke and soot when burned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coal - a black or dark brown combustible mineral substance

Etymology

From Greek anthrakítēs (like coal), from ánthraks (coal).
