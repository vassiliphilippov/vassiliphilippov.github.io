paradox

1. NOUN

/ ˈpærəˌdɑks / (AmE), / ˈparədɒks / (BrE)
A statement or situation that seems to contradict itself or leads to a logical contradiction, often challenging accepted opinions or logic.
self-contradictory statement or situation

Examples

The famous liar’s paradox states, 'This statement is false.'
He pointed out a paradox at the heart of the theory.
The paradox of saving money by spending it puzzled the students.

Synset Examples

‘I always lie’ is a paradox because if it is true it must be false

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contradiction - a statement that is logically inconsistent or directly opposed
  • Synonym: absurdity - something that is wildly unreasonable or illogical
  • Antonym: truism - a statement that is obviously true and says nothing new

Etymology

From Greek *paradoxos* (contrary to expectation), from *para-* (beyond) + *doxa* (opinion).
