scraper

1. NOUN

/ ˈskreɪpər / (AmE), / ˈskreɪpə / (BrE)
a hand tool with a flat or slightly curved blade, used for removing material from a surface by scraping
hand tool for scraping surfaces

Examples

He used a scraper to remove the old paint from the window frame.
The chef picked up a scraper to clean the griddle after cooking.
You’ll need a stiff scraper to get rid of the gum stuck to the table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spatula - a flat-bladed tool used for spreading or scraping
  • Synonym: trowel - a small hand tool with a flat blade, used for applying and spreading plaster or similar materials

Etymology

From Middle English 'scrapen' (to scrape) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
