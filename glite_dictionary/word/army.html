army

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrmi / (AmE), / ˈɑːmi / (BrE)
The permanent organized military land forces of a nation or state.
a nation's organized land military force

Examples

The army was deployed to the border in response to the crisis.
After joining the army, she underwent months of rigorous training.
The army celebrated its anniversary with a grand military parade.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: navy - the branch of a nation's armed services that conducts military operations at sea

Etymology

From Latin 'armata' (armed force), from 'arma' (arms, weapons), via Old French 'armee'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrmi / (AmE), / ˈɑːmi / (BrE)
A large organized group of people joined together for a particular purpose.
large organized group of people

Examples

An army of volunteers helped rebuild the community after the storm.
The company mobilized an army of salespeople to promote the new product.
She was greeted by an army of reporters when she left the courthouse.

Etymology

From Latin 'armata', meaning 'armed force', via Old French 'armee'.
