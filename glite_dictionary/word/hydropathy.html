hydropathy

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdrɑːpəθi / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒpəθi / (BrE)
the treatment of disease by the use of water, both internally and externally
treatment of disease with water
medical

Examples

In the 19th century, hydropathy became popular as a natural cure for many ailments.
Some health resorts still offer hydropathy treatments involving baths and water therapies.
She studied the effects of hydropathy on chronic joint pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrotherapy - the use of exercises in water as part of treatment

Etymology

From Greek 'hydro-' (water) + 'pathos' (suffering).
