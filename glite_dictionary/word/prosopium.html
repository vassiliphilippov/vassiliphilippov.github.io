Prosopium

1. NOUN

/ prəˈsoʊpiəm / (AmE), / prəˈsəʊpiəm / (BrE)
A genus of North American freshwater whitefish in the salmon family (Salmonidae).
a genus of freshwater whitefish

Examples

The lake trout and Prosopium species are both found in cold northern lakes.
Biologists studied the migration patterns of the Prosopium population in the river.
In North America, Prosopium is an important genus for sport fishing enthusiasts.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek prosōpon (face), used in taxonomy.

2. PROPN

/ prəˈsoʊpiəm / (AmE), / prəˈsəʊpiəm / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater whitefishes native to North America and northern Eurasia.
genus of freshwater whitefishes

Examples

Several species within the genus Prosopium are important for commercial fishing in North America.
The Arctic grayling and certain Prosopium species share overlapping habitats in northern lakes.
Scientists studied the distribution of Prosopium in response to climate change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whitefish - a common name for several species of freshwater fish in the salmon family

Etymology

From Greek 'prosōpon' (face), referring to features of the fish.
