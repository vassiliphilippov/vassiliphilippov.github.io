henna

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛnə / (AmE), / ˈhɛnə / (BrE)
a reddish-brown dye made from the powdered leaves of a tropical shrub, used especially to color hair and skin
reddish-brown dye from a shrub

Examples

She used henna to color her hair a vibrant shade of auburn.
Traditional brides in India often decorate their hands with henna designs.
Some people choose henna as a natural alternative to chemical hair dyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dye - a substance used to add color to or change the color of something

Etymology

From Arabic ḥinnāʾ (حناء), referring to the dye plant.

2. VERB

/ ˈhɛnə / (AmE), / ˈhɛnə / (BrE)
to dye or treat (hair) with henna, a reddish-brown vegetable dye derived from the henna plant
dye hair with henna

Examples

Every summer, she hennas her hair a deep red to match the holiday mood.
I decided to henna my hair after seeing how beautiful the color looked on my friend.
He hennaed his beard to cover the gray strands.

Synset Examples

She hennas her hair every month

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dye - to color something with a dye
  • Antonym: bleach - to remove color from hair (the opposite process)

Etymology

From Arabic 'ḥinnāʾ', denoting the henna plant and its dye.
