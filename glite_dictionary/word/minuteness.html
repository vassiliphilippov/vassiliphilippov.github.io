minuteness

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈnuːtnəs / (AmE), / mɪˈnjuːtnɪs / (BrE)
Great precision or attention to small details.
great precision; attention to detail

Examples

The minuteness of his measurements impressed all the scientists at the conference.
She described the antique jewelry with such minuteness that listeners could picture every tiny detail.
Legal contracts often require a level of minuteness that can be tedious but necessary.

Synset Examples

he examined the essay with the greatest minuteness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precision - The quality of being exact, accurate, or careful
  • Synonym: exactness - The state of being precise and accurate

Etymology

From minute (precise, small) + -ness (forming nouns denoting quality or state).

2. NOUN

/ maɪˈnuːtnəs / (AmE), / maɪˈnjuːtnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being very small in size or extent
the state of being very small

Examples

The minuteness of the insect made it nearly invisible to the naked eye.
She described the process with extraordinary minuteness and attention to detail.
The artist was admired for the minuteness of his miniature sculptures.

Synset Examples

hence the minuteness of detail in the painting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smallness - the quality of being small in size
  • Antonym: largeness - the state or quality of being large

Etymology

From minute + -ness, after Latin minutus (small).
