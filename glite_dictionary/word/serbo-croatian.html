Serbo-Croatian

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɜrboʊ kroʊˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɜːbəʊ krəʊˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the South Slavic language historically spoken by Serbs, Croats, Bosniaks, and Montenegrins, with Serbian typically written in Cyrillic and Croatian in Latin script
South Slavic language of Serbs and Croats

Examples

She is fluent in both Serbo-Croatian and English.
Many books from the region are written in Serbo-Croatian.
The university offers a course in Serbo-Croatian literature.

Etymology

From Serb (referring to the Serbs) + Croat (referring to the Croats), reflecting the language shared by the two groups.
