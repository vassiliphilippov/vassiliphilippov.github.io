Kurdistan

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɜrdəˈstæn / (AmE), / ˌkɜːdɪˈstɑːn / (BrE)
an oriental rug woven by Kurds, known for its fine colors and durability
oriental rug woven by Kurds

Examples

He displayed his prized Kurdistan in the center of the living room.
Collectors often seek out an authentic Kurdistan for their vibrant patterns.
The durability of a Kurdistan makes it a valuable addition to any rug collection.

Etymology

From the name of the Kurdistan region, inhabited by Kurds.

2. PROPN

/ ˌkɜːrdɪˈstɑːn / (AmE), / ˌkɜːdɪˈstɑːn / (BrE)
a large region in the Middle East, traditionally inhabited by Kurds, spanning parts of Turkey, Iraq, Iran, and Syria
region of the Middle East inhabited by Kurds

Examples

Many Kurds dream of an independent Kurdistan where they have their own government and rights.
The mountainous terrain of Kurdistan has shaped the culture and history of its people.
Tourists are increasingly drawn to the scenic landscapes of Kurdistan despite regional challenges.

Etymology

From Persian 'Kurd' (referring to the Kurdish people) + 'stan' (land, region) meaning 'land of the Kurds'.
