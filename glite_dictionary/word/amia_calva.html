Amia calva

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.mi.ə ˈkæl.və / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.mi.ə ˈkæl.və / (BrE)
A primitive, long-bodied carnivorous freshwater fish with a very long dorsal fin, found in sluggish waters of North America; also known as the bowfin.
species of freshwater fish (bowfin)

Examples

The Amia calva is often studied for its unique evolutionary features.
Fishermen occasionally catch Amia calva in the quiet lakes of the Midwest.
Unlike many fish, Amia calva can breathe air thanks to a specialized swim bladder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowfin - common name for Amia calva, a North American freshwater fish

Etymology

From Latin: amia (a type of fish) + calva (bald), scientific name used in taxonomy.
