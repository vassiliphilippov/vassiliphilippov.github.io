2. NOUN

/ soʊˌlɛnˈɑːpsɪs / (AmE), / ˌsɒlɪˈnɒpsɪs / (BrE)
A genus of ants, commonly known as fire ants, that are known for their aggressive behavior and painful stings.
genus of fire ants

Examples

The invasive Solenopsis species have disrupted native ecosystems in several countries.
A colony of Solenopsis can inflict dozens of painful stings when disturbed.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Solenopsis in the Amazon rainforest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire ant - a common name for ants of the genus Solenopsis, known for their painful sting

Etymology

From Greek *solen* (pipe, channel) + *opsis* (appearance).
