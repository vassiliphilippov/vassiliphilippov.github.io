thither

1. ADV

/ ˈðɪðɚ / (AmE), / ˈðɪðə / (BrE)
to or toward that place; away from the speaker (now chiefly literary or archaic)
to or toward that place (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The travelers journeyed thither in hopes of finding shelter before nightfall.
She beckoned to me, saying, 'Come thither and see what I have found.'
In the poem, the hero rides thither to confront his destiny.

Synset Examples

go there around noon!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hither - to or toward this place; toward the speaker (archaic)
  • Synonym: there - in, at, or to that place or position

Etymology

From Old English 'þider' (to there).
