radius

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdiəs / (AmE), / ˈreɪdɪəs / (BrE)
A straight line extending from the center of a circle or sphere to its outer edge or surface.
line from center to edge of circle or sphere

Examples

The radius of the circle is 5 centimeters.
Draw a line from the center to any point on the edge; that's the radius.
The planet's radius makes it twice as big as Earth.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: diameter - A straight line passing through the center of a circle or sphere, reaching from one side to the other

Etymology

From Latin 'radius' (staff, spoke, ray).

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdiəs / (AmE), / ˈreɪdɪəs / (BrE)
A circular area around a central point, defined by the length from that point to the edge.
circular area defined by distance from center

Examples

The police set up checkpoints within a radius of five miles from the bank.
Homes within a radius of two kilometers were evacuated as a precaution.
Wildflowers grow in abundance within a radius of the old oak tree.

Synset Examples

they located it within a radius of 2 miles

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: diameter - a straight line passing from side to side through the center of a circle

Etymology

From Latin radius (staff, spoke of a wheel, ray).

3. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdiəs / (AmE), / ˈreɪdɪəs / (BrE)
A spoke or supporting bar that extends from the hub to the rim of a wheel.
spoke of a wheel

Examples

Each radius in the bicycle wheel is carefully tensioned for balance and strength.
The engineer examined the damaged radius to determine if the wheel was still safe to use.
Broken radii can lead to wobbling or even failure of the wheel while in motion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoke - any of the bars connecting the hub to the rim of a wheel

Etymology

From Latin radius (rod, spoke, ray).

5. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdiəs / (AmE), / ˈreɪdɪəs / (BrE)
the outer and slightly shorter of the two bones of the human forearm, extending from the elbow to the wrist on the same side as the thumb
outer bone of the forearm
medical

Examples

The radius is located on the lateral side of the forearm, parallel to the ulna.
After the fall, she fractured her radius and needed a cast for six weeks.
Doctors showed him an X-ray image of the broken radius near his wrist.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ulna - the inner and longer of the two bones of the forearm

Etymology

From Latin 'radius' (ray, spoke, rod).
