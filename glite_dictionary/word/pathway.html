pathway

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæθˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːθweɪ / (BrE)
a bundle of myelinated nerve fibers in the brain that transmits signals along a specific route
bundle of nerve fibers in the brain
medical

Examples

The optic pathway carries visual information from the retina to the brain.
Damage to the motor pathway can result in impaired movement.
Researchers discovered a new neural pathway involved in memory formation.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæθˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːθˌweɪ / (BrE)
a path or track that has been made by the passage of people or animals, especially one that is beaten down by frequent use
a trodden path or track

Examples

They followed the pathway through the dense forest to reach the river.
A narrow pathway wound its way between the gardens and the old stone wall.
After years of use, the pathway across the field was clearly visible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: path - a way or track laid down for walking

Etymology

From Middle English 'pathway', a compound of 'path' + 'way'.
