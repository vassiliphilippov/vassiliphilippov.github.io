paediatrician

1. NOUN

/ ˌpiːdiəˈtrɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpiːdiəˈtrɪʃən / (BrE)
A doctor who specializes in the medical care of children and babies.
doctor specializing in care of children
medical british

Examples

The paediatrician examined the baby thoroughly before giving a diagnosis.
After medical school, she decided to become a paediatrician and work with children.
Parents often consult a paediatrician for advice on vaccinations and nutrition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pediatrician - doctor specializing in children's medical care (US spelling)

Etymology

From Greek 'pais, paidos' (child) and 'iatros' (doctor) via French 'pédiatrie'.
