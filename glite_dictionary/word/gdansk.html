Gdansk

1. PROPN

/ ɡəˈdænsk / (AmE), / ɡəˈdansk / (BrE)
A port city in northern Poland near the mouth of the Vistula River, located on the Baltic Sea.
port city in northern Poland

Examples

The historic city of Gdansk attracts thousands of tourists each year.
Shipyards in Gdansk played a crucial role in Poland's political history.
After visiting Warsaw, they traveled north to see Gdansk.

Etymology

From Polish 'Gdańsk', first attested in the 10th century.
