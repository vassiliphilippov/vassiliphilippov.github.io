to oscillate

1. VERB

/ ˈɑː.səˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒs.ɪ.leɪt / (BrE)
to move or swing back and forth in a regular rhythm
move back and forth regularly

Examples

The fan blades oscillate to distribute air throughout the room.
When the pendulum was set in motion, it began to oscillate smoothly.
The laser beam can oscillate between two frequencies under certain conditions.

Synset Examples

the needle on the meter was oscillating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swing - to move back and forth or from side to side while hanging from a fixed point
  • Synonym: to vibrate - to move back and forth rapidly

Etymology

From Latin oscillare (to swing), from oscillum (a little mask, swing).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01882056-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈɑːsəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒsɪleɪt / (BrE)
to repeatedly change your opinion, feelings, or position about something; to waver between different options or actions
to waver between options or positions

Examples

During the meeting, she oscillated between supporting the proposal and raising concerns about its feasibility.
He tends to oscillate when making important decisions, never fully committing to one choice or another.
Public opinion can oscillate rapidly in response to major political events.

Synset Examples

He oscillates between accepting the new position and retirement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waver - to go back and forth in decision or opinion
  • Synonym: to vacillate - to alternate or waver between different opinions or actions
  • Antonym: to decide - to make a final choice or judgment

Etymology

From Latin 'oscillare' (to swing), from 'oscillum' (little face or mask hung to swing in the wind).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02711906-v
Synset POS: v
