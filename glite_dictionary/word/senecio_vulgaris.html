Senecio vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ səˈniːsi.oʊ vʌlˈɡɛrɪs / (AmE), / sɛˈniːsɪ.əʊ vʌlˈɡɛːrɪs / (BrE)
A common Eurasian weed with small yellow flower heads, often found in cultivated or disturbed ground.
a common Eurasian weed with yellow flowers

Examples

Senecio vulgaris often invades gardens and fields, making it a persistent weed for many gardeners.
Researchers studied the spread of Senecio vulgaris in different soil types across Europe.
You can identify Senecio vulgaris by its small yellow flowers and deeply lobed leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groundsel - common name for Senecio vulgaris

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Senecio' (from 'senex', old man) and species 'vulgaris' (common).
