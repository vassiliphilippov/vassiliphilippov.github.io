impulsion

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpʌlʒən / (AmE), / ɪmˈpʌlʃən / (BrE)
a force or influence that makes something move or prompts action
a driving force or influence

Examples

The sudden impulsion from behind caused the car to move forward.
His decision was guided by an inner impulsion rather than careful thought.
The river's strong current provided enough impulsion to turn the waterwheel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propulsion - the action of driving or pushing something forward
  • Antonym: inertia - a tendency to do nothing or to remain unchanged; resistance to change or motion

Etymology

From French 'impulsion', from Latin 'impulsio', from 'impellere' (to drive on, urge).

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpʌlʃən / (AmE), / ɪmˈpʌlʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act of applying force suddenly; a sudden driving or propelling force
sudden application of force

Examples

The sudden impulsion of the car sent it skidding across the ice.
She felt an impulsion to speak, though she stayed silent.
A strong impulsion from behind pushed the door open abruptly.

Synset Examples

the impulse knocked him over

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impulse - a sudden strong urge or force; a driving force
  • Degree Higher: momentum - the impetus and driving force gained by the development of a process or course of events

Etymology

From French *impulsion* and Latin *impulsio* (a striking against, impulse).
