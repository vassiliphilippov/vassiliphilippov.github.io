cyanuramide

1. NOUN

/ saɪˌæn.jəˈræm.aɪd / (AmE), / saɪˌanjʊˈræmɪd / (BrE)
a white crystalline organic compound, chiefly used in the manufacture of melamine resins
white crystalline compound used in melamine resins
medical

Examples

Cyanuramide is commonly used as a starting material in the production of durable melamine dinnerware.
The researcher measured the solubility of cyanuramide in various organic solvents.
During the reaction, cyanuramide was converted into its corresponding resin form.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melamine - a chemical compound used as a resin or plastic

Etymology

From cyanuric (from 'cyanogen' and 'uric') + amide, indicating its chemical structure.
