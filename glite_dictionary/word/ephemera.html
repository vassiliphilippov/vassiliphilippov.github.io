ephemera

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈfɛmərə / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛmərə / (BrE)
Things that exist or are enjoyed for only a short time, especially items of collectible memorabilia, originally those expected to have only short-term usefulness.
objects or items that are short-lived

Examples

At the flea market, she was drawn to ephemera like old tickets and postcards.
The artist's installation explored themes of memory and ephemera.
Collectors value certain pieces of ephemera because of their historical significance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transience - the state or fact of lasting only for a short time
  • Antonym: permanence - the state or quality of lasting or remaining unchanged

Etymology

From Greek ephēmeros (lasting only a day).
