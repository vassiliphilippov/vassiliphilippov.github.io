important

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɔrtənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɔːtənt / (BrE)
having or showing an attitude or manner that suggests self-importance or awareness of one's high status
showing self-importance in manner

Examples

He walked into the room with a very important expression on his face.
The manager strode past us, taking long important strides to his office.
She recited the instructions in a particularly important manner, as if everyone depended on her.

Synset Examples

recited the decree with an important air
took long important strides in the direction of his office

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-important - showing an exaggerated sense of one's own value or importance

Etymology

From Latin importans, present participle of importare (to bring in).

WordNet ID: oewn-01544055-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɔrtənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɔːtnt / (BrE)
having great authority or influence; possessing power or a commanding position
having authority or influence

Examples

He is an important figure in the company due to his leadership role.
Many important officials attended the international summit.
Being the eldest, she always felt important in family decisions.

Synset Examples

an important official
the captain's authoritative manner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powerful - having great power or influence
  • Synonym: influential - having the power to affect people or events
  • Antonym: insignificant - lacking in importance or influence

Etymology

From Latin importans, present participle of importare (to signify, to bring in).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01836121-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɔrtnt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɔːtənt / (BrE)
having great significance or value; of considerable consequence or influence
of great significance or value

Examples

Family is the most important thing in his life.
It is important to follow the instructions carefully.
The meeting will cover several important issues facing the company.

Synset Examples

important people
the important questions of the day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: significant - having meaning or importance
  • Synonym: crucial - decisive or critical in success or failure
  • Antonym: trivial - of little value or importance

Etymology

From Latin 'importare' (to bring in), via Old French 'important'.

of great significance or value; vital, especially in influencing an outcome or resolving a crisis
of great significance or value; crucial

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: critical - having a decisive importance to the outcome of something
  • Antonym: insignificant - not important or lacking significance

Etymology

From Latin importāns, present participle of importāre (to bring in, to signify).
