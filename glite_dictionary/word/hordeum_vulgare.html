Hordeum vulgare

1. NOUN

/ hɔrˈdiːəm vʌlˈgɛəri / (AmE), / hɔːˈdiːəm vʌlˈgɛːri / (BrE)
a species of grass cultivated for its edible grain, commonly known as barley, used in food, animal feed, and in making malt beverages
species of grass; barley grain plant

Examples

Hordeum vulgare is one of the world's oldest cultivated cereals.
The grains of Hordeum vulgare are used to make malt for brewing beer.
Farmers often grow Hordeum vulgare for use as both livestock feed and human food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barley - the common name for Hordeum vulgare, a cereal grain

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Hordeum' meaning 'barley' and 'vulgare' meaning 'common'.
