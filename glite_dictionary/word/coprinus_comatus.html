Coprinus comatus

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈpraɪnəs koʊˈmeɪtəs / (AmE), / kɒˈprɪnəs kəʊˈmeɪtəs / (BrE)
a common edible mushroom with a long, shaggy white cap and black spores, also known as the shaggy ink cap or lawyer's wig
a shaggy white edible mushroom

Examples

Many foragers collect Coprinus comatus in the wild because it is a delicious edible mushroom.
The Coprinus comatus is easy to recognize by its shaggy white cap and tall, slender shape.
If left unpicked, Coprinus comatus quickly liquefies into a black, inky substance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shaggy ink cap - another common name for Coprinus comatus
  • Degree Higher: mushroom - fleshy spore-bearing fruiting body of a fungus

Etymology

From Latin: Coprinus (from 'dung', referring to its habitat) and comatus ('having hair', referring to its shaggy cap).
