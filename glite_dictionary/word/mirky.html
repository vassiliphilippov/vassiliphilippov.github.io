mirky

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɜːrki / (AmE), / ˈmɜːki / (BrE)
dark, gloomy, or obscure; lacking in clarity or brightness.
dark or gloomy

Examples

The forest was especially mirky after the sun went down.
He peered into the mirky water, searching for any sign of movement.
The old castle was shrouded in a mirky fog that made it look haunted.

Synset Examples

a murky dungeon
murky rooms lit by smoke-blackened lamps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murky - dark, gloomy, hard to see through
  • Antonym: bright - giving out or reflecting much light; shining

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'murky', from Middle English mirke (darkness, gloom), from Old Norse myrkr (darkness).
