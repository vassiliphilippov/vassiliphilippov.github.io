purpose

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːpəs / (AmE), / ˈpɜːpəs / (BrE)
the quality of having strong determination to achieve something; firmness of resolve
determination to achieve something

Examples

Despite many obstacles, she pursued her goals with remarkable purpose.
He spoke and acted with a sense of purpose that inspired the whole team.
Her purpose never wavered, even when things became difficult.

Synset Examples

his determination showed in his every movement
he is a man of purpose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: determination - firmness of purpose; resoluteness
  • Synonym: resolve - strong determination to do something
  • Antonym: indecision - the inability to decide; lack of firmness

Etymology

From Old French 'porpos', from Latin 'propositum' (thing proposed, plan).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɝː.pəs / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.pəs / (BrE)
the reason for which something is done or created, or for which someone acts; an intended or desired result
reason for doing something; intended goal

Examples

The purpose of this study is to investigate how diet influences health outcomes.
She explained her purpose in applying for the job during the interview.
Each tool in the kit has a specific purpose and should be used accordingly.

Synset Examples

his intent was to provide a new translation
good intentions are not enough
it was created with the conscious aim of answering immediate needs
he made no secret of his designs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intention - an aim or plan; what you intend to do
  • Synonym: aim - a purpose or goal that you are trying to achieve
  • Synonym: goal - the object of a person's ambition or effort; an aim or desired result
  • Antonym: accident - an event that happens by chance or unintentionally

Etymology

From Old French 'porpos', from Latin 'propositum' (a thing proposed), from 'proponere' (to put forth).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05991037-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːpəs / (AmE), / ˈpɜːpəs / (BrE)
the reason for which something is done or created, or for which something exists; the intended use or function of something
intended use or function

Examples

The main purpose of this meeting is to discuss the budget cuts.
Each tool in the kit serves a specific purpose.
He questioned the purpose of the new regulations.

Synset Examples

the function of an auger is to bore holes
ballet is beautiful but what use is it?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: function - the special purpose or activity for which a thing exists or is used
  • Synonym: use - the action of using something or the purpose for which something is used

Etymology

From Old French 'porpos', from Latin 'propositum' (something set forth).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05156572-n
Synset POS: n
