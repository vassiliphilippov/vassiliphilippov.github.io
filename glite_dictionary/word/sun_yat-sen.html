Sun Yat-sen

1. PROPN

/ sʊn jæt ˈsɛn / (AmE), / sʌn jæt ˈsɛn / (BrE)
(1866–1925) A Chinese statesman and revolutionary leader who founded the Kuomintang and played a key role in overthrowing the Manchu dynasty, establishing the foundations for modern China.
Chinese revolutionary and political leader

Examples

Sun Yat-sen is often called the 'Father of Modern China' for his role in establishing the republic.
During his studies in Hong Kong, Sun Yat-sen developed his revolutionary ideas.
The mausoleum of Sun Yat-sen in Nanjing is a popular historical site.

Etymology

Mandarin Chinese 孫中山 (Sūn Zhōngshān); his birth name was Sun Wen.
