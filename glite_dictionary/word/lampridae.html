Lampridae

1. NOUN

/ læmˈpraɪdiː / (AmE), / lamˈprɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, colorful, deep-bodied marine fishes known as opahs, found in most oceans.
family of fishes (opahs)

Examples

Fishes of the family Lampridae are known for their striking coloration and large, rounded bodies.
The Lampridae are distributed throughout the world's oceans, especially in temperate and tropical waters.
Researchers studied the evolutionary adaptations of Lampridae to deep-sea environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opah - a large, colorful oceanic fish from the Lampridae family

Etymology

From Greek *lampros* (bright, brilliant), reflecting the fishes' bright coloring; standard family suffix -idae.
