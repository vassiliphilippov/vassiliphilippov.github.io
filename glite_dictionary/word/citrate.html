citrate

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪtreɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪtreɪt / (BrE)
to cause to form a salt or ester of citric acid
form a salt or ester of citric acid
medical

Examples

Chemists often citrate calcium in the lab to study its solubility.
You should citrate the solution before adding the final reagents.
Certain laboratory procedures involve citrating plasma samples to prevent clotting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acetate - a salt or ester of acetic acid

Etymology

From citric, referring to citric acid, and -ate, a suffix used in chemistry for salts and esters.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪˌtreɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪtreɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester of citric acid, commonly used in chemistry and medicine
a salt or ester of citric acid

Examples

Sodium citrate is commonly used as a food preservative and flavoring agent.
The doctor prescribed potassium citrate to help prevent kidney stones.
Some laboratory experiments require the use of buffered citrate solutions.

Etymology

From citric (acid) + -ate; citric from Latin 'citrus' (citron tree).
