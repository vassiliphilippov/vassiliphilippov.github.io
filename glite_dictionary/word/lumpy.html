lumpy

1. ADJ

/ ˈlʌm.pi / (AmE), / ˈlʌm.pi / (BrE)
having or containing small, solid pieces or lumps, often making the surface rough or uneven.
full of lumps or small clumps

Examples

The sauce turned out lumpy because I forgot to stir it properly.
She didn't like the lumpy texture of the mashed potatoes.
After sitting overnight, the porridge became lumpy and thick.

Synset Examples

the dumplings were chunky pieces of uncooked dough

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; free from lumps
  • Synonym: chunky - thick, containing or resembling lumps or chunks

Etymology

From lump + -y, indicating 'having lumps'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlʌmpi / (AmE), / ˈlʌmpi / (BrE)
having small, hard pieces or areas within it; not smooth or even in texture
not smooth; full of lumps

Examples

The cake batter turned out lumpy because I didn't mix it well enough.
After sitting in the fridge overnight, the soup became thick and lumpy.
He tried to smooth out the lumpy mashed potatoes before serving them.

Synset Examples

lumpy gravy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - having an even, regular surface or consistency; not lumpy

Etymology

From 'lump' (Middle English lumpe), meaning a compact mass or piece, plus the adjective-forming suffix '-y'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00915604-s
Synset POS: s
