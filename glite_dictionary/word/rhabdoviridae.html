Rhabdoviridae

1. NOUN

/ ræbˌdoʊvəˈraɪəˌdi / (AmE), / ræbdəʊvɪˈrɪədiː / (BrE)
A family of rod-shaped viruses, some of which are transmitted by arthropods and can cause diseases in animals and plants.
family of rod-shaped viruses
medical

Examples

The Rhabdoviridae family includes the rabies virus, a fatal disease in mammals.
Scientists are studying Rhabdoviridae to understand how these viruses infect crops through insect vectors.
Several members of the Rhabdoviridae are known to infect fish, causing economic losses in aquaculture.

Etymology

From Greek 'rhabdos' (rod) and '-viridae', a suffix for virus families.
