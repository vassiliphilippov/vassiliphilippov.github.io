terseness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrsnəs / (AmE), / ˈtɜːsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being brief and concise in expression; using few words to convey much meaning
conciseness in style or expression

Examples

The editor admired the terseness of the author's style, which conveyed deep meaning in just a few words.
His speech was remarkable for its terseness, avoiding unnecessary detail and getting straight to the point.
In legal writing, terseness can help clarify complex arguments for the reader.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conciseness - the quality of being concise
  • Antonym: verbosity - the quality of using more words than needed

Etymology

From Latin 'tersus', past participle of 'tergere' (to wipe), influencing 'terse' (polished, concise) + '-ness'.
