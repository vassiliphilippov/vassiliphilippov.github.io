giblet

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪblət / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪblɪt / (BrE)
the edible internal organs of a bird, especially a chicken or turkey, such as the heart, liver, and gizzard, often used in cooking
edible internal organs of a bird

Examples

The cook added the giblet mixture to the gravy for extra flavor.
After cleaning the chicken, she set aside the giblet for the stuffing.
Some people prefer to discard the giblet, but others use it for making stock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offal - the entrails and internal organs of an animal used as food

Etymology

From Old French *gibelet* (a stew of game).
