paeony

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːəni / (AmE), / ˈpiːəni / (BrE)
any of various garden plants with large, showy flowers in red, pink, or white, widely cultivated for their beauty
flowering plant with large, showy blooms

Examples

The paeony is prized in gardens for its extravagant blossoms every spring.
She arranged a bouquet of white paeonies for the wedding table.
As a child, he helped his grandmother tend the paeony beds in their backyard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peony - flowering plant with large, showy flowers (alternate spelling)

Etymology

From Latin *paeonia*, from Greek *paiōnia*, named after Paeon, the physician of the gods in Greek mythology.
