Ligustrum vulgare

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈɡʌstrəm vəlˈɡɛr / (AmE), / lɪˈɡʌstrəm vʊlˈɡɑːreɪ / (BrE)
A deciduous or semi-evergreen shrub, commonly known as wild privet, widely grown for hedges.
a shrub used for hedges; wild privet

Examples

The Ligustrum vulgare was chosen for the new garden hedge.
Many birds are attracted to the berries of Ligustrum vulgare in autumn.
She trimmed the Ligustrum vulgare to keep the hedge neat and healthy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: privet - a shrub often used for hedges

Etymology

From Latin: 'ligustrum' meaning privet + 'vulgare' meaning common.
