reenactment

1. NOUN

/ ˌriː.ɪˈnækt.mənt / (AmE), / ˌriː.ɪˈnækt.mənt / (BrE)
the act of performing or recreating an event from the past, often as a public display
recreation of a past event

Examples

The annual reenactment of the historic parade draws thousands of visitors to the town.
Many actors participated in the reenactment of the medieval festival for the documentary series.
He joined a Civil War reenactment group that meets every summer.

Synset Examples

the reenactment of the battle of Princeton

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recreation - the process of recreating or reproducing something

Etymology

From 're-' (again) + 'enact' (to perform or act out); patterned after 'enactment'.
