eudaimonia

1. NOUN

/ juːˌdaɪməˈniːə / (AmE), / juːˌdaɪməˈniːə / (BrE)
a state of happiness, well-being, or flourishing, especially in the sense of fulfilling one's potential, as discussed in ancient Greek philosophy
state of happiness or flourishing

Examples

Many philosophers have debated the true meaning of eudaimonia in the context of living a good life.
The concept of eudaimonia emphasizes not just happiness, but living virtuously and realizing one's potential.
Her pursuit of eudaimonia led her to focus on both personal growth and helping others.

Synset Examples

the town was finally on the upbeat after our recent troubles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: happiness - the state of being happy
  • Synonym: flourishing - living in a state of optimal well-being or development

Etymology

From Greek εὐδαιμονία (eudaimonia), meaning 'happiness' or 'welfare', from εὖ (eu, 'good') + δαίμων (daimōn, 'spirit, fortune').
