Locusta

1. PROPN

/ loʊˈkʌstə / (AmE), / ləʊˈkʌstə / (BrE)
A genus of grasshoppers in the family Acrididae, including the migratory locust.
genus of grasshoppers

Examples

Locusta is best known for containing the well-known species, the migratory locust.
Scientists have studied Locusta to understand the movement patterns of swarming insects.
The genus Locusta can be found in various parts of Africa, Asia, and Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: locust - a large grasshopper that often migrates in swarms

Etymology

From Latin 'locusta' meaning 'locust'.

2. NOUN

/ loʊˈkʌstə / (AmE), / ləˈkʌstə / (BrE)
A genus of grasshoppers in the family Acrididae, which includes locusts known for forming swarms.
a genus of grasshoppers (Acrididae)

Examples

The migratory locust belongs to the Locusta genus, which is notorious for its swarming behavior.
Scientists have studied the lifecycle of Locusta to better understand swarm formation.
A member of the Locusta genus can cause significant agricultural damage during an outbreak.

Etymology

From Latin 'locusta' meaning locust or grasshopper.
