cocaine

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈkeɪn / (AmE), / kəʊˈkeɪn / (BrE)
A powerful, addictive narcotic drug obtained from coca leaves, used medically as a local anesthetic and illegally as a stimulant.
addictive narcotic drug from coca leaves

Examples

The police discovered a large quantity of cocaine hidden inside the suitcase.
Medical professionals sometimes use cocaine as a local anesthetic during delicate surgeries.
He struggled for years with an addiction to cocaine after being introduced to the drug in college.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crack - a potent, smokable form of cocaine
  • Synonym: heroin - a powerful narcotic drug used as a recreational drug for its euphoric effects
  • Synonym: amphetamine - a synthetic drug that is a stimulant of the central nervous system

Etymology

From Spanish 'coca' (the coca plant) + chemical suffix '-ine'.

2. NOUN

/ koʊˈkeɪn / (AmE), / kəʊˈkeɪn / (BrE)
an addictive narcotic drug made from coca leaves, used medically as a local anesthetic and illicitly as a stimulant
an addictive narcotic and stimulant

Examples

The police seized over a kilogram of cocaine in the drug raid.
Medical staff sometimes use cocaine as a local anesthetic in certain surgeries.
He struggled with addiction after becoming dependent on cocaine in his twenties.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: crack - a form of cocaine that can be smoked
  • Synonym: methamphetamine - another powerful stimulant drug
  • Synonym: heroin - another addictive narcotic drug

Etymology

From Spanish 'coca' (the plant) + chemical suffix '-ine'.
