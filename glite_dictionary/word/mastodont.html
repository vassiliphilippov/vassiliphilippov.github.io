mastodont

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæs.təˌdɑnt / (AmE), / ˈmæs.təˌdɒnt / (BrE)
an extinct elephant-like mammal of the genus Mastodon, distinguished from mammoths by the form of its molar teeth, that lived from the Miocene to the Pleistocene epochs.
extinct elephant-like mammal; genus Mastodon

Examples

Fossils of the mastodont have been unearthed in North America and Europe.
Unlike the mammoth, the mastodont had cusp-shaped molars suited for browsing.
Scientists believe the mastodont went extinct due to climate change and human activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mammoth - an extinct large, hairy elephant of the genus Mammuthus

Etymology

From New Latin Mastodon, from Ancient Greek mastos (breast) and odous (tooth), referring to the breast-shaped cusps on its molars.
