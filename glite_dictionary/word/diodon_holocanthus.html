Diodon holocanthus

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪ.əˌdɑn ˌhoʊ.loʊˈkæn.θəs / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.ədɒn ˌhɒl.əˈkan.θəs / (BrE)
a species of spiny pufferfish, also known as the long-spined porcupinefish, found in tropical and subtropical marine waters, distinguished by its elongated spines and ability to inflate when threatened
species of spiny pufferfish

Examples

The Diodon holocanthus inflates its body and extends its long spines to deter predators.
Divers often spot Diodon holocanthus hiding among coral reefs in tropical waters.
The distinctive spines of the Diodon holocanthus make it easily recognizable to marine biologists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porcupinefish - a related species of fish with sharp spines

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Diodon' (two-toothed) and species name 'holocanthus' (all-spined), both from Greek roots.
