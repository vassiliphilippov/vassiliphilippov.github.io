Genipa

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈniːpə / (AmE), / dʒəˈniːpə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American evergreen trees and shrubs, bearing yellow flowers and succulent edible fruit with a thick rind.
tropical American genus of trees and shrubs

Examples

The Genipa genus is known for its attractive yellow flowers and edible fruit.
Botanists are studying various species within the Genipa genus for their medicinal properties.
The fruit from some Genipa trees is used to make beverages in South America.

Etymology

From the Tupi word 'jenipapo', used in Portuguese as 'genipapo'.
