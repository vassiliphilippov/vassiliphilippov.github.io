netherworld

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛðərˌwɜrld / (AmE), / ˈnɛðəwɜːld / (BrE)
(in religion or mythology) the underworld; the place where the dead dwell after death.
the world of the dead (mythology/religion)

Examples

Many ancient cultures believed that souls journeyed to the netherworld after death.
The myth described heroes venturing into the netherworld to rescue lost loved ones.
Legends say that the entrance to the netherworld is guarded by a fearsome creature.

Synset Examples

No one goes to Hades with all his immense wealth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: underworld - the world of the dead in mythology
  • Synonym: hell - the place of punishment for the wicked after death

Etymology

From Middle English 'nether' (lower) + 'world'.
