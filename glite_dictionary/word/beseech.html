to beseech

1. VERB

/ bɪˈsitʃ / (AmE), / bɪˈsiːtʃ / (BrE)
to ask someone earnestly and fervently for something; to implore or beg
ask earnestly; implore

Examples

I beseech you to listen carefully to what I have to say.
She beseeched her friend not to reveal her secret.
The villagers beseeched the king for help during the drought.

Synset Examples

The prophet bid all people to become good persons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to implore - to beg someone earnestly or desperately to do something
  • Synonym: to beg - to ask someone earnestly or humbly for something
  • Antonym: demand - to ask for something forcefully

Etymology

From Middle English 'besechen', from Old English 'besēcan' (to seek, ask).
