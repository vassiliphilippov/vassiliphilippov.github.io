whicker

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪkər / (AmE), / ˈwɪkə / (BrE)
the characteristic whinnying or neighing sound made by a horse
horse's whinnying sound

Examples

We heard the gentle whicker of the horses from the nearby stable.
A sudden whicker broke the silence of the morning as the horses sensed movement.
The mare gave a soft whicker to greet her foal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neigh - a characteristic loud noise made by a horse
  • Synonym: whinny - a soft or gentle neigh from a horse

2. VERB

/ ˈwɪkər / (AmE), / ˈwɪkə / (BrE)
(of a horse) to make a soft, gentle neighing or whinnying sound.
(of a horse) make a gentle neigh or whinny

Examples

The horse began to whicker softly as I approached with an apple.
I heard the mares whickering to each other in the early morning.
She smiled as her pony whickered in recognition of her voice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neigh - to make the loud, high sound of a horse
  • Synonym: whinny - to make a gentle, high-pitched neigh, especially of a horse

Etymology

Of imitative origin, echoing the sound made by a horse; recorded since the late 17th century.
