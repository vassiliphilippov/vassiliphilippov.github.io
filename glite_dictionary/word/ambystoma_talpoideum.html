Ambystoma talpoideum

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmbɪˈstoʊmə tælˈpɔɪdiəm / (AmE), / ˌæmbɪˈstəʊmə tælˈpɔɪdiəm / (BrE)
a small brownish-black burrowing salamander native to the southeastern United States
burrowing salamander of southeastern US

Examples

The Ambystoma talpoideum can often be found in woodland ponds during breeding season.
Researchers studied how Ambystoma talpoideum adapts to different wetland environments.
Unlike some other salamanders, Ambystoma talpoideum spends much of its life underground.

Etymology

From modern Latin genus name 'Ambystoma' and species 'talpoideum', from Latin 'talpa' (mole) and Greek '-oeides' (resembling), referring to its mole-like burrowing habit.
