hollowness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑloʊnəs / (AmE), / ˈhɒləʊnəs / (BrE)
the quality or condition of having a sunken or depressed area
a sunken or depressed quality

Examples

The hollowness in the old tree's trunk was home to several animals.
Her cheeks showed a slight hollowness after weeks of illness.
The artist emphasized the hollowness of the sculpture's eyes to convey sadness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depression - a sunken or lower area
  • Antonym: fullness - the state of being filled or not sunken

Etymology

From Middle English 'holownes', from 'holow' (hollow) + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑl.oʊ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈhɒl.ə.nəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being hollow; having an empty space inside.
state of being hollow

Examples

The hollowness of the tree trunk made it an ideal home for squirrels.
His voice echoed with a certain hollowness that betrayed his sadness.
After years of neglect, the statue had developed a noticeable hollowness inside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emptiness - The quality of containing nothing; lack of content.
  • Antonym: solidity - The state of being solid rather than hollow.

Etymology

Formed from 'hollow' + '-ness', indicating a state or quality.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɑloʊnəs / (AmE), / ˈhɒlənəs / (BrE)
the quality of not being genuine, sincere, or truthful; deceitfulness or hypocrisy
lack of sincerity or truthfulness

Examples

He saw the hollowness behind her friendly smile and empty compliments.
The politician's speech was full of hollowness and failed to convince the audience.
After years of working there, she grew tired of the hollowness of corporate promises.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insincerity - lack of sincerity or genuineness
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest and truthful

Etymology

From Middle English 'holownes', from 'holow' (hollow) + '-ness'.
