riband

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪb.ənd / (AmE), / ˈrɪb.ənd / (BrE)
A ribbon, especially one used as a decoration or award.
decorative ribbon

Examples

She wore a blue riband on her dress to celebrate the occasion.
The winner received a golden riband as a mark of honor.
Bright ribands were tied around the maypole for the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ribbon - a long, narrow strip of fabric, typically used for tying or decorating

Etymology

From Old French 'ruban', itself of Germanic origin, related to 'ribbon'.
