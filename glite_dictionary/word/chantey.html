chantey

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæn(t)i / (AmE), / ˈʃænti / (BrE)
a rhythmic work song, especially one sung by sailors while working together on board a ship
sailors' rhythmic work song

Examples

The sailors sang a chantey to coordinate their work as they raised the anchor.
He collected old maritime chanteys and performed them at folk music festivals.
A traditional chantey echoed over the deck as the crew pulled together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea shanty - a type of chantey sung specifically at sea

Etymology

Alteration of 'chanty', possibly from French 'chanter' (to sing).
