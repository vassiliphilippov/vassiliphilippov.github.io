thyrotropin

1. NOUN

/ ˌθaɪrəˈtroʊpɪn / (AmE), / ˌθaɪərəˈtrəʊpɪn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates the thyroid gland to produce hormones
hormone stimulating the thyroid gland
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a blood test to measure her thyrotropin levels.
Disorders of the pituitary gland can alter thyrotropin production.
An increase in thyrotropin can indicate hypothyroidism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thyroid-stimulating hormone - another term for thyrotropin

Etymology

From Greek 'thyreo-' (shield, referring to the thyroid) + 'tropin' (turning or stimulating).
