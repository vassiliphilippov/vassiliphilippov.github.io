homocyclic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhoʊmoʊˈsaɪklɪk / (AmE), / ˌhəʊməʊˈsɪklɪk / (BrE)
(of a chemical compound) containing a closed ring of atoms of the same element, especially carbon atoms.
having a ring of same-kind atoms (esp. carbon)
medical

Examples

Benzene is a classic example of a homocyclic aromatic compound.
In organic chemistry, homocyclic rings are composed entirely of carbon atoms.
Chemists distinguish between homocyclic and heterocyclic compounds when classifying ring structures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heterocyclic - having a ring composed of atoms of more than one element

Etymology

From Greek homos (same) + kyklos (circle, ring).
