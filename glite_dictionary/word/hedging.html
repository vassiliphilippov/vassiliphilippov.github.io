hedging

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɛdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
A strategy or technique used in finance to reduce or eliminate risk, often by making counterbalancing investments.
financial risk reduction strategy

Examples

Many investors use hedging to protect their portfolios from sudden market downturns.
Currency hedging can help multinational companies manage exchange rate risks.
By hedging their positions, traders can minimize potential losses in volatile markets.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: speculation - investment involving high risk for potential gain

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'hedge' (to limit risk), which derives from Middle English 'hegge' (a fence or boundary).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɛdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making statements that are intentionally vague or noncommittal, often to avoid giving a firm answer or to avoid commitment
vague or noncommittal statement

Examples

His constant hedging during the interview made it hard to know what he really thought.
Politicians are often accused of hedging when faced with difficult questions.
There was a lot of hedging in his response, leaving everyone uncertain about his decision.

Synset Examples

when you say ‘maybe’ you are just hedging

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equivocation - use of ambiguous language to conceal the truth or avoid commitment
  • Synonym: evasion - the act of avoiding giving a direct answer
  • Antonym: directness - the quality of being straightforward

Etymology

From 'hedge' (to limit or qualify), originating from Old English 'hecg' (a fence or boundary).
