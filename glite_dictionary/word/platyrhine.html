platyrhine

1. ADJ

/ ˈplætɪˌraɪn / (AmE), / ˈplætɪˌraɪn / (BrE)
(of a primate or person) having broad, widely separated nostrils; relating to New World monkeys of this type.
having broad, widely separated nostrils

Examples

The scientist studied the platyrhine features of the New World monkeys.
Some anthropologists classify populations based on platyrhine nasal structures.
Howler monkeys, known for their platyrhine noses, are common in Central and South America.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: catarrhine - relating to Old World monkeys with closely set, downward-facing nostrils

Etymology

From Greek platys (broad) + rhis, rhinos (nose).
