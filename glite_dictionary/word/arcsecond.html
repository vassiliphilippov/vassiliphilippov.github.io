arcsecond

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrkˌsɛk.ənd / (AmE), / ˈɑːkˌsɛk.ənd / (BrE)
A unit of angular measurement equal to one sixtieth of an arcminute, or 1/3,600 of a degree.
unit of angular measurement (1/3600 of a degree)

Examples

Astronomers use the arcsecond to precisely locate stars in the sky.
The telescope can resolve details as small as one arcsecond.
Distances between stars may appear tiny, sometimes just a few arcseconds apart.

Synset Examples

the treasure is 2 minutes and 45 seconds south of here

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: arcminute - A unit of angular measurement equal to 1/60 of a degree

Etymology

Formed from 'arc' (curve, part of a circle) and 'second' (as in second minute of arc).
