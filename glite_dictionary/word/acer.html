Acer

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪsər / (AmE), / ˈeɪsə / (BrE)
A genus of trees and shrubs, commonly known as maples, characterized by having winged fruit.
genus of maples (trees/shrubs with winged fruit)

Examples

The Acer genus includes many species known for their vibrant fall foliage.
Japanese Acer trees are popular in ornamental gardens around the world.
Botanists identified the tree as belonging to the Acer genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maple - tree or shrub belonging to the genus Acer

Etymology

From Latin *acer* (maple tree).

2. NOUN

/ ˈeɪsər / (AmE), / ˈeɪsə / (BrE)
a genus of trees and shrubs, known as maples, characterized by winged fruit and palmately lobed leaves
genus of trees/shrubs; maples

Examples

The Acer genus includes over a hundred species of maple trees found throughout the Northern Hemisphere.
Gardeners often select species from the Acer group for their ornamental foliage and autumn color.
The winged seeds, known as samaras, are a distinguishing feature of the Acer genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maple - a tree of the genus Acer, known for its lobed leaves and sap

Etymology

From Latin *acer* (maple tree).
