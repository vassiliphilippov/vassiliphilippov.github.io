sphenion

1. NOUN

/ ˈsfiːni.ɒn / (AmE), / ˈsfiːni.ɒn / (BrE)
the anterior tip of the parietal bone in the human skull
anterior tip of the parietal bone
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully located the sphenion during the procedure to avoid damaging nearby structures.
In the anatomy textbook, the sphenion is marked as a reference point for measurements.
Damage to the area near the sphenion can affect the structural integrity of the skull.

Etymology

From Greek 'sphenion', diminutive of 'sphēn', meaning 'wedge'.
