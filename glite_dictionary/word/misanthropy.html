misanthropy

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈsænθrəpi / (AmE), / mɪˈsænθrəpi / (BrE)
A general dislike, distrust, or hatred of humankind.
hatred or distrust of humankind

Examples

The novel explores themes of misanthropy and alienation from society.
His growing misanthropy made it difficult for him to form close relationships.
Many artists have turned their misanthropy into thought-provoking works of art.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: philanthropy - love of humanity; desire to promote the welfare of others

Etymology

From Greek misanthrōpia, from misanthrōpos (hating mankind), from misein (to hate) + anthrōpos (man, human).

2. NOUN

/ mɪˈsænθrəpi / (AmE), / mɪˈsænθrəpi / (BrE)
A general dislike, distrust, or hatred of humankind.
dislike or distrust of humankind

Examples

Her growing misanthropy made it difficult for her to form close friendships.
The novel explores themes of misanthropy and alienation from society.
Despite his reputation for misanthropy, he donated generously to charity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: philanthropy - a desire to promote the welfare of others, love of humankind
  • Synonym: misanthrope - a person who dislikes or distrusts other people

Etymology

From Greek misanthrōpia (hatred of mankind), from misos (hatred) + anthrōpos (man, human).
