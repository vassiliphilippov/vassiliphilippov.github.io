Holothuridae

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˌlɑːθəˈraɪdiː / (AmE), / hɒˌlɒθjʊˈrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of sea cucumbers in the class Holothuroidea, consisting of various marine invertebrates with elongated bodies and leathery skin.
a family of sea cucumbers

Examples

The Holothuridae family includes many species commonly known as sea cucumbers.
Researchers discovered a new member of the Holothuridae in the coastal waters of Indonesia.
Many edible sea cucumbers belong to the Holothuridae family and are harvested throughout the Asia-Pacific region.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Holothuria, adapted from Greek holothourion (sea cucumber).

2. NOUN

/ hoʊˌlɒθjʊˈrɪdiː / (AmE), / hɒˌlɒθjʊˈrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of sea cucumbers in the class Holothuroidea, characterized by their leathery skin and elongated bodies.
family of sea cucumbers

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species within the Holothuridae family off the coast of Indonesia.
Most members of the Holothuridae have tube feet and soft bodies adapted for life on the sea floor.
The Holothuridae are an important group of echinoderms studied in marine biology.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Holothuroidea - the class of marine animals known as sea cucumbers

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Holothuria (the type genus) and the family suffix -idae.
