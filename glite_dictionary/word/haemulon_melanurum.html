Haemulon melanurum

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːmjʊˈlɒn ˌmɛləˈnʊrəm / (AmE), / ˌhiːmjʊˈlɒn ˌmɛləˈnʊərəm / (BrE)
A species of fish found in warm Atlantic waters, commonly known as the cottonwick grunt.
a fish species in warm Atlantic waters

Examples

The Haemulon melanurum is distinguished by its black-striped tail and silvery sides.
During our snorkeling trip, we spotted several Haemulon melanurum near the coral reef.
Researchers observed a school of Haemulon melanurum feeding in the shallow bay.

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) and Latin 'melas' (black) + 'oura' (tail), referring to its appearance.
