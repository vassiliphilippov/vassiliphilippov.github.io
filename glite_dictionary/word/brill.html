brill

1. NOUN

/ brɪl / (AmE), / brɪl / (BrE)
a flat, edible marine fish (Scophthalmus rhombus) of the turbot family, found in European waters and valued as food
a European flatfish, valued as food

Examples

Fishermen in the North Sea often catch brill along with turbot and sole.
The restaurant served pan-seared brill with lemon and capers for the main course.
Demand for fresh brill has increased among chefs seeking sustainable seafood options.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turbot - a large flatfish related to the brill, valued as food

Etymology

From Middle English 'brille', from Middle Dutch 'brille' (brill fish).
