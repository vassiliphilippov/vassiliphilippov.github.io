crawling

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkrɔːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of moving slowly on hands and knees or by dragging the body along the ground
slow movement on hands and knees

Examples

The baby learned crawling before she could walk.
After the injury, crawling was James's only way to reach the phone.
The narrow tunnel forced the rescuers into crawling for several meters.

Synset Examples

a crawl was all that the injured man could manage
the traffic moved at a creep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creeping - slow movement close to the ground, often on hands and knees
