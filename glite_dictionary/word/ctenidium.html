ctenidium

1. NOUN

/ tɪˈnɪdiəm / (AmE), / tɪˈnɪdɪəm / (BrE)
A comb-like respiratory organ that functions as the gill in certain mollusks.
comb-like gill in mollusks

Examples

The squid's ctenidium allows it to extract oxygen efficiently from the water.
During dissection, the ctenidium was clearly visible along the mantle cavity of the snail.
Marine biologists study the structure of the ctenidium to understand mollusk respiration.

Etymology

From Greek ktenidion, diminutive of kteis (comb).
