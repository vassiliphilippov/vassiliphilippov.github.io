battleground

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬əlˌɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈbætəlˌɡraʊnd / (BrE)
an area where a battle is or has been fought
place where a battle occurs or occurred

Examples

The town became a battleground during the war, with fierce fighting on its streets.
Political debates often turn into a battleground of opposing ideas and heated arguments.
After the conflict, historians visited the battleground to study the remains of the old fortifications.

Synset Examples

they made a tour of Civil War battlefields

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battlefield - an area where a battle is fought
  • Antonym: peaceful area - an area with no fighting or conflict

Etymology

Formed from 'battle' + 'ground'.
