sneezeweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈsniːzˌwiːd / (AmE), / ˈsniːzwiːd / (BrE)
Any of several plants of the genus Helenium, known for their pollen that can cause sneezing.
plant of the genus Helenium

Examples

In the wildflower meadow, tall sneezeweed bloomed beside the goldenrod.
Some gardeners avoid planting sneezeweed due to its irritating pollen.
Native Americans once used dried sneezeweed leaves in snuff to induce sneezing.

Etymology

From 'sneeze' + 'weed', referring to the plant's historical use to induce sneezing.
