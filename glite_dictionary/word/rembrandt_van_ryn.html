Rembrandt van Ryn

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɛmˌbrænt væn ˈraɪn / (AmE), / ˈrɛm.brand væn ˈraɪn / (BrE)
(1606–1669) a highly influential Dutch artist and master of painting, drawing, and etching, renowned for his portraits and use of light and shadow.
Dutch painter, 1606–1669

Examples

The museum features several original works by Rembrandt van Ryn.
Art historians continue to study the legacy of Rembrandt van Ryn in European painting.
Rembrandt van Ryn is famous for his masterful use of light and shadow in his art.

Etymology

From Dutch Rembrandt van Rijn, meaning 'Rembrandt of the Rhine'—his full name was Rembrandt Harmenszoon van Rijn.
