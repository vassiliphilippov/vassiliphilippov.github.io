kHz

1. NOUN

/ kiː eɪtʃ z / (AmE), / keɪ ʔ eɪtʃ zɛd / (BrE)
a unit of frequency equal to one thousand hertz or cycles per second
unit of frequency (kilohertz)

Examples

The radio station broadcasts at 101.7 kHz.
Ultrasound machines often operate at frequencies above 20 kHz.
The crystal oscillator produces a stable signal at exactly 32 kHz.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hertz - a unit of frequency equal to one cycle per second
  • Degree Higher: MHz - a unit of frequency equal to one million hertz

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'kilohertz', from kilo- (thousand) + hertz (after Heinrich Hertz, physicist).
