biosystematy

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊ.sɪˈstɛ.mə.ti / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪ.əʊ.sɪˈstɛm.əti / (BrE)
the use of data, such as cytogenetic or biochemical information, to determine taxonomic relationships, especially within an evolutionary framework
taxonomic classification using modern biological data

Examples

Modern biosystematy employs molecular data to clarify the classification of closely related plant species.
Advances in biosystematy have resolved several long-standing debates among taxonomists.
She specializes in biosystematy, integrating genetics and morphology in her research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biosystematics - the scientific study of the diversity and relationships of organisms using data such as genetics and morphology

Etymology

From bio- (relating to life) + systematy (from systematic, relating to classification).
