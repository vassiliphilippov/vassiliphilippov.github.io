Aplodontia

1. PROPN

/ ˌæpləˈdɒnʃiə / (AmE), / ˌaplɒˈdɒntɪə / (BrE)
a genus of rodents that consists of the mountain beaver, the only living member of the family Aplodontiidae
genus of rodents: mountain beaver

Examples

The genus Aplodontia is represented today by the mountain beaver.
Scientists have studied the habits of Aplodontia in the wild.
Fossils show that Aplodontia has changed little over millions of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain beaver - a burrowing rodent native to the Pacific Northwest of North America

Etymology

From Greek 'haplous' (simple) + 'odous' (tooth), referring to dental characteristics.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæpləˈdɑnʃiə / (AmE), / ˌæpləˈdɒntɪə / (BrE)
A genus of rodents comprising the mountain beavers, native to western North America; the sole genus in the family Aplodontiidae.
genus of rodents (mountain beavers)

Examples

The only living species in the genus Aplodontia is commonly known as the mountain beaver.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Aplodontia in the Pacific Northwest.
The fossil record suggests the genus Aplodontia has existed for millions of years.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek aploos (simple) plus odous (tooth).
