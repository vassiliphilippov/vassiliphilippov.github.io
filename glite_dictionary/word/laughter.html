laughter

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæftɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɑːftə / (BrE)
the action or sound of laughing, often expressing joy, amusement, or sometimes scorn
the act or sound of laughing

Examples

Her infectious laughter filled the entire room at the party.
A burst of laughter erupted when the comedian told his joke.
The children's laughter echoed through the playground on a sunny afternoon.

Synset Examples

he enjoyed the laughter of the crowd

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laugh - the act of producing laughter; to make the characteristic sounds of amusement

Etymology

From Old English *hlahter*, related to 'laugh'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlæftɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɑːftə / (BrE)
the sound that people make when they are amused or happy, typically as an expression of joy or mirth
sound made by laughing

Examples

The room was filled with laughter as the comedian told his jokes.
Her infectious laughter made everyone around her smile.
Sudden laughter erupted from the children playing outside.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: giggle - a light, silly way of laughing
  • Degree Higher: cackle - a loud, harsh laugh

Etymology

From Old English 'hlæhter', from Proto-Germanic *hlahtruz.
