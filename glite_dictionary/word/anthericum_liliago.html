Anthericum liliago

1. NOUN

/ ˌænθəˈraɪkəm lɪˈlaɪəˌɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˌænθəˈrɪkəm lɪˈlaɪəɡəʊ / (BrE)
a southern European plant commonly cultivated for its spikes of small, star-shaped, greenish-white flowers
a plant with star-shaped, greenish-white flowers

Examples

The Anthericum liliago blooms in late spring, adding delicate beauty to rock gardens.
Gardeners often plant Anthericum liliago for its clusters of star-shaped flowers and graceful stems.
The greenish-white flowers of Anthericum liliago attract bees and other pollinators to the area.

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Anthericum' from Greek 'antherikos' (asphodel), and 'liliago' meaning 'like a lily'.
