quaestor

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwiːstər / (AmE), / ˈkwiːstə / (BrE)
In ancient Rome, a public official responsible for financial administration and other duties.
Roman financial official

Examples

The quaestor was tasked with managing the treasury of the Roman Republic.
As a young man, Cicero served as a quaestor in Sicily.
The responsibilities of a quaestor varied depending on the era and position.

Etymology

From Latin 'quaestor' (investigator, examiner, public official in charge of finances).
