modification

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɒdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of changing something, or an instance of this; a slight or partial alteration.
the act of making changes or adjustments

Examples

The new policy required a modification of company procedures.
With each modification, the device became more efficient and user-friendly.
There has been little modification to the original design over the years.

Synset Examples

the change was intended to increase sales
this storm is certainly a change for the worse
the neighborhood had undergone few modifications since his last visit years ago

Related Terms

  • Synonym: change - the act or process of becoming different
  • Synonym: alteration - a small change that makes something slightly different
  • Degree Higher: transformation - a complete or major change in someone or something

Etymology

From Latin modificatio (a measuring, moderation), from modificare (to measure, regulate).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːdəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɒdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the grammatical relation in which a word or phrase qualifies or limits the meaning of another word or phrase
grammatical relation where a word limits another's meaning

Examples

In the phrase 'red car,' the word 'red' is a modification of 'car.'
Adjectives frequently serve as modification in English grammar.
The teacher explained how adverbs function as modification in a sentence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: qualifier - a word or phrase that limits or modifies the meaning of another word

Etymology

From Latin modificatio(n-), from modificare ‘to limit, regulate’.

3. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɒdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a version of something that has been slightly changed or altered; not an exact copy of the original
slightly changed version; not an exact copy

Examples

This car is a modification of last year's model, with improved safety features.
The new software is just a modification of the previous version, not a complete overhaul.
They made a minor modification to the dress design to meet the client's request.

Synset Examples

a modification of last year's model

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adaptation - a version changed to suit a new purpose or conditions
  • Antonym: original - the first or earliest form of something, not a copy or modification

Etymology

From Latin modificatio(n-), from modificare 'to measure, limit'.

4. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɒdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of changing something slightly in order to improve it or make it more suitable
the act of making a change or adjustment

Examples

He requested a modification to the contract before signing it.
The tailor made a simple modification to the dress to improve its fit.
After the modification, the car performed much better on the road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alteration - the process of making a change, especially to clothing
  • Synonym: adjustment - a small change made to improve or make something work better
  • Degree Higher: enhancement - an improvement or increase in quality, value, or extent

Etymology

From Latin modificatio, from modificare (to measure, limit, modify).
