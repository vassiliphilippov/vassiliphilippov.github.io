Occidentalism

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑksɪˈdɛntəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˈdɛntəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the study or representation of Western cultures, languages, and peoples, especially from a scholarly or critical perspective.
study or interpretation of Western cultures

Examples

The book provides a comprehensive analysis of Occidentalism in contemporary art criticism.
Her research focuses on how Occidentalism shapes perceptions of the West in Asian literature.
Some scholars argue that Occidentalism often mirrors the biases found in Orientalism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Orientalism - the study or representation of Eastern cultures, languages, and peoples

Etymology

From Latin 'occidens' (west, sunset) + '-alism'.
