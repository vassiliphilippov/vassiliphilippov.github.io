focusing

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊkəsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəsɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of concentrating one's attention or energy on a particular object or activity.
concentration of attention or energy

Examples

Her focusing on the project helped the team finish ahead of schedule.
Meditation is a useful technique for improving focusing skills.
With better focusing, you can achieve more in less time.

Synset Examples

the focus of activity shifted to molecular biology
he had no direction in his life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concentration - close attention to or mental effort on one subject
  • Antonym: distraction - lack of concentration or attention

Etymology

From the verb 'focus', ultimately from Latin 'focus' (hearth, fireplace).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊkəsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəsɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of adjusting something so that it becomes clear or is brought into focus
the act of bringing into focus

Examples

She adjusted the lens, carefully focusing the camera on the butterfly.
The focusing of the microscope took several minutes to get a clear view.
With better focusing, the blurry shapes became crisp and recognizable on the screen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: focus - the point at which an image is sharp or clear

Etymology

From the verb 'focus', from Latin 'focus' (hearth, fireplace).
