tangibly

1. ADV

/ ˈtæn.dʒə.bli / (AmE), / ˈtan.dʒɪ.bli / (BrE)
in a way that can be perceived by touch or is clearly real and concrete; in a tangible manner.
in a tangible or concrete way

Examples

The company's success was tangibly felt in the community through new jobs and services.
Her disappointment was tangibly reflected in her expression when she heard the news.
Virtue is tangibly rewarded in organizations that value honesty and hard work.

Synset Examples

virtue is tangibly rewarded

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intangibly - in a way that cannot be perceived by touch; not tangible

Etymology

From 'tangible' + '-ly' (adverbial suffix). 'Tangible' from Latin 'tangibilis', from 'tangere' (to touch).
