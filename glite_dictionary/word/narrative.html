narrative

1. ADJ

/ ˈnærətɪv / (AmE), / ˈnærətɪv / (BrE)
consisting of or characterized by the telling of a story; relating to narration or storytelling
relating to storytelling

Examples

The author developed a highly narrative style in her latest book.
He prefers narrative art forms that tell a coherent story.
Much of the film's appeal lies in its narrative structure.

Synset Examples

narrative poetry

Related Terms

  • Antonym: descriptive - giving details about how something or someone looks, sounds, etc., rather than telling a story

Etymology

From Latin narrativus (suited to narration), from narrare (to relate, tell).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00500957-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈnærətɪv / (AmE), / ˈnærətɪv / (BrE)
a spoken or written account of connected events; a story, especially one that is detailed or imaginative
an account or story of events

Examples

The documentary offered a powerful narrative of the community’s struggle with change.
Her new novel weaves a complex narrative that spans three generations.
The film’s narrative shifts between past and present to deepen the story.

Synset Examples

his narrative was interesting
Disney's stories entertain adults as well as children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: story - an account of imaginary or real people and events told for entertainment
  • Synonym: account - a report or description of an event or experience

Etymology

From Latin narrativus (fit to tell), from narrare (to relate, tell).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07235754-n
Synset POS: n
