Harkat-ul-Mujahidin

1. PROPN

/ hɑːrˌkɑːt ʊl muːˈdʒɑːhɪdɪn / (AmE), / hɑːˌkæt ʊl mjuːˈdʒɑːhɪdiːn / (BrE)
an Islamic militant group founded in Pakistan, involved in insurgency operations in Afghanistan during the 1980s and currently designated as a terrorist organization active mainly in Kashmir
militant group active in Pakistan and Kashmir

Examples

The United States has designated Harkat-ul-Mujahidin as a foreign terrorist organization.
Many experts have studied the origins of Harkat-ul-Mujahidin and its activities in the region.
Authorities increased security after threats allegedly linked to Harkat-ul-Mujahidin.

Etymology

From Arabic *ḥarakat* (movement) + *al-mujāhidīn* (the fighters/strugglers in jihad).
