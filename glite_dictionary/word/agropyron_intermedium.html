Agropyron intermedium

1. NOUN

/ ˌæɡrəˈpaɪrɒn ˌɪntərˈmiːdiəm / (AmE), / ˌæɡrəˈpaɪərɒn ˌɪntəˈmiːdiəm / (BrE)
A species of grass native to Asia, introduced into the United States for use as pasture and fodder for livestock.
Asiatic grass used for pasture and fodder

Examples

Agropyron intermedium is widely planted in the Great Plains for its drought resistance.
Ranchers often prefer Agropyron intermedium because it provides nutritious grazing for cattle.
The introduction of Agropyron intermedium has improved pasture yields in many arid regions.

Etymology

From Greek agros (field) + pyros (wheat); Latin intermedium meaning 'intermediate'.
