Polystichum adiantiformis

1. PROPN

/ ˌpoʊliˈstɪkəm ædˌiæn.təˈfɔːrmɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈstɪkəm ædˌiæn.tɪˈfɔːmɪs / (BrE)
A widely distributed fern of the tropical Southern Hemisphere with leathery, pinnatifid fronds.
fern species of the Southern Hemisphere

Examples

The delicate fronds of Polystichum adiantiformis added a lush texture to the garden.
Botanists often study Polystichum adiantiformis due to its widespread presence in tropical regions.
We found a large patch of Polystichum adiantiformis growing wild near the forest stream.

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) and 'stichos' (row) and Latinized from resemblance to 'Adiantum' (a fern genus).
