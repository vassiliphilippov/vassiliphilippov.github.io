Charina

1. NOUN

/ kəˈraɪnə / (AmE), / kəˈraɪnə / (BrE)
A genus of small boas found in western North America, including species such as the rosy boa and rubber boa.
genus of North American boas

Examples

The rosy boa, a member of the genus Charina, is popular among reptile enthusiasts.
Scientists are studying the habitat preferences of Charina in California's deserts.
Charina species are known for their gentle disposition and manageable size.

Etymology

From New Latin Charina, the genus name assigned in zoological classification.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈraɪnə / (AmE), / kəˈraɪnə / (BrE)
A genus of boas native to western North America, including the rosy boas and rubber boas.
genus of North American boas

Examples

Charina species such as the rosy boa are native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.
The rubber boa, a member of the genus Charina, is known for its docile nature.
Herpetologists have studied Charina to understand the evolution of North American boas.

Etymology

From Greek charinos (graceful, pleasant), used as a generic name in taxonomy.
