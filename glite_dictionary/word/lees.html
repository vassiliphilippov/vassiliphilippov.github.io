lees

1. NOUN

/ liz / (AmE), / liÀêz / (BrE)
the sediment that settles at the bottom of a container during the fermentation of wine, beer, or other alcoholic beverages
sediment from alcohol fermentation

Examples

The winemaker carefully separated the wine from the lees before bottling.
A distinct flavor develops in champagne aged on its lees.
Fine particles in the lees can affect the texture and taste of the finished product.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dregs - the remnants of a liquid left in a container, together with any sediment or grounds

Etymology

From Old French *lie* (dregs), from Latin *levare* (to lift), relating to what is left behind.
