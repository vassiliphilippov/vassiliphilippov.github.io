to colourize

1. VERB

/ ˈkʌlɚˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkʌləˌaɪz / (BrE)
to add color to a black-and-white image, film, or object, especially by using technology
add color to something, especially an image or film

Examples

They decided to colourize the classic black-and-white film for a modern audience.
New software makes it easy to colourize old family photos.
The artist carefully colourized the historical map to highlight different regions.

Synset Examples

The child colored the drawings
Fall colored the trees
colorize black and white film

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to colorize - to add color to black-and-white images or films

Etymology

From 'colour' + '-ize', patterned after 'colorize'.
