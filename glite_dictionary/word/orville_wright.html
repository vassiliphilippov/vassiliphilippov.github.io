Orville Wright

1. PROPN

/ ˈɔrvɪl ˈraɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔːvɪl ˈraɪt / (BrE)
(1871–1948) An American aviation pioneer who, with his brother Wilbur Wright, invented and built the first successful airplane.
American aviation pioneer, co-inventor of the airplane

Examples

Orville Wright is credited with piloting the first powered flight in 1903.
The achievements of Orville Wright and his brother changed the course of aviation history.
Many biographies have been written about the life of Orville Wright.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wilbur Wright - Orville's brother and co-inventor of the airplane
