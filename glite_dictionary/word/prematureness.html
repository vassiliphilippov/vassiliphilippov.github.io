prematureness

1. NOUN

/ priˈmeɪtʃɚnəs / (AmE), / priˈmæʧənəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being premature; occurring or developing earlier than expected or desired.
state of being premature

Examples

The prematureness of his decision led to unforeseen complications.
Doctors monitored the baby closely due to the prematureness of her birth.
Some critics pointed out the prematureness in announcing the results before verification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prematurity - the state of being premature; often used medically

Etymology

From Latin 'praematurus' (very early), via 'premature' + '-ness'.
