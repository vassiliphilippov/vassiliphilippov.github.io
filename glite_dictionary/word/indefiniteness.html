indefiniteness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɛfənətnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛfɪnətnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being vague, unclear, or lacking precise definition
quality of being vague or unclear

Examples

The indefiniteness of his response left everyone confused about what he really meant.
Legal documents try to avoid indefiniteness to reduce misunderstandings or disputes.
There was a certain indefiniteness in the plan's details, which made some stakeholders uneasy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uncertainty - the state of being uncertain or not clearly known
  • Antonym: definiteness - the quality of being clearly defined or precise

Etymology

From 'indefinite' + '-ness', modeled on Latin roots 'in-' (not) + 'definitus' (defined).
