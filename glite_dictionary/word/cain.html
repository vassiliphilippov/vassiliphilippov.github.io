Cain

1. PROPN

/ keɪn / (AmE), / keɪn / (BrE)
(in the Bible) The eldest son of Adam and Eve, who killed his brother Abel out of jealousy and was punished by God with exile.
Biblical figure who killed his brother Abel

Examples

The story of Cain and Abel is often cited as a cautionary tale about jealousy.
In many cultures, the mark of Cain is a symbol of guilt and punishment.
Literature frequently references Cain as an archetype of the first murderer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Abel - The second son of Adam and Eve, killed by his brother Cain in the Bible

Etymology

From Hebrew Qayin, a name of uncertain origin, appearing in the Old Testament.
