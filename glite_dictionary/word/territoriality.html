territoriality

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrəˌtɔriˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌtɛrɪˌtɒriˈælɪti / (BrE)
the tendency or behavior of an animal, especially a male, to claim and defend a particular area as its territory
defense of territory by animals

Examples

The territoriality of wolves is often displayed through scent marking and vocalizations.
Studies show that increased territoriality can lead to aggressive encounters between male birds.
During mating season, territoriality becomes more pronounced as animals compete for resources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: territorial - relating to the ownership or defense of an area or territory

Etymology

From Latin territorium (territory) + -ality (forming nouns of quality or state).
