salableness

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪ.lə.bəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.lə.bəl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being able to be sold or marketed
quality of being marketable

Examples

The salableness of the product depends largely on current market trends.
Legal issues affected the salableness of the property, making it hard to sell.
Recent renovations increased the salableness of the old house significantly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salability - the quality of being salable

Etymology

From 'salable' (from French 'vendable', from vendre 'to sell') + '-ness' suffix indicating a state.
