Creon

1. PROPN

/ ˈkriː.ɑn / (AmE), / ˈkriː.ɒn / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the brother of Jocasta and uncle of Antigone, who became king of Thebes after the downfall of Oedipus.
king of Thebes in Greek mythology

Examples

Many scholars have debated whether Creon was justified in his actions against Antigone.
In Sophocles’ play, Creon struggles to maintain order in Thebes after Oedipus’s exile.
Despite his authority, Creon ultimately suffers for his refusal to show mercy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Oedipus - legendary king of Thebes, central figure in the Oedipus myth
  • Synonym: Antigone - daughter of Oedipus, niece of Creon in Greek myth

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Κρέων (Kréōn), meaning 'ruler' or 'lord'.
