to overpower

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈpaʊər / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈpaʊə / (BrE)
to strongly affect or overwhelm someone completely, especially with emotions, sensations, or stimuli
to be strongly affected by something

Examples

He was overpowered by a sudden wave of nostalgia when he entered his old school.
The strong perfume quickly overpowered my senses as I walked into the shop.
She tried to remain calm, but overpowering fear made her voice tremble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overwhelm - to overpower emotionally or physically

Etymology

From over- (above, excessive) + power (from Latin potere, to be able).

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvɚˈpaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈpaʊə / (BrE)
to defeat or gain control over someone or something by using greater strength, force, or influence
defeat or control with superior force

Examples

The security guards managed to overpower the intruder before he could escape.
She was quickly overpowered by sleep after the long journey.
The soldiers tried to overpower their opponents with sheer numbers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overwhelm - to defeat completely; to have a strong emotional effect
  • Synonym: to subdue - to bring under control, especially by force
  • Antonym: to succumb - to fail to resist; to be defeated by

Etymology

From over- (above, superior) + power; first recorded in the late 15th century.

Source: WordNet
