potentiometer

1. NOUN

/ poʊˌtɛn(t)ʃiˈɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / pəˌtɛn.tiˈɒm.ɪ.tə / (BrE)
an electrical device consisting of a resistor with three terminals, one of which is an adjustable center terminal, used to vary voltage in circuits
variable resistor in electronics

Examples

You can adjust the volume of the radio by turning the potentiometer.
The technician replaced the faulty potentiometer to restore the dimmer function.
In electronic circuits, a potentiometer is often used to regulate voltage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rheostat - a resistor used to control current by varying resistance

Etymology

From 'potential' + 'meter', originally from French 'potentiomètre'.

2. NOUN

/ poʊˌtɛnʃiˈɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / pəˌtɛnʃɪˈɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure direct current electromotive forces by comparing them with a known voltage
instrument for measuring electrical voltage

Examples

The technician used a potentiometer to measure the battery's voltage with high accuracy.
In the physics laboratory, students learned how to calibrate a potentiometer.
A potentiometer can provide precise readings of electromotive force in electrical circuits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voltmeter - an instrument for measuring electric potential difference between two points

Etymology

From French 'potentiomètre', from Latin 'potentia' (power) + Greek 'metron' (measure).
