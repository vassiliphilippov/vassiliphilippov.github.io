ebb

1. NOUN

/ ɛb / (AmE), / ɛb / (BrE)
the movement of the tide away from the land, out to sea
the outgoing flow of the tide

Examples

The fishermen waited until the ebb before setting out to sea.
At ebb, many rocks and shells are exposed on the beach.
The strong ebb made it difficult for the small boat to return.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flood - the incoming flow of the tide

Etymology

From Old English 'ebba', related to Old High German 'ebba' (ebb).

2. VERB

/ ɛb / (AmE), / ɛb / (BrE)
(of a quality, feeling, or state) to gradually decrease or decline; to recede or diminish.
to recede, decline, or diminish

Examples

After the excitement of the festival, everyone's energy started to ebb.
Her confidence began to ebb as the interview progressed.
The strength of the tide will ebb by late afternoon.

Synset Examples

The patient's strength ebbed away

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wane - (of a feeling, power, or quality) to decrease in vigor or extent; become weaker
  • Antonym: flow - to move steadily and continuously in a current or stream (opposite sense of ebb)

Etymology

From Old English 'ebba' (receding tide), related to Old Norse 'efja' (backwater).

3. NOUN

/ ɛb / (AmE), / ɛb / (BrE)
a gradual decline or lessening, especially of strength, power, or quantity
gradual decline or lessening

Examples

Interest in the project is at a low ebb right now.
The company's fortunes suffered a long ebb before recovering.
Public confidence has experienced an ebb in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waning - becoming gradually weaker or less important
  • Antonym: flow - the rise or increase (opposite of ebb)

Etymology

From Old English 'ebba', related to the movement of water away from land.

4. VERB

/ ɛb / (AmE), / ɛb / (BrE)
(of tide, water, or other things) to flow back or recede; to move away from the land or diminish in intensity or amount.
flow back or recede (as a tide)

Examples

The tide began to ebb after noon, revealing the rocks along the shore.
Her enthusiasm seemed to ebb as the meeting dragged on.
As the storm passed, the floodwaters slowly ebbed away from the streets.

Synset Examples

the tides ebbed at noon

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flow - to move along steadily and continuously in a current or stream

Etymology

From Old English 'ebba', related to tide and water moving away.
