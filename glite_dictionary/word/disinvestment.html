disinvestment

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsɪnˈvɛstmənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈvɛstmənt / (BrE)
the act of withdrawing or reducing capital investment from a company, industry, or country, often as a deliberate economic, political, or ethical decision
withdrawal or reduction of capital investment

Examples

The government announced a policy of disinvestment from coal-fired power plants to promote clean energy.
International sanctions led to large-scale disinvestment by foreign corporations in the country.
Ethical concerns have prompted universities to consider disinvestment from companies involved in fossil fuels.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: investment - the act of putting money, effort, or time into something to make a profit or get an advantage

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'dis-' (expressing reversal) and 'investment', modeled after 'investment'.
