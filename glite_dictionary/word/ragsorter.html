ragsorter

1. NOUN

/ ˈræɡˌsɔrtər / (AmE), / ˈræɡˌsɔːtə / (BrE)
a worker who sorts rags and old clothing for new uses, especially in papermaking
worker who sorts rags for reuse

Examples

The ragsorter spent hours separating cotton from wool to prepare materials for the paper mill.
In the factory's heyday, dozens of ragsorters handled truckloads of old clothes daily.
Becoming a ragsorter was common work in the 19th-century textile industry.

Etymology

From 'rag' and 'sorter'; denotes one who sorts rags.
