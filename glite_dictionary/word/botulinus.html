botulinus

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈtjuːlɪnəs / (AmE), / bəˈtjuːlɪnəs / (BrE)
an anaerobic bacterium that produces botulin, the toxin responsible for botulism
bacterium producing botulin toxin
medical

Examples

Botulinus is best known for producing the deadly toxin involved in botulism.
Improperly preserved food can contain botulinus, posing serious health risks.
Researchers study botulinus to develop safer food preservation techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Clostridium botulinum - the scientific name for the bacterium causing botulism
  • Degree Higher: botulism - serious illness caused by botulin toxin

Etymology

From Neo-Latin *Clostridium botulinum*, based on Latin 'botulus' (sausage), referring to its association with sausage poisoning.
