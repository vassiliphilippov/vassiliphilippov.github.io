flea

1. NOUN

/ fliː / (AmE), / fliː / (BrE)
a small, wingless parasitic insect that lives by sucking the blood of mammals and birds and is noted for its ability to jump long distances
small jumping parasitic insect

Examples

The family dog brought a flea infestation into the house after playing in the yard.
She felt a tiny bite and spotted a flea jumping on her ankle.
Veterinarians recommend regular treatments to protect pets from flea bites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tick - a small parasitic arachnid that feeds on the blood of mammals, birds, and sometimes reptiles and amphibians

Etymology

Old English *flēa*, related to Old High German *flōh* and Latin *pulicem* (flea).

2. NOUN

/ fliː / (AmE), / fliː / (BrE)
a small, wingless, jumping insect that feeds on the blood of mammals and birds, often infesting cats, dogs, and other animals
parasitic jumping insect

Examples

My dog started scratching because he had a flea infestation.
We had to treat the house to get rid of every last flea.
She found a flea on her cat's ear during grooming.

Synset Examples

She found a flea on her cat during grooming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tick - bloodsucking arachnid parasite
  • Synonym: louse - small parasitic insect infesting humans and animals

Etymology

From Old English fleá, related to Dutch vlo, German Floh.
