Rockies

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɑkiːz / (AmE), / ˈrɒkiːz / (BrE)
the chief mountain range of western North America, extending from British Columbia to northern New Mexico, and forming the continental divide
major mountain range in western North America

Examples

Every summer, thousands of hikers set out to explore the breathtaking beauty of the Rockies.
Grizzly bears are native to several regions within the Rockies, especially in national parks.
She had always dreamed of skiing in the Rockies during the winter holidays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rocky Mountains - the major mountain range in western North America; formal name for the Rockies

Etymology

Shortened form of 'Rocky Mountains'.
