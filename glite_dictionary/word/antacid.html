antacid

1. ADJ

/ ænˈtæs.ɪd / (AmE), / ænˈtæs.ɪd / (BrE)
(of a substance) acting to neutralize acid, especially in the stomach.
counteracting stomach acidity
medical

Examples

Milk of magnesia is an antacid compound used to relieve heartburn.
Doctors often recommend antacid tablets for patients suffering from acid reflux.
An antacid coating on the pills helps protect the stomach lining.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acidic - having the properties of an acid
  • Synonym: alkaline - having the properties of a base; neutralizes acids

Etymology

From Greek 'anti' (against) + 'acid'.

2. NOUN

/ ænˈtæsɪd / (AmE), / antˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a substance that neutralizes stomach acid, used to relieve indigestion and heartburn
substance to reduce stomach acidity
medical

Examples

After eating a spicy meal, he took an antacid to soothe his upset stomach.
Many people keep antacids at home for occasional heartburn or indigestion.
The doctor recommended an antacid to help manage her symptoms of acid reflux.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acid - a substance with a sour taste that reacts with bases; opposite of base or alkali

Etymology

From Greek anti- (against) and acidus (acid).
