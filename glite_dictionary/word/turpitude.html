turpitude

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝː.pɪˌtud / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.pɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
depraved or wicked behavior or character; moral corruption.
moral depravity or corruption

Examples

The judge cited his history of turpitude when delivering the harsh sentence.
She was shocked by the turpitude revealed in the investigation.
Many novels explore the turpitude of corrupt societies.

Synset Examples

the various turpitudes of modern society

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depravity - moral corruption; wickedness
  • Antonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards

Etymology

From Latin turpitudo (baseness, foulness), from turpis (vile, base).
