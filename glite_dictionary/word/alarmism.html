alarmism

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɑːr.mɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈlɑː.mɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice of exaggerating dangers or causing needless public concern by issuing excessive warnings
exaggeration of danger; needless warnings

Examples

The newspaper was accused of alarmism after publishing sensational headlines about the flu outbreak.
Some experts warn that too much alarmism about climate change could cause unnecessary panic.
Critics argue that alarmism only serves to distract the public from reasonable discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panic-mongering - exaggerating threats to spread fear
  • Antonym: reassurance - the act of removing doubts or fears

Etymology

From 'alarm' + '-ism', first used in the 19th century.
