Combretum appiculatum

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈbriːtəm æˌpɪkjʊˈleɪtəm / (AmE), / kɒmˈbriːtəm apɪkjʊˈlɑːtəm / (BrE)
a small deciduous tree native to southern Africa, especially the Transvaal, with spikes of yellow flowers
southern African tree with yellow flower spikes

Examples

The Combretum appiculatum is often planted as an ornamental tree in gardens across South Africa.
Yellow flowers covered the branches of the Combretum appiculatum during the spring season.
Researchers observed that animals shelter under the Combretum appiculatum during extreme heat.

Etymology

From New Latin, where 'Combretum' is the genus name and 'appiculatum' means 'with a small point'.
