Macadamia ternifolia

1. NOUN

/ məˌkæd.əˈmiː.ə tɜr.nəˈfoʊ.li.ə / (AmE), / məˌkæd.əˈmiː.ə tɜː.nɪˈfəʊ.lɪ.ə / (BrE)
A small Australian tree with clusters of pink flowers, cultivated for its sweet, edible nuts known as macadamia nuts.
Australian tree grown for edible nuts

Examples

Macadamia ternifolia is extensively cultivated in subtropical regions for its delicious nuts.
The pink blossoms of Macadamia ternifolia make it an attractive addition to botanical gardens.
Farmers in Hawaii have invested heavily in Macadamia ternifolia orchards due to global demand for macadamia nuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macadamia nut - the edible nut produced by the Macadamia ternifolia tree

Etymology

Macadamia named after John Macadam, a Scottish-Australian chemist; 'ternifolia' from Latin meaning 'with three-parted leaves'.
