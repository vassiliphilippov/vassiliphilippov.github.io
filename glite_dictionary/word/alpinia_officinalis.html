Alpinia officinalis

1. NOUN

/ ælˈpɪniə ˌɑːfɪsɪˈnælɪs / (AmE), / ælˈpɪniə ˌɒfɪsɪˈnælɪs / (BrE)
A Chinese perennial plant with pyramidal clusters of white flowers and pungent aromatic roots, used medicinally and as a flavoring agent.
Chinese plant used in medicine and flavoring

Examples

The roots of Alpinia officinalis are often used in traditional Chinese medicine.
The chef added a small amount of Alpinia officinalis to enhance the flavor of the dish.
Botanists studied the blooming patterns of Alpinia officinalis in its native habitat.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Alpinia' (named after Italian botanist Prospero Alpini) and species name 'officinalis' meaning 'used in medicine'.
