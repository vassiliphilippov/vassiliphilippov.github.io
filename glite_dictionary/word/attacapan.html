Attacapan

1. NOUN

/ ˌætəˈkæpən / (AmE), / ˌætəˈkæpən / (BrE)
the language spoken by the Atakapa people of the Gulf Coast in Louisiana and Texas
language of the Gulf Coast Atakapa people

Examples

Linguists have studied Attacapan to understand the history of Native American languages in the region.
Few fluent speakers of Attacapan survived into the twentieth century.
The documentary explores efforts to preserve Attacapan through recordings and written records.

Etymology

From Atakapa, the name of the indigenous people.

2. NOUN

/ əˈtækəˌpæn / (AmE), / əˈtækəˌpæn / (BrE)
a member of an indigenous people formerly living along the Gulf Coast of Louisiana and Texas
member of an indigenous Gulf Coast people

Examples

The Attacapan once inhabited areas now part of southwestern Louisiana and southeastern Texas.
Historians have studied the traditions of the Attacapan for insights into Gulf Coast history.
Artifacts attributed to the Attacapan have been found near ancient village sites.

Etymology

From French *Attakapa*, name given to the people by neighboring Choctaw, meaning 'man-eater'.
