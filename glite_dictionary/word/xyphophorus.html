Xyphophorus

1. NOUN

/ zaɪˈfɒfərəs / (AmE), / zaɪˈfɒfərəs / (BrE)
A genus of small live-bearing freshwater fish in the family Poeciliidae, known as swordtails and platies, native to North and Central America.
a genus of freshwater fish (swordtails and platies)

Examples

Many aquarists keep Xyphophorus in their community tanks due to their peaceful temperament.
The genus Xyphophorus includes both swordtails and platies, which are popular aquarium fish.
Researchers study Xyphophorus species to understand genetics and behavioral ecology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swordtail - a small freshwater fish with a long, sword-like tail fin

Etymology

From Greek 'xiphos' (sword) and 'phoros' (bearing), referring to the sword-like tail of some species.
