balsa

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑlsə / (AmE), / ˈbælsə / (BrE)
a tropical American tree with soft, light wood, often used in model-making and rafts
tropical tree with very light wood

Examples

The model airplane was constructed from balsa because of its light weight.
They built a raft out of balsa to float down the river.
This region is known for its abundance of balsa trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balsa wood - the lightweight wood from the balsa tree

Etymology

From Spanish *balsa* (raft), due to its wood's use in floatation devices.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːl.sə / (AmE), / ˈbɔːl.sə / (BrE)
A lightweight, strong wood from the balsa tree, often used for making model airplanes, floats, and insulation.
light, strong wood from the balsa tree

Examples

Model airplanes are often constructed from balsa because it is light and easy to shape.
The artist used blocks of balsa for carving small sculptures.
Life jackets can contain balsa wood to provide buoyancy.

Etymology

From Spanish 'balsa' meaning 'raft', referring to its use in making rafts.
