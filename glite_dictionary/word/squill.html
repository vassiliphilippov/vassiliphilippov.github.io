squill

1. NOUN

/ skwɪl / (AmE), / skwɪl / (BrE)
a Mediterranean plant with dense spikes of small white flowers and a bulb used as a medicinal drug, especially as an expectorant and cardiac stimulant.
medicinal Mediterranean plant with white flowers

Examples

The pharmacist recommended a syrup containing squill for his persistent cough.
Several traditional remedies use extracts from the squill plant.
In spring, the fields near the coast bloom with wild squill.

Etymology

From Latin 'scilla', from Greek 'skilla' (a type of sea onion).

3. NOUN

/ skwɪl / (AmE), / skwɪl / (BrE)
a bulbous Old World plant of the genus Scilla, with narrow leaves and racemes of pink, blue, or white flowers, sometimes used in traditional medicine.
a bulbous flowering plant of the genus Scilla

Examples

The squill produces beautiful blue flowers in the early spring.
Traditional remedies sometimes used the dried bulb of the squill for medicinal purposes.
We planted several squill bulbs in the rock garden to add color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scilla - a plant genus closely related to squill

Etymology

From Latin 'scilla', from Greek 'skilla' or 'skilla', a kind of sea onion.
