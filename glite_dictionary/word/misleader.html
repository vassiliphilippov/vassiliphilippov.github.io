misleader

1. NOUN

/ mɪsˈlidər / (AmE), / mɪsˈliːdə / (BrE)
a person who causes others to go astray or believe something untrue, often deliberately
person who misleads others

Examples

The politician was accused of being a misleader after spreading false information to the public.
A true leader inspires trust, but a misleader manipulates others for personal gain.
Beware of any misleader who promises quick solutions without evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceiver - a person who causes someone to believe something that is not true
  • Antonym: guide - a person who shows others the way; one who leads properly

Etymology

Formed from 'mislead' (from Old English 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'lǣdan' (to lead)) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
