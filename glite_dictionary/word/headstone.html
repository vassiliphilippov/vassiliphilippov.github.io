headstone

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈhɛd.stəʊn / (BrE)
the central stone at the top of an arch or vault, holding the structure together
central stone at arch's top

Examples

The architect carefully placed the headstone to complete the archway.
Without a properly fitted headstone, the arch could collapse under its own weight.
Masons selected a perfectly shaped headstone for the ancient stone bridge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keystone - the central stone at the summit of an arch

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈhɛd.stəʊn / (BrE)
a stone placed at the head of a grave, typically inscribed with the deceased's name and dates
grave marker stone

Examples

The family placed fresh flowers next to the headstone every Sunday.
His name and dates were engraved on the headstone in elegant script.
After years of weathering, the inscription on the headstone became difficult to read.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tombstone - stone marker placed at a grave

Etymology

Formed from 'head' + 'stone', literally meaning a stone at the head of something (originally of a grave).
