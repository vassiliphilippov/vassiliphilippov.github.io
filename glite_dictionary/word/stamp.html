stamp

1. NOUN

/ stæmp / (AmE), / stæmp / (BrE)
an officially issued mark or token, especially a small piece of paper, used as proof of payment or as a medium of payment for a specific purpose, such as mailing letters
official mark or token for payment

Examples

He placed a stamp on the envelope before mailing the letter.
Food assistance is sometimes distributed through government-issued stamps.
Each stamp serves as proof that the required fee has been paid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postage stamp - a small sticker used as payment for postal services

Etymology

From Middle English *stampe*, from Old English *stempan* (to stamp, press)
