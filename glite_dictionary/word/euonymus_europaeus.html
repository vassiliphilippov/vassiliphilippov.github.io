Euonymus europaeus

1. NOUN

/ juˈɑː.nɪ.məs ˌjʊː.ɹəˈpiː.əs / (AmE), / juːˈɒn.ɪ.məs ˌjʊə.rəˈpiː.əs / (BrE)
A small, upright deciduous shrub native to Europe, noted for its tough white wood and purgative bark and fruit; also called spindle tree.
a deciduous European shrub; spindle tree

Examples

The Euonymus europaeus in our garden produces bright pink fruits in autumn.
Historically, Euonymus europaeus was valued for its tough wood, used to make spindles.
Gardeners often plant Euonymus europaeus for its ornamental berries and vibrant fall foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spindle tree - common name for Euonymus europaeus

Etymology

From Latin 'Euonymus' (a plant genus), with 'europaeus' meaning 'European'.
