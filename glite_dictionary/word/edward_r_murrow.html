Edward R. Murrow

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛd.wɚd ɑːr ˈmɝː.roʊ / (AmE), / ˈɛd.wəd ɑː ˈmʌr.əʊ / (BrE)
(1908–1965), American broadcast journalist known for his influential reports from London during World War II and his pioneering work in radio and television journalism.
influential American broadcast journalist (1908–1965)

Examples

Edward R. Murrow gained fame for his live broadcasts during the London Blitz.
Many journalists cite Edward R. Murrow as a major influence on modern news reporting.
The legacy of Edward R. Murrow lives on through the awards named after him.
