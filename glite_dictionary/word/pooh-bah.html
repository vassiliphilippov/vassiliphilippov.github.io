pooh-bah

1. NOUN

/ ˈpuːˌbɑː / (AmE), / ˈpuːˌbɑː / (BrE)
a person who has a high opinion of their own importance or authority, often in a self-important or pompous way
self-important or pompous person

Examples

Ever since his promotion, Mark has acted like a pooh-bah around the office.
The town council is run by a few local pooh-bahs who rarely listen to others.
Don’t let yourself become a pooh-bah just because you got a new title.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bigwig - an important or influential person
  • Synonym: grandee - a person of high rank or eminence
  • Antonym: nobody - a person of no importance or influence

Etymology

From the name Pooh-Bah, a character in Gilbert and Sullivan’s operetta 'The Mikado' (1885), who holds many high offices and is pompous.
