John Paul I

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn pɑl ðə fɜrst / (AmE), / dʒɒn pɔːl ðə fɜːst / (BrE)
(1912–1978) Catholic Pope who reigned in 1978 for only 34 days, the first to assume a double papal name.
Catholic Pope who reigned for 34 days in 1978

Examples

John Paul I was elected pope in August 1978 but died unexpectedly after only a month in office.
Many Catholics still remember the brief pontificate of John Paul I.
Historians often discuss the legacy of John Paul I and his impact despite his short reign.
