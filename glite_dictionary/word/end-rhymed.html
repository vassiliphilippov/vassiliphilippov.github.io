end-rhymed

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛnd ˌraɪmd / (AmE), / ˈɛnd ˌraɪmd / (BrE)
(of verse) having rhymes at the ends of lines.
verse with rhymes at line ends

Examples

The poem was entirely end-rhymed, giving it a musical quality.
Many traditional ballads feature end-rhymed stanzas for lyrical effect.
Her sonnet followed a strict end-rhymed pattern.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: internal rhyme - rhyme within a single line of poetry
  • Synonym: rhyme - correspondence of sounds between words or endings of words
