inconvenient

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.kənˈviː.ni.ənt / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kənˈviː.ni.ənt / (BrE)
Causing trouble or difficulty; not suited to one's needs, plans, or comfort.
causing trouble or difficulty

Examples

The road closure was very inconvenient for local residents trying to get to work.
He apologized for making such an inconvenient request at the last minute.
Their flight was scheduled at an inconvenient time early in the morning.

Synset Examples

an early departure is inconvenient for us

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convenient - fitting well with one's needs, causing no trouble or difficulty

Etymology

From Latin inconveniens, inconvenient-, from in- (not) + conveniens (suitable).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪnkənˈviːniənt / (AmE), / ˌɪnkənˈviːniənt / (BrE)
not suited to someone's comfort, purpose, or needs; causing difficulty or discomfort.
not suited to comfort or needs; causing difficulty

Examples

The store's limited hours are really inconvenient for people who work late.
It would be inconvenient for me to meet at that time, as I have another appointment.
An inconvenient location made it hard for guests to attend the party.

Synset Examples

it is inconvenient not to have a telephone in the kitchen
the back hall is an inconvenient place for the telephone

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convenient - fitting well with one's needs or comfort
  • Synonym: awkward - causing difficulty or discomfort

Etymology

From Latin inconveniens, inconvenient- (unsuitable), from in- (not) + conveniens (fitting).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00607505-a
Synset POS: a
