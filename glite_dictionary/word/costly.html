costly

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɔstli / (AmE), / ˈkɒstli / (BrE)
involving great loss, sacrifice, or suffering; gained or achieved at a significant price
involving great loss or sacrifice

Examples

The victory was costly, with many soldiers dying in the final battle.
Her decision to keep fighting proved to be a costly mistake for the company.
They achieved success, but at a costly personal and financial price.

Synset Examples

a dearly-won victory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expensive - having a high price or requiring substantial expenditure

Etymology

From cost + -ly, meaning 'having the nature of cost.'

Synset Examples
