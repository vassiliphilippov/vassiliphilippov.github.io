crescent

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrɛsənt / (AmE), / ˈkrɛsənt / (BrE)
shaped like a segment of a circle, tapering to points at the ends, resembling the new moon.
shaped like a segment of the moon

Examples

The crescent beach curved around the bay, glistening in the morning sun.
She wore a delicate silver necklace with a crescent pendant.
A row of crescent pastries filled the display case at the bakery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arc-shaped - having the shape of an arc or curve
  • Synonym: convex - having a surface that curves outward

Etymology

From Old French 'creissant', from Latin 'crescens' (growing), present participle of 'crescere' (to grow).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɛsənt / (AmE), / ˈkrɛsənt / (BrE)
a curved shape that is wide in the middle and tapers to points at both ends, resembling the shape of the moon in its first or last quarter
curved shape like a quarter moon

Examples

A sliver of cheese was cut in a crescent shape for the appetizer plate.
The crescent of land curved around the bay, sheltering the small village from the wind.
He admired the elegant crescent formed by the delicate silver necklace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arc - a part of the circumference of a circle or other curve
  • Degree Higher: circle - a round plane figure whose boundary consists of points equidistant from the center

Etymology

From Old French *crescente*, from Latin *crescens* (growing), present participle of *crescere* (to grow).

Synset POS: n
