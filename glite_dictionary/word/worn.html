worn

1. ADJ

/ wɔrn / (AmE), / wɔːn / (BrE)
Damaged or altered by long use or exposure; showing signs of wear.
damaged by long use

Examples

His shoes looked worn after years of daily use.
The worn pages of the book showed how often it had been read.
She replaced the worn carpet with a brand new one.

Synset Examples

worn threads on the screw
a worn suit
the worn pockets on the jacket

Related Terms

  • Antonym: new - not previously used or worn
  • Synonym: used - having been used before; not new

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'wear', from Old English 'werian'.

2. ADJ

/ wɔrn / (AmE), / wɔːn / (BrE)
looking tired and affected by overwork, worry, or suffering; showing visible signs of fatigue or hardship
looking tired or showing signs of distress

Examples

After weeks of sleepless nights, his face looked especially worn and tired.
She gave a worn smile that betrayed how much she had been through lately.
The old professor appeared worn from years of hard work and responsibility.

Synset Examples

looking careworn as she bent over her mending
her face was drawn and haggard from sleeplessness
that raddled but still noble face
shocked to see the worn look of his handsome young face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haggard - looking exhausted and unwell, especially from fatigue, worry, or suffering
  • Synonym: careworn - showing the effects of worry or anxiety
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02441812-s
Synset POS: s
