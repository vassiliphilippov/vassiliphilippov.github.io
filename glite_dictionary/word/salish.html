Salish

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪʃ / (BrE)
a member of a group of Indigenous peoples of the northwest coast of North America who speak a Salishan language
Indigenous group of northwest North America

Examples

The Salish have a rich cultural heritage rooted in the Pacific Northwest of the United States and Canada.
Descendants of the Salish still maintain many traditional customs and practices.
Scholars are working to revitalize the Salish language among the younger generation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Salishan - a family of languages spoken by the Salish peoples

Etymology

From the name in a Salishan language for their people, generally rendered as 'Salish'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪʃ / (BrE)
a family of Indigenous languages spoken in the northwestern United States and western Canada, or a member of the people who speak these languages
language family of the Pacific Northwest

Examples

Lushootseed is one of the Salish languages spoken by native peoples in Washington State.
The Salish language family includes over twenty distinct languages and dialects.
Linguists have documented efforts to revitalize many Salish languages in recent years.

Etymology

From Salish, the self-designation of several native groups of the northwestern United States and British Columbia.
