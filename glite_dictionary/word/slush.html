slush

1. VERB

/ slʌʃ / (AmE), / slʌʃ / (BrE)
to spill or splash a liquid heavily or clumsily
spill or splash copiously

Examples

Be careful not to slush water onto the floor when filling the bucket.
She slushed paint onto the canvas without much concern for neatness.
Rain slushed down the windows and pooled along the sill.

Synset Examples

slosh paint all over the walls

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to slosh - to splash or spill liquid in a messy or vigorous way
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01377252-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ slʌʃ / (AmE), / slʌʃ / (BrE)
partially melted snow, typically soft and watery, often found on roads and sidewalks after snow begins to thaw.
partially melted snow

Examples

Be careful driving; there's a lot of slush on the roads this morning.
Her boots were soaked after she walked through the slush in the parking lot.
Children love to play in the slush after a big snowfall starts to melt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snow - frozen precipitation in the form of white flakes

Etymology

Perhaps related to obsolete Norwegian *slusj* (thin mud), or of imitative origin.
