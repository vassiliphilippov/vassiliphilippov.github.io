anti-Catholicism

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.taɪ kəˈθæ.lɪˌsɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti kəˈθæl.ɪ.sɪ.zəm / (BrE)
Hostility to or prejudice against the Roman Catholic Church or its adherents.
hostility or prejudice against Catholicism

Examples

The nineteenth century saw a rise in anti-Catholicism in various Western countries.
Expressions of anti-Catholicism often accompanied political and religious tensions.
He wrote a book examining the history of anti-Catholicism in the United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antisemitism - hostility to or prejudice against Jews
  • Antonym: Catholicism - the faith, practice, and church of Roman Catholics

Etymology

Formed by combining anti- (against) with Catholicism.
