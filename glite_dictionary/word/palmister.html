palmister

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːmɪstər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːmɪstə / (BrE)
A person who claims to predict the future by interpreting the lines and features of a person's palm.
one who predicts future from palms

Examples

The palmister studied the intricate lines on Maria's hand before offering her predictions.
After visiting a palmister, Jake couldn't stop wondering about his future.
She became fascinated with palmistry and aspired to train as a palmister herself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palmist - a person who interprets palms to predict the future; primary variant term
  • Synonym: fortuneteller - a person who claims to predict people's future

Etymology

Formed from 'palm' + '-ister', variant of 'palmist' + agent suffix.
