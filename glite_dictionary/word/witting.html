witting

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
knowing or aware of something; done consciously or deliberately
aware or knowing; conscious

Examples

He was a witting accomplice to the crime, fully aware of what he was doing.
The report revealed the existence of a witting participant in the secret operation.
She gave her approval as a witting decision, not by accident.

Synset Examples

a witting tool of the Communists

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unwitting - not aware or conscious; unintentional

Etymology

From Middle English witen (to know), from Old English witan.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00194357-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
done intentionally or with full knowledge; deliberate or conscious
done intentionally; deliberate

Examples

She made a witting decision to share the confidential information with her supervisor.
His witting involvement in the fraud was discovered during the investigation.
There is a clear difference between witting and unwitting participation in the scheme.

Synset Examples

a conscious effort to speak more slowly
a conscious policy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unwitting - not done intentionally; unaware or unintentional
  • Synonym: deliberate - done consciously and intentionally

Etymology

From Old English 'witan' (to know), related to 'wit'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01340720-s
Synset POS: s
