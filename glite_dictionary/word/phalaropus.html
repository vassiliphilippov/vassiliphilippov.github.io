Phalaropus

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlærəˌpoʊs / (AmE), / fəˈlarəpəs / (BrE)
A genus of small wading birds known as phalaropes, distinguished by their lobed toes and unique feeding behavior.
genus of wading birds: phalaropes

Examples

The Phalaropus species are known for their unusual mating roles among shorebirds.
Researchers spotted a rare Phalaropus in the wetland reserve during migration season.
Unlike most waders, Phalaropus swim and feed while spinning in circles on the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phalarope - Wading bird of the Phalaropus genus

Etymology

From Greek 'phalaris' (coot) + 'pous, podos' (foot).

2. NOUN

/ fəˈlærəpəs / (AmE), / fəˈlarəpəs / (BrE)
The genus of wading birds in the family Phalaropidae, commonly known as phalaropes, characterized by their slender bills and lobed toes.
genus of wading birds (phalaropes)

Examples

The genus Phalaropus includes three species of phalaropes found across the Northern Hemisphere.
Scientists have studied the migratory patterns of birds in the Phalaropus genus extensively.
Members of Phalaropus are known for their unique swimming behavior and spinning movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phalarope - a species of small wading bird in the genus Phalaropus

Etymology

From Ancient Greek phalaris (coot) + pous, podos (foot), referring to the bird's lobed feet.
