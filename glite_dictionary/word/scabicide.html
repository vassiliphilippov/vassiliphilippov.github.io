scabicide

1. NOUN

/ ˈskæbəˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈskæbɪˌsaɪd / (BrE)
a drug used to destroy the itch mite that causes scabies
drug that kills scabies mites
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a scabicide to treat the patient's persistent rash.
All household members applied a scabicide lotion to prevent the spread of infection.
Scabies outbreaks are controlled by using an effective scabicide and washing bedding thoroughly.

Etymology

From Latin 'scabies' (scab, itch) + '-cide' (killer from Latin 'caedere', to kill).
