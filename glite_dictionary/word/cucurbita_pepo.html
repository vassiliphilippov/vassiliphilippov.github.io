Cucurbita pepo

1. NOUN

/ kuːˌkɝː.bɪ.tə ˈpiː.poʊ / (AmE), / kjuːˌkɜː.bɪ.tə ˈpiː.pəʊ / (BrE)
a species of squash plant cultivated for its large, round orange fruit, including pumpkins and some summer and autumn squashes
species of squash plant including pumpkins

Examples

Cucurbita pepo is the scientific name for the common pumpkin found in many gardens and markets.
Many varieties of squash, such as zucchini and pattypan, are classified under Cucurbita pepo.
Botanists study Cucurbita pepo to learn more about the domestication of cultivated gourds.

Etymology

From Latin, where 'cucurbita' means 'gourd' or 'squash'.
