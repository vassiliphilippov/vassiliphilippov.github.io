laparoscopy

1. NOUN

/ ləˈpɛrəˌskɑpi / (AmE), / ləˈpærəskəpi / (BrE)
A surgical procedure in which a fiber-optic instrument is inserted through the abdominal wall to view the organs in the abdomen or to perform minor surgery, typically using small incisions.
minimally invasive abdominal surgery
medical

Examples

The surgeon recommended a laparoscopy to diagnose the source of her abdominal pain.
A laparoscopy is less invasive than traditional open surgery and usually requires less recovery time.
She underwent a laparoscopy to check for ovarian cysts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laparotomy - surgical incision into the abdominal cavity

Etymology

From Greek *lapara* (flank, loin) + *-scopy* (to look or examine).
