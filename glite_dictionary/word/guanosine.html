guanosine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡwɑːnəˌsiːn / (AmE), / ˈɡwɑːnəsiːn / (BrE)
a compound consisting of guanine linked to a ribose sugar, found in RNA and sometimes in DNA as a nucleoside
nucleoside formed from guanine and sugar
medical

Examples

The enzyme specifically removes guanosine residues from the RNA strand.
Researchers measured the levels of guanosine to study cell metabolism.
Supplementing with guanosine can influence certain neurological functions in laboratory studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adenosine - a nucleoside formed from adenine and ribose
  • Synonym: guanine - a purine base found in DNA and RNA
  • Synonym: thymidine - a nucleoside composed of thymine and deoxyribose

Etymology

From guanine + -os(e) + -ine, denoting a nucleoside.
