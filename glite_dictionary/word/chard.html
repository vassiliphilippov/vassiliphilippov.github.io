chard

1. NOUN

/ tʃɑrd / (AmE), / tʃɑːd / (BrE)
A type of beet grown for its edible leaves and stalks, which are used as a vegetable; unlike the common beet, it does not have a swollen root.
vegetable plant with edible leaves and stalks

Examples

We added sautéed chard to the pasta for extra flavor and color.
She grows chard in her garden each summer alongside tomatoes and basil.
The recipe calls for fresh chard leaves and their colorful stems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beet - vegetable grown for its swollen root or edible leaves

Etymology

From French *carde*, from Latin *carduus* (thistle, artichoke thistle).

2. NOUN

/ tʃɑrd / (AmE), / tʃɑːd / (BrE)
A leafy vegetable with large green leaves and thick, succulent white stalks, eaten as a vegetable.
leafy vegetable with thick stalks

Examples

The recipe calls for fresh chard, so I picked some from the garden.
She sautéed the chard with garlic and olive oil for dinner.
Farmers at the market offered several varieties of chard alongside kale and spinach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Swiss chard - A variety of chard with dark green leaves and thick white stalks
  • Synonym: spinach - A green leafy vegetable often used similarly to chard

Etymology

From French 'carde', from Latin 'carduus' (thistle, artichoke).

3. NOUN

/ tʃɑrd / (AmE), / tʃɑːd / (BrE)
A variety of beet grown for its large, edible leaves and stalks rather than its root.
leafy vegetable; type of beet

Examples

I added fresh chard to the soup for extra flavor and nutrition.
The garden is full of colorful Swiss chard ready to be harvested.
You can sauté chard leaves with garlic for a tasty side dish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beet - a plant with a swollen edible root, Beta vulgaris

Etymology

From French *carde* (artichoke thistle), ultimately from Latin *carduus* (thistle).
