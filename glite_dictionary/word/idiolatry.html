idiolatry

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪdiˈɑlətri / (AmE), / ˌɪdɪˈɒlətri / (BrE)
The worship or excessive adoration of oneself; self-worship.
worship of oneself; self-worship

Examples

His obsession with his appearance bordered on idiolatry.
The philosopher warned against the dangers of idiolatry in modern society.
Some critics accused the celebrity of encouraging idiolatry among his followers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: narcissism - excessive self-love or admiration

Etymology

From Greek 'idios' (one's own, personal) + 'latreia' (worship).
