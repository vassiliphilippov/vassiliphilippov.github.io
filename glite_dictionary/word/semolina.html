semolina

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛməˈlinə / (AmE), / ˌsɛmɒˈliːnə / (BrE)
a coarse, pale yellow flour made from durum wheat, used in making pasta and other dishes
coarse flour from durum wheat, used in pasta

Examples

The chef used semolina to prepare fresh homemade pasta.
Some traditional desserts are made with semolina and milk.
Durum wheat is commonly milled into semolina for use in couscous and spaghetti.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flour - powder made by grinding grains, used in baking and cooking
  • Degree Higher: pasta - food made from dough of durum wheat and water, formed into various shapes

Etymology

From Italian 'semolino', diminutive of 'semola', from Latin 'simila' (fine flour).
