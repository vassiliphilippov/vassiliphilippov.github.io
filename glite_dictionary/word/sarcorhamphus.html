Sarcorhamphus

1. NOUN

/ sɑɹ.korˈræm.fəs / (AmE), / sɑː.kɔːˈræm.fəs / (BrE)
A genus of large South and Central American vultures, usually including only the king vulture.
genus of vultures (king vulture)

Examples

The Sarcorhamphus genus is represented today by a single living species, the king vulture.
Researchers studied the fossil record of Sarcorhamphus to understand its evolutionary history.
The distinctive features of Sarcorhamphus set it apart from other vulture genera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king vulture - a large vulture of Central and South America (Sarcorhamphus papa)

Etymology

From Greek sarkos (flesh) + rhamphos (beak), referring to the vulture's fleshy beak.

2. NOUN

/ sɑrˌkɔrˈræmfəs / (AmE), / sɑːˌkɔːˈræmfəs / (BrE)
a genus of large New World vultures, typically containing only the king vulture.
genus of vultures (includes king vulture)

Examples

The Sarcorhamphus genus is known primarily for the king vulture.
Scientists have classified the king vulture in the genus Sarcorhamphus.
Studying Sarcorhamphus helps ornithologists understand vulture evolution in South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king vulture - a large, colorful New World vulture, sole species of Sarcorhamphus

Etymology

From Greek sarkos (flesh) + rhamphos (beak).
