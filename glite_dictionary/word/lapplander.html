Lapplander

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæpˌlændər / (AmE), / ˈlæpˌlændə / (BrE)
a member of the indigenous nomadic people living in northern Scandinavia, traditionally known for herding reindeer; now often referred to as Sami
member of northern Scandinavian indigenous people

Examples

The Lapplander people have a rich tradition of storytelling and music.
Historically, a Lapplander would travel great distances with their reindeer herds.
Many Lapplanders now prefer to be called Sami, reflecting their own language and identity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sami - member of the indigenous Finno-Ugric people of northern Scandinavia, preferred modern term

Etymology

From 'Lapland,' the traditional name for the region inhabited by these people, plus '-er' denoting origin or association.
