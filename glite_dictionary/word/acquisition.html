acquisition

1. NOUN

/ ˌækwəzɪˈʃən / (AmE), / ˌækwɪˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
something that has been obtained, especially an addition to a collection or possession.
something obtained or added

Examples

The new sculpture was a significant acquisition for the city's art museum.
Her most recent acquisition is a vintage car from the 1960s.
The company announced the acquisition of several start-up firms this year.

Synset Examples

a recent acquisition by the museum

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purchase - a thing that has been bought

Etymology

From Latin acquisitio (the act of getting or acquiring), from acquīrere (to acquire).
