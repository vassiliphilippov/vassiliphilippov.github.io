Quebec

1. PROPN

/ kəˈbɛk / (AmE), / kəˈbɛk / (BrE)
the largest province of Canada, located in the eastern part of the country, noted for its French-speaking population and cultural heritage
largest province of Canada

Examples

Many people visit Quebec to experience its unique blend of French and Canadian cultures.
The official language of Quebec is French, unlike most other Canadian provinces.
Winter in Quebec can be very cold, but the province is famous for its lively festivals.

Etymology

Named after the Algonquin word *kebec* meaning 'where the river narrows'.

2. PROPN

/ kəˈbɛk / (AmE), / kwɪˈbɛk / (BrE)
The French-speaking capital city of the province of Quebec, Canada, located on the Saint Lawrence River.
capital city of Quebec province, Canada

Examples

We spent a week exploring the historic streets of Quebec.
The annual winter carnival in Quebec attracts thousands of visitors from around the world.
Quebec is known for its beautiful architecture and rich French heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Montreal - largest city in Quebec province, Canada

Etymology

Named after the Algonquin word 'kebec' meaning 'where the river narrows'.
