furiously

1. ADV

/ ˈfjʊriəsli / (AmE), / ˈfjʊəriəsli / (BrE)
In an impassioned, very angry, or extremely energetic manner.
very angrily or with great energy

Examples

The crowd protested furiously when the referee made a controversial call.
She worked furiously to finish the project before the deadline.
He typed furiously on his laptop, determined to meet the editor's demands.

Synset Examples

she screamed furiously at her tormentors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angrily - in a manner showing anger

Etymology

From 'furious' + '-ly'; ultimately from Latin 'furiosus' (full of rage).

Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ˈfjʊriəsli / (AmE), / ˈfjʊəriəsli / (BrE)
In a manner showing intense or violent energy or emotion.
with intense or violent energy

Examples

The crowd cheered furiously as the team scored a last-minute goal.
She typed furiously to meet her deadline.
Waves crashed furiously against the rocky shore during the storm.

Synset Examples

the boys fought furiously
she went peddling furiously up the narrow street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angrily - in a manner showing anger
  • Antonym: calmly - in a peaceful or relaxed way

Etymology

From 'furious' + '-ly'; 'furious' from Latin 'furiosus' (mad, raging).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00226035-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˈfjʊriəsli / (AmE), / ˈfjʊəriəsli / (BrE)
In a wild, stormy, or violent manner; with great intensity or force, especially of the elements.
in a wild and stormy manner

Examples

The wind howled furiously around the small cabin all night long.
Rain lashed against the windows furiously as the storm intensified.
The waves crashed furiously onto the shore, eroding the sand.

Synset Examples

winds were blowing furiously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violently - with great force or intensity
  • Antonym: calmly - in a peaceful or gentle manner

Etymology

From 'furious' + '-ly'; 'furious' from Latin 'furiosus' (full of rage).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00225764-r
Synset POS: r
