Mammea

1. NOUN

/ mæˈmiːə / (AmE), / mæˈmiːə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American and Asian trees that bear edible, single-seeded fruit, including the mammee apple.
genus of tropical fruit trees

Examples

The Mammea tree is cultivated for its large, juicy fruit in tropical regions.
Botanists often study the unique characteristics of Mammea species found in both Asia and the Americas.
The fruit of the Mammea is prized in some cultures for its sweet taste and nutritional value.

Etymology

From New Latin, taken from Carib or Taino origins referring to the mammee apple.

2. NOUN

/ məˈmiːə / (AmE), / məˈmiːə / (BrE)
A genus of American and Asian trees bearing edible one-seeded fruit, including species such as the mammee apple.
genus of fruit-bearing trees

Examples

The Mammea genus includes the widely cultivated mammee apple tree.
Botanists have long studied the fruiting patterns of trees in the Mammea genus.
Some species of Mammea produce edible fruit valued in local cuisines.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin 'Mammea', adopted as the genus name.
