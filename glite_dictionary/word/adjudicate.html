to adjudicate

1. VERB

/ əˈdʒudɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / əˈdʒuːdɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to make an official decision about a dispute or problem, especially in a court of law; to settle conclusively
to settle a dispute officially

Examples

The committee will adjudicate the dispute between the two parties.
It took the referee several minutes to adjudicate on the contested goal.
A third party was brought in to adjudicate the business disagreement.

Synset Examples

The case was decided
The judge decided the case in favor of the plaintiff
The father adjudicated when the sons were quarreling over their inheritance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decide - to make a choice or judgment about something
  • Synonym: to arbitrate - to reach an authoritative judgment or settlement

Etymology

From Latin adjudicatus, past participle of adjudicare (to judge).

2. VERB

/ əˈdʒuːdɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / əˈdʒuːdɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to make an official decision about who is right in a dispute or competition, especially in a court of law
act as judge in a dispute or case

Examples

The panel will adjudicate the competition and announce the winners next week.
It took several months for the court to adjudicate the complex lawsuit.
An independent body was appointed to adjudicate disputes arising from the contract.

Synset Examples

The football star was tried for the murder of his wife
The judge tried both father and son in separate trials

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judge - to form an opinion or decision about something, especially as a legal authority

Etymology

From Latin 'adjudicare' (to judge, award), from 'ad-' (to) + 'judicare' (to judge).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02506927-v
Synset POS: v
