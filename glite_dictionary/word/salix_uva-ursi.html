Salix uva-ursi

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlɪks ˌjuːvəˈɜːrsaɪ / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪks ˌjuːvəˈɜːsaɪ / (BrE)
A dwarf, mat-forming shrub native to Arctic and alpine regions of North America and Greenland, with deep green, elliptic leaves tapering toward the base.
dwarf Arctic/alpine willow shrub

Examples

The Salix uva-ursi thrives in harsh Arctic tundra environments where few other shrubs can survive.
Botanists study Salix uva-ursi to understand plant adaptation in extreme cold climates.
We spotted a patch of Salix uva-ursi while hiking above the treeline in Greenland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - any tree or shrub of the genus Salix

Etymology

From Latin: *Salix* (willow) and *uva-ursi* (bear's grape), referring to plant features.
