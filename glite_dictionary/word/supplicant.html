supplicant

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌpləkənt / (AmE), / ˈsʌplɪkənt / (BrE)
a person who humbly and earnestly asks or prays for something; a petitioner or beggar, especially in a religious or formal context
one who humbly asks or prays for something

Examples

The desperate supplicant knelt before the altar in silent prayer.
Outside the king's palace, a supplicant awaited an audience to plead for mercy.
At the temple, each supplicant brought offerings and hopes for a better future.

Synset Examples

a suppliant for her favors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppliant - a person making a humble plea to someone in power or authority

Etymology

From Latin supplicantem, present participle of supplicare 'to beseech'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌplɪkənt / (AmE), / ˈsʌplɪkənt / (BrE)
a person who humbly asks for something, especially in prayer or petition to God or a higher authority
person who prays or requests humbly

Examples

The supplicant knelt at the altar with hands clasped in fervent prayer.
In times of crisis, many people become supplicants, seeking divine guidance or intervention.
As a humble supplicant, she asked for forgiveness and a chance to make things right.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petitioner - a person who formally requests something from an authority
  • Synonym: beseecher - someone who earnestly requests or pleads for something
  • Antonym: benefactor - a person who gives help, opposite of someone seeking help

Etymology

From Latin supplicant-, present participle of supplicare (to entreat, beg).

3. ADJ

/ ˈsʌplɪkənt / (AmE), / ˈsʌplɪkənt / (BrE)
humbly or earnestly asking for something; making a plea with humility or submission
humbly entreating; pleading

Examples

His voice took on a supplicant tone as he begged for a second chance.
The petitioners knelt in a supplicant posture before the altar.
With supplicant eyes, she pleaded for understanding and forgiveness.

Synset Examples

a suppliant sinner seeking forgiveness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppliant - humbly or earnestly begging or asking
  • Antonym: demanding - insisting forcefully; not humble in request

Etymology

From Latin supplicantem, present participle of supplicare (to beseech, to entreat).
