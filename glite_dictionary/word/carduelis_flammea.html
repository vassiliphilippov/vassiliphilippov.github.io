Carduelis flammea

1. NOUN

/ kɑːrˈduːəlɪs flæˈmiːə / (AmE), / kɑːˈdjuːəlɪs flæˈmiːə / (BrE)
A small finch of northern regions, known for its red crown and rosy breast and rump; also called the common redpoll.
species of finch with red crown; common redpoll

Examples

During winter, flocks of Carduelis flammea are often seen feeding on birch seeds.
The Carduelis flammea is easily identified by its distinctive red forehead.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a rare Carduelis flammea among the sparrows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redpoll - a small finch with a red patch on its head

Etymology

From Latin: Carduelis (a genus of finches) and flammea (flame-colored).
