calcicolous

1. ADJ

/ kælˈsɪkələs / (AmE), / kælˈsɪkələs / (BrE)
(of a plant or organism) growing or living in soil rich in lime (calcium carbonate).
growing in lime-rich soil

Examples

Several species found in the meadow are calcicolous and flourish in the area’s chalky soils.
Some calcicolous mosses only survive in limestone-rich environments.
The restoration project focused on reintroducing calcicolous plants to the ancient quarry.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calcifuge - a plant that cannot tolerate lime-rich soil

Etymology

From Latin 'calx' (lime, limestone) + '-colous' (dwelling in).
