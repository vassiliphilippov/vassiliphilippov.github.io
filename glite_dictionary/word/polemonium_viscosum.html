Polemonium viscosum

1. NOUN

/ poʊˌlɛˈmoʊniəm vɪˈkoʊsəm / (AmE), / pəʊˌlɛˈməʊniəm vɪˈkəʊsəm / (BrE)
A tall herb native to the Rocky Mountains, with sticky leaves and an unpleasant smell, belonging to the phlox family.
an herbaceous plant of the Rocky Mountains

Examples

Polemonium viscosum is often found growing in alpine meadows high in the Rocky Mountains.
The sticky leaves of Polemonium viscosum help protect it from certain insects.
Many hikers recognize Polemonium viscosum by its unpleasant odor and showy purple flowers.

Etymology

From the genus name Polemonium, originating from Latin and Greek, and the species name viscosum, meaning 'sticky' in Latin.
