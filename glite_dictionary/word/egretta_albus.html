Egretta albus

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡrɛtə ˈælbəs / (AmE), / ɪˈɡrɛtə ˈalbəs / (BrE)
a species of large, white heron commonly known as the great egret, found in the Americas
a species of white heron (great egret)

Examples

The Egretta albus can often be seen wading in shallow wetlands hunting for fish.
During migration season, many Egretta albus gather in large flocks along coastal lagoons.
Her research focused on the nesting habits of Egretta albus in Florida.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: great egret - the common name for Egretta albus, a large white wading bird

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name Egretta from French 'aigrette' (egret), species name from Latin 'albus' (white).
