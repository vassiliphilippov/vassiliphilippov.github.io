Harkat-ul-Mujahideen

1. PROPN

/ ˌhɑːrkət ʊl muːˈdʒɑːhɪˌdiːn / (AmE), / ˌhɑːkət ʊl mjuːˌdʒɑːhɪˈdiːn / (BrE)
an Islamic fundamentalist militant group founded in Pakistan, known for fighting in Afghanistan during the 1980s and subsequently conducting operations in Kashmir and other regions as a designated terrorist organization
Pakistani militant Islamist organization

Examples

The government officially designated Harkat-ul-Mujahideen as a terrorist group in the late 1990s.
Reports indicate that Harkat-ul-Mujahideen has been involved in several attacks in Kashmir.
International pressure increased after allegations against Harkat-ul-Mujahideen in cross-border militancy.

Etymology

From Arabic ḥarakat (movement) al-mujāhidīn (of the fighters, struggler), referring to Islamic fighters.
