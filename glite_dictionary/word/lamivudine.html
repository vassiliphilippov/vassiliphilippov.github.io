lamivudine

1. NOUN

/ ləˈmɪv.juːˌdin / (AmE), / ləˈmɪv.juːˌdiːn / (BrE)
a nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor used in combination with other drugs to treat HIV and AIDS
antiviral drug for HIV and AIDS
medical

Examples

Patients are often prescribed lamivudine alongside other antiretroviral drugs for HIV treatment.
The doctor explained that lamivudine helps reduce the amount of HIV in the body.
Researchers studied the effectiveness of lamivudine in slowing the progression of AIDS.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zidovudine - another nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor, often used with lamivudine
