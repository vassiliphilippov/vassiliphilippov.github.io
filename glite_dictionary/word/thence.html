thence

1. ADV

/ ðɛns / (AmE), / ðɛns / (BrE)
from that place; from there
from that place; from there
archaic

Examples

The train stopped at Berlin and thence continued to Warsaw.
He earned his degree and thence began his professional career.
Goods were shipped to the port and thence to various destinations.

Synset Examples

proceeded thence directly to college
flew to Helsinki and thence to Moscow
roads that lead therefrom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: therefrom - from that source or place

Etymology

From Old English thanon, influenced by Old French dence (from there), ultimately from Proto-Germanic elements.

2. ADV

/ ðɛns / (AmE), / ðɛns / (BrE)
as a result of that fact or action; from that place, source, or circumstance.
from that place or circumstance

Examples

The new evidence emerged, and thence the judge reconsidered the verdict.
The committee drafted a plan and thence submitted it to the council for approval.
He made a decision, and thence all his actions were guided by it.

Synset Examples

atomic formulas and all compounds thence constructible
a natural conclusion follows thence
public interest and a policy deriving therefrom
typhus fever results therefrom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: therefrom - from that (used especially in legal contexts)
  • Synonym: hence - for this reason; as a consequence

Etymology

From Old English thanon, thanon (from there), influenced by Middle English thannes.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00044204-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ðɛns / (AmE), / ðɛns / (BrE)
used to introduce a logical result, consequence, or conclusion; therefore, thus.
therefore; as a logical conclusion
archaic

Examples

The evidence was clear; thence, the jury returned a quick verdict.
He was the eldest son, thence inheriting the estate by right.
There was no food left, thence we decided to leave early.

Synset Examples

therefore X must be true
the eggs were fresh and hence satisfactory
we were young and thence optimistic
it is late and thus we must go
the witness is biased and so cannot be trusted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: therefore - for that reason; consequently
  • Synonym: thus - as a result; consequently

Etymology

From Old English 'ðanone', from 'þanon' (from thence), related to 'then' and 'there'.
