streamliner

1. NOUN

/ ˈstriːmˌlaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈstriːmˌlaɪnə / (BrE)
a train with a streamlined shape designed to reduce air resistance and increase speed
streamlined train

Examples

The streamliner raced across the countryside at record speed.
In the 1930s, the introduction of the streamliner revolutionized rail travel in America.
Rail enthusiasts often restore old streamliners for historical exhibitions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: train - a series of connected railway vehicles

Etymology

From 'streamline' + '-er', denoting something designed with a streamlined form.
