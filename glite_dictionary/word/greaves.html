greaves

1. NOUN

/ ɡrivz / (AmE), / ɡriːvz / (BrE)
the solid residue left after rendering animal fat, often used as animal feed or for other purposes
residue from rendered animal fat

Examples

The butcher collected the greaves after rendering the pork fat.
In some regions, greaves are used as an ingredient in traditional dishes.
After the fat was processed, only the greaves remained at the bottom of the pot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cracklings - crisp residue left after rendering animal fat

Etymology

From Old English *græfa* (scrap, fragment).
