demigod

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛmiˌɡɑd / (AmE), / ˈdɛmiɡɒd / (BrE)
a being who is part human and part god, especially in mythology or legend
part-human, part-god being

Examples

Some ancient myths tell of a demigod who challenged the power of the gods.
Hercules, known as a demigod, possessed strength beyond that of any mortal man.
The story describes the heroic deeds of a young demigod on a quest for immortality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hero - a person admired for courage or noble qualities; often has demi-divine status in mythology

Etymology

From Latin 'semideus' ('half-god'), from 'semi-' (half) + 'deus' (god).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛmiˌɡɑd / (AmE), / ˈdɛmiɡɒd / (BrE)
a person, often in mythology, who possesses powers or abilities greater than those of ordinary people, sometimes used figuratively for someone with exceptional talent or influence
person with extraordinary powers or abilities

Examples

Many ancient myths describe Hercules as a demigod with incredible strength.
In the eyes of his fans, the basketball star seems like a modern demigod.
Some cultures believed their kings to be demigods descended from the heavens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hero - a person admired for courage or outstanding achievements
  • Antonym: mortal - a being that must eventually die; ordinary human

Etymology

From Latin 'demigod', from 'demi-' (half) + 'god'.
