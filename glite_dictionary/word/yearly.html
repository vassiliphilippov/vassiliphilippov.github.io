yearly

1. ADJ

/ ˈjɪrli / (AmE), / ˈjɪəli / (BrE)
happening, done, or paid once every year; annual
occurring each year; annual

Examples

We schedule a yearly conference for all employees each spring.
My yearly subscription will be renewed automatically unless I cancel it.
He received a yearly bonus for outstanding performance.

Synset Examples

an annual trip to Paris
yearly medical examinations
annual (or yearly) income

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annual - happening once every year

Etymology

From Middle English 'yerely', from Old English 'ġēarlic' (yearly), from 'ġēar' (year) + '-lic' (-ly).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01976215-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈjɪrli / (AmE), / ˈjɪəli / (BrE)
a book or publication that is issued once every year, typically containing information or records for the preceding year
annual reference book or report

Examples

The financial yearly provided a comprehensive review of the company's performance.
I look forward to reading the agricultural yearly every spring for the latest farming trends.
Each library keeps a copy of the science yearly for reference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annual - a book or report published yearly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06438138-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADV

/ ˈjɪrli / (AmE), / ˈjɪəli / (BrE)
Happening once every year; annually.
once every year; annually (adv)

Examples

The company holds a yearly conference to discuss its progress.
He receives a yearly bonus at the end of December.
We visit our grandparents yearly during the summer holidays.

Synset Examples

they travel to China annually

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annually - happening every year (adv)

Etymology

From Middle English yerely, from Old English ġēārlīċe (yearly).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00082087-r
Synset POS: r
