Carpobrotus edulis

1. NOUN

/ kɑːr.poʊˈbroʊ.təs ˈɛd.jʊ.lɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɑː.pəˈbrəʊ.təs ˈed.juː.lɪs / (BrE)
A low-growing succulent plant native to South Africa, with fleshy leaves and edible fruit pulp, often used as ground cover.
edible-fruited South African succulent plant

Examples

The coastal dunes were covered with Carpobrotus edulis, providing both stability to the soil and a splash of color.
Local foragers gathered the ripe fruit of Carpobrotus edulis to make jam.
Gardeners often plant Carpobrotus edulis for its drought resistance and ornamental appeal.

Etymology

From Greek 'karpos' (fruit) + 'brotos' (edible), and Latin 'edulis' (edible).
