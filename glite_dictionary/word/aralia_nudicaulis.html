Aralia nudicaulis

1. NOUN

/ əˈreɪliə ˌnjuːdɪˈkɑlɪs / (AmE), / əˈreɪlɪə ˌnjuːdɪˈkeɪlɪs / (BrE)
a perennial herb native to central and eastern North America, with aromatic roots sometimes used as a substitute for sarsaparilla
North American perennial herb; roots used as sarsaparilla substitute

Examples

The roots of Aralia nudicaulis were commonly used by indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes.
In some regions, Aralia nudicaulis is called wild sarsaparilla because of its aromatic roots.
Botanists studied Aralia nudicaulis to better understand its habitat requirements.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Aralia' is the genus name, and 'nudicaulis' means 'bare-stemmed' in Latin.
