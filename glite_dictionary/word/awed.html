awed

1. ADJ

/ ɔd / (AmE), / ɔːd / (BrE)
filled with a feeling of reverential wonder or fear; deeply impressed or inspired by awe
filled with reverential wonder or fear

Examples

The children stood awed by the towering cathedral walls and hushed silence.
She listened in awed silence as the astronaut described walking on the Moon.
His awed expression showed just how deeply he was moved by the ancient ruins.

Synset Examples

awed by the silence
awful worshippers with bowed heads

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amazed - greatly surprised or impressed
  • Degree Lower: fearful - feeling fear or anxiety; frightened
  • Degree Higher: overawed - extremely impressed or intimidated by awe

Etymology

Past participle of "awe," ultimately from Old Norse "agi" (fright, terror).

2. ADJ

/ ɑd / (AmE), / ɔːd / (BrE)
filled with a feeling of reverence, respect, mixed with wonder or in some cases, fear.
filled with reverence and wonder

Examples

The children gazed in awed silence at the stunning fireworks display.
She listened to the scientist's explanation with an awed expression on her face.
His awed tone made it clear that he deeply respected her achievements.

Synset Examples

stood in awed silence before the shrine
in grim despair and awestruck wonder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awestruck - filled with awe; amazed by something remarkable or powerful
  • Antonym: unimpressed - not affected by awe; lacking admiration or respect

Etymology

From the verb 'awe', ultimately from Old Norse 'agi' (fear, dread).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02021929-a
Synset POS: a
