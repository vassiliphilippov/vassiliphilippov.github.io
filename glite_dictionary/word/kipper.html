kipper

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪpɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɪpə / (BrE)
a herring that has been split open, salted, and smoked, typically eaten for breakfast
salted and smoked herring

Examples

For breakfast, he enjoyed a freshly grilled kipper with scrambled eggs.
In the coastal village, kipper is a traditional dish often served to tourists.
The smoky aroma of kipper filled the kitchen as it cooked over the stove.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herring - a small, silvery fish, often used for food

Etymology

From Middle English *kipper*, of uncertain origin, perhaps from Old English *cypera* or Old Norse *kipr* (male salmon during spawning).
