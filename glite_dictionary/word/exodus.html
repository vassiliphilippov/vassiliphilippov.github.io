Exodus

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛɡ.zə.dəs / (AmE), / ˈɛk.sə.dəs / (BrE)
the second book of the Bible, describing the departure of the Israelites from slavery in Egypt under Moses and the giving of the Ten Commandments
second book of the Bible

Examples

Many stories from the Exodus have been retold in literature and film.
The laws in the Exodus are central to Judeo-Christian tradition.
She quoted a passage from Exodus during the discussion.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Genesis - the first book of the Bible
  • Degree Higher: Leviticus - the third book of the Bible

Etymology

From Greek 'Exodos' meaning 'a going out, departure', from 'ex-' (out) + 'hodos' (way, road).
