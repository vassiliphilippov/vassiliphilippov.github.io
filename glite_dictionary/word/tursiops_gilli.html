Tursiops gilli

1. NOUN

/ tɜːrˈsaɪɑps ˈɡɪli / (AmE), / tɜːˈsaɪɒps ˈɡɪli / (BrE)
A species of bottlenose dolphin found in the Pacific Ocean.
Pacific bottlenose dolphin species

Examples

Researchers observed a pod of Tursiops gilli off the coast of California.
Marine biologists are studying the migration patterns of Tursiops gilli.
The Tursiops gilli is distinguished by its robust body and curved dorsal fin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bottlenose dolphin - Common name for dolphins of the genus Tursiops

Etymology

Genus name from Latinized Greek; species named after American zoologist Theodore Nicholas Gill.
