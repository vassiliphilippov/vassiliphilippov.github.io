shaving

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of removing hair from the body, especially the face, by using a razor
removing hair with a razor

Examples

He started his morning with a shaving routine to look fresh for work.
Electric razors have made shaving much faster and easier for many people.
Many men dislike shaving every day because it can irritate their skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depilation - the removal of hair, especially from the body
  • Degree Lower: beard - hair growing on the lower part of a man's face
  • Synonym: hair removal - the general process of eliminating hair from the body

Etymology

From the verb 'shave' + -ing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
A thin slice or small fragment cut or shaved from a surface, especially of wood or metal.
thin fragment or slice (esp. wood, metal)

Examples

The carpenter swept up the shavings that littered the workshop floor.
Delicate chocolate shavings were used to garnish the dessert.
Metal shavings accumulated around the base of the machine after hours of drilling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chip - a small piece broken off from something, especially wood
  • Synonym: splinter - a small, thin, sharp piece of material broken off from a larger piece

Etymology

From shave + -ing, indicating the result of shaving.

WordNet ID: oewn-09408030-n
Synset POS: n
