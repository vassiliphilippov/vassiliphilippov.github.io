stagecraft

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪdʒˌkræft / (AmE), / ˈsteɪdʒˌkrɑːft / (BrE)
the art or skill of writing, designing, or producing plays or theatrical performances
skill in producing or presenting plays

Examples

Her mastery of stagecraft was evident in the way she directed the play.
Learning stagecraft helped the young actor understand the importance of lighting and set design.
He was praised for his innovative stagecraft that brought the story to life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theater - a building or outdoor area for plays or other performances
  • Synonym: dramaturgy - the theory and practice of dramatic composition

Etymology

Formed from 'stage' (platform for performances) + 'craft' (skill or art), first used in the early 20th century.
