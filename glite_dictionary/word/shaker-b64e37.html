shaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪkə / (BrE)
a person who exerts significant power and influence, often bringing about change
influential or powerful person

Examples

Patricia was a true shaker in the local art community, organizing major exhibitions and events.
The technology sector attracts many young shakers eager to revolutionize the industry.
He quickly became known as a shaker for his bold business decisions.

Synset Examples

a shaker of traditional beliefs
movers and shakers in the business world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mover - a person who is active or influential, especially in bringing about changes

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪkə / (BrE)
A container with a perforated top or a tight-fitting lid, used for shaking and dispensing substances such as salt, pepper, or cocktails.
container for shaking and dispensing

Examples

She added a pinch of salt from the shaker to her soup.
He gave the cocktail shaker a good shake before pouring the drinks.
The restaurant keeps a shaker of parmesan cheese on every table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salt shaker - a small container with a perforated top for sprinkling salt
  • Synonym: pepper shaker - a small container with a perforated top for sprinkling pepper
  • Synonym: mixer - a device or container for mixing drinks

Etymology

From shake + -er, meaning one that shakes; first recorded in the early 17th century.
