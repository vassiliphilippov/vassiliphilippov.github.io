biohazard

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪ.oʊˌhæz.ərd / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.əʊˌhæz.əd / (BrE)
A risk to human health or the environment arising from biological agents, such as microorganisms, viruses, or toxins.
danger from biological agents

Examples

The laboratory is equipped to handle any biohazard safely and securely.
Exposure to a biohazard can lead to serious illness if proper precautions are not taken.
They posted warning signs to indicate the presence of a potential biohazard in the area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hazard - something that is a source of danger

Etymology

Formed from 'biological' and 'hazard'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪ.oʊˌhæz.ɚd / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.əʊˌhæz.əd / (BrE)
a biological substance, such as a bacterium, virus, or toxin, that poses a threat to human health or the environment, especially through potential use in warfare or accidents
substance posing biological danger

Examples

The lab implemented strict protocols to prevent any biohazard from escaping.
After the accident, the area was marked as a biohazard zone and evacuated.
Proper disposal of medical waste is crucial to avoid creating a biohazard.

Etymology

Formed from 'biological' and 'hazard', first used in the mid-20th century.
