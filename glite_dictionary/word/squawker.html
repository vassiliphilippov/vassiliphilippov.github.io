squawker

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɑːkər / (AmE), / ˈskwɔːkə / (BrE)
a person who complains or whines excessively
person who complains a lot

Examples

No one wanted to ride with the squawker because he complained about everything.
She became known as the office squawker, always grumbling about assignments.
If you ask me, being a squawker rarely solves any problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complainer - a person who complains
  • Synonym: whiner - a person who complains in an annoying way
  • Antonym: stoic - a person who can endure pain or hardship without complaining

Etymology

From 'squawk' (to make a loud, harsh noise) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskwɑkɚ / (AmE), / ˈskwɔːkə / (BrE)
a loudspeaker specifically designed to reproduce middle-frequency sounds, such as those in an intercom or public address system
speaker for midrange sounds (audio)

Examples

The school principal’s voice crackled over the squawker in every classroom.
Please adjust the squawker so the announcements are clearer.
The old intercom squawker needs to be replaced; it buzzes constantly.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tweeter - a loudspeaker designed to reproduce high-frequency sounds
  • Degree Lower: woofer - a loudspeaker designed to reproduce low-frequency sounds

Etymology

From 'squawk' (imitative of raucous sound) + '-er' (one that does); originally referred to birdcalls, later audio equipment.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04299710-n
Synset POS: n
