siriasis

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈraɪəsɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈraɪəsɪs / (BrE)
A sudden state of collapse or prostration caused by exposure to the sun or extreme heat; sunstroke.
collapse from sun or heat
medical

Examples

The patient was treated for siriasis after collapsing during the heatwave.
In the 19th century, siriasis was frequently reported among soldiers stationed in tropical regions.
Her symptoms of siriasis included sudden faintness and confusion after hours in the sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heatstroke - a condition caused by the body overheating, usually due to prolonged exposure to or physical exertion in high temperatures

Etymology

From Greek *seiriasis* (sunstroke), from *Seirios* (Sirius, the Dog Star), thought to cause extreme heat.
