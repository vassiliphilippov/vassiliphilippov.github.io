multiform

1. ADJ

/ ˈmʌl.tɪ.fɔrm / (AmE), / ˈmʌl.tɪ.fɔːm / (BrE)
having many forms, shapes, or appearances; occurring in a variety of forms.
having many forms or shapes

Examples

The artist's new collection was praised for its multiform expressions and inventive designs.
In literature, symbolism often takes multiform representations, making interpretation complex.
The ancient city was a multiform blend of cultural influences and architectural styles.

Synset Examples

the multiform universe of nature and man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: varied - differing in type or amount, diverse
  • Antonym: uniform - not changing in form or character, remaining the same

Etymology

From Latin multiformis, from multi- (many) + forma (form).
