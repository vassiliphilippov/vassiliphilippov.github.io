botchy

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɑːtʃi / (AmE), / ˈbɒtʃi / (BrE)
Done in a clumsy or unskillful way; of poor quality or workmanship.
of poor quality; clumsily done

Examples

The repairs to the car were botchy and only made things worse.
We had to redo the painting because the first job was far too botchy.
Her botchy attempt at baking the cake left it burnt and uneven.

Synset Examples

a botchy piece of work
it was an unskillful attempt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botched - done badly or carelessly
  • Synonym: shoddy - of poor quality or standards
  • Antonym: skillful - done with skill and competence

Etymology

Formed from 'botch' (to carry out a task badly) + -y.
