woodcutter

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdˌkʌtər / (AmE), / ˈwʊdˌkʌtə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to cut down trees and chop wood
person who cuts down trees

Examples

The woodcutter spent all morning chopping logs for the winter.
In the old story, the woodcutter finds a talking tree in the forest.
A skilled woodcutter can fell even the tallest trees safely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lumberjack - a person whose job is to cut down trees, especially in North America

Etymology

From Old English 'wudu' (wood) + 'cutter', meaning one who cuts.
