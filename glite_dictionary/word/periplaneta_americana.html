Periplaneta americana

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrɪpləˈniːtə əˌmɛrɪˈkænə / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪpləˈniːtə əˌmɛrɪˈkɑːnɪə / (BrE)
A large, reddish-brown species of cockroach native to the southern United States but now found worldwide.
species of large cockroach

Examples

The Periplaneta americana is commonly known as the American cockroach and can be found in damp environments.
Scientists often study Periplaneta americana when researching pest control methods.
Despite its name, Periplaneta americana is now found in many parts of the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American cockroach - common name for Periplaneta americana

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus Periplaneta + specific epithet americana, meaning 'American'.
