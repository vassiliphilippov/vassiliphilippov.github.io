Dacelo gigas

1. NOUN

/ dəˈsiːloʊ ˈɡaɪɡəs / (AmE), / dəˈsiːləʊ ˈɡiːɡəs / (BrE)
a species of Australian kingfisher known for its loud, cackling cry, commonly called the laughing kookaburra
species of Australian kingfisher (laughing kookaburra)

Examples

The distinctive call of the Dacelo gigas can be heard throughout the Australian bush at dawn and dusk.
Many tourists hope to catch a glimpse of the Dacelo gigas during their visit to Australia.
Researchers studied the nesting habits of Dacelo gigas in eucalyptus forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laughing kookaburra - bird species, common name for Dacelo gigas

Etymology

From New Latin, Dacelo (genus name) + gigas (Latin for 'giant').
