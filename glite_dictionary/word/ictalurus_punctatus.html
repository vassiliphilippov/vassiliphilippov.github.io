Ictalurus punctatus

1. NOUN

/ aɪkˈtæljərəs pʌŋkˈteɪtəs / (AmE), / ɪkˈtæljʊrəs pʌŋkˈteɪtəs / (BrE)
a species of freshwater catfish, commonly known as the channel catfish, widely found in rivers and lakes of central North America and valued as a food fish
a North American freshwater catfish species

Examples

The Ictalurus punctatus is a popular choice among anglers in the Mississippi River.
Farm-raised Ictalurus punctatus are a key product in the American aquaculture industry.
You can often find Ictalurus punctatus on the menu in southern American restaurants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: channel catfish - common name for Ictalurus punctatus, a freshwater fish

Etymology

From scientific Latin: genus name Ictalurus (from Greek ichthys ‘fish’ + ailouros ‘cat’) and species epithet punctatus (Latin for ‘spotted’).
