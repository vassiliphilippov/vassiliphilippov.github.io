cosy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊzi / (AmE), / ˈkəʊzi / (BrE)
A padded covering placed over a teapot to keep the tea warm.
teapot cover for keeping tea warm

Examples

She placed a floral cosy over the teapot to keep the tea hot during breakfast.
The knitted cosy added a touch of charm to their afternoon tea service.
Grandma gifted me a hand-stitched cosy for my teapot last Christmas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tea cosy - covering for a teapot to keep tea warm

2. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊzi / (AmE), / ˈkəʊzi / (BrE)
Giving a feeling of comfort, warmth, and friendliness; creating or encouraging an informal and pleasant atmosphere.
warm and friendly atmosphere

Examples

We spent the afternoon in a cosy café, enjoying coffee and conversation.
Her apartment has a cosy atmosphere with soft lighting and warm colors.
They had a cosy family gathering by the fire on a cold evening.

Synset Examples

had a cozy chat
a relaxed informal manner
an intimate cocktail lounge
the small room was cozy and intimate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comfortable - providing physical ease or relaxation
  • Synonym: snug - warm, comfortable, and protected, often in a small space
  • Antonym: cold - lacking warmth or friendliness

Etymology

From English dialectal (Scotland, northern England) 'coose' (snug), origin uncertain.

/ ˈkoʊzi / (AmE), / ˈkəʊzi / (BrE)
(of a place or situation) giving a feeling of comfort, warmth, and shelter, especially in a small space.
giving comfort and warmth, esp. in a small space

Examples

The little café on the corner felt cosy and inviting during the winter storm.
She wrapped a blanket around herself and settled into a cosy armchair.

Synset Examples

snug in bed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snug - giving a feeling of comfort, warmth, and security

From Scottish and northern English 'coose', possibly from Old Norse 'kosa' (to choose, enjoy).

4. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊzi / (AmE), / ˈkəʊzi / (BrE)
(of a relationship or situation) marked by an unusually close and comfortable association that may suggest secrecy or collusion, especially for improper or dishonest reasons.
suggesting close and possibly secret collaboration

Examples

Some journalists accused the mayor of being too cosy with business leaders.
The deal had a cosy feel, raising suspicions of under-the-table arrangements.

Related Terms
