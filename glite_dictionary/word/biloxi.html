Biloxi

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈlɑːksi / (AmE), / bɪˈlɒksi / (BrE)
the Siouan language formerly spoken by the Biloxi people of the United States, now considered extinct.
a Siouan language of the Biloxi people

Examples

Linguists have studied the structure of Biloxi to better understand the Siouan language family.
Few written records remain of Biloxi, making it difficult to reconstruct fully.
The last native speaker of Biloxi passed away in the early twentieth century.

Etymology

From the name of the Biloxi people, of Siouan origin.

3. NOUN

/ bɪˈlʌksi / (AmE), / bɪˈlɒksi / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people originally living in southeastern Mississippi, belonging to the Siouan language family
member of a southeastern Mississippi Native American people

Examples

A group of Biloxi settled along the Mississippi River in the eighteenth century.
He traces his ancestry back to the Biloxi people.
The Biloxi were known for their distinctive language and culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Siouan - a family of North American Indigenous languages

Etymology

From the name of the Biloxi people, of Siouan origin.
