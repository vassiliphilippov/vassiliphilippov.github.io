Aquilegia canadensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌækwəˈliːdʒiə kəˈnædənˌsɪs / (AmE), / ˌakwɪˈliːdʒɪə kəˈnæd(ə)nsɪs / (BrE)
A species of columbine native to eastern North America, with long-spurred red and yellow flowers.
North American columbine species

Examples

The Aquilegia canadensis blooms brightly in woodland gardens each spring.
Many gardeners plant Aquilegia canadensis for its showy red flowers that attract hummingbirds.
Because of its unique floral shape, Aquilegia canadensis is popular in native plant landscaping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: columbine - a plant of the genus Aquilegia, known for its distinctive flowers

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Aquilegia' and species 'canadensis', meaning 'of Canada' or 'from Canada'.
