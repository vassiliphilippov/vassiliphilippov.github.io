frigorific

1. ADJ

/ ˌfrɪɡəˈrɪfɪk / (AmE), / ˌfrɪɡəˈrɪfɪk / (BrE)
Causing cold; producing or promoting a cooling or chilling effect.
causing cold; chilling

Examples

The laboratory used a frigorific mixture to maintain low temperatures during the experiment.
Liquid nitrogen is highly frigorific, causing rapid freezing of most materials it contacts.
The doctor applied a frigorific compress to reduce the swelling.

Etymology

From Latin frigorificus, from frigus (cold) + facere (to make).
