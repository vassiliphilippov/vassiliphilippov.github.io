drinking

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of consuming alcoholic beverages, especially to excess.
excessive consumption of alcohol

Examples

Her father's drinking became a serious problem for the family.
Many health issues can arise from prolonged drinking.
He finally sought help to overcome his drinking habit.

Synset Examples

drink was his downfall

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sobriety - the state of not being under the influence of alcohol

2. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of consuming liquids; the action or process of drinking
the act of consuming liquids

Examples

Regular drinking of water is essential for good health.
He was fined for drinking in public parks.
After a long run, she sat down and began drinking from her water bottle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imbibing - the act of drinking, especially alcohol
  • Antonym: eating - the act of consuming food

Etymology

From Middle English 'drinken', from Old English 'drincan' (to drink), with participial suffix '-ing' forming a noun.
