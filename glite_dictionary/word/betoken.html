to betoken

1. VERB

/ bɪˈtoʊkən / (AmE), / bɪˈtəʊkən / (BrE)
to be a sign or indication of something; to signify or be evidence of
be a sign or indication of

Examples

Dark clouds betoken the approach of a storm.
His worried expression betokened his anxiety about the meeting.
These small gestures often betoken a deep affection.

Synset Examples

These symptoms indicate a serious illness
Her behavior points to a severe neurosis
The economic indicators signal that the euro is undervalued

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to indicate - to show or point out; to be a sign of something
  • Synonym: to signify - to be a sign or symbol of; to mean something

Etymology

From Old English 'betācnian', from 'be-' (about) + 'tācnian' (to show, signify).

2. VERB

/ bɪˈtoʊkən / (AmE), / bɪˈtəʊkən / (BrE)
to be a sign of; to indicate or give evidence of something, especially as an omen
to be a sign of; indicate

Examples

A red sky at night may betoken good weather for sailors.
His sudden smile betokened a change in mood.
The dark clouds betokened an impending storm.

Synset Examples

These signs bode bad news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to indicate - to point out or show; to be a sign of
  • Synonym: augur - to be a sign or omen of something, especially as a prediction

Etymology

From Middle English 'bitokenen', from Old English 'betācnian' (to signify, represent).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00873606-v
Synset POS: v
