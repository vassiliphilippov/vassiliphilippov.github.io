elevator

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛl.əˌveɪ.tər / (AmE), / ˈɛl.ɪ.veɪ.tə / (BrE)
a device consisting of a platform or compartment housed in a shaft for raising and lowering people or goods to different floors in a building
device for moving people or goods between floors
american

Examples

We took the elevator to the tenth floor because the stairs were too tiring.
The elevator was out of order, so everyone had to use the stairs.
Press the button and wait for the elevator to arrive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lift - a device for raising and lowering people or goods between floors (British English)

Etymology

From Latin 'elevare' (to raise), via French and late 19th-century American English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛləˌveɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪˌveɪtə / (BrE)
a movable horizontal airfoil attached to the tailplane of an aircraft, used to control the aircraft's up and down movement (pitch).
aircraft tail control surface

Examples

The pilot adjusted the elevator to make the nose of the plane rise.
A malfunction in the elevator can cause serious control issues during flight.
During the pre-flight check, the crew inspected the elevator for any signs of damage.
