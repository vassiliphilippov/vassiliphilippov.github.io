benefice

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛn.ɪ.fɪs / (AmE), / ˈbɛn.ɪ.fɪs / (BrE)
a permanent church appointment for which property and income are provided to its holder
endowed church office

Examples

The parish priest received a small benefice that helped support his ministry.
Holding a benefice was once a common way for clergymen to earn a living.
Many historical disputes arose over the right to appoint someone to a vacant benefice.

Etymology

From Latin 'beneficium' (generosity, favor, privilege).

WordNet ID: oewn-13268986-n

2. VERB

/ ˈbɛnɪfɪs / (AmE), / ˈbɛnɪfɪs / (BrE)
to endow (a person, especially a member of the clergy) with a benefice
to grant a benefice (to someone)

Examples

The bishop decided to benefice the young priest for his dedicated service.
Many churches historically beneficed their clergy to ensure their financial security.
It was once common practice to benefice scholars who contributed significantly to the church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to appoint - to assign a role or position officially

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *beneficiare*, from *beneficium* (benefit, favor, privilege).
