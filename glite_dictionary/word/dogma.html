dogma

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔɡmə / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡmə / (BrE)
A principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true, especially in religion or ideology.
authoritative principle or doctrine

Examples

The scientist challenged the established dogma that had gone unquestioned for decades.
Some people reject religious dogma in favor of independent thought.
Political parties often create their own dogma that members are expected to follow.

Synset Examples

he believed all the Marxist dogma

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doctrine - a belief or set of beliefs held and taught by a church or other group
  • Antonym: heresy - belief or opinion contrary to orthodox doctrine

Etymology

From Greek *dogma* (opinion, belief), from *dokein* (to seem, think).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɔɡmə / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡmə / (BrE)
An official system of principles or beliefs, especially those laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true, often without evidence.
an authoritative principle or doctrine

Examples

The priest emphasized that the church's dogma must be accepted without question.
Scientific progress often requires challenging established dogma.
He was criticized for adhering to political dogma instead of thinking independently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doctrine - a belief or set of beliefs taught by a church or other group
  • Antonym: heresy - a belief that rejects the official doctrines of a religion or church

Etymology

From Greek dogma (that which seems good, opinion), from dokein (to seem).
