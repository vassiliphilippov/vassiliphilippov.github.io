invite

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvaɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvaɪt / (BrE)
an informal invitation to an event or gathering
informal invitation
informal

Examples

She was thrilled to get an invite to the exclusive launch party.
I never received my invite, so I missed the celebration.
Did you send out the invites for your wedding yet?

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invitation - a written or spoken request to participate or be present

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈvaɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvaɪt / (BrE)
to ask someone in a friendly or polite way to do something, especially to come somewhere or take part in an activity
ask someone to do something or attend

Examples

We would like to invite you to our wedding next month.
She invited her colleagues to join her for lunch after the meeting.
They invited questions from the audience at the end of the presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ask - to request someone to do something

Etymology

From Latin invitare (to invite, to summon).
