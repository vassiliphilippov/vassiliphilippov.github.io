Raphael

1. PROPN

/ ˈræfiˌɛl, ˌræfiˈɛl, rəˈfeɪəl / (AmE), / ˈræfeɪəl, ˈræfiˌel, ˌræfiˈel / (BrE)
In Hebrew tradition, an archangel regarded as one of the chief angels, often associated with healing and guidance.
archangel in Hebrew tradition

Examples

Many artists have depicted Raphael in paintings as a graceful, healing figure.
Jewish tradition often names Raphael as one of the archangels alongside Michael and Gabriel.
Some people pray to Raphael for protection and guidance during times of illness.

Etymology

From Hebrew רְפָאֵל (Rəfāʾēl) meaning 'God has healed'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈræfeɪəl, ˈræfiəl / (AmE), / ˈræfeɪəl, ˈræfiəl / (BrE)
(1483–1520) An Italian painter and architect of the High Renaissance, renowned for his clarity of form and composition in paintings such as 'The School of Athens'.
Italian High Renaissance painter

Examples

The Vatican commissioned Raphael to decorate several rooms with his masterpieces.
Art historians regard Raphael as one of the trinity of great masters of the High Renaissance.
Many admirers traveled to Florence to study the techniques of Raphael firsthand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Michelangelo - Italian High Renaissance sculptor, painter, architect, and poet (1475–1564)
  • Synonym: Leonardo da Vinci - Italian polymath of the High Renaissance (1452–1519)

Etymology

From Italian Raffaello, from Latin Raphael, from Hebrew Rafa'el meaning 'God has healed'.
