ozena

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈziːnə / (AmE), / əʊˈziːnə / (BrE)
A chronic disease of the nose characterized by a foul-smelling nasal discharge and wasting of the nasal tissues.
chronic nasal disease with foul discharge
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with ozena after presenting chronic nasal discharge and tissue loss.
Treatment for ozena often focuses on managing symptoms and improving quality of life.
Historically, ozena was more common before the advent of modern antibiotics.

Etymology

From Greek 'ozaina', from 'ozein' (to smell).
