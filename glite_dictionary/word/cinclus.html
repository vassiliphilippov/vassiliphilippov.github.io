Cinclus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋ.kləs / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋ.kləs / (BrE)
a genus of small aquatic songbirds, known as dippers, found near mountain streams in Europe, Asia, and the Americas
genus of aquatic songbirds (dippers)

Examples

The genus Cinclus includes birds that are uniquely adapted to life in fast-flowing streams.
Ornithologists study Cinclus to understand how these birds thrive in cold, aquatic environments.
Members of Cinclus are notable for their ability to dive and walk underwater.

Etymology

From Latin *cinclus*, from Ancient Greek *kinklos* (a kind of small bird that lived near water).
