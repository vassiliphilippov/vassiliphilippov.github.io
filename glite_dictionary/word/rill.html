rill

1. NOUN

/ rɪl / (AmE), / rɪl / (BrE)
a small stream or channel, especially one formed by the erosion of soil by running water
small stream or channel

Examples

A sudden rainstorm caused a narrow rill to form along the side of the hill.
Tiny fish darted in and out of the rill that ran through the meadow.
The rill wound its way between rocks, barely wider than a finger in some places.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brook - a small stream
  • Degree Higher: gully - a larger channel cut by running water, especially after heavy rain

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *rele* or from German *Rille* (groove).

2. NOUN

/ rɪl / (AmE), / rɪl / (BrE)
A very small stream of water, often naturally forming a shallow channel.
very small stream

Examples

A narrow rill meandered through the meadow, glistening in the morning sun.
After heavy rain, a tiny rill formed along the edge of the hiking path.
The children followed the rill downstream, watching small fish dart beneath the surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brook - a small stream
  • Degree Higher: stream - a small narrow river

Etymology

From German *Rille* (groove), related to Middle Low German *rille* (channel).
