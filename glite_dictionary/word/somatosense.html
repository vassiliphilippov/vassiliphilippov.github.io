somatosense

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈmæt̬.oʊˌsɛns / (AmE), / səˈmat.əʊ.sɛns / (BrE)
any of the bodily senses that mediate touch, pressure, temperature, pain, and body position or movement.
the body's sensory systems for touch, pain, and position
medical

Examples

Damage to certain brain regions can impair a person's somatosense of temperature and pain.
The somatosense allows us to feel pressure, vibration, and the position of our limbs.
New research explores how the brain integrates information from the somatosense and other sensory systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: somatosensory - relating to the sensation of the body or its parts

Etymology

From Greek 'sōma' (body) and 'sense'.
