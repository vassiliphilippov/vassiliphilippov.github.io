stabilitron

1. NOUN

/ stəˈbɪlɪˌtrɑn / (AmE), / stəˈbɪlɪˌtrɒn / (BrE)
a semiconductor diode that shows a sudden increase in reverse current at a specific reverse voltage, used for voltage regulation
type of voltage-regulating diode
medical

Examples

The stabilitron is designed to maintain a constant voltage across the load.
In older electronic circuits, a stabilitron was often used to regulate power supplies.
When the voltage exceeds a certain point, the stabilitron conducts in reverse, protecting the circuit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zener diode - a semiconductor diode used for voltage regulation via breakdown voltage

Etymology

From Russian стабилитрон (stabilitron), from 'stabilize' + '-tron' (device).
