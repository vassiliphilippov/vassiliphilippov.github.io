cacophonic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkækəˈfɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌkakəˈfɒnɪk / (BrE)
Having a harsh, unpleasant, or discordant sound.
having an unpleasant, discordant sound

Examples

The factory produced a cacophonic racket that echoed through the neighborhood.
Her brother's band created a cacophonic blend of drums and electric guitars late into the night.
The cacophonic sirens from the street were impossible to ignore.

Synset Examples

as cacophonous as a henyard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cacophonous - having a harsh, discordant sound

Etymology

From Greek 'kakophōnos' (ill-sounding), from 'kakos' (bad) + 'phōnē' (voice, sound).
