jasper

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæspər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɑːspə / (BrE)
An opaque variety of quartz that comes in red, yellow, brown, or dark green colors and is used as a decorative stone or gemstone.
opaque, colored variety of quartz used as gemstone

Examples

She wore a necklace with a jasper pendant carved into the shape of a leaf.
Ancient artisans often used jasper to create seals and other ornamental objects.
The bracelet was inlaid with stones of jasper and turquoise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quartz - a hard mineral consisting of silica, found widely in the earth's crust

Etymology

From Old French jaspre, from Latin iaspis, from Greek iaspis, ultimately from Semitic languages.
