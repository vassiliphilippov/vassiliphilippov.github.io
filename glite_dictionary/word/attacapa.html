Attacapa

1. NOUN

/ ˌætəˈkæpə / (AmE), / ˌætəˈkæpə / (BrE)
a language once spoken by the Atakapa people of the Gulf Coast region of Louisiana and Texas
language of the Atakapa people

Examples

The Attacapa language is now considered extinct, with no native speakers remaining.
Linguists have documented some vocabulary and grammar from the Attacapa language for research purposes.
Many words in regional place names have their origins in Attacapa.

Etymology

From the name of the Atakapa indigenous people of the Gulf Coast (variant spellings: Attacapa, Atakapa).
