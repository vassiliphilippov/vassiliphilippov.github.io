roly-poly

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊli ˌpoʊli / (AmE), / ˌrəʊli ˈpəʊli / (BrE)
A British pudding made of suet pastry spread with jam or fruit, rolled up and baked or steamed.
British pudding of rolled suet pastry with jam or fruit
british

Examples

My grandmother often makes roly-poly for Sunday dessert, filled with homemade raspberry jam.
The classic roly-poly is served warm with a generous helping of custard.
He remembered the taste of roly-poly from his childhood in England.

Etymology

From 'roly-poly', describing something round and rolling in shape; used for the rolled-up form of the pudding.

2. ADJ

/ ˌroʊli ˈpoʊli / (AmE), / ˌrəʊli ˈpəʊli / (BrE)
(especially of a person) short and plump or round in shape.
short and plump; round in shape

Examples

The toddler looked especially roly-poly in his thick winter coat.
She gave her roly-poly cat an affectionate squeeze before feeding it dinner.
He was a cheerful, roly-poly man with a booming laugh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chubby - having a plump and rounded shape
  • Antonym: thin - having little fat on the body

Etymology

Reduplication of 'rolly', from 'roll' + 'poly', imitating roundness.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˌroʊli ˈpoʊli / (AmE), / ˌrəʊli ˈpəʊli / (BrE)
a person who is round and plump in appearance or build; a rotund individual
a plump or rotund person
informal

Examples

The toddlers in the park were a group of roly-poly little ones running after bubbles.
Everyone loved Uncle Benny, a cheerful roly-poly with a booming laugh.
As a child, I was a roly-poly who adored sweets and pastries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chubby - slightly fat in a way that is endearing
  • Antonym: skinny - very thin, especially in an unattractive or unhealthy way

Etymology

From the reduplication of 'roly' (from 'roll'), imitating roundness and rolling movement.

4. NOUN

/ ˈroʊli ˌpoʊli / (AmE), / ˌrəʊli ˈpəʊli / (BrE)
A toy with a rounded base that rolls and rights itself when pushed, often moving in a playful or tumbling manner.
self-righting rolling toy

Examples

The children giggled as the roly-poly spun across the playroom and popped back upright.
My grandmother gave me a colorful roly-poly when I was a toddler.
He watched the roly-poly sway back and forth before settling upright.

Synset Examples

The kids enjoyed the roly-poly toy that tumbled across the floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wobbly man - A similar self-righting toy that wobbles when pushed

Etymology

Imitative of the rolling action; 'roly-poly' originally used as an adjective describing roundness or rolling motion.
