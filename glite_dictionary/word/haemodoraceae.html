Haemodoraceae

1. NOUN

/ hiːməˌdɔːrəˈsiː / (AmE), / hiːməˌdɔːrəˈsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, commonly known as the bloodwort family, comprising mostly herbs found in tropical and subtropical regions
a family of flowering plants (bloodwort family)

Examples

The Haemodoraceae family is noted for its striking red pigments in roots and stems.
Many species in the Haemodoraceae are native to Australia and South Africa.
Botanists debated whether to classify certain genera under Haemodoraceae or Liliaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Liliaceae - a family of monocotyledonous plants called the lily family

Etymology

From New Latin Haemodoraceae, based on the type genus Haemodorum (from Greek 'haima' meaning blood and 'doron' meaning gift), referencing the reddish color in some species.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhiːmədɔːˈreɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌhiːmədəˈreɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants known as the bloodwort family, primarily found in tropical and subtropical regions, with some genera formerly placed in the family Liliaceae.
family of tropical and subtropical flowering plants
medical

Examples

The Haemodoraceae are known for their vividly colored roots and flowers.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Haemodoraceae in northern Australia.
Some taxonomists used to classify these plants under Liliaceae, but now they are in Haemodoraceae.

Etymology

From scientific Latin, based on the type genus Haemodorum, from Greek haima (blood) + doron (gift), referring to the red coloration of some species.
