Asgard

1. PROPN

/ ˈæzˌɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˈæzˌɡɑːd / (BrE)
In Norse mythology, the heavenly realm of the gods (the Aesir) and slain warriors, regarded as the home of the principal deities.
realm of the gods in Norse mythology

Examples

Many legends describe Asgard as being connected to the world of humans by a rainbow bridge.
According to Norse mythology, Odin ruled over Asgard with wisdom and power.
Marvel comics and films have popularized Asgard as the home of Thor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Valhalla - the hall of slain warriors in Norse mythology

Etymology

From Old Norse 'Ásgarðr', meaning ‘enclosure of the Æsir (gods)’.
