to envision

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
To imagine or picture something in one's mind, especially a future possibility or scenario.
imagine or visualize mentally

Examples

It is difficult for me to envision such rapid progress in just a few years.
She envisioned a world where everyone had access to clean water.
We all sat together and envisioned what our new home would look like.

Synset Examples

I cannot envision him as President

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to imagine - form a mental image or concept
  • Synonym: to visualize - form a mental image of

Etymology

From en- (cause to) + vision (sight), modeled after 'envisage'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01639763-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɛnˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
to imagine or picture something in the mind, especially as a future possibility
to imagine mentally, visualize

Examples

It is hard to envision a world without the internet now.
She could envision herself running her own business one day.
We must envision all possible outcomes before making a decision.

Synset Examples

I can't see him on horseback!
I can see what will happen
I can see a risk in this strategy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to imagine - to form a mental image of something not present
  • Synonym: to visualize - to form a clear picture of something in the mind

Etymology

From French 'envisager', from 'en-' (in) + 'visage' (face, appearance).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01638974-v
Synset POS: v
