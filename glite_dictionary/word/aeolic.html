Aeolic

1. NOUN

/ iˈɑːlɪk, eɪˈɑːlɪk / (AmE), / iˈɒlɪk, eɪˈɒlɪk / (BrE)
the dialect of Ancient Greek spoken mainly in Thessaly, Boeotia, and Aeolis.
Ancient Greek dialect of Thessaly, Boeotia, and Aeolis

Examples

The poet Sappho is renowned for composing her works in the Aeolic dialect.
Scholars study inscriptions in Aeolic to understand regional differences in Ancient Greek.
Much of early lyric poetry was originally written in Aeolic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Doric - another major dialect of Ancient Greek
  • Synonym: Ionic - another major dialect of Ancient Greek
  • Synonym: Attic - the dialect of Ancient Greek spoken in Athens

Etymology

From Latin 'Aeolicus', from Greek 'Aiolikos', relating to Aeolis.
