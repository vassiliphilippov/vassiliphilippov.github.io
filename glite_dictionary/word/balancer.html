balancer

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæl.əns.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈbæl.əns.ə / (BrE)
either of the small, modified hind wings of certain insects, particularly flies, that help maintain balance during flight
small hind wing aiding insect flight balance

Examples

Flies rely on their balancers to help them stabilize while flying.
Damage to the balancers can prevent a dipterous insect from flying properly.
Scientists studied how the balancer moves during rapid turns.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæl.əns.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈbæl.əns.ə / (BrE)
an acrobat who performs or maintains difficult balanced positions as part of an act or show
an acrobat who balances in difficult positions

Examples

The circus balancer amazed the audience by holding a handstand on a single finger.
She trained for years to become a skilled balancer capable of complex poses.
Every balancer in the troupe must perfect their sense of control and concentration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acrobat - a performer skilled in gymnastics and feats of balance

Etymology

From balance + -er, indicating a person who balances.
