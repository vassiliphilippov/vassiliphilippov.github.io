snuggle

1. VERB

/ ˈsnʌɡəl / (AmE), / ˈsnʌɡl̩ / (BrE)
to settle or move into a warm, comfortable, and often close position
settle into a comfortable position

Examples

The children snuggled together under the blanket to keep warm.
She likes to snuggle on the couch with a good book on rainy days.
The puppy snuggled into its bed, looking content and sleepy.

Synset Examples

The baby nestled her head in her mother's elbow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuddle - to hold close for warmth or affection
  • Synonym: to nestle - to settle or lie comfortably against or within something

Etymology

Probably frequentative of 'snug'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01427999-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈsnʌɡəl / (AmE), / ˈsnʌɡl / (BrE)
a close and affectionate embrace or cuddle, often warm and prolonged
close, affectionate embrace

Examples

After a long day, all she wanted was a warm snuggle on the sofa.
The puppy gave his owner a loving snuggle before falling asleep.
Nothing beats a snuggle with a loved one on a cold winter night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuddle - to hold close in one's arms as a way of showing love or affection
  • Synonym: hug - to hold someone tightly in one's arms, especially to show affection

Etymology

Probably formed from 'snug' (close-fitting, comfortable) + the frequentative suffix '-le'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00418656-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈsnʌɡəl / (AmE), / ˈsnʌɡl / (BrE)
to settle or move into a warm, comfortable, and cozy position, often close to someone or something
get into a cozy, comfortable position

Examples

On cold winter nights, I love to snuggle under a soft blanket with a good book.
The puppy snuggled up to its mother and fell asleep quickly.
They snuggled together on the sofa while watching the movie.

Synset Examples

We cuddled against each other to keep warm
The children snuggled into their sleeping bags

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuddle - to hold close in one's arms as a way of showing love or affection
  • Synonym: to nestle - to settle or lie comfortably within or against something

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian dialect snugga (to nestle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01427599-v
Synset POS: v
