passkey

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæsˌki / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsˌkiː / (BrE)
A key that opens all locks in a set, especially one that allows access to every room or area in a building.
key opening all locks in a set

Examples

Only the hotel manager carries the passkey that opens every guest room.
When she lost her passkey, the entire security system had to be updated.
The janitor used his passkey to enter the offices after hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: master key - a key designed to open several different locks

Etymology

From 'pass' + 'key', indicating a key that allows passage everywhere.
