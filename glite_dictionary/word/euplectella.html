Euplectella

1. PROPN

/ juːˈplɛktələ / (AmE), / juːˈplɛktɪlə / (BrE)
A genus of marine glass sponges, known for their delicate, latticed silica skeletons.
genus of marine glass sponges

Examples

Deep in the ocean, scientists discovered a new species within the genus Euplectella.
The intricate skeleton of Euplectella has inspired architects and designers alike.
Marine biologists are fascinated by the unique glass structure of Euplectella sponges.

Etymology

From Greek 'eu-' meaning 'well' and 'plektos' meaning 'twisted', referring to the intricate structure.

2. NOUN

/ juːˈplɛktələ / (AmE), / juːˈplɛktələ / (BrE)
A genus of glass sponges in the class Hyalospongiae, known for their delicate, lattice-like silica skeletons.
genus of glass sponges

Examples

The skeleton of Euplectella is often displayed in natural history museums for its intricate structure.
Marine biologists studied the habitat of Euplectella in the deep ocean.
The unique lattice shell of Euplectella provides protection against predators.

Etymology

From Greek eu- (good, well) + plektos (plaited, twisted).
