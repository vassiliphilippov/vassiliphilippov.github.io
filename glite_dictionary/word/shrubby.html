shrubby

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃrʌbi / (AmE), / ˈʃrʌbi / (BrE)
(of a plant or area) consisting of, relating to, or resembling a shrub or shrubs.
resembling or relating to a shrub

Examples

The garden was dominated by shrubby plants that required little maintenance.
A shrubby hillside separates the cottages from the road.
Some roses have a shrubby growth habit and spread widely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bushy - covered with or resembling bushes; bush-like

Etymology

From 'shrub' + '-y', denoting 'having the quality of'.
