atony

1. NOUN

/ ˈætəni / (AmE), / ˈatəni / (BrE)
lack of normal muscular tension or tone in a muscle or group of muscles
lack of muscle tone
medical

Examples

Uterine atony is a major cause of postpartum hemorrhage.
The patient was diagnosed with gastric atony after complaining of chronic bloating and discomfort.
Treatment of atony depends on the underlying cause and the muscle group affected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypotonia - reduced muscle tone
  • Antonym: hypertonia - increased muscle tone

Etymology

From Greek atonía, from a- (without) + tónos (tone).
