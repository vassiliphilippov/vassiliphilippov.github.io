wake

1. NOUN

/ weɪk / (AmE), / weɪk / (BrE)
a vigil held over a dead body before the funeral, especially one attended by family and friends
a vigil held over a corpse before burial

Examples

After his passing, the family held a wake at their home, inviting everyone to share memories.
Many cultures have traditions similar to a wake, where friends gather before the funeral.
The stories told at the wake brought both laughter and tears to those who attended.

Synset Examples

there's no weeping at an Irish wake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: funeral - ceremony held for a dead person before burial or cremation

Etymology

From Old English *wacu* (a watch, vigil), ultimately from Latin *vigilare* (to keep watch).

2. NOUN

/ weɪk / (AmE), / weɪk / (BrE)
a trail of waves left on water by a moving boat or ship
waves left by a boat

Examples

The speedboat's wake rocked the smaller fishing vessel as it passed by.
Swimmers are advised to avoid the wake of large ships for their safety.
Foam formed in the wake of the ferry as it crossed the bay.

Synset Examples

the motorboat's wake capsized the canoe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trail - a mark or a series of signs left by someone or something moving

4. NOUN

/ weɪk / (AmE), / weɪk / (BrE)
the consequences or results that follow an event, especially a significant or catastrophic one
consequences after an event

Examples

In the wake of the scandal, the company implemented stricter policies.
Many changes were made in the wake of the economic crisis.
The community came together to rebuild in the wake of the devastating hurricane.

Synset Examples

in the wake of the accident no one knew how many had been injured

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aftermath - the consequences or aftereffects of a significant event
  • Synonym: outcome - the result or effect of an action or event

Etymology

From Old English 'wacu' (watch, vigil), but this sense comes from the image of the track left by a moving body in water.
