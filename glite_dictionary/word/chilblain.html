chilblain

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪlˌbleɪn / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪlˌbleɪn / (BrE)
A painful, itchy swelling of the skin, typically on the hands or feet, caused by exposure to cold and moisture.
swelling from cold exposure
medical

Examples

After a winter hike in wet boots, she developed painful chilblains on her toes.
Wearing warm gloves in cold weather can help prevent chilblain outbreaks on your fingers.
The doctor diagnosed him with chilblain after he reported swelling and redness on his feet.

Etymology

From 'chill' (cold) + 'blain' (sore, swelling), first recorded in the 16th century.
