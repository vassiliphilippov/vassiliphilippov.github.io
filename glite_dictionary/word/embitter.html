to embitter

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈbɪtər / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɪtə / (BrE)
to make someone feel bitter or resentful
make resentful or bitter

Examples

The unfair criticism only served to embitter him further.
Years of rejection had embittered her perspective on life.
The harsh policies embittered many people in the community.

Synset Examples

These injustices embittered her even more

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to soften - to make or become less hard, harsh, or severe

Etymology

From Old French 'embatre', from Latin 'in-' (in) + 'bitur' (to make bitter).
