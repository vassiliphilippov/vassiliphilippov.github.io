cyclopropane

1. NOUN

/ saɪkləˈproʊpeɪn / (AmE), / saɪkləʊˈprəʊpeɪn / (BrE)
a colorless, flammable gas, C3H6, consisting of a ring of three carbon atoms, sometimes used as an inhalation anesthetic
a colorless, flammable gas (C3H6)
medical

Examples

The anesthesiologist administered cyclopropane before the surgery began.
Because cyclopropane is highly flammable, precautions must be taken during its use.
In the past, cyclopropane was more commonly used as an anesthetic in operating rooms.

Etymology

From 'cyclo-' (ring) and 'propane' (a three-carbon alkane).
