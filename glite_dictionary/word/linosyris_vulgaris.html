Linosyris vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ lɪˌnɒsɪrɪs vʌlˈgɛrɪs / (AmE), / lɪˌnɒsɪˈrɪs vʌlˈgɑːrɪs / (BrE)
An early-flowering perennial plant native to southern and southeastern Europe, with flower heads that resemble those of goldenrod.
a southern European perennial plant resembling goldenrod

Examples

Linosyris vulgaris can be found in open fields throughout southeastern Europe.
The bright yellow flower heads of Linosyris vulgaris make it easy to mistake for goldenrod.
Botanists were pleased to discover a thriving patch of Linosyris vulgaris during their survey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goldenrod - a plant with yellow flower heads that Linosyris vulgaris resembles

Etymology

From Latin; 'Linosyris' is the genus name and 'vulgaris' means 'common' in Latin.
