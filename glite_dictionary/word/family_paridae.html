family Paridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˈpɛrɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæməli ˈparɪdi / (BrE)
A family of small songbirds including titmice and chickadees, found mainly in the Northern Hemisphere.
family of birds: titmice and chickadees

Examples

The family Paridae includes chicks known as titmice and chickadees.
Birdwatchers often spot several members of the family Paridae in northern woodlands.
Research on the family Paridae has contributed to our understanding of avian social behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: titmouse - a small songbird in the family Paridae
  • Synonym: chickadee - a North American songbird in the family Paridae

Etymology

From Modern Latin Paridae, based on genus Parus (the titmouse).
