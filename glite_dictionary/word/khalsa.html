Khalsa

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːlsə / (AmE), / ˈkɑːlsə / (BrE)
The group of baptized Sikhs who are fully initiated and committed to strict observance of Sikh practices, founded by Guru Gobind Singh in 1699.
initiated body of Sikhs

Examples

Every year, thousands of Sikhs join the Khalsa in a ceremonial initiation called Amrit Sanchar.
Members of the Khalsa adhere strictly to the five Ks and uphold Sikh values.
The Khalsa was established to protect the innocent and promote justice.

Etymology

From Punjabi ਖ਼ਾਲਸਾ (khālsā), meaning 'pure' or 'the pure ones'.
