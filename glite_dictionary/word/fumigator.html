fumigator

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːmɪˌɡeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈfjuːmɪˌɡeɪtə / (BrE)
a device that produces a disinfecting or pest-eradicating gas
device for generating disinfecting or pest-killing gas

Examples

The fumigator was placed in the center of the room to ensure even distribution of the gas.
Professionals used a fumigator to eliminate termites from the wooden beams.
After the outbreak, a large fumigator was brought in to sterilize the warehouse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disinfectant - a substance used to destroy harmful microorganisms
  • Synonym: pesticide - a substance used to kill pests

Etymology

From Latin 'fumigare' (to smoke), from 'fumus' (smoke).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːmɪˌɡeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈfjuːmɪˌɡeɪtə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to remove pests or disinfect an area by using chemical fumes
person who uses fumes to remove pests

Examples

The fumigator arrived early to treat the infested warehouse.
Hiring a professional fumigator is advised for severe termite problems.
Every room was sealed while the fumigator carried out his work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exterminator - a person whose job is to destroy unwanted pests

Etymology

From Latin 'fumigare' (to smoke), via 'fumigate' + '-or'.
