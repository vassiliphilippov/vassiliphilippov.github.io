Canis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.nɪs / (AmE), / ˈkaː.nɪs / (BrE)
The genus of mammals in the family Canidae that includes domestic dogs, wolves, foxes, and jackals.
genus of dogs, wolves, and jackals

Examples

The domestic dog belongs to the genus Canis.
Researchers studied the behavioral patterns of several Canis species in the wild.
Wolves, whose scientific name is Canis lupus, are native to many regions of the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canine - relating to or resembling a dog or dogs

Etymology

From Latin 'canis' (dog).
