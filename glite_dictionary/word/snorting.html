snorting

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnɔrtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsnɔːtɪŋ / (BrE)
an act of forcibly exhaling air through the nose, typically with a loud or harsh sound
forcible exhalation through the nose

Examples

His sudden snorting startled everyone in the quiet room.
The snorting of the horse could be heard from across the stable.
She tried to suppress her laughter, but only managed a series of loud snorting sounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snort - to force air noisily through the nose; related verb

Etymology

From the verb 'snort', first recorded in Middle English, probably of imitative origin.
