sphenoid

1. NOUN

/ ˈsfiː.nɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈsfiː.nɔɪd / (BrE)
a butterfly-shaped bone at the base of the skull that forms part of the eye socket and houses the pituitary gland
bone at the base of the skull
medical

Examples

The sphenoid bone plays a crucial role in supporting the base of the cranium.
Injury to the sphenoid can affect the nerves passing through it.
The pituitary gland is housed within the sphenoid in a depression called the sella turcica.

Etymology

From Greek sphēnoeidēs (‘wedgelike’), from sphēn (‘wedge’) + -oid (‘like’).
