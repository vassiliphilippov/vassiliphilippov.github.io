drumstick

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrʌmˌstɪk / (AmE), / ˈdrʌm.stɪk / (BrE)
a stick used for striking a drum to produce sound
stick for playing a drum

Examples

The drummer snapped his drumstick in half during the performance.
She bought a new pair of drumsticks before her first band rehearsal.
A jazz musician often uses lighter drumsticks for a softer sound.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdrʌmˌstɪk / (AmE), / ˈdrʌm.stɪk / (BrE)
the lower part of the leg of a cooked bird such as a chicken or turkey, especially as food
lower leg of a cooked fowl

Examples

She always saves the drumstick for her favorite guest at Thanksgiving dinner.
The chicken drumstick was crispy and perfectly seasoned.
I'd like a drumstick and some mashed potatoes, please.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: thigh - the upper part of the leg of a fowl
  • Degree Lower: wing - the forelimb of a fowl, often eaten as food
