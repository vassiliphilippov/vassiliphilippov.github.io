undervaluation

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌndɚˌvæljuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˌvæljuˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of assigning too low a value or price to something; an assessment below the actual worth
too low a value assigned

Examples

The company's undervaluation on the stock market attracted potential investors.
Many experts warned about the undervaluation of the housing assets in the report.
She claimed that there was a significant undervaluation of her property by the tax assessor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overvaluation - assigning too high a value or price

Etymology

From under- (meaning below) + valuation (from Latin valorem, value).
