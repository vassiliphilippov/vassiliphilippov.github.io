effortless

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛfərtləs / (AmE), / ˈɛfətləs / (BrE)
achieved with little or no apparent effort; done smoothly and easily without visible strain
done with little or no effort

Examples

She completed the routine with effortless grace, impressing the judges.
His effortless charm made everyone feel at ease during the meeting.
The pianist played the complex piece in an effortless manner that captivated the audience.

Synset Examples

a difficult feat performed with casual mastery
casual grace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: easy - not difficult or needing much effort
  • Antonym: laborious - requiring considerable effort and time

Etymology

From 'effort' plus the suffix '-less', meaning 'without effort'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɛfərtləs / (AmE), / ˈɛfətləs / (BrE)
requiring no physical or mental effort; done with ease
done with little or no effort

Examples

She made an effortless transition from student to professional life.
His effortless charm made him popular at every event.
The athlete completed the marathon in an effortless manner, barely breaking a sweat.

Synset Examples

the swallows glided in an effortless way through the busy air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: easy - achieved without great effort; presenting few difficulties
  • Antonym: laborious - requiring considerable effort and time

Etymology

From 'effort' + '-less', meaning 'without effort'.
