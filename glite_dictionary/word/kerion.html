kerion

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪriˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈkɪəriən / (BrE)
A severe, inflamed fungal infection of the hair follicles, especially of the scalp or beard, characterized by swelling, pus-filled lesions, and oozing.
severe fungal scalp infection
medical

Examples

The child developed a painful kerion on her scalp and needed prompt treatment.
A kerion is often mistaken for a bacterial infection due to its swollen, oozing appearance.
Veterinarians sometimes see kerion in dogs, especially after exposure to infected animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ringworm - fungal infection causing ring-shaped rashes

Etymology

From Greek 'kērion' (honeycomb), referring to the honeycomb-like appearance of the lesions.
