sovietism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊ.vi.ɪˌtɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.vi.ɪˌtɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the system, policies, or ideology of Soviet communism, especially as practiced in the former Soviet Union
the ideology of Soviet communism

Examples

Many historians debate the influence of sovietism on Eastern European politics during the Cold War.
He rejected the ideals of sovietism in favor of a more liberal approach to governance.
The collapse of sovietism led to major changes in Russian society and economy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: communism - political and economic system advocating collective ownership

Etymology

From Russian 'Sovet' (council) + '-ism'.
