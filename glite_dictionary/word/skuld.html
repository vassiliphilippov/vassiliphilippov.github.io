Skuld

1. PROPN

/ skʊld / (AmE), / skʊld / (BrE)
In Norse mythology, one of the three Norns; the goddess who personifies the future.
Norse goddess of the future

Examples

In Norse myth, Skuld is one of the three Norns who govern the fate of gods and men.
The goddess Skuld represents the future, while her sisters embody the past and present.
Many Viking legends include references to Skuld and her role in weaving destiny.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Urd - Norse goddess of the past; one of the three Norns
  • Synonym: Verdandi - Norse goddess of the present; one of the three Norns

Etymology

From Old Norse Skuld (meaning 'debt' or 'future'), associated with the Norns in Norse mythology.
