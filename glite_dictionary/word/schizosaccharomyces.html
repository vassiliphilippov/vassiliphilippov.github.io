Schizosaccharomyces

1. NOUN

/ ˌskɪtsoʊˌsækəroʊˈmaɪsiz / (AmE), / ˌskɪtsəʊˌsækə rəˈmaɪsiːz / (BrE)
A genus of fission yeasts in the family Schizosaccharomycetaceae, commonly used as a model organism in cell biology.
genus of fission yeasts

Examples

Schizosaccharomyces pombe is often used in research to study cell division.
Unlike budding yeasts, Schizosaccharomyces reproduces by fission.
Researchers isolated Schizosaccharomyces from a sample of fermenting tea.

Etymology

From Greek schizo- (split) + sakchar (sugar) + mykēs (fungus).

2. NOUN

/ ˌskɪtsoʊˌsækəroʊˈmaɪsiz / (AmE), / ˌskɪtsəʊˌsækəərəˈmaɪsiːz / (BrE)
A genus of fission yeasts in the family Schizosaccharomycetaceae, important in research and fermentation.
a genus of fission yeasts

Examples

Schizosaccharomyces is widely used as a model organism to study cell division in yeast.
Researchers isolated Schizosaccharomyces from samples of traditional fermented beverages.
Unlike budding yeasts, Schizosaccharomyces reproduces by fission rather than by budding.

Etymology

From Greek 'schizo-' (split) + 'saccharo-' (sugar) + '-myces' (fungus, mushroom).
