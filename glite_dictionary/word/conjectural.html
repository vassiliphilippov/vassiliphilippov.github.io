conjectural

1. ADJ

/ kənˈdʒɛktʃɚəl / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛktʃərəl / (BrE)
Based on or involving conjecture; formed without firm evidence or with little information.
based on conjecture or guesswork

Examples

The historian admitted that his account of the event was largely conjectural due to scarce evidence.
Much of the early research on the disease was conjectural and lacked empirical support.
Her explanation for the missing painting seemed highly conjectural to the art experts.

Synset Examples

theories about the extinction of dinosaurs are still highly conjectural
the supposed reason for his absence
suppositious reconstructions of dead languages
hypothetical situation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypothetical - based on a suggested idea or theory, not actual
  • Synonym: speculative - based on speculation or incomplete evidence
  • Antonym: factual - based on or concerned with actual events or facts

Etymology

From Late Latin conjecturalis, from Latin conjectura (inference, guess).
