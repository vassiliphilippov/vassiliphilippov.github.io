Cancridae

1. NOUN

/ kæŋˈkrɪdiː / (AmE), / kaŋˈkrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of crabs including many well-known edible species, found mainly in the Northern Hemisphere.
family of edible crabs

Examples

The family Cancridae includes some of the most commercially important crabs.
Species within Cancridae are often caught for human consumption.
Researchers are studying how Cancridae adapt to changing ocean temperatures.

Etymology

From New Latin Cancridae, from Latin cancer ('crab') + -idae (zoological family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæŋkrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈkaŋkrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of mostly edible crabs, comprising several well-known species, found especially in temperate waters.
family of edible crabs

Examples

Several commercially valuable species belong to the Cancridae family.
Biologists study the Cancridae to learn about the evolution of edible crabs.
The Cancridae include familiar species such as the Dungeness crab.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crab - a crustacean with a broad carapace and pincers

Etymology

From Latin *Cancer* (crab), the type genus of the family, plus taxonomic suffix -idae.
