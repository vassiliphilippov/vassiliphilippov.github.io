to beleaguer

1. VERB

/ bəˈliɡɚ / (AmE), / bɪˈliːɡə / (BrE)
to surround a place with armed forces, typically to force it to surrender; to besiege
surround and besiege

Examples

Enemy troops continued to beleaguer the city through the winter, cutting off all supplies.
The fortress was beleaguered for months before finally surrendering to the attackers.
During the war, the capital became heavily beleaguered and isolated from reinforcements.

Synset Examples

The Turks besieged Vienna

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to besiege - to surround a place with armed forces and try to capture it
  • Synonym: to encircle - to form a circle around; to surround
  • Antonym: to liberate - to set free, especially from occupation or oppression

Etymology

From Dutch 'belegeren', from 'be-' (about) + 'leger' (camp).

2. VERB

/ bəˈliɡər / (AmE), / bɪˈliːɡə / (BrE)
to cause repeated trouble or harassment to someone; to annoy or beset persistently
annoy or beset persistently

Examples

The press continued to beleaguer the politician with difficult questions.
The department was beleaguered by constant complaints from users.
If you beleaguer your friend with too many requests, he might stop answering your calls.

Synset Examples

The children teased the boy because of his stammer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to harass - to subject to aggressive pressure or intimidation; synonyms for persistent annoyance
  • Synonym: bother - to take the trouble to do something or annoy; less formal synonym
  • Antonym: to soothe - to gently calm or relieve; an opposite of annoying persistently

Etymology

From Dutch 'belegeren' (to besiege), from 'be-' (around) + 'leger' (camp).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
