tailband

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪlˌbænd / (AmE), / ˈteɪlˌbænd / (BrE)
a decorative or structural band at the bottom edge of the spine of a book, often used in bookbinding
bottom band on a book's spine

Examples

The bookbinder carefully sewed a blue tailband onto the bottom of the spine.
Collectors often appreciate finely colored tailbands on antique volumes.
When the tailband began to fray, the library sent the book for restoration.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: headband - a band at the top edge of a book's spine

Etymology

From 'tail' (bottom part) + 'band', in reference to its position at the base of a book spine.
