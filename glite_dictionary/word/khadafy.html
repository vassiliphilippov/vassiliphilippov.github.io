Khadafy

1. PROPN

/ kəˈdɑːfi / (AmE), / kæˈdɑːfi / (BrE)
An alternate spelling of Muammar Gaddafi, the Libyan leader who ruled the country from 1969 to 2011 after overthrowing the monarchy.
alternate spelling of Muammar Gaddafi

Examples

Many news articles in the 1980s referred to the Libyan leader as Khadafy.
The spelling Khadafy was commonly used in American newspapers for decades.
Some books discuss the policies of Khadafy and their impact on Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gaddafi - Libyan leader from 1969 to 2011, overthrew the monarchy

Etymology

Variant transliteration of Arabic القذافي (al-Qaddhāfī).
