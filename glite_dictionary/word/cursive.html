cursive

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɝːsɪv / (AmE), / ˈkɜːsɪv / (BrE)
a style of handwriting in which the letters are connected within words and usually written in a flowing, rapid manner
connected style of handwriting

Examples

She wrote her letter in beautiful cursive, which was hard for some to read.
Many schools have stopped teaching cursive to students.
The old diary was filled with neat cursive writing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: print - handwriting in which the letters are not connected; printed script

Etymology

From Medieval Latin cursīva (scriptus), literally 'running (script)', from Latin currere 'to run'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06361681-n

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɝːsɪv / (AmE), / ˈkɜːsɪv / (BrE)
(of handwriting or script) having successive letters joined together in a flowing manner.
writing with joined-up letters

Examples

He prefers to write in cursive because it looks elegant and flows smoothly.
The instructions were written in neat cursive on the page.
Some students find cursive writing difficult to learn compared to print.

Synset Examples

cursive script

Related Terms

  • Antonym: printed - letters not joined, as in print writing

Etymology

From Medieval Latin cursivus (running), from Latin currere (to run).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02292769-s
Synset POS: s
