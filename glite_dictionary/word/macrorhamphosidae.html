Macrorhamphosidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæk.roʊ.ræmˈfɒsɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌmak.rəʊ.ramˈfɒsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small marine fishes, commonly known as bellows fishes, characterized by long tubular snouts and laterally compressed bodies.
family of marine bellows fishes

Examples

Fishes of the family Macrorhamphosidae are easily recognized by their elongated snouts.
Marine biologists discovered several new species within the Macrorhamphosidae family.
The aquarium recently added a Macrorhamphosidae exhibit, showcasing the unique bellows fishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bellows fish - a small marine fish with a long tubular snout

Etymology

From Greek 'makros' (long) + 'rhamphos' (beak), referring to their long snouts.

family of bellows fishes

Researchers discovered a new species within the Macrorhamphosidae family in the deep Pacific Ocean.
Members of the Macrorhamphosidae family are often referred to as bellows fishes due to their body shape.
