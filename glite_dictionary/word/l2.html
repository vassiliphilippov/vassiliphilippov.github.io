L2

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛl tu / (AmE), / ˌɛl tjuː / (BrE)
a second language learned by a person after their native language, typically in adolescence or adulthood
a second language learned after one's native one

Examples

She became fluent in her L2 after moving abroad for graduate school.
Many language teachers develop methods to help students acquire their L2 more effectively.
Some people struggle to maintain their L2 without regular practice.

Synset Examples

Research on adult-learned second language (L2) has provided considerable insight into the neurocognitive mechanisms underlying the learning and processing of L2 grammar
A period of limited exposure generally leads to attrition (loss) of L2 performance or knowledge
Crucially, it is also desirable to retain them, even in the absence of continued practice or exposure to the L2

Related Terms

  • Antonym: first language - the native or primary language learned from birth
  • Antonym: L1 - a person's first or native language
  • Synonym: foreign language - a language not spoken as one's native or community language

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'second language,' where 'L' stands for 'language' and '2' for 'second.'
