margarine

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrdʒərɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɑːdʒəriːn / (BrE)
A soft, yellowish spread made mainly from vegetable oils, used as a substitute for butter in cooking and on bread.
butter substitute made from vegetable oils

Examples

She spread margarine on her toast instead of butter.
Many people use margarine in baking because it is less expensive than butter.
The recipe calls for a cup of melted margarine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butter - a pale yellow edible fatty substance made by churning cream and used as a spread or in cooking

Etymology

From French margarine, from Greek margaron (pearl), due to its pearly appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːrdʒəˌrin / (AmE), / ˈmɑːdʒəriːn / (BrE)
A soft, spreadable food made mainly from vegetable oils, used as a substitute for butter.
spread made from vegetable oils

Examples

She spread margarine on her toast instead of butter.
Many people prefer margarine because it contains less saturated fat than butter.
The recipe calls for a tablespoon of margarine for baking the cookies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: butter - a solid dairy product made by churning cream, often used as a spread or in cooking

Etymology

From French 'margarine', from Greek 'margaron' (pearl).
