Hg

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪtʃ ˈdʒi / (AmE), / ˌeɪtʃ ˈdʒiː / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for mercury, a heavy silvery toxic metallic element that is liquid at room temperature
chemical symbol for mercury (element)

Examples

The periodic table lists Hg as the symbol for mercury.
Old thermometers often contained Hg, which can be hazardous if broken.
Scientists measure the pressure in millimeters of Hg in barometers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mercury - the element mercury, symbol Hg

Etymology

From Latin 'hydrargyrum', meaning 'water-silver', the historic name for mercury.
