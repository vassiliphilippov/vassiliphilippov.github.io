Spirillum minus

1. NOUN

/ spɪˈrɪləm ˈmaɪnəs / (AmE), / spɪˈrɪlɪm ˈmɪnəs / (BrE)
a species of bacterium that causes rat-bite fever in humans
bacterium causing rat-bite fever
medical

Examples

Rat-bite fever can be caused by the bacterium Spirillum minus when transmitted through a rodent bite.
Doctors identified Spirillum minus in the blood sample of the infected patient.
Laboratory tests confirmed the presence of Spirillum minus as the source of the illness.

Etymology

From Latin 'spirillum', diminutive of 'spira' (coil), and Latin 'minus' (lesser); named for its spiral shape and size.
