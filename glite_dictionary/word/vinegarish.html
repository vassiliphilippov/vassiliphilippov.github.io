vinegarish

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɪnəɡərɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈvɪnɪɡərɪʃ / (BrE)
Having a taste or smell like vinegar; sour or acerbic.
tasting or smelling like vinegar

Examples

The salad dressing tasted distinctly vinegarish, overwhelming the other flavors.
His homemade pickles had a vinegarish aroma that filled the kitchen.
She wrinkled her nose at the vinegarish scent coming from the open jar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sour - having an acidic taste like lemon or vinegar

Etymology

Formed from 'vinegar' + suffix '-ish' (having the quality of).

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɪn.ə.ɡər.ɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈvɪn.ɪ.ɡər.ɪʃ / (BrE)
(of a person or their mood) having a sour or ill-tempered disposition.
ill-tempered or sour in mood

Examples

Her vinegarish remarks put everyone on edge at the meeting.
He grew more vinegarish as the day progressed, snapping at anyone who approached him.
Despite her vinegarish demeanor, she was deeply caring once you got to know her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surly - bad-tempered and unfriendly
  • Antonym: amiable - friendly and pleasant

Etymology

From 'vinegar' + '-ish', comparing disposition to the sourness of vinegar.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
