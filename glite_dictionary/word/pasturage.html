pasturage

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæstʃərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːstjʊərɪdʒ / (BrE)
grass or other plants grown for feeding grazing animals such as cattle or horses; land covered with such vegetation.
land or vegetation for animal grazing

Examples

The cattle enjoyed the fresh pasturage provided by the abundant spring rains.
Farmers were concerned when drought affected the quality of the pasturage.
They rotated the sheep to new fields to prevent overgrazing of the pasturage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pasture - land covered with grass for grazing animals

Etymology

From French 'pasturage', from Latin 'pastor' (shepherd).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæstʃərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːstjʊərɪdʒ / (BrE)
Lush grass or other plants suitable for grazing livestock, especially in a pasture.
grazing vegetation in a pasture

Examples

The cattle thrived on the rich pasturage provided by the valley.
Overgrazing can quickly ruin the quality of pasturage in a field.
She pointed to the lush pasturage as ideal for dairy cows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pasture - land covered with grass for grazing animals

Etymology

From Old French 'pasturage', based on Latin 'pastor' (shepherd).
