self-distrust

1. NOUN

/ sɛlf dɪˈstrʌst / (AmE), / sɛlf dɪsˈtrʌst / (BrE)
lack of confidence in one's own abilities or judgment; mistrust of oneself.
lack of confidence in oneself

Examples

His self-distrust prevented him from applying for the promotion, despite his qualifications.
Moments of self-distrust are normal when facing new challenges in life.
Overcoming self-distrust can lead to greater personal growth and achievement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: self-confidence - belief in one's own abilities or judgment

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' (oneself) and 'distrust' (lack of trust).
