U-boat

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuˌboʊt / (AmE), / ˈjuːˌbəʊt / (BrE)
a German military submarine, especially those used during the First and Second World Wars
German military submarine (World Wars)

Examples

The Allies feared the threat posed by the German U-boat fleet in the Atlantic.
Several merchant ships were sunk by a single U-boat during the war.
The crew survived after their U-boat was forced to surface by depth charges.

Etymology

From German 'U-Boot', short for 'Unterseeboot', meaning 'undersea boat'.
