conveyance

1. NOUN

/ kənˈveɪəns / (AmE), / kənˈveɪəns / (BrE)
the act of transporting or moving something from one place to another
the act of transporting something

Examples

The conveyance of goods by truck is essential to the supply chain.
Proper conveyance of hazardous materials requires strict safety protocols.
He arranged for the conveyance of his furniture to the new apartment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transportation - the action of transporting someone or something

Etymology

From Old French 'conveier' (to accompany, escort), from Medieval Latin 'conviare'.

2. NOUN

/ kənˈveɪəns / (AmE), / kənˈveɪəns / (BrE)
a vehicle or other means of transporting people or goods from one place to another
means of transportation

Examples

The horse-drawn carriage was a popular conveyance in the nineteenth century.
They used a bicycle as their primary conveyance around the island.
Public conveyance is often more efficient in large cities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vehicle - a thing used for transporting people or goods
  • Synonym: transport - a system or means of conveying people or goods

Etymology

From Old French 'conveance' and Medieval Latin 'convehere' (to carry together).

3. NOUN

/ kənˈveɪəns / (AmE), / kənˈveɪəns / (BrE)
a legal document by which property, especially real estate, is transferred from one person to another
legal document for property transfer
legal

Examples

The lawyer prepared a conveyance to transfer the house to the new owners.
Without a proper conveyance, the property sale could not be completed.
The conveyance was signed in the presence of two witnesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deed - a legal document that is signed and delivered, especially one regarding ownership of property

Etymology

From Old French 'conveiance', from 'conveier' (to convey), from Latin 'conviare'.

Source: WordNet

/ kənˈveɪəns / (AmE), / kənˈveɪəns / (BrE)
the legal act of transferring the title of property from one person to another
