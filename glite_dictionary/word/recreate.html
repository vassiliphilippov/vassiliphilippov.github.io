to recreate

1. VERB

/ ˌriː.kriˈeɪt / (AmE), / ˌriː.kriˈeɪt / (BrE)
to make something again or produce a close copy of something that existed before
make again or produce a close copy

Examples

The artist tried to recreate the ancient mural as faithfully as possible.
They used modern technology to recreate the sounds of a 1920s jazz band.
The documentary recreates the events using dramatic reenactments.

Synset Examples

Re-create the boom of the West on a small scale
she recreated the feeling of the 1920's with her stage setting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to replicate - to make an exact copy of something
  • Synonym: to imitate - to copy the behavior or appearance of something
  • Antonym: to originate - to begin or create something for the first time

Etymology

From Latin recreare (to restore, refresh).

2. VERB

/ ˌriː.krieɪt / (AmE), / ˌriː.kriˈeɪt / (BrE)
to give new life, energy, or freshness to someone or something; to refresh or revitalize
give new life or energy to

Examples

A vacation by the sea helped to recreate his spirit after months of hard work.
The new therapy aims to recreate patients weakened by illness.
A short walk in the fresh air can often recreate a tired mind.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revive - to bring back to life or consciousness
  • Synonym: to refresh - to restore strength or animation to
  • Synonym: to invigorate - to give strength or energy to

Etymology

From Latin 'recreare', meaning 'to refresh, revive'.

4. VERB

/ ˌriːkriˈeɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːkrɪˈeɪt / (BrE)
to make a copy or close imitation of something, especially so as to replicate its appearance or effect
make a replica or copy

Examples

The artist managed to recreate the lost painting from old photographs.
Scientists are trying to recreate the conditions of early Earth in the laboratory.
He used special effects to recreate the famous battle scene in the movie.

Synset Examples

copy that drawing
re-create a picture by Rembrandt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copy - to make something that is an exact reproduction of another
  • Synonym: to imitate - to copy the way someone or something looks or behaves
  • Antonym: to originate - to create something original rather than a replica

Etymology

From Latin recreare (to create anew, refresh), from re- (again) + creare (to create).
