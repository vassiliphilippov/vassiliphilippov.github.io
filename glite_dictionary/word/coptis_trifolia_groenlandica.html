Coptis trifolia groenlandica

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑptɪs traɪˈfoʊliə ɡroʊˌlændɪˈkɑ / (AmE), / ˈkɒptɪs traɪˈfəʊlɪə ɡruːˌlændɪˈkə / (BrE)
A low-growing perennial plant native to North American woodlands, characterized by trifoliate leaves, a yellow rootstock, and white flowers.
a woodland plant with trifoliate leaves and white flowers

Examples

The researcher collected samples of Coptis trifolia groenlandica during her fieldwork in the northern forests.
Gardeners cultivate Coptis trifolia groenlandica for its attractive white flowers and unique foliage.
The yellow rootstock of Coptis trifolia groenlandica has been used in traditional remedies by indigenous peoples.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining genus name Coptis and species name trifolia ('three-leaved') with subspecies groenlandica referring to Greenland.
