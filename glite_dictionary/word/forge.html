2. NOUN

/ fɔrdʒ / (AmE), / fɔːdʒ / (BrE)
a workplace where metal is heated and shaped by hammering, often used by a blacksmith
workplace for shaping heated metal

Examples

The blacksmith spent hours in the forge shaping horseshoes.
Steam rose from the forge as metal was plunged into water to cool.
In the old village, the forge was the center of community life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smithy - a blacksmith's workshop

Etymology

From Old French *forger* (to forge), from Latin *fabricare* (to make, build).
