disenchanting

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪsənˈtʃæntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈtʃɑːntɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing (someone) to lose their illusions or false beliefs; making (something) less appealing or magical by revealing the truth.
removing illusions or false beliefs

Examples

The disenchanting truth about the company’s practices disappointed many customers.
After years of excitement, his job slowly became more disenchanting than he'd expected.
Her trip to the city turned out to be a disenchanting experience, far removed from her dreams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disillusioning - causing someone to lose their illusions

Etymology

From 'disenchant' + '-ing', ultimately from Old French 'enchanter' (to enchant), from Latin 'incantare' (to chant a spell).
