corticotropin

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔrtɪkoʊˈtroʊpɪn / (AmE), / ˌkɔːtɪkəʊˈtrəʊpɪn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates the adrenal cortex to secrete corticosteroids
pituitary hormone that stimulates adrenal cortex
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that low corticotropin levels could affect adrenal function.
Researchers measured corticotropin response in patients under stress.
Corticotropin injections may be used in the treatment of certain hormone deficiencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adrenocorticotropic hormone - another name for corticotropin, hormone stimulating the adrenal cortex

Etymology

From cortico- (relating to the cortex) + -tropin (stimulating), patterned after adrenocorticotropic hormone.
