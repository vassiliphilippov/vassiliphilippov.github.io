Sabbatia

1. NOUN

/ sæˈbeɪʃiə / (AmE), / səˈbeɪtɪə / (BrE)
A genus of smooth, slender North American herbs with showy, usually pink or white flowers, belonging to the gentian family.
genus of North American herbs with showy flowers

Examples

The marsh was filled with the delicate pink blossoms of Sabbatia.
Botanists studied several species within the genus Sabbatia during their field research.
Sabbatia plants thrive in wet meadows and attract many pollinators.

Etymology

Named after Liberato Sabbati, Italian botanist (1714–1778).
