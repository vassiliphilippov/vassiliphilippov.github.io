Nemertea

1. NOUN

/ nəˈmɜrtiə / (AmE), / nɛˈmɜːtiə / (BrE)
A phylum of elongated, unsegmented, mostly marine worms characterized by a long proboscis used for capturing prey; also known as proboscis worms.
phylum of unsegmented marine worms

Examples

Nemertea are known for their unique feeding mechanism involving a reversible proboscis.
Marine biologists discovered a new species within the Nemertea phylum off the Pacific coast.
The study of Nemertea has provided insights into early animal evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proboscis worm - another common name for Nemertea

Etymology

From Greek 'Nemertes', a sea nymph, from 'nemertes' (unerring, infallible).

2. NOUN

/ nəˈmɜːrtiə / (AmE), / nɪˈmɜːtɪə / (BrE)
A phylum of mostly marine, elongated, soft-bodied worms, known as proboscis worms, characterized by their eversible proboscis.
phylum of proboscis worms

Examples

Scientists collected several species from the phylum Nemertea during their deep-sea expedition.
Members of Nemertea can be distinguished by their unique eversible proboscis.
The biology class studied Nemertea to understand evolutionary adaptations among worms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proboscis worm - common name for members of Nemertea
  • Antonym: annelid - segmented worms in a different phylum

Etymology

From Greek 'nēmertēs', the name of a mythological figure, used in zoological classification.
