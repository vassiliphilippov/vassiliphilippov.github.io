Helvella acetabulum

1. NOUN

/ hɛlˈvɛlə ˌæsɪˈtæbjələm / (AmE), / hɛlˈvɛlə ˌæsɪˈtæbjʊləm / (BrE)
a species of cup-shaped fungus with a brown interior and a creamy white, ribbed stalk, found in woodland areas.
a cup-shaped woodland fungus species

Examples

Botanists discovered several specimens of Helvella acetabulum during their spring survey in the forest.
The distinctive ribbed stalk makes Helvella acetabulum easy to identify among similar woodland fungi.
We photographed a beautiful Helvella acetabulum growing near a fallen oak tree.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Helvella' is the genus name, and 'acetabulum' means 'small cup'.
