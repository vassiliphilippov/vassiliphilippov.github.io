disingenuousness

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.ɪnˈdʒɛn.ju.əs.nəs / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ɪnˈdʒɛn.juː.əs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being dishonest or insincere, especially by pretending to know less or be less involved than one really is
quality of being insincere or dishonest

Examples

Her disingenuousness became clear when she contradicted herself during the interview.
The politician's disingenuousness eroded the public's trust over time.
He masked his true intentions with a veneer of politeness and disingenuousness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insincerity - lack of sincerity or honesty
  • Antonym: candor - the quality of being open and honest

Etymology

From Latin 'ingenus' (native, honest) with prefix 'dis-' (not) and suffix '-ness'.
