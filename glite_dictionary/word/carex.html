Carex

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛr.ɛks / (AmE), / ˈkɛː.rɛks / (BrE)
a large genus of grass-like plants (sedges) often found in damp areas such as woodlands, bogs, and water margins
a genus of sedge plants

Examples

The marsh was dominated by tall Carex that provided shelter for many birds.
Several species of Carex are used in landscaping for their ornamental foliage.
Botanists discovered a rare Carex growing at the edge of the pond.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedge - a grass-like plant often found in wet areas

Etymology

From Latin *carex* meaning 'sedge'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɛrɛks / (AmE), / ˈkɛːrɛks / (BrE)
a large genus of perennial grass-like plants (sedges) commonly found in damp or wet habitats such as bogs, marshes, and ditches
a genus of sedge plants

Examples

Several species of Carex are commonly used in garden landscaping for their attractive foliage.
The wetland restoration project included planting native Carex to stabilize the stream banks.
Scientists identified the rare sedge as a member of the Carex genus after careful examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedge - a grass-like plant of the family Cyperaceae, often found in wetlands

Etymology

From Latin *carex* (sedge), used as the generic name by Linnaeus.
