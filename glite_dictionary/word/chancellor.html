chancellor

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæn.sə.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːn.səl.ə / (BrE)
the honorary or titular head of a university, often serving in a ceremonial capacity
honorary head of a university

Examples

The university's chancellor presided over the graduation ceremony with great dignity.
Although the chancellor holds an important title, the vice-chancellor manages the university's day-to-day operations.
She was appointed chancellor of the university in recognition of her contributions to education.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vice-chancellor - the chief executive officer of a university, especially in British English

Etymology

From Latin cancellarius (secretary, doorkeeper), later adopted for high-ranking officials in medieval Europe.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæn.sələr / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːn.sələr / (BrE)
the head of government in some countries, especially Germany and Austria; a senior official in some other contexts
head of government (e.g. Germany)

Examples

The German chancellor attended the European summit to discuss economic reforms.
Angela Merkel served as chancellor of Germany for sixteen years.
When the newly elected chancellor took office, she promised to focus on climate policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prime minister - the head of government in parliamentary systems
  • Antonym: president - head of state in some republics (often head of government too)

Etymology

From Old French 'chancelier', from Late Latin 'cancellarius', meaning 'porter, secretary'.
