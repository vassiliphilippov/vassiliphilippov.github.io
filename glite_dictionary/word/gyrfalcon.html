gyrfalcon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɜrˌfælkən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜː.fɔːl.kən / (BrE)
a large, rare falcon of arctic regions, with white, gray, or dark plumage, and the largest of the falcons.
large Arctic falcon species

Examples

A solitary gyrfalcon soared above the tundra, scanning for prey.
The gyrfalcon is highly prized among falconers for its size and strength.
Some gyrfalcons have pure white feathers, while others are dark or mottled.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: falcon - a bird of prey of the family Falconidae

Etymology

From Old French *girfaucon*, from Old Norse *geirfalki* ('spear falcon').
