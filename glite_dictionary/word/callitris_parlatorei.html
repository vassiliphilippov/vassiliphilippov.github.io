Callitris parlatorei

1. NOUN

/ ˌkælɪˈtraɪs ˌpɑːrləˈtɔːreɪ / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈtraɪs ˌpɑːləˈtɔːriː / (BrE)
A species of Australian cypress pine with fibrous inner bark, native to Australia.
species of Australian cypress pine

Examples

The fibrous inner bark of Callitris parlatorei makes it useful for traditional crafts.
Several wildlife species depend on Callitris parlatorei for habitat in Australian woodlands.
Foresters monitor Callitris parlatorei populations to ensure their conservation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cypress pine - common name for trees in the genus Callitris, native to Australia

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name 'Callitris' from Greek 'kallos' (beauty) + 'tryis' (tree), specific epithet honors botanist Parlatore.
