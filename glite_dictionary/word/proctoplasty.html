proctoplasty

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑktəˌplæsti / (AmE), / ˈprɒktəˌplæsti / (BrE)
Surgical reconstruction or repair of the anus or rectum.
reconstructive surgery of the anus or rectum
medical

Examples

The patient underwent a proctoplasty to correct a congenital defect.
Rehabilitation after proctoplasty requires careful monitoring by specialists.
A proctoplasty may be recommended for certain types of rectal injuries.

Etymology

From Greek 'proktos' (anus, rectum) + 'plasty' (molding, surgical repair).
