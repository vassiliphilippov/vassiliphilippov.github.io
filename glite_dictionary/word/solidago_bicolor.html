Solidago bicolor

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɒlɪˈdeɪɡoʊ baɪˈkʌlɚ / (AmE), / ˌsɒlɪˈdeɪɡəʊ baɪˈkʌlə / (BrE)
A species of goldenrod native to eastern North America, noted for its creamy white flowers.
a North American goldenrod with white flowers

Examples

Many wildflower enthusiasts look for Solidago bicolor during late summer hikes in eastern forests.
Unlike most goldenrods, Solidago bicolor has striking white flowers instead of yellow ones.
Gardeners appreciate Solidago bicolor for its unique blooms and ability to attract pollinators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goldenrod - any plant of the genus Solidago, typically with yellow flowers

Etymology

From Latin: solidago (goldenrod), bicolor (two-colored).
