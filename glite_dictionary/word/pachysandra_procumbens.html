Pachysandra procumbens

1. NOUN

/ ˌpækiˈsændrə proʊˈkʌmbənz / (AmE), / ˌpakɪˈsandrə prəˈkʌmbənz / (BrE)
A low, semi-evergreen perennial herb with small spikes of white or pinkish flowers, native to the southern United States but cultivated elsewhere.
semi-evergreen perennial herb, cultivated groundcover

Examples

Gardeners often use Pachysandra procumbens as groundcover in shaded areas.
The delicate flowers of Pachysandra procumbens bloom in early spring each year.
Native to the southeastern United States, Pachysandra procumbens has become popular in ornamental landscaping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Allegheny spurge - another name for Pachysandra procumbens

Etymology

From scientific Latin; genus name 'Pachysandra' from Greek 'pachys' (thick) and 'andros' (male), referring to the thick stamens.
