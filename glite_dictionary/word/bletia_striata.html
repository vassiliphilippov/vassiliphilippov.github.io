Bletia striata

1. NOUN

/ ˈbliːtiə straɪˈeɪtə / (AmE), / ˈbliːtiə straɪˈɑːtə / (BrE)
A Japanese orchid with white-striped leaves and upright clusters of rose to magenta flowers; often grown as an ornamental plant.
species of Japanese orchid

Examples

Many gardeners enjoy growing Bletia striata for its striking magenta flowers in the spring.
The leaves of Bletia striata are easily recognized by their white stripes.
Bletia striata thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade.

Etymology

From Latinized scientific nomenclature: genus name 'Bletia' + species epithet 'striata' meaning 'striped'.
