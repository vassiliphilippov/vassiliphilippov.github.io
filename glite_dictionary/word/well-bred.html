well-bred

1. ADJ

/ ˌwɛlˈbrɛd / (AmE), / ˌwɛlˈbrɛd / (BrE)
having or showing good manners and refined upbringing; polite and well-mannered, especially as a result of a good education or family background.
having good manners and upbringing

Examples

She is a well-bred young woman with impeccable manners.
Despite his well-bred background, he treats everyone with humility and respect.
The well-bred dog followed its owner's commands perfectly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ill-bred - lacking good manners or upbringing; rude or impolite

Etymology

Formed from 'well' (thoroughly) + 'bred' (past participle of 'breed,' meaning to raise or bring up).
