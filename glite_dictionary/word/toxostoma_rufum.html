Toxostoma rufum

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɑksəˈstoʊmə ˈruːfəm / (AmE), / ˌtɒksəˈstəʊmə ˈruːfəm / (BrE)
A large songbird of eastern North America with reddish-brown plumage, commonly known as the brown thrasher.
a large reddish-brown songbird (brown thrasher)

Examples

The Toxostoma rufum is well known for its varied and melodious song.
We spotted a Toxostoma rufum in the bushes near the edge of the forest.
The reddish-brown plumage of the Toxostoma rufum makes it easy to identify among other birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brown thrasher - common name for Toxostoma rufum, a large reddish-brown songbird

Etymology

From Greek toxostoma (bow mouth) and Latin rufum (red).
