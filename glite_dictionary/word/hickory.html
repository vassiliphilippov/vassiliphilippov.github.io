hickory

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪkəri / (AmE), / ˈhɪkəri / (BrE)
A North American hardwood tree of the genus Carya, bearing edible nuts and valued for its tough wood.
North American hardwood tree with edible nuts

Examples

The forest was filled with towering hickory and oak trees.
Squirrels gathered hickory nuts from the ground every autumn.
She used hickory wood to smoke the barbecue meat, giving it a rich flavor.

Etymology

From Virginia Algonquian *pawcohiccora*, referring to a milky drink made from hickory nuts.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɪkəri / (AmE), / ˈhɪkəri / (BrE)
a tough, heavy hardwood obtained from various hickory trees, valued for its strength and used for tool handles, furniture, and flooring
a tough, heavy hardwood from hickory trees

Examples

The carpenter selected hickory for the axe handle because of its strength and durability.
Flooring made from hickory adds a warm, rustic look to any home.
The smoke from burning hickory gives barbecue meats a distinct flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - wood from oak trees, another strong hardwood

Etymology

From an Algonquian language, likely Powhatan *pohickory*, referring to a drink made from hickory nuts.
