nectariferous

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɛktəˈrɪfərəs / (AmE), / ˌnɛktəˈrɪfərəs / (BrE)
(of a plant or part) bearing or producing nectaries that secrete nectar.
bearing nectaries; producing nectar

Examples

Many flowering plants have nectariferous glands to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
The nectariferous tissue in the flower's base helps lure hummingbirds.
Botanists often study nectariferous organs to understand plant-pollinator relationships.

Etymology

From Latin 'nectarifer' (nectar-bearing), from 'nectar' + '-fer' (bearing).
