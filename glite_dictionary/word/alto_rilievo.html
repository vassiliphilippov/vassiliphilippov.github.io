alto rilievo

1. NOUN

/ ˌæltoʊ rɪˈljɛvoʊ / (AmE), / ˌæltəʊ rɪˈljɛːvəʊ / (BrE)
(in sculpture) a relief in which projecting forms are carved deeply and stand out from the background to at least half their depth; high relief.
high relief sculpture

Examples

The figures in the monument were carved in alto rilievo, creating dramatic shadows and depth.
She studied the techniques of alto rilievo to emulate classical masterpieces.
Many ancient temples feature alto rilievo panels depicting mythological scenes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bas-relief - sculptural relief with shallow depth
  • Synonym: relief - sculptural technique where figures are raised from a background

Etymology

From Italian 'alto rilievo', literally 'high relief'.
