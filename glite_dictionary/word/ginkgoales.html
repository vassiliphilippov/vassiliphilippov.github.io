Ginkgoales

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡɪŋkoʊˈeɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌɡɪŋkəʊˈeɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of gymnospermous plants that originated during the Permian period, now represented by the single extant species Ginkgo biloba
order of ancient gymnospermous plants

Examples

Fossil records show that Ginkgoales were widespread during the Paleozoic era.
Today, Ginkgoales is represented only by the Ginkgo biloba tree.
Researchers study Ginkgoales to understand plant evolution and extinction events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ginkgoaceae - family of plants containing the Ginkgo
  • Degree Higher: Coniferales - order containing coniferous trees

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Ginkgo' (from Japanese 'ginkyō') and '-ales', a standard suffix for plant orders.
