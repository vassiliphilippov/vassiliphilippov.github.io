trochaic

1. ADJ

/ troʊˈkeɪɪk / (AmE), / trəʊˈkeɪɪk / (BrE)
(of poetry or verse) consisting of or relating to trochees, which are metrical feet with a stressed syllable followed by an unstressed syllable.
relating to trochees in poetry

Examples

The poet favored a trochaic meter for its strong, rhythmic quality.
Many nursery rhymes are written in a trochaic pattern to create a playful mood.
Her latest poem alternates between iambic and trochaic lines for dramatic effect.

Synset Examples

trochaic dactyl

Related Terms

  • Antonym: iambic - relating to or consisting of iambs (unstressed-stressed metrical feet)

Etymology

From Latin 'trochaicus', from Greek 'trokhaikos' (pertaining to a trochee).
