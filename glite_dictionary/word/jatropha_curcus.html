Jatropha curcus

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈtroʊfə ˈkɜːrkəs / (AmE), / dʒəˈtrəʊfə ˈkɜːkəs / (BrE)
a small tropical American tree whose seeds yield a poisonous purgative oil and whose bark produces a purple dye and tanning extract
tropical tree with poisonous seeds and dye-producing bark

Examples

The oil extracted from Jatropha curcus seeds can be used to produce biodiesel.
Farmers are cautious when handling Jatropha curcus because of the toxic nature of its seeds.
Traditional communities have used the bark of Jatropha curcus for making purple dye.

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name Jatropha and specific epithet from Latinized form of Greek 'kirkos' (a kind of nut), referring to the plant's nuts.
