Gadaba

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈdɑːbə / (AmE), / ɡəˈdɑːbə / (BrE)
a member of an indigenous agricultural people of southeastern India, especially in the states of Odisha and Andhra Pradesh
member of an Indian indigenous group

Examples

The Gadaba are known for their unique traditions and colorful festivals.
A group of Gadaba women were seen weaving traditional textiles at the market.
Anthropologists have studied the farming techniques practiced by the Gadaba.

2. NOUN

/ gəˈdɑːbə / (AmE), / gəˈdɑːbə / (BrE)
The Dravidian language spoken by the Gadaba people in India.
Dravidian language of the Gadaba people

Examples

Few linguists have studied the Gadaba language in detail.
She learned to speak Gadaba during her research in Odisha.
The preservation of Gadaba is important for cultural heritage.

Etymology

From the name of the Gadaba people of eastern India.
