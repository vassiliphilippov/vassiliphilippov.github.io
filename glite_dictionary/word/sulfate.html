sulfate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌl.feɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌl.feɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester of sulfuric acid, containing the anion SO₄²⁻
salt or ester of sulfuric acid

Examples

The chemist analyzed the water for sulfate content to ensure safety standards were met.
Calcium sulfate is commonly used in the construction industry for making plaster.
The presence of sulfate in the soil can affect the growth of certain plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulphate - British spelling of sulfate; a salt or ester of sulphuric acid

Etymology

From French *sulfate*, from Modern Latin *sulfas, sulfat-*

2. VERB

/ ˈsʌlˌfeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌl.feɪt / (BrE)
to treat or react (a substance) with sulfuric acid or a sulfate, or to convert a substance into a sulfate, often through chemical reaction
to convert into a sulfate
medical

Examples

Industrial processes often sulfate metals to produce useful compounds.
The battery plates can sulfate over time if not maintained properly.
Scientists sulfated the organic matter during the experiment to study the reaction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulfonate - treat with or convert into a sulfonate

Etymology

From French sulfate, from Latin sulfur (brimstone).
