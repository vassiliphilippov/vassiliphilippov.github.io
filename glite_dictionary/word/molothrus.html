Molothrus

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈlɒθrəs / (AmE), / məˈlɒθrəs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Icteridae, commonly known as cowbirds, found in the Americas.
genus of birds (cowbirds)

Examples

The Molothrus species are notorious for their brood parasitism.
Researchers observed a Molothrus laying its egg in the nest of another bird.
North American forests are home to several Molothrus species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowbird - a bird of the genus Molothrus known for laying eggs in the nests of other birds

Etymology

From Greek mōlos (struggle) + thrōs (to leap), used as a genus name in taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ məˈloʊθrəs / (AmE), / məˈləʊθrəs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Icteridae, commonly known as cowbirds, native to the Americas.
genus of cowbirds

Examples

The Molothrus genus includes several species of brood-parasitic birds.
Researchers studied how Molothrus species interact with their host birds.
The brown-headed cowbird is a member of the Molothrus genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowbird - a bird of the genus Molothrus, known for laying eggs in the nests of other birds

Etymology

From Greek molos (struggle, battle) and thrus (to rush), referring to the birds' behavior.
