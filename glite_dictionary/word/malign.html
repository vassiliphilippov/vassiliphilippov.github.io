malign

1. ADJ

/ məˈlaɪn / (AmE), / məˈlaɪn / (BrE)
having or exerting a harmful or evil influence; malevolent
having an evil or harmful influence

Examples

The cult was said to worship a malign spirit lurking in the woods.
Some ancient superstitions refer to malign influences of certain planets on human fate.
She believed her misfortune was caused by a malign force beyond her control.

Synset Examples

malevolent stars
a malefic force

Related Terms

  • Antonym: benign - having a kindly or harmless influence
  • Synonym: malevolent - having or showing a wish to do evil to others

Etymology

From Latin malignus (bad-natured, malicious), from malus (bad) + -gnus (born).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

2. VERB

/ məˈlaɪn / (AmE), / məˈlaɪn / (BrE)
to speak about someone in a spitefully critical manner; to say harmful or false things about
to speak ill of someone

Examples

He did not hesitate to malign his competitor during the heated campaign.
It is wrong to malign someone without knowing the full story.
Many celebrities are frequently maligned in the media.

Synset Examples

She badmouths her husband everywhere

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to defame - to damage someone's reputation by making false statements
  • Synonym: slander - to make spoken false statements that damage someone's reputation
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin malignare (to act maliciously), from malignus (bad-natured, malicious).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00849849-v
Synset POS: v

3. ADJ

/ məˈlaɪn / (AmE), / məˈlaɪn / (BrE)
Having an evil or harmful nature or influence; intending to cause harm.
evil or harmful in nature

Examples

There was a malign influence behind the series of unfortunate events.
Doctors were concerned that the tumor might be malign rather than benign.
His malign intentions became clear as the plan unfolded.

Synset Examples

prompted by malign motives
believed in witches and malign spirits
gave him a malign look
a malign lesion

Related Terms

  • Antonym: benign - having a gentle and harmless character or effect
  • Synonym: malevolent - having or showing a wish to do evil to others

Etymology

From Latin malignus (evil, wicked), from malus (bad) + genus (born, kind).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00228595-a
Synset POS: a
