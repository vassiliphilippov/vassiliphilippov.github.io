virucide

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪrjuˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈvɪərʊˌsaɪd / (BrE)
A substance or agent that inactivates or destroys viruses.
agent that destroys viruses
medical

Examples

The laboratory is testing a new virucide to combat emerging viral diseases.
Proper use of a virucide can help prevent the spread of infections in hospitals.
This disinfectant contains a powerful virucide that targets multiple virus strains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disinfectant - a chemical liquid that destroys bacteria
  • Synonym: bactericide - a substance that kills bacteria

Etymology

From Latin virus (poison, slime) + -cide (from Latin caedere, to kill).
