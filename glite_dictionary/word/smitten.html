smitten

1. ADJ

/ ˈsmɪtən / (AmE), / ˈsmɪtən / (BrE)
deeply or suddenly affected by love or strong affection, often to a foolish or unreasonable degree
deeply affected by love

Examples

She has been smitten with her new neighbor since the day they met.
He was completely smitten by her charm and could not stop thinking about her.
Whenever she talks about her dog, you can tell she's truly smitten.

Synset Examples

he was infatuated with her
Narcissus was a beautiful Greek youth who became enamored of his own reflection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infatuated - possessed by an intense, usually short-lived passion or admiration for someone
  • Synonym: enamored - filled with a feeling of love for someone or something
  • Antonym: indifferent - having no particular interest or sympathy; unconcerned

Etymology

Past participle of 'smite', from Old English 'smitan' (to strike).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsmɪtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈsmɪtn̩ / (BrE)
Deeply affected by a strong feeling, especially love or other emotion; overwhelmed by an intense emotion or sensation.
deeply affected by emotion

Examples

She was hopelessly smitten with her new coworker and couldn't think of anything else.
He looked smitten as he watched the fireworks, lost in awe.
After hearing the moving story, the entire audience was smitten with compassion.

Synset Examples

conscience-smitten
awe-struck

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infatuated - intensely but short-livedly in love or attracted
  • Antonym: unaffected - not influenced or touched by a particular emotion

Etymology

Past participle of 'smite', from Old English 'smitan' (to strike).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00071850-s
Synset POS: s
