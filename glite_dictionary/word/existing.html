existing

1. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzɪstɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɪstɪŋ / (BrE)
currently in existence; present or available now.
present or currently existing

Examples

The existing policies need to be reviewed to address current challenges.
She renovated her house instead of demolishing the existing structure.
Many jobs at the company are being affected by changes to the existing system.

Synset Examples

an attempt to refine the existent machinery to make it more efficient
much of the beluga caviar existing in the world is found in the Soviet Union and Iran

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extant - still existing; not destroyed or lost
  • Antonym: nonexistent - not existing; not present or real

Etymology

Present participle of 'exist', from Latin 'existere' (to appear, to exist).

2. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzɪstɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɪstɪŋ / (BrE)
present or currently in existence; used to describe something that is present at the moment, especially in contrast to something new or future.
presently in existence

Examples

The existing system will be replaced by a more efficient one next year.
Many people are dissatisfied with the existing laws regarding environmental protection.
We will renovate the existing building instead of constructing a new one.

Synset Examples

the existing system

Related Terms

  • Antonym: new - recently created or introduced; not existing before

Etymology

Present participle of 'exist', from Latin 'exsistere' (to emerge, to exist).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01736035-s
Synset POS: s
