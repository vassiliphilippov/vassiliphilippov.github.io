Berberis canadensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɝːˈbɛrɪs kæˈneɪdənˌsɪs / (AmE), / ˌbɜːˈbɛrɪs kəˈneɪdənˌsɪs / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, noted for its scarlet autumn leaves, yellow flower clusters, and glossy red berries.
North American deciduous shrub with red berries

Examples

The Berberis canadensis in my garden produces bright red berries every autumn.
Botanists study Berberis canadensis for its unique adaptation to the eastern North American climate.
In fall, the leaves of Berberis canadensis turn a vivid scarlet, making it a popular ornamental shrub.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barberry - any shrub of the genus Berberis

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Berberis' (barberry) + species epithet 'canadensis' meaning 'from Canada'.
