preexistence

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriːɪɡˈzɪstəns / (AmE), / ˌpriːɪɡˈzɪstəns / (BrE)
the state of existing before something else or before a particular event or time
existence before something else

Examples

Many religions include the concept of the preexistence of the soul.
Philosophers have debated the idea of preexistence for centuries.
The preexistence of certain traditions shapes modern cultural practices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to preexist - to exist earlier or before something else

Etymology

From Latin prae- (before) + existentia (existence).
