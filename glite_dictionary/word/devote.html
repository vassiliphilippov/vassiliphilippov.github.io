to devote

1. VERB

/ dɪˈvoʊt / (AmE), / dɪˈvəʊt / (BrE)
to set aside or dedicate something for a specific purpose or use
set aside for a purpose

Examples

The council devoted land for public parks.
A portion of the budget is devoted to research and development.
They are devoting all their resources to the new project.

Synset Examples

this land was devoted to mining

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dedicate - to set apart for a special purpose or use
  • Synonym: reserve - to keep or set aside for a particular person, purpose, or use

Etymology

From Latin 'devotare', meaning to vow or dedicate.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02219499-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dɪˈvoʊt / (AmE), / dɪˈvəʊt / (BrE)
to give all or a large part of one's time, resources, or attention to a particular person, activity, or cause
dedicate time or effort to something

Examples

She decided to devote her weekends to volunteering at the animal shelter.
He devoted several years to writing his first novel.
Many scientists devote their lives to researching ways to cure diseases.

Synset Examples

She committed herself to the work of God
give one's talents to a good cause
consecrate your life to the church

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dedicate - to commit time or effort to a particular task or purpose
  • Synonym: consecrate - to make or declare something sacred; to devote to a purpose with deep dedication
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to give proper care or attention to something or someone

Etymology

From Latin 'devotare' (to vow, dedicate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00888984-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dɪˈvoʊt / (AmE), / dɪˈvəʊt / (BrE)
to give most or all of your time, attention, or resources to something or someone; to dedicate yourself or something to a particular purpose
dedicate resources or attention

Examples

She decided to devote more time to her family after the promotion.
The volunteers devote their weekends to helping the homeless.
He has devoted much of his life to scientific research.

Synset Examples

give thought to
give priority to
pay attention to

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dedicate - to commit time, effort, or resources for a particular purpose
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to give proper attention or care

Etymology

From Latin ‘devotus’, past participle of ‘devovēre’ meaning 'to vow' or 'dedicate'.
