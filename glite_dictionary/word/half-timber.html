half-timber

1. ADJ

/ ˈhæf ˌtɪmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɑːf ˌtɪmbə / (BrE)
(of a building or wall) having exposed wood framing with the spaces between filled with masonry, plaster, or brick, typically characteristic of Tudor architecture.
having exposed wood framing in building

Examples

The village is famous for its half-timber houses dating back to the 16th century.
Architects restored the old inn, carefully preserving its half-timber construction.
Many half-timber buildings can still be seen in parts of rural England.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: half-timbered - constructed with exposed wood framing and infill between

Etymology

From the architectural practice of using half-exposed wooden timbers in building walls.
