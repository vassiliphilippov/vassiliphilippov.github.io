beard

1. NOUN

/ bɪrd / (AmE), / bɪəd / (BrE)
A tuft of strong, hair-like filaments, especially the byssus by which certain mollusks, such as mussels, attach themselves to a surface.
tuft of filaments on some mollusks

Examples

The mussel used its beard to cling tightly to the rock.
Scientists studied the strength of the mussel's beard in different water conditions.
Once the beard is removed, the mussel is ready for cooking.

2. NOUN

/ bɪrd / (AmE), / bɪəd / (BrE)
A tuft or growth of hairs or bristles on certain parts of plants, such as the flowers of irises or the heads of grasses.
tuft of hairs/bristles on plants

Examples

Many varieties of iris have a distinctive beard on their petals.
The beard of the wheat plant can help protect the grain from pests.
Some grasses have a long beard that makes them easy to identify.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barb - a sharp projection often found on plants or animals

4. NOUN

/ bɪrd / (AmE), / bɪəd / (BrE)
a growth of coarse hair on the face, especially on the chin and cheeks of men or around the muzzle of certain animals
hairy growth on the face or near the muzzle

Examples

He decided to grow a beard during his vacation from work.
The mountain goat had a long, shaggy beard under its chin.
She stroked her father's white beard as he told her stories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mustache - strip of hair grown above the upper lip
  • Antonym: clean-shaven - having no hair on the face

Etymology

From Old English *beard*, from Proto-Germanic *bardaz*, related to Latin *barba* (beard).

6. VERB

/ bɪrd / (AmE), / bɪəd / (BrE)
(literary) To border the edge or follow the contour of something, especially by surrounding along the rim, like a beard on a face.
border the rim or edge, like a beard

Examples

Small pine trees bearded the ridges of the valley.
Colorful flowers beard the edge of the old garden fence.
A low wall bearded the circular plaza, enclosing it from all sides.
