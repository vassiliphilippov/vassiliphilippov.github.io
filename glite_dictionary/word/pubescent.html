pubescent

1. ADJ

/ pjuˈbɛsənt / (AmE), / pjuːˈbɛsənt / (BrE)
(of a part of a plant or animal) covered with fine, soft hairs or down.
covered with fine, soft hairs

Examples

Many leaves of the silver birch are pubescent when young.
The stems of the plant were distinctly pubescent to the touch.
Some caterpillars have a pubescent body that helps protect them from predators.

Synset Examples

downy milkweed seeds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: downy - covered with fine, soft hairs

Etymology

From Latin pubescent-, past participle stem of pubescere 'to reach puberty, become hairy'.

2. ADJ

/ pjuˈbɛsənt / (AmE), / pjuːˈbɛsənt / (BrE)
Having reached the stage of puberty but not yet fully mature; undergoing the physical changes of adolescence.
having reached puberty but not yet mature

Examples

The doctor explained that pubescent children often experience rapid growth spurts.
Some pubescent boys develop deeper voices and facial hair.
During the pubescent years, teenagers become more aware of their changing bodies.

Synset Examples

the budding breasts of a pubescent girl and the downy chin of pubescent boy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adolescent - a young person developing from a child into an adult

Etymology

From Latin pubescentem (growing up, downy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01494602-s
Synset POS: s
