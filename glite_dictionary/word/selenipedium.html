Selenipedium

1. NOUN

/ sɛˌlɛnɪˈpiːdiəm / (AmE), / sɪˌliːnɪˈpiːdiəm / (BrE)
A genus of tall, reedlike tropical American orchids, some species of which have pods used locally as a substitute for vanilla.
genus of tropical American orchids

Examples

The rare Selenipedium orchid is prized for its unique, slipper-shaped flowers.
Some villagers use the pods of Selenipedium as a vanilla substitute in traditional recipes.
Botanists are working to preserve several endangered Selenipedium species in South American rainforests.

Etymology

From Greek 'selene' (moon) and 'pedilon' (slipper), referring to the moon-shaped pouch in the orchid's flower.

2. NOUN

/ səˌliːnɪˈpiːdiəm / (AmE), / sɛˌliːnɪˈpiːdɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of tall, reedlike orchids native to tropical America, some of whose pods are used locally as a substitute for vanilla.
genus of tropical American orchids

Examples

The rare Selenipedium orchids are prized for their unusual flowers and tall growth.
Some local communities use the pods of Selenipedium as a natural vanilla substitute.
Conservationists are working to protect Selenipedium species threatened by habitat loss.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek 'selene' (moon) and 'pedion' (plain, flat surface).
