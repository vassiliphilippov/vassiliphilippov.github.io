wrongdoing

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɔŋˌduːɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɒŋˌduːɪŋ / (BrE)
illegal or immoral behavior; actions that break laws or moral standards.
illegal or immoral behavior

Examples

The company launched an investigation into the alleged wrongdoing by its employees.
He admitted to no wrongdoing and insisted on his innocence.
Rumors about corruption and wrongdoing damaged the politician's reputation.

Synset Examples

he denied any wrongdoing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offense - an illegal act; a breach of a law or rule
  • Synonym: crime - an act that breaks the law
  • Antonym: innocence - the state of not being guilty of a crime or offense

Etymology

Formed from 'wrong' + 'doing' (noun from the verb 'do'), meaning the act of doing wrong.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɔŋˌduɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɒŋˌduːɪŋ / (BrE)
behavior or actions that are morally or legally wrong; an act that violates ethical or legal standards.
illegal or unethical behavior

Examples

Corporate wrongdoing was uncovered in the financial audit.
She denied any wrongdoing in connection to the missing funds.
The committee investigated the allegations of wrongdoing by public officials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misconduct - improper or illegal behavior, especially by a professional person
  • Synonym: crime - an action or omission that constitutes an offense punishable by law
  • Antonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards

Etymology

From 'wrong' + 'doing', first recorded in the 14th century.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
