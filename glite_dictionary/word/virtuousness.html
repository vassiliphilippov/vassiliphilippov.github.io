virtuousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɝː.tʃu.əs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.tjuː.əs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of having high moral standards and consistently doing what is right
quality of being morally good

Examples

Her virtuousness earned her the respect of everyone in the community.
Many leaders speak about virtuousness but fail to practice it in private.
He was admired not only for his intelligence but also for his virtuousness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards
  • Antonym: vice - immoral or wicked behavior

Etymology

From Latin 'virtus' (moral excellence) + English suffix '-ousness.'
