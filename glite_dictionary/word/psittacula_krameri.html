Psittacula krameri

1. NOUN

/ sɪˌtækjələ ˈkrɑːmɛri / (AmE), / sɪˌtækjʊlə ˈkrɑːmɛri / (BrE)
A species of African and South Asian parakeet, also known as the ring-necked parakeet, noted for its green plumage and distinctive ring around the neck of adult males.
species of African/South Asian parakeet

Examples

The Psittacula krameri has adapted well to urban environments in many cities around the world.
Several Psittacula krameri were spotted nesting in the ancient trees of the park.
Scientists are studying how Psittacula krameri populations impact local ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ring-necked parakeet - Common English name for Psittacula krameri

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name 'Psittacula' from Latin 'psittacus' (parrot); species honored botanist Wilhelm Heinrich Kramer.
