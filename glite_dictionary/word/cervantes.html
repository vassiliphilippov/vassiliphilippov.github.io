Cervantes

1. PROPN

/ sərˈvɑːntiz / (AmE), / səˈvɑːnteɪz / (BrE)
(1547–1616) Spanish writer famous for 'Don Quixote', which satirizes chivalry and influenced the development of the modern novel.
Spanish novelist and author of 'Don Quixote'

Examples

Many scholars argue that Cervantes transformed the landscape of Western literature.
In her essay, she compares the works of Shakespeare and Cervantes.
The influence of Cervantes is evident in countless modern novels.

Etymology

From the Spanish family name Cervantes.
