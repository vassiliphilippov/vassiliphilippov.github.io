femininity

1. NOUN

/ fəˌmɪnəˈnɪɾi / (AmE), / fəˌmɪnɪˈnɪti / (BrE)
the quality or nature of having qualities traditionally associated with women, such as gentleness, sensitivity, or grace
qualities traditionally associated with women

Examples

Many cultures celebrate femininity in art and literature.
She was proud of her femininity and did not try to hide it.
The fashion industry often shapes perceptions of femininity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: masculinity - the quality or nature of having qualities traditionally associated with men

Etymology

From Latin 'fēminīnitās', from 'fēmina' meaning 'woman'.
