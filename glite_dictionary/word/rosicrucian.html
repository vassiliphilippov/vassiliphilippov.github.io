Rosicrucian

1. NOUN

/ ˌroʊzɪˈkruːʃən / (AmE), / ˌrəʊzɪˈkruːʃən / (BrE)
a member of a secret 17th-century society of philosophers and scholars devoted to mystical, metaphysical, and alchemical traditions
member of a secret mystical 17th-century society

Examples

Many believed the Rosicrucian order possessed secret knowledge of the natural world.
The mysterious symbolism of the Rosicrucian could be seen in various European works of the seventeenth century.
He studied alchemy and metaphysics as a dedicated Rosicrucian for most of his life.

Etymology

From Modern Latin 'Rosicrucianus', from 'rosa crucis' (rose cross), the emblem of the order.

2. ADJ

/ ˌroʊzɪˈkruːʃən / (AmE), / ˌrəʊzɪˈkruːʃən / (BrE)
of or relating to the Rosicrucians, a secretive mystical and philosophical society originating in Europe in the early 17th century.
relating to the Rosicrucians society

Examples

The Rosicrucian movement had a significant influence on certain streams of Western esotericism.
She studied Rosicrucian texts to better understand the society's philosophical underpinnings.
The Rosicrucian tradition emphasizes a symbolic interpretation of nature and the cosmos.

Etymology

From Rosicrucian, referring to the Society of the Rosy Cross, from Latin 'rosa' (rose) + 'crux' (cross).

3. NOUN

/ ˌroʊzɪˈkruːʃən / (AmE), / ˌrəʊzɪˈkruːʃən / (BrE)
a member of any of several organizations inspired by or derived from a secretive philosophical society established in 17th-century Europe, characterized by mystical and esoteric beliefs
member of a mystical society

Examples

During the 18th century, a notable Rosicrucian wrote treatises on alchemy and mysticism.
She joined a modern Rosicrucian order to explore spiritual philosophy and symbolism.
As a dedicated Rosicrucian, he participated in secretive rituals designed to promote enlightenment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Freemason - member of a worldwide fraternal organization, often with secret rituals

Etymology

From Latin 'Rosæ Crucis' or 'Rosicrucis' (rose of the cross), the symbol of the society.
