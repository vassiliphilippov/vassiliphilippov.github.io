grits

1. NOUN

/ ɡrɪts / (AmE), / ɡrɪts / (BrE)
coarsely ground hulled corn that is boiled and eaten as a hot breakfast dish, especially in the southern United States
boiled ground corn breakfast dish
american

Examples

We had a bowl of grits with butter for breakfast every morning during our trip to Georgia.
She served shrimp and grits at the dinner party, impressing all her guests.
He likes his grits creamy, with cheese melted on top.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polenta - Italian dish of boiled cornmeal
  • Synonym: porridge - soft food made by boiling meal of grains

Etymology

Plural of 'grit', from Old English 'grytt' (bran, coarse meal).
