2. NOUN

/ zaɪɡˌnɛm.əˈteɪ.siː / (AmE), / zɪɡˌnɛməˈtiːsiː / (BrE)
a family of freshwater green algae, commonly forming slimy green masses in ponds and streams
family of freshwater green algae

Examples

The slimy green coating in the aquarium was identified as a member of the Zygnemataceae family.
Many freshwater ponds contain abundant Zygnemataceae, also known as pond scum.
Scientists study Zygnemataceae to understand their role in aquatic ecosystems.

Etymology

From the genus name Zygnema, derived from Greek 'zygon' (yoke) and 'nema' (thread), referencing the conjugating threads of the algae.
