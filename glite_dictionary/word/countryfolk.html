countryfolk

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌn.tɹiˌfoʊk / (AmE), / ˈkʌn.triˌfəʊk / (BrE)
people from the same country; compatriots
people from the same country

Examples

During the ceremony, the ambassador greeted his fellow countryfolk with warmth and pride.
Many countryfolk came together to celebrate their national holiday.
She enjoys connecting with her countryfolk when traveling abroad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compatriot - a person from the same country as oneself
  • Antonym: foreigner - a person from another country

Etymology

From 'country' + 'folk', meaning people of a place.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌntriˌfoʊk / (AmE), / ˈkʌntrɪfəʊk / (BrE)
people who live in, or come from, the countryside rather than a town or city
rural people; people from the countryside

Examples

The countryfolk gathered in the square for the annual harvest festival.
Despite the storm, the countryfolk managed to help each other rebuild their homes.
She missed the easy kindness of the countryfolk she grew up around.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: townsfolk - people who live in a town or city

Etymology

Formed from 'country' + 'folk', meaning people from rural areas.
