semasiology

1. NOUN

/ ˌsiːməsiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌsiːməsɪˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of linguistics that studies the meanings of words and the cognitive aspects of meaning
study of meaning in language

Examples

She published a groundbreaking article in semasiology focusing on how words acquire new meanings.
Semasiology explores how the cognitive processes of speakers influence language change over time.
The professor's lecture on semasiology clarified the distinction between meaning and reference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: semantics - the study of meaning in language

Etymology

From Greek sēmasia (signification) + -logy (study of).
