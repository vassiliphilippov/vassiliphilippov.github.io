soled

1. ADJ

/ soʊld / (AmE), / səʊld / (BrE)
(of footwear) having a sole or soles of a specified type, as used in combination with another word.
having a specified type of sole (in compounds)

Examples

My favorite hiking boots are rubber-soled for extra grip.
She bought a pair of leather-soled dress shoes for the event.
The cobbler recommended that I get my shoes half-soled to prolong their life.

Synset Examples

half-soled
rubber-soled

Etymology

Past participle of 'sole', meaning base or bottom of a shoe.
