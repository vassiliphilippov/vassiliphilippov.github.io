da Vinci

1. PROPN

/ də ˈvɪntʃi / (AmE), / də ˈvɪntʃi / (BrE)
Leonardo da Vinci (1452–1519), Italian painter, sculptor, engineer, scientist, and architect; renowned as a leading figure of the Renaissance.
Leonardo da Vinci, Italian Renaissance polymath

Examples

The Mona Lisa, painted by da Vinci, remains one of the world's most famous artworks.
Historians often cite da Vinci as the quintessential Renaissance man for his wide-ranging talents.
The genius of da Vinci is evident in both his art and his scientific sketches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Leonardo da Vinci - full name of da Vinci, the Italian Renaissance polymath

Etymology

Italian, meaning 'from Vinci' (Vinci is a town in Tuscany, Italy).
