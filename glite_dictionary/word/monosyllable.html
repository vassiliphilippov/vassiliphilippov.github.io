monosyllable

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.nəˌsɪl.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.əˌsɪl.ə.bəl / (BrE)
A word or utterance that has only one syllable.
word of one syllable

Examples

She responded to every question with a curt monosyllable.
The word 'yes' is a good example of a monosyllable.
Children often use monosyllables when they are feeling shy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polysyllable - word or utterance with more than one syllable
  • Synonym: syllable - a unit of pronunciation having one vowel sound

Etymology

From Greek monosyllabos, from monos (single) + syllabē (syllable).
