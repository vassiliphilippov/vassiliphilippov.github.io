resourcefulness

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɔrsfəlnɪs / (AmE), / rɪˈzɔːsfʊlnəs / (BrE)
the ability to deal effectively and creatively with difficult or unusual situations
ability to handle difficult situations

Examples

Her resourcefulness impressed everyone during the challenging expedition.
The company's success is due in part to the resourcefulness of its employees.
He faced every obstacle with calm and resourcefulness.

Synset Examples

a man of resource

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ingenuity - the quality of being clever and inventive
  • Synonym: creativity - the use of imagination or original ideas to solve problems
  • Antonym: helplessness - the inability to cope or act effectively

Etymology

From 'resourceful' + '-ness'. 'Resourceful' derives from French 'ressource' (means of supplying a want).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɔrsfəlnəs / (AmE), / rɪˈzɔːsfəlnəs / (BrE)
the ability to find quick and clever ways to overcome difficulties
the ability to deal effectively with problems

Examples

Her resourcefulness helped the team overcome several unexpected challenges during the project.
We admired his resourcefulness in finding shelter when the storm hit suddenly.
Effective leaders show great resourcefulness under pressure.

Synset Examples

a man of great resourcefulness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ingenuity - the quality of being clever, original, and inventive
  • Synonym: improvisation - the act of creating or performing something spontaneously or without preparation
  • Antonym: helplessness - the inability to deal with problems or difficulties

Etymology

From 'resourceful' + '-ness'. 'Resourceful' derives from 'resource', which comes from French 'ressource' and Latin 'resurgere' (to rise again).
