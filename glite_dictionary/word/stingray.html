stingray

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪŋˌreɪ / (AmE), / ˈstɪŋreɪ / (BrE)
a large, flat sea fish with a long, thin tail armed with one or more venomous barbed spines, capable of inflicting painful wounds
large venomous fish with a barbed tail

Examples

We saw a stingray gliding gracefully along the ocean floor during our dive.
The wound from the stingray's tail required immediate medical attention.
Stingrays are often found in shallow coastal waters, where they bury themselves in the sand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ray - flat-bodied cartilaginous fish of the order Batoidea
