Zonotrichia albicollis

1. NOUN

/ zoʊ.nəˈtrɪ.ki.ə æl.bɪˈkɔ.lɪs / (AmE), / zəʊ.nəˈtrɪ.ki.ə æl.bɪˈkɒ.lɪs / (BrE)
A species of North American sparrow with a distinctive white throat patch and black-and-white striped crown, commonly known as the white-throated sparrow.
white-throated sparrow species

Examples

The distinctive song of the Zonotrichia albicollis can be heard throughout the forests of North America in spring.
A Zonotrichia albicollis was spotted at the bird feeder, identified by its striking white throat.
Ornithologists studied the migratory patterns of Zonotrichia albicollis across the eastern United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white-throated sparrow - Common name for Zonotrichia albicollis

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name from Greek zōnē (belt, girdle) + thrix (hair); species name from Latin albus (white) + collis (necked), referring to the bird's white throat.
