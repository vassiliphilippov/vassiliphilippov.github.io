amativeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmətɪvnəs / (AmE), / əˈmætɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being prone to sexual desire or to romantic love
tendency to sexual desire or romantic love

Examples

The phrenologist claimed that the bump above his ear signified great amativeness.
Victorian literature often referred to amativeness in the context of hidden passions.
The psychologist discussed the role of amativeness in human relationships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sexual desire - the feeling of wanting to engage in sexual activity
  • Synonym: romantic love - strong feelings of affection and attraction

Etymology

From Latin 'amativus' (loving), from 'amare' (to love) + English '-ness'.
