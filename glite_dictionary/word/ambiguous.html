ambiguous

1. ADJ

/ æmˈbɪɡjuəs / (AmE), / amˈbɪɡjʊəs / (BrE)
having no definite or intrinsic meaning; open to more than one interpretation; not clearly organized or defined.
open to more than one interpretation

Examples

The instructions for assembling the shelf were so ambiguous that no one could figure them out.
The artist enjoyed creating ambiguous images that viewers could interpret in multiple ways.
Because the rules were ambiguous, players argued over what was allowed during the game.

Synset Examples

an ambiguous situation with no frame of reference
ambiguous inkblots

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vague - not clearly expressed or defined
  • Antonym: clear - easy to perceive, understand, or interpret

Etymology

From Latin ambiguus (uncertain, changeable), from ambigere (to wander, to go about).

2. ADJ

/ æmˈbɪɡjuəs / (AmE), / æmˈbɪɡjuəs / (BrE)
open to more than one interpretation; not having one obvious meaning.
having more than one meaning

Examples

Her answer to the question was deliberately ambiguous to avoid giving too much away.
The instructions were ambiguous, leading to confusion among the students.
The phrase is ambiguous and could be interpreted in several different ways.

Synset Examples

ambiguous words
frustrated by ambiguous instructions, the parents were unable to assemble the toy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clear - easy to understand; not ambiguous
  • Synonym: vague - not clearly expressed or defined; lacking precision

Etymology

From Latin ambiguus (uncertain), from ambigere (to wander, waver).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00103000-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ æmˈbɪɡjuəs / (AmE), / æmˈbɪɡjuəs / (BrE)
having more than one possible meaning; unclear or open to multiple interpretations, often intentionally so.
open to multiple interpretations

Examples

The instructions were so ambiguous that no one knew what to do next.
Her answer was intentionally ambiguous, leaving everyone confused about her true intentions.
Because the law was ambiguous, experts disagreed on how it should be interpreted.

Synset Examples

an equivocal statement
the polling had a complex and equivocal (or ambiguous) message for potential female candidates
the officer's equivocal behavior increased the victim's uneasiness
popularity is an equivocal crown
an equivocal response to an embarrassing question

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equivocal - open to more than one interpretation; ambiguous
  • Antonym: clear - easy to understand; not ambiguous or confusing

Etymology

From Latin ambiguus (uncertain, wavering), from ambigere (to wander about, to doubt).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00898735-a
