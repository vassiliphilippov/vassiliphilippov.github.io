Lancashire

1. PROPN

/ ˈlæŋ.kə.ʃɪr / (AmE), / ˈlaŋ.kə.ʃə / (BrE)
a historic county in northwestern England on the Irish Sea, noted for its role in the textile industry
historic county in northwest England
british

Examples

The mills of Lancashire played a key role in the Industrial Revolution.
Many families in Lancashire have a history in textile manufacturing.
We spent our holiday exploring the scenic countryside of Lancashire.

Etymology

From Old English 'Lancaster' (city name) plus 'shire' (county).
