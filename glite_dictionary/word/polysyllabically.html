polysyllabically

1. ADV

/ ˌpɑː.lɪ.sɪˈlæb.ɪ.kli / (AmE), / ˌpɒ.lɪ.sɪˈlæb.ɪ.kli / (BrE)
In a manner involving or using polysyllabic words; using words with many syllables.
in a manner using many-syllabled words

Examples

The professor spoke polysyllabically during the lecture, making the material difficult for some students to understand.
He tends to write polysyllabically, favoring long, complex words over simple ones.
The child's story was refreshingly simple, avoiding the temptation to speak polysyllabically.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monosyllabically - in a manner using only single-syllable words
  • Synonym: polysyllabic - having or using words of many syllables

Etymology

From 'polysyllabic' + '-ally', ultimately from Greek 'polysyllabos' (having many syllables).
