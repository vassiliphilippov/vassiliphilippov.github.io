Melastomaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛləˌstoʊˈmeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌmɛləˌstɒˈmæsiː / (BrE)
a large family of tropical trees, shrubs, and herbs in the order Myrtales, many of which are grown as ornamental plants
a family of flowering plants

Examples

The rainforest is home to several species belonging to the Melastomaceae family.
Gardeners often choose members of Melastomaceae for their attractive flowers and foliage.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of certain plants within the Melastomaceae.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Melastoma (a type genus) and the standard botanical suffix -aceae for plant families.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmiː.læˌstoʊˈmeɪ.siː / (AmE), / ˌmiː.læˌstɒˈmeɪ.siː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, including trees, shrubs, and herbs, many of which are grown as ornamentals, native to tropical and subtropical regions
family of flowering plants

Examples

The Melastomaceae family includes many species valued for their ornamental flowers.
Botanists often study Melastomaceae due to their diversity in tropical forests.
Several members of the Melastomaceae are grown in botanical gardens worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Myrtales - order of flowering plants that includes Melastomaceae

Etymology

From the genus Melastoma, from Greek melas (black) and stoma (mouth), referring to the staining properties of the fruit.
