Phillyrea

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈlɪriə / (AmE), / fɪˈlɪriə / (BrE)
a small genus of evergreen shrubs native to the Mediterranean region, belonging to the olive family (Oleaceae)
genus of Mediterranean evergreen shrubs

Examples

The Phillyrea shrub is often used for ornamental hedges in Mediterranean gardens.
Botanists have documented several species within the Phillyrea genus.
The leaves of Phillyrea are leathery and remain green throughout the year.

Etymology

From Latin *phillyrea*, from Greek *phullírea*, a type of tree or shrub.

2. NOUN

/ fɪˈlɪriə / (AmE), / fɪˈlɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of small evergreen shrubs native to the Mediterranean region, belonging to the olive family.
genus of Mediterranean evergreen shrubs

Examples

The Phillyrea shrubs thrive in the dry, rocky soils of southern Europe.
Botanists collected samples of Phillyrea for their Mediterranean flora study.
Gardeners often plant Phillyrea species as ornamental hedges due to their dense evergreen foliage.

Etymology

From Greek phillyra (leafy tree), adopted in modern Latin botanical naming.
