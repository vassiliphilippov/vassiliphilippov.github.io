armor

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrmər / (AmE), / ˈɑːmə / (BrE)
a military unit consisting of armored fighting vehicles, such as tanks and personnel carriers
military unit of armored vehicles

Examples

The commander led the armor into the battle zone at dawn.
Intensive training prepares armor units to operate effectively in any terrain.
Modern armor plays a crucial role in ground operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armored division - a large military unit made up primarily of armored vehicles

Etymology

From Old French *armure*, from Latin *armatura* (arms, equipment).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑrmɚ / (AmE), / ˈɑːmə / (BrE)
Strong protective covering, typically made of metal, worn in battle to protect the body from weapons.
protective metal covering for combat

Examples

The knight's armor gleamed in the sunlight as he rode into battle.
Modern soldiers use body armor to protect themselves from gunfire.
The museum displayed a suit of medieval armor among its historical artifacts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shield - a piece of defensive armor used to intercept attacks

Etymology

From Old French 'armure', from Latin 'armatura' (equipment, armor), from 'arma' (arms, weapons).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrmər / (AmE), / ˈɑːmə / (BrE)
a tough, usually rigid protective covering found on some animals or plants, such as shells, scales, or thick skin, that protects against injury or predators
protective outer covering of animals or plants

Examples

The armor of the turtle protects it from predators in the wild.
Certain beetles have a thick armor to shield them from attack.
The cactus developed a natural armor of spines and tough skin to survive in harsh climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shell - hard outer covering of certain animals, such as snails or turtles
  • Synonym: exoskeleton - external skeleton that supports and protects an animal's body

Etymology

From Old French 'armeure', from Latin 'armatura' (equipment, armor).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01905391-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈɑːrmər / (AmE), / ˈɑːmə / (BrE)
to equip or provide with protective armor
to equip with protective armor

Examples

The engineers were tasked to armor the vehicle before it entered the combat zone.
They armored the castle walls to withstand any attack.
Workers are armoring the trucks with reinforced steel panels for extra security.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to protect - to keep safe from harm or injury

Etymology

From Old French 'armeure', from Latin 'armatura' (equipment, armor).
