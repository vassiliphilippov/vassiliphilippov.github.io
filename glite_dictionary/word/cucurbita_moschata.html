Cucurbita moschata

1. NOUN

/ kuːˈkɝːbɪtə mɑːˈskɑːtə / (AmE), / kjuːˈkɜːbɪtə mɒsˈkɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of squash plant native to the Americas, including varieties such as butternut squash, known for hard rinds and elongated curved necks.
species of squash plant

Examples

Farmers in the region grow several vegetables, especially Cucurbita moschata varieties for their hardy characteristics.
The roasted seeds of Cucurbita moschata are a popular snack in many countries.
Some gardeners prefer Cucurbita moschata for its resistance to pests and diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butternut squash - a variety of squash within Cucurbita moschata

Etymology

From Latin: 'cucurbita' meaning 'gourd', and 'moschata' meaning 'musky'.
