propanone

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈpæn.oʊn / (AmE), / prəʊˈpæn.əʊn / (BrE)
a colorless, highly flammable liquid ketone used as an organic solvent and in making plastics; also known as acetone
a flammable liquid ketone (acetone)
medical

Examples

Propanone is commonly used as a solvent in laboratory settings.
The factory stores large quantities of propanone for its plastic manufacturing process.
Because propanone is highly flammable, proper storage and handling are required.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acetone - a colorless volatile liquid used as a solvent and in making plastics

Etymology

From propane + -one (indicating a ketone in chemical nomenclature).
