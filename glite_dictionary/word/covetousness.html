covetousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌvətəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈkʌvɪtəsnɪs / (BrE)
a strong or excessive desire for material possessions or wealth; greed
strong desire for wealth; greed

Examples

His covetousness led him to betray his closest friends for money.
She warned against covetousness as a source of unhappiness and discontent.
The story illustrates how covetousness can destroy families and communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greed - intense and selfish desire for something, especially wealth
  • Antonym: generosity - the willingness to give or share; unselfishness

Etymology

From Old French 'coveitos', from Latin 'cupiditas', meaning desire.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌvətəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈkʌvɪtəsnɪs / (BrE)
an insatiable or inordinate desire for wealth or possessions, especially considered a moral fault; greed
insatiable desire for wealth or possessions; greed

Examples

His covetousness led him to betray even his closest friends for financial gain.
The tale warns against covetousness and stresses the importance of humility and moderation.
She was accused of covetousness after trying to acquire all the family's inheritance for herself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greed - intense and selfish desire for something, especially wealth or power
  • Antonym: generosity - willingness to give or share; opposite of being greedy or covetous

Etymology

From Old French *coveitosnesse*, from *covetous*, ultimately from Latin *cupiditas* (desire).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkʌv.ɪ.təs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkʌv.ɪ.təs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of having an intense desire to possess something, especially something belonging to someone else
strong desire to possess, especially what belongs to others

Examples

He looked at his neighbor's new car with unmistakable covetousness.
Religious teachings often warn against covetousness and greed.
Her covetousness became apparent when she tried to manipulate the will in her favor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greed - intense and selfish desire for something, especially wealth or power
  • Synonym: envy - a feeling of discontented longing for someone else's possessions or qualities

Etymology

From Old French 'covetous', from covet + -ous + -ness.
