organist

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔr.ɡə.nɪst / (AmE), / ˈɔː.ɡənɪst / (BrE)
a person who plays the organ, especially as a professional or in church services
person who plays the organ

Examples

The organist performed a beautiful solo during the wedding ceremony.
After years of practice, she became the lead organist at her local church.
The concert featured a renowned jazz organist from New York.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pianist - a person who plays the piano
  • Degree Lower: organ - a large musical instrument with pipes and a keyboard

Etymology

From organ + -ist, denoting a person who practices or is concerned with something.
