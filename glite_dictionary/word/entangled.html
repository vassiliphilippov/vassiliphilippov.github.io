entangled

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtæŋɡəld / (AmE), / ɪnˈtæŋɡəld / (BrE)
deeply involved in a complicated or difficult situation
involved in a complicated situation

Examples

She found herself entangled in the office politics despite her best efforts to avoid them.
The company became entangled in a web of legal disputes.
He got entangled in something far more complex than he had expected.

Synset Examples

embroiled in the conflict
felt unwilling entangled in their affairs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embroiled - involved deeply in conflict or complicated situations
  • Synonym: involved - taking part in an activity or situation, especially one that is complicated or difficult

Etymology

From the verb 'entangle,' itself from Middle English and Anglo-French, based on 'tangle.'

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtæŋɡəld / (AmE), / ɪnˈtæŋɡəld / (BrE)
twisted together in a confusing or complicated mass; involved in a tangle
twisted together in a tangled mass

Examples

The wires behind the computer were so entangled that I had to unplug everything to sort them out.
She found herself entangled in the wild rose bushes while hiking through the forest.
Negotiations became entangled in a web of legal complications and delays.

Synset Examples

toiled through entangled growths of mesquite

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangled - twisted together in a disorderly mass
  • Antonym: untangled - not twisted together; freed from a tangle

Etymology

From 'entangle' + -ed, where 'entangle' is from Middle English, possibly from Old French 'entangler'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00256448-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtæŋɡəld / (AmE), / ɪnˈtæŋɡəld / (BrE)
involved in complicated or difficult situations that are hard to escape or resolve
involved in complicated difficulties

Examples

She became entangled in a web of political intrigue that was hard to escape.
Their finances were entangled after years of poor investments and risky loans.
He found himself entangled in legal disputes that dragged on for years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embroiled - involved deeply in conflict or difficulty
  • Synonym: ensnared - caught up in or trapped by something, especially difficulties
  • Antonym: free - not involved or trapped in difficulties or complications

Etymology

From 'entangle' + '-ed'; 'entangle' from Middle English, from Old French 'entangler'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01069010-s
Synset POS: s
