cinchona

1. NOUN

/ sɪnˈtʃoʊnə / (AmE), / sɪŋˈkəʊnə / (BrE)
The bark of South American trees of the genus Cinchona, used medicinally as a source of quinine and other alkaloids.
medicinal bark; source of quinine
medical

Examples

For centuries, the bark of cinchona was used to treat malaria.
Researchers extracted quinine from cinchona to develop antimalarial drugs.
The demand for cinchona bark drove exploration in South America during the colonial era.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quinine - a bitter medicinal compound from cinchona bark

Etymology

From Spanish 'cinchona', after the Countess of Chinchón, who introduced the medicinal use to Europe.

2. NOUN

/ sɪnˈtʃoʊnə / (AmE), / sɪŋˈkəʊnə / (BrE)
Any of several South American trees of the genus Cinchona, whose bark is used to produce quinine and other medicinal alkaloids.
tree genus; source of quinine

Examples

The bark of the cinchona tree is famous for its use in treating malaria.
Early explorers in South America collected cinchona bark to export to Europe.
Quinine, derived from cinchona, revolutionized the treatment of tropical diseases.

Etymology

From New Latin Cinchona, after the Countess of Chinchón (d. 1639), who popularized its medicinal use in Europe.
