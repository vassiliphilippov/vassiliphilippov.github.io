Tetrao urogallus

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛtri.oʊ juˈrɒɡələs / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrɪəʊ juːˈrɒɡələs / (BrE)
a large black grouse native to Europe and parts of Asia, known as the Western Capercaillie
a large Old World grouse (Western Capercaillie)

Examples

The Tetrao urogallus inhabits the dense forests of northern Europe.
Researchers track the Tetrao urogallus to study its breeding behavior and habitat preference.
The display call of the male Tetrao urogallus is a distinctive sound in spring woodlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capercaillie - large woodland grouse of Europe (tetrao urogallus)

Etymology

From Latin: 'Tetrao' (grouse) + 'urogallus' (aurochs-cock, reference to its large size and shape).
