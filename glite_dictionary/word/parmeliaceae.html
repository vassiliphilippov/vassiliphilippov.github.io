Parmeliaceae

1. NOUN

/ pɑːrˌmiːliˈeɪsiˌiː / (AmE), / pɑːˌmɛlɪˈeɪsiːiː / (BrE)
a large family of lichen-forming fungi, including many common lichens found worldwide
family of lichen-forming fungi

Examples

The Parmeliaceae are known for their diverse and widespread lichen species.
Many woodland trees are home to members of the Parmeliaceae family.
Researchers are studying the Parmeliaceae to better understand their role in forest ecosystems.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Parmelia + family suffix -aceae.
