schtick

1. NOUN

/ ʃtɪk / (AmE), / ʃtɪk / (BrE)
(informal, sometimes humorous) A devious trick or bit of cheating, especially as a ruse or scheme.
devious trick; bit of cheating
informal

Examples

He pulled a clever schtick to win the game, but the referee noticed.
The investor was wary of any schtick that sounded too good to be true.
I can't believe you fell for that old schtick again!

Synset Examples

how did you ever fall for a shtik like that?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trick - an action intended to deceive
  • Synonym: ruse - a clever trick or strategy used to deceive
  • Antonym: honesty - fairness; lack of deceit

Etymology

From Yiddish shtik (piece, trick), from German Stück (piece).

2. NOUN

/ ʃtɪk / (AmE), / ʃtɪk / (BrE)
a characteristic routine, gimmick, or piece of business that a performer habitually uses to attract attention or amuse an audience
performer's habitual comic routine or gimmick

Examples

His comic schtick always involves pretending to forget people's names.
The magician's latest schtick had the audience in stitches.
After years on television, her schtick has become widely recognized.

Synset Examples

play it straight with no shtik

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gimmick - an ingenious or novel device, scheme, or stratagem, especially one designed to attract attention or increase appeal
  • Synonym: routine - a regular or repeated performance, often by an entertainer

Etymology

From Yiddish *shtik* (piece, bit), ultimately from German *Stück* (piece).

3. NOUN

/ ʃtɪk / (AmE), / ʃtɪk / (BrE)
a joke, stunt, or comic routine, especially one characteristic of a particular performer or style
comic routine or stunt

Examples

The comedian's signature schtick never fails to get big laughs from the audience.
Her presentation was so full of schtick that it felt more like a stand-up act than a regular talk.
He always pulls the same schtick at parties, pretending to be a famous movie star.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gag - a joke or an amusing story, especially one forming part of a comedian's act
  • Synonym: routine - a set sequence in a performance, especially comical or characteristic material

Etymology

From Yiddish *shtik* (piece, trick), from German *Stück* (piece).
