unshaded

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈʃeɪdɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈʃeɪdɪd / (BrE)
(of a drawing or picture) lacking areas of shadow or shading; not depicted with shadow.
not having shading or shadows (esp. in drawings)

Examples

The artist preferred using unshaded lines to create a clean, minimalist effect.
This comic strip is drawn in a completely unshaded style, giving it a bold appearance.
Children often create unshaded sketches before learning how to draw shadows and depth.

Synset Examples

unshaded drawings resembling cartoons

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shaded - (of a picture) with shadowed or shaded areas
  • Synonym: shadowless - lacking or free from shadows

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' meaning 'not' and 'shaded', past participle of the verb 'shade'.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈʃeɪdɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈʃeɪdɪd / (BrE)
not protected from sun or light by shade; exposed to direct sunlight or brightness.
not covered or dimmed by shade

Examples

The flowers thrive best in an unshaded location with plenty of sunlight.
We set up our picnic in an unshaded part of the park and regretted it on such a hot day.
The artist prefers to work in an unshaded studio, filled with natural light.

Synset Examples

an unshaded meadow
a bright and unshaded lane

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shaded - protected from sunlight by shade

Etymology

Prefix 'un-' meaning 'not' + 'shaded', from 'shade'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00278049-a
Synset POS: a
