Eunectes

1. NOUN

/ juˈnɛkˌtiːz / (AmE), / juːˈnɛktiːz / (BrE)
A genus of large aquatic snakes found in South America, commonly known as anacondas.
genus of aquatic snakes (anacondas)

Examples

The Eunectes genus includes some of the largest snakes in the world.
Researchers studied the habitat of Eunectes in the Amazon basin.
Young Eunectes are born live rather than hatching from eggs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anaconda - a large, semi-aquatic snake found in South America

Etymology

From Greek *eu* (good, well) and *nēktēs* (swimmer).

2. NOUN

/ juˈnɛktiːz / (AmE), / juːˈnɛktiːz / (BrE)
A genus of large aquatic snakes native to South America, commonly known as anacondas.
genus of anacondas (large aquatic snakes)

Examples

The Eunectes genus includes the largest snake species in the world, such as the green anaconda.
Herpetologists are studying the behavior of Eunectes in the Amazon Basin.
The distinctive adaptations of Eunectes allow these snakes to thrive in aquatic environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anaconda - Large aquatic snake found in South America, member of the genus Eunectes

Etymology

From Greek 'eu-' (good) + 'nēktēs' (swimmer).
