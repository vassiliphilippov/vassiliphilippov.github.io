dolefully

1. ADV

/ ˈdoʊl.fə.li / (AmE), / ˈdəʊl.fəli / (BrE)
In a way that expresses sorrow or sadness; in a mournful manner.
in a sorrowful or mournful way

Examples

She gazed dolefully out of the window as the rain continued to fall.
He dolefully recounted the story of his lost opportunity.
The dog whimpered dolefully after being left alone in the house.

Synset Examples

his mother looked at him dolefully when he told her he had joined the Army

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mournfully - in a way that expresses sorrow or grief
  • Antonym: cheerfully - in a happy or optimistic way

Etymology

From Middle English *doleful*, from Old French *dol*, from Latin *dolor* (pain, grief) + English adverbial suffix -ly.
