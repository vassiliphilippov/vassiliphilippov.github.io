nor'-east

1. NOUN

/ nɔrˈist / (AmE), / nɔːˈiːst / (BrE)
the compass point lying halfway between north and east, at an angle of 45 degrees
compass point between north and east

Examples

The storm was approaching from the nor'-east, bringing heavy winds.
Sailors adjusted their course to head nor'-east toward the next port.
A cold breeze blew steadily from the nor'-east all morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: north-east - the direction at 45 degrees between north and east
  • Degree Lower: east - the direction toward which the sun rises, at 90 degrees
  • Degree Lower: north - the direction toward the North Pole, at 0 or 360 degrees

Etymology

Contraction of 'north-east', often used in older nautical contexts.

2. ADV

/ nɔrˈist / (AmE), / nɔːˈiːst / (BrE)
To, toward, or in the direction of the northeast; used especially in literary or nautical contexts as a variant of 'northeast'.
toward the northeast (literary/nautical variant)
archaic

Examples

The storm was blowing steadily nor'-east across the channel.
Our ship sailed nor'-east through rough and icy waters.
He glanced nor'-east, searching the sky for a break in the clouds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northeast - to, toward, or in the northeast direction

Etymology

Contraction of 'north' and 'east', with the 'th' elided for euphony, commonly used in older nautical and literary English.
