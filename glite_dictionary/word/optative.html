optative

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑːp.tə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈɒp.tə.tɪv / (BrE)
(of a verb or grammatical mood) expressing a wish or desire, particularly in some languages which have a specific subjunctive or optative form.
relating to a grammatical mood expressing wishes

Examples

In Ancient Greek, the optative mood is used to express wishes or potentiality.
The linguistics professor asked the students to identify the optative forms in the text.
Some languages, such as Sanskrit, have a distinct optative construction for conveying hopes or desires.

optative verb endings

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːp.tə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈɒp.tə.tɪv / (BrE)
(in grammar) a mood, as in Greek or Sanskrit, used to express a wish or hope, often represented in English by modal verbs such as 'may' or 'might'.
grammatical mood expressing wish or hope

Examples

In Ancient Greek, the optative mood was often used to express hopes or potential actions.
Sanskrit makes use of the optative to convey wishes and possibilities.
English does not have a true optative mood, but uses verbs like 'may' or 'might' to express similar meanings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subjunctive - grammatical mood expressing hypothetical or non-real actions or states

Etymology

From Latin optātīvus (relating to wishing), from optāre (to wish).

3. ADJ

/ ˈɑp.tə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈɒp.tə.tɪv / (BrE)
(of a mood in grammar) expressing a wish or a possibility, rather than a statement of fact or command.
expressing a wish; grammatical mood

Examples

In Ancient Greek, the optative mood is used to express wishes or potential actions.
Some grammar books include a detailed discussion of the optative forms in classical languages.
The optative mood has largely disappeared from modern English, surviving only in certain archaic expressions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: imperative - expressing a command; grammatical mood
  • Antonym: indicative - expressing a factual statement or question; grammatical mood
  • Synonym: subjunctive - expressing doubt, possibility, or necessity; grammatical mood

Etymology

From Latin 'optativus' (pertaining to wishing), from 'optatus', past participle of 'optare' (to wish).
