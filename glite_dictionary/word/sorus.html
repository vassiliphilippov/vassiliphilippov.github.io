sorus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / ˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
a spore-producing structure found in certain lichens and fungi
spore-producing structure in lichens/fungi

Examples

The presence of a sorus is a key feature in identifying some fungal species.
Each sorus releases spores when environmental conditions are favorable.
Researchers studied the development of the sorus in various types of lichens.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek sōros (heap, pile).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɔr.əs / (AmE), / ˈsɔː.rəs / (BrE)
A cluster of sporangia, usually found on the underside of a fern frond.
cluster of sporangia on a fern frond

Examples

The underside of the fern was covered in rows of sori, each packed with tiny spores.
A sorus can appear as a small brown spot on the back of a mature fern leaf.
Botanists used a magnifying glass to study the structure of each sorus on the frond.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sporangium - a structure in which spores are produced in fungi, algae, and plants

Etymology

From Latin sorus, from Ancient Greek sōrós (heap, pile).
