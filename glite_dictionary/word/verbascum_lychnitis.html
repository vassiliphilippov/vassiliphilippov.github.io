Verbascum lychnitis

1. NOUN

/ vərˈbæskəm lɪkˈnaɪtɪs / (AmE), / vəˈbaskəm lɪkˈnaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A Eurasian plant species with densely hairy leaves and racemes of white flowers, naturalized in North America.
Eurasian plant with white flowers

Examples

The roadside was lined with tall Verbascum lychnitis in full bloom.
Gardeners appreciate Verbascum lychnitis for its striking white flower spikes.
Verbascum lychnitis has become naturalized in parts of North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white mullein - Common name for Verbascum lychnitis, a Eurasian plant with white flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'Verbascum' (the genus name) and 'lychnitis', an ancient name for a species of mullein.
