pyroelectric

1. ADJ

/ ˌpaɪroʊɪˈlɛktrɪk / (AmE), / ˌpaɪərəʊɪˈlɛktrɪk / (BrE)
(of a material) exhibiting or relating to pyroelectricity, the ability to generate an electric charge in response to temperature changes.
exhibiting or relating to pyroelectricity

Examples

Tourmaline crystals are known for their pyroelectric properties.
The experiment demonstrated the pyroelectric response of the sample to heating and cooling cycles.
Scientists study pyroelectric materials for potential use in infrared sensors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piezoelectric - (of a material) generating electricity when subjected to pressure

Etymology

From pyro- (relating to fire or heat) + electric.
