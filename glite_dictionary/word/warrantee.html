warrantee

1. NOUN

/ ˌwɔːrənˈtiː / (AmE), / ˌwɒrənˈtiː / (BrE)
a person or organization to whom a warranty or guarantee is given, especially regarding goods or services
person receiving a warranty or guarantee
legal

Examples

The warrantee is entitled to free repairs during the coverage period.
Upon buying the appliance, she became the warrantee under the manufacturer's guarantee.
A warrantee can claim compensation if the product fails due to a manufacturing defect.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: warrantor - the person or entity that gives a warranty or guarantee

Etymology

From 'warrant' (guarantee), patterned after other agent nouns like 'grantee'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌwɔːrənˈti / (AmE), / ˌwɒrənˈti / (BrE)
a person who is the recipient of a warrant issued by a court, especially in the United States
recipient of a court warrant (US legal term)
legal american

Examples

The warrantee must appear in court on the assigned date to address the charges.
When the police locate the warrantee, they must serve the legal document in person.
After the judge signed the warrant, the warrantee was notified by law enforcement.

Etymology

Formed on the model of 'warrantor' from 'warrant' + '-ee' (person to whom something is given).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10787940-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌwɔːrənˈtiː / (AmE), / ˌwɒrənˈtiː / (BrE)
a person or party to whom a warranty is made or who is entitled to the benefits of a warranty
person receiving a warranty

Examples

The warrantee must retain the proof of purchase to claim repairs under the agreement.
If the warrantee discovers a defect, the company will provide a replacement free of charge.
Under the contract, the warrantee is protected against manufacturing faults for two years.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: warrantor - person or party who makes or gives a warranty

Etymology

Formed from 'warrant' plus the suffix '-ee', denoting the recipient of the action.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06698201-n
Synset POS: n
