guru

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʊru / (AmE), / ˈɡʊruː / (BrE)
an influential and respected expert or leader in a particular field or movement
influential expert or leader

Examples

She is a guru in the world of sustainable fashion.
Many young entrepreneurs look up to him as a business guru.
After decades of research, she became the leading guru of climate science.

Synset Examples

a guru of genomics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expert - a person with special skill or knowledge in a field
  • Antonym: novice - a person new to a field or activity

Etymology

From Sanskrit guru (weighty, venerable, teacher), adopted into English via Hindi.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡʊru / (AmE), / ˈɡʊruː / (BrE)
A Hindu or Buddhist religious leader and spiritual teacher, often regarded as a guide in spiritual matters.
Hindu/Buddhist spiritual teacher

Examples

He traveled to India to study under a renowned guru of meditation.
The guru gave daily teachings to his devoted followers at the temple.
She sought guidance from her guru on how to overcome life's challenges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teacher - a person who helps others to acquire knowledge or skills

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'guru' (heavy, weighty, venerable; teacher).

Synset POS: n
