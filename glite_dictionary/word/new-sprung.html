new-sprung

1. ADJ

/ ˈnuːˌsprʌŋ / (AmE), / ˈnjuːˌsprʌŋ / (BrE)
Having just appeared, arisen, or come into existence; newly formed or created.
just arisen or newly created

Examples

The new-sprung flowers brightened the entire garden after the rain.
A sense of hope was new-sprung in the community following the announcement.
Many new-sprung ideas are initially met with skepticism before gaining acceptance.

Synset Examples

with newborn fears
new-sprung nations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recent - having happened or started only a short time ago
  • Synonym: newborn - recently born or created
  • Antonym: old - having existed for a long time

Etymology

Formed from 'new' + past participle of 'spring', from Old English 'springan'.
