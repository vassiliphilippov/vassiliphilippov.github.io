trillionth

1. NOUN

/ trɪl.jənθ / (AmE), / trɪl.jənθ / (BrE)
One part in a trillion equal parts.
one part in a trillion

Examples

One trillionth of a second is known as a picosecond in scientific terms.
The concentration of the substance was less than one trillionth of a gram per liter.
Even a trillionth of a difference can be significant in precise scientific measurements.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: billionth - one part in a billion equal parts
  • Degree Higher: quadrillionth - one part in a quadrillion equal parts

Etymology

Formed from 'trillion' and the suffix '-th', indicating an ordinal or fractional part.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtrɪljənθ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪljənθ / (BrE)
The ordinal number matching the quantity one trillion; being number one trillion in a sequence.
the ordinal number of one trillion

Examples

The scientist referenced the trillionth digit of pi in her research.
He finished a close trillionth in the online contest, far behind the leaders.
This data is accurate to the trillionth place, which is impressive for any measurement.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: billionth - the ordinal number of one billion
  • Degree Higher: quadrillionth - the ordinal number of one quadrillion
  • Synonym: trillion - the cardinal number equal to one thousand billion (1,000,000,000,000)

Etymology

Formed from "trillion" + "-th" suffix denoting order.
