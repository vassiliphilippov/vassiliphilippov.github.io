Lobelia inflata

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈbiliə ɪnˈfleɪtə / (AmE), / ləʊˈbiːliə ɪnˈfleɪtə / (BrE)
A North American plant species with small blue flowers and inflated seed capsules, formerly used in herbal medicine as an antispasmodic.
species of wild lobelia with blue flowers
medical

Examples

Native Americans historically used Lobelia inflata for medicinal purposes.
The blue flowers of Lobelia inflata bloom in late summer in woodland areas.
Researchers are studying the compounds found in Lobelia inflata for potential therapeutic use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue lobelia - another lobelia species with blue flowers
  • Degree Lower: Lobelia siphilitica - a related North American lobelia species

Etymology

Genus name from Matthias de l’Obel, a Flemish botanist; species from Latin 'inflata', meaning 'inflated'.
