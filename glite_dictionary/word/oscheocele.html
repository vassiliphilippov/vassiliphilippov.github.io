oscheocele

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑskiəˌsiːl / (AmE), / ˈɒskiəˌsiːl / (BrE)
A swelling of the scrotum, typically due to the accumulation of fluid or other causes.
swelling of the scrotum
medical

Examples

The patient presented with an oscheocele that required further examination.
Treatment of an oscheocele depends on the underlying cause of the swelling.
In rare cases, an oscheocele may be mistaken for other types of scrotal mass.

Etymology

From Greek *oskhos* (scrotum) + *kēlē* (tumor, hernia).
