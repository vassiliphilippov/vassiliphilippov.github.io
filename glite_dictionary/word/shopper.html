shopper

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɑːpər / (AmE), / ˈʃɒpə / (BrE)
a person who visits stores to look for and buy goods
person who shops

Examples

The mall was crowded with weekend shoppers looking for good deals.
As a regular shopper, she always knows where to find the best prices.
Many shoppers prefer to compare products online before making a purchase in store.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: customer - a person who buys goods or services from a store or business

Etymology

From 'shop' (store) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɑpər / (AmE), / ˈʃɒpə / (BrE)
a person employed by a store to shop at competitors' stores to compare prices and merchandise
person comparing competitors' prices for a store

Examples

The shopper visited several rival stores to note differences in product displays and pricing.
A skilled shopper can help a retail chain stay competitive by reporting on trends at other stores.
Many companies hire a shopper to secretly gather detailed information from their competitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comparison shopper - person who shops at various stores to compare prices and quality (general sense)
