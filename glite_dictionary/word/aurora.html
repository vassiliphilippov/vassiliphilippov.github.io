aurora

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɔrə / (AmE), / ɔːˈrɔːrə / (BrE)
A natural light display in the Earth's sky, typically seen in high-latitude regions, caused by charged particles from the sun interacting with the Earth's magnetic field.
natural light display in the sky

Examples

The vivid aurora lit up the night sky over Norway.
Scientists studied the aurora to better understand Earth's magnetic field.
Tourists traveled north hoping to catch a glimpse of the spectacular aurora.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aurora borealis - the northern lights; aurora visible in the northern hemisphere
  • Synonym: aurora australis - the southern lights; aurora visible in the southern hemisphere

Etymology

From Latin 'aurora', meaning 'dawn'.

2. NOUN

/ əˈrɔːrə / (AmE), / ɔːˈrɔːrə / (BrE)
the first light of day; dawn or daybreak, especially in poetic or literary use
the first light of day; dawn

Examples

They made camp just before the aurora crept into the eastern sky.
Poets often write about the beauty of the aurora brushing the horizon.
We set off on our hike at aurora to enjoy the cool morning air.

Synset Examples

we got up before dawn
they talked until morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dawn - the time each morning at which daylight first appears
  • Synonym: sunrise - the time in the morning when the sun appears above the horizon
  • Antonym: dusk - the darker stage of twilight, especially at sunset

Etymology

From Latin *aurora* (dawn, sunrise).
