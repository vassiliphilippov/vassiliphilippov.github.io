Aurora

1. PROPN

/ əˈrɔrə / (AmE), / ɔːˈrɔːrə / (BrE)
(in Roman mythology) the goddess of the dawn, equivalent to the Greek Eos.
Roman goddess of the dawn

Examples

In Roman myth, Aurora rises each morning to renew the daylight.
The painter depicted Aurora in brilliant robes of pink and gold.
Many poets have invoked Aurora as a symbol of new beginnings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eos - Greek goddess of the dawn, counterpart of Aurora

Etymology

From Latin Aurora (dawn).
