to vivify

1. VERB

/ ˈvɪvəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈvɪvɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to give life, energy, or animation to; to enliven or make more vivid
make lively or animate

Examples

The artist's use of color helped to vivify the otherwise plain landscape.
A splash of lemon can vivify a simple salad, giving it a fresh, vibrant taste.
Her laughter seemed to vivify the entire room, lifting everyone's spirits.

Synset Examples

A hot soup will revive me
This will renovate my spirits
This treatment repaired my health

Related Terms

  • Synonym: animate - to bring to life or make lively
  • Synonym: to revive - to give new life or energy to; restore to life
  • Synonym: to invigorate - to give strength or energy to

Etymology

From Latin 'vivificare', from 'vivus' (alive) + 'facere' (to make).

2. VERB

/ ˈvɪvəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈvɪvɪfʌɪ / (BrE)
to make something more lively, animated, or striking; to enliven
make more lively or animated

Examples

The playwright used music and dance to vivify the performance, making it truly unforgettable.
Fresh paint and bright colors can vivify an old, neglected neighborhood.
Her enthusiasm and passion vivified the entire team, inspiring them to work harder.

Synset Examples

his remarks always vivify an otherwise dull story

Related Terms

  • Synonym: animate - give life or spirit to; make lively
  • Synonym: to enliven - make more lively, interesting, or cheerful
  • Antonym: dull - make or become less lively or interesting

Etymology

From Latin 'vivificare' (to make alive), from 'vivus' (alive).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00583222-v
Synset POS: v
