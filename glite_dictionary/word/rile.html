to rile

1. VERB

/ raɪl / (AmE), / raɪl / (BrE)
to make someone irritated or annoyed, especially over minor matters
to annoy or irritate

Examples

His constant interruptions really rile me during meetings.
It doesn't take much to rile her—just a minor inconvenience will do.
The unfair decision riled the entire team, sparking a heated debate.

Synset Examples

Mosquitoes buzzing in my ear really bothers me
It irritates me that she never closes the door after she leaves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to irritate - to make someone annoyed or a little angry
  • Synonym: to annoy - to make someone slightly angry or irritated
  • Antonym: please - to make someone happy or satisfied

Etymology

Possible alteration of 'roil', which comes from French 'rouiller' (to rust).

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ raɪl / (AmE), / raɪl / (BrE)
to make water or another liquid muddy or turbid by stirring up sediment
make turbid by stirring up sediment

Examples

Heavy rains can rile the river, making it unsafe to drink from.
The boat's propeller riled the pond, disturbing fish and plants.
Be careful not to rile the water while collecting samples for testing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to roil - to make a liquid turbid by stirring up sediment

Etymology

Variant of 'roil', probably from Old French 'rouiller' (to rust, make muddy).
