interlacing

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪntərˈleɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈleɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
linked or woven closely together so as to form a connected whole or pattern
closely linked or woven together

Examples

The interlacing branches formed a natural canopy over the path.
The artist used an interlacing pattern to decorate the border of the manuscript.
Ancient Celtic designs are known for their complex interlacing motifs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interwoven - closely woven or blended together
  • Synonym: entangled - twisted together in a confused mass

Etymology

From the verb 'interlace,' from Old French entrelacier, from entre- (between) + lacer (to lace).
