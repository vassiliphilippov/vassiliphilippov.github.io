Eugenia aromaticum

1. NOUN

/ juːˈdʒiːniə æˌrəˈmætɪkəm / (AmE), / juːˈdʒiːniə əˌrəʊˈmætɪkəm / (BrE)
A tropical evergreen tree with red flowers, cultivated for its flower buds which are dried to produce cloves.
tree cultivated for cloves

Examples

Eugenia aromaticum is widely grown in Indonesia and Sri Lanka for its aromatic flower buds.
The dried flower buds of Eugenia aromaticum are harvested to produce cloves used in cooking.
Botanists classify the clove tree as Eugenia aromaticum in older texts, though it is now often called Syzygium aromaticum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clove - the aromatic dried flower bud used as a spice

Etymology

From genus name Eugenia, named after Prince Eugene of Savoy, and Latin aromaticum meaning 'aromatic'.
