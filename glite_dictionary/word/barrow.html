barrow

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛroʊ / (AmE), / ˈbærəʊ / (BrE)
The amount or quantity that can be carried in a single barrow.
quantity carried in a barrow

Examples

He carried a barrow of soil to the edge of the garden.
The workers moved a barrow of bricks to the construction site.
The price was set per barrow of potatoes sold at the market.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈbærəʊ / (BrE)
A large mound of earth or stones placed over a prehistoric tomb; a burial mound.
prehistoric burial mound

Examples

The archaeologists uncovered several ancient barrows in the English countryside.
A Bronze Age barrow can be seen on top of the hill near the village.
Legends often surround the mysterious barrow mounds found across northern Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tumuli - an ancient burial mound; plural of tumulus
  • Synonym: cairn - a mound of stones built as a memorial or landmark, sometimes covering a burial

Etymology

Old English *beorg* (hill, mound).

3. NOUN

/ ˈbæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈbærəʊ / (BrE)
a cart with handles and one or more wheels, used for carrying small loads, especially by hand
handcart with wheels for small loads

Examples

He loaded the sand into the barrow and wheeled it across the yard.
Vendors at the market sell fruit from a barrow on the sidewalk.
The gardener used a barrow to move soil to the flower beds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheelbarrow - a small cart with a single wheel, used for carrying loads

Etymology

From Old English *bearwe* (basket, stretcher) related to the verb 'to bear' (to carry).
