Japan

1. PROPN

/ dʒəˈpæn / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæn / (BrE)
a country in East Asia, consisting of a group of islands in the northwest Pacific Ocean, known for its constitutional monarchy, technological innovation, and manufacturing industries.
country in East Asia

Examples

Many tourists visit Japan to experience its rich culture and history.
Japan is renowned for its advances in technology and manufacturing.
After studying abroad in Japan, she became fluent in both the language and customs.

Etymology

From Portuguese Japão, from Malay Jepang, from Chinese 日本 (Rìběn, 'origin of the sun').

2. PROPN

/ dʒəˈpæn / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæn / (BrE)
an island country in East Asia, consisting of a chain of over 3,000 islands to the east of the Asian continent, between the Sea of Japan and the western Pacific Ocean
island country in East Asia

Examples

She plans to travel to Japan next summer to experience the cherry blossom season.
Japan is known for its rich culture and advanced technology.
An earthquake struck the northern region of Japan last year, causing significant damage.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'Japão', from Malay 'Jepang', from Chinese 日本 (Rìběn, "origin of the sun").
