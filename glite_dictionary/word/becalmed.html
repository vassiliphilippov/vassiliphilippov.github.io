becalmed

1. ADJ

/ bɪˈkɑːmd / (AmE), / bɪˈkɑːmd / (BrE)
unable to move due to lack of wind, especially of a sailing ship.
unable to move for lack of wind (nautical)

Examples

The sailing ship was becalmed for hours, stranded in the middle of the ocean.
With no wind in sight, the fleet remained becalmed until morning.
Many sailors dreaded being becalmed on long voyages, fearing delays and shortages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adrift - floating without control; not anchored or steered

Etymology

From 'be-' (intensive prefix) + 'calm'.
