Girondin

1. NOUN

/ ʒɪˈrɑn.dɪn / (AmE), / ʒɪˈrɒn.dɪn / (BrE)
a member of the moderate republican party in France during the French Revolution, known as the Girondists, who were eventually overthrown by the more radical Jacobins
member of moderate French Revolutionary party

Examples

The Girondins advocated for a constitutional government and opposed the execution of Louis XVI.
Many Girondin leaders were arrested and executed during the Reign of Terror.
In the early stages of the Revolution, the Girondins held significant influence in the National Convention.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Jacobin - a member of the radical revolutionary party during the French Revolution

Etymology

From French 'Girondin', named after the Gironde department in France, where many party leaders originated.
