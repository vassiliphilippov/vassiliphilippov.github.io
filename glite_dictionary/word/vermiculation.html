vermiculation

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɜrmɪkjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɜːmɪkjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a type of ornamentation consisting of carved or incised patterns resembling the tracks or forms of worms, often used in masonry or decorative arts.
wormlike carved ornamentation

Examples

The ancient stone façade is adorned with intricate vermiculation around the main doorway.
Restorers carefully cleaned the delicate vermiculation that decorated the marble columns.
Architectural historians marveled at the rare use of vermiculation in the building's cornices.

Etymology

From Latin 'vermis' (worm), referring to worm-like forms.

2. NOUN

/ vɜrmɪkjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / vəːmɪkjʊˈleɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the wavelike muscle contractions of the digestive tract that move food along
muscle contractions that move food along the gut
medical

Examples

Doctors observed vermiculation in the patient's intestines during the procedure.
Proper vermiculation is essential for effective digestion of food.
Disturbances in vermiculation can lead to digestive disorders.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Latin 'vermiculi' (little worms), referencing the worm-like motions.
