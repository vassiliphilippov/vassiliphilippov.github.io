cymatium

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈmeɪtiəm / (AmE), / sɪˈmeɪtɪəm / (BrE)
(in architecture) a molding at the top of a cornice, typically with an S-shaped profile that is partly concave and partly convex.
molding at the top of a cornice (architecture)
medical

Examples

The ornate cymatium added a final elegant touch to the grand cornice.
Restoration experts carefully replicated the classical cymatium as part of the building's facade renovation.
Students of architecture studied the profiles of the cymatium for their historical design projects.

Etymology

From Latin *cymatium*, from Greek *kymation* (little wave), referencing the molding's wave-like shape.
