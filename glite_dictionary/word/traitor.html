traitor

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈtreɪtə / (BrE)
a person who betrays the trust or allegiance of a country, group, or another person, especially by supporting an opposing side or acting in contradiction to their professed loyalty
person who betrays trust or allegiance

Examples

After selling secrets to the enemy, he was branded a traitor by his own country.
Many considered her a traitor for abandoning the cause when it mattered most.
Trust is broken forever once you are revealed as a traitor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: betrayer - a person who betrays another
  • Antonym: loyalist - a person who remains loyal to a cause or party

Etymology

From Old French 'traitour', from Latin 'traditor' (betrayer), from 'tradere' (to hand over).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈtreɪtə / (BrE)
a person who betrays their country, friends, or a cause, especially by committing treason
person who betrays a country or cause

Examples

He was branded a traitor after leaking confidential documents to a foreign government.
Many considered her a traitor for switching sides during the conflict.
The general was executed as a traitor to his country.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: patriot - a person who vigorously supports their country and is prepared to defend it
  • Synonym: betrayer - a person who betrays someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'traditor' (one who hands over, betrayer), from 'tradere' (to hand over, betray).
