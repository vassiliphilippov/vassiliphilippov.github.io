St. Mary Magdalene

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈmɛri ˌmæɡdəˈlin / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈmɛːri ˌmaɡdəˈliːn / (BrE)
a follower of Jesus who, according to the Bible, was healed by him of evil spirits and later witnessed his crucifixion and resurrection
follower of Jesus in the New Testament

Examples

Many churches are named after St. Mary Magdalene across Europe.
The painting depicts St. Mary Magdalene at the foot of the cross.
Some legends claim that St. Mary Magdalene traveled to southern France after Jesus's resurrection.

Etymology

From Mary of Magdala (a town in Galilee), as recorded in the New Testament.
