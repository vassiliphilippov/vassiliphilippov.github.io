pecs

1. NOUN

/ pɛks / (AmE), / pɛks / (BrE)
the large muscles on each side of the human chest, known in full as the pectoral muscles
chest muscles (pectorals)
informal

Examples

After months of weightlifting, his pecs became much more defined.
You need strong pecs to perform push-ups correctly.
He injured one of his pecs during the competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pectorals - the full name for the chest muscles

Etymology

Shortened from 'pectorals,' from Latin *pectoralis* (of the chest).
