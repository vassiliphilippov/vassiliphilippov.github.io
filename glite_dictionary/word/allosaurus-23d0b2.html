Allosaurus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæləˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / ˌaləˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
a genus of large carnivorous dinosaurs of the Late Jurassic period, found in North America
genus of large Jurassic carnivorous dinosaurs

Examples

Fossil bones of the Allosaurus have been discovered in North America.
The Allosaurus was one of the top predators of the Late Jurassic period.
Visitors to the museum were amazed by the size of the Allosaurus skeleton.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dinosaur - a group of extinct reptiles of the Mesozoic era

Etymology

From Greek allos (other, different) + sauros (lizard).
