drifter

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪftər / (AmE), / ˈdrɪftə / (BrE)
a person who moves from place to place without a permanent home or regular employment
person who moves from place to place

Examples

After losing his job, he became a drifter, traveling from town to town in search of work.
The novel tells the story of a drifter who finds himself caught up in a small-town mystery.
Many people viewed him as a drifter, but he embraced the freedom of his wandering lifestyle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wanderer - a person who travels aimlessly without a fixed destination
  • Synonym: vagrant - a person without a settled home or job, who wanders from place to place
  • Antonym: settler - a person who settles in a new place, especially permanently

Etymology

From 'drift' (to move aimlessly) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
