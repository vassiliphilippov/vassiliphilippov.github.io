Martes foina

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːrtiːz ˈfɔɪnə / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtiːz ˈfɔɪnə / (BrE)
a Eurasian marten species with a brown coat and pale breast and throat, also known as the beech marten
Eurasian marten species (beech marten)

Examples

The Martes foina is frequently sighted in European forests and rural areas.
Farmers sometimes regard the Martes foina as a nuisance due to its raids on poultry.
Wildlife researchers placed tracking collars on several Martes foina to study their nocturnal habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beech marten - common English name for Martes foina

Etymology

From Latin; 'Martes' is the genus name for martens, and 'foina' refers to this particular species.
