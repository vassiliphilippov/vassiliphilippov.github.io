Cocopah

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊkoʊˌpɑː / (AmE), / ˈkɒkəˌpɑː / (BrE)
The Yuman language spoken by the Cocopah people of the lower Colorado River region.
the language of the Cocopah people

Examples

Few people today still speak Cocopah as their first language.
The Cocopah language belongs to the Yuman linguistic family.
Efforts are being made to document and preserve Cocopah for future generations.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊkəˌpɑː / (AmE), / ˈkəʊkəˌpɑː / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people indigenous to the area around the mouth of the Colorado River in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.
member of a Native American group near the Colorado River

Examples

The Cocopah have maintained many aspects of their traditional culture.
He attended a cultural event hosted by the Cocopah tribe.
Several Cocopah elders gathered to share stories about their history.
