abomasal

1. ADJ

/ ˌæboʊˈmeɪsəl / (AmE), / ˌabəʊˈmeɪsəl / (BrE)
relating to the abomasum, the fourth compartment of the stomach in ruminant animals.
relating to the abomasum of ruminants
medical

Examples

The veterinarian diagnosed an abomasal ulcer in the cow after observing her symptoms.
Surgical correction of abomasal displacement is a common procedure in dairy cattle.
Researchers are studying abomasal function to understand digestive disorders in sheep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abomasum - the fourth compartment of the stomach of ruminants

Etymology

From 'abomasum' (fourth stomach of ruminants) + '-al' (adjectival suffix).
