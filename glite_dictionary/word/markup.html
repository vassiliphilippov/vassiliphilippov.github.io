markup

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrkˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkʌp / (BrE)
written instructions or symbols added to copy, indicating how the text should be typeset or formatted before printing.
typesetting instructions added to text

Examples

The editor used red ink to add markup to the author's manuscript.
She learned the traditional markup symbols used in publishing during her internship.
Before digital tools, manual markup was essential to communicate formatting needs to typesetters.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrkˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkˌʌp / (BrE)
an amount added to the cost price of goods to cover overhead and profit when setting a selling price
amount added to cost price for profit

Examples

The store adds a markup of 20% to cover expenses and ensure a profit.
Many retailers use a standard markup when pricing new products.
The markup on imported goods can be surprisingly high.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profit margin - the difference between the cost and the selling price; often expressed as a percentage
  • Antonym: markdown - a reduction in the selling price of goods

Etymology

From the phrase 'mark up', meaning to increase a price, first recorded in early 20th-century American English.
