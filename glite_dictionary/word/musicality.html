musicality

1. NOUN

/ ˌmjuːzɪˈkæləti / (AmE), / ˌmjuːzɪˈkæləti / (BrE)
the quality or property of having a musical sound or character
quality of sounding like music

Examples

The musicality of her speech captivated the entire audience.
Many dancers are praised for their musicality as well as their technique.
The musicality of the language made it pleasant to listen to, even without understanding the words.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tunefulness - the quality of being tuneful or melodious
  • Antonym: cacophony - a harsh, discordant mixture of sounds

Etymology

From 'musical' + '-ity', patterned after similar abstract nouns.
