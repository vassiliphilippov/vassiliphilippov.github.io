iceboat

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪsˌboʊt / (AmE), / ˈaɪs.bəʊt / (BrE)
a boat or ship with a reinforced bow designed for breaking through ice-covered waters and keeping navigation channels open
ship or boat for breaking ice

Examples

The iceboat cleared a path through the frozen harbor, allowing other vessels to follow safely.
During harsh winters, an iceboat is essential for keeping trade routes accessible in the Arctic.
The crew prepared the iceboat for its journey across the icy river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: icebreaker - a ship specifically designed to break through ice

Etymology

Formed from 'ice' + 'boat'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪsˌboʊt / (AmE), / ˈaɪsˌbəʊt / (BrE)
A sailing vessel equipped with runners and a cross-shaped frame, designed for traveling over ice.
sailing vessel for ice travel

Examples

He built a homemade iceboat to glide across the frozen lake.
The annual iceboat races attract enthusiasts from all over the region.
After the storm, the iceboat needed repairs to its sails and runners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ice yacht - a larger, more elaborate iceboat used mainly for racing

Etymology

Formed from 'ice' + 'boat'.
