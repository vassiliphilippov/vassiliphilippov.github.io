Arcadic

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈkeɪdɪk / (AmE), / ɑːˈkeɪdɪk / (BrE)
the Ancient Greek dialect spoken by the Arcadians in the region of Arcadia
Ancient Greek dialect of Arcadia

Examples

The inscription was written in the Arcadic dialect, distinguishing it from other Greek texts.
Linguists have studied Arcadic to understand its relationship to other ancient Greek dialects.
Several ancient manuscripts in Arcadic have survived, providing insights into the language of Arcadia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arcadian - relating to Arcadia or its people, language, or dialect

Etymology

From Latin 'Arcadicus', from Greek 'Arkadikos' (of Arcadia).
