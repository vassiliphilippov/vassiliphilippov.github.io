hygienics

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdʒiːnɪks / (AmE), / haɪˈdʒiːnɪks / (BrE)
the science concerned with the prevention of illness and the maintenance of health, especially through cleanliness and sanitary practices
science of health maintenance and disease prevention
medical

Examples

She studied hygienics to better understand how to prevent the spread of infectious diseases.
Modern hygienics emphasizes the importance of clean water and proper waste disposal.
Public health initiatives often draw on principles established in the field of hygienics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hygiene - the practice of keeping oneself and one's surroundings clean to prevent illness

Etymology

From hygiene + -ics, patterned after similar terms in scientific naming.
