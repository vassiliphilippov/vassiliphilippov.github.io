unavowed

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnəˈvaʊd / (AmE), / ˌʌnəˈvaʊd / (BrE)
not openly acknowledged or expressed; not admitted publicly
not openly admitted or expressed

Examples

He harbored an unavowed resentment toward his former colleague.
There was an unavowed reason for her reluctance to attend the gathering.
His unavowed ambitions influenced many of his decisions.

Synset Examples

a sneaking suspicion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secret - kept hidden or not known
  • Antonym: open - expressed or revealed openly

Etymology

From un- (not) + avowed (from vow, meaning to declare).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnəˈvaʊd / (AmE), / ˌʌnəˈvaʊd / (BrE)
not openly acknowledged, declared, or made known; secret or concealed
not openly acknowledged or declared

Examples

He had an unavowed interest in her activities, which he never confessed to anyone.
There was an unavowed agreement between the two companies to avoid direct competition.
Many of his actions were guided by unavowed motives known only to himself.

Synset Examples

a secret marriage
a secret bride

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secret - kept hidden or not revealed to others
  • Antonym: avowed - openly declared or acknowledged

Etymology

From un- (not) + avowed, from Old French avouer, from Latin advocare (to call to, vouch for).

3. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈvaʊd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈvaʊd / (BrE)
not openly admitted, declared, or acknowledged; not avowed.
not openly admitted or declared

Examples

She harbored an unavowed resentment toward her colleague, never expressing it openly.
The politician's support for the policy was criticized as motivated by unavowed interests.
There was an unavowed agreement between the companies to avoid public confrontation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: avowed - openly admitted or declared

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'avowed' (affirmed, declared), from Old French *avouer*, from Latin *advocare* (to call to, invoke).
