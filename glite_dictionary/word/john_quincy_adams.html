John Quincy Adams

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn ˈkwɪnzi ˈædəmz / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈkwɪnzi ˈædəmz / (BrE)
(1767–1848) The 6th President of the United States, serving from 1825 to 1829; son of John Adams.
6th U.S. President (1825–1829)

Examples

John Quincy Adams was elected president in a controversial House decision after no candidate secured a majority in the electoral college.
As Secretary of State, John Quincy Adams was instrumental in formulating the Monroe Doctrine.
Many historians credit John Quincy Adams for his strong anti-slavery stance during his later congressional career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: John Adams - 2nd President of the United States and father of John Quincy Adams
