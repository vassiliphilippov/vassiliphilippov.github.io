vinegarweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪnəɡərˌwid / (AmE), / ˈvɪnɪɡəˌwiːd / (BrE)
an aromatic plant native to the western United States, known for its strong smell resembling vinegar
aromatic plant of western U.S.

Examples

The ranch was dotted with patches of vinegarweed, releasing a distinctive scent in the summer heat.
Hikers often recognize vinegarweed by its pungent aroma along California trails.
Some gardeners avoid vinegarweed because of its strong smell, despite its attractive flowers.
