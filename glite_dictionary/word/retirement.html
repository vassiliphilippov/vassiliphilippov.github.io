retirement

1. NOUN

/ rəˈtaɪərmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈtaɪəmənt / (BrE)
The act of leaving one's job or career permanently, usually because of age or after a long period of service.
leaving a job permanently, often due to age

Examples

After thirty years of service, David looked forward to his retirement and traveling the world.
Many people start saving early to ensure a comfortable retirement.
Jenny threw a party to celebrate her father's retirement from teaching.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resignation - the act of formally giving up a job or position
  • Antonym: employment - the state of having paid work

Etymology

From French *retraite*, from retirer (to withdraw).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈtaɪərmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈtaɪəmənt / (BrE)
The period of one's life after leaving one's job and ceasing to work, usually because of age.
the period after you stop working

Examples

After forty years as a teacher, he looked forward to retirement.
Many people travel abroad in their retirement.
Her retirement party was attended by all her colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: work - activity involving mental or physical effort for employment

Etymology

From French *retraite* (retreat) and English *retire* (to withdraw).
