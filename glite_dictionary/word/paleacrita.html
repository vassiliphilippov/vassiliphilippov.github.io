Paleacrita

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪliˈækrɪtə / (AmE), / ˌpeɪliˈækrɪtə / (BrE)
A genus of moths in the family Geometridae, known for species such as the spring cankerworm.
genus of geometrid moths

Examples

The Paleacrita moth is best known for its larva, the spring cankerworm.
Researchers observed several Paleacrita along the edges of the forest clearing.
The Paleacrita genus includes species found throughout North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geometrid - any moth of the family Geometridae

Etymology

From New Latin, used in zoological taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪliˈækrɪtə / (AmE), / ˌpeɪliˈækrɪtə / (BrE)
A genus of geometrid moths found in North America, notable for species such as the spring cankerworm moth.
a genus of geometrid moths

Examples

The Paleacrita genus includes species commonly known as cankerworm moths.
Entomologists have studied the life cycle of Paleacrita for many years.
One of the most recognized species in the Paleacrita genus is Paleacrita vernata, or the spring cankerworm.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Geometridae - the family of moths to which Paleacrita belongs
