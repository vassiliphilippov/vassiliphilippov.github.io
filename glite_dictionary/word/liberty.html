liberty

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪbɚti / (AmE), / ˈlɪbəti / (BrE)
a short period of authorized leave granted to a sailor or naval officer
naval/military authorized leave

Examples

The crew was given liberty after the ship docked in port.
He spent his liberty exploring the seaside town with his friends.
Sailors are expected to return to the ship promptly after liberty ends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave - permission to be away from one's military post or duties

Etymology

From Latin 'libertas' (freedom), adopted for military sense in the 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪbɚti / (AmE), / ˈlɪbəti / (BrE)
an act that is too familiar or shows disrespectful intimacy, especially when it oversteps social or personal boundaries
act of undue intimacy or familiarity

Examples

He was offended when a stranger took the liberty of addressing him by his first name.
You have no right to take such liberties with someone you barely know.
She warned him not to take any liberties during their conversation.

3. NOUN

/ ˈlɪbɚti / (AmE), / ˈlɪbəti / (BrE)
The state of being free within society from oppressive restrictions imposed by authority on one's way of life, behavior, or political views.
freedom from oppressive authority

Examples

The constitution guarantees every citizen the liberty to express their opinions without fear of repression.
After years under colonial rule, the country finally achieved full liberty.
Personal liberty is a fundamental principle in democratic societies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freedom - the power to act, speak, or think freely
  • Antonym: oppression - unjust or cruel exercise of authority or power

Etymology

From Latin 'libertas', from 'liber' meaning 'free'.

5. NOUN

/ ˈlɪbərti / (AmE), / ˈlɪbəti / (BrE)
the state of being free to act, speak, or think as one chooses without unnecessary restriction
freedom to act, speak, or choose

Examples

Many people have fought and died to protect their liberty.
He gave his employees the liberty to work from home whenever necessary.
The constitution guarantees every citizen the liberty of free speech.

Synset Examples

liberty of worship

at liberty to choose whatever occupation one wishes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freedom - the state of not being imprisoned or enslaved; ability to act, speak, or think freely
  • Antonym: restraint - a measure or condition that keeps someone or something under control

From Latin libertas (freedom), from liber (free).
