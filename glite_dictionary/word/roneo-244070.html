to roneo

1. VERB

/ ˈroʊniˌoʊ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊniːəʊ / (BrE)
to make copies of a document using a Roneograph (a duplicating machine); to duplicate by stencil process
make copies with a Roneograph

Examples

She used to roneo all the invitations for the school events.
Before photocopiers became common, teachers would roneo worksheets for their classes.
He roneoed the newsletter so everyone in the club could have a copy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duplicate - to make an exact copy of something
  • Degree Higher: photocopy - to make a photographic copy of a document

Etymology

From Roneograph, a trademark for an early duplicating machine invented in the late 19th century.
