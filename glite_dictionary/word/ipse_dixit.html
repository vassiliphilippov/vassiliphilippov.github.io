ipse dixit

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪp.seɪ ˈdɪk.sɪt / (AmE), / ˌɪp.seɪ ˈdɪk.sɪt / (BrE)
A statement asserted but not proved, especially one that is dogmatic and unsupported by evidence.
unsupported dogmatic assertion

Examples

The lawyer objected to the argument, calling it an ipse dixit without factual support.
Relying solely on ipse dixit is not acceptable in scientific debates.
Her claim amounted to an ipse dixit, offering no evidence for others to examine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assertion - a confident and forceful statement of fact or belief
  • Synonym: dogma - a principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true

Etymology

From Latin, literally 'he himself said it,' reportedly used by ancient followers of Pythagoras to justify statements by their master.
