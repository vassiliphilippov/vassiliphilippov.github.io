half-mast

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæfˈmæst / (AmE), / ˌhɑːfˈmɑːst / (BrE)
the position on a flagpole or mast that is some distance below the top, to which a flag is lowered as a sign of mourning or distress
flag position below the top as a sign of mourning

Examples

All government buildings flew their flags at half-mast to honor the late president.
The ship's ensign was lowered to half-mast following the tragic accident.
During the national day of mourning, the flag remained at half-mast throughout the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: half-staff - the position of a flag below the top of its staff, especially in the US

Etymology

From half (middle) + mast (the pole on a ship or flagpole); in reference to the flag's position.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhæf ˈmæst / (AmE), / ˌhɑːf ˈmɑːst / (BrE)
A position of a flag when it is flown about halfway down its pole as a sign of mourning or respect.
flag position for mourning

Examples

All the flags were flying at half-mast after the national tragedy.
The school lowered its flag to half-mast in memory of the former principal.
Officials ordered flags to be raised half-mast to honor the victims.

Synset Examples

We observed a moment of silence with flags at half-mast.
After the tragedy, all flags were raised half-mast nationwide.
The flags were flown at half-mast to honor the deceased.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: full-mast - the position of a flag at the top of the pole

Etymology

From 'half' (meaning halfway) and 'mast' (the pole on a ship or flagstaff).
