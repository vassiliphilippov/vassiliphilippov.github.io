catty

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæti / (AmE), / ˈkæti / (BrE)
A unit of weight used in parts of Southeast Asia, especially China, typically equal to about 500 grams (about 1.1 pounds).
Asian unit of weight (c. 500g)

Examples

He bought rice by the catty at the local market.
A traditional Chinese catty weighs about 500 grams.
Tea was often sold in catties during the colonial period.

Etymology

From Malay 'kati', possibly from Chinese Cantonese 'gat1','catty' became common in English through trade.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkæti / (AmE), / ˈkæti / (BrE)
(of a person or remark) deliberately hurtful or malicious, especially in a sly or spiteful way.
deliberately hurtful or spiteful (remark/person)

Examples

Her catty comments about her colleague surprised everyone at the meeting.
The conversation turned catty when they started discussing their rival.
Don't be so catty; there's no need to make such unkind remarks.

Synset Examples

a catty remark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malicious - characterized by malice or a desire to harm others
  • Synonym: spiteful - showing or caused by malice
  • Antonym: kind - having or showing a friendly, generous, and considerate nature

Etymology

From 'cat' + '-y', likening sharp, spiteful comments to the supposed nature of cats.
