to satisfy

1. VERB

/ ˈsætɪsˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈsætɪsfaɪ / (BrE)
to fulfill or meet a requirement, condition, need, or desire
to fulfill a requirement or need

Examples

The company must satisfy all safety requirements before the product can be released.
Her explanation did not satisfy the panel's questions about the research.
We need to satisfy our basic needs before pursuing higher goals.

Synset Examples

does this paper meet the requirements for the degree?
This job doesn't match my dreams
meet a need

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fulfill - to carry out or bring to realization
  • Synonym: meet - to satisfy or fulfill (a standard or requirement)
  • Antonym: to fail - to be unsuccessful in achieving or meeting (something)

Etymology

From Latin satisfacere, from satis (enough) + facere (to make).

2. VERB

/ ˈsætɪsˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈsætɪsfaɪ / (BrE)
to make someone pleased or content by fulfilling their wishes, needs, or expectations
make pleased or content

Examples

Nothing seemed to satisfy him after the disappointing news.
A warm meal was enough to satisfy her hunger and lift her spirits.
The company tried to satisfy its customers by improving its services.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: please - to make someone feel happy or satisfied
  • Antonym: to dissatisfy - fail to make happy or content

Etymology

From Latin satisfacere, from satis (enough) + facere (to do).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01820398-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈsætɪsˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈsætɪsfaɪ / (BrE)
to meet the requirements, standards, or expectations of someone or something
meet requirements or expectations

Examples

He failed to satisfy the entrance requirements for the university.
The company strives to satisfy customer expectations with high-quality service.
All conditions must be satisfied before the contract is signed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fulfill - to carry out or meet a requirement or desire
  • Antonym: to disappoint - to fail to meet expectations or hopes

Etymology

From Old French satisfaire, from Latin satisfacere (to do enough, satisfy), from satis (enough) + facere (to do).

WordNet ID: oewn-02677669-v
Synset POS: v
