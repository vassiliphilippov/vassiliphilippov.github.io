tuberculin

1. NOUN

/ tuˈbɜrkjəlɪn / (AmE), / tjuːˈbɜːkjʊlɪn / (BrE)
a sterile liquid containing a purified protein derivative of the tuberculosis bacterium, used to diagnose tuberculosis
liquid used to diagnose tuberculosis
medical

Examples

A small amount of tuberculin was injected under the skin for the tuberculosis test.
The presence of a reaction to tuberculin indicates possible exposure to the tuberculosis bacteria.
Doctors use tuberculin to determine whether someone has been infected with tuberculosis.

Etymology

From Latin 'tuberculum' (small swelling, tubercle) with scientific suffix '-in' for proteins.
