vena pylorica

1. NOUN

/ ˌveɪnə paɪˈlɔrɪkə / (AmE), / ˌveɪnə pɪˈlɒrɪkə / (BrE)
A tributary of the right gastric vein, located near the pylorus of the stomach.
vein near stomach's pylorus
medical

Examples

The vena pylorica plays an important role in draining blood from the region near the pylorus.
During the operation, the surgeon identified the vena pylorica to avoid accidental injury.
Knowledge of the vena pylorica is crucial for procedures involving the stomach's lesser curvature.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: right gastric vein - a vein that drains blood from the lesser curvature of the stomach

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) and 'pylorica' (pertaining to the pylorus).
