interval

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.tər.vəl / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tə.vəl / (BrE)
A period of time between two events or points; a measurable length of time separating two moments.
period of time between two events

Examples

The class is divided into three intervals of twenty minutes each.
After a short interval, the concert resumed with the second act.
There was a long interval between their first and second meetings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duration - the length of time that something continues
  • Degree Lower: moment - an exact point in time
  • Degree Higher: era - a long and distinct period of history with a particular feature or characteristic

Etymology

From Latin 'intervallum' (space between ramparts, distance, interval).

2. NOUN

/ ɪn.tɚ.vəl / (AmE), / ɪn.tə.vəl / (BrE)
(Music) The difference in pitch between two notes.
difference in pitch between notes (music)

Examples

The teacher asked us to identify the interval between C and G.
A major third is a musical interval commonly found in chords.
The interval of a perfect fifth is heard at the beginning of 'Twinkle, Twinkle, Little Star.'

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: octave - an interval spanning eight notes in diatonic scale
  • Degree Lower: semitone - an interval of a half step between notes
  • Degree Higher: tone - an interval of a whole step between notes

Etymology

From Latin intervallum (space between).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.tɚ.vəl / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tə.vəl / (BrE)
a set of numbers, especially real numbers, that includes all the points between two given endpoints
set of numbers between two endpoints

Examples

In calculus, an interval represents all the real numbers between two specified values.
The function is only defined on the interval from 0 to 1.
Find the length of the interval [3, 8] on the real number line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: range - the set of values between a minimum and maximum

Etymology

From Latin intervallum (space between, interval).
