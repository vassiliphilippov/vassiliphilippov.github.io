blameable

1. ADJ

/ ˈbleɪ.mə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈbleɪ.mə.bəl / (BrE)
Deserving blame or censure; worthy of being blamed for something wrong, evil, or injurious.
deserving blame or censure

Examples

His actions were clearly blameable and caused unnecessary harm to the project.
While the mistake was unfortunate, it was not entirely blameable given the circumstances.
The judge considered whether the defendant's behavior was truly blameable or simply a result of misunderstanding.

Synset Examples

blameworthy if not criminal behavior
censurable misconduct
culpable negligence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blameworthy - deserving blame or censure
  • Synonym: culpable - deserving blame, guilty
  • Antonym: innocent - not deserving blame; free from guilt

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French *blamable*, from Latin *blamabilis* (that may be blamed).
