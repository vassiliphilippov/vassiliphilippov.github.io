snitch

1. VERB

/ snɪtʃ / (AmE), / snɪtʃ / (BrE)
(informal) To steal something; to take by theft.
steal or take by theft (informal)
informal slang

Examples

Someone snitched my wallet while I was at the café.
I can't believe he snitched my keys and hid them as a prank.
A pickpocket snitched my phone on the subway.

Synset Examples

Someone snitched my wallet!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steal - to take something that does not belong to you without permission
  • Synonym: swipe - to steal something, especially quickly or sneakily (informal)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02327226-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ snɪtʃ / (AmE), / snɪtʃ / (BrE)
a person who secretly informs the police or authorities about someone else's wrongdoing, especially to avoid trouble for themselves
person who informs the police
informal slang

Examples

The thief was caught after a snitch tipped off the police about the robbery.
Nobody likes a snitch in the neighborhood because they can't be trusted.
He refused to be a snitch, even when the police offered him a deal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: informer - a person who gives information to the police, often secretly
  • Synonym: rat - a person who betrays others, especially by informing to the authorities

Etymology

Origin unknown; possibly from dialectal 'snitch' meaning to steal, or related to the older sense of 'snitch' as nose (thief's slang).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10110781-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ snɪtʃ / (AmE), / snɪtʃ / (BrE)
to secretly inform on someone, especially to the authorities or a person in power, often betraying a trust
secretly inform on someone
informal

Examples

He promised not to snitch on his friends, no matter what happened.
If you snitch to the teacher, the class will lose trust in you.
She got in trouble at school because someone snitched on her.

Synset Examples

He told on his classmate who had cheated on the exam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inform - to give information about someone's wrongdoing to an authority
  • Synonym: to betray - to be disloyal to someone by giving information about them

Etymology

Origin unknown; possibly related to earlier slang 'snitch' (to steal, informer) of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00843665-v
Synset POS: v
