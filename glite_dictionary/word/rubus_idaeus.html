Rubus idaeus

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːbəs aɪˈdeɪəs / (AmE), / ˈruːbəs ɪˈdeɪəs / (BrE)
the species of raspberry native to Europe and northern Asia, known for its edible red or orange fruit
European raspberry species

Examples

Rubus idaeus is widely cultivated for its sweet raspberries in gardens across Europe.
The scientific name Rubus idaeus refers specifically to the European variety of raspberry.
Many herbal remedies make use of the leaves of Rubus idaeus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raspberry - an edible soft fruit related to the blackberry, of a bright red colour

Etymology

From Latin: rubus (bramble) + idaeus (of Mount Ida).
