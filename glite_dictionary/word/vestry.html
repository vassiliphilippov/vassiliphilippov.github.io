vestry

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛstri / (AmE), / ˈvɛstri / (BrE)
a room in a church where sacred vessels and vestments are kept and where meetings may be held
church room for vestments and meetings

Examples

The choir gathered in the vestry before the service began.
The church keeps its valuable items locked in the vestry.
The monthly committee meeting was held in the small vestry at the back of the church.

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *vestrie*, from Latin *vestiarium* (wardrobe, dressing room).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɛstri / (AmE), / ˈvɛstri / (BrE)
in the Protestant Episcopal Church, a committee elected by the congregation that works with the churchwardens to manage the church's temporal affairs.
church committee for managing affairs

Examples

The annual meeting of the vestry addressed concerns about the church budget and building repairs.
She was elected to serve on the vestry for a three-year term.
The vestry worked closely with the rector to oversee all financial matters.

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *vestrie*, from Old French *vestiarie*, from Latin *vestiarium* (wardrobe, storeroom).
