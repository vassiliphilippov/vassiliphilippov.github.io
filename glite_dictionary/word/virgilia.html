Virgilia

1. NOUN

/ vərˈdʒɪl.i.ə / (AmE), / vɜːˈdʒɪl.i.ə / (BrE)
A genus of South African trees with pinnate leaves, rose-purple flowers, and leathery seed pods.
genus of South African trees

Examples

The Virgilia tree is prized in gardens for its fragrant rose-purple blossoms.
Several species within the Virgilia genus are native to the Cape region of South Africa.
Leaves of Virgilia trees are pinnate and provide attractive greenery.

Etymology

From Latin 'Virgilia', named after the Roman poet Virgil.

2. NOUN

/ vərˈdʒɪliə / (AmE), / vɜːˈdʒɪliə / (BrE)
A genus of South African trees with pinnate leaves and rose-purple flowers, followed by leathery pods.
genus of South African trees

Examples

The Virgilia in the botanical garden was covered in delicate purple blooms.
Some South African forests are dominated by trees of the Virgilia genus.
Gardeners often choose Virgilia for its ornamental flowers and fast growth.

Etymology

Modern Latin, named after the Roman poet Virgil (Publius Vergilius Maro).
