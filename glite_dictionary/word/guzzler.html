guzzler

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌzlər / (AmE), / ˈɡʌzlə / (BrE)
A person who drinks large amounts of liquid, especially alcohol, greedily or rapidly.
someone who drinks liquids greedily

Examples

As a notorious guzzler, he emptied his glass before anyone else had finished their first sip.
That old truck is a real gas guzzler and costs a fortune to run.
The party was full of guzzlers who couldn't resist the free drinks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drinker - a person who drinks, especially alcoholic beverages
  • Antonym: teetotaler - a person who abstains completely from alcohol

Etymology

From 'guzzle' (to drink greedily) with agent noun suffix -er.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌzlər / (AmE), / ˈɡʌzlə / (BrE)
a person who drinks large quantities of alcohol, often quickly or greedily
person who drinks heavily

Examples

He became known as the neighborhood guzzler after too many wild parties.
At the pub, everyone joked about who was the fastest guzzler in the group.
It's hard to have a serious conversation with a dedicated guzzler at the bar.

Synset Examples

he's a beer guzzler every night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drunkard - a person who is habitually drunk
  • Antonym: teetotaler - a person who abstains from alcohol
  • Synonym: tippler - a person who drinks alcohol regularly

Etymology

From 'guzzle' (to drink greedily) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

Synset POS: n
