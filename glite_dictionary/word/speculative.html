speculative

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɛkjəˌleɪtɪv / (AmE), / spɛkjuˈleɪtɪv / (BrE)
(of an investment or business enterprise) involving a high risk of loss; not financially safe or secure.
involving high financial risk

Examples

The company’s speculative investments led to large gains but also substantial losses.
Buying shares during a market bubble can be incredibly speculative and unpredictable.
He was drawn to speculative ventures, hoping for quick returns despite the risks.

Synset Examples

a bad investment
high risk investments
anything that promises to pay too much can't help being risky
speculative business enterprises

Related Terms

  • Synonym: risky - involving or exposing to danger or loss

Etymology

From Latin 'speculativus', from 'speculari' (to observe, spy out).

2. ADJ

/ ˈspɛkjəˌleɪtɪv / (AmE), / spɛkˈjuːlətɪv / (BrE)
showing or expressing curiosity or a questioning attitude; wondering about the possibilities or reasons behind something.
showing curiosity or wonder

Examples

She gave him a speculative look, trying to guess what he was thinking.
The detective's speculative expression made it clear he was wondering about the strange circumstances.
He raised a speculative eyebrow at the unexpected news.

Synset Examples

if someone saw a man climbing a light post they might get inquisitive
raised a speculative eyebrow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inquisitive - having or showing curiosity

Etymology

From Latin 'speculativus' (contemplative), from 'speculari' (to observe), related to 'specere' (to look at).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00668084-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈspɛkjəˌleɪtɪv, spɛkˈjʊlətɪv / (AmE), / spɛkˈjʊlətɪv, ˈspɛkjʊlətɪv / (BrE)
based on guesses or incomplete evidence rather than on facts or thorough investigation
based on conjecture, not fact

Examples

Her theory remains speculative because there is no concrete evidence to support it.
The report was criticized for being overly speculative and lacking reliable data.
Investors should be wary of speculative claims that have not been validated.

Synset Examples

a notional figure of cost helps in determining production costs
speculative knowledge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjectural - based on or involving guesswork
  • Antonym: factual - based on facts

Etymology

From Latin 'speculativus', from 'speculari' (to observe).

Source: WordNet
