IMU

1. NOUN

/ aɪ ɛm ju / (AmE), / aɪ ɛm ju / (BrE)
an Islamist militant group founded in 1996, known as the Islamic Movement of Uzbekistan, opposing Uzbekistan's secular regime and aiming to establish an Islamic state in Central Asia
Islamic militant group active in Central Asia

Examples

The IMU has been linked to several terrorist activities in Central Asia.
After their defeat in Uzbekistan, members of the IMU relocated to Afghanistan.
Security forces have increased efforts to disrupt the IMU's drug trafficking operations.

Etymology

Acronym from Islamic Movement of Uzbekistan.
