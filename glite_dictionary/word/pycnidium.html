pycnidium

1. NOUN

/ pɪkˈnɪdiəm / (AmE), / pɪkˈnɪdɪəm / (BrE)
a flask-shaped asexual fruiting body in certain fungi that contains conidia
flask-shaped fungal fruiting body
medical

Examples

The botanist identified a pycnidium forming on the surface of the plant leaf.
Many fungi produce conidia inside a pycnidium during their life cycles.
A mature pycnidium may release spores involved in asexual reproduction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conidium - an asexual spore produced by fungi

Etymology

From Greek 'pyknos' (thick, dense) and '-idion' (diminutive suffix).
