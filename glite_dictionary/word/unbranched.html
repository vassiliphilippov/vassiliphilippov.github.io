unbranched

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈbræn(t)ʃt / (AmE), / ʌnˈbrɑːn(t)ʃt / (BrE)
having no branches; not divided into separate parts or offshoots.
having no branches

Examples

The cactus species is easily recognized by its unbranched stem.
Scientists observed an unbranched coral structure on the ocean floor.
Early spring shoots are often unbranched before they develop additional growth.

Synset Examples

a tree with an unbranched trunk

Related Terms

  • Antonym: branched - having branches; divided into separate parts

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'branched'; based on the pattern of forming adjectives indicating absence of features.
