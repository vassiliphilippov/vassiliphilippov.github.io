arbitrative

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑːrbəˌtreɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈɑːbɪtrətɪv / (BrE)
Relating to or having the power or authority to arbitrate disputes.
having power to arbitrate
legal

Examples

The company agreed to settle the dispute through an arbitrative process.
An arbitrative board was established to resolve employee grievances.
The contract included several arbitrative clauses for handling disagreements.

Synset Examples

an arbitrative board

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arbitral - relating to arbitration or arbitrators

Etymology

From Latin arbitrat-, past participle stem of 'arbitrare' (to judge, give a decision).
