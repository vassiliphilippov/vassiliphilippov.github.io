soprano

1. NOUN

/ səˈprɑːnoʊ / (AmE), / səˈprɑːnəʊ / (BrE)
a female singer with the highest vocal range of all voice types; also the highest voice part in choral music
female singer with the highest vocal range

Examples

The soprano impressed the audience with her clear, soaring high notes.
Maria trained as a soprano before joining the opera company.
Many choral pieces feature a challenging melody for the soprano section.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: alto - a singer with the second highest vocal range, below soprano
  • Degree Lower: mezzo-soprano - a female singer with a vocal range between soprano and alto
  • Antonym: tenor - a male singer with the highest ordinary adult male voice; above baritone

Etymology

From Italian 'soprano', from 'sopra' meaning 'above'.

2. NOUN

/ səˈprɑːnoʊ / (AmE), / səˈprɑːnəʊ / (BrE)
the highest type of female singing voice, or the voice of a boy before puberty, also used for a singer with such a voice
highest female singing voice

Examples

She has a beautiful soprano that captivates audiences.
The choir auditioned several young boys with clear soprano voices.
Maria was chosen for the lead role because of her powerful soprano.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: alto - the second highest singing voice, lower than soprano

Etymology

From Italian 'soprano', from 'sopra' meaning 'above'.

4. NOUN

/ səˈprænoʊ / (AmE), / səˈprɑːnəʊ / (BrE)
the highest female singing voice, or the pitch range corresponding to this voice
highest female singing voice

Examples

She has trained for years to perfect her soprano and reach those high notes.
The choir was searching for a new soprano to sing the challenging solo.
Many famous opera singers are celebrated for their powerful soprano.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: alto - the second highest female singing voice
  • Degree Lower: mezzo-soprano - a female voice between soprano and contralto

Etymology

From Italian 'soprano', from 'sopra' (above) + '-ano'.
