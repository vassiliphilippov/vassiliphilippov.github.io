Bluegrass

1. PROPN

/ ˈbluˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈbluːɡrɑːs / (BrE)
an area in central Kentucky, United States, noted for its fertile soil, bluegrass pastures, and thoroughbred horse farms.
region in central Kentucky, USA

Examples

The Bluegrass region of Kentucky is world-famous for its horse farms and beautiful pastures.
Many people visit the Bluegrass every spring to attend the Kentucky Derby festivities.
Historic towns dot the Bluegrass, attracting tourists interested in local culture and history.

Etymology

Named after the bluegrass species (genus Poa) that grows abundantly in the region.
