guessing

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɛsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡɛsɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of forming an opinion or estimate without sufficient information or evidence.
forming an estimate without enough information

Examples

Her decision was based more on guessing than on hard evidence.
Without clear data, success comes down to little more than guessing.
For many students, guessing plays a big role in multiple-choice exams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speculation - the act of forming a theory or conjecture without firm evidence
  • Antonym: knowing - the state of having certain knowledge or information

Etymology

From the verb 'guess' (Middle English 'gessen', probably of Scandinavian origin).
