predetermination

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriː.dɪˌtɝː.məˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriː.dɪˌtɜː.mɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of deciding or arranging something in advance, especially as if it is inevitable or destined to happen
act of deciding something in advance

Examples

The theory of fate relies heavily on the concept of predetermination.
Some religious doctrines teach predetermination of a person's destiny.
Scientific advances challenge the idea that life events are subject to predetermination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: determinism - the philosophical doctrine that all events are determined by existing causes
  • Antonym: free will - the power of acting without the constraint of necessity or fate; the ability to act at one's own discretion

Etymology

From Latin prae- (before) + determinatio (determination).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpriː.dɪˌtɝː.məˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriː.dɪˌtɜː.mɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of deciding or resolving to do something before it happens; an intention formed in advance
prior intention or mental resolve

Examples

Her predetermination to finish the marathon helped her push through the pain.
The predetermination of the committee members influenced the outcome of the vote.
He approached the negotiations with a clear predetermination to reach a fair agreement.

Synset Examples

he entered the argument with a predetermination to prove me wrong

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intent - determined resolve to do something
  • Synonym: decision - the act of making up one's mind

Etymology

From Latin prae- (before) + determinatio (limitation, settlement).

3. NOUN

/ ˌpriː.dɪˌtɝː.mɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriː.dɪˌtɜː.mɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
(Theology) The doctrine that all events, especially regarding salvation, have been decided in advance by divine will.
doctrine of all events being decided in advance

Examples

The concept of predetermination has been debated by theologians for centuries.
Some people find comfort in believing in predetermination, trusting that everything happens for a reason.
Calvinists emphasize predetermination as an essential part of their religious doctrine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predestination - the doctrine that all events are willed by God

Etymology

From Latin prae- (before) + determinare (to determine).
