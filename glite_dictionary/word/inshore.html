inshore

1. ADV

/ ˈɪnˌʃɔr / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌʃɔː / (BrE)
toward the shore, especially from the sea or a body of water.
toward the shore

Examples

The boat drifted slowly inshore as the tide changed.
Fishermen often move inshore during stormy weather.
We watched the dolphins swim inshore early in the morning.

Synset Examples

we swam two miles inshore

Related Terms

  • Antonym: offshore - away from or at a distance from the shore
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00259900-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪnˌʃɔr / (AmE), / ˈɪnʃɔː / (BrE)
(of wind or current) coming from the sea toward the land.
coming toward land from the sea

Examples

A strong inshore wind delayed the boats' departure.
Surfers prefer inshore breezes for better waves near the beach.
The forecast warned of dangerous inshore currents after the storm.

Synset Examples

an inshore breeze
an onshore gale

Related Terms

  • Antonym: offshore - of wind or current: blowing away from land toward the sea
  • Synonym: onshore - (of wind or current) coming from the sea toward the land

Etymology

From 'in' + 'shore', indicating movement towards the shore.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00466228-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈɪnˌʃɔr / (AmE), / ˈɪnʃɔː / (BrE)
situated or occurring close to the shore, especially of the sea.
near the shore

Examples

Many small boats operate inshore during the fishing season.
A strong inshore breeze made sailing difficult.
Environmental groups are monitoring inshore waters for pollution.

Synset Examples

inshore fisheries

Related Terms

  • Antonym: offshore - situated at sea some distance from the shore
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00465341-s
Synset POS: s
