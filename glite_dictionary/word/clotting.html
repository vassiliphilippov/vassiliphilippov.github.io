clotting

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɑtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklɒtɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which a liquid, especially blood, forms into semisolid lumps or clots
formation of clots in a liquid

Examples

Blood clotting is a vital process that helps prevent excessive bleeding after injury.
The abnormal clotting of blood can lead to dangerous blockages in the veins or arteries.
Scientists are studying the mechanisms of clotting disorders to develop better treatments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coagulation - the process of a liquid, especially blood, changing to a solid or semi-solid state
  • Antonym: bleeding - the flow of blood from a wound or ruptured blood vessel

Etymology

From 'clot' (lump or mass), with '-ing' forming the noun of action.
