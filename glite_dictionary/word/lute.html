lute

1. NOUN

/ luːt / (AmE), / luːt / (BrE)
A substance used for packing a joint or coating a porous surface to make it impervious to gas or liquid, especially in laboratory or engineering contexts.
substance for sealing joints or surfaces

Examples

In the laboratory, a thick lute was applied to seal the glass joints during the experiment.
Before welding, the engineer made sure to spread lute over the pipe connections to prevent leaks.
A suitable lute must be used to coat porous surfaces to keep them airtight under pressure.

Etymology

From Latin 'lutum' (mud, clay), referring to sealing material.

2. NOUN

/ lut / (AmE), / luːt / (BrE)
A musical instrument with a pear-shaped body, a bent neck, and a fretted fingerboard, typically played by plucking the strings.
a pear-shaped, plucked string instrument

Examples

During the concert, the musician played the lute with exceptional delicacy.
Medieval paintings often depict angels holding a lute among other musical instruments.
She spent years learning to play the lute as it was popular in Renaissance music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guitar - a stringed instrument with a flat body, played by plucking or strumming
  • Synonym: mandolin - a musical instrument with a rounded body and short neck, similar to the lute

Etymology

From Middle English *lute*, from Old French *lut*, from Arabic *al-ʿūd* (the wood, the lute).
