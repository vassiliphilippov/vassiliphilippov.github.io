severalty

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛv.ɚl.ti / (AmE), / ˈsɛv.əl.ti / (BrE)
The state of being held or owned by an individual person rather than jointly with others; exclusive individual ownership, especially of property.
exclusive individual ownership, especially of property
legal

Examples

The land was held in severalty, allowing the owner to control it exclusively.
When the estate was divided, each heir received his share in severalty.
Modern property law recognizes both joint tenancy and tenancy in severalty.

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *severalte* and Old French *severalte*, from Latin *separalis* (separate).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛvɚlti / (AmE), / ˈsɛvəlti / (BrE)
the state of being separate and distinct; the condition of being held or owned individually, especially with regard to land
state of being separate or individual

Examples

After the treaty, each member received land in severalty rather than in common with the tribe.
Ownership in severalty provides full control to a single individual or entity.
The concept of severalty became important as communal lands were divided among individuals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: joint - shared by two or more; not separate (esp. in legal ownership)

Etymology

From Anglo-French 'severalté', from Medieval Latin 'separalitas' (separateness, individuality).
