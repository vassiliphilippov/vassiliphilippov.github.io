Ammobium alatum

1. NOUN

/ ˌæ.moʊˈboʊ.mi.ə ˈeɪ.lə.təm / (AmE), / ˌæm.əˈbəʊ.mi.əm əˈleɪ.təm / (BrE)
An Australian perennial plant widely cultivated for its showy silvery-white flowers with bright yellow centers on long winged stems.
Australian plant with silvery-white flowers

Examples

Ammobium alatum adds a striking texture to gardens with its silvery-white blooms and yellow centers.
Many florists use Ammobium alatum in dried flower arrangements due to its lasting beauty.
The long winged stems of Ammobium alatum make it a popular choice for ornamental borders.

Etymology

From Greek *ammos* (sand) + Latin *alatus* (winged), describing its habitat and winged stems.
