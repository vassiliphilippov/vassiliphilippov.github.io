aeration

1. NOUN

/ ɛˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɛəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of exposing a substance, especially a liquid or soil, to air in order to improve its quality or to purify it
the process of exposing something to air

Examples

Proper aeration of the lawn helps grass roots grow deeper for a healthier lawn.
The treatment plant uses aeration to remove impurities from the water.
Regular aeration of the soil can significantly improve crop yields.

Synset Examples

the aeration of the soil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ventilation - the provision of fresh air to a room, building, or other space

Etymology

From Latin 'aer' (air) via French 'aération'.

2. NOUN

/ ɛrˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / eəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of introducing air or another gas into a liquid to make it effervescent or to improve its quality
charging a liquid with gas to make it effervescent

Examples

The aeration of drinking water helps to improve its taste and purity.
Modern aquariums rely on aeration systems to keep fish healthy.
The sparkling wine's bubbles are formed during aeration in the fermentation process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbonation - the process of dissolving carbon dioxide gas in a liquid

Etymology

From Latin 'aer' (air) via French 'aération'.
