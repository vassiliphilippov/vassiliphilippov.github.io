Heloderma suspectum

1. NOUN

/ hɛləˈdɜːrmə səˈspɛktəm / (AmE), / hɛləˈdɜːmɐ səsˈpɛktəm / (BrE)
the scientific name for the Gila monster, a large orange and black venomous lizard native to the southwestern United States
scientific name for the Gila monster

Examples

The Heloderma suspectum is renowned for its bright coloration and bead-like scales.
Scientists studied Heloderma suspectum to better understand venom composition in lizards.
The Heloderma suspectum is federally protected due to its limited range and declining population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gila monster - large venomous lizard native to the southwestern US and northwestern Mexico

Etymology

From Greek hēlos (nail, stud) + derma (skin), referring to the lizard's beaded skin; Latin suspectum (suspected).
