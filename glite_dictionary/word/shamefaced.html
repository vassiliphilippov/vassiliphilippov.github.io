shamefaced

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃeɪmˌfeɪst / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪmˌfeɪst / (BrE)
Extremely modest, shy, or showing a feeling of shame or embarrassment.
modest or shy; showing embarrassment

Examples

He gave a shamefaced apology for his late arrival at the meeting.
She looked shamefaced when everyone congratulated her on her achievements.
Despite his shamefaced manner, his friends encouraged him to take pride in his success.

Synset Examples

cheerfully bearing reproaches but shamefaced at praise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modest - having a humble opinion of oneself
  • Antonym: confident - sure of oneself or one's abilities

Etymology

From Middle English 'shamefaced' (altered from 'shamefast', meaning restrained by shame); from 'shame' + 'fast' (meaning firm, fixed).

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃeɪm.feɪst / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪm.feɪst / (BrE)
showing or expressing shame or embarrassment; having a sense of guilt or self-consciousness about an action or situation
showing shame or embarrassment

Examples

He gave a shamefaced apology for forgetting her birthday.
The children looked shamefaced when the teacher caught them whispering.
She returned the lost wallet with a shamefaced expression.

Synset Examples

a guilty look
the hangdog and shamefaced air of the retreating enemy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ashamed - feeling shame or guilt
  • Antonym: proud - feeling deep pleasure or satisfaction (opposite in demeanor)

Etymology

Alteration of Middle English 'shamfast', from 'shame' + 'fast' (firmly fixed).

3. ADJ

/ ˈʃeɪmˌfeɪst / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪmˌfeɪst / (BrE)
showing or expressing embarrassment or guilt
showing embarrassment or guilt

Examples

He gave a shamefaced apology when he realized his mistake.
She looked shamefaced when questioned about the broken vase.
The boy stood shamefaced in front of the teacher after misbehaving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ashamed - feeling shame or guilt
  • Antonym: unashamed - not feeling or showing shame

Etymology

Alteration of earlier 'shamefast', from Old English scamu (shame) + fæst (fast, fixed).
