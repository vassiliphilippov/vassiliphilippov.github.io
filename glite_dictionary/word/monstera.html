Monstera

1. NOUN

/ mɑnˈstɛrə / (AmE), / mɒnˈstɪərə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American climbing plants known for their large, deeply incised leaves, often grown as ornamentals.
genus of tropical climbing plants

Examples

The Monstera is a popular houseplant due to its impressive, perforated leaves.
Gardeners often choose Monstera for indoor landscaping and decorative purposes.
Many species within the Monstera genus are prized for their distinctive foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: philodendron - a genus of tropical climbing plants similar to Monstera

Etymology

From Latin 'monstrum' (monster), referring to the unusual appearance of the leaves.
