audiology

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑdiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌɔːdiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study and measurement of hearing, especially the diagnosis and treatment of hearing disorders.
study and measurement of hearing
medical

Examples

After earning her degree in audiology, she began working at a clinic for hearing disorders.
Modern audiology uses sophisticated equipment to test and diagnose hearing problems.
Pediatric audiology focuses on detecting and managing hearing loss in children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: otology - the scientific study of the ear and its diseases

Etymology

From Latin 'audīre' (to hear) + Greek '-logia' (study of).
