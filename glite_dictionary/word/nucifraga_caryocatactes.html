Nucifraga caryocatactes

1. NOUN

/ nuːˌsɪˈfreɪɡə ˌkæriˌoʊkəˈtæktiːz / (AmE), / njuːˌsɪˈfreɪɡə ˌkærɪəʊkəˈtæktiːz / (BrE)
A Eurasian bird in the crow family (Corvidae), commonly known as the Eurasian nutcracker, noted for its habit of cracking nuts.
Eurasian bird, the nutcracker

Examples

The Nucifraga caryocatactes stores pine nuts in hidden caches for winter feeding.
Birdwatchers hope to spot a Nucifraga caryocatactes in the dense coniferous forests.
Many legends surround the cleverness of the Nucifraga caryocatactes and its ability to open hard shells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nutcracker - bird that cracks nuts, especially in the genus Nucifraga

Etymology

From Latin: *nux* (nut) + *frangere* (to break), *caryocatactes* from Greek *karyon* (nut) + *kataktes* (breaker).
