imminentness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪmənəntnəs / (AmE), / ˈɪmɪnəntnɪs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being imminent; likelihood of occurring very soon.
the state of being imminent

Examples

The imminentness of the approaching storm forced the evacuation of the coastal town.
There was a palpable imminentness in the air as the verdict was about to be announced.
The general warned his troops about the imminentness of the enemy's attack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imminence - the state or fact of being about to happen
  • Antonym: remoteness - the state of being distant in time or space

Etymology

From Latin imminēre (to project over, threaten), via imminent + -ness.
