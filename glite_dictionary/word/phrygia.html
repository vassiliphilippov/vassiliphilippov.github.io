Phrygia

1. PROPN

/ ˈfrɪdʒiə / (AmE), / ˈfrɪdʒiə / (BrE)
an ancient country in western and central Asia Minor, corresponding to parts of modern Turkey
ancient country in Asia Minor

Examples

Archaeologists continue to find artifacts from Phrygia dating back to the Iron Age.
The mythology of Phrygia influenced both Greek and Roman cultures in antiquity.
King Midas was one of the most famous rulers of Phrygia.

Etymology

From Greek *Phrugia*, the name of the region.
