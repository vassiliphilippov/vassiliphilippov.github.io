Cenchrus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.krəs / (AmE), / ˈsɛŋ.krəs / (BrE)
a genus of grasses in the family Gramineae, known for producing spiny burs
genus of grasses with burs

Examples

Cenchrus species are commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions around the world.
The burs of Cenchrus can attach to animal fur and clothing, aiding seed dispersal.
Researchers studied the ecological impact of Cenchrus on local grazing lands.

Etymology

From Greek *kenchros* (millet).
