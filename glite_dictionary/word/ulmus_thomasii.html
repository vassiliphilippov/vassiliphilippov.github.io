Ulmus thomasii

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌlməs θəˈmɑːsi.aɪ / (AmE), / ˈʌlməs θəˈmɑːsɪ.aɪ / (BrE)
A species of tall elm tree native to eastern North America, commonly known as the rock elm or cork elm.
a species of North American elm tree

Examples

The Ulmus thomasii is valued for its strong, durable wood in furniture making.
Restoration projects in eastern forests often include planting Ulmus thomasii to encourage biodiversity.
In autumn, the leaves of Ulmus thomasii turn a beautiful golden-yellow before falling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rock elm - common name for Ulmus thomasii
  • Degree Lower: slippery elm - another North American elm species (Ulmus rubra)

Etymology

Named after John Thomas, an American botanist.
