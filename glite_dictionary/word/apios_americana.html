Apios americana

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ.piˈoʊs əˌmɛr.ɪˈkeɪ.nə / (AmE), / ˌeɪ.piˈɒs əˌmɛr.ɪˈkɑː.nə / (BrE)
A North American climbing vine with fragrant purple-brown flowers and edible tubers, traditionally used as a food source by Native Americans.
North American vine with edible tubers

Examples

The tubers of the Apios americana were a vital food source for many Native American tribes.
Botanists are interested in cultivating Apios americana for its high-protein content.
We planted Apios americana along the fence so we could enjoy both its flowers and its tubers.

Etymology

From Greek 'apios' (pear) referring to the tuber shape, and Latin 'americana' meaning 'of America'.
