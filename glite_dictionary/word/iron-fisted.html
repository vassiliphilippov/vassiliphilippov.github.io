iron-fisted

1. ADJ

/ ˈaɪərn ˌfɪstɪd / (AmE), / ˈaɪən ˌfɪstɪd / (BrE)
using harsh, severe, and authoritarian measures; inflexibly and oppressively strict.
using harsh or authoritarian control

Examples

The new CEO adopted an iron-fisted approach to company discipline.
Under the iron-fisted rule of the regime, protests were quickly suppressed.
His iron-fisted leadership style alienated many of his colleagues.

Synset Examples

His iron-fisted policies led to widespread criticism.
She was known for her iron-fisted management style.
The iron-fisted leader silenced dissent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruthless - having or showing no pity or compassion for others
  • Antonym: lenient - permissive, merciful, or tolerant

Etymology

Formed from 'iron' (suggesting strength or harshness) and 'fisted', meaning having or using a fist.
