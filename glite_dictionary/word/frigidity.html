frigidity

1. NOUN

/ frɪˈdʒɪd.ə.t̬i / (AmE), / frɪˈdʒɪd.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
(medical) Lack of sexual responsiveness, especially in women, often involving inability to experience orgasm during intercourse.
lack of sexual responsiveness
medical

Examples

Some couples seek counseling to address frigidity and improve intimacy in their relationship.
The patient's sense of frigidity caused significant distress in her marriage.
Doctors now recognize that frigidity can have psychological as well as physical causes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anorgasmia - inability to achieve orgasm
  • Antonym: libido - sexual drive or desire

Etymology

From Latin frigidity, from frigidus ‘cold’.

2. NOUN

/ frɪˈdʒɪdəti / (AmE), / frɪˈdʒɪdəti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being very cold; the absence of heat.
extreme coldness; absence of heat

Examples

The frigidity of the air made it difficult to stay outside for long.
He shivered at the unexpected frigidity that seeped into the old house at night.
After sunset, the frigidity of the desert became almost unbearable.

3. NOUN

/ frɪˈdʒɪdəti / (AmE), / frɪˈdʒɪdəti / (BrE)
A lack of warmth, affection, or enthusiasm in manner or feeling.
lack of warmth or affection

Examples

Her frigidity towards her colleagues made it difficult for her to form close friendships at work.
There was a distinct frigidity in his voice whenever the subject was mentioned.
Despite the festive occasion, a sense of frigidity lingered in the room.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: warmth - a quality of showing enthusiasm, affection, or friendliness
