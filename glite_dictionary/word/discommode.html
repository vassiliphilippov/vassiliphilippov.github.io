to discommode

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪskəˈmoʊd / (AmE), / ˌdɪskəˈməʊd / (BrE)
to cause inconvenience or slight discomfort to someone
cause inconvenience or discomfort

Examples

I hope my visit did not discommode you in any way.
The repair work may discommode residents for a few days.
He apologized for having discommoded his colleagues with his request.

Synset Examples

Sorry to trouble you, but …

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inconvenience - to cause difficulty or trouble to someone
  • Synonym: to disturb - to interrupt or bother someone
  • Antonym: to accommodate - to provide convenience or help

Etymology

From French *décommoder*, from Latin *incommodare* (to inconvenience).
