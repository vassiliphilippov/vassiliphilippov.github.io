to colly

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑːli / (AmE), / ˈkɒli / (BrE)
to make something soiled or dirty; to stain or befoul
make dirty; soil
archaic

Examples

Her hands were colly with soot after tending the fire.
The children returned home with their faces colly from playing in the mud.
Do not colly your dress before the recital.

Synset Examples

don't soil your clothes when you play outside!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soil - to make dirty or stain
  • Antonym: clean - to make clean; remove dirt

Etymology

From Middle English 'colien', from 'col(e)' meaning 'coal', referring to blackening.
