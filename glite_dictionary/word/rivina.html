Rivina

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈvaɪnə / (AmE), / rɪˈviːnə / (BrE)
A small genus of erect, perennial shrubby herbs native to tropical and subtropical America.
genus of tropical shrubby herbs

Examples

The Rivina genus includes several attractive ornamental plants.
Botanists studied the distribution of Rivina throughout the Caribbean islands.
Rivina species are known for their small, bright red berries.

Etymology

Named after Augustus Quirinus Rivinus (1652–1723), a German botanist.
