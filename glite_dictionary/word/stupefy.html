to stupefy

1. VERB

/ ˈstuːpəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈstjuːpɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
To make someone unable to think or feel properly, often through shock, tiredness, drugs, or overwhelming emotion.
make unable to think or feel properly

Examples

The strong medication seemed to stupefy him, and he could barely respond to questions.
News of the unexpected victory would stupefy even the most optimistic fans.
The sheer beauty of the landscape could stupefy those seeing it for the first time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daze - to stun or stupefy, especially by a blow
  • Synonym: to bewilder - to confuse or puzzle completely
  • Antonym: to enlighten - to give someone greater knowledge; opposite of stupefy

Etymology

From Latin 'stupefacere' from 'stupere' (be stunned) + 'facere' (to make).

2. VERB

/ ˈstuːpəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈstjuːpɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to make someone so surprised, confused, or unable to think clearly that they are momentarily unable to react; to astonish or bewilder greatly
to astonish or bewilder greatly

Examples

The magician's tricks stupefied the audience, leaving everyone speechless.
Her sudden transformation completely stupefied her classmates and teachers alike.
It still stupefies me how quickly technology advances each year.

3. VERB

/ ˈstuːpəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈstjuːpɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make someone unable to think clearly, often by shocking, stunning, or dazing them
make unable to think clearly; stun

Examples

The harsh news seemed to stupefy everyone in the room.
The blow to his head was enough to stupefy him for several minutes.
The magician’s trick managed to stupefy the entire audience.

Synset Examples

stun fish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stun - to make someone unconscious or unable to react quickly
  • Synonym: daze - to make someone unable to think or react properly
  • Antonym: to revive - to bring someone back to a normal state, especially after being stunned or unconscious

Etymology

From Latin stupēfacere (to make stupid, to stun), from stupēre (to be stunned) + facere (to make).
