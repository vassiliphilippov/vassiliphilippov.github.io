Okapia

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈkɑːpiə / (AmE), / əʊˈkɑːpiə / (BrE)
a genus of mammals that includes the okapi, an African ungulate closely related to the giraffe
genus of African mammals including the okapi

Examples

The genus Okapia contains only one living species, the okapi.
Researchers compared Okapia with related giraffid genera in their study.
Fossil records suggest that the range of Okapia was once much wider in Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: okapi - a large, forest-dwelling African mammal closely related to the giraffe

Etymology

From scientific Latin, based on the Congolese name for the okapi.

2. PROPN

/ oʊˈkæpiə / (AmE), / əʊˈkɑːpiə / (BrE)
A genus of mammals in the giraffe family, comprising the okapi, native to the rainforests of central Africa.
genus including the okapi

Examples

Scientists classified the newly discovered animal under the genus Okapia.
The only living species in Okapia is the okapi, native to the Congo rainforest.
Taxonomists debated the placement of Okapia within the giraffe family for years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: okapi - A large browsing mammal of the giraffe family, native to central Africa

Etymology

Modern Latin, formed from the native name of the animal in the Democratic Republic of the Congo.
