couth

1. ADJ

/ kuːθ / (AmE), / kuːθ / (BrE)
Refined and well-mannered, often used facetiously.
refined and well-mannered

Examples

Despite his reputation for mischief, he was surprisingly couth at the dinner party.
Her couth behavior impressed everyone at the formal event.
They joked that his manners were far too couth for the raucous crowd.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncouth - lacking good manners; uncultured and awkward

Etymology

From Old English *cūth* (known, familiar).

2. NOUN

/ kuːθ / (AmE), / kuːθ / (BrE)
Refinement or cultured manners, often used humorously or facetiously as the opposite of 'uncouth'.
refinement; cultured manners (facetious)
informal

Examples

Despite his rough childhood, he showed remarkable couth at the dinner party.
She admired his couth in handling difficult conversations with tact.
He joked that after years abroad, he had acquired a certain couth missing from his youth.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncouth - lacking good manners or refinement

Etymology

Formed by back-formation from 'uncouth', which comes from Old English 'uncūth' (unknown, unfamiliar).
