daybed

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪˌbɛd / (AmE), / ˈdeɪbɛd / (BrE)
a couch without arms that can be used as a seat during the day and as a bed at night
an armless couch that doubles as a bed

Examples

She placed a bright pillow on the daybed in the sunroom.
The guest room doubles as an office thanks to a comfortable daybed.
He spent the afternoon reading on the daybed by the window.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sofa bed - a sofa that can be converted into a bed

Etymology

From 'day' + 'bed', indicating its dual use as a seat by day and a bed by night.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪˌbɛd / (AmE), / ˈdeɪˌbɛd / (BrE)
a piece of furniture resembling a couch or sofa, often with a back and arms, used for reclining or lounging and sometimes serving as a bed
reclining couch or sofa, sometimes a bed

Examples

She placed a daybed in the sunroom so guests could relax or nap during the afternoon.
The antique daybed was both stylish and comfortable, perfect for reading by the window.
He stretched out on the daybed after work, enjoying a few minutes of quiet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sofa - a long upholstered seat with a back and arms for more than one person
  • Synonym: couch - a long upholstered piece of furniture for sitting or reclining

Etymology

From 'day' + 'bed', referring to use as a bed during the day.
