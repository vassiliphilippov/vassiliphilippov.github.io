bounded

1. ADJ

/ ˈbaʊndɪd / (AmE), / ˈbaʊndɪd / (BrE)
having definite limits or boundaries; confined within fixed limits
having defined limits or boundaries

Examples

The property is bounded by a river on one side and a road on the other.
Her responsibilities at work are clearly bounded to avoid confusion.
Mathematicians often study bounded sets in analysis.

Synset Examples

a delimited frontier through the disputed region

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unbounded - without limits or boundaries
  • Synonym: delimited - having limits or boundaries fixed or marked off

Etymology

From the verb 'bound' meaning 'to set limits'; from Old French 'bonde', from Medieval Latin 'bunda' (boundary).
