agrobiology

1. NOUN

/ ˌæɡroʊbaɪˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌæɡrəʊbaɪˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The scientific study of the nutrition, growth, and yield of crops, particularly in relation to improving agricultural productivity.
study of plant nutrition and crop yield
medical

Examples

She decided to pursue a career in agrobiology to help improve global food security.
Research in agrobiology has led to more efficient fertilizers and better crop yields.
Many universities now offer courses in agrobiology to address challenges in sustainable agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agronomy - the science of soil management and crop production

Etymology

From Greek 'agros' (field) + 'biology'.
