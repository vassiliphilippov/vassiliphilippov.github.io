to home-school

1. VERB

/ hoʊm skuːl / (AmE), / həʊm skuːl / (BrE)
to educate a child at home instead of sending them to a traditional school
teach (a child) at home

Examples

Many families choose to home-school their children for religious or philosophical reasons.
After moving to the countryside, they decided to home-school their two sons.
She began to home-school her daughter during the pandemic.

Synset Examples

The parents are home-schooling their daughter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homeschool - verb meaning to teach (a child) at home; spelling variant

Etymology

From 'home' + 'school', formed as a compound verb from the nouns.
