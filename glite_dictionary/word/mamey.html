mamey

1. NOUN

/ məˈmeɪ / (AmE), / məˈmeɪ / (BrE)
A tropical American tree (Pouteria sapota or Mammea americana) bearing edible fruit with a leathery rind.
tropical American tree with edible fruit

Examples

The mamey is prized in the Caribbean for its sweet and nutritious fruit.
Farmers in Central America often plant mamey trees for their delicious harvest.
She made a smoothie using ripe mamey blended with milk and honey.

Etymology

From Spanish 'mamey', of Taíno origin.

2. NOUN

/ məˈmeɪ / (AmE), / məˈmeɪ / (BrE)
A tropical fruit with a thick russet leathery rind and juicy yellow or reddish flesh, typically globular or ovoid in shape.
a tropical fruit with yellow or reddish flesh

Examples

The smoothie was made with fresh mamey and coconut milk.
You can find mamey at many Latin American markets during the summer.
She had never tasted a mamey before her trip to the Caribbean.

Etymology

From Spanish 'mamey', originally from Taíno.
