lepton

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛp.tɑn / (AmE), / ˈlɛp.tɒn / (BrE)
A small coin and former fractional currency unit in Greece, with 100 lepta equaling one drachma.
former Greek fractional coin/unit

Examples

The antique dealer showed me a bronze lepton from 19th-century Greece.
Before the euro, you would sometimes find a lepton among old Greek coins.
A hundred lepta made up a single drachma in the pre-euro currency system.

Etymology

From Greek λεπτόν (leptón), meaning 'small coin'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛp.tɑn / (AmE), / ˈlɛp.tɒn / (BrE)
a subatomic particle, such as an electron or neutrino, that does not take part in strong nuclear interactions and has a baryon number of zero
type of elementary particle in physics

Examples

The electron is the most familiar example of a lepton in everyday matter.
Neutrinos are elusive leptons that rarely interact with other particles.
Physicists study leptons to understand the fundamental forces of nature.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: baryon - a class of subatomic particles including protons and neutrons
  • Degree Higher: fermion - a particle that follows Fermi–Dirac statistics and includes leptons and baryons

Etymology

From Greek leptos (fine, small).
