Osmanthus americanus

1. NOUN

/ ɑzˈmænθəs əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnəs / (AmE), / ɒzˈmænθəs əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnəs / (BrE)
a small tree of the southern United States, having clusters of dull white flowers and dark purple fruits
tree native to the southern US

Examples

The Osmanthus americanus flourishes in the moist woodlands of the southeastern United States.
Botanists study Osmanthus americanus for its fragrant flowers and ornamental value.
In spring, the Osmanthus americanus produces clusters of white blossoms followed by purple fruit.

Etymology

From Latin, combining Osmanthus (from Greek 'osme' meaning 'smell' and 'anthos' meaning 'flower') and 'americanus' meaning 'American'.
