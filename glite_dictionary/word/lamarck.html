Lamarck

1. PROPN

/ ləˈmɑrk / (AmE), / ləˈmɑːk / (BrE)
(1744–1829) A French naturalist who proposed that evolution results from the inheritance of acquired characteristics.
French naturalist; proposed inheritance of acquired traits

Examples

Jean-Baptiste Lamarck was one of the first to propose a systematic theory of evolution.
In biology class, we discussed the differences between Darwin and Lamarck.
Lamarck's theory of inherited characteristics has been largely discredited, but his influence remains significant.

Etymology

From the surname Lamarck, belonging to Jean-Baptiste Pierre Antoine de Monet, Chevalier de Lamarck.
