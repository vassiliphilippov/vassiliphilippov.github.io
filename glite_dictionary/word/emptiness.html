emptiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛmptinəs / (AmE), / ˈɛmptinɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having no value, purpose, or meaning; futility
quality of being valueless or futile

Examples

After years of chasing wealth, he was left with a profound sense of emptiness.
She was troubled by the emptiness at the core of her daily routine.
Many critics commented on the emptiness of the movie's storyline.

Synset Examples

he rejected the vanities of the world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: futility - the quality of being pointless or useless
  • Antonym: meaningfulness - the quality of having meaning or value

Etymology

From Old English 'æmtig' (empty) + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛmptinəs / (AmE), / ˈɛmptɪnɪs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being empty; the condition of containing nothing.
the state of being empty

Examples

After removing the furniture, a sense of emptiness filled the living room.
The emptiness of the box surprised her; she expected something inside.
He stared into the emptiness of the abandoned warehouse, feeling slightly uneasy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vacuum - a space entirely devoid of matter
  • Antonym: fullness - the state of being full

Etymology

From Old English 'æmtig' (empty) + '-ness' (state, quality).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14478672-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛmptinəs / (AmE), / ˈɛmptinɪs / (BrE)
a state or area containing nothing; an empty space
a state or space with nothing in it

Examples

There was an overwhelming emptiness in the room after everyone left.
He stared into the emptiness of the abandoned warehouse.
Space explorers are fascinated by the emptiness between the stars.

Synset Examples

the huge desert voids
the emptiness of outer space
without their support he'll be ruling in a vacuum

Related Terms

  • Synonym: void - a completely empty space
  • Synonym: vacuum - a space entirely devoid of matter

Etymology

From Old English 'æmtignes', from 'æmettig' (empty) + '-ness'.
