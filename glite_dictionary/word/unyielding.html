unyielding

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈjiːldɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈjiːldɪŋ / (BrE)
not willing to change, give up, or be influenced; firm or resolute, especially in the face of opposition or difficulty.
firm; not giving way

Examples

She remained unyielding in her decision despite mounting pressure from her colleagues.
The mountain presented an unyielding barrier to early explorers.
With unyielding determination, he pursued his dream until he finally succeeded.

Synset Examples

dogged persistence
dour determination
the most vocal and pertinacious of all the critics
a mind not gifted to discover truth but tenacious to hold it
men tenacious of opinion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stubborn - unwilling to change or give up; obstinate
  • Antonym: yielding - willing to give way or be influenced by others

Etymology

From un- (not) + yielding (from yield, meaning to give way or surrender).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈjiːldɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈjiːldɪŋ / (BrE)
not easily bent, shaped, or broken; resistant to physical force or pressure.
resistant to force or pressure

Examples

The helmet was constructed from an unyielding material to protect the wearer.
She admired the unyielding structure of the ancient fortress.
Their bed had an unyielding mattress that provided strong back support.

Synset Examples

an unyielding head support

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rigid - unable to bend or be forced out of shape; not flexible
  • Antonym: flexible - able to bend easily without breaking

Etymology

From un- (not) + yielding, from yield (Old English geldan, to pay, give up).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01156123-s
Synset POS: s
