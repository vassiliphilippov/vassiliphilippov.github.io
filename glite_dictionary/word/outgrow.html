to outgrow

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈɡroʊ / (AmE), / aʊtˈɡrəʊ / (BrE)
to grow faster than something or someone else
grow faster than

Examples

The younger tree will eventually outgrow the older one if conditions remain favorable.
Children sometimes outgrow their siblings in height by their teenage years.
Rapidly expanding cities can outgrow their original infrastructure needs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to surpass - to do or be better, greater, or stronger than

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'out-' (meaning 'beyond' or 'more than') and 'grow'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01107508-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ aʊtˈɡroʊ / (AmE), / aʊtˈɡrəʊ / (BrE)
to become too large or too mature for something so that it is no longer suitable, necessary, or appropriate
become too large or mature for

Examples

As he got older, he outgrew his favorite superhero costume.
Many children eventually outgrow their fear of the dark.
She hopes to outgrow her allergies as she becomes an adult.

Synset Examples

I have outgrown these clothes
She outgrew her childish habits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outwear - to last longer than or become too large for (something in terms of use or wear)

Etymology

Formed from 'out-' (beyond) + 'grow'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00544825-v
Synset POS: v
