Cynara cardunculus

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈnɛr.ə kɑrˈdʌŋ.kjʊ.ləs / (AmE), / sɪˈnɑː.rə kɑːˈdʌŋ.kjʊ.ləs / (BrE)
A southern European plant with spiny leaves and purple flowers, cultivated for its edible leafstalks and roots; also known as cardoon.
southern European edible plant (cardoon)

Examples

The thick stalks of the Cynara cardunculus are often used in Mediterranean cooking.
Gardeners in southern Europe sometimes grow Cynara cardunculus for its ornamental purple flowers.
Experts say that Cynara cardunculus can be blanched to reduce its natural bitterness before eating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardoon - common name for Cynara cardunculus

Etymology

From Latin: Cynara (a genus of thistle-like plants) + cardunculus (diminutive of 'carduus', thistle).
