adjuratory

1. ADJ

/ əˈdʒʊrəˌtɔri / (AmE), / əˈdʒʊərəˌtɔːri / (BrE)
expressing or containing a solemn charge, command, or entreaty
containing a solemn charge or command

Examples

The judge gave an adjuratory warning to the jury before deliberations began.
His speech was adjuratory, urging listeners to uphold justice and truth.
She made an adjuratory appeal for peace during the tense negotiations.

Etymology

From Latin adjūrātōrius, from adjūrāre (to swear, to adjure).

2. ADJ

/ əˈdʒʊr.əˌtɔr.i / (AmE), / əˈdʒʊər.əˌtɔː.ri / (BrE)
expressing an earnest or solemn entreaty; imploring or beseeching especially in a formal or grave manner
earnestly entreating; solemnly imploring

Examples

She spoke in adjuratory tones, hoping to persuade the council to reconsider their decision.
The letter was filled with adjuratory appeals for mercy.
His adjuratory plea moved the jury to show lenience.

Synset Examples

in adjuratory terms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imploring - begging urgently or piteously
  • Synonym: beseeching - asking someone urgently and fervently to do something

Etymology

From Latin adjuratus, past participle of adjurare (to swear to, to entreat solemnly).
