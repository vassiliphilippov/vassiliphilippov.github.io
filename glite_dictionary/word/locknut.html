locknut

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑk.nʌt / (AmE), / ˈlɒk.nʌt / (BrE)
a supplementary nut screwed onto a bolt after a primary nut to prevent it from loosening
supplementary nut to prevent loosening

Examples

The mechanic tightened a locknut over the main nut to keep the wheel secure.
It is important to use a locknut in applications where vibration might loosen the assembly.
Without a locknut, the entire structure could have become unstable over time.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: nut - a fastener consisting of a block with an internal screw thread

Etymology

From 'lock' + 'nut', referring to its function of securing or 'locking' another nut in place.
