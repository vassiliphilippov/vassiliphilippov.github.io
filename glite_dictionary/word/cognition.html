cognition

1. NOUN

/ kɑgˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / kɒgˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
the mental process of acquiring knowledge and understanding through thought, experience, and the senses
mental process of knowing and understanding

Examples

Recent research has greatly expanded our understanding of cognition in both humans and animals.
Language plays an important role in shaping a child's cognition during early development.
Impairments in cognition can be early indicators of neurological conditions like dementia.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: perception - the ability to see, hear, or become aware of something through the senses
  • Degree Higher: reasoning - the action of thinking about something in a logical way

Etymology

From Latin 'cognitio' (a getting to know, knowledge), from 'cognoscere' (to learn).
