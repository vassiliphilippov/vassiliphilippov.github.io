transitorily

1. ADV

/ trænˈsɪtəˌrɪli / (AmE), / trænˈsɪtərɪli / (BrE)
For a very brief period; in a temporary or short-lived manner.
for a very brief time

Examples

His enthusiasm faded transitorily before returning in full force.
The law was transitorily in effect during the emergency period.
She was only transitorily interested in the new project before moving on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temporarily - for a limited period of time
  • Antonym: permanently - in a way that lasts or remains indefinitely

Etymology

From 'transitory' + '-ly'; 'transitory' from Latin 'transitorius' (passing away), from 'transire' (to go across).
