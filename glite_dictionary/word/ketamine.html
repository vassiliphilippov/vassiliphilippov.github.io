ketamine

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛtəˌmin / (AmE), / ˈkiːtəmiːn / (BrE)
a powerful general anesthetic and tranquilizer, often used in veterinary medicine and sometimes as a human anesthetic, known for its hallucinogenic effects at high doses
anesthetic and tranquilizer drug
medical

Examples

The veterinarian administered ketamine to sedate the injured dog before surgery.
Some recreational users seek the hallucinogenic effects of ketamine, but misuse can be dangerous.
Doctors sometimes use ketamine as an anesthetic during certain minor medical procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: PCP - a hallucinogenic drug with effects similar to ketamine

Etymology

Derived from the chemical structure: ketone and amine.
