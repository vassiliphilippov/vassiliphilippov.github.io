Albatrellus dispansus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæl.bəˈtrɛl.əs dɪsˈpæn.səs / (AmE), / ˌæl.bəˈtrɛl.əs dɪsˈpan.səs / (BrE)
A rare species of fungus characterized by a large, yellow fruiting body with several individual caps, a broad central stalk, and a fragrant odor, sometimes growing up to 14 inches wide.
a rare large yellow fungus with multiple caps

Examples

The rare Albatrellus dispansus was discovered during a mushroom foray in the deep woods.
Mycology students were excited to examine the unique features of Albatrellus dispansus in the laboratory.
Because of its fragrant odor and unusual size, Albatrellus dispansus is easily distinguishable from other fungi.

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Albatrellus' as the genus name and 'dispansus' a specific epithet used in taxonomy.
