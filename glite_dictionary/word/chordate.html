chordate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔr.deɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.deɪt / (BrE)
any animal belonging to the phylum Chordata, characterized by having a notochord at some stage of development, including all vertebrates and some related animals
animal with notochord or spinal column

Examples

The blue whale is the largest known chordate to have ever lived on Earth.
All birds, mammals, reptiles, amphibians, and fish are classified as chordates.
Scientists discovered fossil evidence of a primitive chordate dating back over 500 million years.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: vertebrate - animal with a backbone or spinal column

Etymology

From Latin *chorda* (cord, string) with the suffix *-ate*, based on the notochord present in these animals.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɔrˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.deɪt / (BrE)
of or relating to animals belonging to the phylum Chordata, which includes vertebrates and some closely related invertebrates.
relating to the phylum Chordata

Examples

Some species exhibit chordate characteristics only during early development.
The biologist compared chordate and non-chordate animal groups in her research.
All fish, birds, and mammals are considered chordate animals due to shared features.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vertebrate - an animal with a backbone
  • Antonym: invertebrate - an animal without a backbone

Etymology

From Latin *chorda* (cord, string), referring to the notochord found in these animals.
