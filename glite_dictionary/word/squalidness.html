squalidness

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɑːlɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈskwɒlɪdnəs / (BrE)
the state of being extremely dirty and unpleasant, usually due to poverty or neglect
state of extreme dirtiness or filth

Examples

The squalidness of the abandoned building made it unfit for habitation.
She was appalled by the squalidness she witnessed in the city's poorest neighborhoods.
Despite its squalidness, the shelter provided a refuge for those with nowhere else to go.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filthiness - the state of being very dirty
  • Antonym: cleanliness - the state or quality of being clean

Etymology

From Latin 'squalidus' (dirty, filthy) + English suffix '-ness'.
