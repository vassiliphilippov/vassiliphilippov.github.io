endotoxin

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛndoʊˈtɑksɪn / (AmE), / ˌendəʊˈtɒksɪn / (BrE)
a toxin present within certain microorganisms that is released only when the cells are broken down or die
toxin released from dead microorganisms
medical

Examples

Certain bacteria release endotoxin only when they die and their cell walls break down.
Exposure to bacterial endotoxin can cause severe inflammation in humans.
The scientist studied how endotoxin triggers the body's immune response.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exotoxin - a toxin released by a microorganism into its surroundings

Etymology

From endo- (inside) + toxin, modeled after German 'Endotoxin'.
