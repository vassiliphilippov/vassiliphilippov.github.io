bordelaise

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɔrdəˈleɪz / (AmE), / ˌbɔːdəˈleɪz / (BrE)
A rich brown sauce made with red wine and beef marrow, often served with beef dishes.
sauce with red wine and marrow

Examples

The chef prepared a classic steak with bordelaise to enhance the flavor of the meat.
For a touch of elegance, pour bordelaise over the roasted beef before serving.
She learned to make bordelaise during her culinary studies in Paris.

Etymology

From French 'bordelaise', feminine of 'bordelais' (of Bordeaux), referring to the Bordeaux region of France.
