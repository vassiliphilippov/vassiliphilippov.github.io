Sciuridae

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈjʊrɪdiː / (AmE), / saɪˈjʊərɪdiː / (BrE)
the scientific family of rodents that includes all true squirrels, such as ground squirrels, chipmunks, marmots, and flying squirrels
the squirrel family of rodents (scientific)

Examples

The family Sciuridae includes more than 270 species worldwide, ranging from chipmunks to marmots.
Flying squirrels, which belong to Sciuridae, have a special membrane for gliding.
Scientists study the geographic distribution of the Sciuridae family to understand their evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squirrel - a small rodent with a bushy tail, belonging to Sciuridae
  • Degree Higher: rodent - a mammal of the order Rodentia

Etymology

From modern Latin Sciuridae, derived from Greek skíouros (squirrel).

2. NOUN

the family of rodents that includes true squirrels, such as ground squirrels, marmots, chipmunks, and flying squirrels
family of squirrels and their relatives

Examples

The Sciuridae family includes a wide variety of squirrels found all over the world.
All chipmunks belong to the Sciuridae family within the rodent order.
Biologists study the evolutionary relationships among species in the Sciuridae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squirrel - a small rodent with a bushy tail, member of Sciuridae

Etymology

From modern Latin Sciuridae, from Greek skiouros (squirrel).
