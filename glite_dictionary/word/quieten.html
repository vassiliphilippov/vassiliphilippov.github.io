to quieten

1. VERB

/ ˈkwaɪ.ə.tən / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪ.ə.tən / (BrE)
to make or become calm, less noisy, or still; to cause someone or something to become quiet
make or become calm or quiet

Examples

She tried to quieten the children before bedtime, but they were still excited.
The teacher asked the class to quieten down as the exam was about to start.
A gentle lullaby can quieten a restless baby in minutes.

Synset Examples

quiet the dragons of worry and fear

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calm - make or become quiet and relaxed
  • Synonym: to soothe - gently calm or reduce agitation
  • Antonym: to agitate - make someone troubled or nervous

Etymology

From English 'quiet' plus the suffix '-en' meaning 'to make.'

2. VERB

/ ˈkwaɪ.ə.tən / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪ.ɪ.tən / (BrE)
to become quiet or to make someone or something become quiet or quieter
become or make quiet

Examples

It took several minutes for the children to quieten after the fireworks ended.
The wind began to quieten as the evening wore on, bringing a calm to the lake.

Synset Examples

The audience fell silent when the speaker entered

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quiet - to (cause to) become quiet

Etymology

From "quiet" + suffix "-en" to form a verb.

3. VERB

/ ˈkwaɪ.ə.tən / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪ.ə.tən / (BrE)
to make someone or something quiet; to cause to become less noisy or agitated
make or become quiet

Examples

The teacher managed to quieten the class after the lunch break.
She tried to quieten the baby by singing a lullaby.
It took several minutes to quieten the excited crowd.

Synset Examples

Please silence the children in the church!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silence - to make someone or something stop making noise or talking
  • Synonym: to shush - to tell someone to be quiet, often by saying 'shush'

Etymology

From 'quiet' (adj.) + English verb-forming suffix '-en'.
