nonremittal

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑːn.rɪˈmɪt.əl / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.rɪˈmɪt.əl / (BrE)
the failure to pay or send money that is due; neglecting to meet a financial obligation.
failure to pay a financial obligation

Examples

The company faced penalties due to its nonremittal of required taxes.
Repeated nonremittal of rent can result in eviction.
The contract included strict consequences for nonremittal of payment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: remittance - the act of sending money, especially to settle a debt

Etymology

From the prefix 'non-' (not) and 'remittal' (the sending of money), from Latin 'remittere' (to send back).

2. NOUN

/ ˌnɑːnrɪˈmɪtəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnrɪˈmɪtəl / (BrE)
the loss incurred when a debt is not paid; the failure to receive payment owed.
loss from unpaid debt

Examples

The company faced significant nonremittal after several clients failed to pay their invoices.
Insurance policies often exclude claims arising from nonremittal of loans.
The bank recorded a large nonremittal in its financial statements due to uncollected debts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: default - failure to repay a debt

Etymology

Formed from 'non-' (not) and 'remittal' (the act of sending payment).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13321164-n
Synset POS: n
