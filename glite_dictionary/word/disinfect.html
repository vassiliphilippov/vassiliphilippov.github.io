to disinfect

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪsɪnˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈfɛkt / (BrE)
to clean something using chemicals or other means to destroy microorganisms or pathogens
clean to destroy germs
medical

Examples

Before stitching the cut, the doctor will disinfect the wound thoroughly.
Please disinfect all kitchen surfaces after preparing raw meat.
Hospitals routinely disinfect medical equipment to prevent the spread of infection.

Synset Examples

disinfect a wound

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sterilize - make free from all living microorganisms
  • Antonym: to contaminate - make impure by exposure to or addition of a poisonous or polluting substance

Etymology

From Latin 'dis-' (removal) + 'infectare' (to taint or spoil).
