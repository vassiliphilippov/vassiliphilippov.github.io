decampment

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈkæmpmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈkæmpmənt / (BrE)
the act of breaking camp or leaving a place where one has been settled temporarily, especially with others
the act of breaking camp

Examples

The army's decampment was swift and orderly in response to the threat.
After a rainy night, the group's decampment from the campsite began at dawn.
The sudden decampment of protesters surprised the authorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: departure - the act of leaving a place
  • Antonym: arrival - the act of coming to a place

Etymology

From French 'décamper', based on 'camp', meaning to break camp.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈkæmpmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈkæmpmənt / (BrE)
the act of secretly leaving a place, especially to escape arrest or avoid detection
secret departure, often to avoid arrest

Examples

The sudden decampment of the suspect left the authorities puzzled.
After the embezzlement was discovered, there was a swift decampment from the office.
Rumors of an impending investigation triggered the decampment of several executives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flight - the act of fleeing or running away
  • Antonym: arrest - the act of being taken into custody by law enforcement

Etymology

From French décamper (to break camp, depart suddenly), based on Latin campus (field).
