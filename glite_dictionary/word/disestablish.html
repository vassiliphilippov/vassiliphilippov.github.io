to disestablish

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪs.ɪˈstæb.lɪʃ / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ɪˈstæb.lɪʃ / (BrE)
to deprive an established church of its official status as the state church
deprive a church of official state status

Examples

The government voted to disestablish the Church of England in Wales.
Reformers campaigned to disestablish the state-supported church.
He argued that to disestablish the church would create religious equality.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to establish - to set up an organization or system on a permanent basis

Etymology

From 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'establish'.
