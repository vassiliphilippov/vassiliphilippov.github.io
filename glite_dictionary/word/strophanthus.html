Strophanthus

1. NOUN

/ stroʊˈfænθəs / (AmE), / strəʊˈfænθəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical shrubs, woody vines, and small trees native to Asia and Africa, some species of which are known for their toxic seeds used as arrow poisons.
genus of tropical Asian and African plants

Examples

The seeds of Strophanthus plants have historically been used to make arrow poison.
Botanists discovered a new species within the genus Strophanthus in central Africa.
Several Strophanthus vines are cultivated for their attractive, twisted flowers.

Etymology

From Greek 'strophos' (a twisted band) and 'anthos' (flower), referring to the twisted corolla lobes of its flowers.
