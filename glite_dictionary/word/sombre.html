sombre

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɑːmbər / (AmE), / ˈsɒmbə / (BrE)
serious, sad, or gloomy in mood or appearance; lacking brightness or cheerfulness
gloomy or serious in mood or appearance

Examples

The funeral was marked by a sombre silence throughout the hall.
He wore a sombre suit to the important meeting.
After hearing the bad news, she became quite sombre.

Synset Examples

a suit of somber black
a somber mood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: somber - Alternative (American) spelling of 'sombre'
  • Antonym: cheerful - happy or showing good spirits

Etymology

From French sombre (dark, gloomy), from Latin subumbrare (to shadow)

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɑːmbər / (AmE), / ˈsɒmbə / (BrE)
Having a dull or dark colour; lacking brightness or vibrancy.
dull or dark in colour
british

Examples

The room looked sombre with its faded curtains and shadowy corners.
She wore a sombre brown dress that matched the mood of the funeral.
The overcast sky gave a sombre appearance to the entire city.

Synset Examples

drab faded curtains
sober Puritan grey
children in somber brown clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drab - Lacking brightness or interest; dull or dreary.
  • Synonym: dull - Lacking brightness, vividness, or sheen.
  • Antonym: vivid - Producing powerful feelings or strong, clear images in the mind; bright and intense in colour.

Etymology

From French 'sombre' (dark, gloomy), from Latin 'subumbrare' (to shade).
