unaccustomed

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈkʌs.təmd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈkʌs.təmd / (BrE)
not familiar with something; not used to something; unusual or unfamiliar.
not familiar or unusual

Examples

He was unaccustomed to the hot and humid climate of the tropics.
She found the unaccustomed silence in the house unsettling.
After years of routine, the unaccustomed freedom felt both exhilarating and strange.

Synset Examples

an unaccustomed pleasure
many varieties of unaccustomed foods
a new budget of unaccustomed austerity

Related Terms

  • Antonym: accustomed - familiar with something; used to it

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'accustomed' (from Old French acostumer, from Latin accustumare).
