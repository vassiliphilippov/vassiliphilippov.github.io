Halchidhoma

1. NOUN

/ hal.tʃiˈðoʊ.mə / (AmE), / hal.tʃɪˈðəʊ.mə / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people that lived along the Colorado River near the mouth of the Gila River, historically allied with the Maricopa
a Native American people near the Colorado River

Examples

The Halchidhoma moved upriver after conflicts with neighboring groups.
Anthropological studies document the culture and traditions of the Halchidhoma.
Some members of the Halchidhoma eventually joined the Maricopa community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Maricopa - a Native American people historically allied with the Halchidhoma
