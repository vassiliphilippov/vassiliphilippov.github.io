pretentious

1. ADJ

/ prɪˈtɛn(t)ʃəs / (AmE), / prɪˈtɛnʃəs / (BrE)
attempting to impress others by pretending to be more important, sophisticated, or knowledgeable than is actually the case
trying to seem more important or impressive

Examples

The restaurant was decorated in a pretentious style with gold-plated furniture and crystal chandeliers.
Her pretentious speech was meant to impress the audience, but it sounded insincere.
He wore a pretentious outfit that didn't suit the laid-back atmosphere of the party.

Synset Examples

an ostentatious sable coat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostentatious - showy, designed to impress; similar in meaning to 'pretentious'
  • Antonym: modest - not showing off; the opposite of 'pretentious'

Etymology

From French 'prétentieux', from Latin 'praetendere' (to pretend).

2. ADJ

/ prɪˈtɛn.ʃəs / (AmE), / prɪˈtɛn.tʃəs / (BrE)
Attempting to impress others by affecting greater importance, talent, culture, etc., than is actually possessed; showy or ostentatious.
attempting to impress, showy or ostentatious

Examples

The restaurant has a pretentious décor, with gold-plated ornaments and velvet drapes everywhere.
He made a pretentious speech filled with grand words but little substance.
Many critics dismissed the film as pretentious and lacking genuine emotion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostentatious - characterized by vulgar or pretentious display
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance

Etymology

From French 'prétentieux', from Latin 'praetendere' (to pretend, to put forward).

Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ prɪˈtɛnʃəs / (AmE), / prɪˈtɛnʃəs / (BrE)
Attempting to appear more important, intelligent, or cultured than is actually the case, often undeservedly so.
attempting to impress by affecting greater importance

Examples

The critic dismissed the film as pretentious and lacking genuine emotion.
He wore a pretentious suit to impress his colleagues at the meeting.
Their conversation was full of pretentious references to obscure artists and philosophers.

Synset Examples

a pretentious country house
a pretentious fraud
a pretentious scholarly edition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: showy - Displaying wealth, knowledge, etc., in a way that is intended to attract attention or admiration but often lacks substance
  • Antonym: unassuming - Not pretentious or arrogant; modest

Etymology

From French 'prétentieux', from Latin 'praetendere' (to pretend).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
