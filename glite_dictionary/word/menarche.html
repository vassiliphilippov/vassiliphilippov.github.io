menarche

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈnɑrki / (AmE), / mɪˈnɑːki / (BrE)
the first occurrence of menstruation in a young woman
first menstruation in a young woman
medical

Examples

The average age for menarche has declined over the past century in many countries.
Some girls experience menarche as early as age nine, while others may start later.
Health education classes often address the physical and emotional aspects of menarche.

Etymology

From Greek 'men' (month) + 'arche' (beginning).
