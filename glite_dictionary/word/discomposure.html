discomposure

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪskəmˈpoʊʒər / (AmE), / ˌdɪskəmˈpəʊʒə / (BrE)
a state of feeling anxious, embarrassed, or disturbed; a loss of calmness or composure
anxious embarrassment or disturbance

Examples

She tried to hide her discomposure when asked an uncomfortable question.
His sudden arrival caused visible discomposure among the guests.
Despite her discomposure, she managed to speak clearly and calmly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: composure - calmness or self-control
  • Synonym: agitation - a state of nervousness or unrest

Etymology

From dis- (opposite) + composure (from French 'composure', from 'composer', to compose).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪskəmˈpoʊʒər / (AmE), / ˌdɪskəmˈpəʊʒə / (BrE)
A state of being disturbed, agitated, or lacking calmness; the condition of being unsettled or perturbed.
state of being unsettled or perturbed

Examples

The unexpected announcement caused visible discomposure among the guests.
He tried to hide his discomposure, but his trembling hands betrayed him.
Despite his sudden discomposure, he managed to deliver his speech.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: composure - calm and self-control in situations
  • Synonym: agitation - state of being agitated or disturbed
  • Synonym: perturbation - state of anxiety or uneasiness

Etymology

From dis- (prefix expressing reversal) + composure, after late 17th century.
