astigmatism

1. NOUN

/ əˈstɪɡməˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈstɪɡmətɪzəm / (BrE)
a defect of the eye or lens which causes distorted or blurred vision due to uneven curvature of the cornea or lens
eye defect causing blurred vision
medical

Examples

She was diagnosed with astigmatism after complaining of frequent headaches and blurred vision.
Corrective lenses can help people with astigmatism see more clearly.
If left untreated, astigmatism can make reading and driving difficult.

Etymology

From Greek a- (not) + stigmat-, stem of stigma (mark, point).

2. NOUN

/ əˈstɪɡmətɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈstɪɡmətɪzəm / (BrE)
a defect of an optical system, especially the eye, in which light rays from a single point fail to converge at one focal point, causing blurred or distorted vision
defect causing blurred vision
medical

Examples

Her doctor explained that her blurred vision was caused by astigmatism, which affected the shape of her eye.
Many people wear corrective lenses to improve their astigmatism.
The optometrist detected a mild astigmatism during the routine eye examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myopia - condition of nearsightedness (difficulty seeing distant objects)
  • Synonym: hyperopia - condition of farsightedness (difficulty seeing near objects)

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'stigma' (point, mark) + '-ism'; first used in optics in the 19th century.
