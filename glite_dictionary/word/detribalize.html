to detribalize

1. VERB

/ diˈtraɪbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈtraɪbəlaɪz / (BrE)
to cause members of a tribe or a community to lose their traditional culture, customs, or social structure, especially as a result of outside influence
cause loss of tribal identity/culture

Examples

Colonial policies often sought to detribalize indigenous peoples by discouraging their traditional practices.
Efforts to detribalize the region had lasting effects on the community's cultural heritage.
Missionary schools played a key role in attempts to detribalize local children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assimilate - cause to become similar to the dominant culture

Etymology

From de- (removal) + tribal + -ize.
