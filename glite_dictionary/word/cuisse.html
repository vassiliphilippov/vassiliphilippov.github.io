cuisse

1. NOUN

/ kwis / (AmE), / kwiÀês / (BrE)
A piece of armor that covers and protects the thigh.
thigh armor plate
archaic

Examples

The knight's cuisse was dented during the fierce battle.
She inspected the cuisses for any signs of rust before the reenactment.
Full plate armor included a cuisse to shield the upper leg.

Etymology

From Old French 'cuisse' (thigh), ultimately from Latin 'coxa' (hip, thigh).
