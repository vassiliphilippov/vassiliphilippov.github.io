Acocanthera oblongifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˌækoʊˈkænθərə ɒˌblɒŋɡɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˌækəʊˈkanθərə ɒˌblɒŋɡɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A medium-sized shrubby tree native to South Africa, characterized by thick leathery evergreen leaves, white or pink flowers, and globose, usually two-seeded, purplish-black fruits.
South African shrubby tree with leathery leaves and dark fruits

Examples

The Acocanthera oblongifolia is often grown in gardens for its attractive glossy leaves and flowers.
Many animals avoid the berries of Acocanthera oblongifolia due to their toxicity.
Researchers study Acocanthera oblongifolia for its medicinal properties and ecological role in South African forests.

Etymology

From the genus name Acocanthera (from Greek 'akokantha', meaning 'thorny'), and Latin 'oblongifolia' (having oblong leaves).
