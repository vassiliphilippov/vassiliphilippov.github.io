feeblemindedness

1. NOUN

/ ˌfiːbəlˈmaɪndɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˌfiːblˈmaɪndɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the state or condition of having severe mental deficiency; intellectual incapacity, used especially in historical or outdated contexts.
severe mental deficiency (historical)
archaic offensive

Examples

In the early 20th century, institutions were created specifically for people exhibiting feeblemindedness.
The novel's portrayal of feeblemindedness reflects outdated societal attitudes toward mental health.
Historical policies often discriminated against individuals perceived as having feeblemindedness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mental retardation - intellectual disability; outdated medical term
  • Synonym: intellectual disability - significantly reduced ability to understand new or complex information and learn new skills

Etymology

From feebleminded (feeble + minded) + -ness; used in early 20th-century psychological and legal texts.
