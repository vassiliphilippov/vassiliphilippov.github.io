flatterer

1. NOUN

/ ˈflæt̬ɚɚ / (AmE), / ˈflætərə / (BrE)
a person who gives excessive or insincere praise, often to gain favor or advantage
person who gives insincere praise

Examples

The flatterer complimented his boss constantly, hoping for a promotion.
She saw right through the flatterer and ignored his insincere praise.
Politicians often attract flatterers who want to win their favor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sycophant - a person who acts obsequiously toward someone important to gain advantage
  • Antonym: critic - a person who expresses disapproval or finds fault

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'flatter' + agent suffix '-er'.
