sensitiser

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnsɪˌtaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsɪˌtaɪzə / (BrE)
(chemistry) A substance, other than a catalyst, that promotes the initiation of a catalytic reaction.
chemical that initiates a catalytic reaction
medical

Examples

The presence of a sensitiser in the mixture made the reaction proceed more rapidly.
Researchers added a sensitiser to lower the activation energy of the process.
Some industrial processes require a sensitiser to initiate specific chemical transformations.

Etymology

From 'sensitise' + '-er', referring to something that makes another substance more responsive or reactive.
