downstroke

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊnˌstroʊk / (AmE), / ˈdaʊn.strəʊk / (BrE)
A stroke or movement made in a downward direction, especially in handwriting, playing a musical instrument, or machinery.
downward stroke or movement

Examples

The calligrapher's downstroke was bold and confident.
When strumming the guitar, try alternating between downstroke and upstroke for a smoother rhythm.
He noticed that the machine's downstroke was more forceful than its upstroke.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upstroke - a stroke made in an upward direction
