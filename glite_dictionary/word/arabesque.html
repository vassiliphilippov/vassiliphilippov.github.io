arabesque

1. NOUN

/ ˌærəˈbɛsk / (AmE), / ˌærəˈbɛsk / (BrE)
a ballet position in which one leg is extended horizontally backward while the arms are outstretched, creating a conventional pose
ballet position with one leg raised behind

Examples

She moved gracefully into an arabesque at the end of her solo.
The teacher corrected his arabesque, insisting he lift his leg higher.
Many ballet students struggle to achieve a balanced arabesque on pointe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attitude - a ballet position with one leg lifted, bent at the knee

Etymology

From French 'arabesque', originally describing an ornate artistic style, later applied to ballet positions.

2. NOUN

/ ˌær.əˈbɛsk / (AmE), / ˌær.əˈbɛsk / (BrE)
a decorative design or pattern featuring interlaced, flowing lines and stylized foliage, often found in art and architecture
ornament with interlaced foliage design

Examples

The ceiling was richly decorated with a painted arabesque that enchanted the visitors.
The artisan carved delicate arabesques into the wooden door panels.
She admired the intricate arabesque patterns on the ancient tilework.

Etymology

From French *arabesque*, from Italian *arabesco* (in Arabic style), from *arabo* (Arab).
