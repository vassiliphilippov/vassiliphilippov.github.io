Eimeria

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈmɪəriə / (AmE), / aɪˈmɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of parasitic protozoa that infects the digestive tracts of vertebrates and some higher invertebrates, often causing disease.
genus of parasitic protozoa
medical

Examples

Coccidiosis in poultry is commonly caused by various species of Eimeria.
Veterinarians often test for Eimeria when diagnosing digestive issues in livestock.
The study focused on the life cycle of Eimeria in rabbits.

Etymology

From the genus name given by Eduard Heinrich Eimer, German zoologist.
