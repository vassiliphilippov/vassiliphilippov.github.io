dolomite

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊ.ləˌmaɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɒl.əˌmaɪt / (BrE)
a light-colored mineral composed of calcium magnesium carbonate, used as a source of magnesium and in ceramics and fertilizers
a mineral; calcium magnesium carbonate

Examples

The mountain was rich in dolomite, making it valuable for mining operations.
Farmers often use crushed dolomite to improve soil quality and pH balance.
The pottery factory sources dolomite for its ceramic production processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calcite - a carbonate mineral and the most stable polymorph of calcium carbonate
  • Synonym: limestone - a sedimentary rock mainly composed of calcite or aragonite

Etymology

Named after French geologist Déodat de Dolomieu.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊ.ləˌmaɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɒl.əˌmaɪt / (BrE)
a sedimentary rock similar to limestone but containing a high proportion of magnesium carbonate
magnesium-rich sedimentary rock

Examples

The mountain range is composed mainly of dolomite, giving it a distinctive appearance.
When viewed under a microscope, dolomite can be distinguished from limestone by its crystal structure.
Quarries have extracted dolomite for use in construction and as a source of magnesium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limestone - a sedimentary rock composed mainly of calcium carbonate
  • Synonym: marble - a metamorphic rock formed from limestone or dolomite

Etymology

Named after French geologist Déodat de Dolomieu (1750–1801), who first described the mineral.
