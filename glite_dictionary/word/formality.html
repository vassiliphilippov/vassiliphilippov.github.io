formality

1. NOUN

/ fɔrˈmæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / fɔːˈmæl.ə.ti / (BrE)
An established procedure or requirement, especially one following etiquette or social custom.
an established procedure or social custom

Examples

We had to go through the usual formalities before entering the country.
The wedding was small, with few formalities and little ceremony.
He dispensed with the formalities and got straight to the point.

Synset Examples

a mere formality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: custom - a traditional or habitual practice
  • Antonym: informality - lack of formality or ceremony

Etymology

From Latin 'formalis' (formal), from 'forma' (form).

2. NOUN

/ fɔrˈmæləti / (AmE), / fɔːˈmælɪti / (BrE)
the quality or condition of complying with established rules, procedures, or conventions, especially in official or ceremonial contexts
compliance with formal rules

Examples

The judge insisted on strict formality throughout the hearing.
Shaking hands at the start of negotiations is a customary formality.
Guests were expected to observe every formality during the official dinner.

Synset Examples

courtroom formality

Related Terms

  • Antonym: informality - the state of being relaxed and not following strict rules or conventions
  • Synonym: ceremony - a formal event following conventional rules

Etymology

From French 'formalité', from Latin 'formalis' (formal).

3. NOUN

/ fɔrˈmæləti / (AmE), / fɔːˈmælɪti / (BrE)
an action, procedure, or requirement carried out according to custom, etiquette, or rule, often as a mere convention rather than for its practical value
requirement of etiquette or custom

Examples

Submitting the application is just a formality, as you have already been approved.
They shook hands as a formality at the end of the meeting.
Despite the occasion, all the usual formalities were observed.

Synset Examples

a mere formality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ceremony - a formal act or set of acts performed for a special occasion
  • Antonym: informality - the absence of ceremony or strict observance of formal requirements

Etymology

From French formalité, from Late Latin formalitas (shape, form).

4. NOUN

/ fɔːrˈmælɪti / (AmE), / fɔːˈmælɪti / (BrE)
a manner of behaving or speaking that strictly observes established rules, customs, or ceremonies; the quality of being formal
strict observance of customs or ceremonies

Examples

The formality of his introduction impressed everyone present at the meeting.
Despite the relaxed setting, there was a distinct formality in her tone.
Every aspect of the wedding was conducted with great formality and attention to tradition.

Synset Examples

the formality of his voice made the others pay him close attention

Related Terms

  • Antonym: informality - lack of formality or ceremony
  • Synonym: ceremony - a formal event or act performed according to tradition or rules

Etymology

From French 'formalité', from Latin 'formalitas' (form, manner).
