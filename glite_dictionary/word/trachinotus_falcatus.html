Trachinotus falcatus

1. NOUN

/ trəˌkɪnoʊtəs fælˈkeɪtəs / (AmE), / trəˌkɪnətəs fælˈkeɪtəs / (BrE)
a large game fish of the family Carangidae, found in the western Atlantic Ocean, especially around the West Indies, prized by anglers
a large Atlantic game fish (the permit)

Examples

Anglers travel to the Caribbean hoping to catch a Trachinotus falcatus during the peak season.
The silvery body and forked tail make Trachinotus falcatus easy to recognize on a fishing trip.
Scientists have studied the migration patterns of Trachinotus falcatus in the western Atlantic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permit - a large game fish, Trachinotus falcatus, of the western Atlantic

Etymology

From Latin: 'trachinus' (rough) and 'falcatus' (sickle-shaped), referring to the fish's fin shape.
