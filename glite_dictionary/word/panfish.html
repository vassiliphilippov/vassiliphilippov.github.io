panfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈpænˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpænˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
Any of various small edible fish, especially those caught by anglers and not commonly sold commercially.
small edible fish for recreational fishing

Examples

He went fishing this weekend and caught several panfish in the lake.
Many anglers enjoy frying up fresh panfish for a simple meal.
The region's lakes are home to a variety of panfish, including bluegill and crappie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunfish - a common type of panfish in North America
  • Synonym: perch - a species often considered a panfish

Etymology

From 'pan' (referring to frying pan) and 'fish', indicating fish small enough to cook whole in a pan.
