scourer

1. NOUN

/ ˈskaʊɚɚ / (AmE), / ˈskaʊərə / (BrE)
a person who cleans a surface thoroughly by scrubbing or scouring
person who cleans by scrubbing

Examples

As a professional scourer, she made sure every corner of the kitchen shone.
The company hired a scourer to remove years of grime from the old tiles.
In the past, a scourer was often employed to clean public buildings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cleaner - a person or thing that cleans
  • Synonym: scour - to clean or brighten a surface by rubbing it hard

Etymology

From the verb 'scour' plus the agent suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskaʊərɚ / (AmE), / ˈskaʊərə / (BrE)
a person who travels widely and energetically, often in search of something
person who travels widely with energy

Examples

As a young scourer, he roamed across continents in search of adventure.
Reputation as a scourer brought him both fame and a trove of fascinating stories.
Legends grew around the old scourer who was never seen in the same city twice.

Synset Examples

he was a scourer of the seven seas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wanderer - someone who travels aimlessly from place to place
  • Synonym: explorer - a person who investigates unknown regions

Etymology

From the verb 'scour' meaning 'to move over an area quickly in search', plus the agent suffix '-er'.

Synset POS: n
