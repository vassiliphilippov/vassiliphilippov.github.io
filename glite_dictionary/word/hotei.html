Hotei

1. PROPN

/ hoʊˈteɪ / (AmE), / həʊˈteɪ / (BrE)
A figure in Japanese mythology, one of the Seven Gods of Happiness, often depicted as a cheerful, fat, bald man with a large belly, symbolizing contentment and abundance.
one of the Seven Gods of Happiness in Japanese tradition

Examples

A statue of Hotei sits by the entrance to the restaurant, welcoming guests with a smile.
Many people believe that rubbing the belly of Hotei brings good fortune.
In Japanese folklore, Hotei is known as the god of happiness and abundance.

Etymology

From Japanese 布袋 (Hotei), the name of the deity, originally from Chinese Budai (布袋).
