Derain

1. PROPN

/ dəˈrɛ̃ / (AmE), / dəˈrɛ̃ / (BrE)
André Derain (1880–1954), a French painter known for co-founding Fauvism and pioneering the use of bold color.
French painter, Fauvism co-founder

Examples

André Derain was instrumental in the development of Fauvism alongside Henri Matisse.
The vibrant landscapes painted by Derain marked a turning point in modern art.
Several of Derain's masterpieces are exhibited in major European galleries.
