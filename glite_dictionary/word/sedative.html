sedative

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛdəˌtɪv / (AmE), / ˈsɛd.ə.tɪv / (BrE)
having a calming, soothing, or tranquilizing effect, especially in reducing anxiety or making someone feel sleepy
calming or tranquilizing; soothing

Examples

The tea has a sedative effect and can help you relax before bed.
Some herbal remedies are known for their sedative properties.
She took a sedative medication to help calm her nerves.

Synset Examples

valium has a tranquilizing effect
took a hot drink with sedative properties before going to bed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stimulant - having an energizing or arousing effect

Etymology

From Latin sedativus, from sedare ‘to settle’ or ‘to calm’.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛdətɪv / (AmE), / ˈsɛdətɪv / (BrE)
A drug or substance that calms or soothes a person by reducing excitement or anxiety, often used to aid sleep or relaxation.
drug that calms or soothes
medical

Examples

The doctor administered a sedative to help the patient relax before surgery.
Because he was anxious about flying, his physician prescribed a mild sedative.
The strong sedative quickly eased her restlessness after the accident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tranquilizer - a drug used to reduce tension or anxiety
  • Antonym: stimulant - a drug that increases alertness and energy

Etymology

From Latin sedativus (calming, soothing), from sedare (to calm, settle).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
