brogue

1. NOUN

/ broʊɡ / (AmE), / brəʊɡ / (BrE)
A strong, heavy shoe, typically made of leather and often decorated with perforations, originally worn in the countryside.
heavy leather shoe with decorative perforations

Examples

He polished his brogues before heading to the meeting.
Traditional Scottish dancers often wear brogues as part of their costume.
The sturdy brogue is perfect for walking in the countryside.

Etymology

From Irish Gaelic 'bróg' or Scottish Gaelic 'bròg' meaning 'shoe', from Old Norse 'brók' (leg covering, breeches).
