podocarp

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊdəˌkɑrp / (AmE), / ˈpɒdəˌkɑːp / (BrE)
An evergreen tree or shrub of the genus Podocarpus, native to the Southern Hemisphere and bearing a fleshy fruit with a single hard seed.
evergreen tree or shrub of genus Podocarpus

Examples

The podocarp thrives in the temperate forests of New Zealand and Australia.
Botanists studied a rare podocarp specimen found in the wild.
Gardeners appreciate the podocarp for its attractive evergreen foliage and unique fruit.

Etymology

From New Latin *Podocarpus*, from Greek *pous* (foot) and *karpos* (fruit), referring to the stalked fruit.
