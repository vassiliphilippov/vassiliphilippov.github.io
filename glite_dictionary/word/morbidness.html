morbidness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔːrbɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈmɔːbɪdnəs / (BrE)
An abnormally gloomy or unhealthy state of mind, especially involving excessive interest in disturbing subjects.
abnormally gloomy or unhealthy state of mind

Examples

Her morbidness made it difficult for her to enjoy even happy occasions.
There was a certain morbidness in his fascination with tragic events.
Therapists are trained to recognize signs of morbidness in their patients.

Synset Examples

his fear of being alone verges on morbidity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morbidity - abnormally gloomy state of mind
  • Antonym: normality - the state of being normal or healthy in mind

Etymology

From Latin 'morbidus' (diseased), from 'morbus' (disease).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrbɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈmɔːbɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being morbid, especially in reference to unhealthiness or a fascination with unpleasant subjects
state of being morbid or unhealthy

Examples

The morbidness of his obsession with death worried his family and friends.
Many critics noted the morbidness in the themes of the novel.
Doctors warned about the morbidness of certain lifestyle habits affecting long-term health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morbidity - the condition of being diseased or unhealthy
  • Antonym: healthiness - the state of being healthy and beneficial

Etymology

From Latin 'morbidus' (diseased) + English '-ness'.
