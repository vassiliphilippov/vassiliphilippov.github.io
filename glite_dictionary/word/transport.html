transport

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrænspɔrt / (AmE), / ˈtrænspɔːt / (BrE)
the act of moving people or goods from one place to another
the act of moving something

Examples

The transport of goods across the country requires careful planning.
A reliable transport system is essential for economic growth.
New regulations affect the transport of hazardous materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conveyance - the action or process of transporting someone or something from one place to another

Etymology

From Latin trans- (across) + portare (to carry).

6. NOUN

/ ˈtrænspɔrt / (AmE), / ˈtrænspɔːt / (BrE)
the movement of molecules, along with their energy and momentum, across boundaries such as between layers of fluid or through cell membranes

Examples

Molecular transport across the cell membrane is critical for nutrient uptake.
The process of heat transport in fluids involves the exchange of energy between layers.
Active transport allows cells to move ions against a concentration gradient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diffusion - the passive movement of molecules from an area of higher concentration to one of lower concentration
  • Synonym: osmosis - the diffusion of water through a semipermeable membrane
  • Degree Higher: active transport - movement of substances across membranes using energy, usually against a concentration gradient
