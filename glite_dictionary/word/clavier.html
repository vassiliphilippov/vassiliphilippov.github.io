clavier

1. NOUN

/ kləˈviɚ / (AmE), / kləˈvɪə / (BrE)
A keyboard, especially one of a stringed musical instrument such as a piano, organ, or harpsichord.
keyboard of a musical instrument

Examples

Johann Sebastian Bach wrote many pieces for the clavier during the Baroque period.
After tuning the clavier, the musician played a beautiful sonata.
The antique clavier was displayed in the music museum next to the harpsichord.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keyboard - set of keys on a musical instrument or computer

Etymology

From French 'clavier' (keyboard), from Latin 'clavis' (key).

2. NOUN

/ kləˈvɪr / (AmE), / klæˈvɪə / (BrE)
a keyboard musical instrument, especially one from the historical family of stringed instruments such as the harpsichord, clavichord, or early piano
keyboard musical instrument

Examples

The museum has a remarkable collection of historical claviers dating back to the 17th century.
She prefers the delicate touch of the clavier to the power of the modern piano.
Many Baroque composers wrote music specifically for the clavier rather than the organ.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keyboard - a musical instrument played by means of a keyboard

Etymology

From French 'clavier' (keyboard), from Old French, from Latin 'clavis' (key).
