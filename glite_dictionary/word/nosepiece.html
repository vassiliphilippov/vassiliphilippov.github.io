nosepiece

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊzˌpiːs / (AmE), / ˈnəʊzˌpiːs / (BrE)
a strap on a bridle that passes over an animal's nose to help control it
strap on a bridle over an animal's nose

Examples

The horse's nosepiece was adjusted for a better fit before the ride.
She fastened the nosepiece securely as part of the bridle.
A worn nosepiece can cause discomfort to the animal.

Etymology

From 'nose' + 'piece', meaning a part covering or relating to the nose.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊzˌpis / (AmE), / ˈnəʊzˌpiːs / (BrE)
the part of a pair of glasses that connects the two lenses and rests on the nose
part of glasses connecting the lenses

Examples

He had to get his glasses fixed when the nosepiece snapped in half.
My new spectacles have a softer nosepiece for extra comfort.
The nosepiece on these sunglasses is adjustable for a better fit.

3. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊsˌpis / (AmE), / ˈnəʊs.piːs / (BrE)
an armor plate or protective piece that covers or shields the nose, especially as part of a helmet
armor plate protecting the nose

Examples

The knight's nosepiece saved him from a potentially serious injury during battle.
Many medieval helmets featured a sturdy nosepiece for additional face protection.
Experts restored the corroded nosepiece on the ancient suit of armor for the museum display.
