Helotiales

1. NOUN

/ hɛˌloʊtiˈeɪliz / (AmE), / hɛˌlɒtɪˈeɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of fungi characterized by producing spore-containing asci in disk-shaped or goblet-shaped fruiting bodies (apothecia)
order of fungi with disk-shaped apothecia

Examples

The Helotiales comprise numerous species distributed across diverse habitats.
Many fungi in the Helotiales form symbiotic relationships with plants.
Identification of Helotiales relies heavily on the shape of their apothecia.

Etymology

From the genus name Helotium, combined with the taxonomic suffix -ales indicating an order.
