plant

1. NOUN

/ plænt / (AmE), / plɑːnt / (BrE)
a building or group of buildings where an industrial or manufacturing process is carried out
industrial facility or factory

Examples

The company invested millions to upgrade their old plant and increase production.
A new car assembly plant opened on the outskirts of town last year.
The chemical plant was shut down for safety inspections after the incident.

Synset Examples

they built a large plant to manufacture automobiles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: factory - a building or group of buildings where goods are manufactured
  • Degree Lower: workshop - a room or building where things are made or repaired using machines and tools
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ plænt / (AmE), / plɑːnt / (BrE)
to put seeds, seedlings, or plants into the ground so that they can grow
put seeds or plants in the ground

Examples

Every spring, we plant vegetables in our backyard garden.
If you plant the seeds too deep, they may not sprout.
She helped her grandfather plant strawberries along the fence.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sow - to plant seeds by scattering them on or in the earth

From Old English 'plante' (the noun; see also Latin 'planta' meaning sprout, cutting).

7. NOUN

/ plænt / (AmE), / plɑːnt / (BrE)
A living organism that grows in the earth or in water, typically with green leaves, and lacks the ability to move actively, such as a tree, shrub, grass, or fern.
living organism lacking locomotion

Examples

The plant in the corner needs to be watered every week.
Photosynthesis allows a plant to produce its own food from sunlight.
A cactus is a type of plant adapted to dry conditions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: animal - a living organism that can move and respond to its environment
  • Degree Lower: fungus - a group of living organisms distinct from plants and animals, including molds and mushrooms

Etymology

From Old English plante, from Latin planta (sprout, shoot, plant).
