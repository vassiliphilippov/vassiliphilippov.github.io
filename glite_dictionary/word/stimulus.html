stimulus

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪmjələs / (AmE), / ˈstɪmjʊləs / (BrE)
something that provokes a response or arouses activity, especially in the body or mind; an event or information that encourages action or reaction.
something that provokes a response or activity

Examples

The financial crisis acted as a stimulus for reform across the banking sector.
A sudden loud noise can serve as a stimulus for the startle reflex.
Exposure to sunlight is an important stimulus for the production of vitamin D in the skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incentive - something that motivates or encourages someone to do something
  • Antonym: deterrent - something that discourages or is intended to discourage someone from taking action

Etymology

From Latin stimulus (a goad, spur, or incentive).
