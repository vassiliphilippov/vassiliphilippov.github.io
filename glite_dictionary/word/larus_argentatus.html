Larus argentatus

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛrəs ˌɑrdʒənˈteɪtəs / (AmE), / ˈlɑːrəs ˌɑːdʒɛnˈteɪtəs / (BrE)
A large gull of the Northern Hemisphere, commonly known as the herring gull, with grey and white plumage and pink legs.
large gull species (herring gull)

Examples

The Larus argentatus is easily recognized by its distinctive grey wings and loud call.
Many coastal cities have seen a rise in the population of Larus argentatus in recent years.
A Larus argentatus swooped down and snatched a piece of bread from the beachgoers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herring gull - common name for Larus argentatus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Larus' (gull) and 'argentatus' (silvered, referring to grey/silvery plumage).
