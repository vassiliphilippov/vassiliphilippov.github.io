greyed

1. ADJ

/ ɡreɪd / (AmE), / ɡreɪd / (BrE)
having become grey or dulled in color, often from age, dust, or exposure.
having become grey or dulled in color

Examples

The house's walls had greyed after years of exposure to the elements.
Her hair had greyed noticeably over the past decade.
The old photograph had greyed with age, losing its original vibrancy.

Synset Examples

colors dulled by too much sun
greyed with the dust of the road

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grayed - American English variant of 'greyed' (having become grey in color)
  • Synonym: faded - lost brightness, color, or clarity over time
  • Antonym: brightened - having become brighter or more vivid in color
