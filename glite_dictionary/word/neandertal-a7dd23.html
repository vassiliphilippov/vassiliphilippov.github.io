neandertal

1. ADJ

/ niˈæn.dɚ.tɑl / (AmE), / niˈæn.də.tɑːl / (BrE)
(of a person or their behavior) ill-mannered, coarse, and contemptible; lacking refinement or sophistication.
ill-mannered or coarse (figurative)
informal

Examples

His neandertal behavior at the dinner table shocked everyone.
She refused to date anyone with such neandertal manners.
The team was criticized for its neandertal attitude toward women.

Synset Examples

was boorish and insensitive
the loutish manners of a bully
her stupid oafish husband
aristocratic contempt for the swinish multitude

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boorish - rude and insensitive
  • Antonym: refined - polite, cultured, and showing good manners

Etymology

From the name of the extinct human species Neanderthal, used figuratively for primitive or uncivilized behavior.
