incalescence

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.kəˈlɛs.əns / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kəˈlɛs.əns / (BrE)
The process or state of becoming warm or giving off heat.
the process of becoming warm

Examples

The incalescence of the hot coals filled the room with a gentle heat.
During summer, the incalescence of the sun can be felt on the pavement.
He described the gradual incalescence of the water as the kettle began to boil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warming - the process of becoming warm
  • Antonym: cooling - the process of becoming cooler

Etymology

From Latin incalescentia, from in- (in, on) + calescere (to become warm).
