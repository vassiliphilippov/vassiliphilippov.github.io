Makaira nigricans

1. NOUN

/ məˈkaɪrə nɪˈɡrɪkænz / (AmE), / məˈkaɪərə nɪˈɡrɪkænz / (BrE)
a very large marlin species, commonly known as the Atlantic blue marlin, which can reach weights of up to 2000 pounds and is found in warm seas worldwide
Atlantic blue marlin species of fish

Examples

The Makaira nigricans is highly prized by sport fishermen for its size and strength.
Researchers tracked the migration of Makaira nigricans across the Atlantic Ocean.
The striking appearance of Makaira nigricans makes it a popular subject for marine artists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue marlin - common name for Makaira nigricans, large marlin species

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name Makaira (from Greek makaira, 'sword'), species epithet nigricans, Latin for 'becoming black'.
