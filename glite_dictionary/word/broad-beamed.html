broad-beamed

1. ADJ

/ brɔd ˈbiːmd / (AmE), / brɔːd ˈbiːmd / (BrE)
having a wide, broad, or spacious build or structure.
having a wide or broad structure

Examples

The old sailor had a broad-beamed frame that filled the doorway.
They chose a broad-beamed sofa to fit the spacious living room.
That broad-beamed vessel was especially stable during storms.

Synset Examples

She admired the broad-beamed horses at the ranch.
A broad-beamed design gives the room a spacious feel.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: narrow - having a small width or less broad structure

Etymology

From 'broad' (wide) + 'beam' (width of a ship or building), originally used in nautical contexts.
