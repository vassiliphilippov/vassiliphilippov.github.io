centesis

1. NOUN

/ sɛnˈtisis / (AmE), / sɛnˈtiːsɪs / (BrE)
a medical procedure involving the puncture of a body cavity or organ with a hollow needle to remove fluid
surgical puncture to remove fluid
medical

Examples

The doctor performed a centesis to relieve the buildup of fluid in the patient's abdomen.
Paracentesis is one type of centesis used to treat certain medical conditions.
After the centesis, the laboratory analyzed the fluid sample for signs of infection.

Etymology

From Greek *-kentesis* (pricking), from *kentein* (to prick).
