Voltarean

1. ADJ

/ voʊlˈtɛriən / (AmE), / vɒlˈtɛəriən / (BrE)
Characteristic of, or in the manner of, Voltaire; displaying wit, irony, or philosophical skepticism associated with Voltaire.
in the manner of Voltaire

Examples

The critic praised the author's Voltarean wit and biting satire.
Her essay took a distinctly Voltarean approach, challenging established beliefs with irony and reason.
Many Enlightenment thinkers admired Voltaire, but few matched his Voltarean flair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Voltairean - Alternative spelling meaning pertaining to Voltaire

Etymology

Formed from the name 'Voltaire', French Enlightenment writer and philosopher, plus the adjective-forming suffix '-an'.
