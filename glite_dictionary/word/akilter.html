akilter

1. ADJ

/ əˈkɪltər / (AmE), / əˈkɪltə / (BrE)
out of balance or disoriented; not in proper order or alignment
out of balance or disoriented

Examples

After the move, his entire routine felt akilter for weeks.
She noticed the picture frames akilter on the wall after the tremor.
My sense of time was akilter because of jet lag.

Synset Examples

Everything felt akilter when the power went out unexpectedly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: askew - not in a straight or level position
  • Synonym: awry - away from the usual or expected course, amiss

Etymology

Probably formed by analogy with 'askew' and 'awry,' from the prefix 'a-' meaning 'in a state of' plus 'kilter' (good condition).

2. ADJ

/ əˈkɪltər / (AmE), / əˈkɪltə / (BrE)
hanging askew or crooked; not straight or level
askew or crooked

Examples

His tie was akilter after the blustery walk through town.
One painting hung akilter on the otherwise perfect wall.
She noticed her hat sat akilter every time she looked in the mirror.

Synset Examples

His shirt was hanging akilter after the long day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: askew - not in a straight or level position
  • Antonym: straight - in a straight position, not crooked

Etymology

Variant of 'askew', with 'a-' prefix and altered ending to resemble 'kilter' (balance, order).
