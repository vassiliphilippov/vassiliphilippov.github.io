Eucalyptus delegatensis

1. NOUN

/ juːˌkælɪptəs ˌdɛlɪɡəˈtɛnsɪs / (AmE), / juːˌkælɪptəs ˌdɛlɪɡəˈtɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A tall Australian timber tree with hard, heavy pinkish or light brown wood, commonly known as alpine ash or woollybutt.
species of tall Australian timber tree

Examples

The timber from Eucalyptus delegatensis is highly valued in construction for its strength.
Forests of Eucalyptus delegatensis are found in the highlands of southeast Australia.
The pale bark and towering presence of Eucalyptus delegatensis make it easy to identify in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alpine ash - another common name for Eucalyptus delegatensis
  • Degree Lower: mountain ash - closely related Eucalyptus species with similar appearance

Etymology

From genus name 'Eucalyptus' (from Greek 'eu-' meaning well and 'kalyptos' meaning covered) and species name 'delegatensis', named after Delegate, a region in New South Wales, Australia.
