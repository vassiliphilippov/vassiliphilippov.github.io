Chlamydosaurus

1. NOUN

/ klæmˌɪdoʊˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / klæmˌɪdəʊˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of lizards, known as frilled lizards, native to Australia and New Guinea, notable for the large frill around their neck that can be expanded when threatened.
genus of frilled lizards

Examples

The Chlamydosaurus is famous for its frill, which it displays to scare off predators.
A Chlamydosaurus can be seen basking in the sun on a tree trunk in northern Australia.
Scientists have observed the Chlamydosaurus using its frill during mating displays as well.

Etymology

From Greek chlamys (cloak) and sauros (lizard), referring to the frill around the neck.
