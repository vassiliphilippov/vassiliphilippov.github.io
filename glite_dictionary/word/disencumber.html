to disencumber

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪsɪnˈkʌmbər / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈkʌmbə / (BrE)
to free or release from a burden, difficulty, or obstacle
free from burden or difficulty

Examples

The reforms were designed to disencumber the company of excessive regulation.
He finally managed to disencumber himself from a long-standing financial obligation.
She worked hard to disencumber her schedule so she could focus on her family.

Synset Examples

I cannot extricate myself from this task

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to extricate - to free from constraint or difficulty
  • Antonym: to encumber - to burden or weigh down

Etymology

From Old French desencumbrer, from encombrer (to hinder, encumber) with the prefix dis- (expressing removal).
