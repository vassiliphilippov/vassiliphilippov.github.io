to exculpate

1. VERB

/ ˈɛkskʌlˌpeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛkskʌlpeɪt / (BrE)
to officially declare that someone is not guilty of wrongdoing or criminal charges
declare someone not guilty
legal

Examples

The new evidence helped to exculpate the defendant after months of suspicion.
After a thorough investigation, the committee decided to exculpate the employee.
The lawyer argued passionately to exculpate her client from all charges.

Synset Examples

The suspect was cleared of the murder charges

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to absolve - to declare someone free from blame or responsibility
  • Antonym: to incriminate - to make someone appear guilty of a crime or wrongdoing

Etymology

From Latin exculpatus, past participle of exculpare (to clear from blame), from ex- (out) + culpa (blame).
