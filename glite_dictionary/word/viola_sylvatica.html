Viola sylvatica

1. NOUN

/ viˈoʊlə sɪlˈvætɪkə / (AmE), / vʌɪˈəʊlə sɪlˈvatɪkə / (BrE)
A species of violet commonly found in European woods and hedgerows, known for its purple flowers.
species of European violet

Examples

The Viola sylvatica blooms in early spring across the forest floor.
Gardeners often cultivate Viola sylvatica for its delicate fragrance and beauty.
Many woodland creatures rely on Viola sylvatica as a food source.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood violet - another common name for Viola sylvatica

Etymology

From Latin: 'Viola' (violet) and 'sylvatica' (of the woods).
