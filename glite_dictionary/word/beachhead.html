beachhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈbitʃˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈbiːtʃˌhɛd / (BrE)
an area on an enemy shore that an invading force secures and holds as a base for further advance inland, typically established by an amphibious landing
secured landing area on enemy shore

Examples

The marines established a beachhead before advancing inland.
Securing the beachhead was critical to the success of the operation.
The troops faced fierce resistance as they tried to hold the beachhead against counterattacks.

Synset Examples

the Germans were desperately trying to contain the Anzio beachhead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bridgehead - a strong position secured in enemy territory from which further advance can be made

Etymology

Formed from 'beach' + 'head', modeled on 'bridgehead' (a military stronghold on the enemy's side of a river).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbiːtʃˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈbiːtʃˌhɛd / (BrE)
an initial achievement that paves the way for further progress in a particular activity or field
initial achievement enabling further advances

Examples

This partnership with a local company will give us a beachhead in the Asian market.
The new store serves as a beachhead for the brand's expansion across the country.
Securing young talent is seen as a beachhead in strengthening the company's research capacity.

Synset Examples

the town became a beachhead in the campaign to ban smoking outdoors
they are presently attempting to gain a foothold in the Russian market

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foothold - a secure position from which further progress can be made

Etymology

Originally a military term from World War II, referring to a position on a beach secured by invading forces, from 'beach' + 'head'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
