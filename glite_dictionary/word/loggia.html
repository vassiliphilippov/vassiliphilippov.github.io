loggia

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.dʒi.ə / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒ.i.ə / (BrE)
A gallery or room with one or more open sides, often forming part of the front or side of a building and typically at an upper level, with a roof supported by columns or arches.
roofed open-sided gallery or arcade

Examples

The villa featured an elegant loggia overlooking the gardens.
They enjoyed breakfast on the upper loggia each morning, shaded from the sun.
A marble staircase led up to a spacious loggia with intricate columns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: veranda - a roofed platform along the outside of a house, level with the ground floor
  • Synonym: balcony - a platform on the outside of a building, enclosed by a wall or balustrade

Etymology

From Italian 'loggia' (roofed gallery), ultimately from Old French 'loge' (hut, arbor), of Germanic origin.
