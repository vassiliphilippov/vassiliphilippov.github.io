Amphicarpaea bracteata

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmfɪˈkɑːrpiə brækˈtiːətə / (AmE), / ˌæmfɪˈkɑːpɪə brækˈtiːətə / (BrE)
A vine native to eastern North America, known for its clusters of purple to maroon flowers and edible, often underground, one-seeded pods resembling peanuts.
North American vine with edible pods

Examples

The seeds of Amphicarpaea bracteata are prized by foragers for their peanut-like flavor.
Some gardeners cultivate Amphicarpaea bracteata to enrich soil and attract pollinators.
Wildlife such as deer and small mammals often feed on the pods of Amphicarpaea bracteata.

Etymology

From the genus name Amphicarpaea, from Greek amphi- (on both sides) and karpos (fruit), and bracteata, from Latin bractea (thin plate, bract).
