brutality

1. NOUN

/ bruˈtæləti / (AmE), / bruːˈtælɪti / (BrE)
Extreme cruelty or violence; the quality of being brutal.
extreme cruelty or violence

Examples

The police were accused of shocking brutality during the protest.
She was appalled by the brutality of the crime scene.
Human history is filled with both acts of kindness and dreadful brutality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruelty - the quality of being cruel
  • Antonym: kindness - the quality of being kind

Etymology

From French 'brutalité', from Latin 'brutalis' (dull, stupid), related to 'brutus' (heavy, slow, dull).

2. NOUN

/ bruˈtæləti / (AmE), / bruːˈtælɪti / (BrE)
cruel and violent behavior; a savage or ruthless act
cruel and violent act

Examples

The police were condemned for the brutality of their actions during the protest.
War often brings out acts of unimaginable brutality in people.
The movie depicts the brutality of life in the prison camp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruelty - deliberate infliction of pain and suffering
  • Antonym: kindness - the quality of being friendly, generous, and considerate

Etymology

From French brutalité, from brutal (brutal) + -ité (-ity), ultimately from Latin brutalis.
