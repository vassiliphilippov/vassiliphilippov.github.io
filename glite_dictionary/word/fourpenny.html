fourpenny

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔːrˌpɛni / (AmE), / ˈfɔːpəni / (BrE)
(of a nail) measuring 1 1/2 inches (3.8 cm) in length.
describing a nail 1 1/2 in or 3.8 cm long

Examples

The carpenter requested a box of fourpenny nails for the project.
You'll need fourpenny nails to attach the thin wooden panels.
She asked if fourpenny nails would be strong enough for the frame.

Etymology

From 'four penny' (obsolete price unit), used in hardware to denote nail size.
