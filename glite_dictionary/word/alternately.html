alternately

1. ADV

/ ˈɔl.tɚ.nət.li / (AmE), / ˈɔːl.tə.nət.li / (BrE)
In succession, one after the other, in a regular pattern; in alternating sequence or position.
in alternating sequence or position

Examples

The teacher asked the students to sit alternately by gender for the activity.
She filled the boxes alternately with apples and oranges to create a pattern.
The lights flashed alternately red and green throughout the performance.

Synset Examples

They were deglycerolized by alternately centrifuging and mixing
he planted fir and pine trees alternately

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alternatively - used to introduce another possibility; as another option

Etymology

From Latin alternatus (interchanged, alternate), past participle of alternare (to do by turns).
