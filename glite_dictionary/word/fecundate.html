to fecundate

1. VERB

/ fɪˈkʌn.deɪt / (AmE), / fɪˈkʌn.deɪt / (BrE)
to make fertile by fertilization; to cause to produce offspring or fruit
make fertile or productive

Examples

The botanist attempted to fecundate the flowers by hand to ensure a good crop.
Certain plants can only fecundate when specific insects are present to carry their pollen.
The procedure is designed to fecundate multiple eggs in the laboratory for genetic research.

Synset Examples

The course fertilized her imagination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fertilize - to make (an egg or plant) able to develop and grow; provide with the capacity to reproduce

Etymology

From Latin fecundatus, past participle of fecundare (to make fruitful), from fecundus (fruitful).

2. VERB

/ fɪˈkʌn.deɪt / (AmE), / fɪˈkʌn.deɪt / (BrE)
to fertilize by introducing semen into a female; to cause to become pregnant or capable of producing offspring
to make fertile by insemination

Examples

Certain bees help to fecundate the flowers by transferring pollen between them.
The process by which sperm fecundate an egg is crucial to reproduction.
In some species, only one male is allowed to fecundate the females in a group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fertilize - to make fertile by uniting male and female gametes

Etymology

From Latin fecundatus, past participle of fecundare (to make fruitful), from fecundus (fruitful).
