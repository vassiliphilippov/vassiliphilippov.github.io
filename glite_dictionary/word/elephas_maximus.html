Elephas maximus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛlɪfəs ˈmæksɪməs / (AmE), / ˌɛlɪfəs ˈmæksɪməs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the Asian elephant, a species native to Asia characterized by smaller ears and, typically, male tusks
scientific name for Asian elephant

Examples

The Elephas maximus is listed as endangered due to habitat loss and poaching.
Researchers are studying the migration patterns of Elephas maximus in northern India.
Unlike the African elephant, the Elephas maximus has noticeably smaller ears.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Asian elephant - the common name for Elephas maximus
  • Antonym: African elephant - a related species of elephant native to Africa

Etymology

From Latin: 'elephas' (elephant) + 'maximus' (greatest).
