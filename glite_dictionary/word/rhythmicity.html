rhythmicity

1. NOUN

/ ɹɪðˈmɪsɪti / (AmE), / ɹɪθˈmɪsɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of having rhythm, especially as imparted by accents and durations of notes in music
quality of having rhythm in music

Examples

The rhythmicity of the drummer's performance brought the song to life.
Researchers study rhythmicity to understand the impact of timing in musical compositions.
Good rhythmicity is essential for dancers to stay in sync with the music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhythm - a strong, regular, repeated pattern of movement or sound

Etymology

Formed from 'rhythm' + '-icity', modeled on 'electricity'.
