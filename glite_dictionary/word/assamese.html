Assamese

1. NOUN

/ ˌæsəˈmiz / (AmE), / ˌæsəˈmiːz / (BrE)
the Indo-Aryan language spoken by the Assamese people of Assam, closely related to Bengali and Oriya.
language of Assam, India

Examples

Many students in Assam learn to speak both Assamese and English fluently.
Her grandfather still preferred to read newspapers written in Assamese.
The poem was originally written in Assamese before being translated into Hindi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bengali - an Indo-Aryan language of eastern India and Bangladesh

Etymology

From Assam (a region in India) + -ese (denoting nationality or language).

3. NOUN

/ ˌæsəˈmiz / (AmE), / ˌæsəˈmiːz / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of the state of Assam in northeastern India
person from Assam, India

Examples

There is a large community of Assamese living in Guwahati.
Many Assamese have preserved their traditional customs and festivals.
The Assamese are known for their distinctive cuisine and silk weaving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bengali - a native or inhabitant of Bengal; related group in eastern India and Bangladesh

Etymology

From Assam (the state in India) + -ese (denoting nationality or language).
