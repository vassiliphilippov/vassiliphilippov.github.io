Elymus hispidus

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːlɪməs ˈhɪspɪdəs / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪməs ˈhɪspɪdəs / (BrE)
A species of Asiatic grass introduced into the United States for use as pasture and fodder.
species of grass used as pasture/fodder

Examples

Elymus hispidus is valued for its resilience in dry climates and its use in livestock pastures.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Elymus hispidus to evaluate its suitability for American rangelands.
The agricultural extension recommended planting Elymus hispidus as a reliable fodder crop.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Elymus' (a genus of wild rye), 'hispidus' meaning 'bristly'.
