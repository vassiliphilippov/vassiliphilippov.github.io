sledding

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɛdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈslɛdɪŋ / (BrE)
progress toward achieving something, especially used to describe whether the process is easy or difficult (as in 'easy sledding' or 'tough sledding').
progress toward a goal; ease or difficulty of progress

Examples

Convincing the board was not sledding—it actually proved to be quite challenging.
The project team faced some tough sledding when they tried to meet the new deadlines.
After an initial period of easy sledding, the negotiations became increasingly complicated.

Synset Examples

persuading him was easy going
the proposal faces tough sledding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: progress - forward or onward movement toward a goal
  • Antonym: difficulty - the state or quality of being difficult

Etymology

Derivative of 'sled,' extended metaphorically from literal sled travel to describe the ease or difficulty of advancing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈslɛdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈslɛdɪŋ / (BrE)
the sport or activity of riding on a sled or sleigh, especially down a snow-covered hill
riding on a sled for sport or fun

Examples

After the snowfall, the children spent all afternoon sledding down the hill.
Many families visit the park for sledding during the winter holidays.
He remembers sledding with his cousins every year as a favorite childhood memory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tobogganing - the activity of riding on a long, narrow sled called a toboggan
