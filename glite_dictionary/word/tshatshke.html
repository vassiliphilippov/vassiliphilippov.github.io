tshatshke

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑtʃ.kə / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːtʃ.kə / (BrE)
An inexpensive, showy trinket or knick-knack, especially as a Yiddish loanword in English.
inexpensive showy trinket

Examples

She brought back a tshatshke from her trip as a souvenir.
The shelves were covered in small tshatshkes collected over the years.
He gifted her a shiny tshatshke for good luck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trinket - a small ornament or item of jewelry, typically of little value

Etymology

From Yiddish טשאַטשקע (tshatshke), meaning trinket or knick-knack.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑtʃkə / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːtʃkə / (BrE)
(Yiddish, informal) An attractive, unconventional woman, often with a flashy or provocative style.
attractive, flashy woman (Yiddish, informal)
informal

Examples

She walked into the party dressed like a real tshatshke, turning everyone's head.
Some people call her a tshatshke because of her bold fashion sense and lively personality.
In New York's Lower East Side, the word tshatshke is used to describe women who stand out in a crowd.

Etymology

From Yiddish טשאַטשקע (tshatshke), meaning trinket or bauble, later used figuratively for a flashy woman.
