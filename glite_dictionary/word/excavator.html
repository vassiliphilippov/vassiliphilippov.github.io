excavator

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛkskəˌveɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɛkskəveɪtə / (BrE)
A large machine with a long arm and bucket, used for digging and moving earth, especially on building sites.
large digging machine

Examples

The construction crew operated the excavator to dig the foundation for the new building.
A bright yellow excavator rolled onto the site early this morning.
Modern excavators can rotate their cabins 360 degrees for increased efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digger - a machine or person that digs

Etymology

From Latin 'excavare' (to hollow out), via 'excavate' + '-or' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.səˌveɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛk.səˌveɪ.tə / (BrE)
A person whose job is to dig out earth, rock, or other material, especially for foundations or quarrying.
person who excavates earth or rock

Examples

The excavator spent hours digging the foundation for the new building.
Each excavator must be trained to handle the massive machinery used at the quarry.
The site manager consulted the excavator about potential obstacles in the soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digger - a person or machine that digs

Etymology

From Latin excavare (to hollow out), from ex- (out) + cavare (to make hollow).
