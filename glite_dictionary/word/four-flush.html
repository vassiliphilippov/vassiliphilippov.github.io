four-flush

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌflʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌflʌʃ / (BrE)
A person who pretends to have confidence, wealth, or success; also, a bluff or deceitful claim.
person who bluffs or pretends; a deceitful claim
informal american

Examples

Everyone knew Tom was a four-flush and didn't really own that fancy car.
Her big talk about winning the contract turned out to be just a four-flush.
The poker table was full of four-flushes trying to bluff their way to victory.

Synset Examples

Don't believe him; he's just a four-flush trying to impress.
His promises turned out to be a four-flush, nothing behind them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluffer - a person who attempts to deceive by pretending to have a stronger position
  • Synonym: impostor - someone who pretends to be someone else, usually to deceive

Etymology

American English, from the poker term 'four flush'—a hand with four cards of the same suit, which is not a winning hand but can be used to bluff.
