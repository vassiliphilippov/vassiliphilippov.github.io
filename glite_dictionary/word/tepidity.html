tepidity

1. NOUN

/ təˈpɪdəti / (AmE), / tɛˈpɪdəti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being moderately warm; gentle or mild warmth, especially resembling the temperature of the skin
moderate warmth; mild heat

Examples

She was surprised by the tepidity of the water, expecting it to be colder.
The bath's tepidity was soothing after a long day.
Despite the sun, there was only a tepidity in the air, not true heat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warmth - the state of being warm; heat
  • Antonym: coolness - the state of being cool in temperature
  • Synonym: lukewarmness - the state of being only slightly warm; tepidness

Etymology

From Latin 'tepiditas', from 'tepidus' (warm).
