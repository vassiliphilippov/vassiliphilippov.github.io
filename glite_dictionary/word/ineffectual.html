ineffectual

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.ɪˈfɛk.tʃu.əl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.ɪˈfɛk.tʃu.əl / (BrE)
not producing the desired effect; lacking the ability or power to be effective.
not producing desired effect

Examples

The new policy was completely ineffectual in reducing crime rates.
His ineffectual attempts to fix the car only made things worse.
Despite their efforts, the protest proved ineffectual and failed to bring about change.

Synset Examples

a futile effort
the therapy was ineffectual
an otiose undertaking
an unavailing attempt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ineffective - not producing any significant or desired effect
  • Antonym: effective - successful in producing a desired or intended result

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + effectualis (having effect).

3. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.ɪˈfɛk.tʃu.əl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.ɪˈfɛk.tjʊ.əl / (BrE)
Not producing the intended effect; lacking the ability or power to achieve a desired result.
not producing intended effect

Examples

His attempts to solve the problem were ineffectual, and the issue remained unresolved.
The new security measures proved ineffectual against the sophisticated hacking attempt.
She gave an ineffectual response to the critics, failing to address their main concerns.

Synset Examples

an ineffective teacher
ineffective legislation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ineffective - not producing the intended result
  • Antonym: effective - producing a desired or intended result

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + effectus (effect, result) + -ual (suffix forming adjectives).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00839148-a
Synset POS: a
