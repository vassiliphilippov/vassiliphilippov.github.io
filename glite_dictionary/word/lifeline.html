lifeline

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪfˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈlaɪfˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a rope or line thrown to rescue someone in danger of drowning, which they can hold onto for safety
rescue rope for saving people from drowning

Examples

The sailor quickly threw the lifeline to the man struggling in the water.
During the drill, each crew member practiced tossing a lifeline to a simulated victim.
Waves crashed as the rescued swimmer clung to the lifeline until the boat reached him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rescue line - a line used to aid or rescue people in hazardous situations

Etymology

Formed from 'life' + 'line', referring to a line that preserves life.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪfˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈlaɪfˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a crease on the palm, traditionally believed in palmistry to predict the length of a person's life
crease on the palm associated with lifespan

Examples

The palmist traced the lifeline on her hand and smiled reassuringly.
Some people believe that a long lifeline means a long and healthy life.
He examined the shape of his lifeline, curious about what it might reveal.

3. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪfˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf.laɪn / (BrE)
a rope or line used to raise or lower a deep-sea diver or to provide a means of safety while underwater
safety rope for divers

Examples

The diver clung tightly to the lifeline as he descended into the dark waters.
Before entering the water, she checked that her lifeline was secured to the boat.
Workers made sure the lifeline was free of knots and strong enough to support the diver's weight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: safety line - a rope or cable used as a safeguard; especially in dangerous work situations

4. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪfˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf.laɪn / (BrE)
a thing on which someone or something depends or which provides a means of escape from a difficult situation, especially one that is crucial for survival or continued existence.
essential support or connection for survival

Examples

After the natural disaster, international aid became a lifeline for the affected region.
The hotline serves as a lifeline for people struggling with mental health issues.
Her weekly phone calls were a lifeline during his difficult recovery.

Synset Examples

the airlift provided a lifeline for Berlin
she offered me a lifeline in my time of grief

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - provision of help or assistance to someone or something
  • Synonym: aid - help that is given to someone, especially in an emergency
  • Antonym: obstacle - a thing that blocks one's way or prevents progress

Etymology

Formed from 'life' + 'line', originally referring to a rope thrown to rescue someone (mid 19th century).
