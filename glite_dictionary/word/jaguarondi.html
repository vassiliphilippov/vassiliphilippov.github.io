jaguarondi

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒæɡwəˈrʌndi / (AmE), / ˌdʒaɡwəˈrɒndi / (BrE)
A long-bodied, long-tailed wildcat native to Central and South America, also known as the otter cat.
tropical American wildcat species

Examples

The jaguarondi is rarely seen in the wild due to its elusive nature.
Researchers set up cameras to track the movements of the jaguarondi in the rainforest.
Unlike the larger jaguar, the jaguarondi hunts mostly during the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jaguarundi - alternative spelling for jaguarondi, another name for the same wildcat species

Etymology

From Old Guarani (jaguareté) via Spanish and Portuguese, meaning 'small jaguar'.
