kerosene

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛrəˌsiːn / (AmE), / ˈkɛrəsiːn / (BrE)
a clear, flammable oil obtained from petroleum, used as a fuel in lamps, heaters, and engines
flammable oil fuel for lamps/heaters

Examples

They used kerosene to fuel the old lantern during the camping trip.
The heater runs on kerosene, so we need to buy more before winter starts.
A strong smell of kerosene lingered in the garage after the spill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paraffin - another term for kerosene, especially in British English

Etymology

From Greek 'kēros' (wax), coined in the mid-19th century.
