Viverrinae

1. NOUN

/ vɪˈvɛrəˌniː / (AmE), / vɪˈvɛrɪniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of small to medium-sized carnivorous mammals, including civets and genets, native to the Old World.
subfamily of civets and genets

Examples

The Viverrinae are known for their elongated bodies and nocturnal habits.
Many members of the Viverrinae subfamily inhabit tropical forests across Asia and Africa.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Viverrinae during a wildlife survey.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Viverridae - the family of mammals including civets, genets, and their relatives

Etymology

From Latin 'viverra' (ferret) with the zoological suffix '-inae' indicating a subfamily.

2. NOUN

/ vɪˈvɛrɪni / (AmE), / vɪˈvɛrɪniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of small to medium-sized carnivorous mammals including genets and civets, found mainly in Africa and Asia.
subfamily of small carnivorous mammals

Examples

The Viverrinae subfamily includes animal species such as genets and civets.
Researchers discovered a new trait among members of the Viverrinae.
Most Viverrinae are nocturnal and have elongated bodies.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Viverridae - the family that includes the subfamily Viverrinae
  • Synonym: genet - a small African carnivore in the subfamily Viverrinae
  • Synonym: civet - a carnivorous mammal in the subfamily Viverrinae

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Viverrinae', based on 'Viverra', the type genus, from Latin 'viverra' meaning 'ferret', plus the zoological suffix '-inae'.
