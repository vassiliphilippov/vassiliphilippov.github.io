Old French

1. NOUN

/ oʊld frɛntʃ / (AmE), / əʊld frɛntʃ / (BrE)
the form of the French language used from the 9th to the 15th century
historical stage of French (9th–15th c.)

Examples

The poem was originally written in Old French during the 12th century.
Scholars of medieval literature often study texts in Old French.
She is learning Old French to understand historical manuscripts firsthand.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Middle French - the stage of French language used from the 14th to the early 17th century

Etymology

Old French: from Latin 'Francus' (Frank) via Old French 'Franceis'.
