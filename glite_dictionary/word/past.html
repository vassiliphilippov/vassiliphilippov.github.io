past

1. NOUN

/ pæst / (AmE), / pɑːst / (BrE)
The time before the present; all events or periods that have already happened.
time before now

Examples

She often thinks about her past and the people she used to know.
It is important to learn from the past to avoid making the same mistakes again.
They left their troubled past behind and started a new life.

Synset Examples

forget the past

Related Terms

  • Antonym: future - the time yet to come

Etymology

From Latin 'passus' (past participle of 'pati' meaning 'to go through, suffer') via Old French and Middle English.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ pæst / (AmE), / pɑːst / (BrE)
belonging to an earlier time, or no longer current; having occurred or existed before the present
gone by in time; former

Examples

Many people reflect on their past experiences when making important decisions.
The past year has brought many changes to our community.
He often talks about his past achievements during interviews.

Synset Examples

time past
his youth is past
this past Thursday
the past year

Related Terms

  • Antonym: present - existing or happening now
  • Antonym: future - yet to happen; coming after the present

Etymology

From Latin 'pastus', past participle of 'pascere' (to feed). Via Old French and Middle English, originally meaning 'having gone by'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ pæst / (AmE), / pɑːst / (BrE)
A verb tense that expresses actions or states which occurred before the present time.
verb tense for actions before now

Examples

The simple past is used for actions that happened and finished in the past.
In English, we form the past tense of regular verbs by adding '-ed'.
Can you give an example of the past tense in a sentence?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: present - a verb tense expressing actions occurring now
  • Antonym: future - a verb tense expressing actions that will occur

Etymology

From Latin 'praeteritus', meaning 'gone by', via Old French and Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06341255-n
Synset POS: n

4. ADV

/ pæst / (AmE), / pɑːst / (BrE)
Used to indicate movement so as to pass a particular point.
so as to pass a point (adverb)

Examples

The car sped past without stopping at the crosswalk.
We watched the parade go past our house.
She ran past me on her way to the finish line.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: by - so as to go beyond a point, as in 'go by'

5. NOUN

/ pæst / (AmE), / pɑːst / (BrE)
a person's earlier life or experiences, especially those that might be kept secret or may cause embarrassment
a person's earlier life or experiences

Examples

Few people know much about her past, and she prefers to keep it that way.
The film explores how the main character's past influences his present decisions.
He was afraid that details of his past could ruin his reputation.

Synset Examples

reporters dug into the candidate's past

Related Terms

  • Synonym: history - the whole series of past events in someone's life

Etymology

From Latin 'pastus', past participle of 'pandere' (to spread), via Old French 'past', meaning 'gone by'.
