to proselytize

1. VERB

/ ˈprɑːsələˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈprɒsɪlɪˌtaɪz / (BrE)
to try to persuade someone to change their religious beliefs or to join another religion
attempt to convert someone's religion

Examples

Some missionaries travel overseas to proselytize and share their religious beliefs with others.
The group was accused of trying to proselytize students on campus.
He openly admitted that he wanted to proselytize those who had never attended church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convert - to change or cause someone to change their beliefs, often religion

Etymology

From Late Latin proselytizare (to make a proselyte), from Greek prosēlytos (a newcomer, especially a convert to Judaism).
