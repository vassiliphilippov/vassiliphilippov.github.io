Ro

1. NOUN

/ roʊ / (AmE), / rəʊ / (BrE)
An artificial language created for international use, developed to reject all existing words and instead base its vocabulary on an abstract system of categorizing ideas.
constructed language for international use

Examples

The linguist studied the structure of Ro and compared it to other constructed languages.
Unlike Esperanto, Ro uses a system of categorizing words based on meaning rather than borrowing from existing vocabularies.
Only a few people today attempt to learn Ro as a form of linguistic experimentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Esperanto - the most widely spoken constructed international auxiliary language
  • Synonym: Ido - a constructed language derived from Esperanto

Etymology

Created by Rev. Edward Powell Foster in 1904 as a systematic language based on categorization of ideas.
