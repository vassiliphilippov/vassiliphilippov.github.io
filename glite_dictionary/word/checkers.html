checkers

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛk.ɚz / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛk.əz / (BrE)
A board game for two players in which each player has 12 pieces that move diagonally on a checkerboard, the goal being to capture the opponent's pieces by jumping over them.
board game with jumping pieces
american

Examples

We spent the rainy afternoon playing checkers by the fireplace.
He taught his daughter how to play checkers when she was five years old.
The old men gathered in the park to compete in their daily checkers match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: draughts - British term for the same board game as checkers

Etymology

Plural of 'checker', relating to the checkered design of the board.
