Rosh Hashona

1. NOUN

/ roʊʃ həˈʃoʊnə / (AmE), / rəʊʃ həˈʃɒnə / (BrE)
The Jewish New Year festival, celebrated on the first or first and second days of Tishri, marked by prayer and the blowing of the shofar.
Jewish New Year festival

Examples

Many families gather together to celebrate Rosh Hashona with traditional foods.
The sound of the shofar is a distinctive part of Rosh Hashona services.
During Rosh Hashona, people reflect on the past year and make resolutions for the new one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rosh Hashanah - Alternative spelling for the Jewish New Year festival

Etymology

From Hebrew *rosh hash-shānāh* (head of the year).
