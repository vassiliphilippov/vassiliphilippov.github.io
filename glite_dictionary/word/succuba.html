succuba

1. NOUN

/ səkˈjuːbə / (AmE), / sʌˈkjuːbə / (BrE)
(mythology) a female demon or supernatural entity believed to have sexual intercourse with sleeping men.
female demon in mythology

Examples

In the legend, a succuba would visit men in their dreams to drain their energy.
Medieval texts often warned against the dangers posed by a succuba.
The story describes a succuba tormenting a village until a priest intervened.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: succubus - female demon believed to seduce men in their sleep
  • Antonym: incubus - male demon believed to have sexual intercourse with sleeping women

Etymology

From Medieval Latin succuba (paramour), from Latin succubāre (to lie under).
