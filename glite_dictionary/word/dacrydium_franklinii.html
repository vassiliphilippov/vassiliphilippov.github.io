Dacrydium franklinii

1. NOUN

/ ˌdækrɪˈdiːəm fræŋˈklɪniː / (AmE), / ˌdakrɪˈdiːəm fraŋkˈlɪniː / (BrE)
A Tasmanian coniferous tree with yellow, aromatic, wavy-grained wood, used for carving and shipbuilding; sometimes classified in the genus Dacrydium.
Tasmanian timber tree with aromatic wood

Examples

The wood from Dacrydium franklinii is prized for its aromatic scent and workability.
Early shipbuilders in Tasmania often used Dacrydium franklinii for its durable timber.
Botanists are studying Dacrydium franklinii to understand its ecological role in Tasmanian forests.

Etymology

Dacrydium from Greek 'dakryon' (tear), referring to resin drops; species named in honor of Sir John Franklin.
