Sao Francisco

1. PROPN

/ saʊ frɑnˈsɪskoʊ / (AmE), / saʊ frænˈsɪskəʊ / (BrE)
a river in eastern Brazil that flows into the Atlantic Ocean, notable for its length and importance to the region
major river in eastern Brazil

Examples

The Sao Francisco River is vital for transportation and agriculture in northeastern Brazil.
Many hydroelectric plants are found along the Sao Francisco River.
The communities living near the Sao Francisco depend on its water during the dry season.

Etymology

Portuguese for 'Saint Francis', reflecting colonial naming after St. Francis of Assisi.
