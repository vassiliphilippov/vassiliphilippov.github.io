waxflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈwæksˌflaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈwæksˌflaʊ.ə / (BrE)
A twining woody vine of Madagascar with thick, dark waxy evergreen leaves and clusters of large, fragrant, waxy white flowers; widely grown in warm climates.
fragrant vine with waxy white flowers, native to Madagascar

Examples

The waxflower climbed elegantly along the trellis, filling the garden with its sweet scent.
Gardeners in tropical regions often cultivate waxflower for its attractive and fragrant blooms.
The thick, glossy leaves of the waxflower help it retain moisture in hot climates.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwæksˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈwæksˌflaʊə / (BrE)
An epiphytic plant of the Clusia genus, native to British Guiana, known for its waxy flowers.
epiphytic plant with waxy flowers

Examples

The rare waxflower thrives on the tall trees of British Guiana's rainforests.
Botanists studied the waxflower because of its unique epiphytic growth.
The glossy leaves and star-shaped blooms make the waxflower easily recognizable.

3. NOUN

/ ˈwæksˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈwaksˌflaʊə / (BrE)
a small woodland plant with waxy white or pinkish-white nodding flowers, scalelike leaves, and that turns black with age; saprophytic in nature
waxy-flowered saprophytic woodland plant

Examples

In the shaded woodland, the waxflower blooms quietly with its pale, waxy petals.
The waxflower is often found growing among leaf litter and decaying wood.
As it ages, the waxflower changes color and eventually turns black.

Etymology

From 'wax' (for the waxy texture of the flowers) + 'flower'.
