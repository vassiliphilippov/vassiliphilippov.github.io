Toledo

1. PROPN

/ təˈliːdoʊ / (AmE), / təˈliːdəʊ / (BrE)
An industrial city in northwestern Ohio, situated on the western end of Lake Erie.
industrial city in Ohio, USA

Examples

She moved to Toledo for a new job in the automotive industry.
The Maumee River runs through the center of Toledo.
Many glass manufacturers have factories in Toledo.

Etymology

Named after the city of Toledo in Spain.

2. PROPN

/ toʊˈleɪdoʊ / (AmE), / təˈliːdəʊ / (BrE)
a historic city in central Spain, located on the Tagus River, renowned for its medieval architecture and its tradition of steel and sword making since ancient times
city in central Spain, historic steelmaking center

Examples

Many tourists visit Toledo to admire its stunning medieval buildings and ancient city walls.
The swords made in Toledo were prized across Europe for their exceptional quality.
During our trip to Spain, we spent a day exploring the historic streets of Toledo.

Etymology

From Latin Toletum, the Roman name for the city.
