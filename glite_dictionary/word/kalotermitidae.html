Kalotermitidae

1. NOUN

/ kæləˌtɜːrmɪˈtaɪdiː / (AmE), / kælətəˌmɪtɪˈdiː / (BrE)
A family of primitive termites found in warm regions, typically known for living in dry wood.
a family of primitive dry-wood termites

Examples

Members of the Kalotermitidae are often called dry-wood termites because they nest in sound wood.
Entomologists discovered a new species within the Kalotermitidae family in tropical Australia.
Unlike subterranean termites, Kalotermitidae do not require contact with soil.

Etymology

From scientific Latin: genus name 'Kalotermes' + Greek '-idae', a suffix for animal family names.
