Botrychium virginianum

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈtrɪkiəm vɜːrdʒɪˈneɪnəm / (AmE), / bəʊˈtrɪkɪəm vɜːdʒɪˈneɪnəm / (BrE)
A North American fern whose clustered spore cases resemble a snake's rattle, commonly known as rattlesnake fern.
a North American fern species

Examples

The distinctive leaves of Botrychium virginianum often make it easy to spot in the woods.
In her botanical survey, she recorded several specimens of Botrychium virginianum near the creek.
Gardeners sometimes mistakenly identify Botrychium virginianum as a common weed.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'botrys' (bunch of grapes), species name refers to Virginia, USA.
