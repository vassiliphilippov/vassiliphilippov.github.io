armet

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrmɪt / (AmE), / ˈɑːmɛt / (BrE)
a type of medieval helmet with a visor and a neck guard, used in the 15th and 16th centuries.
medieval helmet with visor and neck guard

Examples

The knight lowered the visor of his armet before entering the tournament.
This museum displays a beautifully preserved armet from the 15th century.
Wearing his armet, the soldier was protected during battle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helmet - a protective covering for the head

Etymology

From French 'armet', diminutive of 'arme' (weapon, armor).
