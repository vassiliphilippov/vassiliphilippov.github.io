quinone

1. NOUN

/ kwɪˈnoʊn / (AmE), / kwɪˈnəʊn / (BrE)
a yellow, aromatic organic compound with two ketone substitutions, used in making dyes, and some of which are important in biological processes such as acting as coenzymes or vitamins
yellow aromatic organic compound, used in dyes and biology
medical

Examples

The synthesis of quinone is an essential step in the production of certain dyes.
Vitamin K is a type of quinone that plays a crucial role in blood clotting.
Many bacteria use quinone molecules as electron carriers in their respiratory chains.

Etymology

From Latin 'quino-' (from 'quinic acid') + '-one' (ketone suffix), referring to its chemical structure.
