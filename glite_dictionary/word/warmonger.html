warmonger

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrˌmʌŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈwɔːˌmʌŋɡə / (BrE)
a person who encourages or advocates war or aggressive policies towards other countries
person who advocates war

Examples

The politician was labeled a warmonger for supporting military intervention.
Critics accused the administration of being warmongers eager to start conflicts abroad.
He denied being a warmonger, insisting his goal was to protect the nation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: peacemaker - a person who seeks to make peace or reconcile foes
  • Synonym: hawk - a person who favors an aggressive or warlike policy (esp. in politics)
  • Antonym: dove - a person who advocates peaceful or conciliatory policies (esp. in politics)

Etymology

From 'war' + 'monger' (dealer or promoter), the latter from Old English 'mangere' (merchant).
