pastis

1. NOUN

/ pæˈstis / (AmE), / paˈstiːs / (BrE)
An anise-flavored spirit or liqueur from France, similar to absinthe but without wormwood, often served diluted with water as an aperitif.
anise-flavored French spirit

Examples

He ordered a glass of pastis to enjoy on the terrace as the sun set.
The licorice aroma of pastis is unmistakable in French cafés.
Adding cold water turns pastis a milky white, which many find appealing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absinthe - strong anise-flavored spirit containing wormwood

Etymology

French, from Provençal *pastís* meaning 'mixture' or 'blend'.
