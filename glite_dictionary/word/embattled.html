embattled

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈbætəld / (AmE), / ɪmˈbætld / (BrE)
(of a person, organization, or place) experiencing serious difficulties or conflict, often as a result of criticism or attack.
experiencing difficulties or conflict

Examples

The embattled CEO faced growing pressure from shareholders to resign.
Despite being embattled on all sides, she remained determined to accomplish her goals.
The embattled company struggled to stay afloat amid fierce competition and public scrutiny.

Synset Examples

the state legislature called for the embattled governor's resignation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: besieged - being surrounded and under attack, especially metaphorically
  • Synonym: troubled - experiencing problems or difficulties

Etymology

From Old French 'embataillier' (to fortify for battle), related to battle.

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈbætəld / (AmE), / ɪmˈbætəld / (BrE)
(of a place or person) involved in or prepared for battle or conflict, often under attack or criticism.
involved in or ready for conflict

Examples

The embattled fortress held out against enemy attacks for weeks.
The embattled mayor refused to resign despite mounting criticism.
Residents evacuated the embattled city as the fighting intensified.

Synset Examples

an embattled city

Related Terms

  • Synonym: besieged - (of a place) surrounded and under attack
  • Antonym: defenseless - without defense or protection

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'embatiller' (to fortify with battlements).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01849830-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ɪmˈbætəld / (AmE), / ɪmˈbatəld / (BrE)
(of a structure or object) having repeated square indentations resembling those in a battlement; crenellated.
having battlement-like indentations

Examples

The ancient castle had embattled walls overlooking the valley.
The designer added an embattled cornice to give the house a medieval appearance.
The town hall features an embattled parapet as part of its gothic revival style.

Synset Examples

a crenelated molding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crenellated - having a notched or indented pattern like a battlement

Etymology

From Old French 'embatuiller', related to 'bataille' (battle), referencing the defensive design of battlements.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01800618-s
Synset POS: s
