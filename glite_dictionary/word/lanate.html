lanate

1. ADJ

/ ˈleɪn.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
(of a surface) covered with dense, woolly or cottony hairs or hairlike filaments, especially in botany or zoology.
covered with dense woolly hairs

Examples

Some plants have lanate leaves to help minimize water loss in dry environments.
The caterpillar was easily recognized by its lanate appearance.
Under the microscope, the surface of the seed looked distinctly lanate.

Synset Examples

the woolly aphid has a lanate coat resembling cotton

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woolly - covered with or resembling wool

Etymology

From Latin lanatus, from lana meaning 'wool'.
