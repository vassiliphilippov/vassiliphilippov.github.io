residual

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɪdʒuəl / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪdjuəl / (BrE)
a payment made to a performer, writer, or director for each repeat showing of a television show or commercial
payment for TV/commercial repeats

Examples

Thanks to his residuals, the actor received checks every time the show aired in syndication.
She relied on residuals from her commercial work to supplement her income.
The writers negotiated for higher residuals as part of the new contract.

Synset Examples

he could retire on his residuals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: royalty - a sum paid to authors or creators based on use or sales of their work

Etymology

From Latin residuum (something remaining); adapted to financial meaning in 20th century entertainment industry.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13314012-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈzɪdʒuəl / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪdjuəl / (BrE)
remaining after the main part or quantity has gone or been removed; relating to a remainder.
remaining; relating to a remainder

Examples

The residual chemicals in the water made it unsafe to drink.
After paying all the bills, there was only a residual amount of money left.
Doctors monitored the residual effects of the medication on his system.

Synset Examples

residual quantity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remaining - still existing, present, or left after other parts are gone

Etymology

From Latin 'residuus' (remaining), from 'residere' (to remain).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02846539-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɪdʒuəl / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪdjuːəl / (BrE)
a quantity or part remaining after the main part has been removed or used
something remaining after removal

Examples

The scientist analyzed the residual left after the experiment concluded.
After dividing the estate, there was a small residual for charity.
Most of the money was spent, with only a minor residual remaining in the account.

Synset Examples

there was no remainder
he threw away the rest
he took what he wanted and I got the balance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remainder - the part left over after something is subtracted or removed
  • Synonym: surplus - an amount of something left over when requirements have been met
  • Antonym: deficit - the amount by which something is too small or lacking

Etymology

From Latin 'residuum' (that which remains), from 'residere' (to remain).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13833030-n
Synset POS: n
