unformed

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfɔrmd / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɔːmd / (BrE)
not fully developed, organized, or given shape; not formed yet.
not formed or organized

Examples

The committee's plans were still unformed when the deadline arrived.
His ideas remained unformed, lacking clarity and detail.
In its unformed state, the sculpture was just a lump of clay.

Synset Examples

an as yet unformed government

Related Terms

  • Antonym: formed - having a definite shape or structure

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (meaning 'not') + 'formed', from Latin 'formare' (to form).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01675814-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfɔrmd / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɔːmd / (BrE)
not having a definite form or shape; not shaped or structured
not having a definite form or shape

Examples

The artist began with a lump of unformed clay on the wheel.
Her ideas were still unformed but promising.
The mountain took an unformed shape in the early morning mist.

Synset Examples

unformed clay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formless - lacking a clear or definite shape or structure
  • Synonym: shapeless - without a definite or regular shape
  • Antonym: formed - having form or shape

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'formed' (from Latin 'formare' to form).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02159120-a
Synset POS: a
