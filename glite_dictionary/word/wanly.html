wanly

1. ADV

/ ˈwænli / (AmE), / ˈwɒnli / (BrE)
In a manner that is weak, pale, or showing a lack of energy or vitality.
in a weak or pale manner

Examples

She smiled wanly at her friends, trying to hide her exhaustion.
After the long illness, he waved wanly from his bed.
The little boy looked wanly at his untouched plate of food.

Synset Examples

she was smiling wanly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faintly - in a faint or weak manner
  • Antonym: vigorously - in a strong, energetic manner

Etymology

From 'wan' (pale, sickly), + '-ly' forming the adverb. 'Wan' from Old English 'wann' (dark, pallid).
