to patronize

1. VERB

/ ˈpeɪtrəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpætrənaɪz / (BrE)
to be a regular customer of a shop, restaurant, or business; to support by using its services or purchasing its goods
be a regular customer

Examples

We always patronize our local bakery instead of the big supermarket.
Many people prefer to patronize small, independent bookstores.
Despite moving, she continued to patronize her favorite hair salon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - to give help or encouragement to a business, event, etc.

Etymology

From French *patroniser*, from *patron* (patron, protector).

5. VERB

/ ˈpeɪtrəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpætrənaɪz / (BrE)
to treat someone in a way that shows you think you are more intelligent or important than they are
to treat condescendingly

Examples

Please do not patronize me; I can handle the task myself.
She hates it when people patronize her because of her age.
Stop patronizing your colleagues and try listening to their ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to condescend - to behave as if one is superior to others
  • Antonym: respect - to treat with consideration and esteem

Etymology

From French 'patroniser', from 'patron' (protector, master), ultimately from Latin 'patronus'.
