zori

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɔːri / (AmE), / ˈzɔːri / (BrE)
a traditional Japanese sandal typically made of rice straw or synthetic material, held on the foot by a thong between the toes
Japanese thong sandal

Examples

She wore a pair of zori with her summer kimono during the festival.
Traditional zori are often woven from rice straw or other plant fibers.
He slipped off his zori before entering the house, as is customary in Japan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flip-flop - a light sandal held on the foot by a thong between the big toe and the next toe

Etymology

From Japanese 'zōri'.
