Protohippus

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊtoʊˈhɪpəs / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəʊˈhɪpəs / (BrE)
an extinct genus of three-toed horses related to modern horses, from the late Miocene epoch
extinct genus of three-toed horses

Examples

Fossils of Protohippus have been discovered across North America.
The teeth of Protohippus suggest it was adapted to grazing on grasses.
Researchers believe Protohippus was an important step in horse evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Equidae - the biological family that includes horses, asses, and zebras

Etymology

From Greek proto- (first) + hippos (horse).
