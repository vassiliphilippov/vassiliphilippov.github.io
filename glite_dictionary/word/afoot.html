afoot

1. ADJ

/ əˈfʊt / (AmE), / əˈfʊt / (BrE)
walking; traveling on foot rather than using a vehicle or animal
walking; traveling on foot

Examples

The hikers set out afoot early in the morning to beat the heat.
After the car broke down, she continued her journey afoot.
Most of the villagers were afoot by sunrise, heading to the fields.

Synset Examples

she was afoot when I saw her this morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on foot - traveling by walking rather than by vehicle or animal

Etymology

From Middle English 'a foote', meaning 'on foot'.

2. ADV

/ əˈfʊt / (AmE), / əˈfʊt / (BrE)
(formal or literary) on foot; by walking rather than riding or driving.
walking; on foot

Examples

After the car broke down, we continued our journey afoot through the countryside.
The hunters traveled afoot for miles before spotting any game.
Pilgrims often made their way to holy sites afoot in earlier centuries.

Synset Examples

they went to the village afoot
quail are hunted either afoot or on horseback

Etymology

From Middle English 'a fot' (on foot), from Old English 'on fōt'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00240791-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADJ

/ əˈfʊt / (AmE), / əˈfʊt / (BrE)
happening or in progress; being planned or carried out
happening or in progress

Examples

There are changes afoot within the company that could affect all employees.
Rumors are afoot that the team will get a new manager soon.
It was clear that something mysterious was afoot in the dark woods.

Synset Examples

there is mischief afoot
plans are afoot
preparations for the trial are underway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: underway - in progress, currently happening
  • Synonym: pending - awaiting decision or settlement; ongoing

Etymology

From Middle English 'afot', from Old English 'on fōt' (on foot), originally referring to something being undertaken on foot.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00670007-s
Synset POS: s
