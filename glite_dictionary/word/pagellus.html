Pagellus

1. NOUN

/ pəˈɡɛləs / (AmE), / pəˈɡɛləs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Sparidae, commonly known as sea breams, found in European and Mediterranean waters.
a genus of marine fish (sea breams)

Examples

The Mediterranean is home to several species of Pagellus, prized by local fishermen.
Marine biologists are studying the population decline of Pagellus in coastal waters.
A freshly caught Pagellus can be recognized by its pinkish coloration and forked tail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea bream - any of various marine fish of the family Sparidae, especially those of the genera Pagellus and Pagrus

Etymology

From New Latin Pagellus, diminutive of Latin pagrus (a kind of sea fish).
