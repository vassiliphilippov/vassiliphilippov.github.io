merchandising

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrtʃənˌdaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɜːtʃəndaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity of buying and selling goods, especially in relation to promoting sales in retail or commercial settings
activity of buying and selling goods

Examples

He is in charge of merchandising for the entire company.
Effective merchandising can significantly boost a store's sales.
The new manager introduced innovative merchandising strategies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trade - the act of buying and selling goods and services
  • Synonym: retail - the sale of goods to the public in small quantities

Etymology

From 'merchandise' (goods bought and sold), from Old French 'marchandise', from 'marchant' (merchant).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrtʃənˌdaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɜːtʃəndaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity of promoting and selling goods, especially by advertising or displaying them effectively
promotion and selling of goods

Examples

The company improved its merchandising to attract more customers to its stores.
Effective merchandising requires understanding what appeals to consumers.
She studied merchandising in college to pursue a career in retail management.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marketing - the activity or business of promoting and selling products or services

Etymology

From 'merchandise' (Middle English, from Old French 'marchandise'), with the suffix -ing.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrtʃənˌdaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɜːtʃ(ə)nˌdaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
branded products created or sold to promote a film, pop group, or other entity, especially as a marketing strategy
branded products used for promotion

Examples

The movie's success was boosted by a wide range of merchandising, including toys and clothing.
Pop stars often increase their income through merchandising such as branded posters and T-shirts.
The new video game features extensive merchandising aimed at young fans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merchandise - goods that are bought or sold

Etymology

From 'merchandise' (goods to be bought and sold) with the suffix '-ing'.
