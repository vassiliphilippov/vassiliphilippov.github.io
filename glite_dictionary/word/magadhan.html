Magadhan

1. NOUN

/ məˈɡɑːdən / (AmE), / məˈɡɑːdən / (BrE)
A subfamily of Indic languages spoken in eastern India, including languages such as Magahi, Maithili, and Bhojpuri.
subfamily of Indic languages

Examples

The Magadhan languages have played a significant role in the linguistic history of India.
Magahi and Bhojpuri are both members of the Magadhan subfamily.
Many linguists study Magadhan languages to trace the evolution of Indo-Aryan speech.

Etymology

From Magadha, the ancient kingdom in eastern India where these languages originated.
