exorcist

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛksɔːrsɪst / (AmE), / ˈɛksəsɪst / (BrE)
a person who drives out evil spirits from a place or a person, usually through religious or ritual practices
person who drives out evil spirits

Examples

The exorcist entered the haunted house to perform a cleansing ceremony.
She called for an exorcist after strange events occurred in her home.
In many horror films, an exorcist is portrayed as a brave religious figure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exorcism - the act of driving out evil spirits

Etymology

From Late Latin exorcista, from Greek exorkistēs, meaning 'one who administers an oath'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛkˌsɔrsɪst / (AmE), / ˈɛksəsɪst / (BrE)
in the unreformed Western Church, a member of a minor clerical order whose duty was to exorcise evil spirits; this order is now suppressed in the Roman Catholic Church
minor clerical order in the historical Western Church

Examples

He studied to become an exorcist in the early years of the seminary.
The role of exorcist is no longer part of the current Roman Catholic minor orders.
Historically, an exorcist would assist priests in ceremonies of exorcism.

Etymology

From Late Latin exorcista, from Greek exorkistēs (an exorcist).
