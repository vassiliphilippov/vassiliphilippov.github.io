to spotweld

1. VERB

/ ˈspɑtˌwɛld / (AmE), / ˈspɒtˌwɛld / (BrE)
to join metal surfaces by fusing them together at small spots using heat or pressure, especially with an electric current
join metal by fusing at small spots

Examples

The technician will spotweld the metal pieces to create a secure joint.
These panels were spotwelded before final assembly.
During the repair, the mechanic carefully spotwelded the broken frame back together.

Synset Examples

These pipes are only spotwelded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weld - to join pieces of metal by melting them together

Etymology

From 'spot' + 'weld', describing welding at specific points.
