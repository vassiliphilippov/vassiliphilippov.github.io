versant

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɜːrsənt / (AmE), / ˈvɜːsənt / (BrE)
the side or slope of a mountain or hill
mountain or hill slope

Examples

Snow lingered on the northern versant long after spring arrived in the valley below.
The eastern versant of the mountain receives more rainfall due to prevailing winds.
Dense forests thrive along the southern versant where sunlight is more plentiful.

Synset Examples

conifer forests cover the eastern versant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slope - an area of ground that tends evenly upward or downward
  • Antonym: valley - a low area between hills or mountains, often with a river running through it

Etymology

From French 'versant' (slope), present participle of 'verser' (to turn), from Latin 'versare'.
