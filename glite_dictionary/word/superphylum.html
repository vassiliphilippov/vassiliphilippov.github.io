superphylum

1. NOUN

/ suː.pɚˈfaɪ.ləm / (AmE), / suː.pəˈfaɪ.ləm / (BrE)
(Biology) A taxonomic rank above a phylum and below a class or subclass, used to group related phyla together.
biological taxonomic group above phylum

Examples

Scientists classified the animal as part of the Ecdysozoa superphylum.
Chordates and echinoderms both belong to the Deuterostomia superphylum.
The discovery led biologists to propose a new superphylum for these organisms.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: phylum - a principal taxonomic category, ranking above class and below kingdom in biology

Etymology

From Latin 'super-' (above) + 'phylum' (race, tribe).
