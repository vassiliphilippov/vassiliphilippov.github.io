marang

1. NOUN

/ məˈrɑŋ / (AmE), / məˈræŋ / (BrE)
A tropical fruit native to the Philippines, consisting of a cluster of seeds surrounded by sweet, white pulp within a spiny rind.
tropical Philippine fruit with sweet white pulp

Examples

The marang tree thrives in the southern regions of the Philippines.
She tasted marang for the first time and loved its creamy, sweet flavor.
Vendors displayed piles of fresh marang at the local fruit market.

Etymology

From Cebuano or Tagalog 'marang', the local name for the fruit (Artocarpus odoratissimus).

2. NOUN

/ məˈrɑŋ / (AmE), / məˈrɑŋ / (BrE)
A Philippine tree (Artocarpus odoratissimus) related to the breadfruit, bearing an edible, sweet-smelling fruit.
Philippine tree with edible fruit

Examples

The marang tree produces sweet fruit that is popular in the southern Philippines.
Vendors at the market were selling fresh marang during the harvest season.
She described the flavor of marang as a mix between jackfruit and pineapple.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breadfruit - A tropical tree and its large starchy fruit (Artocarpus altilis)
  • Synonym: jackfruit - A tropical tree and its large edible fruit (Artocarpus heterophyllus)

Etymology

From Cebuano marang, the local Philippine name for the tree.
