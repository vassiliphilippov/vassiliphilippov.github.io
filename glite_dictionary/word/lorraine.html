Lorraine

1. PROPN

/ lɔːˈreɪn / (AmE), / lɒˈreɪn / (BrE)
a region in eastern France known for its rich iron-ore deposits and historical significance
region in eastern France

Examples

She spent the summer exploring the picturesque villages of Lorraine.
Lorraine has played a significant role in European history due to its strategic location.
The steel industry in Lorraine once thrived because of its abundant iron ore.

Etymology

From French Lorraine, derived from the medieval kingdom of Lotharingia.
