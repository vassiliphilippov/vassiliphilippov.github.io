health

1. NOUN

/ hɛlθ / (AmE), / hɛlθ / (BrE)
the general condition of a person's body or mind, especially with regard to fitness and absence of disease
general condition of body and mind

Examples

After weeks of rest, his health finally improved.
Maintaining good health requires regular exercise and a balanced diet.
Her family was concerned about her declining health.

Synset Examples

his delicate health
in poor health

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illness - state of being unwell or diseased
  • Synonym: wellness - state of being in good health

Etymology

From Old English hælth, from hæl (whole, sound, well).

2. NOUN

/ hɛlθ / (AmE), / hɛlθ / (BrE)
the condition of being free from illness or injury; a state of overall physical and mental well-being
state of physical and mental well-being

Examples

Good nutrition and regular exercise are essential for maintaining health.
Government policies can have a huge impact on public health.
Doctors are concerned about the long-term health effects of air pollution.

Synset Examples

physicians should be held responsible for the health of their patients

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illness - state of being unwell or diseased
  • Antonym: disease - a disorder causing illness in a person or animal
  • Synonym: wellness - the state of being in good health

Etymology

From Old English hælth, related to hæl (whole, safe, sound).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14471374-n
Synset POS: n
