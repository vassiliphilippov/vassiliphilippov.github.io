cavalryman

1. NOUN

/ ˈkævəlriˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈkævəlriˌman / (BrE)
A soldier who serves in a unit that attacks and moves swiftly, traditionally on horseback but now often in motorized vehicles.
soldier in a mounted or motorized cavalry unit

Examples

The experienced cavalryman led his squadron across the open plain.
As a young cavalryman, he learned to operate armored vehicles in modern warfare.
The cavalryman was proud of his regiment’s long-standing traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trooper - a private soldier in a cavalry unit

Etymology

From 'cavalry' (mounted troops) + 'man'; 'cavalry' from French 'cavalerie', from 'cheval' (horse).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkævəlriˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈkævəlriˌman / (BrE)
a soldier who fights on horseback, especially as part of a cavalry unit
soldier mounted on horseback

Examples

The cavalryman charged across the battlefield with determination.
Each cavalryman relied heavily on his horse for speed and maneuverability.
In historical reenactments, a skilled cavalryman can demonstrate impressive riding techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horseman - a person who rides horses, especially well or as a soldier

Etymology

From cavalry + man; 'cavalry' from French 'cavalerie', based on Italian 'cavalleria', from 'cavallo' (horse).
