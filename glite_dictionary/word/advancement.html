advancement

1. NOUN

/ ədˈvænsmənt / (AmE), / ədˈvɑːnsmənt / (BrE)
the act of encouraging or promoting the progress, growth, or development of something
encouragement of progress or growth

Examples

The new policy was aimed at the advancement of scientific research.
She dedicated her life to the advancement of human rights in her country.
Increased funding has led to the advancement of several important medical initiatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: promotion - the act of encouraging or supporting something to progress
  • Antonym: hindrance - something that provides resistance or obstruction to progress

Etymology

From Old French *avancement*, from avancer (to advance), from Latin *abante* (from before).

2. NOUN

/ ədˈvænsmənt / (AmE), / ədˈvɑːnsmənt / (BrE)
the process of moving forward or making progress towards a goal; improvement or development in a particular area
progress towards a goal

Examples

Her advancement in the company was the result of years of hard work and dedication.
Recent technological advancement has revolutionized the way we communicate.
The scholarship was intended to support the advancement of women in science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: progress - forward or onward movement toward a goal
  • Antonym: regression - a return to a previous or less developed state

Etymology

From Old French 'avancement', from 'avanter' (to advance), from Latin 'abante' (before, in front).

3. NOUN

/ ədˈvænsmənt / (AmE), / ədˈvɑːnsmənt / (BrE)
gradual improvement, growth, or development in a particular field or situation
gradual improvement or growth

Examples

The advancement of technology has changed how we communicate with each other.
Many organizations support the advancement of education through scholarships and funding.
Her research made a significant advancement in the treatment of heart disease.

Synset Examples

advancement of knowledge
great progress in the arts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: progress - forward or onward movement toward a destination or goal
  • Antonym: regression - a return to a previous less developed state

Etymology

From Old French avancer (to advance), from Late Latin abante (from before).
