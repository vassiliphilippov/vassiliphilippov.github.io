Geoglossum

1. NOUN

/ dʒiːˈɑɡ.lə.səm / (AmE), / dʒiːˈɒɡ.lə.səm / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Geoglossaceae, commonly known as earthtongues, characterized by club-shaped, dark fruiting bodies found on soil or decaying plant material.
genus of fungi (earthtongues)

Examples

The dark, slender fruiting bodies of Geoglossum are often found in wet meadows and woodlands.
Several species within the genus Geoglossum are considered rare and are protected in some regions.
Mycologists photographed a striking clump of Geoglossum growing among mosses in early autumn.

Etymology

From Greek geo- (earth) + glossa (tongue), referring to the tongue-like shape of the fungi.
