aldermanly

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔldərˌmænli / (AmE), / ˈɔːldəˌmænli / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of an alderman, especially in the manner, appearance, or conduct typical of one.
like or relating to an alderman

Examples

He has an aldermanly bearing that commands respect at council meetings.
Her aldermanly wisdom helped guide the committee through challenging decisions.
The event was marked by an aldermanly dignity seldom seen in local politics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alderman - a member of a municipal legislative body

Etymology

Formed from 'alderman' + '-ly', where 'alderman' derives from Old English 'ealdorman' (elder or chief man).
