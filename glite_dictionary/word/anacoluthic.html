anacoluthic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.ə.kəˈluː.θɪk / (AmE), / ˌan.ə.kəˈluː.θɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characterized by anacoluthon; having or involving a break in grammatical sequence within a sentence.
relating to a break in grammatical sequence

Examples

The author's anacoluthic style made the novel challenging but intriguing to read.
Her speech was filled with anacoluthic constructions that left listeners puzzled.
An anacoluthic sentence often mirrors spontaneous spoken language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anacoluthon - an abrupt change in grammatical or syntactical structure in a sentence

Etymology

From Greek anakólouthos (not following) via Late Latin anacoluthus, with English adjectival suffix -ic.
