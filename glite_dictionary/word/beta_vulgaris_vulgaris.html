Beta vulgaris vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ ˌbiːtə ˈvʌl.ɡə.rɪs ˈvʌl.ɡə.rɪs / (AmE), / ˌbiː.tə ˈvʌl.ɡə.rɪs ˈvʌl.ɡə.rɪs / (BrE)
A cultivated subspecies of beet with a large yellowish root, grown chiefly as cattle feed.
a variety of beet grown for cattle feed

Examples

Farmers often plant Beta vulgaris vulgaris to provide nutritious feed for their cattle.
New agricultural policies have encouraged the use of Beta vulgaris vulgaris due to its high yield.
The large yellowish root of Beta vulgaris vulgaris makes it especially suitable for livestock feed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mangel-wurzel - A type of beet grown for cattle feed with a large root

Etymology

From the scientific Latin name, where 'Beta' is the genus (beet) and 'vulgaris' means 'common'; repetition indicates a specific subspecies.
