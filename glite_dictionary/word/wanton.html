wanton

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɑn.tən / (AmE), / ˈwɒn.tən / (BrE)
(of a person or their behavior) sexually unrestrained or having many casual sexual relationships.
sexually unrestrained, promiscuous

Examples

Rumors about her wanton lifestyle quickly spread through the small town.
The play was criticized for its wanton depiction of sexuality and indulgence.
He accused her of wanton behavior, but others defended her personal choices.

Synset Examples

her easy virtue
he was told to avoid loose (or light) women
wanton behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: promiscuous - having or involving many sexual partners

Etymology

From Middle English wantowen (rebellious, lacking discipline), from wan- (lacking) + towen (discipline, upbringing).

/ ˈwɑn.tən / (AmE), / ˈwɒn.tən / (BrE)
(archaic) To engage in playful or flirtatious behavior, especially of a sexual nature.
to engage in amorous play (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The pair wantoned in the garden, laughing and teasing each other in the spring sunshine.
In the old plays, courtiers would often wanton with one another at lavish parties.
Poets wrote of nymphs who wantoned by the riverbanks, singing and dancing freely.

Related Terms

5. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːntən / (AmE), / ˈwɒntən / (BrE)
(archaic) A woman who behaves in a sexually unrestrained or lascivious manner.
sexually unrestrained woman (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The old tale warned of the dangers of becoming a wanton in a conservative society.
In the tragic play, she was reviled as a wanton by the townspeople.

(of an action) occurring without cause, reason, or provocation; deliberate and unrestrained.

Examples

He was accused of a wanton act of violence against his neighbor.
The wanton waste of resources during the festival was widely criticized.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
