Cornus obliqua

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrnəs əˈbli.kwə / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnəs əˈbliː.kwə / (BrE)
A species of shrub native to eastern North America, closely resembling the silky cornel.
North American shrub species

Examples

The wetland restoration project included planting Cornus obliqua to attract local birds and pollinators.
Botanists identified the shrub growing along the riverbank as Cornus obliqua, noting its similarity to silky cornel.
Gardeners value Cornus obliqua for its resilience and vibrant autumn foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silky cornel - A shrub species closely related to Cornus obliqua

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cornus' (dogwood genus) and 'obliqua', meaning 'slanting' or 'oblique'.
