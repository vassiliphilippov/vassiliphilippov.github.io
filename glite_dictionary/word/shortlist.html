shortlist

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɔrtˌlɪst / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːt.lɪst / (BrE)
a list of selected candidates or things that have been chosen from a larger list as the most suitable for a job, prize, or opportunity
list of selected candidates

Examples

The committee announced the shortlist for the literary award yesterday.
Only five applicants made it onto the final shortlist for the position.
After months of interviews, the selection panel compiled a shortlist of three candidates.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: longlist - a more extensive list from which a shortlist is chosen
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06507616-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈʃɔrt.lɪst / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːt.lɪst / (BrE)
to put someone or something on a short list for further consideration, typically for a job, award, or opportunity
to put on a list of selected candidates

Examples

After several interviews, the committee decided to shortlist five applicants for the final round.
Only the best proposals will be shortlisted for the grant funding opportunity.
We need to shortlist the most promising candidates before scheduling the next round of interviews.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: longlist - to put on a preliminary or initial list of candidates

Etymology

From 'short' + 'list'; in use since the mid-20th century for selecting candidates.
