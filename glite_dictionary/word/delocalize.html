to delocalize

1. VERB

/ diˈloʊkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diˈləʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
To remove something from its proper or usual place, especially in a scientific or technical context.
remove from usual place

Examples

In chemistry, electrons may delocalize and become shared among several atoms.
Global economic forces can delocalize industries, causing manufacturing to move abroad.
To delocalize the protein, the scientists altered its amino acid sequence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to localize - to confine or limit to a particular place

Etymology

From de- (removal) + localize; modelled on French délocaliser.
