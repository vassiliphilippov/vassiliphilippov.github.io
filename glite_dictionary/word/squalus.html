Squalus

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɑːləs / (AmE), / ˈskwɑːləs / (BrE)
A genus of small, slender, spiny dogfish sharks found in temperate and subarctic seas.
genus of spiny dogfish sharks

Examples

Marine biologists studied the behavior of Squalus in coastal waters.
Some species within the Squalus genus are important for fisheries.
The Squalus sharks are recognized by their prominent dorsal spines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogfish - a small shark of the family Squalidae

Etymology

From Latin *squalus* (a kind of sea fish or shark).
