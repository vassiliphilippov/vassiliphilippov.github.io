Haastia pulvinaris

1. NOUN

/ hæsˈtiːə pʌlˈvaɪnərɪs / (AmE), / hɑːsˈtiːə pʌlˈvɪnərɪs / (BrE)
A cushion-forming herbaceous plant native to New Zealand, with leaves densely covered in tawny hairs.
New Zealand cushion-forming herb

Examples

The alpine gardens displayed a remarkable specimen of Haastia pulvinaris thriving among the rocks.
Researchers noted that Haastia pulvinaris provides important ground cover in its native habitat.
The dense, tawny leaves of Haastia pulvinaris help protect it from harsh mountain winds.

Etymology

Genus named after John Francis Haast, 19th-century German-born New Zealand geologist; 'pulvinaris' from Latin meaning 'cushion-shaped'.
