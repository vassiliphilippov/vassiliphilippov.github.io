gomphothere

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑːmfəˌθɪr / (AmE), / ˈɡɒmfəˌθɪə / (BrE)
an extinct group of elephant-like mammals of Central and South America from the Miocene and Pleistocene epochs
extinct elephant-like mammal

Examples

Paleontologists discovered gomphothere fossils in a riverbed in Central America.
The gomphothere was distinguished by its elongated lower jaws and shovel-shaped tusks.
Many gomphothere species coexisted with early humans during the Pleistocene epoch.

Etymology

From Greek gomphos (peg, tooth) + thēra (beast), referring to their peg-like teeth.
