contrast

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnˌtræst / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.trɑːst / (BrE)
a clear difference or distinction between two or more things, especially when they are compared
a clear distinction or difference

Examples

There is a sharp contrast between the rural and urban lifestyles represented in the novel.
The contrast between light and dark was striking in the painting.
Her optimistic attitude stood in clear contrast to his pessimism.

Synset Examples

there is a narrow line between sanity and insanity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distinction - a difference or contrast between similar things or people
  • Antonym: similarity - the state of being similar; likeness; resemblance

Etymology

From Latin 'contrastare', meaning 'to stand against'.

7. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnˌtræst / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.trɑːst / (BrE)
the degree of difference between the lightest and darkest parts of an image, photographic negative, print, or television screen, affecting its clarity and detail
degree of light-dark difference in images

Examples

Photographers often adjust the contrast to make black-and-white images look more dramatic.
If the contrast on your television is too high, the colors may appear unnatural.
Increasing the contrast helped reveal the details in the shadows of the old photograph.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: brightness - the quality or state of giving out or reflecting light
  • Degree Higher: contrast ratio - the ratio of the luminance of the brightest color to that of the darkest color displayed on a screen

Etymology

From French 'contraste', from Italian 'contrasto', from 'contrastare' (to stand against).
