epitome

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈpɪtəmi / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪtəmi / (BrE)
A brief summary or abstract, especially of a written work.
brief summary or abstract

Examples

The editor provided an epitome of the lengthy report for quick reference.
This handbook is an epitome of the subject for busy readers.
His lecture offered an epitome of the major points from the article.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summary - a brief statement or account of the main points of something
  • Synonym: abridgment - a shortened version of a written work

Etymology

From Greek *epitomē* (abridgment), from *epi-* (in addition) + *temnein* (to cut).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈpɪtəmi / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪtəmi / (BrE)
a person or thing that is a perfect example of a particular quality or type
a perfect example; embodiment

Examples

She is the epitome of elegance and grace.
His actions were the epitome of generosity and kindness.
The quiet village is often described as the epitome of rural charm.

Synset Examples

he is the prototype of good breeding
he provided America with an image of the good father

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embodiment - a tangible or visible form of an idea, quality, or feeling
  • Synonym: paragon - a person or thing regarded as a perfect example of a particular quality
  • Antonym: antithesis - a person or thing that is the direct opposite of someone or something else

Etymology

From Greek 'epitomē' meaning 'abridgment', later extended to 'perfect example'.
