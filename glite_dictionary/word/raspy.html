raspy

1. ADJ

/ ˈræspi / (AmE), / ˈrɑːspi / (BrE)
(of a voice or sound) harsh and grating; unpleasantly rough or coarse to the ear.
harsh, grating (voice or sound)

Examples

The singer's raspy voice gave the song a unique edge.
After shouting all day, his voice became weak and raspy.
The old record had a raspy sound that added to its charm.

Synset Examples

a gravelly voice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoarse - rough or harsh-sounding voice
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even, gentle sound or texture; the opposite of 'raspy' in sound

Etymology

From 'rasp', meaning 'to scrape', plus the adjective suffix '-y'; related to the coarse effect of a rasp file.
