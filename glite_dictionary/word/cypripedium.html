Cypripedium

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪprɪˈpiːdiəm / (AmE), / ˌsɪprɪˈpiːdiəm / (BrE)
A genus of perennial, leafy-stemmed orchids native mainly to North America, commonly known as lady's slippers.
genus of orchids (lady's slipper)

Examples

The Cypripedium is prized by gardeners for its unique slipper-shaped flowers.
Many species of Cypripedium are native to the temperate forests of North America.
Conservationists are working to protect wild populations of Cypripedium from habitat loss.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek Kupris (epithet of Aphrodite) + pedilon (sandal).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɪprɪˈpiːdiəm / (AmE), / ˌsɪprɪˈpiːdiəm / (BrE)
a genus of perennial leafy-stemmed orchids, known as lady's slippers, found mainly in North America
a genus of orchids called lady's slippers

Examples

The Cypripedium genuses are prized by collectors for their distinctive slipper-shaped flowers.
Several species in the Cypripedium genus are native to temperate regions of North America.
Botanists have debated whether some Asian lady's slippers belong to Cypripedium or Paphiopedilum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Paphiopedilum - a related genus of Asian lady's slipper orchids

Etymology

From Latin *Cypripedium*, from Greek *Kypris* (Aphrodite, Venus) + *pedilon* (sandal), referring to the slipper-shaped flower.
