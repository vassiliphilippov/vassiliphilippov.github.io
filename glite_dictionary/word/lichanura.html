Lichanura

1. NOUN

/ laɪ.kəˈnuːr.ə / (AmE), / lɪ.kəˈnjʊə.rə / (BrE)
A genus of small, non-venomous boas native to western North America, commonly known as rubber boas.
a genus of North American boas

Examples

The Lichanura is often known as the rubber boa due to its smooth, loose skin.
Scientists recently observed a new behavior in Lichanura snakes during the breeding season.
Habitat preservation is important for the survival of the Lichanura population in California.

Etymology

From Greek *lichanos* (forefinger) + *oura* (tail), referring to tail shape.
