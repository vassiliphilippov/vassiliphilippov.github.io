upright

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈʌp.raɪt / (BrE)
A vertical post or structural support, especially one forming part of a framework or structure.
vertical post or support

Examples

The carpenter installed a new upright for the damaged fence.
She leaned her bicycle against the metal upright in the garage.
The ball hit the upright and bounced back onto the field.

Synset Examples

the ball sailed between the uprights

Etymology

Formed from the adjective 'upright', meaning vertical or erect.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04522424-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈʌp.raɪt / (BrE)
a piano in which the strings and soundboard are positioned vertically, allowing it to take up less floor space than a grand piano
a vertical piano

Examples

She learned to play the upright in her grandmother's living room.
The old upright took up little space against the wall.
They moved the heavy upright into the school auditorium.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: grand piano - a large piano with horizontal strings and soundboard
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04522298-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈʌpˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈʌp.raɪt / (BrE)
(of a position or object) vertical; placed or standing straight up, not leaning or sloping.
vertical; standing straight up

Examples

The statue must be kept in an upright position to avoid damage.
After the storm, only a few fence posts remained upright.
Make sure the bottle is standing upright before opening it.

Synset Examples

an upright post

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vertical - at right angles to the horizon; perpendicular
  • Antonym: horizontal - parallel to the horizon; not vertical

Etymology

From Old English upriht, from up + riht (right).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01236903-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈʌpˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈʌp.raɪt / (BrE)
(of a person or object) positioned vertically; standing straight up rather than lying or leaning.
vertical or standing straight up

Examples

He sat upright in his chair, listening attentively to the presentation.
The piano must be kept upright to prevent damage to its internal parts.
Tall trees stood upright along the path, casting long shadows at sunset.

Synset Examples

an erect stature
erect flower stalks
for a dog, an erect tail indicates aggression
a column still vertical amid the ruins
he sat bolt upright

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vertical - at right angles to the horizon; straight up and down
  • Antonym: horizontal - parallel to the ground; not vertical

Etymology

From Old English upriht, from up (up) + riht (right).

5. ADJ

/ ˈʌp.raɪt / (AmE), / ˈʌp.raɪt / (BrE)
(of a person) behaving in a morally correct and honest way; having strong principles.
morally correct and honest (of a person)

Examples

Sarah was known as an upright judge who always did what she believed was right.
Everyone respected him for being an upright citizen who never compromised his values.
They trusted her with their secrets because she had an upright character.

Synset Examples

a genuinely good person
a just cause
an upright and respectable man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: righteous - morally right or justifiable
  • Synonym: virtuous - having or showing high moral standards
  • Antonym: corrupt - having or showing a willingness to act dishonestly for personal gain

Etymology

From Old English 'uprihte', meaning 'erect, standing up'; figurative use for moral character dates from the 14th century.

Source: WordNet
