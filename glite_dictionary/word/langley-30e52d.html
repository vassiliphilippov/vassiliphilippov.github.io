langley

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæŋ.li / (AmE), / ˈlæŋ.li / (BrE)
a unit of solar radiation equal to one calorie per square centimeter, used to measure the amount of solar energy received at the Earth's surface
unit of solar radiation

Examples

The field station recorded over 600 langleys during the peak summer month.
Solar panels are designed to operate efficiently under several hundred langleys of sunlight per day.
Researchers expressed the average daily solar energy in langleys for easy comparison across sites.

Etymology

Named after Samuel Pierpont Langley (1834–1906), American astronomer and physicist.
