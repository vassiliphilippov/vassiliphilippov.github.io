to preconceive

1. VERB

/ ˌpriːkənˈsiːv / (AmE), / ˌpriːkənˈsiːv / (BrE)
to form an idea or opinion about something before having the evidence or experience to support it
form an idea in advance

Examples

It's important not to preconceive what the outcome will be before hearing all the facts.
You shouldn't preconceive ideas about people you've never met.
Many people preconceive issues based on stereotypes rather than objective information.

Synset Examples

a preconceived notion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prejudge - to judge beforehand, especially without sufficient evidence

Etymology

From Latin 'prae-' (before) and 'concipere' (to conceive, take in).
