ytterbium

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈtɜrbiəm / (AmE), / ɪˈtɜːbɪəm / (BrE)
a soft, silvery metal of the lanthanide series, used mainly in scientific research and certain specialized alloys
a chemical element, rare earth metal

Examples

Researchers used ytterbium to develop new, efficient lasers for telecommunications.
The mineral sample was found to contain traces of ytterbium along with other rare earth metals.
Because ytterbium is so reactive, it is stored in airtight containers to prevent oxidation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lanthanide - any of the fifteen metallic chemical elements with atomic numbers 57 through 71

Etymology

Named after Ytterby, a village in Sweden where its ore was first discovered.
