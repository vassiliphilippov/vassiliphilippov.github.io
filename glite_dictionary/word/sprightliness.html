sprightliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈspraɪt.lin.əs / (AmE), / ˈspraɪt.li.nɪs / (BrE)
liveliness and energy in manner, action, or expression
liveliness and energy

Examples

Her sprightliness brought joy to everyone at the party.
The sprightliness of the young dancers captivated the audience.
Despite his age, he moved with remarkable sprightliness and enthusiasm.

Synset Examples

it was a heavy play and the actors tried in vain to give life to it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vivacity - the quality of being attractively lively and animated
  • Synonym: liveliness - the quality of being full of life or energy
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of interest or energy

Etymology

From 'sprightly' + '-ness', 'sprightly' from Middle English directly or via 'sprite' meaning spirit.
