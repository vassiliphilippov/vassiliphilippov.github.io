to promote

1. VERB

/ prəˈmoʊt / (AmE), / prəˈməʊt / (BrE)
to advertise or publicize a product, idea, or event in order to increase sales or public awareness
advertise or publicize to boost sales

Examples

The company hired a famous actor to promote its new line of smartphones.
She works in marketing and helps promote various products on social media.
They launched a campaign to promote awareness of the brand among young adults.

Synset Examples

The salesman is aggressively pushing the new computer model
The company is heavily advertizing their new laptops

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to advertise - to make something known in public, especially to sell it

Etymology

From Latin promotus, past participle of promovēre (to move forward).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00978685-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ prəˈmoʊt / (AmE), / prəˈməʊt / (BrE)
(in chess or checkers) to change a pawn or piece reaching the opponent's end of the board into a more powerful piece, usually a queen in chess.
move pawn/piece to a superior one (in chess/checkers)

Examples

If a pawn reaches the eighth rank, you can promote it to a queen.
He skillfully managed to promote his pawn, turning the game in his favor.
You may choose which piece to promote your pawn to in chess.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to demote - to reduce to a lower rank or less powerful piece (general sense)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01082916-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ prəˈmoʊt / (AmE), / prəˈməʊt / (BrE)
to help something develop, increase, or become more successful; to support or actively encourage the progress of something
to support growth or development

Examples

The organization aims to promote literacy among children in rural areas.
Regular exercise can promote better mental health and overall well-being.
The campaign was launched to promote recycling in the community.

Synset Examples

I am promoting the use of computers in the classroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone or something
  • Antonym: hinder - to create difficulties that slow down progress or growth

Etymology

From Latin promotus, past participle of promovere ‘move forward’.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02560630-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ prəˈmoʊt / (AmE), / prəˈməʊt / (BrE)
to give someone a higher or more important position or rank within an organization
raise to a higher job position

Examples

After years of dedication, she was finally promoted to senior manager.
The company often promotes employees who show leadership potential.
He hopes to be promoted within the next year.

Synset Examples

John was kicked upstairs when a replacement was hired
Women tend not to advance in the major law firms
I got promoted after many years of hard work

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to demote - to lower someone to a less important position
  • Synonym: advance - to move someone forward to a higher position

Etymology

From Latin promovere, from pro- (forward) + movere (to move).

5. VERB

/ prəˈmoʊt / (AmE), / prəˈməʊt / (BrE)
(in chess or checkers) to change a pawn (or checker piece) into a more powerful piece by moving it to the final rank or row.
upgrade a piece in chess or checkers

Examples

If your pawn reaches the eighth rank, you can promote it to a queen.
He managed to promote his last checker and gained a significant advantage.
In the endgame, it's crucial to promote your pawns as quickly as possible.
