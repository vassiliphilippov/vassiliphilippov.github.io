thirdhand

1. ADV

/ ˈθɝdˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈθɜːdˌhænd / (BrE)
(of information) by means of being passed on through two other people; doubly indirect.
by indirect means, twice removed

Examples

She only heard about the accident thirdhand through a friend of a friend.
Much of what we know about that time comes thirdhand, not from direct witnesses.
The rumor was passed along thirdhand before it ever reached the manager.

Synset Examples

I got the news thirdhand

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: firsthand - (of information) coming directly from the original source
  • Degree Lower: secondhand - (of information) passed on by one intervening person

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'firsthand' and 'secondhand'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈθɜrdˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈθɜːdˌhænd / (BrE)
(of information or knowledge) obtained from two intermediaries rather than directly from the original source.
indirect; obtained from someone not directly involved

Examples

She only heard the story thirdhand and wasn't sure how much of it was true.
Much of the news we get about distant countries is thirdhand information.
His opinions were formed from thirdhand accounts rather than personal experience.

Synset Examples

a thirdhand report

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: secondhand - of information: received from another rather than directly

Etymology

Formed from 'third' + 'hand', after the pattern of 'secondhand'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01861099-s
Synset POS: s
