Ambloplites rupestris

1. NOUN

/ ˌæm.bləˈplaɪtiːz ruːˈpɛstrɪs / (AmE), / ˌæm.bləˈplaɪtiːz ruːˈpɛstrɪs / (BrE)
A species of freshwater fish, commonly known as the rock bass, native to the upper Mississippi River and Great Lakes regions and valued as both a game and food fish.
species of freshwater fish (rock bass)

Examples

The Ambloplites rupestris is prized by anglers throughout the Great Lakes region.
Many lakes in the Midwest are home to the Ambloplites rupestris.
Fried Ambloplites rupestris is considered a local delicacy in some areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rock bass - common name for Ambloplites rupestris, a freshwater fish species

Etymology

From Modern Latin; genus name 'Ambloplites', species 'rupestris', meaning 'dwelling among rocks.'
