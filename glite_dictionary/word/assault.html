assault

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɑlt / (AmE), / əˈsɔːlt / (BrE)
Close and direct fighting during the climax of a military attack, often involving intense physical combat.
close fighting in a military attack

Examples

The infantry advanced during the final assault on the fortress, engaging the enemy at close quarters.
Heavy casualties were reported in the assault that took place at dawn.
After hours of shelling, the assault began, with soldiers storming the enemy's defenses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attack - an aggressive military action against a place or enemy forces

Etymology

From Old French *asaut*, from Latin *assultus* (a leap, attack), from *assilire* (to leap upon).
