discriminating

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈskrɪməˌneɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɪmɪneɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
having or showing good judgment or insight, especially in matters of taste or appreciation
showing good judgment or taste

Examples

She has a discriminating palate and can easily notice subtle flavors.
The gallery attracts a discriminating clientele who appreciate fine art.
Only a discriminating reader would notice the clever references in the novel.

Synset Examples

the discriminating eye of the connoisseur

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discerning - showing good or outstanding judgment and understanding

Etymology

From the verb 'discriminate' (to distinguish), from Latin 'discriminare' (to divide, distinguish).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈskrɪməˌneɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɪmɪneɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
having or showing an ability to notice and understand small differences, especially in matters of taste or judgment; showing refined discernment
showing refined judgment or taste

Examples

She is a discriminating reader who appreciates high-quality literature.
Only the most discriminating customers will notice the subtle differences between these wines.
His discriminating taste in art made his collection especially valuable.

Synset Examples

an acute observer of politics and politicians
incisive comments
icy knifelike reasoning
as sharp and incisive as the stroke of a fang
penetrating insight
frequent penetrative observations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perceptive - having or showing sensitive insight
  • Antonym: indiscriminate - not marked by careful distinction; lacking selectivity

Etymology

From 'discriminate' + '-ing', ultimately from Latin 'discriminare' (to divide, distinguish).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01748764-s
Synset POS: s
