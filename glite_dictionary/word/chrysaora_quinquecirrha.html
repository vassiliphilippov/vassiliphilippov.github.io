Chrysaora quinquecirrha

1. NOUN

/ kraɪˈseɪ.ərə kwɪŋ.kwɪˈsɜːr.ə / (AmE), / kraɪˈseɪ.ərə kwɪŋ.kwɪˈsɜː.rə / (BrE)
A species of jellyfish commonly known as the Atlantic sea nettle, found along the Atlantic coast of North America.
species of jellyfish (Atlantic sea nettle)

Examples

Swimmers should be cautious in waters where Chrysaora quinquecirrha is known to inhabit.
Researchers collected several specimens of Chrysaora quinquecirrha for their jellyfish study.
The distinctive tentacles of Chrysaora quinquecirrha can deliver a painful sting.

Etymology

From Greek chrysaoros (golden sword) and Latin quinque (five) + cirrus (curl), describing its appearance.
