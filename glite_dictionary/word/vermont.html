Vermont

1. PROPN

/ vɚˈmɑnt / (AmE), / vəˈmɒnt / (BrE)
a state in the northeastern United States, in the region of New England, with Montpelier as its capital
a state in New England, USA

Examples

She grew up in Vermont and often speaks fondly of its beautiful landscapes.
Every autumn, tourists flock to Vermont to see the vibrant fall foliage.
Montpelier is the smallest U.S. state capital and is located in Vermont.

Etymology

From French 'Vert Mont,' meaning 'Green Mountain.'
