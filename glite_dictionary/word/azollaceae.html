Azollaceae

1. NOUN

/ əˌzɒləˈsiːˌiː / (AmE), / əˌzɒləˈsiːɪi / (BrE)
a family of small, aquatic ferns, often including the genus Azolla, used in some botanical classifications
family of small aquatic ferns

Examples

The Azollaceae are notable for their symbiotic relationship with cyanobacteria, allowing them to fix nitrogen.
Some botanists classify the genus Azolla as the sole member of the Azollaceae.
Wetlands in tropical regions often contain dense mats of ferns from the Azollaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Azolla - a genus of aquatic ferns often included in Azollaceae
  • Antonym: Salviniaceae - a related family of aquatic ferns distinguished from Azollaceae in some classifications

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name Azolla + the standard botanical family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ əˌzɑːləˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / əˌzɒləˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
A family of small, aquatic ferns, commonly known as the mosquito fern family, which includes the genus Azolla.
a family of aquatic ferns

Examples

The Azollaceae play an important role in the nitrogen cycle of freshwater habitats.
Scientists have studied the rapid growth rate of the Azollaceae for use in sustainable agriculture.
Fossils indicate that Azollaceae have existed for millions of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Azolla - a genus of small aquatic ferns in the family Azollaceae

Etymology

From genus name Azolla + Latin -aceae (family suffix in botanical taxonomy).
