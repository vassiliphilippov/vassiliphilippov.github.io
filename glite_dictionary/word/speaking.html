speaking

1. NOUN

/ ˈspiːkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspiːkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of delivering an address or speech to a public audience
delivering an address to an audience

Examples

She has always been nervous about speaking in front of large groups.
The conference featured several hours of speaking by prominent leaders.
His talent for speaking is evident whenever he steps onto the stage.

Synset Examples

people came to see the candidates and hear the speechmaking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speechmaking - the activity or process of making speeches in public

/ ˈspiːkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspiːkɪŋ / (BrE)
able to speak or involving the act of speaking, especially as distinguished from being silent or non-verbal.
capable of or involving speech

Examples

Many animals make sounds, but only humans are truly speaking creatures.
She finally landed a speaking role in the film after months of auditions.
His was the only speaking part among a group of silent performers.

3. NOUN

/ ˈspiː.kɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspiː.kɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of expressing thoughts in spoken words; the utterance of intelligible speech
the act of expressing thoughts in spoken words

Examples

After weeks of silence, her speaking improved remarkably during therapy.
Public speaking is a valuable skill for many professionals.
The event featured a speaking competition for high school students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speech - the expression of or the ability to express thoughts and feelings by articulate sounds

Etymology

From the verb 'speak' + -ing, denoting the action or process.

Source: WordNet

/ ˈspiːkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspiːkɪŋ / (BrE)
(of an animal or being) capable of speech or communicating with spoken language.
capable of speech

Examples

The story described a world where every speaking creature shared its wisdom.
As a speaking species, we are unique among primates.

Synset Examples
