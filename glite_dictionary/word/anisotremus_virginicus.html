Anisotremus virginicus

1. NOUN

/ ˌænɪsəˈtriːməs vərˈdʒɪnɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌænɪsəˈtriːməs vɜːˈdʒɪnɪkəs / (BrE)
A species of black and gold grunt fish found in the western Atlantic, from Bermuda and the Caribbean to Brazil.
a black and gold Atlantic grunt fish species

Examples

Divers often spot the colorful Anisotremus virginicus near coral reefs in the Caribbean.
The Anisotremus virginicus is easily recognized by its distinct black and gold stripes.
Marine biologists studied the habitat preferences of Anisotremus virginicus along the Brazilian coast.

Etymology

From the genus name Anisotremus (Greek aniso-, 'unequal' + trema, 'hole') and species name virginicus (Latin, 'of the Virgin Islands').
