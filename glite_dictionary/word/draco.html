Draco

1. PROPN

/ ˈdreɪkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdreɪkəʊ / (BrE)
a faint constellation in the northern sky, winding between Ursa Major and Cepheus, near the North Celestial Pole.
faint northern sky constellation

Examples

The constellation Draco can be seen winding between Ursa Major and Cepheus.
She pointed out Draco in the clear night sky near the North Celestial Pole.
Legend says Draco represents the dragon slain by Hercules.

Etymology

From Latin dracō (dragon), used for the constellation's resemblance to a dragon.

2. PROPN

/ ˈdreɪkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdreɪkəʊ / (BrE)
An Athenian lawmaker of the 7th century BC, known for creating a harsh legal code that prescribed death for many offenses.
Athenian lawmaker known for harsh laws

Examples

Many historians credit Draco with creating Athens' first written law code.
The word 'draconian' comes from the reputation of Draco's laws for their severity.
Little is known about Draco's life outside of his infamous legal reforms.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: draconian - (of laws or their application) excessively harsh or severe

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Drakōn (Δράκων), the name of the historical legislator.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdreɪkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdreɪkəʊ / (BrE)
a genus of reptiles known as flying dragons or flying lizards, found in Southeast Asia, with elongated ribs and skin flaps that allow them to glide between trees.
a reptile genus; flying dragons

Examples

The Draco lizards can glide through the air for distances of up to 10 meters.
Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Draco in the rainforests of Borneo.
Many people are fascinated by the wing-like membranes of Draco, which help them escape predators.

Etymology

From Latin 'draco' meaning 'dragon', itself from Greek 'drakōn'.
