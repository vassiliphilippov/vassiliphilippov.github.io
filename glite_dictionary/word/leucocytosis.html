leucocytosis

1. NOUN

/ luːkoʊsaɪˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / luːkəʊsaɪˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
an increase in the number of white blood cells in the blood, often indicating infection or disease
increase of white blood cells in blood
medical

Examples

The laboratory results confirmed leucocytosis in the patient's blood sample.
Doctors noted pronounced leucocytosis as a sign of acute infection.
Persistent leucocytosis may require further investigation for underlying causes.

Etymology

From Greek leukos (white) + kytos (cell) + -osis (condition).
