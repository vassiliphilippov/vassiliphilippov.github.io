argon

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrˌgɑn / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡɒn / (BrE)
a chemical element (symbol Ar), a colorless, odorless inert gas that comprises about 1% of the Earth's atmosphere
a colorless inert gas; element Ar

Examples

The light bulbs in this room are filled with argon to prevent the filament from burning out quickly.
Because argon is chemically inert, it is often used in welding to protect metals from oxidation.
Scientists detected traces of argon in the Martian atmosphere similar to levels found on Earth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neon - a colorless inert gas, chemical element Ne
  • Synonym: krypton - a colorless inert gas, chemical element Kr
  • Synonym: helium - a colorless inert gas, chemical element He

Etymology

From Greek 'argos' (inactive), referring to its chemical inertness.
