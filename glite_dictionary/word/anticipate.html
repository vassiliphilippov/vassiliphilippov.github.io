to anticipate

1. VERB

/ ænˈtɪsɪˌpeɪt / (AmE), / ænˈtɪsɪpeɪt / (BrE)
to realize or become aware of something before it happens; to expect or predict a future event or situation
to realize or expect in advance

Examples

She could anticipate his reaction before he even spoke.
Economists anticipate an increase in inflation next year.
We must anticipate possible problems and prepare solutions in advance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expect - to regard something as likely to happen
  • Synonym: to predict - to say what will happen in the future

Etymology

From Latin anticipatus, past participle of anticipare (to take before, anticipate).

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ænˈtɪsɪˌpeɪt / (AmE), / ænˈtɪsɪpeɪt / (BrE)
to look forward to something with excitement or anxiety; to expect with eagerness or apprehension.
expect with excitement or anxiety

Examples

She anticipated the party all week with growing excitement.
Many students anticipate the results of the exam with nervousness.
He anticipated hearing from his friend and checked his phone repeatedly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expect - to regard (something) as likely to happen
  • Antonym: dread - to anticipate with fear or apprehension

Etymology

From Latin anticipare (to take before), from ante (before) + capere (to take).
