stockbroker

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑkˌbroʊkər / (AmE), / ˈstɒkˌbrəʊkə / (BrE)
a person or firm that buys and sells stocks and other securities on behalf of clients
agent buying and selling stocks and bonds

Examples

After graduation, she became a successful stockbroker in New York City.
The stockbroker advised his client to diversify her investments.
Many people rely on a stockbroker for expert financial advice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broker - a person who acts as an agent in financial transactions
  • Degree Lower: client - a person who uses professional services

Etymology

From 'stock' (meaning shares) + 'broker' (agent who buys and sells).
