sisal

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈsaɪs(ə)l / (BrE)
a tropical plant with large fleshy leaves that yield a stiff fiber used especially for making rope and twine; also, the fiber obtained from this plant.
a plant yielding stiff fiber for rope, twine, etc.

Examples

The strong fiber from sisal is used to make durable ropes and twine.
Farmers in Kenya have started cultivating sisal as a cash crop.
He purchased a sisal rug for its natural look and resilience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agave - a genus of plants that includes sisal

Etymology

From Spanish 'sisal', named after the port of Sisal in Yucatán, Mexico, from where the fiber was exported.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪzəl / (BrE)
a strong, durable fiber obtained from the leaves of the agave plant, used especially for making rope and twine
fiber from agave plant for rope or twine

Examples

The company uses sisal to manufacture environmentally friendly rope products.
Traditional mats in the region are woven from sisal fibers.
She bought a roll of sisal twine for her gardening projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemp - a plant fiber used for rope and textiles

Etymology

From Spanish 'sisal', the name of a port in Yucatán, Mexico, from which the fiber was exported.
