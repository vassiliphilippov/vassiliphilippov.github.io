chockablock

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɑk.əˌblɑk / (AmE), / ˈtʃɒk.əˌblɒk / (BrE)
completely full or crowded with people or things; packed to capacity
packed full; overcrowded
informal

Examples

The shelves in the bookstore were chockablock with new titles after the holidays.
During the festival, the streets become chockablock with tourists and stalls.
Her suitcase was chockablock with clothes, leaving no room for souvenirs.

Synset Examples

chowder chockablock with pieces of fish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crammed - filled completely or overfilled
  • Synonym: packed - filled to capacity with people or things
  • Antonym: empty - containing nothing; not filled

Etymology

Originally a nautical term, from 'chock' + 'a' + 'block', referring to blocks in a tackle touching each other (19th century).
