Tammuz

1. NOUN

/ təˈmuz / (AmE), / təˈmuːz / (BrE)
The tenth month of the civil year and the fourth month of the ecclesiastical year in the Hebrew calendar, corresponding to June and July.
month in the Hebrew calendar (June-July)

Examples

The fast of the seventeenth of Tammuz marks the beginning of a period of mourning in Judaism.
According to the Hebrew calendar, they were married in Tammuz.
Many important historical events are commemorated during the month of Tammuz.

Etymology

From Hebrew *Tammuz*, originally the name of an ancient Mesopotamian deity.

2. PROPN

/ tɑːˈmuːz / (AmE), / ˈtæmʊz / (BrE)
An ancient Sumerian and Babylonian god of pastures and vegetation, who was the consort of Inanna.
Sumerian-Babylonian god of vegetation

Examples

Many ancient rituals in Mesopotamia honored Tammuz as the god who died and was reborn each year.
Some stories describe Tammuz as the husband of the goddess Inanna.
The annual mourning for Tammuz reflected the cycle of life, death, and renewal in nature.

Etymology

From Akkadian *Tammūz*, itself from Sumerian *Dumuzi*.
