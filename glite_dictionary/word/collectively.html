collectively

1. ADV

/ kəˈlɛktɪvli / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktɪvli / (BrE)
As a group; together; in combination.
as a group; together

Examples

The award will be shared collectively among all team members.
The changes, taken collectively, will improve the company's efficiency.
Our efforts collectively resulted in a successful project.

Synset Examples

our salaries collectively couldn't pay for the damage

Related Terms

  • Antonym: individually - separately; one at a time

Etymology

From 'collective' + '-ly'. 'Collective' from Latin 'collectivus'.
