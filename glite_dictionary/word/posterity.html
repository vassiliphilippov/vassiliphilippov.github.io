posterity

1. NOUN

/ pəˈstɛrəti / (AmE), / pɒˈstɛrəti / (BrE)
all future generations of people collectively
all future generations

Examples

We must protect the environment for the sake of posterity.
The artist hoped his work would be remembered by posterity.
Important historical documents were preserved for posterity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ancestry - one's family or ancestral line that came before

Etymology

From Latin 'posteritas' (future time, descendants), from 'posterus' (coming after).

2. NOUN

/ poʊˈstɛr.ɪ.ti / (AmE), / pɒˈstɛr.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
All future generations of people; the descendants of a particular person or group.
future generations; descendants

Examples

We want to leave a better world for our posterity.
The ancient laws were intended to guide posterity as well as the present generation.
Artists often hope their work will be appreciated by posterity long after they are gone.

Synset Examples

we must secure the benefits of freedom for ourselves and our posterity

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ancestry - one's family or ancestral line
  • Synonym: descendants - people who come from a particular ancestor

Etymology

From Latin 'posteritas', from 'posterus' (coming after).
