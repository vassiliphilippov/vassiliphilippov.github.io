bass

1. NOUN

/ beɪs / (AmE), / beɪs / (BrE)
a male singer with the lowest vocal range in classical or choral music
low-pitched male singer

Examples

The choir was looking for a bass to complete its harmonies.
He has sung as a bass in several renowned opera companies.
The bass carried the melody in the final chorus.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: baritone - a male singing voice between bass and tenor
  • Degree Higher: tenor - the highest adult male singing voice

Etymology

From Italian basso (low), from Latin bassus (thick, short, low).
