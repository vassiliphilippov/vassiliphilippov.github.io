Elizabeth Taylor

1. PROPN

/ ɪˈlɪzəbəθ ˈteɪlər / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪzəbəθ ˈteɪlə / (BrE)
(1932–2011) An English-American film actress known for her beauty, acting talent, and roles in classic films such as 'Cleopatra' and 'Who's Afraid of Virginia Woolf?'.
English-American film actress (1932–2011)

Examples

Elizabeth Taylor won two Academy Awards for Best Actress during her illustrious career.
The film 'Cleopatra' starred Elizabeth Taylor and Richard Burton and became widely known for its lavish production.
Elizabeth Taylor was celebrated as much for her humanitarian work as for her iconic roles on screen.
