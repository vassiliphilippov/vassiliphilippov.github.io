Cantharellus floccosus

1. NOUN

/ kænˈθæɹələs flɑˈkəʊsəs / (AmE), / kanˈθarələs flɒˈkəʊsəs / (BrE)
a species of fungus with a hollow trumpet-shaped fruiting body, noted for being mildly poisonous
a mildly poisonous trumpet-shaped fungus

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with Cantharellus floccosus, their orange caps standing out among the leaves.
Because Cantharellus floccosus can cause stomach upset, foragers are advised not to eat them.
Mushroom guides warn of the mildly poisonous nature of Cantharellus floccosus.

Etymology

From Greek 'kantharos' (drinking cup) and Latin 'floccosus' (tufted, woolly).
