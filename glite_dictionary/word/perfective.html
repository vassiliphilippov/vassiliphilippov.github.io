perfective

1. NOUN

/ pərˈfɛktɪv / (AmE), / pəˈfɛktɪv / (BrE)
(Grammar) The aspect of a verb that indicates an action or event has been completed.
aspect expressing completed action

Examples

In Russian, verbs distinguish between the perfective and imperfective aspects.
The perfective aspect focuses on actions that have been completed.
Spanish uses the preterite tense to convey a perfective meaning.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: imperfective - aspect indicating ongoing or habitual action

Etymology

From Late Latin 'perfectivus', from Latin 'perfectus' (completed, finished).

2. NOUN

/ pərˈfɛktɪv / (AmE), / pəˈfɛktɪv / (BrE)
a grammatical aspect or tense of verbs that expresses a completed action or state, often contrasted with the imperfective
verb aspect showing completed action

Examples

In Russian, the perfective conveys that an action has been fully completed.
The perfective is commonly contrasted with the imperfective aspect in many languages.
She studied the perfective in her introduction to Slavic grammar.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: imperfective - verb aspect indicating ongoing or incomplete action
  • Synonym: perfect - grammatical tense/aspect expressing completed action

Etymology

From Medieval Latin perfectivus, from Latin perfectus (completed, finished).
