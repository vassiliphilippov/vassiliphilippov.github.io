pounce

1. VERB

/ paʊns / (AmE), / paʊns / (BrE)
to move suddenly down on something, especially in order to attack or seize it
swoop down to attack or seize

Examples

The cat waited silently before it pounced on the unsuspecting mouse.
Reporters pounced on the celebrity as soon as she left the building.
The child watched the hawk pounce from the tree onto its prey.

Synset Examples

The raptor swooped down on its prey
The teacher swooped down upon the new students

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swoop - to sweep or move rapidly downward through the air

2. NOUN

/ paʊns / (AmE), / paʊns / (BrE)
a sudden jump or movement to seize or attack, especially by an animal or in a figurative sense
a sudden leap or attack

Examples

With a swift pounce, the cat caught the mouse before it could escape.
The athlete made a pounce for the ball, stunning the opposing team.
Investors waited for the perfect moment to make their pounce on the undervalued stocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leap - a sudden jump upwards or forwards
  • Antonym: retreat - the act of withdrawing or pulling back

Etymology

Possibly from Middle French 'poncher' (to prick, pierce).
