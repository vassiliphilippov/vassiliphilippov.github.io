deaf-muteness

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛfˈmjutnəs / (AmE), / ˌdɛfˈmjuːtnəs / (BrE)
Congenital deafness that results in the inability to speak.
inability to speak due to congenital deafness
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that deaf-muteness can result from untreated congenital hearing loss.
Society has made significant progress in supporting individuals with deaf-muteness.
Early intervention can sometimes reduce the impact of deaf-muteness on children's language development.

Etymology

From 'deaf' (unable to hear) + 'muteness' (inability to speak).
