barley

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑr.li / (AmE), / ˈbɑː.li / (BrE)
A hardy cereal plant, Hordeum vulgare, whose grain is used for food, brewing, and animal feed.
type of cereal grain

Examples

They harvested the golden barley fields in late summer.
The soup recipe calls for a cup of barley to add texture and flavor.
Barley is often used as a key ingredient in brewing beer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheat - a widely grown cereal grain used for flour
  • Synonym: oat - a cereal plant cultivated for its edible grain
  • Synonym: rice - a cereal grain consumed as a staple food

Etymology

Old English *bærlic*, from *bere* (barley) + *-lic* (like).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrli / (AmE), / ˈbɑːli / (BrE)
A hardy cereal plant cultivated for its grain, used as food, in brewing, and as animal fodder.
a cereal grain plant

Examples

The farmer harvested a field of barley in early summer.
Barley is one of the main ingredients used in the production of beer.
Some health foods include barley as a high-fiber cereal option.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheat - a cereal grain also grown widely as food
  • Synonym: oat - a cereal plant whose grain is used as food and fodder

Etymology

Old English *bærlic*, from *bere* (barley) + *-lic* (like, ly), related to Old Norse *bygg* (barley).
