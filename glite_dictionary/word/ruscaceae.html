Ruscaceae

1. NOUN

/ ruːˈskeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ruːˈskeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants formerly recognized in some classification systems, often treated as a subfamily of Liliaceae; not widely accepted in modern taxonomy
a family of flowering plants (botany)
medical

Examples

The Ruscaceae were once considered a separate family of flowering plants.
Some botanists now classify plants formerly in Ruscaceae within the Asparagaceae family.
Research on Ruscaceae has contributed to our understanding of plant evolution.

Etymology

From the genus name Ruscus, plus the standard botanical family suffix -aceae.
