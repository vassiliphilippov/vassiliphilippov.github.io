developing

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈvɛləpɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈvɛləpɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of treating photosensitive material, such as photographic film, with chemicals to make an image visible
processing film to make images visible

Examples

The photographer spent hours in the darkroom developing his rolls of film.
Proper developing is crucial to achieving high-quality photographic prints.
Advances in technology have made developing photographs much faster and more convenient.

Synset Examples

the development and printing of his pictures took only two hours

Related Terms

  • Synonym: development - the process in which something passes by degrees to a different stage, especially a more advanced or mature stage
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈvɛləpɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈvɛləpɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a country or region) having a less advanced industrial base and lower living standards relative to other countries or regions, often characterized by a shortage of capital needed for industrialization.
having less advanced industry or economy

Examples

Many organizations provide aid to developing countries to support education and health care initiatives.
The conference focused on the challenges faced by developing nations in achieving sustainable growth.
Access to clean water remains a major issue in several developing regions of the world.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: developed - having a highly advanced industrial economy
