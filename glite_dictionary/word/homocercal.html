homocercal

1. ADJ

/ ˌhoʊməˈsɝːkəl / (AmE), / ˌhɒməˈsɜːkəl / (BrE)
(of a fish's tail) having the upper and lower lobes symmetrical and extending beyond the end of the vertebral column, as in most bony fishes.
having a symmetrical fish tail

Examples

Most modern bony fish have a homocercal tail, which aids in efficient swimming.
The homocercal tail distinguishes these fish from older lineages with asymmetrical tails.
Scientists noted the homocercal shape in the fossilized specimen's caudal fin.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heterocercal - having a fish tail with unequal lobes, the vertebral column turning upward into the upper lobe

Etymology

From Greek 'homo-' (same) + 'kerkos' (tail).
