Brasenia

1. NOUN

/ brəˈsiːniə / (AmE), / brəˈsiːniə / (BrE)
A small genus of aquatic plants in the family Nymphaeaceae, commonly known as water-shields, found in still or slow-moving waters.
genus of aquatic plant (water-shield)

Examples

The pond is home to several species, including the aquatic plant Brasenia.
Researchers observed that Brasenia forms a gelatinous coating on its leaves to deter herbivores.
In some regions, young Brasenia leaves are harvested for consumption.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, formed as a genus name in botanical Latin.

2. NOUN

/ brəˈsiːniə / (AmE), / brəˈsiːniə / (BrE)
A small genus of aquatic plants in the water-lily family (Nymphaeaceae), commonly known as water shields.
a genus of aquatic plants (water shield)

Examples

Brasenia is widely distributed in North America and eastern Asia in freshwater ponds and lakes.
The gelatinous coating of Brasenia leaves protects them from herbivorous insects.
Researchers studied the ecological role of Brasenia in wetland habitats.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Nymphaeaceae - The water-lily family of flowering aquatic plants

Etymology

From New Latin, named after C.C. Brasen, a Danish botanist.
