Chorizagrotis auxiliaris

1. NOUN

/ kɔːˌraɪzəˈgroʊtɪs ɔːkˌsɪliˈɛərɪs / (AmE), / kɒˌraɪzəˈɡrɒtɪs ɔːkˌsɪliˈɑːrɪs / (BrE)
A species of noctuid moth whose larvae, known as armyworms, travel in large groups and can destroy grains and alfalfa crops in the midwestern United States.
species of moth harmful to crops

Examples

The larvae of Chorizagrotis auxiliaris can devastate entire fields of alfalfa in just a few days.
Farmers in the Midwest often dread outbreaks of Chorizagrotis auxiliaris due to the damage they cause to grain crops.
Researchers are developing biological controls to combat Chorizagrotis auxiliaris infestations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armyworm - larvae of various moth species that travel in groups and damage crops
