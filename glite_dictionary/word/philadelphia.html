Philadelphia

1. PROPN

/ ˌfɪləˈdɛlfiə / (AmE), / ˌfɪl.əˈdɛl.fi.ə / (BrE)
A large city in southeastern Pennsylvania on the Delaware River, known for its historical significance as the site of Independence Hall and the signing of the Declaration of Independence and the U.S. Constitution.
a major city in Pennsylvania, USA

Examples

The historic sites of Philadelphia attract millions of tourists each year.
After moving to Philadelphia, she quickly adapted to the vibrant city life.
Philadelphia hosted the signing of key documents in American history.

Etymology

From Greek 'philadelphia', meaning 'brotherly love' (philos 'loving' + adelphos 'brother').
