jollity

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
a cheerful and lively state of being full of happiness and good humor; merriment or gaiety
cheerful, lively merriment; gaiety

Examples

The sound of jollity filled the house during the holiday celebration.
Despite the cold weather, the atmosphere at the fair was one of pure jollity.
Her infectious jollity brightened every gathering she attended.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merriment - cheerful fun or enjoyment
  • Antonym: gloom - a state of sadness or depression

Etymology

From Middle English jolite, from Old French jolivet, from joli (pretty, festive).
