Sinapis arvensis

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈneɪpɪs ɑːrˈvɛnsɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈneɪpɪs ɑːˈvɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A weedy Eurasian plant, commonly known as wild mustard, often found as a pest in grain fields.
species of wild mustard plant

Examples

Sinapis arvensis is commonly found in wheat fields, where it can compete with crops for nutrients.
Farmers often try to control Sinapis arvensis because it spreads rapidly in agricultural land.
The yellow flowers of Sinapis arvensis make it easy to spot among other weeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild mustard - common name for Sinapis arvensis

Etymology

From Latin: 'sinapis' meaning mustard and 'arvensis' meaning 'of the field.'
