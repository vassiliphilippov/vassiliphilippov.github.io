to lenify

1. VERB

/ ˈlɛnɪfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈlɛnɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make (someone or something) more gentle or mild; to soothe or appease
make milder or more gentle; appease

Examples

His calm words helped to lenify the tense atmosphere in the meeting room.
The soothing music seemed to lenify her troubled spirit after a stressful day.
She tried to lenify the criticisms by offering a sincere apology.

Synset Examples

She managed to mollify the angry customer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mollify - to calm or soothe someone's anger or feelings
  • Synonym: to appease - to pacify or placate someone by acceding to their demands
  • Antonym: to exacerbate - to make a problem or negative feeling worse

Etymology

From Latin *lenificare* (to soften, make mild), from *lenis* (gentle, mild) + *-ficare* (to make).
