Eira barbara

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪrə bɑːrˈbɑːrə / (AmE), / ˈeɪərə bɑːˈbɑːrə / (BrE)
A long-tailed, arboreal mammal of the weasel family, native to Central and South America, also known as the tayra.
Central and South American mammal, tayra

Examples

The Eira barbara is an agile climber often found in tropical forests.
Scientists observed the Eira barbara hunting small mammals in the rainforest canopy.
The Eira barbara, or tayra, uses its long tail for balance while moving through the trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tayra - another name for Eira barbara, a long-tailed arboreal mustelid

Etymology

From modern Latin, after the genus Eira and species name barbara.
