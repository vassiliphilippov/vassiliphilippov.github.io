Aegina

1. PROPN

/ eɪˈdʒaɪnə / (AmE), / iːˈdʒiːnə / (BrE)
an island in the Saronic Gulf of the Aegean Sea, near the coast of Greece
Greek island in the Saronic Gulf

Examples

Many tourists visit Aegina each year to enjoy its beaches and historic sites.
Ferries to Aegina depart regularly from the port of Piraeus.
Pistachio nuts are one of the famous products of Aegina.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Αἴγινα (Aígina), the name of the island.

2. NOUN

/ iˈdʒaɪnə, ɪˈdʒinə / (AmE), / iːˈdʒiːnə, ɪˈdʒiːnə / (BrE)
A genus of small medusae (jellyfish) found in marine environments.
a genus of small jellyfish

Examples

Researchers recently discovered a new species of Aegina in the Pacific Ocean.
The delicate bell of the Aegina is easily damaged by strong currents.
Marine biologists often study Aegina to learn about jellyfish locomotion.

Etymology

From Latin Aegina, from Ancient Greek Αἴγινα (Aígina), a mythological name.
