mound

1. NOUN

/ maʊnd / (AmE), / maʊnd / (BrE)
a heap or pile formed by gathering objects or substances on top of each other
a pile or heap of objects

Examples

She placed a mound of clothes on the bed to sort through.
There was a mound of paperwork on his desk after the holiday.
The children built a mound of sand at the beach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pile - a heap of things laid or lying one on top of another
  • Synonym: heap - an untidy collection of objects placed on top of each other
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07978094-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ maʊnd / (AmE), / maʊnd / (BrE)
In baseball, the slightly raised area of ground where the pitcher stands to throw the ball to the batter.
raised area where a pitcher stands (baseball)

Examples

The pitcher walked slowly to the mound before starting the next inning.
After his strong performance on the mound, he was named player of the game.
Groundskeepers worked to smooth the mound after the rain delay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitcher's mound - the raised area on a baseball field where the pitcher stands
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03797867-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ maʊnd / (AmE), / maʊnd / (BrE)
to form (a substance or material) into a pile or raised mass; to heap up into a rounded elevation
to form into a pile or raised mass

Examples

After the flood, workers mounded earth along the riverbank to prevent further damage.
She carefully mounded the mashed potatoes on each plate before serving.
The gardener mounded the soil around the base of the plant to protect its roots.

Synset Examples

mound earth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heap - to pile up or amass (something)

4. NOUN

/ maʊnd / (AmE), / maʊnd / (BrE)
an artificial heap or bank of earth or stones, often created for protection, burial, or as a landmark
an artificial heap of earth or stones

Examples

Archaeologists discovered a burial mound dating back thousands of years.
Children played on the grassy mound near the riverbank.
A large mound of earth was built to fortify the campsite.

Synset Examples

they built small mounds to hide behind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hill - a naturally raised area of land, smaller than a mountain

Etymology

From Middle English 'mounde', possibly from Old Norse 'mund' (protection).

6. NOUN

/ maʊnd / (AmE), / maʊnd / (BrE)
a small natural hill, often rounded or gently sloping, sometimes formed by natural or artificial means
a small natural hill

Examples

The children rolled down the grassy mound behind the school.
A small mound of earth marked the entrance to the fox's den.
From the top of the mound, we could see the entire valley.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hill - a naturally raised area of land, larger than a mound
  • Synonym: knoll - a small, rounded hill or mound

Etymology

From Middle English 'mound', probably from Old English 'mund' (hand, protection) or from Anglo-Norman 'mund' (artificial hill).
