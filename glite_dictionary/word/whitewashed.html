whitewashed

1. ADJ

/ ˈwaɪtˌwɑʃt / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtwɒʃt / (BrE)
covered with a white fluid mixture used to whiten or clean surfaces, especially walls or fences.
coated with whitewash

Examples

The whitewashed cottage gleamed in the sunlight at the end of the lane.
Many rural schools were simple whitewashed buildings with wooden floors.
They admired the rows of whitewashed houses along the harbor.

Synset Examples

miles of whitewashed fences

Related Terms

  • Synonym: painted - covered with a layer of paint

Etymology

From 'whitewash' (noun/verb) + '-ed' (past participle/adjective form).
