Anthocerotales

1. NOUN

/ ˌænθoʊˌsɛrəˈteɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌænθəsɛrəˈteɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of hornworts, non-vascular plants characterized by a thalloid gametophyte, sometimes classified under the class Hepaticopsida
an order of hornworts (non-vascular plants)
medical

Examples

The Anthocerotales are distinguished by their elongated, horn-shaped sporophytes.
Botanists study Anthocerotales to understand early land plant evolution.
Specimens from the Anthocerotales order are often found in damp, shaded environments.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Anthoceros + -ales (order suffix in plant taxonomy).
