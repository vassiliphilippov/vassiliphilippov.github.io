Corylopsis

1. NOUN

/ kɔːrɪˈlɑːpsɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɒrɪˈlɒpsɪs / (BrE)
a genus of small deciduous shrubs native to temperate regions of Asia, cultivated for their yellow flowers
genus of Asian deciduous shrubs

Examples

Several species of Corylopsis are prized for their early spring blossoms in gardens.
The Corylopsis shrub thrives in well-drained, slightly acidic soil.
Botanists classify Corylopsis within the witch hazel family, Hamamelidaceae.

Etymology

From Greek 'korylos' (hazel) and 'opsis' (appearance), referring to its hazel-like appearance.

2. NOUN

/ kɔːrɪˈlɑːpsɪs / (AmE), / kɔːrɪˈlɒpsɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small, deciduous shrubs native to temperate regions of Asia, known for their early spring flowers; commonly called winter hazel.
genus of Asian deciduous shrubs (winter hazel)

Examples

Corylopsis is prized in gardens for its fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.
There are several species within the genus Corylopsis, all native to East Asia.
Gardeners often plant Corylopsis as an ornamental shrub for its delicate spring blossoms.

Etymology

From Greek 'korylos' (hazel) and 'opsis' (appearance), referring to its resemblance to hazel.
