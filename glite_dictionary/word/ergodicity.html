ergodicity

1. NOUN

/ ˌɜrɡoʊˈdɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌɜːɡəˈdɪsɪti / (BrE)
In mathematics and statistics, the property of a stochastic system in which its long-term behavior is independent of its initial conditions and is represented by a single limiting distribution.
stochastic system's limiting behavior
medical

Examples

The concept of ergodicity is fundamental in statistical mechanics.
If a process satisfies ergodicity, its time average equals its ensemble average.
The validity of the model depends on the assumption of ergodicity in the underlying system.

Etymology

From Greek 'ergon' (work) + 'hodos' (way), via French 'ergodique'.
