Malcolmia

1. PROPN

/ mælˈkoʊlmiə / (AmE), / malˈkɒlmɪə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Brassicaceae, typically found in coastal habitats from the Mediterranean to Afghanistan.
genus of flowering coastal plants

Examples

The sandy cliffs were dotted with clusters of Malcolmia in full bloom.
Botanists studied the different species within the Malcolmia genus.
Malcolmia plants are often chosen for their resilience in coastal gardens.

Etymology

Named after William Malcolm, an 18th-century Scottish gardener.

genus of coastal flowering plants

Examples

The rocky shore was covered with blooming Malcolmia during the spring.
Gardeners often plant Malcolmia for its attractive lilac flowers and tolerance to salty soils.
Several species within the Malcolmia genus are popular in Mediterranean gardens.

Related Terms
