gynarchy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡaɪˌnɑrki / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪnɑːki / (BrE)
A political system or government ruled by a woman or women.
government ruled by a woman or women

Examples

The small island nation was once famous for its gynarchy, where women held the highest offices for generations.
Historians debated whether ancient societies practiced gynarchy or simply honored certain female leaders.
A fictional kingdom in the novel thrives under a gynarchy established after a long era of male kings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: matriarchy - a social system in which women hold primary power
  • Antonym: patriarchy - a social or political system ruled by men

Etymology

From Greek 'gynē' (woman) + 'arkhē' (rule).
