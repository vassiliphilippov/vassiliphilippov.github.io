Thomomys talpoides

1. NOUN

/ θoʊˈmoʊmɪs tælˈpɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / θəˈmɒmɪs tælˈpɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A species of greyish to brown gopher native to western and central regions of the United States.
species of gopher in western/central US

Examples

The Thomomys talpoides often creates extensive burrow systems in grasslands.
Farmers in the region sometimes regard Thomomys talpoides as a pest due to its digging activities.
Unlike other gophers, Thomomys talpoides has fur that ranges from greyish to brown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gopher - a burrowing rodent of North America

Etymology

From scientific Latin: genus 'Thomomys' and species epithet 'talpoides', meaning 'mole-like'.
