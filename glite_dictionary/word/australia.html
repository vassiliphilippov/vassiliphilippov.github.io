Australia

1. PROPN

/ əˈstreɪljə / (AmE), / ɒˈstreɪlɪə / (BrE)
the smallest continent, located between the South Pacific and the Indian Ocean, comprising the mainland of the country of Australia and its surrounding islands
smallest continent, between Pacific and Indian Oceans

Examples

Many unique animal species are native to Australia, such as the kangaroo and koala.
He traveled across Australia by train, enjoying its diverse landscapes.
Australia is known for its Great Barrier Reef and stunning beaches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Oceania - geographical region including Australia, New Zealand, and the Pacific islands
  • Antonym: Asia - the largest continent, north of Australia

Etymology

From Latin 'Terra Australis' (southern land), used by European mapmakers before European exploration.

2. PROPN

/ əˈstreɪliə / (AmE), / ɒˈstreɪliə / (BrE)
a country and continent in the Southern Hemisphere, comprising the mainland of the Australian continent, Tasmania, and numerous smaller islands
country and continent in the Southern Hemisphere

Examples

Many people dream of visiting Australia to see its unique wildlife and beautiful landscapes.
The climate in Australia varies greatly from region to region.
Sydney, located in Australia, is famous for its Opera House and vibrant culture.

Etymology

From Latin Terra Australis (southern land), later applied to the continent.
