sinkable

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪŋkəbəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkəbl / (BrE)
Able to be made to sink; capable of sinking.
capable of being sunk

Examples

The old wooden boat was barely sinkable, even when filled with water.
Are plastic bottles sinkable or do they always float in the ocean?
Engineers tested how sinkable the new vessel was after modifying its hull.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsinkable - impossible to sink

Etymology

Formed from 'sink' + '-able', a Latin-derived suffix meaning 'able to be'.
