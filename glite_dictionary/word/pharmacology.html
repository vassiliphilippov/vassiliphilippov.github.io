pharmacology

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɑrməˈkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌfɑːməkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of science concerned with the study of drugs, including their preparation, properties, uses, and effects on living organisms
the study of drugs and their effects

Examples

She decided to specialize in pharmacology after becoming fascinated by how drugs interact with the body.
The conference featured leading experts in pharmacology and drug development.
His research in pharmacology has led to innovative treatments for chronic diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toxicology - the study of the adverse effects of chemicals, especially poisons, on living organisms

Etymology

From Greek pharmakon (drug) + -logia (study).
