Unionidae

1. NOUN

/ juːˈnɪ.ə.nɪˌdiː / (AmE), / juːˈnɪ.ə.nɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of freshwater mussels found worldwide, known for their two-part shells and ecological importance
family of freshwater mussels

Examples

The Unionidae are vital for maintaining water quality in many freshwater ecosystems.
Scientists recently discovered several new species within the Unionidae family in the Amazon basin.
Declining populations of Unionidae signal worsening river pollution.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Latin 'unio', a type of freshwater mussel, with the typical taxonomic suffix '-idae' for families.

2. NOUN

/ ˌjuːniˈɒnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌjuːniˈɒnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of freshwater mussels found throughout the world, belonging to the order Unionida
family of freshwater mussels

Examples

The Unionidae are among the most diverse families of freshwater mussels.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Unionidae in the river ecosystem.
Many members of the Unionidae play a crucial role in maintaining freshwater habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mussel - a type of bivalve mollusc living in freshwater or saltwater

Etymology

From the genus name Unionidae, combining 'Unio' (a type genus of mussel) and the family suffix '-idae' used in zoological taxonomy.
