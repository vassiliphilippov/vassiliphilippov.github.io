Danaus

1. NOUN

/ dəˈneɪ.əs / (AmE), / dəˈneɪ.əs / (BrE)
A genus of butterflies in the family Nymphalidae, including the monarch butterfly and its relatives.
genus of monarch butterflies

Examples

The Danaus genus includes several well-known butterfly species found around the world.
Monarch butterflies, classified under the genus Danaus, are famous for their long migrations.
Scientists studied the evolutionary history of Danaus to understand butterfly migration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monarch butterfly - a well-known butterfly species, Danaus plexippus, in North America

Etymology

From Greek Danaos, a legendary king of Argos.

2. NOUN

/ dəˈneɪ.əs / (AmE), / dəˈneɪ.əs / (BrE)
a genus of butterflies including the monarch and other milkweed butterflies, serving as the type genus of the family Danaidae.
a genus of butterflies including the monarch

Examples

The Danaus genus includes the magnificent monarch butterfly, famous for its long migrations.
Several species within Danaus feed on milkweed plants during their larval stage.
Researchers studied the genetic diversity of butterflies in the Danaus genus worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monarch butterfly - a well-known butterfly species within the genus Danaus

Etymology

From Latin Danaus, a mythological king; used in taxonomy for the genus name.
