Cestrum nocturnum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛstrəm nɒkˈtɜrnəm / (AmE), / ˈsɛstrəm nɒkˈtɜːnəm / (BrE)
A species of West Indian evergreen shrub with clusters of yellow-white, funnel-shaped flowers that release a strong fragrance at night; also known as night-blooming jasmine.
nocturnally fragrant evergreen shrub (night-blooming jasmine)

Examples

The Cestrum nocturnum in my garden fills the air with scent every evening.
Botanists studied the pollinators attracted to the Cestrum nocturnum's night-blooming flowers.
Many gardeners plant Cestrum nocturnum near patios to enjoy its fragrance after dusk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: night-blooming jasmine - common name for Cestrum nocturnum, a fragrant night-flowering shrub

Etymology

From Latin: 'cestrum' (a genus of plants) and 'nocturnum' meaning 'of the night.'
