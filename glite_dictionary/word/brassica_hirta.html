Brassica hirta

1. NOUN

/ brəˈsɪkə ˈhɜrtə / (AmE), / brəˈsɪkə ˈhɜːtə / (BrE)
A Eurasian species of mustard plant cultivated for its pungent seeds, used in table mustard and for producing mustard oil.
mustard plant species used for seeds

Examples

Brassica hirta is widely grown in Europe for the production of mustard oil.
The seeds of Brassica hirta are ground to make yellow mustard.
Researchers are studying Brassica hirta for its potential health benefits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mustard plant - plant whose seeds are used for making mustard

Etymology

From Latin: 'Brassica' meaning cabbage or mustard, 'hirta' meaning hairy.
