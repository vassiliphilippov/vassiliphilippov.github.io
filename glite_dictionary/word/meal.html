meal

1. NOUN

/ mil / (AmE), / miːl / (BrE)
coarsely ground grain or seeds, especially of cereal grasses or pulses, used as food or in cooking
coarsely ground grain or seeds

Examples

The recipe calls for two cups of corn meal and one cup of flour.
Farmers often grind wheat into meal for baking traditional breads.
Oat meal is a popular breakfast food in many countries.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: flour - finely ground grain, typically wheat, used in baking

Etymology

From Old English melu (ground grain, flour), related to Proto-Germanic *melwan (to grind).

2. NOUN

/ miːl / (AmE), / miːl / (BrE)
an occasion when people eat food, especially at a regular time such as breakfast, lunch, or dinner
an occasion for eating food

Examples

We usually have our evening meal at seven o'clock.
His favorite meal of the day is breakfast.
They gathered together for a Sunday meal with the whole family.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: snack - a small amount of food eaten between main meals

Etymology

From Old English mǣl (appointed time, meal time, meal), of Germanic origin.

3. NOUN

/ miːl / (AmE), / miːl / (BrE)
an occasion when food is served and eaten, especially at a regular time during the day
food eaten at one time

Examples

We usually eat our evening meal together as a family.
He skipped his breakfast meal because he was running late.
The hotel offers three meals a day to its guests.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: snack - a small amount of food eaten between meals
  • Degree Higher: feast - a large, elaborate meal often for a celebration

Etymology

From Old English mǣl (appointed time, meal), related to Dutch maal (meal, time).
