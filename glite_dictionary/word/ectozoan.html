ectozoan

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.toʊˈzoʊ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɛk.təʊˈzəʊ.ən / (BrE)
an organism that lives on the body surface of another organism and acts as a parasite, such as a flea or tick
external parasitic organism
medical

Examples

Fleas are a classic example of an ectozoan infesting household pets.
The tick, as an ectozoan, remains attached to the skin of its host for a prolonged period.
Researchers studied several types of ectozoans affecting livestock in rural areas.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: endoparasite - a parasite that lives inside the host's body

Etymology

From Greek 'ektos' (outside) + 'zōion' (animal).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɛk.təˈzoʊ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɛk.təˈzəʊ.ən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of epizoa; living on the external surface of an animal.
relating to organisms living on animals' surfaces

Examples

Some parasites are strictly ectozoan and never invade the host's internal tissues.
The fish was infested with ectozoan creatures clinging to its scales.
Researchers identified several ectozoan species living on the whale's skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epizoic - Living on the exterior of an animal

Etymology

From Greek *ektos* (outside) + *zōion* (animal).
