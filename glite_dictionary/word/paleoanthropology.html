paleoanthropology

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪlioʊˌænθrəˈpɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌpæliəʊˌænθrəˈpɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The scientific study of human fossils and the origins and evolution of early humans.
the study of human fossils
medical

Examples

Research in paleoanthropology has provided new insights into the origins of modern humans.
He decided to specialize in paleoanthropology after discovering a fascination with ancient hominid fossils.
Recent advances in paleoanthropology have transformed our understanding of prehistoric human life.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: anthropology - the study of human societies and cultures
  • Synonym: archaeology - the study of human history through artifacts and physical remains

Etymology

From Greek 'palaiós' (ancient) + 'anthrōpos' (human) + '-logy' (study of).
