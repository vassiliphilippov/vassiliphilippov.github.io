NIDDM

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛnˌaɪˌdiːˌdiːˈɛm / (AmE), / ˌɛnˌaɪˌdiːˌdiːˈɛm / (BrE)
a mild form of diabetes mellitus that develops gradually in adults, usually controlled by diet or oral medication and not requiring insulin injections; now more commonly known as type 2 diabetes
non-insulin-dependent diabetes (type 2)
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with NIDDM and prescribed oral hypoglycemic agents.
Proper diet and exercise can help manage NIDDM effectively without insulin injections.
Obesity is a significant risk factor for developing NIDDM in adulthood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: type 2 diabetes - form of diabetes mellitus not requiring insulin therapy
  • Antonym: type 1 diabetes - form of diabetes mellitus requiring insulin

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'non-insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus'.
