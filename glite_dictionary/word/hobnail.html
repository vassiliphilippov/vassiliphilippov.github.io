hobnail

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑbˌneɪl / (AmE), / ˈhɒb.neɪl / (BrE)
A short, thick-headed nail driven into the soles of boots to provide durability and traction.
thick-headed nail for boot soles

Examples

The old boots were reinforced with hobnails for extra grip on rocky paths.
After years of use, the hobnails had worn down but still protected the leather soles.
He heard the sound of hobnails striking the pavement as the soldiers marched by.

Etymology

From 'hob' (dialectal variant for 'heel') + 'nail', possibly influenced by Old English 'habban' (to have, to hold).

2. VERB

/ ˈhɑbˌneɪl / (AmE), / ˈhɒbˌneɪl / (BrE)
to equip or furnish (a shoe or boot) with hobnails; to fasten hobnails into the sole of footwear
to supply with hobnails (esp. footwear)

Examples

The cobbler spent the morning hobnailing a pair of sturdy work boots for the farmer.
In the old days, soldiers would hobnail their boots to make them last longer on marches.
They continued hobnailing shoes well into the twentieth century, despite more modern alternatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nail - to attach or fix with nails

Etymology

From 'hobnail' (noun): small, short nail with a thick head, used in boot soles, from Middle English.
