Gloucester

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡlɑːstər / (AmE), / ˈɡlɒstə / (BrE)
A town in northeastern Massachusetts, United States, located on Cape Ann, known for its historic fishing harbor.
U.S. fishing town in Massachusetts

Examples

Gloucester has been a center of the American fishing industry for hundreds of years.
During our New England road trip, we stopped in Gloucester to visit the famous harbor.
Many artists have been inspired by the coastal scenery around Gloucester in Massachusetts.

Etymology

Named after Gloucester, England.

2. PROPN

/ ˈɡlɑːstər / (AmE), / ˈɡlɒstə / (BrE)
A city in southwestern England, located in Gloucestershire on the River Severn, known for its rich history and cathedral.
city in southwestern England
british

Examples

We visited Gloucester last summer to see the famous cathedral.
The River Severn runs through the city of Gloucester.
Many historic buildings in Gloucester attract tourists from around the world.

Etymology

From Old English 'Glowecestre', based on Latin 'Glevum' (the name of the Roman fort) and 'ceaster' (Roman town).
