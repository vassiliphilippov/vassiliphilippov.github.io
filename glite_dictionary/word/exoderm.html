exoderm

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛksəˌdɜrm / (AmE), / ˈɛksə(ʊ)ˌdɜːm / (BrE)
the outer germ layer in an embryo that develops into the skin and nervous system
outer embryonic germ layer
medical

Examples

The exoderm gives rise to both the epidermis and the nervous system in vertebrate embryos.
Studying the exoderm is essential to understanding early stages of human development.
The cells of the exoderm begin to differentiate soon after fertilization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ectoderm - outer germ layer of an embryo forming skin and nervous tissue

Etymology

From Greek exo- (outside) + derma (skin).
