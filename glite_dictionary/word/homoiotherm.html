homoiotherm

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˈmɔɪ.əˌθɜrm / (AmE), / həʊˈmɔɪ.əˌθɜːm / (BrE)
An animal that maintains a relatively constant body temperature, independent of the surrounding environment.
animal with constant body temperature

Examples

Most birds and mammals are classified as homoiotherms because they regulate their internal temperature.
Unlike reptiles, homoiotherms can remain active in cold environments.
Scientists study how homoiotherms adapt to extreme climates through metabolic changes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: poikilotherm - an animal whose body temperature varies with the environment
  • Synonym: homeotherm - alternate spelling for homoiotherm, animal with constant body temperature

Etymology

From Greek homoiothermos, from homoi- (similar) + thermos (heat).
