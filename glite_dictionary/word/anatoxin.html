anatoxin

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈtɑksɪn / (AmE), / ˌanəˈtɒksɪn / (BrE)
A bacterial toxin that has been rendered non-toxic but can still stimulate the production of antibodies and provide immunity to the associated disease.
non-toxic bacterial toxin used as a vaccine
medical

Examples

The tetanus vaccine contains a purified anatoxin to stimulate immune protection.
After exposure, doctors recommended vaccination with a diphtheria anatoxin.
Modern vaccines often use an anatoxin rather than the live toxin to ensure safety.

Synset Examples

diphtheria toxoid

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toxoid - a bacterial toxin that has been inactivated for use as a vaccine

Etymology

From Greek 'ana-' (up, again) + 'toxin' (poison).
