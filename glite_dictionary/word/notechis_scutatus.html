Notechis scutatus

1. NOUN

/ noʊˈtɛkɪs skuːˈteɪtəs / (AmE), / nəʊˈtɛkɪs skjuːˈteɪtəs / (BrE)
A highly venomous brown-and-yellow snake native to Australia and Tasmania, commonly known as the tiger snake.
venomous snake of Australia and Tasmania

Examples

The venom of the Notechis scutatus is extremely potent and considered dangerous to humans.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Notechis scutatus along the coastal regions of Tasmania.
While bushwalking, we spotted a Notechis scutatus basking in the sun near a stream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiger snake - common name for Notechis scutatus

Etymology

From Greek 'noton' (back) and 'echis' (viper); Latin 'scutatus' meaning 'shielded' or 'having a shield'.
