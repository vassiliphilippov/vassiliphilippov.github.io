jape

1. NOUN

/ dʒeɪp / (AmE), / dʒeɪp / (BrE)
a humorous or playful remark or prank, often intended to provoke laughter
a joke or playful trick

Examples

Many viewed his story as just another jape to lighten the mood.
The students played a harmless jape on their teacher during class.
She couldn't help but laugh at his clever jape during dinner.

Synset Examples

he told a very funny joke

even a schoolboy's jape is supposed to have some ascertainable point

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jest - a thing said or done for amusement; a joke
  • Synonym: prank - a practical joke or mischievous act
  • Synonym: joke - a thing that someone says to cause amusement or laughter
  • Synonym: gag - a joke or an amusing story, especially one forming part of a comedian's act

Etymology

From Middle English *japen* (to joke, mock), probably from Old French *japer* (to yap, chatter).
