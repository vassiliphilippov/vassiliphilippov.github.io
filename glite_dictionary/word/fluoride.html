fluoride

1. NOUN

/ fluˈɔraɪd / (AmE), / ˈflʊəraɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound containing the fluoride ion, especially a salt of hydrofluoric acid
a salt of hydrofluoric acid

Examples

Many toothpastes contain fluoride to help prevent cavities.
The local water supply is treated with fluoride for dental health benefits.
Excessive exposure to fluoride can sometimes lead to dental fluorosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fluorine - a chemical element, symbol F

Etymology

From fluorine + -ide, following chemical naming conventions.
