cane

1. NOUN

/ keɪn / (AmE), / keɪn / (BrE)
a stick used to help someone walk, especially one used by elderly or injured people for support
a walking stick used for support

Examples

She gripped her cane as she crossed the busy street.
After his surgery, he relied on a cane to get around the house.
The old man set his cane beside the door before sitting down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walking stick - a stick used by people to help them walk

Etymology

From Old French *cane* (reed, walking stick), from Latin *canna* (reed).

2. NOUN

/ keɪn / (AmE), / keɪn / (BrE)
the strong, slender, often flexible stem of certain plants such as bamboo, reed, rattan, or sugar cane, used for making furniture, baskets, and other items
flexible plant stem (e.g. bamboo, rattan)

Examples

The chair was woven from strips of cane harvested from the riverbank.
She used a piece of cane to support the tomato plants in her garden.
Baskets made from cane are lightweight and durable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reed - tall, slender-leaved plant of wetlands; also used for its stem

Etymology

From Old French 'cane', from Latin 'canna' (reed, cane), from Greek 'kanna'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-12156723-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ keɪn / (AmE), / keɪn / (BrE)
a long, thin stick or switch, especially one used as a whip or for punishing someone, formerly common in schools
switch/stick used for punishment

Examples

The teacher used a cane to discipline unruly students in the past.
Corporal punishment with a cane has been banned in most schools.
He remembers the pain of being struck with a cane for misbehaving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rod - a straight, thin stick or bar, often used as an instrument for hitting
  • Synonym: strap - a strip of leather used for striking as punishment

Etymology

From Latin canna (reed, cane), via Old French and Old English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02952520-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ keɪn / (AmE), / keɪn / (BrE)
to beat or strike someone with a cane, typically as a punishment
to beat with a cane

Examples

The principal threatened to cane the students for misbehavior.
In some schools, teachers used to cane children as punishment.
He was caned for arriving late to class several times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whip - to strike with a whip
  • Synonym: to flog - to beat severely with a whip or stick

Etymology

From Old French 'cane', from Latin 'canna' (reed, cane).
