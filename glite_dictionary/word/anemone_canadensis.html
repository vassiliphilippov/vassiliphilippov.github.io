Anemone canadensis

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɛməni kəˈnædənsɪs / (AmE), / əˈnɛməni kəˈnædənsɪs / (BrE)
A North American woodland plant with white flowers, commonly found from Labrador to Colorado, flowering in summer.
a North American woodland plant with white flowers

Examples

The shady garden bed is filled with blooming Anemone canadensis in early summer.
Many wildflower enthusiasts plant Anemone canadensis to attract pollinators to their gardens.
We spotted a beautiful patch of Anemone canadensis along the forest trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windflower - any plant of the genus Anemone

Etymology

From Latin: 'Anemone' from Greek 'anemōnē' (windflower); 'canadensis' indicating Canadian origin.
