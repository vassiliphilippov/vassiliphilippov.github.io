Rosmarinus officinalis

1. NOUN

/ rɒzˌmɛrɪnəs əfˌɪsɪˈneɪlɪs / (AmE), / rɒzˌmærɪnəs ɒfˌɪsɪˈneɪlɪs / (BrE)
The scientific name for rosemary, a fragrant evergreen herb with grey-green leaves, widely used in cooking and perfumery.
scientific name for rosemary (herb)

Examples

The essential oils derived from Rosmarinus officinalis are valued in aromatherapy for their refreshing scent.
Chefs often add sprigs of Rosmarinus officinalis to roasted meats for extra flavor.
Recent studies explore the medicinal properties of Rosmarinus officinalis in herbal medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rosemary - A fragrant herb used in cooking and perfumes

Etymology

From Latin: 'rosmarinus' meaning 'dew of the sea', and 'officinalis' indicating use in medicine.
