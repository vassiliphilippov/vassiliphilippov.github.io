firefly

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌflaɪ / (BrE)
a nocturnal beetle that produces light from its abdomen, commonly seen in warm regions
luminescent nocturnal beetle

Examples

A single firefly lit up the garden with its glowing tail.
During summer evenings, children love to catch fireflies in glass jars.
The firefly's light helps it attract a mate in the dark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lightning bug - another name for a firefly, especially in American English

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌflaɪ / (BrE)
A small beetle, especially in warm regions, whose abdomen emits light, typically at night; also called a lightning bug.
luminous beetle; lightning bug

Examples

At dusk, dozens of fireflies flickered above the grass, filling the garden with dots of light.
Children chased a single firefly across the backyard, trying to catch its glow in a jar.
During the tropical night, a firefly glowed gently among the palm leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lightning bug - Another name for a bioluminescent beetle
