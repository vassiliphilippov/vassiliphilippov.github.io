Antedonidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tɪˈdoʊ.nɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈdəʊ.nɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of marine echinoderms known as feather stars, distinguished by their feathery arms and free-moving lifestyle
a family of feather stars (marine animals)

Examples

The Antedonidae are common inhabitants of shallow coastal waters.
Researchers discovered several new species within the Antedonidae family.
Feather stars of the Antedonidae family display a wide range of colors and arm patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feather star - marine animals with feathery arms, members of Antedonidae

Etymology

From New Latin Antedon (a genus of feather stars) + -idae (zoological family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tɪˈdoʊ.nɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈdəʊ.nɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of feather stars, marine animals in the class Crinoidea, characterized by feathery arms and a central mouth, found mainly in shallow seas
family of feather stars (marine animals)

Examples

The Antedonidae family includes many colorful species found in shallow tropical waters.
Scientists studied the reproductive strategies of several members of the Antedonidae.
Unlike sea urchins, Antedonidae attach themselves to rocks by means of cirri.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feather star - a marine animal with feathery arms, in the class Crinoidea

Etymology

From New Latin Antedon (a genus name) + -idae (a standard suffix for animal families).
