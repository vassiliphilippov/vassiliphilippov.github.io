hypnagogic

1. ADJ

/ hɪp.nəˈɡɑ.dʒɪk / (AmE), / hɪp.nəˈɡɒ.dʒɪk / (BrE)
relating to the state immediately before falling asleep or inducing sleep.
relating to the onset of sleep

Examples

She began to experience vivid hypnagogic images just as she was drifting off to sleep.
Researchers studied subjects during the hypnagogic state to understand the transition from wakefulness to sleep.
The soft, hypnagogic music helped him relax and fall asleep quickly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypnopompic - relating to the state immediately after waking

Etymology

From French 'hypnagogique', from Greek 'hypnos' (sleep) + 'agōgos' (leading).
