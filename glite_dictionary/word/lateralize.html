to lateralize

1. VERB

/ ˈlæt̬.ə.rəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈlat.ər.əˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to move or displace something to one side, making it lateral
move to one side; make lateral

Examples

The surgeon decided to lateralize the nerve to avoid accidental damage during the procedure.
In some animals, behavioral functions tend to lateralize to a particular side of the brain.
The therapist used a technique to lateralize the patient's weight distribution during rehabilitation.

Etymology

From 'lateral' (side) + '-ize' (to make or become).
