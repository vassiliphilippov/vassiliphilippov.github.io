transparency

1. NOUN

/ trænˈspɛrənsi / (AmE), / trænˈspærənsi / (BrE)
The quality of allowing electromagnetic radiation, such as light, to pass through so that objects behind can be seen clearly or partially.
quality of letting light (or radiation) pass through

Examples

The transparency of the new glass allowed sunlight to flood into the room.
Scientists measured the transparency of the material using specialized instruments.
The transparency of the atmosphere is crucial for astronomical observations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: opacity - the quality of not allowing light or radiation to pass through

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *transparentia*, from Latin *transparens* (letting light through).

2. NOUN

/ trænˈspærənsi / (AmE), / trænˈspærənsi / (BrE)
a positive photograph or drawing on a transparent base, designed to be viewed with the aid of a projector
projectable transparent photograph or drawing

Examples

The science teacher used a transparency to display the diagram on the overhead projector.
She carefully labeled each transparency for her conference presentation.
Digitizing the old transparencies helped preserve the historical records for future study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slide - a small piece of photographic film mounted for projection

Etymology

From Latin trans- (across) + parere (to appear).

3. NOUN

/ trænˈspɛrənsi / (AmE), / trænˈspærənsi / (BrE)
the quality of being clear, easy to see through, or easy to understand
quality of being clear or easy to understand

Examples

The transparency of the glass made it easy to see the garden outside.
The government promised greater transparency in its decision-making process.
Artists value the transparency of watercolor paints for creating luminous effects.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: opacity - the quality of being opaque, not transparent
  • Synonym: clarity - quality of being clear or easy to understand

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'transparentia', from Latin 'transparens' (letting light through).
