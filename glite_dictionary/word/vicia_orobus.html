Vicia orobus

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪsiə ɔːˈroʊbəs / (AmE), / ˈvɪsɪə ɔːˈrəʊbəs / (BrE)
a European perennial vetch species, known for being toxic to livestock
European toxic perennial vetch plant

Examples

Vicia orobus is often found in meadows and grasslands across western Europe.
Farmers should be cautious, as Vicia orobus can be poisonous to grazing animals.
Botanists identified the plant as Vicia orobus due to its distinct flowers and leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vetch - a climbing plant of the pea family

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Vicia' meaning vetch and 'orobus' being the classical name for certain vetches.
