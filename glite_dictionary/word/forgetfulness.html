forgetfulness

1. NOUN

/ fərˈɡɛtfəlnəs / (AmE), / fəˈɡɛtfəlnəs / (BrE)
the tendency or state of being likely to forget things
tendency to forget

Examples

His forgetfulness often caused him to miss important appointments.
I worry that my forgetfulness is getting worse as I get older.
Her forgetfulness led her to leave her keys in the car again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absent-mindedness - having or showing a forgetful or inattentive disposition
  • Antonym: memory - the power of retaining and recalling past experience

Etymology

From 'forget' + '-fulness', modeled on 'forgetful'.

2. NOUN

/ fərˈɡɛtfəlnəs / (AmE), / fəˈɡɛtf(ʊ)lnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being likely to forget things, especially through neglect or lack of attention
tendency or state of forgetting

Examples

Her forgetfulness often caused her to miss important meetings.
As a sign of aging, increased forgetfulness is fairly common.
He apologized for his forgetfulness in not returning the book on time.

Synset Examples

his forgetfulness increased as he grew older

Related Terms

  • Antonym: memory - the ability to remember things
  • Degree Higher: amnesia - partial or total loss of memory

Etymology

Formed from 'forget' + '-fulness', denoting the state of forgetting.
