eccentric

1. ADJ

/ ɛkˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛntrɪk / (BrE)
conspicuously or grossly unconventional or unusual; not behaving or appearing in a way that is typical or expected.
unconventional; markedly unusual

Examples

The artist was known for his eccentric taste in clothes and decor.
Her eccentric methods often puzzled her colleagues, but they got results.
That house, shaped like a giant shoe, is truly eccentric in design.

Synset Examples

restaurants of bizarre design — one like a hat, another like a rabbit
famed for his eccentric spelling
a freakish combination of styles
his off-the-wall antics
the outlandish clothes of teenagers
outre and affected stage antics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unconventional - not based on or conforming to what is generally done or believed
  • Synonym: bizarre - very strange or unusual, especially so as to cause surprise or amusement
  • Antonym: conventional - based on or in accordance with what is generally done or believed

Etymology

From Greek 'ekkentrikos' (out of the center), from 'ek-' (out) + 'kentron' (center).

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛntrɪk / (BrE)
a person who has habits or opinions that are unconventional and slightly strange
person with unconventional or odd habits

Examples

The town's most famous eccentric was known for wearing brightly colored hats every day.
He's a lovable eccentric who collects vintage teapots and talks to his plants.
Many historical eccentrics have made significant contributions to science and the arts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oddball - a person whose behavior is unconventional or strange
  • Antonym: conformist - a person who accepts established customs and norms

Etymology

From Medieval Latin eccentricus (deviating from the center).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10062653-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛntrɪk / (BrE)
(of two or more objects, especially circles or spheres) not having the same center; not concentric.
not sharing the same center (geometry)

Examples

The diagram shows two eccentric circles that overlap slightly.
An eccentric pulley can cause irregular motion in machinery.
The earth's orbit is slightly eccentric rather than perfectly circular.

Synset Examples

eccentric circles

Related Terms

  • Antonym: concentric - having a common center (geometry)

Etymology

From Greek ekkentros, from ek 'out of' + kentron 'center'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00545420-a
Synset POS: a

4. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛntrɪk / (BrE)
a person who behaves in an unusual or unconventional way, often seen as odd or quirky
person with unconventional behavior

Examples

Everyone thought the old man was an eccentric because of his strange inventions.
As a known eccentric, she wore mismatched shoes to formal events without a second thought.
The village eccentric delighted children with his bizarre stories and colorful outfits.

Synset Examples

a strange character
a friendly eccentric
the capable type
a mental case

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oddity - an unusual or peculiar person
  • Synonym: character - an odd or eccentric person
  • Antonym: conformist - someone who behaves in conventional ways

Etymology

From Greek ekkentros (out of center): ek- (out) + kentron (center).
