nary

1. ADJ

/ ˈner.i / (AmE), / ˈneə.ri / (BrE)
used to emphasize the absence of even a single thing; not a single
not a single; not one
archaic informal

Examples

I looked around the room and saw nary a soul in sight.
She completed the entire hike with nary a complaint.
He spent the evening waiting, but nary a bus arrived.

Synset Examples

heard nary a sound

Related Terms

  • Synonym: not a - used to indicate not even one

Etymology

Contraction of 'ne'er a', meaning 'never a'.
