trombiculid

1. NOUN

/ trɒmˈbɪkjəlɪd / (AmE), / trɒmˈbɪkjʊlɪd / (BrE)
A mite belonging to the family Trombiculidae, whose nymphs and adults feed on the early stages of small arthropods, and whose larvae are parasitic on terrestrial vertebrates.
a type of parasitic mite
medical

Examples

The trombiculid is notorious for its larval stage, which can cause skin irritation in mammals.
Researchers studied the feeding habits of the trombiculid to better understand its role in disease transmission.
Many hikers are wary of trombiculids, especially during the summer months in grassy areas.

Etymology

From New Latin Trombiculidae, the family name, ultimately from Greek trombos (lump) and Latin culex (gnat).
