Oncidium papilio kramerianum

1. NOUN

/ ɑnˈsɪdiəm pəˈpilioʊ ˌkramɛriˈɑnəm / (AmE), / ɒnˈsɪdiəm pəˈpɪlɪəʊ ˌkræmɛrɪˈɑːnəm / (BrE)
A species of orchid native to South and Central America, known for its butterfly-like flowers that are smaller than those of Psychopsis papilio.
species of South and Central American orchid

Examples

The Oncidium papilio kramerianum is prized by orchid collectors for its distinctive butterfly-like blooms.
Botanists sometimes debate whether Oncidium papilio kramerianum should be classified in the genus Oncidium or Psychopsis.
While less showy than its close relative, Oncidium papilio kramerianum is still admired for its delicate yellow and brown flowers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Psychopsis papilio - orchid species with larger flowers, sometimes confused with Oncidium papilio kramerianum

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name Oncidium; Latin papilio meaning 'butterfly,' and kramerianum after the botanist Kramer.
