dibranch

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈbræŋk / (AmE), / daɪˈbraŋk / (BrE)
A cephalopod having two gills, belonging to the subclass Dibranchia.
cephalopod with two gills

Examples

Nautilus is not a dibranch because it has more than two gills.
Modern squids and octopuses are classified as dibranchs in zoology.
The dibranch structure is one of the main differences from other cephalopods.

Etymology

From Greek di- (two) + branchia (gills).
