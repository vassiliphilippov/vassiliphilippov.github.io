revetment

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈvɛtmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈvɛtmənt / (BrE)
A retaining wall or facing, usually of stone or concrete, built to support or protect an embankment or other earthwork.
facing for supporting embankments

Examples

The engineers installed a stone revetment to prevent the riverbank from eroding during heavy rains.
A concrete revetment was built along the shoreline to protect the harbor from strong waves.
During the construction, workers reinforced the embankment with a sturdy revetment to ensure long-term stability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retaining wall - a structure that holds back soil

Etymology

From French *revêtement* (covering), from *revêtir* 'to clothe or cover', from Latin *revestire*.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈvɛtmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈvɛtmənt / (BrE)
A retaining wall or barricade, especially one built of earth, sandbags, or concrete, used to protect against explosions or erosion.
protective barrier or retaining wall

Examples

The army constructed a revetment to protect the ammunition depot from potential blasts.
Each bunker was surrounded by a thick concrete revetment for safety.
After the demolition, only the steel revetment remained standing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barricade - a barrier to block or protect an area
  • Synonym: wall - a vertical structure that defines or protects an area

Etymology

From French 'revêtement' (covering), from 'revêtir' (to clothe, cover), from Latin 'revestire'.
