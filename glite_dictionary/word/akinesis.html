akinesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ.kɪˈniː.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌeɪ.kɪˈniː.sɪs / (BrE)
The loss or absence of voluntary movement, typically caused by paralysis or neurological dysfunction.
loss of movement (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient exhibited akinesis in her right arm following the stroke.
Severe akinesis is a characteristic symptom of advanced Parkinson's disease.
Episodes of akinesis may occur in neurological disorders, affecting a person's ability to initiate movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paralysis - loss of the ability to move in part or most of the body

Etymology

From Greek a- (without) + kinesis (movement).
