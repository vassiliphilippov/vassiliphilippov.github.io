Terre Adelie

1. PROPN

/ tɛr aˈdeɪli / (AmE), / tɛər aˈdeɪli / (BrE)
a coastal region of Antarctica south of Australia, known for its large penguin colonies
Antarctic coastal region south of Australia

Examples

French scientists continue important research in Terre Adelie throughout the Antarctic summer.
The harsh climate of Terre Adelie supports thriving colonies of emperor and Adélie penguins.
Parts of Terre Adelie are protected to conserve its unique wildlife and fragile environment.

Etymology

From French, meaning 'Adelie Land', named by French explorer Jules Dumont d'Urville for his wife, Adèle.
