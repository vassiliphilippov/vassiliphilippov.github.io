Anthericum

1. NOUN

/ ænˈθɛr.ɪ.kəm / (AmE), / ænˈθɛr.ɪ.kəm / (BrE)
a genus of perennial herbs, mainly from Africa, sometimes classed in the family Asphodelaceae
a genus of perennial African herbs

Examples

The botanical garden houses several species of Anthericum in its African section.
Recent research has led botanists to revise the classification of Anthericum species.
She was fascinated by the delicate white flowers produced by Anthericum plants in spring.

Etymology

From Greek antherikos (‘straw, a plant with straw-like leaves’), used by Dioscorides for a plant in this group.

2. NOUN

/ ænˈθɛr.ɪ.kəm / (AmE), / ænˈθɛr.ɪ.kəm / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs native mainly to Africa, sometimes classified in the family Asphodelaceae.
genus of Old World perennial herbs
medical

Examples

Many species in the Anthericum genus have long, grass-like leaves and white flowers.
Botanists recently identified a new member of Anthericum in South Africa.
The flowers of Anthericum thrive in well-drained, sandy soil.

Etymology

From Latin 'Anthericum', from Greek 'antherikos' (asphodel).
