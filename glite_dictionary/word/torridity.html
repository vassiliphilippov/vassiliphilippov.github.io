torridity

1. NOUN

/ tɔːˈrɪdəti / (AmE), / tɒˈrɪdəti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being extremely hot
extreme heat; intense hotness

Examples

The relentless torridity of the desert made travel nearly impossible during midday.
Historical accounts describe the torridity of the summer, when crops withered under an unforgiving sun.
Seeking relief from the region's torridity, residents flocked to air-conditioned shelters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heat - the state of being hot or warm
  • Antonym: coolness - the state of being cool; lack of heat

Etymology

From Latin 'torridus' (scorched, parched) via the adjective 'torrid'.
