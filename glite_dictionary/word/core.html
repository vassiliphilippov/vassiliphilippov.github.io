CORE

1. PROPN

/ kɔr / (AmE), / kɔː / (BrE)
an American civil rights organization founded in 1942 to promote racial equality, known as the Congress of Racial Equality.
US civil rights organization (Congress of Racial Equality)
american

Examples

CORE played a leading role in the Freedom Rides during the 1960s civil rights movement.
Members of CORE organized sit-ins to protest segregation in public spaces.
James Farmer was one of the key founders of CORE in 1942.

Etymology

Acronym from Congress of Racial Equality.
