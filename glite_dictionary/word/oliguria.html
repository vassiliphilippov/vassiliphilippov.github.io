oliguria

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑlɪˈgjʊriə / (AmE), / ˌɒlɪˈgjʊəriə / (BrE)
Abnormally small production of urine, often indicating a medical problem with the kidneys or urinary system.
abnormally low urine output
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with oliguria after producing less than 400 ml of urine in 24 hours.
Oliguria can be a sign of dehydration or acute kidney injury.
Doctors monitor urine output closely in ICU patients for signs of oliguria.

Etymology

From Greek *oligos* (few) + *ouron* (urine).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːlɪˈɡjʊriə / (AmE), / ˌɒlɪˈɡjʊəriə / (BrE)
production of an abnormally small amount of urine, especially as a medical condition
abnormally low urine output (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with oliguria after his daily urine output dropped sharply.
Severe dehydration can result in oliguria and other complications.
The doctor monitored her fluid intake and oliguria during treatment for kidney failure.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: anuria - absence of urine production
  • Antonym: polyuria - production of abnormally large amounts of urine

Etymology

From Greek oligos (small, few) + ouron (urine).
