brisling

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪzlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbrɪzlɪŋ / (BrE)
a small herring, especially one processed and canned in the same way as sardines
type of small herring, like a sardine

Examples

The can was filled with smoked brisling packed in olive oil.
Norwegian brisling are prized for their delicate texture and flavor.
He ordered a salad topped with brisling and capers for lunch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sardine - small, oily fish often canned for food

Etymology

From Norwegian 'brisling', meaning a small herring.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪzlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbrɪzlɪŋ / (BrE)
a small, fatty European fish (Sprattus sprattus), often smoked or canned like sardines
a small fatty European fish

Examples

We bought a tin of brislings at the market for tonight's salad.
He enjoys smoked brisling on toast as a quick snack.
The restaurant serves brisling prepared in a traditional Scandinavian style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sprat - a small marine fish similar to the brisling

Etymology

From Norwegian 'brisling', diminutive of 'brisle' (sprat).
