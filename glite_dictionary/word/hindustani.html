Hindustani

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪnduˈstɑni / (AmE), / ˌhɪndʊˈstɑːni / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Hindustan (historically, northern India) or India
person from Hindustan or India

Examples

Many Hindustanis migrated to other parts of the British Empire during the colonial period.
She is proud of her heritage as a Hindustani and celebrates traditional festivals.
The Hindustani community in the region maintains its rich cultural customs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indian - a native or inhabitant of India

Etymology

From Persian Hindūstānī, meaning 'of Hindustan.'

2. ADJ

/ ˌhɪnduˈstɑni / (AmE), / ˌhɪnduˈstɑːni / (BrE)
relating to Hindustan, its people, or their language
relating to Hindustan, its people, or language

Examples

The classical music of northern India includes many Hindustani traditions.
She is an expert in Hindustani linguistics and teaches the language at university.
The Hindustani culture is known for its rich history and artistic achievements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indian - relating to India, its people, or languages

Etymology

From Hindi 'Hindustānī', from 'Hindustān' (an old name for northern India).

3. NOUN

/ ˌhɪnduˈstɑni / (AmE), / ˌhɪnduˈstɑːni / (BrE)
the form of Hindi-Urdu spoken chiefly in Northern India, especially around Delhi, serving as the basis for standard Hindi and Urdu
form of Hindi-Urdu spoken in Northern India

Examples

Many Bollywood films are produced in Hindustani to appeal to a wider audience across India and Pakistan.
My grandmother speaks Hindustani, which makes it easy for her to communicate in both India and Pakistan.
Linguists often study Hindustani as the basis for both modern Hindi and Urdu.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hindi - the official language of India, derived from Hindustani
  • Synonym: Urdu - the official language of Pakistan, derived from the same base as Hindi

Etymology

From Hindi-Urdu Hindustānī (of Hindustan, i.e., India).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06983047-n
Synset POS: n
