opportuneness

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑː.pərˈtuːn.nəs / (AmE), / ˌɒp.əˈtʃuːn.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being timely or well-chosen for a particular purpose; appropriateness of time or convenience
the quality of being timely or convenient

Examples

The opportuneness of his arrival allowed us to avoid a major problem.
We benefited greatly from the opportuneness of the funding at a critical moment.
She recognized the opportuneness of the suggestion and decided to act swiftly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timeliness - the quality of occurring at a suitable or opportune time
  • Antonym: inopportuneness - the quality of being untimely or inconvenient

Etymology

From 'opportune' (from Latin opportunus, meaning 'favorable, convenient') + -ness.
