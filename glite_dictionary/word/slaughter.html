slaughter

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɔtər / (AmE), / ˈslɔːtə / (BrE)
the violent and excessive killing of a large number of people
mass, violent killing of people

Examples

The documentary depicted the horrific slaughter of innocent civilians during the war.
Public outrage grew after news of the slaughter spread around the world.
History is filled with examples of slaughter caused by conflict and intolerance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: massacre - an indiscriminate and brutal killing of many people
  • Synonym: genocide - the deliberate killing of a large group of people, especially those of a particular nation or ethnic group
  • Antonym: rescue - to free someone from danger or harm; an action to save lives

Etymology

From Old English *slahtere* (killing, murder), related to *slay* (to kill).

2. NOUN

/ ˈslɔtər / (AmE), / ˈslɔːtə / (BrE)
The killing of animals, especially for food.
killing of animals for food

Examples

The slaughter of cattle is strictly regulated by health authorities.
Protests were held against the annual slaughter of seals for their fur.
The factory specializes in the humane slaughter of poultry for supermarkets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butchery - the killing and cutting up of animals for food
  • Synonym: massacre - the killing of many people or animals in a cruel way
  • Synonym: slaughterhouse - a place where animals are killed for food

Etymology

From Old French *eslaughter* (killing), from Proto-Germanic *slakhtiz* (killing, slaughter).

3. NOUN

/ ˈslɔːtər / (AmE), / ˈslɔːtə / (BrE)
an overwhelming or decisive defeat, especially in a contest or competition
decisive defeat
informal

Examples

The football game turned into a slaughter by halftime, with one team leading by fifty points.
Critics described the election outcome as a political slaughter for the incumbent party.
Last night's chess match was a total slaughter; she won every round with ease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: massacre - a crushing defeat, especially in sports or competitions

4. VERB

/ ˈslɔtɚ / (AmE), / ˈslɔːtə / (BrE)
to kill animals, especially for food, usually in large numbers or in a deliberate and controlled way
kill animals for food

Examples

The farmers slaughter the cattle each autumn to stock up on meat for the winter.
Regulations require that animals are slaughtered humanely before processing.
She could not watch as the chickens were being slaughtered in the backyard.

Synset Examples

They slaughtered their only goat to survive the winter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butcher - to kill animals and prepare them for sale as meat
  • Antonym: spare - to refrain from killing or injuring; to save an animal from death

Etymology

From Old French *esclater* (to slaughter), of Germanic origin.

5. VERB

/ ˈslɔtər / (AmE), / ˈslɔːtə / (BrE)
to kill a large number of people in a violent and indiscriminate way
kill many people violently and indiscriminately

Examples

The invading army slaughtered thousands of villagers without mercy.
Historical records reveal that rebels slaughtered prisoners after the failed uprising.
The dictator threatened to slaughter anyone who opposed his regime.

Synset Examples

The Hutus massacred the Tutsis in Rwanda

Related Terms

  • Synonym: massacre - to deliberately and violently kill a large number of people

Etymology

From Old English *slahtrian* (butchering), related to *slēan* (to strike).
