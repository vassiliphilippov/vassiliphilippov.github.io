Leuciscus

1. NOUN

/ luˈsɪskəs / (AmE), / luˈsɪskəs / (BrE)
a genus of freshwater fish in the family Cyprinidae, including the dace and chub.
genus of freshwater fish (dace, chub)

Examples

Fishes of the genus Leuciscus are found throughout European rivers and streams.
The common dace, a member of Leuciscus, is known for its streamlined body.
Researchers identified the specimen as Leuciscus based on its distinctive morphological traits.

Etymology

From Greek leukos (white), referring to the silvery color of some species.

2. NOUN

/ luˈsɪskəs / (AmE), / luːˈsɪskəs / (BrE)
a genus of freshwater fish in the family Cyprinidae, which includes the dace and chub
genus of freshwater fish (dace, chub)

Examples

The genus Leuciscus comprises several species found in European rivers.
Ichthyologists recently discovered a new member of the Leuciscus genus in Central Asia.
Both the chub and the dace belong to the Leuciscus genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dace - a small freshwater fish, member of Leuciscus genus
  • Synonym: chub - a thick-bodied freshwater fish of the Leuciscus genus

Etymology

From Latin 'leuciscus', from Greek 'leukiskos' (a kind of white fish), diminutive of 'leukos' (white).
