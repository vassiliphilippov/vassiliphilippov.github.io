Cotoneaster

1. NOUN

/ kəˌtoʊniˈæstər / (AmE), / kəˌtəʊniˈæstə / (BrE)
a genus of deciduous or evergreen shrubs native to the Old World, widely cultivated for their ornamental berries and foliage
a genus of ornamental shrubs

Examples

The Cotoneaster in her garden produced bright red berries in the autumn.
Many landscapers choose Cotoneaster shrubs for their hardiness and attractive foliage.
Several species of Cotoneaster are popular in parks and public gardens throughout Europe.

Etymology

From Latin 'cotoneum' (quince) and '-aster' (resembling), referring to the plant's quince-like appearance.
