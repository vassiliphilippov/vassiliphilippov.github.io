expansionism

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspænʃəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪkˈspænʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
the policy or doctrine of expanding a nation's territory or extending its economic or political influence
policy of territorial or economic expansion

Examples

The government's commitment to expansionism led to several territorial disputes with neighboring countries.
Many historians argue that economic expansionism shaped international relations during the 19th century.
Critics accused the administration of pursuing reckless expansionism at the expense of peace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imperialism - policy of extending a country's power through colonization or force
  • Antonym: isolationism - policy of remaining apart from the affairs or interests of other groups

Etymology

Formed from 'expand' + '-ism', modeling similar political terms.
