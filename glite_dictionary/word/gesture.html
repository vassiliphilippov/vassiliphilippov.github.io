gesture

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛstʃər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛstʃə / (BrE)
A movement of part of the body, especially a hand or the head, to express an idea or meaning.
movement expressing an idea or feeling

Examples

She made a friendly gesture by waving her hand as we arrived.
His angry gesture left no doubt about how he felt.
The teacher used a simple gesture to quiet the noisy class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signal - an action or gesture that serves as a message
  • Synonym: motion - the act of moving one part of the body
  • Antonym: speech - spoken expression of thoughts

Etymology

From Latin 'gestura' (bearing, posture), from 'gerere' (to carry, bear).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛstʃər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛstʃə / (BrE)
the use of bodily movements, especially of the hands, to express an idea or convey a message, often according to set signals
movement expressing an idea or message

Examples

He waved his hand in a friendly gesture as she approached.
The referee made a gesture to indicate a foul during the match.
Nodding is a common gesture of agreement in many cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signal - an action or movement used to convey information or instructions
  • Synonym: sign - a movement or action conveying information

Etymology

From Latin gestura (bearing, behavior), from gestus (gesture, posture).

3. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛstʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛstʃə / (BrE)
an action performed to convey a feeling or intention, especially as a symbolic act rather than for practical effect
action showing intention or feeling

Examples

As a goodwill gesture, the company donated computers to the local school.
Sending flowers was just a small gesture of appreciation for her help.
His apology seemed more like a political gesture than genuine remorse.

Synset Examples

a political gesture
a gesture of defiance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: action - a deed or act performed, usually for a particular purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'gestura' (bearing, manner, carriage), from 'gerere' (to bear, carry).

4. VERB

/ ˈdʒɛstʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛstʃə / (BrE)
to express or indicate something by making a movement, especially with the hands or head
express with movement

Examples

He gestured toward the door to indicate that it was time to leave.
Without speaking, the teacher gestured for the students to be quiet.
