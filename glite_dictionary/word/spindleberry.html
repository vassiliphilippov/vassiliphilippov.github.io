spindleberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪndəlˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈspɪndlˌbɛri / (BrE)
Any of several shrubby trees or woody vines of the genus Euonymus, often bearing showy, usually reddish berries.
a shrub or vine of the genus Euonymus with reddish berries

Examples

The spindleberry is admired for its vibrant autumn fruit and foliage.
Gardeners often plant spindleberry bushes for their decorative berries.
Birds are attracted to the bright spindleberry fruit in the winter months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: euonymus - genus of shrubs and small trees commonly called spindleberries

Etymology

Named for the spindle tree (Euonymus europaeus), whose hard wood was used for making spindles.
