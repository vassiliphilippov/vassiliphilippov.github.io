refreshment

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈfrɛʃmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈfrɛʃmənt / (BrE)
an activity or period that renews one's energy and spirits through enjoyment and relaxation
restorative activity or relaxation

Examples

After hours in the office, a walk in the park provided much-needed refreshment.
Many people seek refreshment on weekends by spending time in nature.
Yoga offers both physical and mental refreshment after a stressful day.

Synset Examples

time for rest and refreshment by the pool
days of joyous recreation with his friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recreation - activity done for enjoyment and relaxation, not work
  • Synonym: rest - period of relaxing or ceasing activity to recover strength

Etymology

From Old French *refreschement*, based on 'refresh'.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈfrɛʃmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈfrɛʃmənt / (BrE)
snacks and drinks, especially those served as a light meal or during a break at events or meetings.
snacks and drinks served at events

Examples

Light refreshment will be provided after the ceremony.
They set out tea and refreshments for the guests in the garden.
A table near the entrance was covered with refreshment for the travelers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snack - a small amount of food eaten between meals
  • Degree Higher: meal - an occasion when people eat food, especially breakfast, lunch, or dinner

Etymology

From Old French 'refreschement', based on 'refreschir' (to refresh).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07590069-n
Synset POS: n
