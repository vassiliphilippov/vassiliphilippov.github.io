SSW

1. NOUN

/ ɛs ɛs ˈdʌbəl.juː / (AmE), / ɛs ɛs ˈdʌbəl.juː / (BrE)
the compass point that is halfway between south and southwest, corresponding to a bearing of 202.5°.
compass point between south and southwest

Examples

The wind was blowing steadily from the SSW throughout the afternoon.
The hiker adjusted his direction to travel SSW toward the next checkpoint.
The ship's log noted a sudden change to SSW at sunrise.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: south - the compass point at 180°, directly opposite north
  • Degree Higher: southwest - the compass point at 225°, halfway between south and west

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'south-southwest'.
