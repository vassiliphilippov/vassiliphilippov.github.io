cony

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊni / (AmE), / ˈkəʊni / (BrE)
a small, herbivorous mammal of Africa and Asia, resembling a rodent but related to hoofed animals; also called hyrax
small African or Asian mammal, hyrax

Examples

The cony is well adapted to rocky habitats throughout Africa and the Middle East.
Naturalists observe that the cony communicates using a sharp, whistling call.
Our guide pointed out a cony sunning itself on the rocks during our safari.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyrax - small mammal of Africa and the Middle East, also called 'rock rabbit'

Etymology

From Middle English *conei*, from Old French *conil*, from Latin *cuniculus* (rabbit, burrow). Original meaning applied to rabbits, later extended to hyrax.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊni / (AmE), / ˈkəʊni / (BrE)
a small, short-eared, burrowing mammal found in rocky uplands of Asia and western North America, also known as a pika
small mammal of rocky uplands (pika)

Examples

The cony darted between the rocks to escape its predators.
During our hike, we spotted a cony chirping among the boulders.
Some species of cony are threatened by habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pika - small mountain-dwelling mammal related to rabbits

Etymology

From Old French conil, from Latin cuniculus (rabbit, burrowing animal).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊni / (AmE), / ˈkəʊni / (BrE)
an old term for a rabbit, especially the European rabbit, or any of several small burrowing mammals with long ears and short tails
old term for a rabbit
archaic

Examples

The old garden wall was home to a family of conies.
In medieval recipes, a cony was a popular meat for noble feasts.
The ancient text mentions hunting the wild conies of the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rabbit - a small burrowing mammal with long ears and a short tail

Etymology

From Old French conil, from Latin cuniculus (rabbit).
