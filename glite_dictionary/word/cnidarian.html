cnidarian

1. NOUN

/ naɪˈdɛriən / (AmE), / nɪˈdɛːrɪən / (BrE)
a member of a phylum of aquatic animals, such as jellyfish, corals, and sea anemones, characterized by a soft, saclike body with a single opening and tentacles equipped with stinging cells
aquatic animal with stinging tentacles (e.g., jellyfish, coral)
medical

Examples

Jellyfish are a well-known type of cnidarian found in oceans around the world.
The cnidarian body plan includes a single opening surrounded by tentacles.
Coral reefs are formed by colonies of cnidarian polyps living together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jellyfish - free-swimming marine cnidarian with umbrella-shaped body and trailing tentacles
  • Synonym: coral - marine cnidarian forming colonies of tiny polyps, often building reefs
  • Antonym: echinoderm - marine invertebrate such as starfish and sea urchins, related but distinct phylum

Etymology

From Greek knidē (nettle), referring to the stinging cells.
