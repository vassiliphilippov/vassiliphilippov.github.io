amyxia

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɪksi.ə / (AmE), / əˈmɪksi.ə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which no mucus is produced by the body.
absence of mucus production (medical)
medical

Examples

Patients with amyxia may experience dryness in their nasal and oral passages.
Research into amyxia helps doctors understand how mucus protects the respiratory system.
The doctor diagnosed her with amyxia after observing the complete lack of mucus.

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) and 'myxa' (mucus).
