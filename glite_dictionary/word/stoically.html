stoically

1. ADV

/ ˈstoʊɪkli / (AmE), / ˈstəʊɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that shows no emotion, especially when experiencing pain or hardship; in a stoic manner.
in a stoic, unemotional way

Examples

She stoically endured the long and painful treatment without complaining.
Despite the harsh criticism, he stoically accepted the verdict and moved on.
The soldiers stood stoically in the cold, waiting for their orders.

Synset Examples

he stoically accepted all suffering

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stoic - a person who can endure pain or hardship without showing emotion

Etymology

From 'stoic', ultimately from Greek 'Stōïkos' (relating to the Stoic school of philosophy).
