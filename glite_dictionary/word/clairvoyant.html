clairvoyant

1. NOUN

/ klɛrˈvɔɪənt / (AmE), / klɛəˈvɔɪənt / (BrE)
a person who claims to have a supernatural ability to perceive events in the future or beyond normal sensory contact
person claiming supernatural perception

Examples

The woman claimed to be a clairvoyant who could communicate with the spirits of the dead.
Many people visit a clairvoyant hoping to learn what the future holds.
Skeptics often question whether a true clairvoyant has ever existed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychic - person claiming supernatural mental abilities
  • Antonym: skeptic - someone inclined to doubt supernatural claims

Etymology

From French *clairvoyant* (clear-seeing), from *clair* (clear) + *voyant* (seeing), present participle of *voir* (to see).

2. ADJ

/ klɛrˈvɔɪənt / (AmE), / klɛəˈvɔɪənt / (BrE)
Having or claiming to have the ability to perceive events or information beyond normal sensory contact.
able to perceive beyond normal senses

Examples

She claimed to be clairvoyant and could sense things before they happened.
Some believe that animals are clairvoyant and can detect natural disasters in advance.
Despite his clairvoyant instincts, no one believed his warnings until it was too late.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychic - having abilities to perceive information hidden from the normal senses
  • Antonym: rational - based on or in accordance with reason or logic; not supernatural

Etymology

From French clairvoyant, from clair (clear) + voyant (seeing), from voir (to see).

3. ADJ

/ klɛrˈvɔɪənt / (AmE), / kleəˈvɔɪənt / (BrE)
having or claiming to have the ability to see events in the future or beyond normal sensory contact
able to foresee the future

Examples

The fortune-teller claimed to be clairvoyant and predicted great changes in my life.
Her clairvoyant abilities astonished all who visited her for readings.
He dismissed the idea that anyone could truly be clairvoyant about the future.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychic - a person who claims to have supernatural mental abilities, especially to foresee the future

Etymology

From French 'clairvoyant', from 'clair' (clear) + 'voyant' (seeing), from Latin 'clarus' and 'videre' (to see).
