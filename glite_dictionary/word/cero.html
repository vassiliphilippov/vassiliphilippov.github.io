cero

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪəroʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɪərəʊ / (BrE)
a large edible mackerel found in temperate coastal Atlantic waters of the United States, especially valued as a game and food fish
large edible Atlantic mackerel

Examples

Fishermen on the Atlantic coast often seek the cero for its fighting spirit and flavor.
The restaurant served grilled cero with a side of roasted vegetables.
A record-breaking cero was caught near Florida last summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king mackerel - a closely related large Atlantic mackerel species

Etymology

From Spanish 'cero', referring to the species Scomberomorus regalis.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪəroʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɛərəʊ / (BrE)
a large mackerel (Scomberomorus regalis) with a long pointed snout, found off the eastern Atlantic coast south to Brazil, prized as a food and game fish.
a species of large mackerel

Examples

Fishermen off the coast of Florida were thrilled to catch a massive cero during the tournament.
The chef prepared the fresh cero with a blend of local spices for dinner.
Due to its speed and fight, the cero is a favorite among sport fishers in the Caribbean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king mackerel - a related large mackerel species found in the Atlantic

Etymology

From Spanish 'cero', meaning a kind of fish, likely from Latin 'cērus'.
