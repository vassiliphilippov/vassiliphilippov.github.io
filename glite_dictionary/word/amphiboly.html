amphiboly

1. NOUN

/ æmˈfɪbəli / (AmE), / amˈfɪbəli / (BrE)
A grammatical construction in a sentence that creates ambiguity, leading to more than one possible interpretation.
ambiguous grammatical construction

Examples

The sentence 'I saw the man with the telescope' is an amphiboly that could mean two different things.
Writers should try to avoid amphiboly in order to express their ideas more clearly.
The legal document was challenged in court because of an amphiboly that made its intent unclear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambiguity - uncertainty or inexactness of meaning in language
  • Synonym: equivocation - the use of ambiguous language to conceal the truth or to mislead

Etymology

From Greek amphibolia (ambiguity), based on amphibolos (ambiguous, doubtful).
