Senecio triangularis

1. NOUN

/ səˈniːʃioʊ traɪˌæŋɡjuˈɛərɪs / (AmE), / səˈniːsiəʊ traɪˌæŋɡjʊˈeərɪs / (BrE)
A perennial plant with sharply toothed triangular leaves and clusters of yellow flower heads, native to moist mountain areas of western North America.
a mountain wildflower of western North America

Examples

While hiking in the Rockies, we spotted several Senecio triangularis growing near the stream.
The sharply toothed leaves make Senecio triangularis easy to identify among other wildflowers.
Botanists study Senecio triangularis to understand its adaptation to mountain environments.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Senecio' meaning 'old man', referring to the silky white hairs on the seeds; 'triangularis' meaning 'triangular', describing the leaf shape.
