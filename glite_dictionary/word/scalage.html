scalage

1. NOUN

/ ˈskeɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈskeɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
the process or result of estimating the quantity of usable lumber in a log or group of logs
estimation of lumber quantity in logs

Examples

The forester performed a careful scalage before the timber sale negotiations began.
Accurate scalage is essential to ensure fair pricing in the lumber market.
After the scalage, they determined that the logs would yield enough wood for the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scaling - the act of measuring the amount of lumber in logs

Etymology

From 'scale' (to measure) + the suffix '-age', denoting action or process.
