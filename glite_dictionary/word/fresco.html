fresco

1. VERB

/ ˈfrɛs.koʊ / (AmE), / ˈfrɛs.kəʊ / (BrE)
to paint a picture or design onto wet plaster on a wall or ceiling, using the fresco technique
paint onto wet plaster (art technique)

Examples

The artist decided to fresco an elaborate scene on the chapel's main wall.
Historical buildings often feature beautifully frescoed ceilings from the Renaissance period.
She spent months frescoing the dome with images of the heavens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mural - a large painting applied directly to a wall or ceiling

Etymology

From Italian 'fresco' (fresh), referring to the technique of painting on fresh plaster.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛs.koʊ / (AmE), / ˈfrɛs.kəʊ / (BrE)
A technique of painting in which water-based pigments are applied onto freshly laid wet plaster, making the painting an integral part of the wall surface.
wall painting technique on wet plaster

Examples

The church ceiling was decorated with a stunning fresco depicting scenes from the Bible.
Restorers worked carefully to preserve the centuries-old fresco during the renovation.
She studied the techniques of fresco painting in art school in Florence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mural - a painting or other work of art executed directly on a wall

Etymology

From Italian 'fresco' (fresh), referring to the fresh plaster used.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00938921-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛs.koʊ / (AmE), / ˈfrɛs.kəʊ / (BrE)
A painting done rapidly in watercolor on wet plaster on a wall or ceiling, so that the colors penetrate the plaster and become fixed as it dries.
painting on wet plaster

Examples

The artist completed a magnificent fresco on the chapel ceiling.
Restoration of the ancient fresco revealed vibrant original colors.
She studied the techniques of fresco painting during her trip to Italy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mural - A painting or other work of art executed directly on a wall

Etymology

From Italian 'fresco' meaning 'fresh', referring to painting on fresh, wet plaster.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
