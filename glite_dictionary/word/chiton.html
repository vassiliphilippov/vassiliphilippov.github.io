chiton

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaɪtən / (AmE), / ˈkɪtɒn / (BrE)
a primitive marine mollusk with an elongated body and a shell made of eight overlapping calcareous plates
marine mollusk with eight shell plates

Examples

The rocky shore was home to a rare chiton clinging tightly to the stones.
Marine biologists studied the unique shell structure of the chiton.
At low tide, you can often find a chiton nestled among the seaweed.

Etymology

From Greek khitōn (tunic), referring to the tunic-like shell plates.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaɪ.tən / (AmE), / ˈkaɪ.tɒn / (BrE)
A woolen tunic worn by men and women in ancient Greece, typically fastened at the shoulders and belted at the waist.
ancient Greek woolen tunic

Examples

The museum displayed a beautifully preserved chiton from the fifth century BCE.
In ancient Greece, a chiton was commonly worn by both men and women as everyday clothing.
She dressed as a Greek goddess, complete with a flowing white chiton and a golden belt.

Etymology

From Greek khitōn (tunic, garment), via Latin chiton.
