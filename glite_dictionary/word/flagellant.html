flagellant

1. NOUN

/ ˈflædʒələnt / (AmE), / ˈflædʒɪlənt / (BrE)
a person who is whipped or whips themselves, especially for sexual pleasure or gratification
person engaging in whipping for sexual pleasure

Examples

He identified himself as a flagellant and discussed his interests with the group.
The film explores the secret world of flagellants who find pleasure in pain.
As a flagellant, he negotiated boundaries and safety with his partner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masochist - a person who derives pleasure, especially sexual gratification, from their own pain or humiliation

Etymology

From Latin flagellans, present participle of flagellare (to whip).

2. NOUN

/ ˈflædʒələnt / (AmE), / ˈflædʒələnt / (BrE)
a person who subjects themself or others to whipping as a form of religious discipline or penance
person who whips themself for religious reasons

Examples

During the Middle Ages, flagellants were known to parade through towns whipping themselves in public.
He became a flagellant after joining a strict religious sect.
Some historians study the rituals practiced by flagellants in medieval Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penitent - a person who repents for their sins and performs acts of penance

Etymology

From Latin 'flagellans', present participle of 'flagellare' (to whip).
