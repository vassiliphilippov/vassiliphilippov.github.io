Smitane

1. NOUN

/ smɪˈtɑːn / (AmE), / smɪˈtɑːn / (BrE)
A velouté or brown sauce prepared with sautéed chopped onion, dry white wine, and sour cream.
sauce with onion, wine, and sour cream

Examples

The chef poured a delicate Smitane over the veal to finish the dish.
Her recipe calls for a rich Smitane to enhance the flavor of the chicken.
I learned how to make Smitane during my French cooking class.

Etymology

From French 'smitane', adapted from Russian 'smetana' (sour cream).
