vellum

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛləm / (AmE), / ˈvɛləm / (BrE)
A fine parchment made from the skin of a young animal, such as a calf or lamb, used especially for writing or printing.
fine parchment from a young animal's skin

Examples

The medieval manuscript was carefully written on vellum to ensure its longevity.
Artists often prefer vellum for calligraphy because of its smooth surface.
The museum displays rare documents preserved on original vellum sheets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parchment - material made from animal skin for writing

Etymology

From Old French 'velin' (calfskin), from 'veau' (calf), from Latin 'vitellus' (little calf).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɛləm / (AmE), / ˈvɛləm / (BrE)
a heavy, creamy-colored paper resembling parchment, often used for fine documents or artistic purposes
creamy heavy paper resembling parchment

Examples

The artist chose to use vellum for her watercolor illustrations because of its smooth surface.
The invitation was printed on elegant vellum to give it a luxurious feel.
Old manuscripts were often written on vellum due to its durability and texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parchment - material made from the skin of sheep or goats, or paper imitating such material

Etymology

From Old French 'velin' (calfskin), from 'veau' (calf), ultimately from Latin 'vitellus' (calf).
