race

1. VERB

/ reɪs / (AmE), / reɪs / (BrE)
to move or work very quickly toward achieving a goal, often in competition with others

Examples

Scientists around the world are racing to develop a new vaccine.
Our team is racing against the deadline to finish the project.
Companies are racing to release the first electric self-driving car.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - to do something with great speed, often hurriedly

From Old Norse *ras* (a running, rush, race).

4. NOUN

/ reɪs / (AmE), / reɪs / (BrE)
(Biology) A group within a species that has distinct inherited characteristics, often as a result of geographical isolation.
a division of a species (biology)

Examples

Several races of the butterfly have developed due to their separation on different islands.
Biologists debate whether these birds represent distinct races or merely local variations.
Within one plant species, there may be many races adapted to different environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subspecies - a taxonomic category below species, often used for geographically isolated groups

Etymology

From Old French 'rasse', probably from Italian 'razza' (race, breed).
