shut-in

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃʌt ɪn / (AmE), / ˈʃʌt ɪn / (BrE)
a person who is unable to leave their home or bed because of chronic illness or injury
person confined indoors due to illness

Examples

The charity delivers meals daily to local shut-ins who cannot leave their homes.
After her accident, she became a shut-in and rarely saw anyone except close friends.
Volunteers provide companionship and assistance to elderly shut-ins in the community.

Etymology

Formed from phrasal verb 'shut in', literally 'shut inside'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃʌt ɪn / (AmE), / ˈʃʌt ɪn / (BrE)
Reluctant to leave home or mix socially; introverted or withdrawn from society.
introverted; withdrawn from society

Examples

Many shut-in individuals prefer reading or online activities over social gatherings.
Ever since his illness, he has become more of a shut-in and rarely leaves his apartment.
The documentary follows a shut-in artist who finds inspiration without venturing outside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: introverted - more inward-looking and reserved
  • Antonym: extroverted - outgoing; socially confident

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'shut in', meaning to confine within; figuratively extended to personal characteristics.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01354150-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈʃʌt ɪn / (AmE), / ˈʃʌt ɪn / (BrE)
Confined indoors, usually because of illness or disability.
confined indoors by illness or disability

Examples

After the accident, he became a shut-in and rarely left his house.
Many shut-in patients receive visits from volunteers to help with their needs.
As a shut-in, she relied on her neighbors to do her grocery shopping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: housebound - Unable to leave one's home, especially because of illness or old age

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'shut in' (to confine indoors).
