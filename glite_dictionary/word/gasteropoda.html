Gasteropoda

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæstɚˈɑːpədə / (AmE), / ˌɡæstərəʊˈpəʊdə / (BrE)
A large class of mollusks that includes snails, slugs, and their relatives, characterized by a muscular foot used for locomotion and often a coiled shell.
class of mollusks: snails and slugs
medical

Examples

The class Gasteropoda includes a diverse range of marine and terrestrial species.
Scientists study the evolution of Gasteropoda to understand adaptation in mollusks.
Many museum exhibits feature shells from different members of the Gasteropoda.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gastropod - a member of the class Gasteropoda; snail or slug

Etymology

From Greek gastēr (stomach) + pous, podos (foot), referring to their ventral foot.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡæstərəˈpoʊdə / (AmE), / ˌɡastərəˈpəʊdə / (BrE)
a large class of mollusks that includes snails, slugs, and their relatives, characterized by a univalve shell or no shell and a distinct head with sensory organs
class of mollusks: snails and slugs

Examples

Many marine biologists study the anatomy of Gasteropoda to better understand mollusk evolution.
Species in the Gasteropoda class can live in both aquatic and terrestrial environments.
The diversity of shell forms in Gasteropoda is remarkable among mollusks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gastropod - a member of the class Gasteropoda; snail or slug

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek gastēr (stomach) + pous, podos (foot), referring to their ventral foot.
