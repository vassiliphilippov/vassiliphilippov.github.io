stalogometer

1. NOUN

/ stəˌlæɡˈɑːmətər / (AmE), / stəˌlæɡˈɒmɪtə / (BrE)
A laboratory device used to measure surface tension of a liquid by the stalagmometric method.
device for measuring liquid surface tension

Examples

The scientist carefully filled the stalogometer to begin measuring the solution's surface tension.
After calibrating the stalogometer, she recorded the number of drops required for each sample.
A broken stalogometer can lead to inaccurate measurements in surface tension experiments.

Synset Examples

The part of the bottom of the stalagmometer is narrowed down to let the fluid fall out from the tube in a shape of drop.

Etymology

From Greek *stalagma* (drop) and -meter (measuring device).
