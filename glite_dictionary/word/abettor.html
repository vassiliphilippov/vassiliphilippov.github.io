abettor

1. NOUN

/ əˈbɛtər / (AmE), / əˈbɛtə / (BrE)
A person who encourages, assists, or incites another to commit a crime or wrongdoing.
person who helps or encourages wrongdoing
legal

Examples

The court found her guilty as an abettor in the fraud scheme.
Without an abettor, the main perpetrator may have failed to carry out the plan.
Under the law, an abettor can receive the same punishment as the principal offender.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accomplice - a person who helps another in committing a crime
  • Synonym: instigator - a person who initiates or incites a wrongdoing
  • Antonym: opponent - someone who opposes or prevents wrongdoing

Etymology

From Old French abeteur, from abetter (to abet).
