Kodiak

1. PROPN

/ ˈkoʊdiˌæk / (AmE), / ˈkəʊdiːˌæk / (BrE)
an island off southern Alaska in the Gulf of Alaska, notable as the site of the first European settlement in the area, founded by Russians in 1784.
island off southern Alaska, historic Russian settlement

Examples

The remote Kodiak Island is home to a diverse range of wildlife.
Early Russian settlers established a colony on Kodiak in the 18th century.
Fishermen travel to Kodiak for its rich marine resources every year.

Etymology

From Alutiiq (an Alaskan Native language) name 'Qikertaq', adapted by Russian explorers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊdiˌæk / (AmE), / ˈkəʊdiːˌæk / (BrE)
a large subspecies of brown bear native to the coastal regions of Alaska and British Columbia, especially Kodiak Island
large brown bear of Alaska and British Columbia

Examples

The Kodiak is among the largest bears in the world by size and weight.
While hiking in Alaska, they spotted a massive Kodiak fishing for salmon.
A zoo recently welcomed a new Kodiak as part of its bear exhibit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brown bear - a large bear species found throughout Eurasia and North America

Etymology

Named after Kodiak Island in Alaska, where the species is found.
