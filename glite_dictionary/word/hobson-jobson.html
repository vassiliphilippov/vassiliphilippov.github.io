Hobson-Jobson

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɑbsən ˈdʒɑbsən / (AmE), / ˌhɒbsən ˈdʒɒbsən / (BrE)
A term used for an English word or expression that is derived from an Indian language, especially in the context of Anglo-Indian vocabulary; also refers to the title of a dictionary of Anglo-Indian words and phrases.
English word derived from an Indian language; Anglo-Indian dictionary title

Examples

He wrote a paper on the origins of several Hobson-Jobson terms in English.
The word 'bungalow' is a classic example of a Hobson-Jobson in everyday use.
Many linguists consult Hobson-Jobson when researching Anglo-Indian words.

Synset Examples

His interest in linguistics includes studying Hobson-Jobson.
Hobson-Jobson explains many words in Anglo-Indian English.
The term 'pajamas' is a Hobson-Jobson from Indian origins.

Etymology

From the title of the 1886 glossary *Hobson-Jobson* by Henry Yule and A.C. Burnell, itself derived from a British mishearing of the Muslim chant 'Yā Ḥasan! Yā Ḥusayn!'
