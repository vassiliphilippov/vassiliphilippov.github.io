to ensure

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈʃʊr / (AmE), / ɪnˈʃɔː / (BrE)
to make certain that something will happen or is the case
make certain that something happens

Examples

We must ensure that all doors are locked before leaving the building.
The company takes steps to ensure product quality at every stage.
Regular backups ensure that no important data is lost.

Synset Examples

He verified that the valves were closed
See that the curtains are closed
control the quality of the product

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guarantee - to promise or make certain that something will happen
  • Synonym: to assure - to tell someone something positively to dispel any doubts
  • Antonym: to prevent - to stop something from happening

Etymology

From Old French 'enseurer' (to make sure), from 'en-' + 'seur' (sure).

2. VERB

/ ɛnˈʃʊr / (AmE), / ɪnˈʃɔː / (BrE)
to make certain that something will happen or be the case
make certain that something happens

Examples

We must double-check our calculations to ensure accuracy.
Regular maintenance helps ensure the engine runs smoothly.
Please ensure that all doors are locked before you leave.

Synset Examples

This nest egg will ensure a nice retirement for us
Preparation will guarantee success!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guarantee - to promise that something will happen or be done
  • Synonym: to assure - to tell someone confidently to make them feel certain
  • Antonym: to prevent - to stop something from happening

Etymology

From Old French *enseurer* (to make sure), from Latin *securus* (secure).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00892111-v
Synset POS: v
