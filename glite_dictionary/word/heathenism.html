heathenism

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːðəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈhiːðənɪzm / (BrE)
beliefs or religious practices of people who do not follow Christianity, Judaism, or Islam (often used historically or pejoratively)
non-Abrahamic religions; paganism

Examples

Some ancient festivals have roots in heathenism rather than Christian tradition.
Accusations of heathenism were often used to justify religious wars.
The author studied the rituals and beliefs of heathenism in early European societies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paganism - beliefs and practices of pagan religions

Etymology

From heathen + -ism, where 'heathen' is from Old English 'hǣthen' (non-Christian, pagan).
