khaddar

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːdər / (AmE), / ˈkadə / (BrE)
A coarse, handspun cotton cloth traditionally made in India.
coarse Indian homespun cotton cloth

Examples

Many traditional garments in India are made from khaddar for its comfort and breathability.
The artisan demonstrated how khaddar is woven on a handloom.
During the movement for independence, wearing khaddar became a symbol of national pride.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: khadi - coarse homespun cotton cloth, especially in India; often used as a synonym for khaddar

Etymology

From Hindi/Urdu 'khādar', meaning 'coarse cotton cloth'.
