allometric

1. ADJ

/ ˌæl.əˈmɛ.trɪk / (AmE), / ˌæl.əˈmɛt.rɪk / (BrE)
relating to the growth of a part of an organism in proportion to the growth of the whole or to another part, or according to allometry.
relating to proportional biological growth

Examples

Many animal organs show allometric growth compared to overall body size.
Researchers analyzed the allometric relationships between limb length and body mass in mammals.
The study explores allometric patterns in fish development.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: isometric - relating to equal growth or measurement in all parts

Etymology

From allometry, from Greek 'allo-' (other) + 'metron' (measure).
