Pre-Raphaelite

1. ADJ

/ priː ˈræfiəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / priː ˈræfɪəˌlaɪt / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the Pre-Raphaelites, a group of English artists and writers of the mid-19th century who aimed to return to the detail, vivid colors, and complex compositions of art before the time of Raphael.
relating to the Pre-Raphaelite artistic movement

Examples

The painting's Pre-Raphaelite style is evident in its vivid colors and intricate details.
Many Pre-Raphaelite artists sought inspiration from medieval literature and nature.
Her collection includes several beautifully illustrated Pre-Raphaelite books.

Synset Examples

the Pre-Raphaelite painters

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Raphaelite - relating to the style of Raphael

Etymology

Formed from 'pre-' (before) and 'Raphaelite' (referring to Raphael, the Italian Renaissance painter).

2. NOUN

/ priˈræfəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / priːˈræfəlaɪt / (BrE)
A painter or writer belonging to or influenced by a 19th-century movement dedicated to reviving the detail, vivid colors, and complex compositions of early Renaissance art.
member of 19th-century art/literary movement

Examples

She studied the techniques of the Pre-Raphaelites for her dissertation on Victorian art.
Many Pre-Raphaelite paintings feature medieval or literary themes and exquisite attention to detail.
As a writer, he was heavily influenced by the ideals of the Pre-Raphaelite movement.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Romantic - related artistic and literary movement emphasizing emotion and individualism
  • Degree Higher: Impressionist - member of an artistic movement after the Pre-Raphaelites, focusing on light and color
  • Degree Lower: realist - artist or writer depicting subjects truthfully, linked via stylistic differences with Pre-Raphaelites

Etymology

Formed from 'pre-' (before) and 'Raphael,' referring to the Italian painter Raphael (1483–1520).
