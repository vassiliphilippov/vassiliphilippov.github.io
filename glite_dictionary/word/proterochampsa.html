Proterochampsa

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊtəroʊˈkæmpsə / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtərəʊˈkæmpsə / (BrE)
A genus of extinct archosauromorph reptiles from the Triassic period, known from South America.
genus of extinct Triassic reptiles

Examples

Fossils of Proterochampsa have been discovered in Triassic rock formations in South America.
Paleontologists study Proterochampsa to learn about the early evolution of archosaur reptiles.
The skull structure of Proterochampsa shows crocodile-like characteristics.

Etymology

From Greek 'proteros' (earlier) and 'champsa' (crocodile).
