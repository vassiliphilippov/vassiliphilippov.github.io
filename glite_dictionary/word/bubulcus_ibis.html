Bubulcus ibis

1. NOUN

/ buːˈbʌlkəs ˈaɪbɪs / (AmE), / bjuːˈbʌlkəs ˈaɪbɪs / (BrE)
A small white egret commonly found in warm regions, often seen around grazing animals, also known as the cattle egret.
small white egret; cattle egret

Examples

A flock of Bubulcus ibis followed the cattle across the field in search of insects.
The Bubulcus ibis is easily recognized by its snowy white feathers and yellow bill.
Scientists have studied how Bubulcus ibis benefits both itself and large grazing animals through their association.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cattle egret - common name for Bubulcus ibis, a small white egret often seen near livestock

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Bubulcus' meaning 'herdsman' and 'ibis' referring to the Egyptian word for the sacred bird.
