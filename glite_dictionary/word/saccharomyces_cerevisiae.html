Saccharomyces cerevisiae

1. NOUN

/ sæk.əˌroʊ.maɪˌsiz sər.əˈvɪs.i.i / (AmE), / sæk.əˌrɒ.mɪˌsiz sə.rəˈvɪs.i.aɪ / (BrE)
a species of yeast widely used in baking and brewing to ferment sugars, producing carbon dioxide and alcohol
yeast species used in baking and brewing

Examples

Saccharomyces cerevisiae is essential for the fermentation process in bread making.
Brewers rely on Saccharomyces cerevisiae to convert sugars into alcohol in beer production.
Scientists often use Saccharomyces cerevisiae as a model organism in genetic research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yeast - microscopic fungus used in baking and brewing

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Saccharomyces' meaning 'sugar fungus' (Greek 'sakkhar' sugar + 'mykēs' fungus), and 'cerevisiae' meaning 'of beer' (Latin 'cerevisia' meaning beer).
