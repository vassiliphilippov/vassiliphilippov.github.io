bilobate

1. ADJ

/ ˈbaɪˌloʊ.beɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɪl.əʊ.beɪt / (BrE)
(of a leaf or other part) divided into two distinct lobes.
having two lobes (e.g., a leaf)

Examples

The ginkgo tree is known for its distinctive bilobate leaves.
Some ancient coins featured a bilobate shape unlike traditional circles.
The scientist described the flower's bilobate petals in her report.

Synset Examples

a bilobate leaf

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: trilobate - having three lobes
  • Degree Lower: unlobed - having no lobes

Etymology

From Latin bis (twice) + lobus (lobe).
