vis-à-vis

1. NOUN

/ ˌviːzəˈviː / (AmE), / ˌviːzəˈviː / (BrE)
A small sofa designed to seat two people, often arranged so the occupants face each other.
small sofa for two people

Examples

The couple sat on the vis-à-vis in the drawing room, chatting quietly.
She inherited an antique vis-à-vis from her grandmother's estate.
The hotel lobby was decorated with elegant vis-à-vis sofas for guests to relax on.

Etymology

From French 'vis-à-vis' meaning 'face to face'.

2. ADV

/ ˌviːz.ɑːˈviː / (AmE), / ˌviː.zɑːˈviː / (BrE)
Facing or situated opposite; in a position face to face with.
face to face; opposite

Examples

They sat vis-à-vis at the coffee shop, deep in conversation.
The witness found herself vis-à-vis the accused in the courtroom.
He placed the two chairs vis-à-vis so they could talk easily.

Synset Examples

they sat vis-a-vis at the table
I found myself vis-a-vis a burly policeman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opposite - positioned across from something or someone; facing

Etymology

From French, literally 'face to face'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌviːz‿əˈviː / (AmE), / ˌviːz‿əˈviː / (BrE)
a person or thing having the same function or characteristics as another; a counterpart
counterpart; person or thing with same function

Examples

The finance minister met with his German vis-à-vis to discuss the new agreement.
Each department in the company has a vis-à-vis at headquarters to coordinate with.
The senator arranged a meeting with her diplomatic vis-à-vis from France.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterpart - a person or thing corresponding to another in function or characteristics

Etymology

From French, literally meaning 'face to face'.
