oceanaut

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊʃəˌnɔt / (AmE), / ˈəʊʃəˌnɔːt / (BrE)
a person trained to live and work in underwater installations, often conducting scientific research beneath the ocean's surface
person working in underwater research installations

Examples

The team included an experienced oceanaut who supervised the underwater construction project.
As a oceanaut, she spent months living inside a submerged research habitat off the coast.
The oceanauts collected data on marine life from their base deep below the ocean's surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aquanaut - a person trained to live and work underwater for extended periods

Etymology

Formed from 'ocean' and '-naut', modeled after 'astronaut'.
