cheerer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪrər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪərə / (BrE)
a person, especially a spectator at an event, who shouts encouragement or support
one who shouts encouragement

Examples

The loudest cheerer at the game kept the team's spirits high.
Each cheerer in the crowd played a part in motivating the runners.
As a lifelong cheerer for the local football club, she never missed a match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supporter - a person who supports a team, person, or cause
  • Antonym: heckler - a person who interrupts with jeers or rude comments

Etymology

From 'cheer' (to shout in encouragement) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
