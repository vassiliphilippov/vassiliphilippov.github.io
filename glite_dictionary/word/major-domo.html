major-domo

1. NOUN

/ ˌmeɪdʒərˈdoʊmoʊ / (AmE), / ˌmeɪdʒəˈdəʊməʊ / (BrE)
the chief steward or head butler in a large household, responsible for managing household staff and affairs
chief steward or household manager

Examples

The major-domo greeted guests at the entrance and directed the servants with quiet authority.
As major-domo, he oversaw all the arrangements for the weekend gala at the estate.
She was promoted to major-domo after years of dedicated service as an assistant butler.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butler - the chief male servant in a large house

Etymology

From Spanish 'mayordomo', from Latin 'maior domūs' (greater of the house).
