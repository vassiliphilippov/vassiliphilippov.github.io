shorn

1. ADJ

/ ʃɔrn / (AmE), / ʃɔːn / (BrE)
(of hair, wool, or similar) cut or clipped off, especially as if with shears.
having hair or wool cut off

Examples

After the long winter, the sheep emerged from the barn freshly shorn and ready for spring.
The monk appeared shorn of his usual thick hair, following the temple's ritual.
He felt oddly vulnerable with his head completely shorn for the first time.

Synset Examples

picked up the baby's shorn curls from the floor
naked as a sheared sheep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheared - (of hair, wool, etc.) cut or clipped off

Etymology

Past participle of 'shear,' from Old English 'sceran' (to cut).
