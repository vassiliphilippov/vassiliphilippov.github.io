musculus abductor hallucis

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌs.kjəl.əs æbˈdʌk.tɔr həˈluː.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs æbˈdʌk.tɔː həˈluː.sɪs / (BrE)
a muscle in the foot that abducts and flexes the big toe
muscle in the foot moving the big toe
medical

Examples

The musculus abductor hallucis plays a crucial role in the movement of the big toe.
In patients with flat feet, the musculus abductor hallucis may become strained.
Surgeons carefully avoid damaging the musculus abductor hallucis during bunion correction procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abductor muscle - a muscle that draws a body part away from the median line

Etymology

From Latin: 'musculus' (muscle), 'abductor' (that which leads away), 'hallucis' (of the big toe).
