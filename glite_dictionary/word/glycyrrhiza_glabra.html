Glycyrrhiza glabra

1. NOUN

/ ɡlaɪˌsɪrəˌhaɪzə ˈɡlæbrə / (AmE), / ɡlɪˌsɪrəˌhaɪzə ˈɡleɪbrə / (BrE)
a plant native to the Mediterranean region, widely cultivated for its thick sweet roots, commonly known as licorice
the licorice plant (scientific name)

Examples

Glycyrrhiza glabra is the botanical source for natural licorice flavoring in many candies.
Traditional herbal medicine often uses the roots of Glycyrrhiza glabra for its purported health benefits.
Farmers in southern Europe cultivate Glycyrrhiza glabra for commercial extraction of its sweet compounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: licorice - sweet-tasting root from Glycyrrhiza glabra, used in flavoring and medicine

Etymology

From Greek glykyrrhiza (sweet root); glabra means 'smooth' in Latin.
