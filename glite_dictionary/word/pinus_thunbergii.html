Pinus thunbergii

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪnəs θʌnˈbɜrɡiaɪ / (AmE), / ˈpaɪnəs θʌnˈbɜːɡiːaɪ / (BrE)
a species of pine known as the Japanese black pine, native to Japan and valued for its ornamental use and resistance to salt and pollution
Japanese black pine tree species

Examples

The landscaping featured several mature Pinus thunbergii near the entrance for a striking effect.
Horticulturists recommend Pinus thunbergii for coastal gardens due to its tolerance of salt spray.
A healthy Pinus thunbergii can provide shade and beauty for decades in public parks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Japanese black pine - common name for Pinus thunbergii

Etymology

From Latin Pinus (pine) and Thunberg, after Carl Peter Thunberg, Swedish botanist.
