tracheotomy

1. NOUN

/ ˌtreɪkiˈɑtəmi / (AmE), / ˌtrækiˈɒtəmi / (BrE)
A surgical procedure in which an opening is made into the trachea to allow air to reach the lungs, typically when the usual airway is blocked.
surgical opening into the trachea
medical

Examples

After the accident, the doctor performed a tracheotomy to help the patient breathe.
Due to severe swelling of the throat, a tracheotomy was necessary.
The patient recovered well after undergoing a tracheotomy during emergency surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tracheostomy - a surgical opening into the trachea, usually with a tube inserted to maintain the airway

Etymology

From Greek trākheia (windpipe) + tome (cutting).
