citroncirus

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪtrɒnˈsaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˌsɪtrɒnˈsɪərəs / (BrE)
a hybrid plant that is a cross between Citrus sinensis (sweet orange) and Poncirus trifoliata (trifoliate orange), used in citrus breeding
a citrus hybrid plant

Examples

Researchers are studying citroncirus for its resistance to certain citrus diseases.
The citroncirus produces fruit that combines traits from both parent plants.
Many new citrus rootstocks are derived from the citroncirus hybrid.

Etymology

Blend of 'Citrus' and 'Poncirus,' the two parent plant genera.
