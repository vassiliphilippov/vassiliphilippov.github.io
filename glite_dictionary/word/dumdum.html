dumdum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌmˌdʌm / (AmE), / ˈdʌmˌdʌm / (BrE)
a soft-nosed small-arms bullet designed to expand on impact, causing a large, gaping wound
expanding soft-nosed bullet

Examples

The use of dumdum bullets in warfare is prohibited by international law.
The soldier explained that a dumdum round causes much more damage than a regular bullet.
Police discovered illegal dumdum ammunition during the investigation.

Etymology

Named after Dum Dum, a town near Kolkata (Calcutta), India, where such bullets were first manufactured.
