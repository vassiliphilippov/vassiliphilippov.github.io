seascape

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːˌskeɪp / (AmE), / ˈsiːskeɪp / (BrE)
a view or representation of the sea, especially in a painting, photograph, or from a particular place
a view or picture of the sea

Examples

The artist's latest seascape captures the wild beauty of the stormy ocean.
Through the window, we admired the breathtaking seascape stretching out to the horizon.
Her photography exhibit featured several seascapes taken along the rugged coastline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landscape - a view or representation of natural scenery on land

Etymology

Formed from 'sea' + 'scape', modeled on 'landscape'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiːˌskeɪp / (AmE), / ˈsiːskeɪp / (BrE)
a painting, drawing, or artistic representation of the sea, especially as a subject in art
painting or depiction of the sea

Examples

The gallery showcased an impressive seascape that captured the stormy Atlantic Ocean.
She prefers painting seascapes over landscapes, finding inspiration in the shifting waves.
A famous artist was known for his luminous seascape paintings, filled with vibrant blues and greens.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: landscape - a painting or artistic representation of natural inland scenery

Etymology

Formed in English from 'sea' + 'scape', modeled after 'landscape'.
