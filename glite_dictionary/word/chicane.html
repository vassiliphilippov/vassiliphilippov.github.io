chicane

1. NOUN

/ ʃɪˈkeɪn / (AmE), / ʃɪˈkæn / (BrE)
In bridge, a hand that contains no cards of the trump suit.
bridge hand with no trumps

Examples

She was dealt a chicane, making her strategy much more challenging.
Holding a chicane can be both an advantage and a disadvantage in bridge.
If you declare a chicane, the scoring can change significantly.

Etymology

From French chicane (trick, quibble), used in card games to mean a hand with no trumps.

2. NOUN

/ ʃɪˈkeɪn / (AmE), / ʃɪˈkeɪn / (BrE)
a sharp double bend or series of bends in a road or on a racetrack, often formed by movable barriers to slow vehicles down before a dangerous section
a sharp bend or barrier on a racetrack

Examples

A temporary chicane was installed on the track to improve safety before the sharp curve.
Drivers must slow down to navigate the chicane without losing control of their cars.
The addition of a chicane helped to prevent accidents at the high-speed section of the circuit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrier - something that blocks or obstructs passage

Etymology

From French *chicane* (trick, quibble), related to obstacles and evasive maneuvers.

3. NOUN

/ ʃɪˈkeɪn / (AmE), / ʃɪˈkeɪn / (BrE)
The use of trickery or deception to achieve a goal, especially to cheat someone out of money.
trickery or deception to cheat

Examples

The lawyer was accused of chicane in his handling of the case.
Unscrupulous dealers used chicane to swindle unsuspecting customers.
Such blatant chicane should not go unpunished in the business world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trickery - the practice of deception or crafty manipulation
  • Synonym: fraud - wrongful or criminal deception intended for financial or personal gain
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and fair

Etymology

From French 'chicane' (trickery, quibble), probably from Old French 'chicaner' (to quibble).

4. VERB

/ ʃɪˈkeɪn / (AmE), / ʃɪˈkeɪn / (BrE)
to raise trivial or petty objections, often in order to delay or obstruct
raise trivial objections

Examples

The lawyer tried to chicane his way out of answering the main question.
She disliked colleagues who would chicane over minor details instead of moving forward.
Rather than accept the agreement, they continued to chicane about insignificant terms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quibble - to argue or raise objections about a trivial matter

Etymology

From French *chicaner* (to quibble, wrangle), related to *chicane* (a quibble).
