teraton

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛrəˌtɑn / (AmE), / ˈtɛrəˌtɒn / (BrE)
a unit for measuring the explosive energy of a bomb or explosion, equal to one trillion (1,000,000,000,000) tons of TNT
unit for explosive energy equal to one trillion tons of TNT

Examples

The Chicxulub impact released energy estimated at over 100 teratons of TNT equivalent.
Scientists use the term teraton to describe extremely large-scale explosions or impacts.
A teraton is far beyond the destructive capacity of any human-made weapon.

Synset Examples

The approximate energy released when the Chicxulub impact caused the mass extinction 66 million years ago was estimated to be equal to 100 teratons (i.e. 100 exagrams or approximately 220.462 quadrillion pounds) of TNT.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: kiloton - unit of explosive force equal to one thousand tons of TNT
  • Degree Lower: megaton - unit of explosive force equal to one million tons of TNT

Etymology

From 'tera-' (prefix meaning trillion) and 'ton'.
