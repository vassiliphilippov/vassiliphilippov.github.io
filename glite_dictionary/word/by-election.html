by-election

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪ ɪˌlɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ ɪˌlɛkʃən / (BrE)
an election held to fill a political office that has become vacant between regularly scheduled elections
special election between regular elections
british

Examples

After the MP resigned, a by-election was called to fill the seat.
Local issues often dominate the campaigns in a by-election.
A by-election can sometimes serve as a test of public opinion between general elections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: special election - an election to fill an office that becomes vacant between regular elections, chiefly US English

Etymology

From 'by-' indicating secondary or incidental + 'election'; primarily used in British English.
