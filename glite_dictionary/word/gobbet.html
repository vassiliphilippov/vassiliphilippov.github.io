gobbet

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑːbɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡɒbɪt / (BrE)
a piece or lump of raw meat; a small chunk or portion, especially of something moist or fleshy
a piece or lump of raw meat

Examples

The butcher threw a gobbet of meat onto the block with a heavy thud.
She recoiled at the sight of a gobbet of raw flesh on her plate.
Crows picked at a gobbet of beef left on the roadside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chunk - a thick, solid piece of something

Etymology

From Old French 'gobet' (mouthful, morsel), from 'gober' (to swallow).
