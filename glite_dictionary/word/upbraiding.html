upbraiding

1. NOUN

/ ʌpˈbreɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌpˈbreɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
a severe or harsh scolding or criticism
severe scolding or criticism

Examples

The teacher's upbraiding left the students in stunned silence.
After receiving a public upbraiding from her manager, she decided to resign.
His careless mistake earned him a sharp upbraiding from the coach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reprimand - a formal expression of disapproval or criticism
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

From the verb 'upbraid', which comes from Middle English 'upbraiden', meaning to reproach or rebuke.
