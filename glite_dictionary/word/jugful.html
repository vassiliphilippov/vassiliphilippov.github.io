jugful

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌɡˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌɡ.fʊl / (BrE)
An amount that fills a jug; the quantity contained in a jug.
the quantity contained in a jug

Examples

He poured a whole jugful of lemonade for the guests.
There isn't much tea left—just about a jugful to share.
After the walk, they drank a jugful of water to quench their thirst.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cupful - the quantity contained in a cup
  • Synonym: handful - the amount that can be held in one hand
