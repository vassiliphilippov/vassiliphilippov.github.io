especially

1. ADV

/ əˈspɛʃəli / (AmE), / ɪˈspɛʃəli / (BrE)
used to single out one person, thing, or situation above all others or to indicate something applies more to one case than to others.
to a greater extent; in particular

Examples

The museum is especially popular with children during the holidays.
I love all kinds of fruit, but I especially enjoy strawberries.
He paid close attention to detail, especially when it came to accuracy.

Synset Examples

he was particularly fussy about spelling
a particularly gruesome attack
under peculiarly tragic circumstances
an especially (or specially) cautious approach to the danger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: particularly - to a higher degree than usual; especially
  • Synonym: specially - for a special purpose; in a special manner

Etymology

From Middle English, variant of 'especial', from Old French, from Latin 'specialis'.
