FAE

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛf.eɪˈi / (AmE), / ˌɛf.eɪˈi / (BrE)
an explosive weapon that disperses a cloud of fuel which is then ignited to produce a powerful blast wave
fuel-air explosive weapon
american

Examples

The military deployed an FAE to clear the heavily fortified bunker complex.
Experts warned that the blast radius of an FAE could be much greater than that of conventional bombs.
Because of its destructive power, use of the FAE was controversial among international observers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermobaric weapon - an explosive weapon using atmospheric oxygen to generate a high-temperature explosion

Etymology

Acronym for 'fuel-air explosive'.
