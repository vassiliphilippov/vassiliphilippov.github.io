heretic

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛrətɪk / (AmE), / ˈhɛrətɪk / (BrE)
A person who holds religious beliefs that are in conflict with the official teachings or dogma of a particular church, especially the Roman Catholic Church.
person with unorthodox religious beliefs

Examples

In the Middle Ages, the church sometimes executed a heretic for challenging its authority.
Galileo was once accused of being a heretic for supporting the heliocentric model.
She was branded a heretic for her unconventional religious views.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apostate - A person who renounces a religious or political belief or principle

Etymology

From Greek hairetikos (able to choose), via Latin haereticus.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛrətɪk / (AmE), / ˈhɛrətɪk / (BrE)
a person who holds beliefs or opinions that are contrary to the established or accepted doctrine, especially in religion, but also in other fields
person with unorthodox beliefs

Examples

In the scientific community, she was labeled a heretic for challenging the core assumptions of the field.
During the Middle Ages, being a heretic could result in severe punishment or even death.
He was seen as a heretic for suggesting major reforms to the traditional system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissenter - a person who disagrees with commonly or officially held beliefs
  • Antonym: orthodox - conforming to established doctrine, especially in religion

Etymology

From Old French *heretique*, from Late Latin *haereticus* (heretical, heretic), from Greek *hairetikos* (able to choose).
