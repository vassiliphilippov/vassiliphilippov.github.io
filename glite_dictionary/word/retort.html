retort

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈtɔrt / (AmE), / rɪˈtɔːt / (BrE)
a glass or metal vessel with a bulb and long neck, used to distill or decompose substances by heat, especially in chemistry and industry
vessel for distillation or decomposition by heat

Examples

The scientist heated the mixture in a retort to separate its components.
Old alchemical laboratories often contained at least one glass retort for experiments.
After hours on the flame, the substance left a residue in the retort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: still - apparatus used to distill liquids

Etymology

From Latin retorta (something bent back), from retorquere 'to bend back'.

2. VERB

/ rɪˈtɔrt / (AmE), / rɪˈtɔːt / (BrE)
to reply quickly or sharply, often in a witty or angry manner
reply quickly or sharply

Examples

When criticized for being late, she immediately retorted with a clever excuse.
He retorted that he could not have possibly made the mistake.
"Well, that's your opinion," she retorted sharply.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reply - to say or write something as a response
  • Synonym: to rejoin - to answer or reply, especially in a sharp or witty manner

Etymology

From Latin 'retortus', past participle of 'retorquere' (to twist back).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00818014-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈtɔrt / (AmE), / rɪˈtɔːt / (BrE)
a quick, witty, or sharp reply to a remark or question, often in response to criticism
quick, witty or sharp reply

Examples

Unable to resist, she offered a clever retort to his sarcastic comment.
His unexpected retort silenced everyone in the meeting.
She was famous for her quick retorts whenever anyone tried to insult her.

Synset Examples

it brought a sharp rejoinder from the teacher

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rejoinder - a quick or witty reply, especially in a legal or argumentative context
  • Synonym: comeback - a quick, witty reply to criticism or insult
  • Synonym: reply - a response to something someone has said

Etymology

From Latin 'retortus', past participle of 'retorquēre' (to twist back).
