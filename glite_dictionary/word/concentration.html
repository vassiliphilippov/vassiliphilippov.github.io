concentration

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnsənˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsənˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
the amount of a substance present in a given volume of solution, expressed as a measure of its strength
amount of substance in a solution

Examples

The concentration of salt in seawater is much higher than in fresh water.
Chemists measured the concentration of glucose by analyzing the blood sample.
If you add more solute, the concentration of the solution will increase.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dilution - the process of making a solution weaker by adding solvent
  • Synonym: density - the degree of compactness of a substance

Etymology

From Latin 'concentratio' (being concentrated), from 'concentrum' (having a common center).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnsənˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsənˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of increasing the proportion of a substance in a mixture or solution by removing other, usually diluting, components.
process of increasing a substance in a mixture

Examples

The concentration of salt in seawater can be increased by evaporating some of the water.
After concentration, the chemical solution was more potent and ready for further testing.
Industrial processes often require the concentration of raw materials to increase efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dilution - the process of making a solution less concentrated

Etymology

From Latin concentratio (bringing to a center), from con- (together) + centrum (center).
