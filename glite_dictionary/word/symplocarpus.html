Symplocarpus

1. NOUN

/ sɪm.pləˈkɑːr.pəs / (AmE), / sɪm.pləˈkɑː.pəs / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbaceous plants in the arum family, including the skunk cabbage, native to eastern North America and eastern Asia.
a genus of herbaceous plants in the arum family

Examples

The eastern North American Symplocarpus emerges early in spring, often melting the snow around it.
Botanists studied the unique flowering structure of Symplocarpus in the wetland reserve.
The odor of Symplocarpus attracts insects that help with its pollination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skunk cabbage - a common name for plants of the Symplocarpus genus, known for their foul odor

Etymology

From Greek symplōkē (intertwining) + karpos (fruit).

2. NOUN

/ sɪm.ploʊˈkɑːr.pəs / (AmE), / sɪm.pləˈkɑː.pəs / (BrE)
A genus of wetland plants in the arum family, including the skunk cabbage, native to eastern Asia and North America.
genus of wetland plants; includes skunk cabbage

Examples

The wetlands are home to a large population of Symplocarpus plants.
Many botanists study Symplocarpus for its unique pollination methods.
Research on Symplocarpus has contributed to our understanding of wetland ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skunk cabbage - common plant in the genus Symplocarpus, known for its strong smell

Etymology

From Greek 'symplōkḗ' (entwining) and 'karpós' (fruit).
