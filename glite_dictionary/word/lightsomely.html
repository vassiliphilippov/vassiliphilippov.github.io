lightsomely

1. ADV

/ ˈlaɪtsəmli / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtsəmli / (BrE)
In a light-hearted, cheerful, or carefree manner; with gaiety or buoyancy.
in a light-hearted or cheerful manner

Examples

She danced lightsomely across the polished floor, her spirits high and untroubled.
He whistled lightsomely as he walked through the sunny meadow.
The children skipped lightsomely down the lane, laughing as they played.

Synset Examples

he light-heartedly overlooks some of the basic facts of life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: light-heartedly - in a cheerful or carefree manner

Etymology

From the adjective 'lightsome' + the adverbial suffix '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ˈlaɪtsəmli / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtsəmli / (BrE)
In a manner that is quick, light, and graceful in movement.
in a light and graceful manner

Examples

She danced lightsomely across the polished floor, barely making a sound.
The cat moved lightsomely along the top of the fence, tail held high.
Despite her heavy skirt, she stepped lightsomely onto the boat.

Synset Examples

she walked lightsomely down the long staircase

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gracefully - in a smooth and elegant way
  • Antonym: clumsily - in an awkward or ungraceful way

Etymology

Formed from 'lightsome' (light in movement or spirit) + '-ly' (adverbial suffix).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
