detergence

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈtɝːdʒəns / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜːdʒəns / (BrE)
the quality or property of having the power to cleanse or remove dirt
cleansing power; power to clean

Examples

The detergence of the soap made it highly effective for removing grease from dishes.
Scientists measure the detergence of various cleaning agents to determine their effectiveness.
Excessive detergence in laundry products can sometimes cause fabrics to lose their softness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detergent - a substance used for cleaning

Etymology

From Latin 'detergere' (to wipe away).
