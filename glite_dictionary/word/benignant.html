benignant

1. ADJ

/ bəˈnɪɡnənt / (AmE), / bɪˈnɪɡnənt / (BrE)
(formal) Kind and gentle, especially toward people with less power or status; showing goodwill or graciousness.
kind and gracious, especially of those in power

Examples

The benignant ruler was loved by all his people for his fairness and generosity.
Her benignant smile reassured the nervous children as they entered the classroom.
A benignant influence in the community, he offered help to anyone in need.

Synset Examples

our benignant king

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benevolent - well-meaning and kindly
  • Antonym: malevolent - showing a wish to do evil to others

Etymology

From Latin 'benignus' (kind, generous).

2. ADJ

/ bəˈnɪɡnənt / (AmE), / bɪˈnɪɡnənt / (BrE)
Having a gentle, kind, or beneficial nature or influence.
kind and beneficial in nature

Examples

The child's benignant nature put everyone at ease in the room.
She gazed upon the garden with a benignant smile, radiating warmth and goodwill.
Many people were influenced by the leader's benignant attitude during difficult times.

Synset Examples

a benign smile
the benign sky
the benign influence of pure air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benign - gentle and kindly in nature, or mild
  • Antonym: malignant - harmful and likely to produce damage

Etymology

From Latin 'benignus' (kind, good-natured), via Late Latin 'benignantem'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00228210-a
Synset POS: a
