theorem

1. NOUN

/ ˈθi.ə.rəm / (AmE), / ˈθɪə.rəm / (BrE)
A statement or proposition that can be logically deduced from a set of axioms or previously established statements, especially in mathematics or logic.
proven statement in mathematics or logic

Examples

The Pythagorean theorem is fundamental to geometry.
Mathematicians spent years trying to prove the theorem.
Every theorem in the book is accompanied by a detailed proof.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemma - a subsidiary or intermediate theorem in an argument or proof
  • Antonym: axiom - a statement taken to be true without proof, used as a basis for reasoning

Etymology

From Greek theōrēma (something looked at, a proposition), from theōrein (to look at, speculate).

2. NOUN

/ ˈθiː.ə.rəm / (AmE), / ˈθɪə.rəm / (BrE)
A statement or idea that can be demonstrated to be true through logical or mathematical proof.
proven statement in mathematics or logic

Examples

The Pythagorean theorem is fundamental in geometry.
Her proof relied on a well-known theorem about prime numbers.
Mathematicians spend years developing new theorems and finding ways to prove them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proposition - a statement that expresses a judgment or opinion, especially in logic or mathematics
  • Degree Lower: hypothesis - a proposed explanation made on the basis of limited evidence

Etymology

From Greek *theōrēma* (speculation, proposition), from *theōrein* (to look at, consider).
