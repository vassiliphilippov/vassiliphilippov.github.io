Tamarindus

1. NOUN

/ təˈmær.ɪn.dəs / (AmE), / təˈmær.ɪn.dəs / (BrE)
a genus of tropical trees native to Africa, best known for the species Tamarindus indica, whose edible fruit is widely used in cooking
genus of tropical trees (tamarind)

Examples

The Tamarindus tree is prized for its tangy fruit used in many cuisines.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of several Tamarindus species in tropical climates.
A row of Tamarindus lined the edge of the ancient plantation, providing shade and food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tamarind - the fruit or tree of Tamarindus indica

Etymology

From Latin Tamarindus, derived from Arabic 'tamar hindi' meaning 'Indian date'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtæməˈrɪndəs / (AmE), / ˌtæməˈrɪndəs / (BrE)
a genus of tropical trees, originally from Africa, best known for the tamarind tree, which produces edible fruit pods
genus of tropical trees including the tamarind

Examples

The fruit of Tamarindus is popular in cuisines around the world.
Botanists classify the tamarind tree under the genus Tamarindus.
Tamarindus grows well in tropical and subtropical climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tamarind - the tropical tree Tamarindus indica or its edible fruit

Etymology

From scientific Latin, based on Arabic *tamr hindī* (Indian date).
