hahnium

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːniəm / (AmE), / ˈhɑːniəm / (BrE)
A former name for the chemical element with atomic number 105, now known as dubnium.
old name for dubnium (element 105)

Examples

The discovery of hahnium was initially claimed by a team of Russian scientists.
Textbooks from the 1970s sometimes refer to element 105 as hahnium.
Modern charts no longer use hahnium, having replaced it with the name dubnium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dubnium - the chemical element with atomic number 105

Etymology

Named after German chemist Otto Hahn (1879–1968).
