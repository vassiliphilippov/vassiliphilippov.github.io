disproof

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsˈpruf / (AmE), / ˌdɪsˈpruːf / (BrE)
the act or process of proving that something is false or incorrect
evidence or act of proving false

Examples

The scientist presented convincing disproof of the long-standing theory.
No disproof has yet been found for her claim.
His essay offers a clear disproof of the popular misconception.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refutation - the act of proving a statement or theory to be wrong or false
  • Antonym: proof - evidence establishing a fact or the truth of a statement

Etymology

From Late Latin ‘disprobare’ (to disprove), influenced by ‘proof’.

2. NOUN

/ dɪsˈpruf / (AmE), / dɪsˈpruːf / (BrE)
evidence or argument that shows that something is false or incorrect
evidence proving something is false

Examples

The scientist presented a compelling disproof of the old theory.
During the debate, he offered several points of disproof to counter the opposition.
No disproof of the hypothesis could be found, so it remained credible.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: proof - evidence or argument that establishes something as true

Etymology

From dis- (prefix meaning 'not', 'opposite of') + proof; modeled after proof and similar formations.
