to aggravate

1. VERB

/ ˈæɡrəˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈaɡrəveɪt / (BrE)
to make a situation or condition worse or more severe
make worse

Examples

Smoking can aggravate existing respiratory problems and lead to further complications.
Loud noises tend to aggravate my headaches.
The unpaved road aggravated the car’s suspension issues.

Synset Examples

This drug aggravates the pain

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to alleviate - to make something less severe
  • Synonym: to worsen - to become or make worse

Etymology

From Latin 'aggravare', from 'ad-' (to) + 'gravis' (heavy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00208618-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈæɡrəˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈæɡrəveɪt / (BrE)
to make someone feel annoyed or exasperated; to irritate repeatedly or intensely
irritate or exasperate someone

Examples

His constant whistling began to aggravate everyone in the office by midday.
Please stop tapping your pen, as it really aggravates me.
She was aggravated by the children's loud laughter during her phone call.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to annoy - to make someone slightly angry or irritated
  • Antonym: calm - to make someone less angry or upset

Etymology

From Latin aggravare (to make heavier), from ad- (to) + gravis (heavy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01824839-v
Synset POS: v
