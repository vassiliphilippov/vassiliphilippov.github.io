emancipator

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmænsəˌpeɪtər / (AmE), / ɪˈmansɪpeɪtə / (BrE)
a person who frees others from slavery, oppression, or restraint
person who frees others from oppression or bondage

Examples

The emancipator was celebrated for ending centuries of oppression.
Historians often refer to Abraham Lincoln as the Great Emancipator.
Every society values the role of an emancipator who brings about freedom and justice.

Synset Examples

Lincoln is known as the Great Emancipator

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liberator - a person who sets others free, especially from imprisonment or oppression

Etymology

From Latin emancipare (to set free) via emancipation + -or (agent noun suffix).
