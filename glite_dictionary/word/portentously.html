portentously

1. ADV

/ pɔrˈtɛntəsli / (AmE), / pɔːˈtɛntəsli / (BrE)
In a manner that is ominous or gives a sense that something important or calamitous is about to happen.
in an ominous or threatening manner

Examples

The sky portentously darkened as the storm approached.
He portentously paused before delivering the bad news.
The old clock portentously chimed midnight, making everyone uneasy.

Synset Examples

portentously, the engines began to roll

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ominously - in a way that suggests something bad is going to happen

Etymology

From 'portentous' (from Latin 'portentosus', from 'portentum', meaning omen), plus the adverbial suffix '-ly'.
