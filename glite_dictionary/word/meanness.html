meanness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmin.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmiːn.nɪs / (BrE)
Extreme unwillingness to spend money or share resources; stinginess.
extreme stinginess

Examples

Her meanness prevented her from even giving a small tip to the waiter.
The old landlord was notorious for his meanness and refusal to repair anything.
His meanness showed when he charged his friends for every little thing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: generosity - the quality of being kind and generous
  • Synonym: stinginess - unwillingness to spend or give; the quality of being mean with money

Etymology

From mean (in the sense of 'miserly') + -ness, Middle English, ultimately from Old English gemǣne (common, mean).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmiːn.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmiːn.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being deliberately unkind, spiteful, or cruel.
deliberate unkindness or cruelty

Examples

Her meanness towards her classmates made her unpopular at school.
People were shocked by the meanness of his remarks.
Despite his wealth, his meanness kept him from helping others.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: kindness - the quality of being kind
  • Synonym: cruelty - the quality of being cruel

Etymology

From 'mean' + '-ness', the suffix forming nouns denoting a state or quality.
