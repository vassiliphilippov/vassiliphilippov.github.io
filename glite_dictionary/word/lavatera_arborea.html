Lavatera arborea

1. NOUN

/ lævəˈtɛrə ɑrˈbɔriə / (AmE), / lavəˈtɪərə ɑːˈbɔːriə / (BrE)
a shrubby Mediterranean plant with lobed hairy leaves and clusters of showy red-purple flowers, commonly known as tree mallow
a Mediterranean shrub, tree mallow

Examples

The coastal cliffs were dotted with Lavatera arborea in full bloom.
Gardeners often plant Lavatera arborea for its attractive flowers and its ability to thrive in poor soil.
A dense thicket of Lavatera arborea provided shelter for nesting birds along the shoreline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree mallow - common name for Lavatera arborea

Etymology

Genus name from Italian botanist J.B. Lavatera; 'arborea' from Latin meaning 'tree-like'.
