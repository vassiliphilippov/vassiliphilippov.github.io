unjustness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈdʒʌstnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈdʒʌstnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being unjust; lack of fairness or justice.
lack of fairness; injustice

Examples

The unjustness of the policy led to widespread public protests.
He spoke out against the unjustness he witnessed in the legal system.
Many people have suffered due to the unjustness of the law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: injustice - lack of fairness or justice
  • Antonym: fairness - impartial and just treatment or behavior

Etymology

From unjust + -ness, with unjust from Latin injustus (not just, unfair).
