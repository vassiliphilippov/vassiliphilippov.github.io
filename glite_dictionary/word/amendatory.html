amendatory

1. ADJ

/ əˈmɛndəˌtɔri / (AmE), / əˈmɛndətəri / (BrE)
serving to amend or effect an amendment, especially to a document, law, or text.
serving to amend or effect amendment
legal

Examples

The committee proposed several amendatory changes to the bill before its final reading.
The contract was returned with amendatory clauses to address the client's concerns.
Her report included an amendatory section to update outdated information.

Synset Examples

added amendatory phrases to the text

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrective - intended to correct or amend
  • Synonym: supplementary - serving to supplement or add to

Etymology

From Latin 'emendatus' (corrected, improved) + '-ory'.
