curb

1. NOUN

/ kɝb / (AmE), / kɜːb / (BrE)
a type of horse's bit with a chain or strap attached, used to help control a horse
a horse's bit with an attached chain or strap

Examples

The rider adjusted the curb before the competition began.
She purchased a new curb for her horse's bridle.
Some horses respond better to a curb than to a simple snaffle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bit - a metal mouthpiece for controlling a horse

2. NOUN

/ kɝb / (AmE), / kɜːb / (BrE)
the act of restraining or limiting something, especially to prevent excess or uncontrolled behavior.
the act of restraining or limiting

Examples

The new law places a curb on excessive executive bonuses.
A strict diet can act as a curb to unhealthy eating habits.
The government has introduced measures to put a curb on pollution.

Synset Examples

his common sense is a bridle to his quick temper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restraint - the action of keeping something under control
  • Synonym: limit - a restriction or boundary

Etymology

From Old French *korbe* (strap used to restrain), from Latin *curbus* (curved).
