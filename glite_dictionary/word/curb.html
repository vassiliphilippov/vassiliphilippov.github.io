Curb

1. PROPN

/ kɝb / (AmE), / kɜːb / (BrE)
A former stock exchange in New York, originally called the New York Curb Exchange, now known as NYSE American.
former New York stock exchange

Examples

The Curb was known for trading in smaller and more speculative securities.
Many brokers began their careers on the Curb before moving to the main board.
In 1953, the Curb was officially renamed the American Stock Exchange.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: NYSE American - current name of the former New York Curb Exchange

Etymology

From transactions originally conducted on the street curb outside the stock exchange.
