Masticophis flagellum

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæstɪˈkoʊfɪs fləˈdʒɛləm / (AmE), / ˌmæstɪˈkəʊfɪs fləˈdʒɛləm / (BrE)
A species of whipsnake found in the southern United States and Mexico, known for its tail which resembles a braided whip.
species of North American whipsnake

Examples

The Masticophis flagellum moves swiftly across sandy terrain in search of prey.
Herpetologists were excited to spot a rare Masticophis flagellum during their field study.
Due to its slender body and whip-like tail, the Masticophis flagellum is often mistaken for other snakes.

Etymology

From Greek 'mastix' (whip) and 'ophis' (snake) with Latin 'flagellum' (whip).
