stockiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑːki.nɪs / (AmE), / ˈstɒk.i.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being compact, sturdy, and relatively thick in build.
quality of being stocky in build

Examples

His stockiness comes from years of playing rugby and lifting weights.
Despite her stockiness, she moved with surprising speed and agility.
The puppy's stockiness made it look both strong and adorable.

Synset Examples

Make no mistake, his stockiness belies a strenuous daily exercise routine and his vision without the plain glass lenses is excellent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stocky - (of a person) broad, sturdy, and compact in build

Etymology

From 'stocky', itself from 'stock' (trunk or stem), with suffix '-iness'.
