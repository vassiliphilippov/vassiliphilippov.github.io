magnetic

1. ADJ

/ mægˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
Having the properties of a magnet, especially capable of attracting iron or steel.
having properties of a magnet

Examples

The electrician used a magnetic screwdriver to pick up the tiny screws.
A magnetic field surrounds every magnet and attracts certain metals.
Some toys include magnetic components that allow pieces to stick together.

Synset Examples

the hard disk is covered with a thin coat of magnetic material

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonmagnetic - not having the properties of a magnet

Etymology

From Greek 'magnetikos', relating to the mineral magnetite.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01472592-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ mægˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / mæɡˈnetɪk / (BrE)
Capable of being magnetized or attracted by a magnet.
capable of being magnetized

Examples

This metal is magnetic and can be attracted by a simple bar magnet.
Unlike copper, iron is magnetic and will stick to the refrigerator door.
The experiment requires a magnetic material to demonstrate the effect.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonmagnetic - not capable of being magnetized

Etymology

From Greek 'magnetikos', relating to 'magnetis' (Magnesian stone).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01473728-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ mægˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to, or determined by, the Earth's magnetic field, especially as used in navigation.
relating to Earth's magnetic field

Examples

The pilot adjusted his course to follow the magnetic heading indicated by his compass.
The magnetic bearing of the island differs from its true bearing due to local anomalies.
Their map showed both the true north and the magnetic north for accurate navigation.

Synset Examples

magnetic north
the needle of a magnetic compass points to the magnetic north pole

Related Terms

  • Antonym: true - based on actual geographic direction, not affected by magnetic field

Etymology

From Latin 'magneticus', from 'magnes' (magnet).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01473213-a
Synset POS: a

/ mæɡˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to, produced by, or exhibiting magnetism.
relating to magnetism

Examples

The compass needle always points north because it is magnetic.
Some materials, like iron and nickel, are naturally magnetic.
The doctor used a magnetic scanner to look inside the patient's body.

Synset Examples

magnetic forces

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonmagnetic - not affected by or exhibiting magnetism

Etymology

From Latin 'magneticus', from 'magnes' meaning 'magnet'.

5. ADJ

/ mæɡˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
(of a person or their qualities) having a powerful, irresistible, or fascinating attraction.
fascinatingly attractive (figurative sense)

Examples

The speaker's magnetic charm captivated the entire audience.
She has such a magnetic presence that everyone in the room takes notice.
His magnetic personality helped him gain many friends quickly.

Synset Examples

a charismatic leader
a magnetic personality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charismatic - having a compelling charm or appeal
  • Synonym: alluring - powerfully and mysteriously attractive or fascinating

Etymology

From Latin 'magnes', from Greek 'magnēs (lithos)' meaning 'Magnesian stone' (magnetite).
