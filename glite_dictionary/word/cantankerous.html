cantankerous

1. ADJ

/ kænˈtæŋkərəs / (AmE), / kænˈtæŋkərəs / (BrE)
bad-tempered, argumentative, and uncooperative.
bad-tempered and uncooperative

Examples

The cantankerous neighbor yelled at anyone who dared to walk on his lawn.
Despite his cantankerous attitude, everyone respected his expertise.
She became even more cantankerous in her old age, arguing with everyone around her.

Synset Examples

a cantankerous and venomous-tongued old lady

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritable - easily annoyed or angered
  • Antonym: agreeable - pleasant and easy to get along with

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'conteker' (contention) of uncertain origin.

2. ADJ

/ kænˈtæŋkərəs / (AmE), / kænˈtaŋkərəs / (BrE)
unwilling to cooperate and often arguing or complaining; bad-tempered and difficult to deal with
bad-tempered and uncooperative

Examples

The cantankerous old man argued with his neighbors over every little thing.
Despite her cantankerous reputation, she was deeply loved by her family.
The committee became cantankerous when asked to make a difficult decision.

Synset Examples

unions … have never been as bloody-minded about demarcation as the shipbuilders

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstinate - stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or chosen course of action
  • Antonym: agreeable - willing to agree or cooperate, pleasant in manner

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'conteker' (contention, quarrel), influenced by obsolete 'cankerous.'
