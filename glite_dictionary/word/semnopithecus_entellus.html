Semnopithecus entellus

1. NOUN

/ sɛmˌnɒpɪˈθiːkəs ɛnˈtɛləs / (AmE), / sɛmˌnɒpɪˈθiːkəs ɛnˈtɛləs / (BrE)
a species of langur native to southern Asia, especially India, where it is considered sacred
a langur species of southern Asia

Examples

The Semnopithecus entellus is often seen near temples in northern India, where it is protected and revered.
Researchers observed a troop of Semnopithecus entellus displaying complex social behavior in the wild.
The gray fur and long tail distinguish the Semnopithecus entellus from other primates in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hanuman langur - common name for Semnopithecus entellus

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name 'Semnopithecus' from Greek 'semnos' (revered, sacred) + 'pithekos' (ape); species epithet 'entellus' of uncertain origin.
