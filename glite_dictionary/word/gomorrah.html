Gomorrah

1. PROPN

/ ɡəˈmɔɹə / (AmE), / ɡəˈmɒɹə / (BrE)
(In the Bible) an ancient city near the Dead Sea, traditionally destroyed by God for the wickedness of its inhabitants.
biblical city destroyed for wickedness

Examples

Biblical accounts describe Gomorrah as a city punished for its depravity.
Some moralists warn that modern cities risk becoming another Gomorrah.
Archaeologists have debated the historical existence of Gomorrah for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sodom - Ancient city destroyed alongside Gomorrah for vice, in the Bible

Etymology

From Hebrew ‘ʿĂmōrāh’, as found in the Old Testament.
