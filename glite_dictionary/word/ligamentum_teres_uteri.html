ligamentum teres uteri

1. NOUN

/ lɪɡəˈmɛntəm ˈtɪriːz juːˈtɪraɪ / (AmE), / lɪɡəˈmɛntəm ˈtɛːriːz juːˈtɪərɪ / (BrE)
a round ligament attached to the uterus on each side, running in front of and below the opening of the Fallopian tube and passing through the inguinal canal to the labia majora
round ligament of the uterus
medical

Examples

During the surgery, the ligamentum teres uteri was clearly identified and preserved for structural support.
Pain in the groin during pregnancy can sometimes be caused by the stretching of the ligamentum teres uteri.
Medical students must learn the anatomy of the ligamentum teres uteri when studying the female reproductive system.

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'round ligament of the uterus'.
