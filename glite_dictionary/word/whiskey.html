whiskey

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪski / (AmE), / ˈwɪski / (BrE)
A distilled alcoholic drink made from fermented grain mash, typically aged in wooden casks.
type of distilled alcoholic drink

Examples

He poured himself a glass of whiskey after a long day at work.
The bar offered a wide selection of whiskey from around the world.
She prefers her whiskey neat, without any ice or water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bourbon - a type of American whiskey made primarily from corn
  • Synonym: scotch - whiskey made in Scotland from malted barley
  • Antonym: vodka - a clear distilled alcoholic beverage, typically made from grain or potatoes

Etymology

From Irish *uisce beatha* and Scottish Gaelic *uisge beatha* (water of life), via English spelling alterations.
