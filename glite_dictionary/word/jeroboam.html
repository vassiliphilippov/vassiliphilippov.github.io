Jeroboam

1. PROPN

/ dʒɛrəˈboʊəm / (AmE), / dʒɛrəˈbəʊəm / (BrE)
(in the Bible) the first king of the northern kingdom of Israel, traditionally regarded as having led the Israelites into sin; reigned in the 10th century BC.
first king of northern kingdom of Israel in the Bible

Examples

Jeroboam became king after the division of the united Kingdom of Israel following Solomon's reign.
The biblical account describes how Jeroboam introduced new religious practices in Israel.
Many historians debate the true motives behind Jeroboam's rebellion against the House of David.

Etymology

From Hebrew Yarov‘am, meaning 'the people contend' or 'he increases the people'.
