monument

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːn.jə.mənt / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.jʊ.mənt / (BrE)
a site of historical, cultural, or national importance that is officially marked and protected as public property
officially protected historic site

Examples

The area was declared a monument to ensure its preservation for future generations.
Tourists flocked to the national monument to learn about the region's rich history.
The government designated the old battlefield as a historical monument in 1986.

Etymology

From Latin monumentum (memorial, monument), from monere (to remind).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːnjəmənt / (AmE), / ˈmɒnjʊmənt / (BrE)
A structure, often large and enduring, built to commemorate a person or an event.
commemorative structure

Examples

The city built a monument in honor of the fallen soldiers.
Tourists from around the world visit the monument every year to learn about its history.
The ancient monument stood as a reminder of the empire's achievements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memorial - something, especially a structure, established to remind people of a person or event

Etymology

From Latin 'monumentum', from 'monere' (to remind).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːn.jə.mənt / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.jə.mənt / (BrE)
a large structure, especially a burial vault, built to commemorate and mark the burial place of a famous person
burial vault or commemorative tomb

Examples

The king's body was interred in a grand monument built of marble and gold.
Tourists visit the monument where the poet was laid to rest in the 19th century.
The ancient monument stands as a tribute to the ruler's legacy and his burial site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mausoleum - a large and impressive burial chamber
  • Synonym: tomb - a place where someone is buried, typically under a stone or in a vault

Etymology

From Latin 'monumentum' (memorial, reminder).
