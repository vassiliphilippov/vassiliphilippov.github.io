nonindulgence

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑn.ɪnˈdʌl.dʒəns / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.ɪnˈdʌl.dʒəns / (BrE)
the quality or practice of deliberately avoiding indulgence, especially in pleasures or luxuries; self-restraint
self-restraint; avoiding indulgence

Examples

His nonindulgence in rich foods helped him maintain his health.
Many spiritual leaders advocate nonindulgence as a path to discipline and clarity.
Her nonindulgence was admired by those who struggled with temptation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abstinence - the act or practice of refraining from something, especially alcohol or sex
  • Antonym: indulgence - the practice of allowing oneself to enjoy pleasures; lack of restraint

Etymology

From non- (not) + indulgence (from Latin indulgentia, leniency).
