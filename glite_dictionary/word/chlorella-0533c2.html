Chlorella

1. NOUN

/ kləˈrɛlə / (AmE), / kləˈrɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of nonmotile, unicellular green algae, sometimes cultivated as a high-protein food source and for its vitamins.
genus of unicellular green algae

Examples

Scientists are studying Chlorella as a sustainable food source.
The pond water turned green due to the rapid growth of Chlorella.
Supplements made from Chlorella are popular for their nutritional value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: algae - simple non-flowering aquatic plants, including seaweeds and many single-celled forms

Etymology

From Greek 'chloros' (green) and the diminutive suffix '-ella'.
