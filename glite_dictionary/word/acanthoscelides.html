Acanthoscelides

1. NOUN

/ əˌkænθoʊsˈsiːlaɪdiːz / (AmE), / əˌkanθɒsˈsiːliːdiːz / (BrE)
a genus of beetles in the family Bruchidae, commonly known as bean weevils, whose larvae typically infest and feed on legume seeds.
genus of bean weevils

Examples

The larvae of Acanthoscelides species can cause serious damage to stored beans.
Scientists identified several Acanthoscelides beetles in the crop samples.
Research on Acanthoscelides focuses on their impact on agricultural yields.

Etymology

From Greek 'akantha' (thorn) and 'scelis' (leg).

2. NOUN

/ əˌkænθəˈsɛlɪˌdiːz / (AmE), / əˌkænθəˈsɛlɪˌdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of beetles in the family Bruchidae, commonly known as bean weevils, which are pests of legumes.
genus of bean weevils

Examples

Certain species within the Acanthoscelides genus are notorious for infesting stored beans.
The study focused on the life cycle of Acanthoscelides and its impact on agriculture.
Several types of Acanthoscelides beetles are found in both wild and cultivated legumes.

Etymology

From Greek 'akantha' (thorn) + 'skelos' (leg), referring to the spiny legs of these beetles.
