Pedaliaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛd.ə.liˈeɪ.si.i / (AmE), / ˌpɛd.əliːˈeɪ.sɪ.iː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants in the order Lamiales, which includes the sesame plant and related species.
a family of flowering plants

Examples

The Pedaliaceae family includes economically important crops like sesame.
Botanists have identified several rare species within the Pedaliaceae.
Adaptations in the Pedaliaceae make them well-suited to arid environments.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on the type genus Pedalium.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɛdəliˈeɪsiˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˌpɛdəliˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, known as the sesame family, comprising herbs and shrubs belonging to the order Lamiales, including the sesame plant and related species
family of flowering plants (sesame family)
medical

Examples

The Pedaliaceae family includes economically important plants such as sesame.
Research on the Pedaliaceae has revealed unique adaptations to arid environments.
Members of the Pedaliaceae can be found across Africa and parts of Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sesame - an annual plant of the genus Sesamum, grown for its seeds; member of Pedaliaceae

Etymology

From the genus name Pedalium, combined with the standard botanical family suffix -aceae.
