stalemate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪlˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsteɪlˌmeɪt / (BrE)
A situation in chess where a player has no legal moves and their king is not in check, resulting in a drawn game.
draw in chess with no legal moves

Examples

After fifty moves, the game ended in a stalemate, with neither player emerging victorious.
The possibility of stalemate forces chess players to think carefully before making their final moves.
Even though he had more pieces, a clever defense led to a stalemate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: checkmate - in chess, a position in which a player's king is directly attacked and cannot escape capture, ending the game

Etymology

From obsolete 'stalemate' (noun), from Anglo-French 'estale' (standstill) + 'mate' (as in checkmate), ultimately from Arabic 'māt' (he is dead).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪlˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsteɪlmeɪt / (BrE)
a situation in which neither side can make progress or win, resulting in a deadlock
a deadlock or impasse

Examples

The peace talks ended in a stalemate after both sides refused to compromise.
Negotiations reached a stalemate, leaving the two companies unable to move forward.
The match became a stalemate with neither team able to score a decisive goal.

Synset Examples

reached an impasse on the negotiations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impasse - a deadlock or situation where no progress is possible
  • Synonym: deadlock - a situation in which no agreement can be reached
  • Antonym: breakthrough - an important development that removes an obstacle to progress

Etymology

Originally a chess term from Old French 'estale' (standstill) and English 'mate' (checkmate).

3. VERB

/ ˈsteɪlˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsteɪlmeɪt / (BrE)
to bring (a contest, process, or situation) to a point where no further progress can be made by any party; to cause a stalemate.
bring to a deadlock or impasse

Examples

The negotiations have stalled, effectively stalemating any further progress on the agreement.
If neither team scores soon, this game might completely stalemate.
The companies were stalemated by their inability to compromise on key issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deadlock - a situation in which progress is impossible; a state of inaction or neutralization
  • Antonym: breakthrough - an act of overcoming an obstacle or deadlock

Etymology

From the noun 'stalemate', originally from chess, from Middle English stale (stalemate, exclamation) + mate (checkmate).

Synset POS: v
