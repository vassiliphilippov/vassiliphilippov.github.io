monger

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋɡə / (BrE)
a person who buys and maintains a stock of goods for sale; a dealer or trader, especially in a particular commodity.
person who deals in a particular type of goods

Examples

He was a fish monger who supplied restaurants across the city.
The cheese monger offered samples of her finest aged varieties.
As a longtime book monger, she always knew where to find rare editions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dealer - a person whose business is buying and selling a particular product
  • Synonym: trader - a person who buys and sells goods, currency, or stocks

Etymology

From Old English mangere (merchant), from mangian (to trade).

2. VERB

/ ˈmʌŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋɡə / (BrE)
(archaic) To sell or offer for sale (goods), typically by going from place to place.
to sell goods from place to place (archaic)
archaic

Examples

In the 17th century, merchants would monger their wares at the bustling markets.
Itinerant salesmen once mongered commodities from village to village.
He spent his youth mongering fish along the coastal roads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to peddle - to sell goods from place to place

Etymology

From Middle English mongeren, from monger (noun, meaning 'dealer, trader').
