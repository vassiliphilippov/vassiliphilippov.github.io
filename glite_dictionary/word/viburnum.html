Viburnum

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈbɜrnəm / (AmE), / vɪˈbɜːnəm / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous or evergreen shrubs or small trees, often grown for their attractive flowers and berries.
genus of flowering shrubs or small trees

Examples

The Viburnum in our garden blooms with delicate white flowers every spring.
Gardeners appreciate Viburnum for its attractive foliage and clusters of red berries.
We planted a Viburnum hedge to provide privacy and seasonal color.

Etymology

From Latin 'viburnum', originally referring to the wayfaring tree or similar shrubs.

2. NOUN

/ vaɪˈbɝːnəm / (AmE), / vaɪˈbɜːnəm / (BrE)
a genus of deciduous or evergreen shrubs and small trees, including species such as the arrow-wood and wayfaring tree, widely grown for their ornamental flowers and berries
genus of shrubs or small trees (ornamental plants)

Examples

The Viburnum shrub produces clusters of white flowers in early spring.
Gardeners often choose Viburnum for its attractive foliage and berries.
Several species of Viburnum are popular in landscape designs across Europe and North America.

Etymology

From Latin 'viburnum', meaning wayfaring tree or guelder rose.
