Collinsia verna

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪnziə ˈvɜːrnə / (AmE), / kɒˈlɪnziə ˈvɜːnə / (BrE)
A plant native to the eastern United States, characterized by whorls of blue-and-white flowers; commonly known as blue-eyed Mary.
a blue-and-white-flowered plant species

Examples

In the spring, the Collinsia verna lights up the forest floor with its vibrant blue-and-white flowers.
Gardeners appreciate Collinsia verna for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
During the field survey, researchers recorded a healthy population of Collinsia verna in the region.

Etymology

Genus named after British botanist Zaccheus Collins; 'verna' from Latin for 'spring'.
