to elicit

1. VERB

/ ɪˈlɪsɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪsɪt / (BrE)
to succeed in getting a reaction, answer, or feeling from someone, often through questioning or prompting
draw out a response or reaction

Examples

The teacher's question managed to elicit a thoughtful response from the quiet student.
His speech was designed to elicit emotion from the audience.
The experiment elicited strong reactions from participants.

Synset Examples

arouse pity
raise a smile
evoke sympathy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evoke - to bring a feeling, memory, or image into the mind
  • Antonym: to suppress - to prevent the development, action, or expression of something (especially an emotion)

Etymology

From Latin elicere (to draw out), from e- (out) + lacere (to entice).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01763170-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪˈlɪsɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪsɪt / (BrE)
to obtain or draw out information, a response, or a result, often by reasoning or questioning
obtain or draw out something by reasoning or questioning

Examples

The investigator tried to elicit details about the incident from the witnesses.
Her questions were designed to elicit honest answers from the participants.
Careful reasoning can sometimes elicit the correct solution to a complex problem.

Synset Examples

elicit a solution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extract - to remove or take out, often with force or effort
  • Synonym: to evoke - to bring or call forth a feeling, response, or memory
  • Antonym: to suppress - to prevent from being expressed or known

Etymology

From Latin 'elicitus', past participle of 'elicere' (to draw out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00730788-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪˈlɪsɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪsɪt / (BrE)
to infer or deduce (a principle, fact, or meaning) from available information; to construe or extract by reasoning
to deduce or infer by reasoning

Examples

The critic attempted to elicit a deeper meaning from the writer's ambiguous passage.
From the incomplete data, the scientist managed to elicit the law governing the phenomenon.
By analyzing the teacher's questions, we can elicit her intended lesson objective.

Synset Examples

We drew out some interesting linguistic data from the native informant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deduce - to reach a conclusion by reasoning
  • Synonym: to infer - to deduce or conclude from evidence and reasoning
  • Synonym: to construe - to interpret the meaning of something

Etymology

From Latin 'elicitus', past participle of 'elicere' (to draw out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01634074-v
Synset POS: v
