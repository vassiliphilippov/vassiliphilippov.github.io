teacake

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiːˌkeɪk / (AmE), / ˈtiː.keɪk / (BrE)
A small, flat, semisweet cake, cookie, or biscuit, typically served with tea.
small cake or biscuit served with tea

Examples

She enjoyed a warm teacake with her afternoon tea.
Would you like a teacake or a scone with your coffee?
The bakery makes delicious teacakes filled with currants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biscuit - a small baked unleavened cake, typically crisp, sometimes savory or sweet
  • Synonym: cookie - a sweet baked food, typically small, flat, and round

Etymology

From 'tea' + 'cake', reflecting its traditional serving with tea.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtiˌkeɪk / (AmE), / ˈtiːkeɪk / (BrE)
a small, sweet cake or bun, often served with tea, and sometimes containing dried fruit
small sweet cake or bun for tea

Examples

She placed a freshly baked teacake on the plate beside the teapot.
In Britain, a teacake often contains currants or other dried fruit.
Would you like a teacake with your afternoon tea?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biscuit - a small baked unleavened cake, typically crisp, found in British English
  • Synonym: scone - a small cake of soft, sweet dough, often served with tea

Etymology

From 'tea' + 'cake,' referring to a cake eaten with tea.
