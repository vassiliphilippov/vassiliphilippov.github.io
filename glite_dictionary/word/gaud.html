gaud

1. NOUN

/ ɡɔd / (AmE), / ɡɔːd / (BrE)
A piece of cheap and showy jewelry or ornament, especially worn on clothing.
cheap showy jewelry or ornament

Examples

She decorated her dress with a bright gaud at the collar.
The children were drawn to the shiny gauds displayed at the market.
He dismissed the trinket as nothing more than a worthless gaud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bauble - a small, showy trinket or decoration

Etymology

From Old French *gaudir* (to rejoice, make merry), from Latin *gaudere* (to rejoice).
