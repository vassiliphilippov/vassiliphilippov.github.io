luncheon

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌntʃən / (AmE), / ˈlʌntʃən / (BrE)
a formal or special meal eaten in the middle of the day, typically earlier than a dinner
formal midday meal

Examples

The company hosted a luncheon to celebrate the achievements of its employees.
She attended a charity luncheon at the city hall.
After the morning conference, a light luncheon was served to the guests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lunch - a meal eaten in the middle of the day

Etymology

From Middle English 'lunch' with the suffix '-eon', influenced by 'dinner' and 'supper'.
