A1

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ ˈwʌn / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˈwʌn / (BrE)
A standard paper size measuring 594 by 841 millimeters, commonly used internationally.
international paper size (594 × 841 mm)

Examples

The architect printed the building plans on A1 sheets for better readability.
Posters are often produced on A1 paper to ensure high visibility.
We need an A1 drawing for the client presentation tomorrow.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: A0 - paper size 841 x 1189 mm, one size larger than A1
  • Degree Lower: A2 - paper size 420 x 594 mm, one size smaller than A1

Etymology

From the ISO 216 standard, part of the A series of paper sizes adopted internationally.
