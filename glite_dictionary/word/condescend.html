to condescend

1. VERB

/ ˌkɑːndəˈsend / (AmE), / ˌkɒndɪˈsend / (BrE)
to do something that one considers to be below one's dignity, often with an attitude of superiority
act as if lowering oneself; show superiority

Examples

He refused to condescend to arguing with someone so rude.
She would not condescend to do menial chores around the house.
Do you really expect me to condescend to such trivial tasks?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to patronize - to treat with apparent kindness that betrays a feeling of superiority
  • Degree Lower: humble - to lower in dignity or importance

Etymology

From Latin 'condescendere', from 'con-' (together) + 'descendere' (to descend).

2. VERB

/ ˌkɑn.dɪˈsɛnd / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.dɪˈsɛnd / (BrE)
to lower oneself to do something considered beneath one's dignity; to debase oneself by behaving in a way that is unworthy or undignified
lower oneself, behave in an unworthy way

Examples

He refused to condescend to lying, even when pressured by his colleagues.
She would not condescend to cheating, no matter how tempting the offer was.
I won't condescend to argue with someone who deliberately twists my words.

Synset Examples

I won't stoop to reading other people's mail

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stoop - to lower oneself to do something considered beneath oneself

Etymology

From Latin 'condescendere', meaning 'to stoop together, to come down' (com- 'together' + descendere 'to descend').

3. VERB

/ ˌkɑːn.dəˈsɛnd / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.dɪˈsend / (BrE)
to behave as if one is superior to others and show patronizing attitude
act in a patronizing or superior manner

Examples

Please do not condescend to me as if I do not understand basic math.
She tends to condescend when explaining things to her coworkers, which annoys them.
It is offensive when people condescend to others based on their intelligence or experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to patronize - to treat with apparent kindness that betrays a feeling of superiority
  • Antonym: humble - to show or feel modesty or low importance

Etymology

From Latin 'condescendere', from 'con-' (together) + 'descendere' (to descend).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˌkɑːndɪˈsend / (AmE), / ˌkɒndɪˈsend / (BrE)
to behave towards someone as though you are more important or intelligent than they are; to treat someone with patronizing superiority.
to act with patronizing superiority

Examples

She refused to condescend to her colleagues, always treating everyone equally.
It irritated him when people condescended to explain basic concepts to him.
Although she was the expert, she never condescends to her students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to patronize - to treat in a way that is apparently kind or helpful but betrays a feeling of superiority
  • Synonym: to deign - to do something that one considers to be beneath one's dignity
  • Antonym: respect - to treat someone with consideration and regard, without superiority

Etymology

From Latin 'condescendere', from 'com-' (together) + 'descendere' (to descend).
