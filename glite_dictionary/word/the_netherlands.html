The Netherlands

1. PROPN

/ ðə ˈnɛðɚˌlændz / (AmE), / ðə ˈnɛðəˌlændz / (BrE)
A country in western Europe, known officially as the Kingdom of the Netherlands, with much of its land lying below sea level.
country in western Europe

Examples

Many tourists travel to The Netherlands to see the famous tulip fields and windmills.
Amsterdam, the capital of The Netherlands, is renowned for its historic canals and vibrant culture.
Half of The Netherlands lies below sea level, making its water management systems essential.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Holland - an informal name sometimes used for the Netherlands, though it technically refers to two provinces

Etymology

From Dutch 'Nederland' meaning 'low land' (from 'neer' = lower, 'land' = land).
