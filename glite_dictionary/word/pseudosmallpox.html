pseudosmallpox

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːdoʊˈsmɔlˌpɑks / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəʊˈsmɔːlpɒks / (BrE)
A mild form of smallpox caused by a less virulent strain of the virus.
mild form of smallpox
medical

Examples

The outbreak was diagnosed as pseudosmallpox, which reassured health officials due to its mild symptoms.
After exposure, several patients developed pseudosmallpox rather than the more dangerous smallpox.
Research into pseudosmallpox has increased, given its significance for public health.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: smallpox - a serious infectious disease caused by the variola virus

Etymology

From pseudo- (false, resembling) + smallpox.
