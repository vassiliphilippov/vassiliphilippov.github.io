recklessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛkləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɛkləsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of acting without regard to danger or possible consequences
lack of regard for danger; rashness

Examples

His recklessness behind the wheel made everyone nervous.
She admired his courage but was worried by his recklessness.
The accident was caused by sheer recklessness on the part of the driver.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rashness - the quality of acting or deciding quickly and without careful thought
  • Antonym: caution - the trait of being careful to avoid danger or risks

Etymology

From Old English 'receleas' (careless) + '-ness'.
