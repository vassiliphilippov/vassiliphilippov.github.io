impiety

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpaɪəti / (AmE), / ɪmˈpaɪəti / (BrE)
lack of respect for God or for sacred things; irreverence or ungodliness.
lack of respect for God or religion

Examples

His blatant impiety shocked the deeply religious community.
The ruler was accused of impiety after he destroyed several temples.
Literature from that era often viewed impiety as a serious moral failing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impiousness - the quality of being impious; irreverence toward God
  • Antonym: piety - devoutness or reverence for God; opposite of impiety

Etymology

From Latin 'impiētās', from 'impius' (impious).
