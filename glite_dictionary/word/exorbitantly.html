exorbitantly

1. ADV

/ ɪɡˈzɔːrbɪtəntli / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɔːbɪtəntli / (BrE)
to a degree that is much too high or excessive; unreasonably
to an excessive or unreasonable degree

Examples

The restaurant was criticized for charging exorbitantly for simple dishes.
Their new car turned out to be exorbitantly expensive compared to similar models.
Rent in the downtown area has risen exorbitantly over the past few years.

Synset Examples

prices are exorbitantly high in the capital

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excessively - to a very great degree; more than is reasonable
  • Antonym: moderately - to a moderate or reasonable degree

Etymology

From 'exorbitant', from Latin 'exorbitans', present participle of 'exorbitare' (to go out of the track).
