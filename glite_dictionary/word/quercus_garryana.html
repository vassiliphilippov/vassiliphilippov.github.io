Quercus garryana

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs ˌɡæriˈænə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs ˌɡæriˈɑːnə / (BrE)
A small deciduous oak tree native to western North America, known for its crooked branches and pale grey bark.
oak tree native to western North America

Examples

The Quercus garryana provides important habitat for many native birds.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Quercus garryana in different regions.
Several Quercus garryana trees were planted in the reserve to restore local ecosystems.

Etymology

From Latin 'Quercus' (oak); 'garryana' named after Nicholas Garry, a director of the Hudson's Bay Company.
