duplex

1. ADJ

/ ˈduːˌplɛks / (AmE), / ˈdjuːˌplɛks / (BrE)
(of a device or process) having two parts, especially for simultaneous or two-way operation.
having two parts (technical)

Examples

The device supports duplex communication, allowing data to flow in both directions at the same time.
A duplex system is commonly used in modern telephones.
Engineers chose a duplex configuration to ensure reliability and efficiency.

Synset Examples

a duplex transaction

Related Terms

  • Antonym: simplex - having only one part or function, especially in communication systems

Etymology

From Latin 'duplex' (double, twofold): 'duo' (two) + 'plicare' (to fold).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02226037-s
Synset POS: s

2. VERB

/ ˈduːˌplɛks / (AmE), / ˈdjuːˌplɛks / (BrE)
to convert (a building) into a duplex, typically dividing it into two separate living units.
convert into a duplex building

Examples

The developer plans to duplex several older homes to create more rental units.
After moving out, the owner decided to duplex the house for extra income.
They hired a contractor to duplex the property, turning it into two separate apartments.

Etymology

From Latin 'duplex' (double).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00587234-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈduː.plɛks / (AmE), / ˈdjuː.pleks / (BrE)
An apartment or house with rooms on two floors connected by an internal staircase.
apartment on two floors

Examples

After moving to the city, they rented a duplex in a historic neighborhood.
Their new duplex has spacious living areas upstairs and bedrooms downstairs.
She enjoys living in a duplex because it feels more like a house than a single-level apartment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maisonette - a small house, especially one that is part of a larger building, typically with two floors

Etymology

From Latin 'duplex' meaning 'double'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03261768-n
Synset POS: n

5. ADJ

/ ˈduːˌplɛks, ˈdjuːˌplɛks / (AmE), / ˈdjuːplɛks / (BrE)
(of a communications system) allowing transmission in both directions simultaneously.
simultaneous two-way communication

Examples

The radio uses a duplex system to allow both parties to talk at once.
Modern telephones support duplex communication, making conversations more natural.
Fiber optic networks often operate in full duplex mode for faster data transfer.

Synset Examples

duplex system
duplex telephony

Related Terms

  • Antonym: simplex - allowing communication in only one direction at a time

Etymology

From Latin 'duplex' meaning 'double'.
