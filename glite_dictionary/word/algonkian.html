Algonkian

1. NOUN

/ ælˈɡɑŋkiən / (AmE), / ælˈɡɒŋkiən / (BrE)
the Algonquian language spoken by the Algonkian people, indigenous to the northeastern United States and adjacent Canada
language of the Algonkian people

Examples

Many words in English are borrowed from the Algonkian language, such as 'moose' and 'papoose'.
Scholars study the grammar and vocabulary of Algonkian to understand the culture of its speakers.
The Algonkian language once flourished throughout the Great Lakes and Atlantic regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian - a large group of indigenous North American languages related to Algonkian

Etymology

From the name 'Algonkin', used by French explorers, possibly from a Maliseet word.

2. ADJ

/ ælˈɡɑŋkiən / (AmE), / ælˈɡɒŋkɪən / (BrE)
relating to the Algonquian peoples, tribes, or their languages of North America
relating to Algonquian peoples or languages

Examples

The museum displayed artifacts from several Algonkian tribes.
Linguists have studied the structure of the Algonkian language family extensively.
Many Algonkian groups historically lived along the Atlantic coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian - of or relating to the Algonquian peoples or languages

Etymology

Variant of 'Algonquian', from Canadian French 'Algonquin', a Native people of Quebec.

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ælˈɡɑŋkiən / (AmE), / ælˈɡɒŋkiən / (BrE)
a member of an Indigenous people originally living in the Ottawa River valley of Ontario and Quebec, Canada
Indigenous people of Canada

Examples

The Algonkian traditionally inhabited areas along the Ottawa River.
She studied the languages spoken by the Algonkian during her research.
Many Algonkian today live in both Canada and the United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquin - member of an Indigenous people closely related to the Algonkian

Etymology

From Maliseet-Passamaquoddy *elakómkwik* (they are our relatives); related to French *Algonquin*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09666270-n
Synset POS: n
