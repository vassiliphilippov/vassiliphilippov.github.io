retaliator

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈtæliˌeɪtər / (AmE), / rɪˈtæliˌeɪtə / (BrE)
a person who takes revenge or retaliates for an injury or wrong
someone who takes revenge

Examples

As a retaliator, he was feared by those who had wronged him.
The film portrays the hero as a retaliator determined to get justice for his family.
She refused to become a retaliator, choosing forgiveness instead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avenger - someone who seeks revenge or punishes wrongs
  • Antonym: forgiver - someone who forgives or pardons offenses

Etymology

From Latin retaliare (to retaliate) plus English -or (agent noun suffix).
