oilcloth

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪlˌklɔθ / (AmE), / ˈɔɪlˌklɒθ / (BrE)
A strong cotton or linen cloth coated with oil or synthetic resin to make it waterproof and often used for table coverings or shelves.
cloth coated with oil or resin to make it waterproof

Examples

The kitchen table was covered with a brightly patterned oilcloth.
She wiped the crumbs off the old oilcloth after breakfast.
A sheet of oilcloth protected the shelves from spills and stains.

Etymology

From 'oil' + 'cloth', referring to cloth treated with oil to make it waterproof.
