lodging

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of staying or being accommodated somewhere temporarily, especially overnight
the act of staying temporarily

Examples

They were grateful for the lodging provided during the storm.
My company will pay for your lodging while you attend the conference.
Finding affordable lodging in the city can be challenging during peak season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accommodation - a place to stay or be accommodated

Etymology

From Old French *logier* (to shelter), from Frankish *logjan* (to lie, rest).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
structures or rooms used for people to live or stay in, typically temporarily
temporary accommodation or housing

Examples

The hikers found lodging in a remote mountain cabin for the night.
Affordable lodging can be hard to find during the busy tourist season.
Many students seek lodging near the university to reduce their commuting time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accommodation - a place where someone may live or stay
  • Synonym: residence - the place where someone lives

Etymology

From Old French 'logier' (to shelter), from 'loge' (hut, cabin).

Synset POS: n
