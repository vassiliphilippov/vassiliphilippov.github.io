Balaeniceps rex

1. NOUN

/ bæləˈɛnɪsɛps ˈrɛks / (AmE), / balɪˈɛnɪsɛps ˈrɛks / (BrE)
A large stork-like bird native to the swamps of central tropical Africa, especially the White Nile valley, known for its broad, shoe-shaped bill.
African stork-like bird with shoe-shaped bill

Examples

The Balaeniceps rex is renowned for its massive, shoe-shaped bill.
Wildlife photographers travel to Uganda to catch a glimpse of the elusive Balaeniceps rex.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the wetland habitats of Balaeniceps rex.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoebill - The common name for Balaeniceps rex, an African bird with a large bill

Etymology

From Latin: 'balaena' (whale), 'ceps' (head), and 'rex' (king), meaning 'whale-headed king'.
