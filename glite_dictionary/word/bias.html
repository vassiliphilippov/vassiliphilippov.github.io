bias

1. ADJ

/ ˈbaɪ.əs / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.əs / (BrE)
(of fabric) slanted diagonally across the grain, typically at a 45-degree angle, used especially in sewing or tailoring.
diagonal across fabric grain (textiles)

Examples

The dress was cut on the bias to give it a graceful drape.
She made a bias fold to create a decorative edge on the curtain.
Cutting fabric on the bias allows it to stretch more easily around curves.

Synset Examples

2. VERB

/ ˈbaɪ.əs / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.əs / (BrE)
to influence someone or something in a way that is unfair or prejudiced
influence unfairly or with prejudice

Examples

The judge must not bias the jury with personal opinions.
Media reports can inadvertently bias public perception of an event.
Teachers should avoid biasing students during classroom discussions.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prejudice - to influence or judge unfairly

Etymology

From Old French biais (slant, oblique), from Provençal biais, possibly of Germanic origin.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪ.əs / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.əs / (BrE)
A diagonal line or cut across a fabric, especially one that is not at right angles to the side, often used in tailoring for greater stretch or drape.
diagonal line/cut in fabric (sewing)

Examples

The dress was cut on the bias to allow the fabric to flow gracefully.
She marked the bias before cutting the material for the quilt.
Cutting fabric on the bias makes it easier to form smooth curves.

Etymology

From Old French 'biais', meaning slant or slope, and Late Latin 'bifacius' (two-faced).

5. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪ.əs / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.əs / (BrE)
An inclination or prejudice for or against a person or group, especially in a way considered to be unfair, influencing judgment and preventing objective consideration.
unfair prejudice or inclination

Examples

The research was criticized for its bias toward one political party.
It is important to recognize personal bias when making decisions.
Unconscious bias can affect hiring processes in many organizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prejudice - preconceived opinion not based on reason or experience
  • Antonym: impartiality - lack of bias; fairness

Etymology

From Old French *biais* (slant, oblique), of uncertain origin.
