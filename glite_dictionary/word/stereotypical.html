stereotypical

1. ADJ

/ ˌstɛriəˈtɪpɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌstɛəriəˈtɪpɪkəl / (BrE)
lacking originality, individuality, or spontaneity; conforming to a fixed or conventional idea or pattern
conforming to a fixed or conventional pattern

Examples

The characters in the play were disappointingly stereotypical and lacked depth.
She gave a stereotypical answer that failed to surprise anyone.
His jokes were criticized for being stereotypical and uninspired.

Synset Examples

stereotyped phrases of condolence
even his profanity was unimaginative

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conventional - lacking originality or individuality; adhering to accepted standards
  • Antonym: original - not derived from something else; fresh and unusual

Etymology

From stereotype + -ical; 'stereotype' from French 'stéréotype', from Greek 'stereos' (solid) + 'typos' (impression, image).
