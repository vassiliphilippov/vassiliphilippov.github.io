ringer

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪŋər / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋə / (BrE)
a person whose job or duty is to ring church bells, especially to summon people for a service
person who rings church bells

Examples

The ringer climbed the tower early each Sunday to prepare for the morning service.
She became the youngest ringer in the church's history.
The sound of the bells told the villagers that the ringer had begun the ceremony.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪŋər / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋə / (BrE)
A successful throw in the game of horseshoes or quoits where the horseshoe or quoit completely encircles the stake or peg.
successful horseshoe or quoit throw encircling stake

Examples

He cheered as his horseshoe landed perfectly for a ringer around the stake.
Scoring a ringer in quoits can be more challenging than it looks.
She practiced all afternoon but managed just one ringer before sunset.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: leaner - A horseshoe throw that leans against the stake but does not encircle it

Etymology

Related to 'ring' because the thrown object forms a ring around the stake.

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɪŋər / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋə / (BrE)
a person who closely resembles another, especially one who is not related
person who looks very similar to another

Examples

Everyone thought the new guy was a ringer for the famous singer.
She met her ringer at a party and they couldn't believe the resemblance.
In the movie, the spy used a ringer to fool the guards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: double - a person who looks exactly like another, especially an actor's stand-in
  • Synonym: lookalike - a person who closely resembles another in appearance
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10551114-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈrɪŋər / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋə / (BrE)
a person who enters a competition under false pretenses, especially by concealing their true identity or ability in order to gain an unfair advantage
contestant entered under false pretenses

Examples

The coach was disqualified when it was discovered that he had entered a ringer in the youth tournament.
Everyone suspected the trivia team had a ringer after they answered every question perfectly.
Hiring a professional as a ringer for an amateur contest is against the rules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impostor - a person who pretends to be someone else to deceive others

Etymology

Probably from racing slang, originally referring to a horse substituted dishonestly for another in a race.

Source: WordNet
