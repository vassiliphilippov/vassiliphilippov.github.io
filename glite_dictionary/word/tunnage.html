tunnage

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌnɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtʌnɪdʒ / (BrE)
A tax imposed on ships entering a country, calculated according to the tonnage of the ship, especially in historical US law.
tax on ships based on tonnage
legal

Examples

In the 18th century, American ports collected tunnage from every foreign vessel that arrived in the harbor.
The shipping company's expenses rose significantly due to increased tunnage fees imposed by legislation.
Records show that many merchants protested the government's strict enforcement of tunnage in the early colonies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tonnage - charge based on the carrying capacity of a ship

Etymology

Variant of 'tonnage' (from Old French 'tonnage', from 'tonne' meaning cask or barrel).
