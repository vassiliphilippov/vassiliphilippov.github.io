exurbia

1. NOUN

/ ɛɡˈzɜr.bi.ə / (AmE), / ɛɡˈzɜː.bi.ə / (BrE)
A residential area situated beyond the suburbs of a city, often inhabited by people who commute to the city for work.
area beyond suburbs of a city

Examples

Many professionals choose to live in exurbia for the quieter lifestyle and larger properties.
As cities continue to sprawl, exurbia expands further into rural areas.
Commuting from exurbia into the city can take over an hour each day.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: suburbia - residential districts situated on the outskirts of a city

Etymology

Formed from 'exurb' (from Latin 'ex-' meaning 'out of' + 'suburb') + '-ia' denoting a place or area.
