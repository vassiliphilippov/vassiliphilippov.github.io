Otaria byronia

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈtɛəriə baɪˈroʊniə / (AmE), / əʊˈtɛəriə baɪˈrəʊniə / (BrE)
A species of sea lion found along the southern coasts of South America, commonly known as the South American sea lion.
South American sea lion species

Examples

The Otaria byronia population is an important part of the marine ecosystem along the Patagonian coast.
Researchers observed several Otaria byronia basking on the rocky shores of Tierra del Fuego.
The conservation status of Otaria byronia has been monitored due to increased fishing activities in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea lion - large marine mammal with external ears, related to seals

Etymology

Genus 'Otaria' from Greek ὠτάριον (little ear); species name honors Admiral John Byron.
