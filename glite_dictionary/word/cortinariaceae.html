Cortinariaceae

1. NOUN

/ kɔːrˌtɪnəˌriːˈeɪsiːˌaɪ / (AmE), / kɔːˌtɪn.ə.riːˈeɪ.siːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi in the order Agaricales, comprising over 2,100 species of gilled mushrooms, including the genus Cortinarius
family of gilled mushrooms

Examples

Many members of the Cortinariaceae are found growing in temperate forests around the world.
Some mushrooms in the Cortinariaceae family are known to be poisonous, so correct identification is important.
Scientists have recently discovered several new species of fungi belonging to the Cortinariaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Agaricales - an order of fungi that includes many gilled mushrooms
  • Synonym: Cortinarius - a large genus of mushrooms within the Cortinariaceae family

Etymology

From New Latin Cortinarius (a type genus) + -aceae (indicating a family in taxonomy).

2. NOUN

/ kɔrˌtɪnɛriˈeɪʃii / (AmE), / kɔːˌtɪnəriˈeɪsiːiː / (BrE)
A large family of fungi in the order Agaricales, comprising over 2,100 species of gilled mushrooms.
family of gilled mushrooms

Examples

The Cortinariaceae family includes a vast diversity of brown-spored mushrooms found in forests worldwide.
Researchers have recently discovered several new species within the Cortinariaceae family.
Many members of the Cortinariaceae are mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with trees.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Agaricales - order of fungi to which Cortinariaceae belongs
  • Degree Lower: Cortinarius - type genus of Cortinariaceae

Etymology

From the type genus Cortinarius, from Latin 'cortina' (curtain), referring to the cobweb-like veil on young mushrooms, with the family suffix '-aceae'.
