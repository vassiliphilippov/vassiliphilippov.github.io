cabinet

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæbɪnət / (AmE), / ˈkæbɪnɪt / (BrE)
a piece of furniture with shelves, drawers, or compartments for storing clothes, valuables, or other items, often equipped with a lock
piece of furniture for storage, often lockable

Examples

She keeps her jewelry in a locked cabinet beside her bed.
The kitchen cabinet was filled with spices and cooking utensils.
He opened the antique cabinet to reveal a collection of rare coins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cupboard - a piece of furniture with shelves for storing dishes, food, or other items
  • Synonym: closet - a small enclosed space or room for storing clothes or other items

Etymology

From French 'cabinet', diminutive of 'cabin', from Old French 'cabane' (cabin).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæb.ɪ.nət / (AmE), / ˈkæb.ɪ.nɪt / (BrE)
a piece of furniture with doors, shelves, and drawers used for storage or display
piece of furniture for storage

Examples

We installed a new cabinet in the kitchen to store our dishes.
The antique cabinet in the living room displays a collection of rare vases.
He locked the important documents in a secure cabinet at the office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cupboard - a cabinet with shelves for storing dishes, food, or other items
  • Synonym: wardrobe - a large, tall cabinet or closet for storing clothes

Etymology

From French 'cabinet', diminutive of 'cabine' (small room or cabin).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkæb.ɪ.nət / (AmE), / ˈkæb.ɪ.nət / (BrE)
a box or enclosure housing electronic instruments, such as a radio or television
enclosure for electronic instruments

Examples

The wooden cabinet of the vintage radio was beautifully crafted.
He replaced the television cabinet after the old one became warped.
This speaker system comes with a durable cabinet to protect the components.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enclosure - structure that surrounds or contains something
  • Synonym: cupboard - piece of furniture used for storage

Etymology

From French *cabinet* (small room, private room), diminutive of *cabane* (cabin).

4. NOUN

/ ˈkæbɪnət / (AmE), / ˈkæbɪnət / (BrE)
(in government) a group of senior officials appointed by the head of state or government to head executive departments and act as official advisers.
senior government advisory group

Examples

The president announced changes to his cabinet after the recent election.
Several ministers in the new cabinet have backgrounds in education and health.
The cabinet meets weekly to discuss national security and economic policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ministry - the executive branch of government, especially in parliamentary systems
  • Synonym: government - the group of people with authority to govern a country or state

Etymology

From French 'cabinet' (small room, council room), from Old French, from 'cabane' (cabin).
