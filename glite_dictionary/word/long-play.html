long-play

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɔŋˌpleɪ / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋˌpleɪ / (BrE)
(of a record or recording) designed to be played at a slower speed, typically 33⅓ rpm, and for a longer duration than earlier records.
describing a long-duration audio record

Examples

The band released their debut album on a long-play record.
Collectors often prefer the sound quality of vintage long-play vinyls.
He inherited a collection of classic long-play albums from his parents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: LP - long-playing record, a phonograph record played at 33⅓ rpm
  • Antonym: single - a record with one song per side, shorter duration than an LP
