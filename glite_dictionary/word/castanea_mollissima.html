Castanea mollissima

1. NOUN

/ kæˈsteɪniə mɑːˈlɪsɪmə / (AmE), / kaˈstiːnɪə mɒˈlɪsɪmə / (BrE)
A species of chestnut tree native to China and Korea, producing edible, sweet nuts regarded for their culinary use.
species of Chinese chestnut tree

Examples

The Castanea mollissima is prized for its resistance to chestnut blight.
Farmers in China cultivate Castanea mollissima for its sweet, edible nuts.
The leaves of Castanea mollissima are softly hairy underneath, making it easy to distinguish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chinese chestnut - common English name for Castanea mollissima
  • Antonym: American chestnut - species of chestnut tree native to eastern North America

Etymology

From Latin: 'castanea' meaning 'chestnut', and 'mollissima', the superlative of 'mollis' meaning 'soft' (referring to the soft underside of the leaves).
