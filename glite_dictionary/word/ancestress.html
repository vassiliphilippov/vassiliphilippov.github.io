ancestress

1. NOUN

/ ˈænˌsɛstrəs / (AmE), / ˈæn.sɛs.trəs / (BrE)
a woman from whom one is descended, especially one more distant than a grandparent
female ancestor

Examples

The family traced their lineage back to an ancestress who lived in the 17th century.
According to legend, their ancestress was a powerful queen.
She kept a portrait of her ancestress hanging above the fireplace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ancestor - a person from whom one is descended

Etymology

Formed from 'ancestor' + '-ess', denoting feminine gender.
