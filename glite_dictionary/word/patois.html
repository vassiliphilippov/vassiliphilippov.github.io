patois

1. NOUN

/ pæˈtwɑ / (AmE), / ˈpatwɑː / (BrE)
a form of speech or dialect specific to a particular group, region, or social class, often differing from the standard language
regional or group dialect, nonstandard speech

Examples

The villagers spoke in a patois that was difficult for outsiders to understand.
Her grandmother still uses a Caribbean patois at home.
This book explores the unique patois that developed among immigrant workers in the city.

Synset Examples

they don't speak our lingo

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dialect - a particular form of a language specific to a region or group
  • Synonym: lingo - informal language or jargon used by a particular group

Etymology

From French 'patois' (rough speech, dialect), possibly from Old French 'patoier' (to handle clumsily, treat roughly).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpætwɑ / (AmE), / ˈpætwa / (BrE)
a regional form of a language, especially French, that differs from the standard language and is often regarded as substandard or nonstandard
regional dialect, especially of French

Examples

Some communities in Jamaica still speak their native patois alongside English.
The local patois is difficult for outsiders to understand.
She grew up speaking a rural French patois at home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dialect - a particular form of a language specific to a region or social group
  • Antonym: standard language - the form of a language considered the norm or most accepted version

Etymology

From French patois (local dialect, rough speech), of uncertain origin.
