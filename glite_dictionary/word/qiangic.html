Qiangic

1. NOUN

/ tʃjæŋˈɡɪk / (AmE), / tʃiˈæŋɪk / (BrE)
a branch of the Tibeto-Burman languages spoken mainly in Sichuan, China
Tibeto-Burman language group of Sichuan

Examples

Several minority groups in Sichuan speak various Qiangic languages.
The Qiangic languages display significant diversity in both phonology and grammar.
Linguists have debated the status of Qiangic as a distinct branch within the Tibeto-Burman family.

Etymology

From 'Qiang' (an ethnic group of China) + -ic (forming adjectives and nouns).
