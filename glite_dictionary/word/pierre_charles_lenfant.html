Pierre Charles L'Enfant

1. PROPN

/ piˈɛr ʃɑrl lɑ̃ˈfɑnt / (AmE), / pjɛər ʃɑːl lɒ̃ˈfɒ̃ / (BrE)
(1754–1825) A French-born American architect and civil engineer best known for designing the plan of Washington, D.C.
French-born architect who designed Washington, D.C.

Examples

Pierre Charles L'Enfant designed the master plan for the city of Washington, D.C.
The vision of Pierre Charles L'Enfant shaped the layout of the U.S. capital.
Many historians credit Pierre Charles L'Enfant with creating one of the first planned cities in America.

Etymology

French name; 'L'Enfant' literally means 'the child' in French.
