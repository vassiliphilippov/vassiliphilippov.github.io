shelter

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛl.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈʃɛl.tə / (BrE)
a structure that provides privacy and protection from danger, bad weather, or other unpleasant conditions
structure offering protection

Examples

They built a temporary shelter from branches and leaves in the forest.
During the storm, the hikers took shelter under a rocky overhang.
The city set up an emergency shelter for people who lost their homes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refuge - a place or state of safety and protection
  • Antonym: exposure - the condition of being unprotected and open to danger or the elements

Etymology

From Middle English 'sheltren', from Old English 'scield' (shield).

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛltɚ / (AmE), / ˈʃɛltə / (BrE)
a scheme or arrangement used to minimize the amount of tax a business or individual must pay on current earnings
arrangement to reduce tax liability
legal

Examples

The company used a legal shelter to lower its tax payments.
Many wealthy individuals invest in offshore shelters to protect their income from higher taxation.
A government crackdown on tax shelters has closed many loopholes for corporations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tax haven - a country or jurisdiction with low tax rates used to minimize tax liability
Source: WordNet

/ ˈʃɛltɚ / (AmE), / ˈʃɛltə / (BrE)
to invest money or arrange financial matters so as to avoid taxation on the income or assets
avoid paying tax on income or assets

Examples

They look for ways to shelter their earnings from high taxes.

4. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛl.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈʃɛl.tə / (BrE)
a structure or covering that provides protection from the weather or danger.
structure or covering for protection

Examples

The hikers built a shelter from branches to stay dry during the storm.
Many animals seek shelter under rocks or in caves when it rains.
We waited in the small shelter at the bus stop until the downpour ended.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refuge - a place providing protection or safety
  • Antonym: exposure - the state of being left unprotected

Etymology

From Old English 'scildtruma' (shelter, cover); influenced by Old French 'sheltre' (refuge).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

5. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛl.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈʃɛl.tə / (BrE)
a building or place that provides temporary accommodation and protection, especially for homeless or displaced people
temporary accommodation for the homeless

Examples

The city opened an emergency shelter for families displaced by the flood.
After losing his job, he spent several nights in a homeless shelter.
Volunteers serve meals to residents at the local women's shelter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refuge - a place of safety or protection
  • Degree Lower: housing - the provision of accommodation

Etymology

From Old English 'scildtrian' (to shield or protect), ultimately from Proto-Germanic roots.
