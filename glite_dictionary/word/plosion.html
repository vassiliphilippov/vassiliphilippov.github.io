plosion

1. NOUN

/ ˈploʊʒən / (AmE), / ˈpləʊʒən / (BrE)
the sudden release of air pressure built up in the mouth during the articulation of a stop consonant
sudden release of air in speech sounds

Examples

The plosion in the word 'cat' can be clearly heard after the 't' sound.
Phonetic analysis shows the exact moment of plosion when pronouncing a stop consonant.
She studied the difference in plosion between voiced and voiceless stops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burst - a sudden release or explosion, esp. in sound
  • Antonym: closure - in phonetics, the occlusion phase before plosion

Etymology

From French *plosion*, from Latin *plosio* (‘a striking, clapping’), from *plodere* (‘to clap, strike’).
