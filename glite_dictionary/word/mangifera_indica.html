Mangifera indica

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæŋɪˈfɛrə ɪnˈdikə / (AmE), / ˌmaŋɪˈfɪərə ɪnˈdɪkə / (BrE)
A large evergreen tropical tree native to South Asia, widely cultivated for its large, sweet, oval fruit known as the mango.
species of mango tree

Examples

Mangifera indica is commonly grown in tropical and subtropical regions for its delicious mango fruit.
The botanist conducted research on Mangifera indica to improve disease resistance in mango crops.
Many gardens in India feature at least one Mangifera indica tree, prized for both shade and fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mango - the sweet, fleshy fruit of Mangifera indica

Etymology

From Latin: 'Mangifera' meaning 'mango-bearing', and 'indica' meaning 'of India'.
