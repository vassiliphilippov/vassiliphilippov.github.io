seabed

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiˌbɛd / (AmE), / ˈsiːˌbɛd / (BrE)
the ground at the bottom of the sea or ocean
bottom of the sea or ocean

Examples

The submarine slowly descended to the seabed to collect samples.
Coral reefs often form on the seabed in shallow waters.
The earthquake caused minor shifts on the ocean seabed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seafloor - the ground at the bottom of the sea or ocean
  • Antonym: surface - the top layer or outside of something, especially of water

Etymology

Formed from 'sea' + 'bed', referring to the bed or floor of the sea.
