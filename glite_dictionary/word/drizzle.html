drizzle

1. VERB

/ ˈdrɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈdrɪzəl / (BrE)
to rain lightly, with very small drops falling slowly and steadily
to rain lightly

Examples

It began to drizzle just as we left the house for a walk.
The weather forecast said it might drizzle all morning.
Clouds gathered and soon it started drizzling over the quiet city.

Synset Examples

When it drizzles in summer, hiking can be pleasant

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: rain - water falling in drops from the clouds

2. VERB

/ ˈdrɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈdrɪzəl / (BrE)
to pour a liquid in a thin stream or in fine drops over something, especially food
pour liquid in fine drops over something

Examples

After baking, drizzle the cake with a lemon glaze for extra flavor.
She likes to drizzle olive oil over her salad just before serving.
The chef drizzled caramel sauce on top of the dessert.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈdrɪzəl / (BrE)
Rain falling lightly in very fine drops, stronger than mist but less intense than a shower.
very light rain; fine drops

Examples

A light drizzle began to fall, making the streets shiny and damp.
By the time we reached the park, the drizzle had turned into a steady rain.
Despite the persistent drizzle, the football match went ahead as planned.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: shower - a brief and usually light fall of rain
  • Degree Lower: mist - a phenomenon of tiny water droplets suspended in the air
  • Degree Higher: downpour - a heavy fall of rain

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'dreselen' or 'driselen' (to let fall in fine drops), of imitative origin.

4. VERB

/ ˈdrɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈdrɪzəl / (BrE)
To pour a liquid slowly in small, fine drops over something.
pour in fine drops

Examples

Lightly drizzle olive oil over the salad before serving.
The chef instructed us to drizzle the sauce onto the fish.
She likes to drizzle honey on her morning toast.

Synset Examples

drizzle the meat with melted butter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pour - to cause liquid to flow somewhere from a container

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian 'drisle' (to fall in fine drops).
