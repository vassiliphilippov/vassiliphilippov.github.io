lygaeid

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈɡiːɪd / (AmE), / lɪˈɡiːɪd / (BrE)
A bug of the family Lygaeidae, usually brightly colored and known as a pest of cultivated crops and some fruit trees.
a brightly colored crop pest (bug)

Examples

Some species of lygaeid can cause significant damage to sunflower crops.
The orchard suffered an infestation of lygaeid bugs last summer.
Researchers identified the insect as a lygaeid by its distinctive markings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seed bug - A common name for bugs in the family Lygaeidae

Etymology

From modern Latin Lygaeidae, the family name, derived from the type genus Lygaeus.
