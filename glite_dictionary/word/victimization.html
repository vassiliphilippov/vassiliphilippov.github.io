victimization

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɪktəməˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɪktɪmaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making someone a victim, typically by causing them harm or unfair treatment
the process of making someone a victim

Examples

The report detailed the victimization of workers in unsafe factories.
Many charities aim to prevent the victimization of children and vulnerable adults.
She spoke openly about her victimization and the impact it had on her life.

Synset Examples

his victimization infuriated him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abuse - cruel or violent treatment of someone
  • Synonym: persecution - persistent harassment or mistreatment

Etymology

From English 'victim' + '-ization', patterned after Latin roots.

2. NOUN

/ ˌvɪktəməˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɪktɪmaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of treating someone unfairly or exploiting them for personal gain
unfair treatment or exploitation

Examples

The new laws aim to prevent the victimization of vulnerable groups in society.
Widespread victimization of employees triggered protests across the industry.
She spoke out against the victimization she experienced at her previous workplace.

Synset Examples

capitalistic exploitation of the working class
paying Blacks less and charging them more is a form of victimization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exploitation - the action of making use of and benefiting from resources or people
  • Synonym: oppression - prolonged cruel or unjust treatment or control

Etymology

From English 'victimize' + '-ation', ultimately from Latin 'victima' (sacrificial victim).
