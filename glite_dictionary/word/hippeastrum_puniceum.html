Hippeastrum puniceum

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpiˈæstrəm pjuːˈnɪsiəm / (AmE), / ˌhɪpiːˈæstrəm pjuːˈnɪsɪəm / (BrE)
A species of amaryllis native to tropical America, often grown as a houseplant for its large, showy white to red flowers.
tropical American amaryllis cultivated for showy flowers

Examples

The Hippeastrum puniceum brightened the living room with its vibrant red blooms.
Gardeners recommend planting Hippeastrum puniceum bulbs in well-drained soil to encourage flowering.
My grandmother's windowsill is lined with pots of Hippeastrum puniceum in full bloom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amaryllis - bulbous plant with large, showy flowers

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: 'Hippeastrum' from Greek 'hippos' (horse) + 'astron' (star), 'puniceum' from Latin 'puniceus' (reddish or purple-red).
