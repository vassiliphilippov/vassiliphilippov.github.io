devoid

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈvɔɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈvɔɪd / (BrE)
completely lacking in; entirely without.
completely lacking; without

Examples

The abandoned house was devoid of any furniture or decoration.
Her voice was calm but devoid of emotion as she delivered the news.
Without sunlight, the cave is entirely devoid of plant life.

Synset Examples

writing barren of insight
young recruits destitute of experience
innocent of literary merit
the sentence was devoid of meaning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empty - containing nothing; not filled or occupied
  • Synonym: bereft - deprived of or lacking something

Etymology

From Old French 'devoide', from des- (expressing removal) + void (empty).
