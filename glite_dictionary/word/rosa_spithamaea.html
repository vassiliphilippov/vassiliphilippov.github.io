Rosa spithamaea

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊzə spɪˈθæmiə / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzə spɪˈθæmiə / (BrE)
A low-growing bristly shrub native to southern Oregon and California, having creeping rootstocks and usually corymbose clusters of flowers.
species of low-growing bristly shrub

Examples

The Rosa spithamaea thrives in sandy soils across southern Oregon and California.
Gardeners appreciate Rosa spithamaea for its compact size and resilience.
Botanists identified the distinctive corymbose flowers of Rosa spithamaea during the field study.

Etymology

Latin: 'Rosa' meaning 'rose'; 'spithamaea' from Greek 'spithamaia' meaning 'a span long', referring to the plant's low stature.
