Lachnolaimus

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæknoʊˈleɪməs / (AmE), / ˌlaknəʊˈleɪməs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the wrasse family (Labridae), including the hogfish.
genus of wrasse including the hogfish

Examples

The Lachnolaimus genus is best known for the colorful species commonly called hogfish.
Marine biologists have studied the feeding habits of Lachnolaimus in Caribbean coral reefs.
Fishermen in Florida often catch Lachnolaimus for sport and consumption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hogfish - a marine fish (Lachnolaimus maximus) of the wrasse family

Etymology

From Greek 'lachnē' (woolly) + 'laimos' (throat), referring to the appearance of the fish.
