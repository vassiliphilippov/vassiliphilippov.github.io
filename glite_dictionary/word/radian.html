radian

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪ.di.ən / (AmE), / ˈreɪ.di.ən / (BrE)
a unit of angle equal to the angle at the center of a circle subtended by an arc whose length is equal to the radius (approximately 57.295 degrees)
unit of angular measure

Examples

A full circle contains 2π radians, which is about 6.283 radians.
The angle of the sector was measured as one radian.
Trigonometric functions like sine and cosine are often defined in terms of radians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: degree - a unit of angular measure, 1/360 of a full rotation
  • Degree Higher: steradian - the SI unit of solid angle

Etymology

From Latin radius (ray, spoke of a wheel), with -an forming a derivative noun.
