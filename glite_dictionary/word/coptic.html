Coptic

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑp.tɪk / (AmE), / ˈkɒp.tɪk / (BrE)
the liturgical language of the Coptic Church, used primarily in Egypt and Ethiopia, written mainly in the Greek alphabet
liturgical language of the Coptic Church

Examples

The priest recited the prayers in Coptic during the Easter service.
Many ancient manuscripts are preserved in Coptic, providing valuable insights into early Christianity.
Linguists study Coptic to learn about the final stage of the Egyptian language.

Etymology

From Arabic qubṭī (Copt), from Greek Aigyptios (Egyptian).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɑːptɪk / (AmE), / ˈkɒptɪk / (BrE)
relating to the Copts, their church, their language, or their art, especially in Egypt
of the Copts, church, language, or art

Examples

The museum featured several impressive examples of Coptic textiles from Late Antiquity.
He is writing a book about Coptic Christian traditions and rituals.
The ancient manuscripts are written in Coptic, a language now mainly used in liturgy.

Synset Examples

the distinctive Coptic art of 6th-century Christian Egypt

Etymology

From Arabic qubṭī, itself from Coptic kyptios, from Greek Aigyptios (Egyptian).
