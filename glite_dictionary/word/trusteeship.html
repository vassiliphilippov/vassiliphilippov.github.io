trusteeship

1. NOUN

/ trʌsˈtiːʃɪp / (AmE), / trʌsˈtiːʃɪp / (BrE)
A country or territory that is governed by another country under the supervision of the United Nations, as part of an arrangement to prepare it for self-government or independence.
country or territory under UN-administered government

Examples

After World War II, several territories were placed under trusteeship to help them transition toward independence.
The island nation only achieved full sovereignty after decades of trusteeship under the United Nations.
The end of the trusteeship marked a crucial step in the country's path to self-governance.

Etymology

From 'trustee' + 'ship'; used since the 20th century, especially in the context of the United Nations mandates.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌstiˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈtrʌstiːʃɪp / (BrE)
the position, duties, or office of a trustee; the state of being a trustee responsible for managing assets or affairs for others
position or office of a trustee

Examples

He accepted the trusteeship of the charitable foundation with great seriousness.
Her trusteeship ended after five years of dedicated service.
The court appointed him to the trusteeship of the estate during the probate process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trustee - a person or organization responsible for managing assets on behalf of others

Etymology

From 'trustee' + '-ship', denoting the state or office of being a trustee.
