VHF

1. NOUN

/ ˌviː ˌeɪtʃ ˈɛf / (AmE), / ˌviː ˌeɪtʃ ˈɛf / (BrE)
a range of radio frequencies from 30 to 300 megahertz, commonly used for television, radio, and communications
radio frequency range (30–300 MHz)

Examples

Many television and radio stations broadcast on the VHF band.
The pilot switched to the VHF radio to communicate with air traffic control.
Older TV antennas are often designed to receive VHF signals.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: UHF - radio frequencies ranging from 300 to 3000 MHz
  • Degree Lower: HF - radio frequencies ranging from 3 to 30 MHz

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'very high frequency'.

2. NOUN

/ viː ɛtʃ ɛf / (AmE), / viː ɛtʃ ɛf / (BrE)
An infectious disease syndrome caused by certain viruses, characterized by fever, bleeding, and often affecting specific regions.
viral hemorrhagic fever disease
medical

Examples

Many outbreaks of VHF have occurred in tropical regions with inadequate healthcare.
Certain rodents can transmit viruses responsible for VHF to humans.
Healthcare workers take special precautions to prevent the spread of VHF.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ebola - A severe viral hemorrhagic fever caused by the Ebola virus
  • Synonym: Lassa fever - A viral hemorrhagic fever endemic to West Africa

Etymology

Acronym for viral hemorrhagic fever.

Source: WordNet
