het

1. ADJ

/ hɛt / (AmE), / hɛt / (BrE)
(dialect, chiefly US) heated, made warm or hot; often used in the phrase 'het up' to mean agitated or excited.
dialect variant of 'heated'
informal

Examples

After jogging under the sun, he was all het up and thirsty.
Grandma kept the soup het on the stove for supper.
Don't get yourself so het about something so minor!

Synset Examples

a heated swimming pool
wiped his heated-up face with a large bandana
he was all het up and sweaty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heated - made hot or warm; excited; agitated

Etymology

Variant of 'heated', from Middle English 'heaten', past participle of 'heten'.
