New Dealer

1. NOUN

/ nuː ˈdiːlər / (AmE), / njuː ˈdiːlə / (BrE)
a person who supported or was associated with the economic and social policies of the New Deal in the United States during the 1930s and 1940s
supporter of the New Deal policies

Examples

My grandfather was a proud New Dealer who supported Roosevelt throughout the Depression.
The senator, a longtime New Dealer, advocated for social welfare programs.
Historians often study the lives of New Dealers to understand the political climate of the 1930s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: New Deal - series of economic programs in the US (1930s-40s)

Etymology

From New Deal, the name of President Franklin D. Roosevelt's policy program (1930s), plus -er suffix forming agent nouns.
