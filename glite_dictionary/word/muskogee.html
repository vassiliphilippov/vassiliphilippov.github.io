Muskogee

1. NOUN

/ mʌsˈkoʊgi / (AmE), / mʌsˈkəʊgi / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people formerly living in Georgia and eastern Alabama, who formed the core of the Creek Confederacy.
a Native American people; core of the Creek Confederacy

Examples

Many Muskogee descendants today live in Oklahoma and preserve their cultural traditions.
Historians study the role of the Muskogee in the formation of the Creek Confederacy.
The Muskogee language is still spoken by some elders in the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Creek - a member of the same Native American confederacy as the Muskogee

Etymology

From Creek (Muskogean) word for themselves, possibly meaning 'people.'

2. NOUN

/ mʌsˈkoʊɡi / (AmE), / mʌsˈkəʊɡi / (BrE)
the Native American language of the Muskogee people, belonging to the Muskogean language family
language of the Muskogee people

Examples

She is learning to speak Muskogee from her grandmother.
Many historical documents were originally written in Muskogee.
A revival program aims to teach children Muskogee to preserve their cultural heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Creek - another name for the Muskogee language

Etymology

From the name Muskogee, referring to the Native American people and their language.

3. PROPN

/ mʌsˈkoʊɡi / (AmE), / mʌsˈkəʊɡi / (BrE)
A city in eastern Oklahoma, United States, located on the Arkansas River.
city in eastern Oklahoma, US

Examples

My grandfather was born in Muskogee and lived there his whole life.
The Arkansas River passes through Muskogee, shaping much of its history and economy.
On our road trip across Oklahoma, we stopped for lunch in Muskogee.

Etymology

Named after the Muscogee (Creek) Nation, a Native American people.
