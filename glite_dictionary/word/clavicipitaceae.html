Clavicipitaceae

1. NOUN

/ klævɪˌsɪpɪˈteɪsiˌi / (AmE), / klavɪˌsɪpɪˈtiːsiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi in the class Ascomycetes that includes various parasitic and symbiotic species, some of which are important in agriculture and medicine
a family of fungi in Ascomycetes

Examples

Many members of the Clavicipitaceae family produce important alkaloids used in medicine.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Clavicipitaceae during their expedition in the Amazon rainforest.
The Clavicipitaceae are known for infecting grasses and forming symbiotic relationships with certain plants.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Claviceps and the suffix -aceae, used for plant and fungal family names.
