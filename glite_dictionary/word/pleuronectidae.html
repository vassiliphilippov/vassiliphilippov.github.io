Pleuronectidae

1. NOUN

/ plʊəroʊˈnɛktəˌdiː / (AmE), / plʊərəʊˈnɛktɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of flatfish known as righteye flounders, characterized by both eyes being on the right side of the body in adults
family of righteye flounders (flatfish)

Examples

The family Pleuronectidae includes many commercially important species of flatfish.
Biologists study Pleuronectidae to better understand the evolution of eye migration in flatfish.
Some members of Pleuronectidae are commonly found on sandy sea floors around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flounder - a type of flatfish

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'pleura' (side/rib) and 'nekton' (swimming).
