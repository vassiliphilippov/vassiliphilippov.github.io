polemonium

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊləˈmoʊniəm / (AmE), / ˌpəʊlɪˈməʊniəm / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Polemonium, which are low-growing, often foul-smelling plants native to temperate and Arctic regions
a genus of low-growing plants

Examples

The garden was filled with blooming polemonium among other wildflowers.
Many species of polemonium are cultivated for their attractive blue flowers.
Researchers documented how polemonium thrives in cold, mountainous habitats.

Etymology

From Latin 'polemonium', from Greek 'polemonion', the ancient name of the plant.
