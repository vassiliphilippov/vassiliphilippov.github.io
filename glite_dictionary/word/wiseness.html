wiseness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪznəs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪznəs / (BrE)
the quality of having or showing experience, knowledge, and good judgment
quality of being wise

Examples

His wiseness was evident in every decision he made during the crisis.
We admired her wiseness in choosing the right moment to speak up.
The wiseness of the leader helped the group avoid many mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wisdom - the ability to use knowledge and experience to make good decisions

Etymology

Formed from 'wise' + suffix '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪz.nəs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪz.nəs / (BrE)
The ability to use knowledge and experience with insight and good judgment; the quality of being wise.
the quality of being wise

Examples

Her wiseness was evident in every decision she made for the company.
He spoke with a wiseness that belied his young age.
The wiseness of the elders is respected in many cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wisdom - the quality of having experience, knowledge, and good judgment

Etymology

Formed from 'wise' (from Old English 'wīs') + the noun-forming suffix '-ness'.
