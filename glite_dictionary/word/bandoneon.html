bandoneon

1. NOUN

/ ˌbændəˈniːən / (AmE), / ˌbændəˈnɪən / (BrE)
a type of concertina, especially popular in South America, used in tango music
South American concertina instrument

Examples

He played the bandoneon with such passion that the audience was mesmerized.
The haunting melody filled the room, carried by the sound of the bandoneon.
Many classic tango ensembles feature a bandoneon as their central instrument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concertina - a small musical instrument similar to an accordion

Etymology

From German 'Bandoneon', named after Heinrich Band (1821–1860), its inventor.
