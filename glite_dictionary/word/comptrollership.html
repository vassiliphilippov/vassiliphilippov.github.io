comptrollership

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈtroʊlərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / kəmˈtrəʊləʃɪp / (BrE)
the position or office of a comptroller, especially in an organization or government
position of a comptroller

Examples

He was promoted to the comptrollership after serving as assistant for three years.
The comptrollership involves overseeing all financial operations of the agency.
Her experience in accounting made her a strong candidate for the comptrollership.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: controller - person who manages the financial affairs of an organization

Etymology

From comptroller + -ship, modeled after 'controller'.
