firetrap

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪərˌtræp / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌtræp / (BrE)
A building that is unsafe and would be difficult to escape from in the event of a fire.
unsafe building hard to escape in a fire

Examples

The inspector declared the old warehouse a firetrap because of its blocked exits and faulty wiring.
Many tenants feared living in such a firetrap after hearing about frequent fires in the neighborhood.
The city council voted to demolish the firetrap and build safer housing in its place.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deathtrap - a situation or place posing extremely high danger to life

Etymology

From 'fire' + 'trap', indicating a place that traps people in case of fire.
