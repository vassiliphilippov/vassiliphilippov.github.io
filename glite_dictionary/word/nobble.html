to nobble

1. VERB

/ ˈnɑbəl / (AmE), / ˈnɒbəl / (BrE)
to disable or interfere with, especially by drugging, typically in order to cheat in a competition such as a horse race
disable by drugging (esp. in cheating)
british

Examples

The trainer was caught trying to nobble the favorite before the race.
There were rumors that someone had nobbled the team's star player.
Officials suspect an attempt to nobble the competition through illegal substances.

Synset Examples

nobble the race horses

2. VERB

/ ˈnɑːbəl / (AmE), / ˈnɒbəl / (BrE)
(British, informal, slang) To steal or unlawfully take someone else's belongings.
steal or take unlawfully (British slang)
informal slang british

Examples

Someone tried to nobble my bike while I was in the shop.
Keep your eye on your wallet—he'll nobble it if you give him a chance.
The gang nobbled several handbags at the market last week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steal - to take something that belongs to someone else without permission

3. VERB

/ ˈnɑːbəl / (AmE), / ˈnɒbl / (BrE)
to obtain something from someone by deceit; to cheat or trick someone out of something
cheat or trick someone out of something
informal british

Examples

He tried to nobble me out of my share of the profits, but I caught on quickly.
It was reported that the accountant had nobbled several clients over the years.
The new owner was wary of anyone who might attempt to nobble him during the business transition.

Synset Examples

He swindled me out of my inheritance
She defrauded the customers who trusted her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swindle - to cheat someone out of money or property
  • Synonym: to defraud - to illegally obtain money from someone by deception

Etymology

Probably from British dialect 'nob', meaning 'to strike, hit', with altered sense.
