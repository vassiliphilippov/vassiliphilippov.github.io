Moehringia lateriflora

1. NOUN

/ moʊˌhɪrɪndʒiə ˌlætərəˈflɔrə / (AmE), / məʊˌhɪrɪndʒiə ˌlætərəˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A low-growing herbaceous plant with clusters of small white flowers, each having four petals, native to temperate regions.
a low-growing plant with small white flowers

Examples

We found a patch of Moehringia lateriflora growing in the shady part of the forest.
The delicate white flowers of Moehringia lateriflora stood out against the green moss.
Botanists recorded the presence of Moehringia lateriflora during their field survey.

Etymology

From the botanical Latin genus Moehringia, named after German botanist Paul Möhring, and species lateriflora (Latin for 'with flowers on the side').
