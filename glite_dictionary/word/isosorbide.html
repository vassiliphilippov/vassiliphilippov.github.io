isosorbide

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsəˈsɔrbaɪd / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈsɔːbaɪd / (BrE)
A medication used to prevent chest pain (angina) and to treat certain types of heart failure.
drug for angina and heart failure
medical

Examples

The cardiologist prescribed isosorbide to help manage her angina symptoms.
Patients with chronic heart failure may benefit from regular doses of isosorbide.
He experienced relief from chest pain after starting on isosorbide tablets.

Etymology

From iso- (denoting equality or uniformity) + sorbitol (a type of sugar alcohol from which it is derived).
