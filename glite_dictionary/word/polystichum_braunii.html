Polystichum braunii

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊlɪˈstɪkəm ˈbraʊni.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈstɪkəm ˈbraʊni.aɪ / (BrE)
A North American fern with evergreen, leathery fronds covered in pale brown, chafflike scales.
a North American fern species

Examples

The shady garden was home to several Polystichum braunii specimens thriving near the stream.
Botanists admire Polystichum braunii for its distinctive fronds covered in brown scales.
In northern forests, Polystichum braunii is valued for its ability to remain green through winter.

Etymology

The genus name from Greek 'poly-' (many) and 'stichos' (row), referring to rows of sori; species named after Alexander Braun, a German botanist.
