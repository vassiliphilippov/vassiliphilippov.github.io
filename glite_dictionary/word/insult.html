insult

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsʌlt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsʌlt / (BrE)
a remark or action that is rude or offensive, intended to hurt someone's feelings or dignity
offensive or hurtful remark/action

Examples

She took his comment as a personal insult and walked away in anger.
The players ignored the crowd's insults and focused on the game.
Calling someone lazy is a serious insult in many workplaces.

Synset Examples

when a student made a stupid mistake he spared them no abuse
they yelled insults at the visiting team

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to offend - to cause someone to feel hurt or upset by something said or done
  • Antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration

Etymology

From Latin 'insultare' (to jump on, attack), via French 'insulter'.

WordNet ID: oewn-06728162-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈsʌlt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsʌlt / (BrE)
to speak to or treat someone with disrespect or scornful abuse
to offend or disrespect someone

Examples

He didn't mean to insult you, but his jokes were inappropriate.
She felt deeply hurt when they insulted her ideas during the meeting.
Stop insulting your brother and try to be more supportive.

Synset Examples

He insulted her with his rude remarks
the student who had betrayed his classmate was dissed by everyone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to offend - to cause someone to feel upset or annoyed
  • Antonym: compliment - to politely congratulate or praise

Etymology

From Latin 'insultare' meaning 'to leap upon, attack.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00850100-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.sʌlt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.sʌlt / (BrE)
a remark or action that is deliberately rude or disrespectful
deliberately rude remark or act

Examples

He took her comments as a personal insult and refused to speak to her.
Throwing a drink in someone's face is considered a serious insult.
The remarks were meant as an insult, not a joke.

Synset Examples

turning his back on me was a deliberate insult

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offense - an action that causes someone to feel hurt or upset
  • Antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration

Etymology

From Latin insultare (to jump on, attack), from in- (on) + saltare (to jump).
