Gobiesox

1. NOUN

/ ˈgoʊ.biˌsɑks / (AmE), / ˈgəʊ.biˌsɒks / (BrE)
A genus of small marine or freshwater clingfishes in the family Gobiesocidae, found in the Americas.
genus of clingfishes

Examples

The Gobiesox genus includes species known for their ability to adhere to rocks and other surfaces.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Gobiesox group in Central American mangroves.
A Gobiesox can be distinguished by its unique sucker-like disc on its underside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gobiesocidae - the family of clingfishes to which Gobiesox belongs

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from Gobius (a related fish genus) + Greek sōx, meaning 'quick, lively.'

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡoʊ.biˈsɑks / (AmE), / ˌɡəʊ.biˈsɒks / (BrE)
a genus of small, marine or brackish-water clingfishes, the type genus of the family Gobiesocidae
a genus of clingfishes

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species in the Gobiesox genus off the Pacific coast.
Many Gobiesox species are notable for their ability to cling to rocks in fast-flowing waters.
The Gobiesox have a modified pelvic fin forming a suction disc.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gobiesocidae - the clingfish family of which Gobiesox is the type genus

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, formed from 'gobius' (goby) and Greek 'sōx', possibly relating to 'sōzein' (to save, to preserve).
