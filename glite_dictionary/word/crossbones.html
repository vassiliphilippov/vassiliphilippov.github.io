crossbones

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsˌboʊnz / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌbəʊnz / (BrE)
two bones laid across each other, often used as a symbol of danger or death, especially on warning signs or flags
symbol: two bones crossed, sign of danger/death

Examples

The pirate flag featured a skull and crossbones to warn of deadly danger.
The bottle was clearly marked with crossbones to show it contained poison.
A sign with crossbones was posted near the old mine to keep people away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skull and crossbones - a symbol of a human skull above two crossed bones, indicating poison or danger
