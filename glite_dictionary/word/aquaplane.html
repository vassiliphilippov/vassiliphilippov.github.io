aquaplane

1. NOUN

/ ˈækwəˌpleɪn / (AmE), / ˈakwəˌpleɪn / (BrE)
A board towed by a speedboat that a person stands on to skim across the surface of water.
board towed on water by a boat

Examples

He balanced carefully on the aquaplane as the boat picked up speed across the lake.
Learning to ride an aquaplane requires both balance and courage.
They rented an aquaplane for an afternoon of water sports fun.

Etymology

From Latin 'aqua' (water) + plane (flat surface, as in 'airplane').

2. VERB

/ ˈækwəˌpleɪn / (AmE), / ˈakwəpleɪn / (BrE)
(of a motor vehicle) to slide uncontrollably on a wet surface due to a thin layer of water between the tires and the road.
slide on water (motor vehicles)

Examples

After the heavy rain, the car began to aquaplane on the highway, making control difficult.
Drivers are advised to slow down to avoid aquaplaning during storms.
She lost control when her vehicle aquaplaned on the flooded road.

Synset Examples

the car aquaplaned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydroplane - to slide on a wet surface because a layer of water comes between the tires and the road

Etymology

From Latin 'aqua' (water) and 'plane' (surface); originally referred to a board ridden on water, later used as a verb for vehicles losing traction on water.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01960371-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈækwəˌpleɪn / (AmE), / ˈakwəˌpleɪn / (BrE)
to ride over water on a wide board or similar object, usually towed by a motorboat
ride a board on water, often towed

Examples

She learned to aquaplane while on vacation by the lake.
The children wanted to aquaplane behind the speedboat all afternoon.
He had never aquaplaned before but quickly mastered balancing on the board.

Etymology

Formed from 'aqua-' (Latin, meaning water) and 'plane' (from 'airplane').
