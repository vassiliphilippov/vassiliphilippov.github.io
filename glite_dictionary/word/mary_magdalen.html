Mary Magdalen

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɛri ˈmæɡdəˌlən / (AmE), / ˈmɛːri ˈmaɡdələn / (BrE)
a woman mentioned in the New Testament, traditionally believed to have been healed of evil spirits by Jesus and who became one of his followers
follower of Jesus in the New Testament

Examples

Many paintings throughout history depict Mary Magdalen at the foot of the cross.
Religious scholars have debated the role and reputation of Mary Magdalen for centuries.
The story of Mary Magdalen is often referenced in discussions of forgiveness and redemption.

Etymology

From Latin Maria Magdalena, referring to Mary of Magdala (a town on the Sea of Galilee).
