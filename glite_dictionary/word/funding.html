funding

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfʌndɪŋ / (BrE)
the provision of financial resources for a particular purpose, especially by an organization or government
the act of providing financial resources

Examples

The university relies on government funding for much of its research.
After the project lost its funding, work came to a halt.
Securing additional funding was essential for the charity to expand its services.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finance - providing funds for a person or enterprise

Etymology

From 'fund' (noun) + '-ing', formed in the 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfʌndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfʌndɪŋ / (BrE)
money provided, especially by an organization or government, for a particular purpose, such as a project or activity.
money provided for a particular purpose

Examples

The university received additional funding to expand its research facilities.
Government funding for public schools has increased this year.
They are seeking funding to develop a new medical device.

Synset Examples

the foundation provided support for the experiment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finance - the management of large amounts of money, especially by governments or large companies
  • Synonym: grant - an amount of money given for a particular purpose, especially by a government or organization
  • Synonym: subsidy - money given as support, usually by the government, to help an industry or business

Etymology

From 'fund' + '-ing', with 'fund' originating from Latin 'fundus' (bottom, foundation).
