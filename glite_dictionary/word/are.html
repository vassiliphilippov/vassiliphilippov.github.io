are

1. NOUN

/ ɑr / (AmE), / ɑː / (BrE)
a unit of surface area equal to 100 square meters, used primarily in land measurement
unit of area (100 square meters)

Examples

The size of the garden was measured at 5 are.
They bought a plot of land with an area of ten ares.
One are equals exactly 100 square meters in the metric system.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hectare - unit of area equal to 10,000 square meters (100 ares)

Etymology

From French 'are', coined in the metric system, from Latin 'area' (open space).
