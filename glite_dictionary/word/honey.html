honey

1. ADJ

/ ˈhʌni / (AmE), / ˈhʌni / (BrE)
Having the color of honey; a light golden brown or yellowish-brown.
light golden brown (color)

Examples

She wore a honey sweater that complemented her complexion perfectly.
The horse's coat shone in the sunlight, a beautiful honey shade.
They painted the walls a warm honey color to brighten up the room.

2. VERB

/ ˈhʌni / (AmE), / ˈhʌni / (BrE)
to sweeten or cover with honey; to add honey to something
to sweeten with honey

Examples

Before baking, she likes to honey the carrots for extra flavor.
Some teas are best when you honey them just a little.
After roasting, he often honeys the nuts to give them a glossy finish.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhʌni / (AmE), / ˈhʌni / (BrE)
A person who is loved or dear; used as a term of endearment.
term of endearment for a loved one

Examples

Honey, can you please help me with the groceries?
He called her honey as he kissed her goodbye.
Don't worry, honey; everything will be alright.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: darling - a person dearly loved; term of endearment
  • Synonym: dear - a loved or beloved person; term of endearment
  • Synonym: sweetheart - a person someone loves deeply; term of endearment

Etymology

Old English *hunig* (honey, the substance), later figuratively to mean 'darling'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈhʌni / (AmE), / ˈhʌni / (BrE)
A sweet, thick, yellowish liquid made by bees from the nectar of flowers, used as food.
sweet liquid made by bees

Examples

She likes to put honey in her tea instead of sugar.
The bees worked hard to fill the hive with honey.
Drizzle some honey over the pancakes before serving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syrup - a thick sweet liquid made from sugar and water, often used in cooking

Etymology

Old English hunig, related to Dutch honing, German Honig.
