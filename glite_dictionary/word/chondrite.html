chondrite

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnˌdraɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.draɪt / (BrE)
a stony meteorite containing chondrules, typically the most common type of meteorite found on Earth
a stony meteorite with chondrules

Examples

Scientists discovered a rare chondrite in the desert, helping them study the early solar system.
The visible chondrules in the chondrite sample fascinated the geology students.
Unlike achondrites, a chondrite contains round mineral grains that can be seen with the naked eye.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: achondrite - a stony meteorite that does not contain chondrules

Etymology

From Greek 'chondros' meaning 'grain' or 'lump', referencing the chondrules within the meteorite.
