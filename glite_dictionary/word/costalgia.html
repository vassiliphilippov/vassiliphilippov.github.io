costalgia

1. NOUN

/ kɒˈstæl.dʒə / (AmE), / kɒˈstæl.dʒi.ə / (BrE)
Pain in the chest caused by inflammation of the muscles between the ribs.
chest pain from inflamed intercostal muscles
medical

Examples

He complained of costalgia after lifting heavy boxes at work.
A physician diagnosed her with costalgia due to muscle inflammation between her ribs.
Persistent costalgia can significantly affect a patient's ability to breathe deeply.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intercostal neuralgia - pain caused by irritation or damage to the nerves between the ribs

Etymology

From Latin 'costa' (rib) + Greek 'algia' (pain).
