astrophysics

1. NOUN

/ ˌæstrəˈfɪzɪks / (AmE), / ˌæstrəʊˈfɪzɪks / (BrE)
The branch of astronomy that deals with the physical and chemical properties and behaviors of celestial bodies and phenomena.
study of physical properties of celestial bodies

Examples

She decided to major in astrophysics after attending a lecture on black holes.
Recent discoveries in astrophysics have changed our understanding of the universe.
His research in astrophysics focuses on the chemical composition of distant stars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astronomy - The scientific study of celestial bodies, space, and the universe as a whole
  • Synonym: physics - The science of matter, energy, and the interactions between them

Etymology

From Greek astron (star) and physics (the science of matter and energy).
