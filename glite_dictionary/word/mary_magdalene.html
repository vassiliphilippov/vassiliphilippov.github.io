Mary Magdalene

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɛri ˈmæɡdəˌlin / (AmE), / ˌmeəri ˈmæɡdəlɪn / (BrE)
a woman in the New Testament who was healed by Jesus and became one of his most devoted followers
disciple of Jesus in the New Testament

Examples

Many artists have depicted Mary Magdalene in paintings and sculptures throughout history.
According to the Gospels, Mary Magdalene was present at the crucifixion and the resurrection of Jesus.
Scholars debate the various portrayals of Mary Magdalene in religious texts and popular culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disciple - a follower or student of a teacher, leader, or philosopher

Etymology

From Magdalene, meaning 'from Magdala,' a town on the Sea of Galilee. Name appears in the New Testament.
