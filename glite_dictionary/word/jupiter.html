Jupiter

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒuːpɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːpɪtə / (BrE)
In Roman mythology, the supreme god, regarded as the ruler of heaven and god of thunder and lightning; the counterpart of Greek Zeus.
Roman supreme god; ruler of gods

Examples

In ancient Rome, people would pray to Jupiter for protection and guidance.
Many stories recount Jupiter hurling thunderbolts at those who defied him.
The temple dedicated to Jupiter stood atop the Capitoline Hill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zeus - supreme god in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Latin Iuppiter, possibly from Proto-Indo-European words meaning 'father' and 'sky'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈdʒuːpɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːpɪtə / (BrE)
the largest planet in the Solar System, fifth from the sun, known for its many satellites and brightness in the night sky
largest planet in the Solar System

Examples

Jupiter can often be seen shining brightly in the night sky with the naked eye.
The Great Red Spot is a giant storm on Jupiter that has lasted for centuries.
Scientists have discovered dozens of moons orbiting Jupiter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: planet - a celestial body orbiting a star, massive enough to be rounded by its own gravity

Etymology

From Latin Iuppiter (Jupiter), chief god in Roman mythology.
