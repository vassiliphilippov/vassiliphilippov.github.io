quadrillion

1. NOUN

/ kwɑːˈdrɪljən / (AmE), / kwɒˈdrɪljən / (BrE)
The number 1,000,000,000,000,000; one thousand trillion or 10 to the power of 15.
the number 1,000,000,000,000,000; 10¹⁵

Examples

If everyone on earth had a billion dollars, the total wealth would still be less than one quadrillion.
Scientists estimate that there are over a quadrillion individual ants on the planet.
It would take thousands of computers working together to solve a quadrillion calculations in a minute.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: trillion - the number 1,000,000,000,000; 10¹²
  • Degree Higher: quintillion - the number 1,000,000,000,000,000,000; 10¹⁸

Etymology

From Latin *quadri-* (four) + *-illion* (as in million, billion), modelled after earlier numerical terms.

2. NOUN

/ kwɑˈdrɪliən / (AmE), / kwɒˈdrɪliən / (BrE)
the number represented as 1 followed by 24 zeros; in the UK, equal to a million millions of millions (10^24)
a number equal to 1 with 24 zeros (UK)
british

Examples

In historical British texts, a quadrillion refers to a 1 with 24 zeros.
The concept of a quadrillion is used in astronomical calculations in the UK.
A quadrillion is a thousand raised to the power of eight in the British system.

Synset Examples

in England they call a septillion a ‘quadrillion’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quadrillion - the number represented as 1 followed by 15 zeros (10^15) in the US
  • Degree Higher: quintillion - the number represented as 1 followed by 30 zeros (UK) or 18 zeros (US)
  • Degree Lower: trillion - the number represented as 1 followed by 18 zeros (UK) or 12 zeros (US)

Etymology

From French 'quadrillion', based on Latin 'quadri-' (four) + '-illion' (as in million, billion).
