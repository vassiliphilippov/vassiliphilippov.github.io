ameba

1. NOUN

/ əˈmibə / (AmE), / əˈmiːbə / (BrE)
a single-celled freshwater, marine, or parasitic organism that moves and feeds by forming temporary projections of cytoplasm called pseudopods
single-celled organism with pseudopods

Examples

The ameba moves by extending its pseudopods toward food particles.
Scientists observed the ameba under the microscope as it changed its shape.
Some types of ameba can cause illnesses in humans if they enter the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amoeba - variant spelling of 'ameba', same organism

Etymology

From Greek amoibē (change), referring to the organism's changing shape.
