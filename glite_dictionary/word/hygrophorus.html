Hygrophorus

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈɡrɑːfərəs / (AmE), / haɪˈɡrɒfərəs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Hygrophoraceae, comprising species commonly known as woodwaxes or waxy caps.
genus of fungi (waxy caps)

Examples

Several species within the genus Hygrophorus are prized by mushroom collectors for their distinctive appearance.
The Hygrophorus genus includes fungi often found in woodland areas during autumn.
Researchers have recently discovered a new Hygrophorus species in northern Europe.

Etymology

From Greek hygros (moist) + phoros (bearing), referring to the moist texture of these fungi.

2. NOUN

/ haɪˈɡrɑːfərəs / (AmE), / hɪˈɡrɒfərəs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Hygrophoraceae, whose members are commonly known as waxy caps or woodwaxes.
genus of fungi called waxy caps

Examples

The Hygrophorus genus includes mushrooms with waxy, moist caps.
Many species in the Hygrophorus group are found in hardwood forests.
Mycologists often study Hygrophorus for their distinctive spore prints.

Etymology

From Greek 'hygros' (wet, moist) and 'phoros' (bearing), referring to their moist caps.
