homecoming

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊmˌkʌmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmˌkʌmɪŋ / (BrE)
an annual event at a school or university for alumni to return and celebrate with current students
annual alumni reunion at a school or university

Examples

Thousands of alumni return to campus for homecoming every fall.
The university's homecoming parade is a highlight of the year.
She was nominated for homecoming queen by her classmates.

Etymology

From 'home' + 'coming'; originally referring to the return of alumni to their alma mater.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊmˌkʌmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmˌkʌmɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of returning to one's home or to a place where one previously lived or belonged, especially after being away for a period of time.
the act of returning home

Examples

She organized a small party to celebrate her brother's homecoming after a year abroad.
The soldier's homecoming was an emotional event for the whole family.
After graduating, his homecoming to his hometown was filled with mixed feelings.

Synset Examples

on his return from Australia we gave him a welcoming party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: return - the act of coming or going back to a place

Etymology

From 'home' + 'coming', first used in the late 19th century.
