Edecrin

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛdəkrɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛdəkrɪn / (BrE)
A brand name for a diuretic drug (ethacrynic acid) used to treat edema by promoting the excretion of excess fluid from the body.
a brand of diuretic medication
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Edecrin to help reduce her swelling.
Edecrin can be effective when other diuretics are not suitable.
Patients taking Edecrin should monitor their electrolyte levels closely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethacrynic acid - a diuretic drug used to treat edema
