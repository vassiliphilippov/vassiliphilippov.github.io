Loxia

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑksiə / (AmE), / ˈlɒksɪə / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the finch family, known as crossbills, characterized by their crossed mandibles adapted for extracting seeds from conifer cones.
genus of birds called crossbills

Examples

The genus Loxia is distinguished by its unique bill shape.
During our hike, we spotted several Loxia feeding on pine cones.
Members of the Loxia genus are commonly referred to as crossbills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crossbill - a type of finch with a beak adapted for extracting seeds

Etymology

From New Latin Loxia, from Ancient Greek loxos (slanting, crosswise).
