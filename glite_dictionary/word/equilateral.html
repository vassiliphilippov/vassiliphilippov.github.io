equilateral

1. ADJ

/ ˌikwəˈlætərəl / (AmE), / ˌiːkwɪˈlætərəl / (BrE)
(of a shape or figure) having all sides of equal length.
having all sides of equal length

Examples

An equilateral triangle has three equal sides and three equal angles.
The architect designed an equilateral courtyard for symmetry and balance.
Each face of a regular tetrahedron is an equilateral triangle.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: isosceles - having two sides of equal length (of a triangle)
  • Antonym: scalene - having all sides of different lengths (of a triangle)

Etymology

From Latin 'aequilateralis', from 'aequus' (equal) + 'latus' (side).

2. NOUN

/ ˌikwəˈlæt̬ɚəl / (AmE), / ˌiːkwɪˈlætərəl / (BrE)
A geometric figure, especially a polygon, whose sides are all equal in length.
figure with all sides equal

Examples

A regular hexagon is an equilateral because all its sides are of equal length.
The teacher asked the students to draw an equilateral on graph paper.
Among polygons, the square is a common example of an equilateral.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: isosceles - triangle with two sides of equal length

Etymology

From Latin æquilateralis, from æquus (equal) + latus (side).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
