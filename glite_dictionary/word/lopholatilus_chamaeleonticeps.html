Lopholatilus chamaeleonticeps

1. NOUN

/ ˌloʊfoʊləˌtaɪləs kəˌmiːliːˈɑːntɪˌsɛps / (AmE), / ˌlɒfələˌtaɪləs kəˌmiːliːˈɒntɪˌsɛps / (BrE)
A large, yellow-spotted violet food fish found in warm deep waters, commonly known as the tilefish.
species of tilefish

Examples

Commercial fishermen often catch Lopholatilus chamaeleonticeps in the deep waters off the Atlantic coast.
The vibrant colors of Lopholatilus chamaeleonticeps make it a prized species among marine biologists.
Many restaurants serve dishes featuring Lopholatilus chamaeleonticeps due to its delicate flavor.

Etymology

From Greek 'lophos' (crest) + Latin 'latilus' (tile) and 'chamaeleon' (chameleon) + 'ceps' (headed), reflecting its appearance.
