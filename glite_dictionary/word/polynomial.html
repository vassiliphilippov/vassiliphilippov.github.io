polynomial

1. ADJ

/ ˌpoʊ.lɪˈnoʊ.mi.əl / (AmE), / ˌpɒ.lɪˈnəʊ.mi.əl / (BrE)
having the form or nature of a polynomial, especially as related to algebraic expressions consisting of several terms.
having the form of a polynomial

Examples

The equation can be written in a polynomial form for easier analysis.
We studied the behavior of several polynomial functions in the seminar.
Only polynomial time algorithms are considered efficient in computational complexity.

Synset Examples

a polynomial expression

Related Terms

  • Synonym: algebraic - relating to, involving, or using algebra

Etymology

From Latin 'polynomia', from Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'nomos' (part, term).

WordNet ID: oewn-03112208-a

2. NOUN

/ pəˈnoʊmiˌnæl / (AmE), / pɒˈnɒmɪnəl / (BrE)
A mathematical expression consisting of the sum of several terms, each consisting of a variable raised to a non-negative integer power and multiplied by a coefficient.
mathematical expression of terms with variables and coefficients

Examples

The equation can be simplified to a polynomial in two variables.
She learned how to add and subtract polynomials in her math class.
Finding the roots of a polynomial is an important concept in algebra.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: quadratic - a second-degree polynomial; a polynomial of degree two
  • Degree Higher: cubic - a third-degree polynomial; a polynomial of degree three

Etymology

From Latin 'polynoma', from Greek 'poly' (many) + 'nomos' (part, term).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05870439-n
Synset POS: n
