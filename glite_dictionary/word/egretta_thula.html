Egretta thula

1. NOUN

/ iˈɡrɛtə ˈθuːlə / (AmE), / ɪˈɡrɛtə ˈθuːlə / (BrE)
A small white egret species native to the Americas, known as the snowy egret, with slender black legs and yellow feet.
species of small white egret (snowy egret)

Examples

The Egretta thula is easily recognized by its bright yellow feet and slender black bill.
During migration, large flocks of Egretta thula can be seen along the coastal wetlands.
Researchers are studying the nesting habits of Egretta thula to better understand their breeding success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snowy egret - Common name for Egretta thula, a small white heron

Etymology

From the genus name Egretta (from French 'aigrette', meaning 'little egret') and species name 'thula' of uncertain origin.
