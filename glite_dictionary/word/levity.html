levity

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛv.ə.t̬i / (AmE), / ˈlɛv.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
behavior or speech that shows a lack of seriousness, especially when seriousness is expected; lightness or humor.
lack of seriousness; lightness or humor

Examples

Her attempt at levity during the tense meeting was not well received.
A little levity can help lighten the mood in stressful situations.
The professor appreciated the student's levity after a long and difficult lecture.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seriousness - the quality or state of being serious
  • Synonym: frivolity - lack of seriousness; lightheartedness

Etymology

From Latin levitas (lightness), from levis (light).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛvəti / (AmE), / ˈlɛvəti / (BrE)
behavior or speech that shows a lack of seriousness, especially when it is inappropriate or unwelcome.
lack of seriousness; inappropriate lightness

Examples

Her attempt at levity during the tense meeting fell flat.
Sometimes a little levity can help ease difficult conversations.
He was criticized for bringing levity to such a solemn occasion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seriousness - the quality of being serious or earnest
  • Synonym: frivolity - lack of seriousness; lightheartedness or silliness

Etymology

From Latin 'levitas' (lightness), from 'levis' (light in weight).
