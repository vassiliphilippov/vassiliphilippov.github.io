Jonah

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒoʊnə / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊnə / (BrE)
A book in the Old Testament that tells the story of Jonah and the whale.
Old Testament book about Jonah

Examples

Many children first hear the story of Jonah and the whale in Sunday school.
The Book of Jonah is a short yet powerful part of the Old Testament.
Scholars have debated the symbolism of the fish in Jonah for centuries.

Etymology

From Hebrew Yonah, meaning 'dove', the name of the prophet in the Bible.

2. PROPN

/ ˈdʒoʊnə / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊnə / (BrE)
(in the Bible) a Hebrew prophet who was swallowed by a great fish after trying to avoid God's command, and later delivered safely to land.
prophet swallowed by a whale in the Bible

Examples

The story of Jonah and the whale is often taught in Sunday school.
Many scholars debate the symbolic meaning of Jonah's journey and rescue.
In literature, being a Jonah may refer to someone who brings bad luck, based on his tale.

Etymology

From Hebrew Yonah, meaning 'dove'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdʒoʊnə / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊnə / (BrE)
a person believed to bring bad luck to those around them
person thought to bring bad luck

Examples

Whenever Mark joins our poker game, someone calls him a Jonah because he never seems to win.
After the team's terrible season, fans began to view the new coach as a Jonah.
They were wary of inviting Sam on the boat, afraid he might be a Jonah.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jinx - a person or thing that brings bad luck

Etymology

From the biblical prophet Jonah, who brought trouble to those with him by disobeying God.
