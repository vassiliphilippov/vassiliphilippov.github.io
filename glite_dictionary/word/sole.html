sole

1. ADJ

/ soʊl / (AmE), / səʊl / (BrE)
(of something) not shared with anyone else; belonging to or used by only one person or group.
not shared with others; only one

Examples

She was given the sole responsibility for managing the project.
He is the sole owner of the company.
The committee granted her the sole right to make decisions about the event.

Synset Examples

they have exclusive use of the machine
sole rights of publication

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclusive - (of something) not shared; restricted to one person or group

Etymology

From Latin solus (alone, only).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02160400-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ soʊl / (AmE), / səʊl / (BrE)
the underside of a shoe, boot, or other item of footwear, or the lower surface of a golf club
underside of footwear or a golf club

Examples

My boots have thick rubber soles to protect my feet from sharp rocks.
The sole of his shoe wore out after months of daily walking.
He cleaned the sole of his golf club before taking his next shot.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upper - the top part of a shoe, covering the foot

Etymology

From Latin 'solea' (sandal), from 'solum' (bottom, ground).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04266092-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ soʊl / (AmE), / səʊl / (BrE)
a flatfish with both eyes usually on the right side, widely found in warm seas and valued as food, especially in Europe
a type of edible flatfish

Examples

Fresh sole is considered a delicacy in many European restaurants.
He ordered grilled sole with a lemon butter sauce for dinner.
The fisherman caught several sole off the coast this morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flounder - a general term for several similar flatfish
  • Synonym: halibut - a large flatfish with both eyes on the right side, edible

Etymology

From Old French *sole*, from Latin *solea* (a kind of flatfish, originally 'sandal'), referring to its flat shape.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02666479-n
Synset POS: n
