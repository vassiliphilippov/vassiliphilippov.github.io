glamorisation

1. NOUN

/ ɡlæmɚaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡlæməraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something or someone appear more glamorous or attractive, often in a superficial or misleading way
making something seem more glamorous

Examples

The glamorisation of violence in movies can have a negative impact on young audiences.
Many people criticise the glamorisation of unhealthy lifestyles in celebrity culture.
The article condemned the media's glamorisation of drug use among musicians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glamorization - American spelling of glamorisation
  • Synonym: idealisation - the act of regarding or representing something as perfect or better than in reality

Etymology

From glamour + -isation, based on the verb 'glamorise'.
