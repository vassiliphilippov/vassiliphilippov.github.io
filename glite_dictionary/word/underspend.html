to underspend

1. VERB

/ ˌʌndərˈspɛnd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈspɛnd / (BrE)
to spend less money than the amount allocated or available, especially in a budget
spend less than budgeted

Examples

The government managed to underspend its annual budget by several million dollars.
If you regularly underspend on groceries, you can add the savings to your emergency fund.
Last year, the department underspent, so they have extra funds available.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to overspend - to spend more than the available or allocated amount

Etymology

From the prefix 'under-' (meaning 'below, less than') + 'spend'.

2. VERB

/ ˌʌndərˈspɛnd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈspɛnd / (BrE)
to spend less money than was planned or than is usual in a particular period or context
spend less than planned or usual

Examples

The school managed to underspend its annual budget by several thousand dollars.
If you consistently underspend, you'll have money left over for emergency expenses.
The department has underspent on training this year, which may affect staff performance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to overspend - to spend more than is planned or available

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'under-' meaning 'less than', and 'spend'.
