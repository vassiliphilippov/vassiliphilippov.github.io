sagging

1. ADJ

/ ˈsæɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsæɡɪŋ / (BrE)
Hanging down loosely, often as a result of exhaustion, weakness, or loss of strength.
hanging down loosely

Examples

The old couch had sagging cushions that made it uncomfortable to sit on.
After a long day, her shoulders were sagging with fatigue.
The sagging roof needed urgent repairs to prevent a collapse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drooping - hanging or bending downward limply
  • Antonym: taut - pulled tight, not slack or sagging

Etymology

From the verb 'sag', probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian 'sagga' (to sink, subside).
