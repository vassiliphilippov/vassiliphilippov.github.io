gelatin

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛlətən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛlətɪn / (BrE)
A thin, translucent sheet or membrane, often colored, placed over stage lights to create different lighting effects in theater.
colored sheet for stage lighting effects

Examples

The lighting crew fitted a red gelatin over the spotlight for the dramatic scene.
Each gelatin was carefully labeled according to its color for quick changes during the play.
To achieve the sunset effect, a combination of orange and yellow gelatin was used on the stage lights.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛlətən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛlətɪn / (BrE)
a colorless, tasteless, water-soluble protein obtained from animal tissues such as bones and skin, used as a gelling agent in food and in various industrial and medical applications
gelling agent from animal tissues

Examples

The dessert was made with gelatin to give it a firm texture.
Some people avoid products containing gelatin because it is derived from animals.
The pharmaceutical capsules are coated with gelatin to make them easier to swallow.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agar - a substance used as a vegetarian substitute for gelatin

Etymology

From French 'gélatine', from Italian 'gelatina', from 'gelato' meaning frozen, from Latin 'gelare' to freeze.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛlətən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛlətɪn / (BrE)
An edible jelly, often sweet or pungent, made with gelatin and used as a dessert or as a base for salads or a coating for foods.
edible jelly made with gelatin

Examples

The chef prepared a colorful gelatin dessert for the children’s party.
Each salad was topped with a layer of savory gelatin for added texture.
She used fruit juice to flavor the gelatin before letting it set in the refrigerator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jelly - a soft, elastic food made with fruit juice and gelatin or similar agents

Etymology

From French 'gélatine', from Latin 'gelare' (to freeze, congeal).
