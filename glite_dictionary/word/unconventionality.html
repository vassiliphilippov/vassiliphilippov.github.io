unconventionality

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˌkənˌvɛnʃəˈnæləti / (AmE), / ʌnˌkɒnvɛnʃəˈnælɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of not following traditional or accepted ways; the characteristic of being unconventional or unorthodox
quality of being unconventional or unorthodox

Examples

Her unconventionality made her artwork both intriguing and controversial in the traditional art world.
The unconventionality of his ideas challenged everyone in the meeting to think differently.
In literature, unconventionality often leads to innovative and memorable storytelling.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orthodoxy - conformity to traditional or established beliefs and customs
  • Synonym: unorthodoxy - the quality of not conforming to established doctrine or practices

Etymology

Formed from 'unconventional' (not based on accepted standards) + '-ity' (forming nouns of quality).

2. NOUN

/ ˌʌnˌkənˌvɛnʃəˈnælɪti / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌkɒnvɛnʃəˈnælɪti / (BrE)
the quality of not following established customs, rules, or behaviors; originality by not conforming to convention
quality of being unconventional

Examples

Her unconventionality set her apart in the traditional art world.
The designer's unconventionality challenged industry norms and inspired young creatives.
Some admired his unconventionality, while others saw it as mere eccentricity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conventionality - the quality of following established customs or norms
  • Synonym: originality - the ability to think or create in an independent and novel manner

Etymology

From un- (not) + conventional + -ity (quality or state).
