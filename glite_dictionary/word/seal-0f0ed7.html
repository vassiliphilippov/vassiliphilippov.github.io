seal

1. NOUN

/ siːl / (AmE), / siːl / (BrE)
an embossed emblem, stamp, or impression affixed to a document to prove its authenticity or to close it securely
official stamp or emblem for documents

Examples

The contract was made official with the company's seal at the bottom.
A notary public must apply an official seal to the certificate.
She broke the wax seal to read the confidential letter.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

From Old French 'seal', from Latin 'sigillum' (small sign or seal).
