excommunication

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkskəˌmjunɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkskəmjuːnɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
The state of being officially excluded from participation in the sacraments and services of a Christian Church as a punishment.
the state of being excommunicated (religious)

Examples

After the scandal, the priest lived the rest of his life in excommunication.
She feared the threat of excommunication would discourage others from dissenting.
Historical records detail the severe consequences of excommunication in medieval Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to excommunicate - to officially exclude someone from participation in the sacraments and services of the Christian Church

Etymology

From Latin excommunicatio, from ex- (out of) + communicatio (communion, sharing).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛks.kəˌmjunɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛks.kəˌmjuːnɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the formal act of officially excluding someone from participating in the sacraments and services of the Christian Church
formal exclusion from a church

Examples

The priest announced the excommunication of the heretic before the gathered congregation.
Many feared excommunication if they challenged the authority of the church.
His public dissent eventually led to his excommunication from the religious order.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banishment - expulsion from a community or place as punishment
  • Antonym: reconciliation - restoration to church membership after exclusion

Etymology

From Late Latin excommunicatio (removal from communion).
