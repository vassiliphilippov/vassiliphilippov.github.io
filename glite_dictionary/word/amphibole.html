amphibole

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmfəˌboʊl / (AmE), / ˈamfɪbəʊl / (BrE)
any of a group of dark-colored silicate minerals containing iron and magnesium, commonly found in igneous and metamorphic rocks
group of dark-colored silicate minerals

Examples

Geologists identified amphibole crystals embedded in the volcanic rock sample.
The presence of amphibole often indicates the rock has undergone metamorphism.
Different varieties of amphibole can be distinguished by their color and chemical composition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hornblende - a common variety of amphibole

Etymology

From French 'amphibole', from Greek 'amphibolos' (ambiguous), describing the variable composition of these minerals.
