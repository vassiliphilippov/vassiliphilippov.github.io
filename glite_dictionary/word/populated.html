populated

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɑː.pjə.leɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɒp.jʊ.leɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
inhabited or filled with people, animals, or other living beings
inhabited; having inhabitants

Examples

The country is sparsely populated in the north but densely populated in the south.
Scientists discovered a remote island populated mainly by rare bird species.
Many urban areas are heavily populated compared to rural regions.

Synset Examples

the area is well populated
forests populated with all kinds of wild life

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unpopulated - having no inhabitants

Etymology

From Latin 'populatus', past participle of 'populare' (to populate), from 'populus' (people).
