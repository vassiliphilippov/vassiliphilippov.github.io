inordinate

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈɔrdɪnət / (AmE), / ɪnˈɔːdɪnət / (BrE)
Much greater or more than is reasonable, normal, or expected.
excessive; beyond what is reasonable

Examples

Her inordinate appetite for sweets worried her parents.
The inordinate amount of paperwork delayed the project.
He took inordinate pride in his vast collection of stamps.

Synset Examples

excessive charges
a book of inordinate length
his dress stops just short of undue elegance
unreasonable demands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excessive - more than is necessary, normal, or desirable
  • Synonym: undue - more than is appropriate or normal
  • Antonym: moderate - within reasonable limits; not excessive

Etymology

From Latin 'inordinatus' (unordered, excessive), from 'in-' (not) + 'ordinatus' (ordered).
