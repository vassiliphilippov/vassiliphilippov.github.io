youth

1. NOUN

/ juθ / (AmE), / juːθ / (BrE)
young people considered as a group, especially as distinguished from older people
young people collectively

Examples

The government created new programs to support youth in education and employment.
Many members of the youth took to the streets to protest the policy changes.
In some countries, youth face high levels of unemployment and social challenges.

Synset Examples

rock music appeals to the young
youth everywhere rises in revolt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: young - people who are young considered collectively

Etymology

From Old English 'geoguþ' (the period between childhood and full adulthood).

2. NOUN

/ juːθ / (AmE), / juːθ / (BrE)
the period of life between childhood and adulthood, typically characterized by growth, development, and immaturity
period between childhood and adulthood

Examples

She spent most of her youth dreaming of becoming a writer.
The memories of my youth are filled with summer adventures and laughter.
During his youth, he traveled extensively and learned several languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adolescence - the transitional stage from childhood to adulthood
  • Degree Lower: childhood - the period of being a child
  • Degree Higher: adulthood - the state of being fully grown or mature

Etymology

From Old English 'geoguþ' (youth, young people), related to German 'Jugend'.

Synset POS: n

6. NOUN

/ juθ / (AmE), / juːθ / (BrE)
the quality of being young, especially with freshness, vigor, and vitality; the state or appearance of being youthful

Examples

Related Terms
