Platystemon californicus

1. PROPN

/ ˌplætɪˈstɛmən ˌkælɪˈfɔrnɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌplætɪˈstiːmɒn ˌkælɪˈfɔːnɪkəs / (BrE)
a plant native to California, having small pale yellow flowers, also known as creamcups
California plant with yellow flowers

Examples

The wildflowers included Platystemon californicus, which blanketed the field in pale yellow.
Botanists often refer to Platystemon californicus as 'creamcups' because of its delicate flowers.
In spring, Platystemon californicus thrives on California's grassy hillsides.

Etymology

From Greek 'platys' (broad) and 'stemon' (thread, stamen); 'californicus' from California, its native region.
