plaint

1. NOUN

/ pleɪnt / (AmE), / pleɪnt / (BrE)
(British, law) a written statement submitted to a court outlining the grounds of a complaint and requesting redress of a grievance.
legal complaint submitted to a court (Brit.)
legal british

Examples

The solicitor filed a plaint in the county court on behalf of the client.
Under older British law, a plaint was often the first step in civil proceedings.
The judge reviewed the plaint before deciding whether to admit the case.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complaint - a formal expression of dissatisfaction or accusation brought before a court

Etymology

From Old French *plainte* (a complaint), from Latin *plangere* (to lament).

2. NOUN

/ pleɪnt / (AmE), / pleɪnt / (BrE)
a cry or expression of sorrow and grief; a lament
cry of sorrow or grief

Examples

The haunting plaint of the lone bird echoed through the forest at dusk.
Her plaint was met with silent sympathy by her friends.
The poet gave voice to his plaint through verses filled with melancholy.

Synset Examples

their pitiful laments could be heard throughout the ward

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lament - an expression of grief or sorrow
  • Synonym: dirge - a mournful song, especially for the dead
  • Antonym: jubilation - a feeling of great happiness or triumph

Etymology

From Old French 'plainte' (lament), from Latin 'plangere' (to strike, bewail).
