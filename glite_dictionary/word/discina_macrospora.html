Discina macrospora

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsaɪnə ˌmækroʊˈspɔrə / (AmE), / dɪˈsiːnə ˌmakrəʊˈspɔːrə / (BrE)
A species of fungus with a flat or saucer-shaped fertile body, brown on the upper surface, and a short stalk; not recommended for eating.
A species of disk-shaped brown fungus

Examples

The Discina macrospora was growing in clusters near the edge of the pine forest.
Despite its intriguing appearance, foragers know that Discina macrospora is not recommended for consumption.
Botanists often note the distinctive brown upper surface of Discina macrospora when identifying fungi in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Discina - genus of fungi with disk- or cup-shaped fruiting bodies

Etymology

From Latin: 'discina' (relating to a disk) and 'macrospora' (having large spores).
