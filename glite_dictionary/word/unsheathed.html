unsheathed

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈʃiðd / (AmE), / ʌnˈʃiːðd / (BrE)
not having a protective covering; exposed or uncovered, especially referring to something normally enclosed in a sheath or covering.
not covered; exposed

Examples

The technician replaced the unsheathed wires to prevent electrical hazards.
His sword hung at his side, completely unsheathed and glinting in the sunlight.
Be careful when handling unsheathed scalpels in the laboratory.

Synset Examples

unsheathed cables
a bare blade

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sheathed - having a protective covering or sheath

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'un-' (not) + 'sheathed' (provided with a sheath).
