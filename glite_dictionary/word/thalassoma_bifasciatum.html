Thalassoma bifasciatum

1. NOUN

/ θəˌlæs.əˈmə baɪˌfæsiˈeɪtəm / (AmE), / θəˌlæs.əmə bɪˌfæsiˈɑːtəm / (BrE)
A small species of Atlantic wrasse, notable for the male's brilliant blue head, found in coral reefs of the western Atlantic Ocean.
species of small Atlantic wrasse

Examples

The coral reefs were teeming with Thalassoma bifasciatum darting among the rocks.
Marine biologists studied the mating behavior of Thalassoma bifasciatum in its natural habitat.
A brilliant blue head distinguishes the male Thalassoma bifasciatum from the females and juveniles.

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Thalassoma' (genus name) from Greek ‘thalassa’ (sea) + 'sōma' (body), and 'bifasciatum' from Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'fascia' (band), referring to its markings.
