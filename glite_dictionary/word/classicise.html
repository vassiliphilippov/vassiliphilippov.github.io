to classicise

1. VERB

/ ˈklæsɪsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈklæsɪsaɪz / (BrE)
To make something conform to a classical style, or to give it the qualities of a classic.
make classic or classical
british

Examples

The architect tried to classicise the design by adding Roman columns.
Authors in the 18th century often sought to classicise English literature.
Some modern painters attempt to classicise their art with traditional techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to classicize - to make classic or classical (US spelling)

Etymology

Formed from 'classic' + '-ise', following the pattern of British English verb formation.
