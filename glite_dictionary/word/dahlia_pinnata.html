Dahlia pinnata

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪliə pɪˈnɑːtə / (AmE), / ˈdalɪə pɪˈnɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of dahlia with tuberous roots and large, brightly colored flowers, native to Mexico, Central America, and Colombia, and widely cultivated as an ornamental plant.
species of dahlia with showy flowers

Examples

The Dahlia pinnata is prized in gardens for its spectacular, multi-colored blooms.
Many horticulturists have cultivated new varieties from the original Dahlia pinnata.
She entered her best Dahlia pinnata blossom in the annual flower show.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dahlia - a plant of the genus Dahlia, with tuberous roots and brightly colored flowers

Etymology

Dahlia named after Swedish botanist Anders Dahl; pinnata from Latin 'pinnatus' (feathered or finned).
