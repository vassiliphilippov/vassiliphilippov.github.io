cycloolefin

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkloʊˈoʊləˌfɪn / (AmE), / ˌsʌɪkləʊˈəʊlɪfɪn / (BrE)
a hydrocarbon compound containing a closed ring of carbon atoms and one or more double bonds, but lacking aromatic character
cyclic hydrocarbon with double bonds, non-aromatic

Examples

Polyethylene can be modified with cycloolefin copolymers to improve its properties.
The unique structure of a cycloolefin affects its reactivity in certain chemical reactions.
Scientists studied the polymerization of various cycloolefin monomers to develop new materials.

Synset Examples

Due to geometrical considerations, smaller cycloalkenes are almost always the cis isomers, and the term cis tends to be omitted from the names.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alkene - a hydrocarbon containing a carbon-carbon double bond
  • Degree Lower: cycloalkane - a cyclic hydrocarbon with only single bonds
  • Antonym: arene - an aromatic hydrocarbon

Etymology

From 'cyclo-' (circle, ring) and 'olefin' (a type of alkene hydrocarbon).
