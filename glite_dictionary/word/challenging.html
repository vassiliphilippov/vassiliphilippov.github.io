challenging

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃæl.ɪn.dʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃæl.ɪn.dʒɪŋ / (BrE)
stimulating interest or thought; making you think deeply or reconsider ideas
stimulating thinking or interest

Examples

The professor assigned a challenging novel that made us question our assumptions.
She always chooses the most challenging projects to broaden her perspective.
That documentary was intellectually challenging and sparked a lot of debate.

Synset Examples

a challenging hypothesis
a thought-provoking book

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thought-provoking - causing you to think deeply about something

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʃælɪndʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃælɪndʒɪŋ / (BrE)
requiring great effort or determination; testing your abilities or resources.
demanding significant effort or skill

Examples

She enjoys working on challenging projects that push her skills to the limit.
The hike up the mountain was both beautiful and challenging.
Completing the puzzle proved far more challenging than I had expected.

Synset Examples

ambitious schedule
performed the most challenging task without a mistake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demanding - requiring much effort or attention
  • Antonym: easy - not difficult; needing little effort
  • Synonym: arduous - involving or requiring strenuous effort

Etymology

Formed from 'challenge' + '-ing', in use since the late 19th century.

3. ADJ

/ ˈtʃælɪndʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃælɪndʒɪŋ / (BrE)
provocative in a way that stimulates interest, curiosity, or disagreement, sometimes in a disturbing manner
provoking or inviting reaction, often unsettling

Examples

Her challenging remark sparked a heated debate among the panelists.
He met my gaze with a challenging smile that made me uneasy.
The artist's challenging work forced viewers to question their own beliefs.

Synset Examples

an intriguing smile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provocative - causing thought, discussion, or reaction
  • Degree Higher: disturbing - causing discomfort, worry, or concern
  • Degree Lower: interesting - arousing curiosity or attention
