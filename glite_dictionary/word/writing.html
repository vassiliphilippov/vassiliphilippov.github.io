writing

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈraɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity or skill of expressing ideas, stories, or facts in written form
the act of forming words in written form

Examples

She devoted each morning to her writing before the rest of the house woke up.
His writing often reflects his experiences living abroad.
The author has been praised for the clarity of her writing.

Synset Examples

she did the thinking while he did the writing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: composition - the act or process of creating written works

Etymology

From Old English *writan* (to write).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00615511-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈraɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈraɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity or process of creating books, stories, articles, or other texts by putting words together in written form
the act of creating written works

Examples

Writing was a form of therapy for him during difficult times.
Her dedication to writing led to the publication of three successful novels.
He spends several hours each morning on his writing before starting other tasks.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: composition - the act of creating written works, especially as a literary activity

Etymology
