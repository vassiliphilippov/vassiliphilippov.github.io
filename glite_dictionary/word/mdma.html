MDMA

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛmˌdiːˌɛmˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛmˌdiːˌɛmˈeɪ / (BrE)
a synthetic stimulant and hallucinogenic drug, chemically related to amphetamine, used illicitly for its euphoric effects
illicit synthetic stimulant and hallucinogen

Examples

Police seized several tablets of MDMA during the nightclub raid.
Some people mistakenly believe MDMA is harmless because of its popularity at music festivals.
Medical researchers are studying whether MDMA could have potential in treating post-traumatic stress disorder.

Synset Examples

MDMA is often used at parties because it enables partygoers to remain active for long periods of time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecstasy - street name for MDMA when used recreationally
  • Synonym: amphetamine - a related stimulant drug

Etymology

Acronym of 3,4-methylenedioxymethamphetamine, its chemical name.
