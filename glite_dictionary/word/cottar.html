cottar

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.tər / (AmE), / ˈkɒt.ə / (BrE)
A peasant farmer, especially one in the Scottish Highlands who holds a small cottage with a plot of land.
Scottish Highland peasant farmer

Examples

The Highland cottar worked his small plot of land to support his family.
Many cottars faced hardship during the Highland Clearances.
The museum display includes a reconstructed cottar's cottage from the eighteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotter - a peasant farmer, chiefly in Scotland; variant spelling of 'cottar'

Etymology

From Middle English cottar, from Old English cot (cottage) + -ar, influenced by Scots usage.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːtər / (AmE), / ˈkɒtə / (BrE)
a fastener consisting of a wedge or pin that is inserted through a slot to hold two other pieces together
wedge or pin fastener

Examples

The axle was secured by a cottar that prevented it from slipping out of place.
You need to remove the cottar before disassembling the wheel.
A hammered-in cottar keeps the crank firmly attached in many old bicycles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotter pin - a split pin used to fasten parts together

Etymology

From Scots cotter, possibly related to 'cotter pin', itself from 'cot', meaning wedge-shaped piece.

Source: WordNet
