centralist

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛntrəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrəlɪst / (BrE)
Supporting or advocating the concentration of power and authority in a central organization or government, rather than regional or local authorities.
one who advocates centralization

Examples

The party's centralist approach was criticized for ignoring local needs.
She is a known centralist, favoring strong central government control.
Debates between centralist and federalist ideologies have shaped national policy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: federalist - person supporting federal system with division of powers
  • Antonym: decentralist - person advocating decentralization

Etymology

Formed from 'central' + -ist, indicating an advocate or supporter of centralization.
