master

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæstər / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstə / (BrE)
a person highly skilled in a particular trade or activity and qualified to instruct apprentices; an expert authoritative teacher.
highly skilled authority or teacher

Examples

The young carpenter respected his master for his craftsmanship and wisdom.
As a master, she taught her apprentices the secrets of the trade.
Only a true master could restore the painting to its former glory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expert - a person with a high level of knowledge or skill in a particular area
  • Antonym: apprentice - a person who is learning a trade from a skilled employer

Etymology

From Old English *mægister* via Latin *magister* (chief, teacher, master).
