surliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜr.lin.əs / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.lɪ.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being bad-tempered or unfriendly
bad-tempered or unfriendly disposition

Examples

There was a certain surliness in his manner that made people uneasy.
Her surliness often discouraged others from approaching her at work.
Despite his surliness, his colleagues respected his honesty.

Synset Examples

his temper was well known to all his employees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grumpiness - the quality of being easily annoyed or moody
  • Antonym: friendliness - the quality of being friendly and pleasant

Etymology

From 'surly' (bad-tempered and unfriendly), ultimately from Middle English 'serly' (lordly, haughty).
