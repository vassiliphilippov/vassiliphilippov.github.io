gamekeeper

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪmˌkiːpər / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪmˌkiːpə / (BrE)
a person employed to look after game animals and wildlife, especially on a large estate
person who manages wildlife on an estate

Examples

The gamekeeper patrolled the estate every morning to protect the pheasants from poachers.
As a skilled gamekeeper, she managed the wildlife population with great care.
The gamekeeper's duties include feeding the animals and maintaining their habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ranger - person employed to manage and protect an area of countryside, often a park or reserve

Etymology

From 'game' (wild animals hunted for sport) + 'keeper' (one who keeps or looks after).
