Harefoot

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛrˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈheə.fʊt / (BrE)
Harold I (died 1040), King of England from 1035 to 1040, known as Harefoot, the illegitimate son of Canute who seized the throne in 1037.
King of England (1035–1040), son of Canute

Examples

Harefoot was crowned King of England after the death of his father, Canute.
Historians debate the legitimacy of Harefoot's claim to the English throne.
The reign of Harefoot was marked by political turmoil and uncertainty.

Etymology

Nickname derived from Old English 'hara' (hare) and 'fōt' (foot), possibly referring to swiftness.
