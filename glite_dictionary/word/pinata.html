pinata

1. NOUN

/ piˈnɑtə / (AmE), / piˈnɑːtə / (BrE)
A decorated container filled with toys and candy, which is suspended and broken open as part of a party game, especially by blindfolded children.
decorated container broken at parties

Examples

Each child took a turn trying to hit the pinata with a stick.
They filled the pinata with sweets and small toys for the birthday party.
After several tries, she finally broke open the pinata and candy spilled everywhere.

Etymology

From Spanish 'piñata', originally from Italian 'pignatta' (fragile pot).
