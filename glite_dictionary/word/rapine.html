rapine

1. NOUN

/ ˈræp.iːn / (AmE), / ˈræp.aɪn / (BrE)
the violent seizure and plundering of property, especially during war
violent plundering in warfare

Examples

The invading army's rapine left the once-thriving city in ruins.
Historians condemned the era for its violence and rapine against civilian populations.
Years of rapine and war devastated the countryside and its people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plunder - to steal goods, typically during war or civil disorder
  • Synonym: pillage - to rob a place using violence, especially in wartime

Etymology

From Latin rapina (robbery, plundering), from rapere (to seize).
