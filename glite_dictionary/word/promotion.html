promotion

1. NOUN

/ prəˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / prəˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
the act of advancing or encouraging the progress of something, especially a business, career, or enterprise
advancement or encouragement of progress

Examples

Her hard work led to the promotion of her team's new project.
The company's promotion of innovation resulted in several successful ventures.
Without strong promotion, the small business struggled to grow.

Synset Examples

his experience in marketing resulted in the forwarding of his career

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advancement - progress in rank, position, or importance
  • Antonym: demotion - the reduction to a lower rank or position

Etymology

From Latin promotio, promotionis, from promovere 'to move forward'.

2. NOUN

/ proʊˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / prəˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
a publicity message or activity designed to increase awareness or support for a product, cause, idea, or institution
publicity or advertising activity

Examples

The company launched a new promotion to introduce their latest smartphone model.
Charities often rely on promotion to raise awareness for their campaigns.
This promotion highlights the importance of regular exercise for a healthy lifestyle.

Synset Examples

the packaging of new ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advertisement - a public notice promoting a product, service, or event
  • Synonym: publicity - notice or attention given to someone or something by the media

Etymology

From Latin promotio (advancement), from promovere (to move forward).

/ prəˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / prəˈməʊʃən / (BrE)

advancement in rank or position

Examples

His recent promotion to manager surprised many of his colleagues.
Opportunities for promotion are limited in smaller organizations.

4. NOUN

/ prəˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / prəˈməʊʃən / (BrE)

encouragement of progress or acceptance

Examples

Environmental promotion has become increasingly important in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: obstruction - the act of preventing progress or growth

Etymology
