volatility

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɑːləˈtɪləti / (AmE), / ˌvɒləˈtɪləti / (BrE)
the property of a substance that allows it to change readily from a solid or liquid into a vapor
tendency to vaporize easily

Examples

The volatility of the chemical means it must be stored in a sealed container.
Gasoline's high volatility makes it useful as a fuel but also dangerous to handle.
Because of its low volatility, water does not evaporate quickly at room temperature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evaporation - the process of turning from liquid into vapor

Etymology

From Latin 'volatilis' (flying), via French 'volatilité'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌvɑːləˈtɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌvɒləˈtɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being likely to change rapidly and unpredictably, especially for the worse.
tendency to change rapidly and unpredictably

Examples

The volatility of the stock market makes investing risky for some people.
Political volatility can create uncertainty for businesses and citizens.
His mood volatility made it difficult to predict how he would react.

Synset Examples

the volatility of the market drove many investors away

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stability - the quality of being stable or unchanging

Etymology

From French 'volatilité', from Latin 'volatilis' (fleeting, flying).

3. NOUN

/ ˌvɑːləˈtɪləti / (AmE), / ˌvɒləˈtɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being likely to change suddenly and unexpectedly, especially showing rapid emotional or behavioral changes
quality of being easily excited or changeable

Examples

Her volatility made it difficult for her colleagues to predict how she would react to criticism.
Financial markets are known for their volatility, which can unsettle even the most experienced investors.
The volatility of his temper meant he was easily upset by small things.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instability - the state of being unstable or prone to change
  • Antonym: calmness - the quality of being free from agitation or strong emotion

Etymology

From Latin volatilis (flying, fleeting) + -ity; originally referring to substances that evaporate quickly, later expanded to unpredictability.

Synset POS: n
