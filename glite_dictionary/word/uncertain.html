uncertain

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsɝːtən / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɜːtən / (BrE)
not consistent, reliable, or dependable; liable to change or fail
not consistent or dependable

Examples

He has an uncertain memory of what happened that night.
The weather is always uncertain in early spring, making planning difficult.
His future at the company remains uncertain after the recent changes.

Synset Examples

an uncertain recollection of events
a gun with a rather uncertain trigger

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reliable - dependable; able to be trusted

Etymology

From Latin incertus (not sure) via Old French certain (sure, fixed).
