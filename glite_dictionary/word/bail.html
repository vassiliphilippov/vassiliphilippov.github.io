bail

1. NOUN

/ beɪl / (AmE), / beɪl / (BrE)
Money paid as security to ensure that an accused person returns for their trial; it is forfeited if they fail to appear.
money security to ensure court appearance
legal

Examples

The judge set bail at $10,000 for the defendant.
He was released on bail while awaiting trial.
If you cannot afford to pay bail, you may have to stay in jail until your court date.

Synset Examples

the judge set bail at $10,000
a $10,000 bond was furnished by an alderman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bond - a written agreement to pay a sum if a legal obligation is not met

Etymology

From Old French *bail*, from Old French *baillier* (to take charge of), from Latin *bajulare* (to carry a burden).

2. VERB

/ beɪl / (AmE), / beɪl / (BrE)
to remove water from a boat or vessel using a container
remove water from a boat with a container

Examples

She quickly grabbed a bucket and began to bail as the water kept rising.
We took turns bailing so the boat wouldn't sink.
