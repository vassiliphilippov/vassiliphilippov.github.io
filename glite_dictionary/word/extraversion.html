extraversion

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkstrəˈvɝːʒən / (AmE), / ˌɛkstrəˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
(psychology) the quality of being outgoing and socially confident, characterized by concern with external things rather than inward thought.
outgoing and socially confident personality trait
medical

Examples

Her extraversion made her popular at parties and helped her thrive in group settings.
The psychologist assessed his level of extraversion using a standardized personality test.
Certain professions, such as sales, often attract individuals with high extraversion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: introversion - the tendency to be more focused on internal thoughts and feelings than on external things; the opposite trait of extraversion

Etymology

From Latin 'extra-' (outside) + 'vertere' (to turn).
