whinstone

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪnˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈwɪn.stəʊn / (BrE)
a hard, dark-colored rock such as basalt or chert, often used for road surfaces and building
a hard, dark rock (e.g. basalt, chert)

Examples

A layer of whinstone was used as the foundation for the new road.
The ancient wall was built from local whinstone, giving it a dark appearance.
Quarries in the area supply large amounts of whinstone for construction projects.

Etymology

Probably from Scots or northern English dialect 'whin' (hard rock) + 'stone'.
