bortsch

1. NOUN

/ bɔrtʃ / (AmE), / bɔːtʃ / (BrE)
A Russian or Polish soup, typically made with beetroot as the main ingredient, giving it a distinctive red color.
beetroot-based Russian/Polish soup

Examples

Many people add sour cream to their bortsch for a richer flavor.
We enjoyed a bowl of hot bortsch on a cold winter evening.
Her grandmother taught her how to prepare traditional bortsch from scratch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: borscht - Russian beetroot soup; common alternative spelling

Etymology

From Russian борщ (borshch), referring to the traditional Eastern European soup.
