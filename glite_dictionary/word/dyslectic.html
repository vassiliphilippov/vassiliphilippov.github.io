dyslectic

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈlɛktɪk / (AmE), / dɪsˈlɛktɪk / (BrE)
a person who has dyslexia, a condition affecting the ability to read and interpret words
person with dyslexia
medical

Examples

As a dyslectic, he faced many challenges in school but never gave up.
Educational specialists are trained to assist dyslectic children with reading strategies.
The conference will cover the latest research on how technology can help dyslectic students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dyslexic - a person who has dyslexia

Etymology

From dyslexia + -ic, meaning 'relating to dyslexia' or 'affected by dyslexia'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10060414-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ dɪsˈlɛktɪk / (AmE), / dɪsˈlɛktɪk / (BrE)
Having difficulty in reading or comprehending written words, usually due to a neurological disorder such as dyslexia.
having difficulty reading due to neurological disorder
medical

Examples

Her brother is dyslectic and often finds reading long passages challenging.
The new reading software was designed to help dyslectic students improve their literacy skills.
As a dyslectic child, he sometimes confused letters and struggled at school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dyslexic - having difficulty in learning to read or interpret words, letters, and other symbols

Etymology

Formed from 'dyslexia' + '-ic', indicating relation to dyslexia.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01278305-s
Synset POS: s
