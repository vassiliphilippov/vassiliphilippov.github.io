nonabsorbency

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑn.əbˈzɔr.bən.si / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.əbˈzɔː.bən.si / (BrE)
the quality or state of not being able to absorb liquids or other substances
the state of not being absorbent

Examples

The nonabsorbency of the material makes it ideal for waterproof clothing.
Scientists tested the nonabsorbency of several new polymers.
Because of its nonabsorbency, the fabric dries very quickly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: absorbency - the ability to soak up liquid

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' (not) + 'absorbency' (ability to absorb).
