thwack

1. NOUN

/ θwæk / (AmE), / θwak / (BrE)
a hard, sharp blow with a flat object, often producing a loud sound
sharp blow with a flat object

Examples

The tennis player's racket landed with a loud thwack against the ball.
She gave the dusty rug a hard thwack with the broom to loosen the dirt.
The thief was startled by the thwack of a book hitting the table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smack - a sharp, loud blow or slap

Etymology

Imitative of a hitting sound; possibly related to 'thwack' (verb) of similar sound origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00134387-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ θwæk / (AmE), / θwæk / (BrE)
to strike or hit something forcefully with a sharp, loud sound
strike forcefully with a sharp sound

Examples

He raised the stick and brought it down with a loud thwack on the table.
The tennis player thwacked the ball across the court with impressive force.
She thwacked the dust from the rug with an old broom.

Synset Examples

The teacher smacked the student who had misbehaved

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smack - to strike with a sharp or loud sound
  • Synonym: slap - to hit with the palm of the hand or a flat object
  • Antonym: caress - to touch or stroke gently

Etymology

Imitative of the sound made by a sharp blow; probably of onomatopoeic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01417570-v
Synset POS: v
