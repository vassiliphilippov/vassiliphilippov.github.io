two-needled

1. ADJ

/ tuːˈniːdld / (AmE), / tuːˈniːdld / (BrE)
(of conifers) having clusters or bundles consisting of two needles.
having two needles per cluster (conifers)

Examples

The two-needled pine is native to this region and thrives in sandy soils.
Botanists easily distinguish two-needled species from those with three or five needles.
The forest was dominated by two-needled pines with long, slender bundles.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: three-needled - having three needles per cluster (conifers)
  • Degree Higher: five-needled - having five needles per cluster (conifers)
