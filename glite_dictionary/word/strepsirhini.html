Strepsirhini

1. NOUN

/ strɛpˈsɪrəˌhaɪnaɪ / (AmE), / strɛpˈsɪrɪniː / (BrE)
A suborder of primates including lemurs, lorises, and related animals, characterized by a wet nose and other primitive features.
suborder of primates (lemurs, lorises, etc.)

Examples

Members of the Strepsirhini are known for their keen sense of smell.
Lemurs, found only in Madagascar, belong to the suborder Strepsirhini.
The most primitive living primates are classified as Strepsirhini.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Haplorhini - suborder of primates including tarsiers and simians, distinct from Strepsirhini

Etymology

From modern Latin; from Greek strepsis (a turning) + rhis (nose), referring to the twisted nostrils.

2. NOUN

/ ˌstrɛp.sɪˈraɪ.ni / (AmE), / ˌstrɛp.sɪˈraɪ.ni / (BrE)
a suborder of primates that includes lemurs, lorises, and related species, characterized by a wet, curved nostril structure distinct from other primates
suborder of primates: lemurs, lorises, etc.
medical

Examples

Lemurs are members of the Strepsirhini, a suborder distinct from monkeys and apes.
The Strepsirhini include several species with unique adaptations for nocturnal life.
Research on Strepsirhini has provided insight into early primate evolution.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Haplorhini - a suborder of primates that includes tarsiers, monkeys, and apes; distinguished from Strepsirhini

Etymology

From Greek 'strepsis' (a turning) + 'rhis' (nose), referring to the twisted or curved nostrils of these primates.
