bungle

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈbʌŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
a careless or clumsy mistake; an error caused by incompetence or lack of skill
an embarrassing mistake

Examples

His admission of the bungle only made the situation more awkward.
The event was marred by a serious bungle with the guest list.
After that bungle, he was removed from the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunder - a stupid or careless mistake
  • Antonym: success - the achievement of an aim or purpose, the opposite of a failure or mistake

Etymology

From Middle English ‘bungelen’ (to work clumsily).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00075610-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈbʌŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈbʌŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
to carry out a task clumsily or incompetently, leading to failure or a bad result
do something badly or clumsily

Examples

He managed to bungle the entire project despite clear instructions.
If you bungle this assignment, it could affect your grade.
The repairman bungled the job, leaving the door worse than before.

Synset Examples

I botched the dinner and we had to eat out
the pianist screwed up the difficult passage in the second movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botch - do something badly or carelessly
  • Synonym: to mishandle - manage or deal with something badly or wrongly
  • Antonym: to succeed - achieve the desired aim or result

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English 'bung', meaning to work clumsily, of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ ˈbʌŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈbʌŋɡl / (BrE)
to carry out a task or activity clumsily or incompetently, leading to failure or mistakes
do something clumsily or badly

Examples

He completely bungled the job interview by forgetting her name.
The repairman bungled the installation, causing a small flood in the kitchen.
If you bungle the paperwork, it could delay your application for weeks.

Synset Examples

I bungled it!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botch - to do something badly or carelessly
  • Synonym: to mishandle - to manage a situation or task badly
  • Antonym: to manage - to handle or direct with skill

Etymology

Perhaps from dialectal Swedish bungla (to work ineffectively).
