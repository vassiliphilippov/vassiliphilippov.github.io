Nephrolepis

1. NOUN

/ nɛfˈroʊlɪpɪs / (AmE), / nɛfˈrɒlɪpɪs / (BrE)
a small genus of tropical ferns, including several popular houseplants, sometimes classified in the family Polypodiaceae.
genus of tropical ferns

Examples

The Nephrolepis thrives in humid environments, making it a popular choice for bathrooms and greenhouses.
Many gardeners choose Nephrolepis for its lush, feathery fronds and ease of care.
Botanists debated whether Nephrolepis belongs in the Polypodiaceae or Davalliaceae family.

Etymology

From Greek nephros (kidney) and lepis (scale), referring to the shape of certain plant structures.

2. NOUN

/ nɛfˈroʊlɪpɪs / (AmE), / nɛfˈrɒlɪpɪs / (BrE)
a small genus of tropical ferns, commonly known as sword ferns, sometimes classified under the family Polypodiaceae
a genus of tropical sword ferns

Examples

The popular houseplant Boston fern belongs to the genus Nephrolepis.
Many species in the Nephrolepis genus thrive in tropical climates around the world.
Researchers studied the unique reproduction patterns of Nephrolepis ferns.

Etymology

From Greek nephros (kidney) + lepis (scale), referring to the kidney-shaped scales found on the plants.
