Israelites

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪzrəˌlaɪts / (AmE), / ˈɪzrəlaɪts / (BrE)
Members of the ancient Hebrew nation, descendants of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, considered God's chosen people in the Hebrew Bible.
ancient Hebrew people; descendants of Jacob

Examples

The Israelites fled from Egypt under the leadership of Moses.
According to tradition, the Israelites wandered in the desert for forty years.
Archaeologists study ancient sites once inhabited by the Israelites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hebrews - Ancient people of Israel; ancestors of the Jewish people
  • Degree Higher: Jews - Members of the Jewish people; descendants of the ancient Israelites

Etymology

From Latin Israelita, from Greek Israēlitēs, from Hebrew Yiśrā'ēl ('Israel') + -ite
