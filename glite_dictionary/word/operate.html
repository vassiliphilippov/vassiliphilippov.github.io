to operate

1. VERB

/ ˈɑː.pəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒp.əˌreɪt / (BrE)
(of military or naval forces) to execute a maneuver or movement to gain a tactical advantage in an attack or defense.
conduct a tactical movement (military)

Examples

The fleet began to operate to secure a better position before the battle.
The division was ordered to operate north of the river to cut enemy supply lines.
Units must operate with precision to outmaneuver the opposing force in combat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maneuver - to perform a planned movement in a military context

2. VERB

/ ˈɑː.pəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒp.ə.reɪt / (BrE)
to function or perform as intended or expected
function; work as intended

Examples

The new coffee maker will not operate unless it is plugged in properly.
After years of use, his old laptop still operates smoothly.
If the generator doesn't operate during storms, the hospital could lose power.

Synset Examples

The washing machine won't go unless it's plugged in
Does this old car still run well?
This old radio doesn't work anymore

Related Terms

  • Synonym: function - work or operate in the proper or expected way
  • Antonym: malfunction - fail to work properly
  • Synonym: work - perform as expected or intended (machines, devices)

Etymology

From Latin operari (to work), from opus (work).

3. VERB

/ ˈɑː.pəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒp.ə.reɪt / (BrE)
to perform a surgical procedure on a person or animal
perform surgery

Examples

The surgeon will operate on her heart early tomorrow morning.

After the accident, doctors had to operate immediately to stop the bleeding.
