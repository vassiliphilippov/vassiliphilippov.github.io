drubbing

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrʌbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrʌbɪŋ / (BrE)
a decisive or crushing defeat, especially in a competition or contest
a severe defeat

Examples

The team suffered a humiliating drubbing in the championship game.
After the drubbing at the polls, the party considered major reform.
They bounced back quickly after last week's drubbing by their rivals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrashing - a thorough beating or defeat
  • Antonym: victory - success in a contest or struggle

Etymology

From the verb 'drub', likely from Turkish 'dürb' (to beat).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdrʌb.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrʌb.ɪŋ / (BrE)
a severe beating or thrashing, especially as punishment; the act of inflicting repeated blows
a severe beating or thrashing

Examples

The thief received a harsh drubbing from the angry crowd before the police arrived.
After the failed rebellion, the rebels were given a severe drubbing as punishment.
He remembered the drubbing he got as a boy for stealing apples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beating - the act of striking someone repeatedly as punishment
  • Synonym: thrashing - a severe or thorough beating

Etymology

From the verb 'drub', probably from Turkish 'dürb', meaning 'to beat'.
