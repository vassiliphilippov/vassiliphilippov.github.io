Acinos

1. NOUN

/ ˈæsɪnɒs / (AmE), / ˈæsɪnɒs / (BrE)
A small genus of aromatic plants in the mint family, closely related to Satureja and Calamintha.
a genus of aromatic mint plants

Examples

The species of Acinos are valued for their fragrant leaves in gardens.
Botanists have reclassified many plants once included in Acinos.
Several Acinos species grow natively in Europe and the Mediterranean region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Satureja - A genus of aromatic plants in the mint family closely related to Acinos
  • Synonym: Calamintha - A genus of aromatic plants in the mint family closely related to Acinos

Etymology

From Greek 'akinos', a kind of aromatic plant.
