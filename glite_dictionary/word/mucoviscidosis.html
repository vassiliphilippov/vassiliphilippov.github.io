mucoviscidosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmjuːkoʊvɪsɪˈdoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmjuːkəʊvɪsɪˈdəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A hereditary disease in which the lungs, intestines, and pancreas become clogged with thick mucus; also known as cystic fibrosis.
hereditary disease (cystic fibrosis) with thick mucus
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that mucoviscidosis is a severe genetic disorder affecting multiple organs.
Treatment for mucoviscidosis focuses on relieving symptoms and improving quality of life.
A new therapy was developed to help children living with mucoviscidosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cystic fibrosis - an inherited disorder causing thick, sticky mucus in organs

Etymology

From French mucoviscidose, based on Latin mucus (slime, mucus) and viscidus (sticky).
