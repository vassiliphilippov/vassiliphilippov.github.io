rima vestibuli

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːmə vɛˈstɪbjʊˌlaɪ / (AmE), / ˈriːmə vɛˈstɪbjʊli / (BrE)
the opening between the false vocal folds in the larynx
opening between false vocal folds (larynx)
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully examined the rima vestibuli during the laryngoscopy.
A tumor in the larynx can sometimes obstruct the rima vestibuli.
The airflow through the rima vestibuli plays a role in some vocal techniques.

Etymology

From Latin 'rima' (cleft, opening) and 'vestibuli' (of the vestibule).
