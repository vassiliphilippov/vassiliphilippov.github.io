telluride

1. NOUN

/ təˈlʊr.aɪd / (AmE), / tɛˈljʊə.raɪd / (BrE)
A binary compound of tellurium with a more electropositive element.
compound of tellurium and another element

Examples

The mineral calaverite is a natural telluride of gold.
Chemists synthesized a new telluride compound for use in solar cells.
Silver telluride is known for its unusual electrical properties.

Etymology

From tellurium + -ide, following the nomenclature for chemical compounds.
