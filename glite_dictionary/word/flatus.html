flatus

1. NOUN

/ ˈfleɪtəs / (AmE), / ˈfleɪtəs / (BrE)
gas expelled from the digestive tract through the anus; intestinal gas.
intestinal gas; gas expelled from the anus
medical

Examples

The patient complained of excessive flatus after eating beans.
Passing flatus in public often causes embarrassment.
A balanced diet can help reduce the amount of flatus produced by the intestines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flatulence - accumulation or expulsion of gas in the digestive system

Etymology

From Latin *flatus* (a blowing, a breath), from *flare* (to blow).
