Myopus

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈoʊpəs / (AmE), / mʌɪˈəʊpəs / (BrE)
a genus of small rodents in the family Cricetidae, commonly known as the wood lemming.
a genus of rodents (wood lemmings)

Examples

The Myopus genus is known for its distinctive adaptation to boreal forest habitats.
Researchers studied the population cycles of Myopus schisticolor in Scandinavia.
In northern Europe, the only species of Myopus is the wood lemming.

Etymology

From Greek myōp-, related to 'mouse' and 'foot'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪ.oʊ.pəs / (AmE), / ˈmɪ.ə.pəs / (BrE)
A genus of small voles in the family Cricetidae, including the wood lemming.
genus of voles (rodents)

Examples

The wood lemming belongs to the genus Myopus, native to parts of Northern Europe.
Scientists have studied Myopus to understand adaptation to cold environments.
Members of the genus Myopus are distinguished by their short tails and dense fur.

Etymology

From Greek 'myo' (mouse) and 'pus' (foot).
