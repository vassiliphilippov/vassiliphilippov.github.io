mudhif

1. NOUN

/ muˈdiːf / (AmE), / muˈdiːf / (BrE)
A traditional reed hut used by the Marsh Arabs in the marshlands of Iraq, typically constructed for communal or ceremonial purposes.
traditional reed hut of Iraqi marshlands

Examples

The Marsh Arabs built a mudhif to welcome visitors and conduct ceremonies.
After the marshes were drained, very few mudhifs remained standing in the region.
A mudhif is constructed almost entirely from reeds harvested from the nearby waterways.

Etymology

From Arabic مضيف‎ (muḍīf), meaning 'guesthouse' or 'place for hospitality.'
