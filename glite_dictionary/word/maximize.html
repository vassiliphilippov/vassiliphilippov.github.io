to maximize

1. VERB

/ ˈmæk.sɪˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmæk.sɪ.maɪz / (BrE)
to make as large or great as possible; to make the most of something
make as large or effective as possible

Examples

In order to maximize profits, the company invested in new technology.
She always tries to maximize her time by planning each day carefully.
We need to maximize the use of available resources to succeed.

Synset Examples

He maximized his role

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to minimize - to make as small as possible

Etymology

From Latin 'maximus' (greatest) via English 'maximum' + '-ize'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00429823-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈmæk.sɪ.maɪz / (AmE), / ˈmaks.ɪ.maɪz / (BrE)
to make as large or great as possible; to increase something to its highest degree
make as large or great as possible

Examples

The company implemented new strategies to maximize efficiency.
Please adjust the settings to maximize the quality of your video.
He invested carefully in order to maximize his returns.

Synset Examples

Maximize your profits!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to minimize - to make as small as possible

Etymology

From Latin 'maximus' (greatest) via 'maximum' + '-ize'.
