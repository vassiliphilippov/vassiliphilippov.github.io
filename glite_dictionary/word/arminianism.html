Arminianism

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈmɪniəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ɑːˈmɪniənɪzəm / (BrE)
A 17th-century Christian theological movement, based on the teachings of Jacobus Arminius, that rejects the doctrine of absolute predestination and asserts that human free will is compatible with God's sovereignty.
theology opposing predestination; stresses free will

Examples

The debate between Arminianism and Calvinism has shaped Protestant theology for centuries.
Many Methodists embrace Arminianism as a core aspect of their doctrine.
Students of church history often encounter the disputes surrounding Arminianism in early modern Europe.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Calvinism - Christian theological system emphasizing predestination

Etymology

Named after Jacobus Arminius (1560–1609), Dutch theologian.
