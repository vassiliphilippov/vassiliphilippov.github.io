joyride

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɔɪˌraɪd / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔɪraɪd / (BrE)
a ride taken in a vehicle purely for pleasure, often without the owner's permission and sometimes illegally or recklessly
pleasure ride, often unauthorized or reckless

Examples

The teenagers went for a joyride in their parents' new car.
After reporting his car stolen, he discovered it had been taken on a joyride.
The police caught the suspects after a high-speed joyride through the city.

Synset Examples

they took their girlfriends for joyrides in stolen cars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pleasure ride - a ride taken for enjoyment

Etymology

From 'joy' + 'ride', coined in early 20th century American English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00310194-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈdʒɔɪˌraɪd / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔɪraɪd / (BrE)
to drive or ride in a vehicle for pleasure, often without a particular destination or purpose
drive or ride for pleasure without purpose

Examples

On Saturday night, they decided to joyride around the city, laughing and playing music.
He was caught joyriding in his parents' car without their permission.
Teenagers sometimes joyride through town just to pass the time.

Synset Examples

We tooled down the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruise - to travel at a moderate speed for pleasure, especially in a car

Etymology

Formed from 'joy' plus 'ride', first recorded in the early 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01960567-v
Synset POS: v
