millennium

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɛniəm / (AmE), / mɪˈlɛnɪəm / (BrE)
A period of a thousand years, especially when calculated from a particular date.
period of 1,000 years

Examples

Historians debate how civilizations developed over the past millennium.
The year 2000 marked the beginning of a new millennium.
Changes in technology have accelerated dramatically over the last millennium.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: century - a period of 100 years

Etymology

From Latin 'millennium', from 'mille' (thousand) + 'annus' (year).

2. NOUN

/ mɪˈlɛniəm / (AmE), / mɪˈlɛniəm / (BrE)
The 1000th anniversary of a significant event, or the celebration of such an anniversary.
1000th anniversary or its celebration

Examples

The city hosted a grand festival for the millennium of its founding.
Scholars gathered to commemorate the millennium of the university's establishment.
Preparations for the millennium celebration took years of planning and coordination.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: centenary - the 100th anniversary or its celebration
  • Degree Lower: bicentenary - the 200th anniversary or its celebration

Etymology

From Latin 'millennium', from 'mille' (thousand) + 'annus' (year).

3. NOUN

/ mɪˈlɛniəm / (AmE), / mɪˈlɛniəm / (BrE)
In Christian theology, a prophesied thousand-year period during which Christ will reign on earth, especially as described in the Book of Revelation.
prophesied thousand-year reign of Christ

Examples

Many Christian denominations interpret the millennium as a literal thousand-year reign of peace under Christ.
Debates about the nature of the millennium have persisted since the early days of the church.
Some theologians see the millennium as symbolic rather than an actual period of a thousand years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: millenarianism - belief in the coming millennium as prophesied in the Book of Revelation

Etymology

From Latin millennium, from mille (thousand) + annus (year).
