breakstone

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkstəʊn / (BrE)
any of several plants of the genus Saxifraga, often found in rocky places and noted for their ability to grow in crevices
a plant of the genus Saxifraga

Examples

Gardeners often choose breakstone for rock gardens because it thrives in stony soil.
The cliff was dotted with patches of breakstone blooming in the spring.
Some herbal remedies historically used breakstone for its supposed medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saxifrage - plants of the genus Saxifraga, commonly called saxifrages or breakstones

Etymology

From 'break' + 'stone', referring to the plant's ability to grow in and seemingly break rocks.
