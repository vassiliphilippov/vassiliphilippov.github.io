imperatively

1. ADV

/ ɪmˈpɛrəˌtɪvli / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛrətɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that expresses command or authority; in a commanding or peremptory manner.
in a commanding or authoritative way

Examples

She spoke imperatively, leaving no room for argument.
The officer imperatively ordered the troops to advance.
He raised his hand imperatively to silence the crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authoritatively - in a way that shows confidence and authority

Etymology

From 'imperative', itself from Latin 'imperativus' (commanding), with adverbial suffix '-ly'.
