compulsorily

1. ADV

/ kəmˈpʌlsərəli / (AmE), / kəmˈpʌlsərɪli / (BrE)
In a way that is required by law or rule; not optional; mandatorily.
in a required or mandatory way

Examples

By law, all children must be educated compulsorily until a certain age.
Taxes are compulsorily deducted from your paycheck each month.
The property was compulsorily acquired by the government for public use.

Synset Examples

the ministry considers that contributions to such a fund should be met from voluntary donations rather than from rates compulsorily levied.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: voluntarily - in a way that is done by free choice, not compulsion

Etymology

From compulsory + -ly, with 'compulsory' deriving from Medieval Latin 'compulsorius.'
