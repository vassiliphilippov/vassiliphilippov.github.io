grievance

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡriː.vəns / (AmE), / ˈɡriː.vəns / (BrE)
a formal complaint or allegation that something is unfair, illegal, or unjust, especially as a violation of legal rights
formal complaint about injustice or illegality

Examples

The employees filed a grievance against the company, citing unfair working conditions.
She submitted a formal grievance to the board regarding the denial of her legal rights.
Unresolved grievances often lead to disputes between management and workers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complaint - statement expressing dissatisfaction or injustice
  • Synonym: injustice - unfair action or treatment
  • Antonym: satisfaction - fulfillment of a complaint or claim

Etymology

From Old French 'grevance' (burden, suffering), from 'grever' (to burden), from Latin 'gravare' (to load, oppress).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡriːvəns / (AmE), / ˈɡriːvəns / (BrE)
a feeling or statement that a situation is unfair and has caused distress, often seen as a reason to complain or take action
complaint about unfair treatment

Examples

The workers filed a grievance with management over unsafe working conditions.
She felt she had a legitimate grievance after being unfairly dismissed from her job.
Addressing every grievance promptly can help improve workplace morale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complaint - an expression of dissatisfaction or annoyance about something
  • Antonym: praise - an expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

From Old French 'grevance', from 'grever' (to burden), from Latin 'gravare' (to burden, to weigh down), from 'gravis' (heavy).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡriːvəns / (AmE), / ˈɡriːvəns / (BrE)
a feeling of resentment or a sense of being wronged, often regarded as justifying complaint or action
feeling of being wronged or mistreated

Examples

She filed a formal grievance against her manager for unfair treatment.
Many workers harbor a grievance about the lack of proper safety measures at the factory.
After years of silence, he finally voiced his grievance to the board.

Synset Examples

holding a grudge
settling a score

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complaint - an expression of dissatisfaction or annoyance
  • Synonym: resentment - a feeling of persistent ill will at something regarded as a wrong
  • Antonym: forgiveness - the action of forgiving someone for an offense or mistake

Etymology

From Old French 'grevance' (oppression, grievance), from 'grever' (to burden), from Latin 'gravare' (to make heavy).
