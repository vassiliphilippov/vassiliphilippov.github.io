norm

1. NOUN

/ nɔrm / (AmE), / nɔːm / (BrE)
a standard or pattern of behavior, achievement, or belief that is considered typical or expected within a particular group or society
a typical standard or pattern

Examples

Wearing jeans to work has become the norm in many offices today.
In that culture, it is the norm to remove your shoes before entering a home.
Challenging the social norm can lead to innovation and change.

Synset Examples

the current middle-class norm of two children per family

Related Terms

  • Synonym: standard - a level of quality or attainment used as a measure, norm, or model
  • Synonym: custom - a traditional and widely accepted way of behaving or doing something
  • Antonym: exception - something that does not follow the norm or standard

Etymology

From Latin norma (carpenter's square, rule).

2. NOUN

/ nɔrm / (AmE), / nɔːm / (BrE)
a value or statistic that represents the typical or standard position in a distribution of data; a standard or average calculated from a set of data
typical value or standard in data

Examples

The national norm for test scores is calculated annually by the board.
In this study, we compared patient data to the accepted norm within the population.
Anything outside the statistical norm may indicate an anomaly.

Synset Examples

it set the norm for American homes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: average - the result obtained by adding several quantities together and dividing by their number; mean value
  • Synonym: mean - the sum of a list of numbers divided by the number of items in the list
  • Degree Lower: deviation - the amount by which a single measurement differs from a fixed value such as the mean

Etymology

From Latin 'norma' (carpenter's square, rule), later extended in mathematical and statistical senses.
