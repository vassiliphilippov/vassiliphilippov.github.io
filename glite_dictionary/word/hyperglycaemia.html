hyperglycaemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərɡlaɪˈsiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəɡlʌɪˈsiːmɪə / (BrE)
An abnormally high level of glucose in the blood, typically associated with diabetes.
abnormally high blood sugar
medical british

Examples

Untreated hyperglycaemia can lead to serious health complications in people with diabetes.
Symptoms of hyperglycaemia include increased thirst and frequent urination.
The doctor explained that regular monitoring can help prevent episodes of hyperglycaemia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypoglycaemia - abnormally low blood sugar
  • Synonym: hyperglycemia - US spelling of hyperglycaemia

Etymology

From Greek 'hyper-' (over, excessive) + 'glykys' (sweet) + '-aemia' (condition of the blood).
