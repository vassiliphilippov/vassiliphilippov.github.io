Oxalis tuberosa

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈzælɪs tuːˈbɛroʊsə / (AmE), / ɒɡˈzælɪs tjuːˈbɛrəsə / (BrE)
A South American plant of the wood sorrel family, cultivated for its edible tubers, also known as oca.
South American plant; edible tuber (oca)

Examples

Oxalis tuberosa is widely grown in the Andes for its brightly colored, tangy tubers.
Farmers rotate potatoes with Oxalis tuberosa to help maintain soil health.
The traditional Peruvian dish uses boiled Oxalis tuberosa as a key ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oca - Alternative common name for Oxalis tuberosa

Etymology

From Latin genus name Oxalis and species epithet tuberosa, meaning 'tuberous'.
