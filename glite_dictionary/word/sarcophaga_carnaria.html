Sarcophaga carnaria

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑr.kəˈfeɪ.dʒə kɑrˈnɛr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌsɑː.kəˈfeɪ.ɡə kɑːˈnɛə.ri.ə / (BrE)
a species of flesh fly whose larvae feed on carrion or the flesh of living animals
a species of flesh fly

Examples

The larvae of Sarcophaga carnaria are often found on decomposing animal remains.
Biologists studied the behavior of Sarcophaga carnaria to understand its role in decomposition.
Sarcophaga carnaria can sometimes infest wounds in living animals, causing myiasis.

Etymology

From Greek 'sarkophagos' (flesh-eating) and Latin 'carnaria' (of flesh).
