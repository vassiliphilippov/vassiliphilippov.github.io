attrited

1. ADJ

/ əˈtraɪtɪd / (AmE), / əˈtrʌɪtɪd / (BrE)
worn down or reduced by rubbing or friction
worn by rubbing or friction

Examples

The surface of the old coin was attrited from years of handling.
Sediment particles become attrited as they are transported downstream by the river.
After decades of use, the marble steps appeared heavily attrited.

Etymology

From Latin 'attritus', past participle of 'atterere' (to rub against).
