to over-correct

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvɚkəˈrɛkt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəkəˈrɛkt / (BrE)
to make more corrections or adjustments than necessary, typically due to fear of making a mistake, often leading to a different error.
make excessive corrections

Examples

When editing her essay, she began to over-correct and introduced new mistakes.
If you over-correct while steering, you might lose control of the car.
Teachers warn students not to over-correct their grammar as it can make writing awkward.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overcompensate - to take excessive measures in an attempt to correct a perceived problem

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (excessively) + 'correct'.
