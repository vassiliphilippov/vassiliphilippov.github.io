Miltiades

1. PROPN

/ mɪlˈtaɪədiːz / (AmE), / mɪlˈtaɪədiːz / (BrE)
(c. 550–489 BC) An Athenian general celebrated for leading the Greeks to victory over the Persians at the Battle of Marathon.
Athenian general, victor at Marathon

Examples

Miltiades is credited with devising the tactics that led to the Greek victory at Marathon.
Historians often study the leadership style of Miltiades during the Persian Wars.
The Athenian people honored Miltiades for his role in defending their city-state.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Μιλτιάδης (Miltiádēs), a personal name.
