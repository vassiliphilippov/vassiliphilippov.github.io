Porto Rico

1. PROPN

/ ˌpɔrtoʊ ˈriːkoʊ / (AmE), / ˌpɔːtəʊ ˈriːkəʊ / (BrE)
an old name for Puerto Rico, the smallest and easternmost of the Greater Antilles in the Caribbean Sea
former name for Puerto Rico

Examples

In old documents, Porto Rico is frequently used instead of the modern 'Puerto Rico'.
The hurricane caused widespread damage across Porto Rico in the early 20th century.
Trade between the United States and Porto Rico increased dramatically after 1898.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Puerto Rico - the present official name of the island in the Caribbean

Etymology

Variant spelling of Spanish Puerto Rico, meaning 'rich port'.
