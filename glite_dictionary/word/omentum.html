omentum

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈmɛntəm / (AmE), / əʊˈmɛntəm / (BrE)
A fold of the peritoneum extending from the stomach and covering the abdominal organs.
fold of peritoneum covering abdominal organs
medical

Examples

During surgery, the doctor examined the patient's omentum for signs of disease.
Cancer can sometimes spread to the omentum, complicating treatment.
The omentum plays an important role in immune response in the abdomen.

Etymology

From Latin 'omentum' (fat, fatty membrane).
