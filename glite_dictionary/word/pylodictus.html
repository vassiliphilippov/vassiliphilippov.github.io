Pylodictus

1. NOUN

/ paɪ.ləˈdɪk.təs / (AmE), / paɪ.ləˈdɪk.təs / (BrE)
A genus of North American freshwater catfishes known as flathead catfishes.
genus of flathead catfishes

Examples

The Pylodictus genus includes the well-known flathead catfish native to North America.
Biologists are studying Pylodictus to better understand river ecosystems.
Members of Pylodictus are distinguished by their broad, flat heads and large mouths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flathead catfish - A species of catfish belonging to the genus Pylodictus

Etymology

From Greek pylē (gate) and diktuon (net), referencing the fish's mouth shape.
