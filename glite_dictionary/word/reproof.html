reproof

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈpruf / (AmE), / rɪˈpruːf / (BrE)
an act or expression of criticism or disapproval, especially when mild and intended to correct
mild expression of criticism or disapproval

Examples

She offered a gentle reproof when he interrupted the meeting.
His careless remark drew a swift reproof from his supervisor.
Despite the reproof, he continued to defend his position.

Synset Examples

he had to take the rebuke with a smile on his face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebuke - an expression of sharp disapproval or criticism
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

From Middle English 'reproef', from Old French 'reprove', from Late Latin 'reprobare' (to disapprove, reject).
