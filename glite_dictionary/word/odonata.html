Odonata

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈdɑːnətə / (AmE), / əʊˈdɒnətə / (BrE)
An order of insects that includes dragonflies and damselflies, characterized by elongated bodies, large eyes, and two pairs of strong, transparent wings.
order of insects: dragonflies and damselflies

Examples

The order Odonata is known for insects with powerful, transparent wings and keen vision.
Scientists study the habitats of Odonata to monitor changes in wetland ecosystems.
Both dragonflies and damselflies belong to the group called Odonata.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dragonfly - a predatory flying insect belonging to Odonata
  • Synonym: damselfly - a slender-bodied flying insect of Odonata, usually smaller than a dragonfly

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek 'odōn' meaning 'tooth', referencing the toothed jaws of these insects.

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊdəˈneɪtə / (AmE), / ˌəʊdəˈneɪtə / (BrE)
an order of insects that includes dragonflies and damselflies, characterized by large eyes, two pairs of transparent wings, and elongated bodies
order of insects: dragonflies and damselflies

Examples

The Odonata are known for their impressive aerial hunting abilities and agility.
Many freshwater habitats support diverse species of Odonata throughout the summer months.
Biologists study the Odonata to monitor changes in environmental health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dragonfly - a fast-flying insect with a long body and two pairs of large, transparent wings
  • Synonym: damselfly - a slender insect closely related to dragonflies, with similar appearance and habits

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek 'odous, odont-' meaning 'tooth', referring to the toothed mandibles of these insects.
