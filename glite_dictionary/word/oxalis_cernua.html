Oxalis cernua

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈzælɪs ˈsɜr.nju.ə / (AmE), / ɒkˈsælɪs ˈsɜː.njuː.ə / (BrE)
a South African species of wood sorrel with bulbous roots and showy yellow flowers
South African yellow-flowered wood sorrel

Examples

The garden was brightened by a cluster of Oxalis cernua blooming in the early spring.
Botanists often study Oxalis cernua for its striking yellow flowers and bulbous roots.
Many gardeners choose Oxalis cernua for rock gardens due to its hardiness and vivid color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow wood sorrel - a common name for several Oxalis species with yellow flowers

Etymology

From Latin: *Oxalis* (sorrel genus) + *cernua* (drooping, nodding), describing the flower's habit.
