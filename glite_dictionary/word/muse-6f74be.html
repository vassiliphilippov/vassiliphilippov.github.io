muse

1. NOUN

/ mjuz / (AmE), / mjuːz / (BrE)
a person or personified force that is the source of inspiration for an artist, writer, or thinker
source of artistic inspiration

Examples

Throughout his career, his wife was his greatest muse.
Many poets have found their muse in nature and the changing seasons.
She became the muse for several of the artist’s most famous paintings.

Synset Examples

Euterpe was his muse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspiration - the process of being mentally stimulated to do or feel something, especially to do something creative

Etymology

From Greek Mousa, one of the goddesses of inspiration in literature, science, and the arts.

2. VERB

/ mjuz / (AmE), / mjuːz / (BrE)
to think about something carefully and deeply, especially in a reflective or contemplative way
think or reflect deeply

Examples

She likes to muse about the mysteries of the universe late at night.
As he walked along the quiet beach, he began to muse on his future.
After hearing the news, Mira spent the afternoon musing on what to do next.

Synset Examples

I mulled over the events of the afternoon
philosophers have speculated on the question of God for thousands of years
The scientist must stop to observe and start to excogitate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ponder - to think about something carefully
  • Synonym: to consider - to think about something seriously or carefully
  • Degree Lower: daydream - to have pleasant thoughts that distract one’s attention

Etymology

From Old French 'muser' (to ponder, waste time), possibly from Medieval Latin 'musare' (to meditate, to gape).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00632042-v
Synset POS: v
