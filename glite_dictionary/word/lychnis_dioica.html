Lychnis dioica

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪk.nɪs daɪˈoʊ.ɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˈlɪk.nɪs daɪˈəʊ.ɪ.kə / (BrE)
A biennial European plant in the family Caryophyllaceae, commonly known as red campion, with red or pink flowers.
a European flowering plant (red campion)

Examples

The meadows were filled with Lychnis dioica, their bright pink flowers standing out among the grass.
Botanists often study Lychnis dioica due to its interesting reproductive system.
Gardeners sometimes plant Lychnis dioica to attract butterflies and bees.

Etymology

From New Latin: Lychnis (a genus name from Greek lýchnis, meaning 'lampwort') and dioica (from Greek dioikos, 'having two houses', referring to its separate male and female plants).
