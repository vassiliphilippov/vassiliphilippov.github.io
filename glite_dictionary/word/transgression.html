transgression

1. NOUN

/ trænzˈɡrɛʃən / (AmE), / trænzˈɡrɛʃən / (BrE)
an act that goes against a law, rule, or moral principle; a violation or offense
violation of a law or moral principle

Examples

His repeated transgressions eventually led to his dismissal from the company.
Confessing one’s transgression is the first step toward forgiveness.
Historically, certain transgressions have been punished much more severely than others.

Synset Examples

the boy was punished for the transgressions of his father

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offense - an act that breaks a law or rule
  • Synonym: violation - the act of breaking or failing to comply with a rule or law
  • Antonym: obedience - the act of following rules or orders

Etymology

From Latin transgressio (a going across, transgression) from transgredi (to step across, transgress).

2. NOUN

/ trænzˈɡrɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / tranzˈɡrɛʃ.ən / (BrE)
In geology, the spreading of the sea over land areas, resulting in the deposition of marine sediments on top of terrestrial sediments.
geological covering of land by the sea

Examples

The fossil record shows a clear transgression during the Cretaceous period.
Geologists identified a transgression by studying layers of marine limestone above sandstone.
A major transgression caused the coastline to move many kilometers inland.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: regression - the retreat of the sea from land, exposing previously submerged areas

Etymology

From Latin transgressio (a crossing over), from transgredi (to step across).

3. NOUN

/ trænzˈɡrɛʃən / (AmE), / trænzˈɡrɛʃən / (BrE)
an act of breaking a law, rule, or moral principle; the act of going beyond an accepted limit or boundary
an act of breaking a rule or law

Examples

The judge considered the severity of his transgression before passing sentence.
Her latest transgression violated the school's strict code of conduct.
Throughout history, societies have punished transgressions against their moral values.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offense - a breach of a law or rule
  • Synonym: sin - an act regarded as a violation of a moral rule
  • Antonym: compliance - acting in accordance with a law or rule

Etymology

From Latin 'transgressio' (a going across or over), from 'transgredi' (to step across).
