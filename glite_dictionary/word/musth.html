musth

1. NOUN

/ mʌst / (AmE), / mʌst / (BrE)
a periodic state of heightened sexual drive and aggression in adult male elephants and some other mammals, marked by hormonal changes and glandular discharge
period of sexual excitement in male elephants

Examples

During musth, the bull elephant became unusually aggressive and difficult to control.
Keepers observed a sticky secretion from the elephant's temple, a clear sign he was in musth.
Male elephants in musth may wander for days in search of a mate.

Synset Examples

the frenzied elephant was in musth

Etymology

From Hindi *mast* (intoxicated, in rut), from Persian *mast* (intoxicated).
