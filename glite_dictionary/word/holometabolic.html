holometabolic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhoʊloʊˌmɛtəˈbɑlɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɒləʊˌmɛtəˈbɒlɪk / (BrE)
(of an insect) undergoing complete metamorphosis, with life stages including egg, larva, pupa, and adult.
insect with complete metamorphosis
medical

Examples

Butterflies are classic examples of holometabolic insects, passing through distinct larval and pupal stages.
Scientists compare holometabolic and hemimetabolic life cycles to study insect evolution.
The beetle’s holometabolic development ensures it undergoes a dramatic transformation into its adult form.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hemimetabolic - of insects undergoing incomplete metamorphosis
  • Synonym: metamorphosis - a change of form; in insects, transformation during life cycle

Etymology

From Greek holos (whole) and metabolē (change).
