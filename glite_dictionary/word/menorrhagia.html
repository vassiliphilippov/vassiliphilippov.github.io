menorrhagia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛnəˈreɪdʒiə / (AmE), / ˌmɛnəˈreɪdʒɪə / (BrE)
abnormally heavy or prolonged menstrual bleeding, which may be a symptom of underlying health conditions
abnormally heavy or prolonged menstruation
medical

Examples

Women experiencing menorrhagia may require medical evaluation to determine the underlying cause.
Iron supplements are often recommended for those with menorrhagia to prevent anemia.
Persistent menorrhagia can significantly affect a person's quality of life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: amenorrhea - absence of menstruation
  • Synonym: metrorrhagia - uterine bleeding at irregular intervals

Etymology

From Greek 'mēn' (month) + 'rhēgnynai' (to burst forth).
