Amsinckia grandiflora

1. NOUN

/ æmˈsɪŋkiə ˌɡrændɪˈflɔrə / (AmE), / amˈsɪŋkiə ˌɡrændɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A threatened annual plant native to the western United States, known for large coiled flower spikes.
threatened annual plant of western U.S.

Examples

The critically endangered Amsinckia grandiflora can still be found in a few locations in California.
Conservationists are working to protect the remaining populations of Amsinckia grandiflora.
Botanists study the coiled flower spikes of Amsinckia grandiflora to learn more about its unique morphology.

Etymology

The genus name honors German politician Wilhelm Amsinck; species from Latin 'grandis' (large) + 'flora' (flower).
