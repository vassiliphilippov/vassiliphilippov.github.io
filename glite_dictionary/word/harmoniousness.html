harmoniousness

1. NOUN

/ hɑrˈmoʊniəsnəs / (AmE), / hɑːˈməʊniəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being compatible or forming a pleasing, coordinated whole, especially in opinions, actions, or elements
the state of being harmonious or compatible

Examples

The harmoniousness of their collaboration led to a successful project.
There was a notable harmoniousness in the way the policies were implemented across departments.
Many admired the harmoniousness of the design, which balanced color and form.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmony - a pleasing arrangement or combination, especially of sounds or people
  • Antonym: discordance - lack of agreement or harmony

Etymology

From 'harmonious' + '-ness'; 'harmonious' from Latin 'harmonia' (harmony).

2. NOUN

/ hɑːrˈmoʊniəsnəs / (AmE), / hɑːˈməʊniəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being harmonious; a pleasing or consistent arrangement of parts, especially relating to sound
the quality of being harmonious

Examples

The harmoniousness of the choir's singing delighted everyone in the audience.
He admired the harmoniousness in the design of the garden, where every plant seemed perfectly placed.
Critics praised the harmoniousness of the orchestra's performance during the concert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmony - a pleasing arrangement of parts in music or other areas
  • Antonym: dissonance - lack of harmony or agreement, especially in sound

Etymology

From 'harmonious' + '-ness'; 'harmonious' from Latin 'harmoniosus', from Greek 'harmonía' (agreement, concord).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
