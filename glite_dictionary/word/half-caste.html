half-caste

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæf ˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈhɑːf ˌkɑːst / (BrE)
An offensive term for a person whose parents are from different racial groups or cultures.
offensive term for a person of mixed race
offensive

Examples

The book discusses the challenges faced by half-caste children growing up in colonial societies.
He was unfairly labeled a half-caste because of his mixed heritage.
Historical documents often used the term half-caste, though it is now recognized as offensive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mixed-race - a person whose parents are from different racial backgrounds
  • Synonym: mulatto - an offensive term for a person of mixed black and white ancestry

Etymology

From 'half' + 'caste', meaning partially of a particular caste, originally used in colonial contexts.
