unwooded

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwʊdɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈwʊdɪd / (BrE)
(of land) not covered with trees or forest; lacking woods or forestation.
not covered with trees

Examples

The hikers walked across the unwooded plain, exposed to the bright afternoon sun.
The estate features both unwooded meadows and densely forested areas.
Much of the national park is unwooded, offering wide-open views of the surrounding hills.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wooded - covered with trees; having woods

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'un-' (not) and 'wooded' (covered with trees).
