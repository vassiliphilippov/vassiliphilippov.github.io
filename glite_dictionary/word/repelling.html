repelling

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈpɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪˈpelɪŋ / (BrE)
extremely unpleasant or offensive; causing strong dislike or disgust
causing strong disgust or aversion

Examples

The garbage gave off a repelling odor that made everyone wrinkle their noses.
She found the idea of cruelty to animals utterly repelling.
His repelling behavior at the dinner table shocked all the guests.

Synset Examples

a disgusting smell
distasteful language
a loathsome disease
the idea of eating meat is repellent to me
revolting food
a wicked stench

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repellent - arousing intense distaste or aversion
  • Synonym: disgusting - very unpleasant; causing a strong sense of dislike
  • Antonym: attractive - pleasing or appealing to the senses

Etymology

From the verb 'repel', Latin 'repellere' (to drive back) + '-ing' forming the present participle/adjective.
