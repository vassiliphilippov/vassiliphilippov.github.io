salmon

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæm.ən / (AmE), / ˈsæm.ən / (BrE)
The flesh of a salmon, eaten as food.
flesh of a salmon (food)

Examples

I ordered grilled salmon with a side of vegetables for dinner.
Fresh salmon is a popular ingredient in sushi dishes around the world.
The chef prepared a delicious salmon fillet with lemon and herbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trout - flesh of a trout (food)

Etymology

From Old French saumon, from Latin salmo, salmonis.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsæmən / (AmE), / ˈsæmən / (BrE)
a large edible fish of northern waters that is born in freshwater, migrates to the sea, and returns to freshwater to spawn
large migratory fish; food and game fish

Examples

Grilled salmon is one of my favorite dishes for dinner.
Every spring, salmon swim upstream to lay their eggs.
Wild salmon is usually more expensive than farmed salmon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trout - a similar type of fish related to salmon

Etymology

From Old French saumon, from Latin salmo, salmonis.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsæm.ən / (AmE), / ˈsæm.ən / (BrE)
A pale pinkish-orange color, resembling the flesh of a salmon.
pale pinkish-orange color

Examples

She painted her bedroom walls a soft salmon to create a warm, inviting atmosphere.
The invitation was printed on thick salmon-colored paper for a touch of elegance.
He wore a tie in a subtle salmon shade that matched his pocket square.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coral - a pinkish-orange color similar to that of coral
  • Synonym: peach - a soft pinkish-orange color like the outer skin of a peach
  • Degree Lower: rose - a pale red to pink color
  • Degree Higher: orange - a vivid reddish-yellow color

Etymology

From the resemblance to the flesh of the salmon fish.
