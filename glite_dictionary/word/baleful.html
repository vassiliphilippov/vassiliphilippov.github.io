baleful

1. ADJ

/ ˈbeɪlfəl / (AmE), / ˈbeɪlfʊl / (BrE)
threatening harm; menacing or foreshadowing evil or tragedy.
threatening evil or harm

Examples

She gave him a baleful glare as he walked into the room.
The movie's soundtrack set a baleful tone that made the audience uneasy.
Dark, baleful clouds gathered overhead, hinting at an approaching storm.

Synset Examples

a baleful look
forbidding thunderclouds
his tone became menacing
ominous rumblings of discontent
sinister storm clouds
a sinister smile
his threatening behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sinister - suggesting or threatening evil; ominous
  • Synonym: ominous - giving the impression that something bad is going to happen
  • Antonym: benign - gentle and kindly; not harmful

Etymology

From Old English bælfull, from 'balu' (evil, harm) + 'full'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbeɪlfəl / (AmE), / ˈbeɪlfʊl / (BrE)
threatening harm or evil; having a menacing or sinister quality
threatening or menacing

Examples

The villain gave a baleful glare that silenced the room.
Dark clouds loomed overhead, casting a baleful shadow across the landscape.
The owl fixed its prey with a baleful stare from the tree branch.

Synset Examples

the Florida eagles have a fierce baleful look

Related Terms

  • Synonym: menacing - suggesting the presence of danger; threatening
  • Antonym: benign - gentle, kindly, not harmful

Etymology

From Old English *bealeful*, from *bæle* (evil, harm) + *-ful* (full of).
