Vicia faba

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪsi.ə ˈfeɪbə / (AmE), / ˈvɪsi.ə ˈfɑːbə / (BrE)
An Old World upright plant grown mainly for its large flat edible seeds, also used as animal fodder; the broad bean.
broad bean plant (species name)

Examples

Vicia faba is widely cultivated in Mediterranean countries for its nutritious beans.
Farmers often plant Vicia faba to improve soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.
The seeds produced by Vicia faba are a staple ingredient in many traditional dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broad bean - an Old World upright plant with large edible seeds; also known as the fava bean
  • Synonym: fava bean - the seed or plant Vicia faba, used as food or fodder

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Vicia' means vetch and 'faba' means bean.
