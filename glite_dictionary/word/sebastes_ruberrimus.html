Sebastes ruberrimus

1. NOUN

/ səˈbæstiːz ruːˈbɛr.ɪ.məs / (AmE), / sɪˈbæstiːz ruːˈbɛr.ɪ.məs / (BrE)
a large, brightly colored rockfish native to the Pacific coast of North America, noted for its reddish-orange coloration and longevity
a species of large Pacific rockfish

Examples

Commercial fishers must follow strict quotas when catching Sebastes ruberrimus to protect the species.
Divers often spot Sebastes ruberrimus hiding among rocky reefs along the Pacific coast.
The vivid coloration of Sebastes ruberrimus makes it a favorite subject for underwater photographers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yelloweye rockfish - common name for Sebastes ruberrimus

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Sebastes' and species 'ruberrimus' meaning 'very red'.
