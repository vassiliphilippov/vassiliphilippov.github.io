newsdealer

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːzˌdiːlər / (AmE), / ˈnjuːzˌdiːlə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to sell newspapers and sometimes magazines to the public
person who sells newspapers

Examples

The newsdealer greeted his regular customers every morning at the corner stand.
After work, she stopped by the newsdealer to pick up the latest evening edition.
Years ago, a young boy might have worked as a newsdealer in the city center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newsagent - British term for a shopkeeper selling newspapers and periodicals

Etymology

From 'news' + 'dealer', meaning a person who deals in news publications.
