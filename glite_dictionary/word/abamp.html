abamp

1. NOUN

/ əˈbæmp / (AmE), / əˈbamp / (BrE)
a unit of electric current equal to 10 amperes, formerly used in the CGS electromagnetic system
unit of current (10 amperes)

Examples

The old standard measured current in abamp rather than amperes.
One abamp equals ten amperes of current.
Although rarely used today, the term abamp appears in older physics texts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ampere - the SI unit of electric current

Etymology

Shortening of 'abampere', from 'absolute ampere' in the CGS system.
