surf

1. NOUN

/ sɝf / (AmE), / sɜːf / (BrE)
the mass or line of foam formed by waves breaking on the shore of a sea or ocean
waves breaking on the shore

Examples

We watched the surf pound the rocks all afternoon.
Children played in the surf at the edge of the beach.
The sound of the surf helped her fall asleep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wave - a ridge of water moving across the surface of a sea or lake

Etymology

Shortened from obsolete 'suff', possibly from the Hindi 'sarf' (the shoreward surge of the sea).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07358833-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ sɝf / (AmE), / sɜːf / (BrE)
to ride on waves in the sea using a surfboard
ride waves with a surfboard

Examples

Every summer, thousands of people go to Hawaii to surf the famous waves.
She learned how to surf during her vacation in Australia.
He enjoys waking up early to surf before work.

Synset Examples

Californians love to surf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bodyboard - to ride waves lying on a short board (bodyboarding)
  • Synonym: to windsurf - to ride on water on a board with a sail
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01951936-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ sɜrf / (AmE), / sɜːf / (BrE)
to look around casually and randomly, especially through information or channels such as on the internet or television, without having a specific purpose
browse casually (esp. on the internet or TV)

Examples

She likes to surf the internet in the evenings to discover new recipes.
He spent hours surfing channels, hoping to find something interesting to watch.
Many people surf online stores without any intention of buying.

Synset Examples

browse a computer directory
surf the internet or the world wide web

Related Terms

  • Synonym: browse - to look through or glance at casually, especially goods or information

Etymology

Figurative use from surf, referring to riding waves; applied to navigating information sources from the late 1980s.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01317993-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ sɝf / (AmE), / sɜːf / (BrE)
to switch repeatedly from one television channel to another in search of something of interest
switch television channels repeatedly
informal

Examples

He likes to surf late at night, never settling on one show for long.
I was surfing through the channels when I stumbled upon an old movie.
Instead of watching the news, she just surfed until she got bored.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: channel-hop - to repeatedly change television channels

Etymology

Extended from the original sense of 'surf' as moving along the waves, used metaphorically for moving between channels.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00552688-v
Synset POS: v
