sportive

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɔrtɪv / (AmE), / ˈspɔːtɪv / (BrE)
relating to or interested in sports
related to or interested in sports

Examples

The school is known for its sportive atmosphere, with many students participating in athletics.
Her sportive interests include tennis, soccer, and basketball.
He admired her sportive spirit at the annual college games.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: athletic - physically active, strong, and enjoying sports

Etymology

From Middle French *sportif*, from 'sport'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈspɔrtɪv / (AmE), / ˈspɔːtɪv / (BrE)
playful; given to frolicking or light-hearted merrymaking
playful; frolicsome

Examples

Even as adults, their sportive nature kept their spirits young.
The children were sportive in the garden, chasing each other and laughing loudly.
She wore a sportive grin as she pulled another mischievous prank.

Synset Examples

frolicsome students celebrated their graduation with parties and practical jokes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frolicsome - lively and playful
  • Synonym: playful - full of fun and high spirits

Etymology

From Old French *sportif*, from *sport* (amusement, play).
