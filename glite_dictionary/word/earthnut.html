earthnut

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrθˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈɜːθˌnʌt / (BrE)
A truffle; an edible subterranean fungus of the genus Tuber, valued as a delicacy.
edible subterranean fungus; truffle

Examples

French cuisine often features the earthnut as a rare and expensive ingredient.
The earthnut is highly sought after for its unique aroma and flavor in gourmet dishes.
Pigs and dogs are sometimes trained to locate earthnuts underground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: truffle - edible subterranean fungus, especially of the genus Tuber

Etymology

From 'earth' + 'nut', describing the underground, nut-like appearance of the fungus.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɜrθˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈɜːθ.nʌt / (BrE)
any of several edible underground fungi of the genus Tuber, highly valued as delicacies, especially in southwestern Europe; a truffle.
edible underground fungus; truffle

Examples

The chef used freshly harvested earthnuts to add a unique flavor to the dish.
In southwestern Europe, earthnuts are often foraged by specially trained dogs.
The rarity of earthnuts makes them a luxury item in fine dining.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: truffle - an edible subterranean fungus prized as a culinary delicacy

Etymology

From 'earth' + 'nut', referring to an edible thing found underground.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɜrθˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈɜːθ.nʌt / (BrE)
a common European plant (Conopodium majus) with edible tubers that taste like roasted chestnuts
European plant with edible tubers

Examples

The forager eagerly dug up an earthnut from the soft woodland soil.
Traditional recipes often use earthnuts as a flavorful substitute for chestnuts.
She tasted the roasted earthnut and was surprised by its nutty flavor.

Etymology

From Old English eorþnutu, literally 'nut of the earth'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈɚθˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈɜːθ.nʌt / (BrE)
the pod of the peanut plant, containing the edible seeds; also known as groundnut or monkey nut
the pod of the peanut plant

Examples

The farmer harvested a basket full of earthnuts from the field.
Roasted earthnuts are a popular snack in many countries.
She cracked open the earthnut pod to reveal two fresh peanuts inside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groundnut - another term for earthnut, especially in British English
  • Synonym: peanut - edible seed of a leguminous plant; another name for earthnut

Etymology

From 'earth' + 'nut', referring to the nut that develops underground.
