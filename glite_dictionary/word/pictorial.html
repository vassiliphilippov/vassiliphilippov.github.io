pictorial

1. ADJ

/ pɪkˈtɔriəl / (AmE), / pɪkˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
(of language or writing) evoking lifelike images in the mind; vividly descriptive or graphic.
evoking vivid mental images

Examples

The author’s pictorial descriptions made the scenes come alive in my imagination.
Her pictorial language brought the landscape vividly to mind.
The film’s pictorial storytelling drew viewers deep into its world.

Synset Examples

pictorial poetry and prose
graphic accounts of battle
a lifelike portrait
a vivid description

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graphic - describes language or images that are very vivid or lifelike
  • Synonym: vivid - producing powerful feelings or strong, clear images in the mind

Etymology

From Latin pictorius (of painting), from pictor (painter).

2. ADJ

/ pɪkˈtɔriəl / (AmE), / pɪkˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
relating to or consisting of pictures; illustrated with or expressed in pictures
relating to pictures

Examples

The magazine included a pictorial supplement featuring rare photographs of the expedition.
Her presentation was highly pictorial, using images to explain complex ideas.
The exhibition explores the pictorial tradition in nineteenth-century landscape painting.

Synset Examples

pictorial perspective
pictorial records

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illustrative - serving as an example or explanation using pictures
  • Antonym: verbal - relating to words rather than pictures

Etymology

From Latin 'pictorius' (of a painter) from 'pictor' (painter).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02872706-a
Synset POS: a
