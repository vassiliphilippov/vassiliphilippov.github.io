sleeved

1. ADJ

/ sliːvd / (AmE), / sliːvd / (BrE)
(of clothing) having sleeves, especially of a specified kind.
having sleeves (as specified)

Examples

She prefers to wear sleeved dresses even in the summer.
The store offers both sleeveless and sleeved shirts.
I bought a long-sleeved sweater for the winter.

Synset Examples

sleeved garments
short-sleeved

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sleeveless - (of clothing) without sleeves

Etymology

From the noun 'sleeve' + adjective-forming suffix '-ed'.
