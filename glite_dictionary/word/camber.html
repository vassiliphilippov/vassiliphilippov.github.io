camber

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæmbə / (BrE)
the inward or outward tilt of the wheels of a motor vehicle, where the wheels are closer together at the bottom than at the top
inward or outward tilt of vehicle wheels

Examples

A mechanic adjusted the camber to improve the car's handling.
Incorrect camber can cause uneven tire wear and affect steering.
Racing teams often tweak the camber settings for better cornering performance.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæmbər / (AmE), / ˈkæmbə / (BrE)
A slight convex or arched shape in a road or track, especially when the outer edge is higher than the inner edge to help vehicles negotiate curves safely.
road/track slope (outer edge higher)

Examples

Bicyclists appreciate the camber in sharp corners for extra grip.
The racetrack's camber helps cars maintain speed when taking bends.
Proper camber on highways improves drainage and safety in wet conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bank - the incline of a road or track, especially around a curve

Etymology

From Old French *cambre* (arched), from Latin *camber* (curved).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkæmbər / (AmE), / ˈkæmbə / (BrE)
a slight upward curve or convexity along the surface of a road, runway, or other structure to aid drainage or improve function
slight upward curve of a surface (e.g. road)

Examples

The highway was designed with a slight camber to allow rainwater to run off the sides.
You can feel the camber of the road when cycling close to the edge.
Runways are built with a camber to help prevent water from collecting on the surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arch - a curved symmetrical structure spanning an opening

Etymology

From Old French 'camber' (to bend), from Latin 'camur' (arched, bent).

Source: WordNet

4. VERB

/ ˈkæmbər / (AmE), / ˈkæmbə / (BrE)
to curve or cause to curve upward in the middle, typically referring to surfaces such as roads or beams
curve upward in the middle

Examples

The engineers designed the bridge to camber slightly, allowing water to run off easily.
Race car mechanics often camber the wheels to improve cornering performance.
As the beam is installed, workers carefully camber it to prevent future sagging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arch - to curve in the shape of an arch; to have an arched shape

Etymology

From Middle French *camber* (to bend) and Late Latin *camberare* (to arch).
