disposal

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspoʊzəl / (AmE), / dɪˈspəʊzəl / (BrE)
the act or process of getting rid of something
the act of getting rid of something

Examples

The proper disposal of waste is essential to protect the environment.
After dinner, he was responsible for the disposal of leftovers.
Medical facilities have strict rules for the disposal of hazardous materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: removal - the act of taking something away or getting rid of it
  • Antonym: retention - the act of keeping something instead of disposing of it

Etymology

From Old French 'disposer' (to arrange), from Latin 'disponere' (to arrange, set in different places).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈspoʊzəl / (AmE), / dɪˈspəʊzəl / (BrE)
a kitchen appliance installed under a sink for grinding up and disposing of food waste
kitchen appliance for food waste

Examples

The disposal jammed after I tried to grind up potato peels.
Installing a new disposal can make kitchen cleanup much easier.
Never put large bones into the disposal, as they can damage the blades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garbage disposal - a kitchen device for grinding food waste
  • Degree Higher: sink - a basin with a water supply and drain, typically in a kitchen

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈspoʊzəl / (AmE), / dɪˈspəʊzəl / (BrE)
the power or authority to use something or someone as one wishes
power to use something or someone

Examples

You will have several assistants at your disposal during the event.
The committee put all their data at the scientists' disposal.
With so many resources at his disposal, success was almost inevitable.

Synset Examples

used all the resources at his disposal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: command - control or authority over something
  • Synonym: control - the power to direct or manage

Etymology

From Late Latin *dispositio* (arrangement, management) via Old French *disposal*.
