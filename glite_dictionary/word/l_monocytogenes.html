L. monocytogenes

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛl məˌnɑsɪˈtɑdʒəˌniːz / (AmE), / ˌɛl məˌnɒsɪˈtɒdʒəˌniːz / (BrE)
a species of bacteria in the genus Listeria that can cause serious infections such as meningitis, encephalitis, and listeriosis in humans and animals
bacterial species causing listeriosis
medical

Examples

L. monocytogenes is a significant cause of food-borne illness in many countries worldwide.
Pregnant women are advised to avoid certain foods because they may be contaminated with L. monocytogenes.
The medical team conducted tests and confirmed the presence of L. monocytogenes in the patient's system.

Etymology

From New Latin: L. as abbreviation for 'Listeria,' and species name monocytogenes meaning 'monocyte-producing.'
