isomorphous

1. ADJ

/ ˌaɪ.səˈmɔr.fəs / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.səˈmɔː.fəs / (BrE)
Having the same or similar appearance or form, but different in origin or nature.
having similar form but different origin

Examples

The two plant species are isomorphous in appearance but differ genetically.
Although the minerals are isomorphous, their chemical compositions are quite distinct.
Some parasites exhibit isomorphous forms at different stages of their life cycle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homomorphic - having the same form or appearance but not necessarily the same function

Etymology

From Greek isomorphos, from iso- (equal) + morphē (form).
