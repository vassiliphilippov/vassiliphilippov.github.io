Acadia

1. PROPN

/ əˈkeɪdiə / (AmE), / əˈkeɪdiə / (BrE)
The French-speaking region in the Canadian Maritime Provinces, historically settled by the French in the 17th century.
French-speaking region in eastern Canada

Examples

Many descendants of the original settlers still live in Acadia today.
The culture of Acadia is celebrated through music, language, and festivals.
Tourists visit Acadia to learn about its unique history and traditions.

Etymology

From French Acadie, likely from the Mi'kmaq word 'akadie' meaning 'place of abundance.'
