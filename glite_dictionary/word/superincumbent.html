superincumbent

1. ADJ

/ ˌsuːpərɪnˈkʌmbənt / (AmE), / ˌsuːpərɪnˈkʌmbənt / (BrE)
lying or resting on something else, especially so as to exert pressure on it
lying or pressing on top of something

Examples

The superincumbent snow caused the roof to collapse under the weight.
Archaeologists carefully removed each superincumbent layer of soil to reach the ancient structure.
The roots were flattened by the superincumbent rocks above them.

Synset Examples

superincumbent layers of dead plants cut off the air and arrested decomposition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overlying - lying on top of something
  • Antonym: underlying - lying beneath something

Etymology

From Latin superincumbens, superincumbent-, present participle of superincumbere (to lie or rest on).
