fervid

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɝː.vɪd / (AmE), / ˈfɜː.vɪd / (BrE)
having or showing intense passion or feeling; fervent
intensely passionate or emotional

Examples

His fervid speech inspired the entire audience to volunteer for the cause.
She wrote fervid letters expressing her hopes for a brighter future.
The debate was marked by fervid exchanges between the opposing sides.

Synset Examples

ardent love
an ardent lover
a fervent desire to change society
a fervent admirer
fiery oratory
an impassioned appeal
a torrid love affair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fervent - having or displaying passionate intensity
  • Synonym: ardent - very enthusiastic or passionate
  • Antonym: apathetic - showing or feeling no interest, enthusiasm, or concern

Etymology

From Latin fervidus (glowing, boiling), from fervere (to boil, seethe).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɝː.vɪd / (AmE), / ˈfɜː.vɪd / (BrE)
(archaic) Extremely hot, burning, or glowing.
extremely hot; burning (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The travelers sought shelter from the fervid sun that blazed overhead.
A fervid glow emanated from the blacksmith’s forge late into the night.
Even the desert animals hid until the fervid afternoon cooled into early evening.

Synset Examples

the fervent heat … merely communicated a genial warmth to their half-torpid systems
set out … when the fervid heat subsides

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiery - Very hot; burning strongly.
  • Antonym: cool - Of fairly low temperature; not hot or warm.

Etymology

From Latin fervidus (boiling, hot, glowing), from fervere (to boil).
