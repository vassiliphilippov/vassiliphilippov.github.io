inflammability

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌflæməˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪnˌflæməˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being easily set on fire and burning rapidly
ease of catching fire

Examples

The inflammability of the chemical makes it dangerous to store near heat sources.
Manufacturers must label products to indicate their level of inflammability.
Because of its high inflammability, the solvent must be handled with care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flammability - the quality of being easily ignited and burning quickly
  • Antonym: nonflammability - the quality of not being easily ignited or burned

Etymology

From Latin inflammare (to set on fire) + -bility, modeled after 'flammability'.
