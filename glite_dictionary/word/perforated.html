perforated

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɝː.fəˌreɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.fəˌreɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
having one or more holes or a series of holes cut, punched, or bored through
having holes passing through

Examples

The doctor explained that the patient had a perforated eardrum after the accident.
She tore the perforated sheet of stamps along the dotted line.
The metallic filter is made from a perforated plate to allow air circulation.

Synset Examples

pierced ears
a perforated eardrum
a punctured balloon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punctured - having a hole or holes, typically unintentionally, in a surface

Etymology

From Latin perforatus, past participle of perforare (to pierce through, bore through), from per- (through) + forare (to bore).

3. ADJ

/ ˈpɝː.fəˌreɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.fəˌreɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
Having one or more holes made through something, especially in a regular pattern.
having one or more holes made through

Examples

The document was printed on perforated paper that could easily be torn into sections.
The chef used a perforated spoon to drain the vegetables before serving them.
After the accident, the patient was diagnosed with a perforated eardrum.

Synset Examples

pierced ears
a perforated eardrum
a punctured balloon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pierced - having a hole made through, especially for decoration or utility
  • Synonym: punctured - having a hole made by puncturing, usually abruptly or by accident

Etymology

From Latin perforatus, past participle of perforare (to bore through), from per- (through) + forare (to bore).
