dysomia

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈsoʊmiə / (AmE), / dɪˈsəʊmiə / (BrE)
Impairment or disturbance of the sense of smell.
impaired sense of smell
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with dysomia after reporting problems detecting common odors.
Some viral infections can cause temporary dysomia in affected individuals.
Researchers are studying the link between dysomia and certain neurological disorders.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: anosmia - loss or absence of the sense of smell

Etymology

From Greek 'dys-' (bad, difficult) + 'osme' (smell).
