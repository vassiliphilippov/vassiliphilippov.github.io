pre-Columbian

1. ADJ

/ ˌpriː kəˈlʌmbiən / (AmE), / ˌpriː kəˈlʌmbiən / (BrE)
Relating to the history, cultures, or artifacts of the Americas before the arrival of Christopher Columbus in 1492.
before Columbus's arrival in the Americas

Examples

The museum has a remarkable collection of pre-Columbian artifacts from Central America.
Scholars study pre-Columbian civilizations such as the Maya and the Aztec.
Many pre-Columbian structures still stand after centuries of exposure to the elements.

Etymology

From Latin-derived prefix 'pre-' (before) + 'Columbian', referring to Christopher Columbus.
