seaboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsiːˌbɔːd / (BrE)
the part of a country that borders or is next to the sea, especially as a coastal region or resort area
shoreline or coastal region

Examples

Many wealthy families own summer homes along the seaboard.
Tourists flock to the southern seaboard every year for its beautiful beaches.
Fishing villages dot the northern seaboard of the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coast - the land next to the sea; the seashore
  • Antonym: inland - away from the coast, toward the interior of a country

Etymology

From Middle English, formed by combining 'sea' and 'board' (edge).
