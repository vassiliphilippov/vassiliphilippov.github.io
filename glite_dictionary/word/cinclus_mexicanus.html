Cinclus mexicanus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋ.kləs mɛk.sɪˈkɑː.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋ.kləs mɛk.sɪˈkɑː.nəs / (BrE)
a species of aquatic songbird, commonly known as the American dipper, found in western North America and notable for its habit of diving in streams
aquatic North American songbird (American dipper)

Examples

The Cinclus mexicanus is frequently seen foraging along rocky streams.
Researchers observed the Cinclus mexicanus diving underwater to catch insects.
Unlike most songbirds, the Cinclus mexicanus is well adapted to aquatic habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American dipper - common name for Cinclus mexicanus, an aquatic North American bird
  • Synonym: water ouzel - common term for birds in the Cinclus genus

Etymology

Genus name from Latin 'cinclus' (a kind of water bird); species name from Latin 'mexicanus' meaning 'of Mexico'.
