ravenousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈræv.ə.nəs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈræv.ən.əs.nəs / (BrE)
An intense or excessive desire to eat; extreme hunger.
excessive desire to eat

Examples

After two days without food, his ravenousness made it difficult to think about anything but eating.
The hikers' ravenousness was obvious as they devoured their meal in silence.
She tried to ignore her ravenousness during the long, drawn-out meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hunger - the physical sensation of needing food
  • Antonym: satiety - the state of being satisfactorily full and unable to take on more
  • Degree Lower: appetite - a natural desire to satisfy a bodily need, especially for food

Etymology

From 'ravenous', which comes from Old French 'ravineux' (‘greedy’), from Latin 'rapina' (‘plundering’).
