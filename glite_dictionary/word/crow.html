crow

1. VERB

/ kroʊ / (AmE), / krəʊ / (BrE)
to make a loud, triumphant sound or speech expressing joy, pride, or satisfaction
express triumph or satisfaction loudly

Examples

She couldn't help but crow about her success in the competition.
The team crowed with delight after winning the championship.
He crows so much about his achievements that it annoys his colleagues.

Synset Examples

She crowed with joy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boast - to talk with excessive pride and self-satisfaction
  • Synonym: to gloat - to dwell with smugness on one's own success
  • Antonym: to complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance

Etymology

Imitative origin, from the sound of a crowing cock.

2. NOUN

/ kroʊ / (AmE), / krəʊ / (BrE)
The loud, shrill cry of a cock (rooster), or an imitation of this sound.
the cry of a rooster

Examples

The crow of the rooster woke us up at dawn.
He tried to imitate the crow loudly, much to everyone's amusement.
The farm was quiet except for the sudden crow coming from the henhouse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crowing - the act of making a cock's characteristic sound
  • Degree Lower: cackle - the harsh sound made by a hen or goose
Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ kroʊ / (AmE), / krəʊ / (BrE)
a large, black bird with a harsh, loud call, belonging to the genus Corvus
large black bird with a harsh call

Examples

A crow perched noisily on the telephone wire near the house.
In folklore, seeing a crow is sometimes considered an omen.
A flock of crows gathered in the field at dusk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raven - a larger member of the genus Corvus with a heavier bill and shaggier throat feathers
  • Synonym: rook - a Eurasian member of the crow family distinguished by its bare, greyish face

Etymology

Old English *crāwe*, imitative of the bird's call.

5. VERB

/ kroʊ / (AmE), / krəʊ / (BrE)
(of a cock or similar bird) to utter a loud, shrill call, especially at dawn; also, to make a sound resembling this.
to utter a shrill call like a rooster

Examples

The rooster began to crow at the break of dawn, waking the whole farm.
You could hear the peacocks crowing loudly from across the zoo.
Every morning, the cocks crow before sunrise in this village.

Synset Examples

The cocks crowed all morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cackle - to make a sharp, broken noise, like a hen

Etymology

Old English *crāwan* (to crow as a cock), of imitative origin.

6. VERB

/ kroʊ / (AmE), / krəʊ / (BrE)
to talk proudly or boastfully about something with great satisfaction or triumph
to boast or gloat with satisfaction

Examples

She couldn't help but crow about her promotion at the meeting.
After winning the championship, the team crowed over their rivals for weeks.
He likes to crow whenever he gets top marks in class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boast - to talk with excessive pride and self-satisfaction about achievements
  • Synonym: to gloat - to dwell on one's own success or another's misfortune with smugness

Etymology

From Old English *crāwan* (to crow, make a crowing sound), originally imitative of the bird's cry, later extended figuratively to boastfulness.
