Crow

1. NOUN

/ kɹoʊ / (AmE), / kɹəʊ / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people originally living in eastern Montana and surrounding areas
member of a Native American people from Montana

Examples

The Crow were known for their horse culture on the Great Plains.
A proud Crow elder shared stories of his ancestors at the powwow.
Many Crow people still live on their reservation in Montana today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Siouan - a family of North American Indian languages including the Crow language

Etymology

From the Native American people's own name "Apsáalooke," which was interpreted by French traders as "people of the crow."

2. NOUN

/ kroʊ / (AmE), / krəʊ / (BrE)
the Siouan language spoken by the Crow people of the northern Plains of the United States
Siouan language of the Crow people

Examples

She is learning to speak Crow with her grandmother.
Several linguists have published dictionaries of the Crow language.
The ceremony was conducted entirely in Crow by the tribal elders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Siouan - family of languages spoken by Native tribes in North America
  • Synonym: Dakota - another Siouan language spoken by Dakota people

Etymology

Named after the Crow people, whose own name for themselves is Apsáalooke.

3. PROPN

/ kroʊ / (AmE), / krəʊ / (BrE)
A small constellation in the Southern Hemisphere, near Virgo, representing a crow in classical mythology.
constellation in the Southern Hemisphere

Examples

The Crow constellation is easiest to spot in the April night sky.
Ancient astronomers identified the Crow as representing the bird from myth.
The stars of the Crow form a distinctive quadrilateral shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Corvus - the Latin name for the Crow constellation

Etymology

From Latin 'Corvus', meaning 'crow', the name given to this constellation in classical antiquity.
