pondering

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɑndərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɒndərɪŋ / (BrE)
Engaged in deep or serious thought; reflective and contemplative.
deeply thoughtful; reflective

Examples

She sat by the window, pondering the future and what it might hold for her.
After hearing the news, he spent the afternoon pondering his next steps.
The novel features a pondering protagonist who questions the meaning of life.

Synset Examples

Byron lives on not only in his poetry, but also in his creation of the ‘Byronic hero’ - the persona of a brooding melancholy young man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contemplative - engaged in deep thought
  • Antonym: thoughtless - lacking consideration or deep thought

Etymology

From the verb 'ponder,' itself from Latin 'ponderare' (to weigh).
