Tursiops

1. NOUN

/ tɝˈsaɪ.ɑps / (AmE), / tɜːˈsaɪ.ɒps / (BrE)
A genus of marine mammals in the family Delphinidae, which includes the bottlenose dolphins.
genus of dolphins

Examples

The common bottlenose dolphin is classified under the genus Tursiops.
Scientists observed a group of Tursiops in coastal waters.
Several species within Tursiops are known for their high intelligence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dolphin - a small toothed whale of the family Delphinidae

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'tursis' (dolphin) and 'ops' (face).
