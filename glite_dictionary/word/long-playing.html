long-playing

1. ADJ

/ lɔŋˈpleɪɪŋ / (AmE), / lɒŋˈpleɪɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a phonograph record) designed to play for a longer duration at a slower speed than earlier records, typically at 33⅓ revolutions per minute.
record designed for longer play at slower speed

Examples

He still owns a collection of long-playing records from the 1960s.
The album was released on a long-playing vinyl, surprising many music enthusiasts.
Collectors value original long-playing albums for their rich sound and vintage appeal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: LP - abbreviation for 'long-playing' (record)
  • Antonym: single - a record designed for a short playtime, usually one song per side
