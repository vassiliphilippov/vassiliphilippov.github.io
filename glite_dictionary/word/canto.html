canto

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.toʊ / (AmE), / ˈkæn.təʊ / (BrE)
A principal division of a long poem, especially in works such as epics.
division of a long poem

Examples

The epic contains twenty-four cantos, each focusing on a different hero's journey.
In the third canto, the poet introduces the main antagonist.
Students analyzed the themes present in each canto of the poem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chapter - a main division of a book, typically with a number or title

Etymology

From Italian 'canto' (song), from Latin 'cantus' (song).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.toʊ / (AmE), / ˈkæn.təʊ / (BrE)
the highest vocal part, usually the melody, in a piece of choral music
highest part in choral music

Examples

The canto often carries the main melody in Renaissance choral works.
In this arrangement, the canto is sung by the sopranos.
He preferred to sing the canto because of its melodic prominence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soprano - highest singing voice in choral music

Etymology

Italian, literally 'song'; from Latin 'cantus' (song).
