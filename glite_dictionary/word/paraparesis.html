paraparesis

1. NOUN

/ pærəpəˈrisɪs / (AmE), / parəpəˈriːsɪs / (BrE)
partial paralysis or weakness affecting both legs
partial paralysis of both legs
medical

Examples

After the accident, he developed paraparesis and required assistance to walk.
Multiple sclerosis can sometimes result in paraparesis as the disease progresses.
The neurologist diagnosed the patient with paraparesis after noting the weakness in both legs.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: paraplegia - complete paralysis of both legs
  • Antonym: hemiparesis - weakness of one side of the body

Etymology

From Greek 'para-' (beside, abnormal) and 'paresis' (weakness).
