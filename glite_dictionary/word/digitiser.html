digitiser

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪdʒɪtaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈdɪdʒɪtaɪzə / (BrE)
A device that converts analogue signals into digital signals.
device converting analogue to digital signals

Examples

The engineer used a digitiser to convert the old magnetic tapes into digital files.
This laboratory recently upgraded its digitiser to improve the quality of signal processing.
Without a high-resolution digitiser, the accuracy of the data collection would be compromised.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digitizer - alternative spelling (US) for 'digitiser', device converting analogue to digital signals

Etymology

From 'digitize' + '-er'; 'digitize' from Latin 'digitus', meaning 'finger' or 'number'.
