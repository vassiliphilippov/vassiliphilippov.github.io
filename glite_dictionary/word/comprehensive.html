comprehensive

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːmprɪˈhɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ˌkɒmprɪˈhɛnsɪv / (BrE)
A comprehensive examination testing a student's proficiency in a particular field of study, often required for an advanced degree.
academic proficiency examination

Examples

After months of preparation, she finally passed her comprehensive in molecular biology.
Graduate students must often take a comprehensive before proceeding to their dissertation.
He was nervous about the upcoming comprehensive, which would cover all aspects of the curriculum.

Synset Examples

she took her comps in English literature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comps - abbreviation for 'comprehensive examination'

2. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːmprɪˈhɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ˌkɒmprɪˈhɛnsɪv / (BrE)
Including or dealing with all or nearly all elements or aspects of something; broad in scope.
broad in scope; inclusive

Examples

The report offered a comprehensive overview of the country's healthcare system.
We need a comprehensive plan to address all aspects of the project.
She conducted a comprehensive review before making her final decision.

Synset Examples

a comprehensive survey of world affairs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extensive - covering a large area or scope
  • Antonym: narrow - limited in scope or extent

Etymology

From Medieval Latin comprehensivus, from Latin comprehendere (to seize, include).

including all or nearly all elements or aspects of something; complete or thorough.
including all or nearly all aspects

Examples

Related Terms
