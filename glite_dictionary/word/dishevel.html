to dishevel

1. VERB

/ dɪˈʃɛvəl / (AmE), / dɪˈʃɛv(ə)l / (BrE)
to make (hair, clothes, or appearance) untidy or disordered; to rumple or disarrange
make untidy or disordered

Examples

She hurriedly tried to smooth her disheveled hair before the interview.
The children managed to dishevel every cushion on the sofa in five minutes.
A brisk walk in the wind can easily dishevel even the neatest hairstyle.

Synset Examples

The strong wind tousled my hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rumple - to make something creased or wrinkled
  • Synonym: to tousle - to make (especially hair) untidy
  • Antonym: to neaten - to make neat or tidy

Etymology

From Old French descheveler, from des- (dis-) + chevel (hair, from Latin capillus).
