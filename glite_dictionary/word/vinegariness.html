vinegariness

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪnəɡərɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈvɪnɪɡərɪnɪs / (BrE)
a sour or acidic quality resembling that of vinegar
the quality of being sour like vinegar

Examples

The vinegariness of the salad dressing added a sharp tang to the greens.
Some people dislike the vinegariness of certain pickled vegetables.
Over time, the wine developed a noticeable vinegariness that ruined its flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sourness - the quality of having a sour taste

Etymology

From 'vinegar' plus the suffix '-iness', denoting a quality or state.
