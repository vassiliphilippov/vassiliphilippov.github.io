sacculated

1. ADJ

/ ˈsækjəˌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈsakjʊleɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of an organ or structure) formed with or having sac-like expansions or pouches.
having sac-like expansions
medical

Examples

The patient's colon was found to be sacculated during the examination.
Some types of bacteria produce sacculated spores as part of their life cycle.
The lungs of certain mammals are highly sacculated, increasing surface area for gas exchange.

Synset Examples

the alimentary tract is partially sacculated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saccate - having the form of a sac or pouch

Etymology

From Latin 'sacculus' (small bag or pouch) + '-ated' (having).
