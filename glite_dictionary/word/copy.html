copy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːpi / (AmE), / ˈkɒpi / (BrE)
a reproduction of a written or printed document, especially one made to serve as an official record or for reference
reproduction of a written or printed document

Examples

Please bring a copy of your birth certificate to the office.
The school keeps a copy of every student's record for reference.
He requested a certified copy of the legal document.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: original - the first or primary form from which copies are made

Etymology

From Old French copie, from Latin copia meaning 'transcript, abundance'.
