insensitive

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsɛnsətɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛnsɪtɪv / (BrE)
not responsive to physical stimuli; not affected or changed by something such as touch, heat, or light.
not responsive to physical stimuli

Examples

Some bacteria are insensitive to certain antibiotics, making infections harder to treat.
This material is insensitive to changes in temperature and remains stable.
The device is insensitive to minor vibrations, ensuring accurate measurements.

Synset Examples

insensitive to radiation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unresponsive - not reacting to stimuli or influences

Etymology

From Latin 'insensibilis' meaning 'without feeling'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02112655-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsɛn.sə.tɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛn.sɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
lacking concern for the feelings or needs of others; not aware of or affected by what is happening to people around them.
not caring about others' feelings or needs

Examples

She was criticized for her insensitive comments about the tragedy.
It was insensitive of him not to notice how upset she was.
The company was accused of being insensitive to its employees' concerns.

Synset Examples

insensitive to the needs of the patients

Related Terms

  • Synonym: callous - showing or having an insensitive and cruel disregard for others
  • Antonym: sensitive - quick to detect or respond to slight changes, signals, or influences; easily hurt or affected

Etymology

From in- (not) + sensitive, from Latin sentire (to feel).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02114041-a
Synset POS: a
