withdrawn

1. ADJ

/ wɪðˈdrɔn / (AmE), / wɪðˈdrɔːn / (BrE)
quiet and preferring to be alone rather than to be with other people; reserved or introspective in manner.
quiet, reserved; introspective

Examples

She became increasingly withdrawn after moving to a new city.
Although he was withdrawn at first, he eventually opened up to his classmates.
His withdrawn nature made it difficult for him to make friends.

Synset Examples

a quiet indrawn man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reserved - slow to reveal emotions or opinions; shy or restrained
  • Antonym: outgoing - friendly and socially confident; extroverted

Etymology

Past participle of 'withdraw', from Middle English 'withdrauen'.

2. ADJ

/ wɪðˈdrɔn / (AmE), / wɪðˈdrɔːn / (BrE)
not wanting to communicate with other people; preferring to be alone and avoiding social interaction
preferring to be alone; lacking sociability

Examples

Since the incident, he has become noticeably more withdrawn and rarely speaks to anyone.
The withdrawn child preferred reading alone to playing with classmates.
After moving to a new city, she felt withdrawn and isolated for several months.

Synset Examples

lived an unsocial reclusive life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reclusive - avoiding the company of others; living in seclusion
  • Synonym: unsociable - not enjoying or seeking the company of others
  • Antonym: sociable - enjoying or seeking the company of others

Etymology

Past participle of 'withdraw,' from Middle English 'withdrawen'.

WordNet ID: oewn-02258435-s
