thoughtless

1. ADJ

/ ˈθɔt.ləs / (AmE), / ˈθɔːt.ləs / (BrE)
not considering how your words or actions may affect other people; lacking concern for others
not caring about others; inconsiderate

Examples

It was thoughtless of him to leave without saying goodbye.
Her thoughtless remark hurt his feelings.
Many accidents are caused by thoughtless drivers.

Synset Examples

the thoughtless saying of a great princess on being informed that the people had no bread: ‘Let them eat cake’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inconsiderate - not thinking about the feelings of other people
  • Antonym: considerate - showing concern for the needs and feelings of others

Etymology

From 'thought' + suffix '-less' (without).

2. ADJ

/ ˈθɔt.ləs / (AmE), / ˈθɔːt.ləs / (BrE)
showing a lack of consideration or careful thought for others or for the consequences of one's actions
lacking careful thought or consideration

Examples

It was thoughtless of him to leave without saying goodbye.
Her thoughtless remarks hurt his feelings deeply.
The company's thoughtless decision led to many employees losing their jobs.

Synset Examples

the debate turned into thoughtless bickering

Related Terms

  • Antonym: considerate - showing careful thought toward others; thoughtful
  • Synonym: inconsiderate - not showing concern for the needs or feelings of others
  • Synonym: careless - not giving sufficient attention or thought to avoiding harm or errors

Etymology

From 'thought' + '-less', meaning 'without thought'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02429304-a
Synset POS: a
