coriander

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔriˈændər / (AmE), / ˌkɒriˈændə / (BrE)
An aromatic plant of the parsley family, with pungent leaves and seeds used in cooking.
aromatic herb used in cooking

Examples

She sprinkled fresh coriander over the curry before serving.
I bought a bunch of coriander for the salsa recipe.
Some people find the taste of coriander leaves to be quite soapy.

Etymology

From Old French 'coriandre', from Latin 'coriandrum', from Greek 'koriannon'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɔriˈændɚ / (AmE), / ˌkɒriˈændə / (BrE)
An aromatic herb, whose leaves and seeds are used as a seasoning or garnish in cooking.
aromatic herb used as seasoning

Examples

She sprinkled chopped coriander over the salad for extra flavor.
The recipe calls for both ground coriander and cumin.
Fresh coriander leaves add a bright, citrusy note to many Indian dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cilantro - the fresh leaves of the coriander plant, especially as called in American English

Etymology

From Old French coriandre, from Latin coriandrum, from Ancient Greek koriannon.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07833291-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌkɔriˈændɚ / (AmE), / ˌkɒriˈændə / (BrE)
The dried seeds of the coriander plant, used whole or ground as a spice in cooking.
dried seeds used as a spice

Examples

The recipe calls for a teaspoon of coriander to add a warm, citrusy flavor.
She ground the coriander seeds in a mortar and pestle before adding them to the stew.
Indian cuisine often features coriander as a key ingredient in spice blends.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cilantro - leaves of the coriander plant, used as an herb

Etymology

From Latin coriandrum, from Greek koriannon.
