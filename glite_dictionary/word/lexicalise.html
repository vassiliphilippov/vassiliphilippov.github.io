to lexicalise

1. VERB

/ ˈlɛksɪkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈlɛksɪkəlaɪz / (BrE)
(of a concept, phrase, or form) to make or coin into a word, or to accept a new word into the vocabulary of a language.
to make or adopt as a word

Examples

Linguists often lexicalise new concepts when they enter popular discourse.
Some expressions never become lexicalised and remain as idioms or phrases.
It can take years for a borrowed term to fully lexicalise in a language.

Synset Examples

The concept expressed by German ‘Gemuetlichkeit’ is not lexicalized in English

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coin - to invent or create (a new word or phrase)
  • Synonym: to lexicalize - alternate spelling (American) of 'lexicalise'

Etymology

From 'lexical' (relating to words) + '-ise', from Greek lexis (word) via Latin and French.
