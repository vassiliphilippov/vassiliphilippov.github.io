Kazakh

1. NOUN

/ kəˈzɑk / (AmE), / kəˈzɑːk / (BrE)
the Turkic language spoken primarily by the Kazakh people of Kazakhstan and neighboring regions
Turkic language of Kazakhstan

Examples

Many people in Kazakhstan speak Kazakh as their first language.
She is taking classes to learn how to write in Kazakh.
The official documents are available in both Russian and Kazakh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Uzbek - the Turkic language spoken by the Uzbek people
  • Degree Higher: Russian - East Slavic language spoken in Russia and other countries

Etymology

From Russian 'казах' (kazakh), from Kazakh 'қазақ' (qazaq), referring to the people and their language.

2. PROPN

/ kəˈzɑk / (AmE), / kəˈzæk / (BrE)
a country in Central Asia, south of Russia and northeast of the Caspian Sea; formerly part of the Soviet Union as the Kazakh Soviet Socialist Republic from 1936 to 1991.
country in Central Asia formerly a Soviet republic

Examples

The people of Kazakh are proud of their heritage and culture.
She studied the political history of Kazakh during the Soviet era.
Trade relations between Kazakh and its neighbors have strengthened in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kazakhstan - the Republic of Kazakhstan; Central Asian country

Etymology

From Russian 'Kazakh', from Turkic 'qazaq' meaning 'free man, nomad'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09042096-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ kɑːˈzɑk / (AmE), / kəˈzɑːk / (BrE)
a member of a Muslim Turkic ethnic group native to Kazakhstan and parts of western Asia
member of a Turkic ethnic group of Kazakhstan

Examples

The Kazakh people have a rich tradition of hospitality and nomadic heritage.
She met a Kazakh family while traveling through Central Asia.
Many Kazakhs still celebrate the ancient festival of Nauryz.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kazakhstan - country in Central Asia, homeland of the Kazakh people

Etymology

From Russian 'kazakh', from Old Turkic 'qazāq' meaning 'free man, adventurer'.
