Fraxinus

1. PROPN

/ ˈfræksɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈfræksɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees and shrubs commonly known as ash, native to northern temperate regions.
genus of ash trees and shrubs

Examples

The leaves of Fraxinus species are typically pinnate and arranged oppositely on the stem.
Several Fraxinus trees in the park have been affected by emerald ash borer infestation.
Botanists conducted a survey of Fraxinus populations across the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ash - a tree of the genus Fraxinus

Etymology

From Latin *fraxinus* (ash tree).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfræks.ɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfraks.ɪ.nəs / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees known as ashes, found chiefly in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
genus of ash trees

Examples

Fraxinus species are commonly called ash trees in North America and Europe.
The wood of Fraxinus is prized for making baseball bats and tool handles.
Scientists are studying the decline of Fraxinus due to invasive beetles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ash - deciduous tree of the genus Fraxinus

Etymology

From Latin 'fraxinus' (ash tree).
