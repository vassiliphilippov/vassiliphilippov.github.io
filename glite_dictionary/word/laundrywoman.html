laundrywoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔndriˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈlɔːndrɪˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman whose job is to wash clothes and linens for others
woman paid to wash laundry

Examples

The laundrywoman delivered freshly pressed sheets to each guest room.
She worked as a laundrywoman in the big house for over twenty years.
Every week, the laundrywoman picked up the family's dirty clothes for washing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: washerwoman - woman employed to wash clothes for others

Etymology

From 'laundry' (from Old French 'lavanderie', place to wash) + 'woman'.
