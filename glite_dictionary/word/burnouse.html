burnouse

1. NOUN

/ bɜrˈnuːs / (AmE), / bɜːˈnuːs / (BrE)
a long, hooded woolen cloak, usually woven in one piece, traditionally worn by Arabs and Moors
a traditional long hooded cloak

Examples

He wore a traditional burnouse to protect himself from the sun and sand.
The market was full of merchants draped in flowing burnouses.
She admired the intricate embroidery on the old burnouse displayed in the museum.

Etymology

From French 'burnous', from Arabic 'burnus'.
