bloke

1. NOUN

/ bloʊk / (AmE), / bləʊk / (BrE)
a man; a fellow, especially in informal British English.
informal term for a man (British English)
informal british

Examples

He's a really friendly bloke—everyone gets along with him.
The bloke fixing the car is my neighbor.
You wouldn't believe what that bloke did at the party last night.

Synset Examples

that chap is your host
there's a fellow at the door
he's a likable cuss
he's a good bloke

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chap - an informal term for a man or boy (mainly British)
  • Synonym: guy - an informal term for a man
  • Synonym: fellow - a man or boy, especially used informally

Etymology

Origin unknown; early 19th century, possibly from Romani *loke* (man) or Hindi *lōg* (people).
