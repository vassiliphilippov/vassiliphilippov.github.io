divergent

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈvɝːdʒənt / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːdʒənt / (BrE)
Tending to move or develop in different directions from a common point; differing or deviating.
tending to move apart or differ

Examples

The two scientists held divergent views on the origin of the species.
After the fork in the road, their paths became increasingly divergent.
Cultural traditions can become divergent even within neighboring communities over time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convergent - tending to come together from different directions
  • Synonym: different - not the same as another

Etymology

From Latin "divergentem", present participle of "divergere" (to go in different directions).

2. ADJ

/ daɪˈvɝːdʒənt / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːdʒənt / (BrE)
Tending to be different or develop in different directions from a standard or from each other.
tending to differ from a standard or each other

Examples

Researchers proposed two divergent theories to explain the data.
Her approach was refreshingly divergent from traditional methods.
Despite divergent views, the team worked well together.

Synset Examples

a divergent opinion

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convergent - tending to come together at a point or become more similar
  • Synonym: different - not the same as another or each other
  • Antonym: similar - resembling without being identical

Etymology

From Latin 'divergent-', present participle of 'divergere' (to go in different directions).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02074066-s
Synset POS: s
