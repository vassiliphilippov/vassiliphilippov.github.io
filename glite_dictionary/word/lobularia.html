Lobularia

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈbjuləriə / (AmE), / lɒˈbjuːləriə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Brassicaceae, known for sweet-scented, small, white or purple flowers, commonly called sweet alyssum.
a plant genus, sweet alyssum

Examples

Lobularia is often planted in garden borders due to its fragrant white flowers.
Because Lobularia tolerates drought well, it’s a popular choice for rock gardens.
Gardeners frequently use Lobularia as ground cover in flower beds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet alyssum - a common ornamental plant with small fragrant flowers, Lobularia maritima

Etymology

From Latin 'lobulus' (small pod), referring to the shape of the fruit.

2. NOUN

/ loʊˌbjuːˈlɛriə / (AmE), / ləʊˌbjuːˈlɑːriə / (BrE)
a small genus of flowering plants in the family Brassicaceae, known for species such as sweet alyssum, with small, fragrant flowers.
genus of flowering plants (sweet alyssum)

Examples

Lobularia is often grown in gardens for its sweetly scented white flowers.
One of the most popular members of the Lobularia genus is sweet alyssum.
The botanist specialized in Mediterranean species, particularly those in the genus Lobularia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alyssum - a related genus of small, flowering herbaceous plants

Etymology

From Latin 'lobulus', diminutive of 'lobus' (lobe), referring to the shape of the fruit.
