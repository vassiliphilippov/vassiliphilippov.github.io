Gershwin

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡɜrʃwɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡɜːʃwɪn / (BrE)
(1898–1937) United States composer known for incorporating jazz into classical music and composing scores for musical comedies.
U.S. composer, blended jazz and classical
american

Examples

George Gershwin is celebrated for works like "Rhapsody in Blue" and "Porgy and Bess."
Many musicians credit Gershwin as a pioneer of blending jazz with classical traditions.
The orchestra performed a tribute to Gershwin on the anniversary of his birth.
