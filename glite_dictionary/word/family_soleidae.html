family Soleidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈsəʊliˌaɪdi / (AmE), / ˈsəʊlɪɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of flatfishes, commonly known as soles, found in marine and freshwater environments
family of flatfishes (soles)

Examples

The family Soleidae includes several commercially valuable species of soles.
Researchers discovered a new species within the family Soleidae in the coastal waters.
Members of the family Soleidae are characterized by their flat bodies and habit of lying on one side.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sole - a type of flatfish in the family Soleidae

Etymology

From modern Latin Soleidae, based on Latin 'solea' (a kind of flatfish).
