Volvariella bombycina

1. NOUN

/ voʊlˌvɛəriˈɛlə bɒmˈbɪsɪnə / (AmE), / vɒlvəˌriːɛlə bɒmˈbɪsɪnə / (BrE)
A species of edible mushroom with a dry, yellowish to white, fibrous cap.
a species of edible mushroom

Examples

The distinctive cap of Volvariella bombycina makes it relatively easy to identify in the wild.
She photographed a cluster of Volvariella bombycina growing on a decaying oak log.
Mycologists often regard Volvariella bombycina as an important indicator of healthy woodland ecosystems.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Volvariella' (genus name, from 'volva', meaning 'sheath') and 'bombycina' (silky, from 'bombyx', silk).
