biprism

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪˌprɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈbaɪˌprɪzəm / (BrE)
An optical device, typically a glass prism with two angles, used to split a beam of light in order to produce interference fringes.
optical device producing interference fringes

Examples

A Wollaston biprism can be used to demonstrate the phenomenon of light interference in the laboratory.
The experiment required precise alignment of the biprism to obtain clear fringes on the screen.
By adjusting the position of the biprism, students could observe changes in the interference pattern.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prism - a transparent optical element with flat, polished surfaces that refract light

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) and Greek 'prisma' (something sawed or a prism).
