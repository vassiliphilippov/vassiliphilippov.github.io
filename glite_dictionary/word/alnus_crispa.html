Alnus crispa

1. NOUN

/ ˈælnəs ˈkrɪspə / (AmE), / ˈal.nəs ˈkrɪs.pə / (BrE)
A North American shrub with light green leaves and winged nuts, commonly called green alder.
North American green alder shrub

Examples

The Alnus crispa is often found along riverbanks in northern forests.
Botanists studied the unique winged nuts produced by Alnus crispa.
Planting Alnus crispa can help restore areas affected by erosion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alder - A type of tree or shrub in the birch family

Etymology

From Latin: 'alnus' meaning alder, and 'crispa' meaning curled or wavy, referring to leaf shape.
