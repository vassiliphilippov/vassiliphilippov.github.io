Muntingia

1. NOUN

/ mʌnˈtɪndʒiə / (AmE), / mʌnˈtɪndʒɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American trees in the family Muntingiaceae, known especially for the Jamaican cherry (Muntingia calabura).
genus of tropical trees, Jamaican cherry

Examples

The Muntingia tree bears small, sweet fruit enjoyed by children in many tropical countries.
Botanists classify the Jamaican cherry under the genus Muntingia.
Urban parks in Southeast Asia often feature Muntingia for its rapid growth and ornamental value.

Etymology

Named after Abraham Munting, Dutch botanist (1626–1683).

2. NOUN

/ mʌnˈtɪndʒiə / (AmE), / mʌnˈtɪndʒiə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Muntingiaceae, containing the single species Muntingia calabura, commonly known as the Jamaican cherry.
genus of flowering plants (Jamaican cherry)

Examples

The Muntingia tree is cherished for its sweet, edible berries in tropical regions.
Scientists recently studied the medicinal properties of Muntingia leaves.
Muntingia is sometimes called the Jamaican cherry due to its tasty red fruit.

Etymology

Named after Abraham Munting, Dutch botanist (1626–1683).
