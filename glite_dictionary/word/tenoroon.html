tenoroon

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛnəˈruːn / (AmE), / ˌtɛnəˈruːn / (BrE)
A type of bassoon with a higher pitch, sounding a fifth above the standard bassoon.
a bassoon pitched a fifth higher than standard

Examples

The student musician chose to play the tenoroon in the orchestra.
Her skill on the tenoroon impressed both the conductor and her fellow players.
Unlike the standard bassoon, the tenoroon produces a brighter, higher sound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bassoon - a large woodwind instrument with a double reed

Etymology

From 'tenor' and 'bassoon', by analogy with 'bassoon'.
