serology

1. NOUN

/ səˈrɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / sɪəˈrɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of medical science concerned with the study and analysis of blood serums, especially in relation to disease diagnosis
study of blood serums in medicine
medical

Examples

Advances in serology have greatly improved the diagnosis of infectious diseases.
He decided to specialize in serology during his medical training.
The laboratory conducted serology tests to detect antibodies in the blood sample.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immunology - the branch of medicine and biology concerned with immunity
  • Degree Higher: hematology - the study of blood and blood-forming organs

Etymology

From serum + -logy, after French sérologie.
