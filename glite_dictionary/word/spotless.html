spotless

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɑt.ləs / (AmE), / ˈspɒt.ləs / (BrE)
extremely clean; without any stains, marks, or dirt.
completely clean; without any marks

Examples

The kitchen was spotless after hours of cleaning.
She always kept her uniform spotless for inspections.
His reputation for keeping a spotless office impressed his colleagues.

Synset Examples

the apartment was immaculate
in her immaculate white uniform
a spick-and-span kitchen
their spic red-visored caps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immaculate - perfectly clean, free from dirt or stain
  • Synonym: pristine - in its original, perfectly clean state
  • Antonym: dirty - covered or marked with dirt; not clean
  • Antonym: filthy - disgustingly dirty

Etymology

From 'spot' (mark or stain) + '-less' (without).
