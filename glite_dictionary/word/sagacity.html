sagacity

1. NOUN

/ səˈɡæsəti / (AmE), / səˈɡæsəti / (BrE)
the quality of having or showing keen judgment, discernment, and wisdom
keen judgment; wisdom

Examples

Her sagacity in handling negotiations earned her respect from all parties involved.
The teacher was admired for his sagacity and ability to guide students through difficult decisions.
They relied on the sagacity of their elders when faced with complex problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wisdom - the ability to use knowledge and experience to make good decisions
  • Antonym: foolishness - lack of good sense or judgment

Etymology

From Latin 'sagacitas' (acuteness of perception), from 'sagax' (wise).

2. NOUN

/ səˈɡæsəti / (AmE), / səˈɡæsɪti / (BrE)
the quality of having keen judgment and the ability to understand and make wise decisions
keen judgment and wisdom

Examples

Her sagacity in business negotiations earned her widespread respect in the industry.
The elderly judge was known for his sagacity and fairness in every case.
Through years of experience, he developed a level of sagacity that guided all his decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wisdom - the ability to use knowledge and experience to make good decisions
  • Antonym: foolishness - lack of good sense or judgment

Etymology

From Latin 'sagacitas' (shrewdness), from 'sagax' (wise).
