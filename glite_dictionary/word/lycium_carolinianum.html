Lycium carolinianum

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈsiːəm ˌkɛroʊˌlɪniˈænəm / (AmE), / lɪˈsɪəm ˌkærəˌlɪniˈænəm / (BrE)
A spiny evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States, with spreading branches, blue or mauve flowers, and red berries.
spiny shrub with blue flowers and red berries

Examples

The Lycium carolinianum is often found in coastal areas of the southeastern United States.
Birds are attracted to the bright red berries produced by Lycium carolinianum.
Gardeners appreciate Lycium carolinianum for its ornamental flowers and hardy nature.

Etymology

From the genus Lycium, of Greek origin, and the species name 'carolinianum' referring to the Carolinas in the United States.
