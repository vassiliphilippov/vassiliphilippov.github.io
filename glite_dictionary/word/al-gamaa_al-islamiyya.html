al-Gama'a al-Islamiyya

1. PROPN

/ æl ɡæˈmɑ əl ɪs.læˈmi.jə / (AmE), / æl ˌɡæˈmɑː əl ˌɪs.læˈmiː.jə / (BrE)
a militant Islamist organization that emerged in Egypt in the 1970s, known for its fundamentalist ideology and involvement in terrorist activities
Egyptian Islamist militant group

Examples

The Egyptian government banned al-Gama'a al-Islamiyya after several deadly attacks in the 1990s.
Members of al-Gama'a al-Islamiyya were involved in the assassination of President Anwar Sadat.
Reports suggest that al-Gama'a al-Islamiyya has maintained a presence in Egyptian prisons.

Synset Examples

al-Gama'a al-Islamiyya uses force to push Egyptian society toward Islamic rule

Etymology

From Arabic الجماعة الإسلامية (al-jamāʿa al-islāmiyya), meaning 'The Islamic Group'.
