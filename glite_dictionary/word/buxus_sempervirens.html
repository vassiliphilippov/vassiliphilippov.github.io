Buxus sempervirens

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌk.səs ˌsɛm.pərˈvaɪ.rənz / (AmE), / ˈbʌk.səs ˌsɛmp.əˈvaɪ.rənz / (BrE)
A species of evergreen shrub or small tree, commonly known as common box, frequently used in hedges, borders, and topiary.
evergreen shrub, common box

Examples

The formal garden was edged with Buxus sempervirens trimmed into neat shapes.
Gardeners often choose Buxus sempervirens for creating dense hedges and artistic topiary.
The slow-growing nature of Buxus sempervirens makes it ideal for long-lasting landscape designs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boxwood - the common name for Buxus sempervirens, an evergreen shrub

Etymology

From Latin: 'buxus' meaning box tree, and 'sempervirens' meaning ever-living or evergreen.
