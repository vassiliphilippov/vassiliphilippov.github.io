stochastic

1. ADJ

/ stoʊˈkæstɪk / (AmE), / stəˈkastɪk / (BrE)
involving or characterized by a random variable or process; having a random probability distribution or pattern
random or probabilistic (math, statistics)

Examples

The scientist used a stochastic model to predict population growth under uncertain conditions.
Due to stochastic fluctuations in the data, the results must be interpreted carefully.
Machine learning algorithms often incorporate stochastic elements to improve optimization.

Synset Examples

a stochastic variable
stochastic processes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: random - having no definite plan, purpose, or pattern

Etymology

From Greek stokhastikos (able to guess), from stokhazesthai (to aim at, guess).
