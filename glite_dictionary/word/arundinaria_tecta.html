Arundinaria tecta

1. NOUN

/ əˌrʌndɪˈnɛəriə ˈtɛktə / (AmE), / əˌrʌndɪˈnɛːrɪə ˈtɛktə / (BrE)
A species of small cane grass found in watery or moist areas of the southern United States.
species of cane grass in southern US

Examples

Arundinaria tecta is often found growing in dense thickets along riverbanks in the southeastern United States.
Wildlife use Arundinaria tecta as shelter and food during the wet season.
Researchers visited several wetlands to study the ecological role of Arundinaria tecta.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Arundinaria' from 'arundo' (reed), species epithet 'tecta' meaning 'covered'.
