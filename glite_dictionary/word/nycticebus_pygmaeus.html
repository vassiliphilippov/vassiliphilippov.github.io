Nycticebus pygmaeus

1. NOUN

/ nɪkˈtɪsəbəs paɪˈdʒæmiəs / (AmE), / nɪkˈtɪsəbəs paɪˈdʒæmiəs / (BrE)
A small, stocky primate native to southeastern Asia, also known as the pygmy slow loris.
small primate; pygmy slow loris

Examples

The Nycticebus pygmaeus is threatened by habitat loss and illegal pet trade.
Researchers observed the nocturnal behavior of Nycticebus pygmaeus in the wild.
Nycticebus pygmaeus is distinguished by its large eyes and slow movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pygmy slow loris - common name for Nycticebus pygmaeus, a small nocturnal primate

Etymology

From Greek nykt- (night) + kêbos (monkey) and Greek pygmaios (dwarf), referring to its nocturnal habits and small size.
