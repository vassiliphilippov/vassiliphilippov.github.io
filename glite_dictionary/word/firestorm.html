firestorm

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪərˌstɔrm / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə.stɔːm / (BrE)
A large and intense fire in which strong currents of air are drawn in by the rising column of hot air, often occurring over a severely bombed or burned area.
intense, wind-driven fire

Examples

The bombing created a firestorm that devastated the entire city.
Firefighters struggled to contain the firestorm as it swept through the forest.
The firestorm generated its own wind, making the blaze much harder to control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inferno - a large and dangerous fire
  • Synonym: conflagration - a large, destructive fire

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪərˌstɔrm / (AmE), / ˈfʌɪəstɔːm / (BrE)
A sudden and intense outburst of controversy or strong public reaction.
intense outburst of controversy

Examples

The politician's remarks caused a firestorm in the media.
The company's decision created a firestorm of criticism among its customers.
Social media can amplify a small mistake into a nationwide firestorm.

Synset Examples

the incident triggered a political firestorm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uproar - a loud or impassioned noise or disturbance, often of public anger or dissent
  • Synonym: furor - an outbreak of public anger or excitement
  • Synonym: backlash - a strong adverse reaction by a large number of people

3. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪrˌstɔrm / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəstɔːm / (BrE)
A situation in which there is a sudden and intense outburst of public anger or controversy.
intense public outcry or controversy

Examples

The new policy quickly triggered a firestorm among local residents.
After the announcement, a firestorm erupted on social media.

Synset Examples

A firestorm of criticism followed the controversial decision.

Related Terms
