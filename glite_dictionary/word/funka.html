Funka

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌŋ.kə / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋ.kə / (BrE)
A group of robust, clump-forming perennial herbs native to East Asia, known for their racemose flowers; commonly referred to as plantain lilies and sometimes classified in the family Hostaceae.
group of East Asian perennial herbs; plantain lilies

Examples

The gardener planted several Funka along the shaded path to create a lush border.
Funka are prized for their attractive foliage and fragrant flowers in many gardens.
Botanists sometimes classify Funka in the family Hostaceae rather than Liliaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hosta - another name for the plant known as Funka; a genus of plantain lilies

Etymology

From Modern Latin, after Japanese botanist Y. Funakoshi.
