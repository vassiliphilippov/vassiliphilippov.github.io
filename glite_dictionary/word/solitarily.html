solitarily

1. ADV

/ səˈlɪt̬.ɚ.li / (AmE), / səˈlɪt.ər.ɪ.li / (BrE)
in a way that is done alone or without other people; in solitude.
in solitude; alone

Examples

After her family left, she began to eat solitarily at the kitchen table.
The author walked solitarily along the deserted shoreline, deep in thought.
He spent hours solitarily practicing the piano, determined to improve.

Synset Examples

a hermit chooses to live solitarily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alone - without other people; on one's own
  • Antonym: together - with other people; in company

Etymology

Formed from 'solitary' (from Latin 'solitarius', from 'solus' meaning 'alone') + the adverbial suffix '-ly'.
