baptistery

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæp.tɪˌstɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈbæp.tɪ.stər.i / (BrE)
a basin or bowl used to hold water for baptisms in a church
basin for baptismal water

Examples

During the ceremony, the priest poured holy water from the baptistery onto the baby's head.
The ancient marble baptistery was beautifully carved and used for baptisms.
Some churches display the baptistery near the entrance to symbolize welcoming new members.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: font - a receptacle in a church for baptismal water

Etymology

From Greek baptistērion (baptismal font), from baptizein (to baptize).
