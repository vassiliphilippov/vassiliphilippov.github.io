Taiwan

1. PROPN

/ taɪˈwɑn / (AmE), / taɪˈwɑːn / (BrE)
An island in East Asia located 100 miles off the southeast coast of mainland China, in the South China Sea; officially known as the Republic of China (ROC).
island in East Asia, off China's coast

Examples

My friend recently traveled to Taiwan to explore its vibrant cities and beautiful landscapes.
The technology industry is a vital part of Taiwan's economy.
Many people visit Taiwan for its delicious street food and night markets.

Etymology

From Chinese 台灣 (Táiwān), originally meaning 'terraced bay'.

2. PROPN

/ taɪˈwɑn / (AmE), / taɪˈwæn / (BrE)
A state and self-governing island in East Asia, officially the Republic of China, established in 1949 after the government retreated from mainland China.
island nation in East Asia; Republic of China

Examples

Many electronics companies have their headquarters in Taiwan.
After 1949, the Nationalist government settled in Taiwan.
Tourists are often impressed by the natural beauty of Taiwan.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: China - a country in East Asia; the People's Republic of China

Etymology

From the native Formosan word 'Tayowan' or 'Tawan', meaning 'terrace bay'.
