dream

1. NOUN

/ driːm / (AmE), / driːm / (BrE)
an imaginative thought, vision, or fantasy indulged in while awake, often disconnected from reality
imaginative thought or fantasy while awake

Examples

Lost in a dream, she barely noticed the passing cars.
His dream of becoming a famous artist kept him motivated.
Sometimes he escapes reality by living in a dream.

Synset Examples

he lives in a dream that has nothing to do with reality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daydream - a pleasant fantasy experienced while awake
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

From Old English drēam (joy, mirth, noisy merriment), later 'vision during sleep'; meaning broadened to include waking fantasies in Middle English.
