Brigid

1. PROPN

/ ˈbrɪdʒɪd / (AmE), / ˈbrɪdɪd / (BrE)
An Irish abbess and one of the patron saints of Ireland, traditionally considered to have lived from 453 to 523 AD.
patron saint of Ireland

Examples

Many Irish communities celebrate the feast day of Brigid on February 1st.
Brigid is honoured as a founding abbess and miracle worker in Irish tradition.
Several churches throughout Ireland are dedicated to Saint Brigid.

Etymology

From Old Irish Brigit, possibly connected to the Celtic goddess Brigid.

2. PROPN

/ ˈbrɪdʒɪd / (AmE), / ˈbrɪdɪd / (BrE)
In Celtic mythology, the goddess of fire, fertility, agriculture, household arts, and wisdom, later identified with Saint Bridget.
Celtic goddess of fire and wisdom

Examples

The ancient Celts prayed to Brigid for a good harvest and healthy livestock.
Many traditions during Imbolc are dedicated to Brigid and the coming of spring.
Brigid is often depicted holding a flame, symbolizing her association with fire and inspiration.

Etymology

From Old Irish 'Brigit', relating to the Proto-Celtic *Brigantī ('the exalted one').
