to doss

1. VERB

/ dɑs / (AmE), / dɒs / (BrE)
to sleep in a convenient place, especially temporarily or in conditions lacking comfort or proper facilities
sleep temporarily, often in discomfort
informal british

Examples

After missing the last train, we had to doss in the station overnight.
He sometimes dosses at his friend's flat when he's in town.
They were dossing in abandoned buildings before they found a shelter.

Synset Examples

You can crash here, though it's not very comfortable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crash - to sleep, especially in someone else's place or informally

Etymology

Probably from late 19th-century British slang, possibly from Spanish 'dorso' (back), from Latin 'dorsum'.
