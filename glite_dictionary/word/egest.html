to egest

1. VERB

/ ɪˈdʒɛst / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒɛst / (BrE)
(of a living organism) to expel waste matter from the body, especially from the digestive tract.
expel waste from the body
medical

Examples

After digestion, the body must egest the remaining waste material.
Many single-celled organisms egest undigested particles from their bodies.
The process by which animals egest solid waste is vital for their health.

Synset Examples

Pass a kidney stone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to excrete - to separate and eliminate waste materials from the body

Etymology

From Latin 'egestus', past participle of 'egere' (to carry out, discharge).
