stockwoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑkˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈstɒkˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A woman whose occupation is breeding or raising livestock, especially cattle or sheep.
female livestock farmer

Examples

The experienced stockwoman managed the ranch's cattle with remarkable skill.
As a stockwoman, she spends most of her days caring for sheep and horses.
The award for best stockwoman of the year went to Mary, who raised over 500 head of livestock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stockman - person who breeds or raises livestock (esp. male or generic)
  • Synonym: farmer - person who owns or manages a farm

Etymology

Formed from 'stockman' by replacing 'man' with 'woman'.
