salvager

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæl.vɪdʒ.ər / (AmE), / ˈsæl.vɪdʒ.ə / (BrE)
a person or company that salvages property, especially from a shipwreck or after an accident or disaster
person who salvages property

Examples

The salvager managed to recover valuable cargo from the sunken ship.
As a professional salvager, she inspects wrecks for reusable materials.
Local authorities hired a salvager to remove debris after the hurricane.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rescuer - a person who saves someone or something from a dangerous situation
  • Antonym: destroyer - a person or thing that destroys something

Etymology

Formed from 'salvage' (to save from loss or destruction) + '-er', agent suffix.
