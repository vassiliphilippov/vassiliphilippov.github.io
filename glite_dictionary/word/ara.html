Ara

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑːrə / (AmE), / ˈɑːrə / (BrE)
a southern constellation lying between the constellations Telescopium and Norma.
a southern constellation

Examples

The constellation Ara is visible from the Southern Hemisphere during the winter months.
Ancient astronomers named Ara because its pattern resembled an altar.
Ara borders several other constellations, including Telescopium and Norma.

Etymology

From Latin 'Ara', meaning 'altar', which is the Latin name for the constellation.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrə / (AmE), / ˈɑːrə / (BrE)
A genus of large, brightly colored macaws native to Central and South America.
a genus of large macaws

Examples

The Ara genus includes some of the world's most colorful parrots.
Researchers observed an Ara in the Amazon rainforest collecting nuts.
Several species within the Ara genus are threatened due to habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macaw - a large, long-tailed parrot native to Central and South America

Etymology

From New Latin Ara, the genus name assigned in taxonomy.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrə / (AmE), / ˈɑːrə / (BrE)
A genus of brightly colored macaws native to Central and South America, known for their long tails and strong curved beaks.
genus of brightly colored macaws

Examples

The Ara genus includes some of the largest and most recognizable macaws.
Several species in Ara are endangered due to habitat loss and illegal pet trade.
A blue-and-yellow macaw belongs to the Ara genus.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on a local Brazilian name for a macaw.
