amentia

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛnʃə / (AmE), / əˈmɛn.ti.ə / (BrE)
a condition characterized by extreme intellectual disability, now considered outdated in medical usage
extreme intellectual disability (archaic)
archaic medical

Examples

The term amentia is largely obsolete in modern psychiatric classification.
In early medical texts, amentia was used to describe cases of profound intellectual disability.
Some historians study how society's understanding of amentia has changed over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idiocy - very severe intellectual disability (archaic term)
  • Antonym: dementia - chronic or persistent disorder of mental processes marked by memory disorders, personality changes, and impaired reasoning

Etymology

From Latin 'amentia' (madness, folly), from 'amens' (out of one's mind).
