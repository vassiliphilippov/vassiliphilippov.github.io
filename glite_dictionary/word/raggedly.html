raggedly

1. ADV

/ ˈræɡɪdli / (AmE), / ˈræɡɪdli / (BrE)
In an irregular, uneven, or jagged manner.
in an irregular or uneven way

Examples

The abandoned fence ran raggedly along the edge of the property.
She spoke raggedly, her breath coming in uneven gasps after the long run.
The mountain path wound raggedly through the thick forest.

Synset Examples

a stone wall trails raggedly through the woods

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smoothly - in a smooth, even manner

Etymology

From 'ragged' + '-ly', with 'ragged' from Old Norse 'rǫggr' (shaggy) via Middle English.
