chromaticity

1. NOUN

/ kroʊˌmætɪˈsɪti / (AmE), / krəʊˌmatɪˈsɪti / (BrE)
the quality of a color as determined by its dominant wavelength, independent of its brightness
color quality based on dominant wavelength

Examples

The chromaticity of the light determined the overall ambiance of the room.
Scientists used a diagram to map the chromaticity of various color samples.
By adjusting the chromaticity, the display can reproduce a wider range of colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hue - the attribute of a color by which it is discernible as red, green, or yellow, etc.

Etymology

From Greek 'chrōmatikos' (relating to color) + '-ity'.
