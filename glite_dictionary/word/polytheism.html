polytheism

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑliˌθiɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪˌθiːɪzəm / (BrE)
The belief in or worship of more than one god.
belief in multiple gods

Examples

Ancient Greeks practiced polytheism, worshipping a pantheon of gods and goddesses.
The shift from polytheism to monotheism marked a major change in religious history.
Some cultures today still adhere to forms of polytheism in their spiritual traditions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monotheism - belief in the existence of only one god
  • Antonym: atheism - disbelief or lack of belief in the existence of gods
  • Synonym: polytheist - a person who believes in or worships more than one god

Etymology

From Greek 'polytheos' (of many gods), from 'poly-' (many) + 'theos' (god).
