Aneides lugubris

1. NOUN

/ əˈnaɪ.iːdiːz luːˈɡjuːbrɪs / (AmE), / əˈniː.iːdiːz luːˈɡjuːbrɪs / (BrE)
A yellow-spotted brown salamander native to the woodlands of California.
California yellow-spotted salamander

Examples

The Aneides lugubris is often found hiding under logs in California woodlands.
Scientists observed the Aneides lugubris during their field studies on amphibian diversity.
Unlike other salamanders, the Aneides lugubris climbs trees to hunt for insects.

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Aneides' as the genus name and 'lugubris' meaning 'mournful' or 'sorrowful'.
