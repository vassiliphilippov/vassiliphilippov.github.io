simplism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪm.plɪz(ə)m / (AmE), / ˈsɪm.plɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the practice of reducing complex issues, ideas, or explanations to overly simple forms, often leading to distortion or misrepresentation
oversimplification of complex issues

Examples

His argument was criticized for its simplism and lack of nuance.
The report falls into simplism by ignoring the complexity of the problem.
To suggest that poverty has a single cause is pure simplism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oversimplification - the act of simplifying something so much that a distorted impression is given

Etymology

From French 'simplisme', from 'simple'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪm.plɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪm.plɪ.zəm / (BrE)
The practice of making something seem simpler than it really is, often by ignoring important complexities.
excessive simplification

Examples

The article's simplism failed to address the real challenges of the issue.
Critics accused the report of simplism for its overly broad recommendations.
Political debates often suffer from simplism when complex topics are reduced to slogans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oversimplification - the process of simplifying something so much that a distorted impression is given

Etymology

From French 'simplisme', based on Latin 'simplex' (simple).
