Hymenaea courbaril

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌmiːniə kʊrˈbærɪl / (AmE), / hɪˌmiːniːə kɔːˈbærɪl / (BrE)
A tropical American tree, also known as the West Indian locust, with pinnate leaves and clusters of large white or purplish flowers, yielding very hard, tough wood.
tropical tree yielding tough wood

Examples

The durable wood of the Hymenaea courbaril is prized for making furniture and flooring.
In the rainforest, the Hymenaea courbaril stands out for its spreading canopy and fragrant flowers.
Carpenters in South America often choose Hymenaea courbaril due to its strength and resistance to decay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: West Indian locust - common name for Hymenaea courbaril

Etymology

From Latinized form of the genus Hymenaea and the species epithet courbaril, derived from Carib or French Guiana languages.
