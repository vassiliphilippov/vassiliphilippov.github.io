beastliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːstlinəs / (AmE), / ˈbiːstlɪnɪs / (BrE)
deliberate cruelty or meanness; the quality of being unpleasant or nasty
deliberate cruelty or meanness

Examples

His beastliness towards his classmates made him disliked by everyone in the class.
She was shocked by the beastliness of his comments during the meeting.
Sometimes children can show surprising beastliness to each other.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruelty - deliberate causing of pain or suffering
  • Antonym: kindness - the quality of being friendly, generous, and considerate

Etymology

From 'beastly' (cruel, unpleasant) + -ness.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbiːstli nɪs / (AmE), / ˈbiːstlɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely unpleasant, nasty, or disagreeable, especially with reference to weather or behavior
extreme unpleasantness or nastiness

Examples

The beastliness of the winter storm kept everyone indoors for days.
He complained constantly about the beastliness of the food at the old camp.
Despite the beastliness of the rain, we made it to the top of the hill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nastiness - the quality of being unpleasant or disgusting
  • Antonym: pleasantness - the quality of being pleasing or agreeable

Etymology

Formed from 'beastly' (from Middle English *bestly*, from Old French *beste*, from Latin *bestia*) + '-ness'.
