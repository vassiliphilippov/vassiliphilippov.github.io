multiply

1. VERB

/ ˈmʌltəˌplaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmʌltɪplaɪ / (BrE)
to combine numbers or quantities by performing the operation of multiplication
perform multiplication

Examples

When you multiply 6 by 3, the result is 18.
Can you multiply these two fractions for me?
The teacher showed the class how to multiply large numbers quickly.

Synset Examples

multiply 10 by 15

Related Terms

  • Antonym: divide - to separate into equal parts; the arithmetic inverse of multiply
  • Degree Lower: to add - to join numbers to find their total
  • Degree Higher: exponentiate - to raise to a power; a higher degree of multiplication

Etymology

From Latin 'multiplicare', meaning to increase, from 'multus' (much, many) and 'plicare' (to fold).
