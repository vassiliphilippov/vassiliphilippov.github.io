vantage

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæn.tɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvɑːn.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
A place or situation that gives a person a good view or advantage, especially a commanding perspective.
position giving a good view or advantage

Examples

From the vantage of the hilltop, we could see the entire valley spread out before us.
She spoke from the vantage of experience, having worked there for many years.
The balcony provided a perfect vantage to watch the parade go by.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advantage - a condition or circumstance that puts one in a favorable or superior position

Etymology

From Old French 'avantage' (advantage), from 'avant' (before).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvæn.tɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvɑːn.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
a position giving someone a superior or advantageous perspective or opportunity
a position of advantage

Examples

The castle was built on a hill, providing a strategic vantage over the surrounding land.
From her vantage atop the observation deck, she could see the entire city unfold below.
They negotiated from a position of vantage, knowing their resources were superior.

Synset Examples

the experience gave him the advantage over me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advantage - a condition or circumstance that puts one in a favorable position
  • Antonym: disadvantage - an unfavorable circumstance or condition

Etymology

From Old French 'avant' (before), via Middle English 'vantage', influenced by 'advantage'.
