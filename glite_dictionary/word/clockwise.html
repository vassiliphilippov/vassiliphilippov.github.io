clockwise

1. ADV

/ ˈklɑːkˌwaɪz / (AmE), / ˈklɒkˌwaɪz / (BrE)
in the direction in which the hands of a clock move
in the direction of a clock's hands

Examples

The children ran clockwise around the playground during the game.
Turn the screw clockwise to tighten it.
The dancers moved clockwise in a large circle.

Synset Examples

please move clockwise in a circle

Related Terms

  • Antonym: counterclockwise - in the opposite direction to the way the hands of a clock move

Etymology

From 'clock' + '-wise' (in the manner of), meaning in the manner of a clock's movement.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00255849-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADJ

/ ˈklɑkˌwaɪz / (AmE), / ˈklɒkˌwaɪz / (BrE)
moving or turning in the same direction as the hands of a clock
moving in direction of a clock's hands

Examples

Turn the handle clockwise to tighten the screw.
The dancers moved clockwise around the central fire.
Water swirled clockwise down the drain in the sink.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anticlockwise - moving in the opposite direction to the hands of a clock
  • Antonym: counterclockwise - moving in the opposite direction to the hands of a clock (American equivalent of 'anticlockwise')

Etymology

Formed from 'clock' + 'wise' (direction, manner).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00443798-a
Synset POS: a
