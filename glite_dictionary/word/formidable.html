formidable

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrmɪdəbəl, fərˈmɪdəbəl / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmɪdəbl, fəˈmɪdəbl / (BrE)
inspiring fear, dread, or respect through being impressively large, powerful, intense, or capable
inspiring fear or respect

Examples

The champion boxer was a formidable opponent in the ring, known for his strength and speed.
Climbing the formidable mountain left us in awe of nature’s power.
She faced a formidable task at work, but she tackled it with determination.

Synset Examples

the formidable prospect of major surgery
a tougher and more redoubtable adversary than the heel-clicking, jackbooted fanatic
something unnerving and prisonlike about high grey wall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intimidating - making someone feel afraid or nervous
  • Synonym: daunting - seeming difficult to deal with; intimidating
  • Antonym: reassuring - making someone feel less worried; calming

Etymology

From Latin formidabilis, from formidare (to fear).
