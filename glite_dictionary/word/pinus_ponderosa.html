Pinus ponderosa

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪnəs pɒn.dəˈroʊ.sə / (AmE), / ˌpaɪ.nəs pɒn.dəˈrəʊ.sə / (BrE)
a tall and widely distributed species of pine native to western North America, recognized for its dark green needles in clusters of 2 to 5 and thick, dark brown bark
a large pine tree native to western North America

Examples

The Pinus ponderosa is one of the most important timber species in the western United States.
We spent our vacation hiking through a forest dominated by Pinus ponderosa.
Researchers are studying how Pinus ponderosa adapts to changes in climate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ponderosa pine - another common name for Pinus ponderosa

Etymology

From Latin: Pinus (pine) + ponderosa (heavy), referring to the heavy wood.
