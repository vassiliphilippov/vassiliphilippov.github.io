fortuitous

1. ADJ

/ fɔrˈtuː.ə.təs / (AmE), / fɔːˈtjuː.ɪ.təs / (BrE)
happening by chance, especially in a way that is lucky or beneficial.
happening by lucky chance

Examples

It was a fortuitous coincidence that they met at the train station that day.
Her discovery of the old letter was entirely fortuitous.
Thanks to a fortuitous series of events, the company avoided disaster.

Synset Examples

profits were enhanced by a fortuitous drop in the cost of raw materials

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accidental - happening by chance, not planned
  • Antonym: unlucky - having or resulting from bad luck

Etymology

From Latin 'fortuitus' (accidental, by chance), from 'fors' (chance).

2. ADJ

/ fɔrˈtuɪtəs / (AmE), / fɔːˈtjuːɪtəs / (BrE)
happening by chance or accident, especially with no apparent cause; unplanned or unintended.
happening by chance; accidental

Examples

Our meeting at the airport was completely fortuitous, as neither of us planned to travel that day.
A fortuitous set of circumstances led to the discovery of the new painting.
The collapse of the bridge was not fortuitous but the result of years of neglect.

Synset Examples

a causeless miracle
fortuitous encounters — strange accidents of fortune
we cannot regard artistic invention as … uncaused and unrelated to the times

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accidental - happening by chance rather than intention
  • Antonym: intentional - done on purpose; deliberate

Etymology

From Latin 'fortuitus' (by chance), from 'fors' (chance, luck).
