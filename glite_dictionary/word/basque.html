Basque

1. NOUN

/ bæsk / (AmE), / bask / (BrE)
the unique language spoken by the Basque people, unrelated to any other known language family
a unique language of the Basque people

Examples

Many linguists are fascinated by the Basque language and its mysterious origins.
She decided to learn Basque before traveling to northern Spain.
There are several dialects of Basque spoken in different regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Basque - a member of the Basque people

Etymology

From French 'basque', from Latin 'Vascones', the ancient people of the Basque country.

2. NOUN

/ bæsk / (AmE), / bask / (BrE)
a member of an ethnic group of unknown origin, traditionally living in the western Pyrenees region of France and Spain
a member of an ethnic group in the western Pyrenees

Examples

Many Basques still speak the ancient Basque language, known as Euskara.
The Basque have a unique culture that sets them apart from their neighbors.
He is proud to identify as a Basque, with deep roots in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Basque - the language spoken by the Basque people

Etymology

From French Basque, from Gascon Basco, from Latin Vasco (name of the people).
