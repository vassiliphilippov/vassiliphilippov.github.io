grill

1. NOUN

/ ɡrɪl / (AmE), / ɡrɪl / (BrE)
A framework of metal bars used as a partition, grate, or for cooking food over a fire.
metal bars used as a grate or partition

Examples

The prisoners could only see each other through the metal grill between their cells.
He replaced the old fireplace grill with a sturdier one.
She liked the decorative iron grill on the front door.

Synset Examples

he cooked hamburgers on the grill

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grate - a framework of iron bars used to hold fuel in a fireplace

Etymology

From Old French *greille*, from Latin *craticula* (small grating).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03464523-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ɡrɪl / (AmE), / ɡrɪl / (BrE)
a restaurant where food, especially meat or fish, is cooked on a grill and served to customers
restaurant serving grilled food

Examples

We decided to try the new grill downtown for dinner last night.
The grill is famous for its sizzling steaks and vibrant atmosphere.
She works as a chef at a busy grill near the waterfront.

Etymology

From Old French *gril*, from Latin *craticula* (small grating), originally referring to the utensil.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03464846-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ɡrɪl / (AmE), / ɡrɪl / (BrE)
to question someone thoroughly, especially in an aggressive or persistent way, to find out what they know
question thoroughly or intensely

Examples

The police grilled the suspect for hours, trying to get a confession.
During the interview, she was grilled about her previous job experience.
Reporters grilled the politician on his plans for economic reform.

Synset Examples

the student was grilled for two hours on the subject of phonology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to interrogate - to ask someone a lot of questions, often in an official context
  • Synonym: quiz - to question someone, typically as a test of knowledge

Etymology

Figurative use from the sense of cooking with intense heat, likening questioning to subjecting someone to a grill.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00790483-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ɡrɪl / (AmE), / ɡrɪl / (BrE)
To cook (food) over direct heat or under a grill.
cook with direct heat

Examples

We like to grill vegetables in the summer for a smoky flavor.
She grilled the chicken until it was perfectly charred on the outside.
They were grilling burgers in the backyard when the rain started.

Synset Examples

grill the sausages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broil - to cook by direct exposure to radiant heat (mainly American English)

Etymology

From Old French *graille* (grill), from Latin *craticula* (gridiron).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00327574-v
Synset POS: v
