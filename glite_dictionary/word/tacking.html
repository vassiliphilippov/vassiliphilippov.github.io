tacking

1. NOUN

/ ˈtækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtakɪŋ / (BrE)
a loose, temporary sewing stitch used to hold layers of fabric together before final stitching
loose temporary sewing stitch

Examples

She used tacking to keep the fabric in place before sewing the final seam.
Remove the tacking stitches after your dress is properly hemmed.
Good tacking ensures that the layers won't shift while being sewn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basting - temporary stitching to hold fabric before permanent sewing

Etymology

From the verb 'tack', meaning to fasten or attach, from Middle French 'taquer'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtækɪŋ / (BrE)
(nautical) The action of changing a ship's course by turning its bow through the wind so that the wind changes from one side to the other.
nautical: changing direction by turning through the wind

Examples

Sailing into the wind required frequent tacking to maintain our course.
The crew prepared for tacking as the wind shifted suddenly.
Proper tacking is essential for competitive sailboat racing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tack - the action of changing direction in sailing by turning the bow through the wind

Etymology

From the verb 'tack' (to change a sailing ship’s direction); ‘tack’ from Middle Low German 'tak', meaning a branch or a direction.
