frijolito

1. NOUN

/ ˌfriːhoʊˈliːtoʊ / (AmE), / ˌfriːhəˈliːtəʊ / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree with pinnate leaves, highly fragrant blue flowers, and red beans; poisonous to livestock.
shrub with fragrant blue flowers and red beans

Examples

The frijolito is known for its strikingly beautiful blue flowers that fill the air with fragrance.
Ranchers avoid planting frijolito near grazing areas because it is poisonous to livestock.
Botanists study the frijolito for its unique combination of ornamental and toxic properties.

Etymology

From Spanish 'frijolito', diminutive of 'frijol' (bean).
