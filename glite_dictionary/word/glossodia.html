Glossodia

1. PROPN

/ ɡlɑːˈsoʊdiə / (AmE), / ɡlɒˈsəʊdiə / (BrE)
A small genus of Australian orchids, most species of which have purple flowers.
genus of Australian orchids

Examples

Several species in the genus Glossodia are admired for their delicate purple blooms.
The Glossodia grows wild in the woodlands of southeastern Australia.
Researchers discovered a new population of Glossodia orchids near the coastal region.

Etymology

From Greek 'glossa' (tongue) and 'oeidēs' (like), referring to the tongue-shaped labellum.

2. NOUN

/ ɡlɑːˈsoʊdiə / (AmE), / ɡlɒˈsəʊdiə / (BrE)
A small genus of Australian orchids, mostly with purple flowers.
genus of Australian orchids

Examples

The southern Australian forest is home to several species of Glossodia.
Botanists have recently discovered a new variety within the Glossodia genus.
The delicate purple blooms of Glossodia make it a favorite among native orchid enthusiasts.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Greek *glossa* (tongue), referring to the tongue-shaped labellum of the orchid.
