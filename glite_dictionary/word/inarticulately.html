inarticulately

1. ADV

/ ˌɪn.ɑːrˈtɪk.jə.lɪt.li / (AmE), / ˌɪn.ɑːˈtɪk.jʊ.lət.li / (BrE)
In a way that is not able to express ideas or feelings clearly or effectively.
in an unclear or unexpressive way

Examples

He spoke inarticulately during the meeting, struggling to convey his main points.
The exhausted actor mumbled inarticulately as he left the stage.
When nervous, she tends to answer questions inarticulately, searching for the right words.

Synset Examples

the freshman expresses his thoughts inarticulately

Related Terms

  • Antonym: articulately - in a clear and effective manner

Etymology

From 'inarticulate' + '-ly'; 'inarticulate' from Latin 'inarticulatus' (not distinctly uttered).

2. ADV

/ ɪnˌɑɹˈtɪkjələtli / (AmE), / ɪnˌɑːˈtɪkjʊlətli / (BrE)
in a manner that is not able to express oneself clearly or effectively; without clear or distinct speech or expression.
without clear expression

Examples

He explained his feelings inarticulately, struggling to find the right words.
She nodded inarticulately, unable to voice her concerns during the meeting.
After the shock, he spoke inarticulately, making it hard to understand what had happened.

Synset Examples

he talked inarticulately about the accident that had just taken his wife's life

Related Terms

  • Antonym: articulately - in a clear, fluent, and effective manner

Etymology

From 'inarticulate' (not able to speak distinctly) + '-ly', an adverbial suffix.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00269895-r
Synset POS: r
