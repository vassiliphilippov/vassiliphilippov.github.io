differentiation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪfəˌrɛnʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪfəˌrɛnʃɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Biology) The process by which cells or tissues develop specialized structures and functions.
biological specialization of cells or tissues
medical

Examples

During embryonic development, differentiation allows cells to form tissues with specialized functions.
Muscle cells result from the differentiation of unspecialized precursor cells.
Scientists study differentiation to understand how different cell types arise in an organism.

Synset Examples

cell differentiation in the developing embryo

Related Terms

  • Synonym: specialization - the process of becoming suited for a particular function

Etymology

From Latin *differentiatio*, from *differentiare* (to make different).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪfəˌrɛnʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪfəˌrɛnʃiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the mathematical process of finding the derivative of a function
math: process of finding a derivative

Examples

The concept of differentiation is fundamental in calculus and physics.
He mastered differentiation before moving on to more advanced topics in mathematics.
The teacher explained the rules of differentiation using several example functions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: derivative - function representing the rate of change; result of differentiation
  • Antonym: integration - the inverse mathematical operation of finding the area under a curve

Etymology

From differentiate + -ion; ultimately from Latin differentiatus, past participle of differre (to carry apart).

3. NOUN

/ ˌdɪfəˌrɛnʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪfəˌrɛnʃɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of recognizing or showing that two or more things are different and distinct from each other
the act of distinguishing between things

Examples

Proper differentiation between facts and opinions is essential in academic writing.
The differentiation of twins based on subtle physical traits can be challenging.
Modern brands use differentiation to distinguish their products from competitors in the market.

Synset Examples

it is necessary to make a distinction between love and infatuation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distinction - the difference between two similar things
  • Degree Lower: identification - the act of recognizing or establishing as being a particular thing
  • Synonym: discrimination - the ability to perceive fine differences
  • Antonym: equation - the act of treating two things as equal

Etymology

From Latin 'differentiatio', from 'differentiare' (to make different).
