Solea solea

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈliːə soʊˈliːə / (AmE), / səʊˈlɪə səʊˈlɪə / (BrE)
A species of flatfish, commonly known as the common sole, highly valued as a food fish in Europe.
species of edible flatfish

Examples

The chef prepared a delicate dish using fresh Solea solea caught off the coast of France.
Overfishing has led to a decline in wild Solea solea stocks across European waters.
Many seafood lovers consider Solea solea to be one of the finest fish available.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sole - an edible flatfish of the family Soleidae

Etymology

From scientific Latin, from Latin 'solea' (sandal, sole), referring to the fish's shape.
