epiglottis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈɡlɑtɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈɡlɒtɪs / (BrE)
a thin flap of cartilage at the base of the tongue, which closes over the windpipe during swallowing to prevent food or liquid from entering the airway
cartilage flap covering the windpipe during swallowing
medical

Examples

During the act of swallowing, the epiglottis closes tightly to protect the airway.
Inflammation of the epiglottis can cause serious breathing problems in children.
The doctor explained how the epiglottis helps keep food out of the lungs.

Etymology

From Greek 'epi' (upon) + 'glottis' (mouth of the windpipe).
