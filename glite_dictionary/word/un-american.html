un-American

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən / (BrE)
regarded as being contrary to the characteristics, values, or interests of the United States
contrary to American values or interests

Examples

Many people considered the proposal to be distinctly un-American.
He faced accusations of un-American activities during the hearing.
Some argue that restricting free speech is fundamentally un-American.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anti-American - opposed or hostile to the United States or its policies
  • Antonym: patriotic - having or expressing devotion to and support for one's country

Etymology

Formed with the prefix 'un-' meaning 'not' + 'American'.
