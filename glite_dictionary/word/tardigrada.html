Tardigrada

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɑrdɪˈɡreɪdə / (AmE), / ˌtɑːdɪˈɡreɪdə / (BrE)
A phylum of microscopic, water-dwelling invertebrates with four pairs of legs, known for their ability to survive extreme conditions.
phylum of microscopic aquatic invertebrates

Examples

Many scientists study Tardigrada because of their incredible resistance to extreme environmental conditions.
Tardigrada can be found in mosses, lichens, and even deep-sea environments.
The unique anatomy of Tardigrada allows them to survive without water for long periods.

Etymology

From Latin tardigradus (slow-stepping), referring to their slow movement.

2. NOUN

/ tɑːrˈdɪɡrədə / (AmE), / tɑːˈdɪɡrədə / (BrE)
A phylum of microscopic, water-dwelling invertebrates with four pairs of legs, needlelike mouthparts, and a tough cuticle, commonly known as water bears or moss piglets.
phylum of microscopic water-dwelling invertebrates

Examples

Tardigrada are renowned for their ability to survive extreme environmental conditions.
Scientists discovered members of the Tardigrada in the moss samples collected from the forest.
Because of their resilience, Tardigrada have been sent to space for survival experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water bear - common name for organisms of the phylum Tardigrada

Etymology

From Latin 'tardigradus', meaning 'slow-paced'.
