watchman

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑtʃ.mən / (AmE), / ˈwɒtʃ.mən / (BrE)
a man employed to guard or keep watch over a building, property, or area, especially at night
male guard or night watchman

Examples

The watchman made his rounds every hour to ensure the building was secure.
A watchman stood at the entrance, checking identification before letting anyone in.
After hearing a noise outside, the watchman investigated the grounds with his flashlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guard - a person who protects or watches over something
  • Synonym: sentinel - a person stationed to keep watch

Etymology

From Middle English 'waccheman', from 'watch' + 'man'.
