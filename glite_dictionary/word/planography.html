planography

1. NOUN

/ plænˈɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / pləˈnɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
a printing process in which the image areas are ink-receptive and lie on the same plane as the non-image areas, which repel ink
printing process with flat, ink-receptive surfaces

Examples

Lithography is a common form of planography used in art and commercial printing.
In planography, the printing and non-printing areas are on the same level surface.
The development of planography revolutionized the printing industry in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lithography - printing from a flat surface, often using stone or metal plates

Etymology

From Greek 'planos' (flat) + '-graphy' (writing).
