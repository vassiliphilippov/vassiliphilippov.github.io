Swazi

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɑzi / (AmE), / ˈswɑːzi / (BrE)
a member of a Bantu-speaking people living in Eswatini (formerly Swaziland) and adjacent regions of southeast Africa
member of a southeast African ethnic group

Examples

Many Swazi live in rural areas where traditional customs are still important.
The Swazi have a rich cultural heritage that includes music and dance.
A Swazi delegation visited South Africa for regional talks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Swaziland - a country in southeast Africa, now known as Eswatini

Etymology

From Swazi siSwati, the people’s own name for themselves.

2. NOUN

/ ˈswɑːzi / (AmE), / ˈswɑːzi / (BrE)
A Bantu language closely related to Zulu, spoken by about three million people in Swaziland and South Africa.
Bantu language spoken in Swaziland and South Africa

Examples

She is fluent in both English and Swazi.
The lesson was conducted entirely in Swazi.
Many radio stations in Swaziland broadcast news in Swazi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zulu - a Bantu language spoken mainly in South Africa

Etymology

From the name Swazi (the ethnic group), itself from Swazi (siSwati), the people and language.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07007268-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈswɑzi / (AmE), / ˈswɑːzi / (BrE)
of or relating to Eswatini (formerly Swaziland), its people, or their language.
relating to Eswatini or its people

Examples

The Swazi culture places great importance on family and tradition.
She studied Swazi history and learned about the nation's independence.
Many visitors enjoy the vibrant Swazi festivals held throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Swati - the Bantu language spoken in Eswatini
  • Synonym: Eswatini - the country formerly known as Swaziland

Etymology

From the name of the Swazi people, related to the kingdom of Eswatini.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
