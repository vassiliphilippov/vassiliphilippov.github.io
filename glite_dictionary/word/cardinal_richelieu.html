Cardinal Richelieu

1. PROPN

/ ˌkɑrdɪnəl rɪʃəˈlu / (AmE), / ˌkɑːdɪnəl ˈrɪʃəljuː / (BrE)
(1585–1642) French cardinal, statesman, and principal minister to King Louis XIII, known for strengthening the central power of the monarchy.
French cardinal and statesman (1585–1642)

Examples

Cardinal Richelieu played a pivotal role in consolidating royal power in 17th-century France.
Many consider Cardinal Richelieu the architect of modern French statecraft and diplomacy.
The influence of Cardinal Richelieu extended far beyond the church, affecting European politics for decades.
