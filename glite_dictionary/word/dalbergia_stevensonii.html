Dalbergia stevensonii

1. NOUN

/ dælˈbɜr.dʒi.ə stiːvənˈsoʊni.aɪ / (AmE), / dɑːlˈbɜː.dʒi.ə stiːvənˈsəʊni.aɪ / (BrE)
A species of tree native to Central America, known for producing a valuable, dark-streaked rosewood used in fine woodworking and musical instruments.
Central American rosewood tree species

Examples

The dense wood of Dalbergia stevensonii is highly prized by instrument makers.
Timber from Dalbergia stevensonii is often called Honduras rosewood in the trade.
Conservationists are concerned about over-harvesting of Dalbergia stevensonii in its native habitats.

Etymology

Genus name from Swedish botanist Nils Dalberg; species likely named after a person named Stevenson.
