lionfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪ.ən.fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ.ən.fɪʃ / (BrE)
A bright, venomous tropical fish of the Pacific and Indian Oceans, known for its long, spiny fins and bold striped pattern.
a brightly striped spiny tropical fish

Examples

The lionfish is easily recognized by its showy fins and striking stripes.
Divers are often warned to avoid touching a lionfish because of its venomous spines.
The rapid spread of lionfish in the Atlantic Ocean has raised concerns among marine biologists.
