imprisonment

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈprɪzənmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɪznmənt / (BrE)
the state of being confined in prison as a form of legal punishment
the state of being put in prison

Examples

He faced five years of imprisonment for his crimes.
After his imprisonment, he struggled to adjust to normal life.
The judge sentenced her to life imprisonment without parole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incarceration - the act of confining someone in prison
  • Antonym: release - the act of setting someone free from imprisonment

Etymology

From Old French 'emprisonement', based on 'emprisonner' (to imprison).

3. NOUN

/ ɪmˈprɪz.ən.mənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɪz.ən.mənt / (BrE)
the act of confining someone in a prison, especially as a punishment by law
the act of confining a person in prison

Examples

The unjust imprisonment of political opponents drew international criticism.
He faced a lengthy imprisonment for his role in the robbery.
After years of imprisonment, she was finally released on appeal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incarceration - the state of being confined in prison
  • Antonym: freedom - the state of being free rather than imprisoned

Etymology

From Old French emprisonnement, from emprisonner (to imprison) + -ment.
