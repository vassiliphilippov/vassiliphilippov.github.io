subjection

1. NOUN

/ səbˈdʒɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / səbˈdʒɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or condition of being under the control or authority of another, especially by force
forced control by others

Examples

The country remained in subjection for decades after the invasion.
He resisted any subjection to outside authority.
The people longed to be free from subjection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oppression - prolonged cruel or unjust treatment or control
  • Antonym: freedom - the state of being free rather than in confinement or under control

Etymology

From Latin subiectio(n-), from subicere 'bring under'.

2. NOUN

/ səbˈdʒɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / səbˈdʒɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of bringing a person, group, or country under control, especially by conquest or force
the act of bringing under control by force

Examples

The ancient city fell into subjection after months of siege.
Colonial powers imposed subjection on local populations through military force.
She refused to accept the subjection of her people to foreign rule.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: domination - the exercise of control or influence over someone or something
  • Antonym: liberation - the act of setting someone free from imprisonment or oppression

Etymology

From Latin subiectio (a bringing under), from subicere (to bring under).
