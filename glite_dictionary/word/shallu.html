shallu

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑːˈluː / (AmE), / ʃæˈluː / (BrE)
A variety of sorghum with slender dry stalks and small hard grains, originally introduced into the United States from India.
a type of sorghum from India

Examples

Farmers in Texas have started growing shallu for its drought resistance.
The fields were covered in tall shallu swaying gently in the wind.
Unlike other varieties, shallu has small, hard grains that are prized for animal feed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorghum - a genus of grass cultivated for its grain

Etymology

From Hindi शल्लू (śallū), denoting a type of sorghum.
