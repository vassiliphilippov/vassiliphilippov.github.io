rakehell

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪkˌhɛl / (AmE), / ˈreɪkhɛl / (BrE)
A man of immoral or dissolute habits, especially one who is recklessly debauched or promiscuous, often in fashionable society.
dissolute man, usually in society
archaic

Examples

The notorious rakehell squandered his fortune on gambling and lavish parties.
In eighteenth-century novels, a rakehell was often depicted as a cautionary figure.
He tried to reform his rakehell ways after falling in love with a virtuous woman.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rake - a dissolute or immoral person, especially a man

Etymology

From 'rake' (short for 'rakehell', from Middle English 'rakel' meaning 'rash') + 'hell'.
