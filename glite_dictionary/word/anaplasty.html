anaplasty

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.əˌplæsti / (AmE), / ˈanəplasti / (BrE)
a surgical procedure involving the reconstruction or restoration of tissue, often for therapeutic or cosmetic purposes
surgical tissue reconstruction
medical

Examples

The patient underwent anaplasty to repair facial injuries after the accident.
Recent advances in anaplasty have improved outcomes for burn victims.
Doctors recommended anaplasty to restore both function and appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plastic surgery - surgery that repairs or reconstructs body tissue

Etymology

From Greek ana- (again) + plastos (formed, molded).
