Portulacaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɔːrtʃələˈkeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌpɔːtjʊləˈkeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, usually succulent herbs, widely distributed and especially common in the Americas
family of succulent flowering plants

Examples

Many species in the Portulacaceae thrive in arid environments due to their ability to store water.
The plant's thick, fleshy leaves immediately suggested it belonged to the Portulacaceae.
Gardeners often choose members of the Portulacaceae for their resilience and low maintenance.

Etymology

From the Latin genus name Portulaca, with the family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɔrtʃəˌleɪsiˈi / (AmE), / ˌpɔːtjʊlækəˈsiːʌi / (BrE)
a family of usually succulent herbs, widely distributed around the world, especially in the Americas
family of succulent herbs (botany)

Examples

The Portulacaceae family includes many succulent plants adapted to arid climates.
Researchers discovered a new species in the Portulacaceae during their expedition in South America.
Some edible plants, such as purslane, belong to the Portulacaceae.

Etymology

From the genus name Portulaca, which comes from Latin portula (little gate), referring to the lid of the seed capsule.
