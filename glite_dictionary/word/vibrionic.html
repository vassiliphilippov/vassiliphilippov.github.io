vibrionic

1. ADJ

/ vaɪˈbriː.ɑn.ɪk / (AmE), / vɪˈbrɪ.ɒn.ɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or caused by bacteria of the genus Vibrio.
caused by Vibrio bacteria
medical

Examples

The outbreak was traced to a vibrionic infection linked to contaminated seafood.
Doctors identified the illness as a form of vibrionic cholera.
In some regions, vibrionic dysentery remains a significant health concern.

Synset Examples

vibrionic dysentery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vibrio - a genus of bacteria, some species of which cause disease

Etymology

From Vibrio (a genus of bacteria) + -ic, forming adjectives of relation.
