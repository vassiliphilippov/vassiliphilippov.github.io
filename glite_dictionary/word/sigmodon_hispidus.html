Sigmodon hispidus

1. NOUN

/ sɪɡˈmoʊdɑn hɪsˈpɪdəs / (AmE), / sɪɡˈməʊdɒn hɪsˈpɪdəs / (BrE)
a species of long-haired burrowing rodent, known as the hispid cotton rat, found in southern North America and Central America
species of cotton rat from southern North America

Examples

Researchers observed Sigmodon hispidus populations thriving in grassy fields near the river.
The burrows of Sigmodon hispidus can damage agricultural crops in southern regions.
Predators such as owls often prey on Sigmodon hispidus in its natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotton rat - any rodent of the genus Sigmodon

Etymology

From Latin: 'sigmoides' (sigma-shaped) and 'hispidus' (bristly, hairy).
