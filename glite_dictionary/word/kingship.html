kingship

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪŋˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈkɪŋʃɪp / (BrE)
the position, dignity, or authority of a king; the state of being a king
the position or authority of a king

Examples

He dreamed of kingship from a young age and studied history extensively.
The transition of kingship was marked by elaborate ceremonies and ancient rituals.
Many legends question whether his kingship was truly legitimate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: queenship - the position or authority of a queen
  • Synonym: monarchy - a form of government with a monarch at the head

Etymology

From Old English cyning + -ship, meaning the condition or office of a king.
