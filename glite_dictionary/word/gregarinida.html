Gregarinida

1. NOUN

/ ɡrɛˌɡærəˈnaɪdə / (AmE), / ɡrɪˌɡarɪˈniːdə / (BrE)
An order of protozoan parasites in the subclass Telosporidia, comprising the gregarines.
order of protozoan parasites (gregarines)

Examples

The Gregarinida are found as parasites in the digestive tracts of invertebrates.
Researchers studied the life cycle of several species belonging to the Gregarinida.
A detailed classification of Gregarinida was included in the latest protozoology textbook.

Etymology

From New Latin, derived from 'gregarina' (type genus) and '-ida' (taxonomic suffix).
