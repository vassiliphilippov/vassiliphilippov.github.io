Oestridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːstrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈiːstrɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of flies commonly known as warble flies, whose larvae are parasitic in mammals
family of parasitic warble flies

Examples

Larvae of the Oestridae family can cause significant damage to livestock.
The biology textbook contained a section on Oestridae and their life cycles.
Veterinarians must be familiar with Oestridae when treating certain parasitic skin conditions in cattle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warble fly - common name for various flies of the family Oestridae

Etymology

From New Latin Oestridae, based on Greek oistros (gadfly).
