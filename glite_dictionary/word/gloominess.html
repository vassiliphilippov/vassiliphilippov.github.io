gloominess

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡluːmɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡluːmɪnəs / (BrE)
a feeling or atmosphere of depression, sadness, or lack of hope
a feeling or atmosphere of sadness or depression

Examples

A sense of gloominess filled the room after they heard the disappointing news.
The gloominess of winter afternoons always puts me in a quiet mood.
The film captures the gloominess of life in the war-torn city.

Synset Examples

gloom pervaded the office

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cheerfulness - a feeling or atmosphere of happiness or optimism
  • Synonym: melancholy - a feeling of deep sadness; a gloomy mood

Etymology

From 'gloomy' + '-ness', with 'gloomy' deriving from Middle English 'gloumen' (to look sullen).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡluːmi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɡluːmɪnɪs / (BrE)
A state or quality of being sad, depressed, or lacking cheerfulness; a feeling or atmosphere of sadness or melancholy.
state of being sad or melancholy

Examples

There was a sense of gloominess in the air after the disappointing news.
Her persistent gloominess worried her friends and family.
The gloominess of the weather matched his somber mood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melancholy - a deep, persistent sadness or depressive mood
  • Antonym: cheerfulness - the quality of being noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From 'gloomy' + '-ness'; 'gloomy' from Middle English 'gloume' (twilight, darkness).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡluːminəs / (AmE), / ˈɡluːmɪnəs / (BrE)
A feeling of sadness, depression, or uneasy apprehension about the future.
feeling of sadness or melancholy

Examples

She could not hide her gloominess as she waited for the results.
A sense of gloominess settled over the office after the announcement.
Despite the gloominess in his heart, he tried to smile for his friends.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cheerfulness - a feeling or state of being happy and optimistic
  • Synonym: melancholy - a feeling of deep sadness or sorrow

Etymology

From 'gloomy' (late Middle English, possibly from Middle Dutch 'glumen', to glow faintly) + '-ness'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈɡluːminəs / (AmE), / ˈɡluːminɪs / (BrE)
The quality of having a frowning, downcast, or scowling appearance.
having a frowning or scowling appearance

Examples

There was a certain gloominess in his expression that worried his friends.
The gloominess of her face made it clear she was upset about the news.
Despite the gloominess of their boss, the office remained positive.

Synset Examples

Virgil has alluded to the gloominess of his countenance in a passage which has given great disgust to the critics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cheerfulness - the quality of being noticeably happy and optimistic
  • Synonym: melancholy - a feeling of deep sadness or sorrow

Etymology

From 'gloomy' + '-ness', based on Middle English 'gloumen' (to become dark) and Old English 'glōm' (twilight).
