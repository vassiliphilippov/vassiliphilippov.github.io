bidder

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪdər / (AmE), / ˈbɪdə / (BrE)
A person who makes a bid, especially one who announces a bid during a card game.
person making a bid (cards)

Examples

During the bridge tournament, each bidder carefully considered their opening move.
In this round, the bidder called for hearts as the trump suit.
Sometimes the most experienced bidder will bluff to mislead the other players.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: player - someone who participates in a game
  • Antonym: passer - someone who passes, especially in card games

Etymology

From 'bid' + '-er', indicating a person who bids.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɪdər / (AmE), / ˈbɪdə / (BrE)
a person or organization that offers to pay a particular amount of money for something, especially at an auction.
person who makes a bid (esp. at auction)

Examples

The highest bidder won the antique vase after a fierce competition.
Only one bidder showed up for the government contract.
Each bidder must register before participating in the online auction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offeror - a person who makes an offer, especially in legal contexts
  • Antonym: seller - person who sells something, the opposite role to bidder at an auction

Etymology

From 'bid' (to offer), with agent suffix '-er'.

Synset POS: n
