West Tocharian

1. NOUN

/ wɛst toʊˈkɛəriən / (AmE), / west təʊˈkɛəriən / (BrE)
the western dialect of the extinct Tocharian language, formerly spoken in the ancient cities of Kucha, Qarašähär, and Turpan in Central Asia
western dialect of Tocharian language

Examples

Scholars have discovered several ancient texts written in West Tocharian.
The pronunciation of certain words differs greatly between West Tocharian and East Tocharian.
Understanding West Tocharian is key to reconstructing the cultural history of Central Asia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Tocharian A - eastern dialect of the Tocharian language
  • Synonym: Tocharian - an extinct Indo-European language of Central Asia

Etymology

From 'Tocharian', the name of an ancient Indo-European language, with 'West' indicating the western dialect.
