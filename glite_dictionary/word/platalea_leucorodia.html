Platalea leucorodia

1. NOUN

/ pləˌteɪliə ˌluːˌkɔːˈroʊdiə / (AmE), / plaˌtæliə ˌluːkɒˈrəʊdiə / (BrE)
a large, pure white spoonbill species found in southern Eurasia and northeastern Africa, known for its long, flat, spoon-shaped bill and a distinctive crest during breeding season
Eurasian spoonbill species

Examples

The wetlands are an important habitat for the endangered Platalea leucorodia.
Researchers observed a flock of Platalea leucorodia feeding in the shallow lagoon.
Conservation efforts have helped increase the population of Platalea leucorodia in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoonbill - a wading bird with a long, flat bill resembling a spoon

Etymology

From Latin: Platalea (spoonbill) from Greek 'plataleia', and leucorodia meaning 'white heron' from Greek 'leukos' (white) + 'erodios' (heron).
