tire

1. VERB

/ ˈtaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪə / (BrE)
to exhaust or become exhausted through overuse, strain, or stress
to become exhausted or weary

Examples

Long hours of studying can tire even the most dedicated students.
Marathon runners often tire during the final miles of the race.
If you tire yourself out now, you won't have any energy left for tomorrow.

Synset Examples

We wore ourselves out on this hike

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhaust - to make extremely tired
  • Antonym: to refresh - to restore energy or strength

Etymology

From Old English tīerian (to become weary), related to 'tire' in the sense of fatigue.

5. NOUN

/ taɪɚ / (AmE), / taɪə / (BrE)
a thick rubber covering, typically filled with air, that fits around the rim of a wheel of a vehicle and provides traction, absorbs shock, and protects the wheel
rubber covering for a wheel

Examples

I had to replace the tire on my car after it went flat.
The mechanic checked each tire for wear and tear before the long trip.
Bicycle tires can be inflated with a simple hand pump.

Synset Examples

automobile tires are usually made of rubber and filled with compressed air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tyre - British spelling of 'tire' (covering of a wheel)

Etymology

From French *tire* (that which is drawn or pulled), influenced by the idea of the covering being drawn around the wheel.

Source: WordNet
