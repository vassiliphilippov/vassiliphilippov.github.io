chiromancer

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaɪrəˌmænsər / (AmE), / ˈkɪərəˌmænsə / (BrE)
A person who claims to predict people's futures by interpreting the lines on their palms; a palm reader.
palm reader, fortuneteller

Examples

The chiromancer gazed intently at my palm before revealing her predictions.
At the fair, a chiromancer offered to read people's hands for a small fee.
Skeptics often dismiss the work of a chiromancer as mere entertainment rather than true science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palmist - someone who tells fortunes by reading palms

Etymology

From Greek kheir (hand) + manteia (divination).
