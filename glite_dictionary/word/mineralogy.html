mineralogy

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪnəˈrɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌmɪnəˈrælədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of geology concerned with the study of minerals, including their structure, properties, classification, and ways of identification
scientific study of minerals

Examples

She decided to study mineralogy at university because she was fascinated by rocks and minerals.
The museum's exhibition on mineralogy featured rare crystals from around the world.
Understanding mineralogy is essential for geologists who work in mining and natural resource exploration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petrology - the scientific study of rocks

Etymology

From French 'minéralogie', from 'minéral' (mineral), from Medieval Latin 'minerale'.
