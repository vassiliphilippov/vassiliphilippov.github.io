antler

1. NOUN

/ ˈæntlər / (AmE), / ˈæntlɜː / (BrE)
the branched horn of a deer or related animal, which is typically shed and regrown annually
branched horn of a deer or related animal

Examples

The impressive antler of the stag caught the sunlight as he stepped into the clearing.
During the winter, the deer will shed its old antlers to make way for new growth in the spring.
A hunter displayed a pair of magnificent antlers as a prized trophy over his fireplace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horn - a hard, permanent outgrowth on the heads of cattle, sheep, goats, etc.

Etymology

From Middle English 'aunter', from Anglo-Norman 'antelere', probably from Old French *antolier, ultimately from Latin 'ante' (before) + 'oculus' (eye), as deer antlers grow in front of the eyes.
