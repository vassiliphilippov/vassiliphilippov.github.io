tattily

1. ADV

/ ˈtæ.ti.li / (AmE), / ˈtæ.tɪ.li / (BrE)
In a way that is shabby, cheap, or in poor condition.
in a shabby or cheap manner

Examples

She was dressed tattily compared to the rest of the guests at the gala.
The old theater was tattily decorated for the event, with faded banners and cheap streamers.
He furnished his apartment tattily, using mismatched chairs and a cracked coffee table.

Synset Examples

a cheaply dressed woman approached him in the bar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shabbily - in a way that looks untidy or worn out
  • Antonym: smartly - in a stylish or neat manner

Etymology

From 'tatty' (shabby or in poor condition), of uncertain origin, plus the adverbial suffix '-ly'.
