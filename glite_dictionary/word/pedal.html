pedal

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛdəl / (AmE), / ˈpiːdəl / (BrE)
relating to the feet; pertaining to or affecting the feet
of or relating to the feet

Examples

He suffered from a rare pedal disease affecting both feet.
Medical texts often refer to the pedal artery when discussing foot injuries.
The therapist recommended exercises to improve pedal strength and flexibility.

Synset Examples

the word for a pedal extremity is ‘foot’

Related Terms

  • Antonym: manual - relating to the hands

Etymology

From Latin 'pes, pedis' (foot).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02786981-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛdəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛdəl / (BrE)
(music) A sustained note, typically in the bass, over which changing harmonies occur.
sustained bass note (music)

Examples

The organist held a pedal while playing complex harmonies above.
A low pedal created dramatic tension in the symphony's final movement.
Composers often use a pedal to anchor the harmony during transitions.

Etymology

From French 'pédale', from Italian 'pedale', from Latin 'pedalis' (of the foot), because the organ pedal produces such notes.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06871724-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈpɛd.əl / (AmE), / ˈpɛd.əl / (BrE)
to ride or move a bicycle by pressing the pedals with your feet
ride or move a bicycle using pedals

Examples

She loves to pedal through the park on sunny weekends.
After work, he pedaled home as quickly as possible to beat the rain.
They pedaled up the steep hill, encouraging each other along the way.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cycle - to ride a bicycle

Etymology

From Latin 'pedalis' (of the foot), from 'pes, pedis' (foot).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01939335-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈpɛd.əl / (AmE), / ˈpɛd.əl / (BrE)
to operate the pedals on a keyboard instrument (such as a piano or organ) in order to modify or sustain the sound
operate pedals on a keyboard instrument

Examples

To sustain the chord, she carefully pedaled throughout the entire passage.
He learned how to pedal properly on the grand organ to achieve a richer sound.
The music teacher demonstrated how to pedal softly for a gentle effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: play - to perform music on an instrument

Etymology

From Latin 'pedalis' (of the foot), via French 'pédale'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01731931-v
Synset POS: v

5. NOUN

/ ˈpɛdəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛdəl / (BrE)
a lever that is pressed with the foot to control a mechanism, such as on a bicycle, piano, or vehicle
foot-operated lever

Examples

Press the pedal gently to accelerate the car.
She adjusted the pedal height on her bicycle for a more comfortable ride.
The piano's soft pedal creates a much quieter sound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lever - a bar or handle used to operate or control a mechanism

Etymology

From Latin 'pes, ped-' meaning 'foot'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03909502-n
Synset POS: n
