to bedim

1. VERB

/ bɪˈdɪm / (AmE), / bɪˈdɪm / (BrE)
to make something less clear or obscure; to darken or dim, either literally or figuratively
make less clear or obscure
archaic

Examples

A thick fog began to bedim the headlights as we drove through the valley.
Complicated jargon can bedim the meaning of important scientific concepts.
The years bedim his memory of those childhood days, making them seem distant and unclear.

Synset Examples

The distinction was obscured

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obscure - make unclear or hard to understand
  • Antonym: to clarify - make clear or easier to understand

Etymology

From be- (intensive prefix) + dim (to make or become less bright)

2. VERB

/ bɪˈdɪm / (AmE), / bɪˈdɪm / (BrE)
to make darker, obscure, or less distinct by reducing brightness or clarity
make darker or obscure
archaic

Examples

Clouds began to bedim the brightness of the afternoon sun.
Sadness can bedim the joy in anyone's eyes.
A thick fog will bedim the landscape, hiding familiar shapes from view.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dim - make or become less bright; reduce the brightness of
  • Antonym: to brighten - make or become lighter or more vivid

Etymology

From Middle English 'bedymmen', from 'be-' (prefix) + 'dim'.
