autumnal

1. ADJ

/ əˈtʌmnəl / (AmE), / ɔːˈtʌmnəl / (BrE)
relating to, characteristic of, or occurring in autumn
relating to autumn

Examples

The leaves displayed an autumnal palette of reds, oranges, and yellows along the avenue.
Many people enjoy the autumnal chill that signals the start of sweater weather.
The garden was filled with autumnal fruits like apples and pears.

Synset Examples

the autumnal equinox
autumnal fruits

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vernal - relating to spring
  • Antonym: summery - characteristic of summer
  • Antonym: wintry - relating to or characteristic of winter

Etymology

From Latin 'autumnalis', from 'autumnus' (autumn).

2. ADJ

/ ɔːˈtʌmnəl / (AmE), / ɔːˈtʌmnəl / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of late maturity or a period nearing decline, especially in a figurative sense.
characteristic of late maturity or decline

Examples

In his autumnal years, the artist produced some of his most reflective work.
The autumnal atmosphere of the empty park evoked a sense of quiet decline.
She spoke in an autumnal tone, filled with calm wisdom and gentle nostalgia.

Synset Examples

a serene autumnal mood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: declining - becoming less in strength, value, or quality
  • Antonym: youthful - characteristic of youth or early maturity

Etymology

From Latin autumnalis (of autumn), from autumnus (autumn).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01495274-s
Synset POS: s
