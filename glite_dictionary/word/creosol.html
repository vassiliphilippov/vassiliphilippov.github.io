creosol

1. NOUN

/ ˈkri.oʊˌsɔl / (AmE), / ˈkriː.əˌsɒl / (BrE)
a colorless to yellow, aromatic liquid compound found as a constituent of creosote
aromatic compound in creosote

Examples

Researchers isolated creosol from the tar residue for further chemical analysis.
Exposure to creosol vapors can cause irritation of the eyes and respiratory tract.
Creosol is used in some industrial processes due to its antiseptic properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creosote - an oily liquid mixture used as a wood preservative and antiseptic

Etymology

From 'creosote' and '-ol' (denoting alcohols or phenols).
