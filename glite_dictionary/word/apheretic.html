apheretic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæfəˈrɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌæfəˈrɛtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or formed by aphaeresis, the omission of a sound or syllable at the beginning of a word.
pertaining to aphaeresis in linguistics

Examples

The word 'squire' is an apheretic form of 'esquire'.
In historical linguistics, apheretic changes are common in informal speech.
She discussed several apheretic examples in her paper on English phonology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphaeresis - the omission of a sound or syllable at the beginning of a word

Etymology

From Greek aphairesis (taking away), via aphaeretic.
