licentiousness

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈsɛn.tʃəs.nəs / (AmE), / laɪˈsɛn.tʃəs.nəs / (BrE)
Dissolute or immoral behavior, especially excessive indulgence in sensual pleasures.
immoral or dissolute sensual behavior

Examples

The novelist vividly described the licentiousness of the aristocratic parties in eighteenth-century France.
Some critics accused the film of promoting licentiousness among young viewers.
He was dismissed from his post due to allegations of licentiousness and corruption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debauchery - excessive indulgence in sensual pleasures
  • Antonym: prudence - the quality of being prudent or showing self-restraint

Etymology

From Latin 'licentia' (freedom, liberty) + English suffix '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ laɪˈsɛn.tʃəs.nəs / (AmE), / laɪˈsɛn.tʃəs.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being sexually unrestrained or lacking moral discipline, especially in sexual conduct.
sexual immorality; lack of restraint

Examples

The novel shocked readers with its frank portrayal of licentiousness in the royal court.
He was dismissed from office because of repeated acts of licentiousness and corruption.
Critics accused the artist’s work of promoting licentiousness among the youth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lewdness - the quality of being obscene or indecent
  • Synonym: lasciviousness - the state or quality of being driven by lustful desires
  • Antonym: chastity - the practice of refraining from sexual conduct

Etymology

From Latin 'licentia' (freedom, license) + English '-ness'.
