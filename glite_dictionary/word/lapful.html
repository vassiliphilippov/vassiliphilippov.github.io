lapful

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæp.fʊl / (AmE), / ˈlæp.fʊl / (BrE)
as much as can be held in or on one's lap at one time
the amount that fits in the lap

Examples

She scooped a lapful of kittens and started to pet them gently.
A child sat with a lapful of popcorn, happily eating during the movie.
He returned from the orchard with a lapful of fresh apples.

Etymology

Formed from 'lap' + '-ful', a native English noun-forming suffix.
