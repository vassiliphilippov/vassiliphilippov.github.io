Edwin Hubble

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛdwɪn ˈhʌbəl / (AmE), / ˈɛdwɪn ˈhʌbəl / (BrE)
(1889–1953) An American astronomer who discovered the relationship between a galaxy's distance and its speed of recession, providing evidence for the expanding universe.
American astronomer who discovered universe expansion

Examples

The astronomer Edwin Hubble fundamentally changed our understanding of the universe.
Many discoveries in modern cosmology can be traced back to the work of Edwin Hubble.
The Hubble Space Telescope was named in honor of Edwin Hubble and his groundbreaking research.
