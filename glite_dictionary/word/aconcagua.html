Aconcagua

1. PROPN

/ ˌɑːkənˈkɑːgwə / (AmE), / ˌækənˈkɑːgwə / (BrE)
the highest mountain in the Western Hemisphere, located in the Andes in western Argentina, standing at 22,834 feet (6,961 meters) high.
highest mountain in South America

Examples

Many climbers dream of summiting Aconcagua in Argentina.
The ascent of Aconcagua presents significant challenges due to its altitude and weather.
From the summit of Aconcagua, you can see vast stretches of the Andes mountains.

Etymology

From Quechua or Aymara, likely meaning 'stone sentinel'.
