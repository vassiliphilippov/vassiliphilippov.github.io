hypothrombinemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpoʊˌθrɒmbɪˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəʊˌθrɒmbɪˈniːmɪə / (BrE)
a deficiency of prothrombin in the blood, leading to prolonged clotting time and increased risk of bleeding
low prothrombin level in blood
medical

Examples

The diagnosis of hypothrombinemia explained the patient's tendency to bleed easily after minor injuries.
Vitamin K supplements can help treat hypothrombinemia in cases caused by dietary deficiency.
Surgery was delayed due to concerns over hypothrombinemia and the risk of excessive bleeding.

Etymology

From hypo- (meaning 'low') + prothrombin + -emia (meaning 'condition of the blood').
