Edmund Burke

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛdmənd bɝːk / (AmE), / ˈɛdmənd bɜːk / (BrE)
(1729–1797) British statesman, political thinker, and writer known for his support of parliamentary government and opposition to the French Revolution.
British statesman and political thinker (1729–1797)

Examples

Edmund Burke is often cited for his eloquent speeches defending the rights of American colonists.
The writings of Edmund Burke played a crucial role in shaping conservative political thought.
Many historians regard Edmund Burke as a leading figure in 18th-century British politics.
