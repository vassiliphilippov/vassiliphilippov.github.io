acquittal

1. NOUN

/ əˈkwɪt̬əl / (AmE), / əˈkwɪt.əl / (BrE)
an official decision by a court that a person is not guilty of the crime with which they were charged
official finding of not guilty
legal

Examples

The jury returned an acquittal after only two hours of deliberation.
His acquittal was greeted with cheers from his supporters in the courtroom.
After a lengthy trial, the judge announced the acquittal of all defendants.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conviction - a formal declaration that someone is guilty of a criminal offense

Etymology

From Old French *acquitaille*, from acquitter (to acquit), based on Latin *adquitare* (to set free).
