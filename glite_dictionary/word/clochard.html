clochard

1. NOUN

/ kloʊˈʃɑrd / (AmE), / kləʊˈʃɑːd / (BrE)
A homeless person, especially a vagrant or tramp in Paris.
homeless vagrant, especially in Paris

Examples

In Paris, he lived like a clochard on the banks of the Seine.
The old novel describes the adventures of a clochard wandering the city at night.
She photographed a clochard sleeping under the bridge, capturing a piece of Parisian street life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vagrant - a person without a settled home or regular work who wanders from place to place
  • Synonym: tramp - a person who travels from place to place on foot, usually living by begging

Etymology

From French clochard (tramp, vagrant), probably from cloche (bell), alluding to carrying a bell-shaped bundle.
