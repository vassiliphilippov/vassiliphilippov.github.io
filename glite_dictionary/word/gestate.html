to gestate

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒɛsteɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛsteɪt / (BrE)
to develop an idea or plan in the mind over a period of time before it is fully formed or realized
develop an idea or plan over time

Examples

The director began to gestate the concept for his new film over several years.
She spent months gestating the novel's intricate plot before starting to write.
Many great inventions are gestated quietly before they revolutionize the world.

Synset Examples

He conceived of a robot that would help paralyzed patients
This library was well conceived

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to conceive - to form or develop an idea or plan in the mind

Etymology

From Latin gestare (to carry, to bear), frequentative of gerere (to bear).

2. VERB

/ ˈdʒɛˌsteɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛsteɪt / (BrE)
(of a female animal or woman) be pregnant with (a child or offspring); carry in the womb during the period from conception to birth.
be pregnant with; carry in the womb
medical

Examples

The elephant gestates its calf for nearly two years.
She gestated twins, which made the pregnancy especially challenging.
Many animals gestate their young for a fixed period before giving birth.

Synset Examples

She is bearing his child
They are expecting another child in January
I am carrying his child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carry - to be pregnant with and transport in the womb

Etymology

From Latin gestatus, past participle of gestare (to carry), frequentative of gerere (to bear).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00058790-v
Synset POS: v
