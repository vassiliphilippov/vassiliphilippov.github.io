to modernise

1. VERB

/ ˈmɑːdərˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɒdənaɪz / (BrE)
to make (something) more modern by introducing new methods, ideas, or technology
make more modern; update with new technology
british

Examples

Many companies are trying to modernise their offices with new technologies.
As the city continues to modernise, public transport is becoming more efficient and environmentally friendly.
The school decided to modernise its curriculum to include more digital literacy.

Synset Examples

Many countries in Asia are now developing at a very fast pace
Viet Nam is modernizing rapidly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to modernize - to make something modern or bring up to date (US spelling)
  • Antonym: to antiquate - to make old-fashioned or obsolete

Etymology

From modern + -ise; based on the word 'modern' (via Latin 'modernus').

2. VERB

/ ˈmɑːdərˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɒdənaɪz / (BrE)
to make (something) more modern by updating, improving, or renovating it
make something more modern

Examples

The city plans to modernise its public transportation system over the next decade.
They decided to modernise the old library to attract more visitors.
Many companies must modernise their operations to remain competitive in the digital age.

Synset Examples

You should overhaul your car engine
overhaul the health care system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: update - to make something more current or up to date
  • Synonym: to renovate - to restore to good condition or to make new again
  • Antonym: outdate - to make or become out of date

Etymology

From French moderniser, based on Latin modernus (modern).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00262962-v
Synset POS: v
