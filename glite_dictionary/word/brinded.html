brinded

1. ADJ

/ ˈbrɪndəd / (AmE), / ˈbrɪndɪd / (BrE)
having a grey or brown streaked or patchy pattern in coloring, especially describing animal fur
grey/brown streaked or patchy, esp. of fur

Examples

The brinded cat slinked through the garden, its patchy coat barely visible among the leaves.
In the play, the witches mention a brinded cat as one of their familiars.
The farmer admired the brinded pattern on the young calf's hide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brindled - having a grey or brown streaked or patchy pattern, especially of animal fur

Etymology

Variant of 'brindled', from Middle English, of uncertain origin, possibly related to Old Norse 'brúnn' (brown).
