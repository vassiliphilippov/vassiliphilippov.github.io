amplitude

1. NOUN

/ ˈæm.pləˌtuːd / (AmE), / ˈæm.plɪ.tjuːd / (BrE)
(physics) The maximum extent of a vibration or oscillation, measured from the position of equilibrium.
maximum displacement of a wave (physics)

Examples

The sound was barely audible because the amplitude of the wave was very low.
Measuring the amplitude of the oscillation is essential in physics experiments.
If you increase the amplitude, the motion becomes more energetic and the peaks rise higher.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frequency - the number of times a wave repeats per second
  • Synonym: wavelength - the distance between consecutive wave peaks

Etymology

From Latin 'amplitudo' (breadth, width, size), from 'amplus' (large, spacious).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæmplɪˌtud / (AmE), / ˈamplɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
the extent or magnitude of something, especially as measured from its average value to its maximum; often used in mathematics, physics, and engineering
extent or magnitude of something

Examples

Scientists measured the amplitude of the earthquake using a seismograph.
The amplitude of the sound wave determines how loud the noise will be.
A greater amplitude indicates a stronger signal in electrical engineering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnitude - the great size or extent of something
  • Synonym: intensity - the quality of being intense, particularly the strength or force measured

Etymology

From Latin amplitudo (breadth, size), from amplus (large, ample).
