Lambis

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæm.bɪs / (AmE), / ˈlæm.bɪs / (BrE)
A genus of large, ornately spined sea snails found in shallow tropical waters of the eastern hemisphere, commonly known as scorpion shells.
genus of tropical marine snails (scorpion shells)

Examples

The ornate shells of the Lambis are often collected for their distinctive spines.
Many divers in the Indo-Pacific region look for Lambis among the coral reefs.
The Lambis genus includes several species popular in the aquarium trade.

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name 'Lambis', used in zoological classification.
