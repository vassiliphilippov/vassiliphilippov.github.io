Epigaea

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈdʒiə / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdʒiːə / (BrE)
A small genus of creeping evergreen shrubs, also known as trailing arbutus, native to North America and East Asia.
genus of creeping evergreen shrubs

Examples

The woods were dotted with blooming Epigaea in early spring.
Botanists studied the unique growth patterns of Epigaea in the wild.
Many gardeners prize Epigaea for its fragrant pink and white flowers.

Etymology

From Greek epi- (upon) + gaia (earth), referring to the plant's habitat.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈdʒiːə / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdʒiːə / (BrE)
A small genus of low, creeping, evergreen shrubs of the heath family, including the trailing arbutus, native to North America and eastern Asia.
genus of creeping evergreen shrubs

Examples

The Epigaea genus is best known for the fragrant trailing arbutus that carpets forest floors in spring.
Botanists often study Epigaea to understand evergreen groundcover in woodland habitats.
Many wildflower enthusiasts seek out Epigaea during nature walks in April.

Etymology

From Greek epi- (upon) and gaia (earth), referring to plants growing upon the ground.
