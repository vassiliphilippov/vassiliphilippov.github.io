hydroxychloroquine

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌdrɑksiˌklɔːrəˈkwɪn / (AmE), / haɪˌdrɒksɪˌklɔːrəˈkwɪn / (BrE)
a drug used to treat rheumatoid arthritis, malaria, and lupus erythematosus, known for its anti-inflammatory properties
anti-inflammatory drug for arthritis, malaria, and lupus
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed hydroxychloroquine to help manage her lupus symptoms.
Studies have examined the effectiveness of hydroxychloroquine against various autoimmune diseases.
He experienced mild side effects after taking hydroxychloroquine for his rheumatoid arthritis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chloroquine - a related antimalarial drug with similar structure

Etymology

From 'hydroxy-' (chemical prefix) + 'chloroquine', itself from chloro- (relating to chlorine) and quinine (antimalarial compound).
