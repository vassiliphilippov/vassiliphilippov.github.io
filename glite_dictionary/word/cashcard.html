cashcard

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæʃˌkɑrd / (AmE), / ˈkæʃˌkɑːd / (BrE)
a payment card that enables the holder to withdraw cash from an ATM or bank
payment card for withdrawing cash

Examples

I used my cashcard to withdraw money from the ATM.
You can access your funds quickly with a cashcard.
Some banks issue a cashcard instead of a traditional debit card.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debit card - a payment card that deducts money directly from the holder’s account
  • Antonym: credit card - a payment card allowing purchases on credit

Etymology

From 'cash' + 'card', indicating a card used for accessing cash.
