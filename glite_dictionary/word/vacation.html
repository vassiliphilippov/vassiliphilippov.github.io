vacation

1. NOUN

/ veɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / vəˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
A period of time spent away from work or study for rest, travel, or pleasure.
time off for rest or pleasure

Examples

I am planning to take a vacation to Greece this summer.
Many employees save their vacation days for the holidays.
Her family went on a skiing vacation in December.

Synset Examples

we get two weeks of vacation every summer
we took a short holiday in Puerto Rico

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holiday - a period when work or school is suspended for rest or celebration

Etymology

From Latin 'vacatio' (freedom, exemption), from 'vacare' (to be empty, free).

2. VERB

/ veɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / vəˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
to spend a period of time away from home or work, especially for rest or recreation
spend time away for rest or recreation

Examples

We vacation in the mountains every summer to escape the city heat.
Last year, she vacationed in Europe for two weeks.
They are vacationing on an island in the Caribbean this month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holiday - to spend or take a holiday (British English)

Etymology

From Latin 'vacatio' (freedom from occupation, exemption, leisure), via Old French and Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02714581-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ vəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / vəˈkeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act of making something legally void, especially in a legal context
the act of legally voiding something
legal

Examples

The court ordered the vacation of the previous judgment due to new evidence.
His lawyer filed a motion for the vacation of the conviction.
The vacation of the contract restored the parties to their original positions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annulment - the act of declaring something invalid, especially a marriage or legal contract
  • Synonym: to vacate - to cancel or annul a legal decision or order

Etymology

From Latin vacatio (freedom, exemption), also influenced by the verb 'vacate' (to annul or render void).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01256252-n
Synset POS: n
