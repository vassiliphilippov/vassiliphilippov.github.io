prairie

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈprɛə.ri / (BrE)
a large, flat area of grassland in North America, typically without trees and covered with tall grasses
a large, treeless grassy plain

Examples

The vast prairie stretched as far as the eye could see, dotted with wildflowers and tall grasses.
Settlers crossed the prairie in covered wagons during the westward expansion.
Bison once roamed freely across the North American prairie in enormous herds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steppe - a large area of unforested grassland, especially in Europe or Asia

Etymology

From French 'prairie' meaning 'meadow', from Late Latin 'prataria', from Latin 'pratum' (meadow).
