Prunella

1. NOUN

/ pruˈnɛlə / (AmE), / pruˈnɛlə / (BrE)
A small genus of perennial plants, native mostly to Eurasia, with terminal spikes of small purplish or white flowers.
genus of perennial flowering plants

Examples

Prunella is commonly found growing wild in meadows and woodland edges across Europe.
Gardeners often choose Prunella for its long-lasting purple blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of Prunella species in traditional herbal treatments.

Etymology

From Latin 'prunella', the name of the plant.

genus of Eurasian accentor birds

Several species within the genus Prunella can be found across the high mountains of Central Asia.

/ pruˈnɛlə / (AmE), / pruˈnɛlə / (BrE)

Examples

Many herbalists use Prunella in traditional remedies for sore throats and wounds.

Researchers observed that Prunella plants attract a variety of pollinators due to their colorful spikes.

From Medieval Latin *prunella*, referring to the plant; possibly from German *Brunelle*.
