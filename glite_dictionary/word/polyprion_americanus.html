Polyprion americanus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˌpraɪ.ən əˌmɛr.ɪˈkeɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˌprʌɪ.ən əˌmɛr.ɪˈkeɪ.nəs / (BrE)
a large, brown marine fish of the Atlantic and Mediterranean, found near rocky reefs and shipwrecks; also known as the wreckfish
a large marine fish, the wreckfish

Examples

Divers often spot the Polyprion americanus sheltering around old shipwrecks in the Mediterranean Sea.
The Polyprion americanus can grow to impressive sizes, making it a prized catch for commercial fisheries.
Oceanographers are studying the habitats preferred by Polyprion americanus to improve conservation efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wreckfish - common name for Polyprion americanus

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'prion' (saw), and Latin 'americanus' (American).
