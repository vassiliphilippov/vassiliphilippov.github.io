talismanic

1. ADJ

/ ˌtælɪzˈmænɪk / (AmE), / ˌtalɪzˈmanɪk / (BrE)
having or believed to have magical or protective power, like that of a talisman
having magical or protective power

Examples

The villagers believed the talismanic stone would keep their homes safe from harm.
She wore a talismanic necklace for good luck during the exam.
The general carried a talismanic charm into every battle for protection.

Synset Examples

a talismanic amulet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magical - having or using magic; relating to or resembling magic
  • Synonym: protective - intended or adapted to protect someone or something

Etymology

From 'talisman' (magical object), which comes from French 'talisman', from Arabic 'ṭilasm', from Greek 'telesma' (consecration).
