alexic

1. ADJ

/ əˈlɛksɪk / (AmE), / əˈlɛksɪk / (BrE)
relating to or showing symptoms of alexia (the loss of the ability to read)
relating to alexia (inability to read)
medical

Examples

The patient exhibited alexic symptoms following the stroke.
Certain brain injuries may cause an alexic condition affecting reading abilities.
He was diagnosed as alexic after a thorough neurological examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alexia - loss of the ability to read, usually due to brain injury

Etymology

Formed from 'alexia' (loss of the ability to read) and the adjectival suffix '-ic'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03051778-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ əˈlɛksɪk / (AmE), / əˈlɛksɪk / (BrE)
A person who has alexia, a disorder characterized by the loss of the ability to read.
person with alexia (reading disorder)
medical

Examples

The clinic has recently started a program to support individuals who are alexic.
Researchers studied how alexic patients process written language differently from the general population.
After his stroke, he became alexic and required extensive rehabilitation to communicate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alexia - loss of the ability to read; acquired reading disorder

Etymology

From Greek 'alexia', from 'a-' (not) + 'lexis' (word, reading).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09802296-n
Synset POS: n
