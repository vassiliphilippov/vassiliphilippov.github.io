Schizosaccharomycetaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌskɪtsoʊˌsækəroʊmaɪˈsiːtiː / (AmE), / ˌskɪtsəʊˌsækərəʊmaɪˈsiːtɪiː / (BrE)
a family of single-celled fungi in the order Endomycetales, which includes the fission yeasts.
family of fission yeasts (fungi)
medical

Examples

The Schizosaccharomycetaceae were first described in the late 19th century.
Research on Schizosaccharomycetaceae has advanced our understanding of cell division.
Members of the Schizosaccharomycetaceae are important model organisms in molecular biology.

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining 'Schizo-' (split), 'saccharo-' (sugar), 'mycete' (fungus), and the family suffix '-aceae'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌskɪzoʊˌsækəˌroʊmaɪsiːˈteɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌskɪtsəʊˌsakəˌrəʊmaɪsiːˈtiːsaɪ / (BrE)
A family of fungi in the order Endomycetales, known for including certain yeast-like organisms.
family of fungi in Endomycetales

Examples

Species within the Schizosaccharomycetaceae family are often used in research on cell division.
The Schizosaccharomycetaceae are distinguished by their unique mode of yeast reproduction.
Researchers identified a new member of Schizosaccharomycetaceae in a soil sample.

Etymology

From Greek ‘schizo-’ (split), ‘saccharo-’ (sugar), and ‘myces’ (fungus), indicating split sugar fungi.
