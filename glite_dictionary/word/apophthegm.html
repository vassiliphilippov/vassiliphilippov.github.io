apophthegm

1. NOUN

/ ˈæpəˌθɛm / (AmE), / ˈapɒθɛm / (BrE)
a short, concise, and instructive saying or maxim
concise instructive saying

Examples

The philosopher was known for his memorable apophthegms about life and morality.
She concluded her speech with a wise apophthegm that left the audience reflecting on their choices.
Ancient texts are often filled with instructive apophthegms that remain relevant today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphorism - a concise statement expressing a general truth
  • Synonym: maxim - a general truth, rule, or principle, often expressed tersely

Etymology

From Greek *apophthegma* (terse saying), from *apo-* (from) + *phthengesthai* (to speak out).
