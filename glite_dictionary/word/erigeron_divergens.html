Erigeron divergens

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛrɪˈdʒɛrɒn daɪˈvɜrdʒənz / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪˈdʒɪərɒn dɪˈvɜːdʒənz / (BrE)
A well-branched plant of western North America with hairy leaves and stems, each bearing a solitary flower head with narrow white, pink, or lavender rays.
species of fleabane with narrow white or colored rays
medical

Examples

The wildflowers included Erigeron divergens, easily recognized by its narrow rays and solitary flower heads.
Botanists surveyed the area and noted a high density of Erigeron divergens among the native flora.
After the rain, the slopes were dotted with blooming Erigeron divergens in shades of white and lavender.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fleabane - any plant of the genus Erigeron or related genera, often used as a common name

Etymology

From Greek: 'eri' (early) + 'geron' (old man); species name 'divergens' from Latin for 'diverging'.
