thickset

1. ADJ

/ ˈθɪkˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈθɪkˌsɛt / (BrE)
(of plants or trees) growing close together and densely packed.
growing closely and densely (of plants)

Examples

A row of thickset hedges shielded the cottage from the road.
The path wound through thickset shrubbery that blocked much of the sunlight.
Beyond the meadow, thickset trees formed the edge of the forest.

Synset Examples

thickset trees

Etymology

From 'thick' + 'set', indicating dense placement; use dates to the 16th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈθɪkˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈθɪkˌsɛt / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) having a short, solid, and sturdy build.
solid, sturdy build

Examples

The security guard was a thickset man with broad shoulders and a steady gaze.
A group of thickset sheep grazed lazily in the meadow.
Despite his thickset frame, he moved with surprising agility on the court.

Synset Examples

a wrestler of compact build
he was tall and heavyset
stocky legs
a thickset young man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stocky - broad and sturdy in shape, usually of a person
  • Antonym: slender - gracefully thin or slight in build
