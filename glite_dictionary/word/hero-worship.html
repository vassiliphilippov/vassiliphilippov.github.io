to hero-worship

1. VERB

/ ˈhɪəroʊ ˌwɜrʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈhɪərəʊ ˌwɜːʃɪp / (BrE)
to admire or idolize someone excessively and uncritically, often treating them as perfect or beyond reproach
admire or idolize someone uncritically

Examples

As a child, he used to hero-worship famous scientists such as Einstein and Curie.
The younger players tend to hero-worship the team captain, seeing him as flawless.
She hero-worshipped her older sister, copying everything she did.

Synset Examples

Many teenagers idolized the Beatles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to idolize - to admire or love someone very much or too much
  • Synonym: to adore - to love and admire someone deeply or to excess
  • Antonym: to criticize - to find fault with or express disapproval of someone

Etymology

Formed from 'hero' (from Greek hērōs, 'protector, defender') + 'worship'.
