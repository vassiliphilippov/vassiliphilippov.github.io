seidh

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪð / (AmE), / ˈseɪð / (BrE)
an Old Norse form of sorcery involving magic and shamanistic practices, believed to have been performed during the Late Scandinavian Iron Age
Norse sorcery or shamanism

Examples

Historians have found evidence of seidh ceremonies in several Viking burial sites.
Many scholars believe that seidh was practiced mostly by women known as völvas.
Today, some modern pagans attempt to reconstruct seidh rituals in their spiritual practices.

Synset Examples

Of all the reconstructed systems of archaic magickal practice, Seiðr seems to be one of the most misunderstood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witchcraft - the practice of magic, especially involving spells and the invocation of spirits
  • Synonym: sorcery - the use of magic, especially that involving the invocation of spirits

Etymology

From Old Norse *seiðr* (sorcery, magic, enchantment), related to ancient Germanic magical practices.
