to outguess

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈɡɛs / (AmE), / aʊtˈɡɛs / (BrE)
to predict what someone will do or think before they do; to anticipate correctly before another person.
predict or anticipate before another

Examples

In chess, he tried to outguess his opponent and anticipate the next move.
The marketing team hoped to outguess current trends to stay ahead of competitors.
She attempted to outguess the teacher and prepare for unexpected quiz questions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to anticipate - to regard as probable; to expect or predict
  • Synonym: to predict - to say what will happen before it occurs

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'out-' meaning 'to surpass' and 'guess'.
