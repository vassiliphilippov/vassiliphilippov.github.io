Salix cinerea

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlɪks sɪˈnɪriə / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪks sɪˈnɪəriə / (BrE)
A Eurasian shrubby willow with whitish, densely hairy twigs, commonly found along riverbanks and wetlands.
Eurasian shrubby willow species

Examples

The Salix cinerea thrives in damp soils along riverbanks throughout Europe.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Salix cinerea in restored wetland habitats.
Many birds use Salix cinerea shrubs for nesting during the spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - Tree or shrub from the genus Salix

Etymology

From Latin: 'Salix' meaning willow, and 'cinerea' meaning ashen, referring to the greyish color.
