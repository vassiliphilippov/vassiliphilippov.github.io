enteroscope

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.təɹ.oʊˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈɛntərəskəʊp / (BrE)
a medical instrument used to examine the interior of the intestine
instrument for examining the intestine
medical

Examples

The doctor used an enteroscope to investigate the cause of the patient's abdominal pain.
Advances in enteroscope technology have made intestinal examinations more accurate and less invasive.
After the procedure, the enteroscope was carefully cleaned and prepared for the next use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endoscope - an instrument for examining the interior of a bodily organ or cavity
  • Degree Lower: colonoscope - an instrument for examining the colon

Etymology

From Greek 'enteron' (intestine) + 'skopein' (to look at, examine).
