primitive

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɪm.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈprɪm.ɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
Belonging to an early stage of development; characterized by simplicity or crudity.
relating to something early, simple, or crude

Examples

Many isolated tribes still use primitive tools made from stone and wood.
Early computers were primitive compared to today's sophisticated machines.
The explorers faced primitive living conditions in the remote jungle.

Synset Examples

the crude weapons and rude agricultural implements of early man
primitive movies of the 1890s
primitive living conditions in the Appalachian mountains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crude - in a natural or raw state; not yet processed or refined

Etymology

From Latin 'primitivus' meaning 'first of its kind'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɪməˌtɪv / (AmE), / ˈprɪm.ɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
a person who lived in or belongs to an early stage of civilization, especially before the development of complex societies
person from an early civilization

Some anthropologists studied the art created by the primitives of the region.
The novel describes the encounter between explorers and the primitives they discovered.
Early primitives developed tools from stone and wood to survive in their environment.

Etymology

3. NOUN

/ ˈprɪm.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈprɪm.ɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
A mathematical function or expression from which another is derived, commonly by differentiation; an antiderivative.
function from which another is derived (math)

Examples

The primitive of a continuous function can be found using integration.
In calculus, finding a primitive is the inverse process of differentiation.
Physicists often use the primitive of a velocity function to determine displacement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiderivative - a function whose derivative is a given function

Etymology

From Latin primitīvus (first or earliest), from primus (first).

7. NOUN

/ ˈprɪm.ɪ.t̬ɪv / (AmE), / ˈprɪm.ɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
(Linguistics) A word that serves as the basis for the formation of inflected or derived forms.
root word for derivatives

Examples

The verb 'pick' is considered the primitive from which the noun 'picket' is derived.
In language studies, linguists often identify the primitive of a word family to trace its origins.
The dictionary lists 'act' as the primitive for words like 'action', 'active', and 'actor'.

Synset Examples

‘pick’ is the primitive from which ‘picket’ is derived

Related Terms

  • Synonym: root - the basic part of a word, to which affixes can be added in derivation
  • Antonym: derivative - a word formed from another by derivation, such as by adding a prefix or suffix

Etymology

From Latin 'primitivus' (first, original), from 'primus' (first).
