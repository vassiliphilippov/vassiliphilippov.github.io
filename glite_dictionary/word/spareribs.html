spareribs

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛrˌrɪbz / (AmE), / ˈspeəˌrɪbz / (BrE)
A cut of pork from the rib section, with most of the meat trimmed off, often cooked and served as a dish.
cut of pork ribs with little meat

Examples

We ordered a plate of spareribs marinated in barbecue sauce at the restaurant.
The chef slowly roasted the spareribs until the meat was tender and juicy.
She prefers spareribs to other pork cuts because of their rich, smoky flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ribs - the bones in the ribcage or a cut of meat containing these bones

Etymology

From 'spare' (lean, scanty) + 'ribs', marking the small amount of meat left on the ribs.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɛrˌrɪbz / (AmE), / ˈspɛəˌrɪbz / (BrE)
Pork or beef ribs that are baked or roasted, often with a spicy sauce, and typically served as a dish.
baked or roasted ribs dish

Examples

We ordered a plate of spareribs with extra spicy sauce at the restaurant.
The chef basted the spareribs with barbecue sauce before roasting them to perfection.
My favorite dish at the picnic was the honey-glazed spareribs straight from the grill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barbecue - meat cooked outdoors, often over an open fire or grill

Etymology

From 'spare' meaning 'not meaty' and 'ribs', originally referring to the cut of meat with little flesh.
