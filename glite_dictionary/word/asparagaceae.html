Asparagaceae

1. NOUN

/ əˌspærəˈɡeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / əˌspærəˈɡeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of monocotyledonous plants including asparagus and related genera, sometimes formerly included in Liliaceae
plant family including asparagus
medical

Examples

The Asparagaceae family includes many ornamental plants as well as the edible asparagus.
Some taxonomists have reclassified several species into the Asparagaceae from the Liliaceae family.
Recent studies suggest broadening the Asparagaceae to include additional subfamilies formerly recognized as separate.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Liliaceae - the lily family of flowering plants

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Asparagus and the family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ əˌspærəˈɡeɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / əˌspærəˈɡeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants that includes the genus Asparagus and, in some classifications, also Ruscus and related genera
family of flowering plants including asparagus

Examples

The Asparagaceae family includes familiar garden plants such as asparagus and yucca.
Recent studies have led to the reclassification of several genera into the Asparagaceae.
The broad distribution of Asparagaceae makes it an interesting subject for evolutionary research.

Etymology

From Latin *Asparagus*, the type genus, plus botanical suffix *-aceae* indicating a plant family.
