wealth

1. NOUN

/ wɛlθ / (AmE), / wɛlθ / (BrE)
an abundance of valuable possessions or money; the state of being rich and having plentiful resources
abundance of money or possessions

Examples

He accumulated great wealth through years of hard work.
Nations with abundant natural resources often have substantial wealth.
Her wealth allowed her to travel to every continent.

Synset Examples

great wealth is not a sign of great intelligence

Related Terms

  • Antonym: poverty - the state of being extremely poor
  • Synonym: affluence - the state of having a great deal of money; wealth

Etymology

From Middle English 'welthe', from 'weal' (well-being, prosperity).
