palliative

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæliˌeɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈpalɪətɪv / (BrE)
a remedy or treatment that relieves pain or alleviates symptoms without curing the underlying disease
remedy that eases pain without curing
medical

Examples

For patients with terminal illnesses, doctors may recommend a palliative to ease their discomfort.
The new palliative provided temporary relief but did not address the root cause of the disease.
Instead of surgery, she opted for a palliative that would manage her symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analgesic - a drug that relieves pain; painkiller

Etymology

From Latin 'palliare' (to cloak), from 'pallium' (cloak)

2. ADJ

/ ˈpæliəˌtɪv / (AmE), / ˈpal.i.ə.tɪv / (BrE)
Relieving pain or alleviating a problem without dealing with the underlying cause.
relieving pain or symptoms

Examples

The doctor recommended a palliative treatment to help manage her chronic pain.
Hospice care often focuses on providing palliative support for terminally ill patients.
He found the medication to be only palliative, offering some relief but not a cure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: curative - able to cure disease or heal

Etymology

From Medieval Latin palliativus, from Late Latin palliatus (cloaked, covered), from palliare (to cloak), from pallium (cloak).
