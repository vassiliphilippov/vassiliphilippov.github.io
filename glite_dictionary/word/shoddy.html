shoddy

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɑdi / (AmE), / ˈʃɒdi / (BrE)
Wool fiber that has been reclaimed from waste wool or woolen garments, used in the manufacture of cheap cloth.
reclaimed wool fiber

Examples

The factory specializes in processing shoddy into inexpensive blankets.
During the 19th century, shoddy was widely used to produce uniforms for soldiers.
Some manufacturers blend new wool with shoddy to reduce costs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mungo - wool made from rags of hard-finished cloth

Etymology

Probably from a dialectal use of 'shod' (shabby) or from 'shode', obsolete past participle of 'shed'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃɑdi / (AmE), / ˈʃɒdi / (BrE)
dishonest or unethical, especially in business or professional conduct; lacking moral integrity
unethical or dishonest (conduct)

Examples

The company's shoddy accounting practices eventually led to a major scandal.
She refused to be associated with such shoddy political tactics.
Investigators uncovered a series of shoddy deals that exploited customers.

Synset Examples

dirty police officers

shoddy business practices

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sordid - involving immoral or dishonorable actions
  • Synonym: dirty - morally corrupt or dishonest
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit or untruthfulness

Etymology

Originally referred to an inferior woolen fabric made from waste, by extension applied to poor-quality or dishonest practices.

3. ADJ

/ ˈʃɑdi / (AmE), / ˈʃɒdi / (BrE)
badly made or done; of poor quality, especially due to inferior workmanship or materials.
of inferior quality or workmanship

Examples

The construction of the bridge was criticized for its shoddy workmanship and materials.
Avoid buying shoddy products, as they tend to break quickly and are not worth the money.
He was disappointed to find that the repairs had been done in a shoddy manner.

Synset Examples

cheapjack moviemaking … that feeds on the low taste of the mob

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inferior - of lower quality; not as good as something else
  • Synonym: substandard - not meeting the required standard or quality
  • Antonym: superior - of higher quality; better than most

Etymology

Originally from English dialect, possibly from 'shod', but especially popularized in the 19th century for inferior woolen cloth.
