untouchable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtʌtʃəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʌtʃəbl / (BrE)
impossible to criticize, attack, or challenge successfully; immune to negative judgment or action.
beyond criticism or attack

Examples

For years, the company president seemed untouchable, no matter how unpopular his decisions were.
The senator was once untouchable, but a recent scandal damaged his reputation.
Many believed the star athlete was untouchable until investigations uncovered evidence of wrongdoing.

Synset Examples

for the first time criticism was directed at a hitherto untouchable target

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invulnerable - impossible to harm or damage
  • Antonym: vulnerable - open to harm, criticism, or attack
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtʌtʃ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʌtʃ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
not allowed or permitted to be touched; forbidden to the touch.
not allowed to be touched

Examples

The rare vase in the exhibit is strictly untouchable for visitors.
Many items in the old library are untouchable due to their fragile condition.
In the art gallery, all paintings are untouchable to prevent damage.

Synset Examples

in most museums such articles are untouchable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inaccessible - not able to be reached or entered
  • Antonym: permissible - allowed or permitted

Etymology

From un- (not) + touchable (able to be touched).

4. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtʌtʃəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʌtʃəbl / (BrE)
(historical, chiefly South Asian) Regarded as belonging to the lowest social caste, and thus considered defiled or unacceptable for physical contact or social mixing.
lowest caste; socially defiled (esp. South Asian context)

Examples

In some traditional societies, certain people were considered untouchable and faced severe discrimination.
She spoke out against the unjust treatment of untouchable communities in her homeland.
The book explores the harsh realities experienced by those labeled as untouchable in rural India.

5. NOUN

/ ʌnˈtʌtʃəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʌtʃəbl / (BrE)
a member of the lowest social and ritual caste in the traditional Hindu system of India, often considered outside and below the four main castes
member of the lowest caste in India

Examples

Before the reforms, untouchable communities faced severe discrimination in Indian society.
The film highlights the struggles of an untouchable striving for dignity and equality.
Although technically illegal, prejudice against untouchables persists in some rural areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dalit - official term for members of the lowest caste in India, previously called untouchables

6. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtʌtʃəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʌtʃəbl / (BrE)
impossible to defeat, harm, or criticize; not able to be assailed.
impossible to defeat or criticize

Examples

After winning twenty games in a row, the team seemed untouchable to their rivals.
The governor's popularity made her almost untouchable during the election campaign.
For many years, the company was untouchable in the tech industry due to its innovations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invulnerable - impossible to harm or damage
  • Synonym: unassailable - impossible to attack, defeat, or criticize
  • Antonym: vulnerable - able to be easily hurt or attacked

Etymology

Formed from the prefix un- (not) + touchable, from the verb 'touch.'
