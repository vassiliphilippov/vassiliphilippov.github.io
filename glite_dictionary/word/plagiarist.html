plagiarist

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪdʒəriɪst / (AmE), / ˈpleɪdʒərɪɪst / (BrE)
a person who copies another individual's words or ideas and presents them as their own
person who copies others' work as their own

Examples

The university expelled the plagiarist after discovering he had copied his thesis from another student.
Being labeled a plagiarist can ruin a person's academic reputation.
The article exposed the famous writer as a plagiarist who had stolen passages from unknown authors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plagiarizer - someone who commits plagiarism
  • Antonym: original author - person who creates original work

Etymology

From Latin 'plagiarius' (kidnapper, literary thief), from 'plagium' (kidnapping).
