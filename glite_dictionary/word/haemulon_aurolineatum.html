Haemulon aurolineatum

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːmjuˈlɒn ɔːˌroʊlɪˈneɪtəm / (AmE), / ˌhiːmjʊˈlɒn ɔːˌrəʊlɪˈneɪtəm / (BrE)
A species of marine fish in the grunt family, found in the western Atlantic Ocean, especially off the West Indies and Florida.
species of grunt fish from the western Atlantic

Examples

Divers often spot the Haemulon aurolineatum swimming in large schools near coral reefs.
The Haemulon aurolineatum is commonly known as the tomtate.
Marine biologists study Haemulon aurolineatum to understand reef ecosystems in the West Indies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tomtate - the common name for Haemulon aurolineatum, a type of grunt fish

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus Haemulon (from Greek 'haimulos', blood-colored) + species name 'aurolineatum' (Latin 'aureus' gold + 'lineatus' lined).
