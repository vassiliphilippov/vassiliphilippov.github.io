dayspring

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪˌsprɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdeɪˌsprɪŋ / (BrE)
the first appearance of light in the morning; dawn
first light of day; dawn
archaic

Examples

The dayspring touched the mountaintops with a gentle glow.
Ancient poets often praised the beauty of the dayspring.
We set out before dayspring to enjoy the peace of the early morning.

Synset Examples

we got up before dawn
they talked until morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dawn - the time of day when light first appears
  • Antonym: twilight - the soft light before sunrise or after sunset

Etymology

Old English 'dægspring' (day + spring, meaning the spring or rising of the day).
