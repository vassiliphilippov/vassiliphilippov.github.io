Conoclinium coelestinum

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnəˈklɪniəm ˌsiːlɛsˈtaɪnəm / (AmE), / ˌkɒnəˈklɪnɪəm ˌsiːlɛsˈtiːnəm / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant with large, showy heads of clear blue flowers, native to the central and southeastern United States and the West Indies; also known as blue mistflower or wild ageratum.
perennial plant with blue flowers, also called blue mistflower

Examples

The garden was brightened by clusters of Conoclinium coelestinum in full bloom.
Bees and butterflies are especially attracted to Conoclinium coelestinum due to its abundant blue flowers.
Along the riverbank, wild patches of Conoclinium coelestinum created a stunning display in late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue mistflower - common name for Conoclinium coelestinum

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Conoclinium' deriving from Greek 'kōnos' (cone) and 'klinion' (little bed), and 'coelestinum' from Latin 'caelestinus' (heavenly blue).
