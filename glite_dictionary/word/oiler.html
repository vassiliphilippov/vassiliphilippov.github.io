oiler

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈɔɪlə / (BrE)
an oil well; a well that yields or has yielded oil
oil well

Examples

The company drilled a new oiler on the outskirts of town last year.
After years of production, the old oiler finally ran dry.
Several oilers in the region have become major sources of crude oil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oil well - a well from which petroleum is obtained

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪlər / (AmE), / ˈɔɪlə / (BrE)
a cargo ship specifically built to transport crude oil in large quantities.
a ship for carrying crude oil

Examples

The massive oiler docked at the port to unload its shipment of crude oil.
Several oilers were anchored off the coast, waiting for clearance to enter the harbor.
The company invested in a new oiler to expand its global shipping operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tanker - a large ship for transporting liquids in bulk, especially oil or gas

Etymology

From 'oil' + '-er', denoting something associated with oil.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈɔɪlə / (BrE)
a worker responsible for lubricating engines or machinery to ensure smooth operation
worker who lubricates engines or machinery

Examples

The oiler performed routine maintenance on the ship's engines every morning.
As an oiler, she ensured that all machines ran smoothly during her shift.
The factory hired another oiler to keep up with the increasing production demands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lubricator - a person or device that supplies lubricant
