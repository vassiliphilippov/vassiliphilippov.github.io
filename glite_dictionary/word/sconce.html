sconce

1. NOUN

/ skɑns / (AmE), / skɒns / (BrE)
a small protective shelter or screen, especially one used to provide cover from enemy fire or harsh weather
protective military shelter or screen

Examples

The troops built a sconce to protect themselves from the barrage of arrows.
During the siege, the soldiers took cover behind a hastily constructed sconce.
The old battlefield still showed traces of the sconce that sheltered the defenders.

Etymology

From Middle Dutch 'schans' (entrenchment, earthwork); related to German 'Schanze'.

2. NOUN

/ skɑns / (AmE), / skɒns / (BrE)
a decorative bracket fixed to a wall for holding candles or lights
decorative wall bracket for candles or lights

Examples

A pair of antique sconces flanked the fireplace, casting a warm glow over the room.
She replaced the burned-out candle in the hallway sconce before the guests arrived.
The ornate sconce added a touch of elegance to the otherwise simple corridor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bracket - a support projecting from a wall to carry weight

Etymology

From Old French 'esconse', from Latin 'absconsus' (hidden).

3. NOUN

/ skɑns / (AmE), / skɒns / (BrE)
a small fort or defensive earthwork built to protect a passage such as a ford, pass, or castle gate, especially in historical contexts
small defensive fortification or earthwork

Examples

The soldiers built a sconce near the river to guard the crossing point.
During the siege, defenders retreated to the sconce at the castle gate.
Archaeologists discovered the remnants of an old sconce protecting the mountain pass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redoubt - an enclosed defensive emplacement outside a larger fort, usually without flanking defenses

Etymology

From Dutch 'sconce' or French 'esconce', meaning 'covering' or 'protection'.

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ skɑns / (AmE), / skɒns / (BrE)
a wall-mounted holder for a candle, lamp, or torch, typically ornamental
wall-mounted candle or lamp holder

Examples

A pair of antique sconces flanked the fireplace, casting a warm glow over the room.
She lit the candles in the sconce as the evening grew darker.
The old castle was decorated with elaborate iron sconces along its stone corridors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candlestick - a holder for one or more candles

Etymology

From Old French 'esconse' (lantern, hiding place), from Latin 'absconsus' (hidden).
