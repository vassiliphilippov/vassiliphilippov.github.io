comedy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.mə.di / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ə.di / (BrE)
A comic incident or series of incidents, especially one that provokes laughter or amusement.
comic incident or events

Examples

The day was full of unexpected comedy, from spilled coffee to an impromptu dance in the hallway.
The meeting turned into a comedy when the projector failed and everyone started improvising solutions.
Life is sometimes a comedy of coincidences and misunderstandings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farce - a light dramatic work with broadly satirical humor
  • Antonym: tragedy - serious drama or incidents with an unhappy ending

Etymology

From Greek kōmōidia (comic poem), via Latin comoedia.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmədi / (AmE), / ˈkɒmədi / (BrE)
A type of drama or literature that is amusing and light, and usually ends happily.
humorous drama with a happy ending

Examples

The new play is a delightful comedy that had the audience laughing all night.
He prefers comedy over drama because he likes to leave the theater feeling cheerful.
Many Shakespearean plays are classified as comedy due to their humorous plots and happy endings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tragedy - serious drama with a sorrowful or disastrous conclusion
  • Degree Lower: farce - comedy that aims to entertain through exaggerated situations
  • Degree Higher: satire - comedy that ridicules vice or folly, often in a witty or ironic way

Etymology

From Latin 'comoedia', from Greek 'kōmōidia' (revel song).
