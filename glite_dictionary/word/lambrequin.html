lambrequin

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæm.brəˌkɪn / (AmE), / ˈlæm.brɪˌkæn / (BrE)
A decorative cloth, often a scarf, that hangs from and covers a knight’s helmet, used especially in heraldry and medieval armor.
scarf covering a knight’s helmet

Examples

The knight’s helmet was adorned with a flowing lambrequin of red and gold.
In medieval tournaments, the lambrequin served both as decoration and protection from the sun.
Heraldic shields often feature a stylized lambrequin draping from the helmet.

Etymology

From French 'lambrequin' (decorative helmet cloth), of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlæm.brə.kɪn / (AmE), / ˈlam.brɪ.kwɪn / (BrE)
a short decorative drapery or hanging, often along the upper part of a window or shelf edge, used as an ornament or to conceal fittings
decorative drapery for window or shelf

Examples

The living room curtains were topped with a richly embroidered lambrequin.
Our designer suggested adding a velvet lambrequin above the window for a touch of elegance.
The antique bookshelf was adorned with a lace lambrequin that matched the room’s decor.

Etymology

From French 'lambrequin', originally meaning a kind of ornamental cloth covering a helmet.
