double-spacing

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl ˌspeɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl ˌspeɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of typing or printing text so that each line of writing is followed by a blank line, leaving space between lines
typing with a blank line between each line

Examples

The professor asked for the essay to be double-spacing for easier reading.
Using double-spacing makes it much simpler for teachers to add comments between lines.
Most academic papers require double-spacing instead of single spacing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: single-spacing - the practice of typing or printing text with no blank lines between lines
