cilium

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪliəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪəm / (BrE)
A microscopic hairlike structure extending from the surface of certain cells, especially in protozoa and tissue cells, that moves fluid or provides locomotion.
microscopic hairlike cell structure
medical

Examples

Under the microscope, the cilium can be seen moving rhythmically to propel the cell forward.
Each epithelial cell is equipped with at least one cilium that sweeps debris away.
The movement of the cilium is essential for the respiratory tract to clear mucus effectively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flagellum - a long, whip-like appendage used for movement by cells and microorganisms

Etymology

From Latin 'cilium' meaning 'eyelash'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪliəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪliəm / (BrE)
any of the short curved hairs that grow from the edges of the eyelids; an eyelash
eyelash; hair on eyelid
medical

Examples

A single cilium was visible under the microscope, indicating it was an eyelash fragment.
The doctor explained that each cilium plays a role in protecting the eye from debris.
During the makeup tutorial, she accidentally pulled out a cilium with her eyelash curler.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eyelash - hair growing from the edge of the eyelid

Etymology

From Latin 'cilium' meaning 'eyelid' or 'eyelash'.
