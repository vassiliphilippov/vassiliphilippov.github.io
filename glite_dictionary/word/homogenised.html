homogenised

1. ADJ

/ həˈmɑːdʒəˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / həˈmɒdʒənaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a substance) made uniform in composition by blending different elements, often by breaking down particles and dispersing them evenly throughout another substance.
made uniform by blending elements

Examples

Milk is often homogenised to ensure the fat is evenly distributed throughout the liquid.
The company produces homogenised fruit juice to provide a smooth and consistent texture.
During the process, various ingredients were thoroughly homogenised to create a uniform mixture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homogeneous - consisting of parts or elements that are all the same
  • Antonym: heterogeneous - consisting of different or diverse elements

Etymology

From French homogéniser; from Ancient Greek homoios (similar) + genos (kind).

2. ADJ

/ həˈmɑdʒəˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / həˈmɒdʒəˌnaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a substance) made uniform in composition or character throughout, often by mechanical or chemical means.
made uniform in composition

Examples

Many dairy products are homogenised to ensure a smooth texture and consistent flavour.
The curriculum has become so homogenised that schools across the country offer nearly identical courses.
She preferred unprocessed food over homogenised alternatives found in supermarkets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uniform - remaining the same in all parts or at all times
  • Antonym: heterogeneous - consisting of different or diverse elements

Etymology

From the verb 'homogenise,' based on Greek 'homogenēs' (of the same kind).

WordNet ID: oewn-01204160-s
Synset POS: s
