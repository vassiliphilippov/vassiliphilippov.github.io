pignut

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪɡ.nʌt / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡ.nʌt / (BrE)
A North American hickory tree (Carya glabra) with edible but bitter nuts, or the nut produced by this tree.
a type of hickory tree with bitter nuts

Examples

The forest was filled with pignut hickories towering above the undergrowth.
He collected a handful of pignuts and tried one, but found it too bitter to eat.
Pigs used to roam the woodlands, feasting on fallen pignuts in autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hickory - A North American tree of the walnut family producing edible nuts

Etymology

Derived from 'pig', due to use of the nuts as pig feed, and 'nut'.
