enlarged

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈlɑrdʒd / (AmE), / ɪnˈlɑːdʒd / (BrE)
(of a part of the body or an object) larger than normal, usually as a result of swelling, infection, or some abnormal process.
made or become larger than usual

Examples

The doctor noted that the patient's enlarged liver might be a cause for concern.
The museum displays an enlarged photograph of the original painting.
Arthritis can result in enlarged joints that are painful and stiff.

Synset Examples

enlarged joints

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swollen - (of part of the body) abnormally expanded due to fluid or inflammation
