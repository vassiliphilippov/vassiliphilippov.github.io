x

1. NOUN

/ ɛks / (AmE), / ɛks / (BrE)
The twenty-fourth letter of the English alphabet.
the letter 'x'

Examples

She wrote her name on the form and marked an x in the checkbox.
The alphabet song always ends with the letter x followed by y and z.
Please draw a big x on the mistake so we can see it clearly.

Etymology

From Latin 'x', from Greek 'chi', ultimately from Phoenician.

3. ADJ

/ ɛks / (AmE), / ɛks / (BrE)
The Roman numeral for ten; used to indicate the number ten, especially in the enumeration of items, events, or centuries.
Roman numeral for ten (10)

Examples

The xth anniversary of the event was celebrated with great enthusiasm.
The Olympics held in Los Angeles were officially known as the X Olympiad.
She received the X prize for her outstanding achievement in science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ten - the number equivalent to the sum of six and four; one more than nine
  • Degree Lower: ix - the Roman numeral for nine
  • Degree Higher: xi - the Roman numeral for eleven

Etymology

From Latin letter X, used as the Roman numeral for ten.
