subdivision

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌb.dɪˈvɪʒ.ən / (AmE), / ˌsʌb.dɪˈvɪʒ.ən / (BrE)
a part or section that is itself a division of a larger part or section
a part of a part; divided section

Examples

Each chapter of the book is broken down into several subdivisions for clarity.
The city created a new housing subdivision outside the downtown area.
Our research focused on one specific subdivision of the main category.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: division - the act or process of dividing something into parts

Etymology

From Latin 'subdivisio', from 'sub-' (under, below) + 'divisio' (division).
