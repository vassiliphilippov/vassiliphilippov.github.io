to backpedal

1. VERB

/ ˈbækˌpɛdəl / (AmE), / ˈbækˌpɛdəl / (BrE)
to pedal backwards on a bicycle
pedal backwards on a bicycle

Examples

Some older bikes allow you to backpedal to activate the brakes.
He tried to backpedal quickly when the chain slipped off the gears.
Learning to backpedal smoothly helps cyclists maintain better control during certain maneuvers.

Etymology

Formed from 'back' + 'pedal', referring to reverse pedalling motion.

2. VERB

/ ˈbækˌpɛdəl / (AmE), / ˈbakˌpɛdəl / (BrE)
to move backward, especially by stepping back quickly (as in boxing or sports); also, to retreat from an earlier position or statement.
move or step backward (often in sports), or retract a stance

Examples

The boxer began to backpedal when his opponent pressed forward aggressively.
Under tough questioning, the politician tried to backpedal from his previous statements.
She tried to backpedal after realizing her comments had caused offense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retreat - to move back or withdraw, especially in a difficult situation
  • Antonym: advance - to move forward, especially in a contest or conflict

Etymology

From 'back' + 'pedal', originally referring to pedaling a bicycle backward.

3. VERB

/ ˈbækˌpɛdəl / (AmE), / ˈbakˌpɛdəl / (BrE)
to change one's opinion or position on an issue, making it less strong or clear, often in response to criticism or new information
retract or soften one's position

Examples

After facing public criticism, the senator began to backpedal on his earlier statements.
The company tried to backpedal after their controversial policy received backlash.
She quickly backpedaled when she realized her comments had offended her colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to retract - to withdraw a statement or accusation
  • Synonym: to withdraw - to take back an opinion or statement
  • Antonym: to dig in - to refuse to change one's opinion or stance

Etymology

Originally from the cycling term 'backpedal' (to pedal backward), figuratively applied to change in opinion or stance.
