Agropyron cristatum

1. NOUN

/ əˌgroʊ.paɪˌrɒn krɪˈsteɪ.təm / (AmE), / əˌgrɒ.pɪˌrɒn krɪˈstɑː.təm / (BrE)
A Eurasian species of grass cultivated in the United States for forage and erosion control.
species of Eurasian grass used for forage

Examples

Agropyron cristatum is often planted in the Great Plains to prevent soil erosion.
Farmers value Agropyron cristatum as a resilient forage grass for livestock.
Researchers study Agropyron cristatum for its ability to thrive in drought-prone areas.

Etymology

From the New Latin genus and species names; Agropyron from Greek 'agros' (field) and 'pyros' (wheat), cristatum from Latin 'cristatus' (crested).
