paradigmatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpær.ə.dɪgˈmæt̬.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌpær.ə.dɪgˈmæt.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or serving as a grammatical paradigm or model pattern, especially in linguistics.
relating to a grammatical paradigm or pattern

Examples

The word 'run' follows a paradigmatic pattern of verb inflection in English.
A paradigmatic change in a language may involve altering its system of grammatical endings.
Students studied the paradigmatic forms of Latin nouns in their grammar class.

Synset Examples

paradigmatic inflection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paradigm - a typical example or model; in grammar, a set of inflected forms of a word

Etymology

From French *paradigmatique*, from Medieval Latin *paradigmaticus*, from Greek *paradeigma* (pattern, example).

2. ADJ

/ ˌpær.ə.dɪgˈmæt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌpar.ə.dɪgˈmat.ɪk / (BrE)
(Linguistics) Relating to or denoting the relationship of elements that can substitute for each other in a particular context, as members of a class.
relating to substitutable members of a class (linguistics)

Examples

In linguistics, a paradigmatic relation exists when words can fill the same grammatical slot.
The teacher explained paradigmatic word associations using examples from the vocabulary list.
Verb forms in different tenses are paradigmatic variations within the same grammatical structure.

Synset Examples

paradigmatic word associations

Related Terms

  • Antonym: syntagmatic - relating to the combination of linguistic units in sequence

Etymology

From French *paradigmatique*, from Latin *paradigmaticus*, from Greek *paradeigmatikos* (exemplary, from paradeigma 'pattern, example').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02784502-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˌpær.əɡ.dɪˈmæt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌpar.əɡ.dɪˈmat.ɪk / (BrE)
serving as a typical example or pattern; relating to a model or paradigm.
serving as a typical example

Examples

Her research offers a paradigmatic analysis of social change.
The case became paradigmatic of successful business transformation.
His career trajectory is often cited as paradigmatic among young entrepreneurs.

Synset Examples

paradigmatic learning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exemplary - serving as a desirable model; representing the best of its kind
  • Antonym: atypical - not representative of a type, group, or class

Etymology

From Late Latin paradigmaticus, from Greek paradeigma (model, pattern).
