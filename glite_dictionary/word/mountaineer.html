mountaineer

1. VERB

/ ˌmaʊn.tn̩ˈɪr / (AmE), / ˌmaʊn.tɪˈnɪə / (BrE)
to climb mountains, especially as a hobby or sport.
to climb mountains for sport

Examples

She loves to mountaineer in the Alps every summer holiday.
Many adventurers travel to Nepal just to mountaineer among the world’s tallest peaks.
They mountaineered across the rugged terrain for several days, facing challenging climbs and unpredictable weather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: climb - to go up or ascend something

Etymology

From mountain + -eer, modeled after pioneer, engineer, etc.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmaʊn.təˈnɪr / (AmE), / ˌmaʊn.tɪˈnɪə / (BrE)
a person who climbs mountains, especially as a sport
someone who climbs mountains

Examples

The experienced mountaineer reached the summit just as the sun was rising.
As a young mountaineer, she dreamed of climbing the world’s highest peaks.
Several mountaineers set out to scale the challenging north face of the mountain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: climber - a person who climbs, especially mountains

Etymology

From mountain + -eer (as in engineer, pioneer), modelled on French 'montagnard'.

Synset POS: n
