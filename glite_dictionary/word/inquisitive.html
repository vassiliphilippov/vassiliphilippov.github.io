inquisitive

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkwɪz.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈkwɪz.ɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
having a curious or inquiring nature; eager for knowledge or information, sometimes to an excessive or nosy degree
curious; keen to inquire or learn

Examples

The child's inquisitive nature led her to ask countless questions during the museum tour.
He gave me an inquisitive glance as I entered the room.
Her inquisitive mind makes her an excellent scientist.

Synset Examples

the police are proverbially inquisitive
an inquisitive look

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curious - eager to learn or know something

Etymology

From Late Latin inquisitivus (searching; seeking to know), from Latin inquirere (to seek)

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkwɪzətɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈkwɪzɪtɪv / (BrE)
having or showing a strong desire to learn or know more about something; curious, often to the point of being nosy
curious; eager to know

Examples

The inquisitive children kept asking questions about how the machine worked.
She gave him an inquisitive look when he arrived late to the meeting.
An inquisitive mind can lead to many exciting discoveries.

Synset Examples

if someone saw a man climbing a light post they might get inquisitive
raised a speculative eyebrow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curious - eager to know or learn something
  • Antonym: indifferent - lacking interest or curiosity

Etymology

From Late Latin inquisitivus, from Latin inquirere (to inquire).
