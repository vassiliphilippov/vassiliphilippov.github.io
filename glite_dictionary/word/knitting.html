knitting

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪt̬ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
The craft or process of making fabric by interlocking loops of yarn with needles or by machine.
making fabric by interlocking yarn loops

Examples

She spent her evenings knitting scarves for her family and friends.
The art of knitting has been passed down for generations in her family.
Many people find knitting relaxing and enjoyable during the winter months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crochet - Needlework made by looping yarn with a hooked needle

Etymology

From the verb 'knit', from Old English *cnyttan* (to tie, bind).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
The activity or process of making clothing or fabric by interlocking loops of yarn with needles.
craft of making fabric with yarn and needles

Examples

My grandmother taught me knitting when I was a child.
She enjoys knitting sweaters during the winter months.
The knitting club meets every Thursday evening at the community center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crocheting - the craft of making fabric from yarn using a hooked needle
  • Synonym: weaving - the craft of making fabric by interlacing threads

Etymology

From the verb 'knit', from Old English *cnyttan* (to tie in a knot).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00911169-n
Synset POS: n
