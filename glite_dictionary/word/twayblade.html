twayblade

1. NOUN

/ ˈtweɪˌbleɪd / (AmE), / ˈtweɪˌbleɪd / (BrE)
A wild orchid with two broad oval leaves and a long stem of small greenish flowers, found in Europe and central Asia.
a type of wild orchid with two broad leaves

Examples

We spotted a rare twayblade blooming in the shaded woodland clearing.
The twayblade is easily identified by its two broad leaves near the base of the stem.
During our hike, several twayblades lined the path, their greenish flowers barely noticeable among the grass.

Etymology

From Middle English 'twey' (two) + 'blade' (leaf), referring to its pair of leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtweɪˌbleɪd / (AmE), / ˈtweɪbleɪd / (BrE)
A small orchid of the genus Liparis, characterized by having a pair of broad leaves and usually greenish flowers.
a type of orchid with two leaves

Examples

The twayblade can be found in damp woods and meadows across northern Europe.
Botanists were excited to discover a rare twayblade growing in the nature reserve.
The distinctive feature of the twayblade is its two broad, opposite leaves.

Etymology

From Middle English 'tway' (two) + 'blade' (leaf), referring to its pair of leaves.
