guerdon

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɝːdən / (AmE), / ˈɡɜːdən / (BrE)
a reward or payment, especially one given for a service or achievement
a reward or payment

Examples

The hero received a generous guerdon for his bravery in battle.
In medieval times, knights sought guerdon from their lords for loyal service.
The artist’s hard work was finally acknowledged with a fitting guerdon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reward - a thing given in recognition of service, effort, or achievement
  • Synonym: recompense - compensation or reward given for loss or effort

Etymology

From Old French *guerdon*, from Medieval Latin *wardō*, from Frankish *wardōn* (reward).
