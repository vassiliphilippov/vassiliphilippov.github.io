bivouacking

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪvuˌækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɪvuːækɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of setting up and living in a temporary camp, typically in tents or under the open sky
setting up and living in a temporary camp

Examples

The scouts enjoyed bivouacking under the stars during their summer trip.
Military units often practice bivouacking in harsh weather to prepare for field operations.
He developed a love for nature after several weekends of bivouacking in the mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camping - the activity of spending a holiday in a tent or camper

Etymology

From French 'bivouac', likely from Swiss German 'Biwuacht' (night guard).
