nerveless

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɜrvləs / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvləs / (BrE)
lacking strength, energy, or firmness; weak or feeble.
lacking strength or energy

Examples

After the illness, his nerveless arms could barely lift the tray.
Her nerveless handshake revealed just how exhausted she felt.
The team became nerveless and failed to respond to their coach's encouragement.

Synset Examples

a weak, nerveless fool, devoid of energy and promptitude

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weak - lacking physical strength or energy
  • Antonym: strong - having physical power or strength

Etymology

From 'nerve' + '-less', formed in English.

2. ADJ

/ ˈnɜrv.ləs / (AmE), / ˈnɜːv.ləs / (BrE)
calm and self-controlled, especially in a difficult or trying situation; not easily agitated or emotional
calm and self-controlled under pressure

Examples

The pilot remained nerveless during the emergency landing and inspired confidence in everyone on board.
Her nerveless performance in the championship match impressed the judges and spectators alike.
He approached the tense negotiation with a nerveless calm that surprised his colleagues.

Synset Examples

play it cool
keep cool
stayed coolheaded in the crisis
the most nerveless winner in the history of the tournament

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coolheaded - not easily excited or upset; calm in a crisis
  • Synonym: unflappable - not easily perturbed or upset; calm under pressure
  • Antonym: nervous - easily agitated or anxious

Etymology

Formed from 'nerve' + '-less' meaning 'without nerves,' originally referring to literal lack of physical nerve (from 17th century), later extending to composure.
