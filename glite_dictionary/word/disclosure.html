disclosure

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈkloʊʒɚ / (AmE), / dɪsˈkləʊʒə / (BrE)
the act of making new or secret information known to others
the act of revealing information

Examples

The company faced criticism after the disclosure of its financial difficulties.
Her sudden disclosure of the secret surprised everyone at the meeting.
Legal regulations require the disclosure of potential conflicts of interest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revelation - the act of revealing or making known something hidden
  • Antonym: concealment - the action of keeping something secret or hidden

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French *disclosure*, from Latin *disclosus*, past participle of *disclodere* (to unclose).
