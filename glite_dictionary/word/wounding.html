wounding

1. ADJ

/ ˈwun.dɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwuːn.dɪŋ / (BrE)
causing pain or injury, especially emotional or psychological harm
causing emotional or psychological injury

Examples

His wounding comments left her feeling deeply hurt.
She avoided making any wounding remarks during the discussion.
The article made several wounding accusations that damaged his reputation.

Synset Examples

a stabbing remark
wounding and false charges of disloyalty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hurtful - causing emotional pain
  • Synonym: cutting - intended to hurt someone's feelings
  • Antonym: soothing - having a calming or comforting effect

Etymology

Present participle of 'wound'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwuːndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwuːndɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of causing an injury that cuts or breaks the skin or body; the process of inflicting a wound.
the act of inflicting a wound

Examples

The deliberate wounding of another person is a serious crime.
Medical staff treated several cases of gunshot wounding after the accident.
His words left a wounding effect on her confidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: injury - physical harm or damage to the body
  • Antonym: healing - the process of becoming healthy again

Etymology

From verb 'wound' (to injure) + -ing. 'Wound' from Old English 'wund'.
