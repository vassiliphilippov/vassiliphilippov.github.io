funky

1. ADJ

/ ˈfʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋki / (BrE)
stylish and modern in an unconventional or striking way, often with a sense of originality or retro appeal
stylish in an unconventional way

Examples

Her apartment is decorated with funky lamps and colorful artwork.
He always wears funky glasses that make him stand out in a crowd.
The new café has a funky interior design that attracts lots of young people.

Synset Examples

she likes funky clothes
we did it all in black and white in a very funky sixties style

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trendy - very fashionable or up-to-date
  • Synonym: quirky - characterized by peculiar or unexpected traits

Etymology

Originally from 'funk', African-American slang for strong smell or style; sense developed in the 20th century to mean 'stylish and unconventional'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋki / (BrE)
(of jazz music) having a distinctive soulful or bluesy feeling, often with strong rhythms and improvisation.
soulful and rhythmic (jazz)

Examples

The band played a funky jazz set that energized the crowd.
Her saxophone solo had a funky groove reminiscent of old-school blues.
He loves listening to funky jazz records from the 1960s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluesy - resembling or suggestive of blues music
  • Synonym: soulful - expressing deep emotion, especially in music

Etymology

From 'funk', an African-American English term describing a strong smell or earthy quality, later used for a style of music.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00858345-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈfʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋki / (BrE)
Having an unpleasant or offensive smell.
having an unpleasant odor

Examples

The old refrigerator had a funky odor that made us cringe every time we opened it.
Something in the trash is starting to smell really funky—we should take it out.
After days without cleaning, the locker room got a funky smell that was hard to ignore.

Synset Examples

a foul odor
the kitchen smelled really funky

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foul - having a very unpleasant smell or taste
  • Synonym: malodorous - smelling very unpleasant
