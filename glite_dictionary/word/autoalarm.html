autoalarm

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːt̬oʊəˈlɑːrm / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊəˈlɑːm / (BrE)
A radio receiver installed on ships that automatically sounds an alarm when a distress signal is received.
automatic ship distress signal alarm

Examples

The autoalarm was activated when the ship received an SOS signal.
Every vessel must ensure its autoalarm is operational before setting sail.
During the drill, the crew responded quickly to the sound of the autoalarm.

Etymology

From 'auto-' (automatic) + 'alarm'.
