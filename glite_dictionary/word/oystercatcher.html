oystercatcher

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪstərˌkætʃər / (AmE), / ˈɔɪstəˌkætʃə / (BrE)
a black-and-white wading bird with a long, stout orange bill, known for feeding on shellfish such as oysters; found along coasts and estuaries
black-and-white shorebird with long bill

Examples

We watched an oystercatcher probing the sand for shellfish at low tide.
The bright orange bill of the oystercatcher makes it easy to spot along rocky shores.
Several oystercatchers were nesting on the small island near the harbor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shorebird - a bird that lives along shorelines and feeds in shallow water or mud

Etymology

Named for its habit of catching and eating oysters.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪstərˌkætʃər / (AmE), / ˈɔɪstəˌkætʃə / (BrE)
a large, black-and-white shorebird with a long, stout orange bill, known for feeding on shellfish such as oysters
a black-and-white shorebird with a strong bill

Examples

The oystercatcher used its powerful beak to pry open a clam on the shore.
We spotted an oystercatcher among the rocks during our walk along the beach.
Flocks of oystercatchers can often be seen feeding at low tide in coastal areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shorebird - a bird that lives along the seashore

Etymology

Named for its habit of catching and eating oysters; 'oyster' + 'catcher'; first recorded in the 18th century.
