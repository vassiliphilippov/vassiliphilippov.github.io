earthborn

1. ADJ

/ ˈɜrθˌbɔrn / (AmE), / ˈɜːθ.bɔːn / (BrE)
originating on Earth or relating to earthly existence; not celestial or supernatural
of earthly origin or earthly life

Examples

The poet contrasted earthborn desires with spiritual aspirations.
She sought peace amid the earthborn troubles that plagued her daily life.
Some ancient myths describe earthborn creatures rising from the soil.

Synset Examples

earthborn cares and pleasures

Related Terms

  • Antonym: celestial - relating to the sky or heavens; heavenly
  • Synonym: earthly - relating to or characteristic of the earth or human life

Etymology

From Old English 'eorþ' (earth) + 'born' (past participle of 'bear').

having origin on the earth; of this world, as opposed to divine or supernatural
of earthly origin; not divine

Examples

Philosophers have long questioned the purpose of our earthborn existence.
She regarded her earthborn troubles as insignificant compared to the mysteries of the stars.
In the poem, the hero leaves the earthborn life behind in search of the heavens.

3. ADJ

/ ˈɝːθˌbɔrn / (AmE), / ˈɜːθˌbɔːn / (BrE)
originating from or born on the earth, especially as opposed to being divine or extraterrestrial
born or originating from the earth

Examples

Many ancient myths feature earthborn giants as the offspring of the land itself.
The poet described humanity as an earthborn race destined for greatness.
Some science fiction stories contrast alien visitors with earthborn life forms in their struggle for survival.

Synset Examples

earthborn beings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthly - relating to the earth or material existence, not spiritual or heavenly

Etymology

Compound of 'earth' + 'born', modeled after similar English forms like 'newborn' or 'starborn'.
