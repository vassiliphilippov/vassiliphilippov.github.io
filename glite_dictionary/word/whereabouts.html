whereabouts

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛr.əˌbaʊts / (AmE), / ˈweə.rəˌbaʊts / (BrE)
the place or area where someone or something is, usually when the exact location is not known
general location of someone or something

Examples

The police are trying to determine the suspect's whereabouts.
Her whereabouts remain a mystery after she left the city.
No one knows the exact whereabouts of the missing documents.

Synset Examples

I questioned him about his whereabouts on the night of the crime

Etymology

From Old English 'hwǣr' (where) + 'abouts', modelled after 'thereabouts'.
