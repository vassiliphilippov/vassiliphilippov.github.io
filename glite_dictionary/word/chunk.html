chunk

1. VERB

/ tʃʌŋk / (AmE), / tʃʌŋk / (BrE)
to group items together without distinguishing or sorting them; to lump together indiscriminately.
group together indiscriminately

Examples

The teacher decided to chunk all late assignments into one folder.
Analysts often chunk similar data points instead of examining them separately.
It's unhelpful to chunk every complaint as the same issue.

Synset Examples

lump together all the applicants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lump together - group things into a single undifferentiated category

Etymology

Probably from English dialect chunk (lump), origin unknown.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01388574-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ tʃʌŋk / (AmE), / tʃʌŋk / (BrE)
a thick, solid piece or lump of something, especially one that has been cut or broken off from a larger mass
thick or solid piece or lump

Examples

She dropped a chunk of ice into her glass.
A large chunk of bread was enough to satisfy his hunger.
He broke off a chunk of chocolate and handed it to me.

Synset Examples

a ball of mud caught him on the shoulder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lump - a piece or mass of solid material without a definite shape
  • Antonym: sliver - a thin, small, narrow piece cut or split off

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly from an alteration of 'chock'.

Source: WordNet

4. VERB

/ tʃʌŋk / (AmE), / tʃʌŋk / (BrE)
To group or organize items together into larger, manageable units or sections, often for ease of processing or understanding.
to group items together

Examples

The software can chunk similar files together to make searching easier.
Teachers often chunk information so that students can remember it more easily.
You should chunk your work into smaller pieces to avoid feeling overwhelmed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: group - to put or come together into a group
