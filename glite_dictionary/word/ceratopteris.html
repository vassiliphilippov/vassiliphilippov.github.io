Ceratopteris

1. NOUN

/ sɛrəˈtɑptərɪs / (AmE), / sɛrəˈtɒptərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic ferns found in tropical and subtropical regions, often grown in aquariums and wetlands.
genus of tropical aquatic ferns

Examples

The aquarium was filled with lush Ceratopteris providing shelter for the fish.
Researchers study the development of Ceratopteris as a model for plant biology.
Many aquatic gardeners choose Ceratopteris for its fast growth and decorative leaves.

Etymology

From Greek 'keras' (horn) and 'pteris' (fern), referring to the horn-shaped features of the plants.

2. NOUN

/ sɛrəˈtɑptərɪs / (AmE), / sɛrəˈtɒptərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of pan-tropical aquatic ferns, commonly found in still or slow-moving waters.
genus of aquatic tropical ferns

Examples

Ceratopteris is often used in aquariums for its rapid growth and ability to absorb excess nutrients.
The water ferns of the genus Ceratopteris thrive in warm, tropical climates.
Researchers study Ceratopteris as a model organism in plant developmental biology.

Etymology

From Greek keras (horn) + pteris (fern), referring to the horn-shaped features of the plant.
