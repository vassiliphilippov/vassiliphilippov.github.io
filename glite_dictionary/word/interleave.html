to interleave

1. VERB

/ ɪntɚˈliv / (AmE), / ɪntəˈliːv / (BrE)
(of computer data storage) to arrange sectors on a disk so that data can be read or written more efficiently, by separating consecutive sectors with intervening ones.
arrange sectors on a disk for efficient access

Examples

Many older floppy disks interleave data to improve read/write performance.
Modern hard drives may use different methods and no longer need to interleave sectors manually.
The technician explained how to interleave tracks for optimal disk access speed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alternate - to arrange by turns or in rotation, especially in connection with ordering or sequence

Etymology

From inter- (between) + leave (to insert), originally used of weaving or placing things alternately.

2. VERB

/ ˌɪntərˈliv / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈliːv / (BrE)
to insert blank leaves (pages) between the pages of a book
insert blank pages in a book

Examples

The printer was asked to interleave the manuscript with blank sheets for notes.
Archivists sometimes interleave rare books with special paper to protect the pages.
She interleaved each chapter with a blank leaf so readers could write their comments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insert - to put something between other things or parts

Etymology

From inter- (between) + leaf (page of a book).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02340258-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌɪntɚˈliv / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈliːv / (BrE)
to insert something, especially pages or sheets, between the pages of a book or the parts of something else; to alternate layers or elements.
insert sheets or material between others

Examples

The archivist carefully interleaved sheets of tissue paper between the fragile illustrations.
Glossy pages are often interleaved with protective covers in high-quality art books.
Some collectors prefer to interleave their stamp albums with blank pages to prevent damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to interpose - to place something between other things

Etymology

From French 'interlever', based on Latin 'inter-' (between) + 'leaves' (pages).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00189696-v
Synset POS: v
