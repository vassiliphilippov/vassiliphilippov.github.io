logographic

1. ADJ

/ ˌloʊɡəˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌlɒɡəˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to logograms, in which written characters represent words or morphemes rather than sounds
relating to logograms or logographs

Examples

Chinese script is a logographic writing system, meaning each symbol represents a word or morpheme.
The company's logo incorporates logographic elements to convey meaning visually.
Unlike an alphabetic system, a logographic system relies on characters that stand for entire concepts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: phonetic - relating to speech sounds, contrasted with logographic systems

Etymology

From Greek logos (word) + -graphic (pertaining to writing).
