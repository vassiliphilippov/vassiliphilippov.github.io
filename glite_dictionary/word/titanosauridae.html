Titanosauridae

1. NOUN

/ taɪˌtænoʊˈsɔːrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / taɪˌtænəʊˈsɔːrɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of large herbivorous dinosaurs from the Cretaceous period, characterized by long necks and tails, and recognized for their massive size
family of large Cretaceous herbivorous dinosaurs

Examples

The Titanosauridae included some of the largest land animals to ever exist.
Scientists discovered new fossils belonging to the Titanosauridae in South America.
Members of the Titanosauridae were known for their enormous size and long necks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dinosaur - extinct group of reptiles from the Mesozoic era

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek 'Titan' (a giant deity) and 'sauros' (lizard).

2. NOUN

/ taɪˌtænəˈsɔrɪˌdi / (AmE), / taɪˌtænəˈsɔːrɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of large, herbivorous dinosaurs from the Cretaceous period, known for their enormous size and widespread distribution
family of giant Cretaceous herbivorous dinosaurs

Examples

Paleontologists discovered new fossils from the Titanosauridae family in South America.
The Titanosauridae included some of the largest animals to ever walk the Earth.
Children are fascinated by the gigantic Titanosauridae when learning about dinosaurs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sauropod - large herbivorous long-necked dinosaurs

Etymology

From Greek *Titan* (giant deity) + *sauros* (lizard) + scientific family suffix -idae.
