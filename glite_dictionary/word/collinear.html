collinear

1. ADJ

/ koʊˈlɪniər / (AmE), / kəʊˈlɪniə / (BrE)
(of points, lines, etc.) lying on the same straight line.
lying on the same straight line

Examples

The three cities are nearly collinear, making travel between them straightforward.
If points A, B, and C are collinear, they all lie along a single straight path.
In geometry, a set of collinear points means they form no angle between them.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: noncollinear - not lying on the same straight line

Etymology

From Latin 'com-' (together) + 'linea' (line).
