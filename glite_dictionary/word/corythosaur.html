corythosaur

1. NOUN

/ kɔːˈrɪθəˌsɔr / (AmE), / kɒˈrɪθəˌsɔː / (BrE)
a large, duck-billed dinosaur of the genus Corythosaurus, characterized by a crest on its head formed by expanded nasal passages
duck-billed dinosaur with a crested head

Examples

The museum's new exhibit featured a life-sized model of a corythosaur in a prehistoric forest.
Scientists believe the crest of the corythosaur may have been used to produce distinctive sounds.
Fossilized remains of a corythosaur were discovered in Canada, providing new insight into its habitat.

Etymology

From genus name Corythosaurus, from Greek 'korythos' (helmet) and 'sauros' (lizard).
