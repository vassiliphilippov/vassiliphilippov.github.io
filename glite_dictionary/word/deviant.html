deviant

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːviənt / (AmE), / ˈdiːvɪənt / (BrE)
a person whose behavior or morals differ markedly from what is considered normal or acceptable, especially in a sexual context
person whose behavior is abnormal or unacceptable

Examples

Some people labeled him a deviant simply because of his unconventional lifestyle.
The novel explores the mind of a social deviant ostracized by the community.
She argued that not every deviant is harmful to society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pervert - a person whose sexual behavior is considered abnormal and unacceptable
  • Antonym: conformist - a person who behaves in accordance with accepted standards

Etymology

From Latin deviāns, deviānt- (turning away).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdiː.vi.ənt / (AmE), / ˈdiː.vi.ənt / (BrE)
departing markedly from accepted standards, especially in social or moral behavior
markedly different from the norm

Examples

His deviant behavior eventually led him into conflict with the law.
The artist was known for her deviant views on society and culture.
Teachers are trained to recognize deviant conduct in the classroom.

Synset Examples

aberrant behavior
deviant ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aberrant - departing from an accepted standard
  • Antonym: normal - conforming to a standard; usual, typical, or expected
  • Antonym: conventional - based on or in accordance with what is generally done or believed

Etymology

From Latin 'deviare' meaning 'to turn aside' (de- 'away from' + via 'way').

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
