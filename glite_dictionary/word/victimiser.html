victimiser

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪktɪˌmaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈvɪktɪˌmaɪzə / (BrE)
A person who treats someone cruelly or unfairly, especially by making them a victim.
person who victimizes others

Examples

The bully was identified as the main victimiser in the group.
Laws are in place to protect people from any potential victimiser.
In any abusive relationship, it's important to distinguish between the victimiser and the victim.

Synset Examples

I thought we were partners, not victim and victimizer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: victimizer - a person who victimizes others (American spelling)
  • Antonym: victim - a person who is harmed or suffers as a result of actions from others

Etymology

Formed from 'victimize' (from 'victim') + '-er', denoting an agent noun.
