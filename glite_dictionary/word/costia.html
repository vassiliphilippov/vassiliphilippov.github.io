costia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɒstiə / (AmE), / ˈkɒstɪə / (BrE)
a genus of tiny flagellate protozoans that are parasites of fish, causing the disease costiasis
parasitic flagellate causing fish disease
medical

Examples

The veterinarian identified costia as the cause of the outbreak in the aquarium.
Many fish suffered skin damage due to a severe costia infection.
Proper quarantine procedures can prevent the spread of costia in new tanks.

Etymology

From modern Latin, from Russian 'kostya' (bone), possibly referring to its shape.
