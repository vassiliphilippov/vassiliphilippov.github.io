synovium

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈnoʊviəm / (AmE), / sɪˈnəʊviəm / (BrE)
A thin membrane that lines the joint capsule of synovial (freely moving) joints and secretes synovial fluid.
membrane lining synovial joints
medical

Examples

The synovium plays a crucial role in lubricating your joints and providing nutrients to cartilage.
Inflammation of the synovium is a hallmark of conditions such as rheumatoid arthritis.
Damage to the synovium can result in pain, swelling, and reduced mobility in the affected joint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synovial membrane - membrane lining synovial joints and secreting synovial fluid

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'syn-' (together) + Latin 'ovum' (egg), referring to the egg-white-like appearance of synovial fluid.
