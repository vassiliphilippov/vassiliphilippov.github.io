Citrus grandis

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtrəs ˈɡrændɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɪtrəs ˈɡrændɪs / (BrE)
A tree native to Southeast Asia that produces large, thick-skinned citrus fruits resembling grapefruits, commonly known as the pomelo.
tree producing large citrus fruit; pomelo

Examples

The Citrus grandis in her backyard produced dozens of enormous fruits last season.
Researchers studied the Citrus grandis tree to better understand its resistance to certain diseases.
Traditional Asian markets often feature the large, juicy fruits of Citrus grandis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pomelo - the large citrus fruit produced by Citrus grandis

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining 'Citrus' (the genus name) with Latin 'grandis' (large).
