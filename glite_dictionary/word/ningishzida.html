Ningishzida

1. PROPN

/ nɪŋˌɡɪʃˈziːdə / (AmE), / nɪŋˌɡɪʃˈziːdə / (BrE)
In Babylonian and Sumerian mythology, an underworld deity regarded as a patron of medicine.
Babylonian underworld deity; medicine patron

Examples

Ancient texts often depict Ningishzida as a guardian of the underworld.
Scholars believe Ningishzida played an important role in Babylonian medicine.
The symbol of Ningishzida is frequently found on ancient Mesopotamian artifacts.

Etymology

From Sumerian *Nin-g̃iš-zid-da* ('Lord of the Good Tree').
