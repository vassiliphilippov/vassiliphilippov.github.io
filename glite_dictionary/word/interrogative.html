interrogative

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.təˈrɑː.ɡə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈrɒ.ɡə.tɪv / (BrE)
(in grammar) a word, form, or sentence that asks a question; relating to or expressing a question.
a word or sentence that asks a question

Examples

The teacher wrote an interrogative on the board to start the discussion.
Words like 'who' and 'what' are examples of interrogatives in English.
Each interrogative sentence in the worksheet requires an answer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: question - a sentence worded to elicit information; request for information

Etymology

From Late Latin interrogativus, from interrogare (to ask a question).

2. ADJ

/ ɪn.təˈrɑː.ɡə.tɪv / (AmE), / ɪn.təˈrɒ.ɡə.tɪv / (BrE)
having the form or function of a question; relating to or used in asking questions.
relating to questions or inquiry

Examples

The teacher's interrogative tone made the students feel nervous during the test.
Some sentences are interrogative, ending with a question mark.
She raised her eyebrow in an interrogative manner, waiting for an explanation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: declarative - relating to statements rather than questions
  • Antonym: imperative - relating to commands or requests

Etymology

From Latin interrogativus, from interrogare (to ask, question).

expressing or relating to a question (grammar)
