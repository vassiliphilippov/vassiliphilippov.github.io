gruffness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrʌfnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌfnəs / (BrE)
A quality of being rough, harsh, or unfriendly, especially in voice or manner.
rough or harsh quality, esp. in voice

Examples

His gruffness hid a kind and caring nature that only close friends saw.
The gruffness in his voice made him sound intimidating at first.
Despite her gruffness, she always helped those in need.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harshness - the quality of being unpleasantly rough or jarring
  • Antonym: gentleness - the quality of being gentle and mild

Etymology

From 'gruff' + '-ness'; 'gruff' possibly imitative of a rough sound.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡrʌfnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌfnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being abrupt or unfriendly in manner or speech
abrupt or unfriendly manner

Examples

His gruffness sometimes hid a genuinely caring nature.
People were surprised by the gruffness in her response to a simple question.
Despite his gruffness, he quickly gained the team's respect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abruptness - the quality of being sudden or curt in manner
  • Antonym: politeness - the quality of being respectful and considerate

Etymology

From 'gruff' + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting a state or quality).
