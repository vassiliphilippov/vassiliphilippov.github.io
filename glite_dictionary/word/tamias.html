Tamias

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪ.mi.əs / (AmE), / ˈtam.i.as / (BrE)
A genus of chipmunks native to eastern North America.
genus of eastern North American chipmunks

Examples

The eastern chipmunk belongs to the genus Tamias and is common in wooded areas.
Researchers observed several Tamias species storing food for the winter.
The identification guide listed Tamias as the sole native chipmunk genus in eastern North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chipmunk - a small, striped rodent related to squirrels

Etymology

From Greek *tamias* (steward, housekeeper), referring to the animal's food-hoarding behavior.
