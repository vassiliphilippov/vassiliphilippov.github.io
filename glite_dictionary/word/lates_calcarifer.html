Lates calcarifer

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪtiːz ˌkælkəˈrɪfər / (AmE), / ˈleɪtiːz ˌkalkəˈrɪfə / (BrE)
A species of large perch found in marine, estuary, and freshwater habitats, valued for its sporting and eating qualities; commonly known as barramundi.
species of large perch (barramundi)

Examples

The chef prepared grilled Lates calcarifer with a lemon butter sauce for the guests.
Anglers travel to northern Australia to catch trophy-sized Lates calcarifer during the spawning season.
Because Lates calcarifer thrives in both saltwater and freshwater, it is farmed in diverse environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barramundi - the common name for Lates calcarifer, a large edible fish

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Lates' and species epithet 'calcarifer' meaning 'bearing a spur'.
