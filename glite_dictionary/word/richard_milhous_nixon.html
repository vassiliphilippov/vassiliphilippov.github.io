Richard Milhous Nixon

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɪtʃərd ˈmɪlhaʊs ˈnɪksən / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃəd ˈmɪlhaʊs ˈnɪksən / (BrE)
(1913–1994) The 37th President of the United States (1969–1974), who resigned following the Watergate scandal; earlier served as vice president under Eisenhower.
37th U.S. President, resigned after Watergate

Examples

Richard Milhous Nixon resigned from office following the Watergate scandal in 1974.
Many historians analyze the policies implemented during the Richard Milhous Nixon administration.
The presidency of Richard Milhous Nixon remains one of the most controversial in American history.
