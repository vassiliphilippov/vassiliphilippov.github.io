First Baron Rutherford of Nelson

1. PROPN

/ fɜrst ˈbɛɹən ˈrʌðəɹfəld əv ˈnɛlsən / (AmE), / fɜːst ˈbærən ˈrʌðəfəd əv ˈnɛlsən / (BrE)
the formal title of Ernest Rutherford (1871–1937), a British physicist from New Zealand who discovered the atomic nucleus and proposed the nuclear model of the atom
title of Ernest Rutherford, British physicist

Examples

First Baron Rutherford of Nelson made significant contributions to our understanding of atomic structure.
The title First Baron Rutherford of Nelson was awarded in recognition of Rutherford's groundbreaking work in physics.
Many consider First Baron Rutherford of Nelson to be the father of nuclear physics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ernest Rutherford - British physicist who discovered the atomic nucleus

Etymology

Title conferred upon Ernest Rutherford in recognition of his scientific achievements; 'Baron Rutherford of Nelson' refers to his birthplace, Nelson, New Zealand.
