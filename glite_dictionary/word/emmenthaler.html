Emmenthaler

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛmənˌtɑlər / (AmE), / ˈɛmənˌtɑːlə / (BrE)
A type of Swiss cheese with a firm texture and characteristic large holes, originating from the Emmental region of Switzerland.
Swiss cheese with large holes

Examples

She ordered a sandwich with slices of Emmenthaler and ham.
The chef recommended using Emmenthaler in the fondue for its nutty flavor.
At the market, we found imported Emmenthaler alongside other European cheeses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Swiss cheese - A generic term for cheeses originating from Switzerland with holes (eyes)

Etymology

From German 'Emmentaler', meaning 'from Emmental' (a valley in Switzerland).
