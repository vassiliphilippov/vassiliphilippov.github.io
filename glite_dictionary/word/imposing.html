imposing

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpoʊzɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪmˈpəʊzɪŋ / (BrE)
having an impressive or dignified appearance or manner that attracts attention and respect
having a grand or impressive appearance

Examples

The castle's imposing walls could be seen from miles away.
She had an imposing presence that quieted everyone in the room.
His imposing appearance made him stand out at the conference.

Synset Examples

his distinguished bearing
the monarch's imposing presence
she reigned in magisterial beauty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impressive - evoking admiration through size, quality, or skill
  • Antonym: unimposing - not impressive or striking in appearance

Etymology

From French *imposant*, from Latin *imponere* 'to place upon'.

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpoʊzɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪmˈpəʊzɪŋ / (BrE)
impressive in size, appearance, or manner, and likely to attract attention or respect
grand or impressive in appearance

Examples

The imposing cathedral dominated the city skyline for centuries.
She wore an imposing hat to the ceremony, drawing everyone's attention.
Tall and imposing, the judge entered the courtroom with authority.

Synset Examples

a baronial mansion
an imposing residence
a noble tree
severe-looking policemen sat astride noble horses
stately columns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grand - magnificent or impressive in appearance or style
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance or low in condition, rank, or position

Etymology

From French *imposant*, present participle of *imposer* (to impose), from Latin *imponere* (to place upon).
