octoroon

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑktəˈruːn / (AmE), / ˌɒktəˈruːn / (BrE)
(offensive, archaic) A person with one-eighth Black ancestry, specifically the offspring of a quadroon and a white parent.
person with one-eighth Black ancestry (offensive, archaic)
offensive archaic

Examples

The old census records used the term octoroon to categorize people of mixed heritage.
She read about an octoroon in a nineteenth-century novel, noting the period's attitudes toward race.
The play depicted the struggles faced by an octoroon in antebellum America.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: quadroon - a person who is one-quarter Black by descent

Etymology

Formed from Latin 'octo-' (eight) + '-roon' from 'quadroon'.
