Zinnia acerosa

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɪniə əˈsɛɹosə / (AmE), / ˈzɪniə əˈsɛɹosə / (BrE)
A species of zinnia, a low-growing subshrub with slender woolly stems, long narrow leaves, and flower heads with white rays, native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.
species of subshrub with white flowers

Examples

Zinnia acerosa is a hardy plant commonly found in the arid landscapes of Arizona and Texas.
The long, narrow leaves of Zinnia acerosa help it conserve water in its native habitat.
Gardeners appreciate Zinnia acerosa for its ability to thrive in poor, dry soils.

Etymology

From the genus name Zinnia (after German botanist Johann Gottfried Zinn) and Latin acerosa (needle-like, referring to the leaves).
