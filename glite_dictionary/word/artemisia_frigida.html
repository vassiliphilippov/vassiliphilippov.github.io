Artemisia frigida

1. NOUN

/ ɑːr.təˈmɪʒ.i.ə frɪˈdʒaɪ.də / (AmE), / ˌɑː.tɪˈmɪz.i.ə frɪˈdʒɪ.də / (BrE)
a silky-leaved aromatic perennial plant found in dry northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere, with tawny florets; also known as fringed sagebrush
a northern aromatic perennial plant (fringed sagebrush)

Examples

Artemisia frigida thrives in dry, rocky soils across the northern prairies.
Her research focused on the medicinal properties of Artemisia frigida leaves.
The landscape was dotted with the silvery green foliage of Artemisia frigida.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Artemisia, after the Greek goddess Artemis; species epithet frigida meaning 'cold' or 'of the cold'.
