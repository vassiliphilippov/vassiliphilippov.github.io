Kennedia prostrata

1. NOUN

/ kəˈniːdiə prəˈstreɪtə / (AmE), / kəˈniːdiə prɒˈstrɑːtə / (BrE)
a trailing or prostrate vine native to western Australia, with hairy stems and bright scarlet-pink flowers
Australian trailing vine with red-pink flowers

Examples

The garden features several Kennedia prostrata plants cascading over the rock wall.
Botanists have observed Kennedia prostrata blooming throughout the Australian spring.
Due to its vivid flowers, Kennedia prostrata is popular in native landscaping projects.

Etymology

Genus name from German botanist Johann Kennedy; species name from Latin 'prostratus', meaning 'lying flat'.
