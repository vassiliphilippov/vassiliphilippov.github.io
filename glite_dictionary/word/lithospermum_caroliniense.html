Lithospermum caroliniense

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪθoʊˈspɜrməm ˌkæroʊˈlɪniːns / (AmE), / ˌlɪθəʊˈspɜːməm ˌkærəˈlɪniːɛns / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to eastern North America, with hairy leaves, that produces a red or yellow pigment.
perennial North American plant with hairy leaves

Examples

The sand dunes were dotted with clumps of Lithospermum caroliniense in full bloom.
Artists once used pigments derived from the roots of Lithospermum caroliniense for coloring.
Botanists observe that Lithospermum caroliniense thrives in dry, sandy soils.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Lithospermum' (the genus name, from Greek 'lithos' meaning 'stone' and 'sperma' meaning 'seed') and 'caroliniense' referring to the Carolinas region.
