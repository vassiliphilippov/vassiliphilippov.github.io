jounce

1. NOUN

/ dʒaʊns / (AmE), / dʒaʊns / (BrE)
a sudden jarring or jolting impact or movement
sudden jarring or jolting impact

Examples

The car hit a pothole with a loud jounce.
After each jounce, the furniture rattled in the back of the truck.
He felt a sharp jounce as the elevator suddenly stopped.

Synset Examples

the door closed with a jolt
all the jars and jolts were smoothed out by the shock absorbers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jolt - a sudden strong movement or shock

Etymology

Origin uncertain, possibly imitative or related to 'bounce.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07353698-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ dʒaʊns / (AmE), / dʒaʊns / (BrE)
to move up and down repeatedly, usually in a vigorous or jolting manner
move up and down repeatedly

Examples

The car began to jounce over the rough country road, making the passengers laugh.
As the wagon rolled along, the barrels inside would jounce against each other noisily.
Children love to jounce on trampolines for hours on end.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bounce - to spring back or move up and down after hitting a surface

Etymology

Perhaps an alteration of 'jolt' or from Middle English 'jowce', of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01896601-v
Synset POS: v
