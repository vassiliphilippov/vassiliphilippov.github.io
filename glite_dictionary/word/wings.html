wings

1. NOUN

/ wɪŋz / (AmE), / wɪŋz / (BrE)
a badge in the form of stylized bird wings, worn as an insignia by qualified pilots or air crew members
pilot or air crew insignia badge

Examples

After passing his final exam, he proudly pinned his wings to his uniform.
The ceremony honored new graduates by awarding them their wings.
Pilots must earn their wings through rigorous training and dedication.

2. NOUN

/ wɪŋz / (AmE), / wɪŋz / (BrE)
A means of flight or ascent, especially used metaphorically to refer to something that enables progress, rise, or inspiration.
means of flight or ascent (figurative)

Examples

Necessity often lends wings to human creativity.
The news gave her wings, filling her with renewed hope.
Ambition lends wings to those who pursue their dreams.

Synset Examples

necessity lends wings to inspiration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wings - flat limbs for flying (anatomy)
  • Synonym: support - something that helps to uplift or elevate

Etymology

Plural of 'wing', from Old Norse 'vængr' (wing).
