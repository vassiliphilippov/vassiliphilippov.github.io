nucleated

1. ADJ

/ ˈnuː.kli.eɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.kli.eɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of a cell or structure) having a nucleus or occurring in the nucleus.
having a nucleus

Examples

Red blood cells in mammals are typically not nucleated.
Certain algae have highly nucleated cells for complex functions.
The scientist observed a nucleated area within the sample under the microscope.

Synset Examples

nucleated cells

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anucleate - lacking a nucleus

Etymology

From Latin 'nucleus' (kernel) + English adjectival suffix '-ated'.
