emphasised

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛmfəˌsaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈɛmfəsaɪzd / (BrE)
(especially of speech or words) pronounced or expressed with emphasis; stressed to show importance.
spoken or expressed with emphasis

Examples

The most emphasised syllable in the word is the second one.
His emphasised tone made it clear he was serious.
Teachers often use emphasised words to highlight key concepts.

Synset Examples

an emphatic word

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emphatic - showing or giving emphasis; expressing something forcibly and clearly

Etymology

From the verb 'emphasise' (British spelling of 'emphasize'), from Greek 'emphainein' (to exhibit).
