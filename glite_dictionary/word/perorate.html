to perorate

1. VERB

/ ˈpɛrəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɛərəʊreɪt / (BrE)
to conclude a speech with a formal or elaborate summary or recapitulation
conclude a speech formally

Examples

The senator began to perorate, drawing the speech to a grand and formal close.
After nearly an hour, the CEO chose to perorate with a stirring summary of the company's vision.
She would often perorate her lectures with a memorable quote to leave a lasting impression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to summarize - to give a brief statement of the main points

Etymology

From Latin 'peroratus', past participle of 'perorare' (to speak at length; to conclude a speech).

2. VERB

/ ˈpɛrəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɛrəʊˌreɪt / (BrE)
To deliver a speech or oration, especially in a lofty, formal, or grandiloquent style.
speak in a formal or grandiloquent way

Examples

The senator began to perorate at length, captivating the entire audience with his eloquence.
She tends to perorate during meetings, impressing some but exhausting others with the flourish of her language.
After an hour of discussion, he could not resist the urge to perorate on his favorite subject.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to orate - to make a formal speech
  • Degree Lower: to speak - to articulate words or talk

Etymology

From Latin perorare, meaning 'to speak to the end, deliver a set speech', from per- (through) + orare (to speak).
