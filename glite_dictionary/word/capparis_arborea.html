Capparis arborea

1. NOUN

/ kæˈpɛərɪs ɑːrˈbɔriə / (AmE), / kəˈpæːrɪs ɑːˈbɔːriə / (BrE)
a small Australian tree that bears edible fruit resembling the pomegranate
Australian tree with pomegranate-like fruit

Examples

The Capparis arborea produces fruit that is highly valued by local communities.
In northern Australia, Capparis arborea is found in dry rainforest habitats.
Many birds are attracted to the edible fruit of the Capparis arborea tree.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Capparis' (caper bush) and Latin 'arborea' (tree-like)
