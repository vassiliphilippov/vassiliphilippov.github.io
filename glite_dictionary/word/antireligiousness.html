antireligiousness

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ti.rɪˈlɪ.dʒəs.nəs / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.rɪˈlɪ.dʒəs.nəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being opposed to religion or religious beliefs.
opposition to religion

Examples

Their political ideology was marked by a strong sense of antireligiousness in response to traditional values.
The novel explores themes of antireligiousness and the consequences of rejecting spiritual beliefs.
Her outspoken antireligiousness often sparked heated debates within her community.

Synset Examples

It is true, too, that any neat division between religious and secular comedy would seem threatened by the apparent antireligiousness of writers like Erasmus and Rabelais.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irreligion - the absence of religion or indifference to religion
  • Antonym: religiosity - strong religious feeling or belief

Etymology

From 'anti-' (against) + 'religious' + '-ness' (state or condition).
