Mari

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːri / (AmE), / ˈmɑːri / (BrE)
a member of a rural Finno-Ugric people living in eastern Russia, mainly along the Volga and Kama rivers
member of a rural Finno-Ugric people in Russia

Examples

The Mari people have their own distinct language and traditions.
A group of Mari gathered to celebrate a traditional festival in the village.
Several Mari have been recognized for their contributions to Russian folk music.

Etymology

From Russian Марий (Maríy), the self-designation of the Mari people.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːri / (AmE), / ˈmɑːri / (BrE)
the Finnic language spoken primarily by the Mari people (Cheremis) in central Russia
Finnic language of the Mari people

Examples

Linguists have studied the Mari language for its unique grammatical features.
He is fluent in Mari as well as Russian and Tatar.
The Mari alphabet is based on the Cyrillic script.

Etymology

Named after the Mari people, an ethnic group indigenous to central Russia.
