Alnus maritima

1. NOUN

/ ˈælnəs məˈrɪtɪmə / (AmE), / ˈalnəs məˈrɪtɪmə / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States, known for its soft, light brown wood.
a North American shrub or small tree

Examples

The Alnus maritima thrives in moist habitats along streams in the southeastern United States.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Alnus maritima in its native range.
Carpenters sometimes use the light brown wood of Alnus maritima for small projects.

Etymology

From Latin: 'alnus' (alder) and 'maritima' (of the sea).
