theoterrorism

1. NOUN

/ θioʊˈtɛrərˌɪzəm / (AmE), / θɪəʊˈtɛrərɪzəm / (BrE)
Terrorism carried out for religious reasons or with the justification of religious beliefs.
terrorism motivated by religion

Examples

Many experts warn that theoterrorism poses unique challenges to global security.
The rise of theoterrorism has prompted renewed debate about the role of religion in politics.
Scholars study theoterrorism to understand how extremist groups use faith as justification for violence.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: terrorism - the unlawful use or threat of violence and intimidation for political aims

Etymology

From Greek theos (god) + terrorism.
