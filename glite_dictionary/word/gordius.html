Gordius

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡɔrdiəs / (AmE), / ˈɡɔːdɪəs / (BrE)
A legendary king of ancient Phrygia, reputed to have tied the Gordian knot.
legendary Phrygian king of the Gordian knot

Examples

According to legend, Gordius tied the famous knot that only Alexander the Great could unravel.
Many historians debate whether Gordius truly existed or is purely a mythological figure.
The story of Gordius and the Gordian knot has inspired countless metaphors for complex problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gordian knot - an extremely difficult or intricate problem; the knot tied by Gordius

Etymology

From Latin 'Gordius', from Ancient Greek 'Γόρδιος' (Górdios).
