telecast

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛl.iˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈtɛl.ɪˌkɑːst / (BrE)
a broadcast transmitted by television
a television broadcast

Examples

The championship game will be shown as a telecast on Saturday evening.
Millions tuned in to watch the live telecast of the royal wedding.
Yesterday's telecast attracted viewers from around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broadcast - a transmission of radio or television content

Etymology

Blend of 'television' and 'broadcast', first used in the mid-20th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈtɛl.iˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪkɑːst / (BrE)
to broadcast (a program or event) by television.
broadcast by television

Examples

The championship match will be telecast live across the country tonight.
The ceremony was telecast internationally, reaching millions of viewers.
They plan to telecast the documentary on public television next week.

Synset Examples

The Royal wedding was televised

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broadcast - to transmit (a program or some information) by radio or television

Etymology

Blend of 'television' and 'broadcast.' Originated in mid-20th century.
