reexamination

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːɪɡˌzæmɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːɪɡˌzæmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a further medical examination of a patient to assess progress or monitor results of earlier treatment
subsequent medical check-up
medical

Examples

The doctor scheduled a reexamination to determine if the medication was working as expected.
A reexamination revealed significant improvement since her last visit.
After surgery, regular reexamination is necessary to monitor the healing process.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: examination - a medical inspection or test

Etymology

From re- (again) + examination, from Latin 'examinare' (to weigh, to consider).

2. NOUN

/ ˌriˌɛɡˌzæmɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːɪɡˌzæmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
(law) The questioning of a witness by the party that called them, after the witness has been cross-examined.
questioning a witness again in court
legal

Examples

During the reexamination, the lawyer clarified the intention behind the witness's previous statement.
The judge allowed a brief reexamination to address new issues raised in cross-examination.
Reexamination provided the prosecution with an opportunity to reinforce their main arguments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cross-examination - Questioning of a witness by the opposing party after direct examination
  • Antonym: direct examination - Initial questioning of a witness by the party who called them

Etymology

From re- (again) + examination, based on the verb 'to examine.'
