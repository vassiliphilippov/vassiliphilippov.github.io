Koellia

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈɛliə / (AmE), / kəʊˈɛliə / (BrE)
A genus of North American aromatic herbs commonly known as American mountain mint.
genus of American mountain mint

Examples

Koellia is native to many regions of North America, thriving in moist meadows and prairies.
Botanists often identify Koellia by its distinctive minty aroma and square stems.
The herbalist used extracts from Koellia to prepare a tea for soothing sore throats.

Etymology

Named in honor of German botanist G.L. Koelle (1763–1845).

2. NOUN

/ koʊˈɛliə / (AmE), / kəʊˈɛliə / (BrE)
A genus of American mountain mints in the mint family, Lamiaceae, native to North America.
a genus of American mountain mints

Examples

Many species in the Koellia genus have aromatic leaves used in herbal remedies.
The botanist presented a detailed study on the distribution of Koellia across the Midwest.
Koellia can thrive in poor soils, making it valuable for native plant gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pycnanthemum - A related genus of mountain mints in North America

Etymology

Named for German botanist G.L. Koell (1745–1806), with the suffix -ia.
