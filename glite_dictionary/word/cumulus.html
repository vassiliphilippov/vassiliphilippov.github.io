cumulus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːmjələs / (AmE), / ˈkjuːmjʊləs / (BrE)
A type of white, fluffy cloud with a rounded, heaped, or piled-up appearance, often with a flat base and found at low altitudes.
a rounded, fluffy cloud type

Examples

The children lay in the grass and watched a cumulus drift slowly across the blue sky.
On summer afternoons, large cumulus clouds often signal fair weather but can develop into thunderstorms.
A single cumulus hovered above the hills, its shape changing with the wind.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: stratus - a low, flat, layered cloud
  • Degree Higher: cirrus - a high, wispy cloud

Etymology

From Latin 'cumulus' (heap, pile), referring to the piled-up shape of the clouds.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkjuːmjələs / (AmE), / ˈkjuːmjʊləs / (BrE)
of or relating to cumulus clouds, which are dense, white, fluffy clouds with a flat base and rounded outlines, typically seen in fair weather.
relating to cumulus clouds

Examples

The meteorologist described the cumulus formations drifting across the summer sky.
Satellite images revealed a pattern of cumulus cloud development over the region.
Artists often paint landscapes with cumulus clouds to create a sense of calm and clarity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stratus - relating to stratus clouds, which are low, gray, and sheet-like
  • Antonym: cirrus - relating to cirrus clouds, which are thin, wispy clouds at high altitude

Etymology

From Latin cumulus (heap, pile).
