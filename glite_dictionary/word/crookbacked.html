crookbacked

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrʊkˌbækt / (AmE), / ˈkrʊkˌbækt / (BrE)
Having a curved or hunched back, especially as a result of kyphosis.
having a curved or hunched back

Examples

The old man was crookbacked and walked slowly down the street.
Medieval literature sometimes described villains as crookbacked figures.
He grew crookbacked from years of hard labor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hunchbacked - having a humped or hunched back

Etymology

From ‘crookback’, from Middle English crokebak, literally ‘crooked back’.
