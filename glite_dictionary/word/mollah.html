Mollah

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɒlɑ / (AmE), / ˈmɒlɑː / (BrE)
a Muslim scholar or religious leader, especially one trained in Islamic law and doctrine; often the head of a mosque.
Muslim religious scholar or leader

Examples

The Mollah delivered an inspiring sermon during Friday prayers.
She approached the Mollah for guidance on a religious matter.
Several Mollahs gathered to discuss community issues at the mosque.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imam - person who leads prayers in a mosque and may be a religious leader

Etymology

From Persian and Arabic *maulā* (master, lord, protector), adapted into Turkish and other languages.
