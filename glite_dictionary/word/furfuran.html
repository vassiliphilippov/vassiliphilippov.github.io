furfuran

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɝːˌfjʊˌræn / (AmE), / ˈfɜːˌfjʊˌran / (BrE)
a colorless, toxic, flammable liquid chemical used as an intermediate in organic synthesis, notably in the production of nylon
chemical compound used in nylon production

Examples

The laboratory stored the furfuran in tightly sealed containers because of its flammability.
Furfuran derivatives are essential intermediates in several chemical manufacturing processes.
The synthesis of nylon fibers often starts with compounds like furfuran.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furan - heterocyclic organic compound related to furfuran

Etymology

From ‘furfur’, the Latin for ‘bran’, and ‘-an’ from chemical nomenclature, as it is derived from furfural.
