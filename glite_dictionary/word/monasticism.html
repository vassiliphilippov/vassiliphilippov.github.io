monasticism

1. NOUN

/ məˈnæstɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / məˈnastɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
a religious way of life characterized by asceticism, celibacy, poverty, and obedience, often lived in a community under a common rule
way of life in religious communities with ascetic practices

Examples

Many early Christian communities adopted monasticism as a path to spiritual fulfillment.
The decline of monasticism in Europe changed the cultural and religious landscape.
He wrote a thesis on the history and impact of monasticism in Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asceticism - self-discipline and abstention from indulgence, especially for religious reasons

Etymology

From Late Latin monasticismus, from Greek monastēs (monk).
