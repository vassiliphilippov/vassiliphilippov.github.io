Hatiora gaertneri

1. NOUN

/ hæˌtiːəɹə ɡɑːrtˈnɛri / (AmE), / hæˌtɪəɹə ɡɑːtˈnɛːri / (BrE)
A spring-blooming cactus native to South America, known for its oblong joints and coral-red flowers; sometimes classified in the genus Schlumbergera.
a South American spring-blooming cactus

Examples

The Hatiora gaertneri is often grown as a houseplant for its vivid spring flowers.
Gardeners prize Hatiora gaertneri for its unusual jointed stems and ease of care.
During spring, the Hatiora gaertneri produces striking coral-red blooms.

Etymology

Named after botanist Joseph Gaertner and the genus Hatiora.
