tractile

1. ADJ

/ ˈtræk.taɪl / (AmE), / ˈtræk.taɪl / (BrE)
(of a material) capable of being drawn out, especially into a wire; able to be stretched or shaped without breaking.
capable of being drawn out or stretched

Examples

Copper is a highly tractile metal, ideal for making wires.
Gold is both tractile and malleable, making it useful for intricate jewelry designs.
The tractile nature of the plastic allows it to be molded into various shapes.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ductile - capable of being drawn out into a thin wire
  • Synonym: malleable - capable of being shaped by hammering or pressure
  • Antonym: brittle - hard but likely to break easily

Etymology

From Latin tractilis (that may be drawn out), from trahere (to draw).
