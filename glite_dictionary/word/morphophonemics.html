morphophonemics

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɔːrfoʊfəˈniːmɪks / (AmE), / ˌmɔːfəfəˈniːmɪks / (BrE)
the branch of linguistics concerned with the way in which morphemes are pronounced and how their pronunciation changes in different contexts
study of changes in pronunciation of morphemes

Examples

She is conducting research in morphophonemics to understand how suffixes alter pronunciation.
The morphophonemics of English explain why 'electric' becomes 'electricity'.
A good grasp of morphophonemics is essential for analyzing language change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonology - the study of the sound system of languages
  • Synonym: morphology - the study of the structure and formation of words

Etymology

From morpho- (form) + phonemics (study of phonemes).
