coastwise

1. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊstˌwaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkəʊstˌwaɪz / (BrE)
moving along or situated close to a coast, especially relating to the transportation of goods or travel by sea near land.
along or near a coast

Examples

The ship traveled coastwise from New York to Florida, stopping at several ports.
Many regulations govern coastwise shipping within national waters.
During the storm, strong coastwise winds battered the beachfront communities.

Synset Examples

coastal shipping
coastwise winds contributed to the storm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coastal - relating to or near a coast

Etymology

From 'coast' + 'wise', formed on the pattern of direction words (like 'lengthwise').

2. ADV

/ ˈkoʊstwɑɪz / (AmE), / ˈkəʊstwʌɪz / (BrE)
by way of or along the coast; relating to travel or shipping close to the shore rather than across open sea
along or near the coast

Examples

Cargo ships often travel coastwise to avoid rough open seas.
We sailed coastwise from New York to Florida, stopping at various ports.
Many species migrate coastwise during certain times of the year.

Synset Examples

we were travelling coastwise

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inland - away from the coast or shore; toward the interior of a country

Etymology

Formed from 'coast' + 'wise' (in the manner of)
