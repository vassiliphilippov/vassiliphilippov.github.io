Amphiprion percula

1. NOUN

/ æmˌfaɪpriːˈɒn pərˈkjuːlə / (AmE), / æmˌfɪpriːˈɒn pəˈkjuːlə / (BrE)
A species of small, brightly colored orange and white anemone fish native to the western Pacific Ocean, often called the orange clownfish.
species of orange clownfish

Examples

The Amphiprion percula is well known for its bright orange body and white bands.
Scientists studied the symbiotic relationship between Amphiprion percula and sea anemones on coral reefs.
Many aquariums feature Amphiprion percula due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clownfish - a brightly colored tropical fish, typically orange with white markings

Etymology

From New Latin: genus Amphiprion from Greek amphi- (on both sides) + prion (saw), species percula - a Latinized specific epithet.
