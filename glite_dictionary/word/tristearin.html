tristearin

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈstiːərɪn / (AmE), / trɪˈstɪərɪn / (BrE)
A triglyceride consisting of three units of stearic acid attached to a glycerol molecule; a type of fat commonly found in animal and vegetable oils.
a triglyceride of stearic acid

Examples

Tristearin is frequently used in laboratory studies to investigate fat digestion and absorption.
Cocoa butter contains significant amounts of tristearin, contributing to its texture and melting point.
During saponification, tristearin is broken down into glycerol and stearic acid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stearin - a fat consisting mainly of glyceryl stearate
  • Synonym: glyceride - an ester formed from glycerol and fatty acids

Etymology

From 'tri-' (three) + 'stearin', referring to its three stearic acid components.
