waist

1. NOUN

/ weɪst / (AmE), / weɪst / (BrE)
the narrow part of a shoe connecting the heel and the wider part of the sole
narrow part of a shoe's sole

Examples

The cobbler carefully shaped the waist of the shoe for better support.
Some modern shoes feature a reinforced waist to increase durability and stability.
He noticed the waist was too narrow for his comfort.

2. NOUN

/ weɪst / (AmE), / weɪst / (BrE)
the part of the human body below the ribs and above the hips, often narrower than the parts above and below it
narrow part of the body between ribs and hips

Examples

She wore a belt around her waist, accentuating her slim figure.
The tailor measured his waist before altering the trousers.
Her sweater was too tight around the waist, making it uncomfortable to wear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torso - main part of the body excluding head and limbs

Etymology

From Middle English *weste*, from Old English *wæst* (growth, stalk), related to waist as a central part of the body.
