bathyscaph

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæθɪˌskæf / (AmE), / ˈbæθɪˌskɑːf / (BrE)
a self-propelled deep-sea submersible vessel used for underwater exploration at great depths
deep-sea submersible vessel

Examples

The scientists descended to the ocean's deepest trench in a bathyscaph.
In 1960, the bathyscaph Trieste reached the bottom of the Mariana Trench.
A bathyscaph allows researchers to study deep-sea creatures in their natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submarine - a vessel capable of underwater operation

Etymology

From French 'bathyscaphe', from Greek 'bathys' (deep) + 'skaphos' (ship).
