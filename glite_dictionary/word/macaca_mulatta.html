Macaca mulatta

1. NOUN

/ məˈkɑːkə mʊˈlæt̬ə / (AmE), / məˈkɑːkə mʊˈlætə / (BrE)
A species of Old World monkey native to southern Asia, commonly known as the rhesus macaque, often used in medical research.
species of Asian monkey (rhesus macaque)

Examples

The Macaca mulatta is widely used in biomedical research due to its genetic similarity to humans.
Populations of Macaca mulatta can be found across India, Nepal, and parts of China.
Researchers observed social behavior patterns in Macaca mulatta living near human settlements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhesus monkey - common name for Macaca mulatta, an Asian monkey species

Etymology

From Latin, 'Macaca' from Portuguese 'macaco' (monkey), and 'mulatta' meaning 'mulatto' (brown or mixed color) in Latin.
