naupathia

1. NOUN

/ nɔˈpæθiə / (AmE), / nɔːˈpaθɪə / (BrE)
A medical term for motion sickness experienced while traveling on water, such as seasickness.
motion sickness from water travel
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with naupathia after experiencing severe nausea on the ferry.
Many people who have naupathia avoid boat trips altogether.
Treatments for naupathia may include medication or special wristbands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seasickness - motion sickness caused by traveling on water

Etymology

From Greek 'naus' (ship) and 'pathos' (suffering).
