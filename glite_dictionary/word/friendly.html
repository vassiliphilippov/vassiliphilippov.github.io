friendly

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛn(d)li / (AmE), / ˈfrɛn(d)li / (BrE)
A soldier or military unit that belongs to, or is allied with, one's own side in a conflict.
soldier/unit allied with one's own forces

Examples

The pilot accidentally fired on a group of friendlies during the foggy battle.
All friendlies are marked with blue icons on the battlefield map.
The rescue team was relieved to spot friendlies approaching their position at sunrise.

Synset Examples

friendlies came to their rescue

Related Terms

  • Antonym: enemy - a soldier or unit opposing one's own forces in war
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08414446-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈfrɛndli / (AmE), / ˈfrɛndli / (BrE)
(of military forces, territory, or actions) of or belonging to one's own country or allies, not the enemy.
of one's own military or allied forces

Examples

The soldiers moved cautiously through friendly territory after crossing the border.
He survived an attack, only to be wounded by friendly fire during the confusion.
Pilots must identify whether the plane on their radar is friendly or hostile.

Synset Examples

in friendly territory
he was accidentally killed by friendly fire

Related Terms

  • Antonym: enemy - opposing or hostile forces in a military context
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01081326-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈfrɛnd.li / (AmE), / ˈfrɛnd.li / (BrE)
behaving in a kind, helpful, or supportive way; showing the qualities of a friend
kind and supportive; like a friend

Examples

Our new neighbors are very friendly and often invite us over for tea.
She always gives friendly advice when someone needs help.
The hotel staff were extremely friendly during our entire stay.

Synset Examples

friendly advice
a friendly neighborhood
the only friendly person here
a friendly host and hostess

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amiable - having or displaying a friendly and pleasant manner
  • Antonym: hostile - unfriendly; showing opposition

Etymology

From Old English *frēondlic*, from *frēond* (friend) + *-lic* (-ly).

Source: WordNet
