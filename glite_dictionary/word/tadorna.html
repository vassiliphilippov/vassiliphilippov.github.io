Tadorna

1. NOUN

/ təˈdɔrnə / (AmE), / təˈdɔːnə / (BrE)
A genus of large, brightly colored waterfowl in the duck family, commonly known as sheldrakes.
a genus of sheldrake ducks

Examples

The common shelduck belongs to the genus Tadorna.
Many Tadorna species are noted for their striking plumage.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of the Tadorna population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheldrake - a large species of duck in the genus Tadorna

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, adopted for the genus based on local French or Basque names for the shelduck.
