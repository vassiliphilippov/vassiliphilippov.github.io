doh

1. NOUN

/ doʊ / (AmE), / dəʊ / (BrE)
The first and tonic note of any major scale in solmization, often used as the starting point in the movable-do system.
first note of a major scale (solmization)

Examples

In the song, each verse begins with doh to help teach the scale.
She practiced singing the major scale starting from doh every day.
The choir started with doh and went up to the higher notes in perfect harmony.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: re - the second note of a major scale in solmization

Etymology

From Italian 'do', devised by the music theorist Giovanni Battista Doni as a replacement for 'Ut', based on the initial syllable of his own name.
