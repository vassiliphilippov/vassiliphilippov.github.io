harbourage

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrbərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈhɑːb(ə)rɪdʒ / (BrE)
(nautical) A place of shelter or refuge, especially for ships or boats.
shelter or refuge for ships

Examples

The small cove served as a secure harbourage during the violent storm.
Many sailors sought harbourage along the sheltered coastline.
The captain guided the ship towards a welcoming harbourage at dusk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harbor - a place of shelter for ships; especially in American English
  • Synonym: anchorage - a place where boats or ships are anchored

Etymology

From Middle English 'harbour', influenced by the suffix '-age', denoting a place or action.
