cultism

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌl.tɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈkʌl.tɪz.əm / (BrE)
excessive or zealous devotion to a particular religious group, doctrine, or practice, often outside mainstream traditions
zealous religious devotion or sectarianism

Examples

His growing cultism alarmed his family and friends.
Many critics warn against the dangers of cultism in religious movements.
The organization condemned any form of cultism that encouraged blind obedience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sectarianism - excessive attachment to a particular sect or party
  • Antonym: orthodoxy - authorized or generally accepted theory, doctrine, or practice

Etymology

From 'cult' + '-ism,' denoting practice or belief.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌl.tɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈkʌl.tɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the practice or belief in the doctrines of a cult, or excessive devotion to a cult's practices and ideas
devotion to cult beliefs or practices

Examples

Many sociologists study the dangers associated with cultism in modern societies.
The university has implemented programs to prevent cultism among students.
Her book examines the psychological effects of cultism on vulnerable individuals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sectarianism - excessive attachment to a particular sect or party
  • Antonym: mainstream - belonging to or characteristic of the mainstream; opposite of cult practices

Etymology

From 'cult' + '-ism', the suffix denoting practice or ideology.
