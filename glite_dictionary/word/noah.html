Noah

1. PROPN

/ ˈnoʊ.ə / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ.ə / (BrE)
(in the Bible) the Hebrew patriarch who built an ark to save his family and pairs of animals from the Flood, as told in the Book of Genesis.
biblical patriarch who built the ark

Examples

According to the Bible, Noah gathered animals two by two onto the ark.
The story of Noah and the flood is often depicted in art and literature.
Many people know Noah as a symbol of faith and obedience.

Etymology

From Hebrew Noach, meaning 'rest' or 'comfort'.
