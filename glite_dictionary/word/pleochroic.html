pleochroic

1. ADJ

/ pliˈoʊkrioʊk / (AmE), / pliːˈɒkrɪək / (BrE)
(of a crystal or mineral) exhibiting or relating to pleochroism; showing different colors when viewed from different directions under polarized light.
showing pleochroism; color change by direction
medical

Examples

The mineral is pleochroic, shifting from blue to green depending on the viewing angle.
Under polarized light, the crystal appears strongly pleochroic.
Gemologists often rely on pleochroic scales to identify specific minerals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pleochroism - property of showing different colors when viewed from different directions

Etymology

From Greek pleion (more) + khroa (color) + -ic.
