to redefine

1. VERB

/ ˌriːdɪˈfaɪn / (AmE), / ˌriːdɪˈfaɪn / (BrE)
to give a new or different definition to a word or concept
give a new definition

Examples

The committee decided to redefine the term to reflect recent scientific advances.
As language evolves, dictionaries often redefine words to match changing usage.
They hope to redefine leadership by emphasizing collaboration over authority.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to define - to state or describe the exact meaning of a word

Etymology

From re- (again) + define, from Latin definire (to set bounds, define).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00959581-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌriː.dɪˈfaɪn / (AmE), / ˌriː.dɪˈfaɪn / (BrE)
to give a new or different definition or meaning to something; to reconsider or transform the established understanding of something
give a new definition or meaning

Examples

The company decided to redefine its strategy to adapt to changing markets.
Her experience abroad helped her redefine her goals and career path.
Modern artists often redefine what is considered art.

Synset Examples

She redefined his duties

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to define - to state or describe exactly the nature, scope, or meaning of something

Etymology

From re- (again) + define, from Latin 'definire' (to set bounds, determine).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02617862-v
Synset POS: v
