handiwork

1. NOUN

/ ˈhændiˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈhændɪˌwɜːk / (BrE)
Something that has been made or done by hand, especially with skill or careful effort.
something made by hand

Examples

The intricate carvings on the door are a fine example of his handiwork.
She proudly displayed her handiwork at the local craft fair.
Every stitch in the quilt shows the care and attention of her handiwork.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: craftsmanship - the skill or quality of work shown in something made by hand
  • Antonym: mass production - production in large quantities, usually by machinery

Etymology

From Middle English handiwerk, from Old English handġeweorc (hand-work), from hand + geweorc (work).
