audaciousness

1. NOUN

/ ɔˈdeɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / ɔːˈdeɪʃəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being bold, fearless, or willing to take risks
boldness; fearless daring

Examples

Her audaciousness impressed the entire team during the high-stakes presentation.
The explorer's audaciousness led him to uncharted territories despite the dangers.
Critics admired the audaciousness of the new artist's work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: audacity - boldness or daring, especially with disregard for conventional thought

Etymology

From Latin audax, audac- (bold, daring), via audacious + -ness.

2. NOUN

/ ɔːˈdeɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / ɔːˈdeɪʃəsnəs / (BrE)
a bold and fearless quality; unrestrained confidence or daring, sometimes perceived as rude or disrespectful
boldness; daring or impudence

Examples

She was admired for her audaciousness in taking on such a challenging project.
The audaciousness of his remarks shocked everyone at the meeting.
His audaciousness allowed him to speak up when no one else dared.

Synset Examples

he had the audacity to question my decision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: audacity - boldness or daring, especially with confident or arrogant disregard for restrictions
  • Antonym: timidity - the quality of being easily frightened or lacking confidence

Etymology

From Latin audax, audac- (bold, daring) via audacious + -ness.
