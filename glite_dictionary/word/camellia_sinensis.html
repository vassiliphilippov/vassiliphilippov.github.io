Camellia sinensis

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmiliə sɪˈnɛnsɪs / (AmE), / kəˈmiːliə sɪˈnɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a tropical evergreen shrub native to Asia, widely cultivated for its leaves which are used to make tea
the tea plant

Examples

The leaves of Camellia sinensis are used to produce green, black, and white tea.
Researchers are studying the health benefits of Camellia sinensis extracts.
Tea farmers carefully harvest Camellia sinensis leaves by hand during the season.

Synset Examples

tea has fragrant white flowers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tea plant - common name for Camellia sinensis
  • Degree Lower: green tea - type of tea produced from the leaves of Camellia sinensis
  • Degree Higher: black tea - type of tea produced from the leaves of Camellia sinensis, more oxidized than green tea

Etymology

From Latin: Camellia (genus named after Jesuit botanist G.J. Kamel) + sinensis (Latin for 'from China').
