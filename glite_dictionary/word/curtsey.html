curtsey

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɜːrtsi / (AmE), / ˈkɜːtsi / (BrE)
a respectful gesture performed by bending the knees with one foot forward, traditionally made by women
a respectful gesture by women involving bending the knees

Examples

The young girl gave a graceful curtsey before leaving the stage.
She made a curtsey to the queen during the ceremony.
After the dance, all the performers ended with a curtsey and a smile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bow - a gesture of bending at the waist, typically by men as a sign of respect

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'courtesy' in Middle English, from Old French 'courtoisie'.

2. VERB

/ ˈkɜːrt.si / (AmE), / ˈkɜːt.si / (BrE)
(of a woman or girl) to lower the body briefly by bending the knees as a gesture of respect, typically in greeting or before someone of higher rank.
bend knees in respectful greeting

Examples

At the end of the performance, the ballerina curtseyed gracefully to the audience.
Whenever she entered the room, she would curtsey before the queen.
The little girls spent the afternoon practicing how to curtsey properly in dance class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bow - bend the upper part of the body forward as a gesture of respect or greeting

Etymology

From earlier 'courtesy', derived from Anglo-Norman 'curteisie' (courtesy).
