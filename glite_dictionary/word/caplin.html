caplin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæplɪn / (AmE), / ˈkaplɪn / (BrE)
a small northern fish (Mallotus villosus) found in Arctic and sub-Arctic waters, serving as food for seabirds, marine mammals, and other fish
small northern forage fish

Examples

Thousands of seabirds flocked to feed on the spawning caplin along the beach.
In northern waters, caplin are a vital part of the food chain.
Fishermen caught nets full of caplin during the annual run.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smelt - small silvery fish found in cold northern waters

Etymology

From French 'capelan', ultimately from Spanish 'capellán' (chaplain), likely referring to the fish's shape resembling a priest's cap.
