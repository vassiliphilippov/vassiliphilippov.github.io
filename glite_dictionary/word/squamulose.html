squamulose

1. ADJ

/ ˈskwæmjəˌloʊs / (AmE), / ˈskwamjʊləʊs / (BrE)
(especially of a lichen or plant surface) covered with or consisting of small scales.
covered with tiny scales (biology)

Examples

The squamulose texture of the lichen was visible under the microscope.
Certain species of fungi have squamulose caps that flake off easily.
Some desert plants develop a squamulose surface as protection against the harsh sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scaly - covered with or resembling scales

Etymology

From Latin "squamula" (small scale) + -ose (full of).
