volumed

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɑːlmd / (AmE), / ˈvɒlmd / (BrE)
(of smoke, cloud, or similar substances) formed or rising in rounded or billowing masses.
rising or formed in rounded masses

Examples

The fire sent volumed smoke spiraling into the sky.
Thick, volumed clouds gathered above the mountains before the storm.
He coughed, struggling to see through the volumed haze filling the hallway.

Synset Examples

gasping with the volumed smoke

Etymology

From 'volume' + '-ed', describing something with the appearance of volume or mass.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01393712-s

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɑljumd / (AmE), / ˈvɒljuːmd / (BrE)
(often used in combination) consisting of or having a specified number or type of volumes.
having a specified number of volumes

Examples

The library has a rare, three-volumed edition of the novel.
She inherited a beautifully illustrated, six-volumed encyclopedia from her grandfather.
Their research is detailed in a five-volumed report.

Synset Examples

the poet's volumed works
a two-volumed history
multi-volumed encyclopedias
large-volumed editions
