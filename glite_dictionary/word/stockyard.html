stockyard

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑkˌjɑrd / (AmE), / ˈstɒkˌjɑːd / (BrE)
an enclosed area where cattle, pigs, horses, or sheep are kept temporarily, especially before slaughter or transport
enclosed yard for holding livestock

Examples

The cattle were herded into the stockyard before being loaded onto the trucks.
He manages the largest stockyard in the region, where hundreds of animals pass through every week.
The smell from the stockyard drifted across the town on hot summer days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pen - an enclosure for confining animals

Etymology

From 'stock' (livestock) + 'yard' (enclosed area for animals), first attested in the mid-19th century.
