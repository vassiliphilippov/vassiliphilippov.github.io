Mammalia

1. NOUN

/ məˈmeɪliə / (AmE), / məˈmeɪlɪə / (BrE)
A class of warm-blooded vertebrate animals characterized by the presence of mammary glands in females, hair or fur, and typically live birth.
class of warm-blooded vertebrate animals (mammals)

Examples

Scientists classify humans, whales, and dogs as Mammalia due to shared characteristics.
The evolution of Mammalia has been a topic of extensive research in zoology.
Unlike reptiles, all members of Mammalia have hair at some stage of their development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mammal - an animal of the class Mammalia

Etymology

From Latin *mammalis* (of the breast), from *mamma* (breast).

2. NOUN

/ məˈmeɪliə / (AmE), / məˈmeɪlɪə / (BrE)
a class of warm-blooded vertebrate animals characterized by the presence of mammary glands in females, hair or fur, and, typically, live birth
scientific class of mammals

Examples

The blue whale is the largest member of the Mammalia class.
Bats are the only flying representatives of Mammalia.
The evolution of Mammalia is studied extensively in paleontology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mammal - an animal of the class Mammalia
  • Antonym: reptile - a cold-blooded vertebrate of the class Reptilia

Etymology

From Latin 'mamma' (breast), referring to mammals' milk-producing glands.
