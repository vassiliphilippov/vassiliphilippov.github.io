close-up

1. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊsˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈkləʊsˌʌp / (BrE)
A photograph or film shot taken at very short range to show detailed features of the subject, often filling most or all of the frame.
photograph or shot taken at close range

Examples

The director used a close-up of the actor's face to convey emotion.
Magazine covers often feature a close-up of a model or celebrity.
She prefers to take close-ups of flowers to highlight their intricate details.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: long shot - a photograph or film shot taken from a considerable distance, showing much of the surroundings

Etymology

From 'close' + 'up', indicating a view from a close distance.
