edgeways

1. ADV

/ ˈɛdʒˌweɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛdʒˌweɪz / (BrE)
with the edge facing forward, or on, by, or toward the edge.
with the edge facing forward or toward the edge

Examples

She managed to slide the book edgeways into the narrow gap on the shelf.
The coin fell edgeways onto the floor and rolled under the table.
When packing the glass panels, ensure they are placed edgeways rather than flat.

Synset Examples

he sawed the board edgeways
held it edgewise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edgewise - with the edge forward or toward the edge

Etymology

Formed from 'edge' + adverbial suffix '-ways', meaning 'in the direction or manner of the edge'.

2. ADV

/ ˈɛdʒ.wɛɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛdʒ.weɪz / (BrE)
with the edge first or as if by an edge; barely or scarcely (used especially in the phrase 'get a word in edgeways').
with the edge first; barely

Examples

I tried to speak, but I couldn't get a word in edgeways during the heated debate.
She slipped the paper into the narrow slot edgeways to make it fit.
During the lively conversation, he found it hard even to say hello edgeways.

Synset Examples

I could not get a word in edgewise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edgewise - with the edge first or as if by an edge; barely or scarcely (alternate spelling)

Etymology

From 'edge' + adverbial suffix '-ways', meaning 'in the manner or direction of an edge'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00326886-r
Synset POS: r
