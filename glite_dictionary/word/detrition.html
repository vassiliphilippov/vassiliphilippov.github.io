detrition

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈtrɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈtrɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of gradual wearing away or erosion of a substance, especially by friction.
wearing away by friction

Examples

Over centuries, the constant flow of water led to the detrition of the river rocks.
The dentist explained that detrition of the enamel can result from frequent grinding of teeth.
Engineers studied the detrition of machine parts to improve their resistance to wear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abrasion - the process of scraping or wearing away

Etymology

From Latin 'detritio' (a rubbing away), from 'deterere' (to rub away).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈtrɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈtrɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the process of wearing away or grinding down by friction, especially when one object is moved over another with pressure; abrasion.
wearing away by friction

Examples

The detrition of rocks by glaciers contributes to the formation of fine sediment.
Continuous detrition between machine parts can lead to mechanical failure.
Over time, dental detrition naturally occurs as teeth grind against each other.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abrasion - the process of scraping or wearing something away

Etymology

From Latin 'detritio' (a rubbing away), from 'deterere' (to rub away).

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈtrɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈtrɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of wearing down rock particles by friction caused by water, wind, or ice
wearing down of rocks by friction

Examples

Continual detrition by river currents slowly shapes the riverbed over centuries.
Wind-blown sand causes the gradual detrition of exposed rock surfaces in deserts.
Glacial detrition leaves behind smooth, polished stones in the landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abrasion - the process of scraping or wearing away, especially by friction

Etymology

From Latin detritio (a rubbing away), from detritus (worn away).
