adient

1. ADJ

/ ˈæd.i.ənt / (AmE), / ˈæd.i.ənt / (BrE)
(psychology) Characterized by approach, acceptance, or a tendency to move toward a stimulus.
relating to approach/acceptance in psychology
medical

Examples

Infants may exhibit adient behavior when presented with new and interesting stimuli.
His response to criticism was adient, showing a willingness to engage and accept feedback.
Researchers observed both adient and abient reactions in the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abient - characterized by withdrawal or avoidance (in psychology)

Etymology

From Latin adiens, present participle of adīre (to go to, approach).
