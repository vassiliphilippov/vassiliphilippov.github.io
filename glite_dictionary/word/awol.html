AWOL

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪˌwɑl / (AmE), / ˈeɪˌwɒl / (BrE)
a person who is absent from their post or duty without official permission
person absent without leave

Examples

When the manager returned, she found one employee had gone AWOL during the busiest hour.
After three days, the soldier was declared AWOL by his commanding officer.
If a team member goes AWOL, the project can easily fall behind schedule.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deserter - a person who abandons their duty or post without permission

Etymology

Acronym from 'absent without leave.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09777167-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈeɪˌwɑl / (AmE), / ˈeɪˌwɒl / (BrE)
absent from duty or one's post without official permission, especially in a military context
absent without official leave
informal

Examples

The soldier was reported AWOL after failing to return from leave.
Several employees went AWOL during the busiest week of the year.
If a team member goes AWOL, the project can quickly fall behind schedule.

Synset Examples

truant schoolboys
the soldier was AWOL for almost a week

Related Terms

  • Synonym: truant - absent from a place, especially school, without permission

Etymology

Acronym from 'absent without (official) leave', originally U.S. military slang, early 20th century.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
