unprovoked

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.prəˈvoʊkt / (AmE), / ˌʌn.prəˈvəʊkt / (BrE)
(of an action or behavior) done without any apparent reason, cause, or justification.
done without provocation or reason

Examples

The attack on the hikers was completely unprovoked and shocked the community.
He lost his temper in an unprovoked outburst that left everyone surprised.
Police were searching for the motive behind the unprovoked vandalism.

Synset Examples

motiveless malignity
unprovoked and dastardly attack

Related Terms

  • Antonym: provoked - caused by or in response to some stimulus or provocation

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'provoked', from Latin 'provocare' (to call forth).
