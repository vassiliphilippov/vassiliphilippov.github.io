to temporize

1. VERB

/ ˈtɛmpəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpəraɪz / (BrE)
to delay making a decision or taking action in order to gain time or avoid committing oneself
delay to gain time or avoid decision

Examples

The negotiators chose to temporize rather than confront the issue directly.
She tried to temporize by changing the subject whenever the question came up.
If we temporize any longer, we might miss the opportunity.

Synset Examples

The speaker temporized in order to delay the vote

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to procrastinate - delay or postpone action
  • Antonym: to hasten - to act quickly or speed up a process

Etymology

From Medieval Latin temporizare, from Latin tempus (time).
