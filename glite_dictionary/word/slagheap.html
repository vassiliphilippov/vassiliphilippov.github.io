slagheap

1. NOUN

/ ˈslæɡˌhiːp / (AmE), / ˈslæɡˌhiːp / (BrE)
a large pile or mound of waste material left over from the mining of coal or metal ores
pile of mining waste

Examples

The abandoned mine had a massive slagheap looming over the village.
Environmental concerns have arisen due to toxic runoff from the old slagheap.
Children often played near the slagheaps despite warnings from local authorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoil tip - pile of waste material from mining operations
  • Synonym: tailings - residue left after the extraction of metal from ore

Etymology

From 'slag' (waste from metal smelting or coal mining) and 'heap' (pile).
