peregrine

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛrəˌɡrin / (AmE), / ˈpɛrɪɡrɪn / (BrE)
tending to wander or migrate; migratory or traveling from place to place, especially in search of work or opportunity.
migratory; traveling from place to place

Examples

The peregrine lifestyle of traveling musicians often kept them away from home for months at a time.
She admired the peregrine habits of ancient traders who crossed deserts and mountains.
In the spring, the birds become peregrine, migrating hundreds of miles to reach their breeding grounds.

Synset Examples

a restless mobile society
the nomadic habits of the Bedouins
believed the profession of a peregrine typist would have a happy future
wandering tribes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nomadic - having the characteristics of moving from place to place
  • Antonym: settled - not migratory or wandering; permanently established in one place

Etymology

From Latin 'peregrinus' (foreigner, traveler).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛr.ɪ.ɡrɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɛr.ɪ.ɡrɪn / (BrE)
a large, fast falcon (Falco peregrinus), widely distributed and noted for its speed, formerly used in falconry
a species of falcon

Examples

The peregrine is renowned for its incredible diving speed when hunting prey.
Centuries ago, peregrine falcons were highly prized in the art of falconry.
Conservationists have helped restore peregrine populations in many urban areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: falcon - a bird of prey with long, pointed wings

Etymology

From Medieval Latin peregrinus (foreigner, pilgrim), referencing its wandering habits.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01614113-n
Synset POS: n
