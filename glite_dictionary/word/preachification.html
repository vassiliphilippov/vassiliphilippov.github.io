preachification

1. NOUN

/ ˌpritʃɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːtʃɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
Tedious or excessive moralizing, especially in a self-righteous or preachy manner.
tedious moralizing in a preachy way

Examples

His constant preachification about honesty began to annoy everyone at the office.
The author's preachification makes the novel feel less like a story and more like a lecture.
Some critics disliked the film's tendency toward preachification on social issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sermonizing - giving moral advice in a sermon-like or preachy way
  • Synonym: moralizing - speaking or writing in a way that expresses moral judgments

Etymology

Formed from 'preach' + '-ification,' modeled after similar noun formations.
