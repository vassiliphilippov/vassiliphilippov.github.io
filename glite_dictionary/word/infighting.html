infighting

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌfaɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌfaɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
Conflict or rivalry between members of the same organization, group, or team, often hidden from outsiders.
conflict within a group or organization

Examples

There was constant infighting among the board members, which slowed down decision-making.
Despite their public show of unity, rumors of infighting persisted within the party.
The company suffered from years of infighting that hurt its performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discord - disagreement or conflict within a group
  • Antonym: unity - state of being united or joined as a whole

Etymology

Formed from 'in-' (internal) + 'fighting' (from Old English 'feohtan', to fight).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌfaɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌfaɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
Close-range fighting, especially within the sport of boxing, where fighters exchange blows at very short distances.
close-range fighting in boxing

Examples

He excelled at infighting, landing powerful punches in tight spaces.
The trainer taught new techniques for effective infighting during the match.
Her skill in infighting gave her an advantage when the distance between boxers closed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outboxing - Fighting at longer range in boxing, emphasizing distance and reach

Etymology

Formed from 'in-' (within) and 'fighting'; originally used in boxing.
