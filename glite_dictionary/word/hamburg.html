Hamburg

1. PROPN

/ ˈhæm.bɝːɡ / (AmE), / ˈhæm.bɜːɡ / (BrE)
A port city in northern Germany on the Elbe River, founded in the 9th century and now the country's largest port.
major port city in northern Germany

Examples

Hamburg is renowned for its impressive harbor and vibrant cultural scene.
After visiting Berlin, we traveled to Hamburg for a few days by the river Elbe.
The Hanseatic League was founded when Hamburg allied itself with Lübeck in the 13th century.

Etymology

From German 'Hamburg', from Old High German 'Hammaburg' (fortress at the river bend).
