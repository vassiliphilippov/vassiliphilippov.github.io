Sedum acre

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːdəm ˈeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈsiːdəm ˈeɪkə / (BrE)
a mat-forming European stonecrop plant with small yellow flowers, widely used as a ground cover
creeping stonecrop plant with yellow flowers

Examples

Gardeners often choose Sedum acre for its drought tolerance and bright yellow blossoms.
A carpet of Sedum acre can quickly cover rocky or poor soil areas.
Many rock gardens feature Sedum acre because it requires little maintenance and thrives in full sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stonecrop - common name for plants in the Sedum genus

Etymology

From Latin sedum (the plant genus) + acre ('sharp', referring to its acrid taste).
