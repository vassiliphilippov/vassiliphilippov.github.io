DINK

1. NOUN

/ dɪŋk / (AmE), / dɪŋk / (BrE)
a couple in which both partners have careers and no children, especially by choice; acronym for 'dual income, no kids'
childless couple with two incomes
informal

Examples

Many DINK couples choose to travel extensively instead of starting a family early.
The financial stability of being DINK appealed to them in their twenties.
As a DINK, he enjoys more disposable income and flexibility in his lifestyle.

Etymology

Acronym from 'dual income, no kids', first popularized in the 1980s.
