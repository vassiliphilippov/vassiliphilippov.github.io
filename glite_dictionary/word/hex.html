hex

1. ADJ

/ hɛks / (AmE), / hɛks / (BrE)
relating to or using the hexadecimal (base-16) number system, commonly used in mathematics and computing
relating to hexadecimal (base-16) numbers

Examples

Many programmers use hex values to represent colors in web design.
The error code was displayed in hex rather than decimal.
He converted the binary number to hex for easier readability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hexadecimal - the base-16 number system

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'hexadecimal'. From Greek 'hex' meaning 'six' and 'deka' meaning 'ten'.

Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ hɛks / (AmE), / hɛks / (BrE)
An evil spell or magic curse, usually believed to bring bad luck or misfortune.
an evil spell or curse

Examples

The villagers feared that someone had placed a hex on their crops after a poor harvest.
She believed her streak of bad luck was because of a hex cast by a jealous rival.
The old tale tells of a wicked hex that haunted the family for generations.

Synset Examples

a witch put a curse on his whole family
he put the whammy on me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse - a solemn utterance intended to invoke a supernatural power to inflict harm or punishment
  • Antonym: blessing - a prayer asking for divine favor and protection; opposite of a curse

Etymology

From Pennsylvania German 'hexe' (to practice witchcraft), from German 'Hexe' (witch).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07175075-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ hɛks / (AmE), / hɛks / (BrE)
to cast a spell on someone or something; to bring bad luck through supernatural means
cast a spell or jinx

Examples

Some superstitious fans believe that wearing the wrong shirt will hex their favorite team.
She claimed her neighbor hexed her garden so nothing would grow.
The villagers were terrified that the witch would hex anyone who crossed her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse - invoke supernatural harm or misfortune
  • Antonym: to bless - invoke good fortune or protection upon

Etymology

From Pennsylvania German *hexe* (to practice witchcraft), from German *Hexe* (witch).

Source: WordNet
