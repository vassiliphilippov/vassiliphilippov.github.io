pestilent

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛstələnt / (AmE), / ˈpɛstɪlənt / (BrE)
Extremely harmful, destructive, or deadly; causing great trouble or damage.
exceedingly harmful or destructive

Examples

The pestilent disease spread rapidly through the town, leaving many people ill.
He regarded the dictator's pestilent ideas as a threat to civilization.
A pestilent rumor undermined the company's reputation despite their best efforts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: harmless - not able or likely to cause harm

Etymology

From Latin pestilentem (infected, unwholesome), from pestis (plague).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɛstɪlənt / (AmE), / ˈpɛstɪlənt / (BrE)
Likely to cause or spread a serious infectious disease; tending to cause an epidemic.
likely to spread epidemic disease

Examples

The stagnant water became a pestilent breeding ground for mosquitoes.
Doctors worked to contain the pestilent outbreak before it spread any further.
Many feared the pestilent air that swept through the crowded city.

Synset Examples

a pestilential malignancy in the air
plaguey fevers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pestilential - causing or tending to cause pestilence; deadly

Etymology

From Latin pestilent-, pestilens (infected, poisonous), from pestis (plague).
