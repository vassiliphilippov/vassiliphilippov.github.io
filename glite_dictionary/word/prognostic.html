prognostic

1. NOUN

/ prɑːɡˈnɑːstɪk / (AmE), / prɒɡˈnɒstɪk / (BrE)
A sign or indication of a future event; an omen or predictive symptom.
sign or omen of something to come

Examples

The sudden drop in stock prices was seen as a prognostic of economic recession.
Many considered the dark clouds a prognostic of the approaching storm.
Some physicians regard certain early symptoms as a prognostic for the development of the disease.

Synset Examples

he looked for an omen before going into battle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: omen - a sign that is believed to predict the future
  • Synonym: harbinger - a person or thing that announces or signals the approach of another
  • Synonym: sign - an event whose presence or occurrence indicates something

Etymology

From Greek prognōstikos, from pro- (before) + gnōsis (knowledge).

2. ADJ

/ prɑɡˈnɑstɪk / (AmE), / prɒɡˈnɒstɪk / (BrE)
relating to or useful for predicting the likely course or outcome of something, especially in medicine or science
relating to prediction or prognosis

Examples

His symptoms have prognostic significance for his chances of recovery.
Advanced imaging techniques now provide prognostic information about cancer progression.
Researchers are searching for better prognostic markers in heart disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predictive - relating to making predictions about future events
  • Antonym: diagnostic - concerned with the identification of the nature or cause of something, especially illness

Etymology

From Greek prognōstikos (foreknowing), from pro- (before) + gignōskein (to know).
