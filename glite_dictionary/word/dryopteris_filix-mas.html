Dryopteris filix-mas

1. NOUN

/ draɪˈɑptərɪs ˌfɪlɪksˈmæs / (AmE), / draɪˈɒptərɪs ˌfɪlɪksˈmæs / (BrE)
a fern native to North America and Europe whose rhizomes and stalks produce an oleoresin once used to expel tapeworms
species of fern; source of medicinal oleoresin

Examples

The rhizomes of Dryopteris filix-mas were traditionally used in medicine to treat intestinal worms.
You can often find Dryopteris filix-mas growing in shaded woodlands throughout Europe and North America.
Researchers study Dryopteris filix-mas for its unique chemical compounds and potential medicinal applications.

Etymology

From Greek *drys* (oak), *pteris* (fern), and Latin *filix-mas* ('male fern').
