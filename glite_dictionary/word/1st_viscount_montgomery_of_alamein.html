1st Viscount Montgomery of Alamein

1. PROPN

/ fɜrst ˈvɪskaʊnt mɒnˈtɡəməri əv ˌæləˈmeɪn / (AmE), / fɜːst ˈvɪskaʊnt mɒnˈtɡʌməri əv ˌæləˈmeɪn / (BrE)
(1887–1976) An English general who led British forces to victory in North Africa during World War II and later commanded British ground troops in the Normandy invasion.
British WWII field marshal and military leader

Examples

Field Marshal 1st Viscount Montgomery of Alamein played a pivotal role in the Allied victory in North Africa.
The strategies devised by 1st Viscount Montgomery of Alamein are still studied in military academies today.
Britain honored 1st Viscount Montgomery of Alamein for his distinguished service during World War II.

Etymology

Title created for Bernard Law Montgomery, combining peerage rank and the site of the Battle of El Alamein.
