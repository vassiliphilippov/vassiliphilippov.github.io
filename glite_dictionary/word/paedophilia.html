paedophilia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpiːdəˈfɪliə / (AmE), / ˌpiːdəˈfɪliə / (BrE)
A psychiatric disorder characterized by sexual attraction to children, typically prepubescent.
sexual attraction to children
medical

Examples

The law strictly prohibits any form of paedophilia.
He was diagnosed with paedophilia and required therapy.
Society has a strong moral stance against paedophilia for the protection of children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pedophilia - alternative American spelling, sexual attraction to children

Etymology

From Greek pais (child) + philia (friendship, love).
