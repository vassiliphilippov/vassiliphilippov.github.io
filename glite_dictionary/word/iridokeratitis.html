iridokeratitis

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌraɪdoʊˌkɛrəˈtaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪdəʊˌkɛrəˈtaɪtɪs / (BrE)
Inflammation affecting both the iris and the cornea of the eye.
inflammation of iris and cornea
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with iridokeratitis after presenting with eye pain and blurred vision.
Chronic iridokeratitis can lead to permanent vision impairment if not treated promptly.
Ophthalmologists monitor for signs of iridokeratitis in patients with autoimmune conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keratitis - inflammation of the cornea

Etymology

From Greek 'iris' (rainbow, iris of the eye) + 'keratitis' (inflammation of the cornea).
