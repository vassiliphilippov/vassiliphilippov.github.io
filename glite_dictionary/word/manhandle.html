to manhandle

1. VERB

/ ˈmænˌhændəl / (AmE), / ˈmænˌhændl / (BrE)
to handle someone or something roughly, often using physical force
handle roughly with force

Examples

The demonstrators were manhandled by security guards during the protest.
Please don't manhandle the fragile equipment when you move it.
He claimed that officers manhandled him while making the arrest.

Synset Examples

I was manhandled by the police

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mishandle - to manage or deal with something incorrectly or ineffectively
  • Antonym: caress - to touch or stroke gently

Etymology

Formed from 'man' + 'handle', meaning to handle by hand, often roughly.
