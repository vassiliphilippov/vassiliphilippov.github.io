bareback

1. ADV

/ ˈbɛrˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈbeəˌbak / (BrE)
(of riding a horse) without a saddle.
riding without a saddle

Examples

She prefers to ride her horse bareback during the summer months.
The children learned to sit confidently by practicing bareback in the paddock.
He won the race by galloping bareback across the finish line.

Synset Examples

she prefers to ride her horse bareback
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00279400-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ˈbɛrˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈbeəˌbæk / (BrE)
without a saddle; done or performed while riding on a horse without a saddle.
without a saddle (e.g. riding a horse)

Examples

The performer wowed the crowd with a bareback routine at the rodeo.
She learned to ride bareback before ever using a saddle.
Many skilled riders enjoy the challenge of going bareback across open fields.

Synset Examples

a bareback rider

Etymology

From 'bare' + 'back', literally meaning with the back (of the horse) bare.
