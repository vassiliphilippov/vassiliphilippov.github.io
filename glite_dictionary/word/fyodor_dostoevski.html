Fyodor Dostoevski

1. PROPN

/ ˈfjodər ˌdɑstəˈjɛfski / (AmE), / ˈfjɔːdɔː ˌdɒstəˈjɛfski / (BrE)
(1821–1881) Russian novelist known for works depicting human suffering with humor and psychological insight, including 'Crime and Punishment' and 'The Brothers Karamazov'.
Russian novelist, author of 'Crime and Punishment'

Examples

Many consider Fyodor Dostoevski one of the greatest writers in world literature.
I first read Fyodor Dostoevski's 'The Brothers Karamazov' in college and was deeply moved.
Scholars still debate the philosophical themes found throughout Fyodor Dostoevski's novels.

Etymology

Transliteration of Russian Фёдор Достоевский (Fyódor Dostoyévskiy).
