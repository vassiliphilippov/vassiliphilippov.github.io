clotted

1. ADJ

/ ˈklɑt̬ɪd / (AmE), / ˈklɒtɪd / (BrE)
thickened or formed into soft, thick lumps or clots, especially from liquid or semi-liquid substances
thickened into lumps or clots

Examples

The nurse carefully wiped away the clotted blood from the wound.
Thick layers of clotted cream were served with the fresh scones.
After being left out overnight, the paint in the can had clotted and was no longer usable.

Synset Examples

clotted blood
seeds clogged together

Related Terms

  • Synonym: congealed - become semi-solid, especially by cooling
  • Synonym: coagulated - changed from a liquid to a thickened or solid state

Etymology

From the verb 'clot', of Germanic origin; related to Middle English 'clot' (lump).
