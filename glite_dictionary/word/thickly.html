thickly

1. ADV

/ ˈθɪkli / (AmE), / ˈθɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that is difficult to understand, as if the speaker's tongue is too heavy or swollen; with poor articulation
in a poorly articulated, unclear manner

Examples

After the surgery, he could only speak thickly for a few hours.
She muttered thickly, her words hard to understand through her tears.
His words came out thickly, slurred by exhaustion.

Synset Examples

after a few drinks he was beginning to speak thickly

2. ADV

/ ˈθɪk.li / (AmE), / ˈθɪk.li / (BrE)
In a manner that is dense or has a thick consistency.
in a thick and dense manner

Examples

The soup was cooked thickly for a richer flavor.
She spread butter thickly on her toast every morning.
The mist hung thickly over the fields, obscuring the view.

Synset Examples

the blood was flowing thick

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thinly - in a manner that is not thick or dense

Etymology

From 'thick' + '-ly', the adverbial suffix.

3. ADV

/ ˈθɪkli / (AmE), / ˈθɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that is thick or dense; with a large amount or concentration, or covering something in a thick manner.
in a thick or dense manner

Examples

The cake was coated thickly with chocolate icing, making it look delicious.
Snow fell thickly throughout the night, covering the town in white.
He spread butter thickly on his toast before taking a big bite.

Synset Examples

spread 1/4 lb softened margarine or cooking fat fairly thickly all over the surface
we were visiting a small, thickly walled and lovely town with straggling outskirt

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thinly - in a thin or sparse manner

Etymology

Formed from 'thick' + '-ly', standard adverbial suffix.

4. ADV

/ ˈθɪkli / (AmE), / ˈθɪkli / (BrE)
In a concentrated or dense manner; with things or people close together.
in a concentrated or dense manner

Examples

The trees grew so thickly that little sunlight reached the forest floor.
The area is thickly populated, making it difficult to find affordable housing.
He spread the butter thickly on his toast before taking a bite.
