pycnosis

1. NOUN

/ pɪkˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / pɪkˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A degenerative process in which a cell nucleus becomes shrunken and densely stained, typically as part of cell death.
degeneration of a cell nucleus
medical

Examples

Under the microscope, the presence of pycnosis in cells indicates advanced tissue degeneration.
In apoptosis, pycnosis often marks the irreversible stage of nuclear condensation.
Biopsy reports noted widespread pycnosis among neurons in the affected brain region.

Etymology

From Greek *pyknosis*, from *pyknos* (thick, dense).
