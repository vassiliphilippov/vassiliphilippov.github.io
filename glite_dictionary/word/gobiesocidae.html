Gobiesocidae

1. NOUN

/ goʊ.biˈɛs.əˌdi / (AmE), / gəʊ.biˈɛs.əˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of small, marine fishes known as clingfishes, characterized by their sucker-like pelvic fins that enable them to attach to surfaces
family of fishes: clingfishes
medical

Examples

The Gobiesocidae family includes several species equipped with a special adhesive disc.
Marine biologists discovered new habitats for members of the Gobiesocidae along the rocky shore.
The study focused on the evolutionary adaptations of Gobiesocidae to intertidal zones.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Gobiesox, combining 'goby' (a type of small fish) with the Greek 'sōkos' (a shelter).
