tritoma

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈtoʊmə / (AmE), / trɪˈtəʊmə / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Kniphofia, known for its long, grasslike leaves and tall spikes of red or yellow drooping flowers.
flowering plant (Kniphofia genus)

Examples

The gardener planted a tritoma beside the pond to add a splash of color in late summer.
Bright red and yellow tritomas attracted bees and butterflies to the flowerbed.
Known as 'red hot poker,' the tritoma is often chosen for ornamental gardens.

Etymology

From New Latin Tritoma, former genus name, from Greek tritomos ‘three-cut’.
