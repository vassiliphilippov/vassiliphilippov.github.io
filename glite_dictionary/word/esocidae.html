Esocidae

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈsoʊsɪˌdi / (AmE), / ɪˈsəʊsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of freshwater predatory fish, including pikes, pickerels, and muskellunges.
family of freshwater predatory fish

Examples

The family Esocidae comprises several renowned predatory fish species, including the northern pike.
Detailed studies of Esocidae have revealed unique adaptations for ambush hunting.
Biologists often compare members of Esocidae to other freshwater predators in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pike - a large predatory freshwater fish
  • Synonym: pickerel - a small North American freshwater fish related to pike
  • Synonym: muskellunge - a large North American freshwater fish of the pike family

Etymology

From modern Latin Esocidae, based on Esox, the genus of pike.

family of freshwater predatory fish

Examples

The Esocidae family is known for including some of the most formidable freshwater predators.
Members of the Esocidae have elongated bodies and sharp teeth, adapted for catching prey.
The biodiversity of North American lakes is enriched by the presence of the Esocidae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pike - a type of large freshwater predatory fish
  • Synonym: pickerel - small North American freshwater fish related to the pike
  • Synonym: muskellunge - a large North American freshwater fish in the same family as pike

Etymology

From modern Latin Esocidae, based on Esox, the genus name for pike.
