coseismal

1. ADJ

/ ˌkoʊˈsaɪzməl / (AmE), / ˌkəʊˈsaɪzməl / (BrE)
relating to places where the waves from an earthquake arrive at the same time
occurring at the same time as earthquake waves

Examples

The researchers mapped the coseismal lines to study the earthquake's effects across the region.
A coseismal map shows areas where seismic waves reached at the same moment.
Engineers used coseismal data to assess which districts experienced the tremor simultaneously.

Etymology

From co- (together) + seismal (from Greek seismos, meaning 'earthquake').
