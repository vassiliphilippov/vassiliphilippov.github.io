polytonalism

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈtoʊnəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈtəʊnəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The use of two or more different musical keys played simultaneously in a composition.
simultaneous use of multiple keys in music

Examples

The composer's embrace of polytonalism was evident in the simultaneous clash of keys.
Many early twentieth-century pieces explored polytonalism as a way to expand musical harmony.
Stravinsky used polytonalism to create a complex and richly textured soundscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bitonality - the use of two keys at once in music
  • Antonym: atonality - music that lacks a clear key or tonal center

Etymology

From poly- (many) + tonal (relating to tone) + -ism, coined in the early 20th century.
