to unwire

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈwaɪɚ / (AmE), / ʌnˈwaɪə / (BrE)
to remove the wires or the wiring from something
remove wires or wiring

Examples

The technician had to unwire the old alarm system before installing the new one.
She carefully unwired the lamp so she could repaint its base.
Before shipping the electronics for recycling, they unwired all the components.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wire - to provide with wires

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (reversal prefix) + 'wire'.
