predisposition

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriː.dɪs.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌpriː.dɪs.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
A tendency or inclination to interpret information or situations in a particular way, especially before any evidence is presented.
an inclination or tendency to interpret

Examples

His predisposition to distrust strangers made it hard for him to make new friends.
The jury's predisposition toward the defendant was a concern for a fair trial.
A person’s predisposition to believe a story may affect how they understand the facts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bias - a preference or inclination, especially that inhibits impartial judgment
  • Antonym: impartiality - the state of being unbiased or fair

Etymology

From Latin praedispositio (arrangement in advance), from prae- (before) + dispositio (arrangement).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpriːdɪspəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːdɪspəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
a tendency to behave in a particular way or to be affected by a particular condition, often based on prior factors or inherent qualities
a tendency or inclination to act in a certain way

Examples

Her predisposition to anxiety made her cautious in unfamiliar situations.
Genetics can influence a person's predisposition to certain diseases.
Despite his predisposition to skepticism, he kept an open mind during the discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inclination - a tendency to do something
  • Antonym: aversion - a strong dislike or disinclination

Etymology

From Latin 'praedispositio' (arrangement beforehand), from 'prae-' (before) + 'disponere' (to arrange).

3. NOUN

/ ˌpriː.dɪs.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌpriː.dɪs.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
A tendency or susceptibility to develop a particular disease or be affected by a particular condition, often due to genetic or environmental factors.
tendency or susceptibility to disease
medical

Examples

Family history can increase your predisposition to certain illnesses.
Genetic factors may create a predisposition to diabetes in some people.
Her predisposition to allergies made springtime particularly challenging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: susceptibility - likelihood of being influenced or harmed by a particular thing
  • Antonym: immunity - ability to resist a particular infection or toxin

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'praedispositio' (arrangement beforehand), from Latin 'prae-' (before) and 'disponere' (to arrange).
