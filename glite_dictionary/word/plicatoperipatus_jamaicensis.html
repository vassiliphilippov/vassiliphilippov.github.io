Plicatoperipatus jamaicensis

1. NOUN

/ plɪˌkeɪtəˌpɛrɪˈpeɪtəs dʒəˌmeɪɪˈsɛn.sɪs / (AmE), / plɪˌkeɪtəˌpɛrɪˈpeɪtəs dʒəˌmeɪɪˈsɛn.sɪs / (BrE)
A species of velvet worm found in Jamaica, belonging to the phylum Onychophora.
Jamaican velvet worm species

Examples

Researchers discovered a new population of Plicatoperipatus jamaicensis in a remote Jamaican forest.
The conservation of Plicatoperipatus jamaicensis is important due to its limited habitat range.
Biologists study Plicatoperipatus jamaicensis to learn more about velvet worm evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: velvet worm - A soft-bodied, many-legged invertebrate of the phylum Onychophora

Etymology

From Modern Latin: 'Plicatoperipatus' (genus name, from Latin 'plicatus' meaning folded + Greek 'peri-' around + 'patein' to walk), and 'jamaicensis' (of Jamaica).
