cruiser

1. NOUN

/ ˈkruːzər / (AmE), / ˈkruːzə / (BrE)
a large motorboat equipped with living accommodations such as a cabin and plumbing, designed for people to live on board while traveling or at anchor
large motorboat with living quarters

Examples

They spent the summer living aboard their cruiser as they traveled along the coast.
The family bought a new cruiser equipped with two bedrooms and a small kitchen.
He dreams of retiring and sailing a cruiser around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yacht - a medium-sized sailboat or motorboat used for recreation

Etymology

From 'cruise' + '-er', originally describing ships that cruise.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkruːzər / (AmE), / ˈkruːzə / (BrE)
a large, fast warship, ranking below a battleship but above a destroyer in size
large warship between battleship and destroyer

Examples

The fleet included a modern cruiser equipped with advanced radar systems.
During World War II, several cruisers played critical roles in naval battles.
The navy commissioned a new cruiser to enhance their maritime defenses.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: battleship - the largest and most heavily armed type of warship
  • Degree Lower: destroyer - a small, fast, and maneuverable warship

Etymology

From cruise +‎ -er; "cruise" from Dutch *kruisen* (to cross).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03145551-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkruːzər / (AmE), / ˈkruːzə / (BrE)
a police car used for patrolling streets, typically equipped with radio communication
police patrol car

Examples

The officer drove the cruiser slowly down Main Street, keeping an eye out for any trouble.
Several police cruisers arrived at the scene within minutes of the call.
He watched as a cruiser passed by, its lights flashing in the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: police car - a car used by police for patrolling and responding to incidents

Etymology

From 'cruise' (to travel about), indicating its use for patrolling areas.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03145716-n
Synset POS: n
