iodochlorhydroxyquin

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.oʊˌkloʊr.haɪˈdrɒk.si.kwɪn / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.əʊˌklɔː.haɪˈdrɒk.si.kwɪn / (BrE)
a drug used to treat certain fungal infections, such as athlete's foot
antifungal medication
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed iodochlorhydroxyquin to help clear up the patient's athlete's foot.
Although effective, iodochlorhydroxyquin should be used with care due to potential side effects.
Some over-the-counter creams for fungal infections contain iodochlorhydroxyquin as an active ingredient.

Etymology

From iodo- (containing iodine), chloro- (containing chlorine), hydroxy- (containing a hydroxyl group), and quin(oline).
