outfall

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌfɔl / (AmE), / ˈaʊtfɔːl / (BrE)
the place where a river, drain, or sewer discharges its water into another body of water
the outlet of a river or drain

Examples

The city built a barrier at the outfall to reduce pollution in the bay.
Heavy rains caused flooding near the outfall of the old sewer system.
Fish often gather at the river's outfall where fresh water meets the sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mouth - the place where a river enters a larger body of water

Etymology

From 'out' + 'fall', originally meaning 'a place where water falls out.'
