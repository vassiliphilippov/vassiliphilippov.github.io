Stradivarius

1. NOUN

/ ˌstrædəˈvɛriəs / (AmE), / ˌstrædɪˈveəriəs / (BrE)
A violin made by Antonio Stradivari or a member of his family, famous for their quality and craftsmanship.
violin made by Stradivari

Examples

The concertmaster performed on a rare Stradivarius valued at several million dollars.
Several musicians dream of one day playing a Stradivarius because of its legendary sound quality.
Her grandfather's Stradivarius has been in the family for generations and is carefully preserved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violin - a stringed musical instrument of the violin family

Etymology

From Italian Stradivari (the family surname) with the Latinized suffix -us.

2. NOUN

/ ˌstrædəˈværiəs / (AmE), / ˌstradɪˈveəriəs / (BrE)
A violin or other stringed instrument made by Antonio Stradivari, renowned for exceptional craftsmanship and tonal quality; also refers to Stradivari himself, the Italian violin maker (1644?–1737).
famous violin by Stradivari; violin maker's name

Examples

The Stradivarius fetched a record price at the auction, delighting collectors worldwide.
She performed the concerto on a rare Stradivarius loaned by the music foundation.
Many believe that a Stradivarius produces a richer sound than modern violins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violin - a string instrument played with a bow

Etymology

From the Latinized form of Antonio Stradivari's surname.

3. NOUN

/ ˌstrædɪˈvɛriəs / (AmE), / ˌstrædɪˈvɛːriəs / (BrE)
A string instrument made by Antonio Stradivari, especially a violin, renowned for its exceptional quality and high value.
string instrument made by Stradivari

Examples

She played a Stradivarius at the concert, and the audience was captivated by its sound.
The auction house sold a rare Stradivarius for over ten million dollars.
Owning a Stradivarius is a dream for many professional violinists.

Synset Examples

The museum displayed a collection of historic Stradivarius instruments.
Her Stradivarius violin was worth millions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Guarneri - string instruments made by the Guarneri family of luthiers, also highly regarded

Etymology

From Italian Stradivari (surname of Antonio Stradivari, 1644–1737), with Latinized ending -us.
