Parana

1. PROPN

/ ˌpɑːrəˈnɑː / (AmE), / ˌparəˈnɑː / (BrE)
a major river in South America, flowing through Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina before joining the Rio de la Plata
major South American river

Examples

The Parana River is essential for trade and transportation in the region.
Flooding along the Parana has affected several riverside communities this season.
Scientists are studying fish populations in the Parana to assess environmental changes.

Etymology

From Guarani 'Paraná' meaning 'like the sea,' referring to its breadth.
