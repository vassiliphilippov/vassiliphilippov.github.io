belittling

1. NOUN

/ bəˈlɪtəlɪŋ / (AmE), / bɪˈlɪtlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making someone or something seem unimportant or of little value
the act of belittling

Examples

Her constant belittling of her colleagues created a negative atmosphere in the office.
The review's belittling of the artist's work was harsh and unfair.
He could not stand the belittling he received from his peers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disparagement - the act of speaking about someone in a negative or belittling way
  • Antonym: praise - the expression of approval or admiration for someone or something

2. NOUN

/ bɪˈlɪt.lɪŋ / (AmE), / bɪˈlɪt.lɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of making someone or something seem unimportant or unworthy of consideration.
the act of making seem unimportant

Examples

His constant belittling of her work undermined her confidence.
Teachers should avoid any form of belittling when giving feedback to students.
The meeting quickly turned sour due to his belittling comments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demeaning - the act of causing someone to lose their dignity or importance

3. ADJ

/ bəˈlɪt.əl.ɪŋ / (AmE), / bɪˈlɪt.lɪŋ / (BrE)
expressing criticism or making someone or something seem unimportant or less valuable
making seem less important

Examples

Her belittling remarks hurt his confidence during the meeting.
He disliked the belittling tone she used when talking to her peers.
The manager's belittling comments made the team feel undervalued.

Synset Examples

belittling comments
managed a deprecating smile at the compliment
deprecatory remarks about the book
a slighting remark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deprecating - expressing disapproval; criticizing or belittling
  • Antonym: praising - expressing approval or admiration

Etymology

From 'belittle' (late 18th century), formed from 'be-' (as an intensifier) + 'little'.
