indestructibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪˌstrʌktəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˌstrʌktəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being impossible to destroy or damage
quality of being impossible to destroy

Examples

Scientists marveled at the indestructibility of the ancient structure, which survived centuries of earthquakes.
Superheroes in comics are often portrayed with indestructibility as one of their unique powers.
The company's reputation for indestructibility helped it survive numerous economic crises over the decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: durability - the ability to withstand wear, pressure, or damage
  • Antonym: fragility - the quality of being easily broken or damaged

Etymology

From Latin 'indestructibilis' (unable to be destroyed), with the suffix '-ity'.
