computing

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpjutɪŋ / (AmE), / kəmˈpjuːtɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of performing calculations or solving problems, especially using computers or mathematical methods
performing calculations with computers

Examples

Advances in computing have transformed the way we analyze big data.
The field of quantum computing is rapidly developing new possibilities for technology.
She is studying computing at university to become a software engineer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calculation - the process of using mathematics; careful thinking or planning

Etymology

From Latin 'computare' (to compute, to count), via compute + -ing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00870588-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ kəmˈpjut̬ɪŋ / (AmE), / kəmˈpjuːtɪŋ / (BrE)
the study or use of computers and computer systems for processing information, especially as an academic or professional field
the study or use of computers

Examples

She decided to pursue a career in computing after developing a passion for technology.
Advances in computing have transformed the way we communicate and work.
The university offers several courses in computing and information systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: computer science - the study of the principles and use of computers

Etymology

From compute + -ing, meaning the process or activity of calculation, later applied to computers.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06138021-n
Synset POS: n
