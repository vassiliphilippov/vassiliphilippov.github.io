bitstock

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪt.stɑk / (AmE), / ˈbɪt.stɒk / (BrE)
a carpenter's tool with a crank handle, used to hold and turn a bit for boring holes in wood
carpenter's boring tool with crank handle

Examples

The carpenter reached for his bitstock to drill a hole in the wooden beam.
Older workshops often display a collection of hand tools, including a bitstock and a range of bits.
By attaching a different bit to the bitstock, he could easily adjust to the size of hole required.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brace - a similar tool used for boring holes, often with a U-shaped crank
