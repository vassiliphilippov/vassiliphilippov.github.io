Felicia amelloides

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlɪʃə əˌmɛlˈɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / fəˈlɪsiə əˌmɛlˈɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A hairy subshrub native to South Africa or Australia, with daisylike flowers that have blue petals.
a South African/Australian plant with blue daisy-like flowers

Examples

Gardeners often choose Felicia amelloides for its vibrant blue flowers.
The Felicia amelloides thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
A border of Felicia amelloides brightened the edge of the walkway.

Etymology

From Latinized form of the genus name Felicia and species name amelloides, meaning 'like Amellus' (another genus of flowering plants).
