exonerated

1. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzɑːnərˌeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɒnəˌreɪtɪd / (BrE)
freed from blame or found not guilty of a wrongdoing or accusation
freed from blame or guilt

Examples

He was finally exonerated after new evidence proved his innocence.
The court exonerated her of all charges related to the incident.
Thanks to the diligent investigation, the wrongly accused employee was exonerated.

Synset Examples

is absolved from all blame
was now clear of the charge of cowardice
his official honor is vindicated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absolved - freed from blame or responsibility
  • Synonym: vindicated - cleared of blame or suspicion
  • Antonym: convicted - declared to be guilty of a criminal offense

Etymology

From Latin exonerare (to unload, to free from a burden), from ex- (out) + onus (burden).
