to supersede

1. VERB

/ suː.pɚˈsiːd / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈsiːd / (BrE)
to take the place of (someone or something previously in authority or use); to replace or displace.
to replace or take the place of

Examples

Many believe that online learning will supersede traditional classroom education in the future.
The older regulations were superseded by a more comprehensive policy last year.
Digital cameras have almost completely superseded film photography in recent decades.

Synset Examples

Smith replaced Miller as CEO after Miller left
the computer has supplanted the slide rule
Mary replaced Susan as the team's captain and the highest-ranked player in the school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to replace - to take the place of something or someone
  • Synonym: to displace - to cause to move from its usual place or position

Etymology

From Latin supersedēre (to sit above, be superior to, refrain from).
