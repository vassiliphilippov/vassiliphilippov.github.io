Sun

1. NOUN

/ sʌn / (AmE), / sʌn / (BrE)
the star at the center of the solar system that gives light and heat to the Earth and other planets
the star at the center of the solar system

Examples

The Sun rises in the east and sets in the west.
Without the Sun, life on Earth would not exist.
Astronomers study the Sun to understand how stars work.

Synset Examples

the sun contains 99.85% of the mass in the solar system
the Earth revolves around the Sun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: star - a luminous ball of gas in space

Etymology

From Old English sunne, related to German Sonne and Latin sol.
