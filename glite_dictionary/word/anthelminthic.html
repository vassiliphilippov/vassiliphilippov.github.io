anthelminthic

1. NOUN

/ ˌænθɛlˈmɪnθɪk / (AmE), / ˌænθɛlˈmɪnθɪk / (BrE)
a medicine or substance that destroys or helps expel parasitic intestinal worms from the body
medicine against parasitic worms
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an anthelminthic to treat the child's intestinal infection.
Farmers routinely give anthelminthic medications to livestock to prevent worm infestations.
New research is focused on developing more effective anthelminthic drugs with fewer side effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiparasitic - a drug or agent that kills or inhibits parasites

Etymology

From Greek 'anti-' (against) + 'helmins, helminthos' (worm), via modern scientific Latin.

2. ADJ

/ ˌæntɪlˈmɪnθɪk / (AmE), / ˌantɪlˈmɪnθɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance) capable of expelling or destroying parasitic worms, especially those in the intestines.
destroys or expels parasitic worms
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an anthelminthic medication to treat the patient's intestinal parasites.
This anthelminthic drug is effective against a range of parasitic worms in livestock.
Routine use of anthelminthic agents has greatly reduced the incidence of worm infections in many countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vermifuge - a medicine that expels worms from the body
  • Synonym: antiparasitic - destroying or inhibiting parasites

Etymology

From Greek anti- (against) + helmins, helminthos (worm).
