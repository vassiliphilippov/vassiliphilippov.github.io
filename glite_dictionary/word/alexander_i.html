Alexander I

1. PROPN

/ ˌæləgˈzændər ðə fɜrst / (AmE), / ˌalɪgˈzandə ði ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
the Emperor of Russia from 1801 to 1825, known for his initially liberal reforms and for leading Russia during the Napoleonic Wars
Russian emperor (1801–1825)

Examples

Alexander I ruled Russia during a time of great upheaval and conflict with Napoleon.
The reforms introduced by Alexander I were considered progressive for his era.
Historians debate the motives behind Alexander I's foreign policy decisions.
