motmot

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑtˌmɑt / (AmE), / ˈmɒt.mɒt / (BrE)
A tropical American bird with greenish and bluish plumage, known for its racket-shaped tail feathers.
tropical American bird with colorful plumage

Examples

We spotted a vibrant motmot perched on a branch during our rainforest hike.
The motmot uses its unique tail feathers for communication and display.
Motmots are easily recognized by the beautiful colors of their plumage.

Etymology

From Spanish 'motmot' or 'momoto', from indigenous Central American languages.
