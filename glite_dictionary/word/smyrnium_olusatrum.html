Smyrnium olusatrum

1. NOUN

/ smɪrniəm ˌɔləsˈætrəm / (AmE), / ˈsmɜːniəm ˌɒlʊˈsætrəm / (BrE)
A European plant, also known as alexanders, with celery-like leaves, often grown as a potherb and naturalized in coastal Britain.
European herb, also called alexanders

Examples

The leaves of Smyrnium olusatrum can be used in salads or cooked as a vegetable.
Botanists found a large patch of Smyrnium olusatrum growing near the English coastline.
Many gardeners cultivate Smyrnium olusatrum in herb gardens for its edible stems and leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alexanders - Alternative common name for Smyrnium olusatrum

Etymology

From Latin 'Smyrnium' (plant name) and 'olusatrum' ('black herb' or 'garden herb').
