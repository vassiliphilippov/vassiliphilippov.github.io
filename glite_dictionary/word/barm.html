barm

1. NOUN

/ bɑrm / (AmE), / bɑːm / (BrE)
a commercial leavening agent containing yeast used for fermenting alcoholic beverages or raising dough in bread-making
leavening agent with yeast; used in bread and brewing

Examples

The baker added a spoonful of barm to the dough to help it rise.
Traditional recipes for ale often call for fresh barm as a fermenting agent.
After skimming the barm from the beer, he used it to bake a light, airy loaf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yeast - microscopic fungus used in leavening and fermentation

Etymology

Old English *beorma* (yeast, leaven); related to fermentation.
