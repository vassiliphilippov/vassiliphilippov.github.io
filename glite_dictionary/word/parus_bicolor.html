Parus bicolor

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛər.əs ˈbaɪ.kə.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈpaː.rəs ˈbɪ.kə.lə / (BrE)
A crested titmouse of eastern and midwestern United States, also known as the tufted titmouse.
a crested titmouse of eastern U.S.
medical

Examples

Birdwatchers in Ohio often spot the Parus bicolor among oak trees in the spring.
The Parus bicolor is easily recognized by its distinctive crest and gray plumage.
Studying the feeding habits of Parus bicolor can reveal important insights into forest ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tufted titmouse - common name for Parus bicolor

Etymology

From Latin: 'parus' meaning 'titmouse' and 'bicolor' meaning 'two-colored'.
