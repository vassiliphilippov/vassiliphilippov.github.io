conglobation

1. NOUN

/ kənˌɡlɑːˈbeɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌɡlɒˈbeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of gathering various things into a rounded mass; a mass formed by such gathering
aggregation into a rounded mass

Examples

The conglobation of dust and debris under the furniture created a sizeable ball.
Geological processes often result in the conglobation of minerals into nodules or concretions.
Her art piece was a conglobation of various materials, molded into a single rounded form.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aggregation - the process of collecting or forming into a mass or whole
  • Antonym: dispersion - the process of distributing things over a wide area

Etymology

From Latin conglobatio (a gathering into a ball), from conglobare (to form into a ball).

2. NOUN

/ kɑnˌɡloʊˈbeɪʃən / (AmE), / kɒnˌɡlɒˈbeɪʃən / (BrE)
A rounded, spherical form or aggregation; the process or result of forming into a spherical mass.
rounded, spherical form

Examples

The mineral sample exhibited a striking conglobation, standing out among the angular crystals.
The conglobation of the paint droplets gave the surface a unique texture.
Biologists observed the conglobation of cells during the early stages of development.

Etymology

From Latin conglobatio, from conglobare (to form into a ball), from con- (together) + globus (ball).
