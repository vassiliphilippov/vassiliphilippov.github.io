corroboratory

1. ADJ

/ kəˈɹɑːbəˌɹɛˌtɔɹi / (AmE), / kəˈɹɒb.ər.ə.tɔː.ri / (BrE)
serving to support or confirm something; tending to corroborate or strengthen a statement, finding, or piece of evidence.
supporting or confirming

Examples

The scientist provided corroboratory data to strengthen her hypothesis.
These findings are corroboratory and help confirm the original report.
The lawyer introduced corroboratory evidence that supported the witness's testimony.

Synset Examples

collateral evidence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corroborative - serving to corroborate; confirming

Etymology

From Latin 'corroboratus', past participle of 'corroborare' (to strengthen), plus English '-ory'.
