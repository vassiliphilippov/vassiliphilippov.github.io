Leucogenes

1. NOUN

/ luːˈkɑːdʒəˌniːz / (AmE), / luːˈkəʊdʒɪˌniːz / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the daisy family endemic to New Zealand, commonly known as New Zealand edelweiss.
genus of plants from New Zealand

Examples

The alpine gardens feature several species of Leucogenes native to New Zealand.
Botanists are studying how Leucogenes adapts to harsh mountain climates.
Tourists often mistake Leucogenes for the more familiar European edelweiss.

Etymology

From Greek 'leukos' (white) and 'genes' (born, produced), referring to the white, woolly appearance of the plant.
