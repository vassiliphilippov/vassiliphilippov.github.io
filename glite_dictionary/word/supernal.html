supernal

1. ADJ

/ suˈpɜrnəl / (AmE), / suˈpɜːnəl / (BrE)
relating to the heavens or something divine; celestial or heavenly.
celestial; heavenly; divine

Examples

The cathedral was filled with a supernal light that seemed to come from no earthly source.
The choir's voices blended in a supernal harmony that left the audience speechless.
Poets often describe the night sky in supernal terms, as if it belonged to another realm.

Synset Examples

celestial peace
ethereal melodies
the supernal happiness of a quiet death

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celestial - relating to the sky or heavens
  • Antonym: mundane - relating to the earthly or worldly, not spiritual

Etymology

From Latin supernus (above, heavenly), from super (above).

2. ADJ

/ suːˈpɜrnəl / (AmE), / suːˈpɜːnəl / (BrE)
relating to the heavens or something divine; coming from above or from a celestial realm
heavenly, from above or divine

Examples

The painting was admired for its supernal beauty, evoking a sense of awe and wonder.
Ancient texts often describe gods as possessing supernal powers beyond human comprehension.
The choir's voices had a supernal quality that moved the entire audience.

Synset Examples

interpret the plague as a visitation from heaven, a supernal punishment for the sins of men

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celestial - relating to the sky or heavens
  • Antonym: terrestrial - relating to the earth

Etymology

From Latin supernus (situated above, celestial).
