dissimilar

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɪmələr / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪmɪlə / (BrE)
not like each other; different in appearance, nature, or quality
not similar; different

Examples

The two cultures have dissimilar traditions and customs.
Her views on education are quite dissimilar from those of her colleagues.
The results from the two experiments were surprisingly dissimilar.

Synset Examples

a group of very dissimilar people
a pump not dissimilar to those once found on every farm
their understanding of the world is not so dissimilar from our own
took different (or dissimilar) approaches to the problem

Related Terms

  • Antonym: similar - having a resemblance in appearance, character, or quantity, without being identical

Etymology

From Latin 'dissimilis' (unlike, different), from 'dis-' (apart) + 'similis' (like).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɪmələr / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪmɪlə / (BrE)
not alike; different in nature, appearance, or quality
not alike; different

Examples

The twins had dissimilar interests and pursued completely different careers.
Their houses are so dissimilar that you wouldn't guess they're on the same street.
The two paintings are strikingly dissimilar in style and composition.

Synset Examples

as unalike as two people could be

Related Terms

  • Antonym: similar - having a resemblance or likeness

Etymology

From Latin 'dissimilis', from 'dis-' (apart, opposite of) + 'similis' (like).
