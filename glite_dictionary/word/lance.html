lance

1. VERB

/ læns / (AmE), / lɑːns / (BrE)
to cut open with a sharp instrument, especially to release pus or fluid
cut open with a sharp instrument

Examples

The doctor decided to lance the abscess to relieve the pain.
It is important to lance a boil properly to prevent infection.
A specialist should lance the blister instead of draining it at home.

Synset Examples

lance a boil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to incise - to cut into (especially surgically)
  • Synonym: to pierce - to force a way into or through; penetrate

Etymology

From Old French *lance* (lance, spear), from Latin *lancea* (light spear); the verb developed from the idea of piercing.
