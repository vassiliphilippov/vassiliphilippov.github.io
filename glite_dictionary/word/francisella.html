Francisella

1. NOUN

/ ˌfræn.sɪˈsɛl.ə / (AmE), / ˌfræn.sɪˈsɛl.ə / (BrE)
A genus of Gram-negative aerobic bacteria, some species of which are pathogenic and parasitic in animals and humans.
genus of Gram-negative bacteria

Examples

Francisella tularensis is responsible for causing the infectious disease tularemia.
Scientists discovered a new strain of Francisella in wild rodents.
Many species of Francisella are studied due to their potential threat to human health.

Etymology

Named after American bacteriologist Edward Francis.

2. NOUN

/ ˌfræn.sɪˈsɛl.ə / (AmE), / ˌfræn.sɪˈsel.ə / (BrE)
A genus of Gram-negative, aerobic bacteria, some species of which are pathogenic to humans and other animals.
genus of pathogenic Gram-negative bacteria
medical

Examples

Francisella tularensis is the bacterium responsible for the disease tularemia.
The laboratory confirmed the presence of Francisella in the blood sample.
Some species in the genus Francisella are known to infect both animals and humans.

Etymology

Named after American bacteriologist Edward Francis (1872–1957).
