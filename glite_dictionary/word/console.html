console

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsoʊl / (AmE), / kənˈsəʊl / (BrE)
a small, narrow table fixed to a wall or designed to stand against a wall, often used for decorative purposes or to hold objects such as a vase or lamp
small decorative table for a wall

Examples

The antique console in the hallway held a vase of fresh flowers.
She placed her keys on the console table by the front door.
A marble-topped console added elegance to the living room décor.

Etymology

From French 'console', originally from Italian 'consola', meaning a supporting bracket or ledge.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03097339-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.soʊl / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.səʊl / (BrE)
a cabinet or housing designed to accommodate electronic equipment such as a radio, television, or stereo system
cabinet for electronic equipment

Examples

She placed the new flat-screen TV on the wooden console in the living room.
The old stereo system came built into a large console with glass doors.
He repaired the radio console so it could play music again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabinet - a piece of furniture with shelves or drawers used for storage

Etymology

From French *console*, meaning a bracket or support; the usage broadened to furniture and equipment housing in the early 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02936724-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ kənˈsoʊl / (AmE), / kənˈsəʊl / (BrE)
an ornamental bracket, often scroll-shaped, used especially to support a wall fixture, shelf, or bust
decorative bracket or wall support

Examples

The marble statue was displayed on an intricately carved console in the foyer.
A gilded console supported a pair of antique candelabras on each side of the fireplace.
They admired the elegant console holding a vase beneath the grand staircase.

Synset Examples

the bust of Napoleon stood on a console

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bracket - a projecting support for a shelf or other weight

Etymology

From French 'console', from Italian 'consola', from Latin 'consolidare' (to make firm).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03096932-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ kənˈsoʊl / (AmE), / kənˈsəʊl / (BrE)
to comfort someone who is feeling sad or disappointed
to comfort someone

Examples

Nothing I said could console her after she lost her dog.
Friends gathered to console him during the difficult time.
He tried to console his daughter by telling her everything would be alright.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comfort - to make someone feel less sad or worried
  • Antonym: distress - to cause someone to feel upset or anxious

Etymology

From Latin consolari, from con- (together) + solari (to comfort).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01818782-v
Synset POS: v

5. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnˌsoʊl / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.səʊl / (BrE)
a panel or unit with controls, displays, and input devices used for monitoring and operating a system, especially a computer or technical system
control panel for technical systems

Examples

The technician sat at the console monitoring the progress of the experiment.
Each game system comes with its own console for the user to interact with.
She quickly typed commands into the main console to override the system error.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: control panel - an interface with controls for operating a machine or system
  • Synonym: dashboard - an information display panel for monitoring systems

Etymology

From French *console* (support, control panel), from Italian *consola*, originally meaning a support or bracket.

Source: WordNet
