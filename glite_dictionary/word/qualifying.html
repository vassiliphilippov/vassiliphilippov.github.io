qualifying

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑlɪˌfaɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkwɒlɪˌfaɪɪŋ / (BrE)
the grammatical function of modifying or limiting the meaning of another word in a phrase, such as an adjective qualifying a noun
grammatical function of modification

Examples

In the sentence 'the qualifying adjective altered the meaning of the noun,' the role of 'qualifying' is clear.
He explained the qualifying relationship between adjectives and nouns in English grammar.
A qualifying clause can add essential information to a sentence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modifier - a word or phrase that qualifies or describes another word
  • Synonym: restricting - having the effect of limiting or narrowing meaning

Etymology

From Latin qualificare, from qualis (of what kind) + facere (to make).
