kail

1. NOUN

/ keɪl / (AmE), / keɪl / (BrE)
A hardy variety of cabbage with coarse, curly leaves that do not form a compact head; also known as kale.
type of hardy cabbage

Examples

The garden was full of green kail growing in neat rows.
She used fresh kail to make a traditional Scottish soup.
Farmers in the region often grow kail because it thrives in cool climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kale - a hardy variety of cabbage with curled leaves; variant spelling and synonym

Etymology

From Scots *kail*, related to Old Norse *kál* (cabbage).

2. NOUN

/ keɪl / (AmE), / keɪl / (BrE)
A variety of cabbage with coarse, curly leaves, often used in cooking, especially in Scotland.
curly-leafed cabbage variety

Examples

She added chopped kail to the soup for extra flavor.
The farmer's market sells several kinds of kail in winter.
In Scotland, kail is a traditional ingredient in many hearty dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kale - A similar variety of cabbage with curly leaves

Etymology

From Scots kail, from Old Norse kál (cabbage).
