yodel

1. NOUN

/ ˈjoʊ.dəl / (AmE), / ˈjəʊ.dəl / (BrE)
a songlike call or cry in which the voice quickly switches between the normal register and falsetto, typical in certain styles of music, especially in the Swiss Alps
songlike cry alternating chest and falsetto voice

Examples

The echo of a distant yodel could be heard across the mountain valley.
She practiced her yodel until it was clear and melodic.
During the festival, several performers impressed the crowd with a traditional Swiss yodel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yodeling - the act or process of producing yodel calls

Etymology

From German 'jodeln', meaning 'to sing by yodeling'.

2. VERB

/ ˈjoʊdəl / (AmE), / ˈjəʊdəl / (BrE)
to sing by rapidly switching between the normal voice and a high falsetto, producing a distinctive warbling sound, especially as done in Alpine folk music
sing with rapid changes of pitch (as in Alpine folk music)

Examples

The singer began to yodel in the middle of her performance, amazing the crowd.
My grandfather used to yodel when we hiked in the mountains together.
He learned how to yodel during his trip to Switzerland.

Synset Examples

The Austrians were yodeling in the mountains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sing - to produce musical tones with the voice

Etymology

From German 'jodeln', meaning to sing by yodeling, related to 'jo' (an exclamation of joy).

Synset POS: v
