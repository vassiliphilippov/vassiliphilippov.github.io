brilliant

1. ADJ

/ ˈbrɪljənt / (AmE), / ˈbrɪljənt / (BrE)
Exceptionally clever, impressive, or of surpassing excellence.
exceptionally impressive or excellent

Examples

She gave a brilliant lecture that captivated the entire audience.
His brilliant solution solved the problem nobody else could figure out.
The pianist's brilliant performance received a standing ovation.

Synset Examples

a brilliant performance
a superb actor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superb - of the highest quality; excellent
  • Antonym: mediocre - of only average quality; not very good

Etymology

From French *brillant*, from present participle of *briller* 'to shine', ultimately from Latin *beryllus* (beryl, a precious stone).
