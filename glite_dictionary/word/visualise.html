to visualise

1. VERB

/ ˈvɪʒuəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒuəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make an internal part of the body visible, especially by means of an X-ray or similar scanning technique
make visible by medical imaging
medical british

Examples

The doctor was able to visualise the tumour using a CT scan.
Modern MRI machines can visualise areas of the brain in great detail.
The procedure allows surgeons to visualise blood vessels before operating.

Synset Examples

The radiologist can visualize the cancerous liver

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to visualize - to make an internal part of the body visible, especially by means of an X-ray or similar scanning technique (American spelling)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02155824-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈvɪʒəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒuəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something visible, especially by means of an aid or technique; to render visible what is normally hidden or abstract
make something visible, often with techniques
british

Examples

With modern MRI technology, doctors can visualise the structure of the brain in great detail.
Special dyes are used to visualise the different tissues during the procedure.
It is difficult to visualise the tiny blood vessels without advanced imaging techniques.

Synset Examples

With this machine, ultrasound can be visualized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to visualize - make visible; American English spelling

Etymology

From French 'visualiser', from 'visuel' (visual), from Latin 'videre' (to see).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00582282-v
Synset POS: v

/ ˈvɪʒuəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒuəlaɪz / (BrE)
to form a mental image of something; to imagine or see something in your mind
form a mental image; imagine
british

Examples

Athletes often visualise their performance before a big competition.
Can you visualise what your ideal home would look like?

Synset Examples

Related Terms
