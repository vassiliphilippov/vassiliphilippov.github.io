moshav

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈʃɑv / (AmE), / məʊˈʃɑːv / (BrE)
A cooperative Israeli village or settlement consisting of small individual farms, with shared agricultural or economic arrangements.
Israeli cooperative farming village

Examples

After moving to Israel, they settled in a moshav where they ran a small dairy farm.
The moshav community worked together but each family owned its own plot of land.
Unlike the kibbutz, a moshav allows for more private ownership of property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kibbutz - a communal Israeli farm or settlement

Etymology

From Modern Hebrew מושב (moshav, meaning 'settlement' or 'seat').
