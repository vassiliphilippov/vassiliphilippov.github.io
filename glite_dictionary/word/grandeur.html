grandeur

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrændʒər / (AmE), / ˈɡrændjə / (BrE)
the quality of being impressive or great in character, ideals, or conduct
impressive greatness of character or ideals

Examples

The grandeur of her vision inspired everyone in the room.
He approached every challenge with a moral grandeur that earned him respect.
The speech was marked by a certain grandeur of thought and purpose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: majesty - the quality of being stately, dignified, and grand
  • Antonym: humility - the quality of having a modest or low view of one's importance

Etymology

From Old French 'grandeur', from Latin 'grandis' (great).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡræn.dʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡræn.djʊə / (BrE)
impressive beauty, power, or size that inspires admiration or awe
impressive beauty or magnificence

Examples

The palace was renowned for its grandeur and breathtaking architecture.
Even the simplest ceremony was marked by a sense of grandeur and tradition.
He tried to recapture the lost grandeur of his family's estate.
