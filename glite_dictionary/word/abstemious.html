abstemious

1. ADJ

/ əbˈstiːmiəs / (AmE), / əbˈstiːmɪəs / (BrE)
Not allowing oneself much in the way of food or drink; moderate or self-restrained, especially in eating and drinking.
moderate, self-restrained (food/drink)

Examples

Despite the feast laid before him, John remained abstemious and ate only a small portion.
Her abstemious habits contributed greatly to her good health and longevity.
The monks led abstemious lives, avoiding excess in every aspect of their daily routine.

Synset Examples

the pleasures of the table, never of much consequence to one naturally abstemious

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temperate - showing moderation or self-restraint, especially with alcohol or food

Etymology

From Latin 'abstemius' (sober, abstaining from wine).

2. ADJ

/ æbˈstiːmiəs / (AmE), / æbˈstiːmiəs / (BrE)
not allowing yourself much in the way of food or drink; marked by moderation or self-restraint, especially in eating and drinking.
marked by self-restraint in eating or drinking

Examples

He led an abstemious lifestyle, rarely indulging in rich foods or alcohol.
Even during celebrations, she remained abstemious and declined a second glass of wine.
Doctors often recommend an abstemious diet to patients with certain health conditions.

Synset Examples

abstemious with the use of adverbs
a light eater
a light smoker
ate a light supper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temperate - showing moderation or self-restraint, especially in eating and drinking
  • Antonym: indulgent - allowing more than is necessary or healthy

Etymology

From Latin abstemius (sober, refraining from wine), from ab- (from) + temetum (strong drink).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02410587-s
Synset POS: s
