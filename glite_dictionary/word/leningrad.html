Leningrad

1. PROPN

/ ˈlɛnɪnˌɡræd / (AmE), / ˈlɛnɪnˌɡræd / (BrE)
the former name of Saint Petersburg, a major city in the northwest of Russia, used from 1924 to 1991
former name of Saint Petersburg, Russia

Examples

The siege of Leningrad during World War II lasted nearly 900 days.
Many famous Russian writers and artists lived in Leningrad in the 20th century.
After 1991, Leningrad was officially renamed Saint Petersburg.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saint Petersburg - current name of the Russian city previously called Leningrad

Etymology

From Lenin + Russian grad (city), named after Vladimir Lenin following his death in 1924.
