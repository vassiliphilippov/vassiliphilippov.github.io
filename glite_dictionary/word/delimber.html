delimber

1. NOUN

/ diˈlɪmbər / (AmE), / diˈlɪmbə / (BrE)
a machine that removes branches from felled trees, typically using a saw or rotating chains
machine for removing tree branches

Examples

The delimber processed dozens of logs every hour at the lumber mill.
Using a delimber improves efficiency and safety in modern forestry operations.
After the tree was felled, a delimber was brought in to strip away the branches quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debarker - a machine for removing bark from logs

Etymology

From 'de-' (removing) + 'limb' + '-er' (agent noun suffix), indicating a device that removes limbs.
