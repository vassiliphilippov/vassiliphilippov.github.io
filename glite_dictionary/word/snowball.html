6. VERB

/ ˈsnoʊˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌbɔːl / (BrE)
(of something) to increase or accumulate rapidly and at an accelerating rate, especially so as to become difficult to control.
increase rapidly and uncontrollably

Examples

The company's debt began to snowball after several bad investments.
Rumors about the incident quickly snowballed into widespread panic.
If we don't address the problem now, it will only snowball and become harder to solve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to escalate - to become or make something greater, more serious, or more intense
  • Synonym: to accumulate - to gather or amass a quantity over time

Etymology

From the noun 'snowball', referring to its growth as it rolls, + verb formation (mid-19th century).
