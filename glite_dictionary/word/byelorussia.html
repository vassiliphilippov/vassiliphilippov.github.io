Byelorussia

1. PROPN

/ ˌbaɪjəloʊˈrʌʃə / (AmE), / ˌbajələˈrʌsɪə / (BrE)
the former name for the modern country of Belarus, used especially during the Soviet era.
former name for Belarus (country)

Examples

Many historical documents refer to Byelorussia rather than Belarus.
After World War II, Byelorussia became one of the founding members of the United Nations.
My grandfather was born in Byelorussia before it gained independence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Belarus - modern European country formerly called Byelorussia

Etymology

From Russian 'Byelorussiya', meaning 'White Russia'.
