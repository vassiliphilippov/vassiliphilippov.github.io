Ceratopogonidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛrətoʊpəˈɡɒnɪdi / (AmE), / ˌsɛrətəʊpəˈɡɒnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of tiny, bloodsucking flies commonly known as biting midges or sand flies, some of which can transmit diseases to humans and animals.
a family of biting midges or sand flies

Examples

On summer evenings, Ceratopogonidae can become a serious nuisance near wetlands.
Some species of Ceratopogonidae are known to transmit animal diseases.
The tiny Ceratopogonidae are often mistaken for harmless gnats until they start biting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biting midge - a small fly that bites and feeds on blood; a common name for Ceratopogonidae
  • Synonym: sand fly - another common name for certain Ceratopogonidae species

Etymology

From Greek keras (horn) + pygon (rump) + -idae (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).
