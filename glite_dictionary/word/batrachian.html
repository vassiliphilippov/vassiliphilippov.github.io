batrachian

1. ADJ

/ bəˈtreɪkiən / (AmE), / bəˈtreɪkɪən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of frogs and toads.
relating to frogs and toads

Examples

The scientist gave a lecture on batrachian species found in the Amazon rainforest.
The pond was home to a variety of batrachian creatures, including frogs and toads.
Her thesis focused on the unique adaptations in batrachian skin for moisture retention.

Etymology

From Greek batrakhos (frog).

2. NOUN

/ bəˈtreɪkiən / (AmE), / bəˈtreɪkɪən / (BrE)
any amphibian of the group that includes frogs and toads, typically tailless and having long hind limbs for leaping
frog or toad; tailless amphibian

Examples

The biologist spent years studying various species of batrachian in the Amazon rainforest.
Unlike most reptiles, the batrachian thrives in both aquatic and terrestrial environments.
The exhibition featured a rare batrachian specimen discovered in Madagascar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frog - a tailless amphibian with long hind legs
  • Synonym: toad - a tailless amphibian, usually with dry, warty skin

Etymology

From Greek batrakhos (frog).
