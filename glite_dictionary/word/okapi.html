okapi

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈkɑːpi / (AmE), / əʊˈkɑːpi / (BrE)
A large mammal native to central Africa, related to the giraffe but with a shorter neck and zebra-like stripes on its legs.
African mammal related to giraffe

Examples

The okapi is found only in the rainforests of the Democratic Republic of Congo.
Despite its zebra-like stripes, the okapi is actually more closely related to the giraffe.
Zoos around the world work to protect the endangered okapi from extinction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giraffe - A tall African mammal with a very long neck and long legs

Etymology

From scientific Latin Okapia, named after the animal's native name in Congo (Wambuba okapi).

2. NOUN

/ oʊˈkɑːpi / (AmE), / əʊˈkɑːpi / (BrE)
A large mammal of the giraffe family from the rainforests of central Africa, smaller than a giraffe and having a short neck and zebra-like stripes on its legs.
forest mammal related to the giraffe

Examples

The okapi is native to the dense rainforests of the Democratic Republic of the Congo.
With its striped legs, the okapi can easily be mistaken for a relative of the zebra.
Zookeepers closely monitor the diet of the okapi to ensure its health in captivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giraffe - a tall African mammal with a very long neck and legs

Etymology

From Mvuba (Luba-Kasai language of Congo) *okapi*.
