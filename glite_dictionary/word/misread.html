to misread

1. VERB

/ mɪsˈriːd / (AmE), / mɪsˈriːd / (BrE)
to interpret or understand something incorrectly; to make a mistake in comprehending written, spoken, or symbolic information
interpret or understand incorrectly

Examples

She apologized after she misread his intentions at the meeting.
If you misread the instructions, the experiment may not work correctly.
He realized too late that he had misread her silence as agreement.

Synset Examples

I misread Hamlet all my life!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to misinterpret - to interpret something wrongly or incorrectly
  • Antonym: to interpret - to explain the meaning of something

Etymology

From 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'read'.

2. VERB

/ mɪsˈrɛd, ˈmɪsˌriːd / (AmE), / mɪsˈriːd, mɪsˈrɛd / (BrE)
to read or interpret something wrongly or incorrectly
read or interpret wrongly

Examples

She misread the instructions and made an error in the recipe.
He often misreads people's intentions, leading to misunderstandings.
I realized I had misread the schedule after I arrived at the wrong time.

Synset Examples

He misread the data

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to misinterpret - to interpret something wrongly
  • Synonym: to misunderstand - to fail to understand correctly
  • Antonym: to understand - to comprehend correctly

Etymology

Formed from 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'read'.

WordNet ID: oewn-00629355-v
Synset POS: v
