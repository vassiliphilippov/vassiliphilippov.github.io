combinatorial

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːmbɪnəˈtɔːriəl / (AmE), / ˌkɒmbɪnəˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
relating to or involving combinations, especially as applied to mathematics, problems, or methods involving the arrangement or selection of objects.
relating to combinations or arrangements

Examples

The scientist proposed a combinatorial approach to analyze the vast amount of genetic data.
Solving the puzzle requires combinatorial reasoning to consider all possible choices.
Researchers are studying the combinatorial complexity of large networks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permutational - relating to different possible arrangements or orders
  • Synonym: combinatorics - the branch of mathematics dealing with combinations and arrangements

Etymology

From combination + -al; influenced by the field name combinatorics.

2. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːmbəˈnɛtɔriəl / (AmE), / ˌkɒmbɪˌneɪˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
relating to or involving the combination and arrangement of elements in sets, especially in mathematics and logic
relating to combinations or arrangements

Examples

The combinatorial possibilities of arranging a deck of cards are astronomical.
He used combinatorial methods to solve the complex puzzle.
Research in combinatorial chemistry has led to the discovery of new drugs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combinative - relating to or tending to combine

Etymology

From Latin combinare (to combine) plus the English adjective suffix -al.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00474524-s
Synset POS: s
