jacksnipe

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒækˌsnaɪp / (AmE), / ˈdʒækˌsnaɪp / (BrE)
A small wading bird found in North America, known for inflating its chest during courtship displays.
small North American wading bird

Examples

The jacksnipe is rarely seen except during its elaborate courtship display.
During the spring, birdwatchers often search wetlands for the elusive jacksnipe.
Unlike other snipe species, the jacksnipe inflates its chest to attract a mate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snipe - a wading bird with a long straight bill

Etymology

Formed from 'jack' (a diminutive or term for something small) and 'snipe' (the species of wading bird).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒækˌsnaɪp / (AmE), / ˈdʒækˌsnaɪp / (BrE)
a small, short-billed snipe (Lymnocryptes minimus) native to Europe and Asia, known for its mottled brown plumage and distinctive zigzag flight
small, short-billed Old World snipe

Examples

We spotted a jacksnipe darting among the reeds at the edge of the marsh.
The jacksnipe is known for its unique bobbing motion while feeding.
During migration season, birdwatchers hope to catch a glimpse of the elusive jacksnipe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snipe - a wading bird with a long slender bill

Etymology

From jack (a diminutive name) + snipe (the bird).
