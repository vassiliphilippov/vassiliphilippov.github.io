phantasmagoric

1. ADJ

/ ˌfæn.tæz.məˈɡɔr.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌfan.taz.məˈɡɒr.ɪk / (BrE)
having a dreamlike, fantastic, and shifting quality, often with surreal or bizarre imagery
fantastic, dreamlike, and surreal

Examples

The artist's new exhibition is filled with phantasmagoric scenes that seem to come straight from a dream.
Nightmares often take on a phantasmagoric quality, blending reality and fantasy in disorienting ways.
The movie's phantasmagoric visuals mesmerized audiences and left them questioning what was real.

Synset Examples

a great concourse of phantasmagoric shadows
the incongruous imagery in surreal art and literature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surreal - having the qualities of surrealism; bizarre and dreamlike
  • Antonym: realistic - representing things in a way that is accurate and true to life

Etymology

From French 'fantasmagorique', from 'fantasmagorie' (phantasmagoria), a type of magic lantern show.
