firmness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɝːmnɪs / (AmE), / ˈfɜːmnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being steady or securely and immovably fixed in place
quality of being securely fixed

Examples

The firmness of the bridge's foundation increased everyone's confidence in its safety.
She tested the firmness of the mattress before buying it for her new apartment.
Despite the wind, the tent's firmness kept it standing all night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stability - the state of being stable, firm, or unchanging
  • Antonym: instability - lack of ability to remain steady or fixed

Etymology

From Latin firmus (strong, stable) via Old French fermeté and Middle English firmness.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɝːmnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɜːmnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being solid and not giving way when pressed; the state of being unyielding to the touch
solidity; resistance to pressure

Examples

The firmness of the mattress provided excellent support for his back.
She tested the firmness of the avocado by gently squeezing it.
Dentists often examine the firmness of your gums during a check-up.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: softness - the quality of giving way easily to pressure
  • Synonym: hardness - the quality of being hard and resistant to pressure

Etymology

From Middle English fermnes, from Old French fermeté, from Latin firmus (strong, stable).

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɜrmnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɜːmnəs / (BrE)
the quality of having a strong, healthy muscle tone; the degree to which muscle tissue is solid and resilient
muscle tone quality

Examples

The athlete's daily workouts improved the firmness of his arms and legs.
With age, people may notice a decrease in muscle firmness.
Physical therapists often assess the firmness of muscles to monitor patient progress.

Synset Examples

his muscular firmness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flaccidity - lacking firmness or muscle tone

Etymology

From firm + -ness, dating to Middle English.

4. NOUN

/ ˈfɝːmnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɜːmnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being steadfast and determined in character or action
steadfastness; determination

Examples

Her firmness in difficult situations inspired confidence in everyone around her.
He faced the criticism with firmness and did not waver from his beliefs.
The teacher's firmness helped maintain discipline in the classroom.

Synset Examples

his resoluteness carried him through the battle
it was his unshakeable resolution to finish the work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resolve - strong determination to do something
  • Antonym: flexibility - the ability to change easily or adapt; the opposite of firmness

Etymology

From Old French fermete, from Latin firmitas (strength, stability).
