to demagnetise

1. VERB

/ diˈmæɡnəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / diˈmæɡnɪtaɪz / (BrE)
to remove the magnetic field from a magnetic storage device, typically in order to erase stored data
remove magnetism from a device (erase data)

Examples

The technician had to demagnetise the hard drive before disposing of it.
Old videotapes are often demagnetised to permanently erase their contents.
He used a special tool to demagnetise the floppy disks for reuse.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to magnetise - to make magnetic; to impart magnetic properties

Etymology

From de- (removal) + magnetise, from magnet ('magnet') + -ise (verb suffix).

2. VERB

/ diˈmæɡnɪˌtaɪz / (AmE), / diˈmaɡnɪtʌɪz / (BrE)
to make something nonmagnetic, or remove its magnetic properties
remove magnetic properties

Examples

The technician had to demagnetise the steel rod before using it in the experiment.
Some credit cards stop working if you accidentally demagnetise the strip.
You should regularly demagnetise old cassette player heads to maintain sound quality.

Synset Examples

demagnetize the iron shavings
they degaussed the ship

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to magnetise - to make magnetic; to give magnetic properties
  • Synonym: to degauss - to remove unwanted magnetic fields, especially in devices or ships

Etymology

From de- (removal) + magnetise, after French 'démagnétiser'.
