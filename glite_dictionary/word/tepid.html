tepid

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɛpɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɛpɪd / (BrE)
(of a liquid or temperature) only slightly warm; lukewarm.
moderately warm; lukewarm

Examples

She poured herself a glass of tepid water because she didn't like it cold.
The soup was served tepid, much to the customers’ disappointment.
After the power outage, the shower was only tepid instead of hot.

Synset Examples

he hates lukewarm coffee
tepid bath water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lukewarm - slightly warm; not hot or cold

Etymology

From Latin 'tepidus' (lukewarm), from 'tepere' (to be warm).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɛpɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɛpɪd / (BrE)
showing little enthusiasm or interest; unenthusiastic or lukewarm
showing little enthusiasm or interest

Examples

Her tepid response to the proposal made everyone uncertain about her commitment.
The audience gave only tepid applause after the long speech.
He offered tepid support to the new project, barely expressing any interest.

Synset Examples

a halfhearted effort
gave only lukewarm support to the candidate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lukewarm - showing little enthusiasm or warmth
  • Antonym: enthusiastic - showing intense and eager enjoyment or interest

Etymology

From Latin tepidus (lukewarm), from tepēre (to be warm).
