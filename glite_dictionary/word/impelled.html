impelled

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɛld / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛld / (BrE)
feeling urged or compelled to take action, especially by moral or emotional force
urged or compelled to act

Examples

She felt impelled to speak out against the injustice she witnessed.
Many volunteers are impelled by a sense of duty and compassion.
He was impelled to confess after seeing the consequences of his actions.

Synset Examples

felt impelled to take a stand against the issue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compelled - feeling forced or obliged to act
  • Antonym: restrained - prevented from acting or moving forward

Etymology

From Latin 'impellere' meaning 'to drive on'.
