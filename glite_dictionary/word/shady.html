shady

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃeɪ.di / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪ.di / (BrE)
dishonest or likely to be involved in illegal or immoral activities
dishonest; of dubious legality or morality

Examples

He was known for his shady business dealings, which often skirted the law.
Many people avoided the shady company after rumors spread about their practices.
The politician denied any connection to the shady figures accused of fraud.

Synset Examples

He established a dummy company through which he laundered vast sums of cash from shady middlemen and arms dealers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dishonest - not honest, likely to lie, cheat, or steal
  • Antonym: trustworthy - reliable and deserving of trust or confidence

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃeɪdi / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪdi / (BrE)
(of a place) sheltered from sunlight or covered with shade; pleasantly dark or cool.
full of or sheltered by shade

Examples

We chose a shady bench under the trees to escape the afternoon heat.
The garden is cool and shady, making it perfect for summer picnics.
She preferred the shady side of the street to avoid direct sunlight.

Synset Examples

the shady side of the street
the surface of the pond is dark and shadowed
we sat on rocks in a shadowy cove
cool umbrageous woodlands

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sunny - bright with sunlight; exposed to sunlight
  • Synonym: shadowy - full of or resembling shadows; dim or faintly lit

Etymology

From Middle English 'shadey', from 'shade' + '-y'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈʃeɪdi / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪdi / (BrE)
quiet, dark, or concealed; kept from view or not easily seen, often implying something suspicious or secretive
dark or concealed; secretive

Examples

The suspect's shady background made the investors nervous.
They avoided walking through the shady alleys late at night.
Rumors spread quickly about his shady dealings in the company.

Synset Examples

her shady past intrigued him
a shady part of town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shadowy - dim or faintly visible; full of shadows
  • Antonym: open - not concealed or hidden; easily seen

Etymology

From Middle English 'schad(e)', related to 'shade' + '-y'.
