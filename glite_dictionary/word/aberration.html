aberration

1. NOUN

/ ˌæbəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌabəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a state or condition that is very different from what is normal or expected
something unusual or abnormal

Examples

The sudden drop in temperature was an aberration for the region at this time of year.
His angry outburst was considered an aberration and not typical of his usual calm demeanor.
The scientist explained that the data point was an aberration and did not fit the established pattern.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anomaly - something that deviates from what is standard, normal, or expected
  • Antonym: normality - the state of being usual, typical, or expected

Etymology

From Latin aberratio (a wandering, deviation), from aberrare (to wander away, deviate).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæbəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌabəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
An optical phenomenon in which a lens or mirror fails to produce a clear or accurate image, causing blurring or distortion.
optical flaw; image distortion

Examples

Chromatic aberration can cause colored fringes around objects in photographs.
The new telescope was designed to minimize optical aberration for sharper images.
Spherical aberration makes the edges of the image appear blurry through cheap magnifying glasses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distortion - alteration of the original form of an image

Etymology

From Latin 'aberratio' (a wandering), from 'aberrare' (to wander away).

3. NOUN

/ ˌæbəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌæbəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a temporary or permanent disorder or disturbance of the mind or mental state
mental disorder or disturbance

Examples

The sudden aggression was regarded as an aberration caused by illness.
Periods of mental aberration can occur after traumatic experiences.
They attributed her bizarre behavior to a brief aberration rather than a lasting problem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insanity - serious mental illness or disorder
  • Antonym: sanity - the state of having a healthy mind

Etymology

From Latin aberratio, from aberrāre ('to wander away, to go astray').
