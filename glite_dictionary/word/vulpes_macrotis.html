Vulpes macrotis

1. NOUN

/ ˈvʌlpiːz məˈkroʊtɪs / (AmE), / ˈvʌlpiːz məˈkrəʊtɪs / (BrE)
A small gray fox native to the southwestern United States, sometimes regarded as a subspecies of the swift fox (Vulpes velox).
a small gray fox of the southwestern US

Examples

The Vulpes macrotis is well adapted to arid regions and sandy soils.
Researchers observed the feeding habits of Vulpes macrotis in the desert.
Conservation efforts are helping to protect the habitat of Vulpes macrotis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swift fox - a small fox native to the western plains of North America (Vulpes velox)

Etymology

From Latin: 'Vulpes' meaning 'fox' and 'macrotis' meaning 'large-eared.'
