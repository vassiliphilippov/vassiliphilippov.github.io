antiphlogistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.ti.fləˈdʒɪs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌanti.fləˈdʒɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
(of a drug or treatment) counteracting or reducing inflammation.
reducing inflammation (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an antiphlogistic drug to help with the patient's swollen joints.
Early herbalists often sought antiphlogistic remedies to treat various inflammations.
The antiphlogistic properties of the ointment provided relief from redness and swelling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anti-inflammatory - reducing inflammation; used in medicine

Etymology

From Greek anti- (against) + phlogistos (inflamed).
