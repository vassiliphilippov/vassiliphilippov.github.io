rope

1. VERB

/ roʊp / (AmE), / rəʊp / (BrE)
to catch an animal, especially cattle or horses, with a rope or lasso
catch with a rope or lasso

Examples

The cowboy skillfully roped the stray calf before it ran off.
Competing in the rodeo, she managed to rope her horse in seconds.
It takes practice to rope cattle quickly and safely.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lasso - a rope with a noose used for catching animals

2. VERB

/ roʊp / (AmE), / rəʊp / (BrE)
to fasten, tie, or secure something with a rope
fasten or tie with a rope

Examples

They roped the boxes together to keep them from shifting during the move.
The climbers roped themselves to each other before starting the ascent.
Please rope off the area so that no one enters by mistake.

3. NOUN

/ roʊp / (AmE), / rəʊp / (BrE)
a thick, strong cord made by twisting together strands of fiber, used for tying, pulling, lifting, or securing things
thick, strong cord for tying or pulling

Examples

He used a rope to tie the boat to the dock.
Climbers must check that the rope is strong enough to support them.
The package was secured with a length of rope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cord - a thin, flexible string or rope made from twisted strands
  • Degree Lower: string - a thinner length of cord often used for tying or binding

Etymology

From Old English *rāp* (rope, cord), related to Old Norse *reip* and Dutch *roep*.
