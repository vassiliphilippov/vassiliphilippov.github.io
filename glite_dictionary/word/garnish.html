garnish

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrnɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːnɪʃ / (BrE)
an item, such as herbs or vegetables, added to food or drink for decoration or to enhance flavor
decorative or flavor-enhancing addition to food or drink

Examples

He placed a fresh parsley garnish on top of the soup.
A lemon wedge is a common garnish for seafood dishes.
The cocktail arrived with a cherry garnish on the rim of the glass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decoration - something used to decorate food or objects

Etymology

From Old French *garnir* (to equip, adorn), from Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07637227-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈɡɑrnɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːnɪʃ / (BrE)
to decorate food, especially with small amounts of other foods for added color or flavor
decorate food with other items

Examples

She likes to garnish her salads with slices of fresh orange and mint.
The chef will garnish the soup with a sprinkle of chopped parsley before serving.
You can garnish the cake with berries or chocolate shavings for a beautiful presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decorate - to make something look more attractive by adding items to it

Etymology

From Old French 'garnir' (to equip, furnish), from Frankish 'warnjan' (to provide, warn).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01683328-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑːrnɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːnɪʃ / (BrE)
an item used as a decoration, especially on food, to enhance appearance or flavor
decorative item, especially for food

Examples

The chef added a sprig of parsley as a garnish on the plate.
A lemon slice can be used as a colorful garnish for drinks and dishes.
She placed edible flowers as a garnish to impress her dinner guests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decoration - something used to make something look more attractive
  • Synonym: embellishment - a decorative detail or feature added to something

Etymology

From Old French *garnir* (to equip, adorn), from Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03425350-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈɡɑrnɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːnɪʃ / (BrE)
(of an employer or authority) to legally withhold part of someone's wages in order to pay a debt, such as child support.
legally withhold wages to pay a debt
legal

Examples

The court ordered his employer to garnish his wages for unpaid taxes.
Federal law limits how much of your paycheck can be garnished by creditors.
If you fail to pay child support, the state may garnish your income automatically.

Synset Examples

His employer garnished his wages in order to pay his debt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to seize - to take hold of suddenly and forcibly, especially by legal right

Etymology

From Old French garnir (to warn, prepare), later extended in legal sense.

Source: WordNet
