Didelphidae

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈdɛlfɪˌdi / (AmE), / dɪˈdɛlfɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of marsupial mammals, commonly known as opossums, native to the Americas.
family of marsupials (opossums)

Examples

The Didelphidae family is the largest group of marsupials in the Western Hemisphere.
Members of Didelphidae are commonly known as opossums and are found throughout Central and South America.
Scientists studied the evolutionary history of Didelphidae to better understand their adaptation to diverse habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opossum - any marsupial of the family Didelphidae

Etymology

From modern Latin Didelphidae, from genus Didelphis, from Greek di- (two) + delphus (womb).

2. NOUN

/ daɪˈdɛlfɪˌdi / (AmE), / dɪˈdɛlfɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small to medium-sized marsupials native to the Americas, commonly known as opossums
a family of marsupials (opossums)

Examples

The Didelphidae are the largest family of marsupials in the Western Hemisphere.
Members of Didelphidae, such as the Virginia opossum, are known for their adaptability.
Most species in the Didelphidae family are nocturnal and omnivorous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opossum - common name for any marsupial in family Didelphidae

Etymology

From New Latin Didelphis (type genus) and -idae (family suffix).
