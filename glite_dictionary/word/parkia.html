Parkia

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑr.ki.ə / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.ki.ə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical trees in the pea family (Fabaceae), native to the Old World, known as nitta trees.
genus of tropical trees (Fabaceae)

Examples

The Parkia species are commonly found in rainforests of Africa and Southeast Asia.
Researchers studied the ecological role of Parkia trees in supporting local wildlife.
Seeds from the Parkia genus are sometimes used in traditional cuisine.

Etymology

Named after Mungo Park, Scottish explorer.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːr.ki.ə / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.kɪ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical trees in the pea family (Fabaceae), known for their long pods and distinctive flowers, found primarily in the Old World tropics.
genus of tropical trees in Fabaceae

Examples

Species within the Parkia genus are widely cultivated for their edible seeds.
The pods of Parkia trees are often used in traditional Southeast Asian cuisine.
Botanists have discovered several new species of Parkia in recent years.

Etymology

Named in honor of Mungo Park, Scottish explorer (1771–1806).
