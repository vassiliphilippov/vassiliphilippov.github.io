gangster

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæŋstər / (AmE), / ˈɡæŋstə / (BrE)
a member of a group of criminals who work together to commit illegal activities
member of a criminal gang

Examples

The police arrested the notorious gangster after a long investigation.
Many classic films focus on the lives of gangsters during the Prohibition era.
He lived in a neighborhood where gangsters often controlled illegal businesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mobster - member of a criminal gang, especially one associated with organized crime
  • Synonym: criminal - someone who commits a crime
  • Antonym: law-abiding citizen - a person who follows the law and does not engage in criminal acts

Etymology

From 'gang' + '-ster', first used in American English in the late 19th century.
