disreputability

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsˌrɛpjətəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌdɪsˌrɛpjʊtəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being not respectable or having a bad reputation; lack of honor or respectability
lack of respectability; bad reputation

Examples

The disreputability of the venue discouraged many guests from attending the event.
Rumors and scandals had contributed to the disreputability of the politician's name.
He feared that any involvement with such people would taint him with disreputability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disrepute - the state of being held in low esteem by the public
  • Antonym: respectability - the quality of being socially acceptable or good

Etymology

From disreputable + -ity, modeled on Latin-derived noun-forming patterns.
