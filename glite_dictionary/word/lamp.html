lamp

1. NOUN

/ læmp / (AmE), / læmp / (BrE)
A device, often portable, that holds one or more electric light bulbs to provide illumination.
electric lighting device

Examples

She bought a new lamp for her bedside table to help her read at night.
The lamp in the living room casts a warm, cozy glow.
He accidentally knocked over the lamp while cleaning the shelf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: light - visible radiant energy that makes things visible
  • Synonym: lantern - portable case for a light, often with a handle

Etymology

From Latin lampas, from Ancient Greek lampás (torch, lamp).

2. NOUN

/ læmp / (AmE), / læmp / (BrE)
a device that produces light, typically by electricity, oil, or gas, used to illuminate an area
device that produces light

Examples

She turned on the lamp next to her bed to read her book.
The antique lamp in the hallway gives off a warm glow.
We bought a new desk lamp for the study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: light - the natural agent that makes things visible
  • Synonym: lantern - a portable lamp with a protective enclosure for the light source

Etymology

From Latin 'lampas', from Ancient Greek 'lampás' meaning 'torch, lamp'.
