giraffe

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈræf / (AmE), / dʒɪˈrɑːf / (BrE)
A large African mammal with a very long neck and legs, a spotted coat, and small horns, known as the tallest living land animal.
tall African mammal with long neck

Examples

The giraffe stretched its neck to reach the highest leaves on the tree.
A young giraffe can stand and walk within hours of being born.
We saw a herd of giraffes grazing peacefully on the African savanna.

Etymology

From Arabic zarāfa via Italian giraffa and French girafe.
