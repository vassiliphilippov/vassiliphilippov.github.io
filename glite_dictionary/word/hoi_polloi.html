hoi polloi

1. NOUN

/ hɔɪ pəˈlɔɪ / (AmE), / hɔɪ pɒˈlɔɪ / (BrE)
the common people; the masses as distinguished from the elite or upper classes
the common people; the masses

Examples

Tickets to the private event were not available to the hoi polloi.
Some critics argue that popular art caters too much to the tastes of the hoi polloi.
He prefers fine dining over places frequented by the hoi polloi.

Synset Examples

separate the warriors from the mass
power to the people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the masses - the ordinary people in society, as opposed to the elite
  • Antonym: the elite - a group or class seen as having the greatest power or privilege

Etymology

From Greek hoi polloi (οἱ πολλοί) meaning 'the many'.
