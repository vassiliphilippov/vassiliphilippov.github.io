verily

1. ADV

/ ˈvɛrəli / (AmE), / ˈvɛrɪli / (BrE)
truly; certainly; in truth (used to emphasize a statement, especially in formal, literary, or archaic contexts)
truly; certainly (archaic or formal)
archaic

Examples

Verily, I say unto you, this decision was not made lightly.
She verily believed that justice would prevail in the end.
Many old texts use the word verily to express sincerity and truth.

Synset Examples

I verily think so
trust in the Lord … and verily thou shalt be fed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: truly - in a truthful or sincere way
  • Synonym: certainly - without doubt; surely

Etymology

From Old English 'verily', from 'veri' (true) + '-ly'.
