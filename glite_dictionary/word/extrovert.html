extrovert

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛkstrəˌvɝt / (AmE), / ˈɛkstrəˌvɜːt / (BrE)
a person who is outgoing, socially confident, and concerned more with external realities and social interaction than inner thoughts and feelings
an outgoing, socially confident person

Examples

As an extrovert, she thrives in large social gatherings and enjoys meeting new people.
Although he is an extrovert, he sometimes needs time alone to recharge.
The workplace benefits from both extrovert and introvert personalities.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: introvert - a person who is more focused on internal thoughts and feelings than external activities

Etymology

From Latin 'extra' (outside) + 'vertere' (to turn), originally as a psychological term coined in the early 20th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɛk.strə.vɝt / (AmE), / ˈɛk.strə.vɜːt / (BrE)
(of a person) outgoing and socially confident; oriented toward external things or the social environment.
outgoing, socially confident person or trait

Examples

Rebecca is such an extrovert that she loves meeting new people and thrives at parties.
The company's culture appeals more to extrovert personalities who enjoy teamwork and open communication.
As a child, he was quiet, but as an adult, he became much more extrovert.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: introvert - a person who tends to be shy and prefers solitary activities

Etymology

From Latin extrō- (outside) + vertere (to turn), by analogy with 'introvert'.
