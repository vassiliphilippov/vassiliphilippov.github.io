Acropora

1. NOUN

/ əˈkrɑːpərə / (AmE), / əˈkrɒpərə / (BrE)
A genus of corals, commonly known as staghorn corals, found in shallow tropical reefs and known for their branching growth forms.
a genus of staghorn corals

Examples

The vibrant Acropora colonies create complex habitats for many reef fish.
Coral bleaching has severely impacted Acropora populations around the world.
Divers marveled at the intricate branches of the Acropora on the reef.

Etymology

From Greek 'akron' (top, summit) and '-pora' (pore), referring to the form of the coral's structure.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæk.rəˈpɔr.ə / (AmE), / ˌæk.rəˈpɔː.rə / (BrE)
A genus of stony coral in the order Scleractinia, known as staghorn corals, found in shallow tropical reefs.
genus of staghorn corals

Examples

The reef is dominated by Acropora, which provides shelter for many fish species.
Marine biologists studied the bleaching patterns in several colonies of Acropora.
Restoration projects often use fragments of Acropora to rebuild damaged coral reefs.

Etymology

From Greek akron (top, summit) + poros (pore), referring to the growth form and porous skeleton.
