Wolffia columbiana

1. NOUN

/ wʌlˈfiːə ˌkoʊləmˈbiːənə / (AmE), / wɒlˈfiːə ˌkɒləmˈbiːənə / (BrE)
A species of tiny aquatic flowering plant, known as one of the smallest flowering plants in the world, native to the Americas.
species of tiny aquatic flowering plant

Examples

Wolffia columbiana is so small that several plants can fit on the tip of a pencil.
Scientists studied Wolffia columbiana to learn more about rapid aquatic plant reproduction.
Botanical gardens often showcase Wolffia columbiana as the world's smallest flowering plant.

Etymology

From the genus name Wolffia (after German botanist Johann Friedrich Wolff) and the species epithet columbiana (referring to Colombia or the Americas).
