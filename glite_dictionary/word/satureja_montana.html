Satureja montana

1. NOUN

/ sæˈtʊəriə mɒnˈtɑːnə / (AmE), / sæˈtjʊəriə mɒnˈtɑːnə / (BrE)
an erect perennial herb native to southern Europe, with leathery, thyme-flavored leaves and pink or white flowers; also known as winter savory.
herbaceous perennial known as winter savory

Examples

The leaves of Satureja montana add a delightful, peppery flavor to roasted meats.
Gardeners often plant Satureja montana to attract bees and other pollinators.
Traditional Mediterranean dishes sometimes include Satureja montana as a seasoning herb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winter savory - common name for Satureja montana, a culinary herb

Etymology

From Latin: genus Satureja and species montana meaning 'of the mountains'.
