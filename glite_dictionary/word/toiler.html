toiler

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔɪlər / (AmE), / ˈtɔɪlə / (BrE)
a person who works very hard, especially at physically demanding or monotonous tasks
person who works very hard

Examples

The toiler rose before dawn each day to tend the fields.
Factory toilers often faced long hours in harsh conditions during the industrial era.
She admired her grandfather, a lifelong toiler who never shied away from hard work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laborer - person who does unskilled physical work
  • Antonym: idler - person who avoids work or is habitually lazy

Etymology

From English verb 'toil' (to work hard) + -er (agent noun suffix).
