Bahasa Kebangsaan

1. NOUN

/ bɑːˈhɑːsə kəˌbʌŋˈsɑːn / (AmE), / bɑːˈhɑːsə kəˌbæŋˈsɑːn / (BrE)
the Malay language, especially as the official or national language of Malaysia or neighboring countries
official/national Malay language

Examples

Many schools in Malaysia teach in Bahasa Kebangsaan as part of their curriculum.
The government encourages citizens to use Bahasa Kebangsaan in all official communications.
He is fluent in both English and Bahasa Kebangsaan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Malay - the Austronesian language spoken in Malaysia, Brunei, and Indonesia

Etymology

Malay, from 'bahasa' (language) and 'kebangsaan' (national).

2. NOUN

/ bɑˈhɑːsə kəˌbɑːŋˈsɑːn / (AmE), / bɑˈhɑːsə kəˌbæŋˈsɑːn / (BrE)
the official form of the Malay language as spoken in Malaysia and some neighbouring countries
official Malay language of Malaysia

Examples

The Malaysian constitution designates Bahasa Kebangsaan as the official language of the country.
Students are required to take Bahasa Kebangsaan classes in school.
Many government documents in Malaysia are written in Bahasa Kebangsaan to ensure clarity for all citizens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Malay - the Austronesian language of the Malays, official in Malaysia and Indonesia

Etymology

From Malay, where 'bahasa' means 'language' and 'kebangsaan' means 'national'.
