Teleostei

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛliˈɑstiˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˌtɛlɪˈɒstiːˌaɪ / (BrE)
a large and diverse group of bony fishes that includes most living fish species
major group of bony fishes

Examples

The vast majority of modern fish species belong to the Teleostei.
Recent studies have revealed new evolutionary traits within the Teleostei.
Salmon, trout, and cod are all members of the Teleostei group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bony fish - fish with a skeleton made of bone rather than cartilage

Etymology

From Greek 'teleos' (complete) and 'osteon' (bone), referring to their fully ossified skeleton.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtɛliˈɑːstiː / (AmE), / ˌtɛliˈɒstiː / (BrE)
a large subclass of bony fishes that includes most living species
subclass of bony fishes

Examples

The Teleostei encompass a wide variety of fish species found in both fresh and marine waters.
Scientists continue to discover new species within the Teleostei each year.
Most commercially important fish, such as salmon and tuna, belong to the Teleostei.

Etymology

From Greek teleos (complete) + osteon (bone), referring to their fully ossified skeletons.
