chemically

1. ADV

/ ˈkɛmɪkli / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner relating to or by means of chemistry or chemicals.
in a way relating to chemistry

Examples

The two solutions react chemically to produce a new compound.
This vegetable has been chemically treated to increase its shelf life.
The two substances may look alike, but they are chemically very different.

Synset Examples

chemically different substances
chemically related

Related Terms

  • Antonym: physically - in a manner relating to physics or the body

Etymology

From 'chemical' + adverbial suffix '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ˈkɛmɪkli / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪkəli / (BrE)
by means of chemicals; in a way that involves chemistry or chemical processes
by or relating to chemicals

Examples

The soil was chemically treated to kill weeds.
This material breaks down chemically over time when exposed to light.
Food can be chemically altered during processing.

Synset Examples

chemically fertilized

Related Terms

  • Antonym: physically - by means of physical processes, as opposed to chemical processes

Etymology

From 'chemical' + '-ly', with 'chemical' ultimately from Late Latin 'chemicalis'.
