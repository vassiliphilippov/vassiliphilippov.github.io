boudoir

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuː.dwɑr / (AmE), / ˈbuː.dwɑː / (BrE)
a woman's bedroom or private sitting room, typically elegantly furnished
a woman's private bedroom or sitting room

Examples

The lavishly decorated boudoir featured velvet drapes and antique mirrors.
She enjoyed reading quietly in her private boudoir every afternoon.
The artist painted a portrait set in the elegant boudoir of his patron.

Etymology

From French 'boudoir', from 'bouder' (to sulk).
