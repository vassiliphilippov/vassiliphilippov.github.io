Salix alba caerulea

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlɪks ˈælbə siːəˈruːlɪə / (AmE), / ˈsalɪks ˈalba kaɪˈruːlɪə / (BrE)
A Eurasian variety of the white willow tree with greyish leaves and upright, ascending branches, often cultivated for its flexible wood.
a variety of Eurasian white willow tree

Examples

The cricket bat is often made from Salix alba caerulea because of its flexible wood.
Botanists studied the leaves of Salix alba caerulea for their unique greyish color and upright branches.
Several parks have planted Salix alba caerulea to create attractive, shaded walking paths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white willow - a species of willow tree, Salix alba, native to Europe and western and central Asia

Etymology

From Latin: 'salix' (willow), 'alba' (white), 'caerulea' (bluish or sky-colored), scientific name in botanical nomenclature.
