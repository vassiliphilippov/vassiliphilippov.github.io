to federalise

1. VERB

/ ˈfɛdərəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəlaɪz / (BrE)
to join together into a federation, or form a union of states or groups for a common purpose
join to form a federation

Examples

Several provinces agreed to federalise after lengthy negotiations.
Efforts to federalise the region met with strong support from local leaders.
The two countries decided to federalise in order to strengthen their economic ties.

Synset Examples

The republics federated to become the Soviet Union

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unite - to come or bring together for a common purpose
  • Antonym: separate - to divide or break apart

Etymology

From French fédéraliser, from fédéral (federal), ultimately from Latin foedus (treaty, league).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00370095-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈfɛdərəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəlaɪz / (BrE)
to unite or organize on a federal basis; to form a federation or become part of a federation
form into a federation

Examples

After the revolution, several small states decided to federalise for greater strength and unity.
There are ongoing debates about whether to federalise parts of the health care system.
The region was formally federalised in the mid-nineteenth century, creating a new central government.

Synset Examples

The country was federated after the civil war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: federate - to form into a single group with a central government

Etymology

From French 'fédéraliser', based on 'fédéral' (federal). Related to 'federate', from Latin 'foedus' (league, treaty).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00369847-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈfɛdərəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəlaɪz / (BrE)
to bring (a state, organization, etc.) under the control or authority of a federal government
bring under federal government control
british

Examples

The government plans to federalise the regional police forces to improve coordination.
Debates arose about whether to federalise the health care system across all provinces.
Some officials oppose efforts to federalise education, preferring local control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to federalize - to bring under the control of a federal government (American spelling)

Etymology

From federal + -ise (British spelling of -ize); derived from Latin 'foedus' (treaty, compact).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00505545-v
Synset POS: v
