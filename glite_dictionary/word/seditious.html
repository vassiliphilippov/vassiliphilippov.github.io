seditious

1. ADJ

/ sɪˈdɪʃəs / (AmE), / sɪˈdɪʃəs / (BrE)
Inciting or supporting opposition to lawful authority, often intended to cause rebellion against a government.
inciting opposition to authority or government

Examples

The government banned the book, calling its ideas seditious and dangerous.
He was arrested for making seditious speeches that encouraged people to revolt.
During times of unrest, seditious activity is often met with harsh punishment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebellious - resisting authority or control
  • Antonym: loyal - showing support and allegiance to authority or government

Etymology

From Latin 'seditiosus' (full of discord), from 'seditio' (sedition).

2. ADJ

/ səˈdɪʃəs / (AmE), / sɪˈdɪʃəs / (BrE)
inciting or provoking people to rebel against the authority of a state or government
inciting rebellion against authority

Examples

The leader was arrested for making seditious speeches that encouraged an uprising.
The government has banned the publication of seditious materials.
Authorities feared the seditious pamphlet would spark a revolt among the workers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebellious - refusing to obey authority, especially of a government
  • Antonym: loyal - showing firm and constant support to authority or country

Etymology

From Latin 'seditiosus' (full of discord), from 'seditio' (civil discord).
