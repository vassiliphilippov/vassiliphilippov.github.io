sigh

1. NOUN

/ saɪ / (AmE), / saɪ / (BrE)
an audible exhalation, usually expressing sadness, relief, tiredness, or similar emotion
audible exhalation expressing emotion

Examples

There was a long sigh of relief when the test was finally over.
She let out a sigh after hearing the disappointing news.
With a sigh, he sat down and rested his tired feet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groan - a deep, inarticulate sound expressing pain or despair
  • Synonym: moan - a long, low sound made when expressing pain or unhappiness

Etymology

From Old French *sighier*, of imitative origin.

2. NOUN

/ saɪ / (AmE), / saɪ / (BrE)
A long, deep sound expressing sadness, relief, tiredness, or resembling the audible exhalation often made by a person.
long, deep sound (like exhalation)

Examples

A soft sigh of wind rustled the curtains in the evening light.
With a sigh, she put the letter down and wiped away a tear.
The old house seemed to breathe, each board creaking with a sigh as the temperature dropped.

Synset Examples

she heard the sigh of the wind in the trees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groan - a low mournful sound expressing pain or disapproval

Etymology

From Old French *sighier*, from Vulgar Latin *suspirare* (to sigh).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07408356-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ saɪ / (AmE), / saɪ / (BrE)
to let out a long, deep, audible breath expressing tiredness, relief, sadness, or another emotion
let out a deep audible breath

Examples

She sighed with relief when she heard the good news.
He sighs heavily every time he thinks about his old hometown.
After finishing the long hike, they sighed and sat down to rest.

Synset Examples

She sighed sadly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gasp - inhale suddenly with the mouth open, out of pain or astonishment

Etymology

From Old English 'sīcan' (to sigh), related to German 'seufzen'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00004032-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ saɪ / (AmE), / saɪ / (BrE)
to say or express something by letting out a long, audible breath, often to show sadness, relief, or tiredness
utter with a sigh

Examples

“I guess we’ll have to try again,” she sighed quietly, disappointment clear in her voice.
He sighed his answer, barely audible over the hum of the air conditioner.
"It's over," Andy sighed, finally letting go of the tension he'd held all day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whisper - to speak very softly
  • Antonym: shout - to say something loudly

Etymology

From Old French 'sighier', from Latin 'suspirare' (to draw a long breath, to sigh).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00987832-v
Synset POS: v
