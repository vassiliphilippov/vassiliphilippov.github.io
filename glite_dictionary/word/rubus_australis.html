Rubus australis

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːbəs ɔːˈstreɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈruːbəs ɔːˈstreɪlɪs / (BrE)
a stout-stemmed, trailing shrub native to New Zealand, belonging to the rose family and known for scrambling over other vegetation
a New Zealand trailing shrub species

Examples

The dense thicket of Rubus australis made it difficult for hikers to pass through the forest.
Researchers discovered several rare insects living among the Rubus australis vines.
Gardeners often train Rubus australis to cover trellises in native plant displays.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Rubus' (bramble, blackberry) and 'australis' (southern), referring to its southern hemisphere origin.
