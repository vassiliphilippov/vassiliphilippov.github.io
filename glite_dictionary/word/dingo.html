dingo

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪŋgoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdɪŋgəʊ / (BrE)
a wild dog with a wolflike appearance and typically yellowish-brown fur, found mainly in Australia
Australian wild dog

Examples

A dingo howled in the distance as night fell over the outback.
The dingo is native to Australia and plays an important role in its ecosystem.
Tourists are advised not to feed the dingoes on Fraser Island.

Etymology

From Dharug (Aboriginal Australian) *dingo* (a word for the animal).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪŋgoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdɪŋgəʊ / (BrE)
a wild dog of Australia, typically yellowish-brown, resembling a wolf and living in packs
Australian wild dog

Examples

The dingo is native to Australia and is rarely seen near human settlements.
Researchers have studied the impact of dingoes on local wildlife and livestock.
A pack of dingoes was spotted near the edge of the national park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dog - domesticated carnivorous mammal often kept as a pet or for work

Etymology

From Dharug (an Aboriginal Australian language), possibly from 'tingo' meaning dog.
