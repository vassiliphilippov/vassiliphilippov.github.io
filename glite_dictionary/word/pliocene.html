Pliocene

1. NOUN

/ ˈplaɪ.əˌsiːn / (AmE), / ˈplaɪ.əʊˌsiːn / (BrE)
the epoch in the Cenozoic Era, between the Miocene and Pleistocene, lasting from about 5.3 to 2.6 million years ago, characterized by cooler climates and the spread of mammals.
a geological epoch between Miocene and Pleistocene

Examples

Many fossils of early hominids have been found in rocks dating to the Pliocene.
During the Pliocene, glaciers began to form in the Northern Hemisphere.
The Pliocene epoch saw significant changes in Earth's climate and animal life.

Etymology

From Greek 'pleiōn' (more) and 'kainos' (new), coined in the 19th century.
