trachodon

1. NOUN

/ trəˈkɑdɒn / (AmE), / trəˈkəʊdɒn / (BrE)
a large duck-billed dinosaur from the Cretaceous period, now considered an obsolete genus in scientific classification
duck-billed dinosaur genus (obsolete)

Examples

The fossilized teeth of a trachodon were discovered in the rocky cliffs.
Children are often fascinated by dinosaurs like the trachodon and the triceratops.
Although the name trachodon is outdated, it still appears in some older textbooks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hadrosaur - any duck-billed, plant-eating dinosaur of the family Hadrosauridae

Etymology

From Greek 'trachys' (rough) + 'odōn' (tooth).
