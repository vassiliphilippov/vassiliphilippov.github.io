Hakea leucoptera

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪkiə luːˈkɒptərə / (AmE), / ˈheɪkiə ljuːˈkɒptərə / (BrE)
a large bushy shrub with sharp pointed leaves and creamy white flowers, native to central and eastern Australia
Australian shrub with creamy white flowers

Examples

The Hakea leucoptera blooms with clusters of creamy white flowers during the summer months.
Many birds are attracted to the spiky branches of Hakea leucoptera for shelter and food.
On our trip through eastern Australia, we saw a dense stand of Hakea leucoptera shrubs in the arid scrubland.

Etymology

From scientific Latin; genus name 'Hakea' after Baron Christian Ludwig von Hake, German patron of botany, and species name from Greek 'leukos' (white) and 'pteron' (wing).
