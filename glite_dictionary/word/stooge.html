stooge

1. NOUN

/ stuːdʒ / (AmE), / stuːdʒ / (BrE)
a person who is made to look foolish or is the target of pranks or ridicule, often for the amusement of others
person targeted for ridicule or pranks

Examples

He always ended up as the stooge in their elaborate office pranks.
The comedian picked a random audience member to act as the stooge in his show.
Nobody likes being the stooge of the group, always getting laughed at.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dupe - a person who is easily tricked or deceived
  • Synonym: victim - a person who is harmed or tricked by others
  • Antonym: prankster - a person who plays pranks on others

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly related to theatrical slang for a comic's assistant, early 20th century American English.

2. VERB

/ stuːdʒ / (AmE), / stuːdʒ / (BrE)
to act as a subordinate or comic assistant, especially one who sets up jokes for others to deliver punchlines
act as a subordinate or comic assistant

Examples

She was often asked to stooge for the lead comedian during live shows.
In the sketch, Mike had to stooge along with the main performer, drawing laughs from the audience.
He grew tired of always having to stooge for the stars without getting much credit.

Synset Examples

His role was to stooge for the popular comedian

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assist - help someone, especially in a performance
  • Synonym: support - to act in a supporting role, especially in performance

Etymology

From the noun 'stooge', of uncertain origin, possibly from student slang for an assistant or underling.

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ stuːdʒ / (AmE), / stuːdʒ / (BrE)
a person who unquestioningly does what another tells them, often allowing themselves to be used or manipulated
person who unquestioningly obeys another

Examples

He acted like a stooge for the corrupt politician, never questioning any order.
The company needs independent thinkers, not just stooges who do as they're told.
She refused to be anyone's stooge and stood up for what she believed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puppet - a person whose actions are controlled by someone else
  • Synonym: tool - a person used by others for their own ends
  • Synonym: dupe - a person who is easily deceived or controlled

Etymology

Origin unknown; popularized as part of vaudeville slang in early 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10117861-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ stuːdʒ / (AmE), / stuːdʒ / (BrE)
to act in a subordinate or compliant role, often as an assistant to someone more important or as someone doing menial or obedient tasks, especially for someone else's benefit
to act as a compliant subordinate

Examples

He didn't mind stooging for the director as long as he gained experience.
Some politicians are accused of stooging for lobbyists behind the scenes.
She refused to stooge for anyone, insisting on making decisions herself.

Synset Examples

He stooged for the flamboyant Senator

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toady - to act in an overly obedient or servile way to someone important
  • Antonym: independent - acting on one's own authority, not subordinate or compliant

Etymology

Originally from the theatrical term 'stooge', an assistant to a comedian or entertainer (early 20th century, origin uncertain).
