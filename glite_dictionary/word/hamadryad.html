hamadryad

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæməˈdraɪæd / (AmE), / ˌhaməˈdrʌɪad / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a nymph or spirit that lives in and is intimately connected to a particular tree, especially an oak.
tree nymph in Greek mythology

Examples

According to legend, a hamadryad would die if her tree was cut down.
The poet described a hamadryad emerging gracefully from the ancient oak.
In mythology, every tree had its own guardian hamadryad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dryad - a tree nymph in Greek mythology (not restricted to a particular tree)

Etymology

From Greek *hamadryas* (Ὁμαδρυάς), from *hama* 'together with' and *dryas* 'tree nymph'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhæməˈdraɪæd / (AmE), / ˌhaməˈdrʌɪad / (BrE)
a large venomous snake (Ophiophagus hannah) found in South and Southeast Asia, also known as the king cobra, and the largest venomous snake in the world
king cobra; largest venomous snake

Examples

The hamadryad is renowned for its size and potent venom.
Herpetologists tracked the movements of a hamadryad through the dense jungle.
A bite from a hamadryad can be fatal without prompt medical treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king cobra - another name for the species Ophiophagus hannah

Etymology

From Greek hamadryas (a kind of nymph associated with trees), due to the snake's forest habitat.
