anteriority

1. NOUN

/ ænˌtɪriˈɔrəti / (AmE), / ænˌtɪəriˈɒrəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being positioned at the front or, in animals, toward the head
state of being at the front or toward the head

Examples

The anteriority of the heart in the chest cavity protects it from injury.
Some species demonstrate anteriority by always moving head-first when exploring new areas.
Discussions on anteriority and posteriority are vital in comparative anatomy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: posteriority - the state of being behind or toward the rear

Etymology

From Latin 'anterior' (former, more forward) plus '-ity' forming nouns denoting state or condition.

2. NOUN

/ ænˌtɪəriˈɒrəti / (AmE), / ænˌtɪəriˈɒrəti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being earlier or preceding in time, position, or order
the fact of being earlier in time or order

Examples

The anteriority of the document is clear when compared with the later revisions.
Scholars debate the anteriority of these two ancient texts.
Establishing the anteriority of this law helps resolve the legal dispute.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: posteriority - the state of being later or coming after in time or order

Etymology

From French 'antériorité', from Latin 'anterior' (former, earlier).
