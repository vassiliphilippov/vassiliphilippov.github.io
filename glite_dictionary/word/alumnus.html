alumnus

1. NOUN

/ əˈlʌmnəs / (AmE), / əˈlʌmnəs / (BrE)
A man who has graduated from a particular school, college, or university.
male graduate of a school or university

Examples

After graduating from Harvard, he became a proud alumnus of the university.
The alumnus donated generously to support scholarships for future students.
Each year, the college invites every alumnus to a special reunion event.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: alumna - female graduate of a school or university
  • Degree Higher: alumni - male plural or mixed-gender plural form of alumnus

Etymology

From Latin alumnus (foster son, pupil).
