to skimp

1. VERB

/ skɪmp / (AmE), / skɪmp / (BrE)
to provide less than enough of something, especially time, money, or effort; to be stingy or sparing in the use of something
provide less than enough; be sparing

Examples

You shouldn't skimp on quality when buying safety equipment.
The company decided to skimp on employee benefits to save money.
If you skimp on sleep, you'll feel tired all day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scrimp - to be excessively economical or parsimonious
  • Antonym: splurge - to spend freely or extravagantly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02515522-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ skɪmp / (AmE), / skɪmp / (BrE)
to do something in a careless or insufficient way, especially by making too little effort or using too few resources
do something poorly or with too few resources

Examples

If you skimp on studying, you may not do well on the test.
The company was criticized for skimping on safety measures to save money.
She often skimps when cooking, using fewer ingredients than needed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cut corners - to do something in the easiest or cheapest way, often sacrificing quality
  • Synonym: to scrimp - to be excessively sparing or frugal, often at the expense of quality

Etymology

Possibly from Scandinavian origin, related to Danish skimpet (scanty).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ skɪmp / (AmE), / skɪmp / (BrE)
to provide or use less of something than is necessary, often in order to save money or effort
provide insufficiently or sparingly

Examples

Don't skimp on the ingredients if you want the dish to taste good.
Many companies skimp on safety measures to cut costs, which can be dangerous.
She skimped on sleep during exam week and ended up getting sick.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stint - to provide an inadequate amount, to be frugal with
  • Antonym: to squander - to spend or use something wastefully

4. VERB

/ skɪmp / (AmE), / skɪmp / (BrE)
to manage with very little; to live on a small or insufficient allowance
subsist on a meager allowance

Examples

After losing his job, he had to skimp on food just to pay his rent.
Many students skimp by on very little money while attending university.
During the recession, families often skimp to make ends meet.
