exteroception

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.stɚ.oʊˈsɛp.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛk.stər.əʊˈsɛp.ʃən / (BrE)
the perception or sensitivity to stimuli originating outside the body, typically through the senses such as sight, hearing, touch, smell, and taste
perception of external stimuli
medical

Examples

Human senses like vision and hearing are forms of exteroception that allow us to interact with our environment.
Impairments in exteroception may affect a person's awareness of external dangers.
Training that involves mindfulness can increase awareness of both exteroception and interoception.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interoception - sensitivity to stimuli originating inside the body

Etymology

From Latin exter(us) (outside) + -o- + -ception (from perception).
