Martes zibellina

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːrtiːz zɪbəˈliːnə / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtiːz zɪbəˈliːnə / (BrE)
A species of marten of northern Asian forests, known for its luxuriant dark brown fur; commonly called the sable.
species of marten (sable) with dark fur

Examples

The luxurious fur of the Martes zibellina has been prized for centuries in the fur trade.
Researchers tracked the population of Martes zibellina in the dense Siberian forests.
Hunting of Martes zibellina is regulated to protect the species from overexploitation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sable - mammal valued for its fur; common name for Martes zibellina

Etymology

From Latin scientific nomenclature: genus 'Martes' (martens) and species 'zibellina' (sable).
