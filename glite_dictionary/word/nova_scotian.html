Nova Scotian

1. NOUN

/ ˌnoʊvə ˈskoʊʃən / (AmE), / ˌnəʊvə ˈskəʊʃən / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of Nova Scotia, a province in eastern Canada.
a person from Nova Scotia

Examples

My grandmother is a proud Nova Scotian who grew up on the coast.
As a Nova Scotian, he loves seafood and sailing.
Many Nova Scotians celebrate their Scottish heritage each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Canadian - a native or inhabitant of Canada

Etymology

From 'Nova Scotia', Latin for 'New Scotland', after the Canadian province; plus the English suffix '-ian' to denote inhabitant or native.
