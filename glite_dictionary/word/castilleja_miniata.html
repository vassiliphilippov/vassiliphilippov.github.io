Castilleja miniata

1. NOUN

/ kæˌstɪliˈeɪ mɪˈnaɪ.ə.tə / (AmE), / kæˌstɪliˈeɪ mɪˈnaɪ.ətə / (BrE)
A species of wildflower native to western North America, known for its ragged clusters of crimson or scarlet flowers; commonly called the giant red Indian paintbrush.
wildflower with crimson or scarlet flowers

Examples

We saw a patch of Castilleja miniata blooming along the mountain trail.
The brilliant red of the Castilleja miniata stood out among the green grasses of the meadow.
Many photographers are drawn to fields where Castilleja miniata grows in abundance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indian paintbrush - Common name for various species in the genus Castilleja

Etymology

Genus named after Spanish botanist Domingo Castillejo; species name from Latin 'miniatus', meaning colored with red lead.
