clipping

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
a small piece cut from a newspaper or magazine, typically containing an article, photograph, or advertisement
excerpt from a newspaper or magazine

Examples

My grandmother keeps a clipping of my first published article in her scrapbook.
He glued a sports clipping onto the project board for his presentation.
The detective found an old newspaper clipping in the victim's wallet.

Synset Examples

he searched through piles of letters and clippings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutting - a clipping from a newspaper or magazine

Etymology

From the verb 'clip' (to cut), + -ing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06625002-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈklɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of cutting something down to the desired size or shape, or the piece that is cut off
act or result of cutting to size or shape

Examples

She collected the nail clipping and threw it in the trash.
Grass clippings covered the yard after the lawn was mowed.
The tailor made a small clipping from the cloth to show the customer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutting - the act of making an incision or removal by a sharp instrument

Etymology

From the verb 'clip', of Old English origin (cleopian, meaning to cut or shear).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00360729-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈklɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of cutting something, especially with scissors or shears; trimming or snipping
the act of cutting or trimming

Examples

He spent the afternoon clipping the hedges in the garden.
Please sweep up the hair clipping after you finish cutting it.
A quick clipping of the coupons saved her a lot of money.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutting - the act of making an incision or removal by a sharp tool

Etymology

From the verb 'clip', Middle English 'clippen', from Old Norse 'klippa'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00360440-n
Synset POS: n
