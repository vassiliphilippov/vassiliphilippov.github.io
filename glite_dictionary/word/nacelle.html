nacelle

1. NOUN

/ nəˈsɛl / (AmE), / nəˈsɛl / (BrE)
a streamlined housing enclosing an aircraft engine, typically attached to the wing or fuselage
aircraft engine enclosure

Examples

The left engine nacelle was damaged during the landing.
Maintenance crews inspected the nacelles for any signs of oil leaks.
A new design improves airflow around the nacelle to enhance engine efficiency.

Etymology

From French 'nacelle' meaning 'small boat' or 'gondola'.
