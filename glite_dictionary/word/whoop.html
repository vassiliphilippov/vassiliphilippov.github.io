whoop

1. NOUN

/ wup / (AmE), / wuːp / (BrE)
a loud, high-pitched cry expressing excitement, joy, or exuberance
loud, joyful cry

Examples

The players gave a triumphant whoop when they won the match.
A whoop of laughter came from the back of the room.
She let out a loud whoop as she jumped into the pool.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shout - a loud call or cry expressing strong emotion
  • Synonym: cheer - a shout of encouragement or joy

Etymology

Probably imitative in origin, reflecting the sound of the cry itself.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07137996-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ wup / (AmE), / wuːp / (BrE)
to shout loudly with joy, enthusiasm, or excitement
shout with joy or excitement

Examples

The fans whooped and clapped as their team scored the winning goal.
After hearing the good news, she couldn't help but whoop with happiness.
The children whooped and ran through the playground, celebrating the last day of school.

Synset Examples

The children whooped when they were led to the picnic table

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheer - to shout to express encouragement or joy
  • Antonym: boo - to shout disapproval

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound itself; related to Old French 'houper' (to shout).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00916168-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ wuːp, hwuːp / (AmE), / wuːp, hwuːp / (BrE)
to cough with a spasmodic gasping intake of breath, especially as a symptom of whooping cough
to cough spasmodically

Examples

The child continued to whoop late into the night, alarming his parents.
Patients with pertussis may whoop violently during coughing fits.
He began to whoop uncontrollably after inhaling the irritant.

Synset Examples

The patient with emphysema is hacking all day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cough - to expel air from the lungs with a sudden sharp sound

Etymology

Imitative; related to the characteristic sound produced during whooping cough.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00006100-v
Synset POS: v
