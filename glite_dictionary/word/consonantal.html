consonantal

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːnsəˈnæntl / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsəˈnæntl / (BrE)
relating to or having the characteristics of a consonant, especially in speech sounds or writing systems.
relating to a consonant sound or letter

Examples

The Hebrew alphabet contains both consonantal and vocalic letters.
In linguistics, a consonantal sound is produced with significant constriction in the vocal tract.
The writing system is primarily consonantal, meaning most vowels are not represented.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vocalic - relating to vowels or vowel sounds

Etymology

From Latin consonantalis, from consonans (consonant).

2. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːnsəˈnæntl̩ / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsəˈnæntl̩ / (BrE)
relating to, consisting of, or functioning as a consonant or consonants; marked by the presence or characteristics of consonants.
relating to consonants

Examples

In some languages, consonantal writing systems omit most vowels.
The consonantal cluster at the beginning of 'strength' makes it difficult for learners.
Linguists study both vowel and consonantal features of speech.

Synset Examples

consonantal sounds
a consonantal Hebrew text
consonantal alliteration
a consonantal cluster

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vocalic - relating to or consisting of vowels

Etymology

From Latin consonans, consonantis (consonant) + -al.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02295368-a
Synset POS: a
