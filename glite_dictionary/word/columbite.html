columbite

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlʌmˌbaɪt / (AmE), / kəˈlʌmbʌɪt / (BrE)
A black mineral composed chiefly of iron, niobium, and tantalum, used as an ore of niobium and tantalum.
a black mineral and ore of niobium and tantalum

Examples

The mine produces large quantities of columbite for industrial use.
Geologists identified the black crystal as columbite after laboratory analysis.
Extraction of niobium from columbite requires sophisticated processing techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tantalite - a mineral very similar to columbite, an ore of tantalum

Etymology

From Columbia (an early name for America) and the mineral suffix -ite, as it was first discovered in the United States.
