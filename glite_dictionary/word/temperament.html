temperament

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpərəmənt / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəmənt / (BrE)
a tendency to display excessive emotionalism, irritability, or excitability, especially when shown openly
excessive emotionalism or excitability

Examples

Her temperament often led to dramatic outbursts in stressful situations.
The actor's temperament made him difficult to work with during rehearsals.
Due to his sensitive temperament, he easily became upset by criticism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temper - a person's state of mind seen in terms of their being angry or calm

Etymology

From Latin temperamentum (proper mixture), related to temperare (to mix in due proportion).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpɚəmənt / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəmənt / (BrE)
(Music) An adjustment of the regular musical intervals in tuning an instrument, especially a keyboard, so that the scale can be used to play in different keys.
musical adjustment of intervals for tuning

Examples

The piano was tuned using a special temperament to allow it to play in any key.
Historical organs often use a different temperament compared to modern instruments.
Debates about which temperament is best for harpsichord music have gone on for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equal temperament - a system of tuning in which the intervals are adjusted so that the octave is divided into equal steps; used in modern Western music

Etymology

From Latin 'temperamentum' (a mixing, proper measure), from 'temperare' (to mix, moderate).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpərəmənt / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəmənt / (BrE)
a person's characteristic or usual mood, especially as it affects their behaviour
usual mood or disposition

Examples

She has a calm temperament and rarely gets upset, even under pressure.
His artistic temperament made him sensitive to beauty and easily moved by emotion.
Teachers need patience and a balanced temperament to handle young children effectively.

Synset Examples

he has a happy disposition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disposition - a person's inherent qualities of mind and character
  • Synonym: personality - the combination of characteristics or qualities that form an individual's character
  • Degree Lower: mood - a temporary state of mind or feeling

Etymology

From Latin temperamentum (proper mixture), from temperare (to mix in due proportion).
