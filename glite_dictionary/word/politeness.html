politeness

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlaɪtnəs / (AmE), / pəˈlaɪtnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of showing good manners and respect for others in speech or behavior.
quality of showing good manners

Examples

Her politeness made a strong impression on everyone at the dinner party.
You should always treat your elders with politeness and respect.
He struggled to maintain his politeness even when he was frustrated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courtesy - Polite behavior or manners
  • Antonym: rudeness - Lack of manners; being impolite

Etymology

From French 'politesse', from Italian 'politezza', from Latin 'politus' (polished, refined).

2. NOUN

/ pəˈlaɪtnəs / (AmE), / pəˈlaɪtnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or act of showing consideration and respect for other people through good manners and courteous behavior
considerate and respectful behavior

Examples

Her politeness impressed everyone at the dinner party.
Simple acts of politeness can make a big difference in daily life.
He thanked his hosts with genuine politeness before leaving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courtesy - polite behavior; showing respect for others
  • Antonym: rudeness - lack of respect or consideration for others

Etymology

From polite + -ness; polite from Latin 'politus' (polished, refined).
