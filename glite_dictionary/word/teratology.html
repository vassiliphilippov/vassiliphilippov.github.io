teratology

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrəˈtɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəˈtɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of biology that deals with the study of congenital abnormalities and developmental malformations in organisms
study of developmental abnormalities
medical

Examples

She has published several articles in the field of teratology.
Advances in teratology have improved our understanding of birth defects.
Medical researchers often specialize in areas such as genetics or teratology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embryology - the branch of biology studying embryos and their development
  • Synonym: pathology - the study of diseases and their effects on the body

Etymology

From Greek 'teratos' (monster) + 'logia' (study).
