total

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊ.t̬əl / (AmE), / ˈtəʊ.təl / (BrE)
The amount you get when you add together a group of numbers or quantities.
sum of several numbers

Examples

The total for your groceries comes to $45.80.
She calculated the total of her monthly expenses.
Their fundraising efforts resulted in a total of over a thousand dollars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sum - the total amount resulting from the addition of two or more numbers
  • Synonym: aggregate - the total obtained by adding together different amounts

Etymology

From Medieval Latin totalis (entire, whole), from Latin totus (all, whole).
