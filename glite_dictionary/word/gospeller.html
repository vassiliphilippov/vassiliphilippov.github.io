gospeller

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑːspələr / (AmE), / ˈɡɒspələ / (BrE)
a person, especially a preacher, who spreads or interprets the teachings of the Christian Gospel.
preacher or interpreter of the Christian Gospel

Examples

The gospeller delivered a passionate sermon about kindness and forgiveness.
During the ceremony, the gospeller read aloud from the New Testament.
In medieval times, a gospeller played a crucial role in spreading Christian beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preacher - a person who delivers sermons, especially in religious contexts
  • Synonym: evangelist - a person who seeks to convert others to the Christian faith, especially by public preaching

Etymology

From 'gospel' + '-er', denoting a person who delivers or expounds the Gospel.
