Quercus wizlizenii

1. PROPN

/ kwer-kəs wɪz-lɪˈzeɪ-ni-aɪ / (AmE), / kwɜː-kəs wɪz-lɪˈzeɪ-nɪ-aɪ / (BrE)
A small shrubby evergreen oak tree native to western North America, especially in the foothills away from the coast; an important component of chaparral habitats.
a shrubby evergreen oak tree of western North America

Examples

The leaves of Quercus wizlizenii are smaller and tougher than those of other oaks in California.
The chaparral ecosystem relies on species like Quercus wizlizenii for structure and habitat.
Researchers studied how Quercus wizlizenii adapts to fire-prone environments in the foothills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coast live oak - an evergreen oak tree native to coastal California

Etymology

From Latin: Quercus (oak) and wizlizenii (honoring Dr. J. T. von Wizlizen, Austrian botanist).
