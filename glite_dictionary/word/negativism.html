negativism

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛɡəˌtɪvɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡətɪvɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the practice or tendency of consistently opposing or resisting suggestions, commands, or accepted opinions; habitual skepticism or contrariness, especially as a personality trait or a symptom in psychiatry.
habitual opposition or resistance

Examples

Despite the team's optimism, his negativism often dampened the mood in the office.
Therapists sometimes encounter negativism as a symptom in patients with certain mental health disorders.
Her habitual negativism made her difficult to work with during group projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pessimism - the tendency to see the worst aspect of things or believe the worst will happen
  • Synonym: opposition - resistance or dissent, expressed in action or argument
  • Antonym: positivism - a tendency to be positive or to believe only in what can be empirically proven

Etymology

From French 'négativisme', from 'négatif' (negative) + '-isme' (-ism).
