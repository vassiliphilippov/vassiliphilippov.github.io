handmaid

1. NOUN

/ ˈhænˌmeɪd / (AmE), / ˈhanˌmeɪd / (BrE)
a female servant or personal attendant who helps with domestic duties or accompanies a lady.
female personal attendant; maid

Examples

In the historical novel, the wealthy lady was always accompanied by her trusted handmaid.
The handmaid carried fresh linens and prepared the lady's chambers for the evening.
Even as a handmaid, she earned the respect of everyone in the household.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maid - a female domestic servant

Etymology

From Old English handgemaða, influenced by 'hand' and 'maid'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhændˌmeɪd / (AmE), / ˈhændˌmeɪd / (BrE)
A person or thing that serves in a subordinate or supportive role; an assistant or subordinate to another.
subordinate or assistant

Examples

Education is often considered the handmaid of progress in modern societies.
In the report, technology is described as a handmaid to the advancement of medical care.
She argued that philosophy should not merely be a handmaid to science, but an independent pursuit.

Synset Examples

theology should be the handmaiden of ethics
the state cannot be a servant of the church

Related Terms

  • Synonym: servant - a person who performs duties for others, especially a person employed in a house on domestic duties or as a personal attendant
  • Synonym: subordinate - a person under the authority or control of another
  • Antonym: master - a person with authority over others, especially a servant

Etymology

Old English handmægden, from 'hand' + 'maid' (female servant).
