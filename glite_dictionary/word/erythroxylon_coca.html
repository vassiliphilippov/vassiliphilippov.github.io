Erythroxylon coca

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɪθrɒkˈsaɪlɒn ˈkoʊkə / (AmE), / ɛˌrɪθrɒkˈsaɪlən ˈkəʊkə / (BrE)
A South American shrub whose leaves are traditionally chewed in the Andes and are the natural source of cocaine.
South American coca shrub; source of cocaine

Examples

The leaves of Erythroxylon coca have been used for centuries by indigenous peoples in the Andes.
Large plantations of Erythroxylon coca are often found in regions of Peru and Bolivia.
Scientists study Erythroxylon coca to better understand its active chemical compounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coca - the plant Erythroxylon coca whose leaves are chewed or processed for cocaine

Etymology

From Greek 'erythros' (red) + 'xylon' (wood) and the Quechua word 'kuka' (coca).
