to monetise

1. VERB

/ ˈmɑːnɪtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɒnɪtaɪz / (BrE)
to give legal value to or establish (something, especially a currency) as the legal tender of a country
make legal tender; give money status

Examples

In response to the new regulations, the government decided to monetise the digital tokens.
The central bank monetised the currency to stabilize the national economy.
Debates occurred in parliament before lawmakers voted to monetise the new polymer notes.

Synset Examples

They monetized the lira

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to demonetise - to deprive (a currency) of its status as legal tender

Etymology

From French 'monétiser', from Latin 'moneta' (money) + '-ise'.
