osier

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.ʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.zɪə / (BrE)
a flexible twig or branch, especially of a willow tree, often used for weaving baskets and similar crafts
flexible willow twig used in weaving

Examples

The artisan skillfully bent the osier into a basket shape.
Wicker furniture is often crafted from osier collected in the spring.
He gathered a bundle of fresh osier twigs along the riverbank for weaving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - a tree or shrub of the genus Salix, known for flexible branches

Etymology

From Old French *osier* (willow twig), from medieval Latin *auseria*, of unknown origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.ʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.zɪə / (BrE)
any of various willow trees or shrubs with flexible twigs used especially for making baskets and furniture
willow with pliable twigs for basketry

Examples

The artisan skillfully wove baskets from osier collected along the riverbank.
Several pieces of antique furniture were crafted from osier twigs.
The flexible nature of osier makes it ideal for traditional basketry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - a tree or shrub of the genus Salix

Etymology

From Old French *osier* (willow twig, willow tree), of uncertain origin.
