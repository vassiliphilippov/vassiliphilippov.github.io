3. NOUN

/ pɛt / (AmE), / pɛt / (BrE)
a temporary fit of sulkiness or ill temper, especially as a response to a perceived slight
fit of sulkiness; ill temper
archaic

Examples

After being passed over for promotion, he went into a pet and refused to speak to anyone.
She took a pet when she was not invited to the meeting.
The child sat in a silent pet, arms crossed and face turned away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tantrum - an uncontrolled outburst of anger and frustration, usually in a young child
  • Synonym: huff - a fit of petty annoyance or indignation
  • Antonym: good mood - a cheerful or pleasant frame of mind

Etymology

Probably a variant use of 'pet' (beloved person or animal), from Scottish English meaning 'a slight ailment or mood'.

5. NOUN

/ pɛt / (AmE), / pɛt / (BrE)
a domesticated animal kept for companionship or pleasure rather than for utility or food
domesticated animal kept for companionship

Examples

She adopted a pet rabbit from the shelter.
Many people find comfort and joy in owning a pet.
Dogs are often considered the most loyal type of pet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: companion animal - an animal kept primarily for a person's company or entertainment
  • Antonym: livestock - domesticated animals raised in an agricultural setting to produce labor or commodities

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *pet* (tamed animal), of unknown origin.
