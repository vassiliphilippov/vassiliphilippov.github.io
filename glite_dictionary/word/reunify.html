to reunify

1. VERB

/ ˌriːˈjuːnɪfaɪ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈjuːnɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make or become unified again, especially after being divided
to unify again after division

Examples

Leaders hope that the two nations will reunify within the next decade.
Efforts to reunify the company after the split have been ongoing for years.
The treaty helped reunify families separated by the border.

Synset Examples

Will Korea reunify?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unify - combine or bring together to form a single entity

Etymology

Formed from re- (again) + unify, from Latin unus (one) + facere (to make).
