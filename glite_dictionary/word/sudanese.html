Sudanese

1. ADJ

/ ˌsuːdəˈniːz / (AmE), / ˌsuːdəˈniːz / (BrE)
of or relating to Sudan or its people.
relating to Sudan or its people

Examples

Many Sudanese dishes feature rich spices and unique local ingredients.
She attended a seminar on Sudanese culture and history.
The Sudanese team won a silver medal in the athletics championship.

Synset Examples

the Sudanese desert

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sudan - the African country Sudan

Etymology

Formed from Sudan + -ese (denoting belonging or origin).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03132880-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˌsuːdəˈniːz / (AmE), / ˌsuːdəˈniːz / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Sudan
person from Sudan

Examples

Many Sudanese have emigrated to neighboring countries due to political instability.
The Sudanese celebrated their national holiday with music and traditional food.
A group of Sudanese students volunteered at the local community center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Egyptian - a native or inhabitant of Egypt
  • Synonym: Sudanese - relating to Sudan or its people (adjective form)

Etymology

From Sudan, the country in northeastern Africa, plus the English suffix -ese forming demonyms and adjectives.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09751584-n
Synset POS: n
