tertian

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɜrʃən / (AmE), / ˈtɜːʃən / (BrE)
(of music) relating to harmony, chords, or tonal systems that are based on intervals of a third, especially major thirds.
based on intervals of a third in music

Examples

The composer is known for his tertian approach, building harmonies from stacks of major thirds.
Many twentieth-century works experiment with tertian and quartal harmonic systems.
A tertian structure can give the music a unique and modern sound.

Synset Examples

a tertian tonal system

Etymology

From Latin tertianus, meaning 'of the third', from tertius 'third'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03135498-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɜːrʃən / (AmE), / ˈtɜːʃən / (BrE)
(of a fever or its symptoms) recurring every other day, especially in relation to malaria.
recurring every other day (esp. of fever)
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a tertian fever, which recurred every other day.
Cases of tertian malaria were historically common in certain regions.
Doctors recognized the pattern of tertian chills and fever typical of some malaria infections.

Synset Examples

tertian fever

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: quartan - (of a fever) recurring every fourth day

Etymology

From Latin tertianus (of the third), from tertius (third).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03135632-a
Synset POS: a
