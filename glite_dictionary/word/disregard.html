disregard

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsrɪˈɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˌdɪsrɪˈɡɑːd / (BrE)
Deliberate lack of attention or concern about something.
lack of attention or concern

Examples

Her complete disregard for the rules caused many problems at work.
The company acted with disregard for environmental regulations.
He showed a shocking disregard for his own safety during the rescue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neglect - failure to care for something properly
  • Antonym: attention - the act of paying close focus or care

Etymology

From dis- (do the opposite of) + regard, from Old French regarder (to look at).
