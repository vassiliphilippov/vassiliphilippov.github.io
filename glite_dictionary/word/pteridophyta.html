Pteridophyta

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrɪˈdɑfətə / (AmE), / ˌtɛrɪˈdɒfɪtə / (BrE)
A division of vascular plants that reproduce by spores rather than seeds, including ferns, horsetails, club mosses, and whisk ferns.
division of non-seed vascular plants

Examples

The Pteridophyta are distinguished from other plant divisions by their use of spores for reproduction.
Ferns, one of the most familiar groups within the Pteridophyta, thrive in moist, shaded environments.
Research into the Pteridophyta has helped scientists understand the evolution of vascular tissues in plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a member of Pteridophyta with feathery fronds
  • Antonym: seed plant - plants that reproduce by seeds instead of spores

Etymology

From Greek 'pteris' (fern) and 'phyton' (plant).
