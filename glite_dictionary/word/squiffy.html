squiffy

1. ADJ

/ ˈskwɪfi / (AmE), / ˈskwɪfi / (BrE)
slightly or very drunk; intoxicated
slightly or very drunk
informal british

Examples

After three glasses of wine, he was feeling quite squiffy.
She looked a bit squiffy at the party last night.
Don't mind him—he gets squiffy after just one pint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tipsy - slightly drunk
  • Antonym: sober - not affected by alcohol; not drunk
  • Synonym: plastered - very drunk, intoxicated
  • Synonym: drunk - intoxicated by alcohol

Etymology

Originating in late 19th-century British slang, possibly imitative or influenced by words like 'skew-whiff' (askew).
