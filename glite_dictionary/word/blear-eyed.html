blear-eyed

1. ADJ

/ ˈblɪr aɪd / (AmE), / ˈblɪə aɪd / (BrE)
Having eyes that look tired, dim, or blurred, often due to exhaustion or lack of sleep.
having tired or dim-looking eyes

Examples

After studying all night, she arrived at work looking blear-eyed and exhausted.
The blear-eyed travelers stumbled off the overnight flight into the morning light.
He sat at his desk, blear-eyed from a week of sleepless nights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleary-eyed - having eyes blurred or reddened from exhaustion or lack of sleep
  • Antonym: alert - fully awake and attentive; not tired

Etymology

From 'blear' (Old English *blǣr* meaning dim or watery), combined with 'eyed'.
