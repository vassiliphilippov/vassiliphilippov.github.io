debased

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈbeɪst / (AmE), / dɪˈbeɪst / (BrE)
reduced in quality, value, or moral character; corrupted or degraded.
reduced in quality or value

Examples

The country suffered under a debased currency, making daily life more difficult for everyone.
Critics argued that the film promoted a debased view of modern culture.
He regretted the debased form his art had taken over the years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrupt - having been made morally depraved or spoiled
  • Antonym: pure - free of contamination or corruption

Etymology

From the prefix 'de-' (down, away) + 'base' (to lower in value or dignity).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈbeɪst / (AmE), / dɪˈbeɪst / (BrE)
Reduced in quality, value, or purity by being mixed with impurities or having its original state corrupted.
reduced in quality or purity

Examples

The coin was made of debased metal, no longer pure silver.
Some argue that modern art is debased by commercial interests.
The wine tasted strange, clearly debased with water or other cheap additives.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pure - free from impurities; not mixed with anything else

Etymology

From the verb 'debase', which comes from Middle English 'debasen', from Old French 'de-' (expressing removal) + 'base' (low).

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈbeɪst / (AmE), / dɪˈbeɪst / (BrE)
(of something) reduced in quality, value, or moral character.
lowered in quality or value

Examples

After years of inflation, the debased coins were almost worthless.
The once noble traditions became debased by greed and selfishness.
Their language had become so debased that it was hard to understand their meaning.

Synset Examples

a debased currency
The worth of citizenship is devalued when information is censored

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrupt - morally degraded or impure
  • Synonym: devalued - reduced in value
  • Antonym: exalted - raised in rank or value

Etymology

From Old French *debaser* (to lower), from de- (down) + base (low).
