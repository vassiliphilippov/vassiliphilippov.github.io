aptness

1. NOUN

/ ˈæpt.nəs / (AmE), / ˈæpt.nəs / (BrE)
a natural tendency or disposition to behave in a particular way or for something to happen
tendency or disposition

Examples

The aptness of children to imitate adults can surprise their parents.
Her aptness for learning new languages made her an outstanding translator.
There is a certain aptness in the way rumors spread quickly in small communities.

Synset Examples

the aptness of iron to rust
the propensity of disease to spread

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propensity - a natural inclination or tendency
  • Synonym: inclination - a tendency to feel or behave in a particular way

Etymology

From English 'apt' + '-ness', from Latin 'aptus' (fit, suitable).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæpt.nəs / (AmE), / ˈæpt.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being especially fitting or appropriate for a particular situation
quality of being suitable or appropriate

Examples

The aptness of his response impressed everyone at the meeting.
Critics praised the aptness of the film's title.
She admired the aptness with which he chose his words.

Synset Examples

the phrase had considerable aptness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appropriateness - the quality of being suitable or proper in the circumstances
  • Antonym: inappropriateness - the state of being unsuitable or not fitting

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French *aptesse*, from Latin *aptus* (fit, suitable).
