sectionalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛkʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛkʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of dividing something into sections or parts, often by setting boundaries or partitions
the act of dividing into sections

Examples

The sectionalization of the warehouse improved operational efficiency.
Proper sectionalization of the electrical grid can prevent widespread blackouts.
The company underwent sectionalization to better manage its diverse business units.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partition - the action or state of dividing or being divided into parts
  • Synonym: segmentation - the division into separate parts or segments

Etymology

From 'sectional' + '-ization', denoting the process of making into sections.
