crinion

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪniˌɒn / (AmE), / ˈkrɪnɪɒn / (BrE)
the point where the hairline meets the midpoint of the forehead
midpoint of hairline on forehead
medical

Examples

The surgeon marked the crinion before beginning the forehead procedure.
Locating the crinion is essential in anthropological measurements of the skull.
The distance from the crinion to the nasion was carefully recorded during the study.

Etymology

From Greek 'krinion' (lily), used in anatomy for naming landmarks referring to the head.
