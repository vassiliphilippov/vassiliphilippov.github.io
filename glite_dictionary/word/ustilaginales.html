Ustilaginales

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːstɪˌlædʒəˈneɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌjuːstɪˌlædʒəˈneɪliːz / (BrE)
An order of parasitic fungi known for causing smut diseases, sometimes classified under the class Tiliomycetes.
order of parasitic smut fungi

Examples

Many agricultural crops are susceptible to infections from Ustilaginales, which can reduce yield and quality.
The biology class studied the life cycle of fungi in the order Ustilaginales.
Smuts caused by Ustilaginales are particularly problematic for wheat and corn farmers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smut - fungal disease causing dark, powdery masses on plants

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Ustilago (a type of smut fungus), itself from Latin 'ustilare' (to burn), referring to the appearance of infected plants.

an order of parasitic fungi (smuts)
medical

Examples

Many cereal crops are vulnerable to infection by fungi in the Ustilaginales order.
Research on Ustilaginales has contributed to better management of smut diseases in agriculture.
Biologists often debate the exact taxonomic position of Ustilaginales within fungal classification schemes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smut - a parasitic fungus causing plant disease

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Ustilago, from Latin 'ustilare' (to burn), referring to burnt appearance of infected plant tissue.
