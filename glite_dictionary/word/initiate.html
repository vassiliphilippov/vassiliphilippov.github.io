initiate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈnɪʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈnɪʃieɪt / (BrE)
to cause something to begin; to start or launch a process, action, or event
to start or begin something

Examples

The company decided to initiate a new training program for its employees.
Government officials met to initiate discussions on environmental reform.
The scientist initiated a major research project last year.

Synset Examples

He initiated a new program
Start a foundation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to begin - to start doing something; to commence
  • Synonym: launch - to start or introduce something new, especially with excitement or publicity
  • Antonym: to terminate - to bring to an end; to finish

Etymology

From Latin 'initiare' (to begin), from 'initium' (beginning).

WordNet ID: oewn-01631641-v
Synset POS: v
