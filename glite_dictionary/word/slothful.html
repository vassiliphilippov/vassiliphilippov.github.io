slothful

1. ADJ

/ ˈsloʊθ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈsləʊθ.fəl / (BrE)
Lazy; unwilling to work or make any effort; habitually inactive or idle.
unwilling to work; lazy

Examples

He was criticized for his slothful approach to his studies, rarely completing assignments on time.
The slothful workers lounged around instead of finishing their tasks promptly.
A slothful attitude can prevent you from reaching your full potential.

Synset Examples

faineant kings under whose rule the country languished
an indolent hanger-on
too lazy to wash the dishes
slothful employees
the unemployed are not necessarily work-shy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lazy - unwilling to work or use energy
  • Synonym: indolent - habitually lazy or avoiding activity
  • Antonym: industrious - diligent and hardworking
  • Antonym: diligent - showing care and effort in one's work

Etymology

From Middle English, from 'sloth' + '-ful'. 'Sloth' from Old English 'slæwth' (slowness, indolence).
