Lonchocarpus

1. NOUN

/ lɒnˈkoʊkɑːrpəs / (AmE), / lɒnˈkəʊkɑːpəs / (BrE)
a genus of chiefly tropical American shrubs and trees bearing pinnate leaves and red or white flowers
genus of tropical American shrubs and trees

Examples

Many native peoples use the roots of Lonchocarpus species for traditional fishing methods.
The bright white flowers of Lonchocarpus attract a wide range of pollinators in the rainforest.
Researchers studied the insecticidal properties of compounds extracted from Lonchocarpus plants.

Etymology

From Greek lonchē (spear) and karpos (fruit), referring to the shape of the fruit.

2. NOUN

/ ˌlɑntʃoʊˈkɑːrpəs / (AmE), / ˌlɒntʃəˈkɑːpəs / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly tropical American shrubs and trees with pinnate leaves and red or white flowers, some species of which are used as sources of rotenone.
genus of tropical American shrubs and trees

Examples

The roots of Lonchocarpus are traditionally used by indigenous people as a fish poison.
Several species in the Lonchocarpus genus are valued for their medicinal properties.
The colorful flowers of Lonchocarpus trees attract a wide variety of pollinators.

Etymology

From Greek 'lonchos' (spear) and 'karpos' (fruit), referring to the shape of the fruit.
