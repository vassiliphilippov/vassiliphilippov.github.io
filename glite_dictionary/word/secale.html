Secale

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈkeɪli / (AmE), / sɪˈkaːli / (BrE)
A genus of cereal grasses that includes rye, cultivated for its grain.
genus of grass including rye

Examples

Secale is commonly planted in temperate regions for its winter hardiness.
The most familiar member of the genus Secale is Secale cereale, known as rye.
Researchers analyzed the genetic diversity of wild Secale populations for crop improvement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rye - a hardy cereal grass grown for its grain

Etymology

From Latin *Secale*, the scientific genus name for rye.
