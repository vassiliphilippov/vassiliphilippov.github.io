Drymoglossum

1. PROPN

/ ˌdraɪmoʊˈɡlɒsəm / (AmE), / ˌdraɪməˈɡlɒsəm / (BrE)
A small genus of epiphytic ferns native to Madagascar, tropical Asia, and New Guinea.
genus of epiphytic ferns

Examples

The Drymoglossum species are often found growing on tree trunks in tropical rainforests.
Botanists have identified several new ferns belonging to the genus Drymoglossum.
The delicate leaves of Drymoglossum make it a favorite among fern collectors.

Etymology

From Greek 'drymos' (wood, forest) and 'glossa' (tongue), referring to the plant's habitat and leaf shape.

A genus of epiphytic ferns native to Madagascar, tropical Asia, and New Guinea.
genus of tropical epiphytic ferns

Examples

Several species within Drymoglossum are well adapted to life as epiphytes high in rainforest canopies.
Botanists discovered a new member of the Drymoglossum genus in New Guinea.
The delicate leaves of Drymoglossum make it popular among fern enthusiasts.

Etymology

From Greek 'drymos' (woodland) and 'glossa' (tongue), referring to the plant's habitat and tongue-like leaves.
