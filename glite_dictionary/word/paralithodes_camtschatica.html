Paralithodes camtschatica

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˌlɪˈθoʊdiːz kæmˈtʃætɪkə / (AmE), / ˌparəˌlɪˈθəʊdiːz kamˈtʃatɪkə / (BrE)
a large edible crab of northern Pacific waters, especially found along the coasts of Alaska and Japan, known as the red king crab
large edible red king crab of the northern Pacific

Examples

The Paralithodes camtschatica is highly valued for its sweet and tender meat.
Fishing quotas are strictly regulated to protect the Paralithodes camtschatica population in Alaskan waters.
Many seafood lovers consider Paralithodes camtschatica a delicacy on restaurant menus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red king crab - common name for Paralithodes camtschatica

Etymology

From Greek parā (beside) + lithos (stone) and Latinized place name Kamtschatica (Kamchatka).
