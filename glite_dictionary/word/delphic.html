Delphic

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛlfɪk / (AmE), / ˈdɛlfɪk / (BrE)
(of a statement or message) deliberately obscure or ambiguous, especially in a manner that seems prophetic or enigmatic.
obscurely prophetic or ambiguous

Examples

Her response was so Delphic that nobody could tell what she truly meant.
The politician's Delphic remarks left the press guessing about his real intentions.
Analysts struggled to interpret the CEO's Delphic statement about the company's future.

Synset Examples

Delphic pronouncements
an oracular message

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oracular - giving prophecies in a mysterious or ambiguous manner
  • Antonym: clear - easy to understand

Etymology

From Latin Delphicus, from Greek Delphikos, referring to Delphi, site of the ancient oracle.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɛlfɪk / (AmE), / ˈdɛlfɪk / (BrE)
connected with Delphi or the oracle of Apollo at Delphi, especially describing something obscure or ambiguous in meaning.
of or relating to Delphi or its oracle

Examples

The senator made a Delphic remark that left many wondering about his true intentions.
Her advice was so Delphic that nobody in the group understood what she really meant.
Many ancient rulers sought guidance from the Delphic oracle before starting wars.

Synset Examples

Delphic oracle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambiguous - open to more than one interpretation; not having one obvious meaning
  • Antonym: clear - easy to perceive, understand, or interpret

Etymology

From Latin 'Delphicus', from Greek 'Delphikos', relating to Delphi.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03072006-a
Synset POS: a
