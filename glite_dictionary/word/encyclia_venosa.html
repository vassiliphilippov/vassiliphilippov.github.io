Encyclia venosa

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈsɪkliə vəˈnoʊsə / (AmE), / ɛnˈsɪkliə vɪˈnəʊsə / (BrE)
A species of Mexican epiphytic orchid bearing pale green or yellow-green flowers with a white, purple-veined lip.
Mexican epiphytic orchid species

Examples

The greenhouse featured a stunning specimen of Encyclia venosa in full bloom.
Orchid enthusiasts prize Encyclia venosa for its distinctive veined lip.
Botanists documented the habitat of Encyclia venosa in several regions of Mexico.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Encyclia' is a genus name, and 'venosa' means 'veined' in Latin, referring to the flower's veined lip.
