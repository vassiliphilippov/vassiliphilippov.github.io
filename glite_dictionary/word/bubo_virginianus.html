Bubo virginianus

1. NOUN

/ ˈbjuːboʊ vɜːrdʒɪˈneɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈbjuːbəʊ vɜːdʒɪˈneɪnəs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the great horned owl, a large brown owl native to North America, notable for its tufted ears and deep hooting call.
great horned owl's scientific name

Examples

Ornithologists observed a Bubo virginianus perched on a high branch at dusk.
The deep hooting of Bubo virginianus can be heard throughout North American forests.
A Bubo virginianus spreads its wings impressively when threatened by predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: great horned owl - A large North American owl with ear tufts (Bubo virginianus)

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Bubo' meaning 'owl', and species name 'virginianus' indicating Virginia, where the species was first noted.
