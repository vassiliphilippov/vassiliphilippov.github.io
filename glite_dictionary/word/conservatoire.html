conservatoire

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsɝː.və.twɑr / (AmE), / kənˈsɜː.və.twɑː / (BrE)
a school or college for the study of classical music or the fine arts, especially in Europe
school for music or fine arts

Examples

She was accepted into the prestigious conservatoire in Paris to study piano.
Many famous composers have trained at a conservatoire.
The conservatoire offers masterclasses with world-renowned artists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conservatory - a school for teaching music or the arts

Etymology

From French 'conservatoire', from Italian 'conservatorio', originally referring to an orphanage where music was taught.
