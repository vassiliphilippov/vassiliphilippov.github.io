simplification

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪmpləfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪmplɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
an explanation or representation that removes unnecessary details to make something easier to understand
removing complexity or details

Examples

The teacher's simplification of the theory helped the students understand the main concept.
While useful, this simplification may miss some important factors in the analysis.
Their simplification of the process saved both time and resources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explanation - a statement that makes something clear
  • Antonym: complication - a result or process that makes things more difficult or complex

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'simplificatio', from Latin 'simplificare' (to make simple).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɪmpləfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪmplɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something less complicated by removing unnecessary details
making something less complicated

Examples

The simplification of the tax code was welcomed by small business owners.
His report called for the simplification of company procedures to boost productivity.
Effective communication often relies on the simplification of complex ideas.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: complication - the process of making something more complex or difficult
  • Synonym: streamlining - the process of improving efficiency by removing unnecessary parts

Etymology

From French simplification and Latin simplificatio, from simplex (simple) + facere (to make).

3. NOUN

/ ˌsɪmpləfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪmplɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something less complicated or easier to understand
the act of making something simpler

Examples

The new software update is a simplification that makes the interface easier for users.
His explanation was a simplification of a very complex process, but it helped everyone understand.
Too much simplification can sometimes lead to misunderstandings or loss of important details.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: complication - the act of making something more complex
  • Synonym: to simplify - to make something simpler or less complicated

Etymology

From French 'simplification', from Latin 'simplificare' (to make simple).
