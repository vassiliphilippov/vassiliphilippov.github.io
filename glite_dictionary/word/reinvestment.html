reinvestment

1. NOUN

/ ˌriː.ɪnˈvɛst.mənt / (AmE), / ˌriː.ɪnˈvɛst.mənt / (BrE)
the act of investing money again in the same project, asset, or venture after the initial investment
the act of investing again

Examples

The reinvestment of profits helped the company expand more quickly.
Regular reinvestment is key to growing your retirement fund.
Investors approved the reinvestment of dividends into additional shares.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investment - the act of putting money into something for profit

Etymology

Formed from 're-' (again) + 'investment' (from Latin 'investire'), meaning to invest again.
