impoliteness

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪm.pəˈlaɪt.nəs / (AmE), / ˌɪm.pəˈlaɪt.nəs / (BrE)
lack of courtesy or good manners; rude or discourteous behavior.
lack of politeness; rudeness

Examples

His impoliteness at the dinner table surprised everyone present.
The manager refused to tolerate any impoliteness from his staff toward customers.
She apologized for her earlier impoliteness when she realized she had upset her friend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rudeness - lack of manners or refinement; rough or impolite behavior
  • Antonym: politeness - the quality of showing good manners or respect

Etymology

From im- (not) + politeness, ultimately from Latin 'politus' (polished, refined).
