Nafud

1. PROPN

/ nɑːˈfuːd / (AmE), / næˈfuːd / (BrE)
A desert in northern Saudi Arabia, known for its red sand and violent winds.
large desert in northern Saudi Arabia

Examples

The Nafud is renowned for its striking red dunes and challenging terrain.
Many explorers have traversed the Nafud despite its harsh winds and searing temperatures.
Archaeological finds in the Nafud desert have shed light on ancient human migration routes.

Etymology

From Arabic النفود (an-Nafūd), meaning 'the deserts' or 'blown sand'.
