Jakob Hermandszoon

1. PROPN

/ ˈjɑːkoʊb hɛrˈmændz.oʊn / (AmE), / ˈjɑːkɒb hɛˈmanz.suːn / (BrE)
(1560–1609) Dutch Protestant theologian who founded Arminianism, opposing the doctrine of absolute predestination taught by John Calvin.
Dutch theologian, founder of Arminianism

Examples

Jakob Hermandszoon was instrumental in challenging the prevailing Calvinist doctrine in the early seventeenth century.
Many modern Protestant denominations have been influenced by the teachings of Jakob Hermandszoon.
The debate between Jakob Hermandszoon and John Calvin shaped the future of Reformed theology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jacobus Arminius - Latinized name for Jakob Hermandszoon, Dutch theologian
  • Antonym: John Calvin - French theologian whose doctrine of predestination Arminius opposed

Etymology

Dutch form of the name Jacob Hermanszoon; known in Latin as Jacobus Arminius.
