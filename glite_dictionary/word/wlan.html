WLAN

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl.ju ˈɛl ˈæn / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.l̩.juː ˈɛl ˈæn / (BrE)
a local area network that uses radio signals instead of wires to connect devices within a limited area, such as a building or campus
wireless local area network

Examples

Most offices now offer a WLAN so employees can work wirelessly throughout the building.
The hotel lobby provides free WLAN access for all guests.
Before joining the WLAN, make sure your device has the latest security updates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wi-Fi - technology used for wireless local area networking
  • Degree Lower: LAN - local area network; a network connecting computers in a small area

Etymology

Acronym from Wireless Local Area Network.
