resiliency

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɪliənsi / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪliənsi / (BrE)
the ability of a material to return to its original shape or position after being bent, stretched, or compressed, provided the limits of elasticity are not exceeded
ability of material to return to original shape

Examples

The resiliency of rubber allows it to bounce back after being stretched.
Engineers tested the resiliency of the alloy under repeated stress.
Foam mattresses are valued for their resiliency and comfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elasticity - the quality of a material to resume its normal shape after being stretched or compressed
  • Antonym: brittleness - the quality of breaking easily without significant deformation

Etymology

From Latin 'resilire' (to spring back).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɪliənsi / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪliənsi / (BrE)
the ability to recover quickly from setbacks, difficulties, or change; the capacity to spring back into shape or to its original state
ability to recover or spring back

Examples

After the setback, her resiliency allowed her to rebuild her life rapidly.
Engineers test the resiliency of bridges to ensure safety during earthquakes.
The team's resiliency showed when they bounced back to win the championship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resilience - the capacity to recover quickly from difficulties; toughness

Etymology

From resilient + -cy; resilient from Latin resiliens, present participle of resilire (to leap back).
