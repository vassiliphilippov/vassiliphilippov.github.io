bungaloid

1. ADJ

/ ˈbʌŋ.ɡə.lɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈbʌŋ.ɡə.lɔɪd / (BrE)
(of a place or area) characterized by or resembling bungalows, especially architecturally or in style.
resembling or characterized by bungalows

Examples

Many of the new developments were distinctly bungaloid in appearance and layout.
She disliked the bungaloid sprawl that replaced the old city center.
The bungaloid suburbs stretched out for miles beyond the historic district.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bungalow - a low house, typically with a broad front porch

Etymology

Formed from 'bungalow' + '-oid' (meaning 'resembling' or 'like').
