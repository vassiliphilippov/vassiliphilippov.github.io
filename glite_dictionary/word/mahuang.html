mahuang

1. NOUN

/ mɑːˈhwɑːŋ / (AmE), / mɑːˈhwɑːŋ / (BrE)
A Chinese plant of the genus Ephedra, especially Ephedra sinica, used in traditional medicine and as a source of ephedrine.
Chinese plant yielding ephedrine
medical

Examples

The herbalist recommended mahuang to help with respiratory issues.
Researchers are studying the effects of mahuang on athletic performance.
Traditional Chinese medicine has used mahuang for centuries to treat asthma and colds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ephedra - a genus of shrubby plants in dry regions, source of ephedrine

Etymology

From Mandarin Chinese máhuáng (麻黄, meaning 'ephedra').
