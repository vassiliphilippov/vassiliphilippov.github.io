unimposing

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.ɪmˈpoʊ.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌn.ɪmˈpəʊ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
not striking or impressive in appearance or effect; modest or unremarkable.
not impressive or striking

Examples

The building looked rather unimposing from the outside, but it was beautiful inside.
Despite his unimposing appearance, he was a brilliant scientist.
She delivered an unimposing performance that failed to capture the audience's attention.

Synset Examples

on the whole the results of this system are unimposing

Related Terms

  • Antonym: impressive - evoking admiration or awe; striking
  • Synonym: unremarkable - not particularly interesting or noteworthy

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'imposing', from Latin 'imponere' (to place upon).
