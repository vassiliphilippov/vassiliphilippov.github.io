indecent

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdisənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdiːsənt / (BrE)
not conforming to accepted standards of morality or propriety; improper or offensive, especially in social contexts.
improper; not socially acceptable

Examples

Her dress was considered indecent for such a formal occasion.
The comedian was criticized for using indecent language on television.
He was fined for making indecent gestures in public.

Synset Examples

was buried with indecent haste
indecorous behavior
language unbecoming to a lady
unseemly to use profanity
moved to curb their untoward ribaldry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improper - not in accordance with accepted standards or rules
  • Antonym: decent - conforming to accepted standards; proper

Etymology

From Latin 'indecens', meaning 'unbecoming or unsuitable'.

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪnˈdisənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdiːsnt / (BrE)
offending against accepted standards of sexual morality or decency; improper or obscene in conduct or appearance
offending sexual morals; improper

Examples

The teacher found the student's indecent drawing highly inappropriate for the classroom.
Wearing such an indecent outfit on national television caused public outrage.
The film was banned in several countries due to its indecent scenes.

Synset Examples

a bathing suit considered indecent by local standards

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obscene - offensive or disgusting by accepted standards of morality, especially in sexual matters
  • Antonym: decent - conforming to accepted standards of morality or respectability

Etymology

From Latin indecent-, indecens (unbecoming, unseemly), from in- (not) + decent- (fitting, proper).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdisənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdiːsənt / (BrE)
not conforming to accepted standards of morality or good taste, especially with regard to sexual matters; offensive or improper.
offensive or improper, especially sexually

Examples

The comedian’s jokes were considered indecent by many members of the audience.
Wearing such indecent clothing was forbidden at the conservative event.
He was fined for making an indecent gesture in public.

Synset Examples

an earthy but not indecent story
an indecent gesture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improper - not in accordance with accepted rules, standards, or morals
  • Synonym: obscene - offensive or disgusting by accepted standards, especially sexually
  • Antonym: decent - conforming to accepted standards of morality or respectability

Etymology

From Latin indecentem (unbecoming, unseemly), from in- (not) + decentem (becoming, proper).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00686462-a
Synset POS: a
