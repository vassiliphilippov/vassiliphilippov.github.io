deprecative

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛprəkətɪv / (AmE), / ˈdɛprəkətɪv / (BrE)
expressing disapproval or intended to make someone or something seem unimportant or of little value
expressing disapproval or belittling

Examples

Her deprecative tone made it clear she did not value my efforts.
The review was full of deprecative remarks about the novel's style.
He gave a deprecative shrug when asked about his achievements.

Synset Examples

belittling comments
managed a deprecating smile at the compliment
deprecatory remarks about the book
a slighting remark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deprecatory - expressing disapproval or criticism
  • Synonym: belittling - making someone or something seem unimportant
  • Antonym: approving - expressing or showing approval

Etymology

From Latin deprecativus (‘praying against, averting by prayer’), from deprecat- (‘warded off’).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɛprəkəˌtɪv / (AmE), / ˈdɛprəkətɪv / (BrE)
Tending to express disapproval or criticism; disapproving.
expressing disapproval

Examples

His deprecative remarks about the proposal made everyone uncomfortable.
The teacher’s deprecative tone discouraged the students from sharing their ideas.
She cast a deprecative glance at the noisy crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disapproving - expressing an unfavorable opinion; showing disapproval

Etymology

From Latin deprecativus (praying against, deprecating), from deprecari (to ward off by prayer, to avert).
