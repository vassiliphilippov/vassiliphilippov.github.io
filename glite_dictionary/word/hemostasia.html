hemostasia

1. NOUN

/ hiːməˈsteɪziə / (AmE), / hiːməˈsteɪzɪə / (BrE)
The surgical procedure of stopping the flow of blood, typically using medical tools such as a hemostat.
surgical stopping of blood flow
medical

Examples

The surgeon achieved hemostasia before continuing with the operation.
Proper hemostasia is crucial to prevent excessive bleeding during procedures.
Advances in surgical instruments have improved the safety and speed of hemostasia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemostasis - stoppage of bleeding (general process or state)

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'haima' (blood) and 'stasis' (standing, stopping).
