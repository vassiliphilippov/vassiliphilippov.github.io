disconfirming

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪskənˈfɜrmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌdɪskənˈfɜːmɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a test or evidence) indicating the absence of a disease, microorganism, or specific condition; not supporting the presence of something suspected.
indicating absence of disease or condition
medical

Examples

The doctor assured me that the blood test was disconfirming for any infections.
A disconfirming biopsy result can bring relief to patients who fear cancer.
Repeated disconfirming results ruled out the initial diagnosis.

Synset Examples

the HIV test was negative

Related Terms

  • Antonym: confirming - supporting the presence or existence of a disease or condition

Etymology

From prefix dis-, meaning 'not', and confirm, ultimately from Latin confirmare.

2. ADJ

/ ˌdɪskənˈfɝːmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌdɪskənˈfɜːmɪŋ / (BrE)
showing that something is invalid, untrue, or incorrect; refuting.
refuting; showing as untrue or invalid

Examples

The scientist presented disconfirming evidence that challenged the original theory.
His explanation was contradicted by several disconfirming findings in later studies.
If you encounter disconfirming data, you should reconsider your hypothesis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: confirming - showing that something is true or valid

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'confirm' + '-ing'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02365268-s
Synset POS: s
