Deng Xiaoping

1. PROPN

/ dʌŋ ˌʃaʊˈpɪŋ / (AmE), / dɛŋ ˌʃaʊˈpɪŋ / (BrE)
(1904–1997) Chinese statesman who was the paramount leader of China from the late 1970s to the early 1990s, initiating major economic reforms and opening China to the global market.
Chinese leader and reformer (1904–1997)

Examples

Deng Xiaoping introduced sweeping economic reforms that transformed China's economy in the 1980s.
Scholars often credit Deng Xiaoping for modernizing China and improving living standards.
The policies of Deng Xiaoping marked a significant shift from the earlier Maoist era.

Etymology

From Chinese 邓小平 (Dèng Xiǎopíng).
