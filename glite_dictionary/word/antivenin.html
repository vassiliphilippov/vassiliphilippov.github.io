antivenin

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tiˈviː.nɪn / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˈviː.nɪn / (BrE)
a substance containing antibodies, used to counteract the effects of venom from the bite or sting of snakes, insects, or other animals
antitoxin for venomous bites or stings
medical

Examples

After the snake bite, the doctors quickly administered antivenin to save his life.
Supplies of antivenin are stocked in hospitals located in regions with many venomous snakes.
The effectiveness of antivenin depends on how soon it is given after envenomation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antivenom - substance used to counteract venom, synonym for antivenin

Etymology

From anti- (against) + Latin venenum (poison).
