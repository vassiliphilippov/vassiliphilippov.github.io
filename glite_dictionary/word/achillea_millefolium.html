Achillea millefolium

1. NOUN

/ əˈkɪliə ˌmɪliˈfoʊliəm / (AmE), / əˈkɪliə ˌmɪlɪˈfəʊlɪəm / (BrE)
A Eurasian herb, commonly known as yarrow, with strong scent, feathery leaves, and small white or pink flowers, often found in meadows and naturalized in North America.
a herbaceous plant; yarrow

Examples

Achillea millefolium is a popular choice in herbal gardens for its traditional medicinal uses.
Many meadows in Europe and North America are dotted with the white flower heads of Achillea millefolium.
The finely divided leaves of Achillea millefolium make it easily distinguishable from other wildflowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yarrow - common name for Achillea millefolium

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Achillea' after the Greek hero Achilles, who is said to have used plants of this type to treat wounds; 'millefolium' means 'thousand leaves' in Latin, referring to the finely divided foliage.
