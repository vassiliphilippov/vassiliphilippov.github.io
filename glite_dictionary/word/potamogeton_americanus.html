Potamogeton americanus

1. NOUN

/ poʊˌtæməˈdʒiːtən əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnəs / (AmE), / pəʊˌtæməˈdʒiːtən əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnəs / (BrE)
A species of pondweed with floating leaves, native to the northern United States and Europe.
a species of floating-leaf pondweed

Examples

The slow-moving river was filled with Potamogeton americanus thriving beneath the surface.
Many waterfowl feed on the leaves of Potamogeton americanus during the summer months.
Researchers studied the distribution of Potamogeton americanus in freshwater lakes across the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pondweed - an aquatic plant of the genus Potamogeton

Etymology

From Greek potamos (river) and geiton (neighbor), meaning 'neighbor of rivers,' with the species name 'americanus' indicating its presence in America.
