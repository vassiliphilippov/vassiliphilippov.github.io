phantasm

1. NOUN

/ ˈfænˌtæzəm / (AmE), / ˈfæn.tæz.əm / (BrE)
Something that exists only in perception or the imagination; a figment, illusion, or apparition.
illusory perception; apparition

Examples

The old castle was said to be haunted by a phantasm that wandered its halls at night.
Some believe that the shimmering figure was merely a phantasm created by tired eyes.
In his dream, he chased a phantasm through an endless fog, never able to catch up.

Synset Examples

a ghostly apparition at midnight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illusion - something that deceives the senses; a false appearance
  • Synonym: apparition - a ghost or ghostlike image

Etymology

From Greek *phantasma* (image, apparition), via Latin *phantasma*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfæn.tæz.əm / (AmE), / ˈfan.taz.əm / (BrE)
A ghostly or illusory figure; something that appears to exist but has no physical reality, especially as a spectral apparition.
ghostly or illusory figure

Examples

A pale phantasm drifted through the abandoned mansion, sending chills down their spines.
In the dim candlelight, he wondered if the shadow was real or merely a phantasm of his imagination.
The legend spoke of a phantasm that haunted the old forest every moonless night.

Synset Examples

we were unprepared for the apparition that confronted us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apparition - a ghost or ghostlike image of a person
  • Synonym: specter - a ghost; something widely feared as a possible unpleasant or dangerous occurrence

Etymology

From Greek 'phantasma' (image, apparition), from 'phanatzein' (to make visible).
