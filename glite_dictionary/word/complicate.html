to complicate

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑːm.pləˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.plɪ.keɪt / (BrE)
to make something more complex, difficult, or involved than it was before
make more complex or difficult

Examples

Please don't complicate the plan with unnecessary details.
Changing the schedule now would only complicate things further.
She tends to complicate simple problems by overthinking them.

Synset Examples

refine a design or pattern

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to simplify - to make something simpler or easier to do or understand

Etymology

From Latin 'complicatus', past participle of 'complicare' (to fold together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00401789-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑːm.plɪ.keɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.plɪ.keɪt / (BrE)
to make something more difficult or complex by adding new elements or factors
make more difficult or complex

Examples

Additional rules will only complicate the process for everyone involved.
The situation was already tense, but her arrival complicated things even more.
Be careful not to complicate the instructions with too much technical jargon.

Synset Examples

There was a new development that complicated the matter

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to simplify - to make simpler or easier

Etymology

From Latin complicatus, past participle of complicare (to fold together, entwine).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00403446-v
Synset POS: v
