Eriocaulaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛri.oʊkɔːˈleɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌɛri.əʊkɔːˈleɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly tropical aquatic or bog herbs, commonly known as pipeworts.
family of aquatic or bog herbs (pipeworts)
medical

Examples

The Eriocaulaceae family includes many species adapted to wet habitats.
Some botanists specialize in studying Eriocaulaceae and related aquatic plants.
Plants in the Eriocaulaceae are often found in tropical and subtropical wetlands.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Eriocaulon.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛr.i.oʊ.kjuːˈæl.ə.siː / (AmE), / ˌɛr.i.əʊ.kjʊˈæl.ə.siː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly tropical aquatic or bog herbs, commonly known as pipeworts.
family of tropical aquatic or bog herbs (pipeworts)

Examples

The Eriocaulaceae family includes many species adapted to wet, tropical environments.
Botanists studied members of the Eriocaulaceae in the Amazon basin.
Pipeworts, which belong to the Eriocaulaceae, are often found in marshy habitats.

Etymology

From New Latin Eriocaulaceae, based on genus Eriocaulon, from Greek erion (wool) + kaulos (stem).
