woe

1. NOUN

/ woʊ / (AmE), / wəʊ / (BrE)
Great sorrow or distress; intense misery resulting from misfortune or affliction.
great sorrow or misery

Examples

After losing his job and his home, he was overwhelmed by woe.
She poured out her woes to her closest friend over coffee.
Stories of woe filled the news after the disaster struck the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misery - great unhappiness or suffering
  • Antonym: joy - a feeling of great happiness

Etymology

From Middle English *wo*, from Old English *wā* (woe, misery, exclamation of sorrow).

2. NOUN

/ woʊ / (AmE), / wəʊ / (BrE)
Great sorrow or distress; intense sadness or grief.
great sorrow or distress

Examples

The news of her passing filled him with woe.
She wrote a poem expressing her deepest woe.
Years of hardship and woe had left their mark on the village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorrow - a feeling of deep distress caused by loss or disappointment
  • Antonym: joy - a feeling of great pleasure and happiness

Etymology

From Middle English 'wo', from Old English 'wā' (woe, alas), related to German 'Weh' (pain).
