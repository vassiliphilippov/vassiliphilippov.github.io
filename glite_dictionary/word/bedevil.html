to bedevil

1. VERB

/ bɪˈdɛvəl / (AmE), / bɪˈdɛvəl / (BrE)
to cause someone great confusion or difficulty; to perplex or bewilder persistently
to confuse or perplex persistently

Examples

Technical problems continued to bedevil the research team throughout the year.
The ambiguous instructions only served to bedevil the new employees.
Unanswered questions about the policy still bedevil the committee.

Synset Examples

These questions confuse even the experts
This question completely threw me
This question befuddled even the teacher

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confuse - cause (someone) to become bewildered or perplexed
  • Synonym: to bewilder - to cause to become perplexed and confused
  • Antonym: to clarify - to make (an idea or statement) clear

Etymology

From be- (intensive prefix) + devil, originally meaning 'to torment like a devil'.

2. VERB

/ bəˈdɛv.əl / (AmE), / bɪˈdɛv.əl / (BrE)
to treat someone with persistent cruelty or torment
treat cruelly or torment

Examples

The cruel kids would often bedevil their new teacher with endless pranks.
She felt bedeviled by the constant teasing from her classmates.
The captors continued to bedevil their prisoner for days on end.

Synset Examples

The children tormented the stuttering teacher

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torment - to cause severe suffering to; to torture
  • Synonym: to harass - to annoy or trouble someone repeatedly

Etymology

From be- (intensive prefix) + devil, originally meaning to torment as if by devils.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01806967-v
Synset POS: v
