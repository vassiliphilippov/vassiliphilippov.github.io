foresail

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌseɪl / (AmE), / ˈfɔːseɪl / (BrE)
the lowest sail set on the foremast of a square-rigged vessel or the principal sail on the foremast of a two-masted vessel
lowest sail on a ship's foremast

Examples

The crew worked quickly to raise the foresail as the wind picked up.
With the foresail and mainsail set, the ship gained impressive speed.
Stormy weather forced them to drop the foresail for safety.

Etymology

From 'fore-' (front) + 'sail', indicating its position on the vessel.
