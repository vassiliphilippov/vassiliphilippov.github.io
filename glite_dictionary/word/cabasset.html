cabasset

1. NOUN

/ kəˈbæsɪt / (AmE), / kəˈbæsɪt / (BrE)
a light, metal helmet with a high crest, worn by common soldiers in the 16th and early 17th centuries
type of 16th-century metal helmet

Examples

The museum displayed a cabasset alongside other armor from the Renaissance period.
Many infantrymen wore a cabasset for protection during the wars of the 16th century.
His collection includes a rare cabasset in excellent condition.

Etymology

From Spanish 'cabezada', from 'cabeza' (head).
