dicot

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪˌkɑt / (AmE), / ˈdʌɪkɒt / (BrE)
a flowering plant whose seed typically has two embryonic leaves or cotyledons
plant with two seed leaves

Examples

Many garden flowers, such as beans and sunflowers, are classified as dicots.
The leaves of dicot plants usually have a branching vein pattern.
Unlike monocots, dicots have seeds that split into two halves.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monocot - a plant with one embryonic seed leaf (cotyledon)

Etymology

From New Latin 'Dicotyledones', from Greek 'di-' (two) + 'kotyledon' (cotyledon, seed leaf).
