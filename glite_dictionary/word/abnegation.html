abnegation

1. NOUN

/ ˌæb.nəˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæb.nɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of renouncing, denying, or rejecting a doctrine, belief, or practice
the act of denying or rejecting a belief

Examples

The philosopher's abnegation of traditional values was controversial among his peers.
Her abnegation of the group's beliefs led to her exclusion from the community.
In some religions, abnegation of worldly pleasures is considered a virtue.

Synset Examples

abnegation of the Holy Trinity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rejection - the act of dismissing or refusing something
  • Antonym: acceptance - the act of consenting to receive or believe in something

Etymology

From Latin abnegatio (refusal, denial), from abnegare (to deny).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæb.nəˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌab.nɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of giving up or rejecting one's own interests, needs, or desires, often for the sake of others or for a principle
self-denial; renouncing one's interests

Examples

Her abnegation of personal comfort for the sake of her children was admirable.
The priest believed that abnegation was essential for spiritual growth.
Acts of abnegation can inspire others to put the needs of the community before themselves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-denial - the practice of rejecting one's own interests or needs
  • Antonym: self-indulgence - the act of allowing oneself to enjoy pleasures or desires

Etymology

From Latin 'abnegatio', from 'abnegare' (to refuse, deny).
