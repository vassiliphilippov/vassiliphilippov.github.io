pierced

1. ADJ

/ pɪrst / (AmE), / pɪəst / (BrE)
(of a material or body part) having a hole made through it, especially for attaching an ornament or for medical reasons.
having a hole made through something

Examples

She wears delicate earrings in her pierced ears every day.
The bakery sold pierced doughnuts covered in sugar.
After his accident, he had a pierced eardrum that needed to heal.

Synset Examples

pierced ears
a perforated eardrum
a punctured balloon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perforated - having a series of small holes made through something
  • Antonym: intact - not damaged or having no holes; whole

Etymology

Past participle of 'pierce', from Old French 'percier'.
