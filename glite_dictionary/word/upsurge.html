upsurge

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌsɝdʒ / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌsɜːdʒ / (BrE)
a sudden and strong increase in something
sudden strong increase

Examples

There has been an upsurge in online shopping during the holiday season.
The country experienced an upsurge of interest in renewable energy.
After the announcement, an upsurge of excitement swept through the crowd.

Synset Examples

stimulated a surge of speculation
an upsurge of emotion
an upsurge in violent crime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surge - a sudden powerful forward or upward movement
  • Antonym: decline - a gradual and continuous loss or decrease

Etymology

Formed from 'up' + 'surge', meaning to rise suddenly.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌsɝdʒ / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌsɜːdʒ / (BrE)
a sudden and rapid increase or rise in something, especially something undesirable or intense
a sudden rapid increase

Examples

There was an upsurge in unemployment after the factory closed.
An unexpected upsurge of anger swept through the crowd.
Scientists reported an upsurge in COVID-19 cases during the winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surge - a sudden large increase, often of a strong force or emotion

Etymology

From up- (meaning 'upward') + surge (from Latin 'surgere' meaning 'to rise').
