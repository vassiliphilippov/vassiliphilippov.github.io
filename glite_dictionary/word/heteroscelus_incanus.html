Heteroscelus incanus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛtəroʊˈsiːləs ɪnˈkeɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəˈsiːləs ɪnˈkeɪnəs / (BrE)
A species of shorebird, commonly known as the wandering tattler, found in Pacific coastal regions.
Pacific shorebird; wandering tattler

Examples

The Heteroscelus incanus migrates along the Pacific coast every year.
Birdwatchers spotted a Heteroscelus incanus feeding on the rocky shoreline.
Researchers are studying the habitat preferences of Heteroscelus incanus during its breeding season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wandering tattler - A common name for Heteroscelus incanus, a shorebird of the Pacific coast

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus Heteroscelus (from Greek heteros 'different' + skelos 'leg') and species incanus (Latin, 'grayish').
