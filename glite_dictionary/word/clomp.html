to clomp

1. VERB

/ klɑmp / (AmE), / klɒmp / (BrE)
to walk with heavy or clumsy steps, often making a loud sound
walk with heavy or clumsy steps

Examples

The children clomped down the stairs in their heavy boots.
She could hear him clomping around the kitchen early in the morning.
He clomped into the room, leaving muddy footprints on the floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clump - to walk with a heavy tread
  • Synonym: stomp - to walk with heavy, forceful steps
  • Antonym: tiptoe - to walk lightly and quietly on one’s toes
