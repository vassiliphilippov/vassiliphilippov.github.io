superconductivity

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuː.pɚ.kənˌdʌkˈtɪv.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pə.kənˌdʌkˈtɪv.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
a phenomenon in which electrical resistance in certain materials vanishes when they are cooled below a characteristic critical temperature
zero resistance in materials at very low temperatures

Examples

Scientists discovered superconductivity in mercury when it was cooled to below 4 kelvin.
Advances in superconductivity could revolutionize power transmission in the future.
The phenomenon of superconductivity allows electrical current to flow without any energy loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superconductor - a material that exhibits superconductivity
  • Antonym: resistivity - the property of a material that opposes the flow of electric current

Etymology

From 'superconduct' (to conduct without resistance) + '-ivity', influenced by scientific usage.
