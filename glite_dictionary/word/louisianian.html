Louisianian

1. NOUN

/ luˌiːziˈæniən / (AmE), / luːˌiːziˈæniən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of the state of Louisiana in the United States
a person from Louisiana

Examples

As a proud Louisianian, she loves to share her state's rich history with visitors.
The local cuisine is a source of pride for every Louisianian.
Many Louisianians celebrate Mardi Gras with colorful parades and music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Louisianan - another form referring to a native or resident of Louisiana

Etymology

From Louisiana, the US state, with the suffix -ian denoting origin or association.
