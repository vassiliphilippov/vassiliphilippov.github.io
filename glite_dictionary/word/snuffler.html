snuffler

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnʌflər / (AmE), / ˈsnʌflə / (BrE)
a person who breathes noisily, especially due to a blocked nose
person who breathes noisily

Examples

The snuffler in the back row disturbed everyone during the performance.
You could always hear the snuffler coming down the hall.
In winter, the classroom was filled with at least one persistent snuffler.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snuffle - to breathe noisily through the nose

Etymology

From 'snuffle' (to breathe noisily through the nose) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
