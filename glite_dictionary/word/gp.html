GP

1. NOUN

/ dʒiː piː / (AmE), / dʒiː piː / (BrE)
A doctor who provides general medical care to people, treating a wide variety of illnesses and conditions rather than specializing in one area.
general medical doctor (not a specialist)
medical british

Examples

If you feel unwell, you should make an appointment with your GP.
My GP referred me to a specialist for further tests.
Many people rely on their GP for regular health checkups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: general practitioner - a physician who provides general medical care

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'general practitioner'.
