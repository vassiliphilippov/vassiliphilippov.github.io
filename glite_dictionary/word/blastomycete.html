blastomycete

1. NOUN

/ ˌblæstəˈmaɪsiːt / (AmE), / ˌblastəˈmʌɪsiːt / (BrE)
any of various yeastlike fungi of the genus Blastomyces that reproduce by budding and can cause disease in humans and animals
yeastlike disease-causing fungus (Blastomyces genus)
medical

Examples

Researchers identified the presence of blastomycete in several tissue samples from infected patients.
A blastomycete infection can lead to severe pulmonary disease if left untreated.
Veterinarians are studying how blastomycete affects different animal species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yeast - a type of fungus that reproduces by budding and is used in baking and brewing

Etymology

From New Latin Blastomyces (the defining genus) + -ete, indicating a member or type.
