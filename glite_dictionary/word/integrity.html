integrity

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛɡrəti / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛɡrəti / (BrE)
the quality of being honest and having strong moral principles
honesty; strong moral principles

Examples

The judge was respected for her integrity and commitment to justice.
He refused to compromise his integrity for personal gain.
A company known for its integrity attracts loyal customers and talented employees.

Synset Examples

he expects to find in us the common honesty and integrity of men of business
they admired his scrupulous professional integrity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honesty - the quality of being honest; truthfulness
  • Synonym: probity - the quality of having strong moral principles; honesty and decency
  • Antonym: corruption - dishonest or fraudulent conduct by those in power
  • Antonym: dishonesty - lack of honesty or integrity

Etymology

From Latin integritas (wholeness, completeness, blamelessness), from integer (whole).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛɡrəti / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛɡrəti / (BrE)
The state of being whole and undivided; the condition of being complete and unbroken.
state of being whole or unbroken

Examples

The architect emphasized the integrity of the building's original design during the renovation.
The integrity of the data was compromised by the software malfunction.
Earthquake-resistant construction helps maintain the structural integrity of high-rise buildings.

Synset Examples

the integrity of the nervous system is required for normal development
he took measures to insure the territorial unity of Croatia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unity - the state of being united or joined as a whole
  • Antonym: fragmentation - the process or state of breaking into separate parts

Etymology

From Latin integritas (wholeness, completeness), from integer (whole, complete).
