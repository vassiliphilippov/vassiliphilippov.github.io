extemporaneously

1. ADV

/ ɪkˌstɛmpəˈreɪniəsli / (AmE), / ɪkˌstɛmpəˈreɪnɪəsli / (BrE)
in a manner done without prior preparation or planning; spontaneously or impromptu
without prior preparation

Examples

The professor answered the complex question extemporaneously during the lecture.
She delivered the keynote speech extemporaneously, impressing the audience with her eloquence.
He often prefers to speak extemporaneously rather than reading from notes.

Synset Examples

he spoke extemporaneously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impromptu - without being planned, organized, or rehearsed

Etymology

From Latin ex tempore, meaning 'out of the moment'.
