townsfolk

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaʊnzˌfoʊk / (AmE), / ˈtaʊnzˌfəʊk / (BrE)
the people living in a town, especially considered collectively as the ordinary inhabitants
people living in a town

Examples

The townsfolk gathered in the square to celebrate the festival together.
Many townsfolk supported the new mayor's community initiatives.
During the parade, the townsfolk cheered and waved flags enthusiastically.

Synset Examples

the whole town cheered the team

Related Terms

  • Synonym: villager - person living in a village

Etymology

Formed from 'town' and the archaic plural 'folk' (people).
