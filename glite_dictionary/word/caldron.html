caldron

1. NOUN

/ ˈkældrən / (AmE), / ˈkɔːldrən / (BrE)
a large metal pot with a lid and handle, used for boiling over an open fire
large pot for boiling

Examples

The old witch stirred her caldron as the fire crackled beneath it.
Medieval cooks often used a caldron to prepare soups for large groups.
A massive iron caldron sat in the hearth, slowly bubbling with stew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cauldron - alternative spelling of 'caldron'

Etymology

From Latin 'caldaria' (cooking pot), later Old French 'caudron'.
