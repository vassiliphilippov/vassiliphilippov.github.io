belligerence

1. NOUN

/ bəˈlɪdʒərəns / (AmE), / bəˈlɪdʒərəns / (BrE)
hostile or aggressive attitude or behavior; a warlike nature
hostile or aggressive attitude

Examples

Her belligerence made it difficult for the team to work together effectively.
The country's increasing belligerence alarmed its neighbors and the international community.
Despite his initial belligerence, he agreed to sit down and negotiate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hostility - unfriendly or antagonistic behavior
  • Synonym: aggression - hostile or violent behavior or attitudes
  • Antonym: peacefulness - the state of being calm and without conflict

Etymology

From Latin belligerans, belligerant- (waging war), from bellum (war) + gerere (to wage).

2. NOUN

/ bəˈlɪdʒərəns / (AmE), / bəˈlɪdʒərəns / (BrE)
a natural or characteristic tendency to be hostile or aggressive
hostile or aggressive disposition

Examples

Her belligerence made it difficult to resolve the conflict peacefully.
The belligerence displayed by the two leaders alarmed their advisors.
Despite his usual belligerence, he managed to keep calm during the debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aggressiveness - a tendency to attack or confront
  • Antonym: amiability - a friendly and pleasant disposition

Etymology

From Latin 'belligerare' (to wage war), from 'bellum' (war) + 'gerere' (to carry).
