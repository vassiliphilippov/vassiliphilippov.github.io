Amontillado

1. NOUN

/ əˌmɑn.tiˈjɑː.doʊ / (AmE), / əˌmɒn.tɪˈjɑː.dəʊ / (BrE)
A pale, medium-dry sherry wine from Spain, known for its slightly nutty flavor and amber color.
pale, medium-dry Spanish sherry

Examples

He offered his guests a glass of Amontillado before dinner.
The nutty aroma of the Amontillado complemented the savory tapas perfectly.
She purchased a bottle of Amontillado as a special treat for the weekend.

Etymology

From Spanish 'amontillado', originally denoting wine made in the style of Montilla (a region in Spain).
