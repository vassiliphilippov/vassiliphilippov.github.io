grating

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
an optical device with many parallel grooves that disperses light or other electromagnetic radiation into its component wavelengths, producing a spectrum
optical device dispersing light into a spectrum

Examples

The scientist used a grating to separate the different colors of the laser beam.
By rotating the grating, you can analyze various wavelengths of emitted light.
Modern spectrometers often rely on a grating to disperse incoming electromagnetic radiation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diffraction grating - an optical device made of a surface with closely spaced lines used to separate light into a spectrum
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03199222-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡreɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a sound or voice) unpleasantly harsh or jarring to the ears.
harsh, unpleasant sound

Examples

Her laugh had a grating quality that set his teeth on edge.
The grating sound of metal scraping against metal was intolerable.
His grating voice made it difficult to listen to the announcement.

Synset Examples

a gravelly voice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harsh - unpleasantly rough or jarring to the senses
  • Antonym: mellifluous - pleasantly smooth and musical to hear

Etymology

From the verb 'grate' (to make a harsh sound), from Old French 'grater' (to scrape).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00300342-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
a frame of iron bars placed in a fireplace to hold a fire or fuel
frame of iron bars for a fire

Examples

The old cottage still had an iron grating in its fireplace.
She set the logs carefully onto the grating before lighting the fire.
Ash often falls through the grating into the tray below.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grate - framework of iron bars for holding fuel in a fireplace

Etymology

From 'grate' (iron bars for holding fuel in a fireplace), ultimately from Latin *cratis* (wickerwork, lattice).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03459480-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
a frame or cover with parallel or crossed bars, used as a barrier to block access while allowing air, light, or liquids to pass through
barrier of parallel or crossed bars

Examples

The metal grating over the drain prevents debris from clogging the pipes.
Sunlight filtered through the grating and cast patterns on the floor.
He peered through the grating to see what was happening in the next room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grid - a framework of spaced bars that are parallel to or cross each other; a grating

Etymology

From Latin *cratis* (wickerwork, hurdle).

Source: WordNet
