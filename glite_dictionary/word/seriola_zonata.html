Seriola zonata

1. NOUN

/ sɛriˈoʊlə zoʊˈneɪtə / (AmE), / sɛrɪˈəʊlə zəʊˈnɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of fish, commonly called banded rudderfish, found in the coastal waters of North and South America, known for following ships.
a fish species known as banded rudderfish

Examples

The Seriola zonata is often seen swimming near the hulls of boats along the Atlantic coast.
Researchers observed a school of Seriola zonata following a research vessel for several miles.
Fishermen sometimes catch Seriola zonata while trawling in coastal waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banded rudderfish - common name for Seriola zonata

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Seriola' as the genus and 'zonata' meaning 'banded'.
