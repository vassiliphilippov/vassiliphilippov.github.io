Chrysophyllum cainito

1. NOUN

/ ˌkraɪsoʊˈfɪləm kaɪˈniːtoʊ / (AmE), / ˌkrɪsəˈfɪləm kaɪˈniːtəʊ / (BrE)
an evergreen tree native to the West Indies and Central America, known for its edible purple fruit that is star-shaped in cross section and has leaves with golden silky undersides
tropical tree with edible star-shaped fruit

Examples

The fruit of the Chrysophyllum cainito is prized for its sweet, juicy flesh in many tropical countries.
Gardeners in the Caribbean often plant Chrysophyllum cainito for its ornamental foliage and edible fruit.
The silky golden underside of the Chrysophyllum cainito leaf makes the tree easy to identify.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: star apple - common name for Chrysophyllum cainito, the fruit of the tree

Etymology

From Greek 'chrysos' (gold) + 'phyllon' (leaf) for the genus, and the species name from Spanish/Arawak origin.
