compline

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmplaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒmplɪn / (BrE)
the last of the seven canonical hours, said or sung before retiring for the night in certain Christian traditions
last canonical hour of prayer

Examples

The monks gathered in the chapel to pray compline before going to their cells.
After a busy day, she found peace in the quiet recitation of compline.
The service of compline marked the close of the liturgical day.

Etymology

From Old French 'complie', from Late Latin 'completorium' (completion), referring to the completion of the day's work.
