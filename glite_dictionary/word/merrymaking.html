merrymaking

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛriˌmeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɛriˌmeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the lively and cheerful participation in festivities or celebrations, often with singing, dancing, and laughter
lively festive celebration

Examples

The streets were filled with merrymaking on New Year's Eve.
Their wedding reception turned into an evening of joyful merrymaking.
After the announcement, the office erupted in spontaneous merrymaking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celebration - a joyful occasion for special festivities
  • Synonym: revelry - noisy festivities, especially with drinking
  • Antonym: solemnity - a formal, serious occasion; lack of cheerful celebration

Etymology

From merry (cheerful) + making (the act of doing).
