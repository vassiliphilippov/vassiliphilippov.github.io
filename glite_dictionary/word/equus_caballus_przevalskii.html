Equus caballus przevalskii

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛkwəs kəˈbæləs prɛˈvalskiː / (AmE), / ˈɛkwəs kəˈbaləs prɛˈvalskiː / (BrE)
A rare wild horse of central Asia, also known as Przewalski's horse, characterized by its stocky build and short mane; now endangered in the wild.
endangered wild horse of central Asia

Examples

The Equus caballus przevalskii population in Mongolia has seen a gradual recovery due to conservation efforts.
Unlike domestic horses, Equus caballus przevalskii has a stiff, upright mane and no forelock.
Scientists study the genetic diversity of Equus caballus przevalskii to improve its chances of survival in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Przewalski's horse - wild horse native to central Asia, same species as Equus caballus przevalskii

Etymology

From New Latin: Equus (horse), caballus (horse), przevalskii honoring Russian explorer N. M. Przewalski, who discovered the animal.
