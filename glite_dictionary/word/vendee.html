vendee

1. NOUN

/ vɛnˈdi / (AmE), / vɛnˈdiː / (BrE)
a person or party who buys something, especially in legal or business transactions
person who buys, especially in law
legal

Examples

The vendee has the right to inspect the goods before making payment.
In the contract, the vendee agreed to all the specified terms and conditions.
If the vendee fails to pay, legal action may be taken by the vendor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vendor - a person or company offering something for sale; seller

Etymology

From French *vendre* (to sell), from Latin *vendere* (to sell).
