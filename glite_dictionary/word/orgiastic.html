orgiastic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔr.dʒiˈæstɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔː.dʒiˈæstɪk / (BrE)
characterized by wild, excessive, and often drunken celebration or revelry
wild, drunken revelry

Examples

The crowd at the festival erupted into an orgiastic frenzy as the music reached its peak.
The novel describes an orgiastic scene of excess that shocked many readers.
The celebrations turned unexpectedly orgiastic, with dancing and laughter lasting until dawn.

Synset Examples

a night of bacchanalian revelry
carousing bands of drunken soldiers
orgiastic festivity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacchanalian - characterized by drunken revelry or riotous celebration
  • Antonym: sober - not affected by alcohol; serious and sensible

Etymology

From Greek orgiastikos (of an orgy), from orgiazein (to celebrate orgies).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɔr.dʒiˈæst.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔː.dʒiˈæs.tɪk / (BrE)
characterized by or resembling frenzied sexual activity; relating to or suggestive of an orgy.
relating to frenzied sexual activity

Examples

The novel describes the orgiastic celebrations that erupted after the victory.
Their parties were famous for their orgiastic excess and wild dancing.
Rumors circulated about the orgiastic rituals held in the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orgy - a wild party, especially one involving excessive drinking and unrestrained sexual activity

Etymology

From Ancient Greek orgiastikos (pertaining to orgies), from orgia (secret rites, orgies).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
