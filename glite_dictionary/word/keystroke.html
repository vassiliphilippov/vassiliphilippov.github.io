keystroke

1. NOUN

/ ˈkiːˌstroʊk / (AmE), / ˈkiːstrəʊk / (BrE)
A single press of a key on a keyboard or similar device.
one press of a key on a keyboard

Examples

Each keystroke is recorded by the software for analysis.
Improving your typing speed requires practicing until you need fewer keystrokes to complete a task.
A single keystroke can sometimes activate important shortcuts on your computer.

Synset Examples

the number of keystrokes was used as a measure of work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keypress - the act of pressing a key on a keyboard or similar device

Etymology

Formed from 'key' + 'stroke', indicating the action of pressing a key.

Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ ˈkiːˌstroʊk / (AmE), / ˈkiːˌstrəʊk / (BrE)
a single press of a key on a keyboard or typewriter
pressing a single key on a keyboard

Examples

Each keystroke is logged by the security software running on the computer.
Try to minimize the number of keystrokes needed to complete the form.
She can easily type 80 words per minute with very few extra keystrokes.

Synset Examples

the number of keystrokes was used as a measure of work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keypress - pressing a key on a keyboard or device
  • Antonym: mouse click - pressing and releasing a mouse button

Etymology

From 'key' (button on a keyboard) + 'stroke' (single movement).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00350346-n
