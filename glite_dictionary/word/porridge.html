porridge

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɒrɪdʒ / (BrE)
a soft food made by boiling oatmeal or other grains or legumes in water or milk until it thickens
soft food made from boiled grains or legumes

Examples

He had a bowl of porridge for breakfast every morning.
In many cultures, porridge is eaten as a hearty and warming meal.
You can add honey or fruit to make your porridge tastier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oatmeal - porridge made specifically from oats
  • Degree Lower: gruel - a thinner type of porridge

Etymology

From Old French 'pourree' (leek soup), from 'porée', from Latin 'porrum' (leek).
