confederacy

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛd.ɚ.əsi / (AmE), / kənˈfɛd.ər.əsi / (BrE)
A secret agreement between two or more people to commit an unlawful or harmful act; a conspiracy.
secret plot or conspiracy

Examples

The leaders formed a confederacy to overthrow the government.
He was accused of being part of a confederacy to commit fraud.
The discovery of the confederacy shocked the community and prompted an investigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conspiracy - secret plan to do something unlawful or harmful
  • Degree Lower: alliance - a formal agreement between parties to cooperate

Etymology

From Latin 'confederatus' (joined by league), from 'con-' (together) + 'foederare' (to make a league).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛd.ɚ.ə.si / (AmE), / kənˈfɛd.ər.ə.si / (BrE)
a union of states, groups, or organizations formed for a common purpose, especially of a political nature
union of political organizations

Examples

The southern states formed a confederacy during the American Civil War.
Several tribes entered into a confederacy for mutual protection against invaders.
The new confederacy aimed to promote economic cooperation among its members.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: federation - a group of states with a central government but independence in internal affairs
  • Synonym: alliance - a union formed for mutual benefit, especially between states or organizations
  • Synonym: union - the act of joining together or the state of being joined

Etymology

From Latin 'confederatio' (an alliance), from 'con-' (together) + 'foederare' (to league together).

3. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛd.ə.rə.si / (AmE), / kənˈfɛd.ərəsi / (BrE)
a group of people who join together, often secretly, to commit a harmful or illegal act; a conspiracy
group formed to commit a crime

Examples

Several politicians were found to be part of a confederacy planning to fix the election.
The criminal confederacy was finally exposed after months of investigation.
They formed a confederacy with the aim of defrauding the company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conspiracy - a secret plan by a group to do something unlawful or harmful

Etymology

From Latin 'confoederatio', from 'confoederare' (to unite by a league).
