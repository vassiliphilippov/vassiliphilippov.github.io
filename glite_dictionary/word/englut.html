to englut

1. VERB

/ ɛnˈɡlʌt / (AmE), / ɛnˈɡlʌt / (BrE)
To eat excessively or greedily; to devour in large quantities.
to eat greedily or excessively

Examples

He tended to englut himself at every holiday meal, unable to resist the lavish spreads.
After a day of fasting, she was tempted to englut her plate with everything on the table.
If you englut sweet foods, you may soon regret the discomfort that follows.

Synset Examples

She stuffed herself at the dinner
The kids binged on ice cream

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overeat - to eat more food than needed
  • Synonym: gorge - to eat greedily to excess

Etymology

From Latin 'engluttire', from 'in-' (in) + 'gluttire' (to swallow).
