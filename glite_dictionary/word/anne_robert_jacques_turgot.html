Anne Robert Jacques Turgot

1. PROPN

/ æn roʊˈbɛr ʒæk turˈgoʊ / (AmE), / æn rɒˈbɛː ʒak tʊəˈɡoʊ / (BrE)
(1727–1781) A French economist and statesman who served as Controller-General of Finances under Louis XVI and advocated for economic reforms, notably the abolition of feudal privileges.
French economist and reformer under Louis XVI

Examples

Anne Robert Jacques Turgot introduced several economic reforms that greatly influenced later thinkers.
Historians often cite Anne Robert Jacques Turgot as a precursor to modern economic thought.
The reforms proposed by Anne Robert Jacques Turgot faced strong resistance from the French aristocracy.
