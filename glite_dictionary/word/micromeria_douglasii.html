Micromeria douglasii

1. NOUN

/ maɪ.krəˈmɪr.i.ə daʊˈɡlæs.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌmɪ.krəˈmɪər.i.ə daʊˈɡlæsiː / (BrE)
A trailing evergreen herb native to the northwestern United States, with small white flowers, used in traditional medicine.
herbaceous plant native to northwestern US

Examples

Native Americans traditionally used Micromeria douglasii as a medicinal herb for a variety of ailments.
Hikers in California may recognize Micromeria douglasii by its fragrant leaves along shaded trails.
Although rare in cultivation, Micromeria douglasii can be grown in rock gardens for its aromatic foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yerba buena - common name for Micromeria douglasii, used in herbal medicine

Etymology

From Latin; 'Micromeria' meaning 'small part' and 'douglasii' in honor of the Scottish botanist David Douglas.
