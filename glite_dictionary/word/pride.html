pride

1. NOUN

/ praɪd / (AmE), / praɪd / (BrE)
a group of lions forming a social unit living together
group of lions

Examples

A pride of lions lounged in the shade beneath the trees.
The safari guide pointed out a large pride resting near the waterhole.
Young males often leave their pride to start their own groups.

2. NOUN

/ praɪd / (AmE), / praɪd / (BrE)
a feeling or trait that motivates someone to maintain or exceed their own standards and avoid falling below them
drive to maintain high personal standards

Examples

He has too much pride to admit when he's wrong, even to himself.
Her pride pushed her to always deliver her best work, no matter the circumstances.
Despite the setbacks, his pride kept him striving to reach his goals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-respect - regard for one's own character or dignity
  • Antonym: shame - a feeling of guilt or disgrace when failing to meet standards

Etymology

From Old English *prȳde* (proudness), related to *prūd* (proud) from Old French *prud*.

3. VERB

/ praɪd / (AmE), / praɪd / (BrE)
to take great pride or satisfaction in something; to be especially proud of a particular quality, skill, or achievement
take pride in; be proud of

Examples

She prides herself on her punctuality and reliability at work.
The chef prides himself on using only the freshest local ingredients.
He prided himself on never missing a day of practice throughout his career.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boast - talk with excessive pride and self-satisfaction
  • Antonym: humble - feeling or showing modesty rather than pride
