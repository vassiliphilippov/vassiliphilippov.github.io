chunga

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃuŋɡə / (AmE), / ˈtʃuŋɡə / (BrE)
A large, long-legged, crested bird native to South America, especially Argentina, known as the Cariama; scientifically Chunga burmeisteri.
South American Cariama bird

Examples

The chunga is often found in the open grasslands of Argentina.
Unlike many birds, the chunga prefers to run rather than fly when threatened.
Researchers recorded the loud calls of the chunga during their expedition.

Etymology

From Latin genus name Chunga; scientific name Chunga burmeisteri.
