gonadotrophic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɡoʊ.nædəˈtroʊ.fɪk / (AmE), / ˌɡəʊ.nædəˈtrɒf.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving gonadotropin, a hormone that stimulates the activity of the gonads.
relating to gonadotropin hormone
medical

Examples

The gonadotrophic hormones play a crucial role in human reproductive health.
Researchers studied the effects of gonadotrophic stimulation in mammalian ovaries.
Abnormal gonadotrophic activity can lead to hormonal imbalances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gonadotropic - relating to or involving gonadotropin; spelling variant

Etymology

From gonad + Greek trophikos (pertaining to nourishment), referring to the hormone's effect on the gonads.
