battleful

1. ADJ

/ ˈbæt̬.əl.fəl / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əl.fəl / (BrE)
Having a ready disposition to fight; warlike or combative in nature.
disposed to fight; warlike

Examples

The general's battleful spirit inspired his troops before the conflict began.
She spoke in a battleful tone, ready to argue her case to the council.
His battleful attitude often got him into trouble at school.

Synset Examples

bellicose young officers
a combative impulse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bellicose - having or showing a tendency to fight; warlike
  • Synonym: combative - ready or eager to fight; aggressive

Etymology

From 'battle' (from Old French 'bataille') + '-ful' (suffix meaning 'full of').
