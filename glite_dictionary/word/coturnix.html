Coturnix

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈtɝː.nɪks / (AmE), / kəˈtɜː.nɪks / (BrE)
A genus of birds comprising the Old World quails, native to parts of Europe, Asia, and Africa.
genus of Old World quails
medical

Examples

The Coturnix genus is known for its small, ground-dwelling quail species.
Some researchers study the migration patterns of birds in the Coturnix genus.
Farmers often raise Coturnix quails for their eggs and meat due to their rapid growth.

Etymology

From Latin coturnix, meaning 'quail'.
