ecumenism

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈkjuːməˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / iːˈkjuːmənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the principle or aim of promoting unity among the world's Christian churches, or more generally among different religious groups
movement for unity among Christian churches

Examples

The conference focused on ecumenism and fostering dialogue among various Christian denominations.
Her life's work was dedicated to promoting ecumenism and interfaith understanding.
Recent years have seen renewed interest in ecumenism within religious communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecumenical - promoting unity among churches, especially Christians

Etymology

From Greek oikoumenē (the inhabited world), through Late Latin oecumenismus.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈkjuməˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˈkjuːmənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the principle or aim of promoting unity and cooperation among different Christian churches and denominations
promotion of Christian unity and cooperation

Examples

The movement for ecumenism gained momentum after the Second Vatican Council.
Many leaders spoke about the importance of ecumenism during the interfaith conference.
Debates on ecumenism have affected relations between various Christian denominations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecumenical - promoting or relating to unity among Christian churches

Etymology

From Greek oikoumenē (the inhabited world), via late Latin 'oecumenicus'.

Synset POS: n
