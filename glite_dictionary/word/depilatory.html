depilatory

1. NOUN

/ dəˈpɪləˌtɔri / (AmE), / dɪˈpɪlətəri / (BrE)
A substance or cosmetic preparation applied to the skin to remove unwanted hair temporarily.
hair-removal cosmetic

Examples

She applies a depilatory to her legs before going to the beach.
The instructions on the depilatory warned to test a small area first.
After using the depilatory, her skin felt smooth and hair-free.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epilator - a device for removing hair by pulling it out by the roots

Etymology

From Latin depilare (to remove hair), from de- (away) + pilus (hair).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈpɪlətɔːri / (AmE), / dɪˈpɪlətəri / (BrE)
A substance, especially a chemical preparation, used to remove hair, wool, or bristles from the skin or hides.
substance for removing hair

Examples

She applied a depilatory to her legs to remove unwanted hair.
Some tanners use a depilatory during the leather-making process to strip hides of bristles.
A chemical depilatory can be used as an alternative to shaving or waxing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epilator - device or substance used for removing hair

Etymology

From Latin depilare (to remove hair), from de- (off) + pilus (hair).

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈpɪləˌtɔri / (AmE), / dɪˈpɪlətəri / (BrE)
(of a substance) able to remove hair or render hairless.
removing hair; hair-removing

Examples

The depilatory cream left her skin smooth and hairless.
Some people may experience irritation after using a depilatory product.
This depilatory lotion is suitable for sensitive areas such as the face and underarms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epilatory - substance used to remove hair

Etymology

From Latin depilare (to remove hair), from de- (removal) + pilus (hair).
