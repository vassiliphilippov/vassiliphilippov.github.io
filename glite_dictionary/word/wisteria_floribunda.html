Wisteria floribunda

1. NOUN

/ wɪˈstɪriə ˌflɔrɪˈbʌndə / (AmE), / wɪˈstɪəriə ˌflɒrɪˈbʌndə / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant known as Japanese wisteria, with long clusters of pink, mauve, or violet-blue flowers, native to Japan and widely grown as an ornamental vine.
Japanese wisteria plant species

Examples

The Wisteria floribunda in her garden bloomed with spectacular violet-blue flowers this spring.
Our local park planted a trellis covered in Wisteria floribunda, creating a breathtaking display in May.
Gardeners often prefer Wisteria floribunda for its long, fragrant flower clusters and vigorous growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wisteria - A genus of climbing shrubs with showy flowers

Etymology

Genus name 'Wisteria' from American physician Caspar Wistar; species name 'floribunda' from Latin meaning 'abundantly flowering'.
