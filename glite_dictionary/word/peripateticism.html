peripateticism

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrəpəˈtɛtɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪpəˈtɛtɪsɪzm / (BrE)
the philosophy and teachings of Aristotle, encompassing logic, metaphysics, ethics, poetics, politics, and natural science
Aristotle's philosophical system

Examples

She devoted her dissertation to exploring peripateticism and its influence on medieval scholars.
The principles of peripateticism continue to shape philosophical debates today.
He attended a seminar that examined the relationship between peripateticism and modern science.

Synset Examples

Aristotelianism profoundly influenced Western thought

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aristotelianism - the philosophy of Aristotle and his followers

Etymology

From Greek 'peripatētikos' (given to walking about), referring to Aristotle's practice of discussing philosophy while walking.
