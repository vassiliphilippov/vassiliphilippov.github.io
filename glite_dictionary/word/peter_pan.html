Peter Pan

1. NOUN

/ ˌpiːtər ˈpæn / (AmE), / ˌpiːtə ˈpæn / (BrE)
a man who remains boyish or immature and refuses to grow up, after the character in J.M. Barrie's play
immature man; never grows up

Examples

Some critics describe him as a Peter Pan who refuses responsibility.
Her brother is a real Peter Pan, always playing video games instead of working.
The club attracts several Peter Pans who avoid adult commitments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: man-child - an adult man who behaves in a childish way
  • Antonym: adult - a person who is fully grown and mature

Etymology

From the name of the title character in J.M. Barrie's 1904 play 'Peter Pan'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈpiːtər ˈpæn / (AmE), / ˈpiːtə ˈpæn / (BrE)
the main character in the play and novel by J. M. Barrie, depicted as a boy who never grows up
fictional boy who never grows up (J. M. Barrie character)

Examples

The character Peter Pan has inspired countless adaptations in film and literature.
She dressed as Peter Pan for the school play last year.
Some people refer to adults who refuse to mature as having a Peter Pan complex.

Etymology

Created by Scottish author J. M. Barrie (1860–1937), first appearing in 1902.
