directorship

1. NOUN

/ dəˈrɛktɚˌʃɪp, daɪ- / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛktəʃɪp, daɪ- / (BrE)
the position or office of a director, especially in a company or organization
position of a director

Examples

She was offered a directorship at a major electronics company.
His directorship marked a period of significant growth for the business.
After years of hard work, he finally achieved a directorship in the organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: director - person appointed to manage or supervise an organization or part of it

Etymology

Formed from 'director' + '-ship', denoting position or state.
