pyrethrum

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈriːθrəm / (AmE), / paɪˈriːθrəm / (BrE)
a plant of the daisy family, whose flowers are used to produce a natural insecticide; formerly classified with genus Chrysanthemum, now often included in genus Tanacetum.
plant used for natural insecticide

Examples

Many organic farmers use pyrethrum to control pests on their crops.
The active compounds in pyrethrum flowers are toxic to insects but relatively safe for humans.
She grows pyrethrum in her garden as a natural way to keep mosquitoes away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chrysanthemum - any plant of the genus Chrysanthemum

Etymology

From Latin 'pyrethrum', from Greek 'pyrethron' (feverfew).

2. NOUN

/ paɪˈriːθrəm / (AmE), / pɪˈriːθrəm / (BrE)
A substance obtained from the dried flower heads of certain chrysanthemum plants, used as an insecticide.
insecticidal powder from chrysanthemum plants

Examples

The gardeners used pyrethrum to control the aphid population in the greenhouse.
Organic farmers often prefer pyrethrum as a natural alternative to synthetic pesticides.
The powder is made by grinding the dried flower heads of pyrethrum plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insecticide - a substance used for killing insects

Etymology

From Latin *pyrethrum*, from Greek *pyrethron* (wild chamomile).

3. NOUN

/ paɪˈriːθrəm / (AmE), / pɪˈriːθrəm / (BrE)
a spring-flowering perennial plant of Asian origin with finely divided aromatic leaves and white to pink-purple flowers, traditionally used as a source of natural insecticide
flowering plant used as insecticide

Examples

Many gardeners choose pyrethrum for its attractive blooms and natural pest-repellent properties.
The insecticidal powder derived from pyrethrum has been used for centuries to control household pests.
Botanists sometimes classify pyrethrum within the genus Chrysanthemum due to its similar features.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chrysanthemum - a plant genus related to pyrethrum, often with similar flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'pyrethrum', from Ancient Greek 'pyrethron' (feverfew, a plant of the daisy family).

4. NOUN

/ paɪˈriːθrəm / (AmE), / paɪˈrɛθrəm / (BrE)
a Eurasian plant of the daisy family, whose dried flowers are used to make a natural insecticide.
plant used as a natural insecticide

Examples

The dried flowers of pyrethrum are ground to produce a powerful natural insecticide.
Farmers often use pyrethrum as an eco-friendly way to control pests in their crops.
Some gardeners prefer pyrethrum sprays to chemical insecticides for protecting their plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chrysanthemum - a plant genus related to pyrethrum

Etymology

From Greek *pyrethron* (feverfew), from *pyrethros* (wheat, feverfew).
