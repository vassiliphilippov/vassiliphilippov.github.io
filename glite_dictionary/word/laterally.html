laterally

1. ADV

/ ˈleɪ.t̬ɚ.əl.i / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.tər.əl.i / (BrE)
in a way that relates to, moves toward, or is situated at the side; sideways.
toward or at the side

Examples

The fish moved laterally to squeeze through the narrow gap.
Information was shared laterally between the two departments rather than from the top down.
Nerves may be damaged if the bone shifts laterally after the injury.

Synset Examples

such women carry in their heads kinship knowledge of six generations depth and extending laterally among consanguineal kin as far as the grandchildren of second cousin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sideways - towards or from the side

Etymology

From Latin 'lateralis' (of the side), from 'latus' (side).

2. ADV

/ ˈleɪt̬ɚəli / (AmE), / ˈleɪtərəli / (BrE)
in a direction or location that is to the side; sideways or relating to the side
to the side; sideways

Examples

The fish's body is flattened laterally to help it move through crevices.
The pain radiated laterally from her spine to her ribs.
In the experiment, the beam shifted laterally due to the magnetic field.

Synset Examples

the body is spindle-shaped and only slightly compressed laterally

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sideways - in a direction to or toward the side

Etymology

From Latin 'lateralis' (of or belonging to the side), from 'latus' (side).
