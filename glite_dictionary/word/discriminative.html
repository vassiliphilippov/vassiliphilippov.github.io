discriminative

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈskrɪmɪˌneɪtɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɪmɪnətɪv / (BrE)
Able to recognize or make fine distinctions between things; showing or having good judgement in distinguishing between things.
capable of making fine distinctions

Examples

Her discriminative palate made her an exceptional food critic.
A discriminative approach is important when hiring new employees.
The test was designed to be highly discriminative between different skill levels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discriminating - having or showing refined taste or good judgement

Etymology

From Latin discriminativus (serving to distinguish), from discriminare (to divide, separate).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈskrɪmɪˌneɪtɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɪmɪnətɪv / (BrE)
showing careful and discerning judgment or the ability to differentiate finely between things
showing careful judgment; discerning

Examples

The critic was known for his discriminative censure of new films.
Her discriminative analysis helped distinguish subtle differences in the data.
A discriminative approach ensures that only the most relevant details are considered.

Synset Examples

discriminative censure
a biography … appreciative and yet judicial in purpose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discerning - having or showing good judgment
  • Antonym: indiscriminate - done without careful judgment

Etymology

From Latin 'discriminare' (to divide, distinguish), via Late Latin 'discriminativus'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00653157-s
Synset POS: s
