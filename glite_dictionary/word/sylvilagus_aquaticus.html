Sylvilagus aquaticus

1. NOUN

/ sɪlˈvaɪləɡəs əˈkwætɪkəs / (AmE), / sɪlˈvɪlɑːɡəs ɔːˈkwætɪkəs / (BrE)
A species of large cottontail rabbit native to the swamps and lowlands of the southeastern United States, commonly known as the swamp rabbit.
species of swamp rabbit in southeastern US

Examples

The Sylvilagus aquaticus is well adapted to wetland habitats in the southeastern states.
Researchers studied the population of Sylvilagus aquaticus in Louisiana swamps.
Unlike other rabbits, Sylvilagus aquaticus often takes to water to evade predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swamp rabbit - common name for Sylvilagus aquaticus

Etymology

From Latin: 'sylva' (forest), 'lagus' (hare), 'aquaticus' (of water), denoting its habitat.
