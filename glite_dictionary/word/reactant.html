reactant

1. NOUN

/ riˈæktənt / (AmE), / riˈæktənt / (BrE)
a substance that takes part in and undergoes change during a chemical reaction
substance involved in a chemical reaction
medical

Examples

In this experiment, hydrogen is the main reactant that combines with oxygen to form water.
The rate of a reaction depends on the concentration of each reactant.
After the process is complete, there may be some reactant left unconsumed in the mixture.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: product - a substance formed as the result of a chemical reaction

Etymology

From 'react' + '-ant', patterned after 'reagent'.
