sextant

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkstənt / (AmE), / ˈsɛkstənt / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure the angular distance between two visible objects, especially in navigation to determine latitude and longitude
navigational measuring instrument

Examples

The captain used a sextant to calculate the ship's position at sea.
Before GPS, sailors relied on the sextant for celestial navigation.
A well-maintained sextant is still considered valuable for traditional navigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: octant - a similar navigational instrument with a 45-degree arc
  • Synonym: astrolabe - an ancient instrument for measuring the altitude of stars and planets

Etymology

From Latin 'sextans' (a sixth part), referring to the instrument's scale of one sixth of a circle (60°).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkstənt / (AmE), / ˈsɛkstənt / (BrE)
a unit of angular distance equal to sixty degrees, especially in geometry or astronomy
an angular distance of sixty degrees

Examples

In geometry, each interior angle of a regular hexagon is a sextant.
The ancient astronomers divided the sky into sextants to measure positions of stars.
A sextant represents exactly one-sixth of a full circle, or sixty degrees.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: degree - a unit for measuring angles, equal to 1⁄360 of a full rotation
  • Degree Higher: quadrant - an arc of a circle representing a quarter of the circumference, or 90 degrees

Etymology

From Latin 'sextans' meaning 'a sixth part,' related to dividing a circle into six equal angles.
