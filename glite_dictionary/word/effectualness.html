effectualness

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈfɛktʃuəlnəs / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛktʃʊəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of producing a desired result or being effective
ability to produce an effect

Examples

The effectualness of the new policy became apparent after just a few weeks.
Debates about the effectualness of alternative medicine have continued for years.
She questioned the effectualness of the strategy in solving the problem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effectiveness - the ability to achieve intended results
  • Antonym: ineffectiveness - lack of ability to produce desired results

Etymology

From effectual + -ness, modeled after Latin effectualis (efficacious).
