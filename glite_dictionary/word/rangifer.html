Rangifer

1. NOUN

/ ˈræn.dʒɪ.fər / (AmE), / ˈran.dʒɪ.fə / (BrE)
A genus of deer that includes the reindeer or caribou.
genus of deer (reindeer/caribou)

Examples

The genus Rangifer includes both reindeer and caribou, which are found in northern regions.
Scientists study the migration patterns of Rangifer to understand animal adaptation to climate change.
Rangifer populations have declined in some areas due to habitat loss and human activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reindeer - a species of deer native to Arctic regions
  • Synonym: caribou - North American name for reindeer

Etymology

From Latin rangifer (reindeer), from Old Norse hreindýri (reindeer). Used as the genus name in scientific taxonomy.
