secularism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkjələrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsɛkjʊlərɪzəm / (BrE)
The belief or principle that religion should not have a role in government, education, or other public affairs.
principle of separating religion from public life

Examples

Secularism advocates the separation of church and state in modern democracies.
The rise of secularism has influenced education systems in many countries.
Some critics argue that too much secularism can erode cultural traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atheism - the belief that there is no god
  • Antonym: religion - the belief in and worship of a superhuman power or powers

Etymology

From secular + -ism. Ultimately from Latin 'saecularis' meaning 'worldly' or 'temporal'.
