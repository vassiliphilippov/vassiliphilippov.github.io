soothsayer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːθˌseɪər / (AmE), / ˈsuːθˌseɪə / (BrE)
a person who claims to be able to predict the future, typically based on special knowledge or supernatural insight
person who predicts the future

Examples

The old village soothsayer warned us about a terrible storm on the horizon.
In ancient times, kings often consulted a soothsayer before making important decisions.
The soothsayer's predictions were met with both fascination and skepticism from the crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prophet - someone regarded as an inspired teacher or proclaimer of the future
  • Synonym: fortune-teller - a person who claims to predict people's futures
  • Antonym: skeptic - a person who doubts or questions accepted beliefs

Etymology

From Middle English 'soth' (truth) + 'sayer' (one who says).
