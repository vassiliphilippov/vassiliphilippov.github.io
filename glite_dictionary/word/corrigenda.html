corrigenda

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːrɪˈdʒɛndə / (AmE), / ˌkɒrɪˈdʒɛndə / (BrE)
a list of errors in a printed book, together with their corrections, usually published on a separate page
list of book errors and corrections

Examples

At the end of the scholarly volume, the publisher included the corrigenda to ensure readers were aware of several corrected errors.
Please refer to the corrigenda sheet before citing this document in your research.
The corrigenda were distributed after the first edition was published to address typographical mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: errata - a list of errors and corrections in a printed work

Etymology

From Latin 'corrigenda', neuter plural of 'corrigendus', meaning 'things to be corrected'.
