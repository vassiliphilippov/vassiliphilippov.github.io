to circumvent

1. VERB

/ ˌsɝː.kəmˈvɛnt / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.kəmˈvɛnt / (BrE)
to find a way around an obstacle or rule, often by using cleverness or deceit
avoid by cleverness or trickery

Examples

She tried to circumvent the company's strict policies by consulting a loophole in the rules.
They managed to circumvent the security system with an ingenious plan.
Many students attempt to circumvent the deadline by requesting extensions for vague reasons.

Synset Examples

I beat the traffic
She outfoxed her competitors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to avoid - to keep away from or prevent something from happening
  • Synonym: to evade - to escape or get around something, especially by cleverness or trickery

Etymology

From Latin circumventus, past participle of circumvenire (to go around, deceive), from circum (around) + venire (to come).

2. VERB

/ ˌsɝː.kəmˈvɛnt / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.kəmˈvent / (BrE)
to surround or enclose (an enemy) so as to force surrender; to besiege
surround or besiege (military)

Examples

The army planned to circumvent the city and cut off all supplies.
The rebels attempted to circumvent the fortress by laying siege for weeks.
By using superior numbers, they managed to circumvent the enemy stronghold.

Synset Examples

The Turks besieged Vienna

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to besiege - to surround a place with armed forces in order to capture it or force surrender

Etymology

From Latin circumventus, past participle of circumvenire ('to surround, deceive'), from circum ('around') + venire ('to come').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01129837-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌsɜrkəmˈvɛnt / (AmE), / ˌsɜːkəmˈvɛnt / (BrE)
to find a way around (an obstacle, duty, or problem), especially in order to avoid it or not do it directly
avoid or get around (duty, problem, etc.)

Examples

The company tried to circumvent the new regulations by using a loophole.
She cleverly circumvented the question by changing the subject.
Many people attempt to circumvent their responsibilities rather than confront them directly.

Synset Examples

He dodged the issue
she skirted the problem
They tend to evade their responsibilities
he evaded the questions skillfully

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evade - to escape or avoid, especially by cleverness
  • Synonym: dodge - to avoid (something) by a sudden, clever movement or by cleverness
  • Antonym: to confront - to face up to and deal with (a problem or difficult situation)

Etymology

From Latin 'circumvenire' (to come around), from 'circum' (around) + 'venire' (to come).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00811316-v
Synset POS: v
