Dovyalis caffra

1. NOUN

/ doʊˈvaɪ.əlɪs ˈkæf.rə / (AmE), / dəʊˈvaɪ.əlɪs ˈkæf.rə / (BrE)
a vigorous South African spiny shrub grown for its round yellow, juicy, edible fruits, also known as the kei apple
South African shrub with edible yellow fruit

Examples

The Dovyalis caffra produces bright yellow fruits that are both tangy and sweet.
Farmers in some regions cultivate Dovyalis caffra for its ornamental value and edible berries.
The thorny branches of Dovyalis caffra make it useful as a natural hedge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kei apple - common name for Dovyalis caffra, a fruit-bearing shrub native to South Africa

Etymology

Dovyalis from Greek 'dovys' (lance) for the shape of the flower parts; caffra from Latinized form referencing southern Africa (Caffraria).
