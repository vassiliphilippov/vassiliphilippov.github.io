Alcides

1. PROPN

/ ælˈsaɪdiːz / (AmE), / ælˈsiːdiːz / (BrE)
In classical mythology, another name for the hero Heracles (Hercules), famous for his great strength and the completion of twelve labors.
another name for Heracles/Hercules in mythology

Examples

Few mortals could hope to match the strength of Alcides, whose exploits were legendary.
The myth of Alcides describes how he completed twelve labors as penance.
Ancient poets often referred to Heracles as Alcides in their works.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Heracles - Greek mythological hero known for his strength and twelve labors
  • Synonym: Hercules - Roman mythological hero equivalent to Greek Heracles

Etymology

From Latin Alcīdēs, from Greek Alkīdēs, a patronymic of Alcaeus, grandfather of Heracles.
