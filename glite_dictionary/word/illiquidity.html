illiquidity

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪlɪˈkwɪdəti / (AmE), / ˌɪlɪˈkwɪdəti / (BrE)
The state of being difficult to sell quickly without a significant loss in value; a lack of liquidity in assets or markets.
state of difficult sellability of assets

Examples

The company's illiquidity made it difficult to pay its short-term debts.
Investors are often concerned about illiquidity when dealing with real estate.
Periods of financial crisis can expose hidden illiquidity in the banking sector.

Synset Examples

The risk of illiquidity need not apply only to individual investments: whole portfolios are subject to market risk.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: liquidity - the ease with which assets can be sold without affecting their price

Etymology

From prefix in- (not) + liquidity, patterned after liquid + -ity.
