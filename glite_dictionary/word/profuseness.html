profuseness

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈfjusnəs / (AmE), / prəˈfjuːsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being extremely abundant; great or excessive quantity
state of being extremely abundant

Examples

The profuseness of flowers in the garden was breathtaking during spring.
She admired the profuseness of historical references in his book.
Many critics praised the profuseness of imagination in the artist's new collection.

Synset Examples

the profusion of detail
the idiomatic richness of English

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abundance - a large quantity of something
  • Antonym: scarcity - the state of being in short supply

Etymology

From Latin 'profus-', past participle stem of 'profundere' (to pour forth).
