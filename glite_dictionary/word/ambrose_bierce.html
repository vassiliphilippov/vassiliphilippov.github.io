Ambrose Bierce

1. PROPN

/ ˈæm.broʊs ˈbɪrs / (AmE), / ˈæm.brəʊz ˈbɪəs / (BrE)
(1842–c. 1914) An American writer and journalist known for his caustic wit, satire, and stories of horror and the supernatural.
American writer and satirist (1842–1914)

Examples

Many critics admire Ambrose Bierce for his sharp satirical style and dark tales.
Ambrose Bierce's disappearance in 1914 remains one of American literature's great mysteries.
The Devil's Dictionary, written by Ambrose Bierce, offers a cynical twist on common words.
