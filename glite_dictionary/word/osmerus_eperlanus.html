Osmerus eperlanus

1. NOUN

/ ɒsˈmɛrəs ˌɛpərˈlɑːnəs / (AmE), / ɒsˈmɛːrəs ˌɛpəˈlɑːnəs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the European smelt, a small fish native to the coastal waters and rivers of northern Europe.
scientific name for European smelt

Examples

The Osmerus eperlanus population in the Baltic Sea has declined due to overfishing.
Scientists studied the migration patterns of Osmerus eperlanus in European rivers.
Fishing for Osmerus eperlanus is a traditional activity in some coastal communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smelt - a small silvery fish found in northern waters

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining genus 'Osmerus' and specific epithet 'eperlanus', from the French 'eperlan' (smelt).
