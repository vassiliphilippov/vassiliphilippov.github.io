blastomere

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæstəˌmɪr / (AmE), / ˈblɑːstəˌmɪə / (BrE)
a cell produced by the cleavage of a fertilized egg during early embryonic development
cell formed during egg cleavage
medical

Examples

Each blastomere in the early embryo has the potential to develop into a complete organism.
Scientists observed the division of a blastomere under the microscope.
After fertilization, the zygote divides into several blastomeres.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: zygote - a fertilized egg cell

Etymology

From Greek 'blastos' (germ) + 'meros' (part).
