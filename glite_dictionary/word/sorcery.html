sorcery

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔːrsəri / (AmE), / ˈsɔːsəri / (BrE)
the use of magic, especially the supposed use of evil supernatural power to influence events or people
the use of magic or evil supernatural power

Examples

The villagers believed that the sickness had been caused by sorcery.
Legends about ancient sorcery have inspired many fantasy novels and films.
He was accused of practicing sorcery and brought before the court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witchcraft - the practice of magic, especially for malevolent purposes
  • Synonym: magic - the power of apparently influencing events using mysterious or supernatural forces
  • Antonym: science - the systematic study of the structure and behavior of the physical and natural world

Etymology

From Old French *sorcerie*, from *sorcier* (sorcerer), from Medieval Latin *sortiarius* (one who casts lots, magician).
