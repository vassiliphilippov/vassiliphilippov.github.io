cocoyam

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊkoʊˌjæm / (AmE), / ˈkəʊkəʊˌjam / (BrE)
An edible starchy tuberous root of certain tropical plants, such as taro or Xanthosoma, commonly used as a food in Africa and other tropical regions.
edible starchy root (taro or similar plants)

Examples

In many West African dishes, cocoyam is boiled, mashed, or fried as a staple food.
She bought some fresh cocoyam from the market to use in her stew.
Farmers in the region cultivate cocoyam because it grows well in their tropical climate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taro - a tropical plant with a starchy root, often grown for food

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊkoʊˌjæm / (AmE), / ˈkəʊkəʊˌjam / (BrE)
A tropical plant grown for its starchy tuberous root, which is used as food in many regions, especially in West Africa and the Caribbean.
tropical root vegetable

Examples

The farmer harvested several cocoyam roots for the market.
One common dish in Cameroon is made from boiled cocoyam and spinach.
Many people in West Africa fry cocoyam as a snack or side dish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taro - a tropical plant with edible starchy tuberous root, often called cocoyam
  • Antonym: yam - an unrelated edible tuberous root also consumed as a staple
