chiaroscuro

1. NOUN

/ kiˌɑroʊˈskjʊroʊ / (AmE), / kiˌɑːrəˈskjʊərəʊ / (BrE)
a technique in art or photography using strong contrasts between light and dark to create a sense of volume or depth, or a monochrome picture employing this effect
art technique using light and shade

Examples

The painter's use of chiaroscuro adds dramatic depth to her portraits.
I studied a chiaroscuro by Rembrandt at the museum yesterday.
Modern photographers often employ chiaroscuro to enhance the mood of black-and-white images.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sfumato - artistic technique creating soft, gradual transitions between colors and tones
  • Degree Higher: tenebrism - art technique using pronounced chiaroscuro with violent contrasts of light and dark

Etymology

From Italian 'chiaroscuro', from 'chiaro' (clear) + 'oscuro' (dark).
