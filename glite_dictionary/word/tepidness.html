tepidness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛpɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈtɛpɪdnɪs / (BrE)
A lack of enthusiasm, passion, or intensity.
lack of enthusiasm or passion

Examples

His tepidness toward the project disappointed his colleagues.
Many critics noted the tepidness of her response to the proposal.
Public tepidness about the reforms made change slow and difficult.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apathy - lack of interest or concern
  • Antonym: enthusiasm - strong excitement or eagerness
  • Synonym: lukewarmness - the quality of being only moderately warm or mildly interested

Etymology

From Latin tepidus (lukewarm) + -ness (suffix forming nouns denoting quality or state).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛpɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈtɛpɪdnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being only slightly warm; moderate or lukewarm warmth.
the state of being slightly warm

Examples

The tepidness of the bathwater made it relaxing but not overly hot.
She noticed the tepidness of his handshake, neither cold nor especially warm.
After an hour, the tepidness of the soup was no longer appetizing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lukewarmness - the quality of being only moderately warm

Etymology

From Latin 'tepidus' (lukewarm) + English '-ness'.
