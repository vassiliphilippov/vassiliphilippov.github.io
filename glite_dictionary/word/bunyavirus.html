bunyavirus

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌnjəˌvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈbʌnjəˌvʌɪərəs / (BrE)
Any virus belonging to the family Bunyaviridae, many of which infect animals and can be transmitted to humans by insects or rodents.
virus of the Bunyaviridae family
medical

Examples

The outbreak was traced to a new strain of bunyavirus in the region.
Researchers are studying how bunyavirus is transmitted between animals and humans.
A vaccine for bunyavirus infections is currently under development.

Etymology

From Bunyamwera, a town in Uganda where the type virus was first isolated, and virus.
