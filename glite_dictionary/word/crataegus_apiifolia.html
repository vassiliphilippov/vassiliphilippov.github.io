Crataegus apiifolia

1. NOUN

/ krəˈtiːɡəs ˌeɪpiˈaɪfəʊliə / (AmE), / krəˈtiːɡəs ˌeɪpɪˈəʊfɪliə / (BrE)
A species of hawthorn native to the southern United States, noted for its pinnately lobed leaves.
species of American hawthorn

Examples

The Crataegus apiifolia is easily identified by its deeply lobed leaves.
Gardeners in the southeastern states sometimes plant Crataegus apiifolia for its ornamental value.
Botanists recently discovered a large stand of Crataegus apiifolia growing in a protected swamp.

Etymology

From the genus name Crataegus, from Greek 'krataigos' (a kind of thorn-tree), and Latin 'apiifolia' meaning 'with leaves like parsley (Apium)'.
