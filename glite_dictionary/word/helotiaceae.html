Helotiaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːloʊʃiˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌhiːləʊʃɪˈiːsiː / (BrE)
A family of fungi in the order Helotiales, typically found in tropical regions, comprising over 100 genera and hundreds of species.
family of fungi in Helotiales order

Examples

The Helotiaceae are known for their wide range of fruiting body shapes.
Many tropical ecosystems contain several members of the Helotiaceae family.
Researchers have identified rare species of Helotiaceae in South American rainforests.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Helotiales - an order of fungi containing Helotiaceae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Helotia plus the standard suffix -aceae used for plant and fungal families.
