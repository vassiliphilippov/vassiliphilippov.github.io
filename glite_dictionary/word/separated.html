separated

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛpəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɛpəˌreɪtɪd / (BrE)
being or feeling set or kept apart from others; not connected or together with other people or things
set or kept apart from others

Examples

After the disagreement, he felt separated from his closest friends.
The children were separated into small groups for the activity.
She often feels separated from her peers because of her unique interests.

Synset Examples

she felt detached from the group
could not remain the isolated figure he had been
thought of herself as alone and separated from the others
had a set-apart feeling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isolated - being alone or kept apart from others
  • Antonym: connected - joined or linked with others; not separated

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɛpəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɛpəreɪtɪd / (BrE)
not connected or joined together; detached from something else.
not connected or joined

Examples

The separated wires must be kept apart for safety reasons.
After the accident, several separated pieces of metal were found across the road.
The separated rooms offered more privacy to each family member.

Synset Examples

a detached part
on one side of the island was a huge rock, almost detached
the separated spacecraft will return to their home bases

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detached - not joined to or connected with something; separate
  • Antonym: connected - joined or linked together

Etymology

From Latin separatus, past participle of separare (to separate).

(of a joint) having been forced apart, especially so that the bones are no longer in their normal positions.
joint forced apart; out of position

Examples

She could not lift her arm due to the pain from her separated joint.
The doctor explained the difference between a separated shoulder and a dislocated one.

Synset Examples

a separated shoulder

Related Terms
