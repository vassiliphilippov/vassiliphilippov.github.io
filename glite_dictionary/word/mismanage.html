to mismanage

1. VERB

/ mɪsˈmænɪdʒ / (AmE), / mɪsˈmænɪdʒ / (BrE)
to manage something poorly or incompetently
manage badly or incompetently

Examples

Several public officials had mismanaged the city's finances for years.
If you mismanage your team, productivity will suffer.
The school was criticized for mismanaging its resources.

Synset Examples

The funds were mismanaged

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to manage - to be in charge of or control something

Etymology

From 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'manage' (from Old French 'managier').
