butchering

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊtʃərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʊtʃərɪŋ / (BrE)
the trade or business of a butcher, involving the slaughtering and preparation of animals for meat
the work or business of a butcher

Examples

He learned the art of butchering from his father, who ran the family’s local meat shop.
Modern butchering requires careful attention to hygiene and safety regulations.
After years in the restaurant industry, she decided to open her own butchering business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butcher - a person who slaughters animals and sells their meat

Etymology

From butcher + -ing, denoting the act or business.
