shaddock

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃædək / (AmE), / ˈʃadək / (BrE)
A large, pear-shaped citrus fruit similar to a grapefruit but with a thicker rind and coarse, dry pulp; also the tree bearing this fruit.
large citrus fruit like grapefruit

Examples

The shaddock is much larger than a typical grapefruit and has a thicker rind.
In some regions, people use shaddock peel to make candied fruit.
She planted a shaddock tree in her garden for its impressive fruit.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: grapefruit - a large, round citrus fruit with a thin yellow skin and sour pulp

Etymology

Named after Captain Shaddock, who is believed to have introduced the fruit from the East Indies to the West Indies in the 17th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃæd.ək / (AmE), / ˈʃæd.ək / (BrE)
A large, citrus fruit tree (Citrus maxima), native to Southeast Asia, that produces fruits similar to grapefruit; also, the fruit itself.
large citrus tree or fruit similar to grapefruit

Examples

The shaddock tree is prized for its unusually large, thick-skinned fruits.
Farmers harvested the ripe shaddocks and sold them at the village market.
Unlike grapefruit, the shaddock usually has a sweeter and less bitter flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pomelo - another name for the shaddock fruit
  • Degree Lower: grapefruit - a hybrid citrus fruit related to the shaddock

Etymology

Named after Captain Shaddock, who is said to have introduced the plant from the Malay Archipelago to the West Indies in the 17th century.
