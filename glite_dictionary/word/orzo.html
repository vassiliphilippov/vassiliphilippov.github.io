orzo

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrzoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɔːtsəʊ / (BrE)
A type of pasta shaped like grains of barley, commonly used in soups and salads, especially in Mediterranean cuisine.
a rice-shaped pasta

Examples

She added orzo to the chicken soup to make it more filling.
The Greek restaurant served lamb with orzo and tomato sauce.
I bought a bag of orzo to use in my summer salads.

Etymology

From Italian 'orzo' meaning 'barley', referring to its barley-like shape.
