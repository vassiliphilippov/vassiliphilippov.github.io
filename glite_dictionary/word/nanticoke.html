Nanticoke

1. NOUN

/ ˈnæn.tɪˌkoʊk / (AmE), / ˈnæn.tɪ.kəʊk / (BrE)
the Algonquian language traditionally spoken by the Nanticoke and Conoy peoples of the eastern United States
an Algonquian Native American language

Examples

Linguists have been working to document and revive the Nanticoke language.
Few people today are fluent speakers of Nanticoke due to historical displacements.
The museum exhibit included audio recordings of traditional Nanticoke words and stories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian - a family of Native American languages of North America

Etymology

From the name of the Nanticoke people, an Algonquian-speaking tribe.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnæn.tɪˌkoʊk / (AmE), / ˈnæn.tɪ.kəʊk / (BrE)
A member of an Algonquian Indigenous people formerly living in Maryland and eastern Delaware.
a Native American people of the U.S. East Coast

Examples

The Nanticoke were once settled along the rivers of Maryland and Delaware.
A descendant of the Nanticoke shared stories of her ancestors at the local museum.
Historians have studied how the Nanticoke adapted to European colonization in the 17th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian - a group of Indigenous peoples of North America

Etymology

From the Nanticoke people's own language, or from an Algonquian word meaning 'tidewater people'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09682235-n
Synset POS: n
