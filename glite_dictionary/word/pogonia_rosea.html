Pogonia rosea

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈɡoʊniə ˈroʊziə / (AmE), / pəˈɡəʊniə ˈrəʊziə / (BrE)
A species of orchid native to central and northern South America, noted for its racemes of large, showy rose-colored flowers; sometimes classified in the genus Pogonia.
South American orchid species with rose-colored flowers

Examples

The delicate beauty of the Pogonia rosea makes it a favorite among orchid collectors.
Botanists discovered several specimens of Pogonia rosea blossoming in the rainforest.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the natural habitat of the Pogonia rosea.

Etymology

From the genus name Pogonia (Greek: pogon, beard) and Latin rosea (rosy, rose-colored).
