Capsicum frutescens baccatum

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæpsɪkəm fruːˈtɛsənz bəˈkɑːtəm / (AmE), / ˈkæpsɪkəm fruːˈtɛsənz bəˈkætəm / (BrE)
A plant species bearing very small, extremely hot oblong red fruits, including wild forms native to tropical America, considered ancestral to both sweet and hot peppers.
species of hot pepper plant

Examples

The fiery hot fruits of the Capsicum frutescens baccatum are prized in many South American cuisines.
Some horticulturists believe that Capsicum frutescens baccatum is the wild ancestor of today’s sweet bell peppers.
Researchers are studying the unique chemical compounds found in Capsicum frutescens baccatum for their potential health benefits.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pepper - A pungent, usually hot-tasting fruit of the capsicum family

Etymology

From Latin: 'capsicum' (pepper), 'frutescens' (becoming shrubby), 'baccatum' (with berries).
