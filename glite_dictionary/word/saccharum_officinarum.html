Saccharum officinarum

1. NOUN

/ ˌsækəˈruːm ˌɒfɪsɪˈnɛərəm / (AmE), / ˌsakəˈruːm ˌɒfɪsɪˈneərəm / (BrE)
A tall tropical grass native to Southeast Asia, cultivated for its thick jointed stems whose sap is a chief source of sugar; commonly known as sugarcane.
species of sugarcane; source of sugar

Examples

The fields were filled with rows of Saccharum officinarum ready for harvest.
Researchers studied the disease resistance of Saccharum officinarum in varying climates.
The juice extracted from Saccharum officinarum is processed into raw sugar in many countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sugarcane - common name for Saccharum officinarum; tropical tall grass cultivated for sugar

Etymology

From Latin: 'Saccharum' meaning sugar and 'officinarum' meaning of the shops (used in medicines).
