cast-iron

1. ADJ

/ ˈkæst ˌaɪ.ərn / (AmE), / ˌkɑːst ˈaɪ.ən / (BrE)
Extremely strong, tough, or impossible to break or weaken; not likely to fail or be doubted.
very strong or unbreakable

Examples

She had a cast-iron excuse for missing the meeting.
His cast-iron stomach allowed him to eat anything without getting sick.
They constructed their case with cast-iron arguments that convinced everyone.

Synset Examples

an iron constitution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unbreakable - cannot be broken; extremely strong
  • Antonym: fragile - easily broken or damaged

Etymology

From cast iron, a type of hard, brittle iron shaped by being melted and poured into a mould, used metaphorically since the 19th century.
