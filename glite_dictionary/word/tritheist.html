tritheist

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈθiːɪst / (AmE), / traɪˈθiːɪst / (BrE)
A person who believes that the Father, Son, and Holy Ghost are three separate gods, rather than three aspects of one God.
believer in three separate gods (Christian context)

Examples

The theologian was accused of being a tritheist because of his interpretation of the Trinity.
Unlike orthodox Christians, a tritheist sees three distinct gods rather than one.
In the debate, she denied ever holding tritheist beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: trinitarian - someone who believes in the Christian doctrine of the Trinity, that God is three in one

Etymology

From Late Greek tritheos (three gods) + -ist.
