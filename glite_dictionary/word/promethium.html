promethium

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈmiːθiəm / (AmE), / prəˈmiːθɪəm / (BrE)
a soft, silvery, radioactive metallic element of the rare earth group, having no stable isotopes; symbol Pm
radioactive rare earth element (Pm)

Examples

Scientists use promethium in some types of luminous paint for watches and instruments.
Because promethium is radioactive, it must be handled with caution in laboratory settings.
The element promethium was first discovered as a product of uranium fission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: element - a substance that cannot be broken down into simpler substances by chemical means
  • Synonym: rare earth element - any of a set of seventeen chemical elements in the periodic table

Etymology

Named after Prometheus, a figure in Greek mythology, plus the chemical suffix -ium.
