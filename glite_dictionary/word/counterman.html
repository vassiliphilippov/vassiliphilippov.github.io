counterman

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntɚˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəmæn / (BrE)
a man who serves customers at a counter, especially in a diner or lunchroom
man serving at a counter

Examples

The counterman took my order with a friendly smile.
She asked the counterman for a cup of coffee and a sandwich.
During the lunch rush, the counterman worked quickly to serve all the customers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: counterwoman - a woman who serves customers at a counter

Etymology

From 'counter' (serving surface) + 'man'; recorded in the early 20th century.
