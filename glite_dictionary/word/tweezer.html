tweezer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtwiːzər / (AmE), / ˈtwiːzə / (BrE)
A small hand-held tool with two narrow arms for gripping or holding small objects, typically operated by pinching the arms together.
small tool for gripping with two narrow arms

Examples

She used her tweezer to remove the tiny splinter from her finger.
A tweezer is essential for shaping eyebrows precisely.
Laboratory technicians often rely on a tweezer to handle delicate samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forceps - a medical tool for grasping or holding, often similar to tweezers
  • Synonym: pincer - a gripping tool or claw consisting of two hinged parts

Etymology

From French *pincettes*, plural of *pincette* (small tongs), related to 'pinch.'
