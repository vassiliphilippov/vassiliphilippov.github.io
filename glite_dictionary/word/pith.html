pith

1. NOUN

/ pɪθ / (AmE), / pɪθ / (BrE)
the soft, spongy central tissue in the stems of most flowering plants, which stores and transports nutrients
soft core tissue of plant stems

Examples

The cross-section of the branch revealed a white pith at its center.
Some insects feed on the pith of plant stems, causing structural weakness.
The scientist examined the pith to study nutrient transport in the specimen.

Etymology

From Old English pitha (pith or substance).

2. NOUN

/ pɪθ / (AmE), / pɪθ / (BrE)
the most essential or central part of an idea, experience, or matter; the heart or core
the essential or central part; the core

Examples

He summarized the pith of the debate in just a few words.
The pith of her argument was that everyone deserves an equal chance.
Critics praised the novel for capturing the pith of human emotion.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: essence - the intrinsic nature or indispensable quality of something
  • Synonym: core - the central or most important part of something
  • Antonym: surface - the outermost or least central part; as opposed to the essence

Etymology

From Old English pitha (soft inner part of plants); figurative sense from 16th century.

3. VERB

/ pɪθ / (AmE), / pɪθ / (BrE)
to remove the spongy tissue (pith) from the inside of a plant, especially from fruits or stems
remove spongy tissue from a plant

Examples

To make the marmalade less bitter, carefully pith the oranges before adding them to the pot.
You need to pith the stalks to ensure the recipe turns out smooth.
After harvesting the stems, the botanist proceeded to pith them for further study.

Etymology

From Middle English pith, originally a noun meaning 'essential part' or 'spongy tissue' inside a plant.
