genus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs / (BrE)
a class or kind of things that have common characteristics and can be grouped together; a general category
a category or class with common traits

Examples

Many tales belong to the same genus of traditional folklore.
He compared the works as examples of the same literary genus.
Discussions of art often try to define the genus before mentioning particular styles.

Synset Examples

ignore the genus communism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: category - a class or division of people or things regarded as having particular shared characteristics
  • Degree Lower: species - a more specific division or kind within a genus; a distinct sort

Etymology

From Latin genus (kind, type, race).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs / (BrE)
(biology) A taxonomic rank used in the classification of organisms, ranking above species and below family, and consisting of related species.
biological taxonomic rank above species
medical

Examples

The genus Panthera includes lions, tigers, and leopards.
Roses belong to the genus Rosa, which contains over a hundred species.
Scientists discovered a new genus of bacteria in the soil sample.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: species - the basic unit of classification, below genus
  • Degree Higher: family - taxonomic group above genus

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (birth, origin, kind, sort).
