Albertism

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.bɚˌtɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈæl.bətˌɪz.əm / (BrE)
the philosophy or doctrines of Albertus Magnus, a German scholastic philosopher of the Middle Ages
philosophy of Albertus Magnus

Examples

Albertism played a significant role in shaping late medieval scholastic thought.
The rise of Albertism marked a distinct philosophical movement separate from Thomism and Scotism.
Few modern scholars strictly adhere to Albertism, but its influence can still be seen in contemporary metaphysics.

Synset Examples

These differences articulated themselves in medieval thought in the form of philosophical schools, the best-known of which are Thomism, Scotism, Albertism, and nominalism.

Etymology

From the name Albertus Magnus (1193–1280), medieval German philosopher, plus the suffix -ism.
