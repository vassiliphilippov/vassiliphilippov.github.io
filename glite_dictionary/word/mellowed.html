mellowed

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɛloʊd / (AmE), / ˈmɛl.əʊd / (BrE)
(of a person, feeling, or thing) made softer, gentler, or more relaxed, especially as a result of age or experience.
softened or gentler through age or experience

Examples

Her mellowed attitude made family gatherings much more enjoyable.
The wine had mellowed over the years, gaining a smooth, rich flavor.
With age, his sharp criticism mellowed into thoughtful advice.

Synset Examples

mellow wisdom
the peace of mellow age

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mellow - (of a person or thing) pleasantly smooth or soft; matured and softened

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɛloʊd / (AmE), / ˈmɛl.əʊd / (BrE)
(of food or drink) having developed a full, rich, and pleasant flavor as a result of proper aging.
pleasantly aged in flavor

Examples

The mellowed cheese had a deep, nuanced taste after two years of aging.
A glass of mellowed whiskey is the perfect way to end a meal.
These mellowed plums have a sweetness that only comes with time.

Synset Examples

a mellow port
mellowed fruit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mellow - having a smooth, rich, and pleasant flavor or quality

Etymology

Past participle of 'mellow,' which is of uncertain origin but related to Old English 'melu' (meal, flour) and used metaphorically for ripeness and richness.
