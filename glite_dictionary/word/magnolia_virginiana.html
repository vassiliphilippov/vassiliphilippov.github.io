Magnolia virginiana

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˈnoʊliə vɜrˌdʒɪˈneɪnə / (AmE), / mæɡˈnəʊliə vɜːdʒɪˈneɪnə / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States, with small, fragrant white flowers, commonly called sweetbay magnolia.
a North American shrub or small tree; sweetbay magnolia

Examples

The Magnolia virginiana blooms in late spring, filling the air with its sweet fragrance.
Botanists observed that Magnolia virginiana can thrive in both wetland and upland habitats.
Gardeners often choose Magnolia virginiana for its attractive, glossy leaves and delicate white flowers.

Etymology

Magnolia named after Pierre Magnol, a French botanist; virginiana refers to Virginia, the region where the plant is native.
