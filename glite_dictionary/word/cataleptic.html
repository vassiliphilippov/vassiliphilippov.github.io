cataleptic

1. NOUN

/ ˌkætəˈlɛptɪk / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈlɛptɪk / (BrE)
a person who suffers from catalepsy, a medical condition characterized by a trance or seizure with a loss of sensation and consciousness accompanied by rigidity of the body
person suffering from catalepsy
medical

Examples

The doctor observed the cataleptic in a rigid, motionless state.
During the episode, the cataleptic remained unresponsive for several minutes.
A support group was formed for families of cataleptics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catalepsy - a medical condition involving loss of sensation with motor rigidity

Etymology

From French 'cataleptique', from Greek 'katalēptikos', from 'katalepsis' (catalepsy).

2. ADJ

/ ˌkætəˈlɛptɪk / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈlɛptɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or affected by catalepsy, a medical condition characterized by muscular rigidity and lack of response to external stimuli.
relating to catalepsy (medical condition)
medical

Examples

The patient entered a cataleptic state and did not respond to any external stimuli.
Some witnesses described her trance as eerily cataleptic in nature.
Doctors struggled to diagnose the rare cataleptic symptoms that he displayed.

Synset Examples

cataleptic persons
cataleptic state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catalepsy - a medical condition with muscular rigidity and lack of response

Etymology

From Greek katalēptikos, from katalēpsis (catalepsy).
