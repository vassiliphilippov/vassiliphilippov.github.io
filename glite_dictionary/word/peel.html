peel

1. NOUN

/ pil / (AmE), / piﾋ人 / (BrE)
The outer skin or rind of a fruit or vegetable, typically removed before eating.
outer rind of fruit or vegetable

Examples

The banana's peel turns brown as it ripens.
She threw the orange peel into the compost bin.
Lemon peel is often used to add flavor to recipes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rind - the tough outer skin of certain fruits
  • Synonym: skin - the thin outer covering of fruit or vegetable

Etymology

From Middle English 'pel', from Old French 'pel', from Latin 'pellis' (skin, hide).
