torsion

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔr.ʒən / (AmE), / ˈtɔː.ʃən / (BrE)
The act of twisting or the state of being twisted, especially of one end of an object relative to the other. In physics, a twisting force that causes rotation or torsional deformation.
a twisting force; act of being twisted

Examples

The bridge's cables are designed to withstand considerable torsion from wind and vehicles.
A mechanic measures the torsion in the drive shaft to ensure it functions safely.
When you apply torsion to a rod, it twists around its axis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twist - act of twisting or the resulting shape

Etymology

From Latin 'torsio(n-)', from 'torquere' meaning 'to twist'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɔrʃən / (AmE), / ˈtɔːʃən / (BrE)
the state of being twisted or having a distorted shape or position resulting from twisting
the state of being twisted

Examples

The metal rod displayed significant torsion after being bent by the powerful machine.
The sculptor admired the natural torsion in the driftwood he found on the beach.
The gymnast's routine included moments of remarkable torsion and balance.

Synset Examples

they built a tree house in the tortuosities of its boughs
the acrobat performed incredible contortions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contortion - a twisted or bent condition, state, or form
  • Synonym: torsion - rotational force or act of twisting (mechanical sense)
  • Antonym: straightness - state of being straight, not twisted

Etymology

From Latin 'torsio' (a twisting), from 'torquēre' (to twist).
