mutinous

1. ADJ

/ ˈmjuːtənəs / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtɪnəs / (BrE)
refusing to obey authority, or likely to cause a rebellion
refusing authority; rebellious

Examples

The crew became mutinous after months without pay.
She gave him a mutinous look during the heated argument.
There was a mutinous atmosphere among the students, who demanded change.

Synset Examples

mutinous acts
mutinous thoughts
a mutinous speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebellious - showing a desire to resist authority or control
  • Antonym: obedient - willing to obey authority or rules

Etymology

From French 'mutin' (rebellious) + English -ous; from Latin 'movēre' (to move).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmjuːtənəs / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtɪnəs / (BrE)
refusing to obey the orders of a person in authority; showing a will to rebel or revolt, especially against military or naval authority
refusing to obey authority; rebellious

Examples

The sailors grew mutinous after being kept at sea for several months without shore leave.
Her mutinous attitude threatened the stability of the entire classroom.
Several mutinous soldiers refused to follow the commander's orders.

Synset Examples

the men became mutinous and insubordinate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebellious - showing a desire to resist authority; synonym for mutinous
  • Antonym: obedient - willing to comply or follow orders; antonym for mutinous

Etymology

From French mutin (rebellious, mutineer), from mutinier (to rebel); see also 'mutiny'.
