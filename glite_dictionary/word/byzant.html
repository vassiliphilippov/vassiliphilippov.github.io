byzant

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪzænt / (AmE), / ˈbɪzənt / (BrE)
A gold coin of the Byzantine Empire that was widely circulated in Europe during the Middle Ages.
gold coin of Byzantine Empire

Examples

The merchant insisted on payment in byzant rather than silver.
Archaeologists uncovered a rare byzant among the medieval artifacts.
In medieval Europe, a single byzant could buy a small plot of land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solidus - another gold coin from the late Roman and Byzantine Empires

Etymology

From French 'byzantin', from late Latin 'Byzantinus', referring to Byzantium (Constantinople, present-day Istanbul).
