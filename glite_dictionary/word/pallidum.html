pallidum

1. NOUN

/ ˈpælɪdəm / (AmE), / ˈpælɪdəm / (BrE)
the pale yellowish basal ganglia structure in the brain, forming the inner part of the lenticular nucleus
brain structure in the basal ganglia
medical

Examples

The pallidum plays a key role in regulating voluntary movement.
Damage to the pallidum can lead to movement disorders such as Parkinson's disease.
Researchers studied the pallidum to better understand its involvement in brain function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: globus pallidus - major component of the pallidum in the basal ganglia
  • Degree Higher: putamen - the outer part of the lenticular nucleus of the brain

Etymology

From Latin 'pallidus' meaning 'pale', referring to its pale appearance.
