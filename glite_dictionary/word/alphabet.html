alphabet

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.fəˌbɛt / (AmE), / ˈæl.fə.bɛt / (BrE)
the basic or elementary principles of a subject, usually considered as the first things to learn
basic principles of a subject

Examples

After studying the alphabet of music theory, she felt ready to compose her first song.
Children begin with the alphabet of mathematics before moving to more complex topics.
He admitted that he only knew the alphabet of Italian cuisine and wanted to learn more.

Synset Examples

he mastered only the rudiments of geometry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rudiments - the basic or essential elements of something
  • Synonym: fundamentals - the central or elementary principles of a subject

Etymology

From Greek *alphabētos*, formed from alpha + beta, the first two letters of the Greek alphabet.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæl.fəˌbɛt / (AmE), / ˈæl.fə.bɛt / (BrE)
A set of letters or symbols in a fixed order, used to represent the basic sounds of a language in writing.
set of letters used in writing a language

Examples

The English alphabet has twenty-six letters.
Children often learn the alphabet before they start reading words.
The Cyrillic alphabet is used in Russian and several other languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syllabary - a set of written symbols representing syllables

Etymology

From Greek 'alphabētos', from 'alpha' and 'beta', the first two letters of the Greek alphabet.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06509450-n
Synset POS: n
