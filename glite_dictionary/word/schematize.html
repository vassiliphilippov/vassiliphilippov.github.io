to schematize

1. VERB

/ ˈskiːməˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈskiːmətaɪz / (BrE)
to organize or represent something according to a systematic or conventional scheme; to reduce to a simplified or structured form
organize into a systematic scheme

Examples

Modern textbooks often schematize complex scientific concepts for easier understanding.
The architect chose to schematize the building's facade, reducing it to clean, simple lines.
Some artists intentionally schematize nature, representing trees and mountains with geometric shapes.

Synset Examples

some art forms schematise designs into geometrical patterns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to systematize - to arrange according to a system or plan
  • Synonym: to simplify - to make less complex or complicated
  • Antonym: to complicate - to make something more complex or difficult

Etymology

From Greek 'skhēmatizein' (to form, to shape), from 'skhēma' (form, figure).

2. VERB

/ ˈskiːməˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈskiːmətaɪz / (BrE)
to arrange or represent something according to a system, scheme, or formula; to organize methodically
arrange or represent systematically

Examples

The professor asked the students to schematize their research findings for the presentation.
It is useful to schematize complex processes to make them easier to understand.
Her report schematizes the company's organizational structure in clear, labeled diagrams.

Synset Examples

The chemists schematized the various reactions in a coherent fashion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to systematize - to organize according to a system

Etymology

From Greek 'schēmatizein', from 'schēma' (form, figure).
