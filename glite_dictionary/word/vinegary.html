vinegary

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɪnəɡəri / (AmE), / ˈvɪnɪɡəri / (BrE)
(of a person or manner) having a sour or ill-tempered disposition.
ill-tempered; sour disposition

Examples

Her vinegary remarks left everyone feeling slightly offended at the meeting.
He always has a vinegary attitude, especially on Monday mornings.
The critic’s vinegary review discouraged the actors from continuing the play.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surly - bad-tempered and unfriendly
  • Antonym: amiable - having a friendly and pleasant manner

Etymology

From vinegar + -y, referring figuratively to the sourness of vinegar.

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɪnəɡəri / (AmE), / ˈvɪnɪɡəri / (BrE)
having a sharp, sour taste or smell like that of vinegar
tasting or smelling like vinegar

Examples

The salad had a vinegary dressing that gave it a refreshing tang.
A vinegary aroma filled the kitchen as the chef prepared the pickles.
Her expression turned vinegary after tasting the sour lemonade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acidic - having a sour taste or containing acid
  • Antonym: sweet - having a sugary taste, opposite of sour or acidic

Etymology

From vinegar + -y, meaning 'like' or 'having the qualities of'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈvaɪnəˌɡɛri / (AmE), / ˈvɪnɪɡəri / (BrE)
having a sharp, sour, or acidic taste or smell, similar to that of vinegar; also used to describe speech or tone that is harsh or cutting.
having a sour, vinegary taste or tone

Examples

The salad dressing tasted unusually vinegary, overwhelming the fresh vegetables.
Her vinegary tone made everyone in the room uncomfortable.
The cheese had a slightly vinegary aroma, suggesting it had aged too long.

Synset Examples

His comments were vinegary and unkind.
The soup had a vinegary flavor that was unpleasant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acidic - having a sour or sharp taste like acid
  • Antonym: sweet - having a pleasant, sugary taste, opposite of sour or acidic

Etymology

From vinegar + -y, meaning 'having the qualities of vinegar'.
