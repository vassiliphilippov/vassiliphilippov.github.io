surlily

1. ADV

/ ˈsɝːlɪli / (AmE), / ˈsɜːlɪli / (BrE)
in a rude, unfriendly, or bad-tempered manner
in a surly manner

Examples

He spoke surlily to the waiter, barely making eye contact.
She responded surlily when asked about her plans for the weekend.
The guard glared surlily at anyone who came too close to the entrance.

Synset Examples

the store owner treated his customers churlishly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surly - bad-tempered and unfriendly
  • Antonym: pleasantly - in a friendly and agreeable manner

Etymology

From 'surly' + '-ly'; 'surly' from Middle English 'serely', from Old English 'sirlic' (lordly, haughty).
