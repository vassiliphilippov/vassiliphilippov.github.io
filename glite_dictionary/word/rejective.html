rejective

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈdʒɛktɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɛktɪv / (BrE)
inclined to reject or characterized by rejection
having a tendency to reject

Examples

The teacher's rejective attitude discouraged students from asking questions.
He noticed a rejective tone in her response to his invitation.
Some children become withdrawn after repeated rejective experiences from peers.

Synset Examples

rejective or overcritical attitudes of disappointed parents

Related Terms

  • Antonym: accepting - inclined to accept or receive

Etymology

Formed from 'reject' + '-ive', the suffix forming adjectives with the sense 'having a tendency to'.
