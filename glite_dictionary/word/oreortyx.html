Oreortyx

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˈriːəˌrtaɪks / (AmE), / ɒˈriːəˌtɪks / (BrE)
A genus of birds consisting of the mountain quail found in the western United States.
genus of mountain quail

Examples

The Oreortyx is known for its preference for dense chaparral habitats in the Sierra Nevada.
Researchers observed a flock of Oreortyx nesting at higher altitudes this spring.
The distinctive topknot on the Oreortyx makes it easy to identify among other quail species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain quail - a species of quail native to mountainous regions of the western United States

Etymology

From Greek 'oros' (mountain) and 'ortyx' (quail), reflecting the bird's habitat and type.
