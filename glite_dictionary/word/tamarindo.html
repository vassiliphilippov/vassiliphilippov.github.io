tamarindo

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɑːməˈriːndoʊ / (AmE), / ˌtaməˈrɪndəʊ / (BrE)
A tropical evergreen tree with feathery foliage and fragrant flowers, producing long pods with edible, acidic pulp often used in cooking.
tropical tree with edible acidic fruit

Examples

The refreshing drink was made from tamarindo and served over ice.
Local markets often sell candies flavored with tamarindo pulp.
She bought a bag of dried tamarindo to use in her recipes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tamarind - the fruit or tree of Tamarindus indica

Etymology

From Spanish 'tamarindo', from Arabic 'tamr hindī' meaning 'Indian date'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtæməˈrindoʊ / (AmE), / ˌtaməˈrɪndəʊ / (BrE)
A large tropical pod fruit with a tangy pulp, used in cooking or eaten fresh, especially in Latin American and Caribbean cuisines.
tropical fruit with tangy pulp

Examples

The chef made a refreshing drink from tamarindo and sugar.
You can find tamarindo candies in many Latin American grocery stores.
The curry gets its sour flavor from the addition of tamarindo paste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tamarind - a tropical tree and its fruit; the better-known English term

Etymology

From Spanish 'tamarindo', ultimately from Arabic 'tamr hindī' (Indian date).
