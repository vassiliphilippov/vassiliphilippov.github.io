aurochs

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɑks / (AmE), / ˈɔːrɒks / (BrE)
an extinct species of large wild ox (Bos primigenius) that lived in Europe, considered an ancestor of domestic cattle
extinct wild European ox

Examples

Archaeologists found the bones of an aurochs buried beneath the ancient settlement.
The last known aurochs died in Poland in the 17th century.
Many breeds of cattle are thought to descend from the aurochs.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cattle - domesticated bovine animals

Etymology

From German 'Auerochse', from Middle High German 'ūrochse', from Old High German 'ūrohso' (wild ox).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɑks / (AmE), / ˈɔːrɒks / (BrE)
an extinct type of large wild cattle (Bos primigenius) that once roamed Europe, Asia, and North Africa; the ancestor of domestic cattle
extinct large wild cattle

Examples

The last wild aurochs died in Poland in the 17th century.
Scientists study ancient aurochs bones to learn about cattle domestication.
Early cave paintings often depicted aurochs along with other prehistoric animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bison - large wild cattle with a shaggy mane, native to North America and Europe

Etymology

From German 'Auerochs', from Old High German 'ūrohso', from 'ūr' (aurochs) + 'ohso' (ox).
