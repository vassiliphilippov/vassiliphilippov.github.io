Machiavellianism

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæk.i.əˈvɛl.i.əˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌmak.i.əˈvɛl.i.ə.nɪz.əm / (BrE)
the use of cunning, deception, or unscrupulous methods, especially in politics or in advancing one's own power, as advocated by the political philosophy of Niccolò Machiavelli
cunning or unscrupulous political conduct

Examples

Many critics accused the leader of Machiavellianism in his rise to power.
Corporate Machiavellianism can undermine a positive workplace culture.
The novel explores Machiavellianism among politicians seeking control at any cost.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: realpolitik - a system of politics based on practical rather than moral or ideological considerations
  • Antonym: idealism - the practice of forming or pursuing ideals, often unrealistically

Etymology

From Niccolò Machiavelli, Italian Renaissance political philosopher, whose name is associated with cunning political strategy.
