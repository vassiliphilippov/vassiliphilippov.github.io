Alopex

1. NOUN

/ əˈloʊpɛks / (AmE), / əˈləʊpɛks / (BrE)
A former genus name for the arctic fox, now usually classified as Vulpes lagopus.
former genus name for arctic fox

Examples

The species once placed in the genus Alopex is now generally called Vulpes lagopus.
Researchers studied the population decline of Alopex across various arctic regions.
The term Alopex is still used in some zoological texts to refer to the arctic fox.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arctic fox - a small fox of the arctic regions, known for its white winter coat

Etymology

From Greek alōpēx (fox).
