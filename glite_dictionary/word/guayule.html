guayule

1. NOUN

/ gwaɪˈjuːli / (AmE), / gwaɪˈjuːli / (BrE)
a shrub native to Texas and northern Mexico, cultivated for its ability to produce natural rubber from its sap
rubber-producing shrub of Texas and Mexico

Examples

The guayule plant is a promising alternative source of natural rubber.
Researchers have improved the cultivation of guayule in arid climates.
Local farmers are planting guayule to diversify their crops and support sustainable industries.

Etymology

From Spanish 'guayule', from Nahuatl 'huayuli', the indigenous name for the plant.
