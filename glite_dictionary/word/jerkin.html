jerkin

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɜrkɪn / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːkɪn / (BrE)
a tight, sleeveless, and collarless jacket, often made of leather, worn by men in former times
historical sleeveless men's jacket

Examples

The actor wore a jerkin as part of his costume for the Shakespearean play.
In portraits from the Elizabethan era, noblemen are often depicted in elaborate jerkins.
The museum displayed a leather jerkin that once belonged to a 16th-century soldier.

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch *jherken* (a diminutive of *jher* ‘waistcoat’).
