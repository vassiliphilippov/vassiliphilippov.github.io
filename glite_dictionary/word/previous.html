previous

1. ADJ

/ ˈpriːviəs / (AmE), / ˈpriːviəs / (BrE)
(used especially of people) belonging to the immediate past; existing or occurring just before the present one.
of the immediate past

Examples

The previous manager implemented several changes before retiring.
She learned that the previous owner had renovated the kitchen.
Our previous conversation helped clarify many of my questions.

Synset Examples

the former president
our late President is still very active
the previous occupant of the White House

Related Terms

  • Synonym: former - having held a particular position or status before the present one
  • Antonym: successor - a person who comes after another in an office or position

Etymology

From Latin 'praevius' (going before), from 'prae-' (before) + 'via' (way).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpriːviəs / (AmE), / ˈpriːviəs / (BrE)
Happening or existing before the time or event that has just been referred to.
coming before in time or order

Examples

The previous owner of the house left all the furniture behind.
She apologized for missing the previous meeting and promised to attend the next one.
In the previous chapter, the author introduced an important new character.

Synset Examples

the previous owner
my old house was larger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prior - existing or coming before in time, order, or importance
  • Antonym: following - coming after or next in order

Etymology

From Latin 'prævius' (going before), from 'præ-' (before) + 'via' (way).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00128009-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈpriːviəs / (AmE), / ˈpriːviəs / (BrE)
(of an action, statement, etc.) done too soon or with undue haste; premature.
too soon or hasty; premature (of actions/statements)

Examples

I was a bit previous in announcing the results before the official confirmation.
His previous comments ended up causing unnecessary confusion among the team.
They admitted their criticism was previous and retracted their statement after learning the full details.

Synset Examples

our condemnation of him was a bit previous
a premature judgment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: premature - happening or made too early; before the proper time
