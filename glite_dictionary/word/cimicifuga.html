Cimicifuga

1. NOUN

/ sɪˌmɪsɪˈfjuːɡə / (AmE), / sɪˌmɪsɪˈfjuːɡə / (BrE)
A small genus of perennial herbs native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, commonly known as bugbane.
genus of flowering herbs (bugbane)

Examples

The roots of Cimicifuga have long been used in traditional medicine.
Gardeners often plant Cimicifuga for its tall flower spikes and shade tolerance.
Cimicifuga is prized for its ability to repel some garden pests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bugbane - common name for plants of the genus Cimicifuga

Etymology

From Latin 'cimex' (bug) and 'fugare' (to drive away).

2. NOUN

/ sɪˌmɪsɪˈfjuːɡə / (AmE), / sɪˌmɪsɪˈfjuːɡə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs in the buttercup family, native to north temperate regions, commonly known as bugbane.
genus of herbaceous plants (bugbane)

Examples

Native Americans used Cimicifuga roots for medicinal purposes.
Several garden varieties are derived from the Cimicifuga genus, valued for their tall flower spikes.
Researchers are studying the properties of Cimicifuga in treating menopausal symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bugbane - common name for plants of the genus Cimicifuga

Etymology

From Latin 'cimex' (bug) and 'fugare' (to drive away), referring to its use in repelling insects.
