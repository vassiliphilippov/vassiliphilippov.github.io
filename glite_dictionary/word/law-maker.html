law-maker

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔːˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈlɔːˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
a person who makes, amends, or repeals laws, especially as a member of a legislative body
person who creates or changes laws

Examples

The law-makers debated the new tax bill for hours before reaching a decision.
Many citizens voiced concerns to their local law-makers about the proposed environmental regulations.
State law-makers are working on a bipartisan plan to improve education funding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legislator - a person who makes laws, especially a member of a legislative body

Etymology

Formed from 'law' + 'maker', indicating someone who makes laws.
