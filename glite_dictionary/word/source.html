source

1. NOUN

/ sɔrs / (AmE), / sɔːs / (BrE)
a person who provides information, especially confidential or undisclosed information, to journalists or officials
person who gives information

Examples

The journalist refused to reveal her source despite growing pressure.
We always check the credibility of each source before publishing a story.
His identity as the anonymous source was later confirmed by the newspaper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: informant - a person who gives information, especially to the police or as a research participant

Etymology

From Old French 'sourse' (a rising, beginning), from Latin 'surgere' (to rise).
