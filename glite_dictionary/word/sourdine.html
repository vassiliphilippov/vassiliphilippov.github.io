sourdine

1. NOUN

/ suˈrdin / (AmE), / suəˈdiːn / (BrE)
A device used to muffle or soften the sound of a violin (a mute).
violin mute; sound-dampening device

Examples

The violinist placed a sourdine on her instrument to achieve a gentler tone.
Please bring your sourdine to rehearsal for the quiet passages.
You can reduce your practicing volume at night by using a sourdine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mute - a device that muffles or softens the sound of a musical instrument

Etymology

From French 'sourdine', meaning 'mute' or 'dampener.'

2. NOUN

/ suːrˈdin / (AmE), / suːəˈdiːn / (BrE)
an organ stop designed to produce a soft, muted sound
organ stop for soft, muted sound

Examples

The organist selected the sourdine to create a gentle, subdued tone for the hymn.
Using the sourdine, the performer achieved a delicate, almost ethereal sound on the pipe organ.
The composer called for the sourdine in several passages to evoke a sense of quiet reverence.

Etymology

From French 'sourdine' (mute, damper), from 'sourd' (deaf, muted).
