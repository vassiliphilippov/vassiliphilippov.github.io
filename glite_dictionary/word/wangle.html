wangle

1. NOUN

/ ˈwæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈwaŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
An instance of achieving something by clever or dishonest means.
act of gaining by trickery or scheming
informal

Examples

His promotion was a wangle that surprised many in the office.
They pulled off a clever wangle to get free tickets to the concert.
The whole deal was just another wangle by the crafty manager.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scheme - a secret or underhand plan; a plot
  • Synonym: swindle - an act of cheating or defrauding someone
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest or truthful; opposite of scheming

Etymology

Origin uncertain, possibly from English dialectal 'wankle' (unsteady, fickle), or influenced by 'waggle'.

2. VERB

/ ˈwæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈwaŋɡl / (BrE)
to achieve or obtain something, often by using clever, indirect, or dishonest methods
obtain by clever or devious means

Examples

He managed to wangle an invitation to the exclusive party.
She tried to wangle a day off work without losing pay.
They wangled their way into the concert, even though it was sold out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to finagle - to obtain or achieve by trickery or cleverness
  • Synonym: to manipulate - to control or influence in a clever or unscrupulous way
  • Antonym: honestly - in a truthful or sincere way

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from English dialect 'wankle' (unsteady, deceitful).

3. VERB

/ ˈwæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈwaŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
to achieve something or manipulate information or a situation, often by clever or dishonest means
manipulate or obtain by cunning

Examples

She tried to wangle a better grade by persuading the teacher to review her test.
He managed to wangle an invitation to the exclusive party through his connections.
The accountant was caught attempting to wangle the company’s financial reports.

Synset Examples

Fudge the figures
cook the books
falsify the data

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to falsify - to alter information or documents dishonestly
  • Synonym: to manipulate - to control or influence in a clever or unscrupulous way
  • Antonym: to straighten - to make or become honest or truthful, stop being crooked

Etymology

Origin unknown; first attested in early 20th-century Britain, possibly related to 'waggle'.
