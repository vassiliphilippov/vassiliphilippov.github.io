Datura suaveolens

1. NOUN

/ dəˈtʊrə swɑːveɪˈoʊlɛnz / (AmE), / dəˈtʃʊərə swɑːviːˈəʊlɛnz / (BrE)
A South American plant cultivated for its large, trumpet-shaped, nocturnally fragrant flowers, also known as angel's trumpet.
South American plant with fragrant trumpet flowers

Examples

The garden was filled with the intoxicating scent of Datura suaveolens in the evening.
Many horticulturists prize Datura suaveolens for its stunning, trumpet-shaped flowers.
Datura suaveolens blooms at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators with its fragrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angel's trumpet - common name for Datura suaveolens and related plants

Etymology

From Latin: 'datura', a genus of nightshade plants, and 'suaveolens', meaning 'sweet-smelling'.
