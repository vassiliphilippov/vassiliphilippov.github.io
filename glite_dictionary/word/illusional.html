illusional

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈluːʒənəl / (AmE), / ɪˈluːʒənəl / (BrE)
Marked by or producing illusion; having the nature of an illusion.
having the nature of an illusion

Examples

The magician's illusional tricks captivated the audience.
She lived in an illusional world, refusing to accept reality.
The illusional effects created a dreamlike atmosphere on stage.

Synset Examples

illusionary stage effects

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illusory - based on illusion; not real

Etymology

From illusion + -al, after Late Latin 'illusionalis' relating to illusion.
