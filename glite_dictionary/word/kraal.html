kraal

1. NOUN

/ krɑl / (AmE), / krɑːl / (BrE)
A traditional village of huts, typically enclosed by a fence or stockade, used especially by indigenous peoples of southern Africa.
enclosed African village of huts

Examples

The villagers gathered in the kraal to celebrate the festival.
Each family lived in a separate hut within the kraal.
Cattle were driven into the kraal at night for protection.

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'kraal', from Portuguese 'curral' (enclosure for livestock).

2. NOUN

/ krɑl / (AmE), / krɑːl / (BrE)
a traditional enclosure for livestock, especially cattle, in southern Africa, often made of thornbush or sometimes referring to a rural village or homestead
livestock pen in southern Africa

Examples

The cattle were safely herded into the kraal each night to protect them from predators.
Villagers gathered near the central kraal to discuss community matters.
The tourists admired the traditional design of the wooden kraal during their visit to the rural village.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'curral' (pen for cattle), likely adopted via Afrikaans from native Khoikhoi languages.
