Chrysolepis chrysophylla

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪsoʊˈlɛpɪs ˌkrɪsoʊˈfɪlə / (AmE), / ˌkrɪsəʊˈlɛpɪs ˌkrɪsəʊˈfɪlə / (BrE)
A small ornamental evergreen tree native to the Pacific Coast of North America, with glossy yellow-green leaves that are yellow underneath and edible nuts.
species of evergreen tree with edible nuts

Examples

The nuts of the Chrysolepis chrysophylla are edible and prized by wildlife.
Botanists studied the unique leaf structure of the Chrysolepis chrysophylla along the Pacific Coast.
Gardeners sometimes plant Chrysolepis chrysophylla as an ornamental tree for its attractive foliage.

Etymology

From Greek *chryso-* (gold) + *lepis* (scale), referencing the tree's golden, scaly foliage; *chrysophylla* from *chryso-* (gold) + *phylla* (leaf).
