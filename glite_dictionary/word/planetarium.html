planetarium

1. NOUN

/ ˌplænəˈtɛriəm / (AmE), / ˌplænɪˈteəriəm / (BrE)
an apparatus or model designed to show the positions and movements of planets and other celestial bodies in the solar system
apparatus for showing planetary movements

Examples

The school has a planetarium where students can watch simulated night skies.
The old planetarium displays were hand-operated by turning a crank.
A tabletop planetarium can help teach children about the solar system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orrery - a mechanical model of the solar system

Etymology

From Modern Latin, from Latin 'planeta' (planet) + '-arium' (place for).

2. NOUN

/ ˌplænəˈtɛriəm / (AmE), / ˌplænɪˈteəriəm / (BrE)
A building or room equipped with a domed ceiling on which images of stars, planets, and other celestial bodies are projected for educational and entertainment purposes.
building for projecting stars and planets

Examples

The science museum's planetarium offers daily shows about the night sky.
At the planetarium, children watched a simulation of a meteor shower.
Tickets to the planetarium include access to their interactive space exhibits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: observatory - a building for observing astronomical events

Etymology

From Modern Latin, from Latin 'planeta' (planet), patterned after 'sanctuarium' (sanctuary).

3. NOUN

/ plænəˈtɛriəm / (AmE), / plænɪˈteərɪəm / (BrE)
a device or building in which images of stars, planets, and other celestial objects are projected onto a curved ceiling for educational or entertainment purposes
device or venue for projecting images of the night sky

Examples

The school organized a trip to the planetarium to learn about the stars.
During the show, the planetarium projected constellations onto the dome above us.
He operates the planetarium equipment for public astronomy lectures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: observatory - a building equipped for observing celestial events

Etymology

From Modern Latin, from Latin 'planeta' (planet) + '-arium' (a place for).
