celioscopy

1. NOUN

/ siːliˈɑːskəpi / (AmE), / siːliˈɒskəpi / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which an endoscope is inserted through the abdominal wall to examine the abdominal cavity
endoscopic examination of the abdomen
medical

Examples

The surgeon performed a celioscopy to investigate the unexplained abdominal pain.
Advances in celioscopy have made diagnosis of intra-abdominal diseases less invasive.
After the celioscopy, the patient recovered quickly and reported minimal discomfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laparoscopy - minimally invasive examination/surgery of the abdomen using an endoscope

Etymology

From Greek 'koilia' (belly) + '-scopy' (looking, observing).
