to disinter

1. VERB

/ dɪs.ɪnˈtɝː / (AmE), / dɪs.ɪnˈtɜː / (BrE)
to dig up and remove a buried body or object, especially for medical or legal investigation
dig up a buried body or object

Examples

The forensic team had to disinter the remains to perform further tests.
The court ordered that the body be disinterred for a new autopsy.
Historians plan to disinter the time capsule buried under the old building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exhume - to dig out something buried, especially a body

Etymology

From Latin dis- (apart) + interrare (to bury), from in terra (in earth).

2. VERB

/ dɪsˈɪntɚ / (AmE), / dɪsˈɪntə / (BrE)
to dig up and remove a body or object from the place where it has been buried, especially for examination
to exhume or unearth something buried

Examples

The archaeologists plan to disinter the remains for further study.
Authorities needed a court order to disinter the coffin.
It can be controversial to disinter graves for scientific reasons.

Synset Examples

It’s rare to disinter a historical figure from their resting place.
The project aimed to disinter ancient artifacts from the site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exhume - to dig out (something buried, especially a corpse) from the ground
  • Antonym: to bury - to put a dead body or something else in the ground and cover it with earth

Etymology

From Latin 'dis-' (apart) and 'interrare' (to bury).
