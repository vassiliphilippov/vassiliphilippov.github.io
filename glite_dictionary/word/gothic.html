gothic

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡɑθɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡɒθɪk / (BrE)
(of writing, buildings, or art) characterized by an atmosphere of gloom, mystery, and the grotesque, especially as in certain novels or films.
gloomy and mysterious; grotesque style

Examples

Mary Shelley's 'Frankenstein' is a classic example of a gothic novel.
The old mansion had a gothic atmosphere, filled with shadows and strange noises.
His paintings are known for their gothic themes and unsettling imagery.

Synset Examples

gothic novels like ‘Frankenstein’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macabre - gruesome; having a grim or ghastly atmosphere
  • Antonym: romantic - relating to a literary and artistic movement emphasizing emotion and individualism; sometimes contrasted with gothic

Etymology

From Gothic, originally relating to the Goths, later referring to a style of architecture and literature.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡɑːθɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡɒθɪk / (BrE)
Having characteristics of the Middle Ages; old-fashioned and unenlightened in outlook.
medieval or old-fashioned in outlook

Examples

His gothic views on education are out of step with the 21st century.
Some people think the tradition is charming, but others find it rather gothic.
The company abandoned its gothic approach to customer service in favor of modern methods.

Synset Examples

a medieval attitude toward dating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medieval - of, relating to, or characteristic of the Middle Ages
  • Antonym: modern - characteristic of the present or recent times

Etymology

From Latin Gothicus, relating to the Goths, later associated with medieval styles and attitudes.
