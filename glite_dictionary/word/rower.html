rower

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.ər / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.ə / (BrE)
a person who rows a boat, especially as a sport or pastime
person who rows a boat

Examples

The rower kept a steady rhythm as she glided across the lake.
Each rower in the crew must be perfectly synchronized for maximum speed.
He trained for months to become a competitive rower at the national level.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: coxswain - the person who steers and directs a rowing boat
  • Synonym: paddler - a person who propels and steers a canoe or kayak with a paddle

Etymology

From the verb 'row' + -er suffix denoting someone who performs an action.
