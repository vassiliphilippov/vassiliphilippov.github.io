peplus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛpləs / (AmE), / ˈpiːpləs / (BrE)
a garment worn by women in ancient Greece, consisting of cloth fastened at the shoulders and draped in folds to the waist
ancient Greek women's garment

Examples

The museum displayed a beautifully preserved peplus from ancient Greece.
She wrote her thesis on the design and symbolism of the peplus in classical antiquity.
The statue was draped in a peplus, reflecting the fashion of the era.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chiton - a type of tunic worn by women and men in ancient Greece

Etymology

From Latin *peplum*, from Greek *peplos* (robe).
