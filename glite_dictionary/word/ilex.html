Ilex

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪˌlɛks / (AmE), / ˈaɪlɛks / (BrE)
A genus of trees and shrubs in the family Aquifoliaceae, commonly known as hollies, which typically have small flowers and red or black berries.
genus of holly trees and shrubs

Examples

The genus Ilex includes hundreds of species, many of which are cultivated for their ornamental value.
Several types of Ilex are native to temperate and tropical regions around the world.
The spiny leaves and bright berries of Ilex make it a popular choice for holiday decorations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holly - a tree or shrub of the genus Ilex, with shiny leaves and red berries

Etymology

From Latin *ilex*, meaning holm oak or holly.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪlɛks / (AmE), / ˈaɪlɛks / (BrE)
A large genus of evergreen and deciduous trees and shrubs in the family Aquifoliaceae, including the holly.
genus of trees and shrubs, including holly

Examples

The Ilex genus includes both deciduous and evergreen holly species.
Botanists are studying several new species within the Ilex group to understand their medicinal properties.
Leaves of certain Ilex varieties are sometimes used in traditional herbal teas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holly - evergreen shrub or tree with spiny leaves and red berries

Etymology

From Latin *ilex*, meaning holm-oak or holly.
