Rhamnus californicus

1. NOUN

/ ˈræmnəs ˌkælɪˈfɔrnɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈræmnəs ˌkælɪˈfɔːnɪkəs / (BrE)
An evergreen shrub native to the western United States, bearing small red or black fruits; also known as California coffeeberry.
evergreen shrub of western US

Examples

The hillsides were covered with Rhamnus californicus, their glossy leaves catching the afternoon sun.
Wildlife in the preserve often feed on Rhamnus californicus berries during the fall.
Gardeners plant Rhamnus californicus to attract birds and pollinators to their yards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coffeeberry - another common name for Rhamnus californicus

Etymology

From scientific Latin: genus name 'Rhamnus' (from Greek *rhamnos*, 'buckthorn') and species name reflecting California.
