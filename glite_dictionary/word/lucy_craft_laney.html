Lucy Craft Laney

1. PROPN

/ ˈluːsi kræft ˈleɪni / (AmE), / ˈluːsi krɑːft ˈleɪni / (BrE)
(1854–1933) An American educator who founded the first private school for Black students in Augusta, Georgia, and was a pioneering advocate for African-American education.
American educator and school founder

Examples

Lucy Craft Laney was instrumental in advancing education for African-Americans in the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries.
The legacy of Lucy Craft Laney continues to inspire educators striving for equality.
Historians often highlight Lucy Craft Laney as a trailblazer in the field of education.
