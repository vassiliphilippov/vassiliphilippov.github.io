guaiac

1. NOUN

/ ˈgwaɪ.æk / (AmE), / ˈɡwaɪ.ak / (BrE)
the hard, greenish-brown wood obtained from the lignum vitae tree or trees of the genus Guaiacum, valued for its density and medicinal resin
hard wood from the lignum vitae tree

Examples

The craftsman shaped the guaiac into a durable handle for the tool.
Historically, guaiac has been used for its medicinal resins as well as its strong wood.
The density of guaiac makes it ideal for applications that require tough, long-lasting material.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lignum vitae - the wood of the Guaiacum tree, valued for hardness and medicinal use

Etymology

From French 'guaiac,' from Spanish 'guayaco,' from Taíno 'guayacán.'
