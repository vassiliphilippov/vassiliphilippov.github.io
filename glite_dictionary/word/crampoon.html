crampoon

1. NOUN

/ kræmˈpun / (AmE), / kræmˈpuːn / (BrE)
a hinged pair of curved iron bars used for lifting or raising heavy objects
device for lifting heavy objects

Examples

The workers used a crampoon to lift the beam into place safely.
A crampoon is essential for handling large stone blocks at the quarry.
The iron crampoon gripped the object firmly as it was hoisted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cramp - a device for fastening objects together

2. NOUN

/ kræmˈpuːn / (AmE), / kræmˈpuːn / (BrE)
an iron spike attached to a shoe for preventing slipping on ice or during climbing
iron shoe spike for walking on ice

Examples

Before crossing the glacier, he strapped a crampoon onto each boot.
The mountaineer checked that every crampoon was securely fastened before the ascent.
Crampoons are essential equipment for traversing icy slopes safely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crampon - iron plate with spikes for walking on ice or climbing

Etymology

Variant of 'crampon', from French 'crampon' (iron hook or spike).
