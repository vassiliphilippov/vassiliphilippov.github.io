psychosurgery

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkoʊˈsɜrdʒəri / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊˈsɜːdʒəri / (BrE)
Surgery performed on the brain to treat severe mental or behavioral disorders that have not responded to other treatments.
brain surgery for mental disorders
medical

Examples

Recent advances in psychosurgery have focused on making procedures safer for patients with severe depression.
The history of psychosurgery includes controversial practices such as the prefrontal lobotomy.
Some doctors consider psychosurgery only as a last resort when all other therapies fail.

Etymology

From psycho- (mind) + surgery; first used in the 20th century.
