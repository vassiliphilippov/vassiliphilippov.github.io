knightly

1. ADJ

/ ˈnaɪt.li / (AmE), / ˈnaɪt.li / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of knights or the age of chivalry, especially in the Middle Ages.
of or like knights or chivalry

Examples

He wore a knightly suit of armor to the medieval festival.
The tale recalls the knightly virtues of honor and bravery.
She decorated the hall with banners to create a knightly atmosphere for the celebration.

Synset Examples

chivalric rites
the knightly years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chivalric - relating to chivalry or knights

Etymology

From Middle English *knightli*, from *knight* + *-ly* (like, characteristic of).

2. ADJ

/ ˈnaɪtli / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtli / (BrE)
(of a man or his behavior) displaying chivalrous or courteous attention towards women, as an ideal knight would.
chivalrous or courteous towards women

Examples

He acted in a truly knightly manner, holding the door open for her.
His knightly gestures made everyone around him smile with admiration.
In medieval tales, heroes are often praised for their knightly devotion to ladies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chivalrous - showing polite and honorable behavior, especially toward women

Etymology

From Middle English knyghtly, from knyght (knight) + -ly.

Synset POS: s
