conundrum

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnʌndrəm / (AmE), / kəˈnʌndrəm / (BrE)
a confusing or difficult problem or question
a difficult problem or puzzle

Examples

The committee faces a conundrum over how to reduce costs without cutting jobs.
Deciding what to do next was a real conundrum for the team.
The film explores the conundrum of modern life and its challenges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puzzle - something that is difficult to solve or understand
  • Synonym: enigma - something mysterious or difficult to understand
  • Antonym: solution - an answer to a problem or puzzle

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from Latin conundrum (joke, pun) or an alteration of earlier quonundrum (a mock-Latin term).
