Hell

1. NOUN

/ hɛl / (AmE), / hɛl / (BrE)
(in Christianity) the place where Satan and evil spirits dwell, and where sinners are believed to suffer eternal punishment after death.
(Christianity) place of eternal punishment for sinners

Examples

According to the story, those who do evil may be condemned to Hell for eternity.
The preacher spoke of Hell as a place of fire and torment.
Many artists have depicted Hell as a realm of suffering and chaos.

Synset Examples

Hurl'd headlong … To bottomless perdition, there to dwell
a demon from the depths of the pit
Hell is paved with good intentions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heaven - the dwelling place of God and the blessed after death in various religions

Etymology

From Old English 'hell' (nether world, place of torment for the wicked), related to Old Norse 'hel', Old High German 'hella'.
