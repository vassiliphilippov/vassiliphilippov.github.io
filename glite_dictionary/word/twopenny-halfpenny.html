twopenny-halfpenny

1. ADJ

/ ˌtuːˌpɛni ˈhælpəni / (AmE), / ˌtʊpəniˈhælpəni / (BrE)
Of little or trifling value; insignificant or petty.
of little value; insignificant
british

Examples

Many considered the proposal a twopenny-halfpenny solution to a serious problem.
The critics dismissed the film as a twopenny-halfpenny attempt at art.
He refused to waste his time on such twopenny-halfpenny matters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trivial - of little importance or value
  • Synonym: insignificant - too small or unimportant to be worth consideration
  • Antonym: important - of great significance or value

Etymology

From the British coins 'twopenny' (two pence) and 'halfpenny' (half a penny), indicating something of very small monetary value.
