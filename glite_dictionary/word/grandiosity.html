grandiosity

1. NOUN

/ ɡrændiˈɑsəti / (AmE), / ɡræn.diˈɒs.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
A style of expression that is elaborate, extravagant, or excessively ornate, often to the point of pretension or pomposity.
extravagant or pretentious style

Examples

The grandiosity of his prose made the novel difficult to read.
Her speech was filled with unnecessary grandiosity and flowery language.
Critics accused the author of favoring grandiosity over substance in his writing.

Synset Examples

the grandiosity of his prose
an excessive ornateness of language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pomposity - the quality of being pompous; self-importance, especially in speech or writing

Etymology

From French 'grandiosité', from Italian 'grandiosità', from 'grandioso' (grand), ultimately from Latin 'grandis' (large, great).
