micron

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪkrɑn / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrɒn / (BrE)
A metric unit of length equal to one millionth of a meter; also called a micrometer.
one millionth of a meter

Examples

The width of a human hair is typically about 50 microns.
Air filters can remove particles as small as one micron in diameter.
Pollen grains are often measured in microns under a microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: micrometer - A metric unit of length equal to one millionth of a meter

Etymology

From Greek 'mikrón' (small thing), diminutive of 'mikros' (small).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪ.krɑn / (AmE), / ˈmiː.krɒn / (BrE)
a unit of length equal to one millionth of a meter, used to measure very small dimensions
unit of length, one millionth of a meter

Examples

The thickness of the coating was measured in microns to ensure precision.
Particles smaller than one micron are invisible to the naked eye.
Modern filters can remove contaminants as small as a single micron.

Synset Examples

They measured the thickness in microns for accuracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: micrometer - a device for measuring small distances or an alternative name for micron

Etymology

From Greek 'mikron', neuter of 'mikros' (small).
