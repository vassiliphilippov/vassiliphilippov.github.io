standpoint

1. NOUN

/ ˈstændˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈstæn(d)ˌpɔɪnt / (BrE)
an attitude or point of view from which something is considered or evaluated
a particular point of view or perspective

Examples

From an economic standpoint, the decision makes a lot of sense.
She considered the proposal from every possible standpoint before making her choice.
The critics examined the film from a feminist standpoint.

Synset Examples

we should consider this problem from the viewpoint of the Russians
teaching history gave him a special point of view toward current events

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viewpoint - a particular way of considering or judging a situation
  • Synonym: perspective - a particular attitude toward or way of regarding something
  • Synonym: angle - a particular way of approaching or considering an issue or problem

Etymology

From German 'Standpunkt' (standpoint), from 'Stand' (standing, stance) + 'Punkt' (point).
