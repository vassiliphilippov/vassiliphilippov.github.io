carnotite

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˈnoʊtaɪt / (AmE), / kɑːˈnəʊtaɪt / (BrE)
a yellow radioactive mineral that is an ore of uranium, radium, and vanadium
yellow radioactive mineral; uranium ore

Examples

The geologist identified the yellow crystals as carnotite, a valuable uranium ore.
Large deposits of carnotite have been mined in the western United States.
Because it is radioactive, carnotite must be handled with care during extraction and processing.

Etymology

Named after Marie Adolphe Carnot, French mining engineer and chemist.
