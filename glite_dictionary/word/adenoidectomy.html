adenoidectomy

1. NOUN

/ ˌæd.ə.nɔɪˈdɛk.tə.mi / (AmE), / ˌæd.ɪ.nɔɪˈdɛk.tə.mi / (BrE)
a surgical operation to remove the adenoids, usually performed to treat chronic infections or breathing problems
surgical removal of the adenoids
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended an adenoidectomy to help her breathe more easily at night.
Children who suffer from frequent ear infections may need an adenoidectomy.
Recovery from an adenoidectomy is usually quick, with most patients returning to normal activities within a week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tonsillectomy - surgical removal of the tonsils

Etymology

From adenoid + -ectomy, from Greek ektomē (excision).
