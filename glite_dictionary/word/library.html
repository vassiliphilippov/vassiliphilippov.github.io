library

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪˌbrɛri / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ.brəri / (BrE)
a building or room containing a collection of books and other materials available for people to read, borrow, or study
building or room for books and study

Examples

She spends every Saturday studying at the library near her house.
The new city library has a large selection of children's books and multimedia.
Many universities have a digital library accessible to students and faculty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archive - a collection of historical documents or records
  • Synonym: collection - a group of objects or works gathered together

Etymology

From Latin 'librarium' (bookcase), from 'liber' (book).
