cardiomyopathy

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːrdioʊmaɪˈɑːpəθi / (AmE), / ˌkɑːdiəʊmaɪˈɒpəθi / (BrE)
A disease of the heart muscle that reduces its ability to pump blood effectively, often of unknown cause.
heart muscle disease
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that his fatigue might be caused by cardiomyopathy.
Her cardiomyopathy was managed with medication and regular checkups.
Researchers are studying the genetic factors that can lead to cardiomyopathy.

Etymology

From Greek kardia (heart) + mys, myos (muscle) + -pathy (disease).
