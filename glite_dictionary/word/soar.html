to soar

1. VERB

/ sɔr / (AmE), / sɔː / (BrE)
to fly or glide high and freely in the air using a hang glider
to fly using a hang glider

Examples

On weekends, many adventure seekers travel to the mountains to soar above the valleys in their hang gliders.
He learned how to soar over the cliffs safely after taking a hang gliding course.
The group spent the afternoon soaring high over the beach, riding the wind currents in their gliders.

2. VERB

/ sɔr / (AmE), / sɔː / (BrE)
to rise rapidly in amount, value, or level
rise rapidly in value or level

Examples

House prices continue to soar in the city, making it harder for young people to buy.
The temperature is expected to soar to 40 degrees Celsius tomorrow afternoon.
As demand increased, the company's profits soared last quarter.

Synset Examples

the dollar soared against the yen

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plummet - to fall or drop straight down at high speed

Etymology

From Old French 'essaurer', from Vulgar Latin '*exaurare' (to rise high).

3. VERB

/ sɔr / (AmE), / sɔː / (BrE)
to rise or increase quickly to a high level; to move upward, especially with great energy or height
rise quickly; move upward

Examples

The eagle began to soar above the mountains as the sun rose.
After the announcement, housing prices started to soar in the city.
Her spirits soared when she received the good news.

Synset Examples

The stock market soared after the cease-fire was announced

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plunge - to fall or move downward suddenly
  • Synonym: rise - to move from a lower position to a higher one

Etymology

From Latin 'exaurare' (to rise high), via Old French 'essorer'.

4. VERB

/ sɔr / (AmE), / sɔː / (BrE)
to fly or rise high in the air, often with little apparent effort
fly or rise high in the air

Examples

The eagle began to soar above the mountains, gliding effortlessly on the wind.
During the festival, colorful kites soared high into the blue sky.
Watching the paper airplane soar across the room made the children cheer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ascend - to go up or climb; rise
  • Antonym: plummet - to fall suddenly and steeply

Etymology

From Old French *esser*, from Latin *exsurgere* (to rise up).

Source: WordNet

5. VERB

/ sɔr / (AmE), / sɔː / (BrE)
to fly or glide in a plane without an engine, relying on air currents
fly a plane without an engine

Examples

Pilots must learn how to soar skillfully to stay airborne in a glider.
The vintage plane began to soar after its tow rope was released.
She loves to soar silently over the countryside in her sailplane.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glide - to fly without engine power, using air currents
