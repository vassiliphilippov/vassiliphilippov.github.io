Lilium philadelphicum

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪliəm fɪl.əˌdɛl.fɪ.kəm / (AmE), / ˈlɪliəm fɪ.ləˌdɛl.fɪ.kəm / (BrE)
A species of lily native to eastern North America, with orange or orange-red flowers spotted with purple.
North American lily with orange-red spotted flowers

Examples

The Lilium philadelphicum blooms in midsummer, adding a splash of color to meadows and forests.
Gardeners appreciate Lilium philadelphicum for its resilience and striking orange-red petals.
We spotted several Lilium philadelphicum while hiking in the Appalachian Mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lily - A plant with large, prominent flowers of the genus Lilium

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Lilium' (lily) and species name 'philadelphicum', referring to Philadelphia.
