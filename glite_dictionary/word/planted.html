planted

1. ADJ

/ ˈplæn.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈplɑːn.tɪd / (BrE)
(of ideas, feelings, etc.) deeply rooted or firmly fixed so that they are difficult to change or remove.
deeply rooted or firmly fixed

Examples

His distrust of outsiders is a planted conviction that goes back to his childhood.
There is a planted sense of duty in her that influences all her decisions.
Such planted ideas are hard to change, even with new evidence.

Synset Examples

deep-rooted prejudice
deep-seated differences of opinion
implanted convictions
ingrained habits of a lifetime
a deeply planted need

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ingrained - (of habits, beliefs, etc.) deeply embedded and difficult to change
  • Synonym: deep-seated - (of feelings, beliefs, etc.) firmly established and difficult to change

2. ADJ

/ ˈplæn.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈplɑːn.tɪd / (BrE)
(of a plant, bulb, etc.) placed in the soil in order to grow.
put in soil to grow

Examples

The freshly planted trees need to be watered every day.
Everyone admired the neatly planted flowerbeds along the path.
She marked each planted row so she would remember what she had put where.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sown - (of seeds) scattered on the soil for growth
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01837191-a
Synset POS: a
