FM

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛf ˈɛm / (AmE), / ˌɛf ˈɛm / (BrE)
a method of transmitting radio signals by varying the frequency of the carrier wave; also refers to a radio system using this method
radio transmission by frequency modulation

Examples

The radio station broadcasts in FM rather than AM for better sound quality.
Many cars come equipped with both AM and FM radios.
He tuned the stereo to his favorite FM music station.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: AM - transmission by amplitude modulation

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'frequency modulation.'
