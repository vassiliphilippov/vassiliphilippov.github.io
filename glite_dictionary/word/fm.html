Fm

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɜrmiəm / (AmE), / ˈfɜːmiəm / (BrE)
a radioactive transuranic metallic element with atomic number 100, produced artificially in nuclear reactors
radioactive metallic element, atomic number 100

Examples

Scientists first discovered Fm in the debris of a hydrogen bomb test.
The chemical properties of Fm are difficult to study due to its rarity and radioactivity.
No practical uses for Fm have been found outside of scientific research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fermium - a radioactive metallic element with atomic number 100

Etymology

Named after physicist Enrico Fermi.
