alcohol

1. NOUN

/ ˈælkəˌhɔl / (AmE), / ˈæl.kə.hɒl / (BrE)
A drink containing ethanol, especially one consumed as a beverage, such as beer, wine, or spirits.
drink containing ethanol (e.g. beer, wine, spirits)

Examples

He decided to stop drinking alcohol for the sake of his health.
There are many laws regulating the sale of alcohol to minors.
Craft beer has become one of the most popular forms of alcohol in recent years.

Synset Examples

alcohol (or drink) ruined him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liquor - strong alcoholic drink
  • Synonym: spirit - distilled alcoholic beverage
  • Antonym: nonalcoholic - not containing alcohol

Etymology

From Arabic al-kuḥl, originally referring to powdered antimony and later extended to distilled substances.

2. NOUN

/ ˈælkəˌhɔl / (AmE), / ˈælkəhɒl / (BrE)
any of a group of organic compounds containing a hydroxyl group attached to a carbon atom, commonly produced by distilling hydrocarbons, including ethanol and methanol
organic compound with a hydroxyl group

Examples

Methanol is a simple alcohol often used as a solvent.
Many alcohols have industrial applications beyond beverages.
Isopropyl alcohol is commonly used as a disinfectant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethanol - a colorless volatile flammable liquid, the type of alcohol in alcoholic drinks
  • Degree Lower: methanol - a toxic type of alcohol used as an industrial solvent

Etymology

From Arabic 'al-kuḥl' (the kohl, powdered antimony), later used for distilled substances.
