plaza

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɑːzə / (AmE), / ˈplɑːzə / (BrE)
A shopping center consisting of a group of stores and restaurants, often landscaped and with parking, serving as a modern version of a marketplace.
shopping center or mall

Examples

The city built a new plaza with dozens of shops and food outlets near the highway.
On weekends, families like to stroll around the plaza and enjoy the outdoor cafes.
The plaza offers plenty of parking and a wide range of boutiques and restaurants.

Synset Examples

a good plaza should have a movie house
they spent their weekends at the local malls

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mall - large building or group of buildings with many stores
  • Synonym: marketplace - a place where goods are bought and sold

Etymology

From Spanish 'plaza', meaning 'public square'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈplɑː.zə / (AmE), / ˈplɑː.zə / (BrE)
A large open public area in a city or town, often used for gatherings, pedestrian activity, or community events.
open public square or area

Examples

Many tourists gathered in the plaza to watch the street performers.
The new shopping center was built around an open-air plaza with benches and fountains.
The mayor held a rally at the central plaza to address the community.

Synset Examples

they met at Elm Plaza
Grosvenor Place

Related Terms

  • Synonym: square - an open area in a town or city, often used for public gatherings

Etymology

From Spanish 'plaza' (open place, square), from Latin 'platea' (broad street).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
