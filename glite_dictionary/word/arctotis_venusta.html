Arctotis venusta

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrkˈtoʊtɪs vəˈnʌstə / (AmE), / ɑːkˈtəʊtɪs vɪˈnʌstə / (BrE)
A bushy perennial plant native to South Africa, known for its white or violet daisy-like flowers, often covering valley sides with color.
South African flowering perennial plant

Examples

The hills were blanketed in vibrant color as Arctotis venusta bloomed in early spring.
Gardeners often plant Arctotis venusta for its striking white and violet flowers.
On our trip to South Africa, we saw entire fields covered with Arctotis venusta.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: African daisy - general name for various ornamental daisies from Africa, including Arctotis species

Etymology

From Latinized form of Greek 'arktos' (bear) and 'otis' (ear), referring to the genus, and Latin 'venusta' meaning 'beautiful, charming'.
