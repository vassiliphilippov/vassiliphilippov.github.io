to burglarise

1. VERB

/ ˈbɜːrɡlərˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈbɜːɡləraɪz / (BrE)
to break into a building, especially a house, and steal things
break into a building to steal
british

Examples

The police reported that three houses were burglarised in the same neighborhood last night.
After they burglarised the flat, the thieves quickly fled the scene.
He was caught on camera attempting to burglarise the jewelry store.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to burglarize - commit a burglary; American spelling
  • Synonym: to burgle - to commit burglary; informal variant

Etymology

From 'burglar' + '-ise', a British variant of American 'burglarize'.
