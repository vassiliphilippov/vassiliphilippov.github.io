Rupicola

1. NOUN

/ ruːˈpɪkələ / (AmE), / ruːˈpɪkələ / (BrE)
A genus of brightly colored South American birds in the cotinga family, commonly known as cocks-of-the-rock.
genus of South American cocks-of-the-rock

Examples

The male Rupicola displays its vivid orange plumage to attract females during courtship.
Researchers traveled to the rainforest to observe the nesting habits of Rupicola.
The striking appearance of Rupicola species makes them popular among birdwatchers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cock-of-the-rock - a brightly colored bird of the genus Rupicola, noted for its prominent fan-shaped crest

Etymology

From Latin 'rupes' (rock) + '-cola' (inhabitant), meaning 'rock dweller'.

2. NOUN

/ ruːˈpɪkələ / (AmE), / ruːˈpɪkələ / (BrE)
A genus of brightly colored South American birds known as cock-of-the-rock, found in rocky forested areas.
a genus of birds (cock-of-the-rock)

Examples

The most famous representative of the genus is the Andean Rupicola, known for its vivid orange plumage.
Many birdwatchers travel to South America to observe the unique mating displays of Rupicola.
Deforestation poses a threat to the rocky forest habitat preferred by members of the Rupicola genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cock-of-the-rock - a brightly colored bird species found in South America, member of genus Rupicola

Etymology

From Latin 'rupes' (rock) and '-cola' (dweller), meaning rock-dweller.
