Caranx crysos

1. NOUN

/ kəˈræŋks ˈkraɪsɒs / (AmE), / kəˈræŋks ˈkrɪsɒs / (BrE)
a species of fish found in the western Atlantic Ocean, from Cape Cod to Brazil, commonly known as the blue runner
a western Atlantic fish species (blue runner)

Examples

The Caranx crysos is prized by recreational anglers for its fighting spirit.
Researchers studied the migratory patterns of Caranx crysos along the Brazilian coast.
Juvenile Caranx crysos often gather around reefs and shipwrecks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue runner - common English name for Caranx crysos

Etymology

From Latin genus Caranx (a type of fish) and species crysos, from Greek 'chrysos' (gold), possibly referring to its coloration.
