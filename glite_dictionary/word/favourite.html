favourite

1. VERB

/ ˈfeɪvərɪt / (AmE), / ˈfeɪvərɪt / (BrE)
To indicate that something is one's preferred or liked item on a website or social media platform, typically by clicking a designated icon.
mark as preferred on social media
informal

Examples

I always favourite the posts that inspire me on this site.
She favourited the recipe so she could find it easily later.
Many users favourite tweets to bookmark them for future reference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: like - to indicate approval or enjoyment of online content
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-90000601-v
Synset POS: v

2. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪvərɪt / (AmE), / ˈfeɪvərɪt / (BrE)
Preferred above all others, especially receiving special treatment.
preferred above all others
british

Examples

She is clearly the favourite student in the class, always getting extra attention from the teacher.
Among all the desserts, chocolate cake is my favourite because my grandmother always makes it for me.
He was the manager's favourite player, often chosen for the most important matches.

Synset Examples

the favored child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: favorite - preferred above all others; U.S. spelling variant
  • Antonym: unfavoured - not receiving special preference or treatment

Etymology

From Middle English, via Old French 'favorit', from Latin 'favorem' (favor).

3. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪvərɪt / (AmE), / ˈfeɪvərɪt / (BrE)
the competitor or team considered most likely to win a competition or event
competitor most likely to win
british

Examples

After his impressive record this season, he is the clear favourite to win the championship.
Although the favourite lost in the final round, she was gracious in defeat.
Many people were surprised when the favourite was eliminated in the first heat.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underdog - a competitor thought to have little chance of winning

Etymology

From Middle English, via Old French 'favorit', from Italian 'favorito', past participle of 'favorire' (to favor).
