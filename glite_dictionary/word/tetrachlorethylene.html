tetrachlorethylene

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛtrəklɔːrˈɛθɪliːn / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəklɔːˈrɛθɪliːn / (BrE)
A chlorinated hydrocarbon commonly used as a solvent and an anthelmintic agent against hookworm and other nematodes.
chlorinated solvent, anthelmintic agent
medical

Examples

Tetrachlorethylene was once used as a treatment for hookworm infections in humans.
The laboratory used tetrachlorethylene as a cleaning solvent for precision metal parts.
Due to environmental concerns, the use of tetrachlorethylene is now regulated in many countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perchloroethylene - another name for tetrachlorethylene, especially as a solvent

Etymology

From tetra- (four) + chloro- (chlorine) + ethylene (a hydrocarbon).
