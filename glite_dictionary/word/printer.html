printer

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪntər / (AmE), / ˈprɪntə / (BrE)
A machine that produces text or images on paper, especially as an output device connected to a computer.
device that prints documents

Examples

The office printer broke down just as I was about to print an important report.
Most students prefer a wireless printer for printing assignments from their laptops.
I need to replace the ink cartridge in my printer before I can print more documents.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: scanner - a device for converting printed documents or images into digital form
Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɪntɚ / (AmE), / ˈprɪntə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to operate a printing press or produce printed material
person who operates a printing press

Examples

Her grandfather worked as a printer in a local newspaper for over thirty years.
During the Renaissance, the role of the printer was crucial in spreading new ideas.
The printer carefully inspected the type before starting the next batch of books.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pressman - someone who operates a printing press
  • Degree Higher: publisher - person or company that prepares and issues printed material

Etymology

From Middle English printer, from Anglo-Norman and Old French preinte, from Latin premere (to press).

3. NOUN

/ ˈprɪntər / (AmE), / ˈprɪntə / (BrE)
a machine that produces text or images on paper, usually from a computer
machine that prints documents or images

Examples

The office printer ran out of ink in the middle of the report.
She bought a new color printer for her home workspace.
Please connect your laptop to the network printer to print your documents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photocopier - a machine that makes copies of documents or images
