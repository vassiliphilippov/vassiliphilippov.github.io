noncontroversial

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑnkəntrəˈvɜrʃəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkɒntrəˈvɜːʃəl / (BrE)
Not likely to cause disagreement or public debate; unlikely to arouse controversy.
not likely to cause controversy

Examples

The politician gave a noncontroversial speech to avoid upsetting any voters.
They chose a noncontroversial topic for the company newsletter.
The policy was so noncontroversial that it passed unanimously.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: controversial - causing disagreement or public debate

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' (not) and 'controversial' (from Latin controversia, 'dispute').
