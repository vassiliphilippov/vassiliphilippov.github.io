sympathectomy

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪmpəˈθɛktəmi / (AmE), / ˌsɪmpəˈθɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which a nerve pathway in the sympathetic nervous system is interrupted in order to treat certain medical conditions
surgical interruption of sympathetic nerves
medical

Examples

The surgeon recommended a sympathectomy to help manage the patient's severe sweating disorder.
A sympathectomy can relieve certain circulatory problems by disrupting nerve signals.
After undergoing a sympathectomy, she noticed a significant reduction in her symptoms.

Etymology

From Greek 'sympatheia' (sympathy) + 'ektomē' (excision, removal).
