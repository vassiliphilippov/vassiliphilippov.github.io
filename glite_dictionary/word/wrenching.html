wrenching

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛn(t)ʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɛn(t)ʃɪŋ / (BrE)
causing great emotional or physical pain or distress
causing severe pain or distress

Examples

The movie told a wrenching story of loss and survival after the disaster.
She felt a wrenching loneliness when her friends moved away.
He was overcome by a wrenching guilt after hearing the news.

Synset Examples

a wrenching pain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agonizing - causing intense pain or suffering
  • Synonym: painful - causing pain; hurting
  • Antonym: soothing - having a calming or relieving effect

Etymology

From the verb 'wrench' (Middle English 'wrenchen', from Old English 'wrencan'), meaning to twist or pull forcibly.
