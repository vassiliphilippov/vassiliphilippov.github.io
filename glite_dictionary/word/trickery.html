trickery

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪk.ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈtrɪk.əri / (BrE)
the use of tricks or deceit to cheat or mislead someone, especially for personal gain
the use of tricks to deceive

Examples

The magician's show was based on clever trickery and sleight of hand.
He was a victim of financial trickery and lost all his savings.
Politicians accused each other of trickery during the heated debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deception - the act of deceiving someone
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest, not lying or cheating

Etymology

From trick + -ery, patterned after words like 'robbery' and 'bribery'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪkəri / (AmE), / ˈtrɪkəri / (BrE)
The use of dishonest methods or clever deceit to achieve an advantage or to deceive someone.
the use of deceit or cheating

Examples

The company used trickery to convince customers to buy unnecessary products.
Political campaigns are often accused of trickery and manipulation.
He warned me about their trickery before I agreed to the deal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceit - the act of deceiving or misleading
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest; truthfulness

Etymology

From 'trick' + '-ery', modeled after similar forms in French such as 'tricherie' (cheating).
