IQ

1. NOUN

/ aɪ ˈkju / (AmE), / aɪ ˈkjuː / (BrE)
a measure of a person’s intelligence, expressed as a numerical score based on standard tests, typically compared to the average for their age group
intelligence quotient (test score)

Examples

He has a remarkably high IQ for his age.
Some jobs require applicants to take an IQ test as part of the hiring process.
Her IQ scores improved significantly after months of cognitive training.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intelligence quotient - full term for IQ; a score derived from intelligence tests

Etymology

Abbreviation of intelligence quotient.
