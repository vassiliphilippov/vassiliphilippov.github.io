Whittier

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪtiɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɪtɪə / (BrE)
John Greenleaf Whittier (1807–1892), American poet noted for his nostalgic poems about New England.
American poet (1807–1892)

Examples

Students studied the poetry of Whittier when learning about nineteenth-century American literature.
Whittier's verses about rural New England life remain popular among lovers of classic poetry.
Many towns in the United States have been named after Whittier, in honor of his literary contributions.
