navicular

1. ADJ

/ nəˈvɪkjələr / (AmE), / nəˈvɪkjʊlə / (BrE)
shaped like a boat; having a boat-like form, especially used of certain anatomical structures.
boat-shaped; often anatomical
medical

Examples

The navicular bone in the foot is named for its boat-like shape.
Doctors noted the navicular configuration of the bone in the X-ray.
Certain fossils display a distinctly navicular outline.

Etymology

From Latin 'navicula' (small ship, diminutive of 'navis', ship).

2. NOUN

/ nəˈvɪkjəlɚ / (AmE), / nəˈvɪkjʊlə / (BrE)
the largest bone in the wrist on the thumb side, also known as the scaphoid bone
largest wrist bone near the thumb
medical

Examples

The doctor identified a fracture in the navicular after reviewing the X-rays.
Pain in the navicular region can indicate a wrist injury.
The navicular bone plays a key role in wrist movement and stability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scaphoid - the boat-shaped bone on the thumb side of the wrist

Etymology

From Latin 'naviculus' meaning 'little ship', due to its boat-like shape.
