posseman

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːsiˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈpɒsiˌman / (BrE)
a man who serves as a member of a posse, especially one formed to enforce the law or capture a criminal
member of a law-enforcing posse

Examples

The sheriff deputized several possemen to help track down the fugitives.
Each posseman was given a badge and a rifle before the manhunt began.
As a trusted posseman, he quickly responded to calls for assistance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: posse - a group of people assembled to assist law enforcement, especially to apprehend a criminal

Etymology

Formed from 'posse' (from Medieval Latin 'posse comitatus', the power of the county) and 'man'.
