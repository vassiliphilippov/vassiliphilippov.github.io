fixture

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪkstʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪkstʃə / (BrE)
a person who is regularly seen in a particular place; a regular patron or habitual presence
a regular presence in a place

Examples

After years of going to the same coffee shop, she became a fixture there.
The old man is a fixture at the corner bar, always sitting on his favorite stool.
Everyone in the neighborhood knows him—he's a fixture at the local basketball court.

Synset Examples

an habitue of the racetrack
a bum who is a Central Park fixture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: habitue - a regular patron of a place
  • Synonym: regular - a person who frequents a place often

Etymology

From fix + -ture, patterned on its sense of something fixed or established.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪkstʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪkstʃə / (BrE)
an object or piece of equipment that is permanently attached to a house or building and is not intended to be removed
permanently attached object/fitting

Examples

The bathroom fixtures were all modern and stylish.
When you move out, please do not remove any fixtures from the apartment.
Light fixtures should be replaced if they no longer work properly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fitting - a small part or item fixed to something, especially in a home

Etymology

From Latin 'fixus', past participle of 'figere' (to fix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03359459-n
Synset POS: n
