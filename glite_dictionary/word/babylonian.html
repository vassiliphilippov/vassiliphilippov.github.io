Babylonian

1. ADJ

/ ˌbæbəˈloʊniən / (AmE), / ˌbæbəˈləʊniən / (BrE)
of or relating to the ancient city of Babylon, its people, or its culture.
relating to Babylon or its people/culture

Examples

The museum features a collection of Babylonian artifacts dating back thousands of years.
She wrote her thesis on Babylonian law and society.
Many Babylonian myths influenced later religious traditions.

Synset Examples

Babylonian religion

Etymology

From Babylon, the ancient city in Mesopotamia, plus the suffix -ian.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02667244-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˌbæbəˈloʊniən / (AmE), / ˌbabɪˈləʊnɪən / (BrE)
the ideographic and syllabic writing system used to write the ancient Babylonian language
ancient Babylonian writing system

Examples

Researchers have deciphered several clay tablets written in Babylonian.
The Babylonian script contributed significantly to the documentation of early Mesopotamian civilizations.
Understanding Babylonian provides insight into the culture and history of ancient Babylonia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuneiform - the wedge-shaped writing system developed by the ancient Sumerians

Etymology

From Babylon, the ancient city in Mesopotamia, with English suffix -ian.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06363853-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌbæbəˈloʊniən / (AmE), / ˌbæbəˈləʊniən / (BrE)
An inhabitant or native of ancient Babylon.
inhabitant of ancient Babylon

Examples

The ancient Babylonian constructed ziggurats that towered over the city.
A Babylonian would have witnessed the splendor of Nebuchadnezzar's palace.
Archaeologists uncovered tablets written by a Babylonian scribe thousands of years ago.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Assyrian - an inhabitant of ancient Assyria
  • Synonym: Babylonian - relating to ancient Babylon (adjective)

Etymology

From Babylon, the name of the ancient city, plus the suffix -ian denoting origin or association.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09661199-n
Synset POS: n
