abutment

1. NOUN

/ əˈbʌtmənt / (AmE), / əˈbʌtmənt / (BrE)
the place or point where two objects or parts touch or join, especially where a structure meets another structure or support
point of contact or support between structures

Examples

The bridge relies on each abutment to support its weight.
Dental implants are anchored by a titanium abutment fitted into the jawbone.
The stone arch meets the wall at a carefully constructed abutment.

Etymology

From Old French abouter (to join end to end), from a- (to) + bout (end).

2. NOUN

/ əˈbʌtmənt / (AmE), / əˈbʌtmənt / (BrE)
A structure or mass, typically of masonry, built to support the lateral pressure of an arch or bridge, especially at the ends.
supporting structure for an arch or bridge

Examples

The stone abutment at the end of the bridge showed signs of wear after decades of use.
Engineers reinforced the bridge’s abutment to better support the weight of passing vehicles.
During restoration, cracks were found in the northern abutment of the old railway bridge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pier - upright support for a structure, such as a bridge

Etymology

From Old French abouter (to join end to end), from bout (end).
