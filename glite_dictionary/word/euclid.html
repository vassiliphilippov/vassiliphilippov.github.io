Euclid

1. PROPN

/ ˈjuːklɪd / (AmE), / ˈjuːklɪd / (BrE)
A renowned ancient Greek mathematician, known as the 'father of geometry', who lived around the 3rd century BC and whose work greatly influenced the study of mathematics.
ancient Greek mathematician, 'father of geometry'

Examples

Many mathematical concepts taught today are based on Euclid's principles of geometry.
The theorems of Euclid formed the foundation for centuries of mathematical study.
Historians consider Euclid one of the greatest mathematicians of the ancient world.

Etymology

From Greek Εὐκλείδης (Eukleidēs), meaning 'renowned, glorious'.
