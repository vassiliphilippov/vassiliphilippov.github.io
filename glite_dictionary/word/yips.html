yips

1. NOUN

/ jɪps / (AmE), / jɪps / (BrE)
A state of nervous tension that affects an athlete's performance, usually causing them to fail at easy tasks such as short putts in golf.
loss of fine motor skills in sports due to nerves

Examples

Golfers often dread getting the yips during important tournaments.
She developed the yips and struggled to make even simple throws from second base.
Many athletes seek psychological help to overcome the yips and regain confidence.

Synset Examples

to avoid the yips, he changed his style of putting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nerves - tense feeling or anxiety affecting performance
  • Synonym: choke - to fail under pressure in sports or performance
