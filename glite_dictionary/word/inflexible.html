inflexible

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈflɛksəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈflɛksɪb(ə)l / (BrE)
unwilling to change or make concessions; not open to compromise
not willing to change or compromise

Examples

Despite the new evidence, the judge remained inflexible in his decision.
Her inflexible policies led to conflict within the team.
He is known for his inflexible attitudes toward negotiations.

Synset Examples

took an uncompromising stance in the peace talks
uncompromising honesty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uncompromising - not willing to make concessions; not flexible
  • Synonym: rigid - not willing to change opinions or actions
  • Antonym: flexible - willing to change or make concessions

Etymology

From Latin 'inflexibilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'flexibilis' (flexible).
