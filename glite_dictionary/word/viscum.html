Viscum

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪskəm / (AmE), / ˈvɪskəm / (BrE)
A genus of Old World evergreen shrubs, commonly known as mistletoe, that grow as parasites on various trees such as oaks and apple trees.
genus of parasitic mistletoe shrubs

Examples

The berries of Viscum are an important food source for some birds.
Viscum is often used in traditional herbal medicine across Europe.
The botanist specialized in studying species within the Viscum genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistletoe - a common name for plants of the genus Viscum

Etymology

From Latin 'viscum' (mistletoe, birdlime).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɪskəm / (AmE), / ˈvɪskəm / (BrE)
A genus of Old World evergreen shrubs, often parasitic on trees such as oaks, apple trees, poplars, aspens, and cottonwoods; the typical genus of the family Viscaceae.
genus of parasitic evergreen shrubs

Examples

The most well-known member of the genus Viscum is European mistletoe.
Several species within Viscum are used in traditional herbal remedies.
Researchers studied the effects of Viscum extracts on various types of tumors.

Etymology

From Latin *viscum* (birdlime, mistletoe).
