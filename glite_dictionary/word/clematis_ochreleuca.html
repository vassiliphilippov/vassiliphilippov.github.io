Clematis ochreleuca

1. NOUN

/ kləˈmeɪtɪs ˌɑkrəˈluːkə / (AmE), / klɪˈmætɪs ˌɒkrɪˈluːkə / (BrE)
A species of shrubby clematis native to the eastern United States, noted for its curly foliage.
a shrubby clematis plant of eastern U.S.

Examples

The Clematis ochreleuca thrives in sandy soils of the southeastern United States.
Gardeners value the curly leaves of Clematis ochreleuca for their unique appearance.
I spotted a wild Clematis ochreleuca blooming near the forest edge in early spring.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus 'Clematis' and the species name 'ochreleuca', meaning 'pale yellow'.
