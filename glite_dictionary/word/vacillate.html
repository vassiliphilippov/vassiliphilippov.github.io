to vacillate

1. VERB

/ ˈvæsɪˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈvæsɪleɪt / (BrE)
to be indecisive or to alternate between different opinions or actions; to waver
to waver or be indecisive

Examples

She tends to vacillate when faced with difficult decisions, unable to choose a clear direction.
The committee vacillated for weeks before finally reaching an agreement.
He vacillates between wanting to move abroad and staying close to family.

Synset Examples

He oscillates between accepting the new position and retirement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waver - to go back and forth between choices or opinions
  • Antonym: to decide - to make a final choice after considering options

Etymology

From Latin vacillare (to sway to and fro; to waver).

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈvæsəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈvæsɪleɪt / (BrE)
to move or sway in a rising and falling or wavelike motion
move or sway in a wavelike pattern

Examples

As the boat drifted on the gentle sea, it seemed to vacillate with every wave.
The line on the heart monitor began to vacillate as the patient's condition changed.
Watching the kite vacillate in the gusty wind was mesmerizing.

Synset Examples

the line on the monitor vacillated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to oscillate - to move or swing back and forth at a regular speed

Etymology

From Latin vacillare (to sway to and fro).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01880900-v
Synset POS: v
