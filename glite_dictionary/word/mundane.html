mundane

1. ADJ

/ mʌnˈdeɪn / (AmE), / mʌnˈdeɪn / (BrE)
concerned with ordinary, worldly, or everyday matters, rather than spiritual or extraordinary.
of ordinary or worldly matters

Examples

She was tired of her mundane daily routine and longed for adventure.
Even the most mundane tasks can be satisfying if you do them with care.
Philosophers sometimes ignore mundane concerns in search of deeper meaning.

Synset Examples

mundane affairs
he developed an immense terrestrial practicality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worldly - concerned with material rather than spiritual interests; mundane
  • Antonym: spiritual - relating to the spirit or soul rather than worldly matters; opposite of mundane

Etymology

From Latin mundanus (of the world), from mundus (world).

2. ADJ

/ mʌnˈdeɪn / (AmE), / mʌnˈdeɪn / (BrE)
lacking interest or excitement; dull and ordinary; relating to the ordinary, everyday world rather than something extraordinary or spiritual.
ordinary or unremarkable; everyday

Examples

She dreaded returning to her mundane office routine after the excitement of her trip.
He found beauty in the most mundane daily tasks, like making coffee.
Despite its mundane appearance, the building had a fascinating history.

Synset Examples

a placid everyday scene
it was a routine day
there's nothing quite like a real … train conductor to add color to a quotidian commute

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ordinary - with no special or distinctive features; normal
  • Antonym: extraordinary - very unusual or remarkable

Etymology

From Latin mundanus (of the world), from mundus (world).

3. ADJ

/ mʌnˈdeɪn / (AmE), / mʌnˈdeɪn / (BrE)
Relating to the ordinary world as opposed to heaven or the spiritual realm; earthly rather than ideal or heavenly.
of the earthly world; not spiritual or heavenly

Examples

The ancient philosopher wrote extensively about both the spiritual and the mundane aspects of life.
Artists found inspiration in the mundane world, seeing beauty in everyday existence.
Her interests are far from mundane; she seeks knowledge that goes beyond earthly concerns.

Synset Examples

not a fairy palace; yet a mundane wonder of unimagined kind
so terrene a being as himself

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthly - relating to the earth rather than spiritual matters
  • Antonym: heavenly - relating to heaven or the spiritual realm

Etymology

From Latin mundanus (belonging to the world), from mundus (world).
