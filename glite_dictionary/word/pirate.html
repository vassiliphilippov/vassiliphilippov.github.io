pirate

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪrət / (AmE), / ˈpaɪərət / (BrE)
a person who attacks and robs ships at sea, especially without the authority of any country or government
someone who robs ships at sea

Examples

The famous pirate Blackbeard terrorized the Atlantic coast in the 18th century.
A pirate ship was spotted on the horizon near the old port.
Stories about buried treasure and pirates have fascinated children for generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buccaneer - a pirate, especially one who attacked Spanish ships in the Caribbean during the 17th century
  • Degree Lower: privateer - a privately owned ship or its captain allowed by a government to attack enemy ships
  • Synonym: corsair - a pirate, especially along the Barbary Coast of North Africa
  • Antonym: naval officer - a commissioned officer in a navy, opposite of pirate in official status

Etymology

From Latin pirata (sea robber), from Greek peiratēs (brigand, pirate).

2. VERB

/ ˈpaɪr.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈpaɪərət / (BrE)
to copy or use (a film, book, music, software, etc.) illegally and without permission
copy or use published material illegally

Examples

Many people have been prosecuted for trying to pirate movies online.
It is illegal to pirate software and distribute it to others.
He was caught pirating copyrighted music and faced heavy fines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to infringe - to violate or breach the terms of a law or right, especially copyright
  • Synonym: to plagiarize - to copy someone else's work and pass it off as one’s own without permission

Etymology

From Latin 'pirata' (pirate), through Old French and Middle English, originally meaning a sea robber and later extended to copyright infringements.

3. VERB

/ ˈpaɪrət / (AmE), / ˈpaɪərət / (BrE)
to take or seize something arbitrarily or by force, especially in the manner of a pirate.
to seize by force or arbitrarily

Examples

During the revolution, the rebels pirated a supply ship and redistributed its contents.
The hackers attempted to pirate the company's confidential data for their own use.
A notorious gang once pirated a luxury yacht off the coast last year.

Synset Examples

The Cubans commandeered the plane and flew it to Miami

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commandeer - to take arbitrary or forcible possession of something, especially for military purposes
  • Synonym: to seize - to take hold of suddenly or forcibly
  • Synonym: confiscate - to take something away from someone by authority

Etymology

From Old French 'pirate', from Latin 'pirata', from Greek 'πειρατής' (peiratēs) meaning 'brigand, pirate'.
