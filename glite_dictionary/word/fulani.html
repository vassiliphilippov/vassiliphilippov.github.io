Fulani

1. NOUN

/ fuːˈlɑːni / (AmE), / fuːˈlɑːni / (BrE)
a member of a pastoral and nomadic people of western Africa, traditionally Muslim cattle herders
member of a West African ethnic group

Examples

The Fulani are renowned for their expertise in cattle herding across vast regions of West Africa.
She married into a Fulani family that followed traditional nomadic customs.
Many Fulani have settled in urban areas, though some still maintain their pastoral lifestyle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fula - another name for the Fulani people

Etymology

From Hausa Fulāni, the name for the ethnic group.

2. NOUN

/ fuˈlɑːni / (AmE), / fʊˈlɑːni / (BrE)
a family of closely related languages spoken by the Fulani people of West Africa, used as a lingua franca from Senegal to Chad
West African language family

Examples

The Fulani language is spoken by millions of people across West Africa.
She wrote her thesis on the structure of Fulani verbs.
As a lingua franca, Fulani helps people from different ethnic groups communicate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fula - another name for the Fulani language or people

Etymology

From Hausa 'Fula' or 'Fulani', referring to the people and their language.

Source: WordNet
