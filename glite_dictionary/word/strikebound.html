strikebound

1. ADJ

/ ˈstraɪkˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈstraɪkˌbaʊnd / (BrE)
(of a business, service, etc.) closed or unable to operate because of a workers' strike.
closed due to a strike

Examples

The strikebound airline was unable to serve its passengers for days.
Several factories remained strikebound throughout the entire summer, causing supply shortages.
During the 1970s, strikebound industries frequently appeared in the headlines.

Synset Examples

a strikebound airline

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striking - engaged in a strike (of workers)

Etymology

From 'strike' (work stoppage) + 'bound' (held by or restricted).
