voluted

1. ADJ

/ vəˈluːtɪd / (AmE), / vəˈluːtɪd / (BrE)
having the form of a coil or spiral; coiled in shape.
coiled; spiral in shape

Examples

The ancient columns featured a voluted design at their capitals.
The snail's shell is beautifully voluted, curving in a perfect spiral.
We admired the voluted staircase winding gracefully up to the second floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiral - winding in a continuous curve around a central point
  • Antonym: straight - having no curves or bends

Etymology

From Latin 'voluta' (a spiral scroll, a whorl), from 'volvere' (to roll).
