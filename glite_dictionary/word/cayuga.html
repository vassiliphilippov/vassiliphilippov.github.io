Cayuga

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈjuːɡə / (AmE), / kaɪˈjuːɡə / (BrE)
the Iroquoian language spoken by the Cayuga people, native to parts of the northeastern United States and Canada
Iroquoian language of the Cayuga people

Examples

Only a few fluent speakers of Cayuga remain in the traditional communities.
She is studying Cayuga at the local cultural center to preserve her heritage.
The Cayuga language features unique sounds not found in English.

Etymology

From the name of the Cayuga people, an Indigenous group of the Haudenosaunee (Iroquois) Confederacy.

2. NOUN

/ kaɪˈjuːɡə / (AmE), / kaɪˈjuːɡə / (BrE)
A member of an Iroquoian people who formerly lived around Cayuga Lake in New York State.
member of an Iroquoian people

Examples

The Cayuga played a significant role in the history of the Iroquois Confederacy.
Several Cayuga families still live in New York today.
Anthropologists have studied the traditions of the Cayuga for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Seneca - a member of another Iroquoian people of New York State
  • Synonym: Onondaga - a member of an Iroquoian people in central New York

Etymology

From the name of Cayuga Lake and the people's own designation.
