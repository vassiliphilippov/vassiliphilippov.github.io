allergic

1. ADJ

/ əˈlɝːdʒɪk / (AmE), / əˈlɜːdʒɪk / (BrE)
having an allergy to a particular substance, or caused by an allergy
having or caused by an allergy
medical

Examples

She had an allergic reaction to peanuts after eating the candy.
Many people are allergic to pollen in the spring.
If you are allergic to cats, you should avoid visiting my house.

Synset Examples

an allergic reaction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypersensitive - abnormally or excessively sensitive, often in a medical or allergic context
  • Antonym: immune - not affected by a particular substance, especially in an allergic context

Etymology

Formed from 'allergy' + '-ic', indicating 'pertaining to allergy'.

2. ADJ

/ əˈlɝːdʒɪk / (AmE), / əˈlɜːdʒɪk / (BrE)
Having an allergy or showing hypersensitivity to a specific substance.
having an allergy; hypersensitive

Examples

He is allergic to peanuts, so he always checks food labels carefully.
The doctor confirmed she was allergic to certain types of pollen.
Many people become allergic after repeated exposure to an irritant.

Synset Examples

allergic children
hypersensitive to pollen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypersensitive - abnormally or excessively sensitive; particularly to allergens
  • Antonym: immune - not affected by a specific allergen or disease

Etymology

From Greek 'allergia' (allergy) via German 'allergisch' (allergic).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02369499-s
Synset POS: s
