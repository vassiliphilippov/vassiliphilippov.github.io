babble

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæbəl / (AmE), / ˈbæbəl / (BrE)
incoherent or meaningless speech that resembles the sounds made by a baby
incoherent, baby-like speech

Examples

The quiet babble of the infant filled the room with gentle sounds.
Amid the adults' laughter, the toddler's babble provided a sweet background noise.
He listened to the joyful babble coming from the playpen and smiled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gibberish - unintelligible or meaningless speech or writing

Etymology

From Middle English 'babelen', imitative of childish speech.

2. VERB

/ ˈbæbəl / (AmE), / ˈbæbəl / (BrE)
to reveal confidential information or secrets, often carelessly or unintentionally
reveal secrets or confidential information carelessly

Examples

Don’t babble our plans to anyone outside this group.
The rookie accidentally babbled crucial information to the press.
He tends to babble when nervous, sometimes giving away more than he should.

Synset Examples

Be careful — his secretary talks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to blab - to reveal secrets or confidential information indiscreetly

Etymology

From Middle English 'babelen', of imitative origin, related to 'babble' as incoherent talk.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00939238-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈbæb.əl / (AmE), / ˈbæb.əl / (BrE)
(of a stream, etc.) to flow in an irregular way with a gentle, continuous bubbling sound.
make bubbling sounds as water flows

Examples

A small creek began to babble over the smooth stones in the valley.
You could hear the water babbling as we walked along the forest path.
The spring rains made the brook babble more loudly than usual this year.

Synset Examples

babbling brooks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murmur - of a stream: to make a low, continuous sound as water flows
  • Synonym: gurgle - to flow with a bubbling or rippling sound

Etymology

From Middle English babelen, possibly imitative in origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02192368-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈbæbəl / (AmE), / ˈbabəl / (BrE)
to talk quickly and in a way that is silly or impossible to understand
talk foolishly or incoherently

Examples

The child began to babble about her dreams during breakfast.
Tourists often babble excitedly when they see famous landmarks for the first time.
He quietly babbled nonsense to himself as he worked.

Synset Examples

The two women babbled and crooned at the baby

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prattle - to talk at length in a foolish or inconsequential way
  • Synonym: mumble - to speak quietly and not clearly enough so that the words are difficult to understand
  • Antonym: to speak clearly - to talk in a way that is easy to understand

Etymology

Probably from Middle Low German *babbeln* (to babble, prattle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01039522-v
Synset POS: v

5. VERB

/ ˈbæb.əl / (AmE), / ˈbæb.əl / (BrE)
to talk rapidly and continuously in a foolish, excited, or incomprehensible way, often making meaningless or incoherent sounds
speak incoherently or unintelligibly

Examples

The baby began to babble excitedly as he played with his toys.
She couldn't hear what he was saying because he started to babble after the accident.
The brook seemed to babble along the rocks, making a pleasant sound.

Synset Examples

The old man is only babbling — don't pay attention

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prattle - to talk at length in a foolish or inconsequential way
  • Synonym: mutter - to speak in a low, indistinct voice, often in a complaining or discontented manner
  • Antonym: articulate - to express an idea or feeling fluently and coherently

Etymology

From Middle English 'babelen', likely imitative of childish speech.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
