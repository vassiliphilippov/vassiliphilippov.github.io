Wabash

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɑːˌbæʃ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːˌbæʃ / (BrE)
a river in the United States, rising in western Ohio and flowing southwest across Indiana to join the Ohio River
U.S. river, tributary of the Ohio

Examples

The Wabash River played a crucial role in the transportation of goods in the 19th century.
Many towns along the Wabash have grown because of its historical significance in Indiana.
Fishing is a popular activity on the banks of the Wabash during the summer months.

Etymology

From French 'Ouabache', based on a Miami-Illinois word meaning 'white' or 'pure'.
