bloodlust

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌdˌlʌst / (AmE), / ˈblʌdˌlʌst / (BrE)
a strong desire for violence, especially for killing or bloodshed
intense desire for violence or bloodshed

Examples

The soldiers were swept up in bloodlust as the battle raged on.
A chilling bloodlust seemed to overtake the crowd at the gladiator games.
He struggled to control his sudden bloodlust after witnessing the violence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruelty - deliberate infliction of pain
  • Antonym: compassion - sympathetic pity and concern for suffering

Etymology

Formed from 'blood' + 'lust'; 'lust' from Old English 'lust' (desire, pleasure).
