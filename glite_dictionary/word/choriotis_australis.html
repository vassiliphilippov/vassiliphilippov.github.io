Choriotis australis

1. NOUN

/ kɔːriˈoʊtɪs ɔːˈstreɪlɪs / (AmE), / kɒriˈəʊtɪs ɔːˈstreɪlɪs / (BrE)
a large Australian game bird, also known as the Australian bustard, found in grasslands and open woodlands
Australian game bird (Australian bustard)

Examples

The Choriotis australis is sometimes seen striding gracefully across the Australian outback.
Aboriginal Australians have traditional stories involving the Choriotis australis.
Birdwatchers travel great distances to spot a Choriotis australis in its natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Australian bustard - common name for Choriotis australis

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name 'Choriotis' and species name 'australis', meaning 'southern' in Latin.
