moralization

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɔrələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɒrələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making something moral, or more in line with moral principles
the act of making something more moral

Examples

The moralization of public policy is often debated by politicians and philosophers alike.
Many parents believe in the moralization of children's stories to teach important values.
Her moralization of the workplace aimed to promote fairness and respect.

Synset Examples

for years she worked toward the moralization of English literature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to moralize - to interpret or explain in moral terms

Etymology

From French 'moralisation' or Medieval Latin 'moralizatio', from 'moralis' (moral).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɔrəlɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɒrələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of expressing moral judgments or expounding a moral code, often in a preachy or superficial way
preaching or expressing moral judgments

Examples

The moralization of every discussion became tiresome to the other members of the group.
Critics accused the film of excessive moralization, which overshadowed the story.
His speech was full of moralization about society's need for honesty.

Synset Examples

his constant moralizing drove me mad

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moralizing - the act of commenting on issues of right and wrong, often self-righteously

Etymology

From Latin moralis (moral) + -ization (forming nouns of action or process).
