sleepover

1. NOUN

/ ˈsliːpˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈsliːpˌəʊvə / (BrE)
an occasion when someone, especially a child, stays overnight at a friend's house as a social event
overnight social stay, often for children

Examples

Emily invited her friends to a sleepover for her birthday.
We stayed up late telling stories at the sleepover.
My parents said I could go to the sleepover this weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pajama party - a party where guests stay overnight wearing pajamas

Etymology

Formed from the words 'sleep' and 'over', indicating staying overnight.
