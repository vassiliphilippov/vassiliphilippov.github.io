Gavidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡævɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈɡævɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic birds known as loons, characterized by their diving ability and found in northern regions.
the loon family of aquatic birds

Examples

The Gavidae are renowned for their remarkable diving skills and haunting calls.
Scientists conducted a study of the migration patterns of various Gavidae species.
Several members of the Gavidae family breed in northern lakes during the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loon - large aquatic bird of the genus Gavia

Etymology

From New Latin 'Gavia' (the type genus) + '-idae' (zoological family suffix).
