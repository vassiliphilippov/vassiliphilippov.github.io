snootily

1. ADV

/ ˈsnuːtɪli / (AmE), / ˈsnuːtɪli / (BrE)
In a snobbish or superior manner; showing disdain or aloofness due to a sense of superiority.
in a snobbish or superior manner

Examples

She looked snootily at the other guests, clearly unimpressed by their attire.
The critics spoke snootily about the new play, dismissing it as unoriginal.
He snootily declined the offer, thinking it beneath him to participate.

Synset Examples

they snobbishly excluded their less wealthy friends from the party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snobbishly - in a snobbish manner
  • Synonym: haughtily - in an arrogantly superior manner

Etymology

From 'snooty' (affectedly superior or snobbish), itself an alteration of 'snout' or 'snouty', meaning nose, perhaps originally in reference to turning up one's nose.
