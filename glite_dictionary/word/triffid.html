triffid

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪf.ɪd / (AmE), / ˈtrɪf.ɪd / (BrE)
a fictional plant monster, especially as featured in science fiction and horror literature, known for its ability to move and attack animals and humans
fictional plant monster from literature

Examples

The triffid lurked in the shadows, waiting to strike unsuspecting victims.
After the comet, the triffids began to roam the countryside at night.
The gardener's nightmares were filled with images of a giant triffid crashing through the greenhouse.

Synset Examples

The horror film featured a terrifying triffid as the monster.

Etymology

Coined by John Wyndham in his 1951 novel 'The Day of the Triffids'; possibly formed from 'tri-' (three) and 'fid' (split, from Latin 'findere').
