Ivan the Terrible

1. PROPN

/ ˈaɪ.vən ðə ˈtɛr.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.vən ðə ˈtɛr.ɪ.bəl / (BrE)
(1530–1584) The first czar of Russia, known for his reign marked by centralization of power and notorious acts of cruelty.
first czar of Russia, 1530–1584

Examples

The reign of Ivan the Terrible drastically changed the politics of Russia.
Many Russian legends and stories are associated with Ivan the Terrible.
Historians still debate whether Ivan the Terrible was a visionary ruler or a ruthless tyrant.

Etymology

English rendering of Russian 'Ivan Grozny', with 'Grozny' often translated as 'the Terrible', meaning 'the Formidable' or 'the Fearsome'.
