to evolve

1. VERB

/ ɪˈvɑlv / (AmE), / ɪˈvɒlv / (BrE)
to develop gradually, especially from a simple to a more complex form
to develop gradually

Examples

Over centuries, the language evolved into its modern form.
Scientists hope the species will evolve new traits to adapt to climate change.
Her ideas on education evolved as she gained more experience in teaching.

Synset Examples

We have developed a new theory of evolution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to develop - to grow or cause to grow and become more mature or advanced

Etymology

From Latin evolvere (to unroll, unfold), from e- (out) + volvere (to roll).

3. VERB

/ ɪˈvɑlv / (AmE), / ɪˈvɒlv / (BrE)

to develop gradually or undergo evolution

Examples

Her ideas about education have continued to evolve over the years.
Technology tends to evolve rapidly, bringing constant innovation to our daily lives.

Synset Examples
