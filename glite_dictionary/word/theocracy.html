theocracy

1. NOUN

/ θiˈɑkrəsi / (AmE), / θiˈɒkrəsi / (BrE)
A system of government in which priests or religious officials rule in the name of a deity, or the government of a state by divine guidance or by officials regarded as divinely guided.
government by religious authority

Examples

Iran is often cited as a modern example of a theocracy, with its rulers claiming authority from divine law.
In a theocracy, religious leaders may hold both spiritual and political power.
Historically, many ancient societies functioned as theocracies, merging religion with governance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: democracy - government by the people, typically through elected representatives
  • Antonym: autocracy - government by one person with absolute power

Etymology

From Greek theokratia, from theos (god) + -kratia (power, rule).

2. NOUN

/ θiˈɑːkrəsi / (AmE), / θiˈɒkrəsi / (BrE)
A system of government in which priests or religious leaders rule in the name of a deity or by claiming divine guidance.
government by religious authority

Examples

Iran is often cited as a modern example of a theocracy in which religious leaders hold political authority.
Under the theocracy, all laws were based on strict interpretations of religious texts.
Debates about the separation of church and state frequently mention historical theocracy as a cautionary example.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secularism - the principle of separation of government from religious institutions
  • Degree Lower: autocracy - government by a single person with absolute power
  • Degree Lower: monarchy - a form of government with a monarch at the head

Etymology

From Greek theokratia, from theos (god) + kratos (power, rule).
