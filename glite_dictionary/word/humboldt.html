Humboldt

1. PROPN

/ ˈhʌmˌboʊlt / (AmE), / ˈhʌmbɒlt / (BrE)
(1769–1859) German naturalist and explorer renowned for his extensive scientific explorations of Central and South America and his comprehensive descriptions of the physical world.
German naturalist and explorer (1769–1859)

Examples

The writings of Humboldt greatly influenced later scientists and explorers.
Many geographical features in the Americas are named after Humboldt.
Humboldt documented a wide range of plants, animals, and climates during his journeys.

Etymology

From the surname of Alexander von Humboldt, German naturalist and explorer.
