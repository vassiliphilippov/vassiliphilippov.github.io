profitableness

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.fɪ.tə.bəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈprɒf.ɪ.tə.bəl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of producing profit, benefit, or advantage
the quality of being profitable

Examples

The profitableness of the venture convinced investors to support the project.
We must consider both the risks and the profitableness before launching the new product.
Her analysis focused on the long-term profitableness of sustainable farming methods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profitability - the state or quality of yielding a profit

Etymology

Formed from 'profitable' + '-ness', based on Latin 'profiteor' (to declare, acknowledge).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɑfətəblnəs / (AmE), / ˈprɒfɪtəbəlnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being likely to bring success or advantage, especially through financial gain.
quality of being profitable or advantageous

Examples

The profitableness of the investment tempted many new entrepreneurs to participate in the project.
Before launching the new product, they evaluated the profitableness of the entire market segment.
There is much debate about the long-term profitableness of sustainable energy resources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profitability - The degree to which something yields a financial profit or benefit
  • Antonym: unprofitableness - The quality of not yielding profit or benefit

Etymology

From Middle English 'profitable' + '-ness', ultimately from Latin 'profitare' (to make progress, benefit).
