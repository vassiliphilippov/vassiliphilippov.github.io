wurtzite

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːtsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈwɜːtsaɪt / (BrE)
a brownish-black mineral consisting of zinc sulfide, occurring in hexagonal crystals and used as an ore of zinc
zinc sulfide mineral

Examples

Geologists discovered significant deposits of wurtzite in the newly explored mine.
The rare hexagonal structure of wurtzite differentiates it from other zinc sulfide minerals.
Samples of wurtzite are often studied for their unique semiconductor properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sphalerite - a common mineral form of zinc sulfide, often found with wurtzite

Etymology

Named after French chemist Charles-Adolphe Wurtz (1817–1884).
