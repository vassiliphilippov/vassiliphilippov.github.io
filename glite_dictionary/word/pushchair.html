pushchair

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʊʃˌtʃɛr / (AmE), / ˈpʊʃ.tʃeə / (BrE)
a small vehicle on wheels in which a baby or small child is pushed along while seated
baby vehicle with wheels, pushed by an adult
british

Examples

We bought a new pushchair for the baby, and it's easy to fold for travel.
She pushed her son through the park in a bright red pushchair.
Some shops have special spaces where you can leave your pushchair while shopping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stroller - a similar vehicle used for pushing babies or small children, chiefly in American English
  • Synonym: pram - a baby carriage in which a child lies flat, mainly British English

Etymology

From 'push' + 'chair', indicating a chair that can be pushed.
