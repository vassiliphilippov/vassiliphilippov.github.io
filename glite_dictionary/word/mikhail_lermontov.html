Mikhail Lermontov

1. PROPN

/ miˈxaɪl ˈlɛrməntɔv / (AmE), / mɪˈxaɪl ˈlɛəmɒntɒf / (BrE)
(1814–1841) A Russian poet, novelist, and playwright, regarded as one of the great figures in Russian literature.
Russian poet and novelist (1814–1841)

Examples

Mikhail Lermontov is often called the "poet of the Caucasus" because of his fascination with the region.
Many of Mikhail Lermontov's works explore themes of fate, freedom, and national identity.
The poetry of Mikhail Lermontov had a lasting influence on Russian literature.
