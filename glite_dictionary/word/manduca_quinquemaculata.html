Manduca quinquemaculata

1. NOUN

/ mænˈduːkə ˌkwɪŋkwɪmæˈkjʊlətə / (AmE), / manˈduːkə ˌkwɪŋkwɪmæˈkjʊlətə / (BrE)
A species of large moth whose larvae are known as tomato hornworms and are common pests of tomato plants.
species of moth; tomato hornworm

Examples

The leafy tomato plant was infested by the larvae of Manduca quinquemaculata.
Scientists study Manduca quinquemaculata to understand the impact of insect pests on agriculture.
The adult Manduca quinquemaculata is known for its large size and five distinct spots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tomato hornworm - larva of Manduca quinquemaculata, a pest of tomato plants

Etymology

From Latin Manduca (a moth genus) and quinquemaculata ('five-spotted').

2. NOUN

/ mænˈduːkə kwɪnˌkwɛməˈkjuːlətə / (AmE), / mænˈduːkə kwɪnˌkwɛmækjuːˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A large green and white-striped caterpillar (larva of a hawkmoth) that feeds on tomato and potato plants, commonly known as the tomato hornworm.
species of caterpillar, tomato hornworm
medical

Examples

If you notice large holes in your tomato leaves, check for the distinctive Manduca quinquemaculata caterpillar.
Gardeners often handpick Manduca quinquemaculata from their plants to prevent infestations.
The Manduca quinquemaculata is easily confused with its cousin, the tobacco hornworm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tomato hornworm - common name for Manduca quinquemaculata, caterpillar pest of tomato plants
  • Synonym: tobacco hornworm - similar species, Manduca sexta, feeds on tobacco plants

Etymology

From Latin; 'Manduca' meaning 'glutton' and 'quinquemaculata' meaning 'five-spotted.'
