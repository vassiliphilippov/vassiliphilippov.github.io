matzoh

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑtsoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmætsəʊ / (BrE)
A brittle flatbread eaten by Jews during Passover, made without yeast.
unleavened bread eaten at Passover

Examples

During Passover, Jews traditionally eat matzoh instead of regular bread.
She baked homemade matzoh for the holiday celebration.
A piece of matzoh was served alongside the soup at the Seder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: matzo - alternate spelling of matzoh
  • Synonym: unleavened bread - bread made without yeast or other leavening agents

Etymology

From Yiddish *matse*, from Hebrew *maṣṣāh* (unleavened bread).
