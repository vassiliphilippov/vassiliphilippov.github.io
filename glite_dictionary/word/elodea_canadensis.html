Elodea canadensis

1. NOUN

/ ɛˌloʊdiə ˌkænəˈdɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ɛˌləʊdiə ˌkanəˈdɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a North American aquatic plant, commonly known as Canadian waterweed, often found in ponds and widely naturalized in Europe
a North American aquatic waterweed

Examples

Elodea canadensis is often used in aquariums to oxygenate the water.
In many European lakes, Elodea canadensis has become an invasive species.
The pond was filled with lush, green strands of Elodea canadensis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Canadian waterweed - common name for Elodea canadensis

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, with 'Elodea' from Greek 'helôdēs' (marshy), and 'canadensis' meaning 'of Canada' in Latin.
