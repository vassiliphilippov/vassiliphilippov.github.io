patchwork

1. NOUN

/ ˈpætʃˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈpætʃwɜːk / (BrE)
A quilt or other item made by sewing together pieces of different materials, typically in various colors or patterns.
quilt made of sewn-together patches

Examples

She made a beautiful patchwork for her granddaughter's birthday.
The old farmhouse was decorated with colorful patchwork quilts.
Each square in the patchwork told a different story from her family's history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quilt - a warm bed covering made of padding enclosed between layers of fabric

Etymology

From 'patch' + 'work', referring to work made from sewn patches.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpætʃˌwɝːk / (AmE), / ˈpætʃˌwɜːk / (BrE)
a type of needlework in which pieces of different fabrics are sewn together in a pattern to make a larger design, often used for quilts
needlework using sewn pieces of fabric

Examples

She wrapped herself in a colorful patchwork quilt her grandmother had made.
The exhibition showcased a stunning patchwork of old denim and silk.
Over the years, he created a patchwork out of leftover fabric scraps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quilt - a bed covering made from patchwork or other materials, with padding between layers

Etymology

From 'patch' (piece of material) + 'work' (something made or done), first recorded in the 17th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03904197-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈpætʃˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈpatʃwɜːk / (BrE)
A theory, argument, or system composed of miscellaneous or incongruous elements gathered from various sources.
mixture of disparate or incongruous ideas

Examples

The report presented a patchwork of theories rather than a coherent explanation.
Critics argue that the policy is a patchwork of old ideas lacking unity.
Her proposal felt like a patchwork stitched from unrelated concepts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hotchpotch - a confused mixture; a jumble
  • Synonym: hodgepodge - a confused mixture of different things

Etymology

From patch + work, originally referring to a type of needlework combining pieces of fabric.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05962283-n
Synset POS: n
