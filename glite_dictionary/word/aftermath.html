aftermath

1. NOUN

/ ˈæf.tɚˌmæθ / (AmE), / ˈɑːf.tə.mæθ / (BrE)
the period of time following a significant event, especially one that is unpleasant, when its consequences are experienced
consequences following an event

Examples

The country struggled to rebuild in the aftermath of the devastating earthquake.
In the aftermath of the scandal, several officials resigned.
Charities offered support to families in the aftermath of the flood.

Synset Examples

the aftermath of war
in the wake of the accident no one knew how many had been injured

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consequence - a result or effect of an action or condition
  • Synonym: result - something that happens as an effect of an event or action

Etymology

From Middle English 'aftermath', originally referring to a second mowing of grass after the first crop, from 'after' + 'math' (mowing).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæftɚˌmæθ / (AmE), / ˈɑːftəˌmæθ / (BrE)
The consequences or effects of a significant event, particularly an unpleasant one.
consequences following an event

Examples

The hurricane's aftermath left thousands of people without homes or power.
She struggled to rebuild her life in the aftermath of the accident.
The company faced legal troubles in the aftermath of the scandal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consequence - a result or effect of an action or condition
  • Synonym: outcome - the way a thing turns out; a consequence
  • Antonym: cause - a person or thing that gives rise to an action, phenomenon, or condition; opposite of effect/aftermath

Etymology

From Old English 'æfter' (after) + 'math' (mowing, a period of growth after mowing).
