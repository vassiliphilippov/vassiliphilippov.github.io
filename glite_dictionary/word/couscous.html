couscous

1. NOUN

/ ˈkuːskuːs / (AmE), / ˈkʊskuːs / (BrE)
a type of granular pasta made from crushed and steamed semolina, traditionally prepared in North Africa
North African semolina pasta

Examples

I made a salad with roasted vegetables and couscous for dinner.
Moroccan cuisine often features spiced couscous served with lamb or chicken.
She bought a package of whole-wheat couscous from the grocery store.

Etymology

From Arabic *kus-kus*, reduplication of *kus* (well formed, rolled).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkuːsˌkuːs / (AmE), / ˈkʊs.kʊs / (BrE)
a North African dish made of steamed semolina granules, often served with a meat and vegetable stew
North African steamed semolina dish

Examples

We prepared couscous with lamb and vegetables for the family dinner.
The restaurant's specialty is authentic North African couscous.
She learned how to steam couscous properly during her trip to Morocco.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: semolina - granulated durum wheat often used in couscous

Etymology

From Arabic *kuskus*, from Berber *seksu*, referring to the steamed semolina.
