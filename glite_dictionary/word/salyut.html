Salyut

1. PROPN

/ sɑːˈljuːt / (AmE), / ˈsæljuːt / (BrE)
The name of a series of Soviet space stations launched in the 1970s as part of an early space research program.
series of Soviet space stations (1970s)

Examples

The crew aboard the Salyut station conducted various experiments in microgravity.
Salyut was instrumental in advancing long-duration human spaceflight programs.
Several missions to Salyut paved the way for later space stations like Mir and ISS.

Etymology

From Russian 'Салют' (saljut), meaning 'salute' or 'fireworks'.
