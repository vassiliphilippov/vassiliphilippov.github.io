patriarchy

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪ.triˌɑːr.ki / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ.triˌɑː.ki / (BrE)
A social system or organization in which men hold primary power and predominate in roles of political leadership, moral authority, social privilege, and control of property; descent and inheritance are typically traced through the male line.
male-dominated social system

Examples

The ancient society was governed under a strict patriarchy that excluded women from inheritance.
Many scholars critique the effects of patriarchy on gender equality and social structure.
Feminist movements have long challenged the norms established by patriarchy in many cultures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: matriarchy - a social system governed by women or mothers

Etymology

From Greek patriarkhia (rule of the fathers), from patriarkhēs (chief or father).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪtriˌɑːrki / (AmE), / ˈpætriˌɑːki / (BrE)
A social system in which men hold primary power and predominate in roles of leadership, authority, and control.
male-dominated social system

Examples

Many feminist movements seek to challenge the patriarchy in various societies.
The patriarchy has shaped laws and traditions for centuries.
She wrote an essay about the influence of patriarchy on education.

Synset Examples

The patriarchy ensures the survival of their young.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: matriarchy - a social system dominated by women
  • Synonym: patriarch - the male head of a family or group

Etymology

From Greek patriarkhēs (chief of a family), from patriarches (patriarch).
