dumbbell

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌmˌbɛl / (AmE), / ˈdʌm.bɛl / (BrE)
a short bar with a weight at each end, used in pairs for exercising the muscles of the arms, shoulders, and chest
a short bar with weights at each end for exercise

Examples

He lifted the dumbbell above his head as part of his workout routine.
The gym has a wide range of dumbbells for different strength levels.
She bought a pair of dumbbells to exercise at home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barbell - a long bar with weights at each end, used in weightlifting

Etymology

From 'dumb' (silent) and 'bell'; originally referred to devices resembling bells but without sound, used for exercise.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʌmˌbɛl / (AmE), / ˈdʌmˌbɛl / (BrE)
an ignorant or foolish person; someone who is not smart or easily makes silly mistakes (informal, often humorous)
ignorant or foolish person (informal)
informal

Examples

He can be such a dumbbell sometimes, forgetting his keys every day.
I felt like a total dumbbell when I answered the question wrong in front of everyone.
Don’t be a dumbbell—read the instructions before starting the test.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idiot - a very stupid person
  • Antonym: genius - a person with exceptional intellectual or creative power

Etymology

From 'dumbbell' (exercise weight), used figuratively to suggest mental dullness.
