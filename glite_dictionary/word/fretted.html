fretted

1. ADJ

/ ˈfrɛtɪd / (AmE), / ˈfrɛtɪd / (BrE)
(of a musical instrument) having frets set into the fingerboard to guide finger placement.
having frets (on a musical instrument)

Examples

The guitarist preferred a fretted bass for its precise note selection.
Traditional lutes are fretted instruments, unlike the fretless violin.
He customized his guitar from fretless to fretted for better playability.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fretless - (of a musical instrument) without frets on the fingerboard

Etymology

From fret (raised strip on a musical instrument) + -ed.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfrɛtɪd / (AmE), / ˈfrɛtɪd / (BrE)
decorated with or having a pattern of fretwork or latticework, often in architectural or artistic designs
decorated with fretwork or lattice patterns

Examples

The ancient palace featured fretted windows that allowed sunlight to filter in beautifully.
She admired the fretted panels, each intricately carved with geometric patterns.
The fretted ceiling lent an air of elegance to the grand hall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fretwork - ornamental design in wood, metal, or other material made by cutting patterns

Etymology

From the verb 'fret', meaning to decorate with interlaced patterns, from Old French 'frete' (lattice).
