scorch

1. NOUN

/ skɔrtʃ / (AmE), / skɔːtʃ / (BrE)
A plant disease that causes a browning or burnt appearance of leaves or tissues.
plant disease causing leaf browning
medical

Examples

The vineyard suffered significant damage from scorch after several weeks of intense heat.
Many plants developed scorch on their leaves due to drought conditions.
Gardeners are advised to monitor for signs of scorch during exceptionally dry summers.

2. VERB

/ skɔrtʃ / (AmE), / skɔːtʃ / (BrE)
to become superficially burned or singed, especially on the surface without being completely destroyed
become superficially burned

Examples

If you leave the shirt too close to the iron, it might scorch and leave a brown mark.
The grass began to scorch under the intense midday sun.
When the bread stayed in the toaster too long, its edges started to scorch.

Synset Examples

my eyebrows singed when I bent over the flames

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: burn - to be damaged or destroyed by fire or heat
  • Degree Higher: char - to burn the surface of something so that it becomes black
  • Synonym: singe - to burn something lightly so that only the surface is affected

Etymology

Possibly from Old French *escorcher* (to strip off the skin), from Late Latin *excorticare*.
