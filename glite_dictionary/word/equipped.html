equipped

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈkwɪpt / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɪpt / (BrE)
having the necessary tools, equipment, or other things needed for a particular purpose.
having what is needed for a purpose

Examples

The laboratory was fully equipped for advanced research.
She felt equipped to handle any challenge after months of training.
Each classroom is equipped with a smart board and sound system.

Synset Examples

a furnished apartment
a completely furnished toolbox

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furnished - provided with furniture or equipment
  • Antonym: unfurnished - not provided with furniture

Etymology

Past participle of 'equip', from French *équiper* (to fit out).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈkwɪpt / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɪpt / (BrE)
armed or provided with weapons; carrying weapons, especially for combat or security purposes.
carrying weapons; armed

Examples

The guards were equipped with rifles and body armor.
The special police unit is fully equipped for riot control operations.
Every soldier must be properly equipped before entering the combat zone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armed - carrying weapons, especially for combat
  • Antonym: unarmed - not carrying weapons
