Zanthoxylum americanum

1. NOUN

/ zænˈθɒksɪləm əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnəm / (AmE), / zænˈθɒksɪləm əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnəm / (BrE)
A small deciduous aromatic shrub or tree with spiny branches and yellowish flowers, native to eastern North America, also known as Northern prickly ash.
a North American aromatic shrub or tree

Examples

The bark of Zanthoxylum americanum has been traditionally used for medicinal purposes by indigenous peoples.
Gardeners in cold climates appreciate Zanthoxylum americanum for its hardiness and attractive foliage.
The spiny branches of Zanthoxylum americanum make it a distinctive feature in woodland areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prickly ash - common name for Zanthoxylum americanum

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name 'Zanthoxylum' is derived from Greek 'xanthos' (yellow) and 'xylon' (wood).
