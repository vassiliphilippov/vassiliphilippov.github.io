2. NOUN

/ rʌmp / (AmE), / rʌmp / (BrE)
the fleshy part of the hindquarters, especially in animals, located behind the loin and above the round
fleshy hindquarters of an animal

Examples

The chef trimmed the fat from the rump before roasting the beef.
The horse stumbled and fell on its rump during the race.
An injury to the rump can be painful for cattle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttocks - the two rounded parts at the back of the hips in humans

Etymology

From Middle English *rump*, perhaps from Old Norse *rūmpa* (hind part, tail).

3. NOUN

/ rʌmp / (AmE), / rʌmp / (BrE)
the hind part of an animal's body, especially the area above and behind the hind legs, corresponding to the human buttocks
hindquarters of an animal

Examples

The chef prepared a dish using the most tender cut from the cow's rump.
The horse's rump was marked with a white patch of hair.
Farmers often examine a sheep's rump to assess its condition and health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttocks - the two rounded parts at the back of the human pelvis

Etymology

From Middle English rump(e), probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Danish rumpe (rump, buttocks).
