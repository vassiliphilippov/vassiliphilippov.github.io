Tayalic

1. NOUN

/ taɪˈælɪk / (AmE), / taɪˈælɪk / (BrE)
a group of Austronesian languages spoken by indigenous peoples in central Taiwan
Austronesian language group of central Taiwan

Examples

Researchers have documented the grammar of the Tayalic language family extensively.
Few young people today speak Tayalic fluently in the central mountains of Taiwan.
The preservation of Tayalic languages is an important issue for cultural heritage scholars.
