ammonitic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæməˈnɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌaməˈnɪtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of ammonites, an extinct order of fossil cephalopods
relating to ammonites (fossil cephalopods)

Examples

The ammonitic shell pattern is easily recognized in many museum fossils.
Paleontologists use ammonitic features to date certain rock layers.
The ammonitic suture lines are more complex than those of earlier cephalopods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ammonite - an extinct marine mollusk with a spiral shell

Etymology

From New Latin Ammonites (type genus), named after the Egyptian god Ammon whose horns resemble coiled shells + -ic.
