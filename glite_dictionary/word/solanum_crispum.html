Solanum crispum

1. NOUN

/ soʊˌleɪnəm ˈkrɪspəm / (AmE), / səˌleɪnəm ˈkrɪspəm / (BrE)
A hardy climbing shrub native to Chile, grown as an ornamental for its fragrant flowers; not a true potato.
Chilean climbing shrub with fragrant flowers

Examples

Solanum crispum is often planted along garden fences for its attractive blue-purple flowers.
Gardeners appreciate Solanum crispum for its hardiness and sweet scent.
Unlike the common potato, Solanum crispum is grown mainly for ornamental purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potato vine - an ornamental climbing plant related to the potato

Etymology

From Latin: 'Solanum' (the nightshade genus) and 'crispum' meaning 'curled' or 'wavy', referring to the leaves.
