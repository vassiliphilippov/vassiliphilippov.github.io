diviner

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈvaɪnər / (AmE), / dɪˈvaɪnə / (BrE)
a person who claims to gain hidden knowledge or foretell the future through supernatural means
one who claims supernatural insight

Examples

The diviner claimed she could predict the outcome of the battle by studying animal bones.
In many cultures, a diviner is consulted to find missing objects or answer difficult questions.
Though skeptical, they visited the local diviner hoping for guidance about their future.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soothsayer - a person who predicts the future, especially one who claims supernatural insight
  • Synonym: clairvoyant - a person who claims to have a supernatural ability to see events in the future

Etymology

From Old French deviner (to foretell), from Latin divinare (to foresee, to be inspired by a god).
