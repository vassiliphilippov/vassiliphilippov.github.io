hailstorm

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪlˌstɔrm / (AmE), / ˈheɪlˌstɔːm / (BrE)
a storm in which pieces of ice (hail) fall from the sky, often accompanied by thunder and lightning
storm with falling hail

Examples

The sudden hailstorm damaged crops across the entire region.
We had to pull our car over because the hailstorm made it impossible to see the road.
Last night’s hailstorm left a thick layer of ice pellets on our lawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thunderstorm - a storm with thunder and lightning, usually with heavy rain

Etymology

Formed from 'hail' (ice pellets) + 'storm'.
