elkhound

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛlkˌhaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈɛlkˌhaʊnd / (BrE)
A breed of compact, medium-sized dog with a heavy grey coat, developed in Norway for hunting elk.
Norwegian breed of hunting dog

Examples

The elkhound is known for its intelligence and keen sense of smell.
My family adopted a playful elkhound from the animal shelter last year.
During the hunt, the elkhound tracked the elk through the snowy forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Norwegian elkhound - specific breed of elkhound from Norway

Etymology

From English 'elk' + 'hound', referring to its use for elk hunting.
