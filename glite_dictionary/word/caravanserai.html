caravanserai

1. NOUN

/ ˌkærəˌvæn.səˈraɪ / (AmE), / ˌkærəˌvæn.səˈraɪ / (BrE)
a roadside inn with a large courtyard, found especially in the Middle East and Asia, where travelers and caravans could rest and recover
an inn for caravans in the Middle East/Asia

Examples

The ancient caravanserai was a bustling center for merchants and travelers along the Silk Road.
Several restored caravanserais now serve as cultural centers and museums in Turkey and Iran.
After days of journeying across the desert, the traders were relieved to find shelter at a caravanserai.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inn - a place that provides accommodation, food, and drink, especially for travelers
  • Synonym: hostel - a place providing inexpensive lodging, especially for travelers

Etymology

From Persian kārwānsarāy, from kārwān (caravan) + sarāy (palace, house).
