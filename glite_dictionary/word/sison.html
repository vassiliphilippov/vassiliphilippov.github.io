Sison

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪsən / (AmE), / ˈsaɪsən / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the carrot family (Apiaceae), including species such as stone parsley
genus of plants including stone parsley

Examples

The species in the Sison genus are commonly found in fields and meadows across Europe.
Botanists have identified several medicinal herbs within the Sison genus.
Stone parsley is one of the better-known members of the Sison group.

Etymology

From Latin *sison*, a kind of plant.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪsən / (AmE), / ˈsaɪsən / (BrE)

genus of plants including stone parsley

Examples

The plant commonly known as stone parsley belongs to the genus Sison.
Botanists have identified several species within the Sison genus.
Members of the genus Sison are found throughout Europe and parts of Asia.

Etymology

From Latin *sison*, a kind of plant mentioned by ancient authors.
