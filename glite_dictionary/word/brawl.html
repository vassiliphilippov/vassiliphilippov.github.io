brawl

1. NOUN

/ brɔl / (AmE), / brɔːl / (BrE)
a noisy and disorderly fight, especially involving a group of people in a public place
a noisy public fight

Examples

A massive brawl broke out after the soccer match, forcing police to intervene.
The bar was known for the occasional brawl on Friday nights.
He was injured in a brawl outside the stadium last weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fight - a violent confrontation between individuals or groups
  • Synonym: melee - a confused struggle or skirmish involving many people
  • Antonym: peace - absence of violence or conflict

Etymology

From Middle English 'braulen', to quarrel noisily, probably of Scandinavian origin.

2. VERB

/ brɔl / (AmE), / brɔːl / (BrE)
to fight or quarrel in a noisy, disorderly, or violent way, especially in a public place
to fight or quarrel noisily

Examples

Several fans began to brawl in the stands after the controversial call.
The two groups brawled outside the nightclub, attracting the attention of the police.
They were thrown out of the bar for brawling and disturbing the peace.

Synset Examples

The bar keeper threw them out, but they continued to wrangle on down the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fight - to engage in physical combat
  • Degree Lower: squabble - to argue noisily about something trivial
  • Degree Higher: melee - a confused fight, often involving many people

Etymology

From Middle English *braul* (quarrel, disturbance), possibly from Old French *brailler* (to yell, shout).
