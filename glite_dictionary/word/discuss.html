to discuss

1. VERB

/ dɪˈskʌs / (AmE), / dɪˈskʌs / (BrE)
to consider or examine a subject in speech or writing, especially in detail
consider or examine something in speech or writing

Examples

We plan to discuss the new project at the next meeting.
The committee discussed possible solutions for over an hour.
She enjoys discussing books with her friends.

Synset Examples

The author talks about the different aspects of this question
The class discussed Dante's ‘Inferno’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debate - to discuss something, often with opposing viewpoints
  • Synonym: to talk about - to speak about a subject
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to

Etymology

From Latin 'discussus', from 'discutere' meaning to strike asunder, examine.

2. VERB

/ dɪˈskʌs / (AmE), / dɪˈskʌs / (BrE)
to talk about something with one or more people, especially in detail or to exchange ideas or opinions
talk about something in detail

Examples

Let's discuss the details of the project at tomorrow's meeting.
They sat down to discuss their vacation plans over dinner.
The manager discussed the issue with his team before making a decision.

Synset Examples

We discussed our household budget

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debate - to argue about something, formally or informally
  • Antonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to something

Etymology

From Latin 'discussus', past participle of 'discutere' (to shake apart, examine).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00815640-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dɪˈskʌs / (AmE), / dɪˈskʌs / (BrE)
to talk about something with someone, often considering its details or aspects
talk about something; examine details

Examples

We need to discuss our strategy before making any decisions.
The committee will discuss the proposal at tomorrow's meeting.
They sat down to discuss their concerns openly and honestly.

Synset Examples

I had lunch with my coworker to discuss the presentation.
Let’s discuss the project details tomorrow.
They quietly discussed the plan in the corner.
They discussed the plot over dinner excitedly.
They discussed the problem perspicaciously during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debate - to argue about something, often formally
  • Synonym: address - to deal with or give attention to an issue
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something

Etymology

From Latin 'discutere' (to shake apart, investigate), via Old French 'discuter'.
