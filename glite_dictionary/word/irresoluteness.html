irresoluteness

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈrɛzəluːtnəs / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛzəluːtnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being uncertain or indecisive; lack of firmness or determination
lack of firmness or determination

Examples

Her irresoluteness in making important decisions frustrated her colleagues.
The leader's irresoluteness became apparent during the crisis, as he failed to take decisive action.
His irresoluteness made it difficult for the team to move forward with the project.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: resolution - the quality of being firm and determined
  • Synonym: indecisiveness - the quality of being unable to make decisions quickly

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'in-' (not) + 'resolutus', past participle of 'resolvere' (to resolve).
