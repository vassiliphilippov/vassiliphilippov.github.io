unequivocal

1. ADJ

/ ʌnɪˈkwɪvəkəl / (AmE), / ʌnɪˈkwɪvəkəl / (BrE)
leaving no doubt; clear and unambiguous in meaning or intention.
clear and unambiguous

Examples

The board issued an unequivocal statement denying the rumors.
Her support for the proposal was unequivocal and unwavering.
The evidence provided an unequivocal answer to the question.

Synset Examples

the plain and unequivocal language of the laws

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ambiguous - open to more than one interpretation; not having one obvious meaning
  • Synonym: clear - easy to perceive, understand, or interpret

Etymology

From Latin 'aequivocus' (ambiguous), with prefix 'un-' meaning 'not.'
