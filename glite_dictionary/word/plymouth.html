Plymouth

1. PROPN

/ ˈplɪməθ / (AmE), / ˈplɪməθ / (BrE)
a coastal town in Massachusetts, United States, founded by English Pilgrims in 1620 and known as the site of one of the earliest successful colonies in North America
historic U.S. town founded by Pilgrims in 1620

Examples

The Pilgrims landed at Plymouth in 1620, establishing one of the earliest English settlements in America.
Many tourists visit Plymouth each year to see Plymouth Rock and learn about colonial history.
Thanksgiving traditions in the U.S. are often linked to the early settlers of Plymouth.

Etymology

Named after Plymouth, England, from which the Pilgrims departed.
