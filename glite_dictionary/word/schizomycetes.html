Schizomycetes

1. NOUN

/ ˌskɪzoʊmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (AmE), / ˌskɪtsəʊmʌɪˈsiːtiːz / (BrE)
a former classification used for a class of bacteria, especially those reproducing by fission; now obsolete in scientific taxonomy.
obsolete taxonomic class of bacteria

Examples

The term Schizomycetes was long used to describe bacteria that reproduce by fission.
Modern microbiology no longer classifies bacteria as Schizomycetes, but the term appears in older texts.
Research into Schizomycetes contributed to early understanding of bacterial life cycles.

Etymology

From Greek 'schizo-' (split) + 'mycetes' (fungi), referring to their method of reproduction by splitting.

2. NOUN

/ skɪˌzoʊmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (AmE), / skɪˌtsəʊmʌɪˈsiːtiːz / (BrE)
a former class of bacteria comprising various unicellular or non-filamentous microorganisms, now obsolete in bacterial taxonomy
obsolete class of bacteria
archaic medical

Examples

The term Schizomycetes is now largely obsolete in modern bacterial classification.
Many early microbiologists studied Schizomycetes before bacterial taxonomy was revised.
In nineteenth-century textbooks, bacteria were often grouped as Schizomycetes.

Etymology

From Greek 'schizo-' (split) + 'mycetes' (fungi); formerly thought to be fungi.
