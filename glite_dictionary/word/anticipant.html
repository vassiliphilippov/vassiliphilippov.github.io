anticipant

1. ADJ

/ ænˈtɪs.ɪ.pənt / (AmE), / ænˈtɪs.ɪ.pənt / (BrE)
having or showing a strong feeling of eager anticipation or expectation
showing eager anticipation

Examples

The crowd stood in an anticipant silence waiting for the announcement.
Her eyes were anticipant as she watched the door for his return.
There was an anticipant energy in the team before the championship game.

Synset Examples

an expectant hush

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expectant - having or showing an excited feeling that something is about to happen

Etymology

From Latin 'anticipans', present participle of 'anticipare' (to anticipate).

2. NOUN

/ ænˈtɪsəpənt / (AmE), / ænˈtɪsɪpənt / (BrE)
a person who anticipates or looks forward to something; one who expects or predicts
one who anticipates or expects

Examples

As an anticipant of the new policy, she prepared her team for upcoming changes.
The anticipant investors waited eagerly for the company's earnings report.
Each anticipant in the audience hoped to be the first to hear the announcement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anticipator - one who anticipates or expects

Etymology

From Latin anticipantem, present participle of anticipare (to take (care of) beforehand).

Synset POS: n
