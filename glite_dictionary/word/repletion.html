repletion

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈpliːʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈpliːʃən / (BrE)
the state of being excessively full after eating or drinking
state of excessive fullness

Examples

After the holiday feast, a sense of repletion made it difficult to move from the table.
Many diets try to avoid that feeling of repletion that comes with overeating.
He regretted his repletion after sampling every dessert at the buffet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satiety - the feeling of being satisfied or full after eating

Etymology

From Latin repletio (filling up), from replere (to fill again).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈpliʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈpliːʃən / (BrE)
the state of being completely full, especially after eating or drinking; fullness or satiation
state of being fully satisfied or full

Examples

After the holiday feast, a sense of repletion settled over the entire family.
He pushed his plate away, overcome by repletion and unable to eat another bite.
Excessive repletion can sometimes leave you feeling sluggish and uncomfortable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satiety - the feeling of being completely full, especially after eating
  • Antonym: emptiness - the state of being empty

Etymology

From Latin 'repletio', from 'replere' (to fill again, fill up).
