fellowship

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛloʊˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈfɛləʊʃɪp / (BrE)
an association or group of people who share common interests, beliefs, or activities
group sharing interests or beliefs

Examples

She joined a fellowship of writers who met weekly to discuss their work.
The new members felt welcomed into the church's fellowship.
Many scientists value the sense of fellowship that develops at professional conferences.

Synset Examples

the message was addressed not just to employees but to every member of the company family
the church welcomed new members into its fellowship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: community - a group of people living together or sharing common interests

Etymology

From Old English 'feolaga' (companion) and 'scip' (ship, state), modeled after the word 'fellow'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɛloʊˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈfɛləʊʃɪp / (BrE)
A grant of money awarded by a university, foundation, or other agency for advanced study or research.
grant for study or research

Examples

She received a fellowship to conduct research at Oxford University.
Many postdoctoral researchers rely on a fellowship to fund their studies.
The university offers a prestigious fellowship for graduate students each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scholarship - a grant or payment made to support a student's education, awarded on the basis of academic or other achievement

Etymology

From Old English 'felaghscipe', influenced by 'fellow' and '-ship'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɛloʊˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈfɛləʊʃɪp / (BrE)
the state of being with others in a friendly, supportive, or companionable way; companionship or friendship
companionship; friendly association

Examples

After moving to a new city, she longed for the fellowship of old friends.
The hikers found warmth and fellowship by the campfire after a long day.
Many people join clubs to enjoy the fellowship and sense of belonging.

Synset Examples

he missed their company
he enjoyed the society of his friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: companionship - the feeling of friendship or fellowship with others
  • Antonym: solitude - the state of being alone

Etymology

From Old English 'feolaga' (partner) and Middle English 'felaweshipe', influenced by 'fellow'.

Synset POS: n
