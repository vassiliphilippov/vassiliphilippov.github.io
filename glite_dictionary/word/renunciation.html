renunciation

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌnʌnsiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌnʌnsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the formal act of giving up or rejecting something, especially by declaration; the act of relinquishing a right, claim, or belief
formal act of giving up or rejecting

Examples

Her renunciation of her inheritance surprised the entire family.
The monk's renunciation of worldly possessions was sincere and final.
Through a public renunciation, the leader stepped down from all official roles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abandonment - the act of abandoning or giving up something
  • Synonym: rejection - the act of refusing to accept or acknowledge something
  • Antonym: acceptance - the act of consenting or agreeing to something

Etymology

From Latin renuntiatio (an announcing back, a renouncing), from renuntiare ('announce; renounce').

3. NOUN

the formal rejection of a belief, cause, or way of life, especially religious beliefs, often in favor of an opposing cause or belief
formal rejection of a belief or cause

Examples

The ceremony marks the renunciation of earthly desires for spiritual pursuit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rejection - the dismissing or refusing of a proposal, idea, etc.
  • Antonym: acceptance - the act of agreeing to or approving something

4. NOUN

formal act of giving something up

Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abandonment - the act of leaving or giving up completely
  • Antonym: acceptance - the act of consenting to receive or undertake something offered

Etymology

From Latin renuntiatio (a pronouncing, reporting), from renuntiare (to announce back, reject).
