Khmer

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmɛr / (AmE), / kəˈmɛə / (BrE)
the official language of Cambodia, belonging to the Mon-Khmer branch of the Austroasiatic language family
the language of Cambodia

Examples

Many Cambodians speak Khmer as their first language.
She is taking a course to learn how to read and write Khmer.
The ancient temple inscriptions are written in classical Khmer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cambodian - the language spoken in Cambodia (alternative term)

Etymology

From Khmer ខ្មែរ (Khmer), the people and their language.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06950125-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ kəˈmɛr / (AmE), / kəˈmɛə / (BrE)
a member of the predominant ethnic group of Cambodia
member of Cambodia's major ethnic group

Examples

The majority of Cambodians are Khmer, sharing a rich cultural heritage.
The Khmer have a long history and unique traditions.
Many Khmer people practice Theravada Buddhism as their main religion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cambodian - a native or inhabitant of Cambodia

Etymology

From Khmer ខ្មែរ (khmɛː), the self-designation of the people/language.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09753139-n
Synset POS: n
