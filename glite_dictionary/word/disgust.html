disgust

1. NOUN

/ dəˈskʌst / (AmE), / dɪsˈɡʌst / (BrE)
A strong feeling of revulsion or profound disapproval aroused by something unpleasant or offensive.
strong feeling of revulsion or disapproval

Examples

She looked at the rotten food with obvious disgust.
His cruel remarks filled her with disgust.
There was a note of disgust in his voice when he spoke about the corruption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revulsion - a sense of intense disgust or repulsion
  • Antonym: admiration - a feeling of wonder, pleasure, or approval

Etymology

From Old French *desgouster* (to have a distaste for), from des- (expressing reversal) + *gouster* (to taste), from Latin *gustare* (to taste).
