myrmecophile

1. NOUN

/ mɜrˈmɛkəˌfaɪl / (AmE), / mɜːˈmiːkəʊfaɪl / (BrE)
An organism, often an insect, that habitually lives in association with ants, especially within their nests.
organism living with ants

Examples

The butterfly larvae are myrmecophiles that rely on ants for protection and nourishment.
Several beetle species are well-known myrmecophiles, spending their entire lives inside ant colonies.
Scientists discovered a new myrmecophile during their expedition in the tropical rainforest.

Etymology

From Greek 'myrmēx' (ant) + 'philos' (loving, fond of).
