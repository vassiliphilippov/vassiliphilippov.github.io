iconoscope

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪkənoʊˌskoʊp / (AmE), / aɪˈkɒnəskəʊp / (BrE)
an early type of television camera tube that converted images into electrical signals, used in the first practical television cameras
early television camera tube

Examples

The iconoscope revolutionized television by making live broadcasting possible.
Scientists demonstrated the functioning of the iconoscope at the exhibition.
Before solid-state cameras, broadcasters used an iconoscope to capture moving images.

Etymology

From Greek 'eikon' (image) and 'skopein' (to look or examine).
