Podocarpus amara

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊdəˈkɑrpəs əˈmɑrə / (AmE), / ˌpɒdɔːˈkɑːpəs əˈmɑːrə / (BrE)
A large, fast-growing tropical evergreen tree with large, glossy lance-shaped leaves, native to rainforests of Sumatra, the Philippines, and northern Queensland.
tropical evergreen tree species

Examples

The Podocarpus amara thrives in the rainforests of northern Queensland, where it can reach impressive heights.
Botanists studied the leaves of Podocarpus amara for their glossy appearance and unique structure.
Local guides pointed out a mature Podocarpus amara growing near the riverbank in Sumatra.

Etymology

From genus name Podocarpus (Greek podos 'foot' + karpos 'fruit'), species name amara (Latin for 'bitter').
