oculism

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.kjə.lɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɒ.kjʊ.lɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The practice or profession of an oculist; treatment of diseases of the eye.
practice of an oculist; eye treatment

Examples

Early medical texts often mention oculism as an important specialty for treating eye disorders.
She studied oculism before ophthalmology became the more modern term for eye care.
Advancements in oculism greatly improved the success rate of cataract surgeries in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ophthalmology - the branch of medicine concerned with the eye and its diseases

Etymology

From Latin oculus (eye) + -ism (practice or doctrine).
