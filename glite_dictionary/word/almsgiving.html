almsgiving

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːmzˌɡɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɑːmzˌɡɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of voluntarily giving money, food, or other help to people in need, especially as a religious practice
giving money or help to the poor

Examples

Many religions encourage almsgiving as a central moral duty.
The tradition of almsgiving is especially emphasized during religious festivals.
Her regular almsgiving provided food and medicine for families in her community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charity - voluntary giving to those in need, typically through organizations

Etymology

From Middle English almes-giving, combining 'alms' (from Old English ælmesse, from Latin eleemosyna) and 'giving'.
