accountability

1. NOUN

/ əˌkaʊntəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / əˌkaʊntəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The state of being responsible for one's actions and expected to justify them to others.
responsibility to justify actions

Examples

The new policy increases accountability for managers in the organization.
Public officials must demonstrate accountability to the people they serve.
Effective accountability helps prevent corruption in government institutions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: responsibility - the state or fact of having a duty to deal with something
  • Synonym: liability - the state of being legally responsible

Etymology

From 'account' + '-ability', influenced by Middle French 'acomptable' (accountable).
