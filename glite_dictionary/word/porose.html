porose

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɔːroʊs / (AmE), / ˈpɔːrəʊs / (BrE)
having or forming a continuous series of small openings or pores, especially in biological tissues or structures.
having a series of pores

Examples

The surface of the mushroom was distinctly porose, making it easy to identify.
Certain sponges have a porose structure that allows water to circulate freely.
Researchers examined the porose layer under a microscope to study its filtration properties.

Synset Examples

a porose hymenium

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porous - having minute spaces or holes through which liquid or air may pass

Etymology

From Latin 'porosus', from 'porus' (pore).
