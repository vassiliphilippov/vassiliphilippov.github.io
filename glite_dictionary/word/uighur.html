Uighur

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːgər / (AmE), / ˈwiːɡə / (BrE)
an alphabetic script derived from Aramaic, historically used to write the Uighur language and other Central Asian languages
historical Central Asian writing system

Examples

Many manuscripts from Central Asia were written in the Uighur script.
The Uighur script was adapted for use by the Mongols in their own writing system.
Linguists study the evolution of the Uighur alphabet and its influence on neighboring scripts.

Etymology

From the name of the Uighur people; the script ultimately derives from Aramaic through Sogdian.

Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ ˈwiːɡər / (AmE), / ˈwiːɡə / (BrE)
A member of a Turkic ethnic group native to Xinjiang and neighboring regions, especially in northwestern China.
member of Turkic ethnic group in Xinjiang

Examples

Many Uighur people preserve their traditional culture despite external pressures.
The documentary explores the daily life of a young Uighur woman in Xinjiang.
Activists have spoken out about human rights concerns affecting the Uighur community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Uyghur - alternative spelling for a member of the Uighur ethnic group

Etymology

From Turkic Uygur, the name of the ethnic group.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09756018-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈwiːɡər / (AmE), / ˈwiːɡə / (BrE)
the Turkic language spoken mainly by the Uighur people in northwestern China
a Turkic language of northwestern China

Examples

He is fluent in both Mandarin and Uighur.
The Uighur language uses a script based on Arabic.
Many young people are learning Uighur to preserve their cultural heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Uyghur - alternative spelling for the Uighur language and people

Etymology

From Uighur, the name of the people who speak the language, ultimately from Old Turkic.

Source: WordNet
