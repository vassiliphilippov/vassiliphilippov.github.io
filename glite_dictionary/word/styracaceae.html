Styracaceae

1. NOUN

/ staɪrəˈkeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / stɪˌræsɪˈsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering shrubs and trees, widely distributed, belonging to the order Ebenales.
family of shrubs or trees in order Ebenales

Examples

The Styracaceae are known for their attractive flowers and resinous bark.
Botanists recently discovered a new species in the Styracaceae family in Southeast Asia.
Many garden enthusiasts appreciate the ornamental value of shrubs in the Styracaceae.

Etymology

From the type genus Styrax + Latin -aceae, a standard suffix for plant family names.

2. NOUN

/ staɪˌræsiˈi / (AmE), / staɪˌræsiˈi / (BrE)
a family of shrubs and trees widely distributed, belonging to the order Ebenales, including species such as storax.
family of shrubs and trees (botany)

Examples

The Styracaceae includes about eleven genera of flowering plants.
Many members of the Styracaceae are valued for their ornamental properties.
Researchers discovered a new species in the Styracaceae during their expedition.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Styrax with the suffix -aceae denoting a plant family.
