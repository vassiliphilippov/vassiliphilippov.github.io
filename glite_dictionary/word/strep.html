strep

1. ADJ

/ strɛp / (AmE), / strɛp / (BrE)
of or relating to infections or conditions caused by streptococci bacteria.
related to streptococci bacteria
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a strep test to confirm the diagnosis.
She developed a strep infection after her cold.
Many children get strep throat during the winter months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: streptococcal - of or relating to streptococcus bacteria or infections

Etymology

Shortened from 'streptococcus', from Greek 'streptos' (twisted) and 'kokkos' (grain, berry).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03153782-a

2. NOUN

/ strɛp / (AmE), / strɛp / (BrE)
A type of spherical, Gram-positive bacteria that occur in pairs or chains, known to cause diseases such as scarlet fever and tonsillitis.
disease-causing spherical bacteria
medical informal

Examples

The doctor said her sore throat was caused by strep and prescribed antibiotics.
Scientists are developing new treatments to combat strep infections.
Children are especially susceptible to illnesses caused by strep bacteria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Streptococcus - the full scientific name for strep bacteria

Etymology

Shortened from New Latin genus name Streptococcus.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01383685-n
Synset POS: n
