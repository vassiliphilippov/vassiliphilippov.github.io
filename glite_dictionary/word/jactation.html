jactation

1. NOUN

/ dʒækˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / dʒækˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
(medical) Extremely restless tossing and twitching of the body, especially as seen in severely ill patients.
extremely restless bodily tossing (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient's jactation was so severe that he required restraints for his safety.
In cases of high fever, jactation may be observed during the night.
Doctors noted persistent jactation in the report of the neurological symptoms.

Etymology

From Latin *jactatio* (a tossing or throwing), from *jactare* (to throw).
