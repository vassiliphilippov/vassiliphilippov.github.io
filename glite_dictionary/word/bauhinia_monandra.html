Bauhinia monandra

1. NOUN

/ baʊˈhɪniə moʊˈnændrə / (AmE), / baʊˈhɪniə məʊˈnandrə / (BrE)
a shrub or small tree native to northern South America, having clusters of pink flowers streaked with purple
a shrub with pink-purple flowers, Dutch Guiana native

Examples

The Bauhinia monandra is prized for its striking pink flowers in tropical gardens.
Many public parks feature Bauhinia monandra trees as ornamental plants.
Botanists are studying the Bauhinia monandra for its potential medicinal properties.

Etymology

From genus name Bauhinia, honoring Swiss botanists Johann and Gaspard Bauhin, and Latin 'monandra' meaning 'single-stamened'.
