flattering

1. ADJ

/ ˈflæt̬ɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflætərɪŋ / (BrE)
giving excessive or insincere praise, often to gain favor; obsequiously complimentary.
excessively complimentary, insincere praise

Examples

She gave him a flattering compliment that seemed more manipulative than genuine.
The salesman adopted a flattering tone to persuade the clients to buy more products.
He listened to the flattering remarks with a polite smile, unsure of their sincerity.

Synset Examples

they listened with flattering interest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fawning - excessively eager to please or praise
  • Antonym: insincere - not expressing genuine feelings

Etymology

Present participle of 'flatter,' from Old French 'flater' (to flatter), of Germanic origin.

2. ADJ

/ ˈflæt̬.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflæt.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
making someone or something look more attractive or appealing than usual; showing or representing to advantage.
making someone/something look better than usual

Examples

That dress is very flattering on you and really brings out your eyes.
Soft lighting can be remarkably flattering in portraits.
She chose a flattering angle for her profile picture.

Synset Examples

a flattering color

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unflattering - making someone or something look worse or less attractive

Etymology

From the verb 'flatter', which comes from Old French 'flater' (to flatter or caress).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00910883-a
Synset POS: a
