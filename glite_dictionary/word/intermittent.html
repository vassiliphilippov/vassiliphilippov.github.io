intermittent

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪntɚˈmɪtənt / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmɪtnt / (BrE)
Occurring at irregular intervals; not continuous or steady.
stopping and starting irregularly

Examples

The intermittent rain made it difficult to plan outdoor activities.
He suffered from intermittent back pain throughout the year.
Intermittent power outages disrupted the neighborhood all night.

Synset Examples

intermittent rain showers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: constant - happening all the time without interruption

Etymology

From Latin intermittens, intermittent-, present participle of intermittere, meaning 'to leave off, interrupt.'
