counterfeiter

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntərˌfɪtər / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəfɪtə / (BrE)
a person who makes illegal copies of money, documents, or other items, especially with the intent to deceive or defraud
person who makes illegal copies

Examples

The police arrested the notorious counterfeiter after discovering his secret printing press.
A skilled counterfeiter can make fake bills that are hard to detect.
The art world lost millions when a counterfeiter passed off copies as originals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forger - a person who produces fake documents, signatures, or works of art

Etymology

From 'counterfeit' (from Old French 'contrefaire', to imitate), with agent noun suffix '-er'.
