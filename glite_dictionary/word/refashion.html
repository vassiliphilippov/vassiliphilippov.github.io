to refashion

1. VERB

/ riˈfæʃən / (AmE), / riˈfæʃən / (BrE)
to make something into a new or different form; to remake or reshape
remake or reshape

Examples

The designer decided to refashion her old dress into a modern gown.
He hopes to refashion his public image after the scandal.
They refashioned the office to create a more collaborative workspace.

Synset Examples

She is remaking her image

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remake - make something again or differently
  • Synonym: to reshape - give a new shape or form to

Etymology

From prefix 're-' (again) + 'fashion' (to shape or make).
