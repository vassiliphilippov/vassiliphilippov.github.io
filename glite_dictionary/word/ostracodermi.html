Ostracodermi

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːstrəˈkɒdərmaɪ / (AmE), / ˌɒstrəkəʊˈdɜːmaɪ / (BrE)
an extinct group of armored jawless vertebrates from the Paleozoic era, known for their bony shields; their precise classification remains unclear
extinct armored jawless vertebrates

Examples

Fossils of Ostracodermi provide insights into the early evolution of vertebrates.
The distinctive armor plates of Ostracodermi have fascinated paleontologists since their discovery.
Unlike modern fish, Ostracodermi lacked jaws and possessed heavy bony shields.

Etymology

From Greek ostrakon (shell) + derma (skin), referring to their armored bodies.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑ.strə.kəˈdɜr.maɪ / (AmE), / ˌɒ.strə.kəˈdɜː.maɪ / (BrE)
an extinct group of armored jawless aquatic vertebrates from the Paleozoic era, known for their bony head shields; their classification within early vertebrate evolution remains uncertain.
extinct armored jawless vertebrate group

Examples

Fossils of Ostracodermi reveal detailed patterns of their protective bony plates.
Paleontologists debate the precise evolutionary relationships of Ostracodermi to other early vertebrates.
The discovery of new Ostracodermi fossils has shed light on vertebrate evolution.

Etymology

From Greek 'ostrakon' (shell) + 'derma' (skin), referring to their bony armor.
