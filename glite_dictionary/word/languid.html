languid

1. ADJ

/ ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪd / (AmE), / ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪd / (BrE)
displaying or having a disinclination for physical activity or effort; lacking spirit, energy, or enthusiasm.
lacking energy or vigor

Examples

She gave a languid smile as she reclined on the sofa, barely moving.
The summer heat made everyone in the office feel languid and unproductive.
He raised his hand in a languid gesture before sinking back into his chair.

Synset Examples

a lackadaisical attempt
a languid mood
a languid wave of the hand
a hot languorous afternoon

Related Terms

  • Antonym: energetic - having or showing a lot of energy and enthusiasm
  • Synonym: listless - lacking energy or enthusiasm

Etymology

From Latin languidus (faint, weak), from languere (to be weak or faint).
