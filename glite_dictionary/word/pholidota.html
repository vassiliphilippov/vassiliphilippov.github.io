Pholidota

1. NOUN

/ foʊ.lɪˈdoʊ.tə / (AmE), / fəʊ.lɪˈdəʊ.tə / (BrE)
A genus of mostly epiphytic orchids native to Indonesia and the western Pacific.
genus of epiphytic orchids

Examples

The Pholidota genus includes several species prized by orchid collectors for their unusual flowers.
Epiphytic orchids such as Pholidota thrive in the humid forests of Southeast Asia.
Botanists discovered a new Pholidota species on the slopes of an Indonesian volcano.

Etymology

From Greek 'pholis' (scale), referring to the scaly sheaths on some species.

A taxonomic order comprising the pangolins, scaly anteaters native to Africa and Asia.
order of mammals: pangolins

Examples

The order Pholidota consists of the unique, scale-covered mammals known as pangolins.
Scientists study Pholidota to understand their evolutionary relationship to other placental mammals.
