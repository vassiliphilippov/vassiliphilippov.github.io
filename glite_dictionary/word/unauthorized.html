unauthorized

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈɔθəˌraɪzd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɔːθəraɪzd / (BrE)
Not having official permission or approval.
lacking official approval

Examples

The guards detained anyone found in the building after hours as it was an unauthorized presence.
She was reprimanded for making an unauthorized withdrawal from the account.
The company issued a warning about unauthorized use of its trademark.

Synset Examples

an unauthorized strike
wildcat work stoppage

Related Terms

  • Antonym: authorized - having official permission or approval

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'authorized'. 'Authorize' is from Medieval Latin 'auctorizare', to give authority.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈɑːθəˌraɪzd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈɔːθəraɪzd / (BrE)
Not having official permission or approval; not officially allowed.
without official permission or approval

Examples

Access to the building is strictly prohibited for unauthorized personnel.
He was accused of making unauthorized changes to the document.
The company took action against unauthorized use of its trademark.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: authorized - having official permission or approval

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix un- (not) to 'authorized'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00180786-a
Synset POS: a
