Treasury

1. PROPN

/ ˈtrɛʒɚi / (AmE), / ˈtrɛʒəri / (BrE)
The US government department responsible for collecting revenue and managing federal finances.
US federal department for finances and revenue
american

Examples

The Treasury announced new measures to improve economic stability on Monday.
Officials from the Treasury met with lawmakers to discuss tax reforms.
The Treasury handles all federal revenue and public debts in the United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Department of the Treasury - The full official name of the US Treasury department

Etymology

From Old French *tresorie*, from Latin *thesaurus* (treasure, storehouse).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛʒəri / (AmE), / ˈtrɛʒəri / (BrE)
A government bond or other negotiable debt security issued by the United States government, which guarantees timely payment of interest and principal.
U.S. government debt security

Examples

Many investors consider Treasury bonds to be among the safest investments available.
The yield on a ten-year Treasury rose sharply after the economic report.
She diversified her portfolio with stocks and Treasurys to reduce risk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bond - a certificate of debt issued by a government or corporation

Etymology

From Old French *tresorie*; from Latin *thesaurus* (treasure).

Synset POS: n
