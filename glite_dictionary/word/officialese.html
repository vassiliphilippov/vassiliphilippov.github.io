officialese

1. NOUN

/ əˌfɪʃəˈliz / (AmE), / əˌfɪʃɪəˈliːz / (BrE)
the formal, often obscure and jargon-filled style of language used by government officials or bureaucracy
formal, obscure official language

Examples

Reports filled with officialese can be difficult for ordinary citizens to understand.
His memo was so full of officialese that no one could figure out what he actually wanted.
The committee urged the department to replace officialese with plain English in all their communications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bureaucratese - complex, jargon-laden language used in bureaucracy
  • Antonym: plain English - clear, straightforward English

Etymology

Formed from 'official' + '-ese', imitating the pattern of language names.
