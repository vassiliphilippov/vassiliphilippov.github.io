to qualify

1. VERB

/ ˈkwɑːlɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkwɒlɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to describe or characterize the qualities or peculiarities of someone or something
describe the qualities of

Examples

The committee tried to qualify his achievements in their official report.
She attempted to qualify the artwork as revolutionary within its genre.
Historians often qualify events to give them greater context and meaning.

Synset Examples

You can characterize his behavior as that of an egotist
This poem can be characterized as a lament for a dead lover

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to characterize - to describe the distinctive nature or features of

Etymology

From Medieval Latin qualificare, from Latin qualis (of what kind) + facere (to make).
