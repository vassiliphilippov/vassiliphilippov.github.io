machilid

1. NOUN

/ məˈkɪlɪd / (AmE), / məˈkɪlɪd / (BrE)
A wingless insect of the family Machilidae, often found in dark, moist environments and known for making erratic leaps when disturbed.
type of jumping, wingless insect

Examples

The machilid scurried beneath the decaying log when I lifted it carefully.
Scientists studied the behavior of the machilid in its natural, damp habitat.
When disturbed, a machilid can leap several centimeters to escape predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bristletail - primitive, wingless insect of the order Archaeognatha

Etymology

From Modern Latin Machilidae (family name), based on the genus Machilis.
