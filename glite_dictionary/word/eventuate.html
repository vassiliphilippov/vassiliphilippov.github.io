to eventuate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈvɛn.tʃuˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈvɛn.tjʊ.eɪt / (BrE)
to happen, develop, or result as a consequence; to occur in the end
result as a consequence

Examples

The crisis did not eventuate in a war, as many had feared.
Many complications can eventuate from an untreated infection.
It is hoped that these discussions will eventuate in a positive outcome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: result - to happen as an outcome or consequence
  • Synonym: to follow - to come after as a result or consequence

Etymology

From Late Latin 'eventuatus', past participle of 'eventuari', from 'eventus' (event, occurrence).
