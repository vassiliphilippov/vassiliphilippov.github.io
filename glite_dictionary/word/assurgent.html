assurgent

1. ADJ

/ əˈsɜrdʒənt / (AmE), / əˈsɜːdʒənt / (BrE)
(of a stem or leaf) growing or extending upward, especially at an angle rather than strictly vertical.
growing upward (botanical term)

Examples

The plant's assurgent stems allow it to receive more sunlight.
Some species have leaves that are distinctly assurgent, aiding them in climbing.
In the field guide, an assurgent branch was noted for its upward angle.

Synset Examples

an assurgent stem or leaf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ascending - moving or growing upward

Etymology

From Latin assurgere (to rise up), from ad- (to) + surgere (to rise).

2. ADJ

/ əˈsɜrdʒənt / (AmE), / əˈsɜːdʒənt / (BrE)
rising upward, especially from the sea; ascending, as depicted in heraldry or marine biology.
rising upward, especially from the sea

Examples

On the ancient map, a sea serpent is shown assurgent from the waves.
The family crest features a dolphin assurgent against a blue shield.
In some myths, islands are believed to be mountains slowly assurgent from the ocean.

Synset Examples

a seahorse assurgent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ascending - moving or going up; rising
  • Antonym: descending - moving or going down

Etymology

From Latin 'assurgens', present participle of 'assurgere' meaning 'to rise up'.
