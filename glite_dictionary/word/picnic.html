picnic

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪk.nɪk / (AmE), / ˈpɪk.nɪk / (BrE)
an occasion when people have a meal outdoors, usually for pleasure, often as part of a social gathering or outing
outdoor social meal or gathering

Examples

We're planning a family picnic at the park this Saturday.
The weather was perfect for a picnic by the riverbank.
She packed sandwiches and fruit for their afternoon picnic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barbecue - a meal or gathering at which food is cooked outdoors over an open fire
  • Synonym: banquet - a formal, elaborate meal for many people, often as part of a celebration

Etymology

From French 'piquenique', of uncertain origin, appearing in the late 17th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪkˌnɪk / (AmE), / ˈpɪk.nɪk / (BrE)
an informal meal eaten outdoors, especially during an outing or excursion
informal outdoor meal

Examples

We decided to have a picnic by the lake on Sunday afternoon.
The family packed sandwiches and drinks for their afternoon picnic in the park.
Isabella spread a blanket on the grass for their picnic as the sun began to shine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barbecue - an outdoor meal where food is cooked over an open fire or grill
  • Antonym: banquet - a formal, elaborate meal for many people

Etymology

From French pique-nique (a meal where everyone brings something), early 18th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɪk.nɪk / (AmE), / ˈpɪk.nɪk / (BrE)
an activity or situation that is very easy or pleasant to do or deal with, often used in negative phrases to mean something is difficult
something easy or pleasant

Examples

Marketing this product will be no picnic in the current economy.
Raising three kids on your own is certainly not a picnic.
He thought the exam would be a picnic, but it turned out to be quite challenging.

Synset Examples

marketing this product will be no picnic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breeze - something that is very easy to do
  • Synonym: walk in the park - an activity that is very easy or pleasant to do
  • Antonym: challenge - something that is difficult; the opposite of something easy

Etymology

From French pique-nique (a social outing with a shared meal), adopted into English in the 18th century.
