incorrectly

1. ADV

/ ɪn.kəˈrɛkt.li / (AmE), / ɪn.kəˈrɛkt.li / (BrE)
In a way that is not correct; with errors or inaccuracy.
in an inaccurate or wrong way

Examples

The student's answer was marked incorrectly by the teacher.
If you label the samples incorrectly, the experiment results may be invalid.
He assumed, incorrectly, that she was not interested in the project.

Synset Examples

to credit Lister with the first formulation of the basic principle of stratigraphy would be to bestow credit falsely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrongly - in an incorrect or mistaken way
  • Antonym: correctly - in an accurate or true way

Etymology

From 'incorrect' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'incorrectus' (uncorrected, uncorrect).

2. ADV

/ ɪnˈkɝːrɛktli / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɒrɛktli / (BrE)
In a way that is not correct; wrongly or erroneously.
in an incorrect manner; wrongly

Examples

The test was graded incorrectly, leading to confusion among the students.
If you enter your password incorrectly, you won’t be able to log in.
Several names were spelled incorrectly in the conference program.

Synset Examples

he decided to reveal the details only after other sources had reported them incorrectly
she guessed wrong

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrongly - in a way that is not correct
  • Antonym: correctly - in a correct manner

Etymology

Formed by adding the adverbial suffix -ly to 'incorrect'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00205553-r
Synset POS: r
