to saponify

1. VERB

/ səˈpɑːnɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / səˈpɒnɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to convert or become converted into soap by reaction with an alkali, especially through hydrolysis of fats or oils
convert to soap using alkali
medical

Examples

When mixed with an alkali, the fatty acids will saponify after several hours.
If the mixture is not properly heated, the oil may fail to saponify completely.
The laboratory experiment demonstrated how quickly lard can saponify under basic conditions.

Synset Examples

the oil saponified

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hydrolyze - to break down a compound by chemical reaction with water

Etymology

From French saponifier, from Latin sapo (soap) + -fy (make).

2. VERB

/ səˈpɑːnəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / səˈpɒnɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to convert (a fat or oil) into soap by reacting it with an alkali, resulting in the hydrolysis of the fat or oil into an acid and an alcohol
convert fat or oil into soap with alkali

Examples

The lab technician used sodium hydroxide to saponify the animal fat.
Certain oils will saponify readily when mixed with a strong alkali.
They saponified the lipid samples as part of the experiment.

Synset Examples

saponify oils and fats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hydrolyze - to cause to undergo hydrolysis

Etymology

From French 'saponifier', from Latin 'sapo' (soap) + '-ify'.

Source: WordNet
