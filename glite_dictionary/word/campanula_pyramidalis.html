Campanula pyramidalis

1. NOUN

/ kæmˈpæn.ju.lə pɪˌræm.ɪˈdeɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / kamˈpan.ju.lə pɪˌram.ɪˈdeɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
A species of bellflower native to southeastern Europe, known for its tall, pyramidal clusters of blue flowers.
species of bellflower with pyramidal inflorescence

Examples

The striking Campanula pyramidalis brightened the garden with its towering blue flower spikes.
Gardeners admire Campanula pyramidalis for its showy, long-lasting blooms in midsummer.
At the botanical exhibit, we learned that Campanula pyramidalis is native to southeastern Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bellflower - any plant of the genus Campanula, with bell-shaped flowers

Etymology

From Latin: 'campanula' meaning 'little bell' (from the bell-shaped flowers) and 'pyramidalis' referring to a pyramidal shape.
