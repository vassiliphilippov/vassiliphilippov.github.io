to quetch

1. VERB

/ kwɛtʃ / (AmE), / kwɛtʃ / (BrE)
To express complaints or dissatisfaction; to grumble or complain.
complain or express dissatisfaction

Examples

He tends to quetch about the weather whenever it rains.
Instead of finding solutions, she would rather quetch over minor inconveniences.
The children began to quetch when their playtime was cut short.

Synset Examples

My mother complains all day
She has a lot to kick about

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance
  • Synonym: grumble - to complain in a bad-tempered way
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
