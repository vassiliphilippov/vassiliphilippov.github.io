indisposed

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˈspoʊzd / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈspəʊzd / (BrE)
slightly ill or unwilling to do something; feeling unwell.
slightly ill or unwell

Examples

I'm feeling a bit indisposed and might leave work early today.
She was indisposed and unable to attend the meeting.
If he appears indisposed, please let him rest.

Synset Examples

my poor ailing grandmother
feeling a bit indisposed today
you look a little peaked
feeling poorly
a sickly child
is unwell and can't come to work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ill - suffering from an illness or disease
  • Synonym: unwell - not feeling healthy or well
  • Antonym: well - in good health

Etymology

From Latin 'indispositus' meaning 'not arranged, out of order'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˈspoʊzd / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈspəʊzd / (BrE)
(usually followed by 'to') unwilling or reluctant to do something; disinclined.
unwilling or reluctant

Examples

He was clearly indisposed to accept any compromise on the matter.
She seemed indisposed to discuss her personal life with strangers.
The chairman was indisposed to approve the proposal without further consideration.

Synset Examples

antipathetic to new ideas
averse to taking risks
loath to go on such short notice
clearly indisposed to grant their request

Related Terms

  • Synonym: averse - having a strong feeling of opposition or dislike
  • Synonym: antipathetic - feeling or showing a strong aversion
  • Antonym: inclined - willing or favorable towards something
  • Antonym: disposed - willing or prepared to do something

Etymology

From in- (not) + disposed, based on Latin disponere (to arrange).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01296665-s
Synset POS: s
