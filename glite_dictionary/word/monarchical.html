monarchical

1. ADJ

/ məˈnɑrkɪkəl / (AmE), / məˈnɑːkɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to, characteristic of, or suitable for a monarch or monarchy.
relating to a monarch or monarchy

Examples

The country adopted a monarchical system after centuries of republican rule.
His monarchical gestures reflected a sense of authority and dignity.
The ceremony was full of monarchical pomp and tradition.

Synset Examples

monarchical gestures
monarchal pomp

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monarchic - relating to a monarch or monarchy
  • Antonym: republican - relating to a republic or its system of government

Etymology

From Late Latin monarchicus, from Greek monarkhikos (of a monarch), based on monarch.

2. ADJ

/ məˈnɑrkɪkəl / (AmE), / məˈnɑːkɪkəl / (BrE)
ruled by or relating to a system in which a monarch holds supreme power; characteristic of or pertaining to monarchy
having a system ruled by a monarch

Examples

The country has a monarchical form of government where the king is the head of state.
Many monarchical systems have transitioned to constitutional monarchies in modern times.
The palace was built during the height of monarchical rule in Europe.

Synset Examples

monarchal government
monarchical systems

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monarchal - of or relating to a monarchy or monarch
  • Antonym: republican - relating to a political system without a monarch, typically with elected leaders

Etymology

From Late Latin monarchicus, from Greek monarkhikos, from monarkhia (monarchy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00721783-s
Synset POS: s
