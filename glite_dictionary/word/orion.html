Orion

1. PROPN

/ ɔˈraɪ.ən / (AmE), / əˈraɪ.ən / (BrE)
a prominent constellation located on the celestial equator, east of Taurus, containing the stars Betelgeuse and Rigel.
constellation with Betelgeuse and Rigel

Examples

Orion is easy to find in the winter night sky because of its bright belt of stars.
Ancient civilizations used Orion as a marker for seasonal changes.
Betelgeuse marks the shoulder of Orion in the constellation.

Etymology

From Greek Ὠρίων (Ōríōn), a mythical hunter represented in the stars.

2. PROPN

/ ɔːˈraɪ.ən / (AmE), / əˈraɪ.ən / (BrE)
(In Greek mythology) a giant hunter who was placed among the stars as a constellation.
Greek mythological hunter; constellation

Examples

Many legends describe Orion as the great hunter who chased the Pleiades across the night sky.
The constellation Orion is easily recognizable by its 'belt' of three stars.
In Greek mythology, Orion was slain by Artemis and placed among the stars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constellation - a group of stars forming a recognizable pattern

Etymology

From Greek Ὠρίων (Ōríōn), name of the mythological hunter.
