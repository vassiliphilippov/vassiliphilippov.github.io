meat

1. NOUN

/ mit / (AmE), / miːt / (BrE)
the most important or essential part of an idea, discussion, or experience; the central or core element
essential or most important part

Examples

Let's get to the meat of the issue and stop wasting time on details.
The meat of her argument was very convincing.
Most readers skip the introduction and go straight to the meat of the article.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: core - the central part of something
  • Synonym: essence - the intrinsic nature or indispensable quality of something
  • Synonym: gist - the substance or essence of a speech or text

2. NOUN

/ mit / (AmE), / miːt / (BrE)
the edible inner part of a seed, grain, nut, or fruit stone, especially as opposed to the shell or outer covering
edible part inside a seed or nut

Examples

The meat of the almond is rich in healthy fats and protein.
She carefully cracked the walnut shell to extract the meat inside.
Pistachios are enjoyed for their delicious green meat.

Synset Examples

black walnut kernels are difficult to get out of the shell

3. NOUN

/ miːt / (AmE), / miːt / (BrE)
the flesh of animals, including fish and birds, used as food
animal flesh used as food

Examples

The restaurant offers a wide selection of meat dishes, including chicken, beef, and lamb.
Many people choose to avoid meat for health or ethical reasons.
She bought fresh vegetables and meat at the farmer's market.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: fish - cold-blooded vertebrate used as food
  • Degree Lower: poultry - domestic fowl, such as chickens and turkeys, used as food
  • Degree Higher: beef - meat from cattle
  • Degree Higher: pork - meat from pigs
  • Degree Higher: lamb - meat from young sheep

Etymology

From Old English mete (food, meat), related to German 'Mett' (minced pork).
