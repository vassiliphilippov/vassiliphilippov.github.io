gladsomeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlæd.səm.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɡlæd.səm.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being joyful or full of pleasure
state of being joyful or pleased

Examples

The children’s gladsomeness was evident as they played together in the sunshine.
She greeted her old friend with such gladsomeness that everyone in the room smiled.
Despite the rainy weather, his gladsomeness never faded throughout the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joyfulness - the state of being full of joy
  • Antonym: gloominess - the state of being unhappy or sad

Etymology

Formed from 'gladsome' + '-ness'; 'gladsome' from Old English 'glæd' (bright, cheerful) + '-some'.
