udometer

1. NOUN

/ juˈdɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / juːˈdɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used for measuring the amount of precipitation, such as rainfall
instrument measuring rainfall

Examples

The meteorologist checked the udometer to record the rainfall from last night’s storm.
Our weather station uses a digital udometer to measure precipitation with great accuracy.
After the downpour, the udometer showed over two inches of rain had fallen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rain gauge - an instrument for measuring the amount of rainfall

Etymology

From Latin 'udus' (wet) + English '-meter' (measuring device).
