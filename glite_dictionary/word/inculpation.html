inculpation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.kʌlˈpeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kʌlˈpeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of accusing someone of wrongdoing or fault; the state of being held responsible for a lapse or misdeed
the act of accusing or blaming

Examples

The defense attorney argued that the inculpation of his client was unsupported by the evidence.
After the accident, the company faced inculpation for failing to follow safety regulations.
His unexpected arrival led to the inculpation of several others who were present during the incident.

Synset Examples

his incrimination was based on my testimony
the police laid the blame on the driver

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incrimination - the act of making someone appear guilty of a crime or wrongdoing
  • Antonym: exculpation - the act of clearing someone from blame or accusation

Etymology

From Latin *inculpare* (to accuse of blame), from *in-* (in) + *culpa* (fault).
