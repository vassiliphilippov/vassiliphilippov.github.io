infinitesimal

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˌfɪnəˈtɛsəməl / (AmE), / ɪnˌfɪnɪˈtɛsɪməl / (BrE)
extremely small; so small as to be almost impossible to measure or calculate
extremely small; almost immeasurable

Examples

The difference between the two measurements was infinitesimal, almost too small to detect.
Scientists study infinitesimal particles that cannot be seen with the naked eye.
Her chances of winning were infinitesimal, but she decided to try anyway.

Synset Examples

two minute whiplike threads of protoplasm
reduced to a minute scale

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minute - extremely small; very tiny
  • Antonym: enormous - very large; huge

Etymology

From Latin 'infinitesimus' (meaning 'infinitely small part'), from 'infinitus' (infinite).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.fɪ.nəˈtɛs.ə.məl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.fɪ.nɪˈtɛs.ɪ.məl / (BrE)
(mathematics) A variable quantity that approaches zero as a limit, often used in calculus to denote quantities so small that they can be considered as nearly zero, but not exactly zero.
mathematical variable approaching zero
medical

Examples

The concept of an infinitesimal is fundamental to the development of calculus.
Physicists sometimes model changes as if they occur in infinitesimals to simplify calculations.
An infinitesimal can be thought of as a quantity smaller than any real number, but not exactly zero.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limit - the value that a function or sequence approaches as the input or index approaches some value
  • Synonym: differential - an infinitesimal change in a variable in calculus

Etymology

From Latin 'infinitesimus' (the thousandth part), superlative of 'infinitus' (infinite).
