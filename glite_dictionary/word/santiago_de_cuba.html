Santiago de Cuba

1. PROPN

/ sænˌtjɑːɡoʊ də ˈkuːbə / (AmE), / sænˌtjɑːɡəʊ də ˈkjuːbə / (BrE)
a port city in southeastern Cuba, known as an industrial center and for its historical significance
port city in southeastern Cuba

Examples

The band played a concert in Santiago de Cuba during their Caribbean tour.
Many visitors are drawn to the vibrant music scene in Santiago de Cuba.
Sugar is one of the main exports from Santiago de Cuba's busy harbor.

Etymology

From Spanish, named after Saint James (Santiago) and Cuba, the island nation.
