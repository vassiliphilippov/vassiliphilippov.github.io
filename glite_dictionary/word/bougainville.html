Bougainville

1. PROPN

/ ˌboʊɡənˈvɪl / (AmE), / ˌbəʊɡənˈvɪl / (BrE)
an island in the southwestern Pacific Ocean, the largest of the Solomon Islands, and a province of Papua New Guinea
island and province in Papua New Guinea

Examples

The people of Bougainville voted overwhelmingly in favor of independence.
Lush rainforests cover much of Bougainville Island in Papua New Guinea.
During World War II, Bougainville was the site of significant military operations.

Etymology

Named after Louis Antoine de Bougainville, French explorer (1729–1811).
