pusillanimousness

1. NOUN

/ ˌpjuːsɪˈlænɪməsnəs / (AmE), / ˌpjuːsɪˈlænɪməsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of lacking courage or being easily frightened or cowardly
lack of courage; cowardice

Examples

His pusillanimousness became apparent when he refused to support his friends in times of trouble.
The manager's pusillanimousness prevented her from addressing the team's critical issues directly.
Some leaders are known more for their pusillanimousness than for their decisiveness and strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowardice - lack of bravery or courage; being easily frightened
  • Antonym: bravery - courageous behavior or character

Etymology

From Latin pusillanimis (very small-minded) + -ness.
