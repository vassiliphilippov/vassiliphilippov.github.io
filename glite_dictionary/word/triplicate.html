triplicate

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪplɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈtrɪplɪkət / (BrE)
one of three identical copies, or any of a set of three things that are exactly alike
one of three identical copies

Examples

Please complete the form in triplicate for our records.
All contracts must be signed in triplicate before submission.
Each receipt was produced in triplicate for accounting purposes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: duplicate - one of two identical copies
  • Degree Higher: quadruplicate - one of four identical copies

Etymology

From Latin 'triplicatus', past participle of 'triplicare' (to triple), from 'tri-' (three) + 'plicare' (to fold).

2. VERB

/ ˈtrɪplɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈtrɪplɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to make three copies of something; to reproduce threefold
make three copies

Examples

Please triplicate this document before the meeting.
The assistant triplicated the files for each department's review.
We need to triplicate the forms for administrative purposes.

Synset Examples

triplicate the letter for the committee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duplicate - make an exact copy of something
  • Synonym: triplicate - existing in three copies or parts (adjective/noun use)

Etymology

From Latin 'triplicatus', past participle of 'triplicare' (to triple), from 'tri-' (three) + 'plicare' (to fold).
