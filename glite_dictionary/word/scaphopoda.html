Scaphopoda

1. NOUN

/ skəˈfɑːpədə / (AmE), / skəˈfɒpədə / (BrE)
a small class of bilaterally symmetrical marine mollusks, commonly known as tooth shells, characterized by their tubular, tusk-shaped shells
class of marine mollusks (tooth shells)

Examples

The Scaphopoda are easily identified by their elongated, tubular shells.
Researchers collected several specimens of Scaphopoda from the sandy ocean floor.
Fossils of Scaphopoda provide valuable information about ancient marine environments.

Etymology

From modern Latin, from Greek 'skaphē' (boat) + 'pous' (foot), referring to the shape and movement of these mollusks.

2. NOUN

/ skəˈfɑpədə / (AmE), / skæˈfɒpədə / (BrE)
a class of slender, bilaterally symmetrical marine mollusks that have tusk-shaped shells, commonly known as tooth shells
class of marine mollusks (tooth shells)

Examples

The Scaphopoda are distinguished by their tubular, tusk-shaped shells found in marine environments.
Unlike other mollusks, members of Scaphopoda burrow into sand using their elongated foot.
Fossil records show that Scaphopoda have existed since the Devonian period.

Etymology

From Greek 'skaphe' (boat) + 'pous, pod-' (foot), referring to the shape and locomotion of these mollusks.
