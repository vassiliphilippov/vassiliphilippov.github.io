sertraline

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.trəˌliːn / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.trəˌliːn / (BrE)
a selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor (SSRI) used as an antidepressant, commonly prescribed to treat depression, anxiety, and related disorders
an antidepressant medication (SSRI)
medical

Examples

My doctor prescribed sertraline to help manage my anxiety symptoms.
After starting sertraline, she noticed a gradual improvement in her mood.
Some people may experience side effects when taking sertraline for depression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zoloft - brand name for sertraline
  • Synonym: SSRI - class of selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors
  • Synonym: fluoxetine - another common SSRI antidepressant (Prozac)
