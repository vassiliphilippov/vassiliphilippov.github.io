stretcher

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɛtʃər / (AmE), / ˈstrɛtʃə / (BrE)
a stone or brick laid with its long side exposed, especially one forming the top of a wall or building
stone or brick laid lengthwise

Examples

The mason carefully placed a stretcher on top of the wall to finish the row.
Historic buildings often feature decorative stretchers along their upper edges.
The architect specified that only limestone stretchers should be used for the façade.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstrɛtʃər / (AmE), / ˈstrɛtʃə / (BrE)
a wooden frame over which canvas is stretched and fixed, used as a support for oil painting
frame for stretching canvas in painting

Examples

The artist prepared her canvas by mounting it onto a stretcher before beginning the painting.
After removing the finished work, he replaced the torn stretcher with a sturdier one.
A high-quality stretcher ensures the canvas remains taut over time.

3. NOUN

/ ˈstrɛtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈstrɛtʃə / (BrE)
a device used to make something larger or looser by stretching it, such as shoes or gloves
device for stretching items

Examples

He used a stretcher to loosen his new leather shoes.
A glove stretcher was needed to fit her hands comfortably.
Some shoe stores offer a stretcher service for tight footwear.

Etymology

From stretch + -er, denoting an agent or thing that stretches.

4. NOUN

/ ˈstrɛtʃər / (AmE), / ˈstrɛtʃə / (BrE)
a lightweight framework with a canvas or fabric surface, used for carrying ill, injured, or deceased people
device for carrying people, especially the injured

Examples

The paramedics quickly placed the injured hiker on a stretcher and carried him to the ambulance.
During the rescue, two firefighters lifted the patient onto a stretcher for transport to the hospital.
After collapsing at the marathon, she was taken away on a stretcher by medical staff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gurney - a wheeled stretcher used in hospitals

Etymology

From stretch (v.) + -er, in reference to the canvas being stretched across the frame.
