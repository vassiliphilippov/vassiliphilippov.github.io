faun

1. NOUN

/ fɔn / (AmE), / fɔːn / (BrE)
an ancient Roman deity depicted as a man with horns, pointed ears, and a goat's tail, associated with forests and fertility; equivalent to a Greek satyr
Roman mythological woodland deity

Examples

The ancient statue depicts a mischievous faun playing the pipes in the forest.
In Roman mythology, a faun was believed to inspire fear and wonder among travelers.
The painting featured a dancing faun with a playful, goat-like appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satyr - Greek mythological woodland deity, counterpart to a faun

Etymology

From Latin 'faunus', a rural deity.
