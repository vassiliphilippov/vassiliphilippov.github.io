tirade

1. NOUN

/ taɪˈreɪd / (AmE), / ˈtaɪəreɪd / (BrE)
a long, angry speech of criticism or accusation
angry, critical speech

Examples

After the meeting, her tirade against the new policy left everyone in stunned silence.
He launched into a tirade about the failures of the current administration.
Tired of his boss's daily tirades, Marcus decided to look for another job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diatribe - a forceful and bitter verbal attack
  • Synonym: harangue - a lengthy and aggressive speech

Etymology

From French *tirade* (a volley, a speech), from Italian *tirata* (a pulling, a long speech).
