viatical

1. ADJ

/ vaɪˈætɪkəl / (AmE), / vaɪˈatɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the purchase, sale, or transfer of life insurance policies held by terminally ill individuals, often at a discount to the face value
relating to sale of insurance policies by the terminally ill

Examples

The viatical market expanded rapidly in the 1990s as more companies began purchasing life insurance policies from terminally ill individuals.
A viatical company can offer immediate cash to policyholders in exchange for their life insurance benefits.
Regulators have introduced new legislation to oversee viatical settlements and protect both buyers and sellers.

Synset Examples

viatical business
viatical companies
National Viatical Association

Etymology

From Latin viaticum (provision for a journey), relating to money or resources, later adapted in U.S. insurance terminology.
