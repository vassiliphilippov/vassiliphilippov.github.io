burned-out

1. ADJ

/ bɝːnd aʊt / (AmE), / bɜːnd aʊt / (BrE)
extremely tired or exhausted, especially as a result of prolonged stress or overwork
exhausted from prolonged stress or overwork

Examples

After years in a high-pressure job, Mark felt completely burned-out.
Teachers who do not take breaks can become burned-out by the end of the school year.
She was burned-out before she was thirty, despite her early career success.

Synset Examples

she was burned-out before she was 30

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhausted - very tired; drained of energy
  • Antonym: energized - full of energy and enthusiasm

2. ADJ

/ bɝnd aʊt / (AmE), / bɜːnd aʊt / (BrE)
(of a device or component) no longer functioning because excessive heat or friction has caused damage.
no longer working due to heat or friction

Examples

The burned-out motor made it impossible to start the machine.
She replaced the burned-out bulb in the living room lamp.
After years of use, the television ended up with a burned-out picture tube.

Synset Examples

a burned-out picture tube

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broken - no longer working; in a state of disrepair
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02132438-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ bɝːnd aʊt / (AmE), / bɜːnd aʊt / (BrE)
Destroyed or badly damaged by fire; reduced to ruins by burning.
destroyed or damaged by fire

Examples

The firefighters examined the burned-out remains of the warehouse.
She walked past several burned-out cars abandoned along the street.
Only the burned-out shell of the house was left after the blaze.

Synset Examples

a row of burned houses
a charred bit of burnt wood
a burned-over site in the forest
barricaded the street with burnt-out cars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charred - partially burned so as to blacken the surface
  • Degree Lower: scorched - damaged by burning slightly but not destroyed
