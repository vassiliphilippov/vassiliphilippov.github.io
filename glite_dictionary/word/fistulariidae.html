Fistulariidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪstjʊˌlɛrˈiːəˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌfɪstjʊˌlɑːrɪˈiːdɪiː / (BrE)
A family of elongated, marine fishes known as cornetfishes, found in tropical and subtropical seas.
family of marine fishes (cornetfishes)
medical

Examples

The Fistulariidae are easily recognized by their long, tubular snouts and slender bodies.
Many species in the Fistulariidae family are found in coral reef habitats worldwide.
Scientists studied the distribution of Fistulariidae across the Indo-Pacific region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornetfish - long, slender marine fish of the family Fistulariidae

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on the genus Fistularia.
