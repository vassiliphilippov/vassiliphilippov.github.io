battlewagon

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætlˌwæɡən / (AmE), / ˈbætlˌwæɡən / (BrE)
a large, heavily armored warship, especially one equipped with large-caliber guns; battleship
large, heavily armored warship
informal

Examples

The massive battlewagon cut through the waves, its guns ready for action.
During the Second World War, the navy deployed its most advanced battlewagons to the Pacific theater.
Enthusiasts marveled at the restored battlewagon now serving as a museum ship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battleship - a large armored warship with a main battery consisting of heavy caliber guns

Etymology

Formed from 'battle' and 'wagon,' alluding metaphorically to a war vehicle; first used in early 20th century naval slang.
