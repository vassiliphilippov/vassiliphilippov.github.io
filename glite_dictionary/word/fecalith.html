fecalith

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːkəˌlɪθ / (AmE), / ˈfiːkəlɪθ / (BrE)
A hard, stone-like mass of fecal matter that forms in the intestines or bowel.
hard mass of fecal matter
medical

Examples

The surgeon discovered a fecalith blocking the patient's appendix during the procedure.
A large fecalith can cause severe abdominal pain and requires medical attention.
Doctors identified the cause of the obstruction as a fecalith after reviewing the scan.

Etymology

From Latin 'faex' (dregs, sediment) + Greek 'lithos' (stone).
