pulverisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌpʌlvɚɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpʌlvəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
A solid substance broken down into fine, loose particles through the process of pulverizing.
powder; substance in fine particles

Examples

The pulverisation of the ore made it easier to extract valuable minerals.
Years of weathering resulted in the pulverisation of the rocks into fine dust.
The baker used the pulverisation of sugar to create a smooth icing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powder - Fine dry particles produced by crushing or grinding a solid substance

Etymology

From Latin 'pulvis' (dust) via French 'pulvérisation'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpʌlvərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpʌlvəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of grinding or crushing something into a fine powder or dust
the process of making powder

Examples

The pulverisation of coal is an essential first step in most combustion processes.
Repeated pulverisation of the minerals resulted in an extremely fine dust.
Modern cement industries rely on the pulverisation of raw materials for efficient mixing.

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ ˌpʌlvəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpʌlvəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of crushing or grinding something into a fine powder or dust; complete destruction by turning it into powder.
act of reducing to powder; total destruction

Examples

The pulverisation of the rock made it easy to transport and use in construction.
The old building faced total pulverisation during the demolition process.
Modern machines allow the quick pulverisation of grain into fine flour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pulverization - alternate spelling, especially in American English
  • Synonym: crushing - breaking something into smaller pieces by force
  • Synonym: fragmentation - breaking into small parts

Etymology

From Latin 'pulvis' meaning 'dust', via French 'pulvérisation'.
