incommodiousness

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnkəˈmoʊdiəsnəs / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəˈməʊdiəsnəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being inconvenient or uncomfortable.
the state of being inconvenient or uncomfortable

Examples

The incommodiousness of the small attic room made it difficult for anyone to sleep comfortably.
She complained about the incommodiousness of the train seats during the long journey.
Despite its elegance, the building's incommodiousness deterred many potential tenants.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: comfort - a state of physical ease and freedom from pain or constraint
  • Synonym: inconvenience - the quality of causing trouble or difficulty

Etymology

From incommodious + -ness; ultimately from Latin incommodus (inconvenient).
