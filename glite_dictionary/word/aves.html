Aves

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪviːz / (AmE), / ˈeɪviːz / (BrE)
The class of vertebrate animals that comprises all modern birds.
the class of birds in zoology

Examples

Scientists classify all modern birds in the class Aves.
Fossil records provide insight into the evolutionary history of Aves.
There are more than 10,000 known species within the class Aves worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bird - a warm-blooded egg-laying vertebrate animal distinguished by feathers and wings

Etymology

From Latin 'aves' meaning 'birds'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈeɪviːz / (AmE), / ˈeɪviːz / (BrE)
the taxonomic class of animals that includes all birds, characterized by feathers, beaks, and laying hard-shelled eggs
the class of animals comprising all birds

Examples

Many paleontologists study the evolution of Aves from dinosaur ancestors.
Characteristics such as feathers and beaks are unique to Aves.
Among vertebrate animals, only members of Aves can fly extensively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bird - a warm-blooded egg-laying vertebrate distinguished by feathers, wings, and beaks; synonym for members of class Aves

Etymology

From Latin *aves* (birds), plural of *avis* (bird).
