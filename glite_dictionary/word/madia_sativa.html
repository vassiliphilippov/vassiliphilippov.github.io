Madia sativa

1. NOUN

/ ˌmeɪdiə səˈtiːvə / (AmE), / ˌmeɪdiə səˈtiːvə / (BrE)
A South American herb with sticky glandular leaves, cultivated as a source of madia oil.
South American herb; source of madia oil

Examples

Madia sativa is grown in some regions for the extraction of its oil-rich seeds.
The sticky leaves of Madia sativa help protect the plant from herbivores.
Researchers have studied Madia sativa for its potential in sustainable agriculture.

Etymology

From the genus name Madia, from Chilean Spanish, plus Latin sativa (cultivated).
