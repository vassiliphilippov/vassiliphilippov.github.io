unholy

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈhoʊli / (AmE), / ʌnˈhəʊli / (BrE)
Not hallowed or consecrated; not regarded as holy or sacred.
not holy or consecrated

Examples

The ruins are an unholy site, avoided by worshippers for centuries.
The ceremony cannot be held on unholy ground.
He was accused of building his house on unholy land.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: holy - consecrated or sacred

Etymology

From un- (not) + holy.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈhoʊli / (AmE), / ʌnˈhəʊli / (BrE)
(of a person or behavior) morally wrong; having committed unrighteous or impious acts.
morally wrong; impious

Examples

The villain in the play is truly unholy, showing no remorse for his crimes.
She warned them not to engage in unholy practices forbidden by their faith.
The unholy actions of the gang shocked the entire community.

Synset Examples

a sinful person

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sinful - guilty of sin; morally wrong
  • Antonym: holy - morally and spiritually excellent; sacred

Etymology

From Old English un- (not) + hālig (holy).

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈhoʊli / (AmE), / ʌnˈhəʊli / (BrE)
extremely wicked, cruel, or evil; resembling or befitting hell or the devil.
extremely evil or cruel

Examples

The villain's unholy laughter echoed through the dark hallways, sending chills down everyone's spine.
The dictator was known for his unholy acts of cruelty against his own people.
There was an unholy gleam in her eyes as she plotted her revenge.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diabolical - extremely wicked or cruel; devilish
  • Synonym: fiendish - extremely cruel or wicked
  • Synonym: satanic - relating to or characteristic of Satan; extremely evil

Etymology

From un- (not) + holy, originally meaning 'not sacred', later extended metaphorically to mean 'wicked'.
