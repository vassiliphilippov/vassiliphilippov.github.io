candlenut

1. NOUN

/ ˈkændlˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈkændlˌnʌt / (BrE)
the oily seed of the candlenut tree, used as a source of oil in varnishes and sometimes for ornamental or culinary purposes
seed of the candlenut tree; source of oil

Examples

She roasted the candlenut before adding it to the traditional dish.
The varnish was made more durable with oil extracted from the candlenut.
In some cultures, a whole candlenut was strung and burned like a candle.

Etymology

From candle + nut, referring to the nut's use as a primitive candle.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkændlˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈkændlˌnʌt / (BrE)
A large tree native to Southeast Asia, whose nuts yield oil used in varnishes, and whose nut kernels are sometimes strung together and used as candles.
tree with oily nuts used for candles and varnish

Examples

The candlenut tree is cultivated in many tropical regions for its useful oil.
Locals would string candlenuts together and burn them like a primitive candle.
The varnish was made from the oil extracted from the candlenut.

Etymology

From the tree's traditional use of its nuts as candles due to their high oil content.
