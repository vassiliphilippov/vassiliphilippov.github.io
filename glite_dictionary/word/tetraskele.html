tetraskele

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛtrəˌskiːl / (AmE), / ˈtɛtrəˌskiːl / (BrE)
A figure or symbol composed of four stylized human arms, legs, or bent lines radiating from a central point.
figure with four arms or lines radiating from a center

Examples

The ancient pottery was decorated with a prominent tetraskele in red paint.
A tetraskele is often seen in Greek art and symbolizes movement or cycles.
The museum’s display explained the use of the tetraskele symbol in early European cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swastika - an ancient symbol with bent arms radiating from a center
  • Degree Lower: triskelion - a symbol of three spiraling arms or legs around a center

Etymology

From Greek 'tetra-' meaning 'four' and 'skelos' meaning 'leg.'
