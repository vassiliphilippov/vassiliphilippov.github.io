tweak

1. NOUN

/ twiːk / (AmE), / twiːk / (BrE)
an act of pinching or slightly twisting something with the fingers
a pinch or twist with the fingers

Examples

She gave his ear a playful tweak before running away.
The teacher gave the boy's nose a gentle tweak as a joke.
He felt a sharp tweak on his shoulder when his friend tried to get his attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pinch - a squeeze or nipping action with the fingers
  • Synonym: twist - an act of turning something with the fingers

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'tweeken', frequentative of 'tweken' (to pinch, tug).

2. VERB

/ twiːk / (AmE), / twiːk / (BrE)
to pinch or squeeze sharply, especially with the fingers
pinch or squeeze sharply

Examples

She reached over to tweak his ear playfully.
He tweaked my nose as a joke.
The child giggled when her grandmother tweaked her cheek.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pinch - to grip tightly and sharply between two surfaces, especially the fingers

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'twikken' (to pinch).

be high on methamphetamine (slang)
slang american

4. VERB

/ twiːk / (AmE), / twiːk / (BrE)
to make small adjustments or improvements to something, often to achieve the best result
make small adjustments

Examples

She decided to tweak the recipe by adding a pinch of cinnamon.
The sound engineer spent an hour tweaking the microphone settings for the perfect tone.
You may need to tweak your resume before you send out more applications.

Synset Examples

fine-tune the engine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fine-tune - make small and precise adjustments
  • Synonym: to adjust - alter or move slightly to achieve desired fit or function

Etymology

Originally a dialect word meaning 'to pinch or pull sharply'; modern sense of 'adjust finely' from technical jargon (mid-20th century).
