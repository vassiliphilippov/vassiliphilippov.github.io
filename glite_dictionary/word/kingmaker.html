kingmaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪŋˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈkɪŋˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
a person who has great influence in appointing or supporting leaders, especially by helping them gain power or office
person with the power to make leaders

Examples

Many considered him a kingmaker behind the scenes of the political campaign.
The media often described the influential donor as a kingmaker in state elections.
As a power broker, she enjoyed her role as a kingmaker rather than holding office herself.

Synset Examples

the Earl of Warwick was the first kingmaker

Etymology

From 'king' + 'maker', originally used to describe the Earl of Warwick in 15th-century England.
