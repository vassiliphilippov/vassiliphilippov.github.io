tarsal

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑr.səl / (AmE), / ˈtɑː.səl / (BrE)
any of the bones comprising the tarsus, which forms the ankle and back part of the foot in humans and other vertebrates
bone of the ankle or hindfoot
medical

Examples

An injury to a tarsal can affect your ability to walk comfortably.
The doctor explained that the broken tarsal would require several weeks to heal.
Archaeologists discovered well-preserved tarsal bones at the ancient burial site.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: carpal - any of the bones forming the wrist

Etymology

From New Latin *tarsalis*, from Greek *tarsos* (ankle, flat of the foot).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɑrsəl / (AmE), / ˈtɑːsəl / (BrE)
relating to the tarsus, the group of small bones in the foot between the ankle and the metatarsals
relating to the tarsus of the foot
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the injury was to one of the tarsal bones in his foot.
Archaeologists found tarsal fragments that helped identify the animal species.
She studied the tarsal structure carefully in her anatomy class.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: carpal - relating to the carpus (wrist)

Etymology

From New Latin tarsalis, from Latin tarsus (ankle, instep, edge of the eyelid), from Ancient Greek tarsos (flat of the foot, edge)
