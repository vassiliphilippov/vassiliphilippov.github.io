descensus

1. NOUN

/ dɛˈsɛnsəs / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛnsəs / (BrE)
(medicine) the downward displacement or prolapse of an organ, such as the uterus, from its normal position.
downward displacement of an organ
medical

Examples

The gynecologist diagnosed a descensus of the uterus in the patient.
Treatment for descensus may include physical therapy or surgical intervention.
A severe descensus can cause significant discomfort and require medical attention.

Etymology

From Latin 'descensus' (a going down, descent), from 'descendere' (to descend).
