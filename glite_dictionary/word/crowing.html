crowing

1. ADJ

/ ˈkroʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person) boastful or showing self-importance, often in an annoying or exaggerated way.
boastful; showing self-importance

Examples

After winning the match, his crowing attitude irritated his teammates.
She couldn't stand his crowing about his promotion.
The crowing remarks from the manager made the staff uncomfortable.

Synset Examples

big talk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boastful - showing excessive pride and self-satisfaction
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest opinion of one's own importance

2. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
boastful or triumphant talk, especially after a victory or achievement
boastful or triumphant talk

Examples

After winning the match, his crowing could be heard across the locker room.
The team's crowing after their comeback annoyed the defeated players.
Tired of her constant crowing, her colleagues stopped congratulating her successes.

Synset Examples

his brag is worse than his fight
whenever he won we were exposed to his gasconade

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boasting - talking with excessive pride and self-satisfaction
  • Antonym: modesty - the quality of not being boastful or arrogant

Etymology

From the verb 'crow,' meaning 'to boast' or 'exult,' which derives from the noisy cry of a cock.
