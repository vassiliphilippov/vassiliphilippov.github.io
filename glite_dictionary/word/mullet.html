mullet

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌlɪt / (AmE), / ˈmʌlɪt / (BrE)
a marine fish found in warm waters, typically living near the bottom and characterized by two barbels on the chin
a warm-water marine fish with chin barbels

Examples

Fishermen often catch mullet along the sandy shores during summer.
The chef prepared a traditional dish using grilled mullet and fresh herbs.
Juvenile mullet can be found in schools near the river estuary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goatfish - a brightly colored marine fish with a pair of long barbels under the chin

Etymology

From Old French 'molet', from Latin 'mullus' (type of fish).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌlət / (AmE), / ˈmʌlɪt / (BrE)
the flesh of a marine or freshwater fish, valued for its lean, mild flavor and eaten as food
lean fish flesh used as food

Examples

The restaurant featured grilled mullet with lemon and herbs on its seafood menu.
He bought a fresh mullet from the market for tonight’s dinner.
In Mediterranean cuisine, mullet is often served with a side of roasted vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snapper - edible fish valued for lean white flesh

Etymology

From Old French mulet, from Latin mullus (red mullet).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmʌlɪt / (AmE), / ˈmʌlɪt / (BrE)
a freshwater or coastal fish with a spindle-shaped body, found in many parts of the world and valued as food
a type of freshwater or coastal food fish

Examples

The chef prepared grilled mullet with lemon and herbs for dinner.
We spotted several mullet swimming near the riverbank.
Fishermen in the region often catch mullet early in the morning.

Etymology

From Old French 'mulet', from Latin 'mullus' (red mullet).
