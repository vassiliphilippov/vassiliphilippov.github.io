paleoclimatology

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪlioʊˌklaɪməˈtɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌpeɪlɪəʊˌklaɪməˈtɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The scientific study of climates in the geological past, as determined from geological, biological, and chemical evidence.
study of past climates
medical

Examples

Advances in paleoclimatology have shed light on changes in Earth’s climate over millions of years.
Researchers use ice cores and tree rings as evidence in paleoclimatology studies.
A new book explores the fundamentals of paleoclimatology for students and scientists alike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: climatology - the scientific study of climate

Etymology

From Greek 'palaios' (ancient) + 'clima' (region, climate) + 'logy' (study of).
