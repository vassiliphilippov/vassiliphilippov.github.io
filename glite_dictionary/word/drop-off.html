drop-off

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɑp ˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈdrɒp ɒf / (BrE)
a noticeable decrease in level, quality, or performance
noticeable decline or reduction

Examples

There was a significant drop-off in student enrollment after the tuition increase.
The sales manager was concerned about the recent drop-off in customer satisfaction.
We noticed a drop-off in productivity as the project deadline approached.
