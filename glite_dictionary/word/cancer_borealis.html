Cancer borealis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænsər bɔːˈreɪ.əlɪs / (AmE), / ˈkænsə bɔːˈreɪ.əlɪs / (BrE)
A large red deep-water crab native to the eastern coast of North America, also known as the Jonah crab.
deep-water red crab of eastern North America

Examples

Fishermen along the Atlantic coast routinely catch Cancer borealis for commercial sale.
The hard shell of Cancer borealis helps protect it from many predators in deep waters.
Marine biologists studied Cancer borealis to understand its adaptation to colder environments.

Etymology

From Latin: 'cancer' (crab) + 'borealis' (northern), descriptive of its range.
