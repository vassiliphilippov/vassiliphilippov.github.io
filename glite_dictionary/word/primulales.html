Primulales

1. NOUN

/ praɪˌmjuːˈleɪliːz / (AmE), / prɪˌmjuːˈleɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants that includes the families Primulaceae, Theophrastaceae, Myrsinaceae, and sometimes Plumbaginaceae, in some classifications
an order of flowering plants

Examples

Several species within the Primulales are cultivated for their attractive flowers.
Botanists debated the classification of the Primulales in the light of new genetic evidence.
The Primulales were once considered a more extensive order under older taxonomic systems.

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Primulales', based on 'Primula', the type genus.
