citole

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪtoʊl / (AmE), / ˈsɪtəl / (BrE)
a medieval stringed musical instrument, similar to a guitar, with a pear-shaped body and wire strings, popular in the 13th to 16th centuries
medieval stringed musical instrument

Examples

The museum displayed a rare citole from the 14th century, showing intricate carvings on its body.
During the performance, the musician played a replica citole, filling the hall with its distinct sound.
She studied the ways musicians tuned and played the citole in the Middle Ages.

Etymology

From Old French cistole, probably derived from Latin cithara (a kind of lyre or harp).
