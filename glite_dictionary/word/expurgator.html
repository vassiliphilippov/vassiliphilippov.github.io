expurgator

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.spɚˌɡeɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛk.spə.ɡeɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person who edits a text by removing obscene or offensive words or passages
person who removes offensive content from texts

Examples

The expurgator removed several passages deemed inappropriate for younger readers.
Victorian literature often passed through the hands of an expurgator before publication.
As an expurgator, she was responsible for ensuring the scripts adhered to broadcasting standards.

Synset Examples

Thomas Bowdler was a famous expurgator

Related Terms

  • Synonym: censor - a person who examines material and suppresses unacceptable parts

Etymology

From Latin expurgātor, from expurgāre (to cleanse thoroughly, purge).
