Emil Klaus Julius Fuchs

1. PROPN

/ ˈeɪmɪl klaʊs ˈjuːliəs fʊks / (AmE), / ˈeɪmɪl klaʊs ˈjuːliəs fʊks / (BrE)
(1911–1988) A German-born British physicist known for passing secret information about atomic bomb development to the USSR in the 1940s.
German-born British physicist and atomic spy

Examples

Emil Klaus Julius Fuchs was convicted of espionage for sharing atomic secrets with the Soviet Union.
The revelations about Emil Klaus Julius Fuchs shocked the scientific community in the 1950s.
History books often detail the actions of Emil Klaus Julius Fuchs during the Manhattan Project.
