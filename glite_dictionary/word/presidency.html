presidency

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛzɪdənsi / (AmE), / ˈprɛzɪdənsi / (BrE)
the period during which a person holds the office of president
the term of a president in office

Examples

During his presidency, the country underwent significant political reforms.
The presidency of Franklin D. Roosevelt lasted over twelve years.
Many historians debate the impact of her presidency on economic policy.

Synset Examples

things were quiet during the Eisenhower administration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: term - fixed period for which an office is held

Etymology

From Latin praesidentia, meaning 'presiding over' or 'supervision'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɛzɪˌdɛnsi / (AmE), / ˈprɛzɪdənsi / (BrE)
the office, position, or term of a president, especially in a government, organization, or institution
the office or term of a president

Examples

During his presidency, the country experienced significant economic growth.
She announced her candidacy for the presidency next year.
Term limits prevent anyone from holding the presidency for more than two consecutive terms.

Synset Examples

Andrew Jackson expanded the power of the presidency beyond what was customary before his time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: president - the elected head of a republic or organization

Etymology

From Latin 'praesidentia' (presiding), from 'praesidens' (presiding officer).

Synset POS: n
