Ptilonorhynchus violaceus

1. NOUN

/ ˌtaɪləˌnɔːˈrɪŋkəs vaɪoʊˈleɪsiəs / (AmE), / ˌtaɪlənɒˈrɪŋkəs vʌɪəˈleɪsiəs / (BrE)
A species of bowerbird native to southeast Australia, with males that are glossy violet-blue and females that are light grey-green.
species of Australian bowerbird

Examples

The male Ptilonorhynchus violaceus builds elaborate bowers to attract females.
A Ptilonorhynchus violaceus was spotted arranging blue objects around its display area.
Birdwatchers often hope to catch a glimpse of the vibrant Ptilonorhynchus violaceus in Australia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satin bowerbird - Common name for Ptilonorhynchus violaceus

Etymology

From Greek 'ptilon' (feather) + 'ornyx' (claw) and Latin 'violaceus' (violet-coloured).
