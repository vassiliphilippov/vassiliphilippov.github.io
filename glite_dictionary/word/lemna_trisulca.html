Lemna trisulca

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛmnə traɪˈsʌlkə / (AmE), / ˈlɛm.nə traɪˈsʌl.kə / (BrE)
A species of aquatic flowering plant in the duckweed family, commonly found in temperate regions worldwide except North America.
a species of aquatic flowering plant

Examples

Lemna trisulca can form dense floating mats on calm freshwater ponds.
Researchers observed the rapid spread of Lemna trisulca in the region's waterways.
Unlike some other duckweeds, Lemna trisulca often grows submerged just below the water surface.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Lemna' meaning duckweed and 'trisulca' meaning three-furrowed, referring to leaf shape.
