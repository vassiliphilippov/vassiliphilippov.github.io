august

1. ADJ

/ əˈgʌst / (AmE), / ɔːˈɡʌst / (BrE)
respected and impressive; having great dignity or honor
respected; dignified; impressive

Examples

The guests were seated in the august presence of the monarch.
The university's august tradition dates back for centuries.
She was impressed by the august panel of judges at the competition.

Synset Examples

revered holy men

Related Terms

  • Synonym: majestic - having or showing impressive beauty or dignity
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance

Etymology

From Latin 'augustus' (venerable, majestic), from 'augēre' (to increase).

2. ADJ

/ əˈɡʌst / (AmE), / ɔːˈɡʌst / (BrE)
having an impressive dignity, grandeur, or authority; befitting a lord or royalty
imposing or dignified; majestic

Examples

The palace stood in august splendor above the city, visible for miles.
She received them in an august chamber lined with portraits of her ancestors.
The judge carried himself with an august presence that commanded respect.

Synset Examples

heir to a lordly fortune
of august lineage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: majestic - having or showing impressive beauty or dignity
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of importance

From Latin 'augustus' (venerable, majestic).
