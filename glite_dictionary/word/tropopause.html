tropopause

1. NOUN

/ ˈtroʊpəˌpɔz / (AmE), / ˈtrɒpəˌpɔːz / (BrE)
the boundary region between the troposphere and the stratosphere in Earth's atmosphere, marking a change in temperature lapse rate
boundary between troposphere and stratosphere

Examples

The tropopause marks the upper limit of most weather phenomena in the atmosphere.
Pilots monitor the tropopause to anticipate turbulence during flights through different atmospheric layers.
At the tropopause, the temperature stops decreasing with altitude and usually becomes stable or increases slightly.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: stratosphere - the atmospheric layer above the troposphere
  • Degree Lower: troposphere - the lowest region of Earth's atmosphere

Etymology

From 'troposphere' + 'pause', indicating the pause or boundary between atmospheric layers.
