obeah

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.bi.ə / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.bi.ə / (BrE)
(in the Caribbean) a set of spiritual and magical practices involving folk healing, sorcery, and witchcraft, especially as practiced among people of African descent in the West Indies.
Caribbean folk magic and sorcery system

Examples

The villagers believed that obeah was responsible for the strange illness.
She visited an obeah man to seek protection from evil spirits.
Many colonial authorities once banned the practice of obeah due to its association with rebellion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voodoo - a religion involving magic and spirit worship, especially in Haiti and Louisiana

Etymology

Probably from West African Igbo *ọbia* or Ashanti *obayifo* ('sorcerer').

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.bi.ə / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.bi.ə / (BrE)
A set of religious beliefs and practices of African origin involving magic, witchcraft, and sorcery, especially as practiced in parts of the Caribbean and tropical Americas.
Caribbean religious practice involving witchcraft

Examples

Many communities in Jamaica have a longstanding belief in obeah and its powers.
The villagers accused her of practicing obeah after strange events began occurring.
He studied the history and traditions of obeah while traveling through the Caribbean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voodoo - A religion involving magic and ancestor worship, practiced chiefly in Haiti and the Haitian diaspora
  • Synonym: witchcraft - The practice of magic, especially for evil purposes

Etymology

From West African Igbo *obíā* (a kind of medicine or sorcery), entering English via Caribbean creole languages.
