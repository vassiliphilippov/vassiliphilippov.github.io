sandy

1. ADJ

/ ˈsændi / (AmE), / ˈsandi / (BrE)
Containing, covered with, or resembling sand; growing in or suited to sandy areas.
containing or resembling sand

Examples

The beach was known for its sandy shores and gentle waves.
Some plants thrive in sandy soil because it drains quickly.
After the dust storm, the car was left with a sandy coating.

Synset Examples

arenaceous limestone
arenaceous grasses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gritty - containing or covered with grit or sand
  • Antonym: rocky - containing or covered with rocks

2. ADJ

/ ˈsændi / (AmE), / ˈsandi / (BrE)
(of hair) pale yellowish to yellowish brown in color.
pale yellowish-brown (hair color)

Examples

Her sandy hair glowed in the afternoon sunlight.
He was known for his sandy locks and bright blue eyes.
Several young children with sandy hair played by the shoreline.

Synset Examples

flaxen locks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flaxen - (of hair) pale yellow or golden in color
  • Synonym: blonde - fair or pale yellow hair color

Etymology

From Old English sandig, meaning 'of sand', referring to the color resemblance.
