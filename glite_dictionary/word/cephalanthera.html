Cephalanthera

1. NOUN

/ sɛf.əˈlæn.θə.rə / (AmE), / ˌkɛf.əˈlæn.θə.rə / (BrE)
A small genus of terrestrial orchids native to temperate regions of the Old World, with slender stems and tuberous roots.
genus of temperate terrestrial orchids

Examples

Several species in the genus Cephalanthera can be found in European woodlands.
The white helleborine, a member of Cephalanthera, blooms in late spring.
Researchers documented the distribution of Cephalanthera orchids across the Mediterranean region.

Etymology

From Greek kephalē (head) + anthera (anther), referring to the flower's structure.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛfələˈnθɛrə / (AmE), / ˌkɛfələˈnθɪərə / (BrE)
a small genus of terrestrial orchids native to temperate regions of the Old World, known for their upright stems and white or pale flowers
a genus of orchids found in temperate Eurasia and North Africa

Examples

The rare Cephalanthera species can be found growing in shaded European woodlands.
Botanists recently discovered a new population of Cephalanthera in the mountains of northern Greece.
Many conservationists monitor the habitat of Cephalanthera orchids due to their declining numbers.

Etymology

From Greek 'kephalē' (head) and 'anthera' (anther), referencing the flower's structure.
