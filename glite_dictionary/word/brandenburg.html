Brandenburg

1. PROPN

/ ˈbrændənˌbɜːrɡ / (AmE), / ˈbrandənˌbɜːɡ / (BrE)
a former territory in northeast Germany, originally an electorate of the Holy Roman Empire, which expanded and became the core of the Kingdom of Prussia in 1701
historic region in northeast Germany

Examples

During the eighteenth century, Brandenburg played a crucial role in European politics.
The Elector of Brandenburg later became the King of Prussia.
Many castles in Brandenburg date back to the medieval era.

Etymology

From the name of the city of Brandenburg in Germany, itself from Slavic roots referring to a fortified place.
