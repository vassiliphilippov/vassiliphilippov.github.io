anasarca

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.əˈsɑːr.kə / (AmE), / ˌan.əˈsɑː.kə / (BrE)
A severe and generalized form of edema, with widespread swelling caused by accumulation of fluid in the subcutaneous tissues.
severe generalized swelling (edema)
medical

Examples

The patient developed anasarca as a result of severe heart failure.
Uncontrolled liver disease can sometimes lead to anasarca in advanced stages.
Doctors noted widespread anasarca during the physical examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edema - an abnormal accumulation of fluid in tissues

Etymology

From Greek anasarca, from ana- (throughout) and sark- (flesh).
