non-Catholic

1. NOUN

/ nɑn ˈkæθəlɪk / (AmE), / nɒn ˈkæθəlɪk / (BrE)
A person who is not a member of the Roman Catholic Church.
person who is not a Catholic

Examples

She was raised as a non-Catholic, but attended a Catholic school.
The church welcomes both non-Catholics and Catholics to its events.
Some traditions are unfamiliar to non-Catholic visitors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Catholic - a member of the Roman Catholic Church

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' (not) and 'Catholic'; 'Catholic' from Greek 'katholikos' (universal).
