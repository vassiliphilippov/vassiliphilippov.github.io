coigne

1. NOUN

/ kɔɪn / (AmE), / kɔɪn / (BrE)
the keystone or projecting stone at the corner of a building or in an arch, especially in architecture.
keystone or projecting stone (architecture)
archaic

Examples

The mason placed the coigne at the apex of the stone arch, completing its form.
Ancient cathedrals often feature intricately carved coignes at their corners and arches.
With careful precision, he shaped the coigne to fit perfectly into the structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keystone - the central stone at the summit of an arch, locking the whole together

Etymology

From Old French 'coigne', from Latin 'cuneus' (wedge).

2. NOUN

/ kɔɪn / (AmE), / kɔɪn / (BrE)
An adjustable metal or wooden wedge used by printers to securely lock a form in a chase.
printer's wedge for locking up type

Examples

He carefully tapped the coigne into position to hold the type firmly.
Without a coigne, the layout may shift during printing.
The old pressroom still had a drawer full of wooden coignes.

Etymology

From Middle French 'coing' meaning 'wedge' or 'corner', ultimately from Latin 'cuneus' (wedge).
