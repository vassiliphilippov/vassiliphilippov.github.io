vowellike

1. ADJ

/ ˈvaʊəlˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈvaʊ.əl.laɪk / (BrE)
having characteristics similar to those of a vowel sound, especially in phonetics.
resembling a vowel sound

Examples

The linguist explained the vowellike quality of the final 'r' in some dialects.
Some consonants, such as 'l' and 'r', are considered vowellike because of the way they are pronounced.
The child struggled with the vowellike sounds in certain words during speech therapy.

Synset Examples

the vowellike nature of ‘r’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vowel - a speech sound produced without audible constriction of the breath or friction in the vocal tract
  • Antonym: consonant - a basic speech sound in which the breath is at least partly obstructed
