to bequeath

1. VERB

/ bɪˈkwið / (AmE), / bɪˈkwiːð / (BrE)
to leave or give (property, money, etc.) to someone by will after one's death
leave property by will after death
legal

Examples

She decided to bequeath her rare book collection to the local library.
Upon his death, Mr. Turner bequeathed his fortune to his three children.
In her will, she bequeathed all her paintings to the art museum.

Synset Examples

My aunt bequeathed me all her jewelry
My grandfather left me his entire estate

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to inherit - to receive (property, title, etc.) as an heir at the death of the previous holder

Etymology

From Middle English 'bequethen', from Old English 'becwethan' (to declare, make known by will).
