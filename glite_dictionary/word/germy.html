germy

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɜrmi / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːmi / (BrE)
full of germs or microorganisms that can cause disease; dirty or unsanitary.
containing germs; unsanitary

Examples

The children refused to play in the germy sandbox at the park.
She always carries hand sanitizer for wiping down germy surfaces in public places.
After a week without cleaning, the kitchen sponge became incredibly germy.

Synset Examples

the water in New York harbor is oily and dirty and germy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clean - free from dirt, germs, or impurities

Etymology

Formed from 'germ' + '-y' (adjectival suffix).
