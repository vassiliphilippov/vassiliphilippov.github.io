unforbearing

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnfɔrˈbɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌnfɔːˈbɛərɪŋ / (BrE)
not willing to be patient or tolerant with others; lacking forbearance.
not patient or tolerant

Examples

Despite his reputation, he was often unforbearing with minor mistakes.
She grew increasingly unforbearing as deadlines approached.
An unforbearing attitude can discourage those who are struggling.

Synset Examples

she was unforbearing with the slower students

Related Terms

  • Antonym: forbearing - patient and restrained, showing self-control even when annoyed or provoked

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'forbearing.'
