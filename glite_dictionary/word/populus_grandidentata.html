Populus grandidentata

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːpjələs ˌɡrændˌaɪdɛnˈteɪtə / (AmE), / ˈpɒpjʊləs ˌɡrandɪdɛnˈtɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of aspen tree with a narrow crown, native to eastern North America, noted for its large, toothed leaves.
species of aspen tree in eastern North America

Examples

The leaves of Populus grandidentata are easily recognized by their large teeth.
Forests in the northeastern United States often contain stands of Populus grandidentata.
Conservationists are studying Populus grandidentata due to its importance for local wildlife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bigtooth aspen - Common name for Populus grandidentata, a tree with large, toothed leaves

Etymology

From Latin: 'populus' meaning 'poplar' and 'grandidentata' meaning 'large-toothed,' referring to the shape of the leaves.
