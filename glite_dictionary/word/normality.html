normality

1. NOUN

/ nɔrˈmæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / nɔːˈmæl.ə.ti / (BrE)
the state of being normal; being within the usual, typical, or expected range of function or behavior
state of being normal or typical

Examples

After months of disruption, life slowly returned to normality in the town.
Doctors monitored the patient's vital signs until normality was restored.
Economic normality may take years to achieve after such a crisis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abnormality - the state of being abnormal; deviation from the normal

Etymology

From Latin 'normalis' (according to a right angle, rule) via French.

/ nɔrˈmæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / nɔːˈmæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
A measure of concentration in chemistry, defined as the number of gram equivalents of solute per liter of solution.
chemical concentration measure
medical

Examples

The normality of the acid solution must be calculated before proceeding with the experiment.
They prepared a one normality solution by dissolving the required amount of solute.
Chemists often use normality when titrating acids and bases.

4. NOUN

/ nɔrˈmæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / nɔːˈmæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The condition of being normal or typical; the expected or usual state of things.
state of being normal or usual

Examples

After months of disruption, the city slowly returned to normality.
Travelers long for a sense of normality after spending weeks in unpredictable conditions.
Routine brings comfort because it restores normality to our daily lives.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abnormality - the state of being not normal or usual
  • Synonym: normalcy - the state of being normal, especially in American English

Etymology

From Latin 'norma' (a carpenter's square), via Late Latin 'normalitas'.
