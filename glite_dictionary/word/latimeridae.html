Latimeridae

1. NOUN

/ ləˌtɪməˈraɪdiː / (AmE), / ləˌtɪməˈraɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of extinct lobe-finned fishes, known primarily for the living coelacanth, the only surviving member.
family of fishes including the coelacanth

Examples

The discovery of the living Latimeridae shocked scientists who believed the family was extinct.
Members of the Latimeridae family have distinctive lobed fins and a unique evolutionary history.
Paleontologists study ancient fossils to learn more about prehistoric Latimeridae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coelacanth - a rare order of fish, the only living member of Latimeridae

Etymology

From the genus Latimeria, named after Marjorie Courtenay-Latimer, plus the taxonomic suffix -idae.

2. NOUN

/ ˌlætɪˈmɛrɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌlatɪˈmɛrɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of lobe-finned fishes, nearly all extinct except for the coelacanth.
family of ancient lobe-finned fishes

Examples

The discovery of a living Latimeridae member, the coelacanth, astonished scientists in the twentieth century.
Fossils belonging to Latimeridae have been found in ancient sedimentary rocks.
The survival of the coelacanth makes Latimeridae unique among prehistoric fish families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coelacanth - a rare deep-sea fish, the only living member of Latimeridae

Etymology

From genus Latimeria, named after British museum curator Marjorie Latimer, with the zoological family suffix -idae.
