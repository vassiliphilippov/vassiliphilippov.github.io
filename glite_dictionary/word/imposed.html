imposed

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpoʊzd / (AmE), / ɪmˈpəʊzd / (BrE)
(of rules, conditions, or requirements) established by authority as obligatory or enforced rather than chosen.
set by authority or force

Examples

The new regulations were imposed by the government despite public opposition.
Employees resented the imposed overtime schedule during the busy season.
The imposed restrictions changed the way the community celebrated the festival.

Synset Examples

the imposed taxation
rules imposed by society

Related Terms

  • Antonym: voluntary - done by free choice, not imposed by others

Etymology

Past participle of 'impose', from Latin 'imponere' (to place upon).
