slogger

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɔɡər / (AmE), / ˈslɒɡə / (BrE)
a person who works steadily and persistently, often at monotonous tasks for long hours
person who works persistently, often monotonously

Examples

Although the job isn't glamorous, the company relies on each slogger to keep things running smoothly.
He's a real slogger; he never quits until the work is finished, no matter how tedious.
The project demanded long hours, but being a natural slogger, she stuck with it until the end.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard worker - a person who works with diligence and effort

Etymology

From the verb 'slog' (to work hard), possibly of Scandinavian origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈslɑːɡər / (AmE), / ˈslɒɡə / (BrE)
a person who walks in a slow, heavy, and laborious way, as if burdened by effort
person who walks heavily and laboriously

Examples

After hours of hiking through the mud, Miles looked every bit the slogger as he trudged onward.
A group of determined sloggers made their way up the hill despite the pouring rain.
You could tell he was a slogger by the way each step seemed like a mighty effort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plodder - someone who walks or works slowly and steadily, especially in a dull or determined way

Etymology

From 'slog' meaning to walk with effort or plod, likely of Scandinavian origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10751505-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈslɑɡər / (AmE), / ˈslɒɡə / (BrE)
a boxer who is known for delivering powerful and hard punches, rather than for speed or technical skill
boxer who delivers hard punches

Examples

The young slogger stunned the crowd with a brutal left hook in the second round.
Although he lacked finesse, the slogger's power made him a dangerous opponent.
In the heavyweight division, a skilled slogger can end a match with a single punch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puncher - a boxer who relies on powerful punches
  • Degree Lower: boxer - a person who boxes professionally

Etymology

From 'slog' (to hit hard) + '-er'. 'Slog' of uncertain origin, attested since the 19th century.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
