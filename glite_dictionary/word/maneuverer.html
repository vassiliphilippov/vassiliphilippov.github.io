maneuverer

1. NOUN

/ məˈnuːvərɚ / (AmE), / məˈnjuːv(ə)rə / (BrE)
a person who is skilled at planning and carrying out movements or strategies, especially in situations requiring careful handling or negotiation
person skilled in maneuvering or strategy

Examples

He proved himself a brilliant maneuverer during the negotiations, outsmarting his rivals at every turn.
As a political maneuverer, she always seemed to know the right move to make.
The general was admired as a master maneuverer on the battlefield.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strategist - a person skilled in planning, especially military or political
  • Synonym: tactician - a person adept at tactics or maneuvering

Etymology

From 'maneuver' + '-er', originally from Old French 'manoeuvrer' (to work by hand).
