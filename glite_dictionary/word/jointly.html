jointly

1. ADV

/ ˈdʒɔɪntli / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔɪntli / (BrE)
in collaboration or cooperation with one or more others
together with others; in cooperation

Examples

The company and its suppliers worked jointly to improve production efficiency.
Their names were jointly listed as the inventors on the patent application.
The two states declared they would act jointly to address the water shortage.

Synset Examples

this paper was written jointly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: separately - not together; individually

Etymology

Formed from 'joint' (shared, combined) + '-ly', an adverbial suffix.

2. ADV

/ ˈdʒɔɪntli / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔɪntli / (BrE)
together with one or more other people, groups, or things; in combination or cooperation.
together with others; in combination

Examples

The two companies worked jointly on the new project to combine their expertise.
The award was presented jointly to both researchers for their groundbreaking discovery.
We decided to apply for the loan jointly instead of individually.

Synset Examples

our salaries collectively couldn't pay for the damage

Related Terms

  • Antonym: separately - not together; individually
  • Synonym: collectively - as a group; as a whole

Etymology

From 'joint' (from Old French, from Latin 'junctus', past participle of 'jungere' meaning 'to join') + '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00117989-r
Synset POS: r
