Barbados

1. PROPN

/ bɑrˈbeɪdoʊs / (AmE), / bɑːˈbeɪdɒs / (BrE)
an island country in the eastern Caribbean Sea, known for its parliamentary democracy and popular as a tourist destination
island country in the Caribbean

Examples

Many tourists choose Barbados for its beautiful beaches and vibrant culture.
After gaining independence in 1966, Barbados became a parliamentary democracy.
The cricket team from Barbados has produced several famous international players.

2. PROPN

/ bɑːrˈbeɪdoʊs / (AmE), / bɑːˈbeɪdɒs / (BrE)
an island country in the easternmost part of the Caribbean, about 300 miles north of Venezuela
island country in the Caribbean

Examples

We spent our honeymoon in Barbados and loved its beautiful beaches.
The economy of Barbados relies heavily on tourism and offshore finance.
She plans to study marine biology at a university in Barbados.

Etymology

From Portuguese or Spanish 'Barbados', meaning 'the bearded ones', referring to the island's fig trees with hanging roots.
