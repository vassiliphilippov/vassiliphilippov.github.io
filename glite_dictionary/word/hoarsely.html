hoarsely

1. ADV

/ ˈhɔrsli / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsli / (BrE)
in a rough or harsh-sounding voice, as if affected by a sore throat or fatigue
in a rough or husky voice

Examples

He tried to speak, but his voice came out hoarsely after hours of shouting.
She whispered hoarsely, barely able to make herself heard.
'Leave me alone,' he muttered hoarsely in the dim light.

Synset Examples

‘Excuse me,’ he said hoarsely

Related Terms

  • Antonym: softly - in a soft or gentle voice
  • Synonym: hoarse - having a rough or harsh-sounding voice
