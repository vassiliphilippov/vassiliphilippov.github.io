sesamoid

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛsəmɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɛsəmɔɪd / (BrE)
a small, round bone embedded within a tendon, typically found where a tendon passes over a joint, such as the kneecap (patella)
small bone in a tendon near a joint
medical

Examples

The kneecap is the largest sesamoid in the human body.
Some people develop a painful condition when a sesamoid bone becomes inflamed in the foot.
Doctors examined the athlete's swollen toe and found a fractured sesamoid.

Etymology

From Greek 'sēsamoeidēs' (like a sesame seed), from 'sēsamon' (sesame) + 'eidos' (form, shape).
