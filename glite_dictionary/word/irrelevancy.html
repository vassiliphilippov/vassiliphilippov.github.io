irrelevancy

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈrɛləvənsi / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛlɪvənsi / (BrE)
the state or quality of being not connected or related to the matter at hand
lack of relevance or connection

Examples

The lawyer objected to the irrelevancy of the witness's statement.
His remarks were dismissed because of their apparent irrelevancy to the main topic.
We should avoid bringing up issues of irrelevancy during our meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irrelevance - the lack of relevance or connection
  • Antonym: relevance - the quality of being closely connected or appropriate

Etymology

From 'irrelevant' + suffix '-cy,' after 'irrelevance.'
