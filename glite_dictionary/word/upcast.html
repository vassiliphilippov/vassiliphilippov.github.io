upcast

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌkɑːst / (BrE)
an air passage or ventilation shaft in a mine through which air is expelled
mine ventilation shaft

Examples

The miners inspected the upcast regularly to ensure proper ventilation throughout the tunnels.
Fresh air entered through the downcast and exited via the upcast after circulating in the mine.
A blockage in the upcast could pose serious safety risks for workers underground.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: downcast - a ventilation shaft in a mine through which fresh air enters

Etymology

From 'up' + 'cast', referring to air being 'cast up' or expelled upward.
