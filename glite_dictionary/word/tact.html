tact

1. NOUN

/ tækt / (AmE), / tækt / (BrE)
The ability to say or do things in a way that does not upset or offend other people.
sensitivity in dealing with others

Examples

She handled the criticism with great tact and avoided offending anyone.
It requires a lot of tact to negotiate delicate situations.
His lack of tact has led to several uncomfortable moments at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diplomacy - skill in dealing with people without causing bad feelings
  • Antonym: insensitivity - lack of concern for others' feelings or opinions

Etymology

From French *tact* (touch, sense of touch), from Latin *tactus* (touch).
