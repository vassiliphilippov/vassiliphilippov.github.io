Brassica rapa ruvo

1. NOUN

/ brəˈsɪkə ˈræpə ˈruːvoʊ / (AmE), / brəˈsɪkə ˈræpə ˈruːvəʊ / (BrE)
A plant of the species Brassica rapa, grown for its pungent edible leafy shoots, commonly used as a vegetable.
edible leafy plant species

Examples

Farmers in southern Italy often cultivate Brassica rapa ruvo for its flavorful shoots.
The traditional dish uses fresh Brassica rapa ruvo sautéed in olive oil and garlic.
Botanists classify Brassica rapa ruvo as a subspecies of turnip grown for culinary use.

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Brassica' meaning cabbage; 'rapa' meaning turnip; 'ruvo' distinguishing this subspecies.
