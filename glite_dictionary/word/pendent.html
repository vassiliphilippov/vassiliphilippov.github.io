pendent

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛn.dənt / (AmE), / ˈpɛn.dənt / (BrE)
an ornament that hangs from a piece of jewelry, such as a necklace or earring
hanging jewelry ornament

Examples

She wore a gold necklace with a sapphire pendent that sparkled in the sunlight.
The jeweler carefully attached the delicate pendent to the silver chain.
Antique earrings often feature an ornate pendent as their centerpiece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pendant - an ornament that hangs from a necklace or chain

Etymology

From Latin 'pendere' (to hang).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɛn dənt / (AmE), / ˈpɛn dənt / (BrE)
Hanging down or suspended from above.
hanging downward; suspended

Examples

A pendent lamp illuminated the old hallway.
The willow tree's pendent branches touched the water's surface.
Clusters of pendent flowers adorned the archway.

Synset Examples

a pendant bunch of grapes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pendant - hanging or suspended; also, an object that hangs, such as jewelry

Etymology

From Latin 'pendēns', present participle of 'pendēre' (to hang).

WordNet ID: oewn-02359909-s

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɛndənt / (AmE), / ˈpɛndənt / (BrE)
A branched, often ornate lighting fixture that hangs from the ceiling.
ornate ceiling light fixture

Examples

A large pendent dominated the grand entrance hall, casting light over the marble floor.
The restoration included cleaning the antique pendents throughout the ballroom.
She admired the intricate metalwork of the pendent hanging above the dining table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chandelier - an ornate hanging light fixture with branches for several lights
  • Synonym: pendant - a hanging ornament or light fixture

Etymology

From Latin 'pendens', present participle of 'pendēre' (to hang).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03008889-n
Synset POS: n
