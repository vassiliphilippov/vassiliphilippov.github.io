costmary

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑstˌmɛri / (AmE), / ˈkɒstməri / (BrE)
an aromatic herb (Tanacetum balsamita) whose leaves are used sparingly in sauces, soups, and stuffings because of their bitter flavor
aromatic herb used in cooking

Examples

Finely chopped costmary adds a unique aroma to sauces and stews.
In traditional recipes, costmary leaves are used to flavor stuffing for roast meats.
The chef warned that too much costmary could make the soup overly bitter.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'costus' (an aromatic plant) + 'Mary' (the Virgin Mary), possibly for its use in religious gardens.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑstˌmɛri / (AmE), / ˈkɒstməri / (BrE)
A Eurasian perennial herb (Tanacetum balsamita) with a scent reminiscent of tansy, bearing yellow buttonlike flowers, used as a potherb, salad green, or for flavoring tea and potpourri.
aromatic Eurasian herb used in cooking and tea

Examples

She added a few fresh costmary leaves to her salad for a subtle minty flavor.
In Victorian times, costmary was used to scent linens and as a home remedy for headaches.
After harvesting, she dried the costmary to use in potpourri and herbal tea blends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tansy - aromatic Eurasian herb with yellow buttonlike flowers

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'costum', from Greek 'kostos' (the aromatic plant) + Latin 'Maria' (Mary), commonly called 'St. Mary's herb'.
