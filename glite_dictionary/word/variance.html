variance

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛɹiəns / (AmE), / ˈvɛərɪəns / (BrE)
a disagreement or difference between conflicting facts, claims, or opinions
disagreement or difference of opinion

Examples

There was significant variance in the witnesses' accounts of the accident.
The committee members expressed their variance regarding the best approach to solve the problem.
Despite their long friendship, a variance developed over political beliefs.

Synset Examples

a growing divergence of opinion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disagreement - lack of consensus or harmony in opinions
  • Antonym: concord - agreement or harmony between people or groups

Etymology

From Latin variantia (difference), from variāre (to vary).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04756982-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɛə.ri.əns / (AmE), / ˈvɛə.ri.əns / (BrE)
a statistical measure of how much values in a set differ from the mean value; the average of the squared differences from the mean
statistical measure of spread

Examples

The variance of the scores indicated that the students’ performances were fairly consistent.
In statistics, a high variance implies that data points are spread out over a wide range.
Researchers calculated the variance to understand how much the results differed from the average.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: standard deviation - a statistical measure that is the square root of the variance
  • Degree Lower: mean - the average; the sum of values divided by their number

Etymology

From Latin 'variantia' (difference), from 'variant-' (varying).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06034344-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈver.i.əns / (AmE), / ˈveə.ri.əns / (BrE)
an instance or event that differs from what is expected or predicted
event differing from expectations

Examples

The company's profits showed a significant variance from analyst predictions.
Any variance in the test results must be investigated thoroughly.
There was a surprising variance between the official report and eyewitness accounts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anomaly - something that deviates from what is standard or expected
  • Antonym: expectation - a strong belief that something will happen or be the case

Etymology

From Old French *variance*, from Latin *variantia* (difference), from *variare* (to change).

4. NOUN

/ ˈvɛriəns / (AmE), / ˈvɛəriəns / (BrE)
an official permission to do something that is normally not allowed by rules or regulations, especially regarding building or zoning laws
official exception to a rule or regulation
legal

Examples

The city council granted a variance allowing the homeowner to build closer to the property line.
To add an extra floor, the developer needed a variance from the local zoning board.
She applied for a variance because her renovation plans did not comply with current regulations.

Synset Examples

a zoning variance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exception - something that does not follow a rule

Etymology

From Latin 'variantia' (difference, discrepancy), related to 'variare' (to change).

6. NOUN

/ ˈvɛriəns / (AmE), / ˈvɛəriəns / (BrE)
the state or quality of being subject to change or variation; the fact of differing or varying.
state of being changeable or different

Examples

The variance in her mood from day to day makes her hard to predict.
This year's results show more variance than we expected based on earlier studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: variation - the act or process of changing; the state of being varied
  • Antonym: constancy - the quality of being unchanging or unwavering

Etymology

From Old French 'variance', from Latin 'variantia', from 'variant-', stem of 'variare' (to change).
