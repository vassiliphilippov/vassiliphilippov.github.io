agglomerative

1. ADJ

/ əˈɡlɑːm(ə)rətɪv / (AmE), / əˈɡlɒm(ə)rətɪv / (BrE)
clustered or gathered together into a mass, especially without forming a coherent whole
grouped together into a mass

Examples

The study found that the bacteria had formed an agglomerative mass on the surface.
This software uses an agglomerative algorithm to group similar data points together.
The landscape was marked by an agglomerative mix of old and new buildings.

Synset Examples

an agglomerated flower head

Related Terms

  • Antonym: coherent - logically connected, consistent
  • Synonym: amassed - collected or gathered together

Etymology

From Latin 'agglomerare' (to form into a ball), via Medieval Latin 'agglomerativus'.
