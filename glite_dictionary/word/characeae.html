Characeae

1. NOUN

/ kəˈreɪɪˌsiː / (AmE), / kəˈreɪsiːiː / (BrE)
a family of green algae known as stoneworts, typically found in fresh water and superficially resembling horsetail ferns
family of green algae (stoneworts)

Examples

Characeae are often found in clean, calcium-rich lakes and slow-moving streams.
Researchers studied the reproductive habits of Characeae in different aquatic environments.
Unlike many other types of algae, Characeae can form dense underwater meadows.

Etymology

From scientific Latin 'Characeae', based on the type genus Chara.
