bemused

1. ADJ

/ bɪˈmjuzd / (AmE), / bɪˈmjuːzd / (BrE)
puzzled, confused, or bewildered, especially by conflicting situations or information
perplexed or confused

Examples

She looked bemused when she realized she had missed her stop.
His bemused expression showed he didn't understand the joke.
The new employee gave a bemused shrug in response to the complicated instructions.

Synset Examples

obviously bemused by his questions
bewildered and confused
a cloudy and confounded philosopher
just a mixed-up kid
she felt lost on the first day of school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bewildered - confused and puzzled
  • Synonym: confused - unable to think clearly; bewildered
  • Antonym: clear - easily understood or free from confusion

Etymology

From be- (prefix) + muse (to reflect or ponder), originally meaning 'absorbed in thought'.

2. ADJ

/ bɪˈmjuzd / (AmE), / bɪˈmjuːzd / (BrE)
deeply absorbed in thought; showing signs of concentration or abstraction.
deeply absorbed in thought

Examples

She sat with a bemused expression, lost in her own thoughts for several minutes.
He wandered through the gallery, bemused by the complexity of the modern art.
The students looked bemused as the professor explained the difficult concept.

Synset Examples

as distant and bemused as a professor listening to the prattling of his freshman class
lost in thought
a preoccupied frown

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preoccupied - absorbed in thought; lost in one's thoughts
  • Synonym: distracted - unable to concentrate because one's mind is preoccupied

Etymology

From prefix be- + musing, originating in the 18th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02427955-s
Synset POS: s
