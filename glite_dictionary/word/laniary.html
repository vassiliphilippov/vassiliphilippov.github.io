laniary

1. ADJ

/ ˈlæniˌɛri / (AmE), / ˈlanɪəri / (BrE)
relating to or resembling a pointed, conical tooth such as a canine tooth
relating to conical pointed tooth

Examples

The tiger bared its laniary teeth before attacking its prey.
In mammals, the laniary teeth are used for seizing and tearing food.
The fossilized skull showed prominent laniary structures typical of carnivorous animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canine - a pointed tooth between the incisors and premolars

Etymology

From Latin 'laniarius' (of a butcher), from 'lanius' (butcher).
