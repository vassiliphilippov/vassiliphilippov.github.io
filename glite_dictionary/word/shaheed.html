shaheed

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑːˈhiːd / (AmE), / ʃɑːˈhiːd / (BrE)
A person who is regarded as a holy martyr, especially in Islam, having died for their religious beliefs.
Islamic holy martyr

Examples

The people remembered him as a shaheed who gave his life for his faith.
She was honored as a shaheed after dying in service to her community.
Many regard those who defend their beliefs to the end as true shaheeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: martyr - a person who is killed because of their religious or other beliefs

Etymology

From Arabic شهيد (šahīd), meaning 'witness' or 'martyr.'
