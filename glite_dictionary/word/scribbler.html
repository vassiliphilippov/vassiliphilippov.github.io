scribbler

1. NOUN

/ ˈskrɪblɚ / (AmE), / ˈskrɪblə / (BrE)
an informal, often mildly derogatory term for a journalist or writer, especially one considered to lack skill or importance
an informal term for a journalist or writer
informal

Examples

As a seasoned scribbler, she knew how to craft a catchy headline.
The famous novelist dismissed critics as mere scribblers.
He started his career as a young scribbler in a small-town newspaper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: journalist - a person who writes for newspapers, magazines, or news websites

Etymology

From 'scribble' + '-er', originally meaning 'one who scribbles', later applied informally to writers or journalists.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskrɪblɚ / (AmE), / ˈskrɪblə / (BrE)
a person who writes in a careless or illegible hand
person with messy handwriting

Examples

The doctor was known as a notorious scribbler, and no one could read his prescriptions.
As a child, I was often called a scribbler because my teachers couldn't decipher my notes.
Her notes looked like the work of a scribbler, impossible to make sense of.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scribble - to write quickly or carelessly; to make irregular marks or writing

Etymology

From 'scribble' + '-er', formed in English.
