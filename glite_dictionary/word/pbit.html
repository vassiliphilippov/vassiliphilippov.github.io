Pbit

1. NOUN

/ pi bɪt / (AmE), / pi bɪt / (BrE)
a unit of information equal to 1,000 terabits or 10^15 bits
unit of digital information (10^15 bits)

Examples

The new storage device can hold up to 3 Pbits of data, setting a record in the industry.
Large-scale internet traffic is often measured in Pbits per second to assess network capacity.
Scientists transferred a Pbit of information across the fiber optic cable during the test.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petabit - a unit of information equal to 10^15 bits

Etymology

Shortened from 'petabit,' from 'peta-' (denoting a factor of 10^15) and 'bit.'
