Anthoceros

1. NOUN

/ ˌænθəˈsɛrəs / (AmE), / ˌænθəˈsɛərɒs / (BrE)
A genus of hornworts, small non-vascular plants found in moist soil, characterized by their elongated horn-like spore-producing structures.
genus of hornwort plants

Examples

Anthoceros species are often found in damp, shady woodland areas.
The spore capsules of Anthoceros are distinctive and help botanists identify the genus.
Researchers study Anthoceros for insights into non-vascular plant evolution.

Etymology

From Greek 'anthos' (flower) + 'keras, keratos' (horn), referring to the horn-like shape of its spore capsule.
