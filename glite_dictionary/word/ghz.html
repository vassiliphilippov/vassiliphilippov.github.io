GHz

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiː ˌeɪtʃ ˈzɛd / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː ˌeɪtʃ ˈzɛd / (BrE)
A unit of frequency equal to one billion cycles per second; gigahertz.
unit of frequency (1 billion hertz)

Examples

The new processor runs at 3.2GHz for faster performance.
Wi-Fi signals typically use either the 2.4GHz or 5GHz frequency bands.
Her computer's CPU can be overclocked to 4.5GHz.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: MHz - a unit of frequency equal to one million hertz
  • Degree Higher: THz - a unit of frequency equal to one trillion hertz

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'gigahertz'; from 'giga-' (a billion) and 'hertz' (after Heinrich Hertz).
