strangeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈstreɪndʒ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈstreɪndʒ.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being unusual or unfamiliar; the condition of being strange or not well known
quality of being strange or unfamiliar

Examples

The strangeness of the old house made it both eerie and fascinating to visitors.
He was immediately aware of the strangeness of the situation, though he could not explain why.
Despite the strangeness of her accent, everyone understood her perfectly well.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unfamiliarity - the state of being unknown or not recognized
  • Antonym: familiarity - the quality of being well known or easily recognized

Etymology

From strange + -ness, meaning the state of being strange.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstreɪndʒ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈstreɪndʒ.nɪs / (BrE)
(Physics) a property of certain subatomic particles, related to the presence of strange quarks, which is used to explain certain particle interactions and reactions.
physics property of strange quarks
medical

Examples

The conservation of strangeness is important in certain types of particle interactions.
Particles with strangeness are typically produced in high-energy collisions.
The discovery of strangeness led to the identification of new subatomic particles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charm - a flavor of quark; related property in particle physics
  • Degree Lower: up quark - the lightest quark flavor
  • Degree Higher: bottom quark - a heavier quark flavor than strange quark

Etymology

From 'strange' (referring to the strange quark) + '-ness'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈstreɪndʒ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈstreɪndʒ.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being unfamiliar, foreign, or not native; the state of appearing strange or alien
quality of being foreign or unfamiliar

Examples

The strangeness of the new city made her feel uneasy at first.
He was fascinated by the strangeness of the customs he encountered abroad.
There is a certain strangeness in foreign languages that can be both exciting and intimidating.

Synset Examples

the strangeness of a foreigner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foreignness - the quality of being from another country or not native
  • Synonym: alienness - the quality of being alien or different

Etymology

From "strange" + "-ness"; "strange" from Old French *estrange*, from Latin *extraneus* (foreign, external).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
