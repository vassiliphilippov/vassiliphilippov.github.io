ovation

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / əʊˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
an enthusiastic show of appreciation, especially by prolonged applause
enthusiastic applause and recognition

Examples

The audience gave the performer a standing ovation at the end of the concert.
She received a thunderous ovation for her outstanding performance.
After his speech, there was a long ovation that filled the hall with cheers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: applause - approval or praise expressed by clapping

Etymology

From Latin ovatio (a kind of lesser triumph, applause), from ovāre (to rejoice).
