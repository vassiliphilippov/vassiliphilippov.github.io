inventive

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈvɛntɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛntɪv / (BrE)
Having the ability to create or design new things or to think originally.
creative; able to invent new things

Examples

Her inventive solutions to complex problems impressed everyone in the meeting.
The artist is known for his inventive use of recycled materials in sculpture.
We need an inventive approach to reduce costs without compromising quality.

Synset Examples

an imaginative use of material
the invention of the knitting frame by another ingenious English clergyman
had an inventive turn of mind
inventive ceramics
an inventive device

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creative - having the ability to make new things or think of new ideas
  • Synonym: imaginative - having or showing creativity or inventiveness
  • Antonym: unimaginative - lacking creativity or originality

Etymology

From Latin 'inventivus' (able to discover), from 'invenire' (to come upon).
