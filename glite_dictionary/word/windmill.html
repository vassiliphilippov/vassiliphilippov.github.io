windmill

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪn(d)ˌmɪl / (AmE), / ˈwɪnd.mɪl / (BrE)
A structure with sails or blades that turn in the wind and generate energy, especially for grinding grain, pumping water, or producing electricity.
structure using wind for power

Examples

The old windmill on the hill was once used to grind grain for the village.
Modern farms often use a windmill to pump water from the ground.
Tourists took photos of the picturesque windmill turning slowly against the sunset.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wind turbine - a machine for converting wind energy into electricity

Etymology

From Middle English 'windmille', from 'wind' + 'mill'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪndˌmɪl / (AmE), / ˈwɪnd.mɪl / (BrE)
A building or machine with sails or blades that turn in the wind and generate power to perform work such as grinding grain or pumping water.
machine that uses wind power to do work

Examples

The old windmill by the river is a popular landmark in the village.
Farmers once relied on the windmill to grind wheat into flour.
They restored a historic windmill to demonstrate traditional milling methods.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: watermill - a mill powered by water

Etymology

From wind + mill; first recorded in English in the 14th century.
