gastromycete

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæstrəˈmaɪsiːt / (AmE), / ˌɡæstrəˈmiːsiːt / (BrE)
A fungus belonging to the class Gasteromycetes, characterized by producing spores inside a closed fruiting body.
fungus of the class Gasteromycetes

Examples

The earthstar is a well-known gastromycete found in woodland areas.
Certain gastromycetes have unique mechanisms for releasing their spores.
Mycologists studied a rare gastromycete collected from the forest floor.

Etymology

From Greek 'gastēr' (stomach) + 'mykēs' (fungus), referring to the internal spore development.
