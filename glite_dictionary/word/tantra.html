tantra

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæntrə / (AmE), / ˈtæntrə / (BrE)
a religious and philosophical system, especially in Hinduism and Buddhism, focusing on spiritual practices and rituals intended to achieve enlightenment and the realization of the oneness of self and the world, often involving mantras, mudras, and sometimes esoteric or erotic rites
mystical system in Hinduism and Buddhism

Examples

She spent years studying tantra and its spiritual rituals in India.
Many Tibetan monks incorporate tantra into their religious practices.
The book explores the philosophical foundations and rituals of tantra in Eastern traditions.

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'tantra' (framework, doctrine, theory), meaning 'loom' or 'weaving.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːntrə / (AmE), / ˈtæntrə / (BrE)
any of a class of Hindu or Buddhist religious texts and practices that emphasize ritual acts involving body, speech, and mind
Hindu or Buddhist ritual text/practice

Examples

The ancient tantra outlines complex rituals for spiritual development.
She studied various tantras to better understand Tibetan Buddhism.
Much of his knowledge about Hindu philosophy came from reading the tantra literature.

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'tantra' (loom, warp, doctrine, treatise).
