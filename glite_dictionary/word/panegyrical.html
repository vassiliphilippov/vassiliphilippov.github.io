panegyrical

1. ADJ

/ ˌpænɪˈdʒɪrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌpanɪˈdʒɪrɪkəl / (BrE)
expressing formal or elaborate praise.
formally expressing praise

Examples

The professor gave a panegyrical speech honoring the retiring dean.
Her book review was entirely panegyrical, filled with unqualified admiration for the author.
The ceremony ended with a panegyrical tribute to the founder's legacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laudatory - expressing praise or commendation
  • Antonym: critical - expressing negative or disapproving judgments

Etymology

From Greek panēgyrikos (of or for a public assembly), from panēgyris (public assembly).
