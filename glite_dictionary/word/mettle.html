mettle

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛt̬.əl / (AmE), / ˈmɛt.əl / (BrE)
the ability and determination to continue despite difficulties; courage and spirit
courage and determination

Examples

The coach admired her mettle during the challenging season.
It takes real mettle to admit your mistakes and move forward.
His mettle was tested when he faced every setback with resilience.

Synset Examples

he kept fighting on pure spunk
you haven't got the heart for baseball

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spirit - strength of character or determination
  • Antonym: cowardice - lack of courage to face danger or difficulty

Etymology

Variant of 'metal', originally used figuratively for 'quality of temperament.'
