orbit

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrbɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔːbɪt / (BrE)
the bony cavity in the skull that contains and protects the eyeball and its associated structures.
eye socket; bony cavity for the eyeball
medical

Examples

The surgeon repaired the fracture in the orbit to restore the patient's vision.
Trauma to the orbit can cause serious injury to the eye and surrounding tissues.
The optic nerve passes through the back of the orbit to connect with the brain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eye socket - the cavity in the skull that houses the eyeball; synonym for orbit (anatomy)

Etymology

From Latin *orbita* (track, course), referring to a circular path; anatomical use is by extension.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɔrbɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔːbɪt / (BrE)
the curved path that a planet, satellite, or other celestial body follows as it moves around another body, especially in space
path of a body around another in space

Examples

The Earth travels in an orbit around the Sun once every year.
Satellites must be launched at the correct speed to maintain a stable orbit.
Comets sometimes have highly elliptical orbits that bring them close to the sun.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trajectory - the path followed by a moving object through space
  • Synonym: path - the route along which something moves

Etymology

From Latin orbitā (course, track, orbit), from orbis (circle, orb).
