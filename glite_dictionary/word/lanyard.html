lanyard

1. NOUN

/ ˈlænjərd / (AmE), / ˈlanjəd / (BrE)
A cord with an attached hook used to fire certain types of cannon.
cord for firing cannons

Examples

The gunner pulled the lanyard to fire the cannon during the demonstration.
Early artillery relied on a simple lanyard mechanism for ignition.
With a swift tug on the lanyard, the shell was launched toward the target.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lanyard - a cord worn around the neck, shoulder, or wrist to carry an object

Etymology

From Middle English laniard, from Old French laniere (thong, strap).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlæn.jɚd / (AmE), / ˈlæn.jəd / (BrE)
a cord or strap worn around the neck, shoulder, or wrist to carry small objects such as a knife, whistle, or identification card
cord or strap worn for carrying items

Examples

She wore her ID badge on a lanyard around her neck.
The keys were attached to a lanyard to prevent losing them at the beach.
Security handed out a lanyard with each visitor's pass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cord - a length of string or thick thread

Etymology

From Middle French *lanière* (strap, thong).

3. NOUN

/ ˈlæn.jɚd / (AmE), / ˈlan.jəd / (BrE)
(nautical) A short rope or line used for fastening, securing, or extending rigging on a ship.
nautical line for rigging on ships

Examples

The sailor secured the boom with a lanyard before the storm hit.
Each mast was reinforced by several lanyards running from different points.
He checked that every lanyard was properly tied before setting sail.

Etymology

From Middle French *lanière* (strap); related to Old French *las* (cord).
