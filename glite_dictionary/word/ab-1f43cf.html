Ab

1. NOUN

/ ɑːb / (AmE), / ɑːb / (BrE)
The eleventh month of the civil year and the fifth month of the ecclesiastical year in the Jewish calendar, usually falling in July and August.
month in the Jewish calendar (July–August)

Examples

The fast of Tisha B'Av takes place during the month of Ab each year.
In the Jewish calendar, Ab usually corresponds to parts of July and August.
Many significant events in Jewish history are commemorated in the month of Ab.

Etymology

From Hebrew אַב (Av), the name of the month in the Jewish calendar.
