Quercus prinus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜːrkəs ˈpraɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs ˈpriːnəs / (BrE)
A medium to large deciduous tree native to the eastern United States, known as chestnut oak, valued for its durable wood used in timber, basketry, and chair seats.
species of oak tree (chestnut oak) from eastern US

Examples

The forest trail was lined with towering Quercus prinus, their leaves rustling in the breeze.
Artisans prized Quercus prinus wood for its strength and durability in making furniture.
Botanists documented the distribution of Quercus prinus throughout the Appalachian region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chestnut oak - common name for Quercus prinus, an eastern US oak species

Etymology

From Latin: 'Quercus' (oak) and specific epithet 'prinus' from classical Latin for an oak resembling holm oak.
