irreligiousness

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪr.ɪˈlɪdʒ.əs.nəs / (AmE), / ˌɪr.ɪˈlɪdʒ.əs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of lacking religious belief, piety, or devotion
lack of religious belief or devotion

Examples

The philosopher was often criticized for his irreligiousness by more traditional members of his community.
Some viewed his irreligiousness as a sign of open-mindedness, while others saw it as indifference.
Despite her family's deep faith, she was open about her irreligiousness and her secular worldview.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impiety - lack of piety or reverence, especially for a god
  • Antonym: devoutness - the quality of being devout or deeply religious

Etymology

From 'irreligious' (Latin irreligiosus) plus the suffix '-ness'.
