long-chain

1. ADJ

/ lɔŋ tʃeɪn / (AmE), / lɒŋ tʃeɪn / (BrE)
(of a molecule) having a relatively long sequence of atoms bonded together in its structure.
having a long molecular chain of atoms

Examples

The long-chain fatty acids are essential components of human nutrition.
Many plastics are made from long-chain polymer molecules.
Fish oils are rich in long-chain omega-3 fatty acids.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: short-chain - of a molecule with a relatively short sequence of atoms
