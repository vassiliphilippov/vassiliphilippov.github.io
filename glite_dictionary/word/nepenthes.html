Nepenthes

1. PROPN

/ nəˈpɛnθiz / (AmE), / nɪˈpɛnθiːz / (BrE)
A genus of tropical pitcher plants known for their modified leaves that form deep cavities, or 'pitchers', to trap and digest insects.
genus of tropical pitcher plants

Examples

Many species of Nepenthes are found in the rainforests of Southeast Asia.
Botanists study Nepenthes for their unique method of attracting and trapping prey.
The greenhouse featured an impressive collection of Nepenthes with brightly colored pitchers.

Etymology

From Greek 'nēpenthes', meaning 'dispeller of sorrow', originally used for a mythical drug and later applied to the plant genus.

2. NOUN

/ nɪˈpɛnθiːz / (AmE), / nɪˈpɛnθiːz / (BrE)
A genus of tropical pitcher plants known for their distinctive pitfall traps used to capture and digest insects.
genus of tropical pitcher plants

Examples

The Nepenthes in her greenhouse require humid conditions to thrive.
Researchers discovered a new species of Nepenthes in the remote mountains of Borneo.
Many gardeners are fascinated by the carnivorous nature of Nepenthes plants.

Etymology

From Greek *nēpenthes* (drug that chases away sorrow), used by Linnaeus in botanical naming.
