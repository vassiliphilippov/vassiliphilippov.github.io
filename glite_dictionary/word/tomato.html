tomato

1. NOUN

/ təˈmeɪdoʊ / (AmE), / təˈmɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
A glossy, typically red or yellow, mildly acidic fruit with juicy pulp, widely eaten as a vegetable in salads, sauces, and cooking.
a red or yellow edible fruit used as a vegetable

Examples

She sliced a ripe tomato to add to the salad.
This sauce is made from fresh tomatoes and herbs.
A green tomato will continue to ripen if left on the windowsill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vegetable - an edible plant or part of a plant

Etymology

From Spanish 'tomate', from Nahuatl 'tomatl'.

2. NOUN

/ təˈmeɪdoʊ / (AmE), / təˈmɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
A round, fleshy, usually red fruit that is eaten raw or cooked as a vegetable and is native to South America.
a red edible fruit

Examples

She sliced a fresh tomato for her salad.
The chef added chopped tomato to the sauce for extra flavor.
We grew tomatoes in our backyard garden this summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fruit - the sweet and fleshy product of a tree or other plant that contains seed and can be eaten as food
  • Synonym: vegetable - a plant or part of a plant used as food

Etymology

From Spanish 'tomate', from Nahuatl 'tomatl'.
