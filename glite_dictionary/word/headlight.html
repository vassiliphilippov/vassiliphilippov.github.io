headlight

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈhɛd.laɪt / (BrE)
A powerful lamp attached to the front of a vehicle, such as a car or locomotive, to illuminate the road ahead.
front lamp of a vehicle

Examples

He noticed one headlight was dimmer than the other and decided to replace it.
Please remember to turn off your car’s headlights when you leave the parking lot.
The train’s headlight shone brightly through the fog, warning of its approach.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: taillight - a light at the rear of a vehicle
  • Synonym: headlamp - another word for headlight, especially in technical contexts

Etymology

Formed from 'head' + 'light', indicating a light at the front (head) of a vehicle.
