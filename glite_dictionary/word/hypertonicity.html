hypertonicity

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərtəˈnɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpətəˈnɪsɪti / (BrE)
the state or condition in which muscle tissue exhibits an abnormally high degree of tension or tone
excessive muscle tension or tone
medical

Examples

The patient's hypertonicity made it difficult for him to move his arms freely.
Physical therapists work with individuals to reduce muscle hypertonicity after neurological injuries.
A doctor assessed the baby's limbs for signs of hypertonicity during the examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spasticity - increased muscle tone with exaggerated reflexes
  • Antonym: hypotonicity - condition of decreased muscle tone

Etymology

Formed from 'hyper-' (over, excessive) and 'tonicity' (degree of muscle tension); ultimately from Greek 'hyper' + 'tonos'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪ.pɚ.təˈnɪs.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.pə.təˈnɪs.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The condition of having a higher osmotic pressure than another solution, often used in medicine and biology.
higher osmotic pressure (biology/medicine)
medical

Examples

Dehydration can result from the hypertonicity of extracellular fluid, causing water to move out of cells.
The laboratory compared the hypertonicity of several saline solutions under controlled conditions.
Medical students must understand the effects of hypertonicity on red blood cells during osmosis experiments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypotonicity - the condition of having a lower osmotic pressure than another solution
  • Antonym: isotonicity - the condition of having the same osmotic pressure as another solution

Etymology

From Greek 'hyper-' (over, excessive) + 'tonos' (tension) + English '-icity' (quality or state).
