primaeval

1. ADJ

/ praɪˈmiːvəl / (AmE), / prɪˈmiːvəl / (BrE)
belonging to the earliest period of history or the very beginning of something; ancient or original
ancient; from the earliest times

Examples

The explorers trekked through a primaeval forest untouched by modern civilization.
Legends spoke of a primaeval world of darkness and chaos.
Scientists studied the primaeval oceans to learn about the origins of life.

Synset Examples

aboriginal forests
primal eras before the appearance of life on earth
the forest primeval
primordial matter
primordial forms of life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primordial - existing at or from the very beginning
  • Synonym: ancient - belonging to the distant past

Etymology

From Latin 'primaevus', from 'primus' (first) and 'aevum' (age, period).
