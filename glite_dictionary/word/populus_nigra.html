Populus nigra

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑpjələs ˈnaɪɡrə / (AmE), / ˈpɒpjʊləs ˈnʌɪɡrə / (BrE)
A species of large deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia, commonly known as the black poplar, with dark bark and triangular leaves.
a large European poplar tree species

Examples

The Populus nigra is often planted along rivers to prevent soil erosion.
Conservationists are working to protect the endangered Populus nigra in many parts of Europe.
The distinctive leaves of Populus nigra make it easy to identify among other poplars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black poplar - common English name for Populus nigra

Etymology

From Latin: 'populus' meaning 'poplar', 'nigra' meaning 'black'.
