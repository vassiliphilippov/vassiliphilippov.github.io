esophagitis

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌsɑfəˈdʒaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ɪˌsɒfəˈdʒaɪtɪs / (BrE)
Inflammation of the esophagus, often caused by acid reflux or infection.
inflammation of the esophagus
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with esophagitis after he complained of pain when swallowing.
Chronic heartburn can sometimes lead to esophagitis.
Patients with untreated esophagitis may develop difficulty eating solid foods.

Etymology

From esophagus + -itis (Greek for inflammation).
