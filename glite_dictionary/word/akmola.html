Akmola

1. PROPN

/ ɑːkˈmoʊlə / (AmE), / ɑːkˈməʊlə / (BrE)
A former name for the city that became the capital of Kazakhstan in 1998, now known as Astana and later Nur-Sultan.
former name of Kazakhstan's capital city

Examples

In 1998, the government decided to move the capital to Akmola.
The name Akmola was later changed to Astana, and then to Nur-Sultan.
Few people outside Kazakhstan are familiar with the city's old name, Akmola.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Astana - the current (and former) name of the capital city of Kazakhstan
  • Synonym: Nur-Sultan - the more recent former name of Kazakhstan's capital city

Etymology

From Kazakh Ақмола (Aqmola), meaning 'white grave'.
