Polioptila

1. NOUN

/ poʊliˈɑptɪlə / (AmE), / pɒliˈɒptɪlə / (BrE)
A genus of small insect-eating birds, commonly known as New World gnatcatchers, found in the Americas.
genus of New World gnatcatcher birds

Examples

Many birdwatchers hope to spot a Polioptila species during their South American expeditions.
The blue-gray gnatcatcher is one of the most widespread members of the Polioptila genus.
Scientists recently identified a new Polioptila species in the Amazon rainforest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gnatcatcher - a small insect-eating bird of the Americas

Etymology

From Greek polios (gray) + ptilon (feather).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊliˈɑptɪlə / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈɒptɪlə / (BrE)
A genus of small insect-eating birds known as New World gnatcatchers, found in the Americas.
genus of New World gnatcatchers (birds)

Examples

The Polioptila genus includes several species of gnatcatchers found in North and South America.
Researchers studied the nesting behavior of Polioptila birds in various habitats.
The distinctive call of the Polioptila is often heard in southern woodlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gnatcatcher - a small insect-eating bird of the family Polioptilidae

Etymology

From Greek 'polios' (grey) and 'ptilon' (wing), referring to the bird's plumage.
