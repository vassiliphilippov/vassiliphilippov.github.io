Hereford

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛr fərd / (AmE), / ˈhɛr ə fəd / (BrE)
a breed of hardy beef cattle, originally from England, known for their red bodies and white faces, and widely raised in many countries
a breed of beef cattle

Examples

The ranch specialized in raising Hereford cattle for beef production.
We visited a farm where dozens of Herefords grazed in the fields.
The Hereford is prized for its docile temperament and adaptability.

Etymology

Named after Hereford, a county in western England where the breed originated.
