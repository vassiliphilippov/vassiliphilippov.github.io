Triglinae

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈɡlɪniː / (AmE), / traɪˈɡlɪniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of marine fishes in the family Triglidae, commonly known as true searobins, characterized by having ordinary scales and no barbels.
subfamily of true searobins (fishes)

Examples

Scientists studying the Triglinae have identified several new species of searobins in the Atlantic Ocean.
The characteristic lack of barbels helps distinguish Triglinae from other related fish groups.
Marine biologists often refer to the Triglinae subfamily when discussing the classification of true searobins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Triglidae - the family of fishes including searobins and gurnards

Etymology

From Latin 'Trigla' (a genus of fish) + '-inae' (zoological suffix denoting a subfamily).
