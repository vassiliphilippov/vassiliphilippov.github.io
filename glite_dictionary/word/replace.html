to replace

1. VERB

/ rɪˈpleɪs / (AmE), / rɪˈpleɪs / (BrE)
to put something back in its original position or where it belongs
put something back in place

Examples

After finishing the novel, please replace it on the shelf.
She always replaces the remote on the table after using it.
He replaced his keys in the drawer where they belong.

Synset Examples

replace the book on the shelf after you have finished reading it
please put the clean dishes back in the cabinet when you have washed them

Related Terms

  • Synonym: return - to put something back in a place; to give back

Etymology

From French remplacer, from re- (again) + placer (to place).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01311041-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rɪˈpleɪs / (AmE), / rɪˈpleɪs / (BrE)
to put something or someone in the place of another person or thing; to substitute one for another
to put in the place of another

Examples

They had to replace the broken window before winter arrived.
You can replace sugar with honey in many recipes for a healthier alternative.
When my old phone stopped working, I decided to replace it with a newer model.

Synset Examples

the con artist replaced the original with a fake Rembrandt
substitute regular milk for fat-free milk
synonyms can be interchanged without changing the context's meaning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitute - to use in place of another person or thing
  • Antonym: remove - to take away something from a place

Etymology

From French remplacer, from re- (again) + placer (to place).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02262575-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ rɪˈpleɪs / (AmE), / rɪˈpleɪs / (BrE)
to take the place of someone or something, or to move into the position previously held by someone or something
take the place of; succeed someone/something

Examples

After the manager resigned, her assistant was chosen to replace her.
Many factories have begun to replace workers with machines.
He will replace the outgoing president at the end of the year.

Synset Examples

Smith replaced Miller as CEO after Miller left
the computer has supplanted the slide rule
Mary replaced Susan as the team's captain and the highest-ranked player in the school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to supersede - take the place of something or someone previously in authority or use
  • Synonym: to oust - to remove and replace someone from a position or place
  • Synonym: to succeed - take over a job, position, or title from another

Etymology

From Old French replacer, from re- (again) + placer (to place).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02410532-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ rɪˈpleɪs / (AmE), / rɪˈpleɪs / (BrE)
to provide a new thing or person in the place of one that is broken, lost, or no longer effective
substitute for something lost, broken, or outdated

Examples

I had to replace my phone after it stopped working last week.
They replaced the damaged tiles on the kitchen floor with new ones.
If your card is lost, the bank will replace it free of charge.

Synset Examples

He replaced the old razor blade
We need to replace the secretary that left a month ago
the insurance will replace the lost income
This antique vase can never be replaced

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitute - to use in place of something else
  • Antonym: remove - to take something away

Etymology

From French remplacer, from re- (again) + placer (to place).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00162496-v
Synset POS: v
