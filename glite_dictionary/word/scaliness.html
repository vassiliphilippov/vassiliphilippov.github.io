scaliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈskeɪ.li.nəs / (AmE), / ˈskeɪ.li.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being covered with or resembling scales
quality of being scaly

Examples

The scaliness of the fish made it difficult to hold while cleaning.
He noticed a scaliness on his skin and decided to consult a doctor.
The old paint was peeling, giving the wall a rough scaliness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scaly - covered with or resembling scales

Etymology

From 'scaly' + '-ness', forming a noun meaning the state or quality of being scaly.
