vatic

1. ADJ

/ ˈvætɪk / (AmE), / ˈvatɪk / (BrE)
having the qualities or characteristics of a prophet; prophetic or oracular in nature
prophetic; resembling a prophet

Examples

Her writing style is both enigmatic and vatic, foretelling changes long before they become clear to others.
The critics admired his vatic insights into society's future direction.
He delivered a vatic warning that seemed to predict the turmoil that followed.

Synset Examples

the high priest's divinatory pronouncement
mantic powers
a kind of sibylline book with ready and infallible answers to questions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prophetic - having the ability to predict or foretell future events
  • Synonym: oracular - giving wise or authoritative decisions or having prophetic significance

Etymology

From Latin vaticinus (prophetic), from vates (prophet).
