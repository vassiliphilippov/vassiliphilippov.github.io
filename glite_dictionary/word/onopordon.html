Onopordon

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊ.nəˈpɔr.dɑn / (AmE), / ˌɒn.əˈpɔː.dɒn / (BrE)
A genus of Eurasian thistle-like herbs in the family Compositae, with prickly leaves and large purplish flower heads.
genus of thistle-like Eurasian herbs

Examples

The Onopordon genus includes the well-known cotton thistle.
Botanists studied several species belonging to Onopordon during their expedition.
Flowers of Onopordon are often large and display vibrant purple hues.

Etymology

From Greek onos (ass) + pordon (fart), alluding to the plant's supposed effects on donkeys.

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊ.nəˈpɔrd.ɑn / (AmE), / ˌɒn.əˈpɔː.dɒn / (BrE)
A genus of Eurasian thistle-like herbs of the daisy family, with prickly leaves and large purplish flowers.
genus of Eurasian thistle-like herbs

Examples

Many species in the genus Onopordon are commonly known as cotton thistles.
The Onopordon plants thrive in dry, rocky soils throughout Eurasia.
Botanists examined several Onopordon specimens collected in the Mediterranean.

Etymology

From Greek onos (donkey) + pordon (fart), referring to folk beliefs about the plant's effect on donkeys.
