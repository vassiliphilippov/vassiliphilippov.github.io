CJD

1. NOUN

/ si dʒeɪ ˈdi / (AmE), / siː dʒeɪ ˈdiː / (BrE)
a rare, usually fatal brain disorder (Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease) causing progressive dementia and loss of muscle control
rare, fatal brain disease; Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with CJD after showing rapid cognitive decline and motor dysfunction.
Doctors ruled out other causes before confirming that it was indeed CJD.
News reports highlighted the rarity of CJD and its devastating effects on families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease - rare, fatal brain disorder

Etymology

Acronym from Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease, named after German neurologists Hans Gerhard Creutzfeldt and Alfons Maria Jakob.
