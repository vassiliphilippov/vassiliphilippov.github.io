Pipilo erythrophthalmus

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈpaɪloʊ ˌɛrɪˌθrɒpˈθælməs / (AmE), / pɪˈpiːloʊ ˌɛrɪˌθrəpˈθælməs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the eastern towhee, a common North American songbird characterized by its black, white, and rufous plumage
scientific name for the eastern towhee

Examples

The eastern towhee, known scientifically as Pipilo erythrophthalmus, is easily identified by its striking call.
Birdwatchers in the eastern United States often spot Pipilo erythrophthalmus foraging on the forest floor.
Researchers have studied the plumage variations of Pipilo erythrophthalmus across different habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: towhee - a North American songbird of the genus Pipilo

Etymology

From Latin: genus 'Pipilo' (chirp) and species 'erythrophthalmus' (red-eyed), referring to its distinctive eye color.
