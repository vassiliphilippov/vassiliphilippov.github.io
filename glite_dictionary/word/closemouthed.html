closemouthed

1. ADJ

/ ˈkloʊsˌmaʊðd / (AmE), / ˈkləʊsˌmaʊθt / (BrE)
reluctant to speak or give information; inclined to secrecy or silence
reluctant to speak or reveal information

Examples

He remained closemouthed about the details of the agreement.
Despite the rumors, the company has been closemouthed about its future plans.
She is very closemouthed when it comes to discussing her private life.

Synset Examples

although they knew her whereabouts her friends kept close about it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secretive - tending to conceal feelings or intentions
  • Synonym: reticent - reserved or reluctant to speak
  • Antonym: talkative - fond of talking; given to talking a lot

Etymology

Formed in English from 'close' + 'mouthed', describing one who keeps their mouth closed, i.e., silent.
