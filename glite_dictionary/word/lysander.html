Lysander

1. PROPN

/ laɪˈsændər / (AmE), / laɪˈsændə / (BrE)
A Spartan general who defeated the Athenians in the final battle of the Peloponnesian War and died in 395 BC.
Spartan general; defeated Athenians in Peloponnesian War

Examples

Lysander played a pivotal role in the defeat of Athens at the end of the Peloponnesian War.
Many historians debate whether Lysander's ruthless tactics contributed to Sparta's later decline.
The strategy adopted by Lysander is still studied in military academies today.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Λύσανδρος (Lysandros), a personal name.
