zymolysis

1. NOUN

/ zaɪˈmɑːlɪsɪs / (AmE), / zaɪˈmɒlɪsɪs / (BrE)
the chemical process in which an agent, usually an enzyme, causes the breakdown of organic substances into simpler compounds, especially the anaerobic conversion of sugar into alcohol
anaerobic breakdown of sugar into alcohol
medical

Examples

The production of beer relies on zymolysis to convert sugars into alcohol.
During zymolysis, yeast metabolizes glucose without the need for oxygen.
Scientists studied the effect of temperature on the rate of zymolysis in different sugar solutions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fermentation - the process by which substances are broken down by microorganisms; especially yeast converting sugar to alcohol
  • Degree Lower: glycolysis - the process in which glucose is broken down in the body to form pyruvate

Etymology

From Greek *zymē* (leaven) + *lysis* (loosening, dissolving).
