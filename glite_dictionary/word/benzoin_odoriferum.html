Benzoin odoriferum

1. NOUN

/ bɛnˈzoʊ.ɪn ˌoʊ.dəˈrɪf.ər.əm / (AmE), / bɛnˈzəʊ.ɪn ˌəʊ.dəˈrɪf.ər.əm / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, known for its aromatic leaves and bark, yellow flowers, and scarlet or yellow berries.
aromatic shrub of eastern U.S.

Examples

The Benzoin odoriferum flourished along the riverbanks, filling the air with its distinctive scent.
Gardeners appreciate Benzoin odoriferum for both its ornamental flowers and aromatic qualities.
The berries of Benzoin odoriferum attract many species of birds in late summer.

Etymology

From Latin 'benzoin' (a balsamic resin) and 'odoriferum' meaning 'bearing odor'.
