quack

1. ADJ

/ kwæk / (AmE), / kwæk / (BrE)
(of a doctor or medical practitioner) medically unqualified; falsely claiming to have medical knowledge or skills.
medically unqualified or fraudulent (of a doctor)

Examples

The clinic was shut down for employing quack practitioners with no medical licenses.
She was wary of quack remedies advertised online for serious diseases.
A quack doctor was caught prescribing dangerous treatments to unsuspecting patients.

Synset Examples

a quack doctor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charlatan - a person falsely claiming to have special knowledge or skill
  • Antonym: qualified - having the necessary skills, experience, or knowledge for a particular job or activity

Etymology

Imitative, originally from the earlier term 'quacksalver' (Dutch 'kwakzalver'), meaning someone who quacks or boasts about salves.

2. NOUN

/ kwæk / (AmE), / kwæk / (BrE)
the characteristic harsh sound made by a duck
sound made by a duck

Examples

We heard the loud quack of ducks as we approached the pond.
The farmer could tell the difference between each duck's quack.
A sudden quack startled the dog by the water's edge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honk - the harsh sound made by a goose
  • Degree Lower: chirp - a short, sharp sound usually made by a small bird
  • Degree Lower: squeak - a short, high-pitched sound
Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ kwæk / (AmE), / kwæk / (BrE)
(of a duck) to make its characteristic harsh sound.
(of a duck) make a quacking sound

Examples

The ducks quack loudly every morning outside my window.
Children laughed as they watched the ducklings quack and splash in the pond.
As soon as he approached, the mother duck quacked in warning.

Synset Examples

The ducks quacked

Etymology

Imitative; from the sound made by a duck.

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ kwæk / (AmE), / kwæk / (BrE)
a person who dishonestly claims to have medical skills or qualifications; a fraudulent or unqualified doctor
fraudulent or unqualified doctor

Examples

The authorities arrested the quack after his treatments were found to be dangerous and ineffective.
Desperate for help, some patients turned to a quack who promised miraculous cures.
His medical license was revoked when it was revealed he was a quack with no real qualifications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charlatan - a person falsely claiming to have special knowledge or skill
  • Antonym: doctor - a person qualified to practice medicine

Etymology

Originally imitative, shortened from 'quacksalver' (early 17th century), from Dutch 'kwakzalver', meaning 'hawker of salve'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10516787-n
Synset POS: n

5. VERB

/ kwæk / (AmE), / kwæk / (BrE)
to act as a pretended medical expert; to fraudulently claim medical knowledge or skill
pretend to be a medical expert

Examples

The self-proclaimed healer was accused of trying to quack his way into the hospital's confidence.
Some people quack about miraculous cures without any real medical knowledge.
She warned her friend not to quack and mislead patients with false claims.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charlatan - a person who falsely claims to have special knowledge or skill, especially in medicine
  • Synonym: impostor - someone who pretends to be someone else to deceive others

Etymology

Of imitative origin, from Middle Dutch 'quacksalver' meaning one who quacks (boasts) about salves.

Source: WordNet
