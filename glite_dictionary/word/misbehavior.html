misbehavior

1. NOUN

/ mɪs.bɪˈheɪ.vjɚ / (AmE), / mɪs.bɪˈheɪ.vjə / (BrE)
improper, wicked, or immoral behavior; conduct that does not conform to accepted standards.
improper or immoral conduct

Examples

The teacher would not tolerate any misbehavior in her classroom.
Repeated misbehavior can lead to suspension from school.
His parents discussed the consequences of misbehavior with him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bad behavior - unacceptable or improper actions
  • Antonym: good behavior - conduct that conforms to accepted standards

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + behavior, modeled after similar formations like 'misbehave.'
