foolishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfuːlɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈfuːlɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
a thoughtless or unwise action, especially one regarded as a mistake
a stupid or unwise mistake

Examples

She quickly realized her foolishness after sending the email to the wrong person.
His foolishness in ignoring the warning led to an accident.
That foolishness cost them the championship game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunder - a careless or stupid mistake
  • Antonym: wisdom - the quality of being wise

Etymology

From fool + -ish + -ness, ultimately from Old French fol (foolish) and Latin follis (bellows, windbag).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfuːlɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈfuːlɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of lacking good sense or judgment; the state of being foolish
lack of good sense or judgment

Examples

She regretted her foolishness after realizing the consequences of her actions.
It was pure foolishness to go hiking alone in such weather.
His foolishness made everyone around him shake their heads in disbelief.

Synset Examples

trying to drive through a blizzard is the height of folly
adjusting to an insane society is total foolishness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folly - lack of good sense or foolish action
  • Antonym: wisdom - the quality of having experience, knowledge, and good judgment

Etymology

From 'foolish' + '-ness', with 'foolish' from Old French 'fol', from Latin 'follis' (bellows, windbag).

3. NOUN

/ ˈfuːlɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈfuːlɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of lacking good sense or judgment; acting in a silly or imprudent way
lack of good sense; silliness

Examples

She regretted her foolishness after seeing the consequences of her actions.
His foolishness cost him both time and money in the end.
Sometimes the foolishness of youth can lead to valuable lessons.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wisdom - the quality of having experience, knowledge, and good judgment
  • Synonym: folly - lack of good sense; foolish action

Etymology

From Middle English 'folysshnesse', from 'foolish' + '-ness'.
