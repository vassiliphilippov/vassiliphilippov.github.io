to ostracise

1. VERB

/ ˈɑː.strə.saɪz / (AmE), / ˈɒs.trə.saɪz / (BrE)
to deliberately exclude someone from a social group or community
exclude from a group or community

Examples

After the incident, the club decided to ostracise him from all future meetings.
She felt hurt when her classmates began to ostracise her for her opinions.
Some cultures historically ostracised those who broke social norms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exclude - to keep someone out of a place, group, or privilege
  • Synonym: to shun - to deliberately avoid someone or something
  • Antonym: welcome - to greet or accept willingly into a group or place

Etymology

From Greek 'ostrakizein' (to banish by voting with potsherds), via Latin 'ostracizare'.

2. VERB

/ ˈɑstrəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɒstrəsaɪz / (BrE)
to deliberately exclude someone from a group or society by refusing to associate with them
to exclude from a group or society

Examples

After the scandal, the community decided to ostracise him from all local events.
She felt hurt when her friends began to ostracise her at school.
If you speak against the group, they may ostracise you for your opinions.

Synset Examples

Ever since I spoke up, my colleagues ostracize me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shun - to deliberately avoid someone or something
  • Antonym: welcome - to greet or include warmly; the opposite of exclusion

Etymology

From Greek ostrakizein (to banish by voting with pottery shards called ostraka). Spelling influenced by the practice in ancient Athens.
