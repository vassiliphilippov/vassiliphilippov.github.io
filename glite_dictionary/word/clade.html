clade

1. NOUN

/ kleɪd / (AmE), / kleɪd / (BrE)
a group of organisms thought to have evolved from a common ancestor, comprising all its evolutionary descendants
group of organisms from a common ancestor

Examples

Scientists identified a new clade of birds that shows unique evolutionary traits.
All members of the mammalian clade share certain skeletal features.
The study examined the genetic relationships within the clade of flowering plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lineage - direct descent from an ancestor; ancestry or pedigree

Etymology

From Greek 'klados' meaning 'branch'.
