attitude

1. NOUN

/ ˈæ.t̬ɪ.tuːd / (AmE), / ˈæt.ɪ.tjuːd / (BrE)
a deliberate pose or posture assumed for effect, especially in art or theater.
deliberate pose or posture (art/theater)

Examples

The actor struck a dramatic attitude in the spotlight, capturing everyone's attention.
During the performance, she held an elegant attitude that reflected classical ballet.
He adopted a heroic attitude on stage to emphasize the character's strength.

Synset Examples

the actor struck just the right attitude

Etymology

From French attitude (posture, disposition), from Italian attitudine (posture, disposition), from Late Latin aptitūdō (fitness, suitability).

2. NOUN

/ ˈætɪˌtjud / (AmE), / ˈætɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
a settled way of thinking or feeling about someone or something, typically reflected in a person's behavior
a way of thinking or feeling

Examples

Her positive attitude made her a pleasure to work with.
It's important to have the right attitude in difficult situations.
His attitude towards homework changed after he got better grades.

Synset Examples

he had the attitude that work was fun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mindset - a person's way of thinking and their opinions
  • Synonym: disposition - a person's inherent qualities of mind and character
  • Antonym: indifference - lack of interest or concern

Etymology

From French 'attitude', from Italian 'attitudine' (disposition, posture), from Latin 'aptitudo' (fitness).

3. NOUN

/ ˈæt̬ɪˌtud / (AmE), / ˈætɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
the orientation or position of an aircraft or spacecraft in relation to a specific reference point, such as the horizon or the direction of motion
position/orientation of a vehicle in flight

Examples

The pilot adjusted the attitude of the jet to prepare for landing.
Maintaining the correct attitude is critical when docking a spacecraft.
Sensors monitor the satellite's attitude to ensure it remains properly oriented.

Etymology

From Latin 'aptitudo' (fitness, posture, disposition), via French 'attitude'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈæt̬.ə.tuːd / (AmE), / ˈæt.ɪ.tjuːd / (BrE)
A posture or position of the body or its limbs, especially as expressive of a particular feeling or intent.
posture or position of the body

Examples

The instructor showed the dancers the correct attitude for the classical pose.
He adopted a defensive attitude when questioned by the police.
The statue’s attitude suggested movement and grace.

Synset Examples

he assumed an attitude of surrender

Related Terms

  • Synonym: posture - the way in which someone holds their body when standing or sitting
  • Synonym: pose - a particular way of standing or sitting, usually adopted for effect or to be photographed

Etymology

From Latin *aptitūdō* (fitness, posture), via French *attitude* (posture, position).
