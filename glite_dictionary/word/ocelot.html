ocelot

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.səˌlɑːt / (AmE), / ˈɒs.ɪ.lɒt / (BrE)
a wildcat found in Central and South America, noted for its nocturnal habits and distinctive dark-spotted, buff-brown coat
a medium-sized wildcat with spotted coat

Examples

The ocelot prowls the rainforest floor silently at night, hunting small mammals and birds.
Zookeepers monitor the ocelot population closely to ensure their health and well-being.
An ocelot was spotted crossing the road near the national park during dusk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wildcat - any small to medium-sized wild member of the cat family

Etymology

From Nahuatl *ōcēlōtl* (jaguar), adopted into Spanish.
