Cnicus benedictus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪnɪkəs bəˈnɛdɪktəs / (AmE), / ˈsaɪnɪkəs bɛˈnɛdɪktəs / (BrE)
an annual, hairy-stemmed Mediterranean plant with spiny-toothed leaves and large yellow flower heads, also known as blessed thistle, used in herbal medicine
a Mediterranean medicinal plant; blessed thistle

Examples

Herbalists often recommend Cnicus benedictus to aid digestion and stimulate appetite.
The yellow flowers of Cnicus benedictus stand out in Mediterranean gardens during spring.
Researchers are studying the medicinal compounds found in Cnicus benedictus.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Cnicus (from Greek knikos, a thistle) and benedictus ('blessed' in Latin), referring to its reputed medicinal properties.
