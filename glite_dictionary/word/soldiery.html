soldiery

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊl.dʒə.ri / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.dʒə.ri / (BrE)
soldiers collectively; military forces considered as a group
soldiers collectively

Examples

The general praised the bravery of his soldiery after a successful campaign.
Much of the medieval castle was designed for the accommodation of the soldiery.
Throughout history, poets have sung about the loyalty and sacrifice of the soldiery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: military - armed forces collectively
  • Synonym: army - organized military force

Etymology

From Old French 'soudier' (to pay), from 'soude' (pay), referring to the pay of soldiers.
