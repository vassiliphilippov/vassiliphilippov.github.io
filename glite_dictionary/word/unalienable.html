unalienable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈeɪ.li.ə.nə.bəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈeɪ.li.ə.nə.bəl / (BrE)
(of a right or property) impossible to take away, deny, or transfer to another person.
impossible to transfer or take away

Examples

The Declaration of Independence refers to certain unalienable rights, such as life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness.
Many believe that freedom of speech is an unalienable right belonging to every person.
Some legal philosophers argue that human dignity is an unalienable aspect of our identity.

Synset Examples

endowed by their Creator with certain unalienable rights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inalienable - impossible to take away, deny, or transfer to another person

Etymology

From un- (not) + alienable (from Latin alienare, 'to transfer'), meaning 'not able to be transferred'.
