rotundity

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈtʌndɪti / (AmE), / rəʊˈtʌndɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being round or spherical, especially in a three-dimensional object
the state or quality of being round

Examples

The rotundity of the new sculpture attracted the attention of many visitors to the gallery.
Astronomers study the rotundity of planets to understand their formation and structure.
She admired the rotundity of the ripe pumpkin sitting on the porch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roundness - the quality of being round in shape

Etymology

From Latin 'rotunditas', from 'rotundus' (round).

2. NOUN

/ roʊˈtʌndəti / (AmE), / rəʊˈtʌndəti / (BrE)
the quality of being full or rich in tone; resonance or roundness of sound
fullness or richness of sound

Examples

The rotundity of his voice filled the concert hall with warmth.
Her speech was marked by a pleasing rotundity that captivated the audience.
The cello's rotundity contrasted beautifully with the violin's sharpness.

Synset Examples

there is a musky roundness to his wordiness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resonance - the quality in a sound of being deep, full, and reverberating
  • Antonym: thinness - lacking fullness or richness in sound

Etymology

From Latin 'rotunditas', from 'rotundus' meaning 'round'.
