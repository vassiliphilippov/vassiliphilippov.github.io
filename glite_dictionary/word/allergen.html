allergen

1. NOUN

/ ˈælərˌdʒən / (AmE), / ˈælədʒən / (BrE)
A substance that causes an allergic reaction in some people.
substance causing allergy
medical

Examples

Pollen is a common allergen that can trigger hay fever symptoms in many people.
Some foods, such as peanuts and shellfish, are frequent allergens for children.
Doctors recommend avoiding known allergens to reduce the risk of allergic reactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allergy - a condition in which the immune system reacts abnormally to a substance
  • Degree Lower: antigen - a substance that induces an immune response

Etymology

From Greek 'allos' (other) + 'genein' (to produce), meaning 'producing something different.'
