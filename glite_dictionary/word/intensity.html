intensity

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛnsəti / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛnsəti / (BrE)
the quality of being intense; the degree or strength of something such as feeling, activity, or light
degree of strength or force

Examples

The intensity of the storm surprised even veteran meteorologists.
She spoke with such intensity that everyone listened in silence.
This workout increases the intensity of your exercise gradually over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strength - the quality or state of being strong
  • Antonym: weakness - the state or condition of lacking strength
  • Synonym: power - the ability or capacity to do something or act in a particular way

Etymology

From Medieval Latin intensitas, from Latin intensus (stretched, intense).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛnsəti / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛnsɪti / (BrE)
The degree of chromatic purity of a color, describing its vividness or freedom from dilution with white.
degree of chromatic purity in color

Examples

The intensity of the red paint made the artwork especially striking.
Artists often adjust the intensity of a color by mixing it with white or gray.
High intensity hues can create a vivid and lively effect in a painting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saturation - the intensity or purity of a color
  • Antonym: pastel - a color with low intensity; pale hue

Etymology

From Latin 'intensitas' (vehemence, strength), from 'intensus' (stretched, intense).

/ ɪnˈtɛnsəti / (AmE), / ɪnˈtensəti / (BrE)
the strength or amount of energy that something, such as sound, light, or electromagnetic waves, transmits or carries

Examples

By increasing the intensity of the laser, they were able to cut through tougher materials.
