recoverable

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈkʌvərəbəl / (AmE), / rɪˈkʌvərəbəl / (BrE)
able to be recovered, regained, or retrieved; not irretrievably lost.
able to be recovered or regained

Examples

The lost files were surprisingly recoverable thanks to advanced software.
Many natural habitats are only partially recoverable after severe human impact.
Insurance only covers recoverable losses, not sentimental value.

Synset Examples

recoverable truth of a past event

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irrecoverable - not able to be recovered or regained
  • Synonym: retrievable - able to be retrieved or recovered

Etymology

From recover + -able, based on Latin 'recuperare' (to recover).
