remarkable

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈmɑrkəbəl / (AmE), / rɪˈmɑːkəbəl / (BrE)
Worthy of attention or notice; extraordinary or unusual.
worthy of attention; extraordinary

Examples

Her recovery after such a serious illness was truly remarkable.
The scientist made a remarkable discovery that changed the field.
That building is remarkable for its unusual design.

Synset Examples

a noteworthy fact is that her students rarely complain
a noteworthy advance in cancer research
a remarkable achievement
a notable increase in the crime rate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notable - worthy of attention or notice
  • Antonym: ordinary - not unusual or remarkable; normal

Etymology

From French remarquable, from remarquer (to remark, to notice).

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈmɑrkəbəl / (AmE), / rɪˈmɑːkəbəl / (BrE)
unusual or surprising and therefore worth noticing; striking or extraordinary.
unusual or extraordinary; worth noticing

Examples

Her recovery from the illness was truly remarkable and surprised the doctors.
It's a remarkable achievement for someone so young to win an international prize.
The landscape was filled with remarkable rock formations that attracted many tourists.

Synset Examples

a remarkable sight
such poise is singular in one so young

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extraordinary - very unusual or remarkable
  • Antonym: ordinary - not unusual; normal

Etymology

From French remarquable, from remarquer (to notice), from re- (again) + marquer (to mark).
