forthcoming

1. ADJ

/ fɔrθˈkʌmɪŋ / (AmE), / fɔːθˈkʌmɪŋ / (BrE)
willing to talk or give information to others; open and communicative.
willing to talk or share information

Examples

Although he seemed shy at first, Mark was quite forthcoming about his career once we started talking.
The spokesperson was unusually forthcoming with details during the press conference.
She was not very forthcoming when asked about the project's delays.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reticent - not revealing one's thoughts or feelings readily
  • Synonym: communicative - willing to impart information; talkative

Etymology

From Middle English, formed from 'forth' + 'coming'; originally meaning 'approaching', sense of 'communicative' developed later.

2. ADJ

/ fɔrθˈkʌmɪŋ / (AmE), / fɔːθˈkʌmɪŋ / (BrE)
happening or appearing soon; about to take place in the near future
about to happen soon

Examples

The school announced the schedule for the forthcoming exams yesterday.
Tickets for the forthcoming concert went on sale this morning.
She is preparing for her forthcoming trip to Paris.

Synset Examples

the approaching election
this coming Thursday
the forthcoming holidays
the upcoming spring fashions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upcoming - about to happen; occurring soon
  • Synonym: imminent - likely to happen very soon

Etymology

From Old English 'fore' (before) + 'come' (to appear, arrive).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01736850-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌfɔrθˈkʌmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌfɔːθˈkʌmɪŋ / (BrE)
available when needed or promised; ready to be provided or given when required
ready or available when needed

Examples

The report was requested last month, but it is still not forthcoming.
Despite multiple emails, no apology was forthcoming from the company.
Although funds had been promised, government support was not forthcoming when the crisis hit.

Synset Examples

federal funds were not forthcoming

Related Terms

  • Synonym: available - able to be used or obtained; at someone's disposal
  • Antonym: unavailable - not able to be used or obtained

Etymology

From Middle English 'forthcomen' (to come forth), from Old English 'forth' (forward) + 'cuman' (to come).

Synset POS: s
