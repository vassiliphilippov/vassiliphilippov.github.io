Hippobosca equina

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpəˈbɑskə ɪˈkwaɪnə / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈbɒskə ɪˈkwiːnə / (BrE)
A winged parasitic fly that infests horses, known for feeding on their blood.
parasitic horsefly species

Examples

The veterinarian identified the insect on the mare as a Hippobosca equina.
Farmers often take measures to protect their horses from Hippobosca equina infestations.
The bite of a Hippobosca equina can cause irritation and discomfort to horses.

Etymology

From scientific Latin; 'Hippobosca' from Greek 'hippos' (horse) and 'boskein' (to feed); 'equina' from Latin 'equus' (horse).
