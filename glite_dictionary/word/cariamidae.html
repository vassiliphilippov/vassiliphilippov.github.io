Cariamidae

1. NOUN

/ kɛr.iˈæm.ɪˌdi / (AmE), / kær.iˈæm.ɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of crane-like wading birds native to South America, known for their long legs and predatory habits
family of South American crane-like birds

Examples

The Cariamidae are commonly found in grasslands and open forests of South America.
Unlike true cranes, the Cariamidae often hunt on foot for insects and small vertebrates.
Scientists have recently studied the evolutionary history of the Cariamidae family.

Etymology

From New Latin Cariamidae, based on the type genus Cariama.
