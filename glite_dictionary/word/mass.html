Mass

1. NOUN

/ mæs / (AmE), / mɑːs / (BrE)
the celebration of the Eucharist, especially as a central act of worship in the Roman Catholic Church and some Protestant Churches.
Christian celebration of the Eucharist

Examples

We attend Mass every Sunday at our local church.
The priest celebrated Mass in honor of the feast day.
During Christmas Mass, the church was filled with families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eucharist - Christian ceremony commemorating the Last Supper

Etymology

From Latin missa (dismissal), originally referring to the concluding words of the service.

2. NOUN

/ mæs / (AmE), / mæs / (BrE)
the celebration of the Christian Eucharist, especially in the Roman Catholic Church, including a sequence of prayers and rituals conducted by a priest
Christian Eucharistic celebration

Examples

We attended Mass at the cathedral on Sunday morning.
The priest will say Mass every day during Holy Week.
Many people gathered for the Christmas Mass to celebrate together.

Synset Examples

the priest said Mass

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eucharist - Christian rite commemorating the Last Supper
  • Synonym: service - a formal religious ceremony

Etymology

From Latin 'missa', meaning 'dismissal', referring to the concluding words of the service.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07047100-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ mæs / (AmE), / mæs / (BrE)
a musical composition that sets the texts of the Mass (the Roman Catholic liturgy) to music, typically for choir and orchestra
musical composition for the Mass

Examples

The choir performed a Mass by Mozart during the Easter service.
She prefers the solemn beauty of a Renaissance Mass to other sacred music.
Bach's 'B minor Mass' is considered one of his greatest masterpieces.

Synset Examples

they played a Mass composed by Beethoven

Related Terms

  • Synonym: requiem - a Mass for the dead or a musical composition for such a service

Etymology

From Latin 'missa' (the Mass), as in the liturgical service; transferred to the musical sense.
