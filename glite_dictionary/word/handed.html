handed

1. ADJ

/ ˈhændɪd / (AmE), / ˈhændɪd / (BrE)
Having or involving a specified number or use of hands.
having or involving use of hands

Examples

The tournament featured a four-handed bridge match.
Some primates are uniquely handed in their movements.
The new board game can be played in both two-handed and handed versions.

Synset Examples

a handed, tree-living animal
a four-handed card game

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: one-handed - involving the use of one hand
  • Degree Higher: two-handed - involving the use of two hands

Etymology

Formed from 'hand' + '-ed', used to describe possession or use of hands.
