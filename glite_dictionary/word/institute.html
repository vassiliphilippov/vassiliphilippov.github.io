institute

1. VERB

/ ˈɪn.stəˌtut / (AmE), / ˈɪn.stɪ.tjuːt / (BrE)
to begin or start something, especially a legal action; to initiate or establish officially
to formally begin or start (esp. legal action)

Examples

The company decided to institute legal proceedings against its former employee.
She threatened to institute a lawsuit if the issues were not resolved.
After months of investigation, the government finally instituted criminal charges against the organization.

Synset Examples

bring charges
institute proceedings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: initiate - to begin or start something
  • Synonym: to commence - to begin; to start
  • Antonym: to terminate - to bring to an end

Etymology

From Latin 'instituere' meaning 'to set up, establish'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01621566-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪnstɪˌtuːt / (AmE), / ˈɪnstɪtjuːt / (BrE)
an organization founded for a particular purpose, especially one that promotes education, art, or science
organization for education, art, or science

Examples

He was invited to deliver a lecture at the institute last month.
The medical institute conducts pioneering research in cancer treatment.
She received a fellowship from a leading art institute in Paris.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: academy - a society or institution of distinguished scholars, artists, or scientists
  • Synonym: organization - an organized group of people with a particular purpose

Etymology

From Latin institutum (an established plan, institution), from instituere (to set up, establish).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08424287-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈɪn.stəˌtuːt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.stɪ.tjuːt / (BrE)
to start or establish something, especially a system, rule, or process
to set up or establish (something)

Examples

The government plans to institute new regulations to ensure workplace safety.
After the scandal, the organization instituted a strict code of conduct for all employees.
The university will institute an annual award for outstanding research.

Synset Examples

establish a new department

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to establish - to set up or found something
  • Antonym: to abolish - to formally put an end to a system or practice

Etymology

From Latin institutum (an arrangement, purpose), from instituere (to set up, establish).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01650771-v
Synset POS: v
