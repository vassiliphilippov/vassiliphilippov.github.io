xerotes

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɛrəˌtiːz / (AmE), / ˈzɪərɒtiːz / (BrE)
The state or condition of dryness, especially the absence of moisture or liquid.
dryness; lack of moisture

Examples

The xerotes of the desert air made dehydration a constant threat for explorers.
Botanists study how plants survive under extreme xerotes.
She noticed the xerotes of the soil after weeks without rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dryness - the state of being dry; lack of moisture

Etymology

From Greek xērótēs (dryness), from xēros (dry).
