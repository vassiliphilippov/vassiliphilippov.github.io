Lupinus subcarnosus

1. NOUN

/ luˈpaɪnəs sʌbˌkɑːrˈnoʊsəs / (AmE), / ljuːˈpaɪnəs sʌbˌkɑːˈnəʊsəs / (BrE)
A low-growing annual herb native to southwestern United States (especially Texas), with silky foliage and blue flowers; known to cause livestock poisoning in the region.
a Texan blue-flowered lupine species, toxic to livestock

Examples

The fields in Texas bloom each spring with Lupinus subcarnosus, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
Farmers must watch their cattle closely around Lupinus subcarnosus to prevent accidental poisoning.
Identification of Lupinus subcarnosus is important due to its toxic properties for livestock.

Etymology

From Latin 'Lupinus' (wolf-like, a genus of plants) and Latin 'subcarnosus' (somewhat fleshy).
