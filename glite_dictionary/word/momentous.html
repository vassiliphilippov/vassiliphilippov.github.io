momentous

1. ADJ

/ moʊˈmɛn.təs / (AmE), / məʊˈmɛn.təs / (BrE)
very important because of its possible results or effects; of great significance
of great significance

Examples

The signing of the peace treaty was a momentous occasion in the nation's history.
Her graduation day was a momentous milestone for the entire family.
Scientists hailed the discovery as a momentous achievement in medical research.

Synset Examples

a momentous event
deciding to drop the atom bomb was a very momentous decision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: important - having great meaning or value
  • Antonym: insignificant - not important or lacking significance

Etymology

From Latin 'momentosus' (important, momentous), from 'momentum' (movement, importance).
