to explicate

1. VERB

/ ˈɛksplɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛksplɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to make the meaning of something clear and comprehensible, especially through detailed explanation or analysis
to explain something clearly

Examples

The professor took time to explicate the complex theory for her students.
In her essay, she will explicate the main themes of the novel.
The guide carefully explicated the meaning behind each symbol in the painting.

Synset Examples

He explained the laws of physics to his students

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to explain - to make something clear or easy to understand
  • Synonym: to clarify - to make something easier to understand
  • Antonym: obscure - to make something unclear or difficult to understand

Etymology

From Latin explicat-, past participle of explicare (to unfold, explain).

2. VERB

/ ˈɛksplɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛksplɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to explain an idea, theory, or work in detail; to make something clear by elaborating on it
to explain in detail; make clear

Examples

The professor asked her to explicate the underlying theory behind her research.
He spent an hour explicating the poem to the students.
In your essay, you should explicate the main arguments clearly and logically.

Synset Examples

Could you develop the ideas in your thesis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to explain - make (an idea or situation) clear; describe in more detail

Etymology

From Latin explicare (to unfold, explain).

Synset POS: v
