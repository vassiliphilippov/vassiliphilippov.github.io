sinusoidal

1. ADJ

/ ˌsaɪnəˈsɔɪdəl / (AmE), / ˌsɪnjuːˈsɔɪdəl / (BrE)
having the form or characteristics of a sine wave; resembling a series of smooth, regular waves or curves.
resembling a sine wave

Examples

The electrical current followed a sinusoidal pattern over time.
The graph displays a sinusoidal curve representing the oscillating temperature.
Many natural phenomena, such as sound waves, have sinusoidal characteristics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wavy - having a form or surface that moves up and down like waves
  • Antonym: linear - forming a straight line, not curved

Etymology

From 'sine' + '-oidal', from Latin 'sinus' (curve, fold) and Greek '-oeidēs' (form, shape).
