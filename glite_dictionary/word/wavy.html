wavy

1. ADJ

/ ˈweɪvi / (AmE), / ˈweɪvi / (BrE)
(especially of hair) having a series of smooth curves; slightly curly.
having a series of smooth curves

Examples

She admired her friend's wavy hair, which caught the sunlight beautifully.
The stylist suggested adding some wavy layers for a softer look.
After a day at the beach, his usually straight hair became wavy.

Synset Examples

she had long wavy hair

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: curly - (of hair) having curls or tending to curl
  • Antonym: straight - (of hair) having no curls or waves

Etymology

From wave + -y, indicating 'having the quality of waves'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈweɪ.vi / (AmE), / ˈweɪ.vi / (BrE)
having a surface or shape characterized by a series of smooth curves or undulations; not straight or flat
having waves or smooth curves

Examples

The artist painted the wavy lines of the ocean on the canvas.
Her wavy hair glistened in the sunlight after a swim.
The fabric had a wavy texture, making the dress look unique.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straight - having no curves or bends; not wavy or curly

Etymology

From 'wave' + '-y', denoting possession of waves or wave-like qualities.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00914876-s
Synset POS: s
