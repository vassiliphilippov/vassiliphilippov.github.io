Senna alata

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnə əˈlɑtə / (AmE), / ˈsɛnə əˈlɑːtə / (BrE)
A tropical shrub native to the Americas, with yellow flowers and large leaves, whose juice is traditionally used to treat ringworm and poisonous bites.
tropical shrub with medicinal uses

Examples

The leaves of Senna alata are widely used in traditional medicine to treat skin infections.
In the botanical garden, Senna alata stands out for its striking yellow flowers and lush foliage.
Researchers are studying Senna alata for its potential antifungal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cassia alata - another scientific name for Senna alata

Etymology

From Latin: 'Senna' (a plant genus) and 'alata' (winged), referring to the shape of the plant's pods.
