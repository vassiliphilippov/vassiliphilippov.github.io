mimicker

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪmɪkər / (AmE), / ˈmɪmɪkə / (BrE)
a person who imitates the actions, speech, or mannerisms of others, especially as a form of entertainment or performance
person who imitates others

Examples

The actor is a talented mimicker who can perfectly imitate various celebrities.
As a child, she was a natural mimicker, copying her teachers' voices to make her friends laugh.
Many comedians are skilled mimickers, capturing the unique gestures of famous politicians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imitator - a person who copies the behavior or actions of others

Etymology

From mimic + -er (agent noun suffix).
