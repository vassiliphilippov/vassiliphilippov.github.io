Pilgrim

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɪlɡrəm / (AmE), / ˈpɪlɡrɪm / (BrE)
one of the English colonists who sailed to America on the Mayflower and founded the Plymouth Colony in 1620
early English colonist in Plymouth, 1620

Examples

Many Pilgrims faced harsh conditions during their first winter in the New World.
The story of the Pilgrims landing at Plymouth is taught in American schools.
Thanksgiving commemorates the feast shared by the Pilgrims and Native Americans.

Etymology

From Middle French *pèlerin*, from Latin *peregrinus* (foreigner, traveler).
