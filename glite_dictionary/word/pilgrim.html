pilgrim

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪlgrɪm / (AmE), / ˈpɪlgrɪm / (BrE)
a person who journeys to a sacred place for religious reasons
person on a religious journey

Examples

Each year, thousands of pilgrims visit Mecca to perform the Hajj.
The pilgrims walked for days to reach the holy shrine.
During the festival, the city is filled with pilgrims from around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traveler - person who travels, especially to distant or unfamiliar places

Etymology

From Latin 'peregrinus' (foreigner, traveler), via Old French 'pelerin'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪlɡrəm / (AmE), / ˈpɪlɡrɪm / (BrE)
a person who journeys to a foreign land, often for exploration or adventure, typically for a particular purpose
person who journeys in foreign lands

Examples

The lone pilgrim wandered across the desert, marveling at the strange new landscape.
As a young man, he was a pilgrim in South America, searching for meaning in distant lands.
The journal chronicles a pilgrim's adventures while traveling through unfamiliar countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traveler - a person who travels from place to place

Etymology

From Latin peregrinus (foreigner, wanderer), via Old French pelerin.
