braiding

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbreɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
decorative trimming made from interwoven threads or strips, used to adorn clothes, curtains, or upholstery
decorative trimming made by braiding

Examples

The tailor chose gold braiding to embellish the cuffs of the uniform.
Velvet curtains with intricate braiding hung in the grand hall.
She sewed a strip of blue braiding along the hem of the dress for decoration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: braid - a length of interwoven threads or strips used for trimming

Etymology

From braid + -ing, indicating the product of braiding.
