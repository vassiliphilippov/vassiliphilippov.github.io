averageness

1. NOUN

/ ˈæv.ɚ.ɪdʒ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈæv.ər.ɪdʒ.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being average; ordinary or mediocre in character, ability, or extent
the state of being average or ordinary

Examples

The film's averageness made it forgettable among all the summer blockbusters.
He sometimes worries that his averageness will limit his career opportunities.
Striving for more than averageness can lead to surprising achievements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mediocrity - the quality of being only average or not very good
  • Antonym: excellence - the quality of being outstanding or extremely good
  • Degree Higher: superiority - the state of being superior to others
  • Degree Lower: inferiority - the state of being lower in quality or status

Etymology

Formed from 'average' + suffix '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæv.ɚ.ɪdʒ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈæv.ər.ɪdʒ.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being average or ordinary, lacking in distinction or exceptional features.
the quality of being average or unremarkable

Examples

Despite his averageness, he managed to find a place in the team through hard work.
Mary worried that her averageness made her invisible among her more talented peers.
The movie was criticized for its averageness and lack of originality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mediocrity - the state of being average or ordinary
  • Antonym: excellence - the quality of being outstanding or extremely good

Etymology

Formed from 'average' + '-ness', denoting state or quality.
