tartly

1. ADV

/ ˈtɑrt.li / (AmE), / ˈtɑːt.li / (BrE)
in a way that is sharp, bitter, or cutting in tone or manner
in a sharp or cutting manner

Examples

She replied tartly that she was not interested in his opinion.
The teacher tartly reminded the students to finish their assignments on time.
'I can see you're busy,' he said tartly, turning away.

Synset Examples

‘Never mind your immortal soul,’ she said tartly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharply - in a critical or cutting manner
  • Antonym: gently - in a mild or gentle manner

Etymology

From 'tart' (sharp in tone or taste) + -ly (adverb-forming suffix).
