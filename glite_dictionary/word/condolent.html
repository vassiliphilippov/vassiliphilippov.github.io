condolent

1. ADJ

/ kənˈdoʊlənt / (AmE), / kənˈdəʊlənt / (BrE)
expressing sympathy with someone who has experienced the death of a loved one
expressing sympathy in bereavement

Examples

The condolent tone of her letter brought comfort to the grieving family.
A condolent friend attended the funeral to offer his support.
His words were delicately condolent, acknowledging their profound loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sympathetic - feeling or showing sympathy
  • Antonym: unsympathetic - not showing compassion or understanding

Etymology

From Latin 'condolentem', present participle of 'condolere' (to suffer together, sympathize).
