to necrose

1. VERB

/ nɪˈkroʊs / (AmE), / nɪˈkrəʊz / (BrE)
(of tissue or cells) to die or decay, especially as a result of injury or disease; to undergo necrosis.
(of tissue) to undergo necrosis
medical

Examples

The infected skin began to necrose after several days without treatment.
If the blood supply is cut off, the organ may necrose rapidly.
Surgeons removed tissue that had started to necrose due to poor circulation.

Synset Examples

the tissue around the wound necrosed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: necrosis - the death of body tissue

Etymology

From Greek nekros (dead body), related to "necrosis".
