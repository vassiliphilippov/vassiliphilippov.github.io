microsome

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪkrəˌsoʊm / (AmE), / ˈmʌɪkrəˌsəʊm / (BrE)
a tiny granule in the cytoplasm of a cell where protein synthesis occurs under the direction of messenger RNA (mRNA), often formed during cell fractionation
cellular granule for protein synthesis
medical

Examples

The scientist isolated microsomes from the liver cells to study protein synthesis.
Microsome fractions are useful in examining cellular enzyme activity.
Researchers observed that certain drugs are metabolized in the microsomes of the cell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ribosome - cellular structure where proteins are synthesized

Etymology

From Greek mikros (small) + sōma (body).
