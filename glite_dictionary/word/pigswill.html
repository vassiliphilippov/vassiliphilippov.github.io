pigswill

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪɡˌswɪl / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡˌswɪl / (BrE)
Wet food, typically made from kitchen waste and water or milk, used as feed for pigs.
wet pig feed from kitchen waste

Examples

The farmers collected pigswill from local restaurants to feed their animals.
Health regulations restrict the use of untreated pigswill on commercial pig farms.
He described the soup as tasting like pigswill, much to the chef's displeasure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slop - wet food waste fed to pigs or other animals

Etymology

From 'pig' + 'swill' (to wash or feed with refuse); 'swill' from Old English 'swilian' (to wash).
