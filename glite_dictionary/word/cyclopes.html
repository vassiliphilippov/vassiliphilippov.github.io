Cyclopes

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈkloʊpiːz / (AmE), / sɪˈkləʊpiːz / (BrE)
A genus of small arboreal anteaters native to Central and South America, commonly known as silky anteaters.
genus of silky anteaters

Examples

The Cyclopes are remarkable for their small size and nocturnal habits.
Scientists studied the habitat preferences of Cyclopes in tropical forests.
The silky anteater, belonging to the genus Cyclopes, feeds primarily on ants and termites.

Etymology

From Greek 'Kyklōpēs' (singular 'Kyklōps'), the name of mythical one-eyed giants, in reference to the genus name.

2. NOUN

/ saɪˈkloʊpiːz / (AmE), / sɪˈkləʊpiːz / (BrE)
A genus of mammals in the family Cyclopedidae, consisting solely of the silky anteater, a small arboreal anteater found in Central and South America.
genus of silky anteaters

Examples

The Cyclopes genus includes only the silky anteater, native to tropical forests in Central and South America.
Researchers studied the habitat range of Cyclopes to better understand its adaptations to arboreal life.
Unlike other anteater genera, Cyclopes is represented by a single small species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silky anteater - a small arboreal anteater, the only species in the genus Cyclopes

Etymology

From Greek 'kyklōps' (Cyclops), used in taxonomy to denote singularity or distinctiveness.
