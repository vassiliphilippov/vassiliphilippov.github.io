Celastrus scandens

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈlæstrəs ˈskændɛnz / (AmE), / sɪˈlæstrəs ˈskændɛnz / (BrE)
A species of twining shrub native to North America, having yellow capsules that enclose scarlet seeds; commonly known as American bittersweet.
North American shrub with yellow capsules and scarlet seeds

Examples

Celastrus scandens is prized by gardeners for its colorful berries and climbing habit.
Many birds feed on the seeds of Celastrus scandens during the winter months.
The conservation group warned that Celastrus scandens could be threatened by habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American bittersweet - another common name for Celastrus scandens

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Celastrus' plus species epithet 'scandens' (climbing).
