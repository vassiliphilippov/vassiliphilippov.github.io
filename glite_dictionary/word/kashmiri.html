Kashmiri

1. ADJ

/ kæʃˈmiːri / (AmE), / kaʃˈmɪəri / (BrE)
of or relating to Kashmir, its people, or its culture
of or relating to Kashmir or its people

Examples

The chef prepared a traditional Kashmiri curry using authentic spices.
She wore a beautiful shawl woven in the distinctive Kashmiri style.
His research focuses on Kashmiri art and its historical influences.

Synset Examples

the Kashmiri mountains
Kashmiri love poems

Etymology

From Kashmir, the region in the northern part of the Indian subcontinent, with the suffix -i indicating origin or association.

2. NOUN

/ kæʃˈmɪri / (AmE), / kaʃˈmɪəri / (BrE)
the Indo-Aryan language spoken in the Kashmir region, serving as the official language of Jammu and Kashmir
language of the Kashmir region

Examples

Many poets have composed verses in Kashmiri that capture the beauty of the valley.
She is fluent in English, Hindi, and Kashmiri.
The film was dubbed into Kashmiri for local audiences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Urdu - official language of Pakistan and co-official in Jammu and Kashmir

Etymology

From Kashmir (region in northern India and Pakistan) + -i (denoting language or origin).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06985534-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ kæʃˈmɪri / (AmE), / kæʃˈmɪəri / (BrE)
a person who belongs to the native people of Kashmir, a region in the northern part of the Indian subcontinent
a person from Kashmir

Examples

Many Kashmiris have migrated to other parts of India in search of better opportunities.
The traditional dress of a Kashmiri is well suited for the cold climate of the region.
She met a group of Kashmiris who shared stories about life in the valley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kashmiri - the language spoken in Kashmir

Etymology

From Kashmir, the region in northern India and Pakistan, plus English suffix -i for demonymic formation.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09695506-n
Synset POS: n
