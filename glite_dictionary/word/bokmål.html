Bokmål

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊkˌmɔl / (AmE), / ˈbʊkmɔːl / (BrE)
one of two official written standards of the Norwegian language, based on Danish and widely used in Norway
official written standard of Norwegian

Examples

Most official documents in Norway are written in Bokmål.
She learned to read Norwegian using Bokmål textbooks in school.
There is often a choice between Bokmål and Nynorsk in Norwegian publications.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Nynorsk - the other official written standard of Norwegian
  • Synonym: Norwegian - the North Germanic language spoken mainly in Norway
  • Degree Higher: Danish - a North Germanic language closely related to Bokmål

Etymology

From Norwegian Bokmål, literally 'book language' (bok 'book' + mål 'language, speech').

2. NOUN

/ ˈboʊkˌmɔl / (AmE), / ˈbʊkmɔːl / (BrE)
one of two official written standards of the Norwegian language, closely related to Danish and used by a majority of Norwegians
official Norwegian written language standard

Examples

Many Norwegian students are taught to write in Bokmål at school.
The government publishes most official documents in both Bokmål and Nynorsk.
Her novel was originally written in Bokmål before being translated into English.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Nynorsk - the other official Norwegian written standard
  • Synonym: Norwegian - the North Germanic language spoken in Norway

Etymology

From Norwegian Bokmål, literally 'book language' (bok 'book' + mål 'language, speech').
