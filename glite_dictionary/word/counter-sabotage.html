counter-sabotage

1. NOUN

/ ˌkaʊntər səˈbɑːtɑːʒ / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntə səˈbɒtɑːʒ / (BrE)
Counterintelligence activities aimed at detecting and preventing sabotage.
counterintelligence against sabotage

Examples

The military established a counter-sabotage unit to protect critical infrastructure from enemy actions.
Effective counter-sabotage measures helped thwart several planned attacks on factory equipment.
During the war, governments invested heavily in counter-sabotage operations to ensure the security of supply lines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterintelligence - Intelligence activities to prevent spying and sabotage

Etymology

Formed from 'counter-' (against) and 'sabotage' (deliberate destruction), both from French roots.
