scriptural

1. ADJ

/ ˈskrɪptʃərəl / (AmE), / ˈskrɪptʃərəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of writing, especially of religious scriptures
relating to religious writings

Examples

The preacher offered a scriptural interpretation of the passage.
Her argument was supported by several scriptural references.
They debated the scriptural basis for the tradition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biblical - relating to the Bible or sacred writings

Etymology

From Latin scriptura (writing) + -al, meaning 'pertaining to writing.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02949698-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈskrɪptʃərəl / (AmE), / ˈskrɪptʃərəl / (BrE)
relating to, contained in, or consistent with the Bible or other sacred scriptures
relating to the Bible or sacred scripture

Examples

Her argument was firmly grounded in scriptural references.
The church's teachings are based on scriptural authority.
He spent years studying scriptural texts in their original languages.

Synset Examples

biblical names
biblical Hebrew

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biblical - relating to or contained in the Bible

Etymology

From Latin scriptūra (writing), from scrībere (to write).
