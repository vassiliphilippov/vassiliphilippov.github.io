welcome

1. VERB

/ ˈwɛlkəm / (AmE), / ˈwɛlkəm / (BrE)
to greet someone in a friendly way when they arrive, especially at your home or at an event
greet someone on arrival

Examples

We were happy to welcome our neighbors into our home for dinner.
She always welcomes guests with a warm smile.
They welcomed the new student to the class with a small celebration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to greet - to give a polite or friendly reception to someone

Etymology

From Old English 'wilcuma', meaning 'a person whose coming is pleasing'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01472727-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˈwɛlkəm / (AmE), / ˈwɛlkəm / (BrE)
The state of being gladly or hospitably received somewhere; the feeling or action of someone being received with pleasure.
the state of being gladly received

Examples

After three days, their welcome began to wear thin.
Please remember not to overstay your welcome as a guest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hospitality - friendly and generous reception of guests

Etymology

From Old English *wilcuma* (a welcome guest), from *will* (pleasure) + *cuman* (to come).
