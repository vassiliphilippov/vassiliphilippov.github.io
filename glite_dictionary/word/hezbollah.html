Hezbollah

1. PROPN

/ ˌhɛz.bəˈlɑ / (AmE), / ˌhɛz.bəˈlɑː / (BrE)
A Shiite Muslim political and militant group based in Lebanon, known for its strong ties to Iran and involvement in both political and military activities.
Shiite militant and political group in Lebanon

Examples

The Lebanese government has a complex relationship with Hezbollah and its political influence.
International authorities have imposed sanctions on Hezbollah due to its military activities.
Hezbollah has supporters and critics both within Lebanon and internationally.

Etymology

From Arabic ḥizb Allāh (حزب الله), meaning 'Party of God'.
