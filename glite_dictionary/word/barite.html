barite

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛrˌaɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɑːr.aɪt / (BrE)
A white or colorless mineral consisting of barium sulfate, used as the main source of barium.
white mineral; barium source

Examples

The geologists identified layers of barite within the sedimentary rock formation.
Barite is often ground to a fine powder and used in drilling fluids for oil exploration.
Large crystals of barite can be found in certain limestone deposits around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baryte - alternative spelling for barite, primarily British

Etymology

From Greek barys (heavy) due to its high specific gravity.
