Gentiana quinquefolia

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛnʃiˈænə ˌkwɪŋkwɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛnʃɪˈɑːnə ˌkwɪŋkwɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A species of gentian native to eastern North America, known for its clusters of bristly blue flowers.
a North American gentian with blue flowers

Examples

The Gentiana quinquefolia blooms in late summer, displaying vibrant blue flowers along forest edges.
Botanists identified the rare Gentiana quinquefolia growing near the Appalachian trail.
Gardeners prize Gentiana quinquefolia for its ornamental value and striking clusters of blossoms.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Gentiana' (named after Gentius, an ancient Illyrian king) and species epithet 'quinquefolia' meaning 'five-leaved'.
