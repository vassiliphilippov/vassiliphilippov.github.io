completed

1. ADJ

/ kəmˈplitɪd / (AmE), / kəmˈpliːtɪd / (BrE)
having been finished or brought to an end; fully accomplished.
having been finished; accomplished

Examples

The completed report was submitted to the manager for review.
Everyone celebrated when the renovations were finally completed last month.
She felt a sense of relief after the project was completed on time.

Synset Examples

his mission accomplished, he took a vacation
the completed project
the joy of a realized ambition overcame him

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfinished - not completed or brought to an end
  • Synonym: accomplished - successfully completed or achieved

Etymology

From Latin 'complēre' (to fill up, complete), past participle 'complētus'.

2. ADJ

/ kəmˈplitɪd / (AmE), / kəmˈpliːtɪd / (BrE)
(of a marriage) brought to a valid or full state by the first act of sexual intercourse after the ceremony.
of a marriage, consummated by intercourse

Examples

The marriage was not considered completed until the couple spent their first night together.
According to law, a completed marriage required consummation after the ceremony.
Historical records show that some unions were never completed due to annulment before consummation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consummated - (of a marriage) completed by sexual intercourse

Etymology

From Latin 'completus', past participle of 'complēre' (to fill up, complete).

Synset POS: s
