longevity

1. NOUN

/ lɑnˈdʒɛvəti / (AmE), / lɒnˈdʒɛvəti / (BrE)
The fact or quality of having a long life or existence; long duration of life or service.
long life or duration

Examples

The longevity of the tortoise is legendary, with some living more than 150 years.
She attributed her grandmother's longevity to a healthy diet and active lifestyle.
The longevity of that bridge is a testament to its solid construction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lifespan - the length of time something lives or lasts
  • Antonym: brevity - shortness of duration

Etymology

From Latin longaevitas, from longae(vus) (long-lived).

2. NOUN

/ lɑnˈdʒɛvəti / (AmE), / lɒnˈdʒɛvəti / (BrE)
the length of time a person remains in a particular job, position, or organization; duration of service
duration in a job or position

Examples

Her impressive longevity at the company earned her widespread respect among her colleagues.
The longevity of his political career surprised many critics.
Few athletes achieve such longevity in professional sports.

Synset Examples

her longevity as a star
had unusual longevity in the company

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tenure - the period of holding a position or office

Etymology

From Latin *longus* (long) and *aevum* (age).
