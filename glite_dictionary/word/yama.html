Yama

1. PROPN

/ ˈjɑːmə / (AmE), / ˈjɑːmə / (BrE)
In Hindu mythology, the god of death and ruler of the underworld.
Hindu god of death and the underworld

Examples

According to legend, Yama judges the souls of the deceased.
The story of Yama is central to many Hindu rituals concerning death.
In ancient texts, Yama is described as a just and fair ruler of the afterlife.

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Yama', meaning 'restrainer' or 'controller', name of the deity in Hindu tradition.
