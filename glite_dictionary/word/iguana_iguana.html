Iguana iguana

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈɡwɑːnə ɪˈɡwɑːnə / (AmE), / ɪˈɡwɑːnə ɪˈɡwɑːnə / (BrE)
a large, herbivorous, arboreal lizard native to Central and South America, with a spiny crest along the back; commonly known as the green iguana
large tropical arboreal lizard (green iguana)

Examples

The Iguana iguana is a popular species in the pet trade due to its striking appearance.
In some rural areas, people hunt Iguana iguana for meat, considering it a delicacy.
The rainforest canopy provides an ideal habitat for Iguana iguana to bask and forage for leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: green iguana - common name for Iguana iguana
  • Degree Lower: lizard - reptile with a long body and tail, four legs, and movable eyelids

Etymology

From Spanish 'iguana', from Arawak 'iwana'; scientific repetition denotes species name.
