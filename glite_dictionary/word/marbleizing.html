marbleizing

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrbəˌlaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːb(ə)laɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
A texture or effect that resembles the appearance of marble, often achieved by special painting or finishing techniques.
imitation marble texture or effect

Examples

The wallpaper's marbleizing adds a sophisticated touch to the living room.
The artist experimented with various marbleizing techniques on the surface of the vases.
We admired the faux finish, impressed by its convincing marbleizing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marbling - the process or effect of streaking with a marble-like appearance

Etymology

From 'marble' + '-ize' + '-ing', referring to the process of making something look like marble.
