Trombidiidae

1. NOUN

/ trɒmˌbɪdiˈaɪdi / (AmE), / trɒmˌbɪdiˈaɪdi / (BrE)
A family of large, bright red mites commonly known as red velvet mites or rain bugs, found in soil and decaying leaves.
family of red velvet mites

Examples

The Trombidiidae are often seen crawling on the ground after rains in tropical regions.
Scientists study Trombidiidae to better understand their role in controlling pest insect populations.
The distinctive bright red color makes Trombidiidae easily recognizable among other soil-dwelling creatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red velvet mite - Common name for mites of the Trombidiidae family

Etymology

From modern Latin Trombidiidae, the scientific name for this mite family, based on the genus Trombidium.
