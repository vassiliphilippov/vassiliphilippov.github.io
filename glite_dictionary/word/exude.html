to exude

1. VERB

/ ɪgˈzuːd / (AmE), / ɪgˈzjuːd / (BrE)
(of a person) to display a quality or feeling strongly and openly through mood, behavior, or appearance.
show (a quality or feeling) openly

Examples

She exudes great confidence even in challenging situations.
The team exuded enthusiasm throughout the project.
He exudes warmth and kindness whenever he speaks to his colleagues.

Synset Examples

She exudes great confidence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiate - to clearly emanate a quality or feeling

Etymology

From Latin exsudare/exudare (to sweat out), from ex- (out) + sudare (to sweat).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00946447-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪgˈzud / (AmE), / ɪgˈzjuːd / (BrE)
to release or discharge (a liquid or substance) slowly and steadily, often in drops or a small quantity
release a liquid or substance slowly

Examples

The walls of the cave exuded moisture even in the dry season.
After running in the sun, she began to exude sweat from every pore.
Certain trees exude sap when their bark is damaged.

Synset Examples

exude sweat through the pores

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ooze - to flow or leak out slowly; to exude
  • Synonym: to seep - to flow or leak slowly through porous material

Etymology

From Latin exudare (to sweat out), from ex- (out) + sudare (to sweat).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00067770-v
Synset POS: v
