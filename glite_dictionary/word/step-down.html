step-down

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛp daʊn / (AmE), / ˈstɛp daʊn / (BrE)
an act or instance of decreasing or reducing something, especially in amount, intensity, or rank
an act of decreasing or reducing

Examples

After the merger, there was a significant step-down in the number of employees.
The medication requires a gradual step-down in dosage to avoid side effects.
His retirement represented a notable step-down from the company's top position.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reduction - the act of making something smaller or less
  • Antonym: increase - an act of making something larger or greater
