Cotoneaster dammeri

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈtoʊniˌæstər ˈdæməri / (AmE), / kəˈtəʊniˌæstə ˈdæməri / (BrE)
a low-growing, evergreen shrub with small white flowers and bright red berries, often used as ground cover in gardens
a low evergreen shrub used as ground cover

Examples

Cotoneaster dammeri forms a dense mat that suppresses weed growth in the garden.
Landscapers often plant Cotoneaster dammeri for effective and attractive ground cover.
The bright red berries of Cotoneaster dammeri add winter color to many gardens.

Etymology

From the genus name Cotoneaster (from Latin cotoneum, 'quince', and aster, 'resembling') and the specific epithet dammeri, after Carl Ernst Dammer, a German botanist.
