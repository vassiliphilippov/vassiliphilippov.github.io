demeclocycline hydrochloride

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛmɪkloʊˈsaɪklin haɪˌdrɑːkləˈraɪd / (AmE), / ˌdiːmɪkləʊˈsaɪkliːn haɪˌdrɒkləˈraɪd / (BrE)
an antibiotic of the tetracycline class, used to treat various bacterial and rickettsial infections
an antibiotic of the tetracycline class
medical

Examples

The patient was prescribed demeclocycline hydrochloride to address a persistent bacterial infection.
Demeclocycline hydrochloride is less commonly used today due to the availability of newer antibiotics.
Doctors sometimes use demeclocycline hydrochloride off-label to treat certain cases of hyponatremia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tetracycline - a broad-spectrum antibiotic group

Etymology

From demeclocycline (from tetracycline with demethyl and chlorine substitution) and hydrochloride (salt form).
