degrading

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈɡreɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈɡreɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
causing a loss of dignity or respect; dishonoring or humiliating.
causing dishonor or humiliation

Examples

The boss's degrading remarks left her in tears.
It is a degrading experience to be treated with such disrespect.
Forced confessions are often obtained through degrading methods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humiliating - making someone feel ashamed or foolish
  • Antonym: uplifting - making someone feel better or more honorable

Etymology

From degrade + -ing; ultimately from Latin degradare (to reduce in rank).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈɡreɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈɡreɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
causing a loss of moral integrity, respect, or dignity; harmful to the mind or morals
harmful to the mind or morals

Examples

He found the job to be degrading and damaging to his self-esteem.
Many believe that such shows have a degrading influence on young people.
She refused to participate in any degrading activities that would compromise her morals.

Synset Examples

corrupt judges and their corrupting influence
the vicious and degrading cult of violence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrupting - causing someone or something to become immoral or dishonest

Etymology

From degrade + -ing; ultimately from Latin degradare (to reduce in rank).
