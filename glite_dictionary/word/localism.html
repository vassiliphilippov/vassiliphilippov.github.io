localism

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.kəˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.kə.lɪz.əm / (BrE)
a word, phrase, or pronunciation characteristic of a particular region or locality
regional phrase or pronunciation

Examples

The term 'bubbler' is a localism used in Wisconsin to mean a drinking fountain.
Her accent was marked by several localisms unfamiliar to outsiders.
Linguists often study localism to understand regional language development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regionalism - a linguistic feature or phrase characteristic of a particular region

Etymology

From local + -ism, modeled after late Latin and French formations.

2. NOUN

/ ˈloʊkəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəlɪzəm / (BrE)
preference for or loyalty to a particular locality, often resulting in emphasis on local customs, interests, or control
preference for a particular locality

Examples

The politician's localism appealed to voters who valued community traditions.
Intense localism can sometimes hinder cooperation between neighboring towns.
Journalists noted the rise of localism as citizens prioritized nearby issues over national debates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regionalism - strong identification with a specific geographic region
  • Antonym: cosmopolitanism - the idea of being free from local or national limitations; worldliness

Etymology

From 'local' + '-ism', formed after French 'localisme'.
