zumbooruck

1. NOUN

/ zʊmˈbuːrək / (AmE), / zʊmˈbʊərʌk / (BrE)
a camel mounted with a swiveling gun, used as mobile artillery in early modern Arab, Afghan, Persian, and Indian armies.
camel-mounted artillery used in Asian armies

Examples

The Mughal army effectively used zumboorucks during their campaigns in the Deccan region.
Colonial officers were surprised by the mobility of the zumbooruck units in battle.
A zumbooruck consisted of a camel carrying a small swivel gun and its operator.

Etymology

From Persian *zambūrak* (small wasp, light swivel gun).
