corticosteroid

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔrtɪkoʊˈstɛrɔɪd / (AmE), / ˌkɔːtɪkəʊˈstɛrɔɪd / (BrE)
a steroid hormone produced by the adrenal cortex or made synthetically, often used as a drug to reduce inflammation and suppress the immune response
steroid hormone used to reduce inflammation
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a corticosteroid to help reduce the patient’s severe inflammation.
Prolonged use of corticosteroids can lead to unwanted side effects, so they are usually given for a short period.
Athletes should be cautious about using corticosteroids without medical supervision.

Synset Examples

adrenal cortical steroids are used to treat many different conditions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glucocorticoid - a type of corticosteroid affecting glucose metabolism
  • Synonym: mineralocorticoid - a type of corticosteroid involved in regulating minerals and water balance

Etymology

From cortico-, referring to the cortex, and steroid, indicating its chemical structure.
