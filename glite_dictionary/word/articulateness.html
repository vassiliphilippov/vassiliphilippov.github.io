articulateness

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈtɪkjələtnəs / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪkjələtnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being able to express oneself clearly and effectively in speech or writing
ability to speak or write clearly

Examples

Her articulateness impressed everyone during the debate.
Effective leaders are often marked by their articulateness and confidence.
The job requires not only technical skills but also a high degree of articulateness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eloquence - fluent or persuasive speaking or writing
  • Antonym: inarticulateness - the quality of being unable to express oneself clearly

Etymology

From 'articulate' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality.
