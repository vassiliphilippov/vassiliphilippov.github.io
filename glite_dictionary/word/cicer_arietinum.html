Cicer arietinum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪsər æriˈɛtɪnəm / (AmE), / ˈkɪkər æriˈɛtɪnəm / (BrE)
the scientific name for the chickpea, an annual Asiatic herb cultivated for its short pods containing one or two edible seeds
scientific name for the chickpea plant

Examples

Cicer arietinum is widely cultivated in India, the Middle East, and Mediterranean regions.
Botanists classify the chickpea as Cicer arietinum.
The protein-rich seeds of Cicer arietinum are used in many traditional dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chickpea - an edible legume, also known as garbanzo bean

Etymology

From Latin, where 'cicer' means chickpea and 'arietinum' refers to a ram, alluding to the shape of the seed.
