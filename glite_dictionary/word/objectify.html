to objectify

1. VERB

/ əbˈdʒɛktɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / əbˈdʒɛktɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to treat someone as an object or a thing, without regard to their personhood or individuality; to make something impersonal.
treat as an object or make impersonal

Examples

Many critics argue that certain advertisements objectify women and reduce them to mere products.
The film was accused of trying to objectify its characters instead of portraying them as complex individuals.
Some people worry that technology may objectify our relationships by removing emotional connections.

Synset Examples

Will computers depersonalize human interactions?
Pornography objectifies women

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depersonalize - make impersonal or remove individuality
  • Antonym: to humanize - to make more human or emphasize human characteristics

Etymology

From Medieval Latin objectificare, from Latin objectum (object) + -ficare (to make).

2. VERB

/ əbˈdʒɛktɪˌfaɪ, ɑb- / (AmE), / əbˈdʒɛktɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make something external, objective, or concrete; to represent an idea or feeling as an object
make external or concrete

Examples

The artist attempted to objectify her emotions through sculpture.
Philosophers often try to objectify abstract concepts for clarity.
Language helps us objectify our internal experiences.

Synset Examples

language externalizes our thoughts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to externalize - to make external or visible; to express outwardly
  • Antonym: abstract - to consider something theoretically or separately from something else (opposite direction: from concrete to abstract)

Etymology

From Medieval Latin objectificare, from objectum (object) + -fy (make).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00533687-v
Synset POS: v
