region

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːdʒən / (AmE), / ˈriːdʒən / (BrE)
a particular area of activity, thought, or knowledge, especially one that is discussed or considered separately
area of activity, thought, or knowledge

Examples

In the region of mathematics, abstract concepts are explored in great depth.
This debate moves us into the region of moral philosophy rather than practical politics.
The book discusses the region of science fiction that deals with artificial intelligence.

Synset Examples

it was a limited realm of discourse
here we enter the region of opinion
the realm of the occult

Related Terms

  • Synonym: domain - an area of interest or activity; a field
  • Synonym: realm - a field or domain of activity or interest

Etymology

From Latin regio (direction, district), from regere (to rule).

2. NOUN

/ ˈriːdʒən / (AmE), / ˈriːdʒən / (BrE)
an approximate amount or number; used especially in the phrase 'in the region of' to mean 'about' or 'approximately'
an approximate amount or number

Examples

The total cost will be in the region of fifteen thousand dollars.
He earns something in the region of $60,000 a year.
Repairs could take in the region of six weeks to complete.

Synset Examples

it was going to take in the region of two or three months to finish the job
the price is in the neighborhood of $100

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neighborhood - an approximate amount or area, as in 'in the neighborhood of'

Etymology

From Latin regio (direction, district), but this sense developed idiomatically in English.
