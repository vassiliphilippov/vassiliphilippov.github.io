sabertooth

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪbərˌtuːθ / (AmE), / ˈseɪbəˌtuːθ / (BrE)
an extinct type of cat with long, sword-shaped upper canine teeth, found in both the Old and New Worlds from the Oligocene to the Pleistocene period
extinct cat with long upper canine teeth

Examples

The sabertooth was a formidable predator during the Ice Age.
Paleontologists discovered the fossilized remains of a sabertooth in South America.
Children are often fascinated by images of the massive fangs of the sabertooth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saber-toothed tiger - another term for sabertooth, most commonly referring to Smilodon

Etymology

From 'saber' (a type of sword) + 'tooth', describing the animal's characteristic teeth.
