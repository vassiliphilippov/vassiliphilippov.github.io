fleshbag

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɛʃˌbæɡ / (AmE), / ˈflɛʃˌbaɡ / (BrE)
A derogatory term for a living being perceived merely as a biological entity, especially used in science fiction to describe humans.
derogatory term for biological being (sci-fi)
offensive

Examples

The robot rebelled against its fleshbag creators, refusing to serve any longer.
Some aliens in the story viewed humans as inferior fleshbags lacking purpose.
Online, the AI insulted users by calling them mere fleshbags with limited intelligence.

Synset Examples

The sci-fi movie depicted aliens as fleshbags in disguise.
