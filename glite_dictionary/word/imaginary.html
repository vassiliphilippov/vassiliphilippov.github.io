imaginary

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈmædʒəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ɪˈmadʒɪˌnəri / (BrE)
existing only in the mind; not real or based on fact
not real; existing only in the mind

Examples

As a child, she had several imaginary friends who kept her company.
The treasure map turned out to lead to an imaginary island that doesn't exist.
Many myths are based on imaginary creatures invented by storytellers.

Synset Examples

the falsehood about some fanciful secret treaties
a small child's imaginary friends
to create a notional world for oneself

Related Terms

  • Antonym: real - actually existing as a fact; not imagined
  • Synonym: fictitious - not real or true; invented
  • Synonym: fantastic - based on imagination; not real

Etymology

From Latin imaginarius (of imagination), from imago (image).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈmædʒɪˌnɛri / (AmE), / ɪˈmadʒɪnəri / (BrE)
(mathematics) A complex number whose square is negative; specifically, any number of the form a+bi where a and b are real numbers and i is the square root of -1.
complex number with a nonzero imaginary part
medical

Examples

The equation x² + 1 = 0 has solutions that are imaginary numbers.
Engineers sometimes use imaginary numbers when working with alternating current circuits.
In this context, the term imaginary refers to numbers involving the square root of negative one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complex number - a number of the form a+bi, including both real and imaginary parts
  • Antonym: real number - a number representing a value along a continuous line, without imaginary component

Etymology

From the use of 'imaginary' by mathematician René Descartes (French imaginaire) to describe numbers involving the square root of -1.
