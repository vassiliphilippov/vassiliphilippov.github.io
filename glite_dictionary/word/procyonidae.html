Procyonidae

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈsaɪ.ə.nɪ.di / (AmE), / prəʊˈsaɪ.ə.nɪ.diː / (BrE)
A family of small to medium-sized mammals that includes raccoons, coatis, cacomistles, and kinkajous.
a family of mammals including raccoons and coatis

Examples

The Procyonidae family is known for its members' dexterous front paws and omnivorous diets.
Raccoons are perhaps the most familiar representatives of the Procyonidae.
Research on Procyonidae helps scientists understand the evolution of small carnivorous mammals.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Procyon (the raccoon) + -idae (a zoological family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ proʊˌsaɪ.əˈnɪdi / (AmE), / prəʊˌsaɪ.əˈnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small to medium-sized carnivorous mammals that includes raccoons, coatis, cacomistles, and kinkajous, native mainly to the Americas.
family of mammals: raccoons, coatis, etc.

Examples

The Procyonidae family includes raccoons, which are common across North America.
Scientists study the evolutionary history of Procyonidae to better understand their adaptive traits.
Kinkajous are members of the Procyonidae, even though they look very different from raccoons.

Etymology

From New Latin Procyonidae, based on the genus Procyon (raccoons) plus -idae (zoological family suffix).
