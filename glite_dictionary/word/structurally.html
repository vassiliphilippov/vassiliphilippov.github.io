structurally

1. ADV

/ ˈstrʌktʃərəli / (AmE), / ˈstrʌktʃərəli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to or affects the structure of something.
in relation to structure

Examples

Structurally, the bridge was sound despite its age.
The house was structurally modified to withstand earthquakes.
Structurally, the essay is well-organized and easy to follow.

Synset Examples

structurally, the organization is healthy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: structurally - with respect to structure; adverb

Etymology

From 'structural' + '-ly', structural from Latin 'structura' (a fitting together, building).
