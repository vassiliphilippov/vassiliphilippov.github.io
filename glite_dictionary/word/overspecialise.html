to overspecialise

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvɚˈspɛʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈspɛʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to become excessively specialized, often in a way that limits broader knowledge or adaptability
become too highly specialized

Examples

If you overspecialise, you may struggle to adapt to new roles in your career.
Researchers sometimes overspecialise and lose sight of the broader context.
She realised she had overspecialised when she couldn't discuss any topics outside her field.

Synset Examples

She overspecialized when she concentrated on verbs in Fijian

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: to specialise - to focus on a particular area of study or activity
  • Antonym: to generalise - to make less specific, to broaden focus or application

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'over-' (excessively) and 'specialise.'
