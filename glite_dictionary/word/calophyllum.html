Calophyllum

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæl.əˈfɪl.i.əm / (AmE), / ˌkæl.əˈfɪl.i.əm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical evergreen trees native to regions of Asia, Africa, Oceania, and the Americas, known for their glossy leaves and often used for timber and medicinal purposes.
genus of tropical evergreen trees

Examples

The leaves of the Calophyllum tree are often used in traditional medicine.
Local carpenters prefer Calophyllum wood for its durability and smooth finish.
Several species within the Calophyllum genus are important for coastal restoration projects.

Etymology

From Greek *kalos* (beautiful) + *phyllon* (leaf), referring to the attractive foliage.
