Claytonia caroliniana

1. NOUN

/ kleɪˈtoʊniə ˌkæroʊlɪˈnaɪənə / (AmE), / kleɪˈtəʊniə ˌkærəlɪˈnaɪənə / (BrE)
A species of spring-flowering plant native to eastern North America, similar to Claytonia virginica but usually with pink flowers.
a North American spring wildflower species

Examples

In the shady woods, hundreds of Claytonia caroliniana bloomed in delicate shades of pink and white.
She identified the wildflower as Claytonia caroliniana, noting its distinctive paired leaves.
Botanists study Claytonia caroliniana to understand its adaptation to eastern North American forests.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Claytonia virginica - a similar spring wildflower species with typically white flowers

Etymology

From the genus name Claytonia, named after botanist John Clayton, and caroliniana, meaning 'of Carolina'.
