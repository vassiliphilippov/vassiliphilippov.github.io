juggling

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌɡlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌɡlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of cleverly manipulating or rearranging things, especially to create a false or misleading impression
manipulating to mislead

Examples

The company's financial juggling made its profits appear much higher than they actually were.
Her schedule required constant juggling of appointments to keep up appearances.
He was accused of juggling the results to mislead investors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manipulation - controlling or handling something skillfully or unfairly
  • Synonym: deception - the act of deceiving someone by hiding the truth

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌɡlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌɡlɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of continuously throwing and catching several objects, such as balls or clubs, in the air at the same time, usually as a form of entertainment or skill display.
skill of keeping several objects in motion by tossing and catching

Examples

He mastered the art of juggling three balls at once within just a few weeks.
At the circus, the performer's juggling act amazed the entire audience.
Learning juggling requires patience and good hand-eye coordination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juggle - To throw and catch several objects at once as a performance or skill

Etymology

From the verb 'juggle', which comes from Old French *jongler*, from Latin *ioculari* (to jest).
