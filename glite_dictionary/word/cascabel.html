cascabel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæs.kəˌbɛl / (AmE), / ˈkæs.kəˌbɛl / (BrE)
a small bell attached to a sleigh or the harness of a horse pulling a sleigh, often producing a jingling sound
bell on sleigh or horse harness

Examples

The horses trotted through the snow, their harnesses adorned with shiny cascabels.
She treasured the old cascabel that once belonged to her grandfather's sleigh.
As the sled sped down the hill, the clear sound of a cascabel echoed in the crisp air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jingle bell - small bell, especially used on sleighs or harnesses, producing a jingling sound

Etymology

From Spanish 'cascabel' meaning 'little bell' or 'jingle bell'.
