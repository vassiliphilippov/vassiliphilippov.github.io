pollack

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːlæk / (AmE), / ˈpɒlæk / (BrE)
The lean white flesh of a North Atlantic fish, similar to cod, used as food.
flesh of North Atlantic fish (like cod)

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious dish using fresh pollack from the local market.
Many people substitute pollack for cod in traditional recipes because of its mild flavor.
Grilled pollack fillets are a healthy source of protein and omega-3 fatty acids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cod - a large marine fish with firm white flesh
  • Synonym: haddock - white-fleshed North Atlantic fish

Etymology

From the German dialect *Pollack* (a kind of fish), akin to Middle Low German *pollek*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊlæk / (AmE), / ˈpɒlæk / (BrE)
a large marine fish of northern seas, related to the cod and important as food and for sport fishing
a northern marine food fish, related to the cod

Examples

Fishermen caught several pollack near the rocky coast this morning.
The flavor of pollack makes it a popular choice for fish and chips in many regions.
Marine biologists study the migration patterns of pollack in the North Atlantic Ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cod - a large marine fish with a white, flaky flesh, widely eaten as food

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic 'pollag', diminutive of 'poll' meaning 'pool'.
