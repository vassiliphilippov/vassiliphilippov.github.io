jute

1. NOUN

/ dʒuːt / (AmE), / dʒuːt / (BrE)
a long, soft, shiny plant fiber used for making rope, sacks, and coarse cloth.
plant fiber used for rope or sacks

Examples

The sacks were made of jute and were strong enough to carry heavy loads.
Farmers in the region grow jute for the textile industry.
Jute is an environmentally friendly alternative to synthetic materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemp - plant fiber used for rope and textiles
  • Synonym: cotton - soft white plant fiber used for textiles

Etymology

From Bengali *jhuṭo*, meaning 'jute plant.'
