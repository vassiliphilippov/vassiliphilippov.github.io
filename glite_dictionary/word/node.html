node

1. NOUN

/ noʊd / (AmE), / nəʊd / (BrE)
a bulge or swelling in a body structure, especially as found in anatomy
bulge or swelling of a body part
medical

Examples

The doctor detected a small node on the patient's thyroid gland during the examination.
Swollen lymph nodes can indicate an underlying infection or immune response.
Surgeons removed a benign node that had developed on his vocal cord.

Etymology

From Latin nodus (knot, swelling).

Examples

In a standing wave, each node remains stationary while the antinodes oscillate.
A node is formed at each end of a fixed string due to complete destructive interference.

8. NOUN

/ noʊd / (AmE), / nəʊd / (BrE)
(Botany) A small swelling on a plant stem from which one or more leaves, branches, or flowers emerge.
part of a plant stem where leaves emerge

Examples

Each leaf grows from a node along the plant's stem.
The distance between each node is known as the internode.
Buds and branches can also develop at a node.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: internode - the part of a plant stem between two nodes

Etymology

From Latin nodus (knot, swelling).
