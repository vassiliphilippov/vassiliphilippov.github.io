improperium

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈproʊpəriəm / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɒpəriəm / (BrE)
(usually plural) An antiphon and response sung in Christian liturgy, especially on Good Friday, in which Christ reproaches his people; particularly known in Latin as 'Improperia'.
liturgical antiphon of reproach in Good Friday service

Examples

During the Good Friday liturgy, the choir sang the improperia with haunting reverence.
The improperia are an integral part of traditional Catholic Holy Week services.
Many worshippers find the improperia to be deeply moving expressions of Christ's remonstrance.

Synset Examples

In their present form in the Roman Rite, the Improperia are a series of three couplets, sung antiphonally by cantors and followed by alternate Greek and Latin responses from the two halves of the choir; and nine other lines sung by the cantors, with the full choir responding after each with the refrain ‘Popule meus, quid feci tibi? …’

Etymology

From Latin 'improperium' (reproach, insult), plural used in liturgical Latin for these chants.
