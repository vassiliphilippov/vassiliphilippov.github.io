troublesome

1. ADJ

/ ˈtrʌb.əl.səm / (AmE), / ˈtrʌb.əl.səm / (BrE)
causing difficulty, worry, or annoyance; hard to deal with or manage
causing trouble or difficulty

Examples

The troublesome child refused to follow any of the classroom rules.
She found the paperwork especially troublesome and frustrating to complete.
Their car has developed a troublesome noise that won't go away.

Synset Examples

a troublesome infection
a troublesome situation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annoying - causing irritation or mild anger
  • Synonym: problematic - presenting problems or difficulties

Etymology

From Middle English 'troublesom', from 'trouble' + '-some'.
