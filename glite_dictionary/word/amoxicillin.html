amoxicillin

1. NOUN

/ əˌmɑksəˈsɪlɪn / (AmE), / əˌmɒksɪˈsɪlɪn / (BrE)
A semisynthetic penicillin antibiotic used to treat a wide range of bacterial infections.
penicillin antibiotic for bacterial infections
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed amoxicillin to help clear up the infection.
After taking amoxicillin for a week, her symptoms significantly improved.
Some patients may experience side effects while using amoxicillin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penicillin - a group of antibiotics derived from Penicillium fungi

Etymology

From 'amino-' + 'ox-' (for oxygen group) + '-cillin' (from 'penicillin').
