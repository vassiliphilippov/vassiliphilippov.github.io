azymia

1. NOUN

/ əˈzɪmiə / (AmE), / əˈzɪmɪə / (BrE)
Absence or deficiency of an enzyme, especially one necessary for a biological process.
absence of an enzyme
medical

Examples

Liver failure may result in azymia, affecting various metabolic functions.
The genetic disorder is characterized by azymia of a crucial digestive enzyme.
Researchers are investigating treatments for azymia in rare inherited diseases.

Etymology

From Greek a- (without) + zymē (leaven, ferment).
