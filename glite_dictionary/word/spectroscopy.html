spectroscopy

1. NOUN

/ spɛkˈtrɑskəpi / (AmE), / spɛkˈtrɒskəpi / (BrE)
the scientific technique of using spectroscopes to study and analyze the spectra of substances
analysis using spectroscopes
medical

Examples

Modern chemists often use spectroscopy to determine the composition of unknown substances.
The laboratory specializes in spectroscopy and related analytical methods.
Recent advances in spectroscopy have greatly improved our ability to study molecular structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectroscope - an instrument used to observe spectra
  • Synonym: spectrometry - the measurement of spectra

Etymology

From New Latin 'spectroscopium' (spectroscope) + '-y' (denoting a field of study).
