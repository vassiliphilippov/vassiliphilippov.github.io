Israel-firster

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪzreɪəl ˌfɜrstər / (AmE), / ˈɪzriːəl ˌfɜːstə / (BrE)
a person who is seen as placing the interests of Israel above those of their own country
someone who prioritizes Israel's interests

Examples

Some commentators called him an Israel-firster after his remarks in Parliament.
The article criticized certain lobbyists as being unapologetic Israel-firsters.
She denied being an Israel-firster and said she supported a balanced foreign policy.

Synset Examples

The term Israel-firster is often used in political discourse.
He was labeled an Israel-firster for his political views.
Critics accused her of being an Israel-firster in her policies.

Etymology

Formed by combining 'Israel' and the suffix '-firster' (one who regards as first priority), modeled after constructions like 'America-firster.'
