Carya ovata

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛə.ri.ə oʊˈveɪ.tə / (AmE), / ˈkɑːrɪə əʊˈvɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of North American hickory tree with loose, grey, shaggy bark and edible nuts, commonly known as shagbark hickory.
a North American hickory tree (shagbark hickory)

Examples

The Carya ovata produces nuts that are a favorite among squirrels and humans alike.
Many old farmhouses in the Midwest are shaded by towering Carya ovata trees.
The distinctive bark of Carya ovata peels away in long strips, giving it its common name, shagbark hickory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shagbark hickory - North American hickory tree with shaggy bark and edible nuts

Etymology

From Latinized Greek, 'Carya' (nut tree) and Latin 'ovata' (egg-shaped), referring to the nut's shape.
