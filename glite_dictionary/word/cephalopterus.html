Cephalopterus

1. NOUN

/ sɛfəˈlɒptərəs / (AmE), / sɛfəˈlɒptərəs / (BrE)
A genus of large South American birds in the family Cotingidae, commonly known as umbrellabirds.
genus of South American umbrellabirds

Examples

The male Cephalopterus is known for its distinctive umbrella-like crest.
Three species belong to the genus Cephalopterus, all native to Central and South America.
Researchers are studying the unusual vocal sacs of Cephalopterus birds.

Etymology

From Greek 'kephalē' (head) + 'pteron' (wing).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛfəˈlɒptərəs / (AmE), / ˌsiːfəˈlɒptərəs / (BrE)
a genus of large South American birds in the family Cotingidae, known as umbrellabirds, characterized by their prominent crests and inflatable throat wattles
genus of South American umbrellabirds

Examples

The Cephalopterus genus includes three recognized species of umbrellabirds found in South America.
Researchers observed a rare courtship display in the Cephalopterus population of the Amazon.
The striking crest and wattle make the Cephalopterus easily identifiable among other birds of its habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: umbrellabird - large tropical American bird of the genus Cephalopterus

Etymology

From Greek kephalē (head) + pteron (wing), referring to the birds' prominent head and crest.
