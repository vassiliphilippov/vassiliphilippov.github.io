pogonophoran

1. NOUN

/ poʊˌɡɑːnəˈfɔrən / (AmE), / pəˌɡɒnəˈfɔːrən / (BrE)
a slender marine wormlike animal with tentacles and a tubelike outer covering, living on the deep ocean floor
deep-sea tube-dwelling marine animal
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species of pogonophoran near hydrothermal vents.
The pogonophoran builds protective tubes on the ocean floor.
Marine biologists study pogonophorans to learn about life in extreme environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beard worm - another name for pogonophoran, deep-sea tubeworm

Etymology

From Greek 'pōgōn' (beard) + 'phoros' (bearing), referring to their tentacle 'beards'.
