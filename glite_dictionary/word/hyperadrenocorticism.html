hyperadrenocorticism

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərˌædˌriːnoʊˈkɔrtɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpərˌædˌriːnəʊˈkɔːtɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
A medical condition caused by excessive production of cortisol by the adrenal glands.
excessive cortisol production disorder
medical

Examples

Dogs diagnosed with hyperadrenocorticism often exhibit increased thirst and urination.
Treatment for hyperadrenocorticism may include medications that reduce cortisol production.
Veterinarians are skilled at recognizing the clinical signs of hyperadrenocorticism in animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cushing's syndrome - A disorder caused by prolonged exposure to high levels of cortisol

Etymology

From Greek 'hyper-' (over, excessive) + 'adreno-' (referring to the adrenal glands) + 'cortic-' (relating to the cortex) + '-ism' (condition).
