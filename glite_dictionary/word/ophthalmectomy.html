ophthalmectomy

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑfˌθælˈmɛktəmi / (AmE), / ˌɒfˌθælˈmɛktəmi / (BrE)
The surgical removal of an eye.
removal of an eye (surgery)
medical

Examples

After the accident, the doctor recommended ophthalmectomy to prevent further infection.
The patient underwent ophthalmectomy and later received a prosthetic eye.
Advances in reconstructive surgery have improved outcomes for those who need ophthalmectomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enucleation - removal of the eyeball

Etymology

From Greek 'ophthalmos' (eye) + '-ectomy' (removal by surgery).
