limitation

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪmɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌlɪmɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the greatest amount, number, or level of something that is possible or allowed
maximum amount allowed

Examples

The speed limitation on this highway is strictly enforced by the police.
There is a limitation to the number of tickets one person can buy.
The company set a limitation on overtime hours for all employees.

Synset Examples

there are limits on the amount you can bet
it is growing rapidly with no limitation in sight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limit - a point or level beyond which something does not or may not extend or pass

Etymology

From Latin 'limitatio', from 'limitare' meaning to bound or restrict.

2. NOUN

/ ˌlɪməˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌlɪmɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the condition or quality of being limited or restricted; a constraint or shortcoming
the state of being limited or restricted

Examples

The new law imposes several limitations on what businesses are allowed to do.
Despite his talent, his physical limitation kept him from competing at the highest level.
There are some technical limitations that we need to consider before proceeding with the project.

Synset Examples

it is a good plan but it has serious limitations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restriction - a limiting condition or measure
  • Antonym: freedom - the state of not being restricted

Etymology

From Latin 'limitatio', from 'limitare' (to bound), from 'limes' (boundary, limit).

3. NOUN

/ ˌlɪmɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌlɪmɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of controlling, restricting, or placing limits on something, especially by official regulation or rule
the act of restricting or limiting

Examples

The new law imposed a limitation on the amount of water households could use each month.
There should be a clear limitation on how many hours employees can work overtime.
Without a limitation set by regulations, companies could pollute the river freely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restriction - an action that restricts or limits something
  • Antonym: expansion - the act of increasing or enlarging in scope or size

Etymology

From Latin 'limitatio' (a bounding, limiting), from 'limitare' (to bound, limit).

4. NOUN

(law) A legally defined period of time after which legal actions or claims cannot be brought forward in court.
legal time limit for bringing a claim
legal

Examples

You should check the limitation period before filing a lawsuit to ensure your claim is valid.
Due to the limitation in the contract, any disputes must be raised within two years.

Related Terms
