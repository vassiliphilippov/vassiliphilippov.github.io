Mutillidae

1. NOUN

/ mjuːˈtɪlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / mjuːˈtɪlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of wasps known for their dense hair and, in females, a lack of wings; commonly called velvet ants.
a family of wingless wasps (velvet ants)

Examples

Mutillidae are often called velvet ants due to the appearance of the females.
Many species in the Mutillidae family exhibit bright warning colors.
The sting of some Mutillidae can be extremely painful to humans.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Mutilla.

2. NOUN

/ mjuːˈtɪlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / mjuːˈtɪlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of wasps whose females are wingless and often resemble ants, commonly known as velvet ants
a family of wasps, the velvet ants

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species within the Mutillidae family during their expedition in the Amazon.
The females of Mutillidae are wingless and often mistaken for ants due to their appearance.
Many members of the Mutillidae family are known for their extremely painful stings.

Etymology

From New Latin *Mutillidae*, based on the type genus *Mutilla*.
