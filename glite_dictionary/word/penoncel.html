penoncel

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛn.ənˈsɛl / (AmE), / ˌpɛn.ənˈsɛl / (BrE)
A small pennant or flag, especially one carried on a knight's lance in medieval times.
small pennant on a lance
archaic

Examples

The knight's lance was adorned with a bright penoncel fluttering in the breeze.
During tournaments, each warrior displayed a unique penoncel as a mark of identity.
Historians often study the symbols and colors shown on a medieval penoncel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pennon - a long narrow flag or banner, especially carried on a lance
  • Degree Higher: banner - a large flag, especially used as a standard or symbol

Etymology

From Old French 'penoncel', diminutive of 'penon' (pennant, banner).
