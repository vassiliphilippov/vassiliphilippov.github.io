cautery

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːtəɹi / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtəri / (BrE)
A medical procedure that involves burning, freezing, or otherwise destroying tissue to stop bleeding or remove abnormal growths.
medical destruction of tissue by burning or freezing
medical

Examples

The surgeon used cautery to stop the bleeding during the operation.
Electrosurgical cautery is commonly employed in dermatology for minor procedures.
Certain wounds are still treated with cautery to prevent infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cauterization - The process or act of cauterizing tissue

Etymology

From Greek kautērion (branding iron), from kaiein (to burn).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːtəri / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtəri / (BrE)
an instrument or substance used to destroy tissue, typically by burning, freezing, or using chemicals, for medical purposes such as removing warts or stopping bleeding
instrument or substance for tissue destruction (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor used a cautery to stop the bleeding during surgery.
Liquid nitrogen can be applied as a cautery to freeze and remove skin growths.
Early forms of cautery involved heated metal instruments for sealing wounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cauterize - to burn tissue with a heated instrument or chemical for medical reasons

Etymology

From Greek kautērion (branding iron), from kaiein (to burn).
