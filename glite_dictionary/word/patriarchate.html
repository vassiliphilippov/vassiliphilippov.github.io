patriarchate

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪtriˌɑːrkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈpætrɪɑːkeɪt / (BrE)
The office, dignity, or jurisdiction of a patriarch, especially in certain Christian churches.
jurisdiction or office of a patriarch

Examples

The Ecumenical patriarchate of Constantinople holds a special status in Eastern Orthodoxy.
After the schism, a new patriarchate was established in Moscow.
The Coptic Orthodox patriarchate is one of the oldest in the Christian world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bishopric - jurisdiction or diocese of a bishop

Etymology

From Medieval Latin patriarchatus, from patriarcha (patriarch).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪ.tri.ɑːr.keɪt / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ.tri.ɑː.keɪt / (BrE)
A social system or organization in which the male is the head of the family and descent is traced through the male line.
male-headed social organization

Examples

The tribe was organized into a patriarchate, with the eldest man holding authority over the family.
Many ancient societies functioned under a patriarchate, passing property and titles from father to son.
Sociologists have studied the impact of the patriarchate on gender roles throughout history.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: matriarchate - a social system in which females are the family heads and descent is traced through the female line

Etymology

From Greek ‘patriarkhēs’ (male chief or head of a family).
