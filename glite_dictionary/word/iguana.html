iguana

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈɡwɑːnə / (AmE), / ɪˈɡwɑːnə / (BrE)
a large herbivorous lizard native to tropical America, characterized by a spiny crest along its back and often found in trees
large tropical American lizard with a spiny crest

Examples

The iguana basked in the sunlight on a high tree branch.
Some people in Central America consider iguana a delicacy and cook it in traditional dishes.
An iguana can grow up to six feet long, including its tail.

Etymology

From Spanish 'iguana', from Taíno 'iwana'.
