Iguana

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈɡwɑːnə / (AmE), / ɪˈɡwɑːnə / (BrE)
A genus of large, herbivorous lizards native to Central and South America and the Caribbean, known for their distinctive crest and dewlaps.
a genus of large herbivorous lizards

Examples

The Iguana is commonly found in tropical areas of Central and South America.
Researchers studied the diet of the Iguana in its natural habitat.
At the zoo, children were fascinated by the large green Iguana basking in the sun.

Etymology

From Spanish 'iguana', from Arawak 'iwana'.
