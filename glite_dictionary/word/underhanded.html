underhanded

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌndərˈhændɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈhændɪd / (BrE)
(of a throw or stroke) made with the hand moving forward and upward from below shoulder level.
thrown or struck from below shoulder level

Examples

The pitcher threw an underhanded ball during the demonstration.
She prefers an underhanded serve in tennis because it feels more natural.
His underhanded technique gave him more control in table tennis.

Synset Examples

an underhand pitch
an underhand stroke

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overhanded - (of a throw) made with the hand above shoulder level

Etymology

From 'underhand' (below the hand) + -ed.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌndɚˈhændɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈhændɪd / (BrE)
acting or done in a secret or dishonest way; marked by deception or trickery.
secretly dishonest or deceptive

Examples

She suspected his underhanded attempts to gain a promotion over her.
The company was accused of using underhanded tactics to eliminate competition.
Nobody trusted the manager after his underhanded dealings were revealed.

Synset Examples

achieved success in business only by underhand methods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devious - showing a skillful use of underhand tactics to achieve goals
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere

Etymology

From 'underhand' (secret, surreptitious), from under + hand.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02327024-s
Synset POS: s
