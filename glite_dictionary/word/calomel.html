calomel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæləmɛl / (AmE), / ˈkæləmɛl / (BrE)
a white, tasteless, colorless powder (mercurous chloride) formerly used as a cathartic and disinfectant
mercurous chloride used medicinally
medical

Examples

Doctors once prescribed calomel to treat a variety of ailments, despite its mercury content.
The pharmacist warned against the overuse of calomel due to possible toxic side effects.
Historians note that calomel was a common medicine in the eighteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mercurous chloride - chemical name for calomel
  • Synonym: cathartic - substance that induces vomiting or purging of the bowels

Etymology

From French 'calomel', of uncertain origin, possibly from Greek 'kalos' (beautiful) and 'melas' (black).
