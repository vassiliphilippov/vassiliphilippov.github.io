landlubber

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌlʌbɚ / (AmE), / ˈlændˌlʌbə / (BrE)
a person inexperienced at sea, especially a sailor on their first voyage
inexperienced sailor or person unfamiliar with the sea

Examples

As a landlubber, he struggled to keep his balance on the rolling deck.
The experienced crew laughed at the naive questions from the landlubber.
On her first voyage, she was every inch a landlubber, nervous about everything on board.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sailor - a person who works or travels on a ship

Etymology

Probably from 'land' + dialectal 'lubber' (clumsy person), with 'lubber' from Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌlʌbɚ / (AmE), / ˈlændˌlʌbə / (BrE)
a person who is unfamiliar with or inexperienced at sea; someone who lives and works on land rather than as a sailor
person inexperienced at sea

Examples

As a landlubber, he felt out of place on the rolling deck of the ship.
Despite being a seasoned hiker, she's a true landlubber and has never set foot on a boat.
The veteran captain joked that only a landlubber would get seasick in such calm waters.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sailor - a person whose job is working as a member of the crew of a ship or boat

Etymology

From "land" + archaic "lubber" (clumsy person, possibly of Scandinavian origin).
