alibi

1. NOUN

/ ˈæləˌbaɪ / (AmE), / ˈælɪbaɪ / (BrE)
an excuse or explanation offered to avoid blame or justify failing to do something
excuse or pretext to justify failings

Examples

Whenever he missed a meeting, he always had a new alibi to explain his absence.
Her alibi for not finishing the assignment was that she had computer issues.
The manager grew tired of hearing the same alibi every time a deadline was missed.

Synset Examples

he kept finding excuses to stay
every day he had a new alibi for not getting a job
his transparent self-justification was unacceptable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excuse - a reason or explanation given to justify a fault or offense
  • Synonym: justification - the act of showing something to be right or reasonable

Etymology

From Latin 'alibi' meaning 'elsewhere'; originally used in legal contexts, now also figurative.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæləˌbaɪ / (AmE), / ˈælɪbaɪ / (BrE)
(Law) evidence that a person was elsewhere when an alleged act, especially a crime, took place, used as a defense in a court of law.
legal defense of being elsewhere
legal

Examples

He claimed he had an alibi for the night of the robbery.
The defendant's alibi was corroborated by multiple witnesses.
Without a solid alibi, she became the main suspect in the investigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excuse - a reason or explanation put forward to defend or justify a fault or offense

Etymology

From Latin 'alibi' meaning 'elsewhere'.
