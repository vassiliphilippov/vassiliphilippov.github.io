lob

1. NOUN

/ lɑb / (AmE), / lɒb / (BrE)
an act of hitting, throwing, or propelling something, especially a ball, in a high arc through the air
high-arching throw or shot

Examples

She hit a perfect lob over her opponent's head to win the point.
The goalkeeper was caught off guard by a clever lob from midfield.
The artillery fired a lob of shells towards the enemy position.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throw - to send something through the air with force by a movement of the arm and hand

2. VERB

/ lɑb / (AmE), / lɒb / (BrE)
to throw or hit (a ball or other object) in a high, arcing motion
throw or hit in a high arc

Examples

The tennis player decided to lob the ball over her opponent’s head.
He tried to lob the grenade into the enemy trench.
She watched as he expertly lobbed the basketball toward the hoop.

3. NOUN

/ lɑb / (AmE), / lɒb / (BrE)
a ball hit or thrown in a high arc, especially in tennis or similar sports, often as a defensive return
high-arcing shot in sports, esp. tennis

Examples

She won the point with a clever lob over her opponent's head.
His lob landed just inside the baseline, catching the other player off guard.
The tennis coach demonstrated how to perfect a defensive lob during practice.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: drive - a powerful, low shot in tennis

Etymology

Possibly from dialectal English 'lob' meaning to throw slowly or heavily.
