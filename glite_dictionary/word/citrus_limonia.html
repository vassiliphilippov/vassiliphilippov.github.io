Citrus limonia

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtrəs lɪˈmoʊniə / (AmE), / ˈsɪtrəs lɪˈməʊniə / (BrE)
a hybrid citrus fruit, a cross between a mandarin orange and a lemon, known for its very acidic fruit with an orange-colored peel
hybrid citrus (mandarin and lemon)

Examples

The orchard specializes in growing Citrus limonia, prized for its distinctive flavor and tartness.
Chefs sometimes substitute Citrus limonia for lemons in recipes needing extra acidity.
Scientists are studying the genetic traits of Citrus limonia to improve citrus crop resilience.

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Citrus' and species name 'limonia', describing its lemon-like character.
