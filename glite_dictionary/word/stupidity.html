stupidity

1. NOUN

/ stuˈpɪdəti / (AmE), / stjuːˈpɪdəti / (BrE)
A stupid mistake or act; something done that shows a lack of good sense or judgment.
a stupid mistake or act

Examples

It was sheer stupidity to leave the keys in the car overnight.
His biggest stupidity was trusting someone he barely knew.
We all laughed about the stupidity of his last-minute decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunder - a careless or stupid mistake
  • Synonym: folly - a lack of good sense; foolishness
  • Antonym: wisdom - the quality of having experience, knowledge, and good judgment

Etymology

From Latin stupiditas, from stupidus (stupid).

2. NOUN

/ stuˈpɪdəti / (AmE), / stjuːˈpɪdəti / (BrE)
The quality of being unintelligent or lacking good judgment.
lack of intelligence or good judgment

Examples

His stupidity cost the company thousands of dollars.
I was shocked by the sheer stupidity of their plan.
It's not ignorance, it's willful stupidity that frustrates me the most.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foolishness - lack of good sense or judgment
  • Antonym: intelligence - the ability to acquire and apply knowledge and skills

Etymology

From Latin *stupiditas*, from *stupidus* (dull, foolish).

Synset POS: n
