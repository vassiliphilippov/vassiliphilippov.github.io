personally

1. ADV

/ ˈpɜrsənəli / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsənəli / (BrE)
In a personal manner; involving oneself directly or relating to one's own feelings or actions.
in a personal manner

Examples

Please do not take my remarks personally; I was talking in general terms.
She wanted to thank each supporter personally after the event.
He personally inspected every shipment to ensure quality.

Synset Examples

he took her comments personally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: impersonally - in a manner not involving personal feelings or direct involvement

Etymology

From personal + -ly, modeled after Latin 'personaliter'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ˈpɝːsənəli / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsənəli / (BrE)
In person; by oneself without the involvement of anyone else.
in person; without anyone else

Examples

She decided to deliver the package personally instead of asking her assistant.
I am grateful that you personally attended the meeting to address our concerns.
The CEO personally welcomed each new employee on their first day.

Synset Examples

I went there personally
he appeared in person

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in person - directly, by oneself rather than through someone else

Etymology

From person + -ally.

Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˈpɜrsənəli / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsənəli / (BrE)
used to indicate that the statement expresses one's own opinion or involvement rather than someone else's.
in one's own opinion or capacity

Examples

Personally, I think this is the best solution available.
I don't personally know the manager, but I've heard good things about him.
She said she would handle the issue personally to ensure it was resolved quickly.

Synset Examples

personally, I find him stupid

Etymology

From Latin 'personalis' (pertaining to a person), via Old French.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00133132-r
Synset POS: r

4. ADV

/ ˈpɝːsənəli / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsənəli / (BrE)
in a manner relating to someone as an individual rather than in an official or general capacity
in a personal capacity

Examples

The judge found the company liable, but she was not personally involved in the case.
I don't find the actor personally charming, although his movies are good.
Though the policy failed, the manager was not personally at fault.

Synset Examples

he is personally repulsive

Related Terms

  • Antonym: officially - in a formal or authorized manner

Etymology

From personal + -ly; 'personal' from Old French 'personel' and Latin 'personalis'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r

5. ADV

/ ˈpɜrsənəli / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsənəli / (BrE)
used to show that you are giving your own opinion or experience rather than someone else's or a general view.
from one's own point of view

Examples

Personally, I think the new policy will benefit everyone involved.
If you ask me personally, I would choose the second option.
Personally, I've never been a fan of horror movies.

Synset Examples

speaking personally, I would not want to go

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subjectively - in a way that is based on personal feelings or opinions
  • Antonym: objectively - in a way that is not influenced by personal feelings

Etymology

From Latin personalis (personal) + English -ly.
