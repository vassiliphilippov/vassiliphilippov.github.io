Tiliomycetes

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɪlioʊmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (AmE), / ˌtɪlɪəʊmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (BrE)
A group of fungi, in some classification systems, including the two orders Ustilaginales (smuts) and Uredinales (rusts).
group of fungi (smuts and rusts)

Examples

Scientists classified the fungal samples as Tiliomycetes based on their distinctive reproductive traits.
The group Tiliomycetes includes many plant pathogens, such as smuts and rusts.
Although the system is outdated, some older texts still use the term Tiliomycetes in fungal taxonomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ustilaginales - order of smut fungi
  • Synonym: Uredinales - order of rust fungi

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Til-' (a reference to linden trees or derived from Latin genus names) and Ancient Greek 'mykēs' (fungus).

2. NOUN

/ tɪli.oʊmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (AmE), / tɪlɪ.əʊmɪˈsiːtiːz / (BrE)
a category in some classification systems of fungi, comprising the orders Ustilaginales (smuts) and Uredinales (rusts)
fungal class including smuts and rusts

Examples

The class Tiliomycetes encompasses fungal orders that cause significant crop diseases.
Taxonomists once grouped rusts and smuts together under the class Tiliomycetes.
Some older classifications still refer to Tiliomycetes when discussing the Uredinales and Ustilaginales.

Etymology

From New Latin: Greek 'tilio-' (possibly from 'tilos', meaning callus or swelling) + '-mycetes' from Greek 'mykēs' (fungus).
