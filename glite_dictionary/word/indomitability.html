indomitability

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌdɑːmətəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪnˌdɒmɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being impossible to subdue or defeat; unconquerable spirit or persistence.
quality of being impossible to defeat

Examples

Despite overwhelming odds, her indomitability inspired everyone around her.
The team's indomitability was evident as they refused to give up.
His indomitability in the face of adversity made him a legend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invincibility - the quality of being too powerful to be defeated or overcome
  • Antonym: defeat - the act of winning a victory over someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'indomitabillis' (untameable), from 'in-' (not) + 'domitare' (to tame).
