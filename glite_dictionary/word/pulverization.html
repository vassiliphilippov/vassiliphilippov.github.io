pulverization

1. NOUN

/ ˌpʌlvərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpʌlvəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of crushing or grinding something into a fine powder or the state of being reduced to tiny loose particles
the process or result of grinding to powder

Examples

The pulverization of the rock created a fine dust that covered the laboratory surfaces.
Through pulverization, the spices were reduced to a consistent texture for baking.
Modern medicine often involves the pulverization of herbs for use in tablets or capsules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powder - dry substance composed of fine particles

Etymology

From Latin 'pulverizare' (to reduce to dust), from 'pulvis' (dust).

3. NOUN

/ ˌpʌlvɚɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpʌlvəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of crushing or grinding something into a fine powder or dust.
the act of pulverizing; crushing into powder

Examples

The pulverization of the rocks was necessary before any chemical analysis could be performed.
Constant exposure to heavy traffic led to the pulverization of the old concrete road.
Modern mining equipment allows for rapid pulverization of ore on a large scale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grinding - the act of reducing to small particles or powder by crushing or abrasion
  • Synonym: crushing - the act of compressing so as to break or injure

Etymology

From Latin 'pulvis' (dust) via French and Late Latin forms.
