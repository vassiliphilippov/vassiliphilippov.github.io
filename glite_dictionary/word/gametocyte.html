gametocyte

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈmiːtəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ɡəˈmiːtəˌsaɪt / (BrE)
An immature cell in animals or plants that develops into a gamete through the process of meiosis.
precursor cell developing into a gamete
medical

Examples

The malaria parasite passes through a gametocyte stage in its lifecycle.
Researchers observed the transformation of the gametocyte under a microscope.
In plants, the gametocyte undergoes meiosis to produce gametes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gamete - a mature reproductive cell (sperm or egg) capable of fusing with another
  • Degree Higher: zygote - a cell formed by the fusion of two gametes

Etymology

From Greek 'gamete' (wife, or gametēs 'husband') + '-cyte' (cell).
