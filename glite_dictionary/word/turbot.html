turbot

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝː.bət / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.bət / (BrE)
The flesh of a large European flatfish, valued as a delicacy and used as food.
flesh of a large European flatfish (food)

Examples

The chef prepared the turbot with a delicate lemon butter sauce.
Fresh turbot is often featured on the menus of gourmet seafood restaurants.
He enjoyed a generous portion of grilled turbot at the seaside bistro.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halibut - the flesh of a large flatfish, also used as food

Etymology

From Old French *tourbot*, of Germanic origin.
