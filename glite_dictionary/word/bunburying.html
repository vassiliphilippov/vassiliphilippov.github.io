Bunburying

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌn.bɚ.i.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʌn.bəri.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of inventing a fictitious person or excuse to avoid social obligations or responsibilities, especially in a playful or deceptive way
inventing excuses or personas to avoid obligations

Examples

He was always Bunburying to avoid dull family gatherings.
The art of Bunburying was his solution to a hectic social life.
She confessed her Bunburying after being caught in another elaborate excuse.

Synset Examples

Her Bunburying habits were starting to annoy her friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fabrication - the act of inventing something, often with intent to deceive
  • Synonym: deception - the act of deceiving or tricking someone

Etymology

Coined by Oscar Wilde in 'The Importance of Being Earnest' (1895), after the fictitious invalid 'Bunbury.'
