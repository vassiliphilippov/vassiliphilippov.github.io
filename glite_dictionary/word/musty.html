musty

1. ADJ

/ ˈmʌsti / (AmE), / ˈmʌsti / (BrE)
having a stale, moldy, or damp smell; smelling of mildew or mold
having a stale, moldy smell

Examples

The old basement had a musty odor that was impossible to ignore.
She threw away the musty loaf of bread after smelling it in the pantry.
After months without ventilation, the cabin became unpleasantly musty.

Synset Examples

moldy bread
a moldy (or musty) odor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moldy - covered with or smelling of mold
  • Antonym: fresh - recent, not stale or spoiled

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *musty*, influenced by 'must' (new wine), from Latin *mustum* (new wine).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmʌsti / (AmE), / ˈmʌsti / (BrE)
having a stale, damp, or unpleasant smell, often due to age or lack of fresh air
stale or unpleasant-smelling (often damp)

Examples

The old books in the attic smelled musty after years of neglect.
We opened the cellar door and a musty odor greeted us immediately.
Her clothes became musty from being left damp in the suitcase.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moldy - covered with or smelling of mold
  • Antonym: fresh - recently made or obtained; clean or unspoiled

Etymology

From Old French *mouist* (damp or moldy), from Latin *muscidus* (musty, moldy).

Synset POS: s
