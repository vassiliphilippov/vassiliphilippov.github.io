astringent

1. ADJ

/ əˈstrɪndʒənt / (AmE), / əˈstrɪndʒənt / (BrE)
Having a sharp, slightly sour or bitter taste that causes a puckering sensation in the mouth.
tasting sharp, sour, or bitter

Examples

The wine has an astringent flavor that lingers on the palate.
Some unripe fruits are too astringent to be eaten raw.
He grimaced at the astringent taste of the grapefruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bitter - having a sharp, pungent taste
  • Antonym: sweet - having a taste similar to that of sugar or honey

Etymology

From Latin 'astringens', present participle of 'astringere' (to bind fast).

2. NOUN

/ əˈstrɪndʒənt / (AmE), / əˈstrɪndʒənt / (BrE)
a substance or drug that causes the contraction of body tissues and canals, often used to reduce bleeding from minor abrasions.
a substance that contracts body tissues
medical

Examples

The doctor applied an astringent to stop the bleeding from the small cut.
Witch hazel is commonly used as an astringent in skincare products.
The pharmacist recommended an astringent for minor skin irritations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: styptic - a substance able to stop bleeding

Etymology

From Latin 'astringens', present participle of 'astringere' (to bind fast).

3. ADJ

/ əˈstrɪndʒənt / (AmE), / əˈstrɪndʒənt / (BrE)
having the property of drawing together or constricting soft tissue, often used to describe substances that tighten skin or stop bleeding
having a constricting or tightening effect

Examples

This astringent lotion helps tighten the pores of your skin.
The doctor applied an astringent to stop the minor bleeding.
Witch hazel is known for its astringent properties in treating oily skin.
