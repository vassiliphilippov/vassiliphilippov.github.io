to misinterpret

1. VERB

/ ˌmɪsɪnˈtɝːprət / (AmE), / ˌmɪsɪnˈtɜːprɪt / (BrE)
to understand or explain something wrongly; to interpret incorrectly.
understand or explain wrongly

Examples

He tended to misinterpret her silence as disapproval.
The data were misinterpreted, leading to incorrect conclusions.
It's easy to misinterpret a text message without the tone of voice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to misunderstand - fail to understand correctly; get the wrong idea

Etymology

From prefix 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'interpret', from Latin 'interpretari' (explain, translate).

2. VERB

/ ˌmɪs.ɪnˈtɜr.prɪt / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.ɪnˈtɜː.prɪt / (BrE)
to understand or explain something incorrectly; to interpret in a way that is not intended or accurate
understand incorrectly

Examples

It's easy to misinterpret a text message without hearing the person's tone of voice.
Some readers misinterpreted his silence as a sign of agreement.
The instructions were misinterpreted by several students, leading to mistakes.

Synset Examples

Don't misinterpret my comments as criticism
She misconstrued my remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to misconstrue - to interpret something wrongly
  • Antonym: to interpret - to explain or understand the meaning of something

Etymology

From Latin 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'interpretari' (to explain, interpret).

3. VERB

/ ˌmɪsɪnˈtɜrprət / (AmE), / ˌmɪsɪnˈtɜːprɪt / (BrE)
to understand or explain something wrongly; to interpret incorrectly
understand wrongly; interpret incorrectly

Examples

Be careful not to misinterpret his comments as criticism.
She misinterpreted the instructions and made a serious mistake.
It's easy to misinterpret body language in a new culture.

Synset Examples

I misread Hamlet all my life!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to misread - to read incorrectly; to misunderstand written or spoken words
  • Antonym: to interpret - to explain or understand the meaning of something

Etymology

From Latin 'interpreti' (to interpret), with prefix 'mis-' meaning 'wrongly'.
