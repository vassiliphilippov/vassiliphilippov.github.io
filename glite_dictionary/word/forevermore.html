forevermore

1. ADV

/ fəˌrɛvərˈmɔr / (AmE), / fəˌrɛvəˈmɔː / (BrE)
For all time in the future; always from this time onward.
for all time in the future

Examples

They vowed to love each other forevermore, no matter what happened.
This song will be remembered forevermore as a classic by fans around the world.
She left her childhood home, knowing her life would change forevermore.

Synset Examples

lead a blameless life evermore

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forever - for all time; always
  • Antonym: never - at no time in the past or future; not ever

Etymology

From 'forever' + archaic intensifying adverb 'more.'
