Confucianism

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfjuːʃiəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːʃɪəˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
a system of philosophical and ethical teachings founded by Confucius that emphasizes moral integrity, social harmony, respect for elders, and devotion to family
Chinese philosophical and ethical system

Examples

Many aspects of traditional Chinese society are influenced by Confucianism and its emphasis on respect for elders.
She studied Confucianism in college as part of her philosophy major.
The teachings of Confucianism continue to shape values across East Asia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Taoism - a Chinese philosophy based on the writings of Laozi emphasizing living in harmony with the Tao

Etymology

From Confucius (Latinized form of Kong Fuzi, Chinese philosopher) plus -ism.
