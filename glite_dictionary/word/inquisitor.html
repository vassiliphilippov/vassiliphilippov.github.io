inquisitor

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkwɪzət̬ər / (AmE), / ɪnˈkwɪzɪtə / (BrE)
an official of the Roman Catholic Church appointed to investigate and punish heresy, especially during the Inquisition
official investigating heresy (historical)

Examples

During the Spanish Inquisition, the inquisitor had the power to interrogate suspected heretics.
The inquisitor demanded a confession from those accused during the trial.
She studied the stern portrait of the medieval inquisitor in her history book.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inquisition - the historical institution of the Roman Catholic Church for investigating heresy

Etymology

From Latin inquisitor (examiner, searcher), from inquirere (to seek, ask after).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkwɪzɪtər / (AmE), / ɪnˈkwɪzɪtə / (BrE)
a person who asks questions in a harsh or severe manner, often seeking to uncover information or faults
harsh or severe questioner

Examples

The prosecutor acted like an inquisitor, grilling the witness with relentless questions.
She dreaded facing her boss, whose inquisitor style made every meeting tense.
During the interview, the journalist assumed the role of an inquisitor, probing into every inconsistency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interrogator - someone who questions, especially formally or in detail

Etymology

From Latin 'inquisitor' (examiner, investigator), from 'inquirere' (to inquire).
