Picea abies

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪ.si.ə ˈeɪ.biːz / (AmE), / ˈpɪ.si.ə ˈeɪ.biːz / (BrE)
A tall, pyramidal evergreen tree native to northern Europe, also known as the Norway spruce, with dark green foliage, spreading branches, and long, pendulous cones.
a European coniferous tree, Norway spruce

Examples

The majestic Picea abies towers over many European forests, providing habitat for numerous bird species.
Botanists collected samples from a centuries-old Picea abies to study its growth rings.
Many Christmas trees sold in Europe are actually Picea abies due to their symmetrical shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Norway spruce - common English name for Picea abies

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Picea' meaning 'spruce' and 'abies' meaning 'fir'.
