trombone

1. NOUN

/ trɑmˈboʊn / (AmE), / trɒmˈbəʊn / (BrE)
a brass musical instrument with a long, sliding tube that changes the pitch
brass musical instrument with sliding tube

Examples

The trombone adds a deep, rich sound to the orchestra.
She practiced the trombone every evening in preparation for her concert.
A jazz band typically features a trombone alongside other brass instruments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trumpet - a brass instrument with a bright, penetrating tone
  • Synonym: tuba - a large brass wind instrument with a deep sound

Etymology

From Italian 'trombone', augmentative of 'tromba' (trumpet).
