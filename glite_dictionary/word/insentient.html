insentient

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsɛnʃənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛnʃɪənt / (BrE)
lacking feeling, awareness, or consciousness; not capable of sensation or perception.
lacking feeling or consciousness

Examples

Some philosophers argue that an insentient object cannot experience pain or pleasure.
The robot remained insentient, responding only to programmed instructions.
He described the landscape as hauntingly beautiful yet strangely insentient.

Synset Examples

insentient (or insensate) stone

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sentient - able to perceive or feel things
  • Synonym: insensate - lacking physical sensation or compassion

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'in-' (not) + 'sentient' (feeling).
