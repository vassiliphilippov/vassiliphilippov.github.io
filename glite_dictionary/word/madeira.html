Madeira

1. PROPN

/ məˈdɛɹə / (AmE), / məˈdɪərə / (BrE)
an island in the North Atlantic Ocean, off the northwest coast of Africa, and the largest of the Madeira Islands, known for its mild climate and scenic landscapes
island in the North Atlantic, part of Portugal

Examples

Many tourists visit Madeira each year to enjoy its beautiful scenery and mild climate.
Flights to Madeira are available from several major European cities.
She brought back a bottle of local wine from her trip to Madeira.

Etymology

From Portuguese, literally 'wood' or 'timber,' named for its forests.

2. NOUN

/ məˈdɪrə / (AmE), / məˈdɪərə / (BrE)
A rich, amber-colored fortified wine from the Madeira Islands, often served as a dessert wine.
fortified wine from Madeira Islands

Examples

After dinner, they enjoyed a glass of Madeira with dessert.
The rich flavor of Madeira pairs well with sharp cheeses and fruit.
She brought a bottle of Madeira back from her vacation in Portugal.

Etymology

From Portuguese Madeira (the island where the wine originates), meaning 'wood'.

3. PROPN

/ məˈdɛr.ə / (AmE), / məˈdɪə.rə / (BrE)
a river in western Brazil, approximately 3,380 km (2,100 miles) long, which is a major tributary of the Amazon River.
major river in Brazil; Amazon tributary

Examples

The Madeira River is crucial for transportation and trade in western Brazil.
The Amazon receives a significant volume of water from the Madeira and its tributaries.
Scientists studied the biodiversity along the banks of the Madeira.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'Madeira' ('wood,' originally referring to the island and later applied to the river).
