triacetate

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈæsəˌteɪt / (AmE), / traɪˈasɪteɪt / (BrE)
a cellulose acetate compound containing three acetyl groups, used in fibers and film because it is relatively slow to burn
a type of cellulose acetate used in fibers and film

Examples

The new film stock is made from triacetate, making it much less flammable than older materials.
Many synthetic fabrics include triacetate for added durability and wrinkle resistance.
Because triacetate burns slowly, it replaced celluloid in movie film production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cellulose acetate - a derivative of cellulose used for film and fibers

Etymology

From tri- (three) and acetate, indicating three acetyl groups in the compound.
