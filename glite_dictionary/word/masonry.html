masonry

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪsənɹi / (AmE), / ˈmeɪsənɹi / (BrE)
The skill or work of building structures from stone, brick, or similar materials.
the craft of building with stone or brick

Examples

He has studied masonry for years and now restores historic buildings.
The castle walls were built with skilled masonry that has lasted for centuries.
Learning masonry requires patience and a strong attention to detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bricklaying - the process of laying bricks to construct walls or structures

Etymology

From Old French 'maçonnerie', based on 'maçon' (mason).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪsənri / (AmE), / ˈmeɪsənri / (BrE)
A structure or construction made of stone, brick, or similar materials, typically built by a mason.
construction of stone or brick

Examples

The ancient temple's masonry has withstood earthquakes for centuries.
Skilled workers repaired the crumbling masonry on the cathedral walls.
The house features beautiful red brick masonry around its entrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brickwork - construction made primarily of bricks
  • Synonym: stonework - construction made primarily of stone

Etymology

From Old French 'maçonnerie', from 'maçon' (mason).
