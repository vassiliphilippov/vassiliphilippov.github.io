thumbhole

1. NOUN

/ ˈθʌmˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈθʌmˌhəʊl / (BrE)
a hole on a woodwind instrument that is opened or closed with the thumb to change the pitch
hole on woodwind closed by the thumb

Examples

The clarinet has a thumbhole on the back that is crucial for playing higher notes.
She covered the thumbhole while fingering the flute to achieve the correct tone.
Remember to partially open the thumbhole to play the harmonic on the recorder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keyhole - hole in a musical instrument that is covered or uncovered to change pitch

2. NOUN

/ ˈθʌmˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈθʌmhəʊl / (BrE)
a hole designed to fit the thumb, especially in objects like bowling balls, tools, or musical instruments
hole for the thumb in an object

Examples

Please make sure your thumb is fully inserted into the thumbhole before you throw the bowling ball.
The jacket features thumbholes in the sleeves to keep your hands warm during winter runs.
The musician covered the thumbhole to play a different note on his recorder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finger hole - a hole for inserting a finger
