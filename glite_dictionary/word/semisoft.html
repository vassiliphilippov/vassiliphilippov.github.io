semisoft

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛmiˌsɑft / (AmE), / ˈsɛmɪˌsɒft / (BrE)
partially but not completely soft; somewhat soft.
partially soft; somewhat soft

Examples

I prefer semisoft cheeses like Havarti on my sandwiches.
The mattress is semisoft, offering a comfortable balance between support and cushioning.
This clay remains semisoft for several hours, making it easy to work with.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: soft - easily shaped or compressed; not hard or firm
  • Antonym: hard - solid, firm, and resistant to pressure; not soft

Etymology

Formed from 'semi-' (Latin for 'half, partly') and 'soft'.
