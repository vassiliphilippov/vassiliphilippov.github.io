Nadine Gordimer

1. PROPN

/ nəˈdin ɡɔrˌdɪmər / (AmE), / nəˈdiːn ˈɡɔːdɪmə / (BrE)
(1923–2014) South African novelist and short-story writer acclaimed for her works exploring the effects of apartheid.
South African novelist and anti-apartheid writer

Examples

Nadine Gordimer received the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1991 for her insightful portrayal of South African society.
Many schools include works by Nadine Gordimer in their curricula to discuss apartheid's impact.
Nadine Gordimer's novels challenge readers to confront issues of social justice and racial inequality.
