Penicillium

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛnɪˈsɪliəm / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˈsɪliəm / (BrE)
A genus of fungi that commonly appear as green or blue molds on decaying food, some species used in cheese-making and as a source of penicillin.
genus of fungi (mold); source of penicillin
medical

Examples

The discovery of Penicillium led to the development of the first antibiotic drug.
Blue cheeses owe their flavor to colonies of Penicillium species.
Bread that is left out for too long is often covered in blue-green Penicillium mold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mold - a fungus that grows in the form of multicellular filaments

Etymology

From Latin 'penicillus' (paintbrush), referring to the shape of the spore-bearing structures.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɛnɪˈsɪliəm / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˈsɪliəm / (BrE)
A genus of fungi that commonly grow as blue or green molds on decaying food, used in cheese-making and as a source of penicillin.
genus of mold fungi, source of penicillin

Examples

The discovery of Penicillium led to the development of the first antibiotic, penicillin.
Many cheeses owe their unique flavors to strains of Penicillium used in fermentation.
Bread left exposed to air often develops a blue-green mold known as Penicillium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aspergillus - genus of fungi, some species of which are similar to Penicillium
  • Synonym: mold - fungus that grows in the form of multicellular filaments

Etymology

From Latin 'penicillus' (paintbrush), referring to the brush-like structure of the fungus.
