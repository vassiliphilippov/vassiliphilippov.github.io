driftage

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪftɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdrɪftɪdʒ / (BrE)
the deviation of a vessel or aircraft from its intended course as a result of drifting
deviation from course due to drifting

Examples

The captain corrected for driftage caused by strong crosswinds.
A pilot must always account for driftage when plotting a flight path.
Unanticipated driftage delayed the ship's arrival at port.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drift - to be carried slowly by a current of air or water

Etymology

Formed from 'drift' + '-age', a suffix indicating action or result.
