Calvinism

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.vəˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈkæl.vɪn.ɪz.əm / (BrE)
The Protestant theological system of John Calvin and his followers, emphasizing the absolute power of God and salvation by divine grace alone.
Protestant theological system of John Calvin

Examples

Many early American settlers practiced Calvinism as their religious faith.
The debate between Arminianism and Calvinism continues among theologians today.
He wrote an essay on the influence of Calvinism in European history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Presbyterianism - branch of Protestant Christianity influenced by Calvinism

Etymology

From John Calvin (1509–1564), French theologian and reformer, plus -ism.
