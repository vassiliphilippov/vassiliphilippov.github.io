harshly

1. ADV

/ ˈhɑrʃli / (AmE), / ˈhɑːʃli / (BrE)
In a severe, rough, or unkind manner.
in a severe or unkind way

Examples

The teacher spoke harshly to the students who were talking in class.
She criticized him harshly for his careless mistake.
The wind blew harshly against the windows all night.

Synset Examples

‘That's enough!’ he cut in harshly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gently - in a mild or kind manner
  • Synonym: severely - in a very strict or harsh manner

Etymology

From 'harsh' + '-ly'; 'harsh' is ultimately of uncertain origin.

2. ADV

/ ˈhɑrʃ.li / (AmE), / ˈhɑːʃ.li / (BrE)
In a severe, unpleasant, or unkind manner.
in a severe or unkind way

Examples

The teacher spoke harshly to the student after he broke the rules.
Critics judged the film harshly, pointing out every minor flaw.
The judge punished the offender harshly in an effort to deter others.

Synset Examples

her voice fell gratingly on our ears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: severely - in a very strict or harsh manner
  • Antonym: gently - in a mild or soft manner

Etymology

From 'harsh' + '-ly', with 'harsh' from Middle English 'harsh' (rough, coarse).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00352317-r
Synset POS: r
