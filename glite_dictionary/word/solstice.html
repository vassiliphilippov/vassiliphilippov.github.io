solstice

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːl.stɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.stɪs / (BrE)
Either of the two times of the year when the sun is at its greatest distance north or south of the celestial equator, resulting in the longest day (summer solstice) or the longest night (winter solstice).
either of two annual points of the sun's extreme positions

Examples

Many cultures hold festivals around the solstice to celebrate the changing seasons.
The winter solstice marks the shortest day and longest night of the year.
Scientists use precise measurements to determine the exact moment of each solstice.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: equinox - one of the two times in a year when day and night are of equal length
  • Degree Higher: summer solstice - the solstice marking the onset of summer, at the longest day
  • Degree Lower: winter solstice - the solstice marking the onset of winter, at the shortest day

Etymology

From Latin solstitium, from sol (sun) + stitium (standing still).
