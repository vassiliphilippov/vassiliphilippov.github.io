colossus

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɑːsəs / (AmE), / kəˈlɒsəs / (BrE)
a person who is remarkably large and strong, or who is exceptionally important or influential
a person who is very large or influential

Examples

The legendary wrestler was a true colossus in the ring, intimidating all his opponents.
She faced corporate challenges like a colossus, unfazed by even the largest obstacles.
Everyone admired the colossus for both his towering height and his extraordinary strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant - a person who is much larger than usual
  • Antonym: dwarf - a person who is much smaller than usual

Etymology

From Latin 'colossus', from Ancient Greek 'kolossos' (giant statue).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈlɑːsəs / (AmE), / kəˈlɒsəs / (BrE)
A person who is extremely important, influential, or distinguished in a particular field.
person of great importance or influence

Examples

He was regarded as a colossus in the world of literature, admired by critics and readers alike.
After decades of leadership, she became a colossus of the tech industry.
The scientist was a colossus, his influence shaping generations of research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant - a person or thing of unusually great size, power, or importance

Etymology

From Latin 'colossus', from Ancient Greek 'kolossos' (giant statue).
