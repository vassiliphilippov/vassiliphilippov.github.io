endaemonism

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈdiməˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ɛnˈdiːmənɪzəm / (BrE)
An ethical doctrine that evaluates actions according to their ability to promote personal well-being through a life guided by reason.
ethical doctrine of personal well-being via reason

Examples

The philosopher outlined the principles of endaemonism in her lecture on ethical theories.
Many critics argue that endaemonism fails to account for the importance of altruistic actions.
He adopted endaemonism as the foundation for his personal code of ethics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eudaemonism - Ethical system focused on personal happiness as the highest good

Etymology

From Greek *eudaímonia* (happiness, welfare), related to *eu* (good) and *daimōn* (spirit).
