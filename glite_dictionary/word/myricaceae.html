Myricaceae

1. NOUN

/ maɪˌrɪkəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / mɪˌrɪkəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants (the bayberry family) comprising shrubs and small trees, including the genus Myrica, commonly found in temperate and subtropical regions.
family of flowering plants (bayberries)

Examples

The Myricaceae family includes several species of aromatic shrubs.
Researchers studied the distribution of Myricaceae in North America.
Some members of Myricaceae produce berries used in candle making.

Etymology

From New Latin Myricaceae, based on the genus Myrica and the standard botanical suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ maɪˌrɪkəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / maɪˌrɪkəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of aromatic shrubs and small trees, including the bayberry and sweet gale, constituting the order Myricales.
family of aromatic shrubs (bayberry, sweet gale)

Examples

The Myricaceae family includes species like the bayberry and sweet gale.
Some plants in the Myricaceae family are valued for their aromatic leaves.
Botanists study Myricaceae to understand their ecological roles in wetland environments.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Myricaceae', named after the genus 'Myrica'.
