defiantly

1. ADV

/ dɪˈfaɪəntli / (AmE), / dɪˈfaɪəntli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows open resistance or bold disobedience.
in a boldly disobedient way

Examples

She stared defiantly at the teacher, refusing to apologize for her actions.
He crossed his arms defiantly and refused to leave the room.
The child spoke defiantly when told to share his toys.

Synset Examples

he rejected her words rebelliously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebelliously - in a rebellious, resistant manner
  • Antonym: obediently - in a manner showing compliance or obedience

Etymology

From defiant + -ly; 'defiant' from French 'défiant', present participle of 'défier' (to challenge), originally from Latin 'dis-' + 'fidare' (to trust).
