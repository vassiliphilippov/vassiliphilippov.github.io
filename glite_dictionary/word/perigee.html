perigee

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛr.ɪ.dʒi / (AmE), / ˈpɛr.ɪ.dʒiː / (BrE)
the point in the orbit of a satellite or celestial object at which it is nearest to the Earth
point closest to Earth in orbit

Examples

The satellite will reach its perigee at 3:00 AM, coming closest to the Earth's surface.
Because the Moon was at perigee, it appeared slightly larger and brighter than usual.
Engineers scheduled the maneuver to occur shortly after perigee to make use of the lower altitude.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: apogee - the point in an orbit most distant from the Earth

Etymology

From Greek 'perigeion', from 'peri-' (around) + 'gē' (earth).
