captivating

1. ADJ

/ ˈkæp.tɪˌveɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkæp.tɪ.veɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
attracting and holding interest or attention; charming or enchanting
charming; attracting attention or interest

Examples

The captivating performance held the audience spellbound from start to finish.
She was drawn to the captivating colors of the sunrise over the mountains.
His captivating storytelling made even everyday experiences seem extraordinary.

Synset Examples

bewitching smile
Roosevelt was a captivating speaker
enchanting music
an enthralling book
antique papers of entrancing design
a fascinating woman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enchanting - delightfully charming or attractive
  • Synonym: fascinating - extremely interesting or charming
  • Antonym: boring - not interesting; tedious

Etymology

From Latin 'captivare' (to capture), from 'captivus' (captive).
