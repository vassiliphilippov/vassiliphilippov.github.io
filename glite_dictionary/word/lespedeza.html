lespedeza

1. NOUN

/ lɛs.pəˈdiː.zə / (AmE), / lɛs.pəˈdiː.zə / (BrE)
A genus of shrubby or herbaceous plants, widely grown for forage, soil improvement, and especially as hay in the southern United States.
genus of forage plants

Examples

The farmer planted lespedeza in his fields to improve the soil quality.
Bales of lespedeza hay are commonly used to feed livestock in the South.
Researchers found that lespedeza helps prevent soil erosion on sloped pastures.

Etymology

From New Latin, named after Vicente Manuel de Céspedes, Spanish governor of East Florida in the late 18th century.
