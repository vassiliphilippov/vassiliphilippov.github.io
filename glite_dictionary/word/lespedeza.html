Lespedeza

1. NOUN

/ lɛs.pəˈdiː.zə / (AmE), / lɛs.pɪˈdiː.zə / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs or herbs found in tropical Asia, Australia, and the eastern United States, commonly known as bush clover.
genus of shrubs/herbs (bush clover)

Examples

Lespedeza species are widely used in soil conservation to prevent erosion.
Gardeners often plant Lespedeza for its attractive flowers and ability to enrich the soil.
Many wildlife species feed on the seeds produced by Lespedeza.

Etymology

Named after Vicente Manuel de Céspedes, an 18th-century Spanish governor of Florida.
