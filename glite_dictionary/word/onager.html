onager

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːnədʒər / (AmE), / ˈɒnədʒə / (BrE)
an ancient Roman military catapult used to hurl stones or other projectiles
Roman siege weapon; type of catapult

Examples

The Romans used the onager to launch stones at enemy fortifications.
During the reenactment, a replica onager demonstrated ancient siege techniques.
The invention of the onager greatly improved the army's ability to breach city walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catapult - a device for launching objects

Etymology

From Latin onager (wild ass), originally named for the device's kicking action.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːnəɡər / (AmE), / ˈɒnəɡə / (BrE)
a wild ass native to Asia, especially found in Iran, India, and surrounding regions, noted for its speed and stamina
Asiatic wild ass

Examples

The onager is a fast-moving animal that inhabits the deserts of central Asia.
Researchers are studying the migration patterns of the endangered onager.
Ancient texts often mention the onager as a symbol of wildness and freedom.

Etymology

From Latin 'onager', from Greek 'onagros' (wild ass), from 'onos' (ass) + 'agrios' (wild).
