adulthood

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʌltˌhʊd / (AmE), / əˈdʌltˌhʊd / (BrE)
The state of being fully grown or mature, typically marked by legal and social responsibilities.
the state of being an adult

Examples

Many people look forward to the independence that adulthood brings.
Entering adulthood often comes with new challenges and responsibilities.
In some cultures, a ceremony marks the transition from childhood to adulthood.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: childhood - the state of being a child
  • Synonym: maturity - the state of being mature or fully developed

Etymology

From adult + -hood; 'adult' from Latin adultus (grown up), and '-hood' denotes a state or condition.

2. NOUN

/ əˈdʌltˌhʊd / (AmE), / əˈdʌlthʊd / (BrE)
The stage of life after childhood and adolescence, when a person is fully grown and mature.
the stage of being an adult

Examples

She gained a new sense of independence upon reaching adulthood.
Responsibilities often increase significantly during adulthood.
Some friendships last from childhood into adulthood.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: childhood - the period of being a child
  • Degree Lower: adolescence - the period following childhood and preceding adulthood

Etymology

From 'adult' + 'hood', with 'adult' from Latin 'adultus' (grown up, mature) and the suffix '-hood' meaning 'state or condition'.
