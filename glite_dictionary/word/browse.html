browse

1. NOUN

/ braʊz / (AmE), / braʊz / (BrE)
an act of reading quickly or casually, especially without a definite plan or focus
quick or casual reading

Examples

She had a quick browse through the bookstore before meeting her friends.
A browse of the magazine gave him some new recipe ideas.
The website allows users to have a browse and discover interesting articles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scan - to look through something quickly
  • Synonym: skim - to read something quickly to get a general idea
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06611001-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ braʊz / (AmE), / braʊz / (BrE)
the act of feeding on leaves, twigs, or other vegetation by nibbling, especially by animals such as deer or goats
nibbling of vegetation by animals

Examples

The deer moved slowly through the forest, stopping frequently to browse.
Fresh browse was placed in the enclosure to supplement the animals' regular diet.
Goats seek out tender browse along the hedgerows in early spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graze - to feed on growing grass or other vegetation in a field

Etymology

From Old French *broster* (sprout, bud), related to the act of nibbling young shoots.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00840684-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ braʊz / (AmE), / braʊz / (BrE)
(of animals) to feed on leaves, twigs, or other high-growing vegetation, especially in a leisurely or selective way.
(of animals) feed on leaves or twigs

Examples

The deer browse on the lower branches of the bushes every morning.
Goats often browse in the rocky fields instead of grazing on grass.
During winter, the elk browsed the shrubs for food.

Synset Examples

the herd was grazing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graze - (of animals) to feed on growing grass or herbage

Etymology

From Old French 'broster' (to sprout, bud) and Medieval Latin 'brusciare' (to browse, eat buds).

Source: WordNet

7. VERB

/ braʊz / (AmE), / braʊz / (BrE)
to eat small amounts of food, especially by sampling a variety of dishes; to graze lightly rather than have a full meal
eat small amounts; sample dishes

Examples

We decided to browse at the buffet, sampling a little of everything instead of sitting down for a full meal.
She prefers to browse when there's a big spread, rather than piling her plate high.
At food markets, he enjoys browsing on local snacks rather than eating a single dish.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graze - to eat small amounts of food throughout the day
  • Antonym: feast - to eat a lot at a large meal

Etymology

From Old French *broster* (to sprout, bud), of Germanic origin, originally meaning 'to feed on buds'.
