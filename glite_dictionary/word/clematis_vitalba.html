Clematis vitalba

1. NOUN

/ kləˈmætɪs vaɪˈtæl.bə / (AmE), / klɪˈmeɪ.tɪs vɪˈtæl.bə / (BrE)
A vigorous deciduous climbing plant native to Europe through to Afghanistan and Lebanon, bearing fragrant greenish-white flowers in summer and autumn.
species of climbing plant with fragrant white flowers

Examples

The hedgerows were filled with Clematis vitalba in full bloom.
Gardeners often use Clematis vitalba to cover unsightly fences.
The fragrant flowers of Clematis vitalba attract many pollinators in summer.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'klēmatis' (a climbing plant); species name from Latin, meaning 'white vine'.
