party-spirited

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɑːrtiˌspɪrɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtiˌspɪrɪtɪd / (BrE)
strongly supportive of or devoted to a particular political party, often showing loyalty above impartiality.
strongly loyal to a political party

Examples

The debate became heated as one party-spirited member refused to acknowledge any faults within her party.
Some journalists argue that party-spirited reporting undermines the public’s trust in the media.
Despite his party-spirited nature, he occasionally supported legislation from the opposition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partisan - showing strong, sometimes unreasoning support for a political party, cause, or person
  • Antonym: nonpartisan - not biased or affiliated with any political party

Etymology

Formed from 'party' (in the political sense) and 'spirited' (full of spirit).
