podcast

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑdˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈpɒd.kɑːst / (BrE)
A digital audio program, usually part of a series, that is made available on the Internet for downloading or streaming.
digital audio program distributed online

Examples

She listens to her favorite podcast every morning during her commute.
The science podcast featured interviews with leading researchers in medicine.
After launching his own podcast, he quickly gained thousands of subscribers.

Synset Examples

Marbles does a weekly podcast with her boyfriend Julien Solomita called "The Jenna Julien Podcast" (formerly called "The Jenna and Julien Podcast")

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broadcast - a radio or television program distributed widely

Etymology

Blend of 'iPod' (Apple's portable media player) and 'broadcast'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-81787330-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈpɑdˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈpɒdˌkɑːst / (BrE)
to distribute (multimedia files such as audio or video) over the internet for playback on a mobile device or personal computer, typically as a series of episodes
distribute multimedia files as a series online

Examples

Many independent artists now podcast their music to reach a wider audience.
He has podcasted weekly interviews with authors since 2010.
They plan to podcast their lectures for students who can’t attend in person.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broadcast - transmit (a program or some information) by radio or television

Etymology

Formed from 'iPod' (a brand of portable media player) and 'broadcast'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00970994-v
Synset POS: v
