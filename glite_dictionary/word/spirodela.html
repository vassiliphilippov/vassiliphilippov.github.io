Spirodela

1. NOUN

/ spaɪˈroʊdələ / (AmE), / spaɪˈrəʊdəliː / (BrE)
a genus of minute aquatic plants consisting of floating fronds and multiple roots, commonly known as duckweeds
a genus of small floating aquatic plants

Examples

Spirodela is often found covering the surface of calm ponds and lakes.
Researchers studied the rapid growth of Spirodela in nutrient-rich environments.
The broad, shiny fronds of Spirodela distinguish it from other types of duckweed.

Etymology

From Greek 'speira' (coil) and 'delos' (visible), referring to the plant's round shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˌspaɪroʊˈdɛlə / (AmE), / ˌspaɪrəʊˈdiːlə / (BrE)
A genus of tiny aquatic plants that float on the water's surface, consisting of a shiny, leaf-like frond and 2 to 21 roots.
genus of tiny floating aquatic plants

Examples

Spirodela commonly covers the surfaces of ponds and slow-moving streams in temperate climates.
Researchers studied the rapid reproduction rate of Spirodela in nutrient-rich waters.
Unlike its relative duckweed, Spirodela typically has multiple roots beneath each frond.

Etymology

From Greek 'speira' (coil) and 'delos' (visible), referring to the spiral arrangement of the reproductive organs.
