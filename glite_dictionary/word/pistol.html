pistol

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪs.təl / (AmE), / ˈpɪs.təl / (BrE)
a small firearm designed to be held and fired with one hand
small handheld firearm

Examples

The detective drew his pistol and approached the dark alley cautiously.
She keeps a licensed pistol locked in her bedside drawer for protection.
At the firing range, he learned how to handle a pistol safely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revolver - a type of pistol with a revolving cylinder
  • Antonym: rifle - a long-barreled firearm designed to be fired from the shoulder

Etymology

From Middle French *pistolet* (small gun), diminutive of *piste* (track, wick), possibly from Czech *píšt’ala* (pipe, whistle).
