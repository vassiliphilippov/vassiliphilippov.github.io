to environ

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈvaɪrən / (AmE), / ɪnˈvaɪərɒn / (BrE)
to surround or encircle on all sides; to enclose
to surround or encircle

Examples

Ancient city walls environed the settlement, protecting it from invasion.
Tall hedges environ the quiet garden, making it feel hidden from the world.
Clouds began to environ the mountain as the storm approached.

Synset Examples

The forest surrounds my property

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to surround - to be all around (someone or something)
  • Synonym: to enclose - to close in on all sides

Etymology

From Old French environner, from environ (around) + -er (verb suffix).
