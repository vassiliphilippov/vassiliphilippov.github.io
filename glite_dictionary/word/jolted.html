jolted

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒoʊltɪd / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊltɪd / (BrE)
Bumped or shaken suddenly and with force; moved in a jerky, abrupt manner.
shaken or bumped suddenly

Examples

The bus came to a sudden stop, leaving the jolted passengers looking bewildered.
After the earthquake, the paintings hung crookedly on the jolted walls.
She awoke from her nap, jolted by a loud crash outside.

Synset Examples

the jolted passengers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shaken - moved quickly and forcefully up and down or from side to side
  • Antonym: steady - firmly fixed, supported, or balanced; not shaking or moving

Etymology

From the verb 'jolt', possibly from English dialect jolt (to knock), of unknown origin.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʒoʊltɪd / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊltɪd / (BrE)
(of a person or a mental state) disturbed or shocked, as if by a sudden physical jolt.
psychologically disturbed or shocked

Examples

After hearing the unexpected news, she sat there jolted and speechless.
He was jolted out of his routine by the tragic event in his neighborhood.
The survivors described feeling jolted and confused in the aftermath of the explosion.

Synset Examples

retrieved his named from her jolted memory
the accident left her badly shaken

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shaken - disturbed or unsettled, especially emotionally

Etymology

From the verb 'jolt', likely of imitative origin, meaning to shake or jar with a sudden movement.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00086690-s
Synset POS: s
