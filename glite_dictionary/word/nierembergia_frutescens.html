Nierembergia frutescens

1. NOUN

/ niːˌɛrɛmˈbɜrɡiə fruːˈtɛsɛnz / (AmE), / niːˌɛrɛmˈbɜːɡiə fruːˈtɛsɛnz / (BrE)
A shrubby herb native to Chile with bluish-white tubular flowers, commonly grown as an ornamental plant.
a Chilean shrubby ornamental plant

Examples

The garden was brightened by a row of Nierembergia frutescens in full bloom.
Horticulturists recommend Nierembergia frutescens for its hardiness and attractive flowers.
She planted Nierembergia frutescens along the walkway for year-round interest.

Etymology

Genus name honors Spanish Jesuit Juan Eusebio Nieremberg; species epithet from Latin 'frutescens' (becoming shrubby).
