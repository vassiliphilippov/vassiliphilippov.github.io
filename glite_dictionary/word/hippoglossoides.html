Hippoglossoides

1. PROPN

/ ˌhɪpoʊˌɡloʊˈsɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˌɡlɒˈsɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of righteye flounders native to the North Pacific and North Atlantic Oceans, belonging to the family Pleuronectidae.
a genus of righteye flounders

Examples

Several species within the genus Hippoglossoides are important to commercial fisheries in the North Atlantic.
The American plaice is a well-known member of the Hippoglossoides genus.
Ichthyologists are studying the distribution patterns of Hippoglossoides across different oceanic regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flounder - a type of flatfish

Etymology

From Greek 'hippos' (horse) + 'glōssa' (tongue), referring to a related genus; scientific naming convention.

2. NOUN

/ hɪpoʊˌɡlɒsɔɪˈdiːz / (AmE), / hɪpəˌɡlɒsɔɪˈdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of flatfish in the family Pleuronectidae, including several species of righteye flounders native to the North Pacific and North Atlantic Oceans.
genus of righteye flounders

Examples

Several species within the Hippoglossoides genus are important for commercial fishing in northern waters.
The Hippoglossoides are known for their unique eye placement and flattened bodies.
Marine biologists conducted a survey on Hippoglossoides populations in the Bering Sea.

Etymology

From Greek 'hippos' (horse) + 'glossa' (tongue), referring to shape, with scientific Latin suffix -oides (resembling).
