to suffer

1. VERB

/ ˈsʌfɚ / (AmE), / ˈsʌfə / (BrE)
to experience physical or mental pain or distress
experience pain or distress

Examples

Many people suffer from chronic back pain.
She had to suffer in silence after the accident.
After eating the spoiled food, he began to suffer from severe stomach cramps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to endure - to continue to exist or suffer without giving up
  • Antonym: to prosper - to be successful, especially financially; to thrive
  • Degree Higher: to agonize - to suffer great anguish or struggle mentally

Etymology

From Latin 'sufferre', from sub- (under) + ferre (to bear).
