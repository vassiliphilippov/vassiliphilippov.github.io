Zoarces

1. NOUN

/ zoʊˈɑːrsiːz / (AmE), / zəʊˈɑːsiːz / (BrE)
a genus of marine fishes in the family Zoarcidae, known as eelpouts, found in cold northern waters
a genus of eelpouts (marine fish)

Examples

The genus Zoarces includes several species found in northern marine environments.
Researchers have studied Zoarces to understand adaptations to cold water.
A member of the Zoarces genus, the viviparous eelpout, gives live birth rather than laying eggs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eelpout - any fish of the family Zoarcidae

Etymology

From Greek zōarkēs (full of life), used in zoological taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ zoʊˈɑːrsiːz / (AmE), / zəʊˈɑːsiːz / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Zoarcidae, commonly known as eelpouts.
genus of marine eelpout fish

Examples

The Zoarces species are commonly found in cold northern waters.
Marine biologists identified several new members of the Zoarces genus.
The viviparous eelpout belongs to the Zoarces genus.

Etymology

From Greek *zoon* (animal) + *arkes* (strong), used as a genus name in taxonomy.
