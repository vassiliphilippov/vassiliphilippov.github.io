wiliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪ.li.nəs / (AmE), / ˈwɪl.i.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being clever and skilled at deceiving people; cunning or craftiness
cleverness in deceit; cunning

Examples

Her wiliness allowed her to outsmart her competitors without them ever suspecting a thing.
The fox is often a symbol of wiliness in folklore and fairy tales.
He admired the wiliness with which she navigated office politics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cunning - skill in achieving one's ends by deceit
  • Synonym: guile - sly or cunning intelligence
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and sincere

Etymology

From wily + -ness; 'wily' from Old Norse 'vel', meaning craft or trick.
