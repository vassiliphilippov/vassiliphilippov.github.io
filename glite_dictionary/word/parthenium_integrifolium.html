Parthenium integrifolium

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːrˈθiːniəm ɪnˌtɪɡrəˈfoʊliəm / (AmE), / ˌpɑːˈθiːniəm ɪnˌtɪɡrəˈfəʊliəm / (BrE)
a stout perennial herb native to the eastern United States, with whitish flowers and leaves traditionally used by Catawba people to treat burns
perennial herb of eastern US; medicinal uses

Examples

Native Americans used the leaves of Parthenium integrifolium to soothe burns and wounds.
Wildlife frequently visit meadows where Parthenium integrifolium grows in abundance.
Botanists identified the unusual plant as Parthenium integrifolium during their survey.

Etymology

Parthenium from Greek 'parthenion' (maidenly plant); integrifolium from Latin 'integer' (entire) + 'folium' (leaf).
