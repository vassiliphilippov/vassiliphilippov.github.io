to titillate

1. VERB

/ ˈtɪtəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɪtɪleɪt / (BrE)
to excite or stimulate someone, especially in a pleasurable or mildly erotic way
excite or stimulate pleasurably

Examples

The movie's clever humor managed to titillate even the most serious audience members.
The magazine aimed to titillate readers with its daring photographs and stories.
Her playful comments were meant to titillate rather than offend.

Synset Examples

A titillating story appeared in the usually conservative magazine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to excite - to stimulate strong feelings or reactions

Etymology

From Latin titillare (to tickle).

2. VERB

/ ˈtɪtɪˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɪtɪleɪt / (BrE)
to touch lightly so as to excite the surface nerves and cause uneasiness, laughter, or spasmodic movements
lightly stimulate by touching

Examples

The gentle breeze seemed to titillate her bare arms as she walked through the garden.
He tried to titillate her foot by running a feather across her sole.
The puppy's whiskers would titillate my skin whenever it sniffed my hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tickle - to touch the body lightly so as to cause laughter or involuntary movements

Etymology

From Latin titillare (to tickle, excite).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02124605-v
Synset POS: v
