dissoluble

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɑljəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈsɒljʊbəl / (BrE)
Able to be dissolved, especially in a liquid; capable of being dissolved or disintegrated.
able to be dissolved

Examples

The salt is dissoluble in water, making it perfect for the solution.
Most sugars are highly dissoluble and quickly disappear when stirred into tea.
Some substances are not dissoluble in organic solvents, remaining as solids.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insoluble - impossible to dissolve

Etymology

From Latin dissolubilis (able to be dissolved), from dissolvere (to dissolve).
