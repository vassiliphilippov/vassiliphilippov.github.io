wistful

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪstfəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪst.fəl / (BrE)
having or showing a feeling of vague or regretful longing; feeling sad and thoughtful
full of longing or gentle sadness

Examples

She looked out of the window with a wistful expression, remembering her childhood summers.
His voice grew wistful as he talked about old friends he hadn't seen in years.
The faded photographs gave her a wistful sense of longing for days gone by.

Synset Examples

the sensitive and wistful response of a poet to the gentler phases of beauty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melancholy - a feeling of deep sadness or sorrow
  • Antonym: cheerful - showing or expressing happiness or optimism

Etymology

From obsolete English 'wistly' (attentively, closely), from 'wist' (to know, to wish).
