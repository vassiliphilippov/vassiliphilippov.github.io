Cd

1. NOUN

/ siː diː / (AmE), / siː diː / (BrE)
a soft, bluish-white, ductile and malleable toxic metallic element, usually found with zinc ores; chemical symbol for cadmium
chemical element; symbol for cadmium

Examples

Exposure to high levels of Cd can cause serious health problems in humans.
The laboratory measured the concentration of Cd in soil samples near the factory.
Zinc and Cd are often extracted from the same mineral deposits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cadmium - a soft, bluish-white, toxic metallic element; chemical symbol Cd

Etymology

From Latin 'cadmia' (calamine), the ore from which it was first extracted.
