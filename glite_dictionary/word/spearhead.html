spearhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪrˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈspɪəˌhɛd / (BrE)
The leading or forefront part of an attacking military force or a movement.
leading unit or element in an attack

Examples

The 3rd Cavalry served as the spearhead of the advance into enemy territory.
Their unit was chosen to be the spearhead of the operation, moving in ahead of the main force.
As the spearhead broke through the defenses, the rest of the troops followed closely behind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vanguard - the foremost part of an advancing army or movement

Etymology

From spear + head, referring originally to the tip of a spear; figuratively extended to vanguard roles.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɪrˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈspɪəˌhɛd / (BrE)
the pointed head or tip of a spear, typically made of metal or stone, designed to pierce or thrust
the pointed head of a spear

Examples

The ancient warrior attached a steel spearhead to the wooden shaft.
Archaeologists discovered a stone spearhead buried at the site.
A broken spearhead was found among the ruins of the old fortress.

Etymology

From Old English spereheafod, from 'spear' + 'head'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈspɪrˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈspɪəˌhɛd / (BrE)
a person who leads or initiates an activity, movement, or attack
leader or initiator of a movement or attack

Examples

As the spearhead of the movement, she inspired many to join the cause.
The marketing campaign needed a fresh spearhead to drive it forward.
He acted as the spearhead for the new technology initiative at his company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leader - a person who leads a group, organization, or country
  • Antonym: follower - a person who accepts the leadership of another

Etymology

From Old English 'spereheafod', literally 'head of a spear'.

4. VERB

/ ˈspɪərˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈspɪəˌhɛd / (BrE)
to lead or initiate an activity, movement, or attack
to lead or initiate something

Examples

Dr. Lin will spearhead the campaign to improve community health services in the area.
The city mayor spearheaded several initiatives to promote environmental sustainability last year.
A passionate team is spearheading the development of renewable energy technologies at the university.

Synset Examples

She spearheaded the effort to find a cure for the disease

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lead - to go before or guide in direction, course, action, opinion, etc.
  • Antonym: to follow - to go or come after a leader or guide

Etymology

Originally from the noun 'spearhead', meaning the pointed tip of a spear; the verb developed by analogy to leading or initiating action.
