morphemic

1. ADJ

/ mɔrˈfimɪk / (AmE), / mɔːˈfiːmɪk / (BrE)
relating to or consisting of morphemes, the smallest meaningful units in a language
relating to morphemes in language

Examples

The morphemic analysis of the word reveals its complex structure.
Children often make morphemic errors while learning to spell new words.
A morphemic approach can help linguists understand how meaning is constructed in different languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morphological - relating to the form and structure of words in a language

Etymology

From 'morpheme', after 'phonemic'; 'morpheme' from French 'morphème' (from Greek 'morphē' meaning 'form').
