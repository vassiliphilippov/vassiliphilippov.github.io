Meiji Tenno

1. PROPN

/ ˌmeɪdʒi ˈtɛnoʊ / (AmE), / ˌmeɪdʒi ˈtɛnəʊ / (BrE)
the emperor of Japan from 1867 to 1912, under whose rule Japan underwent major modernization and westernization
emperor of Japan (1867–1912); modernized Japan

Examples

The reign of Meiji Tenno marked a turning point in Japan's history.
Many historians credit Meiji Tenno for successfully opening Japan to the West.
The museum's exhibit showcases the accomplishments of Meiji Tenno and his era.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Emperor Meiji - Emperor of Japan, 1867–1912, modernized Japan

Etymology

From Japanese 明治天皇 (Meiji Tennō), meaning 'Enlightened Rule Emperor'.
