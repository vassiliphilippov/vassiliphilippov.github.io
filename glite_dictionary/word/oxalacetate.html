oxalacetate

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑksələˈsiːteɪt / (AmE), / ˌɒksələˈsiːteɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester of oxalacetic acid, commonly involved in biochemical metabolic pathways
a salt or ester of oxalacetic acid
medical

Examples

Oxalacetate plays a crucial role in the citric acid cycle of cellular metabolism.
Some supplements contain oxalacetate for potential cognitive benefits.
The transamination of aspartate produces oxalacetate in the body.

Etymology

From oxalacetic acid, itself from oxalic acid + acetic acid.
