atmosphere

1. NOUN

/ ˈæt.məˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈætməsfɪə / (BrE)
the layer of gases surrounding the Earth or another planet
the Earth's layer of gases

Examples

The Earth's atmosphere protects us from harmful solar radiation.
Scientists study how pollution affects the atmosphere and climate change.
Astronauts wore special suits because the Moon has no atmosphere.

Synset Examples

there was great heat as the comet entered the atmosphere
it was exposed to the air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: air - the invisible gaseous substance surrounding the Earth, a mixture mainly of oxygen and nitrogen

Etymology

From Greek *atmos* (vapor) + *sphaira* (sphere).

5. NOUN

/ ˈæt.məˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈæt.məsˌfɪə / (BrE)
A unit of pressure equal to the pressure needed to support a column of mercury 760 millimeters high at sea level and 0°C (equivalent to 101,325 pascals).
unit of pressure (atm)

Examples

The experimental chamber was kept at one atmosphere of pressure.
At sea level, the standard atmosphere is used as a reference for many scientific calculations.
Pressure in the diving tank exceeded two atmospheres, making it essential to monitor safety protocols.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bar - unit of pressure equal to 100,000 pascals

Etymology

From Modern Latin 'atmosphaera', originally meaning 'envelope of gases surrounding the Earth'.
