daphnia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæfniə / (AmE), / ˈdæfniə / (BrE)
a minute freshwater crustacean of the genus Daphnia, with a rounded body in a transparent shell, moving with jerky motions via branched antennae; also called water flea.
minute freshwater crustacean; water flea

Examples

Daphnia are often used in science classes to study the effects of environmental changes on heart rate.
Pond ecosystems can benefit from the presence of daphnia, which feed on algae and keep water clear.
Microscopes revealed tiny daphnia darting around in the freshwater sample.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water flea - a small freshwater crustacean, especially of the genus Daphnia

Etymology

From modern Latin Daphnia, the genus name, said to be named after the nymph Daphne from Greek mythology.
