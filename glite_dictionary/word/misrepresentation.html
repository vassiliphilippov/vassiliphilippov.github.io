misrepresentation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪsˌrɛprɪˌzɛnˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪsrɛprɪzɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of deliberately giving a false or misleading account of the facts
deliberate false statement of facts

Examples

The company was sued for misrepresentation of its financial status.
Accusations of misrepresentation led to a lengthy investigation.
She lost her job after an intentional misrepresentation of the facts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deception - the act of deceiving or misleading someone
  • Antonym: truth - the quality or state of being true; factual reality

Etymology

From mis- (wrong) + representation, after represent.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɪsˌrɛprɪˌzɛnˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˌrɛprɪzɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
The action or offense of giving a false or misleading account of the nature of something.
false or misleading account

Examples

The company was sued for misrepresentation in its advertising claims.
Her statement was a deliberate misrepresentation of the facts to influence the board's decision.
If you make a misrepresentation on your application, your contract may be voided.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deception - the act of deceiving or misleading someone
  • Antonym: truth - the quality or state of being true
  • Synonym: fraud - wrongful deception intended to result in financial or personal gain

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + representation, from Latin repraesentātiō (representation).
