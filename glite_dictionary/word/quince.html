quince

1. NOUN

/ kwɪns / (AmE), / kwɪns / (BrE)
a small Asian tree with pinkish flowers and pear-shaped, edible fruit; widely cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value
small tree with pear-shaped fruit

Examples

The quince produces aromatic yellow fruit that is often used in jams and jellies.
She planted a quince in her garden for its beautiful spring blossoms.
Many Middle Eastern recipes call for quince as a sweet and tart ingredient in stews.

Etymology

From Old French cooin, from Latin cydonia (malum), meaning 'Cydonian apple'.

2. NOUN

/ kwɪns / (AmE), / kwɪns / (BrE)
An aromatic, acidic, pear-shaped fruit, yellow when ripe, used especially for making preserves and jellies.
aromatic, acidic, pear-shaped fruit

Examples

Homemade jelly made from quince has a bright, tart flavor that many people enjoy.
They harvested several quinces from the old tree in the orchard last autumn.
The market vendor recommended using quince in a sweet-and-sour chutney.

Etymology

From Old French *cooin* (now *coing*), from Latin *cotoneum malum* (Cydonian apple).
