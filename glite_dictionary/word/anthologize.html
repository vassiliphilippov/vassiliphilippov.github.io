to anthologize

1. VERB

/ ænˈθɑl.əˌdʒaɪz / (AmE), / ænˈθɒl.əˌdʒaɪz / (BrE)
to collect and publish a selection of writings, music, or other works in an anthology.
collect works for an anthology

Examples

The editor decided to anthologize her best poems from the last decade.
Works by several contemporary artists were anthologized in the new collection.
She is known for anthologizing lesser-known short stories from her region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to compile - to collect and assemble written material

Etymology

From 'anthology', itself from Greek 'anthologia' (flower gathering), with the suffix '-ize'.
