backspace

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌspeɪs / (AmE), / ˈbæk.speɪs / (BrE)
the key on a typewriter or computer keyboard that moves the cursor or carriage one space backward
a keyboard key that moves the cursor back

Examples

Press the backspace key to erase the last letter you typed.
My old typewriter has a sticky backspace that doesn't move the carriage smoothly.
If you make a mistake, just hit backspace to correct it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delete key - a key on a keyboard that removes the character after the cursor

Etymology

From 'back' + 'space', referring to the action of moving a space backward.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02774009-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈbækˌspeɪs / (AmE), / ˈbækˌspeɪs / (BrE)
to move the cursor or carriage backward by one space, especially to erase the previous character when using a keyboard or typewriter
move cursor/carriage one space back

Examples

If you make a mistake while typing, just backspace to erase the error.
She quickly backspaced the last sentence and started again.
Remember to backspace over any extra spaces before saving the document.

Synset Examples

To erase, you must backspace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to delete - remove or erase text or characters
