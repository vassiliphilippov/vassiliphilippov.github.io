Chaetodontidae

1. NOUN

/ kiˌtoʊdɒnˈtɪdiː / (AmE), / kiˌːtɒdɒnˈtɪdɪi / (BrE)
A family of brightly colored marine fish known as butterflyfishes, found especially on coral reefs in tropical seas.
family of butterflyfishes (marine fish)

Examples

The Chaetodontidae are popular among aquarists for their striking patterns and coloration.
Marine biologists conducted a survey of Chaetodontidae populations on the reef.
Many species within the Chaetodontidae family are indicators of coral reef health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butterflyfish - common name for fishes in the Chaetodontidae family

Etymology

From Greek 'chaite' (hair, bristle) and 'odous, odont-' (tooth), referring to their bristle-like teeth.
