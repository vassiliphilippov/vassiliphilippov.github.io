quoin

1. NOUN

/ kɔɪn / (AmE), / kɔɪn / (BrE)
(architecture) a solid exterior angle of a building, especially one formed by a stone or brick at the corner.
exterior corner of a building (architecture)

Examples

The architect specified large granite quoins for the corners of the mansion.
Distinctive white quoins accentuate the red brick façade of the historic city hall.
Restoration efforts included repairing several damaged quoins to preserve the building's structural integrity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornerstone - a stone that forms the base of a corner of a building, often ceremonial

Etymology

From Old French *coing*, from Latin *cuneus* (wedge).

2. NOUN

/ kɔɪn / (AmE), / kɔɪn / (BrE)
the central stone at the apex of an arch, also called a keystone
the keystone of an arch

Examples

The quoin bears the weight of the arch and locks the other stones into position.
Restorers carefully replaced the damaged quoin during the arch's renovation.
Without a properly shaped quoin, the stability of the arch could be compromised.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keystone - the central, wedge-shaped stone at the apex of an arch

Etymology

From Old French coign (corner, wedge), from Latin cuneus (wedge).

3. NOUN

/ kɔɪn / (AmE), / kɔɪn / (BrE)
An expandable metal or wooden wedge used by printers to lock a form in a chase.
printing wedge; lock forms in a chase

Examples

The printer secured the type with a quoin before starting the press.
Adjusting the quoin tightly is essential to keep the layout stable during printing.
Modern printing rarely uses the traditional quoin in letterpress work.

Etymology

From Old French 'coing' meaning 'wedge' or 'corner'; related to Latin 'cuneus' (wedge).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
