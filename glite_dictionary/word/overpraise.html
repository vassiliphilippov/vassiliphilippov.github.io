overpraise

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈpreɪz / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈpreɪz / (BrE)
to praise someone or something too much; to give excessive praise
praise excessively

Examples

The media often overpraise celebrities for minor achievements.
Teachers should be careful not to overpraise students, as it can lead to complacency.
He felt embarrassed when his boss overpraised his work in front of everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
  • Antonym: to criticize - to express disapproval or point out faults

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (excessively) + 'praise'.

WordNet ID: oewn-00859089-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərˈpreɪz / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈpreɪz / (BrE)
excessive or inappropriate praise that is not fully deserved
excessive or undeserved praise

Examples

The coach warned against giving overpraise to young athletes, as it could hinder their growth.
A teacher's overpraise can sometimes make students arrogant rather than motivated.
Critics accused the media of overpraise for the actor's average performance.

Synset Examples

The piano instructor believes in encouraging her students but avoids any overpraise that might make them complacent.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: praise - express approval or admiration
  • Synonym: flattery - excessive and insincere praise

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (excessively) + 'praise'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-92462511-n
Synset POS: n
