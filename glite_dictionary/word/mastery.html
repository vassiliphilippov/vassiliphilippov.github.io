mastery

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæstəri / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstəri / (BrE)
control or power to dominate, rule, or defeat someone or something
power to dominate or control

Examples

The country’s mastery of the region was unchallenged for decades.
Their mastery of the seas allowed them to expand their empire rapidly.
After years of conflict, the general achieved mastery over his rivals.

Synset Examples

mastery of the seas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: command - the ability or control over something
  • Synonym: dominance - the state of having power and influence over others
  • Antonym: submission - the act of yielding to the authority of another

Etymology

From Old French 'maistrie', from Latin 'magister' (master).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæstəri / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstəri / (BrE)
great skill or knowledge in a particular subject or activity; complete control or command of something
great skill or complete command

Examples

She achieved mastery of the piano after years of relentless practice.
His mastery in mathematics impressed his professors and peers alike.
A deep understanding of language is essential for true mastery of translation.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proficiency - high degree of skill or competence
  • Antonym: incompetence - lack of skill or ability

Etymology

From Old French 'maistrie' (skill, mastery), from 'maistre' (master).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmæstəri / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstəri / (BrE)
control or dominance over someone or something; the state of having power over others.
control over others

Examples

After years of hard work, he finally achieved mastery over his competitors.
The king asserted his mastery by uniting all the tribes under his rule.
Colonial powers often sought mastery of new territories and their populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dominance - the state of being dominant or in control
  • Antonym: submission - the act of accepting authority or control from someone else

Etymology

From Old French 'maistrie', based on Latin 'magister' (master).
