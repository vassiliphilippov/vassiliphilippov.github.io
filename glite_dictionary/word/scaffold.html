scaffold

1. VERB

/ ˈskæfəld / (AmE), / ˈskæfəʊld / (BrE)
to provide (a building or structure) with a temporary platform or framework for support during work or repairs
provide with a support framework

Examples

We need to scaffold the exterior of the museum before beginning restoration work.
Contractors scaffolded the entire tower so they could safely install new windows.
They always scaffold old churches very carefully to prevent accidental damage during repairs.

Synset Examples

scaffold the building before painting it

Etymology

From Old French *eschafault* (platform), from Late Latin *scadafalcum* (platform, scaffold).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskæfəld / (AmE), / ˈskafəld / (BrE)
a raised platform on which criminals were executed by hanging or beheading, especially in the past
platform for executions (historical)
archaic

Examples

The condemned man ascended the scaffold with dignity, ready to face his fate.
Hundreds gathered near the scaffold to witness the execution in the town square.
In historical novels, the scaffold often appears as a symbol of justice or oppression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gallows - a structure for hanging criminals
  • Synonym: guillotine - a device for executing people by beheading

Etymology

From Old French 'eschafaut', from Vulgar Latin *catafalicum (scaffold).

3. NOUN

/ ˈskæfəld / (AmE), / ˈskæfəld / (BrE)
a temporary structure made of wooden planks and metal poles, erected around a building to provide a platform for workers during construction, repair, or cleaning
temporary structure for workers around buildings

Examples

The workers erected a scaffold around the building to repair the damaged roof.
Before painting the exterior, they assembled a sturdy scaffold for easy access to the upper floors.
Safety harnesses are required when working on a scaffold high above the ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: platform - a raised level surface on which people or things can stand

Etymology

From Old French *eschaffaut*, from Vulgar Latin *scadafaldum* (platform, stage).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04148488-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈskæf.əld / (AmE), / ˈskæf.əld / (BrE)
a temporary structure used to support artists or their materials while working on large installations, murals, or other works
temporary structure for artists' works

Examples

The artist climbed the scaffold to put the finishing touches on her mural.
During the installation, paints and tools were spread across the scaffold for easy access.
A crew assembled the scaffold before the sculptor began working on the tall structure.

Synset Examples

The artist plans to go to the scaffold for her next installation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scaffolding - temporary platform used to support workers and materials during construction or repair

Etymology

From Old French 'eschafaut' (platform, scaffold), ultimately from Germanic roots.
