pastorate

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæs.təˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.stər.ət / (BrE)
All the pastors collectively, viewed as a body within a church or religious denomination.
body of pastors collectively

Examples

The annual conference brought together members of the pastorate from across the country.
The pastorate has issued a statement regarding the new church policies.
He has always valued the support of the pastorate in his ministry.

Etymology

From Latin 'pastoratus' (office of a shepherd or pastor).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæs.tɚ.ət / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.stər.ət / (BrE)
the office or position of a pastor, especially in a church; the period during which a pastor serves
the office or period of serving as pastor

Examples

During his twenty-year pastorate, the church grew steadily in both size and spirit.
She was appointed to the pastorate of the historic downtown congregation.
His first pastorate was in a small rural community where he built strong relationships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pastorship - the position or period of being a pastor

Etymology

From Latin 'pastoratus', from 'pastor' (shepherd, pastor).
