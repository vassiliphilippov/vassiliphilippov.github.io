Bidens bipinnata

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈdɛnz baɪpɪˈneɪtə / (AmE), / baɪˈdɛnz baɪpɪˈneɪtə / (BrE)
a species of bur marigold native to the eastern United States, known for its yellow flowers and bristly seeds that cling to fur and clothing
a species of bur marigold (plant)

Examples

Bidens bipinnata often grows in disturbed soils along roadsides and riverbanks.
The seeds of Bidens bipinnata easily attach to animals, aiding in their dispersal.
In late summer, the yellow flowers of Bidens bipinnata brighten many Eastern American meadows.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Bidens', meaning 'two-toothed', and 'bipinnata', meaning 'twice-pinnate', referencing the plant's leaf structure.
