dumbfounded

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʌmˌfaʊndɪd / (AmE), / ˈdʌmˌfaʊndɪd / (BrE)
So shocked or surprised that you are unable to speak; filled with astonishment.
astonished, unable to speak

Examples

She was dumbfounded when she heard she had won the lottery.
The audience sat dumbfounded as the magician finished his final trick.
He stared at the broken vase, completely dumbfounded by what had just happened.

Synset Examples

a circle of policemen stood dumbfounded by her denial of having seen the accident
the flabbergasted aldermen were speechless
was thunderstruck by the news of his promotion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astonished - very surprised
  • Synonym: flabbergasted - extremely surprised or shocked
  • Synonym: thunderstruck - extremely surprised or shocked, as if struck by thunder

Etymology

From 'dumb' (unable to speak) + 'found' in obsolete sense 'confound, stupefy.'

2. ADJ

/ ˌdʌmˈfaʊndɪd / (AmE), / ˌdʌmˈfaʊndɪd / (BrE)
so amazed or shocked that one is unable to speak; extremely astonished or surprised
speechless with astonishment or surprise

Examples

She was dumbfounded when she won the award, unable to say a word.
The students sat dumbfounded as the teacher announced the surprise test.
He looked dumbfounded by the sudden change in her attitude.

Synset Examples

a circle of policemen stood dumbfounded by her denial of having seen the accident
the flabbergasted aldermen were speechless
was thunderstruck by the news of his promotion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astonished - very surprised
  • Synonym: flabbergasted - extremely surprised or shocked
  • Antonym: unimpressed - not feeling admiration or interest

Etymology

From 'dumb' (speechless) + 'found' (from 'confound')
