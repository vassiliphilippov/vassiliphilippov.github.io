scup

1. NOUN

/ skʌp / (AmE), / skʌp / (BrE)
a porgy, especially the species Stenotomus chrysops, found in the southern Atlantic coastal waters of North America and valued as a food fish
a type of porgy fish from Atlantic North America

Examples

Fishermen in Rhode Island often catch scup during the summer months.
The restaurant features grilled scup on its menu, highlighting local seafood.
Marine biologists study the migration patterns of scup along the Atlantic coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porgy - a fish of the family Sparidae, including varieties similar to the scup

Etymology

From Narragansett *mishcùp*, the original Native American name for the fish.

3. NOUN

/ skʌp / (AmE), / skʌp / (BrE)
a marine fish of the western Atlantic, commonly found from South Carolina to Maine, valued as a food fish
a North American Atlantic food fish

Examples

Local fishermen often catch scup during the summer season off Cape Cod.
The grilled scup was served with a lemon-butter sauce at the seaside restaurant.
Regulations now limit the daily catch of scup to preserve the population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porgy - another name for the scup, especially in American English

Etymology

Shortened form of 'escup', from Narragansett *mishcuppauog* (fish), via earlier American English.
