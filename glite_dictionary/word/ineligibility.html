ineligibility

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌɛlɪdʒəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪnˌɛlɪdʒɪˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the state or quality of not being allowed or qualified to do or receive something
the state of being ineligible

Examples

Because of his age, he faced ineligibility for the competition.
The student’s low grades led to her ineligibility for the scholarship program.
A prior conviction can result in ineligibility to vote in certain states.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: eligibility - the quality or state of being eligible
  • Synonym: ineligible - not having the right to do or obtain something; not eligible

Etymology

From in- (not) + eligibility, from Latin eligibilis (that may be chosen).
