Edmontonia

1. PROPN

/ ɛdˌmɒnˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / ɛdˌmɒnˈtəʊniə / (BrE)
a genus of heavily armored, spiked dinosaur from the Late Cretaceous period, known for its defensive body plates and semi-upright posture
genus of heavily armored, spiked dinosaur

Examples

The fossils of Edmontonia reveal an impressive array of defensive spikes along its back and sides.
Paleontologists believe Edmontonia used its armor to protect itself from predators.
A life-sized model of Edmontonia is on display at the natural history museum.

Etymology

Named after Edmonton, Canada, where its fossils were first discovered.
