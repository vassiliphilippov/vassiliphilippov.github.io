ungallant

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɡæl.ənt / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡæl.ənt / (BrE)
rudely discourteous or lacking in polite or chivalrous behavior, especially towards others
offensively discourteous or rude

Examples

His ungallant remarks shocked everyone at the dinner table.
It was ungallant of her to leave without thanking her hosts.
The ungallant actions of the guest were the talk of the evening.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gallant - polite and attentive, especially toward women
  • Synonym: chivalrous - courteous and gallant, especially toward women

Etymology

Formed with the prefix 'un-' meaning 'not' and 'gallant,' from Old French 'galant' (courteous, brave).
