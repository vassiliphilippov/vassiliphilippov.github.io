Populus trichocarpa

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːpjələs traɪkoʊˈkɑːrpə / (AmE), / ˈpɒpjʊləs trɪkəʊˈkɑːpə / (BrE)
A species of cottonwood tree native to western North America, with dark green glossy leaves above and rusty or silvery undersides.
western North American cottonwood tree species

Examples

The Populus trichocarpa thrives along riverbanks in the Pacific Northwest.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Populus trichocarpa in different climates.
The leaves of Populus trichocarpa are lustrous green on top and silvery underneath.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black cottonwood - common name for Populus trichocarpa

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Populus (poplar tree) and Greek tricho- (hair, in reference to hairy capsules) + carpa (fruit).
