Dusicyon

1. NOUN

/ ˌduːsɪˈkaɪən / (AmE), / ˌdjuːsɪˈkaɪən / (BrE)
A genus of South American wild canids, including the crab-eating dog and extinct species such as the Falkland Islands wolf.
genus of South American wild canids

Examples

The extinct Falkland Islands wolf belonged to the genus Dusicyon.
Scientists have reclassified several South American canids under Dusicyon based on genetic evidence.
The crab-eating dog, once placed in Dusicyon, is now usually classified as Cerdocyon thous.

Etymology

From Greek 'dous' (sweet, gentle) + 'kyon' (dog), used in scientific taxonomy.

a genus of South American wild dogs, including the crab-eating dog and related extinct species
a genus of South American wild dogs

Examples

Researchers are studying the genetic diversity within Dusicyon.
The crab-eating fox was previously classified under Dusicyon.

Etymology

From Greek dousikos (of the dusky) + kyon (dog).
