nth

1. ADJ

/ ɛnθ / (AmE), / ɛnθ / (BrE)
referring to the last or greatest in a series, especially of indeterminate or very large number; used to indicate something at an indefinitely high degree
to the last or greatest degree in a series

Examples

He pushed the idea to the nth degree despite the risks.
Her patience was tested to the nth limit by the constant delays.
The formula can be applied to the nth term in the sequence.

Synset Examples

to the nth degree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ultimate - being the last or final; maximum possible
  • Degree Higher: infinite - without limit; a value larger than any assignable quantity

Etymology

From mathematical notation using 'n' as a variable for a position in a series, with '-th' as in 'fourth', 'fifth', etc.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02220813-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ɛnθ / (AmE), / ɛnθ / (BrE)
denoting the last or greatest in an indefinitely large series, often used in expressions to indicate an unspecified but large or extreme value or instance
last or greatest in a long series

Examples

She repeated the instructions for the nth time, hoping he would finally listen.
His excuses became less convincing by the nth attempt.
Pushed to the nth degree, the machine finally broke down.

Synset Examples

to the nth degree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: umpteenth - an indefinitely large number in a sequence

Etymology

From mathematical notation, where 'n' stands for any integer and '-th' denotes ordinal position.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02220813-s
Synset POS: s
