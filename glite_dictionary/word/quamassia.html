Quamassia

1. NOUN

/ kwəˈmɑː.si.ə / (AmE), / kwəˈmas.i.ə / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants native to North and South America, characterized by large edible bulbs
genus of bulbous North and South American herbs

Examples

Scientists studied the distribution of Quamassia in various regions of North America.
The edible bulbs of Quamassia were an important food source for Indigenous peoples.
Hiking through the meadow, she identified several plants as belonging to the Quamassia genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Camassia - a related genus of plants with edible bulbs

Etymology

From the earlier name for camas, from North American Indigenous (Nez Percé 'qém’es').
