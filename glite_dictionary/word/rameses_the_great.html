Rameses the Great

1. PROPN

/ ˈræmɪˌsiz ðə ˈɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˈræmɪˌsiːz ðə ˈɡreɪt / (BrE)
the third pharaoh of the Nineteenth Dynasty of Egypt (reigned c. 1279–1213 BC), renowned for his extensive building projects and military campaigns
Egyptian pharaoh, 1279–1213 BC

Examples

Rameses the Great is often regarded as one of ancient Egypt’s most powerful and accomplished pharaohs.
Many monumental temples, including the famous Abu Simbel, were constructed during the reign of Rameses the Great.
Historians study the military campaigns led by Rameses the Great, especially the Battle of Kadesh.

Etymology

From Greek Rhampsinitos, via Hebrew Ra‘meses, from Egyptian Ra-msj-sw (Ra is the one who bore him).
