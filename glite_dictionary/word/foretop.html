foretop

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌtɑp / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌtɒp / (BrE)
A lock of a horse's mane that grows forward between the ears, often hanging down over the forehead.
lock of horse's mane between ears

Examples

The horse's foretop was neatly braided for the show.
Mud clung to the foretop as the mare galloped through the field.
She smoothed the pony's foretop before placing the bridle on.

Etymology

From 'fore-' (meaning front) + 'top' (tuft of hair).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌtɑp / (AmE), / ˈfɔːtɒp / (BrE)
a platform on a sailing ship at the top of the foremast, used as a lookout or for operating sails
platform at top of foremast on a ship

Examples

The sailor climbed up to the foretop to keep watch over the horizon.
From the foretop, you could spot approaching ships long before they reached the harbor.
Heavy winds shook the foretop as the crew worked to secure the sails.

Etymology

From 'fore-' (front) and 'top' (platform at masthead) in nautical terminology.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
