Turritis

1. NOUN

/ təˈraɪtɪs / (AmE), / təˈraɪtɪs / (BrE)
A small genus of flowering plants in the mustard family, closely related to Arabis.
a genus of flowering plants

Examples

The wild rocket, once classified under Turritis, is now placed in a different genus.
Several species in the Turritis genus can be found in temperate regions worldwide.
Botanists debated whether Turritis should remain a separate genus or be merged with Arabis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arabis - a related genus of flowering plants in the mustard family

Etymology

From Latin 'turritis', meaning 'like a tower', referring to the tall flower spikes.
