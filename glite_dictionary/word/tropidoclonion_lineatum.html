Tropidoclonion lineatum

1. NOUN

/ troʊˌpɪdoʊˈkloʊniən laɪˈneɪtəm / (AmE), / trəˌpɪdɒˈkləʊnɪən lʌɪˈneɪtəm / (BrE)
A small, secretive snake native to central United States, often found in city dumps, parks, prairies, and open woods, primarily feeding on earthworms; commonly known as the lined snake.
a small, secretive snake of central U.S.

Examples

The Tropidoclonion lineatum is rarely seen because it spends much of its time underground.
Biologists often find Tropidoclonion lineatum in city parks during surveys of urban wildlife.
Because the Tropidoclonion lineatum feeds mostly on earthworms, its presence indicates healthy soil conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lined snake - common name for Tropidoclonion lineatum

Etymology

Genus name from Greek *tropidos* (keel) + *clonion* (little branch); species from Latin *lineatum* (lined).
