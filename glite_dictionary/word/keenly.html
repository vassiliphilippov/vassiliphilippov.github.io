keenly

1. ADV

/ ˈkiːnli / (AmE), / ˈkiːnli / (BrE)
in a way that shows deep, penetrating, or intense perception or feeling
with intense perception or feeling

Examples

She was keenly interested in every detail of the investigation.
He felt the loss keenly, unable to hide his disappointment.
They listened keenly as the professor explained the complex theory.

Synset Examples

he was keenly aware of his own shortcomings
she pitied her sister acutely
acutely aware

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acutely - in a sharp or intense manner
  • Antonym: vaguely - in a way that is not clear or sharp

Etymology

Formed from 'keen' + '-ly'; 'keen' from Middle English 'kene', meaning sharp.
