Balkans

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑlkənz / (AmE), / ˈbɔːlkənz / (BrE)
a major mountain range extending across Bulgaria and the central part of the Balkan Peninsula
major mountain range in Bulgaria/Balkan Peninsula

Examples

The Balkans run across Bulgaria, forming one of the country's most prominent natural features.
Several rivers rise in the Balkans before flowing to the surrounding plains.
Mountains in the Balkans are popular destinations for hiking and sightseeing.

Etymology

From Turkish *Balkan* (a mountain); originally of Turkic origin meaning 'a chain of wooded mountains'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑlkənz / (AmE), / ˈbɔːlkənz / (BrE)
a large peninsula in southeastern Europe, including countries such as Albania, Bulgaria, Greece, and others, known for its diverse cultures and turbulent history
southeastern European peninsula

Examples

The Balkans have a rich history influenced by many cultures and empires.
Political tensions in the Balkans have often shaped the history of Europe.
She traveled across the Balkans to explore its unique landscapes and traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Balkan Peninsula - another term for the Balkans, referring to the same geographic region

Etymology

From the Turkish 'Balkan,' meaning 'mountain.'

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɔːlkənz / (AmE), / ˈbɔːlkənz / (BrE)
The countries of Southeast Europe that occupy the Balkan Peninsula, collectively considered as a region distinguished by shared historical, cultural, and geographical features.
the Balkan countries collectively

Examples

The political complexities of the Balkans have shaped European history for centuries.
Many tourists are discovering the beautiful landscapes and cultures of the Balkans.
She traveled extensively through the Balkans to study its diverse traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Balkan Peninsula - a large peninsula in southeastern Europe

Etymology

From 'Balkan', the Turkish word for 'a chain of wooded mountains', referring to the Balkan Mountains in Bulgaria.
