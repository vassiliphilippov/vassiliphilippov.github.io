Haida

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdə / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdə / (BrE)
the language of the Haida people, belonging to the Na-Dene language family and spoken in parts of western Canada and Alaska
a Na-Dene language of the Haida people

Examples

Linguists are working to document the endangered Haida language.
She is fluent in both English and Haida.
The university offers a course in Haida for students interested in indigenous languages.

Etymology

From the name of the Haida people, who call themselves X̲aat Kíl for the language.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdə / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdə / (BrE)
a member of an Indigenous people living on the Pacific coast of British Columbia and southwestern Alaska, known for their seafaring traditions and art
member of a Pacific Northwest Indigenous people

Examples

The Haida have a rich tradition of carved totem poles and seafaring canoes.
Many Haida now reside on Haida Gwaii, formerly known as the Queen Charlotte Islands.
A Haida artist exhibited his work at the national museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: First Nations - Indigenous peoples in Canada south of the Arctic region

Etymology

From the Haida people's own name for themselves, 'Haidagaay.'
