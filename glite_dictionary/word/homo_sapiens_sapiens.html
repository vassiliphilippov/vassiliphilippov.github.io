Homo sapiens sapiens

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊmoʊ ˈseɪpiənz ˈseɪpiənz / (AmE), / ˈhəʊməʊ ˈsapɪɛnz ˈsapɪɛnz / (BrE)
the subspecies of Homo sapiens that includes all modern humans
modern human subspecies

Examples

All members of the species Homo sapiens sapiens are considered modern humans.
Anthropologists study the evolutionary history of Homo sapiens sapiens alongside other hominid species.
Fossils of Homo sapiens sapiens have been discovered on every continent.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Homo sapiens - the species that includes modern humans and their immediate ancestors

Etymology

From Latin: 'homo' (man, human), 'sapiens' (wise), repeated for the subspecies designation.
