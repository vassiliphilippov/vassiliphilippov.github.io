hoodmold

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʊdˌmoʊld / (AmE), / ˈhʊd.məʊld / (BrE)
A projecting molding over the arch of a window or door, usually made of stone, designed to throw off rainwater.
projecting molding over a window or door

Examples

The medieval church featured a decorative hoodmold above each stained glass window.
Restorers carefully repaired the damaged hoodmold on the building’s main entrance.
The rainwater was deflected away from the doorway by the ornate stone hoodmold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dripstone - a stone molding designed to divert rainwater, especially over windows or doors

Etymology

From 'hood' meaning a covering and 'mold' referring to a shaped form in architecture.
