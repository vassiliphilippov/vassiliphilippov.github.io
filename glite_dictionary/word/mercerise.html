to mercerise

1. VERB

/ ˈmɜrsəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɜːsəraɪz / (BrE)
to treat (cotton or other fabrics) with a caustic alkali solution, usually to increase strength and give a shiny appearance
treat fabric for strength and shine
british

Examples

The factory will mercerise the cotton to give it a brighter finish.
Fabrics that have been mercerised often feel smoother and look more luxurious.
They specialize in mercerising textiles for high-end fashion brands.

Synset Examples

mercerize cotton

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mercerize - to treat cotton or fabric to increase luster and strength (American spelling)

Etymology

From the name of John Mercer (1791–1866), the English chemist who invented the process.
