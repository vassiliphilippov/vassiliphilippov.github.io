Frederick William III of Prussia

1. PROPN

/ ˈfrɛdərɪk ˈwɪljəm ðə ˈprʌʃə / (AmE), / ˈfrɛdərɪk ˈwɪljəm ðɪ ˈprʌʃə / (BrE)
(1770–1840) King of Prussia who reigned from 1797 to 1840 and led Prussia through the Napoleonic Wars.
King of Prussia (1770–1840) during Napoleonic Wars

Examples

Frederick William III of Prussia reluctantly allied with Napoleon after Prussia's defeat in 1806.
Several reforms were enacted in Prussia during the reign of Frederick William III of Prussia.
The leadership of Frederick William III of Prussia was critical during the wars against Napoleon.
