tactful

1. ADJ

/ ˈtæktfəl / (AmE), / ˈtaktfʊl / (BrE)
having or showing skill and sensitivity in dealing with others or with difficult issues; considerate and discreet.
showing skill and sensitivity in dealing with people

Examples

She gave a tactful response to his awkward question, avoiding any embarrassment.
It requires a tactful approach to discuss such sensitive matters.
He was always tactful when giving feedback to his students.

Synset Examples

by diplomatic conduct he avoided antagonizing anyone
a tactful way of correcting someone
the agency got the kid-glove treatment on Capitol Hill

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diplomatic - having or showing an ability to deal with people in a sensitive and effective way
  • Antonym: tactless - lacking or showing a lack of skill and sensitivity in dealing with others

Etymology

From tact + -ful, based on 'tact' from French 'tact' (sense of touch; sensitivity).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtæktfəl / (AmE), / ˈtæktfʊl / (BrE)
Having or showing sensitivity and skill in dealing with others or with difficult issues without causing offense.
sensitive and considerate in dealing with others

Examples

She gave a tactful answer that avoided hurting anyone's feelings.
His tactful approach made the meeting much more productive.
It's important to be tactful when delivering criticism to your colleagues.

Synset Examples

she was tactful enough not to shatter his illusion
a tactful remark eased her embarrassment

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tactless - lacking sensitivity or consideration in dealing with others

Etymology

From 'tact' (sensitivity in dealing with others), modeled after French 'tactful', first used in the 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02392951-a
Synset POS: a
