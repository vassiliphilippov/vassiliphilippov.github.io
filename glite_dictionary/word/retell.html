to retell

1. VERB

/ riˈtɛl / (AmE), / riˈtɛl / (BrE)
to tell a story or describe an event again, often in a different way or with additional details
tell again or narrate anew

Examples

The teacher asked the students to retell the story in their own words.
She loves to retell old family tales during holidays.
He retold the events so vividly that everyone felt they were there.

Synset Examples

recite a poem
retell a story

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recount - to tell in detail; narrate
  • Synonym: to summarize - to give a brief statement of the main points
  • Antonym: to invent - to create something not previously existing (contrasted with telling what has already occurred)

Etymology

From re- (again) + tell.

2. VERB

/ riˈtɛl / (AmE), / riːˈtɛl / (BrE)
to tell (a story, event, or information) again, especially in a different way or to a different audience
to narrate again

Examples

She loves to retell stories from her childhood at family gatherings.
The teacher asked the students to retell the fairy tale in their own words.
He retold the incident to the police, making sure not to leave out any details.

Synset Examples

She kept reiterating her request

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repeat - to say or do something again
  • Synonym: recount - to tell someone about something; give an account of an event or experience

Etymology

From re- (again) + tell, dating from the late 16th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00960366-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ riˈtɛl / (AmE), / riˈtɛl / (BrE)
to present an event or story again, especially by adapting, embellishing, or making it into a work of fiction
tell a story anew, often as fiction

Examples

The author decided to retell the legend as a fantasy novel for young readers.
In her play, she retells historical events with a modern twist.
The movie retells a classic folktale, but adds magical creatures to the plot.

Synset Examples

The writer fictionalized the lives of his parents in his latest novel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recount - to tell someone about something, especially something you have experienced
  • Synonym: to fictionalize - to present a true story or event with invented elements, making it fiction

Etymology

From re- (again) + tell, Old English 'tellan' (to count, recount, tell).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01638718-v
Synset POS: v
