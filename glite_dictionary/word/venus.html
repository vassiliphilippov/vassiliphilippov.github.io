Venus

1. PROPN

/ ˈvinəs / (AmE), / ˈviːnəs / (BrE)
the second planet from the Sun, known for its brightness and often visible from Earth as the morning or evening star
second planet from the Sun

Examples

On clear nights, you can often see Venus shining brightly just after sunset.
Astronomers have long been fascinated by the thick clouds that cover Venus.
Because of its brilliance, Venus is sometimes called the 'evening star' or 'morning star'.

Synset Examples

before it was known that they were the same object the evening star was called Venus and the morning star was called Lucifer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Earth - the third planet from the Sun
  • Synonym: Mars - the fourth planet from the Sun

Etymology

From Latin 'Venus', the Roman goddess of love and beauty.

2. PROPN

/ ˈviːnəs / (AmE), / ˈviːnəs / (BrE)
the Roman goddess of love, beauty, and fertility, equivalent to the Greek Aphrodite.
Roman goddess of love and beauty

Examples

In ancient Roman mythology, Venus was revered as the goddess of love and beauty.
The statue depicted Venus emerging from the sea, much like Botticelli's famous painting.
Many temples were dedicated to Venus throughout the Roman Empire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aphrodite - Greek goddess of love and beauty

Etymology

From Latin Venus, the name of the Roman goddess.

3. NOUN

/ ˈviː.nəs / (AmE), / ˈviː.nəs / (BrE)
a genus of edible marine clams with thick oval shells, belonging to the family Veneridae
genus of edible marine clams

Examples

Several species within the genus Venus are prized for their culinary value.
The thick shells of Venus clams make them easy to identify on the seashore.
Marine biologists studied the distribution of Venus species along the coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clam - a type of bivalve mollusk, often edible

Etymology

From Latin 'Venus', the Roman goddess, used as a taxonomic genus name.
