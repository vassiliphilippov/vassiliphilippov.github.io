Cupressus

1. NOUN

/ kjuˈprɛsəs / (AmE), / kjuˈprɛsəs / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen coniferous trees in the cypress family (Cupressaceae), commonly known as cypresses.
genus of evergreen cypress trees

Examples

The ancient Cupressus trees in the cemetery have witnessed centuries of history.
Several species within the genus Cupressus are cultivated for their ornamental value.
Botanists recently classified a new species under the genus Cupressus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cypress - common name for trees of the genus Cupressus

Etymology

From Latin 'cupressus,' meaning 'cypress tree.'

2. NOUN

/ kuˈprɛsəs / (AmE), / kjuːˈprɛsəs / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen coniferous trees in the cypress family (Cupressaceae), commonly known as cypresses, native to warm temperate regions.
genus of evergreen cypress trees

Examples

The Cupressus genus includes the Monterey cypress, well known in California.
Members of the Cupressus genus are often planted as ornamental trees in gardens and parks.
Botanists are studying the natural distribution of Cupressus species across the Mediterranean region.

Etymology

From Latin 'cupressus' (cypress tree), itself from Greek 'kyparissos'.
