noctambulism

1. NOUN

/ nɑkˈtæmbjʊˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / nɒkˈtæmbjʊlɪzəm / (BrE)
the act or condition of walking during sleep; sleepwalking
the act of walking while asleep
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that noctambulism is a common sleep disorder in children.
Her episodes of noctambulism worried her family, as she would often wander outside at night.
Detailed studies of noctambulism have helped neuroscientists understand more about sleep cycles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleepwalking - the act or condition of walking while asleep

Etymology

From Latin 'noct-' (night) + 'ambulare' (to walk) + '-ism'.
