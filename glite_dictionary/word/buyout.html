buyout

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.aʊt / (BrE)
the acquisition of a company or a controlling share of its stock by purchasing a majority of ownership
acquisition of a company by purchase

Examples

The company faced a buyout after a larger competitor made an offer.
Private equity firms often arrange a buyout to gain full control of the business.
Employees were concerned about changes following the recent buyout by shareholders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: takeover - an act of assuming control of another company
