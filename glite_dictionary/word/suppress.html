to suppress

1. VERB

/ səˈprɛs / (AmE), / səˈprɛs / (BrE)
to put an end to something, especially by force or authority, often in an unjust or oppressive way
put down by force or authority

Examples

The regime tried to suppress all forms of political opposition.
Student protests were suppressed by the authorities with mass arrests.
The police brutally suppress any demonstration that challenges the government.

Synset Examples

The government oppresses political activists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to oppress - to keep someone in subservience and hardship, especially by unjust exercise of authority
  • Synonym: to repress - to subdue someone or something by force

Etymology

From Latin 'suppressus', past participle of 'supprimere' (to press down).

2. VERB

/ səˈprɛs / (AmE), / səˈprɛs / (BrE)
to put an end to or subdue something, especially by force or authority
put an end to by force or authority

Examples

The government moved quickly to suppress the rebellion before it could spread.
She struggled to suppress her laughter during the solemn ceremony.
The new regulations are designed to suppress illegal trading activities.

Synset Examples

suppress a nascent uprising
stamp down on littering
conquer one's desires

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to subdue - to bring under control, often by force
  • Synonym: to quell - to put an end to, typically by force
  • Antonym: to encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope (opposite in sense)

Etymology

From Latin suppressus, past participle of supprimere (to press down, to restrain).

3. VERB

/ səˈprɛs / (AmE), / səˈprɛs / (BrE)
to consciously restrain from showing or expressing an emotion, desire, impulse, or behavior
restrain from showing emotion or impulse

Examples

She tried to suppress her laughter during the serious meeting.
He struggled to suppress his excitement when he heard the good news.
It can be unhealthy to continually suppress your true feelings.

Synset Examples

suppress a smile
he let his anger bottle up until he exploded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restrain - to hold back or control an emotion or impulse
  • Antonym: express - to show or make known an emotion or feeling

Etymology

From Latin suppressus, past participle of supprimere, meaning 'to press down'.
