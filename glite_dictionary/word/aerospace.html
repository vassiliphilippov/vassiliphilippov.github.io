aerospace

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛroʊˌspeɪs / (AmE), / ˈeərəʊˌspeɪs / (BrE)
the Earth's atmosphere and the region beyond it, considered as a single expanse for aviation and spaceflight purposes
the atmosphere and outer space as a single area

Examples

The government increased funding for research in aerospace to support new satellite technology.
Jobs in aerospace often require a strong background in physics and engineering.
She dreams of working in the aerospace industry and helping design spacecraft.

Etymology

From aero- (relating to air or the atmosphere) + space (outer space), orig. 20th century technical term.
