hyperlipoidaemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərˌlɪpoʊaɪˈdiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəlɪpɔɪˈdiːmiə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by an abnormally high concentration of lipids in the blood.
abnormally high blood lipid levels
medical british

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with hyperlipoidaemia after reviewing her blood test results.
A diet low in saturated fats can help control hyperlipoidaemia in many patients.
Untreated hyperlipoidaemia increases the risk of heart disease significantly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyperlipidemia - Presence of excess lipids in the blood (American spelling)

Etymology

From hyper- (over, excessive) + lipo- (fat) + -aemia (condition of the blood), patterned after Greek roots.
