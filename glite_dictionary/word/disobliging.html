disobliging

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪs.əˈblaɪ.dʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈblɪdʒ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
deliberately unhelpful or unwilling to do favors for others; unaccommodating.
deliberately unhelpful; unaccommodating

Examples

The receptionist was rather disobliging, refusing to answer our questions politely.
His disobliging attitude made it difficult for the team to cooperate with him.
I found her response unnecessarily disobliging considering the situation.

Synset Examples

the action was not offensive to him but proved somewhat disobliging

Related Terms

  • Antonym: obliging - helpful and willing to do favors
  • Synonym: unhelpful - not providing assistance or support
  • Antonym: accommodating - willing to help or please someone

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'dis-' (not) to 'obliging' (helpful, accommodating).
