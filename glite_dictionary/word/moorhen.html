moorhen

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrˌhɛn / (AmE), / ˈmɔːhɛn / (BrE)
a small, mostly black waterbird of the rail family, with a red bill and long legs, found on ponds and lakes
small black waterbird

Examples

We spotted a moorhen swimming quietly among the reeds.
A moorhen built its nest near the edge of the lake.
The children watched as the moorhen searched for food along the pond.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gallinule - another name for a waterbird in the rail family

Etymology

From 'moor' (a tract of open wasteland) + 'hen' (female bird), referring to its habitat.
