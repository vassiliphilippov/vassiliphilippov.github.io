doodlebug

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːdəlˌbʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈduːd(ə)lˌbʌɡ / (BrE)
a small, makeshift motor vehicle, especially one that is homemade or constructed using spare parts
small, improvised motor vehicle

Examples

The boys spent the summer building a doodlebug from old car parts found at the junkyard.
He drove his homemade doodlebug to the county fair, attracting curious looks along the way.
Their doodlebug sputtered down the back roads, barely managing to keep pace with a bicycle.

2. NOUN

/ ˈduː.dəl.bʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈduː.dl̩.bʌɡ / (BrE)
the larva of certain insects, especially those of the antlion family, known for creating funnel-shaped traps in sandy soil
larva of certain insects (esp. antlion)

Examples

The doodlebug patiently waits at the bottom of its sand pit to catch passing ants.
As children, we spent hours watching doodlebugs build their tiny traps in the playground.
You can often spot a doodlebug by the distinctive patterns it leaves in dry, loose soil.

3. NOUN

/ ˈduːdəˌbʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈduːdəlˌbʌɡ / (BrE)
a small jet-propelled flying bomb, especially the V-1 missile used by Germany during World War II
jet-propelled flying bomb (WWII)

Examples

The first doodlebug attacks on London caused widespread panic in 1944.
People would listen for the distinctive buzzing of a doodlebug overhead.
The museum displays a restored doodlebug among other World War II artifacts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: V-1 - the first jet-powered flying bomb used by Germany in WWII

Etymology

Originally a popular nickname; 'doodle' (foolish) + 'bug', used to describe the V-1 bomb's odd appearance and flight.
