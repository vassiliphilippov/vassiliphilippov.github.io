Dicamptodontidae

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌkæmptəˈdɒntɪdiː / (AmE), / dɪˌkæmptəˈdɒntɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of large and small aquatic salamanders native to western North America, including the Pacific giant salamanders
a family of aquatic salamanders
medical

Examples

Recent studies have provided new insights into the evolutionary history of Dicamptodontidae.
The Dicamptodontidae are known for their predominantly aquatic lifestyle and robust bodies.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Dicamptodontidae family in a remote mountain stream.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus Dicamptodon + suffix -idae, used for zoological family names.

Examples

Members of the Dicamptodontidae family exhibit remarkable adaptations for an aquatic lifestyle.
