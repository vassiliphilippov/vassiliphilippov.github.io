Ameiurus

1. NOUN

/ əˈmaɪjərəs / (AmE), / əˈmaɪjʊrəs / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater catfish known as bullhead catfishes, native to North America.
a genus of North American bullhead catfishes

Examples

The Ameiurus genus includes several species of bullhead frequently found in North American rivers.
Biologists studied the habitat of Ameiurus to understand their role in freshwater ecosystems.
Some anglers prefer fishing for Ameiurus because of their tenacity and size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catfish - a type of ray-finned fish with whisker-like barbels

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'meion' (less) + 'oura' (tail), referring to the tail shape.
