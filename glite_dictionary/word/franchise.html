franchise

1. VERB

/ ˈfræn.tʃaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfræn.tʃaɪz / (BrE)
to grant a franchise to a person or company, allowing them the rights to operate or sell products under an established brand
grant a franchise or rights to operate

Examples

The company decided to franchise its successful restaurant model across the country.
They hope to franchise their cafe brand in several new cities next year.
After years of independent operation, the store was finally franchised by a national chain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: license - to give official permission to do something

Etymology

From Old French 'franchir' (to set free), from 'franc' (free).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02452407-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈfrænˌtʃaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfræn.tʃaɪz / (BrE)
a business that is authorized to sell or distribute a company's goods or services in a particular area under an agreement
business authorized to distribute goods for a company

Examples

The restaurant operates as a franchise of a national chain.
After years of success, she opened a second franchise in the neighboring city.
Many entrepreneurs choose to buy a franchise rather than start a new business from scratch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: branch - a local office or store of a company

Etymology

From Old French 'franchise' (freedom, privilege), from 'franc' (free).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08077029-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈfræn.tʃaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfræn.tʃaɪz / (BrE)
a statutory right or privilege granted by a government to a person or group, especially the right to vote in public elections
a right or privilege granted by government

Examples

Many countries extended the franchise to women in the early twentieth century.
After years of protest, the government finally granted the franchise to all adult citizens.
The movement for civil rights was focused on expanding the franchise and ending discriminatory laws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suffrage - the right to vote in political elections
  • Antonym: disenfranchisement - being deprived of the right to vote

Etymology

From Old French 'franchise' (freedom, exemption), from 'franc' (free).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05194714-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈfrænˌtʃaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfræn.tʃaɪz / (BrE)
an official authorization granted by a company to an individual or group allowing them to carry out specified commercial activities, such as selling the company's products or services in a particular area
authorization to sell company's goods/services

Examples

He opened a franchise of the popular coffee chain on Main Street.
The restaurant is operated as a franchise rather than a company-owned location.
Buying a franchise can be a less risky way to start a business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: license - official permission to do something, especially to sell goods or services

Etymology

From Old French *franchir* (to make free), from *franc* (free), later used for legal and commercial privileges.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06538802-n
Synset POS: n
