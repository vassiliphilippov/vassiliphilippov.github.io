strawwoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɔˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈstrɔːˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A woman who is used as a cover or front for some questionable or illegal activity, often so the real person can remain unidentified.
female cover for questionable activity

Examples

The company used a strawwoman to sign the documents and hide the true owners.
Investigators discovered that the property was actually purchased by a strawwoman acting on someone else's behalf.
He was accused of appointing a strawwoman to evade regulatory scrutiny.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strawman - a person used as a cover for questionable activity

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'strawman', combining 'straw' (as in a dummy or figurehead) and 'woman'.
