confederation

1. NOUN

/ kənˌfɛdəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌfɛdəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the state of being united in an alliance or league, especially of groups or nations
state of being allied; alliance

Examples

The countries created a confederation to strengthen their collective security against external threats.
The spirit of confederation among the tribes allowed them to resist their enemies more effectively.
Their confederation was fragile, breaking apart as soon as their common interests diverged.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alliance - a union formed for mutual benefit
  • Synonym: federation - a group of states with a central government but independence in internal affairs
  • Antonym: disunion - the state of being separated or not united

Etymology

From Latin 'confoederatio' (alliance), from 'confoederare' (to ally together).

2. NOUN

/ kənˌfɛdəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌfɛdəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
A union of political organizations, especially a group of states or nations that retain independence but cooperate for common purposes.
union of political organizations

Examples

Switzerland is often described as a confederation of cantons.
The states chose to form a loose confederation rather than a strong central government.
Historical documents outline the structure of the confederation established in 1867.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: federation - a group of states with a central government but independence in internal affairs
  • Synonym: alliance - a union or association formed for mutual benefit, especially between countries or organizations
  • Antonym: disunion - the breaking up or dissolution of a union

Etymology

From Latin 'confoederatio(n-)', from 'confoederare' (to ally together).

3. NOUN

/ kənˌfɛdəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌfɛdəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of forming an alliance or union between groups or states for a common purpose
the act of forming an alliance or union

Examples

The successful confederation of several tribes led to increased security for the region.
After lengthy negotiations, the countries agreed on the formal confederation of their states.
The confederation of labor unions strengthened their bargaining position.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: federation - the act of uniting in a political or social federation
  • Antonym: disunion - the breaking up of an alliance or union

Etymology

From Latin confederatio (alliance), from con- (together) + foedus (league, treaty).
