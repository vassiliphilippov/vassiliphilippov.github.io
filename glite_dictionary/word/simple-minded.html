simple-minded

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪmpəlˌmaɪndɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəlˌmaɪndɪd / (BrE)
lacking intelligence, subtlety, or insight; naive or unsophisticated.
lacking subtlety or intelligence

Examples

His simple-minded approach to a complex issue missed many important details.
She dismissed the theory as a simple-minded explanation for a complicated situation.
The film's plot was criticized for being too simple-minded and predictable.

Synset Examples

a simple-minded argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naive - lacking experience, wisdom, or judgement
  • Antonym: sophisticated - having or showing a lot of experience and knowledge about the world

Etymology

From 'simple' (not complex, naive) + 'minded' (having a specified type of mind).

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɪmpəlˈmaɪndɪd / (AmE), / ˌsɪmpəlˈmaɪndɪd / (BrE)
lacking intelligence, sophistication, or subtlety in thought or understanding; showing little mental capacity or insight.
lacking intelligence or subtlety

Examples

Despite his age, the boy remained simple-minded and believed every story he was told.
The plan was dismissed as simple-minded and failed to address the real issues.
She spoke in a simple-minded way, ignoring the complexities of the situation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naive - showing a lack of experience, wisdom, or judgement
  • Synonym: gullible - easily persuaded to believe something; credulous
  • Antonym: clever - quick to understand, learn, and devise or apply ideas; intelligent

Etymology

From 'simple' (plain, lacking complexity) + 'minded' (having a specified kind of mind).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
