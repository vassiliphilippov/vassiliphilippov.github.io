Theobroma cacao

1. NOUN

/ θiˈoʊbɹəmə kəˈkaʊ / (AmE), / θiːˈəʊbrəmə kəˈkaʊ / (BrE)
a tropical American evergreen tree whose seeds (cacao beans) are used to make cocoa and chocolate
tree producing cacao beans

Examples

The beans of the Theobroma cacao are the primary source of chocolate.
Farmers in West Africa now cultivate Theobroma cacao on a large scale for export.
The Theobroma cacao thrives best in warm, humid climates near the equator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cacao - bean from Theobroma cacao, used for cocoa and chocolate
  • Degree Lower: cocoa - powder made from roasted and ground cacao seeds

Etymology

From Greek theos (god) + broma (food), and Nahuatl cacahuatl (cacao).
