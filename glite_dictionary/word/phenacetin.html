phenacetin

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnæsətɪn / (AmE), / fɛˈnæsɪtɪn / (BrE)
A white crystalline compound formerly used as an analgesic and antipyretic in medicine.
analgesic and antipyretic compound
medical

Examples

The use of phenacetin was discontinued in many countries due to its harmful side effects.
Researchers studied the effects of phenacetin on fever reduction in clinical trials.
He developed kidney problems linked to prolonged phenacetin consumption.

Etymology

From Greek 'phaino-' (show, appear) and 'acetic', relating to its chemical composition, plus '-in'.
