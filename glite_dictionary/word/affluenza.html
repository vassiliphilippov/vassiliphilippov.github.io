affluenza

1. NOUN

/ ˌæfluˈɛnzə / (AmE), / ˌæfluˈenzə / (BrE)
A psychological or social condition resulting from extreme wealth, characterized by a lack of understanding of the consequences of one's actions and a sense of irresponsibility due to financial privilege.
condition of irresponsibility from extreme wealth

Examples

Some critics argue that affluenza is used as an excuse for irresponsible behavior by the wealthy.
The teenager's lawyers claimed he suffered from affluenza and could not distinguish right from wrong.
In her book, the author examines the effects of affluenza on family relationships and society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affluence - abundant wealth; the state of having a great deal of money

Etymology

Blend of 'affluence' and 'influenza', coined in the late 20th century.
