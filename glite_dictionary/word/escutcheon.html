escutcheon

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈskʌtʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈskʌtʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
(Nautical) A flat plate on a ship's stern displaying the vessel's name.
nameplate on a ship's stern

Examples

The shipbuilder carefully polished the escutcheon before unveiling the vessel.
Her name gleamed in gold letters across the escutcheon at the stern.
After the restoration, the old schooner's escutcheon was once again readable.

Etymology

From Old French 'escuchon' (shield), from Latin 'scutum' (shield).

a shield or emblem bearing a coat of arms, especially as used in heraldry
shield with a coat of arms

Examples

The family escutcheon was proudly displayed above the fireplace.
The museum has a rare collection of medieval escutcheons from various noble families.
He studied the escutcheon to learn more about the history of the house.

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ ɪˈskʌtʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈskʌtʃən / (BrE)
A flat protective plate, usually around a keyhole or door handle, to prevent marking or soiling of the surrounding surface.
protective plate around keyhole or handle

Examples

The brass escutcheon around the keyhole protected the door from scratches.
After years of use, the escutcheon was tarnished but still served its purpose.
He replaced the old escutcheon with a modern, stainless steel one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plate - a flat, thin piece of material covering or protecting something

Etymology

From Old French escusson (shield), from Latin scutio (shield).
