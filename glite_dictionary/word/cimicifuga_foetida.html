Cimicifuga foetida

1. NOUN

/ sɪˌmɪsɪˈfjuɡə fiˈɛtɪdə / (AmE), / sɪˌmɪsɪˈfjuːɡə fiˈɛtɪdə / (BrE)
A perennial plant of Siberia and eastern Asia that produces ill-smelling greenish-white flowers; also known as Siberian bugbane.
species of bugbane plant with ill-smelling flowers

Examples

Cimicifuga foetida is often found growing wild in forests across Siberia.
Gardeners are sometimes deterred by the smell produced when Cimicifuga foetida blooms.
Researchers study Cimicifuga foetida for its potential use in herbal medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bugbane - common name for plants in the genus Cimicifuga

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cimicifuga' meaning 'bug repeller', 'foetida' meaning 'foul-smelling'.
