anxiolytic

1. NOUN

/ ˌæŋziəˈlɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌæŋziəˈlɪtɪk / (BrE)
A drug or substance that relieves anxiety and reduces tension or irritability.
drug relieving anxiety
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an anxiolytic to help her manage her panic attacks.
Some anxiolytic medications can cause drowsiness as a side effect.
He preferred natural remedies over pharmaceutical anxiolytics for his mild anxiety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tranquilizer - a drug used to reduce tension or anxiety

Etymology

From Greek 'an-' (not) + 'xios' (anxious) + '-lytic' (loosening, dissolving).

2. ADJ

/ ˌæŋziəˈlɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌaŋziəˈlɪtɪk / (BrE)
(of a drug or other substance) reducing or relieving anxiety.
reducing anxiety (medical term)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an anxiolytic medication to help manage her panic attacks.
Some herbal teas are said to have mild anxiolytic effects on the nervous system.
He was interested in studying the anxiolytic properties of the new compound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tranquilizer - drug used to reduce tension or anxiety
  • Antonym: anxiogenic - causing anxiety

Etymology

From Greek 'an-' (without) + 'anxiety' + '-lytic' (able to loosen or dissolve).
