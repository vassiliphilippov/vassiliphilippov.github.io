glossoptosis

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɑːs.ɑpˈtoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ɡlɒs.ɒpˈtəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition in which the tongue is abnormally displaced downward or backward, potentially obstructing the airway.
abnormal downward/backward tongue displacement
medical

Examples

Infants with glossoptosis may have difficulty breathing due to airway obstruction.
The doctor diagnosed the baby with glossoptosis after noticing the abnormal position of his tongue.
Treatment for glossoptosis often involves monitoring and, in severe cases, surgical intervention.

Etymology

From Greek 'glōssa' (tongue) + 'ptōsis' (falling).
