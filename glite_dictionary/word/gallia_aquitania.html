Gallia Aquitania

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡæliə ˌækwɪˈteɪniə / (AmE), / ˈɡaliə ˌakwɪˈteɪnɪə / (BrE)
A historical region of southwestern France, located between Bordeaux and the Pyrenees, known as Aquitania under Roman rule.
historical region in southwestern France

Examples

The Romans conquered Gallia Aquitania during the campaigns of Caesar.
Archaeologists have uncovered numerous artifacts in the region once known as Gallia Aquitania.
The boundaries of Gallia Aquitania shifted several times throughout ancient history.

Etymology

From Latin Gallia Aquitania, meaning 'Aquitanian Gaul'.
