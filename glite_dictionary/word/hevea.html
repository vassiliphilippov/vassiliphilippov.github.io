Hevea

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːviə / (AmE), / ˈhiːvɪə / (BrE)
a genus of South American trees whose sap is the source of natural rubber
genus of trees yielding latex

Examples

The Hevea genus is best known for producing the latex used in natural rubber.
Agricultural scientists are experimenting with different species of Hevea to improve latex yield.
Many plantations in Southeast Asia grow Hevea trees for commercial rubber production.

Synset Examples

rubber trees are usually cultivated in plantations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rubber tree - tree (Hevea brasiliensis) cultivated for its latex, source of rubber

Etymology

From Latinized botanical naming, originally from Brazilian Portuguese 'heve', the native name for the rubber plant.
