blae

1. ADJ

/ bleɪ / (AmE), / bleɪ / (BrE)
(chiefly Scottish or literary) Having a bluish-black or grey-blue color.
bluish-black or grey-blue color (esp. literary/Scottish)

Examples

The distant hills looked blae in the early morning mist.
His blae eyes stared out across the moorland, matching the stormy sky above.
Heather grew in wild profusion, turning the slopes a blae shade each autumn.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: blue - a color between green and violet in the visible spectrum
  • Degree Lower: navy blue - a very dark shade of blue
  • Degree Higher: black - the darkest color; absence of light

Etymology

From Old Norse 'blár' (blue, dark blue, livid).
