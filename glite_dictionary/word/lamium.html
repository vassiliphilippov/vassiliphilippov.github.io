Lamium

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪmiəm / (AmE), / ˈleɪmɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of Old World herbs in the mint family, including the dead nettles and henbits.
genus of herbs (dead nettles, henbits)

Examples

Lamium species are often grown as ground cover in shaded gardens.
The purple flowers of Lamium attract many pollinators in spring.
Gardeners appreciate Lamium for its ability to thrive in difficult soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dead nettle - common name for plants of the Lamium genus, resembling nettles but non-stinging

Etymology

From Latin Lamium, from Greek lamion, a kind of plant.

2. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.mi.əm / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.mi.əm / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous plants native to the Old World, commonly known as dead nettles and henbits.
genus of plants: dead nettles, henbits

Examples

The leaves of Lamium are often used as ground cover in gardens.
Several species within the Lamium genus are valued for their distinctive flowers.
Wild Lamium can frequently be spotted in woods and grassy meadows across Europe.

Etymology

From Latin 'lamium', originally the name for 'dead nettle'.
