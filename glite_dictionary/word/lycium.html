Lycium

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪsiəm / (AmE), / ˈlɪsiəm / (BrE)
A genus of mostly spiny, deciduous or evergreen shrubs found in temperate and subtropical regions, including species commonly known as boxthorn and wolfberry.
a genus of spiny shrubs (boxthorn, wolfberry)

Examples

Several species of Lycium are cultivated for their edible berries and medicinal properties.
The garden is home to a thriving Lycium hedge that attracts birds.
Researchers studied the drought resistance of different Lycium varieties in subtropical climates.

Etymology

From Latin Lycium, from Greek Lykion, a plant from Lycia (a region in Asia Minor).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪsiəm / (AmE), / ˈlɪsɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous and evergreen spiny shrubs found in temperate and subtropical regions, including species such as the goji berry.
genus of spiny shrubs; includes goji berry

Examples

The berries of some Lycium species are used in traditional medicine.
Many gardeners plant Lycium shrubs for their ornamental value and resilience.
Several birds feed on the fruits produced by Lycium plants in the wild.

Etymology

From Latin *lycium*, from Greek *lykion*, a plant from Lycia (a region in Asia Minor).
