pentamerous

1. ADJ

/ pɛnˈtæmərəs / (AmE), / pɛnˈtamərəs / (BrE)
(of a plant or animal part) divided into five segments or parts; especially, having each floral whorl composed of five or a multiple of five members.
divided into five parts (biology)

Examples

Many species in the rose family have pentamerous flowers with five petals each.
The structure of a typical sea star is pentamerous, displaying five arms radiating from the center.
The botanist noted that the blossom's pentamerous arrangement was typical for its genus.

Synset Examples

pentamerous flowers

Etymology

From Greek 'penta-' (five) + 'meros' (part).
