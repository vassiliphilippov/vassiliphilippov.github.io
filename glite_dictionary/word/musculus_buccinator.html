musculus buccinator

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌsˈkjuːləs bʌkˈsɪneɪtər / (AmE), / ˌmʌsˈkjuːləs bʌkˈsɪneɪtə / (BrE)
A muscle of the cheek that flattens the cheek and retracts the angle of the mouth, important in actions such as chewing and blowing.
cheek muscle used in chewing and blowing
medical

Examples

During surgery, the musculus buccinator is carefully preserved to maintain facial expression.
The musculus buccinator is essential for blowing air forcefully through the mouth.
Damage to the musculus buccinator may result in difficulty keeping food between the teeth while chewing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buccinator - the same cheek muscle, commonly referred to by this shorter term

Etymology

From Latin 'musculus' (muscle) + 'buccinator' (trumpeter, from 'bucca', cheek).
