haemolysin

1. NOUN

/ hiːˈmɑlɪsɪn / (AmE), / hiːˈmɒlɪsɪn / (BrE)
A substance, often a toxin or antibody, that causes the destruction of red blood cells and the release of hemoglobin.
substance causing red blood cell destruction
medical british

Examples

The bacteria produced a haemolysin that destroyed the host's red blood cells.
Researchers are studying how haemolysin contributes to the severity of certain infections.
Antibodies can sometimes act as a haemolysin in autoimmune diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemolysin - substance causing red blood cell destruction (American spelling)

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) + 'lysis' (loosening/destruction).
