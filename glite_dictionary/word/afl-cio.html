AFL-CIO

1. PROPN

/ ˌeɪ ɛf ɛl si aɪ oʊ / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ɛf ɛl siː aɪ əʊ / (BrE)
the largest federation of labor unions in the United States, formed in 1955 by the merger of the American Federation of Labor and the Congress of Industrial Organizations
major US labor union federation
american

Examples

The AFL-CIO played a major role in advocating for workers' rights during the 20th century.
She attended an AFL-CIO conference to learn more about labor organizing.
Many unions across different industries are affiliated with the AFL-CIO.

Etymology

Acronym from American Federation of Labor and Congress of Industrial Organizations.
