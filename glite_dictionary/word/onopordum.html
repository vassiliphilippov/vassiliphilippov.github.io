Onopordum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːnoʊˈpɔːrdəm / (AmE), / ˌɒnəˈpɔːdəm / (BrE)
A genus of Eurasian thistle-like herbs in the family Compositae, known for their prickly leaves and large purplish flower heads.
genus of Eurasian thistle-like herbs

Examples

The Onopordum plants thrive in dry, open grasslands across Europe and western Asia.
One common species of Onopordum is known for its striking purple flowers and spiny leaves.
Botanists classified the invasive cotton thistle under the genus Onopordum.

Etymology

From Greek 'onopordon', meaning 'ass's thistle', referring to the plant's spiny character.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːnəˈpɔːrdəm / (AmE), / ˌɒnəˈpɔːdəm / (BrE)
A genus of Eurasian thistles in the family Asteraceae, known for their prickly leaves and large, purplish flowers.
a genus of Eurasian thistles

Examples

The Onopordum is easily recognized by its massive, spiny stems and vivid purple flowers.
Several species within the Onopordum genus are considered invasive weeds in Australia.
Botanists are studying the medicinal properties of Onopordum extracts.

Etymology

From Greek onos (donkey) + porde (fart), referring to an old belief about donkeys eating the plant.
