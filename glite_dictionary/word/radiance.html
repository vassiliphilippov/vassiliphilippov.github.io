radiance

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdiəns / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəns / (BrE)
an attractive quality of brightness on someone's face or in their appearance, showing good health and happiness
brightness of health and happiness in appearance

Examples

Her face was lit with a radiance that made everyone around her smile.
After receiving the good news, a radiance spread across his features.
The bride's radiance was evident as she walked down the aisle.

Synset Examples

the radiance of her countenance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glow - a warm, healthy brightness in the face or skin

Etymology

From Latin 'radiantia', from 'radiare' (to shine).

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪ.di.əns / (AmE), / ˈreɪ.di.əns / (BrE)
the quality of being bright or emitting rays of light; brilliant or glowing brightness
bright, glowing quality; brilliant light

Examples

The morning sun filled the room with a warm radiance.
Her face glowed with a radiance that brightened the entire gathering.
The radiance of the fireworks lit up the night sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brilliance - intense brightness or exceptional radiance
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of brightness or radiance

Etymology

From Latin 'radiare' (to shine, emit rays) via Old French 'radiance'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04961131-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈreɪ.di.əns / (AmE), / ˈreɪ.di.əns / (BrE)
the amount of electromagnetic radiation leaving or arriving at a point on a surface, typically measured per unit area and per unit solid angle
amount of electromagnetic radiation at a surface point

Examples

The radiance of the sun at Earth's surface is affected by atmospheric conditions.
Astronomers measure the radiance of distant stars to determine their energy output.
An increase in thermal radiance indicates a hotter surface temperature.

Etymology

From Latin 'radiantia', from 'radiare' (to emit rays) + '-ance' suffix.
