astronomy

1. NOUN

/ əˈstrɑː.nə.mi / (AmE), / əˈstrɒn.ə.mi / (BrE)
The scientific study of celestial objects, space, and the universe as a whole.
science of celestial objects and the universe

Examples

She developed a passion for astronomy after visiting the planetarium.
Astronomy has helped us understand the origins of the universe.
He bought a telescope to pursue his interest in astronomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astrophysics - the branch of astronomy concerned with the physical properties of celestial bodies
  • Antonym: astrology - the study of the movements and relative positions of celestial bodies interpreted as having an influence on human affairs

Etymology

From Greek *astronomía* (star arrangement), from *astron* (star) and *nomos* (law).
