band-aid

1. NOUN

/ ˈbænd ˌeɪd / (AmE), / ˈbænd eɪd / (BrE)
a quick, temporary solution to a problem that does not address the underlying cause
temporary or superficial fix

Examples

The new tax policy is just a band-aid on a much deeper economic problem.
They offered a band-aid solution instead of fixing the issue properly.
Raising tuition slightly may be a band-aid, but it won't solve the university's budget crisis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quick fix - an easy but temporary solution to a problem
  • Antonym: permanent solution - a lasting or definitive resolution to a problem

Etymology

From the trademark Band-Aid, a brand of adhesive bandage by Johnson & Johnson.
