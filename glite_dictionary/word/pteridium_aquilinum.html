Pteridium aquilinum

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrɪˈdɪdiəm ˌækwɪˈlaɪnəm / (AmE), / ˌtɛrɪˈdɪdɪəm ˌakwɪˈlaɪnəm / (BrE)
A large, coarse fern known as bracken, found across temperate and tropical regions worldwide.
widespread bracken fern species

Examples

You can frequently find Pteridium aquilinum growing along woodland edges throughout Europe and North America.
The rapid spread of Pteridium aquilinum can present a challenge for farmers and land managers.
Many animals avoid Pteridium aquilinum because it contains toxic compounds that can be harmful if ingested.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bracken - Large fern with triangular fronds, widespread

Etymology

From Greek pteridion (little fern), and Latin aquilinus (eagle-like).
