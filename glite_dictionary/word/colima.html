Colima

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈliːmə / (AmE), / kəˈliːmə / (BrE)
an active volcano in southwestern Mexico, also known as Volcán de Colima, one of the most active volcanoes in North America
active volcano in southwestern Mexico

Examples

The eruption of Colima was visible for miles around, lighting up the night sky.
Scientists continually monitor Colima due to its frequent volcanic activity.
Many tourists travel to Jalisco for a chance to see Colima from a safe distance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volcano - a mountain or hill with a crater or vent through which lava, rock fragments, hot vapor, and gas are being or have been erupted from the earth's crust

Etymology

From the Nahuatl 'Coliman', possibly meaning 'place in the hand of the grandfather' or 'place of the old god'.
