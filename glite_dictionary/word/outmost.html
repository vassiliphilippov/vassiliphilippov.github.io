outmost

1. ADJ

/ ˈaʊtˌmoʊst / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.məʊst / (BrE)
Situated at the farthest possible point from the center or from the inside; farthest outward.
farthest from the center

Examples

The outmost layer of the atmosphere protects the planet from harmful radiation.
Only the outmost petals of the flower had begun to wilt.
She could see the outmost islands on the horizon under the setting sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outermost - farthest outward or at the greatest distance from the center

Etymology

Formed from 'out' + 'most,' patterned after similar superlatives like 'utmost.'
