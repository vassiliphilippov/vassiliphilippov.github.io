Verbascum thapsus

1. NOUN

/ vɚˈbæskəm ˈθæpsəs / (AmE), / vəˈbæskəm ˈθæpsəs / (BrE)
A tall, woolly plant with densely packed yellow flowers, commonly known as great mullein, used historically as torches by ancient Greeks and Romans.
a tall, woolly plant (great mullein) with yellow flowers

Examples

The roadside was lined with tall Verbascum thapsus swaying gently in the breeze.
Gardeners often admire Verbascum thapsus for its striking yellow flower spikes.
In ancient times, Verbascum thapsus was used as a torch during special ceremonies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: great mullein - common name for Verbascum thapsus, a tall, woolly plant with yellow flowers

Etymology

From Latin: 'Verbascum' is the genus name, and 'thapsus' derives from the ancient Greek city Thapsos.
