to skitter

1. VERB

/ ˈskɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈskɪtə / (BrE)
to move lightly and quickly, often with a skipping or gliding motion over a surface
move lightly and quickly over a surface

Examples

The insect skittered across the surface of the pond, barely making a ripple.
Autumn leaves skitter along the pavement when the wind picks up.
The mouse skittered under the furniture as soon as the lights turned on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scurry - to move quickly with short, light steps
  • Synonym: slide - to move smoothly along a surface while maintaining continuous contact

Etymology

Probably an alteration of 'skit' + '-er', imitating the sound and motion.

2. VERB

/ ˈskɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈskɪtə / (BrE)
To move a fishing hook or lure quickly and lightly across the surface of water by twitching the fishing line.
twitch a fishing hook across water

Examples

He expertly skittered the lure across the surface to attract the fish.
Try to skitter the hook gently so it looks like an insect.
The angler skittered his bait along the water, hoping for a strike.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01451510-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈskɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈskɪtə / (BrE)
to cause something to move lightly and quickly or to skip over a surface
cause to move lightly or skip over a surface

Examples

She tried to skitter a flat stone across the lake's surface.
The boy laughed as he skittered pebbles down the frozen stream.
The wind caused dry leaves to skitter across the sidewalk.

Synset Examples

Skip a stone across the pond

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skip - to move by bouncing or hopping lightly over a surface

Etymology

Probably imitative of a light, quick movement; compare Middle English 'skiteren' (to glide).

4. VERB

/ ˈskɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈskɪtə / (BrE)
to move lightly and quickly, often with a jittery or nervous motion
move quickly and lightly; jitter

Examples

The leaves began to skitter across the pavement as the wind picked up.
A startled mouse skittered into the shadows when the lights turned on.
Raindrops skitter across the windowpane during the storm.

Synset Examples

so terrified by the extraordinary ebbing of the sea that they scurried to higher ground

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scurry - to move quickly with short, rapid steps
  • Synonym: dart - to move swiftly and suddenly
  • Antonym: amble - to walk at a slow, relaxed pace

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *skiteren* (to skip, leap), of Scandinavian origin.

Source: WordNet
