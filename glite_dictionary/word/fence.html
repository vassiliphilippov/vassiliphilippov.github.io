fence

1. NOUN

/ fɛns / (AmE), / fɛns / (BrE)
A structure serving as an enclosure, barrier, or boundary, typically made of wood or wire, erected to surround or divide an area of land.
structure that encloses or divides an area

Examples

The old wooden fence at the back of our house needs repair.
After buying the dog, they built a fence around their yard.
A white picket fence surrounded the charming cottage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wall - a solid vertical structure that encloses or divides an area
  • Synonym: hedge - a fence or boundary formed by closely growing bushes or shrubs

Etymology

From Middle English 'fens', shortening of 'defens' (defense), from Old French.

4. NOUN

/ fɛns / (AmE), / fɛns / (BrE)
a person who buys and sells stolen goods, acting as an intermediary between thieves and buyers
one who deals in stolen goods

Examples

The police arrested the fence who had bought jewelry from the burglar.
He acted as a fence for the stolen electronics, quickly selling them to unsuspecting buyers.
It's hard for thieves to make money without a reliable fence to move the goods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: receiver - a person who receives stolen goods

Etymology

Perhaps from the shortening of 'defence', notionally someone who 'protects' or helps thieves dispose of goods.

5. VERB

/ fɛns / (AmE), / fɛns / (BrE)
to buy, receive, or deal in stolen goods in order to sell them to others
deal in stolen goods

Examples

He was arrested after trying to fence a stash of stolen jewelry.
The gang used a legitimate business as a front to fence electronics.
She didn't steal the cars herself, but she did fence them for a profit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traffick - deal illegally in goods, especially stolen goods or drugs

Etymology

Originally criminal slang from the 17th century, probably by association with a physical fence as something that separates or bars passage.

6. VERB

/ fɛns / (AmE), / fɛns / (BrE)
to engage in a sword fight using foils, épées, or sabres, especially as a sport; to practise the art of fencing
fight with swords (sport)

Examples

They learned to fence at a young age, mastering the basics of offense and defense.
During the tournament, she managed to fence her way to the finals with agility and strategy.
He prefers to fence with the épée rather than the foil, finding the heavier blade more challenging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duel - a formal fight with weapons, especially between two people

Etymology

From Old French 'defenser' (to defend), later shorted and specialized in meaning.
