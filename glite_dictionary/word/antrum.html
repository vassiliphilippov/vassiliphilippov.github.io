antrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.trəm / (AmE), / ˈæn.trəm / (BrE)
a natural cavity or hollow space within a bone, especially in the body
natural cavity in a bone
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the patient's maxillary antrum for signs of infection.
A CT scan revealed a fluid-filled antrum in the temporal bone.
Sinusitis can sometimes affect the antrum, leading to discomfort and swelling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sinus - a cavity or hollow within a bone, particularly those in the skull

Etymology

From Latin antrum (cave, cavity), from Greek antron (cave).
