sand

1. VERB

/ sænd / (AmE), / sænd / (BrE)
to smooth or clean a surface by rubbing it with sandpaper or an abrasive material
to smooth with sandpaper

Examples

You should sand the table before applying varnish.
She sanded the rough edges to make the shelf smoother.
He spent the afternoon sanding the old paint off the window frames.

Synset Examples

sandpaper the wooden surface

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandpaper - to smooth something using sandpaper

2. NOUN

/ sænd / (AmE), / sænd / (BrE)
courage, fortitude, or determination; grit.
courage or determination (archaic usage)
archaic american

Examples

She showed plenty of sand when she stood up to her critics.
The old tales say that those who made it out alive were the ones with real sand.
They doubted he had the sand to finish the race, but he proved them wrong.

Synset Examples

he didn't have the guts to try it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grit - courage and resolve; strength of character
  • Synonym: determination - the quality of being determined; firmness of purpose
  • Antonym: cowardice - lack of courage to face danger or difficulty

Etymology

Figurative use from sand (grains), probably alluding to the firmness or grittiness of sand. Mainly U.S. slang, 19th century.

3. NOUN

/ sænd / (AmE), / sænd / (BrE)
A loose granular material consisting of finely divided rock and mineral particles, often found on beaches, deserts, and riverbeds.
granular material of small rock particles

Examples

The children built a castle out of sand at the beach.
Fine sand covered the floor of the aquarium.
Wind can shift huge amounts of sand across desert landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gravel - small stones and pebbles, coarser than sand
  • Degree Lower: silt - fine sediment finer than sand

Etymology

Old English *sand*, from Proto-Germanic *sandaz*, meaning 'sand'.
