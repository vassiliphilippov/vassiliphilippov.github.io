colouring

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkʌl.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
a substance added to food or drink to give it colour
substance that adds colour to food
british

Examples

The manufacturer uses only natural colouring in its candies.
Some parents prefer to avoid artificial colouring in their children's food.
The ingredient list includes a red colouring derived from beetroot.

Synset Examples

food color made from vegetable dyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: food coloring - substance used to give color to food (American spelling)

Etymology

From the verb 'colour' + suffix '-ing'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkʌl(ə)rɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of changing the color of something, especially by applying dyes, paints, or other substances
the act of changing color
british

Examples

The colouring of the fabric took several hours to complete.
Children often enjoy the colouring of pictures in their activity books.
She specializes in the colouring of intricate designs for textiles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coloring - the act or process of changing the color of something (American spelling)

Etymology

From the verb 'colour' + '-ing', British spelling of 'coloring'; 'colour' from Old French 'colour', from Latin 'color'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkʌl.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the appearance or visual quality of an object as determined by the way it reflects, emits, or transmits light; the color or combination of colors of something
visual quality produced by reflected or emitted light
british

Examples

The bird’s bright colouring helps it attract a mate.
She admired the subtle colouring of the autumn leaves.
Exposure to sunlight can cause a change in your skin’s colouring.

Synset Examples

a white color is made up of many different wavelengths of light

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color - the property possessed by an object of producing different sensations on the eye as a result of the way it reflects or emits light

Etymology

From the verb 'colour' + -ing, derived from Old French 'colour', from Latin 'color' (meaning 'covering, colour')
