Ibsenian

1. ADJ

/ ɪbˈsɛniən / (AmE), / ɪbˈsiːniən / (BrE)
Of or relating to Henrik Ibsen, his works, or his characteristic style, especially in drama.
pertaining to Henrik Ibsen or his works

Examples

Her latest play has a distinctly Ibsenian sense of psychological depth and social critique.
The Ibsenian approach to character development has influenced many modern dramatists.
Critics praised the film’s Ibsenian treatment of family dynamics and hidden tensions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ibsenesque - in the manner or style characteristic of Henrik Ibsen

Etymology

Formed from the name Henrik Ibsen (1828–1906), Norwegian playwright, plus the suffix -ian.
