Nicaean

1. ADJ

/ naɪˈsiː.ən / (AmE), / naɪˈsiː.ən / (BrE)
relating to the ancient city of Nicaea in Asia Minor, especially in reference to its historical or religious significance
of or relating to Nicaea, ancient city

Examples

The Nicaean Council played a crucial role in early Christian history.
Ancient documents from Nicaean scholars provide insight into Byzantine society.
He gave a lecture on the Nicaean influence on early church doctrine.

Etymology

From Latin Nicaenus, from Greek Nikaia (Νίκαια), the name of the city.
