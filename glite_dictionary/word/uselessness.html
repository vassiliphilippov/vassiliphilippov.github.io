uselessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈjusləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈjuːsləsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being of no practical use or value
lack of practical use or value

Examples

He was frustrated by the uselessness of the broken device.
They quickly realized the uselessness of arguing with him.
The report highlighted the uselessness of outdated policies in the modern world.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: usefulness - the quality of being useful or having practical value
  • Synonym: ineffectiveness - lack of efficacy or power to produce a result
  • Synonym: worthlessness - the quality of having no worth or value

Etymology

From 'useless' (without use) + '-ness' (forming nouns of quality or state).
