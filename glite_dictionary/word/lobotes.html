Lobotes

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈboʊtiːz / (AmE), / ləˈbəʊtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the family Lobotidae, known as tripletails, found in tropical and subtropical waters.
genus of fish (tripletails)

Examples

The genus Lobotes includes species commonly known as tripletail.
Marine biologists study Lobotes to better understand their migratory patterns.
The distinctive appearance of Lobotes makes them easy to identify among fish species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tripletail - a common name for fish in the genus Lobotes

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from Greek 'lobos' (lobe), referring to the shape of the fishes' fins.
