teargas

1. VERB

/ ˈtɪrˌɡæs / (AmE), / ˈtɪəˌɡas / (BrE)
to attack or disperse people by releasing teargas
to attack or disperse with teargas

Examples

Police threatened to teargas the protesters if they did not disperse.
Authorities teargassed the crowd during the demonstration last night.
The officers were accused of teargassing peaceful marchers.

Synset Examples

The students were teargassed during the riot

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gas - attack with gas (general sense)

Etymology

From 'tear gas', a compound noun formed from 'tear' (as in tears from eyes) and 'gas'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01127984-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈtiːˌɡæs / (AmE), / ˈtɪəˌɡas / (BrE)
A gas that causes irritation to the eyes and tears, used by police to disperse crowds.
gas used to disperse crowds

Examples

Police used teargas to break up the demonstration.
Exposure to teargas caused many people in the crowd to cough and rub their eyes.
Protesters covered their faces to protect themselves from teargas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pepper spray - an irritant spray used for self-defense and crowd control

Etymology

From 'tear' + 'gas'; first used in early 20th century to describe gases causing eye irritation.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15092444-n
Synset POS: n
