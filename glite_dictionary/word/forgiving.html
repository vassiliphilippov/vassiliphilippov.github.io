forgiving

1. ADJ

/ fərˈɡɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / fəˈɡɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
ready or willing to forgive others and show mercy, even after being wronged or hurt.
inclined to forgive

Examples

She has a truly forgiving heart, always ready to let go of grudges.
His forgiving nature made it easy for people to trust him again.
Despite their argument, she gave him a forgiving smile.

Synset Examples

a kindly forgiving nature
a forgiving embrace to the naughty child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merciful - showing mercy; compassionate or forgiving
  • Antonym: resentful - holding onto anger or unwilling to forgive

Etymology

From English verb 'forgive' + suffix '-ing'.

2. ADJ

/ fərˈɡɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / fəˈɡɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
Willing to forgive or inclined to pardon others for mistakes or wrongdoings.
inclined to forgive

Examples

He has a forgiving nature and rarely holds grudges against anyone.
Her forgiving attitude helped mend their relationship after the argument.
This is a forgiving community, quick to accept apologies and move forward.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merciful - showing mercy or compassion; lenient
  • Antonym: unforgiving - not willing to forgive; holding grudges

Etymology

From Old English forgiefan (to give, grant, forgive).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00926736-s
Synset POS: s
