incorrectness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkɚrɛktnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɒrɛktnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being incorrect or not conforming to fact or truth
quality of being incorrect

Examples

The incorrectness of his statement was obvious to everyone in the room.
She was penalized for the incorrectness of several answers on her test.
The editor pointed out the incorrectness in the article before publishing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: accuracy - the quality of being correct or precise
  • Antonym: correctness - the quality of being correct and in conformity with fact or truth
  • Synonym: error - a mistake or the state of being wrong

Etymology

From 'incorrect' + '-ness'. 'Incorrect' from Latin 'incorrectus' (not corrected).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnkəˈrɛktnəs / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəˈrɛktnəs / (BrE)
the quality of failing to conform to accepted social norms, standards, or expectations
nonconformity to social norms or standards

Examples

The incorrectness of his behavior shocked the older members of the community.
She was criticized for the incorrectness of her speech at the formal dinner.
Social incorrectness can sometimes lead to exclusion from certain groups.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: correctness - conformity to social expectations or accepted standards

Etymology

From in- (not) + correctness, modeled after 'incorrect' (from Latin incorrectus, from in- 'not' + correctus 'made straight, correct').

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
