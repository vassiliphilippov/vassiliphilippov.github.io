reduction

1. NOUN

/ rəˈdʌk.ʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈdʌk.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making something smaller in size, amount, degree, or price
act of making something smaller

Examples

There has been a significant reduction in air pollution over the past decade.
The company announced a reduction in staff to cut costs.
After switching to energy-efficient bulbs, she noticed a reduction in her electricity bill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decrease - the process of becoming less
  • Antonym: increase - the act or process of becoming larger or greater

Etymology

From Latin 'reductio' (a leading back), from 'reducere' (to lead back, bring back).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
the process or result of making something less complex or easier to understand by simplifying it
making something simpler or less complex

Examples

The reduction of the complex equation made it much easier to solve.
Software developers often aim for a reduction in code complexity to improve maintainability.
Effective teaching sometimes requires the reduction of challenging concepts into understandable parts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simplification - the process of making something simpler or easier to do or understand

Etymology

From Latin 'reductio' (a leading back, a bringing back), from 'reducere' (to lead back).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00193149-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ rəˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
in chemistry, the process in which electrons are gained by an atom or ion, often by removing oxygen or adding hydrogen; always occurs simultaneously with oxidation of another substance
gain of electrons in chemistry
medical

Examples

During the reaction, reduction occurs as the iron ions gain electrons.
The chemist explained how hydrogen acts as an agent of reduction in the process.
Oxidation and reduction always take place together in redox reactions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: oxidation - the process of losing electrons, often occurring together with reduction

Etymology

From Latin reductio (a leading back), from reducere (to lead back).
