anencephalic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.ɛn.səˈfæl.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌan.ɛn.sɪˈfal.ɪk / (BrE)
(Medicine) Having a partial or total absence of the brain, usually present from birth; affected by or relating to anencephaly.
lacking a brain (medical, congenital)
medical

Examples

The doctors explained that the fetus was anencephalic and would not survive after birth.
Researchers studied the genetic risk factors for anencephalic conditions.
Caring for a newborn with an anencephalic disorder can be emotionally challenging for families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anencephaly - a congenital condition in which part or all of the brain is absent

Etymology

From Greek an- (without) + enkephalos (brain).
