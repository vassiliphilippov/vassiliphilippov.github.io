semantics

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈmæntɪks / (AmE), / sɪˈmantɪks / (BrE)
the branch of linguistics concerned with the meaning of words, phrases, sentences, and texts
study of word and sentence meaning

Examples

The professor specializes in semantics and teaches courses on meaning in language.
Debates about semantics can sometimes overshadow more important issues.
In linguistics, semantics helps explain how words convey different meanings in context.

Synset Examples

a petty argument about semantics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pragmatics - linguistic study of language in context and use

Etymology

From Greek sēmantikos (significant, having meaning), from sēmainein (to signify).

2. NOUN

/ sɪˈmæntɪks / (AmE), / sɪˈmæntɪks / (BrE)
the study of meaning in language, including how words, phrases, and sentences signify concepts and relationships
the study of meaning in language

Examples

She specializes in semantics and studies how meaning is conveyed through language.
The argument was not about facts but rather about the semantics of the terms involved.
In linguistics, semantics helps explain how a single word can have multiple meanings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: syntax - the arrangement of words and phrases to create sentences in a language
  • Degree Higher: pragmatics - the study of language use in context and the practical aspects of meaning

Etymology

From Greek sēmantikos (significant), from sēma (sign).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06189508-n
Synset POS: n
