potash

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑː.tæʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɒ.tæʃ / (BrE)
A potassium compound, especially potassium carbonate, often used in fertilizers and various industrial processes.
potassium compound used in fertilizers and industry

Examples

Farmers often add potash to the soil to improve crop yields.
The factory produces large quantities of potash for use in the chemical industry.
Prices for potash have risen due to increased demand for fertilizers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potassium carbonate - a specific potassium compound, the main constituent of potash

Etymology

From early Dutch *potaschen* (pot ashes), referring to ashes of burned wood or plants used for potassium compounds.
