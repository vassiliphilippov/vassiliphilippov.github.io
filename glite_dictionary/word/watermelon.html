watermelon

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔːtərˌmɛlən / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌmɛlən / (BrE)
A large, round or oval fruit with hard green rind, sweet juicy red flesh, and usually many black seeds.
a large, juicy fruit with green rind and red flesh

Examples

She sliced the watermelon into thick, juicy wedges for the picnic.
In the summer, eating chilled watermelon is a refreshing treat.
Each watermelon can weigh more than ten kilograms when fully grown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melon - any of several types of large juicy fruit with thick rind

Etymology

From water + melon, originally from Latin 'melonem', influenced by the fruit's watery flesh.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtərˌmɛlən / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌmɛlən / (BrE)
A large fruit with a hard green rind and sweet, juicy red or sometimes yellowish flesh.
large juicy fruit with green rind

Examples

We shared a slice of watermelon at the picnic on a hot summer day.
She bought a large watermelon for the family gathering.
The children enjoyed eating cold watermelon after swimming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melon - a large, juicy fruit with sweet flesh and a hard rind

Etymology

From water + melon, modeled after similar terms in several European languages; 'melon' via French from Latin 'melopepo'.
