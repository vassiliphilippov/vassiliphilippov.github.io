Tanacetum cinerariifolium

1. NOUN

/ təˈnæsətəm sɪˌnɛrəriəˈfoʊliəm / (AmE), / təˈnæsɪtəm sɪˌnɛrəriəˈfəʊliəm / (BrE)
A species of perennial plant with white flowers native to the Balkan region, known as a source of natural insecticide (pyrethrum).
plant species; source of pyrethrum insecticide

Examples

Tanacetum cinerariifolium is widely cultivated for the production of pyrethrum insecticides.
Farmers use extracts from Tanacetum cinerariifolium as an organic method to control pests.
The leaves of Tanacetum cinerariifolium are white and silky-hairy on the underside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyrethrum - a plant extract used as a natural insecticide

Etymology

From Latin: 'Tanacetum' is the genus name, 'cinerariifolium' means 'with leaves like those of cineraria'.
