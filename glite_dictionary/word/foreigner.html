foreigner

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrənər / (AmE), / ˈfɒrənə / (BrE)
a person who comes from a different country or is not a citizen of the country where they are
person from another country

Examples

Many foreigners visit the city every year to experience its culture.
He felt like a foreigner when he first moved to Japan.
As a foreigner, she had to obtain a special permit to work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alien - a person who is not a citizen of the country where they live
  • Antonym: native - a person born in a specified place, especially one who has lived there all their life

Etymology

From Old French 'forain' (stranger, foreigner), from Latin 'foris' (outside).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrənər / (AmE), / ˈfɒrənə / (BrE)
a person who is not accepted as part of a particular group; an outsider
person not accepted in a group

Examples

After moving to the small town, she felt like a foreigner in her own country.
Being the only new employee, he was treated as a foreigner among his colleagues.
Despite growing up there, he remained a foreigner to the traditions of the local people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outsider - a person who does not belong to a group or organization
  • Antonym: member - person who belongs to a group or organization

Etymology

From Old French forain (outsider, stranger), from Latin foris (outside).
