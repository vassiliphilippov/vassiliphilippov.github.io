attractiveness

1. NOUN

/ əˈtræktɪvnəs / (AmE), / əˈtræktɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being sexually alluring or appealing to others
sexual allure or physical appeal

Examples

Her attractiveness made her the center of attention at every party.
He was struck by her natural attractiveness and charm.
The film explores society's obsession with physical attractiveness and beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allure - the quality of being powerfully and mysteriously attractive or fascinating
  • Synonym: sex appeal - the quality of being sexually attractive
  • Antonym: unattractiveness - the quality of lacking attractiveness; the opposite of being attractive

Etymology

From 'attractive' + '-ness'; 'attractive' from Latin 'attrahere' (to draw toward).

2. NOUN

/ əˈtræktɪvnəs / (AmE), / əˈtræktɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being attractive or able to arouse interest or admiration.
the quality of being attractive

Examples

The attractiveness of the city has brought in many new residents over the past year.
His attractiveness is not just about looks, but also about his charm and confidence.
They improved the park's attractiveness by planting more flowers and building new playgrounds.

Synset Examples

her personality held a strange attraction for him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attraction - the action or power of evoking interest, pleasure, or liking for someone or something
  • Antonym: repulsiveness - the quality of being repulsive or causing strong dislike

Etymology

From 'attractive' (from Latin attrahere 'to draw toward') + '-ness' (noun-forming suffix).
