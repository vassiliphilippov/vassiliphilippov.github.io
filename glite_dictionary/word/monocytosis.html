monocytosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːnəsaɪˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəsɪˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
an abnormal increase in the number of monocytes in the blood, often indicating infection, inflammation, or certain diseases
abnormally high monocyte count in blood
medical

Examples

The doctor noted monocytosis on the patient's blood test, prompting further investigation.
Chronic infections can sometimes cause persistent monocytosis.
Monocytosis may be a sign of an underlying inflammatory condition.

Etymology

From Greek monos (single) + kytos (cell) + -osis (condition).
