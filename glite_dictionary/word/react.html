to react

1. VERB

/ riˈækt / (AmE), / riˈækt / (BrE)
to behave or respond in a particular way because of something that has happened or been said
respond to an event or stimulus

Examples

She didn't react when she heard the news, remaining completely silent.
Children often react differently to new environments.
How you react under pressure can reveal a lot about your character.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to respond - to say or do something in reply or as a reaction

Etymology

From Latin 'reactus', past participle of 'reagere', from 're-' (again) + 'agere' (to do, act).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00719282-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ riˈækt / (AmE), / riˈækt / (BrE)
to respond to something, especially by opposing or counteracting it
respond or act in opposition

Examples

Whenever criticized, she tends to react defensively rather than listen calmly.
Many people react strongly to changes in their work environment.
He tried not to react to the rude comments during the meeting.

Synset Examples

She reacts negatively to everything I say

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to respond - to reply or answer to something
  • Synonym: to oppose - to act against or resist something
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something

Etymology

From Latin 'reactus', past participle of 'reagere', meaning 'to do again, to act in response'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02383714-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ riˈækt / (AmE), / riˈækt / (BrE)
(of substances) to undergo a chemical change when mixed with another substance, especially under certain conditions.
undergo a chemical change

Examples

When sodium and water react, hydrogen gas is produced.
The two chemicals will only react at high temperatures.
Carbon dioxide does not react with nitrogen under normal conditions.

Synset Examples

The hydrogen and the oxygen react

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combine - to join or mix together, often chemically
  • Antonym: to decompose - to break down into simpler substances through a chemical reaction

Etymology

From Latin *reagere*, from *re-* (again) + *agere* (to do, act).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00447840-v
Synset POS: v
