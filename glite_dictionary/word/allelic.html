allelic

1. ADJ

/ əˈliːlɪk / (AmE), / əˈliːlɪk / (BrE)
relating to or caused by alleles; involving or pertaining to different forms of a gene.
relating to alleles (genetics)
medical

Examples

The observed traits are determined by the allelic variation present in the population.
Researchers studied the allelic differences responsible for the genetic disorder.
Allelic mutations can lead to significant changes in physical characteristics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allele - any of the alternative versions of a gene

Etymology

From 'allele', derived from German 'Allel', which is short for 'Allelomorph'.
