pleuralgia

1. NOUN

/ pluˈræl.dʒə / (AmE), / plʊəˈral.dʒɪə / (BrE)
Pain in the chest caused by inflammation of the muscles between the ribs.
chest pain from intercostal muscle inflammation
medical

Examples

The patient complained of severe pleuralgia after a bout of intense coughing.
Doctors prescribed pain relief to help him manage his pleuralgia.
Chronic pleuralgia can significantly affect a person's ability to breathe deeply.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pleurisy - inflammation of the pleura, often causing chest pain

Etymology

From Greek 'pleura' (side, rib) + 'algos' (pain).
