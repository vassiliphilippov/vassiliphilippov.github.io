concomitant

1. NOUN

/ kənˈkɑːm.ə.tənt / (AmE), / kənˈkɒm.ɪ.tənt / (BrE)
an event or situation that happens at the same time as, or in connection with, another.
something occurring together with another

Examples

High blood pressure is often a concomitant of obesity.
The festival and its concomitant celebrations attract thousands of tourists each year.
Economic growth frequently brings concomitant challenges such as rising inequality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accompaniment - something that goes with or complements another

Etymology

From Latin concomitant-, present participle of concomitari (to accompany), from con- (together) + comitari (to accompany).

2. ADJ

/ kənˈkɑːmɪtənt / (AmE), / kənˈkɒmɪtənt / (BrE)
happening at the same time as something else, especially as a consequence; accompanying
accompanying, often as a result

Examples

The patient's fever was a concomitant symptom of the underlying infection.
Economic growth is often accompanied by a concomitant increase in energy consumption.
With rapid technological change come concomitant challenges and opportunities.

Synset Examples

an excessive growth of bureaucracy, with attendant problems
snags incidental to the changeover in management
attendant circumstances
the period of tension and consequent need for military preparedness
the ensuant response to his appeal
the resultant savings were considerable
collateral target damage from a bombing run

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attendant - occurring with or as a result of something
  • Synonym: incidental - accompanying but not a major part
  • Synonym: consequent - following as a result or effect

Etymology

From Latin concomitari (to accompany), from com- (together) + comitari (to accompany).
