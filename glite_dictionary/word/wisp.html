wisp

1. NOUN

/ wɪsp / (AmE), / wɪsp / (BrE)
A group or flock of snipe (a type of bird) considered together.
a flock of snipe (birds)

Examples

As dawn broke, a wisp of snipe lifted from the marsh.
We watched a wisp of snipe wheel over the reeds and vanish into the mist.
A passing wisp of snipe startled the fisherman on the riverbank.

2. NOUN

/ wɪsp / (AmE), / wɪsp / (BrE)
a small, thin tuft, lock, or piece of something, especially hair, smoke, or cloud
a small, thin tuft or strand

Examples

A wisp of smoke curled up from the chimney into the morning air.
She brushed a wisp of hair from her forehead.
There was only a wisp of cloud in the otherwise clear blue sky.

Synset Examples

wisps of hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strand - a single thin length of something such as thread or hair
  • Synonym: lock - a piece or strand of hair

Etymology

Possibly from Middle English *wisp* (bundle of hay or twigs).

3. NOUN

/ wɪsp / (AmE), / wɪsp / (BrE)
a small bundle or handful of straw, hay, or similar material tied together
small bundle of straw or hay

Examples

He tied the broom together with a wisp of straw.
A single wisp of hay clung to his boots as he left the barn.
She used a wisp of grass to make a tiny bed for the mouse.

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *wisp* (a handful of hay or the like), from Old Norse *visp* (wisp, bunch).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08024812-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ wɪsp / (AmE), / wɪsp / (BrE)
a small, thin, and delicate person, especially a child or young woman; someone who appears frail or slight
a small, slender or delicate person

Examples

She was just a wisp of a girl, barely making a sound as she entered the room.
Even as a boy, he was a wisp among the hearty village children.
The author described the hero's sister as a wisp, with hair as fine as silk.

Synset Examples

a mere wisp of a girl

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waif - a homeless, thin, or delicate person, especially a child

Etymology

From Middle English *wisp*, meaning a bundle or small amount, probably of Scandinavian origin.
