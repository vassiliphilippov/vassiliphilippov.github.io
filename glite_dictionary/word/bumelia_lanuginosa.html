Bumelia lanuginosa

1. NOUN

/ buˈmiːliə ləˌnuːdʒəˈnoʊsə / (AmE), / bjuːˈmiːlɪə ləˌnjuːdʒɪˈnəʊsə / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States and Mexico, known for its dense, woolly young shoots.
deciduous tree of SE US and Mexico

Examples

The Bumelia lanuginosa thrives in sandy soils across parts of Texas and Florida.
Botanists studied the leaf structure of Bumelia lanuginosa to understand its adaptation to dry climates.
Birds often feed on the fruits of Bumelia lanuginosa during late summer.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'boumelia' (wild olive); species 'lanuginosa' from Latin 'lanuginosus' (downy, woolly).
