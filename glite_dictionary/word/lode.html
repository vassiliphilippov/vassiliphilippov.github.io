lode

1. NOUN

/ loʊd / (AmE), / ləʊd / (BrE)
A layer or vein of valuable mineral ore within the earth, typically separated from surrounding rock by distinct boundaries.
vein or deposit of valuable ore

Examples

The miners struck a rich lode of silver deep beneath the mountain.
This region is famous for its gold lodes dating back to the nineteenth century.
The discovery of a new lode revitalized the town's economy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vein - a thin layer of mineral or ore in rock
  • Synonym: ore - a naturally occurring solid material from which a metal can be extracted

Etymology

From Middle English, of unknown origin, perhaps related to Old English 'lād' (way, course).
