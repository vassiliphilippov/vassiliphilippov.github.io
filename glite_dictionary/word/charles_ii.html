Charles II

1. PROPN

/ ˈtʃɑrlz ðə ˈsɛkənd / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːlz ðə ˈsɛkənd / (BrE)
King of England, Scotland, and Ireland during the Restoration period (1630–1685), known for his reign following the English Civil War and the re-establishment of the monarchy.
King of England, Scotland, and Ireland, 1630–1685

Examples

Historians often debate the legacy of Charles II and his influence on British history.
After years of exile, Charles II was restored to the throne in 1660.
The reign of Charles II marked the start of the Restoration period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Restoration - The re-establishment of the monarchy in England in 1660, marking the start of Charles II's reign
