subnormal

1. ADJ

/ sʌbˈnɔrməl / (AmE), / sʌbˈnɔːməl / (BrE)
below the normal or average level, especially in amount, quality, or performance.
below normal or average

Examples

The company's profits were subnormal compared to previous years.
He was concerned when his test results came back as subnormal.
This summer has been marked by subnormal temperatures throughout the region.

Synset Examples

after the floods the harvests were subnormal
subnormal intelligence

Related Terms

  • Antonym: normal - conforming to a standard; usual or expected

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (under, below) + 'normalis' (according to rule).

WordNet ID: oewn-01602284-s

2. NOUN

/ sʌbˈnɔrməl / (AmE), / sʌbˈnɔːməl / (BrE)
(dated, offensive) A person who has less than normal intellectual ability; a person with intellectual disability.
person of below-normal intelligence (dated, offensive)
offensive archaic

Examples

The institution once housed several subnormal individuals, but the term is now recognized as outdated and offensive.
He was cruelly labeled a subnormal in a time when understanding of learning disabilities was limited.
Staff were trained to help subnormal children adapt to daily life, though such language is no longer acceptable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idiot - a person of very low intelligence (offensive, dated)
  • Antonym: genius - a person of exceptional intellectual or creative power

Etymology

From Latin sub- (under) + normal (according to rule or standard).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10689315-n
Synset POS: n
