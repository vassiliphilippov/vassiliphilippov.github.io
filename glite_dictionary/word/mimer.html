mimer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪmər / (AmE), / ˈmaɪmə / (BrE)
a performer who acts out stories or emotions using only gestures and facial expressions, without speech
performer using gestures and facial expressions

Examples

The French mimer captivated the audience with his silent performance.
As a skilled mimer, she could portray a range of emotions without uttering a word.
Many children were fascinated by the mimer in the park, copying his exaggerated gestures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mime - the theatrical technique of expressing a story or emotion without speech

Etymology

From 'mime' + '-er', indicating a person who performs mime.
