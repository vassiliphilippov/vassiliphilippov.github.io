attritional

1. ADJ

/ əˈtrɪʃənl / (AmE), / əˈtrɪʃ(ə)nəl / (BrE)
Relating to or caused by attrition, especially referring to gradual reduction or wearing down over time.
relating to gradual reduction by attrition

Examples

The conflict developed into a prolonged and attritional struggle, exhausting both sides.
Their playing style is highly attritional, focusing on wearing down their opponents over time.
The company's decline was not sudden but the result of an attritional loss of customers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attrition - gradual reduction or wearing down

Etymology

Formed from 'attrition' plus the suffix '-al'.
