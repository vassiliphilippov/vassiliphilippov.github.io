Cochlearius cochlearius

1. NOUN

/ koʊ.kliˈɛə.ri.əs koʊ.kliˈɛə.ri.əs / (AmE), / kɒk.liˈɪə.ri.əs kɒk.liˈɪə.ri.əs / (BrE)
A tropical American heron, also known as the boat-billed heron, distinguished by its broad, boat-shaped bill and nocturnal habits.
tropical American heron species

Examples

The Cochlearius cochlearius is often seen along mangrove-lined rivers in Central America.
Researchers observed the nesting habits of Cochlearius cochlearius in the wetlands of Panama.
Birdwatchers are usually thrilled to spot a Cochlearius cochlearius because of its unusual bill shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boat-billed heron - common name for Cochlearius cochlearius

Etymology

From Latin cochlearius (spoon-shaped) referring to the bird's distinctive bill.
