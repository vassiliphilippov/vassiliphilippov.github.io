Bubo

1. NOUN

/ ˈbjuːboʊ / (AmE), / ˈbjuːbəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of large owls including the American horned owls and Old World eagle-owls.
genus of large owls, horned and eagle-owls

Examples

The Bubo genus includes some of the largest and most powerful owls in the world.
Scientists have observed that members of the Bubo genus are found on several continents.
The great horned owl, known scientifically as Bubo virginianus, is widespread throughout the Americas.

Etymology

From Latin bubo (owl), originally from Greek boubōn (owl).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbjuːboʊ / (AmE), / ˈbjuːbəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of large owls in the family Strigidae, including the American horned owls and the Old World eagle-owls.
genus of large owls (horned and eagle-owls)

Examples

The great horned owl belongs to the genus Bubo, known for their ear tufts.
Several species in the genus Bubo are among the largest living owls.
Researchers found new behavioral traits in members of the Bubo genus.

Etymology

From Latin 'bubo' (owl).
