quartering

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔrtərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkwɔːtərɪŋ / (BrE)
the division of a shield into four parts to display the coats of arms of four different families, typically to represent the ancestry of an individual
heraldic division of a shield into four

Examples

The old manor displayed the family's quartering over the grand entrance.
Knowledge of heraldry helps to interpret the complex quartering seen on antique shields.
The marriage resulted in a new quartering on the family coat of arms.

Etymology

From the verb 'quarter', ultimately from Latin 'quartarius' (fourth part).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔːrtərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkwɔːtərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of dividing something into four equal parts
division into four equal parts

Examples

The quartering of the cake ensured that everyone received an equal share.
In geometry, quartering a square creates four smaller squares of equal size.
Medieval punishments sometimes involved the quartering of a criminal's body as a gruesome penalty.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: halving - the act of dividing something into two equal parts

Etymology

From Middle English 'quartre', from Old French 'quartier', from Latin 'quartarius' (a fourth part).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔrdərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkwɔːtərɪŋ / (BrE)
accommodations provided, especially to military personnel, for living or lodging
military accommodations or lodging

Examples

The soldiers were grateful for the comfortable quartering provided at the barracks.
During the war, the town objected to the quartering of troops in private homes.
The general inspected the quartering arrangements to ensure they met proper standards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: billet - a place where soldiers are lodged temporarily

Etymology

From Middle English 'quartering', from 'quarter', influenced by the practice of assigning quarters to troops.
