egalitarian

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌɡælɪˈtɛriən / (AmE), / ɪˌɡælɪˈteəriən / (BrE)
a person who believes that all people should be equal and have the same rights and opportunities
person who believes in equality for all

Examples

She has always considered herself an egalitarian and supports equal rights movements.
Many egalitarians advocate for changes in education and healthcare policies.
As an egalitarian, he opposes any form of discrimination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: egalitarian - believing in or based on the principle that all people are equal

Etymology

From French 'égalitaire' (equal) + '-ian'; ultimately from Latin 'aequalitas' (equality).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˌɡælɪˈtɛriən / (AmE), / ɪˌɡælɪˈteəriən / (BrE)
believing in or promoting the principle that all people are equal and deserve equal rights and opportunities
promoting social equality

Examples

The company's egalitarian approach ensures that all employees have a voice in decision-making.
She supports egalitarian policies that aim to reduce income inequality.
Their club operates on an egalitarian basis, with no special privileges for any members.

Synset Examples

a classless society

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equalitarian - believing in and promoting equality
  • Antonym: elitist - favoring an elite as superior

Etymology

From French *égalitaire*, from *égal* (equal), from Latin *aequalis*.

Synset POS: s
