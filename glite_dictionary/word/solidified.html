solidified

1. ADJ

/ səˈlɪdəˌfaɪd / (AmE), / səˈlɪdɪfaɪd / (BrE)
having become solid or turned into a solid mass, typically from a liquid or gas state
turned into a solid

Examples

After being left overnight, the molten metal had solidified into a strong, durable block.
The wax solidified as soon as it touched the cold water.
Over time, their opinions had solidified and were not easily changed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: melted - made or become liquid by heat

Etymology

From the verb 'solidify,' which comes from French 'solidifier', from Latin 'solidus' (solid).
