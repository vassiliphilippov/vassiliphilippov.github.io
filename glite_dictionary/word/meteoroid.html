meteoroid

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːtiəˌrɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈmiːtɪərɔɪd / (BrE)
A small rocky or metallic body traveling through space that may enter a planet's atmosphere.
small body in space, enters atmosphere

Examples

The scientist studied the composition of the meteoroid that entered Earth's atmosphere last night.
A meteoroid burns up and becomes a meteor when it passes through our atmosphere.
Most meteoroids are no larger than pebbles, but some can be as big as boulders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meteor - the visible streak of light from a meteoroid entering Earth's atmosphere

Etymology

From meteor + -oid (from Greek eidos, meaning 'form, shape'). Coined in the 19th century.
