piquant

1. ADJ

/ ˈpiːkənt / (AmE), / ˈpiːkənt / (BrE)
having a pleasantly sharp or appetizing flavor; agreeably pungent in taste
having a pleasantly sharp taste

Examples

The salsa had a piquant flavor that complemented the grilled chicken perfectly.
She enjoys dishes with a piquant sauce, as they add extra excitement to every bite.
A sprinkle of fresh herbs gave the salad a piquant twist.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bland - lacking strong flavor; mild or insipid
  • Synonym: tangy - having a strong, sharp taste or flavor

Etymology

From French piquant, present participle of piquer (to prick, stimulate).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpiːkənt, ˈpiːkwənt / (AmE), / ˈpiːkənt, piˈkɒ̃ / (BrE)
Pleasantly stimulating to the mind or senses; engagingly provocative or lively.
stimulating or provocative

Examples

Her piquant comments kept the conversation lively and engaging all evening.
The novel offers a piquant blend of wit and insight that delights its readers.
His sense of humor is piquant enough to surprise but never to offend.

Synset Examples

a piquant wit
salty language

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bland - lacking strong features or characteristics; uninteresting
  • Synonym: zesty - full of flavor and excitement

Etymology

From French 'piquant', present participle of 'piquer' meaning 'to prick'.

pleasantly stimulating; engaging

Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bland - lacking strong features or characteristics; uninteresting
