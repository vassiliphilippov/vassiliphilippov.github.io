Penstemon rupicola

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnstəmɒn ruːˈpɪkələ / (AmE), / ˈpɛnstɪmən ruːˈpɪkələ / (BrE)
A species of perennial plant native to western North America, notable for its large, bright pink or rose flowers and mat-forming growth on rocky ledges and cliffs.
a flowering plant of western North America

Examples

Hikers admired the Penstemon rupicola blooming vibrantly along the rocky trail.
The Penstemon rupicola is prized by gardeners for its hardiness and vivid color.
Botanists studied the population of Penstemon rupicola on the exposed cliffs.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Penstemon - genus of flowering plants known as beardtongues

Etymology

From the genus name 'Penstemon' and Latin 'rupicola' meaning 'rock-dweller'.
