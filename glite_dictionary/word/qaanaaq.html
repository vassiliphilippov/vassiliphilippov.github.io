Qaanaaq

1. PROPN

/ kɑːˈnɑːk / (AmE), / kɑːˈnɑːk / (BrE)
A town in northwestern Greenland, notable as the site of a United States naval base during World War II.
town in northwestern Greenland

Examples

The remote town of Qaanaaq experiences months of darkness during the polar winter.
During World War II, Qaanaaq was the site of a strategically important U.S. naval base.
Researchers traveled to Qaanaaq to study the effects of climate change in the Arctic.

Etymology

From Greenlandic 'Qaanaaq', the native name of the settlement.
