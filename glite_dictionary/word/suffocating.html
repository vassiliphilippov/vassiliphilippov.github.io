suffocating

1. ADJ

/ ˈsʌfəˌkeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsʌfəkeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a place or atmosphere) making it difficult to breathe, especially because of lack of fresh air, heat, or being tightly enclosed.
making it hard to breathe (heat or lack of air)

Examples

The suffocating atmosphere in the crowded subway made me feel dizzy.
We had to step outside to escape the suffocating heat inside the room.
She opened a window to let in fresh air and break the suffocating stillness.

Synset Examples

the choking June dust
the smothering soft voices
smothering heat
the room was suffocating — hot and airless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stifling - very hot or stuffy; making it difficult to breathe
  • Synonym: oppressive - weighing heavily on the senses or spirit
  • Antonym: airy - well-ventilated, with lots of fresh air

Etymology

From Latin suffocare (to choke, strangle) via suffocate + -ing.
