Solanum carolinense

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈleɪnəm ˌkærəlaɪˈnɛns / (AmE), / səʊˈleɪnəm ˌkarəlɪˈnɛns / (BrE)
A prickly perennial weed, also known as Carolina horsenettle, with pale yellow flowers and yellow berry-like fruit, found in the southern and eastern United States.
a prickly weed of the southern/eastern US

Examples

The pasture was overrun with Solanum carolinense, making grazing difficult for the cattle.
Gardeners often struggle to remove Solanum carolinense due to its deep, spreading roots.
Researchers studied Solanum carolinense to understand its impact on local ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Carolina horsenettle - common name for Solanum carolinense

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Solanum' (nightshade family) and 'carolinense', referring to Carolina, the region where it was first identified.
