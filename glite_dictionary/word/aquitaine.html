Aquitaine

1. PROPN

/ ˈæk.wɪˌteɪn / (AmE), / ˈakwɪteɪn / (BrE)
a historical and administrative region in southwestern France, located between Bordeaux and the Pyrenees
region in southwestern France

Examples

The vineyards of Aquitaine are famous for producing some of the finest wines in France.
During the Middle Ages, Aquitaine was an important duchy under English control for several centuries.
Many tourists visit Aquitaine to enjoy its beautiful coastline and medieval towns.

Etymology

From Latin Aquitania, the Roman name for the region.
