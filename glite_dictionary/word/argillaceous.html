argillaceous

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑrɡəˈleɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˌɑːɡɪˈleɪʃəs / (BrE)
(of rocks or soils) containing or resembling clay.
containing or resembling clay

Examples

The geologist collected samples of argillaceous rock from the riverbank.
Certain argillaceous soils can hinder the growth of some plants due to poor drainage.
The fossil was embedded in an argillaceous layer beneath the limestone.

Synset Examples

argillaceous rocks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clayey - containing, consisting of, or resembling clay

Etymology

From Latin 'argilla' (clay) and the suffix '-aceous' (of the nature of).
