transactinide

1. ADJ

/ ˌtræn.sækˈtɪ.naɪd / (AmE), / ˌtræn.sækˈtɪ.naɪd / (BrE)
relating to chemical elements with atomic numbers greater than 103.
elements with atomic numbers >103
medical

Examples

Scientists study transactinide elements to learn more about nuclear stability.
Most transactinide compounds are highly unstable and radioactive.
The discovery of new transactinide isotopes often requires advanced particle accelerators.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: actinide - elements with atomic numbers from 89 to 103

Etymology

From 'trans-' (beyond) + 'actinide' (elements 89–103).

2. NOUN

/ ˌtræn.sækˈtɪn.aɪd / (AmE), / ˌtranz.ækˈtɪn.aɪd / (BrE)
any of the artificially produced chemical elements with atomic numbers greater than 103
element with atomic number above 103

Examples

Scientists at the laboratory are synthesizing new transactinide elements for further study.
The discovery of each new transactinide challenges our understanding of nuclear physics.
Elements classified as transactinide are all radioactive and do not occur naturally.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: actinide - element with atomic numbers from 89 to 103

Etymology

From prefix trans- (beyond) and actinide, referring to elements beyond actinides in the periodic table.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14651551-n
Synset POS: n
