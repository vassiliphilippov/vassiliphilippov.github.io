Tennyson

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɛnɪsən / (AmE), / ˈtɛnɪsən / (BrE)
(1809–1892) An English poet and the foremost figure of Victorian poetry, known for works such as 'In Memoriam' and 'The Charge of the Light Brigade'.
English Victorian poet (1809–1892)

Examples

Tennyson is celebrated for his lyrical mastery and narrative poetry.
Many students read Tennyson's poems in their literature classes.
In 1850, Tennyson was named Poet Laureate of the United Kingdom.

Etymology

Family name of Alfred, Lord Tennyson; used as a proper noun for the poet.
