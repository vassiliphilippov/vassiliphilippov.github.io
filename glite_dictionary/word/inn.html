inn

1. NOUN

/ ɪn / (AmE), / ɪn / (BrE)
a small hotel or establishment that provides accommodation and food for travelers, often with a historical or rural character
small hotel for travelers

Examples

We spent the night at a charming inn in the countryside.
The old stone inn offered both dinner and breakfast to its guests.
Many travelers prefer a quiet inn instead of a large hotel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hotel - an establishment providing accommodation, meals, and other services for travelers and tourists

Etymology

From Old English 'inn' (house, lodging place), related to the concept of entering or being inside.
