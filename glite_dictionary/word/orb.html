orb

1. NOUN

/ ɔrb / (AmE), / ɔːb / (BrE)
the rounded, ball-shaped capsule containing the vertebrate eye; often used figuratively or in poetic language to refer to the eye
the rounded form of the eye

Examples

His blue orbs sparkled with excitement as he shared the news.
The cat’s yellow orbs glowed in the darkness, watching intently.
Her tear-filled orbs revealed the depth of her sorrow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eye - the organ of sight in humans and animals

Etymology

From Latin orbis (circle, disk, orb).

2. NOUN

/ ɔrb / (AmE), / ɔːb / (BrE)
a spherical object or shape, often used in literary or poetic contexts to refer to round objects such as the sun, the eye, or a ball
spherical object or shape

Examples

A golden orb crowned the top of the cathedral dome, gleaming in the sunlight.
She stared at the glowing orb in the magician's hand, mesmerized by its mysterious light.
The moon appeared as a pale orb in the night sky.

Synset Examples

a ball of fire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sphere - a perfectly round three-dimensional object, like a ball
  • Synonym: ball - a round object used in various games and sports

Etymology

From Latin orbis (circle, disk, orb).
