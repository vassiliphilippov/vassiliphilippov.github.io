Hippodamia convergens

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpəˈdeɪmiə kənˈvɜrdʒəns / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈdeɪmiə kənˈvɜːdʒəns / (BrE)
a common species of ladybug found in North America, recognized by its orange-red wing covers with black spots and often used in biological pest control
a North American species of ladybug

Examples

Researchers released hundreds of Hippodamia convergens into the field to control aphid populations naturally.
The orange and black coloration of Hippodamia convergens makes it easy to distinguish from other ladybug species.
Gardeners often purchase Hippodamia convergens for its effectiveness in managing pests without harmful chemicals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ladybug - a small beetle of the family Coccinellidae, often brightly colored

Etymology

From Greek 'hippos' (horse) + 'damia' (tamer), the genus name; 'convergens' from Latin 'convergere' (to come together), for the converging white lines on its pronotum.
