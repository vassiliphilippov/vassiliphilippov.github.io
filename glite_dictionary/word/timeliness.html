timeliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪmlinəs / (AmE), / ˈtaɪmlinɪs / (BrE)
The quality of occurring or being done at a suitable or proper time.
quality of being at the right time

Examples

Her timeliness in submitting the report impressed her supervisor.
The timeliness of the warning allowed everyone to evacuate safely.
Journalists are judged partly by the timeliness of their news coverage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punctuality - the fact or quality of being on time
  • Antonym: lateness - the quality of being late

Etymology

From 'timely' + '-ness', where 'timely' is from Middle English 'timeli'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪm.linəs / (AmE), / ˈtaɪm.li.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of occurring at a suitable or appropriate time; the state of being timely.
quality of being timely

Examples

The timeliness of the information was critical to our decision-making process.
Her timeliness in submitting the report impressed her supervisor.
They praised the timeliness of the medical intervention in saving lives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: promptness - the quality of acting quickly or without delay
  • Antonym: lateness - the quality of occurring after the expected or proper time

Etymology

From 'timely' + '-ness', formed in English.
