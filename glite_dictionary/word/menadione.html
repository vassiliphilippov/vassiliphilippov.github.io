menadione

1. NOUN

/ mɛˈnædiˌoʊn / (AmE), / mɛˈnadɪəʊn / (BrE)
A synthetic chemical compound that is a form of vitamin K, used as a nutritional supplement and in research.
synthetic form of vitamin K
medical

Examples

Menadione is sometimes added to animal feed as a source of vitamin K activity.
Some studies have investigated the use of menadione in cancer treatment research.
The laboratory ordered a solution containing menadione for the experiment.

Etymology

From 'methyl' + 'naphthoquinone' + suffix '-one', reflecting its chemical structure.
