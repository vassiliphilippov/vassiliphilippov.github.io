touter

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaʊtər / (AmE), / ˈtaʊtə / (BrE)
a person who solicits or advertises for customers, especially in an aggressive or brazen manner
aggressive advertiser or solicitor

Examples

A touter stood at the entrance, waving flyers and shouting special offers to passersby.
We were approached by a touter trying to sell us tickets to a boat tour.
The city has cracked down on illegal touters targeting tourists in the busy market district.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tout - to solicit customers aggressively

Etymology

From 'tout' (to solicit customers) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
