norethindrone acetate

1. NOUN

/ nɔːˌrɛθɪnˌdroʊn ˈæsɪteɪt / (AmE), / nɔːˌrɛθɪnˌdrəʊn ˈæsɪteɪt / (BrE)
a synthetic progestin hormone used in oral contraceptives and to treat endometriosis and menstrual disorders
synthetic hormone used in contraception and hormone therapy
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed norethindrone acetate to help manage her endometriosis symptoms.
Norethindrone acetate is commonly found as an active ingredient in oral contraceptive pills.
Some women experience side effects when taking norethindrone acetate for menstrual regulation.

Etymology

From 'norethindrone', a synthetic hormone, and 'acetate', referring to the acetate ester form.
