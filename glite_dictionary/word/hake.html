hake

1. NOUN

/ heɪk / (AmE), / heɪk / (BrE)
a marine fish of the cod family, valued as food, especially several species of the genus Merluccius
a type of marine food fish related to cod

Examples

Fresh hake was on the menu at the seaside restaurant last night.
The fisherman was proud of his large catch of hake from the Atlantic Ocean.
Many European dishes feature hake as a primary ingredient due to its delicate flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cod - a large marine fish with firm, white flesh

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle Dutch *haeck* or Middle Low German *hake* (hook), referring to the fish's barbed jaw.

2. NOUN

/ heɪk / (AmE), / heɪk / (BrE)
the lean, white flesh of a marine fish related to cod, commonly used as food
lean flesh of a cod-like fish used as food

Examples

We bought a pound of hake for tonight's dinner.
The restaurant's specialty is grilled hake with lemon and herbs.
Many people prefer hake for its mild flavor and delicate texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cod - the flesh or fish of the cod, a common food fish

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *haec* or Old French *haie*, both meaning 'pike' (the fish).
