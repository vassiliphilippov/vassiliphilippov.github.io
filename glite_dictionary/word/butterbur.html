butterbur

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌt̬.ɚ.bɝː / (AmE), / ˈbʌt.ə.bɜː / (BrE)
a perennial Eurasian herb with large broad leaves and clusters of lilac-pink rayless flowers, commonly found in moist habitats.
a Eurasian plant with broad leaves and lilac-pink flowers

Examples

The broad leaves of butterbur make it easy to recognize along riverbanks.
Some people use extracts from butterbur as a natural remedy for headaches.
In wet meadows, butterbur can grow into dense clumps, shading smaller plants.

Etymology

From Middle English butterburre, likely referring to the leaves used to wrap butter.
