scrumpy

1. NOUN

/ ˈskrʌmpi / (AmE), / ˈskrʌmpi / (BrE)
a strong, rough-tasting cider, typically made in western England from apples
strong cider from western England

Examples

The local pub serves a potent scrumpy that packs quite a punch.
He spent the afternoon in the orchard tasting homemade scrumpy.
Many festivals in Somerset feature traditional scrumpy alongside other regional ciders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cider - an alcoholic drink made from fermented apple juice

Etymology

Probably from scrump, a dialect word meaning 'small or withered apple.'
