toller

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊlər / (AmE), / ˈtəʊlə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to collect tolls at a bridge, road, or other point of access
person who collects tolls

Examples

The toller greeted each driver as they pulled up to the booth.
After working as a toller for many years, she knew every commuter by face.
A toller must be alert to ensure all vehicles pay the proper fee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toll collector - person whose job is to collect tolls

Etymology

From 'toll' (charge for passage) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊlɚ / (AmE), / ˈtəʊlə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to ring church bells, especially to summon the congregation for service
person who rings church bells

Examples

The toller climbed the tower before sunrise to prepare for the morning service.
In the old days, a toller would signal important community events from the church tower.
Visitors watched as the toller pulled the ropes to create a melody for the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bell ringer - a person who rings bells, especially in a church

Etymology

From 'toll' (to ring a bell slowly) + '-er' agent suffix.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10734500-n
Synset POS: n
