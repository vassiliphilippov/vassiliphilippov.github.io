gingham

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪŋəm / (AmE), / ˈɡɪŋəm / (BrE)
a lightweight cotton fabric, usually woven with a checked or plaid pattern, often used for clothing and household linens
light cotton fabric with checked pattern

Examples

She wore a cheerful gingham dress to the summer picnic.
The table was covered with a red-and-white gingham cloth.
Classic country kitchens often feature curtains made from gingham.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plaid - fabric with a checkered or tartan pattern
  • Synonym: chintz - printed multicolored cotton fabric, usually glazed

Etymology

From Malay 'genggang' (striped), adopted into English via Dutch.
