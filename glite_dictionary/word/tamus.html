Tamus

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪ.məs / (AmE), / ˈteɪ.məs / (BrE)
a genus of tuberous vines in the family Dioscoreaceae, characterized by twining stems, heart-shaped leaves, and axillary racemes.
genus of tuberous vines in Dioscoreaceae

Examples

Tamus species are often found growing wild in hedgerows and woodlands.
The heart-shaped leaves are a distinctive feature of plants in the Tamus genus.
Botanists studied the tuberous roots of Tamus for medicinal properties.

Etymology

From Latin 'Tamus,' the genus name used in scientific classification.

2. NOUN

/ ˈteɪməs / (AmE), / ˈteɪməs / (BrE)
a genus of tuberous climbing plants in the family Dioscoreaceae, with twining stems, heart-shaped leaves, and racemes of small flowers
genus of tuberous climbing plants

Examples

The plant identified as Tamus is known for its distinctive heart-shaped leaves.
Botanists have classified the new species within the genus Tamus.
Certain medicinal properties are attributed to roots from members of the Tamus genus.

Etymology

New Latin, from Latin 'tamus', a type of climbing plant.
