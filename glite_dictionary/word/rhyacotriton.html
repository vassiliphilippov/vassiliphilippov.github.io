Rhyacotriton

1. NOUN

/ raɪˌækəˈtraɪtən / (AmE), / raɪˌakəˈtrɪtən / (BrE)
A genus of small, aquatic salamanders native to the Pacific Northwest region of North America, commonly known as Olympic salamanders.
genus of Olympic salamanders

Examples

Scientists recently discovered a new species of Rhyacotriton in an isolated mountain stream.
The habitat of the Rhyacotriton requires clean, cold water for the salamanders to thrive.
Biologists are studying the declining populations of Rhyacotriton due to environmental changes.

Etymology

From Greek 'rhyax' (stream) and 'triton' (newt), referring to their habitat and amphibian nature.
