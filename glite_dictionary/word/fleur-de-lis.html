fleur-de-lis

1. NOUN

/ flɜr də ˈliː / (AmE), / flɜː də ˈliː / (BrE)
A stylized lily or iris used as a decorative design or symbol, especially in heraldry.
stylized lily symbol, esp. heraldry

Examples

The royal banner displayed a golden fleur-de-lis at its center.
Many churches in France are decorated with the fleur-de-lis as a symbol of purity.
The knight's shield was emblazoned with a fleur-de-lis to signify his allegiance.

Etymology

From French 'fleur de lis', meaning 'flower of the lily'.

2. NOUN

/ flɝː də ˈliː / (AmE), / flɜː də ˈliː / (BrE)
A plant of the iris family with sword-shaped leaves and showy flowers typically composed of three petals and three drooping sepals.
type of iris plant with showy flowers

Examples

The garden was filled with colorful fleur-de-lis in full bloom.
She painted a delicate fleur-de-lis next to the roses on the vase.
Botanists often study the unique structure of the fleur-de-lis flower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iris - a plant with sword-shaped leaves and showy flowers, genus Iris

Etymology

From French 'fleur-de-lis', meaning 'flower of the lily'.
