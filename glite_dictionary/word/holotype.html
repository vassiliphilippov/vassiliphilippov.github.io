holotype

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊləˌtaɪp / (AmE), / ˈhɒləˌtaɪp / (BrE)
the single specimen on which the description and name of a new species is based
type specimen for a new species

Examples

The holotype of the newly discovered frog species is kept in the national museum.
Researchers examined the holotype to compare it with other known specimens.
The scientist designated a single fossil as the holotype for the extinct species.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: paratype - a specimen other than the holotype cited in the original description of a species
  • Degree Lower: syntype - any specimen cited in the original description when no holotype was designated
  • Degree Higher: lectotype - a specimen later selected to serve as the single type specimen when the original description designated none

Etymology

From Greek 'holos' (whole) + 'typos' (type, model).
