gong

1. VERB

/ ɡɑŋ / (AmE), / ɡɒŋ / (BrE)
to produce a sound by striking a gong
produce a sound by striking a gong

Examples

Every hour, the monk would gong to mark the passing of time.
She gonged loudly to announce the start of the ceremony.
The temple bell was gonging throughout the celebration, creating a mystical atmosphere.

2. NOUN

/ ɡɑŋ / (AmE), / ɡɒŋ / (BrE)
A large percussion instrument consisting of a flat metal disc that produces a resonant sound when struck with a mallet.
large percussion instrument (metal disc)

Examples

The orchestra featured a massive gong that resonated through the concert hall.
After striking the gong, the meditation session began in silence.
He bought a brass gong during his travels in Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cymbal - a percussion instrument consisting of a thin, round metal plate, typically played in pairs

Etymology

From Malay *gong*, referring to the instrument.

3. NOUN

/ ɡɑŋ / (AmE), / ɡɒŋ / (BrE)
a percussion instrument consisting of a set of tuned metal discs or bells that are struck with a mallet, especially used in orchestral music
a percussion instrument with tuned metal discs or bells

Examples

The composer included a gong in the percussion section of the orchestra.
She struck the largest gong to produce a deep, resonant sound.
Several gongs were arranged by pitch for the performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bell - a hollow instrument that makes a ringing sound when struck
  • Degree Lower: xylophone - a percussion instrument with wooden bars struck by mallets
  • Synonym: chime - a set of tuned bells

Etymology

From Malay 'gong', referring to the musical instrument.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03020822-n
Synset POS: n
