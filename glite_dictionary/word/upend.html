to upend

1. VERB

/ ʌpˈɛnd / (AmE), / ʌpˈɛnd / (BrE)
to become turned or set on end; to move so that the top or bottom is upright or vertical
become turned on end

Examples

After the storm, several boats upended along the shoreline.
The vase accidentally upended and water spilled everywhere.
During the turbulence, the model airplane upended and crashed to the ground.

Synset Examples

the airplanes upended

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to invert - to turn something upside down or reverse the position

Etymology

Formed from 'up' + 'end', referring to setting something with its end upward.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01913672-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ʌpˈɛnd / (AmE), / ʌpˈɛnd / (BrE)
to set, turn, or stand something on its end, usually so that its bottom becomes the top
set or turn on end

Examples

He accidentally upended the glass, spilling water across the table.
The movers had to upend the sofa to fit it through the doorway.
Before cleaning, upend the container to remove any remaining crumbs.

Synset Examples

upend the box and empty the contents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to invert - to turn upside down or in the opposite position
  • Antonym: right - to put something back in its correct upright position

Etymology

From 'up' + 'end', first recorded in the 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01520147-v
Synset POS: v
