whit

1. NOUN

/ wɪt / (AmE), / wɪt / (BrE)
a very small amount; the smallest possible part or trace of something
a tiny amount; the slightest bit

Examples

She didn't care a whit about what others thought.
There isn’t a whit of evidence to support his claim.
He was not a whit interested in the proposal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bit - a small amount; a piece or portion
  • Synonym: speck - a very small spot or amount
  • Synonym: trace - a very small indication or evidence of something

Etymology

From Middle English 'whit', from Old English 'wiht' (thing, creature), related to German 'Wicht'.
