archil

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrtʃɪl / (AmE), / ˈɑːtʃɪl / (BrE)
a purple or violet dye obtained from certain lichens, especially of the genera Roccella and Lecanora
a dye from certain lichens

Examples

The purple cloth was dyed with archil, extracted from special lichens.
Textiles colored with archil were highly prized during the Renaissance.
The process of making archil involves fermenting lichen and exposing it to air.

Etymology

From Italian 'oricello' or 'archil', of uncertain origin, possibly related to Spanish 'orseille'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑrtʃɪl / (AmE), / ˈɑːtʃɪl / (BrE)
a purplish-red dye obtained from certain lichens, especially those of the genera Roccella and Lecanora
purplish-red dye from lichens

Examples

The fabric was colored with archil to achieve a vibrant purple hue.
Traditional dyers once valued archil for its rich tones and ability to blend with other dyes.
She studied the production of archil from Mediterranean lichens for her chemistry project.

Etymology

From Old Italian 'oricello', diminutive of 'roccella', a lichen used for dye.
