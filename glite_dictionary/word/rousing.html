rousing

1. ADJ

/ ˈraʊzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈraʊzɪŋ / (BrE)
exciting strong emotion, enthusiasm, or interest; stirring
exciting enthusiasm or emotion

Examples

The band gave a rousing performance that had the whole crowd singing along.
Her rousing speech inspired everyone in the room to take action.
We started the day with a rousing cheer that lifted everyone's spirits.

Synset Examples

a rousing sermon
stirring events such as wars and rescues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stirring - causing great excitement or strong emotion

Etymology

From the verb 'rouse' (from Middle English rousen, meaning 'to shake up; awaken').

2. ADJ

/ ˈraʊzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈraʊzɪŋ / (BrE)
exciting or inspiring enthusiasm, action, or emotion
exciting; inspiring strong emotion or action

Examples

The audience erupted into cheers after the rousing speech.
She started the meeting with a rousing call to action.
His rousing performance inspired everyone in the room.

Synset Examples

tossed a rousing political comment into the conversation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspiring - giving someone the motivation to do something
  • Synonym: stimulating - encouraging interest or activity
  • Antonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement

Etymology

From the verb 'rouse' (to awaken or stir up), with the present participle suffix '-ing'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01903674-s
Synset POS: s
