ascendant

1. NOUN

/ əˈsændənt / (AmE), / əˈsændənt / (BrE)
an ancestor or person from whom one is descended, especially one more remote than a grandparent
remote ancestor; forebear

Examples

Many families can trace at least one ascendant to the early settlers of the region.
The property was inherited from an ascendant who lived several generations ago.
In legal documents, an ascendant refers to any direct ancestor, such as a parent or grandparent.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: descendant - a person descended from a particular ancestor
  • Synonym: ancestor - a person from whom one is descended

Etymology

From Latin 'ascendentem' (going up), from 'ascendere' (to climb).

/ əˈsændənt / (AmE), / əˈsændənt / (BrE)
Having greater power, influence, or importance; dominant or rising in status.
having dominant or rising influence

Examples

With her ascendant influence, she shaped the direction of the organization.
The party's ascendant position in parliament gave it control over key decisions.

Related Terms

/ əˈsændənt / (AmE), / əˈsændənt / (BrE)
rising upward or tending to move in an upward direction; ascending
rising or moving upward

Examples

Her mood was ascendant, growing brighter with each passing day.

Related Terms
