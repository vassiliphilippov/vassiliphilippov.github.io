Castilian

1. NOUN

/ kæˈstɪliən / (AmE), / kæˈstɪliən / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of Castile, a region in central Spain.
native or inhabitant of Castile

Examples

The Castilian led the group with stories from his homeland.
Many Castilians are proud of their regional traditions and cuisine.
During the trip to Spain, we met a friendly Castilian in Toledo.

Etymology

From Spanish 'castellano', from 'Castilla' (Castile).

2. NOUN

/ kæˈstɪliən / (AmE), / kaˈstɪliən / (BrE)
the standard Spanish language, especially as spoken in the region of Castile in Spain
the Spanish language of Castile

Examples

She speaks fluent Castilian after living in Madrid for five years.
The film was released in both Catalan and Castilian versions.
Many Spanish classes in the United States teach standard Castilian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spanish - the Romance language spoken in Spain and Latin America

Etymology

From Spanish 'castellano', from 'Castilla' (Castile).
