unhindered

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈhɪndɚd / (AmE), / ʌnˈhɪndəd / (BrE)
not obstructed, slowed, or interfered with; free to proceed without hindrance or restraint.
not obstructed or interfered with

Examples

The river flowed unhindered through the valley after the dam was removed.
She moved unhindered through the crowd, reaching the exit quickly.
Scientific progress should continue unhindered by unnecessary bureaucracy.

Synset Examples

an outlet for healthy and unhampered action
a priest unhampered by scruple
the new stock market was unhampered by tradition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unhampered - not impeded or hindered
  • Antonym: hindered - obstructed or slowed down

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' meaning 'not' and 'hindered', from Old English 'hindrian' (to injure, check, repress).
