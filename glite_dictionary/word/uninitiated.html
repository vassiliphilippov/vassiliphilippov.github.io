uninitiated

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪˈnɪʃieɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪˈnɪʃieɪtɪd / (BrE)
having no experience or knowledge of a particular subject or activity.
lacking experience or knowledge

Examples

To the uninitiated, the rules of cricket can seem very complicated.
The chef took time to explain the culinary terms to the uninitiated students.
For many uninitiated visitors, the local customs were completely unfamiliar.

Synset Examples

it seemed a bizarre ceremony to uninitiated western eyes
he took part in the experiment as a naive subject

Related Terms

  • Antonym: experienced - having knowledge or skill in a particular area
  • Synonym: novice - a person new to and inexperienced in a job or situation

Etymology

From Latin 'initiare' (to begin), with 'un-' meaning 'not'.
