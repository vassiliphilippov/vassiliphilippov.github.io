meloid

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːlɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈmiːlɔɪd / (BrE)
a beetle of a family whose members produce a secretion that can blister the skin
blister beetle; secretes skin-blistering substance

Examples

The meloid secretes a chemical that creates blisters when it comes into contact with skin.
Researchers collected several specimens of meloid for their toxicity study.
Handling a meloid without gloves can result in painful skin blisters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blister beetle - a beetle that secretes a substance causing blisters on skin

Etymology

From Modern Latin Meloidae, the family name, derived from Greek mē̂lon (apple, later also 'limb'), possibly in reference to swelling caused by blisters.
