Hemiprocnidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛməˈprɑksɪˌniːdi / (AmE), / ˌhɛməˈprɒksɪniːdi / (BrE)
A family of birds known as tree swifts, native to Southeast Asia and Australasia, characterized by their swift-like appearance and arboreal habits.
family of tree swifts (birds)

Examples

The Hemiprocnidae are commonly found in forested regions of Southeast Asia.
Ornithologists study Hemiprocnidae to understand their unique aerial feeding behaviors.
Unlike true swifts, members of the Hemiprocnidae family have soft plumage and long forked tails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hemiprocne - genus of tree swifts within the family Hemiprocnidae

Etymology

From New Latin Hemiprocnidae, based on genus Hemiprocne.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɛmɪˌprɒkˈnɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌhɛmɪˌprɒkˈnɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of birds known as tree swifts, which are related to swifts and found mainly in South and Southeast Asia and Australasia
family of tree swifts (birds)

Examples

The Hemiprocnidae are a family of aerial birds found in Southeast Asia and Australasia.
Unlike true swifts, members of the Hemiprocnidae often perch on branches.
Scientists studied the nesting habits of the Hemiprocnidae to learn more about their evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree swift - a bird of the family Hemiprocnidae, related to true swifts
  • Antonym: swift - a bird of the family Apodidae, similar in appearance and habits to tree swifts

Etymology

From New Latin Hemiprocnidae, from the type genus Hemiprocne + -idae (taxonomic family suffix).
