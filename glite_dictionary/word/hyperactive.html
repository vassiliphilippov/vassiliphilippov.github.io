hyperactive

1. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪpərˈæktɪv / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpərˈaktɪv / (BrE)
very active, especially more active than is usual or desirable, often to an excessive degree.
excessively active

Examples

The teacher found it difficult to control the hyperactive students during the field trip.
After eating too much candy, Ben became unusually hyperactive and couldn't sit still.
Doctors often observe hyperactive behavior in children with ADHD.

Synset Examples

a hyperactive child

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inactive - not active; lacking activity or movement
  • Synonym: energetic - having a lot of energy and enthusiasm

Etymology

From hyper- (meaning 'over, excessive') + active.
