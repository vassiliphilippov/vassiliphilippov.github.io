capibara

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæpɪˈbɑrə / (AmE), / ˌkæpɪˈbɑːrə / (BrE)
a large, pig-sized, tailless rodent native to South America, living near water and known as the largest living rodent
large South American aquatic rodent

Examples

A capibara can weigh up to 65 kilograms, making it the largest rodent in the world.
The zoo recently welcomed a pair of capibaras to its South American animal exhibit.
Capibara are highly social animals and often live in groups near rivers and lakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capybara - large South American aquatic rodent; alternative spelling

Etymology

From Spanish 'carpincho' or Portuguese 'capivara', from Tupi 'kapiʼiwara' meaning 'grass eater'.
