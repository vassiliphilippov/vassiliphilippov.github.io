Wilbur Wright

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪlbɚ ˈraɪt / (AmE), / ˈwɪlbə ˈraɪt / (BrE)
(1867–1912) A United States aviation pioneer who, with his brother Orville Wright, invented and built the first successful airplane.
American aviation pioneer, co-inventor of the airplane

Examples

Many historians regard Wilbur Wright as one of the founders of modern aviation.
Together with his brother, Wilbur Wright achieved the first powered flight in 1903.
In museums around the world, exhibits honor the achievements of Wilbur Wright and his brother.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Orville Wright - Wilbur Wright's brother and co-inventor of the first airplane
