jester

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛstər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛstə / (BrE)
a professional entertainer, typically in the Middle Ages, who amused a king or nobleman by telling jokes, performing tricks, or acting the fool
court entertainer in the Middle Ages

Examples

The king's jester entertained the whole court with his clever jokes and acrobatics.
At the medieval fair, an actor played the jester, delighting children with silly antics.
Every evening, the jester was called upon to make the queen laugh after supper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clown - a comic performer who entertains people, especially in a circus

Etymology

From Middle English 'gestour' meaning 'storyteller, buffoon', from Anglo-Norman 'gestour', from Latin 'gesta' (deeds, exploits).
