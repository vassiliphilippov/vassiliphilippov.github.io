trefoil

1. NOUN

/ ˈtri.fɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈtriː.fɔɪl / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Trifolium, typically having compound leaves with three leaflets; commonly called clover.
plant of the genus Trifolium (clover)

Examples

The field was dotted with blooming trefoil and other wildflowers.
Bee populations thrive where trefoil plants are abundant.
She recognized the distinctive three-leaf pattern of a trefoil among the grasses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clover - a plant of the genus Trifolium

Etymology

From Old French 'trifoil', from Latin 'trifolium' (three-leaved plant).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtriːˌfɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈtriːfɔɪl / (BrE)
a plant of the pea family, especially of the genus Medicago or Trifolium, having compound leaves with three leaflets
plant with three-part leaves

Examples

The field was dotted with wild trefoil, its tiny yellow flowers bright in the sunlight.
Gardeners often plant trefoil to improve soil fertility due to its nitrogen-fixing properties.
He recognized the plant by its distinctive three-lobed trefoil leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clover - a small plant of the genus Trifolium, related to trefoil

Etymology

From Latin trifolium, from tri- (three) + folium (leaf).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtriːˌfɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈtriːfɔɪl / (BrE)
an architectural ornament consisting of three rounded lobes or arcs arranged in a circle, commonly used in Gothic architecture
architectural ornament of three arcs

Examples

The church windows were decorated with a trefoil pattern above the arches.
A carved trefoil can be seen at the top of each column in the cloister.
Medieval architects often used the trefoil as a symbol of the Trinity in their designs.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: quatrefoil - an ornamental design of four lobes

Etymology

From Latin *trifolium* (three-leaved plant), reflecting the shape's similarity to a clover leaf.
