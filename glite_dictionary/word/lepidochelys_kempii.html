Lepidochelys kempii

1. NOUN

/ lɛpɪˈdoʊkəlɪs ˈkɛm.pi.aɪ / (AmE), / lɛpɪˈdəʊkəlɪs ˈkɛm.pi.aɪ / (BrE)
a species of sea turtle found mainly in the Atlantic and Gulf Coasts of North America, also known as the Kemp's ridley sea turtle
a species of sea turtle (Kemp's ridley)

Examples

Researchers are working to protect the endangered Lepidochelys kempii along the Texas coastline.
The Lepidochelys kempii is known for its unique mass nesting events called arribadas.
Conservation efforts have focused on increasing the numbers of Lepidochelys kempii hatchlings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kemp's ridley - common name for Lepidochelys kempii, a small sea turtle found in North America

Etymology

From Greek lepis (scale) + chelys (turtle); species named after Richard M. Kemp, who helped discover it.
