southeaster

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaʊθˈistər / (AmE), / ˌsaʊθˈiːstə / (BrE)
a strong wind blowing from the southeast direction
strong wind from the southeast

Examples

A powerful southeaster battered the coastline overnight, causing widespread damage.
Fishermen avoided going out to sea when a southeaster was forecast.
The trees bent under the force of the southeaster sweeping through the valley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nor'easter - a strong wind from the northeast
