Centropomus

1. NOUN

/ sɛnˈtrɒpəməs / (AmE), / sɛnˈtrɒpəməs / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the family Centropomidae, commonly known as snooks, found in warm coastal waters of the Americas.
genus of snook fish

Examples

The Centropomus genus includes several species popular among recreational anglers.
Scientists collected samples of Centropomus to study their migration patterns.
One of the best-known members of the Centropomus genus is the common snook.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snook - a common name for fish in the Centropomus genus

Etymology

From Greek kentron (spur) + pōma (cover, lid), referring to certain anatomical features.
