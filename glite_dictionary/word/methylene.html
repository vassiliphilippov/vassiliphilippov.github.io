methylene

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛθ.əˌlin / (AmE), / ˈmɛθ.ɪliːn / (BrE)
a bivalent chemical radical (CH₂) derived from methane, occurring in many organic compounds
bivalent chemical radical CH₂
medical

Examples

The compound contains a methylene group attached to the central ring.
In organic chemistry, methylene bridges play a significant role in molecular structure.
Under UV light, the reactivity of methylene increases rapidly.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: methane - a colorless, odorless flammable gas (CH₄)

Etymology

From French méthylène, from Greek methy (wine) + hylē (wood), originally referring to wood alcohol.
