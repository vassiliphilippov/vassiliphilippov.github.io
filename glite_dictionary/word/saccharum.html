Saccharum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsækəˌrəm / (AmE), / ˈsak.ə.rəm / (BrE)
A genus of tall, perennial, reedlike grasses that includes sugarcane, native to southeastern Asia and widely cultivated for sugar production.
genus of grasses including sugarcane

Examples

Saccharum is widely cultivated in tropical regions for its high sugar content.
Most commercial sugar is derived from Saccharum officinarum, commonly known as sugarcane.
Scientists study different species within the Saccharum genus to improve crop yields.

Etymology

From Latin *saccharum*, meaning 'sugar', from Greek *sakkharon* (sugar).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsækərəm / (AmE), / ˈsakərəm / (BrE)
a genus of tall, perennial, reedlike grasses native to southeastern Asia, including sugarcane and related species
genus of grasses including sugarcane

Examples

Botanists classify sugarcane under the genus Saccharum.
Researchers are studying the genetic diversity of Saccharum species to improve crop yields.
Several members of the Saccharum genus are important for agriculture and sugar production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sugarcane - tall grass cultivated for its sweet sap

Etymology

From Latin 'saccharum' meaning 'sugar', from Greek 'sákkharon'.
