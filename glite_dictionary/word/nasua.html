Nasua

1. NOUN

/ nəˈsuːə / (AmE), / nəˈsjuːə / (BrE)
A genus of mammals in the raccoon family, commonly known as coatis, native to Central and South America.
genus of mammals (coatis)

Examples

The Nasua is easily recognized by its long, flexible snout.
Researchers studied the feeding habits of several Nasua in the forest.
In zoos, Nasua are often displayed in enclosures designed to mimic their natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coati - common name for members of the genus Nasua
  • Degree Lower: raccoon - small nocturnal mammals related to Nasua

Etymology

From Latin 'nasua' meaning 'having a nose', referring to the animal's long, mobile snout.

2. NOUN

/ nəˈsuːə / (AmE), / nəˈsuːə / (BrE)
A genus of mammals in the raccoon family Procyonidae, commonly known as coatis, native to Central and South America.
genus of mammals (coatis)

Examples

Scientists classified the ring-tailed coati under the genus Nasua.
There are several species within the genus Nasua, all native to the Americas.
The genus Nasua is characterized by its members' long, flexible snouts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coati - common name for members of the genus Nasua

Etymology

From Latin 'nasua', meaning 'nosed', referring to the animal's elongated snout.
