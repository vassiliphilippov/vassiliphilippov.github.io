Cygnus columbianus columbianus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪɡ.nəs kəˌlʌm.biˈæn.əs kəˌlʌm.biˈæn.əs / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡ.nəs kəˌlʌm.biˈæn.əs kəˌlʌm.biˈæn.əs / (BrE)
A North American subspecies of the tundra swan, distinguished by its soft whistling call.
North American tundra swan subspecies

Examples

Each winter, large flocks of Cygnus columbianus columbianus migrate to wetlands in the southern United States.
Researchers tracked the movements of several Cygnus columbianus columbianus across thousands of miles.
The distinctive whistling calls of Cygnus columbianus columbianus can be heard during migration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tundra swan - A species of swan known for inhabiting arctic and subarctic regions

Etymology

From Latin: Cygnus (swan), columbianus (of the Columbia, connecting to the Columbia River, North America).
