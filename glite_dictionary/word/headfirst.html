headfirst

1. ADV

/ ˌhɛdˈfɝst / (AmE), / ˌhɛdˈfɜːst / (BrE)
With the head leading or foremost, especially when moving or falling.
with the head foremost

Examples

She dived headfirst into the icy water, ignoring the cold.
The boy tumbled headfirst down the hill after tripping on a rock.
Don't rush headfirst into big decisions without thinking them through.

Synset Examples

a headfirst plunge down the stairs
a headlong dive into the pool

Related Terms

  • Synonym: headlong - with the head leading or foremost; recklessly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00205206-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADV

/ ˌhɛdˈfɝst / (AmE), / ˌhɛdˈfɜːst / (BrE)
With the head leading before the rest of the body, especially when moving forward quickly or diving.
with the head leading before the body

Examples

She dove headfirst into the cool lake without hesitation.
The children raced headfirst down the grassy hill, laughing as they rolled.
He tripped and fell headfirst onto the pavement, but only scraped his knee.

Synset Examples

the runner slid headlong into third base

Related Terms

  • Synonym: headlong - with the head leading; in a rush

WordNet ID: oewn-00356323-r
Synset POS: r
