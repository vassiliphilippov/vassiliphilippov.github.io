Anser

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.sɚ / (AmE), / ˈæn.sə / (BrE)
A genus of geese that includes the true or typical geese species found in the Northern Hemisphere.
genus of true geese

Examples

The genus Anser includes both the greylag goose and the pink-footed goose.
Biologists have classified several species under the Anser genus.
Fossil records indicate that Anser species have existed for millions of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goose - a large waterbird with a long neck and webbed feet

Etymology

From Latin *anser* meaning 'goose.'
