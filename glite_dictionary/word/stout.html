2. NOUN

/ staʊt / (AmE), / staʊt / (BrE)
A strong, very dark, heavy-bodied ale made from pale malt, roasted unmalted barley, and often caramel malt with hops.
a strong dark beer

Examples

He ordered a pint of stout at the pub after work.
The brewery is famous for its rich chocolate stout with hints of coffee.
Dark stouts often have a creamy texture and robust flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porter - a dark style of beer developed in London, related to stout

Etymology

Probably from older use describing strong alcoholic beverages, ultimately from Old French 'estout' (proud, brave, strong).

3. ADJ

/ staʊt / (AmE), / staʊt / (BrE)
(of a person) having strong and rugged physical strength; tough and able to withstand fatigue or hardship.
having rugged physical strength

Examples

The stout sailor braved the stormy seas without hesitation.
Only stout men could carry the supplies across the rough terrain.
The team recruited several stout workers for the physically demanding job.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sturdy - strong and solidly built; robust
  • Antonym: weak - lacking physical strength or robustness

Etymology

From Old French 'estout' (bold, brave), from Frankish '*stolt' (proud, stately).
