igniter

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈnaɪtɚ / (AmE), / ɪɡˈnaɪtə / (BrE)
a substance or device used to ignite or kindle a fire
substance or device for starting a fire

Examples

The technician inserted a new igniter before testing the furnace.
Modern grills often use an electronic igniter to start the flame safely.
Without a functioning igniter, the rocket engine failed to launch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignitor - an alternative spelling for igniter; a device for starting combustion

Etymology

From ignite, from Latin ignīre (to set on fire), + -er.

2. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈnaɪtər / (AmE), / ɪɡˈnaɪtə / (BrE)
a device used to ignite fuel, charges, or fires
device for lighting fuel or fire

Examples

The technician replaced the faulty igniter in the gas stove to restore its function.
Modern rocket engines rely on an electric igniter to start combustion safely.
He always carries a spare igniter when camping in case he needs to start a fire quickly.

Synset Examples

do you have a light?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lighter - device that produces a flame for lighting

Etymology

From Latin 'ignis' (fire) via the verb 'ignite' + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03671917-n
Synset POS: n
