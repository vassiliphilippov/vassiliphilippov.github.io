Acheron

1. PROPN

/ ˈækəˌrɒn / (AmE), / ˈækərɒn / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a river in the underworld over which the souls of the dead were ferried by Charon.
river of the underworld in Greek mythology

Examples

In the myth, the souls of the dead must cross the Acheron to enter Hades.
The poet described his despair as being lost on the banks of the Acheron.
Charon collected his coins as he rowed the boat across the Acheron.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Styx - the principal river of the underworld in Greek mythology
  • Synonym: Lethe - a river in Hades whose water causes forgetfulness

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἀχέρων (Acherōn), the name of the mythological river.

2. PROPN

/ ˈækərˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈækərɒn / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a river in the underworld (Hades) across which the souls of the dead were ferried by Charon.
mythological river of the underworld

Examples

In ancient texts, souls of the dead are carried across the Acheron by the ferryman Charon.
The poet described the Acheron as a boundary between the world of the living and the dead.
Scholars have debated whether the Acheron represents despair or hope in classical literature.

Etymology

From Greek Ἀχέρων (Acherōn), a river in the underworld in Greek mythology.
