ft-L

1. NOUN

/ fʊt ˈlæmbərt / (AmE), / fʊt ˈlæmbət / (BrE)
a former unit of luminance equal to one lumen per square foot
unit of luminance (one lumen per square foot)

Examples

The brightness of the screen was measured at 12 ft-L.
Modern cinemas often display films at about 14 foot-lamberts for optimal viewing.
He converted the reading from ft-L to candelas per square meter for the report.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candela per square meter - SI unit of luminance

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'foot-lambert', from 'foot' (unit of length) + 'Lambert' (after Johann Heinrich Lambert, physicist).
