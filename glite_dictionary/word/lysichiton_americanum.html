Lysichiton americanum

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈsɪkɪˌtɒn əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnəm / (AmE), / lɪˈsɪkɪtən əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnəm / (BrE)
A clump-forming perennial plant native to wetlands of western North America, known for its yellow spathe and foul odor.
yellow-spathed wetland plant (western North America)

Examples

The wetland was filled with Lysichiton americanum, its yellow spathes brightening the marshy landscape.
Researchers studied the pollination habits of Lysichiton americanum in its native habitat.
Gardeners in temperate regions sometimes cultivate Lysichiton americanum for its unusual appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Symplocarpus foetidus - A similar plant known as eastern skunk cabbage, found in eastern North America

Etymology

From Greek *lysis* (loosening) + *chiton* (tunic) and Latin *americanum*, referring to its North American origin.
