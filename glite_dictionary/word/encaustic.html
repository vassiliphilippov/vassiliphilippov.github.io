encaustic

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈkɔːstɪk / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɔːstɪk / (BrE)
a paint made of pigment mixed with melted beeswax, which is fixed with heat after being applied to a surface
pigment mixed with beeswax, fixed with heat

Examples

The artist used encaustic to create a richly textured surface on her painting.
Ancient Greeks often decorated sculptures and ships with encaustic for its durability and vibrant color.
Learning to work with encaustic requires special tools to safely heat and apply the beeswax-based paint.

Etymology

From Greek enkaustikos, from enkaiein (to burn in), referring to the process of fixing the paint with heat.
