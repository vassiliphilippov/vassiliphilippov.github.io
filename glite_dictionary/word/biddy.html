biddy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪdi / (AmE), / ˈbɪdi / (BrE)
an adult female chicken; a hen, especially in informal or rural contexts
adult female chicken (hen)

Examples

The farmer has a dozen biddies clucking in the yard every morning.
She gathered fresh eggs from the biddy in the coop.
Old timers used to keep a biddy or two for eggs and company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hen - an adult female chicken

Etymology

Probably from the name 'Biddy,' a diminutive of 'Bridget,' often used informally for chickens in Ireland and America.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɪdi / (AmE), / ˈbɪdi / (BrE)
a young bird, especially a young chicken or domestic fowl
young chicken or domestic bird

Examples

The children played in the yard, chasing after a little biddy that had wandered from the coop.
Grandma showed me how to feed the biddies in the morning.
A tiny yellow biddy hopped onto the fence, peeping loudly for attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chick - a young chicken

Etymology

Probably from a child's call to chickens, such as 'here, biddy!'.
