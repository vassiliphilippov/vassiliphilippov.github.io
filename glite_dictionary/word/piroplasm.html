piroplasm

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪroʊˌplæzəm / (AmE), / ˈpɪərəˌplazəm / (BrE)
a minute protozoan parasite that infects red blood cells of mammals and is transmitted by ticks, causing diseases, especially in domestic animals
a blood parasite transmitted by ticks
medical

Examples

Veterinarians diagnosed the cow with a piroplasm infection after observing symptoms of anemia.
The tick was identified as the carrier of the piroplasm responsible for the outbreak among the herd.
Control of piroplasm transmission is crucial for the health of domestic livestock in affected regions.

Etymology

From Greek 'peiros' (pear, referring to shape) + 'plasma' (something formed or molded).
