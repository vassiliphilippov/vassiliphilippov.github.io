amphictyony

1. NOUN

/ æmˈfɪkti.əˌni / (AmE), / amˈfɪktɪəni / (BrE)
an association of neighboring states or tribes in ancient Greece, especially created to defend and manage a shared religious center
ancient Greek religious association of states

Examples

The Delphic amphictyony played a significant role in maintaining peace among the Greek city-states.
Membership in the amphictyony granted states influence over religious festivals and oracles.
Scholars often reference the amphictyony to explain the origins of some early federal systems.

Etymology

From Greek amphiktyonia (association of neighbors), from amphi- (around) + ktyones (neighbors).
