Nantua

1. NOUN

/ nænˈtuː.ə / (AmE), / nænˈtjuː.ə / (BrE)
A white sauce made with whipping cream and shrimp butter, typically used in French cuisine.
white sauce with cream and shrimp butter

Examples

The chef prepared a delicate Nantua to accompany the poached fish.
Lobster ravioli is often served with a rich Nantua for added flavor.
She learned how to make Nantua during her culinary studies in France.

Etymology

From French, named after the town of Nantua in eastern France, known for its crayfish dishes.
