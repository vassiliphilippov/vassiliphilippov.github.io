polestar

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊlˌstɑr / (AmE), / ˈpəʊlˌstɑː / (BrE)
the brightest star in the constellation Ursa Minor, known as the North Star, toward which the Earth's northern axis points
the North Star; brightest in Ursa Minor

Examples

For centuries, sailors have navigated by the polestar to determine their course at night.
The polestar appears almost stationary in the sky while other stars rotate around it.
Many cultures have myths and legends about the significance of the polestar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: North Star - another term for Polaris, the prominent star near the north celestial pole
  • Synonym: Polaris - the scientific name for the polestar

Etymology

From Middle English pol(e)starre, combining 'pole' (as in celestial pole) and 'star'.
