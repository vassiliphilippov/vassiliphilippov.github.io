to monopolise

1. VERB

/ məˈnɑːpəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / məˈnɒpəlaɪz / (BrE)
to have and control something fully and exclusively, preventing others from sharing or participating
have and control exclusively
british

Examples

He tends to monopolise the conversation whenever we have meetings.
Large corporations often try to monopolise entire markets, limiting competition.
She monopolised the chessboard, leaving her opponent with no moves.

Synset Examples

He monopolizes the laser printer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to monopolize - to have and control fully and exclusively (US spelling)
  • Antonym: share - to have or use something jointly with others

Etymology

From Greek 'monopolion' (exclusive sale), via Latin 'monopolium' and French 'monopole'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02447411-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ məˈnɑːpəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / məˈnɒpəlaɪz / (BrE)
to obtain or maintain exclusive control over a commodity, service, or market
obtain exclusive control over something
british

Examples

Large corporations often try to monopolise entire industries to maximize profits.
The telecommunications company was accused of attempting to monopolise the market.
By purchasing smaller competitors, the firm hoped to monopolise cable TV services in the region.

Synset Examples

OPEC wants to monopolize oil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monopoly - exclusive control over the supply of a commodity or service
  • Antonym: to compete - to strive to gain or win something by defeating others

Etymology

From French 'monopoliser', from 'monopole' (monopoly), from Greek 'monopōlion'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02209024-v
Synset POS: v
