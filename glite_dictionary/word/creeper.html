creeper

1. NOUN

/ ˈkripər / (AmE), / ˈkriːpə / (BrE)
a person who moves slowly and close to the ground, typically by crawling or creeping
person who crawls along the ground

Examples

The toddler was a fast creeper before he learned to walk.
During the game, the spy acted as a creeper, moving stealthily beneath the windows.
A skilled creeper can approach without making a sound.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkriːpɚ / (AmE), / ˈkriːpə / (BrE)
any of several small insect-eating birds of the Northern Hemisphere that climb tree trunks using their stiff tail feathers and feet for support.
a small tree-climbing insectivorous bird

Examples

The brown creeper can be seen scaling the bark of an old pine tree in search of insects.
Naturalists observed how the creeper uses its stiff tail to balance while climbing.
Several creepers were spotted darting up and down the trunks in the snowy forest.

Etymology

From creep + -er, referring to the bird's climbing habit.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkripər / (AmE), / ˈkriːpə / (BrE)
a plant that grows along the ground, up walls, or over other surfaces by extending stems or runners
a plant that grows by creeping

Examples

The creeper quickly covered the old stone wall with its lush green leaves.
English ivy is a common creeper found in many gardens and parks.
We planted a creeper along the fence to add some natural beauty to the yard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: climber - a plant that climbs upward by clinging or twining

Etymology

From the verb 'creep' + -er, meaning one that creeps.
