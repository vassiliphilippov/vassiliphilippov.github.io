Kinetoscope

1. NOUN

/ kɪˈnɛtəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / kɪˈnɛtəsəʊp / (BrE)
an early motion picture device invented by Thomas Edison, which allowed a single viewer to watch a continuous loop of film giving the impression of movement
early motion picture viewing device

Examples

The Kinetoscope allowed only one person at a time to view the moving images.
Edison's invention of the Kinetoscope marked a turning point in the history of cinema.
Many people in the 1890s paid to watch short films on a Kinetoscope in arcades.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: movie projector - a device for projecting motion pictures onto a screen

Etymology

From Greek 'kinetos' (moving) + 'scope' (to look, view).
