battlemented

1. ADJ

/ ˈbæt.əlˌmɛn.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əl.mənt.ɪd / (BrE)
having battlements or resembling the repeated square indentations found in battlements
having battlements or resembling them

Examples

The ancient castle stood tall and battlemented against the evening sky.
A battlemented parapet ran along the edge of the old city's walls.
The architect designed a battlemented roof to give the building a medieval appearance.

Synset Examples

a crenelated molding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crenellated - having battlements in the form of notched parapets

Etymology

From 'battlement' + '-ed', used to form adjectives meaning 'provided with' or 'having'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbæt.əlˌmɛnt.ɪd / (AmE), / ˈbætl̩məntɪd / (BrE)
(of a building or structure) having battlements or parapets with regular indentations for defense or decoration.
having battlements; crenellated

Examples

The castle's battlemented walls rose high above the surrounding countryside.
They restored the old battlemented tower to its former medieval glory.
A battlemented parapet ran along the rooftop, adding to the building's fortress-like appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crenellated - having a notched top, like battlements, for defense

Etymology

From 'battlement' + '-ed', indicating equipped with battlements; ultimately from Old French 'bataillement'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01891481-s
Synset POS: s
