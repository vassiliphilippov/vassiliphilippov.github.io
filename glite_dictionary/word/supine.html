supine

1. ADJ

/ suˈpaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsuːpaɪn / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) lying on the back with the face upward.
lying face upward

Examples

The patient was placed in a supine position for the examination.
She lay supine on the grass, watching clouds drift across the sky.
After the accident, he remained supine until help arrived.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prone - lying face downward

Etymology

From Latin 'supinus' (lying on the back, turned upward).

2. ADJ

/ suːˈpaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsuːpaɪn / (BrE)
(of a person or their attitude) failing to act or protest as a result of moral weakness or indifference; passive, especially through laziness or indifference.
passively indifferent; inert due to laziness

Examples

The administration was criticized for its supine response to the crisis, failing to act when action was needed.
He remained supine in the face of injustice, refusing to raise his voice or take a stand.
Their supine attitude toward change frustrated the more proactive members of the team.

Synset Examples

No other colony showed such supine, selfish helplessness in allowing her own border citizens to be mercilessly harried

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passive - accepting or allowing what happens without active response
  • Synonym: indolent - wanting to avoid activity or exertion; lazy
  • Antonym: active - engaging or ready to engage in physically energetic pursuits

Etymology

From Latin supinus (lying on the back; inactive, indolent).

Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ suˈpaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsuːpaɪn / (BrE)
(of a person) lying flat on one’s back, facing upwards.
lying on one's back, face upward

Examples

For this exercise, you should supine on the mat and keep your arms relaxed.
Medical staff examined the patient while he was supine.
After a long day, I like to lie supine and watch the clouds drift overhead.

Synset Examples

The yoga pose required her to be supine for relaxation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prone - lying flat with the front of the body downward

Etymology

From Latin supinus (bent backward, lying on the back).
