Urtica

1. NOUN

/ ɚˈtaɪkə / (AmE), / ɜːˈtiːkə / (BrE)
A genus of nettle plants whose fibers resemble flax and can be used for making textiles or rope.
genus of nettle plants yielding fiber

Examples

Fibers extracted from Urtica have been traditionally used to make textiles in several cultures.
The stems of Urtica can be processed to obtain a strong, linen-like fiber.
Researchers are exploring the potential of Urtica as an eco-friendly alternative to conventional fiber crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nettle - any of various plants typically with stinging hairs

Etymology

From Latin 'urtica' (nettle).

2. NOUN

/ ɜːrˈtiːkə / (AmE), / ɜːˈtɪkə / (BrE)
a genus of plants in the nettle family, some species of which yield fibers resembling flax
genus of nettle plants

Examples

Several species within Urtica are used to produce fiber for textiles.
The leaves of Urtica are covered with tiny stinging hairs.
Researchers studied the potential of Urtica fiber as an alternative to cotton.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nettle - a herbaceous plant with stinging hairs

Etymology

From Latin *urtica* meaning ‘nettle’.
