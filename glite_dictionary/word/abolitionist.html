abolitionist

1. NOUN

/ ˌæbəˈlɪʃənɪst / (AmE), / ˌabəˈlɪʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or works for the abolition of slavery, especially in the 18th and 19th centuries
person opposed to slavery

Examples

Frederick Douglass was a famous abolitionist who escaped from slavery and advocated for its end.
Many abolitionists risked their lives to help enslaved people escape to freedom on the Underground Railroad.
The abolitionist movement gained momentum in the decades leading up to the American Civil War.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anti-slavery campaigner - someone who campaigns against slavery
  • Antonym: pro-slavery - supporting the continuation of slavery

Etymology

From abolition + -ist, modeled after similar 19th-century coinages.
