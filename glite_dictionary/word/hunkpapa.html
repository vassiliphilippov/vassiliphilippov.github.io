Hunkpapa

1. NOUN

/ hʌŋˈkɑːpə / (AmE), / hʌŋˈkɑːpə / (BrE)
a Siouan language traditionally spoken by the Hunkpapa, a subgroup of the Lakota people
a Siouan language of the Hunkpapa people

Examples

The Hunkpapa language is an important part of Lakota cultural heritage.
Researchers have compiled dictionaries to help preserve Hunkpapa for future generations.
She is learning to speak Hunkpapa from her grandparents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lakota - the Siouan language of the Lakota people

Etymology

From Lakota Húŋkpapȟa, the name of the tribe and their language.

2. NOUN

/ hʌŋˈpɑːpə / (AmE), / hʌŋˈpɑːpə / (BrE)
A member of a division of the Teton Sioux people, originally living in the western Dakotas, known for resisting white expansion into the northern Great Plains.
a division of the Teton Sioux people

Examples

The Hunkpapa played a major role in the Battle of the Little Bighorn.
Sitting Bull was one of the most famous leaders of the Hunkpapa.
Many Hunkpapa descendants continue to live in the Dakotas today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sioux - a group of Native American peoples and their language family

Etymology

From the Lakota term 'Húŋkpapȟa', meaning 'head of the circle'.
