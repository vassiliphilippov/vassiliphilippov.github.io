fumeroot

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːməˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈfjuːməˌruːt / (BrE)
A herbaceous plant of northern Europe and Asia, known for its erect clusters of red flowers, belonging to the genus Fumaria.
herb with red flower clusters in Europe/Asia

Examples

The garden was dotted with fumeroot, its red blooms adding a delicate touch amongst the greenery.
Botanists in northern Europe frequently study fumeroot for its unique floral structure.
In traditional medicine, extracts from fumeroot were once believed to have cleansing properties.

Etymology

From Latin 'fumus terrae' meaning 'smoke of the earth', referring to the plant's smoky appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːmɚˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈfjuːməˌruːt / (BrE)
A delicate European herb with grey-green leaves and spikes of purplish flowers, formerly used for medicinal purposes.
a European herb with purplish flowers

Examples

The herbalist recommended a tea made from fumeroot to soothe minor ailments.
In spring, the delicate purple flowers of fumeroot brighten the woodland floor.
Historically, fumeroot was valued for its supposed medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fumitory - an alternative name for fumeroot, referring to the same plant species

Etymology

From 'fume' plus 'root', referring to the plant's smoky scent and its root.
