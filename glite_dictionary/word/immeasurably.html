immeasurably

1. ADV

/ ɪˈmɛʒərəbli / (AmE), / ɪˈmɛʒərəbli / (BrE)
To a degree that is too great to be measured; extremely.
to an extent impossible to measure

Examples

The team's performance improved immeasurably after hiring a new coach.
She was immeasurably grateful for their support during difficult times.
Living abroad has immeasurably broadened his perspective on life.

Synset Examples

he is infinitely wealthy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infinitely - to an unlimited or incalculable degree

Etymology

From 'immeasurable' + '-ly'; 'immeasurable' from Latin 'in-' (not) + 'mensurabilis' (measurable).

2. ADV

/ ɪˈmɛʒɚəbli / (AmE), / ɪˈmɛʒərəbli / (BrE)
To an extent that is too great to be measured; extremely or infinitely.
to an inestimable or boundless degree

Examples

Her kindness improved my life immeasurably over the years.
The loss of his mentor affected him immeasurably more than he showed.
Technology has advanced immeasurably compared to a century ago.

Synset Examples

the war left him immeasurably fearful of what man can do to man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infinitely - to an infinite or countless degree

Etymology

From 'immeasurable' + '-ly'; 'immeasurable' from Latin 'in-' (not) + 'mensurabilis' (that can be measured).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00399919-r
Synset POS: r
