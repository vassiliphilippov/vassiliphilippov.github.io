Fulah

1. NOUN

/ ˈfuːlə / (AmE), / ˈfuːlə / (BrE)
A member of a pastoral and nomadic ethnic group of western Africa, traditionally cattle herders and predominantly of Muslim faith.
a West African nomadic ethnic group

Examples

The Fulah are well known for their distinctive woven textiles and nomadic lifestyle.
A group of Fulah cattle herders traveled across the savannah in search of better grazing land.
Many Fulah communities maintain traditional customs and speak the Fulfulde language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fulani - an alternative name for the Fulah people
  • Synonym: Hausa - an ethnic group in West Africa, often neighbors of the Fulah

Etymology

From Fulfulde, the language spoken by the Fulah people; also known as Fulani or Peul.
