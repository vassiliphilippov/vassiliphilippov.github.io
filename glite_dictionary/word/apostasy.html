apostasy

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑːstəˌsi / (AmE), / əˈpɒstəsi / (BrE)
the act of formally abandoning or renouncing a religious faith, political party, or principle, especially for cause
the act of abandoning faith, party, or principle

Examples

Her apostasy from the party shocked her colleagues and supporters.
After years of loyalty, his apostasy was seen as a significant betrayal.
Political apostasy is often driven by deep disagreements or ideological rifts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conversion - the act of adopting a new faith or belief
  • Antonym: loyalty - faithfulness to a cause or party

Etymology

From Greek 'apostasia' (defection, revolt) via Late Latin 'apostasia'.

2. NOUN

/ əˈpɑːstəsi / (AmE), / əˈpɒstəsi / (BrE)
the act of abandoning or renouncing a religious faith, a cause, or a strongly held belief, often in favor of opposing views
renunciation of a religious faith or belief

Examples

After his apostasy, he was shunned by members of his former church.
Her public apostasy from the party shocked many of her longtime supporters.
Throughout history, apostasy has often been met with severe social consequences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heresy - belief or opinion that differs from established religious doctrine
  • Antonym: conversion - the process of changing or causing something to change from one form to another (opposite of apostasy in a religious context)

Etymology

From Late Latin apostasia, from Greek apostasia (defection, revolt).
