rancid

1. ADJ

/ ˈræn.sɪd / (AmE), / ˈran.sɪd / (BrE)
Having an unpleasant, stale, or sour smell or taste, especially as a result of decomposition in fats or oils.
having an unpleasant stale smell or taste (like old fats)

Examples

The rancid smell coming from the trash can made it hard to enter the kitchen.
He threw out the rancid oil after noticing its sharp and unpleasant odor.
I can't eat this butter; it's gone rancid and tastes sour.

Synset Examples

rancid butter
rancid bacon

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fresh - recently made or obtained; not stale or spoiled

Etymology

From Latin 'rancidus' (stinking, rank).

2. ADJ

/ ˈræn.sɪd / (AmE), / ˈræn.sɪd / (BrE)
(of food or fat) smelling or tasting unpleasant as a result of being old and stale, especially due to decomposition or fermentation.
having an unpleasant stale or sour smell or taste

Examples

The rancid odor from the butter made it clear it had gone bad.
He opened the container and was met with a rancid smell.
Throw away any oil that has become rancid, as it can ruin the flavor of your food.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fresh - recently made or obtained; not stale or spoiled

Etymology

From Latin 'rancidus' (stinking, rank), from 'rancere' (to stink).
