brief

1. ADJ

/ briːf / (AmE), / briːf / (BrE)
using few words; concise and succinct in expression or duration.
concise; succinct

Examples

Her explanation was remarkably brief yet conveyed all the essential information.
Please keep your comments brief due to limited time.
The meeting ended early because the agenda was quite brief.

Synset Examples

covered the matter in a brief statement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concise - giving a lot of information clearly and in a few words
  • Antonym: lengthy - taking a great deal of time or many words

Etymology

From Latin brevis (short).
