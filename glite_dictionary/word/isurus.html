Isurus

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.sə.rəs / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.sjʊ.rəs / (BrE)
A genus of large, fast-moving predatory sharks known as mako sharks, found in warm seas.
genus of mako sharks

Examples

The genus Isurus includes both the shortfin and longfin mako sharks.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Isurus genus off the coast of Chile.
The sleek shape of Isurus species makes them some of the fastest sharks in the ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mako shark - a fast-swimming shark of the genus Isurus

Etymology

From Greek isos (equal) + oura (tail), referring to the shape of the shark's tail fin.
