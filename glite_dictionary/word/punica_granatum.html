Punica granatum

1. NOUN

/ ˈpjuː.nɪ.kə ɡrəˈneɪ.təm / (AmE), / ˈpjuː.nɪ.kə ɡræˈnɑː.təm / (BrE)
The scientific name for the pomegranate, a shrub or small tree native to southwestern Asia, known for its large red fruit filled with many seeds.
scientific name for the pomegranate plant

Examples

Botanists classify the pomegranate as Punica granatum in scientific literature.
Many gardens feature Punica granatum for both its ornamental flowers and edible fruit.
The genus Punica granatum has long been cultivated for its tasty and nutritious seeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pomegranate - A fruit-bearing shrub or tree with large red fruit full of seeds

Etymology

From Latin: 'Punica' (referring to Carthage) and 'granatum' (seeded or containing grains).
