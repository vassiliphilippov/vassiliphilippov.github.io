KGB

1. PROPN

/ keɪ dʒiː ˈbiː / (AmE), / keɪ dʒiː ˈbiː / (BrE)
the main security and intelligence agency of the Soviet Union, responsible for state security, intelligence, and secret police activities until 1991
Soviet Union security and intelligence agency

Examples

During the Cold War, the KGB played a major role in intelligence gathering and espionage.
He was suspected of being an agent of the KGB during his years in Moscow.
Many documents relating to dissidents were kept secret by the KGB.

Etymology

From Russian abbreviation for 'Komitet Gosudarstvennoy Bezopasnosti' (Committee for State Security).
