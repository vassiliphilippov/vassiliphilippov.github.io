jab

1. NOUN

/ dʒæb / (AmE), / dʒæb / (BrE)
a sudden push or poke with a finger or elbow
a sudden poke with finger or elbow

Examples

He gave his friend a playful jab with his elbow.
She felt a sharp jab in her side during the crowded train ride.
A sudden jab from his finger made everyone laugh.

Synset Examples

she gave me a sharp dig in the ribs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poke - a quick, sharp push with something pointed
  • Synonym: dig - a quick, hard push with the finger or elbow
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ dʒæb / (AmE), / dʒæb / (BrE)
to poke or hit something or someone with a quick, sudden movement, especially with a sharp or pointed object or with a fist
to poke or hit sharply or quickly

Examples

The doctor jabbed the needle into his arm quickly.
She jabbed her finger at the map to show the location.
He kept jabbing at the punching bag to improve his speed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poke - to push or prod with a finger or object
  • Synonym: punch - to strike with a closed fist

/ dʒæb / (AmE), / dʒab / (BrE)
to poke or thrust something quickly and abruptly, especially with a sharp or sudden motion
to poke or thrust suddenly

Examples

She jabbed the elevator button impatiently.
He jabbed his umbrella at the dog to scare it away.
The nurse jabbed the needle into his arm quickly.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poke - to push or prod with a finger or sharp object
  • Degree Higher: stab - to pierce or wound with a pointed object, especially with a quick motion
