countersign

1. VERB

/ ˈkaʊntərˌsaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəsaɪn / (BrE)
to add one’s signature to a document after another person’s signature to confirm its authenticity or validity
sign a document after another as confirmation

Examples

The manager will countersign the check before it is issued.
All orders above this amount must be countersigned by a supervisor.
Please countersign here to verify you have reviewed the agreement.

Synset Examples

You must countersign on this line of the contract

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sign - to write one's name on a document

Etymology

From counter- (against, in return) + sign, influenced by French 'contresigner'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00999477-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntɚˌsaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌsaɪn / (BrE)
a second signature added to a document to confirm or endorse it after the initial signature
confirming signature on a document

Examples

The manager's countersign was required before the contract could be finalized.
Without the necessary countersign, the document was considered incomplete by the auditors.
Every disbursement over a certain amount needs a countersign from the finance office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signature - a person's name written as confirmation or approval

Etymology

From French 'contresigner', from Latin 'contra-' (against) + 'signare' (to sign).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06416663-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntərˌsaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəsaɪn / (BrE)
a secret word or phrase used to identify or authenticate a member of a group, especially as a means of security or access control
secret word or phrase for authentication

Examples

The guard asked for the countersign before allowing us to enter the camp.
Every night the countersign changes to prevent unauthorized entry.
Without the correct countersign, the message could not be delivered to headquarters.

Synset Examples

he forgot the password

Related Terms

  • Synonym: password - a secret word or phrase for authentication, especially for access to a computer system
  • Synonym: challenge - a question or demand for identification, especially in security contexts

Etymology

From counter- (in response) + sign (an identifying gesture or signal); originally from French 'contresigne'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06686933-n
Synset POS: n
