Ojibwa

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈdʒɪbwɑ / (AmE), / əʊˈdʒɪbwə / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people originally from the area around Lake Superior, extending from Canada into the northern United States
Native American people of the Great Lakes region

Examples

The Ojibwa have a rich tradition of storytelling and music.
Many Ojibwa live in communities throughout Canada and the northern United States.
He is studying the language and culture of the Ojibwa for his anthropology research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chippewa - alternative name for the Ojibwa people

Etymology

From Ojibwa *ojiibwe*, their name for themselves.

2. NOUN

/ oʊˈdʒɪbwɑː / (AmE), / əʊˈdʒɪbwə / (BrE)
the Algonquian language spoken by the Ojibwa people of North America
an Algonquian language of the Ojibwa

Examples

She is learning to speak Ojibwa from her grandparents.
Several schools now offer courses in Ojibwa to preserve the language.
The Ojibwa language has beautiful words for natural features.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chippewa - another name for the Ojibwa language or its speakers

Etymology

From Ojibwa *Ojibwe*, the autonym of the people and their language.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06924142-n
Synset POS: n
