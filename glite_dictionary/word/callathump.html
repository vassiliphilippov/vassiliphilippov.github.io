callathump

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.əˌθʌmp / (AmE), / ˈkæl.ə.θʌmp / (BrE)
a noisy, boisterous parade or procession, often accompanied by the clanging of pots, pans, or improvised instruments
noisy, boisterous parade

Examples

The village children organized a lively callathump to celebrate the festival.
We could hear the noise of the callathump echoing down the street as pots and pans clanged together.
Every summer, the town hosts a callathump that brings everyone out to join the fun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parade - a public procession, typically celebrating a special day or event
  • Synonym: procession - an organized group moving forward, especially as part of a ceremony

Etymology

Perhaps a blend of 'callithumpian' and 'thump'; 'callithumpian' originally referred to boisterous processions with discordant noise.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.əˌθʌmp / (AmE), / ˈkæl.əˌθʌmp / (BrE)
A noisy, mock serenade performed by banging pans, kettles, and other objects, typically to celebrate and playfully disturb a newly married couple.
noisy mock serenade for newlyweds

Examples

After the wedding, the neighbors gathered outside for a loud callathump that lasted well into the night.
The startled couple laughed as pots and pans clanged during the traditional callathump.
Their first night as newlyweds was interrupted by a merry callathump from friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charivari - a noisy mock serenade, especially for newlyweds

Etymology

Possibly from dialect or alteration of 'calathumpian,' origin uncertain.
