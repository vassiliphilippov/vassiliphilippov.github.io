epidemic

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈdɛmɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdɛmɪk / (BrE)
A widespread occurrence of an infectious disease in a community at a particular time.
widespread occurrence of disease

Examples

The flu epidemic overwhelmed local hospitals that winter.
Health officials worked together to track the source of the epidemic.
During the cholera epidemic, clean water became a scarce resource.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: pandemic - an outbreak of disease over a whole country or the world
  • Degree Lower: outbreak - a sudden start of something unwelcome, especially disease

Etymology

From Greek *epidēmia* (prevalence of disease), from *epi* (upon) + *dēmos* (people).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɛpəˈdɛmɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdɛmɪk / (BrE)
(of a disease or of something resembling a disease) affecting many people in a community at the same time.
affecting many at once; rapidly spreading

Examples

The city faced an epidemic crisis of measles among young children.
Rumors became epidemic during the political scandal.
Unemployment was almost epidemic in certain parts of the country.

Synset Examples

an epidemic outbreak of influenza

Related Terms

  • Antonym: endemic - (of a disease) regularly found in a particular area or group
  • Degree Higher: pandemic - (of a disease) prevalent over a whole country or the world

Etymology

From Greek epidémos (prevalent among the people), from epi- (upon) + dēmos (people).
