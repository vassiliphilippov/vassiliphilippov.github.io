celom

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiː.ləm / (AmE), / ˈsiː.ləm / (BrE)
the main body cavity in the embryo of most animals, located within the mesoderm, that develops into major cavities such as the pleural, pericardial, and peritoneal cavities in humans
embryonic body cavity developing into major cavities
medical

Examples

During embryonic development, the celom forms within the mesoderm and later divides into several cavities.
The peritoneal cavity in adults can be traced back to the original celom of the embryo.
Scientists study the formation of the celom to understand congenital disorders affecting body cavities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coelom - alternative, more common spelling for the same anatomical cavity

Etymology

From Greek koilōma (hollow, cavity).
