alterative

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔltɚˌeɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈɔːltərətɪv / (BrE)
(archaic) tending to cure or restore health; having a restorative or healing effect, especially in older or alternative medicine.
tending to restore health; healing (archaic)
archaic

Examples

Many traditional herbalists believe certain roots have alterative properties beneficial for the liver.
The physician prescribed an alterative tonic to help restore her strength after illness.
In nineteenth-century medicine, alterative remedies were commonly used for chronic conditions.

Synset Examples

curative powers of herbal remedies
her gentle healing hand
remedial surgery
a sanative environment of mountains and fresh air
a therapeutic agent
therapeutic diets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curative - having the ability to cure diseases or heal
  • Synonym: therapeutic - having a healing effect; of or relating to the treatment of disease

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'alterativus', from Latin 'alterare' (to change).
