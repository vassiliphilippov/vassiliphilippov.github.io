Nebuchadnezzar

1. PROPN

/ ˌnɛbjəkəˈnɛzər / (AmE), / ˌnɛbjʊkəˈnɛzə / (BrE)
the king of ancient Chaldea (Babylon), who captured and destroyed Jerusalem and exiled the Israelites to Babylonia in the 6th century BC
king of Babylonia, destroyed Jerusalem

Examples

The reign of Nebuchadnezzar was marked by both grandeur and destruction in ancient Babylon.
Historians often discuss the policies enacted by Nebuchadnezzar during his time as king.
Biblical stories recount how Nebuchadnezzar ordered the exile of the Israelites to Babylonia.

Etymology

From Hebrew Nəḇūḵaḏne’ṣṣar, via Greek Nabouchodonosor and Latin Nabuchodonosor.
