hemoglobin

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːməˈɡloʊbɪn / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈɡləʊbɪn / (BrE)
A protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen from the lungs to tissues throughout the body.
oxygen-carrying blood protein
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that low hemoglobin levels could indicate anemia.
Scientists study how hemoglobin releases oxygen under different conditions.
Many athletes check their hemoglobin before competitions to ensure good endurance.

Synset Examples

fish have simpler hemoglobin than mammals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myoglobin - an oxygen-binding protein in muscle tissue

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) and Latin 'globus' (sphere), referring to the blood protein's structure.
