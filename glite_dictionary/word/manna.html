manna

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænə / (AmE), / ˈmænə / (BrE)
A sweet, hardened substance exuded by certain trees and shrubs, often used as food or in medicine.
sweet exudate from trees or shrubs

Examples

The Bedouins collected manna that had hardened on the desert shrubs overnight.
Some cuisines use manna as a natural sweetener in traditional desserts.
The manna produced by the tamarisk tree was once valued for its medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sap - the watery fluid circulating through a plant

Etymology

From Latin manna, from Ancient Greek μάννα (mánna), from Hebrew מָן (man), referring to the biblical food miraculously supplied to the Israelites.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmænə / (AmE), / ˈmanə / (BrE)
(in the Bible) the food miraculously provided by God to the Israelites during their journey in the wilderness.
food provided by God in the Bible

Examples

During their long journey, the Israelites were sustained by manna from heaven.
To many, the unexpected donation was like manna in a time of great need.
Stories of manna appear throughout religious texts as symbols of divine providence.

Etymology

From Latin 'manna', from Ancient Greek 'μάννα' (manna), from Hebrew 'mān', referring to the miraculous food in the Bible.
