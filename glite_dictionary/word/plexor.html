plexor

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛk.sɚ / (AmE), / ˈplɛk.sɔː / (BrE)
A small hammer with a rubber head used by doctors to tap parts of the body during examinations, especially to test reflexes or examine the chest.
medical hammer for examining reflexes
medical

Examples

The doctor tapped my knee with a plexor to check my reflexes.
A plexor is an essential instrument in any neurologist's toolkit.
He asked the nurse to hand him the plexor during the physical examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflex hammer - a hammer used to test reflexes in a medical examination

Etymology

From Latin 'plexus' (woven, entwined) via medical Latin terminology.
