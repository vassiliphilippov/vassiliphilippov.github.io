manslayer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænˌsleɪər / (AmE), / ˈmænˌsleɪə / (BrE)
a person who unlawfully kills another human being; a murderer or killer
person who unlawfully kills another

Examples

The court found the accused to be a manslayer after reviewing the evidence.
Ancient laws often prescribed harsh penalties for a manslayer.
He was haunted by the guilt of becoming a manslayer in self-defense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murderer - person who unlawfully and intentionally kills another
  • Synonym: killer - a person who kills someone
  • Antonym: victim - person who is killed or harmed

Etymology

From Old English mannsleahta (man-slayer), from 'man' + 'slayer'.
