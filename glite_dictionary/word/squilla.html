squilla

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɪl.ə / (AmE), / ˈskwɪl.ə / (BrE)
A type of mantis shrimp, especially one of the genus Squilla, known for its elongated body and predatory forelimbs.
a kind of mantis shrimp

Examples

The squilla is notable for its powerful, club-like appendages used to hunt prey.
Marine biologists studied the habitat preferences of the Mediterranean squilla.
Divers occasionally spot a brightly colored squilla hiding in coral reefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mantis shrimp - a marine crustacean with specialized forelimbs and vibrant colors

Etymology

From Latin 'squilla' (a shrimp or prawn).
