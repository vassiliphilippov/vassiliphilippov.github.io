pot

1. NOUN

/ pɑt / (AmE), / pɒt / (BrE)
a deep, round container, typically made of metal or earthenware, used for cooking food, often with a handle and a lid
deep round cooking vessel

Examples

She lifted the heavy pot from the stove and set it on the table.
The soup simmered gently in the pot all afternoon.
He bought a new stainless steel pot for making pasta.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pan - a shallow cooking vessel

Etymology

From Old English 'pott', probably from a West Germanic source, influenced by Old French 'pot' (drinking vessel).

9. NOUN

/ pɑt / (AmE), / pɒt / (BrE)
(Electronics) A variable resistor with three terminals, one of which is a movable center terminal, used to adjust voltage in circuits such as radios and televisions; short for potentiometer.

Examples

The technician replaced the faulty pot in the radio to restore the volume control.
If the pot becomes dirty, it may cause crackling noises when adjusting the knob.
Turn the pot slightly to increase the brightness on the television screen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potentiometer - a variable resistor used to adjust voltage

Etymology

Shortened form of potentiometer, from Italian *potenza* (power) and Greek *metron* (measure).
