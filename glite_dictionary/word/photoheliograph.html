photoheliograph

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊtoʊˈhiːliəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊˈhiːlɪəˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a telescope fitted with a camera, used for photographing the Sun
telescope for photographing the Sun

Examples

The observatory used a photoheliograph to capture detailed images of sunspot activity.
In the nineteenth century, astronomers relied on the photoheliograph for systematic solar observations.
A specialized photoheliograph was installed to document changes on the Sun's surface over time.

Etymology

From Greek 'photos' (light), 'hēlios' (sun), and 'graphō' (to write)
