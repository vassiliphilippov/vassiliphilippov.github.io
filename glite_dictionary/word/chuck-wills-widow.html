chuck-will's-widow

1. NOUN

/ ˌtʃʌk wɪlz ˈwɪdoʊ / (AmE), / ˌtʃʌk wɪlz ˈwɪdəʊ / (BrE)
a large nocturnal bird (Caprimulgus carolinensis) of the southern United States, related to the whip-poor-will, with a distinctive repetitive call
large nightjar bird of the southern US

Examples

At dusk, the haunting call of the chuck-will's-widow echoed through the pine woods.
Birdwatchers were thrilled to spot a chuck-will's-widow roosting on a low branch.
Unlike the similar whip-poor-will, the chuck-will's-widow prefers more forested habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whip-poor-will - a smaller North American nightjar with a distinctive call

Etymology

Imitative of the bird's call, first recorded in American English in the mid-19th century.
