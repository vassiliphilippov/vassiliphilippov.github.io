whack

1. NOUN

/ wæk / (AmE), / wæk / (BrE)
a sharp, loud sound made by a sudden or forceful blow
a sharp, loud sound from a strike

Examples

The stick made a loud whack as it hit the table.
I heard a sudden whack from the next room and rushed to see what happened.
With a single whack, he split the log in two.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smack - a sharp, loud sound produced by a slap or hit
  • Degree Lower: thud - a dull sound made by a heavy object hitting something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07414429-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ wæk / (AmE), / wæk / (BrE)
to hit someone or something with a loud, forceful blow
strike forcefully

Examples

He whacked the ball over the fence with his bat.
The chef whacked the dough onto the table before kneading it.
Please don't whack the remote against the table; it might break.

Synset Examples

The teacher whacked the boy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hit - to strike something with force
  • Degree Lower: tap - to touch lightly, especially with a sound

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly imitative of the sound of a blow.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01418461-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ wæk / (AmE), / wæk / (BrE)
a sharp, forceful blow or hit
a vigorous blow or hit

Examples

He gave the old TV a good whack to get it working again.
With a loud whack, the tennis ball flew over the net.
She received a painful whack on the knee during the game.

Synset Examples

he gave the table a whack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strike - to hit forcibly
  • Degree Lower: tap - a light touch or gentle hit
  • Degree Higher: smash - a very forceful blow

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound of a blow; first recorded in the 18th century.

Source: WordNet
