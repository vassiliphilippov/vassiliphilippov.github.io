strapping

1. ADJ

/ ˈstræpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstræpɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person) tall, strong, and heavily built.
muscular and heavily built

Examples

The farmer's sons grew into strapping young men, able to manage the heavy work around the farm.
She was a strapping girl with broad shoulders and an infectious laugh.
At eighteen, he was already a strapping lad, towering over his classmates.

Synset Examples

a beefy wrestler
had a tall burly frame
clothing sizes for husky boys
a strapping boy of eighteen
‘buirdly’ is a Scottish term

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burly - large and strong; heavily built
  • Antonym: puny - small and weak

Etymology

Present participle of 'strap', likely in reference to vigour or robustness, dating from the 17th century.
