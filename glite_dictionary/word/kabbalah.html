Kabbalah

1. NOUN

/ kəˈbɑːlə / (AmE), / kəˈbɑːlə / (BrE)
A mystical and esoteric tradition of Jewish theosophy originating from rabbinical interpretation of Hebrew scriptures, developed mainly between the 7th and 18th centuries.
Jewish mystical/theosophical tradition

Examples

Many scholars have devoted their lives to the study of Kabbalah and its mystical teachings.
Some contemporary spiritual movements draw inspiration from Kabbalah to explore the nature of the universe.
The museum's new exhibition includes rare manuscripts on Kabbalah from the Middle Ages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mysticism - belief in spiritual truths or powers beyond ordinary understanding
  • Synonym: theosophy - philosophy based on mystical insight into divine nature

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *cabbala*, from Hebrew *qabbālāh* (tradition, received doctrine).
