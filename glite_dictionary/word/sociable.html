sociable

1. ADJ

/ ˈsoʊʃəbəl / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəbl / (BrE)
enjoying or seeking the company of others; friendly and inclined to socialize
enjoying company; friendly

Examples

Our new neighbors are very sociable and invited us over for dinner.
He prefers sociable gatherings where he can meet new people.
The club hosts sociable events every weekend to bring members together.

Synset Examples

a sociable occasion
enjoyed a sociable chat
a sociable conversation
Americans are sociable and gregarious

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsociable - not wanting to be with people or unfriendly
  • Synonym: gregarious - fond of company; sociable (especially of people or animals)

Etymology

From Latin sociabilis (fit to be a companion), from sociare (to join, associate), from socius (companion).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊʃəbəl / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəbl / (BrE)
a social gathering or informal party designed to encourage friendly interaction and communal activity
informal party or gathering

Examples

Our church is hosting a sociable next Friday evening for the whole community.
Everyone brought food to share at the annual spring sociable.
She enjoys organizing a sociable every month to bring her neighbors together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: social - relating to society or its organization; an event involving people gathering together
  • Synonym: party - a social gathering of invited guests

Etymology

From French 'sociable', from Latin 'sociabilis' (companionable, social).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08273290-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈsoʊʃəbəl / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəbl / (BrE)
friendly and willing to talk and engage with others; enjoying the company of people.
friendly and outgoing

Examples

Despite being tired, Maria was very sociable at the party and chatted with everyone.
He prefers a sociable work environment where colleagues collaborate and share ideas.
Their neighbors are extremely sociable, often inviting people over for dinner.

Synset Examples

a sociable gathering

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsociable - not willing to engage or be friendly with others
  • Synonym: gregarious - enjoying the company of others; sociable

Etymology

From Latin 'sociabilis' (fit for companionship), from 'sociari' (to associate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00563165-s
Synset POS: s
