menial

1. ADJ

/ ˈminiəl / (AmE), / ˈmiːniəl / (BrE)
(of work) not requiring much skill and lacking prestige; often referring to routine domestic or manual tasks.
unskilled and low-status (work)

Examples

She was forced to take a menial job cleaning offices late at night.
They complained about having to do the most menial tasks in the company.
Although the pay was low, he performed his menial duties diligently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: menial - person who does menial work

Etymology

From Old French *meignial* (household, domestic), from Latin *mansionile* (household service).
