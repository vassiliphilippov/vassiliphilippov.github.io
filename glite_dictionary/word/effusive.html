effusive

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈfjusɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈfjuːsɪv / (BrE)
expressing feelings or emotions in an unrestrained or excessive way
excessively demonstrative; unrestrainedly emotional

Examples

Her effusive thanks made everyone in the room smile.
He gave an effusive welcome to each of his guests at the party.
The letter was filled with effusive praise for her achievements.

Synset Examples

insincere and effusive demonstrations of sentimental friendship
a large gushing female
write unrestrained and gushy poetry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gushy - excessively sentimental or demonstrative
  • Antonym: restrained - showing control or moderation in expression

Etymology

From Latin 'effūsus', past participle of 'effundere' meaning 'to pour out'.

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈfjusɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈfjuːsɪv / (BrE)
Expressing feelings of gratitude, pleasure, or approval in an unrestrained or heartfelt manner.
expressing unrestrained emotion

Examples

She gave an effusive welcome to each guest as they arrived.
His effusive praise embarrassed the young artist.
The author's effusive style made the story vibrant and engaging.

Synset Examples

a novel told in burbly panting tones

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthusiastic - having or showing intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval
  • Antonym: reserved - slow to reveal emotion or opinions

Etymology

From Latin effusus (poured out), past participle of effundere (to pour out).
