optionally

1. ADV

/ ˈɑpʃənəli / (AmE), / ˈɒpʃənəli / (BrE)
In a way that is permitted but not required; if one chooses.
if desired or chosen

Examples

You may optionally add toppings to your pizza before baking.
The security feature can be optionally enabled during setup.
Participants may optionally provide feedback after the workshop.

Synset Examples

this rule is applied optionally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: obligatorily - in a way that is required or compulsory

Etymology

From 'optional' + '-ly', with 'optional' from Medieval Latin 'optionalis', from Latin 'optio' (choice).
