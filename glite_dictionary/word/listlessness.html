listlessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪstləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈlɪstləsnɪs / (BrE)
a state of having little or no energy, interest, or enthusiasm; lack of vitality or spirit
lack of energy or enthusiasm

Examples

A sense of listlessness overcame her after weeks of stressful work.
The oppressive heat contributed to his listlessness and lack of motivation.
Prolonged listlessness can be a symptom of underlying health issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lethargy - a state of sluggishness, inactivity, and apathy
  • Antonym: vigor - active bodily or mental strength or force

Etymology

From 'listless' + '-ness'; 'listless' from archaic 'list' (desire, wish).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪst.ləs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈlɪst.ləs.nəs / (BrE)
a lack of energy, interest, or enthusiasm; the state of being listless
lack of energy or enthusiasm

Examples

Her listlessness concerned her friends, who urged her to see a doctor.
After a week of illness, he was overcome by a deep listlessness and barely left his bed.
Students sometimes experience listlessness during long, monotonous lectures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apathy - lack of interest or concern
  • Antonym: enthusiasm - intense and eager enjoyment or interest

Etymology

From 'listless' (uninterested), formed from 'list' (desire, inclination) + '-less' + '-ness'.
