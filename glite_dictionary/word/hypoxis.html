Hypoxis

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɑksɪs / (AmE), / hɪˈpɒksɪs / (BrE)
a genus of small perennial plants with grass-like leaves and star-shaped yellow flowers, commonly called star grass, that grow from corms and have leafless flowering stalks
genus of star grass plants

Examples

The gardener planted several Hypoxis bulbs along the edge of the pathway.
Researchers studied how Hypoxis grows in varying soil conditions in southern Africa.
The bright yellow flowers of Hypoxis add a cheerful touch to the spring garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amaryllis - genus of bulbous plants with showy flowers

Etymology

From Greek hupoxis, meaning 'a sour plant' (under- + sharp/acid, referring to taste or characteristic).

2. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɑksɪs / (AmE), / hɪˈpɒksɪs / (BrE)
a genus of small perennial plants, sometimes called star grass, with flowers borne on leafless stalks, resembling amaryllis and growing from a corm; chiefly found in warm regions.
a genus of small flowering plants (star grass)

Examples

The garden featured several species of Hypoxis with bright yellow flowers.
Botanists studied the medicinal properties attributed to the Hypoxis genus in traditional remedies.
Some species in Hypoxis are prized for their star-shaped blossoms and ornamental value.

Etymology

From Greek 'hypo' (under) and 'oxys' (sharp), referring to the pointed leaf bases.
