to calculate

1. VERB

/ ˈkælkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈkælkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to determine something by using mathematics, especially numbers or quantities
find by mathematical computation

Examples

She used a calculator to calculate the total cost of the groceries.
Engineers must calculate the strength of the materials before construction begins.
Before the exam, he practiced how to calculate percentages quickly and accurately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to compute - to use mathematics to find a result
  • Degree Lower: estimate - to roughly calculate or judge a value, number, or amount

Etymology

From Latin calculare (to count), from calculus (small stone used for counting).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00638921-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈkælkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈkælkjəleɪt / (BrE)
to judge or predict something in advance using reasoning or available information
predict or estimate using reasoning

Examples

The meteorologists calculate the storm will hit the coast by Friday.
We calculated that the journey would take about six hours in total.
Investors calculate an increase in profits based on current market trends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estimate - to roughly judge the value, number, or amount of something
  • Synonym: guess - to form an opinion or give an answer about something when you do not know much about it
  • Synonym: measure - to ascertain the size, amount, or degree of something

Etymology

From Latin calculare (to reckon, to compute), from calculus (small stone used for counting).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00928443-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈkælkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈkælkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to believe, expect, or suppose something to be probable; to judge or think likely (chiefly US, dialect)
believe or suppose to be probable
american

Examples

I calculate we should arrive before noon if we leave now.
She calculated he would probably forget her birthday again this year.
You can calculate that the repairs will take at least a week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suppose - to assume something is true
  • Synonym: to expect - to regard something as likely to happen
Source: WordNet

4. VERB

/ ˈkælkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈkælkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to carefully design or plan something for a particular result, person, or group
plan or design for a specific purpose

Examples

The marketing campaign was carefully calculated to appeal to young adults.
She calculated every detail of the event to attract high-profile guests.
His speech was calculated for maximum impact on the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: design - to plan and make decisions about something that is being built or created
  • Antonym: to improvise - to create or perform spontaneously without preparation

Etymology

From Latin 'calculare' (to compute, reckon), from 'calculus' (a small stone for counting).

5. VERB

/ ˈkælkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈkælkjəleɪt / (BrE)
to keep an account of something by making numerical computations; to determine or track by using arithmetic or systematic methods
keep account by computation

Examples

She uses a spreadsheet to calculate her monthly expenses.
The company hired an accountant to calculate the total revenue for the year.
It is important to calculate the cost of supplies before starting the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to compute - to calculate or reckon a figure or amount
  • Synonym: to reckon - to calculate or estimate, especially in context of financial accounts

Etymology

From Latin 'calculare' (to reckon), from 'calculus' (small stone used for counting).
