accroides

1. NOUN

/ əˈkrɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / əˈkrɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
an alcohol-soluble resin obtained from certain Australian trees, used in making varnishes and in the manufacture of paper
resin from Australian trees used in industry

Examples

The varnish was formulated using accroides to enhance durability and shine.
Paper manufacturers sometimes incorporate accroides as a sizing agent to improve product quality.
A shipment of accroides resin arrived from Australia for use in the factory.

Etymology

From Greek 'akros' (top, tip) and 'eidos' (form, shape), originally referring to the appearance of resin exudates.
