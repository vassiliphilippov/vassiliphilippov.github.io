conception

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / kənˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a woman becomes pregnant, involving the fertilization of an ovum by a spermatozoon
the act of becoming pregnant

Examples

The doctor explained that conception occurs when a sperm fertilizes an egg.
They struggled with infertility for years before finally achieving conception.
Certain health conditions can affect the timing and likelihood of conception.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fertilization - the union of an egg and sperm
  • Antonym: miscarriage - the loss of a pregnancy

Etymology

From Latin conceptio (a comprehending, a collection), from concipere (to conceive).
