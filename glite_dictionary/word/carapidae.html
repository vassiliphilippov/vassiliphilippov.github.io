Carapidae

1. NOUN

/ kəˈræpɪˌdiː / (AmE), / kəˈrapɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of slender, elongated marine fishes commonly known as pearlfishes, related to the Brotulidae family
family of marine fishes (pearlfishes)

Examples

Several species in the Carapidae family live symbiotically inside sea cucumbers.
Scientific research on Carapidae has revealed unusual behaviors among pearlfishes.
The Carapidae are sometimes mistaken for young eels due to their slender bodies.

Etymology

From New Latin Carapidae, based on the type genus Carapus.
