unpaid

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈpeɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈpeɪd / (BrE)
not yet paid; for which payment has not been made.
not yet paid

Examples

All the unpaid invoices must be settled before the end of the month.
She worked many unpaid overtime hours this year.
Their unpaid taxes resulted in a large penalty.

Synset Examples

unpaid wages
an unpaid bill

Related Terms

  • Antonym: paid - for which payment has been made

Etymology

Formed from the prefix un- (not) and paid.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈpeɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈpeɪd / (BrE)
done without receiving payment or financial compensation; undertaken as a pastime or for non-monetary reasons.
done without pay or compensation

Examples

Many charities rely on unpaid volunteers to carry out their work.
She took an unpaid internship to gain experience in the field.
The unpaid extras in the movie enjoyed being part of the production.

Synset Examples

an amateur painter
gained valuable experience in amateur theatricals
recreational golfers
reading matter that is both recreational and mentally stimulating
unpaid extras in the documentary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voluntary - done by choice, not for payment
  • Antonym: paid - receiving payment or salary

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + paid, past participle of 'pay'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01875584-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈpeɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈpeɪd / (BrE)
not receiving payment or not compensated for work or services; done or provided without being paid for
not receiving payment for work or services

Examples

Most internships at the organization are unpaid, but they offer valuable experience.
She worked as an unpaid volunteer at the local animal shelter every weekend.
The committee relies heavily on the efforts of unpaid members.

Synset Examples

the soup kitchen was run primarily by unpaid helpers
a volunteer fire department

Related Terms

  • Antonym: paid - receiving money for work or services

Etymology

from un- (not) + paid, past participle of pay (from Old French paier, from Latin pacare ‘to appease, to settle’)

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02531239-s
Synset POS: s
