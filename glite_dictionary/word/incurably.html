incurably

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈkjʊrəbli / (AmE), / ɪnˈkjʊərəbli / (BrE)
In a way that cannot be cured or remedied, especially referring to diseases or conditions.
in a way that cannot be cured

Examples

Doctors said he was incurably ill and could not recover.
Some people are incurably optimistic, no matter what happens.
She was incurably curious about the world around her.

Synset Examples

he is incurably ill

Related Terms

  • Antonym: curably - in a manner that can be cured

Etymology

From 'incurable' + '-ly'; 'incurable' from Latin 'incurabilis' (‘not able to be cured’).

2. ADV

/ ɪnˈkjʊrəbli / (AmE), / ɪnˈkjʊərəbli / (BrE)
In a way that cannot be cured or remedied.
in a way that cannot be cured

Examples

Despite the doctor's efforts, his illness proved incurably resistant to treatment.
She remains incurably optimistic, even in the face of challenges.
The garden was incurably overrun with weeds after years of neglect.

Synset Examples

she was incurably optimistic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: curably - in a way that can be cured

Etymology

From "incurable" + "-ly", ultimately from Latin "incurabilis" (not able to be cured).
