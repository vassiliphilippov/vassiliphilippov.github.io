damning

1. ADJ

/ ˈdæmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdamɪŋ / (BrE)
threatening someone with damnation or condemnation, especially in a religious or moral sense.
threatening with damnation or condemnation

Examples

The sermon was filled with damning warnings for those who refused to repent.
She delivered a damning prophecy about the consequences of sin.
The old preacher's damning words left the congregation silent and uneasy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damnatory - expressing or containing condemnation
  • Antonym: absolving - freeing from blame or guilt; forgiving

Etymology

From the verb 'damn,' from Latin 'damnare' (to condemn).
