Edward VIII

1. PROPN

/ ˌɛd.wɚd ði ˈeɪtθ / (AmE), / ˌɛd.wəd ði ˈeɪtθ / (BrE)
(1894–1972) King of the United Kingdom and Ireland in 1936, who abdicated the throne to marry Wallis Warfield Simpson, causing a constitutional crisis.
former king of the United Kingdom (abdicated 1936)

Examples

After his abdication, Edward VIII became known as the Duke of Windsor.
The reign of Edward VIII lasted less than a year, making it one of the shortest in British history.
Edward VIII's decision to marry Wallis Simpson changed the course of royal history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abdication - the act of formally giving up the throne or power
