Noctiluca miliaris

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑktəˈluːkə mɪlɪˈɛərɪs / (AmE), / ˌnɒktɪˈluːkə mɪlɪˈɛərɪs / (BrE)
a large, bioluminescent marine protozoan found in oceans, often known for causing sea sparkle at night
bioluminescent marine protozoan

Examples

The glowing waves were caused by an abundance of Noctiluca miliaris along the coastline.
Scientists study Noctiluca miliaris to understand marine bioluminescence.
A sudden bloom of Noctiluca miliaris created a spectacular light show in the bay.

Etymology

From Latin 'noctiluca' meaning 'night light' and 'miliaris' meaning 'millet-like'.
