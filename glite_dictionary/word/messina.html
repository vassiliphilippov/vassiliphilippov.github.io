Messina

1. PROPN

/ məˈsiːnə / (AmE), / mɛˈsiːnə / (BrE)
a port city in northeastern Sicily, situated on the Strait of Messina, known for its deep harbor and historical significance
a port city in Sicily, Italy

Examples

The ferry from Messina to the Italian mainland runs several times a day.
After visiting Palermo, they traveled northeast to see the port of Messina.
The earthquake of 1908 caused widespread destruction in Messina.

Etymology

From Italian Messina, from Latin Messana, from Ancient Greek Messēnē.
