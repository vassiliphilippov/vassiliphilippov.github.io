guimpe

1. NOUN

/ ɡɪmp / (AmE), / ɡɪmp / (BrE)
A short blouse with sleeves, usually worn under a jumper or pinafore dress.
short blouse worn under a dress

Examples

She wore a crisp white guimpe under her pinafore for the ceremony.
The traditional school uniform required a navy dress and a matching guimpe.
Her vintage costume included a lace-trimmed guimpe beneath the gown.

Etymology

From French 'guimpe', of Frankish origin, originally referring to a wimple or head covering.

2. NOUN

/ ɡɪmp / (AmE), / ɡɪmp / (BrE)
a piece of starched cloth, often covering the shoulders and sometimes the neck and chest, worn as part of a nun's habit or traditional women's attire
starched cloth covering on nun's habit

Examples

The nun carefully adjusted her guimpe before entering the chapel.
In medieval times, a guimpe was also worn by laywomen as a sign of modesty.
The starched white guimpe contrasted sharply with the dark fabric of her habit.

Etymology

From French 'guimpe', originally referring to a silk or linen garment worn over the neck and shoulders.
