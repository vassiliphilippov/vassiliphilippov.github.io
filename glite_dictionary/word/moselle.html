Moselle

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈzɛl / (AmE), / məˈzɛl / (BrE)
A German white wine produced in the Moselle valley, or a similar style of wine made elsewhere.
German white wine from the Moselle valley

Examples

He ordered a bottle of Moselle to complement the seafood dinner.
The crisp acidity of Moselle pairs well with spicy dishes.
They toured the vineyards along the river and sampled several Moselles during their trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Riesling - A white grape variety commonly used in Moselle wines

Etymology

From the French name of the Moselle river, itself from Latin Mosella, referring to the wine region along the river.
