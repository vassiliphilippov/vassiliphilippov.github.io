diffuseness

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfjusnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being spread out over a wide area or through a large volume
quality of being spread out

Examples

The diffuseness of the gas made it difficult to detect.
In academic writing, too much diffuseness can obscure the main argument.
The diffuseness of the crowd prevented easy communication among attendees.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: concentration - the action or power of focusing or being focused in one place

Etymology

From Latin 'diffusus' (spread out, diffuse) + -ness.
