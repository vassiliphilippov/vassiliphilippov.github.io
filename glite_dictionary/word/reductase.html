reductase

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈdʌkt ˌeɪs / (AmE), / rɪˈdʌkt ɑːz / (BrE)
an enzyme that catalyses the reduction of a specific substance during a biochemical reaction
enzyme catalysing reduction reactions
medical

Examples

The liver contains several types of reductase enzymes essential for detoxification.
Mutations affecting the activity of a particular reductase can lead to metabolic disorders.
Nitrate reductase converts nitrate to nitrite during plant nitrogen assimilation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: oxidase - an enzyme that catalyses oxidation reactions

Etymology

From reduction + -ase (enzyme suffix).
