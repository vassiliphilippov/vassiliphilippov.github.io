signifier

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪɡnɪˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡnɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a linguistic form, such as a word's sound or written appearance, that stands for or represents a concept or object.
a linguistic form that represents something

Examples

In linguistics, the signifier is the form a word takes, whether spoken or written.
The connection between a signifier and its meaning can vary between languages.
The same signifier can refer to different things depending on the cultural context.

Synset Examples

the inflected forms of a word can be represented by a stem and a list of inflections to be attached

Related Terms

  • Antonym: signified - the concept or meaning represented by a signifier

Etymology

From French signifiant, from Latin significare (to signify, indicate).
