Bothrops atrops

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈθrɑps ˈætrɑps / (AmE), / bəʊˈθrɒps ˈætrɒps / (BrE)
a large, extremely venomous species of pit viper found in Central and South America
venomous pit viper of Central and South America

Examples

The bite of the Bothrops atrops can be fatal if not treated promptly with antivenom.
Researchers tracked the habitat range of Bothrops atrops throughout the Amazon rainforest.
Local communities are well aware of the dangers posed by the Bothrops atrops snake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fer-de-lance - a common name for several extremely venomous pit vipers of the genus Bothrops

Etymology

From Greek 'bothros' (pit) and 'ops' (face), referring to the pit organ; 'atrops' means 'not turning', perhaps referencing behavior or appearance.
