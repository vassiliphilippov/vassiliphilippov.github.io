fraudulence

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɔdʒələns / (AmE), / ˈfrɔːdjʊləns / (BrE)
The state or quality of being fraudulent; deceitfulness or dishonesty intended to achieve personal gain.
state of being fraudulent

Examples

The investigator uncovered significant fraudulence in the company’s financial statements.
Any hint of fraudulence in an application can lead to immediate dismissal.
The court found the defendant guilty of fraudulence after weeks of testimony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraud - wrongful or criminal deception intended to result in financial or personal gain
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest or truthful

Etymology

From Latin 'fraudulentia', from 'fraudulentus' (full of deceit).

3. NOUN

/ ˈfrɔdʒələns / (AmE), / ˈfrɔːdjʊləns / (BrE)
the quality or state of being deceitful or dishonest, especially to gain an unfair or unlawful advantage
deceitful or dishonest behavior

Examples

The company's financial fraudulence led to a criminal investigation by authorities.
She was shocked by the level of fraudulence in the election process.
Strict regulations are in place to prevent fraudulence in online transactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraud - wrongful or criminal deception for personal gain
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and free from deceit

Etymology

From Latin 'fraudulentus' (full of deceit) via French 'fraudulent'.
