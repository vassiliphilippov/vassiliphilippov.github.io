Melicocca

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛlɪˈkɑkə / (AmE), / ˌmɛlɪˈkɒkə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American trees and shrubs that produce berry-like fruit.
genus of tropical trees/shrubs with berries

Examples

The Melicocca tree is widely cultivated for its edible fruits in parts of the Caribbean.
Many species within the Melicocca genus thrive in warm, tropical climates.
Botanists have studied Melicocca for its unique berry clusters and ecological value.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Melicocca', derived from Greek 'meli' (honey) and 'kokkos' (berry, seed).
