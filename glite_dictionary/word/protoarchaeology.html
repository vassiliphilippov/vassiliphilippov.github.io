protoarchaeology

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊtoʊˌɑrkiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəʊˌɑːkiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The study of prehistoric human artifacts and human fossils, particularly before the development of written records.
study of prehistoric artifacts and fossils

Examples

Recent advances in protoarchaeology have reshaped our understanding of early human migration.
She published a groundbreaking paper on stone tools in the field of protoarchaeology.
Graduates specializing in protoarchaeology often collaborate with paleontologists and geologists.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: archaeology - the study of human history through excavation of sites and analysis of artifacts and other physical remains.

Etymology

From proto- (first, earliest) + archaeology (study of ancient human activity).
