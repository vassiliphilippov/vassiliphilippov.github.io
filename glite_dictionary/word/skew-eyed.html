skew-eyed

1. ADJ

/ ˈskjuː ˌaɪd / (AmE), / ˈskjuː ˌaɪd / (BrE)
having eyes that look in different directions; affected by strabismus or a squint.
having eyes pointing in different directions

Examples

The artist depicted a skew-eyed character to add a sense of oddity to the portrait.
His skew-eyed stare made it difficult to tell whom he was addressing.
People sometimes used to call him skew-eyed because of his noticeable squint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cross-eyed - having one or both eyes turned inward
  • Synonym: wall-eyed - having one or both eyes turned outward or differently aligned

Etymology

From 'skew', meaning to turn or slant to one side, and 'eyed'.
