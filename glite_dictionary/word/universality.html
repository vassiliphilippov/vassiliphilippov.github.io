universality

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːnɪvɚˈsæləti / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪvəˈsælɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being universal, existing or applicable in all cases or situations.
the quality of being universal

Examples

The universality of mathematics makes it a common language for scientists around the world.
Many philosophers debate the universality of moral values across different cultures.
The theory claims the universality of physical laws throughout the universe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: universality - being universal, general applicability
  • Antonym: particularity - the quality of being particular or unique

Etymology

From Medieval Latin universalitas, from universalis (universal).
