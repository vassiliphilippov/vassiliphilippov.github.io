deedbox

1. NOUN

/ ˈdidˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈdiːdbɒks / (BrE)
a strong, lockable box used for storing money, documents, or other valuables
lockable box for valuables or documents

Examples

The solicitor retrieved the old lease agreements from the deedbox in his office.
Please ensure that the certificates are locked in the deedbox overnight for safekeeping.
Their family deedbox contained property titles dating back over a century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strongbox - a strongly made box for keeping valuables secure

Etymology

From 'deed' (legal document) + 'box', originally referring to boxes used for storing legal documents.
