chokehold

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃoʊkˌhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈtʃəʊkˌhəʊld / (BrE)
A tight grip around a person's neck, usually from behind, that restricts breathing or movement, often used to restrain someone.
restraint by tightly gripping neck

Examples

The officer applied a chokehold to subdue the suspect during the arrest.
After breaking free from the chokehold, he managed to call for help.
Many martial arts instructors teach students how to escape from a chokehold.

Synset Examples

he grabbed the woman in a chokehold, demanded her cash and jewelry, and then fled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stranglehold - a firm grip around the neck with similar restraining effect
  • Synonym: headlock - a restraining hold involving the head rather than the neck

Etymology

From 'choke' (to prevent breathing) + 'hold' (a type of grappling technique).

2. NOUN

/ ˈt͡ʃoʊkˌhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈt͡ʃəʊkˌhəʊld / (BrE)
complete control or dominance over a person, group, or situation, restricting freedom or activity
complete control over something

Examples

The company maintained a chokehold on the local market, blocking out any competition.
Many feel that the new regulations have put a chokehold on small business innovation.
His influence gave him a virtual chokehold over the city's law enforcement agencies.

Synset Examples

corporations have a stranglehold on the media
the president applied a chokehold to labor disputes that inconvenienced the public

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stranglehold - complete power and control over someone or something

Etymology

From 'choke' (to strangle) + 'hold'; originally referring to a grappling hold restricting breathing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05201049-n
Synset POS: n
