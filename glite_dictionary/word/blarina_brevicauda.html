Blarina brevicauda

1. NOUN

/ bləˈraɪnə ˌbrɛvɪˈkaʊdə / (AmE), / bləˈraɪnə ˌbrɛvɪˈkaʊdə / (BrE)
A species of North American shrew distinguished by its relatively short tail, less than half the length of its body.
North American short-tailed shrew species

Examples

The Blarina brevicauda is commonly found in forests and fields across eastern North America.
Researchers studied the habits of Blarina brevicauda to understand its impact on local ecosystems.
Unlike other shrews, Blarina brevicauda is easily identified by its very short tail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: short-tailed shrew - Common name for Blarina brevicauda

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Blarina' and species epithet 'brevicauda' meaning 'short tail' in Latin.
