headache

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌeɪk / (AmE), / ˈhɛd.eɪk / (BrE)
A continuous pain in the head, often caused by tension, illness, or other factors.
pain in the head

Examples

She had a terrible headache after staring at the computer screen all day.
Aspirin helped relieve the pounding headache he woke up with.
Loud noises can sometimes trigger a severe headache in some people.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: migraine - A severe type of headache, often with nausea and visual disturbances

Etymology

From head + ache; 'ache' from Old English 'æce', related to 'acan' (to ache).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌeɪk / (AmE), / ˈhɛd.eɪk / (BrE)
A continuous pain in the head, often caused by tension, illness, or a reaction to substances.
pain in the head

Examples

I've had a splitting headache since this morning.
She took some medicine to relieve her headache.
Stress at work often gives him a headache.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: migraine - a severe headache often accompanied by nausea and sensitivity to light
  • Synonym: pain - an unpleasant physical sensation

Etymology

From Middle English 'hede' (head) + 'ake' (pain), patterned after Old English 'heafod-ece'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌeɪk / (AmE), / ˈhɛdeɪk / (BrE)
A person or thing that causes worry, difficulty, or annoyance.
source of worry or annoyance

Examples

Dealing with the new software has been a real headache for the staff.
Trying to find a parking space in this area is always a headache.
The project turned into a major headache because of unexpected delays.

Synset Examples

New York traffic is a constant concern
it's a major worry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: problem - something that needs to be solved or causes difficulty
  • Synonym: nuisance - a person or thing causing inconvenience or annoyance
  • Antonym: relief - the removal or reduction of worry or stress

Etymology

Figurative use derived from the primary meaning of 'headache' (pain in the head), based on the idea that something troublesome is as bothersome as a headache.
