butt-weld

1. NOUN

/ bʌt wɛld / (AmE), / bʌt wɛld / (BrE)
A type of weld in which the ends of two pieces of metal are joined by placing them edge to edge and welding along the joint.
weld joining two edges together

Examples

The pipes were joined using a strong butt-weld to ensure a leak-proof seal.
A butt-weld is commonly used when constructing steel frames and pipelines.
The technician inspected each butt-weld for quality and durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weld - join pieces of metal or plastic by heating and melting the edges

2. VERB

/ bʌt wɛld / (AmE), / bʌt wɛld / (BrE)
to join two pieces of metal end to end by welding them together in a single plane without overlapping
join metals end to end by welding

Examples

The two steel pipes were carefully butt-welded to form a continuous line.
Fabricators often butt-weld joints for maximum strength in industrial applications.
Before painting, ensure all seams have been fully butt-welded and smoothed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weld - to join materials, especially metals, by applying heat
