Saint Ignatius of Loyola

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ɪɡˈneɪʃəs əv lɔɪˈoʊlə / (AmE), / seɪnt ɪɡˈneɪʃəs əv lɔɪˈəʊlə / (BrE)
(1491–1556) Spanish priest and theologian; founder of the Society of Jesus (Jesuits) and a key figure in the Counter-Reformation.
Spanish priest; founder of the Jesuits

Examples

Saint Ignatius of Loyola founded the Society of Jesus in the 16th century.
Many universities around the world are named after Saint Ignatius of Loyola.
The teachings of Saint Ignatius of Loyola continue to influence the Jesuit order today.
