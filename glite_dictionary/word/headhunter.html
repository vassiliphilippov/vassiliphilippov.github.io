headhunter

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌhʌntər / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌhʌntə / (BrE)
a member of a people or tribe who cuts off and preserves the heads of enemies as trophies
someone who collects and preserves heads as trophies

Examples

Anthropologists have studied the rituals of the headhunter tribes in Borneo.
Many museums display the intricately decorated skulls once collected by headhunters.
He was fascinated by the legends of the fierce headhunter warriors in ancient times.

Etymology

From 'head' + 'hunter', denoting one who hunts heads.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌhʌntər / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌhʌntə / (BrE)
a person or company that identifies and recruits suitable candidates for employment, especially for high-level positions in organizations
recruiter for high-level jobs

Examples

The company hired a headhunter to find a new chief executive officer.
After being contacted by a headhunter, she decided to consider new job opportunities.
Several candidates were referred to us by a professional headhunter specializing in finance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recruiter - a person who encourages people to join a company, organization, or the armed forces

Etymology

From 'head' + 'hunter', originally used for literal hunters of human heads, adopted figuratively in the mid-20th century for corporate recruiting.
