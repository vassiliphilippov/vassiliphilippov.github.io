swede

1. NOUN

/ swid / (AmE), / swiːd / (BrE)
the large, round, yellowish root of a plant (rutabaga), eaten as a vegetable
yellow root vegetable, rutabaga
british

Examples

Cut the swede into cubes and boil them until they are soft.
We had roasted swede with our Sunday lunch last weekend.
A hearty stew often contains potatoes, carrots, and swede.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rutabaga - yellow-fleshed root vegetable, also known as swede

Etymology

From Swedish dialect *rotabagge* (root bag), via Scandinavian languages.

2. NOUN

/ swid / (AmE), / swiːd / (BrE)
a plant of the cabbage family with a large yellowish root, eaten as a vegetable, also known as rutabaga
yellow-rooted vegetable, also called rutabaga
british

Examples

She peeled a swede and chopped it for the stew.
Roasted swede pairs well with carrots and potatoes.
In Scotland, mashed swede is often served with haggis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rutabaga - a large, yellow-fleshed root vegetable; North American term for swede

Etymology

Shortened from Swedish turnip, indicating its origin in Sweden.
