Leguminosae

1. NOUN

/ lɪˌɡjuməˈnosi / (AmE), / lɪˌɡjuːmɪˈnəʊsiː / (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants (legumes) that includes beans, peas, and related species, characterized by seed pods that split along both sides
the legume plant family

Examples

The Leguminosae family includes important crops like beans, peas, and lentils.
Many species in the Leguminosae can fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting agriculture.
Acacia trees are a well-known member of the Leguminosae found in Australia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fabaceae - alternative family name for Leguminosae, widely accepted in botany
  • Degree Lower: bean - an edible seed and member of the Leguminosae family

Etymology

From Latin 'legumen' (bean, pulse), used in botanical Latin for the family name.

2. NOUN

/ lɪˌɡjuːməˈnoʊsiː / (AmE), / lɪˌɡjuːmɪˈnəʊsiː / (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants, including beans, peas, and related species, characterized by the production of pods containing seeds
the legume or bean plant family

Examples

The Leguminosae family includes important crops such as peas, beans, and lentils.
Researchers study nitrogen fixation in plants from the Leguminosae family.
Many ornamental trees, like the acacia, belong to the Leguminosae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fabaceae - alternative name for the family Leguminosae
  • Degree Lower: bean - common edible seed or plant of the Leguminosae family

Etymology

From Latin legumen (bean, pulse), referring to pod-bearing plants.
