legislation

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛdʒɪˈsleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌlɛdʒɪˈsleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making or enacting laws, especially by a legislative body
the making of laws

Examples

The government introduced new legislation to combat climate change.
Parliament spent months debating the legislation before it was passed into law.
The city council is responsible for local legislation affecting businesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statute - a written law passed by a legislative body
  • Antonym: repeal - the action of revoking or annulling a law or act of legislation

Etymology

From Latin 'legislatio', from 'lex' (law) + 'latio' (carrying, proposal).

2. NOUN

/ ˌlɛdʒ.ɪˈsleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌlɛdʒ.ɪˈsleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
Laws or a set of laws passed by a legislative body.
laws made by a legislative body

Examples

The government passed new legislation to address climate change.
This area is protected under environmental legislation.
Debates about gun control often revolve around proposed legislation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statute - a written law passed by a legislative body
  • Synonym: regulation - a rule made by an authority
  • Antonym: custom - a traditional practice not formally codified as law

Etymology

From Latin 'legislatio', from 'lex' (law) + 'latio' (carrying).
