Dicamptodon ensatus

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌkæmptəˌdɒn ɪnˈseɪtəs / (AmE), / dɪˌkæmptədɒn ɛnˈsɑːtəs / (BrE)
A large species of salamander, growing up to 7 inches, native to western North America.
a large salamander of western North America

Examples

The Dicamptodon ensatus is often found in moist forest habitats along streams in California.
Researchers observed a population increase of Dicamptodon ensatus in protected reserves.
Many field guides include the Dicamptodon ensatus due to its distinctive appearance and size.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus Dicamptodon and species epithet ensatus.
