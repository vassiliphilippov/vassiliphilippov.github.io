underdeveloped

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌndərdɪˈvɛləpt / (AmE), / ˌʌndədɪˈvɛləpt / (BrE)
(of a country or region) having a low level of industrialization, infrastructure, and economic development, often due to a lack of capital investment.
lacking economic and industrial development

Examples

The country remains underdeveloped despite efforts to attract foreign investment.
Infrastructure in many underdeveloped regions is inadequate for growth.
Educational opportunities are limited in underdeveloped communities.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: developed - having advanced industrialization and infrastructure
  • Degree Higher: industrialized - having a highly developed industry and economy

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'under-' (not enough) + 'developed'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌndɚdɪˈvɛləpt / (AmE), / ˌʌndədɪˈvɛləpt / (BrE)
not having progressed or grown to a normal or advanced level; not yet fully developed.
not fully developed

Examples

The country invested heavily in infrastructure to support its underdeveloped regions.
The child’s motor skills appeared underdeveloped compared to her peers.
Many technologies remain underdeveloped due to a lack of funding and research.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: developed - having reached an advanced stage or level; matured or fully grown

Etymology

Formed from 'under-' (meaning 'insufficiently') + 'developed'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
