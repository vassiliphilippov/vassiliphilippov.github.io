to venerate

1. VERB

/ ˈvɛnəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɛnəreɪt / (BrE)
to regard someone or something with deep respect, reverence, or awe
regard with deep respect or reverence

Examples

Many people venerate historical leaders for their contributions to society.
The statue was venerated by pilgrims who believed it had miraculous powers.
Some cultures venerate their ancestors through elaborate rituals and ceremonies.

Synset Examples

Fear God as your father
We venerate genius

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revere - to feel deep respect or admiration for something or someone
  • Synonym: to idolize - to admire or love greatly or excessively
  • Antonym: to despise - to feel contempt or a deep repugnance for

Etymology

From Latin venerari (to worship, revere), from veneris (love, charm).
