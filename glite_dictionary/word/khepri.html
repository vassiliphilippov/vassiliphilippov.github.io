Khepri

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɛpri / (AmE), / ˈkɛpriː / (BrE)
An ancient Egyptian god associated with the morning sun and creation, often depicted as a scarab beetle.
Egyptian god of the morning sun

Examples

In ancient mythology, Khepri was believed to renew the sun each day.
Priests in Egypt offered prayers to Khepri at sunrise ceremonies.
The scarab beetle symbol is closely linked to Khepri in Egyptian religious art.

Etymology

From Egyptian *ḫprj*, meaning 'to come into being' or 'to transform.'
