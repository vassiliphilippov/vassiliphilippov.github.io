coltish

1. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊltɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊltɪʃ / (BrE)
energetic, playful, and lively in a way that is reminiscent of a young horse; given to merry frolicking or boisterous play.
playful and lively

Examples

The puppy was coltish, bounding through the grass with endless energy.
Her coltish laughter filled the quiet library, drawing smiles from those nearby.
At the summer party, the children became coltish, chasing each other around the garden.

Synset Examples

frolicsome students celebrated their graduation with parties and practical jokes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frolicsome - full of fun and high spirits; playful
  • Synonym: playful - full of fun and high spirits
  • Antonym: sedate - quiet and rather serious

Etymology

From ‘colt’ (a young, uncastrated male horse) + ‘-ish’ (forming adjectives).
