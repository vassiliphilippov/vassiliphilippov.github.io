Ostryopsis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːstriˈɑːpsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒstrɪˈɒpsɪs / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous shrubs native to China and Mongolia, resembling hop-hornbeams, sometimes placed in the birch or hornbeam family.
genus of Asian deciduous shrubs

Examples

The Ostryopsis shrub is commonly found in northern China and Mongolia.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Ostryopsis in its natural habitat.
Some botanists classify Ostryopsis within the birch family due to its similarities to Ostrya.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ostrya - genus of trees known as hop-hornbeams

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Ostrya' (hop-hornbeam) and Greek 'opsis' (appearance) for similarity in appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ɑːstriˈɑpsɪs / (AmE), / ɒstriˈɒpsɪs / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous monoecious shrubs found in China and Mongolia, resembling trees of the genus Ostrya; sometimes classified in the subfamily or family Carpinaceae.
genus of deciduous shrubs of Asia

Examples

The newly discovered Ostryopsis species displays unique leaf venation patterns.
Botanists debated whether Ostryopsis should be classified with Ostrya or in a separate family.
Several Ostryopsis shrubs were found thriving in the mountain regions of Inner Mongolia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ostrya - genus of hop-hornbeam trees

Etymology

From Greek 'ostrya' (hop-hornbeam) and the suffix '-opsis' (appearance), indicating resemblance to Ostrya.
