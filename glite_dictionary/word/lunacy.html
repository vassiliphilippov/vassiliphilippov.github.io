lunacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːnəsi / (AmE), / ˈluːnəsi / (BrE)
The state of being insane; historically, a legal term for insanity.
historical term for insanity
archaic legal

Examples

The old law once recognized lunacy as a reason for exemption from punishment.
During the Victorian era, people with certain mental illnesses were classified as having lunacy.
He was committed to an asylum for lunacy after being judged unfit to stand trial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insanity - the state of being seriously mentally ill; madness

Etymology

From Latin 'luna' (moon), once believed the moon influenced madness.

2. NOUN

/ ˈluːnəsi / (AmE), / ˈluːnəsi / (BrE)
foolish or senseless behavior; extreme folly or madness.
foolish or senseless behavior

Examples

Calling that dangerous stunt anything less than lunacy would be an understatement.
The plan was pure lunacy and destined to fail from the start.
He couldn't believe the lunacy of driving in such bad weather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folly - foolishness or lack of good sense
  • Antonym: sanity - the state of being sane or reasonable

Etymology

From Latin 'luna' (moon), with the belief that the moon influenced madness.
