Ethelred the Unready

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛθəlˌrɛd ði ənˈrɛdi / (AmE), / ˈiːθəlrɛd ði ʌnˈrɛdi / (BrE)
king of England from 978 to 1016, noted for his unsuccessful struggle against invading Danes and for his epithet 'the Unready', which refers to poor counsel rather than lack of readiness
Anglo-Saxon king of England (978–1016)

Examples

Ethelred the Unready was often criticized for his inability to repel the Danish invasions in England.
Historians still debate whether Ethelred the Unready deserved his infamous nickname or if he was unfairly maligned.
During the reign of Ethelred the Unready, England suffered repeated attacks from Viking raiders.

Etymology

From Old English Æþelræd Unræd, meaning 'noble counsel, no counsel'—a pun on his name.
