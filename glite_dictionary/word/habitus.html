habitus

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæbɪtəs / (AmE), / ˈhæbɪtəs / (BrE)
the physical and constitutional characteristics of an individual, especially as determining susceptibility to particular diseases
physical constitution, esp. re: disease susceptibility
medical

Examples

The doctor noted that the patient's habitus suggested a predisposition to respiratory infections.
Her slender habitus was considered when choosing an appropriate dosage for the medication.
A consumptive habitus was more common in patients with chronic tuberculosis during the 19th century.

Synset Examples

the consumptive habitus

Etymology

From Latin habitus (condition, appearance, dress), from habere (to have, to hold).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhæbɪtəs / (AmE), / ˈhabɪtəs / (BrE)
the physical and constitutional characteristics or build of the human body, especially as assessed in medicine or biology
physical build or constitution of the body
medical

Examples

The physician noted the patient's habitus as part of the general examination.
Body habitus can influence the presentation and detection of certain diseases.
Obesity is a form of altered habitus that often requires special medical consideration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: physique - the form, size, and development of a person's body
  • Synonym: stature - height and build of a person

Etymology

From Latin *habitus* (condition, appearance, demeanor), from *habere* (to have, to hold).
