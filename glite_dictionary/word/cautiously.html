cautiously

1. ADV

/ ˈkɔʃəsli / (AmE), / ˈkɔːʃəsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows care and avoidance of risk; carefully and with attention to potential danger or problems.
with care and avoidance of risk

Examples

She cautiously crossed the icy street to avoid slipping.
He spoke cautiously about the ongoing investigation, choosing his words carefully.
Investors acted cautiously during the economic downturn, preferring low-risk options.

Synset Examples

we estimated the number of demonstrators conservatively at 200,000.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carefully - with care and attention
  • Antonym: recklessly - without care or concern for danger

Etymology

From 'cautious' + '-ly', where 'cautious' derives from Latin 'cautus' (careful, heedful).

2. ADV

/ ˈkɔʃəsli / (AmE), / ˈkɔːʃəsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows care or prudence to avoid danger, risk, or mistakes.
with care or prudence

Examples

She walked cautiously through the dark corridor, alert to every sound.
The driver cautiously navigated the icy road to avoid an accident.
He cautiously approached the dog, not sure whether it was friendly.

Synset Examples

she ventured cautiously downstairs
they handled the incident with kid gloves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carefully - in a way that is done with care and attention
  • Antonym: recklessly - without thinking about the danger or negative results

Etymology

From 'cautious' + '-ly'; 'cautious' from Latin 'cautus', past participle of 'cavēre' (to take care).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00283532-r
Synset POS: r
