to outshine

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈʃaɪn / (AmE), / aʊtˈʃaɪn / (BrE)
to attract more attention or praise than others; to be more impressive or successful than someone or something else
be more impressive than others

Examples

The young actress managed to outshine even the established stars in the play.
His creative ideas outshone those of his colleagues during the meeting.
She works hard to outshine her competitors at every opportunity.

Synset Examples

This film outshone all the others in quality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eclipse - to make someone or something less important by comparison

Etymology

From out- (meaning 'more than') + shine, Old English scīnan (to shine).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01109665-v

2. VERB

/ aʊtˈʃaɪn / (AmE), / aʊtˈʃaɪn / (BrE)
to shine more brightly than someone or something else; to surpass in brilliance or excellence
shine more brightly; surpass in excellence

Examples

The young actor managed to outshine even the most experienced performers in the cast.
No diamond could outshine the brilliance of her smile that evening.
During the festival, one particular lantern outshone all the others with its vivid colors.

Synset Examples

What star outshines the sun?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to surpass - to be better or greater than
  • Synonym: to outdo - to do better than someone else

Etymology

Formed from 'out-' (beyond) and 'shine', originally in literal sense, now also figurative.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02769124-v
Synset POS: v
