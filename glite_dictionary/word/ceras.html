ceras

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪr.əs / (AmE), / ˈsɪə.rəs / (BrE)
a brightly colored, often branching hornlike structure on the back of certain mollusks, such as nudibranchs, that serves as a gill
hornlike gill structure on nudibranchs

Examples

The sea slug's ceras waved gently with the current, displaying its vibrant colors.
Scientists studied the ceras to understand how nudibranchs absorb oxygen underwater.
The branching ceras on the mollusk's back are essential for respiration.

Etymology

From Greek kéras (horn).
