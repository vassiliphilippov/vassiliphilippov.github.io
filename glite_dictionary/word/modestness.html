modestness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.dɪst.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmɒd.ɪst.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being free from vanity or conceit; humility or lack of self-importance
lack of vanity or conceit

Examples

Her modestness earned her the respect of colleagues who appreciated her humility.
Despite his great achievements, his modestness set him apart from other leaders.
People admired her modestness in accepting praise without boasting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humility - the quality of having a modest view of one's importance
  • Antonym: arrogance - the quality of being overly proud or self-important

Etymology

From Latin 'modestus' (moderate, keeping within measure) via French 'modeste'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑdɪstnəs / (AmE), / ˈmɒdɪstnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being moderate, especially in cost, size, or expenditure; not excessive or extravagant
moderation in price or expenditure

Examples

The modestness of the hotel rates made it popular among travelers on a budget.
She appreciated the modestness of his lifestyle despite his considerable wealth.
The modestness of their wedding arrangements reflected their desire to avoid extravagance.

Synset Examples

the store is famous for the reasonableness of its prices
the modestness of the living standards here becomes obvious immediately

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moderation - the avoidance of excess or extremes
  • Antonym: extravagance - excessive or unrestrained spending

Etymology

From 'modest' + suffix '-ness'; 'modest' from Latin 'modestus' (moderate, keeping due measure).
