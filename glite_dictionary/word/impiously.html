impiously

1. ADV

/ ˈɪmpiəsli / (AmE), / ˈɪmpiəsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows a lack of respect for God or sacred things; irreverently.
in an irreverent or disrespectful manner

Examples

Some critics argue that the film portrays religion rather impiously for comedic effect.
He spoke impiously of traditions that others held sacred.
Their leaders impiously dismissed long-held beliefs as mere superstition.

Synset Examples

the young members challenged their leader impiously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irreverently - in a manner showing lack of respect for people or things that are generally taken seriously
  • Antonym: piously - in a manner showing reverence for God or devout fulfillment of religious obligations

Etymology

From Latin 'impius' (irreverent, wicked) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.
