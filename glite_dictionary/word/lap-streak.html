lap-streak

1. ADJ

/ ˈlæpˌstriːk / (AmE), / ˈlæpˌstriːk / (BrE)
(of a boat) having hull planks that overlap along their edges, as in traditional wooden boat construction.
of a boat, with overlapping planks

Examples

The old fishing boat was lap-streak and expertly crafted by hand.
Traditional Scandinavian boats are often lap-streak in their construction.
Due to its lap-streak hull, the boat remained sturdy even in rough waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clinker-built - of a boat, having hull planks that overlap; traditional boatbuilding method
  • Antonym: carvel-built - of a boat, having hull planks laid edge to edge, not overlapping

Etymology

From 'lap' (to overlap) + 'streak' (a strake or plank in a ship's hull).
