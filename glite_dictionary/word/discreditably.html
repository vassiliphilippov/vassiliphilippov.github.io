discreditably

1. ADV

/ dɪsˈkrɛd.ɪt.ə.bli / (AmE), / dɪsˈkrɛd.ɪt.ə.bli / (BrE)
In a way that causes shame or dishonor; disgracefully.
in a shameful or dishonorable way

Examples

The company acted discreditably during the environmental investigation.
He behaved discreditably by ignoring the agreed rules of fair play.
The official was discreditably involved in the corruption scandal.

Synset Examples

his grades were disgracefully low

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgracefully - in a way that brings shame or dishonor

Etymology

From 'discreditable' (from Latin 'dis-' + 'creditum') + '-ly'.
