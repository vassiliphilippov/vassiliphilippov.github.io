heth

1. NOUN

/ hɛθ / (AmE), / hɛθ / (BrE)
the eighth letter of the Hebrew alphabet (ח), pronounced as a voiceless pharyngeal or velar fricative.
eighth letter of Hebrew alphabet

Examples

The Hebrew word for life begins with the letter heth.
In the alphabet, heth comes after zayin and before teth.
Linguists study the pronunciation of heth when analyzing ancient texts.

Etymology

From Hebrew חֵית (ḥēt).
