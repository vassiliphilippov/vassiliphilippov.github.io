mnemometer

1. NOUN

/ nɛˈmɑː.mə.tər / (AmE), / nɪˈmɒ.mɪ.tə / (BrE)
An outdated mechanical device used to present a series of items for memorization, typically in psychological experiments.
outdated device for memorization tasks

Examples

The psychologist demonstrated how the mnemometer once helped researchers study memory retention.
During her presentation on historical lab equipment, she showed a rare mnemometer.
Although digital methods are common now, the mnemometer was once essential for memory research.

Etymology

From Greek mnēmē (memory) + -meter (measuring device).
