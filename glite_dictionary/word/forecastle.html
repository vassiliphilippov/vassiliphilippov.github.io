forecastle

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊksəl / (AmE), / ˈfɒksəl / (BrE)
the forward part of a ship, especially a merchant ship, where the crew's living quarters are located above the main deck
crew's quarters in the forward part of a ship

Examples

The crew gathered in the forecastle after a long day at sea.
He stowed his belongings in a cramped bunk inside the forecastle.
The ship's forecastle was crowded but kept the sailors warm during the storm.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aftcastle - part of a ship at the stern, sometimes with crew quarters

Etymology

From Middle English 'fore-castel', originally referring to a castle-like structure on the ship's deck; now pronounced as 'focsle'.
