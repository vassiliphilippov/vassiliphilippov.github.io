Niobe

1. PROPN

/ ˈnaɪ.ə.bi / (AmE), / ˈnaɪ.ə.bi / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the daughter of Tantalus, whose pride in her numerous children led Apollo and Artemis to kill them; she was turned to stone as she mourned their deaths.
Greek mythological figure turned to stone

Examples

Artists often depict Niobe in sculpture, frozen in grief as she mourns her children.
The story of Niobe warns against excessive pride and defiance of the gods.
In classical literature, Niobe is a symbol of mourning and eternal sorrow.

Etymology

From Greek Νιόβη (Niobē), a figure in Greek mythology.
