parosamia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˈsoʊmiə / (AmE), / ˌparəˈsəʊmɪə / (BrE)
a disorder in which the sense of smell is distorted, causing odors to be perceived differently from how they normally smell
disorder causing distorted sense of smell
medical

Examples

After his illness, John developed parosamia and found that coffee smelled unpleasantly metallic.
Doctors diagnosed her with parosamia after she reported that familiar scents now seemed distorted.
Parosamia can significantly affect a person's enjoyment of food and quality of life.

Etymology

From Greek para- (beside, abnormal) + osmē (smell).
