sludge

1. NOUN

/ slʌdʒ / (AmE), / slʌdʒ / (BrE)
a thick, soft, and often muddy or slimy substance, typically considered as waste or residue
thick, soft, muddy substance

Examples

After the heavy rain, the streets were covered in sludge and debris.
The treatment plant removes sludge from wastewater before releasing it.
He slipped and fell into the thick sludge at the bottom of the pond.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slime - any moist, soft, and slippery substance
  • Synonym: muck - dirt, rubbish, or waste matter

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian *slud* (slush).

2. NOUN

/ slʌdʒ / (AmE), / slʌdʒ / (BrE)
the semi-solid precipitate or residual material produced by sewage treatment processes, often consisting of organic and inorganic matter
residual material from sewage treatment

Examples

Wastewater treatment produces large amounts of sludge that must be disposed of safely.
The sludge from the plant is dewatered before being sent to a landfill.
Strict regulations govern the storage and handling of sludge generated by sewage facilities.

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'slusch' (mud, mire), of uncertain origin.
