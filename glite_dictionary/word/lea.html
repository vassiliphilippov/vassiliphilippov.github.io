lea

1. NOUN

/ liː / (AmE), / liː / (BrE)
a measure of length for yarn or thread, typically equivalent to about 80 or 120 yards, depending on the material and system used
unit of length for yarn or thread

Examples

The weaver requested another lea of silk to finish the tapestry.
Each lea of linen was carefully measured before being dyed.
A lea is a traditional unit used in the textile industry.

Etymology

From Old English *lēa*, meaning a measure of thread.

2. NOUN

/ liː / (AmE), / liː / (BrE)
an open field of grassland, especially one used for grazing livestock
grassy field for grazing
archaic

Examples

The sheep grazed peacefully on the green lea beneath the hills.
Morning dew sparkled on the wide lea as the sun rose.
The cottage was surrounded by a beautiful lea stretching toward the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meadow - a field of grass, often cut for hay

Etymology

From Old English *lēah* (meadow, clearing).
