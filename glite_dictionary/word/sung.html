Sung

1. PROPN

/ sʌŋ / (AmE), / sʌŋ / (BrE)
the imperial Chinese dynasty that ruled from 960 to 1279, notable for its achievements in art, literature, and philosophy
Chinese imperial dynasty (960–1279) noted for culture

Examples

The Sung dynasty is renowned for its advances in painting and calligraphy.
During the Sung period, Neo-Confucianism became the dominant ideology.
Many technological innovations were developed under the Sung emperors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Song Dynasty - the same Chinese dynasty, frequently spelled 'Song' in modern usage
  • Degree Lower: Tang - the Chinese dynasty preceding the Sung, 618–907
  • Degree Higher: Yuan - the Chinese dynasty succeeding the Sung, 1271–1368

Etymology

From Chinese 宋 (Sòng), the name of the dynasty.
