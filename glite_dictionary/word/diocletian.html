Diocletian

1. PROPN

/ ˌdaɪ.əˈkliː.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.əˈkliː.ʃən / (BrE)
(c. 245–313 AD) Roman Emperor who divided the empire between east and west and initiated the last major persecution of Christians.
Roman Emperor (c. 245–313 AD)

Examples

Diocletian implemented important administrative reforms that helped stabilize the Roman Empire.
The last and most severe persecution of Christians began under Diocletian.
After retiring from power, Diocletian lived in his palace in present-day Croatia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Maximian - Roman Emperor who ruled the West alongside Diocletian

Etymology

From Latin Diocletianus, after his birthplace Dioclea (now Duklja, Montenegro).

2. PROPN

/ ˌdaɪəˈkliːʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʌɪəˈkliːʃən / (BrE)
(c. 245–313) Roman Emperor (284–305), known for dividing the Roman Empire between east and west and for initiating the final persecution of Christians.
Roman Emperor who divided the empire and persecuted Christians

Examples

Diocletian reorganized the Roman Empire, introducing the Tetrarchy to stabilize its governance.
The persecution of Christians under Diocletian was one of the most severe in Roman history.
Few emperors left as significant a legacy as Diocletian, particularly in administrative reforms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Maximian - co-emperor of the western Roman Empire with Diocletian
  • Antonym: Constantine - Roman Emperor who succeeded Diocletian and ended Christian persecution

Etymology

From Latin Diocletianus, the regnal name of Gaius Aurelius Valerius Diocletianus.
