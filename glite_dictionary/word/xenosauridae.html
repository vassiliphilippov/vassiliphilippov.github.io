Xenosauridae

1. NOUN

/ ziːnəʊˈsɔːrɪdiː / (AmE), / ziːnəʊˈsɔːrɪdiː / (BrE)
A monotypic family of lizards native to Mexico, comprising a single genus Xenosaurus.
family of Mexican lizards (Xenosaurus)

Examples

The Xenosauridae are only found in certain rocky regions of Mexico.
Scientists studied the scales of a specimen belonging to the Xenosauridae family.
Herpetologists have long debated the evolutionary history of the Xenosauridae.

Etymology

From Greek xenos (strange, foreign) + sauros (lizard) + -idae (family suffix in zoology).

2. NOUN

/ zɛn.oʊˌsɔːrəˈdiː / (AmE), / zɛn.əʊˌsɔːrɪˈdiː / (BrE)
A family of Mexican lizards containing only one genus, notable for their scaly skin and distinct appearance.
family of Mexican lizards (monotypic)
medical

Examples

The Xenosauridae are recognized for their unique keeled scales.
Researchers discovered a new behavior in members of the Xenosauridae family.
Most lizards in the region belong to families other than Xenosauridae.

Etymology

From Greek 'xenos' (strange, foreign) and 'sauros' (lizard), with the taxonomic suffix '-idae' for animal families.
