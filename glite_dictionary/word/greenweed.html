greenweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrinˌwid / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnˌwiːd / (BrE)
a small Eurasian shrub with clusters of yellow flowers, sometimes grown as an ornamental and known for yielding a yellow dye; common as a weed in Britain and the US.
a shrub with yellow flowers, also a weed

Examples

The fields were dotted with greenweed, its yellow blossoms bright against the grass.
Gardeners sometimes plant greenweed as a hardy ornamental shrub.
In some areas, greenweed is considered an invasive weed.
