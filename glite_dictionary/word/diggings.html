diggings

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪɡɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈdɪɡɪŋz / (BrE)
an area where digging is carried out, especially for minerals, precious stones, or archaeological remains
site of excavation for minerals or archaeology

Examples

The gold rush brought thousands of prospectors to the diggings in search of fortune.
Archaeologists uncovered ancient pottery at the diggings last summer.
New safety regulations were introduced to protect workers at the diamond diggings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excavation - the process of digging out and removing earth

Etymology

Plural of 'digging', from the verb 'dig'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪɡɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈdɪɡɪŋz / (BrE)
temporary or makeshift living quarters, especially lodgings occupied for a short period; often used informally or in historical contexts
temporary living quarters; lodgings
informal

Examples

After the fire, we had to find new diggings until our house was repaired.
While studying abroad, he stayed in modest diggings near the university.
The old prospector invited us to his diggings on the edge of town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lodgings - a room or rooms rented out to someone, usually temporary accommodation
  • Synonym: quarters - rooms or accommodations where people live, typically for a temporary period

Etymology

Originally 19th-century colloquialism referring to miners' camps, from 'dig' + '-ings'.
