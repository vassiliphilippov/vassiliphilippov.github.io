discerning

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɜrnɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈsɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
Having or showing good judgment, insight, and sensitivity, often in a subtle or unobtrusive way.
showing insight and good judgment

Examples

She is a discerning shopper who always chooses quality over quantity.
The critic was known for his discerning eye when evaluating new plays.
With a discerning ear, he quickly identified the subtle differences between the two recordings.

Synset Examples

a discerning editor
a discreet silence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perceptive - quick to notice or understand things
  • Synonym: insightful - showing a deep understanding and awareness
  • Antonym: indiscriminate - not showing careful choice or judgment

Etymology

From Latin 'discernere' (to separate, distinguish).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɜrnɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈsɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
having or showing the ability to quickly and accurately understand, notice, or judge things; perceptive or insightful.
quick to perceive or understand; perceptive

Examples

She is a discerning reader who can recognize literary quality in any genre.
The manager made a discerning choice when hiring new employees.
Only the most discerning customers will notice the craftsmanship in these details.

Synset Examples

a kind and apprehensive friend

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perceptive - having or showing sensitive insight
  • Synonym: insightful - having or showing an accurate and deep understanding
  • Antonym: unperceptive - lacking ability to notice or understand things quickly

Etymology

From Latin 'discernere' meaning to separate, distinguish.

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɜrnɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈsɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
having or showing good judgment and insight; able to see and understand things clearly and intelligently
having keen insight or good judgment

Examples

She has a discerning eye for quality artwork and always notices the smallest details.
A discerning reader will appreciate the subtle themes woven into the novel's narrative.
The restaurant is popular with discerning diners who value fresh ingredients and creative cuisine.

Synset Examples

a discerning critic
a discerning reader

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perceptive - having sensitive insight; able to notice and understand things quickly
  • Synonym: insightful - having or showing deep understanding of something
  • Antonym: unimaginative - lacking creativity or insight; not showing good judgment

Etymology

From Latin discernere (to distinguish, separate), via Old French discerner.

4. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɜrnɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈsɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
having or showing good judgment and the ability to notice and understand things easily, especially subtle details or differences.
showing good judgment or insight

Examples

She has a discerning palate and can distinguish the subtlest flavors in wine.
Only a discerning reader would notice the author's hidden references.
His discerning eye quickly spotted the flaw in the painting.

Synset Examples

discerning taste
a discerning eye for color

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perceptive - able to notice or understand things quickly and easily
  • Synonym: insightful - showing a deep understanding of a person or thing
  • Antonym: unperceptive - not able to notice things easily; lacking perception

Etymology

From Old French discerner, from Latin discernere (to separate, distinguish).

Source: WordNet
