Tully

1. PROPN

/ ˈtʌli / (AmE), / ˈtʌli / (BrE)
the surname of Marcus Tullius Cicero, a Roman statesman and orator known for his mastery of Latin prose (106–43 BC)
Roman statesman Cicero's surname

Examples

Many scholars regard Tully as one of the greatest orators in history.
In his essay, he quoted Tully to illustrate the point about rhetoric.
The works of Tully have influenced Western philosophy for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cicero - Roman statesman and orator, full name Marcus Tullius Cicero

Etymology

Latin cognomen of Marcus Tullius Cicero, used in English to refer to Cicero.
