effects

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈfɛkts / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛkts / (BrE)
personal property or belongings, especially items that are portable and not used for business purposes
personal property; belongings

Examples

After the fire, the family salvaged what little of their effects remained.
The hotel staff gathered the guest's effects after he checked out.
Soldiers must keep their effects neatly packed at all times.

Synset Examples

she left some of her personal effects in the house
I watched over their effects until they returned

Etymology

From Latin 'effectus' (accomplishment, result), later used in English to mean 'property' or 'possessions'.
