Mentha rotundifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛnθə roʊˌtʌndɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈmɛnθə rəˌtʌndɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A type of mint plant with round leaves and apple-scented stems, native to southern and western Europe and naturalized in the United States; also known as apple mint.
mint plant with apple-scented stems

Examples

The Mentha rotundifolia in our garden adds a pleasant apple scent to the air.
Herbal teas often include Mentha rotundifolia for its unique flavor and fragrance.
Gardeners recommend planting Mentha rotundifolia in pots to prevent it from spreading too much.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apple mint - A common name for Mentha rotundifolia, a mint plant with apple-scented stems

Etymology

From Latin: 'mentha' meaning 'mint' and 'rotundifolia' meaning 'round-leaved'.
