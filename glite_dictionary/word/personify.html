to personify

1. VERB

/ pərˈsɑːnɪfaɪ / (AmE), / pəˈsɒnɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to represent or embody (a quality, idea, or character), especially in human form or as a character in a play
represent or embody as a person

Examples

The actor managed to personify the spirit of hope on stage.
In her writing, she would often personify love as a gentle guide.
He personifies the villain with chilling authenticity throughout the play.

Synset Examples

Derek Jacobi was Hamlet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embody - to give tangible or visible form to an idea or quality
  • Synonym: to portray - to depict or represent in a work of art or performance

Etymology

From Latin personificare, from persona (person) + -ficare (to make).

2. VERB

/ pərˈsɑːnɪfaɪ / (AmE), / pəˈsɒnɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to represent or embody an abstract quality or idea in a concrete or physical form; to give a human form or characteristics to something abstract
represent or embody as a person

Examples

The author chooses to personify death as a silent woman dressed in black.
In his sculpture, he tried to personify the spirit of freedom.
Poems often personify nature to convey emotions more vividly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embody - to give a tangible or visible form to an idea, quality, or feeling
  • Synonym: incarnate - to embody or be the physical form of a quality or idea

Etymology

From French 'personifier', from Latin 'persona' (person).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02704786-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ pərˈsɑːnɪfaɪ / (AmE), / pəˈsɒnɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to represent a quality, idea, or thing as if it were a person or had human characteristics
attribute human qualities to something

Examples

Writers often personify nature, describing the wind as if it could whisper secrets.
Many cultures personify death as a cloaked figure carrying a scythe.
The company's mascot personifies their brand values in a friendly and approachable way.

Synset Examples

The Greeks personated their gods ridiculous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to anthropomorphize - attribute human characteristics or behavior to a god, animal, or object

Etymology

From French 'personnifier', from 'personne' (person) + '-ifier' (to make).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00729535-v
Synset POS: v
