ecclesiasticism

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌkliːziˈæstɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˌkliːzɪˈæstɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
Excessive attachment to or emphasis on church forms, practices, and ceremonies.
excessive emphasis on church forms or practices

Examples

The church was criticized for its ecclesiasticism rather than its spiritual leadership.
Many reformers argued that ecclesiasticism distracted believers from genuine faith.
His focus on hierarchy and tradition revealed a tendency toward ecclesiasticism.

Synset Examples

their ecclesiasticism overwhelmed their religion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ritualism - Emphasis on ritual in religion or other contexts
  • Antonym: secularism - Belief that religion should not be involved in the affairs of the state or public education

Etymology

From ecclesiastic + -ism, with roots in Greek ekklēsia (assembly, church).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌkliːziˈæstɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˌkliːzɪˈastɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
The principles, practices, or spirit characteristic of a church or its clergy, especially undue emphasis on church ritual or hierarchy.
church principles or practices

Examples

The rise of ecclesiasticism in the Middle Ages gave priests significant power over daily life.
Some reformers challenged the strict ecclesiasticism of the established church.
His study focused on the impact of ecclesiasticism on European art and culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clericalism - undue influence of the clergy in secular or political matters
  • Antonym: secularism - the principle of separation of the state from religious institutions

Etymology

From ecclesiastic + -ism, ultimately from Greek ekklēsiastikos (of the church).
