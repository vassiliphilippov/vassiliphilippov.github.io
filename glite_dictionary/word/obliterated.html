obliterated

1. ADJ

/ əˈblɪtəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / əˈblɪtəreɪtɪd / (BrE)
completely destroyed or wiped out so that nothing remains
completely destroyed; wiped out

Examples

The entire village was obliterated by the tornado, leaving nothing but rubble.
His memories of the accident were almost completely obliterated by trauma.
Once lush and green, the forest was now obliterated by fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destroyed - damaged so badly that it no longer exists or cannot be repaired

Etymology

From Latin 'obliteratus', past participle of 'obliterare' (to wipe out, erase).
