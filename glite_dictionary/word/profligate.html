profligate

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɑːflɪɡət / (AmE), / ˈprɒflɪɡət / (BrE)
recklessly extravagant or wasteful in the use of resources
recklessly wasteful

Examples

The company's profligate spending led to its eventual bankruptcy.
She was criticized for her profligate use of electricity during the energy crisis.
His profligate lifestyle eventually caught up with him, leaving him in debt.

Synset Examples

prodigal in their expenditures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extravagant - lacking restraint in spending resources
  • Antonym: thrifty - using resources carefully and not wastefully

Etymology

From Latin 'profligatus' (overthrown, corrupt), past participle of 'profligare' (to overthrow, ruin).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːflɪɡət / (AmE), / ˈprɒflɪɡət / (BrE)
A person who spends money in a reckless or wastefully extravagant way.
recklessly extravagant person

Examples

The young heir became a profligate, squandering his fortune on gambling and parties.
History is filled with tales of profligates who lost everything to reckless spending.
As a notorious profligate, she was always in debt despite her large inheritance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spendthrift - a person who spends money in an extravagant, irresponsible way
  • Antonym: miser - a person who hoards wealth and spends as little money as possible

Etymology

From Latin profligatus (overthrown, corrupt), from profligare (to overthrow).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːfləɡət / (AmE), / ˈprɒflɪɡət / (BrE)
a person, especially a man, who behaves in a recklessly extravagant or morally unrestrained way, often in fashionable society.
recklessly extravagant or dissolute person

Examples

The young profligate squandered his inheritance on lavish parties and expensive clothes.
Despite his intelligence, he became known as a notorious profligate among the elite circles of Paris.
Rumors spread quickly when the once-respectable gentleman fell into the company of profligates and gamblers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spendthrift - a person who spends money recklessly
  • Synonym: wastrel - a wasteful or good-for-nothing person
  • Antonym: ascetic - a person who practices severe self-discipline and abstention

Etymology

From Latin 'profligatus' (overthrown, corrupted), past participle of 'profligare' (to strike down, ruin).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

/ ˈprɑː.flɪ.ɡət / (AmE), / ˈprɒf.lɪ.ɡət / (BrE)
having or showing a lack of moral restraint; recklessly extravagant or wasteful in the use of resources or in conduct
morally unrestrained or wasteful

Examples

The profligate lifestyle of the wealthy elite was condemned by journalists.
He squandered his fortune in profligate spending and endless parties.
Their profligate disregard for the law shocked their more traditional neighbors.
