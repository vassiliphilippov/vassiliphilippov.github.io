Atropidae

1. NOUN

/ əˈtroʊpɪˌdiː / (AmE), / æˈtrɒpɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects, commonly known as booklice, characterized by small, soft-bodied forms that are often found in old books, papers, and damp environments.
family of insects known as booklice

Examples

Scientists recently discovered several new species within the Atropidae family in South America.
The presence of Atropidae in libraries can cause significant damage to rare manuscripts.
Members of the Atropidae are commonly referred to as booklice due to their affinity for paper and glue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booklouse - a small insect that feeds on mold or paper; member of Atropidae

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from 'Atropos' (a genus of booklice) and the family suffix '-idae'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌætrəˈpaɪdi / (AmE), / ˌatrəʊˈpɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small insects known as booklice, often found feeding on mold or starchy materials such as paper.
a family of small insects, booklice

Examples

The Atropidae are commonly found in humid libraries and archives.
Researchers discovered several new species within the Atropidae family.
Members of the Atropidae can damage old books by feeding on their bindings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booklouse - a small insect that feeds on mold or starchy materials, often found in books

Etymology

From Atropos (a figure in Greek mythology) + -idae (zoological family suffix).
