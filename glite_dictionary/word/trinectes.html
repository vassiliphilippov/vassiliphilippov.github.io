Trinectes

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈnɛktiːz / (AmE), / traɪˈnɛktiːz / (BrE)
A genus of flatfishes in the family Soleidae, including the hogchoker.
genus of flatfishes (Soleidae)

Examples

The hogchoker is classified in the genus Trinectes.
Species in Trinectes are found in fresh and brackish waters of the Americas.
Ichthyologists recently described a new member of the Trinectes genus.

Etymology

From New Latin, used in scientific taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ traɪˈnɛk.tiːz / (AmE), / trɪˈnɛk.tiːz / (BrE)
A genus of flatfish in the family Soleidae, which includes the hogchoker and related species.
a genus of flatfish (hogchoker)

Examples

The hogchoker is the most well-known member of the Trinectes genus.
Scientists have discovered several species within the Trinectes genus in North American rivers.
The Trinectes genus is part of the Soleidae family of flatfish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hogchoker - a small flatfish found in North American rivers and estuaries, species of Trinectes
