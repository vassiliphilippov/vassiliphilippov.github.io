sanguineous

1. ADJ

/ sæŋˈɡwɪniəs / (AmE), / saŋˈɡwɪnɪəs / (BrE)
involving or accompanied by bloodshed; characterized by violence and the shedding of blood
accompanied by bloodshed; bloody

Examples

The battlefield was left in a sanguineous state after the fierce conflict.
Historians described the uprising as particularly sanguineous due to the high number of casualties.
The revolution quickly turned sanguineous, shocking observers with its brutality.

Synset Examples

this bitter and sanguinary war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bloody - involving or characterized by bloodshed
  • Antonym: bloodless - without violence or bloodshed

Etymology

From Latin sanguineus (of blood, bloody), from sanguis (blood).
