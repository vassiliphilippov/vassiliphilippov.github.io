Anabrus simplex

1. NOUN

/ ˈænəbrəs ˈsɪmplɛks / (AmE), / ˈænəbrəs ˈsɪmplɛks / (BrE)
A large, dark, wingless katydid native to arid regions of the western United States, commonly known as the Mormon cricket.
large American wingless katydid (Mormon cricket)

Examples

Each summer, swarms of Anabrus simplex can devastate crops in the western states.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of Anabrus simplex to understand its environmental impact.
The local ecosystem changed after the population of Anabrus simplex dramatically increased.

Etymology

From New Latin, wherein 'Anabrus' is the genus and 'simplex' means simple or single.
