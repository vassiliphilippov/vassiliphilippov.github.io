xanthosis

1. NOUN

/ zænˈθoʊsɪs / (AmE), / zanˈθəʊsɪs / (BrE)
an abnormal yellow discoloration of the skin, especially due to certain diseases or conditions
yellow discoloration of the skin
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the yellowing on his arms as xanthosis.
Patients with liver dysfunction sometimes develop xanthosis as a symptom.
Due to prolonged illness, she noticed a gradual xanthosis spreading on her skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jaundice - a medical condition with yellowing of the skin or eyes, caused by increased bilirubin

Etymology

From Greek xanthos (yellow) + -osis (condition).
