to standardise

1. VERB

/ ˈstændərdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈstændədaɪz / (BrE)
to cause something to conform to a standard or norm, making it consistent, uniform, or regular.
make conform to a standard
british

Examples

The company decided to standardise its production processes across all factories.
Many countries are working to standardise safety regulations for imported goods.
She was asked to standardise the formatting of all the documents before submission.

Synset Examples

The weights and measures were standardized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to standardize - to cause to conform to standard or norm; chiefly American spelling

Etymology

From 'standard' + '-ise', influenced by French 'standardiser' and modelled on similar English formations.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00468630-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈstændɚˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈstændədaɪz / (BrE)
to evaluate or compare something according to a set standard; to make consistent with an accepted reference
to compare or adjust to a standard
british

Examples

The laboratory must standardise its procedures before conducting the new tests.
We need to standardise all measurements to ensure reliable results.
Before analysis, each score was standardised against a common reference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to standardize - American English spelling of 'standardise'

Etymology

From standard + -ise, modelled after French standardiser.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00684355-v
Synset POS: v
