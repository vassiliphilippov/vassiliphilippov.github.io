Arecaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌærəˈkeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌærɪˈkeɪsiː / (BrE)
the botanical family of chiefly tropical trees, shrubs, and vines with tall columnar trunks and large leaves, commonly known as the palm family
the palm family (plants)

Examples

The Arecaceae family includes many species of palms found in tropical regions.
Botanists study the Arecaceae for their ecological and economic importance.
Coconut and date palms are well-known members of the Arecaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palm - a tree from the family Arecaceae, typically with a tall trunk and large leaves

Etymology

From modern Latin *Arecaceae*, from *Areca*, a genus of palms, plus the standard botanical family suffix *-aceae*.

2. NOUN

/ ˌærəˈkeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌærɛˈkeɪsiː / (BrE)
A large family of chiefly tropical trees, shrubs, and vines, usually with tall columnar trunks and crowns of large leaves; the palm family.
the palm family of tropical plants

Examples

Coconut trees, which belong to the Arecaceae family, are widely cultivated in tropical regions.
Botanists studied several species from the Arecaceae in the Amazon rainforest.
The Arecaceae includes ornamental palms as well as economically important crops like dates and coconuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palm - a tree of the family Arecaceae, typically with a straight trunk and large leaves

Etymology

From the type genus Areca + Latin botanical suffix -aceae, denoting families.
