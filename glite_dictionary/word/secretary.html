secretary

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkrəˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˈsɛkrətəri / (BrE)
a person employed to assist with correspondence, record-keeping, and other administrative tasks for an individual or organization
administrative assistant

Examples

The secretary scheduled the meeting and sent out invitations to all participants.
Each department has a secretary to handle paperwork and phone calls.
When I called the office, the secretary answered and transferred me to my manager.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assistant - a person who helps another in their work
  • Synonym: administrator - a person responsible for managing or running an office or organization

Etymology

From Latin secretarius (confidential officer), from secretum (secret).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkrətɛri / (AmE), / sɪˈkrɛtəri / (BrE)
a furniture piece with a hinged writing surface and drawers, used as a writing desk, often with shelves above
writing desk with drawers and a hinged surface

Examples

The antique secretary in the corner once belonged to my grandmother.
She keeps her important documents locked in the drawers of her secretary.
He folded down the top of the secretary to write a letter.

Etymology

From French 'secrétaire,' meaning writing desk or personal assistant, from Latin 'secretarius.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04171929-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkrəˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˈsɛkrətəri / (BrE)
an official who is the head of an administrative department in a government, especially in the United States or some other countries
head of a government department

Examples

The Secretary of Defense briefed the press on national security matters.
She served as the Secretary of Education for four years under the previous administration.
After his appointment as Secretary of State, he traveled abroad to meet with foreign leaders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minister - the head of a government department in other governmental systems

Etymology

From Latin 'secretarius' (confidential officer), via Old French and Middle English.
