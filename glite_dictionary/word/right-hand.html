right-hand

1. ADJ

/ ˈraɪtˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈraɪtˌhænd / (BrE)
located on or directed toward the right side.
on or toward the right side

Examples

Make a right-hand turn at the next intersection.
She writes with her right-hand because she is right-handed.
The right-hand side of the diagram shows the final results.

Synset Examples

a right-hand turn

Related Terms

  • Antonym: left-hand - located on or directed toward the left side

2. ADJ

/ ˈraɪtˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈraɪtˌhænd / (BrE)
designed or intended to be used with the right hand
for or using the right hand

Examples

He needed a right-hand glove to replace the one he lost.
The right-hand controls operate the main engine functions.
This pair of scissors is specially designed as a right-hand model.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: left-hand - designed or intended to be used with the left hand

3. ADJ

/ ˈraɪtˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈraɪtˌhænd / (BrE)
(of a person) most helpful and reliable; serving as a chief assistant or trusted supporter.
most helpful and reliable (person)

Examples

She relied on her right-hand advisor for making important decisions.
He became the boss's right-hand man after years of loyal service.
Among the staff, Jane is clearly the manager's right-hand person.

Synset Examples

my right-hand man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assistant - a person who helps someone else; helper
  • Antonym: left-hand - less important or subordinate; contrasted with 'right-hand' as in 'left-hand man'
