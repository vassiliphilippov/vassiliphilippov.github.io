tweediness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtwiːdi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtwiːdi.nəs / (BrE)
the characteristic quality or style of being informal, homely, and traditionally associated with people who wear tweeds, often evoking a country or outdoor look
informal, outdoorsy style linked to tweeds

Examples

Her tweediness made her stand out at the fashionable city event.
The room had an air of tweediness, filled with plaid fabrics and antique books.
He cultivated a sense of tweediness with his country jackets and practical boots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rusticity - the quality of being rustic or country-like

Etymology

From 'tweed' (a rough woolen fabric) + '-iness' (forming nouns denoting quality or state).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtwiːd.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtwiːd.i.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being loose or rough in texture, especially as in tweed cloth
looseness or roughness in fabric texture

Examples

The tweediness of his jacket gave it a classic, rustic charm.
She preferred the tweediness of traditional fabrics over synthetics for her winter coat.
The interior designer admired the subtle tweediness of the upholstery fabric.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smoothness - the quality of having an even, regular surface or texture

Etymology

From 'tweed', a type of rough woolen cloth, with suffix '-iness' denoting a quality or state.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04955899-n
Synset POS: n
