Scarabaeidae

1. NOUN

/ skəˌræbiˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / skəˌræbiˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
a large family of beetles known as scarab or dung beetles, many of which feed on dung, decaying matter, or plants
family of scarab or dung beetles

Examples

The Scarabaeidae family includes many species important for decomposing animal dung.
Ancient Egyptians considered members of the Scarabaeidae sacred and carved them into amulets.
Entomologists study Scarabaeidae to understand their ecological roles in nutrient cycling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarab - a type of beetle belonging to this family

Etymology

From Latin *Scarabaeus* (scarab beetle) plus the zoological family suffix *-idae*.
