Passiflora mollissima

1. PROPN

/ ˌpæsəˈflɔrə məˈlɪsɪmə / (AmE), / ˌpæsɪˈflɔːrə məˈlɪsɪmə / (BrE)
A species of passionflower cultivated for its edible fruit, native to the Andes region.
species of passionflower grown for its fruit

Examples

The fruits of Passiflora mollissima are popular in juice blends throughout South America.
Gardeners appreciate Passiflora mollissima for its attractive flowers and prolific fruit production.
Some regions restrict the cultivation of Passiflora mollissima because it can become invasive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banana passionfruit - common name for Passiflora mollissima fruit

Etymology

From Latin: genus Passiflora (passionflower), species mollissima (meaning 'very soft').
