woolly

1. ADJ

/ ˈwʊli / (AmE), / ˈwʊli / (BrE)
covered with thick, soft, and often curly or matted hairs or fibers, resembling wool
covered with thick, soft, curly hairs or fibers

Examples

The woolly coats of the sheep kept them warm through the winter.
Her woolly scarf was soft and comfortable against her skin.
We saw a woolly mammoth skeleton at the museum.

Synset Examples

woolly lambs

Etymology

From 'wool' + '-y', meaning 'having the qualities of wool'.
