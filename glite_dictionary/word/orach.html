orach

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrətʃ / (AmE), / ˈɒrətʃ / (BrE)
any of several herbaceous plants of the genus Atriplex, commonly found in dry or saline environments such as deserts and salt marshes
herbaceous plants of the genus Atriplex

Examples

The fields near the salt marsh were covered in orach thriving in the harsh conditions.
Some chefs use orach leaves as a substitute for spinach in salads and soups.
Botanists discovered a rare species of orach growing in the desert.

Etymology

From Old French *arache*, from Latin *atripax*, related to the genus Atriplex.
