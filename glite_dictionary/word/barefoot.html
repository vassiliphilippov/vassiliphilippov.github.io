barefoot

1. ADV

/ ˈbɛrˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈbeə.fʊt / (BrE)
Without wearing any shoes or covering on the feet.
without shoes on

Examples

The children ran barefoot through the garden all afternoon.
She prefers to walk barefoot on the beach whenever she can.
After the rain, he stood barefoot in the wet grass, feeling refreshed.

Synset Examples

he chased her barefoot across the meadow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barefooted - without shoes or socks on

Etymology

From Old English 'bǣrfōt' ('bær' meaning 'bare' + 'fōt' meaning 'foot').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00279508-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADJ

/ ˈbɛrˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈbeəfʊt / (BrE)
Not wearing any shoes or other footwear.
not wearing shoes

Examples

The children ran barefoot across the grass during the summer picnic.
In some cultures, it is common to enter a home barefoot as a sign of respect.
He walked barefoot on the warm sand, enjoying the feeling under his feet.

Synset Examples

the barefoot boy
shoeless Joe Jackson

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoeless - not wearing shoes

Etymology

From Old English 'bærfōt' ('bær' meaning 'bare' + 'fōt' meaning 'foot').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02164101-s
Synset POS: s
