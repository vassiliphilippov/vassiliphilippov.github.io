taboo

1. NOUN

/ təˈbu / (AmE), / təˈbuː / (BrE)
a social or religious custom that prohibits or restricts a particular practice or the mention or use of something, especially because it is considered sacred or forbidden
a social or religious prohibition

Examples

Discussing death remains a taboo subject in many cultures.
Eating pork is a taboo in some religious communities.
The tribe has a taboo against touching certain sacred objects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prohibition - the act of forbidding something by law or social custom
  • Antonym: permission - the act of allowing something

Etymology

From Tongan or Fijian 'tabu' (set apart, forbidden), popularized in English from Captain Cook's Pacific voyages (late 18th century).

2. NOUN

/ təˈbu / (AmE), / təˈbuː / (BrE)
A social or cultural prohibition or restriction regarded as forbidden or improper.
prohibition by social custom

Examples

Speaking about money is considered a taboo in some cultures.
The topic was such a taboo that no one dared mention it in public.
Breaking a social taboo can lead to isolation or criticism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prohibition - The action of forbidding something by law or other authority
  • Antonym: permission - The act of allowing something

Etymology

From Tongan *tabu*, meaning prohibited or sacred; introduced into English in the 18th century through Polynesian languages.

3. ADJ

/ təˈbu / (AmE), / təˈbuː / (BrE)
(of an action, word, or thing) forbidden or prohibited, especially by social or religious custom; not to be discussed, approached, or used.
forbidden by custom or tradition

Examples

In traditional Polynesian societies, certain foods were considered taboo for women.
It was taboo to speak the chief's name aloud.
Some rituals remain taboo and are avoided by the local villagers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forbidden - not allowed; banned by authority or custom
  • Antonym: permitted - allowed by rules or custom

Etymology

From Tongan *tabu* (sacred, forbidden), through early Polynesian languages.
