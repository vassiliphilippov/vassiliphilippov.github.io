initiatory

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈnɪʃiˌɛˌtɔri / (AmE), / ɪˈnɪʃiətəri / (BrE)
serving to initiate or begin something; marking the start of a process or activity.
serving to initiate or begin

Examples

The organization held an initiatory ceremony to welcome new members.
Negotiators took an initiatory step by proposing the first terms of the agreement.
Her initiatory speech set the tone for the entire conference.

Synset Examples

the magazine's inaugural issue
the initiative phase in the negotiations
an initiatory step toward a treaty
his first (or maiden) speech in Congress
the liner's maiden voyage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: initiating - causing something to start
  • Antonym: concluding - bringing something to an end

Etymology

From Late Latin initiatorius (pertaining to beginning), from Latin initiare (to begin).
