disgraceful

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈɡreɪsfəl / (AmE), / dɪsˈɡreɪsfəl / (BrE)
shocking and unacceptable; deserving or causing shame or loss of respect.
causing shame or loss of respect

Examples

His disgraceful actions shocked the entire community.
It is disgraceful that such behavior is tolerated in the workplace.
The athlete was banned from competition due to her disgraceful conduct.

Synset Examples

scandalous behavior
the wicked rascally shameful conduct of the bankrupt
the most shocking book of its time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shameful - deserving or causing shame; similar meaning
  • Synonym: scandalous - causing public outrage or shock; similar meaning
  • Antonym: honorable - deserving respect or high regard; opposite meaning

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + graceful, based on the noun disgrace dating from late Middle English.

2. ADJ

/ dɪsˈɡreɪsfəl / (AmE), / dɪsˈɡreɪsfəl / (BrE)
Deserving or causing shame or dishonor; showing conduct or character that brings disgrace.
deserving or causing shame or dishonor

Examples

The politician's disgraceful behavior shocked even his supporters.
It was disgraceful to see such conditions in a modern city.
The team suffered a disgraceful defeat in the championship game.

Synset Examples

Man … has written one of his blackest records as a destroyer on the oceanic islands
an ignominious retreat
inglorious defeat
an opprobrious monument to human greed
a shameful display of cowardice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shameful - deserving shame; disgraceful
  • Antonym: honorable - deserving honor or respect

Etymology

From disgrace + -ful; 'disgrace' from Old French 'desgrace', from Latin 'dis-' (away, not) + 'gratia' (favor).
