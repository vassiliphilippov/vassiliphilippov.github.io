incorporation

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌkɔːr.pəˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌkɔː.pəˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of uniting two or more things into a single body or entity
act of uniting things into one body

Examples

The incorporation of several smaller companies created a much larger corporation.
The project involved the incorporation of new safety procedures into existing protocols.
Their approach emphasizes the incorporation of traditional techniques into modern design.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: integration - the act of combining or adding parts to make a unified whole

Etymology

From Latin incorporatio, from incorporare (to embody).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˌkɔːrpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌkɔːpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of including something as part of a whole; the process of combining or integrating parts into a single entity
act of including; combining parts

Examples

The incorporation of new evidence changed the direction of the investigation.
Our plan requires the incorporation of feedback from several departments.
The recipe called for the incorporation of fresh herbs into the sauce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: integration - the action of combining things to form a whole
  • Antonym: exclusion - the act of deliberately not including something

Etymology

From Latin incorporatio, from incorporare (to embody, combine).

Synset POS: n
