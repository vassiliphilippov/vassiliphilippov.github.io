philadelphus

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪl.əˈdɛl.fəs / (AmE), / ˌfɪl.əˈdɛl.fəs / (BrE)
A genus of mainly deciduous ornamental shrubs, known for their white, sweet-scented flowers, commonly grown in temperate regions.
genus of ornamental shrubs with scented white flowers

Examples

The philadelphus in our garden blooms beautifully in early summer.
Gardeners often choose philadelphus for its fragrant flowers and attractive foliage.
After pruning the old wood, the philadelphus produced more abundant blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mock orange - common name for species of Philadelphus

Etymology

From Greek *philadelphos* meaning 'brother-loving', the name of Egyptian kings.
