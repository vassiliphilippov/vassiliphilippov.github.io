Vincetoxicum hirsutum

1. NOUN

/ vɪnsɪˈtɑksɪkəm hɜrˈsuːtəm / (AmE), / vɪnsɪˈtɒksɪkəm hɪəˈsjuːtəm / (BrE)
A species of twining vine with hairy leaves and dark purplish-brown flowers, found in certain regions and commonly grown as an ornamental plant.
a species of twining vine with hairy leaves

Examples

The Vincetoxicum hirsutum in her garden produced striking dark purplish-brown flowers in mid-summer.
Botanists studied the leaf structure of Vincetoxicum hirsutum to better understand its hairy texture.
Many ornamental gardens feature Vincetoxicum hirsutum for its attractive and unusual foliage.

Etymology

From Latin: Vincetoxicum (a plant genus name), hirsutum (hairy).
