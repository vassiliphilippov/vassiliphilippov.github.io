humidity

1. NOUN

/ hjuˈmɪdəti / (AmE), / hjuˈmɪdəti / (BrE)
the amount of water vapor present in the air; the quality or state of being humid
moisture or dampness in the air

Examples

The humidity in the rainforest made the air feel heavy and sticky.
A high level of humidity can make hot days feel even hotter.
Our hair tends to frizz when the humidity is high during the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moisture - water or liquid diffused in a small quantity as vapor, within a solid, or condensed on a surface
  • Antonym: dryness - the state or quality of being dry

Etymology

From Latin humiditas (moisture, dampness), from humidus (moist).
