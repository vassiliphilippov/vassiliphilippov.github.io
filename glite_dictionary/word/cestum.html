cestum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛstəm / (AmE), / ˈsɛstəm / (BrE)
In classical mythology, the girdle or belt of Venus (Aphrodite), believed to bestow irresistible charm or attractiveness.
Venus's mythical girdle or belt

Examples

Many poets have written about the magical power of Venus's cestum.
In the myth, whoever wore the cestum would become irresistible to others.
The goddess lent her cestum to Juno to help her win Jupiter’s affection.

Etymology

From Latin *cestus*, from Greek *kēstos* (embroidered girdle).
