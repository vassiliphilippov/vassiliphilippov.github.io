branching

1. ADJ

/ ˈbræntʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːntʃɪŋ / (BrE)
having or producing branches that spread out from a main stem, trunk, or axis
having or producing branches

Examples

The branching pattern of the tree was clearly visible against the sky.
This river has a branching network of streams and tributaries.
They studied the branching structures of coral under a microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: branched - having or consisting of branches

2. ADJ

/ ˈbræn.tʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːn.tʃɪŋ / (BrE)
resembling the branches of a tree, often spreading out or dividing into subdivisions
resembling tree branches

Examples

The branching structure of the river fascinated the geologist.
The plant’s branching stems allow it to spread widely across the garden.
She drew a diagram with branching pathways to illustrate the decision process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ramified - having branches or branch-like structures; divided into branches
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00615514-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈbræn.tʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːn.tʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of dividing into separate branches or subdivisions
the act of dividing into branches

Examples

The branching of the river created several small islands.
In mathematics, decision trees use branching to explore different outcomes.
The branching structure of veins in leaves is a common feature in plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: division - the action of separating something into parts
  • Antonym: convergence - the process of coming together or merging
