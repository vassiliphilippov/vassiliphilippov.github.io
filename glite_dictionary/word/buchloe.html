Buchloe

1. NOUN

/ ˈbjuːkloʊ / (AmE), / ˈbjuːkləʊ / (BrE)
A small genus of North American grasses, including buffalo grass, known for their drought resistance and use as turf grass.
genus of grasses including buffalo grass

Examples

Buchloe is prized for its ability to withstand dry conditions and poor soils.
The most well-known member of the genus Buchloe is buffalo grass.
Native prairies of the Midwest are often dominated by Buchloe and other native grasses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buffalo grass - A type of grass native to North America, often used in lawns

Etymology

From Greek bous (ox) + chloē (grass).
