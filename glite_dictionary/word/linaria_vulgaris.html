Linaria vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈnɛəriə vʌlˈɡɛrɪs / (AmE), / lɪˈnɑːrɪə vʌlˈɡɑːrɪs / (BrE)
A common European perennial plant with showy yellow and orange flowers, known as a weed in North America.
European plant with yellow and orange flowers

Examples

The roadside was dotted with clusters of Linaria vulgaris blooming bright yellow and orange.
Gardeners often mistake Linaria vulgaris for a wildflower, but it can quickly spread and become invasive.
In North America, Linaria vulgaris is sometimes referred to as 'butter-and-eggs' due to its colorful flowers.

Etymology

From Latin Linaria (genus name, from linum 'flax') + vulgaris ('common').
