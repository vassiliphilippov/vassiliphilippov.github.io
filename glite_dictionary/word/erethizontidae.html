Erethizontidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛrəθəˈzɒntɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌɛrɛθɪˈzɒntɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of New World porcupines, primarily arboreal rodents found in North and South America.
family of New World porcupines

Examples

Members of the Erethizontidae family are known for their tree-dwelling habits.
The North American porcupine belongs to the Erethizontidae family.
Researchers studied the adaptation of Erethizontidae species to arboreal environments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Hystricidae - family of Old World porcupines

Etymology

From New Latin Erethizontidae, based on genus Erethizon (North American porcupine) + -idae, a zoological family suffix.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛr.əˌθɪz.ɒnˈtɪ.di / (AmE), / ˌɛr.ɪˌθɪz.ɒnˈtɪ.di / (BrE)
a family of New World porcupines, characterized by their arboreal habits and long, prehensile tails
family of New World arboreal porcupines

Examples

The Erethizontidae are known for their ability to climb trees and their distinctive quills.
Researchers observed a member of the Erethizontidae family foraging in the Amazon rainforest canopy.
Unlike Old World porcupines, the Erethizontidae display remarkable agility in arboreal environments.

Etymology

From New Latin Erethizontidae, based on the type genus Erethizon.
