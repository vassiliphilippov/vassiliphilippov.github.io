Leonidas

1. PROPN

/ liˈɑːnɪdəs / (AmE), / liˈɒnɪdəs / (BrE)
King of Sparta (died 480 BC), famed for leading the Greek forces at the Battle of Thermopylae against the Persians.
King of Sparta, Thermopylae hero

Examples

Many admire Leonidas for his leadership at the Battle of Thermopylae.
The story of Leonidas and his 300 Spartans has become legendary.
Historians continue to study the strategies employed by Leonidas during the Persian invasion.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Λεωνίδας (Leōnídas), meaning 'son of a lion' or 'like a lion'.
