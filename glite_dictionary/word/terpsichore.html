terpsichore

1. NOUN

/ tərpˈsɪkəˌri / (AmE), / təpˈsɪkəri / (BrE)
The art of dancing; dance. Rarely, a dancer. Also, the Muse of dance in Greek mythology.
the art of dancing; the Muse of dance

Examples

Classical terpsichore played a vital role in the court festivities of the Renaissance.
The evening was filled with music, laughter, and spontaneous terpsichore.
She devoted her life to the study and teaching of terpsichore in all its forms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dance - the movement of the body to music, especially in a series of steps

Etymology

From Greek Terpsichorē, the Muse of dance (from terpein 'to delight' + khoros 'dance, chorus').
