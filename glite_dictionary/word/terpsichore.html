Terpsichore

1. PROPN

/ tərpˈsɪkəri, tərpˈsɪkəˌriː / (AmE), / təpˈsɪkəri, tɜːpˈsɪkəri / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the Muse who presides over dance and choral song.
Muse of dance and choral song

Examples

The ancient Greeks believed that Terpsichore inspired dancers and choral performers.
In the mural, Terpsichore is depicted holding a lyre and surrounded by dancers.
Many poets have invoked Terpsichore as a symbol of artistic inspiration.

Etymology

From Greek *Terpsikhórē*, meaning 'delight in dance'.
