leukaemia

1. NOUN

/ luˈkiːmiə / (AmE), / luːˈkiːmiə / (BrE)
A serious disease in which malignant cells crowd out normal blood-forming tissue, leading to an excessive number of abnormal white blood cells in the blood and bone marrow.
cancer of the blood-forming tissues
medical british

Examples

The doctor explained that leukaemia affects the production of white blood cells.
Her child was diagnosed with acute leukaemia and began treatment immediately.
Fundraising events were organised to help support leukaemia research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leukemia - American spelling of 'leukaemia', same meaning
  • Degree Lower: lymphoma - type of cancer affecting lymphatic system

Etymology

From Greek 'leukos' (white) + 'haima' (blood), via modern Latin.
