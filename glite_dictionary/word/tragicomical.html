tragicomical

1. ADJ

/ ˌtrædʒɪˈkɑmɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌtrædʒɪˈkɒmɪkəl / (BrE)
having both tragic and comic elements or qualities at the same time
having both tragic and comic qualities

Examples

The novel's tragicomical tone made me laugh and cry at the same time.
His tragicomical attempts to fix the car only made things worse.
They found themselves in a tragicomical situation when the party's food and music both failed.

Synset Examples

the tragicomic disparity … between man's aspirations and his accomplishments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tragicomic - having both tragic and comic aspects

Etymology

Formed from 'tragic' and 'comical,' modeled on 'tragicomedy.'

2. ADJ

/ ˌtrædʒɪˈkɑmɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌtrædʒɪˈkɒmɪkəl / (BrE)
having both tragic and comic elements; at once pathetic and ludicrous.
both tragic and comic

Examples

The movie took a tragicomical turn when the hero slipped on a banana peel during a funeral.
Her tragicomical experiences made her both laugh and cry at the absurdities of life.
He recounted his tragicomical adventures in such a way that the audience didn't know whether to weep or chuckle.

Synset Examples

her life … presented itself to me as a tragicomical adventure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tragicomic - having both tragic and comic elements

Etymology

Formed from 'tragic' and 'comical', based on 'tragicomedy' (from Latin 'tragicomoedia').
