undulant

1. ADJ

/ ˈʌn.dʒə.lənt / (AmE), / ˈʌn.djʊ.lənt / (BrE)
having a wavy form, outline, or motion; resembling waves
wavy in form or movement

Examples

The undulant surface of the sand dunes shimmered in the afternoon sun.
Smoke rose in an undulant pattern, twisting and turning through the air.
She admired the undulant lines of the rolling hills beyond the valley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wavy - having a form or outline resembling waves
  • Synonym: undulating - moving with a smooth, wave-like motion

Etymology

From Latin 'undulans', present participle of 'undulare' (to undulate, move in waves), from 'unda' (wave).
