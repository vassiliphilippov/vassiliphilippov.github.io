prophesier

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑf.əˌsaɪ.ər / (AmE), / ˈprɒf.ɪˌsaɪ.ə / (BrE)
a person who predicts or claims to foresee future events, especially one regarded as authoritative or inspired
person who predicts the future

Examples

The prophesier stunned the crowd by forecasting the coming storm with remarkable detail.
Some in the village regarded her as a mere storyteller, while others called her a true prophesier.
The ancient texts mention a prophesier who warned of the kingdom’s downfall centuries ago.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prophet - a person regarded as an inspired teacher or proclaimer of the will of God

Etymology

From 'prophesy' + '-er', formed on the model of 'prophet.'
