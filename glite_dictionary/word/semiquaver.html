semiquaver

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛmiˌkweɪvər / (AmE), / ˈsɛmiˌkweɪvə / (BrE)
A musical note with a time value equal to one sixteenth of a whole note; also known as a sixteenth note.
sixteenth note in music
british

Examples

The piece required quick finger movements to play the semiquaver passages smoothly.
He struggled to read the sheet music because of the many semiquavers clustered together.
In British music notation, a semiquaver is equivalent to a sixteenth note in American terminology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sixteenth note - a musical note with the value of one sixteenth of a whole note

Etymology

Formed from semi- (meaning 'half') and quaver (meaning 'eighth note' in British English).
