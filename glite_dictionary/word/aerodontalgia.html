aerodontalgia

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛroʊˌdɒnˈtældʒiə / (AmE), / ˌeərəʊˌdɒnˈtaldʒɪə / (BrE)
pain in the teeth caused by changes in air pressure, such as during flying or mountain climbing
tooth pain from changing air pressure
medical

Examples

Pilots are more likely to experience aerodontalgia due to rapid changes in cabin pressure.
After a long flight, he complained of aerodontalgia in his upper molars.
Mountain climbers should be aware of aerodontalgia if they have untreated dental problems.

Etymology

From Greek 'aero-' (air) and 'odontalgia' (toothache).
