reef

1. NOUN

/ riːf / (AmE), / riːf / (BrE)
A horizontal strip across a sail, which can be taken in or rolled up to reduce the sail area exposed to the wind.
strip in a sail to reduce area

Examples

The sailors quickly took in a reef as the wind began to strengthen.
With two reefs in the mainsail, the boat handled the heavy weather much better.
He adjusted the reef line to reduce the sail before the approaching storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slab reef - a method of reducing sail area by folding it along a horizontal line

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch *rif* or Old Norse *rif* (rib, reef), related to nautical usage.

2. NOUN

/ riːf / (AmE), / riːf / (BrE)
a long, narrow area of rock or coral near the surface of the sea
submerged ridge of rock or coral

Examples

The divers explored the colorful reef just offshore.
Many species of fish make their home among the reef.
A shipwreck was discovered near the rocky reef.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: coral reef - a reef consisting mainly of coral

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *rif*, meaning rib or reef (nautical).

3. VERB

/ riːf / (AmE), / riːf / (BrE)
to roll up and secure part of a sail to reduce its area, especially in high winds
roll up part of a sail

Examples

The sailors quickly reefed the mainsail as the wind picked up.
Before the storm hit, she had to reef both sails for safety.
He reefed the jib to make the boat easier to handle in rough seas.

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *ref*, ‘selvage of a sail’.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ riːf / (AmE), / riːf / (BrE)
to reduce the area of a sail by folding or rolling part of it inboard, usually to adapt to strong winds.
reduce sail area by folding or rolling it

Examples

Before the storm hit, the crew decided to reef the mainsail for safety.
It's important to reef early when winds start picking up at sea.
The captain shouted to the team, 'Quickly reef the sails before we capsize!'

Synset Examples

reef the sailboat's mast

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unreef - to restore a sail to full size by undoing the reef
  • Synonym: to furl - to roll up and secure a sail

Etymology

Probably related to Middle Dutch *reeve* (to reef a sail).

5. VERB

/ rif / (AmE), / riːf / (BrE)
to reduce the area of a sail by folding or rolling it up and securing it, usually to lessen wind pressure
reduce the area of a sail

Examples

The captain ordered the crew to reef the sails as the wind picked up.
We had to reef quickly to avoid being overpowered by the storm.
He learned how to reef the mainsail during his first sailing lesson.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to furl - to roll up and fasten (a sail or flag)

Etymology

From Middle Dutch 'rif' (reef of a sail).
