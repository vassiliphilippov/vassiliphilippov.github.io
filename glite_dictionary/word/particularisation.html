particularisation

1. NOUN

/ pərˌtɪkjələrəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˌtɪkjʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of describing or treating something as a specific individual instance, especially by giving detailed or individualized information
individualized description or specifying of details

Examples

Her report included a thorough particularisation of every step taken during the investigation.
Legal documents often require the particularisation of each claim being made.
In his analysis, he avoided broad generalizations in favor of careful particularisation of the facts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: generalization - the process of making a general or broad statement

Etymology

From Latin 'particularis' (particular) + English suffix '-ation'; influenced by French 'particularisation'.
