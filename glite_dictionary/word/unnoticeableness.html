unnoticeableness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈnoʊtɪsəblnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈnəʊtɪsəblnəs / (BrE)
The quality of not being easily seen, detected, or noticed.
state of being not easily noticed

Examples

Her ability to blend into the crowd gave her a remarkable unnoticeableness.
The unnoticeableness of the mark allowed the forgery to go undetected for years.
He preferred the unnoticeableness of the background rather than being in the spotlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unnoticeable - not easily seen or noticed
  • Antonym: conspicuousness - state of being easily seen or noticed

Etymology

From unnoticeable + -ness.
