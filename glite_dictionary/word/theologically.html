theologically

1. ADV

/ θiːəˈlɑdʒɪkli / (AmE), / θɪəˈlɒdʒɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner related to theology or according to theological principles.
in a manner relating to theology

Examples

She analyzed the text theologically, focusing on religious interpretations.
The debate was approached theologically rather than historically.
He explained his beliefs theologically, referencing key doctrines.

Synset Examples

he dealt with the problem of evil theologically, not philosophically

Related Terms

  • Antonym: philosophically - in a manner relating to philosophy

Etymology

From 'theological' + '-ly', where 'theological' is from late Latin 'theologicus'.

2. ADV

/ ˌθiːəˈlɑːdʒɪkli / (AmE), / ˌθiːəˈlɒdʒɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to theology or religious theory.
in relation to theology

Examples

She is theologically conservative, supporting traditional church teachings.
His argument was theologically sound and convinced many members of the congregation.
The two scholars disagree theologically on the interpretation of this doctrine.

Synset Examples

the candidate was found theologically sound

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theological - relating to the study of God and religion

Etymology

Formed from 'theological' + '-ly', with roots in Greek theologia (study of God).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
