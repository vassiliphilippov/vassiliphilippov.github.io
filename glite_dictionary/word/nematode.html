nematode

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛməˌtoʊd / (AmE), / ˈnɛmətəʊd / (BrE)
a type of unsegmented worm with a long, slender, cylindrical body, many of which are free-living but some are parasitic
unsegmented roundworm

Examples

The scientist observed the nematode using a microscope in the laboratory.
Some nematodes can infect plants and cause significant agricultural damage.
A nematode has a long, smooth body and can live in soil or water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roundworm - another name for nematode, worm of phylum Nematoda

Etymology

From modern Latin Nematoda, from Greek nēma (thread) + -oid (resembling).
