sluggishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈslʌɡɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈslʌɡɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being slow, inactive, or lacking energy.
lack of energy or movement

Examples

His sluggishness in the mornings makes it hard for him to get to work on time.
After the long winter, a general sluggishness affected the entire village.
The medicine caused an unexpected sluggishness that made her feel tired all day.

Synset Examples

the general appearance of sluggishness alarmed his friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lethargy - a lack of energy and enthusiasm
  • Antonym: energy - the strength and vitality required for sustained activity

Etymology

From 'sluggish' + '-ness'; 'sluggish' from Middle English 'sluggi' (lazy).

3. NOUN

/ ˈslʌɡɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈslʌɡɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
A state or quality of being slow, inactive, or lacking energy, sometimes to such a degree as to resemble a deep torpor or lethargy.
lack of energy or slow movement

Examples

After staying up all night, she felt a deep sluggishness during her morning meeting.
The hot, humid weather contributed to the general sluggishness of the team.
Medical tests revealed that the patient's sluggishness was due to an underlying thyroid problem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lethargy - a lack of energy and enthusiasm
  • Antonym: alertness - the quality of being quick to notice things

Etymology

From 'sluggish' + '-ness'; 'sluggish' from Middle English 'slugge' (slothful person).
