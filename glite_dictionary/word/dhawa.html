dhawa

1. NOUN

/ dɑːˈwɑː / (AmE), / dɑːˈwɑː / (BrE)
an Indian tree (Anogeissus latifolia) of the family Combretaceae, valued for its timber and gum
an Indian timber and gum tree

Examples

The resin from the dhawa is commonly used in traditional medicines.
Local carpenters prefer dhawa wood for its strength and durability.
A large dhawa dominated the edge of the forest path.

Etymology

From Hindi 'dhāwā', name of the tree.
