Ramon y Cajal

1. PROPN

/ rəˈmoʊn i kɑˈxɑl / (AmE), / rəˈməʊn i kɑːˈxɑːl / (BrE)
(1852–1934) Spanish histologist renowned for pioneering work on the structure of the nervous system.
Spanish histologist (1852–1934)

Examples

Many consider Ramon y Cajal the father of modern neuroscience.
The discoveries of Ramon y Cajal transformed our understanding of the brain.
Numerous awards and institutions bear the name Ramon y Cajal in his honor.

Etymology

Name of Santiago Ramón y Cajal, from Spanish naming conventions.
