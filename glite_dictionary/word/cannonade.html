cannonade

1. VERB

/ ˌkænəˈneɪd / (AmE), / ˌkanəˈneɪd / (BrE)
to attack or bombard with heavy artillery or cannons over a period of time
attack with heavy artillery

Examples

The rebels attempted to cannonade the fortress throughout the night.
Enemy forces cannonaded the city for days, causing widespread destruction.
They began cannonading the hilltop at dawn to weaken the enemy defenses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombard - to attack with bombs or artillery

Etymology

From French *canonnade*, from *canon* (cannon).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkænəˈneɪd / (AmE), / ˌkanəˈneɪd / (BrE)
a period of intense and continuous artillery fire
intense, continuous artillery fire

Examples

The army launched a fierce cannonade against the enemy fortress at dawn.
A sudden cannonade shook the valley, echoing for miles around.
They took cover as the cannonade continued throughout the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombardment - a continuous attack with bombs, shells, or missiles

Etymology

From French 'cannonade', from 'canon' (cannon).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
