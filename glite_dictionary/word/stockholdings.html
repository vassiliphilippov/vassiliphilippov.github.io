stockholdings

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑːkˌhoʊldɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈstɒkˌhəʊldɪŋz / (BrE)
the total number of stocks or shares owned by an individual or organization
number of stocks or shares owned

Examples

She reviewed her stockholdings before meeting with her financial advisor.
His stockholdings in the technology sector have significantly increased in value this year.
The company encouraged employees to increase their stockholdings through its purchase plan.

Synset Examples

sell stockholdings he has in corporations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: portfolio - a range of investments held by a person or organization

Etymology

From 'stock' (shares in a company) + 'holding' (something owned or possessed).
