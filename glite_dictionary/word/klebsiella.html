Klebsiella

1. NOUN

/ klɛbˈsiːələ / (AmE), / klɛbˈsiːələ / (BrE)
a genus of non-motile, Gram-negative, rod-shaped bacteria with a prominent polysaccharide capsule, some species of which are pathogenic to humans
genus of rod-shaped bacteria, some pathogenic
medical

Examples

Klebsiella is responsible for a variety of healthcare-associated infections, including pneumonia and urinary tract infections.
The laboratory isolated Klebsiella from the patient's blood samples.
Proper hygiene can help prevent the spread of Klebsiella in hospitals.

Etymology

Named after the German bacteriologist Edwin Klebs (1834–1913).
