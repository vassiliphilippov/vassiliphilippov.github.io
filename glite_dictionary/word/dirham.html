dirham

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪr.hæm / (AmE), / ˈdɪə.hæm / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in Morocco, equal to 100 centimes
Moroccan unit of currency

Examples

The price of lunch in Marrakech was 50 dirhams.
She converted her euros into dirhams at the airport.
The Moroccan dirham has remained relatively stable against the dollar.

Etymology

From Arabic درهم (dirham), itself from Greek drachma.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪr.hɑːm / (AmE), / ˈdɪər.hæm / (BrE)
The basic monetary unit of Qatar and several other Arab countries, equal to one hundredth of a riyal in Qatar.
currency unit in Qatar and other Arab states

Examples

It costs fifty dirhams to enter the museum in Doha.
She exchanged her dollars for dirhams at the airport.
The restaurant bill came to one hundred dirhams in total.

Etymology

From Arabic درهم (dirham), from Greek δραχμή (drachmē), meaning 'a handful'.

With just a few dirhams, you can buy a cup of coffee in Casablanca.

From Arabic درهم (dirham), from Greek δραχμή (drachmē, 'drachma').

6. NOUN

/ ˈdɪrˌhɑm / (AmE), / ˈdɪərˌhæm / (BrE)
a unit of currency used in Libya, equal to one hundredth of a Libyan dinar, and also used as the name of currency in other countries.
unit of currency in Libya and other countries

Examples

The price of bread in Tripoli is five dirhams per loaf.
She exchanged her dinars for dirhams at the bank.
In Libya, 100 dirhams make one dinar.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dinar - main currency unit in Libya and other countries; 1 dinar = 100 dirhams in Libya

Etymology

From Arabic درهم (dirham), ultimately from Greek drachmē (drachma, a unit of weight and currency).
