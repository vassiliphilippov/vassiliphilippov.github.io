Lens

1. PROPN

/ lɛnz / (AmE), / lɛnz / (BrE)
A genus of small upright or climbing herbaceous plants with pinnate leaves, small white flowers, and flattened pods; includes the lentil.
genus of plants including the lentil

Examples

The genus Lens includes several species grown for their edible seeds.
Botanists classified the plant as Lens culinaris due to its unique characteristics.
Researchers study wild Lens species to improve lentil cultivation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lentil - the edible seed of the plant Lens culinaris

Etymology

From Latin *lens*, meaning 'lentil'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛnz / (AmE), / ˈlɛnz / (BrE)
A genus of small, erect or climbing herbs in the legume family, including the lentil plant, characterized by pinnate leaves, small white flowers, and flattened pods.
genus of plants including the lentil

Examples

The species Lens culinaris is widely cultivated for its edible seeds.
Botanists classify lentil plants in the genus Lens, which is native to the Mediterranean region.
Several wild species within Lens have been studied for their resistance to disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lentil - the edible seed of plants in the genus Lens

Etymology

From Latin 'lens', meaning 'lentil', referring to the shape of the seed.
