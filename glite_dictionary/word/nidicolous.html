nidicolous

1. ADJ

/ nɪˈdɪkələs / (AmE), / nɪˈdɪkələs / (BrE)
(of birds or other animals) remaining in the nest for a period after hatching or birth and dependent on parents for care.
remaining in the nest after hatching (birds, animals)

Examples

Most songbirds are nidicolous, requiring extensive parental care after hatching.
Owls are nidicolous and remain in the nest until they can fly.
Unlike nidifugous species, nidicolous chicks cannot feed themselves right away.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nidifugous - Leaving the nest soon after hatching

Etymology

From Latin 'nidus' (nest) + '-colous' (inhabiting).
