Syringa amurensis japonica

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈrɪŋɡə æˈmjʊərɛnsɪs dʒəˈpɒnɪkə / (AmE), / sɪˈrɪŋɡə əˈmjʊərɛnsɪs dʒəˈpɒnɪkə / (BrE)
a small tree native to Japan, with narrow pointed leaves and creamy-white flowers
Japanese tree with creamy-white flowers

Examples

The Syringa amurensis japonica is admired for its delicate, creamy-white flowers in spring.
Gardeners often plant Syringa amurensis japonica to add subtle fragrance to their yards.
In Japan, the Syringa amurensis japonica can often be found in temple gardens.

Etymology

From Latin syringa (lilac), amurensis (of the Amur region), japonica (of Japan), used in botanical nomenclature.
