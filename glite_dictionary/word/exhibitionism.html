exhibitionism

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛɡˌzɪbɪˈʃɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɛksɪˈbɪʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
extravagant or attention-seeking behaviour, especially to attract notice or admiration
attention-seeking behaviour

Examples

Her exhibitionism made her the center of attention at every party.
The artist's public stunts were sometimes criticized as mere exhibitionism.
His love of exhibitionism was evident in his flamboyant fashion choices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: showmanship - the skill of entertaining people and getting attention
  • Antonym: modesty - behaviour avoiding drawing attention to oneself

Etymology

From French 'exhibitionnisme', from Latin 'exhibitio' (a showing).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.sɪˈbɪʃ.əˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌɛk.sɪˈbɪʃ.ɪ.nɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the act of deliberately exposing one's genitals in public to attract attention or for sexual gratification
exposure of genitals in public; sexual disorder
medical

Examples

The man was arrested for public exhibitionism after exposing himself in the park.
Psychiatrists classify exhibitionism as a type of paraphilic disorder.
Laws against exhibitionism are designed to protect public decency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voyeurism - sexual gratification from observing others undressed or engaged in sexual activity
  • Antonym: modesty - behavior, manner, or appearance intended to avoid indecency

Etymology

From French *exhibitionnisme*, based on Latin *exhibere* (to display) + -ism.
