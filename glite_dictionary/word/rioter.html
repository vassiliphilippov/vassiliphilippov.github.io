rioter

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪ.ətər / (AmE), / ˈraɪ.ətə / (BrE)
a person who takes part in a violent public disturbance, especially as a protest or against authority
person who participates in a violent disturbance

Examples

The police arrested several rioters during the chaotic demonstration downtown.
Many rioters set vehicles on fire and vandalized stores late into the night.
The mayor condemned the actions of the rioter who attacked the government building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protester - a person who publicly demonstrates strong objection
  • Antonym: peacemaker - a person who tries to create peace

Etymology

From 'riot' (14th century Middle English, from Old French 'riote', meaning dispute or quarrel) + '-er' suffix denoting agent.
