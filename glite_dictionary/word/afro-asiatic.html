Afro-Asiatic

1. NOUN

/ ˌæfroʊ eɪˈʒiːˌætɪk / (AmE), / ˌafrəʊ eɪˈʃɪatɪk / (BrE)
a large family of languages spoken across parts of Africa and Asia, including Semitic, Berber, and Cushitic languages
family of languages of Africa and Asia

Examples

Arabic and Hebrew are both part of the Afro-Asiatic language family.
The origin of the Afro-Asiatic languages has been widely debated among linguists.
Many Berber dialects are classified as Afro-Asiatic languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Semitic - a branch of the Afro-Asiatic language family including Arabic and Hebrew

Etymology

Formed from 'Afro-' (Africa) and 'Asiatic' (Asia), reflecting the geographic distribution of the language family.
