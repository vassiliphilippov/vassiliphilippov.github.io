Blennioidea

1. NOUN

/ blɛn.iˈɔɪ.di.ə / (AmE), / blɛn.iˈɔɪ.di.ə / (BrE)
a superfamily of small, often elongated marine fishes including the blennies, butterfishes, and gunnels
superfamily of small marine fishes

Examples

The Blennioidea are commonly found in shallow coastal waters around the world.
Researchers studied the diversity of Blennioidea in the Mediterranean Sea.
Gunnels, which belong to the Blennioidea superfamily, are known for their elongated bodies.

Etymology

From New Latin, after the type genus *Blennius* and Greek -oeidēs (like, resembling).
