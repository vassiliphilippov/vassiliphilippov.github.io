Aporocactus

1. NOUN

/ əˌpɔroʊˈkæktəs / (AmE), / æˌpɒrəˈkæktəs / (BrE)
A small genus of epiphytic cacti native to Mexico, known for their long, slender, trailing stems.
genus of Mexican epiphytic cacti

Examples

The greenhouse features several specimens of Aporocactus hanging in baskets.
Aporocactus is popular among collectors for its striking, trailing growth habit.
Botanists studied the unique flowers of Aporocactus in its native Mexican habitat.

Etymology

From Greek apo- (from) + kaktos (cactus), referring to its classification among cacti.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæpərəˈkæktəs / (AmE), / ˌæpərəˈkæktəs / (BrE)
A small genus of epiphytic cacti native to Mexico, known for their long, trailing stems and often grown as ornamental plants.
genus of epiphytic Mexican cacti

Examples

The hanging stems of Aporocactus make it popular for growing in hanging baskets.
Botanists have classified several species within the genus Aporocactus.
Aporocactus plants require good drainage and partial sunlight to thrive.

Etymology

From Greek 'aporos' (difficult, impracticable) and 'kaktos' (cactus).
