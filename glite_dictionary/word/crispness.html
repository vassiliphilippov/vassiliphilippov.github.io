crispness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪsp.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkrɪsp.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being pleasantly firm, fresh, and slightly brittle to the touch or bite
pleasing firmness and freshness

Examples

The crispness of the apple made it especially enjoyable to eat.
She admired the crispness of the newly pressed linen napkins.
You can tell lettuce is at its best by its crispness and bright color.

Synset Examples

crispness of new dollar bills
crispness of fresh lettuce

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freshness - the quality of being recently made, obtained, or harvested

Etymology

From "crisp" (from Latin "crispus," meaning "curled") + -ness suffix forming nouns from adjectives.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪspnəs / (AmE), / ˈkrɪspnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being firm and easily broken or snapped, often referring to food or materials
the state of being crisp (firm but easily broken)

Examples

The crispness of the apple made it especially refreshing on a hot day.
Proper baking ensures the crispness of the cookie's edges without burning them.
After a night out in the open, the bread lost its crispness and turned soft.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crisp - firm and easily broken or crunched; fresh and dry in texture

Etymology

From adjective 'crisp' + suffix '-ness', from Old English 'crisp' (curled, wrinkled).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04946724-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪsp.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkrɪsp.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being direct, clear, and to the point in expression or style
directness and clarity of style

Examples

I admired the crispness of her presentation at the meeting.
The crispness of his reply left no room for misunderstanding.
Editors look for crispness and clarity in submitted articles.

Synset Examples

the crispness of his reply

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conciseness - the quality of being brief and to the point
  • Antonym: verbosity - the quality of using more words than necessary

Etymology

From 'crisp' (Old English 'crisp', from Latin 'crispus' meaning 'curled').
