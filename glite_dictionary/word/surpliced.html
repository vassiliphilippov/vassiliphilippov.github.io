surpliced

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɚː.plɪst / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.plɪst / (BrE)
(of a person, especially a member of the clergy or choir) wearing a surplice.
wearing a surplice (clerical garment)

Examples

The surpliced choir filed solemnly into the cathedral for the evening service.
A surpliced priest led the congregation in prayer.
All the surpliced altar servers stood in line during the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vested - dressed in vestments, especially clerical garments

Etymology

From 'surplice' (a white ecclesiastical vestment) + -ed (forming adjectives).
