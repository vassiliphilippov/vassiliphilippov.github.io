trill

1. VERB

/ trɪl / (AmE), / trɪl / (BrE)
to pronounce (a sound, especially the letter 'r') with a rapid vibration of the tongue against the palate or the alveolar ridge
to pronounce a sound with a rapid vibration

Examples

Many Spanish speakers naturally trill their r's when speaking.
Linguists often teach students how to trill an r properly.
He couldn't trill the sound at first, but he improved with practice.

Synset Examples

Some speakers trill their r's

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roll - pronounce (r) with rolling vibration

Etymology

From Italian *trillo* (a quavering or vibrating sound), related to 'trill' as a musical term.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01057138-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ trɪl / (AmE), / trɪl / (BrE)
a rapid vibration of the tongue or uvula when pronouncing certain consonants, especially 'r'
rapid tongue vibration in speech

Examples

The linguist demonstrated a perfect trill when pronouncing the Spanish word 'perro'.
Some English dialects do not use a pronounced trill for the 'r' sound.
Children sometimes struggle to produce a clear trill when first learning to speak.

Synset Examples

he pronounced his R's with a distinct trill

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tap - a single quick touch of the tongue against the palate, as in some 'r' sounds

Etymology

From Italian 'trillo', meaning a quaver or warble.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07148185-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ trɪl / (AmE), / trɪl / (BrE)
to sing or play a rapid alternation between two adjacent musical notes, typically a note and the one above or below it
sing or play a rapid alternation of notes

Examples

The violinist trilled gracefully during the solo, adding a lively touch to the piece.
In her performance, she expertly trilled between the high notes, captivating the audience.
He asked the flautist to trill at the end of each phrase for added expression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warble - to sing in a trilling or quavering voice

Etymology

From Italian *trillo* (a quavering musical sound).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01052768-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ trɪl / (AmE), / trɪl / (BrE)
a rapid alternation between a note and the note a semitone or tone above it, used as a musical ornament
rapid alternation between two musical notes

Examples

The pianist played a beautiful trill at the end of the movement.
Many Baroque pieces feature an elaborate trill in their melodies.
She struggled to master the trill required in the violin solo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tremolo - a trembling effect produced by rapid repetition of a single note or rapid alternation between two notes

Etymology

From Italian 'trillo', meaning a quaver or shake, of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06873227-n
Synset POS: n
