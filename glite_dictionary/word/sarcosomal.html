sarcosomal

1. ADJ

/ sɑrˈkoʊsəməl / (AmE), / sɑːˈkəʊsəməl / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of sarcosomes, which are specialized mitochondria found in muscle cells.
relating to sarcosomes
medical

Examples

The sarcosomal structure is essential for energy production in muscle fibers.
Deficiencies in sarcosomal enzymes can lead to muscle weakness and disease.
Researchers observed changes in sarcosomal activity during intense physical exercise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sarcosome - specialized mitochondria in muscle cells

Etymology

From Greek sarkos (flesh) and soma (body), with -al forming the adjective.
