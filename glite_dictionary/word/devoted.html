devoted

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈvoʊtɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈvəʊtɪd / (BrE)
wholly committed or dedicated to a particular purpose, cause, or person, often used with 'to'
dedicated exclusively to a purpose or cause

Examples

She has always been devoted to her research, spending long hours in the lab.
Many people feel devoted to their families and prioritize them above all else.
The organization's funds are devoted to supporting education in rural areas.

Synset Examples

large sums devoted to the care of the poor
a life devoted to poetry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dedicated - wholly committed to a task or purpose
  • Antonym: apathetic - showing or feeling no interest or concern

Etymology

From Latin 'devotus', past participle of 'devovēre' (to vow, dedicate).

2. ADJ

/ dəˈvoʊtɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈvəʊtɪd / (BrE)
having strong loyalty, affection, or dedication to a person, cause, or activity
loyal and very loving or dedicated

Examples

She was a devoted daughter who cared for her parents until their final days.
As a devoted member of the team, he always put in extra effort for every project.
They are devoted fans who travel to every concert, no matter where it is held.

Synset Examples

a devoted husband and father
devoted friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loyal - faithful and steadfast in allegiance
  • Synonym: faithful - having or showing true and constant support or loyalty
  • Antonym: disloyal - not loyal

Etymology

From the verb 'devote', from Latin 'devotus', past participle of 'devovēre' (to vow, dedicate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00521593-s
Synset POS: s
