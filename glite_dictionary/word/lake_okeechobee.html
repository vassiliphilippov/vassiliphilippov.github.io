Lake Okeechobee

1. PROPN

/ leɪk oʊkiˈtʃoʊbi / (AmE), / leɪk əʊkiːˈtʃəʊbi / (BrE)
a large lake in southeast Florida, north of the Everglades, notable as the largest freshwater lake in the state.
large lake in southeast Florida

Examples

Fishing on Lake Okeechobee is popular among both locals and tourists.
In 1928, a hurricane caused major flooding around Lake Okeechobee.
Lake Okeechobee serves as a critical water resource for communities in southern Florida.

Etymology

From Seminole, meaning 'big water'.
