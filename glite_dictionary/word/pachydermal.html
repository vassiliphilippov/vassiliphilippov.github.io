pachydermal

1. ADJ

/ ˌpæk.ɪˈdɜr.məl / (AmE), / ˌpæk.ɪˈdɜː.məl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of pachyderms (thick-skinned animals such as elephants, rhinoceroses, and hippopotamuses)
relating to pachyderms (thick-skinned animals)

Examples

The elephant's pachydermal hide protects it from most insect bites.
Some medieval bestiaries described dragons as having pachydermal armor that arrows could not pierce.
Her interest in biology led to a fascination with the pachydermal characteristics of certain mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pachydermatous - having thick skin; resembling a pachyderm

Etymology

From Greek 'pakhys' (thick) + 'derma' (skin), via New Latin 'Pachydermata'.
