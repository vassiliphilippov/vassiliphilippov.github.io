Pollux

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɑlək(s) / (AmE), / ˈpɒləks / (BrE)
the brightest star in the constellation Gemini, located near Castor
brightest star in Gemini constellation

Examples

Through the telescope, Pollux appeared as a brilliant orange star beside Castor.
Astronomers can easily locate Pollux in the night sky by finding Gemini's "twin stars."
Many ancient myths mention Pollux as one of the celestial twins with Castor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Castor - the second-brightest star in Gemini, often paired with Pollux

Etymology

From Latin Pollux, one of the twin sons of Zeus in Greek and Roman mythology (the Dioscuri).
