dazed

1. ADJ

/ deɪzd / (AmE), / deɪzd / (BrE)
unable to think clearly or react properly, especially because of a shock, blow, or fatigue.
stunned and confused

Examples

She looked dazed after hearing the unexpected news.
The boxer appeared dazed following the blow to his head.
Waking up suddenly, he felt dazed and unsure of where he was.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stunned - unable to respond or react; shocked or astonished
  • Antonym: alert - quick to notice and respond; attentive

2. ADJ

/ deɪzd / (AmE), / deɪzd / (BrE)
Unable to think clearly or react properly, usually due to shock, a blow, or fatigue.
unable to think clearly, stunned

Examples

He wandered through the wreckage in a dazed state, barely noticing the chaos around him.
After receiving the unexpected news, she looked completely dazed and unable to speak.
The boxer stood in the ring, dazed by the powerful punch he had just received.

Synset Examples

he had a dazed expression on his face
lay semiconscious, stunned (or stupefied) by the blow
was stupid from fatigue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stunned - so shocked that one is temporarily unable to react
  • Antonym: clear-headed - thinking clearly and sensibly

Etymology

From Middle English 'dasen' (to stun or stupefy), of Scandinavian origin.
