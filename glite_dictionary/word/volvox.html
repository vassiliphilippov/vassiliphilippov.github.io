Volvox

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɑl.vɑks / (AmE), / ˈvɒl.vɒks / (BrE)
A genus of minute, spherical green algae that form colonies, with flagella that rotate the colony in water.
genus of colonial green algae

Examples

Volvox colonies can be observed under a microscope in pond water samples.
The spherical shape of Volvox allows it to roll through the water with ease.
Scientists study Volvox to understand the evolution of multicellularity in algae.

Etymology

From Latin 'volvox', meaning 'to roll', from 'volvere' (to roll).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɑl.vɑks / (AmE), / ˈvɒl.vɒks / (BrE)
a genus of minute, pale green, spherical, colonial algae whose flagella enable the colony to rotate as it moves through water
genus of green, spherical colonial algae

Examples

A cluster of Volvox under the microscope reveals hundreds of individual cells forming a moving sphere.
Scientists study Volvox to understand the evolution of multicellularity in algae.
In biology class, students observed how Volvox colonies rotate in water due to their flagella.

Etymology

From Latin volvō ('to roll'), referencing the rolling movement of its colonies.
