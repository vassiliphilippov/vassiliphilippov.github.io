richness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪtʃ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃ.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being abundant or plentiful; the state of having a great quantity or variety of something
the quality of being abundant or plentiful

Examples

The richness of the rainforest can be seen in its diversity of animals and plants.
She admired the richness in the colors of the sunset.
Many appreciate the richness of detail in the artist's paintings.

Synset Examples

the profusion of detail
the idiomatic richness of English

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something
  • Antonym: scarcity - the state of being in short supply; rarity

Etymology

From 'rich' + '-ness', with 'rich' from Old English 'rīce' (powerful, wealthy).
