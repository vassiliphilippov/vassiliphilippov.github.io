Pectinibranchia

1. NOUN

/ pɛkˌtɪnəˈbrænkiə / (AmE), / pɛkˌtɪnɪˈbraŋkɪə / (BrE)
A large order of gastropods consisting of univalve mollusks with a single comb-like gill.
order of gastropods with comb-like gill

Examples

Many marine snails belong to the Pectinibranchia, recognized by their distinctive gills.
Recent studies have revised the classification of Pectinibranchia due to new molecular evidence.
The single comb-like gill is a key characteristic of the Pectinibranchia order.

Etymology

From Latin 'pecten' (comb) + Greek 'branchia' (gills).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɛktɪnɪˈbrænkiə / (AmE), / ˌpɛktɪnɪˈbraŋkɪə / (BrE)
a large order of gastropod mollusks characterized by having a single, comb-like gill
order of gastropod mollusks with a comb-like gill

Examples

The majority of marine snails belong to the order Pectinibranchia.
Researchers discovered several new species within Pectinibranchia in the deep-sea expedition.
The distinctive gill structure of Pectinibranchia aids in efficient respiration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gastropod - a class of mollusks that includes snails and slugs

Etymology

From Latin *pecten* (comb) + *branchia* (gill).
