Limpopo

1. PROPN

/ lɪmˈpoʊpoʊ / (AmE), / lɪmˈpəʊpəʊ / (BrE)
a large river in southern Africa that flows eastwards into the Indian Ocean, forming part of the border between South Africa and neighboring countries
major river in southern Africa

Examples

The Limpopo River forms a natural border between South Africa and Zimbabwe.
Many animals gather along the Limpopo during the dry season for water.
Explorers have long been fascinated by the vast length of the Limpopo.

Etymology

From the Northern Sotho word 'Limpopo', meaning 'strong gushing waterfalls'.
