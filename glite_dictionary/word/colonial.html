colonial

1. ADJ

/ kəˈloʊniəl / (AmE), / kəˈləʊniəl / (BrE)
(of animals or plants) living in colonies; existing or growing as part of a community of the same species.
living in colonies (biology)

Examples

Many species of ants are colonial and form complex social structures.
Coral reefs are created by colonial marine organisms called polyps.
Some wasps are colonial, while others lead a solitary life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solitary - living alone rather than in colonies (biology)

Etymology

From Latin colonia (settlement, colony) + -al.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈloʊniəl / (AmE), / kəˈləʊniəl / (BrE)
a person who lives in or comes from a colony, especially while it is under the control of a foreign country
resident of a colony

Examples

The British colonials established new settlements along the coast.
Many colonials maintained close ties with their homeland.
During the independence movement, colonials and indigenous peoples often had conflicting interests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: settler - a person who moves with a group to live in a new country or area
  • Antonym: native - a person born in a specified place or associated with a place by birth

Etymology

From Latin colonia (settlement, colony), via French colonial.

/ kəˈloʊniəl / (AmE), / kəˈləʊniəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a colony or colonies, especially in reference to periods of colonization
relating to a colony or colonization

Examples

The museum houses many colonial artifacts from the 18th century.
They restored the old colonial house to its original condition.
Several plants and animals are unique to the colonial regions of the island.

Related Terms

4. ADJ

/ kəˈloʊniəl / (AmE), / kəˈləʊniəl / (BrE)
(Biology) Composed of many distinct individuals or organisms living together as a unified group or colony.
composed of organisms forming a colony

Examples

Some jellyfish species are colonial, consisting of multiple individuals functioning as a single organism.
The coral reef is built by colonial animals that secrete calcium carbonate skeletons.
Bryozoans are colonial invertebrates often found attached to rocks in marine environments.

Synset Examples

coral is a colonial organism

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solitary - (Biology) living or existing alone, not in a colony

Etymology

From Latin colonia (settlement) via French colonial.
