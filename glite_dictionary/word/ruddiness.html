ruddiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌdi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈrʌd.i.nɪs / (BrE)
a healthy, reddish color in a person's complexion, especially indicating good health or robust condition
healthy reddish complexion

Examples

The ruddiness of his cheeks suggested he spent a lot of time outdoors.
After the brisk walk, a ruddiness had spread across her face.
The old man's ruddiness belied his actual age.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pallor - unnatural lack of color in the skin
  • Synonym: rosiness - having a pink or reddish color, especially in the cheeks

Etymology

From 'ruddy', ultimately from Old English 'rudig' (reddish) related to 'rēad' (red).
