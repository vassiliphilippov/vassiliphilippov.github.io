oligo

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːlɪɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɒlɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
(biochemistry) a short polynucleotide molecule containing a relatively small number of nucleotides, used especially in genetic research and testing.
short polynucleotide molecule (biochemistry)
medical

Examples

The researchers designed an oligo to specifically bind to the target DNA sequence.
Each oligo was synthesized using automated chemical methods for the experiment.
The laboratory ordered several custom oligos for the gene editing study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oligonucleotide - a nucleotide chain with a small number of nucleotides

Etymology

Short for oligonucleotide, from Greek 'oligos' meaning 'few'.
