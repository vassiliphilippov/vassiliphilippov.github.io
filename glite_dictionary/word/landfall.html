landfall

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌfɔl / (AmE), / ˈlændˌfɔːl / (BrE)
the first sighting or arrival at land after a journey by sea or air
first sighting of land after a sea or air journey

Examples

After weeks at sea, the crew finally made landfall on the distant island.
The sailors celebrated their landfall after a long and treacherous journey across the ocean.
Early explorers recorded the precise moment of landfall in their ship's logs.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: land - the solid part of the earth's surface

Etymology

From 'land' + 'fall', meaning the place where land is encountered.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌfɔl / (AmE), / ˈlan(d)fɔːl / (BrE)
the first arrival at or sighting of land after a voyage by sea or air
arrival or sighting of land after voyage

Examples

After weeks at sea, the sailors finally made landfall on the remote island.
The plane made landfall just before dawn after crossing the Atlantic.
News spread quickly when the hurricane was expected to make landfall along the southern coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: land ho - a traditional shout indicating sighting land from sea

Etymology

From land + fall, originally meaning 'approach or reaching of land' in the 17th century.
