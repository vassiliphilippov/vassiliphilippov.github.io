tattered

1. ADJ

/ ˈtætərd / (AmE), / ˈtætəd / (BrE)
Old and torn; in poor condition because of long or hard use.
old and torn; ragged or worn

Examples

After years of constant use, his old backpack was tattered and frayed at the edges.
The refugees walked in tattered clothes, hoping for help and shelter.
A tattered flag waved atop the abandoned building, weathered by the wind and rain.

Synset Examples

a man in a tattered shirt
the tattered flag
tied up in tattered brown paper
a tattered barefoot boy
a tatterdemalion prince

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ragged - Having a rough, uneven, or torn edge; shabby or worn out

Etymology

From Middle English tatered, from 'tatter' meaning 'rag'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtæt̬ɚd / (AmE), / ˈtætəd / (BrE)
damaged, ruined, or disrupted so as to be in poor condition; often used figuratively to describe something greatly diminished or weakened
damaged or ruined, often figuratively

Examples

After years of conflict, the country's economy was left in a tattered state.
His confidence was tattered by repeated failures.
The old theater is now just a tattered remnant of its grand past.

Synset Examples

our shattered dreams of peace and prosperity
a tattered remnant of its former strength
my torn and tattered past

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shattered - broken into pieces or destroyed; ruined
  • Synonym: ruined - reduced to a state of decay or destruction
  • Antonym: restored - brought back to former or original condition

Etymology

From Middle English tatered, meaning ragged or torn, from 'tatters'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00740215-s
Synset POS: s
