Heterobasidiomycetes

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛtəroʊbəˌsɪdioʊmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərə(ʊ)bəˌsɪdɪəʊmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (BrE)
a category used in some classification systems for various basidiomycetous fungi, including rusts and smuts
class of basidiomycetous fungi
medical

Examples

The Heterobasidiomycetes include important plant pathogens such as rusts and smuts.
A mycologist specializes in studying the Heterobasidiomycetes group of fungi.
Many agricultural diseases are caused by fungi from the Heterobasidiomycetes classification.

Etymology

From Greek hetero- (different) + basidium (little pedestal, referring to a spore-bearing structure) + -mycetes (fungi).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɛt̬əroʊbəˌsɪdioʊˈmaɪsiːts / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəʊbəsɪdɪəʊˈmaɪsiːtiːz / (BrE)
a subclass of basidiomycetous fungi, including rusts and smuts, recognized in some older classification systems
subclass of fungi (rusts and smuts)

Examples

The rust fungus belongs to the Heterobasidiomycetes, a group known for complex life cycles.
Scientists once classified both rusts and smuts under Heterobasidiomycetes in older taxonomic systems.
Interest in the Heterobasidiomycetes has grown because of their impact on agriculture.

Etymology

From Greek hetero- (different) + basidium (small pedestal, a fungal cell) + -mycetes (fungi).
