suspenseful

1. ADJ

/ səˈspɛnsfəl / (AmE), / səˈspɛnsfəl / (BrE)
Creating or characterized by suspense; keeping one in a state of uncertainty or anticipation.
creating suspense or anticipation

Examples

The movie was so suspenseful that I couldn't take my eyes off the screen.
She prefers reading suspenseful novels that keep her guessing until the very end.
The music added a suspenseful atmosphere to the scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrilling - causing excitement and suspense
  • Antonym: relaxing - producing a feeling of relaxation or calm

Etymology

From 'suspense' (Latin suspensus, 'suspended') + '-ful' (suffix forming adjectives).
