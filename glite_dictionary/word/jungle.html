jungle

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
(historical, North American) A place where hoboes camp, typically at the edge of a town.
hobo campsite (historic, N. America)
american

Examples

During the Great Depression, many men seeking work spent nights in the local jungle.
The railroad police would occasionally clear out a jungle near the station.
Stories of life in the hobo jungle paint a vivid picture of hardship and community.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
a situation or environment characterized by fierce competition or struggle for survival
a fiercely competitive environment

Examples

The corporate world can often feel like a jungle, where only the strongest survive.
It's a jungle out there for new graduates trying to find their first job.
Some people thrive in the legal jungle of high-stakes litigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dog-eat-dog - an environment of ruthless competition

Etymology

From Sanskrit *jangala* (rough and arid land), via Hindi (dense forest).

3. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
a dense, tropical forest, typically in an equatorial area, with tangled vegetation that is difficult to penetrate
dense tropical forest with tangled vegetation

Examples

The explorers hacked their way through the thick jungle for days.
Many rare species are found deep within the Amazon jungle.
Sudden rain quickly turned the jungle paths into muddy streams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rainforest - a luxuriant, dense forest rich in biodiversity, typically found in tropical areas with consistent heavy rainfall

Etymology

From Hindi *jangal* (wilderness, forest), from Sanskrit *jaṅgalaḥ* (dry, arid, desert).
