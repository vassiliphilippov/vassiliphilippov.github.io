tripper

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪpər / (AmE), / ˈtrɪpə / (BrE)
(slang) A person who has taken a psychedelic drug and is experiencing its hallucinogenic effects.
person experiencing psychedelic drug effects
slang

Examples

The club was full of trippers dancing to psychedelic music.
He acted like a tripper even though he hadn't taken any drugs.
The documentary interviewed a longtime tripper about his experiences with LSD.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acid head - a person who uses LSD (slang)
  • Synonym: user - a person who takes drugs (especially habitually)
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪpər / (AmE), / ˈtrɪpə / (BrE)
a catch mechanism or device that acts as a switch, especially one that automatically releases or activates a system when actuated
automatic catch mechanism; switch device

Examples

The machine will not run until the tripper is reset.
When the tripper engages, it automatically shuts off the conveyor belt.
Inspect the safety tripper regularly to ensure it operates properly.

Synset Examples

the pressure activates the tripper and releases the water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: switch - a device for making and breaking the connection in an electric circuit

Etymology

From 'trip' (to release or activate a mechanism) + '-er' (one who or that which).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04492482-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪpər / (AmE), / ˈtrɪpə / (BrE)
a person who makes a short journey for pleasure, often to visit places of interest; a tourist, especially on a brief excursion
person on a short visit for pleasure

Examples

The tripper strolled along the promenade, admiring the sea view.
On weekends, the park is crowded with trippers from nearby cities.
Many tripper buses arrive at the castle during the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tourist - person traveling for pleasure
  • Synonym: excursionist - person going on a short journey, especially for pleasure

Etymology

From the verb 'trip' (to go on a journey), with '-er' forming an agent noun.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10616097-n
Synset POS: n
