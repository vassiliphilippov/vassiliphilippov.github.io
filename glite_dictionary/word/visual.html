visual

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɪʒuəl / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒuəl / (BrE)
relating to seeing or to things that can be seen; visible
relating to seeing; visible

Examples

The visual display at the museum was stunning and captured everyone's attention.
Good lighting is essential for clear visual presentation in photography.
He relies more on visual cues than on verbal instructions.

Synset Examples

be sure of it; give me the ocular proof
a visual presentation
a visual image

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ocular - relating to the eyes or vision
  • Antonym: auditory - relating to hearing

Etymology

From Latin 'visualis', from 'visus' (sight).

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɪʒuəl / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒjʊəl / (BrE)
relating to seeing or sight; connected with the sense of vision
relating to sight or seeing

Examples

The film was a visual masterpiece, with stunning imagery throughout.
She used visual aids to help explain the concept to the audience.
His visual memory is impressive; he can recall faces and places perfectly.

Synset Examples

ocular inspection
an optical illusion
visual powers
visual navigation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ocular - relating to the eyes or vision
  • Synonym: optical - relating to light or the science of optics, often sight

Etymology

From Latin visualis (of sight), from visus (sight).
