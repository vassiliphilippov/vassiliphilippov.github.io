Oryzopsis miliacea

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˌraɪˈzɒpsɪs ˌmɪliˈeɪsə / (AmE), / ɒːˌrɪˈzɒpsɪs ˌmɪlɪˈeɪsɪə / (BrE)
A perennial grass, commonly known as mountain rice, native to the Mediterranean region and introduced to North America.
Mediterranean perennial grass species

Examples

Oryzopsis miliacea is often found growing on rocky slopes in Mediterranean climates.
The spread of Oryzopsis miliacea has affected native grass populations in North America.
Researchers studied the ecological impact of Oryzopsis miliacea in disturbed habitats.

Etymology

From Latinized Greek *oryza* (rice) and *opsis* (appearance), and specific epithet *miliacea* from Latin *milium* (millet).
