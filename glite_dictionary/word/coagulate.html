coagulate

1. VERB

/ koʊˈæɡjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / kəʊˈæɡjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to cause (a liquid) to become thickened or solid, usually by a chemical process
change a liquid to a thickened or solid state

Examples

The milk began to coagulate within a few hours at room temperature.
Blood proteins coagulate to form a clot and stop the bleeding.
Adding vinegar will coagulate the proteins in the cheese-making process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to solidify - to make or become hard or solid
  • Antonym: to liquefy - to make or become liquid

Etymology

From Latin coagulare (to curdle, to clot), from coagulatus, past participle of coagulo.

3. VERB

/ koʊˈæɡjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / kəʊˈæɡjʊleɪt / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to change, or cause to change, into a thickened or solid state, especially as a result of a chemical reaction.
to change from liquid to solid or thickened state

Examples

The milk will coagulate if you add lemon juice to it.
After the injury, his blood began to coagulate and form a clot.
Proteins in the egg white coagulate as it cooks, turning the liquid clear to opaque.

Synset Examples

coagulated blood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to solidify - to make or become hard or solid
  • Antonym: to liquefy - make or become liquid

Etymology

From Latin 'coagulatus', past participle of 'coagulare' (to curdle, to clot).
