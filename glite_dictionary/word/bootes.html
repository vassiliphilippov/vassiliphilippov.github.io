Bootes

1. PROPN

/ boʊˈoʊtiːz / (AmE), / bəʊˈəʊtiːz / (BrE)
a constellation in the Northern Hemisphere, located near Ursa Major, often depicted as a herdsman.
constellation near Ursa Major

Examples

The star Arcturus marks the brightest point in the constellation Bootes.
Bootes is easily visible in the spring night sky for observers in the Northern Hemisphere.
Ancient astronomers often described Bootes as a herdsman chasing the bears, Ursa Major and Ursa Minor.

Etymology

From Latin 'Boötes', from Greek 'Βοώτης' (Boōtēs), meaning 'herdsman' or 'plowman'.
