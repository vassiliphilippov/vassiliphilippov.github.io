acarus

1. NOUN

/ ˈækəɹəs / (AmE), / ˈakərəs / (BrE)
A small mite of the genus Acarus or generally, any mite of the order Acarina.
genus of small mites
medical

Examples

An acarus infestation can cause irritation and inflammation in animals.
Scientists discovered a new species of acarus on the plant leaves.
The veterinarian identified the skin problem as being caused by an acarus.

Etymology

From New Latin acarus, from Greek ákari (mite) + -us (genus suffix).
