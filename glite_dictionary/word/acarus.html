Acarus

1. NOUN

/ əˈker.əs / (AmE), / əˈkeə.rəs / (BrE)
A genus of mites in the order Acarina, including several species that are common as pests or parasites.
a genus of mites (Acarina)
medical

Examples

Many stored grain pests belong to the genus Acarus and can cause significant losses.
Scientists identified the specimen as Acarus siro, commonly known as the flour mite.
Infestation by mites of the Acarus genus can be harmful to both food supplies and humans.

Etymology

From Latin 'acarus', from Greek 'akários' (tiny mite).
