jingle

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
a light, metallic ringing sound, often produced by small metal objects striking each other
a light metallic ringing sound

Examples

The jingle of keys echoed through the empty hallway.
She enjoyed the cheerful jingle of the holiday bells.
I heard a jingle every time he moved his pocket of loose change.

Synset Examples

the jingle of coins
the jangle of spurs

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: jangle - a harsh, discordant ringing sound

Etymology

Imitative origin; from the sound 'jing-' as in 'ring' or 'clang'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07400598-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈdʒɪŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪŋɡəl / (BrE)
to make a light, ringing sound, typically one produced by small metallic objects striking each other
make a light metallic ringing sound

Examples

I could hear the coins jingling at the bottom of her purse.
The bells on the dog's collar jingled as it ran across the yard.
She nervously jingled her keys in her hand while waiting for the door to open.

Synset Examples

The keys were jingling in his pocket

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clink - to make a short, sharp, ringing sound like small metal objects striking each other
  • Degree Higher: clang - to make a loud, resonant, metallic sound

Etymology

From Middle English *jingelen*, of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02177127-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪŋɡəl / (BrE)
A comic or catchy verse, often with irregular rhythm, typically intended to amuse or be memorable.
comic or catchy verse

Examples

The poet's latest jingle brought laughter to everyone at the reading.
As a child, she made up a jingle about her favorite pet.
He found himself humming a silly jingle he had heard on the radio.

Synset Examples

he had heard some silly doggerel that kept running through his mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doggerel - comic verse composed in irregular rhythm

Etymology

From Middle English 'ginglen', of imitative origin, related to making tinkling sounds.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06390876-n
Synset POS: n
