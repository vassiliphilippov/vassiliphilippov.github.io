annihilating

1. ADJ

/ əˈnaɪ.ə.leɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈnaɪ.ɪ.leɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
causing or capable of causing complete destruction or obliteration
causing total destruction

Examples

The army launched an annihilating offensive that left nothing standing in its path.
His annihilating criticism destroyed her confidence.
The city suffered an annihilating blow from the earthquake.

Synset Examples

possessing annihilative power
a devastating hurricane
the guns opened a withering fire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devastating - causing severe destruction or shock
  • Synonym: withering - intended to make someone feel humiliated or small; also causing destruction

Etymology

From Latin 'annihilare' (to reduce to nothing), via annihilate + -ing.

(of words, criticism, or effects) extremely harsh or destructive; causing overwhelming defeat or ruin.
extremely harsh or destructive

Examples

The article delivered an annihilating critique of the politician's record.
She stared back with an annihilating glare that could silence a crowd.

Synset Examples
