microsporophyll

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkroʊˈspɔrəˌfɪl / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊˈspɒrəfɪl / (BrE)
In non-flowering plants, a specialized leaf (sporophyll) that bears only microsporangia, which produce microspores.
leaf bearing microsporangia in non-flowering plants

Examples

The pollen cones of conifers contain many microsporophylls arranged in spirals.
Each microsporophyll typically supports two microsporangia in a male pine cone.
In cycads, the microsporophylls are packed densely around the axis of the cone.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: megasporophyll - a sporophyll that bears megasporangia in non-flowering plants

Etymology

From 'micro-' (small) + 'sporophyll' (spore-bearing leaf), both from Greek origins.
