oryx

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrɪks / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪks / (BrE)
A large antelope native to Africa, having long, straight, almost upright horns.
large African antelope with long horns

Examples

The oryx is well adapted to survive in the arid deserts of Africa.
We spotted a herd of oryxes grazing near the waterhole.
The long horns of the oryx make it a striking sight on the savanna.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antelope - a swift-running deerlike animal, native to Africa and Eurasia

Etymology

From Latin 'oryx', from Greek 'óryx' (antelope; gazelle).
