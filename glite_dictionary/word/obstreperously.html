obstreperously

1. ADV

/ əbˈstrɛpərəsli / (AmE), / ɒbˈstrɛpərəsli / (BrE)
In a noisy, unruly, or attention-attracting manner.
in a noisy, unruly way

Examples

The children laughed and shouted obstreperously as they ran around the playground.
During the meeting, he obstreperously interrupted the speaker several times.
The protesters obstreperously voiced their concerns outside the city hall.

Synset Examples

obstreperously, he demanded to get service

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noisily - in a noisy manner

Etymology

From 'obstreperous' + '-ly', 'obstreperous' from Latin 'obstreperus' (noisily resisting).
