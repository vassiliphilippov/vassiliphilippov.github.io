Astreus pteridis

1. NOUN

/ əˈstriː.əs ˈtɛr.ɪ.dɪs / (AmE), / æˈstriː.əs ˈtɛr.ɪ.dɪs / (BrE)
a large species of earthstar fungus with fruiting bodies up to 15 cm across when the rays are expanded
large earthstar fungus species

Examples

The giant fruit body of Astreus pteridis can sometimes be found in moist, wooded areas.
Mycologists were excited to document a new location for Astreus pteridis in the region this year.
The rays of Astreus pteridis expand fully after a heavy rain, creating a striking star-shaped form.

Etymology

From Greek astraios (of the stars) + pterid- (fern), referring to its star-like appearance and habitat with ferns.
