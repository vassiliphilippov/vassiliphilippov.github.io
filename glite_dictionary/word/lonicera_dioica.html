Lonicera dioica

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈnɪsərə daɪˈoʊɪkə / (AmE), / ləʊˈnɪsərə daɪˈəʊɪkə / (BrE)
A species of honeysuckle, a twining deciduous shrub with clusters of purple-tinged yellow-green flowers, native to northeastern North America.
species of honeysuckle shrub

Examples

The Lonicera dioica in her garden produced clusters of purple-tinged flowers this spring.
Botanists found a healthy population of Lonicera dioica thriving in the forest understory.
Many birds are drawn to the berries that appear on Lonicera dioica in late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeysuckle - climbing shrub with sweet-smelling flowers from the genus Lonicera

Etymology

From the genus name Lonicera, named after German botanist Adam Lonitzer (1528–1586); species epithet 'dioica' means 'of two houses' in Latin, referring to plant sexuality.
