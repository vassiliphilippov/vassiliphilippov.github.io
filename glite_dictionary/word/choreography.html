choreography

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːriˈɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌkɒriˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
a show or arrangement involving the art of designing sequences of movements, especially for dancers
artistic dance arrangement or show

Examples

The school's annual recital featured stunning choreography that delighted the audience.
She is famous for her innovative choreography in contemporary ballet performances.
The choreography combined classical and modern dance styles in an impressive way.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dance - a series of rhythmic steps and movements

Etymology

From Greek khoreia (dance) + -graphy (writing/drawing).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːriˈɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌkɒriˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
The representation of dance movements by symbols or notation, analogous to how music is represented by notes.
notation of dance using symbols

Examples

The school teaches choreography using diagrams and symbols to record each movement.
Researchers study historical choreography to reconstruct lost dances from manuscripts.
She learned the choreography by reading the dance notation rather than watching a video.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notation - A system of symbols representing musical or other information

Etymology

From French 'chorégraphie', from Greek 'khoreia' (dance) and 'graphein' (to write).

3. NOUN

/ ˌkɔriˈɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌkɒriˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the art of designing sequences of movements in which motion, form, or both are specified, often for dance performances
art of designing dance movements

Examples

The choreography for the ballet captivated the entire audience.
She studied choreography at a prestigious performing arts school.
The show's success was due in part to its inventive choreography.

Synset Examples

The choreography for the dance was stunning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dance - a series of rhythmic steps and movements usually performed to music

Etymology

From Greek khoreia (dance) + -graphia (writing).
