shrew

1. NOUN

/ ʃru / (AmE), / ʃruː / (BrE)
A small, mouse-like mammal with a pointed snout, belonging to the family Soricidae and related to moles.
small, mouse-like mammal with a pointed snout

Examples

A shrew scurried through the undergrowth, searching for insects to eat.
Despite its small size, the shrew is known for its voracious appetite.
The field biologist trapped several shrews to study their dietary habits.

Etymology

From Old English 'scrēawa' (the shrew-mouse).

2. NOUN

/ ʃru / (AmE), / ʃruː / (BrE)
An offensive term for a woman who is regarded as bad-tempered, scolding, or nagging.
bad-tempered, nagging woman (offensive)
offensive archaic

Examples

Despite her intelligence, some unfairly labeled her a shrew for speaking her mind.
He grew weary of the constant bickering with the village shrew.
The play features a strong-willed shrew who defies traditional expectations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virago - A domineering, violent, or bad-tempered woman
  • Synonym: termagant - A harsh-tempered or overbearing woman

Etymology

From Old English 'scrēawa' (shrew-mouse), later applied figuratively to women of quarrelsome nature.
