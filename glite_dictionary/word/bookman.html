bookman

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊkmən / (AmE), / ˈbʊkmən / (BrE)
A man who is learned or devoted to study, especially in literature or scholarship.
learned man; scholarly person

Examples

He was a dedicated bookman who spent most of his life surrounded by rare manuscripts.
As a renowned bookman, he was respected for his vast knowledge of classical literature.
The conference attracted every bookman in the region to discuss new trends in scholarship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scholar - a learned person, especially in academic study
  • Synonym: academic - a person who teaches or conducts research at a college or university

Etymology

From book + man, indicating a man of books or learning.
