ministrant

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnɪstrənt / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪstrənt / (BrE)
a person who serves as a minister, especially in a religious service
person serving as minister

Examples

The ministrant led the congregation in prayer during the ceremony.
Several ministrants assisted with the distribution of communion at the church.
As a young ministrant, he dedicated his weekends to serving the parish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minister - a member of the clergy, especially in Protestant churches

Etymology

From Latin *ministrans*, present participle of *ministrare* (to serve).
