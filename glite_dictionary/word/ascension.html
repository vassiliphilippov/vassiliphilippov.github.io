ascension

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɛnʃən / (AmE), / əˈsɛnʃən / (BrE)
the act of moving or rising upwards; upward movement or progression
the act of moving upwards

Examples

The rocket's ascension into the sky was watched by thousands of spectators.
His rapid ascension in the company surprised many of his colleagues.
The ascension of the hot air balloon was slow and steady.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: descent - the act of moving downwards

Etymology

From Latin 'ascensio', from 'ascendere' meaning 'to climb up'.

2. NOUN

/ əˈsɛnʃən / (AmE), / əˈsɛnʃən / (BrE)
the action of rising or moving upward, especially into the air or to a higher position
the act of rising upward

Examples

The sudden ascension of the hot-air balloon drew gasps from the crowd.
Her ascension to the top of the mountain was both challenging and exhilarating.
The rocket's ascension was clearly visible against the morning sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rise - an act of moving upward

3. NOUN

/ əˈsɛnʃən / (AmE), / əˈsɛnʃən / (BrE)
(astronomy) The rising of a star or celestial object above the horizon.
the rising of a star above the horizon

Examples

The ascension of Sirius marks the beginning of the ancient Egyptian calendar.
Just before dawn, the ascension of Venus can be observed over the eastern horizon.
Astronomers carefully track the ascension of various stars throughout the year.

Etymology

From Latin 'ascensio', from 'ascendere' (to climb, to go up).
