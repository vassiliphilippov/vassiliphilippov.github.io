Ascension

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɛnʃən / (AmE), / əˈsɛnʃən / (BrE)
(in Christian belief) the bodily rising of Jesus Christ into heaven on the 40th day after the Resurrection.
Christ's ascent to heaven after the Resurrection

Examples

The church celebrates the Ascension forty days after Easter.
Believers regard the Ascension as a key moment in the life of Jesus.
Many paintings depict the Ascension with Jesus rising above his disciples.

Etymology

From Latin 'ascensio' meaning 'a rising', from 'ascendere' (to climb up).

2. NOUN

/ əˈsɛnʃən / (AmE), / əˈsɛnʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
(in Christianity) the celebration of Christ's ascent into heaven, observed on the 40th day after Easter.
Christian feast marking Christ's ascent to heaven

Examples

The church held a special service for Ascension this year.
Many countries recognize Ascension as a public holiday with various traditions.
Pilgrims traveled to Jerusalem to celebrate Ascension at the holy sites.

Etymology

From Latin 'ascensio(n-)' from 'ascendere' (to climb up).
