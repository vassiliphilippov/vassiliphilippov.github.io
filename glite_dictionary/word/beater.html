beater

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːtər / (AmE), / ˈbiːtə / (BrE)
a person who drives wild animals out of hiding so that they can be hunted
person who drives out game for hunters

Examples

The beater walked through the undergrowth, flushing pheasants towards the waiting hunters.
A group of beaters accompanied the hunters on the annual grouse shoot.
Without a skilled beater, it would be much harder to find the game in dense cover.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hunter - a person who hunts wild animals

2. NOUN

/ ˈbiːtər / (AmE), / ˈbiːtə / (BrE)
a tool or implement used for beating, mixing, or stirring substances
tool for beating or mixing

Examples

She used a beater to mix the eggs until they were frothy.
The electric beater made preparing the cake batter much easier.
He grabbed the old carpet beater to clean the rug outside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whisk - a kitchen tool for mixing by beating

Etymology

From Middle English 'beter', from Old English 'bēatan' (to beat).
