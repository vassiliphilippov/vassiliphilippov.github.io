reproachful

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈproʊtʃfəl / (AmE), / rɪˈprəʊtʃf(ə)l / (BrE)
expressing blame or disapproval; showing or suggesting criticism
expressing blame or disapproval

Examples

She gave him a reproachful glance when he arrived late.
His reproachful tone made her feel instantly guilty.
The teacher's reproachful look silenced the noisy students.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: approving - expressing approval or agreement

Etymology

From Middle French 'reprochier' (to reproach) + English '-ful'.
