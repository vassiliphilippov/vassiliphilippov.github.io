Iris xiphium

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrɪs ˈzɪfiəm / (AmE), / ˈaɪrɪs ˈzɪfiəm / (BrE)
a bulbous iris native to the western Mediterranean region, usually bearing violet-purple flowers
bulbous iris species of the western Mediterranean

Examples

Gardeners often cultivate the Iris xiphium for its striking purple blooms.
The Iris xiphium thrives in sunny, well-drained soils in Mediterranean climates.
Botanists have documented the natural habitats of Iris xiphium in Spain and Portugal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iris - a genus of flowering plants with showy flowers

Etymology

Genus name from Greek iris (rainbow, the flower); species name from Greek xiphion, diminutive of xiphos (sword), referring to the shape of the leaves.
