retributive

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈtrɪbjətɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈtrɪbjʊtɪv / (BrE)
relating to or involving retribution; intended as punishment for wrongdoing
relating to retribution or punishment

Examples

Many legal systems have historically emphasized retributive justice over restorative practices.
The judge delivered a retributive sentence, aiming to penalize the offender for his actions.
Critics argue that retributive punishment does not always lead to rehabilitation.

Synset Examples

retributive justice demands an eye for an eye

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punitive - intended as punishment

Etymology

From Late Latin retributivus, from retributio (recompense, repayment).

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈtrɪb.jʊ.tɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈtrɪb.jʊ.tɪv / (BrE)
relating to or marked by the giving of punishment or reward according to what someone deserves
relating to deserved punishment or reward

Examples

The judge's decision reflected a retributive approach, focusing on punishment rather than rehabilitation.
Many philosophers debate whether retributive justice is the best basis for criminal sentencing.
The new policy is seen as more retributive than restorative, prioritizing penalties over reconciliation.

Synset Examples

retributive justice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punitive - relating to or intended as punishment

Etymology

From Latin retributivus, from retributus, past participle of retribuere (to give back, repay).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01373232-s
Synset POS: s
