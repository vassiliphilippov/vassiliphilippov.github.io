elongated

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈlɔŋˌɡeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈlɒŋˌɡeɪtɪd / (BrE)
having a notably greater length than width; long and slender
long and slender in shape

Examples

The snake had an elongated body that allowed it to move swiftly through narrow spaces.
Her shadow appeared strangely elongated on the pavement at sunset.
He admired the sculpture's graceful, elongated form.

Synset Examples

an elongate tail tapering to a point
the old man's gaunt and elongated frame

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long - measuring great distance from end to end
  • Antonym: short - having little length or duration

Etymology

From Latin elongare (to lengthen), from e- (out) + longus (long).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈlɔŋˌɡeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈlɒŋˌɡeɪtɪd / (BrE)
unusually long in relation to its width; stretched out or lengthened
stretched or lengthened in form

Examples

The snake's elongated body allowed it to move swiftly through narrow passages.
The artist painted figures with elongated limbs to create a unique visual effect.
The leaf has an elongated shape compared to others on the tree.

Synset Examples

Picasso's elongated Don Quixote

the extended airport runways can accommodate larger planes
a prolonged black line across the page

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: long - measuring a great distance from end to end
  • Synonym: extended - drawn out; made longer

Etymology

From Latin 'elongare' (to lengthen), from 'e-' (out) + 'longus' (long).
