cayenne

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈɛn / (AmE), / kaɪˈɛn / (BrE)
A tropical American plant of the capsicum family that bears long, slender, very hot red peppers, often used as a spice.
plant with long, hot red peppers

Examples

The chef grows his own cayenne in the backyard garden for use in spicy dishes.
A mature cayenne plant can produce dozens of bright red peppers each season.
They added chopped cayenne to the sauce to give it extra heat.

Etymology

From French 'Cayenne', from the city of Cayenne in French Guiana.

2. NOUN

/ kaɪˈɛn / (AmE), / kaɪˈen / (BrE)
a long, often twisted, hot red pepper used as a spice or seasoning
type of hot red pepper

Examples

She added a pinch of cayenne to the soup to give it a spicy kick.
The cayenne peppers in the garden turn bright red when they are ripe.
He prefers cayenne over other peppers because of its distinctive heat and flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chili - a hot pepper, often used as a spice
  • Degree Lower: jalapeño - a medium-sized hot green or red chili pepper
  • Degree Higher: habanero - an extremely hot variety of chili pepper

Etymology

From French Cayenne, the capital of French Guiana, where the pepper was originally exported.

3. NOUN

/ kaɪˈɛn / (AmE), / kaɪˈɛn / (BrE)
A hot-tasting red powder made from ground dried pods and seeds of certain varieties of capsicum peppers, used as a spice.
hot red pepper spice

Examples

Add a pinch of cayenne to give the soup an extra kick.
The chef sprinkled cayenne over the roasted vegetables for added heat.
She accidentally used too much cayenne in the recipe, making the dish very spicy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chili powder - a spice blend of ground chili peppers and other ingredients
  • Degree Lower: paprika - a mild to hot spice made from ground red peppers

Etymology

From French Cayenne, originally named after the capital of French Guiana, from where such peppers were exported.
