apron

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.prən / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.prən / (BrE)
In golf, the section of closely mown grass that lies between the fairway and the green.
part of a golf course near the green

Examples

The ball landed softly on the apron before rolling onto the green.
She chipped from the apron to get closer to the hole.
Rain made the apron slick, affecting the players' approach shots.

/ ˈeɪ.prən / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.prən / (BrE)

front part of a theater stage

3. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.prən / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.prən / (BrE)
A garment, typically made of cloth, leather, or plastic, tied around the waist and worn over other clothes to protect them from dirt or stains.
protective garment worn over clothes

Examples

She tied her apron around her waist before starting to cook.
The artist's apron was covered in splotches of paint.
Please remember to wear an apron in the workshop to protect your clothes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smock - a loose protective garment worn over clothes

Etymology

From Middle English 'napron' (misdivided as 'an apron'); from Old French 'naperon', diminutive of 'nappe' (tablecloth).

4. NOUN

/ ˈeɪprən / (AmE), / ˈeɪprən / (BrE)
a paved or hard-surfaced area at an airport where aircraft are parked, loaded, or unloaded
paved area at an airport for aircraft

Examples

The plane waited on the apron until it was cleared for departure.
Maintenance crews were working on the jet parked at the apron.
Passengers are often transferred by bus to the apron where smaller planes await.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: runway - long strip where aircraft take off and land at an airport
  • Synonym: taxiway - path connecting runways with terminals or aprons at an airport
