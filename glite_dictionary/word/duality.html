duality

1. NOUN

/ duˈæləti / (AmE), / djʊˈalɪti / (BrE)
The state of having two different or opposite parts or features; a division into two contrasting or complementary aspects.
the quality of having two parts or natures

Examples

The novel explores the duality of human nature through its main characters.
Quantum physics often deals with the duality of particles behaving both as waves and as discrete objects.
There is a duality in her personality: she is both reserved and outgoing depending on the situation.

Synset Examples

the dichotomy between eastern and western culture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dichotomy - a division into two mutually exclusive or contradictory groups
  • Antonym: unity - the state of being united or forming a whole

Etymology

From Latin dualitas, from dualis (dual).

2. NOUN

/ duˈæləti / (AmE), / djuˈælɪti / (BrE)
(Physics) The property of matter and electromagnetic radiation whereby certain phenomena are best described by wave theory and others by particle theory.
property in physics: wave or particle behaviors

Examples

The duality of light was a groundbreaking discovery in twentieth-century physics.
Quantum mechanics explores the duality of electrons and other subatomic particles.
Physicists debated the duality of matter until new experiments confirmed both wave and particle behaviors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wave-particle duality - The concept that every particle or quantum entity can exhibit both wave and particle properties

Etymology

From Late Latin dualitas (twofoldness), from Latin dualis (dual).

3. NOUN

/ duˈæləti / (AmE), / djuˈælɪti / (BrE)
(geometry) The property in projective geometry where points and planes can be interchanged in the statements and theorems, resulting in corresponding dual statements.
geometric property: interchangeability of points and planes in projective geometry

Examples

The principle of duality allows mathematicians to derive new theorems by interchanging points and planes.
In projective geometry, duality provides a powerful method for proving geometric results.
Understanding duality is essential for solving many problems in advanced geometry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reciprocity - mutual relationship or correspondence, especially in geometry or algebra

Etymology

From Late Latin dualitas (the state of being two or double), from Latin dualis (dual).
