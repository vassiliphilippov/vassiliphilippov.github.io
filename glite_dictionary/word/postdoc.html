postdoc

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊstˌdɑk / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstˌdɒk / (BrE)
a scholar or researcher engaged in academic work after earning a doctoral degree, usually on a temporary basis
scholar doing research after a doctorate

Examples

After defending her thesis, she started working as a postdoc at a major university.
The chemistry department is looking to hire a postdoc for their latest research project.
Many postdocs hope to secure permanent faculty positions after completing their fellowship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postdoctoral - relating to study or research done after a doctoral degree

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'postdoctoral'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊs(t)ˌdɑk / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstˌdɒk / (BrE)
a scholar or researcher engaged in academic work after completing a doctoral degree
researcher post-PhD

Examples

After earning her PhD in physics, she accepted a postdoc position at a leading university.
The lab relies heavily on the skills of its postdocs to conduct advanced experiments.
He spent three years as a postdoc before landing a faculty job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postdoctoral - relating to academic work done after a doctoral degree

Etymology

Shortened from 'postdoctoral', from Latin 'post' (after) + 'doctor' (teacher, doctorate).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊstˌdɑk / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstˌdɒk / (BrE)
A grant or fellowship that provides funding for postdoctoral study or research.
grant for postdoctoral research

Examples

She was awarded a postdoc at a top research institute in Switzerland.
Many scientists rely on a postdoc to fund their research projects after earning a PhD.
He applied for several postdocs to continue his work in molecular biology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postdoctoral fellowship - a fellowship awarded for postdoctoral research

Etymology

Shortened from 'postdoctoral', from Latin post- (after) + doctor (teacher).
