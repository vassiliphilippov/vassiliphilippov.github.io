echidna

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈkɪdnə / (AmE), / ɪˈkɪdnə / (BrE)
A small, spiny, egg-laying mammal native to Australia and New Guinea, with a long snout and claws for hunting ants and termites.
spiny egg-laying mammal of Australia and New Guinea

Examples

The echidna uses its sticky tongue to catch ants and termites.
An echidna was spotted crossing the dirt road near the forest edge.
Unlike most mammals, the echidna lays eggs rather than giving birth to live young.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: platypus - another egg-laying mammal (monotreme) of Australia

Etymology

From Greek 'ekhidna' (a viper), used in taxonomic naming due to its unusual appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈkɪdnə / (AmE), / ɪˈkɪdnə / (BrE)
a small, spiny, egg-laying mammal native to Australia and New Guinea, with a long snout and claws used to hunt ants and termites
spiny egg-laying mammal from Australia

Examples

The echidna curled into a ball, using its spines as protection from predators.
Scientists studied the echidna to better understand how monotremes lay eggs.
An echidna was spotted foraging for ants in the outback during the early morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiny anteater - another name for an echidna
  • Synonym: platypus - egg-laying mammal found in Australia
  • Degree Higher: mammal - warm-blooded vertebrate animal with hair or fur, typically giving birth to live young

Etymology

From Greek 'ekhídna' (viper), used in zoology since the 18th century due to the animal's appearance.
