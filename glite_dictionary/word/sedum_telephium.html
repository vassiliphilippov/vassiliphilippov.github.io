Sedum telephium

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːdəm tɪˈlɛfiəm / (AmE), / ˈsiːdəm tɛˈliːfiəm / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to northern temperate regions, with toothed leaves and heads of small purplish-white flowers; commonly known as orpine.
a perennial plant with purplish-white flowers

Examples

The garden border was lined with Sedum telephium, which bloomed late into autumn.
Many gardeners plant Sedum telephium because it attracts butterflies and tolerates drought well.
We identified the succulent as Sedum telephium by its thick leaves and purple-tinged flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orpine - Common name for Sedum telephium

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Sedum' refers to the genus of stonecrops and 'telephium' refers to the specific species, historically linked to the mythological figure Telephus.
