Lapp

1. NOUN

/ læp / (AmE), / læp / (BrE)
an old term for the language spoken by the Sámi people in northern Scandinavia and the Kola Peninsula; now usually called Sámi
old term for Sámi language
archaic offensive

Examples

The Lapp language is still taught in some schools in northern Scandinavia.
She studied traditional folklore written in Lapp while living in Finland.
Many linguists prefer the term 'Sámi' to 'Lapp' when referring to the language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sámi - language spoken by the indigenous Sámi people of northern Europe

Etymology

From Swedish Lapp (referring to the Sámi people or their language).

2. NOUN

/ læp / (AmE), / læp / (BrE)
an indigenous person from northern Scandinavia, traditionally known for reindeer herding; now more commonly referred to as a Sami. The term 'Lapp' is considered outdated and potentially offensive.
an indigenous person from northern Scandinavia (outdated, offensive)
offensive

Examples

The museum exhibit explores the cultural traditions of the Lapp people and their relationship with reindeer.
He traveled to northern Norway to meet with a group of Lapps living near the Arctic Circle.
Modern descendants of the Lapp community often identify as Sami and advocate for the use of that name.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sami - the preferred term for the indigenous people of northern Scandinavia

Etymology

From Swedish 'lapp', of uncertain origin, used historically for the Sami people.
