infliction

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈflɪkʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈflɪkʃən / (BrE)
the act of imposing something unpleasant or unwelcome, such as a tax, punishment, or restriction, on someone or something
act of imposing something unwelcome

Examples

The infliction of higher taxes caused widespread protest among the citizens.
Some organizations criticized the infliction of trade sanctions as being too harsh.
The court ruled against the infliction of additional penalties without proper justification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imposition - the act of establishing or applying by authority

Etymology

From Latin 'inflictio', from 'inflictus', past participle of 'infligere' (to strike against).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈflɪk.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈflɪk.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of causing pain, damage, or suffering to someone or something
the act of causing pain or damage

Examples

The infliction of unnecessary suffering is prohibited by law.
Many viewed his criticism as an unfair infliction of blame.
The victim struggled to recover from the infliction of emotional trauma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imposition - the act of establishing or applying by authority, especially something unwelcome
  • Antonym: relief - the removal or reduction of something painful or burdensome

Etymology

From Latin 'inflictio', from 'inflictus', past participle of 'infligere' (to strike against).

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈflɪkʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈflɪkʃən / (BrE)
a person or thing that causes annoyance, discomfort, or unhappiness; a source of trouble or suffering
person or thing causing annoyance or trouble

Examples

The extra paperwork was an unexpected infliction on the already busy employees.
She always thought of her brother as an infliction when he played loud music.
The constant noise from the construction site became a daily infliction for the residents.

Synset Examples

a bit of a bother
he's not a friend, he's an infliction
washing dishes was a pain in the neck before we got a dish washer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuisance - a person, thing, or circumstance causing inconvenience or annoyance
  • Antonym: blessing - a beneficial thing for which one is grateful; something that brings happiness

Etymology

From Latin 'inflictio', from 'inficere' (to strike on).
