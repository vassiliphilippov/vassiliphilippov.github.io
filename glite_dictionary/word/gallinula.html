Gallinula

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈlɪnjʊlə / (AmE), / ɡəˈlɪnjʊlə / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the rail family, including species commonly known as gallinules, found in wetlands worldwide.
genus of birds (gallinules)

Examples

Wetlands in Europe are home to several species of Gallinula.
Researchers observed the nesting habits of Gallinula during the spring migration.
The birdwatcher was excited to spot a rare Gallinula near the marsh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gallinule - common name for birds of the genus Gallinula

Etymology

From Latin 'gallinula', diminutive of 'gallina' (hen, chicken).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡælɪˈnuːlə / (AmE), / ˌɡælɪˈnjuːlə / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the rail family, including gallinules, notable for their marsh-dwelling habits and long toes.
genus of marsh-dwelling birds (gallinules)

Examples

The Gallinula are characterized by their long toes and preference for wetlands.
Several species within the genus Gallinula can be found across various continents.
Biologists discovered a new population of Gallinula in the remote marshlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gallinule - a marsh bird of the family Rallidae, member of genus Gallinula

Etymology

From Latin 'gallinula', diminutive of 'gallina' (hen).
