FAO

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛf.eɪˈoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɛf.eɪˈəʊ / (BrE)
an international organization, the Food and Agriculture Organization, established by the United Nations to improve global food security and agricultural standards
UN agency for food and agriculture

Examples

The FAO is working to eradicate hunger in developing countries.
Many reports on global food security are published by the FAO each year.
She attended a conference organized by the FAO in Rome.

Etymology

Acronym from Food and Agriculture Organization, established 1945.
