souther

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌðər / (AmE), / ˈsʌðə / (BrE)
a wind that blows from the south
wind from the south

Examples

A hot souther swept across the plains, bringing clouds and humidity.
The sailors braced themselves as a strong souther picked up speed.
After days of cold, a brief souther brought warmer weather to the coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: southerly - a wind coming from the south
  • Antonym: norther - a wind coming from the north

Etymology

Formed from 'south' with the -er suffix, by analogy with 'easter' and 'norther'.
