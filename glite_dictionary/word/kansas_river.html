Kansas River

1. PROPN

/ ˈkænzəs ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈkænzəs ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
A river in northeastern Kansas, flowing east to join the Missouri River.
river in northeastern Kansas

Examples

The Kansas River is known for its scenic beauty and popularity among kayakers.
Heavy rains caused the Kansas River to swell, leading to minor flooding in the area.
Several species of fish can be found in the Kansas River throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Missouri River - major river in the central United States

Etymology

Named after the Kansa Native American tribe, also the source for the state's name.
