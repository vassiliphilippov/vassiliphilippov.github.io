dudeen

1. NOUN

/ duːˈdin / (AmE), / djuːˈdiːn / (BrE)
A clay tobacco pipe with a short stem, traditionally used in Ireland and Scotland.
short-stemmed clay tobacco pipe
archaic

Examples

The old man puffed slowly on his dudeen as he told stories by the fire.
She found an antique dudeen among her grandfather's possessions.
Collectors prize the short clay dudeen for its unique shape and historical significance.

Etymology

From Irish 'dúidín' (small pipe), diminutive of 'dúd' (pipe).
