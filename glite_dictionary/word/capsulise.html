to capsulise

1. VERB

/ ˈkæp.sə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˈkap.sjʊ.laɪz / (BrE)
To put something into a concise form; to summarize or reduce in length or detail.
summarize concisely

Examples

Journalists often capsulise lengthy reports for time-pressed audiences.
Could you capsulise your argument into a few sentences?
The editor capsulised the main points for the summary section.

Synset Examples

capsulize the news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to summarize - to give a summary of something
  • Antonym: to expand - to make something longer or more detailed

Etymology

From 'capsule' + '-ise', meaning to put in the form of a capsule, metaphorically to condense.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00245302-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈkæpsjəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkæpsjʊlaɪz / (BrE)
to enclose something in a capsule, especially for preservation or administration, such as in pharmaceuticals
enclose in a capsule

Examples

The company plans to capsulise the powder for easier consumption.
Researchers have developed a method to capsulise the drug for targeted delivery.
After extraction, vitamins are often capsulised to preserve their potency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to encapsulate - to enclose in or as if in a capsule

Etymology

From 'capsule' + '-ise', modeled after analogous terms in pharmaceutical English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01583919-v
Synset POS: v
