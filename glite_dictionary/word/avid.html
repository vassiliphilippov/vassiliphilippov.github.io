avid

1. ADJ

/ ˈævɪd / (AmE), / ˈævɪd / (BrE)
Having or showing a keen interest or enthusiasm for something.
keenly enthusiastic; eager

Examples

She is an avid reader who finishes several books a month.
His avid interest in photography has led him to take thousands of pictures.
They became avid supporters of the local football team.

Synset Examples

an avid sports fan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eager - strongly wanting to do or have something
  • Synonym: enthusiastic - showing intense and eager enjoyment or interest
  • Antonym: apathetic - showing or feeling no interest or enthusiasm

Etymology

From Latin 'avidus' (eager, greedy), from 'avēre' (to crave).

2. ADJ

/ ˈævɪd / (AmE), / ˈavɪd / (BrE)
having or showing a keen desire for something; very enthusiastic or eager, often to an excessive degree.
keenly desirous or enthusiastic

Examples

She is an avid reader who finishes several books each month.
His avid interest in photography led him to start his own studio.
The students were avid for new experiences during their summer abroad.

Synset Examples

avid for adventure
an avid ambition to succeed
fierce devouring affection
the esurient eyes of an avid curiosity
greedy for fame

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eager - very keen or enthusiastic
  • Antonym: indifferent - having no particular interest or sympathy; unconcerned

Etymology

From Latin 'avidus' (greedy), from 'avēre' (to desire).
