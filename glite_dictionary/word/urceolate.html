urceolate

1. ADJ

/ ˈɜr.si.əˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈɜː.si.ə.leɪt / (BrE)
(of a part or organ) shaped like an urn, being swollen or rounded at the bottom and constricted toward the opening.
urn-shaped; swollen below, constricted at mouth

Examples

The urceolate flowers of the plant are a key feature for its identification.
Several species of fungi produce urceolate fruiting bodies.
The botanist noted the urceolate shape of the calyx in his report.

Etymology

From Latin urceolus (little pitcher), diminutive of urceus (pitcher, jug).
