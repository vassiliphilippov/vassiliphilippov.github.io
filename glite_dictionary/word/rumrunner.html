rumrunner

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌmˌrʌnər / (AmE), / ˈrʌmˌrʌnə / (BrE)
a person who illegally transports or smuggles alcoholic beverages, especially across a border or during Prohibition
smuggler of alcoholic beverages

Examples

The coast guard intercepted a rumrunner attempting to bring whiskey into the country.
During Prohibition, rumrunners became notorious for their daring nighttime voyages.
That old warehouse was once a hideout for rumrunners smuggling liquor from Canada.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bootlegger - someone who illegally makes, distributes, or sells alcohol

Etymology

Formed from "rum" (an alcoholic drink) + "runner" (someone who transports something illicitly); popularized during American Prohibition (1920s).
