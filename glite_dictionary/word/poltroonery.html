poltroonery

1. NOUN

/ pɑlˈtruːnəri / (AmE), / pɒlˈtruːnəri / (BrE)
Abject or contemptible cowardice; complete lack of courage.
utter cowardice

Examples

His poltroonery in the face of danger shocked even his lifelong friends.
History does not look kindly on acts of poltroonery committed during times of crisis.
The general was court-martialed for accusations of poltroonery on the battlefield.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowardice - lack of bravery or courage
  • Antonym: bravery - courageous behavior or character

Etymology

From French 'poltronnerie', from 'poltron' (coward).
