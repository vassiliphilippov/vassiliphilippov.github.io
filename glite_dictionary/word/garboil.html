garboil

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑr.bɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈɡɑː.bɔɪl / (BrE)
A state of noisy confusion or commotion.
commotion; noisy confusion
archaic

Examples

The market square was in complete garboil after the unexpected announcement.
Historians recall the garboil that erupted in the court during the trial.
The adventurers found themselves lost in the garboil of the bustling city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tumult - a loud, confused noise, especially from a crowd
  • Synonym: uproar - a loud or impassioned noise or disturbance
  • Antonym: tranquility - a state of calm and peace

Etymology

From Old French *garbouil*, from Italian *garbuglio* (confusion, muddle).
