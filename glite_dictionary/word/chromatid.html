chromatid

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊ.mə.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊ.mə.tɪd / (BrE)
either of the two identical strands into which a chromosome divides during cell division, joined by a centromere until separated.
one of two identical strands of a chromosome

Examples

During mitosis, each chromosome consists of two chromatids joined together.
A chromatid separates from its sister during anaphase of cell division.
Scientists observed the behavior of each chromatid under a microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chromosome - a thread-like structure of DNA and protein found in cells, carrying genetic information

Etymology

From Greek khrōma (color) + -id, used in scientific naming.
