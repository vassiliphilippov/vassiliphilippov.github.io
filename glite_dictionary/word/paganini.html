Paganini

1. PROPN

/ ˌpɑːɡəˈniːni / (AmE), / ˌpaɡəˈniːni / (BrE)
Niccolò Paganini (1782–1840), renowned Italian violinist and composer known for his virtuosity and influential compositions for the violin.
Italian violinist and composer (1782–1840)

Examples

Many consider Paganini one of the greatest violin virtuosos in history.
The violin concerto composed by Paganini remains a staple in classical music repertoire.
Aspiring violinists often study techniques pioneered by Paganini to improve their playing.

Etymology

From Italian Paganini, surname of Niccolò Paganini.
