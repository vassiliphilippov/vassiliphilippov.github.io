chalcidfly

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.sɪdˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkalsɪdflaɪ / (BrE)
A very small wasp-like insect whose larvae are parasites on the eggs and larvae of other insects, often used as a natural control of pests.
tiny parasitic wasp-like insect

Examples

The chalcidfly is often used in agriculture to control populations of harmful pests.
Scientists observed a chalcidfly depositing its eggs inside the larvae of crop-damaging beetles.
Gardeners appreciate the role of the chalcidfly in maintaining the balance of garden ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wasp - a winged insect with a narrow waist and stinger

Etymology

From Greek chalkos (copper or bronze), from the metallic color of some species, plus 'fly'.
