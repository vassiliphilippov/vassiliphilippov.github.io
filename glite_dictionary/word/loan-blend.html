loan-blend

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊnˌblɛnd / (AmE), / ˈləʊnˌblɛnd / (BrE)
A word formed by combining elements from different languages, especially in its prefix, root, or suffix.
word with elements from different languages

Examples

The word skyscraper is not a loan-blend, but 'monolingual' is, combining Greek and Latin roots.
English has several loan-blends such as 'television', which mixes Greek and Latin elements.
Linguistics students often study loan-blends to understand how languages influence each other.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loanword - a word adopted from another language with little or no modification
  • Synonym: hybrid - a word composed of elements from different languages

Etymology

Formed from 'loan' (here, meaning borrowed element) + 'blend'.
