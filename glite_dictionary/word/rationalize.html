to rationalize

1. VERB

/ ˈræʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈræʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
(Mathematics) To remove irrational quantities, such as roots, from the denominator or numerator of a mathematical expression.
remove irrational quantities in mathematics

Examples

To simplify the fraction, first rationalize the denominator.
You need to rationalize the expression before completing the calculation.
Many students struggle to rationalize square roots in algebra.

Synset Examples

This function can be rationalized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to simplify - make a mathematical expression easier to work with

Etymology

From Latin 'ratio' (reason) and the suffix '-ize'.

2. VERB

/ ˈræʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈræʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
to organize or manage (a system, organization, or process) according to rational or scientific methods, often to make it more efficient
make (a system) more efficient or logical

Examples

The company decided to rationalize its manufacturing process to reduce costs and increase productivity.
The government aimed to rationalize public services by implementing new technologies and management strategies.
They rationalized the workflow to eliminate unnecessary steps and duplication of effort.

Synset Examples

We rationalized the factory's production and raised profits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to streamline - to improve the efficiency of a process or organization
  • Synonym: to organize - to arrange systematically; order

Etymology

From Latin ratio (reason) + -ize (forming verbs).
