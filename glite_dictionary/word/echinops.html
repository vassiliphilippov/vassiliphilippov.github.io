Echinops

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈkaɪnɑps / (AmE), / ɪˈkaɪnɒps / (BrE)
A genus of Mediterranean and Eurasian herbs with globular, thistle-like flower heads, commonly known as globe thistles.
a genus of globe thistles

Examples

Several species of Echinops are cultivated for their striking blue, spherical flowers.
The dry hillsides are dotted with blooming Echinops in midsummer.
Gardeners appreciate Echinops for attracting bees and butterflies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: globe thistle - common name for plants of the genus Echinops

Etymology

From Greek ekhinops, meaning 'like a hedgehog', referring to the spiny appearance of the flower heads.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈkaɪnɑps / (AmE), / ɪˈkaɪnɒps / (BrE)
A genus of Mediterranean and Eurasian herbs known as globe thistles, characterized by spiny leaves and spherical blue or white flower heads.
genus of globe thistle plants

Examples

The garden was filled with Echinops, their spherical blue flowers attracting bees and butterflies.
Botanists classify globe thistles under the genus Echinops.
Dried Echinops heads make striking additions to floral arrangements.

Etymology

From Greek ekhinos (hedgehog, sea urchin) + -ops (appearance), referring to the spiny flower heads.
