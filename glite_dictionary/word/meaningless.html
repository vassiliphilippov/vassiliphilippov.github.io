meaningless

1. ADJ

/ ˈmiːnɪŋləs / (AmE), / ˈmiːnɪŋləs / (BrE)
having no meaning, sense, value, or purpose; lacking significance
having no meaning or purpose

Examples

After years at a job he disliked, he found his work increasingly meaningless.
The symbols on the wall appeared meaningless to anyone who did not understand the code.
She was frustrated by the endless, meaningless paperwork required by the bureaucracy.

Synset Examples

a meaningless endeavor
a meaningless life
a verbose but meaningless explanation

Related Terms

  • Antonym: significant - having meaning or importance
  • Synonym: purposeless - lacking a clear aim or intention
  • Synonym: insignificant - having little or no importance or meaning

Etymology

From 'meaning' + '-less', with '-less' indicating absence or lack.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmiːnɪŋləs / (AmE), / ˈmiːnɪŋləs / (BrE)
Having no effect, result, or purpose; lacking significance or value.
having no effect or purpose

Examples

After hours of work, his efforts seemed meaningless when nothing changed.
She was frustrated by the meaningless meetings that produced no decisions.
The argument quickly became meaningless as both sides refused to listen.

Synset Examples

a futile effort
the therapy was ineffectual
an otiose undertaking
an unavailing attempt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: futile - incapable of producing any useful result
  • Synonym: ineffectual - not producing the intended effect
  • Antonym: purposeful - having a clear aim or purpose

Etymology

From 'meaning' + suffix '-less' (without).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02507270-s
Synset POS: s
