to intumesce

1. VERB

/ ˌɪn.tuˈmɛs / (AmE), / ˌɪn.tjuːˈmɛs / (BrE)
to swell up or expand, especially as a result of heating, often forming bubbles; to bubble up, sometimes used metaphorically
swell or bubble up (esp. from heat)

Examples

The plastic coating will intumesce and form an insulating layer when exposed to high temperatures.
When heated, the substance began to intumesce, releasing small bubbles of gas.
New ideas seemed to intumesce in his mind as the discussion progressed.

Synset Examples

Gases bubbled up from the earth
Marx's ideas have bubbled up in many places in Latin America

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swell - expand in size or volume, often gradually
  • Antonym: to deflate - reduce or collapse in volume, especially after swelling

Etymology

From Latin intumescere (to swell up), from in- (into) + tumescere (to begin to swell).

2. VERB

/ ˌɪn.tuˈmɛs / (AmE), / ˌɪn.tjuːˈmɛs / (BrE)
to swell or enlarge abnormally, especially due to the absorption of fluid or as a reaction to heat
swell or expand abnormally

Examples

The tissue began to intumesce after prolonged exposure to heat.
Some minerals intumesce when subjected to high temperatures, forming a protective barrier.
After absorbing water, the tablet started to intumesce noticeably.

Synset Examples

The bellies of the starving children are swelling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swell - to become larger or rounder in size, typically as a result of an accumulation of fluid

Etymology

From Latin intumescere, from in- (in, into) + tumescere (to swell up).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
