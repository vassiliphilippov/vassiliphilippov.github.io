Deimos

1. PROPN

/ ˈdaɪˌmɒs / (AmE), / ˈdiːmɒs / (BrE)
the smaller and outermost of the two natural satellites of Mars
outer moon of Mars

Examples

Astronomers have studied Deimos since its discovery in 1877.
The spacecraft sent back detailed images of Deimos as it orbited Mars.
Together with Phobos, Deimos makes up the two moons of Mars.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Phobos - the larger and innermost of the two natural satellites of Mars

Etymology

From Greek Δείμος (Deimos), meaning 'terror', son of Ares (Mars) in Greek mythology.
