crosscheck

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsˌtʃɛk / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌtʃɛk / (BrE)
an act of confirming or verifying information by comparing findings from multiple sources
confirmation by comparing sources

Examples

The auditor performed a crosscheck using sales records from three different departments.
A simple crosscheck of the numbers revealed a discrepancy in the report.
Journalists rely on crosscheck to ensure the accuracy of their stories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to verify - to make sure or demonstrate that something is true, accurate, or justified
  • Synonym: to confirm - to establish the truth or correctness of something

Etymology

From 'cross-' (indicating mutual action) + 'check' (to verify or examine), first attested in the mid-20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɑsˌtʃɛk / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌtʃɛk / (BrE)
An illegal move in ice hockey or similar sports, where a player checks an opponent using the shaft of their stick held with both hands.
illegal ice hockey check with stick

Examples

The referee called a penalty for a crosscheck against the defenseman.
He was sent to the penalty box after delivering a hard crosscheck to his opponent.
A deliberate crosscheck can result in a serious injury and suspension.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bodycheck - a legal move to block or stop an opponent in ice hockey
