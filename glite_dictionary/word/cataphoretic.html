cataphoretic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkæt̬.ə.fəˈrɛt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌkæt.ə.fəˈrɛt.ɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or involving electrophoresis, especially the movement of particles through a fluid under the influence of an electric field.
relating to electrophoresis

Examples

The cataphoretic deposition method is widely used to coat metals with protective layers.
Researchers studied the cataphoretic behavior of different particles in solution.
A cataphoretic process was employed to ensure even distribution of the nanoparticles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electrophoretic - Relating to electrophoresis

Etymology

From Greek kata- (down) + phorein (to carry), referring to movement under electric current.
