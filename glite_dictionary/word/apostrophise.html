to apostrophise

1. VERB

/ əˈpɑːstrəˌfaɪz / (AmE), / əˈpɒstrəfaɪz / (BrE)
to use an apostrophe when writing or spelling a word
use an apostrophe
british

Examples

In British English, writers commonly apostrophise words like 'it's' or 'can't'.
If you don't apostrophise correctly, your writing may appear careless.
She carefully apostrophised all the contractions in her essay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to apostrophize - to use an apostrophe (American spelling)

Etymology

From apostrophe + -ise, following British spelling conventions.
