LISP

1. NOUN

/ lɪsp / (AmE), / lɪsp / (BrE)
a flexible, procedure-oriented computer programming language designed for symbolic manipulation and processing lists, widely used in artificial intelligence research
a computer programming language for symbolic processing

Examples

Many early artificial intelligence applications were written in LISP.
She learned both Python and LISP as part of her computer science degree.
LISP supports recursive functions and symbolic computation, making it popular in academic circles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scheme - a minimalist dialect of LISP programming language
  • Antonym: Prolog - a logic programming language often contrasted with LISP in AI

Etymology

An acronym for 'LISt Processing language', developed in the late 1950s.
