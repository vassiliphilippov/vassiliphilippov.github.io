savarin

1. NOUN

/ ˈsævərɪn / (AmE), / ˈsavərɪn / (BrE)
A light sponge cake baked in a ring mold, usually soaked in syrup and sometimes filled with cream or fruit.
ring-shaped sponge cake

Examples

For dessert, she served a delicious savarin soaked in orange liqueur and topped with whipped cream.
The bakery's famous savarin is filled with fresh berries and custard.
He learned to bake a classic savarin during his pastry course in Paris.

Etymology

French, named after Brillat-Savarin, a French gourmet and writer.
