hungry

1. ADJ

/ ˈhʌŋɡri / (AmE), / ˈhʌŋɡri / (BrE)
having a strong desire or craving for something, often used figuratively (usually followed by 'for').
having a strong desire (figurative)

Examples

After months of hard work, she was hungry for recognition from her peers.
The young actor is hungry for success and willing to work day and night.
Reporters are always hungry for the latest news and inside information.

Synset Examples

athirst for knowledge
hungry for recognition
thirsty for information

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eager - having keen or enthusiastic desire
  • Synonym: craving - an intense desire for something
  • Antonym: satisfied - having needs or desires fulfilled

Etymology

From Old English *hungriġ* (hungry), related to *hunger*.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhʌŋɡri / (AmE), / ˈhʌŋɡri / (BrE)
feeling a need or desire to eat; experiencing the sensation of hunger
feeling the need to eat

Examples

I skipped breakfast this morning, so I'm really hungry now.
The hungry children rushed to the dining hall as soon as the bell rang.
After a long hike, everyone felt hungry and ready for a big meal.

Synset Examples

a world full of hungry people

Related Terms

  • Antonym: full - having eaten enough; not hungry
  • Degree Higher: starving - very hungry; suffering from extreme hunger
  • Degree Lower: peckish - slightly hungry (British informal)

Etymology

From Old English 'hungrig', from 'hungor' (hunger), related to Dutch 'hongerig', German 'hungrig'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01272183-a
Synset POS: a
