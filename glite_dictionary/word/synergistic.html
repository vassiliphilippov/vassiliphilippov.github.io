synergistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɪnɚˈdʒɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɪnəˈdʒɪstɪk / (BrE)
involving or resulting from the interaction or cooperation of two or more agents to produce a combined effect greater than the sum of their separate effects
working together for enhanced effect

Examples

The researchers found that the drugs had a synergistic effect when combined.
The project succeeded due to the synergistic collaboration of experts from different fields.
These companies formed a synergistic partnership to innovate more effectively.

Synset Examples

a synergistic effect

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cooperative - working jointly towards the same end
  • Antonym: antagonistic - acting in opposition or hostility

Etymology

From Greek 'synergos' (working together), via synergy + -istic.

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɪnɚˈdʒɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɪnəˈdʒɪstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to the theological doctrine of synergism, which holds that human will cooperates with divine grace in the process of salvation.
linked to the doctrine of synergism (theology)

Examples

The council's debate focused on whether salvation was synergistic or monergistic in nature.
Her essay examines the implications of a synergistic approach to human free will and divine grace.
Some denominations hold a firmly synergistic view of how believers attain salvation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synergism - the theological doctrine that salvation involves cooperation between divine grace and human will
  • Antonym: monergistic - relating to the doctrine that salvation is entirely the work of divine grace without human cooperation

Etymology

From Greek 'synergos' (working together); applied in theology to the concept of cooperation between human will and divine grace.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02958225-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˌsɪnərˈdʒɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɪnəˈdʒɪstɪk / (BrE)
working together in a way that produces an effect greater than the sum of their separate effects, especially referring to drugs or muscles.
working together for enhanced effect

Examples

The two medications were found to have a synergistic effect when used together.
Scientists are studying how the synergistic actions of different muscles improve athletic performance.
A synergistic team can achieve far more than individuals working separately.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: antagonistic - acting in opposition so that the combined effect is less than that of the individual effects

Etymology

From Greek 'synergos' (working together), via synergy + -istic.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00626030-a
Synset POS: a
