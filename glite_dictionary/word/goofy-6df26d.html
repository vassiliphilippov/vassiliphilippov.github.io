goofy

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡuːfi / (AmE), / ˈɡuːfi / (BrE)
silly, ridiculous, or behaving in a foolish way; amusingly absurd.
silly or foolish
informal

Examples

He wore a goofy grin throughout the entire meeting.
Her goofy jokes never fail to make our group laugh.
That was a goofy idea, but somehow it worked out in the end.

Synset Examples

wore a goofy hat
a silly idea
some wacky plan for selling more books

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silly - showing a lack of good sense or judgment; absurd
  • Antonym: serious - acting or speaking sincerely and in a thoughtful way

Etymology

Probably from 'goof' (a foolish person), American English slang of uncertain origin, with '-y' forming the adjective.
