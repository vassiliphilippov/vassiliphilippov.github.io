Agave americana

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡɑːvi əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnə / (AmE), / əˈɡɑːvi əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnə / (BrE)
a large, monocarpic plant native to the Americas, cultivated for its rosette of spiny leaves and tall flowering stalk; blooms once after many years and then dies
large American agave plant, blooms once after years

Examples

The Agave americana produces a towering stalk when it finally blooms after many years.
Gardeners prize Agave americana for its striking, spiky leaves and dramatic flowering.
After blooming just once, the Agave americana plant typically dies, leaving behind new shoots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: century plant - common name for Agave americana, named for its rare blooming cycle

Etymology

From Latin, where 'agave' is from Greek 'agauos' (noble) and 'americana' indicates American origin.
