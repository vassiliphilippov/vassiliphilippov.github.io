baggage

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæɡɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbæɡɪdʒ / (BrE)
the portable equipment and supplies carried by an army, especially for use by soldiers or in camp
army equipment and supplies

Examples

The army's baggage train stretched for miles behind the marching troops.
During the retreat, much of the baggage was abandoned to speed up the escape.
Guards were assigned to protect the baggage from enemy raiders and thieves.

Etymology

From Old French 'bagage' (luggage, equipment), from 'baguer' (to tie up).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæɡɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbæɡɪdʒ / (BrE)
suitcases, bags, or other containers used to carry personal belongings when traveling
suitcases and bags for travel

Examples

She checked her baggage at the airport before boarding her flight.
Travelers are allowed only one piece of baggage in the cabin.
His baggage was lost somewhere between London and Paris.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luggage - suitcases and bags used for traveling

Etymology

From Old French 'bagage', from 'baguer' (to tie up), related to 'bag'.

archaic offensive slang
