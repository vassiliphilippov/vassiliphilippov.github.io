distension

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsten.ʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈsten.ʃən / (BrE)
the state or process of being expanded or swollen due to pressure from within, especially in a bodily organ or part
swelling or expansion by internal pressure

Examples

The doctor noted significant abdominal distension during the examination.
Gas can cause painful distension of the intestines in some patients.
The rapid distension of the balloon made it likely to burst.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swelling - abnormal enlargement of a body part or area

Etymology

From Latin 'distensio' (a stretching apart), from 'distendere' (to stretch out).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈstɛnʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈstɛnʃən / (BrE)
the state or process of being stretched, enlarged, or swollen beyond normal dimensions
the act of being stretched or swollen

Examples

The patient's abdomen showed significant distension after the meal.
Gas buildup can lead to uncomfortable distension of the intestines.
The doctor noted mild distension in the child's stomach during the examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expansion - the process of becoming larger or more extensive
  • Antonym: compression - the act of pressing or being pressed together

Etymology

From Latin 'distensio' (a stretching apart), from 'distendere' (to stretch out).
