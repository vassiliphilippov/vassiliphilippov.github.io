menthol

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛnˌθɔl / (AmE), / ˈmɛnθɒl / (BrE)
A crystalline substance with a minty odor, used in medicines and lotions to provide a cooling sensation.
minty compound used in flavours and medicines

Examples

Applying a menthol lotion can help soothe sore muscles after exercise.
Many cough drops contain menthol to relieve throat irritation.
The menthol in the cream left a refreshing, cool feeling on her skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mint - an aromatic herb with a fresh, cool flavor

Etymology

From German Menthol, from Latin mentha (mint) + -ol (chemical suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛnˌθɑl / (AmE), / ˈmɛnθɒl / (BrE)
a crystalline substance with a cool, minty taste and odor, found in peppermint oil and used as a flavoring and in medicine to relieve itching, pain, and nasal congestion
minty compound used in flavoring and medicine
medical

Examples

Some cough drops contain menthol to help soothe a sore throat.
A cooling sensation is felt when menthol is applied to the skin.
The toothpaste has a refreshing menthol flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peppermint - an aromatic plant whose leaves and oil are the source of menthol

Etymology

From German 'Menthol', from Latin 'mentha' (mint) + '-ol' (chemical suffix for alcohols).
