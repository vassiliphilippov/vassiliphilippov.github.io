hepatica

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈpætɪkə / (AmE), / hɪˈpatɪkə / (BrE)
a small, early-blooming wildflower of the genus Hepatica, often found in temperate forests, with three-lobed leaves and purple, pink, or white flowers
a small forest wildflower (genus Hepatica)

Examples

Every spring, the hepatica carpets the forest floor with its delicate blossoms.
Mary spotted a patch of hepatica growing near the rocky stream.
Botanists study the distribution of hepatica to understand changes in woodland habitats.

Etymology

From medieval Latin 'hepatica', from Greek 'hepatikos' (pertaining to the liver), due to the shape of its leaves.

2. NOUN

/ hɪˈpætɪkə / (AmE), / hɛˈpætɪkə / (BrE)
a small plant of the genus Hepatica, with three-lobed leaves and white, pink, or blue flowers that bloom in early spring, typically found in moist, mossy woodlands of the temperate Northern Hemisphere
a spring-flowering plant with three-lobed leaves

Examples

Each spring, the forest floor is dotted with blooming hepatica blossoms.
She collected a specimen of hepatica during her nature walk in the mountains.
The delicate flowers of hepatica are some of the first to appear after winter.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin hepatica (wort), from Greek hēpatikos (of the liver), referring to the leaf shape resembling a liver lobe.
