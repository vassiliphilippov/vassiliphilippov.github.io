Hepatica

1. NOUN

/ hɛˈpætɪkə / (AmE), / hɪˈpætɪkə / (BrE)
a genus of small perennial herbs native to northern temperate regions, related to the genus Anemone
genus of small perennial herbs

Examples

Botanists classify the liverleaf plant under the genus Hepatica due to its distinctive leaf shape.
Many species within the Hepatica genus are popular for their early spring blooms.
The Hepatica grows in woodlands and is prized by gardeners for its delicate flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anemone - a related genus of herbaceous flowering plants

Etymology

From medieval Latin hepatica (liverwort), from Greek hēpatikos (of the liver), relating to the shape of the leaves.
