centrarchid

1. NOUN

/ sɛnˈtrɑrkɪd / (AmE), / sɛnˈtrɑːkɪd / (BrE)
A small, carnivorous freshwater fish of the family Centrarchidae, native to North America, typically with a laterally compressed body and metallic luster, such as crappies, black bass, and bluegills.
small American freshwater fish (Centrarchidae family)

Examples

The centrarchid population in the lake includes several species of bass and sunfish.
Anglers are especially interested in catching centrarchids such as bluegill and crappie during the summer.
Many centrarchid species thrive in warm, slow-moving rivers and ponds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunfish - a common centrarchid fish, especially Lepomis genus

Etymology

From modern Latin Centrarchidae, from Greek kentron (spur) + arkhē (beginning), referencing fin structure.
