alcazar

1. NOUN

/ ælˈkɑːzɑːr / (AmE), / ælˈkɑːzɑː / (BrE)
A Spanish fortress or palace, especially one built by the Moors.
Spanish fortress or palace built by the Moors

Examples

The alcazar in Seville is a renowned example of Moorish architecture.
Many tourists visit the alcazar every year to admire its stunning gardens.
The King held court in the grand alcazar on special occasions.

Etymology

From Spanish 'alcázar', from Arabic 'al-qasr' (the castle, the palace).
