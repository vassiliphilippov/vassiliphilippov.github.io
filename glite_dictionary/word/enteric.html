enteric

1. ADJ

/ ɛnˈtɛrɪk / (AmE), / ɛnˈtɛrɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or occurring in the intestines; intestinal.
of or relating to the intestines
medical

Examples

The doctor described it as an enteric infection caused by contaminated food.
Enteric diseases are common in areas with poor sanitation.
Some medications are coated to prevent them from dissolving until they reach the enteric tract.

Synset Examples

intestinal disease

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intestinal - relating to the intestines

Etymology

From Greek 'enterikos', from 'enteron' (intestine).
