beauty

1. NOUN

/ ˈbjuːti / (AmE), / ˈbjuːti / (BrE)
A combination of qualities, such as shape, color, or form, that pleases the aesthetic senses, especially the sight.
pleasing qualities to the senses

Examples

The beauty of the sunset took everyone’s breath away.
She admired the beauty of the sculpture in the museum.
Despite its simplicity, there is a certain beauty in this design.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ugliness - the quality of being unpleasant or offensive to look at

Etymology

From Old French 'beaute', from Latin 'bellus' (pretty, handsome).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbjuːti / (AmE), / ˈbjuːti / (BrE)
a person or thing that is an outstanding example of its kind, especially in a pleasing or remarkable way.
outstanding example of its kind

Examples

That goal was a real beauty, perfectly struck from thirty yards out.
He restored a vintage car and now it's an absolute beauty.
You made a cake? Wow, that's a beauty!

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gem - something prized as being outstanding or beautiful
  • Synonym: prize - something especially valued or outstanding

From Old French beaute, from Latin bellus (pretty, handsome).

3. NOUN

/ ˈbjuːti / (AmE), / ˈbjuːti / (BrE)
A woman who is very attractive or sexually appealing.
an attractive or seductive woman

Examples

Everyone at the party was captivated by the beauty in the red dress.
Old Hollywood films often featured a legendary beauty as the leading lady.
He couldn't take his eyes off the beauty sitting across the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stunner - an extremely attractive woman
  • Degree Higher: beauty queen - a woman awarded a prize in a beauty contest
  • Antonym: plain - a woman lacking striking beauty
