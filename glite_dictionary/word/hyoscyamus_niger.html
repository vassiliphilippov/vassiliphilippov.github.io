Hyoscyamus niger

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌɑːsiˈæməs ˈnaɪɡər / (AmE), / haɪˌɒsɪˈeɪməs ˈnaɪɡə / (BrE)
A poisonous Old World herb, also known as henbane, with sticky, hairy leaves and yellow-brown flowers, producing hyoscyamine and scopolamine.
poisonous herb known as henbane
medical

Examples

Hyoscyamus niger has been used in traditional medicine despite its toxic properties.
The toxic alkaloids in Hyoscyamus niger can cause hallucinations if ingested.
Researchers study Hyoscyamus niger for its potential medicinal compounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: henbane - common name for Hyoscyamus niger, a poisonous herb

Etymology

Latin: 'hyoscyamus' (henbane) and 'niger' (black), referring to the plant's dark seeds.
