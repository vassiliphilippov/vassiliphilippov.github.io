Chrysanthemum maximum

1. NOUN

/ krɪˌsænθəˌmjum ˈmæk.sɪ.məm / (AmE), / krɪˌsænθɪˌmjuːm ˈmæk.sɪ.məm / (BrE)
A perennial flowering plant native to Europe, commonly known as the Shasta daisy, with large white flowers similar to the oxeye daisy.
a perennial plant; Shasta daisy

Examples

The gardener planted several Chrysanthemum maximum along the garden's border for a bright summer display.
Many florists use Chrysanthemum maximum as a cut flower due to its long-lasting blooms.
The large, white petals of Chrysanthemum maximum are often mistaken for those of the common oxeye daisy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shasta daisy - common name for Chrysanthemum maximum
  • Degree Lower: oxeye daisy - a similar daisy with white petals and yellow center

Etymology

From Greek khrysos (gold) + anthemon (flower); 'maximum' from Latin, meaning 'greatest'.
