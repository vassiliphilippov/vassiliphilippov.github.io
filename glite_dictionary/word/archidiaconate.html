archidiaconate

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrkɪˈdaɪəkənət / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪˈdaɪəkənət / (BrE)
the office or jurisdiction of an archdeacon in the Christian church
the office of an archdeacon

Examples

He was appointed to the archidiaconate after many years of devoted service.
The archidiaconate oversees several parishes within the diocese.
The history of the archidiaconate dates back to the early church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archdeacon - senior Christian cleric below a bishop

Etymology

From Medieval Latin archidiaconatus, from archidiaconus (archdeacon).
