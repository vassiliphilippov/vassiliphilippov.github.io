steadily

1. ADV

/ ˈstɛdɪli / (AmE), / ˈstɛdɪli / (BrE)
at a regular and even rate, pace, or manner; without interruption or change
at a regular, even rate

Examples

The rain fell steadily throughout the afternoon, making the roads slippery.
She worked steadily towards her goal of becoming a doctor.
His health improved steadily after the surgery.

Synset Examples

his interest eroded steadily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constantly - continuously over a period of time
  • Synonym: gradually - in a slow and even manner

Etymology

From steady + -ly; 'steady' from Middle English stedi, from Old English stæþþig (firm, stable).

2. ADV

/ ˈstɛdɪli / (AmE), / ˈstɛdɪli / (BrE)
in a steady manner; in a way that is regular, continuous, or without change, interruption, or disturbance
in a steady manner

Examples

The number of visitors to the museum has increased steadily over the past year.
She looked steadily at him, refusing to look away.
The patient is steadily improving and should recover soon.

Synset Examples

he could still walk steadily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smoothly - in a smooth manner, without abrupt movements or interruptions
  • Antonym: unsteadily - in an unsteady or shaky manner

Etymology

Formed from 'steady' (from Middle English 'stedi,' meaning firmly placed) + '-ly.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00175977-r
Synset POS: r
