bozo

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊzoʊ / (AmE), / ˈbəʊzəʊ / (BrE)
an informal word for a man or youth who is regarded as foolish, incompetent, or clownish
a foolish or incompetent man
informal american

Examples

I can't believe that bozo forgot to lock the door again.
Some bozo parked right in front of my driveway last night.
Don't be such a bozo; read the instructions before you start.

Synset Examples

a nice guy
the guy's only doing it for some doll
the poor sod couldn't even buy a drink

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clown - a comic entertainer, especially one in a circus, who wears traditional costume and makeup
  • Synonym: fool - a person who acts unwisely or imprudently; a silly person
  • Antonym: wiseguy - a person who acts as if they know everything and is often annoying

Etymology

Originally American slang, possibly from Italian dialect 'bozzo' (blister, bump) or influenced by the name 'Bozo the Clown'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈboʊzoʊ / (AmE), / ˈbəʊzəʊ / (BrE)
an incompetent or foolish man; a stupid person
a foolish or incompetent man
informal

Examples

I can't believe that bozo parked right in front of the fire hydrant.
Some bozo spilled coffee all over the control panel.
Don't let that bozo near the machinery, or something will break.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idiot - a very stupid person
  • Synonym: fool - a person lacking good sense or judgment
  • Antonym: genius - an exceptionally intelligent person

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from circus or vaudeville slang, popularized in the mid-20th century.
