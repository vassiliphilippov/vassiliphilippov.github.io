Charles II of England

1. PROPN

/ ˈtʃɑrlz səˈkənd əv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːlz ðə ˈsɛkənd ɒv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (BrE)
(1630–1685) King of England, Scotland, and Ireland, whose reign marked the Restoration period after the English Civil War.
King of England during the Restoration

Examples

Historians study the reign of Charles II of England to understand the political changes of the Restoration era.
Charles II of England was restored to the throne in 1660 after years of republican rule.
Many architectural styles in London date back to the time of Charles II of England.
