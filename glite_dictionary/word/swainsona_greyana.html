Swainsona greyana

1. NOUN

/ sweɪnˈsoʊnə ˈɡreɪənə / (AmE), / sweɪnˈsəʊnə ˈɡreɪənə / (BrE)
a shrubby perennial plant of southern Australia with downy stems, woolly leaf undersides, and racemes of red to pink flowers
Australian shrubby perennial plant

Examples

The Swainsona greyana produces striking pink flowers that bloom in late spring across the Australian outback.
Botanists documented a thriving colony of Swainsona greyana along the banks of the Murray River.
Gardeners appreciate the drought resistance of Swainsona greyana in native plant landscaping.

Etymology

Named after English botanist Isaac Swainson and George Grey, former Governor of South Australia.
