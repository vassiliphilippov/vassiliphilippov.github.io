bacitracin

1. NOUN

/ ˌbæsɪˈtreɪsɪn / (AmE), / ˌbæsɪˈtreɪsɪn / (BrE)
an antibiotic polypeptide used topically to treat infections caused by Gram-positive bacteria
a topical antibiotic polypeptide
medical

Examples

Some ointments contain bacitracin to help prevent infection in minor cuts and scrapes.
My doctor prescribed a topical cream with bacitracin for the skin infection.
Bacitracin is effective mainly against Gram-positive bacteria and is used externally.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neomycin - an antibiotic often combined with bacitracin in topical ointments

Etymology

From Bacillus (the bacterial genus) and retrac(in), an altered form of 'Tracey' (the name of a patient from whom the producing strain was isolated) plus '-in'.
