abelia

1. NOUN

/ əˈbiːliə / (AmE), / əˈbiːlɪə / (BrE)
a genus of deciduous or evergreen ornamental shrubs, native to Asia and Mexico, with opposite simple leaves and clusters of small white, pink, or purplish flowers
a type of ornamental shrub

Examples

The abelia in the front garden blooms every summer with clusters of delicate pink flowers.
Gardeners often choose abelia for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
We planted several abelias along the walkway to create a colorful, flowering hedge.

Etymology

Named after British physician and naturalist Clarke Abel (1780–1826), who introduced the plant to the West.
