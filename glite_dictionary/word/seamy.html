seamy

1. ADJ

/ ˈsiːmi / (AmE), / ˈsiːmi / (BrE)
Morally degraded, sordid, or unpleasant, especially in a way that is hidden from view.
morally sordid or unpleasant

Examples

The novel explores the seamy underbelly of urban life in the 1960s.
Journalists uncovered the seamy dealings behind the politician's respectable image.
Tourists rarely see the seamy side of the city's nightlife.

Synset Examples

a seedy district
the seamy side of life
sleazy characters hanging around casinos
sleazy storefronts with … dirt on the walls
the sordid details of his orgies stank under his very nostrils
the squalid atmosphere of intrigue and betrayal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sordid - involving immoral or dishonorable actions and motives; dirty or squalid
  • Synonym: sleazy - dishonest or immoral; sordid

Etymology

From 'seam' + '-y', originally meaning 'having seams visible', later figuratively referring to the unattractive or sordid side.
