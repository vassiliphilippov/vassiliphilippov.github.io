Culicidae

1. NOUN

/ kuːˈlɪsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / kjuːˈlɪsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, slender, two-winged insects commonly known as mosquitoes.
mosquito family (scientific)

Examples

Many Culicidae species are vectors for human diseases such as malaria and dengue fever.
Scientists collected several Culicidae specimens for their entomology study.
The Culicidae family includes both biting and non-biting mosquito species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mosquito - common name for insects of the Culicidae family

Etymology

From Latin 'Culex' (gnat, mosquito) + '-idae' (zoological family suffix).
