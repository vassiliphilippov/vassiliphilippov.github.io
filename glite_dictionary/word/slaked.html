slaked

1. ADJ

/ sleɪkt / (AmE), / sleɪkt / (BrE)
(of a thirst, desire, or feeling) quenched or satisfied.
quenched or satisfied (esp. thirst)

Examples

After a long hike, her slaked thirst made her feel refreshed.
With his curiosity finally slaked, he no longer sought answers.
They sat back, their hunger momentarily slaked by a hearty meal.

Synset Examples

his thirst quenched, he was able to continue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quenched - extinguished or satisfied, especially thirst or desire

Etymology

Past participle of 'slake', from Old English 'slacian', meaning to become slack or diminish.
