to overleap

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈliːp / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈliːp / (BrE)
to jump across or leap over something, especially an obstacle
jump or leap across

Examples

The athlete managed to overleap the wide stream with ease.
In her excitement, the dog tried to overleap the garden fence.
He narrowly failed to overleap the barrier during the race.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jump - to spring off the ground by a muscular effort of the legs and feet
  • Synonym: vault - to leap or spring over something, especially using the hands or a pole

Etymology

From Old English oferleapan (‘to leap over’), from ofer (‘over’) + leapian (‘to leap’).

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈliːp / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈliːp / (BrE)
(formal) To defeat or harm oneself by going too far or exceeding limits.
defeat oneself by going too far

Examples

In his ambition, the politician tried to overleap, ultimately losing the support of his party.
One must be careful not to overleap and ruin a success by excessive risk-taking.
By ignoring advice and pushing ahead, she overleapt and ended up failing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overreach - to go beyond acceptable or intended limits, often to one's disadvantage

Etymology

From over- (beyond) + leap, originally meaning to jump over, later used figuratively.
