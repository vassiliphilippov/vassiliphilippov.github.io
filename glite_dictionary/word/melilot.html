melilot

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛlɪˌlɑt / (AmE), / ˈmɛlɪlɒt / (BrE)
An erect annual or biennial plant of the genus Melilotus, grown extensively as fodder and for improving soil.
fodder plant; genus Melilotus

Examples

Farmers often plant melilot to enrich the soil with nitrogen before sowing crops.
The field was full of blooming yellow melilot in early summer.
She studied the medicinal properties of melilot in her herbalism class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet clover - another name for plants of genus Melilotus

Etymology

From Latin 'melilotos', from Greek 'melilōtos', meaning 'honey-lotus'.
