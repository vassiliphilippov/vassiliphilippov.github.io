fibre

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
A leatherlike material made by compressing layers of paper or cloth, used especially in manufacturing.
compressed material; leather substitute

Examples

The case was made of fibre instead of real leather, making it more affordable.
Many suitcases in the past were constructed from compressed fibre for durability.
The technician repaired the equipment using a sheet of fibre as a replacement panel.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
an elongated, threadlike cell found in plants or animals, especially a muscle cell or nerve cell
elongated threadlike cell, esp. muscle or nerve

Examples

The heart is composed of cardiac muscle fibres that contract rhythmically.
Damage to nerve fibres can lead to loss of sensation in the affected area.
Scientists examined the plant's vascular fibres under a microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiber - standard American English spelling for 'fibre'

Etymology

From Latin fibra (fiber, filament), influenced by French fibre.
