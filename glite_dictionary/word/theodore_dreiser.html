Theodore Dreiser

1. PROPN

/ ˈθiː.əˌdɔr ˈdraɪsər / (AmE), / ˈθiː.ədɔː ˈdraɪzə / (BrE)
(1871–1945) A United States novelist known for works depicting the struggles of ordinary people, such as 'Sister Carrie' and 'An American Tragedy'.
American novelist (1871–1945)

Examples

Theodore Dreiser is considered one of the major figures in early 20th-century American literature.
Many critics have studied the influence of Theodore Dreiser on modern realism.
Her thesis focused on the social themes explored in Theodore Dreiser's novels.
