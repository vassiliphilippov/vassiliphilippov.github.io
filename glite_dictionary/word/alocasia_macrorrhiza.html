Alocasia macrorrhiza

1. NOUN

/ æləˈkeɪziə ˌmækrəˈraɪzə / (AmE), / aləˈkeɪzɪə ˌmakrəˈrʌɪzə / (BrE)
a large tropical plant with massive, erect or spreading leaves, grown for its edible rhizome and shoots and often used as an ornamental in wet, warm regions
tropical plant with large leaves, edible rhizome

Examples

The garden featured several Alocasia macrorrhiza plants with their impressive, elephant-ear-like leaves.
Farmers in Southeast Asia cultivate Alocasia macrorrhiza for its edible shoots and rhizomes.
As an ornamental, Alocasia macrorrhiza stands out in tropical landscape designs.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Alocasia (the genus name) and Greek makros (large) + rhiza (root).
