to actualise

1. VERB

/ ˈæk.tʃu.əˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈaktjʊəlaɪz / (BrE)
to represent or describe something in a realistic or true-to-life way
represent or describe realistically
british

Examples

The novelist sought to actualise the struggles of everyday people in her latest book.
His paintings powerfully actualise the harsh realities of urban life.
The documentary aims to actualise the experiences of refugees for the public.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to actualize - to make actual or real; to realize

Etymology

From actual +‎ -ise; parallel formation to 'actualize'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00989902-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈæk.tʃu.əˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈaktjʊəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something real or give it actual existence
make real or give existence to

Examples

She worked tirelessly to actualise her ambitious vision for the company.
Their dreams began to actualise after years of hard work and perseverance.
The organization seeks to actualise social change through community projects.

Synset Examples

our ideas must be substantiated into actions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to realize - to make real; to achieve or fulfill
  • Synonym: to materialize - to become actual fact; to happen or appear

Etymology

From French 'actualiser', from 'actuel' (actual), ultimately from Latin 'actualis'.
