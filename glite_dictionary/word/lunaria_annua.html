Lunaria annua

1. NOUN

/ luːˈnɛəriə ˈænjuə / (AmE), / ljuːˈneəriə ˈanjʊə / (BrE)
A southeastern European plant cultivated for its fragrant purplish flowers and round, flat, silvery-white seedpods often used in indoor decoration.
plant with silver seedpods used in decoration

Examples

She arranged the Lunaria annua seedpods in a vase as a centerpiece.
Gardeners prize Lunaria annua for its beautiful, moon-shaped seed cases.
In early summer, Lunaria annua displays clusters of vibrant purple blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honesty - common name for Lunaria annua, especially referring to its decorative seedpods

Etymology

From Latin: 'lunaria' from 'luna' (moon), referring to the shape of its seedpods; 'annua' means 'annual'.
