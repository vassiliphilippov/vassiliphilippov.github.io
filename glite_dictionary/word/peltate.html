peltate

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛl.teɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɛl.teɪt / (BrE)
(of a leaf) having the stalk attached to the lower surface near the center rather than at the edge; typically round in shape.
leaf shape with stalk attached near center

Examples

The nasturtium has a peltate leaf, with the stem attached near the center.
Many aquatic plants display peltate foliage to maximize sunlight exposure.
The botanist noted the peltate leaves as a key characteristic of the species.

Etymology

From Latin 'pelta' (a small shield), referring to the shield-like shape of the leaf.
