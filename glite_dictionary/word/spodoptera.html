Spodoptera

1. NOUN

/ spoʊˈdɑptərə / (AmE), / spəʊˈdɒptərə / (BrE)
A genus of moths whose larvae are commonly known as armyworms, including several species considered pests in agriculture.
genus of moths (armyworms)

Examples

The caterpillars of Spodoptera species cause significant damage to crops worldwide.
Farmers are particularly wary of the fall armyworm, a member of the Spodoptera genus.
Researchers are studying Spodoptera to develop better pest control methods.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Spodoptera, formed from Greek 'spodos' (ashes) and 'pteron' (wing).

2. NOUN

/ spoʊˈdɑptərə / (AmE), / spɒˈdɒptərə / (BrE)
a genus of moths whose larvae are known as armyworms, including around 30 species, some of which are agricultural pests
genus of moths, includes armyworms

Examples

Several species in the genus Spodoptera are well-known pests of cereal crops.
The fall armyworm, a member of Spodoptera, causes significant damage to maize fields.
Researchers are studying the life cycle of Spodoptera to find better pest control methods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armyworm - larval stage of certain Spodoptera species, notable agricultural pests

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, formed from Greek 'spodos' (ash) + 'pteron' (wing).
