cimetidine

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈmɛtɪdiːn / (AmE), / sɪˈmɛtɪdiːn / (BrE)
A drug used to treat peptic ulcers and other conditions by decreasing the secretion of stomach acid.
drug reducing stomach acid
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed cimetidine to help manage her peptic ulcer symptoms.
Long-term use of cimetidine should be monitored by a healthcare professional.
Some patients may experience mild side effects when taking cimetidine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ranitidine - a similar drug used to reduce stomach acid

Etymology

From chemical nomenclature based on its molecular structure; coined in the 1970s.
