to deform

1. VERB

/ dɪˈfɔrm / (AmE), / dɪˈfɔːm / (BrE)
to become bent, twisted, or misshapen, often as a result of pressure, heat, or damage
become misshapen or distorted

Examples

After years of pressure, the old metal fence began to deform around the edges.
If plastic is heated too much, it can deform and lose its original shape.
The earthquake caused the railway tracks to deform, making travel unsafe.

Synset Examples

The sidewalk deformed during the earthquake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to distort - to twist out of shape

Etymology

From Latin deformare (to disfigure), from de- (away) + forma (shape, form).
