ruthfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːθfʊlnəs / (AmE), / ˈruːθfʊlnəs / (BrE)
A feeling or state of pity, compassion, or sorrow for the suffering of others.
state of pity or compassion

Examples

Her ruthfulness was evident as she comforted the grieving family.
He spoke with ruthfulness, expressing genuine sorrow for the distress he had caused.
A sense of ruthfulness filled the room during the solemn memorial service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compassion - sympathetic pity and concern for others' sufferings
  • Antonym: ruthless - having no pity or compassion

Etymology

From 'ruth' (pity, compassion) + '-fulness' (state or quality).
