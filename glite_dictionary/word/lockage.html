lockage

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːkɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈlɒkɪdʒ / (BrE)
a fee charged for passing through a lock in a canal or waterway
fee for passing through a canal lock

Examples

The lockage for commercial vessels has increased over the past year.
Barges moving through the waterway are required to pay a lockage.
The company factored the cost of lockage into their shipping rates.

Etymology

From "lock" (a device for raising and lowering boats between stretches of water) + "-age" (denoting a fee or result).

/ ˈlɑkɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈlɒkɪdʒ / (BrE)
a series or system of locks in a canal or other waterway, used to raise or lower boats between stretches of water of different levels
system of canal locks

Examples

The construction of the lockage allowed ships to bypass the natural waterfalls on the river.
Engineers inspected the lockage for signs of wear after the heavy storms.
Improvements to the lockage increased canal traffic and boosted the local economy.

Related Terms
