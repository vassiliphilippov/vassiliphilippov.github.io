family Simuliidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli sɪmjuːˈlaɪɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli sɪmjʊˈlɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, bloodsucking flies including blackflies and sand flies, some of which are known for biting humans and animals.
family of blackflies and sand flies

Examples

The family Simuliidae includes species of blackflies that can transmit diseases to humans and animals.
Researchers studied the lifecycle of insects in the family Simuliidae found near the river.
Members of the family Simuliidae are known for their persistent biting behavior in summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackfly - a small, dark, biting fly in the family Simuliidae

Etymology

From New Latin, named after the type genus Simulium.
