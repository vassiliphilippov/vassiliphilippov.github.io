enterics

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈtɛrɪks / (AmE), / ɛnˈtɛrɪks / (BrE)
A group of rod-shaped Gram-negative bacteria, many of which normally inhabit the intestines of humans and animals, but can also be pathogenic.
intestinal Gram-negative bacteria group
medical

Examples

Certain enterics can cause serious digestive illnesses in both humans and animals.
Laboratory tests revealed the presence of enterics in the contaminated water.
Many antibiotics are designed to target harmful enterics without disrupting other gut microbes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enterobacteria - rod-shaped, Gram-negative bacteria in the family Enterobacteriaceae

Etymology

From 'enteric', from Greek enterikos (of the intestine), from enteron (intestine).
