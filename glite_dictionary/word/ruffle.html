ruffle

1. VERB

/ ˈrʌf.əl / (AmE), / ˈrʌf.əl / (BrE)
to disturb the surface of water so as to create small waves or ripples
make ripples on water

Examples

The sudden breeze began to ruffle the calm surface of the pond.
She tossed a pebble into the lake, causing the water to ruffle outward in circles.
A boat passed by and ruffled the usually still water of the canal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disturb - to interfere with the normal state of something
