attractive

1. ADJ

/ əˈtræktɪv / (AmE), / əˈtræktɪv / (BrE)
pleasing or appealing to the senses or mind, especially because of beauty, charm, or qualities that arouse interest.
pleasing or appealing; charming

Examples

She wore an attractive red dress to the party.
The apartment is small but very attractive to young professionals.
He found her confidence even more attractive than her looks.

Synset Examples

a remarkably attractive young man
an attractive personality
attractive clothes
a book with attractive illustrations

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unattractive - not pleasing or appealing in appearance or character
  • Synonym: handsome - good-looking, especially used for men
  • Synonym: beautiful - pleasing the senses or mind aesthetically

Etymology

From Latin 'attrahere' (to draw toward).

2. ADJ

/ əˈtræktɪv / (AmE), / əˈtræktɪv / (BrE)
pleasing or appealing in a way that arouses interest or makes something desirable
arousing interest or appeal

Examples

The company offered an attractive benefits package to draw in new employees.
This investment plan looks particularly attractive to young professionals starting their careers.
Many people found the idea of working abroad attractive due to the opportunity for personal growth.

Synset Examples

an attractive opportunity
the job is attractive because of the pay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appealing - arousing interest or admiration

Etymology

From Latin attrahere (to draw towards).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00172108-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ əˈtræktɪv / (AmE), / əˈtræktɪv / (BrE)
(of a force or object) having the quality of drawing or pulling objects towards itself, as in magnetism or gravitation.
having the property to draw or pull

Examples

In physics, gravity is an attractive force that pulls objects toward one another.
Unlike charges experience an attractive force, causing them to draw closer together.
The attractive power of magnets can be demonstrated with iron filings.

Synset Examples

an attractive force

Related Terms

  • Antonym: repulsive - having the property of pushing things away, opposite of attractive in physics

Etymology

From Latin attractivus (having the power of drawing or pulling).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00171217-a
Synset POS: a
