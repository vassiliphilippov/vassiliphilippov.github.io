libation

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈbeɪʃən / (AmE), / laɪˈbeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act of pouring a liquid, especially wine, as an offering to a deity in a religious ceremony
act of pouring a drink as an offering

Examples

During the ancient ritual, the priest made a libation of wine to honor the gods.
The ceremony began with a libation poured onto the ground as an offering to the ancestors.
In Greek mythology, heroes often performed a libation before embarking on their journeys.

Etymology

From Latin 'libatio' (a pouring out), from 'libare' (to pour as an offering).

2. NOUN

/ laɪˈbeɪʃən / (AmE), / laɪˈbeɪʃən / (BrE)
an act of pouring out a drink, especially wine, as an offering to a deity
a ritual drink offering to a deity

Examples

The priests performed a libation at the altar before the ceremony began.
As part of the ritual, a small libation of wine was poured onto the earth.
Ancient Greeks often offered a libation to the gods during their meals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offering - something presented in religious worship as a gift to a deity

Etymology

From Latin 'libatio' (a pouring out), from 'libare' (to pour as an offering).

3. NOUN

/ laɪˈbeɪʃən / (AmE), / laɪˈbeɪʃən / (BrE)
(facetious) An alcoholic drink, especially when offered or consumed socially.
an alcoholic drink (humorous/facetious)

Examples

After a long week, she enjoyed a well-deserved libation with friends at the bar.
He raised his libation in a toast to the newlyweds.
Let's meet for a libation after work and catch up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drink - an alcoholic beverage
  • Synonym: beverage - a drink, especially one other than water

Etymology

From Latin libatio (the act of pouring out a drink offering), from libare (to pour as an offering).
