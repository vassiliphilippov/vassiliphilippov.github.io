emetic

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɛtɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈmɛtɪk / (BrE)
A medicine or substance that causes nausea and induces vomiting.
medicine that induces vomiting
medical

Examples

The doctor administered an emetic to treat the patient's poisoning.
Some plants contain natural emetic properties that can induce vomiting if eaten.
In emergency medicine, an emetic may be used to help remove toxins from the stomach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antidote - a substance that counteracts a poison

Etymology

From Greek emein (to vomit) via Latin emeticus.
