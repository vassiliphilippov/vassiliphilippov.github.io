pre-existing

1. ADJ

/ pri ɪɡˈzɪstɪŋ / (AmE), / pri ɪɡˈzɪstɪŋ / (BrE)
existing previously or before a particular time or event
previously existing

Examples

The contract does not cover pre-existing medical conditions.
They had to work around pre-existing infrastructure when designing the new building.
These rules only apply to pre-existing agreements made before the new law.

Synset Examples

variations on pre-existent musical themes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: existing - present or currently in existence

Etymology

From Latin 'prae-' (before) and 'exist', meaning to stand forth or exist.
