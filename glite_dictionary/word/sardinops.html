Sardinops

1. NOUN

/ sɑrˈdaɪnɒps / (AmE), / sɑːˈdɪnɒps / (BrE)
A genus of small, oily fish (pilchards) found in various oceans, including the South American pilchard.
genus of small pilchard fish

Examples

The fishery industry heavily depends on the abundance of Sardinops in coastal waters.
Researchers have been studying Sardinops populations to monitor ocean health.
Processed Sardinops are often canned and exported worldwide as a food source.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pilchard - a small, oily fish similar to a sardine

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'sardine' and Greek 'ops' (appearance, face).

2. NOUN

/ sɑrˈdaɪnɑps / (AmE), / sɑːˈdɪnɒps / (BrE)
A genus of small, oily fish in the family Clupeidae, known as pilchards or sardines, including the South American pilchard.
genus of pilchards or sardines

Examples

The fisheries mainly catch Sardinops species off the coast of Peru.
Scientists studied the population dynamics of Sardinops to assess environmental changes.
The South American pilchard, belonging to the genus Sardinops, is an important commercial fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sardine - a small, oily fish used as food

Etymology

From New Latin, from 'sardina' (sardine) + Greek 'ōps' (appearance, face).
