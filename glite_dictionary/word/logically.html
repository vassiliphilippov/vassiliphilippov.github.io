logically

1. ADV

/ ˈlɑːdʒɪkli / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that is based on clear, sound reasoning or logic.
in a logical manner

Examples

She logically explained her decision to change jobs.
If you think logically, the answer becomes clear.
The plan was carefully and logically constructed to solve the problem.

Synset Examples

he acted logically under the circumstances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rationally - in a rational manner; with reason
  • Antonym: illogically - in an illogical manner; lacking logic or clear reasoning

Etymology

From 'logical' + '-ly', with 'logical' from Medieval Latin 'logicālis', based on 'logica' (logic).

2. ADV

/ ˈlɑːdʒɪkli / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that is based on clear, sound reasoning; according to the rules of logic
according to logical reasoning

Examples

Logically, we should leave early to avoid the rush hour traffic.
If you think logically about the problem, the solution becomes clearer.
Logically, the evidence points to only one possible conclusion.

Synset Examples

logically, you should now do the same to him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rationally - in a way that is based on reason or logic
  • Antonym: illogically - in a way that is not logical; without sound reasoning

Etymology

From logic + -ally, forming the adverb from the noun 'logic'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00365540-r
Synset POS: r
