Trachinotus carolinus

1. NOUN

/ trəˌkɪnoʊtəs ˌkæroʊˈlaɪnəs / (AmE), / trəˌkɪnətəs ˌkarəʊˈlaɪnəs / (BrE)
A species of edible marine fish, commonly known as the Florida pompano, found in coastal waters from New England to Brazil, except in the clear waters of the West Indies.
Florida pompano, a marine fish species

Examples

The Trachinotus carolinus is highly prized by anglers for its fighting ability and taste.
Scientists conducted a study on the habitat preferences of Trachinotus carolinus.
Local markets along the Gulf Coast often sell fresh Trachinotus carolinus to seafood lovers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Florida pompano - Common name for Trachinotus carolinus, a marine fish species

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Trachinotus' and species name 'carolinus', indicating connection to Carolina region.
