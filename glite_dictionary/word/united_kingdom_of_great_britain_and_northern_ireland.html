United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland

1. PROPN

/ juˈnaɪtɪd ˈkɪŋdəm əv ˈɡreɪt ˈbrɪtən ənd ˈnɜrðərn ɪrˈlænd / (AmE), / juˈnaɪtɪd ˈkɪŋdəm əv ˈɡreɪt ˈbrɪtən ənd ˈnɜːðən ɪəˈlænd / (BrE)
the official name of the country in northwestern Europe comprising England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland, also referred to as the United Kingdom or UK
country: England, Scotland, Wales, Northern Ireland

Examples

The United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland is a constitutional monarchy with a long parliamentary tradition.
She moved to the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland to pursue her studies at a British university.
The United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland voted to leave the European Union in 2016.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: UK - abbreviation for United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland
  • Synonym: Great Britain - island comprising England, Scotland, and Wales (part of the United Kingdom)

Etymology

‘United Kingdom’ refers to the union of Great Britain and Northern Ireland; ‘Great Britain’ from Latin ‘Britannia’.
