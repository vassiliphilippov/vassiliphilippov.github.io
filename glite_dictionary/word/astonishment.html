astonishment

1. NOUN

/ əˈstɑnɪʃmənt / (AmE), / əˈstɒnɪʃmənt / (BrE)
a strong feeling of great surprise or amazement
great surprise or amazement

Examples

She stared at him in astonishment when he revealed the news.
The magician's trick left the entire audience in astonishment.
To her astonishment, the long-lost painting was found in the attic.

Synset Examples

he looked at me in astonishment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amazement - a feeling of great surprise or wonder
  • Synonym: wonder - a feeling of surprise mixed with admiration, caused by something beautiful, unexpected, or unfamiliar
  • Antonym: indifference - lack of interest, concern, or sympathy

Etymology

From Old French *estonnement*, from *estoner* (to stun), from Latin *extonare* (to thunder out).
