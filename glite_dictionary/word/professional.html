professional

1. ADJ

/ prəˈfəʃənl̩ / (AmE), / prəˈfɛʃənəl / (BrE)
Relating to a profession or suitable for people who do a specific type of skilled work.
relating to a profession

Examples

The company sets strict professional standards for its lawyers and accountants.
She joined several professional organizations after obtaining her medical license.
A professional code of conduct ensures high ethical standards in the workplace.

Synset Examples

professional organizations
a professional field such as law

Related Terms

  • Synonym: occupational - relating to a particular occupation or work

Etymology

From Latin 'professio' (public declaration), from 'profiteri' (declare publicly).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02850580-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ prəˈfɛʃənl / (AmE), / prəˈfɛʃənəl / (BrE)
a person who is engaged in a learned profession, especially one requiring special training or qualifications
person with a learned profession

Examples

She is a respected professional in the field of law.
The team hired a professional to handle the complex project.
As a medical professional, he takes his responsibilities seriously.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: amateur - a person who does something for pleasure, not as a job

Etymology

From Latin 'professio' (public declaration), via Old French 'profession'.

8. NOUN

/ prəˈfɛʃənəl / (AmE), / prəˈfɛʃənəl / (BrE)
