Heliamphora

1. NOUN

/ hiˌliː.æmˈfɔːr.ə / (AmE), / hiˌliː.amˈfɒː.rə / (BrE)
a genus of pitcher plants native to the Guiana Highlands of South America, known for their cup-shaped leaves that trap insects
genus of South American pitcher plants

Examples

The Heliamphora thrives in the humid environment of the Guiana Highlands.
Botanists study the unique trapping mechanisms of Heliamphora species.
She grows several Heliamphora plants in her greenhouse collection.

Etymology

From Greek 'hēlios' (sun) and 'amphoreus' (pitcher, jar).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhiːliˈæmfərə / (AmE), / ˌhiːliˈamfərə / (BrE)
A genus of pitcher plants native to the Guiana Highlands in South America, known for their tubular leaves that trap insects.
genus of South American pitcher plants

Examples

The Heliamphora genus is found primarily in the misty tepuis of Venezuela.
Botanists are fascinated by the water-filled pitchers of Heliamphora species.
Many plant collectors seek rare Heliamphora varieties for their unique appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitcher plant - any carnivorous plant with pitcher-shaped leaves that trap insects

Etymology

From Greek helios (sun) + amphoreus (vessel, jar), referring to the plant's sunlit, vessel-shaped pitchers.
