dawn

1. NOUN

/ dɔn / (AmE), / dɔːn / (BrE)
The beginning of a period of time, especially when something important starts to develop or be noticed.
the beginning of a period

Examples

Many historians consider the dawn of the Internet era to be the early 1990s.
At the dawn of civilization, humans began to form communities and settlements.
The dawn of a new age in medicine brought hope to many patients.

Synset Examples

it was the dawn of the Roman Empire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: start - the point at which something begins
  • Antonym: end - the final part or conclusion of something

Etymology

From Old English 'dagian' (to become day).

2. VERB

/ dɔn / (AmE), / dɔːn / (BrE)
(of an idea, realization, etc.) to begin to be understood or become clear in someone's mind.

Examples
