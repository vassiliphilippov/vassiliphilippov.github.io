San Carlos Apache language

1. NOUN

/ sæn ˈkɑrloʊs əˈpætʃi ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / san ˈkɑːlɒs əˈpætʃi ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Apache language spoken in east-central Arizona, particularly on the San Carlos and neighboring reservations
Apache language of San Carlos, Arizona

Examples

Many elders continue to speak the San Carlos Apache language as their first language.
Efforts to revitalize the San Carlos Apache language include teaching it in local schools.
Linguists have studied the San Carlos Apache language to better understand its structure and history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Western Apache - the Western Apache language group, which includes the San Carlos dialect

Etymology

San Carlos refers to the location of the San Carlos Reservation in Arizona; Apache from the tribal name; language meaning the system of communication.
