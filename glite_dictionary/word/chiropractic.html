chiropractic

1. NOUN

/ ˌkaɪ.ɹəˈpræk.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌkaɪ.rəˈpræk.tɪk / (BrE)
a system of treatment that involves manipulating the spine and other body structures to relieve pain or improve health
manual body manipulation therapy
medical

Examples

After weeks of back pain, she decided to try chiropractic to find some relief.
Many people seek chiropractic care for headaches and joint issues.
Debate continues about the effectiveness of chiropractic as a primary treatment for chronic pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: osteopathy - a system of therapy involving manipulation of the musculoskeletal system

Etymology

From Greek kheir (hand) + praktikos (practical), coined in the late 19th century.
