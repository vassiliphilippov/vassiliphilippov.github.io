minelayer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪnˌleɪər / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnˌleɪə / (BrE)
A ship or vehicle designed and equipped for laying marine mines in the sea as a military operation.
ship/vehicle that lays marine mines

Examples

The navy deployed a minelayer to block access to the harbor.
During the operation, the minelayer released hundreds of mines into the shipping lane.
Enemy forces detected a minelayer approaching their coastline under cover of darkness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: minesweeper - a ship equipped to detect and remove mines

Etymology

From 'mine' (explosive device) + 'layer' (thing that lays).
