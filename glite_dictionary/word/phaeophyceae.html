Phaeophyceae

1. NOUN

/ fiːəˈfaɪsiːiː / (AmE), / fiːəʊˈfʌɪsiːiː / (BrE)
A large class of mostly marine brown algae found chiefly in coastal waters, including kelp and other seaweeds.
class of brown algae

Examples

Most large seaweeds, including kelp, belong to the Phaeophyceae.
Researchers studied the ecological role of Phaeophyceae along the rocky coastline.
The Phaeophyceae are important for marine ecosystems due to their role as primary producers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brown algae - a group of predominantly marine algae that includes kelp and wracks

Etymology

From Greek phaios (dusky, dark) + phyce (seaweed).

2. NOUN

/ fiˌɒfəˈsiːiː / (AmE), / fiːˌəʊfɪˈsiːiː / (BrE)
a class of brown algae, mostly marine and found in littoral zones, characterized by brown pigments and complex multicellular structure
a class of brown seaweeds or algae
medical

Examples

Members of the Phaeophyceae are commonly known as brown algae.
Scientists study the Phaeophyceae to understand marine ecosystems.
The kelp forests are dominated by species from the Phaeophyceae.

Etymology

From New Latin *Phaeophyceae*, from Greek *phaios* (dusky, dark) + *phycos* (seaweed).
