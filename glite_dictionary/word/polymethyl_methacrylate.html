polymethyl methacrylate

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈmiːθəl məˈθækrəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈmiːθ(ə)l mɛˈθækrɪleɪt / (BrE)
a transparent synthetic plastic material, often used as a lightweight or shatter-resistant alternative to glass
transparent plastic substitute for glass

Examples

Shatterproof windows are often made from polymethyl methacrylate instead of traditional glass.
Researchers chose polymethyl methacrylate due to its clarity and light weight for the experiment.
Many aquariums use polymethyl methacrylate panels to provide a clear view with high impact resistance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acrylic - a common term for polymethyl methacrylate plastic

Etymology

From poly- (many) + methyl (chemical group) + methacrylate (salt or ester of methacrylic acid), reflecting the polymer structure.
