expectant

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈspɛktənt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛktənt / (BrE)
feeling or showing anticipation about something that is about to happen; marked by eager waiting or anticipation.
full of eager anticipation

Examples

There was an expectant crowd outside the stadium, waiting for the band to arrive.
Her expectant smile showed she was hoping for good news.
An expectant silence fell over the room as the results were announced.

Synset Examples

an expectant hush

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anticipatory - showing or expressing anticipation

Etymology

From Latin 'expectant-', present participle of 'expectare' (to await, expect).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01231670-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ɪkˈspɛktənt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛktənt / (BrE)
(of a woman) in an advanced stage of pregnancy; soon to give birth.
pregnant, soon to give birth

Examples

The hospital has a special ward for expectant mothers.
She joined a support group for expectant women in her third trimester.
During her expectant months, she read many books about parenting.

Synset Examples

was big with child
was great with child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pregnant - having a baby developing in the body

Etymology

From Latin 'expectare' (to await).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00174652-s
Synset POS: s
