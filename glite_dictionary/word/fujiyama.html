Fujiyama

1. PROPN

/ fuːdʒiˈjɑːmə / (AmE), / fuːdʒiˈjɑːmə / (BrE)
an extinct volcano in south central Honshu, Japan, known as the country's highest peak and noted for its symmetrical, snow-capped summit; regarded as sacred and a frequent site for pilgrimages
highest mountain in Japan; extinct volcano

Examples

Many tourists travel to Japan hoping to see the majestic Fujiyama in person.
The snow-covered peak of Fujiyama has inspired countless artists and poets.
Pilgrims have climbed Fujiyama for centuries, considering it a sacred journey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mount Fuji - the most common English name for Japan's highest mountain

Etymology

From Japanese 富士山 (Fujisan), 'Mount Fuji.' 'Fujiyama' is a reading influenced by the Japanese word for 'mountain' (yama).
