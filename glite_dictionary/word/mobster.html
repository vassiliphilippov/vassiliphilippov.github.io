mobster

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑb.stɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɒb.stə / (BrE)
A member of a criminal gang, especially one involved in organized crime.
member of a criminal gang

Examples

The police arrested a notorious mobster during the raid on the warehouse.
He acted like a mobster in the film, intimidating everyone around him.
Several famous mobsters have been depicted in popular television shows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gangster - a member of a criminal gang, especially a mob
  • Antonym: law-abiding citizen - a person who follows the law and is not a criminal

Etymology

Formed from 'mob' (gang or crowd) + '-ster' (person associated with); popularized in American English in the early 20th century.
