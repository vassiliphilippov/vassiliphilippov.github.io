comment

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑːˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmɛnt / (BrE)
to write or make explanatory notes about words or phrases in a text
add explanations about words or text

Examples

She was asked to comment on the old manuscript by adding definitions for archaic words.
The teacher will comment directly on students’ essays with helpful explanations.
Editors often comment in the margins to clarify difficult passages for readers.

Synset Examples

He annotated on what his teacher had written

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to annotate - to add explanatory notes or comments to a text

Etymology

From Latin commentari (to consider, meditate), from commentum (invention, interpretation).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00963361-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmɛnt / (BrE)
a written explanation, criticism, or note added to a book or other text to provide clarification or opinion
written note or explanation in a text

Examples

He wrote a detailed comment on the manuscript, highlighting its strengths and weaknesses.
The margins of the book were filled with the professor's comments.
Please leave your comment below if you have questions or suggestions.

Synset Examples

he wrote an extended comment on the proposal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annotation - a note by way of explanation or comment added to a text or diagram
  • Synonym: remark - a spoken or written statement expressing an observation or opinion

Etymology

From Latin 'commentum' (invention, interpretation), from 'commentari' (to reflect upon).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06775422-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈkɑːˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmɛnt / (BrE)
to express an opinion or make a remark about something; to make or write a comment on
express an opinion or remark

Examples

He was asked to comment on the recent economic crisis.
Please comment if you have any suggestions or feedback.
She commented that the report was thorough and well-researched.

Synset Examples

he commented the paper of his colleague

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remark - to say or write something as a comment
  • Synonym: critique - to evaluate in a detailed and analytical way

Etymology

From Latin 'commentari' (to explain, interpret), from 'commentum' (interpretation).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01060446-v
Synset POS: v

/ ˈkɑːmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmɛnt / (BrE)

express an opinion or interpretation

Examples

She often comments on current affairs in her blog.
When asked about the decision, he simply commented that it was confidential.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to criticize - to express disapproval or find fault with

Etymology

From Latin commentari (to discuss, explain), from Latin commentum (invention, interpretation).

5. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmɛnt / (BrE)
a spoken or written statement giving an opinion, explanation, or additional information about something
an opinion or explanatory statement

Examples

After reading the article, she left a thoughtful comment underneath.
The teacher made a positive comment on his improved handwriting.
Your comment about the proposal was very helpful.

Synset Examples

from time to time she contributed a personal comment on his account
we would appreciate input from our users on how we can improve our software

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remark - a spoken or written statement expressing an opinion or reaction
  • Synonym: observation - a comment or statement based on what one has noticed or experienced

Etymology

From Latin 'commentum' (invention, interpretation), later 'commentum' and 'commentārius' (notebook, commentary) in Late Latin.
