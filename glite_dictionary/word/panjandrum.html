panjandrum

1. NOUN

/ pænˈdʒændrəm / (AmE), / pænˈdʒændrəm / (BrE)
a powerful or self-important person, especially one who is pompous or overbearing
a self-important or influential person

Examples

The committee was chaired by a panjandrum who loved to hear himself speak.
At the ceremony, various panjandrums delivered long and tedious speeches.
He acts like a panjandrum, but no one outside his department listens to him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bigwig - an important or influential person
  • Antonym: nobody - a person of no importance or influence

Etymology

Coined in 1755 by Samuel Foote, as a nonsensical title in a mock-latin phrase; adopted into English for pompous figures.
