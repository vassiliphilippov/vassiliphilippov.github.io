foxglove

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑksˌɡlʌv / (AmE), / ˈfɒksˌɡlʌv / (BrE)
a tall plant of the genus Digitalis, typically with clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers, often purplish, used ornamentally and as a source of medicine
a tall flowering plant of the genus Digitalis

Examples

The vibrant purple foxglove brightened the edge of the garden path.
Some species of foxglove are used to make important heart medications.
Be careful not to ingest foxglove as it contains toxic compounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digitalis - a drug prepared from foxglove

Etymology

From 'fox' + 'glove', probably referring to the flower's shape resembling the fingers of a glove suitable for a fox's paw.
