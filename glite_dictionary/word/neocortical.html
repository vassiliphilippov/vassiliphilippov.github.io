neocortical

1. ADJ

/ ˌniːoʊˈkɔrtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊˈkɔːtɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the neocortex, the part of the cerebral cortex involved in higher brain functions
relating to the neocortex of the brain
medical

Examples

The neocortical region is responsible for complex cognitive processes such as reasoning and language.
Damage to neocortical areas can lead to severe impairments in memory and perception.
Researchers are studying neocortical plasticity to understand how the brain adapts after injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cerebral - relating to the brain or cerebrum

Etymology

From neo- (new) + cortex, referring to the newer part of the cerebral cortex.
