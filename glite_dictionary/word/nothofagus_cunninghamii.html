Nothofagus cunninghamii

1. NOUN

/ ˌnoʊθəˈfeɪɡəs ˌkʌnɪŋˈhæmiː / (AmE), / ˌnɒθəˈfeɪɡəs ˌkʌnɪŋˈhæmiː / (BrE)
A large evergreen tree native to Tasmania and parts of southeastern Australia, commonly known as the myrtle beech.
Tasmanian evergreen tree (myrtle beech)

Examples

The dense forests of Tasmania are dominated by Nothofagus cunninghamii, also known as myrtle beech.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Nothofagus cunninghamii to understand Tasmania’s ecosystem.
The leaves of Nothofagus cunninghamii are small, glossy, and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name *Nothofagus* (‘southern beech’), species named after botanist Allan Cunningham.
