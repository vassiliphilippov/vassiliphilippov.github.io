Lygus lineolaris

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪɡəs ˌlɪniːoʊˈlɛərɪs / (AmE), / ˈlɪɡʊs ˌlɪniːəʊˈlɑːrɪs / (BrE)
A widespread plant bug of North America, known as the tarnished plant bug, which is a significant pest of crops and fruit.
North American plant pest (tarnished plant bug)

Examples

Lygus lineolaris is a serious threat to cotton and strawberry crops across the United States.
Farmers use integrated pest management techniques to control Lygus lineolaris infestations.
The economic impact of Lygus lineolaris has led to continued research on effective pesticides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tarnished plant bug - common name for Lygus lineolaris

Etymology

Scientific binomial; genus name from Greek lygos (a kind of tree), species epithet from Latin lineola (little line), referring to the insect's markings.
