yucca

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʌkə / (AmE), / ˈjʌkə / (BrE)
A tropical American plant of the genus Yucca, with stiff, narrow, sword-shaped leaves and spikes of white flowers.
a plant with stiff leaves and white flowers

Examples

Several yucca plants lined the path, their white blossoms bright against the dark soil.
The gardener selected a yucca for its drought resistance and striking foliage.
Indigenous peoples traditionally used fibers from yucca leaves for making cord and baskets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agave - a related genus of plants with rosettes of spiny leaves

Etymology

From Taíno (Arawakan language of the Caribbean) *yuca*, referring to cassava/manioc, but adapted in botanical Latin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈjʌkə / (AmE), / ˈjʌkə / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Yucca, typically an evergreen with tall, sturdy stems and a cluster of white flowers, native to warmer regions of North America
evergreen plant of the genus Yucca

Examples

The yucca in the garden blooms with striking white flowers every summer.
Many desert landscapes feature yucca as a drought-resistant ornamental plant.
Some species of yucca are used in traditional Native American crafts and food.

Etymology

From Taíno *yuca*, referring to the cassava plant (though erroneously applied in Linnaean taxonomy).
