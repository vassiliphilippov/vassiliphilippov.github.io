Douglas MacArthur

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʌɡləs məkˈɑrθɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʌɡləs məkˈɑːθə / (BrE)
(1880–1964) United States general who served as Chief of Staff, commanded Allied forces in the South Pacific during World War II, and accepted the surrender of Japan.
American general, WWII Allied commander

Examples

General Douglas MacArthur played a crucial role in the Allied victory in the Pacific during World War II.
The leadership style of Douglas MacArthur was both admired and criticized by his contemporaries.
In 1951, President Truman relieved Douglas MacArthur of his command in Korea due to disagreements over military strategy.
