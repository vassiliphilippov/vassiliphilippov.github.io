Clusia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkluːziə / (AmE), / ˈkluːziə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American aromatic trees or shrubs, often epiphytic and including some species that are stranglers.
genus of tropical American aromatic trees or shrubs

Examples

The Clusia grows well as an epiphytic plant in tropical forests.
Some species in the Clusia genus are known to be stranglers, enveloping other trees as they grow.
Researchers are studying the aromatic properties of Clusia species.

Etymology

Named after Carolus Clusius (1526–1609), Flemish botanist.
