reflectivity

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌflɛkˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / rɪˌflɛkˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being capable of deep thought or contemplation; thoughtfulness or introspection
capacity for deep thought or contemplation

Examples

Her reflectivity set her apart from her peers, who tended to act on impulse.
Writers often value reflectivity when developing complex characters.
Periods of reflectivity can help people gain a clearer sense of themselves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thoughtfulness - the state of being considerate or attentive, often involving deep or careful thinking
  • Synonym: introspection - the examination or observation of one's own mental and emotional processes

Etymology

From reflect + -ivity, patterned after words like 'activity'.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˌflɛkˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / rɪˌflɛkˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
the ability of a surface to reflect a proportion of incident radiant energy, usually expressed as a fraction or percentage
measure of how much energy a surface reflects

Examples

The reflectivity of snow can make it difficult to see without sunglasses.
Scientists measured the reflectivity of the new coating to assess its efficiency.
A mirror has a high reflectivity, returning almost all the light that strikes its surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: albedo - proportion of the incident light or radiation that is reflected by a surface

Etymology

From 'reflect' + '-ivity', patterned after technical terms like 'conductivity'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌrɛflɛkˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌrɪflɛkˈtɪvəti / (BrE)
the quality of a surface or material that reflects beams or rays, especially light, as a measure of its ability to reflect
ability to reflect beams or rays

Examples

The shiny metal surface has high reflectivity, making it suitable for mirrors.
Scientists measured the reflectivity of different materials to find the best heat shield.
Snow has a greater reflectivity than grass, which helps keep temperatures lower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflection - the process of reflecting light, sound, or heat
  • Antonym: absorption - the process of taking in energy instead of reflecting it

Etymology

From 'reflect' + '-ivity', patterned on words like 'conductivity'.
