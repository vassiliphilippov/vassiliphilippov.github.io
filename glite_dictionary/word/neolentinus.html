Neolentinus

1. NOUN

/ niːoʊˈlɛntɪnəs / (AmE), / niːəʊˈlɛntɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Polyporaceae, known for wood-decaying agarics with tough fruit bodies, serrated gill edges, and nonamyloid white binucleate basidiospores.
genus of wood-decaying fungi
medical

Examples

The genus Neolentinus includes fungi commonly found decaying wood in forested areas.
Mycologists recently identified a rare specimen of Neolentinus growing on an old oak log.
Research on Neolentinus is helping us understand fungal decomposition of timber.

Etymology

From Greek 'neo-' (new) and 'Lentinus' (a related fungal genus).

2. NOUN

/ ˌnioʊˈlɛntɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊˈlɛntɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Polyporaceae, characterized by wood-decaying agarics with tough fruit bodies, serrated gill edges, and white binucleate basidiospores.
a genus of wood-decaying fungi

Examples

Species in the genus Neolentinus are known for their role in wood decomposition.
The tough fruit bodies of Neolentinus are adapted to their lignicolous lifestyle.
Mycologists recently discovered a new species within Neolentinus in North America.

Etymology

From Neo- (new) + Lentinus (a related fungal genus) in modern taxonomy.
