spiny

1. ADJ

/ ˈspaɪni / (AmE), / ˈspaɪni / (BrE)
covered with or having sharp points such as spines, barbs, or thorns
covered with spines or thorns

Examples

The cactus is well known for its spiny stems and leaves.
Some sea urchins are dangerously spiny, so handle them with care.
The spiny branches made hiking through the thicket difficult.

Synset Examples

a horse with a short bristly mane
bristly shrubs
burred fruits
setaceous whiskers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thorny - covered with or having many thorns
  • Synonym: bristly - having stiff hairs or bristles
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even or flat surface without projections

Etymology

From spine + -y (forming adjectives meaning 'having the quality of').

2. ADJ

/ ˈspaɪni / (AmE), / ˈspaɪni / (BrE)
covered with or having many spines or sharp projections; thorny.
having spines or sharp projections

Examples

The cactus is known for its spiny stems, which deter animals from eating it.
The fish had a spiny dorsal fin that made handling it difficult.
Certain spiny insects use their sharp projections as a defense mechanism.

Synset Examples

the dorsal fin is spinous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thorny - covered with thorns; difficult or problematic
  • Antonym: spineless - without spines; lacking sharp projections

Etymology

From Latin 'spina' (thorn, spine) + English '-y'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02286487-a
Synset POS: a
