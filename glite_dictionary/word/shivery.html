shivery

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈʃɪvəri / (BrE)
feeling so cold that it causes or is likely to cause shivering
feeling cold enough to shiver

Examples

After walking in the rain, I felt shivery all evening.
It was a bleak, shivery morning in early November.
She wrapped herself in a blanket to keep the shivery feeling away.

Synset Examples

felt all shivery
shivery weather

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chilly - uncomfortably cool or cold
  • Degree Higher: freezing - very cold; at or below the temperature at which water freezes

Etymology

From 'shiver' (to tremble or shake) + '-y' (adjective suffix), attested since the 16th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈʃɪvəri / (BrE)
making you feel afraid, nervous, or excited, as if you might shiver; causing a reaction of fear or terror
causing fear or a feeling of shivering

Examples

The abandoned house had a shivery atmosphere that made us hesitate to go inside.
She loves reading shivery ghost stories late at night.
The movie's shivery soundtrack heightened the suspense in every scene.

Synset Examples

a scary movie
the most terrible and shuddery … tales of murder and revenge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scary - causing fear or alarm
  • Synonym: spine-chilling - causing a strong feeling of fear or excitement

Etymology

From 'shiver', meaning to tremble or quiver, plus the suffix '-y'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00196525-s
Synset POS: s
