trough

1. NOUN

/ trɔf / (AmE), / trɒf / (BrE)
a long, narrow, and usually shallow depression or hollow in the ground, in the sea, or between waves
long, narrow depression in earth or sea

Examples

The boat rocked gently as it passed over a trough between waves.
Scientists mapped a deep oceanic trough off the coast of the island.
After the earthquake, a broad trough formed in the landscape, visible from the air.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ridge - a long, narrow raised land formation, opposite of a trough

Etymology

From Old English *troh*, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch *trogh* and German *Trog*.

2. NOUN

/ trɔf / (AmE), / trɒf / (BrE)
A long, narrow, open container for holding water or food for animals, or for other purposes.
long, narrow container

Examples

The farmer filled the trough with fresh water for the cows.
Rainwater collected in the stone trough beside the barn.
They planted herbs in a wooden trough on the windowsill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basin - a wide, open container for holding liquids

Etymology

From Old English 'troh', from Proto-Germanic *trugaz.

4. NOUN

/ trɔf / (AmE), / trɒf / (BrE)
A long, narrow open container from which animals such as cattle or horses eat or drink.
animal feeding container

Examples

The cows gathered around the trough for their morning feed.
After cleaning the trough, the farmer filled it with fresh water.
Hay was piled high in the wooden trough inside the stable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manger - an open box in a stable from which horses or cattle feed

Etymology

From Old English 'trog' (wooden vessel or boat), related to Old Norse 'trogr'.

/ trɑf / (AmE), / trɒf / (BrE)

Examples

Related Terms

6. NOUN

/ trɑf / (AmE), / trɒf / (BrE)
A long, narrow open container or channel, especially one fixed beneath the edge of a roof to catch and carry away rainwater.
rainwater channel under a roof

Examples

The metal trough along the roof was overflowing after the storm.
Climbing the ladder, she cleaned leaves out of the trough to prevent blockage.
During heavy rain, the old wooden trough struggled to carry all the water away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gutter - a channel at the edge of a roof for carrying off rainwater

Etymology

Old English *troh*, from Proto-Germanic *trugaz*, meaning 'wooden vessel'.
