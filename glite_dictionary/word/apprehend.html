to apprehend

1. VERB

/ ˌæprɪˈhɛnd / (AmE), / ˌaprɪˈhɛnd / (BrE)
to arrest or take someone into custody, especially by legal authority
arrest or take into custody

Examples

The police managed to apprehend the thief before he escaped with the valuables.
After a long chase, authorities finally apprehended the fugitive near the city border.
It took several days for investigators to apprehend all members of the criminal gang.

Synset Examples

the police nabbed the suspected criminals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrest - to seize and take into custody by legal authority

Etymology

From Latin apprehendere (to seize, grasp).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01217701-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌæprɪˈhɛnd / (AmE), / ˌæprɪˈhɛnd / (BrE)
to anticipate or expect something with fear or anxiety
anticipate with fear or anxiety

Examples

Many people apprehend the results of the exam, fearing they will not pass.
She could not help but apprehend what might happen to her family during the storm.
As the deadline approached, he began to apprehend the consequences of his actions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dread - to anticipate with great fear or apprehension

Etymology

From Latin apprehendere, from ad- (to) + prehendere (to seize, grasp, take).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01786251-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌæprɪˈhɛnd / (AmE), / ˌæprɪˈhɛnd / (BrE)
to understand or grasp mentally; to perceive the meaning of something
understand or grasp mentally

Examples

Despite the complexity of his explanation, she managed to apprehend the main points clearly.
Some philosophical concepts are difficult to apprehend without prior knowledge of the subject.
It took her some time to apprehend the full implications of the decision.

Synset Examples

Do you comprehend the meaning of this letter?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to comprehend - to understand; to grasp the meaning of
  • Antonym: to misunderstand - to fail to understand or interpret correctly

Etymology

From Latin apprehendere (to seize, grasp, understand), from ad- (toward) + prehendere (to seize).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00589616-v
Synset POS: v
