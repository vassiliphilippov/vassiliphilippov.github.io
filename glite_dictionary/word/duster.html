duster

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌstər / (AmE), / ˈdʌstə / (BrE)
(Baseball) A pitch thrown intentionally close to the batter to intimidate or force them away from the plate.
baseball pitch close to the batter

Examples

The pitcher threw a duster to move the hitter off the plate.
After that duster, the batter stepped back nervously.
The umpire warned the pitcher for a dangerous duster in the second inning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beanball - a pitch thrown at or near the batter's head

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʌstɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʌstə / (BrE)
a loose, long coat or coverall, often reaching to the ankles, originally worn to protect clothing from dust
long, loose protective coat

Examples

She wore a duster over her dress when riding in the open car.
The old-fashioned duster was once common among travelers on dusty roads.
He hung his duster by the door to keep the sand off his clothes.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdʌstər / (AmE), / ˈdʌstə / (BrE)
a windstorm, especially in dry regions, that lifts up and carries clouds of dust or sand.
windstorm lifting dust or sand

Examples

The sudden duster reduced visibility to almost nothing on the highway.
Farmers rushed to protect their crops when a duster swept across the plains.
Last night's duster left a thick layer of sand covering the entire town.

Synset Examples

it was the kind of duster not experienced in years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dust storm - a strong, violent wind that carries clouds of dust, sand, and dirt over an extensive area

4. NOUN

/ ˈdʌstər / (AmE), / ˈdʌstə / (BrE)
A piece of cloth used for removing dust from surfaces.
cloth used for dusting

Examples

She grabbed a duster to wipe the shelves clean.
He used a soft duster to polish the table.
Make sure to wash the duster after each use to keep it effective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rag - a piece of old cloth used for cleaning or wiping surfaces

Etymology

From 'dust' + '-er', indicating a tool for dusting.
