hinder

1. VERB

/ ˈhɪndər / (AmE), / ˈhɪndə / (BrE)
to make it more difficult for someone to do something or for something to happen; to put at a disadvantage
to make progress more difficult

Examples

Heavy traffic can hinder emergency vehicles from reaching the scene quickly.
Wearing gloves sometimes hinders my ability to type efficiently.
Her injury hindered her progress in the competition.

Synset Examples

The brace I have to wear is hindering my movements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to impede - to slow down or prevent the progress of something or someone
  • Synonym: to obstruct - to block a path, passage, or process; hinder
  • Antonym: to facilitate - to make an action or process easier

Etymology

From Old English 'hindrian' (to harm or impede).

2. VERB

/ ˈhɪndɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɪndə / (BrE)
to make it difficult for someone or something to progress or develop as intended; to be an obstacle to
to be an obstacle; impede progress

Examples

Heavy traffic can hinder emergency vehicles from reaching their destination quickly.
Lack of funding may hinder the progress of scientific research.
She didn't let her injury hinder her from completing the marathon.

Synset Examples

She is impeding the progress of our project

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to impede - to slow the progress of; to obstruct
  • Synonym: block - to prevent movement or progress
  • Antonym: to facilitate - to make (an action or process) easy or easier

Etymology

From Old English 'hindrian' (to harm, check), related to 'hinder' (at the back).

3. ADJ

/ ˈhɪndər / (AmE), / ˈhɪndə / (BrE)
(of a part of an animal) situated at or near the rear or back.
at or near the back (of an animal)

Examples

The hinder legs of the horse were injured during the race.
Researchers studied the hinder part of the animal to understand its movement.
The hinder limbs are generally stronger in jumping mammals.

Synset Examples

back (or hind) legs
the hinder part of a carcass

Related Terms

  • Antonym: front - located at or near the front of an animal
  • Synonym: hind - situated at the back (animal anatomy)

Etymology

From Old English hindra, comparative form of behindan (behind).
