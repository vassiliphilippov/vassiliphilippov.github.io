soundly

1. ADV

/ ˈsaʊndli / (AmE), / ˈsaʊndli / (BrE)
In a way that is deep, thorough, or complete; especially used to describe sleeping deeply or being thoroughly defeated.
in a deep or thorough way

Examples

He soundly defeated his opponent in the debate.
After a long hike, she slept soundly all night.
The baby was resting soundly in her crib despite the noise.

Synset Examples

slept soundly through the storm
the baby is sleeping soundly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deeply - to a great depth or thoroughly

Etymology

From Middle English, from 'sound' + '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ˈsaʊndli / (AmE), / ˈsaʊndli / (BrE)
in a thorough or complete manner; completely or decisively.
thoroughly; completely

Examples

The team was soundly beaten in the final match.
She was soundly criticized for her lack of preparation.
They soundly rejected the proposal after much discussion.

Synset Examples

he was soundly defeated
we beat him good

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thoroughly - in a thorough manner; completely
  • Antonym: partially - only in part; not completely

Etymology

From 'sound' (adj., in the sense of 'in good condition, thorough') + '-ly'.
