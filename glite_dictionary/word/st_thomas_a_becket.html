St. Thomas a Becket

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈtɑːməs ə ˈbɛkɪt / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈtɒməs ə ˈbɛkɪt / (BrE)
(c. 1118–1170) Archbishop of Canterbury who was murdered after opposing King Henry II's attempts to constrain ecclesiastical authority; venerated as a saint and martyr in the Roman Catholic Church.
Archbishop of Canterbury, martyred saint (12th century)

Examples

Many pilgrims visited the shrine of St. Thomas a Becket at Canterbury Cathedral during the Middle Ages.
The murder of St. Thomas a Becket had a profound effect on the relationship between church and state in England.
Historians still debate the motivations behind King Henry II's conflict with St. Thomas a Becket.
