inhabitancy

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈhæbɪtənsi / (AmE), / ɪnˈhæbɪtənsi / (BrE)
The act or state of living or dwelling permanently in a particular place.
the act of living in a place

Examples

The inhabitancy of the old mansion lasted for nearly a century.
Laws regarding inhabitancy can affect eligibility for certain public services.
Animal biologists study patterns of inhabitancy across different ecosystems.

Synset Examples

he studied the creation and inhabitation and demise of the colony

Related Terms

  • Synonym: residence - the fact of living in a particular place
  • Synonym: occupancy - the act of occupying or taking possession of a place
  • Antonym: vacancy - the state of being empty or unoccupied

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *inhabitantia*, from Latin *inhabitare* (to inhabit).
