Graminaceae

1. NOUN

/ ɡræmɪˈneɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ɡræmɪˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
A large family of mostly herbaceous plants known as grasses, including cereals, bamboo, reeds, and sugar cane.
the botanical family of grasses

Examples

Rice, wheat, and corn are important cereals that belong to the Graminaceae family.
Bamboo, though it looks different from lawn grass, is a member of the Graminaceae.
Scientists study the Graminaceae to improve crop yields and understand plant evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Poaceae - the current accepted botanical family name for grasses

Etymology

From Latin 'gramen' (grass) + suffix '-aceae', used for plant family names.
