glossiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɑːsi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɡlɒs.i.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being smooth and shiny, often reflecting light
smooth and shiny quality

Examples

The glossiness of the marble floor made the room appear brighter and more elegant.
After applying the new polish, the car regained its original glossiness.
The artist preferred paint with a high glossiness for a more dramatic effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shininess - the quality of being shiny
  • Antonym: matteness - the quality of being dull and not shiny

Etymology

From 'glossy' + '-ness', where 'glossy' derives from Middle English 'glosie', from 'gloss' (shine, luster).
