tapotement

1. NOUN

/ təˈpoʊtmənt / (AmE), / təˈpəʊtmənt / (BrE)
a massage technique in which the body is tapped rhythmically with the fingers or the sides of the hand, often used to loosen mucus in respiratory therapy
rhythmic tapping massage technique
medical

Examples

The physiotherapist used tapotement to help the patient clear mucus from his lungs.
Swedish massage sometimes includes tapotement as part of the treatment routine.
Learning proper tapotement technique can be beneficial for respiratory therapy.

Etymology

From French 'tapoter' (to tap lightly), based on 'taper' (to tap).
