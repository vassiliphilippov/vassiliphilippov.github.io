to starve

1. VERB

/ stɑrv / (AmE), / stɑːv / (BrE)
to suffer or die from lack of food; to go hungry
to suffer or die from lack of food

Examples

Many animals starve during harsh winters when food is scarce.
If we do not get help soon, the villagers will starve.
After hiking all day without lunch, I was beginning to starve.

Synset Examples

Let's eat — I'm starving!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: famine - extreme scarcity of food
  • Antonym: feed - to give food to; to eat in order not to be hungry

Etymology

From Old English 'steorfan' (to die), originally meaning 'to die' and later narrowing in sense to 'die from hunger'.

2. VERB

/ stɑɹv / (AmE), / stɑːv / (BrE)
to die from lack of food; to suffer or perish as a result of extreme hunger.
die from lack of food

Examples

Thousands of people began to starve during the prolonged famine.
If we don't get help soon, many could starve in the disaster zone.
Some animals starve when their natural food sources are destroyed.

Synset Examples

The political prisoners starved to death
Many famished in the countryside during the drought

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to famish - to suffer from extreme hunger

Etymology

From Old English 'steorfan' (to die).

3. VERB

/ stɑrv / (AmE), / stɑːv / (BrE)
to have a strong craving or intense desire for something, especially in a figurative sense
crave intensely; have a great desire

Examples

After weeks without a vacation, I'm starving for a break from work.
The children were starving for attention after their parents got home.
She was starving for affection in that lonely new city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to crave - to have a strong want or desire for something
  • Synonym: desire - to long for or hope for something strongly

Etymology

From Old English *steorfan* (to die), originally meaning 'to die', later evolving to mean 'to die of hunger' and 'to suffer from lack'.

4. VERB

/ stɑːrv / (AmE), / stɑːv / (BrE)
to deprive someone or something of something necessary, causing suffering or malfunction
deprive of something necessary

Examples

The plants were starved of sunlight and slowly withered away.
If you starve your body of essential vitamins, you can develop serious health problems.
The old computer was starved of updates, making it vulnerable to viruses.

Synset Examples

he is starving her of love
The engine was starved of fuel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deprive - to deny someone the use or possession of something

Etymology

From Old English *steorfan* (to die), which later developed the sense of 'perishing from hunger'.

5. VERB

/ stɑːrv / (AmE), / stɑːv / (BrE)
to deprive someone or something of food, causing extreme hunger or possible death
deprive of food

Examples

The army was accused of trying to starve the city into surrender.
It is cruel to starve animals as a form of punishment.
They were deliberately starved by their captors for several days.

Synset Examples

They starved the prisoners

Related Terms

  • Antonym: feed - to give food to someone or something

Etymology

From Old English 'steorfan' (to die), later influenced in Middle English by Old French 'estover'.
