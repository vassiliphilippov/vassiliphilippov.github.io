trichloroethylene

1. NOUN

/ traɪˌklɔroʊˈɛθɪliːn / (AmE), / traɪˌklɔːrəʊˈiːθɪliːn / (BrE)
a heavy, colorless, highly toxic liquid used as a solvent and in dry cleaning, known to cause cancer and damage to the liver and lungs
toxic solvent liquid chemical
medical

Examples

Many factories stopped using trichloroethylene due to its hazardous effects on health.
Proper protective equipment is required when handling trichloroethylene in laboratories.
The contamination of groundwater with trichloroethylene raised concerns among local residents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solvent - a liquid that dissolves other substances

Etymology

From tri- (three) + chloro- (chlorine) + ethylene, indicating three chlorine atoms in the ethylene molecule.
