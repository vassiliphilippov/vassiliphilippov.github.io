Torpedinidae

1. NOUN

/ tɔrˌpɛdəˈnɪdi / (AmE), / tɔːˌpɛdɪˈnɪːdaɪ / (BrE)
A family of electric rays, marine cartilaginous fishes known for their ability to produce electric shocks.
family of electric rays

Examples

The Torpedinidae family encompasses several genera of electric rays found in tropical and temperate seas.
Members of the Torpedinidae can immobilize their prey using electric shocks produced by specialized organs.
Scientists are studying the nervous system of Torpedinidae to better understand bioelectricity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electric ray - a type of ray fish capable of generating electric shocks

Etymology

From New Latin Torpedinidae, from the genus Torpedo.
