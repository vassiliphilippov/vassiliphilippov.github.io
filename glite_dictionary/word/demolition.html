demolition

1. NOUN

/ dɛməˈlɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛm.əˈlɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the act of deliberately destroying a building or other structure, typically with explosives or machinery
the act of tearing down a structure

Examples

The old stadium was closed for demolition last year.
Careful planning is required before starting the demolition of large structures.
Residents gathered to watch the demolition of the abandoned warehouse.

Synset Examples

the demolition of the huge tower was quite a spectacle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destruction - the act of causing so much damage to something that it no longer exists or cannot be repaired
  • Antonym: construction - the building or creation of something, especially buildings

Etymology

From Latin 'demoliri' (to tear down).

2. NOUN

/ dəˈmoʊ.lɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛm.əˈlɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the act or process of completely destroying a building or other structure, or the event in which this happens
act of destroying a building or structure

Examples

The old stadium was reduced to rubble during the demolition last weekend.
Local residents protested the demolition of the historic theater in the city center.
Safety precautions were taken before the demolition of the abandoned warehouse began.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destruction - the act of destroying something completely

Etymology

From Latin 'demolitiō' (a tearing down), from 'demolīrī' (to tear down).
