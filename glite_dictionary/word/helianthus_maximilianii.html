Helianthus maximilianii

1. NOUN

/ hiˌliːˈænθəs ˌmæksɪˌmɪliˈæni.aɪ / (AmE), / hiˌliːˈænθəs ˌmaksɪˌmɪlɪˈani.aɪ / (BrE)
A tall perennial plant native to central United States and Canada, with golden-yellow flowers, commonly known as Maximilian sunflower.
species of tall perennial sunflower

Examples

The fields were filled with blooming Helianthus maximilianii during late summer.
Gardeners often plant Helianthus maximilianii to attract birds and pollinators.
Botanists documented the distribution of Helianthus maximilianii across the prairie region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunflower - any plant of the genus Helianthus, especially Helianthus annuus

Etymology

Genus name from Greek *helios* (sun) and *anthos* (flower); species honorific for Prince Maximilian of Wied-Neuwied, a German naturalist.
