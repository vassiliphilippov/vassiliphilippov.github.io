tetracycline

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛtrəˈsaɪklin / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˈsaɪkliːn / (BrE)
an antibiotic derived from Streptomyces bacteria, used to treat a wide range of bacterial infections
broad-spectrum antibiotic drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed tetracycline to treat her respiratory infection.
Some bacteria have developed resistance to tetracycline over time.
Tetracycline is also used in veterinary medicine to treat various animal diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibiotic - a drug that destroys or inhibits bacteria

Etymology

From tetra- (four) + cycl- (cycle, referring to ring structure) + -ine; named for its four-ring molecular structure.
