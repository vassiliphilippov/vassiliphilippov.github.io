concussion

1. NOUN

/ kənˈkʌʃən / (AmE), / kənˈkʌʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
A temporary injury to the brain caused by a bump or blow to the head, often resulting in short-term loss of consciousness or confusion.
injury to the brain from a blow
medical

Examples

The football player was diagnosed with a concussion after the collision on the field.
She experienced dizziness and headaches following her concussion.
Doctors recommend rest and monitoring after a concussion to ensure full recovery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trauma - physical injury or wound

Etymology

From Latin 'concussio', from 'concutere' (to shake violently).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈkʌʃən / (AmE), / kənˈkʌʃən / (BrE)
A violent shaking or jarring caused by a sudden impact or blow, especially one strong enough to cause damage.
violent blow or shaking from impact

Examples

The explosion produced a concussion that shattered windows nearby.
The bell rang so loudly that its concussion could be felt throughout the building.
The concussion from the crash knocked him off his feet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shock - sudden or violent disturbance or impact

Etymology

From Latin 'concussio' (a shaking), from 'concutere' (to shake violently).
