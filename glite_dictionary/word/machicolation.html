machicolation

1. NOUN

/ məˌtʃɪkəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / məˌtʃɪkəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
A projecting structure on a castle or fortification wall, supported by corbels, with openings through which defenders could drop objects or substances on attackers below.
projecting castle parapet with defensive openings

Examples

The castle walls were lined with machicolations for improved defense against invaders.
During the siege, boiling oil was poured through the machicolations onto the attackers below.
Restoration work revealed original machicolation structures above the main gate.

Etymology

From Old French 'machicoulis', from 'mâcher' (to crush) + 'col' (neck).
