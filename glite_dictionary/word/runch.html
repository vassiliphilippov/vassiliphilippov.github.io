runch

1. NOUN

/ rʌntʃ / (AmE), / rʌntʃ / (BrE)
A Eurasian wild plant of the mustard family, with yellow, mauve, or white flowers and podlike fruits, commonly considered a weed.
a Eurasian weed with yellow or white flowers

Examples

The field was overgrown with runch after a season without tilling.
Farmers often struggle to remove runch from their wheat crops.
Bright yellow flowers made the expanse of runch easy to spot in the meadow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charlock - wild mustard plant, also called runch
