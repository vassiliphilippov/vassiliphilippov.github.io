jackknife

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒækˌnaɪf / (AmE), / ˈdʒæk.naɪf / (BrE)
a type of dive in which the diver bends at the waist to touch the ankles before straightening out before entry into the water
a dive where the diver bends and then straightens

Examples

She executed a perfect jackknife from the 3-meter board during the competition.
The dive coach asked the students to practice their jackknife repeatedly until their form improved.
Judges gave him high marks for his clean jackknife entry into the water.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒækˌnaɪf / (AmE), / ˈdʒækˌnaɪf / (BrE)
a large folding knife with one or more blades that fold into the handle
large folding knife

Examples

He always kept a jackknife in his tool kit for emergencies.
The jackknife had several blades for different purposes.
As a child, he was fascinated by his grandfather's old jackknife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pocketknife - a small folding knife carried in a pocket

Etymology

From 'jack' (common man) + 'knife'; originally referring to a utility knife for general use.

3. VERB

/ ˈdʒækˌnaɪf / (AmE), / ˈdʒækˌnaɪf / (BrE)
to dive into water by bending at the waist at a right angle and straightening out before entering the water
to dive bending at the waist at a right angle

Examples

She performed a perfect jackknife off the high diving board.
The coach taught us how to jackknife safely into the deep end.
He won the competition with a flawless jackknife in the final round.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dive - to jump headfirst into water

Etymology

From 'jackknife', originally a folding knife, referencing the folding and straightening motion in the dive.

Synset POS: v
