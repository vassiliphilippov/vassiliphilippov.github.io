zettabyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɛtəˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈzɛtəˌbaɪt / (BrE)
a unit of information equal to 1,024 exbibytes or 2^70 bytes, commonly used to measure large amounts of digital data
unit of digital information (2^70 bytes)

Examples

A single data center can now store more than one zettabyte of information.
By 2030, global data creation is expected to reach several zettabytes per year.
Transferring a zettabyte of data would take years over a standard home internet connection.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: exabyte - a unit of information equal to 2^60 bytes

Etymology

From the prefix 'zetta-' (denoting a factor of 10^21, from Latin 'septem' meaning 'seven') and 'byte'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈzɛtəˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈzɛtəˌbaɪt / (BrE)
a unit of information equal to 1,000 exabytes or 10^21 bytes
a digital information unit (10^21 bytes)

Examples

With the growth of the internet, global data storage may soon reach several zettabytes.
A zettabyte is an immense amount of data, equivalent to one trillion gigabytes.
Cloud services manage data on the scale of zettabytes for companies worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: exabyte - a unit of information equal to 10^18 bytes
  • Degree Higher: yottabyte - a unit of information equal to 10^24 bytes

Etymology

From prefix 'zetta-' (denoting a factor of 10^21, from Italian 'sette' meaning seven, as it represents 1000^7) plus 'byte'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13653148-n
Synset POS: n
