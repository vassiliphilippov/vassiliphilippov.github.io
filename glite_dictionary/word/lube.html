lube

1. VERB

/ luːb / (AmE), / luːb / (BrE)
to apply a lubricant to something, such as a machine or vehicle part, to reduce friction or wear
apply a lubricant
informal

Examples

He took the time to lube the chain before the bike race.
Make sure you lube the door hinges to stop them squeaking.
After every oil change, it's a good idea to lube moving engine parts.

Synset Examples

lubricate my car

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lubricate - to apply oil or grease so as to make smooth

Etymology

Shortened form of "lubricate."

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01487608-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ luːb / (AmE), / luːb / (BrE)
A substance, such as oil or grease, that reduces friction by making surfaces smooth or slippery; lubricant.
substance reducing friction; lubricant

Examples

Add some lube to the chain to keep your bike running smoothly.
The mechanic always checks the lube levels during a car service.
Some lube can help stop those annoying squeaks in door hinges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lubricant - A substance used to reduce friction between surfaces in contact

Etymology

Shortened form of 'lubricant.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14914810-n
Synset POS: n
