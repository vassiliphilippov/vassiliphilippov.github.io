accounting

1. NOUN

/ əˈkaʊn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈkaʊn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
A chronological record of related debits and credits in a business, forming part of a ledger of accounts.
record of business debits and credits

Examples

The company's accounting showed every transaction for the previous month.
All entries are carefully added to the accounting for quarterly review.
Proper accounting is essential for tracking business income and expenses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ledger - a book or other collection of financial accounts of a particular type

Etymology

From Old French *acointer* (to account), from Latin *computare* (to calculate).

2. NOUN

/ əˈkaʊn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈkaʊn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
a statement or explanation that makes something clear, especially by revealing its underlying causes
an explanation revealing causes

Examples

The manager demanded an accounting from the team after the project failed.
She was finally forced to provide an accounting for her absence at the meeting.
His accounting of the events was detailed and convincing.

Synset Examples

he was unable to give a clear accounting for his actions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explanation - a statement that makes something clear
  • Synonym: justification - a reason given for something

Etymology

From the verb 'account' meaning to give an explanation or justification; ultimately from Old French 'aconter' and Latin 'computare'.

3. NOUN

/ əˈkaʊn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈkaʊn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or system of recording, classifying, and summarizing financial transactions to provide useful information for decision making
system of recording financial transactions

Examples

She decided to study accounting at university to become a certified public accountant.
Good accounting is essential for any successful business.
Advances in technology have transformed the field of accounting in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bookkeeping - the recording of financial transactions

Etymology

From 'account' + '-ing'; 'account' from Old French *acont* (reckoning) and ultimately from Latin *computare* (to compute).

Source: WordNet

5. NOUN

/ əˈkaʊntɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈkaʊntɪŋ / (BrE)
the profession or process of maintaining, auditing, and preparing financial records and reports for businesses or individuals
the profession of managing financial records

Examples

She decided to pursue a career in accounting after discovering her aptitude for numbers.
The company's accounting department handles all invoices and payroll processes.
Good accounting practices are essential for the financial health of any organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bookkeeping - the activity or occupation of keeping financial records
  • Synonym: auditing - the inspection and verification of financial records

Etymology

From 'account' + '-ing', with 'account' from Old French 'acont', from Latin 'computare' (to calculate).
