solemnization

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑːləmnəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɒləmnʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the formal and public performance of a ceremony or sacrament, especially with officially recognized rituals
formal public performance of a ceremony

Examples

The solemnization of the wedding took place in the cathedral with a full congregation.
Legal documents were required before the solemnization of their marriage could proceed.
Many cultures view the solemnization of important rites as necessary for community recognition.

Synset Examples

the celebration of marriage

Etymology

From Old French solennisation, from Medieval Latin solemnizatio, from Latin sollemnis (solemn).
