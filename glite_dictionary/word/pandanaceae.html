Pandanaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæn.dəˈneɪ.si.i / (AmE), / ˌpændəˈneɪsɪ.iː / (BrE)
A family of woody plants in the order Pandanales that includes the genus Pandanus (screw pines).
family of tropical woody plants (Pandanus family)

Examples

The Pandanaceae are mainly found in tropical and subtropical regions.
Many members of the Pandanaceae have long, narrow leaves and aerial roots.
The screw pine, a member of the Pandanaceae, is often used in landscaping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pandanus - a genus of tropical trees also known as screw pines

Etymology

From the genus name Pandanus + Latin family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpæn.dəˈneɪ.si.i / (AmE), / ˌpan.dəˈneɪ.sɪ.iː / (BrE)
a family of tropical woody plants, including the genus Pandanus, native chiefly to the Old World tropics
family of tropical woody plants (including pandanus)

Examples

Many species in the Pandanaceae family are important for weaving materials and traditional medicines.
The screw pine is one of the most widely recognized members of the Pandanaceae.
Botanists conducted a survey of the Pandanaceae across several Pacific islands.

Etymology

From the genus name Pandanus plus the standard botanical Latin family suffix -aceae.
