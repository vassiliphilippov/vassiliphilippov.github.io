nipper

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪpər / (AmE), / ˈnɪpə / (BrE)
a grasping claw or pincer on the limb of a crustacean or other arthropod, used for seizing or holding
claw or pincer of a crustacean

Examples

The crab used its large nipper to defend itself against predators.
Lobsters have powerful nippers for crushing and tearing their food.
The biologist carefully measured the nipper of the crayfish during her research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pincer - a grasping claw of a crustacean or insect
  • Synonym: chela - a pincer-like organ in some arthropods

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɪp.ər / (AmE), / ˈnɪp.ə / (BrE)
a child or young person; a youngster (mainly British, informal)
child; youngster (informal, British)
informal british

Examples

When I was a nipper, we used to play football in the street.
The park is always full of nippers running around after school.
He's a bright little nipper, always asking questions.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: child - a young human being below the age of puberty
  • Synonym: kid - informal term for a child or young person
  • Antonym: adult - a fully grown person

Etymology

Originally a dialect term; related to 'nip', possibly referring to a quick or lively child.
