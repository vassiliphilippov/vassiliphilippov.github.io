baccate

1. ADJ

/ ˈbækeɪt / (AmE), / ˈbæk.eɪt / (BrE)
(of a fruit or structure) having the appearance of a berry; berry-like.
resembling a berry

Examples

The plant produces baccate fruits that attract birds in the autumn.
Certain fungi form baccate structures that are mistaken for berries.
The baccate appearance of the seed suggests it is adapted for animal dispersal.

Etymology

From Latin baccatus, from bacca (berry).

2. ADJ

/ ˈbækeɪt / (AmE), / ˈbak.eɪt / (BrE)
(of a plant or fruit) producing or bearing berries; resembling or consisting of berries.
producing or bearing berries

Examples

The holly is a baccate shrub with bright red fruit in winter.
Many tropical plants have baccate fruits attractive to birds.
Botanists described the new species as uniquely baccate among its relatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: berry - a small, pulpy, edible fruit

Etymology

From Latin baccatus (berry-like), from bacca (berry).
