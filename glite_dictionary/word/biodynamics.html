biodynamics

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊ.daɪˈnæm.ɪks / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪ.əʊ.daɪˈnæm.ɪks / (BrE)
the branch of biology that studies the energy processes and activity within living organisms
study of energy and activity in living organisms

Examples

Recent research in biodynamics has revealed new insights into cellular energy conversion.
The principles of biodynamics are applied in fields such as physiology and sports science.
Understanding human biodynamics can lead to better treatments for muscular disorders.

Synset Examples

These relations constitute a phenomenological network that guides the theoretical understanding of the biodynamics of the human body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biomechanics - the study of the mechanical laws relating to the movement or structure of living organisms

Etymology

From bio- (life) + dynamics (forces and motion), relating to biological energy and activity.
