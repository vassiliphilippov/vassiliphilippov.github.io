nonsense

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɑnˌsɛns / (AmE), / ˈnɒnsəns / (BrE)
(of words or sounds) having no intelligible meaning; meaningless or senseless.
having no intelligible meaning

Examples

The poem was filled with nonsense syllables that nobody could interpret.
His answer was just a nonsense jumble that left us confused.
Children often invent nonsense words while playing games.

Synset Examples

nonsense syllables
a nonsensical jumble of words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsensical - lacking sense or meaning; absurd
  • Antonym: meaningful - having meaning; significant

Etymology

From English 'non-' (not) + 'sense' (meaning).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01501931-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːnˌsɛns / (AmE), / ˈnɒnsəns / (BrE)
speech, writing, or ideas that are meaningless or do not make sense
meaningless or senseless speech or writing

Examples

He spoke such nonsense that no one understood his point.
The book is full of nonsense and has no scientific value.
Please stop talking nonsense and answer the question seriously.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sense - meaning or intelligibility in speech or writing
  • Synonym: gibberish - unintelligible or meaningless speech or writing

Etymology

From Latin 'nonsensus' (not meaning), formed from 'non-' (not) + 'sensus' (sense).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06619692-n
Synset POS: n
