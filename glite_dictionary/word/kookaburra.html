kookaburra

1. NOUN

/ ˈkuːkəˌbʌrə / (AmE), / ˈkʊkəbʌrə / (BrE)
An Australian kingfisher known for its loud, distinctive cackling call.
Australian bird with a cackling call

Examples

The distinctive laugh of the kookaburra echoed through the Australian bush at dawn.
A kookaburra perched on the fence, watching the garden with keen interest.
Many tourists delight in hearing a kookaburra for the first time during their visit to Australia.

Etymology

From Wiradjuri (an Australian Aboriginal language) 'guuguubarra', imitative of the bird's call.
