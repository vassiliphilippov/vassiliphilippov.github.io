pommel

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌməl / (AmE), / ˈpʌməl / (BrE)
the rounded or raised front part of a saddle that serves as a handgrip for the rider
raised front part of a saddle

Examples

She gripped the pommel tightly as the horse started to gallop.
The old saddle had a worn leather pommel.
He steadied himself by holding the pommel while mounting the horse.

Etymology

From Old French *pomel* (little apple, knob), diminutive of *pomme* (apple).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpʌməl / (AmE), / ˈpʌməl / (BrE)
a rounded knob or ornament, usually at the end of the handle (hilt) of a sword or dagger
knob at the end of a sword hilt

Examples

The knight's sword was adorned with a gold pommel engraved with intricate designs.
She admired the jeweled pommel of the ancient dagger displayed in the museum.
He gripped the pommel tightly as he prepared to draw his sword.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hilt - the handle of a sword or dagger

Etymology

From Old French 'pomel', diminutive of 'pomme' (apple), referencing the knob's shape.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03631563-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈpʌməl / (AmE), / ˈpʌməl / (BrE)
one of the handgrips on a pommel horse, used by gymnasts during exercise routines
handgrip on a gymnastics pommel horse

Examples

The gymnast gripped the pommel tightly as he prepared for his routine.
Newcomers often struggle to keep both hands on the pommel while swinging their legs.
Each pommel on the horse must be securely fastened for safety.

Etymology

From Old French *pomel* (little apple, knob), diminutive of *pomme* (apple), due to the rounded shape.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03986690-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈpʌməl / (AmE), / ˈpʌməl / (BrE)
to strike someone or something repeatedly, usually with the fists
strike repeatedly with the fists

Examples

The boxer pommelled his opponent until the referee stepped in.
Angry at the injustice, the crowd began to pommel the statue with their fists.
During the heated argument, he threatened to pommel his rival if he didn't apologize.

Synset Examples

The pedestrians pummeled the demonstrators

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pummel - to strike repeatedly, especially with the fists

Etymology

From Old French pomeler (to strike with a pommel), from 'pommel' (knob; handle)

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
