objectivity

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑb.dʒɛkˈtɪv.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɒb.dʒɛkˈtɪv.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of making decisions or forming opinions based on observable facts rather than personal feelings or biases
judgment free from personal bias

Examples

The journalist was praised for her objectivity in covering the controversial event.
To achieve scientific progress, researchers strive for objectivity in their experiments.
His objectivity sometimes made him seem detached from emotional issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impartiality - the quality of not being biased or partial
  • Antonym: subjectivity - judgment influenced by personal feelings and opinions

Etymology

From Medieval Latin objectivitas, from Latin objectivus ('presented to the mind').
