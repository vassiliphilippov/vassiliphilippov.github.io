consonant

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.sə.nənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.sə.nənt / (BrE)
A letter of the alphabet that represents a speech sound that is not a vowel.
letter representing a non-vowel sound

Examples

The letter 'b' is a consonant in the English alphabet.
Sometimes 'y' functions as a vowel, other times as a consonant.
She practiced pronouncing each consonant clearly when learning English.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vowel - a letter representing a speech sound made without any closure in the vocal tract

Etymology

From Latin 'consonans', from 'con-' (with) + 'sonare' (to sound).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɑːn.sə.nənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.sə.nənt / (BrE)
in agreement or harmony with something; consistent
in agreement; harmonious

Examples

Her proposal was consonant with the company's long-term goals.
Such actions are not consonant with the spirit of our agreement.
The two theories are consonant in most important respects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compatible - able to exist or work together without conflict
  • Antonym: discordant - not in harmony; clashing or disagreeing

Etymology

From Latin consonans, from con- (together) + sonare (to sound).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.sə.nənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.sə.nənt / (BrE)
a speech sound produced by obstructing or restricting the flow of air somewhere in the vocal tract, as opposed to a vowel
speech sound other than a vowel

Examples

The letter 'b' represents a consonant sound in English.
She found it difficult to pronounce words with many consonants in a row.
In the word 'cat', both 'c' and 't' are consonants.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vowel - a speech sound produced without significant constriction of the airflow, as opposed to a consonant

Etymology

From Latin 'consonans', present participle of 'consonare' (to sound together).
