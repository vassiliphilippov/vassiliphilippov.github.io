Lyginopteris

1. NOUN

/ laɪˌdʒɪnəˈtɛrɪs / (AmE), / lɪˌdʒɪnɪˈtɛːrɪs / (BrE)
a genus of extinct seed ferns from the Carboniferous period, known through fossil records
fossil genus of seed ferns

Examples

Paleobotanists discovered a well-preserved Lyginopteris fossil during their excavation in the coal seam.
The structure of Lyginopteris seeds provides important evidence of plant evolution in the Carboniferous era.
Researchers compared the vascular tissues of Lyginopteris with those of modern ferns and cycads.

Etymology

From Greek lyginos (flexible twig) + pteris (fern).
