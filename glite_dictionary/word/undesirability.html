undesirability

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.dɪˌzaɪ.rəˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌʌn.dɪˌzaɪ.rəˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being undesirable; the characteristic of being something that should be avoided or is not wanted
the quality of being undesirable

Examples

The undesirability of the location kept property prices low for many years.
Many experts discussed the undesirability of increasing pollution in urban areas.
He was aware of the undesirability of raising the issue at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: desirability - the quality of being desired or wanted

Etymology

From un- (not) + desirability, based on desirable from Latin desiderabilis (that may be desired).
