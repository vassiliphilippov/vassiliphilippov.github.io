forgiver

1. NOUN

/ fərˈɡɪvər / (AmE), / fəˈɡɪvə / (BrE)
a person who pardons or forgives a fault, offense, or wrongdoing
one who forgives others

Examples

She proved herself to be a true forgiver, letting go of old grievances easily.
It takes strength to be a forgiver rather than to hold a grudge.
In the story, the forgiver is ultimately the hero who restores peace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pardoner - a person who pardons others
  • Antonym: accuser - one who charges others with a fault or crime

Etymology

From the verb 'forgive' + '-er', denoting a person who performs the action.
