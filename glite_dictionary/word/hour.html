hour

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈaʊə / (BrE)
A period of time equal to sixty minutes or one twenty-fourth of a day.
sixty-minute period of time

Examples

The movie lasted almost three hours and kept everyone entertained.
She needs to finish her homework within the next hour.
We waited for an hour before the bus finally arrived.

Synset Examples

the job will take more than an hour

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: minute - a unit of time equal to 1/60th of an hour
  • Degree Higher: day - a period of 24 hours

Etymology

From Old French *hore*, from Latin *hora* (hour, time, season), from Ancient Greek *hṓra* (any limited time, season, hour).

3. NOUN

/ aʊɚ / (AmE), / aʊə / (BrE)
A distance measured by the time it takes to travel it, especially when referring to how far apart two places are.
distance measured by travel time

Examples

The nearest grocery store is just half an hour from here by car.
She commutes an hour each day to get to her office downtown.
The beach is about an hour away, making it perfect for weekend trips.

Synset Examples

it's just 10 minutes away

Etymology

From Old French *hore*, from Latin *hora* (‘hour’).
