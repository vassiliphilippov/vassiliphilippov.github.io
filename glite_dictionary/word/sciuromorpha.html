Sciuromorpha

1. NOUN

/ skjuːrəˈmɔrfə / (AmE), / skjuːərəˈmɔːfə / (BrE)
a suborder of rodents that includes squirrels, marmots, gophers, beavers, and related animals
suborder of rodents including squirrels and related animals

Examples

The fossil record provides evidence for the early diversification of Sciuromorpha during the Eocene epoch.
Animals in the Sciuromorpha suborder display a wide range of habitats and behaviors.
Scientists debated whether beavers truly belong within Sciuromorpha based on dental characteristics.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Latin *sciurus* (squirrel) + Greek *morphē* (form, shape).
