Phellodendron

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛləˈdɛndrən / (AmE), / ˌfɛləˈdɛndrən / (BrE)
A small genus of aromatic deciduous trees native to East Asia, often characterized by thick corky bark.
genus of East Asian aromatic trees

Examples

The bark of the Phellodendron tree is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
Many gardens feature Phellodendron species for their attractive foliage and tolerance of urban conditions.
The thick, corky bark helps the Phellodendron survive in harsh climates.

Etymology

From Greek 'phellos' (cork) + 'dendron' (tree), referring to the corky bark of the tree.
