Jean Lafitte

1. PROPN

/ ʒɑn ləˈfiːt / (AmE), / ʒɑːn laˈfiːt / (BrE)
A French pirate (1780–1826) who aided the United States during the War of 1812 and was later officially pardoned for his crimes.
French pirate who aided the US in the War of 1812

Examples

The legend of Jean Lafitte continues to fascinate historians and treasure hunters alike.
During the War of 1812, Jean Lafitte offered his support to the Americans in exchange for a pardon.
Many stories about Jean Lafitte's secret hideouts have become part of Louisiana folklore.
