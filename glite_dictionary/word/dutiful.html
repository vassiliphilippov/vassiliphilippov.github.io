dutiful

1. ADJ

/ ˈduːtɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈdjuːtɪfəl / (BrE)
(of a person) obedient and attentive to their duties or obligations, showing a willingness to fulfill what is expected out of a sense of duty or respect.
obedient and responsible; fulfilling duty

Examples

She is a dutiful daughter who visits her parents every weekend.
The dutiful soldier followed every order without complaint.
Even after retiring, he remained a dutiful member of the community.

Synset Examples

a dutiful child
a dutiful citizen
Patient Griselda was a chaste and duteous wife

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obedient - willing to comply with orders or requests
  • Antonym: rebellious - resisting authority or control

Etymology

From 'duty' + '-ful', meaning 'full of duty'.
