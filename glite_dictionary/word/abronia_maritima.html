Abronia maritima

1. NOUN

/ əˌbroʊniə məˈrɪtəmə / (AmE), / əˌbrəʊnɪə məˈrɪtɪmə / (BrE)
A plant species with hemispherical clusters of wine-red flowers, native to coastal dunes from California to Mexico.
coastal plant with red flower clusters

Examples

The sandy dunes were covered with blooming Abronia maritima in the early summer.
Biologists studied Abronia maritima to understand how plants adapt to harsh coastal environments.
Many visitors admired the striking red flowers of Abronia maritima along the trail.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Abronia' (the genus), and 'maritima' meaning 'of the sea' or 'coastal'.
