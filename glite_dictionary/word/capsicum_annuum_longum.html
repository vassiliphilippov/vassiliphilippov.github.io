Capsicum annuum longum

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæpsɪkəm əˈnuːəm ˈlɒŋɡəm / (AmE), / ˌkapsɪkəm əˈnjuːəm ˈlɒŋɡəm / (BrE)
A plant species bearing very hot, slender, and tapering long peppers, usually red in color, commonly grown for culinary use.
species of hot, long red pepper plant

Examples

Many traditional dishes use Capsicum annuum longum to add intense heat and flavor.
The farmer grew several rows of Capsicum annuum longum in his greenhouse for the local market.
Dried Capsicum annuum longum can be ground into a pungent chili powder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chili pepper - the fruit of plants from the genus Capsicum, especially those with a hot flavor

Etymology

From Latin: 'Capsicum' (pepper genus), 'annuum' (annual), 'longum' (long).
