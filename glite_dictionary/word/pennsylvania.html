Pennsylvania

1. PROPN

/ ˌpɛnsəlˈveɪniə / (AmE), / ˌpɛnsɪlˈveɪniə / (BrE)
A state in the northeastern United States, known as one of the original thirteen colonies. Its capital is Harrisburg.
a state in the northeastern U.S.

Examples

She grew up in Pennsylvania and moved to New York after college.
The forests of Pennsylvania are especially beautiful in the autumn.
Many historical events took place in Pennsylvania during the American Revolution.

Etymology

Named after William Penn, the founder, combined with Latin 'Sylvania' meaning 'woods' or 'forest.'

2. PROPN

/ ˌpɛnsəlˈveɪniə / (AmE), / ˌpɛnsɪlˈveɪniə / (BrE)
A state in the northeastern United States; one of the original thirteen colonies, with Philadelphia as its largest city.
U.S. state in the northeast; original colony

Examples

She was born and raised in Pennsylvania before moving to New York.
Many important events of the American Revolution took place in Pennsylvania.
The forests and mountains of Pennsylvania attract hikers and nature enthusiasts from around the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Philadelphia - the largest city in Pennsylvania

Etymology

Named after William Penn, the founder, and the Latin 'sylvania' meaning 'forest'.
