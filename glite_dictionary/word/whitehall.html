Whitehall

1. PROPN

/ ˈwaɪtˌhɔl / (AmE), / ˈwaɪthɔːl / (BrE)
a wide street in London, running from Trafalgar Square to the Houses of Parliament, noted as the site of many government offices; also used to refer to the British government administration collectively
main government street in London; British government
british

Examples

Senior officials at Whitehall declined to comment on the new policy.
The decision is being reviewed by Whitehall before it goes to Parliament.
Many departments are headquartered along Whitehall in central London.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Westminster - district in London home to British Parliament and government offices

Etymology

Named after the former Palace of Whitehall, once the main residence of English monarchs.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪtˌhɔl / (AmE), / ˈwaɪthɔːl / (BrE)
the British government administration and its offices, especially the senior civil service departments collectively
the British civil service or government
british

Examples

Many important decisions are made behind closed doors in Whitehall.
The reforms proposed by Whitehall will affect all public sector workers.
He spent twenty years working in the corridors of Whitehall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Westminster - the UK Parliament and associated political processes

Etymology

Named after Whitehall, a street in London where many UK government departments are located.
