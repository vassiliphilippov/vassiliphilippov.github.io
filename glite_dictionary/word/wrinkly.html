wrinkly

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɪŋkli / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋkli / (BrE)
having many small lines or folds on the surface; marked by wrinkles, especially in skin or fabric.
having many wrinkles

Examples

After the long flight, his wrinkly shirt needed to be ironed.
The old man's wrinkly hands gently held the photograph.
She petted her grandmother's wrinkly dog, laughing at its expressive face.

Synset Examples

tired travelers in wrinkled clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrinkled - having small folds or lines; marked by wrinkles

Etymology

Formed from 'wrinkle' + '-y' suffix.
