Nephthys

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɛfθɪs / (AmE), / ˈnɛfθɪs / (BrE)
An ancient Egyptian goddess associated with the rituals of the dead; sister of Geb and Nut, and wife of Set.
Egyptian goddess linked to funerary rites

Examples

In ancient mythology, Nephthys played a key role in funerary rituals and helped guide souls to the afterlife.
The story describes how Nephthys, along with her sister Isis, mourned for Osiris.
Scholars have studied the symbolism of Nephthys as a guardian of the dead in Egyptian religion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Isis - Egyptian goddess, sister of Nephthys
  • Synonym: Set - Egyptian god, husband of Nephthys and god of chaos

Etymology

From Greek Nephthys, from Egyptian *Nebt-het* ('Lady of the House').
