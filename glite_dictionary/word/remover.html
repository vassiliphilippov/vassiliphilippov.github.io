remover

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈmuːvər / (AmE), / rɪˈmuːvə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to move furniture and other belongings from one place to another, typically as part of a removal service
person who moves furniture for a living

Examples

The remover carefully wrapped the mirrors before loading them onto the truck.
She hired a professional remover to transport her belongings to her new flat.
A team of removers arrived early to help with the office relocation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mover - person or company that moves possessions, especially household goods

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈmuvɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈmuːvə / (BrE)
a substance or product used to take something away, especially by dissolving or cleaning it from a surface
substance used for removing something

Examples

I spilled ink on my shirt and used a stain remover to get it out.
She bought a new paint remover to strip the old cabinets.
Many people use a gentle makeup remover at the end of the day.

Synset Examples

paint remover
rust remover
hair remover

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solvent - a substance, usually liquid, that dissolves another substance

Etymology

From remove + -er, meaning 'one that removes'.
