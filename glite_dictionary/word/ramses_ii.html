Ramses II

1. PROPN

/ ˈræmˌsiz ðə ˈsɛkənd / (AmE), / ˈræmˌsiːz ði ˈsɛkənd / (BrE)
the pharaoh of ancient Egypt who reigned from 1304 to 1237 BC, known for his extensive building projects and military leadership
Egyptian pharaoh (1304–1237 BC)

Examples

Many historians regard Ramses II as one of the most powerful and influential rulers of ancient Egypt.
The temples built by Ramses II still stand as magnificent examples of ancient Egyptian architecture.
In his lifetime, Ramses II led numerous military campaigns to expand and protect the Egyptian empire.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Rhamsēs, from Egyptian Ra-mes-su ('Ra has fashioned him').
