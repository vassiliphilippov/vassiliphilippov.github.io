unction

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˈʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
the quality of expressing earnestness or fervor in a smug, self-serving manner, especially in speech or writing
smug, self-serving earnestness

Examples

The politician's speech was full of moral unction that failed to impress the public.
She delivered her apology with such unction that nobody believed she was truly sorry.
There was a certain unction in his tone that made his compliments sound insincere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unctuousness - excessive or insincere earnestness, especially in speech

Etymology

From Latin unctio (anointing), via Old French unction; figurative senses developed later.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˈʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
the act of anointing with oil as part of a religious ceremony or for healing purposes
anointing with oil in a ceremony

Examples

The priest performed the unction during the baptism ceremony.
Many medieval kings received unction at their coronations as a symbol of divine approval.
The ritual of unction was believed to promote both spiritual and physical healing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anointing - the act of applying oil, especially during a religious ceremony

Etymology

From Latin unctio (anointing), from unguere (to anoint).

3. NOUN

/ ˈʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˈʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
a semisolid preparation, often containing a medicine, applied externally to soothe irritation or as a remedy
medicated ointment or salve

Examples

The doctor recommended applying a thick unction to soothe the rash.
She rubbed the herbal unction onto his sore muscles for relief.
A special medicated unction was prescribed to treat her skin irritation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ointment - a smooth substance applied to the skin for medical purposes
  • Synonym: salve - an oily substance used to soothe or heal the skin

Etymology

From Latin 'unctio' (anointing), from 'unguere' (to anoint).

4. NOUN

/ ˈʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˈʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
excessively smooth or affected flattering speech or manner, often intended to charm or please superficially
affected or excessive flattering speech

Examples

His speech was filled with so much unction that it seemed insincere to everyone present.
She responded to the compliment, recognizing the unction behind his charming words.
Despite the unction of his apology, few believed he truly meant it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flattery - excessive or insincere praise
  • Antonym: sincerity - the quality of being genuine or honest

Etymology

From Latin 'unctio' (an anointing), via Old French 'onction'.

Source: WordNet
