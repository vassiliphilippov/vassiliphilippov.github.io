ratty

1. ADJ

/ ˈræti / (AmE), / ˈræti / (BrE)
(of a place or thing) dirty and in poor condition, suggesting infestation with rats.
dirty and infested with rats
informal

Examples

The old, ratty building at the end of the street was finally demolished.
She refused to rent the ratty apartment because it smelled musty and looked unclean.
After years of neglect, the once-grand hotel had become a ratty and unwelcoming place.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filthy - extremely dirty
  • Antonym: spotless - completely clean and without any dirt

Etymology

From 'rat' + '-y', forming an adjective meaning 'like or characteristic of rats'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00424291-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈræti / (AmE), / ˈræti / (BrE)
(of an object) in poor condition because of long use or lack of care; shabby or worn out.
in bad condition; shabby

Examples

He threw on his ratty old sweater before going outside.
The kids played on a ratty sofa that had seen better days.
She replaced the ratty curtains with new ones for the party.

Synset Examples

a ratty old overcoat
shabby furniture
an old house with dirty windows and tatty curtains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shabby - in poor condition through long use or lack of care
  • Synonym: tatty - worn and shabby; in poor condition

Etymology

From 'rat', suggesting something gnawed or worn as if by rats, plus the adjective-forming suffix '-y'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02593481-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈræti / (AmE), / ˈræti / (BrE)
Resembling or characteristic of a rat; having qualities or an appearance similar to a rat.
resembling or characteristic of a rat

Examples

The old, ratty building looked abandoned and unsafe.
He wore a ratty coat that had holes and torn sleeves.
Stray cats prowled the alleys, giving the neighborhood a slightly ratty feel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rat-like - having features or traits resembling a rat

Etymology

From 'rat' + '-y' suffix, denoting 'like' or 'characteristic of'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02798034-a
Synset POS: a
