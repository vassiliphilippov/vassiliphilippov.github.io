bottleful

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑː.t̬əlˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈbɒt.əl.fʊl / (BrE)
an amount of liquid or substance that fills a bottle
the amount held by a bottle

Examples

He poured a bottleful of wine into the decanter before dinner.
After drinking a bottleful of soda, he still felt thirsty.
The recipe calls for a bottleful of olive oil for the marinade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cupful - the amount contained in a cup
  • Synonym: spoonful - the amount contained in a spoon
  • Synonym: handful - the amount that can be held in one hand

Etymology

Formed from 'bottle' + 'ful', a standard suffix indicating amount that fills a container.
