snowbird

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnoʊˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌbɜːd / (BrE)
a small North American finch, particularly one that is commonly seen in winter, such as the dark-eyed junco.
small North American finch seen in winter

Examples

Flocks of snowbirds gather in the backyards as the weather turns cold.
The snowbird is often identified by its slate-gray plumage and white belly.
During the winter, a snowbird may be one of the few active songbirds in northern areas.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsnoʊˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌbɜːd / (BrE)
a medium-sized Eurasian thrush, especially the fieldfare, that is seen mainly in winter
medium-sized Eurasian thrush (winter)

Examples

The snowbird is often spotted feeding on berries during the cold months.
Birdwatchers gathered to glimpse the arrival of the snowbird in the village park.
As winter approached, flocks of snowbirds became a common sight across the fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fieldfare - a type of Eurasian thrush often called snowbird

3. NOUN

/ ˈsnoʊˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌbɜːd / (BrE)
A small Arctic songbird, especially the snow bunting, known for its predominantly white plumage.
Arctic songbird (snow bunting)

Examples

Flocks of snowbirds appeared near the research station each winter.
The snowbird is well adapted to survive the harsh Arctic environment.
We spotted a rare snowbird during our expedition in Greenland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snow bunting - a small Arctic songbird with white plumage
