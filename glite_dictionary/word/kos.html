kos

1. NOUN

/ koʊs / (AmE), / kəʊs / (BrE)
A traditional unit of length in India, varying in value regionally, usually ranging from about 1.5 to 3 kilometers.
traditional Indian unit of length

Examples

The ancient caravan stopped every kos to rest and water the animals.
He recorded the distance as ten kos, following the traditional route described in old texts.
In some regions, a kos is roughly equal to two miles.

Etymology

From Hindi 'कोस' (kos), from Sanskrit 'क्रोश' (krośa, meaning 'call' or 'cry'), referring to the distance a shout could carry.
