castellated

1. ADJ

/ ˈkæstəˌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkas.tɪ.leɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
having battlements or resembling the repeated square indentations found in the parapet of a castle; constructed to appear like a castle.
having battlements; castle-like in appearance

Examples

The old manor house was castellated to make it look more impressive.
The museum features a castellated tower reminiscent of medieval fortresses.
The architect designed the roofline with a castellated parapet to evoke the style of ancient castles.

Synset Examples

a crenelated molding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crenellated - having a notched or indented parapet, like castle battlements

Etymology

From Latin castellatus, past participle of castellare (to fortify like a castle), from castellum (castle).
