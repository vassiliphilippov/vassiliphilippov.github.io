spellbinding

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɛlˌbaɪndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspɛlˌbaɪndɪŋ / (BrE)
holding your attention completely as if by magic; fascinating or captivating.
fascinating; completely captivating

Examples

The magician's spellbinding performance left the audience speechless.
She gave a spellbinding account of her adventures in the Amazon rainforest.
The film is visually stunning and utterly spellbinding from start to finish.

Synset Examples

read the bedtime story in a hypnotic voice
she had a warm mesmeric charm
the sheer force of his presence was mesmerizing
a spellbinding description of life in ancient Rome

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mesmerizing - fascinating in a hypnotic way; compelling attention
  • Antonym: boring - not interesting; dull or tedious

Etymology

From 'spellbind', itself from 'spell' (magic charm) + 'bind'.
