Eschscholtzia

1. NOUN

/ ɛʃˈʃɔltziə / (AmE), / ɛʃˈʃɒltsɪə / (BrE)
a genus of showy flowering herbs native to western North America, especially known for the California poppy
genus of western North American herbs

Examples

The garden was filled with vibrant Eschscholtzia blooming in early summer.
Botanists often refer to the California poppy as Eschscholtzia in scientific literature.
Many wildflower enthusiasts plant Eschscholtzia for their bright and cheerful colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: California poppy - a species of Eschscholtzia with bright orange flowers

Etymology

Named after German botanist Johann Friedrich von Eschscholtz (1793–1831).
