Gentiana procera

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛnˈtaɪ.ənə proʊˈsɛrə / (AmE), / dʒɛnˈtʃɪ.ənə prəʊˈsɪərə / (BrE)
A small fringed gentian with blue flowers, native to east central North America.
species of blue-flowered gentian

Examples

The delicate blue flowers of the Gentiana procera bloom in late summer in prairies and meadows.
Botanists discovered a rare patch of Gentiana procera growing near the riverbank.
Gardeners appreciate Gentiana procera for its striking, fringed petals and attractive color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gentian - any of various plants of the genus Gentiana

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Gentiana' and species epithet 'procera' meaning tall or slender.
