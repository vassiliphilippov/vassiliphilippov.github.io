vasopressor

1. NOUN

/ ˌveɪsoʊˈprɛsər / (AmE), / ˌveɪsəʊˈprɛsə / (BrE)
a drug or agent that causes blood vessels to constrict and increases blood pressure, especially used in medical treatment
drug causing blood vessel constriction
medical

Examples

The doctor administered a vasopressor to stabilize the patient's dangerously low blood pressure.
Use of a vasopressor may be necessary in critical care settings to manage shock.
Common side effects of a vasopressor include headache and increased heart rate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: antihypertensive - a drug used to lower high blood pressure
  • Antonym: vasodilator - a drug causing dilation of blood vessels

Etymology

From Latin 'vas' (vessel) and 'pressor' from Latin 'pressare' (to press).
