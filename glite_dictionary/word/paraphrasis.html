paraphrasis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˈfreɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈfreɪsɪs / (BrE)
The act of rewording or restating something, usually for the purpose of clarification.
rewording for clarification

Examples

The teacher's paraphrasis of the poem helped the students to understand its meaning.
A careful paraphrasis can reveal underlying assumptions in the original statement.
Legal documents often benefit from paraphrasis to make their language clearer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paraphrase - a restatement of a text or passage

Etymology

From Late Latin paraphrasis, from Greek paráphrasis (a paraphrase).
