polysaccharide

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊliˈsækəˌraɪd / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈsækəraɪd / (BrE)
a carbohydrate whose molecules consist of a number of sugar molecules bonded together, forming a complex chain
complex carbohydrate made of sugar chains

Examples

Cellulose is a common polysaccharide found in the walls of plant cells.
Starch is a polysaccharide that stores energy in plants.
Glycogen, a polysaccharide, serves as an energy reserve in animal cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbohydrate - a compound made up of carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen; main types are sugars, starches, and fibers

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'sacchar' (sugar) + '-ide' (a chemical suffix).
