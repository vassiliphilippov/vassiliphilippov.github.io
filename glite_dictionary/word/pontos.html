Pontos

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɒn.toʊs / (AmE), / ˈpɒn.tɒs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the ancient personification of the sea and father of Nereus and several other sea deities.
Greek mythological personification of the sea

Examples

In Hesiod's Theogony, Pontos is described as the progenitor of many sea gods.
The Greeks viewed Pontos as the primeval spirit of the sea, representing its unending vastness.
Mythology students often encounter Pontos when studying the genealogy of Greek deities.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Πόντος (Póntos), meaning 'sea'.
