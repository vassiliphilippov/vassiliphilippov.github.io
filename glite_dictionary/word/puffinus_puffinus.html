Puffinus puffinus

1. NOUN

/ pʌˈfaɪnəs pʌˈfaɪnəs / (AmE), / pʌˈfɪnəs pʌˈfɪnəs / (BrE)
A small black-and-white shearwater, common in the northeastern Atlantic, known as the Manx shearwater.
a North Atlantic shearwater (Manx shearwater)

Examples

During the summer months, Puffinus puffinus breeds in colonies on offshore islands.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of Puffinus puffinus across the Atlantic Ocean.
The distinctive call of Puffinus puffinus can be heard at night near its nesting sites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Manx shearwater - common name for Puffinus puffinus, a North Atlantic seabird

Etymology

From Latin genus and species naming conventions in ornithology.
