Urochordata

1. NOUN

/ ˌjʊroʊˈkɔrdətə / (AmE), / ˌjʊərəʊˈkɔːdətə / (BrE)
A subphylum of marine animals, commonly known as tunicates, characterized by a sac-like body and a tough outer covering.
subphylum of marine animals (tunicates)

Examples

Scientists have discovered several new species within the Urochordata subphylum in the deep sea.
Urochordata are distinguished by their notochord, which is present only during the larval stage.
The study compares the genetic makeup of Urochordata and other chordates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tunicate - marine animal having a saclike body and a tough outer covering

Etymology

From Greek ourá (tail) + khordē (cord), referring to the presence of a notochord in the tail of the larval stage.
