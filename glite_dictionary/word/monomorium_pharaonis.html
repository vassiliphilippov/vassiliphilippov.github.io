Monomorium pharaonis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɒnoʊˈmɔriəm fəˈroʊnɪs / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈmɔːriəm fəˈrəʊnɪs / (BrE)
a small red ant native to warm regions, commonly infesting households as a persistent pest
a small red household pest ant

Examples

The Monomorium pharaonis is notorious for invading kitchens in search of food.
Researchers conducted a study on how to control Monomorium pharaonis infestations in hospitals.
Tiny trails of Monomorium pharaonis were discovered running along the window sill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pharaoh ant - Another name for Monomorium pharaonis, a small household pest ant

Etymology

From New Latin: Genus 'Monomorium', possibly from Greek 'monos' (single) and 'morion' (a kind of helmet); species 'pharaonis' after the Pharaohs of Egypt.
