Coleridgian

1. ADJ

/ ˌkoʊ.ləˈrɪdʒ.i.ən / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ.ləˈrɪdʒ.i.ən / (BrE)
Of or relating to Samuel Taylor Coleridge or his writings.
relating to Coleridge or his works

Examples

Her poetry is often described as Coleridgian in its use of dreamlike imagery and mysticism.
The critic noted a Coleridgian quality in the philosophical depth of the novel.
Many scholars debate what truly constitutes a Coleridgian approach to the imagination.

Etymology

From the name Samuel Taylor Coleridge (1772–1834), English poet, with suffix -ian.
