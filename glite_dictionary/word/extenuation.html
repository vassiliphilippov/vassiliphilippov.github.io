extenuation

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˌstɛnjuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˌstɛnjʊˈeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act of making an offense or fault appear less serious or more forgivable
making an offense seem less serious

Examples

The lawyer argued in extenuation of his client's actions, citing extreme stress at work.
There can be no extenuation for such reckless behavior behind the wheel.
Her apology served as a partial extenuation of her earlier outburst.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mitigation - the action of reducing the severity, seriousness, or painfulness of something
  • Antonym: aggravation - the act of making something worse or more serious

Etymology

From Latin 'extenuatio', from 'extenuare' (to make thin, lessen).

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˌstɛnjuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˌstɛnjuˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making a wrongdoing seem less serious by providing partial excuses or mitigating circumstances
mitigation of an offense; partial excuse

Examples

The lawyer argued that there was significant extenuation due to the defendant’s difficult childhood.
Despite the jury considering his apology, they found little extenuation for his actions.
In her letter, she offered some extenuation for missing the crucial meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mitigation - the action of reducing the severity, seriousness, or painfulness of something
  • Antonym: aggravation - the act of making an offense or crime more serious

Etymology

From Latin extenuatio (thinning, diminishing), from extenuare (to thin, diminish).
