plumose

1. ADJ

/ ˈpluːˌmoʊs / (AmE), / ˈpluːməʊs / (BrE)
having feathery or plume-like tufts or appendages; resembling a feather.
having feathery or plume-like tufts

Examples

The fern displayed plumose fronds that swayed gently in the breeze.
Some marine worms have plumose gills that help them filter food from the water.
The artist decorated the mask with plumose adornments to create a dramatic effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feathery - having the appearance or texture of feathers

Etymology

From Latin 'plumosus', from 'pluma' (feather).
