Trionyx

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈɑːnɪks / (AmE), / traɪˈəʊnɪks / (BrE)
A genus of soft-shelled turtles, representing the type genus of the family Trionychidae.
genus of soft-shelled turtles

Examples

The genus Trionyx is known for its exceptionally soft shells compared to other turtles.
Many fossils from the Cretaceous period have been classified within the Trionyx genus.
Researchers studied the unique adaptations of Trionyx species in freshwater habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Trionychidae - family of soft-shelled turtles

Etymology

From Greek tri- (three) + onyx (claw), referring to three claws on each limb.

2. NOUN

/ traɪˈɑːnɪks / (AmE), / traɪˈɒnɪks / (BrE)
A genus of soft-shelled freshwater turtles, the type genus of the family Trionychidae, found in Africa and Asia.
genus of soft-shelled freshwater turtles

Examples

The Trionyx genus includes several species of soft-shelled turtles found in rivers and lakes.
Researchers discovered a new species belonging to the Trionyx genus in Southeast Asia.
The carapace of Trionyx turtles is adapted for a highly aquatic lifestyle.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Trionychidae - family of soft-shelled turtles

Etymology

From Greek 'tri-' (three) + 'onyx' (claw), referring to the three claws on each forelimb.
