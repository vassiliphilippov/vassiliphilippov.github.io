divided

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈvaɪdɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈvaɪdɪd / (BrE)
(of a road or highway) having a central strip or barrier separating lanes for traffic moving in opposite directions.
having lanes separated by a median (of a road)

Examples

The city recently expanded the divided highway to improve traffic flow.
It's much safer to drive on a divided road during rush hour.
A divided boulevard runs through the center of town, separating the traffic directions.

Synset Examples

a divided highway

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undivided - (of a road) without a median strip; all lanes together

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈvaɪdɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈvaɪdɪd / (BrE)
separated into parts, sections, or groups that are no longer together or in agreement.
separated into parts or groups

Examples

The country remained divided after years of political unrest.
The property was divided equally among the four siblings.
Public opinion is sharply divided on the issue of climate change.

Synset Examples

opinions are divided

Related Terms

  • Antonym: united - joined together for a common purpose
  • Synonym: split - divided into parts or pieces

Etymology

From the verb 'divide', from Latin 'dividere' (to force apart, distribute).

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈvaɪdɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈvaɪdɪd / (BrE)
(of something) split or distributed into parts, often in equal portions and typically according to a plan or purpose.
split or distributed into parts

Examples

The money was divided equally among the four children after their grandmother passed away.
After hours of negotiation, the property was divided according to the terms of the settlement.
Each group received a divided share of the supplies, ensuring fairness for everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shared - used or occupied jointly with others
  • Antonym: united - joined together or combined into a single entity
