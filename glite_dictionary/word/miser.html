miser

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈmaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who hoards money and possessions and spends as little as possible, often living in poor conditions as a result
a person who hoards money; stingy

Examples

The old miser refused to spend a single penny, even on necessities.
Rumors about the miser who lived alone in the big house spread quickly through the village.
Though he was wealthy, his miser lifestyle left him with few friends and much loneliness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spendthrift - a person who spends money recklessly
  • Synonym: penny-pincher - a person who is very reluctant to spend money

Etymology

From Latin 'miser' (wretched, unhappy), later used in English for one who hoards wealth.
