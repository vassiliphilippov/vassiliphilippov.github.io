web-footed

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɛb ˌfʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˈwɛb ˌfʊtɪd / (BrE)
(of an animal) having feet with the toes connected by a membrane, used for swimming.
having feet with webbed toes

Examples

The duck is web-footed, which helps it swim efficiently in the water.
Penguins are web-footed birds adapted to life in cold aquatic environments.
Many amphibians and reptiles are web-footed to move easily through their habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: webbed - having toes joined by a membrane

Etymology

Formed from 'web' (membranous tissue) + 'footed' (having feet).
