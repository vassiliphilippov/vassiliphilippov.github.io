bashfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæʃfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈbæʃfʊlnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being shy or easily embarrassed, often due to modesty
shyness or modesty causing embarrassment

Examples

He spoke with such bashfulness that everyone could tell he was uncomfortable in the spotlight.
Her bashfulness was evident whenever she had to introduce herself to new people.
Despite his bashfulness, he managed to make new friends at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shyness - the quality of being shy
  • Antonym: confidence - the quality of being self-assured

Etymology

From 'bashful' + '-ness'. 'Bashful' is from Middle English, possibly related to 'abash' (to be embarrassed), from Old French 'esbahir'.
