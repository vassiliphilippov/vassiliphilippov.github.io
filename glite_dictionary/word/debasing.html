debasing

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈbeɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈbeɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
(of conduct) causing a loss of honor or dignity; degrading or dishonorable.
causing loss of honor; degrading

Examples

The corrupt official's debasing actions shocked the entire community.
She was forced to endure debasing treatment in her workplace, which left her feeling humiliated.
The novel explores the effects of debasing behavior on personal integrity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: degrading - causing a loss of self-respect; humiliating
  • Antonym: ennobling - giving someone a higher moral or social status

Etymology

From the verb "debase," from Old French "debaser" (to lower), from "de-" + "base".
