deadlock

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛdˌlɑk / (AmE), / ˈdɛdˌlɒk / (BrE)
a situation in which progress is impossible because two opposing forces or parties are unable to agree or proceed
a situation where progress is blocked

Examples

The negotiations ended in a deadlock, with neither side willing to compromise.
After hours of debate, the committee remained at a deadlock over the issue.
The two drivers created a deadlock at the intersection, neither willing to back up.

Synset Examples

reached an impasse on the negotiations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stalemate - a situation in which no progress can be made
  • Synonym: impasse - a situation in which progress is not possible

Etymology

From 'dead' + 'lock', referring to a complete standstill as if locked.
