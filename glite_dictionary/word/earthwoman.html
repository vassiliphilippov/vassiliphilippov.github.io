earthwoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrθˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈɜːθˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a female inhabitant of the earth, especially in contrast to beings from other planets or worlds
female inhabitant of earth

Examples

In the alien's report, the earthwoman was described as courageous and resourceful.
Science fiction stories often feature an earthwoman who forms a bond with extraterrestrial visitors.
The earthwoman acted as an ambassador between humanity and the Martians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthman - male inhabitant of the earth

Etymology

Formed from 'earth' + 'woman', modeled after terms like 'earthman'.
