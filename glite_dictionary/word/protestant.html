Protestant

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɑːt̬əstənt / (AmE), / ˈprɒtɪstənt / (BrE)
relating to Protestants or Protestantism, especially in regard to Christian denominations that separated from the Roman Catholic Church during the Reformation
relating to Protestant Christianity

Examples

Many Protestant communities observe different religious holidays from those of Catholic tradition.
The church was founded on Protestant principles after the Reformation.
She attended a Protestant service for the first time last Sunday.

Synset Examples

Protestant churches
a Protestant denomination

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Catholic - of or relating to the Roman Catholic Church

Etymology

From Latin 'protestant-', from 'protestari' (to protest), originally used for those who protested against certain Roman Catholic doctrines in the 16th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈproʊt̬.ɪ.stənt / (AmE), / ˈprɒt.ɪ.stənt / (BrE)
A member or follower of any of the Western Christian churches that are separate from the Roman Catholic Church and follow the principles of the Reformation.
adherent of a Western Christian church (non-Catholic)

Examples

She was raised as a Protestant in a small town in Germany.
The church service was attended by both Protestant and Catholic members.
Many Protestants celebrate Reformation Day as an important religious holiday.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Catholic - a member of the Roman Catholic Church
  • Synonym: Christian - a follower of Christianity

Etymology

From Latin 'protestant-', from 'protestari' (to declare publicly), first used in reference to the 16th-century Reformation.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09698712-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːtəstənt / (AmE), / ˈprɒtɪstənt / (BrE)
All the Protestant churches and denominations considered together as a distinct branch of Christianity.
the Protestant churches collectively

Examples

Many Protestant missions played a significant role in providing education during the colonial period.
There is cooperation among various Protestant churches in humanitarian projects worldwide.
The majority of Northern Ireland's population identifies with the Protestant tradition.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Catholic - the Roman Catholic Church as a branch of Christianity

Etymology

From Latin *protestantem* (declaring publicly), from *protestari* (to protest). Popularized after the 16th-century Reformation.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
