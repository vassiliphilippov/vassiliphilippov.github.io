Schubert

1. PROPN

/ ˈʃuːbɚt / (AmE), / ˈʃuːbɜːt / (BrE)
Franz Schubert (1797–1828), an Austrian composer known for his prolific works for voice and piano, as well as orchestral music.
Austrian composer (1797–1828)

Examples

Many music lovers admire Schubert for his lyrical melodies and emotional depth.
The concert will feature several pieces by Schubert, including his famous "Ave Maria."
In her thesis, she analyzed the influence of Schubert on Romantic-era composers.

Etymology

From the German surname Schubert, most famously associated with Franz Schubert, the composer.
