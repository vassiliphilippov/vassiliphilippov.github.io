Pluviôse

1. NOUN

/ pluːˈvjoʊs / (AmE), / pluːˈvjəʊz / (BrE)
the fifth month of the French Revolutionary calendar, corresponding roughly to January and February, known as the rainy month
fifth month in French Revolutionary calendar

Examples

The historical letter was dated the 2nd of Pluviôse, Year IV of the Revolution.
During Pluviôse, frequent rains made travel across the countryside difficult.
Events of Pluviôse often appear in accounts of the French Revolution.

Etymology

From French 'Pluviôse', from Latin 'pluviosus' (rainy), referring to wet weather during this period.
