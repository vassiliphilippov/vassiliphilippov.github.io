metal

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛt̬əl / (AmE), / ˈmɛtəl / (BrE)
a chemical element that is typically a shiny, solid material, conducts heat and electricity, and can be shaped or formed into sheets or wires
a shiny, conductive chemical element

Examples

Gold is a precious metal valued for its rarity and beauty.
Aluminum is a lightweight metal used in making aircraft and cans.
Copper is a good conductor, making it an important metal for electrical wiring.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonmetal - an element that does not have the characteristic properties of metals

Etymology

From Latin 'metallum' (metal, mine), from Greek 'metallon'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛtəl / (AmE), / ˈmɛtəl / (BrE)
a substance formed by mixing two or more metallic elements, or metallic and nonmetallic elements, usually fused together while molten, such as an alloy
substance made by mixing metals; alloy

Examples

Brass is a metal composed of copper and zinc.
The statue was cast from a metal that resists rust and corrosion.
Many household items are made from metal alloys for added strength.

Synset Examples

brass is an alloy of zinc and copper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alloy - a metal made by combining two or more metallic elements

Etymology

From Latin metallum (mine, metal), via Old French metal.
