dirndl

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪrndəl / (AmE), / ˈdɜːndl̩ / (BrE)
A traditional women's dress with a tight-fitting bodice and full skirt, originally worn in Alpine regions of Germany, Austria, and Switzerland.
traditional Alpine dress with bodice and full skirt

Examples

She wore a dirndl to the Oktoberfest celebration.
The museum displayed a collection of traditional dirndls from the 19th century.
Many women choose a colorful dirndl for folk festivals and parades.

Etymology

From German 'Dirndl', literally meaning 'girl', later referring to the dress style.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɜrndl̩ / (AmE), / ˈdɜːndl̩ / (BrE)
a woman's skirt or dress with a tight bodice and a full skirt gathered at the waist, originally worn in Alpine regions
full-gathered skirt or traditional Alpine dress

Examples

She wore a brightly colored dirndl to the Oktoberfest celebration.
The museum displayed an authentic 19th-century Bavarian dirndl.
Tourists often buy a dirndl as a souvenir from their trip to Austria.

Etymology

From German 'Dirndl', originally meaning 'girl' or 'young woman'.
