miserable

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɪz.ɚ.əbəl / (AmE), / ˈmɪz.ər.ə.bəl / (BrE)
Deserving of contempt; mean, wretched, or despicable.
deserving contempt; despicable

Examples

He gave a miserable excuse for his rude behavior, which no one believed.
Her miserable betrayal of their trust shocked everyone at the meeting.
Only a miserable person would take advantage of someone in trouble.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contemptible - deserving contempt; despicable
  • Synonym: despicable - deserving to be despised; morally reprehensible
  • Antonym: honorable - deserving respect or high regard

Etymology

From Latin miserabilis (pitiable, wretched), from miserari (to pity), from miser (wretched).
