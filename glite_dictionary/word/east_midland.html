East Midland

1. NOUN

/ ist ˈmɪdlənd / (AmE), / iːst ˈmɪdlənd / (BrE)
the dialect of Middle English, originating in the East Midlands of England, which replaced West Saxon as the literary language and later developed into Modern English.
historical Middle English dialect

Examples

Linguists study the East Midland dialect for its influence on the development of Modern English.
The rise of the East Midland as the literary standard marked a major turning point in English history.
Chaucer's works are often cited as prominent examples of East Midland Middle English.

Etymology

From the geographic region 'East Midlands' in England; used to refer to the dialect spoken there during the Middle English period.
