Batoidei

1. NOUN

/ ˌbeɪtoʊˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌbeɪtəʊˈiːdaɪ / (BrE)
A superorder of cartilaginous fish with flattened bodies, including rays, skates, guitarfishes, and sawfishes.
superorder of cartilaginous fish (rays, skates)

Examples

The Batoidei are known for their distinctive flattened bodies and broad pectoral fins.
Marine biologists study the diversity of species within the Batoidei superorder.
Unlike sharks, most Batoidei species have mouths located on the underside of their bodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ray - a type of cartilaginous fish with broad, flat bodies; member of Batoidei
  • Synonym: skate - a type of cartilaginous fish related to rays; member of Batoidei

Etymology

From New Latin *Batoidei*, based on *Batos* (a genus of rays) and the suffix *-idei*, used in zoological taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ ˌbeɪtoʊˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌbætɔɪˈdiː / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of fishes with flattened bodies including rays, skates, guitarfishes, and sawfishes.
order of flattened fishes: rays, skates

Examples

The Batoidei include a wide variety of species, such as stingrays and sawfishes.
Researchers discovered a new member of the Batoidei near the coastal reef.
Unlike sharks, most Batoidei have large, wing-like pectoral fins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ray - a type of cartilaginous fish with a flattened body
  • Synonym: skate - a flat-bodied cartilaginous fish related to rays

Etymology

From Greek 'batis' (a type of ray) plus scientific Latin '-oidei' (resembling).
