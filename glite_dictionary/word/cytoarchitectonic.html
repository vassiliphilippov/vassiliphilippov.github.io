cytoarchitectonic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsaɪtoʊˌɑrkɪtɛkˈtɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌsaɪtəʊˌɑːkɪtɛkˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to the cellular structure or arrangement of a tissue, especially in the brain or nervous system
relating to tissue cell structure
medical

Examples

The cytoarchitectonic differences between brain regions help scientists identify functional areas.
Recent studies have mapped the cytoarchitectonic organization of the cerebral cortex in great detail.
Researchers use cytoarchitectonic methods to understand how neurons are arranged in the hippocampus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cytoarchitecture - the arrangement of cells in a tissue, especially the brain

Etymology

From Greek 'kytos' (cell) + architectonic (pertaining to structure or architecture).
