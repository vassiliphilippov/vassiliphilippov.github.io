Epilobium angustifolium

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈloʊbiəm ænˌɡʌstəˈfoʊliəm / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈləʊbiəm ænˌɡʌstəˈfəʊliəm / (BrE)
A tall North American perennial plant with narrow leaves and spikes of pinkish-purple flowers, often found in burned or recently cleared areas; also known as fireweed and valued for its nectar.
species of flowering plant, fireweed

Examples

Epilobium angustifolium quickly colonizes open ground after a forest fire, giving rise to large fields of pink blossoms.
Beekeepers prize Epilobium angustifolium for its rich nectar, making it an important honey plant.
Hikers in North America often see Epilobium angustifolium thriving in areas recovering from wildfires.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Epilobium' meaning 'upon a pod', 'angustifolium' meaning 'narrow-leaved'.
