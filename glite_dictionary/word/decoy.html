decoy

1. VERB

/ dɪˈkɔɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈkɔɪ / (BrE)
to lure or entrap someone or something by using a decoy or by means of deception
lure or entrap using a decoy

Examples

The police used an undercover officer to decoy the suspects into their trap.
Poachers sometimes decoy wild animals with recorded calls.
The thieves attempted to decoy the guard away from the safe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to entrap - to catch or trick into a trap
  • Synonym: lure - to attract, especially by trickery or temptation

Etymology

From Dutch 'de kooi' (the cage, the decoy), influencing English noun and verb forms.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdiˌkɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈdiː.kɔɪ / (BrE)
a person who entices someone into danger or a trap, usually as part of a plan or plot
person who lures into danger or trap

Examples

The undercover agent acted as a decoy to draw out the suspects.
She suspected that the invitation was just a decoy to get her alone.
The spy used a decoy to distract the guards while he entered the building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lure - something that tempts or attracts with the promise of reward
  • Synonym: bait - a person or thing used to lure someone into danger or a trap
  • Antonym: protector - a person who keeps someone from harm or danger

Etymology

From Dutch 'de kooi' (the cage), originally referring to a cage for trapping birds.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈkɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈdiː.kɔɪ / (BrE)
An object or device used to attract animals, especially birds or fish, into a trap or into danger.
object used to lure animals into danger

Examples

Hunters placed a wooden decoy on the pond to attract ducks.
The fisherman used a brightly colored decoy to lure the fish closer.
Several decoys were set up in the field to draw in wild geese.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lure - something that attracts, usually for trapping or catching
  • Synonym: bait - food or item used to entice animals or fish

Etymology

From Dutch 'de kooi' (the cage, the trap).
