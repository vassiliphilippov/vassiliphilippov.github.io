Chloranthaceae

1. NOUN

/ kloʊˌrænˈθeɪsiː / (AmE), / kləʊˌrænˈθiːsɪiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical herbs, shrubs, and trees found mainly in the Old World tropics, characterized by simple, often aromatic leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers.
family of tropical herbs, shrubs, and trees

Examples

The Chloranthaceae family includes a variety of aromatic tropical shrubs and trees.
Botanists have studied the evolutionary history of Chloranthaceae to understand flowering plant diversity.
Many members of the Chloranthaceae are valued in traditional medicine across Asia.

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from Greek *chloros* (green) + *anthos* (flower), referring to the type genus Chloranthus.

2. NOUN

/ kloʊˌrænˈθeɪsiː / (AmE), / klɒˌranˈθeɪsiː / (BrE)
a small family of tropical herbs, shrubs, and trees, found mostly in the Southern Hemisphere, with simple leaves and inconspicuous flowers
family of tropical flowering plants

Examples

The Chloranthaceae family includes several species native to tropical Asia and South America.
Botanists study Chloranthaceae to understand the evolution of flowering plants.
The simple leaves and small flowers are characteristic of Chloranthaceae.

Etymology

From Greek *chloros* (green) + *anthos* (flower), referring to the greenish flowers of the family.
