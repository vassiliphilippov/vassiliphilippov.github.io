capitalist roadism

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæpɪtl̩ɪst ˈroʊdɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəlɪst ˈrəʊdɪzəm / (BrE)
an economic policy or ideology that favors the adoption of capitalist principles and prioritizes economic growth over social equality, often used in discussions of socialist or communist countries adopting market reforms.
favoring capitalism over social equality

Examples

The government's shift toward capitalist roadism sparked heated debates among party members.
Critics argue that capitalist roadism undermines social equality in favor of rapid economic development.
During the reforms, leaders accused some officials of promoting capitalist roadism within the party.

Synset Examples

Many criticized the shift towards capitalist roadism in the government.
Capitalist roadism emphasizes economic growth over social equality.

Etymology

Formed from 'capitalist' and 'road', with '-ism'; originated in Chinese political discourse (资本主义道路) to criticize those seen as deviating from socialism.
