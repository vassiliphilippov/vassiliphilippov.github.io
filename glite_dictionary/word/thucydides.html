Thucydides

1. PROPN

/ θuːˈsɪdɪdiːz / (AmE), / θjuːˈsɪdɪdiːz / (BrE)
(c. 460–c. 395 BC) An ancient Greek historian known for his authoritative account of the Peloponnesian War.
ancient Greek historian

Examples

Thucydides is often regarded as the father of scientific history due to his critical approach.
In his works, Thucydides documented the conflict between Athens and Sparta.
Many scholars study Thucydides to gain insights into the nature of power and politics.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Θουκυδίδης (Thoukydídēs).
