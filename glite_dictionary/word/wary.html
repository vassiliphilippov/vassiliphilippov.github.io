wary

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɛri / (AmE), / ˈweəri / (BrE)
careful or cautious, especially because you think there may be danger or trouble; openly distrustful and unwilling to confide
cautious and distrustful

Examples

She was wary of strangers and rarely spoke about her private life.
Travelers should remain wary when walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.
He gave a wary glance at the offer, not sure if it was entirely honest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cautious - careful to avoid potential problems or dangers
  • Antonym: trusting - tending to believe that other people are good and honest

Etymology

From Latin *varius* (variegated, shifting), via Old North French *warer* (to beware).

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɛri / (AmE), / ˈweəri / (BrE)
careful because you do not completely trust someone or something, or are unsure about what might happen
cautious and watchful

Examples

She remained wary of making promises she could not keep.
Travelers should be wary of pickpockets in busy tourist areas.
He cast a wary glance at the unfamiliar dog before approaching.

Synset Examples

they were wary in their movements
a wary glance at the black clouds
taught to be wary of strangers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cautious - being careful to avoid danger or mistakes
  • Synonym: careful - taking care to avoid danger or mistakes
  • Antonym: reckless - doing something dangerous without thinking about the risks

Etymology

From Latin 'vigil' (watchful), through Old English and Middle English forms.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
