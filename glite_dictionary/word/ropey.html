ropey

1. ADJ

/ ˈroʊpi / (AmE), / ˈrəʊpi / (BrE)
Resembling rope in being long, thick, and strong, especially in appearance or texture.
resembling rope; long and strong

Examples

The tree's ropey roots stretched across the surface of the ground.
He admired the ropey muscles of the athletes as they trained.
The old towels had become ropey and frayed at the edges.

Etymology

From rope + -y (adjective forming suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈroʊpi / (AmE), / ˈrəʊpi / (BrE)
(of a liquid or substance) forming viscous or glutinous threads.
forming viscous or thread-like filaments

Examples

The cheese had turned ropey after being left out overnight.
Some bacteria can cause a milk sample to become ropey as it spoils.
The scientist described the texture of the slime as unusually ropey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viscous - having a thick, sticky consistency

3. ADJ

/ ˈroʊpi / (AmE), / ˈrəʊpi / (BrE)
(British informal) Of very poor quality; unsatisfactory or second-rate.
very poor in quality (British, informal)
informal british

Examples

The team's ropey defense cost them the game.
Sorry about the ropey coffee, it's all I had left in the cupboard.
Her voice was a bit ropey during the performance, likely because she had a cold.
