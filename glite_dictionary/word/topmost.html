topmost

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɑpˌmoʊst / (AmE), / ˈtɒpməʊst / (BrE)
at or nearest to the top; highest in position
highest or nearest to the top

Examples

He placed the vase on the topmost shelf to prevent it from breaking.
From the summit, she enjoyed the view from the topmost peak.
The topmost branches of the tree caught the morning sunlight.

Synset Examples

the uppermost book in the pile
on the topmost step

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uppermost - highest in position; nearest the top
  • Antonym: lowermost - at or nearest to the bottom

Etymology

Formed from 'top' + 'most', following common English formation for superlative adjectives.
