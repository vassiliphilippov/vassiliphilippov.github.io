pyrochemistry

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪroʊˈkɛmɪstri / (AmE), / ˌpaɪərəʊˈkɛmɪstri / (BrE)
the branch of chemistry concerned with chemical reactions and processes occurring at high temperatures
high-temperature chemical processes
medical

Examples

Pyrochemistry plays a critical role in the recycling of spent nuclear fuel.
The laboratory specializes in advanced pyrochemistry techniques to produce rare metals.
Understanding pyrochemistry is essential for scientists working with high-temperature industrial processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermochemistry - the study of the heat involved in chemical reactions

Etymology

From Greek 'pyr' (fire) + 'chemistry'.
