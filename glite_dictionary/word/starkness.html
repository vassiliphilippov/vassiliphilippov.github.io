starkness

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑːrknəs / (AmE), / ˈstɑːknəs / (BrE)
the quality of being complete, absolute, or extreme
quality of being absolute or extreme

Examples

The starkness of the landscape made it both beautiful and intimidating.
Critics noted the starkness of the film’s depiction of poverty.
There was a certain starkness in her words that left no room for doubt.

Synset Examples

the starkness of his contrast between justice and fairness was open to many objections

Related Terms

  • Synonym: severity - the quality of being very harsh or strict
  • Antonym: gentleness - the quality of being mild or tender

Etymology

From 'stark' + '-ness', where 'stark' comes from Old English 'stearc' (stiff, severe).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɑrknɪs / (AmE), / ˈstɑːknɪs / (BrE)
the quality of having an extreme lack of furnishings, decoration, or ornamentation; severe plainness
extreme plainness; lack of decoration

Examples

The starkness of the empty room made it feel cold and unwelcoming.
Many modern buildings embrace starkness in their interior design to create a minimalist atmosphere.
She was impressed by the starkness of the mountain landscape, with its bare rocks and sparse vegetation.

Synset Examples

I was struck by the starkness of my father's room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: austerity - plainness and simplicity, especially relating to style and decoration
  • Antonym: ornateness - the quality of being highly decorated or ornamented

Etymology

From 'stark' (Middle English, from Old English 'stearc', meaning 'stiff' or 'severe') + suffix '-ness'.
