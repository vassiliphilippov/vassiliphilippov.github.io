Myxinoidei

1. NOUN

/ mɪkˌsaɪnɔɪˈdiːˌaɪ / (AmE), / mɪkˌsɪnɔɪˈdiː.aɪ / (BrE)
A taxonomic group comprising hagfishes, distinguished from lampreys.
group of jawless fish (hagfishes)

Examples

Myxinoidei are known for their ability to produce copious amounts of slime as a defense mechanism.
The class Myxinoidei includes several species found in deep-sea environments.
Unlike lampreys, members of the Myxinoidei lack a true vertebral column.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Petromyzontidae - family of lampreys, related jawless fishes

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek myxa (slime, mucus) and eidos (form).
