Zostera marina

1. NOUN

/ zoʊˈstɛrə məˈriːnə / (AmE), / zɒsˈtɪərə məˈriːnə / (BrE)
a submerged marine plant with long, narrow leaves, common along North Atlantic coasts; also known as eelgrass
a marine plant, also called eelgrass

Examples

Zostera marina provides a vital habitat for fish and invertebrates in coastal marine environments.
Restoration projects have focused on replanting Zostera marina to improve water quality and biodiversity.
The health of Zostera marina beds is an indicator of overall marine ecosystem health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eelgrass - another name for Zostera marina, the marine plant

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name Zostera from Greek zōstḗr (girdle), and species marina meaning 'of the sea' in Latin.
