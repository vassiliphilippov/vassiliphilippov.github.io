celebrated

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛl.əˌbreɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.ɪˌbreɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
famous and praised for something special or for past achievements; having an illustrious past.
famous; distinguished by past achievements

Examples

The celebrated university has educated many world leaders over the centuries.
She was invited to perform at the celebrated concert hall known for its rich history.
Their celebrated museum attracts visitors from around the world eager to see its famous collections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: famous - well known by many people
  • Synonym: renowned - known or talked about by many people; famous
  • Antonym: obscure - not well known; unknown to most people

Etymology

From Latin 'celebratus', past participle of 'celebrare' (to frequent, honor, celebrate).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɛl.əˌbreɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.ɪˌbreɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
Known and respected by many people; widely recognized and esteemed.
widely known and esteemed

Examples

The celebrated author drew a large crowd at her book signing event.
His paintings became celebrated throughout Europe for their originality and beauty.
She was celebrated not just for her achievements, but also for her generosity and kindness.

Synset Examples

a famous actor
a celebrated musician
a famed scientist
an illustrious judge
a notable historian
a renowned painter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: famous - known about by many people
  • Synonym: renowned - known and respected; famous
  • Antonym: obscure - not well known; difficult to see or understand

Etymology

From Latin 'celebratus', past participle of 'celebrare' (to frequent, honor).
