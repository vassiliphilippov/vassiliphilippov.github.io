precipitin

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈsɪpɪtɪn / (AmE), / prɪˈsɪpɪtɪn / (BrE)
an antibody that forms an insoluble complex and causes its antigen to precipitate out of solution
antibody causing precipitation with antigen
medical

Examples

The laboratory test detected the presence of precipitin in the blood sample.
Scientists studied how each precipitin reacted with various antigens.
A strong precipitin response is essential for some types of allergy testing.

Etymology

From precipitate + -in, modeled after protein names.
