Insessores

1. NOUN

/ ɪn.səˈsɔː.riːz / (AmE), / ɪn.sɪˈsɔː.rɪˌiːz / (BrE)
a formerly used term for birds with feet adapted for perching, such as passerines; this classification is now obsolete in ornithology
obsolete group of perching birds

Examples

In 19th-century ornithology, many songbirds were classified among the Insessores.
The term Insessores was gradually replaced by more precise taxonomic groupings.
Though no longer used scientifically, Insessores appears in historical classifications of birds.

Etymology

From Latin 'insessor', meaning sitter, from 'insidere' (to sit upon).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˌsɛˈsɔːriz / (AmE), / ɪnˌsɛˈsɔːriːz / (BrE)
a former order of birds characterized by feet adapted for perching; the term is now obsolete in taxonomy.
former bird order for perching birds

Examples

In older ornithological texts, many songbirds were classified under the order Insessores.
Taxonomists have largely abandoned the use of Insessores in modern bird classification.
The order Insessores once included a wide variety of perching birds.

Etymology

From Latin *insessor* (one who sits upon), from *insidere* (to sit upon).
