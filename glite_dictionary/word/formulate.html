to formulate

1. VERB

/ ˈfɔrmjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to express or develop (an idea, theory, or hypothesis) carefully and systematically
develop or state an idea systematically

Examples

The scientists worked together to formulate a new theory about the origin of the universe.
She took several days to carefully formulate her proposal before presenting it to management.
It is important to formulate clear objectives before starting any research project.

Synset Examples

Could you develop the ideas in your thesis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devise - to plan or invent by careful thought
  • Synonym: articulate - to express an idea or feeling fluently and coherently

Etymology

From Latin 'formulatus', past participle of 'formulare' (to form, shape).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00927844-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈfɔrmjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to express an idea, opinion, or plan clearly and precisely in words
express clearly in words

Examples

It took her some time to formulate her response during the meeting.
Scientists often formulate hypotheses before beginning their experiments.
He struggled to formulate his feelings in a way that others could understand.

Synset Examples

He formulated his concerns to the board of trustees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: express - to put thoughts or feelings into words
  • Synonym: articulate - to express clearly and effectively in speech or writing

Etymology

From Latin 'formulatus', past participle of 'formulare' (to shape, form), from 'formula'.

3. VERB

/ ˈfɔrmjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to create or devise (an idea, plan, explanation, theory, or principle) through careful thought and effort
devise carefully (idea, plan, etc.)

Examples

After months of research, the scientists were able to formulate a new theory about climate change.
The committee will formulate a plan to improve public transportation in the city.
She struggled to formulate an effective response to the unexpected question.

Synset Examples

excogitate a way to measure the speed of light

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devise - to plan or invent by careful thought
  • Synonym: to conceive - to form an idea or plan in the mind

Etymology

From Latin formulare (to make according to a formula), from formula (small form, rule).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01635953-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈfɔrmjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to prepare or create according to a set formula or method
prepare according to a formula

Examples

The scientists will formulate a new chemical compound based on established procedures.
To ensure consistency, pharmacists formulate the medicine exactly as prescribed by the standard recipe.
She was tasked to formulate the new cleaning solution in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devise - to invent or plan something carefully
  • Antonym: to improvise - to create spontaneously without preparation

Etymology

From Latin 'formulatus', past participle of 'formulare' (to form according to a formula).
