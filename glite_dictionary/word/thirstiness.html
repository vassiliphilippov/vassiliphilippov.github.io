thirstiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɜrsti.nəs / (AmE), / ˈθɜːsti.nəs / (BrE)
a strong desire or craving for something, especially something intangible such as knowledge or success
intense desire for something

Examples

Her thirstiness for adventure led her to travel the world alone.
The thirstiness for recognition can sometimes overshadow genuine passion.
His thirstiness to learn new languages impressed all his teachers.

Synset Examples

a thirst for knowledge
hunger for affection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hunger - a strong desire or craving for something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04952431-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈθɜrstinəs / (AmE), / ˈθɜːstinəs / (BrE)
the state of feeling a need or desire to drink; the condition of being thirsty
the state of needing to drink

Examples

After her long run, her thirstiness was obvious as she reached for a bottle of water.
Prolonged thirstiness can be a symptom of certain medical conditions.
The intense heat increased their thirstiness throughout the afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thirst - the sensation of needing to drink

Etymology

Formed from 'thirst' + '-iness', following standard English noun formation.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14064142-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈθɜrstinɪs / (AmE), / ˈθɜːstinɪs / (BrE)
a condition of dryness due to a lack of moisture, especially resulting from a long-term absence of rainfall
dryness; lack of moisture (esp. from drought)

Examples

The thirstiness of the region makes farming nearly impossible without irrigation.
After months without rain, the thirstiness of the soil became apparent.
Deserts are defined by their extreme thirstiness and sparse vegetation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aridity - extreme dryness, especially of climate

Etymology

Formed from 'thirst' + suffix '-iness', patterning after 'thirsty'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14560852-n
Synset POS: n
