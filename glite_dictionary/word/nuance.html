nuance

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːˌɑns / (AmE), / ˈnjuːɑːns / (BrE)
a subtle difference in or shade of meaning, expression, or feeling
a subtle distinction or variation

Examples

She listened carefully to every nuance in his voice before responding.
Good writers are attentive to the nuances of language to express their ideas precisely.
His analysis misses the nuance that distinguishes genuine concern from mere politeness.

Synset Examples

without understanding the finer nuances you can't enjoy the humor
don't argue about shades of meaning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distinction - a difference or contrast between similar things or people

Etymology

From French 'nuance', literally 'shade' or 'gradation', from 'nuer' (to shade), from Latin 'nubes' (cloud).
