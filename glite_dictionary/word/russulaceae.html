Russulaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌrʌs.jʊˈleɪ.si.i / (AmE), / ˌrʌs.jʊˈleɪ.sɪ.iː / (BrE)
a family of fungi that includes the genera Russula and Lactarius, known for their gilled mushrooms
family of gilled fungi (Russula, Lactarius)

Examples

The Russulaceae family contains many edible and inedible mushroom species.
Researchers studied the diversity of Russulaceae in temperate forests.
Many brightly colored mushrooms in the woods belong to the Russulaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Russula - a genus of gilled mushrooms in the Russulaceae family

Etymology

From the type genus Russula and the standard botanical family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ ˌrʌsjuˈleɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌrʌsjuːˈleɪsɪiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi including the genus Russula, characterized by their brittle gills and often brightly colored caps
a family of fungi (includes Russula)

Examples

The Russulaceae are commonly found in temperate forest ecosystems around the world.
Some edible mushrooms belong to the Russulaceae family, notably those in the Russula genus.
Researchers study the Russulaceae to understand their ecological role and evolutionary relationships among fungi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Russula - genus of mushrooms in the family Russulaceae

Etymology

From the genus name Russula, from Latin 'russulus' (reddish), with the botanical suffix -aceae denoting a family.
