mucopolysaccharidosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmjuːkoʊˌpɑːliˌsækəˈraɪdoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmjuːkəʊˌpɒliˌsækəˈraɪdəsɪs / (BrE)
Any of a group of inherited disorders affecting the metabolism of mucopolysaccharides, leading to their accumulation in tissues.
genetic disorder affecting mucopolysaccharide metabolism
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that mucopolysaccharidosis is caused by genetic enzyme deficiencies.
Treatment for mucopolysaccharidosis often involves enzyme replacement therapy.
Early diagnosis of mucopolysaccharidosis can improve the quality of life for affected children.

Etymology

From Greek 'mukos' (mucus), 'poly' (many), 'sacchar' (sugar), with medical suffix '-osis' (condition).
