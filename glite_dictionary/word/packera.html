Packera

1. NOUN

/ pəˈkɛrə / (AmE), / pəˈkɛərə / (BrE)
A genus of American and East Asian perennial herbs with yellow, orange, or red flower rays; sometimes included in the genus Senecio.
genus of flowering herbs

Examples

The botanist discovered a new species of Packera thriving in the mountain meadow.
Many Packera plants display bright yellow flowers in the spring.
The genus Packera is closely related to the more widespread Senecio.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈkɛɹə / (AmE), / pəˈkɛərə / (BrE)
A genus of American and East Asian perennial herbs in the daisy family, with yellow, orange, or red flower rays; sometimes classified under the genus Senecio.
a genus of perennial herbs with yellow, orange, or red flowers

Examples

Botanists recently reclassified several species into the genus Packera from Senecio.
Many members of Packera have bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.
The endangered Packera layneae is native to parts of California.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Senecio - a large genus of flowering plants sometimes including Packera

Etymology

Named after botanist John G. Packer.
