preventative

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈvɛntətɪv / (AmE), / prɪˈvɛntətɪv / (BrE)
A substance, device, or measure that is intended to prevent pregnancy; a contraceptive.
substance or device to prevent pregnancy

Examples

The clinic offers a range of preventatives to those seeking birth control options.
Using a preventative can significantly reduce the risk of unintended pregnancy.
She discussed different types of preventative methods with her doctor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contraceptive - substance or device used to prevent pregnancy

Etymology

From 'prevent' + '-ative', modeled after 'contraceptive'.

2. ADJ

/ prɪˈvɛntətɪv / (AmE), / prɪˈvɛntətɪv / (BrE)
Intended to stop something undesirable from happening; serving to prevent or hinder.
serving to prevent or hinder

Examples

Regular exercise can be a preventative measure against many health problems.
The clinic offers preventative services such as vaccinations and screenings.
Wearing sunscreen is a preventative step to avoid sunburn and skin damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preventive - serving to prevent or hinder

Etymology

Formed from 'prevent' plus the -ative suffix, modeled on words like 'preventive'.

4. NOUN

/ prɪˈvɛntətɪv / (AmE), / prɪˈvɛntətɪv / (BrE)
a medicine or treatment designed to prevent disease or slow its progress
remedy to prevent or slow disease

Examples

Doctors often prescribe preventatives to patients during flu season.
Wearing insect repellent is an effective preventative against mosquito-borne illnesses.
The clinic offers several preventative options to help reduce the spread of infection.
