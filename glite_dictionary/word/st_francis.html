St. Francis

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈfræn sɪs / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈfræn sɪs / (BrE)
(also called Francis of Assisi) An Italian Catholic monk (1181–1226) who founded the Franciscan order of friars and is revered as a saint.
Italian Catholic saint, founder of Franciscans
american british

Examples

Many churches and schools are named after St. Francis for his dedication to peace and poverty.
As a young man, St. Francis gave up his wealth to serve the poor and sick.
The teachings of St. Francis continue to inspire people around the world.

Etymology

From Latin 'Franciscus', name meaning 'Frenchman'; 'St.' is an abbreviation of 'Saint', from Latin 'sanctus' (holy).
