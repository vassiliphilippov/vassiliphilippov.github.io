Dirca palustris

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɜːrkə pəˈlʌstrɪs / (AmE), / ˈdɜːkə pəˈlʌstrɪs / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, known for its tough, flexible branches, pliable bark, and small yellow flowers.
deciduous North American shrub

Examples

The flexible branches of Dirca palustris have been used for making rope and baskets by indigenous peoples.
In spring, Dirca palustris produces clusters of small, delicate yellow flowers before the leaves emerge.
Dirca palustris, sometimes called leatherwood, thrives in moist, shaded woodlands.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Dirca' (the genus name) and 'palustris' meaning 'of the marsh', referring to its typical habitat.
