Centaurea nigra

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛn.təˈɹiː.ə ˈnaɪ.ɡrə / (AmE), / ˌsɛn.tɔːˈriː.ə ˈnɪɡ.rə / (BrE)
a Eurasian perennial plant with tough wiry stems and purple flower heads, commonly known as black knapweed and naturalized widely outside its native range
a purple-flowered Eurasian perennial plant (black knapweed)

Examples

The meadow was dotted with Centaurea nigra, adding vibrant purple to the summer landscape.
Botanists studied the rapid spread of Centaurea nigra in North America.
Some farmers consider Centaurea nigra to be an invasive weed among their crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black knapweed - common name for Centaurea nigra

Etymology

From Latin: 'Centaurea' (a plant genus) and 'nigra' (black), referring to the plant's dark bracts.
