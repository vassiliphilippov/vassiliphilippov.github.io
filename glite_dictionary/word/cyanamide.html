cyanamide

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈænəˌmaɪd / (AmE), / saɪˈanəˌmʌɪd / (BrE)
a weak, soluble dibasic acid (HN=C=NH) used especially in the manufacture of fertilizers and other chemicals.
a weak, soluble dibasic acid
medical

Examples

Researchers studied the properties of cyanamide in various chemical reactions.
The fertilizer contains cyanamide to improve soil health.
Exposure to cyanamide should be handled carefully in laboratory settings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dicyandiamide - a related chemical compound used in industry

Etymology

From cyano- (referring to the cyanide group) + amide (chemical suffix).

2. NOUN

/ saɪˈæn.əˌmaɪd / (AmE), / saɪˈæn.əˌmaɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound, NH2CN, used as a fertilizer and as a source of nitrogen compounds in various industrial processes
nitrogen compound used in fertilizers

Examples

Farmers apply cyanamide to their fields to enrich the soil with nitrogen.
Proper handling of cyanamide is important due to its reactive nature in industrial settings.
The company increased production of cyanamide to meet the demand for agricultural fertilizers.

Etymology

From 'cyano-' (referring to cyanogen group) and 'amide' (chemical suffix).
