abjuration

1. NOUN

/ ˌæb.dʒəˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæb.dʒʊəˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the formal act of renouncing, rejecting, or disavowing a belief, claim, or previous assertion
formal renunciation or disavowal

Examples

After much reflection, he made a public abjuration of his earlier controversial remarks.
In medieval times, an abjuration of heresy was often required by the church.
The senator's abjuration was seen as a significant reversal of his previous position.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: renunciation - the formal rejection of something, especially a belief or claim
  • Synonym: retraction - the act of withdrawing a statement or belief

Etymology

From Latin abjuratio (a swearing away), from abjurare (to swear away).
