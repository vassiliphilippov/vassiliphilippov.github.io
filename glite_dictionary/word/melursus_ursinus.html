Melursus ursinus

1. NOUN

/ mɛˈlɜːrsəs ʊrˈsaɪnəs / (AmE), / mɛlˈɜːsəs ʊəˈsaɪnəs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the sloth bear, a coarse-haired, long-snouted bear native to south-central Asia
scientific name for the sloth bear

Examples

Researchers observed a Melursus ursinus foraging for termites in the Indian forest.
The Melursus ursinus is known for its distinctive shaggy coat and white chest mark.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the habitat of Melursus ursinus in Sri Lanka.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sloth bear - a coarse-haired, long-snouted bear of South Asia; common name for Melursus ursinus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Melursus' from 'mel' (honey) + 'ursus' (bear), 'ursinus' meaning 'bear-like'.
