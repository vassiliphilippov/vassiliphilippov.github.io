doorway

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔrˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈdɔːweɪ / (BrE)
the space in a wall that forms an entrance to a room or building, usually where a door is or can be fitted
entrance space in a wall for a door

Examples

She stood in the doorway and watched the rain fall outside the house.
He hurried to the doorway when he heard someone knocking.
The cat sat quietly in the doorway, waiting to be let out.

Synset Examples

he stuck his head in the doorway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entrance - an opening or passage for entering a place
  • Antonym: exit - a way out, especially for leaving a room or building

Etymology

From 'door' + 'way', combining the Old English 'duru' (door) and 'weg' (way, path).
