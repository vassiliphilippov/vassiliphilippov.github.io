lusterlessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌstərˌlɛsnəs / (AmE), / ˈlʌstəlɪsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of lacking shine, gloss, or highlights; dullness
lack of shine or gloss; dullness

Examples

The lusterlessness of the old painting made it appear faded and less attractive.
Jewelry experts can easily detect lusterlessness in imitation gemstones.
The fabric's lusterlessness made it suitable for more understated, casual wear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dullness - the quality of lacking brightness, vividness, or sheen
  • Antonym: luster - a gentle sheen or soft glow; brightness or radiance

Etymology

From 'lusterless' (without shine) + '-ness' (state or quality).
