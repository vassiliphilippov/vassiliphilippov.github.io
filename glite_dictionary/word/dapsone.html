dapsone

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæpˌsoʊn / (AmE), / ˈdæp.səʊn / (BrE)
an antibacterial drug used chiefly to treat leprosy and certain skin diseases such as dermatitis herpetiformis
antibacterial drug for leprosy and skin diseases
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed dapsone to manage her chronic skin condition.
Patients with leprosy often respond well to treatment with dapsone.
Long-term use of dapsone may require regular blood tests to monitor side effects.

Etymology

From chemical name: diaminodiphenylsulfone (DA + PS + ONE, from sulfone).
