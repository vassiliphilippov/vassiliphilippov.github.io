kencur

1. NOUN

/ kɛnˈtʃʊr / (AmE), / kɛnˈkʊə / (BrE)
a dried rhizome or its powdered form from the plant Kaempferia galanga, used as a spice in Indonesian cooking
Indonesian spice; Kaempferia galanga rhizome

Examples

The recipe calls for a tablespoon of kencur to enhance the flavor.
She bought some fresh kencur from the local Asian market.
Ground kencur is often used in traditional Indonesian herbal drinks.

Etymology

From Indonesian 'kencur', originally from Javanese.
