intentness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛntnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛntnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being intensely focused or concentrated on something.
being intensely focused

Examples

The artist painted with such intentness that he lost track of time.
Her intentness on the crossword puzzle made her unaware of the phone ringing.
He listened to the instructions with great intentness to avoid making mistakes.

Synset Examples

the intentness of his gaze

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concentration - the action of focusing one's attention or mental effort
  • Antonym: inattentiveness - lack of attention; the state of not being attentive

Etymology

From 'intent' + '-ness', forming a noun expressing the state or quality of being intent.
