songful

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɔŋ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈsɒŋ.f(ə)l / (BrE)
full of melody; rich and pleasing in sound, like a song.
richly melodious; full of song

Examples

The songful voice of the nightingale filled the quiet garden.
A songful breeze drifted in through the open windows, softening the mood of the evening.
She listened to the songful chirping of birds as she walked through the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melodious - having a pleasant melody
  • Synonym: tuneful - having a pleasing tune or melody
  • Antonym: discordant - harsh or jarring in sound

Etymology

From 'song' + suffix '-ful', indicating 'full of song'.
