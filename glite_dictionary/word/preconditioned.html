preconditioned

1. ADJ

/ ˌpriːkənˈdɪʃənd / (AmE), / ˌpriːkənˈdɪʃənd / (BrE)
having been prepared or made suitable in advance for a particular purpose or condition
prepared or conditioned in advance

Examples

The wood was preconditioned to ensure better durability under harsh weather.
Athletes often arrive preconditioned to handle the stress of high-level competition.
The equipment is shipped preconditioned and ready for immediate installation.

Synset Examples

a surface preconditioned to receive paint
customers preconditioned to buy a product

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prepared - made ready for use or action
  • Synonym: conditioned - accustomed or adapted to a particular condition

Etymology

From 'precondition' + '-ed', indicating something that has been subjected to preconditioning.
