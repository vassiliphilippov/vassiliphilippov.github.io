lasagna

1. NOUN

/ ləˈzɑnjə / (AmE), / ləˈzan.jə / (BrE)
Pasta in the form of very wide, flat strips, typically baked in layers with sauce, cheese, and other ingredients.
wide, flat pasta strips

Examples

I used fresh lasagna noodles to make tonight's dinner.
He layered the cheese and sauce between sheets of lasagna.
You can make vegetarian lasagna by using grilled vegetables instead of meat.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: fettuccine - narrow, flat strips of pasta
  • Degree Lower: spaghetti - long, thin, cylindrical pasta

Etymology

From Italian 'lasagna', from Latin 'lasanum' meaning 'cooking pot'.

2. NOUN

/ ləˈzɑnjə / (AmE), / ləˈzænjə / (BrE)
An Italian baked dish consisting of layers of flat pasta, sauce, cheese, and usually meat or vegetables.
Italian layered pasta dish

Examples

My grandmother's lasagna is always a favorite at family gatherings.
He ordered a slice of vegetable lasagna at the Italian restaurant.
After baking, the cheesy lasagna filled the kitchen with a delicious aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: casserole - a dish cooked slowly in an oven, often with layers or mixed ingredients

Etymology

From Italian 'lasagna', from Latin 'lasanum' (cooking pot).
