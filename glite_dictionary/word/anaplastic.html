anaplastic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.əˈplæs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌan.əˈplas.tɪk / (BrE)
relating to or showing anaplasia, a condition in which cells lose their normal structure and function, typically seen in malignant tumors.
relating to abnormal, undifferentiated cell growth
medical

Examples

The biopsy revealed anaplastic cells, indicating a more aggressive form of cancer.
Doctors are concerned when a tumor appears anaplastic under the microscope, as it suggests rapid progression.
The patient's prognosis worsened due to the anaplastic nature of the carcinoma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anaplasia - loss of differentiation and structure in cells, especially in tumors

Etymology

From Greek ana- (up, again) + plastos (formed, molded).
