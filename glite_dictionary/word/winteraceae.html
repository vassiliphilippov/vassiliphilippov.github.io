Winteraceae

1. NOUN

/ wɪn.təˈreɪ.si.i / (AmE), / ˌwɪn.tərˈeɪ.si.iː / (BrE)
a small family of mainly tropical shrubs and trees, including genera such as Drimys and Pseudowintera, sometimes classified within Magnoliaceae
family of tropical shrubs and trees

Examples

The Winteraceae are notable for their aromatic bark and evergreen leaves.
Some species within the Winteraceae family are used in traditional medicine.
Researchers discovered several new rainforest species belonging to the Winteraceae.

Etymology

From the genus name Wintera + Latin -aceae, a standard suffix for plant family names.

2. NOUN

/ ˌwɪntəˈreɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌwɪntəˈreɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly tropical shrubs and trees, including the genera Drimys and Pseudowintera, sometimes classified within Magnoliaceae
family of tropical shrubs and trees (botany)

Examples

The Winteraceae are notable for their primitive features among flowering plants.
Several species in the Winteraceae family are used in traditional medicine.
Drimys winteri is a representative shrub of the Winteraceae.

Etymology

From the genus name Wintera, after John Winter, English physician and plant collector.
