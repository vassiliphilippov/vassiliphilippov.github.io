procaryotic

1. ADJ

/ proʊˌkæriˈɑtɪk / (AmE), / prəʊˌkæriˈɒtɪk / (BrE)
(of a cell or organism) lacking membrane-bound nuclei; relating to or characteristic of prokaryotes.
lacking membrane-bound cell nuclei
medical

Examples

Bacteria are procaryotic organisms, meaning their cells do not have nuclei.
Researchers compared the DNA replication processes in procaryotic and eukaryotic cells.
The textbook explains how procaryotic cells differ structurally from eukaryotic cells.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: eukaryotic - having cells with membrane-bound nuclei

Etymology

From Greek 'pro-' (before) and 'karyon' (nut, kernel), referring to the nucleus.
