Cyperus esculentus

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪˈpɪrəs ˌɛskjuːˈlɛntəs / (AmE), / ˌsɪpərəs ˌɛskjʊˈlɛntəs / (BrE)
a sedge plant native to Europe and Africa, bearing small edible tubers known as tiger nuts or chufa
plant with edible nutlike tubers (tiger nuts)

Examples

Cyperus esculentus is cultivated in some regions for its sweet, nutty tubers known as tiger nuts.
Researchers studied the potential health benefits of consuming Cyperus esculentus tubers.
The Mediterranean diet sometimes includes beverages made from Cyperus esculentus, such as horchata de chufa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiger nut - another name for the edible tuber of Cyperus esculentus
  • Synonym: chufa - another common name for the tuber of Cyperus esculentus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cyperus' (from Greek 'kypeiros', a kind of sedge), 'esculentus' meaning 'edible'.
