Varanus niloticus

1. NOUN

/ vəˈrænəs naɪˈlɑtɪkəs / (AmE), / vəˈrɑːnəs nɪˈlɒtɪkəs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the Nile monitor, a large African lizard known for inhabiting rivers and swamps
scientific name for Nile monitor lizard

Examples

The Varanus niloticus is often seen basking on riverbanks in sub-Saharan Africa.
Researchers tracked the movements of several Varanus niloticus to study their migration habits.
The Varanus niloticus is known for preying on crocodile eggs in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nile monitor - large African lizard inhabiting rivers and swamps

Etymology

From Latin: Varanus (monitor lizard genus) and niloticus (of the Nile).
