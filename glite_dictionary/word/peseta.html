peseta

1. NOUN

/ pəˈseɪtə / (AmE), / pəˈseɪtə / (BrE)
the former basic monetary unit of Spain, equal to 100 centimos, replaced by the euro in 2002
former currency of Spain

Examples

Tourists often exchanged dollars for pesetas before traveling to Spain.
The museum displays coins from the peseta era alongside old Spanish banknotes.
After 2002, the peseta ceased to be accepted as legal tender.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: euro - the current official currency of Spain and many other European countries

Etymology

From Spanish 'peseta', diminutive of 'peso' (weight, coin).
