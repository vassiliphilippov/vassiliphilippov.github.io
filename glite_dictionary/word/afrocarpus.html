Afrocarpus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæfroʊˈkɑrpəs / (AmE), / ˌafrəʊˈkɑːpəs / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen trees or shrubs native to equatorial, southern, and southeastern Africa, known as yellowwood and similar to the genus Podocarpus.
genus of evergreen African trees/shrubs

Examples

The majestic Afrocarpus trees dominate the highland forests of southern Africa.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Afrocarpus genus.
Timber from Afrocarpus is highly valued for its durability and attractive appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Podocarpus - a related genus of evergreen trees and shrubs

Etymology

From Latinized Greek 'afro-' (Africa) and 'carpus' (fruit).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæfroʊˈkɑːrpəs / (AmE), / ˌaf.rəʊˈkɑː.pəs / (BrE)
a genus of evergreen, dioecious trees or shrubs native to equatorial, southern, and southeastern Africa, known as yellowwoods, resembling members of the genus Podocarpus
genus of African evergreen trees or shrubs

Examples

The rare Afrocarpus trees are prized for their durable yellowwood timber.
Many botanical gardens cultivate Afrocarpus for conservation and research purposes.
The Afrocarpus species thrive in the montane forests of southern Africa.

Etymology

From Latin 'Afro-' (Africa) + 'carpus' (fruit), indicating African fruit-bearing plants.
