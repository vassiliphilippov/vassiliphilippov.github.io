Alexander

1. PROPN

/ ˌælɪɡˈzændər / (AmE), / ˌælɪɡˈzændə / (BrE)
(Alexander the Great) King of Macedon (356–323 BC), who conquered Greece, Egypt, and Persia and founded Alexandria.
king of Macedon; famous conqueror

Examples

Alexander led his armies across Persia and into India, creating one of the largest empires of the ancient world.
Historians often study the military tactics of Alexander to understand his unprecedented success.
The city of Alexandria in Egypt was named after Alexander following his conquest.

Etymology

From Greek Alexandros, meaning 'defender of men'.
