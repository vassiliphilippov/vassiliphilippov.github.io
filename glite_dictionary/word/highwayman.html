highwayman

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌweɪmən / (AmE), / ˈhaɪweɪmən / (BrE)
(historical) A man, typically on horseback, who used to rob travelers on public roads.
robber who attacked travelers on roads

Examples

The notorious highwayman robbed coaches traveling along the countryside roads.
Legends tell of a highwayman who would appear at dusk, demanding travelers’ valuables.
Dressed as a gentleman, the highwayman became a romantic figure in old English ballads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bandit - an outlaw who robs people, especially in a gang
  • Synonym: robber - a person who steals from another, especially using force or the threat of force
  • Antonym: policeman - a member of a police force (opposite in role to 'highwayman')

Etymology

From 'highway' (a main road) + 'man'; originally referring to robbers who operated on highways in the 17th–18th centuries.
