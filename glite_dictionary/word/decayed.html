decayed

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈkeɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈkeɪd / (BrE)
having been damaged or made unsound as a result of decay; rotten or decomposed.
damaged or rotten from decay

Examples

The old tree had a decayed trunk that was hollow inside.
We discovered several decayed beams in the roof structure during the renovation.
The doctor warned that decayed teeth could cause serious infections if left untreated.

Synset Examples

rotten floor boards
rotted beams
a decayed foundation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rotten - suffering from decay; decomposed
  • Antonym: sound - in good condition; not damaged or decayed

Etymology

Past participle of 'decay,' from Old French 'decaïr', from Latin 'decadere' (to fall down or away).
