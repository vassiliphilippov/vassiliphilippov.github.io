sampler

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæmplər / (AmE), / ˈsɑːmplə / (BrE)
a person who tastes food or drink in order to judge its quality
person who tastes for quality

Examples

The company employs a sampler to ensure every batch of chocolate meets their standards.
As a professional sampler, she has developed a refined palate for wine.
Before the new menu launched, a sampler evaluated every dish for flavor and texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taster - person who tastes food or drink to judge quality

Etymology

From Middle English 'sampler' via Old French 'essampler', from Latin 'exemplum' (example, pattern).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsæmplɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɑːmplə / (BrE)
a device or station used to collect samples or make measurements for analysis, especially in scientific or environmental studies
device or station for collecting samples

Examples

The air quality sampler was installed on the rooftop to monitor pollution levels.
Researchers visited each site to maintain the water sampler and collect data.
The laboratory uses an automated sampler to gather soil samples at regular intervals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sample - a small part or quantity intended to show what the whole is like

Etymology

From Middle English 'sampler', from Anglo-Norman French 'essamplere', meaning 'an example, pattern'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsæmplɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɑːmplə / (BrE)
a collection or assortment of different samples, especially of products, food, or artistic works, offered for testing or selection
an assortment of different samples

Examples

The bakery offered a sampler of their most popular cookies to new customers.
She ordered a cheese sampler to share with the table.
The exhibition included a sampler of emerging artists' work.

Synset Examples

a candy sampler
a sampler of French poets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sample - a specimen or small part of something intended to show the quality or character of the whole

Etymology

From Middle English, one who samples, from Old French 'essampler', from 'essample' (example, sample).

4. NOUN

/ ˈsæmplər / (AmE), / ˈsɑːmplə / (BrE)
a piece of embroidery created as a demonstration or test of skill in various stitches, often as a decorative record
embroidery displaying various stitches

Examples

My grandmother's sampler from 1920 hangs in the hallway as a cherished family heirloom.
She stitched alphabets and patterns onto the sampler to practice new embroidery techniques.
The museum's collection includes an 18th-century sampler made by a young girl learning needlework.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embroidery - the art or process of decorating fabric with needlework

Etymology

From Middle English *sampler*, from Anglo-Norman *essamplere* (example, pattern), from Latin *exemplum* (example).
