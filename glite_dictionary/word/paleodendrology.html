paleodendrology

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪlioʊdɛnˈdrɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌpeɪliəʊdɛnˈdrɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of fossilized trees, especially as a branch of paleobotany
study of fossil trees

Examples

She specialized in paleodendrology, focusing her research on ancient forest ecosystems.
Groundbreaking discoveries in paleodendrology have deepened our understanding of prehistoric climates.
The university offers graduate courses in paleodendrology for students of paleobotany.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: paleobotany - the branch of botany dealing with fossil plants

Etymology

From Greek 'palaios' (ancient), 'dendron' (tree), and suffix '-logy' (study).
