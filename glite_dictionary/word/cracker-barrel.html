cracker-barrel

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrækərˌbærəl / (AmE), / ˈkrækəˌbærəl / (BrE)
Displaying the simple, informal qualities or rustic wisdom typical of country life.
homespun or rustic in style

Examples

The politician's cracker-barrel humor appealed to the rural crowd.
Her cracker-barrel advice was full of common sense and plain wisdom.
He admired the cracker-barrel philosophy often found in small-town gatherings.

Synset Examples

cracker-barrel philosophy
the air of homespun country boys

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homespun - simple and unsophisticated, characteristic of country people
  • Antonym: sophisticated - having refined or worldly qualities, opposite of rustic or simple

Etymology

From 'cracker barrel', referring to the barrels of soda crackers once found in country stores, where locals would gather and converse.
