butadiene

1. NOUN

/ ˌbjuːtəˈdaɪiːn / (AmE), / ˌbjuːtəˈdʌɪiːn / (BrE)
a colorless gaseous hydrocarbon (C4H6) used chiefly in the manufacture of synthetic rubber
gaseous hydrocarbon used in making rubber

Examples

Many manufacturers rely on butadiene to produce synthetic rubber for tires and other products.
Exposure to butadiene should be minimized due to its potential health effects.
Cracking petroleum is a common industrial process to obtain butadiene.

Etymology

From 'but-' (referring to four carbon atoms, as in butane) and 'diene' (from Greek 'di-' meaning two, and 'ene' indicating double bonds).
