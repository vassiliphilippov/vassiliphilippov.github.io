cocozelle

1. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊkoʊˈzɛl / (AmE), / ˌkəʊkəʊˈzɛli / (BrE)
A variety of summer squash with dark green and light green stripes, closely resembling zucchini.
type of striped summer squash

Examples

The farmer displayed several cocozelle squashes at the market next to the zucchini.
I decided to use cocozelle in my vegetable stir-fry for a change of flavor and texture.
Her garden produced an abundant crop of cocozelle this summer, much to her delight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zucchini - a green summer squash; also called courgette

Etymology

From Italian 'cocozella', diminutive of 'cocozza' (gourd or squash).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊkoʊˈzɛl / (AmE), / ˌkəʊkəʊˈzɛli / (BrE)
A variety of squash plant with dark green fruit marked with light green or yellow mottling.
a variety of mottled green squash

Examples

We harvested several cocozelle from the garden for tonight’s dinner.
The cocozelle squash has a distinctive striped pattern that makes it easy to identify.
She prefers to cook with cocozelle because of its tender flesh and mild flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zucchini - a type of summer squash with tender green skin

Etymology

From Italian 'cocozella', diminutive of 'cocozza' meaning 'squash'.
