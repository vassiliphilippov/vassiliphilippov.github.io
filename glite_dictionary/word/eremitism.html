eremitism

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛr.əˌmaɪ.tɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈɛr.ɪ.mɪ.tɪ.zəm / (BrE)
A monastic practice characterized by living in solitude and withdrawing from society to focus on religious devotion.
monastic life of religious solitude

Examples

The tradition of eremitism was especially prominent among early Christian saints who sought solitude in the desert.
She studied the rise of eremitism in medieval Europe for her thesis on religious retreats.
His pursuit of eremitism led him to a remote mountain cave where he lived for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hermit - a person living in religious solitude
  • Synonym: monasticism - the religious practice of living as a monk or nun

Etymology

From Greek erēmitēs (hermit), from erēmia (desert, solitude).
