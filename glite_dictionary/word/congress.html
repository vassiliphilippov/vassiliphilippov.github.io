Congress

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑŋɡrəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡrəs / (BrE)
the national legislative body of the United States, consisting of the Senate and the House of Representatives
U.S. legislative body
american

Examples

The Congress passed a new bill to improve healthcare.
The President addressed Congress in his annual State of the Union speech.
Elections for Congress are held every two years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parliament - the supreme legislative body in the United Kingdom and other countries

Etymology

From Latin 'congressus' (a meeting, encounter), from 'congrēdī' (to meet).
