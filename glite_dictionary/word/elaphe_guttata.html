Elaphe guttata

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlæfi ɡʌˈtɑtə / (AmE), / ɪˈlæfi ɡʌˈtɑːtə / (BrE)
A large harmless snake of the southeastern United States, commonly found on farms; also known as the corn snake.
large harmless U.S. snake (corn snake)

Examples

The Elaphe guttata is popular among pet owners for its docile nature and attractive colors.
Farmers often appreciate the presence of Elaphe guttata because it helps control rodent populations.
Unlike venomous snakes, the Elaphe guttata poses no threat to humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corn snake - common name for Elaphe guttata, a North American species of rat snake

Etymology

From New Latin, Elaphe (genus of snakes) + guttata (Latin: spotted).
