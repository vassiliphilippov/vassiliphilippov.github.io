cy pres

1. NOUN

/ siː ˈpreɪ / (AmE), / siː ˈpreɪ / (BrE)
a legal doctrine that allows a court to carry out the intent of a donor or testator as closely as possible when literal compliance with their wishes is impossible or impractical
legal doctrine for closest possible intent compliance
legal

Examples

When the original charity no longer existed, the court applied the cy pres doctrine to find a similar beneficiary.
The judge invoked cy pres to ensure the donor's funds benefited a cause close to the intended purpose.
Legal scholars often debate how broadly cy pres should be interpreted in trust law.

Etymology

From Law French, based on Old French 'cy pres' ('as near as' or 'as close as possible').
