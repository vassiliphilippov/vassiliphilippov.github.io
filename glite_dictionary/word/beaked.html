beaked

1. ADJ

/ bikt / (AmE), / biÀêkt / (BrE)
having a beak or a part that resembles a beak
having or resembling a beak

Examples

The beaked whale surfaced briefly before diving again.
Certain orchids have beaked petals that attract specific pollinators.
Archaeologists found pottery with a distinctive beaked spout.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: beakless - lacking a beak

Etymology

From 'beak' + '-ed', formed after the pattern of adjectives indicating possession of a feature.
