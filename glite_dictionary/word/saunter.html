saunter

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑntɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɔːntə / (BrE)
a slow, relaxed, and unhurried walk or way of walking, often suggesting carefreeness or leisure
a leisurely, relaxed walk

Examples

He went for a saunter through the park after dinner.
Their afternoon saunter by the river took longer than expected.
She enjoyed a gentle saunter around the old town, admiring the scenery.

Synset Examples

he walked with a kind of saunter as if he hadn't a care in the world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stroll - a leisurely walk, especially in a public place
  • Antonym: stride - to walk with long, decisive steps

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English santren, of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɑntər / (AmE), / ˈsɔːntə / (BrE)
a slow, relaxed walk, often taken for pleasure in a public place
a leisurely walk

Examples

We took a pleasant saunter through the park after lunch.
His afternoon saunter around the city helped clear his mind.
They enjoyed a gentle saunter by the riverbank as the sun set.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stroll - a leisurely walk, usually in a public place

Etymology

Origin uncertain, attested from the 17th century; possibly from Middle English sauntren (to muse) or French s'aventurer (to venture).

3. VERB

/ ˈsɑntɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɔːntə / (BrE)
to walk in a slow, relaxed manner, without hurry or apparent purpose
walk slowly and leisurely

Examples

She decided to saunter through the park, enjoying the warm afternoon sun.
The couple sauntered along the beach, not in any hurry to reach their destination.
He often saunters into the office late, carrying a cup of coffee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stroll - to walk in a slow and relaxed way
  • Antonym: stride - to walk with long, decisive steps

Etymology

Origin uncertain; perhaps from Middle English 'santren' or influenced by French 's’aventurer' (to take risks, to adventure).
