den

1. NOUN

/ dɛn / (AmE), / dɛn / (BrE)
the home or resting place of a wild animal
wild animal's home

Examples

The fox returned to its den as night fell.
Bears often hibernate in a cozy den during the winter months.
We found the den hidden among the tree roots near the river bank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lair - an animal's resting or hiding place
  • Synonym: burrow - a hole or tunnel dug by a small animal as a dwelling

Etymology

From Old English denu (valley), later used for an animal's lair.

2. NOUN

/ dɛn / (AmE), / dɛn / (BrE)
a group of 8 to 10 cub scouts forming a unit within a Cub Scout pack
Cub Scout group (8-10 members)

Examples

My son is a member of the Wolf den at his Cub Scout pack.
Each den meets weekly to work on different activities and badges.
The den leader organized a camping trip for all eight cub scouts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patrol - a small group of scouts within a larger troop, especially in Boy Scouts

3. NOUN

/ dɛn / (AmE), / dɛn / (BrE)
a room in a house where people can relax in comfort and privacy, often used for reading, hobbies, or informal gatherings
a comfortable, private room in a house

Examples

We spend most evenings together in the den, watching television or reading.
The children have turned the basement den into their own play area.
She retreated to her den to enjoy some quiet time with a good book.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: study - a room in a house used for reading or working
  • Synonym: living room - a room in a house for general family use and entertaining guests

Etymology

Old English denn (animal lair, den), extended to rooms by analogy.

4. NOUN

/ dɛn / (AmE), / dɛn / (BrE)
a secret or hidden place, often used as a refuge or lair by criminals or outlaws
secret hideout or lair

Examples

The bandits retreated to their den deep in the mountains.
Police discovered the drug lord's secret den hidden beneath the warehouse.
Legends say the forest was once filled with the dens of notorious outlaws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lair - a wild animal's resting place, especially one that is hidden
  • Synonym: hideout - a place where someone goes to hide, especially from the law

Etymology

From Old English denu (valley), later shifting to mean animal or outlaw lair.
