confirmation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnfərˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnfəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
a ceremony in a synagogue, usually at Pentecost, in which young men and women who have completed a course of study in Judaism are admitted as adult members of the Jewish community
Jewish ceremony admitting young adults to the community

Examples

She attended her confirmation at the synagogue with her family watching proudly.
After years of study, Daniel was finally ready for his confirmation into the Jewish community.
The rabbi led a moving confirmation ceremony during the Shavuot holiday.

Etymology

From Latin confirmatio (strengthening, establishing), via Old French and Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnfərˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnfəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something officially valid by formally ratifying or confirming it
formal validation or ratification

Examples

The Senate's confirmation of the nominee came after weeks of debate.
Final confirmation of the contract is expected next week.
The treaty cannot take effect without official confirmation from both governments.

Synset Examples

the ratification of the treaty
confirmation of the appointment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ratification - the official approval or sanction of an agreement or decision

Etymology

From Latin confirmatio, from confirmare meaning 'to strengthen, establish'.

5. NOUN

Christian sacrament, admission to full church membership

After her confirmation, she felt a deeper connection to the church community.
He chose his godfather to be his sponsor at his confirmation last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baptism - the Christian sacrament of initiation and purification with water
