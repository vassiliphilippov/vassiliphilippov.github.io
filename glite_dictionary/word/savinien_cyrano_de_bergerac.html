Savinien Cyrano de Bergerac

1. PROPN

/ sæˌvɪniˈɛn siːˌraːnoʊ də bɛrʒəˈræk / (AmE), / savɪˈnjɛn siˈraːnoʊ də bɛʒəˈrak / (BrE)
(1619–1655) A French soldier, writer, and duelist known for his wit and for inspiring the title character in Edmond Rostand's play 'Cyrano de Bergerac'.
French soldier and writer; inspiration for Rostand's play

Examples

Many scholars regard Savinien Cyrano de Bergerac as an innovative thinker and a precursor to science fiction.
The story of Savinien Cyrano de Bergerac's dueling and wit inspired Edmond Rostand's celebrated play.
Although Savinien Cyrano de Bergerac lived in the seventeenth century, his legend endures through literature and film.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cyrano de Bergerac - the title character in Edmond Rostand's 1897 play, inspired by Savinien Cyrano de Bergerac
