bull's-eye

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊlzˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˈbʊlzˌaɪ / (BrE)
the center of a target, or a shot that hits this point, especially in shooting or archery
center of a target in shooting or archery

Examples

After hours of practice, she finally hit the bull's-eye and cheered in excitement.
Scoring a bull's-eye requires skill and precision in archery.
He managed to get three bull's-eyes in a row during the shooting competition.

Synset Examples

in repeated sets of five shots his numbers of bull's-eyes varied

Related Terms

  • Synonym: center - the middle point of something

Etymology

From the resemblance of the central spot in a target to the eye of a bull.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʊlzˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˈbʊlzˌaɪ / (BrE)
a lantern with a single opening and a sliding panel that can be closed to conceal the light
type of lantern with movable cover

Examples

The night watchman carried a bull's-eye on his rounds to discreetly light his way.
Holmes flicked the panel of his bull's-eye shut as they approached the door.
The thief used a bull's-eye to avoid drawing attention in the dark alley.

Etymology

From 'bull's-eye', originally denoting the thick, circular glass in the center of old crown glass windows, later applied to lanterns using such glass.
