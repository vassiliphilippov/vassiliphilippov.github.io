depravation

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌpreɪˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌpreɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
Moral corruption or wickedness; impairment of virtue and moral principles.
moral corruption or wickedness

Examples

The novel explores the depravation that swept through the city after the collapse of its government.
Critics blamed the film's popularity on society's fascination with depravation and vice.
His actions were viewed as evidence of moral depravation by the community leaders.

Synset Examples

the luxury and corruption among the upper classes
moral degeneracy followed intellectual degeneration
its brothels, its opium parlors, its depravity
Rome had fallen into moral putrefaction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depravity - moral corruption; wickedness
  • Antonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards

Etymology

From Latin 'depravatio' (corruption, distortion), from 'depravare' (to corrupt).
