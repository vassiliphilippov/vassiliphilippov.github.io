apparatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæpəˈrætəs / (AmE), / ˌapəˈreɪtəs / (BrE)
(Anatomy) A group of body parts or organs that work together to perform a specific function.
group of body parts/organs with a function
medical

Examples

The apparatus responsible for digestion includes several major organs.
Damage to the respiratory apparatus can make breathing difficult.
Scientists studied the nervous apparatus to understand reflex actions.

Synset Examples

the breathing apparatus

Etymology

From Latin apparatus (equipment, preparation), from apparare (to prepare).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæpəˈrætəs / (AmE), / ˌæpəˈreɪtəs / (BrE)
A complex device or set of equipment designed for a particular purpose.
specialized equipment or device

Examples

The laboratory apparatus was carefully sterilized before use.
Firefighters checked that all their apparatus was functioning properly.
Modern medical apparatus can monitor a patient's vital signs continuously.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: device - a thing made for a particular purpose, especially a mechanical or electronic one
  • Synonym: equipment - the necessary items for a particular purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'apparatus' (preparation, equipment), from 'apparare' (to prepare).
