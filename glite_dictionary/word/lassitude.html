lassitude

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæsɪˌtud / (AmE), / ˈlasɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
A state of physical or mental weariness; a lack of energy or enthusiasm.
physical or mental weariness

Examples

After weeks of hard work, a deep lassitude settled over the entire team.
The oppressive heat contributed to his lassitude, making it hard to concentrate.
A sense of lassitude prevented her from taking part in the celebration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fatigue - extreme tiredness, typically resulting from mental or physical exertion
  • Synonym: lethargy - a lack of energy and enthusiasm
  • Antonym: energy - the ability to be active; vitality and enthusiasm

Etymology

From Latin lassitūdo (weariness), from lassus (weary).

3. NOUN

/ ˈlæs.ɪ.tud / (AmE), / ˈlæs.ɪ.tjuːd / (BrE)
a state of physical or mental weariness; lack of energy or vitality, often approaching a state of stupor or torpor
state of weariness or lack of energy

Examples

After days of fever, she felt a deep lassitude that made even simple movements difficult.
The endless heat wave left the whole city in a state of lassitude, unable to function normally.
His illness brought on a profound lassitude that kept him bedridden for weeks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lethargy - a lack of energy and enthusiasm; drowsiness
  • Antonym: vitality - the state of being strong and active; energy

Etymology

From Latin lassitūdō (faintness, weariness), from lassus (weary).
