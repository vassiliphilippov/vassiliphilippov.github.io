Wave

1. NOUN

/ weɪv / (AmE), / weɪv / (BrE)
A member of the Women's Reserve of the United States Navy, originally organized during World War II and no longer a separate branch.
member of Women's Navy Reserve (USA, WWII)
american

Examples

Her grandmother served as a proud Wave during World War II.
Many Waves took on essential roles in naval communications and administration.
The museum exhibit honored the contributions made by the Waves in the 1940s.

Etymology

Acronym from 'Women Accepted for Volunteer Emergency Service'.
