Geum rivale

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡiːəm rɪˈveɪli / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːəm rɪˈvɑːli / (BrE)
a perennial flowering plant of the rose family found in northern temperate regions, with pinnate leaves and nodding flowers having a brown-purple calyx and orange to pink petals
species of perennial flowering plant

Examples

Geum rivale thrives in damp meadows and along stream banks throughout northern Europe.
Gardeners often choose Geum rivale for its delicate, nodding flowers and tolerance of wet soils.
The unique coloration of Geum rivale petals makes it a favorite among wildflower enthusiasts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water avens - common name for Geum rivale
  • Degree Lower: dryas - a related genus of flowering plants in the rose family

Etymology

From Latin: Geum (genus name) and rivale (growing by streams).
