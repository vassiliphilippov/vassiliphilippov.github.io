Podocarpus coriaceus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊdəˈkɑːrpəs kɔːriˈeɪsiəs / (AmE), / ˌpɒdəˈkɑːpəs kɒrɪˈeɪsɪəs / (BrE)
A species of West Indian evergreen tree with medium to long leathery leaves, belonging to the yew family.
West Indian evergreen tree species

Examples

The garden boasted a mature Podocarpus coriaceus that provided shade all year round.
Botanists identified the rare Podocarpus coriaceus among the native foliage on the island.
The leathery leaves of the Podocarpus coriaceus make it easy to distinguish from other evergreens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yew - any evergreen tree of the genus Taxus

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Podocarpus' from Greek 'pous, podos' (foot) and 'karpos' (fruit); species epithet 'coriaceus' meaning 'leathery'.
