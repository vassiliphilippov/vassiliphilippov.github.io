orthodox

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔr.θə.dɑks / (AmE), / ˈɔː.θə.dɒks / (BrE)
following or conforming to the traditional or generally accepted beliefs, customs, or practices, especially in religion or philosophy.
conforming to accepted beliefs or practices

Examples

Her orthodox approach to education emphasized traditional teaching methods.
Many scientists challenge the orthodox theories to advance new ideas.
His views were considered highly orthodox within his religious community.

Synset Examples

an orthodox view of the world

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unorthodox - not conforming to established customs or beliefs
  • Synonym: conventional - following accepted customs and proprieties

Etymology

From Greek orthodoxos, from orthos (correct) + doxa (opinion).
