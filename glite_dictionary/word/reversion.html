reversion

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈvɝːʒən / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
the return or reappearance of an earlier characteristic, especially in genetics or biology
return to an earlier trait

Examples

The scientist noticed a reversion to ancestral coloration in the bird population.
This genetic mutation caused a reversion to earlier physical characteristics.
A sudden reversion to primitive traits can sometimes be observed in domesticated animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atavism - the reappearance of ancestral traits

Etymology

From Latin 'reverti', meaning to turn back.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈvɝːʒən / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of turning back to a previous or opposite direction or state
turning back or returning to previous direction

Examples

The sudden reversion of the car's direction startled the passengers.
Scientists noted a surprising reversion to earlier methods after new techniques failed.
His ideas showed a reversion to old-fashioned thinking rather than innovation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reversal - the action of making something the opposite of what it was
  • Antonym: progression - the process of developing or moving forward

Etymology

From Latin 'reversio' (a turning back), from 'revertere' (to turn back).

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈvɜrʒən / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
(genetics) A return to a normal phenotype, usually as a result of a second mutation that restores original function or appearance.
genetic return to a normal phenotype
medical

Examples

Scientists observed a reversion in the bacteria after introducing the corrective gene.
The reversion allowed the organism to display its original physical traits.
Geneticists studied the frequency of reversion in successive generations.

Etymology

From Latin 'reversionem' (a turning back).

6. NOUN

/ rɪˈvɜːrʒən / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
(Law) An interest in property that reverts to the original owner or their heirs after the expiration of a particular estate, such as upon the grantee's death.
property interest returning after an estate ends
legal

Examples

Under the terms of the trust, the property will fall into reversion after the tenant's death.
The original owner's reversion gives them a future interest in the estate.
Law students studied reversion as part of their course on property rights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remainder - (Law) an interest in property that passes to a third party on the expiration of a particular estate

Etymology

From Old French *reversion*, from Latin *reversio* (a turning back).
