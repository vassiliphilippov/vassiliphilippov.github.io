Jungermanniales

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒuːŋɚˌmæniˈɑːliːz / (AmE), / ˌdʒʌŋəˌmɑːnɪˈeɪliːz / (BrE)
A large order of mainly tropical liverworts, comprising numerous families of non-vascular plants.
order of tropical liverworts

Examples

Scientists discovered several new species within the Jungermanniales order in the Amazon rainforest.
The Jungermanniales are notable for their diversity among tropical liverworts.
Ecologists study Jungermanniales to better understand non-vascular plant evolution.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Jungermannia (a genus of liverworts) + -ales (taxonomic suffix for orders).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒuːŋɚˌmænɪˈeɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌdʒuːŋəˌmænɪˈeɪliːz / (BrE)
A large order of chiefly tropical liverworts found primarily in moist habitats, comprising various families in the class Hepaticae.
order of tropical liverworts

Examples

Researchers discovered several new species within the Jungermanniales order in the Amazon rainforest.
Most members of the Jungermanniales thrive in consistently moist environments.
The classification of liverworts includes the Jungermanniales as one of the largest and most diverse groups.

Etymology

From the genus Jungermannia, named for German botanist Ludwig Jungermann (1572–1653), with the scientific taxonomy suffix -ales denoting an order.
