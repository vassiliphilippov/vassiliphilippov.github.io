epigastric

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛpɪˈɡæstrɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈɡæstrɪk / (BrE)
relating to the region of the upper central part of the abdomen, especially the area above the stomach
of the upper central abdomen
medical

Examples

The surgeon made a small incision in the epigastric region to access the patient's stomach.
Patients may experience epigastric pain after eating a heavy meal.
The doctor palpated the epigastric area to check for tenderness.

Synset Examples

epigastric artery

Etymology

From Greek epi- (upon) + gastrikós (of the belly).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɛpɪˈɡæstrɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈɡæstrɪk / (BrE)
relating to or situated over the stomach, particularly the upper central region of the abdomen.
situated over the stomach
medical

Examples

The patient complained of severe epigastric pain after eating a large meal.
On examination, there was tenderness in the epigastric region.
The doctor palpated the epigastric area to check for any abnormalities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abdominal - relating to the abdomen
  • Antonym: hypogastric - relating to the lower central region of the abdomen

Etymology

From Greek epi- (upon) + gastrikos (of the stomach).
