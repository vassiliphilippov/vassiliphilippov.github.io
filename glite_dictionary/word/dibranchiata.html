Dibranchiata

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪbræŋkiˈeɪtə / (AmE), / ˌdʌɪbraŋkɪˈeɪtə / (BrE)
A major subclass of cephalopods that includes all living cephalopods except the nautiluses, specifically octopuses, squids, and cuttlefish.
subclass of cephalopods excluding nautiluses

Examples

Modern squids and octopuses belong to the Dibranchiata, distinguished by having two gills.
Cuttlefish are classified within the Dibranchiata due to their anatomical features.
The only living cephalopods not in the Dibranchiata are the nautiluses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cephalopod - marine mollusks like octopuses, squids, and cuttlefish
  • Antonym: Nautilidae - family of cephalopods including nautiluses, excluded from Dibranchiata

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'di-' (two) and Greek 'branchia' (gill), referring to cephalopods with two gills.

2. NOUN

/ daɪˌbræŋkiˈeɪtə / (AmE), / daɪˌbraŋkɪˈɑːtə / (BrE)
A subclass of cephalopods that includes all living species except those in the family Nautilidae, specifically octopuses, squids, and cuttlefish.
subclass of cephalopods excluding nautiluses

Examples

The Dibranchiata includes all modern cephalopods except for the nautiluses.
Scientists have studied the evolution of the Dibranchiata to better understand cephalopod diversity.
Squids and octopuses are both members of the Dibranchiata subclass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cephalopod - marine mollusc such as an octopus, squid, or cuttlefish

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' (two) + 'branchia' (gills), referring to having two gills.
