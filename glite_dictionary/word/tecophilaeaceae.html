Tecophilaeaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛkəˌfaɪliˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌtɛkəˌfɪliˈiːsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants in some classification systems, formerly included as a subfamily of Liliaceae, but not universally accepted as a distinct family
a family of flowering plants (botany)

Examples

The Tecophilaeaceae are known for their vibrant blue flowers, mainly found in South America and Africa.
Some botanists classify rare alpine plants within the Tecophilaeaceae family.
Although not universally accepted, Tecophilaeaceae are recognized in certain modern botanical taxonomies.

Etymology

Named after the genus Tecophilaea, itself honoring the Swiss botanist Tecophila Billotti.
