Syringa reticulata

1. NOUN

/ səˈrɪŋɡə ˌrɛtɪˈkjuːlətə / (AmE), / sɪˈrɪŋɡə ˌrɛtɪˈkjuːlətə / (BrE)
a small tree native to Japan with narrow, pointed leaves and creamy-white flowers, commonly known as the Japanese tree lilac
Japanese tree lilac species

Examples

The Syringa reticulata is prized for its beautiful clusters of creamy-white flowers in early summer.
Many city parks have planted Syringa reticulata because it thrives in urban environments.
I chose Syringa reticulata for my garden to enjoy its fragrant blossoms and ornamental appeal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Japanese tree lilac - common name for Syringa reticulata

Etymology

From Latin; 'Syringa' is the genus name, from Greek 'syrinx' (pipe), and 'reticulata' means 'netted' or 'network' in Latin, referring to the leaf veins.
