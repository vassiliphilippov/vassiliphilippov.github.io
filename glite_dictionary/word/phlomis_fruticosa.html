Phlomis fruticosa

1. NOUN

/ ˌfloʊmɪs fruːtɪˈkoʊsə / (AmE), / ˌfləʊmɪs fruːtɪˈkəʊsə / (BrE)
A Mediterranean subshrub with dense whorls of purple or yellow flowers, commonly cultivated for its ornamental value; also known as Jerusalem sage.
Mediterranean shrub; Jerusalem sage

Examples

Many gardeners choose Phlomis fruticosa for its drought resistance and striking yellow blooms.
The borders of the courtyard were lined with mature Phlomis fruticosa plants.
Phlomis fruticosa attracts bees and butterflies, making it a popular choice for wildlife gardens.

Etymology

From Greek *phlomis* (a kind of plant) and Latin *fruticosa* (shrubby), reflecting botanical classification.
