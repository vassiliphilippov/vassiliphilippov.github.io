fluorescence

1. NOUN

/ fluˈrɛsəns / (AmE), / flʊəˈrɛsəns / (BrE)
the emission of light by a substance that has absorbed light or other electromagnetic radiation, typically of a different wavelength
emission of light after absorbing radiation

Examples

The jellyfish exhibited a brilliant fluorescence under ultraviolet light.
Scientists use fluorescence to detect specific molecules in biological samples.
The minerals glowed with fluorescence when exposed to the blacklight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phosphorescence - light emitted by a substance without combustion or heat, often after exposure to radiation

Etymology

From Latin 'fluor' (flow) and '-escence' (beginning to be); named after the mineral fluorite, where the effect was first observed.
