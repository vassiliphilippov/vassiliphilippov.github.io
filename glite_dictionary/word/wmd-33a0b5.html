W.M.D.

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʌbəl.juː ˌɛm ˈdiː / (AmE), / ˌdʌbəl.juː ˌɛm ˈdiː / (BrE)
an abbreviation for weapon of mass destruction; a weapon capable of causing widespread and significant harm or death to large numbers of people, including both civilians and military personnel, especially nuclear, chemical, or biological weapons
weapon causing mass casualties (nuclear, chemical, biological)

Examples

The discovery of W.M.D. stockpiles prompted widespread international concern.
He argued against the proliferation of W.M.D. in volatile regions.
Fears about terrorist groups acquiring a W.M.D. have shaped global security policies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weapon of mass destruction - a weapon with the capacity to inflict mass casualties, especially nuclear, chemical or biological

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'weapon of mass destruction'.
