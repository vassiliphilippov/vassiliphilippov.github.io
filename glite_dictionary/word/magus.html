magus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪɡəs / (AmE), / ˈmeɪɡəs / (BrE)
a magician, sorcerer, or member of a priestly caste from ancient times, especially among the ancient Persians
ancient magician or sorcerer

Examples

In ancient Persia, a magus was revered as both a religious scholar and a magician.
Legends tell of a powerful magus who could summon spirits with mysterious rituals.
The travelers sought wisdom from the magus who lived atop the mountain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorcerer - a person who claims or is believed to have magical powers
  • Synonym: wizard - a man who has magical powers
  • Synonym: witch - a woman thought to have magical powers

Etymology

From Latin 'magus', from Ancient Greek 'magos', from Old Persian 'magush' (member of the priestly caste).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪɡəs / (AmE), / ˈmeɪɡəs / (BrE)
a member of the priestly caste of ancient Persia, especially in Zoroastrianism
member of ancient Persian priesthood

Examples

The magus played a central role in Zoroastrian rituals in ancient Persia.
According to legend, a wise magus foretold the rise of a great king.
Artifacts from Persia often depict a magus wearing traditional robes and a tall hat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mage - a magician or learned person, especially one with supernatural powers

Etymology

From Latin 'magus', from Greek 'magos', from Old Persian 'maguš' (priest).
