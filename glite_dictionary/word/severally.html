severally

1. ADV

/ ˈsɛv.ɚ.əli / (AmE), / ˈsɛv.ər.əli / (BrE)
Individually or separately, rather than collectively.
individually; separately

Examples

Each tenant is severally responsible for their portion of the rent.
The committee members acted severally, issuing their statements individually.
The gifts were distributed severally to the children rather than as a group.

Synset Examples

the clothes were hung severally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: jointly - together, collectively, in unison

Etymology

From Latin 'separalis' (separate), via Old French and Middle English.

2. ADV

/ ˈsɛvɚəli / (AmE), / ˈsɛvərəli / (BrE)
Separately from others; individually or one by one.
separately; individually

Examples

Each tenant is severally responsible for his share of the rent.
The players were severally called to the stage to receive their awards.
Liabilities must be considered severally as well as jointly.

Synset Examples

taken individually, the rooms were, in fact, square
the fine points are treated singly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: individually - Separately; one at a time rather than collectively
  • Antonym: together - With or in proximity to others; collectively

Etymology

From Latin 'separalis' (separate), influenced by 'several'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00208995-r
Synset POS: r
