hybridization

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪbrɪdəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhɪbrɪdaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of breeding together different species or varieties of animals or plants to produce a hybrid
breeding different species or varieties
medical

Examples

Hybridization between two closely related plant species can result in unique and resilient offspring.
The agricultural scientist explained the importance of hybridization in improving crop yields.
Genetic hybridization is widely used to enhance disease resistance in livestock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crossbreeding - the breeding of different breeds or species to produce a hybrid
  • Antonym: inbreeding - breeding from closely related animals or plants

Etymology

From Latin 'hybrida' (offspring of a tame sow and wild boar) + -ization.
