sailing

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the sport or activity of traveling in a boat propelled by sails
activity of traveling by sailboat

Examples

We spent the afternoon sailing along the coast in a small yacht.
She has always dreamed of sailing across the Atlantic Ocean.
Many tourists enjoy sailing on the lake during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boating - travel for pleasure in a boat

Etymology

From sail + -ing, denoting the act or process of sailing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the occupation or duties of working as a sailor, especially on a ship or boat
work or duty of a sailor

Examples

He spent his entire career in sailing and knew every aspect of ship life.
The hardships of sailing are not for the faint-hearted.
She wrote a book describing the daily routines and challenges of sailing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seafaring - working or traveling regularly by sea, usually as a sailor

Etymology

From sail + -ing, referring to the activity associated with sailors.

3. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or time of a vessel leaving a port or harbor
departure of a ship from port

Examples

The next sailing to Dublin is scheduled for tomorrow morning.
Due to bad weather, yesterday's sailing was postponed until further notice.
We checked the timetable to find the earliest sailing from the port.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: departure - act of leaving a place

Etymology

From Old English 'seglian', related to 'sail' (to travel by water).

Synset POS: n
