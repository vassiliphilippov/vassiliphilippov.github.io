to nestle

1. VERB

/ ˈnɛsəl / (AmE), / ˈnɛsəl / (BrE)
to settle or move into a comfortable and cozy position, often against something for warmth or comfort
settle comfortably in a position

Examples

She nestled into the soft blanket and quickly fell asleep.
The cat nestled in his lap, purring contentedly.
They nestled together by the fireplace to stay warm.

Synset Examples

We cuddled against each other to keep warm
The children snuggled into their sleeping bags

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snuggle - to settle or move into a warm, comfortable position; very close synonym
  • Synonym: cuddle - hold close for warmth or affection; synonym in context of comfort

Etymology

From Old English *nestlian* (build a nest), from *nest* + verb-forming suffix.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01427599-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈnɛsəl / (AmE), / ˈnɛsəl / (BrE)
to settle or lie comfortably within or against something; to position oneself snugly or cozily
settle comfortably or snugly

Examples

The kitten nestled against its mother's warm fur.
She nestled into the pillow and closed her eyes.
The cottage nestles in a quiet valley surrounded by trees.

Synset Examples

The baby nestled her head in her mother's elbow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snuggle - settle or move into a warm, comfortable position
  • Synonym: cuddle - hold close in one's arms as a way of showing love or affection

Etymology

From Old French *nesler*, from Vulgar Latin *nidiculare* (to build a nest), from Latin *nidus* (nest).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01427999-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈnɛsəl / (AmE), / ˈnɛsəl / (BrE)
to be situated or lie in a sheltered, partly hidden or protected position
to lie in a sheltered position

Examples

The small village nestles at the foot of the mountains, surrounded by trees.
A cozy cabin nestles among the pine trees, almost hidden from view.
The ancient castle nestles in a quiet valley, protected from the harsh winds.

Synset Examples

The little cottage nestles in the forest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snuggle - to settle or move into a warm, comfortable, and cozy position

Etymology

From Old English *nestlian* (to make a nest), related to 'nest.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02697196-v
Synset POS: v
