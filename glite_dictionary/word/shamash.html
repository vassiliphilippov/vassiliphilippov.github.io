Shamash

1. PROPN

/ ˈʃɑːˌmɑːʃ / (AmE), / ˈʃɑːˌmɑːʃ / (BrE)
(in ancient Mesopotamian religion) the chief sun god, associated with justice, compassion, and driving away evil.
chief sun god in Mesopotamian mythology

Examples

In ancient Babylon, Shamash was worshipped as the supreme judge and dispenser of justice.
The temple of Shamash stood as an impressive centerpiece in the city of Sippar.
Scholars often refer to Shamash when studying early concepts of law and morality.

Etymology

From Akkadian Šamaš, the sun god in Mesopotamian mythology.
