sarsenet

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːrs(ə)nɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɑːsənɪt / (BrE)
A fine, soft silk fabric, often used for linings, popular in the 18th and 19th centuries.
fine soft silk fabric for linings
archaic

Examples

The dress was lined with sarsenet for a smooth and luxurious feel.
Antique gowns often feature layers of sarsenet beneath the outer fabric.
She selected a pale blue sarsenet to line her evening cloak.

Etymology

From Old French 'sarcenet', from Medieval Latin 'saracinatum', originally meaning 'Saracen cloth'.
