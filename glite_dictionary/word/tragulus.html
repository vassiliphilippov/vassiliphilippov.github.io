Tragulus

1. NOUN

/ ˈtræɡjələs / (AmE), / ˈtræɡjʊləs / (BrE)
A genus of small, mouse-deer–like mammals of the family Tragulidae, native to Southeast Asia.
genus of small Southeast Asian mammals

Examples

The Tragulus species are found in the forests of Southeast Asia and are known for their small size.
Unlike deer, members of the genus Tragulus do not have antlers or horns.
Scientists discovered a new species of Tragulus in the dense jungles of Borneo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chevrotain - a small, deer-like mammal also known as mouse deer

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Latin 'tragulus' (little goat), from Greek 'tragos' (goat).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtræɡjʊləs / (AmE), / ˈtræɡjʊləs / (BrE)
A genus of small hoofed mammals known as mouse deer belonging to the family Tragulidae, found in South and Southeast Asia.
genus of mouse deer

Examples

The genus Tragulus comprises the smallest hoofed mammals in the world.
Researchers observed a new species within the Tragulus genus in Borneo.
Members of the Tragulus genus are commonly called mouse deer due to their size.

Etymology

From Latin *tragulus* (small goat), diminutive of Greek *tragos* (goat).
