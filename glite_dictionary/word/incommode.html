to incommode

1. VERB

/ ˌɪn.kəˈmoʊd / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kəˈməʊd / (BrE)
to cause inconvenience or discomfort to someone
cause inconvenience or discomfort

Examples

She hoped her late arrival would not incommode her hosts.
Travelers should take care not to incommode local residents with noise.
Would it incommode you if I borrowed your car this afternoon?

Synset Examples

Sorry to trouble you, but …

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trouble - to cause inconvenience or distress to someone
  • Synonym: to disturb - to interrupt or inconvenience someone
  • Antonym: comfort - to make someone feel less uncomfortable or troubled

Etymology

From French 'incommoder', from Latin 'incommodare' (to make uncomfortable).
