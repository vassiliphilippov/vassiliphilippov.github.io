microbiology

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkɹoʊbaɪˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkɹəʊbaɪˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The branch of biology that deals with the study of microorganisms and their effects, particularly on humans.
study of microorganisms

Examples

She decided to study microbiology after developing an interest in infectious diseases.
Thanks to advances in microbiology, we now have better treatments for many bacterial infections.
Microbiology laboratories are essential for diagnosing and researching pathogens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacteriology - the study of bacteria

Etymology

From French 'microbiologie', from Greek 'mikros' (small), 'bios' (life), and 'logia' (study).
