feigning

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfeɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of pretending or giving a false appearance of something
the act of pretending

Examples

Her feigning of surprise was convincing to everyone in the room.
He was criticized for his constant feigning of illness to avoid work.
The actor's skill at feigning emotions made his performance very believable.

Synset Examples

his conformity was only pretending

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretending - the act of behaving as if something is true when it is not
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and sincere

Etymology

From Middle English 'feinen', from Old French 'feign-', stem of 'feindre' (to pretend), from Latin 'fingere' (to shape, form, feign).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfeɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of pretending or faking something, especially with the intention to deceive
pretending or faking to deceive

Examples

His feigning of illness fooled everyone at the office.
Their feigning of surprise seemed unconvincing to the audience.
She was skilled at feigning indifference when she was actually upset.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretending - the act of behaving as if something is true when it is not
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest; not deceiving

Etymology

From the verb 'feign', ultimately from Old French 'feign-', stem of 'feignir', from Latin 'fingere' (to shape, invent).
