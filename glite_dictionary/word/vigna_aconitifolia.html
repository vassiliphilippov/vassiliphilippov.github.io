Vigna aconitifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈviːɡnə ˌækəˌnaɪtɪˈfəʊliə / (AmE), / ˈvɪɡnə ˌækəˌnɪtɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A species of legume native to India, grown for its edible seeds and used as food, forage, and soil conditioner.
species of edible legume native to India

Examples

Farmers in Rajasthan cultivate Vigna aconitifolia for its drought-resistant qualities and valuable protein-rich seeds.
Researchers are exploring Vigna aconitifolia as a sustainable crop for arid regions.
In Indian cuisine, the seeds of Vigna aconitifolia are often ground into flour or cooked whole.

Etymology

Vigna: after Italian botanist Domenico Vigna; aconitifolia: from resemblance of leaves to those of Aconitum (wolfsbane) and Latin 'folia' (leaves).
