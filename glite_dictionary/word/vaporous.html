vaporous

1. ADJ

/ ˈveɪpɚəs / (AmE), / ˈveɪpərəs / (BrE)
filled with or containing vapor; misty or hazy
filled with vapor; misty

Examples

The swamp had a vaporous atmosphere that clung to everything.
Early morning sunlight filtered through the vaporous air above the river.
The valley remained vaporous after the heavy rain, shrouding the trees in mist.

Synset Examples

miasmic jungles
a vaporous bog

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misty - full of or covered with mist
  • Synonym: foggy - filled or abounding with fog

Etymology

From Latin 'vaporosus', from 'vapor' (steam).

2. ADJ

/ ˈveɪ.pɚ.əs / (AmE), / ˈveɪ.pər.əs / (BrE)
Extremely thin or light to the point of being almost transparent or able to transmit light.
almost transparent; thin and light

Examples

She wore a vaporous scarf that fluttered in the breeze.
Through the vaporous curtains, sunlight softly filled the room.
The artist painted vaporous clouds drifting across a pale blue sky.

Synset Examples

gossamer cobwebs
sheer silk stockings
transparent chiffon
vaporous silks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheer - very thin; almost transparent, as of fabric
  • Synonym: diaphanous - light, delicate, and translucent, typically of fabric
  • Antonym: opaque - not able to be seen through; not transparent or translucent

Etymology

From Latin 'vaporosus' (full of vapor), from 'vapor'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈveɪpərəs / (AmE), / ˈveɪpərəs / (BrE)
resembling or characteristic of vapor; having a misty or insubstantial quality.
resembling vapor; misty or insubstantial

Examples

The mountaintops were hidden by vaporous clouds drifting across the sky.
She wore a vaporous scarf that floated elegantly around her shoulders.
His vision of the future remained vaporous and ill-defined, like a dream fading at dawn.

Synset Examples

vaporous clouds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misty - full of or covered with mist
  • Synonym: foggy - filled with fog; hard to see through
  • Antonym: solid - firm and stable in shape; not liquid or gas

Etymology

From Latin vaporosus (full of vapor), from vapor (steam, vapor).
