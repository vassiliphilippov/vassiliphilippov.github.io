Prunus serotina

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs səˈroʊtɪnə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs səˈrɒtɪnə / (BrE)
A large North American wild cherry tree with round black sour edible fruit.
North American wild cherry tree

Examples

Many birds feed on the fruit of the Prunus serotina in autumn.
The wood of Prunus serotina is prized for furniture making.
Prunus serotina can sometimes be found along forest edges and roadsides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black cherry - Common name for Prunus serotina, North American wild cherry tree

Etymology

From Latin: prunus (plum tree) and serotina (late), referring to the late-ripening fruit.
