yobibit

1. NOUN

/ ˈjoʊbɪˌbɪt / (AmE), / ˈjəʊbɪˌbɪt / (BrE)
a unit of digital information equal to 1,208,925,819,614,629,174,706,176 (2^80) bits, primarily used in computing.
binary data unit: 2^80 bits

Examples

Some supercomputers are capable of storing multiple yobibits of data in their systems.
The file system is designed to handle up to one yobibit of information efficiently.
Scientists discussed raw data measurements approaching several yobibits during the conference.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: zebibit - a unit of digital information equal to 2^70 bits
  • Synonym: yottabit - a unit of information equal to 10^24 bits

Etymology

From 'yotta' (the SI prefix for 10^24) and 'bit', with 'yobi-' denoting the binary power 2^80.
