gorget

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɔrdʒət / (AmE), / ˈɡɔːdʒɪt / (BrE)
A piece of armor that protects the throat or neck, typically worn in the Middle Ages.
neck armor plate

Examples

The knight wore a steel gorget to shield his neck during battle.
Historians examined the ornate gorget found among the ruins.
The museum display featured a medieval gorget alongside other pieces of armor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collar - protective covering around the neck

Etymology

From Old French 'gorgete', diminutive of 'gorge' (throat), from Latin 'gurges' (whirlpool, throat).
