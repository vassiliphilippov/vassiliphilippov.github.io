Dilleniaceae

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌlɛniˈeɪsi.i / (AmE), / dɪˌlɛniˈeɪsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly tropical shrubs, trees, and climbers, characterized by leathery leaves or flattened, leaflike stems; includes genera such as Dillenia and Hibbertia.
family of tropical shrubs, trees, and climbers

Examples

The evergreen shrub she found in the forest belongs to the Dilleniaceae family.
Many gardeners appreciate members of the Dilleniaceae for their ornamental flowers.
Botanists have classified both Dillenia and Hibbertia under the Dilleniaceae.

Etymology

From the genus name Dillenia, named after German botanist Johann Jacob Dillenius, with Latin family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˌlɛniˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / dɪˌlɛniˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly tropical shrubs, trees, and climbers with leathery leaves or flattened, leaflike stems, including the genera Dillenia and Hibbertia
family of tropical shrubs, trees, and climbers

Examples

The Dilleniaceae family includes many species native to Southeast Asia and Australia.
Some members of the Dilleniaceae are cultivated as ornamental plants for their attractive flowers.
Botanists study the distinctive leaves of the Dilleniaceae to identify different species within the family.

Etymology

From the genus name Dillenia, named after Johann Jacob Dillenius, German botanist, plus the standard botanical suffix '-aceae.'
