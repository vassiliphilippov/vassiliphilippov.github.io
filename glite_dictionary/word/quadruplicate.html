quadruplicate

1. VERB

/ kwɑːˈdruːplɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / kwɒˈdrʊplɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to make four copies of something; to reproduce in fourfold
make four copies of something

Examples

The secretary was asked to quadruplicate the financial report for distribution.
They quadruplicated the forms to ensure every department had a copy.
Please quadruplicate these invoices as required by the new procedure.

Synset Examples

quadruplicate the bill

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: duplicate - make an exact copy of something
  • Degree Lower: triplicate - make three copies of something

Etymology

From Latin 'quadruplex' (fourfold), from 'quadru-' (four) + 'plicare' (to fold).

2. NOUN

/ kwɑːˈdrʊplɪkət / (AmE), / kwɒˈdrʊplɪkət / (BrE)
one of four identical copies or things; a set of four items that correspond exactly to each other, especially of documents or records
one of four identical copies

Examples

The application form must be submitted in quadruplicate to the office.
They keep financial records in quadruplicate for auditing purposes.
A contract was prepared and signed in quadruplicate by all parties involved.

Synset Examples

it was signed in quadruplicate

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: duplicate - one of two identical copies; an exact copy
  • Degree Lower: triplicate - one of three identical copies; any of three things that correspond exactly

Etymology

From Latin 'quadruplex' (fourfold), from 'quattuor' (four) + 'plicare' (to fold).
