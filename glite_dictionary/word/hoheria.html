Hoheria

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˈhɪəriə / (AmE), / həʊˈhɪəriə / (BrE)
A small genus of shrubs and small trees native to New Zealand, commonly called lacebarks, characterized by their attractive white flowers and bark that peels in lace-like layers.
genus of New Zealand shrubs or trees (lacebarks)

Examples

The Hoheria trees in the garden produce beautiful clusters of white flowers every spring.
Botanists studied several species of Hoheria native to New Zealand's forests.
Gardeners prize Hoheria for its decorative bark and elegant foliage.

Etymology

From the Maori word 'houhere', adapted into botanical Latin as Hoheria.
