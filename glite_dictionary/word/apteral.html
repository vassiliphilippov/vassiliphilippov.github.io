apteral

1. ADJ

/ ˈæptɚəl / (AmE), / ˈæptərəl / (BrE)
(of insects) lacking wings; wingless.
wingless (of insects)

Examples

Many ants are apteral workers with no wings.
The researchers observed several apteral beetles in the sample area.
Some parasitic wasps exhibit an apteral form as adults.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wingless - lacking wings

Etymology

From Greek 'apteros' meaning 'without wings', from 'a-' (without) + 'pteron' (wing).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈæp.tər.əl / (AmE), / ˈæp.tər.əl / (BrE)
(of a building, especially a classical temple) having columns at one or both ends but not along the sides.
having columns at one or both ends of a building

Examples

The ancient temple is apteral, with columns gracing only its front facade.
Unlike the peripteral style, the apteral design omits columns from the sides of the building.
The architect chose an apteral format to create a simpler, more focused entrance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: peripteral - having a single row of columns on all sides of a building
  • Synonym: amphiprostyle - having columns at both ends but not along the sides

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'pteron' (wing), referring to the lack of side 'wings' or colonnades.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00140612-a
Synset POS: a
