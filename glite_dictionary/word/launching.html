launching

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔntʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɔːntʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of starting or introducing something new, especially for the first time.
the act of starting or introducing something new

Examples

The launching of the new smartphone attracted large crowds to the store.
She was responsible for the launching of several successful marketing campaigns.
After months of preparation, the launching finally took place amid great excitement.

Synset Examples

they looked forward to the debut of their new product line

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debut - the first public appearance or presentation of something

Etymology

From the verb 'launch', which comes from Middle English 'launchen', from Old French 'lancier' (to throw), ultimately from Latin 'lancea' (lance).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɔntʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɔːntʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of putting a newly built ship or boat into the water for the first time, usually as part of a ceremony
putting a new ship into water for the first time

Examples

The launching of the naval vessel was attended by dignitaries and the shipyard workers.
Hundreds gathered to witness the launching of the community's first fishing boat.
Traditionally, the launching of a ship includes breaking a bottle of champagne against its hull.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: launch - to put a boat or ship into the water

Etymology

From launch + -ing; 'launch' from Old French 'lancher', meaning to throw or hurl.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00104111-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈlɔntʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɔːntʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of propelling or sending something forward with force, or setting something in motion
the act of sending forth with force

Examples

The launching of the spacecraft was broadcast live on television.
After the launching of the campaign, donations increased significantly.
He watched the launching of the fireworks from his balcony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: launch - to send forth or propel, typically as a verb

Etymology

From the verb 'launch', from Middle French *lancher*, from Late Latin *lanceare* (to wield a lance).

Source: WordNet
