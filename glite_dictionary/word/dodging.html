dodging

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɒdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of deliberately avoiding something or someone, or preventing something from happening
the act of intentionally avoiding

Examples

His constant dodging of difficult questions frustrated the interviewer.
Tax dodging is illegal and can lead to severe penalties.
During the game, her quick dodging allowed her to avoid being tagged.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avoidance - the act of keeping away from something
  • Antonym: confrontation - direct engagement or facing of a situation

Etymology

From the verb 'dodge', likely of uncertain origin, first attested in the early 16th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɑdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɒdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of avoiding or escaping something unpleasant, especially by using deceit or trickery
avoidance by trickery or deceit

Examples

His dodging of responsibility became obvious to everyone in the office.
Tax dodging can result in heavy penalties if discovered by the authorities.
Repeated dodging of the truth damaged his reputation beyond repair.

Synset Examples

his evasion of his clear duty was reprehensible
that escape from the consequences is possible but unattractive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evasion - the act of avoiding something you should do, often by cunning
  • Synonym: avoidance - the act of keeping away from something
  • Antonym: fulfillment - the carrying out of a duty or obligation

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɑdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɒdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
an act or instance of evading a question or issue by cleverness or trickery
evasive statement or trick

Examples

The politician's answer was mere dodging, skillfully avoiding the real issue.
I'm tired of your constant dodging whenever I ask a direct question.
Her dodging during the interview frustrated both the host and the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evasion - the act of avoiding something, especially by trickery or cleverness
  • Synonym: prevarication - the act of avoiding giving a direct answer; evasion

Etymology

From the verb 'dodge,' probably from English dialect dodge (to jog or move quickly), of uncertain origin.
