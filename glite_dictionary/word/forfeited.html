forfeited

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔːrfɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈfɔːfɪtɪd / (BrE)
taken away or lost as a result of breaking a rule, law, or agreement; surrendered as a penalty.
lost or surrendered as a penalty

Examples

The company must return all forfeited funds to the investors after the ruling.
His forfeited driver’s license will not be reinstated for another year.
The tournament organizers announced that forfeited matches would not be replayed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forfeit - to lose or give up as a penalty for wrongdoing

Etymology

Past participle of 'forfeit', from Old French 'forfait' (crime, transgression), from forfait, past participle of 'forfaire' (to transgress).
