sluggard

1. NOUN

/ ˈslʌɡ.ɚd / (AmE), / ˈslʌɡ.əd / (BrE)
A person who is habitually lazy or inactive.
lazy or idle person

Examples

He was called a sluggard for sleeping until noon every day.
The book warns that the sluggard comes to ruin through neglect and laziness.
Instead of being a sluggard, she worked diligently and achieved her goals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idler - a person who avoids work or activity
  • Antonym: hard worker - a person who works diligently

Etymology

From Middle English slugard, from 'slug' (lazy person) + '-ard' (pejorative agent suffix).
