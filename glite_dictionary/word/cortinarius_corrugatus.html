Cortinarius corrugatus

1. PROPN

/ kɔrˌtɪnəriəs kəˌruːˈɡeɪtəs / (AmE), / kɔːˌtɪnəriəs kəˌruːˈɡeɪtəs / (BrE)
A species of fungus characterized by a viscid, wrinkled, tawny cap, a stalk with a basal bulb that diminishes as the stalk elongates, and gills that are dark violet at first but soon turn brown.
a species of wrinkled-capped mushroom

Examples

The Cortinarius corrugatus is often recognized by its wrinkled, sticky cap and violet gills.
Mycologists discovered a patch of Cortinarius corrugatus deep in the forest after the rains.
Identifying Cortinarius corrugatus requires examining the color changes in its gills as it matures.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus Cortinarius and the Latin 'corrugatus' (wrinkled).
