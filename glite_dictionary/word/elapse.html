to elapse

1. VERB

/ ɪˈlæps / (AmE), / ɪˈlæps / (BrE)
(of time) to pass or go by, especially in a regular or unnoticed way.
(of time) to pass or go by

Examples

Several hours elapsed before anyone noticed the problem.
As weeks elapsed, the investigation made little progress.
Five years may elapse before construction is completed.

Synset Examples

three years elapsed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pass - (of time) to move by
  • Synonym: to transpire - (of time or events) to occur or happen

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'elapsari' (to slip away), from Latin 'elapsus', past participle of 'elabi' (to slip away).
