Artemia

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈtiːmiə / (AmE), / ɑːˈtiːmɪə / (BrE)
a genus of small aquatic crustaceans known as brine shrimp, often found in salt lakes and used as food for aquarium fish
genus of brine shrimp

Examples

The laboratory uses Artemia as a food source for young fish.
Many aquarists hatch Artemia eggs to feed their marine animals.
Artemia can survive in extremely salty environments where few other creatures thrive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brine shrimp - small aquatic crustacean adapted to saline waters; common name for Artemia

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek Artemis (the goddess), used in scientific nomenclature.

2. NOUN

/ ɑrˈtiːmiə / (AmE), / ɑːˈtiːmiə / (BrE)
a genus of small aquatic crustaceans commonly known as brine shrimp or fairy shrimp, found in saltwater lakes and ponds.
genus of small brine shrimp

Examples

The laboratory used Artemia as food for young fish and amphibians.
Salt ponds are a natural habitat for Artemia populations worldwide.
Researchers study Artemia because they are tolerant of extreme saline environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brine shrimp - common name for species in the genus Artemia

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, probably based on the Greek name Artemis (goddess associated with nature).
