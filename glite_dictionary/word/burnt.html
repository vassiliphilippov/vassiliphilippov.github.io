burnt

1. ADJ

/ bɝːnt / (AmE), / bɜːnt / (BrE)
destroyed or badly damaged by fire or excessive heat
damaged or destroyed by fire

Examples

The burnt building was declared unsafe and had to be demolished.
She threw away the burnt toast and made a fresh batch for breakfast.
After the wildfire, the landscape was littered with burnt trees and ash.

Synset Examples

a row of burned houses
a charred bit of burnt wood
a burned-over site in the forest
barricaded the street with burnt-out cars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burned - destroyed or badly damaged by fire (alternative spelling, chiefly North American)

Etymology

Past participle of 'burn', from Old English 'biernan' or 'bærnan'.

2. ADJ

/ bɝːnt / (AmE), / bɜːnt / (BrE)
(of a substance) treated by heating to a high temperature but below its melting or fusing point, often resulting in a color or composition change.
heated until changed but not melted

Examples

The artist preferred burnt sienna for its rich, earthy tone in his landscape paintings.
This pottery has a distinctive look due to its burnt clay surface.
Some natural pigments, such as burnt umber, are made by heating the raw material.

Synset Examples

burnt sienna
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01961085-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ bɝːnt / (AmE), / bɜːnt / (BrE)
(of food) spoiled by excessive cooking, especially by being overexposed to heat and developing a charred or blackened surface.
spoiled by excessive cooking

Examples

The toast was completely burnt and inedible.
She apologized for the burnt cookies fresh out of the oven.
His omelette turned burnt because he forgot to flip it in time.

Synset Examples

she served us underdone bacon and burnt biscuits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charred - partially burned, blackened by burning

Etymology

Past participle of 'burn', from Old English 'byrnan' (to burn).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
