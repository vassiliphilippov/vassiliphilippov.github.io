Hipposideridae

1. NOUN

/ hɪˌpɑsɪˈdɛrɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəʊsɪˈdɛrɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of Old World bats known as leafnose bats, distinguished by a leaf-shaped structure on their noses
family of Old World leafnose bats

Examples

The Hipposideridae are widely distributed throughout Africa and Asia.
Members of the Hipposideridae family use echolocation to navigate in the dark.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Hipposideridae in Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rhinolophidae - family of horseshoe bats, closely related

Etymology

From modern Latin Hipposideridae, based on the type genus Hipposideros (from Greek hippos 'horse' + sideros 'iron'), referencing the horseshoe-shaped nose leaf.

2. NOUN

/ hɪpəˌsɪdəˈraɪdiː / (AmE), / hɪpəˌsɪdərˈɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of bats known as Old World leafnose bats, distinguished by their leaf-shaped nose structures and found mainly in Africa, Asia, and Australia
a family of Old World leafnose bats

Examples

The Hipposideridae are known for their distinctive leaf-shaped nose structures, which aid in echolocation.
Unlike other bat families, Hipposideridae prefer to roost in caves and abandoned buildings.
Researchers have discovered several new species within the Hipposideridae in Southeast Asia.

Etymology

From Greek 'hippos' (horse) + 'sideros' (iron), referring to the shape of the nose leaf.
