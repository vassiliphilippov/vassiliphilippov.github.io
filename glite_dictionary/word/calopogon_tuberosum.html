Calopogon tuberosum

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈpoʊɡɒn tuːbəˈroʊsəm / (AmE), / ˌkæləˈpɒɡɒn tjuːbəˈrəʊsəm / (BrE)
A species of North American orchid, commonly known as the grass pink, characterized by its bright pink flowers and tuberous roots.
North American orchid species

Examples

The meadow was dotted with the vibrant blossoms of Calopogon tuberosum.
Botanists are researching the pollination mechanisms of Calopogon tuberosum in eastern woodlands.
I photographed a rare cluster of Calopogon tuberosum orchids in the marsh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grass pink - Common name for Calopogon tuberosum

Etymology

From Greek 'kalos' (beautiful) and 'pogon' (beard), referencing the flower's appearance, with Latin 'tuberosum' (full of tubers).
