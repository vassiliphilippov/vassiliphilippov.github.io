Scyliorhinidae

1. NOUN

/ saɪli.oʊˈraɪnɪdi / (AmE), / saɪli.əʊˈraɪnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, bottom-dwelling sharks commonly known as catsharks, found in temperate and tropical seas worldwide.
family of small bottom-dwelling sharks

Examples

The Scyliorhinidae are often referred to as catsharks due to their elongated cat-like eyes.
Marine biologists discovered several new species within the Scyliorhinidae family off the coast of Australia.
Unlike larger sharks, members of the Scyliorhinidae family typically inhabit the ocean floor and feed on small invertebrates.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'skylla' (a kind of sea monster) and 'rhinos' (nose), used in scientific taxonomy.
