impellent

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɛl.ənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpel.ənt / (BrE)
having the power to drive or force forward; impelling.
driving or forcing forward

Examples

The impellent force of the current swept the boat downstream.
Curiosity acted as an impellent for their scientific discoveries.
His words had an impellent effect on the team's determination to succeed.

Synset Examples

an impellent power
an impellent cause

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impelling - driving or urging forward; stimulating action
  • Synonym: motive - something that causes someone to act

Etymology

From Latin impellens, present participle of impellere (to drive forward).
