individualisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndəˌvɪdʒuəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˌvɪdjʊəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of distinguishing an individual from a group or category; making something distinctively individual
the act of distinguishing as an individual

Examples

The individualisation of students' needs is key in modern education systems.
Her research focuses on the individualisation of medical treatments for better patient outcomes.
Cultural traditions can often hinder the individualisation of personal identity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: individualization - alternative American spelling of 'individualisation'
  • Antonym: generalization - the process of making something general or broad rather than specific or individual

Etymology

From individual + -isation, influenced by French individualisation.
