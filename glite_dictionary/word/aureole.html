aureole

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔriˌoʊl / (AmE), / ˈɔːrɪəʊl / (BrE)
the outermost region of the sun's atmosphere, visible as a bright white halo during a solar eclipse
outermost region of sun's atmosphere

Examples

During the total solar eclipse, the aureole glowed bright around the hidden sun.
Scientists study the aureole to better understand solar winds and magnetic fields.
The sight of the sun's aureole is both rare and breathtaking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corona - the outermost part of the Sun's atmosphere, seen as a white halo during an eclipse

Etymology

From Latin 'aureola' (golden), diminutive of 'aureus' (golden).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔːriˌoʊl / (AmE), / ˈɔːriəʊl / (BrE)
A circle or area of light appearing to surround the head or body of a holy figure, especially in art.
halo of light around holy figure

Examples

In traditional paintings, a golden aureole often surrounds the heads of saints.
The artist carefully painted an aureole behind the angel to symbolize divinity.
A faint aureole seemed to radiate from the statue's crown in the cathedral light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halo - a circle of light shown around the head of a saint or holy figure

Etymology

From Latin aureola (golden), diminutive of aureus (golden).
