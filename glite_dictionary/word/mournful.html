mournful

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɔrn.fəl / (AmE), / ˈmɔːn.fəl / (BrE)
feeling, showing, or expressing sadness or sorrow
expressing sadness or sorrow

Examples

There was a mournful silence after the announcement of her passing.
The music had a mournful tone that brought tears to many listeners.
He looked at the empty chair with a mournful expression on his face.

Synset Examples

the child's doleful expression
stared with mournful eyes
mournful news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sad - experiencing or showing sorrow; unhappy
  • Antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From English 'mourn' (to grieve), from Old English 'murnan' (to grieve, lament) + '-ful' (full of).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɔrn.fəl / (AmE), / ˈmɔːn.fəl / (BrE)
expressing or suggesting sadness or sorrow
expressing sadness or sorrow

Examples

She gave a mournful sigh as she looked at the empty playground.
The mournful music made everyone feel the depth of his sorrow.
His mournful expression spoke louder than words.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorrowful - feeling or showing sorrow
  • Antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From Old French *morne* (sad, dismal), from Latin *maurī*, influenced by 'mourn'.
