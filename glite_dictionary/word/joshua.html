Joshua

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɑːʃuə / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒʃuə / (BrE)
(in the Old Testament) The leader of the Israelites after Moses, who led them into the Promised Land and is known for the destruction of Jericho.
successor of Moses in the Old Testament

Examples

According to the Bible, Joshua led the Israelites after Moses' death.
The story of Joshua and the battle of Jericho is taught in many Sunday schools.
Some parents name their sons Joshua after the biblical leader.

Etymology

From Hebrew Yĕhōshūa‘, meaning 'Yahweh is salvation'.

Synset POS: n

2. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɑːʃuə / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒʃuə / (BrE)
the sixth book of the Old Testament, describing how Joshua led the Israelites into Canaan after Moses' death
book of the Old Testament about Joshua

Examples

Many of the battles described in Joshua are central to biblical history.
We studied the story of Jericho in the book of Joshua during Sunday school.
Joshua recounts how the Israelites crossed the Jordan into Canaan.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Judges - the book following Joshua in the Old Testament
  • Degree Lower: Deuteronomy - the book preceding Joshua in the Old Testament

Etymology

From Hebrew Yehoshua, the name of the biblical figure; also the title of the biblical book.
