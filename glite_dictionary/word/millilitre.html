millilitre

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlɪˌlitər / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪˌliːtə / (BrE)
A metric unit of volume equal to one thousandth of a litre (1 ml = 0.001 litres).
metric unit of volume (0.001 litre)

Examples

The recipe called for 200 millilitres of water.
Each vial contains exactly 5 millilitres of serum.
A standard teaspoon holds about 5 millilitres of liquid.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: liter - metric unit of volume (US spelling: liter)
  • Synonym: cubic centimeter - metric unit of volume equal to one millilitre
  • Degree Lower: microlitre - metric unit of volume equal to one millionth of a litre

Etymology

From French 'millilitre', from 'milli-' (thousandth) + 'litre'.
