Northumbria

1. PROPN

/ nɔrˈθʌmbriə / (AmE), / nɔːˈθʌmbriə / (BrE)
a former Anglo-Saxon kingdom in northern England that existed until 876
historic Anglo-Saxon kingdom in England

Examples

The ruins of monasteries in Northumbria reveal much about early medieval life.
Vikings invaded Northumbria in the 9th century, significantly altering its history.
Bede, the famous historian, was born in the Kingdom of Northumbria.

Etymology

From Old English 'Norðymbre' meaning 'the people or territory north of the Humber'.
