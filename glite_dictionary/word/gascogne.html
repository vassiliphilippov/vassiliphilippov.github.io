Gascogne

1. PROPN

/ ɡæsˈkɔɪn / (AmE), / ɡasˈkɔn / (BrE)
a region in southwestern France, known for its distinct culture, history, and cuisine
region of southwestern France

Examples

They spent their summer traveling through Gascogne and enjoying local food.
The wines from Gascogne have become increasingly popular in recent years.
Gascogne is famous for its picturesque villages and rolling countryside.

Etymology

From French 'Gascogne', from Latin 'Vasconia', originally referring to the land of the Basques.
