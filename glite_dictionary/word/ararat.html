Ararat

1. PROPN

/ ˈærəˌræt / (AmE), / ˈærəræt / (BrE)
a mountain in eastern Turkey traditionally considered the landing place of Noah's Ark according to the Bible
mountain traditionally linked with Noah's Ark

Examples

According to the Book of Genesis, Noah's Ark came to rest on Ararat.
Mount Ararat is visible from miles around and is a symbol of Armenian identity.
Scientists have conducted expeditions to Ararat in search of evidence of the Ark.

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Ararat', via Latin and Greek, referring to a region mentioned in the Bible.
