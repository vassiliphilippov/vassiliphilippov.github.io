spud

1. NOUN

/ spʌd / (AmE), / spʌd / (BrE)
a sharp, narrow-bladed tool used for digging out roots and weeds, especially in gardening
tool for digging out roots and weeds

Examples

He used a spud to remove the stubborn dandelion from the garden.
After sharpening his trusty spud, Jim tackled the thick roots under the hedge.
The gardener reached for her spud to quickly dig up the weeds.

Etymology

Possibly from Scandinavian languages; compare Norwegian 'spyd' (spear) and Swedish 'spjut'.

2. NOUN

/ spʌd / (AmE), / spʌd / (BrE)
(informal) A potato, especially as a common food crop.
informal term for potato
informal

Examples

He always has a baked spud for lunch at the cafeteria.
Farmers harvested tons of spuds this season.
Would you like some spud chips with your sandwich?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potato - the starchy edible tuber, staple food

Etymology

Originally a term for a digging tool; later transferred to the root vegetable (mid-19th century, British slang).

3. VERB

/ spʌd / (AmE), / spʌd / (BrE)
to initiate drilling operations, especially for oil or gas wells.
begin drilling a well (esp. oil/gas)

Examples

The company plans to spud a new well early next year.
After securing the permits, the team spudded the first exploratory well.
Environmental regulations must be considered before you can spud a site for drilling.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
