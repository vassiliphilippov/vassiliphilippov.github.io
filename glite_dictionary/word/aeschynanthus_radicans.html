Aeschynanthus radicans

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːskaɪˈnænθəs ˈrædɪkənz / (AmE), / ˌiːskaɪˈnænθəs ˈrædɪkænz / (BrE)
A Malaysian epiphytic or creeping plant with fleshy leaves and bright red flowers, often grown as a houseplant.
Malaysian plant with red flowers, houseplant

Examples

The Aeschynanthus radicans is commonly called the lipstick plant due to its tubular red flowers.
Many gardeners love to display Aeschynanthus radicans in hanging baskets indoors.
Because it thrives in humid conditions, Aeschynanthus radicans is perfect for bathrooms and kitchens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lipstick plant - Common name for Aeschynanthus radicans, grown for ornamental flowers

Etymology

From Greek aischynein (to be ashamed) + anthos (flower), referring to the flower's appearance; Latin radicans, from radix (root), meaning 'rooting'.
