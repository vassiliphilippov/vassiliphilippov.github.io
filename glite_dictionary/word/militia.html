militia

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɪʃə / (AmE), / mɪˈlɪʃə / (BrE)
the whole body of civilians who are not in the regular army but are eligible by law for military service and may be called to serve in emergencies
body of civilians eligible for military service

Examples

At that time, all able-bodied men were considered part of the militia.
The government has the power to call up the militia in times of national emergency.
Debate continues about whether a modern society still needs a militia.

Synset Examples

their troops were untrained militia
Congress shall have power to provide for calling forth the militia

Related Terms

  • Antonym: army - the organized military force of a nation

Etymology

From Latin militia (military service), from miles (soldier).

2. NOUN

/ məˈlɪʃə / (AmE), / mɪˈlɪʃə / (BrE)
A military force composed of ordinary citizens trained as soldiers but not part of the regular professional army; often called to service in emergencies.
civilian military force

Examples

The new governor called up the militia to help maintain order during the protests.
During the revolution, volunteers joined the local militia to defend their community.
Members of the militia received only basic training, unlike regular soldiers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: army - the regular, professional military force of a country
  • Synonym: national guard - military reserve force made up of civilians, especially in the US

Etymology

From Latin militia (military service), from miles (soldier).
