Stichaeidae

1. NOUN

/ stɪˈkiːɪˌdi / (AmE), / stɪˈkiːɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of slender, elongated marine fishes known as pricklebacks, found primarily in cold waters of the Northern Hemisphere.
family of marine fishes (pricklebacks)

Examples

Several species within the Stichaeidae family are well adapted to rocky coastal environments.
The Stichaeidae are distinguished by their long, eel-like bodies and small fins.
Researchers collected specimens from the Stichaeidae family to study their ecological roles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prickleback - a common name for fish in the Stichaeidae family

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Stichaeus and the suffix -idae used for animal families in taxonomy.
