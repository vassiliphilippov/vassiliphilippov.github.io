smell

1. NOUN

/ smɛl / (AmE), / smɛl / (BrE)
the ability of the nose or brain to detect and identify different scents or odors
ability to detect scents

Examples

Dogs have a much stronger sense of smell than humans do.
She lost her smell after having a bad cold.
The disease can affect a person's ability to smell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taste - the faculty of perceiving flavors
  • Synonym: sight - the faculty or power of seeing

Etymology

From Middle English smellen (to perceive by the nose), of uncertain origin.
