Burhinus

1. NOUN

/ bɜːrˈhaɪnəs / (AmE), / bɜːˈhɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of wading birds in the family Burhinidae, commonly known as stone curlews, found in various regions including Europe, Africa, and Australia.
genus of stone curlews (wading birds)

Examples

The genus Burhinus includes several species of nocturnal wading birds.
Scientists have observed Burhinus species nesting on open ground in grasslands.
The distinctive call of Burhinus birds can be heard during summer nights.

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from Greek 'bous' (ox) and 'rhinos' (nose), referring to the shape of the bill.
