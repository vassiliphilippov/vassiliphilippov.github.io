Lepiota clypeolaria

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛpiˈoʊtə klaɪpiəˈlɛəriə / (AmE), / ˌlɛpɪˈəʊtə ˌklɪpɪəˈlɛːrɪə / (BrE)
A species of agaric mushroom characterized by a ragged stem and a soft, woolly (floccose) cap.
a species of agaric mushroom

Examples

The forest floor was scattered with specimens of Lepiota clypeolaria during autumn.
Mycology students identified the Lepiota clypeolaria by its ragged stalk and distinct cap.
Foragers are cautioned not to confuse Lepiota clypeolaria with edible mushroom species.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name 'Lepiota', from Greek 'lepis' (scale) + specific epithet 'clypeolaria', referencing a shield-like shape.
