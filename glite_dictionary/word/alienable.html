alienable

1. ADJ

/ ˈeɪ.li.ən.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.li.ə.nə.bəl / (BrE)
(of property or a right) able to be transferred to another owner.
able to be transferred to another owner

Examples

Under the terms of the contract, the property is fully alienable.
Certain rights are not alienable and cannot be given or sold to others.
In many legal systems, land is considered alienable unless exemptions apply.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inalienable - not able to be transferred to another owner

Etymology

From Latin alienabilis, from alienare (to make another's, to transfer).
