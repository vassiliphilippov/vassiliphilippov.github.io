concurrently

1. ADV

/ kənˈkɝː.ənt.li / (AmE), / kənˈkʌr.ənt.li / (BrE)
at the same time as something else; overlapping in duration
at the same time; simultaneously

Examples

The two projects were developed concurrently to maximize efficiency and resource sharing.
She was enrolled in two degree programs concurrently, which kept her very busy.
The software updates will run concurrently on all company computers.

Synset Examples

concurrently with the conference an exhibition of things associated with Rutherford was held
going to school and holding a job at the same time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simultaneously - at the same time as something else

Etymology

From Latin 'concurrere' meaning 'to run together'.
