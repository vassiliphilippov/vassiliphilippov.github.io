to overprotect

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərprəˈtɛkt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəprəˈtɛkt / (BrE)
to protect someone too much, especially by preventing them from experiencing difficulties or taking risks
protect someone excessively

Examples

Some parents tend to overprotect their children, not allowing them to walk to school alone.
He accused his sister of trying to overprotect him after his accident.
If you overprotect your pets, they may become anxious in new situations.

Synset Examples

She fusses over her husband

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: to protect - to keep someone or something safe from harm

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'over-' meaning 'excessively' and 'protect'.

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərprəˈtɛkt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəprəˈtɛkt / (BrE)
to protect someone too much, often in a way that prevents them from becoming independent or learning from experience
protect someone excessively

Examples

Some parents tend to overprotect their children, not allowing them to take any risks.
You shouldn't overprotect your dog; a little dirt won't hurt him.
Teachers can overprotect students by not letting them solve problems on their own.

Synset Examples

Don't overprotect your son — he is an adult now!

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: to protect - to keep someone or something safe from harm or danger

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (excessively) + 'protect'
