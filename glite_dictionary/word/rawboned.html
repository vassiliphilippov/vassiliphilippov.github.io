rawboned

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɔˌboʊnd / (AmE), / ˈrɔːˌbəʊnd / (BrE)
having a lean and bony physique, especially in reference to a person or animal
having a lean and bony body

Examples

The rawboned man looked older than his years, with sharp cheekbones and long limbs.
A rawboned horse trotted slowly across the dusty field.
Despite his rawboned appearance, he could outwork men twice his size.

Synset Examples

a rawboned cow hand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaunt - very thin, especially from hunger or illness
  • Antonym: stocky - broad and sturdily built

Etymology

Formed from 'raw' + 'boned', meaning 'having bones prominently visible, as if the flesh were raw'.
