Otaria

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈtɛriə / (AmE), / əʊˈtɑːriə / (BrE)
A genus of sea lions in the family Otariidae, including the South American sea lion.
genus of South American sea lions

Examples

The Otaria genus is known for its large and noisy colonies along South American coasts.
Researchers observed several behaviors unique to Otaria during their expedition.
The South American sea lion, Otaria flavescens, is a key species in its ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea lion - a large marine mammal of the family Otariidae

Etymology

From Greek ὠτάριον (ōtárion), diminutive of οὖς (ous), meaning 'ear', referring to the external ears of these animals.

2. NOUN

/ oʊˈtɛəriə / (AmE), / əʊˈtɑːriə / (BrE)
A genus of eared seals in the family Otariidae, including the South American sea lion.
genus of eared seals (sea lions)

Examples

The South American sea lion belongs to the genus Otaria.
Marine biologists study behavioral patterns within Otaria populations.
Otaria is a key genus used to illustrate diversity in the Otariidae family.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Otariidae - the eared seal family to which Otaria belongs

Etymology

From Latin Otaria, formed from Greek 'ot-' (ear) referring to the external ears of these seals.
