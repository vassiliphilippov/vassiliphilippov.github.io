Glossopsitta versicolor

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɑːsəʊˈsɪtə vɜːr.sɪˌkʌl.ər / (AmE), / ɡlɒs.əʊˈsɪt.ə ˌvɜː.sɪˈkʌl.ə / (BrE)
A species of lorikeet native to northern Australia, known for its brightly colored plumage.
a brightly colored Australian lorikeet

Examples

The Glossopsitta versicolor is renowned for its striking mix of red, green, and yellow feathers.
Researchers observed a flock of Glossopsitta versicolor feeding on eucalyptus blossoms in northern Australia.
The vibrant appearance of the Glossopsitta versicolor makes it popular among birdwatchers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lorikeet - a small, brightly colored parrot

Etymology

Glossopsitta: from Greek 'glossa' (tongue) + 'psitta' (parrot); versicolor: Latin for 'variously colored.'
