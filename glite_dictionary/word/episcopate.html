episcopate

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈpɪskəˌpeɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪskəpət / (BrE)
the office or authority of a bishop in the Christian Church
the office of a bishop

Examples

After years of devoted service, he was finally appointed to the episcopate.
The responsibilities of the episcopate require both spiritual wisdom and administrative ability.
Historically, reforms in the episcopate have shaped the direction of the Church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bishopric - the district or office under a bishop's control

Etymology

From Late Latin episcopatus (office of a bishop), from episcopus (bishop).

3. NOUN

/ ɪˈpɪs.kə.peɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪs.kə.pət / (BrE)
the collective body of bishops in a church or denomination
the group of all bishops

Examples

The Roman Catholic episcopate met to discuss reforms within the church.
Several members of the Anglican episcopate voiced their opinions on the matter.
Throughout history, the episcopate has played a central role in church governance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bishop - a senior member of the Christian clergy, typically in charge of a diocese

Etymology

From Late Latin episcopātus (office of a bishop), from episcopus (bishop).
