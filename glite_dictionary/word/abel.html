Abel

1. PROPN

/ ˈeɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈeɪbəl / (BrE)
(in the Bible) the second son of Adam and Eve, murdered by his brother Cain.
second son of Adam and Eve in the Bible

Examples

Biblical scholars often discuss the story of Abel and his brother Cain in relation to morality.
In literature, Abel is frequently depicted as an innocent or righteous character.
The sacrifice offered by Abel was favored by God, leading to Cain's jealousy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Cain - the first son of Adam and Eve, who killed Abel

Etymology

From Hebrew Hevel, a personal name of uncertain meaning.
