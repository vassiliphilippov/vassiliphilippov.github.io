adz

1. NOUN

/ ædz / (AmE), / ædz / (BrE)
A tool with a curved blade at right angles to the handle, used for shaping wood.
edge tool for shaping wood

Examples

The carpenter used an adz to shape the rough timber into smooth planks.
Ancient tools such as the adz and the axe were essential for early construction.
He bought a new adz for his woodworking workshop.

Etymology

From Old English *adesa*, origin uncertain; variant spelling 'adze' is also common.
