euro-trance

1. NOUN

/ ˌjʊəroʊˈtræns / (AmE), / ˌjʊərəʊˈtræns / (BrE)
A style of electronic trance music originating in Europe, combining elements of hard trance and Eurodance.
European style of trance music

Examples

Many clubs in the late 1990s featured euro-trance nights that attracted large crowds.
The DJ played a mix of euro-trance and hardhouse during his set.
Modern festivals often revisit classic euro-trance tracks for nostalgic fans.

Synset Examples

Euro-Trance emerged as a hybrid of Hard Trance and Eurodance music and was most popular between late 1998 and 2000.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trance - A genre of electronic dance music characterized by repetitive rhythms and melodies.

Etymology

Formed from 'Euro-' (referring to Europe) + 'trance' (a genre of electronic music).
