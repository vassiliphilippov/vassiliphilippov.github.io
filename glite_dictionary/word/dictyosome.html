dictyosome

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪktioʊˌsoʊm / (AmE), / ˈdɪktɪəˌsəʊm / (BrE)
a netlike structure in the cytoplasm of animal cells, especially those producing secretions, functioning as part of the Golgi apparatus
structure in animal cell cytoplasm (Golgi complex)
medical

Examples

Under the microscope, the dictyosome appeared as a series of flattened sacs in the glandular cell.
Proteins are modified and packaged by the dictyosome before leaving the cell.
Scientists observed changes in the structure of the dictyosome during cell division.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Golgi apparatus - cellular organelle involved in secretion and intracellular transport

Etymology

From Greek diktyon (net) + soma (body).
