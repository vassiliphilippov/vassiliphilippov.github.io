exarch

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛg.zɑrk / (AmE), / ˈɛk.sɑːk / (BrE)
a governor or viceroy of a large province in the later Roman or Byzantine Empire, especially one with both civil and military authority.
governor of a Roman or Byzantine province

Examples

The exarch exercised both political and military power in the province.
During the Byzantine era, the exarch of Ravenna held significant authority.
An exarch was responsible for maintaining order in frontier provinces.

Etymology

From Greek exarchos (leader), from ex- (out) + archein (to rule).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛɡ.zɑrk / (AmE), / ˈɛk.sɑːk / (BrE)
A bishop in Eastern Christianity ranking below a patriarch but above a metropolitan.
bishop below a patriarch in Eastern Christianity

Examples

The exarch presided over several dioceses in the region.
An exarch holds significant authority in the hierarchy of Eastern Orthodox churches.
During the synod, the exarch presented a report to the patriarch.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: patriarch - a chief bishop in certain Christian churches
  • Degree Lower: metropolitan - a bishop of higher rank or office below an exarch in Eastern Christianity

Etymology

From Greek exarchos (leader, ruler), from ex- (out) + archein (to rule).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.sɑrk / (AmE), / ˈɛk.sɑːk / (BrE)
a bishop in certain Eastern Orthodox Churches, especially one with authority over other bishops in a particular region or outside the primary territory
Orthodox Church bishop with regional authority

Examples

The patriarch appointed a new exarch to oversee the Orthodox congregations in the Americas.
An exarch holds significant responsibility within the administrative structure of the Church.
After years of service, he was elevated to the position of exarch by the Holy Synod.

Etymology

From Greek exarchos (leader, chief), from ex- (out) + archein (to rule).
