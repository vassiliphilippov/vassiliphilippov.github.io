overtly

1. ADV

/ oʊˈvɝtli / (AmE), / əʊˈvɜːtli / (BrE)
In a way that is open and not secret or hidden; openly or visibly.
openly; not secretly

Examples

The politician stated his position overtly during the debate, leaving no room for doubt.
She overtly challenged the company's decision in a public letter.
Their marketing campaign was overtly aimed at young adults.

Synset Examples

he did it overtly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: covertly - in a secret or hidden manner

Etymology

Formed from 'overt' (from Old French overt, past participle of 'ouvrir,' meaning 'to open') + '-ly' adverbial suffix.
