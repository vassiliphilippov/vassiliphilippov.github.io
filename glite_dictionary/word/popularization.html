popularization

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑpjələrəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɒpjʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making something widely accepted or appealing to the general public
act of making something widely accepted

Examples

The popularization of science has helped make complex topics accessible to everyone.
Through careful popularization, new technologies gain acceptance among everyday consumers.
The popularization of jazz music in the early 20th century changed the cultural landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: promotion - the act of supporting or actively encouraging something

Etymology

From 'popular' + '-ization'; ultimately from Latin 'populus' (people).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːpjələrəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɒpjʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making an idea, subject, or interpretation easily understood and accepted by a wide audience
making something widely understandable

Examples

The popularization of science has made complex topics accessible to everyone.
His popularization of ancient philosophy attracted widespread public interest.
Many documentaries contribute to the popularization of environmental issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simplification - the process of making something simpler or easier to do or understand

Etymology

From 'popularize' + '-ation'; 'popularize' from French 'populariser', based on Latin 'populus' (people).

Synset POS: n
