iconoclastic

1. ADJ

/ aɪˌkɑnəˈklæstɪk / (AmE), / aɪˌkɒnəˈklæstɪk / (BrE)
Characterized by attacking or strongly rejecting established beliefs, traditions, or institutions.
attacking established beliefs or traditions

Examples

The artist is known for her iconoclastic views that challenge the conventions of modern art.
His iconoclastic essays provoked heated debates among traditional scholars.
She developed an iconoclastic approach to education, questioning accepted methods and policies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conventional - following traditional beliefs or practices
  • Synonym: heretical - holding opinions contrary to established beliefs

Etymology

From Greek eikonoklastēs (image destroyer), originally referring to religious image breakers.

2. ADJ

/ ˌaɪkəˈnæklæstɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪkəˈnæklæstɪk / (BrE)
characterized by opposition to the use of religious images; rejecting or destroying icons, especially in a religious context
opposing religious images

Examples

The early Islamic tradition was distinctly iconoclastic, forbidding the depiction of living beings in religious art.
Some Protestant reformers took an iconoclastic approach, destroying religious statues and images.
The iconoclastic movement in Byzantium opposed the veneration of icons in churches.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: iconoclastic - attacking or ignoring cherished beliefs and traditions
  • Antonym: idolatrous - characterized by worship or excessive devotion to idols or images

Etymology

From Greek eikonoklastēs (image breaker), from eikōn (image) + klan (to break).
