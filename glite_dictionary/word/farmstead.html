farmstead

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːrm.stɛd / (AmE), / ˈfɑːm.stɛd / (BrE)
A farmhouse and the buildings and land immediately surrounding it, forming a farm's main site.
main buildings and land of a farm

Examples

The old farmstead was surrounded by fields of wheat and corn.
They renovated the barns on the farmstead to create a charming guesthouse.
Generations of the family have lived on this farmstead since the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homestead - a house, especially a farmhouse, with surrounding buildings and land

Etymology

From 'farm' + 'stead', where 'stead' comes from Old English 'stede' meaning 'place'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɑrm.stɛd / (AmE), / ˈfɑːm.stɛd / (BrE)
A farm and its buildings, typically including the farmhouse and surrounding outbuildings.
farm with its buildings

Examples

The old farmstead has been in the family for generations.
After the storm, the farmstead needed significant repairs to the barn and sheds.
They dreamed of buying a rural farmstead and living off the land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farmhouse - the main house on a farm

Etymology

Formed from 'farm' + 'stead', with 'stead' meaning 'place' or 'site'.
