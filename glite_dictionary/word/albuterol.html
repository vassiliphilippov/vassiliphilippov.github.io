albuterol

1. NOUN

/ ælˈbjuːtəˌrɔl / (AmE), / ælˈbjuːtərɒl / (BrE)
a medication used to relax the muscles in the airways and increase airflow to the lungs, commonly used to treat asthma and other respiratory conditions
medication for asthma and respiratory conditions
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed albuterol to help manage her asthma symptoms.
You should carry your albuterol inhaler with you at all times in case of an attack.
Some patients may experience shakiness after using albuterol.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salbutamol - alternative name for albuterol, mainly used in British English
