closed-ring

1. ADJ

/ kloʊzd rɪŋ / (AmE), / kləʊzd rɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a molecule) having atoms joined by chemical bonds to form a ring structure.
having atoms joined in a ring (chemistry)

Examples

Benzene is a classic example of a closed-ring compound in organic chemistry.
The closed-ring structure gives the molecule unique chemical properties.
Cyclohexane forms a stable closed-ring configuration.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open-chain - (of a molecule) having atoms joined in a linear or branched chain, not a ring
