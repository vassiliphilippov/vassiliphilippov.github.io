pyridoxal

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪrɪˈdɑksəl / (AmE), / ˌpɪrɪˈdɒksəl / (BrE)
a form of vitamin B6 that is essential for the metabolism of amino acids and starch in the body
a form of vitamin B6
medical

Examples

The enzyme requires pyridoxal as a coenzyme to facilitate amino acid metabolism.
Low levels of pyridoxal can lead to neurological problems in some individuals.
Scientists measured the concentration of pyridoxal in the blood samples during the study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitamin B6 - a group of related compounds including pyridoxine, pyridoxal, and pyridoxamine, essential for metabolism
  • Synonym: pyridoxine - another form of vitamin B6
  • Synonym: pyridoxamine - another form of vitamin B6

Etymology

From pyridine (a chemical compound) + -oxal (from 'oxidized' and 'aldehyde').
