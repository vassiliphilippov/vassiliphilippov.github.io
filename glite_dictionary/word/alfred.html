Alfred

1. PROPN

/ ˈæl.frəd / (AmE), / ˈæl.frɪd / (BrE)
(849–899) King of Wessex who defended England against Viking invasions and promoted the use of English in writing.
King of Wessex, defended against Vikings

Examples

Alfred became known as one of England's greatest early kings.
Many view Alfred as the savior of Anglo-Saxon culture.
The reign of Alfred marked a turning point in English history.

Etymology

From Old English "Ælfrēd", meaning 'elf counsel'.
