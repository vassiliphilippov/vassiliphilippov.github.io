postcode

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊstˌkoʊd / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstkəʊd / (BrE)
a group of letters and numbers that forms part of a postal address, used to help sort and deliver mail
postal code used for sorting mail
british

Examples

Please write your postcode clearly on the envelope.
He moved to a new area with a different postcode.
The company database is sorted by postcode for efficient delivery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zip code - postal code used in the United States

Etymology

Formed from 'post' (mail) and 'code', first used in the UK in the 1950s.
