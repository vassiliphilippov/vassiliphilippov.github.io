Nostocaceae

1. NOUN

/ nɑːˌstɑːˈkeɪsiː / (AmE), / nɒˌstɒˈkeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of cyanobacteria (blue-green algae) that live in colonies or filaments, commonly found in freshwater environments
family of blue-green algae (cyanobacteria)

Examples

The Nostocaceae are important contributors to nitrogen fixation in aquatic ecosystems.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Nostocaceae family.
Many Nostocaceae colonies form jelly-like masses in freshwater ponds.

Etymology

From the genus Nostoc, named by Paracelsus, plus the family suffix -aceae in scientific Latin.
