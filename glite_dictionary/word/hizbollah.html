Hizbollah

1. NOUN

/ hɪzˈbɑlɑ / (AmE), / hɪzˈbɒlɑ / (BrE)
A Shiite Islamist political and militant group based in Lebanon, with close ties to Iran, known for its armed operations and political influence.
Lebanese Shiite Islamist group

Examples

The UN has called on Hizbollah to disarm and participate only in Lebanon’s political process.
Many Western governments have designated Hizbollah as a terrorist organization.
Hizbollah has a strong presence in southern Lebanon and influences much of the country's politics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hezbollah - Alternate spelling for the same organization

Etymology

From Arabic ḥizb allāh, meaning 'party of God'.
