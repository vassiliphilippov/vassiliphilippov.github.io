seducer

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈdusər / (AmE), / sɪˈdjuːsə / (BrE)
a man who persuades women to have sexual relations, often by charm or deceit
man who seduces women

Examples

He was known as a charming seducer who effortlessly won the affection of many women.
The story centers around a notorious seducer whose actions eventually led to his downfall.
Despite his reputation as a seducer, he claimed to have genuinely loved each woman he met.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temptress - a woman who entices men into sexual activity
  • Synonym: seductress - a woman who seduces someone, especially a man
  • Antonym: prude - a person who is easily shocked by matters relating to sex

Etymology

From Latin seducere (to lead aside, entice).

2. NOUN

/ sɪˈdusər / (AmE), / sɪˈdjuːsə / (BrE)
a person who entices others into wrongdoing or immoral behavior, often by means of persuasion or temptation
person who entices others into wrongdoing

Examples

The cult leader proved to be a skillful seducer of the vulnerable members of the community.
Many tales warn against the charms of a seducer who can lead honest people astray.
He was branded a seducer after convincing his friends to take part in the illegal scheme.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tempter - a person who tempts others to do something wrong
  • Synonym: corrupter - a person who causes others to become morally depraved or corrupt

Etymology

From Latin seducere (to lead aside, tempt).
