Arctostaphylos uva-ursi

1. NOUN

/ ɑrktoʊˌstæfɪˌloʊs ˌjuːvəˈɜrsaɪ / (AmE), / ɑːktəʊˌstæfɪˌləʊs ˌjuːvəˈɜːsʌɪ / (BrE)
An evergreen, mat-forming shrub native to North America and northern Eurasia, with small white flowers and red berries; its leaves turn red in autumn.
evergreen shrub with red berries

Examples

The low-growing Arctostaphylos uva-ursi provided a dense ground cover in the alpine garden.
Botanists studied the medicinal properties of Arctostaphylos uva-ursi leaves used in traditional remedies.
In autumn, the leaves of Arctostaphylos uva-ursi turn a vivid red, adding color to forest floors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bearberry - common name for Arctostaphylos uva-ursi

Etymology

From Greek arktos (bear) + staphyle (grape), and Latin uva-ursi (bear's grape).
