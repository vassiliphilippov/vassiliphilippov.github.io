clock

1. NOUN

/ klɑk / (AmE), / klɒk / (BrE)
a device for measuring and showing the time of day, typically with hands that move over a dial or with a digital display
device that shows the time

Examples

The old grandfather clock in the hallway still works perfectly.
She glanced at the wall clock and realized she was late.
Please set the clock to the correct time before you leave.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watch - a portable timepiece, typically worn on the wrist
  • Synonym: timer - device for measuring specific amounts of time

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *clocke* (bell), from Medieval Latin *clocca* (bell, clock).

2. VERB

/ klɑk / (AmE), / klɒk / (BrE)
to measure or record the time taken by someone or something, especially in a race or timed event
to measure or record time taken

Examples

She clocked the runners' times during the marathon.
The car clocked a record speed on the track this afternoon.
Every swimmer was clocked precisely down to the hundredth of a second.

Synset Examples

he clocked the runners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: time - to measure the duration of something
