acanthopterygian

1. NOUN

/ əˌkænθəpˈtɛrɪdʒiən / (AmE), / əˌkanθɒpˈtɛrɪdʒɪən / (BrE)
A teleost fish whose fins are supported by sharp, inflexible spines or rays.
teleost fish with spiny fins

Examples

The acanthopterygian lineage includes many familiar marine species found in reefs and open waters.
Ichthyologists study the unique dorsal fin spines characteristic of acanthopterygian fishes.
A close examination of the aquarium revealed an exotic acanthopterygian among the other tropical fish.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: soft-rayed fish - a teleost fish with fins supported by soft, flexible rays

Etymology

From Greek 'akantha' (spine, thorn) + 'pterygion' (fin, diminutive of 'pteryx', meaning wing or fin).
