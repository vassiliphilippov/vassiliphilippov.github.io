Nag Hammadi

1. PROPN

/ nɑɡ hɑˈmɑdi / (AmE), / næɡ hæˈmædi / (BrE)
a collection of thirteen ancient papyrus codices discovered near the town of Nag Hammadi in Egypt in 1945, containing important early Christian and Gnostic texts translated from Greek into Coptic
ancient manuscripts discovered in Egypt (1945)

Examples

The discovery of the Nag Hammadi codices provided scholars with new insights into early Christian and Gnostic beliefs.
Many of the texts found at Nag Hammadi had been lost for centuries and are invaluable to historians of religion.
Researchers continue to study the Nag Hammadi manuscripts to better understand ancient religious thought.

Etymology

Named after Nag Hammadi, the Egyptian town near which the manuscripts were found.
