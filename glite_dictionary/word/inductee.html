inductee

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌdʌkˈti / (AmE), / ɪnˌdʌkˈtiː / (BrE)
a person who is formally admitted as a member of an organization or group
person formally admitted to a group

Examples

The museum will honor each new inductee at a special ceremony next month.
As the youngest inductee in the sports hall of fame, she inspired many fans.
All inductees received certificates to commemorate their achievements.

Synset Examples

Ty Cobb and Babe Ruth were 1936 inductees in the National Baseball Hall of Fame

Related Terms

  • Synonym: member - a person who belongs to a group or organization
  • Antonym: initiator - a person who introduces someone to membership

Etymology

From 'induct', based on Latin 'inductus', meaning 'brought in, introduced'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˌdʌkˈti / (AmE), / ɪnˌdʌkˈtiː / (BrE)
a person who is formally admitted to a group or organization, especially one who is drafted into military service.
person admitted or drafted, especially into military

Examples

After receiving his letter, the nervous inductee reported for basic training.
Every new inductee must complete orientation before joining the unit.
The army ceremony honored each inductee as they took their oath of service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conscript - a person compulsorily enrolled for military service
  • Antonym: volunteer - a person who willingly joins the military

Etymology

Formed from 'induct' + '-ee', where 'induct' is from Latin 'inductus', past participle of 'inducere' (to lead in).
