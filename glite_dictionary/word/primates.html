Primates

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪˌmeɪts / (AmE), / ˈprɪˌmeɪts / (BrE)
an order of mammals that includes lemurs, tarsiers, monkeys, apes, and humans, characterized by large brains, forward-facing eyes, and grasping hands.
an order of mammals including monkeys, apes, and humans

Examples

All humans, monkeys, and apes belong to the order Primates.
Scientists study Primates to understand evolutionary biology and behavior.
Lemurs, one of the earliest Primates, are native to Madagascar.

Etymology

From Latin 'prīmātes', plural of 'prīmās' (principal, of the first rank).
