insensible

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsɛnsəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛnsəbəl / (BrE)
unable to feel or respond to physical sensation; unconscious or unresponsive to external stimuli.
unresponsive or unconscious

Examples

He was found insensible on the floor after the accident.
The patient remained insensible despite repeated attempts to wake him.
After hours in the freezing cold, he became almost insensible to pain.

Synset Examples

he lay insensible where he had fallen
drugged and senseless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unconscious - not awake and aware of and responding to one's environment

Etymology

From Latin insensibilis, from in- (not) + sensibilis (perceptible by the senses).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00574798-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsɛnsəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛnsəbl / (BrE)
not aware of or concerned about something; indifferent or unresponsive to certain feelings, events, or stimuli
unaware or indifferent

Examples

He remained insensible to their pleas for help, showing no emotion at all.
Some leaders are insensible to public criticism and proceed with their plans regardless.
Despite the harsh comments, she seemed completely insensible to any offense.

Synset Examples

insensible to the suffering around him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unaware - not knowing or noticing something
  • Synonym: indifferent - having no particular interest or concern
  • Antonym: sensitive - quick to notice or be affected by slight changes

Etymology

From Latin 'insensibilis' (not sensitive, imperceptible), from 'in-' (not) + 'sensibilis' (perceptible).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02115107-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsɛnsəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛnsəbl / (BrE)
too small or gradual to be noticed or clearly perceived; barely perceptible.
too slight to be noticed; barely perceptible

Examples

The difference in weight was so insensible that most people could not detect it.
There was an insensible shift in his attitude over the years.
The temperature dropped by an insensible amount overnight.

Synset Examples

the transition was almost indiscernible
an almost insensible change

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imperceptible - impossible or difficult to perceive by the senses or mind

Etymology

From Latin 'insensibilis' (incapable of feeling), from 'in-' (not) + 'sensibilis' (perceptible).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01753420-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsɛnsəbl̩ / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛnsəbl̩ / (BrE)
incapable of perceiving or responding to physical sensations; without feeling or sensation.
incapable of physical sensation

Examples

The patient was insensible to the cold due to nerve damage.
After the accident, he lay insensible to pain.
An insensible object like a rock cannot feel heat or cold.

Synset Examples

insensible to pain
insensible earth

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sensitive - having or showing perception or responsiveness to physical sensation

Etymology

From Latin insensibilis (not feeling, imperceptible).
