Caesarea

1. PROPN

/ siːzəˈriːə / (AmE), / siːzəˈriːə / (BrE)
an ancient seaport in northwestern Israel, formerly an important Roman city in ancient Palestine
ancient seaport and Roman city in Israel

Examples

Tourists from around the world visit Caesarea to explore its impressive Roman ruins.
The amphitheater at Caesarea has been remarkably well preserved over the centuries.
Archaeologists discovered ancient mosaics near the harbor of Caesarea.

Etymology

From Latin 'Caesarea', named in honor of Julius Caesar.
