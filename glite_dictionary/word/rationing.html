rationing

1. NOUN

/ ˈræʃənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈræʃənɪŋ / (BrE)
the system or practice of controlling the supply of something, such as food or gasoline, by limiting the amount people can have, usually because of a shortage
controlled distribution of scarce resources

Examples

Rationing was introduced during the war to ensure fair distribution of scarce goods.
The government ended rationing once food supplies became more stable.
Strict rationing meant families had to carefully plan their meals each week.

Synset Examples

during the war the government imposed rationing of food and gasoline

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allocation - the action or process of distributing something
  • Antonym: hoarding - the act of accumulating and storing goods, often during shortages

Etymology

From the verb 'ration', which comes from Latin *ratio* (reckoning, calculation).
