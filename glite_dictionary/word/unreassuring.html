unreassuring

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnriˈʃʊrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌnrɪˈʃɔːrɪŋ / (BrE)
not making someone feel less worried or more confident; tending to cause anxiety or uncertainty
not making someone feel less worried

Examples

His tone was so unreassuring that it only made her more nervous.
The doctor gave me an unreassuring look before she left the room.
Waiting in the dark, I heard an unreassuring sound outside the window.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reassuring - making someone feel less worried or more confident
  • Antonym: comforting - making someone feel less worried or frightened

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' (not) to 'reassuring'.
