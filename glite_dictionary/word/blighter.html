blighter

1. NOUN

/ ˈblaɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈblaɪtə / (BrE)
a person who is persistently annoying or troublesome
annoying or troublesome person
british informal

Examples

He's such a blighter, always turning up when you least expect him.
That blighter next door plays loud music all night long.
I can't stand that blighter who keeps borrowing my tools and never returns them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuisance - a person or thing causing inconvenience or annoyance
  • Synonym: pest - an irritating or bothersome person

Etymology

From 'blight,' possibly influenced by its sense of something harmful; -er suffix forms nouns denoting persons associated with an action or quality.
