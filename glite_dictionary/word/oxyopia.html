oxyopia

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑk.siˈoʊ.pi.ə / (AmE), / ˌɒk.siˈəʊ.pi.ə / (BrE)
Exceptionally sharp or acute vision; unusually keen eyesight.
unusually acute vision
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with oxyopia, which allowed her to see fine details at a distance.
Artists with oxyopia may notice subtle distinctions in color and texture that others miss.
Despite its rarity, oxyopia can sometimes be found in people with unusually well-developed eyesight.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypermetropia - farsightedness; a different vision condition
  • Antonym: myopia - nearsightedness; opposite type of refractive error

Etymology

From Greek oxy- (sharp) + -opia (vision).
