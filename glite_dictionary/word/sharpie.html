sharpie

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɑrpi / (AmE), / ˈʃɑːpi / (BrE)
a shallow-draft sailboat with a sharp prow, flat bottom, and triangular sail, formerly used along the northern Atlantic coast of the United States
shallow-draft sailboat with triangular sail

Examples

The fishermen took their sharpie out into the sheltered bay to check their lobster traps.
Designed for shallow waters, the sharpie easily navigated the tidal flats.
She restored an old sharpie and sailed it along the Connecticut coast.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɑːrpi / (AmE), / ˈʃɑːpi / (BrE)
a professional card player who cheats in order to win money
a professional card cheat

Examples

The casino hired new staff to catch any sharpie trying to cheat at the tables.
Everyone at the poker table was wary of the rumored sharpie among them.
A skilled sharpie can make a fortune before anyone suspects foul play.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharper - a swindler, especially at cards

Etymology

Variant of 'sharper', meaning a swindler or cheat, originally from the word 'sharp' (clever, cunning).
