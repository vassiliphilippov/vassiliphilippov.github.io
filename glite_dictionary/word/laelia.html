Laelia

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.li.ə / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.li.ə / (BrE)
A genus of mostly epiphytic or lithophytic orchids native to Central and South America, known for their showy flowers.
genus of Central and South American orchids

Examples

The Laelia orchid thrives in the mountainous regions of Mexico and Central America.
Botanists often crossbreed Laelia with other orchid genera to produce new hybrids.
He proudly displayed a blooming Laelia on his windowsill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - a plant of the family Orchidaceae, known for diverse and colorful flowers

Etymology

From Latinized form of the Roman family name Laelia, used as a botanical genus name.

2. NOUN

/ ˈleɪliə / (AmE), / ˈliːlɪə / (BrE)
A genus of orchids native to Central and South America, known for their large, showy flowers in various colors.
a genus of brightly colored orchids

Examples

The Laelia orchid in her conservatory bloomed with a striking purple hue.
Botanists prize certain species of Laelia for their vibrant and long-lasting flowers.
He specializes in cultivating rare Laelia hybrids for orchid shows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - a plant of the family Orchidaceae, typically with complex flowers

Etymology

From Latin Laelia, named in honor of the Roman family Laelia.
