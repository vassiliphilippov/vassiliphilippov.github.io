jook-sing

1. NOUN

/ dʒʊkˈsɪŋ / (AmE), / dʒʊkˈsɪŋ / (BrE)
(offensive, sometimes derogatory) A person of Chinese descent who is raised outside Chinese culture, often perceived as lacking knowledge of Chinese language or traditions.
person of Chinese descent raised outside Chinese culture
offensive

Examples

Growing up as a jook-sing meant feeling caught between two cultures.
She was dismissed as a jook-sing by some of her relatives for not speaking Cantonese fluently.
Some community members resented being labeled jook-sing, finding the term insulting.

Synset Examples

As a jook-sing, she often struggled to fit in.
He felt like a jook-sing at the family reunion.
Being a jook-sing made her appreciate her culture more.

Etymology

From Cantonese 竹升 (juk1 sing1), literally 'bamboo pole', metaphorically referring to someone considered 'hollow' or lacking core cultural identity.
