watering

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of wetting or supplying with water, especially to plants or land
the act of supplying water

Examples

Regular watering is essential for keeping these houseplants healthy.
The schedule called for watering the garden twice a week during summer.
Without proper watering, the crops quickly wilted in the heat.

Synset Examples

the lawn needs a great deal of watering

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irrigation - the artificial application of water to land or soil
  • Synonym: watering - the action of providing water, as a verb

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔt̬.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɔː.tər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of fluid (such as tears) flowing from the eyes, often involuntarily
shedding tears; fluid from the eyes

Examples

High pollen counts can lead to watering of the eyes and frequent sneezing.
The spicy food caused intense watering that blurred his vision for a moment.
Allergy season is marked by symptoms like watering and itchy eyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tearing - shedding tears; the secretion of tears from the eyes
  • Antonym: dryness - lack of moisture; absence of tears or fluid in the eyes
