bystander

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪˌstændər / (AmE), / ˈbaɪˌstændə / (BrE)
a person who is present at an event or incident but does not take part
person present but not involved

Examples

A bystander called the police after witnessing the accident.
Several bystanders watched as the parade moved down the street.
He was just a bystander and had no involvement in the altercation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onlooker - a person who watches an event happening without taking part

Etymology

From by + stander (one who stands), dating from the early 18th century.
