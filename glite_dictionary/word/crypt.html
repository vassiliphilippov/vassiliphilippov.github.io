crypt

1. NOUN

/ krɪpt / (AmE), / krɪpt / (BrE)
an underground room or vault, especially beneath a church, used as a burial place
underground burial chamber

Examples

The ancient church still has a crypt where early bishops are buried.
After the service, we descended into the crypt to view the historic tombs.
The tour guide explained that the crypt had been sealed for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catacomb - an underground cemetery with recesses for tombs

Etymology

From Latin 'crypta', from Greek 'kryptē' (a hidden place).
