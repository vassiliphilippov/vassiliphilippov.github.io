unpleasantness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈplɛzəntnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈplɛzəntnəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being unpleasant or disagreeable; the experience of discomfort or displeasure
state of being unpleasant; discomfort

Examples

She was overwhelmed by a sense of unpleasantness when she entered the old, damp cellar.
The unpleasantness of the argument lingered long after it had ended.
Despite the general unpleasantness of the weather, they went ahead with their plans.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pleasantness - the quality of being pleasant or agreeable

Etymology

From 'unpleasant' (meaning disagreeable) plus suffix '-ness' (forming nouns denoting a state or quality).

2. NOUN

/ ʌnˈplɛzəntnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈplɛzəntnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being unpleasant or disagreeable
the state of being unpleasant

Examples

There was a definite unpleasantness in the tone of his voice.
The unpleasantness of the experience made her avoid that restaurant in the future.
They tried to ignore the unpleasantness between the two team members.

Synset Examples

the recent unpleasantness of the weather

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disagreeableness - the quality of being disagreeable or unpleasant
  • Antonym: pleasantness - the quality of being pleasant or agreeable

Etymology

Formed from 'unpleasant' (from un- + pleasant) and '-ness' (forming nouns).
