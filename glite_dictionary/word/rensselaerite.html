rensselaerite

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛn.səˈlɪr.aɪt / (AmE), / ˌrɛn.səˈlɪə.raɪt / (BrE)
A soft mineral variety of talc, occasionally used as a wood filler.
a soft mineral variety of talc

Examples

The craftsman used rensselaerite to fill small gaps in the wooden furniture.
Geologists identified the soft, soapy mineral as rensselaerite during their field survey.
Because rensselaerite is similar to talc, it can serve as an alternative in some industrial applications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talc - a soft mineral used in talcum powder and as a filler

Etymology

Named after Rensselaer County, New York, where it was first discovered, with the suffix -ite for minerals.
