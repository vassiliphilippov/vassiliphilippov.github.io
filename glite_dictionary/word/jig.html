jig

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪɡ / (AmE), / dʒɪɡ / (BrE)
A type of fishing lure with one or more hooks, typically moved up and down in the water to attract fish.
fishing lure jerked in water

Examples

The fisherman attached a colorful jig to his line before casting into the river.
Using a weighted jig, she caught several bass near the lake's edge.
Many anglers prefer a jig when fishing in deep water for walleye.
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ dʒɪɡ / (AmE), / dʒɪɡ / (BrE)
to dance a lively dance with quick, leaping and kicking motions, especially to traditional music
to dance a lively, leaping dance

Examples

After hearing the lively music, she couldn't help but jig around the room.
The children jigged merrily at the summer festival.
He was jigging to the cheerful rhythm of the fiddle when I arrived.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skip - to move with light, bouncing steps

Etymology

Perhaps from Old French 'giguer' (to jump, dance) or from Middle English 'gigge' (a kind of dance).

3. NOUN

/ dʒɪɡ / (AmE), / dʒɪɡ / (BrE)
a lively dance tune, especially in compound time, traditionally used for dancing a jig
lively dance music in compound time

Examples

The band played a traditional Irish jig that got everyone clapping along.
We learned to dance the jig at the folk music festival last weekend.
Her fingers flew over the strings as she performed a lively jig on the violin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reel - a lively Scottish or Irish folk dance, also the music for such a dance

Etymology

From Old French *giguer* (to dance), probably of Germanic origin.

device for guiding tools in machine work

A specialized jig ensures each component is cut to exactly the right shape.

5. NOUN

/ dʒɪɡ / (AmE), / dʒɪɡ / (BrE)
a lively old dance, typically with rapid movements, kicking, and leaping, popular in rural areas and traditional music
lively old rustic dance

Examples

At the festival, everyone joined in the energetic jig by the bonfire.
She learned a traditional Irish jig from her grandmother.
The musicians played a fast jig that got the whole crowd dancing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reel - a lively Scottish or Irish folk dance; often paired with the jig
  • Antonym: waltz - a ballroom dance in triple time

Etymology

Perhaps from Old French *gigue* (a lively dance), of uncertain origin.
