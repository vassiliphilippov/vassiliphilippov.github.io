Hardenbergia comnptoniana

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɑrdnˈbɜrdʒiə kɒmpˈtoʊniænə / (AmE), / ˌhɑːdnˈbɜːdʒɪə kɒmpˈtəʊnɪənə / (BrE)
A vigorous climbing plant native to Western Australia, known for its dense clusters of bright rose-purple flowers.
Australian flowering climbing plant

Examples

The Hardenbergia comnptoniana brightened the garden with its vivid rose-purple flowers.
Gardeners in Western Australia often choose Hardenbergia comnptoniana for its attractive climbing habit.
In spring, the Hardenbergia comnptoniana produces dense racemes that add color to forested areas.

Etymology

Genus name from the botanist Franziska, Countess von Hardenberg; species epithet commemorates Lady Compton.
