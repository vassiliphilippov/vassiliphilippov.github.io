Nb

1. NOUN

/ ɛn bi / (AmE), / ɛn bi / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for niobium, a soft, grey, ductile metallic element used in alloys
chemical symbol for niobium

Examples

The alloy contains a small amount of Nb to improve its strength at high temperatures.
Researchers measured the conductivity of Nb thin films for superconductivity studies.
Nb is commonly used in the manufacture of special steel alloys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: niobium - a soft grey metallic element, symbol Nb, atomic number 41

Etymology

From New Latin 'niobium', named after Niobe, daughter of Tantalus in Greek mythology; symbol 'Nb' chosen from the element's name.
