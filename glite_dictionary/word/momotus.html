Momotus

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈmoʊtəs / (AmE), / məʊˈməʊtəs / (BrE)
A genus of brightly colored birds in the motmot family (Momotidae), native to Central and South America.
genus of motmot birds

Examples

The Momotus genus includes several species of motmot found in tropical forests.
Researchers observed a Momotus in its natural habitat during the field study.
The colorful plumage of Momotus birds makes them popular among birdwatchers.

Etymology

Modern Latin, from Greek 'mōmotēs', a kind of bird.

2. NOUN

/ moʊˈmoʊtəs / (AmE), / məʊˈməʊtəs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Momotidae, known as motmots, native to Central and South America.
a genus of birds (motmots)

Examples

The blue-crowned motmot belongs to the genus Momotus and is found throughout Central America.
Researchers have discovered a new species within the Momotus genus in the Amazon rainforest.
Members of the Momotus genus are easily recognized by their racket-shaped tail feathers.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin *Momotus*, used in scientific classification for this group of birds.
