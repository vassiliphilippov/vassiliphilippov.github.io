bookwoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊkˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈbʊkˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A woman who is very learned or scholarly, typically through extensive reading and study.
a scholarly or learned woman

Examples

The university honored Dr. Lee as a distinguished bookwoman in the field of philosophy.
From a young age, she aspired to become a great bookwoman and contribute to scientific research.
Known as a bookwoman among her peers, she was always seen with a new novel or research paper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bookman - a learned or scholarly man
  • Synonym: scholar - a learned person, especially in academic or literary fields
