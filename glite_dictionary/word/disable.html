to disable

1. VERB

/ dɪˈseɪbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈseɪbəl / (BrE)
to make something or someone unable to function or perform a particular action
make unable to function or act

Examples

Please disable Bluetooth on your phone before takeoff.
The accident disabled the vehicle, leaving it stranded on the road.
You can disable notifications in the app settings if they are too distracting.

Synset Examples

disable this command on your computer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to enable - to make able to perform an action

Etymology

From Old French 'desabler', from Latin 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'habilis' (able).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00513267-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dɪˈseɪbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈseɪ.bəl / (BrE)
to injure someone so severely that they are unable to use part of their body or cannot function normally, often permanently
to injure so as to impair normal function

Examples

The accident disabled him for life, leaving him unable to walk.
Soldiers can be disabled by severe injuries sustained on the battlefield.
She was disabled in a skiing accident and required a wheelchair afterward.

Synset Examples

He was disabled in a car accident

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cripple - cause to lose the ability to move or function normaly

Etymology

From Old French 'desabler' or 'desabler' (to incapacitate), from Latin 'dis-' (not) + 'habilis' (able).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00091720-v
Synset POS: v
