yagi

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɑːɡi / (AmE), / ˈjɑːɡi / (BrE)
a type of sharply directional antenna consisting of multiple parallel elements in a line, used to receive or transmit radio waves
directional antenna for radio waves

Examples

The technician installed a yagi on the roof to improve the radio reception.
A yagi antenna is often used by amateur radio enthusiasts for long-distance communication.
Because of its directionality, a yagi is ideal for targeting specific transmission sources.

Etymology

Named after Hidetsugu Yagi (1886–1976), Japanese electrical engineer who co-invented the antenna.
