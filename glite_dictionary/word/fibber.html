fibber

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪbər / (AmE), / ˈfɪbə / (BrE)
A person who tells lies, especially trivial or harmless ones.
person who tells small lies

Examples

Don't be such a fibber; we all know you didn't finish your homework.
My little brother is a frequent fibber when it comes to eating vegetables.
She laughed and admitted she was a bit of a fibber about her age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liar - a person who tells lies

Etymology

From 'fib', a late 16th-century English word meaning a lie, of uncertain origin, with '-er' forming an agent noun.
