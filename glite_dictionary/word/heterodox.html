heterodox

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɛt̬.ə.rəˌdɑks / (AmE), / ˈhɛt.ər.əˌdɒks / (BrE)
not conforming with established or accepted beliefs, especially in religion or doctrine
not conforming to accepted beliefs

Examples

His heterodox opinions often sparked lively debates at academic conferences.
She was criticized for her heterodox approach to traditional religious practices.
The scientist proposed a heterodox theory that challenged accepted explanations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orthodox - conforming to established beliefs or practices

Etymology

From Greek heterodoxos (of another opinion), from heteros (other) + doxa (opinion).
