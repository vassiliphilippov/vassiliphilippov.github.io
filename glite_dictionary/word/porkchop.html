porkchop

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrkˌtʃɑp / (AmE), / ˈpɔːkˌtʃɒp / (BrE)
A thick slice of pork, usually including a rib or part of a vertebra, cut from a pig and cooked as food.
chop of pork with bone

Examples

She ordered a grilled porkchop with mashed potatoes for dinner.
Each porkchop was seasoned with fresh herbs before being cooked on the barbecue.
The recipe calls for one thick porkchop per person.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lamb chop - a chop cut from a lamb
  • Synonym: pork - the flesh of a pig used as food

Etymology

From 'pork' (from Old French 'porc', from Latin 'porcus', pig) + 'chop' (a cut of meat).
