ha-ha

1. NOUN

/ hɑˈhɑ / (AmE), / hɑːˈhɑː / (BrE)
a loud laugh that sounds like a horse neighing or is especially boisterous
a loud, neighing laugh

Examples

She burst out with a ha-ha that echoed across the room.
His ha-ha made everyone at the table look up in surprise.
You could hear his loud ha-ha from the hallway.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑ hɑ / (AmE), / ˈhɑː hɑː / (BrE)
A ditch with one side as a vertical retaining wall, designed to divide land or prevent access without blocking the view.
landscape ditch with one vertical wall

Examples

The old manor house was bordered by a ha-ha, keeping livestock away without spoiling the view.
She nearly stumbled into the ha-ha that separated the gardens from the parkland.
The landscape architect suggested installing a ha-ha for an unobstructed vista.

Etymology

From French (surprise expression upon encountering it); first used in 18th-century English landscaping.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɑ hɑ / (AmE), / ˈhɑː hɑː / (BrE)
A ditch with one side being a retaining wall, designed to divide land or mark a boundary without obstructing the view or defacing the landscape.
landscape ditch with hidden retaining wall

Examples

The estate's boundary was marked by a ha-ha instead of a visible fence.
A ha-ha allows for an uninterrupted view of the gardens from the house.
Visitors admired the clever design of the ha-ha, which kept livestock out without spoiling the landscape.

Etymology

From French *ha-ha*, an exclamation expressing surprise, referencing the sudden drop.
