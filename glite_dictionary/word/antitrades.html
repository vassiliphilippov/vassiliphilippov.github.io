antitrades

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.tiˌtreɪdz / (AmE), / ˈantɪˌtreɪdz / (BrE)
winds in the upper atmosphere that blow above but in the opposite direction to the trade winds
winds opposite to the trade winds

Examples

Scientists observed that the antitrades significantly influenced the formation of upper-atmosphere clouds.
Pilots must consider the antitrades when planning long-distance flights across the equator.
The antitrades often flow in a direction opposite to the prevailing trade winds near the Earth's surface.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: trade winds - steady winds blowing toward the equator from the northeast in the Northern Hemisphere or the southeast in the Southern Hemisphere

Etymology

Formed from 'anti-' (against) + 'trades', referring to the trade winds.
