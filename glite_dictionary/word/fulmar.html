fulmar

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊlˌmɑr / (AmE), / ˈfʊlmɑː / (BrE)
a large, heavy oceanic bird with a short tail, found in polar and subpolar regions, related to the petrel family
large polar oceanic bird

Examples

A single fulmar soared over the icy waters, searching for food.
During our trip to the Arctic, we spotted colonies of nesting fulmars on steep cliffs.
The fulmar is known for its stiff-winged flight and distinctive tube-shaped nostrils.

Etymology

From Old Norse 'fúll' (foul) + 'már' (gull), referring to the bird's smell.
