defectiveness

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɛktɪvnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛktɪvnəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being defective or flawed
the state of being defective

Examples

The defectiveness of the product led to numerous customer complaints.
Engineers are studying the defectiveness found in several batches of materials.
His argument was weakened by the defectiveness of the evidence presented.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imperfection - the state of being imperfect
  • Antonym: soundness - the state of being whole or in good condition

Etymology

From 'defective' + '-ness', forming a noun indicating state or quality.
