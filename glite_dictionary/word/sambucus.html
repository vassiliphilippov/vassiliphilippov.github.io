Sambucus

1. NOUN

/ sæmˈbjuːkəs / (AmE), / sæmˈbjuːkəs / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs and small trees commonly known as elder or elderberry, producing clusters of white flowers and small, dark berries.
genus of shrubs (elderberry)

Examples

The Sambucus plant is valued for its medicinal properties and edible berries.
Gardeners often grow Sambucus for its attractive flowers and foliage.
The berries of Sambucus are used to make syrups, jams, and wines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elderberry - the small dark fruit of the Sambucus plant
  • Synonym: elder - a shrub or tree of the Sambucus genus

Etymology

From Latin 'sambucus' (elder tree).

2. NOUN

/ sæmˈbjuːkəs / (AmE), / samˈbjuːkəs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering shrubs and small trees, commonly known as elders or elderberries, found in temperate and subtropical regions and noted for their clusters of small white or cream flowers and dark berries.
genus of shrubs called elders/elderberries

Examples

The berries of Sambucus are often used in traditional remedies and syrups.
Many gardeners plant Sambucus for its attractive flowers and foliage.
Some species within the genus Sambucus are native to North America and Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elderberry - the berry or plant from the genus Sambucus
  • Synonym: elder - any shrub or tree of the genus Sambucus

Etymology

From Latin *sambucus* (elder tree).
