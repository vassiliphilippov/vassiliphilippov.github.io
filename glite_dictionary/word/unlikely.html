unlikely

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈlaɪkli / (AmE), / ʌnˈlaɪkli / (BrE)
not likely to happen or be true; having a very low probability or credibility.
not likely to happen or be true

Examples

It is unlikely that it will snow in July in this region.
Her explanation sounded highly unlikely to the investigators.
The team made an unlikely comeback in the final minutes of the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improbable - not likely to be true or to occur
  • Antonym: likely - having a high probability of occurring
  • Degree Higher: possible - able to be done or to happen

Etymology

From un- (not) + likely, dating from the 16th century.

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈlaɪkli / (AmE), / ʌnˈlaɪkli / (BrE)
Not probable to happen or be true; having little chance of occurring.
not likely to happen or be true

Examples

It is unlikely that we will finish the project by tomorrow.
Her explanation sounded highly unlikely to the committee.
They made an unlikely pair, but their partnership was a success.

Synset Examples

an unlikely story
an unlikely candidate for reelection
a butcher is unlikely to preach vegetarianism

Related Terms

  • Antonym: likely - probable; having a good chance of occurring
  • Synonym: improbable - not likely to be true or to happen
  • Degree Higher: impossible - cannot happen or be true

Etymology

From un- (not) + likely, influenced by Old Norse líkligr (likely).
