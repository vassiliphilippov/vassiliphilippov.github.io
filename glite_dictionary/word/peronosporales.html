Peronosporales

1. NOUN

/ pɛrəˌnɑspəˈreɪliz / (AmE), / pɛrəˌnɒspəˈreɪliːz / (BrE)
An order of chiefly parasitic lower fungi, including the families Albuginaceae, Peronosporaceae, and Pythiaceae.
order of parasitic fungi

Examples

The Peronosporales are responsible for many plant diseases, such as downy mildew.
Researchers examined the life cycle of several species within the Peronosporales.
Farmers must monitor crops for signs of infection by fungi belonging to the Peronosporales.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Peronospora (a genus); originally from Greek 'peronē' (pin) + 'spora' (seed, spore).

2. NOUN

/ pɛr.əˌnɑːs.pɔːˈreɪ.liːz / (AmE), / pɛr.əˌnɒs.pɔːˈreɪ.liːz / (BrE)
an order of chiefly parasitic lower fungi, including the families Albuginaceae, Peronosporaceae, and Pythiaceae
order of parasitic lower fungi

Examples

Species in the Peronosporales order are responsible for some devastating plant diseases.
Many agricultural crops are vulnerable to pathogens from the Peronosporales.
The taxonomy of Peronosporales has undergone revisions with advances in molecular biology.

Etymology

From modern Latin Peronosporales, based on Peronospora (a type genus) + -ales (denoting an order in taxonomy).
