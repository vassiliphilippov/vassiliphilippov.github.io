Svengali

1. NOUN

/ svɛnˈɡɑːli / (AmE), / svɛnˈɡɑːli / (BrE)
A person who exercises a controlling or mesmeric influence on another, often for sinister purposes.
manipulative, controlling person

Examples

The author accused his editor of being a Svengali who controlled every aspect of the novel's direction.
In the pop star's early career, her manager played the role of Svengali, orchestrating her public image.
Some believe the politician has a Svengali behind the scenes guiding every decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puppet master - someone who controls others behind the scenes
  • Synonym: manipulator - a person who controls or influences others in a clever or unscrupulous way
  • Antonym: victim - a person harmed, influenced, or dominated by another

Etymology

From the character Svengali in George du Maurier's 1894 novel "Trilby," who controlled the heroine through hypnosis.

2. NOUN

/ svɛnˈɡɑli / (AmE), / svɛnˈɡɑːli / (BrE)
A person who exercises a controlling or hypnotic influence over another, especially for sinister purposes; originally the musician in a novel by George du Maurier who hypnotically controlled Trilby.
manipulative, controlling person; du Maurier character

Examples

He became a sort of Svengali, shaping the actor's public image at every turn.
Many believe the manager acted as a Svengali, controlling the young singer's career entirely.
In press reports, he was labeled a Svengali due to his manipulative influence over the band.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puppetmaster - a person who manipulates others, often behind the scenes

Etymology

From the name of a character in George du Maurier's novel 'Trilby' (1894).
