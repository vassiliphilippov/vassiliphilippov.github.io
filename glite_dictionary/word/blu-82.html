BLU-82

1. NOUN

/ bi ɛl ju eɪ tiː / (AmE), / biː ɛl juː eɪ tuː / (BrE)
a large, powerful conventional bomb weighing 15,000 pounds, designed to explode just above ground with a wide blast radius; used notably by the US military during the late 20th and early 21st century
large US conventional bomb (15,000-pound)

Examples

The BLU-82 was used to clear landing zones in dense jungle during the Vietnam War.
News reports detailed the deployment of the BLU-82 in Afghanistan for its devastating impact on enemy positions.
Military historians often describe the BLU-82 as one of the largest conventional bombs ever used.

Etymology

Acronym for Bomb Live Unit-82, used as a US military designation.
