dwojniak

1. NOUN

/ dvɔˈɲjak / (AmE), / dvɔˈɲjak / (BrE)
A traditional Polish mead made using equal parts of honey and water, resulting in a sweet, strong beverage.
Polish mead made with equal honey and water

Examples

Many meaderies offer dwojniak as a specialty drink at Polish festivals.
The dwojniak is prized for its rich flavor and higher honey content compared to other meads.
We sampled a glass of dwojniak during our trip to Warsaw.

Etymology

From Polish 'dwojniak', from 'dwa' (two), indicating the ratio of ingredients.
