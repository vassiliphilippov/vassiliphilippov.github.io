Stylophorum

1. NOUN

/ staɪˈlɑfəˌrʌm / (AmE), / staɪˈlɒfərəm / (BrE)
A small genus of perennial herbaceous plants in the poppy family (Papaveraceae), commonly called wood poppies, native to eastern North America and East Asia.
a genus of wood poppies

Examples

The leaves of Stylophorum are deeply lobed and soft to the touch.
Gardeners value Stylophorum for its bright yellow spring flowers.
Stylophorum thrives in shady, woodland gardens with moist, rich soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chelidonium - a related genus in the poppy family (greater celandine)

Etymology

From Greek 'stylos' (pillar, style) and 'phero' (to bear), referring to the prominent style in the flower.

2. NOUN

/ staɪˈlɒfərəm / (AmE), / staɪˈlɒfərəm / (BrE)
a genus of herbaceous plants, commonly known as wood poppies, native to eastern North America and eastern Asia, with yellow flowers and lobed leaves.
a genus of wood poppies

Examples

The bright yellow Stylophorum flowers bloom in the woodlands every spring.
Gardeners appreciate Stylophorum for its attractive foliage and vibrant color.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Stylophorum in traditional herbal remedies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chelidonium - a related genus of flowering plants in the poppy family

Etymology

From Greek 'stylos' (pillar, style) and 'phoros' (bearing), referring to the prominent style in the flower.
