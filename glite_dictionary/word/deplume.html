to deplume

1. VERB

/ dɪˈplum / (AmE), / dɪˈpluːm / (BrE)
to remove the feathers from a bird
remove feathers from a bird

Examples

The cook began to deplume the duck before preparing it for dinner.
Farm hands deplumed the chickens as part of the butchering process.
She learned how to deplume game birds during her culinary training.

Synset Examples

pull a chicken
pluck the capon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pluck - to pull the feathers off a bird

Etymology

From French 'déplumer', from Latin 'deplumare' (to pluck feathers).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01386929-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dɪˈplum / (AmE), / dɪˈpluːm / (BrE)
to strip someone or something of honors, possessions, or distinguishing attributes
strip of honors or attributes

Examples

After the scandal, the committee decided to deplume him of his titles and privileges.
Witnessing his fall from grace, his peers watched as he was deplumed of every honor he held.
The organization was deplumed of its awards after the corruption revelations came to light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgrace - to bring shame or loss of honor
  • Synonym: strip - to remove possessions or honors

Etymology

From Latin 'de-' (down from) and 'pluma' (feather), via French 'déplumer' (to pluck feathers).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02320028-v
Synset POS: v
