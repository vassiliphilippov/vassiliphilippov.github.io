Pimenta dioica

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈmɛntə daɪˈoʊɪkə / (AmE), / pɪˈmɛntə dɪˈəʊɪkə / (BrE)
an evergreen aromatic tree native to the West Indies, producing berries known as allspice
aromatic tree producing allspice

Examples

The leaves of Pimenta dioica release a spicy aroma when crushed.
Jamaican cuisine often uses the dried berries of Pimenta dioica as allspice.
Pimenta dioica trees thrive in the warm, humid climate of the Caribbean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allspice - the dried berry produced by Pimenta dioica, used as a spice

Etymology

From the genus name Pimenta, from Portuguese 'pimenta' (pepper); 'dioica' from Greek 'dioikos', meaning 'of two houses', referring to plants with male and female flowers on separate individuals.
