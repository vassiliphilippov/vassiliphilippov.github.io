to ambulate

1. VERB

/ ˈæm.bjə.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈæm.bjʊ.leɪt / (BrE)
to walk from place to place, especially as opposed to being bedridden or unable to walk
to walk, especially after illness
medical

Examples

After her surgery, the doctor encouraged her to ambulate as soon as possible.
Some patients are unable to ambulate without assistance from a nurse or therapist.
He began to ambulate the hospital halls daily during his recovery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walk - to move at a regular pace by lifting and setting down each foot in turn

Etymology

From Latin ambulare (to walk).
