muzzy

1. ADJ

/ ˈmʌzi / (AmE), / ˈmʌzi / (BrE)
indistinct or blurred in outline; lacking clear definition or sharpness.
indistinct or blurred in outline

Examples

The photo was so muzzy that I couldn't recognize the faces.
Through the fog, the buildings appeared muzzy and unreal.
His vision became muzzy after staying up all night working.

Synset Examples

a landscape of blurred outlines
the trees were just blurry shapes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blurry - having an indistinct or fuzzy outline
  • Antonym: sharp - having a clear and distinct outline

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly related to 'muzzy' meaning confused or dull.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmʌzi / (AmE), / ˈmʌzi / (BrE)
unable to think clearly; confused or vague, especially in mind or thought.
confused or unclear in thinking

Examples

After taking the medicine, she felt a bit muzzy and couldn't concentrate on her work.
His muzzy thoughts made it difficult to follow the conversation at the meeting.
I always wake up muzzy after a late night out.

Synset Examples

muddleheaded ideas
your addled little brain
woolly thinking
woolly-headed ideas

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clear-headed - thinking clearly and rationally
  • Synonym: woolly-headed - having confused or vague ideas; not clear in thought

Etymology

Perhaps from dialectal English *muzzy* (dull, confused), of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00437830-s
Synset POS: s
