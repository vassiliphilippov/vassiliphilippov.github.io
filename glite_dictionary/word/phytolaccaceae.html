Phytolaccaceae

1. NOUN

/ faɪtəˌlækəˈsiː.i / (AmE), / fɪtəʊˌlækəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
A family of chiefly tropical plants, including herbaceous species, shrubs, and trees, characterized by racemose flowers; includes genera such as Phytolacca and Rivina.
family of tropical plants with racemose flowers

Examples

The Phytolaccaceae family includes several species known for their racemose flower arrangements.
Research into the Phytolaccaceae has uncovered new information about tropical plant evolution.
Some traditional medicines utilize extracts from plants in the Phytolaccaceae family.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Phytolacca ('pokeweed') + -aceae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ faɪtoʊˌlækəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / faɪtəlækəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
A family of chiefly tropical herbaceous plants, including shrubs and trees, characterized by racemose flowers; includes genera such as Phytolacca, Agdestis, Ercilla, Rivina, and Trichostigma.
tropical plant family with racemose flowers

Examples

Members of the Phytolaccaceae family are commonly found in tropical regions around the world.
The pokeweed, belonging to the Phytolaccaceae, can be toxic if not prepared correctly.
Botanists studied the racemose flowers characteristic of Phytolaccaceae during their fieldwork.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus *Phytolacca*, from Greek *phyton* (plant) and Latinized *lacca* (lac, resin).
