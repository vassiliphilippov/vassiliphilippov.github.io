Glaucium

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɔʊsiəm / (AmE), / ˈɡlaʊsiəm / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the poppy family, known as horned poppies, native to Europe, North Africa, and Asia
a genus of horned poppy plants

Examples

The Mediterranean coast is home to several species of Glaucium.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of Glaucium extracts.
A yellow-flowered Glaucium grew wild along the sandy dunes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horned poppy - a common name for plants in the genus Glaucium

Etymology

From Latin *glaucium*, from Ancient Greek *glaúkion* (a kind of poppy).
