rappel

1. NOUN

/ rəˈpɛl / (AmE), / raˈpɛl / (BrE)
a descent down a vertical surface, such as a cliff or wall, using a rope anchored above and wrapped around the body for control, especially in mountaineering or climbing
a descent down a cliff or wall using a rope

Examples

She prepared for the final rappel down the steep rock face.
The most thrilling part of the hike was a hundred-foot rappel into the canyon.
Each climber took turns making the rappel as the sun began to set.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abseil - to descend a rock face or other near-vertical surface by using a rope

Etymology

From French 'rappeler' (to recall, pull back), referring to the pulling back of the rope.

2. VERB

/ rəˈpɛl / (AmE), / raˈpel / (BrE)
to descend a vertical surface, such as a cliff or wall, by sliding down a rope while controlling the speed with specialized equipment
descend a surface using a rope

Examples

The climber decided to rappel down the steep rock face rather than take the longer trail.
Before you rappel, always check that your harness and rope are secure.
She had never rappelled before but quickly learned the proper technique during the training session.

Synset Examples

The ascent was easy — roping down the mountain would be much more difficult and dangerous
You have to learn how to abseil when you want to do technical climbing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abseil - to descend by means of a rope, especially in climbing (mainly British term)

Etymology

From French *rappeler* (to recall, to pull back), adopted in English mountaineering terminology.

Source: WordNet
