Xenopus laevis

1. NOUN

/ ˌziːnəpəs ˈleɪvɪs / (AmE), / ˌziːnəpəs ˈleɪvɪs / (BrE)
A species of tongueless aquatic frog native to Africa, commonly known as the African clawed frog, used extensively in scientific research.
species of African clawed frog

Examples

The Xenopus laevis is widely used as a model organism in developmental biology.
Scientists introduced Xenopus laevis to new habitats after its use in pregnancy testing.
You can identify Xenopus laevis by its distinctive clawed toes and smooth skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: African clawed frog - common name for Xenopus laevis

Etymology

From Modern Latin, with 'Xenopus' meaning 'strange foot' from Greek, and 'laevis' meaning 'smooth' in Latin.
