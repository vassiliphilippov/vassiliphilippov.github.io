invariability

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌværiəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪnˌvɛərɪəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of remaining unchanged or constant; lack of variation or change.
lack of variation or change

Examples

The invariability of the weather in this region is remarkable, with little change from day to day.
He appreciated the invariability of her good mood, which made her easy to work with.
Scientific experiments depend on the invariability of certain fundamental constants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constancy - the quality of being unchanging or stable
  • Antonym: variability - the quality of being subject to change or variation

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + variabilis (able to be changed).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˌvæɹi.əˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ɪnˌvæɹɪ.əˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of not changing or being resistant to variation
the quality of not changing

Examples

The invariability of the laws of physics fascinates scientists and philosophers alike.
Her routine shows a remarkable invariability despite changes in her personal life.
The research demonstrated the invariability of the material's properties under extreme conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constancy - the quality of being unchanging or unwavering
  • Antonym: variability - the quality of being likely to change or vary

Etymology

From in- (not) + variability; based on Latin variabilis ('changeable').
