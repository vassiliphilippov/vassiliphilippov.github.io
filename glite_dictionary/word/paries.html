paries

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛəriˌiz / (AmE), / ˈpɛəriːz / (BrE)
(Anatomy) A wall or layer, such as a lining or membrane, that encloses or forms the wall of a bodily structure or cavity.
anatomical wall or membrane
medical

Examples

The mucous paries of the stomach secretes digestive enzymes.
Damage to the arterial paries can lead to serious health complications.
A thin paries separates the two chambers of the heart.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wall - the side of a hollow structure; anatomical wall

Etymology

From Latin *paries* (wall).
