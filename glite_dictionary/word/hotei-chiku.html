hotei-chiku

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˈteɪ tʃiːkuː / (AmE), / həʊˈteɪ tʃiːkuː / (BrE)
A species of small bamboo native to southeastern China, with slender, flexible young stems; often grown as an ornamental plant.
a type of small, flexible bamboo

Examples

The hotei-chiku in her garden added an exotic, graceful touch to the landscape.
Gardeners prize hotei-chiku for its slender culms and ornamental foliage.
As the wind blew, the young hotei-chiku bent without breaking, showing off its natural flexibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Buddha's-belly bamboo - Another name for hotei-chiku; a bamboo species with swollen nodes

Etymology

From Japanese ホテイチク (hoteichiku), referring to the plant, itself from Chinese origins.
