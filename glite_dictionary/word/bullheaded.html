bullheaded

1. ADJ

/ ˈbʊlˌhɛdɪd / (AmE), / ˈbʊlˌhɛdɪd / (BrE)
Unwilling to change one's mind or course of action; stubbornly and often foolishly obstinate.
stubbornly obstinate; pigheaded

Examples

Even though everyone advised him to reconsider, he remained bullheaded about his plans.
Her bullheaded refusal to compromise made negotiations difficult.
You can be so bullheaded sometimes—just listen to other opinions for once!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstinate - stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or action
  • Synonym: pigheaded - stubborn and unreasonable
  • Antonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas

Etymology

Formed from 'bull' (suggesting the animal's stubbornness) + 'headed' (having the quality of).
