full-fledged

1. ADJ

/ fʊl ˈflɛdʒd / (AmE), / fʊl ˈflɛdʒd / (BrE)
(of a bird) having reached full development with fully grown adult feathers and ready to fly.
(of a bird) fully grown, ready to fly

Examples

After weeks of care, the young robin is now a full-fledged bird ready to leave the nest.
The sanctuary releases only full-fledged owls back into the wild to ensure their survival.
When the chicks become full-fledged, their first flight is a remarkable sight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fledged - (of a bird) having the feathers needed to fly

Etymology

From 'fledge', meaning to acquire feathers necessary for flight; influenced by the word 'fledged'.

2. ADJ

/ fʊl ˈflɛdʒd / (AmE), / fʊl ˈflɛdʒd / (BrE)
Having reached the full status, development, or qualification; fully developed or established.
having full status or development

Examples

After years of study, she became a full-fledged architect licensed to design buildings on her own.
The internship helped Mark become a full-fledged member of the team.
It took several months of training before he was considered a full-fledged firefighter.

Synset Examples

a full-fledged lawyer
by the age of seventeen I was a full-fledged atheist
sees itself as a fully fledged rival party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fully fledged - having reached full status or development
  • Antonym: junior - not yet fully qualified or developed; lower in rank

Etymology

From ‘fledged’ meaning having grown feathers necessary for flight; ‘full-’ added for emphasis (19th century, American English).
