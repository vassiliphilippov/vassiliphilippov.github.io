dyne

1. NOUN

/ daɪn / (AmE), / daɪn / (BrE)
A unit of force in the centimetre-gram-second system, equal to the force needed to accelerate a mass of one gram at a rate of one centimetre per second squared.
unit of force (CGS system)

Examples

The dyne is used primarily in the CGS system of units.
One newton is equal to 100,000 dynes.
The force required was measured in dynes for the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newton - SI unit of force, equal to one kilogram metre per second squared

Etymology

From Greek 'dynamis' meaning 'power' or 'force'.
