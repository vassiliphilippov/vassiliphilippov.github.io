brackishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrækɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈbrækɪʃnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being slightly salty, especially as found in water that is a mixture of seawater and freshwater
the state of being slightly salty

Examples

The brackishness of the river made it unsuitable for drinking.
Scientists measured the brackishness to determine the health of the estuary.
Over time, increased brackishness has impacted the local ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salinity - the saltiness or amount of salt dissolved in water
  • Antonym: freshness - the quality of being fresh or unsalted, especially in water

Etymology

From 'brackish' (from Middle Dutch brac, 'salty') + '-ness'.
