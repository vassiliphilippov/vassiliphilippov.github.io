windblown

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪndˌbloʊn / (AmE), / ˈwɪndˌbləʊn / (BrE)
(of trees or plants) shaped or grown in a form determined by prevailing winds.
shaped by prevailing winds (esp. trees/plants)

Examples

The windblown trees along the shoreline leaned sharply away from the sea.
From the hilltop, we could see fields dotted with windblown pines and bent grasses.
Artists love to paint the windblown oaks that cling to the cliffs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windswept - (of a place or thing) exposed to and shaped by strong winds

Etymology

Formed from 'wind' and 'blown' (past participle of 'blow').
