weber

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈwiːbə / (BrE)
the SI unit of magnetic flux, equal to the magnetic flux that, linking a circuit of one turn, produces an electromotive force of one volt as it is reduced to zero at a uniform rate in one second
SI unit of magnetic flux

Examples

The strength of the magnetic field was measured in webers per square meter.
This coil generated a flux of one weber during the experiment.
A change of one weber per second induces a voltage of one volt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maxwell - CGS unit of magnetic flux

Etymology

Named after Wilhelm Eduard Weber (1804–1891), German physicist.
