Pteretis struthiopteris

1. NOUN

/ təˈrɛtɪs struːθiˈɑptərɪs / (AmE), / təˈrɛtɪs strʌθɪˈɒptərɪs / (BrE)
A tall fern of northern temperate regions, noted for its graceful, arched fronds and fertile fronds that resemble ostrich plumes.
species of tall fern (ostrich fern)

Examples

The graceful fronds of Pteretis struthiopteris add elegance to any shaded garden.
Pteretis struthiopteris is commonly found along riverbanks in northern temperate forests.
Gardeners often cultivate Pteretis struthiopteris for its ornamental, ostrich plume-like appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostrich fern - Common name for Pteretis struthiopteris, a tall fern with plume-like fronds

Etymology

From Greek 'pteretis' (kind of fern) and 'strouthiopteris' (ostrich feather fern), referencing the shape of the fronds.
