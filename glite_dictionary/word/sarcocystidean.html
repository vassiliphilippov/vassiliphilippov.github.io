sarcocystidean

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑrkoʊˌsɪsˈtɪdiən / (AmE), / ˌsɑːkəʊˌsɪsˈtɪdiən / (BrE)
a parasitic protozoan of the order Sarcocystidea that infects the muscles of vertebrates
muscle parasite of vertebrates (protozoan)
medical

Examples

The veterinarian identified the parasite as a sarcocystidean infecting the cattle's muscle tissue.
Research shows that sarcocystideans have a complex life cycle involving both intermediate and definitive hosts.
Under the microscope, a sarcocystidean can be seen forming cysts within the muscle fibers.

Etymology

From New Latin Sarcocystidea (order name), from Greek 'sarx, sarkos' (flesh) + 'kystis' (bladder, cyst) + '-idea' (order suffix).
