suppliant

1. ADJ

/ ˈsʌpliənt / (AmE), / ˈsʌplɪənt / (BrE)
Humbly asking or pleading for something; making a request with humility.
humbly requesting or pleading

Examples

She approached the judge in a suppliant manner, hoping for leniency.
His suppliant gestures made it clear he was desperate for forgiveness.
With suppliant eyes, the petitioner begged for a second chance.

Synset Examples

a suppliant sinner seeking forgiveness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pleading - asking earnestly or with emotion
  • Antonym: demanding - requiring or asking for something forcefully

Etymology

From Latin *supplicans* (begging, beseeching).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌpliənt / (AmE), / ˈsʌplɪənt / (BrE)
a person who asks for something earnestly and humbly, especially by prayer or petition.
a person making a humble request

Examples

The suppliant knelt before the altar, her hands clasped in silent prayer.
As a humble suppliant, he pleaded for mercy before the king.
Crowds of suppliants gathered at the temple, each hoping for a favorable answer.

Synset Examples

a suppliant for her favors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petitioner - a person who presents a formal request to an authority
  • Synonym: beggar - a person who asks others for charity, usually for money or food
  • Antonym: benefactor - a person who gives help or money, especially for a good cause

Etymology

From Latin suppliantem, present participle of supplicare (to kneel, entreat).
