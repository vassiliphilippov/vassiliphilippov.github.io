fussiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌsi nəs / (AmE), / ˈfʌsi nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being unnecessarily elaborate or detailed
unnecessary elaborateness in details

Examples

The fussiness of the ornate table setting made it difficult to find a place for each dish.
She disliked the fussiness of the new design, preferring something more minimal.
The report is full of fussiness that distracts from the main points.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: simplicity - the quality of being plain or uncomplicated
  • Synonym: elaboration - the process of adding more detail; the quality of being more developed

Etymology

From 'fussy' (over-detailed) + '-ness' (forming nouns of state or quality).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfʌsinəs / (AmE), / ˈfʌsinɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being easily upset, irritable, or difficult to please
the state of being easily upset or irritable

Examples

The baby's fussiness made it difficult for her parents to get any sleep.
He apologized for his fussiness over the minor details of the plan.
Increased fussiness can sometimes be a sign that a child is getting sick.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritability - the state of being easily annoyed or angered
  • Antonym: placidity - the state of being calm and not easily upset

Etymology

From 'fussy' + '-ness'. 'Fussy' is of uncertain origin, possibly imitative.

Source: WordNet
