medico

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdɪˌkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɪkəʊ / (BrE)
an informal term for a doctor or licensed medical practitioner
informal term for doctor
informal

Examples

After the accident, the medico on duty treated all the injuries quickly.
He's not just any medico; he's the leading heart specialist in the region.
During my stay in Italy, I had to consult a local medico for a minor illness.

Synset Examples

I felt so bad I went to see my doctor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doctor - a qualified practitioner of medicine
  • Synonym: physician - a person qualified to practice medicine

Etymology

From Italian 'medico' (doctor), from Latin 'medicus'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdɪˌkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɪˌkəʊ / (BrE)
a student who is attending medical school, especially in informal contexts
medical student (informal)
informal

Examples

The first-year medico struggled with his anatomy classes at university.
Several medicos volunteered at the free clinic to gain hands-on experience.
During finals week, every medico in the library seemed stressed but determined.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medical student - a person studying medicine at a medical school

Etymology

From Italian 'medico' (doctor), from Latin 'medicus' (physician).
