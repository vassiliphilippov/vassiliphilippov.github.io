landholder

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌhoʊldər / (AmE), / ˈlændˌhəʊldə / (BrE)
a person who owns or possesses land, especially real estate
person who owns land

Examples

The largest landholder in the county owns over 5,000 acres.
Disputes between the landholder and the tenants arose over boundaries.
As a landholder, she is responsible for maintaining the fences on her property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landowner - a person who owns land
  • Antonym: tenant - a person who rents land or property from a landlord

Etymology

From 'land' + 'holder', meaning one who holds land.
