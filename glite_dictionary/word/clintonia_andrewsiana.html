Clintonia andrewsiana

1. NOUN

/ klɪnˈtoʊniə ˌændruˈziænə / (AmE), / klɪnˈtəʊniə ˌændruːˈziːanə / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to California and Oregon, having a nearly leafless stalk topped by a cluster of red or reddish lavender flowers.
plant with clusters of red or reddish lavender flowers

Examples

The dense forests of northern California are home to Clintonia andrewsiana and other unique wildflowers.
Each spring, hikers eagerly await the vibrant blooms of Clintonia andrewsiana along the Pacific coastal trails.
Because Clintonia andrewsiana thrives in shady woodlands, it's a striking sight beneath the redwoods.

Etymology

Named after De Witt Clinton, American botanist and politician, and botanist John Andrews.
