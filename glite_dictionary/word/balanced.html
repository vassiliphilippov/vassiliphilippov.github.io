balanced

1. ADJ

/ ˈbælənst / (AmE), / ˈbælənst / (BrE)
being in a state where different elements are in the correct proportions or in a proper equilibrium
in proper equilibrium; well-proportioned

Examples

The chef prepared a balanced meal that included proteins, vegetables, and grains.
Her argument was fair and balanced, considering different perspectives on the issue.
A balanced budget helps ensure stability in government finances.

Synset Examples

the carefully balanced seesaw
a properly balanced symphony orchestra
a balanced assessment of intellectual and cultural history
a balanced blend of whiskeys
the educated man shows a balanced development of all his powers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unbalanced - not in a state of equilibrium; lacking proportion
  • Synonym: steady - firm and not likely to change, move, or fail

Etymology

From the past participle of 'balance', ultimately from Latin 'bilanx' (having two scales).
