Calla palustris

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.ə pəˈlʌs.trɪs / (AmE), / ˈkælə pəˈlʌstrɪs / (BrE)
A perennial plant found in wetlands and bogs of temperate regions, having small greenish flowers partly enclosed in a white spathe and red berries.
wetland plant with white spathe and red berries

Examples

The wet meadow was dotted with the distinctive white flowers of Calla palustris.
Researchers found that Calla palustris provides important shelter for amphibians in bog habitats.
Gardeners should note that Calla palustris prefers moist, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Etymology

From Latin, Calla (genus name) and palustris (of marshes).
