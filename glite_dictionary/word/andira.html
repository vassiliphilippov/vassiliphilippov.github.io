Andira

1. NOUN

/ ænˈdaɪrə / (AmE), / ænˈdɪərə / (BrE)
A small genus of evergreen trees native to tropical America and western Africa, known for their hard wood and some medicinal uses.
genus of tropical evergreen trees

Examples

The Andira species are commonly found in tropical rainforests of Brazil.
Some Andira trees are valued for their timber and medicinal properties.
Botanists have classified several new species within the Andira genus.

Etymology

From New Latin, the genus name, likely from South American indigenous languages.
