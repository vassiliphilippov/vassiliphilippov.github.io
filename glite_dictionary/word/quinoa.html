quinoa

1. NOUN

/ ˈkiːnwɑː / (AmE), / ˈkiːnwɑː / (BrE)
a South American plant whose small edible seeds are high in protein and used as a grain substitute in cooking
high-protein edible seed used as a grain

Examples

Many people include quinoa in salads for extra protein and texture.
After rinsing the quinoa, she cooked it with vegetables for a healthy dinner.
Gluten-free diets often recommend quinoa as a nutritious alternative to wheat.

Synset Examples

Quinoa is rich in protein and much lighter than other grains.
Place two parts water or stock to one part of quinoa in a saucepan over medium-high heat and bring to a boil.
The quinoa should look light and fluffy, and you should be able to see the germ separating from the seed.

Etymology

From Spanish 'quinoa', from Quechua 'kinwa' or 'kinua'.
