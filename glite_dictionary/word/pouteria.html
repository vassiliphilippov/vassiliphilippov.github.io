Pouteria

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈtɪəriə / (AmE), / paʊˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical American trees of the sapote family, some species of which have edible fruits such as the canistel
genus of tropical American trees (canistel, etc.)

Examples

The fruit of the Pouteria tree is commonly eaten in Central America.
Many species of Pouteria provide valuable timber as well as food.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of extracts from various Pouteria species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canistel - an edible yellow fruit from Pouteria campechiana

Etymology

From New Latin, named after French botanist Jean Baptiste Christophe Fusée Aublet 'Pouterie'.

2. NOUN

/ paʊˈtɪəriə / (AmE), / paʊˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical American trees in the family Sapotaceae, some species of which yield edible fruit and valuable timber
genus of tropical American trees

Examples

The Pouteria genus includes several species with edible fruit.
Botanists recently discovered a new Pouteria tree in the Amazon rainforest.
Many types of Pouteria are cultivated for their valuable timber and fruit.

Etymology

From Latinized form of a surname, Charles Poulter, an English botanist.
