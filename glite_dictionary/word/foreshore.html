foreshore

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊrˌʃɔːr / (AmE), / ˈfɔːʃɔː / (BrE)
the part of the seashore that lies between the high-water mark and the low-water mark
shore area between high and low tides

Examples

The foreshore was exposed at low tide, revealing pools and rocky outcrops.
Many legal disputes concern the ownership of the foreshore.
Birds searched for food along the foreshore as the tide went out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shore - the land along the edge of a sea or lake
  • Synonym: beach - an area of sand or pebbles along a shore

Etymology

From fore- (before) + shore, referring to the area at the front of the shore.
