Raffia

1. NOUN

/ ˈræfiə / (AmE), / ˈræfiə / (BrE)
A genus of feather palms native to tropical Africa, Madagascar, and the Americas, widely grown for commercial use, especially for their fibrous leaves.
genus of feather palms used commercially

Examples

The Raffia palm is harvested for its strong and flexible leaf fibers.
Producers in Madagascar rely heavily on Raffia for making baskets and mats.
Botanists are studying several species of the Raffia genus for their ecological benefits.

Etymology

From Malagasy 'rafia', the local name for the palm.
