Rhizopus nigricans

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪ.zə.pəs nɪˈɡrɪ.kænz / (AmE), / ˈraɪ.zə.pəs nɪˈɡrɪ.kənz / (BrE)
a species of mold in the genus Rhizopus, commonly found on decaying organic material and used in various industrial and laboratory processes.
species of mold in the genus Rhizopus
medical

Examples

Rhizopus nigricans is often found on decaying bread and other food sources left at room temperature.
Scientists used Rhizopus nigricans in their research to study fungal growth patterns.
The presence of Rhizopus nigricans in soil can affect the decomposition of organic matter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mold - a fungus that grows in the form of multicellular filaments called hyphae

Etymology

From Greek 'rhiza' (root) and Latin 'nigricans' (blackening or becoming black).
