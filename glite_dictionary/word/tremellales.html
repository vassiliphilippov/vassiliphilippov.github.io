Tremellales

1. NOUN

/ trəˌmɛˈleɪliz / (AmE), / trɪˌmɛˈleɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of fungi with fruiting bodies varying in texture from gelatinous to waxy or horny, most of which are saprophytic
an order of fungi with variable textures
medical

Examples

Fungi in the Tremellales are often identified by their jelly-like appearance in moist forests.
Researchers discovered several new species within the Tremellales during the recent expedition.
Members of the Tremellales play an important role in decomposing wood and plant material.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Tremella.

2. NOUN

/ trɛˌmɛlˈeɪliːz / (AmE), / trɛˌmɛlˈeɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of fungi that vary in texture from gelatinous to waxy or horny, with most being saprophytic.
an order of fungi, often gelatinous or waxy

Examples

Scientists discovered several new species within the Tremellales during their rainforest expedition.
The characteristic jelly-like fruiting bodies are a hallmark of the Tremellales order.
Most members of the Tremellales are saprophytic, feeding on decaying wood and plant material.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tremella - a genus of fungi within Tremellales, known for jelly-like fruiting bodies

Etymology

From New Latin, from the genus Tremella + -ales (taxonomic suffix for orders).
