haematocyturia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːməˌsaɪˈtjʊriə / (AmE), / ˌhiːmətəʊsaɪˈtjʊəriə / (BrE)
the presence of red blood cells in the urine
presence of red blood cells in urine
medical

Examples

A urinalysis revealed haematocyturia, prompting further investigation for kidney disease.
Persistent haematocyturia can be a sign of underlying urinary tract pathology.
The patient presented with pain and haematocyturia after the accident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hematuria - blood in the urine (general term)

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + kytos (cell) + ouron (urine).
