C2H6

1. NOUN

/ si ˈtu eɪtʃ ˈsɪksˈtin / (AmE), / si ˈtuː eɪtʃ ˈsɪksˈtin / (BrE)
a colorless, odorless alkane gas used as a fuel and industrial feedstock; also known as ethane
a colorless alkane gas (ethane)

Examples

Natural gas contains a significant proportion of C2H6 along with methane and propane.
The chemical industry uses C2H6 to produce ethylene through a process called steam cracking.
At room temperature, C2H6 exists as a colorless and odorless gas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethane - a colorless, odorless alkane gas (C2H6)
