frazzle

1. VERB

/ ˈfræzəl / (AmE), / ˈfræzəl / (BrE)
to wear away or damage something through constant rubbing or friction
wear away by rubbing

Examples

The repeated washing would eventually frazzle the fabric.
After months of use, the cables began to frazzle at the ends.
His sleeves started to frazzle from constant contact with the desk.

Synset Examples

The friction frayed the sleeve

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fray - to wear away or come apart by rubbing
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01371869-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈfræzəl / (AmE), / ˈfræzəl / (BrE)
a state of complete physical or mental exhaustion
state of extreme exhaustion

Examples

After working three double shifts, she was worn to a frazzle.
The stressful week left him near a complete frazzle.
You shouldn’t let the kids get you to a frazzle every day.

Synset Examples

he was worn to a frazzle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhaustion - a state of extreme physical or mental fatigue
  • Antonym: refreshment - state of being refreshed or restored

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly related to 'fray' in sense of wear out.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14041526-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈfræz.əl / (AmE), / ˈfræz.əl / (BrE)
to make someone extremely tired or stressed; to exhaust physically or emotionally
exhaust physically or emotionally

Examples

The long shift at the hospital completely frazzled her.
Running after three kids all day can really frazzle you.
Preparing for the big presentation frazzled his nerves to the limit.

Synset Examples

She was frazzled after the visit of her in-laws

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhaust - to make someone very tired; drain of strength or energy
  • Antonym: to refresh - to give new strength or energy to

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; possibly related to 'fray' (to wear out, unravel).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00075461-v
Synset POS: v
