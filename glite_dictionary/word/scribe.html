scribe

1. NOUN

/ skraɪb / (AmE), / skraɪb / (BrE)
a person whose job is to write copies of documents and manuscripts, especially before printing was invented
person who writes documents by hand

Examples

In ancient Egypt, a scribe was highly respected for his literacy skills.
The scribe carefully copied the manuscript onto fresh parchment.
She hired a scribe to document the proceedings of the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copyist - a person who makes copies of written material

Etymology

From Latin scriba (clerk, secretary), from scribere (to write).

2. NOUN

/ skraɪb / (AmE), / skraɪb / (BrE)
an informal term for a journalist or reporter, especially one who writes for newspapers or magazines
informal term for journalist or reporter
informal

Examples

The scribe hurriedly took notes at the press conference, hoping to get a scoop.
Several award-winning scribes attended the launch party for the new magazine.
As a seasoned scribe, she covered political beats for over a decade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: journalist - a person who writes for newspapers, magazines, or news websites
  • Synonym: reporter - a person who reports news for a newspaper or broadcast

Etymology

From Latin scriba (writer, clerk), from scribere (to write).

3. NOUN

/ skraɪb / (AmE), / skraɪb / (BrE)
A sharp-pointed tool, like an awl, used for marking lines on wood or metal surfaces to be cut.
tool used to mark wood or metal

Examples

The carpenter used a scribe to mark a straight line before cutting the plank.
Before drilling, she ran a scribe along the metal sheet to outline where the holes should go.
A scribe is essential for accurate joinery in woodworking projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awl - a pointed tool for making holes or marks in wood, leather, or other materials

Etymology

From Latin scriba (writer, scribe); this tool sense comes from the action 'to scribe' (to mark).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04162518-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ skraɪb / (AmE), / skraɪb / (BrE)
to mark or score a line on a surface with a pointed instrument, especially in metalworking or carpentry, to guide cutting or machining
mark a line with a pointed tool

Examples

The technician used a sharp tool to scribe a line across the metal plate.
Before cutting the glass, she carefully scribed a guideline to follow.
Scribing allows machinists to transfer precise measurements by scribing marks on the material.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: score - make a groove, line, or mark on a surface
  • Synonym: to etch - engrave a design on a surface

Etymology

From Latin scribere (to write).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
