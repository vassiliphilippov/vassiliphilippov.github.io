Patagonia

1. PROPN

/ ˌpæt̬əˈɡoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌpætəˈɡəʊniə / (BrE)
a region in southern South America, situated between the Andes mountains and the South Atlantic, known for its rugged landscape and sparsely populated plains
region in southern South America

Examples

Many travelers dream of exploring Patagonia's vast plains and dramatic mountain ranges.
The wildlife in Patagonia, including penguins and guanacos, attracts nature enthusiasts from around the world.
Hiking in Patagonia can be challenging due to the unpredictable weather and rugged terrain.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Patagonia', possibly from 'patagón', the nickname of a legendary giant encountered by Magellan.
