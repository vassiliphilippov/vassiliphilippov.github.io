coloradillo

1. NOUN

/ koʊˌlɑːrəˈdiːjoʊ / (AmE), / kɒˌlærəˈdiːjəʊ / (BrE)
A handsome shrub with showy orange, scarlet, or crimson flowers, native to Florida, the West Indies, Mexico, and Brazil.
ornamental flowering shrub

Examples

The garden center had a coloradillo in full bloom, attracting visitors with its dazzling red blossoms.
Botanists are studying the coloradillo for its adaptation to subtropical climates.
She planted a coloradillo as a focal point in her butterfly garden.

Etymology

From Spanish 'coloradillo', diminutive of 'colorado' (colored, red), referring to the plant's bright flowers.
