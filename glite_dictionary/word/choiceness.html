choiceness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɔɪsnəs / (AmE), / ˈtʃɔɪsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being of especially high or select quality; excellence
quality of being excellent or select

Examples

The choiceness of the fabrics impressed every customer who visited the boutique.
He admired the choiceness of her words, each one selected with care for its meaning.
Despite its age, the painting retains a choiceness that sets it apart from others.

Synset Examples

the inn is distinguished by the fineness of its cuisine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excellence - the quality of being outstanding or extremely good
  • Antonym: inferiority - the state of being lower in quality or value

Etymology

From 'choice' + suffix '-ness', where 'choice' in earlier English meant 'of high quality' (from Old French chois).
