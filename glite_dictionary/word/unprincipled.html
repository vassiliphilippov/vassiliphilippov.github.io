unprincipled

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈprɪnsəpəld / (AmE), / ʌnˈprɪnsɪpld / (BrE)
Not acting in accordance with moral principles; lacking integrity or scruples.
lacking moral principles

Examples

Many considered the businessman to be unprincipled after his fraudulent dealings became public.
She refused to work for an unprincipled politician.
His unprincipled actions eventually led to his downfall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immoral - not conforming to accepted standards of morality
  • Antonym: principled - acting in accordance with moral principles

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'principled' (having moral principles).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈprɪnsəpəld / (AmE), / ʌnˈprɪnsɪpld / (BrE)
having or showing no moral principles or scruples; not guided by ethical standards.
lacking moral principles

Examples

The politician was described as unprincipled and willing to do anything to gain power.
Her unprincipled actions eventually led to her being dismissed from the company.
We must not let unprincipled people influence important decisions.

Synset Examples

freedom from coarse unprincipled calumny

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immoral - not conforming to accepted standards of morality
  • Antonym: principled - acting in accordance with morality or rules

Etymology

Prefix 'un-' (not) + 'principled', from 'principle' (moral rule or belief).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01555818-a
Synset POS: a
