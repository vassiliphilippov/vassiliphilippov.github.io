Utricularia

1. NOUN

/ juːˌtrɪkjəˈlɛriə / (AmE), / juːˌtrɪkjʊˈlɛəriə / (BrE)
A large genus of aquatic or semi-aquatic carnivorous plants, commonly known as bladderworts, found worldwide.
genus of aquatic carnivorous plants

Examples

Utricularia is famous for its tiny underwater bladders that trap small aquatic creatures.
Scientists discovered a new species of Utricularia in the wetlands of South America.
Many aquarium enthusiasts grow Utricularia for its unusual carnivorous adaptations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bladderwort - common name for Utricularia species, aquatic carnivorous plants

Etymology

From Latin *utriculus* (a small bag or bladder), referring to the bladder-like traps on the plant.
