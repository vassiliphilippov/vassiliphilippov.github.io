meantime

1. ADV

/ ˈmiːnˌtaɪm / (AmE), / ˈmiːnˌtaɪm / (BrE)
During the time between two events; in the intervening period.
during the intervening time

Examples

His flight was delayed; in the meantime, he read a book at the airport.
Dinner will be ready soon. Meantime, why don't you set the table?
She was waiting for the results and, in the meantime, kept herself occupied with other projects.

Synset Examples

meanwhile I will not think about the problem
meantime he was attentive to his other interests
in the meantime the police were notified

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meanwhile - at the same time; during the intervening time

Etymology

From Middle English 'meantime', from mean (intervening) + time.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmiˌntaɪm / (AmE), / ˈmiːntaɪm / (BrE)
the period of time between two events or actions
the interval between two events

Examples

You can watch TV in the meantime while I finish cooking.
In the meantime, try to get some rest before your appointment.
Problems were being solved, but in the meantime, things were quite chaotic.

Synset Examples

meanwhile the socialists are running the government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interval - a period of time between events
  • Synonym: meanwhile - the period of time between two events (similar meaning as noun)

Etymology

From Middle English 'meantime', from 'mean' (intermediate) + 'time'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15297873-n
Synset POS: n
