Coniferales

1. NOUN

/ kəˌnɪfəˈreɪliːz / (AmE), / kəˌnɪfəˈreɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of chiefly evergreen trees and some shrubs with profusely branching and narrow or needlelike leaves, including pines, firs, and related plants
order of evergreen trees and shrubs (pines, firs, etc.)

Examples

The forest was dominated by Coniferales, particularly towering pines and spruces.
Many members of the Coniferales are valued for their timber and ornamental use.
Botanists have studied the evolution of Coniferales for insights into plant adaptation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conifer - a tree that bears cones and evergreen needlelike leaves

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Latin conifer (cone-bearing) + -ales (indicating order).

2. NOUN

/ kəˌnɪfəˈreɪliːz / (AmE), / kɒˌnɪfəˈreɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of chiefly evergreen trees and some shrubs, characterized by profuse branching and narrow or needlelike leaves; conifers
order of evergreen coniferous trees and shrubs

Examples

Many species within the Coniferales are valued for their timber and resin.
Pine, fir, and spruce all belong to the order Coniferales.
The majority of plants in Coniferales retain their leaves throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conifer - a tree that bears cones and evergreen needle-like leaves
  • Antonym: angiosperm - a plant that has flowers and produces seeds enclosed within a carpel

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Latin 'conifer' (cone-bearing) and '-ales' (order suffix in taxonomy).
