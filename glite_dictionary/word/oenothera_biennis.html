Oenothera biennis

1. NOUN

/ iːˌnɑːˈθɪrə baɪˈɛnɪs / (AmE), / iːˌnɒˈθɪərə baɪˈɛnɪs / (BrE)
A coarse biennial plant native to eastern North America, with yellow flowers that open in the evening; commonly known as evening primrose and naturalized in Europe.
evening primrose, a yellow-flowered biennial plant

Examples

Oenothera biennis is often cultivated for its showy yellow flowers that open at dusk.
Many herbal supplements use oil extracted from Oenothera biennis seeds for medicinal purposes.
The fields were covered in Oenothera biennis during midsummer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evening primrose - Common name for Oenothera biennis, an American biennial plant with yellow flowers

Etymology

From scientific Latin: genus Oenothera from Greek oinothēras (possibly referring to a wine-seeking plant); biennis from Latin meaning 'two years'.
