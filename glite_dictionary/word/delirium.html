delirium

1. NOUN

/ dəˈlɪriəm / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪəriəm / (BrE)
A disturbed state of mind characterized by restlessness, confusion, and sometimes hallucinations or violent excitement, often occurring during illness or fever.
disturbed, agitated mental state

Examples

After three days of high fever, she fell into a state of delirium.
The patient shouted incoherently during his delirium, alarming the hospital staff.
In his delirium, he imagined that he was being chased by invisible enemies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confusion - lack of clear and orderly thought

Etymology

From Latin 'delirium', meaning 'madness', from 'delirare' (to rave).

2. NOUN

/ dəˈlɪriəm / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪəriəm / (BrE)
A disturbed state of mind characterized by confusion, excitement, and often hallucinations, typically occurring suddenly and lasting for a short period.
temporary mental confusion and excitement

Examples

After the surgery, he experienced a brief episode of delirium and did not recognize his family.
High fever in children can sometimes lead to delirium, with vivid hallucinations and disorientation.
The patient emerged from delirium after a few days of careful medical supervision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confusion - a state in which someone is unable to think clearly or understand what is happening
  • Antonym: lucidity - clarity of thought, opposite of mental confusion or delirium

Etymology

From Latin 'delirium' (madness), from 'delirare' (to go off the furrow, to be deranged).
