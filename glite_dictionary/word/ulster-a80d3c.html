ulster

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌl.stɚ / (AmE), / ˈʌl.stə / (BrE)
A loose, long overcoat made from heavy fabric, typically with a belt, worn for outdoor use.
long heavy overcoat, usually belted

Examples

He reached for his ulster before stepping out into the chilly night.
Her grandfather always wore an old ulster during the winter months.
The detective's ulster was soaked by the sudden downpour.

Etymology

Named after Ulster, a province in Northern Ireland, where the cloth was originally made.
