exocentric

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛksoʊˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛksəʊˈsɛntrɪk / (BrE)
(of a compound or phrase) not having the same grammatical function as any of its constituent parts.
(linguistics) non-head compound or phrase

Examples

In traditional grammar analysis, a prepositional phrase like 'in the room' is considered exocentric.
The structure of an exocentric compound differs from that of an endocentric one.
Linguists often classify certain sentences as exocentric due to their unique grammatical roles.

Synset Examples

when ‘until last Easter’ serves as an adverb it is an exocentric construction

Related Terms

  • Antonym: endocentric - having the same grammatical role as one of its constituents (opposite of exocentric)

Etymology

From Greek exo- (outside) + kentrikos (centered).
