Fabiana imbricata

1. NOUN

/ fəˌbiːɑːnə ɪmˌbrɪkətə / (AmE), / fəˌbiːɑːnə ɪmˌbrɪkətə / (BrE)
A shrub native to South America, particularly Peru and Chile, known for its small tubular pink to lavender flowers and tonic, diuretic properties derived from its leaves.
a South American flowering shrub with medicinal uses

Examples

The leaves of Fabiana imbricata are sometimes brewed into herbal teas for their diuretic effect.
Researchers are studying Fabiana imbricata for its potential medicinal benefits in traditional medicine.
In its native habitat, Fabiana imbricata grows in dry, mountainous regions and produces delicate, tubular flowers.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Fabiana' after Francisco Fabián y Fuero, and 'imbricata' meaning overlapping or shingled, referencing the leaves.
