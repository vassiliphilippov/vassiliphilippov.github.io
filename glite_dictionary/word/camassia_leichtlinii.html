Camassia leichtlinii

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmæsiə laɪktˈliːniː / (AmE), / kəˈmasɪə laɪktˈliːniː / (BrE)
A species of camas plant native to the western region of North America, especially west of the Cascade Mountains, known for its tall spikes of bluish flowers.
a species of camas plant with blue flowers

Examples

Camassia leichtlinii blooms in late spring, creating a beautiful display of blue flowers in meadows.
Gardeners in the Pacific Northwest often plant Camassia leichtlinii for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
Native bees are attracted to the blossoms of Camassia leichtlinii in local gardens and wild areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camas - plant of the genus Camassia

Etymology

Genus name from the Native American word for the plant, species named after German botanist Maximilian Leichtlin.
