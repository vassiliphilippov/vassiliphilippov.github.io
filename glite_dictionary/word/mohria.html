Mohria

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊ.ri.ə / (AmE), / ˈmɔː.ri.ə / (BrE)
A genus of African terrestrial ferns in the family Anemiaceae, known for their distinctive fronds.
genus of African terrestrial ferns

Examples

Several species of Mohria are cultivated in botanical gardens for their unusual foliage.
The distribution of Mohria is limited mainly to southern Africa's grasslands and woodlands.
Botanists are studying the evolutionary relationships within the Mohria genus.

Etymology

Named after the German botanist P. F. Mohr.
