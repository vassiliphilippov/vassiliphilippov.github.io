croissant

1. NOUN

/ kwɑːˈsɑnt / (AmE), / krwɑːˈsɒ̃ / (BrE)
A rich, buttery, flaky pastry with a crescent shape, originally from France.
crescent-shaped buttery pastry

Examples

She enjoyed a warm croissant with her morning coffee.
The bakery on Main Street makes the best almond croissants in town.
He bought a freshly baked croissant for breakfast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pastry - a baked food made from dough, often with fillings or toppings

Etymology

From French 'croissant', literally 'crescent', from Old French 'crescens', present participle of 'croître' (to grow).
