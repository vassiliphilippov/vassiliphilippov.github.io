to territorialize

1. VERB

/ ˌtɛrəˈtɔriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌtɛrɪˈtɔːrɪəlaɪz / (BrE)
to extend by adding territory or to make into a territory
to extend by adding territory

Examples

The empire sought to territorialize its neighboring regions through military conquest.
Colonizers often territorialized foreign lands, imposing their own boundaries and laws.
Policies were introduced to territorialize disputed zones along the border.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annex - to add territory to one's own territory by appropriation

Etymology

Formed from 'territory' + '-ize', based on Latin 'territorium' (land) and the English verb-forming suffix '-ize'.

2. VERB

/ ˌtɛrɪˈtɔːriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌtɛrɪˈtɔːriəlaɪz / (BrE)
to organize or designate (an area) as a territory, especially for administrative or political purposes
organize as a territory

Examples

The government decided to territorialize the remote region to improve local administration.
Colonial powers often sought to territorialize new lands to assert control and sovereignty.
Modern states may territorialize cyberspace by establishing jurisdictional boundaries for digital activity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deterritorialize - to remove from a territory or reverse territorial organization

Etymology

From 'territory' + '-ize', modeled on similar verbs.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02437822-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ təˈrɪtəriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / təˈrɪtəriəˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to organize, arrange, or treat (something) according to the division of territory; to place or structure on a territorial basis
arrange on a territorial basis

Examples

After the reform, the government decided to territorialize the administration of local services.
The company plans to territorialize its operations to ensure better regional control.
Historically, many empires have sought to territorialize trade routes for economic gain.

Synset Examples

The railways were territorialized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regionalize - organize into regions rather than territories

Etymology

From 'territory' + '-ize', patterning after similar formation such as 'colonize'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
