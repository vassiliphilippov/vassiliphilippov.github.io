noxiousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑk.ʃəs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈnɒk.ʃəs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being harmful, poisonous, or very unpleasant
quality of being harmful or poisonous

Examples

The noxiousness of the chemical fumes made breathing difficult for workers.
Many people underestimate the noxiousness of seemingly harmless household cleaners.
The noxiousness of the waste forced the authorities to close the nearby park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toxicity - the degree to which something is poisonous
  • Antonym: harmlessness - the state of not causing harm

Etymology

From noxious + -ness; ultimately from Latin 'noxius' (harmful).
