Osman I

1. PROPN

/ ɑsˈmɑn ˈwʌn / (AmE), / ɒzˈmɑːn ˈwʌn / (BrE)
(c. 1259–1323) Founder of the Ottoman Empire and its ruling dynasty; conquered much of Asia Minor and assumed the title of emir in 1299.
founder of the Ottoman Empire

Examples

Osman I is regarded as the founder of the Ottoman Empire, laying the foundation for centuries of rule.
Under the leadership of Osman I, the Ottomans expanded rapidly across Asia Minor.
Many historians credit the military and political skills of Osman I as crucial to the success of his dynasty.

Etymology

From Turkish Osman, based on Arabic Uthmān.
