planter

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæn.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈplæn.tə / (BrE)
a decorative container in which a house plant or other small plant can be grown indoors or outdoors
decorative container for plants

Examples

She placed the fern in a ceramic planter by the window.
The balcony was filled with colorful flowers in hanging planters.
He bought a new planter to display his succulents in the living room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flowerpot - a container, usually round and made of clay or plastic, used for growing plants

2. NOUN

/ ˈplæn.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈplæn.tə / (BrE)
a person who owns or manages a plantation, especially in the southern United States or in colonial regions
owner or manager of a plantation

Examples

The wealthy planter built his fortune on the cultivation of cotton and sugarcane.
After the war, many planters struggled to maintain their large estates.
The novel explores the life of a nineteenth-century planter in the American South.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plantation - a large farm or estate, especially in a tropical or subtropical country, where crops are grown for sale

Etymology

From plant + -er, originally meaning 'one who plants', later used specifically for plantation owners in the Americas.

3. NOUN

/ ˈplæn.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈplɑːn.tə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to plant seeds or seedlings in the ground, especially as part of agricultural work
person who plants seeds or seedlings

Examples

The planter worked quickly, sowing row after row under the hot sun.
Many planters are hired during the spring season when fields need to be cultivated.
As a skilled planter, she ensured every seedling was carefully set into the prepared earth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farmer - a person who owns or manages a farm

Etymology

From Middle English planter, from Old French planteor (planter); from Latin plantare (to plant).
