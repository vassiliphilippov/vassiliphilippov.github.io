chelate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkiːˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈkiːleɪt / (BrE)
a compound in which a metal ion is bonded to an organic molecule at two or more points, forming a ring-like structure
compound with a metal ion bonded in a ring
medical

Examples

The scientist synthesized a chelate to improve the solubility of the metal ion.
Iron in plants is often absorbed in the form of a chelate to prevent precipitation.
EDTA is a widely used chelate for removing toxic metal ions from the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complex - a compound in which molecules or ions form coordinate bonds with a central metal atom

Etymology

From Greek 'chele' meaning 'claw', referring to the way the molecule 'grasps' the metal ion.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkiːleɪt / (AmE), / ˈkiːleɪt / (BrE)
(of a molecule or compound) relating to or characterized by chelation, especially by forming a ring structure in which an atom is bound to two or more other atoms.
relating to or involving chelation
medical

Examples

The chelate structure enables the molecule to bind tightly to metal ions.
Certain antibiotics have chelate properties that can remove heavy metals from the body.
In coordination chemistry, chelate ligands play a crucial role in stabilizing metal complexes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chelation - the process by which a molecule forms a complex with a metal ion

Etymology

From Greek 'chēlē' (claw), referring to the claw-like formation of chelate compounds.

3. VERB

/ ˈkiː.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈkiː.leɪt / (BrE)
(of a substance) to combine with a metal ion to form a stable ring-shaped compound, especially in a chemical reaction.
form a stable ring compound with a metal ion (chemistry, verb)
medical

Examples

The ligand will chelate the metal ion to form a highly stable compound.
Some drugs are designed to chelate excess iron in the bloodstream.
When EDTA is added, it chelates calcium ions, effectively removing them from solution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complex - to form a complex with another molecule or ion

Etymology

From Greek 'chēlē' meaning 'claw', via analogy to the compound 'grasping' the metal ion.
