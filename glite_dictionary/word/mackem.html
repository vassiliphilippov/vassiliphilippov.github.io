Mackem

1. NOUN

/ ˈmækəm / (AmE), / ˈmakəm / (BrE)
a person from Sunderland, a city in northeast England
native or resident of Sunderland
informal british

Examples

As a proud Mackem, she always supported Sunderland AFC.
Although he moved to London, he still speaks with a Mackem accent.
It's easy to spot a Mackem at a football match by their chants.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Geordie - native of Newcastle upon Tyne

Etymology

Origin uncertain, possibly from local dialect 'make them' pronounced 'mackem.'
