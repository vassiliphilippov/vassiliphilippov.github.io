sociality

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊʃiˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌsəʊʃiˈælɪti / (BrE)
the tendency or capacity to associate with others and form social groups
tendency to form social groups

Examples

Certain primates exhibit a high degree of sociality in their daily behavior.
The evolution of sociality has greatly influenced the development of human societies.
Ants are well known for their complex sociality and division of labor.

Synset Examples

mammals as a class are not strong on sociality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sociability - the quality of being sociable; willingness to engage with others

Etymology

From Latin socialitas (fellowship, companionship).
