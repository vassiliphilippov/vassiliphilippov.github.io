abruption

1. NOUN

/ əˈbrʌpʃən / (AmE), / əˈbrʌp.ʃən / (BrE)
An instance of sudden interruption or breaking off.
sudden interruption or break

Examples

The unexpected abruption of the meeting left everyone confused and unprepared.
A sudden abruption in the negotiations led to a breakdown in talks.
She was taken aback by the abruption of their usual routine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interruption - the action of interrupting or being interrupted
  • Antonym: continuation - the act of continuing without interruption

Etymology

From Latin 'abruptio' (a breaking off), from 'abrumpere' (to break off).
