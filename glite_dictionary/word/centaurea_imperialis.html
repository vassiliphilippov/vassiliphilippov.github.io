Centaurea imperialis

1. NOUN

/ sɛnˈtɔriə ˌɪmpɪˈriælɪs / (AmE), / sɛnˈtɔːrɪə ˌɪmpɪˈrɪælɪs / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to the mountains of Iran and Iraq, cultivated for its fragrant rose-pink flowers.
a fragrant flowering perennial plant

Examples

Gardeners often choose Centaurea imperialis for its beautiful and fragrant rose-pink blooms.
The slopes of the Zagros Mountains are home to wild populations of Centaurea imperialis.
She planted Centaurea imperialis to add a touch of exotic elegance to her flowerbed.

Etymology

Genus name from Latinized form of Centaur, referencing Greek mythology; species epithet 'imperialis' from Latin meaning 'imperial'.
