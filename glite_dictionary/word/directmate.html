directmate

1. NOUN

/ dəˈrɛkt.meɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkt.meɪt / (BrE)
A chess problem in which White, moving first, must checkmate Black in a specified number of moves, regardless of Black's defence.
chess problem requiring forced mate in set moves

Examples

The composer designed a three-move directmate to challenge experienced solvers.
In a typical directmate, every possible Black defence is carefully anticipated.
We practiced solving a new directmate every morning at our chess club.

Synset Examples

Battery play is much more common in the selfmate than the directmate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: selfmate - a chess problem requiring White to force Black to checkmate White

Etymology

From 'direct' (straightforward) and 'mate' (checkmate in chess).
