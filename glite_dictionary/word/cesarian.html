cesarian

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈzɛəriən / (AmE), / sɪˈzɛəriən / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to deliver a baby by cutting through the abdominal wall and uterus
delivery of a baby by surgical incision
medical

Examples

After complications during labor, the doctors decided to perform a cesarian.
Her first child was born naturally, but the second required a cesarian.
Some women opt for a planned cesarian for medical or personal reasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cesarean section - surgical delivery of a baby; common alternative spelling

Etymology

From Latin 'Caesareus', referring to Julius Caesar, traditionally (though inaccurately) believed to have been born by this method.

2. ADJ

/ sɪˈzɛəriən / (AmE), / sɪˈzɛəriən / (BrE)
relating to a surgical operation in which a baby is delivered through an incision in the mother's abdomen and uterus; relating to a caesarean section.
relating to caesarean delivery
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a cesarian delivery due to medical complications.
Her recovery after the cesarian operation was quick and uneventful.
The patient was prepared for a cesarian birth as the safest option for her baby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caesarean - relating to delivery by surgical operation through the abdominal wall

Etymology

From Latin 'Caesareus', associated with the tradition that Julius Caesar was born by this method.
