Tringa flavipes

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪŋɡə ˈfleɪvaɪps / (AmE), / ˈtrɪŋɡə ˈfleɪvɪpiːz / (BrE)
A species of small North American shorebird, commonly known as the lesser yellowlegs, with long yellow legs and a slender bill.
species of North American shorebird

Examples

The Tringa flavipes is often seen foraging in shallow wetlands during migration season.
Birdwatchers spotted a flock of Tringa flavipes along the lakeshore at dawn.
Due to habitat loss, Tringa flavipes populations are monitored closely by conservationists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lesser yellowlegs - A small North American wader; common name for Tringa flavipes

Etymology

From Latin: Tringa (a genus of birds), flavipes ('yellow-footed').
