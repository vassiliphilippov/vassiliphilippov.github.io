to decode

1. VERB

/ diˈkoʊd / (AmE), / dɪˈkəʊd / (BrE)
to convert coded or encrypted information into ordinary language or a readable form
convert code into ordinary language

Examples

The scientist worked all night to decode the suspicious message.
She was able to decode the encrypted data using special software.
It took hours to decode the hidden meaning behind his words.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to encode - to convert information into a code
  • Synonym: to decipher - to interpret or make out the meaning of something difficult to understand

Etymology

From de- (reverse) + code, modeled after 'encode'.
