slivovitz

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɪvəˌvɪts / (AmE), / ˈslɪv.ə.vɪts / (BrE)
a strong, colorless plum brandy originating from the Balkans, especially popular in Central and Eastern Europe
plum brandy from the Balkans

Examples

After dinner, he offered his guests a glass of slivovitz as a digestive.
In some Balkan countries, slivovitz is traditionally served at family celebrations.
The aroma of homemade slivovitz reminded her of her childhood in Eastern Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brandy - a strong alcoholic spirit distilled from wine or fermented fruit juice

Etymology

From Serbian/Croatian/Slovene 'šljivovica', from 'šljiva' meaning 'plum'.
