reservoir

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛzərˌvwɑr / (AmE), / ˈrɛzəvwɑː / (BrE)
a large natural or artificial container used for storing liquids such as water or oil.
container for storing liquid

Examples

The city built a new reservoir to supply water to the growing population.
Engineers regularly check the oil reservoir to ensure machinery runs smoothly.
During the drought, the water level in the reservoir dropped to a record low.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tank - a large container for storing liquid or gas

Etymology

From French réservoir (storehouse), from réserver (to reserve), ultimately from Latin reservare (to reserve).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛzərˌvwɑr / (AmE), / ˈrɛzəvwɑː / (BrE)
a person, animal, plant, or substance in which an infectious agent normally lives and multiplies, serving as a source of infection.
organism or material that harbors an infectious agent
medical

Examples

Rodents can be a reservoir for diseases such as hantavirus.
The bat population acted as a natural reservoir for the virus.
Identifying the reservoir of infection is crucial in controlling outbreaks.

Synset Examples

an infectious agent depends on a reservoir for its survival

Etymology

From French réservoir, from réserver (to reserve).

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɛzərˌvwɑr / (AmE), / ˈrɛzəvwɑː / (BrE)
A large natural or artificial lake used to store water for community use, such as supplying drinking water or irrigation.
a lake for storing water for use

Examples

The city draws its drinking water from a nearby reservoir.
After the drought, water levels in the reservoir dropped dramatically.
A new reservoir was constructed to help supply water to local farms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lake - a large body of water surrounded by land

Etymology

From French 'réservoir', from reserver (to reserve, keep back).

/ ˈrɛzərˌvwɑr / (AmE), / ˈrɛzəvwɑː / (BrE)
a large supply of something that is available for use as needed; a store or reserve
large supply or reserve

Examples

She drew from her deep reservoir of experience to solve the problem.
After months of stress, he found his inner reservoir of patience running low.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
