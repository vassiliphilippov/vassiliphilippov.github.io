predestinate

1. VERB

/ prɪˈdɛstəˌneɪt / (AmE), / prɪˈdɛstɪneɪt / (BrE)
to determine or ordain in advance, especially by divine will or fate
to foreordain by divine will

Examples

Many people in the congregation believed that God had predestinated their fates from birth.
He argued that future events are predestinated and unchangeable.
The doctrine taught that salvation was predestinated for the chosen few.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to foreordain - to appoint or decree beforehand
  • Synonym: to predestine - to determine in advance, especially by fate or divine will

Etymology

From Latin praedestinare, from prae- (before) + destinare (to determine).

2. ADJ

/ priˈdɛstəˌneɪt / (AmE), / priˈdɛstɪnət / (BrE)
(of a thing or event) established or determined in advance and unable to be changed, often by fate or divine will.
determined or established in advance
archaic

Examples

There was a predestinate air to their encounter, as if fate itself arranged the meeting.
The play suggests that our destinies may be predestinate and not shaped by free will.
Her predestinate role as a leader was spoken of since her childhood.

Synset Examples

his place in history was foreordained
a sense of predestinate inevitability about it
it seemed predestined since the beginning of the world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predestined - determined in advance, especially by fate or divine will
  • Synonym: foreordained - decided or established in advance, usually by destiny or divine decree
  • Antonym: contingent - subject to chance or not fixed; occurring only if certain circumstances are the case

Etymology

From Latin praedestinatus, past participle of praedestinare (to determine beforehand).
