panel

1. NOUN

/ ˈpænəl / (AmE), / ˈpanəl / (BrE)
a piece of cloth, usually triangular or tapering, that is used as part of garments, umbrellas, sails, or similar items
piece of cloth in garments or sails

Examples

Each section of the umbrella is made from a different colored panel.
The dress was sewn with several triangular panels to give it more shape.
They patched the sail by replacing the torn panel with a new piece of canvas.

Etymology

From Old French *panel* meaning 'piece of cloth' or 'section of a wall'.
