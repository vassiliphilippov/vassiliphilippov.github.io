coddler

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːd.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɒd.lə / (BrE)
a person who pampers or spoils someone by giving them excessive care or indulgence
person who pampers or spoils

Examples

As a coddler, she couldn't help but do everything for her children, even their homework.
Some experts warn that being a coddler can lead to children becoming overly dependent.
His friends accused him of being a coddler when he refused to let his dog sleep outside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to coddle - to treat with excessive care or indulgence
  • Antonym: disciplinarian - a person who believes in strict discipline

Etymology

From the verb 'coddle', which likely derives from the dialect word 'caudle' meaning to treat as an invalid, from Middle English.
