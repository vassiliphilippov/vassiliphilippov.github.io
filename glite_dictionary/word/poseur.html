poseur

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈzɜr / (AmE), / pəʊˈzɜː / (BrE)
a person who pretends to be what they are not, often to impress others or fit in with a group
person pretending to be something they are not

Examples

Everyone could tell he was a poseur who cared more about appearances than real art.
She accused her former friend of being a poseur at the punk shows.
Many thought he was a poseur because he only dressed that way to fit in.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imposter - someone who pretends to be someone else
  • Antonym: authentic - genuine, not false or copied

Etymology

French, from 'poser' meaning 'to pose' or 'affect an attitude'.
