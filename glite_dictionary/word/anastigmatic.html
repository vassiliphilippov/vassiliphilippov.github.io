anastigmatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.ə.stɪgˈmæt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌan.ə.stɪgˈmat.ɪk / (BrE)
(of a lens or optical system) designed so as to be free from astigmatism and capable of producing sharp images.
free from astigmatism (in optics)

Examples

The new camera lens is anastigmatic, reducing image distortion significantly.
Microscope objectives are often anastigmatic to ensure clarity across the field of view.
He replaced the old lenses with anastigmatic ones for sharper photographs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: astigmatic - having or relating to astigmatism, an optical defect

Etymology

From Greek ana- (up, again, back) + astigmatic (from astigmatism).

2. ADJ

/ ˌænəstɪgˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌanastɪgˈmatɪk / (BrE)
(of a lens or lens system) designed to reduce or eliminate astigmatism, producing point images without distortion.
(of a lens) free of astigmatism

Examples

This microscope uses an anastigmatic lens to produce sharp, clear images.
Early cameras suffered from blurred images until the invention of anastigmatic optics.
The optical engineer recommended an anastigmatic system for precise scientific measurements.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: astigmatic - (of lenses or vision) affected by or relating to astigmatism

Etymology

From Greek 'an-' (not) + 'astigma' (without a spot or point), relating to 'astigmatism.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02632026-a
Synset POS: a
