cuff

1. VERB

/ kʌf / (AmE), / kʌf / (BrE)
to hit someone with an open hand
to strike with an open hand

Examples

He playfully cuffed his brother on the shoulder as a greeting.
The coach cuffed the boy gently for not paying attention.
She cuffed him across the ear for being cheeky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slap - to hit with the palm or fingers of the hand
  • Antonym: punch - to strike with a closed fist

Etymology

Perhaps imitative or related to earlier English dialect forms meaning 'blow' or 'hit'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01419816-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ kʌf / (AmE), / kʌf / (BrE)
a metal shackle or band that can be locked around the wrist, often used in pairs to restrain a person's hands
a metal wrist shackle for restraint

Examples

The police officer placed a cuff on the suspect's wrist before leading him away.
She struggled against the cold metal cuff that restrained her hands.
The guard checked that each prisoner's cuff was securely locked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handcuff - a metal fastening that can be locked around a wrist, used in pairs to restrain hands
Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ kʌf / (AmE), / kʌf / (BrE)
the end part of a sleeve or trouser leg, where the material is turned back or finished with a separate band
turned-back end of a sleeve or trouser leg

Examples

He wore a white shirt with gold links in the cuffs.
The cuffs of her jeans were frayed after years of wear.
She rolled up her sleeves, exposing the patterned cuffs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hem - the edge of a piece of cloth that is folded under and sewn

Etymology

From Middle English cuffe, possibly from Dutch kuif (crest, tuft).
