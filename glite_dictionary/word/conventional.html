conventional

1. ADJ

/ kənˈvɛnʃənəl / (AmE), / kənˈvɛnʃənəl / (BrE)
(of weapons) using explosive, kinetic, or chemical energy for propulsion or destruction, as opposed to nuclear energy; not nuclear.
(of weapons) not nuclear

Examples

The treaty bans the use of conventional weapons in urban areas.
Some countries maintain large conventional arsenals alongside their nuclear programs.
Modern conventional warfare relies on advanced technology rather than nuclear arms.

Synset Examples

conventional warfare
conventional weapons

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nuclear - (of weapons) using nuclear energy for destruction

Etymology

From Late Latin 'conventionalis' (relating to agreement or convention), from Latin 'conventio' (meeting, agreement).

2. ADJ

/ kənˈvɛnʃənl̩ / (AmE), / kənˈvɛnʃənəl / (BrE)
following established customs, practices, or standards, especially in behavior or attitudes; not new or original
following accepted customs or standards

Examples

Her conventional approach to teaching focused on lectures and textbooks.
He wore a conventional suit to the interview, as expected.
They held rather conventional views on marriage and family.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unconventional - not based on or conforming to accepted customs or standards
  • Synonym: orthodox - conforming to traditional or established beliefs or practices

Etymology

From Latin 'conventionalis' (relating to an agreement or assembly), from 'conventio' (agreement).
