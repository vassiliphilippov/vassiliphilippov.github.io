Leonurus

1. NOUN

/ liˈɑnərəs / (AmE), / lɪˈəʊnʊrəs / (BrE)
A genus of robust Old World herbs of the mint family, characterized by whorled flowers.
genus of herbs with whorled flowers

Examples

The Leonurus genus includes the commonly known motherwort plant.
Researchers studied several species within the Leonurus genus for their medicinal properties.
Leonurus thrives in temperate climates across Europe and Asia.

Etymology

From Greek 'leon' (lion) and 'oura' (tail), referring to the shape of the plant's leaves.

2. NOUN

/ liˈɑːnərəs / (AmE), / liˈəʊnərʊs / (BrE)
a genus of stout Old World herbs in the mint family, with whorled flowers, including motherwort
genus of herbs in the mint family

Examples

Botanists believe the Leonurus genus contains several species valued for their medicinal properties.
The most well-known member of the Leonurus genus is motherwort, used in traditional herbal medicine.
Researchers studied the flowering patterns of Leonurus plants growing in Mediterranean climates.

Etymology

From Greek leōn (lion) + oura (tail), referring to the shape of the leaves or flower clusters.
