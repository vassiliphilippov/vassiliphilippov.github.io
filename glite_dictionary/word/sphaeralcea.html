Sphaeralcea

1. NOUN

/ sfɛrˈælsiə / (AmE), / sfɪəˈrælsiə / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly tropical or subtropical herbs in the mallow family, known for their showy flowers and mostly globular fruits; also called globe mallows.
genus of tropical herbs (globe mallows)

Examples

The vibrant orange flowers of Sphaeralcea are a striking sight in desert landscapes.
Gardeners often plant Sphaeralcea species because they attract bees and butterflies.
Some Sphaeralcea varieties are cultivated for their drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Etymology

From Greek sphaira (sphere) + Alcea (mallow), referring to the globe-shaped fruits.

2. NOUN

/ sfɪəˈrælsiə / (AmE), / sfɪəˈrælsiə / (BrE)
A genus of mainly tropical or warm-region herbs with showy flowers and mostly spherical fruit, commonly known as globe mallows.
genus of flowering plants (globe mallows)

Examples

The Sphaeralcea species are commonly called globe mallows and are grown for their delicate orange blooms.
We identified several Sphaeralcea plants along the road during the field study.
Many gardeners appreciate Sphaeralcea for its drought resistance and ornamental value.

Etymology

From Greek 'sphaira' (sphere) and 'alkea' (mallow), referring to the rounded shape of the fruit and its relation to mallows.
