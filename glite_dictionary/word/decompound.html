decompound

1. ADJ

/ diˈkɑmpaʊnd / (AmE), / diˈkɒmpaʊnd / (BrE)
(of a leaf) divided into parts which are themselves compound.
of a leaf; divided into compound parts

Examples

The fern's decompound leaves give it a delicate, lacy appearance.
In some species, the decompound leaflets allow for more efficient light capture.
The botanist identified the willow tree by its decompound foliage.

Etymology

From Latin 'de-' (down from, away) + 'compound'.
