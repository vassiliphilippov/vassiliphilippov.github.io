immunity

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmjunəti / (AmE), / ɪˈmjuːnəti / (BrE)
the state of not being susceptible to something, especially a disease or harmful influence
state of not being susceptible

Examples

The vaccine provided lifelong immunity against the disease.
Certain plants have developed natural immunity to pests over time.
His immunity to criticism allowed him to persevere under pressure.

Synset Examples

unsusceptibility to rust

Related Terms

  • Antonym: susceptibility - state of being likely to be affected by something

Etymology

From Latin 'immunitas' (exemption from service or duty).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈmjunəti / (AmE), / ɪˈmjuːnəti / (BrE)
official protection or exemption from a duty, penalty, or legal action, especially granted by authorities.
official exemption from duty or penalty
legal

Examples

The witness was offered immunity in exchange for his testimony.
Because of his diplomatic status, he claimed immunity from prosecution.
The court granted her immunity after she agreed to cooperate with the investigation.

Synset Examples

he was granted immunity from prosecution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protection - the act of keeping someone or something safe from harm or loss
  • Antonym: liability - the state of being legally responsible for something

Etymology

From Latin immunitas (freedom from service or obligation), from immunis (exempt, free).

3. NOUN

/ ɪˈmjunəti / (AmE), / ɪˈmjuːnəti / (BrE)
The condition or quality of being unaffected or not influenced by something.
being unaffected by something

Examples

His strong sense of self gave him a surprising immunity to criticism from his peers.
She seemed to have developed an emotional immunity to negative comments over the years.
The politician displayed a remarkable immunity to public opinion, refusing to change his stance.

Synset Examples

immunity to criticism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invulnerability - being immune to harm or attack
  • Antonym: susceptibility - the quality of being easily affected or influenced

Etymology

From Latin 'immunitas', from 'immunis' meaning 'exempt, free from public service'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05041061-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ɪˈmjunəti / (AmE), / ɪˈmjuːnəti / (BrE)
the state of being resistant to a specific disease, especially due to the presence of antibodies or immune cells
resistance to disease
medical

Examples

Vaccines are an effective way to build immunity against many infectious diseases.
After recovering from the illness, she developed natural immunity to the virus.
Infants receive some immunity from their mothers in the first few months of life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: susceptibility - likelihood or tendency to be affected by disease

Etymology

From Latin immunitas (exemption), from immunis (exempt, free from).
