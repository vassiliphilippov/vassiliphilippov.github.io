spiceberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪsˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈspaɪsˌbɛri / (BrE)
a shrub native to East Asia, especially from Japan to northern India, known for its coral-red berries
shrub with coral-red berries

Examples

The spiceberry thrives in well-drained soil and is prized for its vibrant foliage.
Birds are attracted to the spiceberry because of its bright, coral-red fruit.
Gardeners in temperate climates often cultivate spiceberry for ornamental purposes.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪsˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈspaɪsˌbɛri / (BrE)
A spicy, red, berrylike fruit that is a source of wintergreen oil.
spicy red berrylike fruit

Examples

The spiceberry is often used to add a unique flavor to herbal teas.
Many people value the oil extracted from the spiceberry for its refreshing scent.
In the wild, birds are attracted to the bright red spiceberries growing on the shrubs.
