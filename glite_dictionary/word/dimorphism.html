dimorphism

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈmɔːrˌfɪzəm / (AmE), / daɪˈmɔː.fɪz.əm / (BrE)
(Chemistry) The occurrence of a substance in two distinct crystalline forms.
property of having two crystal forms
medical

Examples

Some minerals, like calcium carbonate, exhibit dimorphism by forming both calcite and aragonite crystals.
Researchers studied the dimorphism of the compound to understand its stability under different temperatures.
The discovery of dimorphism in a pharmaceutical compound can affect its manufacturing and efficacy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: polymorphism - the ability of a substance to exist in more than one crystalline form
  • Degree Lower: monomorphism - the occurrence of a substance in only one crystalline form

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' (two) and 'morphē' (form).

2. NOUN

/ daɪˈmɔːrˌfɪzəm / (AmE), / dɪˈmɔː.fɪ.zəm / (BrE)
(Biology) The existence of two distinct forms among individuals of the same animal species, not related to sex differences.
existence of two forms in a species (biology)
medical

Examples

Certain butterflies exhibit dimorphism with two distinct wing patterns in the same population.
Researchers observed dimorphism in beak shapes among the finches studied.
The dimorphism seen in the species is unrelated to male or female characteristics.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: polymorphism - the occurrence of multiple forms (more than two) within a species

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' (two) + 'morphē' (form).
