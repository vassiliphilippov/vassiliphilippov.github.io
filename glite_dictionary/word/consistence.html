consistence

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsɪstəns / (AmE), / kənˈsɪstəns / (BrE)
the quality or state of holding together firmly and retaining shape; firmness or cohesiveness in a substance
firmness or cohesiveness of a substance

Examples

The consistence of the clay was perfect for sculpting detailed figures.
Chefs often judge the quality of dough by its consistence before baking.
After mixing the ingredients, the consistence of the batter became smooth and thick.

Synset Examples

wool has more body than rayon
when the dough has enough consistency it is ready to bake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consistency - the quality of being firm and holding together; cohesiveness

Etymology

From Latin 'consistentia' (standing firm, solidity), via French 'consistence'.

2. NOUN

/ kənˈsɪstəns / (AmE), / kənˈsɪstəns / (BrE)
the quality of being uniform or harmonious; agreement among things or parts
harmonious uniformity or agreement

Examples

There is a pleasing consistence in the design of the building and its surroundings.
Her argument showed a consistence of logic and evidence throughout.
The consistence among their responses suggested a well-coordinated team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consistency - the quality of being consistent; steadiness or uniformity
  • Antonym: discordance - lack of agreement or harmony

Etymology

From Latin 'consistentia' (standing together, agreement), via French 'consistence'.
