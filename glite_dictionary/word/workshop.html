workshop

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːk.ʃɑːp / (AmE), / ˈwɜːk.ʃɒp / (BrE)
a short and intensive educational session or course for a small group, focusing on practical skills or problem solving.
short training course for a small group

Examples

She attended a workshop on creative writing last weekend.
The company organized a workshop to improve teamwork among employees.
During the workshop, participants developed solutions to real-world business problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seminar - a meeting for discussion or training, often academic
  • Antonym: lecture - an educational talk to an audience, especially as part of a class

Etymology

From work + shop, originally referring to a place for manual work, later extended to group training.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːkˌʃɑːp / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkʃɒp / (BrE)
a room or building where goods are made or repaired, often by hand or using simple tools
room or building for making or repairing things

Examples

She set up a small workshop in her garage to create handmade jewelry.
The carpenter spends most of his day working in the workshop.
The old workshop behind the house is filled with woodworking tools.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: factory - a large building where goods are manufactured in large quantities
  • Synonym: studio - a room where an artist, photographer, or musician works

Etymology

From Middle English, from work + shop.
