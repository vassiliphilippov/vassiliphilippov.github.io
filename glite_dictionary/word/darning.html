darning

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑrnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɑːnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of mending a hole or worn area in fabric by weaving thread across it, often by hand
sewing: mending fabric with woven threads

Examples

She spent her evening quietly engaged in darning her husband's socks.
Traditional darning can extend the life of your favorite sweater by years.
The teacher taught us the basics of darning during the sewing class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mending - the act of repairing something, especially fabric or clothes

Etymology

From the verb 'darn,' likely of Scandinavian origin, compare Danish 'darne' (to darn).
