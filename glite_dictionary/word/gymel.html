gymel

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪməl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪməl / (BrE)
A musical technique, found in some medieval English music, in which two voice parts are sung in parallel thirds.
medieval music technique: parallel thirds

Examples

The choir performed a beautiful gymel during the medieval section of the concert.
In early English church music, gymel was often used to enrich the harmony.
Musicologists study the use of gymel as a feature of fourteenth-century English polyphony.

Etymology

From Middle English, probably from Latin 'gemellus' (twin or paired).
