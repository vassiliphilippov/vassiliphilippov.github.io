membered

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɛm.bɚd / (AmE), / ˈmɛm.bəd / (BrE)
(in combination) having members or parts of a specified kind; used especially in chemistry to refer to rings with a certain number of atoms.
having members; used in compounds (esp. in chemistry)

Examples

The molecule contains a five-membered ring which is unusually stable.
Several six-membered heterocyclic compounds are important in medicinal chemistry.
In organic chemistry, three-membered rings are known for their high strain energy.
