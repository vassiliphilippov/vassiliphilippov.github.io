sugar-plum

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃʊɡɚ plʌm / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡə plʌm / (BrE)
An affectionate term of endearment, often used for a child or loved one.
term of endearment, especially for a child

Examples

She hugged her sugar-plum before dropping her off at school.
Come here, my little sugar-plum, and tell me about your day!
He whispered goodnight to his sleeping sugar-plum.

Synset Examples

Her daughter is her little sugar-plum, always making her smile.
He called her his sugar-plum after she made him breakfast.

Etymology

From 'sugar' and 'plum', originally referring to a type of sweet made of sugar, later used affectionately.
