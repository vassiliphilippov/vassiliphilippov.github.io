conflagration

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.fləˈɡreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.fləˈɡreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A large and uncontrollable fire that causes a great deal of destruction.
large, destructive fire

Examples

Firefighters struggled to contain the conflagration that swept through the factory district.
The forest conflagration destroyed hundreds of acres before it was finally brought under control.
A spark from faulty wiring ignited a conflagration that could be seen for miles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inferno - a large fire that is dangerously out of control
  • Synonym: blaze - a very large or fiercely burning fire

Etymology

From Latin conflagratio (burning up), from conflagrare (to burn up).
