bourne

1. NOUN

/ bɔrn / (AmE), / bɔːn / (BrE)
(Archaic) A goal, destination, or fixed limit, especially one that is the object of a journey or pursuit.
archaic: goal or destination
archaic

Examples

After years of struggle, he finally reached the bourne of his ambitions.
They journeyed tirelessly, always pressing onward toward their distant bourne.
In the poem, death is depicted as the final bourne from which no traveler returns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goal - an aim or desired result
  • Synonym: destination - the place to which someone or something is going

Etymology

From Old French *borne* (boundary), from Medieval Latin *bodina* (boundary, limit).

2. NOUN

/ bɔrn / (AmE), / bɔːn / (BrE)
An archaic term for a boundary or limit.
archaic boundary or limit
archaic

Examples

The river served as the bourne between the two kingdoms.
In Hamlet, Shakespeare refers to the undiscovered country from whose bourne no traveler returns.
Villagers rarely crossed the ancient bourne that marked their land's end.

Etymology

From Old French *borne*, from Medieval Latin *bodina* (boundary marker).
