Aphyllanthes

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ.fɪˈlæn.θiːz / (AmE), / ˌæf.ɪˈlæn.θiːz / (BrE)
A genus of perennial plants with fibrous roots, rush-like leaves, and deep blue flowers, native to the Mediterranean region.
genus of perennial flowering plant

Examples

Aphyllanthes is found primarily in the Mediterranean region, thriving in dry, rocky soils.
The striking blue flowers of Aphyllanthes bloom in late spring each year.
Botanists sometimes place Aphyllanthes in its own family, Aphyllanthaceae, due to its unique characteristics.

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' meaning 'without' and 'phyllon' meaning 'leaf', referring to its nearly leafless appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæfɪˈlænˌθiːz / (AmE), / ˌafɪˈlanθiːz / (BrE)
A genus of perennial plants with fibrous roots, rush-like foliage, and deep blue flowers; sometimes classified in its own family, Aphyllanthaceae.
genus of perennial plants with blue flowers

Examples

The Aphyllanthes genus is noted for its striking blue blossoms found in Mediterranean regions.
Botanists sometimes place Aphyllanthes in its own plant family due to its unique characteristics.
I saw a rare Aphyllanthes blooming among the dry grasses during my hike in southern France.

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'phyllon' (leaf) + 'anthos' (flower), referring to its leafless appearance.
