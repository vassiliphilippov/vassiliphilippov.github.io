unimaginatively

1. ADV

/ ˌʌnɪˈmæd͡ʒɪnətɪvli / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪˈmadʒɪnətɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that lacks imagination or creativity.
lacking imagination or creativity

Examples

The proposal was written very unimaginatively, so it failed to impress the panel.
The novel proceeds unimaginatively through a series of predictable plot points.
She decorated the room unimaginatively, choosing bland colors and common furniture.

Synset Examples

the stage sets were designed rather unimaginatively

Related Terms

  • Antonym: creatively - in a way that shows imagination or originality

2. ADV

/ ˌʌnɪˈmædʒɪnətɪvli / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪˈmædʒɪnətɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that lacks creativity, originality, or imagination; in a dull or uninspired manner.
without imagination or creativity

Examples

The report was written unimaginatively, lacking any engaging or original ideas.
She decorated the room unimaginatively, choosing only plain white walls and basic furniture.
His speech, delivered unimaginatively, failed to capture the audience's attention.

Synset Examples

I applied my attention prosaically to my routine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prosaically - in a matter-of-fact or unimaginative way
  • Antonym: creatively - in a way that involves the use of imagination or original ideas

Etymology

From 'unimaginative' (from 'un-' meaning 'not' + 'imaginative') + '-ly'.
