superclass

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuː.pɚ.klæs / (AmE), / ˈsuː.pə.klɑːs / (BrE)
(Biology) A taxonomic rank below a phylum and above a class.
biological rank between phylum and class

Examples

The cartilaginous fishes belong to the superclass Chondrichthyes.
Scientists discovered a new superclass of marine invertebrates.
Birds and reptiles are classified under different superclasses in biological taxonomy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: class - taxonomic rank below superclass and above order
  • Degree Higher: phylum - taxonomic rank above superclass and below kingdom

Etymology

From Latin super- (above) + classis (class).
