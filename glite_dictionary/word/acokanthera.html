Acokanthera

1. NOUN

/ əˌkoʊˈkænθərə / (AmE), / akəʊˈkænθərə / (BrE)
A small genus of African and Arabian trees and shrubs whose parts contain highly toxic cardiac glycosides.
a genus of toxic trees and shrubs

Examples

The deadly sap of the Acokanthera plant has been used for arrow poison in Africa.
Botanists discovered a new species within the Acokanthera genus in Kenya's forests.
Many gardeners avoid planting Acokanthera due to its highly toxic properties.

Etymology

From Greek *akoke* (pointed) and *anthera* (anther), referring to a pointed flower structure.
