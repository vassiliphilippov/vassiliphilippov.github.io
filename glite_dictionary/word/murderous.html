murderous

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɝːdərəs / (AmE), / ˈmɜːdərəs / (BrE)
Having a tendency or capability to kill; extremely violent or likely to cause death.
extremely violent; capable of murder

Examples

The criminal gave her a murderous glare as she testified against him.
His murderous intent was clear from the rage on his face.
The film depicts the murderous violence of the gang's confrontations.

Synset Examples

a homicidal rage
murderous thugs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homicidal - having a tendency or capability to kill another person
  • Synonym: lethal - causing or sufficient to cause death

Etymology

From Middle English murderous, from Old French meurtrous, from meurtre (murder).
