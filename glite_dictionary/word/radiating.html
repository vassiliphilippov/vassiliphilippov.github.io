radiating

1. ADJ

/ ˈreɪdiˌeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪdɪeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
spreading outwards from a common central point; diverging in all directions from a single source.
spreading out from a center

Examples

The spokes of a wheel have a radiating pattern from the hub.
Heat was radiating outwards from the fireplace, warming the room.
Many mountain ranges are formed by radiating ridges spreading from a central peak.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: converging - coming together at a common point

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'radiate', derived from Latin 'radius' (ray, spoke).
