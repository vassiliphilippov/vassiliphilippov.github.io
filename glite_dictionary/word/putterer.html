putterer

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌtərər / (AmE), / ˈpʌtərə / (BrE)
a person who spends time in a leisurely, casual way, often doing small or unimportant tasks without urgency
person who does small tasks casually

Examples

My grandfather was a true putterer, always tinkering in the garden or garage.
You can always spot a putterer by the way they move from one little task to another.
As a putterer, she rarely finishes a project but enjoys the process of doing small jobs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potterer - a person who potters about; similar sense

Etymology

From 'putter' (to occupy oneself in an unhurried, aimless manner) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
