rampant

1. ADJ

/ ˈræmpənt / (AmE), / ˈræmpənt / (BrE)
(of something unwelcome or unpleasant) existing or spreading unchecked and often out of control.
uncontrolled; widespread (usually negative)

Examples

Rumors about the company's financial troubles were rampant throughout the office.
Crime was rampant in the city during the late 1980s.
The garden was overgrown, with weeds rampant among the flowers.

Synset Examples

rampant aggression

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unchecked - not restrained or controlled
  • Antonym: controlled - under restraint or kept within limits

Etymology

From Old French 'rampant', present participle of 'ramper' (to rear, to climb), from a Germanic root.

2. ADJ

/ ˈræmpənt / (AmE), / ˈræmpənt / (BrE)
(heraldry) rearing up on one hind leg with forelegs elevated, typically with the head in profile, as depicted on coats of arms.
heraldic: rearing on hind leg

Examples

The family's coat of arms features a lion rampant holding a sword.
Several medieval banners display a bear rampant as a symbol of strength.
The illustration depicts a unicorn rampant surrounded by golden stars.

Synset Examples

a lion rampant

Etymology

From Old French 'rampant', from 'ramper' (to rear, climb).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01239837-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈræmpənt / (AmE), / ˈræmpənt / (BrE)
(of a plant) growing vigorously and without restraint, especially so as to spread quickly and become difficult to control.
(of a plant) growing unchecked and vigorously

Examples

The garden was overrun with rampant ivy climbing the fences and trees.
A rampant growth of kudzu quickly covered the abandoned house.
He struggled to keep the rampant weeds from taking over his vegetable patch.

Synset Examples

a rampant growth of weeds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unchecked - not controlled or restrained
  • Synonym: invasive - (of a plant) tending to spread prolifically and undesirably

Etymology

From Old French *rampant*, present participle of *ramper* (to rear up).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00016043-s
Synset POS: s
