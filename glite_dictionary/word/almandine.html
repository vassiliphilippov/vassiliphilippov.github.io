almandine

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.mənˌdaɪn / (AmE), / ˈæl.mənˌdiːn / (BrE)
a deep red to purple variety of garnet used as a gemstone
deep red-purple variety of garnet

Examples

The jeweler set an almandine in the center of the gold ring.
Collectors prize certain shades of almandine for their intense color and clarity.
A necklace strung with polished almandine beads gleamed under the light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garnet - a group of silicate minerals used as gemstones and abrasives

Etymology

From Latin *almandinus*, from Alabanda, the name of a town in Asia Minor where the stone was found.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæl.mənˌdin / (AmE), / ˈæl.məndiːn / (BrE)
a deep red variety of garnet, consisting of iron aluminum silicate, used as a gemstone.
a deep red variety of garnet (gemstone)

Examples

The necklace featured a stunning almandine set in gold filigree.
Geologists discovered almandine crystals embedded in the metamorphic rock.
Her ring was adorned with a large, deep red almandine garnet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garnet - a group of silicate minerals used as gemstones and abrasives

Etymology

From Late Latin almandina, from an alteration of alabandina (gem from Alabanda, a city in Asia Minor).
