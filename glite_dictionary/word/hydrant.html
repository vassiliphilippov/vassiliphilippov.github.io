hydrant

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdrənt / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdrənt / (BrE)
A pipe with a valve and spout, typically found above ground, for drawing water from a main, especially for fighting fires.
above-ground water outlet pipe

Examples

The firefighters quickly attached their hoses to the nearest hydrant when they arrived at the scene.
Each street corner is equipped with a red hydrant for emergency use.
During the inspection, it was found that the old hydrant was leaking and needed repairs.

Etymology

From Greek 'hudor' (water) via French 'hydrant'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdrənt / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdrənt / (BrE)
A pipe with a valve and spout at which water may be drawn from a water main, especially for fighting fires.
device for drawing water from a pipe

Examples

The firefighters quickly attached their hose to the nearest hydrant.
Please do not park your car in front of a hydrant.
After the water main broke, water gushed from the open hydrant into the street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire hydrant - an outdoor hydrant used by firefighters for water supply

Etymology

From Greek 'hydr-', meaning water, and the suffix '-ant', denoting an agent or something that performs an action.
