C. H. Best

1. PROPN

/ si eɪtʃ bɛst / (AmE), / siː eɪtʃ best / (BrE)
(1899–1978) Canadian physiologist, born in the United States, who assisted F. G. Banting in the research that led to the discovery of insulin.
Canadian physiologist, co-discoverer of insulin

Examples

C. H. Best worked with Banting on experiments that would lead to the discovery of insulin.
Medical students often study the achievements of C. H. Best in physiology classes.
The Nobel Prize for the discovery of insulin was awarded to Banting, but C. H. Best's contribution was crucial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Frederick Banting - Canadian medical scientist who co-discovered insulin
