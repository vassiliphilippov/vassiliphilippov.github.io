syllogiser

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlədʒaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈsɪlədʒaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who is skilled in or practices syllogistic reasoning, especially in formal logic
logician skilled in syllogistic reasoning

Examples

The seasoned syllogiser dissected the argument with remarkable precision.
During the debate, she proved herself a brilliant syllogiser who could expose logical fallacies easily.
As a syllogiser, his expertise in classical logic was admired by his peers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logician - an expert in or student of logic

Etymology

From ‘syllogise’ + ‘-er’; ‘syllogise’ from Greek syllogizesthai (to infer).
