disposed

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈspoʊzd / (AmE), / dɪˈspəʊzd / (BrE)
having made preparations or ready to do something; prepared
prepared or ready to do something

Examples

He felt disposed to accept the challenging assignment after considering his options.
The team was well disposed to begin the project as soon as funding was approved.
She is always disposed to help others whenever she can.

Synset Examples

prepared to take risks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prepared - ready or made ready for use or action

Etymology

From Latin 'disponere' (to arrange); via past participle 'disposed'.

WordNet ID: oewn-02576155-s

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈspoʊzd / (AmE), / dɪˈspəʊzd / (BrE)
having a tendency or inclination (to do something); likely or willing (to do something), usually followed by 'to' or 'towards'.
inclined or willing to do something

Examples

She seemed disposed to accept their offer after the meeting.
If you are disposed to help, please let us know as soon as possible.
He was not disposed to discuss the details openly.

Synset Examples

he is apt to ignore matters he considers unimportant
I am not minded to answer any questions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inclined - having a tendency toward a particular action or attitude
  • Antonym: unwilling - not wanting or prepared to do something

Etymology

From Old French disposer (to arrange), from Latin disponere (to arrange, put in order).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01295534-s
Synset POS: s
