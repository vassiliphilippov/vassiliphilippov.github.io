Odontoceti

1. NOUN

/ oʊˌdɒntəˈsiːtaɪ / (AmE), / əʊˌdɒntəˈsiːtaɪ / (BrE)
A suborder of whales characterized by having teeth, including dolphins, porpoises, sperm whales, and beaked whales.
suborder of toothed whales

Examples

The Odontoceti include animals such as dolphins, porpoises, and sperm whales.
Unlike baleen whales, members of the Odontoceti have well-developed teeth for hunting prey.
Scientists study Odontoceti to better understand echolocation and marine mammal intelligence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Mysticeti - the suborder of baleen whales

Etymology

From Greek 'odous' (tooth) and 'ketos' (whale, sea monster) via scientific Latin.

2. NOUN

/ oʊˌdɑːntəˈsiːtaɪ / (AmE), / əʊˌdɒntəˈsiːtaɪ / (BrE)
a taxonomic suborder of whales comprising all toothed whales, including dolphins, porpoises, sperm whales, and beaked whales
suborder of toothed whales

Examples

All dolphins and porpoises belong to the suborder Odontoceti.
Members of the Odontoceti group are distinguished by having teeth instead of baleen plates.
Scientists study the Odontoceti to better understand the evolution of echolocation in marine mammals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Mysticeti - suborder of baleen whales

Etymology

From Greek 'odous, odont-' (tooth) + 'ketos' (whale).
