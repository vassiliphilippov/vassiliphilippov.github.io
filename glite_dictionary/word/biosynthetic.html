biosynthetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊ.sɪnˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əʊ.sɪnˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving biosynthesis, especially in living organisms or artificial systems
relating to biosynthesis

Examples

The biosynthetic pathway for this antibiotic involves several enzyme-catalyzed reactions.
Researchers are developing biosynthetic materials that mimic natural processes.
She studied the biosynthetic mechanisms by which plants create essential amino acids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biosynthesis - the production of complex molecules within living organisms or cells

Etymology

From 'bio-' (life) + 'synthetic', from Greek *bios* (life) and *synthetikos* (skilled in putting together).
