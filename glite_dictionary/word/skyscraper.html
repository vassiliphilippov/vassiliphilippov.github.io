skyscraper

1. NOUN

/ ˈskaɪˌskreɪpər / (AmE), / ˈskaɪˌskreɪpə / (BrE)
a very tall building with many floors, typically found in cities
a very tall city building

Examples

The city skyline was dominated by a new skyscraper that opened last month.
From his apartment on the fiftieth floor of a skyscraper, he could see for miles.
Tourists gathered at the base of the skyscraper to take photographs of its impressive height.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: high-rise - a tall building with many stories, especially for residential or office use

Etymology

Formed from 'sky' and 'scraper', first used in the late 19th century to describe tall buildings reaching into the sky.
