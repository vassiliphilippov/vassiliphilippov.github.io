pluck

1. VERB

/ plʌk / (AmE), / plʌk / (BrE)
to remove the feathers from a bird, especially one that is to be cooked
remove feathers from a bird

Examples

She had to pluck the chickens before cooking them for dinner.
The chef taught the apprentice how to properly pluck a duck.
After catching the bird, he began to pluck its feathers.

Synset Examples

pull a chicken
pluck the capon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skin - to remove the skin from an animal or fruit

Etymology

From Old English *pluccian* (to pull off, pluck), from West Germanic.
