Addressograph

1. NOUN

/ əˈdrɛsəˌɡrɑf / (AmE), / əˈdrɛsəɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a machine used for automatically printing addresses onto letters or envelopes for mailing
address-printing machine for mail

Examples

The office used an Addressograph to speed up the process of mailing invoices.
With the old Addressograph, hundreds of labels could be printed in just an hour.
Many companies replaced their Addressograph machines with modern laser printers.

Etymology

From 'address' + 'graph', reflecting its function to print (write) addresses.
