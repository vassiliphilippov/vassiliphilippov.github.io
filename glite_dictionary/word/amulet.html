amulet

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmjələt / (AmE), / ˈæmjʊlɪt / (BrE)
A small object or piece of jewelry, often worn around the neck, believed to provide magical protection against evil, danger, or disease.
magical object worn for protection

Examples

He wore an amulet around his neck to ward off bad luck.
Some people believe that carrying an amulet can protect them from illness.
The ancient tomb contained a golden amulet among the other treasures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talisman - an object thought to have magic powers and bring good luck

Etymology

From Latin amuletum (means of defense).
