carpellary

1. ADJ

/ kɑrˈpɛləri / (AmE), / kɑːˈpɛləri / (BrE)
Relating to or consisting of carpels, the female reproductive organs of a flower.
relating to carpels in plants
medical

Examples

The carpellary tissue develops into the fruit wall after fertilization.
Microscopic examination revealed a thick carpellary wall surrounding the ovules.
The flower's carpellary structure plays a key role in seed protection and dispersal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carpel - the female reproductive organ of a flower

Etymology

From carpel + -ary, following botanical scientific naming patterns.
