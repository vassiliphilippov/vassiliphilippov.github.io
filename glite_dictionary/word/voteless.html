voteless

1. ADJ

/ ˈvoʊt.ləs / (AmE), / ˈvəʊt.ləs / (BrE)
Deprived of the right to vote, especially as a result of being denied citizenship or other qualifications.
lacking the right to vote

Examples

In many countries, women remained voteless until the early twentieth century.
The government faced criticism for leaving minority groups voteless in key elections.
He campaigned tirelessly for the rights of the voteless population.

Synset Examples

labor was voiceless
disenfranchised masses took to the streets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disenfranchised - deprived of the right to vote or other rights
  • Antonym: enfranchised - having the right to vote; granted the rights of citizenship

Etymology

Formed from 'vote' + '-less', meaning 'without a vote'.
