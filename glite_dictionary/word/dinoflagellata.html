Dinoflagellata

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪnoʊflædʒəˈleɪtə / (AmE), / ˌdɪnəʊflædʒəˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A group of single-celled organisms, mostly aquatic, some of which can cause red tides; classified as a phylum within the kingdom Protista or as part of Pyrrophyta in some taxonomies.
group of aquatic single-celled organisms

Examples

Scientists study Dinoflagellata to understand their role in ocean ecosystems.
Some species within the Dinoflagellata can form harmful algal blooms known as red tides.
The bioluminescence seen in certain coastal waters is often caused by Dinoflagellata.

Etymology

From Greek 'dinos' (whirling) + 'flagellum' (little whip).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪ.noʊˌflædʒ.əˈleɪ.tə / (AmE), / ˌdɪn.əʊˌflædʒ.əˈleɪ.tə / (BrE)
a taxonomic group of single-celled organisms with two flagella, considered an order or phylum in different classifications, mainly found in aquatic environments and sometimes included in the plant phylum Pyrrophyta
group of aquatic, flagellated protists

Examples

Species belonging to Dinoflagellata are crucial in marine plankton communities.
Red tides are often caused by blooms of Dinoflagellata.
Scientists discovered new bioluminescent characteristics within several Dinoflagellata species.

Etymology

From Greek 'dinos' (whirling) + 'flagellum' (Latin, little whip).
