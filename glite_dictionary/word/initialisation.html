initialisation

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌnɪʃələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌnɪʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(computing) the process of preparing a storage device, such as a hard disk, by formatting it so that it can be used by an operating system, and setting an initial or starting state for use.
formatting and setting up a computer device
british

Examples

Before installing a new operating system, the disk requires initialisation to ensure it is ready for use.
The initialisation process can take several minutes, depending on the size of the drive.
Errors during initialisation may prevent the software from accessing the storage device.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: initialization - preparing a device by formatting and setting initial state (American spelling)

Etymology

From initialise + -ation; 'initialise' from 'initial' + '-ise', common in British technical English.
