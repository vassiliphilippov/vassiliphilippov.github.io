Malacosoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæləˈkoʊsəmə / (AmE), / ˌmaləˈkəʊsəmə / (BrE)
A genus of moths whose larvae are commonly known as tent caterpillars, recognized for building silk tents in trees.
genus of moths (tent caterpillars)

Examples

The Malacosoma caterpillars built large silk tents in the branches of the cherry tree.
Entomologists studied Malacosoma populations to understand their impact on local forests.
A sudden outbreak of Malacosoma larvae can defoliate entire sections of woodland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tent caterpillar - larvae of Malacosoma moths, known for spinning silk tents

Etymology

From Greek malakos (soft) + sōma (body).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæləkəˈsoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌmæləkəˈsəʊmə / (BrE)
A genus of moths in the family Lasiocampidae, known as tent caterpillars, whose larvae build communal silk tents in trees.
genus of tent caterpillar moths

Examples

Malacosoma species are responsible for the characteristic silk tents seen in many North American hardwood trees.
The destructive outbreak of Malacosoma caterpillars caused widespread defoliation in the region.
Scientists studied the population cycles of Malacosoma to better understand ecological balance in forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tent caterpillar - larval form of moths in the genus Malacosoma

Etymology

From Greek 'malakos' (soft) and 'soma' (body), referring to their soft-bodied larvae.
