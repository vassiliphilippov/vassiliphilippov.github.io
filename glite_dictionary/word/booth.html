booth

1. NOUN

/ buːθ / (AmE), / buːθ / (BrE)
A small temporary stall or structure at a fair, market, or exhibition, used for selling goods or providing entertainment.
small stall or shop at a fair

Examples

We bought homemade jam from a booth at the local fair.
Each charity had its own booth to raise funds and awareness.
They set up a booth at the entrance to sell tickets for the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stall - a small, open-fronted shop or stand in a market

Etymology

From Middle English 'bothe', from Old Norse 'búð' (dwelling, stall).

2. NOUN

/ buːθ / (AmE), / buːθ / (BrE)
A small, partly enclosed area or compartment set off by walls or partitions for a specific purpose, such as voting, selling goods, or dining.
small enclosed area for special purpose

Examples

She cast her vote in a private booth at the polling station.
The art fair had a booth for each local artist to display their work.
We reserved a booth at the back of the diner for extra privacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stall - an open-fronted booth for selling goods in a market

Etymology

From Middle English 'bothe' (hut, stall), from Old Norse 'búð' (dwelling, stall).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02877081-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ buːθ / (AmE), / buːθ / (BrE)
a table in a restaurant or bar surrounded by two high-backed benches, providing a semi-private seating area
partially enclosed restaurant/bar seating

Examples

We asked the waiter if we could sit in a booth by the window.
They reserved a booth for six people at their favorite diner.
The cozy booth made our meal feel more private and comfortable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: table - piece of furniture with a flat top and one or more legs

Etymology

From Middle English bothe, from Old Norse búð (temporary shelter or stall).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
