significance

1. NOUN

/ sɪɡˈnɪfɪkəns / (AmE), / sɪɡˈnɪfɪkəns / (BrE)
the meaning or message that is intended, expressed, or implied by something
meaning or conveyed message

Examples

The full significance of the painting became clear only after the artist's explanation.
Many people fail to understand the significance of this small change in policy.
She pondered the significance of his words for a long time.

Synset Examples

what is the meaning of this sentence?
the significance of a red traffic light
the signification of Chinese characters
the import of his announcement was ambiguous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meaning - the thing that is meant or intended by something
  • Antonym: insignificance - the lack of meaning or importance

Etymology

From Latin significare (to signify, indicate) via Old French signifiance.

2. NOUN

/ sɪɡˈnɪf.ɪ.kəns / (AmE), / sɪɡˈnɪf.ɪ.kəns / (BrE)
an implied or underlying meaning that is not directly stated
underlying or implied meaning

Examples

The true significance of his answer was not noticed until much later.
She smiled with a significance that was understood only by those who knew her well.
The song's lyrics have a cultural significance beyond their surface meaning.

Synset Examples

the significance of his remark became clear only later
the expectation was spread both by word and by implication

Related Terms

  • Synonym: implication - suggested or indirect meaning
  • Antonym: explicitness - the quality of being stated clearly and in detail

Etymology

From Latin significantia (meaning, force, importance), from significare (to signify).

3. NOUN

/ sɪɡˈnɪf.ɪ.kəns / (AmE), / sɪɡˈnɪf.ɪ.kəns / (BrE)
the quality of being important or having a special meaning; the importance or meaning of something
importance or special meaning

Examples

The significance of this discovery cannot be overstated.
She finally understood the significance of his words.
Researchers debated the significance of the new data.

Synset Examples

do not underestimate the significance of nuclear power

Related Terms

  • Synonym: importance - the quality of being important
  • Antonym: insignificance - the quality of being unimportant

Etymology

From Latin signiﬁcantia (importance, meaning), from signiﬁcō (to signify).
