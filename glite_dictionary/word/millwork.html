millwork

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈmɪlˌwɜːk / (BrE)
woodwork such as doors, moldings, or trim that has been shaped or finished in a mill rather than at the building site
machined woodwork from a mill

Examples

The custom millwork in the foyer included hand-carved moldings and elaborate stair railings.
We ordered new millwork for the windows to match the historic details of the house.
Much of the building’s interior millwork was crafted from locally sourced oak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joinery - woodwork, especially connecting parts, made by a joiner

Etymology

From 'mill' (a factory for processing materials) + 'work'.
