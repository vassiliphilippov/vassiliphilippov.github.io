fervor

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɜːrvər / (AmE), / ˈfɜːvə / (BrE)
Intense and passionate feeling; great warmth and enthusiasm.
intense passion or enthusiasm

Examples

The fervor of the crowd at the concert was electrifying.
She spoke with such fervor that everyone listened intently.
Religious fervor can inspire people to extraordinary acts.

Synset Examples

he spoke with great ardor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ardor - intense passion or enthusiasm
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of feeling, interest, or concern

Etymology

From Latin 'fervor' (heat, passion), from 'fervere' (to boil, to glow).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɜrvər / (AmE), / ˈfɜːvə / (BrE)
intense and passionate feeling; a state of emotional excitement or enthusiasm.
intense emotion or enthusiasm

Examples

Her speech was delivered with such fervor that the audience gave a standing ovation.
The team played with great fervor in the final minutes of the match.
Debates on social media are often marked by unexpected fervor.

Synset Examples

his face was flushed with excitement and his hands trembled
he tried to calm those who were in a state of extreme inflammation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passion - strong and barely controllable emotion
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of interest or emotion

Etymology

From Latin 'fervor' (heat, boiling, ardor), from 'fervere' (to boil).
