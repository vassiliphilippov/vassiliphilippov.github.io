Acanthocephala

1. NOUN

/ əˌkænθəˈsɛfələ / (AmE), / əˌkanθəʊˈsɛfələ / (BrE)
A phylum of elongated, wormlike parasitic animals, commonly known as spiny-headed worms, that live in the intestines of vertebrates.
parasitic worm phylum: spiny-headed worms

Examples

The parasitic worms classified under Acanthocephala are noted for their spiny proboscises.
Several fish species are vulnerable to infection by Acanthocephala.
Scientists study Acanthocephala to understand the life cycle of intestinal parasites.

Etymology

From Greek akantha (thorn, spine) + kephalē (head), referring to their spiny proboscis.
