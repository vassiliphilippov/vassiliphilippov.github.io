woodwaxen

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdˌwæksən / (AmE), / ˈwʊdwəksən / (BrE)
a small Eurasian shrub (Genista tinctoria) with clusters of yellow flowers that yield a dye, often found as a weed in Britain and the US, and sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant
a Eurasian shrub with yellow flowers used for dye

Examples

The cottage garden had a patch of woodwaxen blooming beside the path.
Long ago, woodwaxen was cultivated for its yellow dye, favored by craftsmen.
Woodwaxen is sometimes considered a weed when it spreads uncontrollably in meadows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dyer's greenweed - another name for the same shrub (Genista tinctoria)

Etymology

From wood + waxen, referring to the plant's use and appearance.
