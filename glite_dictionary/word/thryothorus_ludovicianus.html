Thryothorus ludovicianus

1. NOUN

/ ˌθraɪ.əˈθɔːr.əs ˌluː.dəˌvɪʃ.iˈæn.əs / (AmE), / ˌθraɪ.əˈθɔː.rəs ˌluː.dəˌvɪʃ.iˈæn.əs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the Carolina wren, a large wren native to the United States known for its musical call
scientific name for the Carolina wren

Examples

The scientific name Thryothorus ludovicianus refers to the Carolina wren, a familiar bird in the southern United States.
Birdwatchers were delighted to spot a pair of Thryothorus ludovicianus building a nest near the trail.
Researchers have studied the unique song patterns of Thryothorus ludovicianus in different regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Carolina wren - a common wren native to the southeastern United States

Etymology

From Greek *thruon* (reed), *thōros* (leaping), and Latin *ludovicianus* (of Louisiana).
