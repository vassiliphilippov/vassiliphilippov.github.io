to migrate

1. VERB

/ ˈmaɪˌɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˈmaɪɡreɪt / (BrE)
to move from one country or region to another, often to live or settle there permanently or for a long period
move to a new country or region

Examples

In the 1800s, thousands of people migrated to the United States in search of better opportunities.
Birds migrate to warmer regions during the winter months.
As economic conditions worsened, many families migrated from rural villages to bigger cities.

Synset Examples

Many Germans migrated to South America in the mid-19th century
This tribe transmigrated many times over the centuries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emigrate - to leave one's own country to settle in another
  • Synonym: to immigrate - to come into another country to live permanently

Etymology

From Latin 'migratus', past participle of 'migrare' (to move from one place to another).

2. VERB

/ ˈmaɪˌɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˈmaɪɡreɪt / (BrE)
(of people or animals) to move from one region or habitat to another, especially according to the season or in search of work or better living conditions.
move from one region or habitat to another

Examples

Every autumn, the geese migrate south to warmer climates.
Some fish species migrate upstream to spawn during the spring.
Thousands of workers migrate to the city each year in search of employment.

Synset Examples

birds migrate in the winter
The workers migrate to where the crops need harvesting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emigrate - to leave one's country to live in another
  • Synonym: to immigrate - to come to live permanently in a foreign country

Etymology

From Latin 'migratus', past participle of 'migrare' (to move from one place to another).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
