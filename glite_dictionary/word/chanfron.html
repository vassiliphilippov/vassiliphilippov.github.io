chanfron

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃænfɹɑn / (AmE), / ˈʃan.frɒn / (BrE)
A piece of medieval plate armor designed to protect a horse's head during battle or tournaments.
medieval horse head armor

Examples

The knight fitted the elaborate chanfron onto his charger before the tournament.
A gilded chanfron was displayed at the museum's medieval armor exhibit.
During battle, the chanfron shielded the horse from arrows and sword strikes.

Etymology

From Old French 'chanfrein', meaning horse's head or nose.
