Andromeda polifolia

1. NOUN

/ ænˈdrɑːmədə ˌpɒlɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ænˈdrɒmɪdə ˌpɒlɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A small evergreen shrub with clusters of white or pink flowers, found in peat bogs and wet acidic areas of northern Europe and North America.
evergreen bog shrub with pink or white flowers

Examples

The Andromeda polifolia thrives in the acidic soils of northern peat bogs.
Gardeners often admire the delicate pink blossoms of Andromeda polifolia in the spring.
Conservationists monitor Andromeda polifolia populations to assess the health of wetland ecosystems.

Etymology

From Latin, the genus name Andromeda after the mythological princess, and polifolia meaning 'having leaves like Polium' (a plant genus).
