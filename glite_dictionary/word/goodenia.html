Goodenia

1. NOUN

/ ɡuːˈdiːniə / (AmE), / ɡuːˈdiːniə / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs and herbs native to Australia, New Guinea, Malaysia, and Southeast Asia, belonging to the family Goodeniaceae.
genus of shrubs and herbs (Goodeniaceae)

Examples

Many species of Goodenia are prized for their attractive yellow flowers in Australian gardens.
The Goodenia genus is known for its adaptability to various climates across Southeast Asia.
Botanists discovered a new Goodenia species in the wilds of New Guinea.

Etymology

Named after Samuel Goodenough (1743–1827), an English bishop and botanist.
