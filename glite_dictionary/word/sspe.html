SSPE

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛs ˌɛs piː ˈiː / (AmE), / ˌɛs ˌɛs piː ˈiː / (BrE)
A rare, chronic and progressive inflammation of the brain, chiefly affecting children and young adults, caused by persistent infection with the measles virus.
rare chronic brain disease from measles virus
medical

Examples

The neurologist diagnosed the child with SSPE after noting progressive cognitive decline and neurological symptoms.
Vaccination against measles is crucial in preventing cases of SSPE in children.
Despite intensive treatment, most patients with SSPE have a poor prognosis.

Etymology

Acronym for subacute sclerosing panencephalitis.
