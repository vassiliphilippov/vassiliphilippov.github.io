milligram

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪləˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪˌɡræm / (BrE)
a unit of mass equal to one thousandth of a gram (0.001 g)
one thousandth of a gram

Examples

The doctor prescribed a dose of 10 milligrams per day.
Each tablet contains only one milligram of the active ingredient.
Trace amounts are measured in milligrams for accuracy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gram - unit of mass in the metric system, equal to one thousandth of a kilogram

Etymology

From French 'milligramme', from Latin 'mille' (thousand) + Greek 'gramma' (a small weight).
