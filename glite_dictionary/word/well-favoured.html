well-favoured

1. ADJ

/ ˌwɛlˈfeɪvərd / (AmE), / ˌwɛlˈfeɪvəd / (BrE)
Attractive or good-looking, especially by conforming to conventional standards of beauty or proportion.
attractive or good-looking
archaic

Examples

She was described as a well-favoured maiden by all who met her.
In the old portraits, the king appeared robust and well-favoured.
He envied his well-favoured cousin, who was admired by everyone in town.

Synset Examples

a fine-looking woman
a good-looking man
better-looking than her sister
very pretty but not so extraordinarily handsome
our southern women are well-favored

Related Terms

  • Synonym: good-looking - physically attractive or pleasing in appearance
  • Synonym: handsome - attractive in appearance, especially of men
  • Antonym: plain - not beautiful or attractive in appearance

Etymology

Formed from 'well' + archaic 'favoured' (having a particular appearance), with 'favoured' from Middle English, past participle of 'faven' (to favor).
