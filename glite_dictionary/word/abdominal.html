abdominal

1. ADJ

/ əbˈdɑːmənl̩ / (AmE), / əbˈdɒmɪn(ə)l / (BrE)
relating to or situated in the abdomen.
relating to the abdomen

Examples

He suffered an abdominal injury during the match, which required immediate medical attention.
The doctor performed an abdominal examination to check for tenderness or swelling.
She focused her workout on abdominal exercises to strengthen her core muscles.

Synset Examples

abdominal muscles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abdominal muscle - a muscle of the abdomen
  • Antonym: thoracic - relating to the chest (thorax)

Etymology

From Latin 'abdōminālis', from 'abdōmen' (belly).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02945931-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ æbˈdɑmənl / (AmE), / æbˈdɒmɪnəl / (BrE)
a muscle of the abdomen, especially any of the muscles forming the abdominal wall; commonly refers to the group of muscles in the front of the abdomen.
abdominal muscle; front torso muscle

Examples

He works out every day to strengthen his abdominal and core muscles.
Pain in the abdominal can be a sign of strain or injury.
Sit-ups are an effective exercise for targeting your abdominal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abs - slang term for abdominal muscles

Etymology

From Latin "abdomen" (belly) + English suffix "-al".

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05564972-n
Synset POS: n
