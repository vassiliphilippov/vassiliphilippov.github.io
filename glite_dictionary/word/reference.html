reference

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛf.ɚ.əns / (AmE), / ˈrɛf.ər.əns / (BrE)
a publication or a specific passage that is mentioned or cited within a text to support information or provide evidence.
a cited publication or passage

Examples

She listed several references at the end of her paper for further reading.
The professor asked for a reference to verify the statistics in the report.
This article includes a reference to an important historical document.

Synset Examples

he carried an armful of references back to his desk
he spent hours looking for the source of that quotation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: citation - a reference to a published or unpublished source
  • Synonym: source - the origin or provider of information, often cited in academic work

Etymology

From Latin referre (to bring back, report).
