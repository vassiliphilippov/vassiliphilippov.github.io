hierarch

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪərˌɑrk / (AmE), / ˈhaɪərɑːk / (BrE)
a person who holds a high position in a hierarchy, especially in a religious or organizational context
person of high rank in a hierarchy

Examples

The religious hierarch delivered an important address to the congregation.
In medieval times, each hierarch had specific duties within the church.
A respected hierarch often guides policy decisions in traditional organizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superior - a person of higher rank or status
  • Antonym: subordinate - a person of lower rank or authority

Etymology

From Greek 'hierarkhēs', from 'hieros' (sacred) + 'archein' (to rule).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪəˌrɑrk / (AmE), / ˈhaɪərɑːk / (BrE)
a senior clergyman or dignitary in a religious hierarchy, especially in the Christian Church
senior clergyman or religious dignitary

Examples

The Orthodox hierarch presided over the ceremony with great solemnity.
As a respected hierarch, he played a key role in church decision-making.
Several senior hierarchs gathered for the important synod.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prelate - a high-ranking member of the clergy
  • Synonym: clergyman - a male member of the clergy

Etymology

From Greek hierarkhēs, from hieros (sacred) + arkhein (to rule).
