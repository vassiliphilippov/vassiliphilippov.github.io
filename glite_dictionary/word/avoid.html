to avoid

1. VERB

/ əˈvɔɪd / (AmE), / əˈvɔɪd / (BrE)
To keep away from someone or something; to stay clear of or prevent coming into contact with.
keep away from; stay clear of

Examples

He tried to avoid making eye contact with his boss.
Many people avoid driving in heavy rain whenever possible.
They avoided the area after hearing about the recent accidents.

Synset Examples

Her former friends now avoid her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evade - to escape or avoid, especially by cleverness
  • Antonym: to confront - to face or deal with a problem or difficult situation

Etymology

From Old French 'evuider', from Latin 'evitare' (to shun, avoid).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00813037-v
Synset POS: v

/ əˈvɔɪd / (AmE), / əˈvɔɪd / (BrE)
to prevent something bad from happening, or to stay away from something or someone
to prevent or stay away from

Examples

She took a different route to avoid heavy traffic.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evade - to escape or avoid, especially by cleverness or trickery
