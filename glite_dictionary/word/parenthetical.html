parenthetical

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrənˈθɛtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌparənˈθɛtɪkəl / (BrE)
an expression, remark, or word inserted within parentheses in a sentence
expression in parentheses

Examples

His essay contained several parentheticals that distracted from the main argument.
The author used a funny parenthetical to share extra information with readers.
Too many parentheticals can make a passage harder to follow.

Synset Examples

his writing was full of parentheticals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aside - a remark or passage that is not part of the main subject

Etymology

From Greek 'parenthesis' (insertion) via Late Latin 'parentheticus'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06326866-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˌpɛrənˈθɛtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌpærənˈθɛtɪkəl / (BrE)
(of a remark or phrase) qualifying or explanatory; inserted as if within parentheses.
qualifying or explanatory, in parentheses

Examples

Her speech was filled with parenthetical remarks that offered extra information.
The author used a parenthetical explanation to clarify the difficult term.
He included a parenthetical comment to add humor without disrupting the main point.

Synset Examples

parenthetical remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incidental - introduced as a minor or secondary element
  • Antonym: essential - absolutely necessary; extremely important

Etymology

From Greek *parenthētikos*, from *parentithenai* meaning 'to put in beside'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01863467-s
Synset POS: s
