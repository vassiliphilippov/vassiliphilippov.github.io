Pinus mugo

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪnəs ˈmjuːɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpʌɪnəs ˈmjuːɡəʊ / (BrE)
A low, shrubby pine native to central Europe, with short, bright green needles in clusters of two; often used in landscaping and horticulture.
low shrubby central European pine

Examples

The Pinus mugo is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its compact size.
Botanists studied the resilience of Pinus mugo in harsh alpine conditions.
Our landscaping plan includes several Pinus mugo for evergreen texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain pine - common name for Pinus mugo, the same plant

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Pinus' means 'pine' and 'mugo' refers to this species.
