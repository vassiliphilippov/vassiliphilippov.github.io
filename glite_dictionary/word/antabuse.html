Antabuse

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.təˌbjuːs / (AmE), / ˈantəˌbjuːs / (BrE)
a brand name for disulfiram, a drug used in the treatment of alcoholism that causes unpleasant effects when alcohol is consumed
drug for alcoholism treatment
medical

Examples

After starting Antabuse, he experienced severe nausea when he tried to drink alcohol.
Doctors may prescribe Antabuse to help patients remain abstinent from drinking.
It is important to consult a physician before beginning a course of Antabuse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disulfiram - the generic name of the drug used to support the treatment of chronic alcoholism

Etymology

Trademark from 'anti-' (against) and 'abuse'.
