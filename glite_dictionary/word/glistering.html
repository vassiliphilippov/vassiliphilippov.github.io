glistering

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡlɪstərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪstərɪŋ / (BrE)
shining with brief, brilliant points or flashes of light; sparkling or glittering.
sparkling or glittering with flashes of light
archaic

Examples

The knight's armor was glistering in the early morning sun as he rode into battle.
Her eyes, glistering with excitement, revealed her true feelings.
The river, glistering under the moonlight, looked almost magical that evening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glistening - shining with reflected light, especially when wet or polished

Etymology

Archaic form of 'glister', from Middle English, probably of Scandinavian origin related to 'glisten'.
