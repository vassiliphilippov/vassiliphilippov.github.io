plainness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪnnəs / (AmE), / ˈpleɪnnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being easy to understand or free from ambiguity; clarity in expression or appearance
clarity; the state of being easy to understand

Examples

The plainness of her speech made her arguments easy for everyone to follow.
He admired the plainness with which the instructions were written.
The document's plainness helped prevent any misunderstanding among team members.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand
  • Antonym: obscurity - the quality of being difficult to understand

Etymology

From Middle English 'plain', from Old French 'plain', from Latin 'planus' (flat, clear).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪnnəs / (AmE), / ˈpleɪnnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being simple, pure, or unmixed with other substances
state of being unmixed or pure

Examples

The plainness of the soup allowed the natural flavors to shine through.
Many appreciated the plainness of the room, which had no unnecessary decorations.
She admired the plainness of the document's design for its simplicity and clarity.

Synset Examples

the plainness of vanilla ice cream

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purity - the condition of being pure, not mixed with anything else

Etymology

From plain + -ness; 'plain' from Old French 'plain' (clear, simple), from Latin 'planus' (flat, clear).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪnnɪs / (AmE), / ˈpleɪnnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of having an appearance that is not attractive or beautiful
lack of attractiveness; not beautiful

Examples

Her plainness did not prevent her from having many friends and admirers.
Some designers intentionally highlight the plainness of certain models to challenge beauty standards.
Despite the plainness of the building, it served its purpose well.

Synset Examples

fine clothes could not conceal the girl's homeliness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homeliness - the quality of being unattractive in appearance
  • Antonym: beauty - the quality of being pleasing to the senses or to the mind

Etymology

From Middle English 'pleyn', from Old French 'plain', from Latin 'planus' (flat, level, simple).

4. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪnnəs / (AmE), / ˈpleɪnnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being simple and without decoration or pretension
simplicity; lack of decoration or pretension

Examples

The plainness of the room made it feel calm and welcoming.
She admired the plainness of his speech, which was honest and direct.
Despite the plainness of her dress, she carried herself with dignity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simplicity - the quality of being simple or easy
  • Antonym: ornateness - the quality of being elaborate and highly decorated

Etymology

From Middle English 'playn' (plain, clear) + '-ness' (state or quality).
