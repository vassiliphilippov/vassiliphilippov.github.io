Argentinidae

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˌdʒɛn.tɪˈniː.di / (AmE), / ɑːˌdʒɛn.tɪˈniː.diː / (BrE)
A family of small marine fishes with long, silvery bodies, related to salmons and trouts.
family of small marine silvery fishes

Examples

The Argentinidae are commonly found in deep ocean waters.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Argentinidae family during their expedition.
Members of Argentinidae are often recognized by their elongated silvery bodies.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Argentinidae', based on the genus 'Argentina' (from Latin 'argentum', silver).

2. NOUN

/ ɑrˌdʒɛntɪˈniːdi / (AmE), / ɑːˌdʒɛntɪˈniːdiː / (BrE)
a family of small marine fishes with long, silvery bodies, related to salmons and trouts
family of small silvery marine fishes

Examples

The Argentinidae are known for their elongated, silvery bodies found in deep marine waters.
Scientists have classified these unique fish as members of the Argentinidae family.
The coloration of most Argentinidae species helps them evade predators in their natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Salmonidae - family of fishes including salmons and trouts
  • Degree Lower: Clupeidae - family of herrings and related fishes

Etymology

From the genus name Argentinus, derived from Latin 'argentum' (silver), referring to the silvery appearance of the fishes.
