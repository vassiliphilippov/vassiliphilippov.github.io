indulgently

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈdʌl.dʒənt.li / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌl.dʒənt.li / (BrE)
in a way that allows someone to have what they want, especially when it is not good for them; in a permissive or lenient manner.
in a permissive or lenient way

Examples

She smiled indulgently at the children as they played in the garden.
He nodded indulgently at his daughter's request for another bedtime story.
The teacher spoke indulgently, understanding the students’ excitement before the holiday.

Synset Examples

I was indulgently advised to give the kids what they wanted unless I wished my son to be socially ostracised

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leniently - in a tolerant or permissive way

Etymology

From 'indulgent', based on Latin 'indulgēre' (to be kind or tender).
