voluptuous

1. ADJ

/ vəˈlʌptʃuəs / (AmE), / vəˈlʌptjʊəs / (BrE)
(of a woman's body) attractively curvaceous and full-figured, especially with a large bosom and rounded shape.
attractively curvaceous (of a woman's body)

Examples

The actress was praised for her voluptuous figure and classic Hollywood glamour.
She wore a dress that accentuated her voluptuous curves at the party.
Artists often depict goddesses as voluptuous, celebrating the beauty of the female form.

Synset Examples

Hollywood seems full of curvaceous blondes
a curvy young woman in a tight dress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curvaceous - (of a woman) having an attractively curved body

Etymology

From Latin 'voluptuosus' (full of pleasure or delights), from 'voluptas' (pleasure).

2. ADJ

/ vəˈlʌptʃuəs / (AmE), / vəˈlʌptjʊəs / (BrE)
Providing or characterized by luxury and sensual pleasure; giving gratification to the senses.
characterized by luxury and sensual pleasure

Examples

The dining room was decorated in a voluptuous style, with deep red velvet curtains and gold accents.
She reclined in a voluptuous armchair, savoring the rich textures and scents of the room.
The hotel suite offered a voluptuous sense of comfort, from the silk sheets to the gourmet chocolates.

Synset Examples

an epicurean banquet
enjoyed a luxurious suite with a crystal chandelier and thick oriental rugs
Lucullus spent the remainder of his days in voluptuous magnificence
a chinchilla robe of sybaritic lavishness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luxurious - comfortable and expensive; providing luxury
  • Antonym: austere - severe or strict in manner, attitude, or appearance; having no luxuries

Etymology

From Latin voluptuosus (full of pleasure, voluptuous), from voluptas (pleasure).

Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ vəˈlʌptʃuəs / (AmE), / vəˈlʌptjʊəs / (BrE)
(especially of a woman's body) attractive and curvaceous in a way that strongly suggests sensual or sexual appeal.
sexually attractive and curvaceous (esp. of a woman)

Examples

She was a voluptuous woman, with curves that drew everyone's gaze at the party.
The actress is famous for her voluptuous figure and confident demeanor on screen.
The artist preferred to paint voluptuous models, celebrating the beauty of the human body.

Synset Examples

juicy barmaids
a red-hot mama
a voluptuous woman
a toothsome blonde in a tight dress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seductive - sexually attractive and likely to seduce or attract attention
  • Synonym: sensual - giving or expressing physical, especially sexual, pleasure
  • Antonym: chaste - not having sexual nature or intention

Etymology

From Latin voluptuosus (full of delight, pleasurable), from voluptas (pleasure).
