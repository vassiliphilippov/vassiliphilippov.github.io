actor-proof

1. ADJ

/ ˈæktər ˌpruːf / (AmE), / ˈæktə ˌpruːf / (BrE)
designed to withstand misuse or poor performance, especially by inexperienced users or performers; not easily affected by mistakes
resistant to misuse or poor performance
informal

Examples

The instructions were so clear that even the most inexperienced member of the cast found them actor-proof.
They installed actor-proof lighting controls to prevent accidental blackouts during the show.
The director insisted on an actor-proof set design to reduce the risk of mistakes on stage.

Synset Examples

They designed the equipment to be actor-proof for safety.
The new safety protocols made the set actor-proof.
Her performance was so solid, it felt actor-proof.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foolproof - designed so as not to fail or be misused

Etymology

Modeled after compounds like 'childproof'; uses 'actor' to refer to performers or users.
