Thraupidae

1. NOUN

/ θræˈjupɪˌdi / (AmE), / θræˈjuːpɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of small to medium-sized songbirds found mainly in the New World tropics, commonly known as tanagers.
family of birds: tanagers

Examples

Many species within the Thraupidae family exhibit vibrant plumage and melodious songs.
Researchers discovered a new member of the Thraupidae in the Amazon rainforest.
The diversity of Thraupidae makes them a popular subject among ornithologists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tanager - any of several species of brightly colored birds in the Thraupidae family

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Thraupis, from Ancient Greek thraupis (a kind of small bird).
