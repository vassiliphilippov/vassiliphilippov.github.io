inflorescence

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnfloʊˈrɛsəns / (AmE), / ˌɪn.fləˈrɛs.əns / (BrE)
the period and process during which flowers bud and open on a plant
process of budding and blossoming flowers
medical

Examples

The inflorescence of the magnolia tree is a spectacular sight each spring.
Gardeners observed the inflorescence period to record the flower development stages.
Prolonged rainfall can affect the inflorescence of certain crops, delaying their bloom.

Etymology

From Latin 'inflorescere' (to begin to bloom), from 'in-' (in) + 'florescere' (to begin to flower).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.fləˈrɛs.əns / (AmE), / ˌɪn.fləˈrɛs.əns / (BrE)
the complete flower head of a plant including stems, stalks, bracts, and flowers, or the way the flowers are arranged on a plant
arrangement of flowers on a plant
medical

Examples

The sunflower's large inflorescence is made up of hundreds of tiny florets.
Botanists classify plants based on the type of inflorescence they produce.
After several weeks, the orchid finally developed its first inflorescence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flower - the reproductive structure of a flowering plant

Etymology

From Latin 'inflorescere', meaning 'to begin to bloom', from 'in-' (in, on) + 'florescere' (to begin to bloom).
