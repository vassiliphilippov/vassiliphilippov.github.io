denotive

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈnoʊtɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈnəʊtɪv / (BrE)
having the property of denoting or explicitly designating something; relating to denotation rather than connotation.
having the property of denoting

Examples

The denotive meaning of the word is its direct, explicit definition.
In legal documents, the language is often carefully denotive to avoid ambiguity.
Poetry tends to exploit both the denotive and connotative aspects of words.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: connotative - relating to or suggesting an additional meaning; opposite of denotive

Etymology

From Latin denotare (to denote) + -ive.
