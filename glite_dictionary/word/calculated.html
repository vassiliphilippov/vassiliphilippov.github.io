calculated

1. ADJ

/ ˈkælkjəˌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkælkjʊˌleɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a plan, action, or remark) carefully planned or intended to achieve a particular effect.
carefully planned to achieve an effect

Examples

Her calculated response ensured she gained the upper hand in the negotiation.
The villain's actions were cold and calculated, leaving nothing to chance.
It was a calculated risk, but he believed the reward would outweigh the potential loss.

Synset Examples

a calculated insult
with measured irony
he made a deliberate decision not to respond negatively

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deliberate - done consciously and intentionally
  • Antonym: spontaneous - done without planning or premeditation

Etymology

From the past participle of 'calculate', from Latin 'calculare' (to count).
