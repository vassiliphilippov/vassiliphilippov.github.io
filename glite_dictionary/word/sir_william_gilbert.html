Sir William Gilbert

1. PROPN

/ sɝː ˈwɪljəm ˈɡɪlbɚt / (AmE), / sə ˈwɪljəm ˈɡɪlbət / (BrE)
(1836–1911) English librettist best known for his collaboration with Sir Arthur Sullivan on a famous series of comic operettas.
English librettist and dramatist

Examples

Sir William Gilbert wrote the librettos for some of the most beloved comic operas in English history.
Many students studied the witty lyrics of Sir William Gilbert during their literature exams.
The legacy of Sir William Gilbert endures through countless performances of Gilbert and Sullivan operettas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gilbert and Sullivan - the collaborative partnership of W. S. Gilbert and A. Sullivan, creators of comic operettas
