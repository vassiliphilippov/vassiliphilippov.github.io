deniable

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈnaɪəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈnaɪəbl / (BrE)
(of a fact, statement, or action) able to be denied or contradicted; not certain or definite.
able to be denied or contradicted

Examples

His involvement in the project was easily deniable due to lack of evidence.
That theory is highly deniable and has not been widely accepted.
The company left a deniable amount of ambiguity in their public statement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indeniable - impossible to deny
  • Antonym: undeniable - impossible to deny or dispute

Etymology

From deny + -able, modeled after Latin forms meaning 'able to be denied'.
