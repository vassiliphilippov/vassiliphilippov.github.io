fibrosity

1. NOUN

/ faɪˈbrɑːsəti / (AmE), / faɪˈbrɒsəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being fibrous; having many fibers or a fibrous texture
the state of being fibrous

Examples

The fibrosity of the plant's stem gives it extra strength and flexibility.
Cheese makers often judge mozzarella by its fibrosity, which affects its texture when melted.
Microscopic examination revealed a high degree of fibrosity in the tissue samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fibrous - consisting of or resembling fibers

Etymology

From Latin 'fibrosus' (full of fibers) + English suffix '-ity'.
