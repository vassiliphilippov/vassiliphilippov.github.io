monotheism

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.nəˌθi.ɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.əʊ.θi.ɪ.zəm / (BrE)
The belief that there is only one God.
belief in a single God

Examples

Judaism, Christianity, and Islam are all religions founded on monotheism.
The development of monotheism marked a major shift in ancient religious beliefs.
Many people find comfort in the idea of monotheism and the worship of a single divine being.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polytheism - belief in more than one god
  • Antonym: atheism - disbelief or lack of belief in the existence of God or gods

Etymology

From Greek monos (single) + theos (god).
