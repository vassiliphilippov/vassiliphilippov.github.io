Hades

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˈheɪdiːz / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the underworld; the realm of the dead, ruled by the god Hades.
the underworld of Greek mythology

Examples

In ancient myths, the souls of the dead would journey to Hades after leaving the world of the living.
The river Styx was said to flow through Hades, separating the living from the dead.
Many heroes in Greek stories attempted perilous voyages to Hades in search of lost loved ones.

Synset Examples

No one goes to Hades with all his immense wealth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Underworld - the mythical realm of the dead or the afterlife

Etymology

From Greek Hádēs, name of the god of the underworld, later used for his realm.

2. PROPN

/ ˈheɪdiz / (AmE), / ˈheɪdiːz / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the god of the underworld; brother of Zeus and husband of Persephone.
Greek god of the underworld

Examples

In Greek mythology, Hades ruled the underworld with strict authority.
The myth of Persephone's abduction by Hades explains the changing seasons.
Many heroes in ancient stories journeyed to the realm of Hades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pluto - the Roman god of the underworld, counterpart to Hades
  • Antonym: Zeus - the king of the Greek gods, brother of Hades

Etymology

From ancient Greek ᾍδης (Haidēs), meaning 'the unseen one'.
