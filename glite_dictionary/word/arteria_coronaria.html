arteria coronaria

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈtɪriə koʊˌroʊˈnɛriə / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə kɒˌrəʊˈneəriə / (BrE)
A coronary artery; the artery that branches from the aorta to supply blood to the heart muscle.
coronary artery of the heart
medical

Examples

A blockage in the arteria coronaria can lead to a heart attack.
The surgeon carefully examined the patient's arteria coronaria for signs of disease.
Damage to the arteria coronaria can severely impact heart function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coronary artery - An artery supplying blood to the heart muscle

Etymology

From Latin: 'arteria' (artery) + 'coronaria' (of a crown), referring to the crown-like encircling of the heart.
