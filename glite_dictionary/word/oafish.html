oafish

1. ADJ

/ ˈoʊ.fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.fɪʃ / (BrE)
clumsy, rude, or lacking good manners in behavior or appearance
clumsy or ill-mannered

Examples

His oafish behavior at dinner embarrassed everyone around him.
She rolled her eyes at his oafish attempt at a joke.
Despite his oafish appearance, he was surprisingly gentle with animals.

Synset Examples

was boorish and insensitive
the loutish manners of a bully
her stupid oafish husband
aristocratic contempt for the swinish multitude

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boorish - rude and insensitive; uncultured
  • Antonym: refined - elegant and cultured in manner or taste

Etymology

From 'oaf' meaning a clumsy or foolish person, originally from early modern English, perhaps from obsolete 'auf' (elf).
