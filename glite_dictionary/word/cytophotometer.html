cytophotometer

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪtoʊfəˈtɑmətər / (AmE), / ˌsʌɪtəʊfəˈtɒmɪtə / (BrE)
a scientific instrument for measuring the intensity of light passing through stained sections of cytoplasm in cells, used to locate and identify chemical compounds within cells
instrument for measuring chemical compounds in cells
medical

Examples

The laboratory's new cytophotometer allows researchers to analyze the chemical composition of single cells.
By using a cytophotometer, scientists can precisely measure the distribution of dyes within the cytoplasm.
The results obtained from the cytophotometer confirmed the presence of the targeted enzyme in the tissue samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photometer - instrument for measuring the intensity of light

Etymology

From Greek 'kytos' (cell) + 'photo-' (light) + 'meter' (measure).
