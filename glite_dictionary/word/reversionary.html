reversionary

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈvɜrʒəˌnɛri / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːʃənəri / (BrE)
relating to or involving a reversion, especially the right to future possession or ownership (commonly in legal or financial contexts).
relating to a legal reversion or future interest
legal

Examples

The contract grants a reversionary interest to the original owner after the lease expires.
He inherited a reversionary annuity, to be paid only when the current recipient dies.
The lawyer explained the significance of a reversionary right in the property deed.

Synset Examples

reversionary annuity
reversionary interest

Etymology

From 'reversion' + '-ary'; 'reversion' from Latin 'reversio(n-)' meaning 'a return'.
