proconsulship

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈkɑːn.səl.ʃɪp / (AmE), / prəʊˈkɒn.səl.ʃɪp / (BrE)
the position or office of a proconsul, especially in ancient Rome
the office of a proconsul

Examples

During his proconsulship, he introduced a number of important legal reforms.
The historian wrote extensively about the policies enacted in Cicero's proconsulship.
Appointments to the proconsulship were often political rewards for loyal senators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proconsul - a governor of a province in ancient Rome, or one with delegated authority

Etymology

Formed from 'proconsul' (from Latin proconsul: substitute for consul) plus '-ship' (denoting state or office).
