shrillness

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃrɪlnəs / (AmE), / ˈʃrɪlnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of having a loud, high-pitched, and often piercing sound
piercing high-pitched sound quality

Examples

The shrillness of the alarm made everyone cover their ears.
She disliked the shrillness in his voice during arguments.
Some birds are known for the shrillness of their calls, which can carry for miles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrill - high-pitched and piercing in sound
  • Degree Lower: harshness - quality of being unpleasantly rough or jarring to the senses

Etymology

From 'shrill' + '-ness', forming a noun indicating the state or quality of being shrill.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃrɪlnəs / (AmE), / ˈʃrɪlnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being sharp, high-pitched, or harsh to the senses, especially to the ear
sharp or harsh quality (especially of sound)

Examples

The shrillness of the alarm made everyone cover their ears.
Critics remarked on the shrillness of his voice during the speech.
The shrillness of her laughter rang through the empty hall.

Synset Examples

the shrillness of her hair color

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharpness - the quality of being sharp, as in taste, sound, or image
  • Antonym: softness - the quality of being gentle or mild to the senses

Etymology

From 'shrill' + '-ness', with 'shrill' from Middle English, likely of Germanic origin.
