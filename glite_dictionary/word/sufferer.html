sufferer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌfərər / (AmE), / ˈsʌfərə / (BrE)
a person who is affected by pain, illness, or hardship
a person affected by pain or illness

Examples

Many sufferers of asthma find that pollen makes their symptoms worse.
The charity provides support for sufferers of chronic pain.
During the flu epidemic, hospitals were crowded with sufferers seeking treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patient - a person receiving medical care or treatment
  • Synonym: invalid - a person made weak or disabled by illness or injury

Etymology

From suffer + -er, formed in Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌf.ɚ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈsʌf.ər.ər / (BrE)
a person who endures pain, hardship, or distress, especially for the sake of a principle or cause
person who endures pain or distress

Examples

As a committed sufferer for justice, he never wavered in his beliefs.
Throughout history, many sufferers have inspired change through their sacrifices.
She viewed herself as a sufferer for the cause, enduring discrimination without giving up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: martyr - one who suffers or is killed for their beliefs or principles

Etymology

From suffer + -er, ultimately from Latin sufferre (to endure).
