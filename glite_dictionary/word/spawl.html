spawl

1. NOUN

/ spɔl / (AmE), / spɔːl / (BrE)
a fragment broken off from the edge or face of stone or ore, having at least one thin edge
stone or ore fragment with a thin edge

Examples

The quarry workers collected several spawls after the latest blasting session.
A spawl of granite landed near the edge of the excavation site.
You could see spawls piled up next to the rock crusher.

Synset Examples

a truck bearing a mound of blue spalls

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spall - a chip or fragment of stone or ore broken off, especially in mining or quarrying

Etymology

Variant of 'spall', possibly from Middle English 'spallen' (to splinter).
