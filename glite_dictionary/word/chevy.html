to chevy

1. VERB

/ ˈtʃɛvi / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛvi / (BrE)
to annoy, harass, or chase persistently and repeatedly.
annoy or harass persistently

Examples

The children decided to chevy the dog around the yard all afternoon.
He was constantly chevied by his older brother about his untidy room.
She grew tired of being chevied by her colleagues for minor mistakes.

Synset Examples

He is known to harry his staff when he is overworked
This man harasses his female co-workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to harry - to persistently harass or attack
  • Synonym: to harass - to subject to aggressive pressure or intimidation repeatedly

Etymology

From the noun 'chevy', an alternative form of 'chevy chase' (chase, pursuit), possibly from the Cheviot Hills.
