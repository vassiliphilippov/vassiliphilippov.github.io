Rebecca

1. PROPN

/ rəˈbɛkə / (AmE), / rɪˈbɛkə / (BrE)
(in the Bible) the wife of Isaac and the mother of Jacob and Esau; a matriarch of the Hebrew people.
wife of Isaac in the Bible

Examples

In Genesis, Rebecca helps her son Jacob obtain Isaac’s blessing.
Many paintings from the Renaissance period depict Rebecca at the well.
The story of Rebecca is often cited in discussions of biblical matriarchs.

Etymology

From Hebrew רִבְקָה (Rivqah).
