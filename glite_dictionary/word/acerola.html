acerola

1. NOUN

/ ˌæsəˈroʊlə / (AmE), / ˌasəˈrəʊlə / (BrE)
A small, acid, red or yellow cherry-like fruit of a tropical American shrub, valued for its high vitamin C content.
tropical cherry-like fruit rich in vitamin C

Examples

The vitamin C content in acerola is much higher than in oranges.
She added fresh acerola to her morning smoothie for an extra nutritional boost.
Some juice blends include acerola to enhance their flavor and health benefits.

Etymology

From Portuguese acerolá, probably from Spanish acerola, a type of cherry.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæs.əˈroʊ.lə / (AmE), / ˌas.əˈrəʊ.lə / (BrE)
A tropical American shrub (Malpighia emarginata) bearing small, bright red, tart fruits resembling cherries, rich in vitamin C.
tropical shrub with cherry-like fruit

Examples

A glass of juice made from acerola is packed with vitamin C.
The acerola tree thrives in warm, tropical climates.
Many health supplements now use acerola extract as a natural source of antioxidants.

Etymology

From Spanish 'acerola', possibly from Arabic 'az-zurayr', referring to a type of cherry.
