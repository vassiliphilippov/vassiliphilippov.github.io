Furnarius

1. NOUN

/ fɚˈnɛəriəs / (AmE), / fjʊəˈnɛəriəs / (BrE)
A genus of South American ovenbirds in the family Furnariidae, known for building oven-shaped nests.
genus of South American ovenbirds

Examples

The Furnarius genus includes several species known for their distinctive mud nests.
Researchers studied the nesting behavior of Furnarius in Argentina.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a Furnarius building its oven-shaped nest.

Etymology

From Latin 'furnarius' (baker), referring to their oven-shaped nests.

2. NOUN

/ fərˈnɛəriəs / (AmE), / fɜːˈnɛːrɪəs / (BrE)
A genus of South American birds in the ovenbird family, Furnariidae, known for building oven-shaped nests.
genus of ovenbirds

Examples

The Furnarius genus is noted for its unique clay nests shaped like ovens.
Among South American birds, Furnarius is easily recognized for its nesting habits.
Ornithologists often study the Furnarius genus to understand avian architecture.

Etymology

From Latin 'furnarius' meaning 'baker' or 'oven-maker', relating to the birds' nest shape.
