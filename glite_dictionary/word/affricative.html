affricative

1. NOUN

/ əˈfrɪk.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / əˈfrɪk.ə.tɪv / (BrE)
a speech sound formed by combining a stop with a fricative, articulated at the same place, as in the initial sounds of 'chair' and 'joy'
a composite speech sound (stop + fricative)

Examples

The English 'ch' in 'chair' is classified as an affricative sound.
Linguists study how affricatives are produced in different languages.
The presence of affricative consonants can distinguish one language from another.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affricate - a speech sound consisting of a stop and a fricative articulated together
  • Degree Lower: fricative - a consonant sound produced by forcing air through a narrow passage
  • Degree Lower: plosive - a consonant sound produced by stopping airflow and then releasing it

Etymology

From Latin affricatus, past participle of affricare (to rub against), in reference to the sound's formation.
