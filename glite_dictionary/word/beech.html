beech

1. NOUN

/ biːtʃ / (AmE), / biːtʃ / (BrE)
the wood from any of several beech trees, used especially for flooring, containers, plywood, and tool handles
wood of a beech tree

Examples

The flooring in the dining room is made of polished beech that shines in the sunlight.
Many tool handles are crafted from beech because of its durability and smooth texture.
They chose to use beech for the kitchen cabinets due to its attractive pale color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - wood of an oak tree, another hardwood
  • Synonym: maple - wood of a maple tree, also used in furniture

Etymology

From Old English *bēce*, related to the German *Buche* and Dutch *beuk*.

2. NOUN

/ bitʃ / (AmE), / biːtʃ / (BrE)
a large deciduous tree of north temperate regions with smooth grey bark, rounded spreading crown, and small triangular edible nuts enclosed in burs
a type of large deciduous tree

Examples

A giant beech towered over the garden, providing shade all summer long.
The forest path was lined with ancient beech trees whose leaves rustled in the wind.
We collected the small nuts that fell from the beech and roasted them over a fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a large tree with lobed leaves and acorns, common in temperate regions
  • Synonym: birch - a slender tree with peeling bark, found in temperate climates

Etymology

Old English *bēce*, related to German *Buche* and Latin *fagus* (beech tree).
