pottle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑt̬.əl / (AmE), / ˈpɒt.əl / (BrE)
a pot or container with a capacity of 2 quarts, formerly used as a measure for liquids or dry goods
old pot or container of 2 quarts
archaic

Examples

The recipe called for a pottle of ale, an old English measurement.
In the market, vendors sold berries by the pottle in small, round baskets.
She discovered a pottle in the attic, once used for measuring milk.

Etymology

From Old French *potel* (diminutive of *pot*), meaning 'small pot.'
