bilocation

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪloʊˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪləʊˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the supposed phenomenon of being present in two places at the same time, especially as attributed to certain saints or supernatural beings
being in two places at once (supernatural)

Examples

Some saints were said to possess the miraculous power of bilocation.
The legend of Padre Pio includes several accounts of his bilocation reported by witnesses.
Stories of bilocation often appear in religious texts and folklore.

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'location' (placing), from Latin 'locatio'.
