Bhutanese

1. NOUN

/ buːˈtɑːniz / (AmE), / buːˈtɑːniːz / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Bhutan
native or resident of Bhutan

Examples

The Bhutanese take great pride in their unique culture and traditions.
Several Bhutanese attended the international conference on Himalayan studies.
He became close friends with a Bhutanese while traveling in South Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bhutanese - the language spoken in Bhutan

Etymology

From Bhutan + -ese, a suffix indicating nationality or language, modelled after similar terms.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09713925-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ buːˈtɑːniz / (AmE), / buːˈtɑːniːz / (BrE)
relating to Bhutan, its people, culture, or language
of or relating to Bhutan or its people

Examples

Many Bhutanese traditions are influenced by Buddhism.
The Bhutanese language, Dzongkha, is spoken widely throughout the country.
She enjoyed the spicy flavors of Bhutanese cuisine during her visit.

Synset Examples

Bhutanese Buddhists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bhutan - a country in South Asia

Etymology

From Bhutan + -ese, a suffix forming demonyms and adjectives from place names.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03059590-a
Synset POS: a
