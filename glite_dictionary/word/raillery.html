raillery

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪlɚi / (AmE), / ˈreɪlɪəri / (BrE)
good-humored teasing or banter; light, playful ridicule
good-humored teasing or banter

Examples

Their raillery was gentle and affectionate, filled with laughter rather than malice.
She enjoyed the raillery of her friends, knowing none of their jests were intended to hurt.
The meeting was lightened by a bit of raillery among colleagues, which helped ease the tension.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banter - friendly and playful exchange of teasing remarks
  • Degree Higher: mockery - teasing or contemptuous language or behavior; more harsh or cruel than raillery

Etymology

From French *raillerie* (banter, mockery), from *railler* (to jest, to banter).
