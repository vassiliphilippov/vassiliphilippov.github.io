to disconcert

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪs.ənˈsɝːt / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ənˈsɜːt / (BrE)
to make someone feel uneasy, embarrassed, or confused; to disturb the composure of someone
make uneasy or unsettled

Examples

Her direct question seemed to disconcert him during the meeting.
The loud noise from outside began to disconcert the performers on stage.
It can disconcert people when their routines are suddenly changed.

Synset Examples

The constant attention of the young man confused her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embarrass - to make someone feel awkward or self-conscious
  • Antonym: comfort - to make someone feel less worried or upset

Etymology

From French 'déconcerter' (to unsettle), from Latin 'concertare' (to contend).

2. VERB

/ ˌdɪs.kənˈsɝt / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.kənˈsɜːt / (BrE)
to disturb the composure of; to unsettle or embarrass
to unsettle or disturb

Examples

The sudden question seemed to disconcert her during the interview.
Loud noises can disconcert even the calmest individuals.
His strange behavior began to disconcert his colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unsettle - to make someone feel anxious or uneasy
  • Antonym: to reassure - to restore confidence or composure

Etymology

From French 'déconcerter', from 'dé-' (dis-) + 'concerter' (to bring into agreement).
