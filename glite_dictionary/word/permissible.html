permissible

1. ADJ

/ pərˈmɪsəbəl / (AmE), / pəˈmɪsəbəl / (BrE)
allowed or permitted by law, rules, or custom; acceptable within certain limits.
allowed; permitted; acceptable

Examples

It is not permissible to smoke in this area of the building.
The use of calculators is permissible during the exam, but only for certain sections.
Some behavior considered permissible in one culture may be frowned upon in another.

Synset Examples

a kind of speculation that was permissible in cosmology but inadmissible in medicine

Related Terms

  • Antonym: forbidden - not allowed; prohibited
  • Synonym: allowed - permitted; acceptable

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'permissibilis', from Latin 'permittere' (to allow).

2. ADJ

/ pərˈmɪsəbəl / (AmE), / pəˈmɪsɪbəl / (BrE)
allowed by law, rule, or custom; permitted.
allowed by rules or law

Examples

Eating in the classroom is only permissible if the teacher allows it.
It is not permissible to park in a handicapped space without a permit.
Some tax deductions are permissible under federal law, while others are not.

Synset Examples

permissible behavior in school
a permissible tax deduction

Related Terms

  • Antonym: impermissible - not allowed by law or rules; not permissible
  • Synonym: permitted - officially allowed; authorized

Etymology

From Medieval Latin permissibilis, from Latin permittere (to allow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01765456-a
Synset POS: a
