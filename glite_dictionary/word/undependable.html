undependable

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.dɪˈpɛn.də.bəl / (AmE), / ˌʌn.dɪˈpen.də.bəl / (BrE)
not reliable or deserving of trust; likely to fail or disappoint
not reliable; untrustworthy

Examples

After several missed deadlines, his behavior became increasingly undependable.
They replaced the old, undependable engine with a newer model.
She found his excuses undependable and stopped relying on him for help.

Synset Examples

in the early 1950s computers were large and expensive and unreliable
an undependable assistant

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reliable - able to be trusted; consistently good in quality or performance
  • Antonym: dependable - trustworthy and reliable

Etymology

From un- (not) + dependable (capable of being depended on).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌndɪˈpɛndəbəl / (AmE), / ˌʌndɪˈpɛndəbl / (BrE)
not able to be relied on or trusted; likely to be erroneous or misleading.
not reliable or trustworthy

Examples

The information provided by that website is notoriously undependable.
Due to his undependable memory, he often forgot important details.
Many critics find the study's conclusions to be undependable.

Synset Examples

an undependable generalization

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reliable - able to be trusted or depended on
  • Synonym: unreliable - not able to be relied upon

Etymology

From un- (not) + dependable (able to be depended on).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00967779-s
Synset POS: s
