Lutjanus apodus

1. NOUN

/ luːˈtʃeɪnəs əˈpoʊdəs / (AmE), / luːˈtjanʊs əˈpəʊdəs / (BrE)
A species of snapper, also known as the schoolmaster snapper, found in warm Caribbean and Atlantic waters and valued as a food fish.
a Caribbean food fish species (schoolmaster snapper)

Examples

Fishermen in the Caribbean often catch Lutjanus apodus for local markets.
The colorful Lutjanus apodus is also known as the schoolmaster snapper.
Many restaurants feature dishes made with fresh Lutjanus apodus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snapper - a family of predatory marine fishes to which Lutjanus apodus belongs

Etymology

From Latin: Lutjanus, the genus of snappers, and apodus, meaning 'without feet'.
