Strongylodon macrobotrys

1. NOUN

/ strɑːŋˌɡɪl.əˌdɑn ˌmæk.rəˈbɑː.trɪs / (AmE), / strɒŋˌɡɪl.ədɒn ˌmæk.rəʊˈbɒ.trɪs / (BrE)
A vigorous evergreen climbing vine native to the Philippines, grown for its striking cascading clusters of blue-green flowers resembling lobster claws.
Philippine vine with striking blue-green flowers

Examples

The Strongylodon macrobotrys at the botanical garden drew crowds with its mesmerizing turquoise blossoms.
Gardeners admire Strongylodon macrobotrys for its ability to cover pergolas with cascading flower clusters.
Visitors marveled at the vibrant colors of the Strongylodon macrobotrys during the annual floral exhibition.

Etymology

From Greek 'strongylos' (round), 'odous' (tooth), and Greek 'makros' (large), 'botrys' (cluster, bunch of grapes), referring to the flower structure.
