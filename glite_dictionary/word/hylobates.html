Hylobates

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪləˈbeɪtiːz / (AmE), / ˌhɪlˈəʊbətiːz / (BrE)
A genus of small apes commonly known as gibbons, native to the forests of Southeast Asia.
genus of gibbons (small apes)

Examples

Several species within the genus Hylobates are noted for their elaborate songs and impressive agility in the trees.
The Hylobates are distinguished from other apes by their long arms and lack of tails.
Researchers studied the social behavior of Hylobates in their natural Southeast Asian habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gibbon - a small ape of the family Hylobatidae

Etymology

From Greek hūlobatēs, meaning 'dweller in the woods' (hūlē ‘forest’ + bates ‘one who walks’).
