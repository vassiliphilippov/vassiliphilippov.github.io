seafaring

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːˌfɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiːˌfeə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity or occupation of working as a sailor or traveling by sea
the work or occupation of a sailor

Examples

He dedicated his life to seafaring and spent decades at sea.
Stories of seafaring adventures have inspired many to explore the oceans.
After years of seafaring, she finally decided to settle on land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sailing - the act of traveling by boat or ship
  • Antonym: landlubber - a person unfamiliar with the sea or seafaring

Etymology

From 'sea' + 'faring' (traveling, journeying), with 'faring' from Old English 'faran' (to travel).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiˌfɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiːˌfeərɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice or activity of traveling by sea, especially as a regular occupation
traveling by sea

Examples

Her grandfather spent his whole life engaged in seafaring and told fascinating stories about distant lands.
After years of seafaring, he settled in a quiet coastal town far from the ocean.
The tradition of seafaring runs deep in their family, spanning several generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: navigation - the process or activity of accurately ascertaining one's position and planning and following a route, especially at sea

Etymology

From sea +‎ faring, where 'faring' comes from Old English 'faran' (to travel).
