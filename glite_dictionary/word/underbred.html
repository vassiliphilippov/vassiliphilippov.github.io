underbred

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌndərˈbrɛd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈbrɛd / (BrE)
Lacking in refinement or good manners; not properly brought up or cultivated.
lacking refinement or good manners

Examples

His underbred behavior embarrassed everyone at the dinner table.
She was dismissed as underbred and unsophisticated by the elite social club.
Despite his wealth, his manners revealed a distinctly underbred character.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uncouth - lacking good manners or refinement
  • Antonym: refined - showing cultured, polished manners

Etymology

Formed from 'under-' (meaning insufficient) and 'bred' (past participle of 'breed').

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌndɚˈbrɛd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈbrɛd / (BrE)
of inferior or mixed breed; not purebred or lacking good breeding
inferior or mixed breed

Examples

The shelter took in several underbred puppies from the local area.
Some breeders look down on underbred animals, but they can make wonderful pets.
The judge disqualified the underbred hound from the competition due to its ancestry.

Synset Examples

an underbred dog

Related Terms

  • Antonym: purebred - of an animal, bred from parents of the same breed or variety
  • Synonym: crossbred - produced by mating animals of different breeds or varieties

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'under-' (meaning 'insufficient' or 'less than') and 'bred', past participle of 'breed'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
