Rana catesbeiana

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪ.nə ˌkæt.ɪsˈbiː.ə.nə / (AmE), / ˈrɑː.nə ˌkæt.ɪzˈbiː.ə.nə / (BrE)
a species of large, aquatic frog native to North America, also known as the American bullfrog, notable for its deep, loud call
large North American aquatic frog (bullfrog)

Examples

The Rana catesbeiana can often be heard croaking loudly in ponds and wetlands during summer nights.
Scientists studied the feeding habits of the Rana catesbeiana in its natural habitat.
The introduction of Rana catesbeiana to non-native areas has impacted local amphibian populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bullfrog - another name for Rana catesbeiana; a large frog native to North America

Etymology

From Latin: genus 'Rana' (frog), species honoring English naturalist Mark Catesby.
