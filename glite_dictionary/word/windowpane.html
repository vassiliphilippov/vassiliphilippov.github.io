windowpane

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪndoʊˌpeɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɪndəʊˌpeɪn / (BrE)
a very thin, translucent flounder native to the Atlantic coast of North America
thin North American flounder

Examples

The windowpane is known for its extremely thin body and almost transparent appearance.
Fishermen along the Atlantic coast often catch windowpane flounders in their nets.
Biologists study the windowpane to understand how its translucent skin helps it avoid predators.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪndoʊˌpeɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɪndəʊˌpeɪn / (BrE)
a single pane of glass set in a window frame
pane of glass in a window

Examples

A crack ran across the whole windowpane after the storm.
She cleaned each windowpane to let more light into the room.
The children watched snowflakes settle softly on the frosty windowpane.

Synset Examples

the ball shattered the window

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pane - a sheet of glass in a window or door

Etymology

From 'window' + 'pane', where 'pane' derives from Old French 'pan' (section, piece).
