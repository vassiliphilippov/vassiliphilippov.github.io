Ulmus crassifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌlməs kræsɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈʌlməs krasɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
a species of elm native to the southern United States and Mexico, characterized by spreading, pendulous branches with corky ridges
a North American species of elm tree

Examples

The Ulmus crassifolia is commonly found in Texas woodlands and is known as the cedar elm.
Many city parks have planted Ulmus crassifolia because of its drought tolerance.
Researchers are studying Ulmus crassifolia to find resistance to elm disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cedar elm - common name for Ulmus crassifolia

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Ulmus' (elm) and 'crassifolia' meaning 'thick-leaved'.
