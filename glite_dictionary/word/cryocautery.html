cryocautery

1. NOUN

/ ˌkraɪ.oʊˈkɔː.tɚ.i / (AmE), / ˌkraɪ.əʊˈkɔː.tər.i / (BrE)
the application of extreme cold to destroy tissue, typically as a medical treatment
destruction of tissue by freezing
medical

Examples

The dermatologist recommended cryocautery to remove the skin lesion.
Doctors often use cryocautery in the treatment of warts and other benign growths.
Cryocautery offers a minimally invasive alternative to surgical excision in some cases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cryotherapy - treatment by applying cold to tissues

Etymology

From Greek kryos (cold, frost) + cautery (burning, branding).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkraɪoʊˈkɔːtəɹi / (AmE), / ˌkraɪəʊˈkɔːtəri / (BrE)
a medical instrument used to destroy tissue by freezing it, typically for surgical or dermatological procedures
instrument for destroying tissue by freezing
medical

Examples

The dermatologist used a cryocautery to remove the wart from the patient's hand.
During the procedure, the surgeon relied on cryocautery to minimize tissue damage.
Advances in cryocautery have made certain treatments less invasive than traditional surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cryosurgery - surgical technique using freezing to destroy tissue
  • Synonym: cautery - instrument or process of burning tissue for medical reasons

Etymology

From Greek 'kryos' (frost) + 'cautery', referring to burning or destroying tissue.
