to spectate

1. VERB

/ ˈspɛkˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈspɛkteɪt / (BrE)
to watch an event, especially a sports event, as a spectator
to watch as a spectator

Examples

Many fans traveled long distances to spectate at the championship match.
She preferred to spectate rather than participate in the annual marathon.
A group of students gathered to spectate the basketball game after school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watch - to look at or observe attentively over a period of time
  • Antonym: to participate - to take part in an event or activity

Etymology

From Latin "spectare" meaning "to look at," from "specere" meaning "to look."
