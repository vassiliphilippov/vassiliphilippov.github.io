forelady

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌleɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌleɪdi / (BrE)
A woman who serves as the foreperson or leader of a jury, responsible for representing the jury and delivering verdicts in court.
female jury foreperson
legal

Examples

She was chosen as the forelady and read out the verdict in court.
After the trial, the forelady answered questions from the press about the jury's deliberations.
The judge instructed the forelady to confirm the decision of the jury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foreman - a man who is the foreperson of a jury

Etymology

Formed from 'fore-' (front, leader) + 'lady' (female form of 'lord'), by analogy with 'foreman'.
