Dravidian

1. NOUN

/ drəˈvɪdiən / (AmE), / drəˈvɪdiən / (BrE)
a family of languages spoken mainly in southern and central India and in Sri Lanka, with communities also in Malaysia and Singapore
language family of southern India and Sri Lanka

Examples

The Dravidian languages include Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, and Malayalam.
Linguists have studied the Dravidian language family to trace ancient migration patterns.
Many Dravidian speakers also use English as a second language in India.

Etymology

From Sanskrit drāviḍa (southern Indian, Tamil person).

2. NOUN

/ drəˈvɪdiən / (AmE), / drəˈvɪdiən / (BrE)
a member of one of the indigenous ethnic groups of southern India, traditionally speaking Dravidian languages
member of indigenous ethnic groups of southern India

Examples

Many Dravidians continue to preserve their cultural traditions in southern India.
The migration patterns of the Dravidians are studied by anthropologists.
Several major languages spoken by Dravidians include Tamil, Telugu, and Kannada.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Indo-Aryan - member of the northern linguistic and ethnic group in India

Etymology

From Sanskrit Drāviḍa, name for the region and peoples of southern India.
