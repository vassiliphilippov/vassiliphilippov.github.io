solidus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːlɪdəs / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪdəs / (BrE)
a gold coin of the Byzantine Empire, widely used in Europe during the Middle Ages
Byzantine gold coin of the Middle Ages

Examples

The museum's collection includes a rare solidus from the reign of Constantine.
Medieval traders often used the solidus in their transactions across Europe.
He was fascinated by the intricate designs engraved on the ancient solidus.

Etymology

From Latin 'solidus' (solid, firm); used as the name of the Roman and later Byzantine gold coin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːlɪdəs / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪdəs / (BrE)
a punctuation mark (/) used to separate related items of information or alternatives
punctuation mark (slash '/')

Examples

Please use a solidus to indicate alternatives, as in 'and/or'.
The fraction three-quarters can be written with a solidus as 3/4.
In some computer directories, a solidus separates folder names.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slash - the '/' punctuation mark, commonly called a slash

Etymology

Latin solidus (a gold coin), later used in Medieval Latin for the slash symbol in writing sums; extension to the punctuation mark.
