to suppurate

1. VERB

/ ˈsʌpjəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌpjʊəreɪt / (BrE)
(of a wound or sore) to form or discharge pus; to cause to ripen and ooze pus.
produce or discharge pus (medically)
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the wound might suppurate if not kept clean.
Certain infections can cause a sore to suppurate over time.
The treatment is designed to help the abscess suppurate and release the pus.

Synset Examples

The oil suppurates the pustules

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fester - (of a wound or sore) to become infected and produce pus

Etymology

From Latin suppurare (to form or discharge pus), from sub- (under) + pus, puris (pus).

2. VERB

/ ˈsʌp.jəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌp.jʊ.reɪt / (BrE)
(of a wound or tissue) to produce or discharge pus as a result of infection.
form or discharge pus

Examples

If left untreated, the wound may begin to suppurate within a few days.
The doctor examined the suppurating sore on his arm with concern.
The infected cut suppurated, requiring medical attention to clean and dress it.

Synset Examples

her wounds are festering

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fester - to become infected and form pus
  • Antonym: to heal - to become healthy or well again, especially after a wound

Etymology

From Latin suppurare, from sub- (under) + pus, puris (pus).
