wandering

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɑndərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɒndərɪŋ / (BrE)
having no fixed course; moving or directed aimlessly or irregularly
having no fixed course

Examples

The wandering river twisted and turned through the valley for miles.
Her thoughts took a wandering path during the long, quiet afternoon.
Astronomers studied the wandering trajectory of the newly discovered comet.

Synset Examples

an erratic comet
his life followed a wandering course
a planetary vagabond

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erratic - not even or regular in pattern or movement; unpredictable

Etymology

Present participle of 'wander', from Old English 'wandrian'.

WordNet ID: oewn-02134890-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɑːndərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɒndərɪŋ / (BrE)
moving from place to place rather than settling permanently in one location; migratory
migratory; moving from place to place

Examples

The wandering tribes crossed the desert in search of water and pasture.
For centuries, their wandering lifestyle shaped their culture and traditions.
Some birds have wandering habits, migrating great distances each year.

Synset Examples

a restless mobile society
the nomadic habits of the Bedouins
believed the profession of a peregrine typist would have a happy future
wandering tribes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nomadic - leading a life of moving from place to place, especially for food or work
  • Synonym: migratory - regularly moving from one place to another, especially referring to animals or people
  • Antonym: settled - living in one place and not moving frequently

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'wander', from Old English 'wandrian' (to roam).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02135465-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɑndɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɒndərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or activity of traveling from place to place without a fixed course or clear destination
aimless traveling or roving

Examples

His wanderings through the countryside inspired many of his stories.
During her years of wandering, she learned several new languages.
We read about the wanderings of early explorers in our history class.

Synset Examples

she followed him in his wanderings and looked after him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roaming - the act of moving about without a fixed course or destination
  • Antonym: journey - an act of traveling from one place to another, often with a purpose

Etymology

From the verb 'wander', from Old English 'wandrian' (to move about, roam).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00297888-n
Synset POS: n

4. ADJ

/ ˈwɑːndərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɒndərɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a path, stream, or route) twisting and turning; not following a straight or direct course.
twisting or winding (of a path or route)

Examples

The wandering river snaked through the valley, carving out a unique landscape.
Tourists enjoyed hiking along the wandering trails deep in the old forest.
A wandering country road led us past farms, fields, and tiny villages.

Synset Examples

meandering streams
rambling forest paths
the river followed its wandering course
a winding country road

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winding - (of a road, path, or stream) repeatedly turning in different directions instead of being straight
  • Synonym: meandering - (of a river or road) following a winding course
  • Antonym: straight - extending or moving in one direction only; not curving or bending

Etymology

From Old English 'wandrian' (to travel, move about), related to 'wander'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00766950-s
Synset POS: s
