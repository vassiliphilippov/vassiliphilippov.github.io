high-mindedness

1. NOUN

/ haɪ ˈmaɪndɪdnəs / (AmE), / haɪ ˈmaɪndɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality of having elevated ideals and principles, especially regarding moral conduct or aspirations
quality of having lofty ideals

Examples

Her high-mindedness inspired others to act with integrity, even in challenging situations.
Despite criticism, he maintained his high-mindedness and stayed true to his principles.
Critics sometimes view high-mindedness as impractical, but it can drive positive change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idealism - the practice of forming or pursuing ideals, especially unrealistically
  • Antonym: cynicism - an inclination to believe that people are motivated purely by self-interest

Etymology

From 'high-minded' (having lofty ideals) + '-ness' (forming nouns indicating a state or quality).
