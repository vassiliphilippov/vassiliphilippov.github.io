subdominant

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˈdɑːmənənt / (AmE), / sʌbˈdɒmɪnənt / (BrE)
(music) the fourth degree of a diatonic scale, next below the dominant.
the fourth note of a diatonic scale (music)

Examples

In the key of C major, F is the subdominant note.
The composer modulated from the tonic to the subdominant to create variety.
Practicing scales, she paused on the subdominant before resolving to the dominant.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dominant - the fifth note of the diatonic scale (music)
  • Degree Lower: tonic - the first note of the diatonic scale (music)

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (below) + 'dominant' (the fifth note in the scale).
