mimicry

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪmɪkri / (AmE), / ˈmɪmɪkri / (BrE)
the close resemblance of one species to another species or to natural objects, often to gain protection from predators
resemblance for protection in animals

Examples

Many harmless butterflies use mimicry to look like poisonous species and avoid being eaten.
The phenomenon of mimicry can also be seen in plants that resemble stones to escape herbivores.
Scientists study mimicry to understand how animals adapt to their environment for survival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imitation - the act of copying the behavior or appearance of someone or something
  • Synonym: camouflage - the act of concealing the identity of something by modifying its appearance

Etymology

From Greek mimēsis (imitation), via Latin mimicus (imitative).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪmɪkɹi / (AmE), / ˈmɪmɪkɹi / (BrE)
the act of imitating the behavior, appearance, or voice of someone or something, often to entertain, deceive, or adapt
imitation of someone or something

Examples

The comedian's talent for mimicry delighted the entire audience.
Some animals use mimicry as a defense mechanism to avoid predators.
Her mimicry of her teacher was so accurate that everyone burst out laughing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imitation - the act of copying someone or something

Etymology

From Greek mimētikos (able to imitate), via Latin mimicus (imitative) and English mimic.
