uncontrollable

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.kənˈtroʊ.lə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kənˈtrəʊ.lə.bəl / (BrE)
impossible to control or restrain; not subject to being kept within limits.
impossible to control or restrain

Examples

His uncontrollable laughter echoed through the room, making everyone smile.
She felt an uncontrollable urge to cry after hearing the news.
The fire spread with uncontrollable speed, leaving little time for evacuation.

Synset Examples

an irrepressible chatterbox

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irrepressible - not able to be controlled or restrained
  • Antonym: controllable - capable of being controlled or restrained

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'controllable'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnkənˈtroʊləbəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnkənˈtrəʊləbəl / (BrE)
impossible to manage, control, or restrain.
impossible to control

Examples

The fire quickly spread and became uncontrollable despite the firefighters' best efforts.
She burst into uncontrollable laughter during the meeting, much to everyone's surprise.
His uncontrollable anger worried his friends and family.

Synset Examples

the little boy's parents think he is spirited, but his teacher finds him unruly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irresistible - too strong to be controlled or resisted
  • Synonym: unruly - difficult to control or discipline
  • Antonym: controllable - capable of being controlled

Etymology

From un- (not) + controllable, originating in Old French 'contreroler' (to check against a list), from medieval Latin 'contrarotulare.'

(of something such as pain, emotion, or a situation) impossible or extremely difficult to control, manage, or alleviate.

Etymology

From un- (not) + controllable, from control (from Old French, from Medieval Latin *contrarotulare* 'register a copy') plus -able.
