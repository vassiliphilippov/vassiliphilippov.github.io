cornice

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrnɪs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnɪs / (BrE)
A decorative horizontal framework fixed at the top of a window to conceal curtain fixtures and provide an ornamental finish.
ornamental frame concealing curtain fixtures

Examples

The elegant cornice above the window hides the curtain rod from view.
We chose a wooden cornice to match our living room décor.
Cleaning the cornice is tricky because it collects dust over time.

Etymology

From Italian 'cornice', meaning 'ledge' or 'frame'.

2. VERB

/ ˈkɔrnɪs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnɪs / (BrE)
to furnish or supply (a building or room) with a cornice
to provide with a molding (cornice)

Examples

The architect decided to cornice the living room to enhance its classical style.
After the renovation, they corniced all the ceilings in the hallway.
Workmen were busy cornicing the top edges of the stately building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decorate - to make (something) look more attractive by adding items or features

Etymology

From Italian *cornice* (frame, ledge, molding), from Latin *cornic-*, *cornix* (ledge).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrnɪs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnɪs / (BrE)
the uppermost, projecting section of an entablature in classical architecture, typically serving as a decorative molding
the projecting top part of an entablature

Examples

The ornate cornice added a touch of elegance to the top of the building.
Restorers carefully cleaned the nineteenth-century cornice above the columns.
Classical temples often feature a prominent cornice as part of their entablature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: molding - a decorative strip used for ornamentation around ceilings or walls

Etymology

From Italian 'cornice' (frame), from Latin 'cornix' (ledge, project).

4. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrnɪs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnɪs / (BrE)
an ornamental molding that runs along the top of a wall just below the ceiling
decorative molding at top of a wall

Examples

The elaborate cornice in the dining room added a touch of elegance to the space.
Painters carefully prepared the cornice before applying a fresh coat of white paint.
The historic building features a beautifully detailed cornice running around the upper edge of every room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crown molding - decorative molding at the intersection of wall and ceiling

Etymology

From Italian 'cornice' (frame) and Latin 'cornix' (ledge, projection).
