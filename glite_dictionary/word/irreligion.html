irreligion

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪrəˈlɪdʒən / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˈlɪdʒən / (BrE)
the absence or rejection of religion, faith, or religious devotion
absence of religious belief or devotion

Examples

The rise of irreligion in modern societies has been noted by sociologists.
His outspoken irreligion made him a controversial figure among religious groups.
Debates about faith often include perspectives from both religion and irreligion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atheism - the belief that there is no deity
  • Antonym: religion - the belief in and worship of a superhuman controlling power, especially a personal God or gods

Etymology

From Latin 'irreligio', from 'in-' (not) + 'religio' (religion).
