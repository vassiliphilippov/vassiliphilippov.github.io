walking

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːkɪŋ / (BrE)
The activity of traveling or moving by foot at a regular pace, especially as a form of exercise or transportation.
the act of traveling by foot

Examples

Walking is recommended by doctors for its health benefits.
They went walking in the park every evening after dinner.
Her love of walking led her to explore many scenic trails.

Synset Examples

walking is a healthy form of exercise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hiking - walking long distances, especially in the countryside
  • Degree Higher: running - moving swiftly on foot, faster than walking

Etymology

From Old English 'wealcian' (to roll, toss), later associated with 'walk' + '-ing'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɔkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːkɪŋ / (BrE)
Located or situated close enough to be reached by walking.
within walking distance; easily walkable

Examples

The store is within walking distance from my apartment.
They chose a hotel that was walking distance to the city center.
Living in a walking neighborhood makes life much more convenient.

Synset Examples

walking distance
the factory with the big parking lot … is more convenient than the walk-to factory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walkable - able to be comfortably walked to or through
