terrestrially

1. ADV

/ təˈrɛstriəli / (AmE), / təˈrɛstriəli / (BrE)
In a manner relating to land or the earth, rather than water or air.
in a manner relating to land or earth

Examples

Many amphibians are able to live terrestrially as adults after beginning life in water.
The device functions terrestrially but can also be adapted for use underwater.
Some plants thrive only when grown terrestrially rather than in aquatic environments.

Synset Examples

terrestrially adapted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrestrial - relating to the earth or land
  • Antonym: aquatically - in a manner relating to water

Etymology

From 'terrestrial', which comes from Latin 'terrestris' (of the earth).
