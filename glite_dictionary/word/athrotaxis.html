Athrotaxis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæθroʊˈtæksɪs / (AmE), / ˌæθrəʊˈtæksɪs / (BrE)
a genus of evergreen gymnosperm trees native to Tasmania, belonging to the family Cupressaceae
genus of Tasmanian evergreen gymnosperm trees

Examples

The ancient forests of Tasmania are home to the rare Athrotaxis species.
Botanists were thrilled to discover a new population of Athrotaxis in the remote highlands.
The unique foliage of Athrotaxis makes it easily distinguishable from other conifers.

Etymology

From Greek 'athroos' (crowded) and 'taxis' (arrangement), referring to the arrangement of cones or leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæθ.rəˈtæksɪs / (AmE), / ˌaθ.rəˈtaksɪs / (BrE)
a genus of evergreen coniferous trees native to Tasmania, belonging to the cypress family (Cupressaceae) and known for their scale-like leaves and small cones.
genus of coniferous Tasmanian trees

Examples

Several rare species in the genus Athrotaxis are restricted to Tasmania's cool mountain forests.
The distinctive appearance of Athrotaxis trees makes them popular among plant collectors.
Botanists studied the reproductive structures of Athrotaxis to understand its evolutionary relationship within the cypress family.

Etymology

From Greek 'athroos' (crowded, close together) and 'taxis' (arrangement).
