to ascend

1. VERB

/ əˈsɛnd / (AmE), / əˈsɛnd / (BrE)
to move or slope upwards; to go up or rise to a higher point or level
move or slope upwards

Examples

The trail began to ascend sharply after the wooden bridge.
We watched the hot air balloon ascend slowly into the morning sky.
The road ascends steeply toward the mountain pass.

Synset Examples

The path ascended to the top of the hill

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to descend - to move or slope downwards

Etymology

From Latin ascendere, from ad- (to) + scandere (to climb).
