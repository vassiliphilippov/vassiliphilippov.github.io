Chlamydomonadaceae

1. NOUN

/ kləˌmɪdəˌmoʊnəˈdeɪsiː / (AmE), / kləˌmɪdəˌmɒnəˈdeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of green algae, some of which are colored red by the pigment hematochrome.
a family of green algae

Examples

Research on Chlamydomonadaceae has led to a better understanding of algal evolution.
The distinctive pigments in Chlamydomonadaceae are used to study photosynthesis.
Some members of the Chlamydomonadaceae family can turn water red due to hematochrome.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Chlamydomonas, with the standard botanical family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

a family of green algae, some of which are colored red by hematochrome pigment
family of green algae, sometimes red by pigment
medical

Examples

The Chlamydomonadaceae family is studied extensively for its unique photosynthetic properties.
Some members of the Chlamydomonadaceae appear red due to a pigment called hematochrome.
Scientists have discovered new species within the Chlamydomonadaceae in freshwater habitats.

Etymology

Formed from the genus Chlamydomonas (from Greek 'chlamys' meaning 'cloak' and 'monas' meaning 'single unit'), plus the standard botanical family suffix '-aceae' (from Latin).
