Magyar

1. ADJ

/ ˈmæɡ.jɑr / (AmE), / ˈmæɡ.jɑː / (BrE)
relating to Hungary or its people, language, or culture
relating to Hungary

Examples

The museum has a collection of Magyar costumes and artifacts.
She is passionate about Magyar traditions and folk art.
A group of Magyar dancers performed at the international festival.

Synset Examples

Hungarian folk music

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hungarian - relating to Hungary, its people, or language

Etymology

From Hungarian 'Magyar' (Hungarian), the self-designation of the Hungarian people.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːɡjɑr / (AmE), / ˈmæɡjɑː / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Hungary, especially a member of the ethnic Hungarian people
person from Hungary or of Hungarian descent

Examples

Several Magyar families settled in the city during the late nineteenth century.
The Magyar culture has greatly influenced Central Europe over the centuries.
She learned about her Magyar ancestry during her trip to Hungary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hungarian - a person from Hungary or of Hungarian ethnicity

Etymology

From Hungarian 'magyar', the name for the Hungarian people and their language.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09733381-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːɡjɑr / (AmE), / ˈmæɡjɑː / (BrE)
the official language of Hungary, also spoken in parts of Romania and other countries; a member of the Ugric branch of the Finno-Ugric language family
the Hungarian language

Examples

She is fluent in both English and Magyar.
The novel was originally written in Magyar before being translated into English.
Linguists classify Magyar as part of the Ugric language family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hungarian - the official language of Hungary

Etymology

From Hungarian 'magyar', meaning both 'Hungarian (person)' and the Hungarian language.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06971576-n
Synset POS: n
