to furcate

1. VERB

/ ˈfɝː.keɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɜː.keɪt / (BrE)
(of a structure) to divide into two or more branches; to separate forming a fork.
divide into branches; form a fork

Examples

The river furcates as it approaches the delta, splitting into several channels.
At the base of the mountain, the trail furcates into two separate paths.
During embryonic development, the main artery furcates to supply blood to different organs.

Synset Examples

The road forks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: branch - to divide into branches
  • Synonym: bifurcate - to divide into two branches or forks

Etymology

From Latin 'furca' (fork) + '-ate', reflecting the meaning 'to fork'.
