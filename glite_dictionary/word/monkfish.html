monkfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌŋkˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋkˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
The flesh of a large-headed anglerfish, especially as used for food, found in the Atlantic waters of North America.
flesh of a North American anglerfish (food)

Examples

Many chefs praise the taste and texture of monkfish in seafood dishes.
The restaurant served grilled monkfish with lemon and herbs.
Fresh monkfish is often compared to lobster in both flavor and consistency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anglerfish - a type of fish known for its bioluminescent lure and large mouth

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌŋkˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋkˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a large marine fish with a broad, flat head and a wide mouth, known for its wormlike filament used to lure prey; also called anglerfish or goosefish.
a large, wide-mouthed marine fish

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious dish using fresh monkfish and Mediterranean herbs.
Divers were excited to spot a large monkfish resting on the ocean floor.
Because of its mild flavor and firm texture, monkfish is sometimes called the 'poor man's lobster.'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anglerfish - a fish with a lure on its head to attract prey
  • Synonym: goosefish - another name for monkfish, a luring marine fish

3. NOUN

/ ˈmʌŋkˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋk.fɪʃ / (BrE)
a large fish with a broad, flat body and winglike pectoral fins, known for its firm white flesh; also called anglerfish or goosefish
a large flat-bodied fish (goosefish/anglerfish)

Examples

The chef prepared the monkfish with garlic and lemon for a hearty seafood dish.
Fishermen in New England often catch monkfish along with cod and haddock.
Because of its meaty texture, monkfish is sometimes called "poor man's lobster".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anglerfish - a fish with a luminescent lure, often called monkfish
  • Synonym: goosefish - another name for monkfish, a fish of North American waters
