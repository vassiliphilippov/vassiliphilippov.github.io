to teem

1. VERB

/ tim / (AmE), / tiːm / (BrE)
(of people, animals, etc.) to be full of or swarming with; to move or be present in large numbers.
move or be present in large numbers

Examples

During the summer, the fields teem with wildflowers and butterflies.
At dusk, the market square teems with vendors and shoppers.
After the storm, the streets teemed with people eager to see the damage.

Synset Examples

people were pouring out of the theater
beggars pullulated in the plaza

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swarm - to move in large numbers or crowds
  • Synonym: to abound - exist in large numbers or amounts

Etymology

From Old English tēman (to give birth to, produce offspring), related to 'team'.

2. VERB

/ tim / (AmE), / tiːm / (BrE)
to be full of or swarming with large numbers of people, animals, or things
be full or swarming with

Examples

The river used to teem with fish in the summer months.
On Black Friday, the mall teemed with eager shoppers hunting for deals.
Her garden teems with colorful butterflies every spring.

Synset Examples

The garden was swarming with bees
The plaza is teeming with undercover policemen
her mind pullulated with worries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swarm - move in large numbers or be present in great quantities
  • Synonym: to abound - exist in large numbers or amounts

Etymology

From Old English tēman (to bring forth, to produce offspring), related to 'team'.
