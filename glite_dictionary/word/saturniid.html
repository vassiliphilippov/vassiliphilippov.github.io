saturniid

1. NOUN

/ səˈtɜr.ni.ɪd / (AmE), / səˈtɜː.ni.ɪd / (BrE)
a large, brightly colored moth, usually found in tropical regions, whose larvae spin silken cocoons; belongs to the family Saturniidae.
tropical moth of the family Saturniidae

Examples

The saturniid moth is known for its impressive wingspan and eye-catching patterns.
Many saturniid caterpillars create elaborate silk cocoons as they pupate.
Researchers discovered a new species of saturniid in the Amazon rainforest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moth - an insect closely related to butterflies

Etymology

From modern Latin Saturniidae, the family name, ultimately from Saturnia (a genus within the family), after the Roman god Saturn.
