manatee

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænəˌti / (AmE), / ˈmanəti / (BrE)
A large, herbivorous aquatic mammal with a rounded, flat tail, found in tropical coastal waters of America.
large aquatic mammal of tropical America

Examples

A manatee slowly surfaced near the boat, grazing on sea grass.
Many conservationists are working to protect the endangered manatee.
Children at the aquarium enjoyed watching the gentle manatee glide through the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dugong - a similar aquatic mammal of the Indian and western Pacific oceans

Etymology

From Spanish 'manatí', from Carib 'manati' (breast, due to the mammary glands).
