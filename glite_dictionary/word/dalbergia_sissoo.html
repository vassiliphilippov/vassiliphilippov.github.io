Dalbergia sissoo

1. NOUN

/ dælˈbɜːrdʒi.ə ˈsɪsoʊ / (AmE), / dælˈbɜːdʒɪə ˈsɪsəʊ / (BrE)
A species of deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent, whose leaves are used as fodder and wood is valued for its strength and durability, especially in shipbuilding and railway construction.
species of tree valued for durable timber

Examples

The heartwood of Dalbergia sissoo is prized for making high-quality furniture.
Farmers often plant Dalbergia sissoo for its ability to provide fodder and timber.
Because of its durability, Dalbergia sissoo is commonly used in railway sleepers in South Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheesham - the common name for Dalbergia sissoo

Etymology

From the genus name Dalbergia, named after Swedish botanist Nils Dalberg, and the Indian vernacular 'sissoo'.
