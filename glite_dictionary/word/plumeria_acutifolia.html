Plumeria acutifolia

1. NOUN

/ pluːˈmɪəriə əˌkjuːtɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / pluːˈmɪəriə əˌkjuːtɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A species of frangipani native to India, having an erect growth habit and conical form, commonly grown in temple gardens.
a species of Indian frangipani plant

Examples

The temple courtyard was filled with the sweet scent of Plumeria acutifolia in full bloom.
Gardeners often choose Plumeria acutifolia for its beautiful, fragrant flowers and distinctive shape.
In India, Plumeria acutifolia is commonly planted near temples for its ornamental value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frangipani - another name for plants of the genus Plumeria

Etymology

Plumeria: named after French botanist Charles Plumier; acutifolia: from Latin 'acutus' (sharp) + 'folia' (leaves), meaning 'sharp-leaved.'
