Marrubium

1. NOUN

/ məˈruːbiəm / (AmE), / məˈruːbiəm / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic perennial herbs in the mint family, including the common horehound, native to Europe, North Africa, and Asia.
genus of aromatic herbs (horehound)

Examples

The species Marrubium vulgare is widely used as a herbal remedy for coughs.
Botanists collected several specimens of Marrubium during their field study in the Mediterranean region.
Her garden includes many medicinal plants, such as Marrubium and lavender.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horehound - common name for plants in the genus Marrubium

Etymology

From Latin *marrubium*, meaning horehound.

2. NOUN

/ məˈruːbiəm / (AmE), / məˈruːbɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic Old World herbs in the mint family, commonly known as horehound, with wrinkled leaves and small white or purple flowers.
a genus of herbs known as horehound

Examples

Marrubium species are often used in herbal remedies for coughs and colds.
The garden featured a border planted with several varieties of Marrubium.
Botanists collected samples of Marrubium from the wild for further study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horehound - a common name for Marrubium species, especially Marrubium vulgare

Etymology

From Latin Marrubium, the classical name for horehound.
