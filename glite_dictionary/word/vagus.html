vagus

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪɡəs / (AmE), / ˈveɪɡəs / (BrE)
the tenth cranial nerve (vagus nerve), which controls the heart, lungs, digestive tract, and other organs in the chest and abdomen
the tenth cranial nerve; controls internal organs
medical

Examples

Stimulation of the vagus has been used to treat certain cases of epilepsy.
The surgeon was careful to avoid damaging the vagus during the operation.
The vagus plays a key role in regulating heart rate and digestion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vagus nerve - the tenth cranial nerve, also known as vagus

Etymology

From Latin 'vagus' (wandering), due to its extensive range throughout the body.
