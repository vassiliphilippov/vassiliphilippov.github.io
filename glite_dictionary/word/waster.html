waster

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪstər / (AmE), / ˈweɪstə / (BrE)
a person who spends or uses resources, time, or money in a wasteful or self-indulgent way
person who wastes resources

Examples

Jake is such a waster; he spends all his money on things he doesn’t need.
The company cannot afford to hire another waster while trying to cut costs.
She was tired of being called a waster by her hard-working family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spendthrift - a person who spends money in an extravagant, irresponsible way
  • Antonym: conserver - a person who uses resources carefully and avoids waste

Etymology

From Middle English wastre, from Anglo-Norman waster (one who lays waste or destroys).

2. NOUN

/ ˈweɪstər / (AmE), / ˈwɑːstə / (BrE)
a person who destroys, ruins, or lays waste to something
person who destroys or ruins

Examples

The invading army was known as a waster of towns and villages, leaving destruction in its path.
Jealousy can become a waster of happiness if left unchecked.
The legends called him the waster of kingdoms for his ruthless campaigns.

Synset Examples

a destroyer of the environment
jealousy was his undoer
uprooters of gravestones

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destroyer - a person or thing that destroys
  • Antonym: preserver - a person who preserves or protects

Etymology

From Middle English 'wastour', from Anglo-Norman and Old French 'wastor' (a destroyer, squanderer), from 'waster' (to waste).
