budgereegah

1. NOUN

/ ˌbʌdʒəriˈɡɑ / (AmE), / ˌbʌdʒəriˈɡɑː / (BrE)
a small Australian parakeet, usually light green with black and yellow markings in the wild, but bred in many colors as a cage bird
small Australian parakeet; cage bird

Examples

The family decided to adopt a beautiful budgereegah as their new pet.
Wild budgereegahs can be seen in large flocks across the Australian outback.
He spends hours each week caring for his colorful budgereegahs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: budgerigar - another term for budgereegah, small Australian parakeet
  • Degree Higher: parakeet - any of numerous small slender parrots with a long tail

Etymology

From Kamilaroi (Australian Aboriginal language) 'budgeri', meaning 'good', and 'gah', meaning 'bird'.
