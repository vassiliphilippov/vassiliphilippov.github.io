weight

1. NOUN

/ weɪt / (AmE), / weɪt / (BrE)
a system or unit of measurement used to express how heavy something is
system of measurement for heaviness

Examples

The metric weight is commonly used in most countries.
He needed to convert the weight from kilograms to pounds for the shipment.
Different industries may use different weight systems, such as avoirdupois or troy weight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mass - quantity of matter in a body; related physical property

Etymology

From Old English *wiht* (thing, weight), from Proto-Germanic *wihti*.
