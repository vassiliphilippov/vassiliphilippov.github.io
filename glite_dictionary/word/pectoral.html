2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛk.tɚ.əl / (AmE), / ˈpɛk.tər.əl / (BrE)
An ornament or piece of jewelry designed to be worn on the chest, often of ceremonial or symbolic significance.
ornament worn on the chest

Examples

The ancient Egyptian king was buried wearing a magnificent pectoral of gold and precious stones.
Priests in certain religious ceremonies would don a jeweled pectoral over their robes.
The museum displayed a collection of Bronze Age pectorals found at the archaeological site.

Etymology

From Latin *pectoralis* (of the breast), from *pectus* (breast, chest).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɛktɚəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛktərəl / (BrE)
Either of the two large muscles on the front of the chest that assist in movements of the shoulder and arm.
a large chest muscle
medical

Examples

He strengthened his pectorals by doing push-ups every day.
Stretching helps prevent injuries to the pectoral muscle group.
The athlete complained of pain in his left pectoral after the workout.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: pectoralis major - the larger of the two pectoral muscles
  • Degree Lower: pectoralis minor - the smaller, underlying pectoral muscle

Etymology

From Latin pectoralis, from pectus (chest).
