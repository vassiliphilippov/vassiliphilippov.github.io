enosis

1. NOUN

/ ɛˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ɛˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
The political movement advocating the union of Greece and Cyprus, particularly as sought by some Greek Cypriots.
union of Greece and Cyprus (political movement)

Examples

The movement for enosis dominated Cypriot politics in the 1950s.
Some Greek Cypriots strongly supported enosis with Greece, while others opposed it.
Debates about enosis have significantly shaped the modern history of Cyprus.

Etymology

From Greek ἔνωσις (énōsis) meaning 'union'.
