causeway

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔzˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɔːzweɪ / (BrE)
A raised road or track built across low or wet ground, or over water.
raised road across water or wet land

Examples

The drive to the island took us over a long causeway surrounded by marshes.
During high tide, the causeway was partially submerged, blocking access to the village.
Engineers reinforced the old causeway to withstand flooding in the rainy season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bridge - structure built to span a gap or obstacle, typically water

Etymology

From Old Northern French 'caucee' (causeway), itself from Vulgar Latin '*calciāta*' (paved road).
