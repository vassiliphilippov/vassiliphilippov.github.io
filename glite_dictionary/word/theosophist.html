theosophist

1. NOUN

/ θiˈɑːsəfɪst / (AmE), / θiˈɒsəfɪst / (BrE)
A person who believes in or studies theosophy, a religious philosophy based on mystical insight into the nature of God and the universe.
believer in theosophy

Examples

As a theosophist, she believed in the hidden spiritual realities governing our world.
The meeting attracted several prominent theosophists from different countries.
His writings reflect the influence of theosophist thinkers of the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theosophy - a religious philosophy seeking direct knowledge of mysteries of the universe and divinity

Etymology

From theosophy + -ist, patterned after French théosophiste.
