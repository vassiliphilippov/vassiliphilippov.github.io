Pteropsida

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrəˈpsaɪdə / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəˈpsɪdə / (BrE)
a former taxonomic division in botany that included all ferns and flowering plants, subdivided into the classes Filicinae, Gymnospermae, and Angiospermae
former plant division (ferns & flowering plants)

Examples

The class Pteropsida was once widely recognized by botanists but is now obsolete.
Many textbooks from the early 20th century still refer to Pteropsida in their classification of plants.
The term Pteropsida grouped together both ferns and all seed plants in older systems.

Etymology

From Greek pteron (feather, wing) + -opsis (appearance) + -ida (used in zoological and botanical taxonomy).
