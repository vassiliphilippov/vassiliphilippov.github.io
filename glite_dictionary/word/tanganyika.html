Tanganyika

1. PROPN

/ ˌtæŋɡəˈniːjə / (AmE), / ˌtæŋɡəˈniːjə / (BrE)
a large lake in central Africa, located in the Great Rift Valley between Tanzania and the Democratic Republic of the Congo; the longest freshwater lake in the world
large lake in central Africa

Examples

Lake Tanganyika is known for its incredible biodiversity and depth.
Fishermen along the shores of Tanganyika rely on the lake for their livelihoods.
The expedition studied the unique species found only in Tanganyika.

Etymology

From the name of the territory of Tanganyika, now part of Tanzania; possibly from Bantu languages meaning 'the great lake spreading out like a plain'.

2. PROPN

/ ˌtæŋɡəˈniːkə / (AmE), / ˌtæŋɡəˈniːkə / (BrE)
a former country in East Africa that united with Zanzibar in 1964 to form Tanzania
former country in East Africa

Examples

The Republic of Tanganyika existed from 1961 until it merged with Zanzibar in 1964.
Many historical documents reference Tanganyika as an independent nation prior to the formation of Tanzania.
The wildlife reserves in former Tanganyika are now a major attraction in Tanzania.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Tanzania - country in East Africa formed by union of Tanganyika and Zanzibar

Etymology

From local ethnic names and Swahili; original use referred to Lake Tanganyika.
