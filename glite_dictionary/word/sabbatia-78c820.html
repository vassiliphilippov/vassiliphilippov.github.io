sabbatia

1. NOUN

/ səˈbeɪtiə / (AmE), / sæˈbeɪtɪə / (BrE)
any of various North American flowering plants of the genus Sabbatia, having typically pink, cymose flowers, often found in acidic bogs or brackish marshes
genus of pink-flowered North American plants

Examples

The rare sabbatia was spotted blooming along the edges of the salt marsh.
Many gardeners admire the delicate pink flowers of the sabbatia in late summer.
Researchers studied how the sabbatia adapted to survive in acidic bog conditions.

Etymology

Named after Liberato Sabbati, an Italian botanist (1714–1778).
