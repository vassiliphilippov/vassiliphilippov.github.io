checkrein

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛkˌreɪn / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛk.reɪn / (BrE)
A rein attached to a horse's bridle, designed to keep the horse's head in a particular position, usually to hold it up.
rein to keep horse's head positioned

Examples

The groom tightened the checkrein before the carriage set out for the parade.
Using a checkrein helps maintain the horse's posture during competitions.
Some people consider the checkrein uncomfortable for horses if used incorrectly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rein - a long, narrow strap attached to a horse’s bridle used to guide or check a horse

Etymology

From 'check' + 'rein', first used in the 18th century for horse tack.
