miserably

1. ADV

/ ˈmɪz.ɚ.əb.li / (AmE), / ˈmɪz.ər.əb.li / (BrE)
In a way that shows great unhappiness, discomfort, or distress.
in a miserable manner

Examples

She sighed miserably as she watched the rain fall outside her window.
He failed the test and walked home miserably in the pouring rain.
After the argument, she looked at him miserably, hoping for forgiveness.

Synset Examples

I bit my lip miserably and nodded

Related Terms

  • Antonym: happily - in a happy manner

Etymology

From miserable + -ly, with 'miserable' from Latin miserabilis (pitiable).
