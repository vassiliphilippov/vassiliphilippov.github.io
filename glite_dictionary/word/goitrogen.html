goitrogen

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɔɪ.tɹə.dʒən / (AmE), / ˈɡɔɪ.trə.dʒən / (BrE)
a substance that induces the formation of a goiter by interfering with thyroid gland function
substance causing goiter formation
medical

Examples

Some vegetables, such as cabbage and broccoli, contain natural goitrogens that may affect thyroid health.
Doctors warned that excessive intake of goitrogen compounds could lead to thyroid gland enlargement.
This research focuses on how goitrogens in the diet might contribute to the risk of developing a goiter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antithyroid - substance that opposes thyroid function

Etymology

From 'goiter' (enlarged thyroid gland) + '-gen' (producer, from Greek 'genēs' meaning 'born of').
