Lappula

1. NOUN

/ ləˈpjuːlə / (AmE), / ləˈpjuːlə / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the borage family, commonly known as stickweed or beggar's lice, with small bristly fruits that stick to clothing or fur.
a genus of plants called stickweed or beggar's lice

Examples

Lappula plants are often found in fields and along roadsides across North America.
The small bristly fruits of Lappula easily attach to animal fur and clothing.
Gardeners sometimes remove Lappula to prevent its spread in cultivated areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stickweed - common name for plants of the genus Lappula
  • Synonym: beggar's lice - another common name for plants of the genus Lappula

Etymology

From Latin lappula, diminutive of lappa (a bur).

2. NOUN

/ ləˈpjuːlə / (AmE), / ləˈpjuːlə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the borage family (Boraginaceae), commonly known as stickweed or beggar's lice, characterized by prickly seeds that cling to fur and clothing.
genus of plants known as stickweed

Examples

The field was full of Lappula plants, their seeds sticking to the animals' fur.
She identified the weed as Lappula during the botany survey.
Many hikers find Lappula seeds clinging to their clothes after a walk in the countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stickweed - common name for plants of the genus Lappula
  • Synonym: beggar's lice - common name for plants in genus Lappula with clinging seeds

Etymology

From Latin 'lappula', diminutive of 'lappa' (bur, burr).
