Ortalis

1. NOUN

/ ɔrˈtælɪs / (AmE), / ɔːˈtælɪs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Cracidae, commonly known as chachalacas, native to Central and South America.
genus of chachalacas (birds)

Examples

The Ortalis birds are recognized for their noisy calls at dawn.
Most species within the genus Ortalis inhabit dense tropical forests.
Researchers studied the diet of Ortalis to better understand its role in seed dispersal.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek orthalos (a kind of bird).

2. NOUN

/ ɔrˈtælɪs / (AmE), / ɔːˈtælɪs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Cracidae, commonly known as chachalacas, found in Central and South America.
genus of chachalaca birds

Examples

The genus Ortalis includes several species of chachalacas native to the Americas.
Researchers observed a group of Ortalis foraging in the lowland forest.
Among the Cracidae family, Ortalis is known for its distinctive loud calls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chachalaca - a bird of the genus Ortalis, known for its noisy call

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Ortalis, used in taxonomic naming.
