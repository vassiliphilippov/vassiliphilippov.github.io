foothold

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtˌhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈfʊthəʊld / (BrE)
A place where your foot can be securely placed, used for support in standing or climbing.
place to support the foot

Examples

The climber searched for a secure foothold on the steep rock face.
I lost my foothold and nearly slipped during the hike.
She carefully tested each foothold before moving higher up the ladder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handhold - a thing to hold with the hand when climbing or moving

Etymology

From 'foot' + 'hold', meaning something to hold the foot.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtˌhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈfʊthəʊld / (BrE)
an initial position or achievement that makes it possible to progress further, especially in a new activity, market, or area
an initial position enabling further progress

Examples

After several failed attempts, the company finally established a foothold in the Asian market.
Winning that contract gave them a crucial foothold in the technology sector.
Non-profit organizations are working to gain a foothold in rural communities to provide essential services.

Synset Examples

the town became a beachhead in the campaign to ban smoking outdoors
they are presently attempting to gain a foothold in the Russian market

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beachhead - an initial position gained that can be used for further advances, especially in a new area
  • Synonym: toehold - a small or initial advantage or support making further progress possible

3. NOUN

/ ˈfuːtˌhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈfʊthəʊld / (BrE)
a small area in enemy or hostile territory that has been captured and is held while awaiting reinforcements or further advancement
captured area in enemy territory

Examples

The army established a small foothold on the enemy's coastline despite fierce resistance.
Securing a foothold in the region allowed the troops to bring in necessary supplies.
Their primary objective was to create a foothold from which future operations could be launched.

Synset Examples

an attempt to secure a bridgehead behind enemy lines
the only foothold left for British troops in Europe was Gibraltar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bridgehead - a position in enemy territory secured as a base for further advance

Etymology

From foot + hold, meaning a place for securing the foot; later extended to a military context.
