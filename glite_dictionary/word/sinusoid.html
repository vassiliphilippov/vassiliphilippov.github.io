sinusoid

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪnəˌsɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪ.njuˌsɔɪd / (BrE)
A curve described by the equation y = sin x; a wave-shaped curve representing the sine function.
wave-shaped curve of y = sin x

Examples

The sinusoid is commonly used to model alternating current in electrical engineering.
When graphed, the sinusoid has a smooth, repetitive wave-like form.
The teacher explained how the sinusoid relates to the properties of the sine function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sine wave - a wave whose shape represents the sine function

Etymology

From Latin 'sinus' (curve, fold) and the mathematical function 'sine'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪnəˌsɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɪn.juː.sɔɪd / (BrE)
a small blood vessel with a thin, endothelium-lined wall, found in certain organs such as the liver, spleen, and bone marrow, allowing blood to pass slowly through tissues
small, thin-walled blood vessel in organs
medical

Examples

Blood passes through the liver's sinusoids where it is filtered before returning to the circulation.
The structure of sinusoids allows them to efficiently exchange nutrients and waste products with surrounding cells.
Damage to the sinusoids can lead to impaired liver function.

Etymology

From Latin sinus (curve, fold, hollow) + -oid (form, resembling); originally referring to a shape, later applied in anatomy.
