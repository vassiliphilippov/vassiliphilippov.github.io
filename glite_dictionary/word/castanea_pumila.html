Castanea pumila

1. NOUN

/ kæsˈteɪniə ˈpjuːmɪlə / (AmE), / kæsˈteɪnɪə ˈpjuːmɪlə / (BrE)
A shrubby chestnut tree native to the southeastern United States, recognized for its small edible nuts.
shrubby chestnut tree with edible nuts

Examples

The Castanea pumila thrives in sandy soils throughout the southeastern states.
Every fall, we gather the nuts from our Castanea pumila for baking.
Several wildlife species rely on the Castanea pumila as a food source.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chinquapin - another name for Castanea pumila
  • Degree Higher: American chestnut - large chestnut tree native to eastern United States

Etymology

From Latin; Castanea for chestnut, pumila meaning 'dwarf' or 'small'.
