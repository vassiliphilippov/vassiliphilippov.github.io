Coptis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑp.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈkɒp.tɪs / (BrE)
A small genus of perennial herbs with yellow rhizomes and white or yellow flowers, native to temperate regions.
genus of perennial flowering herbs

Examples

Several species of Coptis are used in traditional herbal remedies for digestive disorders.
The bright yellow rhizome of Coptis makes the plant easy to identify in the wild.
Researchers are studying Coptis for its potential antibacterial properties.

Etymology

From Greek 'koptis', derived from 'koptein' (to cut), possibly referring to the divided leaves.
