psi

1. NOUN

/ paɪ / (AmE), / paɪ / (BrE)
a unit of pressure equal to one pound force applied per square inch of area
unit of pressure (pounds per square inch)

Examples

The tire pressure is measured in psi and should be checked regularly.
A pressure of 30 psi is recommended for these bicycle tires.
The hydraulic system operates at a maximum of 2,000 psi.

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'pounds per square inch'.

2. NOUN

/ saɪ / (AmE), / saɪ / (BrE)
the twenty-third letter of the Greek alphabet (Ψ, ψ), used in scientific, mathematical, and academic contexts.
Greek letter: Ψ, ψ

Examples

The symbol psi is often used to represent wave functions in quantum mechanics.
In the Greek alphabet, psi comes after chi and before omega.
We learned to write both the uppercase and lowercase forms of psi in class.

Etymology

From Greek ψῖ (psī).
