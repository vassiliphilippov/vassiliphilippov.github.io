measured

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɛʒɚd / (AmE), / ˈmɛʒəd / (BrE)
(of music) having notes of fixed rhythmic value or a regular, steady rhythm.
regular or fixed in musical rhythm

Examples

The composer preferred a measured approach, ensuring each note followed a strict rhythm.
Traditional marches are typically written in a measured style to maintain a steady pace.
Her piano piece contrasted measured sections with moments of free, expressive tempo.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unmeasured - not having a regular or fixed rhythm (in music)

Etymology

From the verb 'measure', from Old French 'mesurer', from Latin 'mensurare'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɛʒɚd / (AmE), / ˈmɛʒəd / (BrE)
(of actions, speech, or movement) done slowly, carefully, and with dignity; deliberate and unhurried.
deliberate and unhurried; careful

Examples

His measured response to the criticism earned him respect.
She spoke in a measured tone, choosing her words carefully.
The guards marched with a slow, measured pace down the hall.

Synset Examples

walking at the same measured pace
with all deliberate speed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deliberate - done consciously and intentionally; purposeful
  • Antonym: hurried - done quickly or with haste, lacking care

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'measure,' from Old French mesurer, from Latin mensurare (to measure).
