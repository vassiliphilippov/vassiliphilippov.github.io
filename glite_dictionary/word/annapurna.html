Annapurna

1. PROPN

/ ˌænəˈpɜrnə / (AmE), / ˌænəˈpɜːnə / (BrE)
a mountain in the Himalayas in Nepal, notable for its height of about 26,500 feet (8,091 meters) and challenging climbing conditions.
mountain in the Himalayas, Nepal

Examples

Many mountaineers dream of climbing Annapurna because of its extreme difficulty and stunning beauty.
The first successful ascent of Annapurna was achieved in 1950 by a French expedition.
Trekking in the Annapurna region offers breathtaking views of the Himalayan landscape.

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Annapūrṇā', meaning 'giver of food' or 'goddess of nourishment'.

2. PROPN

/ ˌænəˈpʊrnə / (AmE), / ˌænəˈpʊːnə / (BrE)
In Hinduism, a goddess who is considered the wife of Shiva and a benevolent aspect of Devi, associated with food and nourishment.
Hindu goddess of plenty and nourishment

Examples

The temple dedicated to Annapurna attracts thousands of worshippers during festival times.
Devotees pray to Annapurna for abundance and nourishment in their households.
Stories of Annapurna emphasize the importance of sharing food with others.

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Anna' (food) + 'purna' (full, complete), meaning 'giver of food' or 'full of food'.
