to conceal

1. VERB

/ kənˈsil / (AmE), / kənˈsiːl / (BrE)
to hide or keep something secret so that it cannot be seen or discovered by others
hide; keep secret

Examples

She managed to conceal her nervousness during the interview.
The suspect tried to conceal the evidence under the floorboards.
He wore sunglasses to conceal his identity in public.

Synset Examples

She conceals her anger well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hide - to put or keep out of sight
  • Antonym: to reveal - to make known or visible

Etymology

From Latin celare (to hide), influenced by concelare (to hide completely).

2. VERB

/ kənˈsiːl / (AmE), / kənˈsiːl / (BrE)
to prevent something from being seen, known, or discovered; to hide
to hide or keep out of sight

Examples

The magician managed to conceal the coin in his hand during the performance.
She tried to conceal her disappointment when she heard the news.
The thief concealed the stolen jewelry under the floorboards.

Synset Examples

hide the money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hide - to put or keep out of sight; prevent from being seen
  • Antonym: to reveal - to make something known or visible

Etymology

From Latin 'concelare', from 'con-' (together) + 'celare' (to hide).
