doctorfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑktərˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdɒktəˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A type of surgeonfish found in the West Indies, known for the sharp spines on its tail.
West Indian surgeonfish

Examples

Snorkeling in the shallow reefs, we spotted a brightly colored doctorfish among the corals.
The doctorfish is easily recognized by the sharp spines near its tail.
Local fishermen in the Caribbean often catch doctorfish for both food and aquariums.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surgeonfish - marine fish with sharp scalpel-like spines on the tail
