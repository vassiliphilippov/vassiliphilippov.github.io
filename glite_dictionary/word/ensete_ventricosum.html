Ensete ventricosum

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈsiːti vɛnˌtrɪkoʊsəm / (AmE), / ɛnˈsiːti vɛnˌtrɪkəʊsəm / (BrE)
A large, evergreen, tree-like herb native to Africa, with huge paddle-shaped leaves and inedible fruit resembling bananas; the young flower shoots are edible. Also known as false banana or enset.
large African herb, also called false banana

Examples

Local farmers cultivate Ensete ventricosum for its edible shoots, which are an important food source in Ethiopia.
Although the fruit of Ensete ventricosum is not eaten, its stem and corm are widely used in traditional dishes.
Researchers are studying Ensete ventricosum for its drought resistance and role in sustainable agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banana - A tropical fruit with a yellow skin and soft, sweet flesh

Etymology

From genus name Ensete, from Greek ensetos ('planted'), and Latin ventricosum ('swollen').
