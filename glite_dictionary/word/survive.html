to survive

1. VERB

/ sərˈvaɪv / (AmE), / səˈvaɪv / (BrE)
to continue to live or exist after someone else has died; to outlive someone
live longer than another person

Examples

Of their four children, only one survived both parents.
She survived her husband by nearly two decades.
He was survived by three sons and a daughter.

Synset Examples

She outlived her husband by many years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outlive - to live longer than someone else

Etymology

From Latin 'supervivere', from 'super-' (over) + 'vivere' (to live).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ sərˈvaɪv / (AmE), / səˈvaɪv / (BrE)
to continue to live or exist, especially after coming close to death or experiencing difficult conditions
continue to live after danger or hardship

Examples

It is a miracle that the hikers managed to survive in the wilderness for a week.
The old traditions still survive in some rural communities.
After the accident, he struggled to survive despite his injuries.

Synset Examples

We went without water and food for 3 days
These superstitions survive in the backwaters of America
The race car driver lived through several very serious accidents
how long can a person last without food and water?
one crash victim died, the other lived

Related Terms

  • Antonym: die - to stop living
  • Synonym: to endure - to remain in existence; to last through hardship

Etymology

From Latin 'supervivere', from 'super-' (over) + 'vivere' (to live).

3. VERB

/ sərˈvaɪv / (AmE), / səˈvaɪv / (BrE)
to continue to live or exist after experiencing something difficult or dangerous, such as an illness, accident, or disaster
continue to live after difficulty or danger

Examples

After the plane crash, only a few passengers survived the ordeal.
The ancient tree has survived many storms over the centuries.
Doctors told her she might not survive the operation, but she made a full recovery.

Synset Examples

He survived the cancer against all odds

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to perish - to die, especially in an accident or disaster
  • Synonym: to endure - to continue to exist, especially in difficult conditions

Etymology

From Latin 'supervivere' (to live beyond), from 'super-' (over) + 'vivere' (to live).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02626004-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ sərˈvaɪv / (AmE), / səˈvaɪv / (BrE)
to manage to live or support oneself, especially with difficulty or on a limited income
manage to live, especially with difficulty

Examples

Many families struggle to survive on the minimum wage in large cities.
After losing his job, he had to survive on his small savings for months.
She managed to survive on very little food during her years in college.

Synset Examples

he could barely exist on such a low wage
Can you live on $2000 a month in New York City?
Many people in the world have to subsist on $1 a day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to subsist - to maintain or support oneself, especially at a minimal level
  • Synonym: to exist - to live, especially under difficult circumstances
  • Antonym: to thrive - to grow or develop well or vigorously

Etymology

From Latin supervivere (to live beyond), from super- (over) + vivere (to live).
