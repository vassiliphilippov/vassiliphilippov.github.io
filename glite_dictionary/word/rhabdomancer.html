rhabdomancer

1. NOUN

/ ˈræbdəˌmænsər / (AmE), / ˈræbdəˌmansə / (BrE)
A person who claims to find underground water, minerals, or other hidden substances using a divining rod.
person who finds water with a divining rod

Examples

The rhabdomancer claimed he could locate a hidden well beneath the old farmhouse.
During the drought, villagers hired a rhabdomancer to search for underground water sources.
Skeptics argued that the rhabdomancer's abilities were based more on luck than science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dowser - a person who uses a divining rod to locate water or minerals

Etymology

From Greek *rhabdos* (rod) + *mantis* (diviner).
