ecclesiastic

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌkliːziˈæstɪk / (AmE), / ɪˌkliːzɪˈastɪk / (BrE)
a member of the clergy or a person in religious orders, especially in the Christian Church
member of the clergy

Examples

The high-ranking ecclesiastic delivered the sermon at the cathedral on Sunday.
As an ecclesiastic, she dedicated her life to serving the church and its members.
The council of ecclesiastics debated important religious reforms during the conference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clergyman - a male member of the clergy
  • Antonym: layman - a non-ordained member of a church

Etymology

From Greek ekklēsiastikos (of the assembly, of the church), from ekklēsia (assembly, church).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˌkliːziˈæstɪk / (AmE), / ɪˌkliːziˈæstɪk / (BrE)
relating to the church, especially the Christian Church or its clergy
relating to the church or clergy

Examples

The cathedral's ecclesiastic architecture draws visitors from around the world.
She dedicated her life to the study of ecclesiastic law and history.
The council was attended by many prominent ecclesiastic figures from different denominations.

Synset Examples

ecclesiastic history

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secular - not connected with religious or spiritual matters

Etymology

From Greek ekklēsiastikos (of the assembly or church) via Late Latin ecclesiasticus.
