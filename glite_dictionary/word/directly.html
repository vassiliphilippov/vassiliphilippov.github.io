directly

1. ADV

/ dəˈrɛktli, daɪˈrɛktli / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛktli / (BrE)
Without anyone or anything intervening; in a manner involving no intermediary or obstruction.
without anything intervening

Examples

The instructions were given directly to the staff, with no middlemen involved.
He solved the problem directly, without seeking outside help or advice.
You can contact her directly by email if you have any questions.

Synset Examples

these two factors are directly related
he was directly responsible
measured the physical properties directly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indirectly - in a way that is not direct; involving an intermediary or steps in between

Etymology

From Latin 'directus' (straight).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00506737-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ dəˈrɛktli, daɪˈrɛktli / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛktli / (BrE)
without any delay or hesitation; immediately or at once
immediately, without delay

Examples

She left the meeting directly after receiving the phone call.
Please call me directly if you have any questions.
The doctor will see you directly, so please take a seat.

Synset Examples

he answered immediately
found an answer straightaway
an official accused of dishonesty should be suspended forthwith
Come here now!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immediately - at once; without delay
  • Synonym: forthwith - immediately; without delay
  • Antonym: eventually - in the end, especially after a period of delay

Etymology

From Middle English directly, from Old French direct, from Latin directus (straight, direct).

3. ADV

/ dəˈrɛktli / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛktli / (BrE)
in a straight line or manner without changing direction or stopping; without deviation
without deviation, straight, or immediately

Examples

The road runs directly from the village to the city limits.
She looked directly at me when she asked the question.
You can access the file directly from the server without any detours.

Synset Examples

the path leads directly to the lake
went direct to the office

Related Terms

  • Synonym: straight - in a direct line or without deviation
  • Antonym: indirectly - not in a straight line or without deviation; by an indirect route

Etymology

From Middle English 'directly', from Old French 'direct', from Latin 'directus' (straight).

in a straightforward or candid manner; frankly or openly
in a straightforward or candid manner

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms
