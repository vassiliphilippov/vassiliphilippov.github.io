Diplotaxis muralis

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪploʊˈtæksɪs mjʊˈrælɪs / (AmE), / ˌdɪpləˈtaksɪs mjʊˈrɑːlɪs / (BrE)
A yellow-flowered plant native to Europe, often growing on old walls and in waste places; considered an introduced weed in North America.
yellow-flowered European wall plant

Examples

Diplotaxis muralis can often be found flourishing in the cracks of old stone walls.
The invasive spread of Diplotaxis muralis has concerned conservationists in several North American regions.
Many gardeners recognize Diplotaxis muralis by its small, bright yellow flowers and distinctive scent.

Etymology

From Latin: Diplotaxis (from Greek, meaning 'double arrangement') + muralis ('of walls').
