defusing

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfjuzɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːzɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of removing the danger from something, especially by making a bomb harmless
making something safe, as with a bomb

Examples

The bomb squad handled the defusing of the suspicious package with great care.
Her calm approach contributed to the defusing of the tense situation.
Special training is required for the safe defusing of explosive devices.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: detonation - the act of causing a bomb or explosive to explode

Etymology

From 'defuse', formed from 'de-' (remove) and 'fuse' (igniter), originally in reference to explosives.
