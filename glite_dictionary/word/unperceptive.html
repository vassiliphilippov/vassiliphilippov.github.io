unperceptive

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnpərˈsɛptɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌnpəˈsɛptɪv / (BrE)
not having or showing an ability to perceive or understand things easily; lacking in sensitivity, taste, or judgment.
lacking perception or sensitivity

Examples

His unperceptive comments revealed a lack of understanding about the issue.
She found her boss too unperceptive to notice the team's low morale.
Because he was unperceptive, he missed several subtle hints in the conversation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: perceptive - having or showing keenness of perception
  • Synonym: insensitive - not sensitive to feelings or needs of others

Etymology

From un- (not) + perceptive.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.pərˈsɛp.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌn.pəˈsɛp.tɪv / (BrE)
not having or showing an ability to notice or understand things quickly or easily; lacking perception or insight.
lacking perception or insight

Examples

The committee was surprisingly unperceptive when it came to recognizing subtle problems.
His unperceptive response upset those who hoped for understanding.
She found him too unperceptive to be a good counselor.

Synset Examples

as unperceptive as a boulder

Related Terms

  • Antonym: perceptive - quick to notice or understand things; insightful
  • Synonym: insensitive - not noticing or caring about others' feelings

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'perceptive.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01750363-a
Synset POS: a
