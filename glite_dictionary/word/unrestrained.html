unrestrained

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.rɪˈstreɪnd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.rɪˈstreɪnd / (BrE)
not controlled or limited; showing feelings, excitement, or emotion in a free or excessive way.
not controlled; excessive in emotion or action

Examples

Her unrestrained laughter echoed through the hall, drawing everyone's attention.
The party erupted into unrestrained joy when the team won the championship.
He expressed his unrestrained excitement at seeing his favorite band live.

Synset Examples

a crowd of delirious baseball fans
something frantic in their gaiety
a mad whirl of pleasure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unchecked - not controlled or restrained; not held in check
  • Synonym: exuberant - full of energy, excitement, and cheerfulness
  • Antonym: controlled - restrained; kept within bounds or limits

Etymology

From un- (not) + restrain (from Old French restreindre, from Latin restringere).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.rɪˈstreɪnd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.rɪˈstreɪnd / (BrE)
not controlled or limited; not held back by restrictions or constraints.
not controlled or limited

Examples

The child’s unrestrained excitement was evident during the party.
She burst into unrestrained laughter at the comedian’s joke.
There was an unrestrained flow of creativity in the artist’s new work.

Synset Examples

unrestrained laughter

Related Terms

  • Antonym: restrained - kept under control or within limits

Etymology

From un- (not) + restrained, from Old French restreindre, from Latin restringere (to bind fast, hold back).
