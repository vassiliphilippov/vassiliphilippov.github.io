enthusiasm

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈθuːziˌæzəm / (AmE), / ɪnˈθjuːziˌæzəm / (BrE)
a strong feeling of energetic interest and excitement about something
lively interest or excitement

Examples

Her enthusiasm for learning new languages was truly inspiring.
The team's enthusiasm was evident during the final game of the season.
He spoke about the project with so much enthusiasm that everyone wanted to join.

Synset Examples

enthusiasm for his program is growing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eagerness - keen or enthusiastic willingness
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of interest or enthusiasm

Etymology

From Greek enthousiasmos (inspiration, ecstasy), from enthousiazein (to be inspired by a god).

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈθuziˌæzəm / (AmE), / ɪnˈθjuːziˌæzəm / (BrE)
intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval; great excitement about something
intense enjoyment or approval

Examples

Her enthusiasm for the new project was contagious and inspired the rest of the team.
Despite the early hour, the children showed remarkable enthusiasm for the field trip.
He greeted every challenge with enthusiasm and a positive attitude.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excitement - a feeling of great enthusiasm and eagerness
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of interest or enthusiasm

Etymology

From Greek entheos (possessed by a god), from en- (in) + theos (god).
