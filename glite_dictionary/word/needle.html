needle

1. VERB

/ ˈniː.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˈniː.dl̩ / (BrE)
to prick or pierce with a needle or something resembling a needle
prick with a needle

Examples

The nurse will needle your arm to draw some blood.
He accidentally needled his finger while sewing.
As she embroidered, she carefully needled the fabric with precision.

2. NOUN

/ ˈniːdəl / (AmE), / ˈniːdl̩ / (BrE)
a small, slender, sharp-pointed implement, typically of steel, used for sewing, knitting, or similar purposes
sharp pointed sewing implement

Examples

She threaded the needle carefully before starting to sew the button.
The doctor used a sterile needle to give the injection.
Knitting with a fine needle requires steady hands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pin - a small, slender, pointed piece of metal for fastening or attaching things

Etymology

From Old English nǣdl, related to Dutch naald, German Nadel, from Proto-Germanic *naðlō.

5. VERB

/ ˈniːdəl / (AmE), / ˈniːdl̩ / (BrE)
to deliberately annoy or provoke someone, especially by repeatedly criticizing or teasing them
provoke or annoy persistently

Examples

Her brother loved to needle her about her messy room until she finally cleaned it.
The journalist kept needling the politician with difficult questions at the interview.
He needled his friend by constantly reminding him of his embarrassing mistake.

Synset Examples

He needled her with his sarcastic remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to provoke - to stimulate or incite someone to feel or do something
  • Synonym: to irritate - to make someone annoyed or impatient

Etymology

Metaphorical use, comparing provoking someone to a sharp needle; derives from the noun 'needle'.
