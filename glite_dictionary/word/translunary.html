translunary

1. ADJ

/ trænˈsluːnəri / (AmE), / trænˈluːnəri / (BrE)
Situated beyond the moon or its orbit; existing outside the earthly realm.
beyond the moon; outside the earthly realm
archaic

Examples

The poet wrote about a translunary world untouched by human sorrow.
He longed for adventures in translunary regions beyond mortal reach.
Such beauty, she thought, could only exist in a translunary realm.

Synset Examples

who can imagine a translunary visitor in Times Square?

Etymology

From Latin trans- (across, beyond) + luna (moon).

2. ADJ

/ trænzˈluːnəri / (AmE), / trӕnzˈluːnəri / (BrE)
Unworldly or ethereal; existing or relating to a realm beyond the physical or material world, especially beyond the moon.
unworldly; ethereal; beyond the moon
archaic

Examples

The poet's vision was filled with translunary beauty and otherworldly inspiration.
Her motives seemed translunary, untouched by the concerns of ordinary life.
The philosopher spoke of a translunary realm unreachable by human senses.

Synset Examples

high translunary dreams

Related Terms

  • Antonym: earthly - relating to the physical world as opposed to the spiritual or transcendent

Etymology

From Latin 'trans-' (across, beyond) and 'lunaris' (of the moon).

WordNet ID: oewn-01184093-s
Synset POS: s
