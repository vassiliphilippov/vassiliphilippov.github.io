activation

1. NOUN

/ ˌæktɪˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌæktɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something active or stimulating activity in an organism or chemical
process of making active; stimulation

Examples

The activation of certain enzymes is essential for digestion.
Exposure to sunlight leads to the activation of vitamin D in the skin.
Recent studies focus on the activation of neural pathways during learning.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inhibition - the process of restraining or preventing activity (in biology or chemistry)
  • Synonym: stimulation - the action of arousing activity, especially in biological contexts

Etymology

From Latin 'activare' (to make active), from 'activus' (active).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæktɪˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌaktɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something active or effective, especially a device such as a bomb
the process of making active or effective

Examples

The sudden activation of the alarm startled everyone in the building.
Special protocols are required for the safe activation of the equipment.
Authorities are investigating the accidental activation of the explosive device.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deactivation - the process of making something inactive or ineffective
  • Synonym: initiation - the act of beginning something

Etymology

From Latin activare (to make active), from activus (active).

3. NOUN

/ ˌæk.təˈveɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæk.tɪˈveɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something active or causing something to have energy
the process of making active

Examples

The activation of the alarm system is automatic when motion is detected.
Scientists are studying the activation of certain areas of the brain during sleep.
The software requires activation before it can be used.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deactivation - the process of making something inactive
  • Synonym: to stimulate - to encourage something to become active or more active

Etymology

From Latin 'activus' (active) + '-ation' (forming nouns of action or process).
