hipster

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪp.stɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɪp.stə / (BrE)
a member of a youth subculture, especially in the 1960s, that embraced nonconformity, communal living, and alternative music and lifestyles
member of a 1960s countercultural youth movement

Examples

Many hipsters in the 1960s embraced communal living and alternative forms of artistic expression.
The hipsters gathered in San Francisco to celebrate free love, peace, and music together.
Some parents worried when their children started dressing like hipsters and listening to psychedelic rock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beatnik - member of the 1950s Beat Generation subculture
  • Antonym: square - a conventional, unadventurous person, opposite of a hipster

Etymology

Probably from 'hip' (aware, in the know) + 'ster' (one who practices), popularized mid-20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɪp.stɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɪp.stə / (BrE)
a person who rejects mainstream culture and embraces alternative styles, ideas, or lifestyles, often associated with progressive views and unconventional tastes
person who rejects mainstream culture

Examples

He dresses like a hipster, always sporting vintage clothes and a quirky hat.
The coffee shop was full of hipsters working on their laptops and discussing indie music.
Being a hipster is often associated with supporting local businesses and alternative culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bohemian - person with unconventional, artistic, or alternative lifestyle
  • Antonym: square - person who is conventional or old-fashioned

Etymology

Originally U.S. slang, from 'hip' (fashionable, in the know) + '-ster' (person), first used in the 1940s jazz subculture.
