Baptisia tinctoria

1. NOUN

/ bæpˈtɪʃiə tɪŋkˈtɔriə / (AmE), / bæpˈtɪsɪə tɪŋkˈtɔːrɪə / (BrE)
A North American herbaceous plant with many branches and bright yellow flowers, native to the eastern United States and known for its dye-producing properties.
herbaceous plant with yellow flowers, native to eastern US

Examples

The root of Baptisia tinctoria was once used to produce a natural dye.
Gardeners often plant Baptisia tinctoria for its attractive clusters of yellow flowers.
Baptisia tinctoria is sometimes called wild indigo due to its dyeing properties.

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Baptisia' from Greek 'baptisis' (dipping), and 'tinctoria' from Latin 'tingere' (to dye).
