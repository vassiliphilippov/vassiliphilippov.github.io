anecdotal

1. ADJ

/ ˌænɪkˈdoʊtəl / (AmE), / ˌænɪkˈdəʊtəl / (BrE)
characterized by or based on personal anecdotes rather than systematic study or analysis
based on personal stories or anecdotes

Examples

His speech was full of anecdotal evidence rather than scientific data.
Many early reports about the new treatment are only anecdotal and need further study.
She entertained us with her anecdotal knowledge of the city's history.

Synset Examples

anecdotal conversation
an anecdotal history of jazz
he was at his anecdotic best

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anecdote - a short, amusing or interesting story about a real incident or person

Etymology

From anecdote + -al, dating to the 18th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˌænɪkˈdoʊtəl / (AmE), / ˌænɪkˈdəʊtəl / (BrE)
(of information or evidence) based on personal accounts rather than facts or research; having the character of an anecdote.
based on personal accounts, not research

Examples

Much of the report is based on anecdotal evidence rather than scientific studies.
The author’s anecdotal stories made the lecture engaging and relatable.
Her conclusions were criticized as being too anecdotal and lacking solid data.

Synset Examples

anecdotal evidence

Related Terms

  • Antonym: empirical - based on observation or experiment rather than theory or personal accounts

Etymology

From anecdote + -al; ‘anecdote’ comes from Greek anekdota ‘things unpublished’.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03055845-a
Synset POS: a
