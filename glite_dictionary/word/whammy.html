whammy

1. NOUN

/ ˈwæmi / (AmE), / ˈwæmi / (BrE)
a serious or devastating setback or misfortune
a devastating setback

Examples

The company took a whammy when its main client went bankrupt.
Losing his job was a real whammy for Tom and his family.
After a series of whammies, the project was finally abandoned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blow - a sudden unlucky event or setback
  • Antonym: boon - a thing that is helpful or beneficial (opposite meaning)

2. NOUN

/ ˈwæmi / (AmE), / ˈwæmi / (BrE)
an evil spell or curse that brings bad luck or misfortune
an evil spell or curse
informal

Examples

The old woman was convinced that someone had put a whammy on her house.
He feared the team might lose if the fans put a whammy on them.
The magician joked about removing the whammy from the unlucky ticket.

Synset Examples

a witch put a curse on his whole family
he put the whammy on me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse - a solemn utterance intended to invoke supernatural power to inflict harm
  • Synonym: hex - an evil spell or charm
  • Antonym: blessing - a prayer asking for divine favor and protection

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly imitative or influenced by 'wham' (a sudden blow or impact).
