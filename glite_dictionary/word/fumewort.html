fumewort

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːˌwɔrt / (AmE), / ˈfjuːˌwəːt / (BrE)
a delicate European herb with grey-green leaves and spikes of purplish flowers, formerly used in medicine
European herb with purplish flowers

Examples

The old herbalist grew fumewort alongside other medicinal plants in her garden.
In spring, fumewort carpets the forest floor with its purplish blooms.
I read that fumewort was once used in folk remedies for various ailments.

Etymology

From Middle English 'fumeterre' (fume of the earth), influenced by 'wort' (plant; herb).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːwɜrt / (AmE), / ˈfjuːwəːt / (BrE)
A herbaceous plant of northern Europe and Asia, with erect racemes of red flowers, belonging to the genus Corydalis or related genera.
herb with red flowers of northern Europe and Asia

Examples

The woodland floor was dotted with patches of fumewort in bloom.
Many gardeners value fumewort for its delicate red flowers in spring.
She identified the unusual plant as a rare species of fumewort.

Etymology

From Middle English 'fumewort', from Latin 'fumus' (smoke) and English 'wort' (plant); named for its smoky odor.
