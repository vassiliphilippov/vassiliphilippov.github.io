Centrosema

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛn.trəˈsiː.mə / (AmE), / ˌsɛn.trəˈsiː.mə / (BrE)
a genus of mainly tropical American vines of the legume family, having trifoliate leaves and large flowers
genus of tropical American vines (legume family)

Examples

The Centrosema vine thrives in warm, humid regions of South America.
Many farmers use Centrosema species to improve soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.
The large, pale purple flowers of Centrosema are attractive to pollinators such as bees.

Etymology

From Greek kentron (spur) and sema (sign), referring to the flower's structure.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛn.trəˈsiː.mə / (AmE), / ˌsɛn.trəˈsiː.mə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American vines in the legume family (Leguminosae), characterized by trifoliate leaves and large flowers.
genus of tropical American vines (Leguminosae)

Examples

Several species of Centrosema are cultivated for their striking flowers and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
The Centrosema vine climbed rapidly up the fence, displaying its purple blossoms in late summer.
Botanists identified the plant as a member of the Centrosema genus due to its distinctive trifoliate leaves.

Etymology

From Greek 'kentron' (spur, point) and 'sema' (sign), referring to the shape of the flower.
