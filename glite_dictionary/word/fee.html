fee

1. NOUN

/ fi / (AmE), / fiː / (BrE)
a legal interest in land, especially one that can be inherited
an inheritable interest in land (legal)
legal

Examples

The family held the land in fee for generations, passing it down from parent to child.
Under medieval law, a fee simple estate provided the owner full inheritable rights.
Many land titles in England originated as a fee held from a feudal lord.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estate - an interest in land or property; the degree, quantity, nature, and extent of interest a person has

Etymology

From Old French 'fief', from Medieval Latin 'feudum' (feudal landholding).

2. NOUN

/ fiː / (AmE), / fiː / (BrE)
an amount of money paid for a particular service or privilege
charge for a service or privilege

Examples

The lawyer charged a high fee for his services.
There is an additional fee to join the club.
Students must pay a registration fee at the beginning of the semester.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charge - a price asked for goods or services
  • Synonym: cost - the amount paid or required for something
  • Antonym: fine - a sum of money exacted as a penalty

Etymology

From Old French 'fie', from Medieval Latin 'feodum', meaning 'payment' or 'property'.
