ballot

1. NOUN

/ ˈbælət / (AmE), / ˈbælət / (BrE)
a process of voting in which choices are counted to determine a decision or select a candidate
method of voting by count

Examples

All the members submitted their ballot before the meeting ended.
The results of the ballot were announced the following morning.
Each voter casts a ballot to choose a new committee chairperson.

Synset Examples

there were only 17 votes in favor of the motion
they allowed just one vote per person

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vote - a formal indication of choice between options
  • Synonym: election - the process of choosing someone for a public office by voting

Etymology

From Italian 'ballotta' (small ball used for voting), diminutive of 'balla' (ball).

2. VERB

/ ˈbælət / (AmE), / ˈbælət / (BrE)
to cast a vote, especially by secret ballot, in an election or decision-making process
to vote by ballot

Examples

All members will ballot on the proposed changes next week.
The union will ballot its employees on whether to accept the offer.
After much debate, the committee decided to ballot on the issue.

Synset Examples

The voters were balloting in this state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vote - to make a choice by casting a ballot or by raising a hand

Etymology

From Italian 'ballotta' (small ball used in voting), diminutive of 'balla' (ball).

3. NOUN

/ ˈbælət / (AmE), / ˈbælət / (BrE)
a paper or document listing candidates or options, used to record votes in an election or poll
voting paper or document

Examples

Each voter marked their choice on the ballot before placing it in the box.
The ballot listed several candidates for the city council position.
After counting every ballot, the election officials announced the winner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vote - a formal expression of choice in an election or poll

Etymology

From Italian 'ballotta' (a small ball used in voting), diminutive of 'balla' (ball).

Synset POS: n
