to anathemize

1. VERB

/ əˈnæθəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / əˈnaθɪˌmaɪz / (BrE)
to curse or formally condemn; to declare someone or something as accursed or evil, often with religious or solemn authority
to formally curse or condemn

Examples

The council decided to anathemize the heretical teachings that had spread through the region.
Many religious leaders would anathemize practices they believe are contrary to scripture.
During the ceremony, the group was formally anathemized by the authorities for their actions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse - to call upon supernatural power to inflict harm; to speak a curse

Etymology

From Medieval Latin anathematizare, from Greek anathematizein (to curse).

2. VERB

/ əˈnæθəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / əˈnæθəˌmaɪz / (BrE)
to formally curse or denounce; to wish harm or evil upon someone or something
formally curse or denounce

Examples

The leader publicly anathemized the traitors, declaring them enemies of the state.
Some religious authorities anathemize certain beliefs as heretical.
Feeling betrayed, she anathemized the one who had wronged her.

Synset Examples

The bad witch cursed the child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse - to invoke evil or misfortune upon someone
  • Antonym: to bless - to invoke good or protection upon someone

Etymology

From Late Latin anathematizare, from Greek anathematizein (to curse).
