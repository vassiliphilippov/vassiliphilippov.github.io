traditionalistic

1. ADJ

/ trəˌdɪʃ.ə.nəˈlɪs.tɪk / (AmE), / trəˌdɪʃ.ə.nəˈlɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
strongly adhering to tradition, especially in cultural or religious practices
adhering to tradition, especially in practices

Examples

Some families remain traditionalistic in their holiday celebrations, following customs passed down through generations.
The community's traditionalistic attitudes influenced its resistance to change.
He argued from a traditionalistic perspective against the introduction of modern technology in rituals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traditional - following existing customs or practices

Etymology

From 'traditional' + '-istic', denoting the quality of being traditionalist.
