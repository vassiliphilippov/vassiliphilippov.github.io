virilisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɪr.ə.lɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɪr.ɪ.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the abnormal development of male sexual characteristics in a female, usually caused by hormone imbalance or certain medical treatments
abnormal male trait development in females
medical

Examples

Certain hormone imbalances can lead to virilisation in women.
The patient showed signs of virilisation, including deepened voice and facial hair.
Doctors monitored her for any symptoms of virilisation during the clinical trial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masculinization - the process of becoming male or developing male characteristics

Etymology

From Latin 'virilis' (male, manly) + '-ation' (action or process).
