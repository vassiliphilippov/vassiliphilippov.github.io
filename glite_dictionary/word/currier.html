currier

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɜːriər / (AmE), / ˈkʌriə / (BrE)
a person who dresses and finishes leather after it has been tanned
leather dresser; person who finishes leather

Examples

The village once supported a currier, blacksmith, and several weavers.
As a skilled currier, he transformed rough hides into supple leather for shoemakers.
In the 18th century, the currier's shop was an essential part of the local economy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tanner - a person who tans animal hides into leather

Etymology

From Old French *corier*, from Late Latin *coriarius* (tanner, leatherworker), from Latin *corium* (leather).
