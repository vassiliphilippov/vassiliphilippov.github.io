Jurassic

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈræsɪk / (AmE), / dʒʊəˈræsɪk / (BrE)
the period of geological time from about 201 million to 145 million years ago, characterized by the dominance of dinosaurs and extensive conifer forests
geological period dominated by dinosaurs

Examples

The famous cliffs at Dorset contain fossils from the Jurassic period.
Dinosaurs flourished during the Jurassic, populating much of the Earth.
Many films depict the Jurassic as an era ruled by giant reptiles.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Triassic - the period preceding the Jurassic in the Mesozoic Era
  • Degree Higher: Cretaceous - the period following the Jurassic in the Mesozoic Era

Etymology

Named after the Jura Mountains (French: 'Jura'), where rocks from this period were first studied.

2. ADJ

/ dʒəˈræsɪk / (AmE), / dʒʊəˈræsɪk / (BrE)
relating to the second period of the Mesozoic Era, characterized by the dominance of dinosaurs and the appearance of the first birds.
of the Jurassic geological period

Examples

The Jurassic period saw a tremendous increase in the diversity of dinosaurs.
Many fossils discovered in the region date back to Jurassic times.
The landscape is dotted with Jurassic limestone formations.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Triassic - earlier period of the Mesozoic Era
  • Degree Higher: Cretaceous - later period of the Mesozoic Era

Etymology

From French 'Jurassique', from Jura (mountain range between France and Switzerland) where rocks from this period were first studied.
