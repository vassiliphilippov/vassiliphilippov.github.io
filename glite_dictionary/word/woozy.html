woozy

1. ADJ

/ ˈwuːzi / (AmE), / ˈwuːzi / (BrE)
feeling weak, dizzy, or unsteady, as if about to lose balance or consciousness.
feeling weak or dizzy

Examples

After standing up too quickly, I felt woozy and had to sit back down.
The medication made her feel a little woozy during the afternoon.
He was still woozy from the effects of the anesthesia.

Synset Examples

had a dizzy spell
a dizzy pinnacle
had a headache and felt giddy
a giddy precipice
feeling woozy from the blow on his head
a vertiginous climb up the face of the cliff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dizzy - feeling as if you might lose your balance or faint
  • Synonym: giddy - feeling light-headed and likely to fall
  • Antonym: steady - firmly fixed or supported; not shaking or likely to fall
