Franche-Comté

1. PROPN

/ fʁãʃ kɔ̃ˈteɪ / (AmE), / fʁãʃ kɔ̃ˈteɪ / (BrE)
a historical region and former province in eastern France, bordering Switzerland; now part of the Bourgogne-Franche-Comté administrative region.
former province in eastern France

Examples

The rolling hills of Franche-Comté are famous for their lush pastures and cheese production.
She spent her summer holiday exploring the medieval villages of Franche-Comté.
Comté cheese gets its name from the Franche-Comté region, where it originated.

Etymology

French, meaning 'Free County' (from 'franche' for 'free' and 'comté' for 'county').
