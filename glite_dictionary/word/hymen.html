Hymen

1. PROPN

/ ˈhaɪmən / (AmE), / ˈhʌɪmən / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the god of marriage and weddings, often invoked in wedding ceremonies.
the god of marriage in Greek mythology

Examples

In ancient Greece, couples would pray to Hymen for a happy and prosperous marriage.
The story of Hymen appears in multiple classical myths related to weddings and unions.
A statue of Hymen was once placed at the entrance of the temple to bless newlyweds.

Etymology

From Greek Hymēn, the personification of the wedding hymn.
