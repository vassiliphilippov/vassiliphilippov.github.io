Pueraria

1. NOUN

/ pjuˈɛər.i.ə / (AmE), / pjʊəˈrɑːrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of woody vines native to Asia, including the kudzu plant, widely known for its rapid growth.
genus of woody Asiatic vines (incl. kudzu)

Examples

The invasive nature of Pueraria species like kudzu has impacted many southern landscapes.
Scientists study Pueraria for its potential medicinal and agricultural uses.
Pueraria roots are used in traditional Asian herbal medicine for their therapeutic properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kudzu - a fast-growing Asian climbing vine, Pueraria montana, known for its invasiveness

Etymology

From the name of Swiss botanist Marc Nicolas Puerari (1766–1845).

2. NOUN

/ puˈɛrəriə / (AmE), / pjʊəˈreəriə / (BrE)
A genus of woody vines native to Asia, including the plant kudzu, known for rapid growth and extensive coverage.
genus of woody Asiatic vines (e.g., kudzu)

Examples

The invasive Pueraria rapidly covered abandoned fields throughout the region.
Scientists are studying Pueraria for its potential use in erosion control.
Several species of Pueraria are valued for their medicinal properties in traditional Asian medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kudzu - fast-growing Asiatic vine in the genus Pueraria

Etymology

From New Latin, named after Swiss botanist Marc Nicolas Puerari.
