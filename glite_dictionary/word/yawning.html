yawning

1. ADJ

/ ˈjɔnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈjɔːnɪŋ / (BrE)
very wide open, especially suggesting an opening that could engulf or swallow something; gaping.
extremely wide open; gaping

Examples

The climbers peered into the yawning chasm below them.
A yawning hole in the road forced drivers to take a detour.
He stood on the edge of the yawning pit, feeling dizzy from its depth.

Synset Examples

the yawning mine shaft
a yawning abyss

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaping - very wide open

2. ADJ

/ ˈjɔnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈjɔːnɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person or group) having the mouth wide open, typically indicating boredom, tiredness, or sleepiness.
mouth open, showing boredom or tiredness

Examples

The students sat in a yawning lecture hall, struggling to stay awake.
A yawning crowd waited impatiently for the event to begin.
She glanced at her yawning coworkers and suggested they take a break.

Synset Examples

a yawning congregation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleepy - feeling drowsy or ready for sleep
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01659451-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈjɔnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈjɔːnɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person or group) characterized by lack of attention or boredom, often shown through yawning.
showing lack of attention or boredom

Examples

The speaker tried to energize the yawning audience with a joke.
A yawning silence filled the room during the dull presentation.
The teacher noticed a yawning class and decided to change her strategy.

Synset Examples

the yawning congregation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bored - feeling weary and uninterested
  • Antonym: attentive - paying close attention

Etymology

Present participle of 'yawn', from Old English 'gewanian' (to open the mouth wide).

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ ˈjɔnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈjɔːnɪŋ / (BrE)
An involuntary intake of breath with the mouth wide open, typically caused by tiredness, boredom, or drowsiness.
involuntary open-mouthed breath, often from tiredness

Examples

His yawning was so contagious that everyone in the room soon started to feel sleepy.
She tried to stifle her yawning during the long lecture, but it was difficult to stay alert.
The teacher noticed the yawning among students after lunch and decided to take a short break.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yawn - an act of opening the mouth wide and inhaling due to tiredness or boredom
