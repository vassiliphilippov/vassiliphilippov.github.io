scheme

1. NOUN

/ skiːm / (AmE), / skiːm / (BrE)
a mental framework or pattern for organizing knowledge, experiences, or actions, often updated as new information is learned
mental structure for organizing knowledge

Examples

Children use different schemes to interpret new experiences and information.
The psychologist explained how a cognitive scheme influences perception and problem-solving.
As she learned more, her original scheme for understanding the topic changed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schema - a structured framework or plan, especially in cognition

Etymology

From Greek 'skēma' (form, shape), via Latin 'schema' and French 'schème'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05936599-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ skiːm / (AmE), / skiːm / (BrE)
to make secret plans, often with a dishonest or deceitful purpose; to devise a plan or system for achieving something
devise a secret or elaborate plan

Examples

The committee began to scheme ways to increase membership.
She would often scheme to get out of doing her chores.
They are scheming to take control of the company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plot - to secretly make plans to achieve something, especially something harmful or illegal
  • Synonym: plan - to decide on and arrange in advance

Etymology

From Greek 'skhēma' (form, figure), via Latin 'schema' and Old French 'scheme'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00710205-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ skim / (AmE), / skiːm / (BrE)
a planned arrangement or system for achieving a particular aim, especially a preliminary or schematic plan
preliminary plan or arrangement

Examples

The architects presented a scheme for the new library at the meeting.
Before implementation, the engineers refined the original scheme to address safety concerns.
The government rejected the proposed scheme due to its high costs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plan - a detailed proposal for doing or achieving something
  • Synonym: design - a plan or drawing that shows how something will look or function

Etymology

From Greek 'skhēma' (form, figure), via Latin 'schema' and Old French 'esquema'.

make secret, often dishonest, plans

Examples

They schemed for months to win the contract unfairly.
You can see him scheming in the background, waiting for his chance to take control.
