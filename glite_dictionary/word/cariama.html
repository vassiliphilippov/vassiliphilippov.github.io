Cariama

1. NOUN

/ kɛɹ.iˈæ.mə / (AmE), / ˌkæ.riˈɑː.mə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Cariamidae, comprising only the crested cariama, a large terrestrial bird native to South America.
genus of crested South American birds

Examples

The Cariama is known for its distinctive crest and long legs.
Scientists classify the crested seriema under the genus Cariama.
Birdwatchers in Brazil hope to spot a member of the Cariama genus in the wild.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'cariama', from Tupi 'cariama' (crested bird).

2. NOUN

/ kæriˈɑːmə / (AmE), / ˌkæriˈɑːmə / (BrE)
A genus of South American birds in the family Cariamidae, comprising only the crested cariama.
genus of South American birds

Examples

The Cariama is the only extant genus in its family, Cariamidae.
Researchers observed the unique hunting techniques of the Cariama in the wild.
The Cariama is known for its distinctive crest and long legs.

Etymology

From Portuguese cariama, from Tupi cariamã (the bird's name).
