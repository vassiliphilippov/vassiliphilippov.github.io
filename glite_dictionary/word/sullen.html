sullen

1. ADJ

/ ˈsʌl.ən / (AmE), / ˈsʌl.ən / (BrE)
(of the sky or weather) dark and gloomy because of clouds; giving the impression of sadness or brooding.
(of sky) dark, gloomy, overcast

Examples

The sullen clouds loomed over the city, threatening to release a heavy rain at any moment.
A sullen sky hung above the park, making the afternoon feel much colder and darker.
The fisherman glanced warily at the sullen horizon, knowing a storm was on its way.

Synset Examples

a heavy sky

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloomy - dark or poorly lit, especially as to appear depressing or frightening
  • Antonym: bright - having lots of light, not dark

Etymology

From Old French *sulain*, perhaps from Anglo-French *solein* (alone), possibly influenced by Middle English *soleyn* (solitary).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsʌlən / (AmE), / ˈsʌl.ən / (BrE)
Bad-tempered or gloomy and silent, showing irritation or ill humor by a gloomy silence.
bad-tempered or gloomy and silent

Examples

After the argument, he sat in the corner with a sullen expression on his face.
Her sullen silence made it clear that she was still upset about the decision.
The sullen teenagers refused to participate in the family activities all weekend.

Synset Examples

a dark scowl
the proverbially dour New England Puritan
a glum, hopeless shrug
he sat in moody silence
a morose and unsociable manner
a saturnine, almost misanthropic young genius
a sour temper
a sullen crowd

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morose - having a sullen and gloomy disposition
  • Antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From Old French *solen* (alone, solitary), from Latin *solus* (alone).
