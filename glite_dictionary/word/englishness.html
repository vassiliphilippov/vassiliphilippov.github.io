Englishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
The qualities, characteristics, or state of being English, especially as distinguishing English people or culture.
quality or character of being English

Examples

The novelist's latest work explores the meaning of Englishness in a changing society.
Many tourists are fascinated by the sense of Englishness they encounter in rural villages.
Tea drinking is often seen as a symbol of traditional Englishness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Britishness - the quality or state of being British

Etymology

Formed from 'English' + '-ness', indicating state or quality.
