Scilla verna

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪl.ə ˈvɜːr.nə / (AmE), / ˈsɪl.ə ˈvɜː.nə / (BrE)
A small European plant with narrow leaves and blue or purple star-shaped flowers, also known as spring squill.
a European plant with blue or purple flowers

Examples

In the coastal meadows, the Scilla verna blooms in early spring.
Botanists study the distribution of Scilla verna along the European coastline.
The delicate blue flowers of Scilla verna are a welcome sign of spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spring squill - common name for Scilla verna, a flowering plant
  • Degree Higher: Scilla siberica - a related scilla species with blue flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'Scilla' (a genus name for squills) and 'verna' (of spring).
