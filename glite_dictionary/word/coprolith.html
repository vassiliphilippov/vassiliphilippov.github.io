coprolith

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.prə.lɪθ / (AmE), / ˈkɒ.prə.lɪθ / (BrE)
A hard mass of dried fecal matter found in the intestines or rectum.
hardened mass of fecal matter
medical

Examples

The surgeon discovered a coprolith causing an obstruction in the patient's intestine.
Large coproliths can sometimes require surgical removal if they cause complications.
A coprolith can lead to pain and even infection if left untreated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fecalith - a stone-like mass of hardened feces

Etymology

From Greek kopros (dung) + lithos (stone).
