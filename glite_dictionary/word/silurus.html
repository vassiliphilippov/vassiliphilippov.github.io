Silurus

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈlʊrəs / (AmE), / sɪˈljʊərəs / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Siluridae, comprising certain catfish species native to Eurasia
type genus of catfish family Siluridae

Examples

The Silurus genus is known for its large European catfish species.
Many scientists study Silurus to understand the diversity of catfishes.
The habitat of Silurus includes slow-moving rivers and lakes throughout Eurasia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catfish - a type of freshwater fish with whisker-like barbels

Etymology

From Latin 'silurus', borrowed from Greek 'silouros' (a kind of large river fish).

2. NOUN

/ sɪˈlʊrəs / (AmE), / sɪˈljʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of large catfishes, especially native to Eurasia, and the type genus of the family Siluridae.
a genus of catfishes

Examples

The Silurus genus includes the European catfish, which can grow to impressive sizes.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Silurus species in Asian rivers.
One well-known member of the Silurus genus is Silurus glanis, Europe's largest freshwater fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catfish - a freshwater fish with whisker-like barbels

Etymology

From Latin Silurus, from Ancient Greek σίλουρος (sílouros, kind of river fish).
