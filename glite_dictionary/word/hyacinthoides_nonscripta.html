Hyacinthoides nonscripta

1. NOUN

/ haɪ.əˌsɪn.θɔɪˈdiːz nɒnˈskrɪptə / (AmE), / haɪ.əˌsɪn.θɔɪˈdiːz nɒnˈskrɪptə / (BrE)
the scientific name for the common bluebell, a wild plant with bell-shaped blue flowers found in woods, especially in Western Europe
the scientific name for the common bluebell plant

Examples

The woodlands are carpeted with Hyacinthoides nonscripta in late spring, creating a vivid sea of blue.
Botanists have reclassified Hyacinthoides nonscripta several times, previously placing it in the genus Scilla.
Many British forests are famous for their displays of Hyacinthoides nonscripta each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluebell - common name for Hyacinthoides nonscripta, a wild plant with blue flowers

Etymology

From Latin: 'Hyacinthoides' (resembling a hyacinth) and 'nonscripta' (unlettered, unmarked).
