thermosetting

1. ADJ

/ ˌθɝːmoʊˈsɛtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməʊˈsɛtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a substance or material) becoming permanently hard and rigid when heated or cured, and unable to be remelted.
becoming rigid when heated or cured

Examples

Phenol-formaldehyde is a classic thermosetting polymer used in making durable electrical parts.
Once cured, thermosetting plastics cannot be reshaped or melted down.
Epoxy adhesives are thermosetting and provide strong bonds for a variety of materials.

Synset Examples

the phenol resins and plastics were the original synthetic thermosetting materials

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thermoplastic - (of a substance) capable of being repeatedly softened by heating and hardened by cooling

Etymology

From thermo-, meaning 'heat', and set, meaning 'to become firm or solid'.
