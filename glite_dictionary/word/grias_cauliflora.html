Grias cauliflora

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡriː.əs ˌkɔː.lɪˈflɔː.rə / (AmE), / ˈɡriː.əs ˌkɔː.lɪˈflɔː.rə / (BrE)
A West Indian tree bearing edible fruits that resemble mangoes, belonging to the family Lecythidaceae.
West Indian tree with mango-like edible fruit

Examples

The fruit of Grias cauliflora is enjoyed fresh by many people in the Caribbean.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Grias cauliflora in its native habitat.
Local markets often sell the sweet, mango-like fruits of Grias cauliflora.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Grias' and species name 'cauliflora', meaning 'stem-flowered'.
