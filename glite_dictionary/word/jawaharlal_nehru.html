Jawaharlal Nehru

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑːwɑːhɑːrˈlɑːl ˈneɪruː / (AmE), / dʒɑːwəˌhɑːlˈlɑːl ˈneɪruː / (BrE)
(1889–1964) Indian statesman and the first Prime Minister of India, serving from 1947 to 1964; a central figure in India's struggle for independence and in shaping the modern Indian nation.
first Prime Minister of India

Examples

Jawaharlal Nehru played a pivotal role in shaping India's foreign policy after independence.
Many schools and institutions in India are named in honor of Jawaharlal Nehru.
The legacy of Jawaharlal Nehru continues to influence Indian politics today.
