Callistephus chinensis

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlɪstəfəs tʃɪˈnɛnsɪs / (AmE), / kəˌlɪstɪfəs tʃɪˈnɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a species of annual flowering plant popularly known as the China aster, cultivated for its large, brightly colored blooms
annual flowering plant grown for its colorful flowers

Examples

Gardeners often grow Callistephus chinensis for its striking variety of colors.
The bouquet featured fresh Callistephus chinensis blooms cut from the garden.
Many annual flower beds are filled with Callistephus chinensis during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: China aster - common name for Callistephus chinensis, cultivated as an ornamental flower

Etymology

From Greek 'kallos' (beauty) + 'stephos' (crown); 'chinensis' meaning 'from China' in Latin.
