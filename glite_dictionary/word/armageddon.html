Armageddon

1. PROPN

/ ˌɑːrməˈɡɛd(ə)n / (AmE), / ˌɑːməˈɡed(ə)n / (BrE)
(in the Bible) the place where the final battle between the forces of good and evil will be fought at the end of the world; more generally, a catastrophic conflict or event leading to widespread destruction.
the final battle at the end of the world (Biblical)

Examples

Many believe that environmental collapse could trigger humanity's own Armageddon.
The armies gathered like something from Armageddon before the final confrontation.
In Revelation, Armageddon is prophesied as the site of the ultimate battle between good and evil.

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Har Megiddo' (mountain of Megiddo), via Greek 'Armagedōn' in the Book of Revelation.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrməˈɡɛd(ə)n / (AmE), / ˌɑːməˈɡɛd(ə)n / (BrE)
a conflict or battle of catastrophic destruction, especially one thought to end or decide something significant
catastrophically destructive battle

Examples

Some fear that the next global conflict could escalate into an Armageddon.
The politicians warned that a nuclear war would be an Armageddon for humanity.
World War I was often referred to as an Armageddon by those who lived through it.

Synset Examples

they called the first World War an Armageddon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apocalypse - the complete final destruction of the world
  • Antonym: peace - freedom from war or violence

Etymology

From Greek 'Armagedōn', referring to the prophesied final battle in the Book of Revelation.
