codfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑdˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɒdˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
The lean white flesh of a cod, an important North Atlantic fish, commonly used as food and usually baked or poached.
lean white flesh of a cod (food)

Examples

The chef recommended baking the codfish with a lemon and herb crust.
For a healthy dinner, she prepared steamed vegetables and codfish.
Many traditional British recipes feature codfish as the main ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cod - a large North Atlantic fish valued for its flesh

Etymology

From Middle English 'codfisch', from 'cod' (fish species) + 'fish'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːdˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɒdˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A large marine fish found in Arctic and cold-temperate waters, known for its white flesh and valued as food.
marine food fish of cold waters

Examples

Fishermen in Norway rely on the annual migration of codfish for their livelihood.
The flaky white meat of codfish makes it popular in many traditional dishes.
In the 18th century, codfish was a crucial export for New England economies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cod - short form for codfish, a large marine food fish

Etymology

From 'cod' (the fish) + 'fish', with 'cod' possibly from Middle Dutch 'cadd' (bag), referring to the fish's appearance.
