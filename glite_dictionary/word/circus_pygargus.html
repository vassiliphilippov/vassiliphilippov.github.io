Circus pygargus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜːrkəs paɪˈɡɑːrɡəs / (AmE), / ˈsəːkəs pʌɪˈɡɑːɡəs / (BrE)
A brownish European harrier, also known as Montagu's harrier, distinguished by its slender body and long wings.
brownish European harrier (Montagu's harrier)

Examples

Circus pygargus is often seen gliding low over fields in search of prey.
The conservation of Circus pygargus populations has become a priority in some European countries.
Scientists observed a nesting pair of Circus pygargus in the wetlands last spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Montagu's harrier - Common English name for Circus pygargus, a European bird of prey

Etymology

From Latin; 'Circus' from Greek 'kirkos' (a type of hawk), 'pygargus' from Greek 'pygargos' (with white rump).
