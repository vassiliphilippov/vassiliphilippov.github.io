Aleksandr Feodorovich Kerensky

1. PROPN

/ əˈlɛksəndər fiˈoʊdərəvɪtʃ kəˈrɛn.ski / (AmE), / əˈlɛksəndə fiˈɔːdərəvɪtʃ kəˈrɛnski / (BrE)
(1881–1970) Russian revolutionary and politician who served as head of state after the abdication of Nicholas II, later overthrown by the Bolsheviks.
Russian revolutionary and provisional leader, 1881–1970

Examples

Aleksandr Feodorovich Kerensky played a key role in the Russian Revolution of 1917 before being deposed by the Bolsheviks.
Many historians debate whether Aleksandr Feodorovich Kerensky could have prevented the rise of the Soviet regime.
After his exile, Aleksandr Feodorovich Kerensky lived in several countries and became a lecturer and writer.
