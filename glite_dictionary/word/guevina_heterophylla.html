Guevina heterophylla

1. NOUN

/ ɡeɪˈviːnə ˌhɛtəroʊˈfɪlə / (AmE), / ɡeɪˈviːnə ˌhɛtərəʊˈfɪlə / (BrE)
A Chilean shrub that produces coral-red fruit with an edible seed resembling a hazelnut.
Chilean shrub with edible hazelnut-like seeds

Examples

The seeds of Guevina heterophylla are prized for their resemblance to hazelnuts.
In southern Chile, Guevina heterophylla shrubs are cultivated for their edible nuts.
Botanists studied Guevina heterophylla because of its unique fruit and adaptability.

Etymology

From the genus name Guevina and species epithet heterophylla, meaning 'different-leaved' in Greek.
