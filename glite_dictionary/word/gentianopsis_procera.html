Gentianopsis procera

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛn.tʃi.əˈnɑp.sɪs prəˈsɪr.ə / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn.tʃi.əˈnɒp.sɪs prəˈsɪə.rə / (BrE)
A species of small, blue-flowered fringed gentian native to east central North America.
a North American blue fringed gentian

Examples

The rare Gentianopsis procera blooms in moist meadows each autumn.
Botanists were excited to discover a cluster of Gentianopsis procera during their field study.
The delicate, fringed petals of Gentianopsis procera make it a striking wildflower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fringed gentian - a common name for several plants of the genus Gentianopsis with fringed petals

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name Gentianopsis (from Gentiana + -opsis, meaning 'resembling gentian'); species name from Latin 'procera' ('tall, slender').
