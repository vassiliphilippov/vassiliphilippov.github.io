Torreya taxifolia

1. NOUN

/ tɔːˈreɪ.ə ˌtæksɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / tɒˈreɪ.ə ˌtæksɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A rare small evergreen tree native to northern Florida, noted for its glossy green leaves that emit an unpleasant smell when crushed.
rare evergreen tree of northern Florida

Examples

The Torreya taxifolia is now considered one of the most endangered trees in North America.
Botanists study the unique odor produced when the leaves of Torreya taxifolia are crushed.
Conservationists are actively working to save Torreya taxifolia from extinction in its native habitat.

Etymology

From the genus name Torreya, after American botanist John Torrey, and Latin taxifolia meaning 'yew-leaved'.
