purposeful

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɜːrpəsfəl / (AmE), / ˈpɜːpəsfʊl / (BrE)
having or showing determination or a clear aim; serving or indicating the existence of a specific purpose or goal.
having a clear aim or goal

Examples

She walked into the meeting with a purposeful stride, ready to present her ideas.
His purposeful approach to studying helped him earn top grades in his class.
The changes to the layout were purposeful and made the website easier to navigate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intentional - done on purpose; deliberate
  • Antonym: aimless - without purpose or direction

Etymology

From 'purpose' + '-ful'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɝːpəsfəl / (AmE), / ˈpɜːpəsfəl / (BrE)
Having an aim or intention; showing determination or resolve.
having an aim or intention

Examples

She walked into the meeting with a purposeful stride, ready to present her ideas.
Living a purposeful life can bring greater satisfaction and fulfillment.
His purposeful actions showed that he was committed to achieving his goals.

Synset Examples

led a happy purposeful life

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aimless - lacking purpose or direction
  • Synonym: intentional - done with intention or on purpose

Etymology

From 'purpose' + '-ful', first attested in the 16th century.
