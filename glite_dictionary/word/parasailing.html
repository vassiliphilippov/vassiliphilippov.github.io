parasailing

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛrəˌseɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpærəseɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
The activity of being towed behind a boat while attached to a specially designed parachute, allowing one to glide through the air above the water.
being towed in a parachute behind a boat

Examples

We tried parasailing for the first time on our vacation in Hawaii.
Many tourists enjoy parasailing along the coast during the summer months.
He watched as colorful parasailing parachutes floated high above the sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paragliding - the sport of gliding with a specially designed parachute launched from a height

Etymology

From 'parachute' + 'sailing', referring to sailing in the air with a parachute.
