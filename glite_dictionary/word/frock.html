frock

1. NOUN

/ frɑk / (AmE), / frɒk / (BrE)
a woman's or girl's dress, typically a one-piece garment with a skirt and bodice
woman's dress; one-piece garment

Examples

She wore a lovely summer frock to the garden party.
The little girl twirled in her new pink frock.
Vintage fashion enthusiasts often seek out a classic 1950s frock for special occasions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dress - a one-piece garment for a woman or girl

Etymology

From Old French *froc* (a monk's habit), from Medieval Latin *frocka*.

2. NOUN

/ frɑk / (AmE), / frɒk / (BrE)
a long, loose garment, especially the habit worn by clergy or monks
clerical or monastic garment

Examples

The priest donned his frock before the ceremony.
Monks in the abbey are easily recognized by their traditional frock.
He dedicated his life to the church and wore the frock with pride.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: habit - a distinctive set of religious clothing, especially for monks and nuns

Etymology

From Old French 'froc' (a monk's habit, robe).

3. NOUN

/ frɑk / (AmE), / frɒk / (BrE)
a long, loose outer garment, typically worn by women or children, or by members of certain religious orders
long, loose outer garment

Examples

She wore a beautiful floral frock to the summer party.
The children in the choir were dressed in simple white frocks.
The nun adjusted her black frock before entering the chapel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dress - a one-piece garment for a woman or girl

Etymology

From Old French *froc* (a monk's habit) and Medieval Latin *frocus*, referring to a cloak or garment.
