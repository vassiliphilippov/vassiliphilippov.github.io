provender

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːvəndər / (AmE), / ˈprɒvəndə / (BrE)
food supplies, especially dry food for livestock; fodder or provisions
stock or supply of food, especially for animals

Examples

The farmer stored enough provender in the barn to last through the winter.
During their journey, they carried provender for the horses and themselves.
The innkeeper prepared fresh provender for the weary travelers and their animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fodder - coarse food for cattle, horses, etc., such as hay or straw
  • Synonym: provisions - supplies of food and other essentials

Etymology

From Old French *provendre* (allowance, provisions), from Latin *praebenda* (things to be supplied).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːvəndər / (AmE), / ˈprɒvəndə / (BrE)
food for domestic animals, especially livestock; fodder or feed.
food for domestic animals or livestock

Examples

The farmer stored hay and provender in the barn for the horses.
Carrying sacks of provender, the workers fed the cattle twice a day.
They needed a steady supply of provender to keep the livestock healthy through winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fodder - coarse food for livestock, such as hay or straw
  • Synonym: forage - food for animals, especially food taken by browsing or grazing

Etymology

From Old French provendre, from Latin praebenda (things to be provided).
