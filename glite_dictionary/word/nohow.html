nohow

1. ADV

/ ˈnoʊˌhaʊ / (AmE), / ˈnəʊˌhaʊ / (BrE)
in no way; not at all
not at all; in no way
archaic

Examples

Despite our best efforts, we could nohow convince him to change his mind.
The message was so garbled that I could nohow understand its meaning.
She nohow managed to finish the assignment on time, no matter how hard she tried.

Synset Examples

We could nohow make out his handwriting

Related Terms

  • Antonym: somehow - in some way; by some means
  • Antonym: anyhow - in any way whatsoever

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'somehow' and 'anyhow', from 'no' + 'how'.
