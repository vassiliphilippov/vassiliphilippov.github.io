Irenidae

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈrɛnɪdiː / (AmE), / aɪˈrɛnɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of birds of the suborder Oscines, including the leafbirds, ioras, and fairy bluebirds
family of songbirds (leafbirds, ioras, fairy bluebirds)

Examples

The Irenidae family includes colorful birds such as the fairy bluebird found in Asia.
Ornithologists study the vocalizations of various species within the Irenidae.
Habitat loss threatens several members of the Irenidae in Southeast Asia.

Etymology

From New Latin Irenidae, based on the type genus *Irena*.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈrɛn.ɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛ.nɪ.diː / (BrE)
A family of small passerine birds in the suborder Oscines, including the leafbirds, ioras, and fairy bluebirds, found in southern Asia.
family of passerine birds (leafbirds, ioras, fairy bluebirds)

Examples

The Irenidae are brightly colored birds native to the forests of Southeast Asia.
Taxonomists recently reviewed the classification of the Irenidae based on genetic evidence.
Leafbirds and fairy bluebirds are both members of the Irenidae family.

Etymology

From Modern Latin 'Irena' (type genus) + '-idae' (zoological family suffix).
