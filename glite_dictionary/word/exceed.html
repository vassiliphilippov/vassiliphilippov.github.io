to exceed

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈsid / (AmE), / ɪkˈsiːd / (BrE)
to be greater or better than a particular amount, standard, or expectation
be greater or better than a standard

Examples

The athlete managed to exceed last season's record.
Sales have exceeded all initial projections this quarter.
Your dedication and hard work exceed what we typically expect from employees.

Synset Examples

She exceeded our expectations
She topped her performance of last year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to surpass - to do or be better than someone or something
  • Antonym: to fall short - to not reach the expected standard or level

Etymology

From Latin 'excedere', from 'ex-' (out) + 'cedere' (to go).

2. VERB

/ ɪkˈsiːd / (AmE), / ɪkˈsiːd / (BrE)
to be greater in number, amount, or degree than something; to go beyond a set limit or standard
to go beyond a limit or standard

Examples

The cost of the repairs may exceed your initial budget.
Her performance consistently exceeds expectations at work.
The river levels exceeded the flood warning threshold after heavy rainfall.

Synset Examples

Their loyalty exceeds their national bonds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to surpass - to be better or greater than
  • Antonym: to fall short - to fail to reach a standard or expected level

Etymology

From Latin 'excedere' (to go out, surpass), from 'ex-' (out) + 'cedere' (go).

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪkˈsiːd / (AmE), / ɪkˈsiːd / (BrE)
to be greater than a particular amount, degree, or limit; to go beyond what is allowed or expected
to be greater than a set limit or amount

Examples

The cost of repairs will exceed the original estimate.
She managed to exceed everyone's expectations with her outstanding performance.
Do not exceed the recommended dosage on the label.

Synset Examples

her performance surpasses that of any other student I know
She outdoes all other athletes
This exceeds all my expectations
This car outperforms all others in its class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to surpass - to do or be better than someone or something
  • Synonym: to outdo - to do better than someone
  • Antonym: to fall short - to not reach the required amount or level

Etymology

From Latin 'excedere' meaning 'to go out or beyond', from 'ex-' (out) + 'cedere' (to go).

Synset POS: v
