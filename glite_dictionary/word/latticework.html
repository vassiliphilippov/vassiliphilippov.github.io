latticework

1. NOUN

/ ˈlætɪsˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈlætɪswɜːk / (BrE)
a framework made from strips of wood or metal arranged in a criss-cross or square pattern, used as a decorative or supporting element
ornamental frame of crossed strips

Examples

The rosebush clings to the latticework along the garden fence.
Sunlight streamed through the latticework, casting patterned shadows on the floor.
The balcony was enclosed with an intricate latticework of wrought iron.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trellis - a framework of light wooden or metal bars, chiefly used as a support for fruit trees or climbing plants

Etymology

From 'lattice' (from Old French 'lattis') + 'work'.
