Pogostemon cablin

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈgoʊstəˌmɒn ˈkæblɪn / (AmE), / pəˈɡɒstɪˌmɒn ˈkab.lɪn / (BrE)
A small, East Indian shrubby mint whose fragrant leaves produce oil used in perfumes, also known as patchouli.
aromatic shrub; source of patchouli oil

Examples

The essential oil of Pogostemon cablin is widely used in the fragrance industry.
Perfume makers highly value Pogostemon cablin for its distinct, earthy aroma.
Pogostemon cablin thrives in warm, tropical climates and is cultivated for its aromatic leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patchouli - common name for Pogostemon cablin, especially in context of the oil

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus name 'Pogostemon' and species name 'cablin', referring to its botanical classification.
