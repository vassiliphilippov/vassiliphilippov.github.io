depilation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛpəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːpɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or condition of removal or loss of hair, especially from the body or face
removal or loss of hair
medical

Examples

Laser treatment can lead to permanent depilation of unwanted hair.
Some creams are formulated for painless depilation of sensitive areas.
The depilation process may leave the skin smooth and hair-free for weeks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epilation - the removal of hair, especially by pulling it from the roots

Etymology

From Latin depilatio, from de- (removal) + pilus (hair).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛpɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːpɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the removal of hair from the body or skin, especially by chemical or mechanical means
removal of hair from skin

Examples

Laser depilation is a popular method for achieving smooth, hair-free skin.
Some ancient cultures practiced depilation as part of ritual cleansing.
Chemical creams for depilation can cause irritation for people with sensitive skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epilation - removal of hair by the roots

Etymology

From Latin depilare (to remove hair), from de- (removal) + pilus (hair).
