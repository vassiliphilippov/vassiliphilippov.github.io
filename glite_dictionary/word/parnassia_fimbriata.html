Parnassia fimbriata

1. NOUN

/ pɑːrˈnæsiə fɪmˈbraɪətə / (AmE), / pɑːˈnasɪə fɪmˈbrɪətə / (BrE)
A bog plant native to western North America, with heart-shaped basal leaves and cream or white, fringed, saucer-shaped flowers.
a North American bog plant with fringed white flowers

Examples

A cluster of Parnassia fimbriata bloomed in the damp meadow near the stream.
Botanists often encounter Parnassia fimbriata during fieldwork in alpine bog areas.
The fringed petals of Parnassia fimbriata distinguish it from other wildflowers in the region.

Etymology

From Latin: Parnassia (a plant genus), from Mount Parnassus in Greece, and Latin fimbriata meaning 'fringed'.
