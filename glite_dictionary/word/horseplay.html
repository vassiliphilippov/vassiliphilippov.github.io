horseplay

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrsˌpleɪ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsˌpleɪ / (BrE)
rough, energetic, or boisterous play, usually intended for fun but lacking restraint
rough or boisterous play

Examples

The teachers warned the students that horseplay in the hallway could lead to injuries.
There was a lot of laughter and horseplay at the summer camp.
Please stop the horseplay before someone gets hurt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roughhousing - rowdy playing, often physical
  • Degree Lower: frolic - playful or lively behavior
  • Degree Higher: violence - extreme physical force intended to hurt

Etymology

From 'horse' (representing strength or unruliness) + 'play'; first attested in the early 16th century.
