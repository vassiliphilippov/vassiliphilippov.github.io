to coddle

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑd.əl / (AmE), / ˈkɒd.əl / (BrE)
to treat someone with excessive care or indulgence, often to the point of spoiling them
treat with excessive indulgence

Examples

You shouldn't coddle your children if you want them to become independent.
He was coddled by his parents all through his childhood.
Some managers believe that too much praise coddles employees and reduces their motivation.

Synset Examples

grandparents often pamper the children
Let's not mollycoddle our students!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pamper - to treat with excessive care or attention
  • Synonym: mollycoddle - to treat someone in an overprotective way

Etymology

Possibly from obsolete verb 'caudle' (to warm, to nurse), from Middle English 'caudle' (a warm drink), from Old North French 'caudel'.

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑː.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkɒd.l̩ / (BrE)
to cook food, especially eggs, gently in water just below the boiling point
cook food in water just below boiling

Examples

To make the perfect breakfast, she likes to coddle her eggs rather than boiling them.
If you coddle tomatoes, their skins will slip off easily for sauce making.
Many chefs prefer to coddle eggs because the whites stay delicate and tender.

Synset Examples

coddle eggs

Etymology

Perhaps from dialectal English 'caudle', influenced by 'caudle' (a warm drink), ultimately from Latin 'calidum' (warm).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
