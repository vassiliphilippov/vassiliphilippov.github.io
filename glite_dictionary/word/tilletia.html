Tilletia

1. NOUN

/ tɪˈlɛtiə / (AmE), / tɪˈlɛtɪə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Tilletiaceae, some species of which are known to cause diseases in cereal crops.
genus of parasitic fungi

Examples

The wheat crop was infected with Tilletia, resulting in significant yield loss.
Scientists are studying the life cycle of Tilletia to develop better crop protection methods.
Several species within the genus Tilletia are responsible for bunt diseases in cereals.

Etymology

Named after French botanist Mathieu Tillet (1714–1791).

2. NOUN

/ tɪˈliːtiə / (AmE), / tɪˈliːtiə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Tilletiaceae, known for causing smut diseases in grasses, especially wheat and other cereals.
genus of smut fungi affecting cereals

Examples

Several species within Tilletia are notorious for damaging wheat crops worldwide.
Microscopic examination revealed spores typical of Tilletia in the infected sample.
Researchers are studying the life cycle of Tilletia to improve fungus control methods.

Etymology

Named after Mathieu Tillet, an 18th-century French botanist.
