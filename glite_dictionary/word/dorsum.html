dorsum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔɹ.səm / (AmE), / ˈdɔː.səm / (BrE)
the back or upper surface of the body of a vertebrate, or the corresponding surface of an organ, appendage, or part
back or upper surface of body or organ
medical

Examples

The dorsum of the tongue is covered with papillae.
When studying frogs, you can easily see the coloration pattern along the dorsum.
Swelling was observed on the dorsum of her left hand.

Synset Examples

the dorsum of the foot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: back - the rear surface of the human body from the shoulders to the hips

Etymology

From Latin 'dorsum' (back).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɔrsəm / (AmE), / ˈdɔːsəm / (BrE)
the back or upper surface of an animal or human, especially from the neck to the end of the spine
the back of a body (anatomical)
medical

Examples

The surgeon made an incision along the dorsum to access the spine.
This species of frog is easily recognized by the distinctive stripes on its dorsum.
The therapist applied heat packs to the patient's dorsum to relieve muscle tension.

Synset Examples

his back was nicely tanned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: back - the rear surface of the human body, from the shoulders to the hips

Etymology

From Latin dorsum (back, ridge).
