Saturniidae

1. NOUN

/ səˈtɜrniˌaɪdi / (AmE), / səˈtɜːniˌaɪdiː / (BrE)
a large family of moths that includes some of the largest known species, widely distributed and important in entomology
family of large moths, Saturniidae

Examples

The Saturniidae family contains some of the world's largest and most colorful moths.
Biologists discovered a new species within the Saturniidae during their expedition.
Many members of the Saturniidae have impressive eye spots on their wings to deter predators.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Saturnia, a genus of moths named after the Roman god Saturn.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsætərˈnaɪɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌsatəˈnɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of moths, widely distributed, including some of the largest known species, such as emperor and luna moths.
family of large moths

Examples

The Saturniidae include the impressive giant silkworm and emperor moths found in many parts of the world.
Biologists studied the unique wing patterns of several Saturniidae species collected during the expedition.
Some moths in the Saturniidae reach up to 15 centimeters in wingspan, making them among the largest insects.

Etymology

From New Latin Saturniidae, from Saturnia (type genus) + -idae (suffix for animal families).
