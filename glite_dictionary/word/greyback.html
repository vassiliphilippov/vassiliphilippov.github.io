greyback

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪˌbæk / (BrE)
a type of sandpiper that breeds in the Arctic and migrates to the Southern Hemisphere for the winter.
a migratory Arctic sandpiper

Examples

Many ornithologists travel north to observe the annual migration of the greyback.
Flocks of greybacks are often seen along coastal estuaries during the winter months.
The greyback is known for its long migratory journey between polar and temperate regions.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪˌbak / (BrE)
(historical, US) A nickname for a Confederate soldier during the American Civil War, derived from their grey uniforms.
Confederate soldier (historical US)
american

Examples

The Union lines were soon attacked by a group of greybacks moving through the woods.
Stories of the bravery of the greybacks were told for generations in the South.
A lone greyback was captured and brought into the Union camp for questioning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Johnny Reb - A typical Confederate soldier in the American Civil War
  • Antonym: Yankee - A Union soldier in the American Civil War

Etymology

From 'grey' (the color of Confederate uniforms) + 'back'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪˌbak / (BrE)
A dowitcher, especially one with a grey back, commonly found in North America.
a dowitcher with a grey back (bird)

Examples

During migration, many greybacks gather along the mudflats to feed.
The greyback can be distinguished from other shorebirds by its slate-colored feathers.
Ornithologists spotted a flock of greybacks near the coastal marsh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dowitcher - a wading bird of the genus Limnodromus
