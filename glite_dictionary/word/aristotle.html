Aristotle

1. PROPN

/ ˈærɪˌstɑtl̩ / (AmE), / ˈærɪstɒtl̩ / (BrE)
(384–322 BC) One of the greatest ancient Greek philosophers, pupil of Plato and teacher of Alexander the Great, known for his contributions to philosophy, science, and logic.
ancient Greek philosopher (384–322 BC)

Examples

Aristotle wrote extensively on subjects ranging from politics to metaphysics.
The teachings of Aristotle have influenced Western thought for centuries.
Students discussed Aristotle's ideas on ethics in philosophy class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Plato - Greek philosopher, teacher of Aristotle
  • Synonym: Socrates - Ancient Greek philosopher before Plato and Aristotle

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἀριστοτέλης (Aristotélēs).
