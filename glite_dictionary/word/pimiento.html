pimiento

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈmjɛntoʊ / (AmE), / pɪˈmiːəntəʊ / (BrE)
A fully ripened sweet red pepper, especially one that is typically used cooked or for stuffing green olives.
sweet red pepper, often used cooked

Examples

She stuffed the green olives with pimiento for the salad.
The chef added chopped pimiento to the creamy cheese spread.
A jar of pimiento peppers sat on the top shelf in the pantry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pepper - the fruit of plants of the genus Capsicum, used as a vegetable or spice

Etymology

From Spanish 'pimiento' meaning 'pepper'.

2. NOUN

/ pɪˈmjɛntoʊ / (AmE), / pɪˈmiːɛntəʊ / (BrE)
A sweet pepper with thick, mild, usually red flesh, typically used in salads or as a stuffing in olives.
a mild, sweet red pepper

Examples

She added chopped pimiento to the salad for color and flavor.
A single green olive, stuffed with pimiento, garnished his martini.
Farmers in Spain cultivate fields of pimiento for both domestic use and export.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bell pepper - a type of large, mild pepper with a bell shape
  • Antonym: chili pepper - a small, hot-tasting variety of pepper

Etymology

From Spanish 'pimiento' (pepper), from Latin 'pigmentum' (pigment, spice).
