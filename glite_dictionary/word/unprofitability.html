unprofitability

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnˌprɑːfɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌprɒfɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the state or condition of not making a profit or not yielding benefit or gain
lack of profitability

Examples

The company decided to close the store due to long-term unprofitability.
Investors are concerned about the continued unprofitability of the new product line.
High operating costs contributed to the unprofitability of the project.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: profitability - the state of yielding profit or financial gain

Etymology

From un- (not) + profitability.
