rajanya

1. NOUN

/ rɑːˈdʒɑːnjə / (AmE), / rɑːˈdʒɑːnjə / (BrE)
(in Hinduism) a member of the noble or warrior class, traditionally regarded as the second highest of the four varnas.
member of Hindu warrior/noble caste

Examples

In ancient India, members of the rajanya had important roles as warriors and rulers.
The social order placed the rajanya below the brahmins but above the vaishyas and shudras.
Historical texts often mention the rajanya as defenders of the kingdom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kshatriya - member of the warrior or ruling caste in Hindu society

Etymology

From Sanskrit राजन्य (rājanya), meaning 'royal, noble'.
