immunology

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪmjəˈnɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌɪmjʊˈnɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of medical science that studies the immune system and its functions and disorders
study of the immune system
medical

Examples

She decided to specialize in immunology after becoming fascinated by how the body fights disease.
Recent advances in immunology have led to new treatments for autoimmune disorders.
His groundbreaking research in immunology earned him an international award.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immunity - the state of being protected against disease
  • Degree Lower: allergy - a damaging immune response to a substance
  • Degree Higher: autoimmunity - immune response against one's own tissues

Etymology

From Latin 'immunis' (free, exempt) + Greek '-logia' (study of).
