summary

1. ADJ

/ ˈsʌm.ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈsʌm.əri / (BrE)
(of an action or decision) done quickly, without the usual formal procedures, often in response to urgent circumstances.
done without usual formalities

Examples

The judge ordered a summary hearing because the facts were clear and undisputed.
He faced summary dismissal after violating the company's code of conduct.
The protesters expressed concern over reports of summary executions in the region.

Synset Examples

a summary execution
summary justice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expedited - done quickly and efficiently
  • Antonym: deliberate - done with careful thought or planning

Etymology

From Latin 'summarius' (of or pertaining to the sum or substance).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01637923-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌməˌri / (AmE), / ˈsʌməri / (BrE)
a brief statement or account giving the main points of something, without details
concise account of main points

Examples

She provided a summary of the research findings at the end of her presentation.
The back cover of the book gives a brief summary of the plot.
After the meeting, he sent everyone a summary of the key decisions made.

Synset Examples

he gave a summary of the conclusions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abstract - a summary of the contents of a book, article, or formal speech
  • Synonym: outline - a general description or plan that gives the main ideas

Etymology

From Latin 'summarium' (summary, sum, total), from 'summarius' (of or pertaining to the sum or total).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06478678-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈsʌm.ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈsʌm.əri / (BrE)
Giving only the main points or basic information, stated briefly and clearly.
giving the main points briefly

Examples

The report gave a summary account of the year's main achievements.
She offered a summary explanation of the findings in the introduction.
In the meeting, he made a summary statement to keep things moving.

Synset Examples

a short and compendious book
a compact style is brief and pithy
succinct comparisons
a summary formulation of a wide-ranging subject

Related Terms

  • Synonym: succinct - brief and clearly expressed
  • Antonym: detailed - giving a lot of information with many details

Etymology

From Latin 'summarium' (summary, gist), from 'summarius' (of the sum or chief points).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
