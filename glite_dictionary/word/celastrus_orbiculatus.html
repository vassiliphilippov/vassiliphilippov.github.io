Celastrus orbiculatus

1. NOUN

/ sɛˈlæstrəs ɔːrˌbɪkjəˈleɪtəs / (AmE), / sɪˈlæstrəs ɔːˌbɪkjʊˈleɪtəs / (BrE)
A species of ornamental vine native to Asia, with yellow-orange fruits and a scarlet aril, commonly known as Oriental bittersweet.
Asian ornamental vine with showy orange-yellow fruit

Examples

Gardeners sometimes plant Celastrus orbiculatus for its attractive berries and foliage.
Celastrus orbiculatus has become invasive in parts of North America, outcompeting native species.
The bright fruit of Celastrus orbiculatus makes it popular in decorative wreaths and floral arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Oriental bittersweet - common English name for Celastrus orbiculatus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Celastrus', the genus name, and 'orbiculatus' meaning rounded, referring to the shape of the leaves.
