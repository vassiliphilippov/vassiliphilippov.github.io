gibberish

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪb.ər.ɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪb.ər.ɪʃ / (BrE)
speech or writing that is meaningless or cannot be understood because it is nonsensical or unintelligible.
unintelligible or nonsensical speech or writing

Examples

The instructions were written in such gibberish that no one could follow them.
After the dentist numbed his mouth, everything he said sounded like gibberish.
Amid the chaos, a child was shouting what seemed to be gibberish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - speech or writing that has no meaning or makes no sense
  • Synonym: babble - continuous, foolish, or incomprehensible talk
  • Antonym: sense - coherent or meaningful speech or writing

Etymology

Perhaps imitative in origin or derived from 'gibber', meaning to speak rapidly and unintelligibly.
