wideness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being wide; unusual largeness in size, extent, or number
the state of being wide

Examples

The wideness of the river made it difficult to build a bridge across it.
She was impressed by the wideness of his knowledge on so many topics.
From the airplane, the wideness of the countryside was truly breathtaking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: width - the measurement or extent of something from side to side
  • Antonym: narrowness - the quality of being narrow; opposite of wideness

Etymology

Formed from the adjective 'wide' with the noun suffix '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪdnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being wide; great breadth or extent from side to side.
the quality of being wide

Examples

The wideness of the river made it difficult to build a bridge across.
She admired the wideness of the avenue, which let in plenty of sunlight.
The wideness of his smile made everyone feel welcome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: width - the measurement or extent of something from side to side
  • Antonym: narrowness - the quality of being narrow

Etymology

From 'wide' + '-ness'.
