to misconstrue

1. VERB

/ ˌmɪskənˈstru / (AmE), / ˌmɪskənˈstruː / (BrE)
to interpret (something, such as words or actions) wrongly; to misunderstand the meaning or intention of
interpret wrongly; misunderstand

Examples

It's easy to misconstrue someone's tone in an email.
She misconstrued his silence as an indication of displeasure.
They warned that the data could be misconstrued without proper context.

Synset Examples

Don't misinterpret my comments as criticism
She misconstrued my remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to misinterpret - to understand or explain something incorrectly
  • Antonym: to interpret - to explain or understand the meaning of something

Etymology

From Old French *mesconstruire* (to interpret wrongly), from Latin *mis-* (wrongly) + *construere* (to construct, build).
