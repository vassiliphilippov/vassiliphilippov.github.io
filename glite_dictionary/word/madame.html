madame

1. NOUN

/ mæˈdæm / (AmE), / maˈdɑːm / (BrE)
A title or form of address for a married woman in French-speaking contexts, equivalent to 'Mrs.'
French title for a married woman

Examples

The concierge addressed her as madame when she entered the hotel lobby.
The letter was neatly written and began with 'Chère madame', following French custom.
At the French restaurant, the waiter politely greeted her, saying, 'Madame, your table is ready.'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: madam - Formal title for a woman, especially in English-speaking contexts

Etymology

From French 'madame', meaning 'my lady', via Old French from 'ma dame'.
