Coryphaenidae

1. NOUN

/ kɔːrɪˈfeɪnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / kɒrɪˈfeɪnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, active, pelagic fish in warm seas, including the dolphinfish (mahi-mahi).
family of pelagic fish (e.g., dolphinfish)
medical

Examples

The Coryphaenidae family includes the popular game fish known as mahi-mahi.
Marine biologists study migration patterns of the Coryphaenidae in tropical waters.
Members of Coryphaenidae are prized for their speed and striking coloration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Coryphaena - the type genus of Coryphaenidae, commonly known as dolphinfish

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Coryphaena + suffix -idae (denoting family).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːrɪfəˈniːɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌkɒrɪfəˈniːɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, active, pelagic percoid fish, including species such as the dolphinfish (mahi-mahi).
family of large pelagic fish (dolphinfish)
medical

Examples

The Coryphaenidae are known for their bright colors and acrobatic leaps.
Fishermen often seek members of the Coryphaenidae family for sport and food.
One of the most famous Coryphaenidae is the dolphinfish, prized in tropical waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dolphinfish - common name for species within Coryphaenidae
  • Synonym: mahi-mahi - popular name for Coryphaena hippurus, a member of Coryphaenidae

Etymology

From New Latin Coryphaenidae, named after the type genus Coryphaena.
