Callitriche

1. NOUN

/ ˌkælɪˈtraɪki / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈtrɪki / (BrE)
A genus of small aquatic plants, commonly known as water starworts, found in freshwater habitats worldwide.
genus of aquatic plants (water starworts)

Examples

Botanists discovered a new species within the genus Callitriche in the wetland reserve.
Callitriche thrives in slow-moving streams and ponds.
The delicate floating leaves of Callitriche make it a popular choice for natural aquariums.

Etymology

From Greek kallos (beauty) + thrix (hair), referring to the delicate appearance of the plants.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪtrɪki / (AmE), / kəˈlɪtrɪki / (BrE)
A genus of small aquatic flowering plants commonly known as water starworts, found in still or slow-moving fresh water.
a genus of aquatic plants (water starworts)

Examples

Several species of Callitriche can be found in ponds across Europe and North America.
The botanist collected samples of Callitriche growing along the riverbank for her research.
Aquariums often use Callitriche as a decorative aquatic plant due to its delicate appearance.

Etymology

From Greek kallitrix, kallitrikhos, meaning 'with beautiful hair,' referring to the plant's delicate leaves.
