pappoose

1. NOUN

/ pəˈpus / (AmE), / pəˈpuːs / (BrE)
a Native American infant or young child, especially as depicted in historical or cultural contexts
Native American infant or young child
archaic

Examples

The tribe welcomed the arrival of a new pappoose into the community.
She carried her sleeping pappoose wrapped securely in a cradleboard.
Old photographs showed a native woman with her pappoose on her back.

Etymology

From Narragansett or related Algonquian languages, meaning 'child'.
