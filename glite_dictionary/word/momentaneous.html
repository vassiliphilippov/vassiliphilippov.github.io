momentaneous

1. ADJ

/ ˌmoʊmənˈteɪniəs / (AmE), / ˌməʊmənˈteɪniəs / (BrE)
lasting for a very brief period of time; fleeting or momentary.
lasting for a very short time
archaic

Examples

Their happiness was momentaneous, vanishing almost as soon as it appeared.
The painter captured a momentaneous expression of surprise on the child's face.
Such momentaneous changes in weather are difficult to predict.

Synset Examples

a fleeting glance
fugitive hours
rapid momentaneous association of things that meet and pass
a momentary glimpse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: momentary - lasting for a very short time
  • Synonym: fleeting - passing quickly; lasting only a short time

Etymology

From Latin 'momentaneus' (lasting only a moment).
