Cheviot

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛviət / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛviət / (BrE)
a hardy breed of hornless sheep from the Cheviot Hills, known for its short, thick wool
breed of sheep from the Cheviot Hills

Examples

The Cheviot is valued for its ability to thrive in rough upland conditions.
Farmers often choose Cheviots for their dense wool and resilience.
The Cheviot breed originated near the border between England and Scotland.

Etymology

Named after the Cheviot Hills, a range of hills on the border between England and Scotland.
