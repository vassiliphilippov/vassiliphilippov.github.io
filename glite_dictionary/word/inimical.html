inimical

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈnɪmɪkəl / (AmE), / ɪˈnɪmɪkəl / (BrE)
Unfriendly; hostile or harmful in effect.
unfriendly or hostile

Examples

His comments were so inimical that they ruined the atmosphere of the meeting.
Certain environmental policies can be inimical to economic growth.
She regarded the new regulations as inimical to the public interest.

Synset Examples

an unfriendly act of aggression
an inimical critic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hostile - very unfriendly or antagonistic
  • Antonym: friendly - kind and pleasant; opposite of hostile

Etymology

From Latin inimicus (enemy), from in- (not) + amicus (friend).
