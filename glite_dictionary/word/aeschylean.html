Aeschylean

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛs.kɪˈliː.ən / (AmE), / ˌiːs.kɪˈliː.ən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of Aeschylus or his works, especially in style or themes of ancient Greek tragedy.
of or relating to Aeschylus or his works

Examples

The play's structure was distinctly Aeschylean, reflecting the grandeur of ancient Greek tragedy.
Her writing style has an Aeschylean quality, focusing on deep moral conflicts.
Critics praised the Aeschylean themes of fate and justice in the new production.

Etymology

From the name of Aeschylus (c. 525–456 BC), ancient Greek tragedian, plus the adjectival suffix -an.
