Cryptophyceae

1. NOUN

/ krɪp.toʊˈfaɪ.siː.i / (AmE), / krɪp.təˈfʌɪ.siː.iː / (BrE)
a class of motile, usually brownish-green, protozoa-like algae found mostly in marine and freshwater environments
a class of brownish-green algae

Examples

Some freshwater lakes contain abundant Cryptophyceae that contribute to the ecosystem's primary production.
Researchers are studying how Cryptophyceae adapt to changing light conditions.
The diversity of Cryptophyceae species can impact water quality significantly.

Etymology

From Greek 'kryptos' (hidden) + 'phyke' (seaweed), referencing the hidden nature of some of their cellular structures.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪptoʊˈfaɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəʊˈfʌɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a class of mostly brownish-green, motile, protozoa-like algae found in freshwater and marine environments
a class of brownish-green motile algae

Examples

Certain lakes are dominated by Cryptophyceae during the spring plankton bloom.
Researchers observed that Cryptophyceae contribute significantly to aquatic food webs.
Some species in the class Cryptophyceae exhibit remarkable adaptability to low-light environments.

Etymology

From Greek 'kryptos' (hidden) + 'phyceae' (algae, from 'phykos' for seaweed).
