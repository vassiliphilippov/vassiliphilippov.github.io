hypotonicity

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪ.poʊ.tɑːˈnɪs.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.pəʊ.tɒˈnɪs.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being hypotonic; the condition in which a solution has a lower osmotic pressure compared to another solution
the state of having lower osmotic pressure
medical

Examples

The hypotonicity of the solution caused water to move into the cells, leading to swelling.
Dialysis fluids are carefully adjusted to control their hypotonicity relative to blood plasma.
You must understand hypotonicity when preparing intravenous fluids for patients.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypertonicity - the state of having higher osmotic pressure compared to another solution
  • Antonym: isotonicity - the state of having equal osmotic pressure compared to another solution

Etymology

From hypo- (under, less) + tonic + -ity, modeled after hypotonic.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpoʊtəˈnɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəʊtəˈnɪsəti / (BrE)
the state or quality of having reduced muscle tone or tension, especially in muscular tissue
reduced muscle tone or tension
medical

Examples

The neurologist noted that the infant exhibited significant hypotonicity in her lower limbs.
Physical therapy can help improve hypotonicity in children with developmental delays.
Babies with Down syndrome often display hypotonicity, making their muscles feel unusually loose.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypertonicity - the state of having increased muscle tone
  • Synonym: flaccidity - the state of being soft or limp, lacking firmness

Etymology

From hypo- (under, less) + tonicity (state of tension), ultimately from Greek tonos (tone).
