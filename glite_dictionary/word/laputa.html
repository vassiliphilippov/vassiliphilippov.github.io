Laputa

1. PROPN

/ ləˈpjuːtə / (AmE), / ləˈpjuːtə / (BrE)
A fictional floating island in Jonathan Swift's 'Gulliver's Travels', known for its inhabitants' obsession with impractical theoretical projects and neglect of practical matters.
fictional island from 'Gulliver's Travels'

Examples

Swift's satirical description of Laputa highlights the folly of intellectual pursuits disconnected from reality.
Many literary critics see Laputa as a symbol of impractical scientific experimentation.
The king of Laputa was more interested in mathematics than governing his people.

Etymology

Invented by Jonathan Swift for 'Gulliver's Travels' (1726); sometimes interpreted as based on Spanish 'la puta' (the whore).
