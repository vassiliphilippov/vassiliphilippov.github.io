bursary

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɜːrsəri / (AmE), / ˈbɜːsəri / (BrE)
the treasury or financial office of a public institution or a religious order
financial office or treasury of an institution

Examples

The monastery's bursary managed all financial contributions from the congregation.
Each college has a bursary responsible for maintaining its funds and accounts.
During his tenure, the head of the bursary brought about numerous financial reforms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treasury - a place where money or valuables are kept; the funds of an organization or government

Etymology

From Medieval Latin bursaria (purse, treasury), from Late Latin bursa (purse, pouch).
