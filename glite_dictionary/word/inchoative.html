inchoative

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkoʊətɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈkəʊətɪv / (BrE)
in an initial or beginning stage; just starting to develop or exist
at an initial or beginning stage

Examples

The project is still in its inchoative stages and far from completion.
His ideas, though inchoative, showed promise and originality.
We must nurture these inchoative initiatives to see them flourish.

Synset Examples

inchoative stages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incipient - beginning to come into existence; initial
  • Antonym: mature - fully developed; having reached a final or advanced stage

Etymology

From Late Latin *inchoativus*, from Latin *inchoatus* (begin, start).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkoʊətɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈkəʊətɪv / (BrE)
(Grammar) Referring to or denoting an aspect of a verb that expresses the beginning of an action or state.
grammatical aspect indicating beginning of action

Examples

The verb 'to bloom' can have an inchoative sense when it means 'to begin to bloom'.
Some languages mark the inchoative explicitly in their verb forms.
Linguists analyze inchoative verbs to study how actions are conceptualized as beginning.

Etymology

From Late Latin *inchoativus*, from Latin *inchoare* (to begin).
