trashy

1. ADJ

/ ˈtræʃi / (AmE), / ˈtræʃi / (BrE)
(of things) cheap and tastelessly showy or gaudy; lacking style or good taste.
cheap and gaudy; lacking style or taste

Examples

She thought the decorations looked trashy with all that glitter and fake gold.
He wore a trashy suit that caught everyone's attention for the wrong reasons.
The movie's trashy special effects couldn't hide its lack of substance.

Synset Examples

a flash car
a flashy ring
garish colors
a gaudy costume
loud sport shirts
a meretricious yet stylish book
tawdry ornaments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaudy - showy in a tasteless or vulgar way
  • Antonym: tasteful - having or showing good taste

Etymology

From 'trash' (refuse, waste) + adjective suffix '-y'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtræʃi / (AmE), / ˈtræʃi / (BrE)
cheap, of poor quality, and lacking in value or worth.
cheap and of poor quality

Examples

Many people consider those gossip magazines to be trashy and not worth reading.
She threw away the trashy souvenirs she bought on her trip because they broke so quickly.
The store sells a lot of trashy jewelry that looks nice but falls apart easily.

Synset Examples

rubbishy newspapers that form almost the sole reading of the majority
trashy merchandise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rubbishy - cheap and of poor quality
  • Synonym: shoddy - badly made or poor in quality
  • Antonym: valuable - worth a great deal; of high value

Etymology

From 'trash' (waste, rubbish) + -y (adjective-forming suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02513701-s
Synset POS: s
