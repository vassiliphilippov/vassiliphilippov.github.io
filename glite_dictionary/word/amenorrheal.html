amenorrheal

1. ADJ

/ əˌmɛn.əˈriː.əl / (AmE), / əˌmɛn.əˈriː.əl / (BrE)
Relating to the absence or suppression of normal menstrual periods for reasons other than pregnancy.
absence of menstrual periods (not due to pregnancy)
medical

Examples

Primary ovarian insufficiency can cause an amenorrheal state in young women.
The patient's amenorrheal symptoms were investigated with a series of hormonal tests.
Certain medications may have amenorrheal side effects that should be monitored by your doctor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amenorrheic - pertaining to or affected by amenorrhea

Etymology

From 'amenorrhea' + suffix '-al'; 'amenorrhea' from Greek 'a-' (not) + 'men' (month) + 'rhoia' (flow).
