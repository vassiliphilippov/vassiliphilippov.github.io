Taoist

1. ADJ

/ ˈtaʊɪst / (AmE), / ˈtaʊɪst / (BrE)
relating to Taoism or its philosophy, especially as developed by Lao-tzu and Chuang-tzu, which advocates a simple, honest, and harmonious way of life
relating to Taoism or its philosophy

Examples

Her approach to life is deeply Taoist, emphasizing harmony with nature.
The Taoist philosophy teaches acceptance and going with the flow.
He attends a Taoist meditation retreat every spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Taoism - the philosophical and religious tradition developed by Lao-tzu and Chuang-tzu

Etymology

From Chinese 'Daojia' (Taoist school), from 'dao' (way) + '-ist' (English agent suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtaʊ.ɪst / (AmE), / ˈtaʊ.ɪst / (BrE)
Relating to Taoism, a Chinese religious and philosophical tradition based on the teachings of Lao-tzu, often involving a pantheon of gods, divination, and magic.
relating to Taoism or its followers

Examples

She studied Taoist philosophy during her stay in China.
The temple is decorated in traditional Taoist style.
Many Taoist rituals involve music, incense, and ornate costumes.

Synset Examples

Taoist temples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Confucian - relating to Confucianism, another major Chinese philosophical and religious tradition

Etymology

From Chinese Daojia (道家, meaning 'school of the Way'), filtered through Latinized spelling 'Taoism'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02936968-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊ.ɪst / (AmE), / ˈtaʊ.ɪst / (BrE)
a person who practices or follows Taoism, a Chinese philosophical and religious tradition
an adherent of Taoism

Examples

She became a Taoist after years of studying Eastern philosophy.
Many Taoists practice meditation to achieve harmony with nature.
The Taoist temple was filled with the sound of chanting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Buddhist - an adherent of Buddhism
  • Synonym: Confucian - an adherent of Confucianism

Etymology

From Mandarin 道教 (Dàojiào, Taoism) + -ist (follower of).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
