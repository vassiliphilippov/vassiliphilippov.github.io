rout

1. VERB

/ raʊt / (AmE), / raʊt / (BrE)
(of an animal, especially a pig) to dig or search for food with the snout.
dig with the snout (animal)

Examples

The pig began to rout for truffles in the forest.
Boars often rout through the undergrowth looking for roots and insects.
You could see where the animal had routed around in the dirt.

2. NOUN

/ raʊt / (AmE), / raʊt / (BrE)
a disorderly or tumultuous crowd of people, especially one engaged in unlawful or violent activity
a disorderly crowd; mob
archaic

Examples

The angry rout surged through the town square, shouting and waving banners.
Medieval chronicles often describe a rout breaking into castles during times of unrest.
A rout of peasants gathered outside the city gates, demanding justice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mob - a large crowd of people, especially one that is disorderly or intent on causing trouble
  • Synonym: rabble - a disorderly crowd; a mob
  • Antonym: peace - a state of tranquility or order

Etymology

From Middle English 'rout,' from Old French 'route' (company, troop, throng), from Latin 'rupta' (broken ranks).

3. VERB

/ raʊt / (AmE), / raʊt / (BrE)
to cut a groove, trench, or channel in a surface, especially with a router or similar tool
cut a groove or channel

Examples

The carpenter used a special tool to rout a decorative groove along the edge of the table.
Before laying the wire, the workers needed to rout a channel in the masonry.
The sign's letters were routed into the wooden board for a professional finish.

Etymology

Probably from Old French *rout* (track, path) or related to the noun ‘route’.

4. NOUN

/ raʊt / (AmE), / raʊt / (BrE)
a complete and overwhelming defeat of someone, especially in a contest or battle
overwhelming defeat

Examples

The game ended in a rout, with the home team winning by fifty points.
The army was forced into a rout after their defenses collapsed completely.
Last week's election was a political rout for the opposition party.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: victory - act of defeating an opponent or enemy
  • Synonym: debacle - a sudden and ignominious failure; a fiasco

Etymology

From Middle English 'rout' (a disorderly crowd; defeat), from Old French 'route' (derivative of 'rompre', to break).
