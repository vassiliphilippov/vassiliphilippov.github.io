shoe

1. NOUN

/ ʃu / (AmE), / ʃuː / (BrE)
a device used in card games, especially in casinos, that holds multiple decks of playing cards and allows them to be dealt one at a time
container for dealing playing cards

Examples

The dealer loaded six decks into the shoe before starting the blackjack game.
A new shoe was brought out to ensure the cards were thoroughly shuffled.
Casinos use a shoe to make it harder for players to predict the next card.

2. NOUN

/ ʃu / (AmE), / ʃuː / (BrE)
A covering for the foot, typically made of leather or another sturdy material, with a sole and often a heel, reaching just below the ankle.
a type of footwear covering the foot

Examples

She bought a new pair of shoes for the wedding.
His shoe was untied, so he stopped to fix it.
Please take off your shoes before entering the house.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: boot - footwear covering the foot and ankle, often higher
  • Degree Lower: slipper - soft footwear for indoor use

Etymology

Old English *scōh*, from Proto-Germanic *skōhaz*.

3. NOUN

/ ʃu / (AmE), / ʃuː / (BrE)
A U-shaped metal plate nailed to the underside of a horse's hoof to protect it from wear.
U-shaped plate for horse's hoof

Examples

The blacksmith carefully fit the shoe to the horse's hoof before nailing it in place.
A lost shoe can make a horse limp until it is replaced.
Each shoe is shaped to follow the curvature of the hoof.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horseshoe - A U-shaped metal plate nailed to a horse's hoof

4. NOUN

/ ʃu / (AmE), / ʃuː / (BrE)
a part of a brake system consisting of a curved piece that presses against a drum to slow or stop a vehicle's wheel
part of a brake system

Examples

The mechanic inspected the worn shoe and recommended replacing it to ensure safe braking.
When the shoe presses against the drum, it creates friction to slow the car.
He was surprised by how quickly the brake shoe needed to be replaced after heavy use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brake shoe - the same part of the brake system; synonym for this sense
