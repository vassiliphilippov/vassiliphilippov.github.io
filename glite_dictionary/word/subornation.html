subornation

1. NOUN

/ səˌbɔːrˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / səˌbɔːˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of persuading someone to commit perjury or give false testimony, especially in a legal context
the act of inducing perjury
legal

Examples

The lawyer faced charges of subornation after allegedly persuading a witness to lie on the stand.
He was convicted not only of perjury but also of subornation for orchestrating false testimony.
The scandal involved widespread subornation of witnesses in order to influence the trial's outcome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perjury - the crime of lying under oath

Etymology

From Latin subornatio (incitement, bribery), from subornare (to bribe or instigate secretly).

2. NOUN

/ səˌbɔrnˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / səˌbɔːnˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of secretly or improperly persuading someone to commit an unlawful or dishonest act, especially a crime such as perjury.
inducing someone to do something unlawful
legal

Examples

The lawyer was accused of subornation after allegedly convincing a witness to lie under oath.
Acts of subornation are taken very seriously in court and can result in criminal charges.
There were rumors of subornation among the officials, casting doubt on the integrity of the investigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incitement - the act of urging someone to behave unlawfully

Etymology

From Latin subornatio (encouragement, instigation), from subornare (to secretly furnish or equip).
