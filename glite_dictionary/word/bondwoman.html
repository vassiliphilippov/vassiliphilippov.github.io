bondwoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑndˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈbɒndˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A woman who is bound to servitude without wages; a female slave.
female slave or woman in bondage

Examples

In ancient times, a bondwoman had no legal rights and could be sold by her owner.
The novel tells the tragic story of a bondwoman struggling to find freedom and dignity.
In the household, the bondwoman was responsible for many of the daily chores and lived under strict control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bondman - a male bound to serve without wages; a male slave
  • Synonym: slave - a person who is the legal property of another and forced to work for them
  • Antonym: freeman - a person who is not a slave or in servitude

Etymology

From 'bond' (referring to bondage or servitude) + 'woman'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑndˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈbɒndˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a female slave, especially one bound to service or labor
female slave
archaic

Examples

The novel tells the story of a bondwoman who gains her freedom after many years of hardship.
In ancient societies, a bondwoman had little control over her life or destiny.
The laws provided different rights for a bondwoman compared to a freewoman.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slave - a person who is the legal property of another and is forced to obey them
  • Degree Lower: bondman - a male slave, especially one bound to service or labor
  • Antonym: freewoman - a woman who is not a slave or servant

Etymology

Formed from 'bond' (meaning bound or tied) + 'woman'; analogous to 'bondman'.
