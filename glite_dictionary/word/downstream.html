downstream

1. ADJ

/ ˌdaʊnˈstriːm / (AmE), / ˌdaʊnˈstriːm / (BrE)
in the direction in which a stream or river flows, or situated further along the course of a stream or river from a given point.
in the direction a stream flows

Examples

The pollution spreads downstream from the factory, affecting nearby towns.
Fishermen prefer spots downstream where the water is deeper.
Wildlife was more abundant in the downstream areas of the river.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upstream - in the opposite direction to a stream's current

Etymology

Formed from 'down' (towards a lower place) and 'stream' (flowing water).

2. ADV

/ ˌdaʊnˈstriːm / (AmE), / ˌdaʊnˈstriːm / (BrE)
in the direction away from the source of a stream or river, or with the current
in the direction with the current of a stream or river

Examples

The boat drifted downstream, carried by the swift current.
After the dam was built, water quality improved downstream.
Several villages are located downstream from the power station.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upstream - in the direction toward the source of a stream or river

Etymology

From 'down' + 'stream', indicating direction along a stream.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00097781-r
Synset POS: r
