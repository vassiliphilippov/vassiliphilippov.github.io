relegation

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛlɪˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛlɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of assigning someone or something to a particular class, category, or status, often of lower rank or importance
assigning to a class or status

Examples

His relegation to the minor leagues disappointed many fans.
Critics have argued against the relegation of the subject to secondary importance.
The constant relegation of poetry to niche audiences alarms many literary enthusiasts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classification - the act of arranging in categories or classes

Etymology

From Latin relegatio (banishment), from relegare (to send away).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛlɪˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛlɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of assigning responsibility or decision-making authority to subordinates; in sports, the act of moving a team to a lower division based on performance.
assigning responsibility; demoting in sports

Examples

The manager's relegation of decision-making to her team increased productivity.
Following their defeat, the club faced relegation to the second division.
His relegation of routine tasks allowed him to focus on strategy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delegation - the act of giving someone else authority to act on your behalf
  • Antonym: promotion - the act of moving a sports team to a higher division

Etymology

From Latin relegatio (banishment), from relegare (to send away).

3. NOUN

/ ˌrɛlɪˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛlɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of assigning someone or something to a lower or less important position, rank, or category
being moved to a lower status or position

Examples

After their poor performance this season, the team faces relegation from the top league.
His outspoken views led to his relegation to a much less significant administrative role.
The company's continual losses resulted in its relegation to a minor player in the market.

Synset Examples

he has been relegated to a post in Siberia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demotion - the act of reducing to a lower rank or position
  • Antonym: promotion - the act of raising to a higher rank or position

Etymology

From Latin relegatio (banishment), from relegare (to banish, remove).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
