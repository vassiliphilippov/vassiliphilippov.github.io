Briard

1. NOUN

/ briˈɑrd / (AmE), / briˈɑːd / (BrE)
a large, strong breed of dog from France, usually with a long, wavy, and slightly stiff coat, often black in color
a French breed of large, wavy-coated dog

Examples

The Briard is known for its loyalty and protective instincts, making it an excellent guard dog.
She groomed her Briard carefully to keep its long, wavy coat looking its best.
At the dog show, the judge praised the Briard for its strong build and graceful movement.

Etymology

From French Briard, named after the Brie region of France.
