Deuteromycetes

1. NOUN

/ ˌduːtərəˈmaɪsiːtiːz / (AmE), / ˌdjuːtərəˈmaɪsiːtiːz / (BrE)
A form class of fungi comprising species whose sexual reproductive stages are unknown; coextensive with the subdivision Deuteromycota.
class of fungi with unknown sexual stages

Examples

Several plant diseases are caused by Deuteromycetes, including some forms of mildew and blight.
Because their sexual stage is unknown, these fungi were traditionally classified as Deuteromycetes.
The group known as Deuteromycetes was established to organize fungi lacking observed sexual reproduction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fungus - a member of the biological kingdom Fungi

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek deuteros (second) + mykēs (fungus).
