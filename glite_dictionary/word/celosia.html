celosia

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈloʊʒə / (AmE), / sɪˈləʊsiə / (BrE)
a genus of annual or perennial herbaceous plants, often grown for their brightly colored, plume-like flowers, native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, Asia, and Africa
a genus of flowering plants with showy plumes

Examples

The vibrant celosia in the garden added a burst of color to the summer landscape.
She planted several varieties of celosia to brighten up the flowerbeds.
Many florists use celosia in bouquets for its unique, feathery appearance.

Etymology

From Greek *kelos* (burned) referring to the flame-like flower heads.
