Canfield

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.fiːld / (AmE), / ˈkæn.fiːld / (BrE)
A form of solitaire played for stakes, in which the object is to build up piles of cards in sequence and suit.
a gambling version of solitaire

Examples

He spent the afternoon patiently playing Canfield with his new deck of cards.
Unlike regular solitaire, Canfield adds a gambling element to the game.
Some casinos once offered Canfield as a way to entertain their patrons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solitaire - a card game played by one person

Etymology

Named after Richard A. Canfield, an American casino owner.
