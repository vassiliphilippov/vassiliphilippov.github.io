gneiss

1. NOUN

/ naɪs / (AmE), / naɪs / (BrE)
A coarse-grained metamorphic rock with a banded or foliated structure, often resembling granite, composed mainly of feldspar, quartz, and mica.
banded metamorphic rock

Examples

The mountain range consists largely of gneiss and other metamorphic rocks.
The geologist identified the layered structure as characteristic of gneiss.
The countertops were crafted from a beautiful piece of polished gneiss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: granite - a coarse-grained igneous rock composed mainly of quartz and feldspar

Etymology

From German 'Gneis', of Slavic origin.
