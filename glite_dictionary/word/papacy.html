papacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪpəsi / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpəsi / (BrE)
the office or authority of the Pope as the leader of the Roman Catholic Church, or the system of government of the Roman Catholic Church under the Pope
office or rule of the Pope

Examples

The power of the papacy increased significantly during the Middle Ages.
He studied the history of the papacy at university.
The new reforms changed the relationship between the papacy and local churches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pontificate - the office or tenure of a pope

Etymology

From Medieval Latin papatia (office of the Pope), from Latin papa (pope).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪ.pə.si / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ.pə.si / (BrE)
the office or authority of the Pope, especially as the head of the Roman Catholic Church
the office of the Pope

Examples

During the Middle Ages, the papacy wielded immense political and spiritual influence in Europe.
Many historians have examined the reforms undertaken by the papacy in the twentieth century.
The election of a new pope marks a significant transition for the papacy.

Synset Examples

The papacy has a rich history and tradition.

Related Terms
