Podicipitiformes

1. NOUN

/ poʊˌdɪsɪpɪˈtɔrmiːz / (AmE), / pɒˌdɪsɪpɪˈtɔːmiːz / (BrE)
An order of aquatic, diving birds known as grebes, characterized by lobed toes and excellent swimming ability.
order of diving birds (grebes)

Examples

Scientists have observed various breeding behaviors among Podicipitiformes in different habitats around the world.
The skeleton of Podicipitiformes is specially adapted for efficient swimming and diving.
Field guides often discuss the unique plumage patterns of Podicipitiformes species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grebe - a diving freshwater bird of the family Podicipedidae

Etymology

From modern Latin Podicipitiformes, from Podiceps (a genus of grebes), Latin podicis (vent) + pes (foot), referring to the location of the legs.

2. NOUN

/ poʊˌdɪsɪpɪtəˈfɔːrmiːz / (AmE), / pɒˌdɪsɪpɪtɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of aquatic birds comprising the grebes
order of aquatic birds: grebes

Examples

The Podicipitiformes are adapted for swimming with their lobed feet and slender bodies.
Researchers studied the breeding patterns of birds within the Podicipitiformes order.
Grebes, classified under Podicipitiformes, are known for their elaborate courtship displays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grebe - aquatic diving bird belonging to Podicipitiformes

Etymology

From modern Latin Podicipitiformes, from Podiceps (type genus: grebe) + -formes (denoting shape or form).
