soddy

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑdi / (AmE), / ˈsɒdi / (BrE)
a house built from blocks of sod (earth with grass roots) or adobe, typically used by settlers on the Great Plains of North America
a house built of sod or adobe

Examples

Early settlers on the plains often lived in soddies because of the lack of timber.
The museum has a replica soddy to show how pioneers built their homes.
A soddy provided excellent insulation against the harsh prairie winters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adobe - a building material made from earth and organic materials

Etymology

From 'sod,' meaning a piece of earth with grass, plus the suffix '-y'.
