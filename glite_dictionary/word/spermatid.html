spermatid

1. NOUN

/ spɚˈmætɪd / (AmE), / spəˈmætɪd / (BrE)
an immature male reproductive cell, formed from a spermatocyte, that develops into a spermatozoon
immature male reproductive cell
medical

Examples

The spermatid undergoes several changes before becoming a fully functional sperm cell.
Researchers observed the transformation of a spermatid into a spermatozoon under the microscope.
Mutations in certain genes can interfere with the development of a healthy spermatid.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: spermatozoon - the mature male reproductive cell (sperm cell)

Etymology

From Greek sperma (seed) + -id (forming nouns of Greek origin indicating offspring or descent).
