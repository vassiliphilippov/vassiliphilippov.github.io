Grigori Rasputin

1. PROPN

/ ɡrɪˈɡɔri ræˈspuːtɪn / (AmE), / ɡrɪˈɡɔːri rɑːsˈpjuːtɪn / (BrE)
(1872–1916) A Siberian mystic and self-proclaimed holy man who became a close advisor to the Russian royal family of Nicholas II, noted for his influence and controversial lifestyle.
Russian mystic and royal advisor

Examples

Many historians debate the true extent of Grigori Rasputin's influence over the Russian royal family.
The mysterious death of Grigori Rasputin has inspired numerous books and films.
Grigori Rasputin was often called the 'mad monk' by his contemporaries.

Etymology

Russian given name Grigori and surname Rasputin.
