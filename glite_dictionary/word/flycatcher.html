flycatcher

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪˌkætʃər / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌkætʃə / (BrE)
A type of bird, especially found in the Americas, that catches insects in flight.
insect-eating bird that catches prey in flight

Examples

We watched a flycatcher swoop through the air, snatching insects as it flew.
The garden has become home to several colorful flycatchers this spring.
That species of flycatcher is known for its acrobatic hunting techniques.

Etymology

From 'fly' (insect) + 'catcher' (one who catches).

2. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪˌkætʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌkætʃə / (BrE)
A small songbird that catches and feeds on insects in flight, belonging to several families, especially Muscicapidae and Tyrannidae.
insect-eating songbird

Examples

The flycatcher darted through the air, snapping up insects with impressive agility.
Several species of flycatchers migrate south for the winter each year.
We spotted a colorful flycatcher perched on a branch, waiting for its next meal.

Etymology

From 'fly' (the insect) + 'catcher' (one that catches).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
