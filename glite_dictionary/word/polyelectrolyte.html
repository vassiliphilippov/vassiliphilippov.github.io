polyelectrolyte

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliɪˈlɛktroʊˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˌpɒliɪˈlɛktrəʊˌlaɪt / (BrE)
A polymer whose repeating units bear an electrolyte group, allowing it to dissolve in water and conduct electricity when ionized.
polymer that acts as an electrolyte
medical

Examples

Some polyelectrolyte solutions can conduct electricity better than salt water.
Researchers studied the interaction between a polyelectrolyte and metal ions.
The properties of a polyelectrolyte depend on its molecular structure and charge density.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electrolyte - a substance that produces ions and conducts electricity when dissolved in water

Etymology

From poly- (many) + electrolyte.
