iteration

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪtəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪtəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
(computing) a single execution of a set of instructions in a loop, especially in a computer program.
one cycle in a program loop

Examples

The program completed the task after just one iteration of the algorithm.
Each iteration checks the value before moving on to the next step.
Debugging revealed an error on the third iteration of the process.

Synset Examples

the solution took hundreds of iterations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cycle - a single complete occurrence of a repeating process

Etymology

From Latin iteratio (a repeating, repetition), from iterare (to repeat).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13525376-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪtəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪtəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
(computing) The process of repeating a set of instructions a specified number of times or until a particular result is achieved.
repetition of instructions in computing

Examples

In programming, iteration allows a task to be repeated efficiently.
The algorithm uses iteration to refine the solution until it is accurate.
Learning about iteration is essential for anyone interested in computer science.

Synset Examples

the solution is obtained by iteration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loop - a sequence of instructions that is continually repeated until a certain condition is reached (computing)

Etymology

From Latin iteratio, from iterare ‘to repeat’.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13525111-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪtəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪtəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the action of repeating something, such as a process or statement; a repeated occurrence or version of something
the act of repeating; repetition

Examples

The new software design was developed after several iterations to improve its performance.
Every iteration of the experiment brought them closer to a solution.
His speech was an iteration of the same points he made last week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repetition - the action of repeating something

Etymology

From Latin iteratio (a repeating), from iterare (to repeat).
