transvestitism

1. NOUN

/ trænzˈvɛstəˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / tranzˈvɛstɪtɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice of dressing and adopting the appearance or role of the opposite sex, often for psychological or personal reasons
the practice of dressing as the opposite sex
medical

Examples

Historically, transvestitism was misunderstood and often stigmatized in many cultures.
The documentary explores the topic of transvestitism in both historical and modern contexts.
Some people engage in transvestitism as a form of self-expression or performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cross-dressing - the act of dressing in clothes typical of the opposite sex

Etymology

From Latin trans- (across) + vestire (to clothe) + -ism (practice or doctrine).
