crimper

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪmpɚ / (AmE), / ˈkrɪmpə / (BrE)
a device consisting of a cylindrical tube around which hair is wound to curl it
device for curling hair

Examples

She carefully placed each section of hair into the crimper before getting ready for her party.
Using a crimper can give your hair tight, bouncy curls in just a few minutes.
I borrowed my sister's crimper to style my hair for the school dance.

Synset Examples

a woman with her head full of curlers is not a pretty sight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curler - a device for curling hair, often cylindrical

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪmpər / (AmE), / ˈkrɪmpə / (BrE)
a person who procures men for service as sailors or soldiers by trickery or coercion, especially during the 19th century
person who coerces men into military or naval service

Examples

During the 1800s, a notorious crimper operated along the docks, tricking men into joining the navy.
Many sailors feared falling prey to a crimper while visiting unfamiliar ports.
The authorities eventually arrested the infamous crimper who had coerced dozens of men into service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recruiter - person who enlists others for service, especially in the military
  • Synonym: press-gang - group forcing men into naval service

Etymology

From 'crimp' (to enlist men by trickery or force) + '-er' (agent noun suffix); 'crimp' of uncertain origin, possibly related to Dutch 'krimpen' (to shrink, contract), implying to restrict.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪmpɚ / (AmE), / ˈkrɪmpə / (BrE)
a hand tool used for joining two pieces of metal or other ductile material, especially by deforming one to hold the other securely, as in attaching a connector to a wire
tool for joining metals by deforming them

Examples

He used a crimper to fasten the electrical connector onto the wire.
Every electrician should have a good quality crimper in their tool kit.
The instructions recommend using a crimper rather than pliers for best results.

Synset Examples

Stripped wire (often stranded) is inserted through the correctly sized opening of the connector, and a crimper is used to tightly squeeze the opening against the wire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crimping tool - a tool designed specifically for making crimped joints in wires or metals

Etymology

From 'crimp' meaning to press or pinch into small ridges or folds, + -er suffix denoting an agent noun.
