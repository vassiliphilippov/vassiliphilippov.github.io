concentrated

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑnsənˌtreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɒnsəntreɪtɪd / (BrE)
showing or involving intense focus or effort directed toward a particular task or purpose
intensely focused or directed

Examples

She gave the project her concentrated attention, ignoring all distractions.
A concentrated effort by the team led to a successful product launch.
His concentrated gaze made it clear he was deeply engaged in the conversation.

Synset Examples

her concentrated passion held them at bay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: focused - directing attention or effort to a specific task
  • Antonym: distracted - unable to concentrate or focus

Etymology

From Latin concentratus, past participle of concentrare (to bring to a center).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɑnsənˌtreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɒnsənˌtreɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a solution) made stronger by reducing the amount of solvent; having a high proportion of solute compared to solvent.
having a high proportion of solute (chemistry)

Examples

The chemist used a concentrated acid to speed up the reaction.
Please wear gloves when handling concentrated cleaning products, as they can be corrosive.
This orange juice is made from concentrated syrup and needs to be diluted with water before drinking.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dilute - (of a liquid) made weaker by adding more solvent; opposite of concentrated

Etymology

From Latin 'concentrare' (to bring to a center).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00760522-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈkɑnsənˌtreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɒnsənˌtreɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of light) transmitted directly from a single, focused source, resulting in increased intensity.
light coming from a focused source

Examples

The surgeon used a concentrated beam of light during the delicate operation.
A concentrated spotlight illuminated the central performer on stage.
Plants placed under concentrated sunlight often grow faster.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diffuse - (of light) spread out and not concentrated in one direction

Etymology

From Latin 'concentratus', past participle of 'concentrare', meaning 'to bring to a center'.
