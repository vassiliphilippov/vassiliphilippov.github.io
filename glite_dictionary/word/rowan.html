rowan

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.ən / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.ən / (BrE)
A small Eurasian tree with compound leaves and clusters of orange-red berrylike fruits, belonging to the genus Sorbus.
a Eurasian tree with orange-red fruits

Examples

The rowan tree is often planted in gardens for its attractive berries.
A flock of birds gathered in the rowan, feasting on the ripe fruit.
Folklore says that the rowan offers protection against evil spirits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain ash - Another name for the rowan tree, especially in North America

Etymology

From Old Norse *reynir*, referring to the rowan tree.
