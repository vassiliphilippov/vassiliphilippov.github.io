arranger

1. NOUN

/ əˈreɪndʒər / (AmE), / əˈreɪndʒə / (BrE)
a musician who adapts a musical composition for particular voices, instruments, or styles of performance
musician who adapts compositions

Examples

The arranger transformed the classical piece into a modern jazz number.
She worked as an arranger for several well-known pop bands.
Each arranger brings a unique interpretation to the original composition.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: composer - someone who writes music
  • Synonym: orchestrator - someone who arranges music for orchestra

Etymology

From French 'arranger', from Old French 'arangier' (to set in order).

2. NOUN

/ əˈreɪndʒər / (AmE), / əˈreɪndʒə / (BrE)
a person who organizes and brings order to an activity or enterprise
someone who organizes or coordinates activities

Examples

As the main arranger of the conference, she ensured everything ran smoothly.
He was well known as a reliable arranger for community projects.
The team's arranger delegated tasks to each member to increase efficiency.

Synset Examples

she was the organizer of the meeting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organizer - a person who arranges, manages, or coordinates an event or activity

Etymology

From arrange + -er, modelled after the verb 'arrange' (from Old French 'arangier').
