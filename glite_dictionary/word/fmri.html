fMRI

1. NOUN

/ ɛf ɛm ɑːr aɪ / (AmE), / ɛf ɛm ɑː r aɪ / (BrE)
a technique for measuring and mapping brain activity by detecting changes in blood flow using magnetic resonance imaging
functional brain imaging technique
medical

Examples

The fMRI scan revealed increased activity in the subject's visual cortex.
Researchers used fMRI to study how the brain processes language in different conditions.
Advances in fMRI technology have improved our understanding of neurological disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: MRI - magnetic resonance imaging, a medical imaging technique

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'functional magnetic resonance imaging.'
