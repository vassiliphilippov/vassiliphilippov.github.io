shifty

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃɪfti / (AmE), / ˈʃɪfti / (BrE)
Looking or seeming dishonest, evasive, or capable of deceit; not trustworthy.
evasive or deceitful in appearance or behavior

Examples

The detective noticed the suspect's shifty eyes as he answered questions.
I don't trust that car salesman; he seems a little shifty to me.
Her shifty behavior made everyone at the meeting uncomfortable.

Synset Examples

a devious character
shifty eyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devious - showing a skillful use of underhanded tactics
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃɪfti / (AmE), / ˈʃɪfti / (BrE)
(of something) changing position or direction, especially frequently or unpredictably.
changing position or direction

Examples

After hours of shifty winds, the sailors found it hard to maintain their course.
The crowd grew shifty as it slowly dispersed in different directions.
Clouds moved across the sky in a shifty manner, blocking and revealing the sun in turns.

Synset Examples

he drifted into the shifting crowd
their nervous shifting glances
shifty winds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unstable - prone to change or fluctuate; not stable
  • Antonym: steady - firmly fixed, supported, or balanced; not shaking or moving
