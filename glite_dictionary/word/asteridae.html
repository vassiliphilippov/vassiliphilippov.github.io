Asteridae

1. NOUN

/ æs.təˈraɪ.di.i / (AmE), / ˌastəˈraɪdiːiː / (BrE)
A large group of mostly herbaceous plants, trees, and shrubs characterized by flowers with fused petals and usually two fused carpels, historically including families such as Solanaceae and Compositae; sometimes treated as a superorder in plant classification.
major group of flowering plants in botany

Examples

The family Solanaceae is traditionally included within the Asteridae.
Some plant taxonomists now divide the Asteridae into several different groups based on genetic evidence.
Many common garden flowers, such as petunias, belong to the Asteridae.

Etymology

From New Latin, from 'Aster', a representative genus, + '-idae', a taxonomic suffix.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæstɚˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌæstəˈraɪdiː / (BrE)
a major subclass of flowering plants, mostly herbs, trees, or shrubs with sympetalous flowers and usually two fused carpels, including many familiar families such as Solanaceae and Compositae
subclass of flowering plants
medical

Examples

The Asteridae include plants that are important both ecologically and economically, such as potatoes and bell peppers.
Recent botanical studies have refined the classification of the Asteridae based on genetic evidence.
Many ornamental flowers, like petunias and morning glories, belong to the Asteridae.

Etymology

From New Latin, derived from 'Aster', a genus of plants, and the suffix '-idae' indicating a taxonomic group.
