victualer

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪtələr / (AmE), / ˈvɪtələ / (BrE)
An innkeeper; a person who keeps an inn or supplies food and drink, especially in British usage.
innkeeper; supplier of food and drink
british

Examples

The local victualer welcomed weary travelers with hearty stews and strong ale.
As a respected victualer, she took pride in the comfort of her guests.
Historic records mention a victualer who operated the town's only inn for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innkeeper - a person who owns or runs an inn

Etymology

From Middle English *vitayler*, from Anglo-Norman *vitailour*, from Old French *vitaillier*, from *victualia* (provisions).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɪtələr / (AmE), / ˈvɪtələ / (BrE)
a person or company that supplies food and provisions, especially to an army or ship.
supplier of food and provisions

Examples

During the campaign, the victualer ensured a steady supply of food for the soldiers.
The victualer arrived with fresh bread and salted meat for the encampment.
In old naval records, a victualer is often listed alongside ship officers and crew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provisioner - a person who supplies provisions, especially food

Etymology

From Old French *vitailleor*, from *vitaille* (food), ultimately from Latin *victualia* (provisions).
