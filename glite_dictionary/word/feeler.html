feeler

1. NOUN

/ ˈfilər / (AmE), / ˈfiːlə / (BrE)
one of a pair of movable, sensory appendages on the head of an insect, crustacean, or similar animal, typically used for touching and tasting
sensory appendage on insect/crustacean head

Examples

The moth extended its feelers to sense the air around it.
Crabs use their feelers to search for food on the ocean floor.
The caterpillar's feelers twitched as it explored the leaf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antenna - a sensory appendage on the head of an insect or other arthropod

Etymology

Formed from 'feel' + '-er', meaning 'one that feels'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfiːlɚ / (AmE), / ˈfiːlə / (BrE)
a slender, sensitive organ, such as an antenna or similar structure, used by an animal (such as a fish or insect) for touching and sensing its environment
slender sensory organ of animals

Examples

The catfish uses its feelers to search for food along the river bottom.
A shrimp's long feelers help it navigate through murky water.
Scientists observed the movement of the insect's feeler as it explored its surroundings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antenna - a long, sensitive appendage on the head of insects and some other animals

Etymology

From 'feel' + '-er', meaning something that feels.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfiːlər / (AmE), / ˈfiːlə / (BrE)
a tentative suggestion or proposal put forward to gauge others' reactions or opinions
tentative suggestion to test reactions

Examples

The company sent out a feeler to see if there was interest in a merger.
She put out a feeler about organizing a joint project, but got little response.
Before making any decision, he dropped a feeler to test how the team might react.
