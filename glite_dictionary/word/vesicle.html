vesicle

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛsɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈvɛsɪkəl / (BrE)
a small sac or bladderlike structure, especially one containing fluid, found in living organisms
small fluid-filled sac in organisms
medical

Examples

Under a microscope, researchers observed a vesicle forming within the cell membrane.
The doctor explained that the rash consisted of tiny fluid-filled vesicles on the skin.
Some drugs are transported in the body within a fatty vesicle to help absorption.

Etymology

From Latin vesicula, diminutive of vesica (bladder).
