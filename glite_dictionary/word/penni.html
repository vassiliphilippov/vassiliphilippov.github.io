penni

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛni / (AmE), / ˈpɛni / (BrE)
a former monetary unit of Finland, equal to one hundredth of a markka.
former Finnish monetary unit

Examples

Before the euro, a single penni was worth one one-hundredth of a Finnish markka.
She found some old pennia while cleaning her grandmother's attic in Helsinki.
Although no longer in use, the penni remains a part of Finnish monetary history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: markka - the former basic monetary unit of Finland, replaced by the euro

Etymology

From Finnish 'penni', itself from German 'Pfennig' (a small coin).
