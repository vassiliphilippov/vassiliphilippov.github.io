campanulate

1. ADJ

/ kæmˈpæn.jə.lət / (AmE), / kamˈpan.jʊ.lət / (BrE)
shaped like a bell, especially used to describe certain flowers or plant structures.
bell-shaped (esp. in botany)

Examples

The campanulate corolla of the bluebell is one of its most distinctive features.
Many wildflowers have campanulate blooms that attract pollinators.
The botanist noted the campanulate shape of the flower during her field study.

Synset Examples

campanulate flowers of the genus Campanula

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bell-shaped - having the shape of a bell

Etymology

From Late Latin campanulatus, from campanula (little bell).
