harborage

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrbərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈhɑːbərɪdʒ / (BrE)
(nautical) A place of refuge or shelter, especially for a ship.
refuge or shelter for a ship

Examples

The narrow bay provided perfect harborage during the sudden storm.
Fishermen sought harborage in the cove as the winds grew stronger.
Early explorers relied on natural harborage along the coastline for safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harbor - a place on the coast where vessels may find shelter

Etymology

From 'harbor' + '-age', meaning a place that provides harbor.
