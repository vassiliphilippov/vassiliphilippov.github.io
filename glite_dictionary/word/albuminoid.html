albuminoid

1. NOUN

/ ælˈbjumɪˌnɔɪd / (AmE), / ælˈbjuːmɪnɔɪd / (BrE)
a simple protein found in horny and cartilaginous tissues and in the lens of the eye
a simple protein in tissues or the eye lens
medical

Examples

Keratin is an albuminoid present in hair and nails.
Collagen, an important albuminoid, provides structure to connective tissues.
Some diseases affect the albuminoid content of the eye lens, leading to vision problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scleroprotein - a type of protein forming connective tissues, similar to albuminoid

Etymology

From 'albumin' (a class of proteins), with '-oid' meaning 'resembling' or 'like'.
