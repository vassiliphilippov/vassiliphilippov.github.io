typification

1. NOUN

/ ˌtaɪpəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɪpɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of representing something by a type, symbol, or typical example; the action of typifying
representation by a type or example

Examples

The typification of certain animal species helps scientists categorize new discoveries.
Art often relies on the typification of human emotions through symbolic figures.
Her analysis criticized the typification of gender roles found in classic literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to typify - to represent or be a typical example of something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin typificatio, from typificare ('to typify').

2. NOUN

/ ˌtaɪpəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɪpɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or result of representing something as a typical example or form; a model or example that embodies the common features of a group or category
representation as a typical model or example

Examples

The architect’s new design was seen as a typification of modern urban housing.
His research focused on the typification of social roles within organizations.
Victorian novels often include a typification of gender norms prevalent at the time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to typify - to be a typical example of something
  • Synonym: stereotype - a widely held but oversimplified idea of a particular type of person or thing

Etymology

From Medieval Latin typificatio (representation as a type), from 'typificare' (to typify).
