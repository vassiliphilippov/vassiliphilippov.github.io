sodom

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑː.dəm / (AmE), / ˈsɒd.əm / (BrE)
A place regarded as notorious for vice and corruption.
place of vice and corruption

Examples

After years of unchecked crime, the city was called a modern sodom by the press.
The novelist described the port as a sodom teeming with corruption and vice.
Some fear the once quaint town is turning into a sodom of gambling and excess.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gomorrah - another biblical city destroyed for its wickedness

Etymology

From the biblical city of Sodom, destroyed for wickedness according to the Book of Genesis.
