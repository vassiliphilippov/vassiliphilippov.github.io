Sodom

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɑː.dəm / (AmE), / ˈsɒd.əm / (BrE)
an ancient city near the Dead Sea, destroyed by God in the Old Testament for the wickedness of its inhabitants
ancient city destroyed in the Old Testament

Examples

The story of Sodom and Gomorrah has been retold for centuries.
Archaeologists have searched for the ancient city of Sodom near the Dead Sea.
Many people use Sodom as a symbol of vice and moral decay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gomorrah - another ancient city destroyed with Sodom for its wickedness, according to the Old Testament

Etymology

From Hebrew *Sǝḏōm*, the biblical name for the city.
