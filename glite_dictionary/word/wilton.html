Wilton

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪltən / (AmE), / ˈwɪltən / (BrE)
a type of carpet woven on a Jacquard loom, featuring loops like a Brussels carpet but with the loops cut to create a dense velvety pile
type of cut-pile carpet

Examples

The grand staircase was covered with a rich Wilton that complemented the historic interior.
They chose a deep red Wilton for the hotel lobby to add an elegant touch.
Over the years, the Wilton in the dining room held up remarkably well to heavy use.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Brussels carpet - a carpet woven with uncut loops to form a textured surface

Etymology

Named after the town of Wilton in Wiltshire, England, where this style of carpet was first produced.
