higher

1. ADJ

/ ˈhaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪə / (BrE)
relating to education beyond the secondary (high school) level, such as that provided by colleges or universities
pertaining to post-secondary education

Examples

After graduating from high school, she went on to pursue higher education at a prestigious university.
Many government policies aim to increase access to higher learning for students from diverse backgrounds.
The importance of higher education in career advancement cannot be overstated.

Synset Examples

higher education
higher learning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tertiary - relating to third-level or post-secondary education

2. ADJ

/ ˈhaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪə / (BrE)
(of a subject, study, or activity) involving an advanced or more complex level; beyond the basic or elementary.
advanced or more complex (e.g., studies or activities)

Examples

She decided to pursue higher mathematics at university.
Many students find higher finance challenging due to its complexity.
The professor specializes in higher logic and advanced philosophy.

Synset Examples

higher finance
higher mathematics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advanced - far on or ahead in development or progress
  • Antonym: elementary - basic, relating to the simplest principles of a subject

Etymology

Comparative form of 'high,' from Old English hēah.
