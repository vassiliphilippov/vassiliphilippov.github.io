spar

1. VERB

/ spɑr / (AmE), / spɑː / (BrE)
to furnish (a ship or structure) with spars (long poles or beams, especially on a ship)
furnish with spars (beams or poles)

Examples

The crew worked late into the night to spar the new vessel before departure.
Older shipyards still occasionally spar wooden schooners for traditional regattas.
They sparred the entire fleet with stronger masts after the storm.

2. NOUN

/ spɑr / (AmE), / spɑː / (BrE)
a strong, thick pole made of wood or metal, used to support rigging and sails on a ship
pole supporting rigging on a ship

Examples

The sailors lashed the broken spar to the deck to prevent it from rolling.
A new spar was installed to replace the one damaged in the storm.
Many ropes were attached to the main spar to control the sails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mast - a tall vertical pole on a ship that supports sails and rigging

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle Low German *sper* (spear, pole), related to Old English *spere* (spear).

3. NOUN

/ spɑr / (AmE), / spɑː / (BrE)
any of several nonmetallic minerals, such as calcite or feldspar, that are light in color, transparent or translucent, and cleavable.
type of cleavable mineral, e.g., calcite or feldspar

Examples

The crystal collection included a beautiful piece of spar with perfect cleavages.
Geologists identified the translucent mineral as a type of spar based on its characteristics.
Some ancient tools were made from spar because it was easy to shape and split.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feldspar - a group of rock-forming minerals that make up about 60% of the Earth's crust
  • Synonym: calcite - a common crystalline form of natural calcium carbonate, CaCO₃

Etymology

From Middle English spar (a piece of mineral), of Germanic origin; related to Old Norse sparr and Old High German spar (gypsum).

5. VERB

/ spɑr / (AmE), / spɑː / (BrE)
to engage in a form of boxing or practice fighting with light blows, often as training or exercise rather than a real contest
to box or practice fighting lightly

Examples

The two fighters began to spar in the ring before the real match started.
We practiced our techniques by sparring lightly every morning.
He often spars with his friend to stay in shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: box - to fight with fists, especially as a sport

Etymology

From Middle English sparren (to defend, ward off), ultimately from Old Norse sperra (to kick, shove, or block).
