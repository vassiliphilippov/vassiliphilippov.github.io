cot

1. NOUN

/ kɑt / (AmE), / kɒt / (BrE)
A small sheath, usually made of rubber or similar material, worn over a finger to protect it from contamination, injury, or contact.
finger-protecting sheath

Examples

The nurse put a cot over her finger before changing the patient's bandage.
He wore a cot while slicing spicy peppers to avoid skin irritation.
A cot can help keep wounds on your finger clean and dry during healing.

2. NOUN

/ kɑt / (AmE), / kɒt / (BrE)
A small bed, often folding, used for sleeping, usually portable and easy to store or transport.
folding portable bed

Examples

The guest slept comfortably on the cot they set up in the living room.
We keep a cot in the closet for when friends stay over.
She quickly folded up the cot after the camping trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camp bed - a portable folding bed used for camping or temporary accommodation
  • Synonym: crib - a small bed with high sides for a baby or young child (US usage: crib, UK usage: cot)

Etymology

From Hindi *khāṭ*, meaning a bedstead or light bed.

3. NOUN

/ kɑt / (AmE), / kɒt / (BrE)
a small bed with high sides, usually for a baby or young child, to prevent them from falling out
a small bed for a baby with high sides
british

Examples

The baby slept soundly in her cot all night.
We set up the cot in the corner of the nursery.
Make sure the cot rails are raised before leaving the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crib - baby's small bed with high sides (American usage)

Etymology

From Hindi *khāṭ* (bedstead, cot), via Portuguese *cote*.
