Chiroptera

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈrɑptərə / (AmE), / kaɪˈrɒptərə / (BrE)
A scientific order of mammals that includes all species of bats, divided into the suborders Megachiroptera (fruit bats) and Microchiroptera (insect-eating bats).
scientific order of bats

Examples

Chiroptera are the only mammals capable of sustained flight, distinguishing them from other animal orders.
The order Chiroptera is divided into two major groups: Megachiroptera and Microchiroptera.
Researchers study Chiroptera to understand their unique adaptations for echolocation and flight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bat - a nocturnal flying mammal of the order Chiroptera

Etymology

From Greek 'cheir' (hand) + 'pteron' (wing), referring to the bat's wing structure.
