teetertotter

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiːtərˌtɑːtər / (AmE), / ˈtiːtəˌtɒtə / (BrE)
A long, narrow board balanced on a central support so that, as one end goes up, the other goes down, used by children at play.
children's seesaw play equipment
american

Examples

The children laughed as they played on the teetertotter in the park.
Tom fell off the teetertotter when his friend jumped off suddenly.
At the playground, the old wooden teetertotter was always busy after school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seesaw - another name for the same playground apparatus

Etymology

Reduplication from 'teeter' (to move unsteadily) and 'totter'; chiefly North American.
