juicer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːsər / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːsə / (BrE)
a device with a conical, ridged center used to extract juice from citrus fruits
device for extracting juice from fruit

Examples

She used a juicer to make fresh orange juice for breakfast.
The lemon was too large to fit in the small juicer.
After squeezing a dozen limes, the juicer needed a good rinse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squeezer - a device for squeezing juice from fruit
  • Degree Higher: blender - a kitchen device for mixing and pureeing food, not specialized for extracting juice

Etymology

From 'juice' + '-er', indicating an agent or device that produces juice.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːsər / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːsə / (BrE)
(informal) A person who drinks alcoholic beverages, especially in large quantities or habitually.
person who drinks alcohol habitually
informal

Examples

He's always been a bit of a juicer, never turning down a drink after work.
The old juicer on the corner was well known at every bar in town.
After years as a juicer, he finally decided to quit drinking for good.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drunkard - a person who is habitually drunk
  • Antonym: teetotaler - a person who abstains from alcohol

Etymology

From 'juice', slang for alcoholic drink, plus the agent suffix '-er'.
