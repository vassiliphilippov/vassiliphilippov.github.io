Citellus leucurus

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈtɛləs luˈkʊrəs / (AmE), / sɪˈtɛləs luˈkjʊərəs / (BrE)
A small species of ground squirrel native to the western United States, recognized by its pale tail.
ground squirrel of western US

Examples

The Citellus leucurus is often observed darting through the sagebrush of the high desert.
Researchers measured the activity patterns of the Citellus leucurus to study desert adaptation.
Hikers sometimes spot a Citellus leucurus scurrying between burrows in the midday sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white-tailed antelope squirrel - common name for Citellus leucurus
  • Degree Higher: ground squirrel - small burrowing rodents known for living in colonies

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Citellus' is a former genus name for ground squirrels, and 'leucurus' means 'white-tailed' in Greek.
