prepossessing

1. ADJ

/ priˌpɑˈzɛsɪŋ / (AmE), / priˌpɒˈzɛsɪŋ / (BrE)
attractive or appealing in appearance or manner; creating a favorable impression
attractive; making a good impression

Examples

Her prepossessing smile made a strong impression during the job interview.
The cottage was small but prepossessing, with flowers blooming in the garden.
He had a prepossessing manner that put everyone at ease immediately.

Synset Examples

strong and vigorous and of prepossessing appearance

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unattractive - not appealing in appearance or manner
  • Synonym: charming - very pleasant or attractive

Etymology

From Late Latin praepossess-, past participle stem of praepossessare, from prae- (before) + possess.
