diverticulosis

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌvɜrtɪkjuˈloʊsɪs / (AmE), / daɪˌvɜːtɪkjuːˈləʊsɪs / (BrE)
A condition in which multiple small pouches (diverticula) form in the walls of the colon, often without symptoms.
colon condition with pouches
medical

Examples

Many people with diverticulosis are unaware of the condition because it often causes no symptoms.
A colonoscopy revealed the presence of diverticulosis in the patient.
A high-fiber diet is often recommended to manage diverticulosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diverticulitis - inflammation of diverticula

Etymology

From Latin 'diverticulum' (bypath, side path) + Greek '-osis' (condition).
