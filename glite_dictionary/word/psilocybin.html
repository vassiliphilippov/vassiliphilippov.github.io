psilocybin

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪloʊˈsaɪbɪn / (AmE), / ˌsɪləˈsaɪbɪn / (BrE)
a naturally occurring hallucinogenic compound found especially in certain mushrooms, known for its psychoactive effects
hallucinogenic compound from mushrooms
medical

Examples

Recent studies have explored the therapeutic potential of psilocybin in treating depression.
The species Psilocybe cubensis contains a significant amount of psilocybin.
Some cultures have used psilocybin-containing mushrooms for spiritual rituals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hallucinogen - a substance that causes hallucinations
  • Degree Lower: psilocin - another psychoactive compound in 'magic mushrooms'

Etymology

From New Latin Psilocybe (a genus of mushroom) + -in (chemical suffix).
