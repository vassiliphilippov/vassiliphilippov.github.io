intolerably

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈtɑlɚəbli / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɒlərəbli / (BrE)
to a degree that cannot be tolerated; unacceptably
to an unacceptable degree

Examples

The weather during the heatwave became intolerably hot, making it hard to sleep at night.
She found the pain intolerably sharp and had to see a doctor immediately.
The noise from the construction site was intolerably loud for anyone living nearby.

Synset Examples

The percentage of lead in our drinking water is unacceptably high

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unbearably - to a degree that cannot be endured
  • Antonym: tolerably - to a degree that can be tolerated

Etymology

From Latin 'intolerabilis' (not bearable), via Old French and Middle English.
