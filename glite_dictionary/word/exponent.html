exponent

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspoʊnənt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspəʊnənt / (BrE)
a symbol or number placed above and to the right of another number to show how many times the number is to be multiplied by itself
symbol indicating repeated multiplication

Examples

The exponent in 3² tells you to multiply 3 by itself once.
In scientific notation, the exponent shows the number of decimal places moved.
Raising a number to a negative exponent gives its reciprocal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: power - the product of multiplying a number by itself a certain number of times

Etymology

From Latin exponent-, exponents, present participle of exponere (to put forth, explain).

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspoʊnənt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspəʊnənt / (BrE)
a person who advocates, explains, or is a leading representative of an idea, cause, or movement
advocate or leading representative

Examples

She has become a leading exponent of renewable energy in her community.
The book's author is an outspoken exponent of education reform.
As an exponent of modern art, he frequently gives public lectures and exhibitions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advocate - a person who publicly supports or recommends a particular cause or policy
  • Antonym: opponent - a person who disagrees with and argues against a cause or idea

Etymology

From Latin 'exponens', present participle of 'exponere' (to put forth, explain).

someone who explains or advocates

Examples

The documentary featured an exponent who explained the origins of the tradition.
He became an enthusiastic exponent of free market principles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: opponent - a person who disagrees with or fights against an idea or cause

From Latin 'exponens', present participle of 'exponere' (to put forth, explain).
