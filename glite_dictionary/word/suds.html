2. NOUN

/ sʌdz / (AmE), / sʌdz / (BrE)
the mass of bubbles or foam formed on the surface of liquids, especially when soap or detergent is added to water
soap or detergent bubbles

Examples

After washing the dishes, the sink was filled with suds.
The dog jumped into the tub and sent suds flying everywhere.
Rinsing your hair thoroughly removes all the shampoo suds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foam - a mass of small bubbles formed on or in liquid

Etymology

Originally a dialect word; probably related to Middle Low German sudse (boiling water).
