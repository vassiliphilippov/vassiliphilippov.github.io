aquamarine

1. NOUN

/ ˌækwəməˈrin / (AmE), / ˌakwəməˈriːn / (BrE)
A transparent, blue-green variety of beryl, used as a gemstone.
blue-green gemstone (beryl variety)

Examples

The ring featured a stunning aquamarine set in white gold.
Collectors prize aquamarine for its vivid blue-green hue and clarity.
She chose an aquamarine necklace to complement her dress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beryl - a mineral of which aquamarine is a variety

Etymology

From Latin 'aqua marina' meaning 'sea water', referring to its color.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːkwəməˈriːn / (AmE), / ˌakwəməˈriːn / (BrE)
a pale blue-green color, similar to the color of the sea
pale blue-green color

Examples

The artist used aquamarine to paint the calm surface of the lake.
She wore a dress in a lovely shade of aquamarine to the party.
The interior designer suggested aquamarine walls to create a soothing atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turquoise - a greenish-blue color
  • Degree Lower: teal - a dark greenish-blue color
  • Degree Higher: cyan - a lighter blue-green color

Etymology

From Latin aqua marina, meaning 'sea water'.
