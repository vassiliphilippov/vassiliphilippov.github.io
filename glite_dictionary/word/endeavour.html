endeavour

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈdɛvər / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛvə / (BrE)
to try very hard to do something; to attempt with serious effort
try hard; attempt with effort
british

Examples

The scientists endeavoured to find a solution to the complex problem.
Despite the difficulties, she endeavoured to complete the project on time.
We must always endeavour to treat others with respect.

Synset Examples

we endeavor to make our customers happy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attempt - to try to do something, especially something difficult
  • Synonym: try - to make an effort to do something

Etymology

From Old French 'endevourer', based on 'devoir' (duty).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02536865-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɛvər / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛvə / (BrE)
an attempt to achieve something, especially one that requires effort and determination
an effort or attempt

Examples

This scientific endeavour could revolutionize medicine in the next decade.
Despite many failures, their endeavour finally met with success.
The restoration project was a challenging endeavour requiring many volunteers.

Synset Examples

he had doubts about the whole enterprise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attempt - an act of trying to do something, especially something difficult
  • Synonym: enterprise - a project or undertaking, especially one that is difficult or requires effort
  • Antonym: idleness - the state of being inactive or not working

Etymology

From Old French 'endeverer', probably from phrase 'en deveir' ('in duty'), later influenced by Modern French 'devoir' ('duty') and English spelling.

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɛvɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛvə / (BrE)
an attempt to achieve a goal, especially one that requires effort and determination
attempt to achieve a goal
british

Examples

Launching a new business is a challenging endeavour that requires dedication and resilience.
This charity supports scientific endeavours across the globe.
Her lifelong endeavour has been to improve access to education for all children.

Synset Examples

made an effort to cover all the reading material
wished him luck in his endeavor
she gave it a good try

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effort - a vigorous or determined attempt
  • Synonym: attempt - an act of trying to do something
  • Antonym: idle - not active or not engaged in work

Etymology

From Old French 'endevoir' (to make an effort), from 'en-' (in) + 'devoir' (duty), ultimately from Latin 'debere' (to owe).
