blunt

1. VERB

/ blʌnt / (AmE), / blʌnt / (BrE)
to make something less sharp, strong, or intense; to dull or reduce the force or effect of something
make less intense or sharp

Examples

The medication helped to blunt the pain after surgery.
His criticism was meant to blunt the enthusiasm of the supporters.
Repeated exposure to tragedy can blunt a person's emotional response.

Synset Examples

blunted emotions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dull - to make less sharp or intense

Etymology

From Old French *blunt* or *blont* (dull, blunt), of Germanic origin.
