unmelted

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈmɛltɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɛltɪd / (BrE)
not having been melted; still in a solid state
not melted; still solid

Examples

There were large patches of unmelted snow along the hiking trail.
The metal remained unmelted despite the intense heat of the fire.
After hours in the sun, only a few unmelted ice cubes were left in the glass.

Synset Examples

streets unpassable because of piles of unmelted snow

Related Terms

  • Antonym: melted - made or become liquid by heat

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'melted' (past participle of 'melt').
