gambit

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæm.bɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡæm.bɪt / (BrE)
An opening move in chess in which a player sacrifices a minor piece to gain a strategic advantage.
chess opening move involving a sacrifice

Examples

He sacrificed his pawn with a daring gambit at the beginning of the game.
Her clever opening gambit took her opponent by surprise.
The King's gambit is a classic strategy favored by many aggressive players.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strategy - a plan of action designed to achieve a long-term aim
  • Synonym: sacrifice - the act of giving up a piece in chess for advantage

Etymology

From Italian 'gambetto' (tripping up), from 'gamba' (leg).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡæm.bɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡæm.bɪt / (BrE)
an action or remark intended to gain an advantage, especially at the beginning of a process or conversation; in chess, an opening move in which a player risks a minor piece to gain a strategic advantage
tactic or strategy to gain advantage

Examples

His opening gambit in the debate caught his opponent off guard.
Sarah's joke was just a clever gambit to break the ice at the meeting.
The chess player's bold gambit paid off several moves later.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ploy - a cunning plan or action designed to turn a situation to one's own advantage
  • Synonym: strategy - a plan of action designed to achieve a long-term or overall aim
  • Antonym: countermove - an action taken to counteract another's maneuver

Etymology

From Italian *gambetto* (tripping up), related to chess terminology.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡæm.bɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡæm.bɪt / (BrE)
a remark or action made at the beginning of a conversation or activity, intended to gain an advantage or initiate discussion
opening remark or strategy for advantage

Examples

Her clever gambit at the start of the meeting caught everyone's attention.
He opened the negotiation with a friendly gambit to break the ice.
As a conversational gambit, his joke was surprisingly effective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ploy - a cunning plan or action designed to turn a situation to one's own advantage
  • Synonym: maneuver - a movement or series of moves requiring skill and care, often to gain advantage

Etymology

From Italian 'gambetto' (a tripping up), from 'gamba' (leg), originally used in chess.
