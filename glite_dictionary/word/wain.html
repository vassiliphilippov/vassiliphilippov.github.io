Wain

1. NOUN

/ weɪn / (AmE), / weɪn / (BrE)
a group of seven bright stars in the constellation Ursa Major, also known as the Plough or Big Dipper
group of stars in Ursa Major

Examples

The Wain is easily visible in the northern sky on a clear night.
Many people use the Wain to help navigate and find the North Star.
Ancient civilizations often told stories about the Wain and its place in the heavens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Big Dipper - a group of seven bright stars in Ursa Major, also called the Plough or Wain
  • Synonym: Plough - another name for the group of stars in Ursa Major known as the Wain or Big Dipper

Etymology

Old English 'wægen', meaning 'wagon' or 'cart'.
