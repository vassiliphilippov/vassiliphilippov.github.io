wain

1. NOUN

/ weɪn / (AmE), / weɪn / (BrE)
a large open farm wagon, typically used for carrying crops or hay
large open farm wagon
archaic

Examples

The farmer loaded the hay onto his wain at dawn.
In the distance, an old wooden wain creaked along the country road.
Villagers gathered to help repair the broken wain after the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wagon - a four-wheeled vehicle for carrying goods

Etymology

From Old English wægn (wagon), related to German Wagen.
