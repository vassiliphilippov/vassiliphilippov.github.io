dermatoglyphic

1. NOUN

/ dɚˌmætəˈɡlɪfɪk / (AmE), / ˌdɜːmətəˈɡlɪfɪk / (BrE)
the patterns of lines and ridges found on the skin of the fingertips, palms, and soles of the feet
patterns of skin lines and ridges
medical

Examples

Each person has a unique pattern of dermatoglyphic ridges on their fingertips.
Scientists analyze dermatoglyphic patterns to help identify genetic conditions.
The study of dermatoglyphic features is important in forensic investigations.

Etymology

From Greek 'derma' (skin) + 'glyphē' (carving).
