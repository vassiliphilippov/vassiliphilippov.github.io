conveying

1. NOUN

/ kənˈveɪ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈveɪ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of transferring the title of property from one person to another, especially in legal contexts
transfer of property title
legal

Examples

The conveying of the estate required several legal documents to be signed.
All fees associated with the conveying of property must be paid by the buyer.
They hired a solicitor to handle the conveying process for their new home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transfer - the act of moving something from one person or place to another
  • Antonym: retaining - the act of keeping possession of property

Etymology

From the verb 'convey', which is from Old French 'convoier' (to accompany, escort), from Medieval Latin 'conviare'.
