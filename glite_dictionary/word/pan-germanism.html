Pan-Germanism

1. NOUN

/ pæn ˈdʒɜːrməˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / pan ˈdʒɜːməˌnɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the political movement advocating the unification of all peoples speaking German or related Germanic languages
movement for unification of Germanic peoples

Examples

The rise of Pan-Germanism influenced the national policies of several European countries in the late 19th century.
Historians debate the long-term impact of Pan-Germanism on the First and Second World Wars.
His writings express his strong support for Pan-Germanism and a united German-speaking Europe.

Synset Examples

After all, Germanist concepts of identity were popular among segments of the radical right, as can be seen in the integral pan-Germanism of Georg von Schönerer and Arthur Seyss-Inquart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pangermanism - alternative spelling of Pan-Germanism

Etymology

From German 'pangermanismus', from 'pan-' (all) + 'German'.
