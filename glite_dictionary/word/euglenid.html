euglenid

1. NOUN

/ juˈɡliːnɪd / (AmE), / juˈɡliːnɪd / (BrE)
A single-celled organism found in marine and freshwater environments, often green or colorless, with one or more whip-like flagella for movement; belonging to the group Euglenida.
microscopic flagellate organism (Euglenida)
medical

Examples

A euglenid swims through the pond water with a whip-like motion of its flagellum.
Scientists studied the role of euglenids in nutrient cycling in freshwater lakes.
Some euglenid species can photosynthesize, while others are colorless and feed on organic matter.

Etymology

From New Latin Euglenida, based on the genus name Euglena.
