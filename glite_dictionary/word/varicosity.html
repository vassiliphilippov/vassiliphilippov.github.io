varicosity

1. NOUN

/ ˌvær.əˈkɑː.sə.ti / (AmE), / ˌvær.ɪˈkɒs.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
a condition in which a vein becomes swollen and twisted, commonly due to a varix or varicose veins
swollen, twisted vein condition
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully treated the varicosity in the patient's leg.
Chronic varicosities may require medical intervention to relieve discomfort.
A varicosity in the esophagus can be a serious medical condition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: varix - an abnormally dilated or swollen vein
  • Synonym: varicose - relating to or affected by varicose veins

Etymology

From Latin varicosus (full of dilated veins), from varix (swollen vein).
