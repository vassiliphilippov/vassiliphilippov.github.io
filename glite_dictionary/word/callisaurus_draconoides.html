Callisaurus draconoides

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlɪsəˈsɔrəs ˌdrækəˈnɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / kəˌlɪsəˈsɔːrəs ˌdrækəˈnɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A species of swift, long-legged lizard with a black-banded tail, native to desert regions of the United States and Mexico.
species of desert lizard native to U.S. and Mexico

Examples

The Callisaurus draconoides is well adapted to the arid landscapes of the southwestern United States.
Researchers observed a Callisaurus draconoides basking on a sunlit rock.
Because of its speed and agility, the Callisaurus draconoides easily escapes predators in the desert.

Etymology

From Greek 'kallis' (beautiful) + 'sauros' (lizard); 'draconoides' meaning 'dragon-like'.
