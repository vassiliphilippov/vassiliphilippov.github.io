to cumber

1. VERB

/ ˈkʌmbər / (AmE), / ˈkʌmbə / (BrE)
to hinder, obstruct, or make free movement difficult; to burden or weigh down
to hinder or weigh down

Examples

The heavy backpack will cumber you during the long hike.
She chose simple furniture so as not to cumber the small room.
Regulations can sometimes cumber progress rather than promote it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to encumber - to restrict or burden in a way that movement is difficult
  • Synonym: to impede - to delay or prevent by obstructing
  • Antonym: to facilitate - to make an action or process easy or easier

Etymology

From Old French *combrer* (to hinder), from *combre* (block, obstacle).
