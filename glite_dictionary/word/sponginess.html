sponginess

1. NOUN

/ ˈspʌndʒ.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈspʌndʒɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being soft, porous, and able to absorb liquids like a sponge
the quality of being soft and porous

Examples

The sponginess of the cake made it incredibly light and pleasant to eat.
You can test the sponginess of a mattress by pressing down on it with your hand.
After the heavy rain, the sponginess of the soil made it perfect for planting new seeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porosity - the quality of being porous
  • Antonym: firmness - the quality of being solid or rigid

Etymology

From 'spongy' + '-ness', where 'spongy' is from Latin 'spongia' (sponge).
