athetosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæθəˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌaθəˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a condition characterized by slow, continuous, involuntary writhing movements, especially of the hands and feet
involuntary writhing movements (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient exhibited clear signs of athetosis during the neurological examination.
Children with cerebral palsy sometimes display athetosis affecting their hands and facial muscles.
Athetosis can make writing and other fine motor tasks challenging.

Etymology

From Greek 'athetos' (unstable, not fixed).
