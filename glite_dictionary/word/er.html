Er

1. NOUN

/ ɝː / (AmE), / ɜː / (BrE)
a chemical element; a soft, silvery, trivalent metallic element of the lanthanide series (symbol: Er)
a metallic chemical element; symbol Er

Examples

The mineral sample contained a significant amount of Er among other rare earth elements.
Scientists use Er compounds in lasers and optical fibers due to their special properties.
He studied the magnetic characteristics of Er during his research on rare earth metals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yttrium - a metallic chemical element chemically related to erbium

Etymology

Named after Ytterby, a village in Sweden where its ore was first discovered.
