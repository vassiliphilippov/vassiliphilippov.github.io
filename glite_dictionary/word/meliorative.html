meliorative

1. ADJ

/ məˈliːjəˌreɪtɪv / (AmE), / mɪˈlɪərətɪv / (BrE)
having the tendency or effect of improving or making something better; ameliorative.
tending to improve or ameliorate

Examples

The organization implemented several meliorative policies to address poverty in the community.
Doctors sought a meliorative treatment to help improve the patient's quality of life.
City planners proposed meliorative measures to revitalize the neglected neighborhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ameliorative - having a tendency to make better; improving
  • Antonym: deteriorative - tending to make worse; causing deterioration

Etymology

From Latin 'meliorare' (to improve), from 'melior' (better).
