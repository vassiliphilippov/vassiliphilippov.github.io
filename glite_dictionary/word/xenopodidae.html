Xenopodidae

1. NOUN

/ ziˌnɑːpəˈdɪdiː / (AmE), / zɛnəʊˈpɒdɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic frogs, sometimes classified separately from Pipidae, including the genus Xenopus.
taxonomic family of aquatic frogs

Examples

The African clawed frog belongs to the family Xenopodidae.
Scientists debated whether Xenopodidae should be a distinct family or included in Pipidae.
Members of Xenopodidae are known for their fully aquatic lifestyles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pipidae - a family of tongueless aquatic frogs

Etymology

From Greek 'xenos' (foreign) and 'pous, podos' (foot), with taxonomic suffix '-idae' meaning 'family'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌzɛnəˈpɑːdɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌzɛnəʊˈpɒdɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic, tongueless frogs, including the genus Xenopus, sometimes separated from Pipidae in certain classifications.
family of tongueless aquatic frogs

Examples

The Xenopodidae are notable for their unique reproductive behaviors and adaptation to aquatic environments.
Within the order Anura, Xenopodidae is sometimes treated as a separate family from Pipidae.
Xenopus laevis, a common experimental species, belongs to the Xenopodidae family in some classifications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pipidae - family of primitive, tongueless aquatic frogs

Etymology

From Greek 'xenos' (strange) + 'pous' (foot), with scientific suffix '-idae'.
