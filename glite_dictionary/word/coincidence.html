coincidence

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈɪnsɪdəns / (AmE), / kəʊˈɪnsɪdəns / (BrE)
an accidental occurrence of events at the same time or in a way that seems planned, though it is not
accidental occurrence; seeming chance event

Examples

It was pure coincidence that we met at the airport on the same day.
Their birthdays falling on the same date was just a happy coincidence.
By a strange coincidence, they both wore identical outfits to the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accident - an event that happens by chance, without apparent cause
  • Antonym: plan - something arranged or intended in advance

Etymology

From Medieval Latin coincidentia, from Latin co- (together) + incidere (to fall).

2. NOUN

/ koʊˈɪnsɪdəns / (AmE), / kəʊˈɪnsɪdəns / (BrE)
the occurrence of two or more events at exactly the same time
two things happening at the same time

Examples

The experiment measured the coincidence of the two signals within microseconds.
A rare coincidence occurred when the alarms all rang at the exact same moment.
The coincidence of thunder and lightning is often just a matter of timing.

Synset Examples

the interval determining the coincidence gate is adjustable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simultaneity - the quality of occurring at the same time

Etymology

From Medieval Latin coincidentia (coincidence), from coincidere, from Latin com- (together) + incidere (to fall upon).

3. NOUN

/ koʊˈɪnsɪdəns / (AmE), / kəʊˈɪnsɪdəns / (BrE)
the state or fact of occupying exactly the same position or area in space
occupying the same position in space

Examples

In optics, the coincidence of two images allows for precise measurements.
The experiment depends on the exact coincidence of the beams at the detector.
A perfect coincidence between the grid lines and the mark confirmed the alignment.

Synset Examples

he waited for the coincidence of the target and the cross hairs

Etymology

From Medieval Latin coincidentia, from coincidere (to fall together).
