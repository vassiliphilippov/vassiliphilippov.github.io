Mitella diphylla

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈtɛlə dɪˈfɪlə / (AmE), / mɪˈtɛlə dɪˈfɪlə / (BrE)
A North American woodland plant of the saxifrage family, usually with two opposite leaves on upright stems ending in racemes of small white flowers; commonly known as miterwort.
a woodland plant of northeastern North America

Examples

The delicate flowers of Mitella diphylla brighten forest floors in the spring.
Botanists often study Mitella diphylla to understand plant diversity in northeastern woodlands.
Many hikers recognize Mitella diphylla by its distinctive pair of opposite leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miterwort - common name for Mitella diphylla, woodland plant with white flowers

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Mitella' meaning 'little mitre', and species epithet 'diphylla' meaning 'two-leaved'.
