effete

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈfiːt / (AmE), / ɪˈfiːt / (BrE)
lacking strength, vitality, or effectiveness; worn out or incapable of action or influence
lacking strength or effectiveness

Examples

Some critics argue that the party has become too effete to challenge its opponents effectively.
The effete aristocracy was out of touch with the needs of the working class.
After years of complacency, the organization was seen as increasingly effete and irrelevant.

Synset Examples

fundamentalism has flowered because it has concluded that liberalism is effete, ineffectual and impoverished

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ineffectual - lacking the ability to produce results; ineffective
  • Antonym: vigorous - strong, active, and energetic

Etymology

From Latin 'effētus' (worn out, exhausted).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈfit / (AmE), / ɪˈfiːt / (BrE)
affected, overrefined, or seen as unmanly or lacking in traditional masculine strength or vigor
affected or overrefined; effeminate

Examples

The critic described the poet's style as effete and lacking substance.
Some saw the aristocrat's mannerisms as excessively effete for their taste.
His speech struck many as effete, marked by a delicacy and elegance uncommon among his peers.

Synset Examples

the young man spoke in effete, accented English

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refined - elegant and cultured in appearance or manner
  • Antonym: virile - having qualities traditionally associated with masculinity

Etymology

From Latin 'effētus' (worn out, exhausted).
