grille

1. NOUN

/ ɡrɪl / (AmE), / ɡrɪl / (BrE)
a framework of metal bars or mesh placed at the front of a vehicle to allow air into the radiator while protecting it from debris
metal framework in front of a car radiator

Examples

The front grille of the car was damaged in the accident.
He carefully cleaned the grille to ensure the radiator had good airflow.
The new model features a prominent chrome grille for a striking appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grating - a frame of metal bars creating a barrier or opening

Etymology

From French 'grille' meaning 'grating', from Latin 'craticula' (little lattice).

2. NOUN

/ ɡrɪl / (AmE), / ɡrɪl / (BrE)
A small opening covered with a grating, often in a door or window, through which transactions or communications can take place.
small screened opening in a door or window

Examples

The teller spoke to me through the grille at the bank counter.
A metal grille in the office door allows staff to communicate with visitors securely.
She slipped the paperwork through the narrow grille built into the reception window.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screen - a mesh or grating that covers an opening

Etymology

From French 'grille', meaning 'grating' or 'grid', from Latin 'craticula' (small grate).
