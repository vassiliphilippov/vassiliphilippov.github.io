Kolkwitzia amabilis

1. NOUN

/ koʊlˈkwɪtsiə əˈmæbɪlɪs / (AmE), / kɒlˈkwɪtsiə əˈmabɪlɪs / (BrE)
A Chinese deciduous shrub with arching branches and clusters of pink, yellow-throated flowers, often grown as an ornamental plant.
Chinese ornamental shrub with pink flowers

Examples

The Kolkwitzia amabilis in our garden produces a mass of pink blooms each spring.
Gardeners often plant Kolkwitzia amabilis for its attractive arching branches and showy flowers.
After flowering, Kolkwitzia amabilis develops bristly fruit, adding interest to the landscape.

Etymology

Named after German botanist Richard Kolkwitz; 'amabilis' from Latin, meaning 'lovely' or 'pleasing'.
