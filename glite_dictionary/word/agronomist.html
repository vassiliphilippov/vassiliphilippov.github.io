agronomist

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡrɑː.nə.mɪst / (AmE), / əˈɡrɒn.ə.mɪst / (BrE)
a person who is an expert in the science of soil management and crop production
expert in crop and soil science

Examples

The agronomist recommended new techniques to improve the soil's fertility.
After years of study, she became a respected agronomist in her community.
Farmers often consult an agronomist when deciding which crops to plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agriculture - science or practice of farming

Etymology

From French agronomiste, from Greek agronomos (overseer of land).
