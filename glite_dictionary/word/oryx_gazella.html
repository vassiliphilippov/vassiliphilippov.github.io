Oryx gazella

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrɪks ɡəˈzɛlə / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪks ɡəˈzɛlə / (BrE)
a large South African antelope with long, straight horns and a broad black band along its flanks; also known as the gemsbok.
large South African antelope (gemsbok)

Examples

The Oryx gazella is well adapted to survive in arid environments with little water.
Wildlife photographers traveled to Namibia hoping to capture images of the elusive Oryx gazella.
The black stripe along its flank distinguishes the Oryx gazella from other antelopes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gemsbok - large antelope native to southern Africa, common name for Oryx gazella

Etymology

From scientific Latin, with 'Oryx' from Greek ὄρυξ (oryx, a type of antelope) and 'gazella' Latinized from Arabic ghazāl (gazelle).
