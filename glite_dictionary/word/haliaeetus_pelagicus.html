Haliaeetus pelagicus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæl.i.ˈiː.təs pə.ˈlæ.dʒɪ.kəs / (AmE), / ˌhæl.i.ˈiː.təs pə.ˈlæ.dʒɪ.kəs / (BrE)
A large species of sea eagle found along the coasts of the northwestern Pacific, known for its striking appearance and powerful build.
large sea eagle of the northwestern Pacific

Examples

The Haliaeetus pelagicus is one of the largest eagles in the world, noted for its massive yellow beak.
Birdwatchers traveled to eastern Russia to catch a glimpse of the rare Haliaeetus pelagicus.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the habitat of Haliaeetus pelagicus along the Pacific coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Steller's sea eagle - common name for Haliaeetus pelagicus

Etymology

From Greek haliaetos (sea eagle) and pelagicus (of the sea, marine).
