Briton

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪt̬.ən / (AmE), / ˈbrɪt.ən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Great Britain
native or inhabitant of Great Britain

Examples

Many Britons travel abroad during the summer holidays.
As a proud Briton, she celebrates her country's diverse culture.
The ancient Britons resisted Roman invasion for many years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: British - relating to Great Britain or its people

Etymology

From Latin 'Britto', 'Briton-', referring to a native of Britain.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪtən / (AmE), / ˈbrɪtən / (BrE)
a member of the Celtic people inhabiting southern Britain before and during the Roman period and before the Anglo-Saxon invasions
ancient inhabitant of Britain

Examples

Archaeologists have uncovered artifacts belonging to a pre-Roman Briton settlement.
The Romans encountered fierce resistance from the native Britons during their conquest.
Legends about King Arthur often refer to him as a proud Briton leader.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Celt - member of a group of Indo-European peoples including ancient Gauls, Britons, and others

Etymology

From Latin 'Britannus', from Ancient Greek 'Βρεττανός' (Brettanos).

Synset POS: n
