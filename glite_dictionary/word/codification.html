codification

1. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkəʊdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of arranging laws, rules, or information into a systematic and coherent code or system
process of systematizing laws or rules

Examples

The codification of these regulations helped ensure consistent enforcement across departments.
Legal scholars debated the merits of the proposed codification of customary laws.
The codification of procedures made the process more efficient and transparent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: systematization - the act of organizing something according to a system or a plan

Etymology

From Latin 'codificare' (to make a code), based on 'codex' (book of laws).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊ.də.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒd.ɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a systematic set of rules, principles, or laws, especially when written and arranged into a code
systematic set of written rules or laws

Examples

The codification of traffic laws has made driving much safer for everyone.
After years of debate, the parliament finally approved the codification of the nation's tax regulations.
Company policies underwent codification, producing a clear handbook for all employees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: code - a systematic collection of laws or regulations
  • Synonym: statute - a written law enacted by a legislative body

Etymology

From French codification, from Latin codex (book of laws) + facere (to make).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06680062-n
Synset POS: n
