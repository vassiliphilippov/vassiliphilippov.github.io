dit

1. NOUN

/ dɪt / (AmE), / dɪt / (BrE)
the shorter of the two signals used in Morse code, representing a dot in written form
short Morse code signal (dot)

Examples

The letter 'E' in Morse code is a single dit.
A sequence of dits and dahs forms every letter in Morse code.
He practiced tapping out dit-dah patterns on the desk.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dah - the longer signal in Morse code (dash)

Etymology

Imitative of the sound of a short Morse code signal; variation of 'dot'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ dɪt / (AmE), / dɪt / (BrE)
A logarithmic unit of information or entropy, based on base 10 logarithms, equal to one decimal digit.
logarithmic unit of information (base 10)

Examples

The storage system can hold up to a million dits of information.
A dit is similar to a bit, but it uses base 10 instead of base 2.
The entropy of the message was calculated as 4 dits.

Synset Examples

The ban pre-dates Shannon's use of bit as a unit of information by at least eight years, and remains in use in the early 21st Century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bit - a binary digit; unit of information (base 2)
  • Synonym: nat - unit of information based on natural logarithm (base e)

Etymology

From 'digit' (decimal digit), using the first three letters, by analogy with 'bit'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-92422125-n
Synset POS: n
