suprematism

1. NOUN

/ suˈprɛmətɪzəm / (AmE), / suˈpriːmətɪzəm / (BrE)
an early 20th-century Russian art movement focused on geometric abstraction, founded by Kazimir Malevich
Russian geometric abstractionist art movement

Examples

Many art historians consider suprematism a pivotal development in the history of abstract art.
The influence of suprematism can be seen in various forms of modern design and architecture.
Kazimir Malevich's painting 'Black Square' is one of the most iconic works of suprematism.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: constructivism - an artistic and architectural philosophy that originated in Russia in the 20th century

Etymology

From Russian супрематизм (suprematizm), coined by Kazimir Malevich from Latin supremus (highest, supreme).
