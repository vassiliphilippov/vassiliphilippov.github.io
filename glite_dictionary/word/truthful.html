truthful

1. ADJ

/ ˈtruθfəl / (AmE), / ˈtruːθf(ə)l / (BrE)
always telling the truth; not lying or cheating
always telling the truth; honest

Examples

She is known to be truthful even when it is difficult.
The witness gave a truthful account of the events.
We need leaders who are open and truthful with the public.

Synset Examples

a true statement
gave truthful testimony
a truthful person

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honest - telling the truth and not lying, cheating, or stealing
  • Antonym: dishonest - not honest; intending to mislead or cheat

Etymology

From truth + -ful, meaning 'full of truth'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtruθ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈtruːθ.fəl / (BrE)
always telling or expressing the truth; conforming to facts or reality
always telling the truth

Examples

She gave a truthful account of what happened during the meeting.
It is important to be truthful when answering questions on official forms.
His truthful nature made him a trusted friend.

Synset Examples

a truthful statement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere
  • Antonym: deceitful - not honest; misleading or lying

Etymology

From truth + -ful, formed in Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02470666-s
Synset POS: s
