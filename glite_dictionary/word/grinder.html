grinder

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraɪndər / (AmE), / ˈɡraɪndə / (BrE)
a machine used for crushing or grinding materials into smaller pieces or powder
machine for grinding materials

Examples

The coffee beans must be put through a grinder before brewing.
The factory invested in a new industrial grinder to process raw materials.
After a decade of use, the grinder needed some replacement parts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mill - a machine for grinding grain into flour

Etymology

From grind (to crush or pulverize) + -er (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡraɪndər / (AmE), / ˈɡraɪndə / (BrE)
a machine tool used for polishing or shaping metal surfaces by abrasion
machine tool for polishing metal

Examples

The technician used a grinder to smooth the rough edges of the metal sheet.
Always wear protective goggles when operating the grinder in the workshop.
A new bench grinder was installed to help with metal finishing tasks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sander - a machine or tool for smoothing or polishing surfaces, usually with sandpaper

Etymology

From Old English grindan (to grind) + -er (agent noun suffix).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡraɪndər / (AmE), / ˈɡraɪndə / (BrE)
a tooth with a broad crown used for grinding food, located behind the premolars; also called a molar
a molar tooth for grinding food

Examples

When her grinder cracked, she experienced severe pain while chewing.
Elephants have enormous grinders to break down tough plant material.
The dentist explained that wisdom teeth are the last grinders to emerge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: molar - a tooth with a broad chewing surface for grinding food

Etymology

From grind + -er, referring to its function of grinding food.

large sandwich with meats and cheese (U.S.)
american

Examples

I ordered a chicken parm grinder from the local deli for lunch.
He prefers his grinder loaded with extra onions and peppers.
In Connecticut, a grinder usually comes toasted and packed with Italian meats.

Related Terms
