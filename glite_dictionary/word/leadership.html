leadership

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːdərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈliːdəʃɪp / (BrE)
the ability to guide, direct, or influence people
the ability to lead others

Examples

Her leadership inspired the team to achieve their best results yet.
Effective leadership is crucial in times of crisis.
He took on a leadership role within the organization.

Synset Examples

he believed that leadership can be taught

Related Terms

  • Synonym: management - the process of dealing with or controlling things or people
  • Antonym: followership - the capacity or willingness to follow a leader

Etymology

From 'leader' + '-ship', formed in English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈliːdərʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈliːdəʃɪp / (BrE)
the action or ability of leading a group of people or an organization
the action of leading

Examples

Her leadership was crucial during the company's transition period.
Good leadership can motivate employees to achieve their best.
The team's success was a testament to his exceptional leadership.

Synset Examples

his leadership inspired the team

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guidance - the act of directing or advising
  • Synonym: authority - the power or right to give orders and make decisions
  • Antonym: followership - the act of following a leader

Etymology

Formed from 'leader' + '-ship', an Old English suffix denoting condition or quality.

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ˈlidɚˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈliːdəʃɪp / (BrE)
the position or status of being a leader; the role of leading a group, organization, or country
status or position of a leader

Examples

Her leadership of the company brought major changes and new growth.
The team questioned his leadership after a series of poor decisions.
She was praised for her leadership during the crisis.

Synset Examples

they challenged his leadership of the union

Related Terms

  • Synonym: command - the position of having authority or control, especially in a military context

Etymology

Formed from 'leader' + '-ship' (a suffix forming nouns denoting condition or status).

4. NOUN

/ ˈliːdərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈliːdəʃɪp / (BrE)
the group of people who direct or manage an organization, country, or other collective entity
the group of leaders of a group or organization

Examples

The leadership has called for a new strategy in response to the crisis.
Many employees disagree with the decisions of the current leadership.
After the election, a new leadership took charge of the organization.

Synset Examples

the national leadership adopted his plan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: management - the people who manage an organization or business
  • Synonym: administration - the group responsible for running an organization or government
  • Antonym: rank and file - the ordinary members of an organization as opposed to its leaders

Etymology

From leader + -ship, forming nouns denoting status, condition, or collective body.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
