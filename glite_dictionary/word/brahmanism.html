Brahmanism

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑː.mə.nɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈbrɑː.mə.nɪz.əm / (BrE)
The religious and social system developed in ancient India and based on the teachings of the Vedas, emphasizing the role and authority of the Brahmin priesthood.
ancient Indian religious and social system

Examples

Many ancient scriptures discuss the rituals of Brahmanism and its influence on Indian society.
Modern Hinduism evolved in part from the traditions of Brahmanism.
Brahmanism emphasizes the authority of the Vedas and the importance of the priestly caste.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Hinduism - the major religion and cultural system of South Asia

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Brāhmaṇa' meaning 'relating to Brahmins', combined with '-ism'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑːmənˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːmənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the ancient religious system of India based on the teachings of the Vedas, Brahmanas, and Upanishads, regarded as the precursor to modern Hinduism
ancient Indian religious system based on the Vedas

Examples

Brahmanism placed great emphasis on rituals and sacrifices performed by priests.
Many scholars consider Brahmanism to be a forerunner of modern Hinduism.
The Upanishads introduced new philosophical ideas into Brahmanism.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Hinduism - the major world religion that developed from Brahmanism

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'brāhmaṇa' (pertaining to Brahmans) + English '-ism'.
