eunuch

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːnək / (AmE), / ˈjuːnək / (BrE)
a man who has been castrated, especially one formerly employed as a guard or servant in a royal or noble household
man who has been castrated

Examples

In ancient courts, eunuchs often served as trusted advisers to rulers.
The emperor's harem was strictly guarded by eunuchs.
Historical records describe how eunuchs held significant power in some Asian empires.

Synset Examples

eunuchs guarded the harem

Etymology

From Latin 'eunuchus', from Ancient Greek 'eunoukhos' (guard of the bed).
