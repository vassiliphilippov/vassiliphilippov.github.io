Tephritidae

1. NOUN

/ tɛˈfrɪtɪˌdiː / (AmE), / tɛˈfrɪtɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A large family of fruit flies, many of which are pests of fruit and vegetable crops, and some are leaf miners.
family of fruit flies (insects)

Examples

Many species in the Tephritidae family are serious agricultural pests affecting fruit crops worldwide.
Scientists studied the lifecycle of Tephritidae to develop better pest control strategies.
Unlike the common vinegar fly, members of Tephritidae are known for their patterned wings.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on 'Tephritis' (type genus) + '-idae' (family suffix in zoological nomenclature).
