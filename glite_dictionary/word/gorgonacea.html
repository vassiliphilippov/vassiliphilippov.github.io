Gorgonacea

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡɔrɡəˈneɪsiə / (AmE), / ˌɡɔːɡəˈneɪsɪə / (BrE)
an order of colonial soft corals, including red corals and sea fans, found mainly in tropical and subtropical oceans
an order of soft corals (sea fans, red corals)

Examples

The Gorgonacea are known for their brightly colored sea fans that sway with ocean currents.
Marine biologists study Gorgonacea to learn more about coral reef ecosystems.
Some members of the Gorgonacea produce a hard skeleton used in jewelry.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek Gorgōn (a mythical being), inspired by the coral's appearance.
