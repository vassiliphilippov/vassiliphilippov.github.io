to polychromize

1. VERB

/ ˌpɑːliˈkroʊmaɪz / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈkrəʊmaɪz / (BrE)
to decorate or color something in several different colors; to make polychrome
color with several colors

Examples

The artisans expertly polychromized the columns to recreate the temple's original splendor.
Modern artists sometimes polychromize sculptures to give them a vibrant appearance.
During restoration, they carefully polychromized the ceiling, matching the historic color palette.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polychrome - to color or decorate with several colors

Etymology

From polychrome, after Greek 'polukhromos' (many-colored), plus -ize.
