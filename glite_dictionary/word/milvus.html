Milvus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlvəs / (AmE), / ˈmɪlvəs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Accipitridae, including the common European kites.
genus of birds (kites)

Examples

The genus Milvus is well known for its members' graceful flight and distinctive forked tails.
Several species within Milvus are widespread across Europe, Asia, and Africa.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of Milvus milvus, the red kite, in detail.

Etymology

From Latin *milvus* (kite, a type of bird of prey).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlvəs / (AmE), / ˈmɪlvəs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the hawk family, including the common European kites.
genus of birds including European kites

Examples

The red kite, a species within the genus Milvus, is native to Europe and parts of North Africa.
Ornithologists recently studied migration patterns of Milvus across Western Europe.
Both the black kite and the red kite belong to the genus Milvus.

Etymology

From Latin *milvus* (kite).
