gay

1. ADJ

/ ɡeɪ / (AmE), / ɡeɪ / (BrE)
Sexually attracted to people of one's own sex; relating to or arousing homosexual desires.
homosexual; relating to same-sex attraction

Examples

He came out as gay during his college years.
The film is celebrated for its honest portrayal of a gay relationship.
He felt more comfortable after joining a gay community group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homosexual - Sexually attracted to members of the same sex
  • Antonym: heterosexual - Sexually attracted to members of the opposite sex
  • Degree Lower: bisexual - Sexually attracted to both men and women

Etymology

Originally meant 'joyful' or 'carefree'; sense of 'homosexual' emerged in the mid-20th century in English.

2. NOUN

/ ɡeɪ / (AmE), / ɡeɪ / (BrE)
a person who is sexually attracted to people of the same sex, especially a man who is attracted to other men
person attracted to same sex

Examples

Many gays fought for equal rights in the 20th century.
He came out as gay to his family last year.
Pride parades celebrate the rights and identity of the gay community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lesbian - a woman who is sexually attracted to other women
  • Antonym: heterosexual - a person attracted to people of the opposite sex
  • Synonym: bisexual - a person attracted to both men and women

Etymology

From Old French 'gai' (joyful, merry), which ultimately derives from a Germanic source. The sense referring to homosexuality emerged in the 20th century.
