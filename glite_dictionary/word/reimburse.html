to reimburse

1. VERB

/ ˌriːɪmˈbɝːs / (AmE), / ˌriːɪmˈbɜːs / (BrE)
to pay back money to someone who has spent or lost it, especially for costs or losses incurred
to pay back money spent or lost

Examples

The company will reimburse you for your travel expenses.
I was reimbursed after submitting the medical bills to my insurance.
Please remember to reimburse any costs you pay on behalf of the organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to compensate - to give someone something, often money, in recognition of loss or expense
  • Synonym: to repay - to pay back money that was borrowed or owed

Etymology

From Late Latin 'reimborsare', from Latin 're-' (back) + 'in' (in) + 'bursa' (purse).

2. VERB

/ ˌriːɪmˈbɝːs / (AmE), / ˌriːɪmˈbɜːs / (BrE)
to pay back money to someone who has spent or lost it on your behalf
pay back money spent or lost

Examples

The company will reimburse you for your travel expenses.
Employees are required to submit receipts to reimburse costs incurred during business trips.
If you pay for the materials, we will reimburse the full amount within two weeks.

Synset Examples

Can the company reimburse me for my professional travel?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to repay - to pay back money owed
  • Synonym: to compensate - to give money or something else to make up for loss or service

Etymology

From Latin 'reimborsare', from 're-' (back) + 'imborsare' (to put into a purse).
