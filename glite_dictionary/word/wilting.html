wilting

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪltɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪltɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of becoming limp and drooping, especially as a result of heat or lack of water
process of becoming limp or drooping

Examples

The wilting of the flowers was caused by the intense afternoon sun.
Signs of wilting appeared after the plants went without water for several days.
During the drought, farmers noted severe wilting in their crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wilt - to become limp through heat, loss of water, or disease
  • Synonym: drooping - the act of hanging down limply
