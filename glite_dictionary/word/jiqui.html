jiqui

1. NOUN

/ hiˈki / (AmE), / hiˈki / (BrE)
A Cuban timber tree (often Krugiodendron ferreum) known for its hard, moisture-resistant wood.
Cuban timber tree with hard, durable wood

Examples

The dense wood of the jiqui makes it highly valued in Cuban carpentry and construction.
Botanists studied the jiqui to learn more about its resistance to tropical humidity.
Furniture made from jiqui is renowned for lasting many years in harsh climates.

Etymology

From Cuban Spanish 'jiquí', likely of indigenous Taíno origin.
