falchion

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæl.tʃən / (AmE), / ˈfɔːl.tʃən / (BrE)
a short, broad, slightly curved medieval sword with a sharp point, used especially in Europe
a short, broad medieval sword

Examples

The knight drew his falchion and prepared for battle.
Collectors prize the falchion for its unique shape and historical significance.
Legends often mention warriors wielding a broad falchion in single combat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sword - a weapon with a long metal blade

Etymology

From Old French 'fauchon', probably from 'faucher' (to mow or cut), from Latin 'falx' (sickle).
