coachbuilder

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊtʃˌbɪldər / (AmE), / ˈkəʊtʃˌbɪldə / (BrE)
a skilled person or company that makes the bodies of motor vehicles, especially by hand
person or company making vehicle bodies

Examples

The coachbuilder crafted a custom body for the classic car restoration.
Many luxury vehicles were produced by renowned coachbuilders in the early twentieth century.
After the invention of the motorcar, the role of the coachbuilder evolved to suit new designs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bodymaker - a person who makes the bodywork for vehicles

Etymology

Formed from 'coach' (carriage) + 'builder'; originally referred to makers of horse-drawn carriages.
