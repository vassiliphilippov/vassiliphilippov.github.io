lifesaving

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪfˌseɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪfˌseɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
the skill or practice of saving people from drowning or from other life-threatening situations, especially in water
saving people from drowning or danger

Examples

He took a course in lifesaving at the local pool.
Proper lifesaving techniques can mean the difference between life and death.
The beach requires all staff to be certified in lifesaving.

Synset Examples

he took a course in lifesaving

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rescue - the act of saving someone from danger or distress
  • Synonym: lifeguard - a person employed to rescue swimmers in danger

Etymology

From 'life' + 'saving', meaning saving of life.
