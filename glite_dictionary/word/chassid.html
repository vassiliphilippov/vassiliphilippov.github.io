Chassid

1. NOUN

/ xɑːˈsid / (AmE), / xɑːˈsid / (BrE)
a member of a Jewish sect (Hasidism) that observes a form of strict Orthodox Judaism, emphasizing mysticism, religious fervor, and close-knit community life
member of strict Jewish sect (Hasidism)

Examples

He became a Chassid after spending several years studying in Eastern Europe.
The Chassid greeted everyone with a warm smile and invited them to the Sabbath table.
Many Chassidim live in close-knit communities focused on tradition and spirituality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hasid - variant spelling: a member of Hasidic Judaism

Etymology

From Hebrew ḥasid (pious), referring to a pious person; the Hasidic movement arose in 18th-century Eastern Europe.
