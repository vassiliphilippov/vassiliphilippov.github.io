crimp

1. NOUN

/ krɪmp / (AmE), / krɪmp / (BrE)
a lock of hair that has been artificially waved or curled
artificially curled lock of hair

Examples

She wore her hair in tight crimps for the party.
The stylist added several crimps to give her hair more volume.
After removing the braids, small crimps remained throughout her hair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curl - a piece of hair that forms a curved or spiral shape
  • Synonym: wave - a loose, curved shape made in hair
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ krɪmp / (AmE), / krɪmp / (BrE)
to press or pinch together material so that it forms ridges or a wavy edge
to pinch into ridges or waves

Examples

She carefully crimped the edges of the pie crust to seal in the filling.
The metal is crimped to create a secure joint that will not come apart.
After braiding her hair, she crimped the ends with small rubber bands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pleat - to fold fabric into pleats
  • Antonym: to flatten - to make something flat and smooth

Etymology

From Middle English 'crympen', from Old English 'crympan' (to bend, curl).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ krɪmp / (AmE), / krɪmp / (BrE)
a person who tricks or coerces men into joining a ship's crew or the military, often by illegal or deceptive means
one who forces men into naval or military service
archaic

Examples

Many sailors avoided the harbor bars to escape the attention of the notorious crimp.
The crimp lured young men aboard ships with false promises and strong drink.
Historical accounts describe how a crimp might conscript men while they were unconscious.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: press-gang - a group authorized to force men into naval service

Etymology

Probably from Low German *krimp* (meaning 'curved, bent'), related to the sense of forcing or constraining.

Examples

You can see a slight crimp where the paper was folded.
The metal wire holds better after a crimp has been applied.
