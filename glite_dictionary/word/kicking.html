kicking

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of delivering a blow or striking something with the foot, especially in sports or physical activity
the act of striking with the foot

Examples

The team's kicking dramatically improved after the new coach arrived.
He scored the winning goal with a precise piece of kicking.
Her kicking was praised by everyone on the soccer team.

Synset Examples

he gave the ball a powerful kick
the team's kicking was excellent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kick - a blow with the foot; the action of striking with the foot

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
a rhythmic thrusting movement of the legs, especially as part of swimming or exercise routines
rhythmic leg movement in swimming or exercise

Examples

She improved her kicking technique to swim faster in freestyle races.
The aerobics instructor demonstrated the proper form for kicking during the warm-up.
Strong kicking is essential for effective propulsion in the butterfly stroke.

Synset Examples

the kick must be synchronized with the arm movements
the swimmer's kicking left a wake behind him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kick - to strike out with the foot or feet
