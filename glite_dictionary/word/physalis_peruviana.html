Physalis peruviana

1. NOUN

/ faɪˈseɪlɪs pəˌruːviˈænə / (AmE), / fɪˈseɪlɪs pəˌruːviˈænə / (BrE)
an annual plant native to tropical South America, cultivated for its small edible orange-yellow fruits, also known as Cape gooseberry or goldenberry
plant with edible orange-yellow fruits

Examples

The farmer planted rows of Physalis peruviana to harvest its sweet fruits in the summer.
Many health food stores now carry dried Physalis peruviana as a nutritious snack.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of Physalis peruviana extract.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Physalis' (from Greek physalis, bladder) and 'peruviana' (of Peru).
