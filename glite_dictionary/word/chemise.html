chemise

1. NOUN

/ ʃəˈmiz / (AmE), / ʃəˈmiːz / (BrE)
A woman's loose, sleeveless undergarment, typically reaching to the knees or lower and worn as underwear or a nightdress.
woman's loose sleeveless undergarment

Examples

She wore a silky chemise beneath her dress for added comfort.
The delicate lace chemise hung in her wardrobe, waiting for a special occasion.
In the hot summer months, she preferred to sleep in a light chemise instead of pajamas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slip - a women's undergarment worn under a dress or skirt

Etymology

From Old French 'chemise' (shirt), from Late Latin 'camisia' (shirt, nightgown).

2. NOUN

/ ʃəˈmiz / (AmE), / ʃəˈmiːz / (BrE)
a loose-fitting dress that hangs straight from the shoulders and does not have a fitted waist
loose, waistless dress

Examples

The designer's new collection features a stunning chemise made of silk.
She chose a light blue chemise for the summer party because it was comfortable and stylish.
In the 1920s, the chemise dress became popular for its simple, unstructured silhouette.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shift - a simple, loose-fitting undergarment or dress

Etymology

From Old French 'chemise' (shirt), from Latin 'camisia' (shirt, tunic).
