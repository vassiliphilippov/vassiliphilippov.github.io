glowering

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡlaʊ.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡlaʊ.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
looking or feeling angry or sullen; showing a dark, brooding, or menacing expression or mood.
showing an angry or sullen expression

Examples

He was glowering at his coworkers during the entire meeting, making everyone uncomfortable.
The glowering sky threatened rain as we walked to school.
She shot a glowering look at her brother after he interrupted her again.

Synset Examples

a dark scowl
the proverbially dour New England Puritan
a glum, hopeless shrug
he sat in moody silence
a morose and unsociable manner
a saturnine, almost misanthropic young genius
a sour temper
a sullen crowd

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scowling - showing a displeased or angry expression; frowning intensely
  • Antonym: beaming - smiling radiantly; having a cheerful expression

Etymology

Present participle of 'glower', from Middle English 'gloren' (to stare), probably of Scandinavian origin.
