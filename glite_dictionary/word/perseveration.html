perseveration

1. NOUN

/ pərˌsɛvɚˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˌsɛvəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
The involuntary and inappropriate repetition or continuation of a particular response, such as a word, phrase, or behavior, even when it is no longer suitable.
involuntary repetition of a behavior or response
medical

Examples

The patient's perseveration made therapy sessions more challenging for the medical staff.
Children with certain developmental disorders may display perseveration when answering questions.
His perseveration on the same topic frustrated both teachers and classmates.

Synset Examples

his perseveration continued to the point where it was no longer appropriate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perseverance - persistent determination to achieve a goal

Etymology

From Latin perseveratio (steadfastness), from perseverare (to persist).

2. NOUN

/ pərˌsɛv.əˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / pəˌsɜː.vəˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the persistent recurrence or repetition of an idea, thought, or response, usually without a new stimulus, often seen as a symptom in certain mental or neurological conditions
persistent repetition of an idea or response
medical

Examples

Patients with frontal lobe damage may display perseveration, repeating the same action even when it is no longer appropriate.
The therapist noted persistent perseveration on the same topic during the session.
In some childhood developmental disorders, perseveration can manifest as repeated questions or statements.

Etymology

From Latin perseveratio (steadfastness), from perseverare (to persist).

3. NOUN

/ pərˌsɜrvəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˌsɜːvəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the uncontrollable and repetitive fixation on a particular thought, idea, or subject, often to the detriment of other thinking or progress
repetitive, uncontrollable fixation on a subject

Examples

His perseveration on the issue prevented progress.
The psychologist noted significant perseveration in the patient's speech patterns.
Despite advice to move on, her perseveration over the mistake distracted her for weeks.

Synset Examples

His perseveration on the issue prevented progress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fixation - an obsessive attachment to a particular thing
  • Synonym: obsession - an idea or thought that continually preoccupies a person's mind

Etymology

From Latin perseveratio (steadfastness, persistence), from perseverare (to persist).
