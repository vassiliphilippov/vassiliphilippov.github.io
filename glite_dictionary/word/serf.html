serf

1. NOUN

/ sɝf / (AmE), / sɜːf / (BrE)
In the Middle Ages, a member of the lowest feudal class, bound to work on a lord's estate and unable to leave without permission.
medieval land-bound peasant

Examples

During the Middle Ages, a serf could not leave the land without the lord's consent.
Most serfs lived in small villages and farmed the lord's fields.
The life of a serf was harsh and full of hard labor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slave - a person who is the legal property of another and forced to obey them
  • Antonym: freeman - a person who is not a serf or slave; a person with full rights

Etymology

From Old French 'serf', from Latin 'servus' (slave).
