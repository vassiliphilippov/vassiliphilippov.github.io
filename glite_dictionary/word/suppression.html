suppression

1. NOUN

/ səˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / səˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
the act of preventing a book or piece of writing from being published or distributed
withholding publication or circulation

Examples

The government ordered the suppression of the controversial book.
Police justified the suppression of the article on the grounds of national security.
There was public outrage over the suppression of independent newspapers during the crisis.

Synset Examples

a suppression of the newspaper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: censorship - the control or banning of speech or writing considered objectionable

Etymology

From Latin suppressio (a pressing down), from supprimere (to suppress).

3. NOUN

/ səˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / səˈprɛʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act of preventing something from being expressed, known, or practiced, especially by force or authority
act of forcefully preventing or stopping

Examples

The government ordered the suppression of the radical newspaper after it published dissenting opinions.
Harsh suppression of protests often leads to further unrest among the population.
The suppression of cultural traditions can have lasting negative effects on a community.

Synset Examples

the suppression of heresy
the quelling of the rebellion
the stifling of all dissent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repression - the action of subduing something or someone by force
  • Antonym: encouragement - the act of giving support or confidence to something

Etymology

From Latin 'suppressio', from 'supprimere' meaning 'to press down'.
