inapplicability

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnəˌplɪkəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪnəˌplɪkəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the state or quality of not being applicable or relevant to a particular matter
the state of not being applicable

Examples

The lawyer argued the inapplicability of the law to the specific circumstances of the case.
Due to the inapplicability of those rules, we had to find an alternative solution.
Her criticism was based on the inapplicability of the theory to real-world situations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irrelevance - the quality of not being connected with or relevant to something
  • Antonym: applicability - the quality of being relevant or appropriate

Etymology

From in- (not) + applicability, from Latin applicabilis (that may be applied).
