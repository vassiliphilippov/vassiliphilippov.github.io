raetam

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪtəm / (AmE), / ˈreɪtəm / (BrE)
A desert shrub native to Syria and Arabia, with small white flowers, regarded as the 'juniper' mentioned in the Old Testament; sometimes classified under the genus Genista.
a Middle Eastern desert shrub with white flowers

Examples

The raetam is mentioned several times in ancient biblical texts.
Nomads often sought shade under the raetam during long journeys across the desert.
Botanists sometimes debate whether raetam belongs to the genus Genista or a different group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juniper - evergreen shrub or tree with berry-like cones

Etymology

From Arabic raṭam, used in biblical references to a desert shrub.
