to compose

1. VERB

/ kəmˈpoʊz / (AmE), / kəmˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to create or formulate plans, policies, or the basic details for something
formulate or create plans/details

Examples

She was asked to compose a strategy for the new project.
The committee will compose a policy to address these issues.
Before implementation, it's important to compose the basic details of the plan.

Synset Examples

frame a policy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to formulate - to create or devise methodically
  • Synonym: devise - to plan or invent by careful thought

Etymology

From Latin componere, composit-, meaning 'to put together'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00708633-v
Synset POS: v

/ kəmˈpoʊz / (AmE), / kəmˈpəʊz / (BrE)

Related Terms

/ kəmˈpoʊz / (AmE), / kəmˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to put together by arranging existing materials or parts, especially in writing or creating a list, document, or work
assemble from existing material

Examples

She sat down to compose a letter to her friend using notes she had collected.
We need to compose a comprehensive list of all the items required for the trip.
The editor asked the team to compose an article from the available research materials.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to compile - to collect and assemble from various sources
  • Synonym: to assemble - to bring together or gather into one place

Etymology

From Latin componere (to put together, arrange), from com- (together) + ponere (to place).
