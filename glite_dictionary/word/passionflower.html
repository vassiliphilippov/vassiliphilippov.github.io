passionflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæʃ.ənˌflaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈpæʃ.ənˌflaʊ.ə / (BrE)
A climbing plant of the chiefly tropical American genus Passiflora, some species of which have showy flowers and edible fruit.
tropical climbing plant with showy flowers

Examples

The garden was filled with the vibrant blooms of the passionflower climbing along the trellis.
Some species of passionflower produce fruit that can be eaten fresh or made into juice.
She planted a passionflower vine to attract butterflies to her backyard.

Etymology

From 'passion' (reference to the Passion of Christ) + 'flower'; so named because parts of the flower were seen by early missionaries as symbols of the Crucifixion.
