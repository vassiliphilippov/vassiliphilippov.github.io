phonics

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑː.nɪks / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.nɪks / (BrE)
a method of teaching people to read by training them to associate letters or groups of letters with the sounds they represent
teaching reading by letter-sound association

Examples

Many schools use phonics to help children learn to read and write.
The phonics approach focuses on the relationship between letters and sounds.
Teachers are trained in phonics instruction to support early literacy skills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonetics - the study and classification of speech sounds

Etymology

From Greek phōnē (sound, voice) + -ics (science or study of).
