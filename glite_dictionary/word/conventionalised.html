conventionalised

1. ADJ

/ kənˈvɛnʃənəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / kənˈvɛnʃ(ə)nəlaɪzd / (BrE)
using established artistic forms or conventions rather than appearing natural or spontaneous; stylized.
following artistic conventions; stylized
british

Examples

The play was performed in a highly conventionalised manner, emphasizing gesture over realism.
Traditional Japanese art often uses conventionalised patterns and motifs instead of depicting nature realistically.
Her painting style is deliberately conventionalised, echoing centuries-old artistic techniques.

Synset Examples

a stylized mode of theater production

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stylized - using a conventional style instead of appearing natural
  • Antonym: natural - not following conventions; spontaneous and lifelike

Etymology

From 'convention' + '-alise' (British spelling of '-alize'), meaning to make conform to convention.
