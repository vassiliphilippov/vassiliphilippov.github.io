Marsileaceae

1. NOUN

/ mɑːrˌsɪliˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / mɑːˌsɪliˈiːsiː / (BrE)
a family of aquatic and semi-aquatic ferns, commonly known as the pepperwort or water-clover family
family of aquatic ferns (pepperwort)

Examples

The Marsileaceae family includes aquatic ferns that are often found in wetlands and ponds.
Some researchers study the reproduction of Marsileaceae to understand heterosporous fern evolution.
Many members of the Marsileaceae display clover-shaped leaves, which help distinguish them from other ferns.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Marsilea, named after Italian naturalist Luigi Ferdinando Marsili (1658–1730), with the suffix '-aceae' indicating a plant family.
