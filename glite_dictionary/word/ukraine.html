Ukraine

1. PROPN

/ juˈkreɪn / (AmE), / juˈkreɪn / (BrE)
A country in eastern Europe, formerly part of the Soviet Union, known as the center of the original Russian state in the ninth century.
country in eastern Europe

Examples

Ukraine declared its independence from the Soviet Union in 1991.
Many tourists visit Ukraine to experience its rich culture and history.
The conflict in eastern Ukraine has attracted global attention.

Etymology

From Ukrainian Україна (Ukrayina), meaning 'borderland.'
