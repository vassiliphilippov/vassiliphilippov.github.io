striker

1. NOUN

/ ˈstraɪkər / (AmE), / ˈstraɪkə / (BrE)
the part of a mechanical device that delivers a blow to make something happen, such as firing a gun or ringing a bell
part of a device that strikes

Examples

The striker in the clock hit the bell precisely at noon.
If the striker of the gun malfunctions, it may not fire correctly.
Watchmakers must carefully fit the striker to ensure the chime rings at the right time.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstraɪkər / (AmE), / ˈstraɪkə / (BrE)
an employee who is taking part in a strike against their employer
employee participating in a labor strike

Examples

The union's strikers demanded improved working conditions and higher pay.
After a week, most strikers returned to their jobs when a new contract was signed.
Management hired temporary workers to replace the strikers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: scab - a person who works while others are on strike

Etymology

From 'strike' (to stop work as protest) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

5. NOUN

/ ˈstraɪkər / (AmE), / ˈstraɪkə / (BrE)
a player whose main role is to score goals for their team in football (soccer)
attacking football player

Examples

The team signed a new striker to improve their chances of scoring goals.
As the leading striker, she scored 25 goals last season.
The coach praised the striker for his decisive goal in the final match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forward - an attacking player in football or hockey
  • Antonym: goalkeeper - the player who defends the goal in football

Etymology

From English 'strike' (to hit), referring to striking or scoring goals.
