rapper

1. NOUN

/ ˈræpər / (AmE), / ˈræpə / (BrE)
a person who performs rap music, typically as a vocalist
person who performs rap music

Examples

The rapper impressed the crowd with his freestyle performance on stage.
She dreams of becoming a famous rapper and writing her own lyrics.
Several well-known rappers have collaborated on the new album.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: MC - master of ceremonies or rapper in hip-hop music

Etymology

From 'rap' (music genre) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10527075-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈræpər / (AmE), / ˈrapə / (BrE)
a metal device attached to a door, typically hinged, used for knocking to announce one's presence
metal device for knocking on a door

Examples

He lifted the heavy rapper and struck it against the door to announce his arrival.
The ornate brass rapper gleamed in the sunlight, catching the attention of visitors.
She reached out and tapped the rapper gently, not wanting to make too much noise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: door knocker - a device used for knocking on a door

Etymology

From 'rap' (to strike or knock) + -er, denoting one who or that which performs an action.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03632051-n
Synset POS: n
