speculator

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛkjəˌleɪtər / (AmE), / ˈspɛkjʊleɪtə / (BrE)
a person who forms theories or conjectures without sufficient evidence or knowledge of the facts
one who guesses without sufficient evidence

Examples

The speculator offered a wild theory about the missing painting without any real evidence.
As a speculator, she often drew conclusions that later proved completely wrong.
You can't trust every speculator you hear on the radio; some just love to stir up rumors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guesser - a person who makes guesses
  • Antonym: analyst - a person who examines or studies something in detail

Etymology

From Latin speculātōr (observer, spy), from speculārī (to observe), from specere (to look at).

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɛkjəˌleɪtər / (AmE), / ˈspɛkjʊleɪtə / (BrE)
a person who invests in stocks, property, or other ventures, taking significant risks in the hope of making large profits.
person who takes financial risks for potential gain

Examples

The real estate speculator bought several properties hoping to sell them at a much higher price.
Some accuse the speculator of driving up food prices during a shortage.
As a speculator, she understood the risks but was drawn by the promise of high rewards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investor - a person who puts money into ventures for profit, often considered less risky than a speculator
  • Synonym: gambler - a person who takes risky chances for financial or other gain
  • Antonym: conservative investor - a person who avoids high risks and prefers steady, safe returns

Etymology

From Latin speculator (observer, spy), from speculari (to observe), from specula (watchtower).
