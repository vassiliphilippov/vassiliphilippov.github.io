assembly

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɛmbli / (AmE), / əˈsɛmbli / (BrE)
a gathering of people, especially for a particular purpose such as a meeting, event, or the exercise of rights
gathering of people for a purpose

Examples

The activists called for an assembly to discuss the environmental concerns in the community.
Freedom of assembly is considered a fundamental human right in many countries.
The school held a special assembly to honor outstanding students.

Synset Examples

they demanded the right of assembly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meeting - an event where people gather to discuss matters or make decisions

Etymology

From Old French assemblee (a gathering), from assembler (to assemble), from Latin assimilare (to make like, gather together).

/ əˈsɛm.bli / (AmE), / əˈsɛm.bli / (BrE)
a unit made by fitting together various parts or components, especially in machinery or construction
unit of fitted-together components

Examples

Each car is made from hundreds of individual assemblies that are put together on the production line.
The technician checked the assembly to ensure all the screws were tightened properly.
Before shipping, the factory tests each assembly for defects and correct operation.

Related Terms

Etymology

6. NOUN

/ əˈsɛmbli / (AmE), / əˈsɛmbli / (BrE)
a group of machine parts that are put together to form a self-contained unit
group of connected machine parts

Examples

The engine assembly was carefully lowered into the car's chassis by the technicians.
Each printer contains a fuser assembly that heats and bonds the toner to the paper.
Before shipping, the manufacturer inspects every gear assembly for quality and performance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: component - a single part of a machine or device
  • Degree Higher: system - a set of connected things or parts forming a complex whole

Etymology

From Old French *asemblee* (gathering), from assembler (to assemble), ultimately from Latin *assimulare* (to bring together).
