Siegfried

1. PROPN

/ ˈsiːɡˌfriːd / (AmE), / ˈsiːɡfriːd / (BrE)
A legendary German warrior hero of the Nibelungenlied, known for slaying a dragon, claiming the Nibelungs' treasure, awakening Brynhild, and meeting a tragic end.
hero of the German legend Nibelungenlied

Examples

The legend of Siegfried slaying the dragon has inspired countless storytellers throughout history.
In the opera by Wagner, Siegfried undergoes a journey of heroic feats and tragic fate.
Many scholars compare Siegfried with his Norse counterpart, Sigurd, in terms of mythological significance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sigurd - Norse counterpart of Siegfried in Scandinavian mythology

Etymology

From German Siegfried, composed of 'Sieg' (victory) and 'Fried' (peace).
