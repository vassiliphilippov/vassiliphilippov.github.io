Graptophyllum

1. NOUN

/ ɡræp.təˈfɑː.ləm / (AmE), / ɡræp.təˈfɒl.əm / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs in the acanthus family, known as the caricature plant, native to Australia and nearby tropical regions.
genus of evergreen shrubs (caricature plant)

Examples

The Graptophyllum in our garden produces lush green leaves throughout the year.
Botanists often study the Graptophyllum for its unique variegated foliage.
Many gardeners appreciate Graptophyllum as an ornamental shrub in tropical landscapes.

Etymology

From Greek 'graptos' (marked, written) and 'phyllon' (leaf) for leaf markings.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡræptəˈfɒləm / (AmE), / ˌɡraptəˈfɒlɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical flowering plants in the family Acanthaceae, including the caricature plant.
a genus of tropical flowering plants

Examples

The Graptophyllum thrives in tropical gardens due to its attractive foliage.
Botanists identified the shrub as a member of the Graptophyllum genus.
Some species of Graptophyllum are cultivated for their ornamental leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caricature plant - common name for Graptophyllum pictum, a species in this genus

Etymology

From Greek 'graptos' (marked, painted) and 'phyllon' (leaf).
