Epacris obtusifolia

1. NOUN

/ iˈpækrɪs ɑːbˌtjuːsɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ɪˈpakrɪs ɒbˌtjuːsɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A small, upright shrub native to Australia and Tasmania, known for its fragrant, ivory-colored flowers.
Australian shrub with ivory flowers

Examples

The Epacris obtusifolia blooms in late winter, adding fragrance to the Australian bushland.
Botanists studied the Epacris obtusifolia due to its resilience in wet heathlands.
Gardeners prize Epacris obtusifolia for its delicate ivory flowers and evergreen foliage.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Epacris', a genus of flowering plants, and 'obtusifolia', meaning 'blunt-leaved'.
