transmitter

1. NOUN

/ trænˈsmɪtər / (AmE), / trænzˈmɪtə / (BrE)
an animal, person, or microorganism that carries and spreads a disease from one individual or organism to another
organism or agent that spreads disease

Examples

In many tropical regions, mosquitoes are the primary transmitters of malaria to humans.
The study identified rats as potential transmitters of the new virus strain.
Controlling insect populations can reduce the risk of transmitter-borne diseases.

Synset Examples

mosquitos are vectors of malaria and yellow fever
fleas are vectors of the plague
aphids are transmitters of plant diseases
when medical scientists talk about vectors they are usually talking about insects

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vector - an organism, typically an insect, that transmits a pathogen

Etymology

From Latin 'transmittere' (to send across).

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ trænzˈmɪtɚ / (AmE), / trænzˈmɪtə / (BrE)
a person who sends or conveys a message to another; the sender in a communication process
person who sends a message

Examples

In any conversation, the transmitter carefully chooses words to ensure the message is clear.
The misunderstanding occurred because the transmitter failed to provide enough context.
A successful transmitter tailors information to the needs of the audience.

Synset Examples

return to sender

Related Terms

  • Antonym: receiver - person who receives a message

Etymology

From transmit + -er, meaning one who transmits.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10598214-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ trænzˈmɪt̬ɚ / (AmE), / trænzˈmɪtə / (BrE)
a device that sends out radio or television signals to be received by receivers at a distance
device for sending radio or TV signals

Examples

The radio transmitter is located on the top of the hill for better signal coverage.
Engineers repaired the broken transmitter to restore television broadcasts in the area.
Every television station relies on a reliable transmitter to reach its viewers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: receiver - device that receives radio or TV signals

Etymology

From Latin 'transmittere' (to send across, transmit) + English '-er' (agent noun suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04479961-n
Synset POS: n
