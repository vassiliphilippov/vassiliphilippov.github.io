Pholiota aurea

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈlaɪ.ə tə ɔːˈriː.ə / (AmE), / fəˈlɪ.ə tə ɔːˈriː.ə / (BrE)
A species of yellow-gilled mushroom found from Alaska south along the North American coast.
yellow-gilled mushroom species

Examples

While hiking in Alaska, we spotted several Pholiota aurea growing on a fallen log.
The cap of the Pholiota aurea is a striking golden color, making it easy to recognize.
Many naturalists document sightings of Pholiota aurea due to its distinctive yellow gills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mushroom - the fleshy, spore-bearing body of a fungus, typically edible

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Pholiota' (from Greek pholis, 'scale') and Latin 'aurea' meaning 'golden'.
