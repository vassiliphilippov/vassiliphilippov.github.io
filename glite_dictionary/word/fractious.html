fractious

1. ADJ

/ ˈfræk.ʃəs / (AmE), / ˈfræk.ʃəs / (BrE)
(of a thing or system) unpredictable and difficult to control; likely to cause trouble or malfunction.
unpredictably difficult; troublesome (of things)

Examples

The new software proved to be fractious, often freezing at the most inconvenient moments.
Engineers struggled to get the fractious machinery working smoothly again.
Replacing fractious components helped stabilize the communication system.

Synset Examples

rockets were much too fractious to be tested near thickly populated areas
fractious components of a communication system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unruly - difficult to control or manage
  • Antonym: manageable - able to be controlled or handled without difficulty

Etymology

From Latin 'frangere' (to break) via fractious + -ous.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfrækʃəs / (AmE), / ˈfrækʃəs / (BrE)
(of a person, animal, or group) difficult to control; unruly or irritable, especially when resisting authority.
difficult to control; unruly

Examples

The teacher struggled to manage the fractious class during the storm.
After hours of waiting, the crowd grew increasingly fractious and impatient.
The puppy became fractious whenever anyone tried to put a collar on him.

Synset Examples

a fractious animal that would not submit to the harness
a refractory child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refractory - stubbornly resistant to authority or control
  • Antonym: compliant - willing to comply, obedient

Etymology

From Latin fractus (broken), past participle of frangere (to break), via fractio (a breaking).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01618681-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈfræk.ʃəs / (AmE), / ˈfræk.ʃəs / (BrE)
easily annoyed or upset, especially in a way that is difficult to control; irritable or quarrelsome.
easily irritated or quarrelsome

Examples

The children became fractious during the long car journey.
After hours of tedious meetings, she grew increasingly fractious.
The manager struggled to calm his fractious team after the loss.

Synset Examples

an incorrigibly fractious young man
not the least nettlesome of his countrymen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritable - easily annoyed or made angry
  • Antonym: placid - calm and not easily upset or excited

Etymology

From Latin *frangere* (to break) via fractus (broken) and English '-ious'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01140041-s
Synset POS: s
