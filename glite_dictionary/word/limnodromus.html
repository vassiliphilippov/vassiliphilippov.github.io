Limnodromus

1. NOUN

/ lɪm.nəˈdroʊ.məs / (AmE), / lɪm.nəˈdrəʊ.məs / (BrE)
A genus of wading birds known as dowitchers, characterized by long bills and found in North America and Asia.
genus of wading birds (dowitchers)

Examples

Three species of Limnodromus are found in North America, all known for their long migration.
During spring, bird-watchers flock to wetlands to observe the arrival of Limnodromus.
The distinctive feeding method known as 'sewing machine' motion is typical of Limnodromus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dowitcher - common name for birds in the genus Limnodromus

Etymology

From Greek *limnē* (marsh) + *dromos* (runner), referring to their habitat and behavior.

2. NOUN

/ lɪm.nəˈdroʊ.məs / (AmE), / lɪm.nəˈdrəʊ.məs / (BrE)
A genus of wading birds in the sandpiper family, commonly known as dowitchers.
genus of wading birds (dowitchers)

Examples

Several species in the genus Limnodromus are difficult to distinguish by sight alone.
The Limnodromus breed in northern wetlands and migrate south in winter.
Researchers observed the nesting behavior of Limnodromus in the marshes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dowitcher - a common name for birds of the genus Limnodromus

Etymology

From Greek 'limnē' (marsh) and 'dromos' (runner).
