St. James

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈdʒeɪmz / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈdʒeɪmz / (BrE)
(in the New Testament) one of the twelve apostles of Jesus, the brother of John, and traditionally credited as the author of the Epistle of James.
apostle of Jesus in the New Testament

Examples

Many pilgrims walk the Camino de Santiago to honor St. James each year.
In the New Testament, St. James is noted as the brother of John.
The Epistle of St. James offers practical advice for Christian living.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: James the Greater - alternative name for St. James, apostle of Jesus
  • Synonym: Saint John - brother of St. James, also an apostle of Jesus

Etymology

From Latin 'Sanctus Iacobus', via Old French 'Saint Jacques', referring to James the Greater in the Bible.
