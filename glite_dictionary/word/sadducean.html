Sadducean

1. ADJ

/ ˌsædjuˈsiːən / (AmE), / ˌsædjʊˈsiːən / (BrE)
of or relating to the Sadducees, a Jewish sect active in Judea during the Second Temple period
relating to the Sadducees (Jewish sect)

Examples

The scholar presented a Sadducean interpretation of the ancient texts.
His views aligned with the Sadducean emphasis on written law over oral traditions.
Archaeological findings revealed artifacts linked to Sadducean practices in Jerusalem.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Pharisaic - of or relating to the Pharisees, another Jewish sect

Etymology

From Sadducee (member of a Jewish sect) + -an (adjectival suffix).
