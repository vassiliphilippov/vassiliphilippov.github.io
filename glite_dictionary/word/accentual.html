accentual

1. ADJ

/ ækˈsɛn.tʃu.əl / (AmE), / ækˈsen.tju.əl / (BrE)
relating to or based on accent or stress, especially in speech or verse.
relating to accent or stress

Examples

In accentual poetry, the number of stressed syllables in a line is more important than the total number of syllables.
Linguists often analyze the accentual patterns of different dialects to understand their rhythmic structure.
The verse followed an accentual rather than a syllabic structure, emphasizing beats over syllable count.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metrical - relating to meter, especially in poetry

Etymology

From Medieval Latin accentualis, from Latin accentus (accent).

2. ADJ

/ ækˈsɛntʃuəl / (AmE), / əkˈsɛntjʊəl / (BrE)
(of verse) using a metrical system based on stressed syllables rather than the total number of syllables or their length.
relating to stress-based poetic meter

Examples

Some Old English poetry is distinctly accentual in its rhythm and construction.
The professor highlighted the difference between accentual verse and syllabic verse during the lecture.
English nursery rhymes often employ an accentual meter, focusing on stressed beats.

Synset Examples

accentual poetry is based on the number of stresses in a line
accentual rhythm

Related Terms

  • Antonym: syllabic - using syllable count as the basis for poetic meter

Etymology

From Latin accentus (accent) + -al.
