Jamesonia

1. NOUN

/ dʒeɪmˈsoʊniə / (AmE), / dʒeɪmˈsəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of xerophytic ferns native to South America.
genus of South American xerophytic ferns

Examples

The Jamesonia genus includes several fern species adapted to dry environments.
Botanists discovered a new species within the Jamesonia group in the Andes mountains.
Unlike most ferns, Jamesonia thrives in areas with little moisture.

Etymology

Named after British botanist William Jameson (1796–1873).
