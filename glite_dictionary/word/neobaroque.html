neobaroque

1. NOUN

/ ˌni.oʊ.bəˈroʊk / (AmE), / ˌniː.əʊ.bəˈrɒk / (BrE)
A style of art or architecture that draws inspiration from or imitates the 17th-century Baroque period, often characterized by elaborate ornamentation and dramatic effects.
modern style inspired by Baroque art or architecture

Examples

The new museum's facade displays a striking neobaroque influence in its columns and sculpted details.
Her paintings are celebrated for their neobaroque style, blending classical motifs with contemporary flair.
Critics describe the building's elaborate interiors as unmistakably neobaroque, referencing the grandeur of earlier centuries.

Synset Examples

Like camp, the neo-baroque of Bartlem's artists address the body as a site of performance and tangible sensation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baroque - Art or architecture style from 17th century with ornate detail

Etymology

From neo- (new) + Baroque, referencing the revival or adaptation of the Baroque style.
