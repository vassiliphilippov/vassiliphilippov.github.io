elastomer

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlæstəmɚ / (AmE), / ɪˈlæstəmə / (BrE)
a polymer material with elastic properties, similar to rubber, that returns to its original shape after being stretched or deformed
an elastic polymer material like rubber

Examples

The engineer chose an elastomer for the seal because it could withstand repeated compression.
Medical gloves are often made from synthetic elastomers due to their flexibility and durability.
When stretched, an elastomer quickly returns to its original shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rubber - a tough, elastic polymer used in making tires and other products
  • Antonym: plastic - a synthetic material made from polymers but usually not elastic

Etymology

From 'elastic' + '-mer' (from Greek 'meros', meaning part), indicating a polymer with elastic properties.
