Cucurbita maxima

1. NOUN

/ kuːˈkɝːbɪtə ˈmæksɪmə / (AmE), / kjuːˈkɜːbɪtə ˈmæksɪmə / (BrE)
A species of winter squash native to South America, producing large, greyish-green, football-shaped fruits with a rough, warty rind.
species of winter squash with large warty fruits

Examples

The giant fruits of Cucurbita maxima are often displayed at harvest festivals.
Many traditional stews in South America feature Cucurbita maxima as a main ingredient.
Farmers prize Cucurbita maxima for its thick, sweet flesh and long storage life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squash - edible fruit of plants in the genus Cucurbita

Etymology

From Latin: cucurbita (gourd, squash) + maxima (largest, greatest).

2. NOUN

/ kuːˌkɝːˈbiːtə ˈmæksɪmə / (AmE), / kjuːˌkɜːˈbiːtə ˈmæksɪmə / (BrE)
a species of squash plant bearing buff-colored, bottle-shaped fruit with fine-textured edible flesh and a smooth thin rind
species of squash plant

Examples

Some varieties of Cucurbita maxima are grown specifically for their sweet, orange flesh.
Pumpkin pies are often made using Cucurbita maxima cultivars for their smooth texture.
The garden featured several rows of Cucurbita maxima plants, heavy with large, buff-colored squashes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squash - edible fruit of plants in the genus Cucurbita

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cucurbita' meaning gourd or squash and 'maxima' meaning largest or greatest.
