cortex

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːrˌtɛks / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.tɛks / (BrE)
The outer layer of tissue surrounding the lens nucleus in the eye.
outer layer of tissue in the eye lens
medical

Examples

During cataract surgery, the surgeon removes the cortex of the lens to restore clear vision.
Changes in the lens cortex can sometimes affect a person’s eyesight.
The cortex surrounds the denser central nucleus in the human eye lens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lens - the transparent structure of the eye that focuses light on the retina
  • Synonym: nucleus - the central part of the lens in the eye

Etymology

From Latin cortex (bark, rind).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔr.tɛks / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.tɛks / (BrE)
the outer layer of an organ or structure, especially the layer of unmyelinated neurons (grey matter) covering the cerebrum in the brain
outer layer of an organ, especially the brain
medical

Examples

The human brain's cortex is responsible for higher cognitive functions such as thought and reasoning.
Damage to the visual cortex can result in loss of sight even if the eyes are healthy.
Scientists studied the thickness of the cortex in different areas of the brain.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: medulla - the inner part of an organ or structure

Etymology

From Latin cortex (bark, outer covering).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɔɹˌtɛks / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.tɛks / (BrE)
the outer layer of tissue of an organ or structure in a plant or animal, especially the brain or kidney
outer tissue layer of an organ
medical

Examples

The cerebral cortex is responsible for many higher brain functions such as thought and action.
In plants, the cortex lies between the epidermis and the vascular tissue.
Damage to the renal cortex can severely affect kidney function.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: medulla - the inner region of an organ or structure

Etymology

From Latin *cortex* (bark, outer covering).
