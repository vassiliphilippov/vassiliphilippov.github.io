bicentennial

1. ADJ

/ ˌbaɪ.senˈten.i.əl / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.senˈten.i.əl / (BrE)
relating to or marking the two-hundredth anniversary of a significant event.
relating to a 200th anniversary

Examples

The city held a bicentennial parade to commemorate its 200th year since founding.
We visited the museum's bicentennial exhibit on the Constitution.
Her book explores the cultural impact of the nation's bicentennial in 1976.

Synset Examples

bicentennial celebration

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: centennial - relating to a 100th anniversary
  • Degree Higher: tercentennial - relating to a 300th anniversary

Etymology

From bi- (two) + centennial (hundred-year anniversary), patterned after centennial.

2. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.sənˈtɛn.i.əl / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.senˈten.i.əl / (BrE)
The 200th anniversary of a significant event, or a celebration held to mark it.
200th anniversary or its celebration

Examples

The country celebrated its bicentennial with fireworks, parades, and historical exhibitions.
A special coin was minted to commemorate the city's bicentennial.
Teachers organized a project on the bicentennial of the Declaration of Independence.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: centennial - the 100th anniversary or celebration of it
  • Degree Higher: tercentenary - the 300th anniversary or celebration of it

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'centennial' (hundred years).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15276411-n
Synset POS: n
