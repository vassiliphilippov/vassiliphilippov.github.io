Perodicticus

1. NOUN

/ pɛroʊˈdɪktɪkəs / (AmE), / pɛrəʊˈdɪktɪkəs / (BrE)
A genus of African primates in the family Lorisidae, comprising the pottos.
genus of African pottos (primates)

Examples

The Perodicticus genus is native to the forests of West and Central Africa.
Zoologists discovered a new species within the Perodicticus genus.
The potto, a member of the Perodicticus genus, is known for its slow movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potto - A small nocturnal primate of West and Central Africa

Etymology

From Greek 'peros' (maimed, incomplete) and 'diktikos' (fit for showing).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrəˈdɪtɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌpɛrəˈdɪtɪkəs / (BrE)
A genus of primates in the family Lorisidae, containing the potto, native to tropical Africa.
genus of primates including the potto

Examples

The Perodicticus genus is distinguished by its slow movements and nocturnal habits.
Researchers recently discovered a new subspecies of Perodicticus in Central Africa.
The potto, a member of the Perodicticus genus, uses its strong grip to cling to branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potto - a nocturnal primate native to Africa, member of Perodicticus

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek 'peros' (maimed) and 'diktuon' (net), likely referring to the slow loris's appearance.
