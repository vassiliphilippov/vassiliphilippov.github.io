pointfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪnt.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪnt.f(ə)l.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of having meaning, relevance, or purpose.
having meaning or relevance

Examples

The pointfulness of his argument became clear after some discussion.
Many people question the pointfulness of certain traditions in modern society.
She defended the pointfulness of artistic expression, even if its value is not always obvious.

Synset Examples

For the same reason, we should reject the earlier argument against the pointfulness of retrospective prayer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meaningfulness - the quality of having meaning
  • Antonym: pointlessness - the quality of lacking meaning or purpose

Etymology

Formed from 'pointful' (having a point or purpose) + '-ness'.
