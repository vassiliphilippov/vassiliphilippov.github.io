dame

1. NOUN

/ deɪm / (AmE), / deɪm / (BrE)
a woman of rank, dignity, or refinement, often used as a title for certain women of honor or distinction
woman of rank or refinement

Examples

The elegant dame arrived at the gala accompanied by two attendants.
She was honored as a dame for her contributions to the arts.
At the ceremony, the young girl admired the poised dame seated in the front row.

Synset Examples

a chauffeur opened the door of the limousine for the grand lady

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lady - a woman of good social position or refinement

Etymology

From Old French 'dame' (lady), from Latin 'domina' (mistress of a house).

2. NOUN

/ deɪm / (AmE), / deɪm / (BrE)
(informal, sometimes dated) A woman, especially used to refer to a woman in a casual or familiar way.
informal term for a woman
informal

Examples

The tough dame at the bar wasn’t afraid to speak her mind.
In old detective movies, the hero always gets mixed up with a mysterious dame.
She’s a smart dame, so don’t underestimate her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woman - an adult human female
  • Synonym: lady - a polite or refined woman
  • Antonym: gentleman - a man of good manners; sometimes paired as the male counterpart

Etymology

Originally from Old French 'dame' (lady), related to Latin 'domina' (mistress, lady of the house).
