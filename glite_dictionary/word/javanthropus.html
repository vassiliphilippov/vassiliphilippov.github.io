Javanthropus

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈvænθrəpəs / (AmE), / dʒəˈvanθrəpəs / (BrE)
A former genus name for a primitive human, now classified as Homo soloensis (Solo man).
obsolete genus of early human (Solo man)

Examples

The discovery of Javanthropus fossils contributed to our understanding of human evolution in Asia.
Although Javanthropus is no longer a valid genus, the name appears in older scientific literature.
Researchers reclassified Javanthropus as Homo soloensis after further study of the remains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Homo soloensis - species name for Solo man, now replacing Javanthropus

Etymology

From Java, the Indonesian island where the fossils were found, plus Greek 'anthrōpos' (man).

2. PROPN

/ dʒəˈvænθrəpəs / (AmE), / dʒəˈvanθrəpəs / (BrE)
A former genus name for primitive humans, now classified as Homo soloensis and commonly referred to as Solo man.
former genus of primitive humans (Solo man)

Examples

Many paleontologists once referred to the Solo man fossil as Javanthropus.
The genus Javanthropus is no longer used in contemporary human evolution studies.
Textbooks from the early 20th century sometimes list Javanthropus as a distinct human ancestor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Homo soloensis - now-accepted species name for Solo man
  • Synonym: Solo man - prehistoric human remains found in Java, Indonesia

Etymology

From Java, the Indonesian island where fossils were found, and Greek 'anthrōpos' (human).
