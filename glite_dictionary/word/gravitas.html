gravitas

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrævɪˌtɑs / (AmE), / ˈɡravɪtas / (BrE)
seriousness and dignity in manner or appearance; a formal and respectable presence.
seriousness; dignified presence

Examples

She delivered her speech with remarkable gravitas, impressing everyone in the audience.
The judge's gravitas lent authority to the courtroom proceedings.
Despite his youth, he carried himself with the gravitas of a seasoned leader.

Synset Examples

he behaved with great dignity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dignity - the state or quality of being worthy of respect
  • Synonym: solemnity - the state or quality of being serious and formal
  • Antonym: levity - lightness of manner or lack of seriousness

Etymology

From Latin gravitas (weight, seriousness, dignity), from gravis (heavy, serious).
