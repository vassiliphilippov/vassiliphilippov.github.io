Marcel Duchamp

1. PROPN

/ mɑrˈsɛl duˈʃɑmp / (AmE), / maːˈsɛl dyˈʃɑ̃ / (BrE)
(1887–1968) French artist and leading figure in the Dada movement, noted for introducing everyday objects as art.
French Dada artist (1887–1968)

Examples

Many art historians regard Marcel Duchamp as one of the most influential artists of the twentieth century.
Marcel Duchamp shocked the art world by submitting a urinal as a sculpture titled 'Fountain.'
The work of Marcel Duchamp challenged traditional concepts of what constitutes art.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Dadaism - An art movement Duchamp was closely associated with
  • Synonym: readymade - Artworks made from ordinary objects, pioneered by Duchamp
