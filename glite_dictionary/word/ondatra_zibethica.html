Ondatra zibethica

1. NOUN

/ ɑnˈdætrə zɪˈbɛθɪkə / (AmE), / ɒnˈdeɪtrə zᵻˈbɛθɪkə / (BrE)
the scientific name for the muskrat, a beaver-like aquatic rodent of North America with dark brown fur
scientific name for the muskrat

Examples

Scientists observed the Ondatra zibethica constructing lodges along the banks of the river.
The Ondatra zibethica is well adapted to wetland environments with its dense, waterproof fur.
In biology class, we studied the behavior and habitat of Ondatra zibethica.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muskrat - common name for Ondatra zibethica

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, genus Ondatra plus species zibethica, from French 'zibeth', meaning 'civet' (alluding to musky scent).
