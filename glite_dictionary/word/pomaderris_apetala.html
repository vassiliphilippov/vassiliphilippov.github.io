Pomaderris apetala

1. NOUN

/ poʊˌmædˈɛrɪs ˌæpəˈteɪlə / (AmE), / pəˌmadˈɛrɪs ˌapəˈteɪlə / (BrE)
An Australian tree valued for its ornamental appearance, fine-grained wood, and edible nuts.
Australian ornamental tree with edible nuts

Examples

The Pomaderris apetala is often planted in gardens for its attractive foliage.
Furniture makers prize the wood of the Pomaderris apetala for its fine texture and durability.
The nuts produced by Pomaderris apetala are a traditional food source for some Australian communities.

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining the genus name 'Pomaderris' (from Greek 'poma' meaning cover, and 'derris' meaning skin/leather) and the species epithet 'apetala' meaning 'without petals' in Latin.
