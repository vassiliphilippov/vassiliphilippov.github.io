slash

1. NOUN

/ slæʃ / (AmE), / slæʃ / (BrE)
an area of forest land covered with debris and branches left from logging, fire, or wind damage
debris-strewn forest land after logging or fire

Examples

After the wildfire, the ground was covered in slash and charred tree stumps.
The logging operation left a wide slash through the center of the forest.
Crews spent days clearing slash left behind by the strong winds.

2. VERB

/ slæʃ / (AmE), / slæʃ / (BrE)
to cut with a sweeping or forceful stroke, especially using an ax, knife, or machete
cut with a sweeping stroke

Examples

He slashed at the thick vines with his machete as he moved forward.
The knight slashed his sword through the air during the battle.
She angrily slashed the envelope open with a letter opener.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hack - to cut with heavy blows
  • Synonym: chop - to cut something into pieces with repeated sharp blows

Etymology

From Middle English 'slashen', probably of imitative origin.

3. NOUN

/ slæʃ / (AmE), / slæʃ / (BrE)
the punctuation mark (/) used to separate alternatives, related items, or parts of information
punctuation mark: /

Examples

Always use a slash to indicate alternatives such as 'and/or' in writing.
The web address should contain only one forward slash after 'http:'.
Dates are often written with a slash, like 12/31/2022.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solidus - another term for the slash punctuation mark
  • Antonym: backslash - the punctuation mark (\) used chiefly in computing
Source: WordNet
