Phoebe

1. PROPN

/ ˈfiːbi / (AmE), / ˈfiːbi / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a Titaness who is associated with the Moon and sometimes identified with Artemis.
Titaness goddess of the Moon in Greek mythology

Examples

In ancient myths, Phoebe was revered as a powerful lunar goddess.
The stories of Phoebe link her closely with Artemis, highlighting her association with the Moon.
Artists often depict Phoebe as a serene figure surrounded by celestial light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Artemis - Greek goddess of the hunt and the Moon
  • Synonym: Selene - Greek goddess of the Moon

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Φοίβη (Phoíbē), meaning 'bright' or 'pure'.
