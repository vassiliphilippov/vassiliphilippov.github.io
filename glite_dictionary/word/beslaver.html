to beslaver

1. VERB

/ bɪsˈleɪvər / (AmE), / bɪsˈleɪvə / (BrE)
to praise or flatter someone excessively or obsequiously
excessively flatter
archaic

Examples

He would beslaver his boss with compliments, hoping for a promotion.
The reporter was accused of trying to beslaver the celebrity at the press event.
Critics say the biography tends to beslaver its subject rather than offer honest analysis.

Synset Examples

She was known to beslaver her teacher with flattery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flatter - to praise excessively, usually insincerely
  • Degree Lower: praise - to express approval or admiration for

Etymology

From prefix 'be-' (intensifying) + 'slaver' (to drool over); figuratively meaning 'to cover with flattery.'

2. VERB

/ bɪˈsleɪvər / (AmE), / bɪˈsleɪvə / (BrE)
to flatter someone excessively, often to gain favor or for insincere reasons
flatter excessively

Examples

He tends to beslaver his friends with compliments.
The ambitious employee would often beslaver his boss in hopes of a promotion.
She disliked people who beslavered her with insincere praise.

Synset Examples

He tends to beslaver his friends with compliments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flatter - to praise excessively, often insincerely
  • Synonym: to adulate - to praise excessively or obsequiously

Etymology

From obsolete 'slaver' (to slobber) with prefix be-, suggesting figurative slobbering with praise.
