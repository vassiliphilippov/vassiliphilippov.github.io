antihaemophilic globulin

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ti.hiˌmoʊ.fɪˈlɪk ˈɡlɑː.bjə.lɪn / (AmE), / ˌanti.hiˌməʊ.fɪˈlɪk ˈɡlɒ.bjʊ.lɪn / (BrE)
a protein in the blood, also known as factor VIII, necessary for normal blood clotting; its deficiency causes hemophilia A
blood protein essential for clotting
medical

Examples

The patient was given antihaemophilic globulin to treat his bleeding disorder.
Deficiency of antihaemophilic globulin leads to impaired blood clotting in hemophilia A sufferers.
Modern therapies use recombinant forms of antihaemophilic globulin for more effective hemophilia management.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: factor VIII - another term for antihaemophilic globulin, a blood clotting factor
  • Degree Lower: hemophilia A - a genetic disorder caused by deficient antihaemophilic globulin

Etymology

From anti- (against) + haemophilic ('blood-loving', referring to hemophilia) + globulin (a type of protein).
