cryaesthesia

1. NOUN

/ kraɪ.iːsˈθiː.ʒə / (AmE), / kraɪ.ɪsˈθiː.zɪə / (BrE)
Extreme sensitivity to cold, especially as a medical symptom.
hypersensitivity to cold
medical

Examples

Patients with cryaesthesia often avoid cold environments due to increased discomfort.
The doctor noted cryaesthesia as a possible side effect of the medication.
Her cryaesthesia made winter activities particularly challenging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cold intolerance - inability to tolerate cold temperatures

Etymology

From Greek 'kryos' (cold) + 'aisthēsis' (sensation).
