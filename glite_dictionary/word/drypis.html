drypis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪpɪs / (AmE), / ˈdrɪpɪs / (BrE)
A spiny-leaved perennial herb native to southern Europe, characterized by terminal clusters of small flowers.
spiny-leaved perennial herb of southern Europe

Examples

Drypis grows in rocky areas throughout much of southern Europe.
The spiny leaves of drypis help protect it from grazing animals.
Botanists studied several species of drypis for their adaptation to arid environments.

Etymology

From Latin 'drypis', originally from Greek 'drupis', meaning a kind of plant.
