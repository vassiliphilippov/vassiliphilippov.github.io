calyptrate

1. ADJ

/ kəˈlɪptrət / (AmE), / kəˈlɪptrɪt / (BrE)
(of certain flies) having calypters, which are small membranous lobes at the base of the wings.
having calypters (in certain flies)

Examples

The calyptrate flies are distinguished from acalyptrate flies by the presence of wing calypters.
Many calyptrate muscoid flies are common in both urban and rural environments.
Entomologists often focus on calyptrate species due to their diverse ecological roles.

Etymology

From New Latin calyptratus, from Greek kalyptēr (cover, lid).

2. ADJ

/ kəˈlɪptrət / (AmE), / kəˈlɪptrət / (BrE)
Having a calyptra (a cap-like covering), especially used to describe certain plants, fungi, or flies possessing such a structure.
having a calyptra or cap-like covering

Examples

Many mosses are calyptrate, meaning their capsules are covered by a small hood or cap.
In entomology, calyptrate flies have a distinctive membranous covering at the base of the wings.
The fruit bodies are calyptrate, with a protective cap that falls off at maturity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acalyptrate - lacking a calyptra or cap-like covering

Etymology

From New Latin calyptratus, from Greek kalyptēr (cover, veil) + -ate.
