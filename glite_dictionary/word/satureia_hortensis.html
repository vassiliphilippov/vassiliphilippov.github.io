Satureia hortensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsætʃəˈriːə hɔrˈtɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌsatjʊˈriːə hɔːˈtɛnsɪs / (BrE)
the scientific name for summer savory, an annual herb with oval leaves and pink flowers, used as a seasoning in cooking
herb used as a seasoning, also called summer savory

Examples

Chefs often add Satureia hortensis to soups and stews for its distinctive flavor.
The garden was full of fragrant herbs, including Satureia hortensis and basil.
You can substitute thyme if you don't have Satureia hortensis for your recipe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summer savory - an annual herb with aromatic leaves used in cooking

Etymology

From Latin *Satureia* (savory) and *hortensis* (of the garden).
