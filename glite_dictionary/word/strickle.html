strickle

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɪk.əl / (AmE), / ˈstrɪk.əl / (BrE)
a straight-edged tool used in a foundry to shape or smooth a mold in sand
tool for shaping molds in sand (foundry)

Examples

The foundry worker carefully used a strickle to smooth the sand mold.
A strickle ensures that the mold has the precise shape required for casting metal parts.
After shaping the mold, he cleaned the strickle and placed it back on the tool rack.

Etymology

From Middle English 'strikyl', related to 'strike' meaning to smooth or level.

3. NOUN

/ ˈstrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈstrɪkəl / (BrE)
a tool or rod used to level off grain or other granular material heaped in a measure
tool for leveling off grain or powder

Examples

The miller used a strickle to level the grain before weighing it.
A strickle ensures accurate measurements by removing excess material from the top of a container.
In traditional baking, a strickle was often employed to create consistent amounts of flour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: straightedge - a bar or tool used as a guide for drawing straight lines or checking straightness

Etymology

From Middle English strikel, from Old English stricel 'something for scraping'
