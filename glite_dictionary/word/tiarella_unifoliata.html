Tiarella unifoliata

1. NOUN

/ tiˈærələ ˌjunəfoʊˈlaɪətə / (AmE), / tiˈærələ ˌjuːnɪfəˈlaɪətə / (BrE)
A perennial plant of moist woods in western North America, having tiny white flowers in loose clusters on leafy stems.
plant with small white clustered flowers

Examples

Tiarella unifoliata can often be found growing in shady, moist forests throughout the Pacific Northwest.
The delicate blooms of Tiarella unifoliata appear in late spring and are popular with native plant gardeners.
Botanists use Tiarella unifoliata as an indicator species for healthy woodland habitats.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Tiarella + species epithet unifoliata ('having one leaf').
