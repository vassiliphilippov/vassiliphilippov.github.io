plinth

1. NOUN

/ plɪnθ / (AmE), / plɪnθ / (BrE)
a heavy base or platform that supports a statue, column, or other structure
base or platform for a statue or column

Examples

The statue was mounted on a marble plinth in the city square.
The ancient temple's columns each stood upon a solid stone plinth.
The artist insisted that her sculpture required a high plinth for proper display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pedestal - a base or support for a column, statue, or structure

Etymology

From Greek 'plinthos' (brick, tile, slab) via Latin 'plinthus'.
