to analyse

1. VERB

/ əˈnælaɪz / (AmE), / ˈæn.əl.aɪz / (BrE)
to examine or study something by separating it into its parts to understand it better
examine by breaking into parts
british

Examples

The scientist will analyse the data collected from each experiment.
Before making a decision, she likes to analyse all of the available information.
He carefully analysed the situation before offering his advice.

Synset Examples

analyze today's financial market

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to synthesize - to combine different parts into a whole

Etymology

From French 'analyser', based on Greek 'analusis' (breaking up).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00738221-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈnælaɪz / (AmE), / ˈænəlaɪz / (BrE)
to consider or examine something in detail in order to discover its essential features or meaning
examine in detail to reveal meaning
british

Examples

Scientists carefully analyse the data before drawing any conclusions.
In English class, we were asked to analyse the poem's structure and themes.
The detective began to analyse the crime scene for any hidden clues.

Synset Examples

analyze a sonnet by Shakespeare
analyze the evidence in a criminal trial
analyze your real motives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to analyze - American English spelling of 'analyse'
  • Synonym: to examine - to inspect or look at something closely
  • Antonym: to synthesize - to combine different parts or ideas to form a whole

Etymology

From French 'analyser', derived from Greek 'analusis' (a loosening, analysis).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00646245-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ əˈnælaɪz / (AmE), / ˈæn.ə.laɪz / (BrE)
(of a person) to subject to treatment using the methods of psychoanalysis.
subject to psychoanalytic treatment

Examples

She was analysed by a renowned psychoanalyst during her time in London.
He decided to analyse his patient to uncover unconscious motivations.
Many patients are analysed for years before significant breakthroughs are made.

Synset Examples

I was analyzed in Vienna by a famous psychiatrist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to psychoanalyze - to subject to psychoanalytic treatment

Etymology

From French 'analyser', from 'analyse', from Greek 'analusis' (a loosening up).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00644859-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ əˈnæl.aɪz / (AmE), / ˈæn.ə.laɪz / (BrE)
to examine something in detail to discover its structure or essential features, especially by separating it into its parts
examine in detail; break into parts
british

Examples

The scientist will analyse the results to identify patterns in the data.
Teachers often ask students to analyse complex sentences in grammar lessons.
The chemist needed to analyse the compound before drawing any conclusions.

Synset Examples

analyze a specimen
analyze a sentence
analyze a chemical compound

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to analyze - American English spelling of 'analyse'
  • Antonym: to synthesize - combine components to form a whole; often seen as the opposite process of analyse

Etymology

From French 'analyser', from Medieval Latin 'analysis'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00645135-v
Synset POS: v
