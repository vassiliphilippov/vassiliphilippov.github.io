senega

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnɪɡə / (AmE), / ˈsɛnɪɡə / (BrE)
the dried root of certain Polygala species, used medicinally for its saponin content
medicinal root from Polygala plants

Examples

Some herbal remedies still use senega to treat respiratory conditions.
The pharmacist prepared a tincture using senega root extract.
Traditional medicine values senega for its expectorant properties.

Etymology

From Seneca, the name of a Native American people, from whom the use was learned.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnɪɡə / (AmE), / ˈsɛnɪɡə / (BrE)
A perennial bushy herb native to central and southern United States, known for its white flowers with green centers and often a purple crest; also called Seneca snakeroot.
North American perennial herb

Examples

Some herbal remedies contain the powdered root of senega for treating respiratory conditions.
Senega is often harvested in the summer when its flowers are in full bloom.
The white, green-centered flowers of senega make it easily identifiable in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Seneca snakeroot - Another name for senega, the same species of herb

Etymology

From Latin 'Senega', named after the Seneca people, who used the root medicinally.
