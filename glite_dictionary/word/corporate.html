corporate

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɔːrpərət / (AmE), / ˈkɔːpərət / (BrE)
relating to, or belonging to a corporation or large company, especially as an organization rather than individual members
relating to a corporation

Examples

The company offered special corporate rates for long-term clients.
Our team is responsible for managing the corporate website and communications.
She has a decade of experience working in corporate law.

Synset Examples

corporate rates
corporate structure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: company - a commercial business

Etymology

From Latin corporatus (combined, formed into a body), past participle of corporare (to form into a body), from corpus (body).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02996213-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɔːr.pɚ.ət / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.pər.ət / (BrE)
Done by or characteristic of people acting together as a group.
relating to people acting as a group

Examples

The decision reflected a corporate effort to protect the environment.
She emphasized the importance of corporate responsibility in tackling social issues.
The award recognized the corporate achievements of the science team.

Synset Examples

the collective mind
the corporate good

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collective - done by people acting as a group

Etymology

From Latin 'corporatus', past participle of 'corporare' (to make into a body), from 'corpus' (body).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02119981-s
Synset POS: s

/ ˈkɔːr.pər.ət / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.pər.ət / (BrE)
(Archaic) Possessing or existing in a bodily or physical form; embodied or incarnate.
archaic: bodily or embodied
archaic

Examples

In Shakespeare's works, a corporate apparition may appear as a ghost in the flesh.
The poet described hope as a corporate presence in the world, not just an idea.
They believed the deity was not merely spiritual, but also corporate, manifest in a human form.

Synset Examples

what seemed corporal melted as breath into the wind
an incarnate spirit
‘corporate’ is an archaic term

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incarnate - embodied in flesh; given a bodily form
  • Synonym: bodily - having a physical body; corporeal
  • Antonym: spiritual - relating to the spirit, not the body

Etymology

From Latin corporatus (formed into a body), from corpus (body).

4. ADJ

/ ˈkɔr.pɚ.ət / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.pər.ət / (BrE)
formed into or pertaining to a legally recognized corporation; organized as a corporation.
organized as a corporation

Examples

The company was restructured as a corporate entity to limit liability.
They adopted a corporate form of governance to ensure compliance with regulations.
Many towns in the state are not corporate, but are instead governed by local councils.

Synset Examples

a special agency set up in corporate form
an incorporated town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incorporated - legally constituted as a corporation
  • Antonym: unincorporated - not organized as a legal corporation

Etymology

From Latin corporatus, past participle of corporare (to form into a body), from corpus (body).
