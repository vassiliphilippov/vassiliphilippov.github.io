superfine

1. ADJ

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈfaɪn / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈfaɪn / (BrE)
extremely delicate, refined, or of exceptionally fine quality; sometimes to an excessive degree.
extremely delicate or refined

Examples

The tailor used only superfine fabrics for his most exclusive suits.
Her manners were so superfine they bordered on artificiality.
The restaurant's superfine desserts delighted even the most demanding gourmets.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: fine - of very high quality or delicacy
  • Synonym: exquisite - extremely beautiful and delicate

Etymology

From Latin 'super-' (above) + 'fine' (refined, delicate).

Their company is known for producing superfine paper ideal for calligraphy.

3. ADJ

/ ˌsuːpərˈfaɪn / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈfaɪn / (BrE)
extremely fine in size or texture; of greater fineness or delicacy than usual.
extremely fine in size or texture

Examples

You should use superfine sugar to ensure a smooth texture in the meringue.
The artist preferred superfine sandpaper for the final polish of the sculpture.
She bought a bolt of superfine wool fabric for her new dress.

Synset Examples

superfine sugar
a superfine file

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: fine - of high quality or very thin in texture
  • Antonym: coarse - having a rough or thick texture; not fine

Etymology

From super- (meaning ‘above, beyond’) + fine; first used in English c. 16th century.
