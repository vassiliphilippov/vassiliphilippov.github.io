garrotte

1. VERB

/ ɡəˈrɑt / (AmE), / ɡəˈrɒt / (BrE)
to kill someone by strangling, especially using a cord or an iron collar, often as a form of execution or murder
to strangle with a cord or collar

Examples

The prisoner was garrotted for his crimes in the early 19th century.
The spy feared he would be garrotted if he was captured by enemy agents.
Historians believe several nobles were garrotted during the political upheavals of that era.

Synset Examples

people were garrotted during the Inquisition in Spain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to strangle - to kill by squeezing the throat

Etymology

From Spanish 'garrote' (a stick or club used for execution by strangulation), ultimately from Vulgar Latin 'garrūta'.

2. NOUN

/ ɡəˈrɑt / (AmE), / ɡəˈrɒt / (BrE)
A device used for execution by strangulation, typically consisting of a cord or wire tightened around the neck.
instrument for execution by strangulation

Examples

The executioner prepared the garrotte before the condemned man was brought in.
A garrotte was once used in Spain as a method of capital punishment.
Historically, the garrotte was a feared instrument during public executions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noose - a loop with a running knot, used for execution by hanging
  • Synonym: guillotine - a device for execution by beheading

Etymology

From Spanish 'garrote', meaning 'stick' or 'club', especially one used in executions.
