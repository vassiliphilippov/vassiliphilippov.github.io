exactitude

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzæktɪˌtud / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzæktɪtjuːd / (BrE)
the quality of being very accurate and precise; great attention to detail or correctness
the quality of being exact or precise

Examples

Her exactitude when proofreading made her an excellent editor.
The chef prepared every dish with remarkable exactitude.
Scientific experiments require a high degree of exactitude to produce reliable data.

Synset Examples

he demanded exactness in all details
a man of great exactitude

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precision - the quality of being exact and accurate
  • Synonym: accuracy - the quality or state of being correct or true
  • Antonym: inexactitude - the quality of not being exact; lack of precision

Etymology

From French 'exactitude', from Latin 'exactus' (precise, accurate).
