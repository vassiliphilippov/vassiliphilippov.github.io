Epidendrum tampense

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈdɛndrəm tæmˈpɛns / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdɛndrəm tæmˈpɛns / (BrE)
A species of orchid native to Florida and the Bahamas, noted for its showy, brightly colored flowers; sometimes grouped within the genus Epidendrum.
a species of orchid native to Florida and the Bahamas

Examples

The Epidendrum tampense blooms every summer, adding spectacular color to the Florida wetlands.
Botanists found a thriving population of Epidendrum tampense during their survey of the Bahamas.
Gardeners prize the Epidendrum tampense for its resilience and vivid flowers.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'epi' (upon) + 'dendron' (tree), referring to its typical habitat; species epithet from Tampa, Florida.
