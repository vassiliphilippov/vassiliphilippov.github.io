sugi

1. NOUN

/ suːˈgiː / (AmE), / suːˈgiː / (BrE)
A tall evergreen tree native to Japan and China (Cryptomeria japonica), valued for its soft timber.
Japanese evergreen tree; source of soft wood

Examples

The ancient sugi in the temple grounds is over a thousand years old.
Carpenters prize sugi wood for building traditional Japanese houses.
We walked through a forest of towering sugi on our visit to central Japan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cryptomeria - genus of trees including sugi

Etymology

From Japanese 杉 (sugi), the native name of the tree.
