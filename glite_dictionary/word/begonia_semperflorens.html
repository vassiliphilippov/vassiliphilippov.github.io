Begonia semperflorens

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈɡoʊniə sɛmpərˈflɔrɛnz / (AmE), / bɪˈɡəʊniə sɛmpəˈflɔːrɛnz / (BrE)
A hybrid fibrous-rooted begonia with broad ovate green to bronze-red leaves and small clusters of white, pink, or red flowers, widely used as a bedding plant.
a popular bedding begonia with colorful flowers

Examples

She planted several Begonia semperflorens along the garden path for a splash of color.
The Begonia semperflorens thrives best in well-drained soil and partial shade.
Gardeners prize Begonia semperflorens for its continuous blooms throughout the summer.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Begonia' named after Michel Bégon, a French patron of botany; 'semperflorens' meaning 'ever-blooming'.
