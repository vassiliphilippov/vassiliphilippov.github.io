to outscore

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈskɔr / (AmE), / aʊtˈskɔː / (BrE)
to score more points than an opponent in a game or competition
score more points than an opponent

Examples

The home team managed to outscore their rivals in the final quarter.
If we outscore them in this round, we'll win the match.
Last season, she outscored every player on the team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defeat - win a victory over (someone) in a contest

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'out-' (surpassing) + 'score' (to gain points).
