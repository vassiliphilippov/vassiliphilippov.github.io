Regalecidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːɡəˈliːsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌriːɡəˈliːsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, elongated marine fishes known as oarfishes, found in deep ocean waters and notable for their ribbon-like bodies.
family of marine fishes (oarfishes)
medical

Examples

The Regalecidae are rarely seen alive because they inhabit deep ocean waters.
Scientists have observed members of the Regalecidae family reaching lengths of up to 11 meters.
My biology professor showed us an illustration of Regalecidae to explain deep-sea fish adaptations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oarfish - a large, elongated, deep-sea fish of the genus Regalecus

Etymology

From Latin *Regalecus*, the genus name, combined with the zoological family suffix *-idae*.

2. NOUN

/ ˌriːɡəˈlisiːdiː / (AmE), / ˌriːɡəˈlɛsɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of large, elongated, ribbon-like deep-sea fishes known as oarfishes
family of oarfishes

Examples

The Regalecidae family contains some of the longest bony fish in the ocean.
Scientists recently documented a rare sighting of a member of the Regalecidae off the coast of Japan.
Marine biologists are fascinated by the unique appearance of fish from the Regalecidae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oarfish - a large, long-bodied deep-sea fish

Etymology

From New Latin Regalecidae, from genus Regalecus (from Latin regalis meaning 'royal').
