Lobotidae

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈbɒtɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ləˈbɒtɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fish known as tripletails, found in warm and tropical waters.
family of fish (tripletails)

Examples

The Lobotidae family includes the widely known tripletail fish found in tropical oceans.
Marine biologists have studied the unique fin structures of Lobotidae species.
Members of the Lobotidae family are prized by recreational anglers for their fighting ability.

Etymology

From Latin lobus (lobe) + -idae, a zoological suffix denoting a family.
