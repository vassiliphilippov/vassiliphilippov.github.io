semi

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛmi / (AmE), / ˈsɛmi / (BrE)
A trailer with wheels only at the rear, the front supported by the towing vehicle, typically used for transporting goods.
trailer with rear wheels, front supported by vehicle
american

Examples

The truck driver attached the semi to his cab before beginning his cross-country journey.
A fleet of semis lined up at the loading dock, waiting to be filled with cargo.
They transported the heavy machinery using a large semi rented from a local hauling company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: semi-trailer - A trailer with rear wheels only; front supported by a vehicle

Etymology

Shortened from 'semi-trailer', where 'semi-' means 'half' in Latin, indicating that the trailer is only partially self-supporting.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛmaɪ / (AmE), / ˈsɛmi / (BrE)
(North American) A large truck consisting of a tractor unit and a trailer, used for hauling freight over long distances.
tractor-trailer truck (North American)
american

Examples

The semi rumbled down the highway, hauling a load of produce to the next city.
After driving the semi all night, the trucker finally stopped for coffee at dawn.
Construction crews had to reroute traffic when a semi overturned on the interstate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tractor-trailer - a combination of a tractor unit and one or more semi-trailers to carry freight

Etymology

Short for 'semi-trailer truck,' where 'semi' refers to the semi-trailer, a trailer without a front axle.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɛmi / (AmE), / ˈsɛmi / (BrE)
one of the two competitions in the round before the final, especially in a sports tournament; a semifinal.
a semifinal round or match
informal

Examples

Liverpool will face Manchester City in the semi next weekend.
She qualified for the semi after a tense quarterfinal match.
Both semis were closely contested, with the winners advancing to the final.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: semifinal - the round before the final in a competition

Etymology

Shortened form of 'semifinal'.
