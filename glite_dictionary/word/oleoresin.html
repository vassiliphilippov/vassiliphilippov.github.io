oleoresin

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊlioʊˈrɛzɪn / (AmE), / ˌəʊliəʊˈrɛzɪn / (BrE)
a naturally occurring mixture of a resin and an essential oil, obtained from certain plants
mixture of resin and essential oil from plants

Examples

Turpentine is a type of oleoresin obtained from pine trees.
The extraction process separates the oleoresin from the rest of the plant material.
Capsicum oleoresin is used to flavor spicy foods and make pepper spray.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resin - a sticky substance exuded by some trees and plants
  • Synonym: essential oil - a volatile aromatic oil extracted from plants

Etymology

From French 'oléorésine', from Latin 'oleum' (oil) + 'resina' (resin).
