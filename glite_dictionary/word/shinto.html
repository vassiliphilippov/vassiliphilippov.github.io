Shinto

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪn.toʊ / (AmE), / ˈʃɪn.təʊ / (BrE)
the traditional indigenous religion of Japan, emphasizing the worship of nature spirits and ancestors, and lacking formal doctrine.
Japanese indigenous religion

Examples

Many Japanese festivals are rooted in Shinto traditions and rituals.
The Shinto belief system deeply values the natural world and ancestral heritage.
She visited a Shinto shrine during her trip to Tokyo.

Etymology

From Japanese 'Shintō' (神道), meaning 'the way of the gods' (神 'shin' god + 道 'tō' way).
