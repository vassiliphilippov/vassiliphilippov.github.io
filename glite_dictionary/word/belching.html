belching

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛltʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɛltʃɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of expelling gas noisily from the stomach through the mouth, usually as a reflex action.
expelling gas from the stomach through the mouth

Examples

Belching after a large meal is common and usually harmless.
He apologized for belching loudly during the meeting.
Frequent belching may be a sign of a digestive disorder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burping - noisily expelling gas from the stomach through the mouth

Etymology

From Middle English 'belchen', of imitative origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛltʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɛltʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of forcefully expelling something, especially gas or smoke, from inside
forceful expulsion of something, like gas or smoke

Examples

The belching of smoke from the chimneys covered the town in a thick haze.
There was a loud belching of steam as the train pulled into the station.
With every belching of gas, the old engine rattled and coughed.

Synset Examples

the belching of smoke from factory chimneys

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eruption - a sudden outburst or expulsion, especially from a volcano

Etymology

From the verb 'belch,' which originates from Middle English 'belchen', related to Old English 'bealcan' (to belch).
