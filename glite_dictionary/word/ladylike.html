ladylike

1. ADJ

/ ˈleɪdiˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈleɪdɪˌlaɪk / (BrE)
(of a woman or her behavior) polite, refined, and behaving in a way considered typical of a well-bred lady.
polite and refined in a traditionally feminine way

Examples

She always tries to act in a ladylike manner at formal events.
Despite wearing jeans, her ladylike grace was unmistakable.
Her parents taught her to speak in a calm and ladylike voice.

Synset Examples

ladylike manners

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unladylike - not behaving in a typically polite or refined feminine manner

Etymology

Formed from 'lady' + 'like', first attested in the 16th century.
