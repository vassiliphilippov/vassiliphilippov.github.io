credulousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɛdʒ.ə.ləs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkrɛd.jʊ.ləs.nəs / (BrE)
a tendency to believe things too easily, making a person easily deceived or tricked
tendency to believe too readily

Examples

The fraudster exploited his victim's credulousness to carry out the scam.
Her credulousness made her the perfect target for the false advertisement.
A healthy dose of skepticism can help counteract credulousness in today's media environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gullibility - easily deceived or tricked
  • Antonym: suspiciousness - inclined to suspect or distrust others

Etymology

From Latin 'credulus' (too ready to believe), from 'credere' (to believe).
