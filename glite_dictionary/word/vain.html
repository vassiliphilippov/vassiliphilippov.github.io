vain

1. ADJ

/ veɪn / (AmE), / veɪn / (BrE)
having or showing an excessively high opinion of one's appearance, abilities, or worth; conceited
excessively proud; conceited

Examples

She was so vain that she spent hours admiring herself in the mirror.
His vain attempts to impress everyone only made him seem arrogant.
Never be too vain to accept good advice from others.

Synset Examples

a conceited fool
an attitude of self-conceited arrogance
an egotistical disregard of others
so swollen by victory that he was unfit for normal duty
growing ever more swollen-headed and arbitrary
vain about her clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conceited - having an excessively high opinion of oneself
  • Antonym: modest - having a humble or moderate view of one's importance

Etymology

From Latin vanus (empty, idle, without substance).

2. ADJ

/ veɪn / (AmE), / veɪn / (BrE)
(of an action, effort, or attempt) producing no result or success; pointless; futile.
producing no result; futile

Examples

Despite days of searching, their vain efforts produced no new evidence.
She made a vain attempt to persuade him to stay, but he had already decided to leave.
All their warnings proved vain as disaster struck the next morning.

Synset Examples

a fruitless search
futile years after her artistic peak
a sleeveless errand
a vain attempt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fruitless - failing to achieve the desired result; unproductive
  • Synonym: futile - incapable of producing any useful result; pointless
  • Antonym: successful - achieving a desired aim or result

Etymology

From Latin vanus (empty, idle, futile).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01872762-s
Synset POS: s
