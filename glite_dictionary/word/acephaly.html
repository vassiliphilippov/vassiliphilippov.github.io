acephaly

1. NOUN

/ eɪˈsɛfəli / (AmE), / eɪˈsɛfəli / (BrE)
The condition of having no head, especially as a congenital abnormality in animals or humans.
absence of the head (medical)
medical

Examples

The rare disorder known as acephaly can occur during early stages of embryonic development.
Veterinarians documented a case of acephaly in a newborn calf.
Scientific reports on congenital acephaly are extremely uncommon.

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'kephalē' (head).
