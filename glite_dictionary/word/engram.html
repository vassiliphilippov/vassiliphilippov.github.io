engram

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛnɡræm / (AmE), / ˈɛnɡræm / (BrE)
a hypothetical physical or biochemical change in neural tissue that acts as a trace of a memory
physical trace of a memory

Examples

Many scientists believe that an engram is formed each time a memory is stored in the brain.
The location of the engram for certain memories remains an active area of research.
Researchers are trying to trace the engram responsible for learned behaviors in mice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memory trace - the physical record of a memory in the brain

Etymology

From Greek 'en-' (in) + 'gramma' (something written).
