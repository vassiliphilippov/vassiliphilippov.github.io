striation

1. NOUN

/ straɪˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / straɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a stripe or a pattern of stripes of contrasting color, especially as seen in rocks, animals, or tissues
stripe or band of contrasting color

Examples

The polished stone displayed beautiful striations of gray and white.
A tiger’s fur is marked by dark striations running across its back.
Geologists study the striations on rocks to understand glacial movement.

Synset Examples

chromosomes exhibit characteristic bands
the black and yellow banding of bees and wasps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stripe - a long, narrow band of a different color or material
  • Synonym: band - a strip of material or color, often forming a pattern

Etymology

From Latin 'striatus' (grooved, striped) via French 'striation'.

2. NOUN

/ straɪˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / straɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
any of a number of parallel lines, grooves, or ridges, especially tiny streaks on a surface, such as those found in muscles or left by geological processes like glaciers
parallel lines or grooves, esp. on surfaces

Examples

Upon close inspection, the geologist noted faint striations running across the rock face.
Muscle tissue appears to have striation under a microscope due to its structure.
The glacier left deep striations on the valley floor, marking its ancient path.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groove - a long, narrow cut or depression on a surface
  • Synonym: ridge - a long, narrow raised part or crest
  • Antonym: smoothness - the quality of having an even surface without roughness or ridges

Etymology

From Latin 'striātus' (furrowed, grooved), from 'stria' (a furrow, channel).
