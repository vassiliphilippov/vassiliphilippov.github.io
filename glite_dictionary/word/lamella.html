lamella

1. NOUN

/ ləˈmɛlə / (AmE), / ləˈmɛlə / (BrE)
a thin layer or plate, especially one of the calcified layers in bone tissue
thin layer in bone tissue
medical

Examples

Each osteon in bone tissue contains several concentric lamellae.
The lamellae add strength and resilience to the bone's structure.
Researchers studied the arrangement of lamellae under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: layer - a single thickness of a material covering a surface or forming part of something

Etymology

From Latin 'lamella', diminutive of 'lamina' (thin plate).

2. NOUN

/ ləˈmɛlə / (AmE), / ləˈmiːlə / (BrE)
A thin plate, layer, or membrane, especially one of a number of such structures in a biological or physical context.
thin plate or layer

Examples

The bone is made up of many lamellae arranged in concentric circles.
A microscope revealed that the leaf's surface was covered with a delicate lamella.
When the material cools, a lamella of crystals can form within its structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: layer - a sheet or thickness of material, typically one of several, covering a surface or body

Etymology

From Latin 'lamella', diminutive of 'lamina' (thin plate, leaf).

3. NOUN

/ ləˈmɛlə / (AmE), / ləˈmiːlə / (BrE)
any of the thin, flat, radiating spore-producing structures found on the underside of the cap of a mushroom or similar fungus
a thin, leaflike structure in fungi

Examples

The lamella of the mushroom are responsible for the production of spores.
When identifying wild mushrooms, carefully examine the color and spacing of the lamellae.
The cap's underside revealed closely packed lamella covered in budding spores.

Etymology

From Latin *lamella*, diminutive of *lamina* (thin plate).
