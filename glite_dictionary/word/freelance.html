freelance

1. NOUN

/ ˈfriˌlæns / (AmE), / ˈfriːˌlɑːns / (BrE)
a person, especially a writer or artist, who works independently for different organizations rather than being employed by one company permanently
self-employed person working for various clients

Examples

She works as a freelance journalist for several national newspapers.
After leaving his job, he decided to become a freelance to have more flexibility.
Many artists choose the freelance life to pursue different creative projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contractor - a person or company that undertakes a contract to provide materials or labor

Etymology

Originally from 'free lance', described in the early 19th century for a mercenary who sold services to anyone; 'lance' refers to a weapon used by soldiers.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10130190-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈfriˌlæns / (AmE), / ˈfriːlɑːns / (BrE)
to work as an independent contractor, usually for different companies and on a temporary basis, rather than as a permanent employee
work as an independent contractor

Examples

She decided to freelance after working for several years at a large law firm.
Many designers freelance to have more flexibility in their schedules.
After being laid off, he began to freelance for several tech startups.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: contract - to be formally hired for a job or task, usually with a legal agreement

Etymology

Originally a noun for a mercenary knight ('free lance'); used as a verb from the 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02582195-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈfriˌlæns / (AmE), / ˈfriːˌlɑːns / (BrE)
a person, especially a writer or artist, who works independently for different employers rather than being permanently employed by one
independent worker for various employers

Examples

She has worked as a freelance for several major magazines over the past decade.
Many artists choose the freelance lifestyle for its flexibility and creative freedom.
As a freelance, he enjoys working with a variety of clients on different projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contractor - a person or firm that undertakes a contract to provide materials or labor
  • Antonym: employee - a person employed for wages or salary, especially at non-executive level

Etymology

From 'free' + 'lance', originally describing a mercenary soldier (a 'free lance') in the early 1800s.

Source: WordNet

5. ADJ

working for different companies or people, usually on a project or contract basis, rather than being permanently employed by one organization
working independently, not permanently employed

Examples

She enjoys the flexibility of her freelance lifestyle.
Many writers choose to work on a freelance basis rather than being tied to one publisher.
After years at the company, he switched to freelance work to pursue his own projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: independent - not connected with or influenced by anything else; self-employed in context
  • Antonym: permanent - holding a stable or lasting position; not temporary or independent work

Etymology

Originally from 'free lance', referring to a mercenary soldier whose lance was not engaged in the service of any one person.
