skeet

1. NOUN

/ skit / (AmE), / skiÀêt / (BrE)
A sport in which participants shoot at clay targets that are launched into the air to simulate the flight of birds.
sport of shooting clay targets

Examples

He has competed in skeet tournaments for several years.
The club offers lessons in both trap and skeet.
She learned the basics of skeet shooting over the weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trapshooting - the sport of shooting at clay pigeons launched from a single machine

Etymology

Originally an American term, possibly from Scandinavian roots meaning 'to shoot.'
