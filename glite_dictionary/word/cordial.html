cordial

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɔr.dʒəl / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.di.əl / (BrE)
(of a feeling or attitude) sincerely and strongly felt; heartfelt.
sincerely and strongly felt

Examples

He offered a cordial welcome to all his guests.
She expressed her cordial thanks for their hospitality.
Their cordial dislike of each other was obvious to everyone.

Synset Examples

a cordial regard for his visitor's comfort
a cordial abhorrence of waste

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heartfelt - genuinely felt; deeply sincere
  • Antonym: insincere - not expressing genuine feelings

Etymology

From Latin 'cordialis', from 'cor' (heart), relating to matters of the heart or feeling.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɔr.dʒəl / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.di.əl / (BrE)
Warm and friendly; expressing genuine affection or goodwill.
warm and friendly

Examples

The manager gave a cordial welcome to the new employees at the meeting.
They have always maintained cordial relations despite the competition between their companies.
Her cordial smile put everyone at ease as they entered the room.

Synset Examples

an affable smile
an amiable gathering
cordial relations
a cordial greeting
a genial host

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friendly - kind and pleasant; showing friendship
  • Antonym: hostile - unfriendly; showing or feeling opposition
  • Synonym: genial - cheerful and friendly

Etymology

From Medieval Latin cordialis, from Latin cor (heart).
