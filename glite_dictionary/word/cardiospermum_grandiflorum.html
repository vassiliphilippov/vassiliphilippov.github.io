Cardiospermum grandiflorum

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑr.di.oʊˈspɜr.məm ˌɡræn.dɪˈflɔːr.əm / (AmE), / ˌkɑː.di.əʊˈspɜː.məm ˌɡræn.dɪˈflɔː.rəm / (BrE)
A species of herbaceous vine native to tropical America and Africa, known for its inflated seed pods and delicate, compound leaves.
herbaceous vine species

Examples

Cardiospermum grandiflorum can be found climbing over fences and trees in tropical regions.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Cardiospermum grandiflorum for its ornamental balloon-like seed pods.
Invasive outbreaks of Cardiospermum grandiflorum have been reported in several countries.

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name from Greek 'kardia' (heart) + 'sperma' (seed), species name from Latin 'grandiflorus' (large-flowered).
