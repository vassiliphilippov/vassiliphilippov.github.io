cryptograph

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪptəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈkrɪptəˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a piece of writing in code or cipher, designed to conceal its meaning
coded message or writing

Examples

The spy left a cryptograph tucked inside the book for his contact to find.
Researchers worked for months trying to decode the mysterious cryptograph discovered in the archives.
She wrote her diary in the form of a cryptograph to keep her thoughts private.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cipher - a secret or disguised way of writing; a code
  • Antonym: to decrypt - to convert coded writing into normal language

Etymology

From Greek kryptos (hidden) + -graph (writing).

Early scientists developed the cryptograph to help protect confidential information.
A malfunction in the cryptograph rendered the encrypted message unreadable.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪptəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈkrɪptəˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a secret or coded method of writing; a message written in a cipher or code to keep its content secret
secret or coded writing system

Examples

The spy transmitted a cryptograph that could only be understood by those with the key.
Researchers studied an ancient cryptograph found in the ruins, hoping to unlock its secrets.
The message was written in a complex cryptograph, making it unreadable to outsiders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cipher - a secret or disguised way of writing; a code
  • Antonym: to decipher - to convert a code into normal language
  • Synonym: to encode - to convert information into a coded form

Etymology

From Greek 'kryptos' (hidden) + '-graph' (writing).
