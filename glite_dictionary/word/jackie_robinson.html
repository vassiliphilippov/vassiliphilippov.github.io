Jackie Robinson

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒæki ˈrɑbɪnsən / (AmE), / ˈdʒæki ˈrɒbɪnsən / (BrE)
(1919–1972) United States baseball player, known for being the first Black athlete to play in Major League Baseball in the modern era.
first Black Major League Baseball player

Examples

Jackie Robinson broke the color barrier in Major League Baseball in 1947.
The legacy of Jackie Robinson continues to inspire athletes and civil rights activists today.
Many stadiums honor Jackie Robinson by retiring his iconic number 42.
