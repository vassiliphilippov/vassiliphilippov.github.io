Wynnea

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪniə / (AmE), / ˈwɪniə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Sarcoscyphaceae, known for their unique fruiting bodies found in soil or decaying wood.
genus of fungi in Sarcoscyphaceae

Examples

Many species in the genus Wynnea are found in temperate woodlands around the world.
The unusual shape of Wynnea fruiting bodies makes them easy to recognize by mycologists.
Researchers identified a new Wynnea specimen growing on decaying logs in the forest.
