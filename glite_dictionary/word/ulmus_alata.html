Ulmus alata

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌlməs əˈleɪtə / (AmE), / ˈʌlməs əˈlɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of elm native to North America, characterized by twigs and young branches with prominent corky wings.
North American elm tree with corky-winged twigs

Examples

The small leaves and corky wings on the branches make Ulmus alata easy to identify.
Gardeners often plant Ulmus alata for its ornamental value in landscapes.
Diseases that affect other elms can also pose a threat to Ulmus alata populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winged elm - Common name for Ulmus alata, a North American elm species with corky twigs

Etymology

From Latin: 'Ulmus' meaning 'elm' and 'alata' meaning 'winged', referring to the wing-like corky growths on the branches.
