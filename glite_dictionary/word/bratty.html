bratty

1. ADJ

/ ˈbræti / (AmE), / ˈbræti / (BrE)
(especially of a child) behaving in a spoiled, rude, or ill-mannered way; acting like a brat.
spoiled or ill-mannered (esp. child)

Examples

The bratty children refused to listen to their babysitter and made a huge mess.
Her bratty attitude made it difficult for her to make friends at school.
He apologized for being bratty and promised to behave better next time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: well-behaved - (of a child) acting properly and politely
  • Synonym: spoiled - (of a child) given everything they want, leading to bad behavior

Etymology

Formed from 'brat' (slang term for a misbehaving child) + -y (adjective suffix).
