creak

1. VERB

/ krik / (AmE), / kriːk / (BrE)
to make a harsh, high-pitched, squeaking sound, especially when being moved or when pressure is applied
make a harsh, squeaking sound

Examples

The old floorboards creak whenever someone walks across the hallway.
She slowly opened the window and heard it creak on its rusty hinges.
If you creak the chair back and forth, it becomes very annoying.

Synset Examples

The door creaked when I opened it slowly
My car engine makes a whining noise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squeak - make a short, high-pitched sound
  • Degree Lower: groan - make a deep, drawn-out sound, often due to strain

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, echoing the sound itself.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ kɹik / (AmE), / kɹiːk / (BrE)
a harsh, high-pitched squeaking sound, typically produced by something being moved or put under strain, such as a floorboard or hinge
a harsh squeaking sound

Examples

The old stairs made a loud creak every time someone climbed them.
She heard the creak of a door opening slowly in the hallway.
A sudden creak from the attic made everyone pause and listen.

Synset Examples

the creak of the floorboards gave him away

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squeak - a short, high-pitched sound
  • Degree Lower: groan - a low, mournful sound, often from strain

Etymology

Imitative origin, representing the sound itself.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07396464-n
Synset POS: n
