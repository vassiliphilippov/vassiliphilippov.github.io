flophouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɑpˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈflɒpˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A very cheap, often run-down lodging house for people who have little money.
cheap lodging house

Examples

After losing his job, he spent several nights in a flophouse downtown.
The detective found the missing man living in a flophouse near the train station.
Many immigrants in the early 1900s resorted to staying in flophouses when they first arrived.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hostel - inexpensive accommodation, often shared

Etymology

From the verb 'flop' (to drop down suddenly) + 'house'; originated in the early 20th century U.S. slang.
