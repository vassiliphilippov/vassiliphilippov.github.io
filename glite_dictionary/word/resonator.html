resonator

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛzəˌneɪtər / (AmE), / ˈrɛzəneɪtə / (BrE)
An electrical circuit containing capacitance and inductance that allows a periodic electric oscillation to reach its maximum amplitude.
circuit that maximizes electric oscillations

Examples

The radio uses a resonator to select the desired frequency.
At certain frequencies, the resonator amplifies electrical oscillations.
Engineers adjusted the resonator circuit to achieve maximum signal strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oscillator - electrical circuit that produces a repeating signal

Etymology

From Latin 'resonare' (to resound) + '-or' (agent suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛzəˌneɪtər / (AmE), / ˈrɛzəneɪtə / (BrE)
a device or system that naturally oscillates at certain frequencies, often amplifying or filtering specific vibrations or waves
system or device that oscillates at specific frequencies

Examples

The guitar body acts as a resonator, amplifying the sound of the vibrating strings.
In microwave technology, a cavity resonator is essential for selecting certain frequencies.
Many musical instruments include a resonator to enrich their tonal quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oscillator - a device that produces regular oscillations

Etymology

From Latin 'resonare' (to resound) + '-tor' (agent suffix).

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɛzəˌneɪtəɹ / (AmE), / ˈrɛzəneɪtə / (BrE)
A hollow chamber or device designed so that electromagnetic or sound waves resonate at particular frequencies.
device that produces resonance

Examples

The violin's sound is amplified by its wooden resonator.
Microwave ovens often use a cavity resonator to contain the energy.
He tuned the resonator to match the frequency of the incoming sound waves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oscillator - device that creates oscillating signals or waves
  • Antonym: dampener - device that reduces or stops resonance

Etymology

From Latin 'resonare' (to resound) + '-tor' (agent suffix).
