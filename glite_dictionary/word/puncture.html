puncture

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌŋk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈpʌŋk.tʃə / (BrE)
a small hole made by a sharp object, especially in a surface such as a tyre or the skin
small hole made by a sharp object

Examples

The doctor said the wound was a simple puncture that would heal quickly.
She found a puncture in the car tire, probably caused by a nail on the road.
Be careful not to get a puncture when walking through the construction site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hole - an opening or gap in something

Etymology

From Latin 'punctura' (a pricking), from 'pungere' (to prick).

/ ˈpʌŋktʃər / (AmE), / ˈpʌŋktʃə / (BrE)
