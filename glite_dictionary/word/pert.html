pert

1. ADJ

/ pɝt / (AmE), / pɜːt / (BrE)
(especially of a person's manner or appearance) attractively lively, exuberant, and slightly irreverent.
attractively lively or slightly irreverent

Examples

She gave the teacher a pert reply that made the whole class laugh.
Her pert smile hinted at a mischievous sense of humor.
The actress played a pert and energetic character in the comedy.

Synset Examples

a certain irreverent gaiety and ease of manner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impudent - not showing due respect for another person; impertinent
  • Antonym: demure - reserved, modest, and shy in manner

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French *aperter* (to show off), from Latin *apertura* (an opening), root sense of boldness or forwardness.
