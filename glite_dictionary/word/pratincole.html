pratincole

1. NOUN

/ ˈprætɪnˌkoʊl / (AmE), / ˈpratɪnkəʊl / (BrE)
An Old World shorebird with long, pointed wings and short legs, closely related to the coursers.
Old World shorebird with pointed wings

Examples

A flock of pratincoles skimmed low over the water at sunset.
The pratincole is easily recognized by its long wings and forked tail.
Researchers observed several pratincoles nesting along the riverbank.

Etymology

From French *pratincole*, from Italian *pratincola*, from *prato* (meadow) + *incola* (inhabitant).
