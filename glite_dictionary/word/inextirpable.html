inextirpable

1. ADJ

/ ɪn.ˈɛk.stɚˌpeɪ.bəl / (AmE), / ɪn.ˈɛk.stɜːˌpə.bəl / (BrE)
Impossible to destroy or root out completely; incapable of being eradicated or exterminated.
incapable of being eradicated

Examples

The belief in justice proved inextirpable even through generations of repression.
After years of trying to rid the garden of bamboo, its roots remained inextirpable.
There are some traditions that seem inextirpable in rural communities.

Synset Examples

some weeds seem inextirpable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indestructible - impossible to destroy
  • Antonym: extirpable - capable of being eradicated or rooted out

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + extirpabilis (able to be rooted out).
