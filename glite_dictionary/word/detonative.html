detonative

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛtəˌneɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈdɛtənətɪv / (BrE)
Exploding or causing to explode almost instantaneously; relating to detonation.
exploding instantly; relating to detonation

Examples

Some chemicals are classified as detonative because they explode almost instantaneously upon ignition.
The engineers studied the detonative properties of the fuel mixture to improve safety.
The blast resulted from a detonative reaction in the confined chamber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explosive - able or likely to explode; causing explosion
  • Antonym: deflagrative - relating to combustion that is less violent and slower than detonation

Etymology

From Latin detonare (to thunder down), with English suffix -ive forming adjectives.
