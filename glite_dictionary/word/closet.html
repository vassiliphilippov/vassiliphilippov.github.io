closet

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɑːzət / (AmE), / ˈklɒzɪt / (BrE)
a tall piece of furniture with a door and rails or hooks, used for storing clothes
furniture for hanging clothes

Examples

She opened the closet and selected a dress for the evening.
There are extra blankets stored in the hallway closet.
After moving in, he assembled a new closet to organize his clothes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wardrobe - a large piece of furniture for storing clothes

Etymology

From Middle English closet (a private room), from Old French, from Latin clausum (enclosure).

2. NOUN

/ ˈklɑːzət / (AmE), / ˈklɒzɪt / (BrE)
(British) A toilet or lavatory; formerly used to refer to a small private room containing a toilet.
toilet or lavatory (British)
british

Examples

She excused herself to use the closet at the end of the hall.
In older British homes, a water closet was often found outside the main building.
The estate agent described the property as having a modern bathroom and a separate closet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lavatory - room or fixture for washing and usually a toilet
  • Synonym: restroom - public toilet, mainly American

Etymology

From Old French 'clos' (enclosure), from Latin 'clausum' (closed space).

3. NOUN

/ ˈklɑːzət / (AmE), / ˈklɒzɪt / (BrE)
a small room, recess, or cabinet used for storage, especially for clothes
small storage room or cabinet

Examples

She hung her coat neatly in the closet by the door.
After cleaning out the closet, she donated her old clothes to charity.
The apartment has a large closet perfect for storing linens and shoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wardrobe - a large, tall cupboard for storing clothes
  • Synonym: cupboard - a cabinet with shelves for storing dishes, food, or other items

Etymology

From Old French *clos* (enclosure), from Latin *clausum* (closed space).

5. VERB

/ ˈklɑzət / (AmE), / ˈklɒzɪt / (BrE)
to shut oneself or someone else in a private room or small space, especially for concentrated work or discussion
shut in a small space for work or discussion

Examples

The board members closeted themselves in the meeting room for hours to finalize the contract.
She closeted herself with her research notes in preparation for the big presentation.
He closeted his team in the study to brainstorm new strategies.

Etymology

From Old French 'closet', diminutive of 'clos' (enclosure), from Latin 'clausum'.
