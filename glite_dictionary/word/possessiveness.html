possessiveness

1. NOUN

/ pəˈzɛsɪvnəs / (AmE), / pəˈzɛsɪvnɪs / (BrE)
an excessive desire to own, control, or dominate someone or something, especially in personal relationships
excessive desire to control or own

Examples

Her possessiveness made it difficult for her boyfriend to maintain friendships with others.
The parent's possessiveness stifled the child's independence and growth.
Some people mistake caring for possessiveness, but healthy relationships require trust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jealousy - a feeling of envy and suspicion, often about a rival in affection
  • Antonym: generosity - the quality of being generous or unselfish

Etymology

From possessive + -ness; 'possessive' from Latin possessivus, from possidere (to possess).
