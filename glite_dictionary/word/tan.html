tan

1. NOUN

/ tæn / (AmE), / tæn / (BrE)
a light brown coloration of the skin resulting from exposure to sunlight
brown skin from sun exposure

Examples

After a week at the beach, she had a noticeable tan on her arms and face.
He wore sunscreen to avoid getting a tan during the summer hike.
Her tan faded quickly once autumn arrived.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sunburn - reddening and inflammation of the skin from excessive sun exposure

Etymology

Shortened from 'tannum', from Medieval Latin, related to the tanning process for leather.

/ tæn / (AmE), / tæn / (BrE)
the ratio of the length of the side opposite a given angle to the length of the side adjacent to it in a right-angled triangle; short for 'tangent' in trigonometry
trigonometric ratio (opposite/adjacent side)

Examples

In trigonometry class, we learned how to calculate the tan of an angle.

The value of tan theta increases rapidly as theta approaches 90 degrees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangent - the trigonometric function relating the angle to its opposite and adjacent sides

4. NOUN

/ tæn / (AmE), / tæn / (BrE)
a light brown color, similar to that of topaz or lightly tanned skin
a light brown color

Examples

She bought a jacket in a soft tan that matched her boots perfectly.
The walls were painted a warm tan to create a cozy atmosphere.
His shoes were a classic tan that complemented his navy suit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beige - a pale sandy yellowish-brown color
  • Degree Higher: brown - a dark or medium-dark color resembling that of wood or earth
  • Degree Lower: cream - a light yellowish-white color

Etymology

From Old English 'tannian' (to convert hide into leather), later referring to the color of tanned leather.

5. VERB

/ tæn / (AmE), / tan / (BrE)
to treat animal skins and hides with tannic acid or other chemicals so as to convert them into leather
convert hides into leather

Examples

Traditional craftsmen still tan hides using natural plant extracts.
The company tans thousands of animal skins every year for the leather industry.
Before machinery, workers manually tanned skins in large pits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leather - material made from the tanned hide of an animal

Etymology

From Old English 'tannian', from Medieval Latin 'tannāre' (to tan), from 'tannum' (oak bark).
