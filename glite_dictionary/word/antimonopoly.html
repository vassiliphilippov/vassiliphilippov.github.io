antimonopoly

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.ti.məˈnɑː.pə.li / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.məˈnɒ.pə.li / (BrE)
(of laws and regulations) intended to prevent or oppose monopolies, especially to protect trade and commerce from unfair business practices.
opposing monopolies; preventing unfair business practices

Examples

The government introduced new antimonopoly laws to promote fair competition.
Several antimonopoly measures were put in place to break up the dominant corporation.
These antimonopoly regulations ensure that no single company can control the entire industry.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monopoly - exclusive control of a commodity or service in a particular market

Etymology

From anti- (against) and monopoly (exclusive control of a commodity or service).
