nonreflective

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑn.rɪˈflɛk.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.rɪˈflɛk.tɪv / (BrE)
Not capable of reflecting light, sound, or other forms of energy; not reflective.
not capable of reflecting

Examples

The museum displayed paintings behind nonreflective glass to minimize glare.
She preferred nonreflective surfaces for her photography studio to ensure even lighting.
We installed nonreflective film on the windows to keep the room cool and comfortable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reflective - capable of reflecting light or other energy

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' (not) and 'reflective'.
