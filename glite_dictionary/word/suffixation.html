suffixation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌfɪkˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsʌfɪkˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the formation of a word by adding a suffix to a base or root word
formation of a word by adding a suffix

Examples

The word 'happiness' is created by suffixation of '-ness' to 'happy'.
In English, suffixation is a common way to form new nouns and adjectives.
Linguists study suffixation to understand how word formation rules work in different languages.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prefixation - formation of a word by adding a prefix

Etymology

Formed from 'suffix' + '-ation', patterned after Latin-derived formation.
