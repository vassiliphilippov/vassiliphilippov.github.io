squab

1. NOUN

/ skwɑb / (AmE), / skwɒb / (BrE)
the meat of a young pigeon or dove, used as food, usually roasted, broiled, or braised
meat of a young pigeon or dove

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious dish of roasted squab with herbs and vegetables.
Roast squab is considered a delicacy in French cuisine.
She ordered squab at the restaurant, intrigued by its reputation as a tender, flavorful meat.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: pigeon - meat of a mature pigeon used as food
  • Synonym: chicken - meat of a domesticated fowl; common poultry meat

Etymology

From Old French esquab (young bird), possibly of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ skwɑb / (AmE), / skwɒb / (BrE)
a young, unfledged pigeon, typically one that is still in the nest
a young, unfledged pigeon

Examples

We found a tiny squab in the nest under the eaves.
The chef prepared a dish using a tender squab roasted with herbs.
A squab cannot fly until its feathers have fully developed.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: pigeon - a bird of the family Columbidae, often found in cities or parks

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse *skvabb* (a loose, fat person or thing), later used for young birds.
