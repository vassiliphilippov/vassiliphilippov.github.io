to respond

1. VERB

/ rɪˈspɑnd / (AmE), / rɪˈspɒnd / (BrE)
to say something in reply; to answer or react verbally to a statement, question, or situation
reply or answer verbally

Examples

She paused before she responded to the reporter's question.
The teacher asked a question, and several students responded with thoughtful answers.
Please raise your hand if you wish to respond during the discussion.

Synset Examples

She didn't want to answer
answer the question
We answered that we would accept the invitation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: answer - to reply to a question or statement
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately not respond to something

Etymology

From Latin 'respondere' (to answer, promise in return).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00817348-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rɪˈspɑnd / (AmE), / rɪˈspɒnd / (BrE)
to say or do something as a reaction to a statement or action
react by saying or doing something

Examples

She didn't respond to my email until the next morning.
The immune system will respond to the presence of bacteria.
How do you usually respond to criticism at work?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reply - to answer, especially in speech or writing
  • Synonym: to react - to behave in a particular way in response to something
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something

Etymology

From Latin respondere (to answer, reply).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00719282-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ rɪˈspɑnd / (AmE), / rɪˈspɒnd / (BrE)
(of an illness or condition) to improve or react in a desired way to a treatment or stimulus.
react favorably to treatment or stimulus

Examples

The patient’s body responded positively to the medication within a few weeks.
Some plants respond quickly to light, while others take longer to adjust.
The new policy responded as expected, reducing crime rates in the area.

Synset Examples

The cancer responded to the aggressive therapy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to improve - to become better or make better, often as a result of treatment
  • Antonym: decline - to become worse, especially in health or condition, opposite to improve

Etymology

From Latin respondere (to answer, reply).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00720661-v
Synset POS: v
