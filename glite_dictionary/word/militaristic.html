militaristic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɪlɪtəˈrɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɪlɪtəˈrɪstɪk / (BrE)
showing or characterized by a belief in or desire for a strong military capability and the primacy of military values or methods.
relating to military values or aggression

Examples

The government adopted a militaristic stance, seeking to increase defense spending.
His militaristic rhetoric alarmed neighboring countries.
Some films glorify a militaristic culture, emphasizing discipline and obedience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: military - relating to the armed forces
  • Antonym: pacifist - opposed to war or violence as a means of settling disputes

Etymology

From militarism + -istic, indicating 'relating to militarism.'
