congregation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːŋɡrəˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒŋɡrɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
A group of people who regularly gather for religious worship, especially in a church.
group attending religious worship

Examples

The congregation stood to sing the final hymn together.
A small congregation gathers at the old chapel each Sunday.
The priest thanked the congregation for their generous donations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parishioners - members of a particular parish or church
  • Antonym: audience - group of people gathered to watch or listen to something

Etymology

From Latin 'congregatio' (a gathering together), from 'congregare' (to collect into a flock or group).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnɡrɪˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒŋɡrɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
a group of people, animals, or things gathered together in one place
group gathered together

Examples

A large congregation filled the hall for the ceremony.
A congregation of geese gathered by the lake at sunset.
As winter neared, a congregation of students studied together in the library.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dispersal - the act or result of spreading or scattering

3. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːŋɡrəˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒŋɡrɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of gathering or coming together, especially in a group.
the act of gathering together

Examples

The sudden congregation of people in the park surprised the local authorities.
Birds formed a large congregation on the wire as dusk approached.
During the festival, there was a notable congregation of residents in the main square.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assembly - the action of gathering together in one place for a common purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'congregatio', from 'congregare' meaning 'to collect into a flock, gather'.
