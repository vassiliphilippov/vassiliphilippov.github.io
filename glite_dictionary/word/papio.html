Papio

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪpi.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈpæp.i.əʊ / (BrE)
A genus of Old World monkeys that includes the baboons.
genus of monkeys (baboons)

Examples

The genus Papio includes several species of baboons found across Africa.
Researchers have studied the behavior of Papio in their natural habitats for decades.
Unlike macaques, Papio species are known for their complex social structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baboon - a large Old World monkey of the genus Papio

Etymology

From Latin, used as the genus name for baboons.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪpi.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpɪəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of Old World monkeys comprising the baboons.
genus of baboons

Examples

The genus Papio includes several species of baboons found throughout Africa.
Researchers have studied Papio to understand primate social structures.
Fossils show that Papio had a wider historical range than today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baboon - large, ground-dwelling Old World monkey belonging to the genus Papio

Etymology

From Latin *Papio*, the scientific genus name introduced in the 18th century.
