to tippytoe

1. VERB

/ ˈtɪpiˌtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtɪpiˌtəʊ / (BrE)
to walk quietly and carefully on the tips of one's toes, often to avoid making noise
walk quietly on tiptoes
informal

Examples

She tippytoed across the creaky floorboards to avoid waking the baby.
The children tried to tippytoe past the kitchen so their parents wouldn't hear them.
He tippytoed into the room, carrying the birthday cake with the candles lit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiptoe - walk on the tips of one's toes

Etymology

Formed from 'tiptoe,' likely with playful reduplication to 'tippy-' for emphasis; first recorded in the late 19th century.
