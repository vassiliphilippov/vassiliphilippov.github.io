deshabille

1. NOUN

/ dɛʒəˈbiːl / (AmE), / deɪˈzæbɪl / (BrE)
the state of being only partly or carelessly dressed
state of partial or careless dress

Examples

Sarah opened the door in a state of deshabille, surprised by the unexpected visitor.
The artist painted his subject in gentle deshabille, adding a sense of intimacy to the portrait.
He felt embarrassed when his friend saw him in deshabille early in the morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undress - the state of not being fully dressed

Etymology

From French déshabillé, past participle of déshabiller (to undress).
