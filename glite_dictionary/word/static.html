2. NOUN

/ ˈstætɪk / (AmE), / ˈstatɪk / (BrE)
A crackling or hissing noise produced by electrical interference, commonly heard on radio or television receivers.
crackling noise from electrical interference

Examples

The old radio was full of static when we tried to tune in the station.
A burst of static interrupted the conversation on the walkie-talkie.
Lightning during the storm caused a lot of static on the TV.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interference - disturbance in a signal that disrupts transmission
