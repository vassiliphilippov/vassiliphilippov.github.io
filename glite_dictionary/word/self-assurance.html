self-assurance

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf əˈʃʊrəns / (AmE), / ˌself əˈʃɔːrəns / (BrE)
Confidence in your own abilities or judgment; freedom from doubt about yourself.
confidence in one's abilities

Examples

Her self-assurance was evident as she delivered the speech without hesitation.
Training and practice helped boost his self-assurance before the audition.
You need self-assurance to handle challenging situations effectively.

Synset Examples

his assurance in his superiority did not make him popular
after that failure he lost his confidence
she spoke with authority

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-confidence - belief in your own abilities or worth
  • Antonym: self-doubt - lack of confidence in oneself

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' + 'assurance', both from Middle English and Old French roots.
