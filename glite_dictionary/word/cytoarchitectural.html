cytoarchitectural

1. ADJ

/ ˌsaɪ.toʊˌɑːrk.ɪˈtɛk.tʃɚ.əl / (AmE), / ˌsaɪ.təʊˌɑː.kɪˈtɛk.tʃər.əl / (BrE)
relating to the cellular composition and arrangement of tissues, especially in the nervous system or brain.
relating to cellular structure of tissues
medical

Examples

The cytoarchitectural differences between brain regions help scientists identify functional areas.
A cytoarchitectural analysis can reveal abnormalities in the cerebral cortex of patients with neurological diseases.
Researchers published a comprehensive map of the human cortex based on cytoarchitectural features.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cytoarchitecture - the arrangement of cells in a tissue

Etymology

From 'cytoarchitecture' (cyto- meaning 'cell' + architecture).
