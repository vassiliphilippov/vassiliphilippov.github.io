Ulvaceae

1. NOUN

/ ʌlˈveɪsiaɪ / (AmE), / ʌlˈviːsiːiː / (BrE)
a family of thin, flat or tubular green algae found mainly in marine environments, including sea lettuce
family of green algae

Examples

Members of the Ulvaceae family are commonly found in coastal waters worldwide.
Sea lettuce, a popular edible algae, belongs to the Ulvaceae.
Scientists are studying the growth habits of Ulvaceae in response to pollution.

Etymology

From Latin 'ulva' (seaweed) and scientific suffix '-aceae' (family).
