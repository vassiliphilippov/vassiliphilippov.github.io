Saint Nicholas

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈnɪkələs / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈnɪkələs / (BrE)
A Christian bishop in Asia Minor during the 4th century who is the basis for the figure of Santa Claus.
4th-century bishop; basis for Santa Claus

Examples

Many Christmas traditions can be traced back to Saint Nicholas and his acts of generosity.
Children in some European countries still receive gifts on Saint Nicholas Day in December.
The legend of Saint Nicholas inspired the modern image of Santa Claus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Santa Claus - The legendary figure who delivers presents at Christmas, based on Saint Nicholas

Etymology

From Greek 'Nikolaos', composed of 'nikē' (victory) and 'laos' (people).

2. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈnɪkələs / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈnɪkələs / (BrE)
A legendary Christian saint regarded as the patron saint of children and, in Western cultures, associated with bringing gifts to children at Christmas.
legendary patron saint of children; Christmas gift-bringer

Examples

Every year, children look forward to the arrival of Saint Nicholas on Christmas Eve.
In many European countries, gifts are exchanged in honor of Saint Nicholas.
The story of Saint Nicholas has inspired countless holiday traditions around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Santa Claus - a mythical figure based on Saint Nicholas who brings gifts at Christmas

Etymology

From Greek *Nikolaos* (victory of the people), via Latin *Nicholaus*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10570230-n
Synset POS: n
