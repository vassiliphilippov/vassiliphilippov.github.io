Cheviots

1. PROPN

/ ˈʃiː.vi.əts / (AmE), / ˈʃiː.vi.əts / (BrE)
a range of hills located on the border between England and Scotland, known for their scenic views and as the source of the River Tweed
hills on the England-Scotland border

Examples

We went hiking in the Cheviots last summer and enjoyed the breathtaking scenery.
The Cheviots form a natural barrier between northern England and southern Scotland.
Many rivers, including the Tweed, have their sources in the Cheviots.

Etymology

Named after the Old English 'Ceviot', possibly of Celtic origin referring to hilly land.
