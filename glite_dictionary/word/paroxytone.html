paroxytone

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəksɪˈtoʊn / (AmE), / ˌparəksɪˈtəʊn / (BrE)
A word in which the stress or acute accent falls on the next to last (penultimate) syllable.
word stressed on the penultimate syllable

Examples

In Spanish, most nouns are paroxytone, with the stress falling on the second-to-last syllable.
The word 'banana' is a paroxytone because the stress is on the 'na'.
Linguists often categorize words as paroxytones, oxytones, or proparoxytones based on their stress patterns.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: oxytone - word stressed on the last syllable
  • Degree Lower: proparoxytone - word stressed on the third-to-last syllable

Etymology

From Greek 'paroxytónos', from 'para-' (beside) and 'oxytónos' (sharp-toned, acute accent).
