inanition

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnəˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnəˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
exhaustion or weakness resulting from lack of nourishment or food; the state of being empty or deprived of vital energy.
exhaustion from lack of food

Examples

After days lost at sea, the sailors were suffering from inanition and could barely move.
The doctor recognized the signs of inanition in the severely malnourished child.
Refugees arriving at the camp showed symptoms of inanition after weeks without adequate food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starvation - suffering or death caused by lack of food
  • Degree Lower: hunger - the physical sensation resulting from lack of food

Etymology

From Latin 'inanitio', from 'inanire' (to make empty), from 'inanis' (empty).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnəˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnəˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
exhaustion or weakness caused by lack of nourishment or energy; a state of being empty or devoid of vitality
exhaustion from lack of energy or nourishment

Examples

After days without food, his body was weakened by inanition.
The patient showed signs of inanition due to prolonged illness.
Her creative spirit was drained to the point of inanition after months of stress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhaustion - a state of extreme physical or mental fatigue
  • Antonym: vitality - the state of being strong and active; energy

Etymology

From Latin inanitionem (emptiness), from inanire (to empty), from inanis (empty).
