souari

1. NOUN

/ suːˈɑːri / (AmE), / suːˈɑːri / (BrE)
A large evergreen tree native to South America, with trifoliate leaves, producing drupes whose seeds are eaten or used for cooking oil.
South American evergreen tree, edible seeds

Examples

The souari provides nutlike seeds that are popular as snacks in some regions.
Oil extracted from souari seeds is often used in traditional South American cuisine.
A mature souari can reach impressive heights in the Amazon rainforest.

Etymology

Probably from a native South American language; used in early botanical literature.
