biometrics

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊˈmɛtrɪks / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əˈmɛtrɪks / (BrE)
The branch of biology that uses statistical methods to analyze biological data and phenomena.
statistical analysis of biological data

Examples

Researchers used biometrics to assess the effectiveness of the new treatment on cell growth.
Advancements in biometrics have improved our understanding of genetic variation in populations.
He specializes in biometrics and often collaborates with statisticians and biologists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biometry - statistical analysis of biological data

Etymology

From Greek bios (life) and metron (measure), via French biométrie.
