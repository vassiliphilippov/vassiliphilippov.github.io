concordat

1. NOUN

/ kɑnˈkɔr dæt / (AmE), / kɒnˈkɔː dæt / (BrE)
a formal agreement, especially one between the Vatican and a government, or between two states, to perform a specific action or establish mutual relations
formal agreement, often between governments

Examples

The concordat was signed by the two nations after months of negotiation.
Under the terms of the concordat, the church gained certain privileges.
Diplomatic relations improved following the historic concordat between the parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treaty - a formally concluded and ratified agreement between countries
  • Synonym: accord - an official agreement or treaty
  • Antonym: disagreement - lack of consensus or harmony; opposite of agreement

Etymology

From French 'concordat', from Medieval Latin 'concordatum' (agreement), from Latin 'concordare' (to agree).
