Spitzbergen

1. PROPN

/ spɪtsˈbɜrɡən / (AmE), / spɪtsˈbɛːɡən / (BrE)
the main island of the Svalbard archipelago in the Arctic Ocean, administered by Norway
island in the Svalbard archipelago, Norway

Examples

The polar bear population of Spitzbergen has been closely monitored by Norwegian scientists.
During their Arctic expedition, the team made landfall on Spitzbergen.
The climate of Spitzbergen is harsh, with long, cold winters and short, cool summers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Svalbard - archipelago in the Arctic Ocean, to which Spitzbergen belongs

Etymology

From German 'Spitzbergen', meaning 'pointed mountains', named by Dutch explorer Willem Barentsz in 1596.
