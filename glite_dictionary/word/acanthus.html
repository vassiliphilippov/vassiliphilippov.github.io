acanthus

1. NOUN

/ əˈkænθəs / (AmE), / əˈkanθəs / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Acanthus, native to the Mediterranean region, with large spiny leaves and spikes of white or purplish flowers, often cultivated for ornament
Mediterranean plant with spiny leaves and flowers

Examples

The decorative motif on the column capital was inspired by the acanthus plant.
Several varieties of acanthus are popular in Mediterranean gardens for their striking foliage.
She planted acanthus along the walkway to add texture and color to the garden.

Etymology

From Greek akanthos, from akantha (thorn, spine).
