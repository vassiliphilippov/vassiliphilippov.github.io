mezzo-soprano

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtsoʊ səˈprænoʊ / (AmE), / ˌmɛtsəʊ səˈprɑːnəʊ / (BrE)
A female singing voice with a range between soprano and contralto, or a singer with such a voice.
female singing voice between soprano and contralto

Examples

The role was perfectly suited to her rich mezzo-soprano voice.
Several distinguished mezzo-sopranos performed at the festival last year.
She trained as a mezzo-soprano before joining the opera company.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: soprano - the highest female singing voice
  • Degree Lower: contralto - the lowest female singing voice

Etymology

From Italian mezzo (middle) + soprano (soprano, from sopra ‘above’).
