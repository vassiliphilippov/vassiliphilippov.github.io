quartzose

1. ADJ

/ ˈkwɔr(t)soʊs / (AmE), / ˈkwɔːtsəʊs / (BrE)
(of rock or material) consisting of or containing quartz.
consisting of or containing quartz

Examples

The geologist noted that the quartzose sandstone was unusually pure.
Many mountain ranges are composed of quartzose rocks that are resistant to weathering.
Her research focused on the properties of quartzose soils in arid environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quartz - a hard mineral composed of silicon dioxide

Etymology

From quartz + -ose, modeled after similar scientific adjectives.
