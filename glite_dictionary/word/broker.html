broker

1. NOUN

/ ˈbroʊkər / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊkə / (BrE)
a person who buys and sells goods or assets for others in exchange for a commission
person who buys/sells for others for commission

Examples

He hired a broker to help him purchase shares in the company.
Real estate brokers often charge a commission on successful sales.
The insurance broker found the best policy for our needs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agent - a person who acts on behalf of another
  • Synonym: dealer - a person who buys and sells a particular product
  • Antonym: customer - a person who purchases goods or services

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *brocour* (dealer), from Old French *broceur* (small trader).

2. VERB

/ ˈbroʊkər / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊkə / (BrE)
to arrange or negotiate a deal, agreement, or settlement between parties as a broker
arrange or negotiate as a broker

Examples

She was able to broker a peace agreement between the two countries.
The consultant will broker the merger of the two companies.
He has brokered several major real estate deals over his career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to negotiate - discuss to reach an agreement
