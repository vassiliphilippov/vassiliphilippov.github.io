switcheroo

1. NOUN

/ ˌswɪtʃəˈru / (AmE), / ˌswɪtʃəˈruː / (BrE)
an unexpected or sudden change or exchange, especially one made in a surprising or deceptive way
sudden or unexpected switch
informal american

Examples

There was a classic switcheroo at the end of the movie that nobody saw coming.
The magician performed a switcheroo with the cards, amazing the audience.
After months of planning, they pulled a last-minute switcheroo that surprised everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: switch - to change or exchange something for another

Etymology

Formed by adding the playful suffix '-eroo' to 'switch', likely popularized in US English in the early 20th century.
