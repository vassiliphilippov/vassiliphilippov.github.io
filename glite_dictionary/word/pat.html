/ pæt / (AmE), / pæt / (BrE)

completely or perfectly (esp. after much practice)

Examples

He recited the poem pat after weeks of practice.
The pianist played the difficult passage pat, impressing the judges.

2. NOUN

/ pæt / (AmE), / pæt / (BrE)
a quick, gentle touch or stroke with the hand or a flat object, usually to show affection, approval, or comfort
a gentle touch or stroke

Examples

He gave the dog a gentle pat on the head before leaving.
With a reassuring pat on her shoulder, he signaled that everything would be alright.
She finished icing the cake, then gave it a final pat to smooth the sides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tap - a light touch or blow, especially with a slight sound

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound or action of a light tap or stroke.

Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ pæt / (AmE), / pæt / (BrE)
(of an answer, explanation, etc.) appearing simple or plausible but lacking real depth, thought, or sincerity; superficial or glib.
superficially plausible; glib

Examples

Her explanation sounded pat, as if she'd prepared it in advance.
He always has a pat answer for every difficult question.
The solution offered by the company seemed a little too pat to be genuine.

Synset Examples

glib promises
a slick commercial

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glib - (of words or people) fluent but insincere and shallow

Etymology

Probable shortening of 'pat answer', meaning a quick, ready reply, origin uncertain.
