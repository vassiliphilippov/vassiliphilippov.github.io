appendicitis

1. NOUN

/ əˌpɛndəˈsaɪtɪs / (AmE), / əˌpɛndɪˈsaɪtɪs / (BrE)
a medical condition in which the appendix becomes inflamed and painful, usually requiring surgery
inflammation of the appendix
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with appendicitis after he complained of severe abdominal pain.
Surgery is often required to treat appendicitis before the appendix bursts.
Children can sometimes develop appendicitis with sudden symptoms.

Etymology

From Latin 'appendix' (appendage) + '-itis' (inflammation).
