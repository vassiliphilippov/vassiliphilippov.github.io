Clusia flava

1. NOUN

/ kluːˈziːə ˈfleɪvə / (AmE), / kluːˈziːə ˈfleɪvə / (BrE)
A species of tree native to the West Indies, known for its fig-shaped fruit.
West Indies tree with fig-shaped fruit

Examples

The gardens feature a rare Clusia flava tree with its distinctive fig-shaped fruit.
Researchers in the West Indies recently discovered new medicinal properties in Clusia flava.
Locals use the fruit of Clusia flava in traditional remedies due to its unique qualities.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Clusia' (named after Carolus Clusius, botanist) and the Latin 'flava' meaning 'yellow'.
