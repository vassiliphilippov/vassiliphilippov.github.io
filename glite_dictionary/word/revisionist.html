revisionist

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈvɪʒənɪst / (AmE), / rɪˈvɪʒənɪst / (BrE)
(in Communist or Marxist contexts) a person who attempts to reinterpret or modify orthodox Marxist doctrines, often to justify a less revolutionary or more moderate approach.
Communist who modifies Marxist doctrine

Examples

He was labeled a revisionist by party hardliners for advocating reform.
The regime accused its critics of revisionist tendencies that betrayed Marxist principles.
Debates erupted within the movement over whether revisionist ideas were corrupting true socialism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orthodox - adhering strictly to traditional Marxist doctrine

Etymology

From 'revision' + '-ist'; the sense developed in early 20th-century Marxist debates.
