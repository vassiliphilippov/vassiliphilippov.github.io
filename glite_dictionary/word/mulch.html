mulch

1. NOUN

/ mʌltʃ / (AmE), / mʌltʃ / (BrE)
Material such as decaying leaves, bark, or compost spread around or over a plant to retain moisture, suppress weeds, and improve soil quality.
protective garden covering material

Examples

Gardeners often add a layer of mulch to keep the soil moist during summer.
After planting the shrubs, she spread mulch around their bases to prevent weeds.
Wood chips make an effective and attractive mulch for flower beds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compost - decayed organic material used as plant fertilizer

Etymology

Originated in the mid-17th century, possibly related to Middle High German 'molsch' (soft, decaying).

2. VERB

/ mʌltʃ / (AmE), / mʌltʃ / (BrE)
to cover the surface of soil with mulch in order to retain moisture, improve fertility, and reduce weed growth
to cover soil with mulch

Examples

Every spring, we mulch the vegetable garden to keep weeds under control.
It is best to mulch around the base of trees to conserve water.
The gardeners mulched the flowerbeds with bark chips after planting.

Synset Examples

mulch the flowerbeds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - to put something on top of something else, especially for protection
  • Antonym: to uncover - to remove a covering from something

Etymology

Probably from Middle English molsh (soft, moist), of Germanic origin.
