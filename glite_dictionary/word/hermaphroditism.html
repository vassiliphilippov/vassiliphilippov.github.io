hermaphroditism

1. NOUN

/ hɚˈmæf.rəˌdaɪ.tɪ.zəm / (AmE), / hɜːˈmæf.rə.daɪ.tɪ.zəm / (BrE)
The condition of having both male and female reproductive organs or characteristics.
condition of having both male and female sexual traits
medical

Examples

Some plants exhibit hermaphroditism, allowing a single flower to produce both pollen and ovules.
In certain species of fish, hermaphroditism is a normal part of their life cycle.
He studied the genetic factors that can lead to hermaphroditism in mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: androgyny - the quality of being neither specifically masculine nor feminine

Etymology

From Greek Hermaphroditos, the offspring of Hermes and Aphrodite, who had both male and female traits.

2. NOUN

/ hɚˈmæf.rəˌdaɪ.tɪz.əm / (AmE), / hɜːˈmæf.rə.daɪ.tɪ.zəm / (BrE)
a congenital condition in which an individual has both male and female reproductive organs or sexual characteristics
possession of both male and female sexual characteristics

Examples

The doctor explained that hermaphroditism is a rare condition in both humans and animals.
Some plants exhibit hermaphroditism, producing both male and female reproductive structures.
Medical textbooks often discuss hermaphroditism in the context of developmental biology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intersex - a general term for a variety of conditions in which a person is born with reproductive or sexual anatomy that doesn’t fit typical definitions of male or female

Etymology

From Greek Hermaphroditos, a mythical figure born of Hermes and Aphrodite, symbolizing male and female qualities.
