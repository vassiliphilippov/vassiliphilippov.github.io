Equisetum hyemale robustum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkwəˈsiːtəm haɪˈɛməli roʊˈbʌstəm / (AmE), / ˌɛkwɪˈsiːtəm hʌɪˈɛməli rə(ʊ)ˈbʌstəm / (BrE)
a subspecies of evergreen horsetail plant with rough-edged, erect stems, formerly used for scouring utensils because of its abrasive texture
a subspecies of evergreen horsetail plant

Examples

Native Americans often used Equisetum hyemale robustum to clean and polish metal tools.
The rough stems of Equisetum hyemale robustum make it effective for scouring pots.
You can find Equisetum hyemale robustum growing along riverbanks throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scouring rush - common name for Equisetum hyemale, used to scrub pots and pans
  • Synonym: horsetail - common name for plants of the genus Equisetum

Etymology

From Latin: Equisetum (genus name, 'horse hair'), hyemale ('of winter'), robustum ('strong, robust').
