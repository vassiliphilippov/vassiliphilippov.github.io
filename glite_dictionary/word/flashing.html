2. NOUN

/ ˈflæʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflæʃɪŋ / (BrE)
A thin sheet of metal, shaped and installed on a roof or other structure to prevent water penetration and provide structural strength, especially at joints or angles.
thin metal sheet for waterproofing joints

Examples

The roofer carefully installed new flashing around the chimney to prevent leaks.
Improperly fitted flashing can allow water to seep into the attic.
Copper is often used for flashing because of its durability and resistance to corrosion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weatherproofing - the process or materials used to make something resistant to weather
