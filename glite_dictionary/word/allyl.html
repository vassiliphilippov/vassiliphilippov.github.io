allyl

1. NOUN

/ ˈælɪl / (AmE), / ˈalɪl / (BrE)
the univalent unsaturated organic radical C3H5, derived from propylene and found in various organic compounds
organic radical C3H5

Examples

Many flavor compounds in garlic are due to the presence of the allyl group.
The synthesis involved attaching an allyl radical to the aromatic ring.
Researchers studied the reactivity of allyl derivatives in the reaction.

Etymology

From English "allium" (genus of onions), referring to its occurrence in garlic oil.
