jampan

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæmpæn / (AmE), / ˈdʒampan / (BrE)
A type of sedan chair formerly used in India for carrying people, typically borne by porters.
sedan chair used in India

Examples

The wealthy merchant was carried through the bustling marketplace in a jampan.
Tourists marveled at the ornate jampan displayed in the museum's colonial India exhibit.
In old photographs, British officers can sometimes be seen riding in a jampan up the mountain paths.

Etymology

From Hindi 'jāmpān', likely of uncertain origin.
