unnecessarily

1. ADV

/ ˌʌn.nəˈsɛs.əˌrɪli / (AmE), / ˌʌn.nɪˈsɛs.ər.ɪli / (BrE)
in a way that is not needed or required
in an unnecessary manner

Examples

She became upset when he unnecessarily criticized her efforts.
The road was unnecessarily blocked for hours after the event.
Don't make the process more difficult unnecessarily.

Synset Examples

they were unnecessarily rude

Related Terms

  • Synonym: needlessly - without necessity
  • Antonym: necessarily - in an essential or unavoidable way

Etymology

Formed from 'unnecessary' + '-ly' (adverbial suffix).

2. ADV

/ ˌʌnˈnɛsəˌsɛrɪli / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈnɛsəsərəli / (BrE)
without need or justification; more than what is required
without need or justification

Examples

She apologized unnecessarily because no one was offended by her comment.
The company spent unnecessarily on luxury furniture for the office.
Please don't worry unnecessarily; everything is going to be fine.

Synset Examples

this marathon would exhaust him unnecessarily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: needlessly - without need; for no good reason
  • Antonym: necessarily - in a way that is required or unavoidable

Etymology

Formed from 'unnecessary' + adverbial suffix '-ly'; 'unnecessary' from Latin 'necessarius' (necessary).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00181163-r
Synset POS: r
