longicorn

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔŋ.ɪ.kɔrn / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋ.ɪ.kɔːn / (BrE)
A long-bodied beetle distinguished by very long antennae, especially those of the family Cerambycidae.
beetle with very long antennae

Examples

The longicorn is easily recognized by its extremely long antennae, often longer than its body.
We found a rare species of longicorn dwelling in the decaying wood of an old tree.
Some farmers monitor longicorn populations because their larvae can damage timber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cerambycid - a beetle of the family Cerambycidae; longhorn beetle
  • Degree Lower: beetle - insect of the order Coleoptera, hard forewings

Etymology

From Latin 'longus' (long) + 'cornu' (horn), referring to the long antennae.
