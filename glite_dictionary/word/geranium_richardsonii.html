Geranium richardsonii

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈreɪniəm rɪʃɑrdˈsəniː / (AmE), / dʒəˈreɪnɪəm rɪtʃɑːdˈsəniː / (BrE)
A species of wild geranium native to western North America, noted for branched clusters of white or pale pink flowers.
a wild geranium of western North America

Examples

The meadows were dotted with blooming Geranium richardsonii throughout the summer.
Botanists identified the specimen as Geranium richardsonii because of its distinct pale pink flowers.
Hikers often admire the delicate clusters of Geranium richardsonii along mountain trails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geranium - any plant of the genus Geranium, known for palmately lobed leaves and showy flowers

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Geranium' and the species epithet honoring botanist John Richardson.
