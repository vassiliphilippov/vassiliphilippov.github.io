syringeful

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈrɪndʒfʊl / (AmE), / sɪˈrɪn(d)ʒfʊl / (BrE)
An amount of liquid that fills a syringe.
quantity a syringe holds
medical

Examples

The nurse drew a syringeful of vaccine before administering it to the patient.
He injected a syringeful of saline solution into the IV line.
Just one syringeful of the antibiotic was needed for each laboratory sample.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoonful - the amount a spoon can hold

Etymology

From 'syringe' + '-ful', patterned after forms like 'cupful'.
