Banksia integrifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋksiə ˌɪntəɡrəˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈbaŋksɪə ˌɪntɪɡrəˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A species of banksia tree native to eastern Australia, with silky green foliage and spikes of cylindrical yellow, nectar-rich flowers.
Australian banksia tree with yellow flower spikes

Examples

The Banksia integrifolia thrives along the sandy coasts of eastern Australia.
Gardeners often choose Banksia integrifolia for its resilience and attractive, nectar-rich flowers.
Several birds and insects visit Banksia integrifolia during its long flowering season.

Etymology

Genus name from Sir Joseph Banks (1743–1820), English botanist; species name from Latin 'integra' (entire) + 'folia' (leaves).
