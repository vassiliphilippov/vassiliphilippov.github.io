Sciaena aquila

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈiːnə ˈakwɪlə / (AmE), / saɪˈiːnə ˈakwɪlə / (BrE)
A large European marine food fish; a species of drum found in eastern Atlantic coasts.
large European marine food fish

Examples

The chef prepared a dish using freshly caught Sciaena aquila for the restaurant's special menu.
Commercial fishing of Sciaena aquila has increased along the Atlantic coasts in recent years.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of Sciaena aquila in the Mediterranean Sea.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Sciaena', Latin for 'sea-fish'; species 'aquila' meaning 'eagle'.
