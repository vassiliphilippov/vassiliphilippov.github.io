Leonotis nepetaefolia

1. NOUN

/ liˌɑːnoʊˈtaɪs nɛˌpɛtəˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / liˌɒnəʊˈtɪs nɛˌpɛtəˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A tropical herbaceous plant of the mint family, notable for its whorls of bright orange, lipped flowers; native to Africa and Asia but now widely naturalized elsewhere.
a tropical plant with orange lipped flowers

Examples

The vibrant orange flowers of Leonotis nepetaefolia attract hummingbirds to the garden.
Traditional medicine in some countries uses Leonotis nepetaefolia for its reputed healing properties.
Wild stands of Leonotis nepetaefolia can often be found along roadsides in tropical regions.

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Leonotis' from Greek 'leon' (lion) and 'otos' (ear), referring to the shape of the flowers; species 'nepetaefolia' means 'with leaves like Nepeta' (catnip).
