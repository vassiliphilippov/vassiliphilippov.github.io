merciless

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɜːrsɪləs / (AmE), / ˈmɜːsɪləs / (BrE)
showing no mercy; having or showing a lack of compassion or forgiveness
showing no mercy

Examples

The dictator was merciless in his treatment of political opponents.
Winter was merciless that year, causing widespread suffering.
Critics gave the film a merciless review, pointing out every flaw.

Synset Examples

the merciless enemy
a merciless critic
gave him a merciless beating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruthless - having or showing no pity or compassion for others
  • Antonym: merciful - showing mercy or compassion

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'merci' (mercy) + '-less' (without).
