Zephaniah

1. PROPN

/ zɛfəˈnaɪə / (AmE), / zɛfəˈnaɪə / (BrE)
A Hebrew minor prophet of the late 7th century BC, known for the biblical Book of Zephaniah.
Hebrew minor prophet (7th century BC)

Examples

Zephaniah is traditionally regarded as the author of the Book of Zephaniah in the Old Testament.
The teachings of Zephaniah emphasized judgment and the hope of restoration.
Biblical scholars often discuss Zephaniah in the context of Israel's prophetic history.

Etymology

From Hebrew Ṣəfanyāh (‘the Lord has hidden’), via Greek Zephania(s).

2. PROPN

/ zɛfəˈnaɪ.ə / (AmE), / zɛfəˈnaɪ.ə / (BrE)
a book of the Old Testament containing the prophecies of Zephaniah, mainly concerning God's judgment on Judah.
book of the Old Testament; prophecies of Zephaniah

Examples

Many scholars have studied the message of Zephaniah to understand the historical context of Judah.
The book of Zephaniah predicts the coming judgment and urges repentance.
In his sermon, the pastor quoted from Zephaniah to emphasize the importance of faithfulness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Habakkuk - Old Testament prophetic book
  • Synonym: Nahum - Old Testament prophetic book

Etymology

From Hebrew Ṣəfanyāh (Zephaniah), meaning 'Yahweh has hidden'.
