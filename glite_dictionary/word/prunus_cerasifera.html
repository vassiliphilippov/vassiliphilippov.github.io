Prunus cerasifera

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs sərəˈsɪfərə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs sɛrəˈsɪfərə / (BrE)
A small tree native to Asia, known for its edible red or yellow fruit; commonly called the cherry plum.
species of tree; cherry plum

Examples

The garden was lined with blooming Prunus cerasifera trees in early spring.
Many farmers cultivate Prunus cerasifera for its delicious fruit.
As an ornamental tree, Prunus cerasifera adds vibrant color to urban landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cherry plum - Another name for Prunus cerasifera, a tree bearing edible fruit

Etymology

From Latin: 'prunus' (plum tree) and 'cerasifera' (cherry-bearing).
