succor

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌkər / (AmE), / ˈsʌkə / (BrE)
assistance and support in times of hardship or distress.
aid or help in difficulty

Examples

Volunteers brought much-needed succor to those affected by the hurricane.
In times of war, humanitarian organizations often provide succor to refugees.
She was deeply grateful for the succor offered by friends during her illness.

Synset Examples

the contributions provided some relief for the victims

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relief - help or assistance given to those in need

Etymology

From Old French 'sucor', from Latin 'succurrere' (to run to help).

2. VERB

/ ˈsʌkər / (AmE), / ˈsʌkə / (BrE)
to give assistance or help to someone in a difficult or dangerous situation
give help in difficulty
archaic

Examples

The volunteers rushed to succor the victims after the flood.
She tried to succor her friend during a time of great distress.
Medical teams were sent to succor those wounded in the earthquake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aid - help or support; synonym of succor
  • Synonym: assist - to help someone; synonym of succor
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for or help; antonym of succor

Etymology

From Old French 'soccourir', from Latin 'succurrere' (to run to the help of).
