one-dimensional

1. ADJ

/ ˌwʌn dɪˈmɛnʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌwʌn dɪˈmɛnʃənəl / (BrE)
having or involving a single dimension or relating to something that extends in only one direction, such as a line.
having only one dimension

Examples

The mathematician spoke about a one-dimensional line extending infinitely in both directions.
In physics, movement along a rail is often modeled as one-dimensional motion.
Their analysis was too one-dimensional and did not consider the problem's complexity.

Synset Examples

a linear measurement

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: two-dimensional - having or relating to two dimensions, such as width and height

2. ADJ

/ ˌwʌn dəˈmɛnʃənl̩ / (AmE), / ˌwʌn dɪˈmɛnʃənl̩ / (BrE)
existing or considered in only one dimension or aspect; lacking depth or complexity.
lacking depth or existing in one dimension

Examples

The scientist described the model as one-dimensional because it only accounted for length.
Critics called the villain one-dimensional, arguing he lacked real motivation or complexity.
Her analysis was too one-dimensional, ignoring the economic and social factors involved.

Synset Examples

a prose statement of fact is unidimensional, its value being measured wholly in terms of its truth
a novel with one-dimensional characters

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: two-dimensional - existing or considered in two dimensions; having width and height but no depth

3. ADJ

/ ˌwʌn.dɪˈmɛn.ʃən.əl / (AmE), / ˌwʌn.dɪˈmen.ʃən.əl / (BrE)
lacking depth, complexity, or variety; superficial or simplistic.
lacking depth or complexity

Examples

The main villain was disappointingly one-dimensional and lacked any real motivation.
Some critics found the plot a bit one-dimensional compared to the novel's complexity.
Her performance avoided being one-dimensional by showing both strength and vulnerability.

Synset Examples

The characters in that movie feel very one-dimensional.
His argument was one-dimensional and lacked depth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superficial - not thorough, deep, or complete; lacking depth
  • Antonym: complex - having many parts or aspects; not simple
