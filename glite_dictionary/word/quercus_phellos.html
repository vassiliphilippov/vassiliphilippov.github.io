Quercus phellos

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs ˈfɛlɒs / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs ˈfɛlɒs / (BrE)
a medium to large deciduous oak tree of the eastern United States with long, narrow leaves and soft, strong wood, commonly known as the willow oak
a species of oak tree (willow oak)

Examples

The Quercus phellos is valued for its rapid growth and graceful habit in landscaping.
Many birds and small mammals rely on the acorns produced by the Quercus phellos for food.
The city planted several Quercus phellos along the new boulevard to provide shade and beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow oak - common name for Quercus phellos, an oak tree with narrow leaves

Etymology

From Latin: Quercus (oak) and phellos (Greek for 'cork').
