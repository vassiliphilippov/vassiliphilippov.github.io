weka

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪkə / (AmE), / ˈwiːkə / (BrE)
a flightless bird of New Zealand, known for its inquisitive and bold behavior, with short wings and a spur used in fighting
flightless New Zealand bird

Examples

The weka is known for stealing food and shiny items from campers in New Zealand.
We spotted a curious weka wandering through the forest during our hike.
Unlike many birds, the weka is unable to fly but is a skilled runner.

Etymology

From Maori 'weka', the name of the bird.
