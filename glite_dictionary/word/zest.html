zest

1. NOUN

/ zɛst / (AmE), / zɛst / (BrE)
a pleasantly sharp or spicy taste; a quality of excitement or piquancy
a sharp or spicy taste or quality

Examples

The lemonade had a refreshing zest that made it perfect for a summer afternoon.
The chef added orange zest to give the dessert an extra layer of flavor.
Her writing had a zest that made even ordinary topics exciting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piquancy - pleasantly sharp or appetizing flavor
  • Antonym: blandness - lacking strong features or characteristics; dullness of taste

Etymology

From French *zeste* (piece of orange or lemon peel).

3. VERB

/ zɛst / (AmE), / zɛst / (BrE)
to add herbs, spices, or flavorings to (food or drink) to enhance its taste
add herbs, spices, or flavorings

Examples

She likes to zest her soups with a pinch of fresh herbs before serving.
The chef decided to zest the marinade with some crushed garlic and thyme.
To create a unique flavor, he would zest his dishes with a variety of spices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: season - to add salt, herbs, or spices to food to enhance flavor

Etymology

From French 'zeste', meaning citrus rind; later extended in English to general flavoring.
