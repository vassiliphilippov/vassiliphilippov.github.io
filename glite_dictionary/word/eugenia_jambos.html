Eugenia jambos

1. NOUN

/ juˈdʒiːniə ˈdʒæmˌboʊs / (AmE), / juːˈdʒiːniə ˈdʒæmbɒs / (BrE)
a tropical tree native to the East Indies, cultivated for its edible, apple-like fruit
tropical tree with edible fruit

Examples

The fruit of the Eugenia jambos is often called rose apple due to its delicate fragrance.
Botanists study Eugenia jambos for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
Farmers in tropical regions commonly cultivate Eugenia jambos for its attractive, edible fruit.

Etymology

Modern Latin, from Latinized form of Greek name Eugeneia (a plant genus) + specific epithet from Portuguese jambos (Malay jamboo, meaning guava or rose apple).
