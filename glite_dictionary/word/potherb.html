potherb

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːθ.ɜːrb / (AmE), / ˈpɒθ.ɜːb / (BrE)
an herbaceous plant whose leaves, stems, or flowers are used as food or for seasoning when cooked
herb used in cooking for food or seasoning

Examples

Many traditional recipes call for a mix of finely chopped potherbs to add flavor and aroma.
She grew several types of potherb in her kitchen garden, ready to be picked for stews and soups.
Spinach, chard, and sorrel are all considered potherbs in various cuisines around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herb - any plant with leaves, seeds, or flowers used for flavoring, food, medicine, or perfume

Etymology

From Middle English 'potherbe', meaning an herb used in the pot; 'pot' (cooking vessel) + 'herb'.
