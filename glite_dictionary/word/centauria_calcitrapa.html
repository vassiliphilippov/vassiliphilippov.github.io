Centauria calcitrapa

1. NOUN

/ sɛnˈtɔːriə ˌkælsiˈtreɪpə / (AmE), / sɛnˈtɔːrɪə ˌkælsɪˈtrɑːpə / (BrE)
A Mediterranean annual or biennial plant with pinkish to purple flowers surrounded by spine-tipped scales, naturalized in America.
a Mediterranean flowering plant species

Examples

The field was scattered with Centauria calcitrapa blooming among the wild grasses.
Gardeners sometimes remove Centauria calcitrapa to prevent its spread in new environments.
The characteristic spiny bracts make Centauria calcitrapa easy to identify in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purple starthistle - a synonym for Centauria calcitrapa, a thistle-like plant
  • Degree Higher: Centaurea - the genus including Centauria calcitrapa

Etymology

From Latinized form of Greek kentauros (centaur) and Latin calcitrapa (a kind of thistle).
