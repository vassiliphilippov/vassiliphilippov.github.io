behemoth

1. NOUN

/ bəˈhiːməθ / (AmE), / bɪˈhiːməθ / (BrE)
a person or thing that is extremely large and powerful
something enormous and powerful

Examples

The new tech company became a behemoth in the industry, dominating competitors worldwide.
Standing next to the behemoth of a machine, she felt tiny and insignificant.
That cruise ship is a floating behemoth packed with amenities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant - a person or thing of great size
  • Synonym: colossus - something huge or very powerful
  • Antonym: dwarf - a person or thing of unusually small size

Etymology

From Hebrew 'bəhēmōth', plural of 'bəhēmāh' (beast).

2. NOUN

/ bəˈhiːməθ / (AmE), / bɪˈhiːməθ / (BrE)
a person or organization that is extremely large and powerful, especially one of great importance or influence
very large or powerful person or organization

Examples

The tech company became a behemoth in the global market, dominating competitors.
He was a financial behemoth, influencing economies with every decision he made.
Only a behemoth like her could organize such a massive event successfully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant - a very large and powerful person, organization, or thing
  • Antonym: dwarf - a person, organization, or thing that is much smaller or less influential than others

Etymology

From Hebrew *behemoth* (a huge beast, in the Book of Job), meaning 'beast.'
