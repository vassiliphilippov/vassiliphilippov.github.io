tyranny

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪrəni / (AmE), / ˈtɪrəni / (BrE)
cruel and oppressive government or rule marked by the use of power through threats, punishment, or violence
cruel and oppressive government or rule

Examples

The people lived under the tyranny of a ruthless dictator for decades.
Revolutionaries rose up to end the tyranny imposed on them by the regime.
History is filled with stories of those who fought against tyranny and oppression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: despotism - exercise of absolute power, especially in a cruel and oppressive way
  • Antonym: liberty - the state of being free within society from oppressive restrictions

Etymology

From Old French *tyrannie*, from Latin *tyrannia*, from Greek *tyrannia* (rule of a tyrant).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɪrəni / (AmE), / ˈtɪrəni / (BrE)
A form of government in which absolute power is held by a single ruler or a small group, often exercised cruelly or oppressively, and not limited by laws, constitution, or opposition.
government by absolute ruler; oppressive rule

Examples

The revolution was sparked by the people’s desire to overthrow the king’s tyranny.
Many historians have written about the tyranny that marked the dictator's reign.
Citizens lived in fear under the constant tyranny of the military regime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: despotism - absolute rule by a despot or autocratic leader
  • Antonym: democracy - a system of government by the whole population

Etymology

From Old French *tyrannie*, from Latin *tyrannia*, from Greek *tyrannia* (rule of a tyrant).

Synset POS: n
