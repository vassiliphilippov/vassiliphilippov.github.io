decagram

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛkəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈdɛkəɡram / (BrE)
A metric unit of mass equal to 10 grams.
unit of mass; 10 grams

Examples

The recipe calls for 5 decagrams of sugar.
Each packet weighs exactly one decagram.
Scientists measured the sample's mass as two decagrams.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: gram - basic metric unit of mass, equal to one thousandth of a kilogram
  • Degree Higher: kilogram - metric unit of mass equal to 1,000 grams

Etymology

From French 'décagramme', from Greek 'deka' (ten) + 'gramma' (a small weight).
