duomo

1. NOUN

/ ˈdwoʊmoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdwəʊməʊ / (BrE)
The principal Christian church building in a city, especially in Italy; a cathedral.
principal church (cathedral), especially in Italy

Examples

The famous duomo in Florence attracts millions of visitors each year.
We marveled at the architecture of the city's ancient duomo.
Milan's duomo is renowned for its stunning Gothic facade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cathedral - the principal church of a diocese, containing the bishop's seat

Etymology

From Italian 'duomo', from Latin 'domus' (house, specifically 'domus Dei' - house of God).
