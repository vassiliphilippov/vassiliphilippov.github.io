tri-iodomethane

1. NOUN

/ traɪˌaɪədoʊˈmiːθeɪn / (AmE), / traɪˌaɪədəʊˈmiːθeɪn / (BrE)
a sweet-smelling yellow solid chemical compound (CHI3), also known as iodoform, used as an antiseptic and in chemical analysis
sweet-smelling yellow solid compound (CHI3)

Examples

Tri-iodomethane is recognized for its distinctive yellow color and antiseptic properties.
Chemists sometimes refer to tri-iodomethane by its common name, iodoform.
The presence of tri-iodomethane can be detected through the iodoform test in organic chemistry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iodoform - alternative name for tri-iodomethane (CHI3), used as an antiseptic

Etymology

From tri-, meaning three, and iodo- referring to iodine, plus methane indicating its chemical structure.
