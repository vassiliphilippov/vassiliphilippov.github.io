calamus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.ə.məs / (AmE), / ˈkal.ə.məs / (BrE)
A tropical Asian palm of the genus Calamus, whose light, tough stems are used as a source of rattan canes.
genus of tropical Asian rattan palms

Examples

The stems of the calamus are harvested to make furniture and baskets.
Rattan, which comes from the calamus palm, is prized for its strength and flexibility.
Several species of calamus are cultivated for their long, slender canes.

Etymology

From Latin *calamus* (reed, cane), from Greek *kálamos*.

Many cultures have valued calamus for its supposed medicinal properties throughout history.

4. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.ə.məs / (AmE), / ˈkal.əməs / (BrE)
A perennial marsh plant, also known as sweet flag, with sword-shaped leaves and aromatic roots, often used in herbal medicine and flavoring.
marsh plant with sword-shaped leaves and aromatic roots

Examples

The roots of the calamus plant have been used in traditional remedies for centuries.
After the flood, the calamus grew thick along the muddy banks of the river.
Botanists identified the specimen as calamus due to its aromatic rhizome and sword-like leaves.

Etymology

From Latin *calamus* (reed, pen), from Greek *kálamos* (reed, stalk).
