Cirsium helenioides

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜːr.si.əm hɛˌliː.niˈoʊ.diz / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.si.əm hɛˌliː.niˈəʊ.diːz / (BrE)
A perennial thistle native to northern Europe, with lance-shaped basal leaves and typically solitary reddish-purple flower heads.
a thistle plant of northern Europe

Examples

Cirsium helenioides is well-adapted to the cool, damp climates of northern Europe.
The reddish-purple flowers of Cirsium helenioides attract many pollinators in late summer.
Botanists studied the distribution of Cirsium helenioides across Scandinavia.

Etymology

From Latin: genus 'Cirsium' (thistle) and species 'helenioides' meaning 'like elecampane' (*Inula helenium*).
