heralded

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɛr.əldɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɛr.əldɪd / (BrE)
publicly announced or acclaimed, especially as important or significant.
publicly announced or acclaimed

Examples

The artist's heralded exhibition attracted crowds from across the country.
Scientists were excited about the heralded discovery of a new exoplanet.
Their heralded return to the stage was met with thunderous applause.

Synset Examples

the royal couple's much heralded world tour

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acclaimed - publicly praised or celebrated
  • Antonym: unheralded - not previously announced or expected

Etymology

From 'herald', from Old French heraut, related to the public announcement function of a herald.
