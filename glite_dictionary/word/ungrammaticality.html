ungrammaticality

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnɡrəˌmætɪˈkæləti / (AmE), / ˌʌnɡrəˌmætɪˈkælɪti / (BrE)
The state or quality of not conforming to the rules of grammar.
state of not being grammatical

Examples

The teacher pointed out the ungrammaticality in the student's sentence during the lesson.
Some linguists debate whether the ungrammaticality of certain constructions reflects real language change.
The novel intentionally includes ungrammaticality to represent authentic dialogue.

Synset Examples

Though the ungrammaticality of split verbs is an urban legend, it found its way into The Texas Law Review Manual on Style, which is the arbiter of usage for many law review journals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: grammaticality - the quality of being grammatical

Etymology

From un- (not) + grammatical + -ity (state or condition).
