pain

1. NOUN

/ peɪn / (AmE), / peɪn / (BrE)
Physical suffering or discomfort caused by illness, injury, or disorder; an unpleasant sensory and emotional experience associated with actual or potential tissue damage.
physical suffering or discomfort

Examples

She felt a sharp pain in her shoulder after the fall.
The medication helped to ease the pain of his injury.
He went to the doctor to find out the cause of his stomach pain.

Synset Examples

the patient developed severe pain and distension

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ache - a continuous or prolonged dull pain
  • Antonym: relief - alleviation or removal of pain or discomfort

Etymology

From Old French 'peine', from Latin 'poena' (penalty, punishment).
