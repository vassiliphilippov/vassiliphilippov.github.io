exobiology

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.soʊ.baɪˈɑ.lə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˌɛk.səʊ.baɪˈɒl.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of life beyond Earth and the effects of outer space on living organisms
study of life beyond Earth

Examples

Her research in exobiology focuses on how bacteria survive in space-like conditions.
Many scientists believe that exobiology will yield exciting discoveries about life on other planets.
The field of exobiology combines chemistry, astronomy, and biology to search for extraterrestrial organisms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astrobiology - study of the origin, evolution, and distribution of life in the universe

Etymology

From Greek 'exo-' (outside) + 'biology' (study of living things).
