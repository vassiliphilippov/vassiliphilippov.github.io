Sundacarpus

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌn.dəˈkɑːr.pəs / (AmE), / ˌsʌn.dəˈkɑː.pəs / (BrE)
A genus of coniferous trees native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific, containing the species Sundacarpus amarus.
genus of coniferous trees

Examples

The Sundacarpus genus is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and Australasia.
Researchers have studied the distribution of Sundacarpus in the highland forests.
Botanists recently discovered a new range for Sundacarpus amarus in Papua New Guinea.

Etymology

From Latinized form, combining Sunda (referring to Sundaland in Southeast Asia) and Greek karpos (fruit).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsʌn.dəˈkɑːr.pəs / (AmE), / ˌsʌn.dəˈkɑː.pəs / (BrE)
A genus of coniferous trees in the family Podocarpaceae, containing the species Sundacarpus amarus, native to parts of Southeast Asia and Australasia.
a genus of coniferous trees

Examples

The forest reserve is home to several Sundacarpus trees, which tower above the understory.
Botanists discovered a rare specimen of Sundacarpus in the mountainous region.
The timber from Sundacarpus is valued for its durability in traditional construction.

Etymology

From Latinized Greek elements: 'Sunda' (from Sundaland, region in Southeast Asia) and 'carpus' (fruit).
