Passiflora incarnata

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæsɪˈflɔrə ɪnˌkɑrnˈeɪtə / (AmE), / ˌpæsɪˈflɔːrə ɪnˌkɑːnˈɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of passionflower native to the southern United States, known for its large, insipid berries and showy flowers.
species of passionflower vine

Examples

The fruit of the Passiflora incarnata vine is often called a maypop in the southern United States.
Gardeners appreciate Passiflora incarnata for its beautiful purple flowers and rapid growth.
Some herbal teas use dried leaves from the Passiflora incarnata plant for their calming effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maypop - common name for Passiflora incarnata
  • Degree Higher: passionflower - any plant of the genus Passiflora

Etymology

From Latin: 'Passiflora' (genus for passionflowers) and 'incarnata' meaning 'flesh-colored' or 'rosy'.
