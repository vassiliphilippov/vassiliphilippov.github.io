bolshie

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊlʃi / (AmE), / ˈbɒlʃi / (BrE)
a person with extreme radical opinions or a rebellious attitude
extreme radical or rebel
british

Examples

The student was considered a bit of a bolshie even among the campus activists.
Some older managers see the new recruits as bolshies who refuse to follow the rules.
During the meeting, she was labeled a bolshie for constantly challenging the directors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radical - a person who advocates thorough or complete political or social reform
  • Synonym: rebel - a person who resists authority or control

Etymology

Diminutive of 'Bolshevik', from Russian Bolshevik (‘member of the majority’).
