Siraj ud-Daulah

1. PROPN

/ sɪˈrɑːdʒ ʊd daʊˈlɑː / (AmE), / sɪˈrɑːdʒ ʊd dɔːˈlɑː / (BrE)
(1733–1757) The last independent Nawab of Bengal, known for resisting British colonization and for his role in the events surrounding the Black Hole of Calcutta.
last independent Nawab of Bengal, India (1733–1757)

Examples

Siraj ud-Daulah is remembered for his role in the Battle of Plassey, which changed the course of Indian history.
The events involving Siraj ud-Daulah led to increased British control over Bengal.
Many historians debate the legacy of Siraj ud-Daulah and his resistance to British rule.

Etymology

From Persian 'Siraj ud-Daulah', meaning 'Light of the State'.
