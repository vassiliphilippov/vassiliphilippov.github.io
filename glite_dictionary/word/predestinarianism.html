predestinarianism

1. NOUN

/ priˌdɛstəˈnɛriənˌɪzəm / (AmE), / priˌdɛstɪˈneəriənɪzəm / (BrE)
The doctrine or belief that all events, especially regarding salvation, are determined in advance by divine will or fate.
belief in predestination (theological doctrine)

Examples

His strict adherence to predestinarianism influenced every aspect of his theology.
Debates about predestinarianism have divided many religious communities over the centuries.
The doctrine of predestinarianism was central to that church's system of beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predestination - the doctrine that all events are willed by God
  • Antonym: free will - the doctrine that individuals exercise control over their actions

Etymology

From 'predestinarian' (one who believes in predestination) + '-ism'.
