minimal

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɪnəməl / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪməl / (BrE)
Very small in amount or degree; the least or smallest possible.
smallest possible amount or degree

Examples

The risk to the environment was kept to a minimal level.
The company made only minimal changes to its original proposal.
She spoke in a minimal voice, barely above a whisper.

Synset Examples

needed to enforce minimal standards
her grades were minimal
minimum wage
a minimal charge for the service

Related Terms

  • Antonym: maximum - the greatest possible amount or degree
  • Synonym: minimalist - characterized by using the least amount of something

Etymology

From Latin 'minimus' (smallest) via French 'minimal'.
