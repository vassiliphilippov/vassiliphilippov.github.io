alien

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.li.ən / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.li.ən / (BrE)
a person who does not feel or is not considered as belonging to a particular group, place, or environment
a person who does not belong in a setting

Examples

After moving to the new city, she often felt like an alien among her classmates.
His unconventional ideas made him seem like an alien in the corporate world.
As a newcomer, he couldn't help feeling like an alien at the family reunion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outsider - a person who is not accepted as a member of a group

Etymology

From Latin alienus (belonging to another, foreign).

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈeɪliən / (AmE), / ˈeɪliən / (BrE)
a person from another country who is not a citizen or subject of the country where they are living
foreigner; non-citizen

Examples

The government issued new regulations for all aliens residing in the country.
As an alien, he had to apply for a visa to work legally.
The law grants aliens certain rights and protections under the constitution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foreigner - a person from another country or place
  • Antonym: citizen - a legally recognized subject or national of a state

Etymology

From Latin alienus (belonging to another, foreign).

6. VERB

/ ˈeɪliən / (AmE), / ˈeɪliən / (BrE)
(law) To transfer the ownership of property or rights to another person.
legal transfer of property ownership
legal

Examples

Under the agreement, the seller may alien the land at any time.
The trust prohibited him from aliening his shares to outsiders.
Her grandfather aliened part of the estate to charity in his will.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transfer - to move ownership or possession to another

Etymology

From Latin 'alienare' (to estrange, transfer), from 'alienus' (belonging to another).
