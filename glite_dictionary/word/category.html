category

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætəˌɡɔri / (AmE), / ˈkatɪɡəri / (BrE)
a group of people or things sharing a common attribute; a type or class
group or class with shared characteristics

Examples

Each item in the survey falls into a distinct category.
Books in the library are arranged by subject category.
The movie doesn't fit neatly into any specific category.

Synset Examples

there are two classes of detergents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: class - a set or category of things with shared characteristics
  • Synonym: type - a category of people or things having common characteristics
  • Synonym: group - a number of people or things that are located, gathered, or classed together

Etymology

From Greek katēgoria (accusation, category), from katēgorein (to accuse, to predicate).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæt̬əˌɡɔri / (AmE), / ˈkatɪɡəri / (BrE)
a class or division of people or things regarded as having particular shared characteristics; a grouping within a system or scheme based on common traits
a class or group with shared characteristics

Examples

These books are arranged by category on the shelves.
Applicants are divided into different categories based on their age and experience.
The Nobel Prize recognizes achievements in several distinct categories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: class - a group with shared characteristics
  • Synonym: type - a category or kind of something
  • Degree Lower: subcategory - a more specific division within a category
  • Degree Higher: supercategory - a more general grouping that includes several categories

Etymology

From Greek 'katēgoria' (accusation, category), from 'katēgoros' (accuser, predicate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05847274-n
Synset POS: n
