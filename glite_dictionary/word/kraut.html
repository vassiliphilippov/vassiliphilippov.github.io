Kraut

1. NOUN

/ kraʊt / (AmE), / kraʊt / (BrE)
An offensive term for a person of German descent.
offensive term for a German person
offensive

Examples

The film depicted British soldiers referring to the enemy as Krauts.
Using the slur Kraut in conversation is highly inappropriate and offensive today.
Some American soldiers in World War II called German troops Krauts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: German - a person from Germany

Etymology

From German 'Kraut' meaning 'herb' or 'cabbage', originally referring to sauerkraut and adopted as a derogatory term for Germans during wartime.
