destabilization

1. NOUN

/ diˌsteɪbəlɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌsteɪbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of making something less stable or secure, especially a government, country, or economy.
the act of making less stable

Examples

The sudden withdrawal of foreign aid led to the destabilization of the country's economy.
Political destabilization can occur quickly if leaders lose control of key institutions.
Environmental disasters can contribute to the destabilization of entire regions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stabilization - the process of making something more stable

Etymology

From de- (reverse) + stabilization; patterned after 'destabilize'.

2. NOUN

/ diˌsteɪbəlɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌsteɪbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of causing something to become unstable or lose its equilibrium
loss of stability or equilibrium

Examples

The unexpected turbulence led to the destabilization of the aircraft.
Political unrest can result in the destabilization of government institutions.
Heavy cargo shifting caused the destabilization of the ship during the storm.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stabilization - the act of making something stable or maintaining its equilibrium

Etymology

From de- (removal) + stabilization, after stabilize.
