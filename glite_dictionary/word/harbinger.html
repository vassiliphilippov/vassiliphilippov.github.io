harbinger

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrbɪndʒər / (AmE), / ˈhɑːbɪndʒə / (BrE)
a person or thing that announces or signals the approach of another; a forerunner or precursor.
one that signals the approach of something

Examples

The arrival of the swallows is a harbinger of spring in many cultures.
Dark clouds are often seen as a harbinger of an approaching storm.
Some believe that technological innovation is a harbinger of societal change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forerunner - a person or thing that precedes the coming or development of someone or something
  • Synonym: precursor - a person or thing that comes before another of the same kind; a forerunner

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'herbergere' (one who provides lodging), from 'herbergier' (to provide lodging), from 'herberge' (lodging).
