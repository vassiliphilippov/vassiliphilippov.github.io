Ethelred

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛθəlˌrɛd / (AmE), / ˈiːθəlˌrɛd / (BrE)
King of Wessex and Kent (died 871); elder brother of Alfred the Great, known for leading battles against the invading Danes.
king of Wessex and Kent, died 871

Examples

Ethelred ruled Wessex and Kent until his death in 871.
King Ethelred fought several battles against the Danes with the help of his brother Alfred.
After Ethelred's death, Alfred the Great became king.

Etymology

Old English name, from ēþel (noble) + ræd (counsel).

2. PROPN

/ ˈɛθəlˌrɛd / (AmE), / ˈiːθəlˌrɛd / (BrE)
(c. 968–1016) King of England, known as Ethelred the Unready, who reigned from 978 to 1016 and struggled to defend England against Danish invasions.
King of England, 10th–11th century

Examples

Ethelred ascended the English throne in 978 after the murder of his half-brother.
The reign of Ethelred was marked by repeated Viking invasions and political instability.
Many historians criticize Ethelred for his inability to unify the country against the Danish threat.

Etymology

Old English personal name Æþelræd, from æþele (noble) + ræd (counsel).
