talipot

1. NOUN

/ ˈtælɪˌpɑt / (AmE), / ˈtælɪˌpɒt / (BrE)
a tall palm tree native to southern India and Sri Lanka, known for its huge fan-shaped leaves that are used as umbrellas, fans, or cut into strips for writing paper
tall palm tree of South Asia

Examples

The gigantic leaves of the talipot are often used as natural umbrellas during the monsoon season.
In ancient times, monks wrote manuscripts on strips made from talipot leaves.
The talipot is one of the largest palms found in Sri Lanka.

Etymology

From Tamil and Malayalam *tāḷi-ppōṭṭu*, referring to the palm tree.
