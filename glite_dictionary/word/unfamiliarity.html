unfamiliarity

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.fəˌmɪl.iˈær.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌʌn.fəˌmɪl.iˈær.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the state of being unfamiliar or not well known; lack of knowledge, recognition, or experience with something
the state of being unfamiliar

Examples

Her unfamiliarity with the local customs made her feel out of place.
The driver's unfamiliarity with the area caused him to get lost.
Despite my unfamiliarity with the subject, I managed to participate in the discussion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: familiarity - the state of being familiar or well known

Etymology

Formed from 'unfamiliar' (from Late Latin 'familiaris') + '-ity', denoting a state or quality.
