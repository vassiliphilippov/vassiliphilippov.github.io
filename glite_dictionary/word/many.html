many

1. DET

/ ˈmɛni / (AmE), / ˈmeni / (BrE)
A large but indefinite number of people or things; used with count nouns to indicate a high quantity.
large indefinite number (count nouns)

Examples

There were many reasons for her decision to move abroad.
Not many students passed the difficult exam this year.
We saw so many stars in the sky last night.

Synset Examples

many temptations
the temptations are many

many directions

too many clouds to see

Related Terms

  • Antonym: few - a small number of people or things
  • Degree Lower: several - more than two but not many
  • Degree Higher: most - the greatest number

Etymology

Old English *manig* (many), of Germanic origin; related to Dutch *menig* and German *manch*.
