Orpheus

1. PROPN

/ ˈɔr.fi.əs / (AmE), / ˈɔː.fi.əs / (BrE)
A legendary musician and poet in Greek mythology who attempted to retrieve his wife Eurydice from the underworld.
legendary musician in Greek mythology

Examples

In the myth, Orpheus descends into the underworld to rescue his beloved Eurydice.
Many composers have found inspiration in the tragic story of Orpheus and his music.
The name Orpheus is often associated with lyrical poetry and enchanting melodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eurydice - wife of Orpheus in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ὀρφεύς (Orpheús), a mythological figure.
