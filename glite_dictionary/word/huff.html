huff

1. VERB

/ hʌf / (AmE), / hʌf / (BrE)
to blow out air forcefully and loudly, often in effort or annoyance
blow air forcefully and loudly

Examples

The runner huffed as he neared the top of the steep hill.
She huffed and wiped her brow after blowing at the dust on the old books.
The train huffed noisily as it pulled out of the station.

Synset Examples

he huffed and puffed as he made his way up the mountain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puff - to breathe or blow in short bursts
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00006802-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ hʌf / (AmE), / hʌf / (BrE)
a fit of anger or annoyance, often expressed by sulking or abrupt speech or action
a state of irritation or annoyance

Examples

She left the meeting in a huff when her proposal was rejected.
After the argument, he sat alone in a huff for the rest of the evening.
There's no need to get into a huff over such a small mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tantrum - an uncontrolled outburst of anger and frustration
  • Antonym: calm - a state of being free from agitation or excitement

Etymology

Probably from Dutch *hof* (court), later influenced by the verb ‘to huff’.

3. VERB

/ hʌf / (AmE), / hʌf / (BrE)
to inhale fumes or vapors from substances such as glue or paint, typically for the purpose of getting high
inhale fumes for intoxication
slang

Examples

The teenagers were caught huffing spray paint behind the old warehouse.
She was worried her son might be huffing glue after finding empty tubes in his room.
Many inhalants can severely harm those who huff them even just once.

Synset Examples

The addict was snorting cocaine almost every day
the kids were huffing glue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sniff - to inhale something through the nose, sometimes for intoxication
