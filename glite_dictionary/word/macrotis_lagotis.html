Macrotis lagotis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmækˈroʊtɪs ləˈgoʊtɪs / (AmE), / ˌmækˈrəʊtɪs ləˈgəʊtɪs / (BrE)
A marsupial native to Australia, commonly known as the greater bilby, recognized by its long, rabbit-like ears and pointed snout.
species of Australian marsupial (greater bilby)

Examples

The Macrotis lagotis is an endangered species found in arid regions of Australia.
Researchers are studying the habitat preferences of Macrotis lagotis to aid in its conservation.
Unlike other marsupials, Macrotis lagotis has distinctive, long ears and a long, bushy tail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bilby - common name for Macrotis lagotis

Etymology

From Greek makrós (large) + oûs, otos (ear), and lagōs (hare); scientific name.
