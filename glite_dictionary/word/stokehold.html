stokehold

1. NOUN

/ ˈstoʊkˌhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈstəʊkˌhəʊld / (BrE)
(nautical) a chamber or compartment in a ship where the furnaces are stoked or fired, especially in steamships.
ship compartment for stoking furnaces

Examples

The crew worked tirelessly in the stokehold to keep the steam engines running.
Thick black smoke poured out as coal was shoveled into the furnaces in the stokehold.
Life in the stokehold was hot and grueling for the ship’s firemen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engine room - room in a ship where the engine is located

Etymology

From 'stoke' (to feed a fire) + 'hold' (ship's compartment or storage area).
