edict

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːdɪkt / (AmE), / ˈiːdɪkt / (BrE)
an official order or proclamation issued by a person in authority, especially one that has the force of law
official order or proclamation

Examples

The government issued an edict banning all public gatherings in the city.
The emperor's edict was carved into stone and displayed in the town square.
Anyone who disobeyed the royal edict faced severe punishment.

Synset Examples

a friend in New Mexico said that the order caused no trouble out there

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decree - an official order issued by a legal authority
  • Synonym: order - an authoritative command or instruction
  • Degree Higher: law - the system of rules recognized by a country or community

Etymology

From Latin edictum (proclamation, ordinance), from edicere (to proclaim).

2. NOUN

/ ˈiː.dɪkt / (AmE), / ˈiː.dɪkt / (BrE)
an official order or proclamation issued by a person in authority
official proclamation or order

Examples

The king issued an edict banning all trade with neighboring countries.
Under the new edict, citizens are required to carry identification at all times.
The emperor’s edict was displayed in every town square for the public to read.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decree - an official order issued by a legal authority
  • Synonym: order - an authoritative command or instruction
  • Synonym: command - an authoritative order

Etymology

From Latin 'edictum' (proclamation), from 'edicere' (to proclaim).
