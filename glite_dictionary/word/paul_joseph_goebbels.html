Paul Joseph Goebbels

1. PROPN

/ ˈpaʊl ˈdʒoʊzəf ˈɡɜrbɛlz / (AmE), / ˈpaʊl ˈdʒəʊzɛf ˈɡɜːbɛlz / (BrE)
(1897–1945) German politician who served as the Minister of Propaganda in Nazi Germany, known for orchestrating propaganda campaigns and persecuting Jews.
Nazi German propaganda minister (1897–1945)

Examples

Historian Simon studied how Paul Joseph Goebbels used media to influence public opinion in Nazi Germany.
The infamous speeches of Paul Joseph Goebbels illustrate the power of propaganda in wartime.
Many documentaries explore the role that Paul Joseph Goebbels played in the Nazi regime.
