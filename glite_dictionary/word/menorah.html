menorah

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɔɹə / (AmE), / məˈnɔːrə / (BrE)
a candelabrum with seven branches used in Jewish ceremonies to symbolize the seven days of Creation
seven-branched Jewish candelabrum

Examples

The ancient menorah stood in the Temple of Jerusalem as an important religious symbol.
During the ceremony, the rabbi carefully lit each candle of the menorah.
The museum displayed a beautifully crafted silver menorah from the 18th century.

Etymology

From Hebrew menorāh (lamp, candelabrum).

2. NOUN

/ məˈnɔrə / (AmE), / məˈnɔːrə / (BrE)
A candelabrum with nine branches used in Jewish homes and synagogues during the festival of Hanukkah.
nine-branched Hanukkah candelabrum

Examples

The family gathered around the menorah to light the candles on the first night of Hanukkah.
Each night, an additional candle is lit on the menorah to celebrate the miracle of Hanukkah.
They placed the silver menorah in the front window for all to see.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candelabrum - a large branched candlestick holder

Etymology

From Hebrew 'menôrāh' (lamp).
