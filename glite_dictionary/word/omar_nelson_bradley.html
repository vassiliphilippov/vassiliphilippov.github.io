Omar Nelson Bradley

1. PROPN

/ ˈoʊˌmɑɹ ˈnɛlsən ˈbrædli / (AmE), / ˈəʊˌmɑː ˈnɛlsən ˈbrædli / (BrE)
A United States Army general (1893–1981) who played a pivotal role in the Allied victory during World War II.
U.S. Army general, WWII leader

Examples

General Omar Nelson Bradley commanded American forces during the D-Day invasion of Normandy.
Historians credit Omar Nelson Bradley with a calm and effective leadership style.
In the late 1940s, Omar Nelson Bradley became the first Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff.
