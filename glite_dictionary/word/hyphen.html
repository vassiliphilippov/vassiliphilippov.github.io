hyphen

1. VERB

/ ˈhaɪfən / (AmE), / ˈhaɪfən / (BrE)
to separate or join words or parts of words with a hyphen.
join with a hyphen

Examples

Editors often hyphen compound adjectives to avoid confusion.
If you hyphen the two names, the meaning becomes clearer.
She was unsure whether to hyphen 'well known' in her essay.

Synset Examples

hyphenate these words and names

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hyphenate - to connect or divide with a hyphen

Etymology

From the noun 'hyphen', from Ancient Greek 'ὑφ' ἕν' (hyph' hén, 'together as one').

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪfən / (AmE), / ˈhaɪfən / (BrE)
The punctuation mark ( – ), used to join words or parts of words, or to divide a word at the end of a line.
punctuation mark used to join or divide words

Examples

The word 'well-known' contains a hyphen between 'well' and 'known'.
Remember to use a hyphen after the prefix in 'pre-existing'.
When a word doesn't fit at the end of a line, you may need to insert a hyphen.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dash - a longer punctuation mark (– or —), used for separating clauses or indicating breaks in thought

Etymology

From Greek 'huphen', meaning 'together'.
