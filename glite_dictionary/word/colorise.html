to colorise

1. VERB

/ ˈkʌlərˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkʌl.ər.aɪz / (BrE)
to add color to something, especially to a black-and-white image, film, or drawing
add color to, especially images or films

Examples

The studio decided to colorise several classic black-and-white movies for modern audiences.
She learned how to colorise old photographs using digital tools.
Autumn winds colorise the leaves with brilliant hues.

Synset Examples

The child colored the drawings
Fall colored the trees
colorize black and white film

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to colorize - to add color to (especially American spelling)
  • Antonym: monochrome - lacking color; black-and-white

Etymology

From 'color' + '-ize', modeled after the American spelling 'colorize'.
