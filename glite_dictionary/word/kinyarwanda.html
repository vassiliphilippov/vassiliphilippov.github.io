Kinyarwanda

1. NOUN

/ kin.jɑːrˈwɑːn.də / (AmE), / kɪn.jɑːˈwæn.də / (BrE)
the Bantu language that is the official language of Rwanda, spoken by the majority of its population and also in neighboring regions
official language of Rwanda

Examples

She is fluent in both English and Kinyarwanda.
The official documents were translated into Kinyarwanda for the local population.
Many Rwandan children learn Kinyarwanda before studying other languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rwanda-Rundi - a Bantu dialect continuum spoken chiefly in Rwanda and Burundi

Etymology

From Kinyarwanda *Ikinyarwanda*, where *ki-* is a language prefix.

2. PROPN

/ kinˌjɑːrˈwɑːndə / (AmE), / kɪnˌjɑːˈwændə / (BrE)
the Bantu language spoken mainly in Rwanda and, as a dialect of Rwanda-Rundi, also in parts of Burundi and southern Uganda; the official language of Rwanda
the Bantu language of Rwanda

Examples

She is fluent in both French and Kinyarwanda.
Several official documents in Rwanda are written in Kinyarwanda.
The teacher offered a course in Kinyarwanda for beginners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kirundi - the Bantu language closely related to Kinyarwanda, spoken mainly in Burundi

Etymology

From Kinyarwanda, meaning 'language of Rwanda' from ki- (language prefix) + Rwanda.
