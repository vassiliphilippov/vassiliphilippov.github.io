wheelbarrow

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːlˌbæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈwiːlˌbærəʊ / (BrE)
a small cart with one wheel at the front and two long handles at the back, used for carrying small loads, especially in a garden or on a building site
small cart with one wheel, for carrying loads

Examples

He used a wheelbarrow to move soil from the garden to the compost heap.
The old wheelbarrow had a flat tire and couldn't be pushed easily.
She loaded the bricks into the wheelbarrow before wheeling them across the yard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cart - vehicle with wheels for carrying loads

Etymology

From Middle English 'wheelbarwe', combining 'wheel' and 'barrow' (a device for carrying loads).

2. VERB

/ ˈwiːlˌbæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈwiːlˌbærəʊ / (BrE)
to transport or move something using a wheelbarrow.
to move using a wheelbarrow

Examples

He wheelbarrowed the heavy stones from the garden to the shed.
Can you wheelbarrow these bags of soil over to the flowerbed?
She wheelbarrowed mulch all afternoon during the garden project.

Etymology

Formed from the noun 'wheelbarrow', itself from Middle English 'whelebarwe', meaning a barrow with a wheel.

Source: WordNet
