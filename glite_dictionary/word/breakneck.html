breakneck

1. ADJ

/ ˈbreɪkˌnɛk / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkˌnɛk / (BrE)
(especially of speed or movement) dangerously or extremely fast.
dangerously fast; extremely rapid

Examples

The car raced down the highway at a breakneck speed, barely making the curve.
They completed the work at a breakneck pace to meet the tight deadline.
The cyclists descended the mountain at breakneck velocity, thrilling the crowd.

Synset Examples

a breakneck pace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reckless - not showing proper caution; careless of danger
  • Antonym: slow - moving with little speed; not fast
  • Synonym: fast - moving quickly or at high speed

Etymology

Formed from 'break' + 'neck', originally referring to something so fast or rash it could break one's neck.
