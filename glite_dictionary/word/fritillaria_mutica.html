Fritillaria mutica

1. NOUN

/ frɪˌtɪl.əˈraɪ.ə ˈmjuː.tɪ.kə / (AmE), / frɪˌtɪl.ɪˈreɪ.ri.ə ˈmjuː.tɪ.kə / (BrE)
a herb native to northwestern America, known for its green-and-purple, bell-shaped flowers
a North American herb with bell-shaped flowers

Examples

The rare Fritillaria mutica can be found growing in alpine meadows of the northwest.
Botanists are studying the unique bell-shaped flowers of Fritillaria mutica.
Gardeners admire Fritillaria mutica for its striking green and purple coloration.

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Fritillaria' from Latin 'fritillus' (dice-box) and the species name 'mutica' meaning 'blunt' or 'short' in Latin.
