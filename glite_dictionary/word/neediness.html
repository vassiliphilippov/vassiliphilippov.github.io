neediness

1. NOUN

/ ˈniːdinəs / (AmE), / ˈniːdɪnɪs / (BrE)
the state of requiring excessive attention, affection, or reassurance from others
excessive need for attention or affection

Examples

His neediness often made friends feel overwhelmed and exhausted.
She worked hard to overcome her neediness and become more independent.
Some people are put off by excessive neediness in relationships.

Synset Examples

he recognized her neediness but had no time to respond to it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dependency - excessive reliance on others for emotional support
  • Antonym: independence - freedom from the need for support or help from others

Etymology

From 'needy' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality of being needy.

WordNet ID: oewn-04727081-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈniːdi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈniːdɪnɪs / (BrE)
the condition of being extremely poor and lacking basic necessities
state of extreme poverty

Examples

The neediness of the region was evident after the natural disaster left many families with nothing.
Government programs aim to alleviate neediness among vulnerable populations.
She dedicated her life to charities that support people living in neediness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poverty - the state of being extremely poor

Etymology

From 'needy' + '-ness', forming a noun from the adjective 'needy'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14517010-n
Synset POS: n
