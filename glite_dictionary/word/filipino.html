Filipino

1. ADJ

/ ˌfɪlɪˈpinoʊ / (AmE), / ˌfɪlɪˈpiːnəʊ / (BrE)
of or relating to the Philippines or its people, language, or culture
relating to the Philippines or its people

Examples

She cooked a traditional Filipino dish for dinner last night.
The team studied Filipino customs and traditions for their report.
He speaks both English and Filipino languages fluently.

Synset Examples

the Philippine President
our Filipino cook

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Philippine - relating to the Philippines, often used in official names or titles

Etymology

From Spanish 'Filipino', denoting a person from the Philippines, named after King Philip II of Spain.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03077608-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˌfɪləˈpinoʊ / (AmE), / ˌfɪlɪˈpiːnəʊ / (BrE)
the official language of the Philippines, based primarily on Tagalog and incorporating words from other Philippine languages
official language of the Philippines

Examples

She is fluent in both English and Filipino.
Many government documents in the Philippines are written in Filipino.
Children learn Filipino as part of the school curriculum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tagalog - a major Philippine language on which Filipino is based

Etymology

From Spanish 'filipino', from 'Filipinas' (the Philippines), named after King Philip II of Spain.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06952518-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌfɪləˈpinoʊ / (AmE), / ˌfɪlɪˈpiːnəʊ / (BrE)
a person who is a native or national of the Philippines
a person from the Philippines

Examples

My colleague is a proud Filipino who was born and raised in Manila.
The city held a festival celebrating Filipino culture last summer.
She met several Filipinos during her vacation in Cebu.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Philippine - relating to the Philippines or its people
  • Antonym: foreigner - a person from another country

Etymology

From Spanish 'filipino', meaning 'of the Philippines', derived from 'Filipinas' (the Philippines).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09746782-n
Synset POS: n
