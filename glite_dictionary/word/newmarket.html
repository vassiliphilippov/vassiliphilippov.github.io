Newmarket

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːˌmɑrkɪt / (AmE), / ˈnjuːˌmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
a card game in which players bet chips on certain cards, aiming to play runs in suits and collect chips by matching cards in the layout
a gambling card game involving betting chips on card sequences

Examples

Every Friday night, our family gathers to play Newmarket for chips and fun.
He learned the rules of Newmarket from his grandparents during summer holidays.
In Newmarket, you can win all the chips on a card by matching it from your hand.

Etymology

Named after the English town of Newmarket, possibly due to a historical association with gambling and horse racing.
