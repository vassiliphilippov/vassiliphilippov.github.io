to vulgarise

1. VERB

/ ˈvʌlɡəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈvʌlɡəraɪz / (BrE)
to debase or make less refined; to render something common, crude, or lacking in originality or dignity
make less refined or debase

Examples

Many critics believe that reality television has vulgarised modern culture.
Some people worry that translating poetry may vulgarise its original beauty.
The tabloid articles have vulgarised the discussion of complex political issues.

Synset Examples

The Press has vulgarized Love and Marriage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to debase - to reduce in quality or value
  • Antonym: to elevate - to raise to a higher level or more refined state

Etymology

From Latin 'vulgaris' meaning 'common, ordinary', via French 'vulgariser'.

2. VERB

/ ˈvʌlɡɚˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvʌlɡəraɪz / (BrE)
to act or behave in a coarse, unrefined, or crude manner; to make something less refined or more commonplace
to act or make something vulgar

Examples

Some reality TV shows tend to vulgarise public taste.
The comedian's jokes vulgarised the otherwise elegant event.
It is disappointing when artists deliberately vulgarise their work to attract a larger audience.

Synset Examples

The drunkard tends to vulgarize

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to coarsen - make or become rough or crude
  • Antonym: to refine - make something more elegant or cultured

Etymology

From Latin vulgaris (common, ordinary), via French vulgariser.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00011691-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈvʌlɡəraɪz / (AmE), / ˈvʌlɡəraɪz / (BrE)
to make an idea or subject simpler or more accessible to the general public, often by reducing its complexity or sophistication
make something more accessible to the public

Examples

The professor tried to vulgarise scientific concepts to make them understandable to laypeople.
Some critics argue that television has vulgarised classic literature for mass consumption.
By simplifying the theory, the author was able to vulgarise it for a wider audience.

Synset Examples

They popularized coffee in Washington State
Relativity Theory was vulgarized by these authors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to popularize - to make something popular or widely known
  • Antonym: to refine - to improve or make something more sophisticated

Etymology

From Latin vulgaris (of the common people), via French 'vulgariser'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00972247-v
Synset POS: v
