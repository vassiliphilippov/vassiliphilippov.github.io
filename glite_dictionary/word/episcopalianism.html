Episcopalianism

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌpɪs.kəˈpeɪ.li.əˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ɪˌpɪs.kəˈpeɪl.jəˌnɪz.əm / (BrE)
The doctrine or system of church government by bishops, especially as practiced in the Episcopal Church or Anglican Communion.
church government by bishops

Examples

Many members of the congregation are drawn to Episcopalianism because of its traditions and liturgy.
Debates about Episcopalianism often focus on the authority and role of bishops.
He wrote an essay comparing Episcopalianism with other forms of church organization.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Presbyterianism - Doctrine of church government by elders
  • Synonym: Episcopal - Relating to a bishop or bishops

Etymology

From Episcopalian + -ism, relating to the government of the church by bishops.
