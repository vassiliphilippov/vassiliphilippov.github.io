Croton tiglium

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊtɑn ˈtaɪdʒliəm / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊtɒn ˈtɪɡliəm / (BrE)
a tropical Asian shrub, belonging to the spurge family, cultivated for its seeds which are the source of croton oil
tropical Asian shrub; source of croton oil
medical

Examples

Croton tiglium was once widely used in traditional Asian medicine for its potent purgative effects.
The seeds of Croton tiglium are pressed to produce croton oil, which must be handled with great caution.
Botanists classified the unfamiliar shrub as Croton tiglium after examining its leaves and seeds.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: croton oil - a strongly purgative oil extracted from Croton tiglium seeds

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name 'Croton' and specific epithet 'tiglium,' from Malay 'ketelu', referring to the tree.
