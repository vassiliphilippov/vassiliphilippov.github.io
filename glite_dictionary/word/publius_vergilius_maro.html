Publius Vergilius Maro

1. PROPN

/ ˈpʌbliəs vərˈdʒɪli.əs ˈmɑːroʊ / (AmE), / ˈpʌbli.əs vəˈdʒɪli.əs ˈmɑːrəʊ / (BrE)
(70–19 BC) A Roman poet known for writing the epic poem the Aeneid, as well as the Eclogues and the Georgics.
Roman poet, author of the Aeneid

Examples

Many scholars regard Publius Vergilius Maro as the greatest poet of ancient Rome.
The influence of Publius Vergilius Maro can be seen throughout Western literature.
Students read selections from Publius Vergilius Maro in their Latin classes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Virgil - The Anglicized name commonly used for Publius Vergilius Maro

Etymology

Latin name; 'Publius' (given name), 'Vergilius' (family name), 'Maro' (cognomen).
