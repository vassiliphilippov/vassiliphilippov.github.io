indentation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɛnˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪndɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the space left between the margin and the beginning of a line, especially at the start of a paragraph, to indicate a new section or idea
space at paragraph or line start

Examples

Please use an indentation of half an inch at the start of each paragraph.
The program automatically adjusts the indentation for nested lists.
Proper indentation makes the text clearer and more organized.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indent - to start a line further from the margin

Etymology

From Latin 'indentare' (to notch) via Medieval French 'indenter'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnˌdɛnˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɛnˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of cutting into an edge with toothlike notches or angular incisions
cutting notches or incisions into an edge

Examples

The craftsman created an indentation along the edge of the wooden panel for decorative effect.
Repeated blows caused a series of small indentations in the metal rim of the shield.
An indentation with sharp notches marked the border between the two pieces of stone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notch - a V-shaped cut or incision made in an edge or surface
  • Synonym: incision - a cut or gash, especially in surgery or on a surface

Etymology

From Medieval Latin indentare (to make notches), from Latin in- + dent- (tooth).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪnˌdɛnˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɛnˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process or result of forming small pits or depressions in a surface, often as a consequence of corrosion or mechanical impact
formation of small pits or depressions

Examples

The metal panel showed significant indentation after years of exposure to seawater.
Corrosion led to the gradual indentation of the copper pipes over time.
Inspectors found several indentations on the bridge's surface caused by rust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pit - a small hollow or depression in a surface
  • Synonym: corrosion - the gradual destruction of materials by chemical reaction

Etymology

From Latin indentare (to notch), from in- (in) + dens (tooth), referring to tooth-like notches.

4. NOUN

/ ˌɪnˌdɛnˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪndɛnˈteɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a recess or hollow made by cutting or pressing into a surface or along an edge
a recess or cut in a surface or edge

Examples

The rocky shoreline was marked by a deep indentation that formed a sheltered cove.
There was a small indentation on the car door where it had been hit.
The indentation in the wooden panel added a distinctive feature to the design.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notch - a small cut or nick in a surface or edge

Etymology

From Medieval Latin indentatio (notching), from Latin 'indenta-' (to make notches).
