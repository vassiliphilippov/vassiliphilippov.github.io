hyoscine

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈɑːsiːn / (AmE), / haɪˈɒsiːn / (BrE)
an alkaloid with anticholinergic properties, used as a sedative, to treat nausea (including motion sickness), and to dilate pupils in ophthalmic procedures
an anticholinergic alkaloid used in medicine
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed hyoscine tablets to prevent motion sickness during the boat trip.
Ophthalmologists sometimes use hyoscine to dilate the pupils before an eye examination.
Some sedatives, such as hyoscine, also have the effect of relieving nausea.

Synset Examples

transdermal scopolamine is used to treat motion sickness
someone sedated with scopolamine has difficulty lying

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scopolamine - another name for hyoscine, used to treat motion sickness and nausea

Etymology

From Greek 'hyoskyamos' (henbane), from which it is derived, combined with '-ine' (a chemical suffix).
