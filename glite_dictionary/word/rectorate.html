rectorate

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛktəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛktəreɪt / (BrE)
the office, position, or term of a rector, especially in a church or academic institution
office or term of a rector

Examples

During her rectorate, many important reforms were implemented at the university.
The church hall was renovated under the new rectorate.
He wrote a memoir reflecting on his experiences throughout his rectorate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rectorship - the position or term of office of a rector

Etymology

From Latin rector (ruler, director) + -ate (denoting office or function).
