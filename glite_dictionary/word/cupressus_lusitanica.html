Cupressus lusitanica

1. NOUN

/ kuːˈprɛsəs luːsɪˈtænɪkə / (AmE), / kjuːˈprɛsəs luːsɪˈtænɪkə / (BrE)
a tall, spreading evergreen conifer native to Mexico, with drooping branches, commonly planted as an ornamental tree; also known as the Mexican cypress
a Mexican evergreen conifer tree

Examples

The Cupressus lusitanica is often planted in gardens for its graceful, weeping branches.
Several Cupressus lusitanica trees bordered the old estate, providing shade and privacy.
Scientists studied the adaptation of Cupressus lusitanica in different climates around the world.

Etymology

From Latin: Cupressus (cypress) + lusitanica (of Lusitania, ancient region corresponding to Portugal).
