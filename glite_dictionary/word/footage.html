footage

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfʊtɪdʒ / (BrE)
film or video material that has been recorded, especially for use in movies, television, or news broadcasts.
recorded film or video material

Examples

The news station played dramatic footage of the rescue operation.
She reviewed hours of surveillance footage to find the suspect.
Documentary filmmakers often rely on archival footage to tell their stories.

Synset Examples

they had stock footage of lightning, tornados, and hurricanes
he edited the news footage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: film - motion picture or moving image recorded on film
  • Synonym: video - recording of moving visual images made digitally or on videotape

Etymology

From foot + -age, originally referring to the length of film measured in feet.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfʊtɪdʒ / (BrE)
A price or rate calculated according to the number of linear feet of work done or material used, especially in construction or similar trades.
rate charged per linear foot

Examples

The contractor's estimate included all materials and labor at a standard footage price.
Carpet installation is often billed based on footage rather than a flat fee.
The electrician gave us a breakdown of the wiring costs according to the footage required.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rate - a measure, quantity, or frequency, typically one measured against another quantity or measure

Etymology

From 'foot' (unit of measurement) + '-age' (noun-forming suffix).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
