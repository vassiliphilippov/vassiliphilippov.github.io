Majidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ.dʒiˌdi / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.dʒɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine crabs known as spider crabs, characterized by their long legs and often triangular bodies.
family of marine spider crabs

Examples

The family Majidae includes many species of spider crabs found in oceans worldwide.
Research on Majidae has led to new insights into crab evolution.
A member of the Majidae can often be identified by its long, spindly legs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spider crab - common name for members of Majidae, crabs with long legs resembling a spider

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus *Maja* plus the standard zoological family suffix *-idae*.
