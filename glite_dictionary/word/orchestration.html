orchestration

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔrkəˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːkɪˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
an arrangement of a musical composition for performance by an orchestra or band, specifying the parts for each instrument
musical arrangement for orchestra or band

Examples

The orchestration of the ballet was praised for its brilliant use of brass and percussion.
Mahler was known for his innovative orchestration, adding unusual instruments to create new sound textures.
This new orchestration brings fresh color to the classic symphony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrangement - a musical adaptation of a piece for performance by particular instruments or voices

Etymology

From French orchestration, from orchestre (orchestra) + -ation.

Examples

A new orchestration was created to suit the smaller ensemble performing the work.

Related Terms

Etymology

From French *orchestration*, from 'orchestre' (orchestra), from Greek 'orchestra'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌɔrkəˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːkɪˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
the careful arrangement or coordination of events or elements to achieve a particular effect or outcome
arrangement of events for effect

Examples

The orchestration of the annual fundraiser required months of planning and teamwork.
She admired the clever orchestration of the surprise party, which left everyone speechless.
The company's orchestration of the product launch was praised for its flawless execution.

Synset Examples

the skillful orchestration of his political campaign

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organization - the act of arranging or structuring things
  • Antonym: disorder - a lack of order or arrangement

Etymology

From orchestra + -ation, by analogy to the musical sense.
