spotlight

1. VERB

/ ˈspɑːt.laɪt / (AmE), / ˈspɒt.laɪt / (BrE)
to illuminate (a person or object) with a spotlight, especially on a stage or in a theater
illuminate with a focused light

Examples

The stage crew spotlighted the main actor during her solo performance.
They spotlight each contestant as they enter the stage.
During his speech, a technician spotlighted him to draw the audience's attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: highlight - to make something stand out; to emphasize

Etymology

Formed from 'spot' + 'light', originally referring to a powerful stage lamp creating a bright, focused beam.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɑtˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈspɒtˌlaɪt / (BrE)
public attention or notice, especially when focused on a particular person or issue
focus of public attention

Examples

After her breakthrough role, she found herself in the spotlight and struggled with sudden fame.
The scandal brought the company's shady practices into the spotlight for months.
He prefers to work behind the scenes rather than seeking the spotlight.

Synset Examples

he enjoyed being in the limelight
when Congress investigates it brings the full glare of publicity to the agency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limelight - public attention and interest, especially intense or celebrity focus
  • Antonym: obscurity - the state of being unknown or not noticed

Etymology

From 'spot' + 'light', originally referring to a lamp that casts a focused beam; figurative use emerged in the early 20th century.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈspɑtˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈspɒtˌlaɪt / (BrE)
a lamp that produces a strong, focused beam of light to illuminate a specific area or performer, especially on a stage
strong lamp with focused beam, used on stage

Examples

The actor stepped into the spotlight and began his monologue with confidence.
A single spotlight illuminated the dancer as she twirled across the dark stage.
Technicians adjusted the spotlight to make sure it was focused on the presenter.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: floodlight - a broad-beamed, high-intensity artificial light
  • Synonym: searchlight - a powerful light with a concentrated beam used to search for objects

Etymology

From 'spot' + 'light', first used in stage lighting contexts in the early 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04293713-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈspɑːtˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈspɒtˌlaɪt / (BrE)
to make something or someone more prominent or noticeable, especially by drawing public attention to it
make more prominent or noticeable

Examples

The article spotlighted the challenges faced by young entrepreneurs.
During the ceremony, the host spotlighted several outstanding volunteers for their service.
The new policy spotlights the importance of environmental protection.

Synset Examples

The introduction highlighted the speaker's distinguished career in linguistics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: highlight - to make something stand out as important
  • Antonym: obscure - to make less visible or unclear

Etymology

From the noun 'spotlight', originally referring to a strong lamp that casts a bright beam onto a particular area or performer.

Source: WordNet
