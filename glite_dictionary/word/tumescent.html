tumescent

1. ADJ

/ tuˈmɛsənt / (AmE), / tjuːˈmɛsənt / (BrE)
(especially of body tissue) swollen or becoming swollen, typically due to the presence of fluid or gas.
swollen or distended, esp. tissue
medical

Examples

After the allergic reaction, his arm became noticeably tumescent and sore.
The surgeon discussed removing the tumescent tissue to reduce swelling in her leg.
During the procedure, they used a tumescent solution to minimize bleeding and bruising.

Synset Examples

hungry children with bloated stomachs
he had a grossly distended stomach
eyes with puffed (or puffy) lids
swollen hands
tumescent tissue
puffy tumid flesh

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swollen - enlarged by fluid or inflammation
  • Synonym: tumid - swollen, distended, or congested

Etymology

From Latin *tumescens*, present participle of *tumescere* (to swell up), from *tumēre* (to swell).
