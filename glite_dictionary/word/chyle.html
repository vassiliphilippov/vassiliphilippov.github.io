chyle

1. NOUN

/ kaɪl / (AmE), / kaɪl / (BrE)
A milky bodily fluid consisting of lymph and emulsified fats, formed in the small intestine during the digestion of fats.
milky bodily fluid formed during fat digestion
medical

Examples

The surgeon noted the presence of chyle leaking from the lymphatic vessels.
A blockage in the lymphatic system can prevent the proper transport of chyle.
After eating a fatty meal, the amount of chyle in the lymphatic system increases.

Etymology

From Greek 'khylos' (juice), via Latin 'chylus'.
