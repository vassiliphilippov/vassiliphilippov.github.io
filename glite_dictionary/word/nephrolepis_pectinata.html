Nephrolepis pectinata

1. NOUN

/ nɛfɹoʊˈlɛpɪs ˌpɛktɪˈneɪtə / (AmE), / nɛfɹəʊˈlɛpɪs ˌpɛktɪˈneɪtə / (BrE)
A tropical American fern cultivated for its finely divided greyish-green foliage, native to the West Indies, southern Mexico, Peru, and Brazil.
species of tropical American fern

Examples

The Nephrolepis pectinata thrives in warm, humid environments and is popular as an ornamental plant.
Botanists have documented Nephrolepis pectinata in forests from Mexico to Brazil.
The lightly scented, feathery fronds of Nephrolepis pectinata make it a favorite for indoor gardens.

Etymology

From Greek 'nephros' (kidney) and 'lepis' (scale), referring to the shape of some parts of the plant; 'pectinata' from Latin 'pecten' (comb), indicating the comb-like appearance of the fronds.
