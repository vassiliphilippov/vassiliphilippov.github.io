belfry

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛlˌfri / (AmE), / ˈbɛl.fri / (BrE)
a bell tower, typically one that is separate from a main building, where bells are housed and rung
a standalone bell tower

Examples

The old stone belfry stood at the center of the town square.
Tourists admired the medieval belfry for its impressive height and architecture.
The sound of the bells ringing from the belfry echoed throughout the village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bell tower - a tower that contains bells

Etymology

From Old French 'berfrei', later associated with 'bell' due to its use in bell towers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛlˌfri / (AmE), / ˈbɛl.fri / (BrE)
a room or framework, typically at the top of a tower, where bells are hung, especially in a church or other public building
a tower room for hanging bells

Examples

The old church has a wooden belfry that houses three large bells.
Tourists climbed the stairs to the belfry for a view of the town.
Pigeons had made their home in the empty belfry above the chapel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bell tower - a tower that holds one or more bells

Etymology

From Old French 'berfrei' (watchtower), later altered to 'belfroi' due to association with 'bel' (bell).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
