motherese

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌðərˈiz / (AmE), / ˌmʌðəˈriːz / (BrE)
language spoken by adults to young children, typically characterized by simple sentences, exaggerated intonation, and a slow, clear pronunciation.
simplified speech used by adults with children

Examples

Many parents naturally switch to motherese when talking to their infants.
Linguists study motherese to understand how children acquire language.
She used motherese to soothe her crying toddler.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baby talk - simple, exaggerated language used when speaking to babies

Etymology

Formed by combining 'mother' and the English suffix '-ese' (indicating language or dialect).
