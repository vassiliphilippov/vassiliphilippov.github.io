Kahlua

1. NOUN

/ kɑːˈluːə / (AmE), / kɑːˈluːə / (BrE)
A coffee-flavored liqueur originally made in Mexico, often used in cocktails and desserts.
coffee-flavored liqueur from Mexico

Examples

She added a splash of Kahlua to her coffee for an after-dinner treat.
The bartender mixed vodka, Kahlua, and cream to make a White Russian.
We enjoyed a chocolate cake flavored with Kahlua at the party.

Etymology

Brand name created in Mexico in the 1930s, possibly from a Nahuatl word for 'house of the Acolhua people.'
