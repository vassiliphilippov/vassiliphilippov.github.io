scantness

1. NOUN

/ ˈskænt.nəs / (AmE), / ˈskænt.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being insufficient in amount or quantity; meagerness.
the quality of being insufficient or meager

Examples

The scantness of food during the winter made life extremely difficult for the villagers.
Her essay was criticized for the scantness of supporting evidence.
Due to the scantness of resources, the project was postponed until next year.

Synset Examples

an exiguity of cloth that would only allow of miniature capes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meagerness - the quality of being lacking in quantity or quality
  • Antonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something

Etymology

From 'scant' + '-ness'. 'Scant' from Old Norse skamt (short, scanty).
