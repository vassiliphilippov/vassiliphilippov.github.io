menstruation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛnstruˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɛnstruˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the monthly flow of blood from the uterus that occurs in women and other female mammals from puberty to menopause, except during pregnancy
monthly discharge of uterine blood
medical

Examples

Irregular menstruation can be a sign of underlying health problems.
She kept track of her menstruation dates in a journal.
Education about menstruation helps reduce stigma and promote good hygiene.

Synset Examples

the women were sickly and subject to excessive menstruation
a woman does not take the gout unless her menses be stopped
the semen begins to appear in males and to be emitted at the same time of life that the catamenia begin to flow in females

Related Terms

  • Synonym: period - the recurring cycle of menstruation in women

Etymology

From Latin 'menstruatio', from 'menstruare' (to menstruate), related to 'mensis' (month).
