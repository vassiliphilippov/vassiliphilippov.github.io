epilithic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛpɪˈlɪθɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈlɪθɪk / (BrE)
(of an organism) growing on the surface of stone or rock.
growing on stone or rock
medical

Examples

Several species of epilithic algae thrive in the rocky streams of the region.
The biologist collected epilithic mosses from the exposed boulders.
These epilithic lichens depend on mineral surfaces for their growth.

Synset Examples

epilithic mosses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epiphytic - (of an organism) growing on the surface of a plant

Etymology

From Greek epi- (upon) + lithos (stone) + -ic.
