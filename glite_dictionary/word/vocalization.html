vocalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌvoʊkələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvəʊkələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the production of sound by the vibration of the vocal folds, modified by the resonance of the vocal tract
production of sound with the vocal folds

Examples

Birds often use vocalization to communicate with each other across long distances.
Speech therapists study vocalization patterns in young children.
The scientist recorded the dolphin’s vocalization for further analysis.

Synset Examples

a singer takes good care of his voice
the giraffe cannot make any vocalizations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voice - the sound produced in a person's larynx

Etymology

From Latin 'vocalis' (voiced) + English '-ization'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌvoʊkələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvəʊkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of producing sounds with the voice, especially as a means of communication
producing sounds with the voice

Examples

The bird's vocalization attracted the attention of researchers in the forest.
Infant vocalization is an important milestone in early language development.
Different species have distinct forms of vocalization to communicate warnings or attract mates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: articulation - the physical production of particular speech sounds
  • Synonym: speech - the expression of thoughts and feelings by spoken words

Etymology

From Latin 'vocalis' (vocal) + English suffix '-ation'.
