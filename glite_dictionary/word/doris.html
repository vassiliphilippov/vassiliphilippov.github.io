Doris

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɔːrɪs / (AmE), / ˈdɔːrɪs / (BrE)
an ancient region of central Greece, known as the home of the Dorian Greeks and the origin of the Doric dialect
ancient region of Greece

Examples

Scholars believe that Doris was the homeland of the Dorian Greeks.
The mountainous terrain of Doris played a significant role in its history.
Ancient travelers often passed through Doris on their way to other regions of Greece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dorian - a member of an ancient Greek people who settled in Doris; also relating to Doric dialect or style

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Δωρίς (Dōrís), related to the Dorian people.

2. PROPN

/ ˈdɔrɪs / (AmE), / ˈdɔːrɪs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the wife of Nereus and mother of the Nereids, sea nymphs.
sea goddess in Greek mythology

Examples

According to the myth, Doris was revered as the mother of the Nereids.
The ancient Greeks believed Doris dwelled in the depths of the ocean.
Many statues depict Doris alongside Nereus and their fifty daughters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nereus - a sea god in Greek mythology, husband of Doris
  • Synonym: Nereid - a sea nymph, daughter of Doris and Nereus, in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Δωρίς (Dōrís), a mythological name.
