dogmatically

1. ADV

/ dɔgˈmætɪkli / (AmE), / dɒgˈmætɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner that is dogmatic; asserting opinions in an authoritative, unyielding way without consideration for others' views.
in a dogmatic or rigid manner

Examples

She argued her point dogmatically, refusing to listen to any opposing evidence.
The theory was dogmatically imposed as the only correct explanation.
Some leaders adhere to tradition dogmatically, rejecting necessary reforms.

Synset Examples

he is a dogmatically opinionated critic of Modern Art

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rigidly - in a stiff or strict manner
  • Antonym: flexibly - in a way that is adaptable or open to change

Etymology

From 'dogmatic' (from Greek 'dogmatikos'), + '-ally' (adverbial suffix).
