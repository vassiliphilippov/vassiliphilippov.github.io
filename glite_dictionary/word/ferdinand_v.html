Ferdinand V

1. PROPN

/ fərˌdɪnænd ˈfaɪv / (AmE), / fəˌdɪnænd ˈfɪfθ / (BrE)
King of Castile and Aragon (1452–1516), who ruled jointly with Isabella I; their marriage unified Spain, completed the Reconquista, established the Spanish Inquisition, and supported Columbus's 1492 voyage.
King of Castile and Aragon, Spanish unifier

Examples

Ferdinand V and Isabella completed the Reconquista with the capture of Granada in 1492.
The reign of Ferdinand V marked a turning point in Spanish history.
Historians credit Ferdinand V with supporting Columbus's first voyage to the Americas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Isabella I - Queen of Castile who ruled jointly with Ferdinand V

Etymology

Ferdinand from Spanish Fernando, from Germanic roots; V refers to 'the Fifth' in regnal numbering.
