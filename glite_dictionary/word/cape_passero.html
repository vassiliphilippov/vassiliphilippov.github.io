Cape Passero

1. PROPN

/ keɪp pəˈsɛəroʊ / (AmE), / keɪp pəˈsɛərəʊ / (BrE)
the site of a naval battle in 1719 off the coast of Sicily, where the Spanish navy was defeated by French and British forces during Spain's attempt to regain Sicily and Sardinia.
site of a 1719 naval battle off Sicily

Examples

The Battle of Cape Passero marked a turning point in the Mediterranean naval wars of the early 18th century.
Historians still debate the strategies used at Cape Passero during the 1719 naval clash.
The defeat at Cape Passero weakened Spain's efforts to reclaim its Mediterranean territories.

Etymology

From Italian 'Capo Passero', meaning 'Sparrow Cape'.
