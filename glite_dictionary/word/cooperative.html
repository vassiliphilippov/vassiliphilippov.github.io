cooperative

1. ADJ

/ koʊˈɑːpəˌreɪtɪv / (AmE), / kəʊˈɒpərətɪv / (BrE)
involving working together with others for a common purpose or benefit
working together with others

Examples

The project was a cooperative effort between several departments.
She always takes a cooperative approach to problem-solving at work.
The students were praised for their cooperative attitude during the group assignment.

Synset Examples

a cooperative effort

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collaborative - involving two or more people working together
  • Antonym: individualistic - acting independently rather than working with others

Etymology

From Latin 'cooperari' (to work together), from 'co-' (together) + 'operari' (to work).

2. ADJ

/ koʊˈɑːpərətɪv, koʊˈɑːprətɪv / (AmE), / kəʊˈɒpərətɪv, kəʊˈɒprətɪv / (BrE)
willing to work with others and adapt in order to reach agreement or achieve a common goal
willing to work with others

Examples

He became less cooperative after the disagreement, making it harder to solve the problem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collaborative - willing to work together with others
  • Antonym: uncooperative - unwilling to work or collaborate with others

Etymology

From Latin 'cooperari' (to work together), from 'co-' (together) + 'operari' (to work).

3. NOUN

/ koʊˈɑːpərətɪv / (AmE), / kəʊˈɒpərətɪv / (BrE)
a jointly owned business or organization, typically run by a group of people with shared interests, in which the profits or benefits are distributed among the members
jointly owned business organization

Examples

The farmers formed a cooperative to sell their crops at better prices.
She shops at a local food cooperative that is owned and run by its members.
By joining the housing cooperative, residents have a say in how the property is managed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: co-op - short form of cooperative; a jointly owned store or enterprise

Etymology

From Latin *cooperari* (to work together), via French *coopérative*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01102782-n
Synset POS: n

5. NOUN

/ koʊˈɑːpərətɪv / (AmE), / kəʊˈɒpərətɪv / (BrE)
An organization or business owned and operated by a group of people for their mutual benefit.
organization owned by those who use it

Examples

All the local farmers joined a cooperative to process and sell their crops collectively.
The housing cooperative allows members to make decisions about their living environment together.
She is a member of a food cooperative that supplies organic produce to the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: co-op - Abbreviation for cooperative, often used informally
  • Antonym: corporation - A company or group of people authorized to act as a single entity

Etymology

From Latin 'cooperari' (to work together), via French 'coopératif'.
