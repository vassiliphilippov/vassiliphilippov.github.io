Lycopus

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪkəpəs / (AmE), / ˈlɪkəpəs / (BrE)
A small genus of herbs in the mint family, commonly known as water horehound or gypsywort, found in wet habitats.
genus of herbs in the mint family

Examples

The wetland was full of Lycopus plants thriving along the water’s edge.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Lycopus species.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Lycopus for its decorative foliage.

Etymology

From Greek 'lykos' (wolf) and 'pous, podos' (foot), referring to the shape of the plant's leaves.
