ern

1. NOUN

/ ɜrn / (AmE), / ɜːn / (BrE)
A bulky greyish-brown eagle with a short, wedge-shaped white tail, native to Europe and Greenland; also known as a sea eagle or erne.
a large European sea eagle
archaic

Examples

An ern soared above the windswept cliffs, searching for prey along the coastline.
The fisherman spotted a solitary ern perched on a rocky outcrop.
Once endangered, the population of erns in Europe is now slowly recovering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erne - bulky sea eagle of Europe and Greenland; variant spelling

Etymology

From Old English *earn* (eagle), related to Old Norse *ǫrn*.
