packet

1. NOUN

/ ˈpækɪt / (AmE), / ˈpækɪt / (BrE)
(computing) A unit of data transmitted over a computer network, often representing a message or a piece of a message.
unit of data in computing/networking

Examples

The email was divided into several packets before being sent over the internet.
Each packet contains information about its destination address.
Loss of a single packet can disrupt a video call's quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: datagram - a self-contained message sent over a network
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07295804-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈpækɪt / (AmE), / ˈpakɪt / (BrE)
a boat used for carrying mail, especially along a fixed route
boat used to carry mail
archaic

Examples

The old harbor was once busy with packets delivering letters from overseas.
In the 19th century, a packet would make regular trips to bring news and supplies.
He worked as a sailor on a packet before steamships became common.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mailboat - a vessel that carries mail

Etymology

From French 'paquet' (package), relating to vessels that carried mail and small parcels.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpæk.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈpæk.ɪt / (BrE)
a small package or container in which a collection of objects is wrapped or boxed together, often for sale or distribution
small package or bundle

Examples

She opened a packet of biscuits and offered them around the table.
Each visitor received a packet containing samples and brochures.
I found a packet of seeds in the garden shed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: package - a wrapped or boxed object; a parcel

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *paket*, diminutive of *pak* (bundle, package).
