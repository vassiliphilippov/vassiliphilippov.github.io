pan

1. VERB

/ pæn / (AmE), / pan / (BrE)
to criticize something very severely, especially in a review
criticize harshly (esp. in reviews)

Examples

Many reviewers panned the movie for its weak plot and poor acting.
His latest book was panned by the critics, but it still became a bestseller.
The new play was panned so harshly that it closed after just one week.

Synset Examples

The critics panned the performance

Related Terms

  • Antonym: praise - express approval or admiration of
  • Synonym: to criticize - indicate the faults of (someone or something) in a disapproving way
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00848023-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ pæn / (AmE), / pæn / (BrE)
A shallow, wide metal container with a flat bottom and low sides, used for cooking food over heat.
shallow metal vessel for cooking

Examples

She fried the eggs in a pan on the stove.
The recipe calls for a nonstick pan to prevent sticking.
After dinner, he washed the dirty pan and put it away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pot - a deep round container used for cooking

Etymology

From Old English 'panna', from Latin 'patina' (shallow pan or dish).

3. VERB

/ pæn / (AmE), / pæn / (BrE)
to wash earth or gravel in a pan in order to separate out valuable minerals such as gold
wash gravel in a pan to find minerals

Examples

During the Gold Rush, many prospectors panned for gold in the rivers of California.
He spent hours panning the stream, hoping to find some flakes of gold.
They taught visitors how to pan for gemstones in the shallow water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sluice - to wash or strain with a stream of water, often to separate precious minerals
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ pæn / (AmE), / pæn / (BrE)
(of a camera or operator) to move or be moved so as to follow an object or show a wider scene.
move a camera to follow or survey

Examples

The director instructed the cameraman to pan slowly across the bustling marketplace.
As the actors entered the stage, the camera panned to follow their movement.
She panned the camera from left to right to capture the entire landscape.

Synset Examples

The camera panned across the room

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tilt - move a camera up or down rather than side to side

5. NOUN

/ pæn / (AmE), / pæn / (BrE)
A shallow, typically metal container used for cooking food or holding liquids.
shallow metal cooking container

Examples

She heated the soup in a large pan on the stove.
After breakfast, he washed all the dirty pans.
The recipe says to use a shallow pan for frying the vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pot - a deep, round container used for cooking

Etymology

From Old English panne, from Latin panna (dish, pan).
