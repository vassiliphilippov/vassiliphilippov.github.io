oink

1. NOUN

/ ɔɪŋk / (AmE), / ɔɪŋk / (BrE)
a short, low, gruff sound made by a pig or hog
sound made by a pig

Examples

The loud oink from the pig startled the children at the farm.
She imitated a pig’s oink during the animal sound game.
We heard an oink coming from the pen as we walked by.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grunt - short guttural sound made by animals or humans
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07399214-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ɔɪŋk / (AmE), / ɔɪŋk / (BrE)
(of a pig) to make a high-pitched grunting or squealing sound characteristic of pigs.
make a pig's grunting sound

Examples

The pigs began to oink loudly when the farmer approached with their food.
Our dog sometimes tries to oink like a pig when he's excited.
Children laughed as they pretended to oink during the farm animal song.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grunt - make a low, guttural sound (of animals or humans)

Etymology

Imitative of the natural sound made by pigs.
