to ensile

1. VERB

/ ɛnˈsaɪl / (AmE), / ɛnˈsaɪl / (BrE)
to store (fodder or forage) in a silo to preserve it as silage
store fodder in a silo

Examples

Farmers often ensile corn and grass in early summer to ensure winter feed for their livestock.
The cooperative decided to ensile the surplus alfalfa after the unexpected rainfall.
You should ensile the fodder while it is still green to retain its nutrients.

Synset Examples

ensile fodder for the cows

Etymology

From French *ensiler*, from 'en-' (in) + 'silo'.
