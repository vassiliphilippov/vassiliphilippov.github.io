amply

1. ADV

/ ˈæmpli / (AmE), / ˈampli / (BrE)
to a large or sufficient degree; more than adequately
to a large or sufficient degree

Examples

His efforts were amply rewarded with a promotion and a bonus.
The room was amply decorated for the wedding reception, with flowers everywhere.
Your explanation should amply cover all the main points for the committee.

Synset Examples

these voices were amply represented
we benefited richly

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: adequately - to a sufficient degree or in a sufficient manner

Etymology

from the adjective "ample", from Latin "amplus" (large, spacious) + -ly (adverb suffix)

2. ADV

/ ˈæmpli / (AmE), / ˈampli / (BrE)
in a way that is more than sufficient; generously or fully
more than sufficiently; fully

Examples

The room was amply large enough to fit everyone comfortably.
She was amply rewarded for her years of hard work.
The needs of the refugees were amply provided for by relief agencies.

Synset Examples

the evidence amply (or fully) confirms our suspicions
they were fully (or amply) fed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sufficiently - to an adequate degree
  • Antonym: insufficiently - to an inadequate or too small degree

Etymology

From Latin 'amplus' meaning 'large, spacious' via French 'ample'.
