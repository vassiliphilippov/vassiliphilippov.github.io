Testudo graeca

1. NOUN

/ tɛˈstuːdoʊ ˈɡreɪkə / (AmE), / tɛˈstjuːdəʊ ˈɡreɪkə / (BrE)
A small species of land tortoise native to southern Europe, commonly known as the Greek tortoise.
a species of land tortoise (Greek tortoise)

Examples

The Testudo graeca is a popular species among reptile enthusiasts due to its manageable size.
Researchers tracked several wild Testudo graeca to study their migration patterns.
In some Mediterranean countries, the Testudo graeca is protected by law.

Etymology

From Latin: testudo (tortoise) + graeca (Greek).
