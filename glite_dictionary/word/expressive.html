expressive

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsprɛsɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈsprɛsɪv / (BrE)
showing or able to show emotions, thoughts, or feelings clearly; effectively conveying meaning or feeling.
showing emotions or feelings clearly

Examples

Her expressive eyes told me everything she wanted to say without a single word.
The actor has an expressive voice that captivates the audience.
Children often have very expressive faces when they are excited.

Synset Examples

a very expressive face

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inexpressive - not showing emotions or feelings clearly
  • Synonym: eloquent - expressing yourself readily, clearly, or effectively

Etymology

From French *expressif*, from Latin *expressivus* (serving to express).

2. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsprɛsɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈsprɛsɪv / (BrE)
showing or able to show feelings, thoughts, or ideas clearly and effectively, often through gestures, features, or manner.
showing feelings or ideas clearly

Examples

Her expressive eyes immediately revealed her excitement.
The dancer's movements were highly expressive and captivated the audience.
His face became more expressive as he told his story.

Synset Examples

Her manner was curiously expressive of a wish for protection and encouragement

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inexpressive - not showing emotions, feelings, or ideas
  • Synonym: eloquent - fluent or persuasive in expressing

Etymology

From French *expressif*, from Latin *expressivus* (forcible, clear).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-85983944-a
Synset POS: a
