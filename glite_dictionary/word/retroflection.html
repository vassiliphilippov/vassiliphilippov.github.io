retroflection

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛtroʊˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəʊˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act of turning the tip of the tongue back toward the roof of the mouth during speech, as in producing certain consonants
articulatory tongue movement in speech
medical

Examples

In Hindi, the letter 'ट' is pronounced with retroflection of the tongue.
Some speech therapists teach exercises to help with retroflection in certain sounds.
The linguist demonstrated how retroflection changes the quality of a consonant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retroflex - pronounced with the tongue curled back against the roof of the mouth

Etymology

From Latin retroflexus, past participle of retroflectere ('to bend backward').

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛtroʊˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of bending or turning backward
bending backward

Examples

The retroflection of the gymnast's spine impressed the judges.
During the procedure, the doctor noted a retroflection of the colon.
Some consonant sounds in language are produced with a retroflection of the tongue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retroflexion - alternative spelling, medical/anatomical context
  • Degree Lower: flexion - the act of bending, especially a limb or joint

Etymology

From Latin retroflexio (a bending back), from retro- (backward) + flexio (a bending).

3. NOUN

/ ˌrɛtroʊˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
the backward turning, bending, or tilting of an organ or body part
backward bending of an organ or body part
medical

Examples

The doctor noted a retroflection of the uterus during the examination.
Certain digestive issues may result from a retroflection in the colon.
A retroflection of the organ was detected through imaging scans.

Synset Examples

retroversion of the uterus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retroversion - backward turning of an organ, especially the uterus

Etymology

From Latin 'retroflexio', from 'retro-' (backward) + 'flectere' (to bend).
