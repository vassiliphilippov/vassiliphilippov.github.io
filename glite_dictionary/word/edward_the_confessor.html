Edward the Confessor

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛd.wɚd ðə kənˈfɛs.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛd.wəd ðə kənˈfɛs.ə / (BrE)
(c. 1003–1066) King of England from 1042 to 1066, known for his piety and for founding Westminster Abbey, where he was later buried.
King of England, 1042–1066; founded Westminster Abbey

Examples

Edward the Confessor was remembered for his role in establishing Westminster Abbey.
Historians regard Edward the Confessor as one of the last Anglo-Saxon kings of England.
The reign of Edward the Confessor ended just before the Norman Conquest.
