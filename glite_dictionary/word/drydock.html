drydock

1. VERB

/ ˈdraɪˌdɑk / (AmE), / ˈdraɪˌdɒk / (BrE)
to move (a ship) into a drydock for repairs or maintenance.
move ship into drydock

Examples

The shipyard plans to drydock the vessel for routine inspection next week.
After several months at sea, the oil tanker was drydocked for essential repairs.
The engineers are preparing to drydock the cruise liner for maintenance over the winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dock - to bring (a ship) into a dock
Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪˌdɑk / (AmE), / ˈdraɪˌdɒk / (BrE)
a large structure or dock from which water can be drained, used for building, maintaining, or repairing ships below the waterline
dock for ship repair and construction

Examples

The ship was taken into the drydock for extensive repairs to its hull.
After months in the drydock, the vessel was finally ready to return to sea.
Engineers inspected the submarine in the drydock to fix a leak below the waterline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: floating dock - a platform that can be submerged and raised to lift ships out of the water
  • Antonym: wet dock - a dock where water is not drained and ships stay afloat

Etymology

From 'dry' + 'dock', referring to the removal of water for maintenance or construction.
