veneering

1. NOUN

/ vəˈnɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / vəˈnɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
a thin layer of high-quality wood glued onto a base of cheaper wood, used as a decorative surface in furniture and paneling
thin decorative wooden layer

Examples

The cabinet featured a walnut veneering that made it look far more expensive than it was.
Many antique tables show signs of aged veneering on their surfaces.
Modern furniture uses veneering to achieve a luxurious appearance without the high cost.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: veneer - a thin decorative layer of fine wood

Etymology

From 'veneer' (from German 'Furnier') + -ing suffix.

2. NOUN

/ vəˈnɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / vɪˈnɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of applying a thin layer of material, such as fine wood, to the surface of an object to improve its appearance or finish
process of applying a veneer layer

Examples

The cabinetmaker demonstrated veneering techniques to the students in the workshop.
Victorian furniture often features intricate veneering with rare wood species.
Modern veneering can give inexpensive materials the appearance of luxury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: veneer - a thin decorative covering of fine wood

Etymology

From 'veneer' (to cover with a thin layer) + '-ing' (action noun suffix).
