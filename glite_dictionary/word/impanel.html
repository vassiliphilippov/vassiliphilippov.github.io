to impanel

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈpænəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpæn(ə)l / (BrE)
to select and enroll people, especially jurors, from a list to form a panel or jury for a trial
enroll people, especially jurors, onto a panel
legal

Examples

The court will impanel a new jury for the trial next week.
It is the judge's responsibility to impanel a fair and impartial group of jurors.
After the lawyers finished their questioning, the clerk began to impanel the selected individuals.

Synset Examples

empanel prospective jurors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to empanel - to select jurors from a list; alternative spelling of 'impanel'

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *empaneller*, from *en-* (in) + *panel* (piece of cloth, legal roll of names).

2. VERB

/ ɪmˈpænəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpænəl / (BrE)
to officially select and enroll people to serve as members of a jury
formally enroll as jury members
legal

Examples

The court will impanel a jury before the start of the trial.
It took almost a day to impanel enough jurors for the high-profile case.
Once the judge decided to impanel a grand jury, the legal process moved forward.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to empanel - to select a jury (alternative spelling)

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *empaneler*, from Old French *empaneller* (to enroll on a list), from *panel* (a piece of parchment with names).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02478126-v
Synset POS: v
