allergenic

1. ADJ

/ ˌælrəˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌaləˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
Having the property of causing an allergic reaction; relating to or containing an allergen.
causing an allergic reaction
medical

Examples

Some foods, such as peanuts, are highly allergenic for certain individuals.
The doctor recommended choosing allergenic tests to identify possible triggers for her symptoms.
Manufacturers often label products as 'non-allergenic' to assure consumers with allergies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonallergenic - not causing an allergic reaction
  • Synonym: allergen - a substance that causes an allergic reaction

Etymology

From allergen + -ic, indicating relating to or having the nature of.
