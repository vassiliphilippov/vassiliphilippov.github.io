Silene caroliniana

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈliːni kɛˌroʊlɪˈnaːnə / (AmE), / sɪˈliːni kaːrəˌlɪˈanə / (BrE)
A perennial plant of eastern and central North America, characterized by short-stalked pink or white flowers in hairy clusters.
a North American flowering perennial plant

Examples

The vibrant blooms of Silene caroliniana attract butterflies in early spring gardens.
Hikers in the Appalachians often spot clusters of Silene caroliniana along shaded trails.
Gardeners value Silene caroliniana for its resilience and delicate pink flowers.

Etymology

From the genus name Silene (named after Silenus from Greek mythology) and the Latinized form of 'Carolina', referring to the region where it is found.
