unfitness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈfɪtnɪs / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɪtnɪs / (BrE)
the state of being in poor physical condition and lacking fitness, especially as a result of inactivity or unhealthy living
state of poor physical condition

Examples

His prolonged period of unfitness made even a short walk exhausting.
The team lost the match due to the unfitness of several key players.
Decades of inactivity contributed to his overall unfitness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fitness - the condition of being physically healthy and strong

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'fitness'.

2. NOUN

/ ʌnˈfɪtnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɪtnəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of not being suitable or appropriate for a particular purpose or position
state of not being suitable

Examples

The committee cited his unfitness for the role due to lack of experience.
Concerns about the candidate's unfitness were raised during the interview process.
Physical unfitness can prevent applicants from entering certain professions.

Synset Examples

the judges agreed on his unfitness for the appointment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unsuitability - the quality of not being right or appropriate for a particular purpose
  • Antonym: fitness - the quality of being suitable or appropriate for a particular purpose

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'fitness' (suitability).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04729649-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ʌnˈfɪtnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɪtnəs / (BrE)
the state of lacking the ability, strength, or qualification to perform a task or role
state of being unable to perform

Examples

His unfitness for the job became clear during the interview process.
Despite repeated training, her unfitness for the marathon was obvious.
The court ruled on his unfitness to stand trial due to medical reasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inability - lack of ability to do something
  • Synonym: incapacity - lack of power, ability, or fitness
  • Antonym: fitness - the quality of being able to perform or be suitable

Etymology

Formed from 'unfit' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality of being unfit.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05214398-n
Synset POS: n
