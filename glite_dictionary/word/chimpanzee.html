chimpanzee

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪm.pænˈzi / (AmE), / tʃɪm.pænˈziː / (BrE)
an intelligent ape native to equatorial African forests, closely related to humans.
intelligent African ape species

Examples

The chimpanzee demonstrated remarkable problem-solving skills during the experiment.
At the zoo, we watched a young chimpanzee play with a stick as a tool.
Jane Goodall is famous for her research on chimpanzees in Tanzania.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ape - a large primate without a tail
  • Synonym: bonobo - a closely related species of ape found in central Africa

Etymology

From French 'chimpanzé', possibly from a Bantu language via Portuguese.
