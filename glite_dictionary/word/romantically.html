romantically

1. ADV

/ roʊˈmæntɪkli / (AmE), / rəˈmantɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that expresses or relates to love or romance.
in a manner relating to love or romance

Examples

They were romantically involved for over a year before getting married.
He surprised her romantically with flowers and a candlelit dinner.
She always dreamed of being whisked away romantically to Paris.

Synset Examples

they were romantically linked

Related Terms

  • Antonym: platonically - in a manner characterized by friendship, not romance

Etymology

From 'romantic' + '-ly'. 'Romantic' from French 'romantique', from medieval Latin 'romanticus'.

2. ADV

/ roʊˈmæntɪkli / (AmE), / rəʊˈmæntɪkli / (BrE)
in an idealistic or unrealistic way, especially with regard to love or romance
in an idealistic or romantic way

Examples

She always thought romantically about her future adventures and love stories.
He was romantically inclined, often imagining perfect relationships that didn't exist.
Magic and mystery filled the air as the city glowed romantically under the moonlight.

Synset Examples

she fantasized romantically about eloping with her boyfriend

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idealistically - in an idealistic manner
  • Antonym: practically - in a realistic or practical manner

Etymology

From 'romantic' + '-ally' (adverbial suffix)

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00472504-r
Synset POS: r
