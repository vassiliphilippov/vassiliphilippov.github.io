C0

1. NOUN

/ siː ˈzɪəroʊ / (AmE), / siː ˈzɪərəʊ / (BrE)
an international standard paper size measuring 917 x 1297 millimeters, commonly used for envelopes and paper products
international paper/envelope size, 917 x 1297 mm

Examples

The document was too large for a standard envelope, so I used a C0.
Architectural plans are often sent in a C0 envelope to prevent folding.
You can request your blueprints to be delivered in a C0 for extra protection.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: C1 - an international standard paper/envelope size, 648 x 917 mm
  • Degree Lower: C2 - an international standard paper/envelope size, 458 x 648 mm

Etymology

International designation from the ISO 269 standard, related to envelope sizes derived from ISO 216 paper formats.
