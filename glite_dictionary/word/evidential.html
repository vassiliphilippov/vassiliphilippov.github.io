evidential

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛvɪˈdɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌɛvɪˈdɛnʃəl / (BrE)
relating to or serving as evidence; based on or providing proof or indication.
serving as or relating to evidence

Examples

The report contained several evidential links between the suspect and the crime scene.
Her theory lacked evidential support and was therefore dismissed by the panel.
Investigators searched for evidential traces to confirm the timeline of events.

Synset Examples

evidential signs of a forced entry
its evidentiary value

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evidentiary - relating to or providing evidence, especially in law

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'evidentialis', from Latin 'evidentia' (evidence).
