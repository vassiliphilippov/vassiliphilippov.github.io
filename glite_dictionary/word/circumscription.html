circumscription

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɜr.kəmˈskrɪp.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.kəmˈskrɪp.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of drawing or marking a boundary around something; the process of restricting, limiting, or defining the limits of something
the act of defining a boundary or limit

Examples

The circumscription of the new park was approved by the city council last week.
Philosophers discussed the circumscription of ethical responsibilities within the profession.
Accurate circumscription of the tumor is essential before surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limitation - the act of limiting or restricting something

Etymology

From Latin circumscriptionem, from circum- (around) + scribere (to write).
