hackmatack

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæk.məˌtæk / (AmE), / ˈhak.məˌtak / (BrE)
A poplar tree native to northeastern North America, distinguished by its broad heart-shaped leaves.
poplar tree of northeastern North America

Examples

The dense forest contained several tall hackmatack trees shading the pathway.
In spring, the hackmatack displays broad, heart-shaped leaves that rustle in the breeze.
Many indigenous communities used hackmatack wood for practical crafts and construction.

Etymology

From Algonquian (eastern Abenaki) *akəmaták* (wood suitable for snowshoes).
