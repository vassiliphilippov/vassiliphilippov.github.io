to house-train

1. VERB

/ ˈhaʊs treɪn / (AmE), / ˈhaʊs treɪn / (BrE)
to train a pet to behave cleanly and appropriately inside a house, especially to urinate and defecate in designated places
teach a pet to be clean indoors

Examples

It can take several weeks to house-train a new puppy.
They successfully house-trained their dog using positive reinforcement.
Some cats are easy to house-train, while others need more patience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to housebreak - to train (a pet) to excrete outdoors or in a designated spot

Etymology

Formed from 'house' + 'train', referring to training a pet for living indoors.
