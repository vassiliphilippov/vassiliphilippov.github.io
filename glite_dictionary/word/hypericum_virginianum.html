Hypericum virginianum

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɛrɪkəm vɜrˌdʒɪniˈænəm / (AmE), / hɪˈpɛrɪkəm vɜːˌdʒɪniˈænəm / (BrE)
A perennial marsh herb with pink to mauve flowers, native to the southeastern United States.
marsh herb of southeastern US

Examples

The wetland was dotted with blooming Hypericum virginianum, adding a splash of color to the scenery.
Botanists surveyed several populations of Hypericum virginianum in the southeastern United States.
Gardeners appreciate Hypericum virginianum for its resilience and attractive mauve flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marsh St. John's-wort - Common name for Hypericum virginianum

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'hyperikon', species named for Virginia, United States.
