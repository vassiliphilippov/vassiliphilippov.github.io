Zygnemales

1. NOUN

/ zaɪɡˈniːməˌliːz / (AmE), / zaɪɡˈniːməˌliːz / (BrE)
an order of green algae, including desmids and filamentous pond scums, commonly found in freshwater environments
order of green algae (pond scums and desmids)
medical

Examples

The Zygnemales play an important role in freshwater ecosystems as oxygen producers.
Microscopic examination revealed that the pond water contained several species from the Zygnemales.
Desmids, which belong to the Zygnemales, are often used as indicators of water quality.

Etymology

From New Latin Zygnema (a genus of green algae) + -ales (denoting taxonomic order).

2. NOUN

/ zaɪɡˈniːməliːz / (AmE), / zɪɡˈniːməliːz / (BrE)
an order of filamentous green algae, found mainly in freshwater, including pond scums and desmids
order of green freshwater algae

Examples

Zygnemales are commonly observed as green scum on the surface of ponds and slow-moving streams.
Several freshwater ecosystems rely on Zygnemales to produce oxygen and serve as food for aquatic organisms.
Researchers examined samples of Zygnemales to study the diversity of algae populations in local lakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desmid - a single-celled green alga of the family Desmidiaceae, often found in peat bogs
  • Degree Higher: green algae - a large group of algae that includes many freshwater and marine plants containing chlorophyll

Etymology

From genus *Zygnema* + Latin plural order suffix -ales, used in taxonomy.
