Ellul

1. NOUN

/ ɛˈluːl / (AmE), / ɛˈluːl / (BrE)
the twelfth month of the civil year and the sixth month of the ecclesiastical year in the Jewish calendar, usually falling in August and September
month in the Jewish calendar (August–September)

Examples

The Jewish festival of repentance begins during Ellul.
Many families reflect on their actions throughout Ellul in preparation for the High Holidays.
This year, Ellul starts in late August and ends in September.

Etymology

From Hebrew *Ĕlūl*, the name of the month in the Jewish calendar.
