Alzheimer's

1. NOUN

/ ælˈzaɪmɚz / (AmE), / ælˈzaɪməz / (BrE)
A progressive and irreversible brain disorder characterized by gradual deterioration of memory, thinking, and the ability to carry out everyday activities, typically beginning in middle age or later.
progressive brain disorder causing memory loss
medical

Examples

Her grandmother was diagnosed with Alzheimer's and now requires constant care.
Early symptoms of Alzheimer's include memory loss and confusion.
Medical research is focused on finding a cure for Alzheimer's disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dementia - a general term for a decline in mental ability severe enough to interfere with daily life

Etymology

Named after Alois Alzheimer (1864–1915), the German neurologist who first described the disease.

2. NOUN

/ ˈælzˌhaɪmərz / (AmE), / ˈaltshaɪməz / (BrE)
A progressive neurological disease that causes memory loss, impaired thinking, and eventual loss of the ability to carry out simple tasks, typically starting in middle or old age.
progressive neurological disease (dementia)
medical

Examples

My grandmother was diagnosed with Alzheimer's in her late sixties.
Caring for someone with Alzheimer's can be emotionally and physically demanding.
Researchers are working hard to find a cure for Alzheimer's disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dementia - general term for a decline in mental ability severe enough to interfere with daily life

Etymology

Named after Alois Alzheimer (1864–1915), German neurologist who first described the disease.
