beeswax

1. VERB

/ ˈbiːz.wæks / (AmE), / ˈbiːz.waks / (BrE)
to cover or polish (a surface) with beeswax, typically to protect or enhance its appearance
to coat or polish with beeswax

Examples

He carefully beeswaxed the antique dresser to restore its shine.
After sanding the floor, they beeswaxed it to protect the wood.
She spends weekends beeswaxing her handmade candles for extra gloss.

Synset Examples

Chris beeswaxed the kitchen table

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wax - to cover or treat with wax

Etymology

From 'beeswax', a substance produced by bees, used as a protective or polishing agent.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01271434-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈbiːzwæks / (AmE), / ˈbiːzwæks / (BrE)
a yellow to brown waxy substance secreted by honeybees and used by them to build honeycombs, often used in making candles, cosmetics, and polishes
yellowish wax from honeybees

Examples

She used beeswax to make natural candles for her home.
The antique table was polished to a shine with beeswax.
Honeybees produce beeswax as they build the hive's honeycombs.

Etymology

bees + wax; referring to the wax produced by bees.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15119391-n
Synset POS: n
