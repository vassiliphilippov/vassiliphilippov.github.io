spoor

1. NOUN

/ spʊr / (AmE), / spɔː / (BrE)
a track, scent, or other sign left by a person or animal, especially as observed by a hunter or tracker
a trail or sign left by an animal or person

Examples

Expert trackers studied the spoor in the mud to locate the herd of elephants.
Fresh spoor on the trail suggested the lion was nearby.
The detective carefully examined the spoor left at the scene to piece together the suspect's movements.

Synset Examples

the hounds followed the fox's spoor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: track - a mark or line left by a moving animal, person, or vehicle
  • Synonym: trail - a mark, path, or sign left by something that has passed

Etymology

From Afrikaans, from Dutch *spoor* (track, footprint), ultimately from Middle Dutch.
