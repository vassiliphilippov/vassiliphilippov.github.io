echocardiography

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkoʊˌkɑrdiˈɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌɛkəʊˌkɑːdiˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
a noninvasive medical procedure that uses ultrasound waves to create images of the structure and movements of the heart
heart ultrasound imaging procedure
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended an echocardiography to assess the patient's heart function.
Advancements in echocardiography have greatly improved the diagnosis of heart conditions.
A routine echocardiography can detect abnormalities in heart valves or chambers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ultrasound - high-frequency sound waves used in medical imaging

Etymology

From echo (sound reflection) + cardiography (recording of heart activity); based on Greek kardia (heart) and graphia (writing).
