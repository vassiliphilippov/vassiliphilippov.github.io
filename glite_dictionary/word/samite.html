samite

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæˌmaɪt / (AmE), / ˈsæmaɪt / (BrE)
A heavy and often ornate silk fabric, sometimes woven with gold or silver threads, used mainly in the Middle Ages for clothing and furnishings.
heavy medieval silk fabric
archaic

Examples

The queen was robed in samite adorned with intricate gold patterns.
Historians marveled at the preservation of the samite found in the ancient tomb.
Knights sometimes wore mantles of samite to signify their wealth and status.

Etymology

From Old French samit, from Medieval Latin samitum, from Byzantine Greek hexamiton (six threads), referring to the fabric's structure.
