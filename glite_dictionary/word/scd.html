ScD

1. NOUN

/ ɛs si ˈdi / (AmE), / ɛs si ˈdiː / (BrE)
a Doctor of Science degree, often awarded as an honorary or advanced academic degree in science
Doctor of Science academic degree

Examples

He received an ScD from Harvard in recognition of his groundbreaking research.
After years of scientific contributions, she was awarded an honorary ScD.
The university conferred the title of ScD upon the distinguished professor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: PhD - Doctor of Philosophy degree, a common advanced academic degree

Etymology

Abbreviation of Latin 'Scientiae Doctor' meaning 'Doctor of Science'.
