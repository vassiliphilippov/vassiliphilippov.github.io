Calopogon pulchellum

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈpoʊɡɑn pʊlˈkɛləm / (AmE), / ˌkaləˈpəʊɡɒn pʊlˈkɛləm / (BrE)
A North American orchid species, commonly known as the grass pink, with showy pink to purple flowers.
a North American orchid species

Examples

In the wet meadows, we spotted several Calopogon pulchellum in full bloom.
Botanists are studying the unique pollination mechanism of Calopogon pulchellum.
The delicate pink petals of Calopogon pulchellum attract many nature photographers each spring.

Etymology

From Greek 'kalos' (beautiful) and 'pogon' (beard), and Latin 'pulchellus' (pretty, little).
