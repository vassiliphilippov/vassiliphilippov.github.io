insubstantial

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnsəbˈstæn(t)ʃəl / (AmE), / ˌɪnsəbˈstænʃl̩ / (BrE)
lacking substance, strength, or solidity; (of food) lacking in nutritive value or satisfying content
lacking substance or nutritional value

Examples

The soup was so insubstantial that I was still hungry after finishing it.
He complained about the insubstantial lunch provided at the conference.
She found the snack insubstantial and needed something more filling.

Synset Examples

an insubstantial and unsatisfying meal

Related Terms

  • Antonym: substantial - having substance or nourishment; ample

Etymology

From Latin 'substantia' (substance), with prefix 'in-' meaning 'not'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪnsəbˈstænʃəl / (AmE), / ˌɪnsəbˈstænʃəl / (BrE)
not solid or strong; lacking substance, strength, or reality.
lacking solidity or strength

Examples

The evidence he provided was dismissed as insubstantial by the judge.
The meal was tasty but rather insubstantial for a long hike.
The rumors about the merger proved insubstantial and quickly faded away.

Synset Examples

a flimsy table
flimsy construction
vinyl siding has become the standard-bearer for cheap, insubstantial construction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flimsy - light and thin; lacking strength or solidity
  • Antonym: substantial - firmly constructed; of considerable size or importance

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + substantialis (of substance).

3. ADJ

/ ˌɪnsəbˈstænʃəl / (AmE), / ˌɪnsəbˈstænʃəl / (BrE)
lacking physical substance or reality; not solid or real.
lacking substance or reality

Examples

His argument seemed insubstantial and was quickly dismissed by the committee.
The castle in the clouds looked beautiful but was entirely insubstantial.
She reached for the insubstantial shadow, but her hand passed right through it.

Synset Examples

as insubstantial as a dream
an insubstantial mirage on the horizon

Related Terms

  • Antonym: substantial - having physical substance or reality; solid or real

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + substantialis (substantial).
