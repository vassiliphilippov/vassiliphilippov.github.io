disposition

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪspəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪspəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of getting rid of something, especially by throwing away, selling, or transferring it
the act of getting rid of something

Examples

The disposition of hazardous waste must be handled carefully to prevent environmental damage.
After the auction, the disposition of the estate's remaining items was arranged by the executors.
Regulations govern the disposition of confidential documents in most offices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disposal - the action or process of getting rid of something

Etymology

From Latin dispositio (arrangement, regulation), from disponere (to arrange).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪspəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪspəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
a habitual or natural tendency or inclination in a person or thing
habitual tendency or inclination

Examples
