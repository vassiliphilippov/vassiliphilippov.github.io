united

1. ADJ

/ juːˈnaɪtɪd / (AmE), / juːˈnaɪtɪd / (BrE)
joined together as a single group or entity; acting in agreement or harmony.
joined or acting as one

Examples

The team stood united against their opponents in the championship match.
A united community can overcome almost any challenge.
The countries issued a united statement calling for peace.

Synset Examples

presented a united front

Related Terms

  • Antonym: divided - separated into parts; not acting as one
  • Synonym: unified - made into or caused to be a unit; brought together

Etymology

From unite + -ed, from Latin 'unitus' (to unite).

2. ADJ

/ juˈnaɪtɪd / (AmE), / juˈnaɪtɪd / (BrE)
involving the combined efforts or actions of two or more people, groups, or things
involving joint effort or action

Examples

The two companies made a united effort to address the environmental issue together.
Only through a united response can the community overcome such challenges.
The nations acted in a united manner to achieve peace.

Synset Examples

concerted action
the conjunct influence of fire and strong wind
the conjunctive focus of political opposition
a cooperative effort
a united effort

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joint - involving two or more people or groups together
  • Antonym: separate - not joined or united; apart

Etymology

From Latin unitus, past participle of unire (to unite).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02119388-s
Synset POS: s
