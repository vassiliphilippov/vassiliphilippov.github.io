individualistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪndəˌvɪdʒuəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˌvɪdjʊəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
characterized by or expressing individuality; emphasizing personal independence and self-reliance
expressing individuality or personal independence

Examples

Her individualistic style of painting made her work instantly recognizable.
The company's culture encourages individualistic thinking and creativity among employees.
He admired her individualistic approach to solving complex problems.

Synset Examples

an individualistic way of dressing

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conformist - adhering to established customs or practices
  • Synonym: individuality - the quality that makes one person or thing different from others

Etymology

From 'individual', from Latin 'individuus' (indivisible) + '-istic' (forming adjectives)

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˌvɪdʒuəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˌvɪdʒuˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
(of a system, policy, or society) characterized by minimally restricted freedom, especially in economic or commercial matters.
favoring minimal restrictions, esp. in commerce

Examples

The country's individualistic approach to the market has encouraged rapid economic growth.
Critics argue that an individualistic economic policy can increase inequality.
Some see the American economy as more individualistic than the European model.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laissez-faire - policy of minimal state intervention in economic affairs
  • Antonym: collectivist - emphasizing group control over individual freedom

Etymology

From 'individual' + '-istic', with 'individual' from Medieval Latin *individualis* (indivisible, single).
