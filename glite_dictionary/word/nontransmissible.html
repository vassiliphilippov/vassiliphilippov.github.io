nontransmissible

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑntrænzˈmɪsəbəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒntrænzˈmɪsəbəl / (BrE)
(of a disease) not capable of being transmitted from one person or organism to another.
not able to be transmitted (esp. disease)
medical

Examples

Unlike the common cold, heart disease is a nontransmissible condition.
One of the main focuses of public health is the prevention of nontransmissible diseases such as diabetes.
It is important to distinguish between nontransmissible and infectious diseases when developing health policies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: communicable - (of diseases) able to be transmitted from one person or organism to another
  • Antonym: infectious - (of diseases) able to be passed from one individual to another, especially by infection

Etymology

From non- (not) + transmissible, from Latin transmissibilis (that can be transmitted).

2. ADJ

/ ˌnɑn.trænzˈmɪs.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.trænzˈmɪs.ɪ.bəl / (BrE)
(of a disease or characteristic) not able to be acquired by inheritance or transmitted from one individual to another.
not able to be inherited or transmitted

Examples

Genetic disorders can be classified as nontransmissible if they cannot be passed down to offspring.
Doctors reassured the public that the condition is nontransmissible and poses no infectious risk.
Some traits are nontransmissible, meaning they are acquired, not inherited.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: transmissible - able to be transmitted or passed from one individual to another

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' (not) and 'transmissible' (able to be transmitted).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01318973-s
Synset POS: s
