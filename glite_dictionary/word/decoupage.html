decoupage

1. NOUN

/ ˌdeɪkuˈpɑʒ / (AmE), / ˌdeɪkuːˈpɑːʒ / (BrE)
the art of decorating a surface by applying cut-out paper designs and covering them with layers of varnish or lacquer
art of decorating surfaces with paper cutouts and varnish

Examples

She learned how to make beautiful trays using decoupage techniques.
The artist's studio was filled with supplies for her decoupage projects.
Many people enjoy decoupage as a relaxing and creative hobby.

Etymology

From French découper (to cut out), from couper (to cut).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdeɪˌkuːˈpɑːʒ / (AmE), / ˌdeɪˈkuːpɑːʒ / (BrE)
the art of decorating an object by gluing colored paper cutouts onto it and finishing with layers of varnish or lacquer
decorating objects with cut paper and varnish

Examples

She spent the afternoon working on a decoupage project for her friend's birthday gift.
The handmade box was beautifully decorated using decoupage techniques.
Many crafters enjoy decoupage because it allows for creativity with simple materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collage - the art of assembling different forms to create a new whole

Etymology

From French 'découpage', from 'découper' meaning 'to cut out'.
