cytomegalic

1. ADJ

/ saɪ.toʊ.məˈɡæl.ɪk / (AmE), / saɪ.təʊ.məˈɡæl.ɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characterized by greatly enlarged cells.
having greatly enlarged cells
medical

Examples

The tissue sample was found to be cytomegalic under microscopic examination.
A cytomegalic cell is often a sign of viral infection in the body.
Researchers noted several cytomegalic changes in the liver cells of affected patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cytomegaly - abnormal enlargement of cells

Etymology

From Greek 'kytos' (cell) and 'megas' (large), with the suffix '-ic' forming an adjective.
