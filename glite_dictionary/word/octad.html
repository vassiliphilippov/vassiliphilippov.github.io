octad

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑk.tæd / (AmE), / ˈɒk.tad / (BrE)
a group or set of eight people or things; the number eight
a set or group of eight

Examples

The scientist noted that the molecule formed an octad structure.
Each section of the orchestra included an octad of musicians.
The team was divided into units of eight, with each octad working independently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: octet - a group or set of eight, especially in music or science

Etymology

From Greek oktás, oktádos, meaning 'a group of eight'.
