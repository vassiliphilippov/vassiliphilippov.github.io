Raphus cucullatus

1. NOUN

/ ˈræfəs kjuːˈkʌlətəs / (AmE), / ˈræfəs kjuːˈkʌlətəs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the dodo, an extinct large flightless bird from Mauritius, related to pigeons.
scientific name for the dodo

Examples

The discovery of Raphus cucullatus remains on Mauritius helped scientists understand the extinction of the dodo.
Taxonomists now classify the dodo under the genus Raphus cucullatus.
Research into Raphus cucullatus has revealed its close relation to modern pigeons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dodo - the common name for Raphus cucullatus, an extinct flightless bird from Mauritius

Etymology

From Latin; 'Raphus' genus name, 'cucullatus' meaning 'hooded', describing the appearance.
