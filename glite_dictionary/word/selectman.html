selectman

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɛktmən / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛktmən / (BrE)
A man elected as a member of the board that governs a town in New England.
male elected town official (New England)

Examples

The annual town meeting elected John as a selectman for the coming year.
As a selectman, he was responsible for overseeing the town's budget and policies.
Several residents voiced their concerns to the selectman about the proposed construction project.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: selectwoman - female elected member of a town's board in New England

Etymology

From the word 'select' and the suffix '-man', originally used in colonial New England.
