Mariposan

1. NOUN

/ ˌmærɪˈpoʊzən / (AmE), / ˌmærɪˈpəʊzən / (BrE)
a Penutian language formerly spoken by the Yokuts people in California's San Joaquin Valley
a language of the Yokuts in California

Examples

Linguists have studied the Mariposan language to better understand the history of the Yokuts people.
Several words in Mariposan have been preserved in anthropological records.
The decline of Mariposan reflects broader language loss among indigenous groups in California.

Etymology

From Mariposa, a county and place name in California, often linked to language groupings in the area.
