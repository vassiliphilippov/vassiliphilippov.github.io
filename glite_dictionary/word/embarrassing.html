embarrassing

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈbærəsɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪmˈbærəsɪŋ / (BrE)
causing discomfort, shame, or awkwardness
causing shame or awkwardness

Examples

She found it embarrassing to speak in front of such a large audience.
An embarrassing silence fell over the room after his comment.
Forgetting my friend's name during the introduction was really embarrassing.

Synset Examples

awkward (or embarrassing or difficult) moments in the discussion
an awkward pause followed his remark
a sticky question
in the unenviable position of resorting to an act he had planned to save for the climax of the campaign

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awkward - causing difficulty or discomfort
  • Antonym: comfortable - causing no discomfort or embarrassment

Etymology

From French 'embarrasser', from Italian 'imbarazzare', meaning to hinder or obstruct.

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈbærəsɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪmˈbærəsɪŋ / (BrE)
causing a person to feel awkward, self-conscious, or ashamed.
causing awkwardness or shame

Examples

It was an embarrassing mistake to call her by the wrong name at the meeting.
She found the question about her salary quite embarrassing to answer in public.
His pants splitting during the presentation was an embarrassing moment.

Synset Examples

the embarrassing moment when she found her petticoat down around her ankles
it was mortifying to know he had heard every word

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mortifying - causing great embarrassment or shame
  • Synonym: humiliating - causing someone to feel ashamed or foolish
  • Antonym: proud - feeling deep pleasure or satisfaction as a result of achievements

Etymology

From French 'embarrasser', from Italian 'imbarazzare' (to entangle, impede).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01808452-s
Synset POS: s
