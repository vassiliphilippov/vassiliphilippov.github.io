Roquefort

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊkfərt / (AmE), / ˈrɒkfɔː / (BrE)
A rich, blue-veined French cheese made from sheep's milk, known for its strong flavor and crumbly texture.
French blue cheese made from sheep's milk

Examples

She crumbled Roquefort over her salad for an extra burst of flavor.
The restaurant's cheese platter featured aged cheddar, brie, and Roquefort.
Many gourmet recipes call for Roquefort as the main ingredient in dressings and sauces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue cheese - Cheese with blue or green mold veins throughout

Etymology

French, named after Roquefort, the region in southern France where the cheese is produced.
