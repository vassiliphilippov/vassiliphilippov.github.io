sappy

1. ADJ

/ ˈsæpi / (AmE), / ˈsæpi / (BrE)
(of a plant, tree, or wood) containing a large amount of sap.
abundant in sap (plant/wood)

Examples

The sappy maple trees are tapped each spring to make syrup.
Freshly cut pine is typically quite sappy and can be difficult to burn.
We need to avoid using sappy kindling wood, as it creates too much smoke.

Synset Examples

sappy maple trees
sappy kindling wood

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sapless - lacking sap; dry

Etymology

From sap + -y, meaning 'full of sap.'

2. ADJ

/ ˈsæpi / (AmE), / ˈsæpi / (BrE)
excessively sentimental or emotional, especially in a way that seems insincere or silly
overly sentimental or emotional
informal

Examples

The movie was so sappy that I nearly rolled my eyes at every romantic scene.
She wrote a sappy letter filled with clichés about true love and destiny.
Critics complained that the commercial's message was a bit too sappy for their taste.

Synset Examples

a bathetic novel
maudlin expressions of sympathy
mushy effusiveness
a schmaltzy song
sentimental soap operas
slushy poetry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sentimental - showing or causing exaggerated emotion or nostalgia
  • Synonym: maudlin - tearfully or weakly emotional; foolishly sentimental
  • Antonym: realistic - representing things in a practical and lifelike way

Etymology

From 'sap', originally meaning full of sap (liquid in plants); figurative modern sense developed in 20th century.
