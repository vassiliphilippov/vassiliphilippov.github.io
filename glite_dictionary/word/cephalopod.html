cephalopod

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛfəloʊˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈsɛfələˌpɒd / (BrE)
relating to, or belonging to the class Cephalopoda, a group of marine animals including octopuses, squids, and cuttlefish
of or relating to cephalopods (marine mollusks)
medical

Examples

The team observed cephalopod behavior in their underwater habitat.
She specializes in cephalopod anatomy, particularly the nervous system of squids.
The museum has a new exhibit featuring cephalopod fossils from the Jurassic period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cephalopod - a marine mollusk of the class Cephalopoda

Etymology

From Greek kephalē (head) + pous, pod- (foot), referring to head-footed animals.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛfələˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈsɛfələˌpɒd / (BrE)
A marine mollusk of the class Cephalopoda, characterized by a well-developed head, large eyes, and tentacles with suckers, such as an octopus or squid.
marine mollusk with tentacles (e.g., octopus, squid)
medical

Examples

The giant cephalopod glided silently through the deep sea, searching for prey.
Scientists studied the behavior of a cephalopod to better understand its problem-solving abilities.
An octopus is one of the most intelligent members of the cephalopod class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: octopus - a soft-bodied, eight-limbed mollusk
  • Synonym: squid - a cephalopod with an elongated body and ten limbs

Etymology

From Greek kephalē (head) + pod (foot).
