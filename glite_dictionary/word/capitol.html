Capitol

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæpɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈkæpɪtəl / (BrE)
The government building in Washington, D.C., where the United States Congress meets.
US Congress's government building in Washington, D.C.
american

Examples

The president delivered the speech at the Capitol in front of thousands of spectators.
Security measures were heightened around the Capitol after the recent events.
A school field trip to the Capitol is planned for next week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capitol building - another term for the Capitol, especially in the US context

Etymology

From Latin 'Capitōlium', the temple of Jupiter in Rome.
