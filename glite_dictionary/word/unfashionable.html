unfashionable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfæʃənəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈfæʃ(ə)nəbl / (BrE)
not in keeping with the current style or trends; not fashionable.
not fashionable or trendy

Examples

He felt self-conscious wearing his unfashionable jacket to the party.
Vintage shops often carry unfashionable garments from previous decades.
It suddenly became unfashionable to use that kind of language in public.

Synset Examples

unfashionable clothes
melodrama of a now unfashionable kind

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fashionable - conforming to the current style or trends

Etymology

Formed by combining the prefix "un-" (not) and "fashionable."

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfæʃənəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈfæʃənəbl / (BrE)
not popular or considered stylish at a particular time; lacking in current fashion or style.
not fashionable; out of style

Examples

His clothes looked unfashionable compared to his classmates' trendy outfits.
The restaurant's décor is now considered unfashionable by modern standards.
She refuses to wear anything unfashionable, always keeping up with current trends.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fashionable - popular, stylish, or in accordance with the latest trends
  • Antonym: stylish - fashionable and elegant

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'fashionable' (from fashion + -able).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01822663-s
Synset POS: s
