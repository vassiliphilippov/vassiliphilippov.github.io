chimer

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaɪ.mər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪmə / (BrE)
A fur-lined coat decorated with braid, worn in the sixteenth century.
sixteenth-century fur-lined coat

Examples

The nobleman wore a richly embroidered chimer to the royal banquet.
Portraits from the sixteenth century often depict gentlemen dressed in a chimer.
Her costume included a reconstructed chimer to reflect the fashion of that era.
