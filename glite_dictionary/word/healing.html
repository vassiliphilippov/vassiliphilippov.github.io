healing

1. ADJ

/ ˈhiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
Having the quality of curing or restoring to health.
tending to cure; restorative

Examples

Many people believe in the healing power of nature when recovering from illness.
She recommended a healing ointment for my skin irritation.
Yoga and meditation can have a healing effect on both mind and body.

Synset Examples

curative powers of herbal remedies
her gentle healing hand
remedial surgery
a sanative environment of mountains and fresh air
a therapeutic agent
therapeutic diets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curative - having healing or remedial properties
  • Synonym: therapeutic - relating to the healing of disease
  • Antonym: harmful - causing harm or damage; the opposite of healing

2. NOUN

/ ˈhiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the natural process by which the body recovers from an injury or illness
recovery process from injury or illness

Examples

After breaking his leg, his doctor monitored the healing carefully.
The medicine is believed to speed up the body's natural healing.
Proper rest is essential for the healing of wounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recovery - a return to a normal state of health, mind, or strength

Etymology

Derived from Old English *hǣlan* (to make whole, heal).
