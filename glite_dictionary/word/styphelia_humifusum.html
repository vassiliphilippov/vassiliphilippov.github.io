Styphelia humifusum

1. NOUN

/ staɪˈfiːliə ˌhjuːmɪˈfjuːsəm / (AmE), / stɪˈfiːliə ˌhjuːmɪˈfjuːsəm / (BrE)
A small, prostrate or ascending shrub native to Australia, with scarlet flowers and succulent fruit resembling cranberries; sometimes classified in the genus Styphelia.
Australian shrub with scarlet flowers and cranberry-like fruit

Examples

The delicate flowers of Styphelia humifusum add a splash of color to the heathland in spring.
Native birds are attracted to the fruit produced by Styphelia humifusum.
Gardeners value Styphelia humifusum for its low-growing habit and ornamental fruit.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Styphelia' (genus name in botanical Latin), 'humifusum' meaning 'spreading on the ground'.
