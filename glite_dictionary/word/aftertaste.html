aftertaste

1. NOUN

/ ˈæftərˌteɪst / (AmE), / ˈɑːftəteɪst / (BrE)
a taste that remains in the mouth after eating or drinking something
taste left in the mouth after eating or drinking

Examples

The coffee had a bitter aftertaste that lingered for several minutes.
This wine leaves a pleasant fruity aftertaste on the palate.
Chewing gum can sometimes mask the aftertaste of certain foods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flavor - the distinctive quality of a food or drink as perceived by the taste buds

Etymology

From after- (following) + taste.
