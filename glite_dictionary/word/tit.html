tit

1. NOUN

/ tɪt / (AmE), / tɪt / (BrE)
A small songbird of the family Paridae, typically with a stout bill and a lively manner, found especially in Eurasia and Africa.
small insectivorous songbird

Examples

The tit flitted from branch to branch searching for insects.
Many garden birdwatchers enjoy seeing blue tits at the feeder.
A great tit sang loudly near the woodland path.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chickadee - a North American bird of the same family as the tit

Etymology

From Old English *titt* (small animal), related to Scandinavian words for small birds.

2. NOUN

/ tɪt / (AmE), / tɪt / (BrE)
a nipple; the small projecting part of a mammary gland, especially in mammals
nipple or small projection of a mammary gland
informal vulgar

Examples

She breastfed her baby using only one tit at a time.
The sculpture featured the realistic depiction of a mother’s tit.
Some mammals have more than one tit on each side.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nipple - the small protuberance on the breast through which milk is drawn
  • Synonym: teat - the fleshy part of a mammal's mammary gland through which milk is sucked

Etymology

Old English titt(e), related to words for nipple or teat in several Germanic languages.

slang vulgar
