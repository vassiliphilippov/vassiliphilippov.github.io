Lupinus luteus

1. NOUN

/ luˈpaɪnəs ˈluːtiəs / (AmE), / ljuˈpaɪnəs ˈluːtiːʌs / (BrE)
A yellow-flowered European species of lupine cultivated as forage and sometimes for its seeds.
yellow-flowered European lupine species

Examples

Lupinus luteus is often grown in temperate climates for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
Farmers plant Lupinus luteus to improve soil fertility and provide livestock feed.
The fields were covered with the bright yellow flowers of Lupinus luteus in early spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lupine - any plant of the genus Lupinus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Lupinus' (the lupine genus) and 'luteus' (yellow).
