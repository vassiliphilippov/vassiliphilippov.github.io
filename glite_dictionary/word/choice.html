choice

1. NOUN

/ tʃɔɪs / (AmE), / tʃɔɪs / (BrE)
one of a set of possibilities from which you can select only one
option; one of several things to choose from

Examples

She was faced with a difficult choice between two good job offers.
Out of all the desserts on the menu, his choice was the cheesecake.
You don't have much choice if you wait until the last minute.

Synset Examples

what option did I have?
there is no other alternative
my only choice is to refuse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: option - one of several possible things you can choose
  • Synonym: alternative - another possibility or choice

Etymology

From Old French 'chois' (action of choosing), from 'choisir' (to choose).
