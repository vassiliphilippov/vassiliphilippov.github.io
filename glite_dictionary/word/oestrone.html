oestrone

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛstroʊn / (AmE), / ˈiːstrəʊn / (BrE)
a naturally occurring weak estrogenic hormone produced by the ovaries, sometimes used in medicine to treat estrogen deficiency
a weak estrogen hormone produced by the ovaries
medical british

Examples

Doctors sometimes prescribe oestrone to treat symptoms of menopause.
The laboratory measured the patient's oestrone levels to assess hormonal balance.
Research has shown that oestrone is less potent than other forms of estrogen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estrone - American English spelling of 'oestrone', same hormone

Etymology

From Greek 'oistros' (frenzy, inspiration) + '-one' (chemical suffix for ketones).
