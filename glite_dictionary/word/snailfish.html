snailfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈsneɪlˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsneɪl.fɪʃ / (BrE)
A small, tadpole-shaped cold-water fish with pelvic fins forming a sucker, related to the lumpfish, found mainly in northern oceans.
a small cold-water fish with sucker-like pelvic fins

Examples

The snailfish lives at depths where few other fish can survive.
Researchers recently discovered a new species of snailfish in the Mariana Trench.
The tadpole-like appearance of the snailfish helps it blend with the rocky seabed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lumpfish - a cold-water fish, related species to the snailfish
