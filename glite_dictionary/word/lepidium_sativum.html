Lepidium sativum

1. NOUN

/ ləˈpɪdiəm səˈtɪvəm / (AmE), / lɪˈpɪdɪəm səˈtɪvəm / (BrE)
an annual herb, also known as garden cress, with aromatic leaves commonly used as a salad green and garnish
annual herb used as salad green

Examples

The chef sprinkled Lepidium sativum over the dish to add a peppery note.
Fresh Lepidium sativum is often included in spring salads for its crisp texture.
Many gardeners grow Lepidium sativum for easy access to its flavorful leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garden cress - another name for Lepidium sativum, a salad herb

Etymology

From Latin Lepidium (a plant genus) + sativum (cultivated).
