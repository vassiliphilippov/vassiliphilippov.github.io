ungraded

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɡreɪdɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡreɪdɪd / (BrE)
not arranged or classified in levels according to a scale or hierarchy.
not classified by level or grade

Examples

The assignments remained ungraded until the teacher had more time.
This produce is sold as ungraded fruit, so quality may vary.
The students were placed in an ungraded classroom where age was not a factor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: graded - arranged or classified in levels or according to a scale

Etymology

From un-, meaning 'not', and grade, from Latin 'gradus' (step).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɡreɪdɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡreɪdɪd / (BrE)
(of a road) not leveled or provided with drainage, and therefore unsuitable for travel throughout the year.
not leveled or drained (of a road)

Examples

The truck got stuck on the ungraded road after the heavy rain.
Travelers were warned to avoid the ungraded sections during the wet season.
An ungraded dirt path led to the remote farmhouse.

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' meaning 'not' and 'graded', meaning 'leveled' or 'sloped'.
