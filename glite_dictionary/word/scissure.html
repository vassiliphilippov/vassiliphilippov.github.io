scissure

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪʒər / (AmE), / ˈsɪʃə / (BrE)
a long, narrow cleft or fissure, especially in anatomy or geology
long, narrow cleft or fissure

Examples

The surgeon carefully avoided the scissure between the two lobes of the liver.
Deep erosion formed a large scissure in the cliff face near the shore.
The brain's central scissure separates the left and right hemispheres.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fissure - a long, narrow opening or line of breakage made by cracking or splitting

Etymology

From Latin 'scissura' meaning 'a cleft' or 'a cutting'.
