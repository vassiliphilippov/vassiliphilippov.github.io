Cetorhinus

1. PROPN

/ ˌsiːtəˈraɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌsiːtəˈrʌɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of sharks comprising only the basking shark, sometimes considered the type genus of the family Cetorhinidae.
genus of basking sharks

Examples

Scientists recently observed a Cetorhinus off the coast of Cornwall.
The genus Cetorhinus includes only a single living species, the basking shark.
Many marine biologists specialize in studying Cetorhinus and its ecological role.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basking shark - large plankton-eating shark (Cetorhinus maximus)

Etymology

From Greek 'ketos' (sea monster, whale) + 'rhinos' (nose).

2. NOUN

/ siːtəˈraɪnəs / (AmE), / siːtəˈraɪnəs / (BrE)
a genus of large sharks comprising only the basking shark species, sometimes classified as the type genus of the family Cetorhinidae.
genus of basking sharks

Examples

The genus Cetorhinus includes only the basking shark, one of the largest fish in the world.
Scientists study Cetorhinus to better understand the evolutionary history of filter-feeding sharks.
Unlike other sharks, members of Cetorhinus are gentle giants that feed on plankton.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basking shark - large plankton-eating shark species in the genus Cetorhinus

Etymology

From Greek 'ketos' (sea monster) and 'rhinos' (nose).
