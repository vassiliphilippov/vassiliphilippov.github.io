Romance

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈmæns / (AmE), / rəˈmæns / (BrE)
relating to the group of languages developed from Latin, including French, Spanish, Italian, Portuguese, and Romanian
group of languages derived from Latin

Examples

French and Italian are both Romance languages spoken across many countries.
Linguists study the similarities among Romance languages to trace their development.
Spanish, as a Romance language, shares many words with Portuguese and French.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Romance language - a language belonging to the Romance group, such as French or Italian
  • Degree Higher: Latin - the language from which Romance languages developed
  • Antonym: Germanic - another family of languages such as English and German

Etymology

From Latin *Romanicus* (Roman), originally referring to the vernacular language of the Roman Empire.

2. ADJ

/ roʊˈmæns / (AmE), / rəˈmæns / (BrE)
relating to the group of languages derived from Latin, such as French, Spanish, and Italian
relating to Latin-derived languages

Examples

Portuguese is a Romance language spoken in several countries around the world.
Linguists classify Italian as a Romance language because of its Latin origins.
The similarities between French and Spanish are due to their Romance roots.

Synset Examples

Romance languages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Romance language - a language derived from Latin, such as Spanish or French

Etymology

From Latin 'Romanicus' (Roman), referring to the language of the Romans.
