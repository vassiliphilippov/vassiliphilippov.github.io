unorganised

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈɔr.ɡə.naɪzd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈɔː.ɡə.naɪzd / (BrE)
not arranged or structured into a system or order; lacking organisation.
not structured or orderly
british

Examples

Many unorganised workplaces struggle with efficiency and productivity.
The files were left in an unorganised heap on the desk.
Several unorganised groups operate independently without any oversight.

Synset Examples

unorganized territories lack a formal government

Related Terms

  • Antonym: organized - arranged or structured into a system or order
  • Synonym: disorganized - lacking order or method; confused or chaotic

Etymology

Prefix 'un-' (not) + 'organised', chiefly British spelling of 'organized'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈɔːrɡəˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈɔːɡənaɪzd / (BrE)
(of workers) not affiliated with or belonging to a trade union; not organized into a union.
not belonging to a trade union
british

Examples

Most of the staff are unorganised and have little protection in the workplace.
The government is seeking to improve conditions for unorganised laborers across the country.
He advocates unionization in industries where workers are largely unorganised.

Synset Examples

the workers in the plant were unorganized

Related Terms

  • Antonym: organized - affiliated in a trade union
  • Synonym: unorganized - not belonging to a trade union; not organized

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'organised', chiefly British spelling of 'unorganized'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02484476-s
Synset POS: s
