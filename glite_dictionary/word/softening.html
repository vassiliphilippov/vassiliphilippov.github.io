softening

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɔf.ən.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɒf.ən.ɪŋ / (BrE)
having a soothing or emollient effect, especially making the skin softer or less irritated
soothing or emollient effect, especially on skin

Examples

This lotion has a softening effect on rough, dry skin.
Apply the softening cream daily for best results.
The soap contains softening ingredients that help soothe sensitive skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emollient - having the quality of softening or soothing the skin
  • Antonym: irritating - causing inflammation or discomfort, especially to the skin

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɑfənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɒfənɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of becoming softer or making something less hard or firm
process of becoming softer

Examples

The softening of the clay made it easier to shape on the potter's wheel.
Recent rain caused a softening of the soil in the garden.
There was a noticeable softening in his voice as he started to apologize.

Synset Examples

refrigeration delayed the softening of the fruit
he observed the softening of iron by heat

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hardening - the process of becoming harder or more firm
