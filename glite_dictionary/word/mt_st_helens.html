Mt. St. Helens

1. PROPN

/ maʊnt sənt ˈhɛlənz / (AmE), / maʊnt sənt ˈhɛlənz / (BrE)
an active volcano in the Cascade Range in southwestern Washington State, known for its catastrophic eruption in 1980.
active volcano in Washington State, USA

Examples

The eruption of Mt. St. Helens in 1980 was one of the most significant volcanic events in U.S. history.
Many scientists have studied Mt. St. Helens to better understand volcanic activity.
Tourists frequently visit the site of Mt. St. Helens to view the aftermath of the eruption.

Etymology

Named after British diplomat Alleyne FitzHerbert, Baron St Helens.
