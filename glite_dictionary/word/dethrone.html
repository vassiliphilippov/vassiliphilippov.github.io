to dethrone

1. VERB

/ dɪˈθroʊn / (AmE), / dɪˈθrəʊn / (BrE)
to remove a monarch or ruler from their position of power or authority
remove from power or authority

Examples

Rebels attempted to dethrone the unpopular king after years of unrest.
If the general succeeds, he may dethrone the president and seize power.
The queen was dethroned after accusations of corruption and abuse of power.

Synset Examples

If the King does not abdicate, he will have to be dethroned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depose - to remove from office suddenly and forcefully
  • Antonym: to enthrone - to install as a monarch or leader

Etymology

From Old French destroner, from de- (down) + throne (seat of power)
