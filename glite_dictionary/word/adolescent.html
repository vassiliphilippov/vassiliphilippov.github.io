adolescent

1. NOUN

/ ˌædəˈlɛsənt / (AmE), / ˌadəˈlɛs(ə)nt / (BrE)
a young person who is developing into an adult, typically between puberty and legal adulthood
young person between puberty and maturity

Examples

During the study, each adolescent completed a questionnaire about health behaviors.
It's common for an adolescent to seek independence from their parents.
Many schools offer counseling services to help adolescents cope with emotional and social challenges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teenager - a person aged between 13 and 19 years old
  • Antonym: adult - a fully grown, mature person
  • Antonym: child - a young human before puberty or adolescence

Etymology

From Latin 'adolescens', present participle of 'adolescere' (to grow up).
