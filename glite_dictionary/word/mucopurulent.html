mucopurulent

1. ADJ

/ ˌmjuːkoʊˈpjʊrələnt / (AmE), / ˌmjuːkjʊˈpjʊərələnt / (BrE)
(of a fluid) containing or composed of both mucus and pus.
containing mucus and pus (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient's sputum was described as mucopurulent during the examination.
A mucopurulent discharge often indicates a bacterial infection.
Doctors noted a mucopurulent fluid in the wound, suggesting a mixed infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purulent - containing or consisting of pus

Etymology

From Latin 'mucus' (mucus) and 'purulentus' (full of pus).
