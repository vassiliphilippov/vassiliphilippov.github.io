rusher

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌʃər / (AmE), / ˈrʌʃə / (BrE)
a person who rushes or acts hastily and without careful consideration
person who rushes or acts hastily

Examples

He's always a rusher in the mornings, never stopping to eat breakfast.
We need someone who is careful and thorough, not a rusher.
As a known rusher, she tends to make simple mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hurrier - a person who hurries
  • Antonym: dawdler - a person who moves slowly or wastes time

Etymology

From the verb 'rush' + agent suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrʌʃər / (AmE), / ˈrʌʃə / (BrE)
(in American football) a player who advances the ball by running with it, rather than passing or kicking.
a football player running with the ball
american

Examples

The team's leading rusher set a new record this season.
He became a successful rusher thanks to his speed and agility.
During the final play, the rusher broke through the defense for a touchdown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ball carrier - a player in possession of the ball in football

Etymology

From 'rush' + '-er', denoting a person who rushes (runs forward) in a game.

3. NOUN

/ ˈrʌʃər / (AmE), / ˈrʌʃə / (BrE)
a person who migrates quickly to a new area, especially during a gold rush or similar migration for wealth or opportunity
person who joins a gold rush or similar migration

Examples

During the California gold rush, thousands of rushers arrived from around the world.
The small town was transformed overnight as rushers flocked in search of fortune.
Some rushers struck it rich, but many left with little more than they arrived with.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prospector - someone who searches for mineral deposits, especially gold

Etymology

From 'rush' (a sudden movement of people) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
