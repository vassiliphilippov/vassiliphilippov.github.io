to redeposit

1. VERB

/ ˌriːdəˈpɑzɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːdɪˈpɒzɪt / (BrE)
to deposit something again, especially money or a cheque into a bank account
deposit again, especially in banking

Examples

After the bank returned the cheque, I had to redeposit it the next day.
You can redeposit funds into your account if the initial deposit failed.
Residents were allowed to redeposit their valuables in the new safe deposit boxes.

Synset Examples

redeposit a cheque

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deposit - to place money or an item somewhere for safekeeping, especially in a bank

Etymology

From re- (again) + deposit.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02316115-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌriː.dɪˈpɑː.zɪt / (AmE), / ˌriː.dɪˈpɒz.ɪt / (BrE)
to deposit again; to put back or lay something down in a place after it has been moved or removed
to deposit again

Examples

After the floodwaters receded, the river had redeposited silt over the farmland.
Customers can redeposit withdrawn funds into their savings accounts at any time.
Over centuries, glaciers redeposited rocks and debris across the landscape.

Synset Examples

The water had redeposited minerals on the rocks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deposit - to put or set down something in a specific place

Etymology

From re- (again) + deposit; modeled after deposit.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01578832-v
Synset POS: v
