Calliphora

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪfərə / (AmE), / kəˈlɪfərə / (BrE)
A genus of blowflies whose members are characterized by their metallic blue or green bodies, important in medical and forensic entomology.
genus of blowflies

Examples

The forensic entomologist identified the maggots as being from the genus Calliphora.
Members of Calliphora are often the first insects to arrive at decomposing remains.
Researchers studied the life cycle of Calliphora to estimate the time of death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blowfly - a common name for flies of the family Calliphoridae

Etymology

From Greek 'kalli-' meaning 'beautiful' and 'phora' meaning 'bearing'.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪfərə / (AmE), / kəˈlɪfərə / (BrE)
A genus of large blowflies, serving as the type genus of the family Calliphoridae.
genus of blowflies

Examples

Researchers collected several Calliphora specimens to study their role in decomposition.
The metallic blue color is characteristic of many Calliphora species.
Forensic entomologists often identify Calliphora larvae on corpses to estimate time of death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blowfly - a common name for flies in the family Calliphoridae

Etymology

From Greek kalliphoros, meaning 'bearing beauty' (kalli- 'beautiful' + phoros 'bearing').
