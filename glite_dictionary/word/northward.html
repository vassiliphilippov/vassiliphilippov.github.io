northward

1. ADV

/ ˈnɔrθwɚd / (AmE), / ˈnɔːθwəd / (BrE)
towards the north; in a northern direction
in a northern direction

Examples

The birds began to fly northward as spring approached.
After crossing the river, the hikers continued northward along the trail.
The explorers set out northward in search of a new route.

Synset Examples

they earn more up north
Let's go north!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: southward - in a southern direction
  • Synonym: north - toward or in the region of the north

Etymology

From Old English 'norðweard', combining 'north' and the suffix '-ward' (toward).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrθ.wɚd / (AmE), / ˈnɔːθ.wəd / (BrE)
the direction or point on the compass corresponding to 0 or 360 degrees; the direction towards the North
direction toward the North (0 or 360° compass point)

Examples

The expedition set a course due northward from the base camp.
A cold wind was blowing from the northward as the sun began to set.
Navigators used the northward as their primary bearing during the early voyage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: north - the direction corresponding to 0 or 360 degrees on a compass
  • Antonym: southward - direction toward the South (180° compass point)

Etymology

From Middle English northward, from Old English norðweard (toward the north).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13853375-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈnɔrθwərd / (AmE), / ˈnɔːθwəd / (BrE)
moving or directed toward the north
moving or directed toward the north

Examples

The hikers continued their northward journey through the mountain pass.
Spring brings a northward migration of birds seeking breeding grounds.
The storm system is expected to shift northward overnight, bringing rain to the region.

Synset Examples

the northbound lane
the northward flow of traffic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: southward - moving or directed toward the south
  • Synonym: northerly - in a northern direction or position

Etymology

From Middle English, combining 'north' + '-ward' (direction suffix).
