conferral

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfɝː.əl / (AmE), / kənˈfɜː.rəl / (BrE)
the act of formally granting an honor, title, or gift to someone
formal granting of an honor or gift

Examples

The conferral of the honorary degree was the highlight of the ceremony.
After the conferral of the award, she gave a heartfelt speech.
The university delayed the conferral of diplomas due to unforeseen circumstances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: award - a prize or honor given to someone
  • Antonym: withdrawal - the act of removing or taking back something granted

Etymology

From confer (to grant), based on Latin conferrre (to bring together).
