enforced

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfɔrst / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɔːst / (BrE)
(of a situation, action, or state) imposed on someone by force or necessity; compelled rather than chosen.
imposed or compelled by force

Examples

Her injury led to a period of enforced rest at home.
The country faced an enforced curfew after the riots.
His resignation was not voluntary, but enforced by company policy.

Synset Examples

a life of enforced inactivity
enforced obedience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compulsory - required by law or a rule; obligatory
  • Antonym: voluntary - done, given, or acting of one's own free will

Etymology

From the verb 'enforce', derived from Old French 'enforcier', from Latin 'in-' (in) + 'fortis' (strong).
