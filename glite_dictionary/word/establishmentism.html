establishmentism

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈstæblɪʃməntˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˈstæblɪʃm(ə)ntˌɪzəm / (BrE)
The doctrine or belief in supporting the social or political establishment.
belief in supporting the establishment

Examples

The party's policies reflect a strong adherence to establishmentism and traditional power structures.
Critics accused the editorial board of promoting establishmentism at the expense of reform.
During the 1960s, there was widespread rejection of establishmentism among younger generations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anti-establishment - opposition to the established authority or order

Etymology

From establishment + -ism, modeled after similar doctrinal nouns in English.
