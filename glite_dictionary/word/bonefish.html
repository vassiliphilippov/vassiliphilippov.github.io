bonefish

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊnˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbəʊnˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a slender, silvery tropical marine fish found in mud flats and mangrove lagoons, prized by anglers for sport fishing
a slender tropical marine fish

Examples

Many anglers travel to the Bahamas in search of the elusive bonefish.
The bonefish is known for its remarkable speed and fighting ability when hooked.
On our trip, we waded through shallow lagoons hoping to spot a bonefish tailing in the clear water.
