dorsal

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɔːr.səl / (AmE), / ˈdɔː.səl / (BrE)
relating to or situated on the back or upper side of an organ or organism.
of or on the back or upper side

Examples

The shark's dorsal fin was clearly visible above the water's surface.
In many animals, the dorsal side is darker than the ventral side for camouflage.
The examiner noted a bruise on the dorsal aspect of the patient's hand.

Synset Examples

the abaxial surface of a leaf is the underside or side facing away from the stem

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ventral - relating to the underside or belly of an organism; opposite of dorsal

Etymology

From Latin dorsalis, from dorsum (back).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɔr.səl / (AmE), / ˈdɔː.səl / (BrE)
Relating to or situated on the back or upper side of an animal or organ.
of or on the back or upper side

Examples

The shark's dorsal fin could be seen cutting through the water.
Damage to the dorsal side of the leaf was visible after the storm.
Many fish have a prominent dorsal stripe that runs along their backs.

Synset Examples

the dorsal fin is the vertical fin on the back of a fish and certain marine mammals

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ventral - relating to or on the underside or front of an animal or organ

Etymology

From Latin 'dorsalis', from 'dorsum' (back).
