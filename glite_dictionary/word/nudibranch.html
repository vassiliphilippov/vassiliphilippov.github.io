nudibranch

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːdiˌbræŋk / (AmE), / ˈnjuːdɪˌbræŋk / (BrE)
A soft-bodied, shell-less marine mollusk of the suborder Nudibranchia, often brightly colored and found in oceans worldwide.
marine mollusk of suborder Nudibranchia

Examples

The coral reef is home to many colorful nudibranchs that attract underwater photographers.
A rare species of nudibranch was spotted by divers last weekend near the coast.
Scientists study nudibranch behavior to understand their unique chemical defenses.

Etymology

From Latin 'nudus' (naked) + Greek 'bránkhia' (gills).
