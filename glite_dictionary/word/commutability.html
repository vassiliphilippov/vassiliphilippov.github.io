commutability

1. NOUN

/ kəˌmjuːtəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / kəˌmjuːtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being capable of being exchanged or replaced with something else
quality of being exchangeable or replaceable

Examples

The commutability of parts allows for efficient repairs in modern machinery.
In scientific calibration, the commutability of reference materials is crucial for accurate results.
The contract specifies that there must be commutability between the two job roles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interchangeability - the ability to be substituted or exchanged without distinction
  • Synonym: replaceability - the capacity to be replaced by another of the same kind

Etymology

From Latin commutabilis (changeable), from commutare (to change).

2. NOUN

/ kəˌmjuːtəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / kəˌmjuːtəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being commutable; the ability to be exchanged, substituted, or converted.
quality of being commutable

Examples

The commutability of certain retirement benefits makes them attractive to employees.
Researchers examined the commutability of reference materials in different testing processes.
In law, the commutability of sentences can affect parole eligibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commutable - able to be exchanged, substituted, or converted; able to have a penalty substituted

Etymology

From Latin commutare (to change), via French commutabilité.
