vizor

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈvʌɪzə / (BrE)
a piece of armor with openings for the eyes, attached to a medieval helmet to protect the face
face-protecting part of a medieval helmet

Examples

The knight lowered his vizor before entering the tournament arena.
Through the narrow eye slits of his vizor, he watched the battlefield unfold.
Her costume included a replica vizor modeled after those worn by medieval soldiers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: visor - face guard of a helmet, especially in medieval armor

Etymology

From Old French *visiere*, related to 'vis' (face).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪ.zər / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ.zə / (BrE)
A projecting brim or shield on a cap, helmet, or similar headwear designed to shade or protect the eyes.
projecting brim to shade the eyes

Examples

He lowered the vizor of his cap as the sun grew stronger.
The football player's helmet included a tinted vizor for extra eye protection.
My old cycling hat had a small vizor that helped block the glare.

Synset Examples

he pulled down the bill of his cap and trudged ahead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: visor - projecting front part on a cap or helmet to protect the eyes

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'visor', from Anglo-French *viser*, from Latin *visus* (sight).

Source: WordNet
