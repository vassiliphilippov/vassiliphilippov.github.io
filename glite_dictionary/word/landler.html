landler

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændlər / (AmE), / ˈlændlə / (BrE)
a traditional Austrian folk dance in triple time, or the music composed for it.
traditional Austrian dance or its music

Examples

The orchestra played a lively landler that filled the hall with energy.
At the village festival, couples performed the traditional landler in colorful costumes.
We learned to dance the landler as part of our music appreciation class.

Etymology

From German 'Ländler', meaning 'country dance', from 'Land' (country).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlændlər / (AmE), / ˈlændlə / (BrE)
a moderately slow Austrian folk dance in triple time, featuring spinning and clapping movements
Austrian folk dance in triple time

Examples

The traditional costumes added to the charm of the landler that the villagers performed.
During the festival, couples joyfully spun and clapped to the rhythmic steps of the landler.
He learned to dance the landler while traveling through Austria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waltz - a ballroom dance in triple time, popular in Austria and Europe

Etymology

From German 'Ländler', meaning 'inhabitant of the countryside' or 'country dance'.
