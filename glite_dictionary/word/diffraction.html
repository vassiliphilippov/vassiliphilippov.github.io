diffraction

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfrækʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈfrækʃən / (BrE)
the bending and spreading of waves, especially light or sound, when they encounter an obstacle or pass through a narrow opening, often resulting in patterns of light and dark bands
bending and spreading of waves around obstacles

Examples

When light passes through a narrow slit, diffraction causes it to spread and form patterns.
Scientists observed the diffraction of sound waves around the corners of the building.
The rainbow-like colors seen in a CD are due to diffraction of light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interference - the combination of two or more waveforms to form a resultant wave

Etymology

From Latin 'diffringere' (to break apart), via French 'diffraction'.
