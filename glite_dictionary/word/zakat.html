zakat

1. NOUN

/ zɑːˈkɑːt / (AmE), / zəˈkɑːt / (BrE)
in Islam, a mandatory charitable giving and the fourth pillar of the faith, involving the donation of a portion of one's wealth to those in need
mandatory charitable giving in Islam

Examples

As part of her religious duty, she gives zakat every year to help those in need.
Muslims are required to pay zakat as one of the five pillars of Islam.
The local mosque organized a zakat collection during Ramadan.

Synset Examples

the zakat is earmarked for the poor and disabled

Etymology

From Arabic *zakāh* (almsgiving, purification).
