oleophilic

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊliəˈfɪlɪk / (AmE), / ˌəʊliəˈfɪlɪk / (BrE)
Having a strong affinity for oils rather than water; tending to absorb or combine readily with oils.
having an affinity for oils

Examples

The oleophilic coating on the fabric allows it to absorb oil spills effectively.
Materials with oleophilic properties are often used in environmental cleanup.
An oleophilic membrane can selectively separate oil from water in filtration systems.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hydrophilic - having a strong affinity for water rather than oil
  • Antonym: oleophobic - repelling or having little affinity for oils

Etymology

From Greek 'oleo-' (oil) + '-philic' (loving).
