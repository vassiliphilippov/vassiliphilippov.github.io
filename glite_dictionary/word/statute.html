statute

1. NOUN

/ ˈstætʃuːt / (AmE), / ˈstatjuːt / (BrE)
a written law passed by a legislative body
written law enacted by a legislature
legal

Examples

The company must comply with every statute related to employee safety.
A new statute was enacted to regulate online commerce.
Violating a statute can result in heavy fines and other penalties.

Synset Examples

statute law
codified written laws

Related Terms

  • Synonym: law - the system of rules recognized by a country or community

Etymology

From Latin 'statutum' (decree, ordinance), from 'statut-', past participle of 'statuere' (to set up).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstætʃuːt / (AmE), / ˈstatjuːt / (BrE)
A written law formally enacted by a legislative body.
law enacted by a legislature
legal

Examples

The new statute requires all businesses to recycle their waste materials.
Under the current statute, you must be at least eighteen to vote.
The city council met to discuss amendments to the existing statute on noise control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: law - system of rules recognized by a country or community
  • Synonym: regulation - a rule made and maintained by an authority
  • Synonym: ordinance - an authoritative order or decree, especially by a municipality

Etymology

From Latin 'statutum' (law, ordinance), from 'statut-', past participle of 'statuere' (to set up).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06576378-n
Synset POS: n
