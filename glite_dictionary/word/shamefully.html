shamefully

1. ADV

/ ˈʃeɪmfəli / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪmfəli / (BrE)
in a way that causes shame or dishonor; in a manner deserving of shame
in a shameful or disgraceful way

Examples

She was shamefully treated by her colleagues after the mistake.
The politician acted shamefully to cover up the scandal.
He shamefully neglected his responsibilities at work.

Synset Examples

his grades were disgracefully low

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgracefully - in a way that causes public shame or embarrassment
  • Antonym: honorably - in a manner showing honor or integrity

Etymology

From 'shameful' + '-ly', forming the adverb from the adjective 'shameful'.
