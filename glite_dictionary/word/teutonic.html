Teutonic

1. ADJ

/ tuˈtɑnɪk / (AmE), / tjuːˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to the ancient Teutons or their languages; sometimes more generally relating to the Germanic peoples or languages.
of the ancient Teutons or their languages

Examples

The museum had a fascinating exhibit on Teutonic tribes and their migrations.
Some scholars argue that English has many Teutonic roots due to its Germanic origins.
The concept of honor played a significant role in ancient Teutonic societies.

Synset Examples

Teutonic peoples such as Germans and Scandinavians and British
Germanic mythology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Germanic - relating to the Germanic peoples or languages

Etymology

From Latin Teutonicus (of the Teutons), from Teutones, the name of a Germanic tribe.
