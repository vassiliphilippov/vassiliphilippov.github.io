frontier

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrʌn.tɪr / (AmE), / ˈfrʌn.tɪə / (BrE)
an area of knowledge or activity at the limits of what is known or achieved, especially one that invites exploration, research, or development
the leading edge of knowledge or achievement

Examples

She is conducting experiments at the frontier of genetic engineering.
Renewable energy is a frontier that attracts cutting-edge research.
Scientists are constantly pushing the frontier of medical knowledge.

Synset Examples

he worked at the frontier of brain science

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boundary - a limit or edge that defines the extent of something

Etymology

From Old French *frontiere* (front line or boundary), from Latin *frons* (forehead, front).

2. NOUN

/ frʌnˈtɪr / (AmE), / ˈfrʌn.tɪə / (BrE)
an area of land at the edge of a settled region, especially where it borders uninhabited wilderness or is being explored or developed
wilderness at country’s settled edge

Examples

The settlers ventured into the frontier in search of new opportunities and land.
During the 19th century, the American frontier moved steadily westward.
Life on the frontier was often harsh and unpredictable, demanding resilience from its inhabitants.

Synset Examples

the individualism of the frontier in Andrew Jackson's day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: border - a line separating two political or geographical areas
  • Degree Lower: hinterland - the remote or less developed parts of a country

Etymology

From Old French *frontiere* (front), ultimately from Latin *frons* (forehead, front).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ frʌnˈtɪr / (AmE), / frʌnˈtɪə / (BrE)
an international boundary or the land immediately inside such a boundary, often considered as a border area between countries, sometimes fortified
the border or boundary area between countries

Examples

The military was stationed along the frontier to protect the nation from invasion.
Trade between the two countries was heavily regulated near the frontier.
After years of dispute, the governments finally agreed to demarcate the frontier more clearly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: border - the line separating two countries, regions, or areas
  • Synonym: boundary - a line which marks the limits of an area

Etymology

From Old French 'frontiere' (frontier, boundary), from 'front' (forehead, front)
