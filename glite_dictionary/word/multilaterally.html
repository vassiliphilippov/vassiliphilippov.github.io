multilaterally

1. ADV

/ ˌmʌl.tɪˈlæt̬.ɚ.li / (AmE), / ˌmʌl.tɪˈlæt.ər.əli / (BrE)
In a manner involving or affecting several parties, governments, or groups, especially in international relations.
involving several parties or groups

Examples

The treaty was negotiated multilaterally to ensure fair representation for all nations involved.
The countries agreed to resolve the issue multilaterally, rather than through separate bilateral talks.
Policies developed multilaterally are often more comprehensive and widely accepted.

Synset Examples

they worked out an agreement multilaterally

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bilaterally - in a manner involving two parties or groups

Etymology

Formed from 'multilateral' + '-ly'; 'multilateral' from Latin 'multus' (many) + 'latus' (side).
