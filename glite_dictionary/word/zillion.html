zillion

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɪljən / (AmE), / ˈzɪlɪən / (BrE)
an extremely large but indefinite number, used chiefly for emphasis
a very large indefinite number (hyperbolic)
informal

Examples

I must have told you a zillion times not to leave your keys there.
She has a zillion things to do before the wedding.
There are a zillion stars visible in the night sky from here.

Synset Examples

there were millions of flies

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: million - A very large number; one thousand thousand
  • Degree Lower: billion - A thousand million (in the US and modern British usage)

Etymology

Modeled on words like million and billion; formed in the 20th century as a humorous exaggeration.

2. NOUN

/ ˈzɪljən / (AmE), / ˈzɪljən / (BrE)
an extremely large but unspecified number; an indefinitely large quantity
very large indeterminate number
informal

Examples

There are a zillion reasons to visit Paris in the spring.
She gave me a zillion excuses for being late to the meeting.
It feels like I've answered this question a zillion times already.

Synset Examples

a zillion people were there

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gazillion - an extremely large, indefinite number (informal synonym)
  • Degree Lower: million - a number equal to one thousand thousand (1,000,000)
  • Synonym: bazillion - an extremely large, indefinite number (informal synonym)

Etymology

Formed in English (20th century) as an extension of words like 'million' and 'billion' with an arbitrary initial letter.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02207588-s
Synset POS: s
