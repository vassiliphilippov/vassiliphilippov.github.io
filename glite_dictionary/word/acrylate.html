acrylate

1. NOUN

/ əˈkrɪleɪt / (AmE), / əˈkrɪleɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester derived from propenoic acid (acrylic acid)
salt or ester of acrylic acid
medical

Examples

Many plastics are formed from acrylates due to their durability and clarity.
The chemist synthesized a new acrylate compound for testing as an adhesive.
Exposure to certain acrylates can cause skin irritation in some people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acrylic - a synthetic resin or plastic; or relating to acrylic acid

Etymology

From acrylic + -ate, referring to the salt or ester of acrylic acid.
