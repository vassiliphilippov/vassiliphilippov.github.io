chamarre

1. NOUN

/ ʃəˈmɑr / (AmE), / ʃəˈmɑː / (BrE)
A fur-lined coat decorated with braid, worn in the sixteenth century.
sixteenth-century fur-lined coat
archaic

Examples

The nobleman wore a richly embroidered chamarre to the royal court.
Paintings from the Renaissance often depict men dressed in a chamarre.
Her costume included a replica of a sixteenth-century chamarre for authenticity.

Etymology

From French 'chamarre' (a decorated cloak or mantle).
