Coscoroba

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːskəˈroʊbə / (AmE), / ˌkɒskəˈrəʊbə / (BrE)
A genus of waterfowl in the family Anatidae, containing only the Coscoroba swan native to southern South America.
genus of waterfowl containing the Coscoroba swan

Examples

The Coscoroba is unique as the only species in its genus.
Among South American waterfowl, the Coscoroba stands out for its swan-like appearance.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of the Coscoroba in Argentina.

Etymology

From Mapudungun (an indigenous language of southern South America), possibly imitative of the bird's call.
