coscoroba

1. NOUN

/ kɑːskəˈroʊbə / (AmE), / kɒskəˈrəʊbə / (BrE)
a large white waterbird native to South America, intermediate in appearance and behavior between ducks and swans
large white South American waterbird

Examples

The coscoroba glided across the calm waters of the Patagonian lake.
Unlike swans, the coscoroba has a shorter neck and a more duck-like bill.
Birdwatchers were delighted to spot a pair of nesting coscorobas during their South American tour.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: swan - large graceful waterbird with a long neck
  • Degree Lower: duck - smaller waterbird with a broad bill and short legs

Etymology

From Spanish 'coscoroba', the local name for the bird in Argentina.
