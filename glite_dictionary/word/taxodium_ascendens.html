Taxodium ascendens

1. NOUN

/ tækˈsoʊdiəm əˈsɛndənz / (AmE), / tækˈsəʊdiəm əˈsendənz / (BrE)
A deciduous coniferous tree of the cypress family, native to the southeastern United States, commonly known as the pond cypress.
deciduous conifer; pond cypress species

Examples

The wetland restoration project included planting several Taxodium ascendens saplings along the pond’s edge.
Unlike the bald cypress, Taxodium ascendens typically grows in still, shallow waters.
Scientists studied the growth rings of Taxodium ascendens to understand regional climate changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pond cypress - common name for Taxodium ascendens
  • Degree Lower: bald cypress - related tree species, Taxodium distichum

Etymology

From Latin; 'Taxodium' from 'taxus' (yew) and '-odium' (resemblance); 'ascendens' meaning ascending or rising.
