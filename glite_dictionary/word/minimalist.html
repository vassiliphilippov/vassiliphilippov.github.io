minimalist

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnɪməlɪst / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪməlɪst / (BrE)
a person who favors only minor or minimal changes, especially in politics or government.
person favoring minimal changes

Examples

He is a minimalist when it comes to government spending, resisting any major policy shifts.
Some party members accused the minimalists of blocking real progress on urgent reforms.
As a political minimalist, she prefers tinkering with the current system rather than overhauling it entirely.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: radical - someone who advocates thorough or complete political or social reform

Etymology

From 'minimal', based on Latin *minimus* (smallest), with '-ist' forming a noun meaning adherent or advocate.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɪnəməlɪst / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪməlɪst / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by minimalism, especially in art, design, or music, involving simplicity and spareness of form or concept
relating to artistic minimalism

Examples

The minimalist style in her paintings is marked by clean lines and simple shapes.
Many modern homes now feature minimalist interiors with neutral colors and little furniture.
His minimalist approach to music strips compositions to their bare essentials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minimal - very small in amount; least possible

Etymology

From minimalism, derived from Latin minimus (smallest)

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnəməlɪst / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪməlɪst / (BrE)
a person who practices or advocates minimalism, especially in art, design, or lifestyle
advocate of minimalism

Examples

The gallery features works by a leading minimalist known for her simple geometric forms.
As a minimalist, he prefers clean spaces with few possessions.
The minimalist argued that less is more in both art and life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: maximalist - a person who holds the opposite approach, favoring complexity or excess
  • Synonym: minimalism - an artistic or lifestyle movement emphasizing simplicity

Etymology

From 'minimal', influenced by the movement Minimalism in art and design, plus '-ist' (a person who practices or is concerned with something).

4. ADJ

/ ˈmɪnɪməlɪst / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪməlɪst / (BrE)
Favoring or advocating only minimal reforms, especially in government or politics.
favoring minimal reforms (politics)

Examples

The party's minimalist approach to reform disappointed many of its progressive supporters.
Some leaders adopted a minimalist strategy, resisting major changes to the constitution.
Critics argue that a minimalist policy will not address the root causes of the problem.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: radical - advocating thorough or complete political or social reform

Etymology

From 'minimal', patterned after similar -ist derivatives; first used in political and artistic contexts in the mid-20th century.
