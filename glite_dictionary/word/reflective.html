reflective

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈflɛktɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛktɪv / (BrE)
Able to reflect light, sound, or heat; having the property of sending back energy, such as light or sound, from a surface.
able to reflect light or sound

Examples

Most road signs are made from a reflective material so drivers can see them at night.
The scientist examined the reflective properties of the new coating.
Her sunglasses had highly reflective lenses that mirrored the sunlight.

Synset Examples

a reflective surface

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonreflective - not capable of reflecting light, sound, or heat
  • Synonym: mirror-like - having a surface that reflects an image clearly, like a mirror

Etymology

From Latin reflectere (to bend back).

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈflɛktɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛktɪv / (BrE)
Thinking deeply or seriously about something; showing careful thought.
deeply thoughtful; contemplative

Examples

After the meeting, she became reflective, considering how her actions had affected the team.
His reflective nature made him an excellent writer of personal essays.
During long walks, Tom often grew reflective and pondered the direction of his life.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contemplative - given to deep thought or contemplation
  • Synonym: thoughtful - absorbed in or involving thought
  • Antonym: superficial - lacking depth of thought or feeling

Etymology

From Latin reflectere (to bend back), via reflection + -ive.
