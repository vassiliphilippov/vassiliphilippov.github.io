causalgia

1. NOUN

/ kɔˈzældʒiə / (AmE), / kɔːˈzældʒɪə / (BrE)
A persistent, severe burning pain in a limb, usually following nerve injury, often accompanied by skin changes.
burning nerve pain in a limb
medical

Examples

The patient developed causalgia in his right leg after the surgery.
Doctors treated her persistent causalgia with physical therapy and medication.
Symptoms of causalgia often include redness, swelling, and burning pain in the affected area.

Etymology

From Greek 'kausis' (burning) + 'algos' (pain).
