Presbytes

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛz.baɪts / (AmE), / ˈprɛz.baɪts / (BrE)
A genus of langurs, a group of Old World monkeys found in Asia.
genus of Asian langurs (monkeys)

Examples

The Presbytes genus includes several species of leaf-eating langurs native to Southeast Asia.
Researchers observed a group of Presbytes in the dense forests of Borneo.
Unlike macaques, members of Presbytes have elongated limbs and tails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: langur - a long-tailed arboreal Asian monkey

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek 'presbys' (old man), referring to the aged appearance of some monkeys.
