sarcodine

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːrkəˌdaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɑːkədʌɪn / (BrE)
any protozoan that moves and feeds by producing temporary projections of its body called pseudopods, especially those previously classified in the group Sarcodina.
protozoan moving and feeding with pseudopods
medical

Examples

Amoebas are common examples of sarcodine protozoa found in freshwater environments.
Under the microscope, the sarcodine extended its pseudopods to engulf a smaller organism.
Biologists often study sarcodine movement to understand cellular dynamics.

Etymology

From New Latin Sarcodina, derived from Greek 'sarx' (flesh) and 'eidos' (form).
