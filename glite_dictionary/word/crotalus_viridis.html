Crotalus viridis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊ.tələs ˈvɪr.ɪ.dɪs / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊ.tæl.əs ˈvɪr.ɪ.dɪs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the prairie rattlesnake, a venomous species found widely between the Mississippi River and the Rocky Mountains
a species of prairie rattlesnake

Examples

The Crotalus viridis is commonly found in grasslands and prairies across the central United States.
Hikers should be cautious of the Crotalus viridis while exploring areas between the Mississippi and the Rockies.
Researchers tagged several Crotalus viridis specimens to track their migration patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prairie rattlesnake - a common name for Crotalus viridis

Etymology

From Latin: 'Crotalus' (rattle, a genus of rattlesnakes) and 'viridis' (green).
