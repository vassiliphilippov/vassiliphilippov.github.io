morbific

1. ADJ

/ mɔːrˈbɪfɪk / (AmE), / mɔːˈbɪfɪk / (BrE)
(of an agent or substance) capable of causing disease; pathogenic.
causing disease; pathogenic
medical

Examples

The presence of morbific agents in the water supply led to a public health scare.
Researchers identified several morbific bacteria responsible for the outbreak.
Doctors must isolate any morbific substances to prevent the spread of infection.

Synset Examples

infective agents
pathogenic bacteria

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pathogenic - capable of causing disease (especially microorganisms)

Etymology

From Latin morbificus, from morbus (disease) + -ficus (making).
