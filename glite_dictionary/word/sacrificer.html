sacrificer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsækrɪˌfaɪsər / (AmE), / ˈsækrɪˌfaɪsə / (BrE)
a person who offers a sacrifice, especially as part of a religious ritual
person who offers a sacrifice

Examples

In ancient times, the sacrificer would make offerings to the gods to ensure a good harvest.
The role of the sacrificer was considered both honorable and perilous in many cultures.
During the ceremony, the appointed sacrificer recited prayers before offering the gift.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: priest - a person authorized to perform religious rites

Etymology

From Middle English, from sacrifice + -er (agent suffix).
