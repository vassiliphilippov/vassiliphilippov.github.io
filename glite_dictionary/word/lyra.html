Lyra

1. PROPN

/ ˈlaɪərə / (AmE), / ˈlaɪərə / (BrE)
a small constellation in the Northern Hemisphere, near Cygnus and Draco, containing the bright star Vega.
constellation in northern sky (with Vega)

Examples

The constellation Lyra is easy to spot in the summer night sky due to the bright star Vega.
Amateur astronomers enjoy looking for Lyra when mapping the northern hemisphere's stars.
My astronomy class traced the outline of Lyra with a laser pointer during our night observation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vega - the brightest star in the constellation Lyra

Etymology

From Latin 'lyra' meaning 'lyre', reflecting the constellation's shape resembling the musical instrument.
