Pterocnemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrəˈknimiə / (AmE), / ˌtɛərəˈkniːmiə / (BrE)
A genus of large, flightless birds in the family Rheidae, native to South America; includes Darwin's rhea.
genus of South American flightless birds

Examples

The Pterocnemia genus includes the lesser rhea, a well-known South American bird.
Scientists debated whether to classify the species under Pterocnemia or Rhea.
Recent studies have revised the taxonomy of Pterocnemia based on genetic evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rhea - genus of large, flightless South American birds

Etymology

From Greek pteron (wing) + knēmē (shin, leg), referring to the bird's feathered legs.
