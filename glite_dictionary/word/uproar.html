uproar

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌrɔr / (AmE), / ˈʌprɔː / (BrE)
a loud and confused noise, typically made by a group of people or things
loud confused noise or commotion

Examples

There was an uproar in the theater when the fire alarm sounded.
The decision to raise taxes caused a public uproar.
Children's laughter and shouting filled the playground with uproar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commotion - noisy disturbance
  • Antonym: silence - complete absence of sound

Etymology

From Middle Dutch oproer, from op (up) + roer (stir).

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌrɔr / (AmE), / ˈʌprɔː / (BrE)
a state of noisy and confused activity or excitement
noisy confusion or commotion

Examples

The controversial announcement caused an immediate uproar among the students.
There was an uproar in the audience when the results were announced.
The meeting descended into uproar as tempers flared over the new policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commotion - a state of confused and noisy disturbance
  • Antonym: calm - a state of peace and absence of noise or disturbance

Etymology

From Dutch 'oproer' (tumult, riot), from 'op-' (up) + 'roer' (stirring, motion).
