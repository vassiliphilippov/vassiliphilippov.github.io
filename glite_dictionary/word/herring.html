herring

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɛrɪŋ / (BrE)
the flesh of a small, fatty fish from the northern Atlantic or Pacific, often eaten smoked, salted, or pickled
edible flesh of a small fish

Examples

In Scandinavia, pickled herring is a traditional dish served during holidays.
She bought smoked herring at the market for dinner.
The appetizer included slices of salted herring with onions and bread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sardine - small, edible fish often canned or smoked

Etymology

From Old English *hæring*; related to Dutch *haring* and German *Hering*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɛrɪŋ / (BrE)
a small silver-colored fish found in large schools in northern Atlantic and Pacific waters, widely used as food.
a small, silvery edible fish from the northern seas

Examples

Fishermen caught a large net full of herring off the Norwegian coast.
Smoked herring is a traditional delicacy in many northern European countries.
The herring migrate in vast schools during the spring and autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sardine - a small, oily fish, often canned, closely related to the herring
  • Synonym: anchovy - a small, schooling saltwater fish used as food and in flavorings

Etymology

From Old English *hæring*, related to Dutch *haring* and German *Hering*, all meaning the fish.
