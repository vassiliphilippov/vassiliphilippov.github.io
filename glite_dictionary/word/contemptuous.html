contemptuous

1. ADJ

/ kənˈtɛmptʃuəs / (AmE), / kənˈtɛmptjʊəs / (BrE)
showing or expressing deep dislike or disrespect; feeling or demonstrating contempt.
showing contempt or disdain

Examples

She gave him a contemptuous glance and walked away without saying a word.
His contemptuous attitude made it clear he didn't value their opinions.
The critic wrote a contemptuous review, dismissing the performance as amateurish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scornful - showing scorn or contempt
  • Antonym: respectful - showing respect or deference

Etymology

From Latin 'contemptuosus' (full of scorn or disdain).
