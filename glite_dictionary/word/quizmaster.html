quizmaster

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪzˌmæstɚ / (AmE), / ˈkwɪzˌmɑːstə / (BrE)
the person who asks the questions and manages a quiz show, especially on radio or television
host of a quiz show

Examples

The quizmaster smiled as he read out the final question to the contestants.
Our school hired a professional quizmaster for the annual trivia night.
She dreamed of becoming a famous quizmaster on national television.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: host - person who presents or introduces a television or radio program

Etymology

Formed from 'quiz' (test of knowledge) and 'master' (person in charge), first attested in early 20th century.
