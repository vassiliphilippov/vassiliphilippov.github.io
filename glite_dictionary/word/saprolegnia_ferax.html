Saprolegnia ferax

1. NOUN

/ sæpɹoʊˈlɛɡniə ˈfɛɹæks / (AmE), / sæprəˈlɛɡniə ˈfɛəraks / (BrE)
a species of aquatic fungus that infects fish, tadpoles, and their eggs, causing a white cotton-like growth on their bodies, especially on fins and other peripheral parts
aquatic fungus infecting fish and tadpoles
medical

Examples

The biologist identified Saprolegnia ferax as the cause of the cottony growth on the fish.
Ponds with high organic content may encourage outbreaks of Saprolegnia ferax.
The white hyphae of Saprolegnia ferax were visible on the fins of the infected tadpoles.

Etymology

From modern Latin Saprolegnia (genus name), from Greek sapros (rotten) + legnon (wood) + species epithet ferax (Latin, fertile).
