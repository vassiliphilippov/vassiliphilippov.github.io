secretase

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈkrɪteɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈkriːteɪz / (BrE)
any of a group of enzymes that cleave certain proteins, especially in the formation of amyloid plaques associated with Alzheimer's disease
enzyme involved in amyloid protein cleavage
medical

Examples

The discovery of secretase inhibitors has opened new avenues for Alzheimer's treatment.
Researchers are studying how secretase activity leads to the formation of amyloid plaques.
Blocking the action of secretase enzymes could slow the progression of neurodegenerative diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enzyme - a protein that speeds up chemical reactions in living things
  • Degree Lower: protease - an enzyme that breaks down proteins and peptides

Etymology

Formed from 'secrete' (to produce and release) and the enzyme suffix '-ase'.
