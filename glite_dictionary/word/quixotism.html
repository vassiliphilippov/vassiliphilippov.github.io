quixotism

1. NOUN

/ kwɪkˈsɑːtɪzəm / (AmE), / kwɪkˈsɒtɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The act or practice of pursuing lofty, romantic, or impractical ideals in a way that is unrealistic or impractical.
impractical idealism; romantic folly

Examples

Her quixotism led her to start projects that were noble but impossible to finish.
The company's decision to donate all its profits to charity was dismissed as quixotism by its competitors.
He was admired for his quixotism, though few believed his dreams would ever come true.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idealism - the belief in or pursuit of noble ideals, often impractical
  • Antonym: realism - the attitude or practice of accepting a situation as it is and being prepared to deal with it accordingly

Etymology

From Don Quixote, the protagonist of the novel by Cervantes, whose actions exemplified impractical idealism.
