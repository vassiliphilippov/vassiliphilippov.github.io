phylogenetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌfaɪloʊdʒəˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌfʌɪlədʒɪˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to the evolutionary history or development of a species or group of organisms.
relating to evolutionary development

Examples

Comparing phylogenetic trees helps scientists understand evolutionary relationships among species.
Recent research reveals the phylogenetic similarities between birds and dinosaurs.
The study focused on the phylogenetic development of flowering plants.

Synset Examples

phylogenetic development

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phylogenesis - the evolutionary development and history of a species
  • Antonym: ontogenetic - relating to the development of an individual organism

Etymology

From Greek phylon (race, tribe) + genesis (origin, birth).
