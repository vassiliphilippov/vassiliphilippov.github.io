spermatocele

1. NOUN

/ spɚˈmætəˌsiːl / (AmE), / spəˈmatəsiːl / (BrE)
a cystic swelling on the epididymis or testis, typically containing sperm
cyst on the epididymis, often with sperm
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the painless lump as a spermatocele after a physical examination.
A large spermatocele may sometimes cause discomfort and require medical intervention.
Ultrasound imaging confirmed the presence of a spermatocele in the patient's scrotum.

Etymology

From Greek sperma (seed, sperm) + kēlē (swelling, hernia).
