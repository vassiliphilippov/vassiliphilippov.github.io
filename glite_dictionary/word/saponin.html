saponin

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæpənɪn / (AmE), / ˈsapənɪn / (BrE)
any of a group of plant-derived compounds that produce a soap-like foam in water and are used in detergents, foaming agents, and emulsifiers
soapy plant compound used in detergents

Examples

Certain plants contain high levels of saponin, which can create a foamy lather in water.
Researchers are studying the potential health benefits of dietary saponin.
The detergent uses natural saponin as a mild, eco-friendly ingredient.

Etymology

From Latin 'sapo' (soap) + '-in' (chemical suffix).
