context

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.tɛkst / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.tɛkst / (BrE)
The circumstances, facts, or conditions that surround and help to explain a situation, event, or statement.
the surrounding circumstances of an event

Examples

It is important to understand the political context before making decisions.
Without proper context, her comments might be misunderstood.
The meaning of a word often changes depending on its context.

Synset Examples

the historical context

Related Terms

  • Synonym: background - the circumstances or situation prevailing at a particular time or underlying a particular event
  • Synonym: situation - a set of circumstances in which one finds oneself; a state of affairs

Etymology

From Latin contextus (a joining together), from contexere (to weave together).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.tɛkst / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.tɛkst / (BrE)
the words or passages that come before and after a particular word or phrase and help to explain its meaning
surrounding words shaping meaning

Examples

You need to understand the context of the sentence to interpret the word correctly.
Without context, her remarks could easily be misunderstood.
The meaning of a phrase often changes depending on its context.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: situation - the circumstances in which something occurs

Etymology

From Latin 'contextus' (a joining together, context), from 'con-' (together) + 'texere' (to weave).

Source: WordNet
