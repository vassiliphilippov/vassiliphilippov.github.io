minatory

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɪnəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈmɪnətəri / (BrE)
expressing or conveying a threat; menacing or threatening
expressing a threat

Examples

The judge delivered a minatory warning to the repeat offender.
Dark, minatory clouds gathered on the horizon, hinting at an approaching storm.
Her letter had a distinctly minatory tone that unsettled the recipient.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reassuring - removing or dispelling doubts and fears

Etymology

From Latin minatorius (threatening), from minari (to threaten).
