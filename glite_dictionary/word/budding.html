budding

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʌdɪŋ / (BrE)
A form of asexual reproduction in which a new organism develops from an outgrowth or bud on the parent, common in yeasts and some other unicellular organisms.
asexual reproduction by outgrowth

Examples

Yeast cells reproduce by budding rather than by binary fission.
Budding allows new individuals to form as small growths attached to the parent organism.
The scientist observed the process of budding under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fission - division of a single organism into two or more parts

Etymology

From the verb 'bud' meaning to produce or form a bud; used in biology since the 18th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbʌdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʌdɪŋ / (BrE)
beginning to develop or show signs of future success in a particular role or activity
beginning to develop

Examples

The young actress is a budding star in the film industry.
He's a budding scientist with a keen interest in genetics.
Their company is still budding, but it shows great promise for the future.

Synset Examples

a budding genius

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emerging - starting to become apparent or prominent
  • Synonym: nascent - just coming into existence and beginning to display signs of future potential
  • Antonym: established - having been in existence for a long time and therefore recognized and generally accepted

Etymology

From the verb 'bud', referring to the growth of new buds on plants.
