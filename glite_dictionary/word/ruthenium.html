ruthenium

1. NOUN

/ ruːˈθiːniəm / (AmE), / ruːˈθiːnɪəm / (BrE)
a rare, silvery-white metallic chemical element of the platinum group, used in electronics and as a catalyst
a rare metallic element of the platinum group

Examples

The alloy contains small amounts of ruthenium to improve its durability.
Scientists examined the properties of ruthenium as a potential catalyst in chemical reactions.
Her research focuses on the use of ruthenium in solar cell technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: platinum - a precious silvery-white metal of the same group

Etymology

From New Latin 'Ruthenia', the Latin name for Russia, where the element was first discovered.
