Chenopodium vulvaria

1. NOUN

/ ˌkiːnoʊˈpoʊdiəm vʌlˈvɛəriə / (AmE), / ˌkɛnəˈpəʊdiəm vʌlˈvɛəriə / (BrE)
A species of goosefoot with strongly scented foliage, native to Europe and also found in eastern North America.
European goosefoot plant with strong odor

Examples

Many botanists are studying Chenopodium vulvaria for its unusual scent and potential uses.
Chenopodium vulvaria thrives in disturbed soils across parts of Europe and has adapted to new environments in North America.
The strong odor of Chenopodium vulvaria distinguishes it from other goosefoot species in the field.

Etymology

From Latin 'Chenopodium' (goosefoot) and species name 'vulvaria'.
