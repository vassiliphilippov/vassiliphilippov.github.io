extraneous

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈstreɪniəs / (AmE), / ɪkˈstreɪnɪəs / (BrE)
Coming from or existing outside of something; not forming an essential part or belonging to it.
coming from the outside; not essential

Examples

The scientist ensured no extraneous substances contaminated the sample.
Extraneous noises from the street made it hard to concentrate.
They filtered out all extraneous information to focus on the main issue.

Synset Examples

extraneous light in the camera spoiled the photograph
relying upon an extraneous income
disdaining outside pressure groups

Related Terms

  • Synonym: external - located or coming from outside; outer
  • Antonym: intrinsic - belonging naturally; inherent

Etymology

From Latin extraneus (external, foreign), from extra (outside).

2. ADJ

/ ɛkˈstreɪniəs / (AmE), / ɛkˈstreɪniəs / (BrE)
not directly related to the subject or matter being considered; irrelevant.
irrelevant, not pertinent

Examples

The report contained several extraneous details that did not help clarify the situation.
Please remove any extraneous information from your answer and focus on the main question.
During the meeting, she brought up an extraneous topic that was not on the agenda.

Synset Examples

an issue extraneous to the debate
the price was immaterial

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irrelevant - not connected with or relevant to something
  • Antonym: pertinent - relevant or applicable to a particular matter

Etymology

From Latin extraneus (foreign, external).
