Psophocarpus

1. NOUN

/ soʊˌfoʊkˈɑːrpəs / (AmE), / səʊˌfɒkˈɑːpəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical climbing herbs native to Asia and Africa, including species such as the winged bean.
genus of tropical climbing herbs

Examples

The Psophocarpus genus includes the winged bean, prized for its edible pods and leaves.
Researchers are studying Psophocarpus for its high protein content and agricultural value.
Several species of Psophocarpus are used as food crops in Southeast Asia.

Etymology

From Greek psophos (sound) + karpos (fruit), referring to the rattling of seeds in the pod.

2. NOUN

/ soʊfəˈkɑr.pəs / (AmE), / sɒfəˈkɑː.pəs / (BrE)
a genus of tropical Asian and African climbing herbs in the pea family, including the winged bean
genus of tropical climbing herbs

Examples

The winged bean is a well-known member of the Psophocarpus genus.
Agricultural scientists study Psophocarpus for its high protein content and edible parts.
Farmers in Southeast Asia have cultivated Psophocarpus to improve food security.

Etymology

From Greek *psophos* (sound, noise) + *karpos* (fruit), referring to the rattling seeds.
