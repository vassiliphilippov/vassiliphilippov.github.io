problematically

1. ADV

/ ˌprɑːb.ləˈmæt.ɪ.kli / (AmE), / ˌprɒb.ləˈmæt.ɪ.kli / (BrE)
In a manner that causes or poses a problem; in a problematic way.
in a problematic way

Examples

The new policy was problematically vague and left many questions unanswered.
Some passages are problematically ambiguous and open to multiple interpretations.
Her plan, while ambitious, was problematically reliant on outdated data.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: problematic - posing a problem or difficulty

Etymology

Formed from 'problematic' + '-ally' (adverbial suffix).
