Menyanthes trifoliata

1. NOUN

/ mɛnˈjænθiːz traɪˌfoʊliˈeɪtə / (AmE), / mɛnˈjanθiːz trɪˌfəʊlɪˈɑːtə / (BrE)
a perennial aquatic plant native to Europe and North America, with clusters of white or purplish flowers and bitter, three-lobed leaves, often found growing in marshy areas
aquatic plant with trifoliate leaves and white flowers

Examples

Menyanthes trifoliata is commonly found along the edges of ponds and lakes in northern Europe.
The intensely bitter leaves of Menyanthes trifoliata were once used in traditional medicine.
In spring, Menyanthes trifoliata produces delicate clusters of white flowers above its floating leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buckbean - common name for Menyanthes trifoliata

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name Menyanthes and Latin trifoliata (three-leaved).
