Olea cunninghamii

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊliə ˌkʌnɪŋˈhæmiːaɪ / (AmE), / ˈəʊliə ˌkʌnɪŋˈhæmii / (BrE)
A northern New Zealand tree with dense, hard, light-brown wood.
tree from northern New Zealand

Examples

The dense wood of Olea cunninghamii is valued for woodworking in New Zealand.
Several Olea cunninghamii trees grow along the northern coastal forests.
Conservationists are trying to protect Olea cunninghamii due to its limited distribution.

Etymology

Genus name from Latin *olea* (olive tree); species named after botanist Allan Cunningham.
