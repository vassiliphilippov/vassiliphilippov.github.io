Saddam Hussein

1. PROPN

/ səˈdɑm huˈseɪn / (AmE), / sæˈdæm hjuːˈseɪn / (BrE)
(1937–2006) Iraqi political leader who served as President of Iraq from 1979 to 2003, known for leading Iraq in wars against Iran and Kuwait and for his authoritarian rule.
Iraqi former president and military leader

Examples

Saddam Hussein was the President of Iraq for over two decades, from 1979 to 2003.
International attention focused on Saddam Hussein following Iraq’s invasion of Kuwait in 1990.
The tribunal found Saddam Hussein guilty of crimes against humanity.

2. PROPN

/ səˈdɑːm huːˈseɪn / (AmE), / sæˈdæm hʊˈseɪn / (BrE)
(1937–2006) Iraqi political leader who served as the President of Iraq from 1979 to 2003; known for leading Iraq during the Iran-Iraq War and the invasion of Kuwait, which led to the Gulf War.
Iraqi leader, President 1979–2003

Examples

Saddam Hussein ruled Iraq with an iron fist for more than two decades.
The 2003 invasion of Iraq resulted in the capture of Saddam Hussein.
Saddam Hussein was a central figure in Middle Eastern politics during the late 20th century.
