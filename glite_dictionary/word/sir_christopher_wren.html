Sir Christopher Wren

1. PROPN

/ sɜɹ ˌkrɪstəfɚ ˈrɛn / (AmE), / sɜː ˌkrɪstəfə ˈrɛn / (BrE)
(1632–1723) an English architect renowned for designing over fifty London churches, including St Paul's Cathedral.
English architect, designer of many London churches

Examples

Many tourists visit St Paul's Cathedral, designed by Sir Christopher Wren, when exploring London.
The legacy of Sir Christopher Wren shapes much of the city's historic skyline.
Students of architecture often study the work of Sir Christopher Wren for its innovation and classical style.
