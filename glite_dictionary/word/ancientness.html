ancientness

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪnʃəntnəs / (AmE), / ˈeɪnʃəntnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being very old or from a distant past; extreme oldness
extreme oldness; being from long ago

Examples

The ancientness of the ruins attracted historians from around the world.
There was a sense of ancientness in the old library's architecture and atmosphere.
Scientists studied the ancientness of the fossils to learn about prehistoric life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiquity - the ancient past, especially before the Middle Ages

Etymology

From 'ancient' + suffix '-ness', ultimately from Latin 'ante' (before).
