Thelypteridaceae

1. NOUN

/ θɛlɪpˌtɛrɪˈdeɪsiː / (AmE), / θɛlɪptɛrɪˈdeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of ferns that includes genera such as Thelypteris and Phegopteris.
a family of ferns

Examples

Members of the Thelypteridaceae family are commonly found in damp woodlands and marshes.
Botanists have revised the classification of several ferns within the Thelypteridaceae.
The genus Phegopteris also belongs to the Thelypteridaceae family.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Thelypteris and suffix -aceae denoting family in taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ θəliˌptɛrɪˈdeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / θɛlɪptɛrɪˈdeɪsɪiː / (BrE)
A large family of ferns that includes the genera Thelypteris, Phegopteris, and others, commonly found in moist, shaded habitats worldwide.
a family of ferns in botany

Examples

The Thelypteridaceae family includes a wide variety of fern species found throughout tropical regions.
Scientists have recently discovered a new species within the Thelypteridaceae.
Members of the Thelypteridaceae can be recognized by their distinctive leaf structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a nonflowering vascular plant with feathery leaves

Etymology

From the type genus Thelypteris, from Greek 'thelys' (female) + 'pteris' (fern).
