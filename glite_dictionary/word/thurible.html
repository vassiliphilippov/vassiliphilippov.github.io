thurible

1. NOUN

/ ˈθʊrəbəl / (AmE), / ˈθjʊərɪbəl / (BrE)
a container, often suspended on chains and swung, used for burning incense during religious ceremonies
container for burning incense in ceremonies

Examples

The priest swung the thurible gently, filling the church with the scent of incense.
An ornate silver thurible was used during the high mass.
The acolyte carried the thurible down the aisle as the choir sang.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: censer - a container in which incense is burned during a religious ceremony

Etymology

From Latin *thuribulum*, from *thus* (incense).
