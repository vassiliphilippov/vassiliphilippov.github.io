pockmark

1. VERB

/ ˈpɑkˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈpɒkˌmɑːk / (BrE)
to mark with small pits or scars, typically as a result of disease or damage
to mark with small pits or scars

Examples

Heavy acne can pockmark a person's skin for life.
The abandoned car was pockmarked with dents and rust spots.
The old walls were pockmarked by years of neglect and weather.

Synset Examples

Her face was pockmarked by the disease

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scar - a mark left on the skin after a wound or injury heals

Etymology

From 'pock' (a pustule from smallpox) + 'mark'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑkˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈpɒkˌmɑːk / (BrE)
a small scar or pit left on the skin by diseases such as smallpox, acne, or other eruptive conditions
scar or pit left by disease on the skin

Examples

He had a single pockmark on his cheek from a childhood illness.
Years after the outbreak, his skin was still dotted with pockmarks.
The dermatologist explained that acne could sometimes leave a pockmark as a lasting reminder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scar - a mark left on the skin after a wound has healed
