sample

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæmpəl / (AmE), / ˈsɑːmpəl / (BrE)
a small part or quantity of something that is collected and preserved to represent its whole or class, especially for study or analysis
specimen collected as an example

Examples

The scientist collected a sample of the soil for analysis.
We sent a water sample to the lab to test for contamination.
Each sample was carefully labeled and stored for later examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: specimen - an individual or part taken as an example of a whole

Etymology

From Old French 'essample', from Latin 'exemplum' (example).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsæmpəl / (AmE), / ˈsɑːmpəl / (BrE)
a small part or quantity of something that shows what the whole is like, often taken for inspection, testing, or analysis
representative part or quantity of something

Examples

The scientist collected a sample of the soil for further analysis.
Would you like to try a free sample of our new product?
Each student provided a sample of their handwriting for the study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: specimen - an individual item or part regarded as representative of a class or whole

Etymology

From Old French 'essample', later assimilated to Latin 'exemplum' (example, specimen).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05830135-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈsæmpəl / (AmE), / ˈsɑːmpəl / (BrE)
a small group or set of items chosen from a larger group, especially for analysis or testing to draw conclusions about the whole
a subset chosen for analysis

Examples

The researchers selected a sample of 500 people to participate in the survey.
A large sample can give more reliable results than a small one.
We analyzed the sample and found significant differences among the groups.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: population - the entire group from which a sample is drawn, in statistics

Etymology

From Latin 'exemplum' (example) via Old French 'essample'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06036061-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈsæmpəl / (AmE), / ˈsɑːmpəl / (BrE)
to taste or try a small amount of something, especially to assess its quality or flavor
to try a small amount

Examples

Visitors were encouraged to sample the award-winning cheeses.
I had the opportunity to sample wines from local vineyards.
She sampled several desserts before choosing her favorite.

Synset Examples

Try these new crackers
Sample the regional dishes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: try - to attempt to do or taste something in order to test it
  • Synonym: taste - to test the flavor of food or drink by eating or drinking a small amount

Etymology

From Old French 'essampler', from Latin 'exemplum' (example, sample).

Source: WordNet
