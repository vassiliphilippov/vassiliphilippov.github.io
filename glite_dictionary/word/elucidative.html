elucidative

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈluːsəˌdeɪtɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈluːsɪˌdeɪtɪv / (BrE)
serving to make something clear or easy to understand; explanatory
serving to clarify or explain

Examples

The professor used an elucidative analogy to help students grasp the complex theory.
Her elucidative comments clarified the main points of the proposal.
The report included several elucidative charts and diagrams for better understanding.

Synset Examples

a clarifying example

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explanatory - serving to explain something
  • Synonym: clarifying - making something clear or easier to understand

Etymology

From Latin 'elucidare' (to make clear), via 'elucidatus' (made clear) + English '-ive'.
