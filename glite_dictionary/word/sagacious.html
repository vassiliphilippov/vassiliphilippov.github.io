sagacious

1. ADJ

/ səˈɡeɪʃəs / (AmE), / səˈɡeɪʃəs / (BrE)
Having or showing keen mental discernment and good judgment; wise or shrewd.
wise; having keen judgment

Examples

Her sagacious decisions helped the company succeed during difficult economic times.
Only a sagacious leader could navigate such a complex situation so skillfully.
His sagacious advice was sought by many friends facing important life choices.

Synset Examples

much too perspicacious to be taken in by such a spurious argument
observant and thoughtful, he was given to asking sagacious questions
a source of valuable insights and sapient advice to educators

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wise - having or showing experience, knowledge, and good judgment
  • Antonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgment

Etymology

From Latin 'sagax' (wise, shrewd) + '-ous'.

2. ADJ

/ səˈɡeɪʃəs / (AmE), / səˈɡeɪʃəs / (BrE)
having or showing keen mental discernment and good judgment; wise or shrewd, especially in practical affairs or management
wise; showing good judgment

Examples

Her sagacious advice helped the company avoid a costly mistake.
Only a truly sagacious leader could have navigated such a complex situation successfully.
The investor made a sagacious decision that doubled his profits within a year.

Synset Examples

an astute and sagacious statesman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wise - having or showing experience, knowledge, and good judgment
  • Antonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgment

Etymology

From Latin sagax (wise, keen-scented) via Latin sagacitas (keenness of perception).
