transverse

1. ADJ

/ trænzˈvɝs / (AmE), / trænsˈvɜːs / (BrE)
extending or lying across something; situated or placed crosswise, especially at right angles to the main axis.
lying or extending across

Examples

The architect designed a transverse hall that crossed the length of the building.
In anatomy, the transverse colon runs horizontally across the abdomen.
A transverse beam supports the bridge by connecting the two sides.

Synset Examples

cross members should be all steel
from the transverse hall the stairway ascends gracefully
transversal vibrations
transverse colon

Related Terms

  • Antonym: longitudinal - extending in the direction of the length; relating to lengthwise orientation
  • Synonym: transversal - lying or extending across, especially in geometry and mathematics

Etymology

From Latin transversus, past participle of transvertere (to turn across).
