Uzbek language

1. NOUN

/ ˈʊzbɛk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈʊzbɛk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Turkic language spoken by the Uzbek people, primarily in Uzbekistan and neighboring countries.
Turkic language of the Uzbek people

Examples

She is fluent in both Russian and Uzbek language.
The Uzbek language uses a modified Latin alphabet for official purposes.
Many folk tales are preserved in the Uzbek language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkic languages - a language family including Uzbek, Turkish, Kazakh, and others

Etymology

From Uzbek (the ethnic name), ultimately from Turkic roots.
