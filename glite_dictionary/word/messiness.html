messiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛsinəs / (AmE), / ˈmɛsinəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being untidy or disorganized
the state of being messy

Examples

The messiness of his desk made it difficult to find anything important.
She was overwhelmed by the messiness that had accumulated after the party.
They hired a professional organizer to address the messiness of their home.

Synset Examples

the house was a mess
she smoothed the mussiness of the bed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tidiness - the quality or state of being neat and orderly
  • Synonym: disorderliness - the state of being disorganized or lacking order
  • Synonym: messy - untidy or disorganized

Etymology

From 'messy' + '-ness', forming a noun indicating quality or state.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛsi nəs / (AmE), / ˈmɛsi nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being untidy or disorganized
the state of being messy or untidy

Examples

The messiness of his room made it hard to find anything.
She apologized for the messiness of the kitchen when guests arrived.
Years of paperwork contributed to the messiness in the office.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tidiness - the quality or state of being neat and orderly
  • Synonym: messy - untidy or disorganized (adjective)

Etymology

From 'messy' + '-ness' (forming abstract nouns of quality).
