bankrupt

1. VERB

/ ˈbæŋ.krʌpt / (AmE), / ˈbaŋ.krʌpt / (BrE)
to make a person or organization unable to pay their debts, typically through business losses or financial mismanagement
make unable to pay debts

Examples

Poor financial decisions can easily bankrupt even a successful business.
The company was bankrupted by a series of bad investments.
He worried that medical bills might bankrupt his family.

Synset Examples

My daughter's fancy wedding is going to break me!
The slump in the financial markets smashed him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insolvent - unable to pay debts owed

Etymology

From Italian *banca rotta* (broken bench), influenced by Medieval Latin *bancus ruptus*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02323145-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋ.krʌpt / (AmE), / ˈbaŋ.krʌpt / (BrE)
a person or organization that is unable to pay their debts and has been legally declared insolvent
insolvent person or business

Examples

After the failed investment, Tom was declared a bankrupt by the court.
The company became a bankrupt following years of financial losses.
As a bankrupt, she lost control over her assets to pay off her creditors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debtor - a person who owes money
  • Antonym: solvent - having enough money to pay one's debts

Etymology

From Italian *banca rotta*, meaning 'broken bench', symbolizing a banker's bankruptcy in medieval Italy.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09857758-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈbæŋkˌrʌpt / (AmE), / ˈbaŋ.krʌpt / (BrE)
Unable to pay debts; declared as legally insolvent or financially ruined.
financially ruined

Examples

The once-thriving retailer was declared bankrupt after several years of declining sales.
Many investors lost everything when the company was officially bankrupt.
She was left virtually bankrupt by her ex-husband's debts.

Synset Examples

a bankrupt company
the company went belly-up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insolvent - unable to pay debts owed
  • Antonym: solvent - able to pay debts owed

Etymology

From Italian *banca rotta* ('broken bench'), indicating a moneylender's table broken to show bankruptcy.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02034007-s
Synset POS: s
