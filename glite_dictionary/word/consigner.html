consigner

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsaɪnər / (AmE), / kənˈsaɪnə / (BrE)
a person or company that delivers goods to another for sale, transportation, or custody, usually retaining ownership until certain conditions are met
person who delivers goods to another

Examples

The consigner must provide all necessary shipping documents for the transaction.
Goods remain the property of the consigner until they are sold by the consignee.
The contract specified the responsibilities of both the consigner and the consignee.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consignee - person or company to whom goods are shipped or delivered

Etymology

From 'consign' (to deliver formally), ultimately from Latin 'consignare' (to mark, seal).
