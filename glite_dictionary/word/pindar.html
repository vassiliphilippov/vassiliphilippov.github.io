Pindar

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɪndar / (AmE), / ˈpɪndɑː / (BrE)
(c. 518–c. 438 BC) A Greek lyric poet celebrated for his odes, particularly those honoring victors in ancient Greek games.
Ancient Greek lyric poet

Examples

Pindar's victory odes are regarded as masterpieces of ancient Greek poetry.
Many classicists have studied the complex structure of Pindar's poems.
The influence of Pindar can be traced in later European poetic traditions.

Etymology

Latin, from Ancient Greek Πίνδαρος (Píndaros).
