hypotonia

1. NOUN

/ haɪpəˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / haɪpəˈtəʊniə / (BrE)
Reduced muscle tone or tension, resulting in decreased resistance to passive movement.
reduced muscle tone
medical

Examples

Infants with hypotonia may appear floppy and have difficulty holding up their heads.
The physical therapist designed special exercises to help the child with hypotonia.
Doctors diagnosed hypotonia after noticing the baby's muscles were unusually soft.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypertonia - increased muscle tone or tension

Etymology

From Greek 'hypo-' (under, less) + 'tonos' (tone) + '-ia' (condition).
