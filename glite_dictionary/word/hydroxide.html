hydroxide

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdrɑksaɪd / (AmE), / hʌɪˈdrɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound containing the hydroxyl group (OH−), often forming a base in water
compound containing the hydroxyl (OH−) group

Examples

Sodium hydroxide is commonly used in soap making.
The neutralization of an acid with a hydroxide produces water and a salt.
Many cleaning agents contain some form of hydroxide as an active ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydroxyl - the OH group consisting of oxygen and hydrogen

Etymology

From Greek 'hydro-' (water) and 'oxide' (compound with oxygen).

2. NOUN

/ haɪˈdrɑksaɪd / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a compound consisting of a metal combined with the hydroxyl ion (OH−), often formed when an oxide reacts with water
a compound containing the hydroxyl ion (OH−)

Examples

Sodium hydroxide is frequently used in soap making.
Calcium hydroxide, also known as slaked lime, is used in construction.
The reaction of magnesium oxide with water produces magnesium hydroxide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oxide - a binary compound of oxygen with another element
  • Synonym: hydroxyl - the OH group in chemistry

Etymology

From hydro- (water) and oxide, describing its composition.
