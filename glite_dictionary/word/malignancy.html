malignancy

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɪɡnənsi / (AmE), / məˈlɪɡnənsi / (BrE)
the presence of cancerous cells that tend to grow uncontrollably and can invade or destroy nearby tissue, often resistant to treatment
cancerous growth; malignant tumor
medical

Examples

The doctors confirmed that the tumor was a malignancy requiring immediate treatment.
Early detection of a malignancy can significantly improve the chances of survival.
Biopsy results revealed no signs of malignancy in the tissue sample.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cancer - disease caused by uncontrolled cell division

Etymology

From Latin malignitas (wickedness), from malignus (malignant, harmful).

2. NOUN

/ məˈlɪɡ.nən.si / (AmE), / məˈlɪɡ.nən.si / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely harmful, evil, or having intense ill will toward others
intense evil or ill will

Examples

He was shocked by the sheer malignancy of her words.
The novel explores the malignancy that can fester within a seemingly perfect family.
Rumors spread with a malignancy that poisoned the entire community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malevolence - the quality of wishing harm to others; ill will
  • Antonym: benevolence - the quality of being well meaning; kindness

Etymology

From Latin malignitas (malice, ill will), from malignus (malicious).
