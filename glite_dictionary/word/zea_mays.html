Zea mays

1. NOUN

/ ˌziː.ə ˈmeɪz / (AmE), / ˌziː.ə ˈmeɪz / (BrE)
The scientific name for maize or corn, a tall cereal plant cultivated for its large ears of starchy kernels.
maize (corn), cereal plant

Examples

The genetic modification of Zea mays has led to higher crop yields worldwide.
Researchers studied the resistance of Zea mays to various fungal diseases.
Zea mays has been a staple food crop in the Americas for thousands of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maize - common name for Zea mays, a cereal plant
  • Synonym: corn - another common name for Zea mays, especially in American English

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Zea' (possibly from Greek 'zeia', a type of grain) and species 'mays' (from Taíno 'mahiz').
