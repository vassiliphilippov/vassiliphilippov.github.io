Korea

1. PROPN

/ kəˈriːə / (AmE), / kəˈriːə / (BrE)
A peninsula in East Asia, lying between the Yellow Sea and the Sea of Japan, which is now divided into North Korea and South Korea.
East Asian peninsula; region of North and South Korea

Examples

The political situation in Korea has attracted global attention for decades.
Many ancient artifacts have been discovered throughout Korea.
The climate of Korea is characterized by cold winters and hot summers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: North Korea - country in northern part of Korea peninsula; officially the Democratic People's Republic of Korea
  • Degree Lower: South Korea - country in southern part of Korea peninsula; officially the Republic of Korea

Etymology

From Korean 한국 (Han-Guk), meaning 'Han nation.'
