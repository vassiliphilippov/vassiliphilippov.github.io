commissioning

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmɪʃənɪŋ / (AmE), / kəˈmɪʃənɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of formally granting authority to someone or something to undertake certain functions or duties
formal granting of authority

Examples

The commissioning of the new officers took place in a formal ceremony.
Proper commissioning is essential before operating any major industrial equipment.
After commissioning, the research team was officially authorized to proceed with the study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appointment - the act of assigning a person to a position or office
  • Antonym: decommissioning - the act of removing something from service or official status

Etymology

From 'commission' + '-ing'; 'commission' from Latin 'committere' (to entrust).
