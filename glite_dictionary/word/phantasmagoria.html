phantasmagoria

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæn.təz.məˈɡɔːr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌfæn.tæz.məˈɡɔː.ri.ə / (BrE)
A shifting series of illusions or deceptive images, as seen in a dream or created by the imagination; a constantly changing scene composed of diverse elements.
a shifting series of dreamlike images

Examples

The story unfolds as a phantasmagoria of vivid memories and surreal visions.
At night, the city lights create a phantasmagoria of shapes and colors on her bedroom walls.
The novel moves fluidly through reality and phantasmagoria, blurring the line between the two.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illusion - something that appears real or true but is not
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

From French 'phantasmagorie', probably from Greek 'phantasma' (apparition) + 'agora' (assembly).
