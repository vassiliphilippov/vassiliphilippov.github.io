parade

1. NOUN

/ pəˈreɪd / (AmE), / pəˈreɪd / (BrE)
an open or ostentatious display of something, such as feelings or qualities, made to attract attention
an ostentatious display; show of something

Examples

She made a parade of her generosity at every opportunity.
Their argument was just a parade of grievances and complaints.
He put on a parade of confidence, though he was nervous inside.

Synset Examples

she made a parade of her sorrows

Related Terms

  • Synonym: display - an act of showing something, usually for people to look at or admire
  • Synonym: show - a display or exhibition; a visible presentation

Etymology

From French *parade* ('show, display'), from Spanish *parada* ('a halt, show'), from *parar* ('to stop, prepare').

2. NOUN

/ pəˈreɪd / (AmE), / pəˈreɪd / (BrE)
A long, often showy, series or succession of people or things passing by or displayed.
showy succession or series

Examples

The fashion show ended with a parade of the designers' most extravagant creations.
During the conference, there was a parade of guest speakers presenting new ideas.
The city witnessed a parade of vintage cars along its main street.

Synset Examples

a parade of strollers on the mall
a parade of witnesses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procession - an organized group or line moving forward in an orderly way, especially for a ceremony

Etymology

From French 'parade' (show, display), from Italian 'parata' (a show, parade), from 'parare' (to prepare).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ pəˈreɪd / (AmE), / pəˈreɪd / (BrE)
to march or walk together in a public procession, often to celebrate or display something
march in a public procession

Examples

The students will parade through the city center for the annual festival.
Thousands of fans paraded in celebration after the team won the championship.
Soldiers parade down the avenue during the national holiday.

Synset Examples

the veterans paraded down the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: march - to walk with regular steps, often as part of a group or procession

Etymology

From French 'parade' (show, display), from Spanish 'parada' (a stop), from 'parar' (to stop), ultimately from Latin 'parare' (to prepare).

Source: WordNet

5. NOUN

/ pəˈreɪd / (AmE), / pəˈreɪd / (BrE)
a ceremonial procession, often involving people marching in celebration, commemoration, or display
ceremonial procession with marching

Examples

We went to watch the Thanksgiving Day parade as it marched through the city streets.
The soldiers led the parade with their colorful uniforms and marching band.
A festive parade takes place every year to celebrate the town's founding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procession - a group of people moving forward in an orderly way, usually as part of a ceremony

Etymology

From French 'parade' (show, display), from Italian 'parata' (a show), past participle of 'parare' (to prepare).
