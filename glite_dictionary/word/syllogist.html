syllogist

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˈsɪlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a person skilled in analyzing or constructing syllogistic arguments; an expert in syllogistic logic
expert in syllogistic reasoning

Examples

The renowned syllogist gave a lecture on deductive reasoning at the philosophy conference.
As a skilled syllogist, she quickly identified flaws in the argument's logic.
Many medieval philosophers were accomplished syllogists who contributed to the study of logic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logician - an expert in or student of logic

Etymology

From Greek syllogizesthai (to reason or infer) via Late Latin syllogista.
