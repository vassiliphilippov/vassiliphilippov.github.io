deprecatively

1. ADV

/ ˌdɛprɪˈkeɪtɪvli / (AmE), / ˌdɛprɪˈkeɪtɪvli / (BrE)
In a manner that expresses disapproval, criticism, or objection.
in a disapproving or critical manner

Examples

She shook her head deprecatively, making it clear she did not agree with the proposal.
The reviewer spoke deprecatively about the film, criticizing its lack of originality.
He glanced deprecatively at the messy room, clearly unimpressed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: approvingly - in a manner showing approval, opposite in meaning to 'deprecatively'
  • Synonym: disapprovingly - in a manner that expresses disapproval, a close synonym to 'deprecatively'

Etymology

From 'deprecative' (expressing disapproval) + '-ly' (adverbial suffix).
