virility

1. NOUN

/ vəˈrɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / vɪˈrɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of having strength, energy, and sexual power traditionally associated with adult men; manliness
manly strength or masculinity

Examples

His athletic achievements were often seen as proof of his virility.
Many ancient cultures valued virility as a sign of leadership.
The advertisement promised to boost a man's virility and confidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masculinity - qualities or attributes regarded as characteristic of men
  • Antonym: effeminacy - the state or quality of being effeminate; lack of manliness

Etymology

From Latin virilitas (manhood), from virilis (manly), from vir (man).

2. NOUN

/ vəˈrɪləti / (AmE), / vɪˈrɪləti / (BrE)
the quality in a man of having strength, energy, and sexual potency; manly vigor or the capacity for copulation and procreation.
male sexual potency; manly vigor

Examples

His remarkable virility made him the subject of many rumors in the town.
Some cultures highly value virility as a symbol of strength and leadership.
The doctor was consulted about concerns regarding a loss of virility with age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manliness - the quality of being manly, masculinity
  • Antonym: impotence - lack of power, especially sexual power in men

Etymology

From Latin virilitas (manhood), from virilis (manly).
