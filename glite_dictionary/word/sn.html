S/N

1. NOUN

/ ɛs ˈɛn / (AmE), / ɛs ˈɛn / (BrE)
the ratio of the strength of a desired signal to the level of background noise, typically expressed in decibels
signal-to-noise ratio

Examples

The S/N of this audio system is high, resulting in clear sound quality.
Engineers work to maximize the S/N in communication devices for better performance.
A low S/N means the output is easily affected by background noise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signal-to-noise ratio - ratio of signal power to noise power

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'signal-to-noise'.
