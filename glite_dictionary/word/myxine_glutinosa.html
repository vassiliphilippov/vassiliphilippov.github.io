Myxine glutinosa

1. NOUN

/ maɪˌksaɪni ɡluːtɪˈnoʊsə / (AmE), / mɪkˌsiːni ɡluːtɪˈnəʊsə / (BrE)
A species of hagfish found in cold waters of the North Atlantic, known for its eel-like appearance and slime-producing abilities.
a species of hagfish

Examples

The North Atlantic is home to Myxine glutinosa, a hagfish famous for its prolific slime production.
Researchers studied the defense mechanisms of Myxine glutinosa in response to predators.
Commercial fishing rarely targets Myxine glutinosa due to its unpalatable slime and tough skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hagfish - eel-shaped, slime-producing marine fish

Etymology

From New Latin: genus 'Myxine' from Greek 'myxa' (mucus) and species 'glutinosa' from Latin (glutinous, sticky).
