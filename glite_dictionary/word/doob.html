doob

1. NOUN

/ dub / (AmE), / duːb / (BrE)
A species of trailing grass native to Europe, now widely found in warm regions, used for lawns and pastures, especially in the southern United States and India.
a type of trailing grass for lawns and pastures

Examples

The garden was carpeted with lush doob that needed frequent trimming.
Farmers in India use doob as pasture for their cattle.
Boys played football on the soft stretch of doob behind the school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bermuda grass - Another name for the same species of grass (Cynodon dactylon)

Etymology

From Hindi दूब (*dūb*), referring to this species of grass.
