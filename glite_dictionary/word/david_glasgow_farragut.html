David Glasgow Farragut

1. PROPN

/ ˈdeɪvɪd ˈɡlɑsɡoʊ ˈfærəɡət / (AmE), / ˈdeɪvɪd ˈɡlɑːzɡəʊ ˈfærəɡət / (BrE)
(1801–1870) A United States admiral who commanded Union naval forces during the American Civil War and is known for his leadership and the famous command, 'Damn the torpedoes, full speed ahead!'
U.S. admiral in the Civil War

Examples

Admiral David Glasgow Farragut became famous for his decisive actions during the Battle of Mobile Bay.
Many historians regard David Glasgow Farragut as one of the greatest naval commanders in American history.
A statue of David Glasgow Farragut can be found in Washington, D.C.'s Farragut Square.
