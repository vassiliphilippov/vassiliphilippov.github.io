bridgework

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪdʒˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈbrɪdʒˌwɜːk / (BrE)
a dental device consisting of artificial teeth supported by natural teeth on either side of a gap
dental device for replacing missing teeth
medical

Examples

After losing two molars, she opted for bridgework to restore her smile.
The dentist explained that bridgework would provide a permanent solution for my missing teeth.
Proper cleaning is essential to maintain bridgework and prevent gum disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: denture - a removable replacement for missing teeth

Etymology

From 'bridge' (structure spanning an opening) + 'work', referring to the bridging of a gap in the teeth.
