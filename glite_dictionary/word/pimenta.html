Pimenta

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈmɛntə / (AmE), / pɪˈmɛntə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical trees in the myrtle family, including the allspice tree (Pimenta dioica), native to Central America and the West Indies.
genus of tropical trees; allspice tree

Examples

The Pimenta tree is cultivated for its aromatic berries known as allspice.
Several species of Pimenta are valued for their culinary and medicinal uses.
Botanists studied the characteristics of the Pimenta genus in the Caribbean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allspice - dried berry of the Pimenta dioica tree used as a spice

Etymology

From Latin 'pimenta', meaning 'pepper', adopted in scientific Latin for this genus name.
