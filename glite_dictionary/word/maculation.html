maculation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmækjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmakjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of marking with spots or stains; the condition of being spotted or stained
the act of spotting or staining

Examples

The maculation of the animal's fur makes it easily identifiable in the wild.
She noticed the maculation on her shirt after spilling some coffee.
The artist used deliberate maculation to achieve a unique pattern on the canvas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: staining - the act of applying a stain or being stained; discoloration
  • Synonym: spotting - the act of marking with spots; speckling

Etymology

From Latin maculatio (the act of spotting), from macula (spot, stain).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæk.jəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌmak.jʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a small spot, stain, or area that contrasts with its surroundings, especially as seen on animals, plants, or objects
small contrasting spot or patch

Examples

The butterfly's wings were decorated with striking maculation.
Scientists studied the patterns of maculation on the leopard's fur to understand camouflage.
That breed of dog is known for its distinctive maculation along the back.

Synset Examples

a bald spot
a leopard's spots
a patch of clouds
patches of thin ice
a fleck of red

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spot - a small round or roundish mark, differing in color or texture from the surface around it
  • Synonym: patch - a small contrasting area on a surface
  • Synonym: stain - a discolored mark that is difficult to remove

Etymology

From Latin maculatio (a spotting), from macula (spot).
