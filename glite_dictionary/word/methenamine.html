methenamine

1. NOUN

/ məˈθɛnəˌmiːn / (AmE), / mɛˈθɛnəmiːn / (BrE)
a white crystalline compound used chiefly as an antibacterial agent to treat urinary tract infections
antibacterial drug for urinary tract infections
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed methenamine to help prevent further urinary tract infections.
Long-term use of methenamine can reduce the risk of recurrent bladder infections.
Some patients may experience side effects when taking methenamine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibiotic - a drug used to treat bacterial infections

Etymology

From 'meth-' (relating to methyl) + 'enamine' (a compound containing a nitrogen–carbon double bond).
