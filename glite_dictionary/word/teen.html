teen

1. NOUN

/ tin / (AmE), / tiːn / (BrE)
a person in their teenage years, typically between the ages of 13 and 19
person aged 13 to 19

Examples

The school organized a workshop specifically for teens about internet safety.
Many teens enjoy spending time with their friends after school.
It's common for teens to develop new interests as they grow older.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adolescent - a young person developing into an adult
  • Antonym: adult - a fully grown person or one who has reached legal age

Etymology

Shortened form of 'teenager', from the 'teen' suffix in numbers thirteen to nineteen.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09791452-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ tin / (AmE), / tiːn / (BrE)
related to a person aged between 13 and 19 years old
relating to ages 13–19

Examples

The program is focused on helping teen athletes balance sports and academics.
Many teen novels deal with issues like identity, friendship, and family.
She volunteers at a clinic that provides support for teen mothers.

Synset Examples

teenage mothers
the teen years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teenage - relating to people aged 13 to 19
  • Antonym: adult - a person who is fully grown or developed
  • Degree Lower: child - a young human being below the age of puberty

Etymology

Shortened from 'teenager', formed from 'teen' (as in 'thirteen' to 'nineteen').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01652425-s
Synset POS: s
