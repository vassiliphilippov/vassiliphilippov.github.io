Lincolnian

1. ADJ

/ ˌlɪŋˈkoʊniən / (AmE), / ˌlɪŋˈkəʊniən / (BrE)
relating to Abraham Lincoln, his ideas, policies, or manner.
relating to Abraham Lincoln or his style

Examples

The senator gave a Lincolnian speech that emphasized unity and justice.
Her leadership style is often described as Lincolnian for its honesty and humility.
Historians debate whether the president’s decisions were truly Lincolnian in principle.

Etymology

From Abraham Lincoln, 16th president of the United States, combined with the adjectival suffix -ian.
