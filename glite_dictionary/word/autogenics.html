autogenics

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔː.təˈdʒɛn.ɪks / (AmE), / ˌɔː.təˈdʒɛn.ɪks / (BrE)
a psychological technique or practice involving self-induced relaxation, often through a series of mental exercises or self-suggestions
self-induced relaxation technique
medical

Examples

Many therapists recommend autogenics to patients suffering from anxiety disorders.
She practiced autogenics daily to manage her stress and improve her concentration.
After learning autogenics, he found it easier to relax before important presentations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autogenic training - a relaxation technique involving self-suggestion and visualization

Etymology

From Greek 'auto-' (self) and 'genesis' (origin), referring to self-generation.
