RBI

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːr.biːˈaɪ / (AmE), / ˌɑː.biːˈaɪ / (BrE)
(in baseball) a run that is scored as a result of a batter's action at the plate, except in cases resulting from fielding errors.
run scored from a batter's action (baseball)

Examples

He finished the game with three RBIs, helping his team secure the win.
Last season, she had over 80 RBIs, setting a new school record.
A timely single brought in an RBI for the home team.

Synset Examples

he had more than 100 rbi last season

Etymology

Acronym from 'run(s) batted in'.
