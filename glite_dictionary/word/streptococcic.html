streptococcic

1. ADJ

/ ˌstrɛptəˈkɑksɪk / (AmE), / ˌstrɛptəˈkɒksɪk / (BrE)
of, relating to, or caused by streptococci (a genus of spherical bacteria, some of which can cause disease)
caused by or relating to streptococci
medical

Examples

The patient developed a streptococcic infection after the surgical procedure.
Scientists are studying new antibiotics to treat streptococcic diseases.
A positive culture confirmed that the wound was streptococcic in origin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: streptococcal - of, relating to, or caused by streptococci

Etymology

From New Latin 'streptococcus' (the bacterial genus) + English '-ic' (forming adjectives).
