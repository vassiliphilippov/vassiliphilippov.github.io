nonpolar

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈpoʊlɚ / (AmE), / nɒnˈpəʊlə / (BrE)
(of a molecule or substance) not having distinct electric poles; not ionic or not exhibiting a significant electric charge separation.
not having distinct electric poles; not ionic

Examples

In water, a nonpolar molecule will not dissolve easily because it lacks charged regions.
Many oils are nonpolar substances and do not mix with water.
Carbon dioxide is a simple example of a nonpolar gas.

Synset Examples

a nonionic substance

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polar - having distinct electric poles
  • Antonym: ionic - relating to, composed of, or producing ions

Etymology

From prefix 'non-' (not) + 'polar' (having poles, especially electric).
