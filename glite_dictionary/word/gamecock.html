gamecock

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪmˌkɑk / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪmkɒk / (BrE)
A person who is a very fierce and determined fighter, especially one who shows courage in difficult situations.
fierce and determined fighter

Examples

The lawyer was a gamecock in the courtroom, never backing down from a challenge.
She earned a reputation as a gamecock during her political campaign.
Despite his small size, he proved himself a real gamecock in every contest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fighter - a person who fights

Etymology

From 'game' (brave) + 'cock' (rooster), originally used to refer to fighting cocks in cockfighting.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪmˌkɑk / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪmˌkɒk / (BrE)
A rooster bred and trained for fighting, especially in cockfighting.
rooster bred for fighting

Examples

The farmer raised a gamecock for the annual village tournament.
Some countries have strict laws against breeding gamecocks for fighting.
He admired the fierce spirit of the gamecock in the arena.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockfight - a contest in which gamecocks are made to fight each other

Etymology

From 'game' meaning brave or spirited, and 'cock' meaning rooster.
