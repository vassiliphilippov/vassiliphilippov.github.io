Vidua

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪdjuːə / (AmE), / ˈvɪdjʊə / (BrE)
A genus of small African songbirds known as whydahs, characterized by long tail feathers in males during the breeding season.
genus of African whydahs (birds)

Examples

During the breeding season, the male Vidua develops strikingly long tail feathers.
Several species within the genus Vidua are known for their intricate mating dances.
The biologist studied the nesting habits of the Vidua in its natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whydah - African songbird with long tail feathers, genus Vidua

Etymology

From Latin vidua, meaning 'widow', likely referring to the black plumage of some species.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪdjuə / (AmE), / ˈvɪdjʊə / (BrE)
A genus of small African birds known as whydahs, noted for their striking breeding plumage and long tail feathers in males.
genus of African whydah birds

Examples

The breeding male Vidua displays long, elegant tail feathers to attract females.
Several species within the genus Vidua are brood parasites, laying their eggs in the nests of other birds.
Researchers studied the mating habits of Vidua to understand sexual selection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whydah - an African finch-like bird with long tail feathers

Etymology

From Latin 'vidua' (widow), referring to the black plumage of some species resembling mourning dress.
