to divulge

1. VERB

/ dɪˈvʌldʒ / (AmE), / dɪˈvʌldʒ / (BrE)
to make information known to people, especially information that was secret or private
make secret information known

Examples

She refused to divulge the name of her source during the interview.
The company will not divulge details about its new product until the official launch.
He accidentally divulged confidential information during the meeting.

Synset Examples

The auction house would not disclose the price at which the van Gogh had sold
The actress won't reveal how old she is
bring out the truth
he broke the news to her
unwrap the evidence in the murder case
The newspaper uncovered the President's illegal dealings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reveal - to make known something that was secret or unknown
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep something secret; to hide

Etymology

From Latin 'divulgare' (to make common, publish).
