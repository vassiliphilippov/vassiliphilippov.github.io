Pablum

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæbləm / (AmE), / ˈpabləm / (BrE)
a soft, bland cereal food, originally formulated for infants and young children
soft infant cereal

Examples

The doctor suggested we start the baby on Pablum at six months.
He bought a box of Pablum for his daughter at the grocery store.
In the 1940s, Pablum was a staple food for many infants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cereal - a common breakfast food made from processed grains

Etymology

Trademarked name for a baby cereal introduced in Canada in the 1930s, from Latin 'pabulum' (food, fodder).
