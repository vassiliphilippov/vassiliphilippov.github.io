Simarouba amara

1. NOUN

/ sɪˌmɑːruːbə əˈmɑːrə / (AmE), / sɪˌmaruːbə əˈmɑːrə / (BrE)
a tree native to the Amazon valley, known for its light brittle timber that is locally valued for its resistance to insect attack
Amazonian tree valued for insect-resistant timber

Examples

The wood of the Simarouba amara is commonly used in local construction for its resistance to insects.
Researchers studied how Simarouba amara trees contribute to the Amazon rainforest ecosystem.
Furniture makers sometimes seek out Simarouba amara because its timber is both lightweight and durable.

Etymology

From Latinized form of indigenous South American names, used in botanical Latin.
