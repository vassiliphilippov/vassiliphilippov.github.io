flounce

1. NOUN

/ flaʊns / (AmE), / flaʊns / (BrE)
a strip of pleated fabric used as a decorative trim, especially on clothing or curtains
strip of pleated decorative fabric

Examples

The wedding dress was adorned with a delicate flounce around the hem.
She added a pink flounce to the edge of the curtain for a touch of elegance.
The skirt’s flounce gave it a graceful, flowing movement when she walked.

Etymology

From Middle English flounce, perhaps from Old Norse *flunsa* (tassel) or Middle Low German *vlunse* (fringe).

2. VERB

/ flaʊns / (AmE), / flaʊns / (BrE)
to move or walk in a dramatic, exaggerated, or angry way, often to attract attention
move or walk dramatically

Examples

She flounced out of the meeting, slamming the door behind her.
He flounced into the room and threw his bag onto the chair.
After reading the email, she flounced away in protest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stomp - to walk with heavy, forceful steps, often angrily
Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ flaʊns / (AmE), / flaʊns / (BrE)
an act of walking with exaggerated, jerky, or showy motions, especially to display anger or annoyance
exaggerated, jerky manner of walking

Examples

She left the room in a dramatic flounce, slamming the door behind her.
His flounce across the stage made everyone laugh.
With a theatrical flounce, she tossed her scarf and stormed away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strut - a proud, stiff-minded walk, often to show off
  • Synonym: stalk - to walk angrily or stiffly

Etymology

Probably from Scandinavian origins; compare Swedish *flunsa* (to hurry, bustle, flounce).
