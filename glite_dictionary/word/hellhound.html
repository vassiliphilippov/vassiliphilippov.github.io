hellhound

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛlˌhaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈhɛlhaʊnd / (BrE)
A very evil or cruel person; someone regarded as utterly wicked.
very evil or cruel person

Examples

The gangster was a hellhound whose crimes terrified the city.
History remembers him as a hellhound driven by a lust for power.
She warned that only a true hellhound could commit such atrocities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: villain - a wicked or evil person
  • Synonym: monster - a cruel or wicked person
  • Antonym: saint - an exceptionally virtuous person

Etymology

Formed from 'hell' + 'hound'; originally denoting a fiendish dog from hell in mythology.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛlˌhaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈhɛl.haʊnd / (BrE)
(in mythology) a supernatural dog, often associated with the underworld or guarding the entrance to hell or Hades.
mythical supernatural underworld dog

Examples

According to legend, a hellhound stood guard at the gates of the underworld.
The monstrous hellhound is often depicted with fiery eyes and an unearthly howl.
Some say a black hellhound haunts the moors, appearing as an omen of death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cerberus - the three-headed dog guarding the entrance to Hades in Greek mythology

Etymology

Formed from "hell" + "hound"; found in Old English as 'hellehund'.
