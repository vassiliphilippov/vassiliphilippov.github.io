decreasing monotonic

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈkriːsɪŋ məˈnɑtəˌnɪk / (AmE), / dɪˈkriːsɪŋ məˈnɒtənɪk / (BrE)
(of a function or sequence) consistently decreasing, never increasing at any point.
consistently decreasing (mathematics)

Examples

The graph of a decreasing monotonic function slopes downward as you move from left to right.
If a sequence is decreasing monotonic, each term is less than or equal to the one before it.
Many physical processes can be modeled by decreasing monotonic functions, such as radioactive decay.

Synset Examples

a decreasing monotonic function

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monotonic - (of a function or sequence) always increasing or always decreasing; never reversing direction
  • Antonym: increasing monotonic - consistently increasing, never decreasing at any point

Etymology

From 'decreasing' (making or becoming smaller or fewer) and 'monotonic' (from Greek monotónos, meaning 'one tone' or 'unchanging in tone').
