musculus scalenus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌs.kjəl.əs skeɪˈliː.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs skeɪˈliː.nəs / (BrE)
Any of four paired muscles extending from the cervical vertebrae to the first and second ribs, involved in neck movement and breathing.
neck muscle group aiding breathing
medical

Examples

The musculus scalenus assists in elevating the first and second ribs during deep inhalation.
Neck pain may sometimes be related to tension in the musculus scalenus.
The surgeon took care to avoid damaging the musculus scalenus during the procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scalene muscle - either of the muscles in the neck known as scalene muscles

Etymology

From Latin ‘musculus’ (muscle) and ‘scalenus’ (uneven, referring to the shape).
