Pyrrophyta

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈrɑːfɪtə / (AmE), / pɪˈrɒfɪtə / (BrE)
a former division of lower algae characterized by unicellular, biflagellate organisms that often form starchy compounds; commonly known as dinoflagellates
division of unicellular, biflagellate algae (dinoflagellates)
medical

Examples

The red tide is often caused by blooms of Pyrrophyta species in coastal waters.
Researchers study Pyrrophyta to understand their impact on marine ecosystems.
Some Pyrrophyta are known for producing bioluminescence in the ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dinoflagellate - unicellular protists, many of which are classified under Pyrrophyta

Etymology

From Greek 'pyr' (fire) + 'phyton' (plant), referring to their sometimes bioluminescent nature.

2. NOUN

/ paɪˈrɑːfɪtə / (AmE), / paɪˈrɒfɪtə / (BrE)
a division of single-celled, biflagellate algae known for forming starchy compounds, often classified among dinoflagellates
a group of unicellular, biflagellate algae
medical

Examples

The blooms of Pyrrophyta can cause red tides in coastal waters.
Scientists studied the unique starchy compounds produced by Pyrrophyta under laboratory conditions.
Many toxic marine plankton are classified within the Pyrrophyta.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dinoflagellate - single-celled organisms related to Pyrrophyta, often marine plankton

Etymology

From Greek 'pyr' (fire) and 'phyton' (plant), referring to their often reddish coloration.
