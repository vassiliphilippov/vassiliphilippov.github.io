lodestar

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊd.stɑr / (AmE), / ˈləʊd.stɑː / (BrE)
a person or thing that serves as an inspiration or guide; a guiding principle
model or guiding principle

Examples

His integrity has long been the lodestar for our company values.
She used her mother's advice as a lodestar throughout her career.
Honesty remains the lodestar of their organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guiding light - a person who influences or inspires others
  • Synonym: model - something to be imitated or followed
  • Antonym: antithesis - the direct or exact opposite

Etymology

From Middle English lodesterre, from 'lode' (way or course) + 'star'; originally referring to the North Star as a guiding star.

2. NOUN

/ ˈloʊdˌstɑr / (AmE), / ˈləʊdˌstɑː / (BrE)
A star, especially the North Star, that is used as a guide by navigators or as a reference point in astronomy.
a star used as a navigational guide

Examples

Sailors once relied on the lodestar to navigate the open seas.
The lodestar served as a beacon of hope on their long journey.
For centuries, the North Star has been humanity’s most trusted lodestar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polestar - the North Star, key navigational reference star
  • Synonym: guide - something that directs or influences a course

Etymology

From Middle English 'lodestere', from 'lode' (way, course) + 'star'.
