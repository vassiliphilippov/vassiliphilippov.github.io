beta-adrenoceptor

1. NOUN

/ ˌbeɪtə əˌdrɛnəˈsɛptər / (AmE), / ˌbiːtə əˌdrɛnəˈsɛptə / (BrE)
a type of receptor in the sympathetic nervous system that responds specifically to beta-adrenergic agents, affecting functions such as heart rate and blood vessel dilation
receptor in the sympathetic nervous system
medical

Examples

The drug targets the beta-adrenoceptor to reduce high blood pressure.
Activation of the beta-adrenoceptor increases the heart rate during stress.
Scientists studied how the beta-adrenoceptor mediates different responses in cardiac tissue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beta-blocker - drug that blocks beta-adrenoceptors

Etymology

From 'beta-' (chemical classification) + 'adreno-' (relating to adrenaline) + 'receptor'.
