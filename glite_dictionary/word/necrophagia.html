necrophagia

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɛk.rəˈfeɪ.dʒi.ə / (AmE), / ˌnɛk.rəˈfeɪ.dʒɪ.ə / (BrE)
the act of feeding on dead bodies or carrion, especially by animals or insects
feeding on corpses or carrion
medical

Examples

Certain beetles exhibit necrophagia as part of their life cycle.
Scientists observed necrophagia among the vulture population in the region.
The textbook described necrophagia as a vital ecological process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scavenging - the act of searching for and eating dead animals or organic matter

Etymology

From Greek nekros (dead body) + -phagia (eating).
