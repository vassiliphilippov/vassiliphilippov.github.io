calycinal

1. ADJ

/ kəˈlɪsɪnəl / (AmE), / kəˈlɪsɪnəl / (BrE)
relating to or resembling a calyx, especially in a flower
relating to a calyx (botany)

Examples

The plant displays calycinal hairs covering the surface of its stem.
In some species, the calycinal tube is fused to protect the developing flower.
The botanist noted the presence of a unique calycinal structure around the bud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calyx - the sepals of a flower, collectively forming the outer floral envelope

Etymology

From New Latin calyx (outer part of a flower) + -al (forming adjectives).
