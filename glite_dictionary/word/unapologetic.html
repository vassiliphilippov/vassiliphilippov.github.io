unapologetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˌpɑ.ləˈdʒɛt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˌpɒl.əˈdʒɛt.ɪk / (BrE)
not showing regret or remorse; not inclined to apologize or express guilt or sorrow for one's actions or beliefs.
not showing regret or remorse

Examples

Despite criticism, she remained unapologetic about her decision.
He gave an unapologetic speech that surprised many in the audience.
The artist's work is bold, raw, and unapologetic in its style.

Synset Examples

an unapologetic believer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: apologetic - showing or expressing an apology or regret
  • Synonym: remorseful - feeling regret or guilt for a wrongdoing

Etymology

Formed from the prefix un- (not) + apologetic (from apology, ultimately from Greek apologetikos).
