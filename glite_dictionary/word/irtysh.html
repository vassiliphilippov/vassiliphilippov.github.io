Irtysh

1. PROPN

/ ɪrˈtɪʃ / (AmE), / ɪəˈtɪʃ / (BrE)
a major river in Asia, originating in the Altai Mountains in northern China and flowing northwest as a tributary of the Ob River
major river in Asia, Ob tributary

Examples

The Irtysh River flows through China, Kazakhstan, and Russia, joining the Ob near Khanty-Mansiysk.
During our trip to Siberia, we sailed along the Irtysh and admired its vast scenery.
Industrial development along the Irtysh has raised environmental concerns in recent decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ob - a major river in western Siberia, Russia, into which the Irtysh flows

Etymology

From Russian Иртыш (Irtysh), likely from Turkic languages.
