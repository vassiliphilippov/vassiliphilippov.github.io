rente

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːnt / (AmE), / ˈrɒnt / (BrE)
income from capital investment paid in a series of regular payments, especially as an annuity.
regular income from investment; annuity

Examples

He invested his inheritance to secure a steady rente for his retirement.
The nobleman lived comfortably on the annual rente from his estates.
She purchased a rente to ensure regular payments after leaving her job.

Synset Examples

his retirement fund was set up to be paid as an annuity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annuity - a fixed sum of money paid to someone each year, typically for the rest of their life

Etymology

From French rente (annual income or annuity), from Latin redditus (income).
