J. P. Morgan

1. PROPN

/ ˌdʒeɪ pi ˈmɔrɡən / (AmE), / ˌdʒeɪ pi ˈmɔːɡən / (BrE)
(1837–1913) An American financier and philanthropist who dominated corporate finance and industrial consolidation in late 19th and early 20th century United States.
American financier and philanthropist

Examples

J. P. Morgan played a major role in the reorganization of railroads and corporations in America.
The art collection donated by J. P. Morgan is now housed in multiple museums.
Financial historians often study the influence of J. P. Morgan on modern banking.
