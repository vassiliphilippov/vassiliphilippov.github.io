homologous

1. ADJ

/ həˈmɑːləɡəs / (AmE), / həˈmɒl.ə.ɡəs / (BrE)
(of organs or parts) having the same evolutionary origin but not necessarily the same function.
having the same evolutionary origin

Examples

The forelimbs of whales and bats are homologous, as they derive from a common ancestral limb.
In evolutionary biology, researchers compare homologous genes to study species relationships.
Although a bat's wing and a human arm serve different purposes, they are homologous structures.

Synset Examples

the wing of a bat and the arm of a man are homologous

Related Terms

  • Antonym: analogous - (of organs or parts) similar in function but not in evolutionary origin

Etymology

From Greek homologos (agreeing, of one mind), from homos (same) + logos (relation).

2. ADJ

/ həˈmɑː.lə.ɡəs / (AmE), / həˈmɒl.ə.ɡəs / (BrE)
Having the same relative position, structure, or origin but not necessarily the same function; especially referring to body parts or structures in different species or tissues derived from the same individual or species.
having a similar structure or origin

Examples

The scientists observed that the wings of bats and the arms of humans are homologous structures.
A homologous chromosome pair carries the same type of genetic information, though not always identical alleles.
Doctors often perform homologous blood transfusions using blood from donors of the same species.

Synset Examples

a homologous tissue graft

Related Terms

  • Antonym: analogous - corresponding in function but not in structure or origin

Etymology

From Greek homologos (agreeing, of one mind), from homos (same) + logos (relation, proportion).

WordNet ID: oewn-01222723-a
Synset POS: a
