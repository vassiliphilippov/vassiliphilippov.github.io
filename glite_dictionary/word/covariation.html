covariation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊˌvɛriˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkəʊˌvɛəriˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the degree to which two or more variables change together in a correlated manner, especially in statistics
correlated variation between variables

Examples

The study analyzed the covariation between smoking rates and lung cancer incidence.
Strong covariation was observed in the test results across different age groups.
Researchers are interested in the covariation of gene expression levels during development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: correlation - a mutual relationship or connection between two variables

Etymology

Formed from 'co-' (together) + 'variation' (from Latin variare, to change).
