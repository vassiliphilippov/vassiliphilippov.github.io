ramate

1. ADJ

/ ˈræmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈræmeɪt / (BrE)
having branches; branched.
having branches

Examples

The ramate structure of the plant allowed it to grow in multiple directions.
Paleontologists described the fossil as having distinctly ramate appendages.
Many corals have a ramate form, spreading their branches across the reef.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: branching - dividing into branches

Etymology

From Latin ramatus (branched), from ramus (branch).
