Betula glandulosa

1. NOUN

/ bəˈtjuːlə ɡlænˈdjuːlə / (AmE), / bɛˈtjuːlə ɡlænˈdjuːlə / (BrE)
A small shrub species of birch found in cold regions of North America and Greenland, commonly known as dwarf birch.
shrub species native to cold North America and Greenland

Examples

Betula glandulosa is commonly found in the tundra regions of Alaska and northern Canada.
Scientists studied the impact of climate change on Betula glandulosa populations in Greenland.
The leaves of Betula glandulosa are small and round, adapting the plant to cold environments.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Betula' meaning 'birch,' and 'glandulosa' meaning 'full of glands.'
