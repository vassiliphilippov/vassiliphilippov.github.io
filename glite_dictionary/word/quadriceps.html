quadriceps

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑdrɪˌsɛps / (AmE), / ˈkwɒdrɪˌsɛps / (BrE)
a large muscle at the front of the thigh responsible for extending the leg at the knee joint
large thigh muscle
medical

Examples

He tore his quadriceps while sprinting during the football match.
Strengthening the quadriceps helps stabilize the knee and prevent injury.
Physical therapy exercises often target the quadriceps after knee surgery.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hamstring - muscle at the back of the thigh

Etymology

From Latin 'quadri-' (four) + 'caput' (head), referring to its four parts.
