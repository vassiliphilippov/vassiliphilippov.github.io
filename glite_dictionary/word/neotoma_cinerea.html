Neotoma cinerea

1. NOUN

/ niˈoʊtə mə sɪˈnɪəriə / (AmE), / niːˈɒtə mə sɪˈnɪəriə / (BrE)
a species of bushy-tailed rodent found in western North America, also known as the bushy-tailed woodrat, noted for hoarding food and other objects
a species of bushy-tailed woodrat

Examples

The Neotoma cinerea is often called the bushy-tailed woodrat because of its distinctive tail.
Biologists studied the nesting habits of Neotoma cinerea in the Rocky Mountains.
Unlike many rodents, Neotoma cinerea is especially known for its habit of collecting shiny objects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pack rat - a rodent known for hoarding food and miscellaneous objects

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name Neotoma and species name cinerea, meaning 'ash-colored' in Latin.
