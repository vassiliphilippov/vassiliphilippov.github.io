twaddle

1. NOUN

/ ˈtwɑː.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˈtwɒd.əl / (BrE)
silly or trivial talk or writing that lacks sense or meaning; nonsense.
silly or trivial talk; nonsense

Examples

He dismissed the rumor as utter twaddle and refused to discuss it further.
Her speech was full of pretentious twaddle that failed to impress the audience.
Don't listen to that twaddle; none of it is true.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - spoken or written words that have no meaning or make no sense

Etymology

Origin unknown; recorded in English since the late 18th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06623500-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈtwɑːdəl / (AmE), / ˈtwɒdəl / (BrE)
to talk rapidly and at length about trivial or insignificant matters; to chatter or babble nonsensically
to talk trivial nonsense at length

Examples

He tends to twaddle on about sports statistics during meetings.
Please stop twaddling; we're trying to focus on the main issue.
She twaddled for an hour about her weekend plans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babble - to talk rapidly and continuously in a foolish, excited, or incomprehensible way
  • Synonym: prattle - to talk at length in a foolish or inconsequential way

Etymology

Probably imitative in origin, dating from the late 18th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01038676-v
Synset POS: v
