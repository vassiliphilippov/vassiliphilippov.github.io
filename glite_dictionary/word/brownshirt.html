Brownshirt

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraʊnˌʃɜrt / (AmE), / ˈbraʊnˌʃɜːt / (BrE)
a member of the Nazi SA (Sturmabteilung), the paramilitary wing of the Nazi Party, known for wearing brown uniforms
member of the Nazi SA paramilitary group

Examples

The Brownshirts patrolled the streets during the rise of the Nazi regime in Germany.
Many feared the violence of the Brownshirts as they intimidated political opponents.
As a young man, he joined the Brownshirts before the outbreak of World War II.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Stormtrooper - member of the Nazi Sturmabteilung or SA

Etymology

From the brown uniforms worn by members of the Nazi Sturmabteilung (SA) in Germany.
