Prumnopitys ferruginea

1. NOUN

/ prumˌnoʊˈpɪtɪs ˌfɛruːˈdʒiːniə / (AmE), / prʌmˌnɒˈpɪtɪs ˌfɛrʊˈdʒiːnɪə / (BrE)
a New Zealand coniferous tree, also known as miro, valued for its dark wood used in interior carpentry and lumber
New Zealand conifer used for lumber

Examples

The dense timber of the Prumnopitys ferruginea is highly prized for furniture making in New Zealand.
Conservationists are working to preserve Prumnopitys ferruginea forests due to their ecological significance.
Interior panelling made from Prumnopitys ferruginea adds a rich, warm tone to modern homes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miro - common name for Prumnopitys ferruginea, a New Zealand conifer

Etymology

From Latinized botanical nomenclature: genus name 'Prumnopitys' from Greek 'prymnos' (hindmost) and 'pitys' (pine), species name 'ferruginea' meaning 'rust-colored' in Latin.
