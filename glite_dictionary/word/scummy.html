scummy

1. ADJ

/ ˈskʌmi / (AmE), / ˈskʌmi / (BrE)
(informal) despicable, contemptible, or morally low; deserving of disgust or contempt.
despicable or contemptible
informal

Examples

I can't believe he pulled such a scummy trick on his friends.
She called him out for his scummy behavior in the meeting.
That was a scummy thing to do, and everyone was disgusted.

Synset Examples

abject cowardice
a low stunt to pull
a low-down sneak
his miserable treatment of his family
You miserable skunk!
a scummy rabble
a scurvy trick

Related Terms

  • Synonym: despicable - deserving hatred or contempt
  • Synonym: contemptible - deserving contempt; despicable
  • Antonym: admirable - deserving respect or approval

Etymology

Formed from 'scum' (dirt, refuse) + '-y' (adjective-forming suffix), likening a person to unpleasant surface residue.

/ ˈskʌmi / (AmE), / ˈskʌmi / (BrE)

covered with scum or filth

Examples
