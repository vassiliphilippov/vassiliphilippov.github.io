foreshank

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌʃæŋk / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌʃæŋk / (BrE)
a cut of meat taken from the upper part of the front leg of an animal, especially cattle or lamb
meat cut from a front leg

Examples

The stew recipe calls for braised foreshank to achieve its rich, hearty flavor.
He ordered lamb foreshank at the restaurant, served with root vegetables.
Beef foreshank is ideal for slow-cooking due to its connective tissues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hindshank - a cut of meat from the upper part of a back leg

Etymology

From 'fore-' (meaning front) + 'shank' (lower part of the leg), referring to its anatomical location.
