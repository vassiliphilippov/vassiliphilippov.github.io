smallholder

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmɔlˌhoʊldɚ / (AmE), / ˈsmɔːlhəʊldə / (BrE)
a person who owns or rents a smallholding, typically farming a relatively small plot of land.
person farming a small plot of land

Examples

Many smallholders rely on family labor to cultivate their land.
The government introduced subsidies to support smallholders in rural communities.
He became a smallholder after inheriting a modest plot from his father.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farmer - person who owns or manages a farm
  • Degree Higher: landowner - person who owns land, especially a large amount

Etymology

Formed from 'small' + 'holder', meaning someone who holds (owns or rents) a small amount of land.
