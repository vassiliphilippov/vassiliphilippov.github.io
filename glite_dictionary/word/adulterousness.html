adulterousness

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʌltəɹəsnəs / (AmE), / əˈdʌltərəsnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being adulterous; the state or condition of engaging in sexual relations by a married person with someone other than their spouse.
quality of being adulterous

Examples

He was unconcerned by the adulterousness of his actions, seeing them as routine.
Her novel explores the adulterousness that permeated upper-class society in the 19th century.
Publicly, he condemned adulterousness while privately engaging in such behavior himself.

Synset Examples

He consequently interprets his own behaviour in sociobiological terms so that, having slept with his ex-wife Charlotte in her current marital bed, he is unnerved not by the adulterousness of the act, which is routine for him, but by the infringement of male territorial boundaries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adultery - voluntary sexual intercourse between a married person and a person who is not their spouse

Etymology

From 'adulterous' + '-ness'. 'Adulterous' from Latin 'adulterāri' (to commit adultery).
