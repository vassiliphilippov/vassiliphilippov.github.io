epicarp

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛpɪˌkɑrp / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪkɑːp / (BrE)
the outermost layer of the pericarp of a fruit, forming its skin, as in peaches or grapes
outer skin of a fruit

Examples

The epicarp of an orange is thick and often bitter.
Grape epicarp contains pigments that give the fruit its color.
When peeling a peach, you remove the epicarp to reveal the sweet flesh inside.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: pericarp - the part of a fruit formed from the ovary wall after flowering

Etymology

From Greek epi- (upon) + karpos (fruit).
