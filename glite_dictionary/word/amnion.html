amnion

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmniˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈamniɒn / (BrE)
the thin, innermost membranous sac that encloses the developing embryo in reptiles, birds, and mammals
membrane surrounding embryo in higher vertebrates
medical

Examples

The amnion protects the embryo by forming a fluid-filled cavity.
In mammals, the amnion plays a crucial role during pregnancy.
Scientists studied the development of the amnion in reptiles for their research.

Etymology

From Greek amnion (little lamb), referring to the saclike membrane.
