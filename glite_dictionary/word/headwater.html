headwater

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌwɔtər / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
the source or upper tributaries of a river
the source of a river

Examples

The explorers traced the river back to its headwater in the mountains.
Conservation efforts have focused on protecting the headwaters of the watershed.
Many species of fish thrive only in the cool, clean headwater streams.

Synset Examples

the headwaters of the Nile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: source - the point where a river begins

Etymology

From 'head' (top, source) and 'water'.
