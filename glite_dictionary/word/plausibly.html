plausibly

1. ADV

/ ˈplɔːzəbli / (AmE), / ˈplɔːzəbli / (BrE)
In a way that seems reasonable or probable, or is likely to be true based on available evidence.
in a way that seems reasonable or likely

Examples

He explained the mistake very plausibly, so everyone believed him.
The witness answered the lawyer's questions plausibly, but some facts didn't add up.
Her theory was plausibly supported by the evidence found at the scene.

Synset Examples

he talked plausibly before the committee
he will probably win the election

Related Terms

  • Synonym: probably - likely to happen or be the case

Etymology

From Latin plausibilis (worthy of applause), from plaudere (to applaud), with the adverbial suffix -ly.
