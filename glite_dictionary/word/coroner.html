coroner

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrənər / (AmE), / ˈkɒrənə / (BrE)
an official whose job is to investigate the causes of deaths that are sudden, unexplained, or happen under suspicious circumstances, especially those not obviously due to natural causes
official who investigates unexplained deaths
legal

Examples

The coroner determined that the cause of death was accidental drowning.
After a thorough investigation, the coroner released her official findings to the press.
The police contacted the coroner immediately after discovering the unidentified body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medical examiner - a doctor who investigates deaths, often overlapping with coroner duties in some regions

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman and Middle English, related to 'corona' (crown), as the coroner originally acted in the name of the Crown.
