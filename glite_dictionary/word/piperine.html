piperine

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪpəriːn / (AmE), / ˈpɪpəriːn / (BrE)
an alkaloid found in black and white pepper, responsible for their pungent taste
alkaloid in pepper causing pungency

Examples

Piperine is what gives black pepper its spicy flavor and pungent aroma.
Some studies suggest that piperine may enhance the absorption of certain nutrients.
The content of piperine in white pepper is slightly less than in black pepper.

Etymology

From Latin 'piper' (pepper) with chemical suffix '-ine'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪpəriːn / (AmE), / ˈpɪpəriːn / (BrE)
a natural compound found in black and white pepper that gives them their pungent flavor
compound giving pepper its pungency
medical

Examples

The hot flavor of black pepper comes from a compound called piperine.
Researchers studied the effects of piperine on digestive enzymes.
Many supplements use piperine to enhance the absorption of nutrients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capsaicin - compound responsible for the hotness of chili peppers
  • Degree Higher: pepper - spicy seasoning from peppercorns

Etymology

From Latin 'piper' (pepper) + chemical suffix '-ine', originally coined in German as 'Piperin'.
