pacific

1. ADJ

/ pəˈsɪfɪk / (AmE), / pəˈsɪfɪk / (BrE)
tending to promote peace; calm and peaceful in nature
peaceful, promoting calm

Examples

Her pacific demeanor helped resolve the tense situation quickly.
The country has a pacific foreign policy that avoids conflict whenever possible.
We admired his pacific attitude in the face of provocation.

Synset Examples

the pacific temper seeks to settle disputes on grounds of justice rather than by force
a quiet and peaceable person
in a peaceable and orderly manner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peaceful - free from disturbance; tranquil or calm
  • Antonym: hostile - unfriendly or antagonistic

Etymology

From Latin pacificus, from pax (peace) + facere (to make).

2. ADJ

/ pəˈsɪfɪk / (AmE), / pəˈsɪfɪk / (BrE)
promoting peace or having a peaceful intent; inclined to avoid conflict or aggression.
promoting peace; peaceful in intent

Examples

The country's new leader adopted a pacific approach to foreign policy.
Her pacific demeanor helped resolve many workplace disputes peacefully.
The organization is known for its pacific mission of promoting global harmony.

Synset Examples

the result of this pacific policy was that no troops were called up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peaceful - not involving war or violence; tranquil
  • Antonym: warlike - inclined to or ready for war; hostile

Etymology

From Latin 'pacificus', from 'pax' (peace) + 'facere' (to make).
