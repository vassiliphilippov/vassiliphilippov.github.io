Pacific

1. PROPN

/ pəˈsɪfɪk / (AmE), / pəˈsɪfɪk / (BrE)
the largest and deepest ocean in the world, stretching between Asia and Australia on the west and the Americas on the east
the world's largest ocean

Examples

She studied the migratory patterns of whales in the Pacific.
Many island nations are scattered throughout the Pacific.
The Pacific covers more area than all the land on Earth combined.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Atlantic - the second largest ocean, between the Americas and Europe/Africa

Etymology

From Latin 'pacificus' meaning 'peace-making'; used in 'Pacific Ocean' to mean 'peaceful sea'.

2. ADJ

/ pəˈsɪfɪk / (AmE), / pəˈsɪfɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to the Pacific Ocean; situated on or near the Pacific Ocean.
relating to the Pacific Ocean

Examples

She planned a vacation to several Pacific islands this summer.
Many Pacific nations are particularly vulnerable to climate change impacts.
The west coast of the United States is known for its Pacific coastline.

Synset Examples

Pacific islands

Etymology

From Latin 'pacificus' (peace-making); used in the name 'Pacific Ocean' by Magellan, who called it 'Mar Pacifico'.
