Suillus albivelatus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːɪləs ˌælbɪˈveɪlətəs / (AmE), / ˈsjuːɪləs ˌal.bɪˈveɪ.lə.təs / (BrE)
A species of edible mushroom with a short, squat form, a reddish-brown cap, and a white stalk, typically found under pines in the spring.
an edible mushroom with reddish-brown cap and white stalk

Examples

We found several Suillus albivelatus growing under the pine trees during our spring hike.
The Suillus albivelatus is prized by some for its unique flavor and squat appearance.
Identifying Suillus albivelatus is easier because of its reddish-brown cap and white stalk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mushroom - the fleshy, spore-bearing fruiting body of a fungus

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Suillus' (meaning 'of swine'), and species 'albivelatus' referring to white (albi-) veil.
