insulant

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.sjə.lənt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.sjʊ.lənt / (BrE)
a material that reduces or prevents the transmission of heat, sound, or electricity; an insulating material
material preventing heat, sound, or electricity transmission

Examples

Foam panels are often used as an insulant in building walls to retain heat.
Rubber acts as an effective insulant against electrical currents.
The engineer chose a new insulant to reduce noise between rooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insulator - a substance that does not readily conduct electricity, heat, or sound
  • Antonym: conductor - a material that readily transmits electricity, heat, or sound

Etymology

From Latin 'insulatus' (made into an island), related to 'insulate'.
