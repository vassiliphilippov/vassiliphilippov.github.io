infringement

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfrɪndʒmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfrɪndʒmənt / (BrE)
A minor violation of a law or regulation; a crime less serious than a felony.
minor violation of the law
legal

Examples

Driving over the speed limit is considered an infringement rather than a serious crime.
The company was fined for a minor infringement of the new environmental law.
Every infringement will be recorded and could result in disciplinary action.

Etymology

From Late Latin *infringere* (to break, violate).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfrɪndʒmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfrɪndʒmənt / (BrE)
the act of violating or encroaching upon a law, right, or agreement
act of violating a law or right

Examples

Copyright infringement can result in significant legal penalties for individuals or companies.
The new law protects employees against any infringement of their rights.
Their actions were found to be a clear infringement of the contract terms.

Synset Examples

he claimed a violation of his rights under the Fifth Amendment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violation - the act of breaking or disregarding a law or right
  • Antonym: compliance - the act of conforming to a rule or law

Etymology

From Latin infringere (to break, shatter), via Medieval Latin infringementum.
