twenty-three

1. NUM

/ ˌtwɛntiˈθri / (AmE), / ˌtwɛntiˈθriː / (BrE)
the cardinal number represented by 23, being one more than twenty-two
cardinal number: 23

Examples

She turned twenty-three years old last month.
There are twenty-three students in my class this semester.
The team won twenty-three games in a row, breaking the school record.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: twenty-two - the cardinal number represented by 22
  • Degree Higher: twenty-four - the cardinal number represented by 24

Etymology

Formed from 'twenty' (from Old English 'twēntig') and 'three' (from Old English 'thrēo').

3. NUM

/ ˌtwɛntiˈθri / (AmE), / ˌtwentiˈθriː / (BrE)
The number 23; the number that is three more than twenty.
the number 23

Examples

A total of twenty-three students attended the workshop this year.
My grandfather served twenty-three years in the navy before retiring.
There are only twenty-three books on the recommended reading list.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: twenty-two - the number 22; one less than twenty-three
  • Degree Higher: twenty-four - the number 24; one more than twenty-three

Etymology

Formed from 'twenty' (20) and 'three' (3).
