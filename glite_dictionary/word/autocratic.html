autocratic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔtəˈkrætɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈkrætɪk / (BrE)
having absolute power and authority; ruling with unlimited control, often oppressively
with absolute ruling power

Examples

The country was ruled by an autocratic leader who tolerated no opposition.
For years, the company maintained an autocratic management style that discouraged feedback from employees.
Her autocratic approach to decision-making alienated many team members.

Synset Examples

an authoritarian regime
autocratic government
despotic rulers
a dictatorial rule that lasted for the duration of the war
a tyrannical government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authoritarian - favoring or enforcing strict obedience to authority
  • Antonym: democratic - relating to or supporting democracy or its principles

Etymology

From Greek autokratēs (self-ruling), from autos (self) + kratos (power).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɔtəˈkrætɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈkrætɪk / (BrE)
behaving in a domineering or dictatorial way; exercising power or authority over others in a self-assured and often unwarranted manner
domineering or dictatorial in behavior

Examples

The new manager has adopted an autocratic style, rarely consulting his team before making decisions.
Her autocratic attitude made it difficult for colleagues to express their own views.
Parents should avoid being autocratic when dealing with their children's choices.

Synset Examples

an autocratic person
autocratic behavior
a bossy way of ordering others around
a rather aggressive and dominating character
managed the employees in an aloof magisterial way
a swaggering peremptory manner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bossy - fond of giving people orders; domineering
  • Synonym: authoritarian - favoring or enforcing strict obedience to authority
  • Antonym: democratic - relating to or supporting democracy or equality in decision-making

Etymology

From Greek autokratēs, from autos (self) + kratos (power, rule).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00791150-s
Synset POS: s
