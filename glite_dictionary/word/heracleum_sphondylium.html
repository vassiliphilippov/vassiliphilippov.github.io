Heracleum sphondylium

1. NOUN

/ hɛˈrækliəm sfɒnˈdɪliəm / (AmE), / hɪˈrækliəm sfɒnˈdɪliəm / (BrE)
A tall, coarse plant of the parsley family, native to Europe and Asia, with thick stems and clusters of white to purple flowers; commonly known as common hogweed.
species of tall plant (common hogweed)

Examples

The stems of Heracleum sphondylium are hollow and often marked with purple spots.
In summer, Heracleum sphondylium produces large umbels of white flowers.
Gardeners should be cautious when handling Heracleum sphondylium due to its sap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hogweed - the common name for Heracleum sphondylium

Etymology

From Latin: genus Heracleum named after Heracles (Hercules) of Greek mythology; species sphondylium from Greek 'sphondulon' (vertebra, referring to jointed stems).
