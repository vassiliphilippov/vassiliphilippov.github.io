to loathe

1. VERB

/ loʊð / (AmE), / ləʊð / (BrE)
to feel intense dislike or disgust for someone or something
feel intense dislike

Examples

She loathes waiting in long queues at the airport.
Many people loathe the taste of licorice, although others love it.
He loathed every minute of the tedious meeting.

Synset Examples

I loathe that man
She abhors cats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to detest - to dislike intensely
  • Synonym: to abhor - to regard with disgust and hatred
  • Antonym: love - to feel deep affection or strong liking for

Etymology

From Old English 'lāðian' (to hate, detest), related to 'lāð' (loathsome).
