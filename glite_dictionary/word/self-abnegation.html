self-abnegation

1. NOUN

/ sɛlf ˌæb.nɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / sɛlf ˌab.nɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of denying or sacrificing one's own interests, needs, or desires for the sake of others
sacrificing one's own interests

Examples

Her lifelong self-abnegation inspired everyone around her to be more generous.
Monastic life often requires a high degree of self-abnegation and discipline.
He was admired for his self-abnegation in putting his family's needs before his own.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-sacrifice - the act of giving up one's own interests for others
  • Antonym: self-indulgence - the act of gratifying one's own desires

Etymology

From self + abnegation, with 'abnegation' from Latin abnegatio (refusal, denial).
