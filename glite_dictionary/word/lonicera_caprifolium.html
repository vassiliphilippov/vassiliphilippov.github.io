Lonicera caprifolium

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈnɪsərə ˌkæprɪˈfoʊliəm / (AmE), / ləˈnɪsərə ˌkæprɪˈfəʊliəm / (BrE)
A deciduous climbing shrub with fragrant yellow-white flowers, commonly known as Italian honeysuckle.
species of honeysuckle; climbing shrub

Examples

The Lonicera caprifolium in the garden bloomed with a delightful, sweet scent every evening.
Many gardeners choose Lonicera caprifolium for its vigorous climbing habit and attractive flowers.
Botanists often refer to Italian honeysuckle by its scientific name, Lonicera caprifolium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeysuckle - a climbing shrub with sweetly scented flowers, genus Lonicera

Etymology

From Latin: *Lonicera*, the genus, named after Adam Lonicer (1528–1586), German botanist; *caprifolium* meaning 'goat's-leaf' in Latin.
