evangel

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈvændʒəl / (AmE), / ɪˈvændʒ(ə)l / (BrE)
the four books of the New Testament (Matthew, Mark, Luke, and John) that tell the story of Christ's life and teachings; the Gospels
the four Gospels in the New Testament

Examples

The preacher read aloud from the evangel during the Sunday service.
Throughout history, artists have depicted scenes from the evangel in their work.
Many scholars devote their lives to studying the evangel and its influence on Christianity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gospel - one of the first four books of the New Testament

Etymology

From Late Latin evangelium, from Greek euangelion (good news).
