to spew

1. VERB

/ spju / (AmE), / spjuː / (BrE)
to forcefully eject the contents of the stomach through the mouth; to vomit
to vomit; throw up

Examples

He rushed to the bathroom to spew after the roller coaster ride.
The smell made her spew all over the kitchen floor.
After eating the spoiled food, he couldn't help but spew repeatedly.

Synset Examples

After drinking too much, the students vomited
He purged continuously
The patient regurgitated the food we gave him last night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vomit - to eject the contents of the stomach through the mouth
  • Synonym: to regurgitate - to bring swallowed food up again to the mouth

Etymology

Old English *spīwan*, related to Dutch *spuwen* and German *spucken* (to spit).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00076153-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ spju / (AmE), / spjuː / (BrE)
to eject or discharge something forcefully and in large amounts, often in a sudden or uncontrolled way; also used metaphorically for producing large amounts rapidly.
eject or discharge forcefully in large amounts

Examples

The factory chimneys spew smoke into the sky every morning.
She tends to spew complaints whenever something goes wrong at work.
The broken pipe began to spew water all over the basement.

Synset Examples

the volcano spews out molten rocks every day
The editors of the paper spew out hostile articles about the Presidential candidate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vomit - to eject stomach contents through the mouth (also used metaphorically)
  • Synonym: gush - to flow or send out suddenly and in large amounts
  • Antonym: to contain - to keep something within limits or under control

Etymology

From Middle English *spewen*, from Old English *spīwan*, related to Old High German *spīwan* and Latin *spuere* (to spit).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00076824-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ spjuː / (AmE), / spjuː / (BrE)
to forcefully expel saliva, phlegm, or sputum from the mouth
to forcibly spit out fluid from the mouth

Examples

He started to spew saliva in anger during the heated argument.
The patient tried not to spew phlegm onto the floor while coughing.
She had to spew sputum into the tissue for the doctor.

Synset Examples

The father of the victim spat at the alleged murderer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spit - to eject saliva from the mouth
  • Synonym: to expectorate - to cough or spit out phlegm from the throat or lungs

Etymology

Old English *spiwian* (to vomit), related to German *speien* (to spit, vomit).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00101710-v
Synset POS: v
