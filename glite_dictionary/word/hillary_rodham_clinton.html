Hillary Rodham Clinton

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɪləri ˈrɑːdəm ˈklɪntən / (AmE), / ˈhɪləri ˈrɒdəm ˈklɪntən / (BrE)
(born 1947) American politician, diplomat, and former First Lady; served as U.S. Secretary of State (2009–2013), U.S. Senator (2001–2009), and was the Democratic nominee for President in 2016.
American politician and former U.S. Secretary of State

Examples

Hillary Rodham Clinton was the first former First Lady to be elected to the U.S. Senate.
In 2016, Hillary Rodham Clinton became the first woman nominated for U.S. president by a major party.
Many admired Hillary Rodham Clinton for her work on healthcare reform during her time as First Lady.
