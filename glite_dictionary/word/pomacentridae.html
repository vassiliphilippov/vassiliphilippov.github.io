Pomacentridae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊməˈsɛntrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌpəʊməˈsɛntrɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, brightly colored marine fishes known as damselfishes, found mainly in tropical coral reefs.
a family of damselfishes (marine fish)

Examples

The Pomacentridae are prominent residents of coral reef ecosystems.
Researchers study the social behavior of Pomacentridae to understand reef fish communities.
Many aquarists keep species from Pomacentridae for their vibrant colors and active behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damselfish - a small, brightly colored tropical marine fish

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'poma' (cover) and 'kentron' (spine), referring to features of the fishes' anatomy.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊ.məˈsɛn.trəˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌpɒm.əˈsɛn.trɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, brightly colored marine fishes known as damselfishes, often found in tropical coral reefs.
a family of damselfishes

Examples

The Pomacentridae are commonly seen darting among coral branches in tropical seas.
Many species in the Pomacentridae family display vibrant colors and intricate patterns.
The research focused on the behavior of Pomacentridae in response to environmental changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damselfish - small, brightly colored marine fish of family Pomacentridae

Etymology

From New Latin Pomacentridae, from genus Pomacentrus, named from Greek poma (cover) + kentron (sting) + -idae (family suffix).
