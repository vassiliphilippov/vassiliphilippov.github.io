redolence

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdələns / (AmE), / ˈrɛdələns / (BrE)
a pleasant, often sweet smell; a fragrance that evokes memories or emotions
a pleasant or evocative smell

Examples

The redolence of blooming roses filled the summer garden.
A faint redolence of spices drifted from the kitchen as dinner was prepared.
The library was filled with the redolence of old paper and leather-bound books.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragrance - a pleasant, sweet smell
  • Antonym: odor - a distinctive smell, particularly an unpleasant one

Etymology

From Latin 'redolentia', from 'redolere' (to emit a scent).
