gravitative

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡrævɪˌteɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈɡravɪˌteɪtɪv / (BrE)
relating to, arising from, or caused by gravitation.
pertaining to gravitation

Examples

The gravitative force between two objects depends on their masses and the distance separating them.
Scientists study gravitative phenomena to understand how planets and stars interact.
The orbit of the satellite is determined by the gravitative pull of the Earth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravitational - relating to or caused by gravity

Etymology

From Latin 'gravitas' (weight, heaviness) + English '-ive'.
