sacerdotalism

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæsɚˈdoʊtəlˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌsasəˈdəʊtəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief that priests have the authority to act as mediators between human beings and God
belief in priestly mediation

Examples

The doctrine of sacerdotalism played a major role in medieval church structure.
Some Christian denominations reject sacerdotalism in favor of direct access to God.
Debates about sacerdotalism have shaped many theological discussions in history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: priesthood - body or office of priests in a religion

Etymology

From Latin 'sacerdotalis' (priestly), from 'sacerdos' (priest) + '-ism'.
