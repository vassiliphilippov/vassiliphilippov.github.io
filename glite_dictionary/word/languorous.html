languorous

1. ADJ

/ ˈlæŋɡərəs / (AmE), / ˈlæŋɡərəs / (BrE)
characterized by a dreamy lack of energy, vitality, or spirit; pleasantly slow or relaxed
pleasantly lacking energy or liveliness

Examples

After lunch, we lay in the shade, feeling languorous in the summer heat.
Her languorous movements gave the impression of someone lost in a daydream.
The painting captures a languorous afternoon by the riverside.

Synset Examples

a lackadaisical attempt
a languid mood
a languid wave of the hand
a hot languorous afternoon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: languid - displaying or having a disinclination for physical exertion or effort
  • Antonym: energetic - showing or involving great activity or vitality

Etymology

From Latin 'languor' (faintness, weakness) via French 'languoureux'.
