pestis bubonica

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛstɪs bjuːˈbɑnɪkə / (AmE), / ˌpɛstɪs bjuːˈbɒnɪkə / (BrE)
the most common form of plague in humans, marked by fever, delirium, swollen lymph nodes (buboes), and not spread directly from person to person
form of plague with swollen lymph nodes
medical

Examples

During the Middle Ages, pestis bubonica killed millions across Europe.
The spread of pestis bubonica was often linked to the movement of rats and fleas.
Early symptoms of pestis bubonica include sudden fever and painful swollen glands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bubonic plague - another name for pestis bubonica, a form of plague

Etymology

From Latin 'pestis' (plague) and 'bubonica', from Greek 'bubo' (swelling of the lymph nodes).
