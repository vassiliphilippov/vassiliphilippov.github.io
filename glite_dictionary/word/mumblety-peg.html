mumblety-peg

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌmbəltiˌpɛɡ / (AmE), / ˈmʌmb(ə)ltiˌpɛɡ / (BrE)
A traditional game in which players throw or flip a jackknife in specific ways so that it sticks into the ground.
game involving tossing a knife into the ground

Examples

Growing up, my grandfather taught me how to play mumblety-peg in the backyard.
The boys challenged each other to a game of mumblety-peg at summer camp.
They used an old pocketknife when playing mumblety-peg after school.

Etymology

From 'mumble the peg', referring to players mumbling or muttering while pulling a peg from the ground, 19th-century US origin.
