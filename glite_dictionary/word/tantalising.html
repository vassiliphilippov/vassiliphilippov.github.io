tantalising

1. ADJ

/ ˈtæn.təˌlaɪ.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtæn.tə.laɪ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
arousing desire or expectation for something that is unobtainable or just out of reach
arousing longing for the unobtainable

Examples

The aroma of fresh bread was tantalising to those waiting outside the bakery.
She felt a tantalising sense of adventure after reading his mysterious letter.
Despite his tantalising promises, the deal never actually materialized.

Synset Examples

a tantalizing taste of success

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tempting - arousing interest or desire
  • Synonym: teasing - provoking or tormenting by keeping something desired just out of reach
  • Antonym: unattainable - impossible to achieve or reach

Etymology

From the mythological figure Tantalus in Greek mythology, who was tormented by being offered fruit and water that receded when he tried to take them.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtæn.təˌlaɪ.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtan.tə.laɪ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
Highly attractive or appealing, especially by arousing desire or expectation for something that remains just out of reach.
very appealing or tempting

Examples

The tantalising smell of freshly baked bread filled the kitchen.
She caught a tantalising glimpse of the artist behind the curtain.
The prospect of a holiday by the sea was tantalising after months of hard work.

Synset Examples

a tantalizing aroma
a tempting repast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tempting - attractive or alluring in a way that makes people want to have or do something
  • Synonym: alluring - powerfully and mysteriously attractive or fascinating

Etymology

From the Greek myth of Tantalus, whose punishment was to have water and fruit just out of his reach.
