Globigerina

1. NOUN

/ ɡloʊˌbɪdʒəˈraɪnə / (AmE), / ɡləʊˌbɪdʒəˈriːnə / (BrE)
A genus of planktonic foraminifera that are important marine microorganisms forming part of oceanic plankton, and the type genus of the family Globigerinidae.
genus of marine planktonic foraminifera

Examples

The shells of Globigerina contribute significantly to the sediment known as globigerina ooze.
Many ocean-floor sediments are made up of the remains of Globigerina.
Scientists study Globigerina fossils to understand past climate changes.

Etymology

From Latin *globulus* (small sphere) and Greek *gerēnos* (carrier), due to the spherical shape of the organism.
