globigerina

1. NOUN

/ ɡloʊˌbɪdʒəˈriːnə / (AmE), / ɡləʊˌbɪdʒəˈriːnə / (BrE)
a marine protozoan of a genus characterized by a rounded, calcareous shell with spiny processes, found in plankton and important in fossil formation
marine protozoan with a rounded, spiny shell

Examples

The seafloor is littered with the shells of globigerina that accumulated over millions of years.
Researchers study globigerina fossils to learn about ancient climate conditions.
Many deep-sea sediments are composed mainly of globigerina remains.

Etymology

From Latin 'globus' (sphere) and 'gerere' (to bear), referring to the spherical, spiny shell.
