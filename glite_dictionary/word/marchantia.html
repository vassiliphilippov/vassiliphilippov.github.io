Marchantia

1. NOUN

/ mɑːrˈkænʃiə / (AmE), / mɑːˈkæntɪə / (BrE)
A genus of liverworts in the family Marchantiaceae, known for reproducing asexually by gemmae and having stalked antheridiophores.
genus of liverworts (Marchantiaceae)

Examples

Marchantia is often used as a model organism in studies of plant development and evolution.
The distinctive reproductive structures of Marchantia set it apart from other liverwort genera.
Researchers collected Marchantia specimens from damp, shaded rocks for their experiment.

Etymology

From Latinized form of the name of Italian botanist Nicholas Marchant (died 1678).
