to gesticulate

1. VERB

/ dʒəˈstɪkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / dʒɛˈstɪkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to make expressive movements with the hands or arms, especially when speaking, to emphasize or convey an idea
to gesture with hands or arms

Examples

She began to gesticulate wildly as she explained the situation.
The speaker would gesticulate with his hands to make his point clear.
He gesticulated toward the exit, urging us to leave quickly.

Synset Examples

He gestured his desire to leave

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gesture - to make a movement of the hand or head to express an idea or feeling

Etymology

From Latin gesticulatus, past participle of gesticulari (to gesture), from gesticulus (a little gesture).
