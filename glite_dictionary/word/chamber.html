chamber

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪmbə / (BrE)
a room where a judge conducts business outside of the courtroom
judge's private room for business
legal

Examples

The judge retired to his chamber to deliberate on the case.
Attorneys often meet with the judge in chamber to discuss pretrial matters.
The order was signed privately in the judge's chamber.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: courtroom - the room where a court of law meets

Etymology

From Old French chambre, from Latin camera (vaulted room, chamber).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪmbər / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪmbə / (BrE)
a natural or artificial enclosed space, especially within a larger structure or organism
an enclosed space, natural or artificial

Examples

The heart has four chambers that pump blood throughout the body.
The explorers discovered a hidden chamber deep inside the cave.
The seeds are stored in a specially designed chamber to keep them dry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cavity - a hollow space within a solid object or body
  • Synonym: room - an enclosed space within a building used for a particular purpose

Etymology

From Old French 'chambre', from Latin 'camera' (vaulted room, chamber).
