admiralty

1. NOUN

/ ˈæd.mɚ.əl.ti / (AmE), / ˈæd.mɪ.rəl.ti / (BrE)
the rank, office, or authority of an admiral
the rank or office of an admiral

Examples

After years of service, he finally achieved the admiralty.
The admiralty decided to increase naval patrols in the region.
Disputes about promotions were settled by the admiralty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admiral - a high-ranking naval officer

Etymology

From Latin admiralitas, from Old French amiralté, from Arabic amīr (commander).

2. NOUN

/ ˈædmɚˌælti / (AmE), / ˈædmɪrəlti / (BrE)
the government department responsible for the command and administration of a navy, especially in Great Britain
department in charge of a navy

Examples

The British Admiralty played a crucial role during the Second World War.
He submitted the plans for the new vessel to the Admiralty for approval.
Naval officers were often appointed by the Admiralty in the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: navy - the branch of a country's armed forces that fights at sea

Etymology

From Latin 'admiralis' (commander) via Old French 'amiral' (admiral).
