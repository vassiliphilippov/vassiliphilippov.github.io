Canis lupus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.nɪs ˈluː.pəs / (AmE), / ˈka.nɪs ˈluː.pʊs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the gray wolf, a large wild canine native to forested regions of North America, Eurasia, and North Africa
scientific name for the gray wolf

Examples

The scientific community refers to the gray wolf as Canis lupus in official documents.
Research on Canis lupus has provided valuable insights into pack behavior and ecosystem dynamics.
While hiking in Alaska, we spotted tracks believed to belong to Canis lupus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wolf - a large wild carnivorous mammal of the genus Canis, especially Canis lupus

Etymology

From Latin 'canis' (dog) + 'lupus' (wolf).
