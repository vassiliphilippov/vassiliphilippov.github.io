earthworm

1. NOUN

/ ˈɝːθˌwɝːm / (AmE), / ˈɜːθˌwɜːm / (BrE)
A small, elongated, terrestrial worm that burrows into soil, helping to aerate it, and is commonly seen after rain or used as bait by anglers.
a type of soil-dwelling worm

Examples

After the heavy rain, several earthworms crawled onto the sidewalk.
Gardeners appreciate earthworms because they help to keep the soil healthy.
He dug up an earthworm to use as fishing bait.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worm - a soft-bodied, legless invertebrate

Etymology

Formed from 'earth' + 'worm', referring to its habitat and organism type.
