ruralism

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʊr.əˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈrʊə.rə.lɪz.əm / (BrE)
a word, expression, or usage that is characteristic of rural areas or country life
expression typical of rural areas

Examples

The novel is full of ruralisms that capture the flavor of country speech.
He collected ruralisms from different regions for his linguistic research.
Some ruralisms can sound quaint or unfamiliar to people from cities.

Etymology

From rural + -ism, patterned after terms like 'regionalism'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrʊr.əˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈrʊə.rəˌlɪz.əm / (BrE)
the characteristic or quality of being rural; rural traits or attitudes
rural characteristic or trait

Examples

The ruralism of the landscape was evident in its open fields and scattered farmhouses.
Critics say that his writing displays a certain ruralism that romanticizes country life.
There is an increasing interest in ruralism as people seek alternatives to urban living.

Synset Examples

a place with the rurality of a turnip field

Related Terms

  • Antonym: urbanism - the characteristic or quality of being urban; urban traits or attitudes

Etymology

From 'rural' (from Latin 'ruralis', country) + '-ism' (denoting a practice or characteristic).

3. NOUN

/ ˈrʊrəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈrʊərəlɪzəm / (BrE)
The advocacy or idealization of rural life and values, often in preference to urban living.
advocacy of rural life over urbanism

Examples

The novelist's work displays a clear ruralism, prioritizing country values and traditions over city life.
An increase in ruralism can be seen as people seek simplicity away from urban environments.
Her essays often critique modernity through the lens of ruralism and traditional farm life.

Synset Examples

By ‘ruralism’ I mean the glorification of country life, and a dissatisfaction with urbanism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: urbanism - The way of life characteristic of cities; advocacy of urban living

Etymology

Formed from 'rural' (from Latin 'ruralis', relating to the countryside) and '-ism'.
