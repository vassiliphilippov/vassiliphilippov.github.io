Vaccinium

1. NOUN

/ vækˈsɪniəm / (AmE), / vækˈsɪniəm / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen or deciduous shrubs in the Northern Hemisphere, including cranberries and blueberries, known for their berry fruits.
genus of berry-producing shrubs (cranberries, blueberries, etc.)

Examples

Many species within the Vaccinium genus are cultivated for their edible berries.
The Vaccinium plants thrive in acidic soils common in northern regions.
Blueberry, a popular fruit, is a member of the Vaccinium genus.

Etymology

From Latin 'vaccinium', a type of berry; possibly related to the Latin 'bacca' (berry).

2. NOUN

/ vækˈsɪniəm / (AmE), / vækˈsɪniəm / (BrE)
a genus of shrubs in the heath family, including blueberries, cranberries, and related berry-bearing plants, native to the Northern Hemisphere.
genus of berry-bearing shrubs (blueberries, cranberries)

Examples

Many wild berries, such as those from the Vaccinium genus, are prized for their flavor and health benefits.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Vaccinium group in the northern forests.
Cranberries and blueberries, both members of the Vaccinium genus, are popular ingredients in many recipes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blueberry - an edible blue or purple berry from shrubs in the genus Vaccinium
  • Synonym: cranberry - a small, red, sour berry from shrubs in the genus Vaccinium

Etymology

From Latin *Vaccinium* (name of a plant, possibly bilberry).
