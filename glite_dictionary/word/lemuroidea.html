Lemuroidea

1. NOUN

/ lɛˌmjʊəˈrɔɪdiə / (AmE), / lɛˌmjʊəˈrɔɪdiə / (BrE)
A former superfamily in primate classification that includes lemurs and related species, sometimes used as an alternative to or subdivision of Prosimii.
superfamily of lemurs and related primates

Examples

The extinct species belongs to the Lemuroidea, which includes all modern lemurs.
Taxonomists once classified many Malagasy primates under Lemuroidea.
Some zoological texts list Lemuroidea as a superfamily within the primate order.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Prosimii - suborder of primates including lemurs, lorises, and tarsiers

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'lemur' and the suffix '-oidea' meaning 'like, resembling' or 'superfamily'.
