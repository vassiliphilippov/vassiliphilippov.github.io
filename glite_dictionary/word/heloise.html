Heloise

1. PROPN

/ ˌheɪ.ˈloʊ.iz / (AmE), / ˈhɛl.ə.wiːz / (BrE)
(c. 1098–1164) French scholar best known for her relationship with Peter Abelard as his student, mistress, and later wife.
French scholar, wife of Abelard

Examples

Many consider Heloise to be one of the most learned women of the Middle Ages.
The letters exchanged between Abelard and Heloise reveal deep intellectual and emotional connections.
Heloise's reputation as a scholar endures to this day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Peter Abelard - French philosopher, theologian, and lover of Heloise

Etymology

From French Héloïse, of uncertain origin, possibly from Germanic Helewidis.
