Pomoxis nigromaculatus

1. NOUN

/ poʊˌmɒksɪs ˌnaɪɡroʊˌmækjʊˈleɪtəs / (AmE), / pəˌmɒksɪs ˌnɪɡrəʊˌmakjʊˈleɪtəs / (BrE)
A species of freshwater fish known as the black crappie, native to North America and recognized for its dark, speckled appearance.
species of freshwater fish (black crappie)

Examples

Anglers often seek Pomoxis nigromaculatus for its delicate flavor and sporting qualities.
The distribution of Pomoxis nigromaculatus spans much of the eastern and central United States.
Scientists conducted a population survey of Pomoxis nigromaculatus in several regional lakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black crappie - common name for Pomoxis nigromaculatus, a freshwater fish species

Etymology

From New Latin; genus Pomoxis and species epithet nigromaculatus meaning 'black-spotted'.
