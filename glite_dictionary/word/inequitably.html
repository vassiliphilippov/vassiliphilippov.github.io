inequitably

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈɛkwətəbli / (AmE), / ɪnˈɛkwɪtəbli / (BrE)
In a manner that is not fair or just; unfairly.
in an unfair or unjust manner

Examples

The bonus was distributed inequitably among the employees, causing resentment.
Land and resources are often inequitably shared in impoverished regions.
She felt she was treated inequitably compared to her peers.

Synset Examples

their father's possessions were inequitably divided among the sons

Related Terms

  • Antonym: equitably - in a fair or just manner

Etymology

From in- (not) + equitable + -ly.
