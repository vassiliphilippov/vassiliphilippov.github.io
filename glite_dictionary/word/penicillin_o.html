penicillin O

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛnɪˈsɪlɪn oʊ / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˈsɪlɪn əʊ / (BrE)
a type of penicillin antibiotic similar in action to penicillin G but produced differently by certain strains of fungi
penicillin antibiotic type
medical

Examples

New research suggests that penicillin O may be effective against certain bacteria resistant to other antibiotics.
The patient was treated with penicillin O due to an allergy to penicillin G.
Laboratories often use penicillin O in comparative studies of antibiotic activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penicillin G - a widely used form of penicillin antibiotic

Etymology

Derived from ‘penicillin’ (from the fungus Penicillium) and the letter ‘O’ as a designation.
