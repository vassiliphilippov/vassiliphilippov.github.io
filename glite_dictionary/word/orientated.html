orientated

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔːr.i.ənˈteɪ.t̬ɪd / (AmE), / ˌɔː.ri.ənˈteɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
adjusted or positioned in relation to surroundings, circumstances, or a specific purpose; often used in combination (e.g., value-orientated)
adjusted in relation to surroundings or purpose
british

Examples

The new employee quickly became orientated to the company's workflow.
This training program is heavily skills-orientated to prepare students for the job market.
Our discussion was more environmentally orientated than last year's meeting.

Synset Examples

the house had its large windows oriented toward the ocean view
helping freshmen become oriented to college life
the book is value-oriented throughout

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oriented - adjusted or positioned in relation to surroundings; standard American form

Etymology

Formed from 'orientate' (variant of 'orient') + '-ed' (past participle suffix), influenced by 'orient' from Latin 'orient-' (rising, east).
