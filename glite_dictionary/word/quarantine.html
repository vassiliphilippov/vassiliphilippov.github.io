quarantine

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔːrənˌtin / (AmE), / ˈkwɒrəntiːn / (BrE)
a period or state of isolation in which people or animals that have been exposed to infectious disease are separated from others to prevent its spread
isolation to stop disease spread

Examples

After returning from travel, she had to spend two weeks in quarantine to ensure she wasn't carrying the virus.
The dog was placed in quarantine after showing symptoms of rabies.
Strict quarantine measures helped contain the outbreak in the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isolation - the process or fact of separating someone or something from others

Etymology

From Italian 'quaranta giorni' (forty days), the period ships were kept isolated during plague outbreaks.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔːrənˌtin / (AmE), / ˈkwɒr.ənˌtiːn / (BrE)
a period or state of enforced isolation to prevent the spread of infectious disease
enforced isolation to prevent disease spread

Examples

During the SARS outbreak, many cities enforced a strict quarantine to limit infections.
After exposure to the virus, she was placed in quarantine for two weeks as a precaution.
Officials lifted the quarantine only when all test results came back negative.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isolation - the state of being separated from others, especially to prevent disease spread

Etymology

From Italian 'quaranta giorni' (forty days), the period ships were isolated to prevent the spread of plague.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14438739-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈkwɔːr.ənˌtin / (AmE), / ˈkwɒr.ənˌtiːn / (BrE)
to put a person, animal, or place in enforced isolation to prevent the spread of disease
to place in enforced medical isolation

Examples

They decided to quarantine the entire farm after the outbreak was confirmed.
Travelers from affected countries were required to quarantine upon arrival.
Authorities may quarantine your pet if it shows signs of illness.

Synset Examples

My dog was quarantined before he could live in England

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to isolate - to separate from others to prevent spread of disease

Etymology

From Italian 'quaranta giorni' (forty days), referring to the period ships were isolated to prevent plague spread.
