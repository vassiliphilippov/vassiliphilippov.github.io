cigarette

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪɡəˈrɛt / (AmE), / ˌsɪɡəˈrɛt / (BrE)
A thin cylinder of finely cut tobacco wrapped in paper for smoking.
thin roll of tobacco for smoking

Examples

He lit a cigarette and leaned back in his chair.
The restaurant has a strict no cigarette smoking policy.
She quit smoking cigarettes three years ago and feels much healthier.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cigar - a thicker roll of tobacco wrapped in tobacco leaf for smoking

Etymology

From French 'cigarette', diminutive of 'cigare' (cigar), from Spanish 'cigarro'.
