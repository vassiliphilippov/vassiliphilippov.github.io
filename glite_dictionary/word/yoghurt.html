yoghurt

1. NOUN

/ ˈjoʊɡərt / (AmE), / ˈjɒɡət / (BrE)
A thick, creamy food made by fermenting milk with bacterial cultures, often eaten plain or flavored.
fermented milk food

Examples

She prefers to eat plain yoghurt with a drizzle of honey for breakfast.
Many Indian dishes use yoghurt to add creaminess and flavor.
Greek yoghurt is known for its thick texture and high protein content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yogurt - alternative spelling for yoghurt (American English)

Etymology

From Turkish 'yoğurt', meaning fermented milk.
