Triglochin

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈɡloʊkɪn / (AmE), / trɪˈɡlɒkɪn / (BrE)
A genus of perennial or annual bog or marsh plants in the family Juncaginaceae, commonly known as arrow grass.
genus of marsh plants (arrow grass)

Examples

Triglochin thrives in wetlands and can often be found growing alongside reeds and sedges.
Botanists studied several species of Triglochin to understand their adaptation to saline environments.
The presence of Triglochin indicates a healthy, undisturbed marsh ecosystem.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'tri-' (three) and 'glochis' (point, barb).
