Coreopsis maritima

1. NOUN

/ kɔːˈɹiː.əp.sɪs ˌmær.ɪˈtiː.mə / (AmE), / kɒːˈɹiː.əp.sɪs ˌmær.ɪˈtiː.mə / (BrE)
A species of stout herbaceous plant with single yellow flowers on each stalk, cultivated as an ornamental and originally native to Mexico.
ornamental plant species native to Mexico

Examples

The coastal garden featured several Coreopsis maritima blooming in the spring sunshine.
She planted Coreopsis maritima to add a splash of yellow to her flower beds.
Coreopsis maritima thrives in well-drained soil and is popular among gardeners for its cheerful appearance.

Etymology

From the genus name Coreopsis (from Greek koris 'bedbug' + opsis 'view', referring to the seed shape) and maritima from Latin, meaning 'of the sea' or 'coastal'.
