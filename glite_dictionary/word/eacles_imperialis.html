Eacles imperialis

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːkliːz ˌɪm.pɪˈriː.ə.lɪs / (AmE), / ˈiːkliːz ˌɪm.pɪˈrɪə.lɪs / (BrE)
A large North American moth with yellow wings marked with purplish or brownish spots; its larvae feed on trees such as maple and pine.
a large North American moth species

Examples

The caterpillars of the Eacles imperialis are often found on pine and maple trees.
Naturalists were excited to spot an adult Eacles imperialis in the region last summer.
The striking coloration of the Eacles imperialis makes it a favorite among moth enthusiasts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imperial moth - common name for Eacles imperialis

Etymology

Genus name from Greek Eakles; species from Latin imperialis meaning 'imperial'.
