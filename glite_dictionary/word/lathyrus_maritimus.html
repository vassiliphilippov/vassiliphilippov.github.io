Lathyrus maritimus

1. NOUN

/ ləˈθaɪərəs məˈrɪtɪməs / (AmE), / læˈθɪərəs məˈrɪtɪməs / (BrE)
A species of wild pea found on seashores in the north temperate zone, characterized by tough roots, purple flowers, and often used to stabilize sand dunes.
wild pea of northern coastal areas

Examples

Lathyrus maritimus thrives in sandy coastal habitats, helping to prevent erosion with its strong root system.
Botanists surveyed the spread of Lathyrus maritimus along the northern seashore after recent storms.
The distinctive purple flowers of Lathyrus maritimus attract many pollinators during the early summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beach pea - common name for Lathyrus maritimus

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Lathyrus' (wild pea) and species name 'maritimus' (of the sea, coastal).
