porcine

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɔr.saɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɔː.saɪn / (BrE)
Resembling or characteristic of a pig, especially in being gluttonous or greedy.
resembling a pig, gluttonous

Examples

Her porcine appetite shocked everyone at the dinner table.
He gave a porcine snort when someone mentioned chocolate cake.
The critic described the tycoon's porcine greed as insatiable.

Synset Examples

piggish table manners
the piggy fat-cheeked little boy and his porcine pot-bellied father
swinish slavering over food

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piggish - resembling a pig or being greedy and coarse
  • Synonym: swinish - resembling or characteristic of swine; coarse or brutish

Etymology

From Latin porcinus (of a pig), from porcus (pig).
