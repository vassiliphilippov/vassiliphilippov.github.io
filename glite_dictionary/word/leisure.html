leisure

1. NOUN

/ ˈliʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɛʒə / (BrE)
time when one is not working or occupied and can choose how to spend it, especially in enjoyable activities.
free time for enjoyment

Examples

I finally have some leisure to read my favorite books this weekend.
During his leisure, he enjoys hiking and painting landscapes.
The resort offers a variety of leisure activities for its guests.

Synset Examples

he lacked the leisure for golf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: free time - time when you are not required to work or perform duties
  • Antonym: work - activity involving mental or physical effort, often for pay or duty

Etymology

From Old French *leisir*, from Latin *licēre* (to be allowed).

2. NOUN

/ ˈliʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɛʒə / (BrE)
time when one is not working or occupied; free time used for relaxation or enjoyment
free time for relaxation

Examples

After finishing her chores, she enjoyed a quiet afternoon of leisure with a good book.
Many people travel during their leisure to relax and explore new places.
Modern technology has increased the amount of leisure people have in their daily lives.

Synset Examples

his job left him little leisure

Related Terms

  • Antonym: work - activity involving mental or physical effort for a purpose

Etymology

From Old French *leisire* (capacity or opportunity), from Latin *licēre* (to be permitted).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15162628-n
Synset POS: n
