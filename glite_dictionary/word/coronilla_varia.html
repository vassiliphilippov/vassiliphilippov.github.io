Coronilla varia

1. NOUN

/ kɔrəˌnɪlə ˈvɛriə / (AmE), / kɒrəˌnɪlə ˈvɛərɪə / (BrE)
A European perennial herb with pink-and-white flower clusters and sharp-angled pods, also known as crown vetch, and naturalized in parts of North America.
European plant with pink-white flower clusters

Examples

Coronilla varia is often planted to prevent soil erosion along highways.
The pink and white blossoms of Coronilla varia appear in late spring and last through summer.
Gardeners appreciate Coronilla varia for its ability to thrive in poor soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crown vetch - common name for Coronilla varia, a plant with pink-and-white flower clusters

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Coronilla' meaning 'little crown', referring to the flower clusters, and 'varia' meaning 'varied'.
