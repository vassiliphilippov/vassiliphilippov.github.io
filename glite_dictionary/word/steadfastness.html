steadfastness

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛdˌfæstnəs / (AmE), / ˈstɛdˌfɑːstnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being very firm, determined, and unwavering in purpose or loyalty
firm and unwavering determination

Examples

His steadfastness in the face of adversity inspired everyone around him.
She admired the steadfastness with which he pursued his goals.
Despite criticism, his steadfastness never wavered throughout the campaign.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resolve - firm determination
  • Synonym: determination - the quality of being determined
  • Antonym: wavering - the act of being indecisive or unsteady

Etymology

From Middle English steadfast, from Old English stedefæst ('firmly fixed').

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɛdˌfæst.nəs / (AmE), / ˈstɛd.fɑːst.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being firmly loyal and unwavering, especially in the face of trouble or difficulty
firm and unwavering loyalty

Examples

Her steadfastness during the crisis earned her the admiration of everyone at work.
Through years of hardship, his steadfastness to his family never wavered.
The team's victory was a result of their steadfastness and determination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fidelity - faithfulness to a person, cause, or belief
  • Synonym: loyalty - the quality of being loyal
  • Antonym: fickleness - the quality of being changeable or unsteady in loyalty

Etymology

From Old English 'stedefæst' (firmly fixed, steadfast) + -ness.
