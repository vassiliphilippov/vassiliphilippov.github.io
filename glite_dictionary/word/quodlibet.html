quodlibet

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑːdləˌbɛt / (AmE), / ˈkwɒdlɪbɛt / (BrE)
A topic or issue raised for formal debate, especially in medieval scholastic philosophy or theology.
issue for formal academic debate

Examples

During the medieval university session, a quodlibet allowed students to challenge renowned scholars with any question they wished.
The theologian prepared thoroughly for the quodlibet, anticipating both expected and surprising inquiries on doctrine.
Such an academic quodlibet often tested the intellectual agility of even the most seasoned professors.

Etymology

From Latin 'quodlibet', meaning 'whatever you please', from 'quod' (what) + 'libet' (it pleases).
