Hymenophyllum

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌmɛnəˈfɪliəm / (AmE), / hɪˌmiːnəˈfɪliəm / (BrE)
A genus of delicate ferns known as filmy ferns, characterized by thin, translucent fronds; the type genus of the family Hymenophyllaceae.
a genus of filmy ferns

Examples

The species in the genus Hymenophyllum are often found in moist rainforest habitats.
Hymenophyllum ferns are known for their extremely thin, almost transparent fronds.
Botanists discovered a new Hymenophyllum species growing on the shaded rocks near the waterfall.

Etymology

From Greek hymēn (membrane) + phyllon (leaf), referring to the ferns' thin, membranous leaves.
