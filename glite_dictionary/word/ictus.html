ictus

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪk.təs / (AmE), / ˈɪk.təs / (BrE)
a sudden occurrence or attack of a disease, especially one such as a stroke or seizure
sudden occurrence of a disease
medical

Examples

After the initial ictus, the patient was rushed to the emergency room.
Doctors monitored her closely after she experienced a cardiac ictus during the procedure.
A second ictus occurred unexpectedly, complicating his recovery process.

Synset Examples

he suffered an epileptic seizure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seizure - a sudden attack of illness, especially a stroke or an epileptic fit

Etymology

From Latin 'ictus' (a blow, a stroke).
