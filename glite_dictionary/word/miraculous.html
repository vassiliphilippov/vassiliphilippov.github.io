miraculous

1. ADJ

/ məˈrækjələs / (AmE), / mɪˈrækjʊləs / (BrE)
Strangely fortunate or timely; seeming to happen as if by divine intervention or an extraordinary force.
timely or fortunate as if by a miracle

Examples

After months of drought, a miraculous downpour brought relief to the farmers.
Her recovery from the illness was considered miraculous by the doctors.
It was a miraculous escape, with everyone surviving the accident unharmed.

Synset Examples

a heaven-sent rain saved the crops
a providential recovery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortunate - receiving good fortune, often unexpectedly
  • Synonym: providential - happening at a favorable time; opportune, as if by divine intervention
  • Antonym: unlucky - not fortunate; marked by bad luck

Etymology

From Latin miraculosus (wonderful), from miraculum (miracle).

2. ADJ

/ mɪˈrækjələs / (AmE), / mɪˈrækjələs / (BrE)
very surprising or extraordinary, as if caused by a miracle; having the nature of a miracle.
extraordinary, like a miracle

Examples

The rescue of the trapped miners was truly miraculous and amazed everyone around the world.
Doctors described her recovery from the illness as miraculous, given the seriousness of her condition.
After the storm, the survival of the small cottage seemed almost miraculous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wonderful - extremely good or impressive
  • Antonym: ordinary - normal; not remarkable

Etymology

From Latin 'miraculosus' (full of wonder), from 'miraculum' (miracle).
