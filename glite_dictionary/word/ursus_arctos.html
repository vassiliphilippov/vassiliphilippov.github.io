Ursus arctos

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrsəs ˈɑrktəs / (AmE), / ˈɜːsəs ˈɑːktɒs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the brown bear, a large, powerful bear species found in Eurasia and North America
the brown bear (scientific name)

Examples

Scientists discovered new behavior patterns in the Ursus arctos population living in the Carpathian mountains.
The Ursus arctos has one of the widest distributions of any bear species in the world.
Researchers often use the term Ursus arctos when referring to the brown bear in academic publications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brown bear - large brown bear species native to Eurasia and North America

Etymology

From Latin: 'ursus' meaning 'bear' and 'arctos' from Greek 'arktos' meaning 'bear'.
