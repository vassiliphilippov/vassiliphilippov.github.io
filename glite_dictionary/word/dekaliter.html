dekaliter

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛkəˌliːtər / (AmE), / ˈdɛkəliːtə / (BrE)
A metric unit of volume equal to 10 liters.
metric unit of 10 liters

Examples

The large container held exactly one dekaliter of water for the experiment.
Wine is sometimes sold in bulk in dekaliters across European vineyards.
To prepare the solution, measure out a dekaliter of distilled water.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: liter - a metric unit of capacity equal to one cubic decimeter
  • Degree Higher: hectoliter - a metric unit of volume equal to 100 liters

Etymology

From French 'décalitre', from Greek 'deka' (ten) + French 'litre'.
