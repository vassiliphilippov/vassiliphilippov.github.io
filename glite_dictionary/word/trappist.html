Trappist

1. NOUN

/ ˈtræp.ɪst / (AmE), / ˈtræp.ɪst / (BrE)
a member of a Roman Catholic monastic order, the Cistercians of the Strict Observance, known for austerity and a vow of silence
member of austere Catholic monastic order

Examples

The Trappist monks devote much of their day to prayer and manual labor.
After visiting the abbey, she became fascinated by the Trappist way of life.
A Trappist rarely speaks, following the tradition of strict silence.

Etymology

From La Trappe, a monastery in France where the order originated.
