lover

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌvɚ / (AmE), / ˈlʌvə / (BrE)
A person who is very enthusiastic about or strongly admires something; an ardent follower or devotee.
enthusiastic admirer or devotee

Examples

As a lover of classical music, she attends concerts whenever she can.
The exhibition was a delight for every art lover in the city.
Any lover of books will appreciate the library’s rare collection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthusiast - a person who is very interested in a particular activity or subject
  • Synonym: fan - a person who has a strong interest in or admiration for something

Etymology

From Old English 'lufere', from 'lufian' (to love).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlʌvər / (AmE), / ˈlʌvə / (BrE)
a person who is in a romantic or sexual relationship with someone
romantic or sexual partner

Examples

She met her new lover at a poetry reading downtown.
After years apart, the old lovers were reunited by chance.
He introduced his lover to the rest of his family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partner - one who is in a relationship with another person
  • Synonym: spouse - a husband or wife; a married person
  • Antonym: enemy - a person who is actively opposed or hostile

Etymology

From Old English lufian (to love) + -er (agent suffix).

3. NOUN

/ ˈlʌvər / (AmE), / ˈlʌvə / (BrE)
a person with whom one has a romantic or sexual relationship but is not married to
romantic or sexual partner (not married)

Examples

She introduced her lover to her friends at the party.
For years, the poet wrote secret letters to his lover.
After their marriage ended, he found comfort in a new lover.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partner - a person with whom one shares a romantic relationship
  • Antonym: spouse - a husband or wife in a marriage

Etymology

From Old English lufere, related to 'love'.
