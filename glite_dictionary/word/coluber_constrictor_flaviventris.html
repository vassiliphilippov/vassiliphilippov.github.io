Coluber constrictor flaviventris

1. NOUN

/ kəˈluːbər kənˈstrɪktər ˌfleɪvɪˈvɛntrɪs / (AmE), / kəˈluːbə kənˈstrɪktə ˌfleɪvɪˈvɛntrɪs / (BrE)
a bluish-green subspecies of racer snake with a yellow belly, found in the United States from Ohio to Texas
bluish-green snake (yellow-bellied racer) of central U.S.

Examples

The Coluber constrictor flaviventris is often seen swiftly moving through open fields in the Midwest.
Many herpetologists study the habits of the Coluber constrictor flaviventris because of its wide distribution.
The Coluber constrictor flaviventris is known for its speed and distinctive yellow underside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eastern yellow-bellied racer - common name for Coluber constrictor flaviventris
  • Degree Higher: racer - a common name for snakes in the genus Coluber

Etymology

From Latin: Coluber (snake), constrictor (one that constricts), flaviventris (yellow-bellied).
