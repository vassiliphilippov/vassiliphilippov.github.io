difficult

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɪfɪˌkʌlt / (AmE), / ˈdɪfɪkəlt / (BrE)
needing much effort or skill to accomplish, deal with, or understand; not easy
not easy; requiring much effort

Examples

The exam was so difficult that very few students passed on the first try.
Climbing the steep mountain proved to be a difficult challenge for the hikers.
She found it difficult to explain the concept to someone unfamiliar with the subject.

Synset Examples

a difficult task
nesting places on the cliffs are difficult of access
difficult times
why is it so hard for you to keep a secret?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: easy - not difficult; requiring little effort
  • Synonym: hard - requiring a lot of effort; not easy

Etymology

From Latin difficilis (hard to do).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɪfɪkəlt / (AmE), / ˈdɪfɪkəlt / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) not easy to control, manage, or deal with; uncooperative or troublesome.
not easy to control or manage

Examples

Their son can be quite difficult when he doesn't get his way.
She found herself in a difficult class where the students refused to listen.
The horse became difficult to control as the storm grew louder.

Synset Examples

a difficult child
an unmanageable situation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unmanageable - not able to be controlled or handled successfully
  • Antonym: manageable - able to be controlled or handled easily

Etymology

From Latin difficilis (hard to do, troublesome), from dis- (not) + facilis (easy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01478425-a
Synset POS: a
