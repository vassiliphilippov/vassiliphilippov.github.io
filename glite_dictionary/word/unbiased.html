unbiased

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈbaɪəst / (AmE), / ʌnˈbaɪəst / (BrE)
showing no prejudice or favoritism; impartial or fair.
impartial; showing no favoritism

Examples

The judge gave an unbiased opinion, ensuring a fair trial for everyone involved.
We need an unbiased report to understand what truly happened in the meeting.
Journalists strive to be unbiased when covering political events.

Synset Examples

a properly indifferent jury
an unbiased account of her family problems

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impartial - treating all rivals or disputants equally; fair and just
  • Antonym: biased - unfairly prejudiced for or against someone or something

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + biased (having or showing bias).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈbaɪəst / (AmE), / ʌnˈbaɪəst / (BrE)
not influenced by personal feelings or opinions; impartial and fair.
impartial; not influenced by bias

Examples

The judge made an unbiased decision based solely on the evidence presented.
Journalists strive to provide unbiased reporting on current events.
We need an unbiased opinion to resolve the dispute fairly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impartial - treating all rivals or disputants equally
  • Antonym: biased - showing an unreasonable preference or prejudice

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'biased'.
