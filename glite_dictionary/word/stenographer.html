stenographer

1. NOUN

/ stəˈnɑɡrəfər / (AmE), / stəˈnɒɡrəfə / (BrE)
a person trained to transcribe spoken words quickly and accurately, especially using shorthand, often in legal or official settings
person who transcribes speech using shorthand

Examples

The stenographer accurately recorded every word spoken during the trial.
As a stenographer, she typed dictation at remarkable speeds using shorthand.
Many courts rely on a skilled stenographer to keep official transcripts of proceedings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transcriber - a person who converts speech or data into written form
  • Synonym: court reporter - a person whose job is to transcribe spoken or recorded speech in court proceedings

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'stenos' (narrow) + 'graphein' (to write); via French 'sténographe'.
