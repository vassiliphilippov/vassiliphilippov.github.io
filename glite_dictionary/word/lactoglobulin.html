lactoglobulin

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæktəˈɡlɑbjələn / (AmE), / ˌlæktəˈɡlɒbjʊlɪn / (BrE)
a globular protein found in the whey of milk, especially cow's milk
protein found in milk whey
medical

Examples

Bovine lactoglobulin is often studied in dairy research laboratories.
People with milk allergies may react to lactoglobulin present in dairy products.
The nutrition label indicated the presence of lactoglobulin among the ingredients.

Synset Examples

Bovine lactoglobulin consists of a chain of 162 amino acids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whey protein - proteins found in the liquid part of milk after curdling

Etymology

From Latin 'lac, lactis' (milk) + 'globulin', a type of protein.
