Allium tricoccum

1. NOUN

/ ˈæliəm traɪˈkɒkəm / (AmE), / ˈæliəm trɪˈkɒkəm / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant with a slender bulb and whitish flowers, commonly known as ramps, valued for its edible leaves and bulbs.
North American wild leek (ramps)

Examples

In Appalachian cuisine, Allium tricoccum is often foraged in early spring for its distinctive flavor.
The woods were filled with patches of Allium tricoccum during the short harvesting season.
Scientists are studying Allium tricoccum to better understand its ecological role in forest ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ramp - North American wild onion, another common name for Allium tricoccum

Etymology

From Latin; 'Allium' meaning garlic, and 'tricoccum' referring to three-seeded (fruit capsules).
