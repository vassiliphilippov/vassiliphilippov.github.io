annexa

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɛk.sə / (AmE), / əˈnɛk.sə / (BrE)
Accessory or adjoining anatomical parts or appendages to an organ, especially in medical or embryological contexts.
accessory anatomical parts or appendages
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the annexa of the patient’s uterus during the ultrasound.
Inflammation of the annexa can sometimes lead to serious complications if not treated promptly.
Medical students must learn to identify the different annexa associated with each organ system.

Synset Examples

Fallopian tubes and ovaries are adnexa of the uterus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adnexa - accessory anatomical parts attached to an organ

Etymology

From Latin annexus (tied or connected to).
