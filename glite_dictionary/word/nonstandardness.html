nonstandardness

1. NOUN

/ nɑnˈstændərdnəs / (AmE), / nɒnˈstændədnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of not conforming to a standard; lack of standardization.
quality of being nonstandard

Examples

The nonstandardness of the research methods was questioned by the review panel.
His essay was criticized for the nonstandardness of its language.
The committee discussed ways to address the nonstandardness in their processes.

Synset Examples

At neither age did nonstandardness of dialect or accent cause a problem for teacher-pupil communication, so it is unlikely that it was in any direct manner a cause of the later associated lower attainment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: standardness - the quality of being standard or conforming to a standard

Etymology

Formed from 'nonstandard' + suffix '-ness'.
