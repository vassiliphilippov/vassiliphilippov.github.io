to delude

1. VERB

/ dɪˈlud / (AmE), / dɪˈluːd / (BrE)
to make someone believe something that is not true; to mislead or deceive someone
mislead or deceive

Examples

He tried to delude his investors into thinking the company was profitable.
She refused to delude herself about the reality of the situation.
Politicians often delude the public with empty promises.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deceive - to cause someone to believe something that is not true
  • Synonym: to mislead - to give someone the wrong idea or impression
  • Antonym: disillusion - to cause someone to realize their belief is false

Etymology

From Latin 'deludere', from 'de-' (down, away) + 'ludere' (to play, mock).
