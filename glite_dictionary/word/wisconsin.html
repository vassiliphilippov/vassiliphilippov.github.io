Wisconsin

1. PROPN

/ wɪˈskɑnsɪn / (AmE), / wɪˈskɒnsɪn / (BrE)
a state in the north-central United States, located in the Midwest region, known for its lakes and dairy industry.
a state in the Midwest region of the U.S.
american

Examples

She grew up in Wisconsin and often talks about the cold winters there.
The cheese produced in Wisconsin is famous throughout the United States.
Our family plans to visit Wisconsin next summer to explore its lakes and forests.

Etymology

From the French adaptation of a Native American (Miami or Algonquian) word, possibly meaning 'river running through a red place'.

2. NOUN

/ wɪsˈkɑnsən / (AmE), / wɪsˈkɒnsɪn / (BrE)
a major river in central Wisconsin, United States, which is a tributary of the Mississippi River
river in Wisconsin (U.S.); tributary of the Mississippi

Examples

The Wisconsin flows south across the state before joining the Mississippi River.
Many canoeists enjoy exploring the scenic waters of the Wisconsin each summer.
Major flooding occurred along the Wisconsin after several days of heavy rain.

Etymology

From an Algonquian word, possibly Ojibwe 'Meskousing', adapted by French explorers.
