quivering

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪvərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkwɪvərɪŋ / (BrE)
A trembling or shaking movement; the act or state of quivering.
a trembling or shaking movement

Examples

A barely perceptible quivering ran through the leaves as the wind picked up.
She felt a quivering in her voice as she began to speak.
The sudden quivering of his hands revealed his nervousness.

Synset Examples

the shaking of his fingers as he lit his pipe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trembling - a shaking movement caused by nervousness, excitement, or cold
  • Synonym: shaking - an act of moving repeatedly back and forth or up and down
  • Antonym: stillness - absence of movement or motion

Etymology

From the verb 'quiver', from Middle English *quiveren* (to tremble).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪvərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkwɪvərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of shaking with a slight, rapid motion; a trembling or vibrating movement
trembling or vibrating movement

Examples

The quivering of her hands was barely noticeable, but it betrayed her nervousness.
A slight quivering ran through the leaves as the wind passed.
He felt a quivering in his voice when he spoke to the large audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trembling - a shaking or quivering motion
  • Synonym: vibration - a rapid movement back and forth

Etymology

From verb 'quiver' (to shake, tremble) + -ing.
