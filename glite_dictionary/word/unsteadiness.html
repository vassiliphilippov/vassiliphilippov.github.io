unsteadiness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈstɛdinəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈstɛdinəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being not steady, stable, or securely fixed in place; lack of firmness or stability.
lack of steadiness or stability

Examples

After the accident, the patient complained of unsteadiness when trying to walk.
The ladder's unsteadiness made it dangerous to climb.
There was a noticeable unsteadiness in his voice during the speech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instability - the state of being likely to change or fail; lack of stability
  • Antonym: steadiness - the quality of being steady, stable, or firmly fixed

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'steadiness' (from 'steady').

2. NOUN

/ ʌnˈstɛdinəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈstɛdinɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being not steady; liable to change, shake, or vary unexpectedly
the quality of being unsteady or unpredictable

Examples

The unsteadiness in her voice revealed her nervousness.
Engineers worried about the unsteadiness of the platform during strong winds.
Excessive alcohol consumption may cause unsteadiness when walking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instability - lack of stability or firmness
  • Antonym: steadiness - the quality of being steady or stable

Etymology

Formed from 'unsteady' + '-ness'. 'Unsteady' comes from Old English 'unstædig'.
