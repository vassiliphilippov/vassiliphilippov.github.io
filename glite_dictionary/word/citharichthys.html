Citharichthys

1. NOUN

/ sɪθəˈrɪkθɪs / (AmE), / sɪθəˈrɪkθɪs / (BrE)
A genus of flatfish in the family Bothidae, including several species commonly known as large-tooth flounders, sanddabs, and whiffs.
a genus of flatfish in the family Bothidae

Examples

The genus Citharichthys includes many flatfish found along the Pacific and Atlantic coasts.
Researchers collected several Citharichthys specimens during the marine survey.
Many species within Citharichthys are important to local fisheries.

Etymology

From Greek kithara (lyre) + ichthys (fish), referring to the fish's body shape.

2. PROPN

/ sɪˌθærɪkθɪs / (AmE), / sɪˌθarɪkθɪs / (BrE)
A genus of flatfishes in the family Bothidae, which includes large-tooth flounders, sanddabs, whiffs, and related species, found primarily in the Americas.
a genus of flatfishes (Bothidae family)

Examples

The species Citharichthys stigmaeus is commonly known as the speckled sanddab.
Researchers studied the distribution of Citharichthys along the Pacific coast.
Several members of the genus Citharichthys are important to commercial fisheries.

Etymology

From Greek 'kithara' (guitar) + 'ichthys' (fish), referring to the shape of some species.
