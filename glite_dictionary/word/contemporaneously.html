contemporaneously

1. ADV

/ kənˌtɛm.pəˈreɪ.ni.əs.li / (AmE), / kənˌtɛm.pəˈreɪ.ni.əs.li / (BrE)
at the same time; during the same period
at the same time

Examples

The two artists developed their styles contemporaneously but in different countries.
Multiple inventions appeared contemporaneously across Europe during the Industrial Revolution.
The reforms were implemented contemporaneously in both regions despite separate governments.

Synset Examples

contemporaneously, or possibly a little later, there developed a great Sumerian civilisation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simultaneously - at the same time

Etymology

From Latin 'contemporaneus' (contemporary) + '-ly'.
