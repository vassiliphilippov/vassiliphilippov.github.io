moldboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊldˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈməʊldˌbɔːd / (BrE)
the curved metal blade on a plow that turns over the soil
curved blade of a plow

Examples

The farmer replaced the old moldboard on his plow to improve its efficiency.
A properly set moldboard turns the soil evenly and prepares the field for planting.
The moldboard and share work together to slice and flip the soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plowshare - the cutting blade of a plow

Etymology

From 'mold' (soil, earth) + 'board' (plank, flat piece), referring to the board that turns the earth.
