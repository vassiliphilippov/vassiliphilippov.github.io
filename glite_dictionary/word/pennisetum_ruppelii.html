Pennisetum ruppelii

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛn.ɪˈsiː.təm ˌruːˈpɛl.i.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌpɛn.ɪˈsiː.təm ˌrʊˈpɛl.iː / (BrE)
A tall perennial ornamental grass with long, nodding flower plumes, native to tropical Africa and Asia.
species of ornamental grass

Examples

The landscaper recommended planting Pennisetum ruppelii for its graceful pink flower plumes.
Gardeners prize Pennisetum ruppelii for adding texture and color to perennial borders.
You often see Pennisetum ruppelii swaying in the breeze in tropical gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fountain grass - another common name for ornamental grasses of the genus Pennisetum

Etymology

Pennisetum: from Latin 'penna' (feather) and 'seta' (bristle); ruppelii: named after the German explorer Eduard Rüppell.
