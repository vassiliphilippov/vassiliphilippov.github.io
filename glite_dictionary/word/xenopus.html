Xenopus

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɛnəpəs / (AmE), / ˈziːnəpəs / (BrE)
A genus of African clawed frogs, widely used in biological research.
genus of African clawed frogs

Examples

Scientists often use Xenopus in developmental biology experiments.
The embryos of Xenopus are easy to observe and manipulate in the laboratory.
The African clawed frog, Xenopus, has contributed greatly to genetic research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frog - a tailless amphibian with smooth skin and long hind legs

Etymology

From Greek xénos (strange) + poús (foot), referring to its unusual feet.

2. NOUN

/ ˈzɛnəpəs / (AmE), / ˈziːnəpəs / (BrE)
A genus of African clawed frogs commonly used in biological research.
genus of African clawed frogs

Examples

The African clawed frog, known as Xenopus, is widely used in laboratory studies.
Researchers studied the regeneration abilities of Xenopus larvae.
Several species within the genus Xenopus inhabit sub-Saharan Africa.

Etymology

From Greek xenos (strange, foreign) + pous (foot), referring to the frog's unusual feet.
