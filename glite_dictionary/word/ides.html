ides

1. NOUN

/ aɪdz / (AmE), / aɪdz / (BrE)
(in the Roman calendar) the 15th day of March, May, July, or October, and the 13th day of any other month.
day in the Roman calendar

Examples

The assassination of Julius Caesar occurred on the ides of March.
Superstitions about the ides have persisted for centuries.
In ancient Rome, important religious ceremonies were held on the ides of each month.

Etymology

From Latin 'idus', referring to specific days in the Roman month.
