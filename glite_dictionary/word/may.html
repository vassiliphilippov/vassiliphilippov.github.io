may

1. NOUN

/ meɪ / (AmE), / meɪ / (BrE)
a thorny Eurasian shrub or small tree, typically with clusters of white to scarlet flowers and deep red berries, often called the may tree or hawthorn
a Eurasian shrub or tree with flowers and red berries

Examples

In spring, the may bursts into a spectacle of white blossoms along the hedgerows.
The may in her garden produced bright red berries every autumn.
Many English villages celebrate the coming of spring by decorating with may branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawthorn - another name for the may tree or shrub

Etymology

From Old English 'mægð' (may-tree), referring to the hawthorn that blossoms in May.
