reversed

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈvɜrst / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːst / (BrE)
(of fabric or clothing) turned inside out and resewn, especially so that the worn parts are concealed and the item appears new or improved.
turned inside out and resewn

Examples

The tailor suggested a reversed cuff to give the shirt a fresh look.
After years of wear, she had the jacket reversed by a skilled seamstress.
The reversed collar looked as good as new.

Synset Examples

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈvɜrst / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːst / (BrE)
turned the opposite way or in the opposite order to what is normal or expected
turned opposite in order or direction

Examples

The magician showed a card with the image reversed to confuse the audience.
The engineer discovered that the wires had been reversed during installation.
Reading a reversed sequence can change the meaning entirely.

Synset Examples

transposed letters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inverted - turned upside down or inside out
  • Antonym: normal - not changed from the usual order or direction

Etymology

From Latin 'revertere' meaning 'to turn back'; related to the verb 'reverse'.
