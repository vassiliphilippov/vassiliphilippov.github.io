abbey

1. NOUN

/ ˈæbi / (AmE), / ˈæbi / (BrE)
A monastery or convent governed by an abbot or abbess, often including the church and other buildings.
monastery or convent ruled by an abbot or abbess

Examples

We visited the ancient abbey during our holiday in Scotland.
The abbey was famous for its beautiful stained glass windows and peaceful gardens.
After the fire, the monks rebuilt the abbey stone by stone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monastery - a building or complex where monks live and worship
  • Synonym: convent - a building where nuns live and worship

Etymology

From Latin 'abbatia', from 'abbas' (abbot), influenced by Old French 'abbeie'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæbi / (AmE), / ˈæbi / (BrE)
A convent or monastery, especially one ruled by an abbess or abbot.
convent or monastery, esp. ruled by an abbess or abbot

Examples

The nuns at the abbey spent their days in prayer and service to the community.
She visited an ancient abbey that once housed hundreds of nuns under the care of an abbess.
A respected abbess governed the abbey with wisdom and kindness for over thirty years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monastery - a building or buildings occupied by a community of monks living under religious vows
  • Synonym: convent - a community of nuns; a building for a community of nuns

Etymology

From Old French 'abaïe', from Late Latin 'abbatia', from 'abbās' (abbot).

3. NOUN

/ ˈæbi / (AmE), / ˈæbi / (BrE)
A large church or building complex that was originally part of a monastery or convent, often housing monks or nuns.
church connected to monastery or convent

Examples

The old abbey stood on a hill, surrounded by ancient trees.
Hundreds of tourists visit the famous abbey every year.
The monks prayed and worked within the quiet walls of the abbey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monastery - a building where monks live and worship
  • Synonym: convent - a building where nuns live and worship

Etymology

From Old French *abaïe*, from Late Latin *abbatia* (abbot's house), from *abbas* (abbot).
