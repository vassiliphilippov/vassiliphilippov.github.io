ameliorative

1. ADJ

/ əˈmiːliəˌreɪtɪv / (AmE), / əˈmiːlɪərətɪv / (BrE)
having the effect of making something better; intended to improve a situation or condition
intended to improve or make better

Examples

The new law had an ameliorative effect on living conditions for the poor.
Her ameliorative approach helped to resolve the conflict peacefully.
City officials introduced several ameliorative measures to address air pollution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ameliorate - to make something better
  • Antonym: to exacerbating - making something worse

Etymology

From Latin ameliorare (to make better) + -ive.
