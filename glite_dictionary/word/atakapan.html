Atakapan

1. NOUN

/ ˌætəˈkɑːpən / (AmE), / ˌatəˈkɑːpən / (BrE)
a language formerly spoken by the Atakapa people of the Gulf Coast region in Louisiana and Texas
extinct language of the Atakapa people

Examples

The Atakapan language is now considered extinct, with only a few recorded words surviving.
Linguists have studied Atakapan to better understand the culture of the Gulf Coast tribes.
Few written records exist of the Atakapan language, making it difficult to reconstruct.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Atakapa - member of the Atakapa indigenous people

Etymology

From Atakapa, the name of the indigenous people who spoke the language.
