stringybark

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɪŋiˌbɑrk / (AmE), / ˈstrɪŋiˌbɑːk / (BrE)
any of several Australian eucalyptus trees with fibrous inner bark, especially those of the genus Eucalyptus.
Australian eucalyptus tree with fibrous bark

Examples

The stringybark forests are common in parts of southeastern Australia.
Many koalas make their homes in stringybark trees.
Bushrangers once hid in the dense stringybark woodlands.

Etymology

From 'stringy' (having a string-like texture) + 'bark', describing the tree's fibrous bark.
