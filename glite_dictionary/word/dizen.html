to dizen

1. VERB

/ ˈdaɪzən / (AmE), / ˈdɪzən / (BrE)
to dress up or adorn in a showy, tasteless, or gaudy manner
dress up gaudily or tastelessly
archaic

Examples

The girls had dizened themselves with colorful ribbons and cheap jewelry for the festival.
He loves to dizen his room with garish posters and flashy lights.
Do not dizen yourself for the masquerade; a simple costume will do.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bedizen - to dress up in a tasteless or gaudy manner

Etymology

16th century; of uncertain origin, possibly related to 'diz-', a dialectal variant of 'dress'.
