Frankish

1. ADJ

/ ˈfræŋkɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈfræŋkɪʃ / (BrE)
of or relating to the Franks, a West Germanic people who lived in parts of Europe during the early Middle Ages
relating to the Franks (people)

Examples

The Frankish Empire played a crucial role in the shaping of medieval Europe.
Archaeologists discovered artifacts from an ancient Frankish settlement near the river.
The Frankish language eventually evolved into several modern European languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Frank - a member of the West Germanic people who settled in France and Western Germany

Etymology

From Frank + -ish; 'Frank' refers to the name of the medieval European people, the Franks.
