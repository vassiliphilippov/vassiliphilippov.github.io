Pomoxis annularis

1. NOUN

/ pəˈmɑksɪs ˌæn.jəˈlɛr.ɪs / (AmE), / pəˈmɒk.sɪs ˌæn.jʊˈlæ.rɪs / (BrE)
A species of freshwater fish, commonly called the white crappie, native to North America and valued as a game fish.
species of freshwater fish (white crappie)

Examples

Anglers often seek Pomoxis annularis in lakes and slow-moving rivers during springtime.
The Pomoxis annularis can be distinguished from its relative, the black crappie, by its body shape and color.
Biologists monitor the population of Pomoxis annularis to ensure the species remains abundant for sport fishing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black crappie - A related species of freshwater fish, Pomoxis nigromaculatus

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; 'Pomoxis' is a genus name; 'annularis' means 'ringed' or 'having rings' in Latin.
