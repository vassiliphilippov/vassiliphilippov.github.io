shrilling

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃrɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃrɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
a continuous, high-pitched, and often piercing sound
a continuous shrill noise

Examples

The shrilling of the alarm clock woke him up abruptly.
We could hear the shrilling of cicadas on the hot summer night.
The shrilling of whistles signaled the end of the match.

Synset Examples

the clash of swords and the shrilling of trumpets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shriek - a loud, sharp, shrill cry or sound
  • Antonym: silence - absence of sound or noise
