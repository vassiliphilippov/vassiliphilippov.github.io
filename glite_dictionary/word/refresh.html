to refresh

1. VERB

/ rɪˈfrɛʃ / (AmE), / rɪˈfrɛʃ / (BrE)
to help someone remember something they may have forgotten; to remind oneself of information
remind; renew memory of something

Examples

He needed to refresh his memory before the meeting to recall all the details.
Could you refresh my memory about how that system works?
Before the exam, she quickly refreshed her knowledge of the main concepts.

Synset Examples

I reviewed the material before the test

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to remind - to cause someone to remember something
  • Antonym: to forget - to fail to remember

Etymology

From Old French *refreschir*, from Latin *re-* (again) + *friscus* (fresh).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00698618-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rɪˈfrɛʃ / (AmE), / rɪˈfrɛʃ / (BrE)
to make someone feel less tired or hot; to make someone feel lively or renewed
make someone feel lively or revived

Examples

A cool shower can really refresh you after a long run.
She stopped to refresh herself with a glass of lemonade.
The breeze refreshed everyone at the outdoor event.

Synset Examples

The cool water refreshed us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revive - to restore to life or consciousness
  • Synonym: to invigorate - to fill with energy or strength
  • Antonym: tire - to make someone feel tired

Etymology

From Old French *refreschir*, from re- (again) + *fresche* (fresh).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00164252-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ rɪˈfrɛʃ / (AmE), / rɪˈfrɛʃ / (BrE)
to make oneself or someone else feel clean, rested, or more energetic again
make oneself or someone else feel fresh again

Examples

After a long walk in the sun, she went inside to refresh herself.
A cool shower in the morning always helps me refresh before work.
He paused during the exam to refresh his mind with a glass of water.

Synset Examples

She freshened up after the tennis game

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revive - to bring back to life or consciousness
  • Synonym: to reinvigorate - to give new energy or strength to
  • Antonym: tire - to become or cause to become in need of rest

Etymology

From Old French *refreschir*, from re- (again) + fres, freis (fresh).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00024807-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ rɪˈfrɛʃ / (AmE), / rɪˈfrɛʃ / (BrE)
to make something feel or appear new, clean, or invigorated again; to restore strength or energy
make new or invigorated again

Examples

A short walk outside will help refresh your mind after a long meeting.
She pressed the button to refresh the webpage and see the updated results.
A cold drink can quickly refresh you on a hot summer day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revive - restore to life or consciousness
  • Synonym: to renew - make new or as if new again
  • Antonym: tire - to make weary or exhausted

Etymology

From Old French 'refreschir', based on Latin 're-' (again) + 'friscus' (fresh).
