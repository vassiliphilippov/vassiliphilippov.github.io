burning

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of setting fire to or consuming something with fire
the act of burning something

Examples

The burning of waste materials is strictly regulated in the city.
Authorities responded quickly to the illegal burning in the vacant lot.
The burning of forests for farmland has serious environmental consequences.

Synset Examples

the burning of leaves was prohibited by a town ordinance

Etymology

Present participle of 'burn', Old English 'byrnan' (to burn).

2. ADJ

/ ˈbɝː.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɜː.nɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a question, issue, or topic) of great urgency or immediate importance.
urgent; of immediate importance

Examples

The cost of living has become a burning issue in political debates.
Climate change is perhaps the most burning question facing our generation.
They discussed the burning problem of youth unemployment at the conference.

Synset Examples

burning issues of the day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urgent - requiring immediate action or attention

Etymology

Figurative use from the literal sense of 'burning' (being on fire), indicating something that cannot be ignored because of its intensity.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of a substance reacting with oxygen to produce heat and light; combustion
the process of combustion

Examples

During the burning of wood, energy is released as heat and light.
The burning of fossil fuels is a major source of air pollution.
Proper ventilation is crucial during the burning of candles to avoid smoke buildup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combustion - the process of burning something

execution by fire as punishment

Historically, burning was used as a form of capital punishment for heresy.

5. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
A sensation of pain that feels hot and as if it were on fire.
pain that feels like heat or fire

Examples

He described a constant burning in his lower back after the accident.
Many people with sunburn experience an intense burning that lasts for hours.
Patients often report a burning in the eyes when exposed to smoke.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sting - a sharp tingling pain or sensation

Etymology

From Middle English 'brennen' (to burn), of Germanic origin.

Examples

Evidence of burning was found on the suspect's arms.
