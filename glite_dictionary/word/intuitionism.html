intuitionism

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnˌtuːˈɪʃənˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɪn.tjʊˈɪʃ.ən.ɪ.zəm / (BrE)
(Philosophy) The doctrine that knowledge or moral truths are primarily obtained by intuition rather than deduction or observation.
philosophical doctrine of knowledge by intuition

Examples

She studied intuitionism as one of the main currents in modern philosophy.
Some mathematicians favor intuitionism over formalism when discussing the foundations of mathematics.
The professor explained that intuitionism rejects knowledge gained solely by logical deduction.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: empiricism - the theory that all knowledge is derived from sense-experience
  • Antonym: rationalism - the doctrine that knowledge is acquired by reason without resort to experience

Etymology

From 'intuition' + '-ism', first used in philosophical context in the late 19th century.
