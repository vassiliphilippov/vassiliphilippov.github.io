calorisator

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlɔːrɪˈzeɪtər / (AmE), / kəˌlɒrɪˈzeɪtə / (BrE)
a device used in beet-sugar factories to heat juice to facilitate the diffusion process
apparatus for heating juice in sugar production

Examples

The beet-sugar factory installed a new calorisator to improve the efficiency of juice diffusion.
Engineers adjusted the temperature settings on the calorisator to ensure optimal processing conditions.
Proper maintenance of the calorisator prevents contamination during the sugar production process.

Etymology

From Latin 'calor' (heat) and the suffix '-isator' denoting an agent or device.
