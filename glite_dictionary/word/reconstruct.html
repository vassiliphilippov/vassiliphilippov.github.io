to reconstruct

1. VERB

/ ˌriːkənˈstrʌkt / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈstrʌkt / (BrE)
to form an impression or idea of past events or situations by piecing together available information
form a mental picture of the past

Examples

The investigators tried to reconstruct the sequence of events from the available evidence.
Historians often reconstruct what daily life was like in ancient cultures.
After listening to the witnesses, she managed to reconstruct the accident in her mind.

Synset Examples

reconstruct the events of 20 years ago

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piece together - to assemble information to form a complete picture
  • Synonym: to imagine - to form a mental image of something

Etymology

From Latin 're-' (again) + 'construere' (to construct, build).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00635752-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌriːkənˈstrʌkt / (AmE), / ˌriːkɒnˈstrʌkt / (BrE)
to help someone adapt or be reformed socially or politically, especially after significant upheaval or change
help someone adapt or reform socially or politically

Examples

After the war, various programs were launched to reconstruct the displaced citizens, giving them new roles in society.
The government aimed to reconstruct communities that had been torn apart by decades of conflict.
Efforts to reconstruct former rebels included education and vocational training to support their integration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reform - to make changes in order to improve something, especially a person's behavior or a system

Etymology

From Latin roots: re- (again) + construere (to build).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00151268-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌriː.kənˈstrʌkt / (AmE), / ˌriː.kənˈstrʌkt / (BrE)
to build or form (something) again after it has been damaged or destroyed, or to make significant changes to its structure
to build or restore again

Examples

The team will reconstruct the old farmhouse using original materials where possible.
After the storm, they decided to reconstruct the damaged wing of their house.
We need permission from the city to reconstruct this historic building.

Synset Examples

We are remodeling these rooms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to renovate - to repair and improve a building, especially an old one
  • Synonym: to restore - to bring back to former or original state
  • Antonym: to demolish - to destroy a building completely

Etymology

From Latin 're-' (again) + 'construere' (to pile up, build).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00199854-v
Synset POS: v

Examples

5. VERB

/ ˌriːkənˈstrʌkt / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈstrʌkt / (BrE)
to restore (something) to its original or usable and functioning condition
restore to original or working state

Examples

After the flood, volunteers worked tirelessly to reconstruct the damaged homes in the community.
The museum plans to reconstruct the ancient statue from its scattered fragments.
Authorities hope to reconstruct the bridge within a year to restore traffic flow.

Synset Examples

restore the forest to its original pristine condition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restore - return (something) to a former condition or position
  • Synonym: repair - to fix or mend (something) that is broken or damaged

Etymology

From re- (again) + construct, from Latin construere (to build).
