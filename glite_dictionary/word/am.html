A.M.

1. ADV

/ ˌeɪ ˈɛm / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˈɛm / (BrE)
used to refer to the time between midnight and noon; before 12 o'clock in the day.
before noon (time)

Examples

My train leaves at 7 A.M. tomorrow morning.
The meeting is scheduled for 10 A.M. sharp, so please be on time.
She usually has her first cup of coffee by 8 A.M..

Synset Examples

let's meet at 11 A.M.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: P.M. - used to refer to the time between noon and midnight

Etymology

Abbreviation of Latin 'ante meridiem' meaning 'before midday'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ ˈɛm / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˈɛm / (BrE)
The period of time between midnight and noon; used to specify hours before midday in the 12-hour clock system.
the time between midnight and noon

Examples

The train is scheduled to depart at 8:30 A.M. sharp.
She usually wakes up around 6 A.M. to go jogging before work.
Breakfast will be served until 10 A.M. in the dining room.

Synset Examples

Made in the A.M.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: P.M. - the period between noon and midnight

Etymology

Abbreviation of Latin 'ante meridiem' meaning 'before midday'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-84502472-n
Synset POS: n
