Monodon

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.nəˌdɑn / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.əˌdɒn / (BrE)
the genus of toothed whales that includes the narwhal, belonging to the family Monodontidae
genus of whales including the narwhal

Examples

The narwhal is the only living species classified under the genus Monodon.
Scientists observed several members of the genus Monodon in the Arctic waters.
Taxonomists debate whether any extinct species should be included in Monodon.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Monodontidae - the family of Arctic whales including the narwhal and beluga

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) + 'odōn' (tooth), referring to the narwhal's single tusk.
