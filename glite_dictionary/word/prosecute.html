to prosecute

1. VERB

/ ˈprɑː.sɪ.kjuːt / (AmE), / ˈprɒs.ɪ.kjuːt / (BrE)
to officially charge someone with a crime in a court of law and try to prove they are guilty
charge and try someone in court for a crime
legal

Examples

The district attorney decided to prosecute the company for environmental violations.
She was prosecuted for fraud after an extensive investigation.
If there is enough evidence, the state will prosecute the suspect in court.

Synset Examples

The State of California prosecuted O.J. Simpson

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to defend - to represent or support someone in a court of law against attack or prosecution

Etymology

From Latin *prosecutus*, past participle of *prosequi* (to follow, pursue).

2. VERB

/ ˈprɑːsɪˌkjuːt / (AmE), / ˈprɒsɪkjuːt / (BrE)
to conduct legal proceedings against someone in a court of law; to bring a criminal case against someone
bring a legal case against
legal

Examples

The state decided to prosecute the company for violating environmental laws.
He was prosecuted for fraud in a federal court last year.
If they find sufficient evidence, the attorney will prosecute the offender.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to defend - to represent the accused person in a court of law
  • Synonym: to sue - to start a civil legal case against someone, especially for compensation

Etymology

From Latin prosecutus, past participle of prosequi (to pursue).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02587985-v
Synset POS: v
