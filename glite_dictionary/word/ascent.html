ascent

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɛnt / (AmE), / əˈsɛnt / (BrE)
the act of moving upward or rising; an upward movement or climb
upward movement or climb

Examples

The group celebrated their successful ascent to the mountain summit.
The ascent of the hot-air balloon drew cheers from the crowd.
Her rapid ascent within the company surprised her colleagues.

Synset Examples

they cheered the rise of the hot-air balloon

Related Terms

  • Antonym: descent - a downward movement or climb
  • Synonym: rise - an upward movement; an instance of moving upward

Etymology

From Latin 'ascensus' (a climb, ascent), from 'ascendere' (to climb up).

2. NOUN

/ əˈsɛnt / (AmE), / əˈsɛnt / (BrE)
the act of rising or moving upwards; an upward movement or climb
an upward movement or climb

Examples

The mountain climbers began their ascent early in the morning to reach the summit by noon.
The steep ascent left everyone out of breath after just a few minutes.
Her successful ascent of the challenging cliff inspired the rest of the group.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: descent - the act of moving downward
  • Synonym: climb - the act of going up, especially using your feet and hands

Etymology

From Latin 'ascensus', past participle of 'ascendere' (to climb up).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00325210-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ əˈsɛnt / (AmE), / əˈsɛnt / (BrE)
an upward slope or incline, especially of a road or path
an upward slope or grade

Examples

The steep ascent challenged even the most experienced hikers.
Drivers are advised to reduce speed when approaching the ascent on icy roads.
The road's sharp ascent made cycling up it particularly strenuous.

Synset Examples

the car couldn't make it up the rise

Related Terms

  • Antonym: descent - a downward slope or grade

Etymology

From Latin ascendere (to climb up) via Old French ascent.
