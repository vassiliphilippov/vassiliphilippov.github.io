directionality

1. NOUN

/ dəˌrɛkʃəˈnæləti / (AmE), / dɪˌrɛkʃəˈnælɪti / (BrE)
the property of having or maintaining a specific direction or orientation
property of having a direction

Examples

Writing systems like Arabic have a different directionality compared to English.
The directionality of sound waves affects how we perceive music in a room.
Scientists studied the directionality of cell growth under different conditions.

Synset Examples

the directionality of written English is from left to right

Related Terms

  • Synonym: directional - relating to or indicating direction

Etymology

From 'directional' + '-ity', denoting the state or condition of being directional.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˌrɛkʃəˈnæləti / (AmE), / dɪˌrɛkʃəˈnælɪti / (BrE)
the property of being more sensitive or responsive in a particular direction than in others, especially in microphones or antennas
property of sensitivity in specific directions

Examples

The directionality of the microphone helps reduce background noise from the sides and rear.
Engineers tested the antenna's directionality to ensure it received signals from the correct source.
Different types of microphones offer varying degrees of directionality depending on their intended use.

Synset Examples

the antenna received signals with no directivity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: directivity - the quality of being directional, especially sensitivity or response in a specific direction

Etymology

From 'direction' + '-ality', patterned after 'actuality' and similar words.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05073795-n
Synset POS: n
