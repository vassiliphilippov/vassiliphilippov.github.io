terror-struck

1. ADJ

/ ˈterɚˌstrʌk / (AmE), / ˈterəˌstrʌk / (BrE)
suddenly overcome or filled with intense fear or terror
overwhelmed by terror

Examples

The children were terror-struck by the sudden thunderstorm.
She woke from a terror-struck dream, her heart pounding in her chest.
His terror-struck face told the whole story before he could speak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrified - extremely frightened
  • Antonym: calm - not feeling fear or anxiety

Etymology

Formed from 'terror' and the past participle 'struck', following the model of similar compounds like 'awe-struck'.
