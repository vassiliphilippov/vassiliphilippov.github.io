Diapheromera femorata

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪ.ə.fəˈroʊ.mə rə fəˈmɔːr.ə.tə / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.ə.fəˈrəʊ.mərə fɛˈmɒ.rə.tə / (BrE)
a species of stick insect native to North America, commonly known as the common walking stick
species of North American stick insect

Examples

The Diapheromera femorata is often found on oak and hazel trees in North American forests.
Biologists studied how Diapheromera femorata camouflages itself among the branches.
A Diapheromera femorata can easily be mistaken for a twig due to its slender body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walking stick - common name for Diapheromera femorata, a stick insect

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Diapheromera' and species 'femorata' meaning 'pertaining to the femur' (thigh).
