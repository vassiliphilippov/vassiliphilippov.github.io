deceitfully

1. ADV

/ dɪˈsiːtfəli / (AmE), / dɪˈsiːtfʊli / (BrE)
In a manner intended to mislead or deceive someone; dishonestly.
in a dishonest or misleading way

Examples

He deceitfully promised to repay the money, knowing that he never would.
The report was deceitfully edited to hide crucial errors.
She answered the questions deceitfully, withholding important information from the investigation.

Synset Examples

he acted dishonestly when he gave the contract to his best friend

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dishonestly - in a dishonest manner
  • Antonym: honestly - in a truthful or sincere manner

Etymology

From 'deceitful' + '-ly'. 'Deceitful' from Old French 'deceit', from Latin 'decipere' (to deceive).
