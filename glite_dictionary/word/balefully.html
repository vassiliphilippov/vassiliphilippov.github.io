balefully

1. ADV

/ ˈbeɪlfəli / (AmE), / ˈbeɪlfəli / (BrE)
In a manner that expresses menace, harm, or malign intent.
in a threatening or harmful way

Examples

She regarded him balefully after hearing his comment.
The villain balefully glared at the captured hero.
He spoke balefully about anyone who crossed him.

Synset Examples

she looked at him balefully

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baleful - threatening harm; menacing
  • Synonym: menacingly - in a way that suggests the presence of danger
  • Antonym: benignly - in a gentle or kindly manner

Etymology

From 'baleful' + '-ly', where 'baleful' comes from Old English 'bealu' (evil).
