hegari

1. NOUN

/ hɛˈɡɑri / (AmE), / hɛˈɡɑːri / (BrE)
A variety of sorghum with white seeds, originally from Sudan, also cultivated in the southwestern United States.
variety of sorghum with white seeds

Examples

Farmers in Texas rely on hegari for its drought-resistant properties and high yield.
The hegari crop was particularly abundant this season due to favorable weather conditions.
Researchers compared the nutritional content of hegari to other varieties of sorghum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorghum - a genus of cereals widely grown as grain crops

Etymology

From Arabic 'ḥijārī', a name for this type of sorghum.
