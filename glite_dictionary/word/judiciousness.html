judiciousness

1. NOUN

/ dʒuˈdɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / dʒuˈdɪʃəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of having or showing good judgment, especially in forming opinions by distinguishing and evaluating carefully
the quality of good judgment

Examples

Her judiciousness was evident in the way she carefully weighed all the options before deciding.
As a manager, his judiciousness helped him resolve conflicts fairly and efficiently.
The judiciousness of the committee's recommendations earned respect from the entire organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wisdom - the quality of having experience, knowledge, and good judgment
  • Antonym: foolishness - the lack of good sense or judgment

Etymology

From 'judicious' + '-ness'; 'judicious' from Latin 'judicium' (judgment).

2. NOUN

/ dʒuˈdɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / dʒuˈdɪʃəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality of showing good judgment and practical sense in making decisions.
good judgment; practical wisdom

Examples

Her judiciousness in handling the negotiations earned her the respect of her colleagues.
Investors rely on the judiciousness of experienced advisers during uncertain economic times.
He showed great judiciousness by waiting for all the facts before reaching a conclusion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wisdom - the quality of having experience, knowledge, and good judgment
  • Antonym: foolishness - lack of good sense or judgment

Etymology

From 'judicious' + '-ness'; 'judicious' from French judicieux, from Latin judicium (judgment).
