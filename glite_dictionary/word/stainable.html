stainable

1. ADJ

/ ˈsteɪnəbəl / (AmE), / ˈsteɪnəbəl / (BrE)
able to be stained, especially referring to cells or cell parts that can take up dye for microscopic examination.
able to be stained (esp. in microscopy)

Examples

The tissue sample was stainable with the standard histological dyes.
Some bacteria are only stainable with special techniques in the laboratory.
The doctor explained that certain cell components are more stainable than others under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unstainable - not able to be stained

Etymology

From 'stain' + '-able', forming an adjective meaning 'capable of being stained'.
