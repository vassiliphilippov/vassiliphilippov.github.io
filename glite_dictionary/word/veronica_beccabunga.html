Veronica beccabunga

1. NOUN

/ vəˈrɑnɪkə ˌbɛkəˈbʌŋɡə / (AmE), / vəˈrɒnɪkə ˌbɛkəˈbʌŋɡə / (BrE)
A European plant species with trailing stems and blue flowers, known as brooklime, sometimes found naturalized in North America.
a European trailing plant with blue flowers

Examples

Veronica beccabunga thrives in wet ditches and along the banks of streams.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Veronica beccabunga for its attractive blue flowers.
In some regions, Veronica beccabunga is valued for its use as a marginal aquatic plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brooklime - Common name for Veronica beccabunga

Etymology

Veronica from the Latin plant genus name; beccabunga from Medieval Latin, of Germanic origin, meaning 'mouthful' or 'gobful'.
