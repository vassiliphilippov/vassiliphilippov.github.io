Crotaphytus

1. NOUN

/ kroʊˈtæfɪtəs / (AmE), / krəʊˈtæfɪtəs / (BrE)
A genus of lizards commonly known as collared lizards, native to arid regions of North America.
genus of collared lizards

Examples

The genus Crotaphytus includes several species of colorful, active lizards found in the southwestern United States.
Researchers observed the mating behavior of Crotaphytus in its natural desert habitat.
A well-known member of the genus Crotaphytus is commonly called the collared lizard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collared lizard - a common name for lizards of the Crotaphytus genus; lizard with black 'collar' markings

Etymology

From Greek krotaphos (temple) and -ytus (suffix used in zoological nomenclature).
