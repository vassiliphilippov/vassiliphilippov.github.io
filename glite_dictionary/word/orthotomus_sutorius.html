Orthotomus sutorius

1. PROPN

/ ɔrˈθɑtəməs suːˈtɔːriəs / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɒtəməs suːˈtɔːriəs / (BrE)
A small tropical Asian warbler, known as the common tailorbird, that stitches leaves together to form and conceal its nest.
tropical Asian warbler, the common tailorbird

Examples

The Orthotomus sutorius is famous for its unique technique of sewing leaves to build its nest.
Bird watchers in India often spot the Orthotomus sutorius in gardens and open forests.
With its distinctive call, the Orthotomus sutorius is a familiar sound in many tropical Asian regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tailorbird - common name for several species of birds that stitch leaves to build nests

Etymology

From Greek 'orthos' (straight) and 'tomos' (cutting), and Latin 'sutorius' (pertaining to a shoemaker), referring to the bird's leaf-stitching behavior.
