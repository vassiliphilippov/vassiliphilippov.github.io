Dracenaceae

1. NOUN

/ dræsɪˈneɪsiˌi / (AmE), / dræsɪˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
A former botanical family, once used in certain classification systems to group some plants now usually included in the Agavaceae.
former plant family (botany)

Examples

Some botanists once classified these desert plants under the Dracenaceae family.
Modern taxonomies rarely recognize Dracenaceae as a distinct family anymore.
The Dracenaceae included species with sword-shaped leaves similar to those in Agavaceae.

Etymology

From the genus name Dracaena, itself derived from Greek drakaina (female dragon).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdræsɪˈneɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌdræsɪˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
a subfamily of flowering plants, occasionally used in taxonomy to classify certain species of the Agavaceae family
a plant subfamily related to Agavaceae

Examples

The Dracenaceae are sometimes included as a subfamily within Agavaceae.
Recent botanical studies have reclassified several species into the Dracenaceae.
Members of the Dracenaceae subfamily are often native to tropical regions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Agavaceae - a family of flowering plants including agaves, yuccas, and others
  • Degree Lower: Dracaena - a genus of plants within Dracenaceae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Dracaena.
