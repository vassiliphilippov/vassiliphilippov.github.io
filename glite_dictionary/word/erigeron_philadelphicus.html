Erigeron philadelphicus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛrəˈdʒɪrɑn ˌfɪlədɛlˈfaɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪˈdʒɪərɒn ˌfɪlədɛlˈfaɪkəs / (BrE)
A North American wildflower of the daisy family, also known as Philadelphia fleabane, with delicate, threadlike white or pink ray flowers surrounding yellow centers.
a North American wildflower (Philadelphia fleabane)

Examples

Erigeron philadelphicus blooms in early spring with clusters of tiny white or pinkish flowers.
Many pollinators are attracted to the delicate blossoms of Erigeron philadelphicus in meadows and fields.
Gardeners sometimes plant Erigeron philadelphicus for its airy spray of fringed flowers and its appeal to butterflies.

Etymology

From Latin: genus 'Erigeron' (from Greek ἐριγέρων 'early old man', referring to the white hairs), species 'philadelphicus' meaning 'of Philadelphia'.
