Rana

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːnə / (AmE), / ˈrɑːnə / (BrE)
A genus of amphibians in the family Ranidae, commonly referred to as true frogs, found widely in Eurasia, Africa, and the Americas.
genus of true frogs

Examples

Many species within the genus Rana are commonly found in North America.
The green frog is a well-known member of the Rana genus.
Scientists studied the distribution of Rana populations across different habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frog - A tailless amphibian with long hind legs, member of Ranidae family

Etymology

From Latin rāna (frog).
