Mohave

1. PROPN

/ moʊˈhɑːvi / (AmE), / məʊˈhɑːvi / (BrE)
A desert area in southern California and western Arizona, known for its arid climate and unique flora and fauna.
desert region in California and Arizona

Examples

The Mohave is famous for its Joshua trees and dramatic landscapes.
Many travelers cross the Mohave on their way to Las Vegas.
Archaeologists have found ancient artifacts in the Mohave desert.

Etymology

From the name of the Mojave people, a Native American tribe indigenous to the region.

2. NOUN

/ moʊˈhɑːvi / (AmE), / məʊˈhɑːvi / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people formerly inhabiting the Colorado River valley in Arizona, Nevada, and California
member of a Native American people

Examples

The Mohave have a rich tradition of storytelling and song.
A group of Mohave elders shared their history with local students.
The museum featured artifacts from the Mohave people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mojave - alternative spelling referring to the same Native American people

Etymology

From Mohave (Mojave), the tribe's own name for themselves.

3. NOUN

/ moʊˈhɑːvi / (AmE), / məˈhɑːvi / (BrE)
the indigenous Yuman language spoken by the Mohave people of the lower Colorado River region in the southwestern United States.
Yuman language of the Mohave people

Examples

There are efforts underway to preserve and revitalize the Mohave language among tribal members.
She studied Mohave as part of her research on Native American linguistics.
The dictionary includes words from Mohave alongside other Yuman languages.

Etymology

From the name of the Mohave people; alternatively spelled Mojave.
