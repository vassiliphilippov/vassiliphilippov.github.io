Adolf Hitler

1. PROPN

/ ˈædɑlf ˈhɪtlɚ / (AmE), / ˈadɒlf ˈhɪtlə / (BrE)
(1889–1945) The German Nazi dictator who led Germany during World War II and was responsible for the Holocaust.
German Nazi dictator, World War II

Examples

Adolf Hitler rose to power in Germany in the 1930s and led the country through World War II.
The policies and actions of Adolf Hitler had catastrophic consequences for Europe and the world.
Many history books analyze Adolf Hitler's role in starting World War II and the Holocaust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dictator - a ruler with total power over a country, typically one who has obtained power by force
  • Synonym: Benito Mussolini - Italian Fascist dictator during World War II
  • Antonym: Winston Churchill - British Prime Minister during World War II (antagonist figure)
