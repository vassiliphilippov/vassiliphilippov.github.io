hypotensive

1. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪpoʊˈtɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəʊˈtɛnsɪv / (BrE)
having abnormally low blood pressure.
having low blood pressure
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed as hypotensive after her blood pressure dropped below normal.
Certain medications can make people hypotensive if taken in large doses.
He felt dizzy and faint because he was hypotensive throughout the morning.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypertensive - having abnormally high blood pressure

Etymology

From hypo- (under) and tension (pressure), referring to blood pressure.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpoʊˈtɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəʊˈtɛnsɪv / (BrE)
a person who has abnormally low blood pressure
person with low blood pressure
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that being a hypotensive can sometimes cause dizziness or fainting.
As a hypotensive, she is advised to avoid medications that lower blood pressure.
Several hypotensives responded well to dietary changes in the clinical study.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypertensive - a person who has abnormally high blood pressure

Etymology

Formed from 'hypo-' (under, below) and 'tensive' (from 'tension', pertaining to pressure); originally medical Latin.

Synset POS: n
