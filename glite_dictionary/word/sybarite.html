sybarite

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪbəˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪbəraɪt / (BrE)
A person who is fond of or addicted to luxury and pleasure.
person addicted to luxury and pleasure

Examples

The wealthy sybarite spent his days surrounded by fine food and art.
As a true sybarite, she could not imagine living without daily comforts.
The novel's protagonist is portrayed as a cynical sybarite uninterested in the hardships of common life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hedonist - a person who believes that the pursuit of pleasure is the most important thing in life
  • Antonym: ascetic - a person who practices severe self-discipline and abstains from indulgence

Etymology

From Greek Sybarītēs, an inhabitant of Sybaris, a city in southern Italy famous for its luxury.
