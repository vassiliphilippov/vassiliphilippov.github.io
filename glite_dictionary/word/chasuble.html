chasuble

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæzjəbəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃæzjʊb(ə)l / (BrE)
a long, sleeveless outer vestment worn by a priest when celebrating Mass in the Christian church
sleeveless priest's vestment for Mass

Examples

The priest donned a richly decorated chasuble before the service began.
During the Easter Mass, the bishop wore a gold chasuble.
She admired the intricate embroidery on the chasuble displayed in the cathedral museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vestment - ceremonial garment worn by clergy

Etymology

From Late Latin *casubula*, diminutive of *casula* (cloak, little house).
