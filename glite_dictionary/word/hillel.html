Hillel

1. PROPN

/ hɪˈlɛl / (AmE), / hɪˈlɛl / (BrE)
A famous Palestinian rabbi (c. 110 BCE – 10 CE) known for interpreting Judaic law and exerting significant influence on Jewish tradition.
prominent ancient Jewish rabbi and legal scholar

Examples

Many of the ethical teachings attributed to Hillel remain influential in Jewish thought today.
Hillel famously said, 'If I am not for myself, who will be for me?'
Scholars often discuss the debates between Hillel and Shammai on matters of Jewish law.

Etymology

From Hebrew Hillel (הלל), a personal name meaning 'praise.'
