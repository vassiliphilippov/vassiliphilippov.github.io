homoeopath

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˈmiːəˌpæθ / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmiəˌpæθ / (BrE)
a person who practices homeopathy, a system of alternative medicine based on treating symptoms with minute doses of substances that in larger amounts would produce those symptoms
practitioner of homeopathy

Examples

She visited a homoeopath to seek alternative treatment for her allergies.
After years of study, he became a respected homoeopath in his community.
A homoeopath may recommend remedies based on a detailed consultation with the patient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homeopath - alternative spelling for homoeopath; practitioner of homeopathy

Etymology

From homoio- (variant of 'homeo-', from Greek 'homoios' meaning 'similar') + -path (from Greek 'pathos' meaning 'suffering').
