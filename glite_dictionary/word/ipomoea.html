Ipomoea

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.pəˈmiː.ə / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.pəˈmiː.ə / (BrE)
A large genus of flowering plants in the morning glory family, known for their funnel-shaped flowers and climbing or trailing habit.
a genus of flowering plants (morning glory)

Examples

Several species of Ipomoea are grown for their colorful, trumpet-shaped flowers.
The invasive Ipomoea quickly covered the garden fence during the summer.
Botanists discovered a new variety of Ipomoea in the tropical forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morning glory - common name for various species of the genus Ipomoea

Etymology

From Greek ipomeia, from ips (worm) and homoios (like), referring to the worm-like twining stems.

2. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.pəˈmiː.ə / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.pəˈmiː.ə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the bindweed family, known as morning glories, with showy trumpet-shaped flowers.
genus of flowering plants (morning glories)

Examples

The bright blue flowers of Ipomoea are a common sight in summer gardens.
Many species in the Ipomoea genus are grown for their attractive, fast-growing vines.
Sweet potatoes belong to the Ipomoea genus, alongside ornamental morning glories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morning glory - any plant of the genus Ipomoea, cultivated for its flowers

Etymology

From Greek 'ipomeia', composed of 'ips' (worm) and 'homoios' (resembling), referring to the twining habit.
