ecological

1. ADJ

/ ˌiːkəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌiːkəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the science of ecology or environmental interactions between living things and their surroundings
relating to ecology or environment

Examples

The new policy encourages ecological farming methods to protect wildlife habitats.
Her thesis focuses on the ecological impacts of deforestation in tropical regions.
Many companies are adopting ecological practices to reduce their environmental footprint.

Synset Examples

ecological research

Related Terms

  • Synonym: environmental - relating to the environment

Etymology

From ecology + -ical; ecology from Greek oikos (house) + -logia (study).

2. ADJ

/ ˌiːkəˈlɑdʒɪkəl, ˌɛkəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌiːkəˈlɒdʒɪkəl, ˌɛkəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or concerned with the relationships between living organisms and their environment.
relating to relationships in the environment

Examples

The construction project raised several ecological concerns about wildlife habitats in the area.
Climate change has led to numerous ecological shifts in the Arctic region.
She is passionate about finding ecological solutions to reduce plastic waste.

Synset Examples

an ecological disaster

Related Terms

  • Synonym: environmental - relating to the environment

Etymology

From Greek oikos (house, dwelling) + -logia (study of), via French écologique.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02918091-a
Synset POS: a
