blackthorn

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌθɔrn / (AmE), / ˈblækˌθɔːn / (BrE)
a thorny Eurasian shrub (Prunus spinosa) with small white flowers and bluish-black, plumlike fruits, often used for making sloe gin
thorny Eurasian shrub with plumlike fruits

Examples

The dense thickets of blackthorn provided shelter for birds during winter.
Farmers often planted blackthorn hedges as natural barriers between fields.
She picked ripe sloes from the blackthorn bushes to make homemade jam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sloe - the small, bluish-black fruit of the blackthorn

Etymology

From Middle English 'blakethorn', from Old English 'blæcþorn', literally 'black thorn'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌθɔrn / (AmE), / ˈblækˌθɔːn / (BrE)
An erect, almost thornless American hawthorn with somewhat pear-shaped berries, distinguished from the European blackthorn.
American hawthorn shrub with pear-shaped berries

Examples

The blackthorn in our yard produces small, pear-shaped berries every autumn.
Unlike European species, the American blackthorn has few thorns and smoother branches.
Botanists compared the American blackthorn to similar hawthorn varieties native to the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawthorn - a shrub or small tree with white or pink flowers and red fruits

Etymology

From Old English 'blæcþorn', referring originally to the dark-colored thorn bush.
