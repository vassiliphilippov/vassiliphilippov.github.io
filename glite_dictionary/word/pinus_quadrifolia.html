Pinus quadrifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪnəs ˌkwɑdrɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈpaɪnəs ˌkwɒdrɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A species of pine tree native to southern California and northern Baja California, known for having clusters of four or five needles.
pine tree of southern California and Baja California

Examples

The Pinus quadrifolia is commonly found on rocky slopes in southern California.
Many botanists study Pinus quadrifolia for its unique needle groupings.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect native stands of Pinus quadrifolia from habitat loss.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Pinus' meaning pine, and 'quadrifolia' meaning four-leaved or four-needled.
