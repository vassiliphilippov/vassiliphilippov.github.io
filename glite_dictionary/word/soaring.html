soaring

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɔː.rɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity or sport of flying a glider or unpowered aircraft, usually riding on air currents
activity of flying a glider

Examples

He took up soaring as a hobby after retiring from his engineering job.
The region is known for its ideal conditions for soaring competitions.
Many enthusiasts travel internationally to pursue their passion for soaring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gliding - the sport of flying in a glider

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɔr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɔː.rɪŋ / (BrE)
Rising or increasing rapidly to a level much higher than usual.
rising rapidly to a high level

Examples

The country's soaring inflation rate has worried economists.
Residents struggled to cope with the soaring cost of living.
There is growing concern about soaring energy prices this winter.

Synset Examples

soaring prices

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skyrocketing - increasing extremely rapidly
  • Antonym: plummeting - falling or dropping rapidly in value or amount

3. ADJ

/ ˈsɔrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɔːrɪŋ / (BrE)
rising high or standing out above others, especially in terms of impressive height
of great, impressive height

Examples

The soaring peaks of the Himalayas attract mountaineers from around the world.
We admired the soaring spires of the medieval cathedral against the sunset.
A soaring skyscraper dominated the city's skyline.

Synset Examples

an eminent peak
lofty mountains
the soaring spires of the cathedral
towering icebergs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: towering - very high or tall, especially compared to the surroundings
  • Synonym: lofty - of great height; elevated
  • Antonym: low - not high or tall; lacking height

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'soar', from Old French 'essorer' (to soar), from Vulgar Latin *exaurare (to expose to the wind).
