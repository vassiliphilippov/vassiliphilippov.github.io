Agathis alba

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡæθɪs ˈælbə / (AmE), / əˈɡæθɪs ˈælbə / (BrE)
a large evergreen tree native to the Moluccas and Philippines, known as a source of dammar resin.
tropical tree; source of dammar resin

Examples

Agathis alba is prized for the dammar resin extracted from its bark.
The forests of the Philippines are home to many specimens of Agathis alba.
Researchers study Agathis alba for its ecological and economic importance.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus Agathis + Latin alba (white), referring to pale wood or bark.
