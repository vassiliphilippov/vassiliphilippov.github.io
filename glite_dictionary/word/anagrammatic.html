anagrammatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌænəˌɡræˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌanəɡraˈmatɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of anagrams; containing, involving, or making use of anagrams.
relating to anagrams

Examples

The author is known for her anagrammatic puzzles that challenge readers to rearrange letters to form new words.
The poem's anagrammatic structure reveals hidden messages upon closer inspection.
His fascination with wordplay led him to create several anagrammatic jokes for the crossword.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anagram - a word or phrase formed by rearranging the letters of another

Etymology

From anagram + -atic, after French 'anagrammatique' or Late Latin 'anagrammaticus'.
