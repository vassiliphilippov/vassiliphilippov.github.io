conciliator

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsɪliˌeɪtər / (AmE), / kənˈsɪlɪeɪtə / (BrE)
a person who tries to bring peace between people in conflict or disagreement
person who seeks to make peace

Examples

As a skilled conciliator, she fostered dialogue between the two sides to resolve the dispute.
The court appointed a conciliator to help settle the labor disagreement.
Throughout the negotiations, his role as conciliator was crucial in achieving a peaceful outcome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mediator - person who attempts to make people involved in a conflict come to an agreement
  • Antonym: antagonist - person who actively opposes or is hostile to someone or something

Etymology

From Latin conciliator (one who brings together), from conciliare (to bring together).
