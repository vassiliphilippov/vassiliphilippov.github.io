fastening

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæsnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɑːsnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of joining two or more things together securely
the act of joining things together

Examples

The fastening of the lid ensures the container stays sealed.
He checked the fastening of his safety harness before climbing.
A strong fastening is essential for heavy doors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attachment - the act of attaching or being attached
  • Antonym: detachment - the act of detaching or separating

Etymology

From Middle English 'fasten' + '-ing', indicating the act or process.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfæsnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɑːsnɪŋ / (BrE)
a device or method used to attach or secure something firmly in place
something that attaches or secures objects

Examples

She checked the fastening on her coat before going outside.
The window fastening was broken, letting in a cold draft.
Make sure the fastening is secure before climbing the ladder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fastener - a device such as a button, pin, or clasp that holds something together

Etymology

From Old English fæstnian (to make fast), related to fasten.
