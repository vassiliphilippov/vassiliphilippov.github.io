to outmarch

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈmɑrtʃ / (AmE), / aʊtˈmɑːtʃ / (BrE)
to march farther or for a longer time than someone else; to surpass in marching endurance
march farther than someone else

Examples

Despite being the youngest recruit, he managed to outmarch his entire unit during the training exercise.
Her stamina allowed her to outmarch even the most experienced soldiers on the expedition.
They tried to keep up, but he quickly outmarched everyone else on the trail.

Synset Examples

This guy can outmarch anyone!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outwalk - to walk farther or longer than someone else
  • Synonym: to outpace - to move faster or farther than another

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'out-' (meaning 'beyond' or 'more than') + 'march' (to walk with regular steps).
