insecure

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.sɪˈkjʊr / (AmE), / ˌɪn.sɪˈkjʊə / (BrE)
not protected, stable, or safe; likely to be lost or at risk of harm or danger
not safe or protected

Examples

Many jobs in this industry are increasingly insecure due to economic changes.
The building was deemed insecure after the storm damaged its foundation.
They worried about their insecure housing situation as the lease was about to expire.

Synset Examples

his fortune was increasingly insecure
an insecure future

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secure - safe, protected from danger or risk
  • Synonym: unsafe - not safe; in danger of harm or loss

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + securus (free from care).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02102035-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.sɪˈkjʊr / (AmE), / ˌɪn.sɪˈkjʊə / (BrE)
not protected from danger or attack; not safe.
not safe; exposed to danger or attack

Examples

Many rural villages remain insecure due to ongoing conflict in the region.
The company's computer network was insecure and vulnerable to hackers.
He felt the abandoned house was too insecure to spend the night in.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secure - protected from danger or attack; safe
  • Synonym: unsafe - not safe; likely to cause harm or danger

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + securus (secure, safe).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02534602-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.sɪˈkjʊr / (AmE), / ˌɪn.sɪˈkjʊə / (BrE)
not firmly fixed; likely to fail, break, or give way
not firmly fixed; unstable

Examples

The ladder felt insecure on the uneven ground, so he climbed carefully.
If the bolts are insecure, the shelf could collapse under weight.
Check that the window is not insecure before locking up at night.

Synset Examples

the hinge is insecure

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secure - firmly fixed; not likely to give way or fail

Etymology

From Latin 'insecurus', from 'in-' (not) + 'securus' (secure).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02103066-a
Synset POS: a

4. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.sɪˈkjʊr / (AmE), / ˌɪn.sɪˈkjʊə / (BrE)
not confident about oneself or one's abilities; lacking self-confidence or assurance.
not confident; lacking self-assurance

Examples

She often feels insecure when speaking in front of large groups.
His insecure nature made it difficult for him to trust others.
It is common for teenagers to feel insecure about their appearance.

Synset Examples

an insecure person lacking mental stability

Related Terms

  • Antonym: confident - having strong belief or assurance in oneself
  • Synonym: self-assured - sure of oneself and one's abilities

Etymology

From Latin 'insecurus' (unsafe, uncertain), formed from 'in-' (not) + 'securus' (secure).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02100773-a
Synset POS: a
