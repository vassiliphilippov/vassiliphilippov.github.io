haunting

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɔn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
Having a deeply unsettling, evocative, or disturbing effect that lingers in the mind.
having a disturbing, lingering effect

Examples

The movie's haunting soundtrack stayed with me long after I left the theater.
Her haunting expression made it difficult for anyone to forget her story.
The abandoned house had a haunting beauty that drew photographers from afar.

Synset Examples

from two handsome and talented young men to two haunting horrors of disintegration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eerie - strangely frightening or mysterious
  • Antonym: comforting - making one feel safe or less worried

Etymology

From the verb 'haunt', ultimately from Old French 'hanter', meaning 'to frequent, visit regularly'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhɔntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːntɪŋ / (BrE)
evoking strong, persistent feelings or memories, often in a way that lingers in the mind
persistently evocative or memorable

Examples

The melody has a haunting quality that stays with you long after the song ends.
She was troubled by haunting memories of her childhood.
There is a haunting beauty in the abandoned village.

Synset Examples

haunting memories
the cathedral organ and the distant voices have a haunting beauty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memorable - easily remembered, especially because of being special or unusual
  • Antonym: forgettable - easily forgotten; not memorable or striking

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'haunt', from Old French hanter (to frequent, visit regularly).
