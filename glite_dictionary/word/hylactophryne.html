Hylactophryne

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌlæk.təˈfraɪ.ni / (AmE), / haɪˌlaktəˈfrʌɪni / (BrE)
A genus of small, typically stream-dwelling frogs known as barking frogs, found in parts of South America.
a genus of barking frogs

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species of Hylactophryne in the Amazon rainforest.
The distinctive call of the Hylactophryne can be heard during the rainy season.
Conservationists are studying Hylactophryne populations to monitor changes in biodiversity.

Etymology

From Greek hylē (forest), aktē (coast), and phryne (toad/frog).

2. NOUN

/ haɪˌlæktəˈfraɪni / (AmE), / haɪˌlæktəˈfraɪni / (BrE)
a genus of frogs known as barking frogs, found in Central and South America.
a genus of barking frogs

Examples

The genus Hylactophryne includes several species known for their distinctive barking calls.
Herpetologists are studying the habitat range of Hylactophryne in the rainforests of South America.
A unique adaptation of Hylactophryne is its ability to communicate using vocalizations that resemble a bark.

Etymology

From Greek hylē (forest) + aktōn (shore) + phrynos (toad).
