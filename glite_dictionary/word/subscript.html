subscript

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbˌskrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌskrɪpt / (BrE)
a character, symbol, or number written or printed just below and to the right of another character, typically to indicate a modified meaning
character written below another

Examples

In chemical formulas, the oxygen in H2O has a subscript 2 to indicate two hydrogen atoms.
Please write the index as a subscript so it is clear you're referring to elements of the sequence.
The subscript in this equation helps distinguish between different variables.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: superscript - a character or symbol written above and to the side of another character

Etymology

From Latin subscriptus, past participle of subscribere (to write below).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsʌbˌskrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.skrɪpt / (BrE)
(of a letter, figure, or symbol) written or printed just below and to one side of another character.
written below another character

Examples

The chemical formula for water, H₂O, uses a subscript to indicate the number of hydrogen atoms.
In mathematics, a subscript is often used to identify the elements of a sequence, like x₁ or x₂.
Please write the variable's index as a subscript in your solution.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: superscript - (of a letter, figure, or symbol) written or printed above and to one side of another character

Etymology

From Latin 'subscriptus', past participle of 'subscribere' (to write beneath).
