autotypy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːtəˌtaɪpi / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəˌtɪpi / (BrE)
a process for producing permanent prints using a carbon pigment, especially in photographic reproduction
carbon pigment printmaking process

Examples

The invention of autotypy revolutionized the way images were reproduced in books and newspapers.
Early photographers relied on autotypy to create durable and high-quality prints.
The artist experimented with autotypy to achieve richer tones in her photographic works.

Etymology

From Greek 'auto-' (self) and 'typos' (impression, mark).
