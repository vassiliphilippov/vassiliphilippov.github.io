gynaecology

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡaɪnəˈkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌɡaɪnɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The branch of medicine concerned with the health, diseases, and hygiene of the female reproductive system.
branch of medicine for women's health
medical british

Examples

She decided to specialize in gynaecology after graduating from medical school.
Gynaecology clinics provide essential services for women's reproductive health.
Advances in gynaecology have greatly improved prevention and treatment of female-specific conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gynecology - the branch of medicine concerned with women's reproductive health (American spelling)

Etymology

From Greek gynaik- (woman) and -logia (study).
