roble

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊb.leɪ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊb.leɪ / (BrE)
A large tropical tree of Trinidad and Guyana with odd-pinnate leaves, violet-scented clusters of yellow flowers, and long smooth pods; often grown as a specimen in parks and gardens.
tropical tree with yellow flowers and large pods

Examples

The roble is often planted in large parks for its striking yellow flowers.
In Guyana, the roble is prized for the pleasant scent of its blossoms.
Botanists were excited to discover a centuries-old roble near the riverbank.

Etymology

Spanish, meaning 'oak', used in English for various trees in the tropics.

2. NOUN

/ ˈroʊb.leɪ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊb.li / (BrE)
A tall, graceful deciduous California oak tree (Quercus lobata) with leathery leaves and slender pointed acorns.
Californian oak tree species

Examples

The old roble in the field is a favorite nesting spot for local birds.
Hikers admired the broad canopy of the roble shading the trail.
Botanists studied the growth rings in samples taken from the ancient roble.

Etymology

From Spanish 'roble' meaning 'oak'.
