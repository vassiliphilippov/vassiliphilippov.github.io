coloniser

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.ləˌnaɪ.zɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɒl.ə.naɪ.zə / (BrE)
A person who establishes or helps to found a colony in a new land.
person who founds a colony

Examples

Many European colonisers arrived in the Americas during the 16th century.
As a coloniser, he played a crucial role in establishing the first settlements.
The impact of colonisers on indigenous cultures was profound and lasting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: settler - a person who settles in a new country or region
  • Synonym: colonist - a person who lives in or helps establish a colony

Etymology

From Latin 'colonus' (farmer, settler), via French 'coloniser' (to colonize).
