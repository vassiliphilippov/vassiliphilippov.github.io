trephination

1. NOUN

/ trɛfəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / trɛfɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
A surgical operation that involves removing a circular section of bone from the skull.
surgical removal of skull bone section
medical

Examples

The patient underwent trephination to relieve the pressure on his brain.
Archaeological evidence shows that trephination was practiced in ancient civilizations.
Modern trephination is performed under sterile conditions to treat certain cranial injuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trepanation - The act of drilling or cutting a hole in the skull, usually for medical reasons

Etymology

From French *tréphination*, based on Greek *trypanon* (borer, auger).
