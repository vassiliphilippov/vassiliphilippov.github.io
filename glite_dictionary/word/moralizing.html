moralizing

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔr.əˌlaɪ.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɒr.əˌlaɪ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of expressing moral judgments or pronouncements, often in a way that seems self-righteous or superficial
expressing moral judgments, often preachily

Examples

Her constant moralizing made her unpopular at parties.
The teacher's moralizing was met with eye-rolls from the students.
Instead of helpful advice, all I received was endless moralizing.

Synset Examples

his constant moralizing drove me mad

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preaching - giving a sermon or delivering moral or religious messages
  • Degree Higher: condemning - expressing strong disapproval or criticism
