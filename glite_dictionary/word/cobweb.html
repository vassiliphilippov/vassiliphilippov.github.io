cobweb

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑbˌwɛb / (AmE), / ˈkɒbˌwɛb / (BrE)
A dense, irregular spider web, often found in neglected places, typically more elaborate and tangled than an orb web.
a tangled spider web

Examples

The old attic was covered in cobwebs, making it look abandoned.
She brushed a cobweb from the corner of the ceiling.
A thick cobweb stretched across the unused bookshelf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spider web - a web spun by spiders; general term for silk structures spun by spiders

Etymology

From Middle English cobweb, cob (spider) + web.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːb.wɛb / (AmE), / ˈkɒb.wɛb / (BrE)
A very fine, delicate, and transparent fabric that resembles a spider's web.
delicate, transparent fabric

Examples

The bride's veil was made of exquisite cobweb that shimmered in the light.
She chose a scarf of cobweb to complement her delicate evening dress.
Museum conservators handled the ancient cobweb lace with utmost care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gossamer - very light, thin, and delicate material

Etymology

From Middle English cobweb, from 'coppe' (spider) and 'web'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03063937-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɑbˌwɛb / (AmE), / ˈkɒbˌwɛb / (BrE)
a web spun by a spider, especially when old or covered with dust
spider's web, usually old or dusty

Examples

Ancient houses often have cobwebs hanging from the ceiling corners.
She brushed a cobweb aside as she entered the abandoned attic.
Sunlight revealed the delicate structure of a cobweb in the window frame.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiderweb - a web made by a spider to catch insects

Etymology

From Middle English 'copweb', with 'cop' meaning 'spider'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03063809-n
Synset POS: n
