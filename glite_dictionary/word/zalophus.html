Zalophus

1. NOUN

/ zəˈlɑːfəs / (AmE), / zəˈləʊfəs / (BrE)
A genus of sea lions found along the coasts of the eastern Pacific Ocean, including the California sea lion.
a genus of sea lions

Examples

The California sea lion belongs to the genus Zalophus, which is native to the Pacific coastline.
Scientists studied the behavior and population of Zalophus in their natural habitat.
The distinctive bark of Zalophus species is often heard along the beaches of California.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea lion - a large marine mammal of the family Otariidae

Etymology

From Greek 'za-' (intensive prefix) + 'lophos' (crested, tufted), referring to a crested seal.

2. NOUN

/ zəˈlɑːfəs / (AmE), / zəˈləʊfəs / (BrE)
A genus of sea lions found in the eastern Pacific, including the California sea lion.
genus of sea lions

Examples

The California sea lion belongs to the genus Zalophus, which inhabits the Pacific coastline.
Researchers studied the vocalizations of Zalophus in their natural environment.
One member of the genus Zalophus is now considered extinct due to overhunting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea lion - large marine mammal of the family Otariidae

Etymology

From Ancient Greek ζῆν (zēn, 'to live') + λόφος (lóphos, 'crest').
