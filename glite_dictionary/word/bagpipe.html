bagpipe

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæɡˌpaɪp / (AmE), / ˈbæɡˌpaɪp / (BrE)
a musical instrument with a bag and pipes, played by blowing air into the bag and squeezing it to force air through the pipes
musical instrument with a bag and pipes

Examples

The haunting sound of the bagpipe echoed through the hills of Scotland.
He learned to play the bagpipe as a tribute to his heritage.
At the parade, a lone bagpipe player led the procession.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipes - a set of bagpipes; informal term, especially in Scotland

Etymology

From Middle English 'bagpype', combining 'bag' and 'pipe'.
