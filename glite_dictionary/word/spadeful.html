spadeful

1. NOUN

/ ˈspeɪdˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈspeɪd.fʊl / (BrE)
The amount that a spade can hold or lift at one time.
amount held by a spade

Examples

He tossed another spadeful of dirt onto the growing pile.
With every spadeful, the hole became deeper and wider.
She carefully dumped a spadeful of compost into the planting bed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shovelful - the amount a shovel can hold

Etymology

From 'spade' (a digging tool) + '-ful' (as much as can be held by).
