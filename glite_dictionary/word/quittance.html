quittance

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪt.əns / (AmE), / ˈkwɪt.əns / (BrE)
the act of repaying or settling a debt or obligation; a release from a debt or claim
repayment or release from a debt

Examples

The full quittance of his debts gave him great relief.
Upon receipt of quittance, the lender released the property documents.
He handed over the money as quittance for his long-standing obligation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: payment - the action or process of paying someone or something

Etymology

From Old French *quittance* (release, discharge), from *quiter* (to release, acquit).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪtns / (AmE), / ˈkwɪt.əns / (BrE)
a formal document or receipt certifying that a person has been released from an obligation, debt, or claim
formal receipt releasing from a debt or claim

Examples

The landlord provided a quittance after all outstanding rent was paid.
He kept the quittance as proof that the debt had been settled.
Without a quittance, she could not confirm that her loan was fully repaid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: receipt - a written acknowledgement of receiving something, such as money or goods

Etymology

From Old French 'quittance' (discharge, release), from 'quiter' (to release, quit).
