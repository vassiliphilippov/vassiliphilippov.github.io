Corydalis solida

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːrɪˈdælɪs səˈlaɪdə / (AmE), / ˌkɒrɪˈdælɪs sɒˈlɪdə / (BrE)
a perennial herb native to northern Europe and Asia, with erect racemes of red or purplish flowers
herb with red racemes, Europe and Asia

Examples

Corydalis solida appears in woodlands throughout northern Europe in early spring.
Gardeners often plant Corydalis solida for its striking red flowers and delicate foliage.
The tuber of Corydalis solida allows it to survive harsh winter conditions.

Etymology

From New Latin: Corydalis from Greek korydalís (crested lark), solida from Latin solidus (solid), referencing the solid root tuber.
