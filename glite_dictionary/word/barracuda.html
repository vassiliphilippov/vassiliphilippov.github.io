barracuda

1. NOUN

/ ˌbærəˈkudə / (AmE), / ˌbærəˈkuːdə / (BrE)
a large, predatory tropical marine fish with a long, slender body and powerful jaws filled with sharp teeth, found chiefly in warm seas
large predatory tropical marine fish

Examples

A school of barracuda swam swiftly past the coral reef, searching for prey.
Fishermen are often wary of barracuda because of their sharp teeth and aggressive behavior.
The barracuda is known for its speed and ferocity in the open ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pike - a large predatory freshwater fish with a long body and pointed snout

Etymology

From Spanish barracuda, of uncertain origin, possibly from Carib or American Spanish.
