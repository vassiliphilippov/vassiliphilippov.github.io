Prumnopitys elegans

1. NOUN

/ prʌm.noʊˈpɪt.is ˈɛl.ɪ.gænz / (AmE), / prʌm.nəʊˈpɪt.ɪs ˈɛl.ɪ.gænz / (BrE)
A South American evergreen tree or shrub belonging to the yew family (Podocarpaceae), native to parts of Chile and Argentina.
South American evergreen tree or shrub species

Examples

The Prumnopitys elegans is prized for its beautiful dark green foliage in botanical gardens.
Researchers studied the growth conditions of Prumnopitys elegans in the Andes mountains.
Timber from Prumnopitys elegans is occasionally used in local construction in southern Chile.

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Prumnopitys' refers to 'prumno-' (plum) and '-pitys' (pine), referencing fruit and coniferous features; 'elegans' means 'elegant, fine'.
