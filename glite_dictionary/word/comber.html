comber

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmər / (AmE), / ˈkɒmə / (BrE)
a machine that separates and straightens the fibers of cotton or wool during textile processing
machine for separating and straightening fibers

Examples

The comber ensures the cotton fibers are uniform before spinning.
Modern textile mills often use automated combers to increase efficiency.
After passing through the comber, the wool was ready for further processing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmər / (AmE), / ˈkɒmbə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to separate and straighten cotton or wool fibers during textile processing
person who straightens fiber in textiles

Examples

The experienced comber ensured the cotton fibers were properly aligned before spinning.
Many women worked as combers in the wool mills during the nineteenth century.
A skilled comber was highly valued in traditional textile manufacturing.

Etymology

From 'comb' + '-er', meaning one who combs fibers.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊmər / (AmE), / ˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
a large, long, curling sea wave, especially one that breaks on the shore
large curling sea wave

Examples

The surfers waited eagerly for the next comber to roll in from the open ocean.
A massive comber crashed onto the rocks, sending up a spray of saltwater.
From the cliff, we watched each comber crest and thunder onto the beach below.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breaker - a heavy sea wave that breaks into white foam

Etymology

From Latin *combere* (to comb), referring to the cresting, comb-like appearance of the wave.
