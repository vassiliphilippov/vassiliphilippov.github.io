Francis Crick

1. PROPN

/ ˈfrænsɪs krɪk / (AmE), / ˈfrɑːnsɪs krɪk / (BrE)
(1916–2004) An English biochemist and co-discoverer of the helical structure of DNA with James Watson.
English biochemist, DNA co-discoverer

Examples

The contributions of Francis Crick to molecular biology revolutionized genetics.
In 1953, Francis Crick and James Watson unveiled the double helix structure of DNA.
Many textbooks highlight Francis Crick as a key figure in the discovery of DNA's structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: James Watson - American molecular biologist, co-discoverer of DNA structure
  • Synonym: Rosalind Franklin - English chemist whose data contributed to the discovery of DNA structure
