polydactyl

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɑːliˈdæktəl / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈdæktɪl / (BrE)
having more than the usual number of fingers or toes on a hand or foot.
having extra fingers or toes
medical

Examples

The polydactyl cat had six toes on each paw, making her quite unique among her littermates.
Doctors confirmed that the newborn baby was polydactyl, with an extra finger on his left hand.
Being polydactyl did not affect his ability to play the piano expertly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: syndactyl - having fused fingers or toes
  • Antonym: normal - having the standard number of digits

Etymology

From Greek 'polydaktulos', from 'poly-' (many) + 'daktulos' (finger).
