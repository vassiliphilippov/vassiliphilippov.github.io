hornstone

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrnˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈhɔːn.stəʊn / (BrE)
a fine-grained, hard metamorphic rock produced by the exposure of clay rocks to heat, typically characterized by its toughness and flinty fracture.
fine-grained metamorphic rock

Examples

The geologist identified a layer of hornstone at the site, indicating past volcanic activity.
Artifacts made from hornstone were found during the archaeological excavation.
Because of its hardness, hornstone was often used for making tools in ancient times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hornfels - a metamorphic rock formed by contact with hot magma

Etymology

From 'horn' + 'stone', denoting its hardness and tough, horn-like appearance.
