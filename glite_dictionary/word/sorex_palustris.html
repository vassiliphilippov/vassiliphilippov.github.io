Sorex palustris

1. NOUN

/ sɔːˈrɛks pəˈlʌstrɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɔːrɛks pəˈlʌstrɪs / (BrE)
A species of water shrew native to North America, known for its semi-aquatic habits.
North American water shrew species

Examples

The Sorex palustris is remarkable for its ability to hunt underwater in streams and ponds.
Researchers observed that Sorex palustris prefers habitats with dense vegetation near water bodies.
Unlike many other shrew species, Sorex palustris has stiff hairs on its feet to aid in swimming.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Sorex' meaning 'shrew', 'palustris' meaning 'of the marsh'.
