Cynodontia

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪ.nəˈdɒn.ʃi.ə / (AmE), / ˌsɪ.nəˈdɒn.tɪ.ə / (BrE)
a division of extinct therapsid reptiles from the Triassic period, characterized by mammal-like teeth and regarded as ancestors of mammals
division of extinct mammal-like reptiles

Examples

Many paleontologists believe that Cynodontia is a crucial group in the evolution from reptiles to mammals.
The fossils of Cynodontia display distinctive mammal-like teeth, setting them apart from other Triassic reptiles.
Research on Cynodontia helps us understand how early synapsids adapted to their environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: therapsid - member of the order of prehistoric synapsids including mammal ancestors

Etymology

From Greek 'kynos' (dog) + 'odous' (tooth), referring to their dog-like teeth.

2. NOUN

/ saɪˈnɒntiə / (AmE), / sɪˈnɒntiə / (BrE)
a division of the order Therapsida from the Triassic period, comprising small carnivorous tetrapod reptiles often with mammal-like teeth, considered important in the evolution of mammals
extinct reptilian division related to early mammals

Examples

The fossils of Cynodontia provide important clues about the transition from reptiles to mammals.
Paleontologists discovered a new species within the Cynodontia near the ancient riverbed.
Comparing jaws of Cynodontia and early mammals has helped scientists understand evolutionary changes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Therapsida - a major group of synapsids to which Cynodontia belongs

Etymology

From Greek 'kynos' (dog) + 'odous' (tooth), referring to dog-like teeth.
