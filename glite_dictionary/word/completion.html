completion

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpliːʃən / (AmE), / kəmˈpliːʃən / (BrE)
the action or process of finishing something; the state of being finished
act or process of finishing

Examples

The project reached completion ahead of schedule, thanks to the team's hard work.
We eagerly awaited the completion of the building so we could move in.
The certificate will be awarded upon successful completion of the course.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completion - state of being finished; the process of bringing to an end
  • Antonym: commencement - the beginning or start of something

Etymology

From Latin 'completio', from 'complere' (to fill up, finish).

2. NOUN

/ kəmˈpliʃən / (AmE), / kəmˈpliːʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
(in American football) a successful forward pass that is caught by a teammate, advancing play.
successful forward pass (American football)
american

Examples

The quarterback threw a completion on third down to keep the drive alive.
Her first completion of the game was a 20-yard pass to the wide receiver.
After several failed attempts, the team finally managed a crucial completion in the final quarter.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incompletion - an unsuccessful forward pass (American football)
