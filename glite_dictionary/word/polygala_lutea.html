Polygala lutea

1. NOUN

/ pəˌlɪɡələ ˈluːtiə / (AmE), / pəˌlɪɡələ ˈluːtɪə / (BrE)
A bog plant native to the pine barrens of the southeastern United States, distinguished by spikes of irregular yellow-orange flowers.
North American bog plant with yellow-orange flowers

Examples

The rare Polygala lutea thrives in boggy areas of the southeastern United States.
We studied Polygala lutea because of its striking yellow-orange flower spikes.
Conservationists are concerned about habitat loss affecting native species like Polygala lutea.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Polygala' meaning 'much milk', and 'lutea' meaning 'yellow'.
