resolutely

1. ADV

/ ˈrɛzəˌluːtli / (AmE), / ˈrɛzəluːtli / (BrE)
In a determined and unwavering way; with firmness of purpose.
with determination; firmly

Examples

He resolutely refused to change his plans despite criticism from others.
She resolutely pursued her goals no matter how difficult the path became.
They stood resolutely together in the face of adversity.

Synset Examples

‘I will come along,’ she said decisively

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firmly - in a strong and determined way
  • Synonym: decisively - in a way that shows the ability to make decisions quickly and effectively
  • Antonym: hesitantly - in a way that shows uncertainty or indecision

Etymology

From Latin 'resolutus' (resolved, determined) + English adverbial suffix -ly.

2. ADV

/ ˈrɛz.ə.luːt.li / (AmE), / ˈrɛz.ə.ljuːt.li / (BrE)
in a way that shows firm determination or purpose
with firm determination

Examples

Despite opposition, she resolutely defended her position in the meeting.
He resolutely pushed forward with his plan despite the risks involved.
When criticized, she resolutely stood by her beliefs without hesitation.

Synset Examples

she resolutely refused to look at him or speak to him
he entered the building resolutely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: determinedly - in a determined way
  • Antonym: hesitantly - in a hesitant or unsure way

Etymology

From 'resolute' + '-ly'; 'resolute' from Latin 'resolutus' (loosened, resolved).
