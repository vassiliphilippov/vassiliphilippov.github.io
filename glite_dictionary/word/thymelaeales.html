Thymelaeales

1. NOUN

/ θaɪˌmɛliˈeɪliːz / (AmE), / θɪˌmiːlɪˈeɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants that includes families such as Thymelaeaceae, known for shrubs and small trees with tough bark
order of flowering plants

Examples

The Thymelaeales include several families of shrubs and small trees with medicinal properties.
Researchers are studying the unique chemical compounds found in Thymelaeales.
Many members of the Thymelaeales are important for their tough fibers and traditional uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Malvales - order of flowering plants related to mallows

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Thymelaea (a genus name) and the botanical suffix –ales (indicating an order).

2. NOUN

/ θaɪˌmiːliˈeɪliːz / (AmE), / θɪˌmiːliˈiːliːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants including the family Thymelaeaceae, characterized by shrubs and small trees with fibrous bark and simple leaves
order of flowering plants

Examples

The family Thymelaeaceae belongs to the order Thymelaeales.
Several economically important plants are classified under the Thymelaeales.
Botanists usually refer to Thymelaeales when discussing the taxonomy of these shrubs.

Etymology

From the family name Thymelaeaceae, itself from Greek 'thymelē' (altar) and 'alea' (wandering), with the botanical order suffix '-ales'.
