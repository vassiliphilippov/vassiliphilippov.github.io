waterworks

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚˌwɝks / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌwɜːks / (BrE)
a public facility or organization that supplies water to a community
public utility supplying water

Examples

The city's waterworks ensure that every household has access to clean tap water.
The government invested in modernizing the aging waterworks infrastructure last year.
Local engineers are responsible for maintaining the waterworks to prevent any disruption in service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water utility - a company or organization supplying water to public or private consumers

Etymology

From 'water' + 'works', referring to the complex of buildings and machinery for supplying water.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚˌwɝks / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌwɜːks / (BrE)
a place or system where water is collected, treated, and supplied for use by a community
place for treating and supplying water

Examples

The city built a new waterworks to provide clean drinking water for its residents.
After the storm, damage to the waterworks disrupted the local water supply.
Tours of the old waterworks plant are offered to teach students about water treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water utility - an organization supplying water to the public
  • Antonym: sewage works - plant for processing and treating wastewater

Etymology

From 'water' + 'works', meaning a facility where water-related work is carried out.
