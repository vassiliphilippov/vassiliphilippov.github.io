colors

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlərz / (AmE), / ˈkʌləz / (BrE)
the flag, insignia, or emblem representing an organization, group, or country, often used to show membership or allegiance
emblem or flag of a group or nation

Examples

He wore his military uniform with pride, displaying his regiment's colors.
The school's colors were prominently featured at the athletic competition.
They marched under their national colors during the parade.

Synset Examples

his tie proclaimed his school colors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flag - a piece of cloth with a special design used as a symbol
  • Synonym: banner - a long piece of cloth bearing a slogan or design, used as an emblem

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlərz / (AmE), / ˈkʌləz / (BrE)
the flag or flags that represent a country, regiment, or organization, especially in a military or ceremonial context
the flag of a country or organization

Examples

The soldiers saluted the colors as they marched past the reviewing stand.
The ship proudly flew its national colors in foreign waters.
Raising the regiment's colors is a tradition at every military ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flag - a piece of cloth with a distinctive design used as a symbol or signal

Etymology

From Middle English 'colours', plural of 'colour' (flag or ensign), from Old French 'color', from Latin 'color' (colour, shade, hue); military use referring to flags dates from the 16th century.
