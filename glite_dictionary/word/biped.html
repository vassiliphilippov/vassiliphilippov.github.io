biped

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪˌpɛd / (AmE), / ˈbʌɪpɛd / (BrE)
An animal that walks on two feet, especially as its regular form of movement.
animal with two feet

Examples

The ostrich is a notable biped capable of running at high speeds.
Early humans evolved from quadrupeds into bipeds millions of years ago.
The museum exhibit explained how being a biped helped some species survive.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: quadruped - an animal with four feet

Etymology

From Latin bi- (two) + ped (foot).

2. ADJ

/ ˈbaɪ.pɛd / (AmE), / ˈbʌɪ.pɛd / (BrE)
(of an animal) having two feet; walking on two legs.
having two feet; walking on two legs

Examples

Humans are biped creatures, unlike most mammals that move on four legs.
The fossil showed a biped dinosaur that once roamed the ancient forests.
Some robots are designed to be biped so they can walk up stairs easily.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: quadruped - an animal having four feet

Etymology

From Latin 'bipedis', from 'bi-' (two) + 'pes' (foot).
