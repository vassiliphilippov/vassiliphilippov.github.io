stiffly

1. ADV

/ ˈstɪfli / (AmE), / ˈstɪfli / (BrE)
In a way that is rigid, lacking ease or flexibility; or in a formal or unfriendly manner.
in a rigid or formal manner

Examples

She walked stiffly after injuring her back.
He nodded stiffly, not wanting to appear too friendly.
The new employee greeted everyone stiffly on his first day.

Synset Examples

his hands lay stiffly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rigidly - in a stiff or inflexible manner
  • Synonym: awkwardly - in an uncomfortable or uneasy manner
  • Antonym: loosely - in a relaxed or flexible manner

Etymology

From 'stiff' + '-ly', with 'stiff' from Old English 'stíf' (rigid).

2. ADV

/ ˈstɪfli / (AmE), / ˈstɪfli / (BrE)
in a rigid, not relaxed, or formal manner; with little flexibility or ease
in a rigid or inflexible way

Examples

She walked stiffly after sitting at her desk for hours.
He greeted the new manager stiffly, betraying his discomfort.
The old flag stood stiffly in the wind, barely moving.

Synset Examples

the body was rigidly erect
he sat bolt upright

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rigidly - in a completely inflexible or strict way
  • Antonym: loosely - in a relaxed or flexible way

3. ADV

/ ˈstɪfli / (AmE), / ˈstɪfli / (BrE)
In a manner that is rigid, not flexible, or lacking in ease; with little or no movement or emotion.
in a rigid or unyielding manner

Examples

He greeted her stiffly, avoiding any display of emotion.
After sleeping awkwardly, she walked stiffly throughout the morning.
The old flag fluttered stiffly in the barely moving wind.

Synset Examples

his hands lay stiffly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rigidly - in a stiff or unbending manner
  • Antonym: loosely - in a relaxed or flexible manner

Etymology

Formed from 'stiff' + adverbial suffix '-ly'.
