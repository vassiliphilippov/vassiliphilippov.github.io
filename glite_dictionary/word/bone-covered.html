bone-covered

1. ADJ

/ ˈboʊn ˌkʌvərd / (AmE), / ˈbəʊn ˌkʌvəd / (BrE)
(of animals) having an outer layer or armor made of bone.
having an outer covering of bone

Examples

The ancient fish was bone-covered, protecting it from most predators.
Many armadillos are not truly bone-covered, but their shells resemble bony armor.
Scientists discovered a bone-covered fossil species that lived in the Devonian period.
