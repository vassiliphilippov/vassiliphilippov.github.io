trenchancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛntʃ.ən.si / (AmE), / ˈtrɛn.tʃən.si / (BrE)
The quality of being keen, sharp, or incisive in thought, expression, or intellect.
keenness or incisiveness of thought or expression

Examples

The trenchancy of her argument left the committee with little room for objection.
Critics have praised the author’s trenchancy in addressing complex social issues.
He spoke with such trenchancy that even his opponents were impressed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incisiveness - the quality of being clear and direct in thought or expression
  • Antonym: bluntness - the quality of lacking sharpness or keenness in expression

Etymology

From 'trenchant' (sharp, incisive), which comes from Old French 'trenchant', present participle of 'trenchier' (to cut).
