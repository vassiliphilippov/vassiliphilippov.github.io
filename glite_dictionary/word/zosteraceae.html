Zosteraceae

1. NOUN

/ zoʊˌstɛr.əˈsi.aɪ / (AmE), / zɒˌstɛr.əˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
A family of marine flowering plants, also known as the eelgrass family, sometimes considered equivalent to Potamogetonaceae in some classifications.
family of marine flowering plants (eelgrass family)

Examples

The Zosteraceae family is commonly known as the eelgrass family.
Coastal ecosystems often benefit from the presence of Zosteraceae plants.
Some botanists group Zosteraceae with Potamogetonaceae in certain classifications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Potamogetonaceae - a family of aquatic flowering plants, the pondweed family

Etymology

From the genus name Zostera, from Greek zōstēr (belt, girdle), referring to the ribbon-like leaves.
