unsuitability

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnˌsuːtəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌsjuːtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of not being appropriate or suitable for a particular purpose or situation
the state of being unsuitable

Examples

The unsuitability of the site for building became clear after heavy rains caused flooding.
Her resume showed the unsuitability of her experience for the technical role.
Environmental concerns highlighted the unsuitability of this method for large-scale farming.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: suitability - the quality of being appropriate or fitting for a purpose

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'suitability', from 'suitable' (fitting, appropriate) + '-ity'.
