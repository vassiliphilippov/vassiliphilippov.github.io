Cnidoscolus

1. NOUN

/ nay-də-ˈskəʊ-ləs / (AmE), / nɪ-dəʊ-ˈskəʊ-ləs / (BrE)
a genus of perennial plants with bristles, belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae, native to the Americas
genus of bristly perennial plants

Examples

Cnidoscolus species are often called 'spurge nettle' due to their bristly stems and leaves.
Botanists study Cnidoscolus for its toxic sap and unique adaptation strategies.
The leaves of Cnidoscolus can cause skin irritation if touched.

Etymology

/ knaɪdəˈskoʊləs / (AmE), / knaɪdəˈskɒləs / (BrE)
A genus of perennial plants with bristles, in the spurge family (Euphorbiaceae), native to the Americas.
genus of bristly perennial plants (Euphorbiaceae)

Examples

The leaves of Cnidoscolus are covered with stiff, stinging hairs that can irritate the skin.
Many species within the genus Cnidoscolus are native to tropical regions of the Americas.
The agricultural potential of Cnidoscolus has been studied because of its drought resistance.

Etymology

From Greek knidē (nettle) + skolós (thorn, prickle), referring to the plant's bristly nature.
