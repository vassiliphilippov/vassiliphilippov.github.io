contaminating

1. ADJ

/ kənˈtæməˌneɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈtæmɪneɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
causing pollution or making something impure, especially by introducing harmful substances such as radioactive material
causing pollution or impurity

Examples

Scientists warned that the contaminating leak could endanger nearby water supplies for years.
Careless disposal of waste can have a contaminating effect on the local ecosystem.
The accident released a contaminating cloud of radioactive material into the atmosphere.

Synset Examples

the air near the foundry was always dirty
a dirty bomb releases enormous amounts of long-lived radioactive fallout

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polluting - causing harmful substances to enter the environment
  • Antonym: purifying - removing contaminants, making clean or pure

Etymology

From Latin contaminare (to defile, pollute), via the verb contaminate + -ing.

2. ADJ

/ kənˈtæməˌneɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈtæmɪneɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
causing something to become impure or harmful by contact with something unclean, infectious, or undesirable
making impure or harmful by contact

Examples

The contaminating bacteria in the water made several people sick.
A contaminating influence spread throughout the laboratory after the chemical spill.
The factory was fined for releasing contaminating substances into the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infecting - causing infection
  • Antonym: purifying - removing contaminants or making pure

Etymology

Present participle of 'contaminate', from Latin 'contaminare' (to defile, pollute).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01306384-s
Synset POS: s
