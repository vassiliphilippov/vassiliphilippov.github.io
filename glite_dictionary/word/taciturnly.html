taciturnly

1. ADV

/ ˈtæsɪˌtɝnli / (AmE), / ˈtæsɪˌtɜːnli / (BrE)
In a manner that is silent and reserved; without speaking or saying much.
in a silent or reserved manner

Examples

She sat taciturnly in the meeting, offering no comments on the matter.
He listened taciturnly to the conversation, his expression revealing nothing.
The child stood taciturnly in the corner, refusing to answer any questions.

Synset Examples

he sat mutely next to her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silently - without making any sound
  • Antonym: talkatively - in a manner involving much talking

Etymology

From taciturn + -ly; taciturn from Latin taciturnus (habitually silent).
