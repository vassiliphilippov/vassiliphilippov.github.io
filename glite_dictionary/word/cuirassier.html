cuirassier

1. NOUN

/ kwɪˈræs.i.ɚ / (AmE), / kwɪəˈræs.i.ə / (BrE)
A soldier in the cavalry armed with a cuirass and often other heavy armor, especially in European armies from the 16th to the 19th centuries.
heavily armored cavalryman

Examples

The cuirassier charged into battle, his metal armor gleaming in the sunlight.
Napoleon's army relied heavily on the strength of its cuirassiers during key engagements.
The museum displayed a full uniform worn by a French cuirassier in the early 1800s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cavalryman - a soldier who fights mounted on horseback

Etymology

From French 'cuirassier', from 'cuirasse' (cuirass).
