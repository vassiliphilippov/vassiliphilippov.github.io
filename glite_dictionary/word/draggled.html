draggled

1. ADJ

/ ˈdræɡəld / (AmE), / ˈdræɡəld / (BrE)
Limp and dirty, as if having been dragged through mud or water.
limp and dirty, as if dragged through mud

Examples

After the storm, the children returned home with their draggled clothes and muddy boots.
The cat looked draggled after being caught in the rain.
His once neat letter fluttered into the room, draggled and torn from the journey.

Synset Examples

the beggar's bedraggled clothes
scarecrows in battered hats or draggled skirts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bedraggled - wet, dirty, and untidy from being dragged through mud or water

Etymology

From 'drag' + '-led', past participle of 'drag'.
