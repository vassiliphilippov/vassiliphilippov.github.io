Salvia clarea

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæl.vi.ə ˈklɑːr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˈsal.vɪ.ə ˈkleə.ri.ə / (BrE)
A stout Mediterranean sage with white, pink, or violet flowers, cultivated for its aromatic oil used in flavoring and perfumery.
species of Mediterranean sage used for aromatic oil

Examples

The essential oil extracted from Salvia clarea is highly valued in aromatherapy.
Gardeners often choose Salvia clarea for its decorative, fragrant blossoms.
Perfume makers prize Salvia clarea for its unique and soothing scent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clary sage - common name for Salvia clarea, an aromatic Mediterranean herb

Etymology

From Latin *Salvia* (the sage genus) + *clarea* (relating to clary).
