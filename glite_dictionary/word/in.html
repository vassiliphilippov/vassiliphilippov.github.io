in

1. ADJ

/ ɪn / (AmE), / ɪn / (BrE)
Fashionable or popular at the present time.
fashionable; popular at this time
informal

Examples

Shoulder pads are really in this season, so everyone is wearing them.
Minimalist décor is in among young homeowners these days.
Bell-bottom jeans were in during the 1970s.

Synset Examples

the in thing to do
large shoulder pads are in

Related Terms

  • Antonym: out - unfashionable; not popular at this time
  • Synonym: trendy - very fashionable or up to date

2. ADV

/ ɪn / (AmE), / ɪn / (BrE)
towards or to the inside of a place, container, or space.
towards the inside

Examples

She walked in after knocking on the door.
Water spilled in through the open window during the storm.
The cat ran in as soon as I opened the door.

Synset Examples

come in
smash in the door

Related Terms

  • Antonym: out - away from the inside or center

Etymology

From Old English 'in' (in, into) from Proto-Germanic '*in' (in, into).
