septic

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛptɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɛptɪk / (BrE)
Containing or caused by disease-causing microorganisms; infected or associated with infection.
infected; relating to disease-causing organisms

Examples

The wound became septic after being left untreated for days.
Doctors prescribed antibiotics to treat the septic infection.
Sewage leaks can create a highly septic environment.

Synset Examples

a septic sore throat
a septic environment
septic sewage

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aseptic - free from contamination by harmful microorganisms; sterile

Etymology

From Greek 'septikos' (causing putrefaction) via Latin 'septicus'.

WordNet ID: oewn-02121107-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɛptɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɛptɪk / (BrE)
relating to or caused by infection with harmful microorganisms, or involving putrefaction.
relating to infection or putrefaction

Examples

The wound became septic after being left untreated for several days.
Sewage treatment plants are designed to manage septic waste safely.
A septic environment can be hazardous to both people and animals.

Synset Examples

the septic action occurs at the bottom of the septic tank

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aseptic - free from infection or contamination
  • Synonym: infectious - causing or capable of causing infection

Etymology

From Greek sēptikos (putrefactive), from sēpein (to make rotten).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03010177-a
Synset POS: a
