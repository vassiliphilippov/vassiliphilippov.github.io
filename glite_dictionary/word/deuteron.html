deuteron

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːtərˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈdjuːtərɒn / (BrE)
the nucleus of deuterium, consisting of one proton and one neutron, often used as a bombarding particle in nuclear accelerators
nucleus of deuterium (one proton, one neutron)

Examples

The deuteron is commonly used in particle accelerators for nuclear physics experiments.
Fusion reactions in stars can involve the collision of a proton with a deuteron.
Scientists measured the magnetic moment of the deuteron to study nuclear forces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proton - positively charged subatomic particle in atomic nuclei
  • Synonym: neutron - uncharged subatomic particle in atomic nuclei

Etymology

From deuterium, with '-on' suffix as in 'proton' and 'neutron'.
