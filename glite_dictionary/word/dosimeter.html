dosimeter

1. NOUN

/ doʊˈsɪmətər / (AmE), / dəʊˈsɪmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure the amount of exposure to ionizing radiation, such as X-rays or radioactive substances
device measuring radiation exposure
medical

Examples

The technician wore a dosimeter to monitor radiation levels during the experiment.
Each hospital staff member is required to check their dosimeter at the end of every shift.
A pocket dosimeter provides an immediate reading of accumulated radiation exposure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Geiger counter - an instrument for detecting and measuring ionizing radiation

Etymology

From Greek 'dosis' (a giving, dose) + '-meter' (measuring device).
