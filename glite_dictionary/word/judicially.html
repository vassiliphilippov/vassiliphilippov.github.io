judicially

1. ADV

/ dʒuˈdɪʃəli / (AmE), / dʒuˈdɪʃəli / (BrE)
In a manner relating to courts of law or judges; in a judicial way.
in a manner relating to judges or courts

Examples

The contract was judicially interpreted by the Supreme Court.
The property was judicially seized after the trial concluded.
All disputes are to be judicially resolved in accordance with the law.

Synset Examples

judicially controlled process

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legally - in a manner relating to the law

Etymology

From 'judicial' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'judicialis' (of judgment).

2. ADV

/ dʒuˈdɪʃəli / (AmE), / dʒuˈdɪʃəli / (BrE)
In a manner ordered or determined by a law court; by or through judicial authority.
by or through judicial authority
legal

Examples

The dispute was judicially resolved after a lengthy court case.
His assets were judicially seized following the verdict.
The company was judicially dissolved due to its failure to comply with regulations.

Etymology

From Latin iudicialis (judicial), from iudicium (judgment) + -ly.
