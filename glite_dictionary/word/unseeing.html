unseeing

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsiːɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈsiːɪŋ / (BrE)
not consciously noticing or registering what is before one's eyes; not observing or perceiving
not consciously observing; not perceiving

Examples

She wandered the street with an unseeing gaze, lost in her thoughts.
His unseeing eyes stared out the window as he replayed the news in his mind.
The child sat unseeing at the breakfast table, too tired to notice anything around her.

Synset Examples

looked through him with blank unseeing eyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blind - unable to see; lacking sight

Etymology

From un- (not) + seeing (present participle of 'see').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01750886-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsiːɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈsiːɪŋ / (BrE)
not able to see; lacking the ability to perceive visually.
lacking sight or visual perception

Examples

The unseeing man walked carefully through the crowded market.
She turned her unseeing eyes toward the window, lost in thought.
After the accident, his gaze became unseeing, fixed on a distant point.

Synset Examples

blind as an eyeless beggar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blind - unable to see; without sight

Etymology

un- (not) + seeing (present participle of see).
