dinoflagellate

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪnoʊˈflædʒəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌdɪnəʊˈflædʒɪlət / (BrE)
a single-celled, mostly marine organism with two flagella, important as a major component of plankton
marine planktonic protozoan
medical

Examples

Many dinoflagellates are photosynthetic and form the base of the ocean's food chain.
A harmful algal bloom was caused by a rapid increase in dinoflagellate populations.
Scientists study dinoflagellates to understand their role in marine ecosystems.

Etymology

From Greek dinos (whirling) + Latin flagellum (whip), referring to their spinning movement and whip-like appendages.
