Discina

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsaɪnə / (AmE), / dɪˈsiːnə / (BrE)
A genus of cup-shaped fungi found chiefly in temperate regions, some of which are known as false morels.
a genus of cup fungi

Examples

Several species of Discina are often found in damp forests during spring and early summer.
The cup-shaped fruiting bodies of Discina are sometimes confused with edible morels.
Mycologists collected specimens of Discina for further study in the laboratory.

Etymology

From Greek 'diskos' (disk), referring to the shape of the fruiting body.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈsaɪnə / (AmE), / dɪˈsiːnə / (BrE)
a genus of fungi in the family Helvellaceae, characterized by cup-shaped or saucer-shaped fruiting bodies and ornamented spores
genus of cup-shaped fungi

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Discina during the forest survey.
The characteristic saucer shape of Discina fruiting bodies helps distinguish them from other fungi.
Many members of Discina grow in conifer forests and are studied for their unique spores.

Etymology

From Greek 'diskos' (disk, dish), referring to the shape of the fruiting body.
