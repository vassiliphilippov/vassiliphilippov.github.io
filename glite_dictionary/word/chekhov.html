Chekhov

1. PROPN

/ ˈtʃɛkˌhɔf / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkɒf / (BrE)
Anton Chekhov (1860–1904), Russian dramatist and short story writer, noted for plays exploring human communication and emotion.
Russian playwright and short story writer

Examples

Many critics consider Chekhov a master of modern theater.
We discussed the subtle humor found in Chekhov's short stories during literature class.
Her performance in the Chekhov play was praised for its depth and emotion.

Etymology

Transliteration of Russian surname Чехов (Čéxov).
