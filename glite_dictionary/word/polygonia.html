Polygonia

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlɪɡəniə / (AmE), / pɒˈlɪɡəniə / (BrE)
A genus of butterflies commonly known as comma butterflies, with angular wings and distinctive markings.
genus of comma butterflies

Examples

The Polygonia flies are known for their jagged wing edges and unique markings.
Scientists studied several species within the Polygonia genus to document their migration patterns.
In early spring, Polygonia butterflies can often be seen basking in sunny woodland clearings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comma butterfly - a butterfly of the species Polygonia c-album, known for the C-shaped mark on its wing

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) and 'gōnia' (angle), referring to the angular shape of the wings.
