Quaoar

1. PROPN

/ ˈkwɑː.wɑr / (AmE), / ˈkwɑː.wɑː / (BrE)
a large planetoid in the outer Solar System, discovered in 2002, located in the Kuiper Belt beyond Pluto
planetoid in the outer Solar System

Examples

Astronomers announced the discovery of Quaoar in 2002, expanding our knowledge of the Solar System.
The orbit of Quaoar lies beyond that of Pluto and within the Kuiper Belt.
A small moon named Weywot was later found orbiting Quaoar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eris - a large trans-Neptunian dwarf planet
  • Synonym: Pluto - a dwarf planet in the Kuiper Belt, formerly considered the ninth planet

Etymology

Named after the creation deity Quaoar of the Tongva people, native to the Los Angeles area.
