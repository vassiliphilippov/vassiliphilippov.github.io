divarication

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌvɛrɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌvɑːrɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the state or process of branching or spreading apart at a wide angle, especially in anatomical or botanical contexts
a wide-angled branching or separation

Examples

The divarication of the tree's main limbs gives it a striking, open appearance.
An unusual divarication was noted in the blood vessels near the heart.
Botanists studied the divarication of the shrub's branches to identify the species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: branching - the act of dividing into branches or subdivisions
  • Antonym: convergence - the process of coming together or meeting at a point

Etymology

From Latin divaricatio (a spreading apart), from divaricare (to spread apart).
