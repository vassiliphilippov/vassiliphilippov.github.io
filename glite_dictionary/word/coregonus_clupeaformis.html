Coregonus clupeaformis

1. NOUN

/ kɔːˈrɛɡənəs kluːˌpiəˈfɔːrmɪs / (AmE), / kɒˈrɛɡənəs kluːˌpiːəˈfɔːmɪs / (BrE)
a species of freshwater whitefish found in the Great Lakes and northward to Alaska, valued for its food and commercial importance
a North American freshwater whitefish species

Examples

Commercial fishermen harvest Coregonus clupeaformis in large numbers from the Great Lakes each year.
The biologist studied the migratory behavior of Coregonus clupeaformis in Alaskan rivers.
Smoked Coregonus clupeaformis is considered a delicacy in many parts of Canada.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lake whitefish - common name for Coregonus clupeaformis

Etymology

From scientific Latin; genus name Coregonus from Greek 'kore' (pupil of the eye) + 'gonos' (seed), species name 'clupeaformis' meaning 'herring-shaped'.
