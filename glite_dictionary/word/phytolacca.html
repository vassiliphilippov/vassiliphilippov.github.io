Phytolacca

1. NOUN

/ faɪtoʊˈlækə / (AmE), / fʌɪtəʊˈlakə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants, commonly known as pokeweed, in the family Phytolaccaceae.
genus of plants known as pokeweed

Examples

The berries of Phytolacca are often toxic to humans but eaten by birds.
Researchers are studying Phytolacca for its potential medicinal properties.
Several species in the genus Phytolacca are known for producing deep purple berries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pokeweed - a common name for plants in the genus Phytolacca

Etymology

From Greek 'phyton' (plant) and 'lakkos' (lac, a kind of resin or red dye).
