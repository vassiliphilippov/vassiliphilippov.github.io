to engender

1. VERB

/ ɛnˈdʒɛndɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɛndə / (BrE)
to produce or bring into existence (offspring) through reproduction
produce offspring; reproduce

Examples

In certain animals, both male and female contribute genetically to engender their young.
Ancient myths describe gods who could engender entire races with a single thought.
The queen bee engenders thousands of offspring each season.

Synset Examples

Abraham begot Isaac
John fathered four daughters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to beget - to father or produce offspring
  • Synonym: father - to be the male parent of a child

Etymology

From Old French *engendrer*, from Latin *ingenerare* (to beget, produce).

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈdʒɛndɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɛndə / (BrE)
to cause a feeling, situation, or condition to exist or develop
cause to exist or develop

Examples

The policy changes are likely to engender greater trust between staff and management.
Her unfair treatment engendered resentment among her colleagues.
A sense of fear may engender hostility in the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to generate - to bring into existence; produce
  • Synonym: cause - to make something happen; bring about
  • Antonym: to destroy - to put an end to the existence of something

Etymology

From Old French engendrer, from Latin engenerare (to beget, produce).

Synset POS: v
