whorled

1. ADJ

/ wɜrld / (AmE), / wɜːld / (BrE)
having a form that is arranged in a circle or spiral about a point, especially in the shape of a coil.
in a coil or spiral shape

Examples

The shell's whorled structure fascinated the marine biologists.
Some plants have leaves arranged in a distinct whorled pattern around the stem.
The artist was inspired by the whorled shapes of fossil ammonites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiral - winding in a continuous curve around a central point
  • Synonym: coiled - wound into circles or spirals

Etymology

From 'whorl', of uncertain origin, possibly related to Old English 'hweorfa' (small wheel).

2. ADJ

/ wɜrld / (AmE), / wɜːld / (BrE)
(of leaves or other structures) arranged in a circle around a central point or stem, forming one or more whorls.
arranged in a circle around a stem

Examples

The plant displays whorled leaves that grow in perfect circles around the stem.
Many herbs have whorled arrangements, making them easy to identify in the field.
The botanist noted the whorled pattern of the flower petals in her sketchbook.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: opposite - (of leaves) arranged in pairs on either side of a stem
  • Antonym: alternate - (of leaves) arranged singly at different heights on the stem

Etymology

From 'whorl' + '-ed', indicating possessing whorls.
