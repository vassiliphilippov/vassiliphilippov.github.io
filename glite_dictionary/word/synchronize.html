to synchronize

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪŋ.krəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋ.krə.naɪz / (BrE)
to make the sound in a motion picture precisely match the action on screen
make film sound match the action

Examples

It took hours to synchronize the dialogue with the actors’ lip movements in the old movie.
Sound engineers must carefully synchronize every audio track with the film’s action.
The team used special software to synchronize the soundtrack before the movie’s release.

Synset Examples

synchronize this film

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sync - to synchronize, esp. in audio-visual contexts

Etymology

From Greek 'synchronos' (occurring at the same time), from 'syn-' (together) + 'chronos' (time).

2. VERB

/ ˈsɪŋkrəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkrənaɪz / (BrE)
to arrange or represent events so that they occur at the same time or in a coordinated manner
arrange events to coincide in time

Examples

Historians often synchronize events from various cultures to understand their global context.
The teacher tried to synchronize the class clocks to ensure everyone finished at the same time.
Scholars synchronize different historical timelines to compare significant milestones.
