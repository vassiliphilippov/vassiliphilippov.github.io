Eurotium

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊtiəm / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊtiəm / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the order Eurotiales, known for species that can occur on stored foods and in indoor environments.
genus of fungi in Eurotiales

Examples

Several species of Eurotium are known to be xerophilic, thriving in dry conditions.
The presence of Eurotium on food can cause spoilage and musty odors.
Microscopic examination revealed colonies of Eurotium on the stored grain samples.

Etymology

From Greek 'eurys' (broad) and 'ous, otos' (ear), referring to the shape of some structures.

2. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊtiəm / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊtiəm / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Trichocomaceae, occurring in soil and food products, and known for its role in food spoilage and some fermentations.
genus of fungi (Eurotiales order)
medical

Examples

The fungus Eurotium is often found in dried fruits and grains.
Scientists isolated several species belonging to the genus Eurotium from the soil sample.
Eurotium plays an important role in the ripening of certain cheeses.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'eu' (good) + 'rotos' (flowing).
