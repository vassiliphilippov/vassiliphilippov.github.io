Marc Chagall

1. PROPN

/ mɑrk ʃəˈɡɑl / (AmE), / mɑːk ʃəˈɡɑːl / (BrE)
(1887–1985) a French painter of Russian origin, renowned for his vivid imagery, use of brilliant colors, and poetic, dreamlike style.
French painter known for vivid, poetic works

Examples

A major exhibition of Marc Chagall's paintings drew thousands of visitors to the museum.
The dreamlike quality and bold colors in Marc Chagall's art set him apart from his contemporaries.
She wrote her thesis on the influence of folklore in the works of Marc Chagall.
