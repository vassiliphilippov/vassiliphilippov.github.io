Oncorhynchus keta

1. NOUN

/ ɑŋkəˈraɪŋkəs ˈkiːtə / (AmE), / ɒŋkɒˈrɪŋkəs ˈkiːtə / (BrE)
a large species of Pacific salmon with small spots on its back, important as a food fish
a species of Pacific salmon

Examples

During their migration, Oncorhynchus keta travel hundreds of miles upstream to spawn.
Commercial fisheries harvest large quantities of Oncorhynchus keta each year.
The diet of wild bears often includes Oncorhynchus keta during the salmon run.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chum salmon - another common name for Oncorhynchus keta
  • Degree Lower: sockeye salmon - a different species of Pacific salmon (Oncorhynchus nerka)
  • Degree Higher: king salmon - a larger species of Pacific salmon (Oncorhynchus tshawytscha)

Etymology

From Greek onkos (hook), rhynchos (snout), and species name keta from Russian кета (keta, a type of salmon).
