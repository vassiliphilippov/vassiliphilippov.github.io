Dracaena draco

1. NOUN

/ drəˈsiːnə ˈdrɑːkoʊ / (AmE), / drəˈsiːnə ˈdrɑːkəʊ / (BrE)
a tall, tree-like plant native to the Canary Islands, known as the dragon tree and noted as a source of dragon's blood resin
the dragon tree of the Canary Islands

Examples

The Dracaena draco is famous for its umbrella-like canopy and unusual appearance.
Botanists visited the Canary Islands to study the rare Dracaena draco specimens.
The resin from Dracaena draco, known as dragon's blood, has long been valued for its medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dragon tree - common name for Dracaena draco

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Dracaena from Greek drakaina (female dragon); species name draco from Latin draco (dragon), referring to the red resin.
