fickleness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪkəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfɪk.əl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being changeable and unreliable in loyalty, affections, or intentions
quality of being changeable or unreliable

Examples

Her fickleness made it difficult for anyone to trust her promises.
The politician's fickleness frustrated his supporters, who wanted clear policies.
Despite his earlier commitments, his fickleness became apparent when he changed sides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inconstancy - lack of stability or steadiness in attachment or purpose
  • Antonym: steadfastness - the quality of being firm and unwavering
  • Antonym: loyalty - the quality of being faithful and devoted

Etymology

From Middle English 'fikel' (deceitful, changeable) + '-ness'.
