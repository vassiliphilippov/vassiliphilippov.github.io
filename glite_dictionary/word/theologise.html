to theologise

1. VERB

/ θiˈɑlədʒaɪz / (AmE), / θɪˈɒlədʒaɪz / (BrE)
to treat a subject from a theological viewpoint or render it theological in character
discuss or interpret theologically

Examples

He tends to theologise every discussion about morality, viewing all actions through a religious lens.
The professor cautioned against attempts to theologise political debates in the classroom.
Some writers theologise historical events, interpreting them as part of a divine plan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to theologize - American English spelling of 'theologise'

Etymology

From theology + -ise, modeled after Latin roots.

2. VERB

/ ˈθiəloʊˌdʒaɪz / (AmE), / θɪˈɒlədʒaɪz / (BrE)
to speculate about or discuss theology in a theoretical manner; to engage in theological reasoning
discuss or speculate about theology

Examples

Scholars often theologise about the nature of salvation and divinity.
He would rather act than theologise about every aspect of faith.
They spent the afternoon theologising on the challenges faced by the modern church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to theologize - alternative spelling, chiefly American

Etymology

From Greek 'theologein' (to discourse on the gods), from 'theologia' (theology).
