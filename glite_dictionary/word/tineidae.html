Tineidae

1. NOUN

/ taɪˈniːɪˌdiː / (AmE), / tɪˈniːɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small moths whose larvae feed on natural fibers, including the clothes moth.
family of small moths, clothes moths

Examples

Members of the Tineidae family are notorious for damaging wool and silk fabrics in homes.
Scientists studied the lifecycle of Tineidae to develop better methods of pest control.
The larvae of Tineidae are commonly referred to as clothes moth larvae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clothes moth - a type of moth whose larvae feed on wool, fur, and other materials

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Tinea.
