toned

1. ADJ

/ toʊnd / (AmE), / təʊnd / (BrE)
having a particular tone or quality of sound or color, often used in combination with descriptive modifiers
having a particular tone (often of sound or color)

Examples

The orchestra played a set of beautifully toned melodies throughout the evening.
She wore a softly toned dress that complemented her complexion.
The artist preferred using warm-toned paints for his landscape paintings.

Synset Examples

full-toned
silver-toned

Etymology

From the noun 'tone' plus the suffix '-ed' used to form adjectives.

2. ADJ

/ toʊnd / (AmE), / təʊnd / (BrE)
having or characterized by a particular tone, especially in sound; often used in combination as in 'full-toned' or 'deep-toned'.
having a specific tone, often of sound

Examples

The church bells had a deep-toned resonance that echoed through the village.
She preferred the rich, full-toned sounds of a grand piano over an electric keyboard.
His shrill-toned voice made everyone stop and listen.

Synset Examples

full-toned
shrill-toned
deep-toned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tonal - relating to tone or tones

Etymology

From tone (noun) + -ed (adjective suffix).

Synset POS: s
