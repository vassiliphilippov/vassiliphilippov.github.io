to double-team

1. VERB

/ ˈdʌbəl tiːm / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl tiːm / (BrE)
(in sports) to defend against a single opponent by assigning two defensive players to cover them at the same time.
defend with two players (sports)

Examples

The star player was double-teamed every time he touched the ball.
They decided to double-team their opponent to prevent an easy score.
Coach told us to double-team the forward in the final quarter.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: triple-team - to cover with three defensive players (sports)

Etymology

From 'double' (twofold) + 'team' (group of players).
