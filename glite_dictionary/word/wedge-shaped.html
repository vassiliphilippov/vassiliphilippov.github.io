wedge-shaped

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɛdʒ ʃeɪpt / (AmE), / ˈwɛdʒ ʃeɪpt / (BrE)
having the shape of a wedge; narrowly triangular, wider at one end and tapering to a point at the other.
having the shape of a wedge

Examples

The leaves are wedge-shaped, widening at the tip and narrowing toward the base.
A wedge-shaped stone was used to split the log in two.
The architect designed a wedge-shaped building to fit the triangular plot of land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triangular - having the shape of a triangle

Etymology

Formed from 'wedge' (Old English 'wecg', a block used for splitting) + 'shaped'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɛdʒ ˌʃeɪpt / (AmE), / ˈwɛdʒ ˌʃeɪpt / (BrE)
having the shape of a wedge; broad at one end and tapering to a point at the other.
shaped like a wedge

Examples

The bird's beak is wedge-shaped to help it crack open seeds.
The architect designed a wedge-shaped building that narrows at the top.
He inserted a wedge-shaped piece of wood under the door to keep it open.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triangular - having the shape of a triangle
