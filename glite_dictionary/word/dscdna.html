dscDNA

1. NOUN

/ di ɛs si ˌdi ɛn ˈeɪ / (AmE), / diː ɛs siː ˌdiː ɛn ˈeɪ / (BrE)
Complementary DNA consisting of two strands bonded together, often synthesized from an mRNA template in molecular biology.
double-stranded complementary DNA
medical

Examples

After reverse transcription, the mRNA is converted into dscDNA for further analysis.
Researchers used dscDNA as a template to clone the gene of interest.
The quality of the dscDNA was confirmed through gel electrophoresis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cDNA - copy DNA; complementary DNA synthesized from an mRNA template

Etymology

Acronym for 'double-stranded complementary DNA.'
