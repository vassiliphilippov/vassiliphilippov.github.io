intemperance

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛmpərəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛmpərəns / (BrE)
Excessive or habitual consumption of alcoholic drinks.
excessive drinking of alcohol

Examples

His intemperance led to financial and health problems over the years.
The preacher spoke passionately against intemperance at the community meeting.
Historic campaigns often encouraged citizens to avoid intemperance and embrace moderation in all things.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sobriety - the state of not being intoxicated
  • Antonym: temperance - moderation or self-restraint, especially in eating and drinking
  • Synonym: drunkenness - the state of being drunk or intoxicated

Etymology

From Latin intemperantia, from in- (not) + temperantia (moderation).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛmpɚəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛmpərəns / (BrE)
lack of moderation or self-control, especially in indulging bodily appetites or passions; excessive indulgence.
excessive indulgence; lack of moderation

Examples

The judge warned that intemperance in speech could undermine civil debate.
Decades of intemperance eventually affected his health and reputation.
She was criticized for her intemperance at social gatherings.

Synset Examples

the intemperance of their language

Related Terms

  • Antonym: moderation - the avoidance of excess or extremes, especially in behavior
  • Synonym: temperance - moderation in action, thought, or feeling; especially abstinence from alcohol

Etymology

From Latin intemperantia (immoderateness, excess), from in- (not) + temperantia (moderation).

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛmpəɹəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛmpərəns / (BrE)
Lack of moderation or self-restraint, especially with regard to drinking alcohol or indulgence in appetites.
lack of moderation or self-restraint

Examples

His intemperance eventually led to health problems and financial ruin.
The preacher condemned intemperance in all its forms during his sermon.
Legislation was introduced to curb the intemperance caused by excessive drinking.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: temperance - moderation or self-restraint, especially in eating and drinking

Etymology

From Latin 'intemperantia', from 'in-' (not) + 'temperantia' (moderation).
