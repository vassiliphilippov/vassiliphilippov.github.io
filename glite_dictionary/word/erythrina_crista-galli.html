Erythrina crista-galli

1. NOUN

/ ɛˌrɪθɹɪnə ˌkrɪstəˈɡɑli / (AmE), / ɛˌrɪθrɪnə ˌkrɪstəˈɡɑːli / (BrE)
A small South American tree with spiny branches and dark crimson to scarlet flowers, often grown for ornamental purposes.
South American spiny tree with scarlet flowers

Examples

The Erythrina crista-galli blooms beautifully in early summer, attracting many hummingbirds to the garden.
We planted an Erythrina crista-galli in our backyard for its vibrant red flowers.
The spiny branches of the Erythrina crista-galli make it unmistakable among other ornamental trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coral tree - common name for trees in the genus Erythrina

Etymology

From Greek 'erythros' (red), referring to the red flowers; Latin 'crista-galli' (cock's comb) referencing the flower's crest-like shape.
