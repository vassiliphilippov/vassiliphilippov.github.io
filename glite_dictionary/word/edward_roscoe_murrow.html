Edward Roscoe Murrow

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛd.wɚd ˈrɑːs.koʊ ˈmɝː.roʊ / (AmE), / ˈɛd.wəd ˈrɒs.kəʊ ˈmʌr.əʊ / (BrE)
(1908–1965) A renowned American broadcast journalist known for his influential news reports from London during World War II.
noted American broadcast journalist (1908–1965)

Examples

Edward Roscoe Murrow is often credited with pioneering modern broadcast journalism.
During World War II, Edward Roscoe Murrow's radio broadcasts brought the reality of the London Blitz to American listeners.
Many aspiring journalists cite Edward Roscoe Murrow as a major inspiration in their careers.
