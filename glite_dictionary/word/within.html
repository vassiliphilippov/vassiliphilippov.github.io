within

1. ADV

/ wɪˈðɪn / (AmE), / wɪˈðɪn / (BrE)
On the inside; in or into the interior or confines of something.
in the interior; inside

Examples

We were finally safe within the walls of the fortress.
The treasure was hidden within, beyond anyone's reach.
You must search within to find the answer.

Synset Examples

inside, the car is a mess

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inside - in the interior; internally
  • Antonym: outside - on or to the exterior

Etymology

From Old English 'wiðinnan' (on the inside), from 'wið' (against, by) + 'innan' (in, within).
