famishment

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmɪʃmənt / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪʃmənt / (BrE)
the state of suffering from extreme hunger or starvation, often over a prolonged period
extreme hunger; starvation

Examples

Many villagers suffered famishment after the drought destroyed their crops.
During the siege, reports of famishment spread throughout the city.
The explorers faced near famishment before they were finally rescued.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starvation - suffering or death from lack of food
  • Degree Lower: hunger - the discomfort or weakness caused by lack of food

Etymology

From obsolete English verb 'famish' (to starve) + '-ment', ultimately from Latin 'fames' (hunger).
