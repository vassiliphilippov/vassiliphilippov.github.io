lapin

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæpɪn / (AmE), / ˈlæpɪn / (BrE)
A castrated male rabbit, especially one raised for meat or fur.
castrated male rabbit

Examples

The farmer separated the lapin from the females to prevent breeding.
After neutering, the rabbit was officially classified as a lapin.
Many commercial rabbit farms keep several lapins for their mild temperament.

Etymology

From French *lapin* (rabbit).

2. NOUN

/ ləˈpæn / (AmE), / læˈpæ̃ / (BrE)
the fur of a rabbit, often used as material for garments or trimmings
rabbit fur used as material

Examples

The coat was made from luxurious lapin, giving it a soft and elegant texture.
She preferred lapin because it was lighter than other types of fur.
A collar of lapin added warmth and style to her winter jacket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rabbit fur - the soft pelt of a rabbit used for clothing or accessories

Etymology

From French 'lapin' (rabbit).
