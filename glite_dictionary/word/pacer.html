pacer

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪsər / (AmE), / ˈpeɪsə / (BrE)
a horse that is used to set or maintain the pace in a race, especially in harness racing
horse used to set pace in racing

Examples

The leading pacer set a fast tempo for the rest of the field.
She bred her champion horse to a famous pacer for the next racing season.
In harness racing, the pacer often establishes the speed early in the race.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trotter - a horse that moves at a trotting gait in harness racing

Etymology

From 'pace' (to move with regular steps) + -er (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪsər / (AmE), / ˈpeɪsə / (BrE)
a horse trained to move with a gait in which both legs on the same side leave the ground at the same time, commonly used in harness racing
horse with a special racing gait

Examples

The pacer surged ahead of the other horses during the race.
Many harness racing fans enjoy watching a skilled pacer in action.
That stable is known for breeding champion pacers every year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trotter - a horse trained for harness racing with a diagonal gait
