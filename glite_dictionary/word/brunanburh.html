Brunanburh

1. PROPN

/ ˈbruːnənˌbʊr / (AmE), / ˈbruːnənbʊə / (BrE)
the site of a decisive battle in 937, where King Athelstan of England defeated a coalition of Scots, Norse, and Strathclyde Britons
historic battle site, 937 AD

Examples

The Battle of Brunanburh marked a turning point in early English history.
Many historians debate the exact location of Brunanburh, as its site remains uncertain.
Brunanburh is often cited as Athelstan's greatest military victory.
