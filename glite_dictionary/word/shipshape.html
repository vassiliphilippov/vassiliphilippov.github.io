shipshape

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃɪpˌʃeɪp / (AmE), / ˈʃɪpˌʃeɪp / (BrE)
neat, clean, and in good order; free from disorder or untidiness
in neat, orderly condition

Examples

She keeps her house shipshape at all times, with everything in its place.
After the renovation, the office looked perfectly shipshape and professional.
The captain insisted the deck be shipshape before setting sail.

Synset Examples

even the barn was shipshape
a trim little sailboat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tidy - orderly and neatly arranged
  • Antonym: messy - untidy or disordered

Etymology

From the phrase 'ship shape and Bristol fashion,' originally referring to the tidy and efficient condition required on ships.
