sentimentality

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛn.tə.mɛnˈtæ.lə.ti / (AmE), / ˌsɛn.tɪ.mɛnˈtæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of being overly emotional or expressing feelings in an exaggerated, affected, or insincere way
excessive or affected emotion

Examples

The movie was criticized for its excessive sentimentality and lack of realism.
Her speech was filled with a kind of forced sentimentality that felt insincere.
He avoided sentimentality, choosing instead a tone that was honest and restrained.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sentiment - a feeling or opinion, especially one based on emotion
  • Synonym: melodrama - exaggerated, emotional, or sentimental behaviour or art
  • Antonym: cynicism - skepticism or distrust of others' motives, the opposite of sentimentality

Etymology

From French 'sentimentalité', from 'sentiment' (feeling).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛn.tə.mənˈtæ.lə.ti / (AmE), / ˌsɛn.tɪ.menˈtæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of being overly emotional, especially in a way that is exaggerated, insincere, or mawkish.
excessive or insincere emotion

Examples

There was a touch of sentimentality in his speech that made some people roll their eyes.
The film avoids sentimentality, instead presenting a raw and honest portrayal of grief.
Her sentimentality often leads her to tears over old photographs or sentimental music.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sentiment - a feeling or opinion, especially one based on emotion
  • Synonym: mawkishness - excessively sentimental, to the point of being sickly

Etymology

From French *sentimentalité* and English *sentimental* (18th century), relating to sentiment or feeling.
