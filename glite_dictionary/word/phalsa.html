phalsa

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑl.sə / (AmE), / ˈfɑːl.sə / (BrE)
A drought-resistant shrub native to South Asia, bearing small, red, pleasantly acidic edible fruits commonly used in drinks and desserts.
South Asian fruit-bearing shrub

Examples

The refreshing summer drink was made from fresh phalsa berries blended with sugar and ice.
A row of phalsa bushes grew along the edge of their farm, providing shade and fruit in the hot season.
Many street vendors in India sell sherbet made with phalsa during the peak summer months.

Etymology

From Hindi फालसा (phālsā), from Sanskrit भल्लातक (bhallātaka).
