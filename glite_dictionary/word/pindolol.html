pindolol

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪn.dəˌlɔl / (AmE), / ˈpɪn.də.lɒl / (BrE)
a synthetic beta blocker drug, usually taken orally, used to treat high blood pressure and certain heart conditions
a beta blocker drug for hypertension
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed pindolol to help control his high blood pressure.
Patients taking pindolol should have their heart rate monitored regularly.
Unlike other medications, pindolol may be less likely to cause fatigue in some patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beta blocker - a class of drugs that block beta-adrenergic substances, reducing heart rate and blood pressure
  • Synonym: propranolol - a beta blocker used to treat hypertension and anxiety
