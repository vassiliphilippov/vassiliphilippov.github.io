astrolatry

1. NOUN

/ ˌæstrəˈlætri / (AmE), / ˌæstrəˈlætri / (BrE)
the worship of planets or stars as deities
worship of stars or planets

Examples

Ancient civilizations such as the Babylonians practiced astrolatry and associated gods with planets and stars.
The rise of monotheistic faiths led to the decline of astrolatry in many societies.
Scholars of religion often study astrolatry to understand the origins of early celestial worship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idolatry - the worship of idols or physical objects as gods
  • Antonym: atheism - the disbelief or lack of belief in gods or deities

Etymology

From Greek 'astron' (star) + 'latreia' (worship).
