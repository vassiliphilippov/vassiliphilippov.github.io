Barbarea vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɑr.bəˈɹi.ə vʌlˈɡɛr.ɪs / (AmE), / ˌbɑː.bəˈrɪə vʌlˈɡɛː.rɪs / (BrE)
A common Eurasian plant of the mustard family, known as yellow rocket or winter cress, with yellow flowers; often considered a weed.
a yellow-flowered Eurasian plant (yellow rocket)

Examples

Fields of Barbarea vulgaris bloom with bright yellow flowers in early spring.
Gardeners often remove Barbarea vulgaris because it spreads quickly as a weed.
The leaves of Barbarea vulgaris are sometimes eaten in salads or cooked as greens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow rocket - common name for Barbarea vulgaris, a yellow-flowered plant
  • Degree Higher: cress - a group of plants in the mustard family with pungent leaves

Etymology

Genus name from Saint Barbara, regarded as its patron; species epithet from Latin 'vulgaris' (common).
