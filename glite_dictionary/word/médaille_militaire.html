Médaille Militaire

1. NOUN

/ meɪˌdaɪ məˈlɪtəri / (AmE), / meɪˌdaɪ məˈlɪtəri / (BrE)
a French military decoration awarded for acts of bravery or long service, especially to non-commissioned officers and enlisted personnel
French military decoration for bravery or service

Examples

The soldier received the Médaille Militaire for his extraordinary bravery during the campaign.
Many veterans proudly display the Médaille Militaire among their most cherished honors.
She was awarded the Médaille Militaire in recognition of her long and distinguished service.

Etymology

French, literally 'Military Medal'.
