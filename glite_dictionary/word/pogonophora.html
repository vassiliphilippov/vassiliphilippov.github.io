Pogonophora

1. NOUN

/ poʊˌɡɑːnəˈfɔːrə / (AmE), / pəʊˌɡɒnəˈfɔːrə / (BrE)
A former class of marine worms known as beard worms, now classified as the family Siboglinidae, characterized by long, slender bodies and living in deep-sea environments.
group of deep-sea marine worms (beard worms)

Examples

The deep-sea vents are home to Pogonophora, remarkable worms that thrive without sunlight.
Scientists study Pogonophora to understand life in extreme ocean environments.
A colony of Pogonophora was discovered attached to the sea floor near hydrothermal vents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beard worm - common name for Pogonophora, deep-sea marine worms

Etymology

From Greek 'pōgōn' (beard) + '-phora' (bearing).
