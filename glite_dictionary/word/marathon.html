Marathon

1. PROPN

/ ˈmærəˌθɑn / (AmE), / ˈmærəθən / (BrE)
a battle fought in 490 BC near Marathon, Greece, in which an outnumbered Athenian force defeated the invading Persian army.
battle between Athenians and Persians, 490 BC

Examples

The Battle of Marathon marked a turning point in Greek history.
Many historians study the strategies used at Marathon to understand ancient warfare.
Athens' victory at Marathon inspired later generations throughout Europe.

Etymology

From the place name Marathon in Greece, site of the battle.
