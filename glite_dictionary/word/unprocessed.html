unprocessed

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈprɑːsɛst / (AmE), / ʌnˈprɒsɛst / (BrE)
(of a substance or material) not altered, treated, or changed from its original or natural state.
not altered from original state

Examples

The market offers a variety of unprocessed grains and legumes for health-conscious shoppers.
She prefers to eat unprocessed foods because they retain more nutrients.
The export of unprocessed minerals is vital to the country's economy.

Synset Examples

unprocessed commodities

Related Terms

  • Antonym: processed - altered or treated from its natural state
  • Synonym: raw - not cooked or processed; in a natural state

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈprɑːsɛst / (AmE), / ʌnˈprɒsɛst / (BrE)
(of substances or materials) not having been refined, altered, or treated by a special process.
not refined or treated

Examples

Many health experts recommend eating more unprocessed foods such as fruits and vegetables.
The factory receives unprocessed cotton before turning it into fabric.
This cheese is made from unprocessed milk, giving it a distinct flavor.

Synset Examples

crude oil

Related Terms

  • Antonym: refined - (of a substance or material) purified or processed to remove impurities

3. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈprɑːsɛst / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈprəʊsɛst / (BrE)
not having been treated, refined, or prepared by a special process; in its natural or original state.
not treated or refined; in original state

Examples

Foods labeled as unprocessed are often preferred by health-conscious consumers.
It's better to eat fresh, unprocessed fruits and vegetables.
The company uses only unprocessed materials in its products.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: processed - treated or prepared by a special process
  • Synonym: raw - in a natural, unrefined state

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' (not) to 'processed'.
