naturalised

1. ADJ

/ ˈnætʃ(ə)rəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈnatʃ(ə)rəlʌɪzd / (BrE)
(of plants) planted or cultivated to give the appearance of wild growth in a particular area.
planted to look wild (plants)

Examples

The gardener prefers naturalised bulbs along the riverbank for a more informal look.
Over time, naturalised wildflowers spread across the old meadow, blending seamlessly with the native flora.
Patches of naturalised snowdrops brightened the woodland edge every spring.

Synset Examples

drifts of naturalized daffodils
