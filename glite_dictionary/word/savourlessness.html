savourlessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪvɚrlɪsnəs / (AmE), / ˈseɪvəlnɪsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having no flavor or taste
lack of flavor or taste

Examples

The savourlessness of the meal disappointed everyone at the table.
He complained about the savourlessness of the soup, calling it bland and uninteresting.
Modern food processing can sometimes result in the savourlessness of convenience meals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insipidity - quality of lacking flavor or zest
  • Antonym: flavorfulness - having a strong or pleasant taste

Etymology

From 'savourless' + '-ness', where 'savour' is from Old French 'savour' (taste), from Latin 'sapor' (taste, flavor).
