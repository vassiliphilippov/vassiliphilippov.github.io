deoxycytidine

1. NOUN

/ diˌɑksiˈsaɪtəˌdin / (AmE), / diˌɒksɪˈsɪtɪdiːn / (BrE)
a nucleoside consisting of cytosine linked to deoxyribose, found as a component of DNA
nucleoside in DNA (cytosine + deoxyribose)
medical

Examples

Deoxycytidine is one of the four nucleosides that make up DNA.
Researchers measured deoxycytidine levels to study DNA synthesis in the lab.
Abnormal amounts of deoxycytidine can indicate problems with DNA replication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cytidine - nucleoside composed of cytosine and ribose, found in RNA

Etymology

Formed from 'deoxy-' (removal of oxygen) and 'cytidine', itself from 'cytosine' + '-idine'.
