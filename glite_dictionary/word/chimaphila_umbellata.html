Chimaphila umbellata

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈmæfɪlə ʌmˈbɛlətə / (AmE), / kɪˈmæfɪlə ʌmˈbɛlətə / (BrE)
A low-growing Eurasian and North American evergreen herb with white or pinkish flowers, commonly known as pipsissewa.
a small evergreen herb with white or pinkish flowers

Examples

Chimaphila umbellata is commonly found in dry, acidic woods across North America and Eurasia.
Herbalists have used Chimaphila umbellata in traditional medicine for various ailments.
The delicate pink flowers of Chimaphila umbellata bloom in late spring to early summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipsissewa - another common name for Chimaphila umbellata, a small evergreen herb

Etymology

From Greek 'cheimaphilos' meaning 'loving winter', referencing its evergreen nature; 'umbellata' from Latin for 'umbel-shaped' (referring to the flower cluster).
