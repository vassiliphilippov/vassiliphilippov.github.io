quiescent

1. ADJ

/ kwiˈɛsənt / (AmE), / kwɪˈɛsənt / (BrE)
in a state of inactivity or dormancy; not active
inactive; not active

Examples

The volcano has remained quiescent for over a century, showing no signs of eruption.
During winter, the trees become quiescent, conserving their energy until spring.
The patient appeared to be in a quiescent state after the medication was administered.

Synset Examples

the quiescent level of centimeter wave-length solar radiation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dormant - in a state of rest or inactivity
  • Antonym: active - engaged in action; operating

Etymology

From Latin quiescent-, present participle of quiescere (to become quiet), from quies (rest, quiet).

2. ADJ

/ kwiˈɛsənt / (AmE), / kwiˈɛsənt / (BrE)
(of a disease or medical condition) not currently active; causing no symptoms.
not active or causing symptoms (medical)
medical

Examples

Despite its size, the quiescent tumor produced no noticeable symptoms in the patient.
The doctor explained that the infection was currently quiescent and did not require immediate treatment.
Some cancers may remain quiescent for years before becoming aggressive.

Synset Examples

a quiescent tumor

Related Terms

  • Antonym: active - (of a disease) producing symptoms, not dormant

Etymology

From Latin 'quiescens', present participle of 'quiescere' (to rest, to be quiet).

Source: WordNet

4. ADJ

/ kwiˈɛsənt / (AmE), / kwiˈesənt / (BrE)
in a state of inactivity or dormancy; quiet and not active
inactive; at rest; dormant

Examples

After the storm passed, the town remained quiescent and peaceful.
The volcano has been quiescent for decades, showing no signs of erupting.
During winter, many animals enter a quiescent state to conserve energy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: active - engaged in action; not inactive

Etymology

From Latin quiescentem, present participle of quiescere (to rest), from quies (rest, quiet).
