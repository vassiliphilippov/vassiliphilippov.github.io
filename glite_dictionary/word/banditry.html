banditry

1. NOUN

/ ˈbændɪtri / (AmE), / ˈbændɪtri / (BrE)
the practice of attacking people and stealing from them, especially by organized gangs
criminal gang robbery or plundering

Examples

The rise of banditry in the region has alarmed local authorities.
Historical records detail frequent banditry along the trade routes.
The government sent troops to crack down on banditry in rural areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robbery - the act of taking property illegally by force or threat of force
  • Synonym: piracy - the practice of attacking and robbing ships at sea
  • Antonym: lawfulness - the quality or state of being lawful

Etymology

From Italian 'bandito' (outlaw or bandit), ultimately from Latin 'banditus'.
