vesica

1. NOUN

/ vəˈsiːkə / (AmE), / vɪˈsiːkə / (BrE)
A thin-walled sac or bladder in the body, typically one that is distensible and contains liquid or gas.
thin-walled bodily sac or bladder
medical

Examples

The vesica of the insect is inflated during mating.
A cyst was discovered in the patient's urinary vesica.
In some species, the vesica serves as an organ for storing reproductive fluids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bladder - a sac in the body that stores urine or other fluids
  • Degree Lower: vesicle - a small fluid-filled sac in the body

Etymology

From Latin vesica (bladder, urinary bladder).
