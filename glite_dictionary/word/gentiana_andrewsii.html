Gentiana andrewsii

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛnʃiˈænə ˌænˈdruːziː / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛnʃɪˈɑːniə ˌænˈdrjuːziː / (BrE)
A species of gentian native to eastern North America, known for its tubular blue or white flowers that remain mostly closed.
North American gentian species with closed blue or white flowers

Examples

The Gentiana andrewsii blooms in late summer and early autumn along the forest edge.
Botanists admire the unique closed flowers of Gentiana andrewsii found in wet meadows.
We spotted several Gentiana andrewsii plants during our hike in the Appalachian Mountains.

Etymology

Named after English botanist Henry C. Andrews.
