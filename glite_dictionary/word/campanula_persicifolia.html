Campanula persicifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæmpəˈnjuːlə pərˌsɪsɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˌkampəˈnjuːlə pəˌsɪsɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A perennial European bellflower with racemose clusters of white or blue flowers.
perennial European bellflower species

Examples

The garden was in full bloom with clusters of Campanula persicifolia lining the walkway.
Botanists admire Campanula persicifolia for its delicate blue bell-shaped flowers.
She planted several Campanula persicifolia to add height and elegance to her flower border.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bellflower - plant of the Campanulaceae family, bell-shaped flowers

Etymology

From Latin: Campanula (diminutive of campana, 'bell') and persicifolia ('with leaves like a peach tree', from 'persica' for peach and 'folia' for leaves).
