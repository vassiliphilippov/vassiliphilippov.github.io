shearwater

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪrˌwɔtər / (AmE), / ˈʃɪəˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
a long-winged oceanic bird, related to the petrel, that typically flies low over the waves
long-winged oceanic bird

Examples

A flock of shearwaters glided effortlessly above the rolling waves.
We spotted several shearwaters while sailing across the Atlantic.
The shearwater is known for its graceful flight just above the ocean surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petrel - small seabird closely related to the shearwater

Etymology

From the verb 'shear' (to cut) + 'water', referring to its habit of skimming or 'shearing' the water with its wings.
