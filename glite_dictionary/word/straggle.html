straggle

1. NOUN

/ ˈstræɡəl / (AmE), / ˈstræɡəl / (BrE)
a group of people or things that are spread irregularly or scattered in a disorderly way
disorderly or scattered group

Examples

After the parade, a straggle of people loitered in the main square.
We passed a straggle of cottages on our hike through the valley.
A straggle of sheep wandered away from the main flock.

Synset Examples

a straggle of outbuildings
a straggle of followers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cluster - a group of closely packed things

Etymology

From Middle English 'straglen', related to 'stroll'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07955399-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈstræɡəl / (AmE), / ˈstræɡl / (BrE)
to move away from a group or wander from a direct or straight course, often in a slow or scattered way
wander away from a direct course

Examples

Several children began to straggle behind the group during the long hike.
As the parade ended, some participants continued to straggle down the empty street.
Do not straggle away from the tour or you might get lost.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wander - to move slowly away from a place or path
  • Synonym: trail - to move slowly or lag behind a group
  • Antonym: keep to - to stay on a set path or with a group

Etymology

From Middle English straglen, probably of Scandinavian origin; related to Swedish dialect straggla (to struggle, sprawl).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02070232-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈstræɡəl / (AmE), / ˈstræɡəl / (BrE)
to move, grow, or spread in an irregular, untidy, or scattered way
to spread or move irregularly

Examples

The town began to straggle across the plains as more people settled.
Some students would straggle in late after the bell had rung.
Vines straggle up the old stone wall in every direction.

Synset Examples

Branches straggling out quite far

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wander - to move about without a fixed course, aim, or goal

Etymology

From Middle English straglen, probably of Scandinavian origin, related to Norwegian dialect stragla (to spread, sprawl).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02036072-v
Synset POS: v
