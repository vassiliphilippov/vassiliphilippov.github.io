say

1. VERB

/ seɪ / (AmE), / seɪ / (BrE)
(used to introduce a suggestion, example, or supposition) to imagine or suppose something for the sake of discussion.
introduce a suggestion or supposition

Examples

Say you miss the bus—how will you get to work on time?
Say we leave at noon, will that give us enough time?
If, say, the weather is bad, we can stay indoors.

Synset Examples

Let us say that he did not tell the truth
Let's say you had a lot of money — what would you do?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suppose - to assume something is true for the sake of argument or discussion
  • Synonym: to imagine - to form a mental image or concept, especially for the sake of discussion
