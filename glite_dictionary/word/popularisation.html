popularisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑː.pjə.lɚ.əˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɒp.jʊ.lə.raɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making something easily understandable and widely accessible, especially to the general public.
making something easy to understand and accessible

Examples

The popularisation of science has made complex theories accessible to everyone.
Her book contributed greatly to the popularisation of modern art.
The documentary aims at the popularisation of environmental issues among young people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simplification - the process of making something simpler or easier to do or understand

Etymology

From French *popularisation*, from Latin *populus* (people).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːpjələrəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɒpjʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something attractive or accessible to the general public
making something accessible to the public

Examples

The popularisation of science has played a key role in public education.
His work contributed greatly to the popularisation of classical music.
The popularisation of the internet changed how people access information.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: popularization - the act of making something attractive to the general public (US spelling)
  • Synonym: promotion - the act of encouraging or trying to increase the popularity of something
  • Antonym: obscurity - the state of being unknown or not well-known by the public

Etymology

From popular + -isation, modelled after the verb 'to popularise'.
