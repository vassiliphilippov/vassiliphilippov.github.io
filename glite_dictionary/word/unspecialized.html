unspecialized

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈspɛʃəlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈspɛʃəlaɪzd / (BrE)
Not adapted or modified for a specific purpose, function, or role; general rather than specialized.
not adapted for a specific function

Examples

The scientist explained how unspecialized cells can develop into any tissue in the body.
Many entry-level positions require unspecialized skills that can be learned on the job.
Early in evolution, most living things had unspecialized organs and simple structures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: specialized - adapted for a specific function or purpose

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'specialized' (made specific or adapted for a particular purpose).
