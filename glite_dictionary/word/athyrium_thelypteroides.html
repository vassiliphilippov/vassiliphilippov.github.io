Athyrium thelypteroides

1. NOUN

/ əˈθɪriəm ˌθɛlɪptəˈrɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / əˈθɪriəm ˌθɛlɪptəˈrɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A species of fern with elongate silvery outgrowths surrounding the developing spores, commonly known as the silvery glade fern.
a fern species with silvery outgrowths

Examples

The shady woodland was home to a rare patch of Athyrium thelypteroides.
Botanists studied the silvery outgrowths on Athyrium thelypteroides to understand its spore development.
Gardeners value Athyrium thelypteroides for its unique ornamental foliage.

Etymology

From the genus name Athyrium (Greek athyros, 'doorless') and species name thelypteroides, meaning 'resembling Thelypteris' (another fern genus).
