impossibility

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˌpɑːsəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪmˌpɒsəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the state or fact of being impossible; something that cannot be done or happen.
something that cannot be done

Examples

Achieving perfection in every task is an impossibility for most people.
With current technology, time travel remains an impossibility.
He refused to accept the impossibility of the mission and continued to search for solutions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: possibility - the state or fact of being possible
  • Antonym: certainty - the state of being certain or sure

Etymology

From Medieval Latin impossibilitas, from Latin impossibilis (not possible).

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˌpɑːsəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪmˌpɒsəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the state or condition of being impossible; incapability of existing, happening, or being done
incapability of existing or occurring

Examples

The impossibility of completing the project on time became clear to everyone.
Many scientists once believed that human flight was an impossibility.
She refused to accept the impossibility of their plan and kept looking for a solution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impossible - not able to be done or to happen
  • Antonym: possibility - state of being possible; a thing that may happen

Etymology

From late Latin impossibilitas, from Latin impossibilis (not possible).
