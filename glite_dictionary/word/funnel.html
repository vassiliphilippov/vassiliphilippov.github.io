funnel

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌnəl / (AmE), / ˈfʌnəl / (BrE)
A tube or utensil with a wide top and narrow bottom, used for guiding liquid or powder into a small opening; also, a conical shape with a wider and a narrower opening at opposite ends.
conical tube or shape with wide and narrow ends

Examples

She used a funnel to pour the oil into the bottle without spilling.
The tornado formed a funnel shape as it touched down on the ground.
The chef grabbed a funnel to transfer the flour into a smaller container.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cone - a three-dimensional geometric shape with a circular base that tapers to a point

Etymology

From Old French fonel, from Latin fundibulum (pouring vessel).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfʌn.əl / (AmE), / ˈfʌn.əl / (BrE)
A utensil with a wide, conical mouth and a narrow tube at the end, used for pouring liquids or powders into containers with small openings.
conical utensil for pouring into small openings

Examples

Pour the flour through a funnel to avoid spilling it on the counter.
She used a funnel to transfer the juice into a bottle.
A plastic funnel is essential for filling bottles with homemade oil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spout - a tube or lip that liquid comes out from

Etymology

From Old French *fonil*, from Latin *fundibulum* meaning 'pouring vessel'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfʌnəl / (AmE), / ˈfʌnəl / (BrE)
(Nautical) The tall chimney on a ship, used to expel smoke and gases from boilers or engines.
ship's smokestack or chimney

Examples

The ship's funnel emitted a plume of black smoke as it left the harbor.
During the storm, the crew secured the canvas covering the funnel to prevent water from entering.
Paintwork on the funnel was bright red, making the liner easily recognizable at sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smokestack - a chimney for discharging smoke from an engine
  • Synonym: chimney - a vertical channel for conveying smoke
Source: WordNet

4. VERB

/ ˈfʌnəl / (AmE), / ˈfʌnəl / (BrE)
to move or pour something through or as if through a funnel, typically directing or channelling it into a narrow space
to pour or channel through a funnel

Examples

She carefully funneled the powder into the test tube without spilling any.
The company funnels money from different sources into the new project.
As rainwater collects on the roof, it funnels down to the gutters and away from the house.

Synset Examples
