ginep

1. NOUN

/ ɡɪˈnɛp / (AmE), / ɡɪˈnɛp / (BrE)
A tropical American tree (Melicoccus bijugatus) that bears small, edible fruits with green leathery skin and sweet, juicy, translucent pulp.
tropical tree with edible fruit

Examples

Street vendors sell fresh ginep by the bag at markets throughout the Caribbean.
She loved the tangy flavor of ginep whenever it was in season.
We picked ripe gineps from the tree during our summer visit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quenepa - another regional name for the same fruit/tree
  • Synonym: mamoncillo - alternative name for Melicoccus bijugatus

Etymology

From Spanish 'guinep', variant of 'quenepa', used in the Caribbean.
