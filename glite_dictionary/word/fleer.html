fleer

1. VERB

/ flɪr / (AmE), / flɪə / (BrE)
to smile or laugh in a contemptuous or derisive manner
smirk or laugh contemptuously
archaic

Examples

He would fleer at every mistake his rival made.
The enemy soldiers fleered from the safety of their walls.
She could not help but fleer at the foolish proposal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sneer - to smile or speak in a contemptuous or mocking manner
  • Synonym: jeer - to mock or taunt openly
  • Antonym: to admire - to look upon with respect or approval

Etymology

From Middle English 'fleryn', possibly of Scandinavian origin, compare Old Norse 'flýra' (to jeer).

2. NOUN

/ flɪr / (AmE), / flɪə / (BrE)
a look or remark expressing scornful mockery or contempt
scornful look or remark; mockery
archaic

Examples

The audience greeted his suggestion with a derisive fleer.
He endured the fleer of his opponents in silence.
With a curling lip and a cutting fleer, she dismissed the idea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sneer - a contemptuous or mocking smile, remark, or tone
  • Synonym: taunt - a remark made in order to anger, wound, or provoke someone
  • Antonym: applause - approval or praise expressed by clapping

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *fleryn* (to grin or laugh scornfully).

Synset POS: n
