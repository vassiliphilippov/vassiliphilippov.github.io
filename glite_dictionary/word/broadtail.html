broadtail

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɔdˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdteɪl / (BrE)
a hardy, coarse-haired breed of sheep from central Asia, whose young lambs are prized for their soft, curly black fur
breed of sheep valued for lambs' soft black fur

Examples

The broadtail is especially valued for the luxurious, curly fur of its newborn lambs.
Furriers often use pelts from broadtail lambs to make high-end garments.
Conservationists monitor the population of broadtail sheep due to their economic significance.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrɔdˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdˌteɪl / (BrE)
the glossy, patterned fur obtained from the skin of a very young karakul lamb, often used in garments
fur of very young karakul lamb

Examples

The coat was made from luxurious broadtail and felt remarkably soft to the touch.
She inherited a vintage broadtail jacket from her grandmother's wardrobe.
Designers in the 1920s popularized broadtail as a fashionable material for high-end outerwear.

Etymology

From 'broad' + 'tail', originally referring to the appearance of the pelt.
