Arctictis

1. NOUN

/ ɑrkˈtɪktɪs / (AmE), / ɑːkˈtɪktɪs / (BrE)
A genus of large, tree-dwelling, bear-like mammals found in Southeast Asia, commonly known as binturongs.
genus of mammals (binturong)

Examples

The Arctictis is the only genus in its family, containing just the binturong species.
Zoologists often study Arctictis to learn more about Southeast Asian rainforests.
Specimens of Arctictis can be found in several natural history museums around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: binturong - a large, tree-dwelling mammal also called 'bearcat'

Etymology

From Greek arktos (bear) + iktis (weasel).
