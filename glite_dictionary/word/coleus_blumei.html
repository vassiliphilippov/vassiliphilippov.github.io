Coleus blumei

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊli.əs ˈbluːmi.aɪ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊlɪəs ˈbluːmi.aɪ / (BrE)
A perennial aromatic herb native to southeastern Asia, known for its large, brightly colored or blotched leaves and spikes of blue-violet flowers, sometimes classified in the genus Solenostemon.
ornamental herb with colorful leaves

Examples

The vibrant leaves of Coleus blumei make it a popular choice for decorative gardens.
Many gardeners appreciate Coleus blumei for its striking foliage and ease of cultivation.
She planted several Coleus blumei alongside her walkway to add a splash of color.

Etymology

From the former genus name Coleus and Latinized after German botanist Carl Ludwig Blume.
