anchorperson

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋkərˌpɜrsən / (AmE), / ˈaŋkəˌpɜːsən / (BrE)
A person who presents and coordinates a television news broadcast to which several reporters or correspondents contribute.
main coordinator of a TV news broadcast

Examples

The anchorperson introduced each story with clarity and professionalism.
She became the first female anchorperson at the network's evening news.
After years as a correspondent, he was promoted to anchorperson for the morning show.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anchorman - a male anchorperson in broadcasting
  • Synonym: anchorwoman - a female anchorperson in broadcasting

Etymology

Formed from 'anchor' (main support) + 'person' to provide a gender-neutral alternative to 'anchorman' or 'anchorwoman'.
