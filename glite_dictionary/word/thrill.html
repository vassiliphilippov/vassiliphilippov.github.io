thrill

1. NOUN

/ θrɪl / (AmE), / θrɪl / (BrE)
a sudden strong feeling of excitement, pleasure, or exhilaration
sudden feeling of excitement

Examples

The roller coaster gave her a thrill she would never forget.
He does extreme sports for the thrill they provide.
Winning the competition was a thrill unlike any other experience.

Synset Examples

they got a great bang out of it
what a boot!
he got a quick rush from injecting heroin
he does it for kicks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - a sudden strong feeling of excitement or pleasure
  • Synonym: kick - informal term for a sudden feeling of excitement or pleasure
  • Antonym: boredom - the state of being bored or lacking excitement

Etymology

From Middle English 'thrillen', related to 'thirl' meaning to pierce, evoking a sudden, piercing feeling.
