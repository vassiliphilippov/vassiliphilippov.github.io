triangular

1. ADJ

/ traɪˈæŋɡjələr / (AmE), / traɪˈæŋɡjʊlə / (BrE)
having three angles or sides; shaped like a triangle.
having three angles or a triangle shape

Examples

The artist drew a triangular pattern on the canvas.
The room was oddly triangular instead of rectangular, making it hard to furnish.
The mountain peak formed a distinct triangular silhouette against the sky.

Synset Examples

a triangular figure
a triangular pyramid has a triangle for a base

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: quadrilateral - a four-sided figure; a polygon with four angles
  • Synonym: triangle - a plane figure with three straight sides and three angles

Etymology

From Latin 'triangulus' (three-cornered), from 'tri-' (three) + 'angulus' (angle).

2. ADJ

/ traɪˈæŋɡjələr / (AmE), / traɪˈaŋɡjʊlə / (BrE)
having three sides or relating to a triangle
having three sides; shaped like a triangle

Examples

The artist drew a triangular shape on the whiteboard during class.
Their new house has a unique, triangular roof that stands out in the neighborhood.
Ancient shields were often triangular to deflect blows from multiple directions.

Synset Examples

a trilateral figure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trilateral - having three sides or involving three parties

Etymology

From Latin 'triangulus' meaning 'three-cornered', from 'tri-' (three) + 'angulus' (angle).
