acaulescent

1. ADJ

/ əˈkɔːləsənt / (AmE), / əˈkɔːlɪsənt / (BrE)
(of a plant) having no visible stem above the ground.
having no above-ground stem (plants)

Examples

Some species of violets are acaulescent, with leaves and flowers arising directly from the rootstock.
The dandelion is a well-known acaulescent plant found in many meadows and lawns.
Unlike woody shrubs, many alpine plants are acaulescent to endure harsh winds above the soil surface.

Etymology

From Latin 'acaulis' (without a stem), from 'a-' (without) + 'caulis' (stem, stalk).
