skirret

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɪrət / (AmE), / ˈskɪrɪt / (BrE)
an Asian perennial herb cultivated in Europe for its sweet, edible, white tuberous root, used as a vegetable
edible root vegetable plant

Examples

The skirret was a popular vegetable in medieval European gardens.
She grew skirret in her allotment for its sweet, delicate roots.
Roasted skirret adds a unique flavor to winter soups.

Etymology

From Middle English 'skirwort', literally 'white root', from Old English 'scir' (bright, pure) + 'wyrt' (plant, root).
