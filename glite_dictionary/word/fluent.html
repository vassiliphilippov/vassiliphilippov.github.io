fluent

1. ADJ

/ ˈfluːənt / (AmE), / ˈfluːənt / (BrE)
moving in a smooth, graceful, and effortless way; not stiff or awkward in movement.
moving smoothly and easily

Examples

The dancer's fluent movements captivated the audience throughout the performance.
She admired the fluent motion of the river as it wound through the valley.
With a fluent sweep of his arm, he completed the intricate painting.

Synset Examples

a long, smooth stride
the fluid motion of a cat
the liquid grace of a ballerina

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clumsy - awkward and lacking grace in movement
  • Synonym: graceful - having or showing grace or elegance in movement

Etymology

From Latin fluens, fluent-, present participle of fluere (to flow).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfluənt / (AmE), / ˈfluːənt / (BrE)
able to express oneself easily and clearly, especially in speaking or writing a language.
able to express oneself easily and clearly

Examples

She is fluent in both Spanish and French, making her an excellent translator.
His fluent presentation impressed everyone at the conference.
After years of practice, Julia gave a fluent performance on the piano.

Synset Examples

able to dazzle with his facile tongue
silver speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: articulate - able to express ideas clearly and effectively in speech or writing
  • Antonym: halting - showing hesitation or lack of fluency in speech

Etymology

From Latin 'fluens', present participle of 'fluere' (to flow).

3. ADJ

/ ˈfluːənt / (AmE), / ˈfluːənt / (BrE)
able to speak, write, or use a language smoothly, easily, and accurately.
able to speak a language easily

Examples

After years of study, she became fluent in French and could converse with native speakers easily.
He is fluent in Spanish, so he translated the document without any trouble.
Being fluent in multiple languages is a great asset in today's global job market.

Synset Examples

She is fluent in English and several other languages.
She speaks fluent Meowese, thanks to her many cats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proficient - competent or skilled in doing or using something
  • Degree Lower: conversational - able to speak a language at a level suitable for basic communication

Etymology

From Latin 'fluēns' (flowing), from 'fluere' (to flow).
