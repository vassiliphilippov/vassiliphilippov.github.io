trumpet

1. VERB

/ ˈtrʌmpət / (AmE), / ˈtrʌmpɪt / (BrE)
to make a loud, trumpet-like sound, especially as an elephant does; to announce something loudly and confidently
make a trumpet-like sound (e.g., elephant)

Examples

The excited elephants trumpeted loudly as they marched through the forest.
She trumpeted her achievements to everyone at the meeting.
The leader trumpeted his plans for the new project with great enthusiasm.

Synset Examples

Elephants are trumpeting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to announce - to make something known publicly
  • Synonym: to proclaim - to state something publicly or officially

2. VERB

/ ˈtrʌmpət / (AmE), / ˈtrʌmpɪt / (BrE)
to proclaim or announce something loudly and publicly, especially in a way that attracts attention
announce or proclaim loudly

Examples

The company decided to trumpet its latest achievements at the press conference.
He likes to trumpet his good deeds to everyone he meets.
Their success was loudly trumpeted in the local newspapers.

Synset Examples

Liberals like to trumpet their opposition to the death penalty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broadcast - to make widely known; to disseminate information
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak or say very softly or secretly

Etymology

From the noun 'trumpet', referring to the brass musical instrument; figurative use dates from the 15th century.

3. VERB

/ ˈtrʌmpət / (AmE), / ˈtrʌmpɪt / (BrE)
to play or blow on a trumpet, a brass musical instrument
to play the trumpet

Examples

He began to trumpet a lively tune in the marching band.
The musician trumpeted beautifully during the concert last night.
She has been trumpeting since she was a child, practicing every afternoon.

Etymology

From the noun 'trumpet', which comes from Old French 'trompette', diminutive of 'trompe' (trump).

4. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌmpət / (AmE), / ˈtrʌmpɪt / (BrE)
a brass musical instrument with a bright, penetrating tone, a narrow tube, and a flared bell, played by blowing and operating valves
brass musical instrument with flared bell

Examples

She played the trumpet in the school orchestra for three years.
The sound of a trumpet announced the arrival of the king.
He polished his trumpet before the concert to make sure it shone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornet - a brass instrument similar to the trumpet but with a mellower tone
  • Degree Lower: trombone - a brass instrument with a telescoping slide, related to the trumpet
  • Degree Lower: bugle - a simple brass instrument with no valves, often used for calls and signals

Etymology

From Old French trompette, diminutive of trompe (trumpet), of imitative origin.
