script

1. NOUN

/ skrɪpt / (AmE), / skrɪpt / (BrE)
handwriting, especially when considered as a style of written characters
handwriting style

Examples

Her script was elegant and easy to read.
The document was written in a delicate script.
He admired the flowing script of the letter's author.

Synset Examples

she recognized his handwriting
his hand was illegible

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handwriting - the style or manner of writing by hand

Etymology

From Latin scriptum (something written), from scribere (to write).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06415036-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ skrɪpt / (AmE), / skrɪpt / (BrE)
a particular system or style of writing letters or characters, used in a specific language or group of languages
system of written characters for a language

Examples

The Latin script is used for writing English, French, and many other languages.
Sanskrit was traditionally written in the Devanagari script.
Children learn to recognize different scripts in their language classes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alphabet - writing system based on a set of letters representing phonemes

Etymology

From Latin 'scriptum' (something written), from 'scribere' (to write).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06363020-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ skrɪpt / (AmE), / skrɪpt / (BrE)
to write the script for a play, film, broadcast, or other performance
write the script for a performance

Examples

She was hired to script the new television series.
He scripted the entire documentary in just two weeks.
They are currently scripting a play based on the novel.

Synset Examples

The playwright scripted the movie

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to write - to compose text; to put words on paper or a screen

Etymology

From Latin 'scriptum' (something written), via Old French 'escript'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01760563-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ skrɪpt / (AmE), / skrɪpt / (BrE)
the written text of a play, film, or broadcast, used as a guide for actors or performers
written text for a play, film, or broadcast

Examples

The actors spent weeks memorizing their script before opening night.
She wrote the entire script for the new television series herself.
Please bring your script to rehearsal so we can start blocking the scenes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screenplay - the script for a film, including directions for camera work
  • Synonym: transcript - a written or printed version of spoken material

Etymology

From Latin 'scriptum' (a writing), from 'scribere' (to write).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07023062-n
Synset POS: n
