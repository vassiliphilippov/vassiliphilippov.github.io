Vaccinium ashei

1. NOUN

/ vækˈsɪniəm ˈæʃaɪ / (AmE), / vækˈsɪniəm ˈæʃiː / (BrE)
A shrub native to the southeastern United States, cultivated commercially for its edible berries, especially used in canning.
shrub cultivated for its edible berries

Examples

Many growers in Georgia cultivate Vaccinium ashei for its high-yield berry harvests.
The berries of Vaccinium ashei are commonly processed in the canning industry.
Unlike some other blueberries, Vaccinium ashei thrives in the hot climate of the southeastern United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rabbiteye blueberry - common name for Vaccinium ashei, a berry-producing shrub

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Vaccinium' (a type of berry plant) and species epithet 'ashei' after American botanist William Willard Ashe.
