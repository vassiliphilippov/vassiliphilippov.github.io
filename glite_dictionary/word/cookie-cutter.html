cookie-cutter

1. ADJ

/ ˈkʊki ˌkʌtər / (AmE), / ˈkʊki ˌkʌtə / (BrE)
having a lack of originality or individuality; appearing the same as many others, as if made by a mold
lacking individuality; identical in appearance

Examples

The development was full of cookie-cutter houses that looked exactly alike.
He didn't want a cookie-cutter job; he wanted something unique and creative.
The film's plot felt cookie-cutter and uninspired, resembling dozens of other movies.

Synset Examples

a suburb of cookie-cutter houses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generic - lacking originality or individuality; not specific
  • Antonym: distinctive - having a quality that makes something clearly different

Etymology

Metaphorical use from the object 'cookie cutter', which forms identical shapes from dough.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkʊki ˌkʌtər / (AmE), / ˈkʊki ˌkʌtə / (BrE)
lacking originality or individuality; standardized and unimaginative
lacking originality; formulaic

Examples

The developer was criticized for building cookie-cutter houses that all looked the same.
Her portfolio stood out among the cookie-cutter designs submitted by other artists.
They wanted a more creative strategy, not another cookie-cutter plan.

Synset Examples

His approach was too cookie-cutterish for this project.
She prefers designs that are unique, not cookie-cutterish.
They criticized the movie for its cookie-cutter plot.
Her ideas felt cookie-cutter and lacked originality.
The presentation felt cookie-cutterish and uninspired.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formulaic - made according to a fixed plan or pattern; unoriginal or predictable
  • Synonym: stereotypical - having the qualities thought to be typical of a particular type of person or thing
  • Antonym: unique - being the only one of its kind; unlike anything else

Etymology

From the literal use of 'cookie-cutter', a device for cutting dough into uniform shapes, applied figuratively to describe things made to the same standard pattern.
