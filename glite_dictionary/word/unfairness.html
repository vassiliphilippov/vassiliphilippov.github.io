unfairness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈfɛrnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈfeənəs / (BrE)
lack of justice or equality; the quality or state of being unfair or unjust.
lack of justice or equality

Examples

The students protested loudly against the unfairness of the new grading policy.
She could not ignore the unfairness in how her coworkers were being treated.
Many people feel that economic unfairness leads to social unrest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: injustice - lack of fairness or justice
  • Antonym: fairness - impartial and just treatment or behaviour

/ ʌnˈfɛrnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɛənɪs / (BrE)
an act or instance of being unjust or not equitable; lack of fairness
an unjust act; lack of fairness

Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: injustice - lack of fairness or justice

3. NOUN

/ ʌnˈfɛr.nəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɛə.nəs / (BrE)
Lack of fairness; the state or quality of being unjust or inequitable.
lack of fairness

Examples

Many employees complained about the unfairness in the company's promotion process.
Children are quick to notice any unfairness between how siblings are treated.
The protest was sparked by perceived unfairness in the election results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: injustice - the absence of justice; an unfair act or occurrence
  • Antonym: fairness - the quality of being fair and just

Etymology

From un- (not) + fairness, where 'fair' is from Old English 'fæger' (lovely, pleasing, agreeable).
