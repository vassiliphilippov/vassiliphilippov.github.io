Scindapsus aureus

1. NOUN

/ skɪnˈdæpsəs ɔˈreɪ.əs / (AmE), / skɪnˈdapsəs ɔːˈriː.əs / (BrE)
an evergreen climbing plant with variegated leaves, commonly grown as a houseplant; also known as pothos or devil's ivy
tropical climbing plant; popular houseplant

Examples

Many offices decorate with Scindapsus aureus because it thrives in low light conditions.
The variegated leaves of Scindapsus aureus make it a popular choice among houseplant enthusiasts.
He watered his Scindapsus aureus every week to keep its foliage lush and healthy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pothos - common name for Scindapsus aureus
  • Synonym: devil's ivy - another common name for Scindapsus aureus

Etymology

From Latin; 'Scindapsus' is the genus, and 'aureus' means 'golden', referring to the plant's variegated, golden-green leaves.
