astrakhan

1. NOUN

/ ˈæstrəkæn / (AmE), / ˈæstrəkən / (BrE)
The tightly curled, glossy fur of very young lambs, especially from Central Asia, often used in coats and hats.
curly fur of young lambs

Examples

Her coat was trimmed with expensive astrakhan along the collar and cuffs.
A traditional cavalry hat was made from black astrakhan.
The designer preferred astrakhan for its distinctive curled texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: karakul - Fur from a type of sheep, closely related to astrakhan

Etymology

From Astrakhan, a region in southern Russia known for this type of fur.
