Aepyorniformes

1. NOUN

/ iːpɪˌɔrnəˈfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / iːpɪˌɔːnɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of huge, extinct, flightless birds native to Madagascar, commonly known as elephant birds
order of extinct flightless elephant birds

Examples

The extinction of the Aepyorniformes greatly altered Madagascar's ecosystem.
Scientists discovered eggs belonging to Aepyorniformes during their excavation.
Madagascar was once home to the gigantic Aepyorniformes, also known as elephant birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elephant bird - giant extinct flightless bird from Madagascar

Etymology

From Greek aepyornis (a genus name, 'tall bird') + Latin -formes (having the form of)

2. NOUN

/ ˌiːpiˌɔːrnɪˈfɔːrmiːz / (AmE), / ˌiːpɪˌɔːnɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an extinct order of huge, flightless birds native to Madagascar, commonly known as elephant birds
order of extinct giant flightless birds

Examples

Scientists believe that Aepyorniformes became extinct several centuries ago.
Eggs laid by Aepyorniformes were among the largest ever found, rivaling those of dinosaurs.
The forests of ancient Madagascar were once home to Aepyorniformes, the legendary elephant birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elephant bird - common name for birds of the order Aepyorniformes

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Aepyornis (the genus) + -formes (order suffix meaning 'in the form of').
