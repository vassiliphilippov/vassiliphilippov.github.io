briskly

1. ADV

/ ˈbrɪskli / (AmE), / ˈbrɪskli / (BrE)
in a quick, energetic, and efficient manner
in a quick and energetic way

Examples

She finished her chores briskly and left for work.
The nurse walked briskly down the hallway to answer the call.
He spoke briskly, eager to move on to the next topic.

Synset Examples

she walked briskly in the cold air
‘after lunch,’ she said briskly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quickly - at a fast speed; rapidly
  • Antonym: slowly - in a slow manner

Etymology

From 'brisk' + '-ly', with 'brisk' possibly from French 'brusque' (lively, abrupt).
