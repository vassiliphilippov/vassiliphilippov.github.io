kibibit

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪbɪbɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɪbɪbɪt / (BrE)
a unit of information equal to 1,024 bits, commonly used in computing and data transmission
unit of 1,024 bits (computing)

Examples

The file size was measured in kibibits rather than kilobits for accuracy.
A kibibit is larger than a kilobit because it uses a binary system.
Internet speeds are often quoted in bits, kilobits, or kibibits.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bit - the basic unit of information in computing
  • Degree Lower: kilobit - a unit of information equal to 1,000 bits
  • Degree Higher: mebibit - a unit of information equal to 1,048,576 bits

Etymology

Formed from 'kilo' (symbol: Ki, denoting 1024 per IEC standard) and 'bit'.
