distressfully

1. ADV

/ dɪˈstrɛsfəli / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɛsfəli / (BrE)
In a manner showing or expressing distress; with anguish or anxiety.
with distress or anguish

Examples

She looked distressfully at the mess, unsure of where to begin cleaning.
He distressfully recounted the events of that difficult night to his friends.
The puppy whimpered distressfully, searching for its lost owner.

Synset Examples

‘Doctor Rother says it's his only chance,’ she added distressfully

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distressingly - in a way that causes distress

Etymology

From 'distressful' + '-ly'; 'distressful' from Middle English, based on 'distress.'
