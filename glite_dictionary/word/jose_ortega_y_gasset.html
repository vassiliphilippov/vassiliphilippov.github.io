Jose Ortega y Gasset

1. PROPN

/ hoʊˌseɪ ɔːrˌteɪgə i ɡəˈset / (AmE), / həʊˌseɪ ɔːˌteɪɡə i ɡæˈset / (BrE)
(1883–1955) A Spanish philosopher known for advocating leadership by an intellectual elite and for his influential works on philosophy and society.
Spanish philosopher and essayist (1883–1955)

Examples

Many students of philosophy study the works of Jose Ortega y Gasset in university courses.
Jose Ortega y Gasset is renowned for his concept of the 'mass man' in his writings.
The ideas of Jose Ortega y Gasset have influenced political thought throughout the twentieth century.

Etymology

Spanish personal name.
