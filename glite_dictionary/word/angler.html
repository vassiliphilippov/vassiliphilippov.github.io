angler

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡlər / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡlə / (BrE)
a person who fishes with a rod, line, and hook, especially as a hobby rather than for commercial purposes
person who fishes with a hook and line

Examples

Every Saturday morning, the angler patiently waits by the lake for a bite.
The skilled angler landed a large trout after hours of casting.
As an amateur angler, she was thrilled to catch her first fish on holiday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fisherman - a person who catches fish for a living or as a hobby
  • Antonym: netter - a person who catches fish using nets

Etymology

From Middle English 'angeler', from Anglo-Norman, from 'angle' (fish-hook), from Latin 'angulus' (angle, corner).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡlɚ / (AmE), / ˈaŋɡlə / (BrE)
a fish with a large mouth and a wormlike filament attached to its head to lure prey; especially an anglerfish
a fish that lures prey with a filament

Examples

The deep-sea angler waits motionless for unsuspecting prey to approach its glowing lure.
Marine biologists study the unusual hunting tactics of the angler.
Some species of angler can be found in the dark depths of the Atlantic Ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anglerfish - fish with a luminescent lure growing from its head

3. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡlər / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡlə / (BrE)
a person who schemes or manipulates situations to gain an advantage
scheming or manipulative person

Examples

He was a skilled angler, always twisting situations to suit his own agenda.
Watch out for anglers in business deals; their intentions may not be genuine.
Although he acted friendly, everyone knew he was an angler with hidden motives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schemer - a person who forms secret or underhanded plans
  • Synonym: manipulator - a person who controls or influences others in a clever or unscrupulous way
  • Antonym: dupe - a person who is easily deceived or tricked

Etymology

From 'angle' (to fish with a hook), extended figuratively to scheming for gain.
