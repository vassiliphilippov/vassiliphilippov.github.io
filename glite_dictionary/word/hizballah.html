Hizballah

1. PROPN

/ hɪz.bɑːˈlɑː / (AmE), / hɪz.bəˈlɑː / (BrE)
A Shiite Muslim militant and political organization based in Lebanon, with strong ties to Iran, known for its paramilitary activities and political influence.
Lebanese Shiite militant and political group

Examples

The Lebanese government faces challenges in managing the influence of Hizballah within its borders.
International negotiations often address the issue of Hizballah's military wing and its activities.
The documentary provided a detailed history of Hizballah since its formation in the 1980s.

Etymology

From Arabic حزب الله (ḥizb allāh), meaning 'Party of God'.
