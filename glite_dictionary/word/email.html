email

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːˌmeɪl / (AmE), / ˈiː.meɪl / (BrE)
a message sent electronically from one computer user to another via the internet or other networks
an electronic message sent via the internet

Examples

I received an important email from my boss this morning.
She checks her email several times a day for new messages.
Could you please send me the details by email?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: message - a piece of information sent from one person or group to another

Etymology

Shortened from 'electronic mail'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-82169826-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈiːˌmeɪl / (AmE), / ˈiː.meɪl / (BrE)
a system of sending messages electronically from one computer user to another over a network, especially the Internet
system for electronic messages over the Internet

Examples

I check my email several times a day for new messages.
Please send the document to me by email as soon as you can.
Email is often faster and more convenient than traditional mail.

Synset Examples

you cannot send packages by electronic mail

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electronic mail - another term for email
  • Antonym: snail mail - conventional postal mail system

Etymology

Blend of 'electronic' and 'mail', first used in the early 1970s.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06289979-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈiːˌmeɪl / (AmE), / ˈiːˌmeɪl / (BrE)
an identifier, typically in the form of an address, that a person uses to send and receive messages electronically
address used to send and receive electronic messages

Examples

Please make sure your email is up to date in our records.
What is your email so I can send you the documents?
He changed his email after getting too much spam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: email address - the specific identifier used to receive electronic mail

Etymology

Shortened from 'electronic mail'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-83993005-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈiːˌmeɪl / (AmE), / ˈiːˌmeɪl / (BrE)
to send a message electronically from one computer to another over the internet
send an electronic message

Examples

I'll email you the details as soon as I get them.
She emailed her boss to request a day off next week.
He was emailing customers all morning about the new promotion.

Synset Examples

she e-mailed me the good news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: message - a communication sent from one person to another
  • Synonym: mail - to send a letter or package

Etymology

From 'electronic mail', first used in the 1980s.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01034323-v
Synset POS: v
