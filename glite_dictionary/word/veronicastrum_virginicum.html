Veronicastrum virginicum

1. NOUN

/ vɛˌɹɑːnɪˈkæstrəm vɜrˈdʒɪnɪkəm / (AmE), / vɛˌrɒnɪˈkæstrəm vɜːˈdʒɪnɪkəm / (BrE)
A tall perennial herb native to eastern North America, with slender spikes of small white or purple flowers; also known as Culver's root.
a North American flowering herb; Culver's root

Examples

Veronicastrum virginicum thrives in moist, well-drained soils in prairies and open woodlands.
Gardeners often choose Veronicastrum virginicum for its tall stature and elegant flower spikes.
The native Veronicastrum virginicum attracts a variety of pollinators to the summer garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Culver's root - another common name for Veronicastrum virginicum

Etymology

Genus name from 'Veronica' (a plant genus) + Latin 'castrum' (fortress); species epithet 'virginicum' refers to Virginia, USA.
