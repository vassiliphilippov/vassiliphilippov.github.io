noesis

1. NOUN

/ noʊˈiːsɪs / (AmE), / nəʊˈiːsɪs / (BrE)
the process of intellectual understanding, especially in philosophy and psychology, referring to the act of perceiving or reasoning
process of understanding or reasoning

Examples

The philosopher argued that all knowledge is acquired through noesis rather than sensory experience.
In cognitive psychology, noesis refers to the mental activity involved in conscious thought.
His research explores the relationship between emotion and noesis in the process of decision-making.

Etymology

From Greek noēsis (intelligence, understanding) and noein (to perceive, think).
