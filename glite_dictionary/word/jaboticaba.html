jaboticaba

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɑːbəˈtiːkəˌbɑː / (AmE), / ˌdʒabəˈtiːkəbə / (BrE)
a small evergreen tree native to Brazil and the West Indies, grown for its edible purple, grape-like fruit that grows directly on its branches
a Brazilian tree with edible purple fruit

Examples

The jaboticaba produces clusters of fruit along its trunk and branches.
I tasted fresh jaboticaba for the first time during my trip to Brazil.
They planted a jaboticaba tree in their backyard for its unusual appearance and delicious fruit.

Etymology

From Portuguese jabuticaba, from Tupi jabuticaba.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɑːboʊtiˈkɑːbə / (AmE), / ˌdʒabəʊtɪˈkɑːbə / (BrE)
A tough-skinned, purple, grapelike tropical fruit native to Brazil, known for its sweet, juicy pulp.
Brazilian grapelike tropical fruit

Examples

The market stall displayed a basket of fresh jaboticaba alongside other exotic fruits.
She tasted jaboticaba for the first time while traveling in Brazil and loved its unique flavor.
Jams and liqueurs made from jaboticaba are popular in South American cuisine.

Etymology

From Brazilian Portuguese 'jabuticaba', of Tupi origin, referring to the fruit of the Plinia cauliflora tree.
