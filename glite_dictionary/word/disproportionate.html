disproportionate

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪsprəˈpɔrʃənət / (AmE), / ˌdɪsprəˈpɔːʃənət / (BrE)
too large or too small in comparison with something else; out of proportion.
out of proportion; unbalanced

Examples

The punishment seemed disproportionate to the minor offense he committed.
A disproportionate number of resources were allocated to a single department.
The costs grew disproportionate to the project's actual needs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: proportionate - in proper or corresponding proportion

Etymology

From Latin dis- (apart) + proportionatus (proportioned).

2. ADJ

/ ˌdɪsprəˈpɔrʃənət / (AmE), / ˌdɪsprəˈpɔːʃənət / (BrE)
too large or too small in comparison with something else; not in correct or balanced proportion.
not in correct proportion

Examples

The punishment seemed disproportionate to the minor offense he had committed.
They allocated a disproportionate amount of resources to one department, neglecting the others.
The media gave disproportionate attention to a relatively small issue.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: proportionate - having the correct or appropriate relationship in size, amount, or degree

Etymology

From dis- (not) + proportionate, based on Latin proportio (proportion).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00484048-s
Synset POS: s
