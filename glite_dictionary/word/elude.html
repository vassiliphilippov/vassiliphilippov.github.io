to elude

1. VERB

/ ɪˈluːd / (AmE), / ɪˈluːd / (BrE)
to escape from or avoid someone or something, especially in a skillful or clever way; or to fail to be understood or achieved by someone
escape or avoid

Examples

The suspect managed to elude the authorities for months before finally being caught.
A clear understanding of the complex theory continues to elude researchers.
The rare bird has eluded all attempts to capture it on camera.

Synset Examples

The thief eluded the police
This difficult idea seems to evade her
The event evades explanation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evade - to avoid or escape from by cunning
  • Synonym: escape - to get away from a place or danger
  • Antonym: capture - to catch or seize

Etymology

From Latin 'eludere' meaning 'to escape from, make a mockery of'.

2. VERB

/ ɪˈlud / (AmE), / ɪˈluːd / (BrE)
(of a fact, quality, or idea) to fail to be understood or remembered by someone.
fail to be understood by

Examples

The true meaning of the poem continues to elude me, no matter how many times I read it.
Despite years of research, a solution to the problem has eluded scientists.
Her name eluded him just when he needed to introduce her.

Synset Examples

What you are seeing in him eludes me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escape - to get free from something; to avoid capture, danger, or harm
  • Synonym: to evade - to avoid or escape from someone or something, especially in a clever way
  • Antonym: to comprehend - to understand or grasp mentally

Etymology

From Latin 'eludere' meaning 'to escape from, to make a game of'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02729107-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪˈlud / (AmE), / ɪˈluːd / (BrE)
to avoid or escape from something, especially by being clever or skillful; to avoid fulfilling, answering, or performing duties, questions, or issues
avoid or escape from (often skillfully)

Examples

The minister managed to elude questions about the budget during the press conference.
She frequently eludes her responsibilities at work by delegating them to others.
Despite repeated attempts, the answer continued to elude him during the interview.

Synset Examples

He dodged the issue
she skirted the problem
They tend to evade their responsibilities
he evaded the questions skillfully

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evade - to escape or avoid, especially by cleverness
  • Synonym: dodge - to avoid (something) by a sudden, quick movement or by cleverness
  • Antonym: to confront - to face up to (something), especially an issue or difficulty

Etymology

From Latin 'eludere', from 'e-' (out of) + 'ludere' (to play).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00811316-v
Synset POS: v
