erose

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈroʊs / (AmE), / ɪˈrəʊs / (BrE)
having an irregularly notched or gnawed margin, especially referring to the edge of a leaf or petal
having an irregularly notched margin

Examples

The leaf margins appeared erose, as if they had been eaten by insects.
Many wildflowers have erose petals that give them a delicate, fringed look.
Botanists noted the erose edges of the specimens collected in the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serrated - having a margin with saw-like notches
  • Antonym: entire - having a smooth, unbroken margin (opposite of erose)

Etymology

From Latin 'erosus', past participle of 'erodere' (to gnaw, eat away).
