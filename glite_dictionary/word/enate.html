enate

1. ADJ

/ ˈiːneɪt / (AmE), / ˈiːneɪt / (BrE)
Relating to or descended from the mother's side of the family; maternal.
on the mother's side (maternal)

Examples

Her enate relatives gathered for the family reunion on her mother's side.
He researched his enate ancestors to learn more about his maternal heritage.
In some cultures, inheritance passes through the enate line rather than the paternal side.

Synset Examples

my maternal grandmother

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agnate - related on the father's side

Etymology

From Latin enatus, past participle of enasci (to be born of) + English -ate.

2. NOUN

/ ˈiːneɪt / (AmE), / ˈiːneɪt / (BrE)
A person who is related through the mother's side of the family.
relative on the mother's side

Examples

In traditional societies, inheritance sometimes passed from an enate rather than an agnate.
She traced her ancestry and discovered she had several distinguished enates.
Anthropologists often distinguish between agnates and enates when studying kinship systems.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agnate - a person related on the father's side

Etymology

From Latin enatus (sprung from, descended from).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10256218-n
Synset POS: n
