Strepsiceros

1. NOUN

/ strepˈsɪsɪˌroʊs / (AmE), / strepˈsɪsɪˌrəʊs / (BrE)
A former genus of African antelopes including species such as the kudu, bongo, nyala, and bushbuck.
former genus of African antelopes

Examples

The greater kudu was once classified under the genus Strepsiceros in early zoological texts.
Early explorers in Africa documented several species within Strepsiceros, including the majestic nyala.
Taxonomists have since reclassified the antelopes once grouped as Strepsiceros into different genera.

Etymology

From Greek strepsis (turning) + keras (horn), referring to the spiral horns.

2. NOUN

/ ˌstrep.sɪˈsɪr.əs / (AmE), / ˌstrep.sɪˈsɪə.rəs / (BrE)
A genus of African antelopes that includes species such as kudus, bongos, nyalas, and bushbucks.
genus of African antelopes

Examples

The greater kudu, once classified under Strepsiceros, is known for its impressive spiral horns.
Older zoological texts often list bongos as belonging to the genus Strepsiceros.
Bushbucks are sometimes referred to as members of Strepsiceros in historical literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tragelaphus - Current genus name for kudus, bongos, nyalas, and bushbucks

Etymology

From Greek 'strepsis' (twisting) + 'keras' (horn), referring to the spiral-shaped horns of species in this genus.
