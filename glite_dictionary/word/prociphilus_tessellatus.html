Prociphilus tessellatus

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈsɪfɪləs tɛˈsɛlətəs / (AmE), / prəˈsɪfɪləs tɛˈsɛlətəs / (BrE)
a species of aphid that attacks alder trees, commonly known as the alder woolly aphid
a type of aphid infesting alders

Examples

The infestation of Prociphilus tessellatus caused a white, woolly appearance on the alder trees in spring.
Researchers studied the effect of Prociphilus tessellatus on local alder populations.
Gardeners are on alert for Prociphilus tessellatus due to its damaging impact on ornamental alder shrubs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphid - a small sap-sucking insect of the family Aphididae
