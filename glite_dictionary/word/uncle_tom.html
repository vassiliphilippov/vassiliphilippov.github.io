Uncle Tom

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌŋkəl tɑm / (AmE), / ˈʌŋkəl tɒm / (BrE)
an offensive term for a Black person who is regarded as excessively subservient or deferential to white people
offensive term for a submissive Black person
offensive

Examples

He was called an Uncle Tom for always agreeing with the boss, even at his own expense.
The accusation of being an Uncle Tom deeply hurt him.
Some activists criticized the politician as an Uncle Tom after his controversial remarks.

Synset Examples

The character was unfairly labeled as Uncle Tom in the debate.
His Uncle Tommery attitude annoyed everyone at the party.
Everyone knew he had an Uncle Tommery streak when he spoke.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sellout - person who betrays their own group for personal gain
  • Synonym: race traitor - someone regarded as betraying their racial group

Etymology

From the character Uncle Tom in Harriet Beecher Stowe's novel 'Uncle Tom's Cabin' (1852).
