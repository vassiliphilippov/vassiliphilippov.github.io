to relax

1. VERB

/ rɪˈlæks / (AmE), / rɪˈlaks / (BrE)
to make someone feel less tense, anxious, or stressed; to cause to feel relaxed
to make someone less tense or anxious

Examples

A warm cup of tea always relaxes me after a long day.
The music was chosen specifically to relax the guests before the ceremony.
She tried to relax her anxious friend with gentle words and a smile.

Synset Examples

A hot bath always relaxes me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calm - to make someone or something less agitated or excited

Etymology

From Latin relaxare (to loosen, stretch out), from re- (again, back) + laxare (to loosen), from laxus (loose).
