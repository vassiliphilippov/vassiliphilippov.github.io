Stictomys

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪk.təˌmɪs / (AmE), / ˈstɪk.təˌmɪs / (BrE)
A genus of rodents commonly known as mountain pacas, found in certain mountainous regions of Central and South America.
genus of rodents (mountain pacas)

Examples

The Stictomys is known for its distinctive spotted fur and nocturnal habits.
Researchers discovered a new population of Stictomys in the Andean highlands.
Unlike lowland pacas, the Stictomys prefers cooler, mountainous habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paca - a large nocturnal rodent of South America

Etymology

From Greek 'stiktos' (spotted) and 'mys' (mouse/rat).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɪk.toʊ.mɪs / (AmE), / ˈstɪk.təʊ.mɪs / (BrE)
A genus of rodents in the family Cuniculidae, commonly known as mountain pacas.
a genus of mountain pacas (rodents)

Examples

The Stictomys is found in mountainous regions of South America.
Researchers are studying the habitat preferences of Stictomys species.
Members of the genus Stictomys are less well-known than their lowland relatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paca - a large rodent found in Central and South America

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'stiktos' (spotted) and 'mys' (mouse, rat).
