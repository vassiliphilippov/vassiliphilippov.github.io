achondroplastic

1. ADJ

/ eɪˌkɑːndroʊˈplæstɪk / (AmE), / eɪˌkɒndrəˈplɑːstɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of achondroplasia, a genetic disorder affecting bone growth
relating to achondroplasia
medical

Examples

Children with achondroplastic features typically have short limbs but average-sized torsos.
The achondroplastic patient required special orthopedic care during surgery.
Researchers are investigating new treatments for achondroplastic dwarfism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: achondroplasia - genetic disorder affecting bone growth

Etymology

From 'achondroplasia' + '-ic'. 'Achondroplasia' from Greek 'a-' (not) + 'chondros' (cartilage) + 'plasia' (formation).
