tetrachloromethane

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛ.trəˌklɔː.rəˈmɛθ.eɪn / (AmE), / ˌtɛ.trəˌklɔː.rəˈmiːθ.eɪn / (BrE)
A colorless, nonflammable liquid chemical (CCl₄) formerly used as a solvent and cleaning fluid, now largely restricted due to toxicity.
toxic chemical solvent (CCl₄)
medical

Examples

Tetrachloromethane was once commonly used as a cleaning agent until its health risks became clear.
Exposure to tetrachloromethane can cause serious liver damage if inhaled or ingested.
The laboratory replaced tetrachloromethane with less hazardous solvents for their experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbon tetrachloride - another name for tetrachloromethane

Etymology

From tetra- (four) + chloro- (chlorine) + methane (a hydrocarbon), reflecting its chemical structure.
