Volgaic

1. NOUN

/ voʊlˈɡeɪɪk / (AmE), / vɒlˈɡeɪɪk / (BrE)
a group of Finnic languages spoken around the Volga River in Russia
group of Finnic languages near the Volga River

Examples

The Volgaic languages are primarily spoken in the region surrounding the Volga River.
Researchers have studied the decline of traditional Volgaic dialects over the past century.
Mordvin is classified as a Volgaic language by many linguists.

Etymology

From Volga (the river) + -ic (forming adjectives and nouns).
