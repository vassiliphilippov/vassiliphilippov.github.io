Cercis

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜːrsɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɜːsɪs / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous shrubs and small trees, known as redbuds, native to eastern Asia, southern Europe, and North America.
a genus of redbud shrubs and trees

Examples

The Cercis in my garden produces beautiful pink blossoms each spring.
Many parks feature Cercis trees for their striking early-season flowers.
Botanists study the growth habits of Cercis across different climates.

Etymology

From Greek kerkís (a weaver's shuttle), referencing the shape of the seed pods.
