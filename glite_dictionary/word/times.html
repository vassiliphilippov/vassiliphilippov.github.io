2. NOUN

/ taɪmz / (AmE), / taɪmz / (BrE)
(in arithmetic) the operation of multiplication; used to indicate that one number is multiplied by another.
arithmetic multiplication

Examples

He said that seven times eight is fifty-six.
If you add five times four marbles, you'll get twenty marbles.
In the equation three times nine, the answer is twenty-seven.

Synset Examples

the multiplication of four by three gives twelve
four times three equals twelve

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multiply - to increase or combine by multiplication

Etymology

From the plural of 'time' in the sense of 'an instance', used historically in expressions referring to repeated addition.
