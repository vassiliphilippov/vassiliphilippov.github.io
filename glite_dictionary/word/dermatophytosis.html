dermatophytosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɜːrmətoʊfaɪˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌdɜːmətəʊfaɪˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A fungal infection of the skin, especially affecting moist areas commonly covered by clothing.
fungal infection of the skin
medical

Examples

Athletes are particularly susceptible to dermatophytosis in warm, humid environments.
Treatment for dermatophytosis often involves antifungal creams applied directly to the skin.
The doctor diagnosed his persistent rash as a case of dermatophytosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ringworm - common name for fungal skin infections caused by dermatophytes

Etymology

From Greek 'derma' (skin) + 'phyton' (plant) + '-osis' (condition).
