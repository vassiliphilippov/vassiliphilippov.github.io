quintette

1. NOUN

/ kwɪnˈtɛt / (AmE), / kwɪnˈtɛt / (BrE)
A musical composition written to be performed by five musicians.
musical work for five performers

Examples

The composer wrote a beautiful quintette for strings.
Their latest album features a piano quintette that received great reviews.
The ensemble enjoyed performing the challenging new quintette.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quintet - a group of five musicians or a composition for five performers

Etymology

From Italian 'quintetto', diminutive of 'quinto' (fifth), from Latin 'quintus'.

2. NOUN

/ kwɪnˈtɛt / (AmE), / kwɪnˈtɛt / (BrE)
a group or set of five similar people or things considered as a unit
group of five

Examples

The award was given to a quintette of talented young poets.
A quintette of paintings by the same artist hung in the gallery.
The project team formed a quintette to tackle the new assignment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quintet - group or set of five people or things, especially in music

Etymology

From Italian 'quintetto', diminutive of 'quinto' (fifth), via French 'quintette'.

3. NOUN

/ kwɪnˈtɛt / (AmE), / kwɪnˈtɛt / (BrE)
A group of five people or things considered as a single entity; especially a group of five musicians performing together.
group of five people or things

Examples

The jazz quintette performed an original composition at the concert.
She joined a vocal quintette that specialized in Renaissance music.
At the awards ceremony, the quintette stood together on stage holding their trophy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: quartet - group of four people or things, especially musicians
  • Degree Higher: sextet - group of six people or things, especially musicians
  • Synonym: quintet - group of five people or things

Etymology

From Italian 'quintetto', diminutive of 'quinto' (fifth), from Latin 'quintus'.

4. NOUN

/ kwɪnˈtɛt / (AmE), / kwɪnˈtɛt / (BrE)
A group of five musicians or singers who perform together as an ensemble.
group of five musicians or singers

Examples

The jazz quintette performed an original piece for the audience.
She joined a vocal quintette specializing in Renaissance music.
Each member of the string quintette contributed a unique sound to the ensemble.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: quartet - group of four musicians or singers performing together
  • Degree Higher: sextet - group of six musicians or singers performing together
  • Synonym: quintet - alternative spelling; a group of five musicians or singers

Etymology

From Italian 'quintetto', diminutive of 'quinto' (fifth), from Latin 'quintus' (fifth).
