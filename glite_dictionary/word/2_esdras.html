2 Esdras

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkənd ˈɛzdrəs / (AmE), / ˈsɛkənd ˈɛzdrəs / (BrE)
an Apocryphal book consisting of angelic revelations, often included in the Old Testament Apocrypha in some Christian traditions
an Apocryphal book of angelic revelations

Examples

Many scholars debate whether 2 Esdras should be considered canonical scripture.
The visions described in 2 Esdras have influenced Christian and Jewish thought for centuries.
She referenced 2 Esdras in her research on apocalyptic literature.

Etymology

From Greek Esdras, a form of the name Ezra; '2' indicates its sequence among books attributed to Ezra.
