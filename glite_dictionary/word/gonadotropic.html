gonadotropic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɡoʊˌnædəˈtrɑpɪk / (AmE), / ˌɡəʊ.nə.dəˈtrɒp.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving gonadotropins, hormones that stimulate the activity of the gonads (ovaries or testes).
related to gonadotropins or their effects
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that gonadotropic hormones play a crucial role in reproductive health.
Certain tumors can cause an increase in gonadotropic hormone levels.
Researchers studied the gonadotropic effects of the new medication on laboratory animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gonadotropin - a hormone that stimulates the gonads

Etymology

From 'gonadotropin' (from 'gonad' + Greek 'tropos' meaning 'turn' or 'change') + '-ic'.
