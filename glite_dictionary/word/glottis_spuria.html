glottis spuria

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɑːtɪs ˈspjʊəriə / (AmE), / ˈɡlɒtɪs ˈspjʊəriə / (BrE)
the opening between the false vocal folds in the larynx
opening between false vocal folds
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully examined the glottis spuria to assess any structural abnormalities.
In certain vocal disorders, the glottis spuria may become partially obstructed.
Detailed imaging revealed swelling near the glottis spuria, affecting the patient's breathing.

Etymology

From Latin 'glottis' (from Greek 'glōttis', 'the vocal apparatus') and Latin 'spuria' (false).
