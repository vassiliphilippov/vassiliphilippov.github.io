Rossbach

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɑs.bɑk / (AmE), / ˈrɒs.bæk / (BrE)
a battle fought in 1757 during the Seven Years' War, in which Prussian forces under Frederick the Great defeated French and Austrian armies
1757 battle in the Seven Years' War

Examples

Historians regard Rossbach as a turning point in the Seven Years' War.
At Rossbach, Frederick the Great achieved a decisive victory over his enemies.
The strategy employed at Rossbach is still studied by military scholars today.

Etymology

Named after the village of Rossbach (now Roßbach) in Saxony-Anhalt, Germany, near where the battle took place.
