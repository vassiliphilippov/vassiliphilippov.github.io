consequence

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.sɪˌkwəns / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.sɪ.kwəns / (BrE)
the result or effect of an action or event, especially as it affects someone or something
result or effect of an action

Examples

Every action you take has a consequence, whether good or bad.
She had to face the consequence of her decision to leave the company.
Pollution can have serious consequences for the environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: result - something that happens or exists because of something else
  • Antonym: cause - a person or thing that gives rise to an action, phenomenon, or condition

Etymology

From Latin 'consequentia', from 'consequor' (to follow closely).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːnsəˌkwəns / (AmE), / ˈkɒnsɪkwəns / (BrE)
a result or effect, typically one that follows logically or naturally from an action or condition
result or effect of an action

Examples

The new law may have serious consequences for small businesses.
He didn't consider the consequences before making his decision.
Failure to follow the instructions could lead to unintended consequences.

Synset Examples

the magnetic effect was greater when the rod was lengthwise

he acted very wise after the event

Related Terms

  • Synonym: result - something that happens or exists because of something else
  • Antonym: cause - a person or thing that gives rise to an action, phenomenon, or condition

Etymology

From Latin consequentia (a following, sequence), from consequor (to follow closely).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːnsəkwəns / (AmE), / ˈkɒnsɪkwəns / (BrE)
The importance or significance of something, especially in terms of its effect or influence.
significance or importance

Examples

Nothing the committee does will be of any consequence unless it addresses the mayor's main concerns.
The discovery was an event of great consequence for the scientific community.
His actions are of little consequence to the outcome of the case.

Synset Examples

decisions of great consequence are made by the president himself
virtue is of more moment than security
that result is of no consequence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: significance - the quality of being important or of giving meaning
  • Synonym: importance - the state or fact of being of great significance or value
  • Antonym: triviality - lack of seriousness or importance; insignificance

Etymology

From Latin consequentia (that which follows), from consequor (follow closely).
