perceptual

1. ADJ

/ pərˈsɛp.tʃu.əl / (AmE), / pəˈsɛp.tjʊ.əl / (BrE)
Relating to the ability or process of perceiving things through the senses.
relating to perception or the senses

Examples

A sudden change in light can trigger a perceptual reaction in the brain.
His artwork explores the boundaries of perceptual experience.
Children at that age are developing their perceptual skills rapidly.

Synset Examples

perceptual stimulus

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cognitive - concerned with thinking, understanding, and knowing
  • Synonym: sensory - relating to sensation or the senses

Etymology

From Latin 'perceptio' (perception) via 'percept-', stem of 'percipere' (to perceive), plus '-al'.
