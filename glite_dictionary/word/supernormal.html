supernormal

1. ADJ

/ ˌsuːpərˈnɔrməl / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈnɔːməl / (BrE)
beyond the range of the normal or scientifically explainable; exceeding what is considered typical or natural.
beyond normal or scientific explanation

Examples

Some people claim to have supernormal abilities, such as telepathy or clairvoyance.
The experimenters were searching for evidence of supernormal perception in their subjects.
The child's supernormal memory amazed even the most skeptical scientists.

Synset Examples

supernormal intimations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paranormal - not scientifically explainable; beyond normal experience

Etymology

From Latin super- (above, beyond) + normalis (according to a rule/norm).

2. ADJ

/ ˌsuːpərˈnɔrməl / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈnɔːməl / (BrE)
greater than what is usual, normal, or average; exceeding a typical level or standard.
exceeding normal or average limits

Examples

The company recorded supernormal profits during the economic boom.
Her reaction to the stimulus was considered supernormal by the research team.
A supernormal influx of tourists overwhelmed the city's infrastructure.

Synset Examples

years of supernormal employment during the war
a phase of supernormal excitability

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abnormal - deviating from what is normal, especially in a negative or strange way
  • Antonym: subnormal - below normal or average
  • Synonym: exceptional - unusually good; outstanding or above the norm

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'super-' (above, beyond) and 'normal.'
