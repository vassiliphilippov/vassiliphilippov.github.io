demeanour

1. NOUN

/ dəˈminɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈmiːnə / (BrE)
The way in which a person behaves or appears to others; outward manner or conduct.
outward behavior or manner
british

Examples

Her calm demeanour impressed the interview panel.
Despite his angry words, his demeanour remained polite and composed.
The teacher's friendly demeanour put the students at ease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demeanor - the American English spelling of 'demeanour'
  • Synonym: manner - a person's way of behaving toward others
  • Synonym: bearing - the way a person carries themselves

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *demener* (to conduct oneself), from Old French *demener*, from Latin *minare* (to drive, lead).
