polyandry

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈændri / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈændri / (BrE)
the practice or condition of having more than one husband at the same time
the practice of having several husbands

Examples

Polyandry is practiced in certain Himalayan communities due to economic and social reasons.
The documentary explores how polyandry affects family dynamics and inheritance.
Unlike polygyny, polyandry is relatively rare across cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polygamy - the practice of having more than one spouse at a time
  • Antonym: polygyny - the practice of having more than one wife at a time

Etymology

From Greek poly- (many) + andros (man, husband).
