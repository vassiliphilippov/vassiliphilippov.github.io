to suspend

1. VERB

/ səˈspɛnd / (AmE), / səˈspɛnd / (BrE)
to officially stop something from continuing, especially for a short time
temporarily stop or halt

Examples

The university decided to suspend classes due to the severe weather conditions.
After failing to meet safety standards, the factory had to suspend its operations temporarily.
The government will suspend aid payments pending further investigation.

Synset Examples

Suspend the aid to the war-torn country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halt - to bring or come to an abrupt stop
  • Antonym: to continue - to keep going without stopping

Etymology

From Latin suspendere, from sub- (up) + pendere (to hang).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00364146-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ səˈspɛnd / (AmE), / səˈspɛnd / (BrE)
to officially prevent someone from attending work, school, or an activity for a limited period, usually as a punishment or while a decision is made
temporarily bar from school or office

Examples

The principal decided to suspend the student for breaking the school rules.
She was suspended from her job while the accusations were investigated.
If you continue to arrive late, they may be forced to suspend you.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: to expel - to officially force someone to leave a school or organization permanently

Etymology

From Latin *suspendere*, from *sub-* (up from under) + *pendere* (to hang).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02507686-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ səˈspɛnd / (AmE), / səˈspɛnd / (BrE)
to hang something from a support so that it is free to move or swing; to cause to hang freely
hang something freely from a support

Examples

They decided to suspend a lamp over the dining table for better lighting.
The artist chose to suspend the mobile from the ceiling, allowing it to move in the breeze.
After filling the piñata, they suspended it from a tree branch for the children to play with.

Synset Examples

The secret police suspended their victims from the ceiling and beat them

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hang - to be attached or supported from above

Etymology

From Latin suspendere (to hang up), from sub- (from below) + pendere (to hang).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01483784-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ səˈspɛnd / (AmE), / səˈspɛnd / (BrE)
to cause (particles or substances) to be dispersed and held within a fluid, so that they do not settle quickly or separate
hold particles in a fluid

Examples

The technician carefully suspended the solid particles in the solution for accurate measurement.
In this experiment, add water slowly to suspend the powder thoroughly.
Colloids occur when fine particles are suspended in a liquid and do not settle out.

Synset Examples

suspend the particles

Related Terms

  • Antonym: precipitate - to cause (a substance) to be separated from a solution as a solid

Etymology

From Latin suspendere, from sub- ‘up from under’ + pendere ‘to hang’.
