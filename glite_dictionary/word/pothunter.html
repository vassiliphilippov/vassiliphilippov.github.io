pothunter

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːthʌntər / (AmE), / ˈpɒthʌntə / (BrE)
A person who enters competitions or contests primarily to win trophies or prizes, rather than for enjoyment or genuine competition.
someone who enters contests to win trophies

Examples

The club discourages pothunters who care more about the prize than the spirit of the game.
As a well-known pothunter, he joins every major cooking contest in the city.
Some people accuse her of being a pothunter, but she insists she truly loves competing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trophy hunter - a person who participates to collect trophies or prizes

Etymology

Originally referred to a person who hunted game for food (for the pot), later applied to those seeking material rewards in competitions.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊˌhʌntər / (AmE), / ˈpəʊˌhʌntə / (BrE)
a person who hunts animals or birds primarily for food rather than for sport
a hunter seeking food, not sport

Examples

The old stories spoke of a pothunter who roamed the forests just to feed his family.
Unlike trophy hunters, a pothunter values every animal for its meat rather than as a prize.
In lean times, many villagers became pothunters out of necessity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hunter - a person who hunts animals

Etymology

From 'pot' (as in cooking pot) + 'hunter', referring to hunting to fill the cooking pot.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10480067-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊthʌntər / (AmE), / ˈpɒthʌntə / (BrE)
a person who excavates archaeological sites for personal gain or without professional standards, often disregarding scientific methods
nonprofessional or unethical archaeologist

Examples

The ancient site was damaged by a pothunter who removed artifacts without permission.
Museums often refuse to acquire items obtained by a pothunter.
Authorities warn that a pothunter can destroy valuable historical context while collecting relics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archeologist - a person who studies human history through excavation and analysis of artifacts

Etymology

From 'pot' (as in pottery) + 'hunter', referring to people seeking artifacts, especially pots.
