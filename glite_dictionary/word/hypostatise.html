to hypostatise

1. VERB

/ haɪˈpɑstəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / haɪˈpɒstəsaɪz / (BrE)
To treat or regard a conceptual or abstract idea as if it has real, concrete existence.
regard an abstract idea as real

Examples

Many philosophers warn that one should not hypostatise social constructs as if they were physical realities.
We often hypostatise emotions, speaking of them as tangible objects.
The theory was criticised for its tendency to hypostatise abstract concepts without empirical evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reify - to make something abstract more concrete or real

Etymology

From Greek 'hypostatoun' (to underpin, to substantiate), from 'hypostasis' (substance, foundation).
