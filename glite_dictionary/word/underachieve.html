to underachieve

1. VERB

/ ˌʌndɚəˈtʃiv / (AmE), / ˌʌndərəˈtʃiːv / (BrE)
to perform less well or with less success than expected or potential
do worse than expected

Examples

Despite his talent, Tom continues to underachieve at school year after year.
Many companies underachieved in the last quarter due to economic challenges.
If you underachieve in this role, it could affect your chances of promotion.

Synset Examples

John consistently underachieves, although he is very able
My stocks underperformed last year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to underperform - to do less well than expected, especially financially or in comparison with others
  • Antonym: to overachieve - to perform better or with more success than expected

Etymology

From 'under-' (meaning below) + 'achieve'. Formed in English in the mid-20th century.
