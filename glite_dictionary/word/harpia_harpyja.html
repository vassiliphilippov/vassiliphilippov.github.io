Harpia harpyja

1. NOUN

/ hɑrˈpiːə hɑrˈpiːdʒə / (AmE), / hɑːˈpɪə hɑːˈpɪdʒə / (BrE)
a large black-and-white crested eagle native to tropical America, also known as the harpy eagle
large crested eagle of tropical America

Examples

The Harpia harpyja is among the largest and most powerful eagles in the world.
Researchers in Brazil are studying the habitat of the rare Harpia harpyja.
With its striking crest, the Harpia harpyja is often called the harpy eagle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harpy eagle - another common name for Harpia harpyja

Etymology

From Latin; genus 'Harpia' and species 'harpyja' alluding to the mythical harpies for their fearsome presence.
