andiron

1. NOUN

/ ˈændˌaɪərn / (AmE), / ˈandʌɪən / (BrE)
One of a pair of metal supports in a fireplace, used to hold up logs while they burn.
metal support for logs in a fireplace

Examples

The brass andiron gleamed in the glow of the fire.
He carefully placed the log onto the andiron before lighting it.
The antique andiron set had intricate designs on each end.

Synset Examples

the andirons were too hot to touch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firedog - another term for a metal support for logs in a fireplace

Etymology

From Old French *andier*, of unknown origin.
