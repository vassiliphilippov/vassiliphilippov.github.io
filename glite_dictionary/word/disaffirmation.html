disaffirmation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.ə.fɚˈmeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ə.fəˈmeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of stating that something is not true or refusing to accept something as valid
the act of denying the truth of a claim

Examples

The company's disaffirmation of the report led to further public skepticism.
Legal proceedings required a formal disaffirmation of the contract terms by the minor.
His repeated disaffirmation of the rumors did little to quell public curiosity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: denial - the action of declaring something to be untrue
  • Antonym: affirmation - the act of declaring something to be true

Etymology

From 'disaffirm' (to deny or reject), modeled on 'affirmation' from Latin 'affirmatio'.
