carcinogenic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːrsɪnəˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌkɑːsɪnəˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
Able to cause or tend to cause cancer.
cancer-causing
medical

Examples

Prolonged exposure to the substance was found to be carcinogenic in laboratory animals.
Many people are concerned about the potentially carcinogenic effects of processed meats.
The report concluded that certain pesticides are highly carcinogenic to humans.

Synset Examples

the carcinogenic action of certain chemicals

Related Terms

  • Antonym: noncarcinogenic - not cancer-causing

Etymology

From carcinoma (a type of cancer) + -genic (producing), from Greek *karkinos* (crab, cancer) and *-genēs* (born of, produced by).
