lovingness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌvɪŋnəs / (AmE), / ˈlʌvɪŋnəs / (BrE)
the quality of having or showing love and affection
quality of being loving or affectionate

Examples

Her lovingness was evident in the way she cared for everyone around her.
There was a certain lovingness in his tone that made me feel safe.
Their relationship was marked by warmth and lovingness rather than conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affection - a gentle feeling of fondness or liking
  • Antonym: hostility - unfriendliness or opposition

Etymology

From 'loving' + '-ness', indicating the state or quality of being loving.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlʌvɪŋnəs / (AmE), / ˈlʌvɪŋnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being loving or showing affection
quality of being loving or affectionate

Examples

Her lovingness toward her children was evident in everything she did.
You could feel the lovingness in his voice when he spoke to his partner.
The teacher's lovingness created a warm and welcoming classroom atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affection - a gentle feeling of fondness or liking
  • Synonym: love - intense feeling of deep affection
  • Antonym: hostility - unfriendly or antagonistic behavior

Etymology

From "loving" + "-ness".
