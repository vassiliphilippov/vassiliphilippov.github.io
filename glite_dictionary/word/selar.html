Selar

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈlɑr / (AmE), / sɪˈlɑː / (BrE)
A type of tropical marine fish, also known as the big-eyed scad, found in warm ocean waters.
tropical fish; big-eyed scad

Examples

Fishermen caught several Selar off the coast during the early morning.
The local market had a fresh supply of Selar for sale.
Marine biologists are studying the migratory patterns of the Selar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: big-eyed scad - another name for the Selar; a tropical marine fish

Etymology

From Malay 'selar', a local name for the fish.

2. NOUN

/ sɛˈlɑr / (AmE), / sɪˈlɑː / (BrE)
A genus of tropical fish in the family Carangidae, commonly known as big-eyed scads.
a genus of big-eyed scad fish

Examples

The Selar genus includes important commercial fish such as the big-eyed scad.
Researchers conducted a study on the migratory patterns of Selar in tropical waters.
Fishermen often catch Selar for both local consumption and export.

Etymology

From modern Latin Selar, the given genus name in zoological nomenclature.
