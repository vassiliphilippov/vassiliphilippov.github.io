gawkiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɔːki.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɡɔːki.nəs / (BrE)
awkwardness in movement or posture; lacking grace or elegance
awkwardness; lack of grace

Examples

Her gawkiness was evident when she tripped over her own feet during the dance.
Despite his gawkiness, he moved with surprising speed on the basketball court.
The boy's gawkiness made him self-conscious during the school play.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gracefulness - elegance and smoothness of movement
  • Synonym: awkwardness - the quality of being awkward or lacking grace

Etymology

From 'gawky' (clumsy, awkward) + '-ness'.
