Zairean

1. NOUN

/ zaɪˈɪriən / (AmE), / zaɪˈɪəriən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Zaire (now known as the Democratic Republic of the Congo)
person from Zaire

Examples

She spoke passionately about her experiences as a Zairean during the 1980s.
A group of Zaireans gathered to celebrate their national holiday.
Though he now lives abroad, he still proudly identifies as a Zairean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Congolese - a native or inhabitant of the Congo region

Etymology

From Zaire, the former name (1971–1997) of the Democratic Republic of the Congo, plus the suffix -an.

2. ADJ

/ zaɪˈɪəriən / (AmE), / zaɪˈɪəriən / (BrE)
Relating to Zaire (now the Democratic Republic of the Congo) or its people.
of or relating to Zaire or its people

Examples

The Zairean government faced international scrutiny during the 1990s.
Many Zairean artists gained recognition for their music and paintings.
She researched the role of women in Zairean society.

Synset Examples

Zairean elections

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Congolese - relating to the Republic of the Congo or the Democratic Republic of the Congo or their people

Etymology

From Zaire, the former name of the Democratic Republic of the Congo (1971–1997), plus English suffix -an.
