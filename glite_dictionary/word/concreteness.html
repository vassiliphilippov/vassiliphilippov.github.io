concreteness

1. NOUN

/ kɑnˈkrit.nəs / (AmE), / kɒŋˈkriːt.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being specific, tangible, or based on real, perceptible things rather than ideas or abstractions
quality of being concrete, not abstract

Examples

The concreteness of his explanation made it easy for everyone to understand.
Writers should aim for concreteness in their descriptions to engage readers' senses.
Children generally prefer stories with greater concreteness rather than abstract ideas.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abstraction - the quality of being abstract rather than concrete
  • Synonym: specificity - the quality of being specific or clearly defined

Etymology

From concrete + -ness, modelled on other abstract nouns.
