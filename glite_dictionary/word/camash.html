camash

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmæʃ / (AmE), / kəˈmæʃ / (BrE)
any of several North and South American perennial plants of the genus Camassia, known for their star-shaped blue or white flowers and starchy edible bulbs.
North/South American plant of genus Camassia

Examples

The fields were covered with blooming camash in late spring.
Native Americans traditionally harvested camash bulbs as a food source.
We identified several camash plants along the riverbank during our hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camas - alternative name for camash, plant of the genus Camassia

Etymology

From Nez Percé *qém’es* (the plant), adopted into scientific Latin as Camassia.
