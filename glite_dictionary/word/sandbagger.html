sandbagger

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændˌbæɡər / (AmE), / ˈsandˌbaɡə / (BrE)
a person who conceals their true abilities or intentions in order to deceive others, especially to gain an unfair advantage
person who deceives to gain advantage

Examples

They realized too late that the friendly newcomer at poker was a sandbagger who ended up winning all the money.
The chess tournament was ruined by a notorious sandbagger pretending to be a beginner.
She warned us about sandbaggers who enter competitions with fake credentials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hustler - person who deceives or tricks others to win or gain advantage
  • Synonym: bluffer - person who tries to deceive by pretending
  • Antonym: honest - person behaving in a truthful and fair way

Etymology

From 'sandbag' (to hit with a sandbag or to deliberately underperform) + '-er'.
