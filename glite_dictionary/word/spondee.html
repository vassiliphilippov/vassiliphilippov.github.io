spondee

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɑnˌdi / (AmE), / ˈspɒn.diː / (BrE)
A metrical foot in poetry consisting of two syllables, both of which are stressed.
metrical foot with two stressed syllables

Examples

The poet used a spondee to create a sense of heaviness in the line.
In classical poetry, a spondee often replaces a dactyl at the end of a verse.
Analyzing the poem's meter, the critic identified several spondees in the second stanza.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: iamb - metrical foot: unstressed followed by stressed syllable
  • Antonym: trochee - metrical foot: stressed followed by unstressed syllable

Etymology

From Latin spondeus, from Greek spondeios (of a libation).
