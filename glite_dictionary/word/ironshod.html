ironshod

1. ADJ

/ ˈaɪərnˌʃɑd / (AmE), / ˈaɪənˌʃɒd / (BrE)
fitted with a covering or case of iron, especially for added strength or durability.
fitted with iron coverings

Examples

The blacksmith made new ironshod shoes for the horse.
The heavy wagon rattled along the road on its ironshod wheels.
Her boots were ironshod to withstand the rough mountain paths.

Synset Examples

ironshod hooves
ironshod wheels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steel-shod - fitted with coverings or cases of steel
  • Antonym: unshod - not fitted with shoes, especially of animals or wheels

Etymology

Formed from 'iron' + 'shod', meaning fitted with iron shoes or coverings.
