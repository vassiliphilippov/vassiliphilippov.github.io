thievishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈθiːvɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈθiːvɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having a tendency or disposition to steal; the characteristic of being thievish.
the tendency to steal

Examples

His thievishness eventually led to his dismissal from the store.
She suspected her colleague's thievishness after several items went missing.
The child's thievishness concerned his parents deeply.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dishonesty - lack of honesty; tendency to lie, cheat, or steal
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest and not stealing or cheating

Etymology

From 'thievish' + '-ness'; 'thievish' from Old English 'þēof' (thief).
