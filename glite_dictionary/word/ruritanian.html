Ruritanian

1. NOUN

/ ˌruːrɪˈteɪniən / (AmE), / ˌrʊərɪˈteɪnɪən / (BrE)
An imaginary inhabitant of Ruritania, a fictional Eastern European country often used as a setting in literature.
imaginary inhabitant of Ruritania

Examples

Many adventure novels feature a Ruritanian as a central character involved in court intrigue.
He dressed as a Ruritanian nobleman for the costume party, complete with medals and sash.
The playwright set his new comedy in a kingdom ruled by eccentric Ruritanians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ruritania - a fictional country in Eastern Europe, especially in literature

Etymology

From Ruritania, the fictional country in Anthony Hope's 1894 novel 'The Prisoner of Zenda'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌruːrɪˈteɪniən / (AmE), / ˌrʊərɪˈteɪnɪən / (BrE)
Of or relating to Ruritania, a fictional country, or to any imaginary or idealized country; often used to describe romance or intrigue typical of such fictional settings.
relating to a fictional or imaginary country

Examples

The novel is filled with Ruritanian intrigue and swashbuckling adventure.
Critics praised the film for its lavish, Ruritanian setting, complete with castles and royal courts.
His costume had a certain Ruritanian flair, reminiscent of storybook kingdoms.

Etymology

From Ruritania, the name of a fictional country in Anthony Hope's novel 'The Prisoner of Zenda' (1894), plus the English suffix -an.
