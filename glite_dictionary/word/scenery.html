scenery

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːnəri / (AmE), / ˈsiːnəri / (BrE)
the natural features of a landscape considered as a whole, especially when they are beautiful
natural features of a landscape

Examples

The mountain scenery was breathtaking during our drive.
Visitors come here to enjoy the beautiful coastal scenery.
He stopped to take a photo of the rural scenery stretching before him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landscape - the visible features of an area of land

Etymology

From Italian *scenaria* (stage properties), from Latin *scaena* (scene) via French *scène*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiːnəri / (AmE), / ˈsiːnəri / (BrE)
the painted backgrounds and structures used on a stage to represent a particular setting or location in a play or performance
painted stage backgrounds and structures

Examples

The scenery for the play was so realistic that the audience felt transported to another world.
After every act, the stage crew changed the scenery to reflect the new setting.
They designed the scenery to evoke a bustling city street in the 1920s.

Synset Examples

they worked all night painting the scenery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: set - the scenery and properties arranged for a performance of a play or film
  • Antonym: props - objects used on stage in a play or film, excluding scenery and costumes

Etymology

From Italian *scenaria* (stage effects), from Latin *scaenaria* (stage properties), from *scaena* (scene).

Synset POS: n
