Paralichthys

1. NOUN

/ pærəˈlɪkθɪs / (AmE), / parəˈlɪkθɪs / (BrE)
A genus of large-tooth flounders in the family Bothidae, found in coastal waters of the Americas.
genus of large-tooth flounders

Examples

The biologist identified the specimen as belonging to the genus Paralichthys.
Several species within Paralichthys are important for commercial fisheries.
Young marine biologists studied the life cycle of Paralichthys along the Atlantic coast.

Etymology

From Greek 'para' (beside) + 'ichthys' (fish), used in scientific taxonomy.
