recourse

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈkɔrs / (AmE), / rɪˈkɔːs / (BrE)
A source of help or protection that is turned to when in trouble or difficulty.
source of help in difficulty

Examples

If negotiations fail, our last recourse may be legal action.
She had no recourse but to ask her friends for help when she lost her job.
The villagers' only recourse during the flood was the community center on higher ground.

Synset Examples

his only recourse was the police
took refuge in lying

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resort - a course of action or source of help turned to as a last option
  • Synonym: refuge - something providing safety or shelter

Etymology

From Old French 'recours', from Latin 'recursus' (a running back, return), from 'recurrere' (to run back).

2. NOUN

/ ˈriːˌkɔrs / (AmE), / ˈriːˌkɔːs / (BrE)
the use of someone or something as a source of help or protection when in difficulty
the act of seeking help or protection

Examples

If negotiations fail, you may have recourse to legal action.
He felt he had no recourse but to resign from his position.
In times of crisis, families often find recourse in their close friends.

Synset Examples

have recourse to the courts
an appeal to his uncle was his last resort

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resort - a person or thing turned to for help, especially as a final option
  • Synonym: appeal - an earnest request for help or aid

Etymology

From Old French recours, from Latin recursus (a running back, return), from recurrere (to run back, recur).
