canyon

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.jən / (AmE), / ˈkæn.jən / (BrE)
A deep, narrow valley with steep sides, often formed by a river in an area with little rainfall.
deep, steep-sided valley

Examples

The Colorado River carved the Grand canyon over millions of years.
We hiked along the edge of a stunning canyon at sunrise.
A narrow river flows quietly at the bottom of the deep canyon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gorge - a deep, narrow passage with steep rocky sides
  • Degree Lower: valley - an elongated low area often with a river

Etymology

From Spanish 'cañón' (tube, cannon), originally from Latin 'canna' (reed, cane).
