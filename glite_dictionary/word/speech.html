speech

1. NOUN

/ spiːtʃ / (AmE), / spiːtʃ / (BrE)
the faculty or power of expressing thoughts and feelings by articulate sounds; vocal communication
faculty of vocal communication

Examples

The development of speech distinguishes humans from other animals.
Her ability to produce speech was impaired after the accident.
Language and speech are central to human society.

Synset Examples

language sets homo sapiens apart from all other animals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: language - the system of spoken or written communication used by a particular country or people

Etymology

From Old English 'spǣc' or 'sprǣc' (speech, discourse), related to 'sprecan' (to speak).
