curbside

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɜːrb.saɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɜːb.saɪd / (BrE)
The side or edge of a sidewalk or street that is next to the curb.
side or edge next to a curb

Examples

The delivery driver left the package on the curbside for easy pickup.
Trash is collected from the curbside every Tuesday morning in this neighborhood.
We waited by the curbside for our taxi to arrive.

Synset Examples

policemen stood at intervals along the curbside

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curb - a raised edge or border of a street, typically of stone or concrete

Etymology

Formed from 'curb' (from Old French courbe, Latin curvus, curved) + 'side'.
