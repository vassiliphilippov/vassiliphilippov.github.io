logicism

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːdʒɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
(philosophy) the theory that all of mathematics can be derived from formal logic.
theory that mathematics derives from logic

Examples

The program of logicism sought to show that mathematics is reducible to pure logic.
Many philosophers debated the consequences of logicism during the 20th century.
Russell and Whitehead's Principia Mathematica is a classic work in the tradition of logicism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: formalism - the theory that mathematics is just the manipulation of symbols according to rules
  • Antonym: intuitionism - the theory that mathematics is a creation of the human mind

Etymology

From logic + -ism, coined in early 20th century to describe the philosophical position of Frege, Russell, and others.
