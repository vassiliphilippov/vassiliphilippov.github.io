chipping

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of breaking off small pieces from something; or removing small fragments by chipping
the act of chipping

Examples

There was a lot of chipping during the renovation, leaving debris everywhere.
Repeated chipping of the paint weakened the surface of the old door.
The sculptor spent hours on careful chipping to reveal the figure hidden in the stone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chip - to break off a small piece from something

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'chip' + the suffix '-ing'.
