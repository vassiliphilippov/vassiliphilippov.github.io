Kingston upon Hull

1. PROPN

/ ˌkɪŋstən əˈpɒn hʌl / (AmE), / ˌkɪŋstən əˌpɒn ˈhʌl / (BrE)
a city and large seaport in northeastern England, situated on the River Hull at its junction with the Humber Estuary; commonly known as Hull
city and port in northeastern England
british

Examples

Hull's fishing industry helped Kingston upon Hull grow rapidly during the 19th century.
The University of Kingston upon Hull attracts students from around the world.
She was born and raised in Kingston upon Hull, in northern England.

Etymology

From the full historic name of the city, referring to its location on the River Hull; granted the name Kingston by Edward I.
