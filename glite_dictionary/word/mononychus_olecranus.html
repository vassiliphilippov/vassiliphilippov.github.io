Mononychus olecranus

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈnɒnɪkəs oʊˈlɛkrənəs / (AmE), / məˈnɒnɪkəs əʊˈliːkrənəs / (BrE)
an extinct turkey-sized, long-legged animal from the Late Cretaceous of the Gobi Desert, known for its single-clawed short forelimbs, bird-like fused wrist bones, keeled breastbone, and long tail; its exact classification as a bird or a dinosaur is debated
extinct bird-like fossil from the Cretaceous

Examples

Scientists discovered a Mononychus olecranus fossil in the Gobi Desert in 1993.
The unusual anatomy of Mononychus olecranus has puzzled paleontologists regarding its place in evolutionary history.
A mounted skeleton of Mononychus olecranus is now displayed at the national natural history museum.

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) + 'onyx' (claw) and 'olecranon' (elbow).
