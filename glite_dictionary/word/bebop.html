bebop

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiˌbɑp / (AmE), / ˈbiːbɒp / (BrE)
a style of modern jazz developed in the early 1940s, characterized by fast tempos, complex harmonies, and improvisation
a style of modern jazz music

Examples

She loves listening to old bebop records from the 1940s.
The musicians played a lively set of bebop at the jazz club last night.
Charlie Parker was a pioneer of bebop and revolutionized modern jazz.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jazz - a genre of music characterized by improvisation and swing

Etymology

Imitative of a repeated melodic phrase found in jazz; the term is thought to echo the sound of certain musical figures.
