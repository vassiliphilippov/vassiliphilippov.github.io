mumble

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈmʌmbəl / (BrE)
a soft, indistinct utterance that is difficult to understand
soft, indistinct utterance

Examples

He responded with a barely audible mumble that left everyone confused.
Her nervous mumble made it hard to catch what she was saying.
The teacher asked him to repeat his answer because his mumble was unclear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murmur - a low, continuous, indistinct sound; synonym for quiet utterance
  • Synonym: mutter - to speak or say something in a low or barely audible voice; also a synonym for indistinct speech

Etymology

From Middle English *mombeln* (to murmur, mutter), probably of imitative origin.

2. VERB

/ ˈmʌm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈmʌm.bəl / (BrE)
to speak in a low, unclear voice so that words are difficult to understand
speak unclearly in a low voice

Examples

He tends to mumble when he's nervous, making it hard to understand him.
Please don't mumble your answers; speak clearly so everyone can hear.
She was so tired that she could only mumble a weak reply.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutter - to speak in a low or barely audible manner, often in dissatisfaction
  • Antonym: to enunciate - to pronounce words clearly and distinctly

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, reflecting the sound of indistinct speech.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01046405-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈmʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈmʌmbəl / (BrE)
to chew food without teeth, moving the jaws and gums with difficulty
chew food without teeth

Examples

The elderly woman could only mumble her bread after losing her last tooth.
He watched the baby mumble a cracker, gumming it slowly as he tried to eat.
Without his dentures, Grandpa had to mumble his dinner carefully.

Synset Examples

the old man had no teeth left and mumbled his food
