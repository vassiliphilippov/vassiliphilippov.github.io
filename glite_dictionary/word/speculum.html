speculum

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛkjələm / (AmE), / ˈspɛkjʊləm / (BrE)
a medical instrument used to dilate a bodily passage or cavity to permit examination of the interior
medical instrument for examination of body cavities
medical

Examples

The doctor inserted a speculum to perform the pelvic examination.
A nasal speculum was used to widen his nostrils for inspection.
Some patients find the use of a speculum uncomfortable during routine check-ups.

Etymology

From Latin 'speculum' (mirror), from 'specere' (to look at).

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɛkjələm / (AmE), / ˈspɛkjʊləm / (BrE)
a mirror, typically made of polished metal, used in optical instruments such as telescopes to reflect light
mirror in optical instruments

Examples

The antique telescope contained a speculum made of highly polished metal.
Astronomers once crafted the speculum by hand to ensure maximum reflectivity.
Unlike glass mirrors, a metal speculum was prone to tarnishing over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mirror - a reflective surface, usually glass coated with a metal amalgam, that reflects a clear image

Etymology

From Latin 'speculum' (mirror), from 'specere' (to look at).
