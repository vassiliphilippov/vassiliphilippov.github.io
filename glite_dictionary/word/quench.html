to quench

1. VERB

/ kwɛntʃ / (AmE), / kwɛntʃ / (BrE)
to satisfy one's thirst by drinking
satisfy thirst by drinking

Examples

She drank a glass of lemonade to quench her thirst after the long run.
Nothing can quench your thirst like a cold bottle of water on a hot day.
The hikers quickly found a stream to quench their thirst.

Synset Examples

The cold water quenched his thirst

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to slake - to satisfy thirst or a desire

Etymology

From Old English 'cwencan' (to extinguish, to quench).
