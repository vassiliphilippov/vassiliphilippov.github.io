fustian

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌstiən / (AmE), / ˈfʌstɪən / (BrE)
a strong fabric made of cotton and flax, usually with a slight nap, used especially in the past for clothing and upholstery
strong cotton and linen fabric

Examples

The merchant displayed bolts of fustian and velvet to the tailor.
During the Middle Ages, fustian became popular for making everyday clothing.
He wore a coat made of fustian, sturdy enough for work in the fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corduroy - a durable fabric, usually cotton, with a distinctive ribbed surface

Etymology

From Old French *fustaigne*, from Medieval Latin *fustaneum*, possibly from Latin *fustis* (wooden stick), relating to the wooden looms used in its manufacture.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfʌstʃən / (AmE), / ˈfʌstɪən / (BrE)
language or writing that is pompous, pretentious, or inflated in style or expression
pompous or pretentious language

Examples

The candidate's speech was full of fustian and empty promises.
Literary critics dismissed the novel's dialogue as mere fustian.
He criticized the professor's lectures for their fustian rather than clear insight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombast - pretentious or inflated language
  • Antonym: plainness - the quality of being simple or clear, without adornment

Etymology

Originally from Old French *fustaigne*, later used figuratively in English for inflated speech.
