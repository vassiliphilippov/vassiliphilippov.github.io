liqueur

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈkɜr / (AmE), / lɪˈkjʊə / (BrE)
a strong, sweet, highly flavored alcoholic drink, typically served after a meal
sweet, flavored alcoholic drink

Examples

He finished his meal with a glass of liqueur and a small dessert.
The restaurant offers a variety of fruit-flavored liqueurs on their after-dinner menu.
A splash of orange liqueur can enhance the flavor of this cake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cordial - a sweet alcoholic drink, often used as a synonym for liqueur

Etymology

From French *liqueur* (liquid, liquor), from Latin *liquor* (liquid).
