Emilia-Romagna

1. PROPN

/ ɛˌmiliə roʊˈmɑnjə / (AmE), / ɛˌmɪliə rəʊˈmɑːnjə / (BrE)
a region in north-central Italy, bordering the Adriatic Sea, known for its rich history, culture, and cuisine
region of north-central Italy

Examples

We traveled through Emilia-Romagna to experience some of Italy's best food and wine.
Parma, located in Emilia-Romagna, is famous for its cheese and ham.
The rolling hills of Emilia-Romagna are dotted with medieval towns and castles.

Etymology

From the names of the historic regions Emilia (from Latin Aemilia) and Romagna (from Latin Romania).
