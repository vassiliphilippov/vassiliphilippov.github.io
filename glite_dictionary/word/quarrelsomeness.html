quarrelsomeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔrəlˌsəmnəs / (AmE), / ˈkwɒrəlˌsəmnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being inclined to argue or quarrel frequently
tendency to argue or quarrel

Examples

Her constant quarrelsomeness made her difficult to get along with at work.
The quarrelsomeness between the siblings worried their parents.
His quarrelsomeness often ruined what could have been peaceful discussions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarrelsome - inclined to argue or quarrel
  • Antonym: amicability - the quality of being friendly and pleasant

Etymology

From 'quarrelsome' (prone to quarrel) + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting state or quality).
