Clethraceae

1. NOUN

/ klɛˈθreɪsi.i / (AmE), / klɛθˈræsiːiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants consisting solely of the genus Clethra.
a family of flowering plants

Examples

The Clethraceae are native mainly to temperate and tropical regions.
Botanists classify sweet pepperbush in the family Clethraceae.
There is ongoing research on the evolutionary relationships within the Clethraceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Clethra - a genus of flowering plants in the family Clethraceae

Etymology

From the botanical name of the genus Clethra plus the standard family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ klɛθˈreɪsiˌi / (AmE), / klɛθˈreɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants whose only genus is Clethra
family of flowering plants (Clethra)

Examples

The Clethraceae are known for their fragrant flowers and are commonly found in subtropical regions.
Some botanists debate the classification of plants within the Clethraceae family.
Several species in the Clethraceae thrive in acidic soil conditions.

Etymology

From New Latin, named after the type genus Clethra.
