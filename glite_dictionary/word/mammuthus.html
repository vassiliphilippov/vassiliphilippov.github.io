Mammuthus

1. PROPN

/ məˈmjuːθəs / (AmE), / məˈmjuːθəs / (BrE)
a genus of extinct large, hairy, elephant-like mammals known as mammoths, which lived from the Pliocene to the Holocene epochs
extinct genus of mammoths

Examples

Several species of Mammuthus have been discovered in the fossil record across Eurasia and North America.
The best-known member of the genus Mammuthus is the woolly mammoth, which lived during the Ice Age.
Scientists have studied Mammuthus bones to learn more about the extinction of large mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mammoth - large, extinct, hairy, tusked animal related to the modern elephant

Etymology

From Latin genus name Mammuthus, formed from mammut (“mammoth” in German) + Latin suffix -us.

2. NOUN

/ məˈmjuːθəs / (AmE), / məˈmjuːθəs / (BrE)
a genus of extinct large, hairy elephants known as mammoths, which lived during the Pleistocene epoch
genus of extinct mammoths

Examples

The extinct genus Mammuthus includes several species of mammoths that once roamed the Northern Hemisphere.
Many fossils of Mammuthus have been found in the Siberian permafrost.
Scientists are studying DNA extracted from Mammuthus remains to learn more about their extinction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mammoth - large extinct elephant of the genus Mammuthus

Etymology

From Latin scientific name, from Russian 'mamont' (mammoth).
