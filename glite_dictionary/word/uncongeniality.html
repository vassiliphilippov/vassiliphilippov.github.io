uncongeniality

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnkənˌdʒiːniˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌʌnkənˌdʒiːniˈælɪti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being not congenial; lack of friendliness, compatibility, or pleasantness
lack of congeniality or friendliness

Examples

The uncongeniality of the work environment made collaboration difficult for everyone involved.
She was troubled by the uncongeniality of her new neighbors, who rarely spoke to her.
Despite the uncongeniality between the team members, they managed to finish the project on time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: congeniality - the quality of being friendly and pleasant

Etymology

From 'uncongenial' + '-ity', patterned after 'congeniality' (from Latin 'congenialis').

2. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.kənˌdʒiː.niˈæ.lə.ti / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kənˌdʒiː.niˈæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality of being unpleasant, disagreeable, or unsuitable; lack of friendliness or harmony.
the state of being unpleasant or disagreeable

Examples

His quick departure reflected the uncongeniality of the atmosphere at the meeting.
Many employees left the firm due to the uncongeniality of the new management.
Despite the uncongeniality of the climate, the explorers pressed on with their research.

Synset Examples

Notwithstanding the uncongeniality of his surroundings, he had found opportunities for study, and never had his treasured volumes seemed more precious to him than during those long winter months, when despair haunted him like a shadow from which there seemed no means of escape.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: congeniality - the quality of being pleasant or agreeable

Etymology

From un- (not) + congenial + -ity, modeled after 'congeniality.'
