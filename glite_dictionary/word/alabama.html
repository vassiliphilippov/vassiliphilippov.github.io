Alabama

1. PROPN

/ ˌæləˈbæmə / (AmE), / ˌæləˈbɑːmə / (BrE)
A state in the southeastern United States, bordered by Tennessee, Georgia, Florida, Mississippi, and the Gulf of Mexico.
U.S. state in the Southeast
american

Examples

Many civil rights protests took place in Alabama during the 1960s.
She grew up in a small town in Alabama near the Gulf Coast.
The football team from Alabama won the national championship last year.

Etymology

From the Alabama people, a Native American tribe; their name likely derives from a Choctaw word meaning 'vegetation gatherers'.

/ ˌæl.əˈbæ.mə / (AmE), / ˌæl.əˈbæ.mə / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people formerly living in what is now the state of Alabama, historically part of the Creek Confederacy.
member of a Native American people of Alabama

Examples

Many Alabama now reside in Oklahoma after forced relocation in the 19th century.
The traditions of the Alabama have influenced southeastern Native American culture.
He is descended from a long line of Alabama who were skilled artisans.

the Alabamas were members of the Creek Confederacy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Creek - a Native American group of the southeastern United States

Etymology

From the name of the Alabama people, possibly from Choctaw *albah amo* (vegetation gatherers).
