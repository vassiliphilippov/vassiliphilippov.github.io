Listera

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪstərə / (AmE), / ˈlɪstərə / (BrE)
A genus of terrestrial orchids, usually with a single pair of broad, shiny leaves near the stem's middle, found in temperate regions of Asia, North America, and Europe.
genus of terrestrial orchids

Examples

The Listera orchid can be found growing wild in the forests of Europe.
Botanists classified the rare specimen under the genus Listera.
Many Listera species have two distinctive leaves halfway up the stem.

Etymology

Named after Martin Lister (1639–1712), English naturalist.

2. NOUN

/ lɪˈstɪrə / (AmE), / lɪˈstɪərə / (BrE)
A genus of terrestrial orchids with a single pair of broad shining leaves near the middle of the stem, native to temperate regions of Asia, North America, and Europe.
genus of terrestrial orchids

Examples

The rare Listera orchid is found in the woodlands of northern Europe.
Botanists discovered a new species within the Listera genus in Asia.
Some Listera plants are notable for their distinctive twin leaves.

Etymology

Named after Martin Lister (1639–1712), an English naturalist.
