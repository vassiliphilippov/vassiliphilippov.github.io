to gelatinize

1. VERB

/ dʒəˈlætəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / dʒɪˈlætɪnaɪz / (BrE)
to become gelatinous or change into a jelly-like substance, or to cause something to do so
become or make gelatinous

Examples

When starch is heated with water, it begins to gelatinize and thickens the mixture.
Certain plant-based thickeners will gelatinize at lower temperatures than animal-based ones.
If you cook the sauce too long, the cornstarch may gelatinize excessively and turn it into a paste.

Synset Examples

the starch gelatinized when it was heated

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to solidify - become solid or make something solid

Etymology

From gelatin + -ize, meaning to make into gelatin or resembling gelatin.

2. VERB

/ dʒəˈlætəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / dʒɪˈlatɪnaɪz / (BrE)
to coat, treat, or impregnate with gelatin; to convert into or become like gelatin, especially in cooking or science
to coat or treat with gelatin

Examples

To create a shiny finish, you can gelatinize the fruit tart before serving.
The chef chose to gelatinize the sauce to improve its texture and presentation.
After mixing, the solution will begin to gelatinize as it cools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gel - to become or make something semi-solid, like jelly

Etymology

From gelatin + -ize.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00566558-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dʒəˈlæt̬.əˌnaɪz / (AmE), / dʒɪˈlæt.ɪ.naɪz / (BrE)
to convert or become converted into a gelatinous or jelly-like form
become or make gelatinous

Examples

When heated in water, cornstarch will gelatinize and thicken the mixture.
Most puddings require the starch to gelatinize for the correct consistency.
Certain cooking techniques are designed to gelatinize the natural starches in food.

Synset Examples

hot water will gelatinize starch

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to solidify - to make or become solid or hard

Etymology

From gelatin + -ize, modeled after French 'gélatiniser'.
