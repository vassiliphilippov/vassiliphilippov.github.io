Malvaceae

1. NOUN

/ mælˈveɪsi.i / (AmE), / mælˈveɪsiː.iː / (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants including mallows, cotton, and okra, consisting of herbs, shrubs, and some trees
the mallow plant family

Examples

The Malvaceae family includes economically important crops such as cotton and okra.
Many garden flowers, like hollyhocks, belong to the Malvaceae.
Botanists have classified the hibiscus plant under the Malvaceae family.

Etymology

From Latin 'Malva' (mallow) + '-aceae' (family suffix in botanical Latin).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmælˈveɪsi.i / (AmE), / ˌmælˈveɪsiː.iː / (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants including mallows, cotton, and okra, consisting mostly of herbs, shrubs, and some trees
family of flowering plants (mallows, cotton, okra)

Examples

The Malvaceae family includes economically important crops such as cotton and okra.
Several species in the Malvaceae family are known for their showy, colorful flowers.
Researchers are studying drought resistance in plants belonging to the Malvaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mallow - a plant of the genus Malva, a typical member of Malvaceae

Etymology

From Latin *malva* (mallow), the type genus of the family.
