doomed

1. ADJ

/ duːmd / (AmE), / duːmd / (BrE)
Condemned to certain destruction or to eternal punishment, especially in Hell.
condemned to destruction or eternal punishment

Examples

Many believed the traitor was doomed to suffer in the afterlife.
In the story, lost souls wander the earth, forever doomed for their sins.
He prayed for forgiveness, fearing he was doomed beyond hope.

Synset Examples

poor damned souls

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damned - condemned to eternal punishment in Hell
  • Antonym: saved - delivered from sin or its consequences; not condemned

Etymology

From Middle English 'domed', past participle of 'doom' (to judge, condemn).

2. ADJ

/ duːmd / (AmE), / duːmd / (BrE)
certain to fail or suffer a terrible fate; marked by inevitable bad fortune.
certain to fail or meet disaster

Examples

Everyone thought the project was doomed from the very beginning.
Their relationship seemed doomed by constant arguments and misunderstandings.
Despite their courage, the expedition was doomed in the face of the harsh winter.

Synset Examples

their business venture was doomed from the start
an ill-fated business venture
an ill-starred romance
the unlucky prisoner was again put in irons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ill-fated - marked by bad fortune; destined to fail
  • Antonym: fortunate - having good luck or success

Etymology

Past participle of 'doom', from Old English 'dōm' (judgment, decree).

3. NOUN

/ ˈduːmd / (AmE), / ˈduːmd / (BrE)
people who are destined or fated to die soon, often as a result of unavoidable circumstances.
those destined to die soon

Examples

The doomed waited in silence for their fate.
In the final act, the playwright focuses on the doomed and their sense of resignation.
He looked into the eyes of the doomed and saw only despair.

Synset Examples
