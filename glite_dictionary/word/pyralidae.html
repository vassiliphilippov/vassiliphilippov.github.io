Pyralidae

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈrælɪˌdiː / (AmE), / pɪˈralɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A large family of moths in the order Lepidoptera, which includes bee moths, corn borers, and flour moths.
family of moths including bee moths and corn borers

Examples

Several species within the Pyralidae family are known as agricultural pests.
The Pyralidae are commonly referred to as snout moths due to their protruding mouthparts.
Entomologists studied the behavior of Pyralidae larvae in storage facilities.

Etymology

From New Latin Pyralidae, based on the genus Pyralis.
