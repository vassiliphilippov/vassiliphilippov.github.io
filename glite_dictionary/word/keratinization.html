keratinization

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛrətɪnəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɛrətɪnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which keratin is deposited in cells, causing them to become hard and horny, as in the formation of nails, hair, or the outer layer of skin
process of cells becoming keratinized
medical

Examples

The process of keratinization is essential for the formation of healthy nails and hair.
Abnormal keratinization can lead to conditions such as psoriasis or calluses.
During skin healing, rapid keratinization helps restore the protective barrier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keratin - a tough, fibrous protein found in skin, hair, and nails

Etymology

From keratin + -ization, referring to the process of becoming keratinized.
