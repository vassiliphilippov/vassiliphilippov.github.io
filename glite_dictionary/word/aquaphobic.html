aquaphobic

1. ADJ

/ ˌækwəˈfoʊbɪk / (AmE), / ˌakwəˈfəʊbɪk / (BrE)
abnormally afraid of water; having an intense or irrational fear of water
abnormally afraid of water
medical

Examples

After nearly drowning as a child, Mark became aquaphobic and avoided swimming pools for years.
Many aquaphobic individuals experience anxiety even when near small bodies of water.
Her aquaphobic tendencies made it difficult for her to enjoy family trips to the beach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrophobic - repelled by water or unable to mix with water; also, fear of water (in medical context)
  • Antonym: aquaphilia - an attraction to, or fondness for, water

Etymology

From Latin 'aqua' (water) and Greek '-phobos' (fear).
