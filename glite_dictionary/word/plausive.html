plausive

1. ADJ

/ ˈplɔːzɪv / (AmE), / ˈplɔːzɪv / (BrE)
expressing or showing praise or approval, especially in a manner that commends or applauds
expressing praise or approval
archaic

Examples

The queen gave the artist a plausive remark during the ceremony.
Her plausive words encouraged him to continue his research.
A plausive smile from the teacher made the student proud.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approbative - expressing approval or praise
  • Synonym: affirmative - expressing agreement or assent
  • Antonym: disapproving - expressing a negative or critical opinion

Etymology

From Latin plausivus (deserving applause), from plaudere (to applaud).
