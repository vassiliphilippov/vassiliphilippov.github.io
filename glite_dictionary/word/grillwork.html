grillwork

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɪlˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪlˌwɜːk / (BrE)
an ornamental or functional openwork structure made of crossed bars, wires, or strips, used as a screen, grate, or decorative feature
decorative or functional mesh of crossed bars or wires

Examples

The antique radiator was covered by elaborate grillwork that showcased intricate patterns.
Black iron grillwork protected the windows while adding an elegant touch to the façade.
He admired the car's front grillwork, which gleamed in the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grille - an openwork screen of metal bars

Etymology

Formed from 'grill' + 'work', indicating something made in the style of a grille.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɪlˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪlˌwɜːk / (BrE)
a framework of metal bars arranged as a partition, grate, or decorative feature, often used in architecture, furniture, or for protection.
metal framework of bars; grate or partition

Examples

The ornate grillwork on the old bank's windows was crafted by a local blacksmith.
He admired the intricate grillwork on the garden gate as he passed by.
Wrought iron grillwork added an elegant touch to the facade of the building.

Synset Examples

he cooked hamburgers on the grill

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grille - a metal or wooden framework of bars, often decorative, used as a screen or barrier

Etymology

From 'grill' (a grating or lattice) + 'work'; patterned after similar compounds like 'ironwork'.
