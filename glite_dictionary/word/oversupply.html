oversupply

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərsəˈplaɪ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvə səˈplaɪ / (BrE)
an excessive amount of something that is available, especially causing prices to fall
an excessive amount of supply

Examples

The oversupply of oil has caused global prices to drop significantly.
Last year's oversupply led to warehouses being filled with unsold goods.
A sudden oversupply in the housing market can depress property values.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surplus - an amount that is more than needed
  • Antonym: shortage - a lack of something that is needed
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərsəˈplaɪ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəsəˈplaɪ / (BrE)
to provide or supply with more than is needed or can be used
provide too much of something

Examples

The company tends to oversupply its stores, leading to wasted products.
If farmers oversupply the market with apples, prices can drop dramatically.
An influx of cheap goods could oversupply local retailers and hurt small businesses.

Synset Examples

flood the market with tennis shoes
Glut the country with cheap imports from the Orient

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glut - to supply or fill to excess
  • Synonym: flood - to provide with a very large amount, more than needed

Etymology

From 'over-' (excessive) + 'supply'; modelled after similar verb formations in English.
