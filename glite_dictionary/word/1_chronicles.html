1 Chronicles

1. PROPN

/ fɜrst ˌkrɑːnɪklz / (AmE), / fɜːst ˈkrɒnɪklz / (BrE)
the first of two Old Testament books recounting the history of Judah and Israel up to the return from the Babylonian Captivity in 536 BC
first Old Testament history book

Examples

The genealogies in 1 Chronicles offer insight into ancient Israelite history.
He cited a passage from 1 Chronicles during his sermon.
Many biblical scholars analyze the structure of 1 Chronicles to understand Old Testament narratives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: 2 Chronicles - the second Old Testament book continuing the history of Judah and Israel

Etymology

From the Greek 'paraleipomenon' (things left out) via Latin 'Chronica', meaning annals or chronicles.
