defilade

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛfɪˈleɪd / (AmE), / ˌdɛfɪˈleɪd / (BrE)
the arrangement or positioning of defenses or fortifications to shield troops or positions from enemy fire
protection of troops by fortification arrangement

Examples

The soldiers took cover in a defilade to avoid incoming artillery fire.
Proper use of defilade can greatly reduce a unit’s casualties during battle.
The engineers designed the trench with defilade in mind, shielding it from direct enemy view.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: enfilade - a position or formation allowing gunfire to sweep along the length of a line

Etymology

From French 'défilade', from 'défiler' (to screen from view).
