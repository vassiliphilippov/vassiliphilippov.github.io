ploughwoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈplaʊˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈplaʊˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A woman who ploughs land, especially with a plough and draft animals or machinery.
woman who ploughs land

Examples

The ploughwoman guided the horses skillfully across the muddy field.
As a ploughwoman, she became well-known for her hard work and resilience.
In the early morning, the ploughwoman set out before sunrise to prepare the land for planting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ploughman - a man who ploughs land

Etymology

Formed from 'plough' + 'woman', by analogy with 'ploughman'.
