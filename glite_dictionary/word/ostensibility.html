ostensibility

1. NOUN

/ əˌstɛnsəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɒˌstɛnsəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of appearing to be true, real, or professed, but not necessarily being so in fact.
apparent or professed quality

Examples

The ostensibility of his concern was questioned by everyone in the room.
Many critics have challenged the ostensibility of their environmental efforts.
We must distinguish between the ostensibility of fairness and its genuine practice.

Synset Examples

They reveal the ostensibility of this systematic order, or point out that it is in fact only temporary and exists on the basis of a more fundamental instability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostensible - seeming or stated to be true or real, but possibly not so
  • Antonym: genuineness - the quality of being truly what something is said to be; authenticity

Etymology

From 'ostensible' (apparent, professed) + '-ity', after French or possibly Late Latin roots.
