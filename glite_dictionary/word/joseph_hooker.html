Joseph Hooker

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒoʊzəf ˈhʊkər / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊzɪf ˈhʊkə / (BrE)
(1814–1879) United States general in the Union Army during the American Civil War, known for his defeat by Robert E. Lee at the Battle of Chancellorsville.
Union general defeated at Chancellorsville

Examples

During the Civil War, Joseph Hooker led the Union Army at the Battle of Chancellorsville.
Historians often analyze the strategy used by Joseph Hooker against Robert E. Lee.
Joseph Hooker's leadership was both praised and criticized by his contemporaries.
