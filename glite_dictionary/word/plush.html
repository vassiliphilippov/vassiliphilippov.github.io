plush

1. NOUN

/ plʌʃ / (AmE), / plʌʃ / (BrE)
a soft, rich fabric with a thick, long pile, softer and longer than velvet
soft, thick, luxurious fabric

Examples

The sofa was covered in a deep red plush that felt incredibly soft.
She wore a jacket lined with gray plush to keep her warm.
Toys made from plush are especially popular with young children.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: velvet - a soft fabric with a short, dense pile

Etymology

From French *peluche* (a plush fabric).

2. ADJ

/ plʌʃ / (AmE), / plʌʃ / (BrE)
Richly luxurious or extravagant in appearance, quality, or style.
richly luxurious or extravagant

Examples

The hotel lobby was so plush that guests felt instantly indulged.
They dined in a plush restaurant, surrounded by crystal chandeliers and velvet drapes.
Her plush office was decorated with expensive art and rich mahogany furniture.

Synset Examples

a lavish buffet
a lucullan feast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lavish - sumptuously rich or extravagant
  • Synonym: luxurious - extremely comfortable or elegant
  • Antonym: austere - severe or strict in manner; having no comforts or luxuries

Etymology

From French *peluche* (plush, shaggy cloth).
