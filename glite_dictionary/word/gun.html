3. NOUN

/ ɡʌn / (AmE), / ɡʌn / (BrE)
A large but transportable piece of artillery, such as a cannon or field gun, used as a weapon in warfare.
large, mobile artillery weapon

Examples

The soldiers positioned the gun on the hillside to cover the valley below.
During the battle, the artillery gun was moved forward by a team of horses.
The museum displays a historical field gun used in the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cannon - a large, heavy gun typically mounted on wheels, used in warfare
  • Degree Lower: rifle - a long-barreled firearm designed to be fired from the shoulder

Etymology

From Middle English 'gunne', possibly short for 'Gunilda', the name given to an early cannon; also related to Old Norse ‘gunnr’ (war).

7. NOUN

/ ɡʌn / (AmE), / ɡʌn / (BrE)
A weapon that fires projectiles at high speed, typically through a metal barrel, such as a rifle or pistol.
weapon that fires projectiles

Examples

The police found a loaded gun under the suspect's jacket.
She learned how to safely use a gun at the shooting range.
The antique gun on display dates back to the 18th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firearm - a portable gun, such as a rifle or pistol
  • Degree Higher: cannon - large, heavy gun typically mounted on wheels or fixed
  • Degree Lower: pistol - small, handheld firearm

Etymology

From Middle English 'gunne', feminine proper name for early cannon, later generalized. Possibly from Old Norse 'Gunnhildr' (female name), used as a name for early firearms.
