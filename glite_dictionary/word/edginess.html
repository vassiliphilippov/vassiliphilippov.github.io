edginess

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛdʒi.nɪs / (AmE), / ˈɛdʒɪnɪs / (BrE)
a quality or state of being nervous, tense, or easily irritated
state of being nervous or tense

Examples

He felt a growing edginess as the interview approached.
There was an unmistakable edginess in her voice during the argument.
You could sense his edginess while he waited for the test results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxiety - a feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease
  • Antonym: calmness - a mental state of being calm and peaceful

Etymology

From 'edgy' (irritable, tense) + '-ness'.
