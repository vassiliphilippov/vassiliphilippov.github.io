kamikaze

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːməˈkɑːzi / (AmE), / ˌkamɪˈkɑːzi / (BrE)
a Japanese aircraft loaded with explosives and making a deliberate suicidal crash on an enemy target, especially during World War II
WWII Japanese suicide attack plane

Examples

The kamikaze pilots launched their deadly attack on the naval fleet.
During World War II, the Allies feared kamikaze assaults on their ships.
A museum exhibit displays a restored kamikaze aircraft from 1945.

Etymology

From Japanese 'kamikaze', meaning 'divine wind', originally referring to legendary typhoons that protected Japan from Mongol invasion.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːməˈkɑːzi / (AmE), / ˌkamɪˈkɑːzi / (BrE)
a Japanese pilot trained and willing to carry out a suicide attack, especially by crashing their aircraft into an enemy target during World War II
Japanese suicide pilot in WWII

Examples

The kamikaze willingly gave his life as part of the attack on the fleet.
During World War II, many kamikaze pilots crashed their planes into Allied ships.
She read a book about a surviving family member of a kamikaze pilot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suicide bomber - a person who carries out a bombing attack with the intention of dying in the attempt

Etymology

From Japanese 神風 (kamikaze), meaning 'divine wind', originally referring to storms that protected Japan from Mongol invasions.
