Polygonatum

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːlɪˈɡoʊnətəm / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈɡəʊnətəm / (BrE)
a genus of perennial plants, commonly known as Solomon's seal, characterized by arching stems and clusters of tubular flowers, native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere
a genus of plants, Solomon's seal

Examples

The shady garden bed was filled with Polygonatum, its white flowers hanging gracefully from arching stems.
Botanists classified several species under the genus Polygonatum due to their distinctive rhizomes.
In traditional medicine, the roots of Polygonatum have been used for their supposed health benefits.

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'gonu' (knee), referring to the jointed rhizomes.

2. NOUN

/ pɑːˌlɪɡəˈneɪtəm / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪɡəˈneɪtəm / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbaceous plants in the lily family, commonly known as Solomon's seal, found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
a genus of plants; Solomon's seal

Examples

Many woodland gardens feature Polygonatum for its graceful arching stems and white flowers.
The rhizomes of Polygonatum have been used in traditional medicine in several cultures.
In spring, Polygonatum produces clusters of tubular flowers that hang from its leaf axils.

Etymology

From Greek 'polygonaton' meaning 'many knees', referring to the jointed rhizomes.
