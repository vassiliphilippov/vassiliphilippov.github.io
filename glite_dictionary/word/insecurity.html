insecurity

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.sɪˈkjʊr.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪn.sɪˈkjʊə.rɪ.ti / (BrE)
The state of being vulnerable to danger, threat, or harm; lack of safety or protection.
state of being unsafe or at risk

Examples

The ongoing conflict created a sense of insecurity among local residents.
Travelers expressed concern about their insecurity in unfamiliar neighborhoods at night.
Economic instability can lead to social insecurity in many regions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: security - the state of being free from danger or threat

Etymology

From Latin 'insecuritas', from 'in-' (not) + 'securus' (secure, safe).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnsəˈkjʊrəti / (AmE), / ˌɪnsɪˈkjʊərɪti / (BrE)
A feeling of uncertainty, anxiety, or lack of confidence about oneself or a situation.
lack of confidence or certainty; anxiety

Examples

She often struggles with insecurity in social situations, especially when meeting new people.
His insecurity about his abilities made him avoid taking on new challenges.
Open communication helped reduce the insecurity that had been building in the team.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: security - the state of feeling safe and free from anxiety or doubt
  • Antonym: confidence - a feeling of self-assurance; certainty

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + securus (secure, safe).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07540157-n
Synset POS: n
