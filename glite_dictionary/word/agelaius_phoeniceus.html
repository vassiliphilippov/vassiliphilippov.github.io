Agelaius phoeniceus

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʒiːliəs foʊˈnɪʃiəs / (AmE), / əˈdʒiːleɪ.əs fɪˈnɪs.i.əs / (BrE)
A North American blackbird species characterized by scarlet patches on its wings; commonly known as the red-winged blackbird.
North American blackbird with red wing patches

Examples

During spring, the Agelaius phoeniceus can be spotted in marshes across North America.
Ornithologists observe the plumage of the Agelaius phoeniceus to study mating behaviors.
The distinctive red patches make the Agelaius phoeniceus easy to identify among other blackbirds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red-winged blackbird - Common name for Agelaius phoeniceus

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus 'Agelaius' from Greek 'agelaios' (gregarious), species 'phoeniceus' from Latin 'phoeniceus' (scarlet or purple-red).
