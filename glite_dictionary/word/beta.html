beta

1. ADJ

/ ˈbeɪ.tə / (AmE), / ˈbiː.tə / (BrE)
Second in rank, importance, or quality; not the most dominant or primary.
second in rank or importance

Examples

In the corporate hierarchy, she was often viewed as a beta leader rather than the top decision-maker.
He played a beta role in the project, supporting rather than directing the team.
As a beta candidate, she knew the odds of winning were slim compared to the favorite.

Synset Examples

the candidate, considered a beta male, was perceived to be unable to lead his party to victory

Related Terms

  • Antonym: alpha - first in rank or importance; dominant

Etymology

From Greek beta, the second letter of the Greek alphabet.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪ.tə / (AmE), / ˈbiː.tə / (BrE)
the second letter of the Greek alphabet (Β, β), often used in mathematics and science
second letter of Greek alphabet

Examples

The professor used beta to represent the second variable in the equation.
Greek letters such as beta and alpha are common in scientific terminology.
The fraternity's name begins with beta, followed by two other Greek letters.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: alpha - first letter of the Greek alphabet
  • Degree Higher: gamma - third letter of the Greek alphabet

Etymology

From Greek beta, from Phoenician beth (house).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06847295-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈbeɪtə / (AmE), / ˈbiːtə / (BrE)
(of a software or hardware product) in a preliminary stage of development, available for testing but not yet in final form.
preliminary/testing version (software/hardware)

Examples

The new app is currently in beta, so some features might not work perfectly.
After months of testing, the beta version is finally available for users to try.
Many tech companies invite feedback during the beta phase to improve their products.

Synset Examples

a beta version
beta software

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: alpha - the earliest testing stage of a software or hardware product, before beta
  • Degree Higher: release - the final, public version of a software or hardware product

Etymology

From the Greek letter beta (β), used as the second in a sequence after alpha.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00881247-s
Synset POS: s
