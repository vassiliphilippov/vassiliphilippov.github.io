bulletin

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊlətɪn / (AmE), / ˈbʊlɪtɪn / (BrE)
a short official statement or report, especially one issued for immediate publication or broadcast
short official report or statement

Examples

The president issued a bulletin to update the nation on the emergency situation.
A weather bulletin interrupted the evening television program with news of the approaching storm.
The company released a bulletin announcing its new product line to the press.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: announcement - a public or formal notice or statement
  • Synonym: communiqué - an official announcement or statement, especially to the media

Etymology

From French 'bulletin', diminutive of 'bulle' (official document), from Latin 'bulla'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06695035-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈbʊlətən / (AmE), / ˈbʊlɪtɪn / (BrE)
to announce or make public by issuing a bulletin
announce formally in a bulletin

Examples

The news agency will bulletin the latest updates at noon.
The company bulletined the policy changes to all employees yesterday.
They are bulletining important weather alerts to the public.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to announce - to make something known publicly or officially

Etymology

From French 'bulletin', diminutive of 'bulle' (document), from Latin 'bulla'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00957065-v
Synset POS: v
