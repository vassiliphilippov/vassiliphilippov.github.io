kelpie

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛlpi / (AmE), / ˈkɛlpi / (BrE)
A breed of Australian sheepdog with pointed ears, known for its agility and intelligence, commonly used for herding livestock.
Australian sheepdog breed

Examples

The farmer relied on his kelpie to round up the flock efficiently.
At the dog show, a sleek black kelpie impressed the judges with its agility.
Many ranchers consider the kelpie to be an indispensable working dog.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheepdog - a dog bred or trained to herd sheep

Etymology

From Scottish folklore 'kelpie', a water spirit, possibly referring to the dog's agility and spirited nature.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɛl.pi / (AmE), / ˈkɛl.pi / (BrE)
In Scottish folklore, a supernatural water spirit, usually appearing as a horse, said to lure and drown travelers.
water spirit from Scottish folklore

Examples

According to legend, the kelpie would appear by the loch to lure unwary travelers onto its back.
Stories warn children never to ride a strange horse by the water for fear it might be a kelpie.
The sculpture in the park depicts a mythical kelpie rising gracefully from the river.

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic 'cailpeach' or 'colpach' (heifer, colt).
