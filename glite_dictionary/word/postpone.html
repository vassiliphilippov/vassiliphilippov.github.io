to postpone

1. VERB

/ poʊstˈpoʊn / (AmE), / pəʊstˈpəʊn / (BrE)
to arrange for something to take place at a later time than first planned, usually because of a reason such as a delay or inconvenience
delay to a later time

Examples

Due to the heavy rain, we had to postpone the picnic until next weekend.
The meeting was postponed because the key speaker was unavailable.
She is considering postponing her vacation until after the project deadline.

Synset Examples

let's postpone the exam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to defer - put off (an action or event) to a later time
  • Antonym: advance - move (an event) to an earlier date or time

Etymology

From Latin 'postponere', from 'post' (after) + 'ponere' (to put, place).
