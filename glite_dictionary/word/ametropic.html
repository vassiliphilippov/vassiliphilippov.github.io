ametropic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæməˈtrɑpɪk / (AmE), / ˌæmɪˈtrɒpɪk / (BrE)
relating to or affected by a condition of the eye in which visual images do not focus properly on the retina, resulting in blurred vision.
relating to abnormal focusing of the eye
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed as ametropic and prescribed corrective lenses.
Children who are ametropic may experience difficulties in school due to poor vision.
Regular eye exams are important for identifying ametropic conditions early.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: emmetropic - relating to normal refraction of the eye

Etymology

From Greek ametros (without measure) + -opia (vision).
