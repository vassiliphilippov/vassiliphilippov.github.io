Benjamin Disraeli

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɛn.dʒə.mɪn dɪzˈreɪ.li / (AmE), / ˈbɛnjəmɪn dɪzˈreɪli / (BrE)
(1804–1881) British statesman and novelist who served as Prime Minister, noted for his role in expanding the British Empire and purchasing a controlling interest in the Suez Canal.
British statesman and Prime Minister (1804–1881)

Examples

Benjamin Disraeli was instrumental in making Queen Victoria the Empress of India.
As Prime Minister, Benjamin Disraeli played a key role in the expansion of the British Empire.
The political rivalry between William Gladstone and Benjamin Disraeli shaped nineteenth-century British politics.

Etymology

Disraeli is an Italian-Jewish surname; Benjamin is a Hebrew given name.
