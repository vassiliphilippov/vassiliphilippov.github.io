boysenberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔɪzənˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪzənˌbɛri / (BrE)
a large, dark reddish-purple edible berry, a hybrid bramble fruit developed in California, with a flavor resembling raspberries and blackberries
a large reddish-purple hybrid berry

Examples

The fresh boysenberry pie was a highlight at the bakery.
Farmers in California first cultivated the boysenberry in the early twentieth century.
She spread boysenberry jam on her morning toast for a sweet and tart flavor.

Etymology

Named after Rudolph Boysen, American horticulturist who developed the hybrid.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɔɪzənˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪz(ə)nˌbɛri / (BrE)
A large, reddish-black berry, with a flavor similar to raspberries, developed from a cross between blackberries, raspberries, and loganberries.
large berry, blackberry-raspberry cross

Examples

She made a delicious pie using fresh boysenberries from the market.
The boysenberry is prized for its sweet and tangy flavor in jams and desserts.
We picked a basket of ripe boysenberries during our visit to the farm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackberry - a small dark purple fruit
  • Synonym: raspberry - a small soft red fruit

Etymology

Named after Rudolph Boysen, the horticulturist who first grew it in California in the 1920s.
