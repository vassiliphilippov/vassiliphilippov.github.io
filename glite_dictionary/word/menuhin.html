Menuhin

1. PROPN

/ məˈnjuːɪn / (AmE), / məˈnjuːɪn / (BrE)
Yehudi Menuhin (1916–1999), British-American violinist and conductor renowned for his virtuosity and contribution to classical music.
famous British-American violinist and conductor

Examples

Menuhin was celebrated for his extraordinary technique and deep musicality on the violin.
The concert featured rare recordings of Menuhin performing with major orchestras.
Many young musicians cite Menuhin as an inspiration for pursuing classical music.
