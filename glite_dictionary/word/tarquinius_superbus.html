Tarquinius Superbus

1. PROPN

/ tɑːrˈkwɪniəs suːˈpɜːrbəs / (AmE), / tɑːˈkwɪnɪəs sjuːˈpɜːbəs / (BrE)
the legendary seventh and last king of Rome, expelled for his cruelty; reigned from 534 to 510 BC
last king of Rome, legendary ruler

Examples

According to Roman tradition, Tarquinius Superbus was overthrown and driven into exile.
Historians often debate the actual existence of Tarquinius Superbus.
The tyrannical rule of Tarquinius Superbus led to the founding of the Roman Republic.

Etymology

Latin, meaning 'Tarquin the Proud'.
