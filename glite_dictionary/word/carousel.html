carousel

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛrəˈsɛl / (AmE), / ˌkærəˈsɛl / (BrE)
a conveyor belt in an airport that delivers checked luggage to arriving passengers
airport conveyor for baggage claim

Examples

After landing, we waited beside the carousel for our suitcases to appear.
The airport installed a new carousel to handle increased passenger traffic.
Please check the number of your carousel before collecting your baggage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conveyor belt - moving belt for transporting objects

Etymology

From French 'carrousel', meaning a turning display or ride; modern sense refers to rotation of luggage.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɛr.əˈsɛl / (AmE), / ˌkær.əˈsɛl / (BrE)
a large, rotating machine at a fair or amusement park with seats, often shaped like animals, for people—especially children—to ride on
rotating amusement ride with seats

Examples

The children laughed as they rode the brightly painted carousel at the fair.
A beautiful old carousel spins in the center of the city park every summer.
He bought a ticket so his daughter could have a turn on the carousel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merry-go-round - a rotating amusement ride with seats, usually for children

Etymology

From French 'carrousel', from Italian 'carosello,' originally meaning a tilting match or tournament.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02969645-n
Synset POS: n
