Marcus Aurelius Antoninus

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɑrkəs ɔˈriːliəs ænˈtɑnənəs / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkəs ɔːˈriːliəs ænˈtɒnɪnəs / (BrE)
(121–180 AD) Roman emperor, nephew and adoptive son of Antoninus Pius, renowned as a Stoic philosopher; under his reign, the decline of the Roman Empire began.
Roman emperor and Stoic philosopher (121–180)

Examples

Marcus Aurelius Antoninus wrote his famous work "Meditations" during military campaigns.
The reign of Marcus Aurelius Antoninus marked a significant turning point in Roman history.
Many admire Marcus Aurelius Antoninus for his commitment to Stoic philosophy despite political turmoil.

Etymology

Latin name; 'Marcus' (a personal name), 'Aurelius' (family name), 'Antoninus' (adopted name).
