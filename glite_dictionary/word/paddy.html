Paddy

1. NOUN

/ ˈpædi / (AmE), / ˈpædi / (BrE)
An offensive term for a person of Irish descent.
offensive term for Irish person
offensive

Examples

The use of the word Paddy to describe someone from Ireland is considered highly offensive.
She was upset when they called her a Paddy at school.
The play highlighted the discrimination faced by Irish immigrants, including slurs like Paddy.

Etymology

From the Irish given name Pádraig (Patrick), often used as a generic reference to Irish men.
