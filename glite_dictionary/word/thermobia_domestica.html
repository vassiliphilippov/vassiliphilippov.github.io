Thermobia domestica

1. NOUN

/ θɜrˈmoʊ.bi.ə doʊˈmɛs.tɪ.kə / (AmE), / θɜːˈməʊ.bɪ.ə dəʊˈmɛs.tɪ.kə / (BrE)
A species of small, wingless insect commonly known as the firebrat, which typically lives in warm, moist environments such as near furnaces.
species of insect, the firebrat

Examples

Thermobia domestica can often be found near boilers and other sources of constant heat in buildings.
The presence of Thermobia domestica indicates a warm, humid environment that may encourage other pests.
Researchers studied the behavior of Thermobia domestica to understand how insects adapt to hot conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firebrat - common name for Thermobia domestica, a small, heat-loving insect
  • Synonym: silverfish - another species of small, wingless insect (Lepisma saccharina) often found in homes

Etymology

From Greek 'thermos' (warm) and Latin 'domestica' (domestic, of the house).
