rosemary

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊzmɛri / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzməri / (BrE)
An aromatic evergreen shrub with needle-like leaves, used as a seasoning for food, especially meats.
aromatic herb used in cooking

Examples

She added fresh rosemary to the lamb before roasting it.
A sprig of rosemary can enhance the flavor of grilled chicken.
Dried rosemary is often found in Mediterranean recipes.

Etymology

From Latin 'rosmarinus', meaning 'dew of the sea'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈroʊzmɛri / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzməri / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub with fragrant, narrow grey-green leaves, used as a culinary herb and in perfumery.
fragrant herb used in cooking

Examples

She sprinkled fresh rosemary on the roasted potatoes for extra flavor.
A potted rosemary plant grows well on a sunny windowsill.
The scent of rosemary filled the kitchen while dinner was being prepared.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thyme - aromatic herb used in cooking

Etymology

From Latin 'rosmarinus', meaning 'dew of the sea'.
