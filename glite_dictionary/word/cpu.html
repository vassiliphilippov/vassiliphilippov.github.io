CPU

1. NOUN

/ si pi ju / (AmE), / siː piː juː / (BrE)
(Computer science) The part of a computer, usually a microprocessor chip, that performs most of the processing operations and executes instructions.
central processing unit of a computer

Examples

The new laptop has a faster CPU than my old desktop computer.
If your CPU overheats, your computer may shut down unexpectedly.
Many modern devices use a multi-core CPU for improved performance.

Synset Examples

the CPU and the memory form the central part of a computer to which the peripherals are attached

Related Terms

  • Synonym: central processing unit - the full form of 'CPU'; main computing component

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'central processing unit'.

2. NOUN

/ si pi ˈju / (AmE), / siː piː ˈjuː / (BrE)
the electronic part of a computer that processes instructions and manages most operations; the central processing unit
central processing unit of a computer

Examples

The new laptop's CPU is much faster than the previous model's processor.
If your CPU overheats, it can cause the computer to shut down unexpectedly.
Upgrading the CPU can greatly improve the performance of your desktop PC.

Synset Examples

the CPU and the memory form the central part of a computer to which the peripherals are attached

Related Terms

  • Synonym: processor - the main component in a computer that interprets and executes instructions
  • Synonym: microprocessor - an integrated circuit that contains the functions of a central processing unit

Etymology

Abbreviation of central processing unit.
