herrerasaur

1. NOUN

/ hɛˈrɛrəˌsɔr / (AmE), / hɛˈrɛərəˌsɔː / (BrE)
an extinct genus of early theropod dinosaurs found in Argentina, dating from the Late Triassic period
Triassic theropod dinosaur from Argentina

Examples

The fossilized remains of a herrerasaur were first uncovered in northwestern Argentina.
Scientists believe that the herrerasaur was one of the earliest theropods.
Unlike later dinosaurs, the herrerasaur had a flexible jaw and unique hip structure.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: theropod - a group of bipedal, mainly carnivorous dinosaurs

Etymology

From the genus name Herrerasaurus, named after Argentine rancher Victorino Herrera who discovered the first specimen.
