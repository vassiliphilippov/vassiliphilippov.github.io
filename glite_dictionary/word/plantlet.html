plantlet

1. NOUN

/ ˈplænt.lət / (AmE), / ˈplɑːnt.lət / (BrE)
a very young or small plant, especially one produced from another plant rather than from seed
a very young or small plant

Examples

The plantlet was carefully separated from the parent plant and replanted in its own pot.
After a few weeks, several plantlets began to grow along the edges of the mother leaf.
Each plantlet needs plenty of sunlight to develop strong roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seedling - a young plant grown from a seed

Etymology

From 'plant' with diminutive suffix '-let', indicating smallness.
