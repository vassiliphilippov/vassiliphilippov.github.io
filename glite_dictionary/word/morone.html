Morone

1. NOUN

/ məˈroʊni / (AmE), / məˈrəʊni / (BrE)
A genus of carnivorous fishes found in both fresh and salt water, including species such as the striped bass and white perch.
a genus of carnivorous fishes

Examples

The striped bass belongs to the genus Morone, which also includes the white perch.
Many anglers seek out Morone species for their challenging fights and excellent taste.
Several freshwater lakes are home to different types of Morone fish.

Etymology

From New Latin Morone, used as a genus name in taxonomy.
