dukedom

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːk.dəm / (AmE), / ˈdjuːk.dəm / (BrE)
the rank, title, or domain held by a duke.
the rank or domain of a duke

Examples

He was granted a dukedom for his loyal service to the king.
The responsibilities of a dukedom often included managing large estates and overseeing local governance.
After his father's death, he inherited the dukedom and all its privileges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duchy - the territory governed by a duke or duchess

Etymology

From Middle English dukedom, from duke + -dom.

2. NOUN

/ ˈduːk.dəm / (AmE), / ˈdjuːk.dəm / (BrE)
The territory or domain ruled by a duke or duchess.
territory ruled by a duke or duchess

Examples

The ancient dukedom was once the most powerful region in the kingdom.
Upon his father's death, he inherited the dukedom and its vast lands.
Historians studied the history of the dukedom and its ruling family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duchy - the territory ruled by a duke or duchess

Etymology

Formed from 'duke' + '-dom' (domain, state). 'Duke' from Old French 'duc', from Latin 'dux' (leader).

Source: WordNet
