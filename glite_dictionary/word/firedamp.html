firedamp

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪərˌdæmp / (AmE), / ˈfaɪədæmp / (BrE)
A mixture of gases, primarily methane, found in coal mines, which becomes explosive when mixed with air.
explosive mine gas mixture, mainly methane

Examples

Working deep underground, miners were always wary of firedamp accumulating in the tunnels.
A single spark could ignite firedamp, causing a deadly explosion in the coal mine.
Detecting firedamp early was essential to ensure the safety of everyone working below ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: methane - a colorless, odorless, flammable gas (CH4); main component of firedamp

Etymology

From 'fire' + 'damp', where 'damp' was an old word for 'vapor' or 'gas'.
