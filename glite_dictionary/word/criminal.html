criminal

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrɪm.ɪn.əl / (AmE), / ˈkrɪm.ɪ.nəl / (BrE)
Guilty of crime or a serious offense; involving or relating to crime.
guilty of crime or serious offense

Examples

The act was deemed criminal under both national and international law.
He was found criminal in his neglect of safety procedures.
Such behavior is not only unethical but also criminal in some countries.

Synset Examples

criminal in the sight of God and man

Related Terms

  • Antonym: innocent - not guilty of a crime or offense

Etymology

From Latin criminalis (relating to crime), from crimen (crime).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪmɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈkrɪmɪnl / (BrE)
a person who has committed a crime or has been legally convicted of a crime
person who has committed a crime

Examples

The police arrested the criminal after a long investigation.
Many believe that education can help reform a criminal and reduce repeat offenses.
The criminal was finally brought to justice after years of evading the authorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offender - a person who commits an illegal act
  • Synonym: lawbreaker - a person who violates the law
  • Antonym: innocent - a person not guilty of a crime

Etymology

From Medieval Latin criminalis (relating to crime), from Latin crimen (crime).
