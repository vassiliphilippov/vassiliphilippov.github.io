scrutiny

1. NOUN

/ ˈskruː.tən.i / (AmE), / ˈskruː.tɪni / (BrE)
a close and thorough examination or a prolonged intense look, especially when someone or something is being watched very carefully
close, careful examination or observation

Examples

The document was subjected to intense scrutiny by legal experts.
Her every move was under the scrutiny of the press.
After much scrutiny, they discovered the error in the report.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspection - careful examination or checking
  • Degree Lower: glance - a brief or hurried look

Etymology

From Latin 'scrutinium' (a search, inquiry), from 'scrutari' (to search, examine).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskruː.t̬ən.i / (AmE), / ˈskruː.tɪ.ni / (BrE)
close and careful examination or inspection, especially when looking for mistakes or problems
close and thorough examination

Examples

The proposal is currently under intense scrutiny by government officials.
Every detail of the report was subjected to careful scrutiny before publication.
She welcomed the public scrutiny and answered all the journalists' questions honestly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspection - careful examination
  • Synonym: oversight - supervision or careful observation
  • Antonym: neglect - failure to care for something

Etymology

From Latin scrutinium (a search), from scrutari (to search or examine).
