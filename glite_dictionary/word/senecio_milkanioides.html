Senecio milkanioides

1. NOUN

/ səˈniːsi.oʊ mɪlˌkæniˈɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / sɪˈniːsi.əʊ mɪlˌkaniˈɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A South African succulent evergreen climber with yellow flowers, often grown as a houseplant for its foliage; sometimes classified in the genus Senecio.
South African succulent climber; houseplant

Examples

The Senecio milkanioides is popular among houseplant enthusiasts because of its attractive foliage.
I saw a beautiful Senecio milkanioides climbing along the greenhouse trellis.
Botanists sometimes debate whether Senecio milkanioides should remain in the genus Senecio.

Etymology

Senecio is from Latin for 'old man', referring to the hoary pappus of the seeds; species epithet is modern Latin.
