grume

1. NOUN

/ ɡruːm / (AmE), / ɡruːm / (BrE)
a thick, viscous liquid or mass, especially a clot of blood.
thick viscous mass or clot

Examples

After the surgery, a dark grume formed beneath the skin.
The mixture thickened into a sticky grume as it cooled.
Blood pooled on the floor, congealing into a large grume.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clot - a thick mass of coagulated liquid, especially blood

Etymology

From Latin grumus (a little heap or clod).

2. NOUN

/ ɡruːm / (AmE), / ɡruːm / (BrE)
A small, semisolid mass or clot, especially of coagulated blood.
semisolid blood clot or mass

Examples

The surgeon noted a dark grume at the site of the injury.
Microscopic examination revealed a grume consisting of both red and white blood cells.
The grume blocked the narrow blood vessel, impeding normal circulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clot - a thickened mass of blood or other liquid
  • Synonym: coagulum - a coagulated mass, especially of blood

Etymology

From Latin 'grumus' meaning 'a little heap' or 'clod'.
