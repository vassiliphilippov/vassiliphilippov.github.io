calorifacient

1. ADJ

/ ˌkælərɪˈfeɪʃənt / (AmE), / ˌkalərɪˈfeɪsɪənt / (BrE)
(of a substance, especially food) producing heat in the body.
producing heat, especially in foods
medical

Examples

Some spices are considered calorifacient because they increase body warmth when eaten.
The dietitian recommended calorifacient foods for people living in cold climates.
Chili peppers are known as calorifacient ingredients due to their warming effect.

Synset Examples

calorifacient chili peppers

Etymology

From Latin 'calor' (heat) + 'facient' (making, producing).
