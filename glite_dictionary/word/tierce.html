tierce

1. NOUN

/ tiɚs / (AmE), / tɪəs / (BrE)
the third of the canonical hours of the Christian Church, traditionally observed around 9 a.m.
third canonical hour (9 a.m.)
archaic

Examples

The monks gathered for tierce in the chapel as the sun rose higher.
According to the monastic rule, tierce was a time for prayer and reflection.
He studied the ancient texts describing the proper observance of tierce.

Etymology

From Latin 'tertia' (third hour).

2. NOUN

/ tiɚs / (AmE), / tɪəs / (BrE)

one of three equal parts
archaic

Examples

The land was split into tierce shares among the three sons.
Each member received a tierce of the inheritance.
The contract stipulated a payment of a tierce of the total profits.

Synset Examples
