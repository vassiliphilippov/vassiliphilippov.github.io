lolly

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːli / (AmE), / ˈlɒli / (BrE)
(British) A sweet frozen snack on a small wooden stick, typically made of ice cream or flavored ice.
ice cream or ice on a stick (UK)
british

Examples

She bought a lolly from the ice cream van on a hot afternoon.
Children enjoyed their strawberry lollies at the park.
Would you like an orange lolly or a chocolate one?

Synset Examples

in England a popsicle is called an ‘ice lolly’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: popsicle - a similar frozen treat on a stick, US term

/ ˈlɑːli / (AmE), / ˈlɒli / (BrE)
(informal) Money; cash.
informal term for money
informal british

Examples

The prize money is a nice bit of lolly for the winner.
She spent all her lolly on concert tickets.

Related Terms
