unrepentant

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.rɪˈpɛn.tənt / (AmE), / ˌʌn.rɪˈpɛn.tənt / (BrE)
Not feeling or expressing remorse or regret for one's actions or beliefs.
not showing remorse or regret

Examples

Despite his harsh words, the politician remained unrepentant about his actions.
The criminal was unrepentant even after hearing the victims' stories in court.
She delivered her opinion in an unrepentant tone, refusing to back down.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: remorseful - feeling or expressing regret or guilt
  • Antonym: contrite - feeling or expressing remorse or penitence
  • Antonym: penitent - feeling or showing sorrow for wrongdoing

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'un-' (not) + 'repentant' (from Latin 'paenitēre', to feel regret).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.rɪˈpɛn.tənt / (AmE), / ˌʌn.rɪˈpen.tənt / (BrE)
not showing remorse or regret for one's actions or bad behavior; stubbornly refusing to change.
not feeling or showing remorse

Examples

Despite evidence of his guilt, he remained unrepentant and refused to apologize.
The politician was unrepentant about her controversial remarks, even after public outcry.
Her unrepentant attitude toward cheating shocked her classmates.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: remorseful - feeling or expressing regret or guilt
  • Antonym: repentant - feeling or showing that you are sorry for something bad or wrong that you have done

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' (not) to 'repentant,' from Latin 'paenitēre' (to regret).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01964522-s
Synset POS: s
