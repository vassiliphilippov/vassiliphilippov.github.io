cirrostratus

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪroʊˈstreɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌsɪrəʊˈstreɪtəs / (BrE)
a type of high-altitude cloud forming a thin, hazy, and uniform layer across the sky
high, thin, hazy cloud layer
medical

Examples

A thin layer of cirrostratus draped the sky, giving the sun a faint halo.
The pilot identified the presence of cirrostratus before descending through the cloud layers.
Weather forecasters predicted cirrostratus clouds would precede the approaching warm front.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cirrus - a type of wispy, high-altitude cloud
  • Degree Lower: altostratus - a mid-altitude gray cloud layer
  • Degree Higher: nimbostratus - a thick, rain-bearing cloud layer

Etymology

From Latin 'cirrus' (curl of hair, tuft) + 'stratus' (spread out, layer).
