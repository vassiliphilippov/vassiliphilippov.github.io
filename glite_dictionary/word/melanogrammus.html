Melanogrammus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛl.ə.nəˈɡræm.məs / (AmE), / ˌmɛl.ə.nəˈɡræm.məs / (BrE)
a genus of marine fish in the family Gadidae, comprising the haddock
genus of marine fish (haddock)

Examples

The haddock is the only species in the genus Melanogrammus.
Scientists classified the newly discovered fish within the genus Melanogrammus.
Melanogrammus is distinguished by the dark lateral line that runs along its body.

Etymology

From Greek melas (black) + gramma (line, letter), referring to the dark lateral line on the haddock.
