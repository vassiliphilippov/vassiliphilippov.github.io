redemptive

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈdɛmptɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈdɛmptɪv / (BrE)
relating to or bringing about redemption or salvation; serving to save or improve someone or something morally or spiritually.
serving to bring redemption or salvation

Examples

The film's ending had a deeply redemptive quality, offering hope to the main character.
Many spiritual leaders preach a redemptive message of forgiveness and transformation.
Her redemptive actions inspired others to make positive changes in their lives.

Synset Examples

a redemptive theory about life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redemptory - serving to redeem; relating to redemption

Etymology

From Late Latin redemptivus (of redemption), from Latin redimere (to redeem).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03121049-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈdɛmptɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈdɛmptɪv / (BrE)
relating to or bringing about salvation or freedom from sin, guilt, or evil
bringing about salvation or redemption

Examples

Her work with the charity had a truly redemptive effect on her life.
Many religions teach the importance of redemptive sacrifice and forgiveness.
The film explores the redemptive power of love and compassion.

Synset Examples

saving faith
redemptive (or redeeming) love

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redeeming - serving to compensate for faults or bad aspects

Etymology

From Latin redemptio (a buying back; redemption), from redimere (to buy back).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01134233-s
Synset POS: s
