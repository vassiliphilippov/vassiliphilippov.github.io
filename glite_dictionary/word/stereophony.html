stereophony

1. NOUN

/ ˌstɛriəˈfoʊni / (AmE), / ˌstɪəriˈfɒni / (BrE)
the technique of using two or more channels to create the impression of sound coming from different directions, producing a three-dimensional auditory effect
three-dimensional sound reproduction

Examples

Modern music recordings often use stereophony to create a rich listening experience for audiences.
The sense of space in stereophony makes it easier to distinguish between different instruments playing simultaneously.
Before the advent of stereophony, all music recordings were produced in monophonic sound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stereo - sound reproduction using two or more channels for a spatial effect

Etymology

From Greek stereos (solid) + phōnē (sound); modeled after 'stereophonic'.
