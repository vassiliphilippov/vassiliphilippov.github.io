recoverer

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈkʌvərər / (AmE), / rɪˈkʌvərə / (BrE)
a person who saves or retrieves something from danger or loss
someone who retrieves something from danger or loss

Examples

The diligent recoverer managed to save the ancient manuscript from a burning building.
After the shipwreck, the recoverer located several valuable artifacts underwater.
As a skilled recoverer, she specializes in retrieving lost data from damaged computers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rescuer - a person who saves others from danger

Etymology

From 'recover' + agent suffix '-er'. 'Recover' from Old French *recovrer*, from Latin *recuperare* (to regain).
