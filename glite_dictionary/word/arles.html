arles

1. NOUN

/ ɑrlz / (AmE), / ɑːlz / (BrE)
a sum of money given as earnest to confirm a contract, especially in buying or hiring.
money given to confirm a contract

Examples

The buyer handed over arles to seal the agreement for the property.
Before employment began, the farm worker received his arles.
Accepting arles made the deal legally binding in traditional practice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earnest - money paid as a deposit to demonstrate commitment to a contract

Etymology

From Old French *erles*, from Medieval Latin *arlæ*, possibly of Germanic origin.
