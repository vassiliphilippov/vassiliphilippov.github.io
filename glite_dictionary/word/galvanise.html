to galvanise

1. VERB

/ ˈɡælvəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɡælvənaɪz / (BrE)
To shock or excite someone into taking action.
stimulate into action

Examples

The speech managed to galvanise the crowd into demanding change.
Her sudden resignation galvanised the company into addressing the underlying issues.
Seeing the devastation firsthand can really galvanise people to donate and volunteer.

Synset Examples

the loud noise startled him awake
galvanized into action

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stimulate - to encourage something to develop or become more active
  • Synonym: to motivate - to provide someone with a reason or incentive to act
  • Antonym: to dissuade - to persuade someone not to take a particular action

Etymology

From French 'galvaniser', from Luigi Galvani, Italian scientist.

2. VERB

/ ˈɡæl.vəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɡæl.və.naɪz / (BrE)
To stimulate nerves or muscles by applying an electric shock.
stimulate with electric shock
medical british

Examples

The doctor used a device to galvanise the patient's leg muscles during therapy.
Researchers discovered that they could galvanise nerves to provoke movement in paralyzed limbs.
Early experiments attempted to galvanise frog legs with electric currents to study muscle contractions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stimulate - to encourage activity or increased function

Etymology

From French 'galvaniser', after Luigi Galvani, Italian physician and scientist, who studied the effect of electricity on muscles.
