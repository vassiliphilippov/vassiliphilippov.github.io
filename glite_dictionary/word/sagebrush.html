sagebrush

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪdʒˌbrʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈseɪdʒˌbrʌʃ / (BrE)
A woody shrub of arid North American regions, especially of the genus Artemisia, with silvery-grey leaves and a strong aroma.
aromatic shrub of arid North America

Examples

The desert is dotted with sagebrush as far as the eye can see.
Coyotes often hide among the sagebrush while hunting for food.
Early settlers used sagebrush for fuel and shelter in the barren plains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wormwood - aromatic plant of the genus Artemisia

2. NOUN

/ ˈseɪdʒˌbrʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈseɪdʒˌbrʌʃ / (BrE)
a woody shrub with silvery-grey leaves, common in arid regions of western North America, chiefly of the genus Artemisia
a silvery shrub of western North America

Examples

Vast areas of the western United States are covered in sagebrush.
Pronghorn antelope can often be seen grazing among the sagebrush in the desert.
The aroma of sagebrush filled the air after the rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wormwood - woody, aromatic plants of the genus Artemisia

Etymology

Combines 'sage' (from its resemblance to true sage) and 'brush' (meaning shrubbery or scrub vegetation).
