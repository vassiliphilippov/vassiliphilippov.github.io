Deuteromycota

1. NOUN

/ duːˌtɛrəˈmaɪkoʊtə / (AmE), / djuːˌtɛərəˈmaɪkətə / (BrE)
a large and varied group of fungi for which no sexually reproductive stage has been observed or identified
group of fungi without known sexual reproduction
medical

Examples

Some plant diseases are caused by Deuteromycota species due to their asexual reproduction.
Scientists have reclassified many members of Deuteromycota as more genetic data becomes available.
The classification of Deuteromycota highlights the challenges of grouping fungi without observed sexual cycles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fungus - a group of spore-producing organisms feeding on organic matter

Etymology

From Greek deuteros (second) + mykes (fungus), referring to their classification as a 'second' group of fungi.

2. NOUN

/ ˌduːtərəˌmaɪˈkoʊtə / (AmE), / ˌdjuːtərəˌmaɪˈkəʊtə / (BrE)
a large and varied group of fungi in which no sexually reproductive stage has been observed or classified
group of fungi without known sexual reproduction
medical

Examples

Many plant pathogens belong to the Deuteromycota, making them important in agriculture.
Because they lack a known sexual stage, fungi in the Deuteromycota are sometimes called 'imperfect fungi'.
Researchers are working to reclassify many Deuteromycota as their reproductive methods become better understood.

Etymology

From Greek 'deuteros' (second) and 'mykes' (fungus), referring to their secondary placement in fungal classification.
