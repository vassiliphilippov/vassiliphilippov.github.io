antithetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.təˈθɛt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌæntɪˈθetɪk / (BrE)
directly opposed or contrasted in character or purpose; mutually incompatible
directly opposed or contrasting

Examples

The values of the two groups were antithetic to one another, leading to frequent disagreements.
Her actions were clearly antithetic to the company's stated mission.
Their philosophy was antithetic to everything we believed in.

Synset Examples

practices entirely antithetical to her professed beliefs
hope is antithetic to despair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antithetical - directly opposed, forming an antithesis
  • Synonym: opposite - completely different or contrary
  • Antonym: similar - having a resemblance or likeness

Etymology

From Greek antithetikos (setting in opposition), from antitithenai (to set against).
