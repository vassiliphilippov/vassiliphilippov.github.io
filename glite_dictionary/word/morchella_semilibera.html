Morchella semilibera

1. NOUN

/ mɔrˈkɛl.ə ˌsɛm.əˈlaɪ.bə.rə / (AmE), / mɔːˈkɛl.ə ˌsɛm.ɪˈliː.bə.rə / (BrE)
A species of morel mushroom with a ridged and pitted cap attached to the stem for about half its length.
species of morel mushroom

Examples

The Morchella semilibera is prized by foragers for its delicate flavor and unique appearance.
Unlike other morels, the cap of Morchella semilibera is attached only halfway down the stem.
In spring, experienced mushroom hunters search forests for Morchella semilibera among decaying leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morel - an edible mushroom genus Morchella with honeycomb-like cap

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; 'Morchella' from German 'Morchel' (morel), and 'semilibera' meaning 'half-free' in Latin.
