furtiveness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɝː.tɪv.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfɜː.tɪv.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being secretive or stealthy, suggesting a desire to avoid notice or attention
secretive or stealthy quality

Examples

There was a certain furtiveness in his manner that made everyone suspicious.
The cat moved with furtiveness through the tall grass, stalking its prey.
Her furtiveness aroused curiosity among her colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stealth - cautious and secretive action or behaviour
  • Antonym: openness - lack of secrecy or concealment, frankness

Etymology

From Latin furtivus (stolen, secret), from furtum (theft).
