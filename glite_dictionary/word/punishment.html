punishment

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌnɪʃmənt / (AmE), / ˈpʌnɪʃmənt / (BrE)
the act of punishing or imposing a penalty on someone for an offense or wrongdoing
the act of penalizing wrongdoing

Examples

The new law introduces harsher punishment for repeat offenders.
He accepted his punishment without complaint after breaking the rules.
Corporal punishment is now banned in many schools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penalty - a punishment imposed for breaking a law, rule, or contract
  • Antonym: reward - something given in recognition of service or achievement; opposite of punishment

Etymology

From Old French punissement, from punir (to punish), from Latin punire (to inflict a penalty).
