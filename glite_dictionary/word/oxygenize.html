to oxygenize

1. VERB

/ ˈɑksɪdʒəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɒksɪdʒəˌnaɪz / (BrE)
to impregnate, combine, or supply with oxygen
to supply or treat with oxygen

Examples

Medical devices can oxygenize blood outside the body during surgery.
Engineers aim to oxygenize water in lakes to support aquatic life.
Researchers studied how efficiently the machine could oxygenize various fluids.

Synset Examples

oxygenate blood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to oxygenate - to supply, treat, or enrich with oxygen
  • Antonym: to deoxygenate - to remove oxygen from

Etymology

Formed from 'oxygen' + '-ize', following common pattern for verbs denoting 'to cause to become.'

2. VERB

/ ˈɑksɪdʒəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɒksɪdʒəˌnaɪz / (BrE)
to combine or treat (a substance) with oxygen; to oxidize, especially by increasing the proportion of oxygen in a compound or raising the oxidation state of an atom, ion, or molecule
treat or combine with oxygen

Examples

If you oxygenize the solution, it will change color as the iron oxidizes.
The laboratory process was designed to oxygenize the metal ore efficiently.
Aquatic systems may suffer if water is not properly oxygenized to support fish life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to oxidize - to combine with oxygen or increase the proportion of oxygen

Etymology

From oxygen + -ize, modeled after French oxygéniser.

3. VERB

/ ˈɑksɪdʒəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɒksɪdʒəˌnaɪz / (BrE)
to treat, combine, or react with oxygen; to supply or enrich with oxygen
combine or react with oxygen

Examples

To improve respiration, doctors sometimes oxygenize the patient's blood using a special machine.
Industries frequently oxygenize water to enhance chemical reactions during treatment.
The lab oxygenized the sample to test its effects on metal corrosion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to oxidize - to combine or become combined chemically with oxygen

Etymology

From oxygen + -ize.
