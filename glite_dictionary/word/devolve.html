to devolve

1. VERB

/ dɪˈvɑlv / (AmE), / dɪˈvɒlv / (BrE)
to pass or delegate (a duty, responsibility, etc.) to another person or group, especially at a lower level of authority
pass or delegate duties/responsibility

Examples

The manager decided to devolve some of his tasks to junior staff members.
With the new policy, more authority will devolve to regional offices over time.
The responsibility for maintenance has devolved from the central government to local councils.

Synset Examples

The representative devolved his duties to his aides while he was in the hospital

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delegate - to assign responsibility or authority to another
  • Antonym: to retain - to keep or hold onto responsibilities or authority

Etymology

From Latin devolvere, from de- (down) + volvere (to roll).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02397527-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dɪˈvɑlv / (AmE), / dɪˈvɒlv / (BrE)
to develop or change gradually into a worse or less ordered state; to deteriorate over time
become worse; deteriorate

Examples

After several hours, the peaceful protest devolved into chaos.
The polite conversation quickly devolved into an argument.
Without strong leadership, the situation may devolve further in the coming weeks.

Synset Examples

Her condition deteriorated
Conditions in the slums degenerated
The discussion devolved into a shouting match

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deteriorate - to become progressively worse
  • Synonym: degenerate - to decline or deteriorate physically, mentally, or morally
  • Antonym: to improve - to become better; to make progress or advancement

Etymology

From Latin devolvere (to roll down), from de- (down) + volvere (to roll).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00092045-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dɪˈvɑlv / (AmE), / dɪˈvɒlv / (BrE)
(of property, rights, or responsibilities) to be transferred or passed on to another, especially as an inheritance.
be transferred as an inheritance
legal

Examples

The title will devolve to the eldest son upon the lord's death.
After the owner's passing, the property devolved to his niece as specified in the will.
According to the agreement, all liabilities devolve to the heirs of the estate.

Synset Examples

The estate fell to my sister
The land returned to the family
The estate devolved to an heir that everybody had assumed to be dead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inherit - to receive (property or rights) as an heir

Etymology

From Latin devolvere, meaning 'to roll down, sink, or hand over'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02234332-v
Synset POS: v
