dominee

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːməˌni / (AmE), / ˈdɒmɪni / (BrE)
a clergyman, especially a minister or parson in some Protestant churches, notably in South Africa and formerly in Dutch Reformed communities
a Protestant clergyman or minister

Examples

The local dominee led the Sunday service with a thoughtful sermon.
Many people in the village sought advice from the dominee during hard times.
After years abroad, he returned to his hometown as the new dominee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minister - a member of the clergy, especially in Protestant churches
  • Synonym: parson - a Protestant clergyman, especially in the Church of England

Etymology

From Dutch *dominee* (pastor, minister), ultimately from Latin *domine* (Lord).
