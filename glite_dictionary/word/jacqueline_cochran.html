Jacqueline Cochran

1. PROPN

/ ˌdʒækəlɪn ˈkoʊkræn / (AmE), / ˌdʒækəlɪn ˈkɒkrən / (BrE)
(1910–1980) United States aviator known for holding several speed records and leading the Women's Airforce Service Pilots during World War II.
American aviator and wartime leader

Examples

Jacqueline Cochran was the first woman to break the sound barrier.
During World War II, Jacqueline Cochran played a crucial role in training female pilots.
Many young women were inspired by Jacqueline Cochran's achievements in aviation.
