to oppose

1. VERB

/ əˈpoʊz / (AmE), / əˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to be against something; to express disagreement or disapproval of an idea, plan, or action
be against; express opposition

Examples

Many people oppose the proposed increase in taxes.
She openly opposed the decision during the meeting.
Environmental groups have been opposing the construction of the new highway.

Synset Examples

We oppose the ban on abortion

Related Terms

  • Antonym: support - to agree with and give encouragement to something or someone

Etymology

From Latin 'opponere' (to set against).

3. VERB

/ əˈpoʊz / (AmE), / əˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to disagree with and try to stop or resist something, especially actively or strongly
to resist or fight against

Examples

Several lawmakers oppose the new tax proposal and have promised to vote against it.
Many people opposed the construction of the factory near their homes.
She is planning to oppose the motion at the next meeting.

Synset Examples

The senator said he would oppose the bill
Don't fight it!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: support - to agree with and help or approve of something or someone
  • Synonym: to resist - to withstand the effect of; to oppose actively

Etymology

From Latin opponere, from ob- 'against' + ponere 'to place'.

4. VERB

/ əˈpoʊz / (AmE), / əˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to place or set in contrast or opposition, often with equal force or emphasis
set in contrast or opposition

Examples

In the debate, she opposed his arguments point by point, highlighting the flaws in his logic.
The artist opposed warm and cool colors in the painting to create a dramatic effect.
These two theories are often opposed to illustrate the different schools of thought in philosophy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrast - to compare in such a way as to emphasize differences
  • Antonym: support - to back up or strengthen rather than oppose

Etymology

From Latin 'opponere' (to set against), from 'ob-' (against) + 'ponere' (to place).

6. VERB

/ əˈpoʊz / (AmE), / əˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to set in opposition or rivalry; to place or pit against someone or something in competition or conflict
set in opposition or competition

Examples

The principal decided to oppose the top students from each class in a debate competition.
The manager would often oppose team members to encourage healthy rivalry and innovation.
Reality TV shows frequently oppose contestants with conflicting personalities to create drama.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pit - to set in direct competition or conflict with
  • Synonym: match - to oppose or set against for competition
  • Antonym: to align - to place or arrange in a line with or alongside; the opposite of setting in opposition
