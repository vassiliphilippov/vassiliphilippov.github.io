Rigveda

1. PROPN

/ ˌrɪɡˈveɪdə / (AmE), / ˌrɪɡˈveɪdə / (BrE)
the oldest of the four Vedas, consisting of a collection of ancient Sanskrit hymns and poems composed before 2000 BC, forming an important part of Hindu sacred literature
ancient Hindu sacred text; oldest Veda

Examples

The Rigveda contains some of the oldest known religious hymns in human history.
Scholars have studied the Rigveda to learn about early Indo-Aryan culture and language.
Many rituals in Hinduism can be traced back to hymns found within the Rigveda.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Veda - each of the four ancient sacred texts forming the earliest Hindu scriptures

Etymology

From Sanskrit ṛgvedaḥ, from ṛc (praise, verse) + veda (knowledge, sacred lore).
