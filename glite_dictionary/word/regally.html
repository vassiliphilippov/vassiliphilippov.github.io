regally

1. ADV

/ ˈriːɡəli / (AmE), / ˈriːɡəli / (BrE)
In a manner that is magnificent, dignified, or characteristic of a king or queen.
in a regal or royal manner

Examples

The actress swept into the room regally, commanding everyone's attention.
He always carries himself regally, as if he were born to lead.
The old tapestry hung regally behind the throne, lending an air of majesty to the hall.

Synset Examples

a regally appropriate representative

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regal - resembling or fit for a king or queen; royal

Etymology

Formed from 'regal' (from Latin 'regalis', royal) + -ly.
