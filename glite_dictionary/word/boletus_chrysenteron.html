Boletus chrysenteron

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈliːtəs ˌkraɪsɛnˈtɛrɒn / (AmE), / bəʊˈliːtəs ˌkrɪsɛnˈtɛrən / (BrE)
a species of fungus with a convex cap, a dull yellow pore surface beneath, and a dry stalk, commonly found in woodland areas
a species of bolete mushroom

Examples

The Boletus chrysenteron is often found growing near oak and beech trees in the fall.
Mushroom hunters can identify Boletus chrysenteron by its cracked cap and yellowish pores.
You should always consult an expert before consuming wild fungi like Boletus chrysenteron.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; 'Boletus' from Greek 'bōlitos' (mushroom), and 'chrysenteron' from Greek 'chrysos' (gold) and 'enteron' (intestine), referring to the golden coloring in the flesh.
