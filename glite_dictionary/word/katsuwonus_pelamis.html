Katsuwonus pelamis

1. NOUN

/ kætsuːˈwoʊnəs pəˈlamɪs / (AmE), / katsjuːˈwəʊnəs pɪˈleɪmɪs / (BrE)
a species of tuna, also known as skipjack tuna, whose flesh is often dried and flaked for use in Japanese cookery
species of tuna used in Japanese cooking

Examples

Dried flakes of Katsuwonus pelamis are used to make the Japanese broth called dashi.
The Katsuwonus pelamis is also commonly known as skipjack tuna in English.
Many sushi dishes contain Katsuwonus pelamis as a key ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skipjack tuna - a species of tuna, also known as Katsuwonus pelamis

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'katsuwon' (Bonito) and 'pelamis' (a young tuna or a type of fish).
