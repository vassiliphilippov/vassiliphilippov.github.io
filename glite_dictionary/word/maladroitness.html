maladroitness

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæləˈdrɔɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˌmælədˈrɔɪtnəs / (BrE)
Lack of skill or dexterity; unskillfulness or clumsiness in action or behavior.
lack of skill; clumsiness

Examples

His maladroitness during the performance made his inexperience evident to everyone.
The maladroitness of the new employee led to several mistakes on her first day.
She quickly overcame her initial maladroitness with thorough practice and guidance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clumsiness - the quality of being awkward or lacking coordination
  • Antonym: dexterity - skill and grace in physical movement

Etymology

From French 'maladroit' (clumsy), from 'mal' (badly) + 'adroit' (skilful).
