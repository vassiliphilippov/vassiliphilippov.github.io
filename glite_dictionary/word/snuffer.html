snuffer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnʌfər / (AmE), / ˈsnʌfə / (BrE)
a person who extinguishes candles, especially by pinching or with a tool
person who extinguishes candles

Examples

The snuffer moved quietly through the hall, extinguishing each candle after the guests had left.
In old mansions, a snuffer would ensure all lights were out before bedtime.
As a professional snuffer, he carried a special tool for the task.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extinguisher - a person or thing that puts something out, especially fire

Etymology

From 'snuff' (to extinguish a candle) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsnʌf.ər / (AmE), / ˈsnʌfə / (BrE)
a cone-shaped tool with a handle used to extinguish candles by smothering the flame
tool for extinguishing candles

Examples

She used a snuffer to extinguish the candles without scattering hot wax.
The old brass snuffer on the mantelpiece belonged to my grandmother.
After dinner, he reached for the snuffer to put out the dining room candles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candle snuffer - a tool used to extinguish candles

Etymology

From the verb 'snuff' (to extinguish a candle), from Middle English 'snuffen', influenced by Old Norse 'snufa' (to sniff).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04260278-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈsnʌfər / (AmE), / ˈsnʌfə / (BrE)
a person who uses snuff, especially powdered tobacco taken by inhalation or sniffing
person who uses snuff (powdered tobacco)

Examples

The old gentleman was a habitual snuffer, keeping a silver box of tobacco in his pocket.
She watched as the snuffer prepared a pinch of snuff with practiced fingers.
In the eighteenth century, it was common to see a snuffer in the social clubs of London.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snuff-taker - a person who takes snuff

Etymology

From 'snuff' (powdered tobacco for inhalation) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

Source: WordNet
