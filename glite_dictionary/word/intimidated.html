intimidated

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtɪməˌdeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɪmɪdeɪtɪd / (BrE)
feeling fearful or lacking in confidence because of someone else's threats or presence
feeling fearful or threatened

Examples

She felt intimidated by the large crowd watching her performance.
Many new employees are intimidated when attending their first company meeting.
He admitted he was intimidated by his boss's reputation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: confident - having a strong belief in one's abilities or qualities
  • Synonym: threatened - feeling unsafe or at risk due to possible harm or danger

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'intimidate', from Latin 'intimidare' (to make afraid).
