tetherball

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛðərˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈtɛðəbɔːl / (BrE)
A game played by two people who hit a ball attached to a pole by a string, aiming to wrap the string completely around the pole in their direction.
outdoor game with ball on a pole

Examples

The children played tetherball at recess every day.
She won the tetherball match by skillfully wrapping the ball around the pole.
At the summer camp, a tetherball court stood near the playground.

Etymology

From 'tether' (to tie with a rope or chain) and 'ball'.
