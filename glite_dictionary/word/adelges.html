Adelges

1. NOUN

/ ˈædɛlˌdʒiːz / (AmE), / ˈædɛlˌdʒiːz / (BrE)
a genus of small insect pests (plant lice) that feed on coniferous trees, some species of which cause galls or other damage to their hosts
genus of plant lice feeding on conifers

Examples

The Adelges genus includes several insects harmful to spruce and fir trees.
A severe infestation of Adelges can cause abnormal growths called galls on conifers.
Researchers are studying how Adelges affect forestry across North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphid - small sap-sucking insect (Aphidoidea)

Etymology

From Greek 'adelos' (unseen, obscure), as adopted in scientific classification.

2. NOUN

/ əˈdɛldʒiz / (AmE), / əˈdɛldʒiːz / (BrE)
A genus of small plant-feeding insects in the family Adelgidae, also known as adelgids or plant lice, which often infest coniferous trees.
type genus of plant lice (insects)

Examples

The spruce trees were infested with Adelges, causing the needles to drop prematurely.
Scientists studied the life cycle of Adelges to develop better methods of pest control.
Damage to fir forests was largely attributed to the spread of Adelges.

Etymology

From New Latin, named as a genus by entomologists.
