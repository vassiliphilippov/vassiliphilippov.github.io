everlasting

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛvɚˈlæstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɛvəˈlɑːstɪŋ / (BrE)
any of various plants in the daisy family (Compositae) whose flowers retain their form and color when dried
plant with flowers that can be dried

Examples

The bouquet was made of everlasting to ensure it would last all year.
Many gardeners plant everlasting for dried flower arrangements.
She pressed the everlasting between book pages to preserve its beauty.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɛvɚˈlæstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɛvəˈlɑːstɪŋ / (BrE)
lasting forever; without end or seeming to last for an indefinitely long time.
lasting forever; endless

Examples

Their everlasting friendship survived every hardship and test over the years.
She promised him her everlasting love in the wedding vows.
The mountain's everlasting snow caps glistened in the morning sun.

Synset Examples

the ageless themes of love and revenge

the unending bliss of heaven

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eternal - lasting or existing forever; without end
  • Synonym: perpetual - never ending or changing; occurring repeatedly
  • Antonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period of time

Etymology

From Old English 'ǣfre' (ever) + 'lastian' (to endure).
