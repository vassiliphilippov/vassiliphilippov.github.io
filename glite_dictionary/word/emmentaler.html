Emmentaler

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛmənˌtɑlɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛmənˌtɑːlə / (BrE)
A hard, yellow, Swiss cheese with large holes and a mild, nutty flavor, traditionally made in the Emmental region of Switzerland.
Swiss cheese with large holes

Examples

The sandwich was filled with slices of Emmentaler, giving it a mild and creamy taste.
For the fondue, choose a high-quality Emmentaler to ensure a smooth melt.
She bought some Emmentaler from the specialty cheese shop for her cheese platter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Swiss cheese - Generic term for cheeses from Switzerland with characteristic holes

Etymology

From German 'Emmentaler Käse' (cheese from the Emmental valley), after the Emmental region in Switzerland.
