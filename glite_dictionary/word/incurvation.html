incurvation

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌkɜːrˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌkɜːˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the action or process of curving or making something curved; the state of being curved
the process or state of being curved

Examples

The incurvation of the tree branch was caused by years of heavy snow.
Chronic incurvation of the spine may result in discomfort or pain.
The sculptor's design featured a dramatic incurvation in the metalwork.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curvature - the fact of being curved or the degree of curving

Etymology

From Latin incurvatio, from incurvare (to bend inwards).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˌkɝːˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌkɜːˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the state or process of being curved or bent inward.
inward curve or bending

Examples

The incurvation of the spine can indicate certain medical conditions.
She admired the delicate incurvation of the antique mirror's frame.
An incurvation along the coastline created a sheltered bay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curvature - the state of being curved or the degree of curving
  • Antonym: convexity - the state of being curved outward

Etymology

From Latin incurvare (to bend inward) + -ation (forming nouns of action or process).
