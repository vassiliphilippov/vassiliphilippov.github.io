Hermissenda

1. NOUN

/ hɜrˌmɪˈsɛndə / (AmE), / hɜːˌmɪˈsendə / (BrE)
A genus of brightly colored marine sea slugs found in the Pacific Ocean, known for their use in neuroscience research.
genus of marine sea slugs

Examples

The Hermissenda is a popular model organism in neurobiological studies due to its simple nervous system.
Colorful Hermissenda sea slugs can be observed grazing on algae in tide pools along the Pacific coast.
Marine biologists collected several Hermissenda specimens to study their behavior in controlled environments.

Etymology

From Greek elements hermes (Hermes, the messenger god) and isos (equal) referenced in scientific Latin naming conventions.
