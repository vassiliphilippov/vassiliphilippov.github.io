caravan

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærəˌvæn / (AmE), / ˈkærəvan / (BrE)
(British) A vehicle equipped for living in, typically towed by a car and used for holidays or travel; a camper with living quarters.
vehicle for living/traveling, typically towed
british

Examples

They spent their holidays touring the countryside in a caravan.
The family bought a new caravan to take on their summer trips.
She decorated the inside of the caravan with colorful cushions and fairy lights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camper - motor vehicle or trailer equipped with living space
  • Synonym: motorhome - motor vehicle equipped as a self-contained living area

Etymology

From Persian 'karwan' (group of desert travelers), originally referring to a group traveling together, later applied to the vehicle.

2. VERB

/ ˈkær.ə.væn / (AmE), / ˈkær.ə.væn / (BrE)
to travel together in a group, especially with vehicles or animals, as a caravan
travel in a convoy or group

Examples

They decided to caravan across the country with friends for the summer.
A group of tourists caravaned through the desert to reach the ancient ruins.
We often caravan to music festivals, traveling together for safety and fun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convoy - to travel together, especially for protection

Etymology

From Persian *karwan* (group of desert travelers).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɛrəˌvæn / (AmE), / ˈkærəvæn / (BrE)
A group of people, especially traders or pilgrims, traveling together in a line with animals or vehicles for safety across a desert or through dangerous regions.
group traveling together (often with animals)

Examples

The caravan moved slowly across the desert, winding between the dunes under the blazing sun.
Merchants formed a caravan for protection as they carried goods along the Silk Road.
A caravan of camels and traders camped beneath the stars after a long day of travel.

Synset Examples

we were part of a caravan of almost a thousand camels
they joined the wagon train for safety

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convoy - a group of vehicles or ships traveling together for protection
  • Synonym: train - a group of people, animals, or vehicles traveling together

Etymology

From Persian 'karwan' (group of desert travelers).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
