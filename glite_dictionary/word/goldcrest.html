goldcrest

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldˌkrɛst / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldˌkrɛst / (BrE)
A very small European songbird (Regulus regulus) with a distinctive black-bordered yellow crown patch.
very small European songbird

Examples

The goldcrest is often seen flitting among the branches of pine trees.
Birdwatchers were delighted to spot a goldcrest during the morning walk.
With its striking yellow crown, the goldcrest is easy to recognize even though it's Europe's smallest bird.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kinglet - Any small songbird in the genus Regulus

Etymology

From 'gold' + 'crest', referring to the bird's yellow crown patch.
