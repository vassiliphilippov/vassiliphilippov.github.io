funfair

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌnˌfɛr / (AmE), / ˈfʌnˌfeə / (BrE)
a traveling amusement fair with rides, sideshows, and games
traveling amusement fair with rides and games
british

Examples

The children were excited to visit the funfair that set up in the park.
We spent the afternoon enjoying rides and snacks at the funfair.
There will be a traveling funfair in town for just one week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnival - a public event with games, rides, and entertainment

Etymology

Compound of 'fun' and 'fair'. Chiefly British.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfʌnˌfɛr / (AmE), / ˈfʌnˌfeə / (BrE)
an event or place with rides, games, and amusements, typically set up temporarily for entertainment
amusement event with rides and stalls
british

Examples

We took the kids to the funfair for some rides and candyfloss.
The funfair comes to town every summer, attracting crowds from nearby villages.
After school, the children excitedly ran to the brightly lit funfair in the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amusement park - a permanent park with rides and entertainment
  • Synonym: fairground - an area where fairs with rides and attractions are held
