cleanly

1. ADV

/ ˈklin.li / (AmE), / ˈkliːn.li / (BrE)
in a way that causes little or no dirt or pollution; in a tidy or uncontaminated manner.
in a tidy, uncontaminated manner

Examples

The new engine runs cleanly and produces fewer emissions.
Please cut the cake cleanly so that each piece looks neat.
Solar panels generate electricity cleanly without releasing pollutants.

Synset Examples

the motor burns cleanly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clean - free from dirt or impurities

2. ADV

/ ˈklinli / (AmE), / ˈkliːnli / (BrE)
in a smooth, precise, and effective way; without unnecessary difficulty or error
smoothly and precisely (adverb)

Examples

The soccer player struck the ball cleanly into the top corner of the net.
The surgeon made an incision cleanly and efficiently for the procedure.
She completed the dance routine cleanly, without missing a single step.

Synset Examples

the gymnast landed flawlessly
she played the piano accompaniment cleanly
he bounced it cleanly off the wall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smoothly - in a way that is without difficulty, interruption, or hesitation
  • Synonym: precisely - in a strictly accurate or definite manner
  • Antonym: clumsily - in an awkward, careless, or unskilful way

Etymology

Formed from 'clean' + adverbial suffix '-ly'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈklinli / (AmE), / ˈkliːnli / (BrE)
(of a person, animal, or place) habitually clean; characterized by regular cleanliness.
habitually clean; regularly clean

Examples

The children grew up in a cleanly household where hygiene was always valued.
He is a cleanly man who takes pride in his appearance and environment.
The animals are surprisingly cleanly, regularly grooming themselves throughout the day.

Synset Examples

cleanly in their persons and habitations

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncleanly - not habitually clean
  • Synonym: clean - free from dirt or impurities

Etymology

From Middle English 'clenli', from 'clene' (clean) + '-ly'.
