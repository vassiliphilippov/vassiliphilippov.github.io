Solea

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈliːə / (AmE), / səʊˈliːə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Soleidae, comprising certain species of flatfish known as soles.
genus of flatfishes (soles)

Examples

The genus Solea includes several important species of flatfish consumed as seafood.
Researchers studied the spawning habits of Solea in coastal waters.
The common sole, a member of the genus Solea, is prized by chefs for its delicate flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Soleidae - the family of flatfishes to which Solea belongs

Etymology

From Latin Solea, meaning 'a slipper' or 'sandal', referring to the fish's flat shape.

2. PROPN

/ soʊˈliːə / (AmE), / səʊˈliːə / (BrE)
A genus of flatfish in the family Soleidae, including the common sole and related species.
genus of flatfish (Soleidae)

Examples

The common sole belongs to the genus Solea, which includes several species of flatfish.
Biologists classified the newly discovered flatfish as a member of Solea.
Many species in the genus Solea are important for commercial fisheries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sole - a flatfish valued as food

Etymology

From Latin *solea* (a kind of flatfish, also 'sandal'), related to the fish's shape.
