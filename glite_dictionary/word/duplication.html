duplication

1. NOUN

/ ˌduːplɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdjuːplɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making a copy or copies of something
the act of making a copy

Examples

The duplication of files can lead to unnecessary confusion in data management.
We need to avoid duplication of effort in our research team.
Regular duplication of sensitive information should be minimized to protect privacy.

Synset Examples

this kind of duplication is wasteful

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copying - the act of making a copy of something
  • Antonym: originality - the quality of being new or unique, not copied

Etymology

From Latin duplicatio (a doubling), from duplicare (to double).

2. NOUN

/ ˌduːplɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdjuːplɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
an exact copy of something, especially one that corresponds exactly to the original
an exact copy

Examples

A duplication of the contract was sent to both parties for their records.
The museum kept a duplication of each artifact's documentation in a secure vault.
Please avoid unnecessary duplication of documents to reduce paper waste.

Synset Examples

he made a duplicate for the files

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copy - something made to look exactly like another
  • Antonym: original - the first or earliest form of something

Etymology

From Latin 'duplicatio', from 'duplicare' (to double).
