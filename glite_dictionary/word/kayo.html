kayo

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.əʊ / (BrE)
A blow that renders an opponent unconscious, especially in boxing; a knockout.
knockout blow in boxing
informal

Examples

The boxer landed a powerful kayo in the final round to win the match.
Fans cheered as the unexpected kayo left the champion sprawled on the canvas.
That uppercut was a classic kayo, ending the fight in seconds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knockout - A blow that makes an opponent unconscious, especially in boxing

Etymology

Alteration of 'K.O.', initialism of 'knockout'.

2. VERB

/ ˈkeɪ.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.əʊ / (BrE)
to knock someone unconscious or senseless, especially in a boxing match or fight
knock someone unconscious
informal

Examples

The boxer managed to kayo his rival with a single punch in the third round.
If you’re not careful in the ring, you might get kayoed before you even know it.
She watched the movie hero kayo two bad guys in under a minute.

Synset Examples

the boxing champion knocked out his opponent in a few seconds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to knock out - to make someone unconscious, especially by hitting them

Etymology

Variant of K.O., abbreviation of 'knockout'.
