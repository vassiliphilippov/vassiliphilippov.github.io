idol

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪdəl / (AmE), / ˈaɪdəl / (BrE)
a person or thing regarded as a perfect example or embodiment of a particular quality, idea, or concept
perfect embodiment of a concept

Examples

To her fans, she was not just a singer, but the very idol of grace and talent.
The athlete became an idol for millions, representing perseverance and dedication.
For centuries, Leonardo da Vinci has been considered the idol of artistic genius.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paragon - a person or thing viewed as a model of excellence
  • Synonym: exemplar - a person or thing serving as a typical example or excellent model
  • Synonym: model - a person or thing regarded as an excellent example

Etymology

From Greek 'eidōlon' (image, phantom) via Latin 'idolum'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪdəl / (AmE), / ˈaɪdəl / (BrE)
An image or representation of a god used as an object of worship.
object of worship; effigy of a deity

Examples

Many ancient cultures created idols to represent gods and goddesses in their temples.
The villagers gathered to pray before the golden idol during the festival.
Religious texts often forbid the worship of idols made by human hands.

Synset Examples

thou shalt not make unto thee any graven image
money was his god

Related Terms

  • Synonym: icon - a representation or picture, especially in religious contexts
  • Degree Higher: deity - a god or goddess; a divine being
  • Degree Lower: statue - a three-dimensional representation, usually of a person or animal

Etymology

From Greek eidōlon (image, phantom), via Latin idolum.

3. NOUN

/ ˈaɪdəl / (AmE), / ˈaɪdəl / (BrE)
a person who is admired or adored blindly and excessively
person admired excessively

Examples

For millions of fans, the pop star became an idol and a role model.
The young athlete is treated like an idol by his hometown supporters.
Some people believe you should never put a celebrity on an idol pedestal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hero - a person admired for their courage or achievements
  • Synonym: icon - a person regarded as a representative symbol or as worthy of veneration

Etymology

From Greek eidōlon (image, phantom, idol) via Latin idolum.
