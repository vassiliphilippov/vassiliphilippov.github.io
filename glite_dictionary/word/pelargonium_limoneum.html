Pelargonium limoneum

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛl.ɑːrˈɡoʊ.ni.əm lɪˈmoʊ.ni.əm / (AmE), / ˌpɛl.əˈɡəʊ.ni.əm lɪˈməʊ.ni.əm / (BrE)
A species of geranium with lemon-scented leaves, often cultivated in gardens for its aromatic foliage and attractive flowers.
lemon-scented garden geranium

Examples

The Pelargonium limoneum in her patio garden filled the air with a fresh lemon fragrance.
Many gardeners grow Pelargonium limoneum for its decorative leaves and pleasant scent.
A sprig of Pelargonium limoneum can add a subtle citrus aroma to floral arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geranium - a genus of flowering plants commonly known as cranesbills

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining the genus name Pelargonium (from Greek pelargos, meaning 'stork') and the Latinized epithet limoneum, referring to its lemon scent.
