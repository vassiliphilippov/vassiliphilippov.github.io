entente

1. NOUN

/ ɑnˈtɑnt / (AmE), / ɒnˈtɒnt / (BrE)
an informal agreement or alliance between states or countries for cooperation or mutual understanding, especially in political or military matters
informal alliance between countries

Examples

The two nations formed an entente to promote regional stability.
During the early 20th century, the Triple Entente played a crucial role in global politics.
Negotiators sought an entente rather than a formal treaty to avoid provoking other countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alliance - a formal agreement between countries or groups to work together

Etymology

From French 'entente' (understanding), from Old French 'entendre' (to understand).

2. NOUN

/ ɑnˈtɑnt / (AmE), / ɒnˈtɒnt / (BrE)
an informal alliance or friendly understanding between states or political powers, especially for mutual benefit or cooperation
friendly understanding between political powers

Examples

The two countries formed an entente to promote peace and stability in the region.
A secret entente between the nations shaped the course of the negotiations.
The entente allowed the governments to coordinate their foreign policies without a formal treaty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alliance - a formal agreement between countries or organizations for mutual benefit

Etymology

From French 'entente' (understanding), from Old French 'entendre' (to intend, understand).
