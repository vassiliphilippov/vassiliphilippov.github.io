chick

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪk / (AmE), / tʃɪk / (BrE)
A young bird, especially a newly hatched domestic chicken.
young bird, especially a baby chicken

Examples

The mother hen protected her chick from predators in the yard.
Each chick in the nest eagerly awaited its turn to be fed.
We watched a chick hatch from its egg at the farm.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: chicken - a domestic fowl kept for its eggs or meat
  • Synonym: hatchling - a very young animal that has recently emerged from an egg

Etymology

From Old English 'cicen', meaning young chicken.

2. NOUN

/ tʃɪk / (AmE), / tʃɪk / (BrE)
(informal, sometimes offensive) a young woman.
informal term for a young woman
informal offensive

Examples

He was always trying to impress the chicks at parties.
That movie is popular with chicks in their twenties.
Some people think calling women chicks is disrespectful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: girl - a female child or young woman
  • Synonym: woman - an adult female human

Etymology

Slang usage from 1920s US, likely from the idea of a chick as a young, cute creature.
