aesthetician

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛsˌθɛˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌiːsˌθɛˈtɪʃən / (BrE)
a person trained to give beauty treatments such as facials, manicures, and skin care.
person trained in beauty treatments

Examples

After her facial, she thanked the aesthetician for making her skin look radiant.
The aesthetician recommended a new skincare routine during my visit to the spa.
She decided to become an aesthetician after developing an interest in beauty treatments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cosmetologist - a person licensed to provide cosmetic treatments to hair, skin, and nails

Etymology

From French esthétique, from Greek aisthētikos (of sense perception); influenced by 'esthetic'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛsˌθiˈtɪʃən, ˌæsˌθiˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌiːsˌθɛtˈɪʃən, ˌæsˌθɛtˈɪʃən / (BrE)
a philosopher who specializes in the study of the nature of beauty and aesthetic experience
philosopher of aesthetics/beauty

Examples

The aesthetician presented a compelling theory about the origins of beauty in nature.
Many great thinkers, such as Kant, have worked as aesthetician in the field of philosophy.
During the symposium, a leading aesthetician discussed how art influences our sense of beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aesthete - a person who has or professes to have refined sensitivity toward the beauties of art or nature
  • Synonym: philosopher - a person engaged or learned in philosophy

Etymology

From Greek aisthētēs (one who perceives) + -ician.
