disjointed

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈdʒɔɪntɪd / (AmE), / dɪsˈdʒɔɪntɪd / (BrE)
(of a bone or joint) separated or disconnected at the joint, often as the result of injury.
separated at the joint (medical)
medical

Examples

The X-ray revealed that the patient's shoulder was disjointed after the fall.
He suffered a disjointed ankle during the basketball game.
A disjointed finger can be extremely painful and requires immediate attention.

Synset Examples

a dislocated knee
a separated shoulder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dislocated - (of a bone or joint) moved out of its normal position

Etymology

From Latin dis- (apart) + iunctus (joined).

2. ADJ

/ dɪsˈdʒɔɪntɪd / (AmE), / dɪsˈdʒɔɪntɪd / (BrE)
(of a body, bird, or limb) taken apart at the joints; separated at the natural junctions.
taken apart at the joints

Examples

The chef prepared the disjointed chicken before marinating it for roasting.
The butcher delivered a disjointed duck ready for the casserole.
Recipes often require a disjointed fowl to ensure even cooking.

Synset Examples

a disjointed fowl

Related Terms

  • Antonym: jointed - having joints or being jointed together

Etymology

From 'disjoint' + '-ed', meaning 'separated at the joint'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02491032-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ dɪsˈdʒɔɪntɪd / (AmE), / dɪsˈdʒɔɪntɪd / (BrE)
lacking a clear, logical, or orderly connection; not well organized or coherent
lacking logical or orderly connection

Examples

Her explanation was so disjointed that no one could follow her argument.
The film's plot felt disjointed and confusing, making it hard to understand the story.
He presented his ideas in a disjointed manner, jumping from one topic to another.

Synset Examples

a confused set of instructions
a confused dream about the end of the world
disconnected fragments of a story
scattered thoughts

Related Terms

  • Antonym: coherent - logically connected and consistent
  • Synonym: disconnected - lacking a logical or meaningful connection

Etymology

From 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'jointed', originally referring to bones or joints and later to things lacking connection.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00467212-s
Synset POS: s
