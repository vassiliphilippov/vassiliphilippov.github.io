adactyly

1. NOUN

/ əˈdæk.tɪ.li / (AmE), / əˈdæk.tɪ.li / (BrE)
A congenital condition characterized by the absence of one or more fingers or toes.
absence of fingers/toes from birth
medical

Examples

The newborn was diagnosed with adactyly affecting both hands.
Genetic studies have linked certain mutations to cases of adactyly in humans and animals.
Researchers are developing prosthetics to assist people living with adactyly.

Etymology

From Greek a- (without) + daktulos (finger).
