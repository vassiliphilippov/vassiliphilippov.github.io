Pericallis hybrida

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrɪˈkælɪs hɪˈbrɪdə / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˈkælɪs hɪˈbrɪdə / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant cultivated for its profuse, colorful blooms, with florets in shades ranging from white to blue and purple.
ornamental flowering plant species

Examples

The garden center had several pots of Pericallis hybrida in full bloom.
New varieties of Pericallis hybrida feature striking purple and blue petals.
She chose Pericallis hybrida for her window box because of its vibrant flowers.

Etymology

From Latinized botanical name, with 'Pericallis' from Greek 'peri-' (around) and 'kallos' (beauty), 'hybrida' meaning hybrid.
