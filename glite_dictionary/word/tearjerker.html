tearjerker

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪrˌdʒɜrkər / (AmE), / ˈtɪəˌdʒɜːkə / (BrE)
A book, film, or play that is excessively sentimental and designed to make people feel very sad or emotional.
overly sentimental story or film

Examples

The movie was a real tearjerker, moving the entire audience to tears.
I'm not in the mood to watch a tearjerker tonight; let's choose something funny instead.
Her new novel is another tearjerker that tugs at the heartstrings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weeper - A sentimental film or book that makes people cry
  • Antonym: comedy - A work intended to make people laugh, not cry

Etymology

From 'tear' (a drop of liquid from the eye) + 'jerker' (something that jerks or causes jerking), indicating something that elicits tears.
