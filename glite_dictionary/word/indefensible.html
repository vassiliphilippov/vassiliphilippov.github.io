indefensible

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˈfɛnsəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈfɛnsəbl / (BrE)
(of actions, arguments, etc.) not able to be justified or defended against criticism.
impossible to defend or justify

Examples

The company's decision to pollute the river was completely indefensible.
He realized his theory was indefensible once new evidence was presented.
They considered her actions legally and morally indefensible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: untenable - (of arguments) not able to be maintained or defended
  • Antonym: defensible - able to be defended or justified

Etymology

From Latin 'indefensibilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'defendere' (to defend).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˈfɛnsəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈfɛnsəbəl / (BrE)
Not able to be justified or excused; impossible to defend by reasoning.
not able to be justified or excused

Examples

The company's decision to cut corners on safety was indefensible.
Many critics viewed his remarks as indefensible and demanded an apology.
Her behavior during the meeting was simply indefensible and shocked everyone present.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: justifiable - able to be defended or shown to be right
  • Synonym: untenable - (especially of an opinion or position) not able to be maintained or defended against attack or objection

Etymology

From Latin 'indefensibilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'defendere' (to defend).

3. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˈfɛnsəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈfɛnsəbl / (BrE)
not able to be defended or protected against attack or criticism.
not able to be defended or protected

Examples

The fortress was in such a state of disrepair that it was completely indefensible.
His actions were clearly indefensible in the eyes of the law.
They abandoned the outpost when they realized it had become indefensible against enemy forces.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: defensible - able to be defended or protected
  • Synonym: vulnerable - exposed to the possibility of attack or harm

Etymology

From Late Latin indefensibilis, from in- (not) + Latin defendere (to defend).
