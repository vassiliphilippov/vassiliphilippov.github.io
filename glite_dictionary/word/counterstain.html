counterstain

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊn.tɚˌsteɪn / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.təˌsteɪn / (BrE)
a stain of contrasting color used in microscopy to highlight structures not seen with the principal stain
contrasting stain in microscopy
medical

Examples

The scientist applied a counterstain to make the cell walls visible under the microscope.
After the primary stain, a counterstain helped differentiate the bacterial types.
Using a counterstain can reveal additional structures not shown by the main stain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stain - a substance used to color materials for examination under a microscope

Etymology

From 'counter-' meaning 'contrasting or complementary' + 'stain'.
