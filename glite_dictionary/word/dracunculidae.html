Dracunculidae

1. NOUN

/ drəˌkʌŋkjəˈlaɪdiː / (AmE), / drækʌŋˌkjuːlɪˈdiː / (BrE)
A family of greatly elongated parasitic roundworms, some of which infect vertebrates, including humans.
family of elongated parasitic roundworms
medical

Examples

Members of the Dracunculidae family are known for causing dracunculiasis in humans.
Scientists studied the life cycle of Dracunculidae to understand how these parasites infect their hosts.
Guinea worm disease is caused by a species within the Dracunculidae family.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek drakōn (serpent, dragon), referencing the worm's appearance.

2. NOUN

/ drəˌkʌŋˈkjulɪˌdiː / (AmE), / drəˌkʌŋˈkjʊlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of elongated parasitic roundworms, including species that infect mammals, such as the Guinea worm.
a family of parasitic roundworms
medical

Examples

The parasitic worms of the Dracunculidae family can cause serious diseases in humans and animals.
Dracunculus medinensis, a member of the Dracunculidae, is responsible for Guinea worm disease.
Research on the Dracunculidae has contributed to the near eradication of Guinea worm infections worldwide.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus name Dracunculus and the standard taxonomic suffix -idae.

Synset POS: n
