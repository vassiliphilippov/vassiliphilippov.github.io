Shangri-la

1. NOUN

/ ˌʃæŋɡrɪˈlɑ / (AmE), / ˌʃæŋɡrɪˈlɑː / (BrE)
an imaginary place of perfect happiness, peace, and contentment; a paradise or utopia
imaginary paradise; place of perfect happiness

Examples

After years of searching, they finally found their Shangri-la in a quiet mountain village.
Her garden became a personal Shangri-la, where she escaped from the stress of daily life.
Many people dream of retiring to a Shangri-la far from the noise and crowds of the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paradise - an ideal or perfect place; heaven
  • Synonym: utopia - an imagined perfect society or place
  • Antonym: dystopia - an imagined society of great suffering or injustice

Etymology

Coined by British author James Hilton in his 1933 novel 'Lost Horizon,' where it refers to a fictional Himalayan paradise.
