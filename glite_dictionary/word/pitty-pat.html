pitty-pat

1. ADV

/ ˈpɪtiˌpæt / (AmE), / ˈpɪtiˌpat / (BrE)
With a light, rapidly repeated sound, as of footsteps or a heartbeat.
with a quick, light, repeated sound

Examples

She heard the pitty-pat of raindrops against the window as she fell asleep.
The children came running, their shoes making a pitty-pat along the wooden floor.
His heart went pitty-pat when he saw her smile.

Synset Examples

he came running pit-a-pat down the hall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pit-a-pat - with a light, rapidly repeated sound, especially footsteps or a heartbeat

Etymology

Reduplicative formation imitating the sound of light, quick steps or beats.

2. ADV

/ ˈpɪtiˌpæt / (AmE), / ˈpɪtiˌpat / (BrE)
with a light, rhythmic, quickly repeated beat or sound, like that of a heartbeat or light footsteps.
with a light, rhythmic beating sound

Examples

I could hear my heart go pitty-pat as I waited for the results.
The rain fell pitty-pat on the rooftop, soothing me to sleep.
Her footsteps sounded pitty-pat down the empty hallway.

Synset Examples

his heart went pit-a-pat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pit-a-pat - with a light, rapid, rhythmic beating sound

Etymology

Imitative, from the sound of a light, rhythmic beat.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00426498-r
Synset POS: r
