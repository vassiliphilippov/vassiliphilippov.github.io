Cacicus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæsɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈkæsɪkəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American orioles in the family Icteridae, known for their striking plumage and colonial nesting habits.
a genus of tropical American orioles

Examples

The vibrant yellow and black feathers distinguish the Cacicus species from other orioles in the region.
Scientists observed a Cacicus colony constructing elaborate hanging nests in the rainforest canopy.
Several new behaviors have been documented within the genus Cacicus during recent field studies.

Etymology

From Latin 'cacicus' (chieftain, chief); adopted as a genus name in ornithology.
