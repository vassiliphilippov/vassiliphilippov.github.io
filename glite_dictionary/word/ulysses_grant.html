Ulysses Grant

1. PROPN

/ juːˈlɪsiz ɡrænt / (AmE), / juːˈlɪsiːz ɡrɑːnt / (BrE)
(1822–1885) The 18th President of the United States and commander of the Union armies during the American Civil War.
18th U.S. President and Civil War general

Examples

Ulysses Grant played a crucial role in the victory of the Union during the Civil War.
After the war, Ulysses Grant became the 18th President of the United States.
Many historians regard Ulysses Grant as one of America's most effective military leaders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Abraham Lincoln - 16th President of the United States; led the nation during the Civil War
