carbamate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrbəˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.bə.meɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester derived from carbamic acid, often used in chemical and pharmaceutical contexts
salt or ester of carbamic acid

Examples

Many pesticides contain the carbamate group as their active ingredient.
The toxicity of carbamates makes them effective as insecticides.
Researchers studied the effects of carbamate exposure on human health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urethane - a compound also known as ethyl carbamate, related chemically

Etymology

From 'carbamic acid' + '-ate', following chemical nomenclature.
