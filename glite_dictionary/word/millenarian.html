millenarian

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɪləˈnɛriən / (AmE), / ˌmɪlɪˈneəriən / (BrE)
relating to the belief in a future thousand-year era of peace and happiness, especially as foretold by religious prophecy; or someone who holds such beliefs.
believing in a thousand-year era of peace

Examples

Some sects hold millenarian beliefs, expecting an imminent era of peace and justice.
The leader inspired his followers with a millenarian vision of a transformed future.
Historians studied the rise of millenarian movements in the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: millennial - relating to a thousand years or a millennium; often used similarly

Etymology

From Latin 'millenarius' (containing a thousand), from 'mille' (thousand).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɪləˈnɛriən / (AmE), / ˌmɪlɪˈnɛːrɪən / (BrE)
a person who believes in the coming of a millennium, especially a period of great peace and happiness
someone who believes in a coming millennium

Examples

Many millenarians in history have predicted a new era of peace and prosperity.
The early 19th century saw several millenarian movements emerge in response to social unrest.
He studied the writings of millenarian thinkers who expected an imminent transformation of society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: millennialist - a person who believes in a coming millennium of peace and happiness

Etymology

From Latin *millenarius* (consisting of a thousand), from *mille* (thousand).
