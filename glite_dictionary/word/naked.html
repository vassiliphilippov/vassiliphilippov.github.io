naked

1. ADJ

/ ˈneɪkəd / (AmE), / ˈneɪkɪd / (BrE)
(of the eye, ear, etc.) not using any aid or device; unaided by instruments.
unaided by instruments (eye, ear, etc.)

Examples

With the naked eye, you can see the stars, but not the planets in detail.
The bacteria were invisible to the naked eye and required a microscope for observation.
This sound is too faint to be detected by the naked ear.

Synset Examples

visible to the naked eye

Etymology

From Old English *nacod* (bare, unclothed).

/ ˈneɪkɪd / (AmE), / ˈneɪkɪd / (BrE)
not wearing any clothes; having no covering on the body
not wearing any clothes

Examples

3. ADJ

/ ˈneɪkəd / (AmE), / ˈneɪkɪd / (BrE)
(of feelings, truth, or qualities) not disguised, concealed, or moderated; expressed in a pure, direct, or unembellished manner.
undisguised or direct (emotion, truth, etc.)

Examples

His naked fear was clear on his face during the interview.
In her speech, she expressed naked honesty about her struggles.
The report revealed the naked truth about the company's failures.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: concealed - kept hidden or disguised

Etymology
