hawker

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔkər / (AmE), / ˈhɔːkə / (BrE)
a person who breeds and trains hawks, especially for the sport of falconry
person who trains hawks for falconry

Examples

The village hawker displayed his majestic birds to the crowd.
She became a skilled hawker after years of studying the art of falconry.
Every hawker must develop a strong bond with his or her bird to succeed.

Etymology

From Middle English *hauker*, from Old English *hafocere* (one who keeps hawks).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɔkər / (AmE), / ˈhɔːkə / (BrE)
a person who travels from place to place selling goods, especially in the street or at markets
person who sells goods in public places

Examples

The hawker offered fresh fruit from his cart to passersby in the busy market.
In some cities, hawkers line the streets selling newspapers and snacks to commuters.
A hawker shouted out deals for souvenirs at the entrance to the fair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peddler - someone who travels about selling goods
  • Synonym: vendor - a person selling something, especially in the street

Etymology

From Middle Low German *hoker* (peddler, huckster).
