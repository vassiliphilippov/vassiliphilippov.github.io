dichloride

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈklɔːraɪd / (AmE), / daɪˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound that contains two chlorine atoms in each molecule, typically combined with another element or group
compound with two chlorine atoms per molecule

Examples

Methylene dichloride is a solvent commonly used in paint removers.
The reaction produced a pale green dichloride compound.
Certain metal dichlorides are used as catalysts in polymer chemistry.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: chloride - a compound containing chlorine

Etymology

From di- (meaning 'two') and chloride, indicating the presence of two chlorine atoms.
