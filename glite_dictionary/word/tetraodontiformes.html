Tetraodontiformes

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛt̬.ɹi.oʊˌdɑn.tɪˈfɔːr.miːz / (AmE), / ˌtɛt.ɹi.əʊˌdɒn.tɪˈfɔː.miːz / (BrE)
an order of mainly marine fishes including puffers, triggerfishes, boxfishes, and ocean sunfishes, characterized by their unique body shapes and spiny skins
an order of fishes including puffers and boxfishes

Examples

The Tetraodontiformes include some of the world's most oddly shaped fishes.
Researchers studied the defensive behaviors of puffers and other members of the Tetraodontiformes.
The Tetraodontiformes are known for their spiny skins and diverse body forms.

Etymology

From Greek *tetra-* (four), *odous* (tooth), and Latin *forma* (form), referring to characteristic dental structure.
