orthomyxovirus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔr.θəˈmɪk.səˌvaɪ.rəs / (AmE), / ˌɔː.θəˈmɪk.səˌvaɪ.rəs / (BrE)
a virus of a group that includes those causing influenza, characterized by an RNA genome and an outer envelope
virus group including influenza viruses
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered a new strain of orthomyxovirus that is highly contagious.
The orthomyxovirus family includes all known influenza viruses affecting humans and animals.
Researchers are studying how the orthomyxovirus adapts to resist antiviral drugs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: influenza virus - virus that causes influenza

Etymology

From Greek 'orthos' (straight) + 'myxa' (mucus) + 'virus'.
