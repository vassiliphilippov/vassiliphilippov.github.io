washbasin

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑʃˌbeɪsən / (AmE), / ˈwɒʃˌbeɪs.ən / (BrE)
A fixed bowl in a bathroom, connected to a water supply and drain, used for washing hands and face.
fixed bathroom sink for washing

Examples

She splashed cold water from the washbasin onto her face to wake up.
The hotel room comes with a small washbasin next to the shower.
Please rinse your hands thoroughly in the washbasin before dinner.

Synset Examples

he ran some water in the basin and splashed it on his face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sink - a fixed basin with water supply and drain used in kitchens or bathrooms

Etymology

From 'wash' + 'basin', with 'basin' from Old French 'bacin', from Late Latin 'bacchinon'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɑʃˌbeɪsən / (AmE), / ˈwɒʃˌbeɪsɪn / (BrE)
a fixed bowl in a bathroom or washroom, used for washing the hands and face
basin for washing hands and face

Examples

The washbasin in the guest bathroom was sparkling clean.
He rinsed his hands quickly at the washbasin before dinner.
She installed a modern washbasin in her newly renovated bathroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sink - a basin, typically in a kitchen, for washing dishes, hands, etc.

Etymology

Formed from 'wash' + 'basin'.
