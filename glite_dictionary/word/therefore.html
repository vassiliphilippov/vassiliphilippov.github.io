therefore

1. ADV

/ ˈðɛr.fɔr / (AmE), / ˈðɛə.fɔː / (BrE)
for that reason; as a result; consequently
as a result; consequently

Examples

The road was icy; therefore, driving conditions were dangerous.
Prices have increased. Therefore, many people are finding it harder to afford basic goods.
He forgot his umbrella and therefore got soaked in the rain.

Synset Examples

therefore X must be true
the eggs were fresh and hence satisfactory
we were young and thence optimistic
it is late and thus we must go
the witness is biased and so cannot be trusted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thus - in this way; as a result
  • Synonym: hence - for this reason
  • Synonym: so - for that reason; therefore

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old English 'þærfore', from 'þær' (there) + 'fore' (for)

2. ADV

/ ˈðɛr.fɔr / (AmE), / ˈðeə.fɔː / (BrE)
as a result of something that has just been mentioned; for that reason
for that reason; as a result

Examples

The evidence was clearly unreliable; therefore, the judge dismissed the case.
All the tickets have been sold, and therefore no more seats are available for tonight's show.
She forgot her umbrella and therefore got soaked when it started to rain.

Synset Examples

he had good reason to be grateful for the opportunities which they had made available to him and which consequently led to the good position he now held

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thus - as a result; consequently
  • Synonym: hence - for this reason; as a result
  • Antonym: nevertheless - in spite of that; notwithstanding

Etymology

From Middle English 'therfor', from Old English 'þǣrfore' ('for that'), equivalent to there + for.
