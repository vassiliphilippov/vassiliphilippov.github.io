convertible

1. ADJ

/ kənˈvɝː.t̬ə.bəl / (AmE), / kənˈvɜː.tɪ.bəl / (BrE)
able to be changed into a different form, function, or use
able to be converted or adapted

Examples

The guest room has a convertible sofa that doubles as a bed.
Many parents choose a convertible car seat that grows with their child.
Their living room features a convertible table that expands for extra guests.

Synset Examples

a convertible sofa
a convertible coupe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adaptable - able to be changed to suit different conditions or uses
  • Antonym: fixed - unable to be changed or adapted

Etymology

From Latin 'convertibilis' (able to be turned about or changed), from 'convertere' (to turn around).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01031603-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ kənˈvɝː.tə.bəl / (AmE), / kənˈvɜː.tɪ.bəl / (BrE)

sofa that becomes a bed

Examples

She pulled out the convertible and quickly prepared it for her cousin to sleep on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sofa bed - a sofa that can be made into a bed

Etymology

From Latin 'convertere' (to turn around, transform) via French and English.

3. NOUN

/ kənˈvɝːtəbəl / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːtɪbəl / (BrE)
a corporate security, such as a bond or preferred stock, that can be exchanged for another form of security, typically common stock
security exchangeable for another, esp. in finance

Examples

The company issued a new series of convertibles to attract investment from shareholders.
Many investors prefer convertibles because they offer the potential for conversion to common stock if the company's fortunes improve.
The value of the convertible increased after the corporation's stock price surged.

Etymology

From the verb 'convert' + suffix '-ible', originally from Latin 'convertibilis' (able to be turned around or changed).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13440498-n
Synset POS: n

5. ADJ

/ kənˈvɜrtəbəl / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːtəbəl / (BrE)
able to be exchanged for or replaced by something else of equal value, especially in a financial or legal context.
able to be exchanged for something of equal value

Examples

Many bonds are issued as convertible securities, allowing holders to exchange them for company stock.
The new vouchers are convertible to cash at any branch of the bank.
He invested in convertible shares, hoping their value would increase before conversion.

Synset Examples

convertible securities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exchangeable - able to be exchanged for something else
  • Antonym: nonconvertible - not able to be converted or exchanged

Etymology

From Latin 'convertere' meaning 'to turn around, transform'.
