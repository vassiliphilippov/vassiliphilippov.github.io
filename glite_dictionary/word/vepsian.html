Vepsian

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛpsiən / (AmE), / ˈvɛpsiən / (BrE)
a member of a Finno-Ugric ethnic group native to northwestern Russia
member of a Finnish people of Russia

Examples

A group of Vepsians gathered to celebrate their traditional festival.
The Vepsian population has declined significantly over the past century.
Many Vepsian customs have influenced neighboring cultures in northwestern Russia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Veps - the language of the Vepsian people

Etymology

From Russian Ве́пс (Veps), self-name of the people.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɛpsiən / (AmE), / ˈvɛpsiən / (BrE)
the Finnic language spoken by the Veps people, primarily in northwestern Russia
Finnic language of the Veps people

Examples

Very few people still speak Vepsian as their first language.
The government provides some educational materials in Vepsian to support minority languages.
She is studying Vepsian linguistics at the university.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Finnic - relating to the group of Finno-Ugric languages including Vepsian
  • Synonym: Veps - the people who speak Vepsian

Etymology

Named after the Veps people; Veps is the English form of the native name 'vepsläižed'.
