Tricholoma pessundatum

1. NOUN

/ trɪkəˈloʊmə pɛˌsʌnˈdeɪtəm / (AmE), / trɪkəˈləʊmə pɛˌsʌnˈdeɪtəm / (BrE)
a species of mildly poisonous mushroom with a sticky reddish-brown cap and white gills and stalk
species of mildly poisonous mushroom

Examples

Foragers should avoid Tricholoma pessundatum because it is mildly poisonous and may cause gastrointestinal upset.
The sticky cap and white gills help identify Tricholoma pessundatum in the wild.
Some field guides list Tricholoma pessundatum as inedible due to its toxicity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tricholoma - a genus of agaric fungi including several species

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Tricholoma from Greek 'trikhos' (hair) + 'loma' (fringe); species epithet from Latin 'pessundatum' (smashed, destroyed).
