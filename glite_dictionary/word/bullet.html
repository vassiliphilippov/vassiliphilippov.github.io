bullet

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊlət / (AmE), / ˈbʊlɪt / (BrE)
A small metal projectile that is fired from a gun.
projectile fired from a gun

Examples

The detective examined the bullet that was lodged in the wall.
Soldiers are trained to avoid incoming bullets during combat.
The police recovered the bullet as evidence from the crime scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cartridge - a complete unit of ammunition, including the bullet, casing, propellant, and primer

Etymology

From Middle French *boulette* (little ball), diminutive of *boule* (ball).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʊlɪt / (AmE), / ˈbʊlɪt / (BrE)
a high-speed passenger train, especially one resembling or named after the Japanese Shinkansen
high-speed passenger train

Examples

The new bullet will cut travel times between the cities by half.
Tourists flocked to experience the speed of the Japanese bullet on their trip to Tokyo.
Plans to construct a bullet across the country have attracted much attention.

Etymology

Used since the mid-20th century, inspired by the resemblance of the train's shape and speed to a bullet; popularized by the Japanese Shinkansen ('bullet train').
