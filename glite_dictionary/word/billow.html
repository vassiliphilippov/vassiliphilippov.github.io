billow

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈbɪləʊ / (BrE)
a large sea wave, often used poetically or in reference to a rolling mass of water
large sea wave

Examples

From the cliff, they watched billows crashing onto the jagged rocks below.
After the storm, gigantic billows rolled across the surface of the ocean.
The poet described the billows as mountains moving restlessly beneath the moon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wave - a long body of water curling into an arched form and breaking on the shore

Etymology

From Old Norse *bylgja* (a billow, wave).

2. VERB

/ ˈbɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈbɪləʊ / (BrE)
to swell or fill with air and rise or roll outward in waves
to swell or bulge outward

Examples

The white curtains began to billow in the summer breeze.
Smoke started to billow from the chimney as the fire grew stronger.
The flag would billow dramatically whenever the wind picked up.

Synset Examples

The sails ballooned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balloon - to expand or swell out, usually with air or gas

Etymology

From Old Norse *bylgja* (a wave or swell).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00257003-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈbɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈbɪləʊ / (BrE)
(of smoke, cloud, fabric, etc.) to rise, move, or swell outward in a rolling mass like a wave.
rise or swell in rolling waves

Examples

Thick black smoke began to billow from the windows of the burning building.
Her white dress could be seen billowing in the wind as she walked along the beach.
Clouds of steam billowed up when he opened the dishwasher after the cycle finished.

Synset Examples

smoke billowed up into the sky

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surge - to move suddenly and powerfully forward or upward

Etymology

From Old Norse 'bylgja' (a wave), related to Old English 'bylg' (a swelling, wave).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02045267-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈbɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈbɪləʊ / (BrE)
(of smoke, cloud, fabric, etc.) to rise or move in a great rolling mass, typically in a wave-like motion.
move or rise in waves or surges

Examples

Dark smoke began to billow from the burning building, filling the sky.
The sails billowed in the strong wind as the ship moved out to sea.
Clouds of dust billowed up behind the cars on the dirt road.

Synset Examples

The army surged forward

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surge - to move suddenly and powerfully forward or upward

Etymology

From Old Norse 'bylgja' (a wave).
