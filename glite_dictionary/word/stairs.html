stairs

1. NOUN

/ stɛrz / (AmE), / stɛəz / (BrE)
A set of steps that allows people to go from one level of a building to another.
set of steps between floors

Examples

She ran quickly up the stairs to answer the phone.
The old house has a creaky wooden stairs leading to the attic.
Please be careful going down the stairs when carrying heavy boxes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: staircase - a set of stairs with its supporting framework and railings
  • Antonym: ladder - a portable set of steps for climbing up or down

Etymology

From Middle English staires, plural of 'stair', from Old English 'stæger' (a flight of steps).
