candid

1. ADJ

/ ˈkændɪd / (AmE), / ˈkændɪd / (BrE)
telling the truth or saying what you really think, especially about something difficult or embarrassing; straightforward and without holding back
honest and straightforward

Examples

She gave a candid account of her experiences during the meeting.
I appreciate his candid feedback, even when it isn't exactly what I want to hear.
Her candid response surprised everyone in the room.

Synset Examples

his candid eyes
an open and trusting nature
a heart-to-heart talk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honest - truthful and sincere; not lying or cheating
  • Synonym: frank - open, honest, and direct in speech or writing
  • Antonym: guarded - careful not to give too much information; cautious

Etymology

From Latin candidus (white, pure, honest).

expressing opinions and feelings openly and honestly, without hiding anything or being insincere

Examples

3. ADJ

/ ˈkændɪd / (AmE), / ˈkændɪd / (BrE)
(of a photograph or interview) taken informally or without the subject's knowledge, showing people in a natural, unposed state.
natural, unposed (photograph/interview)

Examples

The article included several candid photos of the actors taken backstage.
She preferred giving candid interviews where she could speak honestly without preparation.
A candid shot of the children playing in the garden captured their genuine laughter.

Synset Examples

a candid photograph
a candid interview

Related Terms

  • Antonym: posed - deliberately arranged or planned, especially in photographs

Etymology

From Latin candidus (white, pure, honest).
