consoling

1. ADJ

/ kənˈsoʊlɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈsəʊlɪŋ / (BrE)
Giving comfort or solace to someone who is sad or distressed.
giving comfort or solace

Examples

Her consoling words helped him get through a tough day.
The dog offered a consoling presence during the family's grief.
He placed a consoling hand on her shoulder as she wept.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comforting - providing comfort or reassurance
  • Antonym: distressing - causing anxiety, sorrow, or pain

Etymology

Present participle of 'console', from Latin 'consolari' (to comfort).
