stealing

1. NOUN

/ ˈstiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of taking another person's property without permission or legal right; theft
theft; unlawful taking of property

Examples

The store installed cameras to help prevent stealing after hours.
He was caught stealing from his neighbor's garage.
Because of frequent stealing, the library now monitors all exits.

Synset Examples

the thieving is awful at Kennedy International

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theft - the act of stealing; general term for unlawfully taking property
  • Synonym: robbery - the act of stealing from a person or place, often with force or threat
  • Synonym: burglary - breaking into a building to commit theft

Etymology

Present participle of 'steal', from Old English 'stelan'.
