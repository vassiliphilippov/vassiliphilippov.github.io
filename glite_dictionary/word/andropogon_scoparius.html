Andropogon scoparius

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.drəˈpoʊ.ɡɑn skoʊˈpɛə.ri.əs / (AmE), / ˌan.drəˈpəʊ.ɡən skəʊˈpɛə.rɪ.əs / (BrE)
A species of hardy North American prairie grass with blue-green stems and leaves that turn bronze in autumn; also known as little bluestem.
a North American prairie grass species

Examples

The prairie restoration project included large plantings of Andropogon scoparius to support native wildlife.
In autumn, the leaves of Andropogon scoparius turn a striking bronze color across the fields.
Many gardeners choose Andropogon scoparius for its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: little bluestem - common name for Andropogon scoparius, the same species

Etymology

From Greek 'andros' (man) + 'pogon' (beard), referring to the beard-like appearance of some species; 'scoparius' from Latin for 'broom-like'.
