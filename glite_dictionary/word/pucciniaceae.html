Pucciniaceae

1. NOUN

/ puːˌtʃɪniˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / pjuːˌtʃɪnɪˈɑːsɪiː / (BrE)
A large family of rust fungi, many of which are plant pathogens.
family of rust fungi

Examples

The Pucciniaceae are responsible for many significant diseases in agricultural crops.
Researchers discovered a new member of the Pucciniaceae on wild grasses.
Understanding the life cycle of Pucciniaceae is crucial for managing rust infections in wheat.

Etymology

Named after Italian botanist Tommaso Puccini, with the suffix -aceae denoting a family in taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ puˌtʃɪniˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / pjuːˌtʃɪniˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a large and important family of rust fungi, many of which are plant pathogens
family of rust fungi

Examples

The Pucciniaceae include many species that infect crops like wheat and barley.
Scientists are studying the Pucciniaceae to understand how rust fungi spread among plants.
Several members of the Pucciniaceae are responsible for significant agricultural losses worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rust fungus - fungi of the order Pucciniales, often causing disease in plants

Etymology

Named after Italian botanist Tommaso Puccini with the standard botanical suffix -aceae for plant families.
