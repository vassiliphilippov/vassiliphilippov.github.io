feline

1. ADJ

/ ˈfiːlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈfiːlaɪn / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of cats
relating to cats

Examples

She admired the feline grace of the leopard as it moved through the trees.
The veterinarian specialized in treating feline diseases and conditions.
He was drawn to her feline features and elegant movements.

Synset Examples

feline fur

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catlike - resembling or characteristic of a cat
  • Antonym: canine - relating to or characteristic of dogs

Etymology

From Latin 'felinus' (of or belonging to a cat), from 'felis' (cat).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfiːlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈfiːlaɪn / (BrE)
a mammal belonging to the cat family (Felidae), typically with a slender body, round head, and often sharp retractile claws; includes domestic cats, lions, tigers, and related animals
a mammal of the cat family

Examples

The tiger, being a large feline, stalks its prey with incredible stealth.
She adopted several felines from the animal shelter, including both big and small cats.
Lions, leopards, and house cats all belong to the feline family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cat - a small domesticated mammal of the species Felis catus

Etymology

From Latin felinus (of a cat), from felis (cat).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
