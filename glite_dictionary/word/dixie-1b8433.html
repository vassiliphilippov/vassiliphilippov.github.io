dixie

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪk.si / (AmE), / ˈdɪk.si / (BrE)
a large metal pot, typically of 12-gallon capacity, used for cooking in military camps
large military camp cooking pot
british

Examples

The soldiers boiled their tea in the big dixie by the campfire.
After a long march, dinner was served straight from the dixie.
Every morning, the cook would clean the dixie before preparing breakfast.

Etymology

Originally British military slang, possibly from the Hindi/Urdu 'degchi' (a small pot).
