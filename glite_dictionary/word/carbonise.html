to carbonise

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑːr.bəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.bə.naɪz / (BrE)
To convert (an organic substance) into carbon, usually by heating or burning it in the absence of air.
convert into carbon by heating

Examples

Engineers developed a process to carbonise wood for use in water filters.
If paper is left too long in the kiln, the heat will carbonise it completely.
Certain manufacturing techniques carbonise fabric to make it more durable.

Synset Examples

carbonize coal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to carbonize - convert into carbon, usually by heating (US spelling)

Etymology

From French *carboniser*, based on 'carbone' (carbon).

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑrbəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbənʌɪz / (BrE)
to unite or combine a substance with carbon, especially by chemical treatment or heating
add carbon to a substance

Examples

Steel can be strengthened when it is carbonised during manufacturing.
The process will carbonise the surface of the metal, making it harder and more durable.
Some fuels are made by carbonising organic material at high temperatures.

Synset Examples

carburize metal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to carburize - add carbon to a metal, especially steel, by heating
  • Antonym: to decarburize - remove carbon from a metal or substance

Etymology

From 'carbon' + '-ise', patterned after French 'carboniser'.
