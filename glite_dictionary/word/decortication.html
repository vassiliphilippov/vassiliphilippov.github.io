decortication

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌkɔːrtɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌkɔːtɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the removal of the outer covering from an organ, structure, or part, especially in a medical or biological context
removal of outer covering (medical)
medical

Examples

The surgeon performed a decortication to relieve pressure around the patient's lung.
In botanical studies, decortication is used to examine the inner structure of seeds.
After a successful decortication, the patient's symptoms improved significantly.

Etymology

From Latin decorticatio (stripping of bark), from de- (removal) + cortex (bark).
