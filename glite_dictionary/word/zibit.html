Zibit

1. NOUN

/ ˈziːˌbɪt / (AmE), / ˈziːbɪt / (BrE)
a unit of digital information equal to 1,024 exbibits, or 2 to the power of 70 bits
unit of digital information (2^70 bits)

Examples

One Zibit represents a massive amount of data storage in modern computing.
Data centers rarely handle files that reach even a single Zibit.
The upgrade increased their network capacity to several Zibits per second.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: exabit - a unit of information equal to 2^60 bits
  • Degree Higher: yobibit - a unit of information equal to 2^80 bits

Etymology

Blend of 'zebi-' (from 'zeta binary') and 'bit'.
