to enliven

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈlaɪvən / (AmE), / ɪnˈlaɪv(ə)n / (BrE)
to make something more interesting, lively, or intense; to give life, energy, or excitement to something
make more lively or intense

Examples

Her witty remarks enlivened the conversation and made everyone laugh.
A splash of color would really enliven this dull room.
The music enlivens the party, turning it into a lively celebration.

Synset Examples

These paintings exalt the imagination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: animate - give life or energy to
  • Synonym: to invigorate - give strength, energy, or vitality to
  • Antonym: dull - make lacking in interest or excitement

Etymology

From en- (make) + lively; formed in English in the 16th century.

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈlaɪvən / (AmE), / ɪnˈlaɪvən / (BrE)
to make something more lively, interesting, or cheerful
make more lively or interesting

Examples

Her humor helped to enliven the dull meeting.
The artist used bright colors to enliven the portrait.
A little music will enliven the party.

Synset Examples

let's liven up this room a bit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: animate - to give life or spirit to; make lively
  • Synonym: to liven - to make or become more lively or interesting
  • Antonym: dull - to make or become less lively or interesting

Etymology

Formed from 'en-' (make) + 'liven' (from 'life').
