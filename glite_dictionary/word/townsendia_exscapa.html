Townsendia exscapa

1. NOUN

/ taʊnˈsɛndiə ɛkˈskeɪpə / (AmE), / taʊnˈsɛndiə ɛkˈskeɪpə / (BrE)
A small, nearly stemless North American herb with a rosette of woolly leaves and large white-rayed flower heads, native to central Canada and the western United States.
species of tufted, nearly stemless North American herb

Examples

The rocky slopes were covered with Townsendia exscapa, their white-rayed blossoms bright in the sun.
Researchers studied how Townsendia exscapa survives in its dry prairie habitat.
She found a rare specimen of Townsendia exscapa growing near the creek.

Etymology

Townsendia: named for American botanist David Townsend (1787–1858); species epithet from Latin 'exscapa', meaning 'without a scape' (flower stalk).
