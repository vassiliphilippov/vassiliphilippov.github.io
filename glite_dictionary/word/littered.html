littered

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪtɚd / (AmE), / ˈlɪtəd / (BrE)
covered or scattered with a large number of things in an untidy way, especially rubbish or debris.
covered with scattered objects or rubbish

Examples

After the parade, the street was littered with confetti and empty bottles.
Her desk is always littered with papers and old coffee cups.
The beaches were littered with plastic waste after the storm.

Synset Examples

the storm left the driveway littered with sticks and debris
his library was a cluttered room with piles of books on every chair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cluttered - filled with a messy accumulation of things
  • Antonym: clean - free from disorder or unwanted objects

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'litter', from Middle English 'litteren' (to provide with bedding), from Old French 'litiere'.
