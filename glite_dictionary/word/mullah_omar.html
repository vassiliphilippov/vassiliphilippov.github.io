Mullah Omar

1. PROPN

/ ˈmʌlɑː oʊˈmɑːr / (AmE), / ˈmʌlɑː ˈəʊmɑː / (BrE)
(c. 1950–2013) Reclusive Afghan political and religious leader, founder and head of the Taliban, who imposed strict sharia law in Afghanistan during his rule.
Afghan Taliban leader (c. 1950–2013)

Examples

Mullah Omar led the Taliban during their rule over Afghanistan from 1996 to 2001.
Many international agencies sought information about the whereabouts of Mullah Omar.
The leadership style of Mullah Omar had a lasting impact on Afghanistan's political landscape.

Etymology

Mullah: from Arabic 'mullā' (learned man); Omar: Arabic personal name.
