rhabdosarcoma

1. NOUN

/ ræbˌdoʊsɑːrˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / ræbˌdəʊsɑːˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
A highly malignant tumor that arises from striated muscle tissue.
malignant tumor of striated muscle
medical

Examples

The doctors diagnosed the child with rhabdosarcoma, which required immediate and aggressive treatment.
Research into rhabdosarcoma has led to new therapies for muscle-derived cancers.
A biopsy confirmed the mass was a rhabdosarcoma rather than a benign muscle growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sarcoma - a malignant tumor of connective or other non-epithelial tissue

Etymology

From Greek 'rhabdos' (rod) and 'sarcoma' (fleshy tumor).
