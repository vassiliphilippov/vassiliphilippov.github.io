Kazakhstani

1. NOUN

/ kəˌzɑkˈstɑni / (AmE), / kəˌzækˈstɑːni / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Kazakhstan
native or resident of Kazakhstan

Examples

The Kazakhstani greeted visitors with warmth and hospitality.
Many Kazakhstanis are proud of their multicultural heritage.
She met a Kazakhstani at the international conference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kazakh - a member of the Kazakh ethnic group or the Kazakh language

Etymology

Formed from Kazakhstan + English suffix -i (denoting nationality).

2. ADJ

/ kəˌzækˈstɑːni / (AmE), / kazækˈstɑːni / (BrE)
Relating to Kazakhstan, its people, or its culture.
relating to Kazakhstan or its people

Examples

The Kazakhstani government announced a new cultural festival in Astana.
She specializes in Kazakhstani history and literature at university.
Many Kazakhstani athletes competed in the international sports tournament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kazakh - a member of a Turkic ethnic group from Kazakhstan

Etymology

From Kazakhstan, the country name, with the suffix -i denoting association.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03089873-a
Synset POS: a
