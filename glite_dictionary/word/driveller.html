driveller

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪvələr / (AmE), / ˈdrɪvələ / (BrE)
a person who talks nonsense or utter trivial, pointless remarks
person who talks nonsense

Examples

He dismissed the old man as a driveller who never said anything meaningful.
The politician was accused of being a driveller during the heated debate.
She refused to listen to the driveller any longer and walked away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babble - incoherent or foolish talk
  • Synonym: chatterbox - a person who talks a lot, especially about trivial things

Etymology

From the verb 'drivel', from Middle English 'drivelen', related to drooling or slobbering, figuratively applied to foolish talk.
