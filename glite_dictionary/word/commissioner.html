commissioner

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmɪʃənər / (AmE), / kəˈmɪʃənə / (BrE)
an official in charge of a government department or a particular area of activity, especially in the public sector
government official in charge of a department

Examples

The city appointed a new commissioner to oversee public safety.
As commissioner of education, she introduced several major reforms.
The commissioner will address the concerns of local residents at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: administrator - a person responsible for managing an organization or part of one

Etymology

From French 'commissaire', from Latin 'commissarius' (person entrusted with a responsibility).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈmɪʃənər / (AmE), / kəˈmɪʃənə / (BrE)
an official appointed to a commission, especially as a member of a group responsible for managing or overseeing a particular area or activity
member of a commission

Examples

The city appointed a new commissioner to oversee public transportation improvements.
Each commissioner brought valuable expertise to the environmental review board.
As a commissioner, she attended quarterly meetings to discuss policy changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: director - person who manages an organization or department
  • Synonym: chairperson - person who leads a committee or organization

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman 'commissionaire' and Medieval Latin 'commissionarius', from 'commissio' (commission).
