Go

1. NOUN

/ ɡoʊ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ / (BrE)
a board game for two players who take turns placing black or white counters on the intersections of a grid, aiming to encircle and capture the opponent's pieces
strategic board game from East Asia

Examples

He has been playing Go competitively for ten years.
Go requires deep strategic thinking and patience.
They spent the afternoon learning how to play Go together.

Etymology

From Japanese 'go', ultimately from Chinese 'wéiqí' (圍棋, encircling game).
