Ladyship

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪdiˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈleɪdiʃɪp / (BrE)
a title or form of address used for a woman who holds the rank of a peeress below a duchess, such as a countess, viscountess, or baroness
title for a female peer (except duchess)

Examples

We await Ladyship's decision on the matter.
You must address the letter to Her Ladyship at the manor.
The servants bowed respectfully as Your Ladyship entered the room.

Synset Examples

Your Ladyship
Her Ladyship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lordship - title or address for a male peer, such as a baron or viscount

Etymology

From 'lady' + 'ship', using the suffix to denote rank or office, common in titles such as Lordship.
