julep

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːləp / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːlɪp / (BrE)
a sweet cocktail, especially one made with bourbon, sugar, mint, and crushed ice, notably served in the southern United States
sweet mint and bourbon cocktail

Examples

She ordered a refreshing julep at the bar during the hot summer afternoon.
The Kentucky Derby is famous for its traditional julep cocktails served over crushed ice.
He carefully muddled the mint before adding it to the julep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cocktail - an alcoholic mixed drink

Etymology

From Arabic *julāb* (rose water), via Old French *julep*.
