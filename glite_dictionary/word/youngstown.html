Youngstown

1. PROPN

/ ˈjʌŋs.taʊn / (AmE), / ˈjʌŋz.taʊn / (BrE)
A city in northeast Ohio, United States, noted for its industrial history.
city in northeast Ohio, USA

Examples

Youngstown was once considered the heart of the American steel industry.
Her family moved from Youngstown to Cleveland when she was a child.
The documentary explores how Youngstown coped with the decline of manufacturing jobs.

Etymology

Named after founder John Young, who established the community in the late 18th century.
