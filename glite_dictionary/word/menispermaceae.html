Menispermaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛnɪspərˈmeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌmɛnɪspɜːˈmeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical and subtropical climbing plants, typically herbaceous or woody, including many lianas.
a family of climbing plants
medical

Examples

The Menispermaceae family is well known for its medicinal compounds derived from various species.
Many Menispermaceae are found climbing trees in tropical rainforests.
Researchers study the chemistry of Menispermaceae to explore new drug possibilities.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'mēniskos' (crescent) and 'sperma' (seed), referring to the shape of the seeds in many species.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛnɪspərˈmeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌmɛnɪspəˈmeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of mainly tropical herbaceous or woody climbing plants, often with kidney-shaped seeds, known as the moonseed family
family of climbing plants; moonseed family

Examples

Members of the Menispermaceae are mostly found in tropical regions around the world.
The Menispermaceae include several plants used in traditional medicine.
Botanists conducted a study on the climbing mechanisms in Menispermaceae.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Menispermum (the type genus) and -aceae (denoting plant family).
