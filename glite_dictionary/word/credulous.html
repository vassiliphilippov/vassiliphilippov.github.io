credulous

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrɛdʒʊləs / (AmE), / ˈkrɛd.jʊ.ləs / (BrE)
too ready to believe things and therefore easily tricked or deceived; tending to believe with little evidence
easily fooled; gullible

Examples

Only the most credulous tourists would fall for such an obvious scam.
She gave a credulous look as she listened to the wild story.
Children are often credulous and believe whatever adults tell them.

Synset Examples

the gimmick would convince none but the most credulous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gullible - easily persuaded to believe something; easily deceived
  • Antonym: skeptical - not easily convinced; having doubts or reservations

Etymology

From Latin 'credulus' (believing), from 'credere' (to believe).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkrɛdʒələs / (AmE), / ˈkrɛd.jʊ.ləs / (BrE)
having or showing too great a readiness to believe things, especially without adequate evidence or due judgment
too ready to believe things

Examples

Despite repeated warnings, he remained credulous and fell for every scam.
Children can be especially credulous, often believing stories without question.
The journalist was not credulous enough to accept the politician's claims without verification.

Synset Examples

so credulous he believes everything he reads

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gullible - easily fooled or deceived
  • Antonym: skeptical - not easily convinced; having doubts

Etymology

From Latin credulus (ready to believe), from credere (to believe).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02280055-s
Synset POS: s
