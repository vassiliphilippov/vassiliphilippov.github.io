Turkestan

1. PROPN

/ ˌtɜrkəˈstæn / (AmE), / ˌtɜːkɪˈstæn / (BrE)
a historical region of Central Asia, formerly a major center for trade between the East and the West
historical region in Central Asia

Examples

Many ancient trade routes passed through Turkestan, connecting China and Europe.
The rich cultural heritage of Turkestan reflects centuries of interaction between diverse peoples.
Historians often study Turkestan for its role in the Silk Road trade network.

Etymology

From Persian 'Turkistān', meaning 'land of the Turks' (Turk + -stan 'land').
