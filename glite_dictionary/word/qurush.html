qurush

1. NOUN

/ kuˈruːʃ / (AmE), / kuˈruːʃ / (BrE)
A monetary unit of Saudi Arabia, equal to one twentieth of a riyal.
Saudi Arabian currency unit

Examples

A cup of coffee in Riyadh might cost five qurush.
The price increased by twenty qurush over the past year.
He exchanged a few qurush for a snack at the market.

Etymology

From Arabic 'qurush', plural of 'qirsh', meaning 'small coin'.
