consanguineous

1. ADJ

/ kɑn.sæŋˈɡwɪ.ni.əs / (AmE), / kɒn.sæŋˈɡwɪː.ni.əs / (BrE)
(of people) relating to or descended from the same ancestor; related by blood.
related by blood

Examples

The two families were discovered to be consanguineous through their shared great-grandparents.
Certain genetic disorders are more common in consanguineous marriages.
The law prohibits consanguineous unions in order to prevent hereditary diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: related - connected by blood or marriage
  • Antonym: unrelated - not related by blood or kinship

Etymology

From Latin consanguineus (of the same blood), from con- (together) + sanguis (blood).
