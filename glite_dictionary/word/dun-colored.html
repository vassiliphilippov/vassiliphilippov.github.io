dun-colored

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʌn ˌkʌlɚd / (AmE), / ˈdʌn ˌkʌləd / (BrE)
having a dull grayish-brown color resembling dun
of a dull grayish-brown color

Examples

The horse was a striking dun-colored mare with a black mane and tail.
She wore a dun-colored coat that blended with the winter landscape.
The dun-colored clouds signaled the approach of a rainstorm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dun - a dull grayish-brown color

Etymology

From 'dun' (meaning dull grayish-brown) + 'colored'. 'Dun' from Middle English 'dunn', of Celtic origin.
