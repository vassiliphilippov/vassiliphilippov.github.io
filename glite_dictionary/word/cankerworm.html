cankerworm

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæŋkərˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈkaŋkəˌwɜːm / (BrE)
A green caterpillar of a geometrid moth, known as a pest of various fruit and shade trees, especially through its feeding on leaves.
green caterpillar pest of trees

Examples

The outbreak of cankerworms caused significant damage to the orchard's apple trees.
Gardeners sprayed insecticide to protect their plants from cankerworm infestations.
Many shade trees in the city lost their leaves early because of a heavy cankerworm presence this spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inchworm - a caterpillar that moves by looping its body, also a type of geometrid larva
