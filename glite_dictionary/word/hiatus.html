hiatus

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈeɪtəs / (AmE), / haɪˈeɪtəs / (BrE)
A natural opening or passage through a bone or a membranous structure in the body.
anatomical opening or passage
medical

Examples

The esophageal hiatus is the opening in the diaphragm through which the esophagus passes.
The surgeon examined the hiatus in the bone for any abnormalities.
A hiatal hernia occurs when part of the stomach pushes up through the hiatus in the diaphragm.

Etymology

From Latin hiatus (opening, gap), from hiare (to gape, yawn).

2. NOUN

/ haɪˈeɪt̬əs / (AmE), / haɪˈeɪtəs / (BrE)
An interruption or missing section in a manuscript, text, or sequence, especially where part of the material is lost.
gap or missing part in a manuscript or sequence

Examples

There was a significant hiatus in the ancient manuscript where several pages were missing.
Scholars noted a hiatus in the poem, indicating part of the original text had been lost.
Because of the hiatus in the record, the history of that period is unclear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gap - an unfilled space or interval; a break in continuity

Etymology

From Latin hiatus (opening, gap), from hiare (to gape, open).

3. NOUN

/ haɪˈeɪɾəs / (AmE), / haɪˈeɪtəs / (BrE)
a pause or gap in a process, activity, or series, often a temporary interruption in continuity or intensity
a pause or interruption

Examples

After a long hiatus, the band returned with a new album.
The television show went on hiatus during the summer months.
Her research was delayed by a brief hiatus due to illness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pause - a temporary stop in an activity
  • Antonym: continuity - the uninterrupted flow of something

Etymology

From Latin hiatus (opening, gap), from hiare (to gape, yawn).
