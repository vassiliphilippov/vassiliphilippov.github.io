Cairina

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈriːnə / (AmE), / kaɪˈriːnə / (BrE)
A genus of ducks in the family Anatidae, including two known species.
genus of ducks

Examples

The muscovy duck is the most well-known species within the genus Cairina.
Scientists have studied the distribution of Cairina in Central and South America.
The conservation status of both Cairina species is assessed regularly by ornithologists.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'kairinos', meaning 'of the right time'.

2. NOUN

/ kaɪˈriːnə / (AmE), / kaɪˈriːnə / (BrE)
a genus of ducks in the family Anatidae, which includes species such as the Muscovy duck and the white-winged wood duck
genus of ducks (family Anatidae)

Examples

The Muscovy duck is classified under the genus Cairina in avian taxonomy.
Researchers observed a rare member of the Cairina genus in the wetlands of Southeast Asia.
The genus Cairina is distinguished from other Anatidae by particular features of bill and plumage.

Etymology

From Latinized form of a South American native word (possibly Tupi) for the Muscovy duck.
