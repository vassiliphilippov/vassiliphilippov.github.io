deciding

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsaɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈsaɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of making a choice or coming to a conclusion about something
the act of making a decision

Examples

Her deciding on which university to attend took several months.
Quick deciding is essential in emergency situations.
He left the deciding to his business partner.

Synset Examples

a good executive must be good at decision making

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decision-making - the process of making decisions
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05796154-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈsaɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈsaɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
having the power or quality of determining the outcome of something; conclusive
having determining power; conclusive

Examples

The committee's chairperson cast the deciding vote to break the tie.
It was the team's deciding game of the season, determining whether they would advance to the finals.
His injury in the deciding match changed the outcome of the tournament.

Synset Examples

cast the deciding vote
the determinative (or determinant) battle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: determinant - serving to determine or decide something
  • Synonym: decisive - able to make decisions quickly and confidently or producing a definite result

Synset POS: s
