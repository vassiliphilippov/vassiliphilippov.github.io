echogram

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛkoʊˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈɛkəʊˌɡram / (BrE)
an image produced by ultrasonography, used to visualize internal body structures such as organs or a fetus
ultrasound-produced image of body structures
medical

Examples

The doctor showed us the echogram to monitor the fetus's development.
Technicians rely on a clear echogram to identify abnormalities in internal organs.
After the examination, the echogram revealed no signs of heart disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ultrasonography - the technique of using ultrasound waves to create images of the inside of the body
  • Synonym: sonogram - an image produced by ultrasound examination, especially of a fetus

Etymology

From Greek 'ēkhō' (sound, echo) + '-gram' (record, drawing).
