cheddar

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛdər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛdə / (BrE)
a hard, smooth-textured cheese, originally made in the village of Cheddar in southwestern England, now produced worldwide
a type of hard cheese

Examples

She ordered a sandwich with melted cheddar and tomatoes for lunch.
Sharp cheddar adds a tangy flavor to many dishes.
Many people enjoy a slice of cheddar with crackers as a snack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cheddar cheese - full term for cheddar as a type of cheese

Etymology

Named after the village of Cheddar in Somerset, England, where it was first made.
