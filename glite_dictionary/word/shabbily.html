shabbily

1. ADV

/ ˈʃæbɪli / (AmE), / ˈʃæbɪli / (BrE)
In a mean, ungenerous, or unkind manner; with a lack of generosity or consideration.
in a mean or ungenerous manner

Examples

He treated his old friend shabbily, refusing to lend him a small sum of money.
She felt that she had been shabbily dismissed after years of loyal service.
The villagers were shabbily refused any compensation for their loss.

Synset Examples

the two were haggling shabbily in the drawing-room

Related Terms

  • Antonym: generously - in a way that shows a willingness to give more than expected or required

Etymology

From 'shabby' + '-ly', with 'shabby' possibly from Low German schabbig (scabbed).

2. ADV

/ ˈʃæbɪli / (AmE), / ˈʃæbɪli / (BrE)
In a way that appears worn, threadbare, or dilapidated.
in a worn or dilapidated manner

Examples

The old house was shabbily furnished, with peeling wallpaper and worn-out chairs.
He arrived shabbily dressed, attracting disapproving looks from the audience.
The man lived shabbily after losing his job, unable to afford new clothes.

Synset Examples

a shabbily dressed man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shabbily - in a careless or untidy manner
  • Antonym: smartly - in a neat and stylish manner

Etymology

From 'shabby' + '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00452786-r
Synset POS: r
