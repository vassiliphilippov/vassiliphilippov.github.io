Asplenium platyneuron

1. NOUN

/ æsˈpliːniəm ˌplætɪˈnɪərɒn / (AmE), / æsˈpliːniəm ˌplætɪˈnɪərɒn / (BrE)
A common North American fern with slender, upright fronds and distinctive polished black stalks.
species of North American fern

Examples

The Asplenium platyneuron grows well in shaded woodland gardens across eastern North America.
Many hikers can recognize Asplenium platyneuron by its shiny black stalks and narrow green fronds.
Botanists studied the distribution of Asplenium platyneuron in different forest habitats.

Etymology

From the genus name Asplenium (from Greek 'asplēnon', a kind of fern) and the species epithet 'platyneuron' (from Greek 'platys' meaning 'broad' and 'neuron' meaning 'nerve' or 'vein').
