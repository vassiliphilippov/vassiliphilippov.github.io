Bahai

1. NOUN

/ bəˈhaɪ / (AmE), / bəˈhɑːi / (BrE)
a follower or adherent of the Bahá'í faith, a monotheistic religion emphasizing the spiritual unity of humankind
a follower of the Bahá'í faith

Examples

He is a devoted Bahai serving his local community.
Several Bahais gathered to celebrate their holy day.
As a Bahai, she believes in the unity of all religions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bahaism - the monotheistic religion founded by Bahá'u'lláh

Etymology

From Persian Bahā’ī, from Bahāʾ (meaning 'glory', referring to Bahá'u'lláh, the founder) plus the suffix -i (denoting belonging).

2. ADJ

/ bɑːˈhaɪ / (AmE), / bɑːˈhaɪ / (BrE)
relating to the Baháʼí Faith, its followers, or its teachings.
of the Baháʼí Faith or its followers

Examples

The Bahai temple in Delhi is a famous landmark known for its lotus-like architecture.
He studied Bahai writings to understand the religion's principles of unity and peace.
Several Bahai festivals are celebrated annually by followers around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bahaism - the religion founded by Bahá’u’lláh in the 19th century

Etymology

From Persian بهائی (bahā’ī), relating to Bahá’u’lláh, founder of the Baháʼí Faith.
