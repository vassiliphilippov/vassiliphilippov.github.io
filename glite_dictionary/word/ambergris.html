ambergris

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmbərˌɡrɪs / (AmE), / ˈambəɡriː / (BrE)
A waxy, grayish substance produced in the intestines of sperm whales, often found floating at sea or washed ashore, and formerly used in the manufacture of perfumes.
waxy substance from sperm whales used in perfume

Examples

The rare ambergris was highly valued by perfumers in the eighteenth century.
He found a lump of ambergris washed up on the beach during his morning walk.
Scientists study ambergris to better understand the diet and biology of sperm whales.

Etymology

From Old French *ambre gris* (gray amber).
