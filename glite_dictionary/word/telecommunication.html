telecommunication

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛlɪkəˌmjunəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɛlɪkəˌmjuːnɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the transmission of information over long distances by electronic means, such as telephone, radio, or television; the field of technology concerned with such communication.
long-distance electronic communication

Examples

Advances in telecommunication have revolutionized the way we connect with each other.
She chose to study telecommunication engineering at university.
Improved telecommunication systems are essential for global business operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: communication - the act of exchanging information

Etymology

From French télécommunication, from Greek tele- (far) + Latin communicatio (communication).

2. NOUN

/ ˌtɛlɪkəˌmjunɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɛlɪkəˌmjuːnɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
communication over a distance by electronic means, especially using systems like telephone, radio, or television.
communication over distance by electronic means

Examples

Modern telecommunication has made it possible to connect with people all over the world in seconds.
The company specializes in providing telecommunication solutions to rural areas.
Advances in telecommunication have revolutionized how businesses operate globally.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: communication - the act of exchanging information or messages

Etymology

From French télécommunication, from Greek tele (far) + communication.
