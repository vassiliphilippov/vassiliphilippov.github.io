Y2K

1. NOUN

/ waɪ tuː keɪ / (AmE), / waɪ tuː keɪ / (BrE)
The year 2000, especially in reference to computer systems and the widespread concerns over the 'millennium bug' affecting data handling during the transition from 1999 to 2000.
the year 2000, often in tech contexts

Examples

Many businesses invested heavily in Y2K remediation to ensure their computer systems would function properly on January 1, 2000.
After Y2K, experts reviewed the effectiveness of preparations made to avoid potential disruptions.
The Y2K problem highlighted the importance of date handling in legacy computer software.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: millennium bug - a computer flaw related to the year 2000

Etymology

Short for 'Year 2000,' with 'Y' for 'year' and 'K' for 'kilo' (thousand).
