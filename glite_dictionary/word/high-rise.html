high-rise

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌraɪz / (BrE)
a tall building with many floors, usually containing offices or apartments
tall multistoried building

Examples

The city skyline is dominated by a new high-rise near the river.
She bought an apartment on the twelfth floor of a high-rise.
Many modern high-rises use environmentally friendly building materials.

Synset Examples

‘tower block’ is the British term for ‘high-rise’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tower block - a tall building with many floors for flats or offices (British term)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03524550-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈhaɪˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌraɪz / (BrE)
(of a building) having many stories and usually equipped with elevators; tall.
tall building with many stories

Examples

The city center features several high-rise office blocks that dominate the skyline.
They moved into a high-rise apartment overlooking the river.
Many high-rise buildings were constructed during the housing boom.

Synset Examples

avenues lined with high-rise apartment buildings

Related Terms

  • Antonym: low-rise - (of a building) having only a few stories
