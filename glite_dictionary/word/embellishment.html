embellishment

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈbɛlɪʃmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɛlɪʃmənt / (BrE)
the addition of decorative or imaginative details, often to make a story or account more interesting, sometimes at the expense of factual accuracy
addition of decorative or fictitious detail

Examples

The story of the haunted house gained new embellishments with each retelling.
His speech was impressive, but some embellishment of the facts was obvious.
The journalist's article contained more embellishment than objective reporting.

Synset Examples

the mystery has been heightened by many embellishments in subsequent retellings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ornamentation - the act of adding decorations; making something more attractive
  • Degree Higher: exaggeration - the act of making something seem more important or larger than it really is
  • Antonym: plainness - the quality of being simple or lacking adornment

Etymology

From Old French embellir (to embellish), from Latin bellus (pretty, handsome).

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˈbɛlɪʃmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɛlɪʃmənt / (BrE)
the act of adding decorative details or features to something to make it more attractive
adding decorative details

Examples

The cake was finished with delicate sugar flowers as an embellishment.
He added a few personal touches for embellishment to his story.
Without any embellishment, the room looked quite plain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decoration - something added to make something more attractive
  • Synonym: adornment - a detail or feature added to something to make it more attractive
  • Antonym: simplification - the process of making something simpler and plainer

Etymology

From Old French *embelissement*, from *embellir* (to beautify), based on Latin *bellus* (beautiful).

3. NOUN

/ ɪmˈbɛlɪʃmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɛlɪʃmənt / (BrE)
an unnecessary decorative detail or feature added to something to make it more attractive but not essential
unnecessary decorative detail or feature

Examples

The architect added a touch of embellishment to the otherwise simple façade.
She removed the unnecessary embellishments from the dress to keep it elegant.
The story was full of embellishments that made it more entertaining than factual.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decoration - something added to make something more attractive
  • Synonym: adornment - a decorative addition or ornament
  • Antonym: necessity - something that is necessary or essential

Etymology

From Old French embellissement, from embellir (to embellish), from Latin bellus (beautiful).
