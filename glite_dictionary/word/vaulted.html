vaulted

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɔltɪd / (AmE), / ˈvɔːltɪd / (BrE)
(of a ceiling, roof, or structure) having an arched or dome-shaped form.
having an arched or domed ceiling or roof

Examples

The cathedral's vaulted ceiling gave the entire space a sense of grandeur.
They admired the vaulted arches that supported the ancient stone bridge.
Sunlight streamed through the windows beneath the vaulted roof of the gallery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: domed - having a rounded or hemispherical roof or ceiling

Etymology

From Middle English, past participle of 'vault', derived from Old French 'voute', from Latin 'voluta' (a roll, spiral).
