William Tindale

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪljəm ˈtɪndeɪl / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm ˈtɪndeɪl / (BrE)
(1494–1536) English translator and Protestant martyr known for translating the Bible into English, influencing later versions such as the King James Bible.
English Bible translator and Protestant martyr

Examples

William Tindale was instrumental in making the Bible accessible to English-speaking people.
Many scholars study the impact of William Tindale's translations on later English versions of the Bible.
The life and work of William Tindale are often discussed in church history courses.
