to energize

1. VERB

/ ˈɛnərˌdʒaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛnədʒaɪz / (BrE)
to give energy or vitality to; to make someone feel more active or alert
give energy or make active

Examples

A quick walk in the morning can energize you for the day ahead.
The coach used encouraging words to energize the team before the game.
This new leadership style has really energized the whole company.

Synset Examples

Coffee and tea stimulate me
This herbal infusion doesn't stimulate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stimulate - to make someone or something more active or alert
  • Synonym: to invigorate - to make someone feel strong and full of energy
  • Antonym: to depress - to make someone feel less active or energetic

Etymology

From Greek energein (to work, activate), from energos (active).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00022679-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈɛnərˌdʒaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛnədʒaɪz / (BrE)
to increase the energy of something, especially by raising it to a higher energy state or level
raise to a higher energy level

Examples

The laser is used to energize the atoms in the gas.
Applying a voltage can energize the circuit and cause electrons to flow.
Microwave radiation can energize water molecules, making them vibrate faster.

Synset Examples

excite the atoms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to excite - to increase the energy level of particles or atoms

Etymology

From Greek 'energein' (to work), via Medieval Latin 'energizare'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00560179-v
Synset POS: v
