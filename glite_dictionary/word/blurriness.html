blurriness

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɜːri.nəs / (AmE), / ˈblʌr.i.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being indistinct or lacking sharpness in detail or outline
the state of being blurry

Examples

The blurriness of the photo made it hard to recognize who was in it.
Eye strain can sometimes cause temporary blurriness in your vision.
The scene was filled with a dreamy blurriness that gave it a surreal quality.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear or easy to see
  • Antonym: sharpness - the quality of having clear, distinct edges or detail
  • Synonym: blur - an indistinct or hazy appearance or effect

Etymology

From 'blurry' + '-ness'.
