to italicize

1. VERB

/ ɪˈtæləˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈtælɪsaɪz / (BrE)
to print or write in italic type
print or write in italics

Examples

The publisher decided to italicize all foreign words in the manuscript.
Please italicize the book titles in your essay.
She italicized the important points for emphasis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: romanize - to print in ordinary roman type, as opposed to italics

Etymology

Formed from 'italic' (type style) + '-ize'.

2. VERB

/ ɪˈtælɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈtælɪsaɪz / (BrE)
to print or write (text) in italics, especially to emphasize particular words or phrases
set text in italics for emphasis

Examples

You should italicize book titles in your bibliography.
Please italicize any technical terms the first time you use them.
Writers often italicize foreign words to distinguish them from the main text.

Synset Examples

To emphasize, you should italicize those key phrases.
Don't forget to italicize your citations in the essay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emphasize - to give special importance to something

Etymology

Formed from 'italic' (style of typeface) + '-ize' (verb-forming suffix).
