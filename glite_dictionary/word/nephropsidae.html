Nephropsidae

1. NOUN

/ nəˌfrɑpsɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / nɪˌfrɒpsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of clawed lobsters that includes the Norway lobster and related species.
family of clawed lobsters

Examples

The Nephropsidae family includes several species of commercially important lobsters.
Marine biologists studied the distribution of Nephropsidae along the continental shelf.
Fishermen in Europe catch large numbers of Nephropsidae, especially the Norway lobster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Homaridae - family of lobsters closely related to Nephropsidae

Etymology

From the genus name Nephrops, combined with the zoological family suffix -idae, from Greek roots: nephros (kidney) and ops (appearance).

2. NOUN

/ nəˌfrɑpsɪˈdiː / (AmE), / nɛˌfrɒpsɪˈdiː / (BrE)
a family of decapod crustaceans, including lobsters such as the Norway lobster; in some classifications coextensive with Homaridae.
a family of decapod crustaceans

Examples

The Nephropsidae family includes species like the Norway lobster, which is of commercial importance.
Scientists debated whether certain lobsters belong to the Nephropsidae or Homaridae.
Recent studies have updated the classification of the Nephropsidae based on genetic analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Homaridae - a family of crustaceans including true lobsters

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name 'Nephrops' and the suffix '-idae' used for animal family names.
