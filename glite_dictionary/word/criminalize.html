to criminalize

1. VERB

/ ˈkrɪm.ə.nəl.aɪz / (AmE), / ˈkrɪm.ɪ.nə.laɪz / (BrE)
to make an action or behavior illegal, or to treat someone as a criminal
declare illegal; treat as criminal

Examples

The new law will criminalize the sale of certain substances.
Activists argue that it is unfair to criminalize homelessness.
Several countries have recently criminalized texting while driving.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to legalize - to make something legal

Etymology

From crime + -al + -ize, modeled after criminal + -ize.

2. VERB

/ ˈkrɪmɪnəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkrɪmɪnəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make (an action or activity) illegal by making it a criminal offense by law
make illegal by law

Examples

Some governments have begun to criminalize hate speech to prevent violence.
The city council voted to criminalize public camping in parks.
It is controversial whether to criminalize the possession of certain substances.

Synset Examples

Marijuana is criminalized in the U.S.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to decriminalize - to make no longer a criminal offense
  • Synonym: outlaw - to make illegal by law

Etymology

From 'criminal' + '-ize', modeled on French 'criminaliser'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02486512-v
Synset POS: v
