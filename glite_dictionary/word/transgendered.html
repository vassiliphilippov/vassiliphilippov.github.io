transgendered

1. ADJ

/ trænzˈdʒɛndɚd / (AmE), / tranzˈdʒɛndəd / (BrE)
(of a person) having undergone a partial or complete change of gender, especially by medical or surgical means. Now considered outdated and often replaced by 'transgender'.
having undergone a change of gender (outdated)
archaic

Examples

The term transgendered is now considered outdated in most communities.
Several people who identified as transgendered shared their stories at the conference.
She described herself as transgendered in the early 2000s, but now prefers the term 'transgender'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transgender - relating to or being a person whose gender identity differs from the sex assigned at birth

Etymology

Formed from 'transgender' + '-ed'

2. NOUN

/ trænzˈdʒɛndɚd / (AmE), / trænzˈdʒɛndəd / (BrE)
a person who has undergone a sex reassignment operation to change from one gender to another
person who has had a sex change operation

Examples

The rights of transgendered individuals are increasingly being recognized worldwide.
She supports organizations that advocate for transgendered people and their families.
Discrimination against transgendered persons is still a concern in many countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transsexual - person who has undergone sex reassignment surgery
  • Antonym: cisgender - person whose gender identity matches their sex assigned at birth

Etymology

From 'transgender' + suffix '-ed', meaning having undergone the process.

Synset POS: n
