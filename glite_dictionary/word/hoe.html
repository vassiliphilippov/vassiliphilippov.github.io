hoe

1. VERB

/ hoʊ / (AmE), / həʊ / (BrE)
to use a hoe to break up, turn, or dig soil, especially in gardening or farming
use a hoe to dig or break soil

Examples

He spent the morning hoeing the vegetable garden.
It is important to hoe the soil before planting seeds.
She hoed around the tomato plants to remove weeds.

Synset Examples

He is hoeing the flower beds

Etymology

From Old English *hōh* (heel; projecting ridge of land), later influenced by Old French *hoe* (hoe), from Latin *hama* (hook).

2. NOUN

/ hoʊ / (AmE), / həʊ / (BrE)
(slang, offensive) A sexually promiscuous woman, used as a derogatory insult.
slang term for a promiscuous woman (offensive)
slang offensive

Examples

Some people use the word hoe as an insult to demean women.
The rapper's lyrics frequently use the term hoe in a derogatory way.
She was unfairly called a hoe by classmates because of rumors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whore - a woman who engages in sexual activity for payment (also used as insult)
  • Synonym: slut - an offensive term for a sexually promiscuous woman

Etymology

Alteration of 'whore', influenced by African-American Vernacular English (AAVE).

3. NOUN

/ hoʊ / (AmE), / həʊ / (BrE)
a gardening tool with a flat blade attached at right angles to a long handle, used for breaking up soil or removing weeds
gardening tool with flat blade

Examples

She used a hoe to loosen the soil in her vegetable garden.
After weeding the flowerbeds, he put the hoe back in the shed.
The old wooden handle on the hoe finally broke after years of use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rake - a tool with a row of metal or wooden teeth, used for gathering leaves or loosening soil

Etymology

From Middle English *hoe*, from Old French *hoe*, from Latin *hōs* (hoe, mattock).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03529754-n
Synset POS: n
