hypo

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪpoʊ / (AmE), / ˈhʌɪpəʊ / (BrE)
a chemical compound, especially sodium thiosulfate, used as a fixing agent in photographic developing
photographic fixing agent

Examples

The photographer used hypo to fix the negative after developing it.
Please refill the hypo tray before starting your next roll of film.
He explained that hypo is essential in making images permanent during the photographic process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sodium thiosulfate - a compound commonly used as 'hypo' in photography

Etymology

Shortened from hyposulphite (older name for thiosulfate), from Greek 'hypo' (under, less).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪpoʊ / (AmE), / ˈhʌɪpəʊ / (BrE)
a syringe fitted with a hypodermic needle for giving injections
syringe with a hypodermic needle
medical

Examples

The nurse prepared the hypo before administering the vaccine.
He carried a spare hypo in case of allergic reactions.
Doctors once used a glass hypo for injections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypodermic syringe - a syringe with a hollow needle used to inject substances under the skin

Etymology

Short for hypodermic syringe.
