stentor

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛn.tɔr / (AmE), / ˈstɛn.tɔː / (BrE)
A genus of large, trumpet-shaped ciliate protozoans found in freshwater, known for their contractile bodies and distinct shape.
trumpet-shaped freshwater protozoan

Examples

Scientists observed a stentor swimming gracefully under the microscope.
The stentors contracted rapidly when disturbed by a sudden change in light.
In biology class, we studied the structure of a stentor to understand ciliates.

Etymology

From Greek Stentōr, a mythological herald noted for his loud voice, alluding to the organism's trumpet-like shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɛntər / (AmE), / ˈstɛntə / (BrE)
a person with an exceptionally loud or powerful voice, especially a speaker
person with a very loud voice

Examples

The announcer was a real stentor, his voice echoing through the stadium with ease.
Her reputation as a stentor made her the obvious choice to lead the outdoor assembly.
Even in a noisy crowd, his stentor voice commanded everyone's attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orator - a public speaker, especially one who is skilled

Etymology

From Stentor, a Greek herald in the Iliad famous for his loud voice.
