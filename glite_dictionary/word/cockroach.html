cockroach

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑkˌroʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɒk.rəʊtʃ / (BrE)
a flat, brown or black insect that is active mainly at night and is often found in warm, humid places, sometimes as a domestic pest.
a nocturnal insect, often a household pest

Examples

As soon as she turned on the kitchen light, a cockroach scurried across the floor.
The apartment was infested with cockroaches hiding behind the refrigerator.
Scientists study the cockroach because of its remarkable ability to survive in extreme conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roach - an informal term for cockroach

Etymology

From Spanish 'cucaracha', of uncertain origin.
