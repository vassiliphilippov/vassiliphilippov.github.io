Jacquinia keyensis

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈkwɪniə kaɪˈɛnsɪs / (AmE), / dʒəˈkwɪnɪə kaɪˈɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree of the West Indies with leathery, soap-like leaves and very hard wood.
West Indian shrub or small tree

Examples

Researchers are studying the ecology of Jacquinia keyensis in its native habitat in the Caribbean.
The dense wood of Jacquinia keyensis is prized for its durability in woodworking.
You can identify Jacquinia keyensis by its leathery leaves and compact growth.

Etymology

Named after the genus Jacquinia, after Austrian botanist Nikolaus Joseph von Jacquin, and 'keyensis,' referring to the Florida Keys.
