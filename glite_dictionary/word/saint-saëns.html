Saint-Saëns

1. PROPN

/ sæ̃ ˈsæ̃(z), sæ̃ ˈsænz / (AmE), / sɛ̃ ˈsɑ̃ːz, sæ̃ ˈsænz / (BrE)
(1835–1921) A French composer and pianist known for works such as 'The Carnival of the Animals' and 'Danse Macabre'.
French pianist and composer (1835–1921)

Examples

Many orchestras feature works by Saint-Saëns in their concert repertoire.
Saint-Saëns composed 'The Carnival of the Animals' as a lighthearted suite for friends.
The music of Saint-Saëns combines romantic expressiveness with classical formality.

Etymology

From the French surname Saint-Saëns, after the composer's birthplace in Normandy.
