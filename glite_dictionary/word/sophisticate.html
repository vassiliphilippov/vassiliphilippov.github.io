sophisticate

1. VERB

/ səˈfɪstɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / səˈfɪstɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to use sophistry; to change the meaning of something or use ambiguous language in order to mislead or deceive
mislead using sophistry or ambiguity

Examples

When pressed for the truth, he tried to sophisticate his earlier statement to avoid admitting fault.
It is unethical for a lawyer to sophisticate the facts of the case.
Don't try to sophisticate your explanation—it only makes things more confusing.

Synset Examples

Don't twist my words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twist - to distort or misrepresent the truth or words
  • Antonym: to clarify - to make something clear or easier to understand

Etymology

From Greek *sophizesthai* (to become wise or subtle), through Latin *sophisticare* (to adulterate, to tamper with).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00934760-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ səˈfɪstɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / səˈfɪstɪkeɪt / (BrE)
A person who is knowledgeable, refined, and experienced in the ways of the world, especially in matters of culture or fashion.
worldly-wise, cultured person

Examples

The party was attended by every known sophisticate in the city.
As a true sophisticate, she navigated the art world with ease and confidence.
His reputation as a sophisticate preceded him wherever he traveled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sophisticated - having worldly experience and knowledge of fashion and culture

Etymology

From Medieval Latin sophisticatus (tampered with, sophisticated).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10644647-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ səˈfɪstɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / səˈfɪstɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to make (a person or thing) less natural, naive, or innocent, typically through exposure to experience or culture
make less natural or innocent

Examples

The city life tended to sophisticate the newcomers, changing their simple ways.
Traveling abroad can sophisticate a person's tastes and manners.
Some believe that too much education can sophisticate a child's innocence.

Synset Examples

Their manners had sophisticated the young girls

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to refine - to make someone or something more polished or cultured
  • Antonym: corrupt - to make someone or something less pure or innocent

Etymology

From Latin 'sophisticatus', past participle of 'sophisticare' (to tamper with); related to 'sophist'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02393906-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ səˈfɪstɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / səˈfɪstɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to make (something) more complex, refined, or advanced; to improve by introducing subtlety or complexity
make more complex or refined

Examples

The engineer tried to sophisticate the machine so it could handle multiple tasks at once.
As societies evolve, they often sophisticate their political systems and institutions.
Modern technology tends to sophisticate even the simplest devices over time.

Synset Examples

a sophisticated design

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to refine - improve something by making small changes, often to make it more sophisticated
  • Antonym: to simplify - make less complex, more simple

Etymology

From Latin 'sophisticatus', past participle of 'sophisticare' (to tamper with, to render artificial).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

5. VERB

/ səˈfɪstɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / səˈfɪstɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to alter something in order to make it impure, especially with the intention to deceive
to adulterate or make impure by deception

Examples

Unscrupulous manufacturers sometimes sophisticate wine by adding artificial flavorings.
Authorities discovered that the merchant had sophisticated the olive oil with lesser oils.
Efforts to sophisticate the product were quickly uncovered by industry inspectors.

Synset Examples

Sophisticate rose water with geraniol

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adulterate - to make something impure by adding inferior substances

Etymology

From Latin *sophisticatus* (tampered with), past participle of *sophisticare*, from *sophisticus* (sophistic).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00489366-v
Synset POS: v
