Lappish

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæpɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈlapɪʃ / (BrE)
any of the languages spoken by the Sámi (Lapp) people, generally classified as Uralic languages
language of the Sámi (Lapps)

Examples

The Lappish language is spoken across parts of Norway, Sweden, Finland, and Russia.
She decided to study Lappish as part of her research into Uralic languages.
Many traditional songs are still sung in Lappish by the Sámi people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sámi - the Uralic languages spoken by the Sámi people

Etymology

From 'Lapp' (an old term for the Sámi) + '-ish'.
