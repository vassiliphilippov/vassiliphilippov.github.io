Hedeoma pulegioides

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛdiˈoʊmə pjuːˌliːdʒiˈoʊdiːz / (AmE), / ˌhiːdiˈəʊmə pjuːˌliːdʒiˈəʊdiːz / (BrE)
A North American herb in the mint family with hairy, branching stems and purple-blue flowers, yielding an essential oil sometimes used as an insect repellent or in folk medicine.
a North American herb used for its oil as insect repellent

Examples

Hedeoma pulegioides is sometimes used in folk remedies and to repel insects naturally.
Botanists collected samples of Hedeoma pulegioides along the forest edge for study.
The essential oil extracted from Hedeoma pulegioides should be used cautiously, as it can be toxic in large amounts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American pennyroyal - Common name for Hedeoma pulegioides

Etymology

From the genus name Hedeoma (Greek hēdys, 'sweet' + ōma, 'odor') and Latin pulegioides ('resembling pennyroyal').
