peristome

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛrəˌstoʊm / (AmE), / ˈpɛrɪstəʊm / (BrE)
(in botany) The fringe of toothlike appendages surrounding the mouth of a moss capsule, aiding in spore release.
fringe around moss capsule mouth
medical

Examples

The peristome regulates the release of spores from the moss capsule.
Under the microscope, the peristome appears as a series of tiny, toothlike structures.
Botanists often examine the peristome to identify different species of moss.

Etymology

From Greek 'peri-' (around) + 'stoma' (mouth).

the area or region surrounding the mouth in certain invertebrate animals or the opening of certain plant structures such as moss capsules

medical

Examples

The scientist studied the structure of the peristome in various marine worms.
Mosses use their peristome to help disperse spores when conditions are right.
