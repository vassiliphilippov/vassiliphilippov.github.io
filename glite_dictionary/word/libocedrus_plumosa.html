Libocedrus plumosa

1. NOUN

/ ˌlaɪboʊˈsiːdrəs pluːˈmoʊsə / (AmE), / ˌlɪbəˈsiːdrəs pluːˈməʊsə / (BrE)
A species of timber tree native to New Zealand, resembling the cypress and valued for its wood.
New Zealand timber tree resembling cypress

Examples

The native Libocedrus plumosa thrives in the moist forests of New Zealand's North Island.
Timber from Libocedrus plumosa is prized for its durability in construction and carpentry.
Many conservationists are working to protect Libocedrus plumosa from habitat loss and overexploitation.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Libocedrus' (from Greek 'libanos' meaning 'frankincense' and 'kedros' meaning 'cedar'); species name 'plumosa' meaning 'feathery'.
