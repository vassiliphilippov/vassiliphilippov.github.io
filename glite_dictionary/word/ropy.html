ropy

1. ADJ

/ ˈroʊpi / (AmE), / ˈrəʊpi / (BrE)
(British informal) Of very poor quality; not good.
of very poor quality
informal british

Examples

The meal at that restaurant was ropy and left us disappointed.
He gave a ropy performance during last night's concert.
The weather has been pretty ropy all week, with constant drizzle.

Synset Examples

ropey food

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bad - of poor quality or a low standard
  • Antonym: excellent - of extremely high quality

2. ADJ

/ ˈroʊ.pi / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.pi / (BrE)
resembling rope, especially in being long, strong, or having a stringy or fibrous texture.
long and strong like rope

Examples

The dough became ropy after being overworked and stretched too much.
His ropy muscles stood out as he lifted the heavy box with ease.
A ropy strand of molten cheese stretched from the pizza to his mouth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stringy - having a string-like texture or appearance
  • Synonym: cordlike - resembling a cord in form or texture

Etymology

From rope + -y, formed in English to mean 'like rope'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈroʊpi / (AmE), / ˈrəʊpi / (BrE)
forming long, sticky threads; having a viscous or glutinous, thread-like quality
forming sticky, thread-like strands

Examples

After sitting out for days, the honey became ropy and difficult to pour.
The scientist described the bacteria's secretions as ropy and glue-like.
Cheese left in warm conditions sometimes develops a ropy texture that is unappetizing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viscous - having a thick, sticky consistency

Etymology

From rope + -y, meaning 'resembling rope'.
