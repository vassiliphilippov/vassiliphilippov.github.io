gloom

1. NOUN

/ ɡlum / (AmE), / ɡluːm / (BrE)
A feeling of deep sadness or depression, often accompanied by anxiety or apprehension about the future.
a feeling of sadness or melancholy

Examples

A sense of gloom settled over the room after they heard the disappointing news.
He tried to shake off the gloom that had followed him all week.
Despite the gloom in her eyes, she managed a small smile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depression - a state of severe sadness or hopelessness
  • Antonym: cheerfulness - a state of being noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From Middle English 'gloome' or 'gloume', probably from Old Norse 'glúmr' (moon), later influenced by connotations of darkness.

3. NOUN

/ ɡlum / (AmE), / ɡluːm / (BrE)
Partial or total darkness, especially when it creates an atmosphere of sadness or mystery.
partial or total darkness

Examples

The old house was filled with gloom after the sun went down.
Fog and gloom made it hard to see the path ahead.
She turned on the lamp to chase away the evening gloom.

Synset Examples

he struck a match to dispel the gloom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: darkness - the absence of light
  • Antonym: light - the brightness that makes things visible

Etymology

From Middle English 'gloum', related to Old Norse 'glúmr' (darkness).
