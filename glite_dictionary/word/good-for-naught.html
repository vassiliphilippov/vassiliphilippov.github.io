good-for-naught

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡʊd fər ˈnɔt / (AmE), / ˌɡʊd fə ˈnɔːt / (BrE)
An idle or worthless person; someone regarded as having no value or usefulness.
idle or worthless person

Examples

The old man called his neighbor's son a good-for-naught who refused to find a job.
Tired of being treated like a good-for-naught, she finally decided to prove herself.
They dismissed Mark as a good-for-naught, but he eventually started his own business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: good-for-nothing - An idle or worthless person

Etymology

Variant of 'good-for-nothing', combining 'good' (of value), 'for', and 'naught' (nothing, zero).
