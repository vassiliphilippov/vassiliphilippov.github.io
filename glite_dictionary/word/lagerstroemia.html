Lagerstroemia

1. NOUN

/ læɡərˈstriːmiə / (AmE), / læɡəˈstriːmiə / (BrE)
a genus of shrubs or small trees native to tropical Asia and Africa, usually with showy white, pink, or purplish flowers; commonly known as crape myrtle.
genus of flowering shrubs and small trees (crape myrtle)

Examples

The Lagerstroemia in her garden bloomed with hundreds of delicate pink flowers this summer.
Many landscapers recommend planting Lagerstroemia for its long-lasting, colorful blossoms.
Botanists have identified several species of Lagerstroemia native to Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crape myrtle - common name for plants in the Lagerstroemia genus

Etymology

Named after Swedish merchant Magnus von Lagerström and the Greek -stroemia (relating to turning, referencing genus suffixes).
