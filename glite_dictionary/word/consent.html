consent

1. VERB

/ kənˈsɛnt / (AmE), / kənˈsɛnt / (BrE)
to give permission or agree to do something
agree or give permission

Examples

After much discussion, she finally consented to the proposal.
The chairman will not act unless all members consent.
He refused at first, but later consented to help with the arrangements.

Synset Examples

I cannot accept your invitation
I go for this resolution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to agree - have the same opinion or give approval
  • Antonym: refuse - to indicate unwillingness or reject

Etymology

From Latin consentire (to agree), from con- (together) + sentire (to feel).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈsɛnt / (AmE), / kənˈsɛnt / (BrE)
permission for something to happen or agreement to do something, especially given formally or explicitly
permission or agreement

Examples

The patient gave her consent before the operation could proceed.
Parental consent is required for students under 18 to participate in the trip.
Without written consent, the data cannot be shared with third parties.

Synset Examples

he indicated his consent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approval - agreement or official permission
  • Antonym: refusal - denial or rejection of permission or agreement

Etymology

From Latin 'consensus' (agreement, accord), from 'consentire' (to agree, feel together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06702412-n
Synset POS: n
