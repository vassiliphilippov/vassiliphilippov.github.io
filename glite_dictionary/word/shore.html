shore

1. NOUN

/ ʃɔr / (AmE), / ʃɔː / (BrE)
the land along the edge of a sea, lake, or other large body of water
land at the edge of water

Examples

We walked for hours along the shore, collecting seashells as we went.
The boat drifted slowly toward the shore after the storm.
Children were playing on the shore while their parents watched nearby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coast - the land next to the sea; seashore
  • Synonym: beach - a pebbly or sandy shore, especially by the sea

Etymology

From Old English 'scor' (coast, shore), of Germanic origin.

2. VERB

/ ʃɔr / (AmE), / ʃɔː / (BrE)
(of a ship, boat, or passenger) to arrive at or reach the shore; to land.
to arrive at or reach the shore

Examples

The boat will shore at dawn after crossing the lake.
After weeks at sea, the sailors were relieved to finally shore safely.
Several canoes shored on the sandy beach during the storm.

Synset Examples

The ship landed in Pearl Harbor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: land - to arrive at or reach land, especially from a journey by sea or air

3. NOUN

/ ʃɔr / (AmE), / ʃɔː / (BrE)
a beam or timber that is placed against a building or structure to support it and prevent collapse
support beam for a structure

Examples

The workers placed a shore against the damaged wall to prevent it from collapsing.
Several wooden shores were used to support the ceiling during renovations.
A steel shore was installed to brace the sagging roof temporarily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prop - a rigid support used to keep something in position

Etymology

From Middle English 'shoren', from Old English 'scora' (prop, support).

Source: WordNet

4. VERB

/ ʃɔr / (AmE), / ʃɔː / (BrE)
to support or strengthen a structure by placing something solid against it for reinforcement or stability
to support or reinforce with a prop

Examples

Workers used beams to shore the crumbling wall and prevent its collapse.
Engineers had to shore up the bridge after the earthquake for safety reasons.
She tried to shore her argument with several convincing examples.

Synset Examples

shore and buttress an old building

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttress - to support or strengthen, especially with a projecting structure
  • Antonym: to undermine - to weaken or destabilize (the opposite process)

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse *skor* (a prop, shore).
