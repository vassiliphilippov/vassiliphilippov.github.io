migrant

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪɡrənt / (AmE), / ˈmaɪɡrənt / (BrE)
a person who moves from one region or country to another, especially to find work or better living conditions
person who moves to a different region or country

Examples

The country has seen an influx of migrants from neighboring regions in recent years.
Many migrant workers travel long distances to find seasonal employment on farms.
She spoke passionately about the challenges faced by migrants adapting to a new country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immigrant - a person who comes to live permanently in a foreign country
  • Synonym: refugee - a person forced to leave their country due to war or persecution
  • Antonym: native - a person born in a specified place or associated with a place by birth

Etymology

From Latin migrans, migrantis, present participle of migrare (to move, migrate).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmaɪɡɹənt / (AmE), / ˈmaɪɡɹənt / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) habitually moving from place to place, especially in search of seasonal work or better living conditions.
habitually moving from place to place

Examples

The migrant workers traveled south for the harvest each year.
Authorities set up temporary housing for the migrant population during the planting season.
She researched the challenges faced by migrant families moving between farming communities.

Synset Examples

appalled by the social conditions of migrant life
migratory workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: migratory - moving from one place to another at different times of the year, especially of animals

Etymology

From Latin migrans, migrantis, present participle of migrare (to move from place to place).
