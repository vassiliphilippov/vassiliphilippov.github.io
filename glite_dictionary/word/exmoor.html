Exmoor

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛksˌmʊr / (AmE), / ˈɛksˌmɔː / (BrE)
A breed of hardy, horned sheep native to Devon, England, valued especially for its mutton.
breed of sheep from Devon, England

Examples

The farm specializes in raising Exmoor for their excellent quality mutton.
An Exmoor is well adapted to the rugged terrain of southern England.
Many British farmers prize the Exmoor for its resilience and flavorful meat.

Etymology

Named after Exmoor, a region in Devon, England.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛksˌmɔr / (AmE), / ˈɛksˌmɔː / (BrE)
a small, sturdy breed of pony native to southwest England, characterized by a fawn-colored nose and a thick winter coat
stocky breed of pony from southwest England

Examples

The Exmoor is well adapted to the harsh weather conditions of its native moorland.
Children enjoyed riding the gentle Exmoor ponies at the county fair.
Conservationists work hard to protect the rare Exmoor breed in England.

Etymology

Named after the Exmoor region in southwest England, where the breed originated.
