mikado

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈkɑːdoʊ / (AmE), / mɪˈkɑːdəʊ / (BrE)
the emperor of Japan, especially when regarded as a religious leader; a term used chiefly in historical contexts
the emperor of Japan (historical term)

Examples

During the Meiji period, the mikado played a vital role in Japan's modernization.
Many traditional ceremonies were held at the court of the mikado.
The foreign ambassadors were received with great honor by the mikado.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emperor - a sovereign ruler of great power and rank
  • Synonym: tenno - official Japanese title for the emperor as a religious leader

Etymology

From Japanese 'mikado' (literally 'honourable gate'), formerly used as a title for the emperor of Japan.
