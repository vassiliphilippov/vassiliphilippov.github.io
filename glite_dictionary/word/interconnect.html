to interconnect

1. VERB

/ ˌɪntərkəˈnɛkt / (AmE), / ˌɪntəkəˈnɛkt / (BrE)
to connect with each other so that things are linked or joined together
connect together; be linked

Examples

Modern communication systems interconnect people across the globe in real time.
The rooms in the hotel interconnect by sliding doors to form a large suite.
All devices in this network are interconnected for seamless data sharing.

Synset Examples

The bones are interconnected via the muscle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to connect - to join or link together
  • Antonym: disconnect - to separate or break the connection between

Etymology

From Latin inter- (between) + connectere (to bind together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02629050-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌɪntɚkəˈnɛkt / (AmE), / ˌɪntəkəˈnɛkt / (BrE)
to connect two or more things with each other so that they work together or are linked
connect things together

Examples

The new system will interconnect all the regional offices for smoother communication.
Modern devices are designed to interconnect seamlessly with one another.
The project aims to interconnect different modes of transportation in the city.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disconnect - to separate or break the connection between things
  • Synonym: to connect - to join two or more things together

Etymology

From Latin 'inter-' (between) + 'connect' (to join together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01358349-v
Synset POS: v
