jejunity

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪˈdʒunəti / (AmE), / dʒɪˈdʒuːnɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of lacking in nutritive value or substance; emptiness or insubstantiality
lack of nutritive value or substance
archaic

Examples

Despite the colorful packaging, the cereal's jejunity left consumers unsatisfied and hungry soon after breakfast.
The critic remarked on the jejunity of the novel, noting its lack of intellectual nourishment.
Scholars debated the jejunity of the new curriculum, arguing it offered little substantive value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jejuneness - the quality of being jejune; lack of substance or interest

Etymology

From Late Latin jejunitas (fasting, emptiness), from jejunus (fasting, empty).

2. NOUN

/ dʒɪˈdʒuːnɪti / (AmE), / dʒɪˈdʒuːnɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being dull, simplistic, or lacking interest or sophistication.
dullness; lack of sophistication

Examples

The speech was criticized for its jejunity and lack of insight into the issue.
Many found the novel's jejunity disappointing after the author's previous complex works.
His argument was marked by a surprising jejunity given his extensive education.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vapidness - The quality of being dull or lacking in liveliness
  • Antonym: sophistication - The quality of being complex or refined

Etymology

From Latin 'jejunus' (empty, meager) via Late Latin 'jejunitas'.
