andrenid

1. NOUN

/ ænˈdrɛnɪd / (AmE), / ænˈdrɛnɪd / (BrE)
A bee that belongs to the genus Andrena, which includes many species of solitary, ground-nesting bees known for their early spring activity.
a bee of the genus Andrena

Examples

Several species of andrenid bees are important pollinators of early spring flowers.
The andrenid constructs its nest in sandy soil and lines the chambers with a waterproof secretion.
Entomologists identified the specimen as an andrenid based on its hairy facial features and wing veins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Andrena - genus of solitary mining bees

Etymology

From New Latin Andrena (the bee genus) + -id (suffix for zoological family/member).
