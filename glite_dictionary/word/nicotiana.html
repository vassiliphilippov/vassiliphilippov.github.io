Nicotiana

1. NOUN

/ nɪˌkoʊʃiˈænə / (AmE), / nɪˌkəʊʃiˈɑːnə / (BrE)
a genus of American and Asiatic aromatic herbs and shrubs, including tobacco plants, with sticky foliage
genus of tobacco and related plants

Examples

The leaves of Nicotiana species are harvested to make tobacco products.
Several ornamental flowers belong to the Nicotiana genus.
Researchers study Nicotiana plants for their resistance to certain pests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tobacco - the leaves of Nicotiana plants processed for smoking, chewing, or snuff

Etymology

Named after Jean Nicot, a 16th-century French diplomat who introduced tobacco to France.

2. NOUN

/ ˌnɪkəˈtiːənə / (AmE), / ˌnɪkəʊˈtʌɪənə / (BrE)
A genus of American and Asiatic aromatic herbs and shrubs, including tobacco plants, with sticky foliage.
genus of aromatic herbs and shrubs (tobacco plants)

Examples

The species Nicotiana tabacum is cultivated worldwide for its leaves.
Gardeners often grow ornamental varieties within the Nicotiana genus for their fragrant flowers.
Several Nicotiana species are native to South America and have been used for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tobacco - a product of plants in the Nicotiana genus, used for smoking or chewing

Etymology

From the modern Latin Nicotiana, named after Jean Nicot, a French diplomat who popularized tobacco in Europe.
