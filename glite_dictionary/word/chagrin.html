chagrin

1. NOUN

/ ʃəˈɡrɪn / (AmE), / ʃəˈɡrɪn / (BrE)
A feeling of distress or humiliation caused by failure, disappointment, or embarrassment.
distress or embarrassment at failure or disappointment

Examples

To her great chagrin, her mistake was pointed out in front of the entire team.
He hid his chagrin behind a forced smile when he was not invited to the party.
Much to his chagrin, the project was canceled after months of hard work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embarrassment - a feeling of self-consciousness, shame, or awkwardness
  • Antonym: delight - a feeling of great pleasure or happiness

Etymology

From French 'chagrin' (melancholy, sorrow).
