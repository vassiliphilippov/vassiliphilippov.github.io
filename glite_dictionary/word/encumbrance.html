encumbrance

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkʌm.brəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌm.brəns / (BrE)
something that restricts or burdens movement or progress; an obstacle or hindrance.
an obstacle or burden

Examples

The project was abandoned when the cost of removing each encumbrance became prohibitive.
Her lack of education proved to be an encumbrance in her search for employment.
The heavy backpack was an unnecessary encumbrance during the hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks progress or movement
  • Synonym: impediment - a hindrance or obstruction in doing something
  • Antonym: assistance - the act of helping or supporting

Etymology

From Old French *encombrer* (to block up, hinder), from Late Latin *incombrare*, from Latin *in-* (in) + *cumbere* (to lie down).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkʌm.brəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌm.brəns / (BrE)
A legal claim or liability on property, such as a lien or mortgage, that may affect its transfer or use.
legal claim against property
legal

Examples

Before selling his house, he had to clear all encumbrances from the property title.
A mortgage is an encumbrance that must be disclosed during the sale of real estate.
The buyer was relieved to learn that the land was free of any encumbrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lien - a legal right to keep possession of property until a debt is paid
  • Synonym: mortgage - a legal agreement by which a bank lends money to purchase property and keeps title as security
  • Antonym: asset - something valuable owned, especially property or investments

Etymology

From Old French 'encombrer' (to hinder), based on Latin 'in-' (in) + 'combrus' (heap).

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkʌm.brəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌm.brəns / (BrE)
a burden or difficult responsibility that makes life or progress harder
burden; difficult responsibility

Examples

The legal proceedings proved to be a significant encumbrance on the company's growth.
He carried the encumbrance of debt for years before finally paying it off.
For some, family obligations can feel like an encumbrance rather than a source of support.

Synset Examples

the burden of responsibility
that's a load off my mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burden - a heavy load or responsibility
  • Antonym: relief - the removal or reduction of a burden or distress

Etymology

From Old French *encombrer* (to hinder), from Late Latin *incombrare*.
