stir-fry

1. VERB

/ stɪr ˈfraɪ / (AmE), / stɜː ˈfraɪ / (BrE)
to cook (food) quickly by frying it in a small amount of hot oil while stirring continuously, usually in a wok or frying pan
fry food quickly while stirring

Examples

He decided to stir-fry the chicken with garlic and ginger for dinner.
You should stir-fry the broccoli and carrots for only a few minutes to keep them crisp.
I love to stir-fry mixed vegetables for a quick and healthy meal.

Synset Examples

stir-fry the vegetables in a wok

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sauté - to fry quickly in a small amount of fat, similar cooking method

2. NOUN

/ stɝː ˌfraɪ / (AmE), / stɜː ˌfraɪ / (BrE)
a dish made by quickly frying small pieces of food in a little hot oil while stirring them continuously
dish quickly fried with stirring

Examples

Last night, I made a delicious stir-fry with chicken and vegetables for dinner.
Vegetarians often enjoy a tofu stir-fry served over steamed rice.
She ordered a spicy beef stir-fry at the Asian restaurant.

Synset Examples

He loves to cook with pork, especially in stir-fries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stir-fry - to cook by frying quickly in a small amount of oil while stirring

Etymology

From 'stir' + 'fry', describing the cooking technique; popularized from Chinese cuisine.
