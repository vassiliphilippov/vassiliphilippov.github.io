ageratum

1. NOUN

/ ˌædʒəˈreɪtəm / (AmE), / ˌædʒəˈreɪtəm / (BrE)
A plant native to the Americas, especially Central and Southeast United States and the West Indies, having large clusters of showy blue flowers; sometimes classified in the genus Eupatorium.
plant with blue flowers, native to Americas

Examples

The garden was filled with the soft blue blossoms of ageratum.
Many gardeners choose ageratum for its ability to attract butterflies and add color to borders.
She planted ageratum alongside marigolds for a striking contrast in her flowerbeds.

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'geras' (old age), referring to the long-lasting flowers.

2. NOUN

/ ˌædʒəˈreɪtəm / (AmE), / ˌædʒəˈreɪtjʊm / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Ageratum, typically with opposite leaves and small blue or white flower heads, often grown as an ornamental.
a flowering plant of the genus Ageratum

Examples

The gardener planted several ageratum along the border to add a splash of blue to the flower bed.
Ageratum thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a favorite in summer gardens.
She picked a bouquet of ageratum and daisies for the centerpiece.

Etymology

From Latin 'ageratum', from Greek 'agēratos' (ageless, long-lived), referring to the long-lasting flowers.
