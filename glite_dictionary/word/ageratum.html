Ageratum

1. NOUN

/ ˌædʒəˈreɪtəm / (AmE), / ˌædʒəˈreɪtəm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American herbs belonging to the family Asteraceae, grown for their clusters of small, colorful flowers.
genus of tropical American flowering herbs

Examples

Ageratum species are commonly used as ornamental plants in gardens across temperate regions.
The bright blue flowers of Ageratum make it a popular choice for borders and bedding displays.
Gardeners appreciate Ageratum for its long blooming season and easy maintenance.

Etymology

From Greek agēraton (ageless), referring to the long-lasting color of the flowers.
