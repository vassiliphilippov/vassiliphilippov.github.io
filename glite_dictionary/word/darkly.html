darkly

1. ADV

/ ˈdɑrkli / (AmE), / ˈdɑːkli / (BrE)
in a manner that lacks light; in darkness or shadow
in darkness; without light

Examples

The cave stretched darkly beneath the ancient ruins, giving travelers an uneasy feeling.
He spoke darkly of dangers that lay ahead, warning us to be careful.
The alleyway shimmered darkly after the rain, reflecting distant lights in its puddles.

Synset Examples

the river was sliding darkly under the mist

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brightly - in a manner full of light or brightness

Etymology

Formed by adding the adverbial suffix -ly to 'dark'.

2. ADV

/ ˈdɑrkli / (AmE), / ˈdɑːkli / (BrE)
In a dark, glowering, or menacing manner.
in a menacing or glowering way

Examples

He looked darkly at his rival during the heated debate.
The clouds hung darkly over the castle, adding to the sense of foreboding.
She spoke darkly of secrets that should remain hidden.

Synset Examples

he stared darkly at her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ominously - in a way that suggests something bad will happen
  • Synonym: gloomily - in a sad or depressed manner

Etymology

From "dark" + "-ly", forming an adverb.
