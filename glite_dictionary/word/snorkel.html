snorkel

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnɔr.kəl / (AmE), / ˈsnɔː.kəl / (BrE)
a tube that allows a swimmer to breathe while keeping their face in the water, with one end in the swimmer's mouth and the other above the water's surface
tube for breathing while swimming facedown

Examples

While using a snorkel, she could observe the colorful fish below without lifting her head.
The instructor handed each participant a snorkel and explained how to use it.
He bought a new snorkel and fins for his vacation in Hawaii.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diving mask - a face mask that allows clear vision underwater

Etymology

From German 'Schnorchel', originally referring to a submarine airshaft.

2. VERB

/ ˈsnɔrkəl / (AmE), / ˈsnɔːkəl / (BrE)
to swim or dive just below the surface of water while breathing through a snorkel
to swim or dive with a snorkel

Examples

We plan to snorkel along the coral reef this afternoon.
She snorkeled for hours, watching colorful fish pass by beneath her.
Many tourists snorkel in these clear waters every summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scuba dive - to swim underwater using self-contained breathing apparatus
  • Degree Higher: dive - to jump or plunge into water, especially head first

Etymology

Back-formation from the noun 'snorkel', which comes from German 'Schnorchel' (a breathing tube for submarines).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01967802-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈsnɔrkəl / (AmE), / ˈsnɔːkəl / (BrE)
A retractable device with intake and exhaust pipes that provides an air passage, allowing a submarine to remain submerged for extended periods while running its diesel engines.
air device for submarines

Examples

The submarine used its snorkel to take in air while staying just below the surface.
Engineers repaired the damaged snorkel so the submarine could operate its diesel engines underwater.
During extended missions, the crew relied on the snorkel to avoid surfacing and detection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: periscope - optical device on submarines for viewing above surface while submerged

Etymology

From German 'Schnorchel', relating to breathing apparatus for submarines.

Synset POS: n
