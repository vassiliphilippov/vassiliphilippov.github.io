analogist

1. NOUN

/ əˈnælədʒɪst / (AmE), / əˈnælədʒɪst / (BrE)
a person who finds analogies or reasons by analogy
person using analogies

Examples

As an analogist, she often compared historical events to modern situations.
The analogist explained the theory by drawing parallels to everyday experiences.
His reputation as an analogist made his lectures both engaging and accessible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analogy - a comparison highlighting similarity between two things

Etymology

Formed from 'analogy' + '-ist', based on Greek 'analogia' (proportion, analogy).
