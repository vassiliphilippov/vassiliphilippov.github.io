pandowdy

1. NOUN

/ pænˈdaʊdi / (AmE), / pænˈdaʊdi / (BrE)
A deep-dish dessert of sweetened fruit, typically apples, baked with a rich crust that is broken up during cooking.
deep-dish fruit dessert with broken crust

Examples

My grandmother baked a delicious pandowdy for our holiday dinner.
The recipe calls for the crust of the pandowdy to be broken up half-way through baking.
A warm apple pandowdy is perfect with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apple pie - a baked dessert with a filling of apples and a pastry crust

Etymology

Origin uncertain; probably an Americanism dating from early 19th century.
