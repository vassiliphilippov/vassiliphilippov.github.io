landscaping

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌskeɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlæn(d)skeɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
The process of arranging and modifying the features of an area of land, especially a garden, for aesthetic or practical purposes.
design and arrangement of gardens or grounds

Examples

The new house came with professional landscaping that included a small pond and stone pathways.
They hired a company to do the landscaping for their backyard.
The landscaping around the building makes it look much more inviting.

Synset Examples

they spent a great deal of money on the landscaping

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gardening - the practice of cultivating and maintaining a garden

Etymology

From landscape + -ing; 'landscape' from Dutch 'landschap' (region, scenery).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌskeɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlan(d)skeɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity or profession of designing, creating, and maintaining gardens and other outdoor areas to make them attractive
designing and maintaining outdoor areas

Examples

He started a landscaping business after studying horticulture at college.
The landscaping around the new office complex features native plants and walking paths.
Good landscaping can significantly increase the value of a property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gardening - the activity of cultivating and maintaining a garden

Etymology

From 'landscape' + '-ing'; 'landscape' from Dutch 'landschap'.
