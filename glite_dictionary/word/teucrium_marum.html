Teucrium marum

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːkriəm ˈmærəm / (AmE), / ˈtjuːkriəm ˈmærəm / (BrE)
A Mediterranean plant with small hairy leaves and reddish purple flowers, known for attracting cats; also called cat thyme.
a Mediterranean plant (cat thyme) attracting cats

Examples

Many gardeners grow Teucrium marum because its scent attracts cats to the garden.
The leaves of Teucrium marum are soft, hairy, and aromatic when crushed.
Botanists often refer to Teucrium marum as cat thyme due to its effect on felines.

Etymology

Genus name from Latin 'Teucrium' (a plant used by King Teucer), and Latin 'marum' (a kind of thyme).
