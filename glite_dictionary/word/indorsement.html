indorsement

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɔrsmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɔːsmənt / (BrE)
formal and explicit approval or support for someone or something, especially in an official capacity
formal approval or support

Examples

The new policy received the unanimous indorsement of the board members.
Without the mayor's indorsement, the project will struggle to gain public trust.
Her candidacy gained momentum after winning the union's official indorsement.

Synset Examples

a Democrat usually gets the union's endorsement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endorsement - formal and explicit approval or support

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *indorsare*, influenced by Latin *dorsum* (back); variant of 'endorsement'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06700103-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɔrsmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɔːsmənt / (BrE)
the act of giving one's public approval or support to something or someone; also, a signature or written statement of approval, especially on a document such as a check.
the act of endorsing or approving

Examples

The company offered him a lucrative contract for the indorsement of their products.
A check is invalid without the proper indorsement on the back.
Her political indorsement helped the candidate gain trust among undecided voters.

Synset Examples

a star athlete can make a lot of money from endorsements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endorsement - the act of endorsing or approving (modern standard spelling)

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'endorsement,' from Medieval Latin 'indorsare' (to write on the back), from Latin 'in-' (on) + 'dorsum' (back).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01217658-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɔrsmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɔːsmənt / (BrE)
a signature, typically on a document, especially a check, that authorizes or validates it
signature of validation, especially on a check
legal

Examples

The check was invalid until the payee's indorsement was added on the back.
Bank policies require your indorsement before they can deposit the check into your account.
Without an authorized indorsement, the document will not be legally recognized.

Synset Examples

the cashier would not cash the check without an endorsement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endorsement - a signature that validates something; the more common spelling

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'endorsement', from Medieval Latin 'indorsare' (to write on the back).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06416841-n
Synset POS: n
