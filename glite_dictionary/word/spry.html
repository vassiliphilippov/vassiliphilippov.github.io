spry

1. ADJ

/ spraɪ / (AmE), / spraɪ / (BrE)
(especially of an older person or animal) moving quickly and lightly; agile and energetic.
moving quickly and lightly

Examples

Despite his age, the gardener remained remarkably spry and tended to his plants every day.
The spry puppy dashed through the yard, chasing its tail in circles.
She was surprisingly spry on the dance floor, spinning and leaping with ease.

Synset Examples

sleek and agile as a gymnast
as nimble as a deer
nimble fingers
quick of foot
the old dog was so spry it was halfway up the stairs before we could stop it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agile - able to move quickly and easily
  • Synonym: nimble - quick and light in movement or action
  • Antonym: sluggish - moving slowly or lacking energy

Etymology

Origin unknown, perhaps related to Middle English *spryngen* (to spring).
