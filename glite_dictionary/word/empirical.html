empirical

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɪrɪkəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪrɪkəl / (BrE)
Based on observation or experience rather than theory or pure logic.
based on observation or experience

Examples

The scientists collected empirical data to test their hypothesis.
Her research is notable for its thorough empirical analysis of the results.
We need empirical evidence to support these claims.

Synset Examples

an empirical basis for an ethical theory
empirical laws
empirical data
an empirical treatment of a disease about which little is known

Related Terms

  • Antonym: theoretical - concerned with theory rather than practical application
  • Synonym: experimental - based on or involving experimentation and trial

Etymology

From Greek empeirikos (experienced), via Latin empiricus.
