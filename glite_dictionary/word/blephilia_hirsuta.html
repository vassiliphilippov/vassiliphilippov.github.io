Blephilia hirsuta

1. NOUN

/ blɪˈfɪliə hɪrˈsuːtə / (AmE), / blɪˈfɪliə hɪəˈsuːtə / (BrE)
A species of perennial herb in the mint family, commonly known as hairy wood mint, with light blue, pale purple, or white flowers dotted with purple spots.
species of mint plant (hairy wood mint)

Examples

Blephilia hirsuta is often found growing in rich woodlands of eastern North America.
Gardeners appreciate Blephilia hirsuta for its attractive foliage and clusters of colorful flowers.
The conservation status of Blephilia hirsuta varies across its native range due to habitat loss.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'blepharis' (eyelash), referring to hairy characteristics; species name 'hirsuta' from Latin (hairy).
