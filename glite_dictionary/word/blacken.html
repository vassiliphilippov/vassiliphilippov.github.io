to blacken

1. VERB

/ ˈblæk.ən / (AmE), / ˈblæk.ən / (BrE)
to make something black or dark, or to become black or dark
make or become black or dark

Examples

The smoke blackened the walls after the fire.
If you leave the bananas out too long, their skins will blacken.
Charcoal was used to blacken the actor’s face for the performance.

Synset Examples

The smoke blackened the ceiling
The ceiling blackened

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to whiten - make or become white
  • Synonym: to darken - make or become dark or darker

Etymology

Formed from 'black' + '-en', a common verb-forming suffix.

2. VERB

/ ˈblækən / (AmE), / ˈblækən / (BrE)
to burn or cook something so that its surface becomes dark or black, especially as a result of charring or scorching
cause to become black by burning

Examples

The chef used a special spice blend to blacken the fish fillets on the grill.
Smoke from the fire began to blacken the walls of the cabin.
Be careful not to blacken the toast by leaving it in too long.

Synset Examples

The cook blackened the chicken breast
The fire charred the ceiling above the mantelpiece
the flames scorched the ceiling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: char - to burn or scorch, especially so as to blacken the surface
  • Synonym: scorch - to burn the surface so as to discolor or damage it
  • Antonym: to whiten - to make or become white or lighter in color

Etymology

From Middle English blacknen, from black + -en.
