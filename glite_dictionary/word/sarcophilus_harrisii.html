Sarcophilus harrisii

1. NOUN

/ sɑːrˈkɑːfɪləs ˈhærɪsiː / (AmE), / sɑːˈkɒfɪləs ˈhærɪsiː / (BrE)
a small, fierce carnivorous marsupial from Tasmania with a mostly black coat and a long tail, commonly known as the Tasmanian devil
scientific name of the Tasmanian devil

Examples

The Sarcophilus harrisii is renowned for its loud, disturbing screeches.
Researchers have been working to save Sarcophilus harrisii from a deadly facial tumor disease.
Many tourists hope to see a Sarcophilus harrisii in the wild during visits to Tasmania.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tasmanian devil - common name of Sarcophilus harrisii, a carnivorous marsupial of Tasmania

Etymology

From Greek *sarkos* (flesh) + *philos* (loving), and named for George Harris, zoologist.
