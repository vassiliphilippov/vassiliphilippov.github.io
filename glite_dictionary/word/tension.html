tension

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
a feeling of hostility or strained relations between individuals or groups, especially when not openly expressed
latent hostility or strained relations

Examples

There was a noticeable tension between the two colleagues during the meeting.
Negotiators worked hard to ease the tension after the recent conflict.
A sense of tension filled the room as the rivals avoided direct eye contact.

Synset Examples

he could sense her latent hostility to him
the diplomats' first concern was to reduce international tensions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strain - a state of tension caused by pressure
  • Antonym: harmony - a situation in which people are peaceful and agree with each other

Etymology

From Latin 'tensio' (a stretching), from 'tendere' (to stretch).
