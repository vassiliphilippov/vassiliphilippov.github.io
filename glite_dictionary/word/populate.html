to populate

1. VERB

/ ˈpɑː.pjə.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɒp.jʊ.leɪt / (BrE)
(of people or animals) to live in or inhabit a particular place or area.
live in or inhabit a place

Examples

Many migratory birds populate the wetlands throughout the year.
As cities grow, new suburbs are quickly populated by young families.
After the disaster, only a few hardy species managed to populate the barren land.

Synset Examples

People lived in Africa millions of years ago
The people inhabited the islands that are now deserted
this kind of fish dwells near the bottom of the ocean
deer are populating the woods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inhabit - to live in a place as an inhabitant
  • Antonym: desert - to leave a place so that it is no longer inhabited

Etymology

From Latin populatus, past participle of 'populare' (to people, to populate), from 'populus' (people).

2. VERB

/ ˈpɑː.pjə.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɒp.jə.leɪt / (BrE)
to cause people or animals to live in a place or to fill an area with inhabitants
fill with inhabitants

Examples

Government programs helped populate the remote regions of the country with farmers.
The island was populated by settlers in the seventeenth century.
Wildlife officials aim to populate the lakes with native fish species.

Synset Examples

populate the forest with deer and wild boar for hunting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inhabit - to live in a place
  • Antonym: desert - to leave a place empty of inhabitants

Etymology

From Latin 'populus' (people), via the Medieval Latin 'populatus' (populated).

Source: WordNet
