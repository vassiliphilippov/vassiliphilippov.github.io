lineup

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪ.nʌp / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ.nʌp / (BrE)
a line of people arranged by police for inspection or identification, especially to help witnesses identify a suspect
police identification procedure

Examples

The witness picked the suspect out of the lineup at the police station.
Five men stood in the lineup, waiting to be identified by the victim.
After the robbery, the police conducted a lineup to help find the perpetrator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: identity parade - a British term for a police lineup

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪn.ʌp / (AmE), / ˈlaɪn.ʌp / (BrE)
the schedule of television or radio programs planned for a particular period
schedule of TV or radio programs

Examples

Our favorite show was moved to a new time in the Friday evening lineup.
The network announced its fall lineup during the press conference.
They're hoping their new comedy will boost the station's Sunday night lineup.

Synset Examples

3. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪnˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnʌp / (BrE)
(Baseball) a list of batters on a team, arranged in the order in which they will bat during a game.
order of batters in baseball

Examples

The coach changed the lineup before the big game to improve their chances of winning.
After checking the lineup, it was clear that the rookie would be batting third.
Each team submitted its lineup to the umpire before the tournament started.

Synset Examples

the managers presented their cards to the umpire at home plate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: batting order - the order in which batters play in a team sport

Etymology

From 'line' + 'up', referring to arranging people in sequence.
