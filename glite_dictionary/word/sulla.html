sulla

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌlə / (AmE), / ˈsʌlə / (BrE)
A perennial plant of southern Europe, cultivated as forage and valued for its nectar-rich pink flowers, which are important for honey production.
Mediterranean forage plant; source of honey

Examples

Farmers often plant sulla to improve soil quality and provide forage for livestock.
The sulla fields were buzzing with bees, attracted by the fragrant pink flowers.
High-quality honey is produced from the nectar of blooming sulla.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honey clover - a plant also cultivated for honey production

Etymology

From Italian 'sulla', the name for the plant, ultimately from Latin.
