Sulla

1. PROPN

/ ˈsʌlə / (AmE), / ˈsʌlə / (BrE)
(138–78 BC) A Roman general and dictator, known for his military campaigns and leadership during the late Roman Republic.
Roman general and dictator (138–78 BC)

Examples

Sulla seized control of Rome and implemented a series of constitutional reforms.
Historians often compare Sulla and Julius Caesar for their roles as Roman dictators.
The rivalry between Sulla and Marius shaped the political landscape of the late Republic.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Julius Caesar - Roman general and statesman, later dictator
  • Synonym: Marius - Roman general and political rival of Sulla
