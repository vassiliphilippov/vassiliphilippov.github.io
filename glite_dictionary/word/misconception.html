misconception

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪskənˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪskənˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
a view or idea that is incorrect because it is based on faulty thinking or understanding
an incorrect idea or belief

Examples

There is a common misconception that bats are blind, but they actually have good eyesight.
Many people have the misconception that the Great Wall of China is visible from space.
The teacher clarified a misconception in the students' understanding of the topic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fallacy - a mistaken belief, especially one based on unsound argument
  • Antonym: truth - the true or actual state of a matter

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + conception, from Latin conceptio (a comprehending, a collection, a pregnancy).
