to regularise

1. VERB

/ ˈrɛɡjələrˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊləˌaɪz / (BrE)
to make something regular or more regular; to bring order or uniformity to something, often by enforcing rules or consistency
make regular or more uniform
british

Examples

The doctor attempted to regularise the patient's heartbeat using medication.
New policies were introduced to regularise the process of granting building permits.
The company sought to regularise its accounting procedures after the audit.

Synset Examples

regularize the heart beat with a pace maker

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to regularize - make regular or more regular (American spelling)

Etymology

From regular + -ise (suffix forming verbs), modelled after regular.

2. VERB

/ ˈrɛɡjələrˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊləraɪz / (BrE)
to make something conform to rules, regulations, or standard practices; to apply rules or controls to something
bring into conformity with rules or regulations
british

Examples

The government plans to regularise the informal settlements by providing official titles to residents.
Several countries have decided to regularise undocumented workers to address labor shortages.
Attempts were made to regularise the procedure so that all departments would follow the same guidelines.

Synset Examples

We cannot regulate the way people dress
This town likes to regulate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to regulate - to control or direct by rules
  • Antonym: to derestrict - to remove restrictions from

Etymology

From Latin 'regularis' (regular) via French 'régulariser'.
