to collimate

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑː.lə.meɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒl.ɪ.meɪt / (BrE)
to adjust the line of sight or axes of (an optical instrument) so that its parts are correctly aligned for accurate use
align an optical instrument

Examples

The technician carefully collimated the telescope before making any observations.
If you don't collimate the lenses correctly, the measurements may be inaccurate.
After cleaning the microscope, she needed to collimate it for precise imaging again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to align - to arrange in a straight line or in correct relative positions
  • Synonym: to adjust - to alter or move something to achieve the desired fit or alignment

Etymology

From Latin 'collimare', altered from 'collineare' (to make straight, direct in a straight line).

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑləˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒlɪmeɪt / (BrE)
to make rays of light, particles, or objects parallel to each other; to adjust so that they are aligned accurately along the same direction
make or adjust parallel to each other

Examples

The technician needed to collimate the laser beams for the experiment to work accurately.
Before using the telescope, it's important to collimate its mirrors for precise focus.
Engineers collimated the lenses to ensure the light rays traveled in parallel paths.

Synset Examples

They paralleled the ditch to the highway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to align - to place or arrange things in a straight line

Etymology

From Latin collimare, a mistaken alteration of 'collimare', from 'collineare' (to make straight).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00526361-v
Synset POS: v
