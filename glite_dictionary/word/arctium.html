Arctium

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrktiəm / (AmE), / ˈɑːktɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the daisy family, commonly known as burdock, with broad leaves and edible roots.
genus of plants; burdock

Examples

Several species of Arctium are cultivated for their edible roots in Asia.
The leaves of Arctium can grow quite large and are often used in traditional remedies.
Researchers are studying the medicinal potential of Arctium extracts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burdock - a coarse, broad-leaved plant with burrs; common name for Arctium

Etymology

From Latin 'arctium', from Greek 'arktion' (burdock).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrk.ti.əm / (AmE), / ˈɑːk.ti.əm / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the daisy family (Asteraceae), commonly known as burdocks, noted for their large leaves and burr-like flower heads.
a plant genus including burdocks

Examples

The genus Arctium is widespread in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
Many herbal remedies use the roots of plants from Arctium.
The burrs of Arctium plants often stick to clothing and animal fur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burdock - a coarse, broad-leaved weed bearing prickly heads

Etymology

From Greek arction, meaning 'bear', referring to the hairy fruit.
