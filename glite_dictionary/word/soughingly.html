soughingly

1. ADV

/ ˈsaʊɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈsaʊɪŋli / (BrE)
in a manner characterized by a soft or murmuring sound, like a gentle sigh or rustle
with a soft, murmuring sound

Examples

The wind passed soughingly through the reeds beside the lake.
At dusk, the rain fell soughingly on the old tin roof, lulling him to sleep.
She sat by the open window, listening as the pines whispered soughingly in the evening breeze.

Synset Examples

the branches of the trees moved soughingly in the breeze

Related Terms

  • Synonym: softly - in a gentle or quiet manner

Etymology

From 'soughing' (making a soft murmuring sound) + -ly (adverbial suffix), 'sough' from Old English 'sūgan' (to hum, to sigh).
