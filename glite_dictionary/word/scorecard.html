scorecard

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɔrˌkɑrd / (AmE), / ˈskɔːkɑːd / (BrE)
A card or sheet on which scores, especially in a game or sport such as golf, are recorded.
card recording scores in a game

Examples

You must submit your scorecard to the clubhouse after finishing your round.
She carefully filled in each hole on her scorecard during the golf tournament.
The player realized he lost his scorecard somewhere between the 12th and 13th hole.

Synset Examples

you have to turn in your card to get a handicap

Related Terms

  • Synonym: score sheet - sheet for recording scores in a game or sport

Etymology

From 'score' + 'card'; 'score' originally from Old Norse 'skor' (notch, tally) and 'card' from Latin 'charta' (paper).
