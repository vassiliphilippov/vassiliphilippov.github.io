thermal

1. ADJ

/ ˈθɝː.məl / (AmE), / ˈθɜː.məl / (BrE)
Relating to heat or temperature, or caused by heat.
relating to heat

Examples

The thermal conductivity of the material determines how well it can transfer heat.
She wore special thermal clothing to stay warm during the harsh winter.
The engineers measured the thermal expansion of the metal under different temperatures.

Synset Examples

thermal movements of molecules
thermal capacity
thermic energy
the caloric effect of sunlight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermic - relating to heat

Etymology

From Greek thermos (hot, warm).

2. ADJ

/ ˈθɜr.məl / (AmE), / ˈθɜː.məl / (BrE)
Relating to, caused by, or designed to retain heat.
relating to or retaining heat

Examples

The hikers wore thermal socks to keep their feet warm in the snow.
She bought a new thermal flask to keep her coffee hot during the trip.
The building uses thermal insulation to maintain a comfortable temperature inside.

Synset Examples

a thermal burn
thermal underwear

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermic - relating to heat

Etymology

From Greek thermē (heat), via French thermique.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01253649-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈθɝː.məl / (AmE), / ˈθɜː.məl / (BrE)
of or relating to a hot spring, especially referring to naturally heated water used for bathing or health treatments
relating to a hot spring

Examples

The town is famous for its thermal baths, which attract tourists from around the world.
Many people visit spas to bathe in naturally thermal waters believed to have healing properties.
A thermal spring was discovered nearby, leading to the development of a wellness resort.

Synset Examples

thermal water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geothermal - related to the heat from the Earth's interior
  • Synonym: mineral - relating to or containing minerals, often describing springs

Etymology

From Greek thermē (heat), via Latin thermal.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02825291-a
Synset POS: a

4. NOUN

/ ˈθɜrməl / (AmE), / ˈθɜːməl / (BrE)
a rising current of warm air, especially one used by birds or gliders to gain height
rising current of warm air

Examples

Glider pilots use thermals to stay aloft for hours.
Large birds such as eagles soar on thermals to conserve energy.
The desert landscape generates powerful thermals during the heat of the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: updraft - an upward current of air

Etymology

From Greek thermē (heat) via French or New Latin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-11539078-n
Synset POS: n
