pliable

1. ADJ

/ ˈplaɪ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈplaɪ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
easily bent, flexible, or able to be shaped without breaking.
easily bent or flexible

Examples

The pliable branches bent easily under the weight of the snow.
This wire is pliable enough to twist into any shape you want.
As the clay warmed in her hands, it became soft and pliable.

Synset Examples

a flexible wire
a pliant young tree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flexible - able to bend easily without breaking
  • Antonym: rigid - unable to bend or be forced out of shape

Etymology

From Old French 'pliable' (bendable), from 'plier' (to bend), from Latin 'plicare' (to fold).
