Phyllium

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪliəm / (AmE), / ˈfɪliəm / (BrE)
A genus of leaf insects in the family Phylliidae, noted for their flat, leaf-like appearance as a form of camouflage.
genus of leaf insects

Examples

The Phyllium genus includes several species of insects that mimic leaves for camouflage.
Researchers observed a Phyllium in the rainforest, amazed by its leaf-like appearance and slow movements.
Collectors consider Phyllium specimens particularly beautiful because of their remarkable mimicry.

Etymology

From Greek 'phyllon' meaning 'leaf', referring to the insects' resemblance to leaves.
