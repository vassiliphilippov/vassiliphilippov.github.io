lockdown

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑkˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈlɒkˌdaʊn / (BrE)
a situation in which people, especially prisoners, are confined to their rooms or cells as a security measure, often to regain control during an incident or threat
prisoners confined to cells as a security measure

Examples

The prison was placed under lockdown after the fight broke out between inmates.
During the lockdown, all prisoners were kept in their cells for 24 hours a day.
After the riot, the warden initiated a full lockdown to restore order.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curfew - a regulation requiring people to remain indoors during designated hours

Etymology

From 'lock' + 'down', first used in the context of prison security in the 1970s US.
