chalky

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɔːki / (AmE), / ˈtʃɔːki / (BrE)
containing, resembling, or made of chalk; having a powdery or pale quality like chalk
resembling or containing chalk

Examples

The soil in this area is chalky and drains water quickly.
She noticed a chalky residue left on her hands after touching the rock.
The medicine left a chalky taste in my mouth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powdery - having the texture or appearance of powder
  • Antonym: gritty - having a texture like grit or sand

Etymology

From 'chalk' + '-y'. 'Chalk' from Old English 'cealc', from Latin 'calx' (limestone).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02684706-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɔːki / (AmE), / ˈtʃɔːki / (BrE)
having the white or pale color of chalk
pale white or chalk-colored

Examples

After hearing the news, his face became chalky with shock.
She wore a chalky white dress that almost glowed in the dark room.
The walls had a chalky appearance after years of sun exposure.

Synset Examples

she turned chalky white

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milky - having a white, opaque color like milk

Etymology

From 'chalk' (Old English 'cealc', from Latin 'calx', limestone) + '-y'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00389632-s
Synset POS: s
