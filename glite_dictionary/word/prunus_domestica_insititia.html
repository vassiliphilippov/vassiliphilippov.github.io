Prunus domestica insititia

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs doʊˈmɛstɪkə ɪnˌsɪtiːʃə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs dəˈmɛstɪkə ɪnˌsɪtɪtiːə / (BrE)
a subspecies of plum tree that has long been cultivated for its edible fruit, including greengages and damsons.
cultivated subspecies of plum tree

Examples

Some traditional English orchards still contain Prunus domestica insititia, valued for their diverse plum varieties.
The Prunus domestica insititia is known for producing greengages and damsons, which are popular for jams and desserts.
Horticulturists recommend planting Prunus domestica insititia in well-drained soil for optimal fruit production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plum - an edible, fleshy stone fruit produced by the plum tree
  • Degree Lower: damson - a type of small, dark purple plum, usually of Prunus domestica insititia
  • Degree Lower: greengage - a cultivated variety of greenish plum, a type of Prunus domestica insititia

Etymology

From Latin: Prunus (plum), domestica (domesticated), insititia (grafted or cultivated).
