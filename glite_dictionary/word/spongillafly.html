spongillafly

1. NOUN

/ spʌnˈdʒɪləˌflaɪ / (AmE), / spʌnˈdʒɪləˌflaɪ / (BrE)
A hairy-bodied insect of the family Sisyridae whose larvae feed on freshwater sponges.
insect whose larvae eat freshwater sponges

Examples

A spongillafly was spotted near the pond, indicating the presence of freshwater sponges.
The larvae of the spongillafly feed exclusively on Spongilla species in clean streams.
Researchers collected several spongillaflies during their study of aquatic insects.

Etymology

From New Latin *Spongilla* (a genus of freshwater sponges) + English 'fly'.
