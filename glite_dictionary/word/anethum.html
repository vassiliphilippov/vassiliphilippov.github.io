Anethum

1. NOUN

/ əˈniːθəm / (AmE), / əˈniːθəm / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic herbs in the parsley family, including dill, used as a herb or spice.
genus of herbs including dill

Examples

Anethum is commonly cultivated for its aromatic seeds and leaves known as dill.
Researchers studied the oil compounds found in Anethum to analyze its medicinal properties.
The culinary use of Anethum dates back to ancient civilizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dill - a herb used in cooking, from the same genus

Etymology

From Latin 'anethum', from Greek 'ánēthon', meaning 'dill'.
