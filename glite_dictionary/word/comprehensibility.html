comprehensibility

1. NOUN

/ kəmˌprihɛn.səˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / kəmˌpriː.hɛn.səˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of being easy to understand; the state of being comprehensible
quality of being easy to understand

Examples

The comprehensibility of the instructions made the assembly process much easier.
Experts praised the comprehensibility of her writing, which made complex ideas accessible to everyone.
Teachers often adjust their language to increase comprehensibility for non-native speakers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comprehensible - able to be understood; intelligible
  • Antonym: incomprehensibility - the quality of being impossible or very difficult to understand

Etymology

From Latin 'comprehensibilis' (able to be understood) + English suffix '-ity'.
