to stipple

1. VERB

/ ˈstɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈstɪp(ə)l / (BrE)
to engrave or draw by making small dots or short strokes to create shading or texture
engrave or draw using dots or strokes

Examples

The artist began to stipple the copper plate to achieve a subtle shading effect.
If you stipple the surface gently, you can create a soft, textured look.
He stippled tiny dots along the edge of the image to add depth and dimension.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to engrave - to cut or carve a design into a hard surface

Etymology

From Dutch 'stippelen', meaning 'to dot'.

2. VERB

/ ˈstɪp.əl / (AmE), / ˈstɪp.əl / (BrE)
to apply paint or ink in small dots or short strokes, often to create texture or shading
apply paint or ink in dots or strokes

Examples

The artist stipples paint onto the canvas to achieve a textured background.
After stippling blue dots along the edge, she set the painting aside to dry.
He carefully stippled the shadows to give the portrait a lifelike appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dab - to touch or apply lightly with a repeated motion

Etymology

From Dutch *stippelen* (to dot).

3. VERB

/ ˈstɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈstɪpəl / (BrE)
to produce a mottled or speckled effect, especially by applying small dots or marks
produce a mottled or speckled effect

Examples

The sunlight stippled the forest floor with patches of gold.
An artist stipples the canvas to create texture and depth.
Raindrops stippled the surface of the lake, creating a pattern of tiny circles.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

4. VERB

/ ˈstɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈstɪpəl / (BrE)
to mark or produce a pattern, shading, or image by applying many small dots or short touches, especially in drawing, painting, or engraving
create a pattern or effect with small dots or touches

Examples

The artist decided to stipple the background to achieve a smoother gradient.
If you stipple the paint lightly, you'll create a soft, textured effect.
She carefully stipples each shadow on the canvas using the tip of her brush.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dot - to mark with a small round spot; pointillism technique

Etymology

From Dutch *stippelen*, diminutive of *stip* (spot), meaning to make dots.
