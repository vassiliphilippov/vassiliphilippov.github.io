prison

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪzən / (AmE), / ˈprɪzən / (BrE)
A place or situation in which someone feels very confined or restricted, often used metaphorically.
situation or place of seeming confinement

Examples

For some, a small town can feel like a prison when they long for adventure.
He described his cubicle as a prison from which he couldn't escape.
Her strict schedule was a self-imposed prison that left little room for spontaneity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confinement - the state of being restricted within certain limits
  • Antonym: freedom - the condition of being free or unrestricted

Etymology

From Old French *prison* (prison, jail) and Latin *prēhensiō* (a seizing).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɪzən / (AmE), / ˈprɪz(ə)n / (BrE)
A building or institution where people are kept as punishment for a crime or while awaiting trial.
facility for confining people as punishment

Examples

After the trial, he was sentenced to five years in prison.
Many families are affected when a loved one is sent to prison.
She works as a counselor in a high-security prison downtown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jail - a place for the confinement of people accused or convicted of a crime
  • Antonym: release - the act of allowing someone to go free from prison

Etymology

From Old French *prison* (imprisonment, jail), from Latin *prensio*, later *prehensio* (a taking hold, seizing).
