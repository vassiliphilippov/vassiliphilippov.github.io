lava

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑː.və / (AmE), / ˈlɑː.və / (BrE)
molten rock that flows from a volcano during an eruption and solidifies as it cools
molten rock from a volcano

Examples

The lava poured down the mountainside, destroying everything in its path.
After the eruption, the lava cooled and formed a new layer of rock.
Tourists marveled at the red-hot lava flowing from the volcano's crater.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magma - molten rock beneath the earth's surface

Etymology

From Italian 'lava' (stream), from Latin 'lavare' (to wash), originally describing the flow of water.
