Puseyism

1. NOUN

/ ˈpjuziˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpjuːziˌɪzəm / (BrE)
The principles and doctrines of the Oxford Movement within the Church of England, as advocated by Edward Bouverie Pusey and expounded in the 'Tracts for the Times'.
principles of the Oxford Movement (Anglicanism)

Examples

The theological debates of the 19th century often centered around Puseyism and its influence on Anglican doctrine.
Many clergy opposed the ritual practices promoted by Puseyism.
Her granduncle was an ardent supporter of Puseyism during the peak of the Oxford Movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tractarianism - the Oxford Movement and its doctrines within Anglicanism

Etymology

From the name of Edward Bouverie Pusey (1800–1882), a leader of the Oxford Movement, plus -ism.
