blondness

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɑnd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈblɒnd.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of having light-colored hair or complexion, especially naturally so.
quality of having light-colored hair or skin

Examples

Her blondness made her stand out in the crowd of brunettes.
The photographer admired the natural blondness of his model's hair.
He inherited his mother's striking blondness and blue eyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blonde - a person with light-colored hair
  • Antonym: darkness - state of being dark in color

Etymology

From blond (from French blond, from Latin blundus) + -ness.
