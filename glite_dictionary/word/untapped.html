untapped

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtæpt / (AmE), / ʌnˈtapt / (BrE)
(of a resource or object) not yet used, exploited, or drawn upon.
not yet used or exploited

Examples

There are numerous untapped markets in rural areas waiting for business development.
She believes her creative potential remains largely untapped.
The region is rich in untapped natural resources.

Synset Examples

an untapped keg
an untapped sugar maple

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unused - not yet used or employed
  • Antonym: exploited - already used or utilized to benefit

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtæpt / (AmE), / ʌnˈtæpt / (BrE)
(of resources or potential) not yet used, developed, or exploited.
not yet used or exploited

Examples

Some countries still have vast untapped natural resources beneath their soil.
There is a lot of untapped talent in small towns across the country.
The marketing team is looking for untapped markets to expand their products.

Synset Examples

untapped reserves of coal
the untapped stockrooms of our minds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unused - not being used or occupied
  • Antonym: exploited - used fully or developed for benefit

Etymology

From un- (not) + tapped (from tap, meaning to draw off liquid or access).
