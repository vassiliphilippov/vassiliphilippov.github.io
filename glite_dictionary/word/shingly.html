shingly

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈʃɪŋli / (BrE)
(of ground or a surface) covered with or abounding in small stones or pebbles.
covered with small stones or pebbles

Examples

The path was shingly and difficult to walk on without sturdy shoes.
We picnicked on a shingly beach by the river, listening to the waves.
After the rain, the shingly banks sparkled in the afternoon sun.

Synset Examples

landed at a shingly little beach

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stony - covered with or full of stones
  • Synonym: rocky - full of or abounding in rocks

Etymology

From 'shingle' (gravel or small stones) + -y (adjectival suffix).
