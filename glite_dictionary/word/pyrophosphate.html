pyrophosphate

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪrəˈfɑsˌfeɪt / (AmE), / ˌpaɪərəˈfɒs.feɪt / (BrE)
A salt or ester formed from pyrophosphoric acid, often used in biochemistry and chemistry.
salt or ester of pyrophosphoric acid
medical

Examples

The enzyme requires pyrophosphate as a cofactor to function efficiently.
Some toothpaste formulations contain pyrophosphate to help prevent the formation of dental tartar.
Researchers measured the concentration of pyrophosphate in the biological samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phosphate - salt or ester of phosphoric acid

Etymology

From Greek pyr (fire) + phosphate, relating to pyrophosphoric acid.
