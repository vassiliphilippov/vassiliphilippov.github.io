Sauromalus obesus

1. NOUN

/ sɔːˈrɒmələs oʊˈbiːsəs / (AmE), / sɔːˈrɒmæləs əʊˈbiːsəs / (BrE)
A large, herbivorous lizard native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and Mexico, commonly known as the common chuckwalla.
large herbivorous lizard of SW US and Mexico

Examples

The Sauromalus obesus is well adapted to the rocky deserts of Arizona and California.
Researchers observed a Sauromalus obesus basking on a sun-warmed boulder.
A Sauromalus obesus can grow quite large, with some adults reaching up to 76 centimeters in length.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chuckwalla - Common name for Sauromalus obesus, a large lizard found in the southwestern US and Mexico

Etymology

From Latin: 'Sauromalus' (genus name), possibly from Greek 'sauros' (lizard) + 'omalus' (flat); 'obesus' from Latin meaning 'fat, stout.'
