Boykinia elata

1. NOUN

/ bɔɪˈkɪniə ɪˈleɪtə / (AmE), / bɔɪˈkɪniə ɪˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A plant species native to western North America, characterized by basal leaves and clusters of small white flowers.
a plant with basal leaves and white flower clusters, western North America

Examples

Boykinia elata grows along streams in the forests of Oregon and California.
The clusters of small white flowers on Boykinia elata appear in late spring each year.
Gardeners prize Boykinia elata for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.

Etymology

Boykinia: named after American botanist Samuel Boykin; elata from Latin 'elatus' (tall, lofty).
