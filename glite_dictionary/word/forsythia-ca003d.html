Forsythia

1. NOUN

/ fɔːrˈsɪθiə / (AmE), / fɔːˈsɪθiə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering shrubs in the olive family, known for their bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring.
genus of shrubs with yellow spring flowers

Examples

In early spring, the Forsythia bushes in our garden explode with yellow blooms.
Gardeners often use Forsythia as a hedge because of its attractive flowers.
After a long winter, the arrival of Forsythia signals the start of the gardening season.

Etymology

Named after Scottish botanist William Forsyth (1737–1804).
