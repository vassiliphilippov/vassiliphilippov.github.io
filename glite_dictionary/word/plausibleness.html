plausibleness

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɔːzəblnəs / (AmE), / ˈplɔːzɪblnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being plausible or appearing to be reasonable or probable.
quality of being plausible

Examples

The plausibleness of his explanation convinced the interviewer.
She admired the plausibleness of the proposed solution, even though she remained skeptical.
Its plausibleness made it easy for others to accept the story without much questioning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plausibility - the quality of seeming reasonable or probable

Etymology

From plausible + -ness; plausible from Latin plausibilis (deserving applause).
