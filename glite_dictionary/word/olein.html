olein

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊliɪn / (AmE), / ˈəʊliːɪn / (BrE)
a naturally occurring triglyceride of oleic acid, found in animal and vegetable fats and oils
glyceride found in fats and oils

Examples

Olive oil contains a high percentage of olein, contributing to its liquid state at room temperature.
Scientists analyzed the olein content in various vegetable oils.
The separation of olein from stearin is an important step in oil processing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oleic acid - a fatty acid present in fats and oils

Etymology

From Latin 'oleum' meaning oil, with the suffix '-in' used for chemical substances.
