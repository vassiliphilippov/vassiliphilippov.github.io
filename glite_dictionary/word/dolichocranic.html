dolichocranic

1. ADJ

/ ˌdoʊlɪkoʊˈkrænɪk / (AmE), / ˌdɒlɪkəʊˈkranɪk / (BrE)
(of a skull or head) having a relatively long shape, with a cephalic index under 75.
having a long-headed skull shape
medical

Examples

Early anthropologists often classified populations as dolichocranic based on skull measurements.
The dolichocranic form is characterized by a long and narrow head.
In the study, most of the ancient skeletons were identified as dolichocranic.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brachycranic - having a relatively broad head (cephalic index over 80)
  • Degree Lower: mesocranic - having a head of intermediate width (cephalic index 75–80)

Etymology

From Greek dolikhos (long) + kranion (skull).
