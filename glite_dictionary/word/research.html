research

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɜrtʃ / (AmE), / rɪˈsɜːtʃ / (BrE)
the systematic investigation and study of materials and sources to establish facts and reach new conclusions
systematic investigation to discover facts

Examples

His team is conducting important research into renewable energy sources.
The university received a grant to fund scientific research programs.
Her research on the topic earned her an international award.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: study - application of the mind to learning and understanding a subject
  • Synonym: experiment - a scientific procedure to test a hypothesis
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or information

Etymology

From Old French 'recercher', from re- (expressing intensive force) + cerchier (to search).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɜrtʃ / (AmE), / rɪˈsɜːtʃ / (BrE)
the systematic investigation or study of materials, sources, or phenomena in order to discover facts or reach new conclusions
systematic investigation for knowledge

Examples

Extensive research was conducted before the new medicine was approved.
Her research into ancient manuscripts led to an exciting discovery.
The project required months of research and analysis.

Synset Examples

their pottery deserves more research than it has received

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investigation - careful inquiry or examination, especially officially or for facts
  • Synonym: experiment - a scientific procedure to make a discovery or test a hypothesis

Etymology

From Old French *recercher* (to search closely), from re- (again) + *cercher* (to search).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05805605-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ rɪˈsɜrtʃ / (AmE), / rɪˈsɜːtʃ / (BrE)
to study or investigate a subject in detail to discover facts or reach new conclusions
to study or investigate thoroughly

Examples

As part of her thesis, she had to research the effects of global warming on agriculture.
He spent weeks researching different companies before choosing where to apply.
Before publishing the article, the journalist researched all the relevant facts thoroughly.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to investigate - to carry out a systematic or formal inquiry
  • Synonym: to explore - to examine or discuss a subject in detail

Etymology

From Old French *recercher* (to seek out), from re- (again) + cerchier (to search).
