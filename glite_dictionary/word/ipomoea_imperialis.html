Ipomoea imperialis

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.pəˈmi.i ɪmˌpɪəriˈælɪs / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.pəˈmiː.ə ɪmˌpɪərɪˈælɪs / (BrE)
A hybrid plant in the morning glory family, derived from Ipomoea nil, known for its ornamental flowers.
a hybrid morning glory plant

Examples

The gardener planted several Ipomoea imperialis vines along the fence for their vibrant blooms.
Botanists have studied Ipomoea imperialis for its unique hybrid characteristics.
The morning sunlight highlights the beauty of Ipomoea imperialis in full bloom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ipomoea nil - a species of flowering plant in the morning glory family

Etymology

From the genus name Ipomoea, from Greek 'ips' (worm) and 'homoios' (resembling), referring to twining plants; 'imperialis' from Latin meaning 'imperial' or 'regal'.
