otorhinolaryngologist

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊtoʊˌraɪnoʊˌlærɪnˈɡɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˌəʊtəʊˌraɪnəʊˌlærɪŋˈɡɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a doctor who specializes in the medical and surgical treatment of diseases and disorders of the ear, nose, and throat
specialist in ear, nose, and throat disorders
medical

Examples

After months of sinus issues, she finally consulted an otorhinolaryngologist for a thorough examination.
The otorhinolaryngologist recommended surgery to correct his hearing problem.
Medical students shadowed an otorhinolaryngologist to learn more about ENT procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ENT - abbreviation for ear, nose, and throat specialist

Etymology

From Greek *ous, ōtos* (ear), *rhīnas, rhīnos* (nose), *larynx* (throat), and *-logia* (study).
