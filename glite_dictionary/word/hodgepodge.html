hodgepodge

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːdʒˌpɑːdʒ / (AmE), / ˈhɒdʒˌpɒdʒ / (BrE)
A confused mixture of various things; a jumble or medley.
a mixed jumble of things

Examples

The artist's living room was a hodgepodge of vintage furniture and modern art.
His explanation sounded like a hodgepodge of unrelated ideas thrown together at the last minute.
For dinner, we ended up eating a hodgepodge of leftovers from the fridge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jumble - a confused mixture or mess
  • Synonym: medley - a varied mixture of people or things
  • Synonym: mixture - a combination of different things

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'hochepot' (stew, mixture), from Old French.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑdʒˌpɑdʒ / (AmE), / ˈhɒdʒˌpɒdʒ / (BrE)
A confused or jumbled mixture of different things, ideas, or elements.
confused mixture; jumble

Examples

The museum's new exhibition is a hodgepodge of modern and classical artwork.
Her novel is criticized for being a hodgepodge of disconnected themes and plots.
The report was a hodgepodge of statistics, opinions, and personal anecdotes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hotchpotch - another term for a mixed or jumbled collection

Etymology

Probably from Anglo-Norman *hochepot* (stew, mixture), influenced by Middle English forms.
