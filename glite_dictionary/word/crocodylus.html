Crocodylus

1. PROPN

/ ˌkrɑː.kəˈdaɪ.ləs / (AmE), / ˌkrɒ.kəˈdaɪ.ləs / (BrE)
the genus of large reptiles comprising the true crocodiles, found in tropical regions of Africa, Asia, the Americas, and Australia
genus of true crocodiles

Examples

The species Crocodylus niloticus is known as the Nile crocodile.
Researchers compared skulls from various members of the Crocodylus genus.
Crocodylus includes several species found in both the Old and New Worlds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crocodile - large aquatic reptile with a long snout and powerful jaws

Etymology

From Latin Crocodylus, from Ancient Greek krokódilos (crocodile).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkroʊˈkɑdəˌləs / (AmE), / ˌkrɒkəˈdaɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of large, predatory, semi-aquatic reptiles comprising the true crocodiles, native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, the Americas, and Australia.
genus of true crocodiles

Examples

The Nile crocodile, a member of the genus Crocodylus, is found throughout much of Africa.
Several species in the genus Crocodylus are considered threatened or endangered.
Biologists studied the distribution of Crocodylus populations across different river systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crocodile - large aquatic reptile; member of genus Crocodylus

Etymology

From Latin 'crocodylus', from Ancient Greek 'krokódilos' meaning 'crocodile'.

Synset POS: n
