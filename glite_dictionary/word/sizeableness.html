sizeableness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪ.zə.bəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsaɪ.zə.bl̩.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being sizeable; largeness in size, extent, or number
state of being sizeable or large

Examples

The sizeableness of the stadium allows it to host international sporting events with ease.
We were impressed by the sizeableness of the company's annual profit.
She admired the sizeableness of the ancient redwood trees towering above her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: largeness - the quality of being large in size or extent
  • Antonym: smallness - the quality of being small in size or extent

Etymology

From 'sizeable' + '-ness', forming a noun meaning the state of being sizeable.
