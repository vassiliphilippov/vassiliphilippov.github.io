Amerind

1. NOUN

/ ˈæm.ə.rɪnd / (AmE), / ˈæm.ər.ɪnd / (BrE)
a member of the indigenous peoples of the Americas, or any of the languages spoken by these peoples
member/language of indigenous peoples of the Americas

Examples

Many Amerind languages are spoken throughout Central and South America.
Some linguists study the grammar and structure of various Amerind languages.
The museum features artifacts from different Amerind cultures across North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Amerindian - an indigenous person of the Americas

Etymology

Blend of American and Indian.

2. ADJ

/ ˈæməˌrɪnd / (AmE), / ˈamərɪnd / (BrE)
Relating to Native Americans or their languages and cultures, especially as a classification in anthropology or linguistics.
relating to Native Americans/cultures/languages

Examples

The museum features many Amerind artifacts from the region.
He specializes in the study of Amerind languages.
The Amerind tradition of storytelling is rich and varied.

Synset Examples

Native American religions
Indian arrowheads

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Native American - A member of the indigenous peoples of the Americas

Etymology

Blend of 'American' and 'Indian,' originally used in anthropological and linguistic classification.
