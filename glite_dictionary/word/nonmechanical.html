nonmechanical

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑnməˈkænɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnmɪˈkænɪkəl / (BrE)
(of a person) lacking mechanical skills or aptitude; not good at understanding or working with machines or technical devices.
lacking mechanical skills

Examples

Because she is nonmechanical, she prefers creative hobbies over fixing appliances.
His nonmechanical nature made assembling the furniture a frustrating experience.
The company offered special training for nonmechanical staff who struggled with the new equipment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mechanically minded - having an aptitude for understanding or working with machinery

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' meaning 'not', and 'mechanical'.

2. ADJ

/ nɑn.məˈkæn.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / nɒn.mɪˈkæn.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
Not operated by or involving machinery or mechanical means; not mechanical.
not operated by machinery

Examples

The artist prefers nonmechanical tools like pencils and brushes for her work.
Many traditional watches are nonmechanical and use digital technology instead.
He developed a nonmechanical method for measuring the temperature.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mechanical - operated by machinery; relating to machines

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' (not) and 'mechanical'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01504445-a
Synset POS: a
