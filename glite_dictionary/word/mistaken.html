mistaken

1. ADJ

/ mɪˈsteɪkən / (AmE), / mɪˈsteɪkən / (BrE)
Wrong or incorrect in opinion or action; arising from an error or misunderstanding.
wrong or incorrect; arising from an error

Examples

She realized she was mistaken about the time of the meeting.
His mistaken belief led to unnecessary confusion.
Many people are mistaken in thinking this rule applies to everyone.

Synset Examples

a false assumption
a mistaken view of the situation

Related Terms

  • Antonym: correct - free from error; accurate
  • Synonym: erroneous - incorrect; based on error

Etymology

From past participle of 'mistake', from Middle English 'mistaken', influenced by the verb 'take'.

2. ADJ

/ mɪˈsteɪkən / (AmE), / mɪˈsteɪkən / (BrE)
wrong in one's belief, opinion, or understanding; based on an incorrect assumption or idea
wrong in belief or judgment

Examples

She realized she was mistaken about the date of the meeting.
His mistaken assumption led to confusion.
Many people hold the mistaken belief that bats are blind.

Synset Examples

well-meaning but misguided teachers
a mistaken belief
mistaken identity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrong - incorrect; not true or accurate
  • Antonym: correct - free from error; true or accurate

Etymology

Past participle of 'mistake', from Middle English 'mistaken' (to misunderstand), from Old Norse 'misteka' (to take wrongly).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02043804-s
Synset POS: s
