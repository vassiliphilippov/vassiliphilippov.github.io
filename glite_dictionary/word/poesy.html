poesy

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊ.ɪ.si / (AmE), / ˈpəʊ.ɪ.si / (BrE)
literary works in verse; poetry, especially in the form of metrical composition.
poetry; verse literature

Examples

Many scholars have devoted their lives to the study of ancient poesy.
The evening's performance was filled with music and poesy that delighted the audience.
She found solace in reading the poesy of the Romantic poets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poetry - the art or work of a poet; poems collectively

Etymology

From Old French 'poesie', from Latin 'poēsis', from Ancient Greek 'ποίησις' (poiēsis, 'making; poetry').
