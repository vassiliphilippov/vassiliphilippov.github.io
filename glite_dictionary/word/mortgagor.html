mortgagor

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɔrɡəˈdʒɔr / (AmE), / ˌmɔːɡɪˈdʒɔː / (BrE)
a person who borrows money by giving a mortgage on their property as security for the loan
person who gives a mortgage as security for a loan
legal

Examples

The mortgagor is required to make monthly payments until the loan is fully repaid.
If a mortgagor falls behind on payments, the property may be subject to foreclosure.
The bank approved the loan after confirming the mortgagor's creditworthiness.

Synset Examples

we became mortgagors when the bank accepted our mortgage and loaned us the money to buy our new home

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mortgagee - the person or institution who lends money and receives the mortgage as security

Etymology

From Old French 'mort gageur', based on 'mortgage' and the legal suffix '-or'.
