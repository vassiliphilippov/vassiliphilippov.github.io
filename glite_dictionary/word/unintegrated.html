unintegrated

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈɪntəˌɡreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈɪntɪɡreɪtɪd / (BrE)
not combined or incorporated into a whole; not integrated.
not joined or combined as a whole

Examples

The new employee felt unintegrated in the team during his first few weeks.
Several unintegrated features caused confusion among the software's users.
The unintegrated data sets made analysis difficult and time-consuming.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: integrated - combined into a whole; made a part of something

Etymology

From un- (not) + integrate.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈɪntəˌɡreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈɪntɪˌɡreɪtɪd / (BrE)
not joined or incorporated into a larger group; separated or kept apart from others or from the main group.
not incorporated into a larger group

Examples

The committee remained unintegrated, with each member working separately on their tasks.
Many minority communities felt unintegrated within the broader society.
The museum's collection is still largely unintegrated with little effort to connect the exhibits.

Synset Examples

a segregated school system
a segregated neighborhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: segregated - separated or set apart from others or from the majority group
  • Antonym: integrated - combined into a whole or made part of a larger group

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (meaning 'not') + 'integrate' (from Latin integrare, 'to make whole').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01329869-a
Synset POS: a
