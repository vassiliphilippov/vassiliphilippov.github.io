trier

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪər / (AmE), / ˈtraɪə / (BrE)
a person, especially a judge, who examines and settles a case or dispute, particularly in legal contexts
person who examines and settles a case
legal

Examples

The trier of fact must consider all the evidence before reaching a verdict.
In some legal systems, the jury serves as the trier of fact.
The role of the trier is critical in ensuring a fair trial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judge - an official who decides cases in a court of law

Etymology

From Old French *trier* (to sift, to select), from Late Latin *tritare* (to thresh).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪər / (AmE), / ˈtraɪə / (BrE)
a person who makes persistent efforts or attempts to do something, especially despite difficulties or failures
a person who persistently tries

Examples

Even after several failures, she remained a trier and never lost hope.
Although he doesn't always succeed, everyone agrees he is a real trier.
Every team needs at least one trier who never gives up, no matter the odds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attempt - to make an effort to achieve or complete something
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop making an effort; to cease trying

Etymology

From try + -er, forming agent nouns in English.
