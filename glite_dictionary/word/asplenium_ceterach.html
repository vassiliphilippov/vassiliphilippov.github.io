Asplenium ceterach

1. NOUN

/ æsˈpliːniəm səˈtɛræk / (AmE), / æsˈpliːniəm sɛˈtɛrək / (BrE)
A small European fern with chaffy, leathery fronds, commonly known as rustyback.
a small European fern (rustyback)

Examples

The dry stone walls were covered in Asplenium ceterach thriving in the cracks.
Botanists identified the fern as Asplenium ceterach due to its rusty-colored scales.
In southern Europe, Asplenium ceterach is a common sight on rocky slopes.

Etymology

From Latin; Asplenium is a genus name, ceterach from medieval Latin for this fern.
