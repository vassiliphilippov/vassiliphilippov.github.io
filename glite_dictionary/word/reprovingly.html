reprovingly

1. ADV

/ rɪˈpruvɪŋli / (AmE), / rɪˈpruːvɪŋli / (BrE)
In a way that shows disapproval or criticism of someone's actions or behavior.
in a disapproving or critical manner

Examples

She looked at him reprovingly after his careless remark.
The teacher shook her head reprovingly when the student arrived late.
He sighed and spoke reprovingly, warning them not to repeat the mistake.

Synset Examples

she spoke to him reprovingly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disapprovingly - in a manner showing disapproval

Etymology

From 'reproving' + '-ly', where 'reproving' comes from 'reprove' (from Old French 'reprover', from Latin 'reprobare', meaning 'disapprove').
