pica

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˈpiː.kə / (BrE)
A medical condition, especially in children, characterized by the persistent eating of substances that are not food and have no nutritional value.
eating disorder involving non-food items
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the child with pica after learning she regularly ate paper and chalk.
Children with pica may crave objects like dirt, clay, or hair.
Long-term pica can lead to serious health complications if not addressed.

Etymology

From Latin *pica* (magpie), referencing the bird's indiscriminate eating habits.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪkə / (AmE), / ˈpaɪkə / (BrE)
a unit of measurement in printing, equal to 1/6 of an inch, used chiefly for measuring type, lines, and column widths
printing unit, 1/6 inch

Examples

The text was set using a pica measurement to ensure uniform column width.
A margin of two picas was left between the images and the text.
Designers often convert pica values to pixels when preparing layouts for digital use.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: point - a smaller unit of length in printing, 1/72 of an inch

Etymology

From Latin 'pica', meaning magpie; adopted in printing terminology for the type size and later, measurement.
