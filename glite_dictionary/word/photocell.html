photocell

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊtəˌsɛl / (AmE), / ˈfəʊtəʊˌsɛl / (BrE)
A device that detects and measures light or other electromagnetic radiation by converting it into an electrical signal.
light-detecting electrical device

Examples

The security system uses a photocell to detect when someone enters the room.
Streetlights often turn on automatically at dusk thanks to a photocell sensor.
A faulty photocell can cause automatic doors to malfunction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photoelectric cell - Device that generates electricity or changes resistance when exposed to light

Etymology

From photo- (relating to light) + cell (basic unit, here an electrical cell).
