albite

1. NOUN

/ ˈælˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈæl.baɪt / (BrE)
a white or pale mineral of the feldspar group, containing sodium and forming rocks commonly found in igneous and metamorphic environments
a sodium feldspar mineral

Examples

The granite sample contained a significant amount of albite mixed with quartz and mica.
Geologists identified the white crystals as albite after examining their structure under the microscope.
Albite is often found alongside other feldspar minerals in metamorphic rocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feldspar - a group of rock-forming minerals that includes albite

Etymology

From Latin 'albus' (white), referring to its typical color.
