placating

1. ADJ

/ ˈpleɪˌkeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpleɪkeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
intended to pacify or calm someone, especially by yielding to their demands or making concessions
intended to calm or pacify

Examples

He offered a placating smile in hopes of easing the tension in the room.
The prime minister made several placating gestures to calm the protesters.
Her words took on a placating tone as she tried to resolve the argument.

Synset Examples

the appeasing concessions to the Nazis at Munich
placating (or placative) gestures
an astonishingly placatory speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appeasing - intended to pacify or placate by acceding to demands
  • Antonym: provocative - intended to provoke or anger

Etymology

From Latin placatus, past participle of placare (to calm, to appease).
