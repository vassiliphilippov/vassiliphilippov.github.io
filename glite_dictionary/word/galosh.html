galosh

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈlɑʃ / (AmE), / ɡəˈlɒʃ / (BrE)
a waterproof overshoe, typically made of rubber, worn over ordinary shoes to protect them from water or snow
waterproof overshoe for rain or snow

Examples

He put on his galoshes before heading out into the rainy street.
During the winter, children often wear galoshes to keep their feet dry in the snow.
My grandfather always kept a pair of galoshes near the door for stormy days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overshoe - a shoe worn over another shoe, often for protection

Etymology

From French 'galoche', from Medieval Latin 'galopia', meaning a wooden shoe.
