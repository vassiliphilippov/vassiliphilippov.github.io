Tashunca-Uitco

1. PROPN

/ təˌʃuːŋkə ˈwiːtʃoʊ / (AmE), / təˌʃʊŋkə ˈwiːtʃəʊ / (BrE)
A chief of the Sioux who resisted the invasion of the Black Hills and joined Sitting Bull in the defeat of General Custer at Little Bighorn (1849–1877). Also known as Crazy Horse.
Sioux chief; leader at Little Bighorn

Examples

Tashunca-Uitco played a central role in organizing resistance at the Battle of the Little Bighorn.
The legacy of Tashunca-Uitco remains significant in Sioux history and culture.
Many historians regard Tashunca-Uitco as one of the most important Native American leaders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Crazy Horse - Sioux chief; alternate name for Tashunca-Uitco
  • Synonym: Sitting Bull - Hunkpapa Lakota leader; allied with Tashunca-Uitco

Etymology

From the Lakota language, meaning 'His Horse is Crazy' or 'Crazy Horse'.
