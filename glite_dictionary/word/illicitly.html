illicitly

1. ADV

/ ɪˈlɪsɪtli / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪsɪtli / (BrE)
in a way that is not allowed by law, rules, or custom; unlawfully or improperly.
in an unlawful or forbidden manner

Examples

The company was fined after it was discovered they had acted illicitly in obtaining contracts.
He acquired the confidential documents illicitly, bypassing proper authorization channels.
She imported rare artifacts illicitly, violating international trade laws.

Synset Examples

He acted illegitimately when he increased the rent fourfold

Related Terms

  • Antonym: legally - in a way that is allowed by law
  • Antonym: lawfully - in a lawful or permitted manner

Etymology

From Latin illicitus (not allowed), from in- (not) + licitus (lawful).

2. ADV

/ ɪˈlɪsɪtli / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪsɪtli / (BrE)
In a way that is not allowed by law or rules; illegally or unlawfully.
in an illegal or unlawful manner

Examples

He was arrested after he illicitly obtained confidential information from the database.
The group was accused of illicitly importing goods into the country.
She had been illicitly selling prescription drugs online for months.

Synset Examples

they dumped the waste illegally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: legally - in a way that is allowed by law
  • Antonym: lawfully - in a manner permitted by law

Etymology

From illicit + -ly; ultimately from Latin illicitus (unlawful).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00155669-r
Synset POS: r
