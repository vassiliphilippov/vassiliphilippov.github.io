antimeson

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tiˈmɛ.sɑn / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˈmiː.sɒn / (BrE)
the antiparticle of a meson, a subatomic particle made up of a quark and an antiquark
the antiparticle of a meson
medical

Examples

In high-energy collisions, an antimeson can annihilate with a meson, producing energy.
Researchers studied the properties of the antimeson in the laboratory to understand matter-antimatter interactions.
Each type of meson has a corresponding antimeson with opposite quantum numbers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: meson - a subatomic particle made of one quark and one antiquark

Etymology

Formed from 'anti-' (against, opposite) and 'meson' (from Greek 'mesos', meaning 'middle').
