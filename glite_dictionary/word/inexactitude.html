inexactitude

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnɪɡˈzæktɪˌtud / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪɡˈzæktɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
The quality or state of being not exact; inaccuracy or imprecision.
lack of exactness; inaccuracy

Examples

The inexactitude of his measurements led to errors in the experiment.
She criticized the report for its inexactitude and lack of supporting data.
Legal documents must avoid inexactitude to prevent misunderstandings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inaccuracy - the state of being not accurate
  • Antonym: precision - the state of being accurate and exact

Etymology

From French 'inexactitude', from Latin roots 'in-' (not) + 'exactus' (precise).
