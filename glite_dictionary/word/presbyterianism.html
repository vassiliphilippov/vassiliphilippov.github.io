Presbyterianism

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɛzbɪˈtɪriənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌprɛzbɪˈtɪəriənɪzəm / (BrE)
The doctrines, practices, and church government associated with the Presbyterian Church, based on the teachings of John Calvin.
doctrines and practices of the Presbyterian Church

Examples

Many of the early settlers in the region practiced Presbyterianism and built their own churches.
He studied the history of Presbyterianism as part of his religious education courses.
The structure of Presbyterianism emphasizes leadership by elected elders rather than by bishops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Calvinism - the theological system of John Calvin, emphasizing predestination and sovereignty of God

Etymology

From Presbyterian + -ism, relating to the Presbyterian form of church governance.
