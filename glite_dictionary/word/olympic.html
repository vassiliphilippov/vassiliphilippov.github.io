Olympic

1. ADJ

/ əˈlɪmpɪk / (AmE), / əˈlɪmpɪk / (BrE)
relating to the region of Olympia in Greece or its inhabitants
of Olympia, Greece or its people

Examples

The Olympic plain is known for its ancient history and fertile land.
Several artifacts from the Olympic region are housed in the national museum.
The Olympic people developed unique religious festivals in antiquity.

Synset Examples

Olympian plain

Etymology

From Greek Olympos, the mountain associated with the area; Olympia being the site of the ancient Olympic Games.

2. ADJ

/ əˈlɪmpɪk / (AmE), / əˈlɪmpɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to the Olympic Games, an international sports competition held every four years.
relating to the Olympic Games

Examples

She won an Olympic gold medal in swimming at the last Games.
Many athletes train for years to compete at the Olympic level.
The city was transformed in preparation for the Olympic Games.

Synset Examples

Olympic winners

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Paralympic - relating to the Paralympic Games, an international sports event for athletes with disabilities

Etymology

From Greek 'Olympikos', relating to Olympia, where the ancient Olympic Games were held.
