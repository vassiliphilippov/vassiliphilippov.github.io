Meccano

1. NOUN

/ məˈkɑːnoʊ / (AmE), / məˈkɑːnəʊ / (BrE)
a brand of construction set consisting of metal strips, wheels, and other parts, used for building mechanical models
brand of model construction set

Examples

My brother used his Meccano set to build a working crane.
The museum had an impressive display of Meccano models from the early 20th century.
He spent his childhood tinkering with Meccano to create miniature bridges and vehicles.

Etymology

From the trademark 'Meccano', created in 1907 by Frank Hornby, possibly based on 'mechanics'.
