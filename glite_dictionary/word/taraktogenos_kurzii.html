Taraktogenos kurzii

1. NOUN

/ təˌræktəˈdʒiːnoʊs ˈkɜːrziː / (AmE), / təˌræktəˈdʒiːnɒs ˈkɜːziː / (BrE)
An East Indian tree whose oily seeds yield chaulmoogra oil, formerly used to treat leprosy.
East Indian tree; source of chaulmoogra oil

Examples

The seeds of Taraktogenos kurzii are prized for their medicinal oil.
Researchers studied extracts from Taraktogenos kurzii to evaluate their efficacy in treating skin diseases.
Chaulmoogra oil is pressed from the fruit of Taraktogenos kurzii.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaulmoogra oil - oil obtained from Taraktogenos kurzii seeds, used for medicinal purposes
