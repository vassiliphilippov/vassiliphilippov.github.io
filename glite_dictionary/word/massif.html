massif

1. NOUN

/ mæˈsif / (AmE), / ˈmæs.iːf / (BrE)
a compact group of connected mountains or a block of the earth's crust that is bounded by faults and forms peaks within a mountain range
connected group of mountains or crustal block

Examples

The Mont Blanc massif dominates the landscape of the French Alps.
This region's geology is shaped by a large granite massif.
Hikers attempted to cross the rugged terrain of the Pyrenees massif.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain range - a series of connected mountains forming a long line

Etymology

From French massif (massive, massive structure), from masse (mass).
