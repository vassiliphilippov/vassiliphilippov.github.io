vade mecum

1. NOUN

/ ˌveɪdi ˈmiːkəm / (AmE), / ˌveɪdi ˈmiːkəm / (BrE)
a small handbook or guide, kept for ready reference and carried at all times
handbook for ready reference

Examples

He always carried a vade mecum in his pocket for quick reference.
This travel vade mecum includes maps and local customs for every city.
The physician’s vade mecum was consulted regularly during the emergency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handbook - a book of instructions or guidance
  • Synonym: manual - a handbook, especially one giving practical instructions

Etymology

From Latin vade mecum, meaning 'go with me'.
