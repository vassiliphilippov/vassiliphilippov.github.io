Hydrangeaceae

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌdreɪndʒiˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / haɪˌdrændʒɪˈiːsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants that includes hydrangeas, sometimes classified within the Saxifragaceae family
a family of flowering plants (hydrangeas, etc.)

Examples

Many ornamental shrubs in the Hydrangeaceae family are popular in gardens worldwide.
The Hydrangeaceae family is native to Asia and North America.
Botanists sometimes debate whether Hydrangeaceae should be included in Saxifragaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saxifragaceae - a family of mostly herbaceous flowering plants

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Hydrangea (the type genus) + -aceae (a standard suffix for plant family names).

2. NOUN

/ haɪˌdreɪndʒiˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / hʌɪˌdrandʒɪˈiːsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, sometimes classified within Saxifragaceae, that includes hydrangeas and related genera
family of flowering plants including hydrangeas

Examples

The Hydrangeaceae family is known for its ornamental garden shrubs.
Some botanists classify Hydrangeaceae within the Saxifragaceae family.
Hydrangeas, a popular garden plant, belong to the Hydrangeaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saxifragaceae - a family of flowering plants sometimes including Hydrangeaceae

Etymology

From the genus name Hydrangea, itself from Greek 'hydor' (water) and 'angeion' (vessel), referring to the shape of the seed capsules.
