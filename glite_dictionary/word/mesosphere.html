mesosphere

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛsəˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈmiːsəsˌfɪə / (BrE)
the atmospheric layer of the Earth situated between the stratosphere and the thermosphere, typically between 50 and 85 kilometers above the surface
atmospheric layer above the stratosphere

Examples

The mesosphere is where most meteorites burn up before reaching the Earth's surface.
Temperatures in the mesosphere decrease with altitude, making it the coldest atmospheric layer.
Scientists study the mesosphere to better understand atmospheric tides and noctilucent clouds.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: stratosphere - the atmospheric layer below the mesosphere
  • Degree Higher: thermosphere - the atmospheric layer above the mesosphere

Etymology

From Greek 'mesos' (middle) and 'sphaira' (sphere).
