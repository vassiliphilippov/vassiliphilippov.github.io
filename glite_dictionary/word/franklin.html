franklin

1. NOUN

/ ˈfræŋklɪn / (AmE), / ˈfraŋklɪn / (BrE)
in medieval England, a free landowner who was not of noble birth, especially in the 14th and 15th centuries
historical free landowner (not noble)
archaic

Examples

In Chaucer's tales, the franklin is portrayed as a generous and hospitable man.
A franklin held land but did not possess the privileges of the nobility.
By the fifteenth century, the status of a franklin became increasingly rare in English society.

Etymology

From Middle English frankeleyn, from Anglo-Norman *fraunclein* (freeholder), from Old French *franc* (free).
