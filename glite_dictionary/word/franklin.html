Franklin

1. PROPN

/ ˈfræŋklɪn / (AmE), / ˈfræŋklɪn / (BrE)
(1706–1790) An American statesman, author, printer, scientist, and inventor; a Founding Father who helped draft the Declaration of Independence and the Constitution, and is noted for his research in electricity.
American statesman, author, and inventor

Examples

Many history textbooks describe Franklin's contributions to the drafting of the U.S. Constitution.
Franklin conducted famous experiments with electricity in the 18th century.
The city of Philadelphia honors Franklin with numerous monuments and museums.
