shovelful

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃʌvəlfʊl / (AmE), / ˈʃʌvəlfʊl / (BrE)
An amount of something that fills a shovel.
amount that a shovel holds

Examples

He tossed another shovelful of earth onto the pile.
With every shovelful, the hole grew deeper and wider.
She collected a shovelful of snow and dumped it on the driveway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spadeful - the quantity a spade can hold

Etymology

From 'shovel' + 'ful', following the pattern of words like 'handful' or 'cupful'.
