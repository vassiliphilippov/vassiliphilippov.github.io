damascene

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæməˌsin / (AmE), / ˈdaməsiːn / (BrE)
A design produced by inlaying gold or silver into steel, especially for decorative purposes on weapons or armor.
decorative metal inlay design

Examples

The sword's hilt was decorated with an intricate damascene of gold and silver.
The museum showcased a collection of medieval armor featuring beautiful damascene designs.
He learned the ancient art of creating damascene patterns for luxury daggers in Toledo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damascening - the art or process of making damascene designs

Etymology

From Damascus, the capital of Syria, historically known for metalwork.
