Olearia argophylla

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈlɪəriə ˌɑːrɡoʊˈfɪlə / (AmE), / əʊˈlɪəriə ˌɑːɡəʊˈfɪlə / (BrE)
A musk-scented shrub or tree native to southern and southeastern Australia, with creamy-yellow flower heads.
Australian musk-scented shrub or tree

Examples

The lush foliage of Olearia argophylla stood out in the damp forest undergrowth.
Gardeners value Olearia argophylla for its silky silver leaves and aromatic flowers.
During spring, the Olearia argophylla produces clusters of creamy-yellow blossoms that attract native insects.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Olearia', named after the botanist Adam Olearius, and Greek 'argophylla' meaning 'silver-leaved'.
