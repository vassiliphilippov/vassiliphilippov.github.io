to unroll

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈroʊl / (AmE), / ʌnˈrəʊl / (BrE)
to open or spread out something that has been rolled up, or to become open or spread out in this way
to open or spread out something rolled

Examples

She began to unroll the carpet in the living room.
With a single motion, he unrolled the map across the table.
The flag will unroll as soon as the wind picks up.

Synset Examples

unfurl a banner

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to roll up - to form something into a roll or coil
  • Synonym: to unfurl - to spread something out after it has been rolled or folded

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (reversing an action) + 'roll' (from Old French 'rolle', from Latin 'rotulus').

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈɹoʊl / (AmE), / ʌnˈrəʊl / (BrE)
to open or spread out (something) that has been rolled or twisted up; to undo the rolling of
reverse rolling or twisting; open up

Examples

She began to unroll the old map on the table.
Carefully unroll the bandage to examine the wound.
The teacher asked the student to unroll the poster for the presentation.

Synset Examples

unwind a ball of yarn

Related Terms

  • Antonym: roll - to turn something over and over to form a cylinder or ball
  • Antonym: wind - to twist or coil something around itself or another object

Etymology

From un- (prefix meaning 'reverse') + roll (from Old French 'roler', from Latin 'rotulus', a small wheel).

Source: WordNet
