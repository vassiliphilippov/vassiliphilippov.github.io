to sightsee

1. VERB

/ ˈsaɪtˌsi / (AmE), / ˈsaɪtsiː / (BrE)
to visit famous or interesting places, especially as a tourist
visit famous/interesting places

Examples

We plan to sightsee in Rome during our trip to Italy.
They spent the afternoon sightseeing around the old city center.
I sightsee whenever I travel to a new country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tour - to travel for pleasure, often visiting places of interest

Etymology

Formed from 'sight' (view, spectacle) and 'see', first used in the mid-19th century.
