Latvian

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæt.vi.ən / (AmE), / ˈlat.vi.ən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Latvia
native or inhabitant of Latvia

Examples

The Latvian greeted us warmly when we arrived in Riga.
As a proud Latvian, she celebrates all the national holidays.
Many Latvians speak multiple languages fluently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Latvian - the Baltic language spoken in Latvia

Etymology

From Latvia, the name of the country, plus the suffix -an.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlæt.vi.ən / (AmE), / ˈlæt.vi.ən / (BrE)
relating to Latvia, its people, or its language
of or relating to Latvia

Examples

The Latvian capital, Riga, is famous for its Art Nouveau architecture.
She studied traditional Latvian music during her time abroad.
Many Latvian dishes feature potatoes and pork as main ingredients.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Lithuanian - relating to Lithuania or its people or language
  • Degree Lower: Estonian - relating to Estonia or its people or language

Etymology

From Latvia + -an (denoting origin or association).

3. NOUN

/ ˈlæt.vi.ən / (AmE), / ˈlat.vi.ən / (BrE)
the official language spoken in Latvia, belonging to the Baltic branch of the Indo-European language family
language of Latvia

Examples

He is fluent in both Russian and Latvian.
The book was originally written in Latvian before being translated into English.
Many signs in Riga are printed in both English and Latvian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lithuanian - the language spoken in Lithuania, also a Baltic language

Etymology

From 'Latvia' (the country) + English suffix '-an'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06959117-n
Synset POS: n
