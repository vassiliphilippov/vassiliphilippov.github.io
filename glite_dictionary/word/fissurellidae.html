Fissurellidae

1. NOUN

/ fɪʃəˈrɛlɪˌdi / (AmE), / fɪsjʊˈrɛlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine gastropod mollusks that includes keyhole limpets and slit limpets, characterized by a shell with a hole or slit at the apex.
family of marine mollusks (keyhole and slit limpets)

Examples

Many researchers study the diverse shell shapes found in Fissurellidae species.
The Fissurellidae family is distributed in oceans across the globe.
Keyhole limpets, part of the Fissurellidae, play an important role in their marine ecosystems.

Etymology

From New Latin, named after the type genus Fissurella.
