bradawl

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrædˌɔl / (AmE), / ˈbrædˌɔːl / (BrE)
a small hand tool with a pointed blade used for making holes in wood, especially for small nails or screws
tool for making holes in wood

Examples

He used a bradawl to make a pilot hole before inserting the screw.
Always keep a bradawl in your toolkit for precise woodworking tasks.
This old bradawl belonged to my grandfather, who was a carpenter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awl - a pointed tool for piercing holes, generally in leather or wood

Etymology

From 'brad' (a small nail) + 'awl' (a pointed tool), both of uncertain origin but related to Old English and Old Norse roots.
