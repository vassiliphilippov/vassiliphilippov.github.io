to impede

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈpid / (AmE), / ɪmˈpiːd / (BrE)
to delay or prevent the progress or movement of someone or something by obstructing them; to hinder
to hinder or obstruct

Examples

Heavy traffic can impede your ability to arrive on time.
Strict regulations may impede innovation in certain industries.
The fallen tree impeded access to the hiking trail for several hours.

Synset Examples

She is impeding the progress of our project

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hinder - to make it difficult for something to happen; to obstruct
  • Synonym: to obstruct - to block or get in the way of something; to hinder
  • Antonym: to facilitate - to make a process or action easier or help it happen

Etymology

From Latin impedire (to entangle, hinder), from in- (in) + pes, ped- (foot).

2. VERB

/ ɪmˈpid / (AmE), / ɪmˈpiːd / (BrE)
to delay or prevent the progress or movement of someone or something by obstructing them
to hinder or obstruct progress

Examples

Road construction will impede the flow of traffic throughout the week.
Heavy snowfall can impede rescue efforts in remote areas.
Poor communication may impede progress on the project.

Synset Examples

obstruct the path

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hinder - to make it difficult for someone to do something or for something to happen
  • Antonym: to facilitate - to make an action or process easy or easier

Etymology

From Latin impedire, meaning 'to shackle the feet', from in- (in) + pes, ped- (foot).
