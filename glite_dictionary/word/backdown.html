backdown

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈbæk.daʊn / (BrE)
an act of withdrawing from a stated position or commitment
withdrawal from a position

Examples

The company’s sudden backdown surprised industry analysts.
After public criticism, the government issued a backdown on the new policy.
Her refusal to issue a backdown made negotiations difficult.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retreat - the act of withdrawing or backing away from a position
  • Synonym: concession - the act of yielding or compromising on a previously held position
  • Antonym: stand - refusal to back down or hold one's ground
