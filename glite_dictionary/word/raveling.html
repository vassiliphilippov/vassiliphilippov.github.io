raveling

1. NOUN

/ ˈrævəlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈravəlɪŋ / (BrE)
a loose thread or fiber that has come undone from woven fabric
loose thread from fabric

Examples

She picked a raveling off the hem of her skirt.
The old sofa had ravelings sticking out from the cushions.
Be careful not to pull on that raveling, or the fabric might tear further.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thread - a long, thin strand of cotton, nylon, or other fibers used in sewing
  • Synonym: fray - an unravelled or worn edge of fabric

Etymology

From 'ravel' (to untangle or become undone), with the -ing suffix forming a noun.
