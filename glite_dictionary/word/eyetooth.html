eyetooth

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪˌtuθ / (AmE), / ˈaɪˌtuːθ / (BrE)
one of the four pointed teeth between the incisors and the premolars in each jaw; also called a canine tooth.
pointed tooth between incisors and premolars

Examples

She had a gold cap fitted on her upper eyetooth after it chipped.
Vampires in stories are always depicted with long, sharp eyeteeth.
He grinned, revealing his prominent eyetooth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canine - another term for eyetooth; pointed tooth between incisors and premolars

Etymology

From 'eye' + 'tooth', referring to the upper canine’s position beneath the eye.
