Lancelot

1. PROPN

/ ˈlænsəˌlɑt / (AmE), / ˈlæn.sə.lət / (BrE)
A legendary knight of the Round Table in Arthurian legend, noted as King Arthur's close companion and, in many versions, the lover of Queen Guinevere.
Arthurian knight; lover of Guinevere

Examples

Lancelot is often depicted as both brave and deeply conflicted in stories of the Round Table.
The tale of Lancelot and Guinevere is one of the most famous romances in Arthurian legend.
Many poets have written about the tragic fate of Lancelot after his affair with Queen Guinevere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: King Arthur - Legendary king of Britain in Arthurian tales
  • Synonym: Guinevere - Arthur's queen, Lancelot's lover in the legend

Etymology

From Old French Lancelot, of uncertain origin, possibly a diminutive of Lance or derived from Germanic elements.
