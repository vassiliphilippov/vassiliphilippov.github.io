babbler

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæblər / (AmE), / ˈbæblə / (BrE)
any of various insectivorous Old World songbirds known for their loud, continuous calls, formerly classified within the family Muscicapidae.
a songbird noted for its loud, persistent calls

Examples

A flock of babblers filled the forest with constant, noisy calling.
The guide pointed out a rare species of babbler in the undergrowth.
Some babblers are well-camouflaged, making them harder to spot despite their loud songs.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæblər / (AmE), / ˈbab.lər / (BrE)
a person who speaks incessantly in a foolish or irritating manner; someone who talks excessively without making much sense
person who talks foolishly or excessively

Examples

The meeting was disrupted by a babbler who wouldn't stop talking over everyone else.
She found herself sitting next to a babbler at the dinner party and couldn't get a word in edgewise.
Though he considered himself charming, most people saw him as a babbler who rarely had anything important to say.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatterbox - a person who talks a lot, often about trivial matters
  • Synonym: windbag - a person who talks too much, typically in a boring or dull way
  • Antonym: silent - not speaking; not making or accompanied by any sound

Etymology

From Middle English 'bablen' (to prattle), imitative origin.
