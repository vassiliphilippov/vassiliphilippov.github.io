caretaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛrˌteɪkər / (AmE), / ˈkeəˌteɪkə / (BrE)
a person who temporarily performs the duties of an official or office until a permanent replacement is appointed
temporary official; interim office-holder

Examples

She was appointed caretaker after the manager resigned unexpectedly.
The board named him caretaker until a new CEO could be found.
As caretaker, his primary role was to maintain stability during the transition.

Synset Examples

he acted as a caretaker until a new president could be elected

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interim - a temporary or provisional official
  • Synonym: acting - someone temporarily filling a position or office
  • Antonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely, not temporary

Etymology

From 'care' + 'taker', first used in the 19th century to mean a person who takes care temporarily.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɛrˌteɪkər / (AmE), / ˈkeəˌteɪkə / (BrE)
a person employed to look after a building, property, or sometimes a person, especially when the owner is absent or unable to do so.
person who looks after property or someone

Examples

The school caretaker opens the gates every morning and keeps the halls clean.
When her parents traveled abroad, they hired a caretaker to watch over their house.
As a caretaker, Tom was responsible for the elderly man's medication and daily needs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: custodian - a person responsible for cleaning and maintenance in a building
  • Synonym: guardian - someone legally responsible for another person, especially a child

Etymology

From care + taker; first recorded in the mid 19th century.
