Brachychiton

1. NOUN

/ ˌbrækiˈkaɪtən / (AmE), / ˌbrækɪˈkaɪtən / (BrE)
A genus of Australian trees, typically having swollen trunks, belonging to the family Malvaceae.
genus of Australian trees

Examples

The Brachychiton is well known for its bottle-shaped trunk and resilience to drought.
Several species of Brachychiton are widely planted as ornamental trees in Australia.
Aboriginal Australians have traditionally used Brachychiton seeds as a food source.

Etymology

From Greek brakhys (short) + chitōn (tunic), referring to features of the seed covering.
