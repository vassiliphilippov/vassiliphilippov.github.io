Italian

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈtæliən / (AmE), / ɪˈtalɪən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Italy
a person from Italy

Examples

There are many Italians living in New York City.
She met an Italian during her trip to Rome.
The Italian greeted us with a warm smile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Italian - the Romance language spoken in Italy

Etymology

From Latin 'Italicus' (of Italy) via Italian 'italiano'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈtæliən / (AmE), / ɪˈtal.i.ən / (BrE)
the Romance language spoken mainly in Italy, as well as parts of Switzerland and elsewhere, and used as the official language of Italy
the Romance language of Italy

Examples

He is studying Italian at university.
Many people in Switzerland speak both German and Italian.
The novel was translated from Italian into English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: French - the Romance language spoken mainly in France
  • Synonym: Spanish - the Romance language spoken mainly in Spain

Etymology

From Latin 'Italianus', from 'Italia' (Italy).

4. NOUN

/ ɪˈtæliən / (AmE), / ɪˈtal.i.ən / (BrE)
the Romance language spoken primarily in Italy, and also in parts of Switzerland and other countries
the Romance language of Italy

Examples

She is fluent in Italian after living in Rome for five years.
We took a course to improve our Italian before traveling to Florence.
Many English words are derived from Italian, especially in music and art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: French - the Romance language spoken in France and other countries
  • Synonym: Spanish - the Romance language spoken in Spain and in Latin America
  • Antonym: German - the West Germanic language spoken in Germany, Austria, and parts of Switzerland

Etymology

From Latin 'Italianus,' meaning 'of Italy.'
