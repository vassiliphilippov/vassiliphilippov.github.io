Origanum vulgare

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˌraɪɡənəm vʌlˈɡɛri / (AmE), / ˌɒrɪˈɡeɪnəm vʌlˈɡɑːriː / (BrE)
a Eurasian perennial herb with aromatic leaves, commonly known as oregano, used especially for seasoning food
aromatic herb, oregano species

Examples

The chef added fresh Origanum vulgare to the tomato sauce for extra flavor.
Gardeners often plant Origanum vulgare to attract bees and butterflies.
Dried leaves of Origanum vulgare are popular in Mediterranean cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oregano - the common name for Origanum vulgare, an aromatic herb used in cooking

Etymology

From Greek *origanon* (oregano) and Latin *vulgare* (common).
