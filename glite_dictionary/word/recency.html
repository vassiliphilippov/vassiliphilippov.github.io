recency

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːsənsi / (AmE), / ˈriːsənsi / (BrE)
the quality or state of having occurred or appeared not long ago; the period of being recent
the state of being recent

Examples

The recency of the data makes the findings more relevant to current trends.
Because of its recency, the event was fresh in everyone's mind.
Researchers studied the impact of recency on memory recall in their latest experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novelty - the quality of being new, original, or unusual
  • Antonym: antiquity - the ancient past; great age

Etymology

From Latin recens (recent) + English -cy (forming nouns denoting state or condition).

2. NOUN

/ ˈriːsənsi / (AmE), / ˈriːsənsi / (BrE)
the quality or state of having occurred or appeared not long ago
quality of being recent

Examples

The recency of his arrival explains why few people know him yet.
Because of its recency, the event was still fresh in everyone's mind.
In psychology, the recency effect refers to better recall of the most recent items on a list.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: antiquity - the ancient past, or the condition of being very old
  • Synonym: novelty - the quality of being new or original

Etymology

From recent + -cy, patterned after words like frequency.
