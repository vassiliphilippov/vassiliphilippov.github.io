Gymnopilus spectabilis

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪmˌnɒpɪləs spɛkˈteɪbɪlɪs / (AmE), / dʒɪmˌnɒpɪləs spɛkˈteɪbɪlɪs / (BrE)
A species of fungus with a brownish-orange fruiting body and a ring near the top of the stalk, known for its bitter taste and containing psychoactive compounds such as psilocybin and psilocin.
species of psychoactive fungus

Examples

Gymnopilus spectabilis grows in clusters on hardwood stumps in the autumn.
The caps of Gymnopilus spectabilis have a distinct brownish-orange color and a bitter flavor.
Researchers have studied Gymnopilus spectabilis for its psilocybin content and psychoactive properties.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: 'Gymnopilus' meaning 'naked cap' (from Greek 'gymnos' meaning 'naked' and 'pilos' meaning 'cap'); 'spectabilis' from Latin meaning 'remarkable'.
