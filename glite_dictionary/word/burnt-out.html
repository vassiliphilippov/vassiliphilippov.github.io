burnt-out

1. ADJ

/ bɝːnt aʊt / (AmE), / bɜːnt aʊt / (BrE)
Extremely tired or exhausted, especially as a result of prolonged stress or overwork.
exhausted from prolonged stress or overwork

Examples

After months of working overtime, he felt completely burnt-out and needed a vacation.
Many teachers become burnt-out by the end of the school year.
She realized she was burnt-out and took some time off to recover.

Synset Examples

she was burned-out before she was 30

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhausted - very tired; depleted of energy
  • Antonym: refreshed - full of energy after rest

2. ADJ

/ bɝːnt aʊt / (AmE), / bɜːnt aʊt / (BrE)
destroyed or badly damaged by fire, especially so that only the shell remains
destroyed or damaged by fire

Examples

The burnt-out building was condemned and fenced off for safety reasons.
A row of burnt-out cars lined the street after the riots.
Firefighters arrived too late to save the burnt-out warehouse.

Synset Examples

a charred bit of burnt wood
a burned-over site in the forest
barricaded the street with burnt-out cars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charred - burned and blackened by fire, often partially destroyed
  • Antonym: intact - not damaged or impaired in any way; complete

3. ADJ

/ bɝnt aʊt / (AmE), / bɜːnt aʊt / (BrE)
No longer working or functioning because of damage caused by heat or friction.
damaged and inoperative due to heat or friction

Examples

The burnt-out fuse needed to be replaced before the lamp would work again.
After years of use, the burnt-out motor failed to start.
We had to remove the burnt-out bulb and put in a new one.

Synset Examples

a burned-out picture tube

Related Terms
