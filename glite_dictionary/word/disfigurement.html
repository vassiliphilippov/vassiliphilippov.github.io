disfigurement

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈfɪɡjɚmənt / (AmE), / dɪsˈfɪɡjəmənt / (BrE)
The condition of having one's appearance spoiled or made misshapen, especially as a result of injury, disease, or congenital anomaly.
spoiling of appearance; misshapen condition

Examples

After the accident, he lived with the disfigurement left by his injuries.
Victims of acid attacks often struggle to cope with the social effects of their disfigurement.
The artist depicted the disfigurement caused by war in his paintings.

Synset Examples

there were distinguishing disfigurements on the suspect's back
suffering from facial disfiguration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deformity - the condition of being deformed, often congenital or due to injury
  • Degree Lower: injury - physical damage to the body

Etymology

From Old French 'desfigurer' (to disfigure), based on Latin 'disfigūrāre', from 'dis-' (apart, away) + 'figūra' (form, shape).

2. NOUN

/ dɪsˈfɪɡjɚmənt / (AmE), / dɪsˈfɪɡjəmənt / (BrE)
the act of spoiling or damaging the appearance or surface of something, making it less attractive or less recognizable
the act of spoiling appearance

Examples

The ancient statue suffered significant disfigurement after years of neglect and vandalism.
Campaigners protested against the disfigurement of the countryside by large-scale industrial projects.
After the accident, she struggled to cope with the emotional effects of her facial disfigurement.

Synset Examples

the defacement of an Italian mosaic during the Turkish invasion
he objected to the dam's massive disfigurement of the landscape

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defacement - the act of spoiling the surface or appearance of something

Etymology

From disfigure + -ment, with roots in Old French 'desfigurer' and Latin 'dis-' (apart, away) + 'figura' (shape).
