Betula lenta

1. NOUN

/ bəˈtʃuːlə ˈlɛntə / (AmE), / bɛˈtjuːlə ˈlɛntə / (BrE)
a species of birch tree native to eastern North America, with dark, spicy-scented bark and hard wood used for furniture and flavoring
species of birch tree from eastern North America

Examples

The wood of the Betula lenta is prized for its strength and durability in furniture making.
Oil distilled from the bark of Betula lenta is used for flavoring and medicinal purposes.
In the forest, Betula lenta is easily recognized by its shiny, dark bark and aromatic scent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet birch - another name for Betula lenta
  • Degree Higher: birch - any tree of the genus Betula

Etymology

From Latin; 'Betula' meaning birch, 'lenta' meaning pliant or flexible.
