to characterise

1. VERB

/ kəˈræk.tə.raɪz / (AmE), / kəˈræk.tə.raɪz / (BrE)
to be a typical feature or quality of something; to distinguish as a characteristic.
be a typical feature of

Examples

A love of freedom characterises the nation's culture.
The region is characterised by its rocky landscape and dry climate.
What really characterises her paintings is their vibrant use of color.

Synset Examples

What characterizes a Venetian painting?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to distinguish - to be a distinguishing feature of
  • Synonym: to typify - to be a typical example or representative of

Etymology

From French 'caractériser', based on 'caractère' (character), ultimately from Greek 'kharaktēr'.

2. VERB

/ ˈkɛrəktəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈkærɪktəraɪz / (BrE)
to describe or define the distinctive qualities, traits, or features of someone or something
describe the qualities of
british

Examples

The critics characterise the film as a modern classic.
She characterised her boss as both demanding and fair.
It would be unfair to characterise his comments as mere negativity.

Synset Examples

You can characterize his behavior as that of an egotist
This poem can be characterized as a lament for a dead lover

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to characterize - to describe or define the qualities of (American spelling)

Etymology

From French caractériser, from caractère (character), ultimately from Greek kharaktēr.
