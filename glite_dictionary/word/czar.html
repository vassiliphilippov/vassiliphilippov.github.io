czar

1. NOUN

/ zɑr / (AmE), / zɑː / (BrE)
a male monarch or emperor, especially of Russia before the revolution of 1917
Russian emperor before 1917

Examples

The last Russian czar, Nicholas II, was overthrown in 1917.
Historians often debate the impact the czar had on Russian society.
Many treasures were collected by the czar during his reign.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emperor - a sovereign ruler of great power and rank, especially one ruling an empire
  • Synonym: king - the male ruler of an independent state, especially one who inherits the position by right of birth
  • Antonym: queen - the female ruler of an independent state, especially one who inherits the position by right of birth

Etymology

From Russian ‘tsar’, from Latin ‘caesar’ (emperor).

2. NOUN

/ zɑr / (AmE), / zɑː / (BrE)
a person invested with great power or authority, especially one appointed to direct a particular area of policy or activity
a powerful official; policy chief

Examples

The president appointed a czar to coordinate the country's response to the crisis.
As the new energy czar, she implemented sweeping reforms to improve efficiency.
Critics argued that giving so much authority to a single czar could undermine existing agencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingpin - the most important person in an organization or activity
  • Synonym: dictator - a ruler with total power over a country, typically one who has obtained control by force
  • Antonym: underling - a person lower in status or rank, subordinate

Etymology

From Russian 'tsar', meaning emperor, via Latin 'caesar'.
