enterprise

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.tɚˌpraɪz / (AmE), / ˈen.tə.praɪz / (BrE)
A business or company, especially one that is new, difficult, or requires initiative.
a business or company

Examples

She started a new enterprise focused on renewable energy solutions.
The software enterprise grew rapidly over the last five years.
Success in such a competitive enterprise requires innovation and determination.

Synset Examples

a growing enterprise must have a bold leader

Related Terms

  • Synonym: company - an organization that sells goods or services in order to make money
  • Synonym: corporation - a large company or group of companies authorized to act as a single entity

Etymology

From Old French 'entreprise', from 'entreprendre' (to undertake), from Latin 'inter-' (between) + 'prehendere' (to take).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.tɚˌpraɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛn.tə.praɪz / (BrE)
the willingness or readiness to take bold risks or embark on new ventures; initiative and resourcefulness
readiness to take bold risks; initiative

Examples

Her enterprise led to the development of several innovative products.
It takes a lot of enterprise to start a new business in a competitive market.
Young people are encouraged to show enterprise and creativity in problem-solving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: initiative - the ability to assess and initiate things independently
  • Antonym: timidity - lack of courage or confidence

Etymology

From Old French *entreprise* (undertaking), from *entreprendre* (to undertake).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.tɚˌpraɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛn.tə.praɪz / (BrE)
a project or undertaking, especially one that is challenging, requires effort, or involves boldness
a challenging or bold undertaking

Examples

Launching a community garden was a new enterprise for the neighborhood volunteers.
Building the bridge proved to be a costly and time-consuming enterprise.
He doubted whether his latest business enterprise would succeed.

Synset Examples

he had doubts about the whole enterprise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: project - a planned set of tasks to achieve a particular aim
  • Synonym: venture - a risky or daring journey or undertaking

Etymology

From Old French 'entreprise', from 'entreprendre' (to undertake), from Latin 'inter' (between) + 'prehendere' (to take).
