unpopulated

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈpɑːpjəˌleɪtəd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈpɒpjʊleɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a place) having no people living there; not inhabited.
having no people living there

Examples

Much of the region remains unpopulated due to harsh weather conditions.
We drove through miles of unpopulated desert before reaching the nearest town.
The island was once unpopulated, but now a small community lives there.

Synset Examples

vast unpopulated plains

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inhabited - occupied by people; having residents

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'populated', from Latin 'populatus' (populated, peopled).
