taxi

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæksi / (AmE), / ˈtæksi / (BrE)
A car with a driver paid to carry passengers to a destination, usually for a fare.
a car for hire with a driver

Examples

We took a taxi from the airport to our hotel.
She waved her hand to hail a taxi on the busy street.
After midnight, it's hard to find a taxi in this part of the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cab - a car driven by a paid driver and hired by passengers

Etymology

Short for 'taxicab', derived from taximeter cab; 'taximeter' from French 'taximètre', based on 'taxa' (tax, charge) and Greek 'metron' (measure).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02934150-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈtæksi / (AmE), / ˈtaksi / (BrE)
to travel or ride in a taxicab
ride in a taxicab

Examples

We decided to taxi home after the concert instead of taking the bus.
She taxied to the airport early in the morning to catch her flight.
They were too tired to walk, so they taxied back to their hotel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cab - to travel in a taxi or taxicab

Etymology

From French *taximètre* (taximeter), referring to the device in the vehicle; noun shifted into verb use.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01952866-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈtæksi / (AmE), / ˈtæksi / (BrE)
(of an aircraft) to move slowly along the ground under its own power before takeoff or after landing.
(of an aircraft) move on the ground

Examples

The plane taxied slowly towards the terminal after landing.
Passengers remained seated while the aircraft taxied to the gate.
After takeoff clearance, the pilot taxied the jet onto the runway.

Synset Examples

The plane taxied down the runway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roll - (of an aircraft) to move forward on the ground
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
