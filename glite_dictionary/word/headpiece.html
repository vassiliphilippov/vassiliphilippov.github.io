headpiece

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌpis / (AmE), / ˈhɛd.piːs / (BrE)
the band of a bridle that fits around a horse's head, holding the rest of the bridle in place
band of a bridle fitting around a horse's head

Examples

The leather headpiece of the bridle was worn and needed replacing.
She adjusted the headpiece to fit the horse more comfortably before the ride.
Every bridle in the stable had a sturdy headpiece with brass fittings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bridle - the headgear used to control a horse, consisting of reins, a bit, and headstall

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌpis / (AmE), / ˈhɛd.piːs / (BrE)
a protective covering or helmet worn on the head to provide safety or defense
protective helmet for the head

Examples

The ancient warrior put on his headpiece before entering the battlefield.
All construction workers are required to wear a headpiece for safety.
Her costume included a shining headpiece that looked like a medieval helmet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helmet - a hard or padded protective hat

Etymology

From Middle English 'heedpiece', from 'head' + 'piece'.
