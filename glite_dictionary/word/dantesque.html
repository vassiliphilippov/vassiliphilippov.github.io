Dantesque

1. ADJ

/ dɑnˈtɛsk / (AmE), / dænˈtɛsk / (BrE)
relating to or reminiscent of Dante Alighieri or his writings, especially in reference to their vivid imagery or epic style
relating to Dante or his works

Examples

The novel's depiction of chaos and torment had a distinctly Dantesque quality.
The artist created a series of Dantesque illustrations inspired by 'The Divine Comedy'.
The film's Dantesque imagery left a powerful impression on the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dantean - relating to Dante Alighieri, especially his literary style

Etymology

From Dante Alighieri (1265–1321), Italian poet, with the suffix -esque (in the style of).
