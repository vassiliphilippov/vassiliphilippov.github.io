liftoff

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪftˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈlɪftɒf / (BrE)
the moment when a rocket leaves its launching pad and begins its ascent
rocket's initial ascent from launch pad

Examples

The countdown began and, moments later, the liftoff of the rocket was broadcast live.
Spectators cheered as the spacecraft achieved liftoff on a clear morning.
High winds delayed the liftoff, forcing mission control to wait for better conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: launch - the act of sending a spacecraft or missile into the air or into space

Etymology

From 'lift' + 'off', coined in context of rocket launches in the mid-20th century.
