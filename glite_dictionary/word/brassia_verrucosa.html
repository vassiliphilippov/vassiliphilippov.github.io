Brassia verrucosa

1. NOUN

/ ˈbræsiə vəˈruːkosə / (AmE), / ˈbrasɪə vɛˈruːkəʊsə / (BrE)
A species of Central American orchid notable for its spiderlike flowers with prominent green warts.
Central American spiderlike orchid species

Examples

The Brassia verrucosa thrives in humid Central American forests, displaying its unique spiderlike blooms each spring.
Orchid collectors often prize Brassia verrucosa for its unusual flowers with green warts.
Botanists discovered several wild Brassia verrucosa plants growing in the shaded understory of the rainforest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brassia - A genus of orchids known as spider orchids

Etymology

From the genus name Brassia, honoring British botanist William Brass, and Latin verrucosa meaning 'warty'.
