operant

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑː.pɚ.ənt / (AmE), / ˈɒp.ər.ənt / (BrE)
Having influence or producing an effect, especially in causing a particular result.
having influence; producing an effect

Examples

The new policy was operant in reducing absenteeism across the company.
His operant personality shaped the team's dynamics considerably.
Certain environmental factors are operant during child development.

Synset Examples

many emotional determinants at work
an operant conscience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effective - successful in producing a desired or intended result
  • Antonym: ineffective - not producing any significant or desired effect

Etymology

From Latin 'operari' (to work).
