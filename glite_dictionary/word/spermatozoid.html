spermatozoid

1. NOUN

/ spɚˌmætəˈzoʊɪd / (AmE), / spɜːˌmætəˈzəʊɪd / (BrE)
a motile male reproductive cell (gamete) in certain plants, such as algae, ferns, or gymnosperms, responsible for fertilization
motile male reproductive cell in some plants
medical

Examples

Under the microscope, the spermatozoid moves swiftly towards the ovule.
Ferns reproduce by releasing spermatozoids that swim through water to fertilize eggs.
The biologist studied the spermatozoid in gymnosperms to better understand plant reproduction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spermatozoon - a motile male gamete, especially in animals

Etymology

From French spermatozoïde, from Ancient Greek 'sperma' (seed) + 'zōidēs' (having the form of).
