summit

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌm.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌm.ɪt / (BrE)
an official meeting or conference attended by the leaders of governments, especially to discuss important issues
meeting of heads of governments

Examples

World leaders gathered at the G7 summit to discuss the global economy.
The summit ended with an agreement on climate action between the countries.
Next month’s peace summit will bring together presidents from across the continent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conference - a formal meeting for discussion
  • Synonym: summitry - the practice of holding summits

Etymology

From French 'sommet' (summit, top, apex), from Latin 'summum' (highest).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌmɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌmɪt / (BrE)
the highest point or top of something, especially a mountain or hill
highest point; top of a mountain or hill

Examples

It took the hikers six hours to reach the summit of Mount Snowdon.
From the summit, they could see miles of rolling landscape in every direction.
Clouds drifted around the summit as the sun began to set.

Synset Examples

the view from the peak was magnificent

the region is a few molecules wide at the summit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peak - the pointed top of a mountain; the highest point of something
  • Antonym: base - the lowest part or foundation of something; opposite of summit

Etymology

From Old French 'somete' (summit), diminutive of 'som' (top), from Latin 'summum' (the highest).

3. VERB

/ ˈsʌmɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌmɪt / (BrE)
to reach the highest point or summit of a mountain, especially as an achievement in mountaineering.
reach the highest point of a mountain

Examples

After several days of climbing, they finally summited Mount Kilimanjaro at sunrise.
Our goal is to summit before noon to avoid the afternoon storms.
She trained for months so she could summit the tallest mountain in the region.

Synset Examples

They breasted the mountain
Many mountaineers go up Mt. Everest but not all summit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peak - reach the top of a mountain

Etymology

From the noun 'summit', originally from Old French somete (summit, top), diminutive of som (summit), from Latin summum (highest point). Verb use derives from the noun.

5. VERB

/ ˈsʌm.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌm.ɪt / (BrE)
(of a climber) to reach the highest point of a mountain, especially Mount Everest or other notable peaks.
reach the highest point of a mountain

Examples

After weeks of acclimatization, the team finally summited Mount Everest.
Only a handful of climbers manage to summit the world's highest peaks each year.
She trained for years before attempting to summit Mount Everest.

Synset Examples

He trained for months to summit Mount Everest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: climb - to go up or ascend, especially a mountain or hill
  • Antonym: to descend - to move or fall downward, opposite of ascending a mountain

Etymology

From noun sense 'summit' (highest point), used as a verb in mountaineering since the late 20th century.
