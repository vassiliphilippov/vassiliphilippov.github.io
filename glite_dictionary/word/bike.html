bike

1. NOUN

/ baɪk / (AmE), / baɪk / (BrE)
a vehicle with two wheels that you ride by pushing its pedals with your feet
two-wheeled pedal vehicle

Examples

She rides her bike to work every morning, even in the rain.
I bought a new bike for my son's birthday last week.
It's important to wear a helmet when you ride your bike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bicycle - a vehicle with two wheels powered by pedals

Etymology

Shortened form of 'bicycle', from French 'bicyclette' and ultimately from Latin 'bi-' (two) and Greek 'kyklos' (circle, wheel).

Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ baɪk / (AmE), / baɪk / (BrE)
A motorcycle; a motor vehicle with two wheels and a strong frame.
motorcycle with two wheels

Examples

He rode his bike across the country last summer.
She saved up for months to buy her first bike.
The loud roar of his bike echoed through the neighborhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motorcycle - A two-wheeled motor vehicle
  • Degree Lower: bicycle - A vehicle with two wheels powered by pedalling

Etymology

Originally from short form of 'bicycle'; later used for motorcycle.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03796045-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ baɪk / (AmE), / baɪk / (BrE)
to ride a bicycle
ride a bicycle

Examples

Every morning, she likes to bike to work instead of taking the bus.
They biked across the country to raise money for charity.
He is biking around the neighborhood this afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bicycle - to ride a bicycle (verb form: to travel by bicycle)

Etymology

Shortened from 'bicycle'.
