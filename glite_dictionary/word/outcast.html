outcast

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌkɑːst / (BrE)
a person who has been rejected by society or by their family and friends
person rejected by society

Examples

After the scandal, he lived as an outcast in his own community.
She felt like an outcast after moving to a new city where she knew no one.
Historically, lepers were treated as outcasts and forced to live outside the city walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pariah - a person who is despised or avoided by others

Etymology

From Middle English, past participle of 'outcasten,' meaning to cast out.

2. ADJ

/ ˈaʊtˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌkɑːst / (BrE)
A person who has been rejected or excluded from a society or group.
person excluded from society

Examples

After his controversial opinion, he felt like an outcast among his former friends.
The novel tells the story of an outcast struggling to find acceptance in a new city.
She was treated as an outcast because she refused to follow the community's strict rules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pariah - a person who is despised or avoided by others

Etymology

From Middle English *outcasten* (to cast out), from Old Norse *út kast* (to cast out).

Synset POS: s
