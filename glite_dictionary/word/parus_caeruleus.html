Parus caeruleus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛɹəs siˈruːliəs / (AmE), / ˌpɑːrəs siːˈruːliəs / (BrE)
a small European titmouse with bright blue wings, tail, and crown, commonly known as the blue tit
European songbird with blue wings and crown

Examples

The Parus caeruleus is easily recognized by its striking blue plumage.
Researchers studied the nesting habits of Parus caeruleus in urban environments.
A pair of Parus caeruleus visited the garden feeder every morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue tit - common name for Parus caeruleus, a small blue-crowned songbird

Etymology

From Latin: 'parus' (titmouse) + 'caeruleus' (blue).
