fineable

1. ADJ

/ ˈfaɪnəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪnəbl̩ / (BrE)
Liable to be punished by a fine; subject to a monetary penalty.
liable to a fine
legal

Examples

Parking in that spot is a fineable offense in this city.
The company was found guilty of several fineable violations of safety regulations.
Littering in the park is fineable under the new municipal code.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exempt - not subject to a penalty or fine

Etymology

Formed from 'fine' + '-able', meaning able to be fined.
