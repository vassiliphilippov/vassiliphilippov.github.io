Hypoxis hirsuta

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɑksɪs hɪrˈsuːtə / (AmE), / hɪˈpɒksɪs hɪəˈsuːtə / (BrE)
A species of perennial star grass native to North America, known for its yellow flowers and hairy leaves and stems.
species of North American star grass

Examples

Hypoxis hirsuta is commonly found in prairies and open woodlands across the Midwest.
The yellow flowers of Hypoxis hirsuta bloom in early spring.
Gardeners value Hypoxis hirsuta for its ability to thrive in poor soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: star grass - any plant of the genus Hypoxis

Etymology

From Greek hypoxis (a plant name), and Latin hirsuta (hairy).
