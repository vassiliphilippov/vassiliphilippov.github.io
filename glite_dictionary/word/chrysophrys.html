Chrysophrys

1. NOUN

/ kraɪˈsɑːfrɪs / (AmE), / krɪˈsɒfrɪs / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the family Sparidae, including several species such as the Australian snapper.

The Chrysophrys genus includes some of the most sought-after fish by anglers in Australia.

2. NOUN

/ kraɪˈsɑːfrɪs / (AmE), / krɪˈsɒfrɪs / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the family Sparidae, commonly known as the Australian snapper, found primarily in Australian waters.
a genus of fish; Australian snapper

Examples

The Chrysophrys is a popular catch among recreational fishers in Australia.
Marine biologists studied the breeding habits of Chrysophrys along the coast.
The silver-pink coloration is characteristic of the Chrysophrys species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snapper - a common name for fish in the family Sparidae, including the Australian snapper

Etymology

From Greek *chrysos* (gold) and *ophrys* (eyebrow), referring to the golden markings above the eyes in some species.
