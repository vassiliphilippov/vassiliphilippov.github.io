conventual

1. ADJ

/ kənˈvɛntʃuəl / (AmE), / kənˈvɛntjʊəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a convent or the communal religious life led there, sequestered from the outside world under religious vows.
relating to a convent or monastic life

Examples

The nuns lived a conventual life, separated from the distractions of the secular world.
He admired the conventual discipline that structured their daily routines.
The architecture reflects the conventual ideals of peace and seclusion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monastic - relating to monks or monasteries and their way of life

Etymology

From Medieval Latin conventualis (pertaining to a convent), from Latin conventus (assembly, convent).
