venae esophageae

1. NOUN

/ ˈviːni ɪˌsoʊfəˈdʒiː / (AmE), / ˈviːni iːˌsɒfəˈdʒiː / (BrE)
the small veins originating from the esophagus that drain into the brachiocephalic vein or the azygos veins
small veins of the esophagus
medical

Examples

During surgery, careful dissection of the venae esophageae is necessary to prevent bleeding.
Varices can occur in the venae esophageae due to portal hypertension.
The venae esophageae drain into either the azygos vein or the brachiocephalic vein.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: esophageal vein - a vein that carries blood from the esophagus

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) + 'oesophagea' (of the esophagus), meaning veins of the esophagus.
