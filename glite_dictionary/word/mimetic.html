mimetic

1. ADJ

/ mɪˈmɛtɪk / (AmE), / mɪˈmɛtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to, characterized by, or using imitation or representation, especially in art or nature.
relating to imitation or representation

Examples

The play was praised for its mimetic qualities, vividly capturing real human emotions.
Certain butterflies display mimetic patterns to resemble toxic species and avoid predators.
The artist's work is highly mimetic, closely imitating scenes from everyday life.

Synset Examples

a mimetic dance
the mimetic presentation of images

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imitative - tending to imitate or copy

Etymology

From Greek mimētikos (imitative), from mimeisthai (to imitate).

2. ADJ

/ mɪˈmɛtɪk, maɪˈmɛtɪk / (AmE), / mɪˈmɛtɪk, maɪˈmɛtɪk / (BrE)
exhibiting imitation or mimicry, especially of natural forms or behaviors; imitative.
imitative; showing mimicry

Examples

The butterfly's mimetic coloring helps it avoid predators by resembling a poisonous species.
During infancy, children display a strong mimetic tendency towards the actions of adults.
Some poets admire the mimetic qualities of literature that closely reflect real life.

Synset Examples

mimetic coloring of a butterfly
the mimetic tendency of infancy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imitative - copying or trying to copy something or someone
  • Antonym: original - existing from the beginning; not a copy or imitation

Etymology

From Greek mimētikos (able to imitate), from mimeisthai (to imitate).
