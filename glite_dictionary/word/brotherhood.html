brotherhood

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrʌðɚˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈbrʌðəhʊd / (BrE)
The relationship between a male sibling (brother) and his other siblings.
the state of being a brother

Examples

The brotherhood he felt with his siblings was unbreakable.
Their brotherhood grew stronger as they supported each other through life's challenges.
He cherished the sense of brotherhood that came from having two brothers and a sister.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sisterhood - the relationship between a female sibling (sister) and her other siblings

Etymology

From Old English 'brōþorrǣden' (brotherhood, brotherly relationship), later influenced by 'brother' + '-hood'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrʌðərˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈbrʌðəhʊd / (BrE)
an organization of employees, especially one formed to represent and negotiate for workers' rights and interests
employees' labor organization

Examples

After John joined the brotherhood, he gained access to better benefits at work.
The brotherhood met weekly to discuss contract negotiations with management.
Many workers relied on the brotherhood to help resolve disputes with their employer.

Synset Examples

you have to join the union in order to get a job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: union - an organized association of workers formed to protect and further their rights

Etymology

From Middle English 'brotherhed', from Old English 'brōþor' (brother) + '-hed' (state, condition).

3. NOUN

/ ˈbrʌðərˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈbrʌðəhʊd / (BrE)
a group of people sharing a common profession, interest, or purpose, often seen as having close relationships or a sense of camaraderie
group sharing a profession or interest

Examples

The brotherhood of firefighters held a ceremony for their retiring chief.
Many doctors feel a strong sense of brotherhood with their colleagues.
She was welcomed into the brotherhood of engineers after passing her final exams.

Synset Examples

the medical fraternity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraternity - a group of people sharing a common profession or interests

Etymology

From Old English 'broþorhod' (state or condition of being brothers).

solidarity; feeling of brotherly support

Examples
