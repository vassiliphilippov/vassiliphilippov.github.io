to wham

1. VERB

/ wæm / (AmE), / wæm / (BrE)
to strike or hit with great force
strike or hit with force

Examples

She whammed the ball over the fence with a powerful swing.
The door whammed shut in the strong wind, startling everyone in the room.
He accidentally whammed his knee against the desk while standing up.

Synset Examples

The teacher whacked the boy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smash - to break into pieces violently or noisily by striking or shattering
  • Synonym: whack - to strike forcefully with a sharp blow
  • Degree Lower: tap - to strike lightly
