Scaphiopus hammondii

1. PROPN

/ skæˈfaɪ.oʊ.pəs hæˈmɒn.di.aɪ / (AmE), / skæˈfiː.ə.pəs hæˈmɒn.dɪ.aɪ / (BrE)
A species of spadefoot toad found in California, known for its burrowing behavior and distinctive call.
species of spadefoot toad in California

Examples

The Scaphiopus hammondii is well adapted to the dry climate of California's Central Valley.
Researchers studied the Scaphiopus hammondii to better understand its unique breeding habits.
Local conservation efforts have helped increase the population of Scaphiopus hammondii in recent years.

Etymology

From modern Latin, Scaphiopus (genus name from Greek 'skaphe' meaning 'spade' and 'pous' meaning 'foot'); hammondii honors the American explorer William A. Hammond.
