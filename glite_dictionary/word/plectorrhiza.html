Plectorrhiza

1. PROPN

/ plɛkˌtɔːrˈraɪzə / (AmE), / plɛkˌtɒrˈraɪzə / (BrE)
A small genus of Australian orchids including species such as Plectorrhiza brevilabris, Plectorrhiza erecta, and Plectorrhiza tridentata.
genus of Australian orchids

Examples

The rare Plectorrhiza orchid can be found in certain mountainous regions of eastern Australia.
Botanists are studying Plectorrhiza to better understand the diversity of Australian native orchids.
One of the best-known species within Plectorrhiza is Plectorrhiza brevilabris.

Etymology

From Greek 'plektos' (twisted, plaited) and 'rhiza' (root), referring to the plant's root morphology.
