Playlobium obtusangulum

1. NOUN

/ plaɪˈloʊbiəm ˌɑb.tuˈsæŋɡjələm / (AmE), / plaɪˈləʊbɪəm ˌɒb.tuːˈsæŋɡjʊləm / (BrE)
A low, spreading evergreen shrub native to southern Australia, with triangular or heart-shaped leaves, orange-yellow flowers, and flat winged pods.
Australian evergreen shrub species

Examples

Playlobium obtusangulum is commonly found in the coastal regions of southern Australia.
The leaves of Playlobium obtusangulum are distinctly triangular and provide a unique visual appeal to gardens.
Botanists studied Playlobium obtusangulum to understand its adaptation to dry environments.

Etymology

From the genus 'Playlobium' and the Latin 'obtusangulum' meaning 'blunt-angled', referring to the shape of its leaves.
