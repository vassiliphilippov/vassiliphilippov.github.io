Felis wiedi

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːlɪs ˈwiːdi / (AmE), / ˈfiːlɪs ˈwiːdi / (BrE)
A small, spotted wildcat native to Central and South America, ranging from southern Texas to Brazil; also known as the margay.
species of small spotted wildcat (margay)

Examples

The elusive Felis wiedi is rarely seen in the wild due to its nocturnal habits.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the habitat of Felis wiedi in Brazil.
Researchers tracked a Felis wiedi through the dense rainforest to study its behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: margay - common name for Felis wiedi, a small spotted wildcat

Etymology

From Latin: 'Felis' meaning 'cat', and 'wiedi' honoring German explorer Prince Maximilian of Wied-Neuwied.
