Euderma maculata

1. NOUN

/ juːˈdɜːrmə məˈkjuːlətə / (AmE), / juːˈdɜːmə mækˈjuːlɑːtə / (BrE)
A large bat of the southwestern United States, known for its distinctive spots and very large ears; commonly called the spotted bat.
a species of spotted, large-eared bat

Examples

The Euderma maculata can be identified by the white spots on its dark fur.
Researchers tracked the movements of Euderma maculata in the Grand Canyon region.
Habitat loss poses a significant threat to Euderma maculata populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotted bat - common English name for Euderma maculata

Etymology

From Greek 'eu-' (good, well), 'derma' (skin), and Latin 'maculata' (spotted).
