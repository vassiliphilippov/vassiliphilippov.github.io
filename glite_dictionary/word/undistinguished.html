undistinguished

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.dɪˈstɪŋ.wɪʃt / (AmE), / ˌʌn.dɪˈstɪŋ.wɪʃt / (BrE)
not having any special quality or outstanding features; lacking distinction or individuality.
lacking special qualities or distinction

Examples

The film received mixed reviews and was considered rather undistinguished by critics.
He had an undistinguished career in the company before he decided to retire early.
The design of the building is practical but undistinguished, blending into its surroundings.

Synset Examples

run-of-the-mill boxing
your run-of-the-mine college graduate
as unexceptional an incident as can be found in a lawyer's career

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unexceptional - not out of the ordinary; unremarkable
  • Synonym: ordinary - with no special or distinctive features; normal
  • Antonym: distinguished - very successful, authoritative, and commanding great respect

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'distinguished', from Latin 'distinguere' (to separate, distinguish).
