shallot

1. NOUN

/ ʃəˈlɑt / (AmE), / ʃəˈlɒt / (BrE)
a small, mild-flavored variety of onion with clustered bulbs, used in cooking to add flavor
a mild-flavored onion used in cooking

Examples

She added chopped shallot to the vinaigrette for extra flavor.
The recipe calls for two finely sliced shallots and a handful of parsley.
A shallot imparts a subtle, sweet taste to the sauce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onion - edible bulb used for seasoning

Etymology

From Old French 'escalogne', from Latin 'Ascalonia caepa' (onion of Ascalon, an ancient city).

2. NOUN

/ ʃəˈlɑt / (AmE), / ʃəˈlɒt / (BrE)
A small, mild-flavored type of onion with an aggregate bulb composed of several cloves, used in cooking.
a mild, clove-like onion bulb

Examples

Finely chop one shallot and add it to the vinaigrette for a subtle, sweet flavor.
The recipe calls for two shallots, peeled and thinly sliced.
She prefers using shallots instead of onions in her French recipes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onion - a round vegetable with white, yellow, or red skin and a sharp taste and smell

Etymology

From Old French 'eschalote', diminutive of 'eschaloigne' (shallot), from Latin 'Ascalonia (caepa)' (onion of Ascalon).

3. NOUN

/ ʃəˈlɑt / (AmE), / ʃəˈlɒt / (BrE)
a small bulb in the onion family with a mild flavor, used in cooking to add seasoning or aroma; the plant that produces such bulbs
mild-flavored onion-like bulb used in cooking

Examples

The recipe called for a finely chopped shallot to enhance the flavor of the sauce.
She sautéed the shallots in butter until they were golden and fragrant.
A single shallot can make a big difference in a salad dressing's taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onion - large bulbous plant used as a vegetable and for seasoning
  • Synonym: garlic - pungent bulb used as seasoning and medicine

Etymology

From French 'échalote', derived from Latin 'ascalonia', meaning 'of Ascalon' (an ancient city; related to early European trade routes).
