Circus aeruginosus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrkəs ɛˌruːdʒɪˈnoʊsəs / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.kəs ɪˌruː.dʒɪˈnəʊ.səs / (BrE)
A large Old World harrier (western marsh harrier) that inhabits marshy regions of Europe, Asia, and northern Africa.
Old World marsh harrier species

Examples

The Circus aeruginosus is often seen gliding low over the reeds in search of prey.
Birdwatchers spotted a pair of Circus aeruginosus nesting by the lakeside.
Habitat loss poses a significant threat to the Circus aeruginosus population in several countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: western marsh harrier - common name for Circus aeruginosus, large harrier inhabiting marshes

Etymology

From Latin: Circus (a genus of harriers), aeruginosus (coppery or rust-colored).
