Louis IX

1. PROPN

/ luˈi naɪnθ / (AmE), / ˈluːi ðə ˈnaɪnθ / (BrE)
(1214–1270) King of France renowned for his piety and justice, who led two Crusades and was later canonized as Saint Louis.
King of France (1214–1270) and Crusader

Examples

Many historians consider Louis IX an exemplary medieval ruler.
The city of St. Louis in the United States was named after Louis IX.
As king, Louis IX was known for his commitment to justice and his religious devotion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saint Louis - another name for Louis IX, after his canonization

Etymology

Louis from French, from Old Frankish *Hludwig (famous warrior), the ninth king named Louis in France.
