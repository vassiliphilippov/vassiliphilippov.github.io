Melanoplus

1. NOUN

/ mɛˈlænəˌploʊs / (AmE), / mɛˈlanəˌpləʊs / (BrE)
A genus of grasshoppers found mainly in North America, including several species that are migratory locusts.
genus of American grasshoppers

Examples

The damage to crops was caused by a swarm of Melanoplus grasshoppers.
Researchers studied the behavior of Melanoplus species in their natural habitat.
Many farmers are concerned when outbreaks of Melanoplus occur in the Midwest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grasshopper - a jumping insect with long hind legs and typically green or brown coloration

Etymology

From Greek 'melas' (black) and 'hoplon' (shield), referring to body coloration and shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛləˈnoʊpləs / (AmE), / ˌmɛləˈnəʊpləs / (BrE)
a genus of grasshoppers found in the Americas, including New World migratory locusts and common American grasshoppers.
genus of American grasshoppers

Examples

Many crop fields in North America are threatened by outbreaks of Melanoplus species each year.
Scientists have identified several subspecies within the Melanoplus genus due to their diverse habitats.
A sudden increase in the population of Melanoplus can devastate local agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grasshopper - a plant-eating insect with long hind legs for jumping
  • Synonym: locust - a migratory grasshopper, often causing plagues

Etymology

From Greek melas (black) + hoplon (weapon, shield), referring to the characteristic coloration and structure.
