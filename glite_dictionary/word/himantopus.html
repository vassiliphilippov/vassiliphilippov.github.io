Himantopus

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈmæntəpəs / (AmE), / hɪˈmantəpəs / (BrE)
A genus of long-legged wading birds known as stilts, characterized by long, slender legs and straight bills.
genus of wading birds (stilts)

Examples

The black-winged stilt belongs to the genus Himantopus.
Several species in Himantopus are found in wetlands around the world.
Ornithologists debated whether the American stilt should be placed in Himantopus or in a separate genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Recurvirostra - genus of avocets, related wading birds with upcurved bills

Etymology

From Greek himantopous, meaning 'strap-footed.'

2. NOUN

/ hɪˈmæntəpəs / (AmE), / hɪˈmantəpəs / (BrE)
A genus of wading birds known as stilts, characterized by long, slender legs and straight bills.
genus of long-legged wading birds (stilts)

Examples

The Himantopus genus includes several species of stilts found in wetlands worldwide.
Ornithologists studied the migratory patterns of Himantopus in South America.
A Himantopus was observed nesting along the salt marsh this spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avocet - a wading bird with an upturned bill, related to stilts

Etymology

From Greek 'himantos' (strap, thong) + 'pous' (foot), referring to the thin legs of these birds.
