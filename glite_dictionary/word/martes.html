Martes

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːrtiːz / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of carnivorous mammals in the weasel family, including the martens, found in northern forests of Eurasia and North America.
genus of carnivorous mammals (martens)

Examples

The American pine marten is classified in the genus Martes.
Species within Martes are known for their sleek bodies and bushy tails.
Biologists are studying the population genetics of Martes in eastern Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marten - any animal of the genus Martes, a small carnivorous mammal

Etymology

From Latin *martes* (marten). Used as the scientific genus name.
