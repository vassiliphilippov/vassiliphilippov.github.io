Abramis

1. NOUN

/ əˈbreɪmɪs / (AmE), / əˈbraːmɪs / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater fishes found in Europe, including the common bream.
a genus of European freshwater fishes

Examples

The bream, a member of the genus Abramis, is widespread across European rivers and lakes.
She studied the anatomy of several Abramis species for her research project.
Many anglers in Europe are familiar with Abramis because of its popularity as a sport fish.

Etymology

From Greek abramis, a type of fish.

2. NOUN

/ əˈbreɪmɪs / (AmE), / əˈbreɪmɪs / (BrE)
a genus of freshwater fishes native to Europe, including several species of bream.
genus of European freshwater fishes

Examples

The genus Abramis is well known among European anglers for its various bream species.
Research on Abramis helps scientists understand the evolution of European freshwater fish.
The silver bream, belonging to the genus Abramis, inhabits slow-moving rivers and lakes.

Etymology

From Latin *Abramis*, a genus name used in zoological classification, originally from Greek *abramis*, a kind of fish.
