interlude

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.tɚˌlud / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tə.ljuːd / (BrE)
A short performance, such as a piece of music or dance, given between the sections of a longer play, concert, or event.
short performance between acts or scenes

Examples

The orchestra played a lively interlude between the first and second acts.
During the interlude, dancers performed a short routine to entertain the audience.
A comic interlude provided some relief before the dramatic final scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entr'acte - a piece performed between acts of a play or opera

Etymology

From Latin 'interludium', from 'inter-' (between) + 'ludus' (play, game).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪntɚˌlud / (AmE), / ˈɪntəˌluːd / (BrE)
an intervening period of time or episode, especially one that interrupts or contrasts with what comes before and after
an intervening period or episode

Examples

The rainstorm was merely an unexpected interlude during an otherwise sunny afternoon.
The play was divided by a short interlude featuring music and dance.
There was a peaceful interlude in their argument before tempers flared again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interval - a period of time between events

Etymology

From Latin *interludium*, from *inter* (between) + *ludus* (play, game).
