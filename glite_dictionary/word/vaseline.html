Vaseline

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæsəliːn / (AmE), / ˈvæsəliːn / (BrE)
A trademarked brand of soft, clear or pale yellow substance made from petroleum, used as a skin ointment, lubricant, and in cosmetics.
trademarked petroleum jelly ointment

Examples

She applied some Vaseline to her chapped lips to relieve the dryness.
A thin layer of Vaseline can help protect cuts and scrapes from infection.
He always keeps a jar of Vaseline in his medicine cabinet for minor skin irritations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petroleum jelly - a semi-solid mixture of hydrocarbons used as an ointment and lubricant

Etymology

Coined as a trademark in the 19th century by Robert Chesebrough, combining German 'Wasser' (water) and Greek 'elaion' (oil).
