schizothymia

1. NOUN

/ skɪz.oʊˈθaɪ.mi.ə / (AmE), / skɪts.əʊˈθɪm.i.ə / (BrE)
A personality type characterized by traits resembling those of schizophrenia but without crossing into pathology or disorder.
personality type similar to mild schizophrenia
medical

Examples

Her reserved demeanor and tendency to withdraw socially suggest a schizothymia rather than a psychiatric disorder.
The psychologist noted a mild schizothymia in his evaluation but found no evidence of actual schizophrenia.
Some researchers argue that schizothymia may confer certain advantages, such as increased creativity.

Etymology

From Greek 'schizo-' (to split) and 'thymia' (mind, emotion).
