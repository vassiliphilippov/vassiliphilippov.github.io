nationalistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌnæʃəˈnɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌnaʃəˈnɪstɪk / (BrE)
having or showing strong devotion to the interests or culture of one's nation, often with preference for it above others
strongly devoted to one's nation

Examples

The politician's nationalistic rhetoric appealed to voters concerned about foreign influence.
Some people criticized the policy as too nationalistic and exclusionary toward outsiders.
History has shown that nationalistic movements can both unite and divide nations.

Synset Examples

nationalist aspirations
minor nationalistic differences

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patriotic - having or expressing devotion to and vigorous support for one's country

Etymology

From 'nationalist' + '-ic', with 'nationalist' from 'nation' (Latin 'natio', meaning 'birth, nation').

2. ADJ

/ ˌnæʃə.nəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌnaʃ.ə.nəˈlɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
having strong and sometimes fanatical patriotic feelings, showing excessive support for one's own nation, often to the exclusion or detriment of other countries or cultures.
excessively patriotic

Examples

The new government's nationalistic policies have caused tension with neighboring countries.
He gave a nationalistic speech, urging citizens to support only local businesses.
Critics warned that the rising nationalistic sentiment might lead to isolationism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patriotic - having or expressing devotion to and vigorous support for one's country
  • Antonym: cosmopolitan - having an outlook that is open to different cultures or nationalities

Etymology

From 'nationalist' (one who supports the interests of their nation) + '-ic' forming adjectives.
