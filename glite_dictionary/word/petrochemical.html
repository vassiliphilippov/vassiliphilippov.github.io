petrochemical

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛtroʊˈkɛmɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌpɛtrəʊˈkɛmɪkəl / (BrE)
a chemical substance obtained from petroleum or natural gas, used as a raw material in the chemical industry
chemical derived from petroleum or natural gas

Examples

The production of plastics relies heavily on petrochemical feedstocks from refineries.
Many everyday products, such as detergents and fertilizers, are made using petrochemical derivatives.
Environmental concerns have led to research into alternatives to petrochemical materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chemical - a substance obtained by or used in a chemical process

Etymology

From petro- (relating to petroleum) + chemical.
