lancet

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæn.sət / (AmE), / ˈlæn.sɪt / (BrE)
an acutely pointed arch, especially one of the narrow, tall type commonly used in Gothic architecture
pointed arch in Gothic architecture

Examples

The cathedral's windows are framed by elegant lancet arches that reach towards the sky.
Each lancet in the nave is adorned with intricate stained glass panels.
The architect specified a series of tall lancets to enhance the building's vertical emphasis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ogee - an arch with a double curve, forming an S-shape, used in Gothic architecture

Etymology

From Old French lancette, diminutive of lance (lance), referring to its spear-like shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlæn.sət / (AmE), / ˈlɑːn.sɪt / (BrE)
a small, sharp surgical knife with a pointed double-edged blade, used for making punctures or small incisions
small surgical knife
medical

Examples

The doctor used a lancet to make a small incision in the patient's skin.
Before checking her blood sugar, she prepared a new lancet for the test.
Sterilizing the lancet is important to prevent infection during minor procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scalpel - a small straight knife used in surgery

Etymology

From Old French *lancette*, diminutive of *lance* (lance), from Latin *lancea* (light spear).
