lip

1. NOUN

/ lɪp / (AmE), / lɪp / (BrE)
either the outer or inner edge of the opening of a gastropod's shell

Examples

Careful measurement of the lip helps identify the species of this gastropod.
The snail retracted into its shell, barely visible behind the thickened lip.

3. NOUN

/ lɪp / (AmE), / lɪp / (BrE)
either of the two soft, fleshy edges that form the opening of the mouth in humans and many animals
fleshy edge of the mouth

Examples

She pressed her lip together to stop herself from laughing.
He cut his lip while eating a sharp piece of bread.
The child pouted, sticking out his bottom lip in disappointment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mouth - the opening through which an animal takes in food

Etymology

Old English *lippa*, related to Dutch *lip* and German *Lippe*.

5. NOUN

/ lɪp / (AmE), / lɪp / (BrE)
(Botany) Either of the two parts of a bilabiate corolla or calyx, often resembling lips in appearance.
botanical part of corolla or calyx

Examples

The orchid's lower lip is vividly colored to attract pollinators.
Many mint plants have flowers with an upper and lower lip in their corolla.
In some species, the lip forms a landing platform for insects.
