bearing

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɛərɪŋ / (BrE)
a design or image depicted on a shield in heraldry
heraldic design on a shield

Examples

The knight's shield displayed a distinctive bearing of a golden lion.
Each noble family had its own unique bearing in medieval times.
The historian studied the bearing on the coat of arms for clues to the family's lineage.

Etymology

From Old French *baran* (bearing), from *bearer* ‘to carry’; in heraldry, it refers to what is borne on a shield.

Examples
