marabou

1. NOUN

/ ˈmærəˌbu / (AmE), / ˈmærəbuː / (BrE)
the soft, downy feathers of marabou storks, often used for trimming garments and accessories
soft feathers used for trimming garments

Examples

The dress was decorated with a marabou trim along the neckline.
He bought a vintage hat adorned with pink marabou feathers.
A soft marabou stole added a touch of glamour to her evening attire.

Etymology

From French 'marabout', from Arabic 'murābit' (holy man), originally referring to the stork species.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmærəˌbu / (AmE), / ˈmærəˌbuː / (BrE)
A large African stork with black-and-white plumage that feeds on carrion; also, the soft downy feathers from its underwings used to trim garments.
large African stork or its feathers

Examples

The zoo's new aviary houses a marabou alongside other exotic birds.
She wore a hat elegantly trimmed with white marabou feathers.
A solitary marabou stalked the edge of the watering hole, searching for food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stork - large, long-legged wading bird

Etymology

From French 'marabout', from Arabic 'murābiṭ' (a holy man), originally referring to the bird's perceived solemnity.
