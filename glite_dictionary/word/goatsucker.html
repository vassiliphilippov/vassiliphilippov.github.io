goatsucker

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊtˌsʌkər / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊtˌsʌkə / (BrE)
A nocturnal or crepuscular bird of the family Caprimulgidae, with mottled plumage and large eyes, that feeds on insects.
nocturnal insect-eating bird (Caprimulgidae)

Examples

The goatsucker is most active just after sunset when insects are plentiful.
Local legends once claimed the goatsucker could steal milk from goats at night.
A goatsucker perched silently on the fence, waiting for its next meal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nightjar - another name for goatsucker, a nocturnal insect-eating bird

Etymology

From the belief that these birds sucked milk from goats, from 'goat' + 'sucker'.
