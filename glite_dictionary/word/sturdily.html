sturdily

1. ADV

/ ˈstɝdəli / (AmE), / ˈstɜːdɪli / (BrE)
In a strong, firm, or solid manner.
in a strong or firm way

Examples

Despite the strong wind, she stood sturdily on the deck.
The bridge was sturdily built to withstand earthquakes.
He planted his feet sturdily apart before lifting the heavy box.

Synset Examples

feet sturdily apart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firmly - in a strong or secure manner
  • Antonym: weakly - in a feeble or fragile manner

Etymology

From sturdy + -ly (adverbial suffix), after Middle English and Old French.
