vegetarian

1. ADJ

/ ˌvɛdʒəˈtɛriən / (AmE), / ˌvɛdʒɪˈteəriən / (BrE)
(of food) not containing meat or fish; suitable for vegetarians.
not containing meat or fish

Examples

I'm looking for a vegetarian option on the menu, please.
This restaurant is famous for its vegetarian dishes.
She prepared a vegetarian lasagna for the family dinner.

Synset Examples

AKA every vegetarian thing that Ash has in her fridge that will remotely go well together

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: vegan - not containing any animal products

Etymology

From Latin 'vegetarius', related to 'vegetare' (to enliven, to animate).

2. NOUN

/ ˌvɛdʒəˈtɛriən / (AmE), / ˌvɛdʒɪˈtɛəriən / (BrE)
a person who does not eat meat or fish, and sometimes avoids all animal products, usually eating mainly plants, grains, nuts, and fruits
person who does not eat meat or fish

Examples

My sister has been a vegetarian since she was twelve years old.
Many restaurants now offer a variety of vegetarian options on their menus.
At the dinner party, they prepared a special dish for their vegetarian guest.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: vegan - person who abstains from all animal products
  • Antonym: omnivore - person who eats both plant and animal foods

Etymology

From Latin 'vegetus' (lively) and English '-arian'; formed in the mid 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10766787-n
Synset POS: n
