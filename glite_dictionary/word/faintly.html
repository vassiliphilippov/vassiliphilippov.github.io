faintly

1. ADV

/ ˈfeɪntli / (AmE), / ˈfeɪntli / (BrE)
In a way that is only just noticeable, weak, or slight.
in a weak or barely noticeable way

Examples

The perfume could be faintly detected in the crowded room.
He smiled faintly when he heard the news, unsure how to react.
The sound of music was faintly audible through the closed window.

Synset Examples

between him and the dim light a form was outlined faintly
stars shining faintly through the overcast
could hear his distant shouts only faintly
the rumors weren't even faintly true

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dimly - in a way that is not bright or clear
  • Antonym: strongly - in a strong or intense way

Etymology

From 'faint' + '-ly', with 'faint' from Old French 'feint' (feigned, weak) and ultimately from Latin 'fingere' (to shape, pretend).
