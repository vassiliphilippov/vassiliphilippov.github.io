deceiver

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsiːvər / (AmE), / dɪˈsiːvə / (BrE)
a person who makes others believe something that is not true; someone who deceives others
someone who deceives others

Examples

She realized too late that he was a skilled deceiver who had manipulated her trust.
The story warns against trusting a deceiver who promises more than they can deliver.
History remembers him as a famous deceiver who tricked his enemies with clever strategies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liar - a person who tells lies
  • Antonym: honest person - someone who tells the truth and does not deceive others

Etymology

From Old French deceveor, from decevoir (to deceive), from Latin decipere (to ensnare, cheat).
