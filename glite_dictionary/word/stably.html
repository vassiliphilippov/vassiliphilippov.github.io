stably

1. ADV

/ ˈsteɪbli / (AmE), / ˈsteɪbli / (BrE)
In a way that is steady, firm, or unlikely to move, change, or fail.
in a firm, steady manner

Examples

The scaffolding was constructed stably to ensure worker safety.
Despite the rough waters, the boat floated stably throughout the journey.
The temperature must remain stably within this range for the experiment to succeed.

Synset Examples

the boulder was balanced stably at the edge of the canyon

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unstably - in an unstable or unsteady manner

Etymology

From the adjective 'stable', from Latin 'stabilis' (firm, steadfast), plus the adverbial suffix '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ˈsteɪbli / (AmE), / ˈsteɪbli / (BrE)
in a stable or unchanging manner; without significant change or fluctuation.
in a stable manner

Examples

The climate in this region has remained stably mild for decades.
The company's profits increased stably over the last five years.
The patient’s heart rate was monitored and found to be stably within the normal range.

Synset Examples

the death rate in Russia has been stably high

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steadily - at a regular rate or in a regular manner
  • Antonym: unstably - in an unstable or changeable manner

Etymology

Formed from 'stable' + '-ly'; 'stable' from Latin 'stabilis' (firm, steadfast).
