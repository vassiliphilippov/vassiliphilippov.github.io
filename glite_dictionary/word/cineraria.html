cineraria

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪnəˈrɛriə / (AmE), / ˌsɪnəˈreəriə / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Pericallis, native to the Canary Islands and widely cultivated for its colorful daisy-like flowers
ornamental plant with colorful daisy-like flowers

Examples

The gardener planted several cineraria along the edge of the flowerbed for a splash of color.
In spring, the vibrant blue and purple blooms of the cineraria brightened the greenhouse.
Many people choose cinerarias as indoor ornamentals because of their long-lasting, colorful flowers.

Etymology

From Latin 'cinis' (ashes), referring to the ash-colored down on some species.
