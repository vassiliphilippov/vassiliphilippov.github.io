harshness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈhɑːʃnəs / (BrE)
excessive sternness or severity in attitude, judgment, or conditions.
excessive sternness or severity

Examples

The harshness of his punishment shocked everyone in the courtroom.
Many recruits struggle with the harshness of military training in their first weeks.
She regretted the harshness of her words after seeing her friend's reaction.

Synset Examples

severity of character
the harshness of his punishment was inhuman
the rigors of boot camp

Related Terms

  • Synonym: severity - the quality of being severe; strictness or harshness
  • Antonym: gentleness - the quality of being gentle, kind, or mild

Etymology

From harsh + -ness, first attested in the 16th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrʃ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈhɑːʃ.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being unpleasantly rough, severe, or grating to the senses
quality of being rough or severe

Examples

His voice lost some of its harshness after he calmed down.
Some critics complained about the harshness of the lighting onstage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: severity - the quality of being very harsh or extreme
  • Antonym: gentleness - the quality of being gentle or mild

Etymology

From 'harsh' + '-ness', modeled after other English abstract nouns.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrʃ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈhɑːʃ.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being rough or coarse in texture so as to cause discomfort or abrasions
rough, abrasive texture

Examples

The harshness of the sandpaper left scratches on the wooden table.
She disliked the harshness of the wool sweater against her skin.
Repeated contact with the harshness of the concrete caused blisters on his hands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roughness - the quality of having an uneven or irregular surface
  • Antonym: smoothness - the quality of having an even and regular surface

Etymology

From 'harsh' + '-ness', with 'harsh' from Middle English, possibly from Old French 'hars' (rough, coarse).
