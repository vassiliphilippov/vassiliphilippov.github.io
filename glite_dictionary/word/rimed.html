rimed

1. ADJ

/ raɪmd / (AmE), / raɪmd / (BrE)
Covered with a thin layer of frost or ice crystals.
covered with frost

Examples

The old fence was rimed with glittering crystals after the cold night.
On winter mornings, the grass appears rimed and silvery under the pale sunlight.
He walked through the rimed meadows, leaving tracks in the frozen grass.

Synset Examples

a frosty glass
hedgerows were rimed and stiff with frost

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frosted - covered with frost or made to look like it
  • Antonym: frost-free - not covered with frost

Etymology

From Middle English *rimen*, meaning 'to cover with hoarfrost', from Old French *rimé*.
