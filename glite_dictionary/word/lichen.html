lichen

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪkən / (AmE), / ˈlɪkən / (BrE)
A composite organism made up of a fungus and an alga or cyanobacterium, typically growing in patches on rocks, trees, and other surfaces.
composite organism on rocks or trees

Examples

The lichen on the old stone wall gave the garden a touch of wild beauty.
Scientists study lichen as an indicator of air quality in forests and urban areas.
A close examination revealed bright yellow lichen covering the surface of the boulder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moss - small flowerless green plant often confused with lichen

Etymology

From Greek leikhēn (tree moss, lichen).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪkən / (AmE), / ˈlɪkən / (BrE)
Any of several skin diseases marked by hard, thick, clustered lesions resembling the plant lichen.
eruptive skin disease with thick lesions
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with lichen after a dermatologist observed the thick, grouped lesions.
Chronic lichen can cause discomfort and itching for those affected by the skin condition.
Topical treatments are often prescribed to manage the symptoms of lichen on the skin.

Etymology

From Greek *leikhēn* (tree moss, lichen; also lichen-like eruption on the skin).
