Leonardo da Vinci

1. PROPN

/ liˌɑːrnədoʊ də ˈvɪntʃi / (AmE), / liˌɑːnɑːdəʊ də ˈvɪntʃi / (BrE)
(1452–1519) Italian artist, scientist, and engineer renowned as a leading figure of the Renaissance, famed for works such as the Mona Lisa and The Last Supper.
Italian Renaissance artist and polymath

Examples

Many scholars consider Leonardo da Vinci to be one of the greatest artists in history.
The museum houses several sketches by Leonardo da Vinci that provide insight into his inventive mind.
Few figures in history have matched the diverse talents of Leonardo da Vinci.

Etymology

Italian, literally 'Leonardo from Vinci' (his place of birth).
