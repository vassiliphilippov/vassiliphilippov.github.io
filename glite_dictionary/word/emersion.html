emersion

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɜrʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈmɜːʃən / (BrE)
The act of emerging or coming into view after being concealed.
the act of emerging or coming out

Examples

The emersion of the submarine was greeted with cheers from the crew.
After hours of anticipation, the emersion of the sun marked the end of the eclipse.
Scientists observed the emersion of new volcanic islands above the sea surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emergence - the process of coming into view or becoming exposed

Etymology

From Latin emersio (a rising up), from emergere (to emerge).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɜrʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈmɜːʃən / (BrE)
(astronomy) the reappearance of a celestial body after being obscured, especially after an eclipse or occultation.
reappearance after eclipse (astronomy)

Examples

The emersion of the moon from behind the shadow was visible to the naked eye.
Astronomers carefully recorded the exact time of the satellite's emersion after the eclipse.
The emersion of Jupiter's moons can provide valuable data for scientists studying orbital dynamics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immersion - disappearance of a celestial body when it is obscured (astronomy)

Etymology

From Latin 'emergere' (to come forth, rise up), related to 'emerge'.
