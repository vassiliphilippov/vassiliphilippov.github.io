lizard

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪzərd / (AmE), / ˈlɪzəd / (BrE)
slang A man who idles in hotel lounges or bars seeking women who might support him financially.
slang: idler seeking women to support him
slang

Examples

In the 1920s, a lizard might spend hours lingering in the hotel lobby, hoping to attract a wealthy companion.
He was known as a lizard among the bar staff because he never bought drinks, just waited for women to approach.
The detective quickly identified the lizard who was always present when affluent ladies arrived.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪzərd / (AmE), / ˈlɪzəd / (BrE)
A reptile with a long body, usually two pairs of legs, and a tapering tail, belonging to the order Squamata.
a type of reptile with legs and a tail

Examples

A green lizard basked in the sun on the garden wall.
She kept a small lizard as a pet in a glass terrarium.
The lizard darted across the rock to catch an insect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reptile - a cold-blooded, scaly vertebrate animal including snakes, lizards, and crocodiles

Etymology

From Old French *lesard*, from Latin *lacerta* (lizard).
