Entomostraca

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛntəˈmɑstrəkə / (AmE), / ˌɛntəməˈstrɑːkə / (BrE)
(in older zoological classifications) a group of small crustaceans that included branchiopods, copepods, ostracods, and barnacles, no longer used in modern taxonomy.
obsolete group of small crustaceans

Examples

In nineteenth-century zoology, Entomostraca was regarded as a major subclass of crustaceans.
Many older textbooks still include descriptions of Entomostraca despite changes in classification.
The group Entomostraca is now considered obsolete, but it once encompassed a wide variety of small aquatic animals.

Etymology

From Greek entomon (insect) + ostrakon (shell).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛntəˈmɑstrəkə / (AmE), / ˌɛntəˈmɒstrəkə / (BrE)
a former subclass of small crustaceans, including Branchiopoda, Copepoda, Ostracoda, and Cirripedia, no longer in technical use
obsolete subclass of small crustaceans

Examples

The order Copepoda was formerly classified within the Entomostraca subclass.
Many reference books from the 19th century mention Entomostraca as a primary group of crustaceans.
Due to advances in taxonomy, Entomostraca is no longer recognized as a valid classification.

Etymology

From Greek entomon (insect) + ostrakon (shell).
