Silene latifolia

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈliːni læˈtɪfoliə / (AmE), / sɪˈliːni læˈtɪfəlɪə / (BrE)
A bluish-green herbaceous plant with sticky stems and clusters of large, white, evening-blooming flowers, belonging to the carnation family.
herbaceous plant with white evening flowers

Examples

The fields were dotted with the delicate white blooms of Silene latifolia at dusk.
Botanists often study Silene latifolia for its unique evening-flowering patterns.
Gardeners sometimes plant Silene latifolia to attract nocturnal pollinators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white campion - the common name for Silene latifolia

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Silene' and species name 'latifolia' meaning 'broad-leaved'.
