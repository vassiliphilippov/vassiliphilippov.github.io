chabasite

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæb.ə.saɪt / (AmE), / ˈkab.ə.saɪt / (BrE)
a mineral of the zeolite family, consisting of a hydrous silicate of calcium and aluminum, typically forming colorless or white rhombohedral crystals
a mineral of the zeolite group

Examples

Crystals of chabasite are often found in cavities within basaltic rocks.
The mineral chabasite can be used in water purification systems due to its porous structure.
A geologist identified the specimen as chabasite after examining its crystal shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zeolite - a group of silicate minerals with a porous structure

Etymology

From French 'chabazite', derived from Greek 'chabazios', a stone mentioned by Pliny.
