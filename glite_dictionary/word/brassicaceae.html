Brassicaceae

1. NOUN

/ brəˌsɪkəˈsiːi / (AmE), / brəˌsɪkəˈsiːiː / (BrE)
a large family of plants with four-petaled flowers, including mustards, cabbages, broccoli, turnips, and cresses
plant family including mustards and cabbages
medical

Examples

Many vegetables in the grocery store, such as broccoli and kale, belong to the Brassicaceae family.
Scientists are studying the genetic diversity within Brassicaceae to improve crop resilience.
The pungent taste of mustard is characteristic of plants from Brassicaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruciferous - relating to plants of the family Brassicaceae

Etymology

From the type genus Brassica, from Latin 'brassica' (cabbage), plus the botanical family suffix '-aceae'.

2. NOUN

/ brəˌsɪkəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / bræˌsɪkəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a large family of plants with four-petaled flowers, including mustards, cabbages, broccoli, turnips, cresses, and their relatives; commonly known as the mustard family
plant family including cabbages and mustards

Examples

The Brassicaceae family includes many common vegetables, such as broccoli, cabbage, and cauliflower.
Researchers have studied disease resistance among members of the Brassicaceae to improve crop yields.
Mustard oil, derived from seeds of the Brassicaceae, is widely used in cooking and traditional medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mustard family - another common name for Brassicaceae

Etymology

From the genus name Brassica (Latin for 'cabbage') + -aceae, a standard botanical family suffix.
