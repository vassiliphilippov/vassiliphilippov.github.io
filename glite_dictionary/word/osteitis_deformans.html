osteitis deformans

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑstiˈaɪtɪs dɪˈfɔrmənz / (AmE), / ˌɒstɪˈaɪtɪs dɪˈfɔːmənz / (BrE)
a chronic disease of bone, mainly affecting middle-aged and elderly people, characterized by excessive bone destruction and abnormal bone regrowth, sometimes resulting in pain, fractures, and deformities
disease causing abnormal bone growth and deformity
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with osteitis deformans after complaining of persistent bone pain and swelling.
Radiographic examination revealed thickened bones attributed to osteitis deformans.
Effective management of osteitis deformans often involves medication to slow bone remodeling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Paget's disease - another name for osteitis deformans

Etymology

From Greek osteon (bone) + -itis (inflammation) + Latin deformans (deforming).
