Usbeg

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌzbɛɡ / (AmE), / ˈʌzbɛɡ / (BrE)
an older or variant term for the Uzbek language, a Turkic language spoken chiefly in Uzbekistan and surrounding regions
variant term for the Uzbek language
archaic

Examples

He is fluent in both Russian and Usbeg.
Many historical documents from Central Asia are written in Usbeg.
She studied the grammar of Usbeg during her linguistic research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Uzbek - the Turkic language spoken in Uzbekistan and by Uzbeks elsewhere

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'Uzbek', from Uzbek (Oʻzbek tili), referring to the people and their language.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌzbɛɡ / (AmE), / ˈʌzbɛɡ / (BrE)
a member of a Turkic ethnic group native to Uzbekistan and surrounding regions in Central Asia.
member of a Turkic ethnic group from Uzbekistan

Examples

The Usbeg people have a long history in Central Asia.
Many Usbeg communities celebrate traditional festivals with music and dance.
She interviewed a local Usbeg leader about the region's cultural heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Uzbek - a member of the same Turkic ethnic group; alternative spelling

Etymology

From Turkic Uzbek, the name of the group, ultimately from the personal name Öz Beg, a 14th-century Mongol leader.
