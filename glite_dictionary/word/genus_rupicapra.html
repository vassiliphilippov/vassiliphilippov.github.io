genus Rupicapra

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌruː.pɪˈkæp.rə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌruː.pɪˈkæp.rə / (BrE)
A genus of goat-antelopes that includes the chamois, found in mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.
genus of goat-antelopes (chamois)

Examples

The genus Rupicapra comprises species commonly known as chamois.
Scientists studied the adaptations of the genus Rupicapra to mountainous terrain.
Both species in the genus Rupicapra are native to rugged alpine environments.

Etymology

From Latin 'rupicapra', meaning chamois or wild goat, from 'rupes' (rock) + 'capra' (goat).
