to foreordain

1. VERB

/ ˌfɔrɔrˈdeɪn / (AmE), / ˌfɔːrɔːˈdeɪn / (BrE)
to decide or determine something in advance, especially by a divine or supernatural power
to decide or determine in advance

Examples

Many believe that their destinies are foreordained by a higher power.
The outcome of the negotiations seemed almost foreordained from the start.
History often makes it appear as though major events were foreordained, though that is rarely the case.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to predestine - to determine in advance, especially by divine will
  • Synonym: to predetermine - to decide or establish in advance

Etymology

From fore- (before) + ordain, from Old French ordener, from Latin ordinare (to put in order).

2. VERB

/ ˌfɔːrɔrˈdeɪn / (AmE), / ˌfɔːrɔːˈdeɪn / (BrE)
(of a deity or fate) to decide or determine (an event or outcome) in advance, especially by divine will or decree.
to predetermine, especially by divine decree

Examples

Many believe that their future is foreordained by a higher power.
The outcome seemed foreordained, given the overwhelming odds.
Some religions teach that all events are foreordained and cannot be changed by human effort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to predestine - to determine an outcome or fate in advance, especially by divine will

Etymology

From Latin 'praeordinare' (to arrange beforehand), via Old French 'forordener'.
