palpation

1. NOUN

/ pælˈpeɪʃən / (AmE), / palˈpeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of examining a part of the body by touch, often to assess size, shape, firmness, or location
examination by touch
medical

Examples

The doctor used palpation to check for any unusual lumps in the patient's abdomen.
During the physical exam, palpation revealed tenderness in the lower back.
Nurses are trained in the technique of palpation to assess patient conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: percussion - method of tapping body parts for examination

Etymology

From Latin 'palpatio', from 'palpare' (to touch gently).
