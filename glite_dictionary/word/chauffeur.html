chauffeur

1. NOUN

/ ʃoʊˈfɜr / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊfə / (BrE)
a person employed to drive a private car for someone else
a person paid to drive a private car

Examples

The wealthy family hired a chauffeur to drive them everywhere in their new limousine.
As a chauffeur, he is always punctual and keeps the car spotless.
During the gala event, their chauffeur waited patiently outside the venue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: driver - a person who drives a vehicle
  • Antonym: passenger - a person who is transported in a vehicle but not operating it

Etymology

From French 'chauffeur' meaning 'stoker' or 'driver', from 'chauffer' (to heat).

2. VERB

/ ʃoʊˈfɝː / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊfə / (BrE)
to drive someone, particularly in a car, as a paid driver or in a manner similar to a chauffeur
to drive someone as a chauffeur

Examples

He offered to chauffeur his guests to the airport early in the morning.
For the weekend, she chauffeured her friends around the city in her new car.
I spent my summer chauffeuring tourists from the hotel to major attractions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drive - to control and operate the movement of a vehicle

Etymology

From French chauffeur (stoker, driver), ultimately from chauffer (to heat).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02061973-v
Synset POS: v
