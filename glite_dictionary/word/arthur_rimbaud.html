Arthur Rimbaud

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑrθər ræmˈboʊ / (AmE), / ˈɑːθə ræ̃ˈbo / (BrE)
A French poet (1854–1891) whose innovative and influential work greatly impacted modern literature and inspired the surrealist movement.
French poet influential in modern literature

Examples

Arthur Rimbaud was a leading figure in the symbolist movement, despite his brief literary career.
Many modern poets cite Arthur Rimbaud as a major influence on their work.
The passionate letters between Paul Verlaine and Arthur Rimbaud are famous in literary history.

Etymology

Arthur Rimbaud is a proper name; the surname is French.
