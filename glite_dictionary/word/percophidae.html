Percophidae

1. NOUN

/ pərˈkɑːfɪˌdiː / (AmE), / pəˈkɒfɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fishes, commonly known as percoid flatheads, characterized by elongated bodies and a flattened head, found in southern hemisphere waters.
family of marine fishes (percoid flatheads)

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species within the Percophidae family off the coast of South America.
The elongated jaw is one of the distinguishing features of the Percophidae.
Members of Percophidae are often found in deep marine environments.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Percophis and suffix -idae indicating a family.
