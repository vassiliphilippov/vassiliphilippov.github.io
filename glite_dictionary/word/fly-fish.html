to fly-fish

1. VERB

/ ˈflaɪˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
to fish using artificial flies as lures, typically with a special rod, reel, and casting technique
to fish with artificial flies as lures

Examples

He loves to fly-fish in the mountain streams every summer.
Many anglers travel to Montana specifically to fly-fish for trout.
She learned how to fly-fish from her grandfather when she was a child.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angle - to fish with rod and line

Etymology

From 'fly' meaning artificial fishing lure and 'fish' meaning to catch fish.
