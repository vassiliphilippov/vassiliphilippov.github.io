ethnic

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛθ.nɪk / (AmE), / ˈɛθ.nɪk / (BrE)
a person who belongs to a particular ethnic group, especially as a minority within a larger society
member of an ethnic group

Examples

In the city's census, the number of ethnics from diverse backgrounds has steadily increased.
The club aimed to provide support for young ethnics adjusting to life in a new country.
Some policies are designed to promote the inclusion of ethnic minorities in education and employment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minority - a group that is smaller in number within a larger society

Etymology

From Greek ethnikos (of a nation or people), from ethnos (nation, people).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10085354-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈɛθ.nɪk / (AmE), / ˈɛθ.nɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the cultural traditions, customs, or identity of a particular group of people
relating to cultural group or traditions

Examples

Her restaurant specializes in ethnic cuisine from Southeast Asia.
The city hosts an annual festival celebrating its ethnic diversity.
He collects ethnic textiles and artifacts from around the world.

Synset Examples

influenced by ethnic and cultural ties
ethnic food

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cultural - relating to the ideas, customs, and social behaviour of a society

Etymology

From Greek ethnikos (national, foreign), from ethnos (nation, people).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02256437-s
Synset POS: s
