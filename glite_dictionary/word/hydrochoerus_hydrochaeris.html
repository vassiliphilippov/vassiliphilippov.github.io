Hydrochoerus hydrochaeris

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdrəˈkɪərəs ˌhaɪdrəˈkɪərɪs / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪdrəˈkɪərəs ˌhʌɪdrəˈkɪərɪs / (BrE)
a large, tailless South American rodent, also known as the capybara, which lives near water and has partly webbed feet; the largest living rodent.
largest living rodent; capybara

Examples

The Hydrochoerus hydrochaeris is often seen relaxing next to rivers in South America.
Zoo visitors were fascinated by the size of the Hydrochoerus hydrochaeris in its habitat.
Farmers sometimes encounter the Hydrochoerus hydrochaeris grazing near water sources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capybara - common name for Hydrochoerus hydrochaeris

Etymology

From Greek hydōr (water) + choiros (pig), reflecting its semi-aquatic nature.
