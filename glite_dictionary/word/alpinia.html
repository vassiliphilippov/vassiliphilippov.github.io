Alpinia

1. NOUN

/ ælˈpɪniə / (AmE), / ælˈpɪniə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs in the ginger family, native to Asia, Australia, and Polynesia, known for their aromatic rhizomes.
a genus of ginger-like perennial herbs

Examples

Gardeners often grow Alpinia for its striking foliage and bright flower spikes.
The roots of Alpinia species are used in traditional Asian medicine.
Some Alpinia varieties are popular as ornamental plants in tropical gardens.

Etymology

Named after Italian botanist Prospero Alpini (1553–1617).

2. NOUN

/ ælˈpɪniə / (AmE), / ælˈpɪniə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial rhizomatous herbs native to Asia, Australia, and Polynesia, known for their ginger-scented rhizomes.
a genus of ginger-scented herbs

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species within the Alpinia genus growing in the rainforest.
The roots of Alpinia are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.
Many gardeners cultivate Alpinia for its ornamental flowers and fragrant foliage.

Etymology

Named after Italian botanist Prospero Alpini (1553–1617).
