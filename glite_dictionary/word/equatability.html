equatability

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌkweɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪˌkweɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being capable of being equated or considered equal in value, function, or meaning.
capability of being equated

Examples

The equatability of different currencies is essential in financial markets.
Scientists question the equatability of two seemingly similar chemical processes.
Debates continue about the equatability of test scores from different countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equivalence - the condition of being equal or interchangeable in value or function

Etymology

From 'equate' + '-ability', following regular English word formation.
