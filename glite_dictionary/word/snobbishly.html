snobbishly

1. ADV

/ ˈsnɑːbɪʃli / (AmE), / ˈsnɒbɪʃli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows disdain or superiority towards those considered to have a lower social status.
in a snobbish or superior way

Examples

She snobbishly rejected the invitation to the neighborhood picnic.
He spoke snobbishly about his expensive education, making others feel uncomfortable.
The couple snobbishly avoided restaurants they considered too casual.

Synset Examples

they snobbishly excluded their less wealthy friends from the party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrogantly - in an arrogant manner, with an exaggerated sense of self-importance
  • Antonym: humbly - in a humble or modest way, without arrogance or superiority

Etymology

From 'snobbish' + '-ly', where 'snob' possibly from 19th-century slang for a shoemaker, later generalizing to mean someone who imitates those of higher social status.
