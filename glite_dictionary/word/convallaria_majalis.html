Convallaria majalis

1. NOUN

/ kənˌvæl.əˈrɪə məˈdʒeɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / kɒnˌvæl.əˈrɪə məˈdʒeɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
A low-growing perennial plant with two large leaves, small fragrant bell-shaped white flowers, and bright red berries; commonly known as lily of the valley.
species of plant; lily of the valley

Examples

Convallaria majalis blooms in spring, filling the garden with a sweet fragrance.
The berries of Convallaria majalis are toxic if ingested, so care should be taken around children.
Many florists include Convallaria majalis in wedding bouquets because of its delicate, elegant appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lily of the valley - common name for Convallaria majalis

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Convallaria' from Latin 'convallis' (valley) and 'majalis' (of May), indicating its habitat and flowering season.
