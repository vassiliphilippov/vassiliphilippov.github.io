Berberis

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɜrbərɪs / (AmE), / ˈbɜːbərɪs / (BrE)
A large genus of spiny shrubs, known as barberries, native to temperate regions in both the New World and the Old World.
genus of spiny shrubs (barberries)

Examples

The garden includes several varieties of Berberis for their colorful leaves.
Berberis species are often planted as ornamental hedges because of their dense thorny branches.
Botanists have identified nearly 500 species within the genus Berberis.

Etymology

From Latin *berberis* (barberry), from Arabic *barbārīs*.
