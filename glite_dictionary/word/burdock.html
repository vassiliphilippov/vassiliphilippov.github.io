burdock

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːˌdɑk / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdɒk / (BrE)
a large biennial plant of the genus Arctium, native to Eurasia, with broad leaves and burrs that stick to clothing or fur
a plant with large leaves and burrs

Examples

The burdock plant is easily recognized by its prickly burrs that cling to animals and people.
She brewed a tea using dried burdock root, believing it had medicinal properties.
Gardeners often struggle to remove burdock because its deep roots make it hard to pull up.

Etymology

From Middle English bur (rough seed-case) + dock (plant name).
