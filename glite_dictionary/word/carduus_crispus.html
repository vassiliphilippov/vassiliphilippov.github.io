Carduus crispus

1. NOUN

/ kɑːrˈduːs ˈkrɪspəs / (AmE), / kɑːˈduːs ˈkrɪspəs / (BrE)
A species of thistle native to Europe, now found in North America, with biennial growth and dense clusters of pinkish-purple flower heads at the ends of its branches.
a European species of thistle

Examples

Botanists found a patch of Carduus crispus thriving along the roadside.
The spiny leaves of Carduus crispus can make it challenging to handle without gloves.
Carduus crispus has become invasive in some North American habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thistle - a group of flowering plants with prickly leaves and stems

Etymology

From Latin: 'Carduus' meaning 'thistle', 'crispus' meaning 'curled' or 'wavy', referring to the leaf edges.
