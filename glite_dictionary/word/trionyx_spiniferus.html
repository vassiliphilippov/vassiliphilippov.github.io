Trionyx spiniferus

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈɒnɪks spaɪˈnɪfərəs / (AmE), / traɪˈɒnɪks spɪˈnɪfərəs / (BrE)
A species of river turtle native to the western United States, known for its warty shell and preference for quiet waters.
species of river turtle of the western US

Examples

The Trionyx spiniferus is easily recognized by the distinctive warty texture on its shell.
Biologists observed a Trionyx spiniferus basking on a riverbank in Wyoming.
Due to habitat loss, the population of Trionyx spiniferus has been declining in several states.

Etymology

From Greek 'trionyx' (three-clawed) and Latin 'spiniferus' (spiny-bearing), referring to the turtle's claws and spiny shell.
