Glengarry

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɛnˈɡæri / (AmE), / ɡlɛnˈɡæri / (BrE)
a Scottish cap with straight sides and a crease along the top, traditionally worn as part of Highland military dress
Scottish military cap with a crease

Examples

The soldier wore his Glengarry proudly as part of his uniform.
Several members of the pipe band donned traditional kilts and a Glengarry.
He gently adjusted the badge on his Glengarry before the ceremony.

Etymology

Named after the Glengarry region in Scotland, where the style is thought to have originated.
