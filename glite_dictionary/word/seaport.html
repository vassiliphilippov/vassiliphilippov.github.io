seaport

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiˌpɔrt / (AmE), / ˈsiːpɔːt / (BrE)
a town or city with a sheltered port where ships load or unload cargo and passengers
town/city with a port for ships

Examples

The city grew rapidly after its seaport was expanded to handle larger ships.
Exports from the seaport have increased significantly over the last decade.
Many goods arrive in the country through its busiest seaport.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harbor - a place on the coast where vessels may find shelter
  • Antonym: airport - a place where aircraft regularly take off and land

Etymology

From 'sea' + 'port', attested since the 15th century.
