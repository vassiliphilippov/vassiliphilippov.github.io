unclimbable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈklaɪməbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈklaɪməbəl / (BrE)
(of a surface or object) impossible to climb or surmount.
impossible to climb

Examples

The icy cliff was completely unclimbable without specialized equipment.
Some walls in the old fortress are now considered unclimbable due to their smooth stone surfaces.
The explorers encountered an unclimbable barrier on their way to the summit.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: climbable - able to be climbed

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) and 'climbable' (able to be climbed).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈklaɪməbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈklaɪməbl / (BrE)
impossible to climb or ascend, especially due to steepness or difficulty
impossible to climb

Examples

The north face of the mountain was considered unclimbable until modern equipment was developed.
Overgrown thorns and jagged rocks made the wall completely unclimbable.
Some trees have trunks so smooth and tall that they seem almost unclimbable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: climbable - able to be climbed; possible to ascend

Etymology

Formed from the prefix un- (not) and climbable (able to be climbed).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02090662-a
Synset POS: a
