Edirne

1. PROPN

/ ɛˈdɪrnə / (AmE), / ɛˈdɪːnə / (BrE)
A city in northwestern Turkey, formerly known as Adrianople, notable for its historical significance and location near the borders of Greece and Bulgaria.
city in northwestern Turkey

Examples

The city of Edirne is known for its magnificent Ottoman architecture.
We drove from Istanbul to Edirne to visit the famous Selimiye Mosque.
Historically, Edirne served as the capital of the Ottoman Empire before Istanbul.

Etymology

From Turkish 'Edirne', ultimately from Roman Emperor Hadrian's name (Latin: 'Hadrianopolis').
