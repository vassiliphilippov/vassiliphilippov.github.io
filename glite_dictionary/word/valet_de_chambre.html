valet de chambre

1. NOUN

/ væˌleɪ də ˈʃɒmbrə / (AmE), / ˌvæleɪ də ˈʃɒ̃bʁə / (BrE)
a male servant who acts as a personal attendant to his employer, especially in a household or at court
male personal attendant or manservant

Examples

The duke's valet de chambre announced his master's arrival with quiet efficiency.
As a valet de chambre, he was entrusted with the gentleman's wardrobe and daily affairs.
Many noble households kept a valet de chambre to ensure personal service and discretion.

Synset Examples

Jeeves was Bertie Wooster's man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valet - a male servant attending to personal needs of his employer

Etymology

From French valet de chambre, literally 'valet of the chamber'.
