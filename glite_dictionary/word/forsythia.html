forsythia

1. NOUN

/ fɔːrˈsɪθiə / (AmE), / fɔːˈsɪθɪə / (BrE)
a shrub of the genus Forsythia, known for its early-blooming bright yellow flowers, native to eastern Asia and southeastern Europe and commonly grown as an ornamental plant
an early-blooming shrub with yellow flowers

Examples

The forsythia in our garden bloom bright yellow every spring, signaling the end of winter.
She cut a few branches from the forsythia to brighten up the living room.
After a long winter, the appearance of forsythia flowers lifts everyone's spirits.

Etymology

Named after William Forsyth (1737–1804), Scottish botanist.
