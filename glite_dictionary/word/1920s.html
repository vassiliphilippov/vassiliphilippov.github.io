1920s

1. NOUN

/ naɪnˈtiːn tˈwɛntiz / (AmE), / naɪnˈtiːn tˈwɛntiz / (BrE)
the decade from 1920 to 1929, often associated with significant cultural and social change
the decade 1920–1929

Examples

Jazz music became wildly popular in the 1920s, influencing culture around the world.
Many historians call the 1920s the 'Roaring Twenties' because of the era's social and economic vibrancy.
Women gained more freedoms and began wearing shorter dresses during the 1920s.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: 1930s - the decade from 1930 to 1939
  • Degree Lower: 1910s - the decade from 1910 to 1919

Etymology

Formed by combining '1920' (the year) and the plural 's' to indicate the decade.
