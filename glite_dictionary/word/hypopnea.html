hypopnea

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɑpniə / (AmE), / haɪˈpɒpniə / (BrE)
Abnormally slow or shallow breathing, often occurring during sleep and used as a medical term.
abnormally slow or shallow breathing
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with hypopnea after noting reduced airflow during sleep.
People suffering from sleep disorders may experience episodes of hypopnea during the night.
Chronic hypopnea can lead to daytime sleepiness and fatigue if left untreated.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: apnea - temporary cessation of breathing

Etymology

From Greek 'hypo-' (under) + 'pnoē' (breath).
