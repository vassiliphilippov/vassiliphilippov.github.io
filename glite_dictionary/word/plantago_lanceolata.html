Plantago lanceolata

1. NOUN

/ plænˈteɪɡoʊ ˌlænsiəˈleɪtə / (AmE), / planˈteɪɡəʊ ˌlansɪəˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A species of plantain with long, narrow, ribbed leaves, native to Europe and Asia but widely naturalized in temperate regions.
a plantain species with narrow ribbed leaves

Examples

Plantago lanceolata is commonly found growing along roadsides and in meadows.
The leaves of Plantago lanceolata are used in traditional herbal remedies.
Researchers studied the pollination patterns of Plantago lanceolata in various climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ribwort plantain - common name for Plantago lanceolata
  • Synonym: buckhorn plantain - another common name for Plantago lanceolata
  • Degree Lower: Plantago major - broadleaf plantain, related species with wider leaves

Etymology

From Latin: 'plantago' (plantain) and 'lanceolata' (lance-shaped).
