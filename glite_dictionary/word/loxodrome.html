loxodrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑksəˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈlɒksəˌdrəʊm / (BrE)
a line on the surface of a sphere that crosses all meridians at a constant angle, typically describing the path of constant compass bearing
line cutting all meridians at constant angle on a sphere

Examples

The navigator plotted a loxodrome to ensure the ship maintained a constant compass direction across the ocean.
On a globe, a loxodrome spirals towards the pole, never actually reaching it.
Unlike a great circle, a loxodrome does not represent the shortest distance between two points on a sphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhumb line - a line crossing all meridians at a constant angle (synonym of loxodrome)

Etymology

From French 'loxodromie', from Greek 'loxos' (oblique) + 'dromos' (running, course).
