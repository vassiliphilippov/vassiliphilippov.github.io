Kore

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɔːreɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɔːreɪ / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the daughter of Zeus and Demeter, later made queen of the underworld by Pluto; also known as Persephone.
Greek goddess, daughter of Zeus and Demeter

Examples

In ancient myths, Kore was abducted by Pluto and became queen of the underworld.
The story of Demeter and Kore explains the origins of the seasons.
Some classical texts refer to Persephone as Kore to emphasize her youthful innocence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Persephone - Daughter of Zeus and Demeter, also queen of the underworld in Greek mythology
  • Synonym: Proserpina - Roman name for Persephone, queen of the underworld

Etymology

From ancient Greek Κόρη (Kórē, meaning 'maiden' or 'girl'), an epithet of Persephone.
