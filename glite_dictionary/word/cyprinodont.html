cyprinodont

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈprɪnəˌdɑnt / (AmE), / sɪˈprɪnədɒnt / (BrE)
a small, often brightly colored freshwater or brackish-water fish of the family Cyprinodontidae, widely found in warm regions
small freshwater fish of the Cyprinodontidae family

Examples

The aquarium was home to a rare species of cyprinodont collected from South America.
Many cyprinodonts are popular among aquarists due to their vibrant colors and ease of care.
Researchers found that the cyprinodont adapts well to environmental changes in brackish pools.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Cyprinodontidae, from cyprinus (carp) + -odont (toothed).
