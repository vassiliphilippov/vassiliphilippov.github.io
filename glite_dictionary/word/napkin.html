napkin

1. NOUN

/ ˈnæp.kɪn / (AmE), / ˈnap.kɪn / (BrE)
A piece of cloth or folded fabric worn by infants between the legs and fastened at the waist, used to catch excrement.
cloth worn by infants to catch excrement

Examples

The nurse changed the baby's napkin every few hours to keep him comfortable.
In the past, reusable cloth napkins were commonly used for infants instead of disposable diapers.
She neatly folded the clean napkin before fastening it around the baby's waist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diaper - a piece of absorbent material worn by babies to catch excrement

Etymology

From Old French nappe (cloth) + diminutive suffix -kin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnæp.kɪn / (AmE), / ˈnæp.kɪn / (BrE)
A small piece of cloth or paper used at meals to wipe the mouth and hands or to protect clothing.
piece of table linen for wiping mouth or hands

Examples

Please place your napkin on your lap before you start eating.
She wiped her mouth delicately with a white napkin.
At the restaurant, the waiter handed each guest a clean napkin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serviette - British term for napkin

Etymology

From Middle English nappekyn, from Old French nappe (tablecloth) + -kin (diminutive).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
