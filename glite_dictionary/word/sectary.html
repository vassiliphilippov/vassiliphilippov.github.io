sectary

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛktɛri / (AmE), / ˈsɛktəri / (BrE)
a member of a sect, especially a religious group regarded as heretical or separate from an established church
member of a sect

Examples

The religious debate was heated, and one sectary accused another of distorting doctrine.
In the 17th century, a sectary might risk persecution for his beliefs.
Few outsiders understood the rituals practiced by the sectaries of the small community.

Synset Examples

most sectarians are intolerant of the views of any other sect

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sectarian - a member or supporter of a sect or group characterized by sectarianism
  • Synonym: heretic - a person believing in or practicing religious heresy

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'sectarius', from Latin 'secta' (school of thought, party, following).
