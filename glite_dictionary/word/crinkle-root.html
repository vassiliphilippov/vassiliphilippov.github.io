crinkle-root

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪŋkəl ˌrut / (AmE), / ˈkrɪŋkəl ˌruːt / (BrE)
A North American plant (Cardamine diphylla) with toothed leaves and pungent, scaly roots, sometimes used for flavoring or medicinal purposes.
North American herb with pungent roots

Examples

The crinkle-root is found in moist woods across eastern North America.
Early settlers used the pungent roots of crinkle-root as a seasoning.
Botanists studied the habitat preferences of crinkle-root in forested regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toothwort - Another common name for Cardamine diphylla, the same plant as crinkle-root
