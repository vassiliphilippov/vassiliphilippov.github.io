aught

1. NOUN

/ ɔt / (AmE), / ɔːt / (BrE)
Nothing; a quantity of no importance.
nothing; zero
archaic

Examples

After searching for hours, he found aught to help him.
There was aught left of their fortune once the debts were paid.
In truth, his promise meant aught in the end.

Synset Examples

it looked like nothing I had ever seen before
reduced to nil all the work we had done
we racked up a pathetic goose egg
it was all for naught
I didn't hear zilch about it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naught - nothing; zero
  • Synonym: nothing - not anything; no single thing

Etymology

From Old English āwiht (ever anything), later confused with 'naught' (nothing).
