hypnotized

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɪp.nəˌtaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈhɪp.nə.taɪzd / (BrE)
having one's attention fixed as if under a spell or trance; deeply fascinated or absorbed
fascinated as if under a spell

Examples

The children sat hypnotized by the magician's performance, unable to look away.
She was hypnotized by the flickering flames in the fireplace for hours.
He listened hypnotized to the storyteller as if under a spell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mesmerized - having your attention completely absorbed or transfixed
  • Antonym: uninterested - not paying attention or lacking interest

Etymology

From the verb 'hypnotize', formed from 'hypnosis' (from Greek 'hypnos', meaning sleep) plus the suffix '-ize'.
