NRA

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn ɑr ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛn ɑː ˈreɪ / (BrE)
(in the US) an organization that advocates for gun rights and opposes government regulation of firearms.
US gun rights advocacy organization
american

Examples

The NRA is often mentioned in debates over gun control legislation.
Several politicians have received endorsements and funding from the NRA.
After the tragic shooting, critics called for the NRA to change its stance on gun regulations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: National Rifle Association - Full name of the NRA, an American gun advocacy group

Etymology

Abbreviation of National Rifle Association, founded in the United States in 1871.
