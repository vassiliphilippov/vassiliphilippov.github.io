creel

1. NOUN

/ kriːl / (AmE), / kriːl / (BrE)
A wicker basket used by anglers for carrying or holding caught fish.
fishing basket for holding fish

Examples

He placed the freshly caught trout into his creel before continuing downstream.
Her grandfather wove a new creel for her as a birthday present.
With his creel slung over his shoulder, he set off for a day of fishing.

Etymology

From Middle English 'crele', possibly from Old French 'greille' (grill, grating).
