to surface-mine

1. VERB

/ ˈsɝː.fɪs ˌmaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.fɪs ˌmaɪn / (BrE)
to extract minerals or ore from the ground by removing the surface layer, typically by strip mining, rather than by underground mining
extract ore by strip mining

Examples

Many companies surface-mine coal to meet the rising demand for energy.
Environmental concerns have increased as more land is surface-mined each year.
They plan to surface-mine iron ore from that region in the coming decade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to strip-mine - to mine by removing a long strip of overlying soil and rock

Etymology

Formed from 'surface' + 'mine', referring to mining activities on the earth's surface.
