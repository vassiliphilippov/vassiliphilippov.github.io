Schizachyrium

1. NOUN

/ skɪzəˈkɪriəm / (AmE), / skɪzəˈkɪəriəm / (BrE)
A genus of grasses found in many parts of the world, sometimes overlapping with the genus Andropogon.
genus of grasses

Examples

Several species in the Schizachyrium genus are important for pasture and soil conservation.
The botanist specialized in the study of Schizachyrium across North America.
Bluestem, a common native grass, belongs to the Schizachyrium genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Andropogon - a related genus of grasses, sometimes overlapping with Schizachyrium

Etymology

From Greek 'schizo' (split) + 'achyron' (chaff), referring to features of the grass.

2. NOUN

/ skɪz.əˈkɪr.i.əm / (AmE), / skɪz.əˈkɪər.i.əm / (BrE)
A genus of grasses in the family Poaceae, some species of which are known as bluestems and are native to warm regions worldwide.
a genus of grasses in Poaceae

Examples

Several species of Schizachyrium are important forage grasses in tropical climates.
The tall bluestem, a member of the Schizachyrium genus, dominates many native prairies.
Botanists collected samples of Schizachyrium for their research on grassland ecosystems.

Etymology

From Greek 'schizo-' (to split) and 'achyron' (chaff), referring to features of the grass.
