noncombinative

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈkɑm.bəˌneɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / nɒnˈkɒm.bɪ.nə.tɪv / (BrE)
Not involving or characterized by combination.
not involving combination

Examples

The two chemicals are noncombinative and do not react with each other.
His theory proposes that certain elements in language are fundamentally noncombinative.
The design uses noncombinative strategies to maintain the separation of components.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: combinative - involving or characterized by combination

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' meaning 'not', and 'combinative' from Latin 'combinare' (to combine).
