bracteole

1. NOUN

/ ˈbræk.ti.oʊl / (AmE), / ˈbræk.tɪəʊl / (BrE)
a small bract, especially one situated on the pedicel of a flower
a small bract in botany
medical

Examples

Each flower on the stem is subtended by a tiny bracteole near its base.
The botanist pointed out the difference between a bracteole and a full-sized bract.
During identification, note the presence or absence of a bracteole below each flower.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bract - a modified leaf or scale, typically small, bearing a flower or inflorescence

Etymology

From Latin 'bracteola', diminutive of 'bractea' (thin plate, scale, bract).
