to prioritise

1. VERB

/ praɪˈɔrɪtaɪz / (AmE), / praɪˈɒrɪtaɪz / (BrE)
to decide which tasks or things are more important so that you can deal with them first
decide order of importance
british

Examples

You need to prioritise your assignments if you want to meet all the deadlines.
The manager will prioritise projects based on their impact and urgency.
She learned to prioritise her health over her work after falling ill.

Synset Examples

we have too many things to do and must prioritize

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prioritize - assign a priority to, especially in American English

Etymology

From 'priority' + '-ise', modelled after 'prioritize' in American English; 'priority' from Anglo-Norman and Old French.
