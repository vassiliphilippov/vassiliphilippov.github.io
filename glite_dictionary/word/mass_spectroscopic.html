mass spectroscopic

1. ADJ

/ mæs spɛk.trəˈskɑ.pɪk / (AmE), / mæs spɛk.trəˈskɒ.pɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving mass spectroscopy, a technique for analyzing the mass and composition of chemical substances
relating to mass spectroscopy

Examples

The mass spectroscopic analysis provided detailed information on the chemical structure of the compound.
Researchers used mass spectroscopic techniques to identify unknown substances in the sample.
Advances in mass spectroscopic methods have improved the accuracy of forensic investigations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mass spectrometry - the science or process of measuring masses of particles

Etymology

Formed from 'mass spectroscopy' (identifying chemical substances by mass) and suffix '-ic'.
