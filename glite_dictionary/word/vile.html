vile

1. ADJ

/ vaɪl / (AmE), / vaɪl / (BrE)
extremely unpleasant or morally bad; wicked or disgusting.
morally bad; extremely unpleasant

Examples

He was sentenced for committing a vile act against his own community.
Her vile remarks shocked everyone in the meeting.
They protested the vile treatment of the animals at the facility.

Synset Examples

would do something as despicable as murder
ugly crimes
the vile development of slavery appalled them
a slimy little liar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: despicable - deserving hatred and contempt; similar in sense
  • Antonym: virtuous - having high moral standards; opposite in meaning

Etymology

From Latin vilis (of little worth, cheap).
