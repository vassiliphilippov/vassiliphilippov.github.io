Clemenceau

1. PROPN

/ klɛˈmɑːnsoʊ / (AmE), / klɛˈmɒnsəʊ / (BrE)
the surname of Georges Clemenceau (1841–1929), a French statesman who played a major role in negotiating the Treaty of Versailles after World War I
French statesman (1841–1929), key Versailles negotiator

Examples

Clemenceau was instrumental in ensuring harsh terms for Germany in the Treaty of Versailles.
Historians often credit Clemenceau with helping to end World War I through diplomatic negotiations.
During the Paris Peace Conference, Clemenceau represented France's interests with determination and vigor.

Etymology

French surname; from Georges Clemenceau, noted French politician.
