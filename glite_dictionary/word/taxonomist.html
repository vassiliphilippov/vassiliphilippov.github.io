taxonomist

1. NOUN

/ tækˈsɑnəmɪst / (AmE), / tækˈsɒnəmɪst / (BrE)
a biologist who specializes in the classification of organisms based on their structure, origin, and behavior
biologist specializing in organism classification

Examples

As a taxonomist, she discovered several new plant species in the rainforest.
The findings of the taxonomist helped clarify the relationships between different species of birds.
Every major museum employs at least one taxonomist to curate its natural history collections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taxonomer - a person who classifies things; sometimes used synonymously with 'taxonomist'

Etymology

From taxonomy (from Greek taxis 'arrangement' + -nomia 'method') + -ist (denoting a person).
