to fumigate

1. VERB

/ ˈfjuːmɪˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈfjuːmɪɡeɪt / (BrE)
to treat (a place or an object) with chemical fumes in order to disinfect it or destroy pests
treat with chemical fumes for disinfection or pest control

Examples

They decided to fumigate the entire house after discovering a termite infestation.
The storage facility was regularly fumigated to prevent the spread of mold and pests.
Before moving in, the landlord arranged to fumigate the apartment for safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disinfect - to clean something with the aim of killing germs
  • Synonym: to sterilize - to make something free from bacteria or other living microorganisms

Etymology

From Latin 'fumigare' (to smoke), from 'fumus' (smoke).
