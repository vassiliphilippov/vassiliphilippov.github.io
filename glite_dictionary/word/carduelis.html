Carduelis

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˈduːəlɪs / (AmE), / kɑːˈdjuːəlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of finches that includes goldfinches, siskins, redpolls, and linnets, classified in the subfamily Carduelinae.
genus of finches (goldfinches, siskins, etc.)

Examples

The genus Carduelis includes a variety of colorful finches found worldwide.
Some ornithologists debate the current classification of Carduelis species.
Goldfinches were originally placed in the genus Carduelis by early taxonomists.

Etymology

From Latin carduelis (goldfinch).

2. NOUN

/ kɑrˈdjuːəlɪs / (AmE), / kɑːˈdjuːəlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of finches including species such as goldfinches, siskins, redpolls, and linnets, formerly classified as the type genus of the subfamily Carduelinae.
genus of finches (goldfinches, siskins, etc.)

Examples

The European goldfinch belongs to the genus Carduelis.
Researchers recently revised the classification of several birds previously included in Carduelis.
Species in the genus Carduelis are common across Europe and Asia.

Etymology

From Latin carduelis (goldfinch, thistle finch).
