Harpullia pendula

1. NOUN

/ hɑrˈpʌliə ˈpɛndjələ / (AmE), / hɑːˈpʌliə ˈpɛndjʊlə / (BrE)
An Australian tree known for its ornamental foliage and for yielding variegated tulipwood, often used in landscaping and woodworking.
Australian tree yielding variegated tulipwood

Examples

The Harpullia pendula is commonly planted along city streets in eastern Australia.
Woodworkers prize the variegated timber of the Harpullia pendula for its decorative grain.
Many gardeners appreciate how Harpullia pendula provides dense shade and bright fruit in parks.
