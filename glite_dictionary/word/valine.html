valine

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæliːn / (AmE), / ˈveɪliːn / (BrE)
an essential amino acid found in proteins, important for growth in children and for maintaining nitrogen balance in adults
an essential amino acid
medical

Examples

Branched-chain amino acids like valine are crucial in many metabolic processes.
People with certain genetic conditions may require dietary supplements containing valine.
Eggs and dairy products are good natural sources of valine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leucine - an essential amino acid similar in structure to valine
  • Synonym: isoleucine - an essential amino acid closely related to valine and leucine

Etymology

From valeric acid, with the suffix -ine; ultimately from Latin 'valere' (to be strong).
