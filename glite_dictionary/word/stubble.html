stubble

1. NOUN

/ ˈstʌb.əl / (AmE), / ˈstʌb.əl / (BrE)
Material consisting of seed husks and small fragments of stem or leaf that remain after seeds have been separated, often as a byproduct of threshing or processing grain.
seed husks and stem fragments left after threshing

Examples

After the wheat harvest, heaps of stubble were left scattered across the field.
The old mill was filled with stubble and other remnants from last season's crops.
Farmers often burn stubble to clear their fields for the next planting season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaff - the husks of grains and grasses separated during threshing

2. NOUN

/ ˈstʌb.əl / (AmE), / ˈstʌb.əl / (BrE)
short, stiff hairs growing on a man's face when he has not shaved for a few days
short facial hair growth

Examples

He hadn't shaved for a week, so thick stubble covered his chin.
A few days of stubble gave him a rugged look.
She brushed her fingers against the rough stubble on his cheek.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: beard - hair growing on the lower part of a man's face
  • Antonym: clean-shaven - having no facial hair due to shaving

Etymology

From Middle English stobble, from Latin *stipula* (stalk, stem).
