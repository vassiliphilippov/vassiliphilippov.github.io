to accede

1. VERB

/ əkˈsiːd / (AmE), / əkˈsiːd / (BrE)
to assume an official position or office, especially with reference to a throne or leadership role
to assume an office or position

Examples

After the king's death, his daughter acceded to the throne and began her reign.
He will accede to the presidency next month, following the official ceremony.
It is customary for the heir apparent to accede immediately upon the ruler's abdication.

Synset Examples

accede to the throne

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ascend - to rise to a position, such as a throne or office
  • Antonym: to abdicate - to formally give up a position of power, especially a throne

Etymology

From Latin 'accedere' (to approach, to enter upon), from 'ad-' (to) + 'cedere' (go).

2. VERB

/ əkˈsiːd / (AmE), / əkˈsiːd / (BrE)
to agree to a request, proposal, or demand, often after initially opposing it; to yield to another's wish or opinion
to yield or agree to a request

Examples

After much debate, the committee finally acceded to the public's demands for change.
She refused to accede to her friend's persistent requests until she heard all the facts.
The company acceded to the union's terms in order to avoid a strike.

Synset Examples

The government bowed to the military pressure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yield - to give way to arguments, demands, or pressure
  • Synonym: to comply - to act in accordance with a wish or command
  • Antonym: refuse - to indicate or show that one is not willing to do something

Etymology

From Latin accedere (to approach, assent), from ad- (to) + cedere (to go, yield).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00879879-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ əkˈsiːd / (AmE), / əkˈsiːd / (BrE)
to agree to a request, proposal, or demand, often after initial hesitation or negotiation
to agree or give consent

Examples

The board will accede to the company's demands if it ensures stability.
After much discussion, she finally acceded to their proposal.
He refused at first, but later acceded to our request.

Synset Examples

The Maestro assented to the request for an encore

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assent - to agree or approve, especially after thoughtful consideration
  • Antonym: refuse - to indicate or show that one is not willing to do something

Etymology

From Latin *accedere* (to approach, agree to), from *ad-* (to) + *cedere* (to go).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
