Gastrophryne olivacea

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæstroʊˈfraɪni oʊˈlɪveɪsiə / (AmE), / ˌɡastrəʊˈfraɪni əʊˈlɪviːsiə / (BrE)
A small, secretive toad species native to central and western North America, known for its smooth, tough skin.
a North American toad species

Examples

The Gastrophryne olivacea spends most of its life hidden beneath logs or in loose soil.
Biologists observed several Gastrophryne olivacea near the western edge of Texas after a rainfall.
Because of its secretive habits, the Gastrophryne olivacea is rarely seen by casual hikers.

Etymology

From Greek roots *gastro-* (stomach) and *phryne* (toad); *olivacea* from Latin meaning 'olive-colored'.
