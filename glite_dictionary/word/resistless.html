resistless

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈzɪstləs / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪstlɪs / (BrE)
(literary) Not offering any resistance; incapable of opposing or withstanding.
offering no resistance

Examples

The resistless tide swept away everything in its path.
He was overwhelmed by the resistless force of her argument.
The resistless prisoners made no attempt to escape.

Synset Examples

resistless hostages

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irresistible - impossible to resist; too attractive or appealing

Etymology

From 'resist' + the suffix '-less', meaning 'without resistance'.

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈzɪstləs / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪstlɪs / (BrE)
Too strong or attractive to be resisted; overpowering or irresistible, now chiefly archaic or literary.
impossible to resist; overpowering (archaic/literary)
archaic

Examples

He felt a resistless urge to confess everything to her that night.
The army advanced with resistless force, overwhelming all opposition in its path.
She was swept away by a resistless tide of emotion she could not control.

Synset Examples

irresistible (or resistless) impulses
what happens when an irresistible force meets an immovable object?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irresistible - too attractive or appealing to be resisted

Etymology

Formed from 'resist' + '-less', indicating without the ability to be resisted.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
