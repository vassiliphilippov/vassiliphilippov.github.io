aboriginal

1. ADJ

/ ˌæbəˈrɪdʒənl / (AmE), / ˌabəˈrɪdʒɪnəl / (BrE)
relating to the people or things that have existed in a region from the earliest times; indigenous.
relating to original inhabitants

Examples

The aboriginal people of the region have a rich cultural heritage.
Some aboriginal tribes still live according to traditional customs.
The study focused on the aboriginal languages of North America.

Synset Examples

native Americans
the aboriginal peoples of Australia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indigenous - originating or occurring naturally in a place; native

Etymology

From Latin ab origine (from the beginning).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01040830-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌæbəˈrɪdʒənəl / (AmE), / ˌabəˈrɪdʒɪnəl / (BrE)
existing from the earliest times; in an original or primitive state or form.
existing from the earliest times

Examples

The region is covered in aboriginal forests dating back thousands of years.
Scientists study aboriginal rock formations to understand the Earth's early history.
Some species are considered aboriginal to this remote island, having evolved in isolation.

Synset Examples

aboriginal forests
primal eras before the appearance of life on earth
the forest primeval
primordial matter
primordial forms of life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primordial - existing at or from the beginning of time
  • Synonym: primeval - belonging to the earliest ages

Etymology

From Latin aborigines (original inhabitants, earliest ancestors).

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ˌæb.əˈrɪdʒ.ə.nəl / (AmE), / ˌab.əˈrɪdʒ.ɪ.nəl / (BrE)
a member of the original inhabitants of a region, especially before colonization or settlement by another group
an indigenous person of a place

Examples

The new policy aims to protect the rights of aboriginals in remote areas.
Many aboriginals maintain unique languages and cultural traditions.
Museums now feature exhibitions highlighting the histories of local aboriginals.

Synset Examples

the art of the natives of the northwest coast
the Canadian government scrapped plans to tax the grants to aboriginal college students

Related Terms

  • Synonym: native - a person born in a particular place or associated with a place by birth
  • Antonym: settler - a person who moves to a new region and establishes a permanent residence, often contrasting with indigenous peoples

Etymology

From Latin ab origine (from the beginning, from the origin)
