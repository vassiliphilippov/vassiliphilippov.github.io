invigilation

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌvɪdʒəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌvɪdʒɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of supervising students during an examination to prevent cheating
supervision of exams to prevent cheating

Examples

The university requires strict invigilation to maintain the integrity of its exams.
She was responsible for the invigilation of over one hundred students during the final tests.
Without careful invigilation, cases of academic dishonesty might go unnoticed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proctoring - the act of supervising an exam

Etymology

From Latin 'invigilare' (to keep watch), via French 'invigilation'.
