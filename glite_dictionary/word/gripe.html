gripe

1. NOUN

/ ɡraɪp / (AmE), / ɡraɪp / (BrE)
An informal complaint or expression of dissatisfaction, especially about something relatively minor.
an informal complaint or objection
informal

Examples

My only gripe about the hotel is the slow Wi-Fi.
She aired her gripe with management during the meeting.
Despite his gripe about the noise, he renewed his lease.

Synset Examples

I have a gripe about the service here

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complaint - an expression of dissatisfaction or annoyance
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

Probably from Old English *grīpan* (to seize, grasp), shifting in meaning over time.

2. VERB

/ ɡraɪp / (AmE), / ɡraɪp / (BrE)
to complain persistently, especially about something minor or trivial
to complain persistently

Examples

She tends to gripe about the weather whenever it rains.
Instead of working, he would rather gripe about his assignments all day.
Stop griping and help us solve the problem.

Synset Examples

What was he hollering about?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

From Middle English 'gripen' (to seize, grasp), from Old English 'grīpan'. Sense of 'complain' emerged in the 20th century.

Synset POS: v
