properness

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːpərnəs / (AmE), / ˈprɒpənəs / (BrE)
the quality of being correct, appropriate, or suitable for a particular situation or purpose
quality of being proper or appropriate

Examples

His sense of properness made him uncomfortable with casual conversations during formal meetings.
The properness of her attire impressed everyone at the ceremony.
Some people question the properness of strict dress codes in modern workplaces.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: impropriety - the state of being improper or not in accordance with accepted standards
  • Synonym: propriety - conformity to accepted standards of behavior or morals

Etymology

From 'proper' + '-ness', with 'proper' from Latin 'proprius' (one's own, proper to).
