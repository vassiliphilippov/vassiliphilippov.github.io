Ishmael

1. PROPN

/ ɪʃˈmeɪəl / (AmE), / ˈɪʃmeɪl / (BrE)
In the Old Testament, the son of Abraham who was cast out after the birth of Isaac, traditionally regarded as the ancestor of 12 Arabian tribes.
son of Abraham in the Old Testament

Examples

Many historians discuss the legacy of Ishmael in the context of Middle Eastern ancestry.
The biblical story of Ishmael highlights themes of exile and survival.
Ishmael is considered a patriarch by many Arab peoples.

Etymology

From Hebrew Yishma'el, meaning 'God hears'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪʃˌmeɪl / (AmE), / ˈɪʃ.meɪl / (BrE)
A person who is rejected or cast out from society or home, typically an outcast.
social outcast

Examples

After the scandal, he became an Ishmael among his former friends.
In the novel, the main character is portrayed as an Ishmael living on the fringes of society.
She felt like an Ishmael at the new school, unable to find anyone to talk to.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outcast - A person who has been rejected by society or a social group
  • Synonym: pariah - A person who is despised or avoided by others

Etymology

From the biblical figure Ishmael, Abraham's son, who was cast out in the Book of Genesis.
