jessamine

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛsəmɪn / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛsəmɪn / (BrE)
a climbing shrub with fragrant white, yellow, or red flowers, often used in perfume or to flavor tea
fragrant climbing shrub; used in perfume and tea

Examples

The delicate fragrance of jessamine filled the evening air in the garden.
She added dried jessamine petals to the tea blend for a sweeter aroma.
The old fence was covered with flowering jessamine, creating a colorful display in spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jasmine - a shrub with fragrant flowers, used in perfume and flavoring

Etymology

Variant of 'jasmine', from Old French 'jasmin', from Arabic 'yās(a)mīn'.
