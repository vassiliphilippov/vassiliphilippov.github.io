wrestling

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛs.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɛs.lɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of engaging in close hand-to-hand combat, often as a sport or struggle
close hand-to-hand combat

Examples

Wrestling is a popular sport in many countries around the world.
The two boys began wrestling on the playground after an argument.
He took lessons in wrestling to improve his strength and agility.

Synset Examples

they had a fierce wrestle
we watched his grappling and wrestling with the bully

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grappling - the act of engaging in close combat by seizing

Etymology

From Old English 'wræstlian', related to 'wrest', meaning to twist or pull.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛslɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɛslɪŋ / (BrE)
A sport in which two people fight by holding, pushing, or throwing each other, attempting to force their opponent’s shoulders to touch the ground.
competitive hand-to-hand sport

Examples

He started practicing wrestling when he was in high school.
The Olympic Games include several styles of wrestling in their competitions.
She watched a wrestling match on television last night with her friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grappling - hand-to-hand combat technique involving seizing or locking

Etymology

From Old English 'wraestlian' (to wrestle), from 'wræstlian', related to 'wræstan' (to twist).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00448525-n
Synset POS: n
