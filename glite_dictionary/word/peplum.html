peplum

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛpləm / (AmE), / ˈpɛpləm / (BrE)
A flared or ruffled strip of fabric attached at the waistline of a dress, jacket, or blouse, used especially as a decorative detail.
decorative waist ruffle on clothing

Examples

The designer added a peplum to the jacket for a more dramatic silhouette.
Her vintage blouse features a delicate lace peplum at the waist.
Many evening gowns incorporate a peplum to accentuate the wearer's shape.

Etymology

From Latin 'peplum', from Greek 'peplos' (a tunic or garment).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛpləm / (AmE), / ˈpɛpləm / (BrE)
A short overskirt or a flared, gathered, or pleated piece of fabric attached at the waist of a garment, originally worn by women in ancient Greece and featured in modern fashion.
a short overskirt or fabric flare at the waist

Examples

The designer added a peplum to the dress to create a dramatic silhouette.
In ancient Greece, the peplum was a common element of women’s attire.
Her jacket featured a stylish peplum that gave it a modern twist.

Etymology

From Latin 'peplum', from Greek 'peplos' (a garment, robe).
