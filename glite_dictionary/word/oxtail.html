oxtail

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑkˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈɒks.teɪl / (BrE)
The skinned tail of a cow, typically used as an ingredient in soups and stews.
the tail of a cow, used in cooking

Examples

The chef simmered the oxtail for hours to create a rich, flavorful stew.
My grandmother's recipe for oxtail soup is a family favorite during the winter months.
You should remove excess fat from the oxtail before adding it to the broth.

Etymology

From 'ox' (a type of cattle) + 'tail', literally referring to the tail of an ox.
