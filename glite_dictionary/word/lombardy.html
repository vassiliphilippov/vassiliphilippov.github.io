Lombardy

1. PROPN

/ ˈlɑm.bər.di / (AmE), / ˈlɒm.bə.di / (BrE)
a region in north central Italy, bordering Switzerland, known for its economic importance and the city of Milan.
region of northern Italy

Examples

Many tourists visit Lombardy each year to explore its beautiful lakes and historic cities.
Milan, the largest city in Lombardy, is renowned as a global fashion and finance center.
Agriculture and industry both play major roles in Lombardy's prosperous economy.

Etymology

From Italian 'Lombardia', derived from the Lombards, a Germanic people who settled in this region.
