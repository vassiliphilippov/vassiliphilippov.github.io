staggerer

1. NOUN

/ ˈstæɡɚɚ / (AmE), / ˈstæɡərə / (BrE)
a person who walks unsteadily as if about to fall, often due to weakness, intoxication, or illness
person who walks unsteadily

Examples

The drunk man was a clear staggerer as he tried to cross the street.
After running the marathon, she became a staggerer on tired legs.
A sudden illness turned him into a staggerer, barely able to keep his balance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stagger - walk or move unsteadily, as if about to fall

Etymology

From the verb 'stagger' (to walk unsteadily) + '-er' (agent suffix).
