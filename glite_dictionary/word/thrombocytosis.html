thrombocytosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌθrəmbəˌsaɪˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌθrɒmbəsʌɪˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
an increase in the number of platelets in the blood, which increases the risk of blood clot formation and is often associated with certain diseases
abnormally high platelet count in blood
medical

Examples

The hematologist diagnosed thrombocytosis after reviewing the patient's blood test results.
Thrombocytosis can increase the likelihood of developing dangerous blood clots in veins or arteries.
Many cases of thrombocytosis are secondary to underlying inflammatory or malignant diseases.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thrombocytopenia - abnormally low platelet count in the blood

Etymology

From Greek 'thrombos' (clot) + 'kytos' (cell) + '-osis' (condition).
