budgerygah

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌdʒəriˌɡɑ / (AmE), / ˈbʌdʒəriɡɑː / (BrE)
a small Australian parakeet, usually light green with black and yellow markings in the wild but bred in many colors as a pet
Australian parakeet species

Examples

The budgerygah is known for its cheerful vocalizations and bright feathers.
Many households keep a budgerygah as a lively and friendly pet.
In the wild, the budgerygah typically sports green, yellow, and black plumage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: budgerigar - the more common name for this Australian parakeet species

Etymology

From the Wiradjuri Australian Aboriginal word 'bˆdiriɡaː' (good).
