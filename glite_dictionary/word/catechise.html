to catechise

1. VERB

/ ˈkætɪˌkaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkatɪkʌɪz / (BrE)
to instruct someone in the principles of Christianity, typically by means of questions and answers
give religious instruction, especially orally

Examples

The priest would catechise new members of the parish every Sunday afternoon.
Parents are encouraged to catechise their children at home as part of their religious education.
During Lent, the church elders catechise those preparing for baptism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to instruct - to teach or provide information

Etymology

From Late Latin catechizare, from Greek katekhizein, from katekhizein (to instruct orally).

2. VERB

/ ˈkætəˌkaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkatɪkʌɪz / (BrE)
to instruct or examine by means of questions and answers, especially in preparation for religious instruction
instruct by questioning, especially religiously

Examples

The priest would catechise the children every Sunday before the service.
The teacher catechised her students to check their understanding of the lesson.
He was thoroughly catechised about the rules of conduct before joining the group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to interrogate - to ask questions formally, often aggressively
  • Synonym: grill - to question intensely

Etymology

From Late Latin catechizare, from Greek katēkhein ‘to instruct orally’.
