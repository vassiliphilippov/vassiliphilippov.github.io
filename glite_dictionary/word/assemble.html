to assemble

1. VERB

/ əˈsɛm.bəl / (AmE), / əˈsɛm.bəl / (BrE)
to put together the parts of something to form a whole; to gather components or people into a group or structure
to put parts or people together

Examples

Engineers will assemble the parts of the machine in the workshop.
The students assembled a robot as part of their project.
Volunteers assemble every Saturday to build houses for families in need.

Synset Examples

She pieced a quilt
He tacked together some verses
They set up a committee

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to disassemble - to take apart something that was assembled
  • Synonym: gather - to collect things or people together

Etymology

From Old French 'assembler', from Latin 'assimulare' (to bring together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01660471-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈsɛmbəl / (AmE), / əˈsɛmbəl / (BrE)
to gather together in a single place or bring people or things together for a particular purpose
gather together; collect in one place

Examples

The students assembled in the auditorium for the presentation.
A crowd quickly assembled at the scene of the accident.
She assembled the participants in the conference room for the briefing.

Synset Examples

We assembled in the church basement
Let's gather in the dining room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather - to come together; to collect in one place
  • Antonym: to disperse - to go or send in different directions; to scatter

Etymology

From Latin 'assemblare', from 'ad-' (to) + 'simulare' (to make like, together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02433771-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ əˈsɛmbəl / (AmE), / əˈsɛmbəl / (BrE)
to bring people together in a group for a particular purpose
bring people together

Examples

The manager asked everyone to assemble in the conference room for the meeting.
Volunteers began to assemble outside the community center before the event started.
Police ordered the crowd not to assemble without a permit.

Synset Examples

assemble your colleagues
get together all those who are interested in the project
gather the close family members

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather - to bring or come together into a group or crowd
  • Antonym: to disperse - to go or cause to go in different directions or to separate

Etymology

From Old French assembler, from Latin assemblare (to gather together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02604140-v
Synset POS: v
