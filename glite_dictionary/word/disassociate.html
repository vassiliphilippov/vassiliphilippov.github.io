to disassociate

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪsəˈsoʊsi.eɪt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈsəʊsieɪt / (BrE)
to break or end an association or connection with a person, group, or idea; to separate oneself from something
stop being associated with someone or something

Examples

After the scandal, she decided to disassociate herself from the political party.
The company made efforts to disassociate its brand from the controversial campaign.
He tried to disassociate his personal opinions from his professional role.

Synset Examples

She disassociated herself from the organization when she found out the identity of the president

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dissociate - to separate oneself from an association or relationship
  • Antonym: associate - to connect or link in one's mind or in reality

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'dis-' (reversal or removal) to 'associate', from Latin 'associatus'.
