Indiana

1. PROPN

/ ˌɪndiˈænə / (AmE), / ˌɪndiˈɑːnə / (BrE)
A state in the midwestern region of the United States, with its capital at Indianapolis.
U.S. state in the Midwest
american american

Examples

She moved to Indiana to attend university in Indianapolis.
The cornfields of Indiana are famous for their productivity.
Each autumn, Indiana hosts a popular state fair.

Etymology

Named after indigenous people; from 'land of the Indians'.
