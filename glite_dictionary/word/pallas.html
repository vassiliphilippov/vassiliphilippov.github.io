Pallas

1. PROPN

/ ˈpæləs / (AmE), / ˈpaləs / (BrE)
the second asteroid to be discovered, one of the largest asteroids in the asteroid belt, officially known as 2 Pallas
a large asteroid (2 Pallas)

Examples

Astronomers first observed Pallas in 1802, following the discovery of Ceres.
Studying the orbit of Pallas has provided new insights into the evolution of the asteroid belt.
Unlike most other asteroids, Pallas has a highly inclined orbital path.

Etymology

Named after the Greek goddess Pallas Athena.

2. PROPN

/ ˈpæl.əs / (AmE), / ˈpæl.əs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the goddess of wisdom, the arts, and warfare, often identified with Athena and regarded as the protector of Athens.
Greek goddess of wisdom and war

Examples

In ancient art, Pallas is often depicted with a shield and an owl.
The citizens of Athens built a grand temple to honor Pallas on the Acropolis.
Legends say that Pallas inspired heroes with wisdom and strategy in times of war.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Athena - Greek goddess of wisdom, war, and the arts, sometimes called Pallas Athena
  • Synonym: Minerva - Roman goddess of wisdom and war, counterpart to Athena

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Παλλάς (Pallás), an epithet of Athena.
