Potentilla

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈtɛn.tɪ.lə / (AmE), / pəʊˈtɛn.tɪ.lə / (BrE)
a genus of chiefly perennial herbs and shrubs in the rose family, commonly known as cinquefoils, native to the Northern Hemisphere
genus of perennial herbs/shrubs (cinquefoil)

Examples

The vibrant yellow flowers of Potentilla are a highlight in many summer gardens.
Botanists studied several species of Potentilla found in the Alpine meadows.
Many gardeners choose Potentilla for its hardiness and long flowering period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinquefoil - any plant of the genus Potentilla; a plant with five-lobed leaves

Etymology

From Latin *potens* meaning 'powerful', referring to alleged medicinal properties.

2. NOUN

/ poʊˈtɛntɪlə / (AmE), / pəʊˈtɛntɪlə / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly perennial herbs and shrubs known as cinquefoils, found mostly in the Northern Hemisphere, with compound leaves and five-petaled flowers.
genus of cinquefoil plants

Examples

Several species of Potentilla are cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens.
The Potentilla flowers added a splash of yellow to the rocky hillside.
Botanists often study Potentilla to understand plant adaptation in different climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinquefoil - any plant of the genus Potentilla

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *potentilla* (diminutive of Latin *potens*, powerful), referring to supposed medicinal properties.
