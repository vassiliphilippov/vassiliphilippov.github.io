tumbler

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌmblɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʌmblə / (BrE)
a breed of pigeon known for performing backward somersaults in flight or on the ground
a pigeon that flips backward in flight

Examples

The breeder specialized in raising tumblers for their impressive aerial tricks.
At the show, several tumblers delighted the audience with their acrobatic flips.
He watched as a tumbler performed a series of backward somersaults above the coop.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʌm.blɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʌm.blə / (BrE)
a gymnast who specializes in performing acrobatic movements such as rolls, somersaults, and twists
gymnast specializing in acrobatics

Examples

She trained for years to become a world-class tumbler and compete in international championships.
The team needed a skilled tumbler to perform daring flips during the halftime show.
As a child, he dreamed of joining the circus as a professional tumbler.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gymnast - person trained in gymnastics

Etymology

From Middle English *tomblen* (to dance, leap about).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtʌm.blɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʌm.blə / (BrE)
a drinking glass with a flat bottom and no handle or stem, typically used for water, juice, or spirits; originally designed with a rounded bottom
a flat-bottomed drinking glass

Examples

He poured himself a drink into a tumbler and sat by the window.
She accidentally knocked over the tumbler, spilling water across the table.
Please hand me that tumbler from the shelf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glass - a container for drinking, typically made of glass

Etymology

From Middle English *tomblen* (to tumble), as the original round-bottomed glasses would tip over easily.

4. NOUN

/ ˈtʌmblɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʌmblə / (BrE)
a movable part in a lock mechanism that must be correctly positioned, typically by a key, before the lock can be opened
movable locking mechanism part

Examples

The locksmith explained how the tumbler works inside a standard door lock.
If dirt jams a tumbler, the key may no longer turn the lock.
Modern security locks use several types of tumbler mechanisms to prevent unauthorized access.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pin - a slender part within a lock mechanism, often part of a pin tumbler lock
