decumbent

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈkʌmbənt / (AmE), / dɪˈkʌmbənt / (BrE)
lying down or reclining, especially in a position of comfort or rest; also used in botany to describe stems that lie along the ground but turn upward at the tip
lying down or reclining

Examples

After the long hike, he lay decumbent on the soft grass, enjoying a moment of rest.
Several decumbent stems grew along the ground before rising at their tips.
The patient remained decumbent in bed as instructed by the doctor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reclining - leaning or lying back in a relaxed position
  • Antonym: erect - upright in position or posture

Etymology

From Latin 'decumbere' (to lie down), from 'de-' (down) + 'cumbere' (to lie).
