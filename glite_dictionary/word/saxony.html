Saxony

1. PROPN

/ ˈsæk.səni / (AmE), / ˈsæks.əni / (BrE)
a historical region in central and eastern Germany, originally inhabited by the Saxons, and now a federal state known as Saxony (Sachsen in German) around the upper Elbe river.
historical region/state in Germany

Examples

She traveled across Saxony to explore its medieval castles and towns.
The fabric originally came from Saxony, famous for its high-quality wool.
Dresden, a major city in Saxony, is renowned for its cultural heritage.

Etymology

From Old French 'Saxon', from Late Latin 'Saxones', meaning 'the Saxons', a Germanic tribe.
