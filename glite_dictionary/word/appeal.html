appeal

1. VERB

/ əˈpil / (AmE), / əˈpiːl / (BrE)
to make a serious or urgent request for help, support, or sympathy
request earnestly for help or support

Examples

She tried to appeal to the manager's sense of fairness.
The charity will appeal to the public for more donations next month.
He appealed to his friends for support during the crisis.

Synset Examples

appeal to somebody for help
Invoke God in times of trouble

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to plead - to make an emotional or earnest appeal
  • Synonym: to beg - to ask for something earnestly or desperately
  • Antonym: demand - to ask for something forcefully or authoritatively

Etymology

From Latin appellare (to address or call upon).

8. NOUN

/ əˈpil / (AmE), / əˈpiːl / (BrE)
(law) A request made to a higher court to review and change the decision of a lower court.
legal request for judgment review
legal

Examples

The lawyer filed an appeal to overturn the verdict after new evidence emerged.
Their appeal was denied by the state supreme court, leaving the original decision in place.
After losing the case, they decided to pursue an appeal hoping for a different outcome.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petition - a formal written request, especially to an authority
  • Degree Lower: trial - a formal examination of evidence by a court
  • Degree Higher: retrial - a new trial ordered by an appellate court

Etymology

From Old French *apel* (call, summons), from Latin *appellare* (to call upon, appeal to).
