monetarism

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌnɪtəˌrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmʌnɪtərɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the theory or policy that variations in unemployment and inflation are generally caused by changes in the money supply
theory linking money supply to economy

Examples

Many economists debate the practical effects of monetarism on controlling inflation.
In the 1980s, monetarism influenced several governments' economic policies.
Critics argue that monetarism oversimplifies the complexities of modern economies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Keynesianism - an economic theory emphasizing government intervention to manage the economy

Etymology

From 'monetary' + '-ism', coined in the 1960s to describe a distinct economic doctrine.
