to pub-crawl

1. VERB

/ pʌb krɔl / (AmE), / pʌb krɑːl / (BrE)
to go from one pub or bar to another, typically drinking alcohol at each, often as a social activity
visit multiple pubs or bars, drinking at each
informal british

Examples

We decided to pub-crawl across the city center for my birthday.
They spent the night pub-crawling with a large group of friends.
Last weekend, she pub-crawled around Manchester and discovered several new bars.

Synset Examples

he pub-crawled around Birmingham

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bar-hop - to visit several bars in succession, drinking at each
