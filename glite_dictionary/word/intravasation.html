intravasation

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌtrævəˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌtrævəˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which foreign material, such as fluid or cells, enters a blood vessel
entry of material into a blood vessel
medical

Examples

The spread of tumor cells by intravasation is a critical step in the process of metastasis.
Researchers studied the factors that facilitate intravasation during cancer progression.
During the procedure, accidental intravasation of contrast dye was carefully monitored.

Etymology

From Latin 'intra-' (within) + 'vas' (vessel) + '-ation' (action, process).
