defunct

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈfʌŋkt / (AmE), / dɪˈfʌŋkt / (BrE)
no longer existing, operating, or functioning; dead or extinct
no longer existing or operating

Examples

The company became defunct after its final product launch failed.
Many laws from the 19th century are now considered defunct.
She researched a defunct language that was once spoken in the region.

Synset Examples

the will of a defunct aunt
a defunct Indian tribe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extinct - no longer in existence (especially of a species or group)
  • Synonym: obsolete - no longer in use, outdated
  • Antonym: existent - currently existing or living

Etymology

From Latin 'defunctus', past participle of 'defungi' meaning 'to finish or die'.

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈfʌŋkt / (AmE), / dɪˈfʌŋkt / (BrE)
no longer existing, operating, or in use; inactive or obsolete
no longer existing or working

Examples

The company became defunct after failing to adapt to new technologies.
Many old websites have now become defunct and are no longer accessible online.
The law is defunct and no longer enforced by the authorities.

Synset Examples

a defunct law
a defunct organization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obsolete - no longer produced or used; out of date
  • Antonym: active - currently in use or in operation

Etymology

From Latin 'defunctus', past participle of 'defungi' (to finish, die).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00837587-s
Synset POS: s
