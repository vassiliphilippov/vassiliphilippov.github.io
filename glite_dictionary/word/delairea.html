Delairea

1. NOUN

/ dəˈlɛə.ri.ə / (AmE), / dɪˈliːə.rɪə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, known for the species German ivy (Delairea odorata).
genus of plants (German ivy)

Examples

Delairea is native to southern Africa but has become invasive in many countries.
Botanists have reclassified German ivy as the only species in the genus Delairea.
Gardeners often need to control the rapid spread of Delairea in warmer climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: German ivy - A species of climbing plant in the daisy family, Delairea odorata

2. NOUN

/ dəˈlɛr.i.ə / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪə.ri.ə / (BrE)
A small genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae, with one species known as German ivy.
a plant genus; includes German ivy

Examples

The genus Delairea contains only a single species, commonly known as German ivy.
Many gardeners struggle to control invasive growth of Delairea in their yards.
Botanists recently revised the classification of Delairea after molecular studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: German ivy - a trailing evergreen plant, Delairea odorata, native to southern Africa

Etymology

Named after the French botanist François Delaire.
