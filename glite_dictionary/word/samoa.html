Samoa

1. PROPN

/ səˈmoʊ.ə / (AmE), / səˈməʊ.ə / (BrE)
An island country in the South Pacific Ocean, consisting of the western part of the Samoan Islands, and officially known as the Independent State of Samoa.
island country in the South Pacific

Examples

Tourists are drawn to the natural beauty and culture of Samoa.
The government of Samoa announced new environmental policies this year.
Traditional ceremonies play a significant role in the daily life of Samoa.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: American Samoa - an unincorporated territory of the United States on the eastern part of the Samoan Islands

Etymology

From the native Samoan name for the islands, "Sāmoa."

2. PROPN

/ səˈmoʊ.ə / (AmE), / səˈməʊ.ə / (BrE)
a group of volcanic islands in the South Pacific, known for its tropical climate, scenic landscapes, and Polynesian culture; includes the independent nation of Samoa and American Samoa.
South Pacific island group

Examples

Samoa attracts thousands of visitors each year with its unspoiled beaches and rich traditions.
After spending three weeks in Samoa, she became fascinated by Polynesian dance.
The rugby team from Samoa is famous for its strength and athleticism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American Samoa - a US territory in the Samoan islands
  • Synonym: Western Samoa - former name for the independent country of Samoa

Etymology

From Samoan 'Sāmoa', the native name for the islands.
