Lebanese

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛbəˈniz / (AmE), / ˌlɛbəˈniːz / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Lebanon
person from Lebanon

Examples

Many Lebanese have settled in cities around the world.
The Lebanese celebrate their independence day on November 22 each year.
She enjoys learning about Lebanese culture and traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lebanon - a Middle Eastern country on the Mediterranean

Etymology

From Lebanon + -ese (denoting origin or nationality).

WordNet ID: oewn-09739991-n

2. ADJ

/ ˌlɛbəˈniːz / (AmE), / ˌlɛbəˈniːz / (BrE)
of or relating to Lebanon or its people, culture, or language
relating to Lebanon or its people

Examples

The Lebanese cuisine is famous for its rich flavors and fresh ingredients.
Her family has Lebanese roots and often visits relatives in Beirut.
They attended a festival celebrating Lebanese music and dance.

Synset Examples

Lebanese mountains

Etymology

From Lebanon + -ese (denoting origin or nationality, from Italian -ese).
