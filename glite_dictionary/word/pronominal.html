pronominal

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈnɑːmɪnəl / (AmE), / prəˈnɒmɪnəl / (BrE)
A phrase or word that functions as a pronoun in a sentence.
phrase or word with pronoun function

Examples

The phrase 'each other' is a pronominal used to refer to two people mutually.
In linguistics, a pronominal often stands in for a noun or noun phrase.
Some languages have special pronominals for formal or informal address.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pronoun - a word used in place of a noun

Etymology

From Latin pronominalis, from pronomen (pronoun).
