Pascal

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæskəl / (AmE), / ˈpæskəl / (BrE)
A high-level programming language developed for teaching structured and modular programming techniques.
high-level programming language

Examples

Many universities used Pascal to introduce students to structured programming concepts.
Although less popular today, Pascal remains a significant milestone in the history of computer languages.
The software was originally developed in Pascal, requiring translation for modern platforms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ada - a high-level programming language developed in the late 1970s for the U.S. Department of Defense
  • Synonym: FORTRAN - one of the earliest high-level programming languages, designed for scientific computation

Etymology

Named after Blaise Pascal (1623–1662), French mathematician and philosopher.
