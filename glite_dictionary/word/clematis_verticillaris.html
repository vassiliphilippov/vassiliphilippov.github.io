Clematis verticillaris

1. NOUN

/ kləˈmeɪtɪs vərˌtɪsɪˈlɛrɪs / (AmE), / klɪˈmætɪs vɜːˌtɪsɪˈleərɪs / (BrE)
A climbing plant native to northeastern North America, known for its waxy, purplish-blue flowers.
a North American climbing plant with blue flowers

Examples

The Clematis verticillaris in her garden bloomed with striking purplish-blue flowers in late spring.
Gardeners often choose Clematis verticillaris for its ability to climb trellises and stone walls.
Unlike some other clematis species, Clematis verticillaris prefers cooler climates and well-drained soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clematis - a genus of climbing plants with showy flowers

Etymology

From genus name Clematis (from Greek 'klema', vine branch) and species epithet 'verticillaris', from Latin 'verticillus' (whorl), referring to the arrangement of plant parts.
