Actinomyxidia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæktɪnoʊmaɪkˈsɪdiə / (AmE), / ˌæktɪnəʊmɪkˈsɪdɪə / (BrE)
an order of parasitic single-celled organisms that infect worms, characterized by spores with three-valved shells and three polar capsules
an order of parasitic single-celled organisms

Examples

The scientist discovered a new species within the order Actinomyxidia during her research on parasitic worms.
Spores of Actinomyxidia are noted for their unique three-valve shells and polar capsules.
The life cycle of Actinomyxidia involves infecting aquatic worms as intermediate hosts.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'actino-' (ray) and Greek 'myxa' (mucus), referencing the spore structure.
