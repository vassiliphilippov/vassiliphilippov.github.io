dowered

1. ADJ

/ ˈdaʊ.ɚd / (AmE), / ˈdaʊ.əd / (BrE)
provided with a dower or dowry; endowed with property or wealth on marriage.
provided with a dower or dowry

Examples

The dowered bride entered the great hall with quiet dignity.
She was richly dowered and much sought after by noble families.
In the novel, the dowered heiress becomes a central figure in the unfolding drama.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dowry - property or money brought by a bride to her husband on marriage

Etymology

From 'dower' (Middle English from Old French douaire, from Latin dotarium, from dos ‘dowry’) with the suffix -ed.
