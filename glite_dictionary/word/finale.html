finale

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnæli / (AmE), / fɪˈnɑːli / (BrE)
the last and most important part of a performance, event, or piece of music
concluding part of a performance or event

Examples

The orchestra's spectacular finale brought the audience to its feet in applause.
The fireworks display ended with a dazzling finale that lit up the night sky.
Everyone was eagerly awaiting the finale of the popular television series.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: climax - the most intense point in the development of something
  • Antonym: overture - an introduction to something, especially a piece of music

Etymology

From Italian 'finale' (final), from Latin 'finalis'.

2. NOUN

/ fəˈnæli / (AmE), / fɪˈnɑːleɪ / (BrE)
the last part of a piece of music, a performance, or an event, especially when this is very exciting or emotional
the closing section of a musical composition

Examples

The finale of the symphony received a standing ovation from the audience.
Everyone held their breath as the orchestra moved into the finale of the performance.
The concert ended with a dramatic finale that left the crowd cheering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coda - the concluding passage of a piece of music

Etymology

From Italian 'finale', meaning 'final' or 'end', from Latin 'finalis'.
