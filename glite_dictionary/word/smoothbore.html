smoothbore

1. ADJ

/ ˈsmuðˌbɔr / (AmE), / ˈsmuːðˌbɔː / (BrE)
(of a firearm or artillery) having a barrel without rifling or internal spiral grooves.
firearm without rifled barrel

Examples

The old cannon was a smoothbore, making it less accurate over long distances.
Unlike modern rifles, shotguns are typically smoothbore firearms.
Collectors are often interested in antique smoothbore muskets from the 18th century.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rifled - (of a firearm) having spiral grooves inside the barrel

2. NOUN

/ ˈsmuðˌbɔr / (AmE), / ˈsmuːðˌbɔː / (BrE)
a firearm with a barrel that has no rifling, resulting in a smooth interior surface
firearm without rifling

Examples

The museum displays a 19th-century smoothbore used in early battles.
Unlike a rifle, a smoothbore fires projectiles without spin, reducing accuracy.
He collected antique firearms, including both rifled and smoothbore muskets.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rifle - firearm with a barrel that has spiral grooves (rifling)

Etymology

From 'smooth' + 'bore', referring to the smooth interior surface of the barrel.
