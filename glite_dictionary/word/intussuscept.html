to intussuscept

1. VERB

/ ˌɪn.tə.səˈsɛpt / (AmE), / ˌɪn.tə.səˈsɛpt / (BrE)
(of a part of a tubular organ, especially the intestine) to be drawn in so as to form an invagination within itself or into another section.
be drawn in, as an intestine
medical

Examples

The surgeon observed that the bowel had intussuscepted during the procedure.
If a segment of the intestine intussuscepts, it can cause an obstruction.
After surgery, the doctor monitored the patient to ensure the gut did not intussuscept again.

Synset Examples

the intussuscepted gut

Etymology

From Latin 'intussuscipere', from 'intus' (within) + 'suscipere' (to take up).
