guttural

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡʌt̬.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / ˈɡʌt.ər.əl / (BrE)
(of a sound) produced in the throat; relating to or articulated in the throat.
throat-produced sound

Examples

The singer's voice had a distinctive guttural quality that captivated the audience.
Certain languages feature guttural sounds that are unfamiliar to English speakers.
She spoke in a low, guttural tone that carried across the room.

Synset Examples

the glottal stop and uvular ‘r’ and ‘ch’ in German ‘Bach’ are guttural sounds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throaty - having a deep, harsh sound as if produced in the throat

Etymology

From Medieval Latin gutturalis, from Latin guttur (throat).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌt̬ɚəl / (AmE), / ˈɡʌtərəl / (BrE)
A consonant sound produced in the back of the mouth or throat.
consonant produced in the throat

Examples

The Arabic alphabet contains several gutturals that are unfamiliar to English speakers.
In linguistic studies, gutturals are often discussed when comparing language sound systems.
She struggled to pronounce the gutturals found in Hebrew words.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: velar - a consonant produced with the back of the tongue against the soft palate
  • Synonym: pharyngeal - a consonant articulated with the root of the tongue against the pharynx

Etymology

From Medieval Latin gutturalis, from Latin guttur (throat).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07134884-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈɡʌt̬.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / ˈɡʌt.ər.əl / (BrE)
(of a sound) harsh and produced in the throat, resembling the croak of a frog or the call of a crow.
produced in the throat; harsh, throaty sound

Examples

The actor's guttural shout echoed through the empty hall.
Her laughter had a distinctly guttural quality, deep and raspy.
As night fell, we heard the guttural calls of frogs drifting from the pond.

Synset Examples

a guttural voice
acres of guttural frogs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throaty - (of a voice or sound) produced in or involving the throat
  • Synonym: harsh - (of a sound) unpleasantly rough or jarring

Etymology

From Latin guttur (throat) + -al.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00300179-s
Synset POS: s
