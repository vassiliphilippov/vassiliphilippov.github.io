accommodator

1. NOUN

/ əˈkɑməˌdeɪtər / (AmE), / əˈkɒməˌdeɪtə / (BrE)
a person who performs a service or does a favor for someone else
person who does a favor or service

Examples

The company valued Sarah as an accommodator who always went out of her way to help colleagues.
In every community, there is at least one accommodator who volunteers whenever needed.
As an accommodator, he was known for his willingness to assist strangers without expecting anything in return.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helper - a person who helps someone
  • Synonym: benefactor - a person who gives help or a favor, especially financially
  • Antonym: recipient - a person who receives something

Etymology

From accommodate + -or (agent noun suffix).
