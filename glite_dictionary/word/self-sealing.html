self-sealing

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlfˈsiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈsiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
(of an object or material) able to seal itself automatically, without the need for water or external moisture.
able to seal itself automatically

Examples

These self-sealing envelopes make mailing quicker and easier.
The self-sealing bags kept the snacks fresh during the long hike.
Our lab uses self-sealing containers to prevent chemical leaks.

Synset Examples

self-sealing envelopes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-adhesive - able to stick to surfaces by itself, without additional glue

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlfˈsiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈsiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
able to seal itself automatically, especially after being pierced or opened
able to seal itself after being pierced

Examples

This self-sealing envelope saves time by eliminating the need for glue or tape.
Modern self-sealing fuel tanks greatly reduce the risk of leaks after damage.
If you drive often on rough roads, consider installing self-sealing tires on your car.

Synset Examples

self-sealing tires

Etymology

Formed from 'self' (oneself, automatic) and 'seal' (to close tightly).
