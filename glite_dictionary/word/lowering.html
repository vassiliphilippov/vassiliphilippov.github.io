lowering

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of causing something to become less in amount, value, or degree
the act of making something less

Examples

There was a noticeable lowering of prices throughout the summer.
The doctor advised the lowering of salt in her diet.
Environmental concerns have led to the lowering of emissions limits in many countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reduction - the process or result of reducing
  • Antonym: increase - the act of becoming greater or making something greater

Etymology

From the verb 'lower' + the suffix '-ing'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlaʊ.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlaʊ.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
(of the sky or weather) darkened by clouds, threatening, or gloomy.
darkened or threatening, especially of the sky

Examples

The lowering sky threatened to break into a storm at any moment.
A lowering afternoon cast a shadow over the deserted park.
The hikers hurried home as the lowering clouds gathered above.

Synset Examples

a heavy sky

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloomy - dark or poorly lit, especially so as to appear depressing or frightening
  • Antonym: bright - giving out or reflecting much light; shining

Etymology

Present participle of 'lower' (verb: to look sullen), probably related to Middle English 'louren'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00464442-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of moving something to a lower position or level
the act of making something lower

Examples

The lowering of the flag marked the end of the ceremony.
He supervised the lowering of the heavy machinery into the basement.
The lowering of prices made the products more affordable for everyone.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: raising - the act of moving something to a higher position or level

Etymology

From the verb 'lower', derived from Middle English 'louren'.

Source: WordNet
