inequality

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪniˈkwɑləti / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪˈkwɒlɪti / (BrE)
The state of being unequal, especially in rights, opportunities, or social position.
state of being unequal

Examples

Many activists fight against income inequality in society.
Educational inequality remains a persistent challenge in many countries.
The report highlights inequality in healthcare access among different communities.

Synset Examples

the growing inequality between rich and poor

Related Terms

  • Antonym: equality - the state of being equal, especially in status, rights, or opportunities
  • Synonym: disparity - a great difference or inequality

Etymology

From Latin inaequalitas, from in- (not) + aequalitas (equality).
