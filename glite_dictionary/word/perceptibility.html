perceptibility

1. NOUN

/ pərˌsɛptəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / pəˌsɛptəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being able to be perceived by the mind or the senses.
the quality of being perceptible

Examples

The perceptibility of the warning signs allowed the hikers to avoid danger.
Certain colors have greater perceptibility to the human eye under low light conditions.
She questioned the perceptibility of subtle flavors in the dish.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: imperceptibility - the quality of being impossible or difficult to perceive
  • Synonym: perceptible - able to be perceived by the senses or the mind

Etymology

From Latin percipere (to perceive) + -ibility.
