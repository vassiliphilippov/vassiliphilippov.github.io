acquaintance

1. NOUN

/ əˈkweɪntəns / (AmE), / əˈkweɪntəns / (BrE)
Knowledge or awareness of someone or something, gained through experience but less intimate than familiarity.
personal knowledge of someone or something

Examples

She had only a passing acquaintance with classical music.
His acquaintance with the subject made the conversation easier.
My acquaintance with her is purely professional.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: familiarity - close knowledge or understanding of something
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness

Etymology

From Old French acointance (familiarity, friendship), from acointier (to make known), ultimately from Latin accognoscere (to know).

2. NOUN

/ əˈkweɪntəns / (AmE), / əˈkweɪntəns / (BrE)
a person one knows slightly, but who is not a close friend
person one knows slightly

Examples

She ran into an old acquaintance from university at the conference.
I wouldn't call him a friend; he's more of a casual acquaintance.
My network of acquaintances has grown since I moved to the city.

Synset Examples

I have trouble remembering the names of all my acquaintances
we are friends of the family

Related Terms

  • Antonym: friend - a person with whom one has a bond of mutual affection

Etymology

From Old French 'acointer' (to make known) and Medieval Latin 'accognitare', from Latin 'cognoscere' (to know).

3. NOUN

/ əˈkweɪntəns / (AmE), / əˈkweɪntəns / (BrE)
a relationship with someone you know, but who is not a close friend
a person known but not a close friend

Examples

Although I see Sarah at work, our relationship is mostly just an acquaintance rather than a friendship.
He has many acquaintances in the city, but very few close friends.
Our dinner was pleasant, but I consider him only an acquaintance, not someone I truly trust.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: friendship - a close and trusting relationship between people
  • Degree Higher: friend - a person with whom one has a close personal relationship

Etymology

From Old French 'acointance', from 'acointier' (to make known).
