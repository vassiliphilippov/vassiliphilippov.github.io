rambotan

1. NOUN

/ ˌræmˈboʊtɑn / (AmE), / ˌræmˈbəʊtan / (BrE)
A tropical tree of Southeast Asia (Nephelium lappaceum) that bears spiny red fruit with a sweet, translucent flesh, also called rambutan.
a tropical tree bearing spiny red fruit

Examples

I tried a rambotan for the first time while visiting Malaysia.
The market had a fresh shipment of rambotan alongside other tropical fruits.
Farmers in Southeast Asia cultivate rambotan for both local consumption and export.

Etymology

From Malay 'rambutan', diminutive of 'rambut' (hair), referring to the fruit's spiny appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˈræm.boʊ.tɑn / (AmE), / ˈræm.bə.tæn / (BrE)
A bright red, oval fruit from Southeast Asia, covered with soft spines and having a pleasantly acid taste.
tropical Southeast Asian fruit with soft spines

Examples

We bought a kilo of rambotan at the street market in Kuala Lumpur.
The children were curious about the spiky shell of the rambotan before tasting its sweet-tart flesh.
At the restaurant, the dessert included fresh rambotan served with coconut cream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lychee - a small, round sweet fruit with rough skin and a large seed

Etymology

From Malay 'rambutan', from 'rambut' meaning 'hair', referring to the fruit's hairy appearance.
