Cistothorus palustris

1. NOUN

/ sɪsˌtɒθərəs pəˈlʌstrɪs / (AmE), / sɪsˌtɒθərəs pəˈlʌstrɪs / (BrE)
A species of North American wren that lives in tall reed beds and marshes; commonly known as the marsh wren.
marsh-dwelling North American wren

Examples

The Cistothorus palustris is often heard singing amid the cattails of North American wetlands.
Scientists observed the nesting behavior of Cistothorus palustris in restored marsh habitats.
Birdwatchers frequently seek out Cistothorus palustris for its distinctive bubbling song.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marsh wren - common name for Cistothorus palustris

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cistothorus' (genus name), possibly from Greek 'kisto' (a box) + 'thouros' (leaping), and 'palustris' meaning 'of the marsh'.
