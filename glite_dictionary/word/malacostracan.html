malacostracan

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæl.əˈkɑ.strə.kən / (AmE), / ˌmal.əˈkɒs.trə.kən / (BrE)
a crustacean belonging to the largest subclass of the phylum Arthropoda, including crabs, lobsters, shrimp, and others, typically characterized by a segmented body with 19 to 20 body segments.
member of a large crustacean subclass
medical

Examples

The lobster is a type of malacostracan found in many ocean habitats around the world.
Scientists discovered a new species of malacostracan in the deep sea.
Most large shrimp belong to the malacostracan subclass of crustaceans.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: crustacean - an aquatic arthropod such as a crab, lobster, or shrimp

Etymology

From New Latin Malacostraca, from Greek malakos (soft) + ostrakon (shell).
