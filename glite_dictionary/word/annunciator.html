annunciator

1. NOUN

/ əˈnʌn.siˌeɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / əˈnʌn.si.eɪ.tə / (BrE)
a device that indicates and announces which electrical circuit has been activated, often used in control panels or switchboards
device showing active electrical circuits

Examples

The technician glanced at the annunciator to see which system had triggered the alarm.
When the annunciator lit up, the operator knew exactly which circuit needed attention.
A modern fire panel is equipped with an electronic annunciator for easy monitoring.

Etymology

From Latin 'annuntiare', meaning 'to announce'.
