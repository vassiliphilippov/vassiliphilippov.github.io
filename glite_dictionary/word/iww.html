I.W.W.

1. PROPN

/ ˌaɪ ˌdʌbəlju ˈdʌbəlju / (AmE), / ˌaɪ ˌdʌbəljuː ˈdʌbəljuː / (BrE)
a former international labor union and radical labor movement in the United States, founded in 1905 and aimed at overthrowing capitalism; also known as the Industrial Workers of the World
former radical U.S. labor union (Industrial Workers of the World)

Examples

The I.W.W. played a significant role in organizing American industrial workers in the early 20th century.
Members of the I.W.W. advocated for direct action and the rights of all laborers, regardless of skill.
The decline of the I.W.W. began after World War I due to suppression and internal divisions.

Etymology

Abbreviation of Industrial Workers of the World.
