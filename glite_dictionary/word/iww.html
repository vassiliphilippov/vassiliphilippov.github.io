IWW

1. PROPN

/ ˌaɪ ˌdʌbəlˈju ˈdʌbəlˈju / (AmE), / ˌaɪ ˌdʌbəlˈjuː ˈdʌbəlˈjuː / (BrE)
a former international labor union and radical labor movement in the United States, founded in 1905 and known as the Industrial Workers of the World
former radical US labor union (Industrial Workers of the World)

Examples

The IWW played a major role in organizing workers in the early 20th century.
Members of the IWW were often called "Wobblies."
After World War I, the influence of the IWW significantly declined.

Etymology

Acronym formed from Industrial Workers of the World.
