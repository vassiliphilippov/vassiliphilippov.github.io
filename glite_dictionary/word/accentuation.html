accentuation

1. NOUN

/ ˌæk.sən.tʃuˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæk.sɛn.tjuːˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of emphasizing certain syllables, words, or accents in speech or writing
emphasis of syllables or accents

Examples

The accentuation of the second syllable distinguishes the meaning of the word.
Her accentuation in French sounded different from native speakers.
Proper accentuation is crucial when learning to pronounce new words.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stress - emphasis given to a syllable or word in speech

Etymology

From Latin accentuatio (accenting), from accentuare (to accent).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæk.sɛn.tʃuˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæk.sɛn.tjuˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of giving special importance or emphasis to something
the act of emphasizing something

Examples

There was a noticeable accentuation of the positive aspects of the project in the report.
Careful accentuation of key words helps the speaker convey their message clearly.
The accentuation of her eyes with makeup made them stand out in the photograph.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emphasis - special importance or prominence given to something
  • Antonym: understatement - the presentation of something as being smaller or less important than it actually is

Etymology

From Latin accentuatio, from accentuare (to accentuate), from accentus (accent).
