bugged

1. ADJ

/ bʌɡd / (AmE), / bʌɡd / (BrE)
(of a place or device) containing hidden electronic eavesdropping devices used to secretly listen to conversations.
containing hidden listening devices

Examples

The embassy office was thoroughly checked for bugged telephones and furniture.
She suspected their hotel room might be bugged after the strange noises on the line.
Journalists found that their phones had been bugged during the investigation.

Synset Examples

wired hotel rooms
even the car is bugged

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wiretapped - illicitly monitored by means of a wiretap

Etymology

From 'bug' (to secretly listen in), from the sense of 'bug' meaning a hidden listening device, first used in espionage slang in the 20th century.
