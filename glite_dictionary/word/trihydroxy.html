trihydroxy

1. ADJ

/ traɪˈhaɪdrɒksi / (AmE), / traɪˈhɪdrɒksi / (BrE)
(of a chemical compound) containing three hydroxyl (–OH) groups.
having three hydroxyl groups (chemistry)
medical

Examples

The scientist analyzed a trihydroxy derivative of the compound for its reactivity.
Many naturally occurring flavonoids are trihydroxy compounds.
A trihydroxy molecule can act as a strong antioxidant due to its multiple -OH groups.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dihydroxy - containing two hydroxyl groups
  • Degree Higher: tetrahydroxy - containing four hydroxyl groups

Etymology

From 'tri-' (Greek for 'three') + 'hydroxy', referring to the –OH group.
