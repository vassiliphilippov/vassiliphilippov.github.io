soldiership

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊl.dʒɚˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.dɪə.ʃɪp / (BrE)
the skills, qualities, or conduct required to be an effective soldier
skills and qualities of a soldier

Examples

Her soldiership was evident in her disciplined approach to every mission.
The general praised the unit's soldiership during the challenging campaign.
Throughout history, soldiership has demanded courage, loyalty, and resilience from those who serve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soldiering - service or duties in the role of a soldier
  • Antonym: civilianship - the state or condition of being a civilian

Etymology

Formed from 'soldier' + '-ship,' a suffix denoting quality, state, or condition.
