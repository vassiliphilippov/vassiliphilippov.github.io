chatter

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃætə / (BrE)
a series of high-pitched, rapid, and continuous noises made by animals such as birds or monkeys
animal high-pitched rapid noise

Examples

The chatter of sparrows filled the early morning air.
Monkey chatter echoed through the rainforest canopy as the sun rose.
The constant chatter from the parakeets made it hard to concentrate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chirp - a short, sharp sound made by small birds or insects
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07393381-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃætə / (BrE)
a rapid, repeated series of noises made by parts of a machine, often when moving or operating
rapid, repetitive machine noise

Examples

The engine's chatter grew louder as the car accelerated down the road.
There was an odd chatter coming from the washing machine during the spin cycle.
Factory workers wore ear protection to guard against the constant chatter of the machines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattle - a series of short, sharp noises made by machine parts
Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ ˈtʃætər / (AmE), / ˈtʃætə / (BrE)
(of a cutting tool) to cut a surface unevenly, typically due to rapid vibration or instability in the tool.
cut unevenly due to tool vibration

Examples

If the tool is not set properly, it may chatter and leave visible marks on the metal.
The machinist adjusted the speed to prevent the lathe from chattering during the process.
Improper clamping can cause the drill to chatter as it moves through the material.

4. VERB

/ ˈtʃæt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃætə / (BrE)
(of animals, objects, or people) to make a rapid series of short, high-pitched noises, often resembling speech or communication.
make rapid, repeated noises

Examples

The birds chattered excitedly in the early morning light.
Her teeth chattered from the cold wind blowing across the lake.
Squirrels chattered angrily when the dog approached their tree.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

/ ˈtʃæt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃætə / (BrE)
continuous and sometimes meaningless talk, usually in a lively or noisy way.

Examples
