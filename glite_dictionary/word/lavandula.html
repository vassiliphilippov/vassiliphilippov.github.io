Lavandula

1. NOUN

/ ləˈvændjulə / (AmE), / ləˈvændjʊlə / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic flowering plants in the mint family, commonly known as lavender, native to the Mediterranean region.
genus of lavender plants

Examples

Lavandula angustifolia is widely cultivated for its fragrant flowers and essential oils.
Gardeners often plant Lavandula to attract bees and butterflies to their gardens.
The genus Lavandula includes several species valued for their ornamental and aromatic properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lavender - common name for plants of the genus Lavandula

Etymology

From Latin 'lavandula', meaning lavender; ultimately from 'lavare' (to wash), referring to its historical use in bathing.

2. NOUN

/ ləˈvændjələ / (AmE), / ləˈvændjʊlə / (BrE)
a genus of aromatic shrubs and herbs in the mint family, including lavender, widely cultivated for their fragrant flowers and oils
genus of aromatic plants, including lavender

Examples

The Lavandula genus is renowned for its fragrant purple flowers and essential oil.
Many gardeners prefer to plant various species of Lavandula in their herb gardens.
Research has shown that extracts from Lavandula have calming properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lavender - a small aromatic evergreen shrub of the mint family, with narrow leaves and bluish-purple flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'lavandula', denoting the lavender plant.
