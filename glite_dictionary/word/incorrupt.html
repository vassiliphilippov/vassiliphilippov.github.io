incorrupt

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.kəˈrʌpt / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kəˈrʌpt / (BrE)
(of a body) not decayed or decomposed; remaining free from physical corruption, especially after death.
not decayed or decomposed

Examples

The saint's body was found to be incorrupt centuries after his death.
Historians marveled at the incorrupt state of the ancient mummy.
Pilgrims visited the tomb, amazed to see the incorrupt remains on display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incorruptible - not subject to decay or corruption

Etymology

From Latin incorruptus, from in- (not) + corruptus (destroyed, spoiled).

WordNet ID: oewn-01072130-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪnkəˈrʌpt / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəˈrʌpt / (BrE)
not subject to corruption or immorality; morally upright and honest.
not subject to corruption or immorality

Examples

He was known throughout his career as an incorrupt judge, respected by colleagues and citizens alike.
The company prides itself on having an incorrupt management team that values transparency.
In times of crisis, truly incorrupt leaders are rare and deeply valued.

Synset Examples

a policeman who was incorrupt and incorruptible

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incorruptible - not able to be corrupted or morally tainted
  • Antonym: corrupt - willing to act dishonestly in return for money or personal gain

Etymology

From Latin incorruptus, from in- (not) + corruptus (corrupted).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00625237-a
Synset POS: a
