achenial

1. ADJ

/ əˈkiːniəl / (AmE), / əˈkiːnɪəl / (BrE)
Relating to achenes, which are dry, one-seeded fruits that do not split open at maturity.
relating to achenes (dry, one-seeded fruits)

Examples

The sunflower's achenial fruit allows it to disperse seeds efficiently.
Scientists study achenial structures to understand plant reproduction in the daisy family.
The achenial surface is often covered with tiny hairs that aid in dispersal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: achene - a small, dry, one-seeded, indehiscent fruit

Etymology

From 'achene' + '-ial', modelled after botanical terminology.
