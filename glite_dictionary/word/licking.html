licking

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
an act of inflicting punishment or defeat on someone, especially by hitting them several times; a thrashing
a beating; a thrashing
informal

Examples

After losing the game, the team admitted they had taken a real licking on the field.
If you don't clean your room, you might get a licking from your mother.
The boxer suffered a brutal licking in the final round.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beating - an act of striking repeatedly; a physical punishment
  • Synonym: thrashing - a heavy defeat or beating
  • Antonym: victory - the act of defeating an opponent

Etymology

From the verb 'lick' in the sense of 'to beat or thrash,' attested since the 18th century in English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
A decisive or thorough defeat in a contest or struggle, especially in a sporting or competitive context.
decisive defeat; thrashing
informal

Examples

Last night, our team took a real licking from their opponents.
After a humiliating licking, the coach vowed to rebuild the squad.
The senator suffered a political licking in the primary election.

Synset Examples

it was a narrow defeat
the army's only defeat
they suffered a convincing licking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrashing - a severe or overwhelming defeat
  • Antonym: victory - success or triumph in a contest

Etymology

Originally a verbal noun from 'lick' in the sense of 'to beat' or 'strike'.
