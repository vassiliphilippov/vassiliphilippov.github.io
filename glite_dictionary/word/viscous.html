viscous

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɪskəs / (AmE), / ˈvɪskəs / (BrE)
having a thick, sticky consistency between solid and liquid; having a high resistance to flow.
having a thick, sticky consistency

Examples

The syrup became more viscous as it cooled.
Motor oil can turn viscous at low temperatures, affecting engine performance.
The lava flowed slowly, thick and viscous down the hillside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thick - having a relatively great degree of density or viscosity; not fluid
  • Antonym: fluid - a substance that flows easily; a liquid or gas (opposite of viscous)
  • Degree Lower: runny - having a low viscosity; flowing easily

Etymology

From Latin 'viscosus' (full of birdlime, sticky), from 'viscum' (birdlime).

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɪskəs / (AmE), / ˈvɪskəs / (BrE)
having a thick, sticky consistency between solid and liquid; resembling an adhesive in stickiness.
having a thick, sticky consistency

Examples

The syrup became so viscous that it was hard to pour from the bottle.
Oil can turn viscous in cold weather, affecting a car's performance.
The scientist described the lava as viscous due to its sticky and slow-flowing nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sticky - tending to stick to surfaces; adhesive
  • Antonym: fluid - able to flow easily; not viscous

Etymology

From Latin 'viscosus' (full of birdlime, sticky), from 'viscum' (birdlime).
