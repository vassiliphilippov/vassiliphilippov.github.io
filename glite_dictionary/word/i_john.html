I John

1. PROPN

/ ˈfaɪrst ˌdʒɑn / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˌdʒɒn / (BrE)
the first of three epistles in the New Testament traditionally attributed to Saint John the Apostle
first epistle of John in the New Testament

Examples

Many Christians study I John to learn about love and faith.
The sermon focused on a passage from I John, chapter 4.
Biblical scholars often discuss the themes present in I John.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: II John - the second of three epistles of John in the New Testament

Etymology

From Latin 'Epistula Ioannis Prima' (First Epistle of John).
