sunglass

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌɡlæs / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌɡlɑːs / (BrE)
A convex lens used to focus sunlight, especially to start a fire.
convex lens for focusing sunlight

Examples

He used a sunglass to concentrate the rays of the sun and ignite the dry leaves.
In the eighteenth century, travelers sometimes carried a sunglass as a fire-starting tool.
She demonstrated how the sunglass could set a piece of paper alight in seconds.

Etymology

From sun + glass, originally referring to glass used to focus sunlight.
