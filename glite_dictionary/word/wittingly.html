wittingly

1. ADV

/ ˈwɪtɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈwɪtɪŋli / (BrE)
In a way that is done with full knowledge, awareness, or intention; deliberately.
knowingly; deliberately

Examples

It was clear that he wittingly misled the investigators.
The law punishes those who wittingly violate safety regulations.
She wittingly omitted certain details from her report.

Synset Examples

he wittingly deleted the references

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knowingly - in a way that shows awareness or intention
  • Antonym: unwittingly - without being aware; unintentionally

Etymology

Formed from 'witting' (knowing, aware) + the adverbial suffix '-ly'; 'witting' from Old English *witan* (to know).
