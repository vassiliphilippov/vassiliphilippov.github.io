plunger

1. NOUN

/ ˈplʌndʒər / (AmE), / ˈplʌndʒə / (BrE)
a device consisting of a long handle with a solid, flexible end, used to clear blockages in drains or to produce a plunging or thrusting action in machinery
device with a plunging or thrusting motion

Examples

He used a plunger to unblock the kitchen sink after it became clogged.
The mechanic pressed the plunger to activate the hydraulic system.
She bought a new plunger for the bathroom in case of emergencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suction cup - a cup-shaped device that sticks to surfaces by suction, sometimes part of a plunger

Etymology

From plunge + -er, denoting one who or that which plunges.

2. NOUN

/ ˈplʌndʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈplʌndʒə / (BrE)
a person who risks significant losses in the hope of making considerable gains, especially in gambling or speculation
one who takes big risks for possible gains

Examples

He earned a reputation as a plunger on the stock market, often investing large sums on a hunch.
Only a true plunger would bet everything on a single hand of poker.
In the world of high-stakes gambling, every plunger hopes for that one big win.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: risk-taker - someone who is willing to take risks
  • Antonym: cautious - someone who avoids risk

Etymology

From 'plunge' (to jump in boldly), indicating someone who takes a leap into risk.

3. NOUN

/ ˈplʌndʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈplʌndʒə / (BrE)
A hand tool with a rubber suction cup attached to a stick, used for clearing blocked drains or pipes.
tool for clearing blocked drains

Examples

He grabbed the plunger from under the sink to fix the clogged toilet.
After a few strong pushes with the plunger, the water started to drain again.
Every household should have a plunger in case of plumbing emergencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drain cleaner - a chemical or device used to remove blockages in pipes
