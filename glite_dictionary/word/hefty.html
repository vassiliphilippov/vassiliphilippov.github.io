hefty

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɛfti / (AmE), / ˈhɛfti / (BrE)
(of an amount or size) large, significant, or substantial.
large or substantial in amount or size

Examples

He paid a hefty fine for breaking the law.
They received a hefty bonus at the end of the year.
The new textbook comes with a hefty price tag.

Synset Examples

it cost a considerable amount
a goodly amount
received a hefty bonus
a respectable sum
a tidy sum of money
a sizable fortune

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substantial - large in size, value, or importance
  • Synonym: considerable - notably large in size, amount, or extent
  • Antonym: modest - relatively small in amount, extent, or degree

2. ADJ

/ ˈhɛfti / (AmE), / ˈhɛfti / (BrE)
(of a person) large, heavy, and strong; having a big, powerful build.
large and strong in build

Examples

He was a hefty man with broad shoulders and a booming voice.
The hefty linebacker easily tackled his opponent.
Despite her hefty build, she moves with surprising grace.

Synset Examples

a hefty athlete
a muscular boxer
powerful arms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burly - strong and heavily built
  • Antonym: slight - small and delicate in build

Etymology

Originated in the United States in the late 19th century, perhaps an alteration of 'heft' meaning 'weight, heaviness'.

Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ ˈhɛfti / (AmE), / ˈhɛfti / (BrE)
large and heavy; of considerable weight and size.
large and heavy

Examples

He placed a hefty box on the table with a thud.
The library boasts a hefty collection of reference books.
She handed him a hefty stack of paperwork to review.

Synset Examples

a hefty dictionary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heavy - of great weight; difficult to lift or move
  • Antonym: light - not heavy; of little weight

Etymology

Probably from heft (weight, heaviness) + -y, dating from early 19th century American English.
