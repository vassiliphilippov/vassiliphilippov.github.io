sanctioning

1. ADJ

/ ˈsæŋkʃənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsaŋkʃənɪŋ / (BrE)
(formal) Giving, expressing, or serving as approval or authorization for something.
giving or serving as authorization

Examples

The regulations have a sanctioning effect on employee behavior within the company.
Her sanctioning role made her decisions pivotal in finalizing the policy changes.
The organization's sanctioning authority stems directly from federal law.

Synset Examples

the guardian's duties were primarily sanctionative rather than administrative

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authorizing - giving official permission or approval for something
  • Antonym: prohibiting - formally forbidding something by law, rule, or other authority

Etymology

From 'sanction', from Latin 'sanctio' (decree, law), with the present participle suffix '-ing'.
