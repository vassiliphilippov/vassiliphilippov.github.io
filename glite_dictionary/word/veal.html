veal

1. NOUN

/ vil / (AmE), / viːl / (BrE)
the pale, tender meat from a young calf, used as food
meat from a young calf

Examples

The restaurant is famous for its veal cutlets and creamy mushroom sauce.
She prefers veal to beef because of its delicate flavor and tenderness.
He ordered a plate of veal scallopini garnished with fresh herbs.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: beef - meat from a cow, especially an adult bovine
  • Synonym: lamb - meat from a young sheep

Etymology

From Old French *veel*, from Latin *vitellus* (young calf).

2. NOUN

/ vil / (AmE), / viːl / (BrE)
the meat from a young calf, especially one that is raised for food
meat from a young calf

Examples

We had veal cutlets with a delicate mushroom sauce for dinner.
The chef recommends using veal instead of beef for a more tender stew.
Some people choose not to eat veal due to animal welfare concerns.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: beef - meat from adult cattle
  • Degree Lower: lamb - meat from a young sheep

Etymology

From Old French 'veel', from Latin 'vitellus' (diminutive of 'vitulus', calf).
