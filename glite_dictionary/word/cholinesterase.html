cholinesterase

1. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊlɪˈnɛstəˌreɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɒlɪˈnɛstəˌreɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that breaks down acetylcholine into choline and acetic acid, especially in nerve tissues
enzyme that breaks down acetylcholine
medical

Examples

Low levels of cholinesterase in the blood may indicate exposure to certain pesticides.
Doctors measured cholinesterase activity to diagnose the patient's condition.
The inhibition of cholinesterase can result in the accumulation of acetylcholine at nerve endings.

Etymology

From choline + esterase (an enzyme that splits esters), indicating its action on acetylcholine.
