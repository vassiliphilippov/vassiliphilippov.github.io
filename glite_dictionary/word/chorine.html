chorine

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːriːn / (AmE), / ˈkɒriːn / (BrE)
a woman who dances in a chorus line, especially in musical theatre or revues
female chorus line dancer

Examples

She once worked as a chorine on Broadway before pursuing solo roles.
Many aspiring actresses in the 1920s began their careers as a chorine in musical revues.
The documentary offers a behind-the-scenes look at the life of a chorine during the golden age of theatre.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chorus girl - a young woman who dances and sings in a chorus line

Etymology

From 'chorus' + '-ine,' a feminine suffix, influenced by French formation
