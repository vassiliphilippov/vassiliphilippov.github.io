simoon

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈmun / (AmE), / sɪˈmuːn / (BrE)
a hot, dry, dust-laden wind that blows across the deserts of North Africa and Arabia, often of violent force
hot desert wind of North Africa/Arabia

Examples

The simoon swept across the desert, making travel impossible for days.
Villagers took shelter from the fierce simoon that carried clouds of sand into their homes.
Many legends describe the deadly power of the simoon in the Arabian deserts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sirocco - a hot, dusty wind from the Sahara affecting Italy and southern Europe

Etymology

From Arabic samūn (poison wind), from samma (to poison).
