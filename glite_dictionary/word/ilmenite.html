ilmenite

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪlməˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪlmɪnaɪt / (BrE)
a black, weakly magnetic mineral composed of iron titanium oxide, found in metamorphic and igneous rocks and used as a major source of titanium
a mineral and source of titanium

Examples

The mining company discovered a large deposit of ilmenite in the mountain range.
Ilmenite is processed to extract titanium dioxide for use in pigments.
Scientists analyzed the crystal structure of ilmenite under a microscope.

Etymology

Named after the Ilmen Mountains in Russia, where it was first identified.
