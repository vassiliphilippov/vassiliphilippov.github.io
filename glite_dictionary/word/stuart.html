Stuart

1. PROPN

/ ˈstuːərt / (AmE), / ˈstjuːət / (BrE)
the royal family that ruled Scotland from 1371 to 1714 and England from 1603 to 1649 and again from 1660 to 1714
royal family of Scotland and England (1371–1714)

Examples

The Stuart dynasty faced numerous challenges during its reign over England and Scotland.
Mary, Queen of Scots, was a prominent member of the Stuart family.
The Stuart monarchy was restored in 1660 with the return of Charles II.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Tudor - the royal dynasty that preceded the Stuarts as monarchs of England
  • Antonym: Hanoverian - the royal dynasty that succeeded the Stuarts as monarchs of Britain

Etymology

From the Old French 'estuiart', meaning steward, originally referring to the office held by the family.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstuːərt / (AmE), / ˈstjuːət / (BrE)
A member of the royal family that ruled Scotland from 1371 and England from 1603 to 1714.
member of the British royal Stuart dynasty

Examples

Mary, Queen of Scots, was one of the most famous Stuarts in history.
The Stuarts faced many political challenges during their reign.
After the Civil War, the monarchy was restored under the Stuart dynasty.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Tudor - A member of the royal family that preceded the Stuarts in England

Etymology

From the Old French 'estuiart' (steward), originally a title, later the surname of the royal family.
