adjacent

1. ADJ

/ əˈdʒeɪsənt / (AmE), / əˈdʒeɪsənt / (BrE)
next to or adjoining something else; having a common boundary or edge
next to; having a common boundary

Examples

Our office is adjacent to the main conference room.
The two countries have adjacent borders along the river.
There is a parking lot adjacent to the restaurant for customers.

Synset Examples

Rhode Island has two bordering states: Massachusetts and Connecticut
the side of Germany conterminous with France
Utah and the contiguous state of Idaho
neighboring cities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neighboring - situated next to or very near; nearby

Etymology

From Latin 'adjacens', present participle of 'adjacēre' (to lie near).

2. ADJ

/ əˈdʒeɪsənt / (AmE), / əˈdʒeɪs(ə)nt / (BrE)
next to or very near something, though not necessarily touching it
next to or very near

Examples

The bakery is adjacent to the grocery store, making shopping very convenient.
They purchased the lot adjacent to their current property for expansion.
A small park lies adjacent to the school, providing space for outdoor activities.

Synset Examples

lands adjacent to the mountains
New York and adjacent cities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neighboring - situated or living nearby
  • Antonym: remote - far away in distance

Etymology

From Latin 'adjacentem', from 'adjacere' meaning 'to lie near'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00447018-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ əˈdʒeɪsənt / (AmE), / əˈdʒeɪ.sənt / (BrE)
next to or very near something; having a common border, edge, or point
next to or adjoining

Examples

The library is adjacent to the science building on campus.
They booked two adjacent rooms for their family vacation.
A small garden sits adjacent to the restaurant, providing fresh herbs for the kitchen.

Synset Examples

had adjacent rooms
in the next room
the person sitting next to me
our rooms were side by side

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adjoining - next to or joined with something
  • Antonym: distant - far away in space or time

Etymology

From Latin 'adjacens, adjacentis', present participle of 'adjacēre' meaning 'to lie near'.
