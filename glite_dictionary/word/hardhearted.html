hardhearted

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɑrdˌhɑrtɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdˌhɑːtɪd / (BrE)
lacking compassion, pity, or sympathy; unfeeling or unsympathetic.
lacking compassion or pity

Examples

Despite her pleas, the manager remained hardhearted and refused to grant an extension.
His hardhearted decision left many employees without jobs before the holidays.
People described him as hardhearted after he ignored his friend's suffering.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compassionate - showing sympathy and concern for others
  • Synonym: callous - emotionally insensitive or unfeeling

2. ADJ

/ hɑrdˈhɑrtəd / (AmE), / hɑːdˈhɑːtɪd / (BrE)
lacking compassion or sympathy for others; unfeeling or merciless.
without compassion or sympathy

Examples

Despite her pleas, the hardhearted landlord refused to give her more time to pay the rent.
The dictator's hardhearted decisions left many citizens suffering without aid.
He was too hardhearted to even consider forgiving his old friend.

Synset Examples

an unfeeling wretch

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compassionate - showing sympathy and concern for others
  • Synonym: merciless - showing no mercy or pity
  • Synonym: callous - emotionally insensitive or unfeeling

Etymology

Formed from 'hard' + 'hearted', with 'hearted' meaning 'having a specified kind of heart'.
