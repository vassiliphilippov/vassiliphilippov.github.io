Chlorura

1. NOUN

/ kloʊˈrʊrə / (AmE), / klɔːˈrʊərə / (BrE)
A former genus of North American birds in the sparrow family, commonly known as green-tailed towhees, now usually classified in the genus Pipilo.
former bird genus (green-tailed towhee)

Examples

The green-tailed towhee was formerly classified in the genus Chlorura by ornithologists.
Some older field guides refer to certain species as belonging to Chlorura rather than Pipilo.
Researchers debated for years whether to keep the green-tailed towhee within Chlorura.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pipilo - genus containing towhees, including green-tailed towhee

Etymology

From Greek *khlōros* (green) + *oura* (tail), referencing the green-tailed characteristic of the birds.
