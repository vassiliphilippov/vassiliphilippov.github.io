Genyonemus lineatus

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛnˌjoʊˈniːməs laɪˈneɪtəs / (AmE), / dʒɛnˌjəʊˈniːməs laɪˈneɪtəs / (BrE)
A small silvery marine fish found off the coast of California, commonly known as white croaker.
species of marine fish in California

Examples

Fishermen often catch Genyonemus lineatus along the Southern California coast.
The aquarium features a tank dedicated to local species like Genyonemus lineatus.
Due to environmental concerns, consumption of Genyonemus lineatus is sometimes discouraged.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white croaker - Common name for Genyonemus lineatus

Etymology

Genyonemus from Greek 'genys' (jaw) and 'nemus' (thread); lineatus is Latin for 'lined' or 'striped'.
