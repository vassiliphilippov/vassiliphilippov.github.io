bung

1. NOUN

/ bʌŋ / (AmE), / bʌŋ / (BrE)
A stopper or plug used to seal the opening of a barrel, cask, or flask.
stopper for a barrel or flask

Examples

The winemaker removed the bung from the barrel to sample the wine.
After cleaning the flask, she replaced the bung to prevent leaks.
A wooden bung sealed the cask during the aging process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stopper - an object used to close the opening of a container
  • Synonym: cork - a stopper made of cork for bottles or barrels

Etymology

From Middle English bong, from Old Norse bunki (a stopper).

2. VERB

/ bʌŋ / (AmE), / bʌŋ / (BrE)
to close or seal (a container) with a cork or stopper
seal with a cork or stopper

Examples

After filling the barrel, the winemaker bunged it tightly to prevent air from entering.
You need to bung the bottle if you want to keep it fresh.
She bunged the flask with a rubber stopper before shaking it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plug - to close or block a hole or opening, especially with a plug

Etymology

From Middle English bong, possibly from Middle Dutch bonghe (stopper).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01348451-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ bʌŋ / (AmE), / bʌŋ / (BrE)
to give a tip or small payment to someone for a service, in addition to the agreed pay, especially informally or secretly
give a tip or gratuity
informal british

Examples

He bunged the bartender a fiver for his quick service.
Don't forget to bung the porter a little extra for carrying your bags.
She always bungs the delivery driver a tip during the holidays.

Synset Examples

Remember to tip the waiter
fee the steward

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tip - to give a small amount of extra money for a service
  • Synonym: gratuity - a sum of money given to someone for a service performed
