squeamish

1. ADJ

/ ˈskwiːmɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈskwiːmɪʃ / (BrE)
easily made to feel disgusted, especially by unpleasant sights, smells, or ideas.
easily disgusted or upset

Examples

He is so squeamish that even the sight of a scraped knee makes him turn pale.
Some people are too squeamish to watch horror movies with lots of blood.
She grew squeamish at the idea of eating snails for dinner.

Synset Examples

too nice about his food to take to camp cooking
so squeamish he would only touch the toilet handle with his elbow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: queasy - feeling sick or uneasy, especially with nausea
  • Antonym: unfazed - not easily upset or disturbed

Etymology

Perhaps from earlier 'squemous', from Old French 'escoymous' (delicate, squeamish).
