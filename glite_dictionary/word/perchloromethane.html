perchloromethane

1. NOUN

/ pɚˌklɔːroʊˈmɛθˌeɪn / (AmE), / pəˌklɔːrəʊˈmiːθeɪn / (BrE)
a colorless, nonflammable liquid used as a solvent for fats and oils, now less commonly used because of its toxicity
toxic solvent and cleaning fluid
medical

Examples

The laboratory disposed of the perchloromethane in accordance with hazardous waste regulations.
Because of its toxicity, perchloromethane is rarely used for dry cleaning today.
Scientists use perchloromethane as a solvent in certain chemical extractions.

Etymology

From 'per-' (denoting maximum amount) + 'chloro-' (chlorine) + 'methane' (root hydrocarbon name).
