canteen

1. NOUN

/ kænˈtin / (AmE), / kænˈtiːn / (BrE)
a restaurant or cafeteria in a factory, office, or other workplace, where workers or staff can buy and eat meals
workplace cafeteria or restaurant

Examples

The company canteen serves hot meals to employees during lunch breaks.
Many students gather in the canteen to eat and socialize.
The factory installed a new canteen to improve worker morale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cafeteria - a restaurant where you serve yourself and pay at a counter

Etymology

From French 'cantine' meaning 'cellar, store-room,' later 'mess-room.'

2. NOUN

/ kænˈtin / (AmE), / kænˈtiːn / (BrE)
a place within a school, institution, or camp where food and other personal items are sold to staff or students
place in an institution selling food and items

Examples

The school canteen offers a variety of healthy lunches for students every day.
During their break, the workers gathered at the canteen for coffee and snacks.
She forgot her wallet and couldn't buy anything from the canteen at camp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cafeteria - a place where people get food at a counter and carry it to a table

Etymology

From French *cantine* (a cellar, store-room), ultimately from Italian *cantina* (wine cellar).

3. NOUN

/ kænˈtin / (AmE), / kænˈtiːn / (BrE)
a small container or flask for carrying water or other drinks, typically used by soldiers, campers, or travelers
water flask for soldiers or travelers

Examples

The soldier took a long drink from his canteen before continuing the march.
Hikers are advised to carry a canteen of water on long trails.
She filled her canteen at the stream before setting up camp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flask - a small container for liquids, often for carrying alcohol or water

Etymology

From French 'cantine', originally meaning a store of provisions. The term evolved to include the drinking flask used by soldiers.

4. NOUN

/ kænˈtin / (AmE), / kænˈtiːn / (BrE)
a recreation room in an institution, such as a school or factory, where people can relax, eat, or socialize
recreation room in institution

Examples

The hospital's canteen is a comfortable place for staff to unwind during breaks.
Students gathered in the canteen after classes to chat and play board games.
The company upgraded its canteen to include table tennis and a coffee bar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common room - a lounge or recreation room, especially in an institution

Etymology

From French *cantine* (corner, provision store), originally from Italian *cantina* (cellar).

5. NOUN

/ kænˈtin / (AmE), / kænˈtiːn / (BrE)
a restaurant or refreshment room in a workplace, military camp, or other institution, often serving soldiers, workers, or police officers
restaurant in a workplace or for soldiers

Examples

The army canteen serves meals to soldiers three times a day.
After their shift, the police officers relaxed together in the station canteen.
Many factories have a canteen where workers can buy lunch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cafeteria - a restaurant in which customers serve themselves from a counter
  • Synonym: mess hall - a place where soldiers eat meals

Etymology

From French 'cantine' (store of provisions), Italian 'cantina' (wine cellar).
