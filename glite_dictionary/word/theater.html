theater

1. NOUN

/ ˈθiːətər / (AmE), / ˈθɪətə / (BrE)
The art or activity of writing, producing, and performing plays.
the art of writing and producing plays

Examples

She has devoted her life to theater, working as both a playwright and a director.
Many universities offer courses in theater to help students develop their acting skills.
Modern theater often explores social and political themes on stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drama - the art or activity of writing and producing plays

Etymology

From Greek *theatron* (place for viewing), via Latin *theatrum* and Old French *theatre*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈθiːətər / (AmE), / ˈθiːətə / (BrE)
A building or room where plays, shows, or movies are presented to an audience.
building for plays or movies

Examples

The city just opened a new theater downtown that seats over a thousand people.
We arrived early to find good seats in the theater for the play.
The old theater was restored and is now showing classic films every weekend.

Synset Examples

the house was full

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinema - building for watching films or movies
  • Synonym: auditorium - large hall for public events or performances

Etymology

From Greek theatron (place for viewing), via Latin theatrum and Old French theatre.

3. NOUN

/ ˈθiːətər / (AmE), / ˈθɪətə / (BrE)
a large area where important military actions are occurring during a war; a region in which active military operations are taking place
military region where active operations occur

Examples

He was stationed in the European theater during World War II.
Several countries contributed troops to the Gulf War theater.
The pacific theater was a major area of conflict in the Second World War.

Synset Examples

the army was in the field awaiting action
he served in the Vietnam theater for three years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battlefield - the site or area of a battle
