multipartite

1. ADJ

/ ˌmʌl.tɪˈpɑːr.taɪt / (AmE), / ˌmʌl.tɪˈpɑː.taɪt / (BrE)
involving or composed of more than two parties or elements
involving more than two parties

Examples

The countries signed a multipartite agreement to address regional economic issues.
A multipartite negotiation can be more complex than a discussion between two parties.
This treaty marks the first multipartite effort to tackle climate change in the region.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bipartite - involving two parties or elements

Etymology

From Latin 'multipartitus', from 'multi-' (many) + 'partitus' (divided, parted).
