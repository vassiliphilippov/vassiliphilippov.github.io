Polaris

1. PROPN

/ poʊˈlɛərɪs / (AmE), / pəˈlɑːrɪs / (BrE)
the brightest star in the constellation Ursa Minor, also called the North Star, situated nearly at the north celestial pole and used for navigation.
the North Star in Ursa Minor

Examples

Ancient sailors used Polaris to help them navigate the northern seas.
Polaris remains almost stationary in the night sky due to its position near the celestial north pole.
The Little Dipper’s handle ends with Polaris, making it easier to locate in the sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: North Star - the star closest to the north celestial pole

Etymology

From Latin 'Polaris' (of the pole), from 'polus' (pole).
