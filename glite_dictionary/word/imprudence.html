imprudence

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpruːdəns / (AmE), / ɪmˈpruːdəns / (BrE)
the quality of being unwise, lacking caution or good judgment, especially in practical matters
lack of caution or good judgment

Examples

Her financial troubles were the result of imprudence in managing her investments.
Driving without wearing a seatbelt is an example of imprudence.
The board was criticized for its imprudence in approving the risky project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recklessness - the quality of being careless and taking foolish risks
  • Antonym: prudence - the quality of being careful and wise

Etymology

From Latin imprudentia (lack of foresight), from imprudens (unwise, unaware).
