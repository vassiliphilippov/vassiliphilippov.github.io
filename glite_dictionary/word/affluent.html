affluent

1. NOUN

/ ˈæfluənt / (AmE), / ˈæfluənt / (BrE)
a person who has a great deal of money and is financially well off
wealthy person

Examples

Many affluents are investing in sustainable housing projects.
The new marketing strategy targets urban affluents seeking luxury brands.
Programs are designed to appeal to both the middle class and affluents.

Synset Examples

the so-called emerging affluents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rich - having a lot of money or valuable possessions
  • Antonym: poor - lacking sufficient money to live at a standard considered comfortable or normal in society

Etymology

From Latin 'affluēns' (flowing to, abundant).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæfluənt / (AmE), / ˈæfluənt / (BrE)
a stream or river that flows into a larger one; a tributary (archaic)
tributary stream or river (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The smaller affluent merges with the main river a few miles downstream.
Geographers mapped every affluent that joined the mighty Nile.
In old texts, an affluent is another word for a tributary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tributary - a river or stream that flows into a larger river or lake

Etymology

From Latin 'affluens', present participle of 'affluere' (to flow towards).

3. ADJ

/ ˈæf.lu.ənt / (AmE), / ˈæf.lu.ənt / (BrE)
having a great deal of money, wealth, or valuable possessions; wealthy
having a lot of money; wealthy

Examples

The city's affluent neighborhoods are known for their grand houses and tree-lined streets.
Only the most affluent families could afford private education in that region.
Several affluent donors contributed to the university's new library.

Synset Examples

an affluent banker
a speculator flush with cash
not merely rich but loaded
moneyed aristocrats
wealthy corporations
a substantial family

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wealthy - having a great deal of money, resources, or assets; rich
  • Antonym: poor - lacking sufficient money to live at a standard considered comfortable

Etymology

From Latin 'affluens', present participle of 'affluere' (to flow to, abound).
