raisin

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪ.zən / (AmE), / ˈreɪ.zɪn / (BrE)
a small, sweet, dried grape, often used in baking and cooking
dried grape

Examples

She added a handful of raisins to the oatmeal cookies.
My favorite snack is a box of raisins and some nuts.
The raisin gave a burst of sweetness to the salad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grape - the fruit from which raisins are made

Etymology

From Old French 'raisin' meaning grape, from Latin 'racemus' (a bunch of grapes).
