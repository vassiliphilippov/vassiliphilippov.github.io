to perplex

1. VERB

/ pərˈplɛks / (AmE), / pəˈplɛks / (BrE)
to make (a situation or problem) more complicated or difficult to understand
make more complicated or confusing

Examples

The discovery of a second set of documents only served to perplex the investigation.
Adding this new rule would perplex an already confusing process.
The negotiations were perplexed by unexpected legal issues.

Synset Examples

There was a new development that complicated the matter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to complicate - to make more difficult or complex

Etymology

From Latin perplexus (entangled, confused), from per- (through) + plexus (entwined, interwoven).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00403446-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pərˈplɛks / (AmE), / pəˈplɛks / (BrE)
to make someone feel completely baffled or confused
cause to feel confused or puzzled

Examples

The complicated instructions perplex many new users.
Her sudden change of attitude perplexed everyone at the meeting.
The magician's trick never failed to perplex his audience.

Synset Examples

This beats me!
Got me — I don't know the answer!
a vexing problem
This question really stuck me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confuse - to make someone unable to think clearly or understand something
  • Antonym: to enlighten - to give someone greater knowledge or understanding

Etymology

From Latin perplexus (entangled, confused), from per- (through) + plexus (entwined).
