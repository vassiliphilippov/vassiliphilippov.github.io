painstakingness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪnzˌteɪkɪŋnəs / (AmE), / ˈpeɪnzteɪkɪŋnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely careful and thorough in work or effort
quality of being very careful and thorough

Examples

Her painstakingness ensured that every detail of the project was flawless.
The painstakingness of his research impressed even the most critical reviewers.
Without painstakingness, such high-quality results would not have been possible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meticulousness - the quality of being very careful and precise
  • Antonym: carelessness - lack of care or attention

Etymology

From 'painstaking' + '-ness', where 'painstaking' means showing great care and effort.
