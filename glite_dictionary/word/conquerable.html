conquerable

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑŋkərəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋkərəbl̩ / (BrE)
Able to be overcome, defeated, or brought under control.
able to be conquered

Examples

The mountain seemed unconquerable, but with determination, it proved conquerable.
Many challenges in life are difficult but ultimately conquerable with enough effort.
The fortress was believed to be secure, yet history showed it was indeed conquerable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: invincible - impossible to defeat or overcome

Etymology

From Latin 'conquerere' (to conquer) + English suffix '-able'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɑŋ.kɚ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋ.kər.ə.bəl / (BrE)
able to be conquered or overcome
able to be conquered

Examples

Many believed the fortress was conquerable with the right strategy and enough determination.
She realized her anxiety was conquerable, and began taking steps to overcome it.
The mountain, though daunting, proved conquerable by experienced climbers.

Synset Examples

knew her fears were ultimately conquerable

Related Terms

  • Antonym: invincible - impossible to conquer or defeat
  • Antonym: unconquerable - impossible to overcome or conquer

Etymology

From 'conquer' + '-able', after Old French conquere and Latin conquērere.
