boughless

1. ADJ

/ ˈbaʊləs / (AmE), / ˈbaʊləs / (BrE)
(of a tree) having no boughs; lacking large branches.
without boughs (branches)

Examples

The old willow stood boughless after the storm had broken its branches.
Winter revealed the boughless silhouette of the tree against the pale sky.
A boughless trunk provided little shade on the exposed hillside.

Etymology

From 'bough' + suffix '-less' meaning 'without'.
