applecart

1. NOUN

/ ˈæpəlˌkɑrt / (AmE), / ˈaplkɑːt / (BrE)
A handcart from which apples and other fruit are sold on the street, especially in markets or by street vendors.
handcart for selling apples and fruit

Examples

The street vendor pushed his applecart through the busy market every morning.
During the festival, several children helped decorate the old wooden applecart.
A sudden rainstorm forced her to cover her applecart with a tarp.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæpəlˌkɑrt / (AmE), / ˈæp(ə)lkɑːt / (BrE)
used to refer to a person's carefully made plans or arrangements, especially when they are disrupted or disturbed.
one's plans or arrangements

Examples

He worked for weeks on the project, but one mistake was enough to upset his applecart.
Don't let unexpected news disturb your applecart and ruin your plans.
Every time we're close to finishing, something comes along and overturns the applecart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plan - a detailed proposal for doing or achieving something
  • Synonym: scheme - an organized plan or arrangement
  • Synonym: arrangement - plans or preparations for a future event

Etymology

From the literal 'apple cart', a cart for selling apples, later used figuratively in the idiom 'upset the applecart'.
