lineament

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪniəmənt / (AmE), / ˈlɪniəmənt / (BrE)
a distinctive feature or characteristic, especially one of the outlines or contours of a person's face
distinctive facial feature or contour

Examples

The artist skillfully captured the delicate lineaments of her subject in the portrait.
His strong lineaments gave him a look of quiet determination.
She studied the lineaments of his face, searching for signs of emotion.

Synset Examples

an expression of pleasure crossed his features
his lineaments were very regular

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feature - a distinctive part of someone's face

Etymology

From Latin 'lineamentum' (contour, outline), from 'linea' (line).

distinctive or characteristic feature

Examples

Geologists study the lineaments of the earth's surface to understand its history.
Every great leader has certain lineaments that set them apart from others.
