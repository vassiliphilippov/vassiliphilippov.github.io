apparatchik

1. NOUN

/ ˌæpəˈrɑːtʃɪk / (AmE), / ˌæpəˈratʃɪk / (BrE)
an official or bureaucrat in the administrative system of a communist party, especially one who is blindly devoted to its policies
official in a communist party bureaucracy

Examples

As a loyal apparatchik, he carried out the party's orders without question.
Many Soviet apparatchiks lost their positions after the collapse of the Communist Party.
She criticized the rigid mindset typical of the average apparatchik.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bureaucrat - official in a bureaucracy

Etymology

From Russian аппаратчик (apparatčik), from аппарат (apparat, 'apparatus') + -чик (-chik, agent suffix)

2. NOUN

/ əˈpɑːrətʃɪk / (AmE), / əˈparətʃɪk / (BrE)
an official or functionary in a large organization, especially a political party, often regarded as blindly devoted to the organization
official in a large, often political, organization

Examples

The organization promoted her to apparatchik, entrusting her with overseeing compliance across all departments.
As a loyal apparatchik, he always followed orders without question or hesitation.
Critics accused the council of being run by apparatchiks instead of true public servants.

Synset Examples

Democratic apparatchiks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bureaucrat - an official in a government department, concerned with procedural correctness
  • Degree Lower: party member - a member of a political party

Etymology

From Russian аппаратчик (apparatchik), from аппарат (apparat, 'apparatus' or 'bureaucratic organization').
