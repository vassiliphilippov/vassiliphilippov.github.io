Porifera

1. NOUN

/ pɔːˈrɪfərə / (AmE), / pɒˈrɪfərə / (BrE)
A phylum of simple aquatic animals known as sponges, characterized by their porous bodies and ability to filter feed.
a phylum of aquatic animals; sponges

Examples

Scientists collected samples of Porifera from the coral reef for analysis.
The Porifera are among the oldest animal phyla on Earth.
Marine biologists study the structure and function of Porifera in order to understand early animal evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sponge - an aquatic animal with a porous body, member of Porifera

Etymology

From Latin 'porus' (pore) + '-fera' (bearing), meaning 'pore-bearing'.
