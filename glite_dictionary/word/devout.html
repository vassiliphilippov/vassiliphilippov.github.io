devout

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈvaʊt / (AmE), / dɪˈvaʊt / (BrE)
having or showing deep religious feeling or commitment
deeply religious or pious

Examples

Her grandmother was a devout Catholic who never missed Sunday mass.
The devout members of the community gathered for the evening prayer service.
Although he was raised in a religious family, he became less devout as he grew older.

Synset Examples

a god-fearing and law-abiding people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pious - having or showing religious devotion
  • Antonym: irreverent - not showing respect for religious practices or beliefs

Etymology

From Old French *devot*, from Latin *devotus* (devoted, pious).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈvaʊt / (AmE), / dɪˈvaʊt / (BrE)
(of a feeling or wish) deeply sincere and earnest; heartfelt.
sincerely earnest; heartfelt

Examples

She sent her devout wishes for our safe journey.
His devout hope was that the communities would unite in peace.
With devout attention, she listened to their concerns and offered heartfelt advice.

Synset Examples

one's dearest wish
devout wishes for their success
heartfelt condolences

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heartfelt - deeply and sincerely felt; earnest
  • Antonym: insincere - not expressing genuine feelings; not earnest

Etymology

From Old French *devot*, from Latin *devotus* (‘devoted, pious’).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02187588-s
Synset POS: s
