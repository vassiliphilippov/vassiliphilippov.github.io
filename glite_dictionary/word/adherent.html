adherent

1. ADJ

/ ədˈhɪrənt / (AmE), / ədˈhɪərənt / (BrE)
(of a substance or surface) sticking firmly to something; able to stick fast.
sticking firmly to something

Examples

The adherent tape held the package tightly together during shipping.
These leaves have an adherent quality, making them difficult to separate.
An adherent residue was left on the pan after cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cohesive - tending to stick together or remain united
  • Synonym: adhesive - able to stick fast to a surface or object

Etymology

From Latin adhaerent-, present participle of adhaerēre (to stick to).

2. NOUN

/ ədˈhɪrənt / (AmE), / ədˈhɪərənt / (BrE)
a person who supports a particular idea, party, or leader, and helps to promote or spread its views
a supporter or follower of a belief or leader

Examples

He has long been an adherent of the environmental movement.
The politician's adherents gathered outside the courthouse to show their support.
Religious adherents often play a major role in community events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: follower - a person who supports and admires a particular person or set of ideas
  • Synonym: supporter - a person who backs or promotes a cause or person
  • Antonym: opponent - someone who disagrees with or resists a particular idea or person

Etymology

From Latin 'adhaerent-', present participle of 'adhaerere' (to stick to).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10035803-n
Synset POS: n
