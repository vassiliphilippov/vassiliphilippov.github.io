glia

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡliːə / (AmE), / ˈɡliːə / (BrE)
Cells in the central nervous system that surround, support, and protect neurons, collectively making up the supporting tissue.
supporting cells in the nervous system
medical

Examples

The human brain is composed of billions of glia and neurons working together.
For many years, scientists underestimated the importance of glia in brain function.
Damage to glia can result in various neurological disorders.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: neuron - a nerve cell that transmits electrical signals
  • Synonym: glial cell - another name for glia, used interchangeably

Etymology

From Greek 'glia' meaning 'glue.'
