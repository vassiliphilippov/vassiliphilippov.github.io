Ulfila

1. PROPN

/ ʌlˈfiːlə / (AmE), / ʌlˈfiːlə / (BrE)
(c. 311–383) A Christian bishop of the Visigoths, traditionally credited with inventing the Gothic alphabet and translating the Bible into Gothic.
Christian bishop and inventor of Gothic alphabet

Examples

Ulfila is known for translating the Bible into the Gothic language.
Scholars have long studied Ulfila's role in early Christian missionary work among the Goths.
The invention of the Gothic alphabet is often attributed to Ulfila.

Etymology

From Gothic Wulfila, diminutive of 'wolf'.
