hyperthermia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərˈθɜrmiə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈθəːmɪə / (BrE)
An abnormally high body temperature, sometimes induced deliberately for medical treatment.
abnormally high body temperature
medical

Examples

Heat stroke is a dangerous form of hyperthermia that requires immediate medical attention.
Doctors sometimes use controlled hyperthermia as a treatment for certain cancers.
Symptoms of hyperthermia include dizziness, confusion, and nausea.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypothermia - abnormally low body temperature

Etymology

From Greek hyper- (over) + thermē (heat).
