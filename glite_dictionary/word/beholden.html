beholden

1. ADJ

/ bɪˈhoʊldən / (AmE), / bɪˈhəʊldən / (BrE)
owing thanks or having a duty to someone because of help or a favor received; under an obligation.
under obligation to someone

Examples

I will always feel beholden to you for saving my life.
She did not want to be beholden to anyone for her success.
After receiving the loan, he was beholden to his uncle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indebted - owing gratitude or owing something to someone
  • Synonym: obliged - grateful or bound by obligation to someone

Etymology

From Middle English 'beholden,' past participle of 'behold', meaning to hold or keep.
