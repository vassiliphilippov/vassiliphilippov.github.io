teaberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈtiːˌbɛri / (BrE)
A small, spicy red fruit from certain North American plants, often used as a source of wintergreen oil.
spicy red berry; source of wintergreen oil

Examples

The children picked teaberries in the forest and tasted their spicy flavor.
Teaberry oil is often used to add a wintergreen scent to candies and gum.
In the cool woods, the carpet of teaberry plants produced both flowers and berries.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtiˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈtiːˌbɛri / (BrE)
A low-growing shrub of eastern North America with white bell-shaped flowers and spicy red berrylike fruit, whose shiny aromatic leaves yield wintergreen oil.
North American shrub with spicy red berries

Examples

The woods were carpeted with teaberry plants and their glossy green leaves.
She chewed a teaberry for its distinctive, minty flavor while hiking.
The oil extracted from teaberry leaves is used to make wintergreen flavoring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wintergreen - a plant whose leaves yield wintergreen oil

Etymology

From 'tea' + 'berry', referring to use of the leaves for tea and the plant's berrylike fruit.
