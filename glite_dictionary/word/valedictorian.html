valedictorian

1. NOUN

/ ˌvælɪdɪkˈtɔriən / (AmE), / ˌvalɪdɪkˈtɔːrɪən / (BrE)
the student with the highest academic achievements in a graduating class, who usually delivers the valedictory address at commencement ceremonies
top graduating student

Examples

As the valedictorian, she was invited to address her classmates at the graduation ceremony.
He worked hard throughout high school to achieve the honor of valedictorian.
The valedictorian delivered an inspiring speech about perseverance and hope.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: salutatorian - student ranking second highest in a graduating class, delivers the salutatory address

Etymology

From Latin 'valedicere' (to say farewell) + '-ian', referring to the student giving the farewell speech.
