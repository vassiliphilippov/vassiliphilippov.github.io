crustacean

1. ADJ

/ krəˈsteɪʃən / (AmE), / krʌˈsteɪʃən / (BrE)
relating to or belonging to the large class Crustacea, which includes animals such as crabs, lobsters, and shrimps
relating to Crustacea (crabs, lobsters, etc.)

Examples

The marine biologist collected several crustacean specimens from the tide pools.
Their diet mainly consists of various crustacean species found in shallow water.
The research focuses on the crustacean exoskeleton and its role in environmental adaptation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crustacean - an animal of the class Crustacea

Etymology

From Latin 'crusta' (crust, shell) + '-acean' (forming taxonomic names).

2. NOUN

/ krəˈsteɪʃən / (AmE), / krʌˈsteɪʃən / (BrE)
an animal of a large group of mainly aquatic arthropods with a hard outer shell and segmented body, such as crabs, lobsters, and shrimps
aquatic arthropod with hard shell

Examples

The scientist studied a rare species of crustacean found only in deep-sea vents.
Many crustaceans such as crabs and lobsters are popular in seafood cuisine.
A crustacean's hard exoskeleton protects it from predators and harsh environments.

Etymology

From Latin 'crusta' (shell) + '-acean', denoting belonging to a group.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
