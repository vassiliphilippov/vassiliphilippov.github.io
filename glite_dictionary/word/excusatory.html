excusatory

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈskjuːzəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ɪkˈskjuːzətəri / (BrE)
offering or expressing an excuse or apology
expressing an excuse or apology

Examples

She wrote an excusatory letter explaining her tardiness to the meeting.
His excusatory tone made it clear he hadn't meant any offense.
After the mishap, the engineer replied in an excusatory manner, hoping to avoid blame.

Synset Examples

an apologetic note
an apologetic manner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apologetic - expressing or showing regretful acknowledgment of an offense or failure

Etymology

From Latin 'excusatorius', from 'excusare' (to excuse).
