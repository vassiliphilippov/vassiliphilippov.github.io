mercantile

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɝː.kən.taɪl / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.kən.taɪl / (BrE)
Primarily concerned with making a profit or engaged in trade or commerce.
profit-oriented; commercial

Examples

The company's mercantile strategies focused on maximizing profit at every opportunity.
He viewed her interest in the project as entirely mercantile, lacking any artistic motivation.
The city has a bustling, mercantile atmosphere, shaped by centuries of trade.

Synset Examples

a commercial book
preached a mercantile and militant patriotism
a mercenary enterprise
a moneymaking business

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commercial - concerned with commerce or making profit
  • Synonym: mercenary - primarily motivated by profit; profit-oriented

Etymology

From Latin mercantilis (pertaining to trade), from mercari (to trade), from merx (goods).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɜr.kənˌtaɪl / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.kən.taɪl / (BrE)
relating to trade or commercial activities; characteristic of merchants or trading.
relating to trade or commerce

Examples

The city's mercantile reputation grew quickly during the 18th century.
Her family comes from a proud mercantile background.
Foreign investments have boosted the mercantile ties between the two countries.

Synset Examples

the mercantile North was forging ahead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commercial - concerned with commerce or business
  • Antonym: noncommercial - not conducted for profit or commercial purposes

Etymology

From Latin 'mercantilis', from 'mercari' (to trade) and 'mercatus' (market).

3. ADJ

/ ˈmɜːrkənˌtaɪl / (AmE), / ˈmɜːkəntaɪl / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the economic theory or practices of mercantilism, especially as applied to trade and commerce.
relating to mercantilism or commercial trade

Examples

The policy reforms were intended to dismantle the nation's mercantile system.
Many mercantile theories emphasize the importance of accumulating precious metals.
In the seventeenth century, European nations often adopted a mercantile approach to boost their economies.

Synset Examples

mercantile theories
mercantile system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mercantilist - relating to a supporter or practitioner of mercantilism

Etymology

From French *mercantile*, from Italian *mercantile* (trading), from *mercante* (merchant).
