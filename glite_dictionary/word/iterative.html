iterative

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪt̬.ɚ.eɪ.t̬ɪv / (AmE), / ˈɪt.ər.ə.tɪv / (BrE)
A grammatical aspect of a verb that indicates an action is performed repeatedly or habitually.
verb aspect showing repeated actions

Examples

In some languages, the iterative distinguishes between single and repeated actions.
The verb form is marked as iterative to show it happens regularly.
Linguists study how the iterative differs from other aspects in grammar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: habitual - A verb aspect indicating actions that occur regularly or habitually

Etymology

From Latin iterativus (repeating), from iterare (to repeat).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪt̬.əˌreɪ.t̬ɪv / (AmE), / ˈɪt.ər.ə.tɪv / (BrE)
involving repetition; relating to or marked by repeated cycles or processes
involving repetition or cycles

Examples

The iterative process of editing drafts helped improve the clarity of the report.
Many algorithms use iterative methods to reach a solution step by step.
Her approach was iterative, refining the design with each round of testing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repetitive - involving or characterized by repetition
  • Synonym: recursive - characterized by recurrence or repeated application, especially in mathematics or computer science

From Latin iterativus (repeating), from iterare (to repeat).
