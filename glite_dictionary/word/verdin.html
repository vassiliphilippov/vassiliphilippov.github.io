verdin

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɜrdɪn / (AmE), / ˈvɜːdɪn / (BrE)
a very small songbird of western North America, with a yellow head and primarily gray body
small yellow-headed songbird of North America

Examples

We spotted a beautiful verdin perched among the desert shrubs.
The verdin built a complex nest out of thorny branches.
Birdwatchers are often delighted to observe the bright yellow head of the verdin.

Etymology

From French 'verdin', diminutive of 'vert' (green), referring to the bird's plumage.
