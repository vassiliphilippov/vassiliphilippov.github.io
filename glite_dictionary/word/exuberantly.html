exuberantly

1. ADV

/ ɪɡˈzuːbərəntli / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzjuːbərəntli / (BrE)
in a lively, enthusiastic, or energetic manner; with great excitement or vitality
with energy or enthusiasm

Examples

She danced exuberantly across the stage, captivating everyone with her energy.
The garden bloomed exuberantly after weeks of heavy rain and sunshine.
He greeted his old friends exuberantly, unable to hide his excitement.

Synset Examples

the exuberantly baroque decoration of the church

Related Terms

  • Synonym: energetically - with a lot of energy and enthusiasm
  • Antonym: gloomily - in a sad or depressed manner

Etymology

From 'exuberant' + '-ly', with 'exuberant' from Latin exuberare (to be abundant).

2. ADV

/ ɪgˈzuːbərəntli / (AmE), / ɪgˈzjuːbərəntli / (BrE)
in a lively, energetic, and enthusiastic manner
in a lively or enthusiastic way

Examples

The children danced exuberantly at the party, filling the room with laughter and joy.
She greeted her old friend exuberantly, arms wide with excitement.
The fans cheered exuberantly when their team scored the winning goal.

Synset Examples

Khrushchev ebulliently promised to supply rockets for the protection of Cuba against American aggression

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthusiastically - in a way that shows intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval
  • Antonym: gloomily - in a way that is sad or lacking in cheerfulness

Etymology

From Latin 'exuberare' (be abundant).
