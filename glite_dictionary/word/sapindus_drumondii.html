Sapindus drumondii

1. NOUN

/ sæˈpɪndəs druːˈmʌndiːˌaɪ / (AmE), / sæˈpɪndəs drʌˈmɒndiːˌaɪ / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States, known for its pulpy fruit that contains saponin.
tree with pulpy fruit from southwestern US

Examples

The leaves of the Sapindus drumondii turn bright yellow in the fall.
Native peoples used the fruit of Sapindus drumondii as a natural soap.
You can find Sapindus drumondii growing in dry, rocky soils across Texas and Oklahoma.

Etymology

Genus 'Sapindus' from Latin 'sapo' (soap) + 'indicus' (of India); 'drumondii' named after Scottish botanist Thomas Drummond.
