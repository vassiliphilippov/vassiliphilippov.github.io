malathion

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæləˈθaɪən / (AmE), / ˌmæləˈθaɪɒn / (BrE)
a synthetic yellow insecticide used to control garden pests, houseflies, and mites
a synthetic insecticide
medical

Examples

Do not use malathion near edible plants unless the instructions specifically allow it.
The gardeners sprayed malathion to get rid of the aphids on the roses.
Exposure to malathion can be harmful without proper safety equipment.

Etymology

From mal- (in reference to its use against malarial mosquitoes) + -thion (from an organic chemical suffix).
