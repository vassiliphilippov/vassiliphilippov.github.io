inelasticity

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnɪlæsˈtɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪlæsˈtɪsɪti / (BrE)
the quality of not returning to original shape after being stretched or the inability to change readily in response to force or influence
lack of elasticity

Examples

The inelasticity of the rubber band made it unsuitable for the experiment.
In economics, the inelasticity of demand means that consumers are not very responsive to price changes.
Doctors noted the inelasticity of the patient's skin as a potential symptom of the disease.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: elasticity - the ability of an object or material to resume its normal shape after being stretched or compressed

Etymology

From in- (not) + elasticity, modeled on classical formation in scientific vocabulary.
