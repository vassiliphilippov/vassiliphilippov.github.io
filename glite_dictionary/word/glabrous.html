glabrous

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡleɪ.brəs / (AmE), / ˈɡleɪ.brəs / (BrE)
(of a surface) smooth and having no hair or similar growth, especially used to describe skin, leaves, or stems.
smooth and hairless (especially in plants or skin)

Examples

The scientist noted the glabrous surface of the plant's stem.
With age, his once thick hair gave way to a glabrous scalp.
This species is easily identified by its glabrous leaves compared to its close relatives.

Synset Examples

glabrous stems
glabrous leaves
a glabrous scalp

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hairy - covered with hair
  • Synonym: smooth - having a surface free from roughness or projections

Etymology

From Latin glaber (smooth, hairless).
