momism

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːmɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmɒmɪzəm / (BrE)
excessive or overprotective motherly behavior, often to the detriment of a child's independence or development
excessive maternal protectiveness
american

Examples

His mother's momism made it hard for him to make his own decisions.
Critics of the era blamed rising momism for stifling boys' emotional growth.
The psychologist spoke about the negative impacts of momism on adolescent development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overprotection - excessive protection, especially of children
  • Synonym: smothering - restricting someone's independence by being overprotective

Etymology

From 'mom' (mother) + '-ism' (denoting a practice or doctrine), primarily American in origin.
