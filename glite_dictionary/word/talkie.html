talkie

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔki / (AmE), / ˈtɔːki / (BrE)
a motion picture with synchronized spoken dialogue and sound, as opposed to a silent film
film with synchronized sound

Examples

The arrival of the talkie revolutionized the movie industry and captivated audiences worldwide.
Many actors from the silent era struggled to adapt to the rise of the talkie.
She owns a collection of classic talkies from the 1930s and 1940s.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: silent film - a motion picture with no synchronized recorded sound or dialogue

Etymology

From 'talk' + -ie, coined in the late 1920s to describe early sound films.
