marquee

1. NOUN

/ mɑrˈki / (AmE), / mɑːˈkiː / (BrE)
a large tent used for social or commercial functions, especially one that is elegant or elaborate
large, elaborate tent for events
british

Examples

They hosted the wedding reception in a beautiful marquee on the lawn.
A large marquee was erected at the festival to accommodate the guests.
The garden party was held under a spacious marquee to protect everyone from the rain.

Etymology

From French 'marquise', feminine of 'marquis', originally meaning a canopy placed over a tent.

2. NOUN

/ mɑrˈki / (AmE), / mɑːˈkiː / (BrE)
A large permanent canopy projecting over the entrance of a hotel, theater, or other building, often bearing a sign or lights.
canopy over building entrance

Examples

The hotel's marquee was brightly lit, welcoming guests to the grand opening.
Fans waited eagerly under the theater's marquee to see the movie stars arrive.
The old cinema still has its original marquee displaying the current film titles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awning - a secondary covering attached to the exterior wall of a building

Etymology

From French 'marquise', originally referring to a type of tent.
