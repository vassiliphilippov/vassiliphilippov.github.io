to coggle

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑɡəl / (AmE), / ˈkɒɡəl / (BrE)
to walk unsteadily with short steps, as if unsure or unsteady
walk unsteadily with short steps

Examples

After her surgery, she began to coggle across the living room, supported by her daughter.
The puppy tried to coggle toward its mother, legs wobbling with every step.
Elderly men sometimes coggle down the street on cold, slippery mornings.

Synset Examples

small children toddle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to toddle - to walk with short, unsteady steps, like a young child

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑːɡəl / (AmE), / ˈkɒɡəl / (BrE)
to move or walk unsteadily, as if about to fall; to wobble
move unsteadily; wobble

Examples

The old man began to coggle after tripping over a loose stone in the path.
Her bicycle started to coggle as she rode over the uneven ground.
The toddler tried to walk but could only coggle from one side of the room to the other.

Synset Examples

His knees wobbled
The old cart wobbled down the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wobble - to move unsteadily from side to side
  • Synonym: stagger - to walk or move unsteadily, as if about to fall
  • Antonym: steady - to make or become stable and not shake or move unsteadily
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01872996-v
Synset POS: v
