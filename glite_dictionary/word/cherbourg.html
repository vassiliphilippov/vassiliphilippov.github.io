Cherbourg

1. PROPN

/ ʃɛrˌbʊərɡ / (AmE), / ʃɛəˈbʊəɡ / (BrE)
a port town in northwestern France on the English Channel, known for its naval base and historical significance
port town in northwestern France

Examples

The ferry from Portsmouth docks at Cherbourg after a six-hour crossing.
During World War II, Cherbourg played a vital role as an Allied supply port.
We spent a delightful afternoon exploring the historic streets of Cherbourg.
