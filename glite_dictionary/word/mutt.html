mutt

1. NOUN

/ mʌt / (AmE), / mʌt / (BrE)
A dog that is of mixed breed, often considered inferior or of no particular pedigree.
dog of mixed breed

Examples

She adopted a mutt from the animal shelter instead of buying a purebred puppy.
The mutt wagged its tail happily, grateful for the attention.
Some people believe a mutt can be just as loyal as any pedigree dog.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mongrel - a dog of no definable type or breed
  • Antonym: purebred - a dog of unmixed lineage or pedigree

Etymology

Informal American English, origin unknown, possibly related to 'muttonhead' (a fool).
