Urocyon cinereoargenteus

1. NOUN

/ ˌjʊroʊˈsaɪɔn sɪˌnɪəroʊɑːrˈʤɛntəs / (AmE), / ˌjʊərəˈsaɪən sɪˌnɪəriəʊɑːˈdʒɛntɪəs / (BrE)
A species of fox native to Central America and the southern United States, commonly known as the gray fox, with dark grey fur.
species of gray fox

Examples

The Urocyon cinereoargenteus is notable for its ability to climb trees, unlike most other foxes.
Researchers are tracking the population of Urocyon cinereoargenteus in southern Texas.
Several wildlife parks have successfully bred Urocyon cinereoargenteus in captivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gray fox - common name for Urocyon cinereoargenteus, a North American fox species

Etymology

From Latin: 'Urocyon' (tail dog) and 'cinereoargenteus' (ash-silvered, greyish-silver).
