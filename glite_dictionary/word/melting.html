melting

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɛltɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɛltɪŋ / (BrE)
becoming liquid as a result of heat or another process.
becoming liquid

Examples

The melting snow created streams of water along the sidewalks.
She watched the melting chocolate drip slowly over the cake.
The melting ice cubes watered down her drink.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solidifying - becoming solid; opposite of melting

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛltɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɛltɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which heat causes a solid to turn into a liquid
process of turning solid to liquid by heat

Examples

The melting of ice in spring often leads to flooding in some areas.
High temperatures accelerated the melting of glaciers in the region.
The scientist carefully observed the melting of the metal in the furnace.

Synset Examples

the power failure caused a refrigerator melt that was a disaster
the thawing of a frozen turkey takes several hours

Related Terms

  • Antonym: freezing - the process of turning from liquid to solid
  • Synonym: fusion - the process or result of joining two or more things together to form a single entity; also a term for melting in physics

Etymology

From the verb 'melt,' which comes from Old English 'meltan' (to liquefy, digest).
