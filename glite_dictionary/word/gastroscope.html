gastroscope

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæstrəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈɡæstrəskəʊp / (BrE)
a medical instrument used to visually examine the interior of the stomach
instrument for examining the stomach
medical

Examples

The doctor used a gastroscope to check for ulcers in the patient's stomach.
Before the procedure begins, the nurse prepares the gastroscope for the examination.
Advances in gastroscope technology have made stomach examinations much less invasive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endoscope - an instrument for examining inside the body

Etymology

From Greek 'gastēr' (stomach) + 'skopein' (to look or examine).
