Romneya

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːmniə / (AmE), / ˈrɒmniə / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs in the poppy family, known for their large white flowers, especially the matilija poppy.
genus of shrubs; matilija poppy

Examples

The Romneya is prized in gardens for its striking, crepe-like blooms.
Botanists classify the matilija poppy under the genus Romneya.
Drought-tolerant and showy, Romneya species thrive in the Californian landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: matilija poppy - species of Romneya with large white flowers

Etymology

Named after Irish astronomer Thomas Romney Robinson (1792–1882).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːmnɪə / (AmE), / ˈrɒmnɪə / (BrE)
a small genus of flowering plants in the poppy family, comprising chiefly the matilija poppy of California and northern Mexico
genus of flowering plants (matilija poppy)

Examples

The Romneya is prized in gardens for its large, showy white flowers.
Botanists often study the Romneya for its striking resemblance to other members of the poppy family.
Plant collectors seek out Romneya species because of their unique appearance and drought tolerance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: matilija poppy - a tall poppy with large white flowers native to California, in the genus Romneya

Etymology

Named after Irish astronomer Thomas Romney Robinson.
