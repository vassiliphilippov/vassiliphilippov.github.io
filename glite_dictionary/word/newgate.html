Newgate

1. PROPN

/ ˈnuːˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈnjuːˌɡeɪt / (BrE)
a former prison in London, notorious for its harsh conditions, demolished in 1902
famous former London prison

Examples

Many notorious criminals were once imprisoned in Newgate during the 18th century.
Charles Dickens often referenced Newgate in his novels to portray the grim realities of Victorian justice.
The infamous Newgate prison was demolished at the beginning of the 20th century.

Etymology

Named after the Newgate (new gate) of the City of London, near which the prison was built.
