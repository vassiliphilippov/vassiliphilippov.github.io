monodical

1. ADJ

/ məˈnɑːdɪkəl / (AmE), / məˈnɒdɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to or having a single vocal part; consisting of only one melody line, especially in music.
having a single vocal part

Examples

The composer chose a monodical setting for the hymn, focusing on a single vocal melody.
Medieval chant was often monodical, without any accompaniment or harmony.
A monodical structure can give the music a pure and direct emotional impact.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polyphonic - having two or more independent melodic lines; antonym of monodical in music

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single, alone) + 'ēdē' (song), via medieval Latin and French roots in musical terminology.
