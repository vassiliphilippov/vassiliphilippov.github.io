Calochortus elegans

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæloʊˈkɔrtəs ˈɛlɪɡənz / (AmE), / ˌkæləkɔːˈtʌs ˈɛlɪɡənz / (BrE)
A small flowering plant native to northwestern United States, with slender bent stems and branched clusters of white star-shaped, cat's-ear-like flowers.
species of flowering plant in northwestern US

Examples

The blooming Calochortus elegans added a delicate beauty to the mountain meadow.
We spotted several Calochortus elegans during our hike through northeastern Oregon.
Botanists are studying the distribution of Calochortus elegans in Montana's grasslands.

Etymology

From Greek 'kalos' (beautiful) + 'chortos' (grass), species name 'elegans' from Latin for 'elegant' or 'graceful'.
