bullion

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊliən / (AmE), / ˈbʊliən / (BrE)
Gold or silver in bulk form, such as bars or ingots, valued by weight rather than face value.
gold or silver in bars or ingots

Examples

The price of bullion has risen sharply due to economic uncertainty.
Banks often store large quantities of gold bullion in secure vaults.
Silver bullion is commonly traded by investors as a tangible asset.

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *bullion* (melting, refining), probably from Old French *bouillir* (to boil).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʊliən / (AmE), / ˈbʊljən / (BrE)
Gold or silver in bulk, typically in the form of bars or ingots, before coining or being made into jewelry.
precious metal in bars or bulk

Examples

The central bank's vaults hold thousands of tons of gold bullion.
Silver bullion is often purchased as an investment against inflation.
The pirates buried a chest filled with bullion on the deserted island.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ingot - a block of metal, typically oblong, cast into a shape for convenient handling or storage

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *bullion* (boiling, melting), later specialized to 'precious metal in mass', from Old French *bouillon* (boiling, broth).
