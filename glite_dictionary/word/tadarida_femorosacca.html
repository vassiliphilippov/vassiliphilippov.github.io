Tadarida femorosacca

1. NOUN

/ təˈdɛrɪdə ˌfɛmərəˈsɑkə / (AmE), / təˈdærɪdə ˌfɛmərəˈsakə / (BrE)
A species of small brown bat found in California and northern Mexico, also known as the pocketed free-tailed bat.
species of small brown bat

Examples

The Tadarida femorosacca can often be seen flying at dusk in southern California.
Researchers studied the roosting behavior of Tadarida femorosacca in arid habitats.
Unlike other bats, Tadarida femorosacca is noted for the distinctive pocket near its thigh.

Etymology

From Latinized Greek genus name Tadarida; species name from Latin 'femur' (thigh) and 'saccus' (bag), referring to a pocket near the thigh.
