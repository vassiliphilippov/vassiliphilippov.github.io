Ceratopteris thalictroides

1. NOUN

/ sɛrəˈtɑptərɪs θəˌlɪktrɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / sɛrəˈtɒptərɪs θəˌlɪktrɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A species of aquatic fern found in tropical and subtropical regions around the world, often used in aquariums and wetlands.
tropical aquatic fern species

Examples

Ceratopteris thalictroides is popular among aquarists for its fast growth and adaptability in freshwater tanks.
The wetlands were dominated by Ceratopteris thalictroides, providing habitat for various aquatic species.
Researchers study Ceratopteris thalictroides due to its unique reproductive biology and environmental significance.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus Ceratopteris + species epithet thalictroides, indicating resemblance to the genus Thalictrum.
