foregoing

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrˌgoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːɡəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
just mentioned or stated; previously mentioned in the text or speech
previously mentioned; earlier in the text

Examples

According to the foregoing discussion, we can draw several important conclusions.
The foregoing remarks are intended to clarify the committee's recommendation.
Please refer to the foregoing paragraph for a detailed explanation of the procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preceding - existing or occurring before in time or order
  • Antonym: ensuing - coming immediately after in time or order

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'forego,' from Middle English 'forgon' (to go before).
