Salvia leucophylla

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæl.vi.ə ˌluː.kəˈfaɪ.lə / (AmE), / ˈsal.vɪ.ə ˌluː.kəˈfʌɪ.lə / (BrE)
A species of sage native to California, noted for its silvery leaves and purple flowers.
a sage species with silvery leaves, native to California

Examples

Salvia leucophylla is often used in California native plant gardens for its drought tolerance.
Hummingbirds are attracted to the vibrant purple flowers of Salvia leucophylla.
The silver foliage of Salvia leucophylla adds contrast to the landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sage - any plant of the genus Salvia, especially used as herbs

Etymology

From Latin, 'salvia' meaning 'sage' and 'leucophylla' from Greek 'leukos' (white) + 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to its white leaves.
