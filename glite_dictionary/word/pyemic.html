pyemic

1. ADJ

/ paɪˈiːmɪk / (AmE), / paɪˈiːmɪk / (BrE)
relating to or caused by pyemia, a type of blood poisoning characterized by widespread abscesses caused by pus-forming bacteria in the blood.
relating to pyemia (blood poisoning)
medical

Examples

The autopsy revealed several pyemic abscesses in the patient's vital organs.
In the nineteenth century, pyemic infections were often fatal due to lack of antibiotics.
Her symptoms suggested a pyemic complication following the surgical procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyemia - blood poisoning with pus-forming bacteria

Etymology

From Greek pyon (pus) + -emia (blood condition), via New Latin pyæmia.
