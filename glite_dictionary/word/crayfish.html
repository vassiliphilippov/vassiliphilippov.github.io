crayfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈkreɪˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkreɪ.fɪʃ / (BrE)
A large edible marine crustacean with a spiny shell and no large pincers, resembling but distinct from true lobsters.
large spiny marine crustacean (no big pincers)

Examples

The restaurant served fresh crayfish caught off the coast that morning.
We watched a crayfish scuttle under a rock in the shallow bay.
Scientists studied the diet of the local crayfish population for conservation purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobster - Large marine crustacean with large pincers

Etymology

From Middle English 'crevis', from Old French 'crevice', influenced by 'fish'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkreɪ.fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkreɪ.fɪʃ / (BrE)
A small freshwater crustacean with a hard shell and pincers, resembling a small lobster.
freshwater crustacean resembling a small lobster

Examples

We caught several crayfish in the stream during our camping trip.
The restaurant serves a delicious dish made with fresh crayfish.
Children watched as the crayfish scuttled across the bottom of the aquarium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobster - large marine crustacean with claws, related to crayfish

Etymology

From Middle English 'crevis', from Old French 'crevice', from Old High German 'krebiz' (crab).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkreɪˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkreɪ.fɪʃ / (BrE)
A small freshwater crustacean resembling a tiny lobster, often found in rivers and streams and sometimes used as food.
small freshwater crustacean similar to a lobster

Examples

We found a crayfish under the rock while wading in the stream.
She prepared a spicy stew with fresh crayfish for dinner.
Children enjoy catching crayfish during their summer camping trips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobster - large marine crustacean with a similar appearance
  • Degree Lower: shrimp - small, slender marine or freshwater crustacean

Etymology

From Middle English 'crevice', from Old French 'crevice'; influenced by the word 'fish'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈkreɪˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkreɪˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A large edible warm-water crustacean, resembling a lobster but without claws, found especially in Australia, South Africa, Florida, and California, and often sold as frozen tails.
warm-water clawless lobster-like crustacean

Examples

The restaurant specializes in grilled crayfish caught off the coast of Australia.
Fishermen in Florida often catch crayfish for the seafood market.
We ordered a plate of crayfish tails imported from South Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiny lobster - a warm-water lobster without claws, with a spiny body
