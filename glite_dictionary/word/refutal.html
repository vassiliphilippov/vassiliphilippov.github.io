refutal

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈfjutəl / (AmE), / rɪˈfjuːtəl / (BrE)
the act of proving that something is false or incorrect; a rebuttal or denial
the act of proving something false

Examples

The scientist's refutal of the old theory changed the direction of the research.
She offered a detailed refutal of the accusations made against her.
His refutal was based on newly discovered evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refutation - the act of proving a statement or theory to be wrong
  • Antonym: confirmation - the act of confirming something as true

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'refute' + -al suffix; modeled on similar noun formations.
