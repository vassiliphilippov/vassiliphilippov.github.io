prolusion

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈluːʒən / (AmE), / prəˈluːʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
a short introductory essay or preface that comes before the main text of a book
short introduction to a book

Examples

The author's prolusion provides helpful background information for the reader.
A brief prolusion sets the tone for the themes explored in the following chapters.
Without the prolusion, some passages in the book might be difficult to interpret.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preface - an introduction to a book, typically stating its subject, scope, or aims

Etymology

From Latin 'prolusio' (preliminary exercise, prelude), from 'proludere' (to play beforehand).

2. NOUN

/ proʊˈluːʒən / (AmE), / prəˈljuːʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
an action or exercise carried out as preparation for a more strenuous activity; a preliminary performance or practice
preparatory exercise or action

Examples

The concert began with a brief prolusion by the orchestra, setting the mood for the evening.
Every training session starts with a prolusion to prepare athletes for the main workout.
Her essay served as a thoughtful prolusion to the more complex arguments presented later.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prelude - an introductory performance or action

Etymology

From Latin *prolusio(n-)*, from *proludere* (to play beforehand).
