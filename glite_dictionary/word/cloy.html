to cloy

1. VERB

/ klɔɪ / (AmE), / klɔɪ / (BrE)
to supply or indulge someone to excess, especially so as to cause distaste or weariness
supply or indulge to excess

Examples

After so many sweet desserts, the richness began to cloy my palate.
Endless praise can cloy even the most eager recipient over time.
The air was cloyed with the heavy scent of overblown roses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satiate - to satisfy fully or to excess
  • Synonym: surfeit - to supply or feed with too much of something
  • Antonym: to starve - to suffer from lack of food

Etymology

From Old French *cloyer* (to hinder), from Latin *clavus* (nail, a metaphor for restraining).

2. VERB

/ klɔɪ / (AmE), / klɔɪ / (BrE)
to make someone feel sick or bored by giving them too much of something originally pleasant, especially food or sweetness
become unpleasant through excess

Examples

The constant praise began to cloy rather than motivate her.
Rich desserts can cloy the palate if eaten in excess.
His overly sentimental tone soon cloyed the audience.

Synset Examples

Too much spicy food cloyed his appetite

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satiate - to satisfy fully or to excess
  • Antonym: to refresh - to make fresh or pleasant again after tiredness or excess

Etymology

From Middle English *cloy*, variant of *acloyen* (to hinder, stop up), from Old French *encloyer* (to stop up with a nail), from Latin *clavus* (nail).
