Louis Henry Sullivan

1. PROPN

/ luˈiː ˈhɛnri səˈlɪvən / (AmE), / luˈiː ˈhɛnri səˈlɪvən / (BrE)
(1856–1924) United States architect, renowned for his steel-framed skyscrapers and for coining the phrase 'form follows function.'
American architect; coined 'form follows function'

Examples

Louis Henry Sullivan is often called the 'father of skyscrapers' for his pioneering work in American architecture.
The phrase 'form follows function' is famously attributed to Louis Henry Sullivan.
Many modern architects cite Louis Henry Sullivan as a major influence on their design philosophies.
