taken

1. ADJ

/ ˈteɪkən / (AmE), / ˈteɪkən / (BrE)
(of a person) suddenly affected by a specified condition, especially illness; used in phrases such as 'be taken ill' or 'be taken drunk'.
suddenly affected (illness, etc.)

Examples

She was suddenly taken ill at the party and had to go home early.
He is sometimes taken drunk after just one or two drinks.
You may be taken ill without any warning, so always be prepared.

Synset Examples

the child was taken ill
couldn't tell when he would be taken drunk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stricken - suddenly affected by something, especially an unpleasant condition
Source: WordNet

2. ADJ

/ ˈteɪkən / (AmE), / ˈteɪkən / (BrE)
interpreted or understood in a particular way, especially as specified by context
interpreted or understood in a certain way

Examples

His comment was taken as a joke, though he was actually serious.
The instructions can be taken in more than one way, which caused confusion.
My silence was taken for agreement, even though I disagreed.

Synset Examples

a word taken literally
a smile taken as consent
an open door interpreted as an invitation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interpreted - explained or understood in a particular way
  • Antonym: misconstrued - interpreted wrongly or misunderstood

Etymology

Past participle of 'take', from Old English *tacan*, from Old Norse *taka* (to take).
