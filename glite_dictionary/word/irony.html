irony

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrəni / (AmE), / ˈaɪərəni / (BrE)
the use of words to express something different from and often opposite to their literal meaning, especially in a way that expresses scorn or insults
witty language expressing scorn or insults

Examples

His irony often left his audience unsure whether he was joking or criticizing.
She spoke with such cutting irony that her remarks felt like veiled insults.
Some people don't understand subtle irony and take the words at face value.

Synset Examples

he used sarcasm to upset his opponent
irony is wasted on the stupid
Satire is a sort of glass, wherein beholders do generally discover everybody's face but their own

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sarcasm - the use of irony to mock or convey contempt

Etymology

From Latin ironia, from Ancient Greek eirōneía (dissimulation, feigned ignorance).

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrəni / (AmE), / ˈaɪərəni / (BrE)
A situation or event in which what actually happens is opposite to what was expected, especially in a way that seems deliberately contrary or humorous.
incongruity between expectation and reality

Examples

It was a cruel irony that the fire station itself burned down.
The irony of the situation was lost on no one in the audience.
There is an irony in how the fastest runner tripped at the starting line.

Synset Examples

the irony of Ireland's copying the nation she most hated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sarcasm - the use of irony to mock or convey contempt
  • Antonym: sincerity - the quality of being free from pretense or deceit

Etymology

From Greek eirōneía (dissimulation, pretended ignorance).

3. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrəni / (AmE), / ˈaɪrəni / (BrE)
The use of words to express something other than and especially the opposite of the literal meaning, or a situation where the result is different from what was expected.
expression or situation opposite to expectation

Examples

There is a certain irony in calling a huge man 'Tiny'.
The irony of her quitting right after being promoted was not lost on anyone.
He smiled at the irony that his safety training caused him to break his arm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sarcasm - the use of irony to mock or convey contempt
  • Antonym: sincerity - the quality of being honest or genuine; lack of irony

Etymology

From Ancient Greek eirōneía (feigned ignorance).
