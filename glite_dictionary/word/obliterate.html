to obliterate

1. VERB

/ əˈblɪtəˌreɪt / (AmE), / əˈblɪtəreɪt / (BrE)
to make something impossible to see, read, or recognize, especially by covering or removing it completely
make impossible to see or recognize

Examples

The rain managed to obliterate the chalk writing on the sidewalk overnight.
Censors tried to obliterate sensitive details from the classified documents.
A smudge of ink had completely obliterated the signature on the contract.

Synset Examples

a hidden message
a veiled threat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erase - to remove written or recorded information so nothing remains
  • Synonym: to conceal - to hide or keep from view
  • Antonym: to reveal - to make something visible or known that was hidden

Etymology

From Latin obliterare (to blot out, efface), from ob- (over) + littera (letter).

2. VERB

/ əˈblɪtəˌreɪt / (AmE), / əˈblɪtəˌreɪt / (BrE)
to destroy something completely so that nothing remains; to remove all signs of something
destroy completely; erase all traces

Examples

The storm managed to obliterate nearly every trace of the old road.
The historian feared that time would obliterate these important records forever.
New buildings completely obliterated the view of the mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to eradicate - to completely get rid of something bad or unwanted
  • Synonym: to annihilate - to destroy completely
  • Antonym: preserve - to keep something safe or in existence

Etymology

From Latin oblitterare (to erase), from ob- (against) + littera (letter).

3. VERB

/ əˈblɪtəˌreɪt / (AmE), / əˈblɪtəreɪt / (BrE)
to remove all traces of something so that it cannot be recognized or remembered
remove all traces; destroy completely

Examples

The terrible event was so traumatic that he tried to obliterate it from his memory.
Centuries of erosion have obliterated the ancient carvings on the stone walls.
She desperately wanted to obliterate any trace of her former life.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erase - to remove something completely, especially written or recorded information
  • Synonym: to destroy - to put an end to the existence of something by damaging or attacking it
  • Antonym: preserve - to keep something in its original state or in good condition

Etymology

From Latin obliterare, meaning 'to cause to be forgotten', from ob- (over) + littera (letter).
