Priacanthus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpraɪəˈkænθəs / (AmE), / ˌprɪəˈkanθəs / (BrE)
A genus of brightly colored, deep-bodied marine fishes in the family Priacanthidae, commonly known as bigeyes.
genus of marine fishes (bigeyes)

Examples

The Priacanthus genus includes several species known for their striking red coloration and large eyes.
Marine biologists discovered a new species of Priacanthus in the coastal waters of Indonesia.
The diet of Priacanthus typically consists of smaller fish and invertebrates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bigeye - Common name for species within Priacanthus; marine fishes with large eyes

Etymology

From Greek 'priēin' (to saw) + 'akantha' (thorn/spine), referring to the serrated spines of the fish.

2. NOUN

/ praɪˈækənθəs / (AmE), / praɪˈækanθəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical marine fishes, the type genus of the family Priacanthidae, known for their large eyes and often reddish coloring.
genus of tropical marine fish

Examples

The deep red coloration is characteristic of the Priacanthus genus.
Several species within Priacanthus are popular among aquarium enthusiasts.
Researchers discovered a new species of Priacanthus in the Pacific Ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Priacanthidae - the family of fish to which Priacanthus belongs

Etymology

From Greek prion (saw) and akantha (thorn or spine), referring to features of the fish.
