curvature

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɝː.vɚ.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.və.tʃə / (BrE)
(medicine) a curving or bending of a bodily part, often referring to an abnormal condition.
medical: abnormal bending (e.g., of the spine)
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed a slight curvature of the patient's lower spine.
Scoliosis is a condition marked by abnormal curvature of the back.
Regular check-ups can help detect any curvature developing in a child's spine early.

Synset Examples

curvature of the spine

Etymology

From Latin 'curvatura' (a bending, curve), from 'curvare' (to bend).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɝː.vɚ.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.və.tʃə / (BrE)
the rate at which the direction of a curve changes at a particular point; a measure of how sharply a curve bends
rate a curve changes direction

Examples

The curvature of the road made it difficult for large vehicles to navigate safely.
Mathematicians use calculus to precisely calculate the curvature at any given point on a curve.
The curvature of the mirror allowed it to focus light to a single point.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straightness - the quality or state of being straight
  • Synonym: bend - a curve or turn in a physical object

Etymology

From Latin curvatura (a bending, curvature), from curvare (to bend).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɝː.vɚ.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.və.tʃər / (BrE)
the condition of being curved or the degree to which something bends, especially a line or surface
the state or degree of being curved

Examples

The curvature of the road makes it dangerous to drive at high speeds.
Scientists analyzed the curvature of the newly discovered planet's surface.
She measured the curvature of the metal beam to ensure it met the specifications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curve - a line or surface that bends smoothly; not straight
  • Antonym: straightness - the quality of being straight; not curved

Etymology

From Late Latin 'curvatura', from Latin 'curvus' (bent, curved).
