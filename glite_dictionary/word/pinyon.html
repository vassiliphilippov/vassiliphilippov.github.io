pinyon

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪn.jən / (AmE), / ˈpɪn.jən / (BrE)
A type of low-growing pine tree native to western North America, especially known for producing edible seeds called pine nuts.
low-growing pine tree of western North America

Examples

The pinyon thrives in the dry, rocky soils of the American Southwest.
Many Native American tribes harvested pinyon nuts as a nutritious food source.
A stand of pinyon trees provided welcome shade along the hiking trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pine - a coniferous tree of the genus Pinus

Etymology

From Spanish 'piñón', meaning 'pine nut', derived from Latin 'pīnea' (pine cone).
