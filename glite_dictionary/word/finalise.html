to finalise

1. VERB

/ ˈfaɪnəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfaɪnəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make final; to put the finishing touches on something or bring it to a completed form
bring to completion; make final
british

Examples

We need to finalise the agenda before sending it to all participants.
The contract was finalised after weeks of negotiation between both parties.
I will finalise my travel plans tomorrow morning.

Synset Examples

let's finalize the proposal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to finalize - make final; complete the last steps (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'final' + '-ise', after Latin 'finalis' (of the end) and influenced by 'finalize'.
