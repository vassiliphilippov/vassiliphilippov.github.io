nonphotosynthetic

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˌfoʊtoʊsɪnˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / nɒnˌfəʊtəʊsɪnˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
(of an organism or tissue) not capable of carrying out photosynthesis.
not capable of photosynthesis

Examples

Some fungi are nonphotosynthetic and must absorb nutrients from organic material.
The study focused on the role of nonphotosynthetic bacteria in soil ecosystems.
Unlike green plants, these algae are completely nonphotosynthetic and obtain energy by consuming other organisms.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: photosynthetic - able to carry out photosynthesis

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' (not) and 'photosynthetic' (related to photosynthesis).
