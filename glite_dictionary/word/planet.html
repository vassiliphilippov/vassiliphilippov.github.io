planet

1. NOUN

/ ˈplænət / (AmE), / ˈplænɪt / (BrE)
a celestial body (other than a comet or satellite) that revolves around a star, especially the sun
celestial body orbiting a star

Examples

Earth is the only planet in our solar system known to support life.
Astronomers recently discovered a new planet orbiting a distant star.
Each planet in the solar system has a unique set of characteristics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: star - a luminous celestial body made of plasma, visible in the night sky
  • Antonym: satellite - a celestial body that orbits a planet, such as a moon

Etymology

From Greek 'planētēs' (wanderer), via Old French and Latin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈplænɪt / (AmE), / ˈplænɪt / (BrE)
A large celestial body that orbits a star, especially any of the major bodies in the solar system that orbit the sun and shine by reflected light.
large body orbiting a star

Examples

Earth is the only known planet in the universe that supports life.
Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, with a mass more than twice that of all the others combined.
Astronomers once classified Pluto as the ninth planet, but it is now considered a dwarf planet.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: star - A luminous celestial body producing its own light, like the sun
  • Degree Lower: dwarf planet - A celestial body resembling a small planet but lacking certain criteria to be considered a full planet

Etymology

From Old French planète, via Latin planeta, from Greek planētēs (wanderer), from planasthai (to wander).
