susceptibleness

1. NOUN

/ səˈsɛptəbəlnəs / (AmE), / səˈsɛptɪb(ə)lnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being easily affected or influenced by something.
state of being easily affected

Examples

Her susceptibleness to flattery made her an easy target for manipulation.
The susceptibleness of the crops to disease worried the farmers.
Some children have a greater susceptibleness to colds than others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: susceptibility - the quality or state of being susceptible (more common form)

Etymology

From susceptible + -ness, modeled after 'susceptibility.'
