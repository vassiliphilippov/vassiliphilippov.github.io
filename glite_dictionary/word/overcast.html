overcast

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvɚˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəkɑːst / (BrE)
A state in which the sky is completely covered with clouds.
sky covered with clouds

Examples

The weather report predicted overcast throughout the day with occasional showers.
An overcast in the morning often makes the temperature cooler.
We canceled the picnic because of the persistent overcast.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cloudy - sky partially or mostly covered with clouds

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvərˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəkɑːst / (BrE)
A state of gloomy semidarkness caused by thick cloud cover, especially in the sky.
gloomy dimness from cloud cover

Examples

A sudden overcast settled over the city, muting the colors of the afternoon.
The photographer waited for the perfect overcast to soften the harsh shadows.
After lunch, a thick overcast made the landscape look almost monochrome.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14007953-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈoʊvərˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəkɑːst / (BrE)
(of the sky or weather) covered with clouds; dull or gloomy.
cloudy or dull (weather)

Examples

The sky was overcast all day, making everything look gray and dreary.
It looks like rain is coming with such an overcast morning.
We postponed our picnic because the weather was so overcast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloudy - (of the sky) full of or covered with clouds
  • Antonym: clear - (of the sky) free from clouds or mist

Etymology

From Middle English 'overcasten' meaning 'to cover over.'

to sew over the edge of fabric with long, slanting stitches to prevent fraying
sew fabric edge with slanting stitches

Examples

After overcasting the seams, she pressed them flat for a tidy finish.

6. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈkæst / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈkɑːst / (BrE)
To sew the edges of fabric with closely spaced stitches passing over the edge, securing or joining sections together.
sew with overcast stitches

Examples

She carefully overcast the raw edges to prevent fraying.
The artisans overcast the sections of the handmade book for durability.
Before assembling the garment, he overcast the seams with matching thread.

Synset Examples

overcast books

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whipstitch - a similar sewing technique of joining fabric edges with over-and-over stitches

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (across) and 'cast' (throw), referring here to the looping motion of the stitch.

7. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvərˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəkɑːst / (BrE)
a long whipstitch or overhand stitch sewn over the edge of fabric to prevent it from unraveling
stitch to prevent fabric fraying

Examples

She used an overcast to finish the raw edges of the cloth.
The seamstress taught us how to make an overcast for extra durability.
An overcast is often used on fabrics that tend to fray easily.

Etymology

From the verb 'overcast', meaning to sew over the edge; from Middle English 'overcasten' (to cover over).
