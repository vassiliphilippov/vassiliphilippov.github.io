ament

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.mənt / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.mənt / (BrE)
A cylindrical, spike-like inflorescence composed of many small flowers, typically found on trees such as willows and poplars.
cylindrical cluster of small flowers

Examples

The willow tree's branches were covered with aments in early spring.
Male flowers appear on drooping aments before the leaves emerge.
The botanist examined the ament to identify the plant species.

Etymology

From Latin *amentum* (a thong or strap), referring to the straplike appearance.
