to fertilize

1. VERB

/ ˈfɜrtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɜːtɪlaɪz / (BrE)
to add fertilizer or nutrients to soil to help plants grow
add fertilizer to soil

Examples

Farmers often fertilize their fields in the spring to boost crop yields.
It's important to fertilize houseplants regularly to keep them healthy.
She carefully fertilized the vegetable garden before planting the seeds.

Synset Examples

We should fertilize soil if we want to grow healthy plants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enrich - to improve the quality of something, especially by adding something to it

Etymology

From Latin 'fertilis' (fruitful or bearing in abundance).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00503838-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈfɜrtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɜːtɪlaɪz / (BrE)
to cause (a female animal or plant) to conceive by introducing male reproductive cells, especially by introducing semen into the female
to make (a female) conceive by introducing semen

Examples

Modern breeding techniques allow scientists to fertilize eggs outside the body.
In mammals, only one sperm is required to fertilize the female's egg.
The veterinarian explained how to artificially fertilize the cows to improve the herd's genetics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to impregnate - to make pregnant by introducing semen

Etymology

From Latin fertilis (fruitful) via French fertiliser.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00052265-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈfɜrtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɜːtɪlaɪz / (BrE)
to make land, soil, or something else fertile or productive; to enrich or stimulate growth or development
make fertile or productive

Examples

The teacher's guidance helped to fertilize his curiosity about science.
A good book can fertilize your mind with new ideas.
Creative workshops are meant to fertilize innovation within the company.

Synset Examples

The course fertilized her imagination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enrich - to improve the quality or value of something
  • Antonym: to impoverish - to make something poor or less productive

Etymology

From Latin 'fertilis' (fruitful, fertile), via French 'fertiliser'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00505351-v
Synset POS: v
