indention

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɛnʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛnʃən / (BrE)
the blank space between the margin and the beginning of a line that has been indented, as in printed or written text
space at margin before indented line

Examples

Please start each new paragraph with an indention of half an inch.
Some word processors automatically create an indention when you press the tab key.
The teacher marked off points for missing the required indention at the start of each paragraph.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indentation - the action or process of indenting or the state of being indented

Etymology

Alteration of 'indentation', from Latin 'indentāre' (to make notches), influenced by Old French 'denter' (toothed).
