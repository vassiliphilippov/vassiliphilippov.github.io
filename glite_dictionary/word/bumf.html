bumf

1. NOUN

/ bʌmf / (AmE), / bʌmf / (BrE)
documents, paperwork, or written information—often official or bureaucratic—that is considered tedious or boring to read
boring official paperwork or documents
informal british

Examples

The desk was covered in bumf that I needed to sort through before lunch.
She sighed at the pile of bumf her manager handed her to read.
Most of the bumf sent out by the council ends up in the recycling bin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paperwork - written work such as forms or documents, especially in an office
  • Synonym: red tape - excessive bureaucracy or formalities, especially involving tedious paperwork

Etymology

Originally British slang, short for 'bum-fodder' (toilet paper), later applied humorously to excess paperwork.
