woof

1. NOUN

/ wʊf / (AmE), / wʊf / (BrE)
the yarn woven across the warp in weaving; the weft.
the weft yarn in weaving

Examples

The colorful pattern was created by alternating the woof threads with the warp in the loom.
She carefully selected silk for the woof to contrast with the cotton warp.
Traditional tapestries rely on the interplay of woof and warp to achieve intricate designs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weft - the threads that are passed over and under the warp in weaving

Etymology

From Middle English *woof*, from Old English *ōwef* (woven in), from *wefan* (to weave).
