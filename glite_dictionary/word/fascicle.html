fascicle

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæsɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈfæsɪkəl / (BrE)
A bundle of fibers, especially nerve or muscle fibers, bound together within the body.
bundle of fibers (nerve or muscle)
medical

Examples

Each fascicle in the muscle contains many individual fibers.
Damage to a nerve fascicle can impair sensation in the affected area.
During dissection, the scientist separated a fascicle from the main nerve trunk.

Etymology

From Latin 'fasciculus', diminutive of 'fascis' (a bundle).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfæ.sə.kəl / (AmE), / ˈfæs.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
one of the separately published installments of a book or other printed work
installment of a printed work

Examples

The novel was originally released in monthly fascicles before being bound as a complete volume.
Scholars eagerly awaited each new fascicle of the historical dictionary over several years.
This fascicle contains chapters five through eight of the textbook.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: installment - a part of a book published separately
  • Synonym: volume - a book forming part of a larger work

Etymology

From Latin fasciculus, meaning 'little bundle'.
