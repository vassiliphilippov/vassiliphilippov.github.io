Medicago lupulina

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛdɪˈkeɪɡoʊ luːˈpjuːlɪnə / (AmE), / ˌmɛdɪˈkeɪɡəʊ luːˈpjuːlɪnə / (BrE)
A prostrate European herb with small yellow flowers and curved black seed pods, commonly known as black medick or hop clover; naturalized in North America.
a European herb with yellow flowers, black medick

Examples

Medicago lupulina is often found growing in lawns and fields throughout temperate regions.
The small yellow flowers of Medicago lupulina attract various pollinators in the spring.
Farmers sometimes use Medicago lupulina as a forage crop for livestock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black medick - another common name for Medicago lupulina

Etymology

From Latin: *Medicago* (genus name) and *lupulina* (from 'lupulus', meaning 'small wolf' or 'hoplike').
