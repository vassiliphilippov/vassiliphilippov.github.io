Corydalus cornutus

1. NOUN

/ kɔːˈraɪdələs kɔˈnjuːtəs / (AmE), / kɒˈraɪdələs kɔːˈnjuːtəs / (BrE)
A large North American dobsonfly, the male of which has long slender mandibles, with aquatic larvae often used as bait by anglers.
a species of large North American dobsonfly

Examples

Fishermen often use the larvae of Corydalus cornutus as bait.
The male Corydalus cornutus is easily recognized by its large mandibles.
Entomologists study Corydalus cornutus to understand aquatic insect life cycles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dobsonfly - common name for Corydalus cornutus

Etymology

From New Latin, where *Corydalus* is the genus name, and *cornutus* means 'horned' in Latin.
