espionage

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛspiəˌnɑʒ / (AmE), / ˈɛspiənɑːʒ / (BrE)
the practice of spying or using spies to obtain secret information about governments, organizations, or other entities, especially for political or military advantage
the use of spies to get secret information

Examples

He was arrested and charged with espionage after passing classified documents to a foreign agent.
Throughout history, espionage has played a critical role during times of war.
The agency specializes in countering acts of industrial espionage committed by rival companies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spying - the act of secretly collecting information, especially for a government

Etymology

From French 'espionnage', from 'espionner' (to spy).
