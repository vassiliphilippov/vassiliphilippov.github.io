dusky

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʌski / (AmE), / ˈdʌski / (BrE)
Lit or appearing as if illuminated by twilight; dim or shadowy in a way reminiscent of dusk.
dimly lit as by twilight

Examples

A dusky glow lingered on the horizon long after the sun had set.
They walked through the dusky streets just as the city lights began to appear.
Mist hovered over the lake in the dusky stillness of early evening.

Synset Examples

The dusky night rides down the sky / And ushers in the morn
the twilight glow of the sky
a boat on a twilit river

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twilit - dimly illuminated as by twilight
  • Synonym: gloomy - dimly lit or dark with little light
  • Antonym: bright - radiating or reflecting much light

Etymology

From Middle English duske (dark) with -y, meaning 'somewhat dark'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʌski / (AmE), / ˈdʌski / (BrE)
(of a person's skin) naturally having a dark complexion.
having a dark skin complexion

Examples

She wore a vibrant scarf that contrasted beautifully with her dusky skin.
The artist admired the model's dusky complexion as he painted her portrait.
His dusky features are often highlighted in the family’s photo albums.

Synset Examples

a dark-skinned beauty
gold earrings gleamed against her dusky cheeks
a smile on his swarthy face
‘swart’ is archaic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swarthy - having a dark complexion
  • Antonym: pale - having a light complexion

Etymology

From Middle English *duske*, possibly from Old Norse *dǫkk* (dark).
