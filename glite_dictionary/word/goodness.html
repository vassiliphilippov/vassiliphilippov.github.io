goodness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʊdnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡʊdnɪs / (BrE)
things that are valuable, useful, or desirable; the beneficial or positive qualities of something.
valuable or desirable qualities; benefit

Examples

The homemade bread has all the goodness of natural ingredients.
She always tries to find the goodness in every situation, no matter how difficult.
Let's not forget the goodness that this project will bring to the community.

Synset Examples

weigh the good against the bad
among the highest goods of all are happiness and self-realization

Related Terms

  • Antonym: badness - the quality of being bad or lacking value
  • Synonym: virtue - moral excellence or goodness

Etymology

From Old English gōdnes (goodness, virtue), from gōd (good).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡʊdnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡʊdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being morally good or having admirable character
moral excellence; virtue

Examples

She believed in the inherent goodness of all people.
Acts of goodness can inspire others to do the same.
The story reminds us of the goodness that still exists in the world.

Synset Examples

there is much good to be found in people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virtue - behaviour showing high moral standards
  • Antonym: wickedness - the quality of being morally wrong or evil

Etymology

From Old English 'gōdnes' (goodness, virtue), from 'gōd' (good) + '-nes' (suffix forming nouns of quality).
