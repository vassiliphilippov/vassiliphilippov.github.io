tympanic

1. ADJ

/ tɪmˈpænɪk / (AmE), / tɪmˈpænɪk / (BrE)
relating to or associated with the eardrum (tympanum), especially in anatomy and medicine.
relating to the eardrum
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the tympanic membrane for signs of infection.
A tympanic thermometer measures body temperature inside the ear canal.
In some animals, the tympanic cavity helps transmit sound to the inner ear.

Etymology

From Latin *tympanum* (drum, eardrum), via French *tympanique*.

2. ADJ

/ tɪmˈpænɪk / (AmE), / tɪmˈpænɪk / (BrE)
having the qualities or appearance of a drum; drumlike.
drumlike in quality or appearance

Examples

The hollow vase had a tympanic sound when tapped.
Doctors examined the tympanic membrane for signs of infection.
The room's acoustics created a tympanic echo with every loud noise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drumlike - having qualities resembling a drum

Etymology

From Greek *tympanikos*, from *tympanon* (drum).
