proctitis

1. NOUN

/ prɑkˈtaɪtɪs / (AmE), / prɒkˈtaɪtɪs / (BrE)
Inflammation of the rectum, often causing symptoms such as bloody stools and an urgent need to defecate.
inflammation of the rectum
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with proctitis after she reported rectal pain and bleeding.
Individuals suffering from proctitis may experience a frequent urge to pass stools.
Crohn's disease and ulcerative colitis are common causes of chronic proctitis.

Etymology

From Greek proktos (anus, rectum) + -itis (inflammation).
