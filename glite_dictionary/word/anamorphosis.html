anamorphosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.əˈmɔr.fə.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌæn.əˈmɔː.fə.sɪs / (BrE)
the gradual evolution of one type of organism into another through a long series of small changes
evolutionary transformation by gradual change

Examples

The fossil record reveals the anamorphosis of early reptiles into birds over millions of years.
Darwin's theory explains the anamorphosis of complex life forms from simpler ancestors.
Researchers studied the anamorphosis evident in the transitional features of prehistoric mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evolution - the gradual development of organisms over time

Etymology

From Greek anamorphōsis (transformation), from ana- (again) + morphē (form).

2. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈmɔrfəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌanəˈmɔːfəsɪs / (BrE)
A distorted projection or image that appears normal only when viewed from a particular angle or with a special device.
distorted image seen correctly from a certain angle

Examples

The artist used anamorphosis to create an image that looks normal only when reflected in a cylinder.
The museum display demonstrates anamorphosis with a painting that can only be understood from a certain viewpoint.
Leonardo da Vinci experimented with anamorphosis to hide images within his sketches.

Etymology

From Greek anamorphōsis (transformation), from ana- (again) + morphē (form).
