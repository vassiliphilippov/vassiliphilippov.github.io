Petasites hybridus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛtəˌsaɪtiːz hɪˈbrɪdəs / (AmE), / ˌpiːtəˌsaɪtiːz hɪˈbrɪdəs / (BrE)
A small Eurasian perennial herb with broad leaves and lilac-pink, rayless flowers, commonly found in moist areas; also known as butterbur.
a Eurasian herb with broad leaves and pinkish flowers

Examples

The large leaves of the Petasites hybridus plant provide shelter for small creatures in wet meadows.
Herbalists sometimes use extracts from Petasites hybridus to treat headaches and allergies.
You can often find Petasites hybridus growing along riverbanks and in other damp environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butterbur - common name for Petasites hybridus

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name from Greek petasos (broad-brimmed hat), referring to the shape of the leaves. Species epithet from Latin 'hybridus' (of mixed origin).
