prattle

1. NOUN

/ ˈprætl̩ / (AmE), / ˈprætəl / (BrE)
idle or foolish, often childish, talk; meaningless chatter
idle or meaningless talk

Examples

His endless prattle made it hard for anyone to concentrate on their work.
The prattle of the children filled the playground with cheerful noise.
She grew tired of the meaningless prattle at the party and left early.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatter - quick, incessant, trivial talk
  • Synonym: babble - foolish or meaningless talk, often by children
  • Antonym: silence - complete absence of speech or noise

Etymology

From Middle English pratelen, of imitative origin, suggesting meaningless chatter.

2. VERB

/ ˈprætəl / (AmE), / ˈprætəl / (BrE)
to talk at length in a foolish or inconsequential way, often about unimportant things
talk at length about unimportant things

Examples

Children sometimes prattle on about their favorite TV shows for hours.
He would prattle endlessly, barely noticing if anyone was listening.
She prattled excitedly about her day at the amusement park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatter - to talk rapidly or incessantly about trivial matters
  • Synonym: babble - to talk quickly in a way that is difficult to understand or about unimportant things
  • Degree Higher: gossip - to talk about the personal lives of other people, often in a trivial way

Etymology

From Middle English 'pratelen', likely of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
