Mycobacteriaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkoʊˌbæktɪriˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌmʌɪkəʊˌbaktɪərɪˈeɪsɪiː / (BrE)
A family of bacteria that includes species causing tuberculosis and leprosy, such as Mycobacterium tuberculosis and Mycobacterium leprae.
family of bacteria causing tuberculosis and leprosy
medical

Examples

Mycobacteriaceae include the bacteria responsible for both tuberculosis and leprosy.
Scientists are studying the resistance patterns of Mycobacteriaceae to new antibiotics.
Members of the Mycobacteriaceae family can be found in a variety of environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mycobacterium - genus of bacteria in Mycobacteriaceae family, causes diseases

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'myco-' (fungus), 'bacterium', and '-aceae' (family suffix in taxonomy).

2. NOUN

/ maɪˌkoʊbækˌtɪəriˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / mʌɪˌkəʊˌbaktɪəriˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of bacteria that includes species causing tuberculosis and leprosy, such as Mycobacterium tuberculosis and Mycobacterium leprae.
family of bacteria including TB and leprosy agents
medical

Examples

Species in the Mycobacteriaceae family are responsible for serious diseases such as tuberculosis and leprosy.
The laboratory identified the pathogen as a member of the Mycobacteriaceae.
Research into Mycobacteriaceae has led to better treatments for infectious diseases like tuberculosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mycobacterium - genus of bacteria including TB and leprosy agents

Etymology

From New Latin: Greek 'mykes' (fungus) + 'bakterion' (small rod) + '-aceae' (family suffix in taxonomy).
