pneumonic

1. ADJ

/ nuːˈmɑːnɪk / (AmE), / njuːˈmɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to, characterized by, or affected by pneumonia.
relating to pneumonia or its effects
medical

Examples

The patient suffered from a severe pneumonic infection that required hospitalization.
Doctors are concerned about the spread of pneumonic plague in the region.
Early treatment is critical for survival in cases of pneumonic disease.

Synset Examples

pneumonic plague

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pneumatic - filled with or containing air or gas
  • Synonym: pulmonary - relating to the lungs

Etymology

From Greek pneumonikos, from pneumōn (lung).

2. ADJ

/ nuˈmɑnɪk / (AmE), / njuːˈmɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to or affecting the lungs.
of or relating to the lungs
medical

Examples

The patient developed a pneumonic infection following exposure to bacteria.
Doctors identified the illness as a pneumonic complication, which required immediate treatment.
Certain types of the plague can manifest in a pneumonic form, affecting the lungs.

Synset Examples

pulmonary disease

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pulmonary - relating to the lungs

Etymology

From Greek pneumonikos, from pneumōn (lung).
