Ananas comosus

1. NOUN

/ əˈnænəs koʊˈmoʊsəs / (AmE), / əˈnanəs kəˈməʊsəs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the tropical plant known as the pineapple, which bears a large, fleshy, edible fruit with a crown of stiff leaves
pineapple plant (scientific name)

Examples

Many commercial growers cultivate Ananas comosus to produce pineapples for the global market.
Botanists classify the pineapple as Ananas comosus, native to South America.
The spiky crown of leaves is a distinctive feature of Ananas comosus plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pineapple - the common name for the fruit of Ananas comosus

Etymology

From Latinized form of Guaraní 'nanas' (pineapple) and Latin 'comosus' (tufted, hairy).
