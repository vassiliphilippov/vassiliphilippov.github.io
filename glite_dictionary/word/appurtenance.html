appurtenance

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɝː.tən.əns / (AmE), / əˈpɜː.tɪ.nəns / (BrE)
an accessory or additional item that adds functionality or convenience to something larger or more important
accessory or supplementary item

Examples

The new software comes with several appurtenances to enhance its basic features.
The garage and shed are considered appurtenances to the main house.
He bought a tool kit and other appurtenances for his new motorcycle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accessory - an additional item that is not essential but adds usefulness or attractiveness

Etymology

From Old French 'apartenir' (to belong) via Late Latin 'appertinere'.

2. NOUN

/ əˈpɜːrtənəns / (AmE), / əˈpɜːtɪnəns / (BrE)
an accessory or piece of equipment that is used for a particular activity or operation, but is not essential to its main function
accessory item or equipment

Examples

The tool box contained every appurtenance a mechanic could wish for.
Skiing requires several appurtenances, such as poles, boots, and goggles.
The laboratory was well stocked with all the necessary appurtenances for chemical experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accessory - an item that can be added to something else to make it more useful or attractive
  • Synonym: equipment - the necessary items for a particular purpose

Etymology

From Old French 'apartenir' (to belong) and Medieval Latin 'appurtenantia'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03435869-n
Synset POS: n
