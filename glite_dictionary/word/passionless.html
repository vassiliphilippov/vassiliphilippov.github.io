passionless

1. ADJ

/ ˈpæʃ.ən.ləs / (AmE), / ˈpæʃ.ən.ləs / (BrE)
Lacking passion, intense emotion, or enthusiasm.
without passion or emotion

Examples

Her passionless voice revealed nothing about how she truly felt.
The passionless marriage had long lost its spark and excitement.
He spoke in a passionless tone, making the lecture seem even more tedious.

Synset Examples

passionless observation of human nature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dispassionate - not influenced by strong emotion, able to be rational and impartial
  • Antonym: passionate - showing or caused by strong feelings or a strong belief

Etymology

Formed from 'passion' (from Latin 'passio' – suffering, feeling) + suffix '-less' (without).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpæʃənləs / (AmE), / ˈpæʃənləs / (BrE)
Showing or feeling no passion, emotion, or enthusiasm.
without passion or emotion

Examples

Her passionless response suggested she was no longer interested in the project.
He delivered the news in a passionless voice, barely betraying any emotion.
Despite the team's efforts, their performance on stage felt passionless and uninspired.

Synset Examples

he kept his emotionless objectivity and faith in the cause he served
this passionless girl was like an icicle in the sunshine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impassive - not showing or feeling emotion
  • Antonym: passionate - showing or caused by strong feelings or a strong belief

Etymology

Formed from 'passion' + suffix '-less', meaning without passion.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01261062-s
Synset POS: s
