Laminaria

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæməˈnɛriə / (AmE), / ˌlæmɪˈneəriə / (BrE)
A genus of large perennial brown seaweeds (kelps) in the family Laminariaceae, found in cold marine waters.
genus of brown seaweeds (kelps)

Examples

Laminaria is widely harvested for use in food, fertilizer, and industrial products.
Marine biologists study Laminaria to better understand coastal ecosystems.
The dense forests formed by Laminaria provide habitats for many marine species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kelp - large brown seaweeds of the order Laminariales

Etymology

From Latin 'lamina' (thin plate), referring to the blade-like form of the kelp.

2. NOUN

/ ˌlæməˈnɛriə / (AmE), / ˌlæmɪˈnɛəriə / (BrE)
A genus of large, perennial brown seaweeds (kelps) in the family Laminariaceae, commonly found in cold coastal waters.
genus of brown kelp seaweeds

Examples

Laminaria thrives in cold, nutrient-rich waters along rocky coastlines.
Researchers studied the rapid growth rate of Laminaria species in the lab.
Harvested Laminaria is sometimes used as a thickening agent in food products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kelp - large brown seaweeds of the order Laminariales

Etymology

From Latin 'lamina' (thin plate), referring to the flat blades of these seaweeds.
