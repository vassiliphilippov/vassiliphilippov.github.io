self-depreciation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf dɪˌpriʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf dɪˌpriːʃɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
A feeling or expression of having little worth or value in oneself.
sense of low self-worth

Examples

Her constant self-depreciation made her friends worry about her confidence.
Therapy helped him overcome his feelings of self-depreciation and gain self-respect.
Excessive self-depreciation can lead to serious problems with self-esteem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-deprecation - Modest or critical remarks about oneself, often as a form of humor

Etymology

From 'self-' + 'depreciation', based on the root 'depreciate' (Latin depretiatus, 'to lower the price of').
