rose-tint

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊzˌtɪnt / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzˌtɪnt / (BrE)
an optimistic or overly positive view, especially when recalling past events or experiences
idealized or optimistic view of the past

Examples

As the years passed, he began to see his time at school with a rose-tint.
We should be careful not to let a rose-tint distort our memories of history.
Her stories always have a rose-tint, ignoring the hardships she actually faced.

Synset Examples

Looking back, everything seemed to have a rose-tint.
Her memories of childhood have a rose-tint to them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rose-colored - overly optimistic or idealized
  • Synonym: nostalgic - characterized by sentimental longing for the past

Etymology

Formed from 'rose' (the flower, associated with the color pink) and 'tint' (a slight coloration), alluding to the expression 'rose-tinted glasses'.
