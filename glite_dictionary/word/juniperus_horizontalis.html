Juniperus horizontalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒuːnɪˈpɪərəs hɔrɪˌzɒnˈtælɪs / (AmE), / ˌdʒuːnɪˈpɪərəs hɒrɪˌzɒnˈtælɪs / (BrE)
A species of low-growing evergreen shrub native to Canada and the northern United States, commonly known as the creeping juniper, with foliage that turns bronzed purple in winter.
a creeping juniper shrub species

Examples

The Juniperus horizontalis is popular in gardens for its low, spreading growth habit.
In winter, the foliage of Juniperus horizontalis often takes on a striking purple-bronze hue.
Wild populations of Juniperus horizontalis can be found across rocky slopes in northern North America.

Etymology

From Latin: *Juniperus* (juniper) and *horizontalis* (horizontal), referring to its low, spreading growth habit.
