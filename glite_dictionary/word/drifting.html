drifting

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪftɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrɪftɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity or process of moving from place to place without a definite purpose or destination
aimless wandering

Examples

After losing his job, he spent a year drifting through the country with no real plans.
Her novel tells the story of a young man's drifting across Europe in search of meaning.
There was a sense of drifting in his life after college, as he couldn't settle anywhere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wandering - traveling aimlessly from place to place
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00298230-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈdrɪftɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrɪftɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person or group) continually moving or changing, especially from one place, job, or situation to another, often with no clear direction or purpose.
continually moving or changing; unsettled

Examples

After college, he lived a drifting life, never staying in one city for long.
The novel portrays a drifting generation, uncertain about their futures.
Several members of the drifting community finally settled down in the small town.

Synset Examples

a drifting double-dealer
the floating population
vagrant hippies of the sixties

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vagrant - person with no settled home or job, wandering from place to place
  • Synonym: unsettled - lacking stability or permanence

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'drift', from Middle Dutch driften (to drive, be driven).
