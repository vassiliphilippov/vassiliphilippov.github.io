Pinus monophylla

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪ.nəs ˌmɒn.oʊˈfaɪ.lə / (AmE), / ˈpʌɪ.nəs ˌmɒn.ə(ʊ)ˈfɪl.ə / (BrE)
A species of pine tree native to the southwestern United States, with single needles and edible nuts, commonly known as the single-leaf pinyon.
a pine tree of the southwestern US with edible nuts

Examples

The nuts of the Pinus monophylla are an important food source for wildlife and humans alike.
We saw a grove of Pinus monophylla thriving in the high desert during our hike.
Many desert landscapes in Nevada are dominated by Pinus monophylla, also known as single-leaf pinyon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pinyon - Type of pine tree with edible nuts, closely related species

Etymology

From Latin: 'Pinus' (pine) and 'monophylla' (single-leaved).
