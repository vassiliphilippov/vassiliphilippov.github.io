depressive

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈprɛsɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈprɛsɪv / (BrE)
a person suffering from psychological depression
person with clinical depression
medical

Examples

The clinic specializes in treating depressives and those with mood disorders.
Some depressives find it difficult to maintain social relationships during severe episodes.
Researchers study the causes and risk factors that contribute to the experience of depressives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depressed person - someone suffering from depression
  • Antonym: optimist - a person who generally expects good outcomes

Etymology

From Latin deprimere (to press down), via French dépressif.
