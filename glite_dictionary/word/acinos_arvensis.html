Acinos arvensis

1. NOUN

/ əˈsaɪnəs ɑrˈvɛnsɪs / (AmE), / əˈsaɪnɒs ɑːˈvɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A fragrant European mint plant bearing clusters of small violet-and-white flowers, often found naturalized in eastern North America.
a species of fragrant European mint

Examples

The meadow was dotted with Acinos arvensis, filling the air with a gentle minty fragrance.
Researchers documented the spread of Acinos arvensis in the eastern regions of North America.
She carefully identified Acinos arvensis among the wildflowers during her botany field trip.

Etymology

From Latin: 'acinos' (basil thyme), 'arvensis' meaning 'of the field'.
