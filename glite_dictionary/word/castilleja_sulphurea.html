Castilleja sulphurea

1. NOUN

/ kæˌstɪlˈjeɪə sʌlˈfjʊriə / (AmE), / kæsˌtɪlˈjeɪə sʌlˈfjʊəriə / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant native to moist highland meadows, known for its ragged clusters of pale yellow flowers.
a plant with pale yellow flowers in highland meadows

Examples

During our hike, we spotted a cluster of Castilleja sulphurea growing alongside the trail.
Botanists are interested in Castilleja sulphurea because of its unique yellow blooms.
The moist meadows were dotted with Castilleja sulphurea every spring.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Castilleja' named after Spanish botanist Domingo Castillejo, and 'sulphurea' meaning 'sulfur-colored'.
