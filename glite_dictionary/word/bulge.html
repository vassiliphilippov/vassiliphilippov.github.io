bulge

1. NOUN

/ bʌldʒ / (AmE), / bʌldʒ / (BrE)
a rounded swelling or protrusion that sticks out from a surface or its surroundings
a rounded swelling or protrusion

Examples

There was a noticeable bulge in the side of the suitcase where it was overpacked.
The bulge in the wall was a sign that water was trapped behind the paint.
Doctors examined the small bulge on the patient's abdomen to determine if it was a hernia.

Synset Examples

the gun in his pocket made an obvious bulge
the hump of a camel
he stood on the rocky prominence
the occipital protuberance was well developed
the bony excrescence between its horns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protuberance - a thing that protrudes from something else
  • Antonym: depression - a sunken or lower region, opposite of a bulge in surface shapes

Etymology

From Old French 'bouge', from Latin 'bulga' (leather bag, sack).

5. VERB

/ bʌldʒ / (AmE), / bʌldʒ / (BrE)

Examples

Etymology

From Middle French 'bouge' (wallet, leather bag), from Latin 'bulga' (leather bag, sack).
