bullock

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊlək / (AmE), / ˈbʊlək / (BrE)
A castrated male bovine animal, especially one raised for work or meat.
castrated male cattle

Examples

The farmer decided to keep a bullock for plowing the fields.
In many countries, bullocks are prized for their strength and docility.
A bullock is different from a bull because it has been castrated.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bull - an uncastrated adult male bovine animal
  • Synonym: steer - a young castrated male bovine raised for beef

Etymology

Old English *bulluca*, diminutive of *bula* (bull).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʊlək / (AmE), / ˈbʊlək / (BrE)
a young bull, especially one that has not yet reached maturity
young bull

Examples

The farmer raised a bullock on his smallholding for local markets.
A bullock can be distinguished from an adult bull by its smaller size and more docile behavior.
They purchased several bullocks to work on the farm.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bull - adult male of cattle
  • Synonym: steer - castrated male cattle

Etymology

Old English *bulluc* (a young bull), diminutive of *bula* (bull).
