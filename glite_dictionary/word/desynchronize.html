to desynchronize

1. VERB

/ diˈsɪŋkrəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / diːˈsɪŋ.krə.naɪz / (BrE)
to cause to lose synchronization or to occur at different or unrelated times
cause to lose synchronization

Examples

Jet lag can desynchronize your internal clock from the local time.
If the audio and video are out of sync, a technician will need to desynchronize and then re-sync them.
Network errors may desynchronize the time across computers, causing serious issues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to synchronize - cause to occur at the same time or rate

Etymology

From de- (expressing reversal) + synchronize (to occur at the same time); modelled on 'synchronize'.
