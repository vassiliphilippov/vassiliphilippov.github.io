ichthyosaurus

1. NOUN

/ ɪkθioʊˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / ɪkθɪəˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
an extinct genus of marine reptiles from the Jurassic period, resembling dolphins, known for their fish-like appearance
extinct marine reptile of the Jurassic period

Examples

The museum's fossil collection includes a nearly complete ichthyosaurus skeleton from the Jurassic period.
Scientists believe that the ichthyosaurus was an agile swimmer, well-adapted to hunting in ancient seas.
The distinctive, fish-like shape of ichthyosaurus fossils has fascinated paleontologists for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ichthyosaur - any marine reptile of the order Ichthyosauria

Etymology

From Greek 'ikhthys' (fish) and 'sauros' (lizard).
