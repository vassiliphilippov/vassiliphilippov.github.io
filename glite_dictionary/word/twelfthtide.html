Twelfthtide

1. NOUN

/ ˈtwɛlfθ.taɪd / (AmE), / ˈtwɛlfθ.taɪd / (BrE)
The season of Epiphany, especially the period following the Twelfth Day of Christmas in the Christian calendar.
season of Epiphany
archaic

Examples

In some traditions, the celebrations continue throughout Twelfthtide until Candlemas.
During Twelfthtide, churches are often decorated with special colors and ornaments.
The customs of Twelfthtide vary greatly between different Christian communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Epiphany - Christian festival celebrating the revelation of Christ to the Gentiles

Etymology

Formed from 'twelfth' (the ordinal number corresponding to twelve) + 'tide' (season or period).
