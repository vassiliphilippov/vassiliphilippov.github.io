psychotherapeutic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsaɪkoʊˌθɛrəˈpjuːtɪk / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊˌθɛrəˈpjuːtɪk / (BrE)
connected with or having the effect of psychotherapy, especially in causing emotional healing or release.
relating to psychotherapy or emotional healing

Examples

Many people find that journaling can be a psychotherapeutic activity, helping to process deep emotions.
The retreat offered several psychotherapeutic workshops aimed at emotional recovery.
Some forms of art can have a psychotherapeutic effect, allowing individuals to express and release their feelings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: therapeutic - having healing or curative properties

Etymology

From psycho- (mind) + therapeutic (healing), based on Ancient Greek origins.

2. ADJ

/ ˌsaɪkɵˌθɛrəˈpjutɪk / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊˌθɛrəˈpjuːtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving psychotherapy; intended to treat mental or emotional disorders through psychological methods
relating to psychotherapy
medical

Examples

The patient attended several psychotherapeutic sessions to help manage her anxiety.
Cognitive behavioral therapy is one form of psychotherapeutic treatment.
His research focuses on new psychotherapeutic techniques for depression.

Synset Examples

psychotherapeutic sessions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychotherapy - the treatment of mental disorder by psychological rather than medical means

Etymology

From 'psycho-' (mind) + 'therapeutic' (healing); see psychotherapy.
