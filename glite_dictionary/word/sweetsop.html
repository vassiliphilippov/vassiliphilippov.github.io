sweetsop

1. NOUN

/ ˈswitˌsɑp / (AmE), / ˈswiːtˌsɒp / (BrE)
A tropical American tree (Annona squamosa) bearing sweet, pulpy fruit with a thick, scaly rind and shiny black seeds.
tropical tree with sweet, scaly fruit

Examples

The ripe sweetsop has a creamy, fragrant flesh that is enjoyed by many in tropical regions.
We planted a sweetsop tree in our backyard last summer, and it has just started to bear fruit.
Every morning for breakfast, she slices a fresh sweetsop to eat with yogurt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sugar apple - another name for Annona squamosa, the sweetsop fruit
  • Synonym: custard apple - a related tropical fruit of the Annona genus

Etymology

From 'sweet' + 'sop', meaning a sweet fruit used as a sop (a piece of bread dipped in liquid), referring to the fruit's soft edible pulp.

2. NOUN

/ ˈswiːtˌsɑp / (AmE), / ˈswiːtˌsɒp / (BrE)
A tropical fruit with a sweet, soft pulp, thick scaly rind, and shiny black seeds, also known as sugar apple.
tropical fruit with sweet pulp and scaly rind

Examples

The sweetsop is prized for its creamy, custard-like flesh and sweet taste.
She bought a sweetsop from the market to try a new tropical fruit.
Although the sweetsop looks unusual, its flavor is delicious and refreshing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sugar apple - another name for sweetsop, a tropical fruit with sweet pulp and scaly rind

Etymology

From 'sweet' + 'sop', referring to its sweet, soft flesh.
