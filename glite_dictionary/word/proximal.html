proximal

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɑksɪməl / (AmE), / ˈprɒksɪməl / (BrE)
Situated nearer to the center of the body or the point of attachment.
nearer to the center or origin
medical

Examples

The proximal end of the femur connects to the hip socket.
An injury to the proximal portion of the nerve may affect function throughout the limb.
Doctors examined the proximal segment of the intestine for signs of disease.

Synset Examples

the proximal end of a bone

Related Terms

  • Antonym: distal - situated away from the center or point of attachment

Etymology

From Latin proximus (nearest, next).
