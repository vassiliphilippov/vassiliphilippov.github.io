aerosol

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛr.əˌsɑl / (AmE), / ˈeə.rəˌsɒl / (BrE)
A container in which a substance is held under pressure and released as a fine spray, usually by means of a propellant gas.
container for spray under pressure

Examples

She used an aerosol to spray the paint evenly onto the wall.
Air fresheners are often sold in aerosol containers for convenience.
Always shake the aerosol well before applying the product.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spray can - a can or bottle with a nozzle for spraying liquid

Etymology

From Greek 'aēr' (air) and Latin 'solutio' (solution), referring to particles suspended in air.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛr.əˌsɑl / (AmE), / ˈeə.rə.sɒl / (BrE)
A suspension of fine solid particles or liquid droplets in a gas, such as air.
suspension of particles in a gas

Examples

Airborne viruses can travel in tiny aerosols produced when a person coughs or sneezes.
The laboratory measured the concentration of dust aerosols in the atmosphere following the eruption.
Household sprays release chemical aerosols that may contribute to air pollution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mist - tiny droplets of water suspended in air
  • Synonym: smoke - solid particles suspended in air due to burning

Etymology

From French 'aéro-' (air) and 'sol' (solution), coined in the 20th century.
