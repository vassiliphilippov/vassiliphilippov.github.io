affixial

1. ADJ

/ əˈfɪksiəl / (AmE), / əˈfɪksiəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a linguistic affix; involving or pertaining to the use of affixes
relating to linguistic affixes

Examples

The affixial elements in the word 'happiness' include both the prefix and the suffix.
Several affixial changes can alter the grammatical category of a word.
Linguists study affixial processes to understand word formation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affix - a morpheme added to a word to modify its meaning

Etymology

From 'affix' + '-ial', with '-ial' forming adjectives meaning 'relating to'.
