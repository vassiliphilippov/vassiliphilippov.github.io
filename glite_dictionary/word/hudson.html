Hudson

1. PROPN

/ ˈhʌd.sən / (AmE), / ˈhʌd.sən / (BrE)
a major river in eastern New York State flowing south into New York Bay, noted for its historical significance and exploration by Henry Hudson in the early 17th century
river in eastern New York, USA

Examples

The Hudson played an essential role in the economic development of New York State.
We took a scenic cruise along the Hudson and enjoyed breathtaking views of the city skyline.
Henry Hudson explored the Hudson in 1609 while searching for a passage to Asia.

Etymology

Named after the English explorer Henry Hudson (died 1611), who navigated the river in 1609.
