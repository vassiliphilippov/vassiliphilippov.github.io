ninepenny

1. ADJ

/ ˈnaɪnˌpɛni / (AmE), / ˈnaɪnpəni / (BrE)
(of nails) having a size of 2 3/4 inches (7 cm) long; used to describe a standard nail size.
of nails: 2 3/4 inches (7 cm) long

Examples

The carpenter used ninepenny nails to fasten the wooden boards together.
Make sure you buy ninepenny nails for this type of framing work.
The instructions specifically call for ninepenny nails to ensure a sturdy construction.

Etymology

From the historical British pricing system, where 'ninepenny nail' referred to a nail costing nine pence per hundred.
