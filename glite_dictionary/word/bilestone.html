bilestone

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪlˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈbaɪlˌstəʊn / (BrE)
a hard mass (calculus) formed in the gall bladder or its ducts; a gallstone
a stone formed in the gall bladder or ducts
medical

Examples

The surgeon removed a bilestone that was blocking the patient's bile duct.
Pain in the upper abdomen can be a symptom of a bilestone.
Ultrasound revealed several small bilestones in her gall bladder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gallstone - a small, hard crystalline mass in the gall bladder

Etymology

Formed from 'bile' and 'stone'.
