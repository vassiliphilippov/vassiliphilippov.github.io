adulterator

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʌltəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / əˈdʌltəreɪtə / (BrE)
a substance that reduces the purity or effectiveness of another substance
substance that reduces purity or effectiveness

Examples

The laboratory test detected an adulterator in the food sample.
Strict quality controls are needed to prevent any adulterator from entering the drug supply.
Removal of every adulterator is crucial for the safety of the final product.

Synset Examples

it is necessary to remove the adulterants before use

Related Terms

  • Antonym: purifier - a substance that removes impurities

Etymology

From Latin 'adulterare' (to corrupt), through Middle English and Old French.

2. NOUN

/ əˈdʌltəreɪtər / (AmE), / əˈdʌltəreɪtə / (BrE)
a person or thing that reduces the purity or effectiveness of a substance by adding inferior or prohibited ingredients
person or thing that adulterates

Examples

The health inspector caught the adulterator mixing chemicals into the olive oil.
As an adulterator of medicines, he harmed many unsuspecting people.
Strict laws punish any adulterator who contaminates food products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adulterate - to make something poorer in quality by adding another substance
  • Antonym: purifier - person or thing that removes impurities

Etymology

From Latin 'adulterare' (to corrupt, defile) via English 'adulterate'.
