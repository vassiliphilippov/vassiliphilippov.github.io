homebound

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊmˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmˌbaʊnd / (BrE)
People who are confined to their homes, especially because of illness or disability.
people confined to their homes

Examples

The local charity provides meals for the homebound who cannot shop for themselves.
Social workers visit the homebound regularly to ensure their well-being.
Many homebound suffer from feelings of isolation and loneliness.

Etymology

From 'home' + 'bound', meaning 'confined to or unable to leave home'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊmˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmˌbaʊnd / (BrE)
unable to leave one's home, typically because of illness, disability, or old age.
unable to leave home (often due to illness)

Examples

After her surgery, Mary was homebound for several weeks.
Meals on Wheels delivers food to homebound seniors in the community.
As his arthritis worsened, he became increasingly homebound and relied on daily assistance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: housebound - unable to leave one's house, especially because of illness or infirmity

Etymology

Formed from 'home' + 'bound', meaning restricted or confined to home.
