bushing

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʊʃɪŋ / (BrE)
an insulating sleeve or lining inserted into an opening to protect or insulate an electric conductor passing through it
insulating lining for conductors

Examples

A bushing is used to prevent electrical shorts where the wire passes through the metal panel.
The electrician installed a bushing to insulate the power cable at the entry point.
Replacing the damaged bushing improved the safety of the equipment.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʊʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʊʃɪŋ / (BrE)
A cylindrical metal lining fitted into a hole to reduce friction and wear, or to constrain or guide moving parts.
cylindrical metal lining to reduce friction

Examples

The mechanic replaced the worn-out bushing in the car's suspension system.
A nylon bushing helps the shaft rotate smoothly within the housing.
Engineers chose a bronze bushing for its durability and low friction properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bearing - a part that supports and reduces friction in a moving part

Etymology

From 'bush' (metal lining) + -ing; 'bush' from Dutch 'bus' (tube, box).
