lease

1. NOUN

/ lis / (AmE), / liːs / (BrE)
property or land that is leased or rented to another person or party
property that is rented out

Examples

The farmland was available only as a lease from the local council.
After inheriting the estate, she decided to sell the lease on the apartment building.
They inspected several commercial properties before signing a lease for the warehouse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rental - an amount paid or property rented for use

Etymology

From Old French *lais*, from Latin *laxare* (to loosen).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13269292-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ liːs / (AmE), / liːs / (BrE)
a period of time for which a property, building, or piece of land is rented, according to a contract
fixed term of a rental contract

Examples

Our apartment lease expires at the end of July, so we need to decide whether to renew.
She signed a five-year lease for the new office space downtown.
The company terminated its lease early due to relocation plans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tenancy - the period during which a tenant has possession of a property
  • Antonym: ownership - the state or fact of owning something, often contrasting with leasehold

Etymology

From Middle English *les*, from Anglo-Norman *les*, from Old French *lais*, from *laisser* (to leave, let).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15299849-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ lis / (AmE), / liːs / (BrE)
a legal contract in which one party gives another the right to use property or land for a specified period in exchange for payment
contract to use property for payment
legal

Examples

The company signed a lease on the new office building for five years.
Before moving in, you must agree to the terms of the lease.
Their lease expires at the end of the month, so they are looking for a new apartment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rental agreement - a document forming the terms for renting property
  • Synonym: rent - payment made periodically by a tenant to a landlord for use of land or property
  • Antonym: sell - to transfer ownership of property in exchange for money

Etymology

From Old French *lais*, from Latin *laxare* (to loosen, let).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06535123-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ lis / (AmE), / liːs / (BrE)
to allow someone to use property or land in exchange for payment, usually for a fixed period
let property for payment

Examples

They decided to lease their house while they worked overseas for a year.
The company leases office space in the city center to small businesses.
She leased her apartment to a couple of university students.

Synset Examples

We rented our apartment to friends while we were abroad

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rent - to allow someone to use property or land in return for regular payments

Etymology

From Old French *laisser* (to let, leave) and Medieval Latin *laxare* (to let go, release).
