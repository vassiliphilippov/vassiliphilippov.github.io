tempest-tost

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɛmpəst tɔst / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpɪst tɒst / (BrE)
Battered or buffeted by storms or severe difficulties; pounded repeatedly by adversity.
battered by storms or troubles
archaic

Examples

The tempest-tost sailors finally reached a safe harbor after months at sea.
He welcomed the tempest-tost strangers with compassion and warmth.
Through years of tempest-tost hardship, she never lost hope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storm-tossed - buffeted or thrown about by storms
  • Antonym: serene - calm and untroubled

Etymology

From 'tempest', meaning storm, and archaic past participle of 'toss'. Popularized by Emma Lazarus's poem, 'The New Colossus' (1883).
