allis

1. NOUN

/ ˈælɪs / (AmE), / ˈælɪs / (BrE)
a European river fish of the herring family, also known as the European shad (Alosa alosa), found in coastal waters and rivers during spawning
European shad, a type of river fish

Examples

Fishermen in the region often catch allis during the spawning season.
The population of allis has declined due to river pollution and overfishing.
An allis can be recognized by its silvery appearance and size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shad - a common name for several species of fish in the herring family

Etymology

From French 'alose', ultimately from Latin 'alosa', referring to the shad.
