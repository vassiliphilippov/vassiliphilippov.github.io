Siberian

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈbɪriən / (AmE), / saɪˈbɪəriən / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of Siberia.
someone from Siberia

Examples

She met a Siberian who told her fascinating stories about life in the region.
Many Siberians are adapted to extremely cold climates.
As a proud Siberian, he often spoke about his homeland's traditions.

Etymology

From 'Siberia,' the name of the region in Russia, + -an suffix denoting origin/nationality.

2. ADJ

/ saɪˈbɪriən / (AmE), / saɪˈbɪəriən / (BrE)
Relating to Siberia or its people, culture, or natural features.
of or relating to Siberia or its people

Examples

The Siberian wilderness is known for its harsh winters and remote beauty.
Many Siberian tigers are now protected in wildlife reserves.
A Siberian scientist helped discover a new species of plant.

Synset Examples

Siberian natural resources
Siberian coal miners
the Siberian tundra

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Russian - of or relating to Russia or its people

Etymology

From Siberia, the name of the region in Russia, with English adjectival suffix -an.
