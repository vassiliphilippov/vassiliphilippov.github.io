infirmary

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɜrməri / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɜːməri / (BrE)
a room or building in a school, prison, or other institution where people who are ill or injured are given care and treatment
medical care room or facility

Examples

The school infirmary was equipped to handle minor injuries and illnesses.
After his surgery, he spent a week recovering in the infirmary.
Prisoners with flu-like symptoms were immediately sent to the infirmary for examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hospital - an institution providing medical and surgical treatment and nursing care for sick or injured people

Etymology

From Latin infirmaria (a place for the sick), from infirmus (weak, infirm).
