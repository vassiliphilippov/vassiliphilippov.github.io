arability

1. NOUN

/ ˌær.əˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌær.əˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of land being suitable for growing crops or cultivation
suitability of land for cultivation

Examples

Soil arability determines whether farmers can grow wheat in a given region.
Changes in rainfall may affect the arability of marginal lands.
Researchers conducted a study on the arability of reclaimed mining sites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arable - (of land) suitable for growing crops

Etymology

From Latin 'arare' (to plough) + '-ability'.
