Breughel the Elder

1. PROPN

/ ˈbrɔɪɡəl ði ˈɛldər / (AmE), / ˈbrɔɪɡl̩ ði ˈɛldə / (BrE)
(1525–1569) A Flemish painter renowned for his landscapes and peasant scenes, regarded as one of the greatest painters of the Northern Renaissance.
Flemish painter, 16th century

Examples

The works of Breughel the Elder are famous for their detailed depictions of peasant life.
Art historians often compare Breughel the Elder with other Renaissance masters.
A major exhibition of paintings by Breughel the Elder was held at the museum.

Etymology

From Dutch, Pieter Bruegel; 'the Elder' distinguishes him from his son.
