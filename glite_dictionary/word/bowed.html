bowed

1. ADJ

/ boʊd / (AmE), / bəʊd / (BrE)
(of the legs) curving outward at the knees, so that the knees are further apart and the lower legs curve outward.
curving outward at the knees (legs)

Examples

The child had bowed legs from a vitamin D deficiency.
His bowed legs gave him a distinctive walk.
Some athletes develop slightly bowed legs due to the nature of their training.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: knock-kneed - having knees that touch or are close together and legs that curve inward
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ boʊd / (AmE), / bəʊd / (BrE)
(of a stringed instrument) played or sounded by drawing a bow across its strings.
played with a bow (of stringed instrument)

Examples

The violinist chose a bowed passage to emphasize the melody.
Unlike the guitar, the cello is typically bowed rather than plucked.
The composer specified that the section should switch from bowed to pizzicato.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plucked - (of a stringed instrument) sounded by pulling and releasing the strings with the fingers or a plectrum

Etymology

From the noun 'bow' (a curved, flexible rod with horsehair, used for playing stringed instruments) + -ed.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00948720-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ baʊd / (AmE), / baʊd / (BrE)
Displaying an attitude or manner that is excessively humble or deferential.
excessively deferential in manner

Examples

He entered the room with a bowed head, barely meeting anyone's gaze.
Her bowed demeanor made it clear she would not challenge authority.
The servant's bowed attitude irritated his proud employer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submissive - willing to submit to others; meek or obedient
  • Antonym: assertive - having or showing a confident and forceful personality

Etymology

Past participle of 'bow', meaning to bend respectfully; in extended use for demeanour.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00792947-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ boʊd / (AmE), / bəʊd / (BrE)
curved so as to form or resemble an arch
curved in an arch shape

Examples

The old bridge had a bowed structure that added to its elegance.
After years of pressure, the wooden beam became slightly bowed.
They admired the bowed branches arching gracefully over the pathway.

Synset Examples

an arched ceiling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arched - having a curved shape like an arch
  • Antonym: straight - not curved or bent; having no bends or curves

Etymology

From bow (to bend) + -ed (past participle/adjective ending). Root from Old English 'būgan' (to bend).
