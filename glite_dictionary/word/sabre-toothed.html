sabre-toothed

1. ADJ

/ ˈseɪbər tuːθt / (AmE), / ˈseɪbə tuːθt / (BrE)
(of an animal) having long, curved, saber-shaped canine teeth.
having long, saber-shaped canine teeth

Examples

The sabre-toothed cat was one of the most formidable predators of its era.
Scientists discovered fossils of a sabre-toothed animal in the tar pits.
Many museums have impressive sabre-toothed skulls on display.

Synset Examples

a saber-toothed tiger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saber-toothed - Alternate spelling, chiefly American English

Etymology

From 'sabre' (a type of curved sword) + 'toothed', referring to sword-like teeth.
