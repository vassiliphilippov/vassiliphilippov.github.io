James Whitcomb Riley

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒeɪmz ˈwɪtkʌm ˈraɪli / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪmz ˈwɪtkəm ˈraɪli / (BrE)
(1849–1916) An American poet known for his poems in dialect and for works celebrating rural and Midwestern life.
American poet (1849–1916)

Examples

Many schoolchildren in Indiana study the poetry of James Whitcomb Riley in literature class.
The house where James Whitcomb Riley lived is now a historic museum.
His poem 'Little Orphant Annie' is one of James Whitcomb Riley's most famous works.
