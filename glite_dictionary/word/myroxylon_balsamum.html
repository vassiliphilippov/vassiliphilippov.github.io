Myroxylon balsamum

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈrɑksɪlɒn ˈbælsəməm / (AmE), / maɪˈrɒksɪlən ˈbælsəməm / (BrE)
A medium-sized tropical American tree that produces tolu balsam and yields fragrant, hard wood used in high-quality furniture and cabinetwork.
tropical tree yielding tolu balsam and hard wood

Examples

The fragrant wood of Myroxylon balsamum is often used to make fine cabinetry.
Tolu balsam, extracted from Myroxylon balsamum, is valued in perfumery and medicine.
Many conservationists advocate protecting Myroxylon balsamum due to its ecological and economic importance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balsam - an aromatic resinous substance from plants

Etymology

From Greek 'myron' (ointment, balsam) and 'xylon' (wood); 'balsamum' from Latin 'balsamum' (balsam).
