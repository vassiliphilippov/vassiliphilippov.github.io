self-destruction

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf.dɪˈstrʌk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌself.dɪˈstrʌk.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of intentionally causing one's own death; suicide
the act of killing oneself

Examples

After years of battling depression, he was at risk of self-destruction.
The film explores the tragic consequences of self-destruction and addiction.
She overcame her tendency toward self-destruction with therapy and support.

Synset Examples

it is a crime to commit suicide

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suicide - the act of intentionally killing oneself

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'destruction', derived from Latin 'destructio' (a pulling down).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf dɪˈstrʌkʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf dɪˈstrʌkʃən / (BrE)
the act of deliberately causing harm to oneself or ruining one's own interests
the act of destroying oneself

Examples

His reckless behavior was clearly a form of self-destruction.
By sabotaging her own career, she engaged in quiet self-destruction.
Addiction can be a slow process of self-destruction.

Synset Examples

his insistence was pure self-destruction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suicide - the act of intentionally killing oneself
  • Antonym: self-preservation - the act of keeping oneself alive and safe

Etymology

From 'self' (one's own person) + 'destruction' (the act of destroying), originally from Latin 'destructio'.
