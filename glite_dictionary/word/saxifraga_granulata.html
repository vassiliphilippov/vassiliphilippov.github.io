Saxifraga granulata

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæksɪˈfreɪɡə ɡrəˈnjuːlətə / (AmE), / ˌsæksɪˈfrɑːɡə ɡrəˈnjuːlətə / (BrE)
A perennial European plant with rosette-shaped leaves and compact clusters of white flowers; commonly known as meadow saxifrage.
a European perennial with white flowers

Examples

The Saxifraga granulata bloomed splendidly in the wildflower meadow each spring.
Gardeners often plant Saxifraga granulata for its delicate clusters of white flowers.
She identified the species as Saxifraga granulata by its characteristic rosette of leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meadow saxifrage - Common name for Saxifraga granulata, a European plant with white flowers

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Saxifraga' from saxum (rock) + frangere (to break), referring to the plant's ability to grow in cracks; 'granulata' from Latin granulum (little grain), alluding to the small tubers.
