to catholicize

1. VERB

/ ˈkæθ.ə.lɪ.saɪz / (AmE), / ˈkaθ.ə.lɪ.saɪz / (BrE)
to cause someone or something to adopt Catholic beliefs or practices; to convert to Catholicism
convert to Catholicism

Examples

The missionaries hoped to catholicize the indigenous population through education and outreach.
During the reign of the new monarch, there was an attempt to catholicize the entire country.
Some historians debate how successful the government was in its effort to catholicize the region.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: protestantize - cause to adopt Protestant beliefs
  • Synonym: convert - cause someone to change their religion

Etymology

From Catholic + -ize, patterned after similar verbs.
