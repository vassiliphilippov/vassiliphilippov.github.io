cuddle

1. VERB

/ ˈkʌd.əl / (AmE), / ˈkʌd.əl / (BrE)
to move or arrange oneself into a comfortable and cozy position, often for warmth or security
settle comfortably and cozily

Examples

On chilly nights, I like to cuddle under a thick blanket with a good book.
The cat would cuddle into the corner of the couch whenever there was a storm.
After the long hike, she found a soft chair and cuddled into it to rest.

Synset Examples

We cuddled against each other to keep warm
The children snuggled into their sleeping bags

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snuggle - settle or move into a warm, comfortable position
  • Synonym: to nestle - settle or lie comfortably within or against something
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈkʌdəl / (AmE), / ˈkʌd(ə)l / (BrE)
to hold someone or something close in one's arms to show affection, comfort, or for warmth
hold close for affection or warmth

Examples

The little boy wanted his mother to cuddle him after he woke up from his nap.
They sat on the couch and cuddled together while watching the movie.
She loves cuddling her cat on cold winter nights.

Synset Examples

I cuddled the baby

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hug - to hold closely in one's arms, especially as a sign of affection
  • Synonym: snuggle - to settle or move into a warm, comfortable, and cozy position

Etymology

Possibly from the obsolete verb 'cuddle,' frequentative of 'cud' (to embrace), of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01428162-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈkʌd.əl / (AmE), / ˈkʌd.əl / (BrE)
a close and affectionate embrace, often for comfort or warmth
affectionate embrace

Examples

The mother gave her baby a gentle cuddle before bedtime.
After a long day, a warm cuddle on the couch was all she needed.
The puppy looked for a cuddle when it was scared by the thunderstorm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hug - a close embrace with the arms, typically to show affection

Etymology

Probably from English dialectal 'cuddle' (to embrace), of unknown origin.
