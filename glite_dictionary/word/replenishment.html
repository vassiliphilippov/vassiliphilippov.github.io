replenishment

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈplɛnɪʃmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈplɛnɪʃmənt / (BrE)
the act of filling something again by supplying what has been used up
the act of refilling or restocking

Examples

Regular replenishment of the water supply is essential in dry climates.
The store scheduled a replenishment of their popular items every Friday.
Following heavy use, timely replenishment of medical supplies was crucial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refill - to fill something again
  • Antonym: depletion - the reduction in the quantity of something

Etymology

From Middle English 'replenishen', from Old French 'repleniss-', from Latin 're-' (again) + 'plenus' (full).
