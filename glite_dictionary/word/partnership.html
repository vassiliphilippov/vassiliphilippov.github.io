partnership

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrtnərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtnəʃɪp / (BrE)
a contract between two or more people who agree to combine their resources, skills, and share any resulting profits or losses
business contract for shared resources and profit/loss

Examples

They entered into a partnership to open a new restaurant downtown.
Each partnership must file annual tax returns documenting shared profits and losses.
After the dissolution of their partnership, both lawyers started independent practices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: company - an organization engaged in commercial business
  • Antonym: sole proprietorship - a business owned and run by one person

Etymology

From partner + -ship; partner from Old French 'parçonier' and -ship from Old English '-scipe'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrtnərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtnəʃɪp / (BrE)
a cooperative relationship between people or groups who agree to share responsibility to achieve a specific goal
cooperative relationship for a common goal

Examples

The research project was made possible through a partnership between the university and several technology companies.
The city entered into a partnership with local artists to beautify public spaces.
Strong partnership between parents and teachers can improve students' academic performance.

Synset Examples

effective language learning is a partnership between school, teacher and student
the action teams worked in partnership with the government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alliance - a union or association formed for mutual benefit
  • Synonym: collaboration - the action of working with someone to produce something
  • Antonym: rivalry - competition for the same objective or superiority

Etymology

From Middle English 'partenershippe', from Old French 'partenaire' (partner) + '-ship'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrtnərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtnəʃɪp / (BrE)
the group of people who are the members of a business venture formed by contract
members of a business venture
legal

Examples

The accounting firm is one of the oldest partnerships in the city.
All six lawyers became part of the partnership after the merger.
Our partnership decided to expand into new markets this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: company - a commercial business; a firm
  • Synonym: corporation - a large company or group of companies authorized to act as a single entity
  • Antonym: sole proprietor - an individual who owns and runs a business alone

Etymology

From Middle English 'partenership', from 'partner' + '-ship', influenced by Old French 'partenaire'.
