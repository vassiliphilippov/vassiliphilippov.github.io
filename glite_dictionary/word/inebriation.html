inebriation

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌniːbriˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌniːbrɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
A temporary state of intoxication caused by excessive consumption of alcohol.
state of intoxication from alcohol

Examples

After several drinks, his inebriation became apparent to everyone at the party.
She regretted her actions during last night's inebriation.
Laws against public inebriation are strictly enforced in this city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intoxication - the state of being intoxicated, especially by alcohol
  • Antonym: sobriety - the state of being sober, not affected by alcohol

Etymology

From Latin inebriatio (drunkenness), from inebriare (to intoxicate).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌniː.briˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌniː.brɪˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a state of intoxication caused by alcohol; habitual or excessive drinking leading to dependence and health problems
state of alcohol intoxication or addiction

Examples

The doctor explained that chronic inebriation can lead to serious health complications.
After years of inebriation, he finally sought help to overcome his addiction.
Her reckless inebriation at social events often worried her friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intoxication - the state of being under the influence of alcohol or other drugs
  • Antonym: sobriety - the state of not being affected by alcohol; opposite of inebriation

Etymology

From Latin inebriatio (drunkenness), from inebriare (to intoxicate).
