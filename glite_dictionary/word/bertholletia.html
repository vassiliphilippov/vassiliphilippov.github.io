Bertholletia

1. NOUN

/ bɜrˌθɒˈliːtiə / (AmE), / bɜːˌθɒˈliːtɪə / (BrE)
A genus of South American trees in the family Lecythidaceae, best known for the Brazil nut tree (Bertholletia excelsa).
genus of the Brazil nut tree

Examples

The Bertholletia genus includes the tree responsible for producing Brazil nuts.
Scientists are studying the growth patterns of Bertholletia in the Amazon rainforest.
Conservation efforts have focused on protecting wild populations of Bertholletia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brazil nut - the edible seed of Bertholletia excelsa

Etymology

Named after French chemist Claude Louis Berthollet (1748–1822).
