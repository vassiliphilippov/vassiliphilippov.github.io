inland

1. ADV

/ ˈɪnˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈɪnlənd / (BrE)
towards or into the interior of a country or region, away from the coast or border.
towards the interior of a region

Examples

We drove inland for several hours before reaching the mountain village.
After landing at the port, the goods were transported inland by train.
Tropical storms often lose strength as they move inland from the sea.

Synset Examples

the town is five miles inland

Etymology

From Old English 'inlond' (the interior of a land).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00259792-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪnˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈɪnlənd / (BrE)
(of a place) situated away from the coast or the border of a country or region.
situated away from the coast or border

Examples

The town is several miles inland from the nearest beach.
Farmers in inland regions often face different climate challenges than those near the sea.
She moved to an inland city for work, leaving the coast behind.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: coastal - located near or on a coast

Etymology

From Old English 'innan' (inside) + 'land' (land).
