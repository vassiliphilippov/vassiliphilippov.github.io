sabbatic

1. ADJ

/ səˈbætɪk / (AmE), / səˈbætɪk / (BrE)
relating to or appropriate to the Sabbath; of or pertaining to a day or period of rest observed for religious reasons
relating to the Sabbath or a religious day of rest

Examples

Many religious traditions have a sabbatic day dedicated to prayer and rest.
The family observed a sabbatic silence from sunset on Friday until sunset on Saturday.
His sabbatic duties included leading the evening service at the synagogue.

Synset Examples

Friday is a sabbatical day for Muslims

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sabbatical - relating to a period of rest or leave, especially for study or travel
  • Antonym: profane - not relating to what is sacred or religious

Etymology

From Late Latin 'sabbaticus', from Greek 'sabbatikoʊs', from 'sabbaton' (Sabbath).
