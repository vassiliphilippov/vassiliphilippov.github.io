goblin

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑːblɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡɒblɪn / (BrE)
(in folklore) a small, grotesque, mischievous creature that is said to cause trouble for humans.
mischievous creature from folklore

Examples

According to legend, a goblin haunted the old castle and played tricks on the villagers.
The children dressed up as a goblin for Halloween, complete with pointy ears and a crooked nose.
In many fantasy novels, a goblin is portrayed as a sneaky and troublesome creature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elf - a supernatural creature of folk tales, typically depicted as small and elusive
  • Synonym: sprite - an elf-like, fairy creature in folklore
  • Synonym: troll - a mythical creature of Scandinavian folklore, often antagonistic to humans

Etymology

From Middle English *gobelin*, possibly from Old French *gobelin*, of uncertain origin.
