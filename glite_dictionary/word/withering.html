withering

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪðərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪðərɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of becoming weak or deteriorated, especially as a result of neglect or lack of use
weakening or degeneration

Examples

The withering of his skills was evident after years away from the sport.
Some plants exhibit withering of leaves during periods of drought.
She feared the withering of her creativity due to her monotonous routine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deterioration - the process of becoming progressively worse
  • Synonym: decay - gradual destruction or decline
  • Antonym: strengthening - the process of becoming stronger

Etymology

From the verb 'wither', from Middle English 'widren', possibly of Scandinavian origin.
