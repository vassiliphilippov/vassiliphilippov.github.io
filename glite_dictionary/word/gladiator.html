gladiator

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlædiˌeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɡlædɪeɪtə / (BrE)
(in ancient Rome) a man trained to fight with weapons against other men or wild animals in an arena for public entertainment.
armed combatant in ancient Rome

Examples

The ancient Roman gladiator fought bravely in the Colosseum, facing both wild animals and skilled opponents.
Many gladiators were slaves or prisoners, forced to entertain the crowds with deadly combat.
The movie depicted the life of a gladiator seeking freedom through victory in the arena.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fighter - a person who fights, especially as a profession
  • Antonym: pacifist - a person who believes that war and violence are unjustifiable

Etymology

From Latin gladiator, from gladius (sword).
