AAC

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ ˌeɪ ˈsi / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˌeɪ ˈsiː / (BrE)
a lightweight, precast concrete building material that provides structure, insulation, and resistance to fire and mold.
lightweight, insulating concrete building material

Examples

The contractor recommends using AAC blocks for better thermal insulation in the new building.
Because AAC is so lightweight, it reduces construction time and labor costs.
Many modern homes incorporate AAC panels for both their strength and energy efficiency.

Synset Examples

Better thermal efficiency of AAC makes it suitable for use in areas with extreme temperature as it eliminates need for separate materials for construction and insulation leading to faster construction and savings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autoclaved aerated concrete - a lightweight, precast form of concrete made with an aerating agent

Etymology

Abbreviation of autoclaved aerated concrete.
