sympathetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɪmpəˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɪmpəˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or controlled by the sympathetic nervous system, which is part of the autonomic nervous system and is involved in the body's response to stress or emergency situations.
related to the sympathetic nervous system
medical

Examples

Sympathetic stimulation causes an increase in heart rate and blood pressure.
Damage to the sympathetic nerves can disrupt normal sweating patterns.
Activation of sympathetic neurons prepares the body for a fight-or-flight response.

Synset Examples

sympathetic neurons
sympathetic stimulation

Etymology

From Greek sympathetikos, from sympatheia (sympathy).

WordNet ID: oewn-03019521-a
Synset POS: a

showing understanding of others' feelings, being kind, and willing to offer help or support
showing understanding and kindness

Examples

3. ADJ

/ ˌsɪmpəˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɪmpəˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
(of a vibration or oscillation) occurring as a result of vibrations in a nearby object, rather than from direct contact or application of force.
caused by nearby vibrations (in physics, music)

Examples

The strings of the guitar began to move due to sympathetic vibrations from the speaker.
When struck, the bell produced a sympathetic resonance in the nearby glass.
Certain musical instruments use sympathetic strings to enrich the sound.

Synset Examples

sympathetic vibration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resonant - (of a sound or vibration) increasing amplitude due to matching frequency
