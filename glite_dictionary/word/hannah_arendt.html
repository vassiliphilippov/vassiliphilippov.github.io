Hannah Arendt

1. PROPN

/ ˈhænə ɑːrˈɛnt / (AmE), / ˈhanə ɑːˈrɛnt / (BrE)
(1906–1975) German-born American political philosopher and historian noted for her works on totalitarianism, authority, and the nature of power.
German-born American political philosopher (1906–1975)

Examples

Hannah Arendt famously wrote about the origins of totalitarianism in the twentieth century.
Many universities offer seminars focused on Hannah Arendt and her political philosophy.
The works of Hannah Arendt continue to influence debates on authority and power.
