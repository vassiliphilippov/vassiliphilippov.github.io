mortarboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrtərˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtəbɔːd / (BrE)
a stiff, square academic cap with a flat top and a tassel, worn for graduation and other academic ceremonies
square academic cap with tassel

Examples

She adjusted her mortarboard before walking onto the stage to receive her diploma.
All the graduates tossed their mortarboards into the air at the end of the ceremony.
His mortarboard slipped off as he hugged his family after graduation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cap - a type of soft, flat hat

Etymology

From its resemblance to a mason’s mortarboard (a board for carrying mortar).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrtərˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtəbɔːd / (BrE)
A flat, square board with a handle underneath, used by masons for holding or carrying mortar.
mason's board for holding mortar

Examples

The mason scooped mortar from the mortarboard and spread it on the brick.
Before starting the wall, he loaded the mortarboard with fresh cement.
After hours of use, the surface of the mortarboard was coated with dried mortar.
