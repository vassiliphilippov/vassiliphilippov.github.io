gean

1. NOUN

/ dʒiːæn / (AmE), / dʒiːən / (BrE)
a wild or seedling sweet cherry tree (Prunus avium), often used as a stock for grafting cultivated cherries
wild sweet cherry tree used for grafting

Examples

The old orchard was home to a solitary gean that bloomed every spring.
Gardeners often use gean as the rootstock for cultivated cherry varieties.
A mature gean can provide shelter and food for many kinds of wildlife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild cherry - a cherry tree growing uncultivated in the wild

Etymology

From Scots *gean*, from Old French *guigne* (wild cherry).
