deficiency

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɪʃənsi / (AmE), / dɪˈfɪʃənsi / (BrE)
The state of not having enough of something that is necessary; an insufficiency or shortage.
lack or insufficiency of something

Examples

A vitamin C deficiency can lead to serious health problems.
The project was delayed due to a deficiency of skilled workers.
There is a deficiency in funding for public schools in some areas.

Synset Examples

the inadequacy of unemployment benefits

Related Terms

  • Antonym: surplus - an excess amount or quantity
  • Synonym: shortage - a state in which there is not enough of something

Etymology

From Latin 'deficientia', from 'deficere' (to lack, fail).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɪʃənsi / (AmE), / dɪˈfɪʃənsi / (BrE)
the state of not having enough of something that is necessary or required; a lack or shortage
a lack or shortage of something needed

Examples

The company suffered from a serious deficiency of skilled workers.
A vitamin D deficiency can lead to health problems.
Farmers in the region struggle with a deficiency of rainfall.

Synset Examples

there is a serious lack of insight into the problem
water is the critical deficiency in desert regions
for want of a nail the shoe was lost

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lack - the state of not having or not having enough of something
  • Antonym: surplus - an amount that is more than is needed

Etymology

From Latin 'deficientia', from 'deficere' meaning 'to fail, be lacking'.
