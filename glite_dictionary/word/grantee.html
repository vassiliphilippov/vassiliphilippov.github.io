grantee

1. NOUN

/ ɡrænˈti / (AmE), / ɡrænˈtiː / (BrE)
A person or organization that receives a grant, especially in legal or official contexts.
recipient of a grant
legal

Examples

The university announced that each grantee must submit a final report by the end of the year.
As a grantee, she was responsible for managing the funds according to the terms of the contract.
All grantees will be notified of the decision by email next month.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: grantor - the person or entity who gives a grant

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman and Old French 'graunter' (to grant) + '-ee' (denoting the person to whom something is granted).

2. NOUN

/ ɡrænˈti / (AmE), / ɡranˈtiː / (BrE)
a person to whom the title or rights to property are transferred, especially by a legal document
person receiving property/title
legal

Examples

The grantee must sign all the necessary documents to receive the deed.
Once the contract is finalized, the property will legally belong to the grantee.
In the agreement, the grantee acquires full ownership rights from the grantor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: grantor - a person or entity who transfers property or rights to another

Etymology

From Anglo-French grante, based on grant (meaning 'give, transfer') plus -ee, denoting the recipient.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
