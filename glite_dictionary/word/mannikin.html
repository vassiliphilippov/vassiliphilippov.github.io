mannikin

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænɪkɪn / (AmE), / ˈmænɪkɪn / (BrE)
a person who is very small in size but not otherwise deformed or abnormal
very small person (not otherwise abnormal)

Examples

The circus featured a mannikin who amazed crowds with his agility.
Despite being a mannikin, she had an enormous presence on stage.
In folklore, a mannikin often appears as a magical helper to the protagonist.

Etymology

From Dutch 'mannetje', diminutive of 'man' (man).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmænɪkɪn / (AmE), / ˈmænɪkɪn / (BrE)
a life-size dummy shaped like a human, used to display clothes in shops or for fitting garments
life-size dummy for displaying clothes

Examples

The fashion designer dressed the mannikin in the latest collection for the store window display.
She adjusted the sleeves on the mannikin to make the garment fall perfectly.
After hours, the mannikins stood silently by the shopfront, each wearing a different outfit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mannequin - a life-size dummy used to display clothes

Etymology

From Dutch 'manneken' (little man), diminutive of 'man' (man). Variant of 'mannequin.'
