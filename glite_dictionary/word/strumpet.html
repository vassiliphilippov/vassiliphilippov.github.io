strumpet

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrʌmpət / (AmE), / ˈstrʌmpɪt / (BrE)
a woman who is considered sexually promiscuous or an adulterer, often used in a derogatory or archaic sense
sexually promiscuous woman (archaic/derogatory)
archaic offensive

Examples

The old play cast her as a strumpet condemned by society.
He called her a strumpet in a fit of jealousy, shocking everyone present.
Medieval literature often refers to the fallen woman as a strumpet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prostitute - a woman who engages in sexual activity for payment
  • Synonym: harlot - a woman considered to have loose sexual morals; prostitute (archaic)
  • Synonym: adulterer - a person who commits adultery

Etymology

From Middle English 'strumpet', from Old French 'strumpet', probably of Germanic origin.
