loach

1. NOUN

/ loʊtʃ / (AmE), / ləʊtʃ / (BrE)
a slender freshwater fish of Eurasia or Africa, resembling a catfish, typically found in rivers and streams
a slender freshwater fish resembling a catfish

Examples

The aquarium housed several loach species that helped keep the tank clean.
We spotted a loach swimming among the rocks in the shallow stream.
Many aquarists appreciate the loach for its distinctive appearance and active behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catfish - a freshwater fish with whisker-like barbels around the mouth

Etymology

From Middle English loche, from Old French loche (a kind of fish).
