say-so

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪˌsoʊ / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.səʊ / (BrE)
permission or authority to do something, often based only on someone's word or decision; sometimes an arbitrary assertion.
permission or authority; arbitrary assertion
informal

Examples

You can't go on the trip without your parents' say-so.
His promotion was granted purely on the manager's say-so.
We started the project on John's say-so, but I wish we'd checked further.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permission - consent or authorization to do something
  • Synonym: authorization - official permission or approval

2. NOUN

/ ˈseɪ ˌsoʊ / (AmE), / ˈseɪ ˌsəʊ / (BrE)
the right or authority to make decisions or give orders
the authority to decide or order
informal

Examples

She finally got the say-so from her manager to proceed with the project.
Nothing gets approved in this department without the boss's say-so.
You can't just spend company money without the proper say-so.

Synset Examples

he has the authority to issue warrants
deputies are given authorization to make arrests
a place of potency in the state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authority - the power or right to give orders, make decisions, or enforce obedience
  • Degree Lower: permission - the act of allowing someone to do something
