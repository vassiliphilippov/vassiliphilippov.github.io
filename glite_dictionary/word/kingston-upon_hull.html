Kingston-upon Hull

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɪŋstən əˌpɒn ˈhʌl / (AmE), / ˈkɪŋstən əˌpɒn ˈhʌl / (BrE)
A city and major fishing port in northeastern England, also known simply as Hull.
city and port in northeastern England
british

Examples

Many cargo ships dock at Kingston-upon Hull before distributing goods throughout the UK.
She grew up in Kingston-upon Hull and still has family there.
The university in Kingston-upon Hull attracts students from all over Europe.

Etymology

From Old English personal name Cyningestun (King's estate) and Old Norse hulr (the name of the river), indicating a royal settlement on the River Hull.
