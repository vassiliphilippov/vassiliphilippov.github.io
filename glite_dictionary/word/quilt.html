quilt

1. VERB

/ kwɪlt / (AmE), / kwɪlt / (BrE)
to sew two or more layers of fabric together with patterns of stitching, often with padding in between
sew layers of fabric together

Examples

She decided to quilt a blanket for her friend's newborn baby.
The artisan will quilt the jacket with a special decorative pattern.
He spent the weekend quilting the layers together to create a warm bedspread.

Synset Examples

quilt the skirt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sew - to join or attach by stitches, especially using a needle and thread

Etymology

From Middle English 'quilte', from Old French, from Latin 'culcita' (mattress, cushion).

2. VERB

/ kwɪlt / (AmE), / kwɪlt / (BrE)
to make (a bed covering or similar item) by stitching together layers of fabric, typically with padding between them
make by stitching layers of fabric

Examples

My grandmother loves to quilt in her spare time, creating beautiful patterns by hand.
The club meets every Tuesday to quilt blankets for the local hospital.
She quilted a cover for her baby using scraps of colorful fabric.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sew - to join or repair something by stitches using a needle and thread
  • Synonym: patch - to mend or put together with pieces of fabric

Etymology

Middle English, from Latin culcita (a mattress or cushion).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01671199-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ kwɪlt / (AmE), / kwɪlt / (BrE)
A warm bed covering made of two layers of fabric, filled with soft material and stitched together, often in decorative patterns.
bed covering of stitched layers

Examples

She placed a beautiful patchwork quilt on the bed for extra warmth.
The old quilt was handmade by my grandmother.
During winter, I sleep under a thick quilt to stay cozy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duvet - a soft quilt, usually with a removable cover, used as a bed covering
  • Degree Lower: blanket - a large piece of soft material used as a bed covering for warmth

Etymology

From Latin 'culcita' (a mattress or cushion), via Middle English and Old French.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04040471-n
Synset POS: n
