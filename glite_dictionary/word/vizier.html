vizier

1. NOUN

/ vɪˈzɪr / (AmE), / vɪˈzɪə / (BrE)
a high-ranking official in certain Muslim countries, especially in the former Ottoman Empire and in some Islamic states, acting as a minister or advisor to the ruler.
high official in Muslim government

Examples

The sultan relied heavily on his vizier for advice on matters of state.
Many stories from the Arabian Nights feature a powerful vizier serving a great king.
In the Ottoman Empire, the grand vizier was often the most powerful man after the sultan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minister - a senior government official, often in charge of a department
  • Degree Higher: prime minister - the chief minister in some governments

Etymology

From Arabic 'wazīr' (helper, adviser), through Turkish and Persian.
