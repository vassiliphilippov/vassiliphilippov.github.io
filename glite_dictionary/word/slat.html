slat

1. VERB

/ slæt / (AmE), / slæt / (BrE)
to fit or cover with slats, or provide with thin narrow strips of wood or metal
fit or cover with slats

Examples

In hot climates, families often slat their windows for better ventilation.
The old bench was slatted to allow rainwater to drain through easily.
Workers plan to slat the rooftop to provide partial shade.

2. NOUN

/ slæt / (AmE), / slæt / (BrE)
a thin, narrow strip of wood, metal, or another material, often used as part of a structure such as a fence, bed, or blind
thin strip of wood or metal

Examples

The wooden slat on the bed frame broke under the heavy mattress.
Sunlight filtered through the slat of the window blind, casting lines on the floor.
Each slat in the fence was painted a different color, creating a cheerful border.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strip - a long, narrow piece of material

Etymology

Perhaps from Old French esclat (splinter, sliver), of Germanic origin.

3. VERB

/ slæt / (AmE), / slæt / (BrE)
to close or cover with slats, especially referring to the adjustable strips on window blinds or shutters
to close window slats

Examples

She quickly slatted the blinds to keep out the harsh sunlight.
If you slat the shutters at night, the room stays cooler in the morning.
He always forgets to slat the window before leaving the office.
