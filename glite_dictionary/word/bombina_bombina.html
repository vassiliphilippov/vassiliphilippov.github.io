Bombina bombina

1. NOUN

/ bɑmˈbiː.nə bɑmˈbiː.nə / (AmE), / bɒmˈbɪn.ə bɒmˈbɪn.ə / (BrE)
A species of toad native to central and eastern Europe, notable for its red or orange patches mixed with black on its underside.
European toad with red-orange underside patches

Examples

The Bombina bombina is often found in shallow, sunlit ponds throughout eastern Europe.
Scientists are monitoring Bombina bombina populations to help protect their natural habitats.
The distinctive coloration of the Bombina bombina warns predators of its toxicity.

Etymology

From Latin, where 'bombina' is a genus name derived from Latin 'bombus' (buzzing, humming), imitative of the frogs' call.
