Oryzopsis hymenoides

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˌraɪˈzɒpsɪs haɪˌmiːnəʊˈiːdiːz / (AmE), / ɒˌrɪˈzɒpsɪs hɪˌmiːnəʊˈiːdiːz / (BrE)
a valuable perennial grass native to dry upland areas and plains of western North America, important as forage for livestock
a western North American forage grass species

Examples

Oryzopsis hymenoides is often planted to restore rangelands in arid regions of the western United States.
She identified the clump of Oryzopsis hymenoides growing near the edge of the canyon.
Ranchers appreciate Oryzopsis hymenoides for its drought tolerance and nutritional value as livestock forage.

Etymology

From genus name Oryzopsis (from Greek 'oryza' meaning rice) and species name hymenoides (from Greek 'hymen' meaning membrane and '-oides' meaning resembling).
