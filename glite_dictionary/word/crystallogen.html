crystallogen

1. NOUN

/ krɪˈstæləˌdʒɛn / (AmE), / krɪˈstæləˌdʒɛn / (BrE)
a chemical element belonging to the carbon group of the periodic table, including carbon, silicon, germanium, tin, lead, and flerovium.
element of the carbon group

Examples

Silicon is a crystallogen that is widely used in the electronics industry.
Each crystallogen shares similar chemical properties due to its position in the periodic table.
Lead, though less common in daily life, is also classified as a crystallogen.

Synset Examples

Carbon, the lightest carbon group element, sublimates at 3825 °C.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbon - a nonmetallic element, atomic number 6, a member of the carbon group
  • Synonym: carbon group - group 14 elements in the periodic table: carbon, silicon, germanium, tin, lead, flerovium

Etymology

From German 'Kristallogen,' from 'Kristall' (crystal) + '-gen' (producing), referring to elements that can form crystals.
