colleague

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.liːɡ / (AmE), / ˈkɒl.iːɡ / (BrE)
a person with whom one works, especially in a profession or business
a person one works with

Examples

My colleague and I are collaborating on a new research project.
She introduced her colleague to the rest of the team at the meeting.
As a colleague, he is always willing to offer advice and support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coworker - a person who works with you, typically in the same company or organization
  • Antonym: superior - a person of higher rank or status at work
  • Antonym: subordinate - a person of lower rank or status at work

Etymology

From French 'collègue', from Latin 'collega' (partner in office), from 'com-' (with) + 'legare' (to depute, appoint).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.liːɡ / (AmE), / ˈkɒl.iːɡ / (BrE)
a person with whom one works, especially in a profession or business
person you work with

Examples

My new colleague helped me settle into my job on the first day.
She discussed the project with a group of trusted colleagues before making a decision.
At the conference, I had the chance to meet colleagues from other law firms.

Synset Examples

the surgeon consulted his colleagues
he sent e-mail to his fellow hackers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coworker - a person who works with someone at the same job or company
  • Synonym: associate - a person with whom one is connected in a business or profession

Etymology

From French collègue, from Latin collega (‘partner in office’), from com- (‘together’) + legare (‘to appoint’)
