Kalapuya

1. NOUN

/ kæləˈpuːjə / (AmE), / kæləˈpuːjə / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people who lived in the Willamette Basin of Oregon, United States
Native American people of Oregon

Examples

The Kalapuya lived for thousands of years in the Willamette Valley before European settlement.
A Kalapuya elder spoke about the traditions and stories of her people.
Archaeologists have found artifacts linked to the Kalapuya across western Oregon.
