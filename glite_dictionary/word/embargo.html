embargo

1. VERB

/ ɛmˈbɑrɡoʊ / (AmE), / ɛmˈbɑːɡəʊ / (BrE)
to officially prohibit or restrict trade or other commercial activity with a particular country or organization
to impose a trade ban

Examples

The U.S. announced it will embargo all oil imports from the country.
Several nations threatened to embargo goods unless the policy changed.
In response to the crisis, the government decided to embargo medical supplies.

Synset Examples

The U.S. embargoes Libya

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ban - to officially or legally prohibit something
  • Synonym: sanction - to impose official restrictions, especially in international trade

Etymology

From Spanish 'embargar' (to restrain, impede), from Latin 'imbarricare' (to barricade).

WordNet ID: oewn-02458189-v

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˈbɑrɡoʊ / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɑːɡəʊ / (BrE)
an official government order that restricts or prohibits trade with a particular country or the exchange of specific goods
government trade restriction

Examples

The government imposed an embargo on all arms shipments to the country.
The United Nations voted to lift the oil embargo after the peace agreement was signed.
Due to the embargo, many factories faced shortages of essential materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ban - an official or legal prohibition
  • Synonym: boycott - an act of refusing to do business with a country, organization, or person

Etymology

From Spanish 'embargo', from 'embargar' (to impede, restrain).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06673265-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ɛmˈbɑrɡoʊ / (AmE), / ɛmˈbɑːɡəʊ / (BrE)
to officially prohibit the publication or release of information or documents, especially for reasons of security or copyright
officially ban the release of information

Examples

The government decided to embargo the report until the investigation was complete.
Journalists were informed that the data would be embargoed until Monday morning.
Sensitive documents are often embargoed to prevent leaks before an official announcement.

Synset Examples

embargoed publications

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ban - to officially or legally prohibit something

Etymology

From Spanish 'embargar' (to impede, restrain), from Latin 'imbarricare' (to barricade).

Synset POS: v
