Petrogale

1. PROPN

/ ˌpɛtrəˈɡeɪli / (AmE), / ˌpɛtrəˈɡɑːli / (BrE)
A genus of marsupials known as rock wallabies, native to Australia and characterized by their agility on rocky terrains.
genus of Australian rock wallabies

Examples

The Petrogale are specially adapted to living in rugged rocky environments.
Several species of Petrogale have become endangered due to habitat loss.
Researchers observed a colony of Petrogale thriving among the cliffs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rock wallaby - small marsupial native to rocky areas in Australia

Etymology

From Greek 'petra' (rock) and 'gale' (weasel), referring to the animal's habitat and appearance.
