Selene vomer

1. NOUN

/ səˈliːni ˈvoʊmər / (AmE), / sɛˈliːni ˈvəʊmə / (BrE)
A species of silvery marine fish with a deep, flattened body and eyes high on its truncated forehead, commonly known as the lookdown.
a species of silvery marine fish (lookdown)

Examples

The Selene vomer is easily recognized by its almost transparent silvery body.
Aquariums often display Selene vomer due to its distinctive appearance and graceful swimming.
Researchers studied the feeding behavior of Selene vomer in coastal waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moonfish - a similar type of silvery, laterally compressed marine fish

Etymology

From Latin: 'Selene,' genus name from the Greek goddess of the moon; 'vomer' meaning 'ploughshare' in Latin, possibly referencing shape.
