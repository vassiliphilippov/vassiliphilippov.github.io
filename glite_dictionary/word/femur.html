femur

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːmɚ / (AmE), / ˈfiːmə / (BrE)
the bone of the upper leg or thigh, extending from the hip to the knee, and the longest and strongest bone in the human body
thigh bone; upper leg bone
medical

Examples

A broken femur can take months to heal properly.
The surgeon examined the X-ray to check for damage to the femur.
Athletes are at risk of femur fractures in high-impact sports.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thigh bone - another term for femur
  • Degree Lower: tibia - the inner and typically larger bone of the lower leg
  • Synonym: humerus - the bone of the upper arm or forelimb, forming joints at the shoulder and elbow

Etymology

From Latin 'femur' (thigh)
