slight

1. VERB

/ slaɪt / (AmE), / slaɪt / (BrE)
to treat someone with disrespect or as if they are unimportant; to ignore or snub.
to treat with disrespect or indifference

Examples

She felt hurt when her coworkers slighted her contribution to the project.
He was careful not to slight anyone during the meeting, greeting each guest warmly.
After the argument, she slighted him by refusing to speak to him at the party.

Synset Examples

She cold-shouldered her ex-fiance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snub - to ignore or spurn disdainfully
  • Synonym: to ignore - to refuse to take notice of
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. ADJ

/ slaɪt / (AmE), / slaɪt / (BrE)
(of a person or their build) delicately thin or slender in appearance; not strongly built.
delicately thin or slender (of build)

Examples

She was known for her slight frame and graceful movements.
The coat hung loosely on his slight shoulders.
A slight girl stood silently at the door, hardly making a sound.

Synset Examples

she was slender as a willow shoot is slender
a slim girl with straight blonde hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slender - gracefully thin, especially of a person's build
  • Synonym: slim - thin in an attractive way (of a person's figure)

Etymology

From Old Norse slétt (smooth, plain).

3. ADJ

/ slaɪt / (AmE), / slaɪt / (BrE)
small in degree or amount; not considerable or important
small in amount or importance

Examples

There is a slight possibility of rain this afternoon, but most people expect sunshine.
She felt a slight pain in her shoulder after carrying the heavy bag.
The difference in taste was slight, but I could tell the chocolate was a bit richer.

  • Synonym: minor - lesser in importance, seriousness, or significance
  • Antonym: substantial - of considerable importance, size, or worth

Etymology

4. NOUN

/ slaɪt / (AmE), / slaɪt / (BrE)
an intentional act or remark that shows a lack of respect or consideration for someone
an act of disrespect or insult

Examples

She took his refusal to greet her as a slight that she could not forgive.
Despite the slight, he remained calm and responded graciously.
He apologized quickly when he realized his remarks were taken as a personal slight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insult - an action or comment that is rude or disrespectful
  • Antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration

Etymology

From Middle English slight (insult), likely via Middle Dutch slecht (plain, simple) in the sense of 'contemptible'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

5. ADJ

/ slaɪt / (AmE), / slaɪt / (BrE)
not strong, important, or substantial; small in degree or amount
not substantial or important

Examples

The evidence against the suspect was only slight, so the case was dismissed.
There is a slight chance of rain tomorrow, but it's mostly sunny.
Her influence on the decision was slight compared to her colleagues'.

Synset Examples

a tenuous argument
a thin plot
a fragile claim to fame

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insignificant - too small or unimportant to be worth consideration
  • Antonym: substantial - of considerable importance, size, or worth
  • Synonym: trivial - of little value or importance

Etymology

From Old English *slīht* (smooth, level, slight), of Germanic origin.
