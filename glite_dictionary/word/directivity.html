directivity

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌrɛkˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / dɪˌrɛkˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
The property of being directional, especially in emitting or receiving energy more effectively in certain directions than others.
quality of being directional or focused in a direction

Examples

The directivity of this microphone makes it ideal for recording on stage.
By increasing the directivity of an antenna, you can improve signal strength in a specific area.
Scientists measured the directivity of the sound waves produced by the speaker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: directionality - the degree to which something operates in a particular direction

Etymology

Formed from 'direction' + '-ivity', on the model of 'sensitivity'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪrɛkˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌdɪrɛkˈtɪvəti / (BrE)
The property of a device, such as a microphone or antenna, of being more sensitive to signals or sounds from certain directions than from others.
directional sensitivity of a device

Examples

The studio engineer chose a microphone with high directivity to avoid picking up background noise.
Antenna directivity is crucial when trying to isolate weak signals from one direction.
Because the speaker had low directivity, the sound spread evenly throughout the room.

Synset Examples

the antenna received signals with no directivity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: directionality - the quality of being directional; tendency to react more to stimuli from certain directions

Etymology

From 'direct' + '-ivity', patterned after similar nouns like 'sensitivity'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05073795-n
Synset POS: n
