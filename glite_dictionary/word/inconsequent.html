inconsequent

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkɑːnsəkwənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɒnsɪkwənt / (BrE)
having little or no worth or importance; trivial or insignificant.
lacking importance; trivial

Examples

The manager dismissed the complaint as inconsequent and not worthy of further attention.
Despite his elaborate plans, most of his efforts proved inconsequent in the end.
She considered the missing documents inconsequent compared to the broader issues at stake.

Synset Examples

his work seems trivial and inconsequential
the quite inconsequent fellow was managed like a puppet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inconsequential - not important or significant
  • Antonym: significant - having meaning or importance

Etymology

From Latin 'inconsequens', from 'in-' (not) + 'consequens' (following).
