coelenterate

1. NOUN

/ siˈlɛntəˌreɪt / (AmE), / siːˈlɛntərət / (BrE)
Any of a group of simple, radially symmetrical aquatic animals with saclike bodies, a single opening, and stinging tentacles, including jellyfish, corals, and sea anemones.
group of simple aquatic animals (e.g. jellyfish, coral)
medical

Examples

Jellyfish and corals are both classified as coelenterates.
The simple body structure of a coelenterate distinguishes it from more complex animals.
Scientists study coelenterates to better understand early animal evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cnidarian - any aquatic animal with stinging cells, like jellyfish and coral

Etymology

From Greek koilos (hollow) + enteron (intestine), referring to their single body cavity.
