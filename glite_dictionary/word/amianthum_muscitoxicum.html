Amianthum muscitoxicum

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmiˈænθəm ˌmʌskɪˈtɒksɪkəm / (AmE), / ˌæmiˈænθəm ˌmʌskɪˈtɒksɪkəm / (BrE)
a species of highly toxic plant with all parts poisonous; the bulb is sometimes used as a fly poison; occasionally classified under the subfamily Melanthiaceae
a highly toxic plant species

Examples

Researchers have documented that Amianthum muscitoxicum is dangerous to livestock due to its toxic properties.
The bulb of Amianthum muscitoxicum was historically used as a natural fly poison.
Botanists sometimes debate whether Amianthum muscitoxicum belongs in the subfamily Melanthiaceae.
