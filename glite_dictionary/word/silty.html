silty

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪlti / (AmE), / ˈsɪlti / (BrE)
Containing, full of, or resembling silt.
full of silt

Examples

The river banks are made up of silty soil, which helps support lush vegetation.
After the flood, a layer of silty sediment covered the fields.
The silty water settled slowly, leaving a muddy deposit at the bottom.

Synset Examples

silty soil

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sandy - Containing or resembling sand
  • Degree Lower: clayey - Containing or resembling clay; full of clay

Etymology

From "silt" (fine sediment) + "-y" (adjectival suffix).
