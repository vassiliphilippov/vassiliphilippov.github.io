benthos

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛnθɑs / (AmE), / ˈbɛnθɒs / (BrE)
The community of organisms, both plants and animals, that live on or near the bottom of a sea, lake, river, or stream.
organisms living on a water body's bottom

Examples

Scientists study the benthos to understand changes in aquatic ecosystems.
The benthos includes worms, crustaceans, and certain types of algae.
Oil spills can have a devastating effect on the benthos of affected areas.

Etymology

From Greek benthos (depth of the sea).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛnθoʊs / (AmE), / ˈbɛnθɒs / (BrE)
the ecological region comprising the bottom of a sea, lake, or other body of water, including the littoral zones
sea or lake bottom region

Examples

The rich biodiversity of the benthos attracts marine biologists from around the world.
Many species of fish feed directly on the benthos for nutrients and energy.
Pollution can have a severe impact on the delicate organisms found in the benthos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benthic - relating to or occurring on the bottom of a sea or lake

Etymology

From Greek *benthos* (depth of the sea).
