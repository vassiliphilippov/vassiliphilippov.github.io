lass

1. NOUN

/ læs / (AmE), / læs / (BrE)
a girl or young woman, especially one who is unmarried
girl or young woman

Examples

The young lass danced gracefully across the stage.
That lass works in the bakery down the street.
He wrote a song about a bonnie lass from Glasgow.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lad - a boy or young man
  • Synonym: girl - a female child or young woman

Etymology

From Middle English 'las', from Old Norse 'lǫskr' (idle, weak, frail person).
