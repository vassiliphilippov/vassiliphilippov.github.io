Viola pedata

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈoʊlə pɪˈdeɪtə / (AmE), / vʌɪˈəʊlə pɛˈdeɪtə / (BrE)
A species of violet native to eastern North America, notable for its large pale blue or purple, pansy-like flowers.
species of violet with pansy-like purple flowers

Examples

The Viola pedata blooms in early spring across many eastern forests.
Gardeners prize Viola pedata for its distinctive pansy-like flowers.
Many butterflies are attracted to the Viola pedata in our native plant garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bird's foot violet - another common name for Viola pedata, referring to its leaf shape

Etymology

From Latin: 'viola' meaning violet and 'pedata' meaning foot-shaped, referencing the shape of the leaves.
