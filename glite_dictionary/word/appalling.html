appalling

1. ADJ

/ əˈpɔlɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈpɔːlɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing shock, horror, or dismay; extremely bad or unpleasant.
causing shock or dismay

Examples

The refugees endured appalling conditions during their journey.
It is appalling that so many children go hungry every day.
The crime scene was found in an appalling state.

Synset Examples

appalling conditions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shocking - causing a strong feeling of surprise or dismay
  • Synonym: horrifying - causing horror or shock
  • Antonym: wonderful - causing admiration or pleasure; extremely good

Etymology

From verb 'appall', from Old French 'apalir' (to grow pale), from Latin 'pallere' (to be pale).
