articulatio coxae

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˌtɪkjʊˈleɪʃioʊ ˈkoʊksaɪ / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪkjʊˈleɪʃɪəʊ ˈkɒksiː / (BrE)
the ball-and-socket joint formed between the head of the femur and the acetabulum of the pelvis; also known as the hip joint
the anatomical hip joint
medical

Examples

The articulatio coxae allows the leg to move in multiple directions.
Damage to the articulatio coxae can result in limited mobility and pain.
Orthopedic surgeons often operate on the articulatio coxae to replace a damaged hip joint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hip joint - the joint between the femur and pelvis; synonym of articulatio coxae

Etymology

From Latin articulatio (joint) + coxae (of the hip).
