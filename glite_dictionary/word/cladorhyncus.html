Cladorhyncus

1. NOUN

/ klædəˈrɪŋkəs / (AmE), / klædəˈrɪŋkəs / (BrE)
A genus of wading birds in the stilt family, similar to avocets but distinguished by their straight bills.
genus of wading birds with straight bills

Examples

The Cladorhyncus can be found in shallow wetlands across Australia.
Unlike avocets, Cladorhyncus have distinctly straight bills.
Researchers studied the nesting habits of the lone species in the genus Cladorhyncus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avocet - type of wading bird with upturned bill

Etymology

From Greek klados (branch) + rhynchos (bill, beak), referring to the bird's beak shape.
