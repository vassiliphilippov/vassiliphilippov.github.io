perversion

1. NOUN

/ pərˈvɜrʒən / (AmE), / pəˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
a sexual practice that is considered abnormal or unacceptable by societal standards
abnormal or unacceptable sexual practice

Examples

Some critics accused the book of glorifying sexual perversion.
He was arrested on charges related to acts of perversion.
Society's view of what constitutes a perversion has changed over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deviance - behavior that departs from social or moral standards
  • Antonym: normality - the state of conforming to a standard; typical behavior

Etymology

From Latin perversionem (a turning around, corruption), from pervertere (to turn around, corrupt).

2. NOUN

/ pərˈvɜrʒən / (AmE), / pəˈvɜːʃn / (BrE)
the act of distorting or corrupting something from its original course, meaning, or state to a wrong or improper use
distorting something to a wrong use

Examples

The judge called the ruling a perversion of the legal process.
Many critics saw the film's depiction as a perversion of the original story's message.
He regarded censorship as a perversion of freedom of expression.

Synset Examples

it was a perversion of justice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distortion - the action of twisting or altering something out of its true, natural, or original state
  • Synonym: corruption - the process by which something is changed from its original form to one that is morally wrong
  • Antonym: rectification - the act of putting something right; correction

Etymology

From Latin perversio (overthrow, subversion), from pervertere (to overturn, corrupt).
