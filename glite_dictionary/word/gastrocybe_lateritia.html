Gastrocybe lateritia

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæstroʊˈsaɪbi ˌlætəˈrɪʃə / (AmE), / ˌɡæstrəʊˈsɪbi ˌlætəˈrɪtia / (BrE)
A species of fungus in the genus Gastrocybe, characterized by a conic cap, thin upright-to-drooping stalk, and a gelatinous cap that disperses spores as the stalk collapses.
a species of conic-capped fungus

Examples

The Gastrocybe lateritia can be identified by its gelatinous, reddish-brown cap and slender stalk.
As the Gastrocybe lateritia matures, its stalk bends downward and the cap becomes slimy.
Researchers collected samples of Gastrocybe lateritia from decaying wood in the forest.

Etymology

From Greek 'gastro-' (stomach) + '-cybe' (head), referring to the shape; 'lateritia' from Latin 'lateritius' (brick-red), describing its color.
