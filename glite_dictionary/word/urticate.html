to urticate

1. VERB

/ ˈɜrtɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɜːtɪˌkeɪt / (BrE)
to sting or cause a stinging pain or sensation, as with nettles
cause a stinging sensation, like nettles

Examples

Brushing against the plant will urticate the skin, leaving a painful rash.
Some caterpillars urticate predators with their tiny venomous hairs.
The jellyfish's tentacles can urticate swimmers, causing an intense burning sensation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sting - to wound or pierce with a sting and cause sharp pain

Etymology

From Latin urtica (nettle).

2. VERB

/ ˈɜrtɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɜːtɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to sting or whip with or as with nettles, causing an itching or stinging sensation
to whip or sting like nettles

Examples

The leaves of the plant can urticate the skin if handled without gloves.
During the ritual, participants would urticate themselves with fresh nettles.
Some insects protect themselves by secreting chemicals that urticate potential predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sting - to prick or wound with a sharp-pointed organ or instrument
  • Antonym: to soothe - to ease pain or discomfort, especially of the skin

Etymology

From Latin urtica (nettle).
