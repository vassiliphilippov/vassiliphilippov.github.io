Woodsia ilvensis

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊd.si.ə ɪlˈvɛn.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈwʊd.si.ə ɪlˈvɛn.sɪs / (BrE)
a species of fern found on rocks in northern temperate regions, with rusty-brown stalks and lance-shaped, pinnate fronds
a rock-dwelling northern temperate fern species

Examples

The rusty hairs on the stipes of Woodsia ilvensis distinguish it from other ferns.
Botanists found several clusters of Woodsia ilvensis growing in the crevices of the granite outcrop.
Conservationists are monitoring Woodsia ilvensis populations because of their sensitivity to habitat disturbance.

Etymology

From genus name 'Woodsia' after English botanist Joseph Woods + specific epithet 'ilvensis', from Latin referring to Elba (Ilva), the type locality.
