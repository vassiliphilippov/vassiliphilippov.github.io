Arctic hare

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrktɪk hɛr / (AmE), / ˈɑːktɪk heə / (BrE)
a large hare native to northern North America, which turns almost completely white in winter
a white-furred northern North American hare

Examples

The Arctic hare can survive in extremely cold environments due to its thick fur.
During winter, the fur of the Arctic hare becomes almost completely white for camouflage.
We spotted a group of Arctic hares while hiking in northern Canada.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hare - a fast-running, long-eared mammal related to rabbits
