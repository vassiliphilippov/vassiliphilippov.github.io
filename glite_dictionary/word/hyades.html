Hyades

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ.əˌdiz / (AmE), / ˈhʌɪ.ə.diːz / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the seven daughters of Atlas who, as half-sisters of the Pleiades, were placed among the stars by Zeus; also, a star cluster in the constellation Taurus named after them.
mythological sisters; star cluster in Taurus

Examples

The Hyades are easily visible in the night sky as a V-shaped cluster in Taurus.
According to Greek myth, the Hyades were transformed into stars by Zeus as a reward for caring for Dionysus.
The ancient Greeks believed that the rising of the Hyades brought rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pleiades - sister group of nymphs in Greek mythology and a nearby star cluster

Etymology

From Greek Ὑάδες (Huádes), the nymphs of rain.
