Coffea

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑfiə / (AmE), / ˈkɒfiə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical plants whose seeds are used to produce coffee; commonly referred to as coffee trees or shrubs.
genus of tropical coffee trees

Examples

The species Coffea arabica is well known for producing high-quality beans.
Many plantations cultivate different varieties of Coffea for unique flavors.
Botanists classify Coffea as a genus within the Rubiaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coffee tree - a tree or shrub of the genus Coffea whose seeds are coffee beans

Etymology

From Latinized form of Arabic *qahwa* (coffee).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.fi.ə / (AmE), / ˈkɒf.i.ə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical flowering plants in the family Rubiaceae that includes the coffee trees, cultivated for their seeds used to produce coffee
genus of plants including coffee trees

Examples

Several species within the Coffea genus are grown commercially to produce coffee beans.
Researchers study Coffea plants to develop crops resistant to disease and drought.
The most widely known member of the Coffea genus is Coffea arabica.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coffee tree - tree of the genus Coffea cultivated for coffee beans

Etymology

From Latinized form of Arabic 'qahwa' (coffee).
