chilli

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪli / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪli / (BrE)
A small, very hot pepper with a tapered shape, used to flavor food with pungency.
hot, pungent pepper

Examples

The chef added a chilli to the curry to give it extra heat.
She accidentally touched her eyes after slicing a chilli and felt a burning sensation.
Dried chillies are often used to spice up traditional Asian dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pepper - A general term for fruits of plants in the genus Capsicum, both hot and sweet varieties
  • Degree Lower: bell pepper - A mild, sweet variety of pepper lacking pungency

Etymology

From Nahuatl *chīlli*, adopted into Spanish as *chile*.
