Prunella vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ pruˈnɛlə vʌlˈgɛrɪs / (AmE), / pruˈnɛlə vʌlˈɡɑːrɪs / (BrE)
A decumbent blue-flowered perennial plant of the mint family, native to Europe and naturalized in North America, traditionally believed to have medicinal properties.
a blue-flowered medicinal perennial plant

Examples

Herbalists often use Prunella vulgaris as a remedy for sore throats and minor wounds.
Clusters of Prunella vulgaris bloom along many meadows across Europe and North America in summer.
Scientists are studying the medicinal properties of Prunella vulgaris in hopes of developing new treatments.

Etymology

From Latin: 'prunella' (a kind of plant or 'self-heal') and 'vulgaris' (common).
