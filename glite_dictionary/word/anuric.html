anuric

1. ADJ

/ əˈnʊrɪk / (AmE), / əˈnjʊərɪk / (BrE)
unable to produce urine or relating to the absence of urine production.
unable to produce urine
medical

Examples

The patient became anuric following the severe kidney injury.
Doctors were concerned when his urine output dropped and he turned anuric.
Treatment protocols differ for anuric cases compared to those with partial kidney function.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: oliguric - producing an abnormally small amount of urine
  • Degree Higher: polyuric - producing an abnormally large amount of urine

Etymology

From Greek 'an-' (not) + 'ouron' (urine), meaning 'without urine'.
