crosshatch

1. VERB

/ ˈkrɔsˌhætʃ / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌhætʃ / (BrE)
to shade or fill an area with intersecting sets of parallel lines, especially in drawing or engraving
to shade with crossing lines (in drawing)

Examples

The artist began to crosshatch the sky to create texture and depth.
She crosshatched the shaded area to make the drawing look more realistic.
You can crosshatch gently to suggest shadow without overpowering the sketch.

Synset Examples

the draftsman crosshatched the area

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hatch - to shade with closely spaced parallel lines

Etymology

From 'cross' (meaning 'across') + 'hatch' (to make lines), originally from early techniques in engraving and drawing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsˌhætʃ / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌhætʃ / (BrE)
a method of shading consisting of intersecting sets of parallel lines, often used in drawing to indicate shadow or texture
shading with intersecting lines

Examples

The artist used crosshatch to create deep shadows on the portrait.
You can see the fine crosshatch pattern in the background of the drawing.
Learning to crosshatch effectively is an important skill for illustrators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hatching - shading with closely spaced parallel lines

Etymology

From 'cross-' + 'hatch', referring to crossing hatched (engraved) lines.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04689154-n
Synset POS: n
