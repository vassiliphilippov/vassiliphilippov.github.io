percipient

1. NOUN

/ pərˈsɪpiənt / (AmE), / pəˈsɪpiənt / (BrE)
a person who perceives or becomes aware of things or events, especially through the senses
a person who perceives through the senses

Examples

As a trained percipient, she noticed subtle changes in her surroundings that others missed.
The study required each percipient to report their sensory experiences in detail.
In parapsychology, a percipient is someone who claims to receive information through extrasensory perception.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: observer - a person who watches or notices something

Etymology

From Latin 'percipient-', present participle of 'percipere' (to perceive).

2. ADJ

/ pərˈsɪpiənt / (AmE), / pəˈsɪpiənt / (BrE)
having keen perception or insight; able to perceive or understand things quickly and easily
having keen perception

Examples

The percipient journalist quickly understood the significance of the leaked documents.
Her percipient remarks helped clarify the confusing situation during the meeting.
A percipient observer can detect subtle changes in people's behavior.

Synset Examples

clear mind
a percipient author

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perceptive - having or showing sensitive insight

Etymology

From Latin percipiens, present participle of percipere (to perceive).
