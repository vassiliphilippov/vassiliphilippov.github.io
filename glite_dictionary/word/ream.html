ream

1. NOUN

/ rim / (AmE), / riːm / (BrE)
a standard quantity of paper, usually consisting of 480 or 500 sheets (20 quires)
a quantity of paper (480 or 500 sheets)

Examples

We bought a ream of paper for the office printer.
She used almost a full ream printing the draft of her thesis.
The contract was so long it filled an entire ream of paper.

Etymology

From Old French 'raime', from Spanish 'resma', from Arabic 'rizmah' (bundle), from Aramaic 'rismā'.

2. VERB

/ riːm / (AmE), / riːm / (BrE)
to squeeze the juice from a fruit, especially citrus, using a reamer
extract juice with a reamer

Examples

I need to ream several oranges before making the salad.
The chef quickly reamed the limes to add their juice to the marinade.
