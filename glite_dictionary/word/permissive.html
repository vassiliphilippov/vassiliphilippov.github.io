permissive

1. ADJ

/ pɚˈmɪsɪv / (AmE), / pəˈmɪsɪv / (BrE)
allowing or characterized by great or excessive freedom of behavior; not strict or preventive.
allowing freedom; not strict

Examples

Her parents have a permissive attitude towards her staying out late.
The school's permissive policies allowed students to use their phones in class.
Some critics argue that a permissive society leads to a lack of discipline.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: restrictive - imposing limitations or constraints; not allowing much freedom
  • Synonym: lenient - not strict; tolerant or merciful

Etymology

From Latin 'permissivus', from 'permittere' meaning 'to allow'.

2. ADJ

/ pərˈmɪsɪv / (AmE), / pəˈmɪsɪv / (BrE)
allowing or characterized by great freedom of behavior; not strict in discipline or rules.
not strict; allowing freedom

Examples

Many schools have adopted a permissive approach to dress codes in recent years.
His parents were quite permissive, rarely setting any firm boundaries.
A permissive attitude in the workplace can sometimes lead to a lack of discipline.

Synset Examples

direct primary legislation is largely permissive rather than prescriptive
permissive parents

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strict - demanding that rules are closely obeyed; not permissive
  • Synonym: lenient - tolerant or not strict in discipline

Etymology

From Latin permissivus (allowing), from permittere (to permit).
