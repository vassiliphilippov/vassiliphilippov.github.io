chips

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪps / (AmE), / tʃɪps / (BrE)
thin strips or slices of potato that are fried in deep fat and eaten hot, especially as a snack or side dish; known as 'French fries' in American English and 'chips' in British English.
fried strips or slices of potato

Examples

We ordered a burger and a side of chips at the pub.
She sprinkled salt and vinegar on her chips before eating them.
In the UK, fish and chips is a classic meal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: French fries - thin strips of potato deep-fried and eaten hot (American English)
  • Antonym: crisps - thin slices of potato fried until crunchy (British English: potato chips)
