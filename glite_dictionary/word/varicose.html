varicose

1. ADJ

/ ˈværɪˌkoʊs / (AmE), / ˈværɪkəʊs / (BrE)
(of a vein or blood vessel) abnormally swollen, enlarged, and often twisted, usually due to malfunctioning valves or weakened walls.
abnormally swollen (esp. of veins)
medical

Examples

Many people develop varicose veins in their legs as they get older.
Her doctor warned that prolonged standing could worsen her varicose condition.
The surgery was performed to remove a particularly painful varicose vein.

Synset Examples

varicose veins

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swollen - enlarged due to fluid, pressure, or inflammation

Etymology

From Latin varicosus (full of dilated veins), from varix (dilated vein).
