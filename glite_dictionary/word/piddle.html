to piddle

1. VERB

/ ˈpɪdəl / (AmE), / ˈpɪd.əl / (BrE)
(informal) to urinate, especially used when referring to animals or children
urinate (informal, esp. for animals/children)
informal

Examples

The puppy piddled on the carpet while we were out.
She rushed her toddler to the bathroom before he could piddle in his pants.
If the cat piddles on the rug again, we’ll have to take her to the vet.

Synset Examples

Again, the cat had made on the expensive rug

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to urinate - to discharge urine
  • Antonym: to defecate - to discharge feces from the body

2. VERB

/ ˈpɪd.əl / (AmE), / ˈpɪd.əl / (BrE)
to spend time idly or aimlessly, often without accomplishing anything important
waste time idly
informal

Examples

Stop piddling around and get to work on your assignment.
He tends to piddle away his mornings doing nothing in particular.
Instead of making progress, we just piddled most of the afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dawdle - to waste time or move slowly
  • Synonym: fritter - to waste time, money, or energy on trifling matters
  • Antonym: rush - to do something with great speed, opposite of wasting time

Etymology

Probably a diminutive of 'piddle' (to urinate), extended figuratively to mean frittering away time.
