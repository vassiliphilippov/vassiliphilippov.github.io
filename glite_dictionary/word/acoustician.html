acoustician

1. NOUN

/ əˌkuːˈstɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌkuːˈstɪʃən / (BrE)
a person who is an expert in acoustics, especially a physicist specializing in the science of sound
expert in acoustics

Examples

The acoustician was called in to improve the sound quality in the concert hall.
As an acoustician, she designs spaces for optimal audio performance.
After the renovation, an acoustician measured the room’s reverberation to ensure perfect clarity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acousticist - a person who studies acoustics

Etymology

From 'acoustic' + '-ian', formed after the pattern of words denoting professions.
