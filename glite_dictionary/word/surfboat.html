surfboat

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrfˌboʊt / (AmE), / ˈsɜːfˌbəʊt / (BrE)
a boat specially designed to be launched from or landed through heavy surf, often used in rescue operations at sea
boat for launching/landing in heavy surf

Examples

The rescue team used a sturdy surfboat to reach the stranded swimmers beyond the breakers.
Launching a surfboat requires skill to navigate through powerful waves along the shore.
Old photographs showed a wooden surfboat used by the coastguard in the early 1900s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lifeboat - a boat kept for emergency use to save lives at sea

Etymology

From 'surf' (waves breaking on shore) + 'boat'.
