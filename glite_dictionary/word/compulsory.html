compulsory

1. ADJ

/ kəmˈpʌl.sə.ri / (AmE), / kəmˈpʌl.sə.ri / (BrE)
Required by law or a rule; obligatory.
required by law or rule

Examples

Wearing a helmet is compulsory for all cyclists in this city.
Participation in the training session is compulsory for new employees.
Education is compulsory for children up to the age of sixteen in many countries.

Synset Examples

in most schools physical education is compulsory
attendance is mandatory
required reading

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandatory - required by law or rules; obligatory
  • Antonym: optional - available as a choice; not required

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'compulsorius', from Latin 'compellere' (to force).
