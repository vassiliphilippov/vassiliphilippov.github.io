explicit

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsplɪsɪt / (AmE), / ɪkˈsplɪsɪt / (BrE)
stated clearly and in detail, leaving no room for confusion or doubt; precisely and openly expressed.
stated clearly and precisely

Examples

The teacher gave explicit instructions for completing the assignment.
Some movies contain explicit content and are not suitable for children.
She was explicit about her requirements for the job.

Synset Examples

explicit instructions
she made her wishes explicit
explicit sexual scenes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: implicit - implied though not directly expressed
  • Synonym: clear - easy to perceive, understand, or interpret

Etymology

From Latin explicitus (unfolded, set forth), past participle of explicare (to unfold).
