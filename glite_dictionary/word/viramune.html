Viramune

1. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪrəˈmjun / (AmE), / ˌvɪərəˈmjuːn / (BrE)
A trademarked non-nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor used in the treatment of HIV and AIDS.
HIV/AIDS drug (brand name)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Viramune as part of his antiretroviral therapy.
Patients taking Viramune should be regularly monitored for liver function.
After starting Viramune, she noticed fewer side effects than with her previous medication.
