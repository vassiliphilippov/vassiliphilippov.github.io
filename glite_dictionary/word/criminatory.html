criminatory

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrɪmɪnəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈkrɪmɪnətəri / (BrE)
Suggesting or implying guilt or blame, especially in a legal context.
implying guilt or blame
legal

Examples

The witness provided criminatory evidence that led to the suspect's arrest.
Any criminatory document must be handled carefully in court proceedings.
She refused to answer questions that could be seen as criminatory towards her coworkers.

Synset Examples

incriminatory testimony

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incriminatory - implying or charging with guilt or blame
  • Antonym: exculpatory - tending to clear from guilt or blame

Etymology

From Latin 'criminatorius', from 'crimen' (accusation, crime).
