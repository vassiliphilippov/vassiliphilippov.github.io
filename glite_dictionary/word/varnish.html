varnish

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɑrnɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈvɑːnɪʃ / (BrE)
a clear, hard, and shiny coating applied to a surface for protection or decorative purposes
clear protective finish for surfaces

Examples

The wooden table was coated in varnish to protect it from scratches.
After applying a fresh layer of varnish, the floor gleamed beautifully.
You need to let the varnish dry completely before using the chair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lacquer - a clear coating similar to varnish, often used for protection

Etymology

From Old French *vernis*, from Medieval Latin *vernix* (odorous resin).

2. VERB

/ ˈvɑrnɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈvɑːnɪʃ / (BrE)
to cover or coat a surface with varnish in order to protect or enhance its appearance
apply a coating of varnish

Examples

The carpenter decided to varnish the table to protect it from spills and stains.
She carefully varnished each wooden frame to give it a glossy finish.
After sanding the chair, he began varnishing the surface to bring out the wood's natural color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lacquer - to coat with lacquer, a protective or decorative finish

Etymology

From Old French vernix, from Medieval Latin vernicium, from Late Latin vernix (odorous resin).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
