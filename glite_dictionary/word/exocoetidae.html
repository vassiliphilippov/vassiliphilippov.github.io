Exocoetidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛksoʊˈsiːtɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌɛksəʊˈsiːtɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fishes known as flying fishes, distinguished by their enlarged pectoral fins which allow them to glide above the water's surface; closely related to the halfbeaks.
family of flying fishes

Examples

The Exocoetidae are remarkable for their ability to glide above the ocean surface.
Researchers observed several species within Exocoetidae during their expedition in the Pacific.
Members of Exocoetidae have specially adapted pectoral fins that help them escape predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flying fish - common name for members of Exocoetidae, known for gliding over water

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'exokoitos' (sleeping outside), referring to their gliding above water.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛksoʊˈsiːtɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌɛksəʊˈsiːtɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fish known as flying fishes, noted for their ability to glide above the surface of the water.
family of flying fishes

Examples

The Exocoetidae are well known for their ability to leap out of the water and glide long distances.
Biologists recently discovered several new species in the Exocoetidae family.
Members of the Exocoetidae are found in warm ocean waters around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flying fish - marine fish that can glide above the water, belonging to Exocoetidae

Etymology

From New Latin *Exocoetus* (a genus name) + -idae (family suffix in zoology).
