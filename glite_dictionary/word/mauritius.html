Mauritius

1. PROPN

/ mɔːˈrɪʃəs / (AmE), / mɔːˈrɪʃəs / (BrE)
an island nation in the southwestern Indian Ocean, east of Madagascar
island country in the Indian Ocean

Examples

They spent their honeymoon in Mauritius, enjoying its beautiful beaches and warm climate.
Many rare plant species are native to Mauritius.
Flights to Mauritius are available from most major international airports.

Etymology

From Dutch 'Mauritius', named after Prince Maurice of Nassau.

2. PROPN

/ məˈrɪʃəs / (AmE), / məˈrɪʃəs / (BrE)
an island country in the Indian Ocean, east of Madagascar, known for its parliamentary democracy
island country in the Indian Ocean

Examples

Next summer, we plan to visit Mauritius for its beautiful beaches and cultural sites.
The biodiversity of Mauritius attracts researchers and nature lovers from around the world.
After gaining independence in 1968, Mauritius became known for its stable government and strong economy.

Etymology

From the Dutch 'Mauritius,' named in honor of Prince Maurice of Nassau.
