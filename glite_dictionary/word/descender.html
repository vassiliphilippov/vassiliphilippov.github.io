descender

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛndər / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛndə / (BrE)
a person or thing that descends from a higher place to a lower one.
a person who descends

Examples

The first descender reached the base camp ahead of the others.
As an experienced descender, she navigated the steep slope with ease.
Each descender wore a helmet for safety while rappelling down the cliff.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: climber - someone who climbs up, especially mountains or rocks

Etymology

From Latin 'descendere' (to go down), via Old French 'descendre'.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛndɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛndə / (BrE)
the part of a lowercase letter, such as 'g' or 'y', that extends below the baseline of other lowercase letters
part of a letter extending below baseline

Examples

The letter 'g' has a descender that extends well below the other letters on the line.
In designing a new font, careful attention was paid to the shape of each descender.
Lowercase 'y' and 'p' are examples of letters with descenders in most typefaces.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ascender - the part of a lowercase letter that rises above the main body of the letter

Etymology

From Latin 'descendere' (to go down).

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛndər / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛndə / (BrE)
a lowercase letter or part of a letter that extends below the baseline of other lowercase letters, such as 'g', 'j', or 'y'
part of a letter extending below the baseline

Examples

The letter 'g' is a descender in most fonts.
In typesetting, attention to descender length is important for readability.
Lowercase 'y' and 'p' both contain a descender.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ascender - the part of a lowercase letter that extends above the x-height

Etymology

From English 'descend' + '-er', referring to something that descends or goes lower.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06843758-n
Synset POS: n
