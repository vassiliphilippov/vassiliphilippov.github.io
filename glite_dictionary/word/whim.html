whim

1. NOUN

/ wɪm / (AmE), / wɪm / (BrE)
a sudden and often unexplained desire or change of mind
a sudden desire or impulse

Examples

On a whim, she decided to book a flight to Paris for the weekend.
He painted his bedroom purple on a whim.
At the last minute, they changed their plans just for a whim.

Synset Examples

he bought it on an impulse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impulse - a sudden strong urge to act
  • Synonym: caprice - a sudden, unpredictable change, as of one's mind or the weather

Etymology

Possibly from 18th-century English 'whim-wham' (a fanciful object).

2. NOUN

/ wɪm / (AmE), / wɪm / (BrE)
a sudden and fanciful or capricious idea, especially one that is odd or unpredictable
sudden fancy or capricious idea

Examples

On a whim, she decided to take a weekend trip to the coast, leaving her plans behind.
He bought the bright red shoes on a whim, with no real need for them.
Sometimes, the best adventures happen when you follow a whim rather than a plan.

Synset Examples

the theatrical notion of disguise is associated with disaster in his stories
he had a whimsy about flying to the moon
whimsy can be humorous to someone with time to enjoy it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notion - a belief or idea, especially a vague or fanciful one
  • Synonym: impulse - a sudden strong and unreflective urge or desire to act
  • Antonym: plan - an intention or decision about what one is going to do

Etymology

Origin uncertain; perhaps from 'whim-wham', a fanciful object or notion (17th c.).
