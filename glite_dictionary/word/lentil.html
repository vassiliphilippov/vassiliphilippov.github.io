lentil

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛn.təl / (AmE), / ˈlɛn.təl / (BrE)
a small, round, edible seed of a widely cultivated plant, or the plant itself, used as food and fodder
edible seed or plant (Lens culinaris)

Examples

She added chopped carrots and lentils to the soup for extra flavor and nutrition.
A spicy lentil stew is a staple dish in many Middle Eastern countries.
After harvesting, the lentils are dried and sorted before packaging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pea - edible round seed of the pea plant

Etymology

From Old French lentille, from Latin lenticula, diminutive of lens (lentil).

3. NOUN

/ ˈlɛn.təl / (AmE), / ˈlɛn.tɪl / (BrE)
the small, round, edible seed of a plant of the pea family, commonly used as food
small edible seed of a legume plant

Examples

He added a cup of lentil to the soup for extra protein.
Vegetarian diets often include lentil dishes as a source of nutrients.
The grocery store sells organic red lentil in bulk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pulse - an edible seed of any leguminous plant, often used as food
  • Degree Lower: bean - an edible seed, typically kidney-shaped, of various leguminous plants

Etymology

From Old French 'lentille', from Latin 'lenticula', diminutive of 'lens' (lentil).
