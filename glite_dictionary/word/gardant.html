gardant

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡɑrdənt / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdənt / (BrE)
(Heraldry) Of an animal, especially a beast, represented with the head facing the viewer.
heraldry: animal with head facing forward

Examples

In the family crest, the lion is shown gardant, staring directly at the viewer.
The arms featured a bear gardant as a symbol of vigilance.
A dragon gardant appears on the town's ancient heraldic shield.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: regardant - (heraldry) animal depicted with the head turned toward the viewer's left

Etymology

From French 'gardant', present participle of 'garder' (to guard, to look).
