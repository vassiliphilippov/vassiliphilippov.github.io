starch

1. VERB

/ stɑrtʃ / (AmE), / stɑːtʃ / (BrE)
to stiffen (fabric or clothing) with starch, especially during laundering to give it a crisp appearance
to stiffen fabric with starch

Examples

She always starches her shirts to keep them crisp for work.
The laundry starched the tablecloths, so they looked brand new.
You need to starch the collars so they don't wrinkle.

Synset Examples

starch clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: size - to treat or stiffen fabric with a gluelike substance

2. NOUN

/ stɑrtʃ / (AmE), / stɑːtʃ / (BrE)
a substance in powder or liquid form, made from plants and used to stiffen fabric in laundering
substance for stiffening clothes in laundry

Examples

She added extra starch to the laundry to make the collars crisp.
The maid used liquid starch to give the tablecloth a firm finish.
After washing his shirts, he always sprayed them with starch before ironing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sizing - a substance used to stiffen or glaze fabrics or paper

Etymology

From Old English 'stercan' (to stiffen), related to the Proto-Germanic *starkaz (stiff, strong).

3. NOUN

/ stɑrtʃ / (AmE), / stɑːtʃ / (BrE)
A white, tasteless, odorless complex carbohydrate occurring chiefly in seeds, fruits, tubers, and other plant parts and used widely as a foodstuff, as well as in adhesives, paper, and textiles.
complex carbohydrate in plants; used as food and in industry

Examples

Corn and potatoes are rich sources of starch in many diets around the world.
Excessive consumption of starch can lead to weight gain if not balanced with other nutrients.
The laundry process used liquid starch to make the shirts crisp and wrinkle-free.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbohydrate - a compound made of carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen atoms, a major source of energy in food

Etymology

From German Stärke (stiffness, starch), related to Old English sterc (stiff).
