Bierce

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɪrs / (AmE), / ˈbɪəs / (BrE)
(Ambrose Bierce) (1842–1914), an American writer known for his sharp wit, satirical works, and short stories such as 'An Occurrence at Owl Creek Bridge.'
American writer of wit and satire (1842–1914)

Examples

Many readers appreciate Bierce for his incisive satire and darkly humorous observations.
Bierce's collection 'The Devil's Dictionary' is still widely quoted today.
In literature courses, students often study stories by Bierce for their innovative use of irony.
