Clostridium botulinum

1. NOUN

/ klɒˌstrɪdiəm ˌbɑːtʃəˈlaɪnəm / (AmE), / klɒˌstrɪdɪəm ˌbɒtjʊˈlɪnəm / (BrE)
an anaerobic bacterium that produces botulin, the toxin responsible for botulism in humans and animals
bacterium causing botulism
medical

Examples

Clostridium botulinum spores can survive in improperly canned foods, leading to dangerous foodborne illness.
Scientists study Clostridium botulinum to better understand how its toxin affects the nervous system.
Proper sterilization techniques are necessary to prevent Clostridium botulinum contamination in the food industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botulism - illness caused by botulinum toxin

Etymology

From New Latin: Clostridium (genus name) + botulinum, from Latin 'botulus' (sausage), as the disease was first associated with tainted sausage.
