contiguous

1. ADJ

/ kənˈtɪɡjuəs / (AmE), / kənˈtɪɡjuəs / (BrE)
sharing a common border; touching or adjoining each other.
sharing a common border; touching

Examples

Rhode Island shares its western border with the contiguous state of Connecticut.
The two properties are contiguous, separated only by a wooden fence.
The hotel rooms are contiguous, allowing easy access between them.

Synset Examples

Rhode Island has two bordering states: Massachusetts and Connecticut
the side of Germany conterminous with France
Utah and the contiguous state of Idaho
neighboring cities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adjacent - next to or adjoining something else
  • Antonym: separate - not touching or connected; distinct

Etymology

From Latin 'contiguus' (touching, bordering upon), from 'contingere' (to touch upon).

3. ADJ

/ kənˈtɪɡjuəs / (AmE), / kənˈtɪɡjuəs / (BrE)
sharing a common border or touching each other without interruption; next to or adjacent in space or time.
sharing a common border; adjacent

Examples

There are 48 contiguous states in the mainland United States, excluding Alaska and Hawaii.
The two properties are contiguous, making it convenient to merge them into a single plot.
Their offices occupy contiguous floors in the building, allowing easy collaboration between teams.

Synset Examples

the 48 conterminous states
the contiguous 48 states

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adjacent - next to or adjoining something else
  • Antonym: noncontiguous - not sharing a common border or not adjacent

Etymology

From Latin 'contiguus', from 'contingere' meaning 'to touch upon'.
