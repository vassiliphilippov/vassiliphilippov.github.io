Satureja douglasii

1. NOUN

/ ˌsætʃəˈriːə ˈdʌɡləsiː / (AmE), / ˌsætjʊˈriːə ˈdʌɡləsiː / (BrE)
A trailing perennial evergreen herb native to the northwestern United States, with small white flowers, traditionally used for medicinal purposes.
herb native to northwest US, medicinal use

Examples

Native Americans traditionally used Satureja douglasii as a remedy for headaches and colds.
The creeping stems of Satureja douglasii make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
Researchers have studied the medicinal properties of Satureja douglasii for potential modern applications.

Etymology

From Latin genus name Satureja; species named after Scottish botanist David Douglas.
