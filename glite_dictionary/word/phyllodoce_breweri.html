Phyllodoce breweri

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪləˈdoʊsi ˈbruːəri / (AmE), / ˌfɪlɪˈdəʊsi ˈbruːəri / (BrE)
A semi-prostrate evergreen herb native to the western United States, belonging to the heath family.
a western U.S. evergreen herb

Examples

The Phyllodoce breweri thrives in the rocky alpine slopes of the Sierra Nevada.
Botanists identified a patch of Phyllodoce breweri near the trail.
The evergreen leaves of Phyllodoce breweri distinguish it from other mountain plants.

Etymology

From Latinized genus name Phyllodoce, from Greek *phyllon* (leaf) + *dokos* (beam); species honorific for botanist William Henry Brewer.
