Chichewa

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪˈtʃɛwə / (AmE), / tʃɪˈtʃɛwə / (BrE)
the Bantu language spoken by the Chewa people of east central Africa, primarily in Malawi and surrounding countries
Bantu language of the Chewa people

Examples

Many people in Malawi speak Chichewa as their first language.
The book was translated from English into Chichewa for local readers.
She is fluent in both Chichewa and English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nyanja - alternative name for the Chichewa language

Etymology

From Chewa, the name of the ethnic group who speak the language.

2. NOUN

/ tʃɪˈtʃeɪwə / (AmE), / tʃɪˈtʃeɪwə / (BrE)
a member of a Bantu-speaking ethnic group native to Malawi, eastern Zambia, and northern Zimbabwe
member of a Bantu group in Malawi and nearby regions

Examples

Many Chichewa maintain strong cultural traditions passed down through generations.
The Chichewa have played a significant role in the history of Malawi.
Several villages are primarily inhabited by Chichewa in the eastern region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chewa - an alternative name for the same ethnic group

Etymology

From the Chichewa language, where 'Chi-' is a prefix for language, and 'Chewa' refers to the people.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09717167-n
Synset POS: n
