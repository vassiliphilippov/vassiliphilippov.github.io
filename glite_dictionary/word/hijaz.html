Hijaz

1. PROPN

/ hiˈdʒɑz / (AmE), / hiˈdʒɑːz / (BrE)
a coastal region in the western Arabian Peninsula, bordering the Red Sea; historically significant for including Mecca and Medina and formerly an independent kingdom before uniting with Nejd to form Saudi Arabia.
historic region of western Arabia, includes Mecca and Medina

Examples

The Hijaz region played a central role in Islamic history as the location of Mecca and Medina.
During the early 20th century, the Hijaz was an independent kingdom before joining with Nejd.
Many pilgrims travel through the Hijaz on their way to visit the holy cities each year.

Etymology

From Arabic الحجاز (al-Ḥijāz), meaning 'the barrier' or 'the partition'.
