Charales

1. NOUN

/ kəˈreɪliːz / (AmE), / kəˈreɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of macroscopic fresh and brackish water algae, known as stoneworts, with a distinctive stem-like axis
order of aquatic algae (stoneworts)

Examples

The freshwater lake is home to various species belonging to the Charales.
Scientists study the Charales to better understand aquatic ecosystems.
The distinct axis of Charales helps differentiate them from other green algae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stonewort - common name for algae of the order Charales

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Chara' (a type of algae) plus botanical suffix '-ales' (order).
