Platymiscium pinnatum

1. NOUN

/ ˌplætɪˈmɪs.i.əm pɪˈneɪ.təm / (AmE), / ˌplætɪˈmɪs.i.əm pɪˈneɪ.təm / (BrE)
A large shrub native to Colombia, with large pinnate leaves, sizable leaflets, and clusters of fragrant yellow flowers.
shrubby plant native to Colombia

Examples

The wood of Platymiscium pinnatum is valued for its strength and beauty.
Botanists discovered a thriving Platymiscium pinnatum population in the remote forest.
The fragrant yellow flowers of Platymiscium pinnatum attract many pollinators.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; Platymiscium from Greek 'platys' (broad) + 'misos' (middle) for the genus, pinnatum from Latin 'pinnatus' (feathered, pinnate).
