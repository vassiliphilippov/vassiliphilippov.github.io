emporium

1. NOUN

/ ɛmˈpɔriəm / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɔːrɪəm / (BrE)
a large store selling a wide variety of goods, often organized into departments
large department store

Examples

The new emporium downtown attracts hundreds of shoppers every weekend.
She managed a toy emporium filled with games, puzzles, and books for children.
The historic building was once an emporium renowned for its luxury goods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: department store - a large retail store offering a wide variety of goods in different departments
  • Degree Lower: boutique - a small shop selling fashionable or specialized goods

Etymology

From Latin *emporium* (market, marketplace), from Greek *emporion* (trading place).
