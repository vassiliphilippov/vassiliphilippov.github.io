milling

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the raised or corrugated edge around a coin, designed to prevent clipping or counterfeiting
corrugated edge of a coin

Examples

Collectors value coins with well-preserved milling along the edge.
The distinctive milling on the coin helped protect it from forgery.
A worn-down milling can affect a coin's value to numismatists.

Etymology

From the verb 'mill', referring to the machine process used to create the edge.
