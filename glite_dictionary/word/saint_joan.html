Saint Joan

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈdʒoʊn / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈdʒəʊn / (BrE)
(c. 1412–1431) A French heroine and military leader, known for leading French resistance against the English and for having Charles VII crowned, before being executed for heresy. Also known as Joan of Arc.
French heroine and saint, also known as Joan of Arc

Examples

Many historians view Saint Joan as a symbol of courage and faith.
The play about Saint Joan dramatizes her trial and execution.
Saint Joan was canonized by the Roman Catholic Church in 1920.

Related Terms
