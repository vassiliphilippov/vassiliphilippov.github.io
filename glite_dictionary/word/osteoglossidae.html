Osteoglossidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːstiəʊˈɡlɑːsɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌɒstiəʊˈɡlæsɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of large freshwater fishes that includes bandfish and bonytongues
family of freshwater fishes

Examples

The Osteoglossidae family includes some of the world's largest freshwater fish species.
Researchers studied the diet of Osteoglossidae to better understand aquatic food webs.
Several aquarium enthusiasts are fascinated by the rare members of the Osteoglossidae family.

Etymology

From Greek osteon (bone) + glōssa (tongue), referring to bony structure on the tongue.
