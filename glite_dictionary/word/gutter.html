gutter

1. VERB

/ ˈɡʌtər / (AmE), / ˈɡʌtə / (BrE)
to fit or install gutters along the edge of a roof or structure
fit with gutters

Examples

The workers guttered the new apartment building in only two days.
Next week, we plan to gutter our old farmhouse to prevent water damage.
All structures on this street were recently guttered as part of the city's drainage improvement project.

cut channels into a surface

Examples

Over years, flowing water slowly guttered the stone path.
The melting ice guttered the pavement, leaving irregular grooves.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡʌtə / (BrE)
a situation of failure, ruin, or complete misfortune resulting in wasted effort or loss
state of failure or ruin

Examples

After his business went bankrupt, he felt like his life was in the gutter.
Years of hard work ended up in the gutter after the project was canceled.
Many fear financial mistakes could land their retirement plans in the gutter.

Synset Examples

his career was in the gutter

pensions are in the toilet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruin - a state of complete destruction or loss
  • Synonym: failure - lack of success or falling short
  • Antonym: success - the accomplishment of an aim or purpose

Etymology

Metaphorical use of 'gutter' (a channel for waste water), suggesting being in a low or degraded state.

7. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌt̬.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡʌt.ər / (BrE)
A narrow channel fixed to the edge of a roof or along the eaves to collect and carry away rainwater.
rainwater channel on roof

Examples

Clogged gutters can cause water to overflow and damage your house.
The old house needed new gutters to protect its foundation from rainwater.
After the heavy storm, leaves filled the gutter along the roof.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drainpipe - pipe that carries rainwater from a gutter down a building
  • Synonym: eavestrough - Canadian term for a gutter fixed to roof eaves

Etymology

From Old French *goutiere* (gutter, spout), from *goute* (a drop), from Latin *gutta* (drop).
