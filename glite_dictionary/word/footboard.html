footboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊt.bɔrd / (AmE), / ˈfʊt.bɔːd / (BrE)
a narrow platform or board on which to stand or place one's feet, often found on vehicles such as trains or beds for support or protection
narrow board for standing or foot support

Examples

The old steam train had a metal footboard running along its side for passengers to step on.
He rested his feet on the footboard while sitting on the hospital bed.
The carpenter replaced the broken footboard on the vintage sleigh.

Etymology

Formed from 'foot' + 'board', referring to a board for the feet.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈfʊtbɔːd / (BrE)
a vertical board or panel at the foot end of a bedstead
board at end of a bed

Examples

The antique bed had an ornate footboard made of carved oak.
Children sometimes sit on the footboard to tie their shoes in the morning.
He accidentally bumped his shin against the footboard while making the bed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: headboard - a board at the head end of a bedstead

Etymology

From 'foot' + 'board', referring to its position at the foot of the bed.
