Dasyprocta

1. NOUN

/ ˌdæsiˈprɑːktə / (AmE), / ˌdæsiˈprɒktə / (BrE)
A genus of rodents in the family Dasyproctidae, commonly known as agoutis, native to Central and South America.
genus of rodents (agoutis)

Examples

The Dasyprocta genus includes several species of rodents commonly called agoutis.
Researchers observed the feeding habits of Dasyprocta in the Amazon rainforest.
A Dasyprocta was seen foraging for nuts near the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agouti - a rodent of the genus Dasyprocta

Etymology

From Greek dasys (hairy) + proktos (anus), referring to the animal's hairy hind parts.

/ ˌdæsiˈproʊktə / (AmE), / ˌdasiˈprɒktə / (BrE)
A genus of South American rodents known as agoutis, belonging to the family Dasyproctidae.
genus of rodents called agoutis

Examples

The Dasyprocta genus is native to Central and South America, where agoutis play important ecological roles.
Scientists observed several species within the Dasyprocta genus in the Amazon rainforest.
Unlike many other rodents, members of the Dasyprocta genus are diurnal and live in forested habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agouti - a rodent of the genus Dasyprocta, native to Central and South America

Etymology
