Polygala paucifolia

1. NOUN

/ pəˌlɪɡələ ˌpɔːsəˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / pɒˌlɪɡələ ˌpɔːsɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A trailing perennial plant of eastern North America, also known as fringed polygala, with leaves resembling wintergreen and typically rosy-purple flowers with wing-like sepals.
North American perennial plant with rosy-purple flowers

Examples

The forest floor was carpeted with Polygala paucifolia, its purple flowers standing out among the green foliage.
Botanists often seek Polygala paucifolia in springtime for its distinctive winged sepals and rosy color.
She identified the tiny plant as Polygala paucifolia because of its wintergreen-like leaves and delicate blossoms.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Polygala' (the genus name, meaning 'much milk') and 'paucifolia' ('few-leaved').
