to derange

1. VERB

/ dɪˈreɪndʒ / (AmE), / dɪˈreɪndʒ / (BrE)
to cause someone to lose mental clarity or balance; to make insane or mentally disturbed.
make insane; cause mental disturbance

Examples

The shocking events seemed to derange his mind completely.
Grief can temporarily derange even the most rational person.
The scientist warned that the drug might derange normal brain functions.

Synset Examples

The death of his parents unbalanced him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unbalance - to throw out of mental balance
  • Synonym: to madden - to make someone become insane or extremely angry

Etymology

From French 'déranger', meaning to disturb or disarrange.

2. VERB

/ dɪˈreɪndʒ / (AmE), / dɪˈreɪndʒ / (BrE)
To cause (something or someone) to be in a state of confusion or disorder; to disrupt the normal order or arrangement of.
to throw into confusion or disorder

Examples

The unexpected news served to completely derange his thoughts.
A sudden power outage can derange the operations of a busy office.
Their arrival late in the process threatened to derange the entire schedule.

Synset Examples

Fundamentalists threaten to perturb the social order

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disorder - to disturb the regular arrangement or functioning of
  • Antonym: to organize - to arrange or order methodically

Etymology

From French *déranger* (to disturb, disarrange), from Old French *desrengier*, from *des-* (dis-) + *rengier* (to arrange).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00277400-v
Synset POS: v
