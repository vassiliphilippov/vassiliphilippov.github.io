locket

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːk.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɒk.ɪt / (BrE)
A small ornamental case, typically worn on a chain around the neck, that often holds a picture, a lock of hair, or other small keepsake.
small case on a necklace for keepsakes

Examples

She wore a gold locket with a tiny photo of her grandmother inside.
The antique locket contained a faded picture and a single curl of hair.
For her birthday, he gave her a silver locket engraved with her initials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pendant - an ornament that hangs from a chain worn around the neck

Etymology

From Old French 'locquet' (a small lock), diminutive of 'loc' (lock).
