James Monroe

1. PROPN

/ dʒeɪmz mənˈroʊ / (AmE), / dʒeɪmz mənˈrəʊ / (BrE)
(1758–1831) The fifth President of the United States (1817–1825), known for the Monroe Doctrine, a key element of U.S. foreign policy.
5th U.S. President; author of Monroe Doctrine

Examples

James Monroe served two terms as the President of the United States in the early 19th century.
The James Monroe presidency is often associated with the 'Era of Good Feelings.'
The Monroe Doctrine, crafted under James Monroe, shaped American foreign policy for decades.
