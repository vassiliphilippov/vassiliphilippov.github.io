cyclonical

1. ADJ

/ saɪˈklɑnɪkəl / (AmE), / saɪˈklɒnɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a violent tropical storm or cyclone
relating to a tropical storm or cyclone

Examples

The region suffered cyclonical winds that uprooted most of the trees.
Experts warned residents to prepare for cyclonical activity during the coming months.
The damage was attributed to cyclonical forces accompanying the tropical storm.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyclonic - relating to a cyclone; violently stormy or windy

Etymology

Formed from 'cyclone' (from Greek 'kyklōn', meaning 'moving in a circle') and the suffix '-ical.'

2. ADJ

/ saɪˈklɑnɪkəl / (AmE), / saɪˈklɒnɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the atmospheric conditions around a low-pressure center, as in a cyclone
relating to atmospheric low-pressure centers

Examples

Meteorologists observed a cyclonical movement of air developing over the Atlantic early in the week.
The sudden drop in pressure created cyclonical turbulence near the coastal regions.
Satellite imagery showed a distinct cyclonical swirl forming in the cloud patterns.

Synset Examples

cyclonic cloud pattern

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyclonic - relating to or characteristic of a cyclone or low-pressure center
  • Antonym: anticyclonic - relating to a high-pressure weather system, opposite of cyclonic

Etymology

From 'cyclone' + '-ical', patterned after 'cyclonic'.

WordNet ID: oewn-03069628-a
Synset POS: a
