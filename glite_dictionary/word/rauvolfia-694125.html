rauvolfia

1. NOUN

/ raʊˈvɑlfiə / (AmE), / raʊˈvɒlfɪə / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs and small trees native to tropical regions, with leaves in whorls and cymose flowers, yielding substances used medicinally as emetics, purgatives, or antihypertensive agents.
genus of plants used in medicine
medical

Examples

The rauvolfia plant has been used in traditional medicine to treat high blood pressure.
Researchers are studying extracts from rauvolfia for their potential antihypertensive effects.
Some modern pharmaceuticals are derived from the roots of rauvolfia species.

Etymology

Named after the German physician Leonhard Rauwolf (1535–1596).
