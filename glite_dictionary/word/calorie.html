calorie

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæləri / (AmE), / ˈkaləri / (BrE)
A unit of energy equal to the amount of heat required to raise the temperature of one kilogram of water by one degree Celsius, commonly used to indicate the energy content of food.
unit of energy (food/nutrition)

Examples

It is important to know how many calories you consume each day.
This snack contains only fifty calories per serving.
Athletes often use up thousands of calories during intense training sessions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kilojoule - SI unit of energy, used as alternative to calorie
  • Degree Lower: joule - basic SI unit of energy

Etymology

From French *calorie*, from Latin *calor* (heat).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæləri / (AmE), / ˈkæləri / (BrE)
A unit of heat energy, equal to the amount of heat needed to raise the temperature of 1 gram of water by 1 degree Celsius at standard atmospheric pressure.
unit of heat energy

Examples

Nutrition labels often list the number of calories per serving.
One calorie is the amount of energy needed to raise 1 gram of water by 1°C.
Scientists accurately measure the calorie content of different foods in a laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kilojoule - unit of energy equal to 1000 joules

Etymology

From French 'calorie', from Latin 'calor' (heat).
