mortifying

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɔrtəˌfaɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtɪfaɪɪŋ / (BrE)
causing someone to feel extreme embarrassment, shame, or humiliation
causing embarrassment or shame

Examples

It was mortifying to trip on stage in front of so many people.
She found it mortifying when her mistake was announced in the meeting.
The mortifying experience haunted him for weeks.

Synset Examples

the embarrassing moment when she found her petticoat down around her ankles
it was mortifying to know he had heard every word

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embarrassing - causing embarrassment, awkward or uncomfortable
  • Synonym: humiliating - making someone feel ashamed and foolish

Etymology

From Latin 'mortificare' (to put to death, subdue), via Old French 'mortifier'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɔːrtɪˌfaɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtɪfaɪɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing embarrassment, shame, or the painful awareness of your shortcomings.
causing embarrassment or shame

Examples

It was mortifying to forget my lines in front of the whole class.
She found the experience of being corrected in public deeply mortifying.
Missing the open goal was a mortifying moment for the young player.

Synset Examples

golf is a humbling game

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humiliating - making someone feel ashamed or foolish; similar in meaning
  • Synonym: embarrassing - causing someone to feel awkward or self-conscious; synonym
  • Antonym: exalting - raising in rank, power, or character; opposite meaning

Etymology

From Latin 'mortificare' meaning 'to put to death', later used figuratively to mean 'humiliate'.

Source: WordNet
