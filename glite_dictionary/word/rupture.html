rupture

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌptʃər / (AmE), / ˈrʌptʃə / (BrE)
the act or process of breaking or bursting suddenly and noisily
sudden, noisy break or burst

Examples

The dam collapsed with a deafening rupture that echoed across the valley.
A sudden rupture in the gas pipe caused an explosion.
The rupture of the balloon startled everyone in the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break - the action of separating into pieces
  • Synonym: burst - an act of bursting suddenly

Etymology

From Latin ruptūra (a breaking), from rumpere (to break).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrʌptʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈrʌptʃə / (BrE)
a serious break or separation in a relationship or between groups, often causing hostility or tension
serious break in relations

Examples

The political rupture between the two parties led to years of mutual distrust.
After the argument, there was a clear rupture in their long-standing friendship.
Efforts to repair the rupture in diplomatic relations continued for months.

Synset Examples

they hoped to avoid a break in relations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break - an interruption or ending of friendly relations
  • Antonym: reconciliation - the restoration of friendly relations

Etymology

From Latin ruptūra (a breaking, rupture), from rumpere (to break).

3. VERB

/ ˈrʌptʃər / (AmE), / ˈrʌptʃə / (BrE)
to break or cause to break suddenly and violently; to separate or burst apart abruptly
break or burst apart suddenly

Examples

The intense pressure caused the pipe to rupture and leak water.
He feared that the stress would rupture the thin membrane.
If you drop the glass bottle, it may rupture on impact.

Synset Examples

The rope snapped
tear the paper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tear - to pull apart or destroy by force
  • Synonym: snap - to break suddenly with a sharp sound

Etymology

From Latin ruptūra (a breaking), from rumpere (to break, burst).

4. NOUN

/ ˈrʌptʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈrʌptʃə / (BrE)
The state of being torn or burst open, especially as a result of force or injury.
state of being torn open

Examples

The rupture of the dam caused flooding in the valley.
He suffered a rupture of an artery during surgery.
A sudden rupture in the pipeline interrupted the water supply.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tear - an act of tearing or the result of being torn

Etymology
