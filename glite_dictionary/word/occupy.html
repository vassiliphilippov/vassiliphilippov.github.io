to occupy

1. VERB

/ ˈɑːkjəˌpaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɒkjʊˌpaɪ / (BrE)
to live in or have one's home in a particular place
to live in (a place)

Examples

After the renovation, the family will occupy the newly expanded apartment.
She was delighted to occupy a small cottage by the sea for the summer.
Several graduate students occupy the upper floor of the old house.

Synset Examples

She resides in Princeton
he occupies two rooms on the top floor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inhabit - to live in a place
  • Antonym: to vacate - to leave a place that was previously occupied

Etymology

From Latin occupare (to seize, take possession of).
