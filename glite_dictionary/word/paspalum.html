Paspalum

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæspələm / (AmE), / ˈpaspələm / (BrE)
A genus of perennial grasses native to warm regions, including several species important as forage and turf grasses.
genus of perennial grasses

Examples

The Paspalum genus includes many species used for golf course turf.
Researchers studied the drought tolerance of various Paspalum varieties.
Some types of Paspalum are commonly found in tropical pastures.

Etymology

From Latin *Paspalum*, from Greek *paspalos* (a kind of millet).
