tilt

1. NOUN

/ tɪlt / (AmE), / tɪlt / (BrE)
a slant or inclination away from a vertical or horizontal position
a slant or inclination

Examples

The old chair had a noticeable tilt to the left side.
After the earthquake, the building's tilt was detected by engineers.
You could see the tilt of the picture frame from across the room.

Synset Examples

the tower had a pronounced tilt
the ship developed a list to starboard
he walked with a heavy inclination to the right

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inclination - the angle or degree of something sloping away from the vertical or horizontal
  • Synonym: list - a tilt to one side, especially of a ship
  • Antonym: vertical - being at a right angle to the horizon

a contest or combat between two mounted knights who charge at each other with lances, especially as a form of medieval tournament
medieval jousting contest

Examples

The knights prepared for the tilt, adjusting their armor carefully before riding into the arena.

Related Terms
