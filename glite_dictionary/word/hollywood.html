Hollywood

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɑliˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈhɒliˌwʊd / (BrE)
A district of Los Angeles known as the historic center of the American film industry.
district of Los Angeles, film-industry center

Examples

Many aspiring actors move to Hollywood hoping to make it big in movies.
The golden age of Hollywood produced some of the most iconic films in history.
Tourists flock to Hollywood to see the famous Walk of Fame.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Los Angeles - a large city in California, USA

Etymology

Named after the holly trees once growing in the area; adopted as a name for the district in 1887.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09086427-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑliˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈhɒliˌwʊd / (BrE)
the American film industry, especially as represented by the studios and celebrities based in Hollywood, Los Angeles
the American film industry, especially in Los Angeles

Examples

Many aspiring actors move to Hollywood in hopes of making it big in the film industry.
The latest trends in Hollywood often influence movie productions around the world.
She dreams of one day writing a script that will be produced in Hollywood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the silver screen - the film industry or movies collectively
  • Degree Lower: Bollywood - the Indian Hindi-language film industry

Etymology

Named after Hollywood, a district in Los Angeles, California, known as the center of the US film industry.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08085433-n
Synset POS: n

/ ˈhɑliˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈhɒliˌwʊd / (BrE)
of or relating to the film industry in the United States, especially as represented by movies produced in or associated with Hollywood, California.
relating to the U.S. film industry

Examples

She landed a role in a Hollywood blockbuster that became an international hit.
Many Hollywood scripts tend to follow familiar formulas.
He dreams of becoming a Hollywood director one day.

Synset Examples

Etymology

Named after Hollywood, a district in Los Angeles, known as the center of the American film industry since the early 20th century.

an atmosphere or style that is showy, glamorous, or superficially attractive, regarded as characteristic of the American film industry
flashy style typical of US film industry

Examples

5. ADJ

/ ˈhɑːliˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈhɒliˌwʊd / (BrE)
(of style or behavior) flashy, showy, and often in poor taste; reminiscent of the extravagance and superficiality associated with the American film industry.
flashy or showy in a vulgar way

Examples

The club's neon lights and Hollywood decor gave it a gaudy atmosphere.
He arrived at the party in a Hollywood suit that turned every head in the room.
She prefers elegance over Hollywood glamour when she chooses her evening outfits.

Synset Examples

young white women dressed Hollywood style
Hollywood philandering

Related Terms

  • Synonym: showy - attracting attention by being bright or ostentatious
  • Synonym: flashy - bright or ostentatious in a way that attracts attention
  • Antonym: understated - presented or expressed in a subtle and effective way; not showy

Etymology

From Hollywood, the district in Los Angeles known for the American film industry, noted for its glamour and excess.
