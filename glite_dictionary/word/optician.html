optician

1. NOUN

/ ɑpˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / ɒpˈtɪʃən / (BrE)
A person qualified to make and supply eyeglasses and contact lenses to correct vision defects, usually according to prescriptions from an optometrist or ophthalmologist.
maker and supplier of eyeglasses or contact lenses

Examples

The optician adjusted my glasses to fit perfectly.
She took her prescription to the optician to have new lenses made.
After my eye test, the optician helped me choose a stylish frame.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ophthalmologist - a medical doctor specializing in the diagnosis and treatment of eye disorders
  • Synonym: optometrist - a person trained to examine eyes and prescribe corrective lenses

Etymology

From French *opticien*, from Greek *optikos* (of sight or seeing).
