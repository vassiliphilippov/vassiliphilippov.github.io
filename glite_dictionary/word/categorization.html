categorization

1. NOUN

/ ˌkætəɡəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkætəɡəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of placing people or things into groups or classes based on shared qualities or characteristics
the act of grouping into categories

Examples

The categorization of books by genre helps readers find what they like.
Effective categorization is essential for storing digital photos.
Her job involves the categorization of customer feedback into different groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classification - the process of arranging in classes or categories

Etymology

From Greek 'katēgoria' (category) via Medieval Latin 'categorization'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkætəɡəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkætəɡəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of placing people or things into groups or categories based on shared characteristics
grouping by type or class

Examples

The categorization of books by genre makes them easier to find in the library.
Accurate categorization of data is crucial for effective analysis.
Many people dislike the categorization of individuals based solely on age or background.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classification - the act of arranging things into classes or categories

Etymology

From 'category' plus the suffix '-ization', ultimately from Greek 'katēgoria' (accusation, category).

3. NOUN

/ ˌkæt.ə.ɡə.rəˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkat.ɪ.ɡə.raɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of placing things or ideas into groups or classes based on shared qualities or characteristics
process of grouping things into categories

Examples

The categorization of animals helps scientists understand their relationships.
Her research focuses on the categorization of emotions in children.
Effective categorization can make large sets of data easier to analyze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classification - the act of organizing something by class or category

Etymology

From category + -ization; first attested in English in the 19th century.
