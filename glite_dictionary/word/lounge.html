lounge

1. VERB

/ laʊndʒ / (AmE), / laʊndʒ / (BrE)
to sit, lie, or stand in a relaxed or lazy way; to recline comfortably without effort
sit or recline comfortably

Examples

After a long day, she liked to lounge on the balcony and read a book.
We spent the afternoon lounging by the pool, enjoying the sunshine.
He lounged in his favorite chair, completely at ease.

Synset Examples

He was lounging on the sofa

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recline - to lean or lie back in a relaxed position
  • Synonym: sprawl - to sit or lie with arms and legs spread out in a relaxed way

Etymology

Probably from French 's'allonger' (to stretch out), from Latin 'longus' (long).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01531127-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ laʊndʒ / (AmE), / laʊndʒ / (BrE)
a room in a public place, such as a hotel or airport, with comfortable seating where people can wait or relax
waiting or relaxation room in public places

Examples

We waited in the airport lounge until our flight was called.
The hotel lounge offered free drinks and snacks for guests.
He relaxed in the comfortable lounge while waiting for his meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waiting room - a room where people wait, especially before being seen by someone such as a doctor
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03697143-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ laʊndʒ / (AmE), / laʊndʒ / (BrE)
to spend time in a place without any clear purpose, often in a relaxed or lazy manner
to linger or loiter aimlessly

Examples

The teenagers would often lounge around the shopping mall after school.
Several people were lounging near the entrance, waiting for their friends to arrive.
It's not allowed to lounge in the hotel lobby unless you are a guest.

Synset Examples

The high school students like to loiter in the Central Square
Who is this man that is hanging around the department?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to loiter - to stand or wait around idly without apparent purpose
  • Synonym: to linger - to stay in a place longer than necessary, typically because of a reluctance to leave

Etymology

From French *s'allonger* (to stretch oneself out), influenced by English dialect forms.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02645159-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ laʊndʒ / (AmE), / laʊndʒ / (BrE)
A long upholstered seat designed to accommodate more than one person, often used for relaxing in living rooms or lounges.
long upholstered seat for several people

Examples

We bought a new lounge for the living room to fit all our guests comfortably.
She relaxed on the big lounge while reading her favorite book.
The hotel suite featured a spacious lounge upholstered in soft leather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sofa - an upholstered seat with arms and a back, for two or more people
  • Synonym: couch - a long, comfortable seat for two or more people

Etymology

From French *s'allonger* meaning 'to stretch oneself out'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04263630-n
Synset POS: n
