attic

1. NOUN

/ ˈætɪk / (AmE), / ˈætɪk / (BrE)
(informal) A person's head, especially used in a humorous way.
informal term for head
informal slang

Examples

You should use your attic before making such a decision.
He gave himself a bump on the attic when he stood up too quickly.
She needs to keep her attic clear if she wants to ace the test.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noggin - informal term for head

2. NOUN

/ ˈætɪk / (AmE), / ˈætɪk / (BrE)
(architecture) A low wall built above the main cornice of a building, often used to conceal the roof.
a low architectural wall above a cornice

Examples

The classical temple featured an elegant attic that hid the sloping roof from view.
The restoration project required repairs to the damaged attic above the entablature.
Many Renaissance buildings are characterized by a prominent attic crowning the façade.

Etymology

From Attic, referring to the architectural style of Attica, the region around Athens in Greece.

3. NOUN

/ ˈætɪk / (AmE), / ˈætɪk / (BrE)
A space or room just below the roof of a house, often used for storage.
space or room below roof for storage

Examples

We found a box of old photographs in the attic while cleaning the house.
During storms, the attic often makes strange creaking sounds.
She converted the dusty attic into a quiet office space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loft - an upper room or space just under the roof, often used for storage
  • Antonym: basement - a floor of a building that is partly or entirely below ground level

Etymology

From Latin 'Atticus', relating to Attica, a region of Greece; originally describing a style of architecture.
