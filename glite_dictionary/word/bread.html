bread

1. VERB

/ brɛd / (AmE), / brɛd / (BrE)
to cover food with bread crumbs before cooking, typically as preparation for frying or baking
coat with bread crumbs (culinary)

Examples

Make sure to bread the chicken thoroughly before placing it in the oven.
Some recipes require you to bread the vegetables for extra crunch.
She breaded the fish fillets before frying them in hot oil.

Synset Examples

bread the pork chops before frying them

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coat - to cover with a layer of a substance

2. NOUN

/ brɛd / (AmE), / brɛd / (BrE)
A staple food made by baking dough of flour or meal, typically raised with yeast or baking powder.
baked food made from flour dough

Examples

He bought a fresh loaf of bread from the bakery this morning.
A slice of bread with butter makes a simple snack.
Homemade bread often tastes better than store-bought varieties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loaf - a quantity of bread shaped and baked in one piece

Etymology

From Old English 'bread' (piece of food, loaf).

3. NOUN

/ brɛd / (AmE), / brɛd / (BrE)
(informal) Money.
slang for money
informal slang

Examples

He worked extra hours to earn more bread for his family.
I've got to go to the bank and pick up some bread before shopping.
They decided to save their bread for a summer vacation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dough - (slang) money
  • Synonym: cash - money in the form of notes and coins
  • Synonym: money - medium of exchange for goods and services

Etymology

From the association between bread as a staple food and the means to earn a living; popularized in American slang from the 20th century.
