George II of Great Britain

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɔrdʒ ðə ˈsɛkənd əv ɡrəˈbɪtən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːdʒ ðə ˈsɛkənd əv ˌɡreɪt ˈbrɪt.ən / (BrE)
(1683–1760) King of Great Britain and Ireland and Elector of Hanover, who reigned from 1727 to 1760.
British king (1727–1760)

Examples

George II of Great Britain was crowned in 1727 and reigned until his death in 1760.
During the War of Austrian Succession, George II of Great Britain led British forces at the Battle of Dettingen.
The reign of George II of Great Britain saw the expansion of British influence in North America.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: George I of Great Britain - His father, King of Great Britain before George II
  • Degree Higher: George III of Great Britain - His grandson and successor as King of Great Britain
