counterdemonstrator

1. NOUN

/ ˌkaʊntərˈdɛmənˌstreɪtər / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəˈdɛmənstreɪtə / (BrE)
a person who takes part in a public protest to oppose another demonstration
person who opposes a protest with a counter-protest

Examples

Hundreds of counterdemonstrators gathered across the street from the main protest, chanting and holding signs.
Police kept a close watch as counterdemonstrators arrived to voice their opposition to the rally.
As the event progressed, tensions rose between the protesters and the counterdemonstrators nearby.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: demonstrator - person participating in a public protest

Etymology

From counter- (opposing) + demonstrator (one taking part in a public protest or demonstration).
