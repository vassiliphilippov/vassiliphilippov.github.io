St. Thomas Aquinas

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈtɑməs əˈkwaɪnəs / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈtɒməs əˈkwaɪnəs / (BrE)
(c. 1225–1274) Italian philosopher, theologian, and Doctor of the Church, noted for integrating faith and reason and presenting philosophical arguments for the existence of God.
