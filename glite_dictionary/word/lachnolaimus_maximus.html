Lachnolaimus maximus

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæk.noʊˈleɪ.məs ˈmæk.sə.məs / (AmE), / ˌlak.nəʊˈleɪ.məs ˈmak.sɪ.məs / (BrE)
A large wrasse found in the western Atlantic Ocean, known as the hogfish, with males having a snout resembling that of a pig.
a large Atlantic wrasse; hogfish species

Examples

Marine biologists observed a group of Lachnolaimus maximus near the coral reefs of Florida.
The distinctive snout of the male Lachnolaimus maximus gives the species its common name, hogfish.
Sport fishermen in the Caribbean often prize Lachnolaimus maximus for its size and fighting spirit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hogfish - a large wrasse of the western Atlantic, known for its pig-like snout

Etymology

From Greek *lachne* (woolly) + *laimos* (throat) and Latin *maximus* (greatest).
