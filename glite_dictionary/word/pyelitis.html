pyelitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪəˈlaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌpʌɪəˈlʌɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the renal pelvis, typically due to bacterial infection
inflammation of the renal pelvis
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with pyelitis after presenting with fever and lower back pain.
Antibiotic treatment is usually prescribed to manage cases of pyelitis.
Untreated pyelitis can sometimes lead to more serious kidney infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nephritis - inflammation of the kidney

Etymology

From Greek 'pyelos' (trough, basin, pelvis) + '-itis' (inflammation).
