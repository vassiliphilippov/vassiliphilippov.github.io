holometabola

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊloʊməˈtæbələ / (AmE), / ˌhəʊləməˈtæbələ / (BrE)
A group of insects that undergo complete metamorphosis, including distinct larval, pupal, and adult stages.
insects with complete metamorphosis

Examples

Beetles and butterflies are examples of holometabola because they have distinct larval, pupal, and adult stages.
Scientists study holometabola to understand the evolution of insect development.
Unlike grasshoppers, which are hemimetabolous, flies belong to the holometabola group.

Etymology

From Greek holos (whole) + metabole (change).
