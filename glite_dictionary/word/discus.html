discus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪs.kəs / (AmE), / ˈdɪs.kəs / (BrE)
A heavy flat round object that athletes throw as far as possible in a sporting competition.
heavy round object thrown in athletics

Examples

She set a new record by throwing the discus over sixty meters.
The athlete trained every day to improve his discus technique.
During the competition, each participant was given three attempts to throw the discus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: javelin - A light spear thrown in track and field competitions
  • Synonym: shot put - Heavy ball thrown in athletics

Etymology

From Latin 'discus', from Greek 'diskos' (quoit, disk).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪskəs / (AmE), / ˈdɪskəs / (BrE)
an athletic event in which a heavy flat disc is thrown as far as possible; also, the disc used in this sport
athletic event or object thrown in it

Examples

She trained for months to compete in the discus at the national championships.
The athlete's discus landed several meters beyond the previous record.
Officials measured the distance from where the discus hit the ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: javelin - athletic event in which a spear is thrown
  • Synonym: shot put - athletic event in which a heavy spherical object is thrown

Etymology

From Latin 'discus' (disk, quoit), from Greek 'diskos'.
