augury

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɡ.jə.ri / (AmE), / ˈɔː.ɡjʊ.ri / (BrE)
a sign or event interpreted as an indication of future events, especially of an important or significant nature
a sign or omen of future events

Examples

Some believed the sudden storm was an augury of doom.
The flight of birds at dawn was seen as an augury by the ancient priests.
Many people nervously watched for an augury before beginning the ceremony.

Synset Examples

he hoped it was an augury
it was a sign from God

Related Terms

  • Synonym: omen - an event regarded as a portent of good or evil
  • Synonym: portent - a sign or warning that something momentous is likely to happen

Etymology

From Latin augurium (divination, omen), from augur (a religious official who interpreted omens).
