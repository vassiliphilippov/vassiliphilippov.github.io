HTLV-1

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪtʃ ˌtiː ˌɛl ˈviː wʌn / (AmE), / ˌeɪtʃ ˌtiː ˌɛl ˈviː wʌn / (BrE)
a retrovirus (Human T-cell Leukemia Virus type 1) that causes T-cell leukemia and other diseases in humans
retrovirus causing T-cell leukemia
medical

Examples

HTLV-1 infection can lead to serious illnesses such as adult T-cell leukemia.
Researchers continue to study the transmission patterns of HTLV-1 in different populations.
Proper screening for HTLV-1 is important for blood donation safety.

Etymology

Abbreviation of Human T-cell Leukemia Virus type 1.
