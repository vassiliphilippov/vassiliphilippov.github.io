albescent

1. ADJ

/ ælˈbɛsənt / (AmE), / ælˈbɛsənt / (BrE)
becoming or shading into white; turning whitish in colour.
becoming whitish; tending towards white

Examples

The flowers are albescent at the tips, fading gently from pink to a creamy white.
As the fruit ripens, its skin becomes albescent, signaling it is ready to eat.
Dawn revealed an albescent sky, hinting at the coming daylight.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: white - of the color white
  • Degree Lower: pale - having a lighter or less intense color

Etymology

From Latin 'albescent-' (becoming white), from 'albesco' (to become white), from 'albus' (white).
