twang

1. NOUN

/ twæŋ / (AmE), / twæŋ / (BrE)
a quality of speech characterized by an exaggerated nasal sound, often associated with certain regional accents or dialects
nasal quality in speech or accent

Examples

Her Southern twang was unmistakable as she recited the poem.
He tried to hide his regional twang when speaking on the radio.
The comedian exaggerated his twang for comic effect during the performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drawl - a slow, lazy way of speaking, often with prolonged vowel sounds

Etymology

Related to the verb 'twang' (to make a vibrating sound), from Middle English 'twangen'; evolved to refer to the nasal resonance in speech.

2. VERB

/ twæŋ / (AmE), / twæŋ / (BrE)
to pronounce or say (something) with a nasal tone
pronounce in a nasal tone

Examples

She tends to twang her vowels when she gets nervous.
The announcer twanged certain words so much that his accent was unmistakable.
He twangs his speech in a way that makes his regional origin obvious.

Etymology

Imitative origin, representing the sound of a vibrating string or nasal voice.

3. VERB

/ twæŋ / (AmE), / twæŋ / (BrE)
to cause to make a sharp, vibrating sound, especially by plucking a string or similar object
make a sharp, vibrating sound

Examples

She carefully twanged the banjo string, producing a bright note.
He twanged the rubber band and watched it shoot across the room.
The musician twanged his guitar several times to tune it.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pluck - to pull and release (a string), causing it to vibrate

Etymology

Imitative, representing the sound itself; first recorded in the 16th century.

5. NOUN

/ twæŋ / (AmE), / twæŋ / (BrE)
a sharp, vibrating sound, especially one produced by plucking a string
sharp vibrating sound, as from a plucked string

Examples

The twang of the guitar string filled the silent room.
Every arrow was released with a satisfying twang from the bow.
She heard a metallic twang as he snapped the wire with his fingers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ring - a resonant or ringing sound
  • Antonym: thud - a dull sound, as of something heavy hitting a surface

Etymology

Imitative; of echoic origin, referring to the sound itself.

6. VERB

/ twæŋ / (AmE), / twæŋ / (BrE)
to produce or emit a sharp, ringing, vibrating sound, especially as made by a plucked string or tightly stretched object
make a sharp, vibrating sound

Examples

She accidentally bumped the ruler, causing it to twang loudly on the desk.
As the archer released the arrow, the bowstring twanged sharply in the quiet air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clang - make a loud, resonant metallic sound
  • Synonym: thrum - make a continuous rhythmic humming sound

Etymology
