progestational

1. ADJ

/ proʊˌdʒɛˈsteɪʃənəl / (AmE), / prəʊˌdʒɛˈsteɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to the phase of the female reproductive cycle following ovulation, marked by physiological changes that prepare the body for possible pregnancy.
relating to the phase after ovulation in the reproductive cycle
medical

Examples

Certain hormones cause progestational changes in the uterine lining to support potential implantation.
The progestational phase of the menstrual cycle is critical for preparing the body for pregnancy.
Doctors may prescribe progestational agents to treat specific reproductive health conditions.

Etymology

From 'pro-' (before) and 'gestational,' from Latin 'gestatio' (carrying, pregnancy).

2. ADJ

/ proʊˌdʒɛˈsteɪʃənl / (AmE), / prəʊˌdʒɛˈsteɪʃənl / (BrE)
Relating to or denoting progesterone or drugs that produce effects similar to those of progesterone.
relating to progesterone or similar drugs
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a progestational agent to help regulate her menstrual cycle.
Certain birth control pills contain progestational compounds to mimic the effects of natural hormones.
Researchers are studying the progestational activity of new synthetic hormones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: progesterone - a hormone involved in the menstrual cycle and pregnancy

Etymology

From 'progesterone' + '-al', indicating relation to progesterone.
