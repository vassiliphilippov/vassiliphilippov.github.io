to caseate

1. VERB

/ ˈkeɪsiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkeɪsiːeɪt / (BrE)
(of tissue) to become cheeselike in appearance, especially due to certain types of cell death or necrosis.
become cheeselike (esp. tissue)
medical

Examples

Tuberculous lesions often caseate at their centers, giving a cheese-like appearance.
If the tissue continues to caseate, it may eventually liquefy and form a cavity.
Pathologists observed that the lymph node began to caseate as the infection progressed.

Synset Examples

necrotic tissue caseates

Etymology

From Latin 'caseus' (cheese) with scientific suffix '-ate'.

2. VERB

/ ˈkeɪsieɪt / (AmE), / ˈkeɪsiːeɪt / (BrE)
(of a substance, especially tissue) to turn into a cheese-like mass, as in certain forms of tissue degeneration or spoilage.
to turn into a cheese-like mass

Examples

Spoiled milk may caseate under certain conditions, forming a thick, cheese-like texture.
In tuberculosis, dead tissue can caseate and become crumbly and white.
If left in a warm place, the yogurt may begin to caseate over time.

Synset Examples

The milk caseated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheese - a solid food made from milk curds

Etymology

From Latin caseus (cheese).
