elder

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛldɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛldə / (BrE)
a senior member or official in certain Christian churches, often responsible for spiritual oversight and leadership within the congregation.
senior church official

Examples

The elder presided over the weekly church meeting and guided the congregation.
She was recently appointed as a elder in her local Presbyterian church.
The board of elders discussed important matters concerning the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deacon - a church officer who assists in ministry and service
  • Synonym: pastor - a minister in charge of a Christian church or congregation

Etymology

From Old English 'eldra', comparative of 'eald' (old), meaning 'older person'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛldɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛldə / (BrE)
a shrub or small tree of the genus Sambucus, found in temperate and subtropical regions of the Northern Hemisphere, which has white flowers and produces berrylike fruit
shrub or small tree with white flowers and berries

Examples

The elder in the garden is covered with fragrant white blossoms every spring.
Wildlife are often attracted to the elder for its rich, dark berries.
She harvested some berries from the elder behind the fence to make syrup.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: elderberry - the fruit of the elder tree

Etymology

From Old English *ellærn*, related to Middle Low German *ellen* (elder tree).

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛldɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛldə / (BrE)
a person who is older, especially someone who has more experience or authority due to their age
an older person

Examples

You should always respect your elders and listen to their advice.
At the meeting, the community's elders shared stories from the past.
She asked her elder for guidance before making a big decision.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: younger - a person who is younger than another
  • Synonym: senior - an older or more experienced person

Etymology

From Old English 'eldra,' comparative of 'eald' (old).

Synset POS: n

4. ADJ

/ ˈɛldɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛldə / (BrE)
used after a person's name to indicate that they are the older of two family members with the same name, especially to distinguish a father from his son.
the older of two people with the same name

Examples

His father, elder John Smith, founded the company in 1950.
James Miller, elder, was a respected judge in the community.
The artwork was created by Samuel Adams, elder, in the late eighteenth century.

Synset Examples

Bill Adams, Sr.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: junior - used after a person's name to show that they are the younger of two family members with the same name

Etymology

From Old English 'eldra', comparative of 'eald' (old).
