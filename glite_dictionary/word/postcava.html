postcava

1. NOUN

/ poʊstˈkeɪvə / (AmE), / pəʊstˈkeɪvə / (BrE)
a large vein that carries blood from the lower parts of the body to the right atrium of the heart, formed by the union of the two iliac veins
large vein returning blood to the heart
medical

Examples

The postcava is crucial for returning deoxygenated blood from the lower body to the heart.
A blockage in the postcava can lead to significant health complications.
During the surgery, the surgeon carefully identified the postcava before proceeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inferior vena cava - the large vein that carries blood from the lower body to the heart, synonymous with postcava

Etymology

From Latin 'post-' (after, behind) + 'cava' (hollow), relating to the vena cava.
