wolverine

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊl.vɚˌin / (AmE), / ˈwʊl.vəriːn / (BrE)
A large, stocky, and shaggy-furred carnivorous mammal (Gulo gulo) found in northern regions of North America and Eurasia, known for its strength and ferocity.
large, stocky carnivorous mammal

Examples

The elusive wolverine roamed the snow-covered forests far from human settlements.
A wolverine is known for its remarkable strength, often preying on animals much larger than itself.
Conservationists are concerned about the declining wolverine population due to climate change.

Etymology

From French *wolverine*, perhaps an alteration of *wolvering*, possibly influenced by the word 'wolf.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈwʊlvəˌrin / (AmE), / ˈwʊlvəriːn / (BrE)
A large, carnivorous mammal of the weasel family found mainly in northern Eurasia and North America, known for its strength and ferocity.
large, fierce musteline mammal

Examples

The wolverine is known for its incredible strength relative to its size.
Spotting a wolverine in the wild is extremely rare due to its elusive nature.
Researchers are studying the wolverine populations in Scandinavian forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weasel - small carnivorous mammal related to the wolverine
  • Synonym: badger - burrowing mammal related to the wolverine

Etymology

From French *wolverine*, from *wolver* (obsolete form), possibly from 'wolf'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
