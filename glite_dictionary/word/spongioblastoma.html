spongioblastoma

1. NOUN

/ spʌn.dʒioʊ.blæˈstoʊ.mə / (AmE), / spʌn.dʒi.əʊ.blæˈstəʊ.mə / (BrE)
a rapidly growing malignant brain tumor made up of spongioblasts, usually fatal
malignant brain tumor of spongioblasts
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with a spongioblastoma after reviewing the MRI results.
Despite aggressive treatment, spongioblastoma often has a poor prognosis.
Research into new therapies offers hope for those affected by spongioblastoma.

Etymology

From spongioblast, a type of embryonic cell, and -oma, a suffix meaning tumor.
