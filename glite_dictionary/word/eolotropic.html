eolotropic

1. ADJ

/ ˌiːəˈloʊtrəpɪk / (AmE), / ˌiːəˈlɒtrəpɪk / (BrE)
(of a material or substance) having properties that vary according to the direction along different axes.
having direction-dependent properties

Examples

The crystals were found to be eolotropic, showing different refractive indices along different axes.
Because the material is eolotropic, its strength varies depending on the direction of applied force.
Researchers analyzed the eolotropic characteristics to design a more efficient electronic device.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: isotropic - having identical properties in all directions

Etymology

From Greek 'eos' (dawn, east) and 'tropos' (turn), indicating turning or varying with direction.
