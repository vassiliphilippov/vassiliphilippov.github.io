squabbler

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɑb.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈskwɒb.lə / (BrE)
a person who quarrels or argues, especially over trivial matters
person who quarrels about small matters

Examples

Every group has at least one squabbler who turns minor issues into major arguments.
The siblings, known as constant squabblers, argued over who would sit in the front seat.
Instead of solving the problem, the squabbler kept bringing up unimportant details.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarreler - a person who quarrels

Etymology

From 'squabble' + '-er', where 'squabble' is of obscure origin, possibly imitative.
