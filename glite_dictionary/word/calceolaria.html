calceolaria

1. NOUN

/ ˌkælsiəˈlɛəriə / (AmE), / ˌkalsɪəˈlɛːrɪə / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Calceolaria, often grown in gardens, having showy flowers with a large, inflated, slipper-shaped lower lip.
genus of garden plants with slipper-shaped flowers

Examples

The vibrant flowers of the calceolaria brighten up many spring gardens.
She planted a variety of calceolarias along the patio for their unusual appearance.
A single calceolaria can be the focal point in a flower arrangement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slipperwort - an alternative common name for calceolaria

Etymology

From Latin *calceolaria*, diminutive of *calceolus* (little shoe), referring to the slipper-shaped flowers.
