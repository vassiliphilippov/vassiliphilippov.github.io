Calceolaria

1. NOUN

/ ˌkælsiəˈlɛəriə / (AmE), / ˌkælsiəˈleəriə / (BrE)
A large genus of tropical American herbs and shrubs with showy, often slipper-shaped flowers, commonly known as slipperworts.
genus of showy-flowered tropical American plants

Examples

The botanical garden features several varieties of Calceolaria in its tropical greenhouse.
Horticulturists prize Calceolaria for their unusually shaped, colorful blossoms.
Many gardeners grow Calceolaria indoors to admire their slipper-like flowers.

Etymology

From Latin 'calceolarius', meaning 'shoemaker', from 'calceolus' (little shoe), referring to the shape of the flowers.
