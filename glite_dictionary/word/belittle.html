to belittle

1. VERB

/ bəˈlɪt̬.əl / (AmE), / bɪˈlɪt.əl / (BrE)
to make someone or something seem unimportant or less valuable; to disparage or play down
make seem less important

Examples

He tried to belittle her achievements in front of the whole team.
It's unfair to belittle the efforts of others, especially when they are trying their best.
She felt insulted when her boss belittled her ideas during the meeting.

Synset Examples

Don't belittle his influence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disparage - to speak of as unimportant or inferior
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

First used in the late 18th century, from 'be-' (intensive prefix) + 'little'.

2. VERB

/ bəˈlɪt̬.əl / (AmE), / bɪˈlɪt.əl / (BrE)
to make someone or something seem unimportant or less valuable; to disparage or criticize in a way that diminishes worth
make seem unimportant; disparage

Examples

She felt hurt when her boss tried to belittle her achievements in front of everyone.
It is unfair to belittle someone's opinion just because you disagree with them.
Instead of offering support, he belittled her efforts, making her feel discouraged.

Synset Examples

She disparaged her student's efforts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disparage - to speak about someone or something in a derogatory or belittling manner
  • Antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration of someone or something

Etymology

Coined by Thomas Jefferson in 1781, from be- + little.

3. VERB

/ bɪˈlɪt̬.əl / (AmE), / bɪˈlɪt.əl / (BrE)
to make someone or something seem unimportant or less impressive; to disparage or diminish the value or reputation of.
make seem unimportant or less valuable

Examples

You shouldn't belittle your accomplishments, even if they seem small to you.
Her comments were meant to belittle his achievements in front of the team.
It's important not to belittle others when discussing differences of opinion.

Synset Examples

don't belittle your colleagues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disparage - to speak of or treat with disrespect; to belittle
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration; the opposite of belittle

Etymology

Coined by Thomas Jefferson, from 'be-' (intensive prefix) + 'little'.
