carunculous

1. ADJ

/ kəˈrʌŋkjələs / (AmE), / kəˈrʌŋkjʊləs / (BrE)
resembling or having the characteristics of a caruncle (a small, fleshy growth or protuberance)
resembling a caruncle (fleshy growth)
medical

Examples

The bird's carunculous growths around its beak made it easily identifiable.
Some lizards develop carunculous skin under certain conditions.
The doctor described the mass as carunculous in appearance during the examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caruncle - a small fleshy protuberance, especially at the corner of the eye or on animals

Etymology

From 'caruncle' (from Latin 'caruncula', diminutive of 'caro' meaning 'flesh') + '-ous'.
