biologistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌbaɪələˈdʒɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪələˈdʒɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to biologism; interpreting human life or society primarily in terms of biological factors or natural laws
relating to biologism or biological explanations

Examples

Some critics argue that biologistic theories oversimplify complex social behaviors.
The professor warned against a strictly biologistic interpretation of gender differences.
Her research challenges the biologistic approach that reduces culture to genetic predisposition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biologism - the interpretation of human life based on biological principles

Etymology

From 'biologism' + '-ic'. 'Biologism' based on 'biology' and '-ism'.
