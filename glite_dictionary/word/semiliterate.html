semiliterate

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛmiˈlɪtərət / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈlɪtərət / (BrE)
only able to read and write to a very limited degree; having basic or partial literacy.
barely able to read and write

Examples

Many adults in the region are still semiliterate and struggle to fill out forms.
Her semiliterate scrawl made the letter hard to decipher.
The program aims to help semiliterate workers improve their reading skills.

Synset Examples

an semiliterate scrawl

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: illiterate - unable to read or write at all
  • Degree Higher: literate - able to read and write well

Etymology

From semi- (partly) + literate, from Latin litteratus (learned), meaning partially literate.

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɛmiˈlɪtərət / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈlɪtərət / (BrE)
Able to read but not to write, or having only partial proficiency in reading and writing.
able to read but not write; partly literate

Examples

Though she was semiliterate, Maria managed to understand most of the labels in the grocery store.
The charity focused on helping semiliterate adults improve their writing skills.
Many rural communities still have a significant number of semiliterate individuals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illiterate - unable to read or write
  • Antonym: literate - able to read and write fluently

Etymology

From Latin semi- (half) + literate (able to read and write).

3. ADJ

/ ˌsɛmiˈlɪtərət / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈlɪtərət / (BrE)
having only partial ability to read and write, or being poorly informed and only somewhat educated.
partly literate or poorly informed

Examples

Despite attending school for years, he remained semiliterate and struggled with many texts.
The company's semiliterate statement was riddled with errors and unclear information.
Educational programs were created to help semiliterate adults improve their reading and writing skills.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: literate - able to read and write fluently
  • Degree Lower: illiterate - unable to read or write

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'semi-' (meaning 'half' or 'partly'), and 'literate', ultimately from Latin 'literatus' (learned, lettered).

Source: WordNet
