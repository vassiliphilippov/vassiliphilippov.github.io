enterprising

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛn.tɚˌpraɪ.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈen.təˌpraɪ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
having the ability and readiness to undertake new and difficult projects, especially with initiative and imagination.
showing initiative and resourcefulness

Examples

The company is always looking for enterprising employees who bring fresh ideas to the table.
With her enterprising spirit, she started a successful business while still in college.
An enterprising chef introduced fusion cuisine to the small town, attracting curious diners.

Synset Examples

an enterprising foreign policy
an enterprising young man likely to go far

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resourceful - able to deal well with new or difficult situations
  • Antonym: unimaginative - lacking in original ideas or initiative

Etymology

From French *entreprendre* (to undertake), based on Latin *inter* (between) + *prehendere* (to take).
