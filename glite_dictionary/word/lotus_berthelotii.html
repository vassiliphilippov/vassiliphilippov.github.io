Lotus berthelotii

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊtəs bɛrθəˈloʊtaɪ / (AmE), / ˈləʊtəs bɛːθɛˈlɒtiː / (BrE)
a low-growing, much-branched perennial plant native to the Canary Islands, with orange-red to scarlet or purple flowers, sometimes naturalized elsewhere
ornamental perennial plant with orange-red flowers

Examples

The Lotus berthelotii adds a striking splash of color to many Mediterranean gardens.
Gardeners appreciate Lotus berthelotii not only for its vibrant flowers but also for its delicate, trailing foliage.
In recent years, Lotus berthelotii has become popular in hanging baskets and window boxes.

Etymology

From Latin 'Lotus' (genus name) and dedicated to Sabin Berthelot, a French naturalist.
