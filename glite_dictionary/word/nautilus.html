nautilus

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔdələs / (AmE), / ˈnɔːtɪləs / (BrE)
a submarine that is powered by nuclear energy, especially referring to the USS Nautilus, the world's first operational nuclear-powered submarine
nuclear-powered submarine

Examples

The USS nautilus made history as the first submarine to complete a submerged transit beneath the North Pole.
Many advances in naval engineering were tested on the nautilus before being adopted by other submarines.
The development of a nuclear-powered nautilus marked a new era in maritime technology.

Etymology

From the name of the USS Nautilus, itself named after the marine mollusk, and earlier fictional submarine in Jules Verne's novel.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɔːdələs / (AmE), / ˈnɔːtɪləs / (BrE)
A cephalopod mollusk found in warm seas, known for its spiral, chambered shell.
marine mollusk with spiral shell

Examples

The nautilus glided gracefully through the clear waters, its shell shining in the sunlight.
Scientists have studied the nautilus to understand how its shell's chambers help with buoyancy.
She admired the intricate pattern on the nautilus shell she found on the beach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cephalopod - marine mollusk group including squids and octopuses

Etymology

From Greek nautilos, meaning 'sailor', referring to the animal's shell, which resembles a ship's sail.

3. NOUN

/ ˈnɔː.tɪ.ləs / (AmE), / ˈnɔː.tɪ.ləs / (BrE)
A marine cephalopod mollusk found in the Indian and Pacific oceans, known for its spiral, pearly, chambered shell.
spiral-shelled marine mollusk

Examples

The nautilus uses its many tentacles to catch small prey in deep ocean waters.
Scientists study the nautilus shell to understand principles of natural engineering.
She admired the delicate, swirling pattern of the nautilus shell in the museum display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuttlefish - marine cephalopod related to squid and octopus
  • Synonym: octopus - eight-armed marine cephalopod mollusk

Etymology

From Latin 'nautilus', from Ancient Greek 'nautilos' meaning 'sailor', originally referring to certain marine animals.
