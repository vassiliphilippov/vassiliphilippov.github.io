Nautilus

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔtələs / (AmE), / ˈnɔːtɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of marine mollusks with coiled, chambered shells, considered the only living representatives of the family Nautilidae.
a genus of chambered marine mollusks

Examples

The Nautilus is known for its beautiful, spiraled shell that is internally divided into chambers.
Scientists study the Nautilus to understand evolutionary links among cephalopods.
A live Nautilus can adjust its buoyancy by controlling gases within its shell.

Etymology

From Latin *nautilus* (paper nautilus), from Greek *nautilos* (sailor, a kind of mollusk), referring to its ability to move by jet propulsion.
