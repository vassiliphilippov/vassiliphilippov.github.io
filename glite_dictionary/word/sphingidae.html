Sphingidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈsfɪŋɡɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈsfɪŋɡɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of moths commonly known as hawkmoths, known for their robust bodies and rapid, sustained flying ability.
family of moths (hawkmoths)

Examples

The Sphingidae family includes some of the fastest-flying moths in the world.
Butterfly enthusiasts often study Sphingidae due to their remarkable hovering skills.
There are over 1,200 species classified under the Sphingidae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawkmoth - a moth of the same family as Sphingidae, with narrow wings and a robust body

Etymology

From New Latin Sphingidae, based on Greek 'sphinx' (sphinx), referencing the sphinx moth type.
