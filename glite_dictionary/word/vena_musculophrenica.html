vena musculophrenica

1. NOUN

/ ˌveɪnə ˌmʌs.kjuloʊ.frəˈnɪkə / (AmE), / ˌviː.nə ˌmʌs.kjʊ.ləʊ.frəˈnɪkə / (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the upper abdominal wall and the lower intercostal spaces
anatomical vein of the abdominal wall
medical

Examples

The vena musculophrenica plays a crucial role in draining blood from the lower ribs and upper abdomen.
During the procedure, surgeons carefully avoided damaging the vena musculophrenica.
Anatomical variations in the vena musculophrenica can affect surgical approaches to the diaphragm.

Etymology

From Latin: 'vena' (vein), 'musculus' (muscle), and 'phren' (diaphragm).
