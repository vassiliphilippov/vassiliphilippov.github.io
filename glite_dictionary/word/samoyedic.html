Samoyedic

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæməˈjɛdɪk / (AmE), / ˌsaməˈjɛdɪk / (BrE)
a group of Uralic languages spoken by the Samoyed people in northwestern Siberia.
Uralic languages of the Samoyed people

Examples

Many linguists have studied the relationship between Samoyedic and other Uralic languages.
The Samoyedic languages are primarily spoken in the northern regions of Siberia.
Nenets is the most widely spoken of the Samoyedic languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Uralic - a family of languages including Finno-Ugric and Samoyedic

Etymology

From Russian 'samoyedskij', relating to the Samoyed people.
