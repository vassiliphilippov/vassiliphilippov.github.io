mask

1. VERB

/ mæsk / (AmE), / mɑːsk / (BrE)
to cover or shield something in order to prevent light from reaching it
shield from light

Examples

You should mask the sensor to prevent direct sunlight from affecting the readings.
Artists sometimes mask certain areas of a canvas to protect them from paint or light exposure.
To photograph the eclipse safely, astronomers often mask their telescopes' lenses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: block - to prevent something from passing through or being seen
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01361440-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ mæsk / (AmE), / mɑːsk / (BrE)
an action, behavior, or quality used to hide the truth about something or someone
something used to conceal the truth

Examples

She wore a mask of confidence to hide her nervousness during the interview.
His cheerful attitude was just a mask for his disappointment.
The company’s promises of change turned out to be a mask for maintaining the status quo.

Synset Examples

no mask could conceal his ignorance
they moved in under a mask of friendship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - something that covers or conceals the real nature of something
  • Synonym: pretense - an attempt to make something that is not the case appear true
  • Synonym: disguise - something that is used to hide something else

Etymology

From Middle French masque, from Italian maschera, possibly from Medieval Latin masca (mask, spectre).
