firmamental

1. ADJ

/ ˌfɜːrməˈmɛntəl / (AmE), / ˌfɜːməˈmɛntəl / (BrE)
Relating to the sky or the firmament, especially in a literary or old-fashioned context.
relating to the sky or firmament

Examples

The poet described the stars' firmamental dance across the night sky.
She gazed upward, lost in the firmamental expanse above.
Ancient myths often spoke of gods ruling from their firmamental thrones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celestial - relating to the sky or heavens
  • Antonym: terrestrial - relating to the earth, as opposed to the sky or heavens

Etymology

From firmament, ultimately from Latin 'firmamentum' (support, sky).
