brown

1. VERB

/ braʊn / (AmE), / braʊn / (BrE)
to fry or cook food until it turns brown in color, often to improve flavor and appearance
fry or cook until browned

Examples

For better flavor, always brown the onions before adding them to the stew.
The recipe says to brown the chicken pieces in a large skillet.
She carefully browned the garlic in butter to enhance its aroma.

Synset Examples

brown the meat in the pan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sear - to quickly brown the surface of food at high heat

2. ADJ

/ braʊn / (AmE), / braʊn / (BrE)
(of skin) deeply suntanned, having a dark brown coloration as a result of exposure to the sun.
deeply suntanned (of skin)

Examples

After a summer spent on the beach, her brown skin glowed with health.
He returned from his vacation looking much more brown than before.
The children came back from the camp with faces brown from the sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tan - (of skin) having a light brown color due to sun exposure

3. ADJ

/ braʊn / (AmE), / braʊn / (BrE)
having a color similar to that of wood, earth, or coffee; between red and yellow in the color spectrum, of medium to dark shade.
of a color like wood or earth

Examples

The walls of the living room were painted a soft brown, creating a cozy atmosphere.
She wore a brown jacket that matched her boots perfectly.
After walking in the sun, his skin turned a deeper brown.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tan - pale tone of brown
  • Degree Lower: beige - light brown color
  • Degree Higher: chocolate - dark brown color
  • Degree Higher: black - darkest color, absence of light

Etymology

From Old English 'brūn', of Germanic origin, related to Dutch 'bruin' and German 'braun'.

4. VERB

/ braʊn / (AmE), / braʊn / (BrE)
to make or become brown in color, especially as a result of cooking, exposure to the sun, or drying
make or become brown in color

Examples

The chef let the onions brown before adding the tomatoes.
Too much sun can brown the tips of your houseplants' leaves.
She browned the bread in the toaster until it was crispy.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to darken - to make or become dark or darker

Etymology

5. NOUN

/ braʊn / (AmE), / braʊn / (BrE)
a color that is a mixture of red, yellow, and black, resembling the color of wood or earth
a dark reddish-yellow color

Examples

The old wooden table was painted a rich brown.
Chestnuts turn a deep brown when they are roasted.
She chose a warm shade of brown for her living room walls.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tan - a light shade of brown
  • Degree Lower: beige - a very pale brown color
  • Degree Higher: chocolate - a rich, dark brown color
  • Degree Higher: mahogany - a deep reddish-brown color

Etymology

From Old English brūn (brown, dark, dusky), from Proto-Germanic *brūnaz.
