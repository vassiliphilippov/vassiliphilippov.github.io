worm

1. NOUN

/ wɜrm / (AmE), / wɜːm / (BrE)
A type of gear in the form of a screw thread that meshes with the teeth of a worm wheel or rack to transmit motion at an angle.
helical gear component in machinery

Examples

The gearbox contains a worm that engages with the main wheel to control speed.
A worm and worm wheel setup is often used in lifting equipment to prevent back driving.
To reduce the rotational speed, the engineer selected a worm as the primary gear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worm wheel - a gear wheel meshed with a worm to form a worm drive
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

a person who is regarded as contemptible, sneaky, or morally corrupt and undeserving of respect
contemptible or immoral person

She dismissed him as a worm not worthy of her attention.

3. VERB

/ wɜrm / (AmE), / wɜːm / (BrE)
to move with twisting or wriggling motions, especially while trying to get free or make progress through a narrow space
move with twisting motions

Examples

The child managed to worm his way through the gap in the fence.
She tried to worm out of the tight corner by twisting her body.
The snake began to worm its way across the forest floor, searching for food.

Synset Examples

4. NOUN

/ wɝːm / (AmE), / wɜːm / (BrE)
a small, elongated, soft-bodied invertebrate animal, typically lacking limbs and often living in soil or water; also used for some insect larvae
small elongated limbless animal

Examples

While digging in the garden, she found a worm wriggling through the soil.
Birds often eat worms after a rainstorm when they come to the surface.
Some types of worms, like earthworms, help improve the quality of the soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: larva - the immature, wingless, feeding stage of an insect

Etymology

Old English wyrm (serpent, dragon, worm), from Proto-Germanic *wurmiz.

Source: WordNet

5. NOUN

/ wɝːm / (AmE), / wɜːm / (BrE)
a self-replicating software program that spreads across computers or networks, often causing harm or consuming resources
self-replicating computer program

Examples

The company suffered a major security breach after a worm infected all of its servers.
A worm can spread rapidly, sometimes affecting thousands of machines within minutes.
Unlike a virus, a worm does not need to attach itself to existing files in order to propagate.

Synset Examples

worms take advantage of automatic file sending and receiving features found on many computers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virus - a malicious computer program that attaches itself to files and spreads to other systems

Etymology

Derived by analogy from the idea of a parasitic worm that spreads through an organism, with the term used for computer programs since the 1970s.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
