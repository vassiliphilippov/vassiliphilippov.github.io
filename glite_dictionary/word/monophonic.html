monophonic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɑːnəˈfɑːnɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈfɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of sound reproduction or transmission) using a single channel for recording or playback, rather than stereo or multiple channels.
single-channel sound transmission or recording

Examples

The old record player only supports monophonic sound, not stereo.
Some classic albums were originally released in monophonic format.
She prefers the richer experience of stereo over monophonic recordings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stereophonic - using two or more channels for sound reproduction

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) + 'phōnē' (sound, voice).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00242685-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˌmɑːnəˈfɑːnɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈfɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of music) consisting of a single melodic line without accompaniment or harmonies.
having a single melodic musical line

Examples

The chant was sung in a monophonic style, with no instrumental accompaniment.
Early Gregorian music is primarily monophonic, lacking harmony or counterpoint.
Unlike polyphonic compositions, monophonic pieces focus on a single musical line.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polyphonic - having two or more independent melodic lines
  • Antonym: homophonic - having one main melody with accompanying chords or parts

Etymology

From Greek 'mono-' (single) + 'phōnē' (sound, voice).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01547939-a
Synset POS: a
