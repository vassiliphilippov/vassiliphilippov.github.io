Carcharinus longimanus

1. NOUN

/ kɑːrˌkærɪˈnaɪnəs lɒnˌdʒɪˈmeɪnəs / (AmE), / kɑːˌkarɪˈnaɪnəs lɒŋˌɪˈmɑːnəs / (BrE)
A large deep-water shark, widely distributed, with a white-tipped dorsal fin; considered one of the most dangerous sharks to humans.
species of large oceanic shark

Examples

Carcharinus longimanus is often spotted in open oceans far from land.
The white-tipped fins of Carcharinus longimanus make it easy to identify.
Due to frequent attacks on shipwreck survivors, Carcharinus longimanus has a fearsome reputation among sharks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oceanic whitetip shark - Common name for Carcharinus longimanus, large oceanic shark

Etymology

From Latin: 'Carcharias' (shark genus) + 'longimanus' meaning 'long-handed', referring to its long pectoral fins.
