Grevillea banksii

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈvɪliə bæŋkˈsaɪ.aɪ / (AmE), / ɡrɛˈvɪliə bæŋkˈsiː.aɪ / (BrE)
A tall Australian shrub with cylindrical clusters of red flowers and pinnatifid leaves that are silky and grey beneath.
Australian shrub with red flowers

Examples

The stunning red flowers of Grevillea banksii attract many native birds to Australian gardens.
Many gardeners choose Grevillea banksii for its hardiness and beautiful foliage.
Grevillea banksii thrives in well-drained soil and a sunny location.

Etymology

Named after the genus Grevillea and Sir Joseph Banks, English botanist.
