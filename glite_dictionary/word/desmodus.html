Desmodus

1. NOUN

/ dɛzˈmoʊdəs / (AmE), / dɛzˈməʊdəs / (BrE)
A genus of bats in the family Desmodontidae, commonly known as vampire bats, native to Central and South America.
genus of vampire bats

Examples

Desmodus is the genus to which the common vampire bat belongs.
Scientists studied the feeding behavior of Desmodus species in the wild.
The characteristics of Desmodus distinguish it from other genera in the Desmodontidae family.

Etymology

From Greek 'desmos' (bond) and 'odous, odont-' (tooth).

2. NOUN

/ dɛzˈmoʊdəs / (AmE), / dɛsˈməʊdəs / (BrE)
A genus of bats that includes the common vampire bat, representing the type genus of the family Desmodontidae.
genus of vampire bats

Examples

The common vampire bat, Desmodus rotundus, is found throughout Central and South America.
Scientists studied the feeding habits of Desmodus to better understand its adaptation to a blood-based diet.
Desmodus is notable for its heat-sensing ability, which helps it locate prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vampire bat - A bat that feeds on the blood of other animals; member of Desmodus genus

Etymology

From Greek desmos (bond) + odous, odontos (tooth).
