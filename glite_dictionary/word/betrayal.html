betrayal

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈtreɪəl / (AmE), / bɪˈtreɪəl / (BrE)
The act of being disloyal to a person, group, or cause, often by revealing secrets or acting against their interests.
an act of disloyalty or treachery

Examples

Her closest friend's betrayal left her feeling shocked and alone.
The company's collapse was seen as a betrayal of its employees' trust.
Writing a tell-all book was considered an act of betrayal among his former colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treachery - betrayal of trust; deceptive action or nature
  • Antonym: loyalty - a strong feeling of allegiance or support

Etymology

From betray + -al, from Old French 'betrayer'.

2. NOUN

/ bɪˈtreɪəl / (AmE), / bɪˈtreɪəl / (BrE)
the act or quality of helping an enemy or revealing secrets in violation of trust
act of disloyalty; aiding an enemy

Examples

She considered his actions a deep betrayal of her trust.
The general's betrayal changed the course of the war by giving secrets to the enemy.
Nothing can excuse the betrayal of one's comrades in such a dangerous situation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treason - the crime of betraying one's country, especially by aiding enemies
  • Antonym: loyalty - faithfulness or a strong feeling of allegiance

Etymology

From betray + -al, from Old French trahir (to betray).
