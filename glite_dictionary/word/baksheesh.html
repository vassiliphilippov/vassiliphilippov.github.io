baksheesh

1. NOUN

/ bækˈʃiʃ / (AmE), / bækˈʃiːʃ / (BrE)
A small sum of money given as a tip or gratuity, especially in South Asian or Middle Eastern countries, often for services rendered.
a small tip or gratuity, esp. in South Asia/Middle East

Examples

Tourists are often asked for a little baksheesh when visiting historic sites.
He slipped the porter some baksheesh for carrying his bags.
Refusing to pay baksheesh can sometimes delay your paperwork.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tip - a small amount of money given for services
  • Synonym: gratuity - a sum of money given to someone for a service

Etymology

From Persian *bakhshīsh* (gift, present, tip), via Hindi/Urdu *bakhshīsh*.
