cutch

1. NOUN

/ kʌtʃ / (AmE), / kʌtʃ / (BrE)
a tannin-rich extract obtained from the bark of certain tropical trees, especially mangroves or acacias, used mainly in dyeing and tanning
tannin extract from tropical tree bark

Examples

The textile industry uses cutch as a natural dye for fabrics.
Traditional leather tanning often relies on the astringent properties of cutch.
High-quality cutch is extracted from the heartwood of acacia trees in India.

Etymology

From Hindi 'kac', from Sanskrit 'khadira' (acacia tree).
