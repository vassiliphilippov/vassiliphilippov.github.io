outdoors

1. NOUN

/ aʊtˈdɔɹz / (AmE), / aʊtˈdɔːz / (BrE)
the open air; outside, especially away from buildings or shelter
the open air, outside

Examples

We spent the afternoon outdoors, enjoying the fresh air and sunshine.
Many people prefer exercising outdoors rather than at the gym.
The park is a great place to experience the outdoors.

Synset Examples

he wanted to get outdoors a little
the concert was held in the open air
camping in the open

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indoors - inside a building; not outside
  • Synonym: outside - the external side or surface; open air

2. ADV

/ aʊtˈdɔɹz / (AmE), / aʊtˈdɔːz / (BrE)
outside, not within a building; in or into the open air
outside, not indoors

Examples

The children spent all afternoon outdoors playing games in the garden.
You should dry your clothes outdoors when it's sunny and breezy.
She loves eating breakfast outdoors on the patio whenever she can.

Synset Examples

in summer we play outside

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indoors - inside a building
