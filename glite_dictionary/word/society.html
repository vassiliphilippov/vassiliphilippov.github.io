society

1. NOUN

/ səˈsaɪəti / (AmE), / səˈsaɪəti / (BrE)
the state of being in the company of other people; companionship
companionship; being with others

Examples

He missed their society after moving to a new city.
She valued the society of her childhood friends above all else.
During his illness, he rarely sought the society of others.

Synset Examples

he missed their company
he enjoyed the society of his friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: company - companionship; being with someone

Etymology

From Old French 'societé', from Latin 'societas' (fellowship, association), from 'socius' (companion).

2. NOUN

/ səˈsaɪəti / (AmE), / səˈsaɪ.ə.ti / (BrE)
An organized group of people who share a common interest or purpose, usually meeting regularly.
organized group with shared interest

Examples

She is a member of the local historical society which meets every month.
The photography society organizes exhibitions for amateur photographers in the region.
After graduation, he joined a professional society to network with others in his field.

Synset Examples

he joined a golf club
they formed a small lunch society
men from the fraternal order will staff the soup kitchen today

Related Terms

  • Synonym: club - an organized group of people with a common purpose
  • Synonym: association - a group of people organized for a joint purpose

Etymology

From French 'société', from Latin 'societas' (fellowship, association).

3. NOUN

/ səˈsaɪəti / (AmE), / səˈsaɪəti / (BrE)
the group of people in a community who are considered fashionable, wealthy, and influential, often seen as a social elite
the fashionable social elite

Examples

She was introduced to society at her debutante ball.
Many novels describe the strict rules of society in Victorian England.
He always dreamed of being accepted into fashionable society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aristocracy - the highest class in certain societies, especially those holding hereditary titles or offices
  • Synonym: the elite - a select group that is superior in terms of ability or qualities to the rest of a group or society
  • Antonym: the masses - the ordinary people in society who are not part of the elite

Etymology

From Old French 'societé', from Latin 'societas' (association, alliance), from 'socius' (companion).

4. NOUN

/ səˈsaɪ.ə.ti / (AmE), / səˈsaɪ.ə.ti / (BrE)
an organized group of people sharing a distinctive culture, institutions, and often a common economic system
organized group with shared culture

Examples

Modern society places a high value on technological progress and innovation.
In many societies, family structure and customs play an important role in daily life.
The festival celebrates the diverse cultures that make up our multicultural society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: community - a group of people living in the same place or having a particular characteristic in common
  • Degree Lower: population - all the inhabitants of a particular place

Etymology

From Old French 'société', from Latin 'societas' (association, alliance), from 'socius' (companion, ally).
