Lanius ludovicianus excubitorides

1. NOUN

/ ləˈnaɪ.əs ˌluː.dəˌvɪʃ.iˈæn.əs ɛkˌskjuː.bɪtəˈraɪdiːz / (AmE), / ləˈnaɪ.əs ˌluː.dəˌvɪʃ.iˈæn.əs ɛkˌskjuː.bɪtəˈraɪdiːz / (BrE)
A subspecies of loggerhead shrike, a butcherbird found in western North America, with grey upperparts and white underparts.
a subspecies of loggerhead shrike

Examples

The Lanius ludovicianus excubitorides is known for impaling its prey on thorns or barbed wire.
Birdwatchers spotted a Lanius ludovicianus excubitorides perched on a fence in New Mexico.
The gray and white plumage distinguishes the Lanius ludovicianus excubitorides from other shrikes in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loggerhead shrike - a North American songbird known as the 'butcherbird', Lanius ludovicianus
  • Degree Higher: Lanius excubitor - the great grey shrike, a related species

Etymology

From Latin: 'Lanius' meaning 'butcher', 'ludovicianus' after Louisiana, 'excubitorides' referring to its resemblance to the great grey shrike (Lanius excubitor).
