claustrophobe

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɔstrəˌfoʊb / (AmE), / ˈklɔːstrəˌfəʊb / (BrE)
a person who suffers from claustrophobia, the fear of enclosed or confined spaces
person with claustrophobia

Examples

As a claustrophobe, he avoids elevators and prefers taking the stairs.
The small, windowless room made the claustrophobe feel uneasy and panicked.
Her friends understood her reluctance to travel by subway because she is a claustrophobe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: claustrophobic - relating to or suffering from claustrophobia
  • Antonym: agoraphobe - a person who suffers from agoraphobia (fear of open or crowded spaces)

Etymology

From claustrophobia + -phobe, from Latin clausus (shut) and Greek phobos (fear).
