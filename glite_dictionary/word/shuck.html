shuck

1. VERB

/ ʃʌk / (AmE), / ʃʌk / (BrE)
to remove the outer covering or husk from something, especially from corn or shellfish.
remove outer covering, e.g., from corn or shellfish

Examples

It's easier to shuck corn when it's fresh.
She expertly shucked a dozen oysters for the guests.
Before cooking, he had to shuck all the shellfish.

  • Synonym: husk - to remove the husk from (something)

2. VERB

/ ʃʌk / (AmE), / ʃʌk / (BrE)
to remove the outer shell or husk from something, especially shellfish or corn
remove shell or husk

Examples

She expertly shucked a dozen oysters in just a few minutes.
Before boiling the corn, you need to shuck it and remove the silk.
Watching the chef shuck clams was fascinating to the guests at the party.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peel - remove the outer covering of something, especially fruit or vegetables

3. NOUN

/ ʃʌk / (AmE), / ʃʌk / (BrE)
the outer covering of a seed or the husk and other small plant pieces separated from seeds, especially after threshing or processing
outer covering of seeds; seed husk

Examples

The process left piles of shuck on the ground after the corn was sorted.
Farmers use the shuck as animal bedding or compost.
After shelling the beans, he gathered the shuck into a basket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: husk - the dry outer covering of some fruits or seeds

Etymology

Possibly of imitative origin, first recorded in American English in the late 17th century.
