clannish

1. ADJ

/ ˈklæn.ɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈklæn.ɪʃ / (BrE)
tending to associate mainly with a close group and to exclude others; socially exclusive or insular
socially exclusive; insular

Examples

The small town was known for its clannish attitude toward outsiders.
Their clannish behavior made it difficult for new employees to feel accepted.
He found the club too clannish for his liking and preferred a more open community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclusive - tending to exclude others; restricting to a select group
  • Antonym: welcoming - friendly and open to outsiders or newcomers

Etymology

From 'clan' (a group of close-knit families), with '-ish' forming adjectives.

2. ADJ

/ ˈklænɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈklænɪʃ / (BrE)
tending to associate closely with one’s own group and to exclude outsiders; resembling or characteristic of a clan, especially in showing strong group loyalty
showing strong group loyalty or exclusiveness

Examples

Their clannish behavior made it difficult for newcomers to feel welcome.
The small village was known for its clannish traditions and tightly knit families.
Some critics described the organization as too clannish and resistant to change.

Synset Examples

clannish loyalty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclusive - tending to exclude others or not readily allowing others to join
  • Antonym: open - welcoming to outsiders, inclusive

Etymology

From 'clan' (from Scottish Gaelic 'clann' meaning 'family') + '-ish'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02119261-s
Synset POS: s
