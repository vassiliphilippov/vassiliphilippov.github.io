infinitely

1. ADV

/ ˈɪn.fɪt.li / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fɪt.lɪ / (BrE)
without any limits; to an unlimited or extremely great degree.
without limits; to a very great degree

Examples

The possibilities for learning in this field are infinitely vast.
She is infinitely patient with young children.
Their designs are infinitely more creative than anything I've seen before.

Synset Examples

he is infinitely wealthy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endlessly - without end; unceasingly
  • Antonym: finitely - having limits; in a limited way

Etymology

From Latin infinitus (boundless) + English adverbial suffix -ly.

2. ADV

/ ˈɪn.fə.nət.li / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fɪ.nɪt.li / (BrE)
to an unlimited or endless degree; without any limit or end
without limit or end

Examples

The universe may be infinitely large, extending beyond what we can observe.
She has infinitely more patience than I do in stressful situations.
Numbers can be divided infinitely without ever truly reaching zero.

Synset Examples

there are infinitely many possibilities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endlessly - continuing without end
  • Antonym: finitely - with limits; in a way that has an end

Etymology

From Latin 'infinitus' (without end) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00226558-r
Synset POS: r
