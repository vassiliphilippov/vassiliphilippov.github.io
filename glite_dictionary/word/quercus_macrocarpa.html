Quercus macrocarpa

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs ˌmækroʊˈkɑrpə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs ˌmækrəʊˈkɑːpə / (BrE)
A medium to large deciduous oak tree native to central and eastern North America, known for its large fringed acorn cups and tough, close-grained wood.
species of oak tree native to North America

Examples

The Quercus macrocarpa is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its hardy wood.
Many wildlife species feed on the distinctive acorns produced by Quercus macrocarpa.
In the park, several mature Quercus macrocarpa provide deep shade in the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bur oak - common name for Quercus macrocarpa

Etymology

From Latin: 'quercus' meaning 'oak' and 'macrocarpa' from Greek 'makros' (large) and 'karpos' (fruit).
