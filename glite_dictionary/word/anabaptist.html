Anabaptist

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈbæptɪst / (AmE), / ˌanəˈbaptɪst / (BrE)
A member of a Protestant Christian movement that originated in the 16th century, advocating adult baptism and rejecting infant baptism.
member of a Protestant Christian movement

Examples

The Anabaptists faced severe persecution in Europe during the Reformation.
Many Anabaptist groups emphasize pacifism and community living.
He studied the beliefs and history of the Anabaptists for his thesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mennonite - member of a Protestant sect stemming from Anabaptist movement

Etymology

From Modern Latin Anabaptista, from Greek ana- (again) + baptizein (to baptize).
