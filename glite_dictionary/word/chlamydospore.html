chlamydospore

1. NOUN

/ kləˈmɪdoʊˌspɔr / (AmE), / kləˈmɪdəʊˌspɔː / (BrE)
a thick-walled asexual spore formed as a resting stage in the life cycle of certain fungi and algae, helping to withstand unfavorable conditions
thick-walled asexual fungal/algal spore

Examples

The fungus produces chlamydospores to survive extreme drought conditions.
Microscopic examination revealed the presence of chlamydospores in the infected plant tissue.
Some algae form chlamydospores during adverse environmental periods.

Etymology

From Greek 'chlamys' (cloak) + 'spora' (seed, spore)
