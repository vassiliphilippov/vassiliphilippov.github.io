expertness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛkspərtnəs / (AmE), / ˈɛkspɜːtnəs / (BrE)
the quality of having special skill or knowledge in a particular area
skill or knowledge in a particular field

Examples

Her expertness in negotiation helped the company secure a better contract.
The surgeon's expertness was evident during the complex procedure.
His expertness in chess makes him a formidable opponent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expertise - specialized skill or knowledge in a particular field
  • Antonym: incompetence - lack of skill or ability

Etymology

From 'expert' + '-ness', modeled after Latin expertus (experienced) and the English suffix -ness denoting a state or quality.
