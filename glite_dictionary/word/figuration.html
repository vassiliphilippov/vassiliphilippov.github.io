figuration

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪɡjəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɪɡjʊˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of decorating something with a design or pattern, especially in art or architecture
decorating with a design or pattern

Examples

The intricate figuration on the vase revealed the artist’s delicate craftsmanship.
Medieval churches often display elaborate figuration along their arches and doorways.
The designer chose a subtle figuration for the wallpaper to add elegance to the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ornamentation - the act of decorating with ornaments or embellishments

Etymology

From Latin 'figuratio', meaning 'a shaping or forming', from 'figurare' (to form, fashion).

2. NOUN

/ ˌfɪɡjəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɪɡjʊˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the representation of something in a symbolic, figurative, or allegorical way, especially in art or literature
representation by symbol or allegory

Examples

The artist's use of figuration transformed ordinary objects into powerful allegories.
Modern poetry often favors ambiguity over clear figuration of its themes.
In Renaissance painting, figuration was a popular technique for expressing religious ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symbolism - the use of symbols to represent ideas or qualities
  • Antonym: literalness - representation in a strictly literal, non-figurative manner

Etymology

From Latin figuratio, from figurare (to form, shape).
