marksmanship

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːrksmənˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈmɑːksmənʃɪp / (BrE)
skill or ability in shooting accurately with a gun or other projectile weapon
skill in shooting

Examples

The soldier's marksmanship was so precise that he never missed a target.
Improving your marksmanship requires patience, practice, and steady hands.
Her excellent marksmanship helped the team win the national shooting competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shooting - the act or skill of firing a weapon
  • Antonym: ineptitude - lack of skill or ability

Etymology

From marksman + -ship; 'marksman' from 'mark' (target) and 'man' (person).
