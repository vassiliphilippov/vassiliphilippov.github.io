to redline

1. VERB

/ ˈrɛdˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌlaɪn / (BrE)
to refuse to sell or rent housing to people in particular areas, especially based on race or ethnicity, often as a form of discrimination
deny housing in areas based on discrimination
legal

Examples

It became illegal for banks to redline minority neighborhoods during the 1970s.
Real estate agents were accused of redlining certain districts to prevent minority homeownership.
Regulations were put in place to stop lenders who had previously redlined entire communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discriminate - to treat a person or group differently, especially in an unfair way

Etymology

From the practice of marking areas on a map with a red line to indicate neighborhoods where banks would not invest, especially based on racial criteria.
