unconcealed

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.kənˈsiːld / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kənˈsiːld / (BrE)
not hidden, disguised, or kept secret; open or obvious
not hidden or disguised

Examples

She greeted the news with unconcealed joy and excitement.
His unconcealed anger made everyone in the room uncomfortable.
The mayor listened with unconcealed interest to the citizens' concerns.

Synset Examples

her unconcealed hostility poisoned the atmosphere
watched with unconcealed curiosity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: open - not concealed or hidden; exposed to view or knowledge
  • Antonym: concealed - kept hidden or secret

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'concealed', from Old French 'concele', from Latin 'concelare' (to hide).
