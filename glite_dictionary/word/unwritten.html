unwritten

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈrɪtən / (AmE), / ʌnˈrɪtən / (BrE)
(of remarks, actions, etc.) not planned or written in advance; spontaneous or improvised.
not planned or written in advance

Examples

His unwritten speech impressed the audience with its sincerity and spontaneity.
The actor gave an unwritten response to the unexpected question on stage.
Much of the conversation at the meeting was unwritten and developed naturally.

Synset Examples

he made a few ad-lib remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improvised - said or done without previous preparation
  • Antonym: planned - prepared or arranged in advance

Etymology

From un- (not) + written (past participle of 'write').

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈrɪtn̩ / (AmE), / ʌnˈrɪtən / (BrE)
(of a rule, law, or custom) not formally recorded in writing but accepted and followed in practice.
not formally written; customary

Examples

There is an unwritten rule about taking off your shoes before entering the house.
Many office customs remain unwritten but are strongly observed by all employees.
Their agreement was unwritten, relying solely on trust and mutual respect.

Synset Examples

an unwritten law
rites … so ancient that they well might have had their unwritten origins in Aurignacian times

Related Terms

  • Antonym: written - recorded in writing or print
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02294659-a
Synset POS: a
