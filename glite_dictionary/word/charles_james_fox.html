Charles James Fox

1. PROPN

/ ˈtʃɑrlz ˈdʒeɪmz fɑks / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːlz ˈdʒeɪmz fɒks / (BrE)
(1749–1806) English statesman known for supporting American independence and the French Revolution.
English statesman (1749–1806)

Examples

As a prominent Whig politician, Charles James Fox was a loud critic of the British monarchy.
Many historians credit Charles James Fox with advancing liberal causes during a turbulent period in Europe.
The speeches of Charles James Fox had a significant impact on parliamentary debates in his era.
