nail-biting

1. ADJ

/ ˈneɪlˌbaɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈneɪlˌbaɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a situation or event) causing great suspense or anxiety; very exciting or tense.
causing suspense or anxiety

Examples

The final moments of the match were truly nail-biting for the whole stadium.
She watched the nail-biting conclusion to the thriller with wide eyes.
It was a nail-biting race that kept everyone on the edge of their seats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tense - making people feel nervous or anxious
  • Antonym: relaxing - making people feel relaxed; opposite of suspenseful or anxious

Etymology

From the literal act of biting one's nails due to nervousness or anxiety.

2. ADJ

/ ˈneɪlˌbaɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈneɪlˌbaɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
causing intense suspense or anxiety; extremely tense or exciting
extremely suspenseful or tense

Examples

It was a nail-biting finish to the championship game, with both teams tied until the last second.
We watched a nail-biting thriller that kept us guessing until the very end.
Waiting for the exam results was a nail-biting experience for the students.

Synset Examples

Her nail-biting story captivated the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suspenseful - causing a state of nervous excitement or uncertainty about what may happen
  • Synonym: tense - causing feelings of anxiety, strain, or suspense
  • Antonym: relaxing - making you feel relaxed, calm, or at ease

Etymology

From the literal act of biting one's nails due to anxiety or suspense, used figuratively by the mid-20th century.
