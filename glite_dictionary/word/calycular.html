calycular

1. ADJ

/ kəˈlɪkjələr / (AmE), / kəˈlɪkjʊlə / (BrE)
relating to or resembling a calyculus, especially a cup-shaped structure or a whorl of small leaves beneath a flower in certain plants and animals.
relating to a calyculus (cup-shaped structure)

Examples

The botanist noted the calycular bracts at the base of the flower.
Some species have a calycular structure that resembles a whorl of tiny leaves.
Under the microscope, the tissue appeared distinctly calycular in form.

Etymology

From New Latin calyculus (small cup), diminutive of Latin calyx (cup, flower cup).
