Holothuria

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊ.ləˈθʊr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌhɒl.əˈθjʊə.rɪ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of sea cucumbers that is the type genus of the family Holothuridae.
genus of sea cucumbers

Examples

The species in the genus Holothuria are important members of marine ecosystems.
Marine biologists discovered a new Holothuria species off the Pacific coast.
Many tropical reefs are home to various Holothuria sea cucumbers.

Etymology

From Latin, originally from Greek holothourion (sea cucumber).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊ.ləˈθʊr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌhɒl.əˈθjʊə.ri.ə / (BrE)
A genus of sea cucumbers in the family Holothuridae, serving as the type genus of the family.
genus of sea cucumbers

Examples

The genus Holothuria includes many common species of sea cucumber.
Marine biologists often study Holothuria to understand the diversity of echinoderms.
Several edible sea cucumbers belong to the Holothuria genus, making them important in some cuisines.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Holothuridae - the family of sea cucumbers to which Holothuria belongs

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek holothourion (sea cucumber).
