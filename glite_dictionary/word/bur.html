bur

1. NOUN

/ bɝː / (AmE), / bɜː / (BrE)
A small cutting or grinding tool, typically a rotating bit, used in dentistry or surgery for cutting hard tissue or bone.
surgical or dental cutting bit
medical

Examples

The dentist selected a diamond bur to shape the filling precisely.
Surgical burs must be sterilized after every procedure to prevent infection.
He used a high-speed bur to remove the decayed portion of the tooth.

Etymology

From Old French *bur* (tool), possibly of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ bɝː / (AmE), / bɜː / (BrE)
a seed vessel or fruit with hooks or prickles that attach to fur or clothing
seed vessel with hooks or prickles

Examples

The dog's fur was full of burs after our walk through the meadow.
Children often find bur stuck to their socks after playing outdoors.
A bur can be difficult to remove from clothing due to its tiny hooks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burr - alternative spelling for a seed vessel with hooks or prickles

Etymology

From Middle English *burre*, of uncertain origin.

3. VERB

/ bɝː / (AmE), / bɜː / (BrE)
to remove burrs (rough edges or projections) from a material, especially metal, usually by filing or grinding
remove burrs from a material

Examples

After cutting the metal pipe, the worker had to bur the ends to prevent injury.
Engineers spent two hours carefully burring the sharp edges of each panel.
Make sure you bur all components before starting the assembly process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deburr - remove burrs from an object (especially metal)
  • Synonym: burr - rough edge or projection left on metal after cutting

Etymology

From 'burr', referring to a rough edge left after cutting or shaping metal.

Source: WordNet
