sago

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈseɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
a starchy substance obtained from certain tropical palms, used as a food thickener and in making puddings and textiles
starch from palms, used in food

Examples

The pudding was made with sago and sweetened coconut milk.
In Malaysia, sago pearls are often used in traditional desserts.
The textile industry sometimes uses sago as a natural stiffener.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tapioca - starchy substance from cassava, used similarly to sago

Etymology

From Malay 'sagu', meaning sago palm.
