Cyathea medullaris

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈiːθi.ə mɛdʊˈlɛrɪs / (AmE), / saɪˈæθɪə mɛdʊˈlɑːrɪs / (BrE)
A tall, showy tree fern native to New Zealand and Australia, with a crown of large, dark green, pinnate fronds and whitish undersides.
tall tree fern of New Zealand and Australia

Examples

The lush fronds of the Cyathea medullaris make it a dramatic feature in subtropical gardens.
In New Zealand forests, the Cyathea medullaris stands out for its towering black trunk and graceful foliage.
Botanists studied the white undersides of Cyathea medullaris leaves to understand their adaptation to low light.

Etymology

From genus name Cyathea and Latin medullaris, meaning 'of the marrow,' referring to its internal stem structure.
