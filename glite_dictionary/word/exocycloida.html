Exocycloida

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛksoʊˈsaɪklɔɪdə / (AmE), / ˌɛksəʊˈsʌɪklɔɪdə / (BrE)
A former order of flat sea urchins, characterized by a dorsoventrally flattened body shape.
order of flat sea urchins

Examples

Scientists studying deep-sea ecosystems have identified unique features in Exocycloida.
The fossil record includes several remarkable specimens belonging to the order Exocycloida.
Unlike most sea urchins, members of Exocycloida exhibit a flattened body plan.

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining exo- (outside) and Greek kyklos (circle), referring to body structure.
