osteoarthritis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːstioʊɑːrˈθraɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒstiəʊɑːˈθraɪtɪs / (BrE)
A chronic disease characterized by the breakdown of cartilage in the joints, typically occurring in older adults and resulting in pain and stiffness.
joint disease causing cartilage breakdown
medical

Examples

Many elderly people suffer from osteoarthritis in their knees and hips.
Physical therapy can help manage the symptoms of osteoarthritis.
Joint pain associated with osteoarthritis often worsens after physical activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arthritis - inflammation of one or more joints

Etymology

From Greek osteon (bone) + arthron (joint) + -itis (inflammation).
