prime

1. NOUN

/ praɪm / (AmE), / praɪm / (BrE)
a natural number that has exactly two distinct natural number divisors: 1 and itself
number divisible only by 1 and itself

Examples

The number 7 is a prime, while 8 is not.
Mathematicians study the distribution of primes among natural numbers.
Two is the only even prime number.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: composite number - a natural number that is not prime, having more than two divisors

Etymology

From Latin 'primus' meaning 'first'.

Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ praɪm / (AmE), / praɪm / (BrE)
(of a number) divisible only by itself and one; not composite.
of a number: divisible only by itself and one

Examples

Mathematicians have proven that there are infinitely many prime numbers.
A prime factor is a factor of a number that is itself prime.

Related Terms
