Norn

1. NOUN

/ nɔrn / (AmE), / nɔːn / (BrE)
(in Norse mythology) any of the three goddesses of destiny who control the fate of gods and men, comparable to the Greek Fates.
Norse goddess of destiny

Examples

In Norse legends, a Norn decides the fate of every newborn child.
The stories tell of three Norns who dwell beside the Well of Urd.
Scholars often compare the role of a Norn to that of the Greek Fates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wyrd - Anglo-Saxon fate goddess, related to the Norns
  • Synonym: Moirae - Greek goddesses of fate, comparable to the Norns
  • Synonym: Parcae - Roman goddesses of destiny, equivalent to the Norns

Etymology

From Old Norse 'norn', referring to powerful female beings shaping fate.
