whopping

1. ADV

/ ˈwɑːpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɒpɪŋ / (BrE)
To a very great extent; extremely (informal, chiefly as an intensifier before adjectives or adverbs).
to a very great extent; extremely
informal

Examples

He was whopping drunk after the party last night.
That new laptop is whopping expensive for a student budget.
The crowd was whopping loud at the championship game.

Synset Examples

they all were whopping drunk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enormously - to a very great extent; extremely
  • Antonym: slightly - to a small extent; not very much

Etymology

From 'whop' (to strike heavily) + -ing, used as an intensifier since the 19th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɑːpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɒpɪŋ / (BrE)
Extremely large; very great in size, degree, or amount (informal).
very large (informal)
informal

Examples

The company posted a whopping profit last year, surprising even the analysts.
She made a whopping mistake that cost the team the game.
The new stadium can hold a whopping 80,000 spectators.

Synset Examples

a thumping loss

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enormous - very large in size or extent
  • Synonym: gigantic - extremely large, huge
  • Antonym: tiny - very small

Etymology

Of imitative origin, probably related to 'whop', meaning to strike heavily, with the sense of something strikingly big.
