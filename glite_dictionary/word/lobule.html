lobule

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.bjuːl / (AmE), / ˈlɒb.juːl / (BrE)
a small lobe or subdivision of a lobe, especially in an organ such as the liver, lung, or gland
small lobe or lobe subdivision
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully examined the damaged lobule of the liver before proceeding.
Each lobule in the breast contains smaller structures involved in milk production.
Microscopic analysis revealed that the lung lobule was inflamed due to infection.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: lobe - a rounded projection or division of an organ

Etymology

From Latin *lobulus*, diminutive of *lobus* (lobe).
