effervescent

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛfərˈvɛsənt / (AmE), / ˌɛfəˈvɛsənt / (BrE)
(of a liquid) giving off bubbles; fizzy or sparkling.
giving off bubbles (liquid)

Examples

When she poured the soda into the glass, it became immediately effervescent.
The effervescent tablet fizzed rapidly as it dissolved in water.
Champagne is known for its effervescent quality and lively bubbles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sparkling - (of a liquid) containing bubbles of gas; fizzy
  • Antonym: flat - (of a beverage) lacking bubbles or fizz

Etymology

From Latin effervescere (to boil up), from ex- (out) + fervescere (begin to boil).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɛfərˈvɛsənt / (AmE), / ˌɛfəˈvɛsənt / (BrE)
(of a liquid, especially wine or water) containing or releasing many small bubbles; fizzy or sparkling.
fizzy or sparkling (of drinks)

Examples

The effervescent water left a pleasant tingling sensation on my tongue.
They celebrated with an effervescent champagne toast at midnight.
This winery produces an effervescent rosé that is perfect for summer picnics.

Synset Examples

sparkling wines
sparkling water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sparkling - (of a drink) containing bubbles of gas; fizzy

Etymology

From Latin 'effervescere' meaning 'to boil up, bubble'.

3. ADJ

/ ˌɛfərˈvɛsənt / (AmE), / ˌefəˈvɛsənt / (BrE)
(of a person or their manner) lively, enthusiastic, and full of energy.
lively and full of energy

Examples

Her effervescent personality brightened up every room she entered.
The speaker’s effervescent enthusiasm was contagious, inspiring the whole audience.
He approached each new challenge with effervescent optimism and creativity.

Synset Examples

his fertile effervescent mind
scintillating personality
a row of sparkly cheerleaders

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vivacious - (especially of a woman) attractively lively and animated
  • Synonym: scintillating - brilliantly and excitingly clever or skillful
  • Antonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement

Etymology

From Latin effervescere (to boil up), from ex- (out) + fervescere (begin to boil).
