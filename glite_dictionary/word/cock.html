cock

1. NOUN

/ kɑk / (AmE), / kɒk / (BrE)
The part of a gunlock that strikes the percussion cap when the trigger is pulled.
part of a gunlock for firing

Examples

The old revolver has a broken cock, preventing it from firing.
He carefully pulled back the cock before aiming the pistol.
Early muskets required the user to manually set the cock before shooting.

Etymology

From Old English *cocc*, meaning 'rooster', later applied by analogy to parts that resembled a rooster's comb or head, such as in firearms.
