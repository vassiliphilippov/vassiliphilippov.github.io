ringed

1. ADJ

/ rɪŋd / (AmE), / rɪŋd / (BrE)
having colored or marked rings, often around the body of an animal or object
having colored or marked rings

Examples

The ringed snake was easily identified by the distinctive bands on its body.
Many birds have ringed patterns on their feathers, helping them blend into their surroundings.
The ringed planet Saturn can be seen clearly through a telescope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banded - having stripes or bands of color
  • Antonym: spotted - marked with spots

Etymology

From the noun 'ring' + -ed, denoting possession of rings.

2. ADJ

/ rɪŋd / (AmE), / rɪŋd / (BrE)
having the shape of a ring; circular and typically hollow in form
shaped like a ring

Examples

The birds nested on a ringed island in the middle of the lake.
A ringed pattern appeared as the water droplets dried on the table.
The ancient fortress had a ringed wall surrounding its center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circular - having the form of a circle
