hydrometric

1. ADJ

/ haɪdrəˈmɛtrɪk / (AmE), / haɪdrəˈmɛtrɪk / (BrE)
relating to the measurement of the properties, flow, or distribution of water, especially as performed in hydrology or hydrometry.
relating to water measurement

Examples

The river's flow was recorded using hydrometric instruments at several monitoring stations.
A network of hydrometric gauges provides detailed data on water levels and discharge.
Scientists conducted a hydrometric survey to assess changes in the watershed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrometry - the science of measuring the properties or flow of water

Etymology

From Greek 'hydro-' (water) + 'metron' (measure), via French or Latin roots.
