Icterus spurius

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪktərə s ˈspjʊəriəs / (AmE), / ˈɪktərəs ˈspjʊəriəs / (BrE)
A species of North American oriole, commonly known as the orchard oriole, in which the adult male has chestnut and black plumage.
species of North American oriole

Examples

Many birdwatchers hope to spot the elusive Icterus spurius during spring migration.
The male Icterus spurius is easily recognized by his chestnut-and-black coloring.
Habitat loss threatens the breeding grounds of the Icterus spurius in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchard oriole - common English name for Icterus spurius

Etymology

From Latin: icterus (a yellow bird, oriole) and spurius (false or spurious).
