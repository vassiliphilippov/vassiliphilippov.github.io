Chewa

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪ.wə / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛw.ə / (BrE)
a member of a Bantu-speaking ethnic group native to Malawi, eastern Zambia, and northern Zimbabwe
Bantu-speaking ethnic group of south-central Africa

Examples

The Chewa are known for their rich traditions in music and dance.
A Chewa chief visited the village to participate in the festival.
Many Chewa people still practice the customs passed down from their ancestors.
