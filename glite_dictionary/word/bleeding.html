bleeding

1. NOUN

/ ˈbliːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbliːdɪŋ / (BrE)
the loss or flow of blood from a ruptured blood vessel, typically from a wound or injury
loss or flow of blood

Examples

The nurse quickly applied pressure to stop the bleeding from the wound.
If bleeding continues for more than ten minutes, seek medical attention.
Severe internal bleeding can be life-threatening if not treated promptly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemorrhage - profuse or uncontrolled bleeding

Etymology

Present participle of 'bleed', from Old English *blēdan* (to bleed).
