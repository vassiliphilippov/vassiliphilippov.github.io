wingspread

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪŋˌsprɛd / (AmE), / ˈwɪŋˌsprɛd / (BrE)
the distance from the tip of one wing to the tip of the other when the wings are fully extended, as in a bird or insect
distance between tips of fully extended wings

Examples

The albatross has an impressive wingspread of over three meters.
Scientists measured the butterfly's wingspread carefully to identify the species.
Heron species are easily recognized by their large wingspread in flight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wingspan - distance from the tip of one wing to the other when outstretched

Etymology

From 'wing' + 'spread', referring to the extent of wing extension.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪŋˌsprɛd / (AmE), / ˈwɪŋˌsprɛd / (BrE)
The linear distance from one tip of a wing or airfoil to the other; the span measured across the widest part of wings or similar structures.
distance from tip to tip of wings

Examples

The eagle’s wingspread can exceed two meters, allowing it to soar for long distances.
When designing the new glider, engineers increased the wingspread to improve lift and efficiency.
At the museum, visitors marveled at the ancient pterosaur fossil with its remarkable wingspread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wingspan - distance from one wing tip to the other

Etymology

Formed from 'wing' + 'spread,' indicating the extent or range of the wings.
