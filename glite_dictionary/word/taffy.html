taffy

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæfi / (AmE), / ˈtæfi / (BrE)
A chewy candy made from sugar or syrup boiled until thick and pulled until it is glossy and elastic.
chewy pulled candy

Examples

My grandmother used to make homemade taffy every summer for the family picnic.
We watched as the colorful taffy was pulled and stretched at the candy shop.
Saltwater taffy is a popular treat at many seaside towns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toffee - a hard, chewy candy made by caramelizing sugar or molasses with butter

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from 19th-century dialect (Welsh or English) for 'toffee'.
