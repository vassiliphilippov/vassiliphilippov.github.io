Alban Berg

1. PROPN

/ ˈæl.bɑːn bɛrɡ / (AmE), / ˈal.bæn bɛːɡ / (BrE)
(1885–1935) An Austrian composer known for his works in the twelve-tone technique developed by Schoenberg, including operas such as 'Wozzeck' and 'Lulu'.
Austrian composer; twelve-tone music pioneer

Examples

Many music students study Alban Berg's opera 'Wozzeck' as an example of early 20th-century innovation.
The works of Alban Berg often explore intense emotions through complex harmonies.
Alban Berg was influenced by his teacher, Arnold Schoenberg, and became a prominent figure in modern classical music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arnold Schoenberg - Austrian composer who developed the twelve-tone technique
  • Synonym: Anton Webern - Austrian composer and fellow member of the Second Viennese School
