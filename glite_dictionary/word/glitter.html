glitter

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɪt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪtə / (BrE)
A bright, sparkling shine or gleam; the quality of reflecting light with a shining brilliance.
bright sparkling shine or gloss

Examples

The glitter of the city lights could be seen from miles away.
Her dress caught the glitter of the chandelier as she entered the room.
The glitter of fresh snow on the ground made the morning seem magical.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gleam - a brief or faint appearance of light
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of brightness or shine

Etymology

From Middle English 'gliteren', probably of imitative origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɪt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪtə / (BrE)
a small flash or spark of light; a brief or shimmering brightness
a small flash or spark of light

Examples

The dew on the grass caught the glitter of the morning sun.
In the darkness, there was a glitter of excitement in her eyes.
Tiny pieces of broken glass gave off a glitter in the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sparkle - a small bright flash of light
  • Degree Lower: glow - a steady light or brightness, often soft
  • Degree Higher: dazzle - a strong, blinding brightness

Etymology

From Middle English gliteren, probably of Scandinavian origin; related to Norwegian glitre (to sparkle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07427268-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈɡlɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪtə / (BrE)
to shine with a bright, shimmering, reflected light, as if wet or covered in small sparkles
shine brightly with reflected light

Examples

The lake began to glitter under the afternoon sun.
Her diamond ring glittered on her finger, catching everyone's eye.
Dewdrops glittered like tiny jewels on the grass.

Synset Examples

His eyes were glistening

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to glisten - to shine with a sparkling or reflected light
  • Synonym: shine - to emit light or be bright
  • Antonym: dull - not shiny or bright; lacking luster

Etymology

From Middle English gliteren, probably of Germanic origin, related to German glittern.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
