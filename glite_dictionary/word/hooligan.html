hooligan

1. NOUN

/ ˈhuːlɪɡən / (AmE), / ˈhuːlɪɡən / (BrE)
a violent or rowdy person who causes trouble, often in public places or during sporting events
violent or unruly troublemaker

Examples

The police arrested a hooligan who started a fight after the football match.
A group of hooligans vandalized cars in the city center last night.
Many businesses increased security to deter hooligan activity during the celebrations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thug - a violent person, especially a criminal
  • Synonym: vandal - a person who deliberately damages property
  • Antonym: law-abider - a person who obeys the law

Etymology

Probably from the Irish surname Houlihan, popularized in late 19th-century London.
