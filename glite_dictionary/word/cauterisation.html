cauterisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːtərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɔːtəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of burning or freezing part of the body to stop bleeding or remove abnormal tissue, usually for medical reasons
burning or freezing tissue in medical treatment
medical british

Examples

The surgeon performed cauterisation to prevent further bleeding during the operation.
Early medical practices often relied on cauterisation to treat wounds and infections.
Cauterisation of the wound helped reduce the risk of postoperative complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cauterization - American spelling of cauterisation

Etymology

From Greek *kautērion* (branding iron) via Latin *cauterizare*.
