alkalosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌælkəˈloʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌalkəˈləʊsɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition in which the blood and other body tissues have abnormally high alkalinity (low hydrogen-ion concentration).
abnormally high blood alkalinity
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with alkalosis after a series of blood tests revealed high blood pH.
Metabolic alkalosis can result from excessive vomiting or overuse of diuretics.
If untreated, severe alkalosis may cause muscle cramps, irritability, and confusion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acidosis - abnormally high acidity (high hydrogen-ion concentration) of the blood and other body tissues

Etymology

From New Latin, from 'alkali' and '-osis' (condition).
