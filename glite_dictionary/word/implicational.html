implicational

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪmplɪˈkeɪʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌɪmplɪˈkeɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or concerned with logical implication; expressing or involving the relationship that if one thing is true, another must also be true.
relating to logical implication

Examples

The paper presented a new implicational framework for analyzing logical relations.
In linguistics, implicational universals help explain patterns shared across many languages.
His argument relied on the implicational connections between different propositions.

Synset Examples

implicational language universals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: implication - the conclusion that can be drawn from something, though it is not explicitly stated

Etymology

From 'implication' + '-al', forming an adjective.
