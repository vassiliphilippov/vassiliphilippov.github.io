monarch

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːnɑːrk / (AmE), / ˈmɒnək / (BrE)
a nation's ruler or head of state, especially one who inherits the position by birth
hereditary ruler or head of state

Examples

Queen Elizabeth II was the longest-reigning monarch in British history.
The country abolished its monarch and became a republic in 1946.
Each monarch is expected to serve as a symbol of national unity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king - male hereditary ruler of a nation or territory
  • Synonym: queen - female hereditary ruler of a nation or territory
  • Antonym: president - elected head of a republic, contrasted with monarch

Etymology

From Greek 'monarkhēs' (sole ruler), via Latin 'monarcha'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑnɑrk / (AmE), / ˈmɒnək / (BrE)
a large, migratory North American butterfly (Danaus plexippus) with deep orange wings, black-bordered with white spots; its larvae feed on milkweed plants.
migratory orange and black North American butterfly

Examples

A single monarch butterfly can migrate thousands of miles to reach its winter sanctuary in Mexico.
The bright wings of the monarch warn predators that it is toxic and should not be eaten.
We watched a monarch emerge from its chrysalis in our school garden.
