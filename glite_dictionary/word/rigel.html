Rigel

1. PROPN

/ ˈraɪdʒəl / (AmE), / ˈraɪdʒəl / (BrE)
the brightest star in the constellation Orion, also known as Beta Orionis
brightest star in Orion

Examples

Ancient astronomers used Rigel and Betelgeuse to locate Orion in the night sky.
Through his telescope, he saw Rigel shining brightly below the Orion's Belt.
Rigel is much more luminous than our Sun, making it visible from hundreds of light-years away.

Etymology

From Arabic 'Rijl Jauzah al-Yusrā' meaning 'the left leg of the central one (Orion)'.
