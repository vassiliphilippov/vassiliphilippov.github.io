FSB

1. NOUN

/ ɛf ɛs bi / (AmE), / ɛf ɛs bi / (BrE)
the main internal security and counterintelligence agency of the Russian Federation, and successor to the Soviet KGB
Russian security and counterintelligence agency

Examples

The FSB has played a significant role in Russia's domestic security since the 1990s.
Journalists reported that the FSB was involved in the investigation into the cyber attacks.
Vladimir Putin, before becoming president, once led the FSB during a turbulent period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: KGB - the former Soviet Union's main security agency, predecessor to the FSB

Etymology

Acronym for Russian Федеральная служба безопасности (Federal'naya sluzhba bezopasnosti, 'Federal Security Service').
