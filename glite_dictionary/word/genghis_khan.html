Genghis Khan

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɛŋɡɪs ˌkɑn / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛŋɡɪs ˌkɑːn / (BrE)
the founder and first Great Khan of the Mongol Empire, who united the Mongol tribes and established an empire stretching from the Black Sea to the Pacific Ocean (c. 1162–1227)
Mongol emperor and founder of the Mongol Empire

Examples

Genghis Khan united the Mongol tribes and created one of the largest empires in history.
Historians have studied the military strategies of Genghis Khan for centuries.
The legacy of Genghis Khan can still be seen in many cultures across Eurasia.

Etymology

From Mongolian Chinggis Khan (universal ruler), title adopted by Temüjin.

2. PROPN

/ ˈɡɛŋɡɪs ˈkɑn / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛŋɡɪs ˈkɑːn / (BrE)
the founder and first Great Khan of the Mongol Empire (c. 1162–1227), whose empire became the largest contiguous land empire in history
Mongol emperor, founder of largest land empire

Examples

Genghis Khan united the Mongol tribes and began a campaign of conquest across Asia.
Many historians study the military tactics of Genghis Khan to understand his unprecedented success.
The influence of Genghis Khan can still be seen in many regions once ruled by the Mongols.

Etymology

From Mongolian Чингис Хаан (Chingis Khaan), meaning 'universal ruler'.
