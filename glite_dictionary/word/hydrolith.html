hydrolith

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdrəˌlɪθ / (AmE), / ˈhʌɪdrəlɪθ / (BrE)
A saltlike binary compound, especially calcium hydride (CaH₂), used as a reducing agent and a source of hydrogen.
a chemical compound, usually calcium hydride
medical

Examples

The laboratory stored the hydrolith in sealed containers to prevent moisture exposure.
Production of hydrogen gas can be achieved by reacting hydrolith with water.
The chemist explained that hydrolith serves as both a reducing agent and a hydrogen source.

Etymology

From hydro- (water) and -lith (stone), reflecting its role in generating hydrogen.
