isogamete

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsəˈɡæmiːt / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəʊˈɡæmiːt / (BrE)
either of a pair of gametes that are similar in size and structure and can fuse to form a zygote
gamete of similar size and form
medical

Examples

Many algae reproduce using isogametes, which are indistinguishable except by mating type.
The fusion of two isogametes results in the formation of a diploid zygote.
In some species, isogametes are motile, allowing them to seek out one another for fertilization.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anisogamete - a gamete that differs in size or form from another with which it fuses

Etymology

From Greek 'isos' (equal) and 'gamete' (wife, spouse).
