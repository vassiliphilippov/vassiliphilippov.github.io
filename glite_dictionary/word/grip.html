grip

1. NOUN

/ ɡrɪp / (AmE), / ɡrɪp / (BrE)
the act of holding something tightly or firmly; a strong grasp
the act of holding tightly

Examples

His grip on the steering wheel tightened as the car skidded.
She lost her grip and the glass fell to the floor.
The child maintained a firm grip on her mother's hand in the crowd.

Synset Examples

he released his clasp on my arm
he has a strong grip for an old man
she kept a firm hold on the railing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hold - the act of grasping or holding something tightly

Etymology

From Old English 'grippan' (to grasp).
