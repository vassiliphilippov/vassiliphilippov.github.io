aphakic

1. NOUN

/ əˈfeɪkɪk / (AmE), / əˈfeɪkɪk / (BrE)
a person who lacks the natural lenses of the eyes, typically as a result of disease or surgery
person lacking eye lenses
medical

Examples

After cataract surgery, the patient became an aphakic and needed corrective lenses.
The study compared visual outcomes in aphakic and pseudophakic children.
An aphakic may require special contact lenses to restore normal vision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphakia - absence of the natural lens of the eye

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) and 'phakos' (lens).

2. ADJ

/ əˈfeɪkɪk / (AmE), / əˈfeɪkɪk / (BrE)
relating to or affected by aphakia (the absence of the eye's natural lens)
without a natural eye lens; relating to aphakia
medical

Examples

The surgeon explained that an aphakic patient would need a special type of corrective lens.
After cataract removal, she became aphakic in her right eye.
Some children are born aphakic and require immediate treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphakia - absence of the eye's natural lens

Etymology

From aphakia (absence of lens) + -ic (adjective suffix); aphakia from Greek 'a-' (without) + 'phakos' (lens).
