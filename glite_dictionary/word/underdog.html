underdog

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌdɑg / (AmE), / ˈʌndədɒg / (BrE)
a person or group in a competition who is expected to lose or is at a disadvantage
person or group expected to lose

Examples

Despite the odds, the underdog team won the championship last night.
Everyone loves a good underdog story where the least expected triumphs.
She always roots for the underdog in any contest.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: favorite - the competitor expected to win
  • Synonym: long shot - a competitor given little chance to succeed

Etymology

From 'under' (below in rank) + 'dog', first recorded in the late 19th century, originally referring to the dog lower down in a dogfight.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌndərˌdɔg / (AmE), / ˈʌndədɒg / (BrE)
A person, team, or competitor thought to have little chance of winning a contest or struggle.
competitor expected to lose

Examples

The crowd cheered as the underdog made a surprising comeback in the final quarter.
Despite being the underdog, she never gave up hope of winning the championship.
In many stories, the underdog overcomes obstacles to achieve unexpected success.

Synset Examples

The underdog surprised everyone by beating Banaghan.
The underdog aimed to dethrone the reigning champion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: favorite - competitor expected to win

Etymology

From 'under-' + 'dog', originally referring to the dog expected to lose in a dog fight.
