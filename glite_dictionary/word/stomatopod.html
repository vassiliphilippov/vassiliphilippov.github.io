stomatopod

1. NOUN

/ stoʊˈmætəˌpɒd / (AmE), / stəʊˈmatəpɒd / (BrE)
A predatory marine crustacean of the order Stomatopoda, known for their powerful claws and complex eyes, commonly called mantis shrimp.
a predatory marine crustacean (mantis shrimp)
medical

Examples

Researchers studied the vision systems of stomatopods to understand color perception in marine environments.
The aquarium recently acquired several colorful stomatopods for their new marine exhibit.
A stomatopod can strike its prey with incredible speed and force.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mantis shrimp - Common name for a stomatopod, a predatory marine crustacean

Etymology

From Modern Latin Stomatopoda, from Greek stoma (mouth) + pous, pod- (foot).

2. NOUN

/ stoʊˈmætəˌpɑd / (AmE), / stəˈmatəˌpɒd / (BrE)
A marine crustacean of the order Stomatopoda, known for its powerful front limbs and complex eyes; also called mantis shrimp.
marine crustacean (mantis shrimp)

Examples

The colorful stomatopod quickly darted into its rocky burrow.
Scientists study the vision of the stomatopod because of its remarkably complex eyes.
My aquarium houses a small stomatopod that occasionally rearranges the gravel with its claws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mantis shrimp - common name for stomatopod, known for striking predatory appendages

Etymology

From Greek *stoma* (mouth) + *pous* (foot), referring to mouthpart appendages.
