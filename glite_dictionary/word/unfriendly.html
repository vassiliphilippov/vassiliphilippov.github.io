unfriendly

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfrɛndli / (AmE), / ʌnˈfrɛndli / (BrE)
not kind, pleasant, or supportive; lacking goodwill toward others
not kind or friendly

Examples

Her unfriendly tone made the meeting uncomfortable for everyone.
The neighbors were surprisingly unfriendly when we moved in.
His unfriendly gesture discouraged any further conversation.

Synset Examples

an unfriendly coldness of manner
an unfriendly action to take

Related Terms

  • Antonym: friendly - kind and pleasant; showing kindness or support

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' (meaning 'not') to 'friendly.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01080481-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfrɛndli / (AmE), / ʌnˈfrɛndli / (BrE)
(of an environment or conditions) not suitable for life, growth, or development; harsh or inhospitable.
harsh or inhospitable (environment, conditions)

Examples

The Sahara Desert is an unfriendly environment for most forms of life.
Scientists study how certain plants can survive in unfriendly climates.
A sudden drop in temperature created unfriendly conditions for the seedlings.

Synset Examples

a hostile climate
an uncongenial atmosphere
an uncongenial soil
the unfriendly environment at high altitudes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hostile - very harsh or antagonistic to life or growth
  • Antonym: congenial - pleasant or suitable (as an environment)

Etymology

From un- (not) + friendly (from Old English 'freondlic').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01246247-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfrɛn.dli / (AmE), / ʌnˈfrɛn.dli / (BrE)
not kind or pleasant towards others; showing a lack of friendliness or warmth.
not friendly or warm

Examples

Her unfriendly attitude made guests feel unwelcome.
The new neighbors were surprisingly unfriendly when we tried to introduce ourselves.
He received an unfriendly response to his request for help.

Synset Examples

an unfriendly act of aggression
an inimical critic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: friendly - kind and pleasant toward others
  • Synonym: hostile - openly antagonistic or opposed

Etymology

From un- (prefix meaning 'not') + friendly.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01249262-s
Synset POS: s
