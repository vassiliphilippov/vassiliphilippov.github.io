microchip

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪkroʊˌtʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌtʃɪp / (BrE)
a small piece of silicon containing an integrated circuit that performs various electronic functions in computers and other devices
integrated circuit on a small silicon chip

Examples

The smartphone's performance is powered by a sophisticated microchip.
Dogs and cats are often implanted with a microchip for identification purposes.
Advances in microchip technology have revolutionized consumer electronics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: integrated circuit - an electronic circuit formed on a small piece of semiconductor material

Etymology

From 'micro-' (small) + 'chip' (thin slice of semiconductor material).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪkroʊˌtʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌtʃɪp / (BrE)
A tiny piece of silicon that contains an integrated circuit for processing electronic signals and data.
integrated circuit on silicon chip

Examples

The new phone uses a state-of-the-art microchip to improve its processing speed.
Doctors can implant a small microchip under the skin to track your pet.
Recent advances have enabled microchip technology to become even smaller and more powerful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: integrated circuit - an electronic circuit formed on a small piece of semiconducting material

Etymology

From 'micro-' (small) + 'chip', referring to the miniature size of the silicon component.
