festoon

1. NOUN

/ fɛˈstuːn / (AmE), / fɛˈstuːn / (BrE)
a decorative curtain or strip of fabric, draped and tied at intervals to form graceful curves
a decorative draped curtain or fabric

Examples

The wedding hall was adorned with silk festoons draping elegantly from the ceiling.
A bright red festoon hung above the stage, adding a touch of color to the ceremony.
During the festival, each window was decorated with a colorful festoon of fabric.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garland - a wreath or chain of flowers and leaves, used as decoration

Etymology

From French *feston*, from Italian *festone* (a festive garland), from Latin *festum* (festival).

2. NOUN

/ fɛˈstun / (AmE), / fɛˈstuːn / (BrE)
a decorative chain or strip hung in a curve between two points, often made of flowers, leaves, or ribbons, used as ornamentation
a decorative hanging chain or strip

Examples

The ceiling was adorned with a delicate festoon of roses and ivy.
Classical buildings often feature a stone festoon carved above doorways.
The wedding hall was decorated with colorful festoons made from paper flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garland - a wreath or chain of flowers and leaves, worn or hung for decoration

Etymology

From French *feston* (ornamental garland), from Italian *festone*, from *festa* (feast, festival).

4. VERB

/ fɛˈstuːn / (AmE), / fɛˈstuːn / (BrE)
to decorate a place with chains, garlands, or strings of flowers, ribbons, or similar decorations, especially by draping them in loops.
decorate with strings of flowers or garlands

Examples

The town square was festooned with colorful banners for the festival.
She festooned the hallways with paper chains and balloons for the celebration.
The trees along the avenue were festooned with twinkling lights for the winter holiday.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decorate - to make something look more attractive by putting things on it
