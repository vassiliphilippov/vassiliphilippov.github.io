organelle

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔrɡəˈnɛl / (AmE), / ˌɔːɡəˈnɛl / (BrE)
a specialized structure within a cell that performs a specific function, analogous to an organ in the body
specialized structure in a cell
medical

Examples

The mitochondrion is an organelle responsible for energy production within the cell.
Each organelle has a unique function essential to the cell's survival.
Plant cells contain an organelle called the chloroplast, which is responsible for photosynthesis.

Synset Examples

the first organelle to be identified was the nucleus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organoid - a structure resembling an organ, especially one produced artificially in cell culture

Etymology

From Modern Latin organella, diminutive of organum ('organ').
