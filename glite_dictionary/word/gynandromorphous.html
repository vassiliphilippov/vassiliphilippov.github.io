gynandromorphous

1. ADJ

/ ˌdʒaɪˌnændrəˈmɔrfəs / (AmE), / ˌdʒʌɪˌnandrəˈmɔːfəs / (BrE)
Having both male and female morphological characteristics, especially as observed in certain animals or insects.
having both male and female characteristics

Examples

The butterfly displayed a striking gynandromorphous pattern, with one wing patterned as male and the other as female.
Scientists were fascinated by the gynandromorphous bird because of its split plumage coloration.
Some insects are born gynandromorphous, a rare condition resulting in both male and female traits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hermaphroditic - having both male and female reproductive organs

Etymology

From Greek 'gynē' (woman), 'anēr, andros' (man), and 'morphē' (form).
