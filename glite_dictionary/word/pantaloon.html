2. NOUN

/ ˌpæn.təˈlun / (AmE), / ˌpæn.təˈluːn / (BrE)
(in pantomime and commedia dell’arte) a foolish or comical old man, typically the butt of jokes or a stock buffoon character.
a comic buffoon character in pantomime

Examples

The pantaloon stumbled across the stage, prompting laughter from the audience.
In the traditional Christmas pantomime, the pantaloon is always outwitted by the younger characters.
He was cast as the pantaloon, playing the role of a doddering old man full of comic antics.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Italian 'Pantalone', the name of a stock character in commedia dell’arte.

3. NOUN

/ ˌpæn.t̬əˈlun / (AmE), / ˌpæn.təˈluːn / (BrE)
A man's close-fitting pair of trousers, especially those of a type worn in former times.
old-fashioned close-fitting trousers

Examples

During the masquerade ball, the nobleman wore an elaborate mask and silk pantaloons.
In some plays, the old clown character is depicted in baggy pantaloons.
He studied portraits from the 18th century that showed men dressed in pantaloons and waistcoats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trousers - an outer garment covering the body from the waist to the ankles, with a separate part for each leg

Etymology

From Italian 'pantalone', the name of a character in commedia dell'arte who wore such trousers.
