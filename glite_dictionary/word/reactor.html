reactor

1. NOUN

/ riˈæktər / (AmE), / riˈæktə / (BrE)
A device in which a controlled nuclear reaction is maintained and used to produce energy or artificial elements.
device for controlled nuclear reactions

Examples

The city is building a new reactor to supply clean energy to households.
Scientists at the facility monitor the reactor around the clock for safety.
After the accident, operations at the reactor were suspended for several months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuclear reactor - A type of reactor specifically using nuclear fission to generate energy

Etymology

From Latin 'reactor' ('one who does something'), from 're-' (again) + 'agere' (to do).

2. NOUN

/ riˈæktɚ / (AmE), / riˈæktə / (BrE)
an electrical device used to introduce reactance into a circuit
device introducing reactance in circuits

Examples

The electrical engineer replaced the faulty reactor to restore stability to the circuit.
A reactor limits current flow by adding inductive reactance to the electrical system.
Modern power grids rely on at least one reactor to manage voltage fluctuations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inductor - an electrical component that stores energy in a magnetic field

Etymology

From Latin 'reactor', from 're-' (again) + 'agere' (to do), influenced by technical word formation.
