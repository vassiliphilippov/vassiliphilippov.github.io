territorialisation

1. NOUN

/ təˌrɪtɔːriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / təˌrɪtəriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of organizing, defining, or demarcating an area as a territory, often for administrative, political, or social purposes
organization of space as a territory

Examples

The territorialisation of land by the government affected the traditional ways of local communities.
Urban territorialisation often shapes how neighborhoods develop distinct identities.
The process of territorialisation can result in new borders and regulations within a region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: territorialization - the American spelling of territorialisation; the act of organising as a territory

Etymology

From 'territory' + '-alisation', patterned after French 'territorialisation'.
