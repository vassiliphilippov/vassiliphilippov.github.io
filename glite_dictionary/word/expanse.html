expanse

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspæns / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɑːns / (BrE)
a wide and open area or extent; a large, continuous space or scope
a wide, open area or scope

Examples

The expanse of the desert seemed to stretch on forever under the blazing sun.
From the hilltop, they admired the endless expanse of water below.
Her eyes swept across the expanse of the open meadow, taking in the wildflowers.

Synset Examples

the sweep of the plains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stretch - a continuous length or area of land or water
  • Antonym: limit - the point or edge beyond which something does not extend

Etymology

From Latin 'expansus', past participle of 'expandere' (to spread out).

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspæns / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɑːns / (BrE)
a wide, continuous area of open space, especially land, water, or sky
wide open area or space

Examples

From the top of the hill, a vast expanse of forest stretched as far as the eye could see.
The calm expanse of the lake mirrored the evening sky with perfect clarity.
They crossed an open expanse of desert before reaching the mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stretch - a continuous area or length of land or water
  • Synonym: tract - an area of land, typically a large one

Etymology

From Latin 'expandere' (to spread out) via French 'expanse'.

Synset POS: n
