Tanacetum

1. NOUN

/ təˈnæsətəm / (AmE), / təˈnæsɪtəm / (BrE)
A large genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, often resembling chrysanthemums, including tansies and related species.
genus of flowering plants resembling chrysanthemums

Examples

Several species in the Tanacetum genus are prized for their ornamental flowers.
The leaves of Tanacetum plants are sometimes used in traditional remedies.
Gardeners often confuse Tanacetum with related genera like Chrysanthemum.

Etymology

From Latin 'tanacetum', meaning 'tansy'.

2. NOUN

/ təˈnæsɪtəm / (AmE), / təˈnæsɪtəm / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, closely related to chrysanthemums and including the tansies and some species often formerly placed in other genera such as Chrysanthemum.
a genus of flowering plants related to chrysanthemums

Examples

The garden featured several species of Tanacetum with bright yellow flowers.
Botanists recently reclassified some plants into the genus Tanacetum.
Common tansy is one of the most recognizable members of Tanacetum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chrysanthemum - a genus of flowering plants closely related to Tanacetum

Etymology

From Latin 'tanacetum', meaning 'tansy'.
