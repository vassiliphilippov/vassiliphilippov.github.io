sporocarp

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɔɹ.əˌkɑɹp / (AmE), / ˈspɒɹ.əˌkɑːp / (BrE)
a specialized structure in certain aquatic ferns that encloses sori or clusters of sporangia, serving as a reproductive organ
reproductive structure in some aquatic ferns
medical

Examples

The sporocarp of the water fern contains the spores necessary for reproduction.
Researchers examined the structure and development of the sporocarp under a microscope.
Each sporocarp is adapted to protect the sori from harsh aquatic environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorus - cluster of sporangia (spore-producing structures) in ferns and fungi

Etymology

From Greek sporos (seed, spore) + karpos (fruit).
