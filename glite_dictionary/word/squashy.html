squashy

1. ADJ

/ ˈskwɑʃi / (AmE), / ˈskwɒʃi / (BrE)
soft and easily crushed or compressed, often with a texture like a sponge
soft and easily compressed

Examples

The ripe peach was so squashy that it left juice on my fingers.
Please avoid sitting on the squashy cushions, as they lose their shape easily.
After the rain, the ground felt squashy under our shoes.

Synset Examples

spongy bread

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spongy - soft and full of holes or spaces like a sponge
  • Antonym: firm - solid and not easily pressed down or changed in shape

2. ADJ

/ ˈskwɑːʃi / (AmE), / ˈskwɒʃi / (BrE)
(of soil or material) soft, wet, and easily crushed or squashed under pressure.
soft and wet; easily crushed

Examples

After the rain, the garden became so squashy that your boots sank with every step.
The children loved jumping on the squashy mud near the pond.
He set up the tent on some surprisingly squashy soil, making it unstable.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spongy - soft and able to absorb moisture like a sponge
  • Antonym: firm - solid and not easily compressed

Etymology

From 'squash' (to crush with force), formed with the -y suffix.

3. ADJ

/ ˈskwɑʃi / (AmE), / ˈskwɒʃi / (BrE)
soft and easily crushed, like overripe fruit or pulp; lacking firmness or stiffness.
soft and easily squashed

Examples

The overripe peach felt squashy in my hand.
After the rain, the ground became squashy and muddy.
She didn't like squashy toys because they lost their shape easily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soft - not hard or firm to the touch
  • Antonym: firm - solid or hard, not easily pressed
  • Synonym: squishy - soft and yielding to pressure
