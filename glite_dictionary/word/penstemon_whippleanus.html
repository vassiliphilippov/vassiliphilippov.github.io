Penstemon whippleanus

1. NOUN

/ pɛnˈstiːmɒn wɪpˈliːænəs / (AmE), / pɛnˈstiːmən wɪpˈliːænəs / (BrE)
A species of beardtongue with wine to purple or black flowers in clustered arrangements, native to the Rocky Mountains region of North America.
a species of Rocky Mountain beardtongue

Examples

The wildflower Penstemon whippleanus adds vibrant color to many mountain meadows in Colorado.
Botanists identified several clusters of Penstemon whippleanus along the high-altitude trail.
Gardeners value Penstemon whippleanus for its hardy nature and striking purple flowers.

Etymology

Named after American botanist Amiel Weeks Whipple; genus name from Greek 'penta' (five) and 'stēmōn' (thread, referring to stamens).
