Parcheesi

1. NOUN

/ pɑːrˈtʃiːzi / (AmE), / pɑːˈtʃiːzi / (BrE)
A modern board game for two to four players in which players move pieces around a cross-shaped board based on rolls of dice, derived from the Indian game pachisi.
modern board game based on pachisi
american

Examples

We played Parcheesi with the kids on a rainy Saturday afternoon.
My favorite board games growing up were Parcheesi and Monopoly.
The rules of Parcheesi are simple enough for beginners to learn quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pachisi - traditional Indian board game from which Parcheesi is derived

Etymology

Trademarked American adaptation of the Indian game pachisi.
