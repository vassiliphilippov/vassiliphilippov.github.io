hirudinean

1. NOUN

/ həˌruːdəˈniən / (AmE), / hɪˌruːdɪˈniːən / (BrE)
a carnivorous or bloodsucking aquatic or terrestrial worm, especially a leech, typically having a sucker at each end
carnivorous or bloodsucking worm (leech)
medical

Examples

The pond was home to several species of hirudinean that fed on the fish and amphibians.
Research on the digestive systems of hirudineans has provided insights into anticoagulant substances.
Biologists collected a rare hirudinean from the muddy banks during the expedition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leech - bloodsucking aquatic or terrestrial worm of the class Hirudinea

Etymology

From Latin 'hirudo', 'hirudinis' (leech), with the suffix '-ean' indicating belonging to.
