Ahab

1. PROPN

/ ˈeɪˌhæb / (AmE), / ˈeɪhab / (BrE)
A king of Israel in the Old Testament, known for promoting pagan worship and being the husband of Jezebel in the 9th century BC.
Old Testament king of Israel, husband of Jezebel

Examples

Many scholars study Ahab to understand the religious conflicts in ancient Israel.
In the Bible, Ahab is often criticized for his alliance with Jezebel.
The story of Ahab and Elijah is a central theme in several Old Testament chapters.
