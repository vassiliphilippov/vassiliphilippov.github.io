tophus

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊ.fəs / (AmE), / ˈtɒf.əs / (BrE)
a deposit of uric acid crystals, especially around a joint or in the ear, typically occurring in advanced gout
uric acid deposit in gout
medical

Examples

The doctor identified a tophus on the patient's big toe, confirming a diagnosis of gout.
Treatment is required when a tophus becomes painful or restricts movement.
A visible tophus in the ear is often a sign of long-standing gout.

Etymology

From Latin tophus (stone, chalky stone), also used in medical Latin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊfəs / (AmE), / ˈtəʊfəs / (BrE)
A hard, calcareous deposit formed on the teeth and gums, often referred to as dental tartar.
calcareous dental deposit
medical

Examples

The dentist discovered a large tophus on her lower molar during the examination.
Regular brushing helps prevent the formation of tophus and other dental problems.
After years of neglect, a noticeable tophus had developed along his gum line.

Etymology

From Latin tophus, tufa (a type of porous stone), referring to its stone-like hardness.
