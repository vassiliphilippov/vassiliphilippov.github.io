Macadamia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæk.əˈdeɪ.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˌmak.əˈdeɪ.mi.ə / (BrE)
A genus of trees and shrubs native to Madagascar and Australia, including species that produce macadamia nuts.
genus of trees producing macadamia nuts

Examples

The Macadamia is prized for its delicious, creamy nuts used in confectionery.
Several species within the Macadamia genus are cultivated in Australia for nut production.
Botanists discovered a wild Macadamia growing deep in the rainforest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macadamia nut - edible nut produced by Macadamia trees

Etymology

Named after John Macadam (1827–1865), a Scottish-Australian chemist and politician.
