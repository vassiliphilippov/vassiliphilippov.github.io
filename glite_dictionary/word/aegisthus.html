Aegisthus

1. PROPN

/ ɪgˈɪsθəs / (AmE), / iːˈɪsθəs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the lover of Clytemnestra and murderer of Agamemnon, who ruled Mycenae until he was slain by Orestes.
Greek mythological figure; murderer of Agamemnon

Examples

In many classical plays, Aegisthus is depicted as the main antagonist plotting against Agamemnon.
After Agamemnon’s death, Aegisthus seized power in Mycenae with Clytemnestra by his side.
The story of Aegisthus illustrates the tragic cycles of revenge in Greek mythology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Clytemnestra - wife of Agamemnon in Greek mythology, lover of Aegisthus
  • Antonym: Agamemnon - King of Mycenae, killed by Aegisthus and Clytemnestra
  • Antonym: Orestes - Son of Agamemnon, avenger of his father against Aegisthus

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Αἴγισθος (Aígisthos).
