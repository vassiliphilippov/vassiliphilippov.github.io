nursemaid

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɝsˌmeɪd / (AmE), / ˈnɜːs.meɪd / (BrE)
a woman employed to care for and look after children, especially in a private household
woman who cares for children

Examples

The wealthy family hired a nursemaid to care for their three young children.
As a nursemaid, she spent most of her day feeding, bathing, and playing with the kids.
She worked as a nursemaid in London during the early twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nanny - a person, usually a woman, employed to care for children in a household

Etymology

Formed from 'nurse' and 'maid', both of Middle English origin.
