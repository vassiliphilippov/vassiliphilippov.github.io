nutshell

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌtˌʃɛl / (AmE), / ˈnʌtʃɛl / (BrE)
the hard outer shell that surrounds the kernel of a nut
hard shell of a nut

Examples

He cracked the nutshell open to reveal the fresh walnut inside.
The floor was littered with pecan nutshells after the party.
Squirrels are experts at breaking open a nutshell to get the treat within.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shell - the hard outer covering of something, such as a nut, egg, or seed

Etymology

From Middle English *nuteshell*, from Old English *hnutu* (nut) + *scealu* (shell).
