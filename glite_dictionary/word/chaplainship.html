chaplainship

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæplɪnˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈtʃæplɪnʃɪp / (BrE)
the position or office of a chaplain
the office of a chaplain

Examples

He was honored to accept the chaplainship at the university hospital.
After years of service, she finally attained the chaplainship she had worked toward.
The chaplainship at the military base required significant dedication and compassion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaplain - a clergy member who provides religious services in institutions

Etymology

Formed from 'chaplain' + suffix '-ship' indicating office or position.
