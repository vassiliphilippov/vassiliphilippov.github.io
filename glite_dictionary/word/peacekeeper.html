peacekeeper

1. NOUN

/ ˈpisˌkipər / (AmE), / ˈpiːsˌkiːpə / (BrE)
(historical, US) A pistol carried by a law officer in the Old West, particularly the Colt Single Action Army revolver.
law officer's pistol (Old West)
american

Examples

The sheriff always wore his peacekeeper at his hip when patrolling the dusty streets.
Legendary lawmen in the Old West relied on their trusty peacekeeper to maintain order.
He polished his peacekeeper every night, ready for whatever trouble might come.

Etymology

From 'peace' + 'keeper,' referring to an object (the gun) used to keep or enforce peace.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpisˌkipər / (AmE), / ˈpiːsˌkiːpə / (BrE)
a member of a military or police force sent, often by an international organization, to help maintain or restore peace in a conflict area
member of a force maintaining peace

Examples

United Nations peacekeepers arrived in the region to monitor the ceasefire.
The role of a peacekeeper often requires patience and diplomacy as well as military skill.
Several countries contributed peacekeepers to help stabilize the post-war area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peacekeeping - the practice of maintaining peace, especially through international military or police forces
  • Antonym: combatant - a person or nation engaged in fighting during a war

Etymology

Formed from 'peace' + 'keeper', modeled after 'peacekeeping' and used since the mid-20th century, especially in international contexts.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpisˌkipər / (AmE), / ˈpiːsˌkiːpə / (BrE)
a person who keeps or enforces peace, often by mediating conflicts or maintaining order
a person who keeps or enforces peace

Examples

As the peacekeeper in our group, she always calms arguments before they escalate.
UN peacekeepers were sent to the region to help restore order.
He acted as a peacekeeper during the heated debate.

Synset Examples

she's the peacekeeper in that family

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mediator - a person who attempts to make people involved in a conflict come to an agreement

Etymology

Formed from 'peace' + 'keeper'.
