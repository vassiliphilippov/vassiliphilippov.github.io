var

1. NOUN

/ vɑr / (AmE), / vɑː / (BrE)
a unit of reactive power in an alternating current circuit, equal to the power dissipated when one volt produces a current of one ampere (symbol: var)
unit of reactive power in AC circuits

Examples

The generator supplies 500 vars to the circuit under load.
Engineers often calculate the var to assess power efficiency in AC systems.
A low var value can indicate problems with reactive power in electrical networks.

Etymology

Acronym from 'volt-ampere reactive'.
