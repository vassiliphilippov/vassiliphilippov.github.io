cheliferous

1. ADJ

/ ˌkiːlɪˈfɛrəs / (AmE), / ˌkɛlɪˈfɛrəs / (BrE)
(of an animal) having chelae or pincer-like appendages.
having pincer-like appendages

Examples

Certain species of scorpions are cheliferous, possessing large pincers at the end of their legs.
The zoologist classified the mysterious arthropod as cheliferous due to its prominent claws.
Unlike spiders, many crustaceans are cheliferous, using their pincers for defense and feeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chelate - having chelae (claws or pincers)

Etymology

From Greek 'chēlē' (claw, pincer) + Latin '-ferous' (bearing, carrying).
