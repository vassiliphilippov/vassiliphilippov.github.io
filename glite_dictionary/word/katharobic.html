katharobic

1. ADJ

/ kæθəˈroʊbɪk / (AmE), / kæθəˈrəʊbɪk / (BrE)
(of an organism) living or able to function in an oxygen-rich environment that lacks organic matter.
organism in oxygenated, organic-free environment
medical

Examples

Certain microbes are katharobic, thriving only in highly oxygenated, organic-free environments.
The katharobic conditions of the treatment plant help prevent the growth of unwanted bacteria.
Researchers studied katharobic life forms in deep, oxygen-rich aquifers.

Etymology

From Greek katharos (clean, pure) + -bic from bios (life).
