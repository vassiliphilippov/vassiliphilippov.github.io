million

1. NUM

/ ˈmɪljən / (AmE), / ˈmɪljən / (BrE)
The number one thousand thousand (1,000,000).
one thousand thousand; 1,000,000

Examples

There are over million books in the university library.
The company aims to reach one million customers by next year.
He won a million dollars in the lottery.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: billion - the number one thousand million (1,000,000,000) in American English
  • Degree Lower: thousand - the number one thousand (1,000)

Etymology

From Italian 'milione', augmentative of 'mille' (thousand).

2. NUM

/ ˈmɪljən / (AmE), / ˈmɪljən / (BrE)
A number equal to 1,000,000; a quantity consisting of one thousand thousand items or units.
the number 1,000,000

Examples

There are over a million species of insects in the world.
He won a million dollars in the lottery last year.
The city has a population of nearly one million people.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: billion - the number 1,000,000,000
  • Degree Lower: thousand - the number 1,000

Etymology

From Old French *milion*, from Italian *milione*, augmentative of *mille* (thousand).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02206846-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɪljən / (AmE), / ˈmɪljən / (BrE)
An extremely large but indefinite number, often used as hyperbole rather than the exact figure.
very large indefinite number (hyperbole)

Examples

I've told you a million times to turn off the lights when you leave.
There must have been a million people in the crowd at the concert.
She has million things to do before the trip.

Synset Examples

there were millions of flies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zillion - an extremely large but indefinite number (slang)
  • Degree Lower: thousand - a large but smaller indefinite number

Etymology

From Old French *milion*, from Italian *milione*, from *mille* (thousand).
