encompassment

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkʌmpəsmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌmpəsmənt / (BrE)
the act of surrounding or including something completely
act of surrounding or including completely

Examples

The encompassment of the entire village by the new highway changed its character forever.
Their argument relied on the encompassment of all viewpoints, no matter how unpopular.
The project's success depended on the encompassment of both technical and human factors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inclusion - the act of including someone or something within a group or structure

Etymology

From 'encompass' + '-ment', with 'encompass' from Old French 'encompasser'.
