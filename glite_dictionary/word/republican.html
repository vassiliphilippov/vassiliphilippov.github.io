republican

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈpʌblɪkən / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌblɪkən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of a republic, especially a form of government in which power is held by the people or their elected representatives.
relating to a republic or its principles

Examples

The constitution guarantees a republican form of government to every state.
She expressed her support for republican values over hereditary rule.
Their political system is based on republican principles rather than monarchy.

Synset Examples

the United States shall guarantee to every state in this union a republican form of government
a very republican notion
so little republican and so much aristocratic sentiment
our republican and artistic simplicity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: democratic - relating to a democracy, where power is with the people
  • Antonym: aristocratic - relating to government by a privileged class or nobility

Etymology

From Latin 'res publica' (public affair), through French 'républicain'.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈpʌblɪkən / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌblɪkən / (BrE)
a person who supports or advocates a republic, especially as opposed to a monarchy
advocate of a republic

Examples

During the revolution, he became a prominent republican campaigning against royal rule.
Irish republicans have long opposed the British monarchy in Northern Ireland.
As a lifelong republican, she attended many protests in favor of a republic.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monarchist - a supporter of a monarchy
  • Synonym: democrat - a supporter of democracy or democratic government

Etymology

From Latin 'res publica' (the public affair), via French 'républicain'.

Synset POS: n
