Republican

1. NOUN

/ rəˈpʌblɪkən / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌblɪkən / (BrE)
A member or supporter of the Republican Party in the United States.
member of the US Republican Party
american

Examples

He has always identified himself as a proud Republican in local politics.
Several Republicans spoke out against the proposed legislation.
The debate between the Republican and Democratic candidates was broadcast nationwide.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Democrat - a member of the Democratic Party in the United States

Etymology

From republic + -an, originally referring to supporters of republican government, later to the US political party founded in 1854.

2. PROPN

/ rɪˈpʌblɪkən / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌblɪkən / (BrE)
a river in the central United States, a tributary of the Kansas River flowing from eastern Colorado east through Nebraska and Kansas
river in central U.S.; tributary of Kansas River

Examples

The Republican has played an important role in the development of agriculture in Kansas.
After heavy rains, the Republican sometimes overflows its banks in Nebraska.
The town sits near the confluence of the Republican and the Kansas River.

Etymology

Named after the Republican Pawnee, a band of the Pawnee people.
