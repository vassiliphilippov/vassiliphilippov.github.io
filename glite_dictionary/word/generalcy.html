generalcy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnərəlsi / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnərəlsi / (BrE)
the office, rank, or authority of a general in the armed forces
office or authority of a general

Examples

After years of service, he finally attained the generalcy he had long desired.
The powers associated with the generalcy give considerable influence over military operations.
Several officers aspired to the generalcy as vacancies were expected in top positions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generalship - the skill or position of being a general

Etymology

Formed from 'general' and the suffix '-cy', denoting rank or condition.
