Sam Adams

1. PROPN

/ sæm ˈædəmz / (AmE), / sæm ˈædəmz / (BrE)
(1722–1803) An American Revolutionary leader and patriot, organizer of the Boston Tea Party and signer of the Declaration of Independence.
American Revolutionary leader and patriot

Examples

Sam Adams played a vital role in guiding the American colonies toward independence.
The Boston Tea Party was organized in part by Sam Adams and fellow patriots.
Many historians regard Sam Adams as a key architect of the revolutionary movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: John Adams - Second President of the United States and cousin of Sam Adams
  • Synonym: Boston Tea Party - A protest against British taxation organized by Sam Adams
