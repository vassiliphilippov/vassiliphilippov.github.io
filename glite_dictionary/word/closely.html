closely

1. ADV

/ ˈkloʊsli / (AmE), / ˈkləʊsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows careful attention or observation.
with careful attention or observation

Examples

The detective watched the suspect closely as he entered the room.
She listened closely to every detail of the instructions.
The committee is monitoring the situation closely for any developments.

Synset Examples

he remained close on his guard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carefully - with care or attention
  • Antonym: casually - in a relaxed or unconcerned manner

Etymology

From the adjective 'close' + adverbial suffix '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ˈkloʊsli / (AmE), / ˈkləʊsli / (BrE)
in a close manner; in a way that shows strong connection, relation, or proximity
in a close manner

Examples

She watched the proceedings closely to catch every detail.
The two companies are closely linked through shared ownership.
Examine the evidence closely before drawing any conclusions.

Synset Examples

the two phenomena are intimately connected
the person most nearly concerned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intimately - in a very close or personal way
  • Antonym: remotely - in a distant or not close manner

Etymology

From 'close' + '-ly', from Old French 'clos' (shut, enclosed) and Middle English '-ly'.

3. ADV

/ ˈkloʊsli / (AmE), / ˈkləʊsli / (BrE)
in a way that is near in position, time, or relationship; with little space or time between.
near in position, time, or relation

Examples

The two events were scheduled closely together to maximize attendance.
The houses on this street are built closely side by side.
She examined the evidence closely before drawing any conclusions.

Synset Examples

the onsets were closely timed
houses set closely together
was closely involved in monitoring daily progress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tightly - in a firm manner, with little or no looseness

Etymology

From 'close' + '-ly'. 'Close' from Old French 'clos', from Latin 'clausus' (shut, closed).
