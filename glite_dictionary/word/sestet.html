sestet

1. NOUN

/ sɛsˈtɛt / (AmE), / sɛsˈtɛt / (BrE)
A group of six lines of verse, especially forming a distinct unit in a poem or the last six lines of an Italian sonnet.
a group of six lines of verse

Examples

The Petrarchan sonnet concludes with a sestet that often resolves the initial theme.
She analyzed the structure of the poem, noting the importance of the sestet.
In his latest work, the sestet offered a poignant reflection on loss.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: octave - a group of eight lines of verse

Etymology

From Italian 'sestetto', based on 'sei' meaning 'six'.

2. NOUN

/ sɛsˈtɛt / (AmE), / sɛsˈtɛt / (BrE)
a group or set of six things, especially six lines of verse forming a unit in a poem
group of six; six-line stanza

Examples

The poem concludes with a sestet that contrasts with the preceding stanzas.
The choir performed a sestet, featuring six distinct voices in harmony.
He arranged the paintings in a sestet on the gallery wall.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: octave - group of eight, especially lines of verse in a poem

Etymology

From Italian 'sestetto', from 'sesto' (sixth), ultimately from Latin 'sex' (six).
