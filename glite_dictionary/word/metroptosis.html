metroptosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtrɑpˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmiːtrɒpˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a medical condition in which the uterus prolapses or descends from its normal position
prolapse of the uterus
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with metroptosis after a thorough examination.
Metroptosis can cause discomfort and may require medical intervention depending on its severity.
Advances in surgical procedures have improved outcomes for women suffering from metroptosis.

Etymology

From Greek 'mētra' (womb) + 'ptōsis' (falling).
