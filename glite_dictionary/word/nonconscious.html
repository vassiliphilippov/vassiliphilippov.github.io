nonconscious

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈkɑn.ʃəs / (AmE), / nɒnˈkɒn.ʃəs / (BrE)
not possessing or relating to consciousness; lacking awareness or the faculty of consciousness, especially as applies to inanimate objects or processes.
lacking consciousness or awareness

Examples

Many philosophers debate whether artificial intelligence can ever be more than nonconscious algorithms.
A rock is considered a nonconscious object, incapable of thinking or feeling.
The processes governing cell division in bacteria are entirely nonconscious and automatic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unconscious - not conscious; lacking awareness, often temporarily or in reference to mental processes

Etymology

Formed from 'non-' (not) + 'conscious' (aware).

2. ADJ

/ nɑnˈkɑnʃəs / (AmE), / nɒnˈkɒnʃəs / (BrE)
Relating to mental processes or functions that are not present in or accessible to conscious awareness.
not represented in consciousness

Examples

Some nonconscious biases may influence our decisions without us realizing it.
Researchers study the nonconscious mind to understand automatic behaviors.
Certain nonconscious processes occur in the brain without entering our awareness.

Synset Examples

nonconscious psychic processes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unconscious - not conscious; lacking awareness, especially mentally
  • Antonym: conscious - aware of and responding to one's surroundings; awake

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' (not) and 'conscious.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00575121-s
Synset POS: s
