rest-harrow

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛstˌhæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈrɛstˌharəʊ / (BrE)
a European woody plant (genus Ononis), with pink flowers, unifoliate leaves, and tough roots, often spreading by underground runners
a European woody plant with pink flowers

Examples

The field was covered with rest-harrow, making plowing difficult for the farmers.
The pink blossoms of rest-harrow appeared in the meadow every summer.
Due to its tough roots, rest-harrow is sometimes cultivated to prevent soil erosion.

Etymology

From the plant's tough roots, which could stop (rest) a harrow in the field. From rest (to stop) + harrow (agricultural implement).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛstˌhæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈrɛstˌharəʊ / (BrE)
A Eurasian plant with spiny stems, tough roots, and loose clusters of pink or purple flowers, belonging to the genus Ononis.
a spiny Eurasian plant with pink or purple flowers

Examples

The field was dotted with rest-harrow in full bloom, adding a splash of color to the landscape.
Farmers often complain about rest-harrow because its roots make ploughing difficult.
She identified the spiny stems and purple flowers as those of the rest-harrow.

Etymology

Named for its tough roots, which can 'arrest' the progress of a harrow while ploughing.
