Eamon de Valera

1. PROPN

/ ˈeɪmən də vəˈlɛrə / (AmE), / ˈeɪmən də vəˈlɛərə / (BrE)
An Irish statesman and political leader (1882–1975), president of the Irish Free State and later Taoiseach and President of Ireland, noted for his role in drafting the 1937 constitution that established Ireland as a sovereign nation.
Irish statesman and political leader

Examples

Eamon de Valera played a crucial role in the development of modern Irish statehood.
The 1937 Irish constitution was largely the work of Eamon de Valera.
Many historians debate the legacy of Eamon de Valera in twentieth-century Ireland.
