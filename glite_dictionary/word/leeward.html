leeward

1. ADV

/ ˈliːwɚd / (AmE), / ˈliːwəd / (BrE)
In the direction away from the wind.
away from the wind

Examples

The sailors moved leeward to find shelter from the storm.
As the weather shifted, we adjusted our course leeward.
He tied the boat leeward to keep it steady against the strong breeze.

Synset Examples

they were sailing leeward

Related Terms

  • Antonym: windward - in the direction from which the wind is blowing

Etymology

From lee (shelter from the wind) + ward (in the direction of).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00095742-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADJ

/ ˈliːwɚd / (AmE), / ˈliːwəd / (BrE)
Situated on or toward the side sheltered from the wind; facing away from the direction from which the wind is blowing.
on or toward the sheltered side from wind

Examples

They anchored the boat on the leeward side of the bay to escape the strong winds.
The campsite was set up in a leeward position for protection from the weather.
Vegetation grows more densely on the leeward side of the mountain.

Synset Examples

on the leeward side of the island

Related Terms

  • Antonym: windward - on or toward the side facing the wind

Etymology

From Middle English le-ward, from 'lee' (shelter) + 'ward' (direction).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01403102-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ ˈliːwərd, ˈluːərd / (AmE), / ˈliːwəd, ˈluːəd / (BrE)
the side or direction away from the wind; the sheltered side of something
side sheltered from the wind

Examples

The tents were pitched on the leeward side of the hill for better shelter.
They took cover on the leeward of the ship during the storm.
Plants tend to grow more on the leeward side of the dunes where the wind is blocked.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: windward - the side or direction from which the wind blows

Etymology

From Middle English le-ward, from Old English hleo (shelter) + -ward (direction towards).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08609908-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈliˌwɝd / (AmE), / ˈliːwəd / (BrE)
the direction toward which the wind is blowing
direction wind is blowing toward

Examples

The captain ordered the crew to steer toward the leeward so they could move with the wind.
We anchored on the leeward of the island for protection from the strong breeze.
Clouds gathered on the leeward side of the mountain, away from the prevailing wind.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: windward - the direction from which the wind is blowing

Etymology

From Middle Dutch 'lijwaarts', from 'lij' (shelter) + 'waarts' (-ward).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13852047-n
Synset POS: n
