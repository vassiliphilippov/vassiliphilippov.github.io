cumulative

1. ADJ

/ ˈkjuːmjəˌleɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈkjuːmjʊlətɪv / (BrE)
increasing or increased in quantity, degree, or force by successive additions
increasing by successive additions

Examples

The cumulative effect of stress can have serious consequences on health.
Interest is calculated on a cumulative basis, growing each year.
The damage was not immediate but rather the cumulative result of years of neglect.

Synset Examples

the benefits are cumulative
the eventual accumulative effect of these substances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accumulative - increasing by gradual accumulation
  • Antonym: diminishing - becoming smaller or less over time

Etymology

From Latin 'cumulativus', from 'cumulare' (to heap up).
