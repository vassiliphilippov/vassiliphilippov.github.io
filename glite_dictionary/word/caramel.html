caramel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærəˌmɛl, ˈkɑrməl / (AmE), / ˈkærəməl, ˈkærəmɛl / (BrE)
a firm, chewy candy made from caramelized sugar, butter, and milk
chewy candy made from caramelized sugar

Examples

She offered me a caramel after dinner.
The caramel stuck to my teeth as I chewed it slowly.
He bought a box of assorted caramels as a gift.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toffee - a candy made by boiling sugar and butter together

Etymology

From French caramel, from Spanish caramelo, possibly from Portuguese caramelo, of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkærəmɛl, ˈkɑːrməl / (AmE), / ˈkærəməl, ˈkɑːrəmɛl / (BrE)
A brown, sweet substance made by heating sugar until it melts and is used to color and flavor foods.
brown, sweet burnt sugar

Examples

She added a spoonful of caramel to her coffee for extra sweetness.
The apples were dipped in melted caramel at the fall festival.
The dessert was topped with a layer of smooth, golden caramel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toffee - a hard, chewy sweet made by boiling sugar and butter together

Etymology

From French caramel, from Spanish caramelo, of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07610751-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈkærəˌmɛl / (AmE), / ˈkærəməl / (BrE)
of a moderate yellow-brown color, resembling that of caramelized sugar.
moderate yellow-brown color

Examples

She wore a caramel sweater that complemented her skin tone perfectly.
The new sofa comes in a rich caramel shade, adding warmth to the room.
His eyes were a striking caramel color in the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tan - pale tone of brown, used for color
  • Synonym: beige - light yellowish-brown color
  • Degree Higher: chocolate - deep brown color, like chocolate

Etymology

From French *caramel*, from Spanish or Portuguese *caramelo*, ultimately from Late Latin *cannamellis* (cane honey).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

4. NOUN

/ ˈkær.ə.məl / (AmE), / ˈkær.ə.məl / (BrE)
a medium to dark tan color, resembling the shade of caramelized sugar
medium to dark tan color

Examples

The living room was painted a soft caramel that gave it a warm, inviting feel.
She wore a jacket in a rich caramel that perfectly complemented her complexion.
The designer chose caramel for the sofa upholstery to add a cozy touch to the space.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tan - a pale tone of brown
  • Degree Higher: brown - a color produced by mixing red, yellow, and blue; darker than caramel

Etymology

From French 'caramel', ultimately from Spanish 'caramelo', referring originally to melted or browned sugar.
