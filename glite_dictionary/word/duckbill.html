duckbill

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌkˌbɪl / (AmE), / ˈdʌkˌbɪl / (BrE)
a large, primitive freshwater fish of the Mississippi valley with a long, paddle-shaped snout, also called a paddlefish.
large freshwater fish with a paddle-shaped snout

Examples

The duckbill is easily recognized by its broad, flat snout resembling a paddle.
Fishermen in the Mississippi valley sometimes catch duckbills that weigh over fifty pounds.
Scientists study the duckbill to learn more about ancient species of freshwater fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paddlefish - a large freshwater fish with a long paddle-shaped snout

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʌkˌbɪl / (AmE), / ˈdʌkˌbɪl / (BrE)
(of an animal) having a beak or snout shaped like that of a duck.
having a duck-like beak or snout

Examples

The duckbill platypus uses its sensitive beak to detect prey underwater.
Scientists discovered a new species of duckbill dinosaur in North America.
The mask's front is shaped like a duckbill to allow easier breathing.

Synset Examples

a duck-billed dinosaur

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duck-billed - having a beak resembling that of a duck

Etymology

From 'duck' + 'bill', referring to the shape of a duck's beak.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdʌkˌbɪl / (AmE), / ˈdʌkˌbɪl / (BrE)
A small, aquatic, egg-laying mammal native to Australia and Tasmania, with dense fur, a broad bill, webbed feet, and a flat tail; also called a platypus.
aquatic mammal of Australia, platypus

Examples

The duckbill is one of the few mammals that lay eggs.
Children at the zoo were excited to see the duckbill swimming in its enclosure.
Researchers studied the unique features of the duckbill, particularly its bill and webbed feet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: platypus - aquatic egg-laying mammal native to Australia, also called duckbill

Etymology

Formed from 'duck' and 'bill', referring to its broad, duck-like bill.
