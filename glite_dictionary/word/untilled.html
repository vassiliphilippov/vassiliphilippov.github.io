untilled

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtɪld / (AmE), / ʌnˈtɪld / (BrE)
(of land) not plowed, harrowed, or cultivated for crops.
not plowed or cultivated (of land)

Examples

The farmer left several acres of untilled land at the edge of the field.
After years of neglect, the once-productive field stood untilled and overgrown.
Many wildflowers now grow in the untilled meadows near the river.

Synset Examples

untilled land

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tilled - land that has been plowed or cultivated

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'tilled' (past participle of 'till', meaning to prepare land for crops).
