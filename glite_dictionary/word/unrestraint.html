unrestraint

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.rɪˈstreɪnt / (AmE), / ˌʌn.rɪˈstreɪnt / (BrE)
a lack of restraint; the quality or state of being unrestrained, free from control or limitation
lack of restraint; freedom from control

Examples

Her unrestraint at the party surprised even her closest friends.
The artist’s unrestraint is evident in the bold colors and energetic brushstrokes.
Some critics argue that too much unrestraint can lead to chaos in society.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: restraint - the action or quality of holding something back or keeping it under control
  • Synonym: freedom - the state of being free rather than in confinement or under physical restraint

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'restraint' (from Old French restreinte, from Latin restringere, to bind back).
