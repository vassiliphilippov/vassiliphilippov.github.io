Pasch

1. NOUN

/ pæsk / (AmE), / pɑːsk / (BrE)
The Jewish festival of Passover, commemorating the liberation of the Israelites from Egyptian slavery.
Jewish festival of Passover

Examples

Every spring, the family gathers to celebrate Pasch according to tradition.
The story of the Exodus is retold during Pasch in Jewish households worldwide.
Special foods are prepared for the occasion of Pasch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Passover - The Jewish festival commemorating the Exodus from Egypt

Etymology

From Latin *pascha*, from Hebrew *pesach* (Passover).

2. NOUN

/ pæsk / (AmE), / pɑːsk / (BrE)
A Christian festival celebrating the resurrection of Jesus Christ, also known as Easter; sometimes used for Passover in older usage.
Christian festival of Easter
archaic

Examples

The celebration of the Pasch was an important event in the medieval church calendar.
Many hymns sung at Pasch reflect the theme of resurrection and renewal.
He wrote a scholarly article tracing the history of the Pasch in European tradition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Easter - the principal Christian festival celebrating the resurrection of Jesus Christ

Etymology

From Latin 'pascha', from Greek 'pascha', from Hebrew 'pesach' (Passover).
