Tagore

1. PROPN

/ təˈɡɔr / (AmE), / təˈɡɔː / (BrE)
Rabindranath Tagore (1861–1941), Indian writer, poet, and philosopher who pioneered the use of colloquial Bengali in poetry and was awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1913.
Indian poet and philosopher (1861–1941)

Examples

Tagore was the first non-European to win the Nobel Prize in Literature.
Many of Tagore's poems explore the relationship between humanity and nature.
The works of Tagore continue to influence writers around the world.
