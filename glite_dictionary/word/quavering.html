quavering

1. ADJ

/ ˈkweɪvɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkweɪvərɪŋ / (BrE)
(of the voice) quivering or trembling, typically as a result of weakness, fear, or emotion.
trembling or shaky (voice)

Examples

Grandfather spoke in a quavering voice as he told the story.
She sang the final note with a quavering tremor due to nerves.
His quavering reply revealed just how frightened he was.

Synset Examples

the old lady's quavering voice
spoke timidly in a tremulous voice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tremulous - shaking or quivering slightly, especially from nervousness or weakness
  • Antonym: steady - not shaking or trembling; firm; used of voices or hands

Etymology

From the verb 'quaver,' which comes from Middle English 'quaven' (to tremble), possibly related to Old Norse 'kveifa' (to tremble).
