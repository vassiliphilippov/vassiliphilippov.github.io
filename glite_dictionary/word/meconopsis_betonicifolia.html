Meconopsis betonicifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛkəˈnɑpsɪs ˌbɛtənɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˌmɛkəˈnɒpsɪs ˌbɛtənɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to China, known for its drooping cymes of mauve-pink to bright sky blue flowers; commonly called the Himalayan blue poppy.
Chinese perennial plant with blue flowers

Examples

Gardeners prize the Meconopsis betonicifolia for its stunning blue flowers and delicate form.
The Meconopsis betonicifolia blooms in late spring, adding vibrant color to the shade garden.
Many visitors were captivated by the display of Meconopsis betonicifolia at the botanical garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Himalayan blue poppy - Common name of Meconopsis betonicifolia

Etymology

From modern Latin: 'Meconopsis' (from Greek mēkōn, poppy, and ópsis, appearance), and 'betonicifolia' (from Latin referring to leaves resembling those of Betonica).
