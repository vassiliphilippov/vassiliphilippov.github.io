armourer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrmərər / (AmE), / ˈɑːmərə / (BrE)
a person or company that manufactures or repairs firearms and other weapons
manufacturer or repairer of firearms

Examples

The respected armourer crafted custom rifles for the royal court.
During the war, the armourer worked tirelessly to keep the soldiers' weapons in top condition.
She trained for years to become a skilled armourer in her family's factory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gunsmith - someone who makes or repairs guns

Etymology

From Old French *armurier*, from *arme* (weapon); related to 'armor'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrmərər / (AmE), / ˈɑːmərə / (BrE)
a person skilled in making or repairing armor or weapons
maker of armor or weapons

Examples

The armourer carefully repaired the knight's damaged breastplate before the next battle.
In medieval times, every castle employed an armourer to maintain its weapons and armor.
The famous English armourer was commissioned to forge a new sword for the king.

Synset Examples

a sword made by a famous English armorer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armorer - American spelling of 'armourer'

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman armurer, from Old French armurier, from arme (weapon, armor).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrmərər / (AmE), / ˈɑːmərə / (BrE)
an enlisted person responsible for the maintenance and repair of small arms and machine guns, especially in the military
military weapons maintainer

Examples

The armourer inspected each rifle before the soldiers went on patrol.
Our battalion's armourer fixed my machine gun after it jammed during training.
Every weapon must be checked regularly by the armourer to ensure safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gunsmith - a person who makes or repairs firearms

Etymology

From Old French 'armurier', from 'arme' (weapon), ultimately from Latin 'arma' (arms, weapons).
