gelly

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛli / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛli / (BrE)
A type of dynamite in which nitroglycerin is absorbed in a base of wood pulp and sodium or potassium nitrate.
a type of dynamite

Examples

Miners used gelly to blast through the solid rock.
The construction crew was careful when handling boxes of gelly.
Early forms of gelly were considered safer than pure nitroglycerin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gelignite - a blasting explosive consisting of a mixture of nitroglycerin and other materials

Etymology

From 'gelignite', itself from 'gelatin' (referring to its jelly-like texture) and 'ignite'.
