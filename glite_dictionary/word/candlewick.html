candlewick

1. NOUN

/ ˈkændlˌwɪk / (AmE), / ˈkændlˌwɪk / (BrE)
A soft tufted cord or yarn, often forming patterns on fabric, especially used in bedspreads or textiles.
soft tufted yarn or fabric pattern

Examples

She inherited a beautiful candlewick bedspread from her grandmother.
The detailed candlewick pattern added texture to the quilt.
Old-fashioned candlewick was popular in linens during the early twentieth century.

Etymology

From 'candle' + 'wick', originally referring to the resemblance of the yarn to the wick used in candles.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkændlˌwɪk / (AmE), / ˈkændlˌwɪk / (BrE)
the cord or strand that forms the core of a candle, which is lit to produce a flame
the wick inside a candle

Examples

She carefully trimmed the candlewick before lighting the candle.
The old candlewick burned down quickly and needed to be replaced.
Wax had hardened around the candlewick, making it difficult to relight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wick - the cord or strand inside a candle or oil lamp that draws up fuel for burning

Etymology

Formed from 'candle' and 'wick'.
