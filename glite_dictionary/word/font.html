font

1. NOUN

/ fɑnt / (AmE), / fɒnt / (BrE)
a set of printed or digital type of a particular size and design used for letters and numbers
typeface in a specific size or style

Examples

She chose a bold font for the headline to grab attention.
This document looks better when printed in a serif font.
You can change the size and style of the font in the settings menu.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typeface - the design of lettering that includes variations in size, weight, slope, etc.

Etymology

From Old French *fonte* (something that has been melted), from Latin *fundere* (to melt, cast), originally referring to casting metal type.

2. NOUN

/ fɑnt / (AmE), / fɒnt / (BrE)
a large bowl, typically of stone, in a church, used for holding the water for baptisms
bowl for baptismal water in a church

Examples

The baby was christened at the font in the village church.
The ornate marble font dates back to the twelfth century.
Visitors admired the ancient font carved with religious symbols.

Etymology

From Old French fonte (fount, fountain), from Latin fons, fontis (spring, fountain).
