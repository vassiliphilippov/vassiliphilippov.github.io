celtuce

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛl.tus / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.tjuːs / (BrE)
A lettuce variety cultivated for its thick, edible stem and tender leaves, eaten raw or cooked.
lettuce with edible stem and leaves

Examples

The stir-fry featured sliced celtuce stems mixed with garlic and chili.
She bought a fresh bunch of celtuce from the Asian market for tonight's salad.
Raw celtuce adds a pleasant crunch to sandwiches and spring rolls.

Etymology

Blend of 'celery' and 'lettuce'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛl.tus / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.tjuːs / (BrE)
a variety of lettuce grown for its thick, edible stem, used as a vegetable in cooking
a type of lettuce with edible stems

Examples

Stir-frying celtuce brings out its mild, nutty flavor and crisp texture.
At the market, I found fresh celtuce alongside other Asian vegetables.
After peeling, the crunchy stem of celtuce can be eaten raw in salads.

Etymology

Blend of 'celery' and 'lettuce'.
