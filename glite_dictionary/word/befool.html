to befool

1. VERB

/ bɪˈfuːl / (AmE), / bɪˈfuːl / (BrE)
to make a fool of someone; to deceive or dupe someone into appearing foolish
to make a fool of; to deceive

Examples

She tried to befool her classmates by faking her illness.
It is unwise to befool others for personal gain.
He realized too late that he had been befooled by a clever scam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deceive - to cause someone to believe something that is not true
  • Synonym: dupe - to trick or cheat someone

Etymology

From be- (intensive prefix) + fool, modeled after similar English formations.

2. VERB

/ bɪˈfuːl / (AmE), / bɪˈfuːl / (BrE)
to deceive or trick someone; to make a fool of someone
to deceive or make a fool of
archaic

Examples

Do not let false promises befool you into making bad decisions.
The clever swindler befooled many people with his elaborate scheme.
She warned her friends not to be befooled by the persuasive advertisements.

Synset Examples

The immigrant was duped because he trusted everyone
You can't fool me!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deceive - to cause someone to believe something that is not true, typically to gain some personal advantage
  • Synonym: trick - to deceive or cheat someone
  • Antonym: to enlighten - to give someone greater knowledge or understanding about a subject or situation

Etymology

Formed by prefix be- + fool, from Middle English.
