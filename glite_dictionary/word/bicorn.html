bicorn

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪkɔrn / (AmE), / ˈbaɪkɔːn / (BrE)
a hat with the brim turned up to form two points, worn especially in the 18th and early 19th centuries
two-cornered cocked hat

Examples

The general wore a distinctive bicorn adorned with gold trim during the ceremony.
In historical paintings, Napoleon is often depicted wearing his iconic bicorn.
Collectors of military memorabilia value an authentic bicorn from the Napoleonic era.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tricorne - a hat with three points, similar style to the bicorn

Etymology

From Latin 'bicornis', from 'bi-' (two) + 'cornu' (horn).

2. ADJ

/ ˈbaɪ.kɔrn / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.kɔːn / (BrE)
having two horns or horn-shaped parts
having two horns

Examples

The animal had a distinctive bicorn skull structure.
A bicorn uterus is a congenital uterine anomaly with two horns.
The statue depicts a mythical bicorn creature with two prominent horns.

Synset Examples

a bicornuate uterus

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unicorne - having one horn or horn-shaped part
  • Degree Higher: tricorn - having three horns or horn-shaped parts

Etymology

From Latin 'bicornis', from 'bi-' meaning 'two' and 'cornu' meaning 'horn'.

Synset POS: s
