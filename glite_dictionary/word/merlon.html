merlon

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜr.lən / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.lɒn / (BrE)
A solid section of a battlement between two openings (crenels) in a crenelated parapet or wall, typically found in fortifications.
solid part between crenels in a battlement

Examples

The soldiers took cover behind each merlon as arrows flew overhead.
The castle wall featured alternating merlons and crenels along its battlements.
During restoration, one damaged merlon was carefully rebuilt to match the original design.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: crenel - an opening or gap between merlons in a battlement

Etymology

From French 'merlon,' of uncertain origin, possibly from Italian 'merlone.'
