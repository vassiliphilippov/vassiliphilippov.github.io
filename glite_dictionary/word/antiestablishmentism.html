antiestablishmentism

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ti.ɪˌstæb.lɪʃ.mənˌtɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.ɪˌstæb.lɪʃ.mənˌtɪz.əm / (BrE)
The doctrine or belief in opposing the established social or political order or institutions.
opposition to the established order

Examples

The rise of antiestablishmentism reflected growing discontent with the government's policies.
His views were shaped by a strong sense of antiestablishmentism during his youth.
Antiestablishmentism often appeals to those dissatisfied with mainstream institutions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiestablishment - opposed to the established social or political system

Etymology

From anti- (against) + establishment (the ruling social or political order) + -ism (doctrine or belief).
