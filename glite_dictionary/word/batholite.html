batholite

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæθəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈbæθəlʌɪt / (BrE)
A large mass of intrusive igneous rock that solidified deep within the earth's crust.
large mass of deep igneous rock

Examples

The Sierra Nevada is known for its extensive batholite formations.
Geologists study batholites to understand the history of Earth's crust.
A massive batholite lies beneath the mountain range, shaping the region's geology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: batholith - a large mass of intrusive igneous rock, synonymous with batholite

Etymology

From Greek *bathos* (depth) and *lithos* (stone).
