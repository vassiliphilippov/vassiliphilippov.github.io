mooring

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʊrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːrɪŋ / (BrE)
a place or structure where a boat or ship can be fastened to remain stationary
place for mooring a boat or ship

Examples

We found an empty mooring at the marina for our sailboat.
The yacht stayed at its mooring during the storm.
He arranged for a mooring near the city centre.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: berth - a place in a harbour where a ship can be moored

Etymology

From the verb 'moor', from Middle Low German 'mōren' (to moor, fasten a ship), probably ultimately of Scandinavian origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʊrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːrɪŋ / (BrE)
a rope, chain, or device used to hold a boat or ship in place
device or line that keeps a boat in place

Examples

The boat was secured to the dock with a sturdy mooring.
After the storm, we checked every mooring to ensure the boats hadn't drifted.
He untied the mooring and gently pushed the canoe away from the pier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anchor - a device dropped to the bottom to prevent a vessel from moving

Etymology

From the verb 'moor', ultimately from Middle Low German 'mōren' (to moor, anchor a ship).
