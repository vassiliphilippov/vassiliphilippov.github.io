totalistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌtoʊ.t̬əˈlɪs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌtəʊ.təˈlɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of totalitarian principles, especially where the state seeks to control every aspect of life.
relating to totalitarian control

Examples

Many critics argue that the regime has become increasingly totalistic in its approach to governance.
The cult employed totalistic practices to maintain absolute control over its members.
Scholars study societies with totalistic systems to understand the effects of extreme state power.

Synset Examples

totalitarian theory and practice
operating in a totalistic fashion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: totalitarian - relating to a centralized government that does not tolerate differing opinions and exercises dictatorial control
  • Antonym: democratic - relating to a system of government by the whole population, typically through elected representatives

Etymology

From 'total' + '-istic', patterned after 'totalitarian'.
