dimorphous

1. ADJ

/ daɪˈmɔːrfəs / (AmE), / daɪˈmɔː.fəs / (BrE)
occurring or existing in two distinct forms, especially in biology or mineralogy.
existing in two forms

Examples

Some species of butterflies are dimorphous, showing two distinct color patterns among adults.
The mineral exhibited dimorphous crystal structures under different conditions.
Scientists observed that the fungus has a dimorphous growth cycle.

Synset Examples

dimorphic crystals
dimorphous organisms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dimorphic - occurring in two forms

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' (two) + 'morphē' (form).
