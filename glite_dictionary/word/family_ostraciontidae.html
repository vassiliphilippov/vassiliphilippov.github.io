family Ostraciontidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmɪli ɒˌstræsiˈɒntaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ɒˌstræsiˈɒntɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of box-shaped marine fishes known as boxfishes, typically characterized by a hard, bony carapace covering most of the body.
a family of box-shaped marine fishes

Examples

Many aquariums feature species from the family Ostraciontidae because of their unique box-shaped bodies.
Scientists studied the defensive adaptations of fishes in the family Ostraciontidae.
The family Ostraciontidae is found in tropical and subtropical marine waters worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boxfish - a species of fish with a box-shaped body, belonging to the family Ostraciontidae

Etymology

From scientific Latin; Ostraciontidae, based on the genus Ostracion + -idae (suffix used in zoological family names).
