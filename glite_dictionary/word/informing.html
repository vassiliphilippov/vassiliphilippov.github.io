informing

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɔrmɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɔːmɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of conveying information or making something known to others
the act of giving information

Examples

The informing of employees about the new policy was handled by email.
Accurate informing of the public plays a critical role in public health campaigns.
She appreciated his skillful informing of the team during the crisis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notification - the act of notifying or informing; giving notice

Etymology

Gerund of 'inform', from Latin 'informare' (to shape, form, instruct).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɔrmɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɔːmɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of providing incriminating evidence to law enforcement, typically in exchange for some benefit or leniency
giving evidence to the police for favors

Examples

The gang leader punished him for informing on his associates to the police.
She considered informing as a betrayal, even if it meant going to prison herself.
Rumors spread that there had been informing among the members to reduce their sentences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snitching - acting as an informer, especially to the police
