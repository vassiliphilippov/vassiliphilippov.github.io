ground-breaking

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡraʊndˌbreɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndˌbreɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
(1) The ceremonial act of breaking ground to begin a construction project. (2) Introducing new ideas or methods; innovative or pioneering.
Innovative or ceremonial start of construction

Examples

The architect received an award for her ground-breaking designs.
The mayor attended the ground-breaking for the new library.
His ground-breaking discovery changed the way scientists viewed the problem.

Synset Examples

They celebrated the ground-breaking of the new community center.
Her research was ground-breaking in the field of genetics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innovative - introducing or using new ideas or methods
  • Synonym: pioneering - being the first to use or apply a new method or activity
  • Antonym: conservative - favoring traditional views and values; not innovative
