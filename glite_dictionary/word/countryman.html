countryman

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌntrimæn / (AmE), / ˈkʌntrɪmən / (BrE)
a man from the same country as oneself
man from the same country

Examples

He was delighted to meet a fellow countryman so far from home.
At the event, I recognized a countryman among the crowd.
During the interview, she was asked how she supported her countryman abroad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compatriot - a fellow countryman; someone from the same country

Etymology

From Middle English 'contriman', from country + man.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌn.tri.mæn / (AmE), / ˈkʌn.tri.mən / (BrE)
a man who lives in the countryside, especially one with traditional rural habits or ways
man living in the country

Examples

The countryman greeted us warmly as we walked past his farmhouse.
As a lifelong countryman, he preferred fresh air and open fields to city crowds.
The old countryman knew every trail and stream in the valley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: countrywoman - a woman who lives in the country and has country ways

Etymology

From Middle English 'contreman', from Old English 'cūntrēman', meaning 'man of the country'.
