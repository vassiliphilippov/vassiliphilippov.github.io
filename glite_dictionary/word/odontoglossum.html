Odontoglossum

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊdɑːntəˈɡlɑːsəm / (AmE), / ˌəʊdɒntəˈɡlɒsəm / (BrE)
a genus of tropical American orchids, mostly epiphytic, known for their attractive flowers; widely cultivated, with some species now placed in other genera
genus of tropical American orchids

Examples

Many orchid enthusiasts grow Odontoglossum species for their showy and colorful blooms.
Recent studies have reclassified several Odontoglossum species into other genera.
The delicate fragrance of the Odontoglossum filled the greenhouse.

Etymology

From Greek 'odous' (tooth) + 'glōssa' (tongue), referring to the shape of the flower's lip.
