spate

1. NOUN

/ speɪt / (AmE), / speɪt / (BrE)
a sudden and strong outpouring or flow, especially of water or events
sudden and forceful outpouring

Examples

After weeks of dry weather, a spate of heavy rain caused the river to overflow.
The company experienced a spate of new orders following its advertising campaign.
Recently, there has been a spate of burglaries in the neighborhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flood - an overflowing of a large amount of water beyond normal limits

Etymology

Probably from Old French 'espaté' (flood, outpouring).

2. NOUN

/ speɪt / (AmE), / speɪt / (BrE)
a large number of similar things or events occurring in quick succession, especially unpleasant ones
a large number occurring quickly

Examples

After the election, there was a spate of resignations across the company.
The city has experienced a spate of burglaries in recent weeks.
A spate of new restaurants has opened downtown this month.

3. NOUN

/ speɪt / (AmE), / speɪt / (BrE)
A sudden flood or rush of water, especially after heavy rain or melting snow.
sudden flood or rush of water

Examples

After the storm, the river was in spate and overflowed its banks.
Spring often brings a spate caused by melting mountain snow.
Heavy rains sent a spate of water rushing through the valley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flood - an overflow of a large amount of water beyond normal limits
  • Antonym: drought - a prolonged period of abnormally low rainfall

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse spjót (flood, surge) or of uncertain origin, first recorded in Scots.
