high-tech

1. ADJ

/ haɪ tɛk / (AmE), / haɪ tɛk / (BrE)
using, involving, or resembling the most advanced and up-to-date technology.
involving advanced technology

Examples

The hospital is equipped with high-tech medical devices that improve patient care.
She prefers a high-tech kitchen with smart appliances and automated systems.
Many start-ups focus on creating high-tech solutions for common problems.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: low-tech - using simple or old-fashioned technology

Etymology

Shortened form of 'high technology', first used in the late 20th century.

2. ADJ

/ haɪˈtɛk / (AmE), / haɪˈtɛk / (BrE)
using or involving advanced technological development, especially in electronics or computing.
involving advanced technology

Examples

The new hospital has a high-tech security system installed at every entrance.
He works for a high-tech startup specializing in artificial intelligence and robotics.
These days, even kitchens are filled with high-tech gadgets that make cooking easier.

Synset Examples

The lab invested in a high-tech science oven for research.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: low-tech - using simple or traditional technology
  • Synonym: cutting-edge - at the most advanced stage of development

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'high technology', first used in the late 20th century.
