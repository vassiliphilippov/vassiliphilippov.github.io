environmental

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˌvaɪrənˈmɛntəl / (AmE), / ɪnˌvaɪərənˈmɛntl / (BrE)
relating to or concerned with the protection of the natural world and the impact of human activity on it
relating to ecology/the environment

Examples

The company is committed to using environmental practices to reduce waste and conserve energy.
She studied the environmental impact of the new highway on local wildlife.
Many laws now require environmental assessments before major construction projects.

Synset Examples

environmental pollution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecological - relating to ecology and the environment

Etymology

From 'environment' (French, from environner 'to surround') + -al.

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˌvaɪrənˈmɛntəl / (AmE), / ɪnˌvʌɪrənˈmɛntəl / (BrE)
Relating to the external conditions or surroundings, especially as they affect people, animals, or plants.
relating to surroundings or conditions

Examples

The study focused on environmental factors influencing childhood development.
Many companies are trying to find environmental solutions to reduce pollution.
New laws have been enacted to address environmental concerns in the city.

Synset Examples

environmental factors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecological - concerned with the relation of living organisms to their environment

Etymology

From 'environment' + '-al'; 'environment' from French 'environner' (to surround).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02954640-a
Synset POS: a
