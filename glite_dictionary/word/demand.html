demand

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈmænd / (AmE), / dɪˈmɑːnd / (BrE)
a forceful or urgent request for something, especially one that must be met
urgent or forceful request

Examples

The workers presented a demand for higher wages to management.
Her demand that the meeting be postponed surprised everyone in the room.
Parents often face the demand of balancing work and family responsibilities.

Synset Examples

his demands for attention were unceasing

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: request - an act of asking for something politely or formally
  • Degree Higher: command - an authoritative order

Etymology

From Old French *demander* (to request), from Latin *demandare* (to entrust, demand).
