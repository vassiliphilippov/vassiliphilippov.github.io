pyrolysis

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈrɑːləsɪs / (AmE), / paɪˈrɒlɪsɪs / (BrE)
The chemical decomposition of a material or substance caused by exposure to high temperatures.
decomposition of a substance by heat

Examples

Much of the charcoal used in industry is produced by pyrolysis of wood.
During pyrolysis, organic material is broken down into simpler compounds by heat.
Engineers study pyrolysis to develop new methods for recycling plastics.

Etymology

From Greek 'pyr' (fire) + 'lysis' (loosening, decomposition).
