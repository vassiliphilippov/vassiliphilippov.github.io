coldness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊld.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkəʊld.nəs / (BrE)
an absence of warmth or friendliness in manner, showing a lack of affection or enthusiasm
lack of warmth or affection

Examples

Her coldness towards her colleagues was obvious at the meeting.
He was surprised by the coldness in her reply to his invitation.
There was a noticeable coldness between the two old friends.

Synset Examples

a distressing coldness of tone and manner

Related Terms

  • Antonym: warmth - friendliness and affection in manner
  • Synonym: aloofness - a state of being emotionally distant or reserved

Etymology

From Old English 'ceald', meaning 'cold', with -ness forming a noun.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊld.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkəʊld.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or sensation of having a low temperature; the state of being cold.
the sensation of low temperature

Examples

The coldness of the room made it hard to sleep without a blanket.
She could feel the coldness in the air as winter approached.
His hands were numb from the coldness outside.

Synset Examples

he shivered from the cold
the cold helped clear his head

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chilliness - the state or sensation of being slightly cold
  • Antonym: warmth - the quality or sensation of being warm
  • Degree Lower: coolness - moderate coldness, less intense than coldness

Etymology

From Old English 'cealdnes', formed from 'cold' + '-ness' (state, quality).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊld.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkəʊld.nəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being cold; the absence or low presence of heat
the state of being cold

Examples

The coldness of the water shocked him as he jumped into the lake.
A sudden wave of coldness filled the room when the window was opened.
The coldness outside makes it necessary to wear a heavy coat.

Synset Examples

the coldness made our breath visible
come in out of the cold
cold is a vasoconstrictor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chill - a moderate but perceptible degree of coldness
  • Antonym: heat - a state of being hot; high temperature
  • Degree Lower: coolness - a moderately low temperature; less cold than coldness

Etymology

From Old English 'ceald' (cold) + '-ness' (state, quality).
