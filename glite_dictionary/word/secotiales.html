Secotiales

1. NOUN

/ ˌsiːkoʊˈtaɪəˌliːz / (AmE), / ˌsiːkəʊˈtaɪəliːz / (BrE)
An order of fungi in the class Gasteromycetes, characterized by their enclosed spore-bearing structures.
order of fungi in Gasteromycetes

Examples

The order Secotiales includes fungi with unique enclosed fruiting bodies.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Secotiales in Australia.
The taxonomy of Secotiales has been subject to revision with advances in molecular techniques.

Etymology

From the genus Secotium + Latin suffix -ales (denoting orders in taxonomy).
