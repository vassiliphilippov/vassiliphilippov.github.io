to know

1. VERB

/ noʊ / (AmE), / nəʊ / (BrE)
to be familiar or acquainted with (a person, place, or thing) through experience, association, or information
be familiar or acquainted with

Examples

I know most of the people in my neighborhood.
Have you ever known anyone who climbed Mount Everest?
She doesn't know this song, so she can't sing along.

Synset Examples

I know him under a different name
This flower is known as a Peruvian Lily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recognize - to identify someone or something seen before
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to; refuse to acknowledge

Etymology

From Old English *cnāwan* (to know, perceive), ultimately from Proto-Germanic *knewaną*.
