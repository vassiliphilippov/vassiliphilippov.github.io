bellflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛlˌflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɛlˌflaʊə / (BrE)
any of several plants of the genus Campanula, typically with blue or white bell-shaped flowers
plant with blue or white bell-shaped flowers

Examples

The garden was filled with bellflowers that bloomed throughout the summer.
She planted a variety of bellflower to attract bees and butterflies to her yard.
The delicate blue bellflower stood out among the green foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Campanula - the genus of plants including bellflowers

Etymology

From 'bell' referring to the flower's shape and 'flower'.
