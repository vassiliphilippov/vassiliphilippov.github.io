ready-ish

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛdi ɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈrɛdi ɪʃ / (BrE)
Somewhat ready; almost but not fully prepared.
somewhat ready or prepared
informal

Examples

I'm feeling ready-ish, but I could use a few more minutes to finish up.
We left for the airport when we were only ready-ish, so we forgot a few things.
Are you ready-ish for your presentation, or do you need more time?

Synset Examples

He said he was ready, but it was more ready-ish.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ready - fully prepared or available for use

Etymology

Formed by adding the suffix '-ish' to 'ready', following a common colloquial pattern meaning 'somewhat'.
