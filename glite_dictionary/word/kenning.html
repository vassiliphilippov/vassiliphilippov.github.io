kenning

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɛnɪŋ / (BrE)
a conventional metaphoric expression used in Old English and Old Norse poetry, typically replacing a simple noun with a compound phrase such as 'whale-road' for 'sea'
metaphoric compound in early Germanic poetry

Examples

The poet used a kenning to refer to the sea as the 'whale-road'.
Students analyzed several kennings found in Beowulf for their creative imagery.
In Old Norse poetry, a kenning often enriches the language with vivid metaphors.

Etymology

From Old Norse 'kenna' (to know, to name by a characteristic), related to Old English 'cennan'.
