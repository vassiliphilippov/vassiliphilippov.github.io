liberally

1. ADV

/ ˈlɪbərəli / (AmE), / ˈlɪb(ə)rəli / (BrE)
In a generous or abundant manner; giving or done without restraint.
generously; abundantly

Examples

She always donated liberally to causes she believed in.
He seasoned the soup liberally with herbs and spices.
The teacher praised students liberally for their efforts.

Synset Examples

he gave liberally to several charities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generously - in a generous manner
  • Antonym: stingily - in a mean or miserly manner

Etymology

From Latin 'liberalis' (of freedom, generous).

2. ADV

/ ˈlɪb.ɚ.ə.li / (AmE), / ˈlɪb.ər.ə.li / (BrE)
In a way that is free and not literal, often involving exaggeration or embellishment.
freely or nonliterally; with exaggeration

Examples

He used metaphors liberally when describing his childhood adventures.
The novelist liberally embellished the facts to make the story more exciting.
She quoted Shakespeare liberally, sometimes twisting the lines for dramatic effect.

Synset Examples

he embellished his stories liberally

Related Terms

  • Synonym: figuratively - in a metaphorical or nonliteral sense
  • Antonym: literally - in a strict, literal sense

Etymology

From Latin 'liberalis' (of freedom), via Old French 'liberal'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00198286-r
Synset POS: r
