concision

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / kənˈsɪʒən / (BrE)
The quality of expressing much in few words; terseness and brevity in speech or writing.
brevity or terseness in expression

Examples

The effectiveness of his argument relied on the concision of his statements.
Writers are often praised for their concision and clarity.
Good editors value concision in news reporting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brevity - shortness and conciseness, especially in writing or speech
  • Antonym: verbosity - using more words than needed; wordiness

Etymology

From Latin 'concisionem' (a cutting up), from 'concidere' (to cut up).
