Mahabharatam

1. PROPN

/ məˌhɑːbəˈrɑːtəm / (AmE), / məˌhɑːbəˈrɑːtəm / (BrE)
a sacred ancient Sanskrit epic of India, recounting in many episodes the struggle between two rival families, the Pandavas and the Kauravas
ancient Hindu Sanskrit epic poem

Examples

Many of life's dilemmas are vividly portrayed in the Mahabharatam.
The Mahabharatam is one of India's greatest literary treasures.
He spent years studying the verses of the Mahabharatam in their original Sanskrit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mahabharata - The more common English name for the epic Sanskrit poem

Etymology

From Sanskrit महाभारतम् (Mahābhāratam), literally 'Great Bharata' (Bharata being a legendary king and ancestor).
