ruddles

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌdəlz / (AmE), / ˈrʌdəlz / (BrE)
A common European annual marigold (Calendula officinalis), known for its bright yellow or orange flowers.
common European annual marigold

Examples

The garden was filled with ruddles blooming brightly in the summer sun.
She planted ruddles along the path to add a touch of golden color.
Bees are often attracted to ruddles because of their vibrant flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marigold - a plant with bright yellow or orange flowers
