statistician

1. NOUN

/ stəˌtɪsˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / stəˌtɪsˈtɪʃən / (BrE)
A person who is an expert in the collection, analysis, and interpretation of numerical data, especially in statistics.
expert in statistics

Examples

The company's lead statistician designed a new model to predict customer trends.
As a statistician, she plays an important role in clinical research studies.
The government hired a statistician to analyze census data for the upcoming report.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mathematician - a person skilled in mathematics

Etymology

From statistic + -ian, modeled after mathematician.

2. NOUN

/ stəˌtɪsˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / stəˌtɪsˈtɪʃən / (BrE)
a person who is skilled in the collection, analysis, interpretation, and presentation of numerical data
expert in statistics

Examples

The company's chief statistician developed a new model for predicting sales trends.
After analyzing the data, the statistician presented her findings to the research team.
Many sports teams employ a statistician to evaluate player performance and develop strategies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mathematician - a person skilled or expert in mathematics
  • Synonym: statistical analyst - a person who analyzes and interprets statistics

Etymology

From statistics + -ian (suffix denoting a person skilled in a subject); coined in the 19th century.
