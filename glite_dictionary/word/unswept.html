unswept

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈswɛpt / (AmE), / ʌnˈswɛpt / (BrE)
not cleaned by sweeping; not having been swept clean of dirt or debris
not having been swept clean

Examples

The kitchen floor was still unswept after the party.
She complained about the unswept hallway in the apartment building.
Several leaves accumulated in the unswept corner of the porch.

Synset Examples

unswept floors

Related Terms

  • Antonym: swept - made clean by sweeping

Etymology

formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'swept' (past participle of 'sweep')

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00425300-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈswɛpt / (AmE), / ʌnˈswɛpt / (BrE)
not swept; not having a backward slant or angle (especially describing aircraft wings)
not swept; lacking an angled form

Examples

The living room remained unswept after the children played inside all day.
The museum displayed a World War II fighter with unswept wings.
Dust collected in the corners of the unswept hallway.

Synset Examples

a boxy little plane with square unswept wings

Related Terms

  • Antonym: swept - having a backward slant or angle (for wings); cleaned by sweeping

Etymology

From un- (not) + swept, past participle of 'sweep'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02379783-a
Synset POS: a
