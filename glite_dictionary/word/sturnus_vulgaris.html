Sturnus vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ stɝː.nəs vʌlˈɡɛər.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈstɜː.nəs vʌlˈɡɑː.rɪs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the common starling, a widely distributed, gregarious bird with glossy dark plumage, known for nesting near human dwellings.
the common starling (bird, scientific name)

Examples

The scientific name of the common starling is Sturnus vulgaris.
Researchers observed Sturnus vulgaris nesting in close proximity to urban buildings.
Flocks of Sturnus vulgaris are often seen performing impressive aerial displays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starling - a small to medium-sized passerine bird of the family Sturnidae

Etymology

From Latin: 'sturnus' meaning starling (the bird), 'vulgaris' meaning common.
