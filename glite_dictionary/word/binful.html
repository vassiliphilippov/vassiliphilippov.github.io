binful

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪnfəl / (AmE), / ˈbɪnfʊl / (BrE)
An amount of something that fills a bin.
amount that fills a bin

Examples

He emptied a binful of apples onto the conveyor belt.
After harvest, there was almost a binful of corn left in the shed.
They donated several binfuls of clothes to charity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handful - the quantity that fills a hand
  • Synonym: bucketful - the amount that fills a bucket

Etymology

Formed from 'bin' and '-ful' (denoting as much as fills).
