to philosophize

1. VERB

/ fəˈlɑːsəˌfaɪz / (AmE), / fəˈlɒsəˌfaɪz / (BrE)
to speculate or reason about fundamental or abstract matters in a philosophical way
reason in a philosophical manner

Examples

He loved to philosophize about the meaning of life over a cup of coffee.
Rather than act, she would often philosophize about every possible outcome.
In their late-night talks, the friends would philosophize until dawn about happiness and truth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reflect - to think deeply or carefully about something
  • Synonym: muse - to think about something in a thoughtful way

Etymology

From French 'philosophiser', from Greek 'philosophos' (philosopher).
