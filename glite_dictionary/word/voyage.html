voyage

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɔɪɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvɔɪɪdʒ / (BrE)
a long journey by sea or through water, especially in a ship
a long journey by sea

Examples

The first voyage across the Atlantic was a remarkable achievement.
During his voyage, he encountered many storms and rough seas.
The captain prepared the crew for a long voyage to distant lands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: journey - an act of traveling from one place to another
  • Synonym: expedition - a journey undertaken for a specific purpose, often exploration
  • Synonym: cruise - a journey by sea, usually for pleasure

Etymology

From Old French 'voiage', from Latin 'viaticum' (provision for a journey).

a long journey, especially by sea or in space
a long journey, usually by sea or space

Examples

The novel describes the voyage of a young explorer in search of new lands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trip - a journey or excursion, especially for pleasure

From Old French 'voiage' (journey), from Latin 'viaticum' (provision for a journey).

3. VERB

/ ˈvɔɪ.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvɔɪ.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
to travel by water, typically over a long distance, especially by ship or boat
travel by water, often by ship

Examples

The explorers decided to voyage across the Atlantic in a wooden ship.
They voyaged for months before reaching land.
Many ancient peoples voyaged along these trade routes in search of new lands.

Synset Examples

The QE2 will sail to Southampton tomorrow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sail - to travel on water in a ship or boat, especially using sails

Etymology

From Old French *voiage*, from Latin *viaticum* (provision for a journey), related to *via* (way, road).
