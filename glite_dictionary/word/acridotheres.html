Acridotheres

1. NOUN

/ ˌækrɪˈdɑːθəˌriːz / (AmE), / ˌakrɪˈdɒθəriːz / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the starling family, commonly called the ‘typical’ mynas, native to South and Southeast Asia.
genus of 'typical' mynas

Examples

The common myna, belonging to the genus Acridotheres, is a familiar sight in many Asian cities.
Several species in Acridotheres have adapted remarkably well to urban environments.
Researchers examined the genetic diversity within the Acridotheres genus to understand the spread of mynas.

Etymology

From Greek *akrid-*, meaning 'grasshopper', and *theres*, meaning 'hunter'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæk.rɪˈdɒθ.əˌriːz / (AmE), / ˌakrɪˈdɒθəriːz / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the starling family, containing the typical mynas, native to southern Asia and known for their loud calls and adaptability.
genus of typical mynas

Examples

The Acridotheres genus includes several well-known species of mynas found in South Asia.
Researchers studied the behavior of birds belonging to the Acridotheres genus.
Introduced populations of Acridotheres have become established in many urban environments.

Etymology

From Greek 'akris' (locust) and 'theres' (hunter), referencing the birds' feeding habits.
