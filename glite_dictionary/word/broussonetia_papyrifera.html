Broussonetia papyrifera

1. NOUN

/ bruːsəˈniːʃə ˌpæpəˈrɪfərə / (AmE), / bruːsəˈniːʃɪə ˌpapɪˈrɪfərə / (BrE)
An Asian tree, also known as the paper mulberry, with bark resembling cloth, grown as a shade tree and noted for its use in making tapa cloth.
Asian tree (paper mulberry) used for tapa cloth

Examples

The bark of Broussonetia papyrifera has been used for centuries to make traditional tapa cloth.
Gardeners appreciate Broussonetia papyrifera as an ornamental shade tree in many parts of the world.
The leaves and fruits of Broussonetia papyrifera attract a variety of birds and insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paper mulberry - Common name for Broussonetia papyrifera, the Asian tree with bark used for cloth

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining the genus name Broussonetia (after Pierre Marie Auguste Broussonet) and Latin papyrifera (paper-bearing).
