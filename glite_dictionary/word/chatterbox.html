chatterbox

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃætərˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈtʃætəˌbɒks / (BrE)
a person, especially a child, who talks a lot, often excessively or incessantly
someone who talks a lot

Examples

My younger sister is such a chatterbox; she never stops talking at dinner.
The teacher reminded the chatterboxes at the back of the class to be quiet during the test.
Although he's a chatterbox at home, he becomes shy around strangers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blabbermouth - a person who talks too much, especially about things that should be secret
  • Antonym: quiet - not talking much

Etymology

From 'chatter' (to talk quickly and continuously) + 'box', first recorded in the 18th century.
