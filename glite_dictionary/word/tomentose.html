tomentose

1. ADJ

/ toʊˈmɛnˌtoʊs / (AmE), / təˈmɛn.təʊs / (BrE)
(of a surface) covered with densely matted, woolly hairs or filaments, especially in reference to plants or animals.
covered with dense, matted hairs

Examples

The leaves of the plant are tomentose, giving them a soft, velvety texture.
Many alpine plants have tomentose stems to reduce water loss in their harsh environment.
The caterpillar appeared tomentose, covered in thick, woolly hairs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: glabrous - having a surface that is smooth and without hairs

Etymology

From Latin tomentosus (covered with matted wool or down), from tomentum (cushion stuffing, down).

2. ADJ

/ toʊˈmɛnˌtoʊs / (AmE), / təʊˈmɛnˌtəʊs / (BrE)
(of a plant or plant part) densely covered with short, matted, woolly hairs.
covered with woolly plant hairs

Examples

The leaf surface is tomentose, giving it a silvery appearance.
Some alpine plants develop tomentose stems to help protect against cold winds.
The botanist noted the tomentose texture of the undersides of the oak leaves.

Synset Examples

a tomentose leaf

Etymology

From Latin 'tomentosus', from 'tomentum' (cushion stuffing, flock of wool).
