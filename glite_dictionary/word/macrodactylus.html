Macrodactylus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæk.roʊˈdæk.tɪ.ləs / (AmE), / ˌmæk.rəʊˈdæk.tɪ.ləs / (BrE)
A genus of beetles in the family Melolonthidae, commonly known as May beetles or June bugs.
a genus of beetles

Examples

The Macrodactylus beetle is often found feeding on grapevine leaves during the summer months.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Macrodactylus species across different regions.
Several agricultural pests in North America belong to the genus Macrodactylus.

Etymology

From Greek 'makros' (large) + 'daktylos' (finger), referring to the large tarsi of the beetles.

genus of beetles (Melolonthidae)

Examples

The Macrodactylus genus includes the well-known rose chafer beetle.

Several North American species belong to the Macrodactylus genus.

Etymology

From Greek makros (long) + dactylos (finger), referring to the long legs of these beetles.
