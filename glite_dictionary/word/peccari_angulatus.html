Peccari angulatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛkəˌraɪ æŋˈɡjuːlətəs / (AmE), / ˌpɛkəˌraɪ æŋˈɡjuːlətəs / (BrE)
A species of peccary, dark grey with a faint white collar, found in semi-desert areas of Mexico and the southwestern United States; also known as the collared peccary.
a species of peccary, collared peccary

Examples

The Peccari angulatus is well adapted to arid environments where water is scarce.
Local ranchers often see the Peccari angulatus foraging at dawn and dusk.
The Peccari angulatus travels in small herds throughout the southwestern United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collared peccary - common name for Peccari angulatus

Etymology

From Latin, 'peccari' (peccary) and 'angulatus' (angled or collared).
