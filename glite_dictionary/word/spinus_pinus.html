Spinus pinus

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪnəs ˈpaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈspɪnʊs ˈpɪnʊs / (BrE)
a small finch native to North American coniferous forests, also known as the pine siskin
small North American finch (pine siskin)

Examples

Many birdwatchers hope to spot a Spinus pinus among the conifers during migration season.
The streaked plumage of Spinus pinus helps it blend into the pine forests it inhabits.
Researchers banded several Spinus pinus to study their winter migratory patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pine siskin - common name for Spinus pinus, a small North American finch

Etymology

From Latin; 'Spinus' is the genus name for siskins and related finches, 'pinus' means 'pine', referring to its habitat.
