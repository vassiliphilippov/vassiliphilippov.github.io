thiopentobarbital sodium

1. NOUN

/ ˌθaɪoʊˌpɛntoʊˈbɑːrbɪˌtɔl ˈsoʊdiəm / (AmE), / ˌθaɪəʊˌpɛntəʊˈbɑːbɪˌtɒl ˈsəʊdiəm / (BrE)
A barbiturate drug used as a fast-acting general anesthetic, usually in the form of a hygroscopic powder; commonly known by the trade name Pentothal.
fast-acting general anesthetic barbiturate
medical

Examples

The anesthesiologist administered thiopentobarbital sodium to induce unconsciousness before the surgery.
Some hospitals have replaced thiopentobarbital sodium with other anesthetics due to its rapid onset and short duration.
Veterinarians may use thiopentobarbital sodium during certain procedures to quickly sedate animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thiopental - another name for thiopentobarbital sodium

Etymology

From 'thiopento-' (referring to the sulfur atom and pentyl group) + 'barbital' (from 'barbituric acid') + 'sodium', indicating the sodium salt form.
