plasmacytoma

1. NOUN

/ plæs.mə.saɪˈtoʊ.mə / (AmE), / plaz.mə.sɪˈtəʊ.mə / (BrE)
A tumor consisting of abnormal plasma cells, usually found in bone marrow but sometimes in other tissues.
tumor of plasma cells
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a plasmacytoma after a biopsy of the spinal lesion.
Radiation therapy is often used to treat a solitary plasmacytoma in the bone.
Unlike multiple myeloma, a single plasmacytoma can usually be removed surgically.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: multiple myeloma - a cancer formed by malignant plasma cells, often widespread

Etymology

From Greek 'plasma' (something molded) and 'kytos' (cell) and the suffix '-oma' (tumor).
