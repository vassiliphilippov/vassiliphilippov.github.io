parachute

1. NOUN

/ ˈpærəˌʃut / (AmE), / ˈpærəʃuːt / (BrE)
A device made of fabric that fills with air and slows the fall of a person or object dropped from an aircraft.
device for slowing a fall from an aircraft

Examples

The pilot was able to escape the burning plane by using his parachute.
During training, each soldier must learn how to pack and deploy a parachute safely.
The skydiver floated gracefully to the ground under her colorful parachute.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paraglider - lightweight, free-flying, foot-launched glider aircraft with no rigid primary structure

Etymology

From French 'parachute', from Italian 'para-' (protect) + 'scendere' (to descend).

2. VERB

/ ˈpærəˌʃut / (AmE), / ˈpærəʃuːt / (BrE)
to descend from an aircraft by using a parachute
descend with a parachute from an aircraft

Examples

She always wanted to parachute out of a plane to experience the thrill of free fall.
Dozens of soldiers will parachute into the remote area during tomorrow's exercise.
He had never parachuted before, so his first jump was both exciting and nerve-wracking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to skydive - to jump from an aircraft and perform acrobatic maneuvers before deploying a parachute
