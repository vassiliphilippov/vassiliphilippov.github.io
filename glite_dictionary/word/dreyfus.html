Dreyfus

1. PROPN

/ ˈdreɪ.fəs / (AmE), / ˈdreɪ.fəs / (BrE)
(Alfred Dreyfus) A French army officer of Jewish descent whose wrongful conviction for treason in 1894 became a major public scandal, symbolizing the dangers of anti-Semitism in France.
French army officer falsely convicted of treason

Examples

The case of Dreyfus exposed deep divisions in French society.
Historians still debate the political impact of the Dreyfus affair.
Emile Zola famously defended Dreyfus with his open letter 'J'accuse.'
