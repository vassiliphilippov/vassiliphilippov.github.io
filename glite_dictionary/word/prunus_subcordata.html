Prunus subcordata

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs ˌsʌb.kɔːrˈdeɪ.tə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs ˌsʌb.kɔːˈdeɪ.tə / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree native to the Pacific coast of the United States, bearing small red, tasteless fruit.
Pacific coast shrub with small red fruit

Examples

Wild Prunus subcordata can often be found growing along forest edges in northern California.
The fruit of Prunus subcordata is considered insipid and not commonly eaten.
Researchers studied the ecological role of Prunus subcordata in the local wildlife habitat.

Etymology

From Latin: 'prunus' meaning 'plum tree' and 'subcordata' meaning 'somewhat heart-shaped'.
