Silene uniflora

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈliːni ˌjuːnɪˈflɔrə / (AmE), / sɪˈliːni ˌjuːnɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to Arctic Europe, noted for its large white flowers and inflated calyx.
a perennial Arctic European plant with white flowers

Examples

The rocky coastline was dotted with clusters of Silene uniflora in full bloom.
Botanists are studying the adaptation strategies of Silene uniflora to harsh Arctic climates.
Gardeners often cultivate Silene uniflora for its attractive, white, balloon-like flowers.

Etymology

From Latin: Silene (the genus name) and uniflora ('one-flowered').
