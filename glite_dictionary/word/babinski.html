Babinski

1. NOUN

/ bəˈbɪnski / (AmE), / bəˈbɪnski / (BrE)
An upward extension of the toes when the sole of the foot is firmly stroked, normal in infants but indicative of nerve damage in older children and adults.
medical sign of upward toe extension
medical

Examples

The neurologist checked for the Babinski by stroking the patient's foot.
A positive Babinski in adults can indicate damage to the central nervous system.
In infants, the Babinski is a normal reflex and usually disappears by age two.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Babinski reflex - A reflex movement where the big toe extends upward, particularly in infants

Etymology

Named after Joseph Babinski (1857–1932), French-Polish neurologist who described the sign.
