webbed

1. ADJ

/ wɛbd / (AmE), / wɛbd / (BrE)
(of feet, fingers, etc.) having the toes or fingers connected by a membrane, or resembling the structure of a web.
having toes or fingers joined by a membrane

Examples

The duck has webbed feet, which help it swim efficiently in water.
Her costume included gloves with webbed fingers to mimic a frog.
Some frogs are recognizable by their highly webbed hind feet.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unwebbed - lacking webbing between toes or fingers

Etymology

From 'web' + '-ed', denoting having the quality of a web.

2. ADJ

/ wɛbd / (AmE), / wɛbd / (BrE)
(of animals' feet) having the toes or fingers connected by a thin membrane of skin.
having toes or fingers joined by skin

Examples

The duck's webbed feet help it swim efficiently in the water.
She noticed the frog's webbed toes as it hopped away.
Some aquatic mammals, like otters, have webbed feet to aid in swimming.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unwebbed - lacking a membrane connecting the digits

Etymology

From 'web' (Old English 'webb', meaning woven fabric), referring to the membrane connecting digits.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02601403-a
Synset POS: a
