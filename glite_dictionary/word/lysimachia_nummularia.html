Lysimachia nummularia

1. NOUN

/ lɪˌsɪməˈkaɪ.ə ˌnʌmjəˈlɛəriə / (AmE), / lɪˌsɪməˈkɪə ˌnʌmjʊˈlɑːrɪə / (BrE)
A low-growing perennial plant in the loosestrife family, commonly known as creeping Jenny or moneywort, often used as ground cover in gardens.
creeping plant; also called creeping Jenny

Examples

Lysimachia nummularia is often planted as ground cover because of its rapid spread and bright yellow flowers.
Gardeners appreciate Lysimachia nummularia for its ability to thrive in wet and shady areas.
The round leaves of Lysimachia nummularia inspired its common name, moneywort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creeping Jenny - common name for Lysimachia nummularia
  • Synonym: moneywort - another common name for Lysimachia nummularia

Etymology

From Latin, genus Lysimachia named after Lysimachus (ancient Greek king), and 'nummularia' from Latin 'nummularius' (like a coin), referring to the round leaves.
