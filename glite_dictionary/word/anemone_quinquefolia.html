Anemone quinquefolia

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɛməni kwɪnkwəˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / əˈnɛməni kwɪŋkwɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A North American wildflower with solitary pink-tinged white flowers, commonly found in eastern woodlands.
North American wildflower with pinkish white flowers

Examples

In the spring, the forest floor was dotted with Anemone quinquefolia in full bloom.
She identified the delicate white flower as Anemone quinquefolia during her hike.
Botanists study the habitat preferences of Anemone quinquefolia in eastern North American woods.

Etymology

From Latin: 'anemone' (windflower), 'quinquefolia' (five-leaved).
