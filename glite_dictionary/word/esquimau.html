Esquimau

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛskɪˌmoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɛskɪməʊ / (BrE)
an outdated term for a member of the indigenous peoples inhabiting the Arctic regions of northern Canada, Greenland, Alaska, or eastern Siberia; now more commonly called Eskimo or Inuit
outdated term for Arctic indigenous peoples
archaic

Examples

In outdated literature, the word Esquimau is often used instead of Inuit or Eskimo.
The explorers encountered a small group of Esquimaux living near the ice floes.
Historical documents frequently describe the Arctic peoples as Esquimau, though this term is no longer commonly used.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eskimo - a member of the indigenous peoples of the Arctic
  • Synonym: Inuit - an indigenous people inhabiting northern Canada and Greenland

Etymology

From French 'Esquimau', itself from an Algonquian word meaning 'eater of raw flesh'.
