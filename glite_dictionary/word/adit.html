adit

1. NOUN

/ ˈædɪt / (AmE), / ˈadɪt / (BrE)
a nearly horizontal passage leading from the surface into a mine, used for access, drainage, or ventilation
horizontal mine entrance or tunnel

Examples

The miners entered the old adit to begin their shift deep underground.
Rainwater often seeps into the mine through the main adit.
A team of inspectors checked the safety conditions in the newly built adit.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shaft - a vertical or steeply inclined passage in a mine

Etymology

From Latin 'aditus' (approach, entrance), from 'adīre' (to approach).
