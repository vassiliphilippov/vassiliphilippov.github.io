municipality

1. NOUN

/ mjuˌnɪsɪˈpæləti / (AmE), / mjuːˌnɪsɪˈpælɪti / (BrE)
An urban district or town with corporate status and powers of self-government.
urban district with self-government

Examples

The municipality is responsible for providing essential services such as water and waste management.
Elections will be held next month to choose new leaders for the municipality.
Each municipality has the authority to enact its own local regulations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: city - a large and significant town, often with its own local government
  • Antonym: village - a small community or group of houses in a rural area, typically smaller than a town

Etymology

From Latin 'municipalis' (of a free town), from 'municipium' (free town with its own laws).

2. NOUN

/ mjuːˌnɪsɪˈpæləti / (AmE), / mjuːˌnɪsɪˈpælɪti / (BrE)
the people living in a town or city who are governed by a local authority; the local self-governing body of such an area
local self-governing town or city

Examples

The municipality voted to allocate funds for the new community center.
Residents of the municipality are encouraged to participate in the upcoming election.
Each municipality has its own set of local regulations and services.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: city - a large town, often with its own local government
  • Synonym: town - an urban area smaller than a city and larger than a village

Etymology

From French 'municipalité', from Latin 'municipalis' (pertaining to a citizen of a free town).
