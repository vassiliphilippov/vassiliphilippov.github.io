myrmecophyte

1. NOUN

/ mərˈmiːkəˌfaɪt / (AmE), / məˈmiːkəˌfaɪt / (BrE)
A plant that provides shelter or food to ants, forming a symbiotic relationship with them.
plant that lives symbiotically with ants
medical

Examples

The rainforest is home to many myrmecophytes that shelter ant colonies in their hollow stems.
Some myrmecophyte species rely on ants for protection against herbivores.
Researchers study the mutual benefits between myrmecophytes and their resident ants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epiphyte - a plant that grows on another plant but is not parasitic

Etymology

From Greek 'myrmēk-', meaning 'ant', and 'phyton', meaning 'plant'.
