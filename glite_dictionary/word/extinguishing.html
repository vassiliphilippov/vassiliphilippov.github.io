extinguishing

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of putting out a fire, light, or something burning
the act of putting something out

Examples

The extinguishing of the campfire was quick and efficient.
After the extinguishing of the candles, the room was plunged into darkness.
The firefighter was praised for his swift extinguishing of the blaze.

Synset Examples

the extinction of the lights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extinction - the act of extinguishing or the process of becoming extinct

Etymology

From the verb "extinguish," which comes from Latin *extinguere* (to quench, put out).
