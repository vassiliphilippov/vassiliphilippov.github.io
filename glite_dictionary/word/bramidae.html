Bramidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈbræmɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈbræmɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of deep-bodied percoid fishes found in the open seas, known as pomfrets.
family of deep-bodied marine fishes (pomfrets)

Examples

The Bramidae are distributed worldwide in tropical and temperate oceans.
Members of the Bramidae family, commonly called pomfrets, have deep, laterally compressed bodies.
Researchers discovered a new species belonging to the Bramidae off the coast of Japan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pomfret - a marine fish of the family Bramidae

Etymology

From New Latin Bramidae, based on the type genus Brama.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbræmɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈbramɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of deep-bodied percoid fishes found in the open seas, commonly known as pomfrets.
family of deep-bodied oceanic fishes (pomfrets)

Examples

The Bramidae are distributed widely through tropical and temperate oceans.
Marine biologists study the migratory patterns of Bramidae species.
Some members of the Bramidae are prized as food fish in various cultures.

Etymology

From New Latin Bramidae, from the type genus Brama, ultimately from Greek brama (type of fish).
