to mummify

1. VERB

/ ˈmʌməˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmʌmɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to preserve a dead body by removing the internal organs and drying it, typically to prevent decay
preserve a body by drying and removing organs

Examples

Ancient Egyptians were famous for their ability to mummify their pharaohs.
The museum displayed a naturally mummified body found in the desert.
Modern scientists sometimes mummify small animals for research purposes.

Synset Examples

The Egyptians mummified their pharaohs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embalm - to preserve a body from decay by treating it with preservatives

Etymology

From French *momifier*, from *momie* (mummy), from Medieval Latin *mumia*, ultimately from Arabic *mūmiyāʼ* (bitumen, embalmed body).

2. VERB

/ ˈmʌməˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmʌmɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to preserve in such a way that something becomes lifeless, often by drying or embalming; to make lifeless or stagnant
preserve in a lifeless state

Examples

Ancient Egyptians were experts at mummifying bodies to preserve them for the afterlife.
Some traditions can become mummified if they never adapt to modern realities.
The institution has been so mummified by bureaucracy that it can barely function anymore.

Synset Examples

mummified ideas and institutions should be gotten rid of

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embalm - to preserve a body by treating it with chemicals
  • Antonym: to revitalize - to give new life or vigor to

Etymology

From French *momifier*, from *momie* (mummy), from Medieval Latin *mumia*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02686168-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈmʌməˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmʌmɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to dry up and preserve (a body or other organic matter), often resulting in a shriveled state due to complete loss of moisture
to dry up and shrivel from moisture loss

Examples

The hot desert air can mummify animal remains quickly.
Ancient Egyptians mummified their dead to preserve the bodies for the afterlife.
If you leave a piece of fruit out in the sun, it may eventually mummify.

Synset Examples

a mummified body was found

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desiccate - to dry out thoroughly; to remove moisture
  • Synonym: to dehydrate - to lose or remove water from; to become dry

Etymology

From Latin 'mummia' (embalmed body), via French 'momifier'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
