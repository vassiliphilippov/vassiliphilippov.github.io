Uropsilus

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈrɑːpsɪləs / (AmE), / jʊəˈrɒpsɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of small, mole-like mammals known as shrew moles, native to parts of Asia, including species such as Anderson's shrew mole and the Chinese shrew mole.
genus of Asian shrew moles

Examples

The genus Uropsilus consists of several small Asian shrew moles found primarily in mountainous regions.
Research on Uropsilus has helped scientists better understand the evolution of subterranean mammals.
Specimens from the Uropsilus genus are rarely seen due to their elusive nature and remote habitats.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'ourá' (tail) and 'psilos' (bare or smooth).
