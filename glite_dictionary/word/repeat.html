repeat

1. VERB

/ rɪˈpit / (AmE), / rɪˈpiːt / (BrE)
to do, make, or perform something again; to say or write something that has already been said or written.
do or say again

Examples

She had to repeat the experiment several times before getting the correct results.
Can you repeat what you just said more slowly?
He didn’t want to repeat the same mistake twice.

Synset Examples

He could never replicate his brilliant performance of the magic trick

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to replicate - to make an exact copy of; to repeat for verification
  • Synonym: to recur - to occur again, repeatedly
  • Antonym: stop - to cease doing something

Etymology

From Latin 'repetere' (to seek again, to demand back), from 're-' (again) + 'petere' (to seek).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ rəˈpit / (AmE), / rɪˈpiːt / (BrE)
an event or action that happens again or is repeated, especially in the same way as before
an event that happens again

Examples

The team's victory was not a repeat of last season's performance.
The festival was so popular that organizers planned a repeat for the following year.
Fans were disappointed when the match turned out to be a repeat of the previous game.

Synset Examples

the events today were a repeat of yesterday's

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recurrence - the fact of occurring again
  • Antonym: first - the original event before any repeat

Etymology

From Latin 'repetere' (to seek again, repeat), via Old French and Middle English.
