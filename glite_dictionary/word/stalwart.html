stalwart

1. ADJ

/ ˈstɔlwɚt / (AmE), / ˈstɔːlwət / (BrE)
(of a person) loyal, reliable, and hardworking; strong and sturdy in character or physique.
loyal and dependable person

Examples

Her father was a stalwart supporter of the local community.
Despite many challenges, the team remained stalwart in their commitment to success.
As a stalwart defender, she rarely missed a game.

Synset Examples

a stalwart knight
a stouthearted fellow who had an active career in the army

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reliable - able to be depended on; trustworthy
  • Synonym: loyal - showing firm support or allegiance
  • Antonym: unreliable - not able to be trusted or depended on

Etymology

From Old English 'stælwierðe', meaning serviceable, worthy, becoming 'stalwart'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɔːlwɚt / (AmE), / ˈstɔːlwət / (BrE)
a loyal and reliable supporter, especially one who remains steadfast during difficult times
loyal and steadfast supporter

Examples

She has always been a stalwart of the community, volunteering her time without fail.
During the crisis, he emerged as a stalwart who rallied others to defend their cause.
Many party stalwarts attended the convention to show their loyalty and support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supporter - a person who supports a cause or another person
  • Synonym: adherent - a person who supports a leader, party, or idea
  • Antonym: traitor - a person who betrays their allegiance or friends

Etymology

From Middle English 'stalworth', from Old English 'stǣlwierthe' (serviceable, valiant).

Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ ˈstɔlˌwɑrt / (AmE), / ˈstɔːlwət / (BrE)
having strong and sturdy physical strength; able to withstand fatigue or hardship
physically strong and resilient

Examples

The stalwart climber persevered through biting winds and heavy snow to reach the summit.
She relied on her stalwart bodyguards during the chaotic parade.
Despite the difficult trek, the team's stalwart members never wavered or complained.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robust - strong and healthy; hardy; vigorous
  • Antonym: weak - lacking physical strength or resilience
  • Synonym: hardy - capable of enduring difficult conditions; robust

Etymology

From Middle English *stalworth*, from Old English *stǣlwierthe* (serviceable), from *stæl* (place, position) + *weorth* (worth).
