snip

1. NOUN

/ snɪp / (AmE), / snɪp / (BrE)
the act of cutting something with quick, small strokes, especially with scissors or shears
act of cutting with small strokes

Examples

With a quick snip, she trimmed the loose thread from her sleeve.
He gave the rose bush a gentle snip to encourage new growth.
You could hear the snip of scissors as the tailor worked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut - the act of making an incision or opening

Etymology

Imitative; related to the verb 'snip', possibly from Middle Dutch 'snippen' (to snip).

2. NOUN

/ snɪp / (AmE), / snɪp / (BrE)
a small piece cut off from something, especially with scissors or shears
small piece cut off

Examples

She used a snip of fabric to patch the hole in her jeans.
He handed me a snip of paper with the address written on it.
With a quick snip, she trimmed the loose threads from the hem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snippet - a small piece or brief extract

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound of snipping; related to Middle Dutch snippen (to snip, cut).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04257136-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ snɪp / (AmE), / snɪp / (BrE)
to cut something quickly and neatly, usually with scissors or a similar tool
cut quickly, often with scissors

Examples

She used small scissors to snip a loose thread from her shirt.
The gardener snipped the dead leaves off the plant with ease.
He snipped a coupon from the newspaper before heading to the store.

Synset Examples

nip off the flowers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut - to divide or remove with a sharp instrument
  • Synonym: clip - to cut off with a sharp instrument, especially scissors
