to ill-treat

1. VERB

/ ɪlˈtrit / (AmE), / ɪlˈtriːt / (BrE)
to treat someone or something in a cruel, unkind, or unfair way
treat in a cruel or unfair way

Examples

It is wrong to ill-treat animals for entertainment purposes.
The teacher was criticized after being accused of ill-treating the students.
He often ill-treats his staff, making unreasonable demands and insulting them.

Synset Examples

This boss abuses his workers
She is always stepping on others to get ahead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mistreat - to treat someone badly or unfairly
  • Synonym: abuse - to use or treat someone in a cruel or violent way
  • Antonym: to pamper - to treat with excessive care and attention

Etymology

From 'ill-' (badly) + 'treat'.
