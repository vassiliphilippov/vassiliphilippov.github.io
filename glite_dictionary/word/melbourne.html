Melbourne

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɛl.bɝːn / (AmE), / ˈmɛl.bən / (BrE)
A city and resort town in east central Florida, known for its beaches and as a tourist destination.
city and resort town in Florida, USA

Examples

We spent our winter vacation in Melbourne, enjoying the beautiful Atlantic coast beaches.
Melbourne is known for its annual arts festival, attracting tourists from all over the country.
After moving to Florida, she settled in Melbourne to be closer to the ocean.

Etymology

Named after Melbourne, Australia, which in turn was named after Viscount Melbourne, a British Prime Minister.

2. PROPN

/ ˈmɛl.bɚn / (AmE), / ˈmɛl.bən / (BrE)
The capital city of the state of Victoria, Australia, and the country's second-largest city, known as a major financial and commercial center.
Australian city, capital of Victoria

Examples

Many international companies have their headquarters in Melbourne due to its vibrant business environment.
She moved to Melbourne to study at one of its renowned universities.
The cultural diversity of Melbourne makes it a popular destination for tourists.

Etymology

Named after William Lamb, 2nd Viscount Melbourne, former British Prime Minister.
