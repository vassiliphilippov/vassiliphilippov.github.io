suggestion

1. NOUN

/ səɡˈdʒɛstʃən / (AmE), / səɡˈdʒɛstʃən / (BrE)
an idea or proposal put forward for consideration or acceptance
proposal or idea offered

Examples

She made a suggestion to improve our workflow during the meeting.
After considering his suggestion, the team decided to try a new approach.
Your suggestion for dinner tonight sounds delicious.

Synset Examples

it was a suggestion we couldn't refuse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proposal - a plan or suggestion, especially a formal one, put forward for consideration

Etymology

From Latin 'suggestio', from 'suggestus', past participle of 'suggerere' (to bring up, suggest).

5. NOUN

/ səgˈdʒɛstʃən / (AmE), / səgˈdʒɛstʃən / (BrE)
An idea or plan put forward for consideration, often intended to persuade someone to act or think in a particular way.
an idea put forward for consideration

Examples

Her suggestion that we start early was persuasive enough to convince the whole group.
He made a suggestion that helped us solve the problem more quickly.
The committee welcomed any suggestion that could improve the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proposal - a plan or suggestion, especially a formal one, put forward for consideration
  • Synonym: recommendation - an idea or piece of advice suggested for consideration
  • Antonym: order - a command or instruction, opposite of a suggestion

Etymology

From Latin 'suggestio', from 'suggestus', past participle of 'suggerere' (to put under, bring up, suggest).
