Heteroscelus

1. PROPN

/ ˌhɛtərəˈsiːləs / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəˈsiːləs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the sandpiper family, commonly known as tattlers, found in coastal regions of Asia and the Americas.
genus of birds (tattlers)

Examples

The Heteroscelus genus includes species commonly called tattlers due to their shrill calls.
Scientists observed Heteroscelus along the rugged coastline during their migratory survey.
Many birdwatchers travel to spot rare Heteroscelus species in the wild.

Etymology

From Greek heteros (different) + skelos (leg), referring to the bird's distinctive legs.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɛtərəˈsiːləs / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəˈsiːləs / (BrE)
A genus of wading birds in the family Scolopacidae, commonly known as tattlers, found in coastal regions of Asia, Australia, and North America.
genus of wading birds (tattlers)

Examples

The Heteroscelus is characterized by its distinct calls and migratory habits.
Two species within the genus Heteroscelus are commonly referred to as tattlers.
Researchers observed the migratory routes of Heteroscelus along the Pacific coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tattler - a common name for birds in Heteroscelus or closely related genera

Etymology

From Greek heteros (different) + skelos (leg), referring to the structure of the bird's legs.
