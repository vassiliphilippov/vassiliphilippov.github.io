unlikable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈlaɪkəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈlaɪkəbl / (BrE)
(of a person or character) not easy to like or evoking feelings of dislike.
not easy to like; evokes dislike

Examples

The villain in the story was so unlikable that readers cheered for his defeat.
She found her new coworker surprisingly unlikable despite his impressive resume.
The film's main character is intentionally unlikable, challenging viewers to question their sympathies.

Synset Examples

all the characters were peculiarly unsympathetic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: likable - easy to like; pleasant
  • Synonym: amiable - having a friendly and pleasant manner
  • Synonym: unpleasant - causing discomfort or dislike; disagreeable

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'likable' (pleasant or agreeable).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈlaɪkəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈlaɪkəbəl / (BrE)
not easy or possible to like; having qualities that make people dislike them or it.
not easy or possible to like

Examples

Despite his success, many found the CEO unlikable and distant.
The main character was so rude and arrogant that she became increasingly unlikable as the story progressed.
Her unlikable behavior made it difficult for her to make friends at her new school.

Synset Examples

a disagreeable and unlikable old woman

Related Terms

  • Antonym: likable - pleasant and easy to like

Etymology

From un- (not) + likable (pleasing, easy to like).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01464602-s
Synset POS: s
