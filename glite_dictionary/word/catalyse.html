to catalyse

1. VERB

/ ˈkætəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkætəlaɪz / (BrE)
to cause or accelerate a chemical reaction by acting as a catalyst
cause a process or reaction by catalysis
british

Examples

Enzymes in your body catalyse thousands of essential biochemical reactions every day.
The scientist wanted to catalyse the process to increase the yield of the experiment.
Sometimes, even a small amount of a substance can catalyse a dramatic change in the environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to catalyze - American spelling of 'catalyse'
  • Antonym: to decompose - to break down into simpler components, often the opposite of catalysis in chemistry

Etymology

From catalyst, modelled after analyse/analyze; originally from Greek katalusis (dissolution).
