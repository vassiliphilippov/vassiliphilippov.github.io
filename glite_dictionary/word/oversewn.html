oversewn

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərˈsoʊn / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈsəʊn / (BrE)
sewn together using overhand stitches, which pass over and draw two edges together.
sewn using overhand stitches

Examples

The edges of the fabric were oversewn to prevent fraying.
This antique book features an oversewn binding for added durability.
The seamstress oversewn the collars for reinforcement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to oversew - to sew over the edge of a material with close stitches

Etymology

From 'oversew' + -n, past participle formation.
