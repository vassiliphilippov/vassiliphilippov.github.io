esoterica

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛsəˈtɛrɪkə / (AmE), / ˌɛsəˈtɛrɪkə / (BrE)
things that are intended for or likely to be understood by only a small number of people with specialized knowledge or interest.
secret or specialized knowledge

Examples

The professor delighted in sharing esoterica about ancient manuscripts with his graduate students.
Collectors of rare books often value the esoterica found in obscure academic texts.
Most people found the club’s discussions to be full of esoterica that went over their heads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arcana - mysterious or specialized secrets known only to a few
  • Synonym: secrets - things kept hidden or unknown to most people
  • Antonym: exoterica - things suitable for or likely to be understood by the general public

Etymology

From Greek 'esōterikos' (inner), via Late Greek 'esōterikos' (belonging to an inner circle).
