Abies alba

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.bi.iːs ˈæl.bə / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.bi.iːs ˈæl.bə / (BrE)
A tall evergreen fir tree native to central and southern Europe, with a regular crown and smooth grey bark, often used for timber.
species of European fir tree

Examples

The forests of the Alps are dominated by Abies alba and other coniferous trees.
Abies alba is prized for its high-quality timber in the construction industry.
Many Christmas trees in Europe are varieties of Abies alba due to their symmetrical shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silver fir - common name for Abies alba

Etymology

Latin: abies (fir tree) + alba (white).
