Andromeda

1. PROPN

/ ænˈdrɑːmədə / (AmE), / ænˈdrɒmɪdə / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) an Ethiopian princess, daughter of Cassiopeia, who was chained to a rock as a sacrifice to a sea monster, but was rescued by Perseus and became his wife.
princess in Greek mythology rescued by Perseus

Examples

The story of Andromeda and Perseus is depicted in many classical paintings.
According to myth, Andromeda was chained to a rock to appease a sea monster.
In the legend, Andromeda was saved from certain death by the hero Perseus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Perseus - hero who rescued Andromeda in Greek mythology
  • Synonym: Cassiopeia - mother of Andromeda in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Greek Ἀνδρομέδα (Androméda), the mythological figure.

2. PROPN

/ ænˈdrɑːmədə / (AmE), / ænˈdrɒmɪdə / (BrE)
a constellation in the northern sky, situated between Cassiopeia and Pegasus, which contains the Andromeda galaxy
a northern constellation containing the Andromeda galaxy

Examples

The constellation Andromeda is best seen in the autumn sky in the Northern Hemisphere.
Astronomers often observe the Andromeda galaxy within the boundaries of the Andromeda constellation.
My telescope can easily find Andromeda if I start from Cassiopeia and move toward Pegasus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cassiopeia - a constellation adjacent to Andromeda
  • Synonym: Pegasus - a constellation adjacent to Andromeda
  • Synonym: Andromeda galaxy - galaxy located within the Andromeda constellation

Etymology

From Greek Andromeda, a mythological princess, whose name means 'ruler of men.'
