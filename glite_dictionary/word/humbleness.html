humbleness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌmbəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈhʌmb(ə)lnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being modest and having a low view of one's own importance
quality of being humble

Examples

Despite his success, his humbleness made him well-liked by everyone around him.
She showed great humbleness in accepting the award, giving credit to her entire team.
True humbleness comes from recognizing one's strengths and weaknesses without arrogance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humility - the quality of having a modest or low view of one's importance
  • Antonym: pride - a feeling of deep pleasure or satisfaction in achievement or qualities

Etymology

From Middle English 'humble' + '-ness', from Latin 'humilis' (low, humble).

3. NOUN

/ ˈhʌmbəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈhʌmb(ə)lnəs / (BrE)
the quality of having a modest view of one's importance; the absence of pride or arrogance
modesty; lack of pride

Examples

Her humbleness was evident in the way she accepted the award, always giving credit to others.
Despite his achievements, he showed great humbleness in conversation with colleagues.
They admired the leader's humbleness and willingness to listen to everyone.

Synset Examples

not everyone regards humility as a virtue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humility - the quality of having a modest or low view of one's own importance
  • Antonym: arrogance - the quality of being arrogant, having an exaggerated sense of one's own importance or abilities

Etymology

From humble + -ness, formed from Latin humilis (low, humble) + English suffix -ness.
