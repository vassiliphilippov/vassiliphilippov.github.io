Prumnopitys taxifolia

1. NOUN

/ prumˌnɒpɪtɪs tæksɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / prʌmˌnɒpɪtɪs tækˈsɪfəʊliə / (BrE)
a species of evergreen conifer native to New Zealand, commonly known as matai or black pine
New Zealand conifer tree species

Examples

The dense foliage of Prumnopitys taxifolia provides shelter for native birds in New Zealand forests.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Prumnopitys taxifolia in response to changing climate conditions.
Timber from Prumnopitys taxifolia has traditionally been used for flooring and furniture in New Zealand.

Etymology

From genus name Prumnopitys and Latin taxifolia, meaning 'yew-leaved'.
