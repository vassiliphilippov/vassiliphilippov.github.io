Cucumis melo cantalupensis

1. NOUN

/ kuːˈkjuːmɪs ˈmɛloʊ kæntəˌluːˈpɛnsɪs / (AmE), / kjuːˈkjuːmɪs ˈmiːləʊ kæntəˌluːˈpensɪs / (BrE)
a cultivated variety of muskmelon with a tan rind and orange flesh, commonly known as cantaloupe
variety of muskmelon (cantaloupe)

Examples

The Cucumis melo cantalupensis is prized for its sweet, fragrant orange flesh.
Farmers in the region specialize in growing Cucumis melo cantalupensis for local markets.
Cucumis melo cantalupensis requires warm temperatures and plenty of sunlight to thrive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cantaloupe - a round melon with orange flesh and a netted rind

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cucumis' meaning 'cucumber', 'melo' meaning 'melon', 'cantalupensis' indicating origin from Cantalupo, Italy.
