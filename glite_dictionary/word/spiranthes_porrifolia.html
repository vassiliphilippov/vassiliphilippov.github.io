Spiranthes porrifolia

1. NOUN

/ spaɪˈrænθiz ˌpɔrɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / spaɪˈrænθiːz ˌpɔːrɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A species of orchid native to western North America, known for its spiral arrangement of small white flowers.
a species of orchid native to western North America

Examples

The rare Spiranthes porrifolia can be found blooming in wet meadows of California.
Botanists participated in a survey to document populations of Spiranthes porrifolia along the Pacific coast.
Unlike its close relatives, Spiranthes porrifolia prefers marshy habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spiranthes romanzoffiana - A related species of orchid

Etymology

From the genus name Spiranthes (Greek 'speira' meaning coil, 'anthos' meaning flower) and the species epithet porrifolia ('porri' from Latin for leek, 'folia' meaning leaves).
