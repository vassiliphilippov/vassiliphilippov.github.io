to peddle

1. VERB

/ ˈpɛd.əl / (AmE), / ˈpɛd.əl / (BrE)
to sell goods, especially by traveling from place to place or going from house to house
sell goods door-to-door or while traveling

Examples

Vendors used to peddle fresh fruit from carts in the neighborhood.
She spent her summers peddling handmade jewelry at local fairs.
The company peddled its products door to door in rural areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawk - to sell goods aggressively or by calling out, often in public places

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *ped* (a basket, especially one carried by peddlers).
