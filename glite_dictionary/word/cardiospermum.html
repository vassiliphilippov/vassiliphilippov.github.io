Cardiospermum

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑrdioʊˈspɜrməm / (AmE), / ˌkɑːdɪəʊˈspɜːməm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical and subtropical climbing plants, some of which have seeds marked with a white, heart-shaped spot.
genus of tropical climbing plants

Examples

The climbing vines of Cardiospermum thrive in tropical climates around the world.
Botanists identified the plant as Cardiospermum due to the distinctive heart-shaped spot on its seeds.
Some traditional medicines use extracts from Cardiospermum for skin conditions.

Etymology

From Greek 'kardia' (heart) and 'sperma' (seed), referring to the heart-shaped mark on the seeds.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːrdioʊˈspɜːrməm / (AmE), / ˌkɑːdiəʊˈspɜːməm / (BrE)
a genus of tendril-climbing herbs or shrubs, known for seeds with a white heart-shaped spot, commonly called balloon vine
genus of climbing herbs with heart-shaped seed spots

Examples

The Cardiospermum vine is often grown in gardens for its ornamental balloons and foliage.
Seeds of Cardiospermum feature a distinctive white, heart-shaped spot, making them easy to identify.
Botanists have studied Cardiospermum for its unique method of seed dispersal and climbing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balloon vine - common name for species in the genus Cardiospermum

Etymology

From Greek *kardia* (heart) + *sperma* (seed), referring to the heart-shaped spot on the seeds.
