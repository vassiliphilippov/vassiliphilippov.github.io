bookstall

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊkˌstɔl / (AmE), / ˈbʊkˌstɔːl / (BrE)
A small shop or stall where books are sold, often found in train stations, markets, or public places.
a small shop or stall selling books

Examples

She bought a novel from the bookstall at the railway station before her journey.
During the festival, a temporary bookstall was set up in the central square.
The bookstall carried both new and second-hand books for all ages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bookstore - a shop where books are sold

Etymology

From 'book' + 'stall', meaning a booth for selling goods.
