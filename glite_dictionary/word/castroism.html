Castroism

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæstroʊˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈkæstrəʊˌɪzəm / (BrE)
the political and economic policies and practices associated with Fidel Castro's regime in Cuba, characterized by a form of communism.
Cuban communism under Fidel Castro

Examples

Many Latin American revolutionaries were influenced by Castroism in the 1960s.
The country's shift towards Castroism led to widespread land reforms and nationalizations.
Debates on the merits of Castroism continue among political scientists around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: communism - a political and economic doctrine seeking classless society and common ownership

Etymology

From the name Castro (Fidel Castro) + -ism, denoting a distinctive political doctrine.
