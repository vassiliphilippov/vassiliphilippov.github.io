to sharpshoot

1. VERB

/ ˈʃɑrpˌʃut / (AmE), / ˈʃɑːpʃuːt / (BrE)
to aim and shoot at a target with great accuracy and precision
shoot with great precision

Examples

A skilled marksman can sharpshoot moving objects from a considerable distance.
The soldier was trained to sharpshoot in challenging weather conditions.
During the competition, she managed to sharpshoot each target with impressive accuracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sniper - a person who shoots with great accuracy, especially from a concealed position

Etymology

From 'sharp' + 'shoot', originally relating to 'sharpshooter' (accurate marksman).
