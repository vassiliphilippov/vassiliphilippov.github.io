hereupon

1. ADV

/ ˌhɪrəˈpɑn / (AmE), / ˌhɪərəˈpɒn / (BrE)
immediately after this; at this point as a consequence.
immediately after this

Examples

Hereupon, the committee decided to end the meeting.
The door was locked; hereupon they knocked loudly.
He disagreed with the proposal; hereupon a debate ensued.

Synset Examples

hereupon, the passengers stumbled aboard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thereupon - immediately or shortly after that
  • Synonym: immediately - at once; without delay

Etymology

From Middle English, combining 'here' + 'upon'.
