polyp

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑː.lɪp / (AmE), / ˈpɒl.ɪp / (BrE)
a small, typically benign, vascular growth that forms on the surface of a mucous membrane, such as in the nose, colon, or uterus
small growth on mucous membrane
medical

Examples

The doctor discovered a polyp during the patient's routine colonoscopy.
A nasal polyp can cause breathing difficulties and may require removal.
If left untreated, some types of polyps have the potential to develop into cancer.

Etymology

From French polype, from Latin polypus, from Ancient Greek polypous (‘many-footed’).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑː.lɪp / (AmE), / ˈpɒl.ɪp / (BrE)
a small, usually sedentary, hollow cylindrical animal of group Coelenterata (such as hydras or corals), typically with a ring of tentacles around its mouth
sessile cylindrical animal form (e.g. hydra, coral)

Examples

A coral reef is built by thousands of tiny polyps living together.
The polyp attaches itself to a rock and catches food with its tentacles.
Hydras are freshwater animals that remain in the polyp stage throughout their lives.

Synset Examples

in some species of coelenterate, polyps are a phase in the life cycle that alternates with a medusoid phase

Etymology

From Greek *polypous* (many-footed).
