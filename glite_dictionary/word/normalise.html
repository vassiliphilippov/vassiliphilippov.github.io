to normalise

1. VERB

/ ˈnɔrməˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnɔːməlaɪz / (BrE)
to become normal or return to a normal state after being disrupted or irregular
become or return to normal
british

Examples

After an initial period of uncertainty, the situation began to normalise by the end of the year.
It took several months for the market to normalise after the sudden crash.
As the fever subsided, her body temperature started to normalise again.

Synset Examples

Let us hope that relations with this country will normalize soon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to normalize - to become normal or return to its normal state (US spelling)

Etymology

From Latin 'normalis' (according to a rule) via French 'normaliser'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00469496-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈnɔːrməlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnɔːməlaɪz / (BrE)
to make normal or bring into conformity with a standard or norm
make conform to a standard or norm

Examples

The two countries agreed to normalise diplomatic relations after years of conflict.
We should normalise the data before analyzing the results.
They hope to normalise trade between the regions by removing unnecessary restrictions.

Synset Examples

normalize relations with China
normalize the temperature
normalize the spelling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to standardize - to make things conform to a standard
  • Synonym: to regularize - to make regular or conform to rules
  • Antonym: abnormalize - to make abnormal; to cause to deviate from the normal

Etymology

From French normaliser (to make normal), from normal (standard).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00469149-v
Synset POS: v
