large-capitalization

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɑrdʒ ˌkæpɪtl̩ʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈlɑːdʒ ˌkapɪtələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(of stocks) relating to companies with a market capitalization of five billion dollars or more.
stocks of very large companies

Examples

Many investors prefer large-capitalization stocks for their stability and lower risk.
The index tracks the performance of large-capitalization companies listed on the exchange.
Analysts expect large-capitalization firms to outperform smaller companies in the coming year.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: small-capitalization - (of stocks) relating to companies with a relatively small market capitalization
  • Antonym: mid-capitalization - (of stocks) relating to companies with a medium market capitalization
