postdiluvian

1. ADJ

/ ˌpoʊst.dəˈluː.vi.ən / (AmE), / ˌpəʊst.dɪˈluː.vi.ən / (BrE)
Existing or occurring after the time of the biblical flood of Noah.
after the biblical flood of Noah

Examples

The story explores postdiluvian societies grappling with the knowledge of the great flood.
Many ancient myths have postdiluvian settings, shaped by a new world after disaster.
The exhibit focuses on postdiluvian changes in art and architecture.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: antediluvian - existing or occurring before Noah's flood

Etymology

From Latin 'post-' (after) + 'diluvium' (flood, deluge).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊst.dɪˈluː.vi.ən / (AmE), / ˌpəʊst.dɪˈluː.vi.ən / (BrE)
A person, animal, or thing living or existing after the biblical flood of Noah.
thing or being after the biblical Flood

Examples

Some ancient scholars were fascinated by the mysteries of postdiluvian civilizations.
After the flood, Noah and his family became the first postdiluvian people.
Legends often describe strange postdiluvian animals that supposedly appeared after the deluge.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: antediluvian - being or occurring before the biblical flood

Etymology

From Latin post- (after) + Latin diluvium (flood).
