winner

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪnər / (AmE), / ˈwɪnə / (BrE)
A gambler who wins a bet.
a gambler who wins a bet

Examples

The horse racing winner celebrated his good fortune with friends at the track.
Only one winner walked away with the jackpot last night at the casino.
She was thrilled to be the winner of the high-stakes poker match.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: loser - a gambler who loses a bet

Etymology

From win + -er, meaning 'one who wins'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪnər / (AmE), / ˈwɪnə / (BrE)
a person who achieves frequent success or has a track record of accomplishments
person with frequent successes

Examples

She has always been a winner, both academically and professionally.
The company only hires proven winners to lead important projects.
If you want to be a winner, you have to keep working hard every day.

Synset Examples

his son would never be the achiever that his father was
only winners need apply
if you want to be a success, you have to dress like a success

Related Terms

  • Synonym: achiever - a person who achieves success
  • Antonym: loser - a person who does not succeed

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɪnər / (AmE), / ˈwɪnə / (BrE)
a person or team that wins a competition, game, or contest
person who wins a contest

Examples

After weeks of competition, she was finally crowned the winner of the tournament.
The winner of the raffle received an all-expenses-paid vacation.
Every winner will receive a trophy at the end of the event.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: loser - person or team that does not win; opposite of winner
  • Synonym: champion - person who wins first place, often in sports or competitions

Etymology

From Old English 'winnan' (to strive, struggle, win).
