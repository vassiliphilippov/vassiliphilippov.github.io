zygospore

1. NOUN

/ ˈzaɪɡəˌspɔr / (AmE), / ˈzaɪɡəˌspɔː / (BrE)
a thick-walled spore formed by the fusion of two similar sexual cells, especially in certain fungi and algae.
spore formed by fusion of sexual cells
medical

Examples

The fungus produces a zygospore after sexual reproduction between two similar cells.
Under unfavorable conditions, certain algae form a resistant zygospore to survive.
A zygospore remains dormant until environmental conditions become suitable for growth.

Etymology

From Greek 'zygos' (yoke) + 'spora' (seed, sowing).
