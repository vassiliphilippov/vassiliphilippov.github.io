Grewia

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡriːwiə / (AmE), / ˈɡriːwiə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical and subtropical climbers, shrubs, or trees in the mallow family, native to the Old World
genus of tropical and subtropical plants

Examples

The Grewia genus includes many species with edible fruits and medicinal properties.
Several varieties of Grewia are cultivated in tropical regions for their ornamental value.
Botanists have classified new species within the Grewia genus in recent decades.

Etymology

Named after Nehemiah Grew (1641–1712), English botanist.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡriːwiə / (AmE), / ˈɡriːwiə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical and subtropical Old World climbing plants, shrubs, or small trees, many species of which bear edible fruits.
genus of tropical shrubs and trees

Examples

Many species in the Grewia genus are valued for their edible fruits and medicinal uses.
The Grewia plants thrive in both tropical and subtropical regions across Africa and Asia.
Botanists studied the unique climbing habits of Grewia varieties found in the Indian subcontinent.

Named after Nehemiah Grew, English botanist (1641–1712).
