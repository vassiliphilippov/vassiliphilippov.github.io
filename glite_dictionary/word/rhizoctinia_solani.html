Rhizoctinia solani

1. NOUN

/ raɪˌzɑkˈtɪniə soʊˈlɑni / (AmE), / raɪˌzɒkˈtɪniə səʊˈlɑːni / (BrE)
a species of fungus that causes black scurf disease on potatoes, resulting in black, scurfy spots on the tubers
fungus causing black scurf on potatoes
medical

Examples

Black scurf on potatoes is often caused by the fungus Rhizoctinia solani.
Farmers monitor their crops for signs of Rhizoctinia solani to prevent losses.
The spread of Rhizoctinia solani can significantly reduce potato yields if left untreated.
