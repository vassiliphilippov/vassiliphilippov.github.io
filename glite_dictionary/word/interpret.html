to interpret

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈtɚprət / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːprət / (BrE)
to explain the meaning of something; to make something clear or understandable
explain or clarify meaning

Examples

It is sometimes difficult to interpret the true meaning of abstract art.
The teacher asked the students to interpret the poem in their own words.
Different cultures may interpret the gesture in various ways.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to explain - make something clear by giving details
  • Antonym: to misinterpret - to understand incorrectly

Etymology

From Latin 'interpretari' (to explain, translate).

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈtɜrprət / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːprɪt / (BrE)
to translate spoken words from one language into another, especially as an interpreter in real time
translate spoken words between languages

Examples

During the press conference, she interpreted the ambassador's remarks for the audience.
He was hired to interpret for the international panel discussion.
The student volunteers interpreted the guest speaker's answers in real time.

Synset Examples

I have to translate when my in-laws from Austria visit the U.S.
Can you interpret the speech of the visiting dignitaries?
She rendered the French poem into English
He translates for the U.N.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to translate - to render written or spoken words from one language into another

Etymology

From Latin interpretari (to explain, translate).

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈtɜrprət / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːprɪt / (BrE)
to translate spoken words from one language into another, or to understand the meaning of spoken or written language
translate or understand a language

Examples

He was asked to interpret for the French guest during the meeting.
It can be difficult to interpret ancient texts written in a lost language.
She learned to interpret German conversations with ease.

Synset Examples

She understands French
Can you read Greek?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to translate - to render words from one language into another
  • Antonym: to misinterpret - to interpret wrongly or incorrectly

Etymology

From Latin 'interpretari', meaning 'to explain, translate'.

4. VERB

/ ɪnˈtɝːprət / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːprət / (BrE)
to perform a piece of music, a role, or a work of art in a way that shows your own understanding or feelings about it
to perform or render artistically

Examples

The actor interpreted the character in a way that surprised the audience.
She interpreted the song with great emotion during the competition.
Many critics praised how the orchestra interpreted the complex symphony.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: render - to perform or present something, especially artistically

Etymology
