Athene

1. PROPN

/ əˈθini / (AmE), / əˈθiːni / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the goddess of wisdom, practical skills, and strategic warfare, and the protector of Athens; identified with the Roman goddess Minerva.
Greek goddess of wisdom and arts

Examples

Many ancient statues depict Athene wearing a helmet and carrying a shield.
The people of Athens built a great temple to honor Athene on the Acropolis.
In Greek mythology, Athene emerged fully grown and armored from Zeus's head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Minerva - Roman goddess of wisdom, identified with Greek Athene

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἀθηνᾶ (Athēnā), the name of the goddess.

2. NOUN

/ əˈθiːni / (AmE), / əˈθiːni / (BrE)
a genus of owls in the family Strigidae, including the little owl, spotted owlet, forest owlet, and burrowing owl.
a genus of owls

Examples

The genus Athene includes several small owl species found across Europe and Asia.
Conservation efforts have helped increase the population of the forest owlet, a rare member of the Athene genus.
Ornithologists studied the habitat preferences of Athene species in different regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: owl - a bird of prey with a rounded head and forward-facing eyes

Etymology

From Greek Athena – named after the Greek goddess associated with the owl.

3. NOUN

/ əˈθini / (AmE), / əˈθiːniː / (BrE)
A genus of small owls in the family Strigidae, including the little owl, spotted owlet, forest owlet, and burrowing owl.
a genus of small owls

Examples

The Athene genus includes the well-known little owl found in Europe and Asia.
Scientists have recently studied the evolutionary history of Athene species.
Among the members of Athene is the burrowing owl, which nests in underground burrows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: owl - a nocturnal bird of prey with large eyes, a flat face, and silent flight

Etymology

Modern Latin; named after the Greek goddess Athena, traditionally associated with owls.
