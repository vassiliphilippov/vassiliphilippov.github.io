Heloderma horridum

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːləˈdɜːrmə hɒˈrɪdəm / (AmE), / ˌhiːləˈdɜːmə hɒˈrɪdəm / (BrE)
a species of venomous lizard with black and yellowish beadlike scales, native to western Mexico
venomous Mexican lizard species

Examples

The Heloderma horridum is one of only a few venomous lizard species in the world.
Researchers studied the habitat of the Heloderma horridum in western Mexico.
The Heloderma horridum has beadlike scales that are both distinctive and functional.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mexican beaded lizard - common name for Heloderma horridum, a venomous lizard of Mexico

Etymology

From Greek helos (nail, stud), derma (skin), and Latin horridum (rough, bristly).
