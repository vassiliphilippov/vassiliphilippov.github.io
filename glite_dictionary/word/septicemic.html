septicemic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛp.tɪˈsiː.mɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɛp.tɪˈsiː.mɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of septicemia (the presence of pathogenic organisms in the bloodstream).
relating to septicemia; blood infection
medical

Examples

The patient developed a septicemic reaction following surgery.
Doctors monitored for any septicemic symptoms after the infection spread to the bloodstream.
A sudden septicemic episode can be life-threatening if not treated promptly.

Synset Examples

a septicemic temperature curve

Related Terms

  • Synonym: septicemia - the presence of bacteria or their toxins in the blood

Etymology

From septicemia + -ic, where 'septicemia' comes from Greek 'sēptikos' (putrefying) and 'haima' (blood).
