Oleandra neriiformis

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈliː.ændrə nɛˈriː.ɪ.fɔːrmɪs / (AmE), / əʊˈliː.əndrə nɛˈrɪ.ɪ.fɔːmɪs / (BrE)
A species of tropical fern with leathery fronds resembling those of the oleander, native to regions from Asia to Polynesia.
a tropical fern species with leathery fronds

Examples

The leathery fronds of Oleandra neriiformis are often mistaken for those of the oleander plant.
Several botanic gardens cultivate Oleandra neriiformis due to its interesting appearance and resilience.
In tropical forests from Asia to Polynesia, Oleandra neriiformis thrives on rocky outcrops and tree trunks.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'oleandra' referring to oleander-like leaves; 'neriiformis' from Latin, meaning 'formed like Nerium (oleander)'.
