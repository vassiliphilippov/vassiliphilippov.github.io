copra

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːprə / (AmE), / ˈkɒprə / (BrE)
the dried kernel or meat of the coconut, from which coconut oil is extracted
dried coconut meat for oil extraction

Examples

The factory imports copra from several tropical countries to produce coconut oil.
After harvesting the coconuts, they spread the copra out to dry under the sun.
Exporting copra is an important source of income for many island communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coconut - the large brown seed of a tropical palm tree

Etymology

From Malayalam *koppara* (dried coconut), via Portuguese.
