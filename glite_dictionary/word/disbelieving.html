disbelieving

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪsbɪˈlivɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌdɪsbɪˈliːvɪŋ / (BrE)
expressing or showing a lack of belief; refusing to accept the truth or validity of something, especially religious tenets
showing lack of belief or skepticism

Examples

She gave him a disbelieving look when he told his outrageous story.
Many people in the audience appeared disbelieving of the speaker's claims.
His disbelieving attitude toward miracles set him apart from his peers.

Synset Examples

a skeptical approach to the nature of miracles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skeptical - doubting or questioning accepted beliefs
  • Antonym: credulous - willing to believe or trust too readily

Etymology

Formed from 'disbelieve' + '-ing'.
