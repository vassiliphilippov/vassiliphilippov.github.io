Trachinotus

1. NOUN

/ trəˈkɪnoʊtəs / (AmE), / trəˈkɪnətəs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Carangidae, which includes the pompanos and related species.
genus of marine fish including pompanos

Examples

Several species in the genus Trachinotus are prized as game fish by anglers.
The Florida pompano, a member of Trachinotus, is known for its delicate flavor.
Marine biologists studied the migration patterns of Trachinotus populations along the Atlantic coast.

Etymology

From Greek 'trachys' meaning 'rough' and 'notos' meaning 'back'.

/ trəˈkɪnəˌtʌs / (AmE), / trəˈkɪnətəs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Carangidae, known for species commonly called pompanos and jacks.
genus of fish: pompanos and jacks

Examples

The genus Trachinotus includes several species prized by anglers for their fighting ability.
Researchers observed a new behavior among juvenile Trachinotus in shallow coastal waters.
Some species within Trachinotus are important to both commercial and recreational fisheries.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'trachys' (rough) and 'notos' (back).
