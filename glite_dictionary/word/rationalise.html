to rationalise

1. VERB

/ ˈræʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈræʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
(Mathematics) To eliminate irrational numbers, especially roots, from the denominator of a fraction or from an expression.
remove irrational quantities (maths)

Examples

To simplify the denominator, you need to rationalise the expression.
In mathematics, students often learn how to rationalise fractions containing square roots.
The equation can be rationalised to make further calculations easier.

Synset Examples

This function can be rationalized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to simplify - make a mathematical expression easier or more straightforward

Etymology

From Latin *rationalis* (rational), via French *rationaliser*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00569956-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈræʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈræʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
to think logically and use reason when making decisions or solving problems
think logically; use reason

Examples

It is important to rationalise your options before making a major life decision.
She tried to rationalise her fears by looking for logical explanations.
If we rationalise this process, we can avoid unnecessary mistakes.

Synset Examples

When one wonders why one is doing certain things, one should rationalize

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reason - to think, understand, and form judgments logically
  • Antonym: emotionalize - to base judgments or actions on emotions rather than reason

Etymology

From Latin rationālis (rational), from ratio (reason).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00630919-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈræʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈræʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
to organise or run something, such as a business or system, according to rational or scientific principles, often to make it more efficient
organise a system or business efficiently

Examples

The company decided to rationalise its operations to cut unnecessary costs.
After being acquired, the new owners rationalised all the manufacturing processes.
The government is rationalising the healthcare system in an effort to improve efficiency.

Synset Examples

We rationalized the factory's production and raised profits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to streamline - make (an organization or system) more efficient and effective

Etymology

From Latin rationalis (of reason), via French rationaliser.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02274330-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈræʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈræʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
to remove or eliminate unnecessary things in order to make a system or process more efficient
remove inefficiencies or redundancies

Examples

The company decided to rationalise its operations by closing several underperforming branches.
To save costs, the hospital has begun to rationalise its inventory of medical supplies.
We need to rationalise our product line to focus on our most successful items.

Synset Examples

We had to lose weight, so we cut the sugar from our diet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to streamline - make an organization or system more efficient by removing unnecessary parts
  • Synonym: to simplify - make something simpler by removing complexity or unnecessary elements

Etymology

From Latin rationalis (rational) via French rationaliser.

5. VERB

/ ˈræʃəˌnɑlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈræʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
to attempt to explain or justify behavior or an attitude with logical reasons, even if these are not true or appropriate
justify or explain with reasons, often to excuse

Examples

She tried to rationalise her decision to quit her job by citing health concerns.
He often rationalises his mistakes instead of taking responsibility for them.
They rationalised their poor performance by blaming difficult market conditions.

Synset Examples

rationalize the child's seemingly crazy behavior
he rationalized his lack of success

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to justify - to show or prove to be right or reasonable
  • Synonym: excuse - to attempt to lessen blame attaching to a fault or offense

Etymology

From Latin 'rationalis' (of reason), via French 'rationaliser'.
