umbra

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌm.brə / (AmE), / ˈʌm.brə / (BrE)
The area of complete shadow cast by an opaque object, where all direct light is blocked.
region of total shadow

Examples

During the solar eclipse, the moon's umbra traveled across the Earth, creating a path of darkness.
The astronaut stood in the umbra of the spacecraft, shielded from the sun's glare.
Within the umbra of the tree, the temperature was noticeably cooler.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: penumbra - the partially shaded outer region of a shadow

Etymology

From Latin 'umbra' meaning 'shadow'.
