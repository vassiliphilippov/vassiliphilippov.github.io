to hypophysectomize

1. VERB

/ haɪˌpɑfəˈsɛktəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / hɪˌpɒfɪˈsɛktəmaɪz / (BrE)
to surgically remove the pituitary gland from the body
to remove the pituitary gland
medical

Examples

The surgeon had to hypophysectomize the patient as part of the treatment for the tumor.
In animal studies, researchers often hypophysectomize rats to study hormonal changes.
After the patient was hypophysectomized, hormone replacement therapy was initiated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ablate - to remove or destroy tissue surgically

Etymology

From 'hypophysis' (pituitary gland) + '-ectomize' (remove surgically), after 'hypophysectomy'.
