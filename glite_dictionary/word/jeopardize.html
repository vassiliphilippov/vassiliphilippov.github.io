to jeopardize

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒɛpərˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛpədaɪz / (BrE)
to put someone or something in a situation in which there is a danger of loss, harm, or failure
put at risk; endanger

Examples

Revealing his plans could jeopardize the entire operation.
She didn't want to jeopardize her friendship by telling the truth.
His careless remarks may jeopardize his chances of promotion.

Synset Examples

I will stake my good reputation for this

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to endanger - to put in danger; to expose to harm or risk
  • Antonym: to protect - to keep safe from harm or injury

Etymology

From late Middle English 'jeopard(e)' (danger, risk) plus '-ize'. Related to 'jeopardy'.

2. VERB

/ ˈdʒɛpərˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛpədaɪz / (BrE)
to put someone or something in a situation in which there is a risk of loss, harm, or danger
to put at risk or in danger

Examples

Cutting corners on safety can jeopardize the entire project.
He refused to jeopardize his reputation by taking part in the risky deal.
Pollution can jeopardize the health of local communities.

Synset Examples

The pollution is endangering the crops

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to endanger - to put someone or something in danger or at risk of harm

Etymology

From late Middle English 'jeopardie' (risk, danger) + '-ize'.
