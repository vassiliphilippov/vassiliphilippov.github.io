Sterninae

1. NOUN

/ stɜːrˈnɪni / (AmE), / stɜːˈnɪniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of birds in the family Laridae comprising the terns, typically slender, long-winged seabirds.
subfamily of birds; the terns

Examples

The Sterninae are well known for their aerial agility and long migratory journeys.
Several species within the Sterninae can be observed nesting along coastal regions worldwide.
Ornithologists have debated the proper classification of birds in the Sterninae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sterna - genus of seabirds known as terns

Etymology

From New Latin Sterninae, formed from genus name Sterna (tern) and the suffix -inae, denoting an animal subfamily.

2. NOUN

/ stɝːˈnaɪni / (AmE), / stɜːˈnaɪniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of birds in the family Laridae, commonly known as terns, characterized by slender bodies, long pointed wings, and forked tails.
subfamily of birds: terns

Examples

Members of the Sterninae are widely distributed across coastal regions worldwide.
Several conservation efforts target species within the Sterninae to protect their natural habitats.
The distinctive forked tail is a common feature of birds belonging to the Sterninae subfamily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tern - a slender seabird of the subfamily Sterninae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus name Sterna (tern).
