Carelian

1. NOUN

/ kəˈriːliən / (AmE), / kəˈrɪːliən / (BrE)
the Finnic language spoken by the people of Karelia, a region in Russia and Finland
Finnic language of Karelia

Examples

The Carelian language has several dialects unique to different parts of the Karelia region.
Linguists are working to preserve Carelian among younger generations in both Russia and Finland.
He can speak Finnish, Russian, and Carelian fluently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Karelian - the Finnic language spoken by Karelians; alternative spelling

Etymology

From Karelia, the region where the language is spoken.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈriːliən / (AmE), / kəˈriːliən / (BrE)
a member of a Finnish ethnic group native to Karelia, a region in northwestern European Russia and eastern Finland
member of a Finnish ethnic group in Karelia

Examples

Many Carelians maintain strong cultural traditions unique to their homeland.
The population of Carelians declined during the 20th century due to migration and war.
A group of Carelians gathered to celebrate their traditional festivities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Karelian - a member of a Finnish ethnic group in Karelia (alternative spelling)

Etymology

From Karelia, the name of the region, plus English suffix -ian.
