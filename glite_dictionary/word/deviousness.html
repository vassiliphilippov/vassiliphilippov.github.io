deviousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːviəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈdiːviəsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being willing to use dishonest or indirect methods to achieve something
quality of being deceitful or indirect

Examples

She admired his intelligence but distrusted his deviousness in business dealings.
The politician's deviousness eventually led to the scandal that ruined his career.
Stories of corporate deviousness often make headlines in the financial press.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and fair
  • Antonym: sincerity - the quality of being free from pretense or deceit

Etymology

From 'devious' + '-ness'; 'devious' from Latin 'deviare' (to turn aside).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdiː.vi.əs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdiː.vi.əs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being deceitful, indirect, or willing to use underhanded tactics to achieve a goal
quality of being deceitful or indirect

Examples

Her deviousness was apparent in the way she twisted the facts to suit her needs.
The negotiations were prolonged by the deviousness of both parties, who avoided giving clear answers.
He admired her intelligence but was wary of her deviousness in business dealings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and straightforward

Etymology

From 'devious', itself from Latin 'devius' (out of the way, remote).
