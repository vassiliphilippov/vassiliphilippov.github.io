Virginia Woolf

1. PROPN

/ vərˈdʒɪnjə wʊlf / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪnɪə wʊlf / (BrE)
(1882–1941) An English author known for her innovative narrative techniques, such as stream of consciousness and interior monologue, and as a leading figure in the Bloomsbury Group.
English novelist and essayist (1882–1941)

Examples

Many readers admire Virginia Woolf for her groundbreaking novel 'To the Lighthouse.'
The influence of Virginia Woolf is evident in much of modern literature.
She wrote her dissertation on the works of Virginia Woolf and her role in the Bloomsbury Group.

2. PROPN

/ vərˈdʒɪniə wʊlf / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪniə wʊlf / (BrE)
(1882–1941) English author known for pioneering modernist novels and literary techniques such as stream of consciousness, and a key figure in the Bloomsbury Group.
English modernist author (1882–1941)

Examples

Virginia Woolf explored new styles of storytelling in her groundbreaking novels.
Many literary scholars study the influence of Virginia Woolf on modernist fiction.
The life and works of Virginia Woolf are often discussed in university literature courses.
