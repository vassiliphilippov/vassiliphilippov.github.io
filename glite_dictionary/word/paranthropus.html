Paranthropus

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrænθrəpəs / (AmE), / pəˈranθrəpəs / (BrE)
a genus of extinct hominids, formerly classified as Australopithecus robustus, that lived in Africa during the Pleistocene epoch
genus of extinct hominids

Examples

Fossils belonging to Paranthropus have been found in southern and eastern Africa.
Some researchers argue that Paranthropus deserves its own genus separate from Australopithecus.
The robust skull features are characteristic of Paranthropus species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Australopithecus - genus of extinct hominids related to human ancestry

Etymology

From Greek 'para-' (beside) + 'anthrōpos' (man).
