Papio ursinus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪpi.oʊ ɜːrˈsaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌpæp.i.əʊ ɜːˈsaɪ.nəs / (BrE)
a greyish species of baboon found in southern and eastern Africa, also known as the chacma baboon
species of baboon in southern and eastern Africa

Examples

The Papio ursinus is the largest species of baboon in Africa.
Researchers observed a troop of Papio ursinus foraging near the river.
Due to habitat loss, the Papio ursinus population is under careful monitoring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baboon - large Old World monkey with a doglike snout

Etymology

From Latin: genus 'Papio' (baboon) and species 'ursinus' (bear-like), referring to its robust build.
