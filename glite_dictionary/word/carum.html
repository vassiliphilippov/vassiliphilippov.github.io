Carum

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrəm / (AmE), / ˈkɑːrəm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Apiaceae, including the caraway plant, cultivated for its aromatic seeds.
genus of plants including caraway

Examples

Research shows that Carum seeds are rich in essential oils, making them valuable in herbal medicine.
The chef added a pinch of Carum to the bread for a distinctive flavor.
Carum species are cultivated in many parts of Europe and Asia for their culinary uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caraway - a biennial plant of the same genus as Carum, known for its aromatic seeds

Etymology

From Latin 'carum', from Ancient Greek 'káron' (caraway).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɛərəm / (AmE), / ˈkɑːrəm / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the parsley family, including caraway and related species
plant genus including caraway

Examples

Researchers identified the plant as Carum in their botanical survey.
The seeds of Carum species are commonly used in European cuisine.
She studied various medicinal properties of Carum during her thesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caraway - an aromatic plant in the parsley family, Carum carvi, whose seeds are used as spice

Etymology

From Latin *carum*, the name for caraway.
