disciplinal

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɪplənl̩ / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪplɪnəl / (BrE)
intended to promote discipline or relating to the enforcement of discipline.
intended to promote discipline

Examples

The school implemented disciplinal measures to address disruptive behavior in the classroom.
Her parenting style was notably disciplinal, emphasizing structure and consistency.
The committee adopted a disciplinal approach to maintain order among its members.

Synset Examples

the teacher's action was corrective rather than instructional
disciplinal measures
the mother was stern and disciplinary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disciplinary - relating to discipline or used for enforcing discipline

Etymology

From Latin disciplina (instruction, discipline) + English suffix -al.
