forefoot

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːfʊt / (BrE)
the front foot of a four-footed animal
front foot of a quadruped

Examples

The horse injured its forefoot while running across the field.
Veterinarians often check the forefoot for signs of lameness in dogs.
A lion's forefoot is powerful and equipped with sharp claws for hunting.

Etymology

From Middle English; 'fore-' (meaning 'front') + 'foot'.
