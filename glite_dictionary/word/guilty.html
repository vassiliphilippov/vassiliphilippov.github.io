guilty

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡɪlti / (AmE), / ˈɡɪlti / (BrE)
showing or suggesting a feeling of guilt or responsibility for a wrongdoing
showing a feeling of guilt

Examples

She gave him a guilty look after breaking the vase.
He wore a guilty expression when asked about the missing cookies.
The child's eyes looked guilty as he stood by the mess.

Synset Examples

a guilty look
the hangdog and shamefaced air of the retreating enemy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ashamed - feeling embarrassment or guilt for something wrong done
  • Antonym: innocent - not guilty or not showing guilt

Etymology

From Old English *gyltig* (offending, guilty); related to *guilt*.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡɪlti / (AmE), / ˈɡɪlti / (BrE)
Responsible for committing a specified offense, crime, or wrong; deserving blame or punishment.
responsible for an offense or wrongdoing

Examples

The jury found him guilty of all charges after the lengthy trial.
She looked guilty when confronted about the missing documents.
Many people feel guilty for mistakes they made in the past.

Synset Examples

guilty of murder
the guilty person
secret guilty deeds

Related Terms

  • Antonym: innocent - not guilty of a crime or wrongdoing

Etymology

From Old English *gyltig* (offending, delinquent), related to *gylt* (guilt).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01323940-a
Synset POS: a
