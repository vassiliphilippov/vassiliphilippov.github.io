Buena Vista

1. PROPN

/ ˌbweɪnə ˈvɪstə / (AmE), / ˌbweɪnə ˈvɪstə / (BrE)
a battle during the Mexican-American War in 1847, where United States forces under Zachary Taylor defeated Mexican forces under Santa Anna in northern Mexico
battle in the Mexican-American War, 1847

Examples

The Battle of Buena Vista was a turning point in the Mexican-American War.
Historians often discuss the tactics used at Buena Vista by Zachary Taylor's army.
Santa Anna's defeat at Buena Vista had significant consequences for Mexico's military strategy.

Etymology

Spanish for 'good view', originally referring to the location in Mexico.
