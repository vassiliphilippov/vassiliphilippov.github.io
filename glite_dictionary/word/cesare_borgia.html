Cesare Borgia

1. PROPN

/ ˈtʃeɪzəreɪ ˈbɔrdʒə / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛzɑːreɪ ˈbɔːdʒə / (BrE)
(1475–1507) An Italian cardinal and military leader, often considered the model for Machiavelli's 'The Prince'.
Italian cardinal and military leader (1475–1507)

Examples

Machiavelli used Cesare Borgia as a key example in his treatise on political power.
Many historians consider Cesare Borgia a prototype of the ruthless Renaissance leader.
The rise and fall of Cesare Borgia continue to fascinate scholars and writers alike.

Etymology

From Italian Cesare Borgia, Italian noble family name.
