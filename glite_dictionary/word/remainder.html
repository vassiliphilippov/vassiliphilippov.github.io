remainder

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈmeɪndɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈmeɪndə / (BrE)
a piece of cloth or other material that is left over after the rest has been used or sold
piece of leftover material or cloth

Examples

The shop sold the remainder of the fabric roll at a discount.
She picked up a colorful remainder from the cloth store for her craft project.
Only a small remainder of blue velvet was left on the shelf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remnant - a small remaining quantity of something, especially fabric or cloth

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *remeindre* (to remain), from Latin *remansus*, past participle of *remanēre* (to remain).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈmeɪndər / (AmE), / rɪˈmeɪndə / (BrE)
the amount left after subtraction; in mathematics, the number that remains after subtracting one number from another, or that which, when added to the subtrahend, equals the minuend
amount left after subtraction

Examples

After dividing 17 by 5, the remainder is 2.
If you subtract 8 from 13, the remainder is 5.
Add the remainder to the subtrahend to check your answer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: difference - the result of subtracting one number from another

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *remaindre*, from Old French *remaindre* (to remain).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13751346-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈmeɪndər / (AmE), / rɪˈmeɪndə / (BrE)
the amount left over after division when one number does not divide another exactly
the amount left after division

Examples

When you divide 17 by 5, the remainder is 2.
The math teacher asked us to write down the remainder from each calculation.
If the remainder is zero, it means the number divides exactly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quotient - the result of division
  • Synonym: dividend - the number to be divided in division
  • Synonym: divisor - the number by which another number is divided

Etymology

From Latin remanere (to remain).

4. NOUN

/ rɪˈmeɪn.dɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈmeɪn.də / (BrE)
the part of something that is left after the other parts have been used, removed, or taken away
what is left after other parts are gone

Examples

After dividing the cake, only a small remainder was left for later.
The remainder of the day was spent reading and relaxing.
She donated most of her clothes and kept the remainder for herself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rest - the part that remains after the others are gone
  • Synonym: balance - the amount left over after other parts have been subtracted

5. VERB

/ rɪˈmeɪndər / (AmE), / rɪˈmeɪndə / (BrE)
(of a publisher) to sell off unsold copies of a book at a reduced price, usually as surplus stock.
sell surplus books cheaply

Examples

The publisher decided to remainder the unsold titles after a year in stores.
Copies of the novel were remaindered following disappointing sales.
Many bookshops sell remaindered stock at heavily discounted prices.

Synset Examples

The publisher remaindered the books
