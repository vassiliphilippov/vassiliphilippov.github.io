nontoxic

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈtɑksɪk / (AmE), / nɒnˈtɒksɪk / (BrE)
Not containing, producing, or resulting from poison; not harmful to living organisms.
not poisonous or harmful

Examples

All of the cleaning products in this store are labeled as nontoxic and safe for use around children.
The artist prefers to use nontoxic paints in all her work to avoid harmful fumes.
The plant produces a nontoxic substance that is harmless to pets.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: toxic - containing or being poisonous

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' (not) and 'toxic' (poisonous), from Latin 'toxicum' (poison).

2. ADJ

/ nɑnˈtɑksɪk / (AmE), / nɒnˈtɒksɪk / (BrE)
Not containing or producing substances that are poisonous or harmful; safe to eat, touch, or be exposed to.
not poisonous; safe to eat or touch

Examples

The company developed a nontoxic cleaner that is safe for both pets and children.
All the paints used in the classroom are completely nontoxic.
This new packaging is made from nontoxic materials and can be safely recycled.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: toxic - containing or producing poisonous substances; harmful

Etymology

From non- (not) + toxic (from Greek toxikos, meaning 'of or for a bow,' later 'poisoned').

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
