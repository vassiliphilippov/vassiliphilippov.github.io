downswing

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊnˌswɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌswɪŋ / (BrE)
the part of a golf swing in which the club is swung downward toward the ball after reaching its highest point
downward movement in a golf swing

Examples

Improving your downswing will help you achieve greater distance with your drives.
She practiced her downswing to improve accuracy on the golf course.
A quick downswing can lead to a slice if not controlled properly.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊnˌswɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdaʊn.swɪŋ / (BrE)
a period when business or economic activity becomes worse or decreases
a period of economic decline

Examples

The company struggled to survive the downswing that hit the industry in 2008.
Analysts are concerned about the recent downswing in the housing market.
Many small businesses closed during the severe downswing in economic activity.

Synset Examples

the market took a downturn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: downturn - a decline in economic, business, or other activity
  • Antonym: upturn - an improvement or upward trend in business or economic activity
