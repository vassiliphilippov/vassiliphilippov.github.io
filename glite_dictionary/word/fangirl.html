fangirl

1. NOUN

/ ˈfænˌɡɜrl / (AmE), / ˈfænˌɡɜːl / (BrE)
A female fan who is extremely enthusiastic and sometimes obsessively interested in a particular person, group, or field, especially in entertainment or pop culture.
female fan, highly enthusiastic (esp. pop culture)
informal

Examples

She became a total fangirl when she met her favorite actor at the convention.
The online forum is filled with fangirl debates about the latest comic book adaptation.
As a proud fangirl, she collects every piece of merchandise related to the band.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fanboy - a male fan who is obsessively enthusiastic about something

Etymology

Blend of 'fan' and 'girl', patterned after 'fanboy'; emerged in late 20th century pop culture.

2. VERB

/ ˈfænˌɡɝl / (AmE), / ˈfænˌɡɜːl / (BrE)
(informal) To behave in an overtly enthusiastic or obsessive way as a devoted fan, especially about celebrities, TV shows, or fictional characters.
act as an overenthusiastic devoted fan
informal

Examples

She couldn't help but fangirl over her favorite singer when she saw him backstage.
Every time a new episode is released, fans fangirl on social media for hours.
They totally fangirled when they met the cast at Comic-Con.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fanboy - to behave as an excessively devoted male fan

Etymology

From the noun 'fangirl', modeled after 'fan' + 'girl', with the verb sense developing in the early 21st century.
