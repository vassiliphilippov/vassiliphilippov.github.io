mirror

1. VERB

/ ˈmɪrər / (AmE), / ˈmɪrə / (BrE)
to reflect something, as a mirror does; to show or represent something clearly
reflect as a mirror does

Examples

The lake mirrored the stunning colors of the sunset perfectly.
Her expression mirrored his concern as they listened to the news.
Art often mirrors society by showing both its beauty and its flaws.

Synset Examples

The smallest pond at night mirrors the firmament above

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reflect - to throw back light, heat, or an image from a surface
  • Synonym: echo - to repeat or copy a sound or idea

Etymology

From Old French 'mirer' (to look at), from Latin 'mirari' (to wonder at).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02772883-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈmɪrɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɪrə / (BrE)
to reflect or closely resemble something
reflect or closely resemble

Examples

Her emotions mirrored the fear she saw in the crowd.
The company’s growth mirrors the expansion of the entire industry.
The novel mirrors real-life events that happened in the author’s hometown.

Synset Examples

The plane crash in Milan mirrored the attack in the World Trade Center

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reflect - to show an image of something, or to embody or represent something
  • Synonym: to imitate - to copy the actions or appearance of something or someone
  • Antonym: contrast - to be strikingly different from something

Etymology

From Old French *mirer* (to look at), from Latin *mirari* (to wonder at).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00823605-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɪrər / (AmE), / ˈmɪrə / (BrE)
something that accurately represents or reflects something else, especially in a faithful or true way.
faithful representation or reflection

Examples

History often serves as a mirror for society's values and beliefs.
His novels are a mirror of the times in which he lived.
A true friend can be a mirror for your character, reflecting your strengths and flaws.

Synset Examples

the best mirror is an old friend

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflection - an image, representation, or counterpart
  • Antonym: distortion - a representation that twists or misrepresents the facts

Etymology

From Old French 'mirour', from Latin 'mirari' (to wonder at).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05775242-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈmɪrɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɪrə / (BrE)
a smooth, highly polished surface, typically made of glass, that reflects images by bouncing light
reflective glass surface for images

Examples

She checked her hair in the mirror before leaving the house.
A full-length mirror hangs on the bedroom door.
The sunlight reflected off the mirror and brightened the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: looking glass - an old-fashioned term for a mirror

Etymology

From Old French 'mirour', from Vulgar Latin *miratorium, from Latin 'mirari' (to wonder at).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03778568-n
Synset POS: n
