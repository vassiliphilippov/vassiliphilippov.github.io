mussitation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌsɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmʌsɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
A low, continuous, indistinct sound resulting from murmuring, often with lip movements but without producing clear speech.
low indistinct murmuring sound
medical

Examples

The patient's mussitation during the examination made it difficult to determine if he was attempting to communicate.
In the dimly lit room, a constant mussitation could be heard from the corner where she sat.
Her mussitation was barely audible, the words lost before they formed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murmur - a low, continuous sound, especially of people speaking quietly

Etymology

From Latin mussitatio (a mumbling or murmuring).
