haziness

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪzi.nɪs / (AmE), / ˈheɪzɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being vague, unclear, or not sharply defined
vagueness or lack of clarity

Examples

There was a certain haziness in her explanation that left everyone confused.
The haziness of the plan made it difficult to make concrete decisions.
His memory of the incident was marked by haziness and uncertainty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vagueness - the quality of being unclear or indistinct
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear or easy to understand

Etymology

From 'hazy' + '-ness'; 'hazy' likely from Old English 'hes' (fog, mist).

2. NOUN

/ ˈheɪzinəs / (AmE), / ˈheɪzinəs / (BrE)
the state of being hazy, particularly due to haze, mist, or vapor that makes the air less clear
state of being hazy or cloudy

Examples

The haziness of the air made it hard to see the mountains in the distance.
Early morning haziness often lingers over the lake until the sun rises higher.
Through the haziness created by the mist, the city lights looked blurred and soft.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloudiness - the quality of being cloudy, especially in the sky
  • Antonym: clarity - the state of being clear, especially visually

Etymology

From "hazy" (origin uncertain, perhaps of Dutch or Low German origin) + "-ness".
