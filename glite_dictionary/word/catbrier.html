catbrier

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætˌbraɪər / (AmE), / ˈkat.brɪə / (BrE)
A very prickly, woody vine of the eastern United States, with tough round stems, shiny leathery leaves, small greenish flowers, and clusters of inedible shiny black berries. Also called greenbrier.
prickly vine of eastern U.S.; also greenbrier

Examples

The gardener wore gloves to avoid being scratched by the catbrier growing along the fence.
Hikers often encounter dense thickets of catbrier in the eastern woodlands.
After removing the catbrier, they discovered an old stone wall hidden beneath the tangled vines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greenbrier - another name for catbrier, a prickly vine
