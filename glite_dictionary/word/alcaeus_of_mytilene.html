Alcaeus of Mytilene

1. PROPN

/ ælˈsiːəs əv ˌmɪtɪˈliːni / (AmE), / ælˈsiːəs əv ˌmɪtɪˈliːni / (BrE)
a Greek lyric poet from Lesbos (circa 611–580 BC), known as the reputed inventor of Alcaic verse.
Greek lyric poet of Lesbos, c. 611–580 BC

Examples

Alcaeus of Mytilene was admired by later poets for his passionate verses and political themes.
Scholars often cite Alcaeus of Mytilene as the innovator of the Alcaic stanza.
The poetry of Alcaeus of Mytilene reflects the turbulent political life of ancient Lesbos.

Etymology

From ancient Greek Ἀλκαῖος (Alkaîos), from Mytilene on Lesbos.
