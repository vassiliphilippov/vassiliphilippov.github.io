cleanup

/ ˈklinˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈkliːnˌʌp / (BrE)

2. NOUN

/ ˈkliːnˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈkliːnʌp / (BrE)
the process or activity of making a place or object clean
the act of making something clean

Examples

After the party, the cleanup took nearly two hours.
The annual beach cleanup attracts hundreds of volunteers.
They organized a cleanup of the old playground last weekend.

Synset Examples

he gave his shoes a good cleaning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cleaning - the act of removing dirt or impurities

Etymology

From 'clean' + 'up' (phrasal verb), later nouned; first attested in late 19th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈklinˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈkliːnʌp / (BrE)
(Baseball) The fourth position in the batting order, typically assigned to the team's most powerful hitter.
fourth batter in baseball lineup

Examples

Batting cleanup, he hit a home run that cleared the bases.
The manager decided to move her into the cleanup spot for tonight's game.
He was proud to be chosen for the cleanup position after a strong start to the season.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: leadoff - the first batter in the batting order
