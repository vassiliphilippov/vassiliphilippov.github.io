brachiopodous

1. ADJ

/ ˌbrækiˈɑpədəs / (AmE), / ˌbrækiˈɒpədəs / (BrE)
Of or relating to the phylum Brachiopoda, a group of marine animals with bivalve shells.
relating to brachiopods (marine animals)

Examples

The fossil record contains many brachiopodous species from the Paleozoic era.
Researchers discovered a new brachiopodous organism off the Antarctic coast.
The museum's collection includes several brachiopodous shells dating back millions of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brachiopod - a marine animal of the phylum Brachiopoda

Etymology

From Modern Latin Brachiopoda (the animal phylum) + -ous (adjectival suffix).
