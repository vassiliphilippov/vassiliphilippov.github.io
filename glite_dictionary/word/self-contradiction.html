self-contradiction

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf.kɑːntrəˈdɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf.kɒntrəˈdɪkʃən / (BrE)
the act of making statements or holding beliefs that are inconsistent or in direct opposition to each other, especially within one's own words or thinking
the act of contradicting oneself

Examples

His argument was filled with self-contradiction and confusion.
The politician’s self-contradiction undermined his credibility.
She pointed out the self-contradiction in his explanation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contradiction - a statement or situation in which two or more things are opposed to each other

Etymology

From self- + contradiction, the latter from Latin contra (against) + dictio (saying).
