to G.I.

1. VERB

/ ˌdʒiːˈaɪ / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːˈaɪ / (BrE)
(of soldiers) To clean thoroughly in preparation for inspection, especially in a military context.
clean thoroughly for inspection (military)
informal american

Examples

Before the inspection, the squad had to G.I. the entire barracks from top to bottom.
The recruits G.I.ed their gear to meet the sergeant's strict standards.
We spent all morning G.I.-ing the mess hall until it sparkled.

Synset Examples

the soldiers GIed the barracks

Etymology

From the abbreviation 'G.I.' for 'Government Issue,' used to refer to U.S. soldiers and their equipment, later used as a verb within military slang.
