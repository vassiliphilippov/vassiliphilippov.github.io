innkeeper

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌkiːpər / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌkiːpə / (BrE)
a person who owns or manages an inn, often responsible for the day-to-day operation and hospitality of guests
person who owns or runs an inn

Examples

The innkeeper welcomed us warmly as we arrived late in the evening.
During the festival, the innkeeper was busy preparing rooms for all the guests.
She asked the innkeeper if breakfast would be served early the next day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landlord - person who owns or manages a rented property, especially an inn or pub

Etymology

From Middle English 'innkepere', from 'inn' + 'keeper'.
