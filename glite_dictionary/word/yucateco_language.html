Yucateco language

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːkəˈteɪkoʊ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌjuːkəˈteɪkəʊ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Mayan language spoken by the Yucatec people of the Yucatán Peninsula in Mexico and parts of Belize and Guatemala
Mayan language of the Yucatec people

Examples

Many ancient inscriptions in the Yucatán Peninsula are written in Yucateco language.
She is fluent in Spanish, English, and Yucateco language.
A dictionary of the Yucateco language was published to support language preservation efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mayan language - any of the languages of the Mayan family

Etymology

From Spanish 'Yucateco', meaning 'of Yucatán'.
