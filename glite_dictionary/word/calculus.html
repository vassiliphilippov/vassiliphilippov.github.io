calculus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælkjələs / (AmE), / ˈkælkjʊləs / (BrE)
the branch of mathematics that deals with limits, differentiation, and integration of functions
branch of mathematics involving limits and derivatives

Examples

Many students find calculus to be one of the most challenging courses in their first year of university.
Isaac Newton and Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz are credited with developing the foundations of calculus.
Understanding the basics of calculus is essential for careers in engineering and physical sciences.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: differential calculus - calculus dealing with rates of change and slopes of curves
  • Degree Higher: integral calculus - calculus dealing with accumulation and areas under curves
  • Degree Higher: mathematics - the abstract science of number, quantity, and space

Etymology

From Latin calculus (small stone used in reckoning), from calx (limestone).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkælkjələs / (AmE), / ˈkalkjʊləs / (BrE)
a hard, yellowish or whitish deposit that forms on the teeth and gums, commonly known as tartar
deposit on teeth and gums (tartar)
medical

Examples

The dentist found a layer of calculus on his lower teeth during the check-up.
Regular brushing and flossing can help prevent the build-up of dental calculus.
After removing the calculus, her teeth looked much cleaner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tartar - hard deposit that forms on teeth

Etymology

From Latin *calculus* meaning 'small stone', originally a diminutive of *calx* (lime).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkælkjələs / (AmE), / ˈkalkjʊləs / (BrE)
A hard mass formed by the accumulation of mineral salts, typically found in hollow organs or ducts of the body.
hard mass of mineral salts in the body
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a calculus in his kidney, which required surgical removal.
Gallstones are a common type of calculus that can develop in the gallbladder.
A urinary calculus may cause severe abdominal pain if it blocks the flow of urine.

Synset Examples

renal calculi can be very painful

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stone - a hard mass formed in the body, especially in the kidneys or gallbladder

Etymology

From Latin *calculus* (small stone, pebble).
