sewer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːɚ / (AmE), / ˈsjʊə, ˈsuːə / (BrE)
An underground pipe or channel that carries off waste water and sewage.
underground waste water pipe

Examples

The city is repairing the broken sewer under Main Street this week.
Heavy rain caused the sewer to overflow into the street.
Workers had to clear a blockage from the sewer beneath the hospital.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drain - a pipe or channel that carries away liquid waste

Etymology

From Old French *sewiere* (channel to drain overflow) or Medieval Latin *exaquaria*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊər / (AmE), / ˈsəʊə / (BrE)
a person who sews, especially one who makes or mends clothes by hand or machine
person who sews clothes
archaic

Examples

Maria is an accomplished sewer who creates beautiful wedding dresses.
In the past, every village had at least one skilled sewer to repair torn garments.
Although machines are common now, a hand sewer can add unique details to clothing.

Synset Examples

a sewer of fine gowns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tailor - a person whose occupation is making fitted clothes such as suits

Etymology

From Middle English *sewere*, from Old French *seweor*, from *sewir* (to sew).
