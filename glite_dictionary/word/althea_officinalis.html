Althea officinalis

1. NOUN

/ ælˈθiːə ˌɑːfɪˈsɪnəlɪs / (AmE), / ælˈθiːə ˌɒfɪˈsɪnælɪs / (BrE)
a European perennial herb with triangular, ovate leaves and lilac-pink flowers, commonly known as marsh mallow
a plant species, marsh mallow

Examples

The roots of Althea officinalis have traditionally been used to soothe sore throats.
Gardeners appreciate Althea officinalis for its delicate lilac-pink blossoms in summer.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of Althea officinalis extracts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marsh mallow - another common name for Althea officinalis

Etymology

From the genus name 'Althaea', from Greek 'althainein' (to heal), and Latin 'officinalis' (used in medicine).
