extemporarily

1. ADV

/ ɪkˌstɛmpəˈrɛrəli / (AmE), / ɪkˌstɛmpəˈrɛːrəli / (BrE)
in a manner done without prior preparation or planning; spontaneously or impromptu
without prior preparation; impromptu

Examples

The president delivered his address extemporarily, drawing admiration for his eloquence.
She answered the complex questions extemporarily and impressed the interview panel.
The band decided to play the final song extemporarily after requests from the audience.

Synset Examples

he spoke extemporaneously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improvisationally - in a manner that is improvised or created on the spot
  • Antonym: preparedly - with prior preparation or planning

Etymology

From Latin 'ex tempore' (out of the time), via 'extemporary', with the adverbial suffix '-ly'.
