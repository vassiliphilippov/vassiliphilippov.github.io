aged

1. ADJ

/ eɪdʒd / (AmE), / eɪdʒd / (BrE)
(of rock or land) at an advanced stage of erosion, having been worn down by natural processes over a long period.
worn by erosion (of rocks/land)

Examples

The aged rocks of the canyon have unique patterns from centuries of erosion.
Geologists examined the aged limestone formations for signs of past river currents.
An aged hillside reveals different layers of sediment deposited over millennia.

Synset Examples

2. ADJ

/ eɪdʒd / (AmE), / eɪdʒd / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) having reached or possessing a specified age; used before stating the number of years someone has lived.
having a specified age

Examples

The boy, aged ten, won first prize in the competition.
My grandmother adopted a cat aged three years.
Children aged five and under are admitted free.

Synset Examples

Etymology

From Old French *agé*, ultimately from Latin *aetaticum*, from *aetas* (age).
