legendary

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɛdʒənˌdɛri / (AmE), / ˈlɛdʒəndəri / (BrE)
very famous, admired, or talked about for a long time, often to the point of becoming part of legends or myths.
extremely famous; almost mythical

Examples

The legendary musician was known for his incredible performances and lasting influence.
Her legendary generosity inspired countless people in the community.
The legendary exploits of ancient heroes are still told today.

Synset Examples

the legendary exploits of the arctic trailblazers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mythical - existing only in myths or legends
  • Degree Lower: famous - known about by many people
  • Antonym: obscure - not well known

Etymology

From Medieval Latin legendarius (pertaining to a legend).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɛdʒənˌdɛri / (AmE), / ˈlɛdʒəndəri / (BrE)
Famous or celebrated in myths, stories, or legends; having the qualities of a legend.
famous in myths or legends

Examples

The legendary King Arthur is said to have pulled a sword from a stone.
Her bravery became legendary after rescuing the entire crew.
The legendary creature was said to haunt the nearby woods.

Synset Examples

the fabled Paul Bunyan and his blue ox
legendary exploits of Jesse James

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fabled - famous especially by myth or legend
  • Synonym: mythical - existing only in myths
  • Antonym: real - actually existing; not imagined or mythical

Etymology

From Medieval Latin legendarius (relating to legends), from legere (to read).

Synset POS: s
