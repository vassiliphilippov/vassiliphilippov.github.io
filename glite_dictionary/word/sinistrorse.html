sinistrorse

1. ADJ

/ sɪˈnɪstrɔrs / (AmE), / sɪˈnɪstrɔːs / (BrE)
(of a plant or its parts) spiraling or twisting upward from right to left, especially said of stems or vines.
spiraling upward from right to left (botany)

Examples

Some morning glory varieties have sinistrorse stems that twine upward from right to left.
The botanist explained how sinistrorse twining distinguishes certain species of vines.
It can be fascinating to observe the sinistrorse growth patterns on these climbing plants.

Synset Examples

sinistrorse vines

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dextrorse - spiraling upward from left to right (botany)

Etymology

From Latin 'sinistrorsum', from 'sinister' (left) + '-orsum' (toward).
