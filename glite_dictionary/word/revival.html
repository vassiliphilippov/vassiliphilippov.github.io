revival

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈvaɪvəl / (AmE), / rɪˈvaɪvəl / (BrE)
a Christian religious meeting held to inspire renewed interest in faith, typically featuring preaching, prayer, and enthusiastic worship
an evangelistic religious meeting

Examples

The church hosted a week-long revival to reignite spiritual passion among its members.
Hundreds attended the outdoor revival led by the charismatic preacher.
That summer, he found his faith renewed after participating in a lively revival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crusade - a vigorous campaign or movement for a cause, often religious

Etymology

From revive + -al; 'revive' from Latin revivere, meaning 'to live again'.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈvaɪvəl / (AmE), / rɪˈvaɪvəl / (BrE)
an instance of something becoming active, important, or popular again after a period of decline
the act of bringing back into activity

Examples

The city saw a cultural revival as galleries and music venues opened downtown.
There has been a revival of interest in classic literature.
The theater company announced a revival of Shakespeare's play for the new season.

Synset Examples

the revival of trade
a revival of a neglected play by Moliere
the Gothic revival in architecture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resurgence - an increase or revival after a period of little activity
  • Antonym: decline - the process of becoming weaker or less active

Etymology

From Latin 'revivere' (to live again), via Old French 'revivre'.
