unobtrusiveness

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnəbˈtruːsɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˌʌnəbˈtruːsɪvniːs / (BrE)
the quality of not attracting attention or not being noticeable in an unwelcome or inappropriate way
quality of being discreet or not noticeable

Examples

Her unobtrusiveness allowed her to observe the group without being noticed.
The unobtrusiveness of the security system makes it ideal for homes that value discretion.
He admired the unobtrusiveness of the waiter, who never interrupted their conversation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discreetness - the quality of being careful and not attracting attention
  • Antonym: noticeability - the quality of being easily seen or attracting attention

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + obtrusive + suffix -ness, meaning the state of not being obtrusive.
