coralbells

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrəlˌbɛlz / (AmE), / ˈkɒrəlˌbɛlz / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to the western United States, known for its bright red feathery flower spikes, often grown as an ornamental.
perennial plant with red feathery flowers

Examples

The garden was full of coralbells, their bright blooms catching the morning sun.
Many gardeners plant coralbells for a splash of red color in shady spots.
She bought a pot of coralbells to add to her perennial border.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heuchera - Genus of plants commonly called coralbells
