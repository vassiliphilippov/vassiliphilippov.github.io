Josue

1. PROPN

/ ʒoʊˈsuː / (AmE), / ʒɒˈsjuː / (BrE)
The name for the Book of Joshua in some versions of the Bible, particularly in translations or references influenced by Romance languages.
variant title of the Book of Joshua

Examples

In the Vulgate, the Book of Joshua is referred to as Josue.
Many Spanish Bibles use the title Josue for this book of the Old Testament.
The themes of conquest and faith are central in Josue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Joshua - the sixth book of the Old Testament, describing Joshua's leadership after Moses

Etymology

From Latin 'Josue', from Hebrew 'Yehoshua', via Greek 'Iēsous'.
