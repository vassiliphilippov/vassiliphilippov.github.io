to expend

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈspɛnd / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛnd / (BrE)
to use or spend resources such as money, time, or energy for a particular purpose
use or spend resources

Examples

The company will expend a large sum on research and development this year.
She refused to expend any more energy on a hopeless project.
Before making a big purchase, consider how much you are willing to expend.

Synset Examples

spend money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spend - to use money to pay for something
  • Antonym: conserve - to avoid using something to save it for later

Etymology

From Latin expendere (to weigh out, pay out, spend), from ex- (out) + pendere (to weigh).

2. VERB

/ ɪkˈspɛnd / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛnd / (BrE)
to use or spend resources such as energy, time, or money, especially for a particular purpose
use or spend resources

Examples

You shouldn't expend all your energy at the start of the race.
The company will expend significant funds on the new project this year.
She tried not to expend more effort than necessary on the assignment.

Synset Examples

this car consumes a lot of gas
We exhausted our savings
They run through 20 bottles of wine a week

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spend - to pay out money; to use up time, effort, or resources
  • Synonym: to consume - to use up a resource or substance
  • Antonym: conserve - to protect from loss or depletion; to use sparingly

Etymology

From Latin expendere, meaning 'to weigh out, pay out.'
