Sir Joseph John Thomson

1. PROPN

/ sər ˈdʒoʊzəf dʒɑn ˈtɑmsən / (AmE), / sə ˈdʒəʊzɪf dʒɒn ˈtɒmsən / (BrE)
(1856–1940) An English physicist noted for discovering the electron and measuring its charge and mass, and for his experiments with the conduction of electricity through gases.
English physicist who discovered the electron

Examples

Sir Joseph John Thomson was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1906 for his groundbreaking research on electrons.
Many physics textbooks discuss the cathode ray experiments of Sir Joseph John Thomson.
The discovery made by Sir Joseph John Thomson reshaped the understanding of atomic structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electron - a subatomic particle with a negative electric charge
