Chaenopsis

1. NOUN

/ kiːˈnɑːp.sɪs / (AmE), / kiːˈnɒp.sɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small marine fish in the family Chaenopsidae, commonly known as pikeblennies.
genus of pikeblenny fish

Examples

The Chaenopsis species are often found sheltering in abandoned wormholes in the coral reef.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Chaenopsis genus in the Caribbean Sea.
Many aquarists find Chaenopsis fish fascinating due to their unusual gaping mouths.

Etymology

From Greek 'chainein' (to gape) + 'opsis' (appearance).

2. NOUN

/ kiˈnɑːp.sɪs / (AmE), / kiˈnɒp.sɪs / (BrE)
A genus of fishes in the family Chaenopsidae that includes the pikeblennies.
genus of pikeblenny fishes

Examples

The Chaenopsis genus is best known for the distinctive pikeblenny species.
Marine biologists discovered a new member of the Chaenopsis genus off the Pacific coast.
Many Chaenopsis species inhabit burrows in sandy substrates.

Etymology

From Greek 'chainein' (to gape) + 'opsis' (appearance), referring to the appearance of the fish.
