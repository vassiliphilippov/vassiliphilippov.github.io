Dermestidae

1. NOUN

/ dɚˈmɛstəˌdi / (AmE), / dɜːˈmɛstɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of beetles, commonly known as carpet beetles, whose larvae feed on dry animal or plant matter such as wool, fur, or stored food
family of beetles known as carpet beetles

Examples

The larvae of Dermestidae can cause significant damage to carpets and clothing stored in homes.
Many museums take precautions against Dermestidae to protect their textile collections.
Adult Dermestidae are often found near windows, attracted by light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carpet beetle - a common name for beetles in the Dermestidae family

Etymology

From Greek 'derma' meaning 'skin' and 'esthio' meaning 'to eat', referring to their feeding habits.

2. NOUN

/ dərˈmɛstəˌdi / (AmE), / dɜːˈmɛstɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of beetles commonly known as carpet beetles, whose larvae feed on animal fibers and are pests of carpets, stored products, and museum specimens.
a family of beetles called carpet beetles

Examples

The larvae of Dermestidae can cause significant damage to woollen fabrics.
Museums must take precautions against infestations of Dermestidae in their collections.
Several species within the Dermestidae family are notorious for invading stored food products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carpet beetle - the common name for several species of beetles in the family Dermestidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek dermēstēs (skin-eater), from derma (skin) + edein (to eat).
