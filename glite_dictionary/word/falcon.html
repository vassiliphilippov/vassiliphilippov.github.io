falcon

1. VERB

/ ˈfælkən / (AmE), / ˈfɔːlkən / (BrE)
to hunt using trained falcons or other birds of prey
hunt with trained birds of prey

Examples

The desert nomads would often falcon during the cooler months.
She spent her weekends learning how to falcon with her prized birds.
It was a tradition in that region to falcon at dawn.

Synset Examples

The tribes like to falcon in the desert

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawk - to hunt with hawks

Etymology

From Old French 'faucon', ultimately from Late Latin 'falco'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01148232-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈfælkən, ˈfɔːlkən / (AmE), / ˈfɔːlkən, ˈfælkən / (BrE)
A diurnal bird of prey with long pointed wings, known for its swift flight and keen hunting skills.
a swift-flying bird of prey

Examples

The peregrine falcon is known as the fastest animal in the world when in a dive.
Trained falcons were used for hunting in medieval Europe.
We watched a falcon soar high above the cliffs, searching for prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawk - a bird of prey similar to a falcon but with broader wings
  • Synonym: eagle - a large bird of prey with a massive hooked bill and long broad wings

Etymology

From Old French *faucon*, from Late Latin *falco* (falcon).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01613596-n
Synset POS: n
