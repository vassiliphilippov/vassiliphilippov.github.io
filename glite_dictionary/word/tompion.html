tompion

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɒmpiən / (AmE), / ˈtɒmpiən / (BrE)
A plug placed in the muzzle of a firearm or cannon to keep out dust and moisture.
plug for gun muzzle to keep out dust

Examples

During the inspection, the officer noticed that the tompion was missing from the cannon.
Old naval cannons were often protected by a wooden tompion when not in use.
A metal tompion helps to prevent water and debris from entering the barrel of the field gun.

Etymology

From French 'tampion', from Dutch 'tampion' (plug, stopper).
