emphatically

1. ADV

/ ɛmˈfætɪkli / (AmE), / ɪmˈfætɪkli / (BrE)
without any doubt; in a forceful or definite way
forcefully, without doubt

Examples

She emphatically denied any involvement in the scandal during the press conference.
The teacher emphatically stated that cheating would not be tolerated in her classroom.
He emphatically supports the new policy and urges others to do the same.

Synset Examples

it was decidedly too expensive
she told him off in spades
by all odds they should win

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decidedly - without hesitation or doubt; definitely
  • Antonym: doubtfully - in a way that shows uncertainty or lack of conviction

Etymology

From Greek emphatikos (significant, forceful) via Late Latin emphaticus and English 'emphatic'.
