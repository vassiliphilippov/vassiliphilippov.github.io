duckweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌkˌwid / (AmE), / ˈdʌk.wiːd / (BrE)
a tiny floating aquatic plant of the family Lemnaceae, often forming dense, green mats on the surface of still or slow-moving water
tiny floating aquatic plant

Examples

Tiny duckweed covered the surface of the old pond, creating a green carpet.
You can often see frogs resting on floating duckweed in quiet lakes.
The researcher collected samples of duckweed for the water quality study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waterweed - another type of aquatic plant, usually submerged
