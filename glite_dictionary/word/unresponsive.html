unresponsive

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.rɪˈspɑn.sɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌn.rɪˈspɒn.sɪv / (BrE)
not reacting or responding to influence, stimulus, or communication.
not reacting to influence or stimulus

Examples

After the accident, the patient was unresponsive to all attempts to wake her.
The manager was unresponsive to employees' requests for flexible schedules.
His phone remained unresponsive despite several resets and updates.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: responsive - reacting quickly or positively to something

Etymology

Formed from the prefix un- (not) + responsive.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.rɪˈspɑn.sɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌn.rɪˈspɒn.sɪv / (BrE)
not reacting or showing interest, especially in an aloof or indifferent manner.
not showing interest or reaction

Examples

Despite her persistent questions, he remained unresponsive throughout the discussion.
She grew frustrated with his unresponsive attitude during the meeting.
The child was unresponsive to the teacher's attempts to encourage participation.

Synset Examples

was unresponsive to her passionate advances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indifferent - having no particular interest or sympathy; unconcerned
  • Synonym: aloof - not friendly or forthcoming; cool and distant
  • Antonym: responsive - reacting quickly and positively

Etymology

From un- (not) + responsive (from Latin responsivus, from respondere ‘to answer’).

3. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.rɪˈspɑn.sɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌn.rɪˈspɒn.sɪv / (BrE)
not reacting or susceptible to suggestions, requests, or influence.
not reacting to influence or suggestion

Examples

Despite repeated requests, the committee remained unresponsive to feedback from the community.
He is completely unresponsive to peer pressure and stands by his decisions.
The management was criticized for being unresponsive to the needs of the employees.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: responsive - reacting quickly and positively; easily influenced or affected

Etymology

From un- (not) + responsive, from Latin respondere (to answer).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02372915-s
Synset POS: s
