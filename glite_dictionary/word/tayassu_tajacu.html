Tayassu tajacu

1. NOUN

/ taɪˈæs.u taɪˈdʒɑː.ku / (AmE), / taɪˈæs.u taɪˈdʒɑː.ku / (BrE)
A species of peccary found in semi-desert areas of Mexico and the southwestern United States, characterized by a dark grey body and an indistinct white collar.
species of wild peccary

Examples

The Tayassu tajacu is often seen foraging in small groups across the arid regions of Arizona.
Hunters must acquire special permits to track Tayassu tajacu in Mexico's protected areas.
Researchers observed the migratory patterns of Tayassu tajacu throughout the desert habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collared peccary - common name for Tayassu tajacu, a wild pig-like mammal of the Americas

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Tayassu' refers to the genus of peccaries and 'tajacu' is derived from indigenous Tupi language of Brazil, meaning a kind of small wild pig.
