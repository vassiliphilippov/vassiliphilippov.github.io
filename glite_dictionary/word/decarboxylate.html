to decarboxylate

1. VERB

/ ˌdiː.kɑrˈbɑk.sɪˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌdiː.kɑːˈbɒk.sɪ.leɪt / (BrE)
(of a compound) to remove or lose a carboxyl group, usually as carbon dioxide, in a chemical reaction.
remove a carboxyl group (chemistry)
medical

Examples

When heated, this acid will decarboxylate and release carbon dioxide gas.
Several enzymes in the body can decarboxylate amino acids during metabolism.
Once you decarboxylate the compound, it becomes biologically active.

Synset Examples

the compound decarboxylated

Etymology

From de- (removal) + carboxyl (the chemical group -COOH) + -ate (verb-forming suffix).

2. VERB

/ ˌdiː.kɑːrˈbɑːk.səˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌdiː.kɑːˈbɒk.sɪ.leɪt / (BrE)
to remove a carboxyl group (–COOH) from a chemical compound, especially in a chemical reaction
remove carboxyl group from a compound
medical

Examples

Certain enzymes can decarboxylate amino acids during metabolism.
When heated, the compound will decarboxylate, producing carbon dioxide gas.
Scientists decarboxylated the molecule to study its effect on cell function.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to carboxylate - to add a carboxyl group to a chemical compound

Etymology

From de- (removal) + carboxyl (chemical group) + -ate (verb-forming suffix).
