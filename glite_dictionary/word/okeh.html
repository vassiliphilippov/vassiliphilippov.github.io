okeh

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊˈkeɪ / (AmE), / ˌəʊˈkeɪ / (BrE)
An endorsement, acceptance, or approval, especially as a formal or official sign-off.
endorsement, approval (archaic)
archaic american

Examples

The manager gave his okeh to start the new project.
We can't proceed without the director's okeh.
The contract was signed after receiving the official okeh.

Synset Examples

they gave us the O.K. to go ahead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: OK - acceptance or approval; authorization (as a noun)

Etymology

Early 20th century American English, a variant spelling of 'OK', possibly influenced by Choctaw 'okeh' meaning 'it is so'.
