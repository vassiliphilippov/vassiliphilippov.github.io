chinkapin

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪŋkəˌpɪn / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪŋkəˌpɪn / (BrE)
A small, edible nut from either of two species of chestnut tree native to the southern United States, resembling a hazelnut.
small nut from a southern US chestnut tree

Examples

The harvest of chinkapin nuts was a favorite autumn activity for local families.
I found several chinkapin shells scattered beneath the old tree.
Roasting chinkapin nuts brings out their sweet, nutty flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chinquapin - Alternate spelling: nut from southern US chestnut trees

Etymology

Variant of 'chinquapin', from Virginia Algonquian *chechequamin*.
