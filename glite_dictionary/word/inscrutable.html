inscrutable

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈskrutəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈskruːtəb(ə)l / (BrE)
impossible to understand or interpret; mysterious or unfathomable in nature or expression.
impossible to understand; mysterious

Examples

Her face remained inscrutable, giving no hint of her true feelings.
The outcome of the negotiation was as inscrutable as ever, leaving everyone guessing.
He found the ancient text inscrutable, filled with symbols he could not decipher.

Synset Examples

the new insurance policy is written without cryptic or mysterious terms
a deep dark secret
the inscrutable workings of Providence
in its mysterious past it encompasses all the dim origins of life
rituals totally mystifying to visitors from other lands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mysterious - difficult or impossible to understand, explain, or identify
  • Antonym: transparent - easy to perceive or detect; obvious, clear

Etymology

From Late Latin inscrutabilis, from in- (not) + scrutabilis (able to be examined).
