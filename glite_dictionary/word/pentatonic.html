pentatonic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɛn.təˈtɑ.nɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɛn.təˈtɒ.nɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or based on a scale of five notes per octave, especially used in music.
relating to a five-note scale (music)

Examples

Many traditional folk melodies are written in a pentatonic scale.
The song uses a simple pentatonic pattern that is easy for beginners to play.
Her compositions often feature the pentatonic mode found in East Asian music.

Etymology

From Greek 'pente' (five) + 'tonos' (tone, sound).
