fetich

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːtɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈfiːtɪʃ / (BrE)
A charm or object believed, especially by certain cultures or groups, to have magical powers or to embody a spirit.
object believed to have magical powers

Examples

Many African tribes were once said to worship a fetich as a protective symbol.
The explorers were shown a curious fetich made from animal bones and feathers.
He wore a small fetich around his neck for good luck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fetish - an object believed to have magical powers; more common variant spelling

Etymology

Variant of 'fetish', from French *fétiche*, from Portuguese *feitiço* (artificial, charm), from Latin *facticius* (artificial).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfiːtɪʃ, ˈfɛtɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈfiːtɪʃ, ˈfɛtɪʃ / (BrE)
An object or activity to which one shows excessive or irrational devotion.
object of irrational devotion
archaic

Examples

She seemed to have made a fetich of punctuality in her daily routine.
For him, order was more than a habit—it became a fetich.
Their strict dietary rules were elevated to the status of a fetich among the members.

Synset Examples

made a fetish of cleanliness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fetish - object or activity showing excessive or irrational devotion; standard spelling

Etymology

From French 'fétiche', from Portuguese 'feitiço' (charm, sorcery), from Latin 'facticius' (artificial).
