reconciled

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛkənˌsaɪld / (AmE), / ˈrɛkənˌsaɪld / (BrE)
Having been made compatible or consistent after a disagreement, difference, or conflict.
made compatible or consistent

Examples

After much discussion, the two theories were finally reconciled.
The family became reconciled after years of estrangement.
His beliefs were not easily reconciled with his actions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irreconcilable - unable to be made compatible or consistent
  • Synonym: harmonized - made consistent or brought into agreement

Etymology

From Medieval Latin reconciliare (to bring together again).
