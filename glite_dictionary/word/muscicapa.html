Muscicapa

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌsɪˈkeɪpə / (AmE), / ˌmʌsɪˈkɑːpə / (BrE)
A genus of small passerine birds known as Old World flycatchers, the type genus of the family Muscicapidae.
genus of Old World flycatchers

Examples

The familiar spotted flycatcher belongs to the genus Muscicapa.
Muscicapa species are widespread throughout Europe, Asia, and Africa.
Ornithologists debated whether a new specimen should be classified under Muscicapa.

Etymology

From Latin 'muscicapa', meaning 'flycatcher', from 'musca' (fly) + 'capere' (to catch).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmʌsɪˈkɑːpə / (AmE), / ˌmʌsɪˈkæpə / (BrE)
A genus of small insect-eating birds in the family Muscicapidae, commonly known as flycatchers.
genus of small flycatcher birds

Examples

Several species within the genus Muscicapa migrate long distances each year.
The spotted flycatcher, a member of the Muscicapa genus, is common throughout Europe.
Ornithologists discovered a new Muscicapa species in Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Muscicapidae - the family of birds containing the Old World flycatchers

Etymology

From New Latin, from Latin musca (fly) + capere (to catch).
