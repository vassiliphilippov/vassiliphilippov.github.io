synchroflash

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋkroʊˌflæʃ / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkrəʊˌflæʃ / (BrE)
a device used in photography to synchronize the timing of a flash with the opening of the camera shutter
device that syncs flash with camera shutter

Examples

Many vintage cameras require a synchroflash unit to properly match the flash with the shutter release.
The photographer installed a synchroflash to ensure accurate exposure in the studio.
A malfunctioning synchroflash can result in poorly lit or completely dark photographs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synchronizer - a device for synchronizing the operation of two apparatuses

Etymology

From 'synchronization' and 'flash'; relates to synchronization of camera flash timing.
