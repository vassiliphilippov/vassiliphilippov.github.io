Nigella sativa

1. NOUN

/ naɪˈdʒɛl.ə səˈtiː.və / (AmE), / nɪˈdʒɛl.ə səˈtiː.və / (BrE)
A plant of the buttercup family, native to the Mediterranean, whose pungent seeds are used as a spice similar to caraway.
Mediterranean herb; seeds used as spice

Examples

Nigella sativa seeds are often sprinkled on flatbreads in Middle Eastern cuisine.
Many traditional remedies include Nigella sativa for its supposed health benefits.
The chef used Nigella sativa to add a distinct flavor to the vegetable stew.

Etymology

From Latin, literally 'black cumin,' with 'Nigella' from 'niger' meaning black, and 'sativa' meaning sown or cultivated.
