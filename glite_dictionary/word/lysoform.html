Lysoform

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪsəˌfɔrm / (AmE), / ˈlaɪsəˌfɔːm / (BrE)
A disinfectant composed of a mixture of liquid soap and formaldehyde, used as an antiseptic.
antiseptic mixture of soap and formaldehyde

Examples

The laboratory was disinfected using Lysoform before any procedures began.
Historically, hospitals relied on Lysoform to maintain sterile environments.
It's important to handle Lysoform carefully due to its formaldehyde content.

Etymology

From Greek *lysis* (loosening, dissolution) and Latin *forma* (form), originally a trademarked name.
