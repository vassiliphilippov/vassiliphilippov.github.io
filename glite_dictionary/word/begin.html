to begin

1. VERB

/ bɪˈɡɪn / (AmE), / bɪˈɡɪn / (BrE)
to start doing or experiencing something, especially an activity or event characterized by the object involved
start doing or experiencing something

Examples

She began her speech with a humorous story to relax the audience.
We began dinner as soon as everyone arrived.
He began a new book during his vacation.

Synset Examples

begin a cigar
She started the soup while it was still hot
We started physics in 10th grade

Related Terms

  • Synonym: start - to take the first step or action in an activity

Etymology

From Old English 'beginnan', combining 'be-' (about) and 'ginnan' (to begin).
