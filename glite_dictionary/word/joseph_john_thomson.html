Joseph John Thomson

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒoʊzəf dʒɑn ˈtɑmsən / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊzɪf dʒɒn ˈtɒmsən / (BrE)
(1856–1940) English physicist who discovered the electron and determined its charge and mass.
English physicist; discoverer of the electron

Examples

Joseph John Thomson was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1906 for his contributions to the understanding of electrons.
The discovery of the electron by Joseph John Thomson revolutionized the field of physics and chemistry.
Many textbooks highlight Joseph John Thomson's groundbreaking experiments on the conduction of electricity through gases.
