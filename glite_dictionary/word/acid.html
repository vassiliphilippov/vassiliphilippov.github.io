4. NOUN

/ ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a chemical substance that typically has a sour taste, turns litmus red, reacts with bases to form salts, and is water-soluble
a substance with sour taste, reacts with bases

Examples

Lemon juice is an acid that can make your mouth pucker because of its sour taste.
In chemistry class, we learned how an acid reacts with a base to form a salt.
You should handle this acid carefully, as it can cause skin irritation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: base - a substance that reacts with an acid to form a salt
  • Antonym: alkali - a type of base, especially one that is soluble and forms hydroxide ions in water
  • Degree Lower: neutral - describes a solution that is neither acidic nor basic

Etymology

From Latin acidus (sour, sharp), from acēre (to be sour).
