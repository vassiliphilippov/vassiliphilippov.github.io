passim

1. ADV

/ ˈpæs.ɪm / (AmE), / ˈpæs.ɪm / (BrE)
used in books and articles to indicate that something occurs in various places throughout the cited work, rather than in just one place.
occurring at various places in a cited work

Examples

References to this theme appear passim throughout the novel and are not limited to one chapter.
The author's use of irony is evident passim in his collected essays.
You will find allusions to this doctrine passim in the legal commentary.

Etymology

From Latin 'passim', meaning 'here and there' or 'throughout'.
