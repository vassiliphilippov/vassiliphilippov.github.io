confederate

1. ADJ

/ kənˈfɛd.ər.ət / (AmE), / kənˈfɛd.ər.ət / (BrE)
(of persons, groups, or states) united in a confederacy or league for a common purpose.
united in a confederacy or league

Examples

The confederate states worked together to achieve their political goals.
Several confederate tribes formed an alliance to defend their territory.
The confederate nations met annually to discuss mutual interests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allied - joined or united in alliance
  • Synonym: federated - formed into a single group or federation
  • Antonym: independent - not united or governed by others; acting alone

Etymology

From Latin *confoederatus*, past participle of *confoederare* ‘to unite by a league’.

2. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛdərət / (AmE), / kənˈfɛdərət / (BrE)
a person who works together with others in a common purpose, especially in secret or for an unethical or illegal plan; an accomplice.
accomplice; partner in wrongdoing

Examples

The thief escaped with the help of a confederate waiting outside.
She acted as a confederate in the elaborate scheme to defraud investors.
Police discovered that one of the suspects had a confederate inside the company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accomplice - a person who helps another commit a crime
  • Synonym: conspirator - a person who takes part in a conspiracy

Etymology

From Latin 'confoederatus', past participle of 'confoederare' (to unite by a league).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ kənˈfɛdərˌeɪt / (AmE), / kənˈfɛdərəɪt / (BrE)
to join together in a confederation, especially of nations or states, for a common purpose or alliance
join together in a confederation

Examples

Several small states decided to confederate for mutual defense against larger neighbors.
If these regions confederate, they could form a powerful economic bloc.
The provinces had confederated under a single federal government by the end of the decade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ally - to unite or join with another for a common purpose

Etymology

From Latin *confoederatus*, past participle of *confoederare* (to unite by a league), from *con-* (together) + *foedus* (league).
