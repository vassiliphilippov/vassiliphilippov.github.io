strict

1. ADJ

/ strɪkt / (AmE), / strɪkt / (BrE)
(of rules or laws) enforced or adhered to in a way that allows for no exceptions or flexibility.
enforced without exceptions (rules, laws)

Examples

All employees must follow the company's strict safety protocols at all times.
The school operates under strict rules regarding attendance and punctuality.
There is a strict no-smoking policy inside the building.

Synset Examples

hard-and-fast rules

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rigorous - (of rules or procedures) extremely thorough and precise

Etymology

From Latin strictus, past participle of stringere meaning 'to draw tight'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02516312-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ strɪkt / (AmE), / strɪkt / (BrE)
Unable or unwilling to change rules or make exceptions; showing little tolerance for deviation or flexibility.
rigid; not willing to allow exceptions

Examples

My parents are very strict about my curfew and never allow me to come home late.
The strict rules at the boarding school left little room for personal expression.
He was known as a strict manager who never tolerated any delays or excuses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rigid - unable to bend or adapt; inflexible
  • Antonym: lenient - permissive; allowing more freedom

Etymology

From Latin strictus (drawn tight, rigid), past participle of stringere (to draw tight).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02446199-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ strɪkt / (AmE), / strɪkt / (BrE)
enforcing rigid accuracy or exactness; allowing no deviation from a rule or standard.
rigidly accurate or precise

Examples

The teacher follows a strict grading policy to ensure fairness for all students.
He keeps a strict diet and never allows himself to break the rules.
Admission to the event is by strict invitation only, with no exceptions.

Synset Examples

rigorous application of the law
a strict vegetarian

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rigorous - extremely thorough and accurate
  • Antonym: lenient - permitting more freedom; not strict

Etymology

From Latin strictus (drawn tight), past participle of stringere (to draw tight, bind).

4. ADJ

/ strɪkt / (AmE), / strɪkt / (BrE)
demanding that rules or standards are closely followed, or showing little tolerance for deviation or relaxation; severe or rigorous.
demanding close obedience; severe

Examples

My parents were very strict about bedtime when I was a child.
Ms. Lee is a strict teacher who expects students to follow all the rules.
The hospital enforces strict hygiene protocols in the operating rooms.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lenient - permissive, not strict or harsh

Etymology

From Latin 'strictus' (drawn tight, rigid), past participle of 'stringere' (to draw tight).

5. ADJ

/ strɪkt / (AmE), / strɪkt / (BrE)
demanding that rules or standards are followed exactly and with little tolerance for deviation; uncompromisingly severe in discipline or requirements.
demanding rigid adherence to rules or standards

Examples

The school has strict rules about student behavior and dress code.
Her father was strict, insisting on curfews and regular chores.
You must meet these strict standards to be accepted into the program.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stern - serious and unrelenting in discipline or punishment
  • Synonym: exacting - making great demands on one's skill, attention, or resources; demanding

Etymology

From Latin 'strictus' (drawn tight, tight, rigid), past participle of 'stringere' (to draw tight).
