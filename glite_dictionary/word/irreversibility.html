irreversibility

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɪvərsəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪːvɜːsəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The fact or condition of being impossible to reverse or undo; the quality of a process or action that cannot be changed back to its original state.
quality of being impossible to reverse

Examples

The irreversibility of climate change is a major global concern.
Many chemical reactions in nature are marked by their irreversibility.
Surgical removal of organs often carries the consequence of irreversibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irreversible - impossible to reverse or undo
  • Antonym: reversibility - quality of being able to be reversed or undone

Etymology

From 'irreversible' + '-ity', modeled on French 'irréversibilité'.
