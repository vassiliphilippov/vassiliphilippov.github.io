cannoneer

1. NOUN

/ ˌkænəˈnɪr / (AmE), / ˌkænəˈnɪə / (BrE)
a person who operates a cannon, especially as a member of the artillery in a military force
person who operates a cannon

Examples

The cannoneer adjusted the aim of the artillery piece before giving the signal to fire.
During the reenactment, each cannoneer wore an authentic uniform from the 18th century.
It took several cannoneers to operate the massive cannons on the old warships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gunner - a soldier who operates a gun or artillery piece
  • Synonym: artilleryman - a soldier serving in an artillery unit

Etymology

From cannon + -eer (patterned after French -ier, agent suffix).
