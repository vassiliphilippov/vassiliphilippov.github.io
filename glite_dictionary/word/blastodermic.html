blastodermic

1. ADJ

/ ˌblæstəˈdɜrmɪk / (AmE), / ˌblæstəˈdɜːmɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of a blastoderm (the layer of cells forming the outer layer of a blastula in early embryonic development).
relating to a blastoderm (embryology)
medical

Examples

The blastodermic layer gives rise to major tissues during embryonic development.
Researchers studied blastodermic cell formation in early chick embryos.
Abnormalities in the blastodermic structure can lead to developmental disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blastoderm - The layer of cells forming the outer layer of a blastula

Etymology

From 'blastoderm' (from Greek 'blastos' meaning 'germ, bud' and 'derma' meaning 'skin') plus the suffix '-ic'.
