neoclassicism

1. NOUN

/ ˌniː.oʊˈklæs.ɪˌsɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌniː.əʊˈklæs.ɪ.sɪ.zəm / (BrE)
A style in art, literature, architecture, or music characterized by a revival of classical forms and themes, typically from ancient Greece or Rome, often interpreted with a new perspective or motivation.
revival of classical style in the arts

Examples

The rise of neoclassicism in the 18th century brought renewed interest in ancient Greek and Roman art.
Her music is inspired by neoclassicism, blending modern rhythms with classical forms.
The museum's new exhibit showcases the evolution of neoclassicism in architecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classicism - adherence to classical principles in art or literature
  • Antonym: romanticism - a movement emphasizing inspiration and emotion over classical form

Etymology

From neo- (new) and classicism, itself from Latin classicus (of the highest class).
