Agropyron repens

1. NOUN

/ əˌɡroʊˈpaɪrɒn rɪˈpɛnz / (AmE), / əˌɡrɒˈpaɪrɒn rɪˈpɛnz / (BrE)
A European grass species that spreads rapidly by creeping rhizomes and is considered a weed in North America.
species of European grass; persistent weed

Examples

Agropyron repens can quickly take over gardens, making it difficult for other plants to thrive.
Farmers often struggle to control Agropyron repens in their fields due to its tough, spreading roots.
The invasive nature of Agropyron repens has made it a significant weed problem across North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: couch grass - common name for Agropyron repens
  • Synonym: quackgrass - another common name for Agropyron repens

Etymology

From Greek agros (field) + pyros (wheat); Latin repens (creeping).
