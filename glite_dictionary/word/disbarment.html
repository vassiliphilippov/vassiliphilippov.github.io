disbarment

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈbɑrmənt / (AmE), / dɪsˈbɑːmənt / (BrE)
the official act of expelling a lawyer from the legal profession, preventing them from practicing law
expulsion of a lawyer from the bar
legal

Examples

The attorney faced disbarment after being found guilty of professional misconduct.
His disbarment marked the end of a long and distinguished legal career.
The process of disbarment is typically initiated by a complaint to the bar association.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disbar - to expel a lawyer from the bar

Etymology

From prefix dis- (expressing reversal) + bar (the legal profession) + -ment (noun suffix).
