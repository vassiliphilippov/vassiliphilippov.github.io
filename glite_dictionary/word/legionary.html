legionary

1. NOUN

/ ˈlidʒəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˈliːdʒənəri / (BrE)
a soldier who is a member of a legion, especially the French Foreign Legion or a Roman legion
member of a legion (soldier)

Examples

The legionary marched alongside his fellow soldiers under the hot desert sun.
She read about the life of a legionary in ancient Rome.
After years as a legionary, he returned home with many stories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legionnaire - a member of the French Foreign Legion

Etymology

From Latin legionarius (of a legion), from legio (legion).
