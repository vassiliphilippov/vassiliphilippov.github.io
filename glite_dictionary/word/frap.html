to frap

1. VERB

/ fræp / (AmE), / fræp / (BrE)
to make secure by tying firmly, especially with rope or cord; to bind tightly by lashing
secure by tying tightly with rope

Examples

After the storm, the sailors had to frap the loose sails to prevent damage.
You should frap the cargo before the ship leaves port to keep it from shifting.
The crew quickly frapped the bundles with sturdy ropes during rough seas.

Synset Examples

frap a sail

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lash - to tie or bind with a rope or cord
  • Antonym: to loosen - to make less tight or secure

Etymology

Probably from French *frapper* (to strike or fasten), related to nautical uses.

2. VERB

/ fræp / (AmE), / fræp / (BrE)
to tighten or secure (something, especially a rope or cable) by drawing it taut or binding it tightly
tighten or bind tightly (esp. a rope)

Examples

Before lifting the load, be sure to frap the ropes to prevent slipping.
The deckhand quickly frapped the line around the post for extra security.
Experienced climbers know how to frap their gear for maximum safety.

Synset Examples

frap a rope

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tighten - to make or become tight or tighter

Etymology

Probably from French 'frapper' (to strike), used in nautical sense for binding or tightening.

Source: WordNet
