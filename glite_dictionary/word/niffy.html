niffy

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɪfi / (AmE), / ˈnɪfi / (BrE)
(British informal) Having a strong, unpleasant smell; malodorous.
having an unpleasant smell (British informal)
informal british

Examples

I had to throw out the niffy cheese from the fridge.
Her gym bag was suspiciously niffy after a week without washing.
The old trainers in the hallway were particularly niffy in the summer heat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smelly - having a strong or unpleasant odor
  • Antonym: fragrant - having a pleasant or sweet smell

2. ADJ

/ ˈnɪfi / (AmE), / ˈnɪfi / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly British) Slightly chilly or cool, especially as of the weather.
slightly chilly or cool (informal)
informal british

Examples

By late afternoon, the air had grown quite niffy and everyone reached for their jackets.
It always gets niffy by the lake after sunset, even in summer.
The breeze made the evening unexpectedly niffy at the outdoor concert.

Synset Examples

The weather turned niffy as the sun set.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chilly - uncomfortably cool in temperature
