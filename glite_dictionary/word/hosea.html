Hosea

1. PROPN

/ hoʊˈziːə / (AmE), / həʊˈziːə / (BrE)
A Hebrew prophet of the 8th century BC, who called for repentance among the people of Israel and condemned idolatry; also, the title of a book in the Old Testament named after him.
Hebrew prophet; Old Testament book

Examples

Many scholars study the book of Hosea to understand the history of ancient Israel.
Hosea warned the Israelites about the consequences of idolatry.
In Sunday school, we learned about the prophet Hosea and his messages of repentance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Joel - another minor prophet of the Old Testament
  • Synonym: Obadiah - another minor prophet of the Old Testament

Etymology

From Hebrew Hōšēa‘, meaning 'salvation'.

2. PROPN

/ hoʊˈziːə / (AmE), / həʊˈziːə / (BrE)
a book of the Old Testament containing the prophecies attributed to the prophet Hosea
Old Testament prophetic book

Examples

Many scholars study the themes of love and faithfulness found in Hosea.
The Book of Hosea is part of the collection known as the Minor Prophets.
During the sermon, the pastor quoted several verses from Hosea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Amos - an Old Testament prophetic book
  • Synonym: Joel - an Old Testament prophetic book
  • Synonym: Micah - an Old Testament prophetic book

Etymology

From Hebrew Hōšēaʿ, the name of the prophet.
