easterly

1. ADJ

/ ˈistərli / (AmE), / ˈiːstəli / (BrE)
(of a wind or direction) coming from the east.
(of wind) from the east

Examples

A strong easterly wind battered the coastline overnight.
The weather forecast predicts easterly breezes tomorrow afternoon.
Sailing was difficult due to the persistent easterly current.

Synset Examples

an eastern wind
the winds are easterly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: westerly - (of a wind or direction) coming from the west

Etymology

Formed from 'east' + '-erly'; pattern as with 'westerly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00827233-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈistɚli / (AmE), / ˈiːstəli / (BrE)
A wind blowing from the east.
wind from the east

Examples

A strong easterly blew across the coastline, bringing cooler air to the region.
Fishermen delayed their departure because a persistent easterly was making the seas rough.
The crops suffered after several days of a dry easterly sweeping through the valley.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: westerly - wind from the west
  • Antonym: northerly - wind from the north
  • Antonym: southerly - wind from the south

Etymology

From 'east' + '-erly', denoting direction or origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-11468680-n
Synset POS: n

situated in or toward the east

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms
