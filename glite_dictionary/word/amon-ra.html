Amon-Ra

1. PROPN

/ ˌɑːmənˈrɑː / (AmE), / ˌɑːmənˈrɑː / (BrE)
An Egyptian sun god, regarded as the supreme deity of the universe in whom the gods Amen and Ra were merged, especially venerated during the period of Theban supremacy.
Egyptian sun god, supreme deity

Examples

The temple was dedicated to Amon-Ra, the most powerful god in ancient Egypt.
Priests of Amon-Ra played a central role in Theban society.
Legends describe Amon-Ra as the creator and ruler of all gods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ra - ancient Egyptian sun god; major deity
  • Synonym: Amun - ancient Egyptian god; merged with Ra to form Amon-Ra

Etymology

From Egyptian, merging 'Amun' (Amen) and 'Ra', two major deities in ancient Egyptian religion.
