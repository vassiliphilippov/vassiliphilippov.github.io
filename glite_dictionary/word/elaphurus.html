Elaphurus

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlæfjʊrəs / (AmE), / ɪˈlæfjʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of deer in the family Cervidae, known especially for Pere David's deer.
genus of deer (Cervidae)

Examples

The Elaphurus genus includes the rare and fascinating Pere David's deer.
Scientists have studied Elaphurus to understand the evolutionary history of deer species.
Recent conservation efforts have focused on populations within the Elaphurus genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cervidae - the biological family of deer

Etymology

From Greek elaphos (deer).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈlæf.jʊər.əs / (AmE), / ɪˈlæfjʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of deer in the family Cervidae, notable for including the extinct Père David's deer.
a genus of deer (Cervidae)

Examples

The extinct Père David's deer is the only living member of the genus Elaphurus.
Scientists have studied fossils belonging to Elaphurus to understand its evolutionary history.
The genus Elaphurus is of great interest to zoologists focusing on cervids.

Etymology

From Greek elaphos (deer) + -urus (tail), referring to characteristics of the deer.
