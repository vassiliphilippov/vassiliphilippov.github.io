Mesembryanthemum edule

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛzɛmˌbraɪænˌθɪməm ˈɛdjuːl / (AmE), / ˌmɛsɛmˌbrɪænˌθɛməm ˈɛdjuːl / (BrE)
A low-growing South African succulent plant with a fleshy fruit containing edible pulp, also known as the Hottentot fig or sour fig.
South African succulent with edible fruit pulp

Examples

The sandy dunes were covered in Mesembryanthemum edule, adding a splash of green and purple to the landscape.
Local markets often sell the tangy fruit of Mesembryanthemum edule as a regional delicacy.
Hikers are advised not to eat unknown plants, but Mesembryanthemum edule is known to be safe and edible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hottentot fig - common name for Mesembryanthemum edule

Etymology

From Greek 'mesēmbria' (noon) and 'anthemon' (flower), referencing plants that open their flowers at midday; 'edule' from Latin 'edulis' (edible).
