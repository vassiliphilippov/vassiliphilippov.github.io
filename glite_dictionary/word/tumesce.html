to tumesce

1. VERB

/ tuˈmɛs / (AmE), / tjuˈmɛs / (BrE)
(formal) To swell or become enlarged, especially abnormally or as a result of fluid accumulation.
to swell or become enlarged

Examples

Medical conditions can cause tissues to tumesce rapidly and painfully.
After the allergic reaction, her lips began to tumesce visibly.
The botanist observed how the stem would tumesce after heavy rainfall.

Synset Examples

The bellies of the starving children are swelling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swell - to increase in size or volume, usually as a result of internal pressure
  • Antonym: to deflate - to reduce or let out the air or gas from; to shrink or reduce in size

Etymology

From Latin tumescere, meaning 'to swell up.'
