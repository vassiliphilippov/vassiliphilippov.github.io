to coerce

1. VERB

/ koʊˈɜrs / (AmE), / kəʊˈɜːs / (BrE)
to make someone do something by using force or threats
compel by force or threat

Examples

He tried to coerce her into signing the contract against her will.
The rebels coerced villagers to provide them with supplies.
No one should be coerced into making a confession.

Synset Examples

She forced him to take a job in the city
He squeezed her for information

Related Terms

  • Synonym: force - to make someone do something against their will
  • Synonym: to compel - to pressure or drive someone to do something
  • Antonym: to persuade - to convince someone to do something through reasoning or discussion

Etymology

From Latin 'coercere' (to restrain, confine), from 'co-' (together) + 'arcere' (to enclose, confine).
