mace

1. NOUN

/ meɪs / (AmE), / meɪs / (BrE)
a ceremonial staff carried as a symbol of authority or office, often used in formal events or by officials
ceremonial staff of authority

Examples

The university's chancellor carried the mace during the graduation ceremony.
The ornate mace is kept in a special case at the city hall.
Traditionally, the mace signifies the speaker's authority in parliament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sceptre - a staff held by a sovereign as a symbol of authority

Etymology

From Old French 'macier', from Vulgar Latin *mattea, from Latin 'mateola' (a mallet or club).

2. NOUN

/ meɪs / (AmE), / meɪs / (BrE)
an official whose duty is to carry a ceremonial mace as a symbol of authority in formal events or legislative assemblies
official carrying a ceremonial mace

Examples

During the ceremony, the mace led the procession into the hall.
The mace took his position at the front of the parliamentary session.
In full regalia, the mace walked ahead of the dignitaries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macebearer - person who carries a ceremonial mace

Etymology

From Middle English 'masse', from Old French 'mace' meaning 'club, scepter'.

3. NOUN

/ meɪs / (AmE), / meɪs / (BrE)
A spice made from the dried, lacy covering (aril) of the nutmeg seed, used for its aromatic flavor in cooking and baking.
spice from covering of nutmeg seed

Examples

The recipe calls for a pinch of mace to enhance the flavor of the sauce.
Ground mace is often used in desserts and baked goods for its warm, aromatic taste.
She sprinkled a little mace into the stew, giving it a subtle nutty aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nutmeg - spice made from the seed of the nutmeg tree

Etymology

From Middle English 'macis', from Old French, from Latin 'macis', from Greek 'makirē'.
