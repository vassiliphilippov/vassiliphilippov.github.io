dais

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪəs / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ.ɪs / (BrE)
A raised platform at the front of a room, used for important speakers or guests to stand or sit on.
raised platform for speakers or guests

Examples

The mayor stood on the dais to address the audience at the ceremony.
The award winners were seated together on the dais in front of the crowd.
A microphone had been set up on the dais for the guest speakers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: podium - a small platform for a speaker or conductor

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman and Old French *dais* (table, platform), from Latin *discus* (disk, plate).
