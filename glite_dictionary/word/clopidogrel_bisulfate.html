clopidogrel bisulfate

1. NOUN

/ kləˌpɪdəˌɡrɛl baɪˈsʌl.feɪt / (AmE), / kləˌpɪdəˌɡrɛl baɪˈsʌl.feɪt / (BrE)
A medication used to prevent blood clots by inhibiting platelets, commonly prescribed to reduce the risk of heart attack and stroke.
blood-thinning medication
medical

Examples

After his heart attack, the doctor prescribed clopidogrel bisulfate to reduce the risk of another clot.
Patients taking clopidogrel bisulfate should avoid activities that increase their risk of bleeding.
The nurse explained how clopidogrel bisulfate works to help prevent strokes.
