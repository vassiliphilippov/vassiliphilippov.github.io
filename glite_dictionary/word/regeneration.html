regeneration

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌdʒɛnəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌdʒɛnəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of being formed again, especially with improvements or the removal of defects; renewal or reconstitution
the act of renewing or restoring

Examples

The city's economic regeneration has attracted new businesses and created jobs.
The lizard's ability for limb regeneration impressed the biologists.
After the fire, the forest showed signs of regeneration within a few months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: renewal - the act of renewing or being renewed
  • Synonym: restoration - the act of restoring something to its former condition

Etymology

From Latin regeneratio (renewal, rebirth), from regenerare (to bring forth again).

regrowth of tissue or organs (biology)
medical

Examples

Scientists are studying the regeneration of nerve cells to help treat spinal injuries.
The regeneration of a starfish's arm can take several months.

Related Terms

4. NOUN

/ rɪˌdʒɛnəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌdʒɛnəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
(technical) The process in which feedback is in phase with and augments the input, especially in electronics or signal processing.
feedback in phase with input (technical)

Examples

The regeneration in the amplifier circuit increases the overall signal strength.
By adjusting the amount of regeneration, the radio operator enhanced reception clarity.
Certain oscillators use regeneration to maintain continuous wave generation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: positive feedback - feedback that increases the amplitude of the input signal
  • Antonym: negative feedback - feedback that diminishes the input signal

Etymology

From Latin regeneratio (a renewing), from regenerare (to regenerate).
