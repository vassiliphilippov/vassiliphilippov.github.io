Abrocoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌæ.brəˈkoʊ.mə / (AmE), / ˌabrəˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
A genus of South American rodents known as chinchilla rats, typically found in the Andes.
genus of South American chinchilla rats

Examples

The Abrocoma is native to the rocky slopes of the Andes Mountains.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the genus Abrocoma.
Due to their soft fur, some Abrocoma species face threats from trapping.

Etymology

From Greek abros (delicate) + kome (hair); refers to their soft fur.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæbrəˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌabrəˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
A genus of rodents known as chinchilla rats, native to South America, forming the type genus of the family Abrocomidae.
a genus of South American chinchilla rats

Examples

The Abrocoma genus includes several species of chinchilla rats found in the Andes.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Abrocoma genus during the expedition.
The soft fur of Abrocoma rodents is highly valued among mammalogists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chinchilla rat - a common name for rodents in the genus Abrocoma

Etymology

From Greek abros (delicate, soft) + kome (hair), referring to the soft fur of the animals.
