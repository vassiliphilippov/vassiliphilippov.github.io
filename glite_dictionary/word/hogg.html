hogg

1. NOUN

/ hɑɡ / (AmE), / hɒɡ / (BrE)
a young sheep up to the age of one year, especially one that has not yet been sheared
young sheep under one year

Examples

The farmer separated the hoggs from the rest of the flock in the spring.
Each hogg will be sheared for the first time next month.
The shepherd counted the hoggs before leading them to fresh pasture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lamb - a young sheep, especially less than one year old

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse *hoggr* (castrated animal).
