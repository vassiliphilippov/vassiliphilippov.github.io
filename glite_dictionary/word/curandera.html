curandera

1. NOUN

/ kuɾɑnˈdɛɾə / (AmE), / kjʊərænˈdɛərə / (BrE)
A female traditional healer in Mexican or Latin American cultures who uses folk remedies and spiritual healing methods.
female folk healer in Latin American cultures

Examples

The village curandera was known for her herbal remedies and spiritual cleansings.
After falling ill, Maria visited a curandera for traditional healing.
Some families rely on a curandera rather than a doctor for minor ailments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curandero - male traditional healer in Mexican/Latin American cultures
  • Synonym: shaman - a person regarded as having access to, and influence in, the world of spirits, especially among some peoples of northern Asia and North America

Etymology

From Spanish 'curandera', feminine of 'curandero' (healer), from 'curar' (to heal).
