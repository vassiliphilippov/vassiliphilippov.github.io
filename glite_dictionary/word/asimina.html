Asimina

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɪmɪnə / (AmE), / əˈsɪmɪnə / (BrE)
A small genus of North American trees and shrubs, including the pawpaw, bearing large edible fruits.
genus of North American trees (pawpaw)

Examples

The Asimina tree produces large, sweet pawpaw fruits in late summer.
Many botanists study different species within the Asimina genus for their unique characteristics.
Native Americans traditionally used the fruit of Asimina triloba as a food source.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pawpaw - an edible fruit and the common name for Asimina triloba

Etymology

From a Latinized form of a North American native term, influenced by American English.

2. NOUN

/ əˈsɪmɪnə / (AmE), / əˈsɪmɪnə / (BrE)
A genus of small trees or shrubs in the custard apple family (Annonaceae), native to North America and known for producing the pawpaw fruit.
genus of North American trees producing pawpaw

Examples

The Asimina genus is notable for producing the largest edible fruit native to North America.
Many wildlife species feed on the fruit of Asimina trees each autumn.
Botanists study Asimina to understand its unique tolerance to cold climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pawpaw - the edible fruit produced by trees of the genus Asimina

Etymology

From a Native American (possibly Powhatan) word for the pawpaw fruit.
