pretentiously

1. ADV

/ prɪˈtɛnʃəsli / (AmE), / prɪˈtɛnʃəsli / (BrE)
In a way that attempts to impress others by affecting greater importance or merit than is actually possessed.
in a pretentious manner

Examples

The critic accused the film of being pretentiously artistic without real substance.
She pretentiously quoted obscure philosophers at every opportunity to impress her classmates.
The restaurant is decorated pretentiously, with gold trim and elaborate chandeliers.

Synset Examples

this author writes pretentiously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretentious - attempting to impress by pretending greater importance or knowledge
  • Antonym: modestly - in a modest manner, without pretension or showiness
