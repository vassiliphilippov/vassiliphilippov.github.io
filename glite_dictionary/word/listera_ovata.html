Listera ovata

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪstərə oʊˈveɪtə / (AmE), / ˈlɪstəɹə əʊˈvɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of orchid with two broad, oval leaves and a long spike of greenish flowers, native to Europe and central Asia.
a species of orchid with oval leaves

Examples

The forest floor was carpeted with Listera ovata in early summer.
Botanists recorded several specimens of Listera ovata near the woodland edge.
Listera ovata is easily recognized by its distinctive pair of broad leaves.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, after the genus Listera, named for Martin Lister (1639–1712), an English naturalist, and Latin 'ovata' meaning 'egg-shaped, ovate'.
