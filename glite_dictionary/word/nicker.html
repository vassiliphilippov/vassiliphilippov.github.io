nicker

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪkɚ / (AmE), / ˈnɪkə / (BrE)
the soft, throaty sound made by a horse, often as a greeting or sign of anticipation
a horse's soft, throaty sound

Examples

The horse gave a friendly nicker when it saw its owner approach.
Early in the morning, the soft nicker of the mare filled the stable.
As I entered the barn, I was greeted by the gentle nicker of several horses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neigh - a loud, high-pitched sound made by a horse

2. VERB

/ ˈnɪkər / (AmE), / ˈnɪkə / (BrE)
(of a horse) to make a soft, characteristic neighing or whinnying sound.
(of a horse) make a soft neigh

Examples

The horse nickered softly when it saw its owner approaching.
As she entered the stable, several colts began nickering in anticipation of food.
He loves the way his old mare nickers whenever he calls her name.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neigh - (of a horse) to make a loud, characteristic whinnying sound
  • Synonym: whinny - (of a horse) to make a gentle, high-pitched neigh

3. NOUN

/ ˈnɪkər / (AmE), / ˈnɪkə / (BrE)
a soft, low sound made by a horse, usually as a greeting or in anticipation of food
soft, low horse sound

Examples

The gentle nicker of the stallion greeted her every morning.
As she approached, the horse gave a low, welcoming nicker.
The stable fell quiet except for the soft nicker of a foal missing its mother.

Synset Examples

She heard the nicker of the stallion from the stable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neigh - a loud, high-pitched sound made by a horse

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound itself; first recorded in the 17th century.
