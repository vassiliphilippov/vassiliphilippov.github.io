vociferator

1. NOUN

/ voʊˈsɪf.əˌreɪ.tər / (AmE), / vəʊˈsɪf.əˌreɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person who speaks or protests loudly and with strong emotion
person who speaks loudly and vehemently

Examples

The mayor grew irritated by the constant interruptions from the vociferator in the front row.
As a leading vociferator for climate action, she was known for her impassioned speeches.
Security intervened when a group of vociferators began to drown out the speaker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vociferate - to speak or shout loudly

Etymology

From Latin 'vociferari' (to shout, yell), via English 'vociferate' + '-or'.
