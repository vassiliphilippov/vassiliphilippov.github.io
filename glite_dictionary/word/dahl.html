dahl

1. NOUN

/ dɑl / (AmE), / dɑːl / (BrE)
A tropical woody herb with showy yellow flowers and flat pods, widely cultivated in tropical regions.
tropical cultivated woody herb

Examples

The farmers planted rows of dahl to take advantage of the warm climate.
In some regions, dahl is grown primarily for its attractive yellow flowers.
Local markets often sell the edible pods of the dahl plant.

2. NOUN

/ dɑl / (AmE), / dɑːl / (BrE)
the small, highly nutritious seed of the tropical pigeon-pea plant, often used in South Asian cooking
pigeon-pea seed used in cooking

Examples

The chef added a handful of dahl to the spicy stew.
Many vegetarian dishes in India use dahl as a primary protein source.
After boiling the dahl, she seasoned it with cumin and turmeric.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lentil - small edible pulse in the legume family
  • Synonym: pulse - edible seed of various leguminous plants

Etymology

From Hindi 'dāl' (split pulse), ultimately from Sanskrit 'dal' (split).
