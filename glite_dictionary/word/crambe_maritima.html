Crambe maritima

1. NOUN

/ ˌkræm.bi məˈrɪ.t̬ə.mə / (AmE), / ˌkræm.bi məˈrɪ.tɪ.mə / (BrE)
a perennial plant of coastal areas in northern Europe, with large fleshy blue-green leaves and white flowers, commonly known as sea kale and often used as a vegetable
sea kale, coastal perennial plant

Examples

The young shoots of Crambe maritima are harvested in spring and eaten as a delicacy.
Crambe maritima thrives best on well-drained sandy soils along the coastline.
Gardeners in Britain often cultivate Crambe maritima for its edible leaves and attractive appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea kale - common name for Crambe maritima

Etymology

From Latin crambe (cabbage) and maritima (of the sea/coast).
