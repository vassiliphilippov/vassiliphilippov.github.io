Cercidiphyllaceae

1. NOUN

/ sərˌsɪdəˌfɪləˈsiː.i / (AmE), / sɜːˌsɪdəˌfɪləˈsiː.i / (BrE)
A small family of flowering plants consisting of a single genus, Cercidiphyllum, native to eastern Asia.
plant family with one genus, Cercidiphyllum

Examples

The Cercidiphyllaceae are primarily represented by the katsura tree in Japan and China.
In botanical classifications, Cercidiphyllaceae is recognized as a distinct family.
Researchers are studying the evolutionary history of the Cercidiphyllaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cercidiphyllum - the sole genus in the Cercidiphyllaceae family

Etymology

From Greek *kerkis* (weaver's shuttle) and *phyllon* (leaf), referring to the genus Cercidiphyllum, with the standard botanical family suffix -aceae.
