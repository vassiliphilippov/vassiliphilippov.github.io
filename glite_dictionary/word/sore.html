sore

1. NOUN

/ sɔr / (AmE), / sɔː / (BrE)
An area of skin that is infected, inflamed, or ulcerated, often causing pain or discomfort.
an area of infected or ulcerated skin

Examples

He had a painful sore on his leg that wouldn't heal.
The doctor treated the sore with an antibiotic cream.
Wearing tight shoes gave her a sore on her heel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ulcer - an open sore on the skin or a mucous membrane
  • Synonym: wound - an injury to body tissue, typically involving skin breakage

Etymology

Old English sār (painful, wound), related to Old High German sēr (pain, sore).
