Carex pseudocyperus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛrɛks ˌsjuːdoʊˈsaɪpərəs / (AmE), / ˈkɛːrɛks ˌsjuːdəʊˈsɪpərəs / (BrE)
A species of tufted sedge found in temperate regions, widely distributed across the world.
tufted sedge species of temperate regions

Examples

Carex pseudocyperus commonly grows in wet meadows and along the edges of ponds.
Botanists identified several clusters of Carex pseudocyperus in the nature reserve.
The characteristic tufts of Carex pseudocyperus help prevent soil erosion in marshy environments.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Carex' (sedge) + 'pseudo-' (false) + 'cyperus' (relating to Cyperus, another sedge genus).
