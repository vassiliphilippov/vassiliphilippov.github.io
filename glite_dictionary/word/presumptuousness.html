presumptuousness

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈzʌmp.tʃu.əs.nəs / (AmE), / prɪˈzʌmp.tjʊ.əs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being too confident, especially in a way that is rude or fails to respect social limits or authority
quality of being overly bold or arrogant

Examples

His presumptuousness offended everyone at the meeting.
She was taken aback by the presumptuousness of his request.
Do not mistake confidence for presumptuousness in the tone of her letter.

Synset Examples

he despised them for their presumptuousness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrogance - the quality of being arrogant; excessive pride or self-importance
  • Antonym: modesty - the quality of being humble and not too confident about oneself

Etymology

From Latin 'praesumptuosus' (full of boldness), from 'praesumere' (to presume).
