amendment

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛndmənt / (AmE), / əˈmɛndmənt / (BrE)
the act of amending or making a change to something, especially to improve it or correct an error
the act of amending or correcting

Examples

The company made an amendment to its privacy policy after receiving customer feedback.
He proposed an amendment to the document to fix a minor error.
The law allows for the amendment of contracts under certain conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: correction - the act of making something right or accurate
  • Synonym: revision - the process of making changes to improve or update something

Etymology

From Old French 'amender' (to amend) and Latin 'emendare' (to correct).

2. NOUN

/ əˈmɛndmənt / (AmE), / əˈmɛndmənt / (BrE)
a change or addition designed to improve a text, piece of legislation, or legal document
change or addition to a document

Examples

The senator proposed an amendment to the bill to address privacy concerns.
Several amendments have been made to the constitution over the years.
The committee voted to reject the amendment during the final reading.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modification - the act of making something different
  • Synonym: addition - something added to an existing document or proposal
  • Antonym: repeal - the action of revoking or annulling a law or act

Etymology

From Old French 'amendement', from Latin 'emendare' (to correct).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06736384-n
Synset POS: n
