constitution

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnstəˈtuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃən / (BrE)
the act of forming, creating, or establishing something, such as an organization or system
act of forming or establishing something

Examples

The constitution of the committee marked a turning point for the organization.
Following the constitution of the new school board, several changes were implemented.
He was present at the constitution of the association in 1995.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑn.stɪˈtu.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.stɪˈtjuː.ʃən / (BrE)
The way in which something is made up or arranged; the composition or structure of something.
composition or structure

Examples

The chemical constitution of the alloy makes it unusually strong.
Her tough physical constitution enabled her to complete the marathon.
The soil's constitution affects which plants can thrive in the area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: structure - the arrangement or organization of parts to form an entity
  • Synonym: composition - the makeup or constitution of something

Etymology

From Latin 'constitutio' (establishment, arrangement), from 'constituere' (to set up, establish).

3. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnstɪˈtuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃən / (BrE)
The system of fundamental principles or established precedents according to which a state or organization is governed, especially as embodied in a written document.
fundamental law or principles of a government

Examples

The United States Constitution established the framework for the federal government.
Amending a constitution often requires a special legislative process.
Many countries have written their own constitutions to outline the structure of their governments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charter - a formal written document that defines rights and privileges, especially of an organization or institution
  • Synonym: statute - a written law passed by a legislative body
  • Degree Lower: regulation - a rule or directive made and maintained by an authority
  • Degree Lower: amendment - a change or addition to a legal or statutory document, such as a constitution

Etymology

From Latin 'constitutio' (an arrangement, regulation), from 'constituere' (to establish).
