inflexion

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
a change in the form of a word, often by adding a suffix, to express different grammatical functions such as tense, mood, person, or number
grammatical word form change

Examples

English verbs undergo inflexion to indicate tense, such as adding '-ed' for the past.
In Latin, noun inflexion shows the grammatical case and number.
She studied the patterns of inflexion in ancient Greek language texts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inflection - alternative spelling: change in a word's form for grammatical function
  • Antonym: derivation - formation of a word from another base word

Etymology

From Latin inflexio, from inflectere (to bend in).
