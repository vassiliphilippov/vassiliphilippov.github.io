prophylactic

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊfləˈktɪk / (AmE), / ˌprɒfləˈktɪk / (BrE)
A medicine or treatment intended to prevent or slow the development of an illness or disease.
preventive medicine or remedy
medical

Examples

The hospital implemented the use of a new prophylactic to prevent infection in surgical patients.
Doctors often prescribe a prophylactic during flu season to at-risk individuals.
Many travelers take a malaria prophylactic before visiting tropical regions.

Synset Examples

the doctor recommended several preventatives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preventative - something that prevents disease or harm
  • Antonym: curative - a medicine or treatment that cures disease

Etymology

From Greek 'prophylaktikos', from 'prophylassein' (to guard before).

2. NOUN

/ ˌproʊ.fɪˈlæk.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌprɒ.fɪˈlæk.tɪk / (BrE)
A contraceptive device, especially a thin sheath of rubber or latex worn over the penis during intercourse to prevent pregnancy or the transmission of diseases; a condom.
contraceptive sheath (condom)

Examples

The use of a prophylactic can greatly reduce the risk of sexually transmitted infections.
He discreetly took a prophylactic from his wallet before the encounter.
Doctors recommend carrying a prophylactic when engaging in sexual activity with new partners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condom - A sheath worn during intercourse as a contraceptive or as protection against infection.

Etymology

From Greek prophylaktikos, from prophylassein (to take precautions against), from pro- (before) + phylassein (to guard).
