testiere

1. NOUN

/ tɛsˈtiːəɹ / (AmE), / tɛsˈtɪə / (BrE)
A piece of medieval plate armor designed to protect a horse's head.
medieval armor for a horse's head

Examples

The knight's horse wore a polished testiere into battle, gleaming in the sunlight.
Museum visitors admired the ornate testiere on display in the medieval armor collection.
During tournaments, a testiere provided crucial protection for the warhorse's head.

Etymology

From Italian 'testiera', from 'testa' meaning 'head'.
