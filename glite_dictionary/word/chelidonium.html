Chelidonium

1. NOUN

/ kɛlɪˈdoʊniəm / (AmE), / kɛlɪˈdəʊniəm / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous plants in the poppy family, chiefly known for its only species, the greater celandine (Chelidonium majus).
genus of herbs; includes greater celandine

Examples

Chelidonium is widely recognized for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
Botanists have classified the greater celandine under the genus Chelidonium.
The milky sap of Chelidonium was once used to treat warts and skin conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celandine - common name for Chelidonium majus, the principal species in the genus Chelidonium

Etymology

From Latin 'chelidonium', from Ancient Greek 'khelidōn' (swallow, the bird).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɛləˈdoʊniəm / (AmE), / ˌkɛlɪˈdəʊnɪəm / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the poppy family, containing the species known as greater celandine
plant genus including greater celandine

Examples

The only species in the Chelidonium genus is commonly known as greater celandine.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Chelidonium in traditional herbal remedies.
Botanists classify greater celandine within the genus Chelidonium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celandine - a yellow-flowered herbaceous plant of the poppy family

Etymology

From Greek khelidōn (swallow), referring to the timing of the plant's flowering with the return of swallows.
