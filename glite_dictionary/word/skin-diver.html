skin-diver

1. NOUN

/ skɪnˌdaɪvər / (AmE), / skɪnˌdaɪvə / (BrE)
a person who swims underwater using a mask and fins, and sometimes a snorkel or air tank, for recreation or sport
person who swims underwater with mask and fins

Examples

The experienced skin-diver explored the coral reef with ease.
Every summer, hundreds of skin-divers visit the marine park to observe aquatic life.
He became a skin-diver to pursue his passion for underwater photography.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scuba diver - a person who swims underwater using a self-contained underwater breathing apparatus
  • Degree Lower: free diver - a person who dives underwater without breathing apparatus

Etymology

From 'skin' (referring to the swimmer, not protected by a heavy diving suit) + 'diver'.
