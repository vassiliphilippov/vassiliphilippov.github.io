offertory

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔfɚˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈɒfətəri / (BrE)
the part of a Christian service, especially the Eucharist, during which offerings of bread and wine are presented to God
part of a church service for offering bread and wine

Examples

During the offertory, choir members sang a hymn as the bread and wine were placed on the altar.
The priest explained the significance of the offertory to the congregation.
Some churches collect donations from parishioners at the offertory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eucharist - the Christian ceremony commemorating the Last Supper

Etymology

From Medieval Latin offertorium, from Latin offerre (to offer).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.fɚˌtɔːr.i / (AmE), / ˈɒf.ə.tər.i / (BrE)
the offerings of money or other gifts presented by the congregation during a religious service, especially in Christian churches
offering of gifts at a religious service

Examples

During Sunday service, the offertory was collected in woven baskets passed down each row.
The choir sang a hymn while the congregation presented their offertory at the altar.
He placed an envelope with his weekly offertory inside the church's collection box.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collection - money collected during a religious service
  • Synonym: donation - something that is given to a charity or religious institution

Etymology

From Medieval Latin offertorium (place of offering), from Latin offerre (to offer).
