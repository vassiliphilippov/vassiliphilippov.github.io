basset

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæsɪt / (AmE), / ˈbæsɪt / (BrE)
a breed of short-legged, smooth-haired hound with long ears, originally bred for hunting.
a short-legged breed of hound dog

Examples

The basset is known for its keen sense of smell and distinctive appearance.
Many families choose the basset for its gentle temperament and loyalty.
At the dog show, the basset drew attention with its long, droopy ears and soulful eyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basset hound - a dog breed with short legs and long ears, noted for its scenting ability

Etymology

From French 'basset', diminutive of 'bas' (low), referring to its short stature.
