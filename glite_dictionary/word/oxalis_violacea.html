Oxalis violacea

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈzælɪs vaɪ.oʊˈleɪsi.ə / (AmE), / ɒkˈsælɪs vaɪ.əˈleɪsɪə / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to eastern North America, characterized by palmately compound leaves and typically rose-purple flowers.
a North American perennial herb with rose-purple flowers

Examples

The delicate blooms of Oxalis violacea add a splash of color to woodland gardens.
Researchers studied the medicinal uses of Oxalis violacea among indigenous peoples.
You can find Oxalis violacea growing wild in moist, shaded areas throughout the Midwest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violet wood sorrel - common name for Oxalis violacea

Etymology

From Latin: 'Oxalis' (sorrel), 'violacea' (violet-colored).
