Corydalis claviculata

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːrɪˈdeɪlɪs kləˌvɪkjʊˈleɪtə / (AmE), / ˌkɒrɪˈdeɪlɪs klævɪkjʊˈlɑːtə / (BrE)
an annual climbing plant with divided leaves and racemes of yellow and pink flowers, native to parts of Europe
a climbing plant with yellow and pink flowers

Examples

Corydalis claviculata grows wild in the damp woods of northern Europe.
Botanists admire the delicate two-toned flowers of Corydalis claviculata in springtime.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Corydalis claviculata for its attractive climbing habit.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus Corydalis from Greek korydalis 'crested lark', and specific epithet claviculata from Latin 'with little tendrils'.
