wisdom

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪzdəm / (AmE), / ˈwɪzdəm / (BrE)
The ability to make sensible decisions and give good advice, especially based on experience and knowledge.
sensible and prudent judgment

Examples

Her wisdom helped the company avoid making costly mistakes.
Listening to elders is often a way to gain wisdom about life.
He handled the situation with great wisdom and patience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prudence - the quality of being prudent; cautiousness
  • Antonym: folly - lack of good sense; foolishness

Etymology

From Old English wisdōm (wisdom; knowledge), equivalent to wise + -dom.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪzdəm / (AmE), / ˈwɪzdəm / (BrE)
the ability to use knowledge and experience with good judgment and insight
ability to use experience and good judgment

Examples

She showed remarkable wisdom in making that difficult decision.
Many cultures value the wisdom of elders and listen to their advice.
His wisdom comes from years of experience and careful thought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intelligence - the ability to acquire and apply knowledge and skills
  • Antonym: folly - lack of good sense or judgment

Etymology

From Old English 'wisdōm' (wisdom), from 'wis' (wise) + '-dōm' (condition or state).

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɪzdəm / (AmE), / ˈwɪzdəm / (BrE)
the quality of having experience, knowledge, and good judgment; the accumulated knowledge and insight acquired over time
the quality of being wise; accumulated knowledge

Examples

She relied on her grandfather’s wisdom to solve the difficult problem.
Over the years, the professor’s wisdom earned him great respect among his peers.
We seek the wisdom of those who have experienced these challenges before us.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knowledge - facts, information, and skills acquired through experience or education
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge, understanding, or awareness

Etymology

From Old English 'wisdom', from 'wis' (wise) + '-dom' (state, condition).

4. NOUN

/ ˈwɪzdəm / (AmE), / ˈwɪzdəm / (BrE)
the ability to use knowledge, experience, and good judgment to make sound decisions and act effectively
sound judgment based on experience or knowledge

Examples

Her grandmother’s wisdom was valued by everyone in the family.
He showed great wisdom in choosing not to invest in the risky venture.
The ancient stories often highlight the wisdom of older generations.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: knowledge - facts, information, and skills acquired through experience or education
  • Antonym: foolishness - lack of good sense or judgment; stupidity

From Old English wisdōm (wise + -dom), meaning 'state of being wise'.
