Wisdom

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪzdəm / (AmE), / ˈwɪzdəm / (BrE)
an Apocryphal book of the Bible, chiefly a meditation on wisdom, ascribed to Solomon but likely written in the first century BC
biblical/apocryphal book on wisdom

Examples

Many scholars study the Wisdom of Solomon for its philosophical and theological insights.
The Book of Wisdom is included in the Catholic and Orthodox biblical canons.
He referenced the Wisdom in his essay about ancient religious texts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ecclesiasticus - another Apocryphal book, also called the Wisdom of Sirach
