arteria circumflexa humeri

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈtɪriə ˌsɜːrkəmˈflɛksə ˈhjuːmɚaɪ / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə ˌsɜːkəmˈflɛksə ˈhjuːməri / (BrE)
an artery that supplies blood to the shoulder joint and nearby muscles
shoulder joint artery
medical

Examples

Injury to the arteria circumflexa humeri can lead to impaired blood flow in the shoulder area.
Surgeons must be cautious to avoid damaging the arteria circumflexa humeri during shoulder operations.
The arteria circumflexa humeri plays a crucial role in supplying blood to the deltoid muscle.

Etymology

Latin, meaning 'circumflex artery of the humerus'.
