Lonicera involucrata

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈnɪsərə ɪnˌvɑːl.jʊˈkreɪtə / (AmE), / ləˈnɪsərə ɪnˌvɒl.jʊˈkreɪtə / (BrE)
a species of shrubby honeysuckle native to western North America, bearing purple flowers and black berries
a shrubby honeysuckle of western North America

Examples

Lonicera involucrata is commonly known as the bearberry honeysuckle in western North America.
Botanists are studying how Lonicera involucrata adapts to different elevations in mountain regions.
The dark berries of Lonicera involucrata attract many species of birds to the area.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Lonicera' (honeysuckle), species 'involucrata' (having an involucre/whorl of bracts).
