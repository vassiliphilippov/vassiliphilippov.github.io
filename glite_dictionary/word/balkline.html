balkline

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔkˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɔːkˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a line marked across a billiard table, behind which the cue balls are placed at the beginning of a game
line on a billiard table for starting play

Examples

The referee carefully positioned the balls behind the balkline before announcing the start of play.
In certain billiards games, players must ensure the cue ball stays within the balkline until their first shot.
The faded balkline made it difficult for beginners to know where to place the balls.

Etymology

From balk (a ridge or boundary) + line; related to the 'balk' area in billiards.
