Geoglossaceae

1. NOUN

/ dʒiː.əˌɡlɑːs.əˈsiː.i / (AmE), / dʒiː.əˌɡlɒs.əˈsiː.i / (BrE)
A family of fungi in the order Helotiales, commonly known as earth tongue fungi.
a family of fungi (earth tongues)
medical

Examples

Species in the Geoglossaceae family are often found in moist, mossy woodland habitats.
The Geoglossaceae are sometimes called earth tongue fungi because of their tongue-like appearance.
Researchers identified a new member of the Geoglossaceae during their forest survey.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'geo-' (earth) and 'glōssa' (tongue), reflecting the typical shape, with the family suffix '-aceae'.

2. NOUN

/ dʒioʊˌɡlɑsəˈsiː / (AmE), / dʒiːəʊˌɡlɒsəˈsiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi within the order Helotiales, characterized by tongue- or club-shaped fruiting bodies found mainly in damp soils or mosses
a family of fungi in Helotiales

Examples

Many species in the Geoglossaceae family are known for their unique tongue-shaped fruiting bodies.
The damp, mossy ground provided ideal conditions for several Geoglossaceae fungi to thrive.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Geoglossaceae during their study of forest ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Helotiales - an order of fungi containing various families, including Geoglossaceae

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'ge' (earth) + 'glossa' (tongue), referring to the tongue-like appearance of the fungi.
