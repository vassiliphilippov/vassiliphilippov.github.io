hairdressing

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛrˌdrɛsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈheəˌdrɛsɪŋ / (BrE)
a substance, such as tonic or pomade, used to groom, style, or manage hair
a grooming substance for hair

Examples

He uses a light hairdressing to keep his hair neat for work.
A new hairdressing promises to add shine without making hair greasy.
Some hairdressings contain natural oils to nourish the scalp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hair tonic - a liquid cosmetic for the hair to improve its appearance or manageability
  • Synonym: pomade - a scented ointment applied to the hair for styling or shine

Etymology

From 'hairdress' (to arrange the hair) + -ing, originally meaning the act or occupation of dressing the hair.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛrˌdrɛsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈheəˌdrɛsɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity or profession of cutting, styling, and caring for people's hair, including washing, coloring, and arranging it
the cutting and styling of hair

Examples

She decided to study hairdressing at the local vocational school.
Many people visit salons for professional hairdressing before special occasions.
Advances in hairdressing have introduced new techniques for styling and coloring hair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hairstyling - the practice or process of arranging hair
  • Synonym: barbering - the cutting and grooming of hair, especially men's hair

Etymology

From 'hair' + 'dressing', referring to the practice of attending to the appearance of hair.
