Cryptogramma

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪptoʊˈɡræmə / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəˈɡræmə / (BrE)
A genus of ferns sometimes placed in the family Polypodiaceae or Cryptogrammataceae, commonly known as rockbrakes.
a genus of ferns (rockbrakes)

Examples

The Cryptogramma genus can be found growing in rocky mountainous regions.
Botanists recently classified several species within the Cryptogramma group.
Many members of Cryptogramma are known for their delicate, divided fronds.

Etymology

From Greek 'kryptos' (hidden) + 'gramma' (letter, line), referring to the scarcely visible sori (spore clusters).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪptoʊˈɡræmə / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəʊˈɡræmə / (BrE)
A genus of ferns sometimes placed in the family Polypodiaceae or Cryptogrammataceae.
a genus of ferns

Examples

The Cryptogramma genus is found in rocky habitats in North America and Eurasia.
Many Cryptogramma species are commonly known as rockbrakes.
Botanists debated whether Cryptogramma should be classified in Polypodiaceae or in its own family.

Etymology

From Greek 'kryptos' (hidden) and 'gramma' (letter, line).
