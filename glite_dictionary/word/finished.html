finished

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɪnɪʃt / (AmE), / ˈfɪnɪʃt / (BrE)
(of an object) having a surface coating or treatment applied, such as polishing, painting, or varnishing, as the final stage of production.
having a surface coating or treatment applied

Examples

The finished table gleamed under the lights of the showroom.
He prefers to buy finished products instead of assembling them himself.
The carpenter delivered the finished cabinets on schedule.

Synset Examples

the finished bookcase costs much more than the unfinished ones

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfinished - not having a surface coating or final treatment
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɪnɪʃt / (AmE), / ˈfɪnɪʃt / (BrE)
having been completed or brought to an end; no longer in progress or unfinished.
completed; brought to an end (adj.)

Examples

I'll let you know when I am finished with the report.
The project is finally finished after months of hard work.
Is dinner finished yet, or do you need more time?

Synset Examples

are you finished?
gave me the finished manuscript

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfinished - not completed or brought to an end

3. ADJ

/ ˈfɪnɪʃt / (AmE), / ˈfɪnɪʃt / (BrE)
No longer successful, powerful, or able to continue; ruined.
no longer successful; ruined

Examples

After the scandal, the once-popular politician was finished in the public eye.
If our team loses this match, we're finished for the season.
Once word got out about the fraud, the company's reputation was finished.

Synset Examples

after the revolution the aristocracy was finished
the unsuccessful run for office left him ruined politically and economically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruined - having lost all success, money, or position; destroyed

4. ADJ

/ ˈfɪnɪʃt / (AmE), / ˈfɪnɪʃt / (BrE)
(of materials or goods) having been brought to the desired or completed final state, ready for use or sale.
brought to the desired final state

Examples

The furniture is a finished product, ready to be delivered to the customer.
Only finished goods may be stored in the shipping warehouse.
This table has a beautifully finished surface that resists stains and scratches.

Synset Examples

a finished product

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfinished - not brought to the desired final state

Etymology

From the verb 'finish', ultimately from Latin 'finire' (to end, limit).

5. ADJ

/ ˈfɪnɪʃt / (AmE), / ˈfɪnɪʃt / (BrE)
(of skills or products) having attained the highest degree of excellence or refinement; perfected.
having highest excellence or refinement

Examples

Her finished performance captivated the audience with its grace and assurance.
The novel is a finished work, displaying mastery of both language and structure.
He became known as a finished craftsman, admired for his attention to detail.

Synset Examples

a dazzling and finished piece of writing
a finished violinist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perfected - brought to completion or highest excellence
  • Antonym: unfinished - not brought to completion or perfection

Etymology

From the verb 'finish', from Middle English *finisshen*, from Old French *finiss-, finir*, from Latin *finire* (to end, limit).
