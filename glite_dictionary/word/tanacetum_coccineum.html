Tanacetum coccineum

1. NOUN

/ ˌtænəˈsiːtəm kɒkˈsɪniəm / (AmE), / ˌtanəˈsiːtəm kɒkˈsɪniəm / (BrE)
A perennial plant of Asiatic origin cultivated for its divided aromatic leaves and pink-purple to white daisy-like flowers; also used as a source of natural insecticide (pyrethrum).
a garden perennial with pink or white flowers; source of pyrethrum

Examples

Tanacetum coccineum is commonly grown as a border plant for its attractive pink flowers.
The dried flowers of Tanacetum coccineum are used to make natural insecticides.
Gardeners value Tanacetum coccineum both for its beauty and its pest-repelling properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyrethrum - A natural insecticide obtained from certain chrysanthemum flowers, including Tanacetum coccineum

Etymology

From New Latin, genus Tanacetum and species coccineum meaning 'scarlet-colored'.
