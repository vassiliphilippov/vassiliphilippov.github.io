dive-bombing

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪv ˌbɑmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdaɪv ˌbɒmɪŋ / (BrE)
A bombing tactic in which an aircraft releases its bomb while descending steeply toward the target.
bombing from a steep dive

Examples

The squadron practiced dive-bombing techniques on training targets near the coast.
Dive-bombing played a crucial role in several battles during World War II.
The pilot executed a precise dive-bombing maneuver against the enemy position.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strafe - to attack with gunfire from low-flying aircraft

Etymology

Formed from 'dive' and 'bomb', referring to aircraft tactics developed in the early 20th century.
