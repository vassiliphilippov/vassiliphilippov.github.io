Psittaciformes

1. NOUN

/ sɪˌtætəˈfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / sɪˌtatɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of birds that includes parrots, cockatoos, amazons, lorikeets, lories, macaws, and parakeets
order of birds including parrots and cockatoos

Examples

The Psittaciformes are renowned for their bright plumage and ability to mimic sounds.
Researchers studied the evolutionary history of Psittaciformes in the rainforests of South America.
Many pet owners are fascinated by the intelligence of birds within the Psittaciformes order.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek psittakos (parrot) + -formes (having the form of)

2. NOUN

/ sɪˌtætəˈfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / sɪˌtatɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
the order of birds that includes parrots, cockatoos, macaws, lorikeets, lories, and parakeets
order of birds including parrots and relatives

Examples

The Psittaciformes are known for their intelligent behavior and ability to mimic human speech.
Many species in the Psittaciformes order are brightly colored and live in tropical regions.
Researchers study the vocal learning abilities of birds in the Psittaciformes group to understand animal communication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parrot - a bird of the Psittaciformes order, known for its ability to mimic sounds
  • Antonym: Passeriformes - an order of perching birds/songbirds, distinct from Psittaciformes

Etymology

From Modern Latin *Psittaciformes*, from *psittacus* (parrot) + *-formes* (shaped, form).
