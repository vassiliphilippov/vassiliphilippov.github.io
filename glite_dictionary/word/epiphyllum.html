epiphyllum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈfɪləm / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈfɪləm / (BrE)
a tropical cactus of the genus Epiphyllum, characterized by flattened, branching stems and large, showy tubular flowers, often grown as a houseplant
a tropical cactus with flat stems and showy flowers

Examples

My epiphyllum bloomed last night, producing a single beautiful pink flower.
She collects rare species of epiphyllum for her indoor garden.
The flattened stems of the epiphyllum make it easy to distinguish from other cacti.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cactus - a succulent plant with a thick, fleshy stem that typically bears spines

Etymology

From Greek epi- (upon) + phyllon (leaf), referring to its flowers appearing on the modified stems.
