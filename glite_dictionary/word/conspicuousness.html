conspicuousness

1. NOUN

/ kənˈspɪk.ju.əs.nəs / (AmE), / kənˈspɪk.ju.ə.snəs / (BrE)
the quality of being easily seen, noticed, or attracting attention
state of being easily noticed

Examples

The conspicuousness of her bright red dress made her easy to spot in the crowd.
The animal's markings contribute to its conspicuousness in its natural habitat.
He avoided conspicuousness by dressing in plain, dark-colored clothing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: visibility - the state of being able to be seen
  • Antonym: obscurity - the state of being difficult to see or not easily noticed

Etymology

From Latin conspicuus (visible) + -ness.

2. NOUN

/ kənˈspɪkju.əs.nəs / (AmE), / kənˈspɪkju.əs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being easily seen or attracting notice; the state of standing out clearly
quality of being easily noticed

Examples

The conspicuousness of the bright yellow car made it easy to spot in the parking lot.
He tried to blend in at the party, but his height contributed to his conspicuousness.
Some animals use their conspicuousness for mating displays, attracting the attention of potential partners.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: invisibility - the quality of not being able to be seen
  • Synonym: prominence - the state of being important, well-known, or easily noticed

Etymology

From Latin 'conspicuus' (visible, remarkable) + English suffix '-ness'.
