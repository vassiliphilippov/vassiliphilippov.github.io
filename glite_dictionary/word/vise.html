vise

1. NOUN

/ vaɪs / (AmE), / vaɪs / (BrE)
a device attached to a workbench, consisting of two jaws that can be brought together to hold an object firmly while work is done on it
device with jaws to hold workpiece

Examples

He secured the wooden plank in the vise before sawing it in half.
The metalworker tightened the vise to keep the pipe from slipping.
A vise is an essential tool in any carpenter's workshop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clamp - device for holding things tightly together

Etymology

From Old French 'vis', from Latin 'vīsum' (that which is seen) referring to the spiral of a screw.
