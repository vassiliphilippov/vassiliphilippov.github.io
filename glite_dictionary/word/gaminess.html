gaminess

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪ.mi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪ.mɪ.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of language or behavior that is bold, risqué, or slightly indecent
borderline indelicacy in language or behavior

Examples

The play's gaminess delighted some and shocked others in the audience.
Critics noted the gaminess of the comedian's jokes during the performance.
Despite the author's reputation, the novel contained little of the gaminess found in his previous works.

Etymology

From 'gamey', meaning risqué or slightly indecent, plus the noun-forming suffix '-ness'.
