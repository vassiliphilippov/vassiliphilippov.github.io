exponentiation

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˌspoʊnənʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˌspəʊnənʃɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of raising a number or quantity to a given power, as in calculating an exponent
raising to a power; mathematical operation

Examples

The operation of exponentiation is fundamental in mathematics, especially in algebra and calculus.
Calculators can quickly perform exponentiation with very large numbers.
Understanding exponentiation helps when learning about logarithms and scientific notation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: power - the result of exponentiation; a number produced by raising another to an exponent

Etymology

From Latin 'exponere' (to set forth) + English suffix '-ation'.
