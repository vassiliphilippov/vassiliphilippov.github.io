stifle

1. NOUN

/ ˈstaɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈstaɪf(ə)l / (BrE)
the joint between the femur and tibia in a quadruped animal, corresponding to the human knee
joint in quadrupeds, like the knee

Examples

The veterinarian examined the horse's stifle carefully for signs of swelling.
A torn ligament in the dog's stifle may require surgery to heal properly.
Lameness in the hind leg is often caused by problems in the stifle joint.

Etymology

From Middle English *stifil*, probably related to Old French *estivel* (weak part of the leg).

2. VERB

/ ˈstaɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈstaɪfəl / (BrE)
to die or suffer from lack of air; to be prevented from breathing normally, often resulting in asphyxiation
die or suffer from lack of oxygen

Examples

Several victims stifled in the smoke-filled building before help arrived.
If you stifle in this cramped attic, open a window for fresh air.
He felt he might stifle under the heavy covers on such a hot night.

Synset Examples

The child suffocated under the pillow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suffocate - to die or have serious difficulty breathing due to lack of air
  • Synonym: choke - to have severe difficulty breathing because of a blocked throat

Etymology

From Old French *estouffer* (to smother), of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈstaɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈstaɪfəl / (BrE)
to suppress or restrain something so as to lessen its intensity or prevent it from developing fully
suppress or restrain

Examples

Sometimes, it's necessary to stifle your laughter in serious situations.
Strict rules can stifle creativity in the workplace.
He struggled to stifle his anger during the heated debate.

Synset Examples

Stifle your curiosity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suppress - to put an end to something forcibly; to prevent development or expression
  • Antonym: to encourage - to give support or confidence, the opposite of restraining or suppressing

Etymology

From Old French *estouffer* (to smother), of Germanic origin.

5. VERB

/ ˈstaɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈstaɪfəl / (BrE)
to impair someone's ability to breathe or obstruct their air passage
impair ability to breathe

Examples

The thick smoke began to stifle the firefighters inside the burning building.
She covered his mouth, trying to stifle his cries and prevent anyone from hearing.
The airtight room would stifle anyone who stayed there too long.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suffocate - to die or cause to die from lack of air

Etymology

From Old French *estoufler* (to stifle, smother), from Vulgar Latin *stuphulare*.
