enkephalin

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈkɛfəlɪn / (AmE), / ɛnˈkɛfəlɪn / (BrE)
a type of endorphin in the brain and spinal cord with opiate-like effects, involved in pain modulation and other processes
an endorphin with opiate effects in the brain
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered that enkephalin levels increase in response to stress and pain.
The body's natural enkephalin helps reduce the perception of pain.
Certain drugs mimic the effect of enkephalin in the nervous system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endorphin - any of a group of hormones secreted within the brain and nervous system, which have pain-relieving properties
  • Degree Higher: dynorphin - another type of endogenous opioid peptide involved in pain regulation

Etymology

From Greek 'enkephalos' (brain) + '-in'.
