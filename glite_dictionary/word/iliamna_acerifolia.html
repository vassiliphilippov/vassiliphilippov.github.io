Iliamna acerifolia

1. NOUN

/ ɪliˈæmnə ˌæsəriˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ɪliˈamnə ˌæsəriˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to northwestern United States and western Canada, resembling a hollyhock and bearing white or pink flowers.
a perennial plant with white or pink flowers

Examples

The Iliamna acerifolia thrives in the cool, moist environments of western Canada.
Gardeners admire the delicate pink blooms of the Iliamna acerifolia each summer.
Wild Iliamna acerifolia can often be found growing along mountain streams.

Etymology

From the genus name Iliamna and the species name acerifolia, meaning 'maple-leaved'.
