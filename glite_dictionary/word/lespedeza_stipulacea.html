Lespedeza stipulacea

1. NOUN

/ lɛsˌpɛdəziə stɪpjəˈleɪsiə / (AmE), / lɛspɪˈdiːziə stɪpjʊˈleɪsɪə / (BrE)
An annual leguminous plant, native to Korea, widely grown in hot, dry regions as forage and hay.
annual forage plant native to Korea

Examples

Farmers in dry regions often grow Lespedeza stipulacea as a reliable source of forage.
Because Lespedeza stipulacea is drought-tolerant, it thrives where other hay crops may fail.
The introduction of Lespedeza stipulacea improved livestock feeding options in many countries.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'lespedeza'; species from Latin 'stipulacea' meaning 'having stipules'.
