coelacanth

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiː.lə.kænθ / (AmE), / ˈsiːləkænθ / (BrE)
a rare, deep-sea fish believed to have been extinct since the Cretaceous period but rediscovered in 1938 off the coast of Africa; notable for its lobe-finned appearance and evolutionary importance
ancient lobe-finned fish

Examples

The discovery of the coelacanth surprised scientists, who thought it had vanished millions of years ago.
A live coelacanth was first caught off the coast of South Africa in 1938.
Paleontologists studied the anatomy of the coelacanth to learn more about the evolution of vertebrates.

Etymology

From Greek *koilos* (hollow) + *akantha* (spine), referring to the hollow spines in its fins.
