discolouration

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsˌkʌlɚˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsˌkʌləˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or state of losing natural color, usually by becoming dull, faded, or stained.
loss or change of natural color

Examples

The old photograph showed significant discolouration around the edges.
Repeated washing can cause discolouration of dark fabrics over time.
He was concerned about the discolouration developing on his bathroom tiles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stain - a mark that is difficult to remove, caused by a substance being absorbed
  • Degree Higher: bleaching - the process of removing or fading color, usually to whiten
  • Antonym: coloration - the process of coloring or the appearance of color

Etymology

From late Middle English, from 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'colouration' (coloring).

2. NOUN

/ dɪsˌkʌlərˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪsˌkʌləˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
an area or the process of becoming stained, faded, or marked with a different color, often making something look dirty or less attractive
change of color, usually to something undesirable

Examples

The discolouration on the ceiling was caused by a water leak.
Frequent swimming in chlorinated pools can lead to discolouration of light-colored hair.
Sunlight had produced a slight discolouration on the pages of the old book.

Synset Examples

the wine left a dark stain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stain - a colored mark that is difficult to remove
  • Synonym: blemish - a small mark or flaw that spoils the appearance

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + coloration, after French décoloration.
