remnant

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛm.nənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛm.nənt / (BrE)
a small piece of cloth left over after the rest has been used or sold
piece of leftover cloth

Examples

She bought a remnant of fabric for a discount to use in her sewing project.
The store had a basket full of colorful remnants on display.
I used a remnant to patch a hole in my jacket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrap - a small piece of something, especially cloth, left over after the main part has been used

Etymology

From Old French *remenant*, from Latin *remanēre* (to remain).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛm.nənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛm.nənt / (BrE)
a small part, quantity, or piece that remains after the greater part has been used, removed, or destroyed
small remaining part or quantity

Examples

Only a remnant of the ancient wall remains standing today.
She used a remnant of fabric to make a small pillow.
The city was quiet, with only a remnant of its former population left.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remainder - what is left over after part has been taken
  • Synonym: relic - something surviving from the past
  • Antonym: whole - something complete with all its parts

Etymology

From Old French *remanant* (remaining), from Latin *remanēre* (to remain).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13833396-n
Synset POS: n
