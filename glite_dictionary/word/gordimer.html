Gordimer

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡɔːrdɪmər / (AmE), / ˈɡɔːdɪmə / (BrE)
Nadine Gordimer (1923–2014), a South African novelist and short-story writer known for works exploring the effects of apartheid.
South African author and anti-apartheid activist

Examples

Gordimer was awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1991 for her powerful writing on moral and racial issues.
Many students study works by Gordimer to better understand South Africa's history under apartheid.
The short stories of Gordimer are acclaimed for their subtle exploration of social injustice.
