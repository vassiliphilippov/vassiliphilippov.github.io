brain-fag

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪn ˌfæɡ / (AmE), / ˈbreɪn ˌfæɡ / (BrE)
A state of mental exhaustion or fatigue that diminishes one's ability to think clearly or concentrate.
mental exhaustion; mental fatigue
archaic british

Examples

After hours of continuous studying, she complained of brain-fag and took a break.
Writers often experience brain-fag when working under tight deadlines for extended periods.
A short walk outside helped him recover from the brain-fag that set in after a long meeting.

Etymology

Formed from 'brain' (mind) and 'fag' (fatigue or weariness, British English).
