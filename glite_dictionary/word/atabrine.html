Atabrine

1. NOUN

/ ˈætəˌbriːn / (AmE), / ˈatəˌbriːn / (BrE)
A trade name for quinacrine, a drug formerly used to treat malaria and certain worm infestations.
trade name for antimalarial drug quinacrine
medical

Examples

During World War II, soldiers were given Atabrine to prevent malaria in tropical regions.
Some patients experienced yellowing of the skin after prolonged use of Atabrine.
The doctor prescribed Atabrine as part of the treatment for parasitic infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quinacrine - a synthetic drug used as an antimalarial and to treat certain parasitic infections
