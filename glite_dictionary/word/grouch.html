grouch

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊtʃ / (AmE), / ɡraʊtʃ / (BrE)
a person who is habitually bad-tempered or irritable, often complaining or grumbling
bad-tempered or irritable person

Examples

My neighbor is such a grouch that he yells at anyone who walks near his yard.
Don't be a grouch just because it's raining today.
After a long day at work, he can turn into a real grouch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grump - a person who is habitually gloomy or irritable
  • Antonym: optimist - a person with a hopeful or positive outlook

Etymology

Probably from American English slang, of uncertain origin, possibly related to grumble or grouse.

2. VERB

/ ɡraʊtʃ / (AmE), / ɡraʊtʃ / (BrE)
to complain or express unhappiness in a sullen or critical way
complain or grumble sullenly

Examples

He tends to grouch whenever things do not go his way at work.
Stop grouching about the weather and enjoy the day.
She grouched all evening about the noise coming from next door.

Synset Examples

He scolded about anything that he thought was wrong
We grumbled about the increased work load

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grumble - to complain quietly or in a bad-tempered way
  • Synonym: to complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance

Etymology

From earlier noun 'grouch' (grumpiness), possibly originally American slang of unknown origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00911526-v
Synset POS: v
