Adansonia gregorii

1. NOUN

/ əˌdænˈsoʊni.ə ɡrɪˈɡɔri.aɪ / (AmE), / əˌdanˈsəʊnɪə ɡrɪˈɡɔːri.aɪ / (BrE)
an Australian tree of the baobab genus, known for its bottle-shaped trunk and gourd-like, acidic fruit
Australian baobab tree

Examples

The striking form of the Adansonia gregorii is a defining feature of the Kimberley landscape.
Many indigenous communities value the fruit of the Adansonia gregorii as a traditional food source.
Scientists are studying the adaptation strategies of Adansonia gregorii to survive in arid environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baobab - a genus of trees known for their swollen trunks

Etymology

From Latinized genus name Adansonia (after French botanist Michel Adanson) and species greogrii, honoring explorer Augustus Charles Gregory.
