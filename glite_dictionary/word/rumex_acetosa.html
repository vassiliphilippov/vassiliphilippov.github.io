Rumex acetosa

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːmɛks əˈsiːtəsə / (AmE), / ˈruːmɛks əˈsiːtəʊsə / (BrE)
A species of sorrel native to Europe and Asia, with large, slightly acidic leaves used in salads and as a spring green.
a species of sorrel with acidic edible leaves

Examples

Many chefs use Rumex acetosa to add a tangy flavor to spring salads.
The broad leaves of Rumex acetosa are prized in European cuisine for their refreshing taste.
Gardeners often cultivate Rumex acetosa for its edible, vitamin-rich greens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorrel - a common name for various plants with sour-tasting leaves, including Rumex acetosa

Etymology

From Latin: genus Rumex and species acetosa, meaning 'sour.'
