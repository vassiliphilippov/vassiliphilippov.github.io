promotional

1. ADJ

/ prəˈmoʊʃənəl / (AmE), / prəˈməʊʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to the advancement of someone to a higher position or rank; connected with promotion within an organization or system.
relating to advancement or promotion

Examples

He took a promotional exam last year to qualify for a higher position.
Only employees with at least two years of service are eligible for promotional opportunities.
The department announced several promotional openings for senior staff members.

Synset Examples

promotional exams

Related Terms

  • Synonym: promotion - the act of raising someone to a higher position or rank

Etymology

From promote + -ional, patterned after promotion.

Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ prəˈmoʊʃənəl / (AmE), / prəˈməʊʃənəl / (BrE)
Relating to the publicizing or advertising of a product, event, or service, especially to increase sales or public awareness.
relating to publicity or advertising

Examples

The company is launching a promotional campaign to increase its market share.
Many airlines offer promotional fares during the off-season to attract more tourists.
We received a free sample as part of a promotional event at the supermarket.

Synset Examples

promotional fares

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advertising - the activity or profession of producing advertisements
  • Synonym: marketing - actions for promoting and selling products or services

Etymology

From 'promotion' + '-al', with 'promotion' from Latin promotio (advancement).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03115272-a
Synset POS: a
