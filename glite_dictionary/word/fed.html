Fed

1. NOUN

/ fɛd / (AmE), / fɛd / (BrE)
the central bank of the United States, officially known as the Federal Reserve System, which regulates monetary policy and oversees the banking system.
the U.S. central bank; Federal Reserve
american

Examples

The Fed raised interest rates for the second time this year.
Investors closely watch the decisions made by the Fed.
The chairman of the Fed gave a speech on economic growth.

Synset Examples

the Fed seeks to control the United States economy by raising and lowering short-term interest rates and the money supply

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Federal Reserve - the full name of the U.S. central banking system

Etymology

Short for Federal Reserve.

2. NOUN

/ fɛd / (AmE), / fɛd / (BrE)
an officer working for a federal law-enforcement agency in the United States
U.S. federal law-enforcement officer
informal american

Examples

The Feds arrived early in the morning to execute the search warrant.
She believed her phone was being tapped by the Feds.
Many criminals fear getting attention from the Feds more than the local police.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: federal agent - an agent working for a federal law enforcement agency

Etymology

Short for 'federal agent' or 'federal officer'.
