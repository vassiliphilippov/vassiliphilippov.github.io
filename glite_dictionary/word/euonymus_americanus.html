Euonymus americanus

1. NOUN

/ juːˈɑː.nɪ.məs əˌmɛr.ɪˈkeɪ.nəs / (AmE), / juːˈɒn.ɪ.məs əˌmɛr.ɪˈkeɪ.nəs / (BrE)
A species of upright deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, noted for its crimson fruit pods and seeds.
upright North American shrub with crimson pods

Examples

The Euonymus americanus brightened the autumn forest with its vivid red pods.
Gardeners appreciate the Euonymus americanus for its attractive fall foliage and ornamental seeds.
Wildlife often feed on the berries of Euonymus americanus in wooded areas.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Euonymus and species name americanus meaning 'of America'.
