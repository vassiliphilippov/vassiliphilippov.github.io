bonesetter

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊnˌsɛtər / (AmE), / ˈbəʊnˌsɛtə / (BrE)
a person who sets broken or dislocated bones, traditionally without formal medical training
person who sets broken bones

Examples

The village bonesetter was called after Samuel fell from the tree and broke his arm.
Before modern medicine, people often relied on a local bonesetter for fractures and dislocations.
She apprenticed with an experienced bonesetter to learn traditional healing techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orthopaedist - a medical doctor specializing in treating bones and joints

Etymology

From 'bone' + 'setter', referring to the act of setting bones.
