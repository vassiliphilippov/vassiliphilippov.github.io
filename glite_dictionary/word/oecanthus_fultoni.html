Oecanthus fultoni

1. NOUN

/ oʊˌiːˈkænθəs fʊlˈtoʊni / (AmE), / əʊˌiːˈkænθəs fʊlˈtəʊni / (BrE)
A pale yellowish species of tree cricket native to and widely distributed in North America.
North American pale yellow tree cricket

Examples

The sweet, continuous song of the Oecanthus fultoni can be heard on warm summer nights.
Entomologists often study the Oecanthus fultoni for insights into insect communication.
Many gardeners welcome Oecanthus fultoni because it helps control small pest populations.

Etymology

Oecanthus is from Greek oikos (house) and anthos (flower); fultoni honors American entomologist Betty F. Fulton.
