humanist

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjuːmənɪst / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmənɪst / (BrE)
A scholar of the humanities, especially of classical languages, literature, and culture; a student of the liberal arts.
scholar of humanities or liberal arts

Examples

The Renaissance humanist dedicated his life to studying ancient Greek philosophy.
Many humanists contributed to the revival of classical learning in Europe.
As a humanist, she translated several Latin texts into English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classicist - a specialist in classical languages and literature

Etymology

From Italian umanista, originally meaning a teacher or student of classical literature.

6. NOUN

/ ˈhjuːmənɪst / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmənɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or practices humanism, emphasizing the value and agency of human beings individually and collectively
advocate or follower of humanism

Examples

As a lifelong humanist, she believes in the inherent dignity of every person.
Many Renaissance thinkers were prominent humanists who emphasized education and critical inquiry.
He joined a humanist organization dedicated to promoting secular ethics and social justice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humanitarian - a person who seeks to promote human welfare, sometimes by direct aid
  • Antonym: misanthrope - a person who dislikes or distrusts humankind

Etymology

From Latin *humanista* (one who studies the humanities).
