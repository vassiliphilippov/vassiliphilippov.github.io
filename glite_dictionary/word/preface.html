preface

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛfəs / (AmE), / ˈprɛfɪs / (BrE)
an introduction at the beginning of a book, usually written by the author, explaining its purpose or background
introductory essay before a book's main text

Examples

The author wrote a heartfelt preface describing the inspiration for the novel.
In the preface, she acknowledges the help of her research assistants.
Before reading the main story, I always glance at the preface to gain some context.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: introduction - opening section giving preliminary information
  • Antonym: epilogue - a section at the end of a book, often providing closure

Etymology

From Latin 'praefatio' (proclamation), from 'prae' (before) + 'fari' (to speak).

2. VERB

/ ˈprɛf.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈprɛf.ɪs / (BrE)
to introduce something, especially a speech or written work, with a preliminary statement or remarks
introduce with a preliminary statement

Examples

He prefaced his speech with a short personal anecdote.
The author prefaces each chapter with a relevant quotation.
Please preface your remarks with a brief introduction of yourself.

Synset Examples

She always precedes her lectures with a joke
He prefaced his lecture with a critical remark about the institution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to introduce - to begin or present something formally

Etymology

From Latin 'praefatio' (proclamation, introduction), from 'praefari' meaning 'to say beforehand'.
