surefooted

1. ADJ

/ ʃʊrˈfʊtɪd / (AmE), / ʃɔːˈfʊtɪd / (BrE)
confident and stable when moving, not likely to stumble or fall; able to move safely over rough or uneven ground
able to move steadily without stumbling

Examples

Mountain goats are known for being surefooted on rocky cliffs.
The hiker was surefooted as she navigated the uneven terrain.
He chose a surefooted horse for the steep mountain trail.

Synset Examples

on surefooted donkeys

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clumsy - prone to stumbling or losing balance

Etymology

Formed from 'sure' + 'footed', meaning having sure or steady feet.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʃʊrˈfʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʃɔːˈfʊtɪd / (BrE)
Confident and reliable in action or judgment; unlikely to make mistakes.
confident, reliable, not prone to error

Examples

Her surefooted handling of the crisis impressed everyone at the company.
The journalist is known for his surefooted analysis of complex political issues.
Despite fierce competition, her surefooted decisions led the team to success.

Synset Examples

most surefooted of the statesmen who dealt with the depression
demonstrates a surefooted storytelling talent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confident - having strong belief or full assurance in oneself or one's abilities
  • Antonym: uncertain - lacking confidence or assurance; not sure

Etymology

From 'sure' + 'footed', originally describing animals adept at walking on difficult terrain; later extended figuratively to describe confident judgment.
