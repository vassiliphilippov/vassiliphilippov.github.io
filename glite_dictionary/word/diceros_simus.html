Diceros simus

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈsɛrəs ˈsaɪməs / (AmE), / daɪˈsɪərɒs ˈsɪməs / (BrE)
a large, light-grey African rhinoceros with two horns, also known as the white rhinoceros; now endangered
African white rhinoceros species

Examples

The Diceros simus population has declined dramatically due to poaching.
Conservationists are working to protect Diceros simus in its natural habitat.
Diceros simus is distinguished by its wide mouth and two prominent horns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white rhinoceros - the common name for Diceros simus, a large African rhinoceros species
  • Antonym: black rhinoceros - another large African rhinoceros species, Diceros bicornis

Etymology

From Greek dikeros (two-horned) and Latin simus (flat-nosed)
