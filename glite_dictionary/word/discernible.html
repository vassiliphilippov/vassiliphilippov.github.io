discernible

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɜrnəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈsɜːnəbəl / (BrE)
Able to be perceived, noticed, or recognized; detectable.
able to be noticed or recognized

Examples

There was a discernible improvement in her mood after the holiday.
The noise from the street was barely discernible through the thick walls.
Over time, the effects of pollution became more discernible in local wildlife.

Synset Examples

a discernible change in attitude
a clearly evident erasure in the manuscript
an observable change in behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perceptible - able to be perceived; noticeable
  • Antonym: indiscernible - impossible to see or clearly distinguish

Etymology

From Latin discernere (to separate, distinguish) via French discerner + English suffix -ible.

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɜrnəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈsɜːnəbəl / (BrE)
able to be seen, noticed, or understood; perceptible by the senses or the mind.
able to be perceived or detected

Examples

There was no discernible change in his attitude after the announcement.
The difference between the original and the copy is barely discernible to the naked eye.
Her influence is clearly discernible in the company's new policy.

Synset Examples

things happen in the earth and sky with no discernible cause
the newspaper reports no discernible progress in the negotiations
the skyline is easily discernible even at a distance of several miles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perceptible - able to be perceived or noticed
  • Antonym: indiscernible - impossible to see, notice, or understand

Etymology

From Latin 'discernibilis,' from discernere meaning 'to separate, distinguish'.
