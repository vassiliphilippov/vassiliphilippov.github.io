squashed

1. ADJ

/ skwɑʃt / (AmE), / skwɒʃt / (BrE)
crushed or pressed with force so as to lose shape or be flattened
crushed or flattened by pressure

Examples

The bread was completely squashed in my backpack after the long hike.
He picked up the squashed can from the sidewalk and threw it away.
The squashed berries stained the bottom of the shopping bag.

Synset Examples

the squashed looking nakedness of the fledgling birds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crushed - pressed or squeezed with force so as to break or deform

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'squash', from late Middle English (originally meaning 'crush, squeeze'), of imitative origin.
