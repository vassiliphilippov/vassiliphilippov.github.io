Agaricus arvensis

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡærɪkəs ɑrˈvɛnsɪs / (AmE), / əˈɡærɪkəs ɑːˈvɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a coarse edible mushroom with a hollow stem and a broad white cap, commonly found in grasslands and meadows
species of edible mushroom

Examples

The Agaricus arvensis is often found growing near pastures and open fields.
Many foragers prize the Agaricus arvensis for its delicate flavor and large size.
Be sure to properly identify Agaricus arvensis before consuming it, as it can be confused with toxic species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horse mushroom - common name for Agaricus arvensis, an edible mushroom

Etymology

From Latin: 'Agaricus' (a genus of mushrooms) + 'arvensis' (of the field).
