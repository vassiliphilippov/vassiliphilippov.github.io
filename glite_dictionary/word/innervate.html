to innervate

1. VERB

/ ˈɪnərˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪnəveɪt / (BrE)
to supply (an organ or a part) with nerves; to stimulate to action through nerve impulses
supply or stimulate with nerves
medical

Examples

The nervous system innervates every muscle in the human body.
Properly functioning nerves innervate the skin, allowing us to feel touch and temperature.
Doctors studied how electrical impulses innervate cardiac tissue to regulate heartbeat.

Synset Examples

innervate a muscle or a nerve

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stimulate - to encourage something to become more active; to prompt to action
  • Antonym: to paralyze - to cause a loss of ability to move or feel in part or all of the body

Etymology

From Latin "innervare," from "in-" (into) + "nervus" (nerve).

2. VERB

/ ˈɪn.ɚ.veɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.ə.veɪt / (BrE)
to supply (an organ or body part) with nerves; to stimulate with nerve fibers
supply with nerves
medical

Examples

Sensory neurons innervate the skin and enable us to feel touch and temperature.
After the surgery, the doctor explained how the new tissue would be innervated by surrounding nerves.
Nerve fibers gradually innervate the muscle during fetal development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stimulate - to encourage activity or growth in a body part

Etymology

From Latin 'innervare', from 'in-' (into) + 'nervus' (nerve).
