extremum

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstriːməm / (AmE), / ɪkˈstriːməm / (BrE)
The greatest or least value of a function or quantity, especially in mathematics; a maximum or minimum point.
maximum or minimum value (math)

Examples

The function reaches its extremum at x = 2, where the slope is zero.
Physicists often look for the extremum in energy to determine a system's stable state.
This graph shows several extrema corresponding to local peaks and troughs.

Synset Examples

voltage peak

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maximum - the largest value attained by a function or quantity
  • Synonym: minimum - the smallest value attained by a function or quantity

Etymology

From Latin extremum, neuter of extremus meaning 'utmost, last'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstriməm / (AmE), / ɪkˈstriːməm / (BrE)
the point that is farthest from the center or middle, especially in mathematics or science; a maximum or minimum point of a function or system
point farthest from the center; maximum or minimum

Examples

The function reaches its extremum at the boundary of the interval.
Mathematicians often study the extremum of a curve to find its highest or lowest points.
To optimize the design, engineers must locate every extremum in the system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maximum - the greatest value or point in a mathematical function or set
  • Synonym: minimum - the least value or point in a mathematical function or set

Etymology

From Latin extremum, neuter of extremus meaning 'outermost, farthest'.
