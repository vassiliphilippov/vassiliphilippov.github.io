centroidal

1. ADJ

/ sɛnˈtrɔɪdəl / (AmE), / sɛnˈtrɔɪdəl / (BrE)
Relating to or passing through a centroid, especially in reference to geometric shapes or masses.
relating to a centroid

Examples

The centroidal axis of the beam determines how it bends under load.
This calculation finds the centroidal distance needed for engineering analysis.
Rotational inertia is minimized when the axis passes through the centroidal point.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centroid - the center of mass of a geometric object

Etymology

From 'centroid' (mathematical center of mass) + suffix '-al' (relating to).
