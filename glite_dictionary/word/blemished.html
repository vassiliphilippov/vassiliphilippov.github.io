blemished

1. ADJ

/ ˈblɛmɪʃt / (AmE), / ˈblɛmɪʃt / (BrE)
having marks or imperfections that spoil the appearance or quality of something
having marks or imperfections

Examples

The antique vase was blemished by several small chips along its rim.
The company sold the blemished products at a discounted price.
Despite her blemished record, she was given a second chance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flawed - having a defect or imperfection
  • Antonym: unblemished - free from any stain or imperfection

Etymology

From 'blemish' + suffix '-ed'; 'blemish' from Old French 'blemiss-' (stem of 'blemir', to make pale or discolored).

2. ADJ

/ ˈblɛmɪʃt / (AmE), / ˈblɛmɪʃt / (BrE)
Having a mark, flaw, or imperfection that spoils the appearance or quality of something.
having a flaw or imperfection

Examples

The antique vase was blemished by a small crack near the rim.
Despite being blemished, the fruit was still perfectly edible.
The reputation of the company was blemished by the recent scandal.

Synset Examples

a flawed diamond

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flawed - having flaws or imperfections
  • Antonym: unblemished - free from marks or flaws

Etymology

From 'blemish' + -ed, with 'blemish' from Old French 'blemir' (to make pale or discolored).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01757051-s
Synset POS: s
