pogonip

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊɡənɪp / (AmE), / ˈpɒɡənɪp / (BrE)
a dense, icy fog that forms in valley areas during very cold winter weather, consisting of suspended ice particles
dense icy winter fog

Examples

Early settlers in the Great Basin region often warned newcomers about the dangerous pogonip that could sneak in overnight.
A thick pogonip blanketed the valley, coating trees and fences with a layer of frost.
Travelers were stranded due to sudden pogonip that reduced visibility to almost zero.

Etymology

From Shoshone *paγɨnappɨh* ('cloud'), borrowed into American English in the 19th century.
