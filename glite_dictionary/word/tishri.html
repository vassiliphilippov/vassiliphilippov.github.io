Tishri

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪʃri / (AmE), / ˈtɪʃri / (BrE)
the first month of the civil year and the seventh month of the religious year in the Jewish calendar, usually falling in September and October
month in the Jewish calendar

Examples

The Jewish High Holy Days, including Yom Kippur, take place in Tishri every year.
In the Jewish calendar, Tishri marks a time of reflection and celebration.
Many important Jewish festivals fall during Tishri, such as Rosh Hashanah and Sukkot.

Etymology

From Hebrew *tišrī* (תִּשְׁרֵי), the name of the month in the Jewish calendar.
