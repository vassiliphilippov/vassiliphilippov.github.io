immigration

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪmɪˈɡreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪmɪˈɡreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or collective body of people coming to live permanently in a foreign country, especially during a specified time period
body or process of people coming to live in a foreign country

Examples

There was a surge in immigration after the new jobs were announced.
The government set new policies to control immigration into the country.
Recent immigration has changed the cultural landscape of the city.

Synset Examples

the increased immigration strengthened the colony

Related Terms

  • Antonym: emigration - the process of leaving one's country to live elsewhere

Etymology

From Latin immigratio (a going into, entering), from immigrare (to go into).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪməˈɡreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪmɪˈɡreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of coming to live permanently in a country that is not your own
moving to a new country to live

Examples

The government has introduced new policies to control immigration.
Many families sought a better life through immigration to the United States.
Debates about immigration often focus on jobs and cultural integration.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: emigration - the act of leaving one's own country to live elsewhere
  • Synonym: migration - the movement from one place to another, often referring to people or animals

Etymology

From Latin 'immigratio' (a going into), from 'immigrare' (to go into), modelled after 'emigration'.
