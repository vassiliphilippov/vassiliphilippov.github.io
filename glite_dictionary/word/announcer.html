announcer

1. NOUN

/ əˈnaʊnsər / (AmE), / əˈnaʊnsə / (BrE)
a person who introduces programs, reads news or commercials on radio or television, or provides commentary during broadcasts
person who presents or comments on radio/TV

Examples

The radio announcer introduced the evening news with a calm, steady voice.
She worked as a television announcer before becoming a producer.
The sports announcer described each play with enthusiasm during the match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: presenter - a person who introduces or hosts radio or television programs
  • Synonym: commentator - a person who provides spoken commentary during broadcasts

Etymology

From announce + -er, formed from Latin annuntiare (to announce).

2. NOUN

/ əˈnaʊnsɚ / (AmE), / əˈnaʊnsə / (BrE)
a person who makes public announcements or introduces programs, especially on radio, television, or at events
person who makes announcements

Examples

The announcer introduced the performers to the excited audience.
She dreamed of becoming a television announcer when she was a child.
At the baseball game, the announcer described every play over the loudspeakers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: presenter - person who introduces or hosts programs, especially on television or radio
  • Synonym: commentator - person who gives commentary, often during broadcasts or events

Etymology

From announce + -er, modelled on French announceur.
