Elaeagnus angustifolia

1. NOUN

/ ɛliˈæɡnəs æŋˌɡʌstɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ɛliˈæɡnəs æŋˈɡʌstɪˌfəʊliə / (BrE)
A deciduous shrubby tree of Europe and western Asia, with narrow grey leaves and small yellow fruits covered in silvery scales; sometimes spiny, also known as Russian olive.
a deciduous tree; Russian olive

Examples

Planting Elaeagnus angustifolia can provide windbreaks and shelterbelts in arid climates.
The silver leaves of Elaeagnus angustifolia make it a distinctive addition to many gardens.
Researchers are studying Elaeagnus angustifolia for its potential medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Russian olive - Common name for Elaeagnus angustifolia, a spiny deciduous tree

Etymology

From Greek 'elaia' (olive) and 'agnos' (chaste tree), Latin species epithet 'angustifolia' meaning 'narrow-leaved'.
