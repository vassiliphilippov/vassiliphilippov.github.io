backlog

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæk.lɑːɡ / (AmE), / ˈbæk.lɒɡ / (BrE)
a quantity of work or orders waiting to be dealt with that have accumulated over time
accumulated uncompleted work or orders

Examples

We have a significant backlog of customer support requests to address this week.
The factory worked overtime to clear the production backlog caused by last month's strike.
She was hired to help manage the growing backlog of legal cases in the office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: queue - a line or sequence of things awaiting their turn to be processed
  • Synonym: inventory - a list of items or goods to be dealt with or accounted for

Etymology

Originally referring to the largest log in the back of a fire, later extended metaphorically to mean a reserve of tasks.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæk.lɔg / (AmE), / ˈbæk.lɒg / (BrE)
a quantity of work or orders that has accumulated and needs to be dealt with; something saved for future use
accumulated work or orders awaiting attention

Examples

The company is working through a backlog of customer orders that built up last month.
After her vacation, she returned to a backlog of emails that needed a response.
There is a significant backlog in the court system, causing long delays for cases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: queue - a line or sequence of things awaiting their turn

Etymology

Originally referred to a large log at the back of a hearth fire; figurative modern sense dates from the 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13389225-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈbæk.lɔɡ / (AmE), / ˈbæk.lɒɡ / (BrE)
to accumulate work or tasks that have not yet been completed or dealt with
to accumulate uncompleted tasks

Examples

Due to staff shortages, orders began to backlog quickly in the warehouse.
If you don't answer your emails daily, they will backlog and become overwhelming.
The repair requests continued to backlog during the busy season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accumulate - to gather or collect a quantity over time
Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌlɑɡ / (AmE), / ˈbak.lɒɡ / (BrE)
the large log placed at the back of a hearth fire, traditionally used to keep the fire burning for a long period
large log at back of a hearth fire
archaic

Examples

The old cabin had a backlog in the fireplace to ensure the room stayed warm through the night.
She placed a fresh backlog behind the kindling to slow the fire’s burn.
Throughout winter, they relied on a sturdy backlog to keep the hearth glowing.

Etymology

Originally referred to a log placed at the back of a hearth to keep the fire burning; later broadened in meaning.
