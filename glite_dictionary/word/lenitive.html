lenitive

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɛnɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈlɛnɪtɪv / (BrE)
having the effect of soothing or alleviating pain or distress; soothing or palliative.
soothing or alleviating pain or distress

Examples

The doctor prescribed a lenitive remedy to ease her discomfort.
Music can have a lenitive effect on a troubled mind.
Aloe vera is often used for its lenitive properties on sunburned skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palliative - relieving pain without dealing with the cause
  • Synonym: soothing - having a calming or comforting effect

Etymology

From Latin lenitivus (soothing), from lenire (to soften, soothe).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛnɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈlɛnɪtɪv / (BrE)
A remedy that soothes pain or discomfort.
a soothing remedy; pain reliever

Examples

After the operation, the doctor prescribed a lenitive to ease the patient's discomfort.
Herbal teas were often used as a lenitive in traditional medicine.
The ointment acted as a gentle lenitive for his sunburned skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analgesic - a drug or remedy that relieves pain
  • Synonym: palliative - a remedy or treatment that relieves symptoms without curing the cause

Etymology

From Latin 'lenitivus', from 'lenire' (to soften or soothe).
