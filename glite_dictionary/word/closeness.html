closeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊsnəs / (AmE), / ˈkləʊsnɪs / (BrE)
a close or warm relationship or friendship between people; the quality of being emotionally near or intimate with someone
close or warm friendship; emotional intimacy

Examples

Their closeness helped them support each other through difficult times.
There was a genuine closeness between the two sisters that everyone admired.
After years of working together, a strong closeness developed among the team members.

Synset Examples

the absence of fences created a mysterious intimacy in which no one knew privacy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intimacy - close familiarity or a very personal relationship

Etymology

Formed from 'close' + noun suffix '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊznɪs / (AmE), / ˈkləʊznəs / (BrE)
the quality of being secretive or not open, especially about one's actions or intentions
lack of openness; secretiveness

Examples

Her closeness made it difficult for her colleagues to trust her intentions.
He spoke with a closeness that made others wonder what he was hiding.
The closeness of their discussions led many to suspect a hidden agenda.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: openness - the quality of being open and transparent
  • Synonym: secrecy - the quality of keeping things secret; concealment

Etymology

From 'close' (secretive) plus the suffix '-ness' forming nouns denoting a state or quality.
