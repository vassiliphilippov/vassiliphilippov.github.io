reader

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːdər / (AmE), / ˈriːdə / (BrE)
a person employed to read manuscripts and assess their suitability for publication
person who assesses manuscripts for publishing

Examples

The publishing house hired an experienced reader to evaluate unsolicited manuscripts.
She worked as a reader for a major literary agency, reviewing dozens of submissions each month.
Before the book reached the editor, it was first assessed by an in-house reader.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: editor - person who prepares content for publication
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10546502-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈriːdɚ / (AmE), / ˈriːdə / (BrE)
a person who reads written material, especially proofs, in order to find errors and mark corrections
person who checks written materials for errors

Examples

She worked as a reader at the publishing house, reviewing manuscripts for spelling and grammar mistakes.
The reader found several typographical errors that the author had missed.
His job as a reader required exceptional attention to detail and a strong command of the language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proofreader - a person whose job is to check written texts for errors before publication

Etymology

From Old English rǣdere, meaning 'one who reads'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10502353-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈriːdər / (AmE), / ˈriːdə / (BrE)
a person who enjoys reading books, magazines, or other written material regularly
person who enjoys reading

Examples

My sister is an avid reader who finishes several novels every month.
The club welcomes every reader eager to discuss literature.
As a child, he was already a passionate reader of adventure stories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bookworm - a person devoted to reading and studying

Etymology

From Old English *rǣdere*, from *rǣdan* meaning 'to read'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10528303-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈriːdər / (AmE), / ˈriːdə / (BrE)
a person who reads aloud during a church service, especially in certain Christian denominations, or someone holding a minor clerical office who has this duty.
person reading lessons in church service

Examples

The reader stood at the lectern and read the gospel to the congregation.
In some churches, the reader is responsible for leading the scripture readings.
He was appointed reader after years of dedicated service to the parish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lector - a person who reads aloud in church services, especially in the Roman Catholic Church

Etymology

From Old English rǣdere, related to 'read.'

5. NOUN

/ ˈriːdər / (AmE), / ˈriːdə / (BrE)
a person who can read, or who is literate
a person able to read

Examples

The school aims to ensure that every child becomes a confident reader.
New programs are helping adults become readers for the first time.
She is a skilled reader who enjoys challenging novels.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illiterate - a person unable to read or write

Etymology

From Old English rǣdere (one who reads), from rǣdan (to read).

a book, especially one in a graded series, used by students learning to read or improve reading skills.
a book for students learning to read

Examples

Children begin with a simple reader before progressing to more complex books.
The teacher assigned chapters from the second-grade reader for homework.

Related Terms

7. NOUN

/ ˈriːdər / (AmE), / ˈriːdə / (BrE)
a senior academic lecturer, usually ranking below a professor, at certain universities, especially in the UK and Commonwealth countries.
senior academic lecturer (UK, Commonwealth)

Examples

She was recently promoted to reader in English literature at Cambridge University.
The university appointed him as a reader in physics after years of exemplary research.
Several renowned scientists have held the position of reader before becoming full professors.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: professor - a senior academic rank above reader at many universities
  • Degree Lower: lecturer - an academic or teacher below the rank of reader or professor
