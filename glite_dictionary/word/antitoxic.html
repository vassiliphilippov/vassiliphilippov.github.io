antitoxic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.tɪˈtɑk.sɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈtɒk.sɪk / (BrE)
Counteracting the effects of a toxin or poison; neutralizing or inhibiting poison.
counteracting toxins or poisons
medical

Examples

The doctor administered an antitoxic injection to the patient after the snakebite.
Some plants contain antitoxic compounds that help protect them from harmful bacteria.
Researchers are developing more effective antitoxic treatments for various forms of poisoning.

Etymology

From Greek anti- (against) + toxikon (poison).
