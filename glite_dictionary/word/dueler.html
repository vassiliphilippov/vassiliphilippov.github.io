dueler

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːələr / (AmE), / ˈdjuːələ / (BrE)
a person who participates in a duel
person who fights in a duel

Examples

The dueler waited calmly, pistol in hand, as the sun rose over the field.
Both duelers shook hands before facing each other at ten paces.
In the nineteenth century, being a skilled dueler was considered a mark of honor among gentlemen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duelist - a person who fights duels; common spelling variant

Etymology

From 'duel' (from Latin 'duellum', meaning 'war, combat') + '-er' (agent suffix).
