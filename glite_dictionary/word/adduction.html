adduction

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / əˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
(physiology) The movement of a limb or other body part toward the midline or central axis of the body.
movement of body part toward the midline
medical

Examples

The physical therapist explained that adduction strengthens muscles near the core of the body.
An injury to the hip can limit adduction and make some movements painful.
During adduction of the arm, the limb moves inward toward the chest.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abduction - (physiology) movement of a body part away from the midline

Etymology

From Latin adductio (a bringing to), from ad- (to) + ducere (to lead).
