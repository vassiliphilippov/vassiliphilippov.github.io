slurry

1. NOUN

/ ˈslʌri / (AmE), / ˈslʌri / (BrE)
a semi-liquid mixture, typically of fine particles of solid suspended in water or another liquid
semi-liquid suspension of fine particles

Examples

The construction workers poured the wet slurry into the molds to create the foundation.
During mining, large pipes are used to transport slurry to separation facilities.
The engineer tested the density of the cement slurry before it was pumped into the well.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suspension - a liquid containing solid particles that do not dissolve

Etymology

Probably from obsolete English 'slur' (mud) or related to slurry in Middle English, meaning muddy or slimy material.
