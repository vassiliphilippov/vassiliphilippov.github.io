Pontederia cordata

1. NOUN

/ pɑnˌtidɛriə kɔrˈdeɪtə / (AmE), / pɒnˌtɛdəriə kɔːˈdeɪtə / (BrE)
A North American aquatic plant with spikes of blue flowers, commonly found in shallow water of streams and ponds.
North American aquatic plant with blue flowers

Examples

The pond was edged with the striking blue flowers of Pontederia cordata.
Many wetland restoration projects include Pontederia cordata for its beauty and ecological benefits.
We identified Pontederia cordata growing alongside cattails in the shallow stream.

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name 'Pontederia' after Italian botanist Giulio Pontedera, and species epithet 'cordata' from Latin for 'heart-shaped,' referring to its leaves.
