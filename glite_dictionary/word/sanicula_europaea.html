Sanicula Europaea

1. NOUN

/ səˈnɪkjʊlə juˈroʊpiə / (AmE), / səˈnɪkjʊlə jʊəˈrəʊpiə / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to Europe and Asia, with white to pale pink flowers, commonly known as sanicle.
a herbaceous plant native to Europe and Asia

Examples

The leaves of Sanicula Europaea were once used in traditional remedies for wounds and sores.
Sanicula Europaea grows in woodlands and shady areas across much of Europe.
While hiking, we identified several clusters of Sanicula Europaea by their pale pink flowers.

Etymology

From Latin *Sanicula* (the genus name), and *Europaea* (European), denoting its origin.
