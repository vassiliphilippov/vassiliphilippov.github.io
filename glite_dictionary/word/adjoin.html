to adjoin

1. VERB

/ əˈdʒɔɪn / (AmE), / əˈdʒɔɪn / (BrE)
to be next to or in direct contact with something; to border or be contiguous with
be next to or in contact with

Examples

Their house adjoins the local park, so it's only a short walk to the playground.
The conference room adjoins the main office, making it easy to organize meetings.
The two countries adjoin each other along a peaceful border.

Synset Examples

The two buildings touch
Their hands touched
The wire must not contact the metal cover
The surfaces contact at this point

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to abut - to be adjacent or border on; to adjoin

Etymology

From Old French 'ajoinre', from Latin 'adiungere' (to join to).

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈdʒɔɪn / (AmE), / əˈdʒɔɪn / (BrE)
to be next to or share a boundary with something
be next to; share a boundary with

Examples

Their property adjoins a large national park, making it very desirable for nature lovers.
The conference rooms adjoin each other on the second floor of the building.
The kitchen adjoins the dining area, making it easy to serve meals.

Synset Examples

Canada adjoins the U.S.
England marches with Scotland

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neighbor - to be situated next to or near (of places or buildings)
  • Antonym: separate - to set or keep apart; to not be adjoining

Etymology

From Old French joindre (to join), from Latin adiungere (to join to).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01469607-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ əˈdʒɔɪn / (AmE), / əˈdʒɔɪn / (BrE)
to attach, add, or join something to another; to be next to or joined with
to attach or be next to

Examples

The new building will adjoin the existing library.
Please adjoin the relevant documents to your application form.
The backyard adjoins a small woodland area, offering beautiful views.

Synset Examples

I adjoin a copy of your my lawyer's letter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to attach - to fasten or join one thing to another
  • Antonym: to detach - to separate or remove something from something else

Etymology

From Latin adjoinere, from ad- (to) + iungere (to join).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00183528-v
Synset POS: v
