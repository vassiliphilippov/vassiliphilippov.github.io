distiller

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈstɪlɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪlə / (BrE)
a person or company that makes alcoholic liquors by the process of distillation
maker of distilled alcoholic liquor

Examples

The Scottish distiller is renowned for producing some of the world's finest whiskies.
After years of training, she became a master distiller at the local gin company.
Many laws now require a distiller to label the origin of their spirits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brewer - a person or company that makes beer
  • Synonym: winemaker - a person or company that makes wine

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French *distiller*, from Latin *distillare* (to trickle down).
