Manannan mac Lir

1. PROPN

/ məˈnænən mək ˈlɪr / (AmE), / məˈnænən mək ˈlɪə / (BrE)
In Celtic mythology, a god of the sea and son of Ler.
Celtic sea god, son of Ler

Examples

According to legend, Manannan mac Lir traveled across the waves in a magical chariot.
Many stories describe Manannan mac Lir as a guardian of the Otherworld.
Some Irish poems pay tribute to Manannan mac Lir as the powerful lord of the sea.

Etymology

From Old Irish Manannán mac Lir, meaning 'Manannan, son of Ler (the sea)'.
