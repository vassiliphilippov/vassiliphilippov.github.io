Marshals

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrʃəlz / (AmE), / ˈmɑːʃəlz / (BrE)
(in the United States) members of the oldest federal law enforcement agency responsible for protecting the federal judiciary, transporting prisoners, protecting witnesses, and managing assets seized from criminals.
members of a U.S. federal law enforcement agency
american

Examples

The Marshals escorted the prisoner from the courthouse to a secure facility.
Federal Marshals are responsible for protecting judges and other officials in the judiciary.
After the verdict was announced, the Marshals took charge of the defendant's transfer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: United States Marshals Service - the federal agency employing marshals in the U.S.

Etymology

From Old French 'mareschal' (marshal), originally referring to a commander or officer.
