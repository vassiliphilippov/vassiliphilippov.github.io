to inhibit

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈhɪbɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɪbɪt / (BrE)
to prevent someone from doing something, or to make something less likely to happen
prevent or restrain

Examples

Strict laws can inhibit the growth of small businesses.
Some medications may inhibit the body's natural response to stress.
Parental supervision is needed so as not to inhibit children's creativity.

Synset Examples

Contact between the young was inhibited by strict social customs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prevent - to stop something from happening or someone from doing something
  • Antonym: to allow - to let someone do something or let something happen

Etymology

From Latin *inhibere* (to restrain, prevent), from *in-* (in) + *habere* (to hold).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00463563-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈhɪbɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɪbɪt / (BrE)
to make someone self-conscious or unable to act in a relaxed and natural way
make someone unable to act naturally

Examples

Being watched by the teacher can inhibit students from speaking freely in class.
His strict upbringing tended to inhibit his ability to express emotions.
Worrying about mistakes may inhibit your performance during the interview.

Synset Examples

his father's cold and distant demeanor inhibited him emotionally

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restrain - to hold something back or keep under control
  • Antonym: to encourage - to give someone confidence or support to do something

Etymology

From Latin 'inhibere' (to hold in, restrain), from 'in-' (in) + 'habere' (to have, hold).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01794202-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈhɪbɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɪbɪt / (BrE)
to limit, block, or decrease the action or effect of something
limit or decrease action or effect

Examples

Certain medications can inhibit the absorption of essential nutrients.
The scientist explained how cold temperatures inhibit the growth of bacteria.
Strong emotions may inhibit your ability to think clearly during difficult situations.

Synset Examples

inhibit the action of the enzyme
inhibit the rate of a chemical reaction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restrain - to prevent something from happening; to hold back
  • Antonym: to stimulate - to encourage something to happen or develop; the opposite of inhibit

Etymology

From Latin *inhibere* meaning 'to hold in check'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02456941-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ɪnˈhɪbɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɪbɪt / (BrE)
to consciously restrain or suppress the expression of emotions, desires, impulses, or behavior
consciously restrain or suppress

Examples

She tried to inhibit her laughter during the serious meeting.
People often inhibit their true feelings in unfamiliar situations.
His shyness tended to inhibit him from speaking up in class.

Synset Examples

suppress a smile
he let his anger bottle up until he exploded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suppress - forcibly put an end to or prevent the expression of feelings or actions
  • Synonym: to restrain - prevent someone or something from doing something; keep under control
  • Antonym: express - to show or make apparent, especially emotions or thoughts

Etymology

From Latin 'inhibere' (to hold in, check), from 'in-' (in) + 'habere' (to have, hold).

Synset POS: v
