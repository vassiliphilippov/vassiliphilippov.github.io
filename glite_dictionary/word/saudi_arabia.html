Saudi Arabia

1. PROPN

/ ˌsaʊdi əˈreɪbiə / (AmE), / ˌsaʊdi əˈreɪbɪə / (BrE)
A country in southwest Asia occupying most of the Arabian Peninsula; known as an absolute monarchy with vast oil reserves dominating its economy.
country in southwest Asia

Examples

Many expatriates move to Saudi Arabia for work opportunities in the oil industry.
Women’s rights have seen recent progress in Saudi Arabia, including the right to drive.
Mecca, located in Saudi Arabia, is the holiest site in Islam.

Etymology

Named after the ruling Al Saud family; Arabia denotes its regional location.
