husking

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌskɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhʌskɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of removing the outer covering or husks, especially from corn or other seed crops
removal of outer covering (esp. of corn)

Examples

After the husking of the corn, the kernels were separated from the cob.
The festival included a traditional husking competition, where teams raced to remove corn husks.
He injured his hand during the husking of dozens of ears of corn for the dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shelling - removing the shell or outer covering (esp. of nuts or seeds)

Etymology

From 'husk' + suffix '-ing'; 'husk' from Middle Dutch 'huesche' (pod, shell).
