leadplant

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛdˌplænt / (AmE), / ˈlɛdˌplɑːnt / (BrE)
a shrub of sandy woodlands and stream banks in the western United States, noted for its hoary pinnate leaves and dull racemose flowers, and sometimes believed to indicate the presence of lead ore
a shrub of western U.S. woodlands and stream banks

Examples

The leadplant thrives in sandy soils along the riverbanks.
Botanists identified several leadplants during their survey of the western prairie.
Some settlers believed the presence of leadplant indicated rich mineral deposits underground.

Etymology

Named for the belief it marks areas with lead ore.
