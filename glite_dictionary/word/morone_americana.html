Morone americana

1. NOUN

/ məˈroʊni əˌmɛrɪˈkænə / (AmE), / məˈrəʊni əˌmɛrɪˈkænə / (BrE)
A small silvery fish of eastern United States streams, valued as a food and game fish; commonly known as the white perch.
a small silvery fish of eastern US streams

Examples

Anglers in the Chesapeake Bay often catch Morone americana during the spring spawning run.
The Morone americana is prized for its mild flavor and firm texture.
Researchers are studying the migration patterns of Morone americana in eastern U.S. rivers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white perch - common name for Morone americana, a small silvery fish

Etymology

From Modern Latin, combining the genus name 'Morone' (of unknown origin) and 'americana', meaning 'of America'.
