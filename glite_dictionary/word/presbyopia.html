presbyopia

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɛz.biˈoʊ.pi.ə / (AmE), / ˌprɛz.bɪˈəʊ.pi.ə / (BrE)
A gradual loss of the eye's ability to focus on nearby objects, typically occurring with age due to reduced elasticity of the lens.
age-related difficulty focusing on near objects
medical

Examples

Many people develop presbyopia as they reach their mid-40s and find reading small print more difficult.
Reading glasses are commonly prescribed to correct presbyopia.
Her eye doctor explained that presbyopia is a natural part of aging, not an eye disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyperopia - farsightedness; difficulty seeing near objects clearly
  • Antonym: myopia - nearsightedness; difficulty seeing distant objects clearly

Etymology

From Greek 'presbys' (old man) + 'opia' (vision, sight).
