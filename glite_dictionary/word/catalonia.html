Catalonia

1. PROPN

/ ˌkætəˈloʊniə / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈləʊniə / (BrE)
a region in northeastern Spain with a distinct history, culture, and language, known for its autonomy movements and capital, Barcelona
region in northeastern Spain

Examples

The architecture of Catalonia is famous for its unique blend of Gothic and modernist styles.
Political tensions between Catalonia and the central Spanish government have made international headlines.
Many tourists visit Catalonia each year to enjoy its beaches, cuisine, and vibrant cultural traditions.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'Catalonia,' the origin of which is uncertain, possibly from 'Gothia' (land of the Goths) or from the name of the Laietani people.
