dugong

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːˌgɑŋ / (AmE), / ˈdjuːˌgɒŋ / (BrE)
a large, herbivorous marine mammal (Dugong dugon) found in warm coastal waters from eastern Africa to Australia; it has a bilobed tail and is related to the manatee
large marine mammal (Dugong dugon)

Examples

The dugong is often spotted grazing on seagrass in shallow coastal waters.
Scientists are concerned about declining dugong populations due to habitat loss.
A dugong can weigh up to 400 kilograms and live more than 70 years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manatee - a large, plant-eating aquatic mammal related to the dugong

Etymology

From Malay *duyung* (lady of the sea).

2. NOUN

/ duˈɡɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈdjuːɡɒŋ / (BrE)
A large marine mammal, related to the manatee, found in warm coastal waters from eastern Africa to Australia, with a bilobed, fluked tail and downward-facing snout.
large marine mammal related to the manatee

Examples

We spotted a dugong grazing on seagrass near the coral reef.
The dugong is sometimes called the 'sea cow' because of its gentle nature.
Conservationists are working to protect the endangered dugong populations in coastal regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manatee - a similar sirenian marine mammal with paddle-shaped tail

Etymology

From Malay *duyung* (meaning 'lady of the sea').
