icing

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
A sweet, flavored mixture made primarily of sugar, used to coat and decorate cakes and other baked goods.
sweet topping for cakes

Examples

She spread a thick layer of icing over the chocolate cake.
The cookies were decorated with colorful icing and sprinkles.
Add more sugar to the icing if you want it to be sweeter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frosting - sweet sugary coating for cakes, especially in American English

Etymology

From the verb 'ice' (to cover with a sugar glaze), from 'ice' (frozen water), referencing the glossy appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
the formation of ice or frost on a surface, especially as a natural or hazardous process
formation of ice on a surface

Examples

Aircraft must avoid icing conditions during flight to ensure safety.
Icing can occur on roads overnight, making driving dangerous.
The weather report warned of possible icing on the power lines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frosting - formation of a thin layer of ice or frost
  • Antonym: melting - the process of becoming liquid as a result of heat

3. NOUN

/ ˈaɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
(in ice hockey) the act of shooting the puck from behind the center line across the opponent's goal line, illegal unless touched by another player.
illegal long-distance shot in ice hockey

Examples

The team was penalized for icing when their player shot the puck across the entire rink.
The referee blew the whistle after a clear icing call in the second period.
Players often use icing as a defensive tactic when under pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offside - a play in various sports where a player is illegally ahead of the play, e.g. in hockey or soccer
