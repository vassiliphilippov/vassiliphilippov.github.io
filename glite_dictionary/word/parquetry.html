parquetry

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrˌkɛtɹi / (AmE), / ˈpɑːkɪtri / (BrE)
A decorative technique for flooring in which small pieces of wood are arranged in geometric patterns to create an inlaid surface.
decorative patterned wood flooring

Examples

The ballroom's elaborate parquetry reflected the grandeur of the historic mansion.
They restored the original parquetry to preserve the home's period charm.
Sunlight danced across the intricate parquetry patterns in the living room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marquetry - decorative inlay work, usually of wood, for surfaces such as furniture

Etymology

From French parquetterie, from parquet (small compartment).
