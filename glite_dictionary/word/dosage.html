dosage

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊsɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdəʊsɪdʒ / (BrE)
the amount of a substance, such as a medicine or radiation, taken or administered at one time or over a period
amount of medicine or substance taken
medical

Examples

Be sure to follow the prescribed dosage on the label for best results.
An incorrect dosage of the drug can be dangerous.
She asked the doctor if her dosage needed to be adjusted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dose - a measured quantity of a medicine or drug

Etymology

From French *dosage*, from *doser* (to dose).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊ.sɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdəʊ.sɪdʒ / (BrE)
the amount of a medicine or drug that is measured and taken at one time
measured amount of medicine taken

Examples

The doctor increased the dosage to help manage her symptoms.
You should not take more than the recommended dosage of this medication.
Missing a dosage can reduce the effectiveness of the treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dose - a measured amount of something, especially medicine, to be taken at one time

Etymology

From French 'dosage', from 'dose' (dose).
