infinitude

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɪnɪˌtud / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪnɪtjuːd / (BrE)
An infinite amount or extent; the quality of being infinite.
the state of being infinite

Examples

The infinitude of the universe has fascinated scientists and philosophers alike.
She was struck by the infinitude of possibilities that lay before her.
Some mathematicians explore the concept of an actual infinitude of numbers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infinity - the state or quality of being endless or unlimited

Etymology

From Late Latin infinitudo (boundlessness), from Latin infinitus (infinite).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɪnɪˌtjud / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪnɪtjuːd / (BrE)
the state or quality of being infinite; having no limit or end
limitless or infinite quality

Examples

The sheer infinitude of the universe has long fascinated astronomers.
Meditation can give a sense of peace and even infinitude to some people.
Philosophers often debate the nature and infinitude of time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infinity - the state or quality of being endless or unlimited
  • Antonym: finitude - state of having limits or bounds

Etymology

From Medieval Latin infinitudo, from Latin infinitus (infinite, boundless).
