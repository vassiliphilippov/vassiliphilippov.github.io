peat

1. NOUN

/ pit / (AmE), / piÀêt / (BrE)
A brown, soil-like material consisting of partially decomposed vegetable matter, often found in bogs, used as fuel and for improving soil.
partially decomposed plant material, used as fuel

Examples

Farmers in Ireland have traditionally used peat as a fuel for heating their homes.
The wetland was restored to preserve its valuable peat ecosystem.
Dried peat bricks are stacked near the bog, ready to be burned for warmth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turf - an alternative term for peat, especially when used as fuel

Etymology

From Middle English 'pete', from Medieval Latin 'peta' (peat).
