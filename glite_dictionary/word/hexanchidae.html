Hexanchidae

1. NOUN

/ hɛkˈsæn.kɪˌdiː / (AmE), / hɛkˈsan.kɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of primitive sharks characterized by having six or seven gill slits, found in deep waters worldwide.
family of primitive sharks

Examples

The Hexanchidae are known for their extra gill slits compared to most other sharks.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Hexanchidae in the deep Pacific Ocean.
Members of the Hexanchidae are sometimes called cow sharks due to their distinctive appearance.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'hexa-' (six) and 'anchos' (bend, angle), referring to the gill slits.

a family of primitive sharks with six or seven gill slits, known for their ancient lineage and found in deep waters worldwide.
a family of primitive sharks
medical

Examples

The Hexanchidae family includes several deep-sea shark species with ancient features.
Researchers found a new member of the Hexanchidae near the ocean floor.
Unlike most modern sharks, the Hexanchidae have more than five gill slits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cow shark - a shark belonging to the family Hexanchidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'hex' (six) and 'anchos' (gill), with taxonomic suffix '-idae'.
