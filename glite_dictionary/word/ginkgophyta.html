Ginkgophyta

1. NOUN

/ gɪŋˈgoʊfəˌtə / (AmE), / gɪŋˈgɒfɪtə / (BrE)
a subdivision or class of gymnosperm plants containing the ginkgo and its extinct relatives
group of gymnosperm plants including the ginkgo

Examples

Scientists classify the Ginkgophyta as a distinct evolutionary group within the gymnosperms.
Fossil evidence shows that Ginkgophyta had a much wider distribution in the past.
The most famous living member of Ginkgophyta is the Ginkgo biloba tree.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Gymnospermophyta - the division of seed plants known as gymnosperms
  • Synonym: Ginkgo - the only living genus in Ginkgophyta, a distinctive tree

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining 'Ginkgo' (the genus) and Greek 'phyta' (plants).
