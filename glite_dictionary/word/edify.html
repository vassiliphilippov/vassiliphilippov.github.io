to edify

1. VERB

/ ˈɛdəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɛdɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to instruct or improve someone intellectually or morally; to enlighten
to instruct or enlighten

Examples

The teacher sought to edify her students by sharing inspiring stories from history.
A good book can edify as well as entertain its reader.
He spoke with the hope that his words would edify those who listened.

Synset Examples

Can you enlighten me — I don't understand this proposal?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enlighten - to give someone greater knowledge or understanding about a subject
  • Synonym: to teach - to impart knowledge or instruct someone
  • Antonym: to confuse - to make someone unable to think clearly or understand

Etymology

From Latin 'aedificare' (to build), later used figuratively for building up the mind or character.
