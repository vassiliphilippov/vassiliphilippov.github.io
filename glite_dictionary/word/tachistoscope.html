tachistoscope

1. NOUN

/ təˈkɪstəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / təˈkɪstɪˌskəʊp / (BrE)
a scientific instrument that briefly presents visual stimuli for psychological and perception experiments
device for brief visual stimulus exposure

Examples

The experiment involved showing words on a tachistoscope for just 200 milliseconds.
Researchers use a tachistoscope to study how quickly people recognize images.
A classic tachistoscope can control exactly how long a subject sees each slide.

Etymology

From Greek 'tachistos' (swiftest) and 'skopein' (to look or examine).
