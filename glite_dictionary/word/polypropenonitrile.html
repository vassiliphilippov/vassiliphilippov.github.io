polypropenonitrile

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑliˌproʊˌpɛnəˈnaɪtrəl / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˌprəʊˌpɛnɪˈnaɪtrɪl / (BrE)
A synthetic acrylic resin used to make strong, soft, crease-resistant fabrics, known commercially as Acrilan.
synthetic acrylic fiber (Acrilan)

Examples

The laboratory developed a new blend using polypropenonitrile for enhanced durability.
Many winter garments are made from polypropenonitrile fibers due to their softness and strength.
The crease-resistant property of polypropenonitrile makes it popular in the textile industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acrylic fiber - Synthetic fiber made from acrylic resin, used in textiles

Etymology

From 'poly-' (many), 'propeno' (propene, a hydrocarbon), and 'nitrile' (organic compound with a -C≡N group).
