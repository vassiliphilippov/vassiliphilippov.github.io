bunion

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌn.jən / (AmE), / ˈbʌn.jən / (BrE)
A painful swelling of the joint at the base of the big toe, often causing the toe to deviate towards the other toes.
swelling at big toe joint
medical

Examples

She had difficulty finding comfortable shoes because of her painful bunion.
The doctor recommended surgery to correct the bunion on his right foot.
Wearing tight shoes for years contributed to the development of her bunion.

Etymology

Perhaps from French 'oignon' (onion), referring to the lump's shape.
