botulinal

1. ADJ

/ ˌbɒtʃəˈlaɪnəl / (AmE), / ˌbɒtjʊˈlɪnəl / (BrE)
relating to or produced by the bacterium Clostridium botulinum, which causes botulism.
relating to botulinus bacterium or botulism
medical

Examples

The patient showed symptoms consistent with botulinal toxin exposure.
A botulinal infection requires immediate medical intervention to prevent paralysis.
Researchers are studying botulinal antitoxins to counteract the effects of contaminated food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botulinic - relating to botulinus or botulism

Etymology

From New Latin botulinus, referring to the bacterium Clostridium botulinum.
