cationic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkætaɪˈɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌkætaɪˈɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to or containing cations (positively charged ions)
relating to cations (positive ions)
medical

Examples

The dye used in this process is cationic and binds easily to the fabric.
In a cationic solution, the positive ions are attracted to the cathode.
Many detergents have cationic surfactants that help remove dirt.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anionic - relating to anions (negatively charged ions)

Etymology

Formed from 'cation' (from Greek 'kato', meaning 'down') and '-ic' (pertaining to).
