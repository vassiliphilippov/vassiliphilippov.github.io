lifetime

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪftaɪm / (AmE), / ˈlaɪftaɪm / (BrE)
The period of time during which something exists or is functional, especially from its beginning to its end.
period during which something exists or functions

Examples

This computer has a projected lifetime of five years before it becomes obsolete.
The warranty covers the product for its entire lifetime.
Her achievements will be remembered long after her lifetime.

Synset Examples

the battery had a short life
he lived a long and happy life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lifespan - The length of time for which a person or animal lives or a thing is functional

Etymology

From 'life' + 'time', first recorded in the early 19th century.
