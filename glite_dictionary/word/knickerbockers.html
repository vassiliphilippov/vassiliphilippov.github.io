knickerbockers

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪkərˌbɑkərz / (AmE), / ˈnɪkəbɒkəz / (BrE)
Loose-fitting trousers that are gathered at the knee or just below, traditionally worn by boys and for sports, especially in the early 20th century.
loose knee-length trousers

Examples

He wore a pair of knickerbockers and high socks for the school play.
Vintage photographs often show boys dressed in knickerbockers and caps.
The golfers preferred knickerbockers for their comfort and style on the course.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breeches - short trousers fastened just below the knee

Etymology

From the surname Knickerbocker, used by Washington Irving as a pseudonym; later associated with early Dutch settlers in New York and their style of dress.
