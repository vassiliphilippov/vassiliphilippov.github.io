Psoralea

1. NOUN

/ sɔːˈreɪliə / (AmE), / sɒˈreɪliə / (BrE)
a genus of herbs or shrubs with glandular compound leaves and spicate or racemose purple or white flowers, widely distributed in various regions
genus of herbs or shrubs in botany

Examples

The species of Psoralea are commonly found in dry, open habitats in many parts of the world.
Botanists have identified several medicinal properties in the roots of Psoralea plants.
A purple-flowered Psoralea shrub added color and texture to the native plant garden.

Etymology

From Greek psōraleos (having the itch or mange, scurfy), referring to the glandular texture of some species.

2. NOUN

/ soʊˈreɪliə / (AmE), / sɒˈreɪlɪə / (BrE)
A genus of herbs or shrubs with glandular compound leaves and spicate or racemose purple or white flowers, found in various regions.
a genus of herbaceous plants or shrubs

Examples

The seeds of Psoralea have been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
Many species in the Psoralea genus thrive in dry, open habitats.
Botanists recently reclassified several Psoralea species into a new genus.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'psoralea', meaning a plant connected with 'psora' (itching), referring to its traditional medicinal use.
