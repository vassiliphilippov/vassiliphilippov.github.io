hypochondriasis

1. NOUN

/ haɪ.poʊˌkɑn.driˈæ.sɪs / (AmE), / haɪ.pəʊˌkɒn.drɪˈeɪ.sɪs / (BrE)
excessive and persistent worry about having a serious illness, based on imaginary or exaggerated symptoms
anxiety disorder over imagined illness
medical

Examples

She visited several doctors due to her hypochondriasis, even though no illness was found.
People with hypochondriasis often misinterpret normal bodily sensations as signs of serious disease.
His hypochondriasis caused constant distress for both himself and his family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypochondria - excessive worry about one's health; another term for hypochondriasis
  • Synonym: health anxiety - persistent fear of illness despite little or no medical evidence

Etymology

From Greek hypochondria (upper abdomen), historically believed to be the origin of melancholic feelings.
