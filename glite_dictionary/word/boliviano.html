boliviano

1. NOUN

/ boʊˌliviˈɑːnoʊ / (AmE), / bəʊˌliviˈɑːnəʊ / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in Bolivia, equal to 100 centavos
Bolivian unit of currency

Examples

Tourists must exchange dollars for bolivianos when traveling in Bolivia.
The price of lunch was 50 bolivianos at the local restaurant.
The boliviano has remained relatively stable in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: centavo - a monetary unit equal to one hundredth of the boliviano

Etymology

Spanish, named after Simón Bolívar, a South American independence leader.
