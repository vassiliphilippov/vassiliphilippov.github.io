falloff

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔlˌɑf / (AmE), / ˈfɔːlɒf / (BrE)
a decrease or decline in level, amount, quality, or performance
a decline or reduction

Examples

There has been a significant falloff in sales since last quarter.
The manager noticed a falloff in employee productivity after the restructuring.
A sudden falloff in temperature surprised everyone during the event.

Synset Examples

the team went into a slump
a gradual slack in output
a drop-off in attendance
a falloff in quality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decline - a gradual decrease in quality, quantity, or importance
  • Synonym: drop-off - a noticeable reduction or decrease
  • Antonym: increase - an instance of rising or becoming greater
