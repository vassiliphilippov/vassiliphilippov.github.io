dibber

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪb.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈdɪb.ə / (BrE)
a pointed hand tool used for making holes in the ground to plant seeds, bulbs, or seedlings
pointed gardening tool for planting

Examples

He used a dibber to make evenly spaced holes for his tomato plants.
The gardener picked up her dibber and started planting tulip bulbs in neat rows.
A sturdy wooden dibber makes planting small seeds much easier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dibble - a gardening tool for making planting holes (synonym of dibber)

Etymology

From the dialectal use of 'dib' (to thrust) plus the suffix '-er'.
