bionics

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈɑnɪks / (AmE), / baɪˈɒnɪks / (BrE)
The study and application of biological methods and systems found in nature to the design and engineering of electronic and mechanical systems.
application of biology to engineering systems

Examples

Advances in bionics have led to making more lifelike and functional prosthetic limbs.
The field of bionics inspired engineers to design self-healing materials.
She studied bionics to learn how animal movement can improve robotic design.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biomimetics - the design and production of materials or systems modeled on biological entities or processes

Etymology

Blend of 'biology' and 'electronics', coined in the 1960s.
