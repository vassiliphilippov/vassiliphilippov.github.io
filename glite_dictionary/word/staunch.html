staunch

1. ADJ

/ stɑntʃ / (AmE), / stɔːntʃ / (BrE)
firm and dependable, especially in supporting someone or something; loyal and strong in commitment.
firm and loyal; dependable supporter

Examples

Despite criticism, she remained a staunch supporter of the cause.
As a staunch friend, he always stood by me in difficult times.
The senator is known as a staunch defender of civil liberties.

Synset Examples

a steadfast ally
a staunch defender of free speech
unswerving devotion
unswerving allegiance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steadfast - very firm and unwavering in support or belief
  • Synonym: loyal - showing firm and constant support
  • Antonym: unreliable - not dependable or trustworthy

Etymology

From Old French *estanchier* (to stop, quench), likely via Latin *stagnare* (to make stagnant), later developing figurative sense of steadfastness.

2. VERB

/ stɑntʃ / (AmE), / stɔːntʃ / (BrE)
to stop the flow of a liquid, especially blood, from a wound or source
stop the flow of liquid, especially blood

Examples

The nurse tried to staunch the bleeding with a clean cloth.
Quick action can staunch the flow of water from a broken pipe.
He pressed hard on the cut to staunch the blood.

Synset Examples

staunch the blood flow
stem the tide

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stem - to stop or restrict the flow of something, especially liquid
  • Synonym: block - to obstruct or prevent movement or flow

Etymology

From Old French *estanchier* (to stop, stem), related to Latin *stagnare* (to stagnate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00355106-v
Synset POS: v
