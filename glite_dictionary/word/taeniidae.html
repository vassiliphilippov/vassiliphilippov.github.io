Taeniidae

1. NOUN

/ tiːˈnaɪɪˌdiː / (AmE), / tiːˈnaɪɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of tapeworms in the class Cestoda, which includes species that infect the intestines of vertebrates
family of tapeworms (Cestoda)
medical

Examples

Infections with Taeniidae are most commonly diagnosed through examination of stool samples.
The Taeniidae family includes several species responsible for diseases in humans and animals.
Medical researchers are studying the life cycles of Taeniidae to develop better treatments.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Taenia, from Greek tainia (ribbon, band).
