Cypripedium parviflorum

1. NOUN

/ saɪprəˈpidiəm ˌpɑːrvɪˈflɔrəm / (AmE), / sɪprɪˈpiːdiəm ˌpɑːvɪˈflɔːrəm / (BrE)
A species of orchid with maroon to purple-brown flowers and a distinctive yellow lip, native to Europe, North America, and Japan, also known as the yellow lady's slipper.
species of orchid; yellow lady's slipper

Examples

The Cypripedium parviflorum blooms attract hikers and photographers each spring in the forest.
Botanists study the Cypripedium parviflorum to better understand orchid pollination mechanisms.
Conservation efforts are in place to protect wild populations of Cypripedium parviflorum from habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow lady's slipper - common name for Cypripedium parviflorum; a yellow-flowered wild orchid

Etymology

From Greek *kypris* (Venus, goddess of love) and *pedilon* (slipper) plus Latin *parviflorum* (small-flowered).
