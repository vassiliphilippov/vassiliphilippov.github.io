smothering

1. ADJ

/ ˈsmʌðərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsmʌðərɪŋ / (BrE)
(of air, heat, or atmosphere) causing difficulty in breathing, especially due to lack of fresh air and oppressive heat.
causing difficulty in breathing; oppressive (of air/heat)

Examples

The smothering heat in the attic made it hard to breathe.
Thick, smothering smoke filled the small room after the fire started.
She felt trapped in the smothering air of the crowded subway car.

Synset Examples

the choking June dust
the smothering soft voices
smothering heat
the room was suffocating — hot and airless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suffocating - making breathing difficult due to lack of fresh air
  • Synonym: oppressive - uncomfortably hot and close
  • Antonym: refreshing - giving a sense of coolness and fresh air; invigorating

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'smother' + present participle suffix '-ing'.
