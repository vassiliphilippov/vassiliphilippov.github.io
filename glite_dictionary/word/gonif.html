gonif

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊnɪf / (AmE), / ˈɡɒnɪf / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly US) A thief or dishonest person; a scoundrel, often used as a term of mild abuse.
a thief or dishonest person
informal american

Examples

They finally caught the gonif who had been stealing from the local market.
Don’t trust him—he’s a real gonif if you ask me.
That gonif tried to sell me a fake watch!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crook - a dishonest person, especially a criminal
  • Synonym: thief - a person who steals
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere

Etymology

From Yiddish גנב‎ (ganév, 'thief'), from Hebrew גַּנָּב (gannāb).
