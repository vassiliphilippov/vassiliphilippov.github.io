Alnus serrulata

1. NOUN

/ ˈælnəs sərʊˈleɪtə / (AmE), / ˈalnəs səˈruːleɪtə / (BrE)
A common shrub of the eastern United States with smooth bark, belonging to the alder genus.
a shrub species of alder in eastern US

Examples

The wetlands were filled with Alnus serrulata, creating a dense, green thicket along the water's edge.
Researchers studied the effect of flooding on Alnus serrulata populations in the eastern United States.
Gardeners appreciate Alnus serrulata for its ability to stabilize stream banks and provide wildlife habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alder - a genus of trees and shrubs in the birch family

Etymology

From Latin: 'Alnus' meaning alder, and 'serrulata' meaning finely serrated (referring to leaf edges).
