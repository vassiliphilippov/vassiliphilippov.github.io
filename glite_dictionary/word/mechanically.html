mechanically

1. ADV

/ məˈkænɪkli / (AmE), / mɪˈkænɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that is related to machines or carried out by a mechanism; also, in an automatic or uninspired way.
in a mechanical or automatic manner

Examples

The factory doors are designed to open mechanically during emergencies.
She replied mechanically, barely listening to the question.
Grass was cut mechanically by the new automated mower.

Synset Examples

this door opens mechanically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: automatically - in a way that happens without conscious thought or effort

Etymology

From 'mechanical' + '-ly', with 'mechanical' from Latin 'mechanicus' (of or belonging to machines).

2. ADV

/ məˈkænɪkli / (AmE), / mɪˈkænɪkəli / (BrE)
In a way that shows no emotion or spontaneity, as if performed by a machine.
in a machinelike, emotionless way

Examples

She nodded mechanically, barely listening to the discussion.
He responded mechanically to each question, lacking genuine enthusiasm.
After years of practice, she played the piano piece almost mechanically.

Synset Examples

he smiled mechanically

Related Terms

  • Antonym: emotionally - in a way that shows emotion or feeling

Etymology

From mechanical + -ly. 'Mechanical' ultimately from Greek mēkhanē (machine).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00114811-r
Synset POS: r
