megacycle

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛɡəˌsaɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈmɛɡəˌsaɪkəl / (BrE)
A unit of frequency equal to one million cycles per second, chiefly used in electronics and radio.
unit of frequency; one million cycles per second

Examples

The frequency of the transmitter was set to 3 megacycles for the experiment.
Early radio equipment often referred to frequencies in megacycle units.
The scientist explained that one megacycle is equivalent to one megahertz.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: megahertz - unit of frequency equal to one million hertz

Etymology

From mega- (meaning 'million') and cycle ('complete oscillation or wave'), following metric system conventions.
