prosody

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.sə.di / (AmE), / ˈprɒs.ə.di / (BrE)
the patterns of rhythm, stress, and intonation in spoken language, often affecting meaning or emotion
patterns of stress and intonation in language

Examples

The teacher explained how prosody can change the meaning of a sentence.
Actors must master prosody to convey different emotions through speech.
Studying prosody helps linguists understand language rhythm and stress patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intonation - the rise and fall of the voice in speaking

Etymology

From Latin 'prosodia', from Greek 'prosōidia' (song sung to music, intonation, accent).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːsədi / (AmE), / ˈprɒsədi / (BrE)
The patterns of rhythm, sound, and meter in poetry; the study or system of versification in language.
study of poetic rhythm and meter

Examples

The professor gave an insightful lecture on prosody and its importance in English poetry.
Aspiring poets often study prosody to master the technical aspects of verse.
Her understanding of prosody allowed her to compose beautifully rhythmic sonnets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meter - the rhythmic structure of verse
  • Synonym: versification - the art or practice of composing verse

Etymology

From Latin 'prosodia', from Greek 'prosōidia' (song sung to music, accent).

3. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːsədi / (AmE), / ˈprɒsədi / (BrE)
The study of poetic meter, rhythm, and the patterns of sounds in verse.
study of poetic meter and rhythm

Examples

Her dissertation focused on prosody in English poetry of the Victorian era.
A deep understanding of prosody can enhance both the reading and the writing of verse.
The textbook includes several chapters detailing the rules and patterns found in prosody.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metrics - the study of poetic meter and versification
  • Synonym: scansion - the analysis of verse to show its meter

Etymology

From Latin prosodia, from Greek prosōidia (accent of a syllable, song sung to music).
