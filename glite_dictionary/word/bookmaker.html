bookmaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊkˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈbʊkˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
a person or company that accepts and pays out bets, especially on sporting events such as horse races
person who takes bets (esp. on sports)

Examples

She placed her wager with a well-known bookmaker at the local racetrack.
The bookmaker adjusted the odds after several large bets were made on the favorite.
Online bookmakers have made sports betting more accessible than ever before.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bookie - informal synonym for bookmaker

Etymology

Formed from 'book' (record of bets) + 'maker'; first recorded in the 19th century.
