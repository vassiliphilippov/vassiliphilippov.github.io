maligner

1. NOUN

/ məˈlaɪnər / (AmE), / məˈlaɪnə / (BrE)
a person who maliciously attacks or criticizes the reputation of another, especially by slander or libel
one who slanders or defames others

Examples

The internet has made it easier for a maligner to spread false information about others.
Despite the claims of the maligner, the politician's reputation remained intact.
She refused to let the words of a maligner affect her confidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defamer - a person who damages the reputation of another
  • Synonym: libeler - a person who publishes a false statement that causes people to have a bad opinion of someone
  • Synonym: slanderer - a person who makes false and damaging statements about someone

Etymology

From 'malign' (to speak evil of), ultimately from Latin 'malignus' (having an evil nature).
