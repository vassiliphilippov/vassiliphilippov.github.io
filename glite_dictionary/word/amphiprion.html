Amphiprion

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmfɪˈpraɪɒn / (AmE), / ˌamfɪˈprʌɪɒn / (BrE)
A genus of small, brightly colored marine fishes known as clownfish or anemonefish, commonly found in association with sea anemones.
genus of clownfish or anemonefish

Examples

The Amphiprion genus is best known for its symbiotic relationship with sea anemones.
Many marine aquarists keep Amphiprion species for their vivid colors and interesting behaviors.
Biologists study Amphiprion to learn about mutualism in reef ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clownfish - small, brightly colored marine fish of the subfamily Amphiprioninae

Etymology

From Greek 'amphi' (both, around) + 'prion' (saw), referring to the serrated preopercle of some species.

2. NOUN

/ æmˌfaɪˈpriːən / (AmE), / amˌfɪˈprʌɪən / (BrE)
A genus of small, brightly colored marine fishes, commonly known as clownfish or anemonefish, found in the Indo-Pacific region and noted for their association with sea anemones.
genus of clownfish or anemonefish

Examples

The genus Amphiprion includes the famous orange-and-white clownfish seen in coral reefs.
Researchers conducted a study on the symbiotic relationship between Amphiprion and sea anemones.
Many species within the Amphiprion genus exhibit vibrant colors and protective behaviors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clownfish - common name for fishes of the genus Amphiprion

Etymology

From Greek 'amphi-' (both, around) and 'prion' (saw), referring to the saw-like edge on the fish's gill cover.
