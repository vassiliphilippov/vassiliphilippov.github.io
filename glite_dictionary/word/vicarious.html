vicarious

1. ADJ

/ vaɪˈkɛriəs / (AmE), / vɪˈkeəriəs / (BrE)
(of a function or occurrence) happening in an abnormal part of the body instead of the usual site involved in that function, especially in medical contexts.
occurring in an abnormal body part (medical)
medical

Examples

She was diagnosed with vicarious menstruation when bleeding occurred from her nose instead of the uterus.
In rare cases, a vicarious discharge of blood may come from the skin during menstruation.
Medical literature documents instances of vicarious lactation, where milk is produced outside the normal glands.

Synset Examples

vicarious menstruation

Etymology

From Latin vicarius (substitute, deputy), meaning acting as a substitute.

2. ADJ

/ vaɪˈkɛriəs / (AmE), / vɪˈkeəriəs / (BrE)
experienced indirectly, through the feelings or actions of another person
experienced indirectly

Examples

She felt vicarious joy as she watched her friend win the award.
Many people experience vicarious thrills by reading adventure novels.
His vicarious participation in the game was almost as intense as playing it himself.

Related Terms

3. ADJ

/ vaɪˈkɛriəs / (AmE), / vɪˈkeəriəs / (BrE)
experienced in the imagination through the feelings or actions of another person, or done or suffered by one person as a substitute for another.
experienced or done as a substitute for another

Examples

Many Christians believe in the concept of vicarious atonement, where one person's sacrifice redeems others.
He found vicarious satisfaction in his daughter's achievements, as if they were his own.
The punishment was vicarious, falling on the innocent brother instead of the true culprit.

Synset Examples

vicarious atonement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitute - a person or thing acting or serving in place of another

Etymology

From Latin 'vicarius' (substitute, deputy).
