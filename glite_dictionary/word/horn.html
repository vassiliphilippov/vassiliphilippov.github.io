horn

1. NOUN

/ hɔrn / (AmE), / hɔːn / (BrE)
the high, curved projection at the front of a Western saddle, used for securing a rope or holding onto, often metal and covered with leather
metal projection on Western saddle

Examples

The cowboy gripped the horn tightly as the horse bucked under him.
She wrapped the rope around the horn of her saddle during the cattle drive.
The leather covering on the horn was fraying from years of use.

2. NOUN

/ hɔrn / (AmE), / hɔːn / (BrE)
a hard, pointed growth projecting from the head of certain animals, such as cattle, goats, or rhinoceroses, or anything resembling such a growth
hard, pointed growth on animal's head

Examples

The bull charged with its sharp horn lowered toward its opponent.
Some beetles have a prominent horn on their heads.
The unicorn is a mythical creature said to have a single horn in the middle of its forehead.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: antler - branched bony projection on the head of a deer

Etymology

Old English *horn*, from Proto-Germanic *hurnan* (horn).
