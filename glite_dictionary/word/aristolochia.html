Aristolochia

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛrɪstəˈloʊkiə / (AmE), / ˌærɪstəˈləʊkiə / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the birthwort family, including many species such as birthworts and Dutchman's-pipe, known for their distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers.
a plant genus (birthworts, Dutchman's-pipe)

Examples

The garden was home to a rare species of Aristolochia that attracted unusual butterflies.
Some traditional medicines used extracts from Aristolochia, though many are now considered toxic.
The distinctive flowers of Aristolochia resemble the shape of a curved pipe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birthwort - A common name for certain species of Aristolochia

Etymology

From Latin Aristolochia, from Ancient Greek aristolochia: 'aristos' (best) + 'lokheia' (childbirth), due to its ancient medicinal use in childbirth.

2. NOUN

/ ˌærɪstəˈloʊkiə / (AmE), / ˌærɪstəˈləʊkɪə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants known as birthworts or Dutchman's-pipe, comprising herbaceous or woody vines and shrubs commonly found in warm regions.
genus of flowering plants (birthworts)

Examples

The Aristolochia species are well known for their pipe-shaped flowers that attract pollinators.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Aristolochia vines for their unusual appearance and rapid growth.
Some Aristolochia plants have been used in traditional medicine, though caution is advised due to their toxicity.

Etymology

From Latin 'Aristolochia', from Ancient Greek 'aristolochia', meaning 'best childbirth', due to the plant's traditional medicinal use.
