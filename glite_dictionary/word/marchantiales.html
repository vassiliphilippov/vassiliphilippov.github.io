Marchantiales

1. NOUN

/ mɑːrˌkænʃiˈeɪliːz / (AmE), / mɑːˌkantɪˈeɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of liverworts in which the gametophyte is internally differentiated
an order of liverworts (plants)
medical

Examples

The Marchantiales are commonly found in damp, shaded environments.
Researchers studied the reproductive cycle of Marchantiales to better understand liverwort diversity.
Several members of the Marchantiales are important indicators of ecosystem health.

Etymology

From New Latin, after the genus Marchantia.

2. NOUN

/ mɑrˌkæn.tiˈeɪl.iːz / (AmE), / mɑːˌkan.tiˈeɪ.liːz / (BrE)
an order of liverworts with a gametophyte that is internally differentiated
an order of liverworts (plants)

Examples

The Marchantiales are commonly studied for their unique reproductive structures.
Botanists have found new species within the Marchantiales in tropical regions.
Many Marchantiales play an important role in soil and water conservation.

Etymology

From the genus name *Marchantia*, named after Italian botanist N. Marchant, plus the taxonomic suffix -ales.
