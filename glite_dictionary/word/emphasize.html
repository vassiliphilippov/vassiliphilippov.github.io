to emphasize

1. VERB

/ ˈɛmfəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈemfəsaɪz / (BrE)
to give special importance or attention to something; to stress
give special importance to

Examples

The teacher emphasized the importance of submitting assignments on time.
We should emphasize safety procedures during our training sessions.
In her speech, she emphasized the need for more community involvement.

Synset Examples

Dr. Jones emphasizes exercise in addition to a change in diet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stress - to give particular importance or significance to something
  • Synonym: highlight - to make something stand out as important or noticeable
  • Antonym: to downplay - to make something seem less important

Etymology

From Greek 'emphasis' (appearance, show, significance) via Latin 'emphasizare'.

2. VERB

/ ˈɛmfəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛmfəsaɪz / (BrE)
to give special importance or attention to something when speaking or writing; to make something more noticeable
give special importance or attention

Examples

The manager used charts to emphasize his main points during the meeting.
Teachers often emphasize the importance of reading outside the classroom.
He emphasized his disappointment with the results in his closing remarks.

Synset Examples

Her gesture emphasized her words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stress - to place emphasis on something in speech or writing
  • Synonym: highlight - to make something stand out or be more noticeable
  • Antonym: to downplay - to make something seem less important

Etymology

From Greek emphasis (appearance, significance), via Latin emphasizare; later influenced by the noun 'emphasis'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01016618-v
Synset POS: v
