surfbird

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːfˌbɝːd / (AmE), / ˈsɜːfˌbɜːd / (BrE)
a sandpiper-like shorebird (Aphriza virgata) found on the Pacific coasts of North and South America, known for its association with rocky shores and surf zones
Pacific shorebird, similar to a sandpiper

Examples

We spotted a surfbird picking its way along the rocky shoreline.
The surfbird migrates thousands of miles between its breeding and wintering grounds.
Several surfbirds were feeding near the crashing waves, undisturbed by the spray.

Etymology

Formed from 'surf' (waves breaking on the shore) + 'bird', referring to its habitat.
