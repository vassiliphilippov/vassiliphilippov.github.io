postage

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊstɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstɪdʒ / (BrE)
the amount of money required to send a letter or package by mail
money charged for mailing

Examples

The postage for international packages is much higher than for domestic mail.
Make sure you put enough postage on the envelope before mailing it.
She forgot to pay the postage, so her letter was returned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shipping - the charge for transporting goods, especially by mail or courier

Etymology

From French 'postage', from 'poste' (mail)' + '-age' (action or result).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊstɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstɪdʒ / (BrE)
A small adhesive token, such as a stamp, affixed to mail to show that postal charges have been paid.
adhesive token for paid postal fees

Examples

You need to put sufficient postage on the envelope before mailing it.
The postage for international letters is higher than for domestic ones.
She collects old postage from different countries as a hobby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stamp - a small piece of paper indicating payment for post

Etymology

From post (mail) + -age, meaning charges or payment for sending mail.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06808977-n
Synset POS: n
