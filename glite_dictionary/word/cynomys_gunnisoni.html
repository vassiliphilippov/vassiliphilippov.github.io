Cynomys gunnisoni

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪnoʊˌmɪs ɡʌˈnɪsəni / (AmE), / ˌsɪnəmɪs ɡʌˈnɪsəni / (BrE)
A species of prairie dog native to the southwestern United States, recognized for its white-tipped tail.
a species of prairie dog

Examples

The population of Cynomys gunnisoni in New Mexico has declined due to habitat loss.
Biologists observed several colonies of Cynomys gunnisoni during the summer survey.
The distinctive white-tipped tail helps identify Cynomys gunnisoni from other prairie dog species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prairie dog - a burrowing rodent native to grasslands of North America

Etymology

From the genus name Cynomys (from Greek kun-, 'dog' + mys, 'mouse') and the specific epithet gunnisoni, after explorer John Gunnison.
