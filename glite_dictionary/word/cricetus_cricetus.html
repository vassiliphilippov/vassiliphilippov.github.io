Cricetus cricetus

1. PROPN

/ kraɪˈsiːtəs kraɪˈsiːtəs / (AmE), / krɪˈsiːtəs krɪˈsiːtəs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the European hamster, a rodent species native to Europe and parts of Asia
the European hamster (rodent species)

Examples

The Cricetus cricetus population has declined sharply in many countries due to habitat loss.
Researchers observed the burrowing behavior of Cricetus cricetus in the wild.
Unlike pet hamsters, Cricetus cricetus is much larger and leads a solitary life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: European hamster - a large hamster native to Europe and Asia

Etymology

From Latinized Greek; 'cricetus' is from Latin, originally from Slavic roots for 'hamster'.
