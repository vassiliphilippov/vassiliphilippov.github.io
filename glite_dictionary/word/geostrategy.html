geostrategy

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiː.oʊˈstræt.ə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː.əʊˈstræt.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
The branch of geopolitics concerned with strategic planning and policy based on the geographic and political features of a region.
strategic planning in geopolitics

Examples

The country's foreign policy is heavily influenced by geostrategy and its location between major powers.
Experts in geostrategy often analyze military and economic interests on a global scale.
Understanding geostrategy is essential for crafting long-term national security plans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geopolitics - study of the effects of geography on politics

Etymology

From geo- (relating to the earth) + strategy, modeled after similar terms in political science.
