Dicranales

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈkreɪnəˌliːz / (AmE), / dɪˈkreɪnəˌliːz / (BrE)
an order of mosses characterized by upright stems (gametophores) and sporophytes that develop at the tips of stems
order of mosses with upright stems

Examples

The Dicranales are noted for having sporophytes borne at the tips of upright shoots.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Dicranales in the northern forests.
Many mosses in the Dicranales play an important role in stabilizing soil and retaining moisture.

Etymology

From New Latin Dicranum (a moss genus) + -ales (indicating order).

2. NOUN

/ daɪˈkreɪneɪlz / (AmE), / dɪˈkrɑːneɪliːz / (BrE)
An order of mosses widely distributed, characterized by erect reproductive stems (gametophores) and sporophytes at the tips of the stems.
order of mosses with erect gametophores

Examples

The Dicranales are easily recognized by their upright stems and capsule-bearing sporophytes.
Many forest floors are covered by mosses belonging to the Dicranales.
Researchers studied the reproductive adaptations found in various species of the Dicranales.

Etymology

From New Latin, from 'Dicranum' (a genus of mosses) + '-ales' (order suffix in taxonomy).
