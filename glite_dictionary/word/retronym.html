retronym

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛtrəˌnɪm / (AmE), / ˈrɛtrəʊˌnɪm / (BrE)
a newly created word or phrase used to distinguish an original object or concept from a more recent version or form
word created to distinguish original version

Examples

The term retronym refers to words like 'acoustic guitar', which differentiate from newer types like 'electric guitar'.
With the rise of e-mail, the phrase 'postal mail' became a common retronym.
The introduction of color television made 'black-and-white television' a necessary retronym.

Synset Examples

Nobody ever heard of analog clocks until digital clocks became common, so ‘analog clock’ is a retronym

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neologism - a newly coined word or expression

Etymology

From retro- (backwards) + -nym (name); coined in the 1980s.
