Moloch

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊ.lɑk / (AmE), / ˈmɒl.ək / (BrE)
A powerful force or system that demands extreme sacrifice or obedience, often at great human cost.
force demanding sacrifice or obedience

Examples

Some view the relentless pursuit of profit as a modern Moloch demanding people's well-being.
The dictatorship became a Moloch that consumed the hopes of its citizens.
He refused to sacrifice his principles to the Moloch of political ambition.

Synset Examples

the great Moloch of war
duty has become the Moloch of modern life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juggernaut - a massive, inexorable force, campaign, or object that crushes whatever is in its path
  • Synonym: tyrant - a cruel and oppressive ruler

Etymology

From Hebrew mólokh, a Canaanite deity to whom children were sacrificed.

/ ˈmoʊ.lɑk / (AmE), / ˈmɒl.ək / (BrE)
an ancient god worshipped by the Canaanites and Phoenicians, to whom children were said to be sacrificed.
ancient god associated with child sacrifice

Examples

Many ancient texts depict Moloch as a symbol of cruel, demanding sacrifice.
The poet used Moloch as a metaphor for the destructive nature of industrial society.
Archaeologists discovered references to Moloch in ancient Phoenician inscriptions.

Etymology

From Hebrew *Mōlek*, a name for a Canaanite deity.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊˌlɑk / (AmE), / ˈməʊlɒk / (BrE)
A genus of Australian desert lizards, known for the thorny devil species (Moloch horridus).
genus of Australian desert lizards

Examples

The thorny devil, scientifically known as Moloch horridus, is native to Australia’s arid regions.
Researchers are studying the adaptations of the Moloch genus to extreme desert climates.
Many tourists hope to spot a member of the Moloch genus during their visits to the Outback.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Moloch horridus - the thorny devil, a species within the genus Moloch

Etymology

From Greek 'Moloch', a Canaanite deity, likely referencing the lizards' fearsome appearance.
