creationism

1. NOUN

/ kriˈeɪʃənˌɪzəm / (AmE), / kriˈeɪʃənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the doctrine that the universe and life originated from specific acts of divine creation, as described in the Book of Genesis, rather than by natural processes such as evolution
belief in divine creation as described in Genesis

Examples

Many schools have debated whether to include creationism alongside evolution in their science curriculums.
He opposes the teaching of creationism in public schools.
Supporters of creationism often interpret the Bible's Book of Genesis as a literal account of history.

Synset Examples

creationism denies the theory of evolution of species

Related Terms

  • Antonym: evolution - the scientific theory that species develop over time through natural selection

Etymology

From 'creation' + '-ism', denoting belief or ideology.
