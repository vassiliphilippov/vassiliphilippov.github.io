lessened

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɛsənd / (AmE), / ˈlɛsənd / (BrE)
made smaller in amount, degree, or significance; diminished or reduced by some degree.
diminished; made smaller or less

Examples

Her enthusiasm for the project has lessened after months of delays.
The pain lessened over time, making it easier for him to walk again.
Because of the intervention, the impact of the crisis was greatly lessened.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diminished - made smaller or less; decreased
  • Antonym: increased - become greater in amount or degree

Etymology

Past participle of 'lessen,' from 'less' + '-en' (verb-forming suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɛsənd / (AmE), / ˈlɛsənd / (BrE)
Decreased in severity, intensity, or extent; made less harsh or strong.
decreased in severity or intensity

Examples

The pain had lessened after taking the medication, allowing her to rest comfortably.
With lessened restrictions, more people were able to travel freely this summer.
His disappointment was lessened by the support of his friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diminished - made or become less; decreased
  • Antonym: intensified - increased in degree or strength
