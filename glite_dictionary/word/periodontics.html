periodontics

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪr.i.oʊˈdɑn.tɪks / (AmE), / ˌpɪə.ri.əˈdɒn.tɪks / (BrE)
the branch of dentistry concerned with the diagnosis and treatment of diseases affecting the gums and other structures supporting the teeth
branch of dentistry for gum diseases
medical

Examples

After years of gum problems, she was referred to a periodontics specialist for advanced treatment.
The dental college offers a postgraduate program in periodontics.
Advancements in periodontics have greatly improved methods for treating gum disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dentistry - the medical field concerned with the care of teeth and mouth
  • Synonym: periodontology - the study of the supporting structures of teeth and related diseases

Etymology

From Greek 'peri-' (around) + 'odous' (tooth), referring to tissues around the tooth.
