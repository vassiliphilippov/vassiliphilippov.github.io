anamnesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəmˈnisɪs / (AmE), / ˌanəmˈniːsɪs / (BrE)
A medical history of a patient as recounted by the patient, typically used in clinical assessment.
patient's recalled medical history
medical

Examples

During the initial consultation, the doctor carefully recorded the patient's anamnesis to identify potential risk factors.
Accurate anamnesis is crucial for diagnosing conditions that may not show obvious symptoms.
The nurse asked for a detailed anamnesis to better understand the patient's current complaints.

Etymology

From Greek anamnesis ('remembrance, recollection'), from ana- ('again') + mnesis ('memory').

2. NOUN

/ ˌænəmˈnisɪs / (AmE), / ˌan.amˈniː.sɪs / (BrE)
the ability to recall or remember past events or experiences; recollection
recollection of past events

Examples

Through deep meditation, she experienced a sudden anamnesis of her childhood.
Philosophers often discuss anamnesis as the soul's recollection of knowledge from previous lives.
The therapist encouraged anamnesis to help the patient process traumatic memories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recollection - the act of remembering something
  • Antonym: amnesia - loss of memory

Etymology

From Greek anamnesis (recollection, remembrance), from ana- (again) + mimneskesthai (to remember).
