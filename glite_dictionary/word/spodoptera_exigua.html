Spodoptera exigua

1. NOUN

/ spoʊˈdɑp.tərə ɛkˈsɪg.wi.ə / (AmE), / spɒˈdɒp.tərə ɛkˈsɪg.wɪə / (BrE)
A species of moth whose larvae, known as beet armyworms, are agricultural pests that feed on a variety of crop plants.
species of moth, beet armyworm

Examples

Spodoptera exigua is a major pest in vegetable crops around the world.
The larvae of Spodoptera exigua are commonly referred to as beet armyworms.
Farmers monitor their fields for signs of Spodoptera exigua infestations to protect their harvests.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus Spodoptera + Latin 'exigua', meaning 'small' or 'meager'.

a moth species whose larvae, known as the beet armyworm, feed on the foliage of beets and various other vegetables, often as an agricultural pest
a moth species; pest on vegetables

Examples

Spodoptera exigua can devastate crops if not controlled early in the planting season.
Farmers often check for signs of Spodoptera exigua when monitoring their vegetable fields.
Biological control agents are being used to reduce Spodoptera exigua populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beet armyworm - larval stage of Spodoptera exigua; vegetable pest

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name 'Spodoptera' and species 'exigua', meaning 'small' in Latin.
