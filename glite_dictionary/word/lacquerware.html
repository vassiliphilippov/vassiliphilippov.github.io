lacquerware

1. NOUN

/ ˈlækɚˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˈlakəˌwɛə / (BrE)
Decorative objects made of wood and coated with lacquer, often inlaid with ivory or precious metals.
decorative wooden object coated with lacquer

Examples

The museum displays an impressive collection of Japanese lacquerware from the Edo period.
Her family treasures a set of ornate lacquerware bowls hand-crafted by her grandfather.
Chinese lacquerware is renowned for its durability and intricate designs.

Etymology

From 'lacquer' (from Portuguese 'laca', from Arabic 'lakk', from Persian and Hindi), and 'ware' meaning manufactured articles.
