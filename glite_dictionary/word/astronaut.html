astronaut

1. NOUN

/ ˈæstrəˌnɔt / (AmE), / ˈæstrənɔːt / (BrE)
a person who is trained to travel and operate in a spacecraft.
space traveler; person trained for spaceflight

Examples

The first astronaut to walk on the moon was Neil Armstrong.
Training to become an astronaut requires years of preparation and dedication.
During the mission, the astronaut conducted several experiments in zero gravity.

Synset Examples

the Russians called their astronauts cosmonauts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cosmonaut - a Russian astronaut
  • Synonym: taikonaut - a Chinese astronaut

Etymology

From Greek 'astron' (star) + 'nautēs' (sailor).
