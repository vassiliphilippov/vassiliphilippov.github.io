arsine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrˌsin / (AmE), / ˈɑːsiːn / (BrE)
a poisonous, colorless, flammable gas (AsH₃) used in organic synthesis, in doping semiconductors, and formerly as a chemical weapon
poisonous chemical gas (AsH₃)

Examples

Exposure to arsine gas can lead to severe poisoning and is a serious occupational hazard.
The company uses arsine in the production of semiconductors despite its toxic nature.
During World War I, arsine was considered for use as a chemical weapon due to its high toxicity.

Etymology

From 'arsenic' + '-ine', formed by analogy with similar chemical names.
