cirrhosis

1. NOUN

/ səˈroʊsɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A chronic disease of the liver marked by degeneration of cells, inflammation, and fibrous thickening of tissue, leading to loss of normal liver function.
chronic liver disease
medical

Examples

After years of heavy drinking, he was finally diagnosed with cirrhosis.
The doctor explained that cirrhosis can be caused by hepatitis, not just alcoholism.
With advanced cirrhosis, the liver cannot perform its essential functions.

Etymology

From Greek *kirrhos* (tawny) + -osis (disease condition), referencing the yellowish color of a diseased liver.
