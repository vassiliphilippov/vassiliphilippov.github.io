Togaviridae

1. NOUN

/ toʊˌgɑːvəˈrɪdiː / (AmE), / təʊˌɡævɪˈrɪdɪiː / (BrE)
A family of viruses transmitted by arthropods, especially mosquitoes, that includes several important human and animal pathogens.
family of viruses carried by mosquitoes
medical

Examples

Many diseases transmitted by mosquitoes are caused by viruses belonging to the Togaviridae family.
Researchers are studying the replication mechanisms of Togaviridae to develop antiviral treatments.
Chikungunya virus is a well-known member of the Togaviridae family.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'toga' (cloak, referencing the virus's envelope) and 'viridae' (the standard suffix for virus families).
