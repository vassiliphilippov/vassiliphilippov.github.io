misconstruction

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪskənˈstrʌkʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪskənˈstrʌkʃən / (BrE)
an incorrect interpretation of words, actions, or intentions, especially when misrepresenting their true meaning
incorrect interpretation or misreading

Examples

His comments were subject to misconstruction by the press, sparking unnecessary controversy.
A minor mistake in phrasing led to the misconstruction of her intentions.
To avoid misconstruction, he clarified his statement several times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misinterpretation - an incorrect understanding of something said or done
  • Antonym: interpretation - the action of explaining the meaning of something

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + construction, from Latin constructio (a building or arranging).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɪskənˈstrʌkʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪskənˈstrʌkʃən / (BrE)
an incorrect or ungrammatical arrangement of words or phrases in a sentence; a grammatical error in structure
an ungrammatical arrangement; grammatical error

Examples

The sentence contains a misconstruction that confuses the intended meaning.
Linguists often study common misconstructions found in spoken language.
A frequent misconstruction occurs when clauses are improperly joined.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solecism - a grammatical mistake in speech or writing

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + construction, influenced by Latin 'construere' (to build).
