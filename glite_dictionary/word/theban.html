Theban

1. ADJ

/ ˈθiːbən / (AmE), / ˈθiːbən / (BrE)
relating to the ancient Greek city of Thebes, its people, or their culture
of or relating to Thebes (Greece) or its people

Examples

The Theban army was renowned for its discipline and bravery in battle.
Her dissertation focuses on Theban religious practices during the classical period.
The legend of Oedipus plays a central role in Theban mythology.

Etymology

From Latin Thebanus, from Greek Thebai (Θῆβαι), the name of the city.

2. NOUN

/ ˈθiː.bən / (AmE), / ˈθiː.bən / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of ancient Thebes, especially in Egypt.
inhabitant of ancient Thebes

Examples

The ancient Theban civilization left behind impressive monuments and tombs.
Archaeologists discovered the remains of a prominent Theban in the Valley of the Kings.
Many myths center around legendary Theban rulers and their conflicts with neighboring cities.

Etymology

From Latin 'Thebanus', from Greek 'Thēbaios' (of Thebes).
