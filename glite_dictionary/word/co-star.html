co-star

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ stɑr / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ stɑː / (BrE)
one of two or more actors who have equal importance in a play, film, or television show
an actor with equal starring status

Examples

The two actors are co-stars in the new romantic comedy.
She quickly became friends with her co-star during filming.
At the award ceremony, the director praised both co-stars for their performances.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: star - the main actor or performer in a play or film

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'co-' (together) and 'star' (leading performer).

2. VERB

/ ˈkoʊ ˌstɑːr / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ ˌstɑː / (BrE)
to appear as one of the two or more leading performers in a film, play, or show; to share major billing with another performer.
appear as one of the leading performers

Examples

She will co-star with her brother in the upcoming Broadway production.
The two leading actors co-starred in several romantic comedies together.
He is excited to co-star alongside some of Hollywood's brightest talents.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: star - to play the most important role in a performance

Etymology

Formed from 'co-' (together) and 'star' (leading performer), first used in 20th-century entertainment industry.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01725292-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈkoʊ stɑr / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ stɑː / (BrE)
to appear as one of the leading actors, alongside another, in a performance such as a film, play, or television show
appear as a fellow leading actor

Examples

She will co-star with a famous actor in the new comedy film.
The project is her first opportunity to co-star in a Broadway production.
They co-starred in a critically acclaimed television drama last year.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: star - to play the most important role in a performance

Etymology

From 'co-' (together) and 'star' (leading performer); first used in the mid-20th century.
