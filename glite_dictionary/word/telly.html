telly

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛli / (AmE), / ˈtɛli / (BrE)
An informal term, especially in British English, for a television set or the system of television broadcasting.
informal term for television set (British)
informal british

Examples

Let's watch the football match on the telly tonight.
She spent the whole afternoon sitting in front of the telly.
They bought a new telly for the living room last week.

Synset Examples

the British call a tv set a ‘telly’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: television - a system for transmitting visual images and sound that are reproduced on screens

Etymology

Shortened from 'television'.
