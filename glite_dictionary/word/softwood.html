softwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑftˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈsɒftˌwʊd / (BrE)
wood from coniferous trees such as pine or fir, generally softer and easier to cut than hardwoods
wood from conifers, easy to cut

Examples

Pine is a common type of softwood used in furniture making.
Builders often prefer softwood for framing because it is easy to saw and shape.
The deck was constructed from treated softwood to resist weathering.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hardwood - wood from broad-leaved trees, generally harder than softwood
