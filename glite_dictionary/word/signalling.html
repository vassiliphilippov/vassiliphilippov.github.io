signalling

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪɡ.nəl.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡ.nəl.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the action of sending a message by means of nonverbal actions or gestures
nonverbal communication by actions or gestures

Examples

The signalling between the two drivers prevented a collision.
Certain bird species rely heavily on visual signalling during courtship displays.
Traffic police use hand signalling to control vehicles at busy intersections.

Synset Examples

signals from the boat suddenly stopped

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signaling - U.S. spelling for act of sending signals
  • Synonym: gesturing - making gestures to communicate

Etymology

From signal + -ing; 'signal' from Latin 'signum' (mark, sign).
