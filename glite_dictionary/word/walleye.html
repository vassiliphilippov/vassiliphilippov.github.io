walleye

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːlˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːlˌaɪ / (BrE)
A condition of strabismus in which one or both eyes are directed outward.
eye condition with outward gaze
medical

The doctor diagnosed him with walleye after noticing his eye drifted outward.
Correcting walleye may require specialized surgery or therapy in some cases.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːl.aɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːl.aɪ / (BrE)
A large, pike-like freshwater fish (Sander vitreus) of North America, valued for food and sport.
type of North American freshwater fish

Examples

Fishermen travel from all over to catch walleye in the lake every spring.
The walleye is known for its glassy, reflective eyes and is a popular game fish.
We grilled the fresh walleye we caught yesterday for dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pike - a long-bodied predatory freshwater fish
  • Synonym: perch - a common name for fish of the family Percidae

Etymology

From 'wall' + 'eye', referring to the pale, opaque appearance of the fish's eye.
