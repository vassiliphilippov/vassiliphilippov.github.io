phylogenesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌfaɪloʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌfʌɪlə(ʊ)ˈdʒɛnɪsɪs / (BrE)
the evolutionary development and diversification of a species or other taxonomic group
evolutionary development of a species
medical

Examples

The study of phylogenesis reveals how species adapt to changing environments over time.
Scientists debated the influence of external factors on the phylogenesis of modern birds.
Understanding phylogenesis helps us trace the evolutionary relationships among different organisms.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ontogenesis - the development of an individual organism
  • Synonym: phylogeny - the evolutionary history of a species or group

Etymology

From Greek phylon (race, tribe) + genesis (origin, creation).
