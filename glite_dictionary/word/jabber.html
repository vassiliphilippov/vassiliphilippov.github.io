jabber

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæb.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒæb.ə / (BrE)
rapid and indistinct speech that is difficult to understand
rapid, indistinct speech

Examples

He couldn't make sense of the children's jabber in the playroom.
The announcer's excited jabber was hard to follow after the winning goal.
Over coffee, their morning jabber filled the quiet café.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babble - speech that is foolish, excited, or incoherent
  • Synonym: mumble - speak or say something indistinctly

Etymology

Perhaps imitative in origin, reflecting the sound of rapid speech.

2. VERB

/ ˈdʒæb.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒæb.ə / (BrE)
to talk or speak rapidly and excitedly, often in a way that is hard to understand
talk rapidly and excitedly

Examples

The children began to jabber excitedly as soon as the bell rang for recess.
He tends to jabber on the phone when he's nervous, barely stopping to breathe.
They were jabbering so loudly in the café that I couldn't concentrate on my work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prattle - to talk at length in a foolish or inconsequential way
  • Synonym: mutter - to speak in a low or barely audible voice
  • Degree Lower: chat - to talk in a friendly and informal way

Etymology

Probably imitative in origin, representing the sound of rapid speech.

Synset POS: v
