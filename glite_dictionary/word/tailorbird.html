tailorbird

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪlərˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈteɪləˌbɜːd / (BrE)
A small tropical Asian warbler known for stitching leaves together to make and conceal its nest.
tropical Asian warbler known for sewing nests

Examples

The tailorbird constructs its nest by sewing leaves together with plant fibers.
Several species of tailorbird can be found in gardens throughout South and Southeast Asia.
We watched a tailorbird hard at work, stitching two large leaves to hide its eggs.

Etymology

Named for its nest-building method, likened to a tailor sewing cloth.
