potholder

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑtˌhoʊldər / (AmE), / ˈpɒtˌhəʊldə / (BrE)
a thick pad, typically made of fabric or other insulating material, used for holding hot pots or pans to protect the hands
insulated pad for holding hot cookware

Examples

She grabbed the hot pan using a potholder to avoid burning her hands.
Always keep a potholder nearby when you're cooking on the stove.
He bought a set of colorful potholders for his new kitchen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oven mitt - a glove made of insulated material to handle hot items in the kitchen

Etymology

Formed from 'pot' + 'holder,' indicating something that holds a pot.
