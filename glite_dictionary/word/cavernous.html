cavernous

1. ADJ

/ ˈkævərnəs / (AmE), / ˈkævənəs / (BrE)
(of tissue) containing spaces that can fill with blood and become distended and rigid; especially relating to erectile tissue.
filled with blood spaces; erectile (medical)
medical

Examples

The cavernous tissue in the penis enables it to become erect during arousal.
Erectile dysfunction can occur if the cavernous bodies do not fill with blood properly.
Damage to cavernous structures can lead to loss of normal erectile function.

Synset Examples

erectile tissue
the penis is an erectile organ

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erectile - (of tissue) capable of becoming rigid when filled with blood

Etymology

From Latin 'cavernosus', from 'caverna' meaning 'cave'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkævərnəs / (AmE), / ˈkævənəs / (BrE)
(of a space or opening) very large, vast, and resembling or suggestive of a cavern, especially in being dark, deep, or hollow.
like a cavern; vast and deep

Examples

The abandoned warehouse had a cavernous interior that echoed with every step.
She spoke in a cavernous hall, her voice fading into the darkness above.
His eyes looked sunken and cavernous after weeks of illness.

Synset Examples

vast cavernous chambers hollowed out of limestone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spacious - large and having ample space
  • Antonym: cramped - uncomfortably small or restricted

Etymology

From Latin 'cavernosus' (full of hollows, full of caves), from 'caverna' (cave).
