Fernao Magalhaes

1. PROPN

/ fɛrˈnaʊ məɡəlˈjɛs / (AmE), / fɜːˈnaʊ məˈɡaljɛs / (BrE)
Portuguese explorer (1480–1521) who led the first expedition to circumnavigate the globe, sailing under the Spanish flag.
Portuguese explorer, led first world circumnavigation

Examples

Fernao Magalhaes is renowned for leading the first successful circumnavigation of the Earth.
Many historians study the route taken by Fernao Magalhaes during his famous voyage.
The achievements of Fernao Magalhaes had a lasting impact on world exploration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ferdinand Magellan - Portuguese navigator known for leading the first circumnavigation of the globe

Etymology

Portuguese form of Ferdinand Magellan's name; 'Fernão de Magalhães' in Portuguese.
