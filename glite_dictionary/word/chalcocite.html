chalcocite

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælkəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkalkəsʌɪt / (BrE)
a heavy, gray mineral consisting of copper(I) sulfide, an important ore of copper
a gray copper ore mineral

Examples

The mine produces large quantities of chalcocite used in copper extraction.
Geologists identified chalcocite veins running through the host rock.
High-grade chalcocite is especially valuable for smelting operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: covellite - a blue copper sulfide mineral, an ore of copper

Etymology

From Greek khalkos (copper) + -ite (mineral suffix).
