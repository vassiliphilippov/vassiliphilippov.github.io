zinnia

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɪniə / (AmE), / ˈzɪniə / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Zinnia, widely cultivated for its bright, colorful flower heads
a brightly colored garden flower

Examples

She planted zinnias along the walkway to add a splash of color to her garden.
The vibrant petals of the zinnia attracted butterflies all summer long.
After the frost, most zinnias in the flowerbeds wilted and lost their blooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marigold - a bright garden flower, sometimes confused with zinnia

Etymology

Named after Johann Gottfried Zinn (1727–1759), a German botanist.
