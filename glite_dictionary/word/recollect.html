to recollect

1. VERB

/ ˌrɛkəˈlɛkt / (AmE), / ˌrɛkəˈlɛkt / (BrE)
to remember something; to recall knowledge or an event from the past
to remember; recall from memory

Examples

She tried hard to recollect where she had seen the man before.
I can't recollect the exact date, but it was sometime last year.
Do you recollect what happened at the meeting yesterday?

Synset Examples

I can't remember saying any such thing
I can't think what her last name was
can you remember her phone number?
Do you remember that he once loved you?
call up memories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to remember - to have or be able to bring to one's mind an awareness of someone or something
  • Antonym: to forget - to fail to remember

Etymology

From Latin recollectus, past participle of recolligere, meaning 'to gather again, recall'.
