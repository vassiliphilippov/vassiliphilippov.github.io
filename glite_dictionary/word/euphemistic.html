euphemistic

1. ADJ

/ juːfəˈmɪstɪk / (AmE), / juːfəˈmɪstɪk / (BrE)
Using or involving a mild or indirect word or expression substituted for one considered to be too harsh or blunt.
using a mild or indirect expression

Examples

Many news outlets use euphemistic language to report on sensitive topics.
The manager's euphemistic explanation tried to soften the blow of the bad news.
Describing someone as 'vertically challenged' is a euphemistic way of saying they are short.

Synset Examples

‘peepee’ is a common euphemistic term

Related Terms

  • Synonym: euphemism - a mild or indirect word substituted for one considered harsh or blunt
  • Antonym: dysphemistic - using deliberately harsh or offensive expressions instead of mild ones

Etymology

From 'euphemism', which derives from Greek 'euphēmismos' (speaking with good words).
