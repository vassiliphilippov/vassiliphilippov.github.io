lakefront

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪkˌfrʌnt / (AmE), / ˈleɪkˌfrʌnt / (BrE)
the land along the edge of a lake
land along the shore of a lake

Examples

They bought a beautiful house on the lakefront.
The city built a park along the lakefront to attract visitors.
Joggers enjoy the scenic paths that run beside the lakefront.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waterfront - land or property along the edge of a sea, river, or lake

Etymology

From 'lake' and 'front', modeled after similar compounds such as 'waterfront'.
