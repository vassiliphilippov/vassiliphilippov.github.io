to gluttonize

1. VERB

/ ˈɡlʌtəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɡlʌtənaɪz / (BrE)
to eat in an excessive or greedy manner, without restraint
eat excessively or greedily

Examples

Every Thanksgiving, he tends to gluttonize at the dinner table.
Tourists often gluttonize at all-you-can-eat buffets.
She regretted gluttonizing on sweets after the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gorge - to eat greedily or to excess
  • Antonym: to starve - to suffer or die from lack of food

Etymology

Formed from 'glutton' plus verb suffix '-ize'; ultimately from Latin 'gluttire' (to swallow).
