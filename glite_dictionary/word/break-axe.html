break-axe

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkˌæks / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkˌæks / (BrE)
A West Indian timber tree known for its extremely hard wood.
West Indian tree with hard wood

Examples

The wood of the break-axe is so dense that it is difficult to cut with ordinary tools.
Many local craftsmen prefer break-axe for building, thanks to its remarkable strength.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of several break-axe trees in the region.
