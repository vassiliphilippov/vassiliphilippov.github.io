Jugoslavian

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːɡoʊˈslɑːviən / (AmE), / ˌjuːɡəˈslɑːviən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of the former country Yugoslavia.
person from Yugoslavia

Examples

Her grandmother was a proud Jugoslavian who often spoke about her childhood in Belgrade.
During the 1980s, many Jugoslavians migrated to Western Europe for better opportunities.
He interviewed several former Jugoslavian athletes for the documentary on Balkan sports history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yugoslav - a native or inhabitant of Yugoslavia

Etymology

From Yugoslavia, comprising the Slavic words for 'South' (jug) and 'Slav'.
