exarchate

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛkˌsɑr.keɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛk.sɑː.keɪt / (BrE)
A district or diocese under the jurisdiction of an exarch, especially in the Eastern Orthodox Church.
Eastern Orthodox Church diocese

Examples

The exarchate was responsible for several parishes within the region.
He studied the history of the Russian exarchate in Eastern Europe.
The church was elevated to the status of an exarchate by the patriarch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diocese - a district under the pastoral care of a bishop in the Christian Church

Etymology

From Medieval Latin exarchatus, from Greek exarchēs (leader).
