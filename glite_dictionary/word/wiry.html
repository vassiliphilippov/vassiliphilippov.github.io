wiry

1. ADJ

/ ˈwaɪəri / (AmE), / ˈwaɪəri / (BrE)
(of hair) stiff and coarse like wire.
stiff, coarse hair texture

Examples

Her wiry red hair stood out in tight, springy curls.
He ran his hands through his wiry beard, scratching his chin thoughtfully.
The dog's wiry fur made it difficult to brush smoothly.

Synset Examples

wiry red hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coarse - rough in texture; not smooth or soft

2. ADJ

/ ˈwaɪɹi / (AmE), / ˈwaɪəɹi / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) lean, strong, and having little fat, with muscles that are tough and sinewy.
lean and sinewy in build

Examples

Despite his age, the old man was wiry and incredibly strong.
Her wiry frame made her an excellent long-distance runner.
The wiry dog darted through the underbrush with remarkable agility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sinewy - having well-developed muscles; lean and strong
  • Degree Higher: muscular - having well-developed muscles
  • Degree Lower: slender - thin in an attractive way

Etymology

From wire + -y, originally referring to something made of wire; later used figuratively to describe lean, sinewy strength.
