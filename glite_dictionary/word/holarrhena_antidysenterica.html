Holarrhena antidysenterica

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˌlɑːˈriːnə ˌæntidaɪsənˈtɛrɪkə / (AmE), / həʊˌlæˈriːnə ˌæntɪdɪsənˈtɛrɪkə / (BrE)
a tropical Asian tree with hard white wood, whose bark was formerly used as a remedy for dysentery and diarrhea
tropical Asian medicinal tree
medical

Examples

The bark of Holarrhena antidysenterica was once widely used in traditional medicine to treat digestive disorders.
Botanists are studying the active compounds found in Holarrhena antidysenterica for potential medicinal uses.
You can find Holarrhena antidysenterica growing in subtropical forests across India and Southeast Asia.

Etymology

From Greek holos (whole) + arrhena (male), and Neo-Latin antidysenterica (anti-dysentery).
