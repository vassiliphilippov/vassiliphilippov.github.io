broody

1. NOUN

/ ˈbruːdi / (AmE), / ˈbruːdi / (BrE)
A domestic hen that is ready to sit on eggs and incubate them until they hatch.
a hen ready to incubate eggs

Examples

The farmer bought a new broody to help hatch more chicks this spring.
She separated the broody from the flock to ensure the eggs were safe.
This broody has been sitting on her nest for almost three weeks.

Etymology

From brood + -y, meaning tending to incubate eggs.

3. ADJ

/ ˈbruːdi / (AmE), / ˈbruːdi / (BrE)
(of a bird, especially a hen) physiologically ready to incubate eggs and inclined to sit on them to hatch them.
ready to incubate eggs (bird)

Examples

The broody hen refused to leave her nest for several days.
When a duck becomes broody, she will try to hatch any eggs she finds.
Farmers can tell when a chicken is broody by her protective behavior toward her nest.

Synset Examples

a broody hen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maternal - showing motherly instincts or behavior, especially towards offspring or eggs
