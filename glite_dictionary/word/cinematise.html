to cinematise

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪnəmətaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪnəmətaɪz / (BrE)
to make a film adaptation of a story, event, or work; to turn into a film
make into a film
british

Examples

It can be difficult to cinematise a complex historical event without oversimplifying it.
The director plans to cinematise the bestselling novel next year.
Some authors are reluctant to have their works cinematised for fear of losing creative control.

Synset Examples

cinematize history

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cinematize - to make a film adaptation of; to turn into a film
  • Synonym: to dramatise - to adapt for dramatic presentation; especially on stage or film

Etymology

From cinema + -ise, modeled after verbs like dramatise.
