to strengthen

1. VERB

/ ˈstrɛŋθən / (AmE), / ˈstrɛŋθən / (BrE)
to make (a part of the body or a muscle) healthier, firmer, or more elastic, especially through exercise
make body or muscle firmer and healthier

Examples

Regular exercise will strengthen your leg muscles and improve flexibility.
Swimming is a good way to strengthen your back and core.
Physical therapy helped strengthen her injured knee after surgery.

Synset Examples

Let's tone our muscles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tone - make muscles firmer and healthier through exercise
  • Antonym: to weaken - make something less strong or effective

Etymology

From Middle English 'strengthen', formed from 'strength' + '-en'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00101031-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈstrɛŋθən / (AmE), / ˈstrɛŋθ(ə)n / (BrE)
to make or become stronger or more effective
make or become stronger

Examples

Regular exercise can strengthen your heart and muscles.
The government took steps to strengthen the economy after the crisis.
Diplomats hope to strengthen relations between the two countries through trade agreements.

Synset Examples

This exercise will strengthen your upper body
strengthen the relations between the two countries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fortify - make stronger, especially to protect against attack
  • Antonym: to weaken - make or become weaker

Etymology

From strength + -en (Old English suffix forming verbs indicating the cause to be or become).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00221295-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈstrɛŋθən / (AmE), / ˈstrɛŋθ(ə)n / (BrE)
to become stronger or to make something become stronger
become or make stronger

Examples

After months of physical therapy, his muscles finally strengthened.
Daily exercise can strengthen your immune system and overall health.
The team's resolve strengthened as they faced new challenges.

Synset Examples

His body strengthened

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to weaken - to make or become weaker
  • Synonym: to fortify - to make stronger, especially to protect against attack

Etymology

From Middle English 'strengthnen', from 'strength' + '-en' (verb suffix).
