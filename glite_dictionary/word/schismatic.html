schismatic

1. ADJ

/ skɪˈzmætɪk, sɪˈzmætɪk / (AmE), / skɪzˈmatɪk, sɪzˈmatɪk / (BrE)
involving or characteristic of schism; relating to or favoring division or separation, especially within a religious group or organization
relating to division or schism

Examples

The movement was condemned as a schismatic group by church authorities.
Several schismatic sects emerged during the turbulent period.
Her schismatic views threatened unity within the organization.

Synset Examples

schismatic sects

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schism - a division, especially within a religious organization

Etymology

From Medieval Latin schismaticus (causing division); from Greek skhisma (cleft, division).
