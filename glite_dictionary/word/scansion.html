scansion

1. NOUN

/ ˈskænʃən / (AmE), / ˈskænʃən / (BrE)
the analysis of a line of verse to determine its rhythm and metrical pattern
analysis of verse meter

Examples

Students learned how to perform scansion on a Shakespearean sonnet.
Her paper included a detailed scansion of each stanza in the poem.
Accurate scansion can reveal subtle shifts in a poet’s rhythm and style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prosody - the patterns of rhythm and sound in poetry

Etymology

From Latin 'scandere' (to climb), via Latin 'scansion' (act of scanning verse).
