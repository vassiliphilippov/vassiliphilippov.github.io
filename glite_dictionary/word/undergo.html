to undergo

1. VERB

/ ˌʌndərˈɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈɡəʊ / (BrE)
to experience or be subjected to (something, typically something unpleasant, difficult, or involving a process or change)
experience or go through (a process or change)

Examples

He had to undergo surgery after the accident.
Many people undergo significant changes during their teenage years.
The proposal underwent several revisions before it was approved.

Synset Examples

The chemical undergoes a sudden change
The fluid undergoes shear
undergo a strange sensation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: experience - to encounter or feel something; to live through an event or situation
  • Synonym: to suffer - to experience or be subjected to something bad or unpleasant

Etymology

From Middle English 'undergon', from Old English 'underġān' (to undergo, to go under, to submit to).
