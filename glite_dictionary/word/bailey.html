bailey

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪli / (AmE), / ˈbeɪli / (BrE)
the outer wall and enclosed courtyard of a castle, especially in medieval fortifications
outer courtyard of a castle

Examples

The castle's bailey was once bustling with soldiers and merchants.
Archaeologists uncovered remnants of the bailey during the excavation.
The children played games in the grassy bailey beneath the high stone walls.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: motte - a raised earthwork on which a castle keep is built

Etymology

From Old French *baille* (enclosure, court), related to Latin *bā̆gillus* (minor enclosure).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪli / (AmE), / ˈbeɪli / (BrE)
the outer defensive wall that surrounds the outer courtyard of a castle, especially in medieval fortifications.
outer defensive wall of a castle

Examples

The castle's outer bailey was reinforced to withstand attacks from enemy forces.
Visitors can walk along the remnants of the bailey and imagine its former strength.
Within the bailey, markets and craftsmen supported the castle's daily life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curtain wall - the main outer wall of a castle without towers
  • Antonym: keep - the central, most secure tower of a castle

Etymology

From Old French *baille* (enclosure, outer wall of a castle), from Latin *bālia* (palisade, fence).
