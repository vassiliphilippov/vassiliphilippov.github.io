aesthetics

1. NOUN

/ ɛsˈθɛtɪks / (AmE), / iːsˈθɛtɪks / (BrE)
the branch of philosophy concerned with the nature and appreciation of beauty, art, and taste
philosophy of beauty and art

Examples

He is particularly interested in aesthetics and the philosophy of art.
Debates over the meaning of beauty have been central to aesthetics for centuries.
Her lectures on aesthetics inspired students to see art in a new light.

Synset Examples

traditional aesthetics assumed the existence of universal and timeless criteria of artistic value

Related Terms

  • Synonym: esthetic - pertaining to beauty or artistic taste

Etymology

From Greek 'aisthētikos' (of sense perception), via modern Latin 'aestheticus'.
