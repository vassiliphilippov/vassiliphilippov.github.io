to sorcerise

1. VERB

/ ˈsɔːrsəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɔːsəˌraɪz / (BrE)
to transform or change something by the use of sorcery or magical powers
change by means of sorcery

Examples

The wicked witch threatened to sorcerise the villagers if they entered her forest.
He claimed he could sorcerise any object, turning stone to gold with a secret spell.
In the legend, a mighty wizard sorcerised the prince, transforming him into a bird.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enchant - to fill with great delight or to put under a spell

Etymology

From 'sorcerer', based on Old French 'sorcier', and the suffix '-ise'.
