Drymarchon corais

1. NOUN

/ draɪˈmɑːrkɒn koʊˈraɪs / (AmE), / draɪˈmɑːkɒn kəʊˈraɪs / (BrE)
a large, nonvenomous dark-blue snake native to southern North America and Mexico, also called the indigo snake
a large nonvenomous indigo snake

Examples

The Drymarchon corais is admired for its striking dark-blue scales and impressive size.
Biologists observed a Drymarchon corais invading rodent burrows in its natural habitat.
While hiking in southern Mexico, we encountered a Drymarchon corais basking in the sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indigo snake - common name for Drymarchon corais, a large nonvenomous snake

Etymology

From Greek 'drymarchon' (lord of the forest) and 'corais', possibly a reference to coloration.
