strangling

1. NOUN

/ ˈstræŋɡlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstræŋɡlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of killing or injuring someone by compressing their throat and preventing them from breathing
act of suffocating by throat compression

Examples

The police concluded that the victim died as a result of strangling.
There were clear marks on his neck indicating attempted strangling.
Strangling is a method of homicide that leaves obvious forensic evidence.

Synset Examples

no evidence that the choking was done by the accused

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strangulation - the act or process of strangling

Etymology

Present participle/noun sense from 'strangle,' from Middle English stranglen, from Old French estrangler, from Latin strangulare.
