cupflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌpˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈkʌpˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A Mexican evergreen climbing plant with large, solitary, funnel-shaped, fragrant yellow flowers marked with purple-brown ridges in the throat.
a Mexican climbing plant with yellow, funnel-shaped flowers

Examples

The cupflower bloomed early this year, filling the garden with its sweet fragrance.
She planted a cupflower beside the trellis to let it climb and add color to her patio.
Hummingbirds are attracted to the vibrant cupflower in our greenhouse.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌpˌflaʊ.ər / (AmE), / ˈkʌpˌflaʊ.ə / (BrE)
any of several plants of the genus Nierembergia, distinguished by their upturned, bell-shaped flowers.
plant with upturned bell-shaped flowers (Nierembergia)

Examples

The garden was filled with purple cupflowers blooming in the summer sun.
She planted a row of cupflowers along the edge of the path for a splash of color.
Botanists admire the delicate structure of the cupflower and its bell-shaped blossoms.

Etymology

From the resemblance of the flower to a cup.
