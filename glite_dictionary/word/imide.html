imide

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmaɪd / (AmE), / ˈɪm.aɪd / (BrE)
a compound belonging to a class of organic chemicals containing the divalent group –CONHCO–, formed formally by replacing two oxygen atoms in an acid anhydride with NH groups
organic compound with –CONHCO– group
medical

Examples

Polymeric materials containing the imide group are known for their exceptional thermal stability.
Scientists synthesized a new imide to study its pharmaceutical properties.
The reaction mechanism involves the conversion of an amide to an imide intermediate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amide - an organic compound containing the –CONH₂ group

Etymology

From im- (from 'amide') + -ide, modeled after 'amide'.
