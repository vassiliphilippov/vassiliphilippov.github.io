Fratercula

1. NOUN

/ frəˈtɛrkjələ / (AmE), / frəˈtɛːkjʊlə / (BrE)
A genus of seabirds in the auk family, commonly known as puffins, found in the North Atlantic and North Pacific oceans.
genus of seabirds: puffins

Examples

The Atlantic Fratercula is distinguished by its colorful beak during the breeding season.
Researchers observed a colony of Fratercula nesting on the remote islands.
Among seabirds, the Fratercula are noted for their swimming and diving abilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puffin - a seabird with a colorful beak belonging to the genus Fratercula

Etymology

From Latin 'fratercula' (little brother), referring to the birds' monk-like appearance.

2. NOUN

/ frəˈtɛrkjələ / (AmE), / fræˈtɛːkjʊlə / (BrE)
A genus of seabirds in the auk family, commonly known as puffins, characterized by their colorful beaks and northern habitat.
genus of seabirds (puffins)

Examples

The Fratercula genus is famous for its members' brightly colored bills during the breeding season.
Scientists observed several Fratercula nesting on the remote cliffs of the island.
All species in the Fratercula genus are commonly referred to as puffins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puffin - a seabird of the genus Fratercula with a colorful beak

Etymology

From Latin *fratercula* meaning 'little brother', referring to their black-and-white, monk-like appearance.
