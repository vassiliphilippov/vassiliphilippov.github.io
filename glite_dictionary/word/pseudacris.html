Pseudacris

1. NOUN

/ suːˈdækrɪs / (AmE), / sjuːˈdækrɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small North American frogs known as chorus frogs, noted for their distinctive breeding calls.
genus of North American chorus frogs

Examples

The springtime singing of the Pseudacris can be heard in wetlands across the region.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of various Pseudacris species during the breeding season.
One of the distinguishing features of Pseudacris is its loud, trilling call.

Etymology

From Greek 'pseudo-' (false) and 'akris' (locust, grasshopper), referring to the sound made by the frogs.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsuːdəˈkraɪs / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəˈkraɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small North American chorus frogs known for their distinctive calling songs, commonly called chorus frogs.
genus of North American chorus frogs

Examples

The Pseudacris species are well known for their loud breeding choruses in spring.
Scientists discovered a new member of the Pseudacris genus in the wetlands.
Biologists study the habitat preferences of Pseudacris to aid in conservation efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chorus frog - a small frog known for its loud calling, mostly of genus Pseudacris

Etymology

From Greek pseudes (false) + akris (locust or grasshopper), referring to its resemblance to grasshoppers.
