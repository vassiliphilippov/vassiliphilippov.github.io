spatialism

1. NOUN

/ ˈspeɪ.ʃəl.ɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈspeɪ.ʃəl.ɪz.əm / (BrE)
an art movement founded in the late 1940s aiming to integrate colour, sound, space, movement, and time into new forms of art, initiated by Lucio Fontana.
art movement integrating space and time

Examples

Fontana is regarded as the founder of spatialism in the post-war European art scene.
The museum's exhibit explores how spatialism challenged traditional ideas of painting and sculpture.
Her latest installation demonstrates clear influences from the principles of spatialism.

Synset Examples

Originally a figurative artist like many experimentalists, he was influenced by the spatialism of Fontana.

Etymology

From Italian 'spazialismo', from 'spaziale' meaning 'spatial' + '-ismo' (ism). Coined by Lucio Fontana.
