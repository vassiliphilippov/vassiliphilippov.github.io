parenterally

1. ADV

/ pəˈrɛntɚəli / (AmE), / pəˈrɛntərəli / (BrE)
In a manner involving administration of substances by injection or infusion, rather than through the digestive tract.
by injection or infusion; not orally
medical

Examples

The vaccine must be given parenterally to achieve the desired immune response.
Some medications are less effective when taken orally and are therefore administered parenterally.
In emergency cases, fluids are often supplied parenterally to ensure rapid absorption.

Synset Examples

the drug is intended to be administered parenterally

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parenteral - administered other than by mouth, especially by injection
  • Antonym: orally - by mouth, especially in reference to medication administration

Etymology

From 'parenteral,' itself from Greek 'para' (beside) + 'enteron' (intestine), with '-ly' forming the adverb.
