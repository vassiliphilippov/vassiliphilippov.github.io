stupefied

1. ADJ

/ ˈstuːpəˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈstjuːpɪfaɪd / (BrE)
unable to think clearly, usually because of shock, astonishment, or a sudden blow; in a state of mental numbness.
in a state of mental numbness or shock

Examples

She looked stupefied when she heard the unexpected news.
After the accident, he sat stupefied in the middle of the road, unable to move.
The magician’s final trick left the whole audience stupefied with amazement.

Synset Examples

he had a dazed expression on his face
lay semiconscious, stunned (or stupefied) by the blow
was stupid from fatigue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dazed - unable to think clearly, often due to shock or a blow
  • Antonym: alert - able to think clearly; fully awake and aware

Etymology

From Latin stupēfactus, past participle of stupēfacere (to stun, amaze).

2. ADJ

/ ˈstuː.pəˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈstjuː.pɪ.faɪd / (BrE)
so shocked, surprised, or astonished that you are unable to think or react; in a daze
in a shocked or dazed state

Examples

The crowd was stupefied by the magician's final trick and sat in silence for a moment.
After hearing the shocking verdict, she stood stupefied in the courtroom doorway.
He was stupefied when he opened the door to find his long-lost brother standing there.

Synset Examples

a circle of policemen stood dumbfounded by her denial of having seen the accident
the flabbergasted aldermen were speechless
was thunderstruck by the news of his promotion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astonished - very surprised; amazed
  • Synonym: dumbfounded - greatly astonished or shocked
  • Synonym: thunderstruck - extremely surprised or shocked
  • Antonym: unfazed - not shocked or affected by something surprising

Etymology

From Latin *stupefacere* (to stun or astonish).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02366833-s
Synset POS: s
