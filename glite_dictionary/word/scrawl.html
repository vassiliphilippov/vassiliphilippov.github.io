scrawl

1. NOUN

/ skrɔl / (AmE), / skrɔːl / (BrE)
writing that is messy or difficult to read; poor handwriting.
messy or illegible handwriting

Examples

His signature was nothing more than an illegible scrawl at the bottom of the page.
The note was written in a hurried scrawl that I could barely decipher.
Children often hand in their homework in a messy scrawl.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scribble - careless or untidy handwriting or drawing

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'scrawlen', form of 'scrabble'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06415790-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ skrɔl / (AmE), / skrɔːl / (BrE)
to write or draw something quickly and carelessly, often making it difficult to read
write or draw carelessly

Examples

He scrawled his name at the bottom of the form before rushing out the door.
Someone had scrawled graffiti on the wall overnight, making it hard to read.
She quickly scrawled a note and left it on the table for her roommate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scribble - to write or draw something carelessly or hurriedly
  • Degree Lower: to write - to mark letters, words, or symbols on a surface

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Swedish skrávla (to sprawl).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01751011-v
Synset POS: v
