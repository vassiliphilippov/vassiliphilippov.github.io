fuselage

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːzəlɑːʒ / (AmE), / ˈfjuːzəlɑːʒ / (BrE)
the main body of an aircraft, to which the wings and tail are attached, and which accommodates the crew, passengers, or cargo
main body of an aircraft

Examples

After the crash, the fuselage was found mostly intact in the field.
Engineers inspected the fuselage for any signs of structural damage before the flight.
The cargo was loaded into the spacious fuselage of the military transport plane.

Etymology

From French fuselage, from fuseler (to shape like a spindle), from fuseau (spindle).
