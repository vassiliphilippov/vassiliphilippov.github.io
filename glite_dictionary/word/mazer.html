mazer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪzər / (AmE), / ˈmeɪzə / (BrE)
A large drinking bowl made of hardwood, used especially in the Middle Ages.
large medieval hardwood drinking bowl
archaic

Examples

The museum displayed an intricately carved mazer from the fifteenth century.
He raised his mazer in a toast to the assembled guests.
Collectors often seek out antique mazers for their historical value.

Etymology

From Middle English *maser*, from Old French *masere*, from Germanic origin related to the wood's grain.
