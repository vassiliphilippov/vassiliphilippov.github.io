whalesucker

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪlˌsʌkər / (AmE), / ˈweɪlˌsʌkə / (BrE)
a large remora with a bluish coloration, native to the Pacific Ocean, that attaches itself to whales and dolphins
a remora fish that attaches to whales and dolphins

Examples

The whalesucker clung firmly to the bottlenose dolphin as it swam through the Pacific.
Marine biologists observed a whalesucker hitching a ride on the back of a migrating humpback whale.
Unlike other remoras, the whalesucker is rarely seen unattached from its large marine hosts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remora - a fish known for attaching itself to larger marine animals

Etymology

From 'whale' + 'sucker', referring to its habit of attaching to whales.
