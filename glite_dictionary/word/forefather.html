forefather

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌfɑðɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌfɑːðə / (BrE)
a male ancestor, especially one who founded a family or lineage
male ancestor or family founder

Examples

He takes pride in the values passed down by his forefathers.
The traditions established by our forefathers still shape our society today.
Many monuments honor the forefathers who founded the town.

Synset Examples

keep the faith of our forefathers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ancestor - a person from whom one is descended
  • Antonym: descendant - a person descended from a particular ancestor

Etymology

From Old English fore- (before) + father; meaning 'ancestor' dates from early Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌfɑðər / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌfɑːðə / (BrE)
a person in an earlier generation, especially one who was an ancestor or a founder of a family, nation, or tradition
ancestor or founder from an earlier generation

Examples

Many Americans honor their forefathers on national holidays like Independence Day.
The traditions of our forefathers are still celebrated in this region.
She researched her family tree and discovered several notable forefathers.

Synset Examples

our forefathers brought forth a great nation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ancestor - a person from whom one is descended
  • Antonym: descendant - a person descended from a particular ancestor

Etymology

From Middle English forfader, from fore- (before) + father.
