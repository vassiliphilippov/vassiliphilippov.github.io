consumption

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsʌmpʃən / (AmE), / kənˈsʌmpʃən / (BrE)
the act of consuming or using up something, especially food, resources, or energy
the act of consuming or using up

Examples

The country's energy consumption has increased by 10% over the last year.
Limiting salt consumption is important for a healthy diet.
The rapid consumption of natural resources has raised concerns among environmentalists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: use - the act of employing something for a purpose
  • Synonym: expenditure - the act of spending or using up something
  • Antonym: production - the process of making or manufacturing something

Etymology

From Latin 'consumptio', from 'consumere' meaning 'to use up, eat'.

2. NOUN

/ kənˈsʌmpʃən / (AmE), / kənˈsʌmpʃən / (BrE)
the act of taking food or drink into the body by eating or drinking
the act of eating or drinking

Examples

Increasing fiber consumption can improve digestive health.
Overeating leads to excessive consumption of calories.
Alcohol consumption should be limited to maintain good health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ingestion - the process of taking food, drink, or another substance into the body

Etymology

From Latin consumptio (a consuming, wasting), from consumere (to consume).

4. NOUN

/ kənˈsʌmpʃən / (AmE), / kənˈsʌmpʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
(historical) A wasting disease, especially pulmonary tuberculosis, characterized by progressive wasting of the body.
historical term for tuberculosis
archaic

Examples

In the 19th century, many poets succumbed to consumption before reaching old age.
She was said to have died of consumption, a common fate in Victorian England.
The sanatorium was established for those suffering from consumption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuberculosis - an infectious disease usually affecting the lungs

Etymology

From Latin 'consumptio' (a consuming), from 'consumere' (to consume).
