to ignite

1. VERB

/ ɪɡˈnaɪt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈnaɪt / (BrE)
to start or maintain a fire in something, such as a furnace or stove
start or keep a fire burning

Examples

It only took a small spark to ignite the pile of dry wood in the fireplace.
The caretaker will ignite the old furnace every morning during winter.
Make sure to ignite the stove carefully to avoid any accidents.

Synset Examples

fire the furnace

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to extinguish - to put out a fire or light

Etymology

From Latin ignīre (to set on fire), from ignis (fire).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00589392-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪɡˈnaɪt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈnaɪt / (BrE)
To catch fire or cause something to catch fire; to start to burn or burst into flames.
begin to burn or set on fire

Examples

A single spark can ignite dry grass in seconds.
She forgot to turn off the stove, causing the oil to ignite.
The fireworks are engineered to ignite precisely at midnight.

Synset Examples

Marsh gases ignited suddenly
The oily rags combusted spontaneously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to combust - to catch fire and begin burning
  • Antonym: to extinguish - to cause a fire or light to stop burning

Etymology

From Latin ignīre, from ignis (fire).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02766580-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪɡˈnaɪt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈnaɪt / (BrE)
to cause something to start burning; to set on fire
cause to start burning

Examples

A single match can easily ignite a pile of dry leaves.
The firework failed to ignite, disappointing the crowd.
Placing paper near the heater could ignite it accidentally.

Synset Examples

Great heat can ignite almost any dry matter
Light a cigarette

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to kindle - to start a fire; to cause something to begin burning
  • Antonym: to extinguish - to cause a fire to stop burning; put out

Etymology

From Latin ignīre (to set on fire), from ignis (fire).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02765572-v

4. VERB

/ ɪɡˈnaɪt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈnaɪt / (BrE)
to arouse or excite strong feelings or passions in someone
arouse strong feelings or passions

Examples

The leader's passionate speech ignited a wave of enthusiasm among the crowd.
Unfair treatment can ignite feelings of resentment within a community.
The documentary ignited a national conversation on climate change.

Synset Examples

The refugees' fate stirred up compassion around the world
Wake old feelings of hatred

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stir up - to provoke or incite strong feelings

Etymology

From Latin ignīre (to set on fire), from ignis (fire).
