Mentha spicata

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛn.tə spɪˈkɑː.tə / (AmE), / ˈmen.tə spɪˈkɑː.tə / (BrE)
A species of mint, commonly known as spearmint, with clusters of small purplish flowers, often used for flavoring and as a garden herb.
species of mint; spearmint plant

Examples

Mentha spicata is often planted in home gardens for its fragrant leaves and culinary uses.
The essential oil extracted from Mentha spicata is widely used to flavor gum and toothpaste.
Botanists can distinguish Mentha spicata from other mints by its pointed, lance-shaped leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spearmint - common name for Mentha spicata

Etymology

From Latin 'mentha' (mint) and 'spicata' (bearing spikes), referring to its spiked flower clusters.
