Spinacia

1. NOUN

/ spɪˈneɪʃə / (AmE), / spɪˈneɪsiə / (BrE)
A genus of leafy flowering plants in the amaranth family, which includes spinach.
genus of spinach plants

Examples

The botanical name for common spinach is Spinacia oleracea.
Researchers studied the growth habits of plants in the genus Spinacia.
Most cultivated varieties of spinach belong to the Spinacia genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spinach - an edible leafy green plant related to beets and chard

Etymology

From Latin 'spinacia', referring to spinach.
