debonair

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɛbəˈnɛr / (AmE), / ˌdɛbəˈneə / (BrE)
(of a man) confident, stylish, and charming; having a sophisticated charm.
charming and sophisticated (of a man)

Examples

He looked debonair in his crisp suit and polished shoes at the gala.
Everyone admired his debonair manner and easy confidence at the party.
The actor played a debonair spy who always kept his cool under pressure.

Synset Examples

a debonair gentleman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suave - smoothly agreeable and courteous
  • Synonym: urbane - (of a person, especially a man) courteous and refined in manner
  • Antonym: awkward - lacking grace or sophistication

Etymology

From Old French 'debonnaire' (courteous, kind), from de bon aire (of good race, disposition).
