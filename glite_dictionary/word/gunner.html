gunner

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌnər / (AmE), / ˈɡʌnə / (BrE)
a member of the armed forces who operates or is responsible for guns, especially in the artillery
artillery serviceman; gun operator

Examples

The gunner adjusted the cannon before the order to fire was given.
Each gunner was trained to calculate range and elevation for maximum effectiveness.
During the war, her grandfather served as a gunner in the artillery unit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artilleryman - a person who operates large guns or artillery
  • Antonym: infantryman - a soldier in the infantry, not artillery

Etymology

From 'gun' + '-er', meaning one who operates guns.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌnər / (AmE), / ˈɡʌnə / (BrE)
a person who operates or is skilled at shooting a gun, especially in a military or competitive context
someone who shoots a gun

Examples

The army's best gunner was chosen for the dangerous mission.
As a gunner on the team, he rarely missed his target.
The experienced gunner took careful aim before firing the final shot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shooter - a person who shoots, especially with a gun

Etymology

From 'gun' + agent suffix '-er', meaning one who uses a gun.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
