oysterfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪstərˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɔɪstəˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A type of toadfish found in marine environments, known for its broad, flat head and association with oyster beds.
a type of toadfish

Examples

The oysterfish is often found hiding among oyster beds along the coast.
Scientists studied the unique camouflage patterns of the oysterfish for their research.
Fishermen occasionally catch oysterfish when trawling for oysters and other shellfish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toadfish - any of various bottom-dwelling fish with a broad, flattened head

Etymology

From 'oyster' (the mollusk) + 'fish', likely referring to its habitat near oyster beds.
