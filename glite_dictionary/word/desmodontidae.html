Desmodontidae

1. NOUN

/ dɛz.məˈdɑn.tɪ.di / (AmE), / dɛz.məˈdɒn.tɪ.di / (BrE)
a family of bats native to the Americas, comprising the true vampire bats that feed on the blood of other animals
family of true vampire bats

Examples

Members of the Desmodontidae family are known for their blood-feeding habits.
Scientists have studied the saliva of Desmodontidae for its anticoagulant properties.
The habitat of Desmodontidae ranges from Mexico to Argentina.

Etymology

From New Latin Desmodont-, genus name Desmodus, + -idae (taxonomic family suffix from Greek -idēs).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛz.moʊˈdɒn.tɪ.di / (AmE), / ˌdɛz.məʊˈdɒn.tɪ.di / (BrE)
A family of bats known as true vampire bats, comprising species that feed on the blood of mammals or birds.
family of true vampire bats

Examples

Researchers studied the feeding behavior of Desmodontidae in South American rainforests.
The Desmodontidae family is unique among bats for its blood-feeding habits.
Although only three species belong to Desmodontidae, they have a wide geographic range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vampire bat - a bat that feeds on blood, especially of mammals and birds

Etymology

From New Latin Desmodontidae, formed from the genus Desmodus and -idae (indicating an animal family).
