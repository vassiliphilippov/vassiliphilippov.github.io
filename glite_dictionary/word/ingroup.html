ingroup

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌɡruːp / (BrE)
a small, exclusive group of people who share a common interest or identity, often with a sense of belonging or favoritism toward each other
exclusive group with shared identity

Examples

She felt left out because she was not part of the ingroup at work.
In high school, many students try to join the ingroup to increase their popularity.
The company's ingroup controlled most of the important decisions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outgroup - a group outside one's own, often viewed with disfavor or competition
  • Synonym: clique - a small, exclusive group of people

Etymology

Formed from 'in' + 'group', first used in early 20th-century sociology.
