protriptyline

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈtrɪptɪliːn / (AmE), / prəʊˈtrɪptɪliːn / (BrE)
a tricyclic antidepressant drug used to treat clinical depression
antidepressant drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed protriptyline to help manage her symptoms of depression.
Some patients may experience dry mouth as a side effect of protriptyline.
Due to its stimulant effects, protriptyline is often taken in the morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antidepressant - drug used to relieve symptoms of depression
