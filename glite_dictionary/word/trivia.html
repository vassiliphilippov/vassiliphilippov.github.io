trivia

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪviə / (AmE), / ˈtrɪvɪə / (BrE)
Unimportant facts or details; items of little value or significance, often interesting or entertaining.
unimportant or interesting facts

Examples

The quiz was full of trivia about movie history and pop culture.
She enjoys reading books filled with quirky trivia from around the world.
I can never remember sports trivia when playing games with my friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detail - a small part of something; a minor fact
  • Antonym: significance - importance or meaning

Etymology

From Latin 'trivium' (place where three roads meet), later used to mean commonplace or of little importance.
