transplantation

1. NOUN

/ ˌtrænsplænˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtrænzplɑːnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of removing something from one place and introducing it in another, especially a living organism or tissue.
act of moving or transferring something

Examples

The surgeon performed a successful transplantation of the kidney.
Frequent transplantation of seedlings can stress the plants.
The family faced many challenges after their transplantation to a new country.

Synset Examples

the transplant did not flower until the second year
too frequent transplanting is not good for families
she returned to Alabama because she could not bear transplantation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transfer - the act of moving something from one place to another

Etymology

From Latin 'transplantatio', from 'transplantare' (to transplant).

2. NOUN

/ ˌtrænsplænˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtrænzplɑːnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the medical operation in which an organ, tissue, or cell is removed from one organism (the donor) and placed in another (the recipient) to replace a damaged or missing part
medical operation moving an organ or tissue to another body
medical

Examples

The hospital is renowned for its expertise in transplantation of vital organs such as the heart and liver.
After undergoing a kidney transplantation, he required medication to prevent rejection of the new organ.
Advances in transplantation have improved survival rates for thousands of patients worldwide.

Synset Examples

he had a kidney transplant
the long-term results of cardiac transplantation are now excellent
a child had a multiple organ transplant two months ago

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transplant - an organ or tissue that is transplanted, or the act of transplanting

Etymology

From Latin 'transplantatio', from 'trans-' (across) + 'plantare' (to plant).
