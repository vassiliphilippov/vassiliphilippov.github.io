saver

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈseɪvə / (BrE)
a person who saves money regularly, especially by putting it in a bank or account
a person who saves money

Examples

She is a disciplined saver who puts aside part of her salary every month.
Banks often offer higher interest rates to encourage savers.
As a lifelong saver, he always plans for unexpected expenses.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spender - a person who spends money freely

Etymology

From save + -er, forming agent nouns in English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈseɪvər / (AmE), / ˈseɪvə / (BrE)
a person who rescues someone or something from danger or harm
person who rescues from danger

Examples

The lifeguard acted as a true saver when he pulled the child from the pool.
Firefighters are often seen as heroes and savers of lives in their communities.
She will always remember her dog as the saver who barked and warned her of the fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rescuer - a person who saves someone or something from danger or difficulty
  • Synonym: savior - a person who saves someone or something, especially from danger
  • Antonym: endangerer - a person who puts someone or something in danger

Etymology

From Middle English saveour, from Old French sauveour, from Latin salvator (saver, deliverer).
