heartbreaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrtˌbreɪkər / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtˌbreɪkə / (BrE)
A person who is very attractive or charming but does not take emotional relationships seriously, often causing others emotional pain.
charming but irresponsible lover

Examples

Everyone warned me that he was a real heartbreaker, but I didn't listen.
The handsome actor gained a reputation as a heartbreaker in Hollywood.
She turned out to be a heartbreaker, leaving several admirers devastated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flirt - a person who behaves as though attracted to someone, but not seriously
  • Synonym: cad - a man who behaves dishonorably towards a woman

Etymology

Formed from 'heart' + 'breaker'; popularized in 20th-century English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrtˌbreɪkər / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtˌbreɪkə / (BrE)
a defeat or loss, especially one that is very close, sudden, or disappointing, often at the last moment
narrow or last-minute defeat

Examples

The team lost the championship game in a real heartbreaker with only seconds remaining.
Losing by a single point made it a heartbreaker for the players and fans alike.
It was a heartbreaker when the opposing team scored a last-minute goal.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: blowout - a one-sided victory with a large margin

Etymology

From 'heartbreak' + '-er', originally meaning 'something or someone causing heartbreak'.
