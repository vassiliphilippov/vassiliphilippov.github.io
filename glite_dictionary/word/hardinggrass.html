hardinggrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrdɪŋˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdɪŋˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a tall perennial grass native to Australia and South Africa, introduced into North America as a forage crop
a perennial forage grass

Examples

Farmers often plant hardinggrass in pastures because it grows well in dry conditions.
The hardinggrass on the hillside provided excellent grazing for the sheep.
After its introduction, hardinggrass became a common sight in Californian rangelands.
