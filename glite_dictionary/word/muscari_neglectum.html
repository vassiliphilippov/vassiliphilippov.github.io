Muscari neglectum

1. NOUN

/ mʌsˈkɑːri nɪˈɡlɛktəm / (AmE), / mʌsˈkɑːri nɪˈɡlɛktəm / (BrE)
A species of grape hyacinth with abundant, deep blue flowers, commonly cultivated as an ornamental plant.
species of grape hyacinth with dark blue flowers

Examples

Every spring, clusters of Muscari neglectum bloom vibrantly in her garden.
The dark blue flowers of Muscari neglectum attract bees and other pollinators.
Gardeners often plant Muscari neglectum to add early color to flower beds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grape hyacinth - common name for plants in the genus Muscari

Etymology

From modern Latin, Muscari from Greek 'muskos' (musk), referring to the scent, and Latin 'neglectum' (neglected).
