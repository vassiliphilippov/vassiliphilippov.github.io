Gempylus serpens

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛm.pɪ.ləs ˈsɝː.pɛnz / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛm.pɪ.ləs ˈsɜː.pɛnz / (BrE)
A species of predatory tropical fish, known for its elongated body, jutting jaws, and strong teeth; commonly called the snake mackerel.
a species of predatory tropical fish

Examples

Fishermen off the coast of Brazil occasionally catch the elusive Gempylus serpens in their nets.
The distinctive jaws of Gempylus serpens allow it to hunt smaller fish efficiently in open waters.
Marine biologists study Gempylus serpens to better understand the behavior of deep-sea predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snake mackerel - common name for Gempylus serpens, a long-bodied predatory fish

Etymology

From the genus name Gempylus (Greek: 'gendos' meaning a kind of fish), and Latin 'serpens' meaning 'snake'.
