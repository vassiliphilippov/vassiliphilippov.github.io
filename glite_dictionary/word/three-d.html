three-D

1. ADJ

/ θɹiː ˈdiː / (AmE), / θɹiː ˈdiː / (BrE)
(of a film, image, or object) giving the illusion of depth or relating to three dimensions; short for 'three-dimensional'.
giving the illusion of depth; three-dimensional

Examples

The entire movie was shot in three-D, so the action scenes felt incredibly lifelike.
Many modern video games feature three-D graphics for a more immersive experience.
She wore special glasses to watch the three-D film at the cinema.

Synset Examples

lifelike three-dimensional characters
a three-dimensional account of conditions under the new government
they shot the movie in three-D

Related Terms

  • Synonym: three-dimensional - having or relating to three dimensions or aspects
  • Antonym: 2D - having or relating to two dimensions; lacking depth

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'three-dimensional', where 'D' stands for 'dimension'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌθriː ˈdiː / (AmE), / ˌθriː ˈdiː / (BrE)
having or showing three dimensions; giving the illusion of depth, as opposed to being flat or two-dimensional.
three-dimensional; having depth

Examples

The movie was shot entirely in three-D for a striking immersive experience.
With the new technology, children can learn geometry by manipulating objects in three-D.
He prefers video games in three-D to the flat, old-fashioned ones.

Synset Examples

aren't dreams always in 3-D?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: three-dimensional - having or showing three dimensions; not flat
  • Antonym: 2D - two-dimensional; lacking depth

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'three-dimensional'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05948666-n
Synset POS: n
