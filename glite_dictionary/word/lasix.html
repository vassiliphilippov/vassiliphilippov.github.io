Lasix

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪsɪks / (AmE), / ˈleɪsɪks / (BrE)
A proprietary name for the drug furosemide, a diuretic used especially to treat hypertension and edema.
trade name for the diuretic furosemide
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Lasix to help reduce the patient's swelling.
Many patients with heart failure are treated with Lasix to remove excess fluid from the body.
After starting Lasix, she noticed a significant decrease in her blood pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furosemide - diuretic drug for treating edema and hypertension
