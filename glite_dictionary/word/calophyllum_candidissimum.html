Calophyllum candidissimum

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈfɪləm ˌkændɪˈsɪməm / (AmE), / ˌkaləˈfɪləm ˌkandɪˈsɪmɪm / (BrE)
A tropical American tree valued for its hard, durable wood, used in carpentry and construction.
tropical American tree with hard wood

Examples

The wood of Calophyllum candidissimum is highly prized for making sturdy furniture.
In parts of Central America, Calophyllum candidissimum is commonly planted for shade and timber.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of extracts from Calophyllum candidissimum leaves.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Calophyllum' from Greek 'kalos' (beautiful) + 'phyllon' (leaf), and 'candidissimum' meaning 'most bright or shining'.
