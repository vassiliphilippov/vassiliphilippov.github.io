pyroxene

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈrɑksiːn / (AmE), / pɪˈrɒksiːn / (BrE)
any of a group of crystalline silicate minerals commonly found in igneous and metamorphic rocks
group of silicate minerals in rocks

Examples

The basalt sample contained a high proportion of pyroxene crystals.
Geologists identified the mineral as pyroxene based on its structure and composition.
Many metamorphic rocks are characterized by the presence of pyroxene.

Etymology

From German 'Pyroxen' from Greek 'pyr' (fire) + 'xenos' (stranger), as these minerals were thought to be accidental in igneous rocks.
