Fundulus

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌn.djʊ.ləs / (AmE), / ˈfʌn.djʊ.ləs / (BrE)
a genus of small, freshwater or brackish fish commonly known as killifish or topminnows, found in North America.
a genus of small killifish

Examples

The researcher collected several Fundulus species from the coastal marsh for her study.
Fundulus are popular with biologists because of their adaptability to a wide range of salinities.
In aquariums, Fundulus species are appreciated for their hardiness and vivid coloration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: killifish - small fish of various genera, including Fundulus, found in fresh and brackish water

Etymology

From Latin, diminutive of 'fundus' meaning 'bottom', referencing bottom-dwelling habits.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfʌndjʊləs / (AmE), / ˈfʌndjʊləs / (BrE)
A genus of small freshwater fish known as killifish or topminnows, found primarily in North America.
a genus of small North American killifish

Examples

Scientists studying coastal ecology often collect Fundulus specimens for their research.
The aquarium featured several brightly colored Fundulus swimming near the water's surface.
Many anglers are familiar with Fundulus as hardy baitfish.

Etymology

From Latin *fundus* (bottom), possibly referring to the fish's habitat.
