sandpit

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændˌpɪt / (AmE), / ˈsændˌpɪt / (BrE)
a shallow container or an area outdoors filled with sand for children to play in
area or box of sand for children to play in

Examples

The children spent the afternoon building castles in the sandpit.
After the rain stopped, the sandpit was too wet to use.
Many parks have a sandpit next to the playground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandbox - a box filled with sand for children to play in (American English)

2. NOUN

/ ˈsændˌpɪt / (AmE), / ˈsæn.pɪt / (BrE)
A large excavation in sandy ground from which sand is extracted, often for industrial or construction purposes.
pit for digging sand

Examples

The workers extracted tons of sand from the sandpit every day.
A new sandpit was opened near the river to supply construction materials for the city.
After heavy rain, the sandpit partially filled with water, creating a temporary pond.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarry - an open excavation from which stone or minerals are extracted
