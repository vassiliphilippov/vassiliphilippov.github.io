acrostic

1. NOUN

/ əˈkrɑː.stɪk / (AmE), / əˈkrɒs.tɪk / (BrE)
A poem or verse in which certain letters in each line, such as the first or last, spell out a word, message, or alphabet when read in sequence.
verse with hidden message by initial letters

Examples

The poet wrote an acrostic, with each line spelling her name down the margin.
Children enjoyed creating an acrostic to celebrate the start of spring.
Hidden within the acrostic was a secret message to the reader.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek akrostikhis, from akron (end) + stikhos (line, verse).

2. NOUN

/ əˈkrɑː.stɪk / (AmE), / əˈkrɒ.stɪk / (BrE)
A word puzzle in which letters from each line or row form a word or message when read vertically or horizontally, or a composition in which certain letters of each line spell out a word or message.
word puzzle forming words across and down

Examples

She loves solving acrostic puzzles in the newspaper every Sunday morning.
The teacher assigned an acrostic as part of the creative writing lesson.
Each acrostic revealed a hidden motivational word when completed correctly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crossword - a word puzzle that typically involves filling in squares with words based on clues

Etymology

From Greek akrostikhis, from akron (end) + stikhos (line, verse).
