inappreciable

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.əˈpri.ʃi.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.əˈpriː.ʃə.bl̩ / (BrE)
too small or insignificant to be noticed or to make a meaningful difference
too small to be noticed or matter

Examples

The difference in taste between the two brands was inappreciable to most consumers.
There was an inappreciable amount of movement during the experiment, so we considered the results stable.
His contribution to the discussion was so inappreciable that few people noticed he had spoken at all.

Synset Examples

inappreciable fluctuations in temperature

Related Terms

  • Antonym: appreciable - large or noticeable enough to be significant

Etymology

From Latin prefix in- (not) + appreciable (able to be valued).
