twentieth

1. ADJ

/ ˈtwɛn.ti.əθ / (AmE), / ˈtwɛntiɪθ / (BrE)
coming after the nineteenth in position; 20th in a sequence
ordinal number: 20th in sequence

Examples

She finished in twentieth place in the marathon, a personal best for her.
This year marks the twentieth anniversary of the foundation's establishment.
On the twentieth floor of the building, you can see the city skyline.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: nineteenth - coming after the eighteenth in position; 19th
  • Degree Higher: twenty-first - coming after the twentieth in position; 21st

Etymology

From Middle English 'twentithe', based on 'twenty' + '-eth' (ordinal suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtwɛn.ti.əθ / (AmE), / ˈtwɛn.ti.ɪθ / (BrE)
The position of someone or something that is number 20 in a sequence; one of twenty equal parts.
position number 20 in a sequence

Examples

She finished in twentieth place in the marathon, just ahead of her friend.
Only a twentieth of the cake remained after the party was over.
On the twentieth of June, the company will announce its quarterly results.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: nineteenth - position number 19 in a series
  • Degree Higher: twenty-first - position number 21 in a series

Etymology

From Old English 'twēntig' (twenty) + '-eth' (ordinal suffix).

Synset POS: n
