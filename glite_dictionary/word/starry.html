starry

1. ADJ

/ ˈstɑri / (AmE), / ˈstɑːri / (BrE)
full of or resembling stars; covered with or shining like stars.
full of or resembling stars

Examples

We sat outside, gazing up at the starry sky on a summer evening.
Her dress sparkled with a starry pattern that caught everyone's eye.
The poem describes a starry expanse stretching across the heavens.

Synset Examples

a starry night
starry illumination

Related Terms

  • Antonym: starless - without stars; having no stars visible

Etymology

From 'star' + '-y', an adjective-forming suffix.
