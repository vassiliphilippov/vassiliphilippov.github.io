lacuna

1. NOUN

/ ləˈkunə / (AmE), / ləˈkjuːnə / (BrE)
an unfilled space, gap, or missing part, especially in a manuscript or other text
a gap or missing part

Examples

The historian pointed out a significant lacuna in the ancient records.
There is a lacuna on page five where a paragraph is missing.
His essay had a lacuna that made the argument hard to follow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gap - an unfilled space or interval
  • Synonym: void - a completely empty space
  • Synonym: absence - the state of being away or missing

Etymology

From Latin 'lacuna' (pool, gap, hole), diminutive of 'lacus' (lake).

2. NOUN

/ ləˈkuːnə / (AmE), / ləˈkjuːnə / (BrE)
an empty space, gap, or missing part in something, especially in a manuscript, text, or body of knowledge
a gap or missing part

Examples

The historian noted a significant lacuna in the record of that period.
Her argument is weakened by a lacuna in the supporting evidence.
The manuscript contains a lacuna where several pages are missing.

Synset Examples

The research revealed a lacuna in the existing studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gap - an empty space or opening in something

Etymology

From Latin 'lacuna' (ditch, gap), diminutive of 'lacus' (lake, hollow).
