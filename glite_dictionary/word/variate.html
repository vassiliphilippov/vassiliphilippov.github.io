variate

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛriˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɛəriˌeɪt / (BrE)
a quantity or variable whose value is subject to randomness, especially in statistics.
random variable (statistics)

Examples

The scientist analyzed the variate to understand the spread of measurement errors.
Each variate in the model represents a different aspect of the system's randomness.
Statisticians often study how a variate behaves under different conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: random variable - a variable whose value is subject to variations due to chance

Etymology

From Latin variatus, past participle of variare (to change).
