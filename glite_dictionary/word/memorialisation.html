memorialisation

1. NOUN

/ məˌmɔɹiəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / mɪˌmɔːrɪəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of commemorating someone or something, especially through a ceremony or the creation of a monument
commemoration of someone or something
british

Examples

The annual memorialisation of the fallen soldiers brings the community together each November.
His speech was a moving part of the memorialisation for those who lost their lives in the disaster.
Public art has often played a role in the memorialisation of historical events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commemoration - the act of remembering and showing respect for someone or something

Etymology

From memorial + -isation, influenced by British spelling of 'memorialization'.
