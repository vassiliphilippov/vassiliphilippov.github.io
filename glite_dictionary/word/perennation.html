perennation

1. NOUN

/ pəˌrɛˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˌrɛˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a plant or other organism survives from one growing season to the next, often across several years, typically by means of specialized structures such as bulbs, tubers, or rhizomes.
survival of plants across years
medical

Examples

The perennation of certain plants is achieved through the growth of underground storage organs.
Daffodils rely on perennation to bloom year after year without replanting.
Adaptations for perennation help species survive harsh winters and recurring drought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perennial - a plant that lives for more than two years

Etymology

From Latin 'perennis' (lasting throughout the year).
