Christoph Willibald Gluck

1. PROPN

/ ˈkrɪstɒf ˈvɪlibɑld ɡlʊk / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstɒf ˈvɪlɪbald ɡlʊk / (BrE)
(1714–1787) a German composer known for his influential operas and reforms to opera seria.
German composer of operas (1714–1787)

Examples

Christoph Willibald Gluck's operas played a key role in the development of classical music.
Many scholars consider Christoph Willibald Gluck a pioneer of dramatic opera reform in the 18th century.
The Paris premier of a new Christoph Willibald Gluck opera drew considerable attention from the press.
