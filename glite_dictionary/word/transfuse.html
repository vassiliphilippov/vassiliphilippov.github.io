to transfuse

1. VERB

/ trænsˈfjuz / (AmE), / trænsˈfjuːz / (BrE)
to gradually impart or infuse a quality, feeling, or idea into a person or group
gradually impart a quality or idea

Examples

The mentor sought to transfuse a sense of curiosity into her students through creative lessons.
His stories transfuse hope and resilience into everyone who listens.
A great teacher can transfuse enthusiasm for learning into a whole classroom.

Synset Examples

Her presence instilled faith into the children
transfuse love of music into the students

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to instill - to gradually impart an idea or feeling

Etymology

From Latin transfundere (to pour from one vessel into another), from trans- (across) + fundere (to pour).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02330038-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ trænsˈfjuːz / (AmE), / trænsˈfjuːz / (BrE)
to transfer blood or another fluid into a person's vein or circulatory system, especially as a medical treatment
transfer blood or fluid medically
medical

Examples

The doctor decided to transfuse two units of blood to stabilize the patient.
After losing so much blood in the accident, she had to be transfused immediately.
They are transfusing plasma to support his recovery during surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transfusion - the act of transferring blood or fluid

Etymology

From Latin 'transfundere', meaning 'to pour from one to another' ('trans-' across, 'fundere' to pour).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00081120-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ trænsˈfjuz / (AmE), / trænsˈfjuːz / (BrE)
to pour (a liquid) from one vessel or container into another; to transfer by pouring
pour liquid from one vessel to another

Examples

The chemist carefully transfused the solution from the beaker into a flask.
After boiling, the herbal tea was transfused into a smaller container for serving.
They transfuse the wine from one barrel to another to improve its flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pour - to cause a liquid to flow from one container to another

Etymology

From Latin transfundere, transfus-, meaning 'to pour from one to another'; from trans- 'across' + fundere 'to pour'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01857426-v
Synset POS: v
