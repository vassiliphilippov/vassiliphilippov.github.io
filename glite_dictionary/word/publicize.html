to publicize

1. VERB

/ ˈpʌblɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪsʌɪz / (BrE)
to make something known to the public; to give publicity to something or someone
make widely known; give publicity

Examples

The company decided to publicize its new environmental policy to attract more customers.
She used social media to publicize her art exhibition.
The government will publicize the changes in the tax system next week.

Synset Examples

She aired her opinions on welfare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to advertise - to make something known, especially to promote sales
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep something secret; to not make public

Etymology

From 'public' + '-ize', first attested in early 19th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈpʌblɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪˌsaɪz / (BrE)
to make something widely known; to bring to public attention
make widely known; announce publicly

Examples

The company plans to publicize its new product launch through various media channels.
She did not want to publicize her achievements and preferred to remain humble.
Efforts to publicize the fundraising event resulted in a large turnout.

Synset Examples

Please don't advertise the fact that he has AIDS

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to advertise - to draw attention to a product or service, especially for commercial purposes
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep something secret or hidden

Etymology

From public + -ize, dating to the mid-19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00977934-v
Synset POS: v
