allegoriser

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɛɡəˌraɪzər / (AmE), / əˈlɛɡəˌraɪzə / (BrE)
a person who expresses ideas or communicates using allegories
person who speaks or writes in allegories

Examples

The poet was renowned as an allegoriser, weaving complex moral lessons into his verse.
As a skilled allegoriser, she often used symbolism to critique society.
In literary circles, an effective allegoriser can provoke deep reflection among readers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allegorist - a person who composes or uses allegories

Etymology

From 'allegorise' + '-er'; 'allegorise' from Medieval Latin allegorizare, from allegoria (allegory).
