amidopyrine

1. NOUN

/ əˌmɪdoʊˈpaɪrin / (AmE), / əˌmɪdəʊˈpʌɪriːn / (BrE)
A white crystalline compound formerly used as an analgesic and antipyretic to relieve pain and reduce fever.
white crystalline analgesic and antipyretic compound
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed amidopyrine to help lower the patient's high fever.
Researchers studied the side effects of amidopyrine in clinical trials during the 20th century.
Due to health concerns, amidopyrine is rarely used in modern medicine.

Etymology

From amido- (containing an amino group) and pyrene (relating to the pyrazolone ring structure).
