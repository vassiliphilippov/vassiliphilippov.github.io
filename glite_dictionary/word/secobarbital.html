secobarbital

1. NOUN

/ ˌsiːkoʊˈbɑːrbɪtɔːl / (AmE), / ˌsiːkəʊˈbɑːbɪtɒl / (BrE)
A barbiturate drug used as a sedative and to treat convulsions, often in the form of its sodium salt.
barbiturate sedative drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed secobarbital to help the patient manage severe insomnia.
Excessive use of secobarbital can lead to dependence and serious health risks.
After the seizure, she received a dose of secobarbital to control her convulsions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barbiturate - A class of drugs that act as central nervous system depressants.

Etymology

From 'seco-' (indicating cleavage in a ring structure) and 'barbital' (a barbiturate compound).
