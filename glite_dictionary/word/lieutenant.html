lieutenant

1. NOUN

/ luːˈtɛnənt / (AmE), / lɛfˈtɛnənt / (BrE)
a police officer of middle or junior rank, typically ranking below captain
police officer of mid-rank

Examples

The lieutenant led the investigation into the robbery downtown.
After ten years on the force, she was promoted to lieutenant.
The lieutenant supervised a team of ten detectives in the department.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: captain - senior police officer, typically ranks above lieutenant
  • Degree Lower: sergeant - police officer typically ranking just below lieutenant

Etymology

From French *lieutenant*, meaning 'place holder' (from 'lieu' meaning 'place' and 'tenant' meaning 'holding').

2. NOUN

/ luːˈtɛnənt / (AmE), / lɛfˈtɛnənt / (BrE)
An officer holding a commissioned rank in the United States Navy or Coast Guard, above lieutenant junior grade and below lieutenant commander.
US Navy or Coast Guard commissioned officer rank

Examples

After five years of dedicated service, Sarah was promoted to lieutenant in the Coast Guard.
The lieutenant reported the ship's condition to the captain every morning.
He served as a lieutenant during his deployment with the U.S. Navy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: lieutenant commander - a commissioned officer rank above lieutenant in the US Navy and Coast Guard
  • Degree Lower: lieutenant junior grade - a commissioned officer rank below lieutenant in the US Navy and Coast Guard

Etymology

From French 'lieutenant', from 'lieu' (place) and 'tenant' (holding).

3. NOUN

/ luːˈtɛnənt / (AmE), / lɛfˈtɛnənt / (BrE)
A commissioned officer of a rank in the armed forces, above second lieutenant and below captain in the army, and below lieutenant commander in the navy.
commissioned military officer rank

Examples

After years of hard work, she was promoted to lieutenant in the navy.
The lieutenant led his platoon through the dense forest.
As a lieutenant, he was responsible for training new recruits.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: captain - a commissioned officer of higher rank than lieutenant in the army or navy
  • Degree Lower: second lieutenant - a commissioned officer of lower rank than lieutenant in the army

Etymology

From French 'lieutenant', from 'lieu' (place) + 'tenant' (holding).

4. NOUN

/ luːˈtɛnənt / (AmE), / lɛfˈtɛnənt / (BrE)
A person who acts as an assistant or deputy, especially with authority to exercise power when their superior is absent.
assistant or deputy with delegated authority

Examples

He served as the CEO's trusted lieutenant during the company's reorganization.
When the director was away, her lieutenant managed the operations smoothly.
The mayor relied on his lieutenant to oversee the budget committee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deputy - a person who is appointed to assist or act for another
  • Synonym: assistant - a person who helps or supports someone else
  • Antonym: superior - a person of higher rank or authority

Etymology

From French 'lieutenant', from 'lieu' (place) and 'tenant' (holding); literally 'place holder'.
