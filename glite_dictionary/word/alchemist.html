alchemist

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.kə.mɪst / (AmE), / ˈæl.kəmɪst / (BrE)
a person who practiced alchemy, especially in the Middle Ages, seeking to transform base metals into gold or discover an elixir of life
practitioner of alchemy

Examples

The alchemist spent years searching for the philosopher's stone in his hidden laboratory.
According to legends, an alchemist could transform lead into gold.
Many an alchemist promised a panacea to kings and nobles throughout history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alchemy - the medieval forerunner of chemistry, concerned with the transmutation of matter

Etymology

From Old French *alquimiste*, from Medieval Latin *alchimista*, from Arabic *al-kīmiyā* (the art of transformation).
