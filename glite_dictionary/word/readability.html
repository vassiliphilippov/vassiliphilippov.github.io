readability

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːdəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌriːdəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of writing or print that is easy to read and understand
quality of being easy to read

Examples

The readability of the document was improved by using a larger font.
Her handwriting has poor readability, making grading papers difficult for her teacher.
Designers assess the readability of websites to ensure visitors can easily find information.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legibility - the quality of being clear enough to read, especially handwriting or print

Etymology

From 'read' + '-ability', modeled after 'readable'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌriːdəˈbɪlət̬i / (AmE), / ˌriːdəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of written language that makes it easy to read and understand
ease of reading and understanding text

Examples

The readability of this report makes it ideal for a general audience.
Designers often use larger fonts to improve the readability of web pages.
Complex jargon reduces the readability of scientific articles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand
  • Antonym: obscurity - the quality of being difficult to understand

Etymology

From 'read' + '-ability', patterned on similar abstract nouns.
