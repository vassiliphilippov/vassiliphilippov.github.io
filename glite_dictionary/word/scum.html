scum

1. NOUN

/ skʌm / (AmE), / skʌm / (BrE)
a layer of dirt, impurities, or floating matter, such as foam or algae, that forms on the surface of a liquid
film of impurities on liquid surface

Examples

After the storm, a thick layer of scum covered the surface of the pond.
Be sure to remove the scum from the top of the boiling soup.
Algae can quickly create green scum that floats on stagnant water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foam - a mass of small bubbles formed on or in liquid

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch schum or Middle Low German schūm (foam, froth).

2. VERB

/ skʌm / (AmE), / skʌm / (BrE)
to remove a layer of scum or impurities from the surface of a liquid
remove impurities from liquid surface

Examples

After boiling the soup, he carefully scummed the surface to remove the impurities.
The brewer needs to scum off the foam during fermentation.
She scummed the jam as it cooked to ensure it stayed clear and smooth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skim - remove a substance from the surface of a liquid
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02371350-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ skʌm / (AmE), / skʌm / (BrE)
a term used to refer to people regarded as despicable, worthless, or contemptible
despicable or worthless people

Examples

He called the criminals scum who deserved no sympathy.
Some considered the corrupt officials to be nothing but scum.
The protesters shouted that the company executives were scum for polluting the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rabble - a disorderly or worthless crowd; the riffraff
  • Synonym: vermin - offensive term for people regarded as despicable or causing harm

Etymology

Originally from Middle Dutch 'schum' (foam, froth); transferred sense to contemptible people dates from the 18th century.
