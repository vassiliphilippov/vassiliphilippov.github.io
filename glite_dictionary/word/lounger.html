lounger

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaʊndʒər / (AmE), / ˈlaʊndʒə / (BrE)
a comfortable armchair whose back can be lowered and footrest raised to allow the sitter to recline
reclining armchair

Examples

He spent the afternoon reading in his favorite lounger by the window.
The living room was furnished with a large lounger and a matching footstool.
After a long day at work, she relaxed in the lounger and watched television.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recliner - a chair that reclines; similar to a lounger
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04069540-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaʊndʒər / (AmE), / ˈlaʊndʒə / (BrE)
a loose-fitting piece of clothing worn for comfort, usually at home or while relaxing
comfortable garment for leisure wear

Examples

After a long day at work, she changed into her favorite lounger to relax on the sofa.
The store's new collection includes a soft cotton lounger perfect for weekend mornings.
He bought a matching lounger and robe set for his wife as a birthday gift.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loungewear - casual clothing designed for relaxing at home

Etymology

From 'lounge' (to recline or relax) + '-er' suffix forming nouns.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03697330-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈlaʊndʒər / (AmE), / ˈlaʊndʒə / (BrE)
a person who spends time in a lazy or idle way
person who idles or loafs

Examples

He became a habitual lounger at the local coffee shop, rarely getting anything done.
Don't be such a lounger; there's plenty of work to do around here.
The beach was filled with loungers soaking up the afternoon sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idler - a person who avoids work or spends time lazily
  • Synonym: loafer - a person who is habitually lazy or does not work
  • Antonym: workaholic - a person who compulsively works hard and long hours

Etymology

From the verb 'lounge' (to idle, to recline) + -er.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10008241-n
Synset POS: n
