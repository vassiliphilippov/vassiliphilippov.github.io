to modify

1. VERB

/ ˈmɑdəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɒdɪfaɪ / (BrE)
(in linguistics) To add a word or phrase that restricts or adds to the meaning of another word or phrase within a sentence.
add a modifier to a constituent

Examples

In the phrase 'very tall man,' the word 'very' modifies the adjective 'tall.'
Adverbs are often used to modify verbs in a sentence.
A prepositional phrase can modify a noun to give more specific information.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to qualify - to limit or modify the meaning of another word

Etymology

From Latin *modificare* (to measure, limit), from *modus* (measure) and *facere* (to make).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00171140-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈmɑːdəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɒdɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make small changes to something, usually in order to improve it or make it more suitable for a particular purpose
make small changes to something

Examples

We should modify our plans to account for the new restrictions.
The software allows users to easily modify their documents.
She modified her speech after receiving feedback from the audience.

Synset Examples

The advent of the automobile may have altered the growth pattern of the city
The discussion has changed my thinking about the issue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to alter - to change or make different in some respect
  • Degree Higher: to transform - to make a thorough or dramatic change in form or appearance
  • Degree Lower: to adjust - to make minor changes to fit or suit conditions

Etymology

From Latin modificare (to measure, regulate, limit), from modus (measure, manner).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00126072-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈmɑːdəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɒdɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make something less severe, harsh, or extreme; to soften or moderate
make less severe or extreme

Examples

To reduce negative feedback, the teacher decided to modify her tone.
He agreed to modify his opinion after listening to their concerns.
The policy was modified to be less strict after public protests.

Synset Examples

please modify this letter to make it more polite
he modified his views on same-gender marriage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to soften - make less severe, harsh, or extreme
  • Antonym: to intensify - make more severe or extreme

Etymology

From Latin modificare (to measure, limit), from modus (measure, manner).
