pea

1. NOUN

/ piː / (AmE), / piː / (BrE)
a climbing plant of the genus Pisum, with small white flowers and long green pods containing edible round green seeds
edible leguminous plant; its green seed

Examples

She added fresh peas to the salad for extra crunch and sweetness.
The children planted pea seeds in the garden last spring.
After shelling a basket of peas, he cooked them for dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bean - an edible seed, typically kidney-shaped, of various legumes

Etymology

From Latin 'pisum'; ultimately from Greek 'pison'.

2. NOUN

/ piː / (AmE), / piː / (BrE)
a small, round, green seed that is eaten as a vegetable, taken from the pod of the pea plant
small round green seed used as food

Examples

She added fresh peas to the salad for extra flavor.
The children were picking peas in the garden on a sunny afternoon.
Frozen pea soup is a quick and healthy lunch option.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bean - edible seed usually larger than a pea, from different leguminous plants

Etymology

From Latin 'pisum', from Greek 'pison' (pea), influenced by Old English 'pise'.

3. NOUN

/ piː / (AmE), / piː / (BrE)
a small, round, green seed that is eaten as a vegetable and comes from the pod of the pea plant
small green vegetable seed

Examples

Please add some peas to the salad for extra color and flavor.
Children often enjoy eating peas because they are sweet and easy to chew.
We harvested a basket full of fresh peas from the garden this morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bean - an edible seed, typically kidney-shaped, of various plants of the legume family
  • Synonym: lentil - a small, round, edible seed from the lentil plant

Etymology

From Middle English 'pease', from Old English 'pise', from Latin 'pisum'. The singular 'pea' was formed by mistaken interpretation of 'pease' as a plural.
