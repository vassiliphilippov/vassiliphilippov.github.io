irreparably

1. ADV

/ ɪˈrɛpərəbli / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛpərəbli / (BrE)
In a way that is impossible to repair or put right.
in a way that cannot be repaired

Examples

The damage to the historic painting was irreparably done during the accident.
Their friendship was irreparably harmed after the heated argument.
If the data is lost, it could be irreparably gone forever.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irreparable - impossible to repair or make right

Etymology

From 'irreparable' + '-ly'. Ultimately from Latin 'irreparabilis' (not able to be repaired).
