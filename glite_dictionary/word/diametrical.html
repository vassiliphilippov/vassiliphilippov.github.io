diametrical

1. ADJ

/ ˌdaɪəˈmɛtrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈmɛtrɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or situated along a diameter; extending or measured across a diameter.
along or relating to a diameter

Examples

The engineer drew a diametrical line to divide the circle into two equal halves.
In geology, a diametrical section of a rock can reveal internal structures.
The design required a diametrical measurement to ensure symmetry.

Synset Examples

the diametral plane

Etymology

From diameter + -ical, modeled after Latin diametricus.

2. ADJ

/ ˌdaɪəˈmɛtrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈmɛtrɪkəl / (BrE)
completely opposed or being at opposite extremes
completely opposed; at extremes

Examples

Their opinions were diametrical, making compromise almost impossible.
The two proposals represent diametrical approaches to the issue.
Her reaction was in diametrical contrast to what I expected.

Synset Examples

in diametric contradiction to his claims
diametrical (or opposite) points of view
opposite meanings
extreme and indefensible polar positions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opposite - being completely different or contrary

Etymology

From Late Latin diametricus (of a diameter), from Greek diametros (diameter), referring originally to points on opposite sides of a diameter.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02073716-s
Synset POS: s
