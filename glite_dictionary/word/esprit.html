esprit

1. NOUN

/ ɛˈspri / (AmE), / ɛˈspri / (BrE)
Intelligence, liveliness, or vivacity of mind or spirit; wit.
liveliness or wit of mind

Examples

Her esprit made her the center of conversation at every dinner party.
The team relied on his esprit to lift their spirits during challenging times.
The novel is distinguished by the esprit of its dialogue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wit - cleverness and humor in expression
  • Synonym: vivacity - liveliness or animation, especially of speech or manner

Etymology

From French 'esprit' (spirit, wit), from Latin 'spiritus' (spirit, breath).
