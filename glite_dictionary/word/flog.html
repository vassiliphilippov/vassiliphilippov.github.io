to flog

1. VERB

/ flɑg / (AmE), / flɒg / (BrE)
to beat someone severely with a whip or rod as a form of punishment
beat with a whip or rod

Examples

The captain threatened to flog any sailor caught stealing.
In the past, teachers sometimes flogged students as discipline, which is now illegal.
He was flogged publicly for breaking the strict rules of the community.

Synset Examples

The teacher often flogged the students
The children were severely trounced

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whip - to strike or beat with a whip
  • Synonym: birch - to beat someone, especially with a birch rod, as punishment
  • Antonym: spare - to refrain from punishing or beating

Etymology

Origin unknown; possibly related to 18th-century cant or Scandinavian roots.

2. VERB

/ flɑg / (AmE), / flɒg / (BrE)
to beat someone with a whip or stick as a punishment or for discipline
beat with a whip or stick

Examples

The sailor was flogged for disobeying orders.
He threatened to flog the prisoner if he did not confess.
In the past, students were sometimes flogged for misbehavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whip - to strike with a flexible instrument such as a whip
  • Degree Lower: spank - to slap someone, especially a child, on the bottom as punishment
  • Degree Higher: thrash - to beat repeatedly and violently

Etymology

Perhaps from earlier slang or dialectal forms (uncertain origin).
