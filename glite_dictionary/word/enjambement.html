enjambement

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈdʒæmbmənt / (AmE), / ɒnˈʒæmbmənt / (BrE)
The continuation of a sentence or phrase from one line of poetry to the next without a pause or break.
continuation of a poetic line without pause

Examples

The poet used enjambement to create a sense of movement and urgency in her verses.
Many modern poems feature enjambement to connect images and ideas across lines.
The lack of punctuation at the end of the line is a clear example of enjambement in this stanza.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enjambment - the same poetic device, alternative spelling

Etymology

From French enjambement, literally 'straddling', from enjamber (to stride over).
