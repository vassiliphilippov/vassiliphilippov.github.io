to opalesce

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊ.pəˈlɛs / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.pəˈlɛs / (BrE)
to display a play of colors similar to that seen in an opal; to exhibit an iridescent or shimmering appearance
show colors like an opal

Examples

As the sunlight streamed through the glass, it began to opalesce beautifully.
Certain minerals opalesce when exposed to ultraviolet light, showing remarkable colors.
The surface of the lake seemed to opalesce at dawn, glimmering with shifting hues.

Synset Examples

This nitrate opalesces under certain conditions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opalescent - displaying a milky iridescence like that of an opal

Etymology

From opal + -esce (from Latin -escere, denoting the beginning of an action or a process).

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊ.pəˈlɛs / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.pəˈlɛs / (BrE)
to reflect light or display colors in a way similar to an opal, showing a play of rainbow-like hues
to shine with opal-like colors

Examples

The dewdrops on the grass began to opalesce in the early morning sun.
Her silk dress seemed to opalesce with every step she took under the ballroom lights.
As the soap bubble drifted through the air, its surface would opalesce beautifully.

Synset Examples

Distant clouds opalesce like pale brocade
raindrops caught in a sunbeam seem to opalesce

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to iridesce - to show luminous colors that seem to change when seen from different angles

Etymology

From opal +‎ -esce, patterning after 'adolescere' or 'coalesce'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02771189-v
Synset POS: v
