asylum

1. NOUN

/ əˈsaɪləm / (AmE), / əˈsaɪləm / (BrE)
A place offering shelter and protection from danger or hardship; a refuge.
place of protection or refuge

Examples

After escaping the war, the family sought asylum in a neighboring country.
The old church became an asylum during the storm, sheltering anyone in need.
Throughout history, many have risked everything to find asylum from persecution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refuge - a place providing safety or shelter
  • Synonym: sanctuary - a place of safety, especially for people in danger

Etymology

From Greek asylon (refuge, sanctuary), from a- (not) + sylē (seizure).

2. NOUN

/ əˈsaɪləm / (AmE), / əˈsaɪləm / (BrE)
a hospital or institution for the care of people with mental illnesses, especially in the past; a psychiatric hospital
hospital for mentally ill people (historical)
archaic

Examples

After his breakdown, he was admitted to an asylum for treatment.
Many old asylums have now been converted into community centers.
The novel tells the story of a young woman confined to an asylum in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychiatric hospital - a modern medical facility for treating mental illness
  • Synonym: hospital - a place where sick or injured people are cared for

Etymology

From Greek asylon (refuge), from a- (without) + sylon (right of seizure).
