Eichhornia

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈkɔrniə / (AmE), / aɪˈkɔːniə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical aquatic plants known as water hyacinths, noted for their floating leaves and violet-blue flowers.
genus of aquatic plants (water hyacinths)

Examples

The invasive Eichhornia species have created dense mats on the surface of local lakes.
Researchers studied the rapid growth of Eichhornia in tropical river systems.
Farmers struggled to control Eichhornia blocking their irrigation channels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water hyacinth - common name for Eichhornia species, aquatic plants with floating leaves and purple flowers

Etymology

Named after German nobleman Johann Albrecht Friedrich Eichhorn.

2. NOUN

/ aɪkˈhɔrniə / (AmE), / aɪˈkɔːniə / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic plants in the family Pontederiaceae, commonly known as water hyacinths.
genus of aquatic plants (water hyacinths)

Examples

Many invasive species of Eichhornia can clog waterways and disrupt local ecosystems.
The Eichhornia genus is well known for its attractive purple flowers and rapid growth.
Researchers studied the spread of Eichhornia in tropical rivers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water hyacinth - A free-floating aquatic plant, especially Eichhornia crassipes, with glossy leaves and lavender flowers

Etymology

Named after Johann Albrecht Friedrich Eichhorn, a German minister of education.
