Hermann Wilhelm Göring

1. PROPN

/ hɛrˈmɑːn ˈwɪlhɛlm ˈɡɜrɪŋ / (AmE), / hɛˈmaːn ˈvɪlhɛlm ˈɡɜːrɪŋ / (BrE)
(1893–1946) German politician, leading member of the Nazi Party, founder of the Gestapo, and key figure in mobilizing Germany for World War II.
German Nazi Party leader (1893–1946)

Examples

Hermann Wilhelm Göring played a major role in the leadership of Nazi Germany during World War II.
The establishment of the Gestapo is often attributed to Hermann Wilhelm Göring in historical accounts.
After the war, Hermann Wilhelm Göring was tried at Nuremberg for his crimes.
