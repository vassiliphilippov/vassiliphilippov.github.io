partizan

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrtɪˌzæn / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtɪzan / (BrE)
a pike with a long, tapering, double-edged blade and lateral projections, used chiefly in the 16th and 17th centuries
type of historical pole weapon

Examples

The museum's collection included a partizan from the Renaissance era.
Soldiers wielded the partizan during ceremonies as well as in battle.
The ornate design on the partizan marked it as the weapon of a high-ranking guard.

Etymology

From Middle French 'partisane', from Italian 'partigiana' (partisan pike).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrtɪzən / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtɪzæn / (BrE)
An ardent and enthusiastic supporter of a person, group, or cause, often showing a biased allegiance.
enthusiastic, biased supporter

Examples

She was a devoted partizan of the artist's work, defending it at every opportunity.
In political debates, partizans often overlook the flaws of their chosen party.
The historian tried to avoid being seen as a partizan in the controversy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partisan - an ardent and enthusiastic supporter of a person or cause

Etymology

Alternative spelling of 'partisan', from French 'partisan' and Italian 'partigiano' (member of a faction).
