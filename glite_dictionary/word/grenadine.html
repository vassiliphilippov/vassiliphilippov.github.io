grenadine

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡrɛn.əˈdiːn / (AmE), / ˌɡrɛn.əˈdiːn / (BrE)
a sweet, red syrup made from pomegranate juice or artificial flavoring, used in cocktails and other drinks
red syrup used in drinks

Examples

Add a splash of grenadine to the cocktail for a burst of color and sweetness.
She ordered a Shirley Temple, which is made with ginger ale and grenadine.
The bartender mixed vodka, orange juice, and grenadine to create a vibrant sunrise effect.

Etymology

From French *grenadine*, from *grenade* (pomegranate).
