INS

1. NOUN

/ aɪ ɛn ɛs / (AmE), / aɪ ɛn ɛs / (BrE)
a former agency of the U.S. Department of Justice responsible for enforcing immigration and naturalization laws
former U.S. immigration authority
american

Examples

After 2003, the INS was replaced by the Department of Homeland Security's immigration agencies.
She worked for the INS as an immigration officer for ten years.
The INS handled all applications for citizenship before it was disbanded.

Etymology

Acronym of Immigration and Naturalization Service.
