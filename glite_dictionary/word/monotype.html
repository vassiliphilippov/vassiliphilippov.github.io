monotype

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.nə.taɪp / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.ə.taɪp / (BrE)
(biology) A taxonomic group containing only one member, such as a genus with a single species.
taxonomic group with one member

Examples

The bird genus Apteryx is a monotype because it contains only one living species.
In some cases, a monotype can provide important insight into evolutionary history.
Many rare plants are classified as a monotype within their genus.

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) + 'typos' (type, form).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.nəˌtaɪp / (AmE), / ˈmɒ.nə.taɪp / (BrE)
A single print made by pressing paper against a painted or inked glass or metal plate, producing a unique image.
single print from painted plate

Examples

The artist created a monotype by pressing paper onto an inked sheet of glass.
Each monotype is unique and cannot be exactly reproduced.
She exhibited several monotypes at the gallery, each with vivid colors and distinct textures.

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) + 'typos' (impression).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.nəˌtaɪp / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.əˌtaɪp / (BrE)
a typesetting machine operated by a keyboard that casts and sets separate characters.
typesetting machine for separate characters

Examples

The newspaper adopted a monotype machine to speed up its printing process.
Skilled operators were required to run the old monotype typesetting equipment efficiently.
After years of use, the monotype in the print shop was finally retired.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: linotype - a typesetting machine that casts lines of type as single pieces

Etymology

From the trade name ‘Monotype’, based on Greek ‘monos’ (single) and ‘typos’ (type).
