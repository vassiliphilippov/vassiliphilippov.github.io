denuded

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈnudɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈnjuːdɪd / (BrE)
lacking the natural or usual covering, especially of vegetation or surface layer.
without natural or usual covering

Examples

After the storm, the hillside was denuded of trees and shrubs.
The denuded branches revealed a bird's nest hidden inside the tree.
Centuries of farming have left the land denuded and prone to erosion.

Synset Examples

a bald spot on the lawn
bare hills

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bare - without covering or vegetation
  • Synonym: stripped - having had covering or contents removed
  • Antonym: clothed - covered or provided with clothing or a covering

Etymology

From Latin denudare (to make naked), from de- (removal) + nudus (naked).
