elongate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈlɔŋɡeɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɒŋɡeɪt / (BrE)
to make something longer, especially by stretching or pulling it
make or become longer by stretching

Examples

The muscles will elongate when stretched regularly during exercise.
Scientists use a device to elongate metal wires for testing.
If you pull the dough too hard, it will elongate into a thin strand.

Synset Examples

stretch the fabric

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stretch - to make something longer or wider without tearing it
  • Antonym: to shorten - to make or become shorter

Etymology

From Latin 'elongare' meaning 'to lengthen', from 'e-' (out) + 'longus' (long).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈlɔŋˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɒŋˌɡeɪt / (BrE)
having a shape that is much longer than it is wide; long and slender
long and slender in shape

Examples

The snake's elongate body allowed it to slip easily through narrow gaps.
Some fish have an elongate shape to help them move swiftly in water.
The leaves of this species are noticeably elongate and narrow.

Synset Examples

an elongate tail tapering to a point
the old man's gaunt and elongated frame

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long - having great length; measuring a large distance from end to end
  • Antonym: short - having little length or not far from one end to the other

Etymology

From Latin *elongatus*, past participle of *elongare* (to lengthen, make longer).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01436882-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ɪˈlɔŋˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɒŋ.ɡeɪt / (BrE)
having a shape that is long and narrow, especially referring to leaves or other objects.
long and narrow in shape

Examples

The willow tree is known for its elongate leaves that sway in the wind.
Many fish species have an elongate body, helping them move swiftly through the water.
The cactus had several elongate stems growing straight upwards.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: oval - egg-shaped or broadly elliptical shape
  • Antonym: broad - having a wide shape rather than long and narrow

Etymology

From Latin elongare (to lengthen), from e- (out) + longus (long).
