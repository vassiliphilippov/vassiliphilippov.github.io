konini

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈniːni / (AmE), / kɒˈniːni / (BrE)
An erect deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand, growing up to 10 feet tall and bearing maroon flowers.
New Zealand shrub or tree with maroon flowers

Examples

The konini attracts native birds with its sweet flowers and fruit.
Botanists studied the unique growth patterns of the konini in the wild.
Local communities sometimes use wood from the konini to craft traditional tools.

Etymology

From Maori konini, the native name for this plant.
