to immortalize

1. VERB

/ ɪˈmɔːr.t̬ə.laɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈmɔː.tə.laɪz / (BrE)
to make someone or something famous and likely to be remembered forever
make famous forever

Examples

The artist's sculpture will immortalize the hero for generations to come.
Authors often hope their words will immortalize their ideas long after they are gone.
The groundbreaking invention immortalized her name in the history of science.

Synset Examples

This melody immortalized its composer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commemorate - to honor the memory of someone or something
  • Antonym: to forget - fail to remember; to cease to keep in memory

Etymology

From French 'immortaliser', from Latin 'immortalis' (undying).

2. VERB

/ ɪˈmɔrtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈmɔːtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to honor or preserve the memory of a person or event, often through a lasting tribute such as a monument, work of art, or ceremony
preserve in memory; commemorate

Examples

The foundation built a statue to immortalize the achievements of the city's founder.
Her poetry sought to immortalize the struggles of everyday people.
Historians immortalized the event in textbooks so future generations would remember it.

Synset Examples

This sculpture commemorates the victims of the concentration camps
We memorialized the Dead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commemorate - to honor the memory of someone or something
  • Antonym: to forget - cease to remember; fail to recall

Etymology

From Latin 'immortalis' (immortal) + '-ize', meaning to make immortal.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
