orthophosphate

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔrθoʊˈfɑsˌfeɪt / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəʊˈfɒs.feɪt / (BrE)
a salt or anion of phosphoric acid, especially one containing a single phosphate group (PO4^3−)
salt or anion of phosphoric acid
medical

Examples

The laboratory results showed elevated orthophosphate concentrations in the water sample.
Some fertilizers contain orthophosphate as a readily available source of phosphorus for plants.
The reaction produces calcium orthophosphate, which is used in making ceramics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phosphate - a salt or ester of phosphoric acid

Etymology

From Greek orthos (straight, correct) + phosphate, indicating the simplest phosphate ion.
