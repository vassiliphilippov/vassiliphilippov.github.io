trichopteron

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈkɑptərɒn / (AmE), / trɪˈkɒptərɒn / (BrE)
A caddis fly, an insect of the order Trichoptera, characterized by aquatic larvae and adults with hairy wings.
caddis fly; insect of the order Trichoptera

Examples

The larvae of the trichopteron build protective cases from sand and plant debris.
A trichopteron adult can often be found near freshwater streams and ponds.
Researchers studied the life cycle of the trichopteron to better understand aquatic ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caddis fly - common name for insects of the order Trichoptera

Etymology

From Greek trikhos (hair) + pteron (wing), referring to the hairy wings of these insects.
