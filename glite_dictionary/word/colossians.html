Colossians

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɑːʃənz / (AmE), / kəˈlɒʃənz / (BrE)
a book of the New Testament consisting of an epistle from Saint Paul to the Christians in Colossae, an ancient city in Phrygia
a New Testament epistle by Paul to the Colossians

Examples

Many scholars believe that Colossians was written while Paul was imprisoned.
Our Bible study group will discuss Colossians next week.
The message of Colossians emphasizes the supremacy of Christ.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ephesians - another Pauline epistle in the New Testament
  • Synonym: Philippians - New Testament epistle addressed to the people of Philippi
  • Synonym: Thessalonians - New Testament letters addressed to the Thessalonians

Etymology

From Latin 'Colossenses', from Greek 'Kolossaeis' (inhabitants of Colossae)
