clear-cut

1. ADJ

/ ˈklɪr ˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈklɪə ˌkʌt / (BrE)
(of land) having had all trees removed at one time, especially as part of logging or forestry operations.
of land: all trees removed

Examples

The clear-cut forest left a barren landscape vulnerable to erosion.
After the hillside was clear-cut, wildlife habitats were dramatically reduced.
Many environmentalists oppose clear-cut practices due to their environmental impact.

Synset Examples

clear-cut hillsides are subject to erosion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deforested - of land: deprived of forest or trees
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01293151-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈklɪr ˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˌklɪə ˈkʌt / (BrE)
definite and easy to perceive, recognize, or understand; sharply defined or distinct
sharply defined; easily recognizable

Examples

The committee reached a clear-cut decision on the matter.
It was a clear-cut victory for the home team in last night's game.
There is no clear-cut answer to this complex problem.

Synset Examples

clear-cut evidence of tampering
Claudius was the first to invade Britain with distinct … intentions of conquest
trenchant distinctions between right and wrong

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distinct - clearly different or separate; easy to recognize
  • Antonym: ambiguous - open to more than one interpretation; not having one clear meaning

Etymology

From the past participle of 'clear-cut', originally referring to land cleared by cutting, extended figuratively to mean sharply defined.

3. VERB

/ ˈklɪrˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈklɪəˌkʌt / (BrE)
to remove all the trees from an area of land at one time, especially as part of logging operations.
remove all trees from an area at once

Examples

The company plans to clear-cut 200 acres of forest this year.
Environmentalists are protesting the decision to clear-cut the entire valley.
After they clear-cut the land, the landscape changed dramatically.

Synset Examples

clear-cut an acre of forest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deforest - to clear trees from an area of land

Etymology

From 'clear' + 'cut', referring to making a clean sweep through an area of forest.

4. ADJ

/ ˈklɪrˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈklɪəˌkʌt / (BrE)
easily seen, recognized, or distinguished; clear and definite.
clearly defined; easy to perceive

Examples

There was a clear-cut decision after all the evidence was presented.
The border between the two colors was clear-cut and unmistakable.
Her instructions were clear-cut, leaving no room for confusion.

Synset Examples

as clear as a whistle
clear footprints in the snow
the letter brought back a clear image of his grandfather
a spire clean-cut against the sky
a clear-cut pattern

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distinct - clearly different or easily distinguishable
  • Antonym: vague - not clearly defined or easy to perceive

Etymology

Formed in English from 'clear' + 'cut' (originally in reference to trees cleared by cutting, then applied metaphorically).
