vitellus

1. NOUN

/ vəˈtɛləs / (AmE), / vɪˈtɛləs / (BrE)
The yolk of an egg, consisting of nutritive material stored to nourish an embryo, especially in birds and reptiles.
yolk of an egg (biological term)
medical

Examples

The vitellus provides essential nutrients for the developing embryo within the egg.
Researchers studied how the vitellus changes during the incubation process.
In bird eggs, the vitellus is easily visible as the large yellow portion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yolk - the yellow internal part of a bird's egg, rich in protein and fat

Etymology

From Latin 'vitellus' meaning 'yolk; little life,' diminutive of 'vitulus' (calf).
