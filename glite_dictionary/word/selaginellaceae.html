Selaginellaceae

1. NOUN

/ sɛləˌdʒɪnəˈleɪsiː / (AmE), / sɛləˌdʒɪnɪˈleɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of chiefly tropical, terrestrial plants known as lesser club mosses, resembling true mosses.
family of tropical moss-like plants

Examples

The Selaginellaceae are often found in shaded, humid areas of tropical forests.
Unlike true mosses, plants in the Selaginellaceae possess vascular tissue.
Botanists have identified over 700 species within the Selaginellaceae family.

Etymology

From the genus name Selaginella (type genus), which is derived from Latin, combined with the standard botanical suffix -aceae for plant families.

2. NOUN

/ səˌlædʒəˌnɛləˈsiː.iˌaɪ / (AmE), / sɛˌlædʒɪˌnɛlɪˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly tropical, terrestrial plants resembling mosses, commonly known as lesser club mosses.
family of moss-like tropical plants

Examples

The Selaginellaceae are easily recognized by their moss-like appearance.
Several members of the Selaginellaceae family thrive in humid tropical forests.
Botanists study Selaginellaceae to understand the evolution of vascular plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clubmoss - any plant of the family Lycopodiaceae or Selaginellaceae, resembling mosses but more complex

Etymology

From the genus name Selaginella, itself from Latin.
