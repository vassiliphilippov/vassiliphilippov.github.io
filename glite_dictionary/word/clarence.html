clarence

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɛr.əns / (AmE), / ˈklɑː.rəns / (BrE)
a closed, four-wheeled horse-drawn carriage with seats for four passengers
type of horse-drawn carriage

Examples

The wealthy merchant arrived in his clarence, attracting the attention of passersby.
Historical novels often describe characters riding in a clarence through city streets.
The museum displayed a restored clarence among its collection of vintage carriages.

Etymology

Named after Prince William, Duke of Clarence (later King William IV of the United Kingdom).
