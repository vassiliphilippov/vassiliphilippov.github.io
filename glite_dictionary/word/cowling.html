cowling

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊlɪŋ / (BrE)
a removable metal covering that protects and streamlines an engine, especially on an aircraft
engine's protective metal covering

Examples

The technician lifted the cowling to inspect the turbine blades for damage.
Before takeoff, the pilot checked that the cowling was securely fastened.
Replacing a damaged cowling can improve the aircraft's aerodynamics.

Synset Examples

there are powerful engines under the hoods of new cars
the mechanic removed the cowling in order to repair the plane's engine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowl - a hood-shaped covering for a vehicle or engine

Etymology

From 'cowl' (a hood, covering) + '-ing'.
