Canavalia gladiata

1. NOUN

/ ˌkænəˈvæliə ɡlædˈaɪtə / (AmE), / ˌkænəˈvæliə ˌɡlædɪˈɑːtə / (BrE)
A tropical climbing plant of the legume family, native to the Old World, bearing long pods with red or brown beans and cultivated for food in parts of Asia.
tropical bean plant; cultivated food legume

Examples

Canavalia gladiata is often grown as a vegetable crop in tropical regions.
The long pods of Canavalia gladiata are used in various Asian cuisines.
Researchers study Canavalia gladiata for its potential in sustainable agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sword bean - common name for Canavalia gladiata

Etymology

From Latin; genus Canavalia, species name gladiata from Latin 'gladius' (sword), referring to the shape of the pod.
