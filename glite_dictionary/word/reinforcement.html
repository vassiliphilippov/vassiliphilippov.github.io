reinforcement

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːɪnˈfɔːrsmənt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈfɔːsmənt / (BrE)
a device or material used to provide additional strength to something
device or material for added strength

Examples

The engineer inserted a metal reinforcement to improve the bridge's stability.
He placed small plastic reinforcements on the holes to keep the binder pages from tearing.
The wall needed extra reinforcement to support the heavy shelves.

Synset Examples

the cardboard backing was just a strengthener
he used gummed reinforcements to hold the page in his notebook

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strengthener - something that makes another object stronger

Etymology

From reinforce + -ment, ultimately from Old French 'reinforcer' (to strengthen) and Latin 'fortis' (strong).

2. NOUN

/ ˌriːɪnˈfɔːrsmənt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈfɔːsmənt / (BrE)
the process of encouraging or establishing a pattern of behavior by offering rewards or other incentives.
process of encouraging behavior with rewards

Examples

The teacher used reinforcement to encourage students to complete their homework on time.
Positive reinforcement is an effective strategy for shaping a child's behavior.
Managers often rely on reinforcement to motivate employees to achieve their goals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: punishment - the act of inflicting suffering for undesirable behavior

Etymology

From reinforce + -ment; ultimately from Old French renforcer, from Latin re- (again) + in- (in) + fortis (strong).

5. NOUN

/ ˌriːɪnˈfɔrsmənt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈfɔːsmənt / (BrE)
(psychology) A stimulus or event that strengthens or weakens the behavior it follows, increasing or decreasing the likelihood of that behavior occurring again.
stimulus that modifies behavior (psychology)
medical

Examples

The teacher used praise as a form of reinforcement to encourage good behavior in class.
Food can serve as a powerful reinforcement when training animals to perform specific actions.
In behavioral therapy, positive reinforcement is often used to promote healthy habits.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: punishment - a stimulus that decreases the likelihood of a behavior (psychology)
  • Synonym: reward - something given in recognition of service, effort, or achievement; positive reinforcement
  • Degree Higher: positive reinforcement - presentation of a pleasant stimulus to increase behavior
  • Degree Lower: negative reinforcement - removal of an unpleasant stimulus to increase behavior

Etymology

From reinforce + -ment; reinforce from Old French 'reinforcer', from Medieval Latin 'reinfortiare'.
