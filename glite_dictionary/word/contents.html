contents

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.tɛnts / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.tɛnts / (BrE)
a list at the beginning of a book or document showing its chapters or sections and the page numbers where they start
list of chapters or sections in a book

Examples

You can find the chapter title in the contents near the front of the book.
She always checks the contents first to plan her reading.
The contents listed all the topics covered in the manual.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: table of contents - a table listing the chapters or sections of a book with page numbers
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06501650-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.tɛnts / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.tɛnts / (BrE)
the things that are inside something, such as a container, book, or space
things inside a container or collection

Examples

She poured the contents of the box onto the table and began sorting through them.
Please list all the contents of your luggage for inspection.
The jar was labeled, but the contents looked unfamiliar.

Synset Examples

he emptied the contents of his pockets
the two groups were similar in content

Related Terms

  • Synonym: content - the things that are inside something or included in something

Etymology

From Latin 'contentum', past participle of 'continere' (to contain).
