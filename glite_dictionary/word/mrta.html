MRTA

1. NOUN

/ ɛm ɑr ti eɪ / (AmE), / ɛm ɑː tiː eɪ / (BrE)
a Marxist-Leninist guerrilla group in Peru, active mainly in the 1980s and 1990s, known for its efforts to overthrow the Peruvian government.
Peruvian Marxist guerrilla group (1980s–1990s)

Examples

The Peruvian government launched a major offensive against the MRTA in the early 1990s.
In 1996, the MRTA made global headlines by taking hundreds of hostages at the Japanese ambassador's residence.
Links between the MRTA and other guerrilla groups in South America have been documented.

Etymology

Spanish initials for Movimiento Revolucionario Túpac Amaru, named after the 18th-century Peruvian revolutionary Túpac Amaru II.
