impudence

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪmpjədəns / (AmE), / ˈɪmpjʊdəns / (BrE)
the quality of being rude and not showing respect for others, or an impudent statement or act
rude behavior or lack of respect

Examples

His impudence during the meeting surprised everyone in the room.
She had the impudence to question the teacher's authority.
The boss was taken aback by the impudence of his employee’s response.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insolence - rude and disrespectful behavior
  • Synonym: impertinence - lack of respect; rudeness
  • Antonym: respect - polite regard for others

Etymology

From Latin impudentia, from impudens (shameless, without decency).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪmpjədəns / (AmE), / ˈɪmpjʊdəns / (BrE)
rude or disrespectful behavior; lack of modesty or shamelessness in speech or conduct.
rude or disrespectful behavior

Examples

Her impudence during the meeting shocked everyone present.
He had the impudence to interrupt the speaker several times.
The teacher would not tolerate such impudence from her students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impertinence - lack of respect; rudeness
  • Antonym: respect - politeness or esteem for others

Etymology

From Latin impudentia (shamelessness), from impudēns (shameless).
