to supervise

1. VERB

/ ˈsuː.pɚˌvaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsuː.pəˌvaɪz / (BrE)
to watch a person or activity to ensure everything is done correctly, safely, or as intended
to oversee or watch over

Examples

A teacher must supervise students during exams to prevent cheating.
Parents often supervise their children while they play in the park.
He was hired to supervise the construction workers on the site.

Synset Examples

we are monitoring the air quality
the police monitor the suspect's moves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to oversee - to supervise a person or activity
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to provide necessary care or attention

Etymology

From Latin supervidere, from super- (over) + videre (to see).

WordNet ID: oewn-02167732-v

2. VERB

/ ˈsuː.pɚ.vaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsuː.pə.vaɪz / (BrE)
to watch and direct the work or activities of others to ensure tasks are carried out correctly
watch and direct activities

Examples

She was hired to supervise the new interns during their training period.
The teacher must supervise students during the exam to prevent cheating.
He prefers to supervise projects personally to ensure everything runs smoothly.

Synset Examples

Who is overseeing this project?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to oversee - to watch over and direct; supervise
  • Synonym: to manage - to be in charge of; administer

Etymology

From Latin 'supervidere' meaning 'oversee', from 'super-' (over) + 'videre' (to see).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02447914-v
Synset POS: v
