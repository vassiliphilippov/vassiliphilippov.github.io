marocain

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːrəˈkeɪn / (AmE), / ˌmærəˈkeɪn / (BrE)
A type of crepe fabric with a ribbed texture, typically used in dresses and other garments.
ribbed crepe fabric for dresses

Examples

She chose a beautiful evening gown made of rich marocain for the gala.
The designer’s new collection features several pieces crafted from navy blue marocain.
You can feel the luxurious drape of marocain fabric in this dress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crepe - a light, thin fabric with a wrinkled surface

Etymology

From French 'marocain', meaning 'Moroccan', referring to the fabric's origin or style.
