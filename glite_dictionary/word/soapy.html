soapy

1. ADJ

/ ˈsoʊpi / (AmE), / ˈsəʊpi / (BrE)
(of a person's manner or speech) unpleasantly smooth, suave, or ingratiating, often in a way that is insincere or excessive.
excessively ingratiating or smooth in manner

Examples

His soapy compliments made everyone at the meeting uncomfortable.
I don't trust politicians who give soapy speeches to gain favor with the public.
The waiter's soapy charm seemed insincere and over the top.

Synset Examples

buttery praise
gave him a fulsome introduction
an oily sycophantic press agent
oleaginous hypocrisy
smarmy self-importance
the unctuous Uriah Heep
soapy compliments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unctuous - excessively flattering or ingratiating; oily
  • Synonym: smarmy - excessively flattering in an insincere way
  • Antonym: genuine - honest and sincere in manner or speech

2. ADJ

/ ˈsoʊpi / (AmE), / ˈsəʊpi / (BrE)
having the texture, appearance, or qualities of soap; slippery, foamy, or lathered like soap
having qualities of soap

Examples

After washing with the new shampoo, her hair felt soapy and slick.
The river became soapy after the detergent spill, turning the water white with suds.
He wiped his hands on a towel because they were still soapy from the dishes.

Synset Examples

a soapy consistency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slippery - difficult to hold or stand on because of a smooth surface
  • Synonym: greasy - having a smooth, oily surface or texture
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
