Campephilus

1. NOUN

/ kæmˈpɛfɪləs / (AmE), / kamˈpɛfɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of large woodpeckers in the family Picidae, including the ivory-billed woodpecker and related species found in the Americas.
genus of large woodpeckers

Examples

The genus Campephilus includes some of the largest and most striking woodpeckers in the world.
Researchers are still searching for evidence that the ivory-billed woodpecker, a species in Campephilus, survives in the wild.
Many ornithologists study the distribution and behavior of Campephilus across Central and South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodpecker - a bird known for pecking wood to find insects

Etymology

From Greek 'kampe' (caterpillar) + 'philos' (loving), referring to the birds' feeding habits.

/ kæmˈfiːləs / (AmE), / kamˈfiːləs / (BrE)

genus of large American woodpeckers

Examples

The ivory-billed woodpecker belongs to the genus Campephilus, which includes several other large woodpeckers.
Researchers discovered a new species within Campephilus in South America.
Campephilus is distinguished by its strong bill and striking plumage.

From Greek *kampe* (caterpillar) + *philos* (loving), referring to the bird's diet.
