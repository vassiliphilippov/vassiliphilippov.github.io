Ribes sativum

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪbiːz səˈtiːvəm / (AmE), / ˈraɪbiːz səˈtɪvəm / (BrE)
A species of garden currant that bears small white berries, commonly cultivated for its edible fruit.
garden currant with small white berries

Examples

The Ribes sativum in our garden produced a generous crop of white berries this year.
Jam made from Ribes sativum is delicately sweet and less tart than that from red currants.
She planted several Ribes sativum bushes along the fence for their attractive fruit and flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white currant - A type of currant fruit that is white in color and less tart than red currant
  • Degree Higher: Ribes rubrum - A related species known as the red currant

Etymology

From Latin: 'Ribes' (currant genus) and 'sativum' (cultivated).
