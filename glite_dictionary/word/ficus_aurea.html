Ficus aurea

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪkəs ɔːˈriːə / (AmE), / ˈfaɪkəs ɔːˈriːə / (BrE)
A species of strangler fig native to southern Florida, the West Indies, and Central America, known for growing as an epiphyte and producing thick aerial roots that eventually cover large areas.
species of strangler fig native to Florida and the West Indies

Examples

The canopy of the old forest was dominated by a giant Ficus aurea whose roots snaked across the ground.
Researchers studied how the Ficus aurea interacts with local wildlife in southern Florida.
Many animals find both food and shelter among the dense roots of the Ficus aurea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strangler fig - a type of fig tree that grows around and eventually overtakes host trees

Etymology

From Latin: 'ficus' meaning 'fig' and 'aurea' meaning 'golden'.
