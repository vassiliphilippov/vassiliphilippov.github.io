Holothuroidea

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˌlɒθjʊrɔɪˈiːdə / (AmE), / hɒlˌɒθjʊərəˈɪdiə / (BrE)
A class of echinoderms that includes the sea cucumbers, characterized by an elongated body and leathery skin.
class of echinoderms (sea cucumbers)

Examples

The Holothuroidea are remarkable for their ability to regenerate lost body parts.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Holothuroidea while exploring the deep sea.
Most Holothuroidea are found on the ocean floor, where they play an important role in the ecosystem.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'holothourion' (sea cucumber) + '-oidea' (resembling).
