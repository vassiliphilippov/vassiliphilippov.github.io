pesthole

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛstˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈpɛstˌhəʊl / (BrE)
a place where infectious diseases are likely to spread quickly; a breeding ground for epidemic disease.
place where disease spreads

Examples

During the outbreak, the overcrowded tenement became a pesthole filled with suffering residents.
The soldiers feared the camp would turn into a pesthole if conditions did not improve.
In the past, many cities were considered pestholes due to poor sanitation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hotbed - place where something, especially something undesirable, develops quickly

Etymology

pest (Latin pestis, plague) + hole (Old English hol, hollow place).
