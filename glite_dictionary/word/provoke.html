to provoke

1. VERB

/ prəˈvoʊk / (AmE), / prəˈvəʊk / (BrE)
to continually annoy or irritate someone, especially in order to get a reaction
annoy continually; irritate

Examples

Please don't provoke your sister while she's doing her homework.
His constant complaints provoked the team until they finally confronted him.
They accused her of provoking arguments during every meeting.

Synset Examples

He is known to harry his staff when he is overworked
This man harasses his female co-workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to annoy - to make someone a little angry or irritated
  • Synonym: to harass - to bother or trouble someone persistently
  • Antonym: to appease - to calm someone, especially after being provoked

Etymology

From Latin provocare (to call forth, challenge), from pro- (forth) + vocare (to call).

2. VERB

/ prəˈvoʊk / (AmE), / prəˈvəʊk / (BrE)
to cause a particular reaction, feeling, or condition by triggering it; to provide the necessary stimulus for something to happen
cause a reaction or response

Examples

The new study may provoke a change in public policy.
Exposure to bright light can provoke the growth of certain plants.
The announcement was intended to provoke discussion among community members.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trigger - to cause something to start or happen suddenly
  • Synonym: prompt - to cause someone to take a course of action
  • Antonym: to inhibit - to hinder or prevent an occurrence or process

Etymology

From Latin provocare (to call forth, challenge), from pro- (forth) + vocare (to call).

/ prəˈvoʊk / (AmE), / prəˈvəʊk / (BrE)

Examples

4. VERB

/ prəˈvoʊk / (AmE), / prəˈvəʊk / (BrE)
to cause a particular reaction or feeling, especially by stimulating, inciting, or arousing it
cause or incite a reaction

Examples

Her harsh words provoked an angry response from the audience.
The smell of smoke provoked memories of summer camp in his mind.
Nothing he said could provoke her into losing her composure.

Synset Examples

Her behavior provoked a quarrel between the couple

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to incite - to encourage or stir up a particular action or feeling
  • Synonym: to evoke - to bring a feeling, memory, or image into the mind
  • Antonym: calm - to make someone or something quiet or less agitated

Etymology

From Latin provocare (to call forth, challenge), from pro- (forth) + vocare (to call).
