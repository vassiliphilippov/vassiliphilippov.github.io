investigative

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈvɛstəˌɡeɪtɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛstɪɡətɪv / (BrE)
intended to discover information or uncover facts, especially through detailed examination or research.
intended to find information

Examples

The newspaper has a dedicated team for investigative journalism.
She played a key role in several investigative committees during her career.
The investigative report uncovered evidence of corruption within the company.

Synset Examples

a fact-finding committee
investigative reporting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fact-finding - designed to discover facts or information
  • Synonym: exploratory - intended to investigate or examine

Etymology

From 'investigate' plus the suffix '-ive', from Latin 'investigare' (to track, trace).
