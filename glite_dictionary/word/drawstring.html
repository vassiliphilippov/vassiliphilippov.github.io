drawstring

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɔˌstrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrɔːˌstrɪŋ / (BrE)
a cord threaded through a seam or hem, used to gather and secure the opening of a bag or garment
cord for tightening a bag or garment

Examples

She pulled the drawstring to close her purse securely.
The hoodie's drawstring became tangled in the laundry.
Many gym bags have a drawstring closure for convenience.

Synset Examples

he pulled the drawstring and closed the bag

Etymology

From 'draw' + 'string', referring to a string used to draw or tighten an opening.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdrɔˌstrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrɔː.strɪŋ / (BrE)
a cord threaded through a seam or hem, used to tighten or close an opening in clothing or bags
cord for tightening or closing an opening

Examples

He tightened the hood of his jacket by pulling the drawstring.
The bag closed securely with a drawstring at the top.
She replaced the broken drawstring in her sweatpants.

Synset Examples

he pulled the drawstring and closed the bag

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cord - a thin, flexible length of twisted or woven material

Etymology

From 'draw' + 'string', indicating a string that is drawn to close or tighten.
