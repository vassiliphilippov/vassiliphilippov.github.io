multi-coloured

1. ADJ

/ ˌmʌltiˈkʌlɚd / (AmE), / ˌmʌltiˈkʌləd / (BrE)
Having sections or patches in two or more different, often bright, colours.
having many different colours
british

Examples

The artist wore a multi-coloured scarf during the festival.
Children love multi-coloured balloons at birthday parties.
The garden was full of multi-coloured flowers in early spring.

Synset Examples

a jester dressed in motley
the painted desert
a particolored dress
a piebald horse
pied daisies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multicolored - having several colors; U.S. spelling
  • Antonym: monochrome - having or consisting of only one color

Etymology

From Latin 'multi-' (many) + 'coloured', from Old French 'colur' (colour).
