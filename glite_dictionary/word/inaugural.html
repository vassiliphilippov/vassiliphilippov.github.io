inaugural

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɔɡjərəl / (AmE), / ɪˈnɔːɡjərəl / (BrE)
a formal speech given at the beginning of an important event or ceremony, especially by a newly appointed leader, such as a president
formal speech at the start of an event, especially by a new president

Examples

The new president gave an inspiring inaugural that resonated with millions of people.
People around the world tuned in to watch the televised inaugural.
Her inaugural focused on themes of unity and hope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inaugural address - the formal speech given at an inauguration ceremony by someone beginning an official position
  • Antonym: valedictory - a farewell speech, especially one given at the end of a career or event

Etymology

From Latin 'inaugurālis', from 'inaugurare' (to install after taking omens).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈnɔɡjərəl / (AmE), / ɪˈnɔːɡjʊərəl / (BrE)
marking the beginning of an institution, activity, or period; relating to or serving as the first in a series.
marking a beginning; first in a series

Examples

The new president delivered his inaugural address to a large audience.
They published the inaugural issue of the science journal last month.
The company held an inaugural ceremony to launch its latest product line.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: initial - existing or occurring at the beginning
  • Synonym: first - coming before all others in time or order
  • Antonym: final - coming at the end; last

Etymology

From Latin 'inaugurare', meaning to install or consecrate (originally by augury).

3. ADJ

/ ɪˈnɔɡjərəl / (AmE), / ɪˈnɔːɡjʊərəl / (BrE)
relating to or marking the beginning of an official position or event, especially a formal ceremony or speech marking the start of something significant
relating to a beginning ceremony or event

Examples

The president delivered his inaugural speech to a packed audience.
Guests dressed in formal attire for the inaugural ball at the city hall.
Her inaugural lecture as professor of physics attracted students from across the university.

Synset Examples

the President's inaugural address
an inaugural ball

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ceremonial - relating to or used for formal events or ceremonies

Etymology

From Latin 'inauguralis', from 'inaugurare' (to install under good omens).
