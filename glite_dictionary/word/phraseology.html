phraseology

1. NOUN

/ ˌfreɪziˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌfreɪziˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the way in which ideas or statements are expressed in words, especially in terms of style or choice of phrases
manner of verbal expression

Examples

The legal document was full of complex phraseology that confused most readers.
She admired the poetic phraseology of his speech.
Different cultures have their own unique phraseology for expressing common ideas.

Synset Examples

use concise military verbiage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wording - the words used in a particular piece of writing or speech
  • Synonym: diction - the choice and use of words and phrases in speech or writing
  • Synonym: verbiage - the way in which something is expressed; often implies excessive wordiness

Etymology

From Greek phrasis (speech) + -logia (study or collection).
