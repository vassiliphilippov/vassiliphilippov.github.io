Marduk

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɑr.duːk / (AmE), / ˈmɑː.duːk / (BrE)
the chief god of Babylonian mythology, regarded as the patron deity of the city of Babylon
chief god of Babylonian mythology

Examples

Many ancient texts describe Marduk as the creator of the world and the leader of the gods.
During the festival of Akitu, the Babylonians honored Marduk with elaborate rituals.
The myth of Marduk defeating Tiamat symbolizes the victory of order over chaos in Babylonian lore.

Etymology

From Akkadian *Marduk*, name of the deity in Mesopotamian mythology.
