lemon

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛmən / (AmE), / ˈlɛmən / (BrE)
A small evergreen tree (Citrus limon) originating in Asia and widely cultivated for its yellow, acidic fruit.
evergreen tree producing lemons

Examples

The lemon in our backyard produces dozens of fruits every year.
Farmers in Spain grow lemon trees for export worldwide.
The lemon is a hardy plant that thrives in warm climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemon (fruit) - the yellow, acidic fruit of the lemon tree

Etymology

From Arabic *laymūn*, via Old French *limon*.

3. NOUN

/ ˈlɛmən / (AmE), / ˈlɛmən / (BrE)
A yellow, oval citrus fruit with juicy, acidic flesh, commonly used for its juice and zest in food and drinks.
yellow, acidic citrus fruit

Examples

She squeezed a fresh lemon to make lemonade for the party.
The tangy flavor of lemon brightens up many desserts and salad dressings.
A slice of lemon was added to the glass of iced tea.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: lime - a small, green, acidic citrus fruit

Etymology

From Middle English 'lymon,' from Old French 'limon,' from Arabic 'laymūn' or Persian 'limun'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈlɛmən / (AmE), / ˈlɛmən / (BrE)
an item, especially a car, that is defective or unsatisfactory
defective or unsatisfactory item (esp. car)
informal american

Examples

He realized he'd bought a lemon when the car broke down after a week.
The used laptop turned out to be a total lemon.
Consumer laws help protect buyers from accidentally purchasing a lemon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clunker - an old or badly functioning vehicle
  • Synonym: dud - something that fails to work properly

Etymology

Originally from the name of the fruit, later extended in American slang to mean 'something undesirable' in the early 20th century.
