onerousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.nɚ.əs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.nər.əs.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being burdensome, difficult, or unpleasant to deal with.
the quality of being burdensome or difficult

Examples

The onerousness of the new regulations discouraged many small businesses.
He spoke about the onerousness of his responsibilities at work.
They underestimated the onerousness involved in caring for a historic property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burdensomeness - the quality of being heavy or troublesome
  • Antonym: ease - absence of difficulty or effort

Etymology

From Latin 'onerosus' (burdensome).
