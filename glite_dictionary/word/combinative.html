combinative

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑːmbɪˌneɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈkɒmbɪnətɪv / (BrE)
relating to or involving the process of combining or the result of combinations.
relating to combinations

Examples

The combinative properties of the substance allow it to form new compounds.
His research focuses on the combinative aspects of language development.
Successful innovation often requires a combinative approach, blending ideas from different fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combinatorial - relating to or involving combinations, especially in mathematics

Etymology

From combine + -ative, following the pattern of other Latinate adjectives.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɑːmbəˌneɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈkɒmbɪnətɪv / (BrE)
relating to or resulting from the act of combining or combination.
relating to combination

Examples

The researcher proposed a combinative approach to analyze the data.
His success can be attributed to the combinative effects of hard work and talent.
A combinative reaction takes place when two chemicals form a new compound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combinatory - relating to the act of combining; alternative form

Etymology

From Latin combinare, meaning 'to combine', with the English suffix -ive.

Synset POS: a
