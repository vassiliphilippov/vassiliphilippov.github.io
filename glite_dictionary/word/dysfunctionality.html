dysfunctionality

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.fʌŋk.ʃəˈnæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.fʌŋk.ʃəˈnæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The state or condition of functioning abnormally or incorrectly.
state of abnormal or faulty function

Examples

The dysfunctionality of the current system makes effective communication almost impossible.
Many family problems can be traced back to the dysfunctionality present in early childhood environments.
The report highlighted the dysfunctionality of certain departments within the organization.

Synset Examples

The dysfunctionality of most churches and nations emanates from poor parental upbringing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dysfunction - abnormal or impaired functioning
  • Antonym: normality - state of being normal or functioning properly

Etymology

From dysfunction + -ality; 'dysfunction' from Greek 'dus-' (bad, abnormal) and 'function' from Latin 'functio' (performance).
