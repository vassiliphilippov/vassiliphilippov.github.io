Sphacelotheca

1. NOUN

/ sfæsəˌloʊˈθikə / (AmE), / sfæsəˌləʊˈθiːkə / (BrE)
A genus of smut fungi that infects grasses and other plants, causing various plant diseases.
genus of smut fungi

Examples

Sphacelotheca species are known to cause serious diseases in cereal crops worldwide.
The biologist studied the life cycle of Sphacelotheca under a microscope.
Farmers monitor their fields for signs of Sphacelotheca infections to protect their crops.

Etymology

From Greek sphakelos (gangrene) + theca (case), referring to the appearance of the infected plant tissues.

2. NOUN

/ sfæsɛloʊˈθikə / (AmE), / sfæsɪləʊˈθiːkə / (BrE)
a genus of smut fungi belonging to the family Ustilaginaceae, known for parasitizing certain grasses and causing plant diseases.
genus of smut fungi

Examples

The smut fungus in question belongs to the genus Sphacelotheca.
Researchers discovered several new species within Sphacelotheca infecting local grasses.
Symptoms of infection by Sphacelotheca include the development of black spore masses on the host plant.

Etymology

From Greek 'sphakelos' (gangrene, decay) and 'theca' (case, container).
