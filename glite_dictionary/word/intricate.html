intricate

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪntrɪkət / (AmE), / ˈɪntrɪkət / (BrE)
having many small parts or details arranged in a complex or elaborate way; complicated and difficult to understand or produce
elaborately detailed or complex

Examples

The carpet featured an intricate pattern woven with hundreds of tiny knots.
She marveled at the intricate designs on the antique jewelry box.
Solving the puzzle required careful analysis of its intricate mechanisms.

Synset Examples

intricate lacework

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complex - made up of many connected parts; not simple
  • Antonym: simple - easy to understand or not complicated

Etymology

From Latin 'intricatus', meaning entangled or perplexed, from 'intricare' (to entangle).
