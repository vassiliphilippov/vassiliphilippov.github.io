gaoler

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒeɪlər / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪlə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to guard prisoners in a jail
person who guards prisoners; British spelling of 'jailer'
british

Examples

The gaoler checked each cell carefully before his shift ended.
She pleaded with the gaoler to let her see her brother one last time.
In old novels, the gaoler is often portrayed as a harsh and unfeeling character.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jailer - a person who guards prisoners in a jail

Etymology

From Old French *jaiole* (cage, prison), from Medieval Latin *gabiola* (cage).
