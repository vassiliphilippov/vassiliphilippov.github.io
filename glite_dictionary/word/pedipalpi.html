Pedipalpi

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛdɪˈpælpaɪ / (AmE), / ˌpiːdɪˈpalpʌɪ / (BrE)
an order of arachnids comprising the whip scorpions, characterized by elongated pedipalps and a whip-like tail
order of whip scorpions (arachnids)

Examples

The Pedipalpi are distinguished by their large, spiny pedipalps and a segmented whiplike tail.
Many species within the Pedipalpi order are found in tropical and subtropical regions.
Researchers studied the behavior of Pedipalpi in controlled laboratory environments.

Etymology

From Latin 'pedis' (foot) and 'palpus' (feeler).
