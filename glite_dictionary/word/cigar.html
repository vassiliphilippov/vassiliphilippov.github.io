cigar

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈɡɑr / (AmE), / sɪˈɡɑː / (BrE)
A cylindrical roll of tobacco leaves for smoking, typically wrapped in a tobacco leaf or paper containing tobacco.
roll of tobacco for smoking

Examples

He pulled a cigar from his jacket pocket and lit it carefully.
The smell of cigar smoke lingered long after the party ended.
She bought a box of fine Cuban cigars for her father's birthday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cigarette - small roll of finely cut tobacco wrapped in paper for smoking

Etymology

From Spanish 'cigarro', probably from Mayan 'sikar' (to smoke tobacco).
