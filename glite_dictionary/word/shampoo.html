shampoo

1. NOUN

/ ʃæmˈpu / (AmE), / ʃamˈpuː / (BrE)
a liquid or cream cleansing product used for washing hair, typically made with soaps or detergents
cleansing product for washing hair

Examples

He always uses a gentle shampoo to wash his hair every morning.
This shampoo helps prevent dandruff and keeps your scalp healthy.
We bought a new shampoo with a fresh lavender scent.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conditioner - a product used after shampoo to improve hair texture
  • Synonym: soap - a cleansing substance used for washing

Etymology

From Hindi 'chāmpo', imperative of 'chāmpnā' (to press, knead, massage).

WordNet ID: oewn-04190559-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ʃæmˈpu / (AmE), / ʃamˈpuː / (BrE)
the act of washing hair with shampoo
the act of washing hair

Examples

She went to the salon for a relaxing shampoo and haircut.
A weekly shampoo keeps his hair clean and manageable.
The cost of a shampoo and blow-dry is included in the price.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wash - the act of cleaning something

Etymology

From Hindi 'chāmpo', imperative of 'chāmpnā' (to press, knead the muscles), introduced to English in the 18th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00257982-n

3. VERB

/ ʃæmˈpu / (AmE), / ʃamˈpuː / (BrE)
to wash or treat (the hair or scalp) with shampoo
wash or treat hair with shampoo

Examples

After swimming, she always shampoos her hair to remove the chlorine.
The hair stylist shampooed my hair before giving me a cut.
He shampooed and dried his dog's fur after their walk in the rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wash - to clean with water or another liquid

Etymology

From Hindi 'chāmpo', imperative of 'chāmpnā' (to press, knead, massage).
