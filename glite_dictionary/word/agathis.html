Agathis

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡæθɪs / (AmE), / əˈɡæθɪs / (BrE)
A genus of large evergreen coniferous trees in the family Araucariaceae, commonly known as kauri pines, native to Australasia and Southeast Asia.
a genus of coniferous trees (kauri pine)

Examples

The Agathis forests of New Zealand are home to ancient trees towering above the canopy.
Some species of Agathis are valued for their resin, which is used in varnishes and adhesives.
Botanists are studying the growth patterns of Agathis to better understand their longevity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kauri - a large coniferous tree native to New Zealand, known for its valuable timber

Etymology

From Greek agathis (ball of thread or cluster), referring to cone shape.
