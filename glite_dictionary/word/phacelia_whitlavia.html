Phacelia whitlavia

1. NOUN

/ fəˈsiːli.ə wɪtˈleɪ.vi.ə / (AmE), / fəˈsiːli.ə wɪtˈleɪ.vi.ə / (BrE)
a desert plant native to southern California, noted for its blue or violet tubular flowers borne in terminal racemes
desert plant with blue or violet flowers

Examples

The hills were covered in Phacelia whitlavia blooming after the winter rains.
Gardeners value Phacelia whitlavia for its vibrant blue flowers and ability to attract pollinators.
We spotted several specimens of Phacelia whitlavia while hiking in southern California.

Etymology

From genus name Phacelia and species epithet 'whitlavia', named after botanist Thomas J. Whitlave.
