buret

1. NOUN

/ bjʊˈrɛt / (AmE), / bjʊəˈrɛt / (BrE)
A narrow, graduated glass tube with a tap at the bottom, used for carefully measuring and dispensing liquids, especially in titration.
graduated glass tube for measuring liquid (titration)

Examples

The chemist used a buret to add acid drop by drop to the solution.
Rinse the buret thoroughly before beginning the titration to ensure accuracy.
After filling the buret with the standard solution, she carefully noted the initial volume.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipette - a laboratory tool used to transport a measured volume of liquid

Etymology

From French *burette*, meaning a small flask or vial.
