jigsaw

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪɡˌsɔ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪɡsɔː / (BrE)
a portable power saw with a narrow reciprocating blade, used for cutting curves and intricate shapes in wood, metal, or other materials
portable power saw with a reciprocating blade

Examples

He used a jigsaw to cut out the curved pattern in the plywood.
Make sure the blade is sharp before operating the jigsaw.
The jigsaw is ideal for creating detailed shapes and corners in woodwork projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saber saw - another word for a jigsaw, especially in American English

Etymology

From the noun 'jig' (a device holding a piece in place) and 'saw'; originally describing a type of saw making intricate cuts.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪɡˌsɔ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪɡˌsɔː / (BrE)
a fine-toothed power saw with a narrow blade, used for cutting curved lines or shapes in wood, metal, or other materials
fine-toothed power saw for curved cuts

Examples

He used a jigsaw to cut the intricate pattern into the wood.
The carpenter switched on the jigsaw to make curved cuts along the line.
A jigsaw is perfect for creating decorative edges and detailed designs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saber saw - power saw for cutting curves (synonym for jigsaw)

Etymology

From 'jig' (a device with a patterned movement) + 'saw', named for its ability to cut intricate shapes.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
