Acocanthera spectabilis

1. NOUN

/ ˌækoʊˈkænθərə spɛkˈteɪbɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˌækəʊˈkænθərə spɛkˈteɪbɪlɪs / (BrE)
A medium-sized shrubby tree native to South Africa, with thick, leathery evergreen leaves, white or pink flowers, and typically two-seeded, purplish-black globe-shaped fruits.
South African shrubby tree species

Examples

The gardens boasted a rare Acocanthera spectabilis blooming near the entrance, attracting many visitors.
Researchers studied the fruit of Acocanthera spectabilis for its unique chemical compounds.
Local guides pointed out the distinctive leathery leaves of Acocanthera spectabilis on the nature walk.

Etymology

From New Latin: Acocanthera (genus name) + spectabilis (Latin, meaning 'remarkable' or 'notable').
