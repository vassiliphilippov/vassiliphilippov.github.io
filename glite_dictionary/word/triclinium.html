triclinium

1. NOUN

/ trɪˈklɪniəm / (AmE), / trɪˈklɪnɪəm / (BrE)
A formal dining table in ancient Rome, typically surrounded by three couches for reclining diners.
Roman dining table with couches

Examples

The wealthy Romans held elaborate feasts in the triclinium of their homes.
Visitors to the villa were impressed by the marble floors in the triclinium.
Archaeologists discovered a well-preserved triclinium at the ancient site.

Etymology

From Latin 'triclinium', from Greek 'triklinion': tri- (three) + klinē (couch).

2. NOUN

/ trɪˈklɪniəm / (AmE), / trɪˈklɪnɪəm / (BrE)
In ancient Roman times, a formal dining room with a dining table and couches arranged on three sides for reclining while eating.
Roman dining room with couches

Examples

The wealthy Roman family entertained their guests in the beautifully decorated triclinium.
Museum displays often include a reconstructed triclinium to show how ancient Romans dined.
The guide explained that Romans reclined on couches in the triclinium while enjoying elaborate feasts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dining room - A room used for eating meals

Etymology

From Latin 'triclinium', from Greek 'triklinion' (three couches), from 'tri-' (three) + 'klinē' (couch).
