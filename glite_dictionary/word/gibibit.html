gibibit

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪbɪˌbɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡɪbɪˌbɪt / (BrE)
A unit of information equal to 1,024 mebibits, or 2^30 (1,073,741,824) bits, used in computing to measure digital information.
unit of digital information (1,073,741,824 bits)

Examples

The file size was measured in gibibits to ensure accuracy for large data transfers.
A high-speed network might transmit several gibibits per second.
The capacity of the storage device was labeled as 8 gibibits.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: mebibit - unit of information equal to 2^20 (1,048,576) bits
  • Degree Higher: terabit - unit of information equal to 10^12 (1,000,000,000,000) bits
  • Synonym: gigabit - unit of information equal to 10^9 (1,000,000,000) bits

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'gibi-' (giga binary) and 'bit', following the IEC standard for binary multiples.
