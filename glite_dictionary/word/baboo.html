baboo

1. NOUN

/ bəˈbu / (AmE), / bəˈbuː / (BrE)
a Hindi courtesy title used especially during British colonial times, equivalent to 'Mr' or 'sir'; also used to address a person of respect
Hindi courtesy title, like 'Mr'
archaic

Examples

The clerk, a well-dressed baboo, greeted us with a polite bow.
In old correspondence, Indian officials were often addressed as baboo followed by their surname.
During the British Raj, the title baboo was commonly used for educated Indian gentlemen.

Etymology

From Hindi 'bābū' (gentleman, sir).
