hectometre

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛktəˌmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈhɛktəˌmiːtə / (BrE)
a metric unit of length equal to 100 metres
metric unit of length (100 metres)

Examples

The race track was exactly one hectometre in length, making calculations easy.
A hectometre is not commonly used in daily measurements, but it is useful for scientific purposes.
Satellite images can cover several hectometres of terrain in a single scan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meter - the base unit of length in the metric system

Etymology

From French 'hectomètre', from Greek 'hekaton' (hundred) + 'metron' (measure).
