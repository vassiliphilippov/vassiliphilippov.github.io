bismuth

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪzməθ / (AmE), / ˈbɪzməθ / (BrE)
a heavy, brittle, silver-white diamagnetic trivalent metallic element (symbol: Bi), chemically resembling arsenic and antimony, typically found as a by-product in the smelting of lead, copper, tin, silver, and gold ores
chemical element (symbol Bi, atomic number 83)

Examples

The purest form of bismuth can be isolated by reducing its oxide with carbon.
Alloys containing bismuth are used in fire detection and sprinkler systems.
The mining process often yields bismuth as a by-product of refining lead and copper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arsenic - a brittle steel-gray metalloid element (atomic number 33)
  • Synonym: antimony - a brittle silvery-white metalloid element (atomic number 51)

Etymology

From German 'Bismut', variant of earlier 'Wismut', of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɪzməθ / (AmE), / ˈbɪzməθ / (BrE)
a brittle, heavy, silvery-white metallic element, used in alloys and pharmaceuticals, with the chemical symbol Bi
a metallic chemical element (Bi)

Examples

The mineral contains traces of bismuth, which are extracted during processing.
Scientists used bismuth in the alloy because of its low melting point.
Many medications use compounds that include bismuth for treating stomach problems.

Etymology

From German Bismut, possibly from Middle High German wissmuth.
