chrysolite

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪsəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkrɪsəlaɪt / (BrE)
a yellow-green or brown variety of olivine used as a gemstone, found in igneous and metamorphic rocks
a yellow-green gemstone variety of olivine

Examples

A ring set with a chrysolite glittered on her finger under the lamplight.
The museum's mineral collection features a rare specimen of chrysolite.
Geologists identified the gemstone as chrysolite due to its distinctive yellow-green color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: olivine - a magnesium iron silicate mineral group

Etymology

From Greek *chrysolithos* (golden stone), from *chrysos* (gold) + *lithos* (stone).
