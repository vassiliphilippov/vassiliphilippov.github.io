Palaquium gutta

1. NOUN

/ pəˌlɑːkwi.əm ˈɡʌtə / (AmE), / pəˌlakwiːəm ˈɡʌtə / (BrE)
a tropical Southeast Asian tree that yields a type of gutta-percha, especially of the varieties known as getah taban merah or getah taban sutra
Southeast Asian tree yielding gutta-percha

Examples

The latex extracted from the Palaquium gutta tree was once widely used in electrical insulation.
The natural habitat of Palaquium gutta includes the lowland rainforests of Southeast Asia.
Botanists studied the Palaquium gutta to understand how gutta-percha is produced.

Etymology

From genus name Palaquium (Malay origin) and species epithet gutta (Malay 'getah', meaning 'gum' or 'sap').
