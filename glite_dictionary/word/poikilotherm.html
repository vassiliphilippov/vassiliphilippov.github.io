poikilotherm

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪkəloʊˌθɜrm / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪkɪl əʊˌθɜːm / (BrE)
An animal whose body temperature changes with the temperature of its environment, typically excluding birds and mammals.
animal with variable body temperature
medical

Examples

Frogs are classic examples of poikilotherms, adjusting their activity depending on the weather.
Unlike mammals, poikilotherms cannot regulate their internal temperatures when it gets cold.
The aquarium featured several species of poikilotherms, including fish and reptiles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ectotherm - animal whose body temperature depends on external sources
  • Antonym: homeotherm - animal that maintains a constant body temperature

Etymology

From Greek 'poikilos' (varied) + 'thermē' (heat).
