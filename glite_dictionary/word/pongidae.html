Pongidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːndʒɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈpɒndʒɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A former family of primates including the great apes such as orangutans, gorillas, and chimpanzees, sometimes also gibbons.
family of great apes (zoological)
medical

Examples

Many older textbooks classify great apes in the family Pongidae rather than Hominidae.
With the advent of molecular genetics, the classification of Pongidae was re-evaluated.
Taxonomic changes have moved chimpanzees from Pongidae to the family Hominidae.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Hominidae - the biological family that now includes all great apes and humans
  • Degree Higher: ape - a member of the primate group including gorillas, chimpanzees, orangutans, and gibbons

Etymology

From modern Latin, from 'Pongo' (genus name for orangutans) + -idae (zoological family suffix).
