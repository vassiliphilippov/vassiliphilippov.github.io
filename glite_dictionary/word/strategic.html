strategic

1. ADJ

/ strəˈtidʒɪk / (AmE), / strəˈtiːdʒɪk / (BrE)
relating to the identification of long-term aims and interests and the means of achieving them; concerned with or designed as part of a plan to achieve a particular goal.
relating to strategy or planning

Examples

The company made several strategic acquisitions to expand its market share.
The location of the new factory is strategic for reaching international customers.
She took a strategic approach to solving the complex problem.

Synset Examples

strategic weapon
the islands are of strategic importance
strategic considerations

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tactical - relating to actions planned to achieve a specific short-term aim

Etymology

From Greek strategikos (of a general), from strategia (generalship).

WordNet ID: oewn-02962048-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ strəˈtiːdʒɪk / (AmE), / strəˈtiːdʒɪk / (BrE)
important or essential as part of a plan, especially for achieving success in war, business, or similar situations requiring careful planning.
important for achieving a plan or goal

Examples

The company made a strategic decision to enter the Asian market.
During the game, her strategic moves gave her a clear advantage.
The army carried out a strategic withdrawal to regroup its forces.

Synset Examples

a strategic chess move
strategic withdrawal
strategic bombing missions

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tactical - relating to actions or plans intended to achieve immediate or short-term goals

Etymology

From Greek stratēgikos (of a general), from stratēgos (general, leader of an army).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01282845-s
Synset POS: s
