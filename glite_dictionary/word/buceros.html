Buceros

1. NOUN

/ bjuːˈsɪərɒs / (AmE), / bjuːˈsɪərɒs / (BrE)
A genus of large tropical hornbills native to Asia, serving as the type genus of the family Bucerotidae.
genus of tropical hornbills

Examples

The Buceros genus includes the iconic rhinoceros hornbill and other striking species.
Researchers observed several members of Buceros during their expedition in the rainforest.
The distinctive casque on the beak is a hallmark of birds in the Buceros genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hornbill - a tropical bird with a large curved bill, of the family Bucerotidae

Etymology

From Latin 'buceros', from Greek 'boukerōs' meaning 'ox-horned'.

2. NOUN

/ bjuˈsɪərɒs / (AmE), / bjuˈsɪərɒs / (BrE)
A genus of large hornbills, the type genus of the family Bucerotidae, native to Asia and Africa.
genus of large hornbills

Examples

The Buceros genus includes several impressive species known for their distinctive casques.
Researchers observed the nesting behavior of a Buceros hornbill in the wild.
The rhinoceros hornbill is one of the most famous members of the Buceros genus.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Bucerotidae - family of hornbills in the animal kingdom

Etymology

From Latin *būceros*, from Greek *boukerōs* ('cow-horned').
