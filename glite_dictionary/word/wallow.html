wallow

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːloʊ / (AmE), / ˈwɒləʊ / (BrE)
a muddy or wet place where animals, especially large ones like pigs or buffalo, go to roll about or lie to cool off or for comfort.
muddy area where animals roll

Examples

The buffaloes gathered at the wallow to escape the midday heat.
After the rain, the pigs enjoyed a long afternoon in the muddy wallow.
Wild boars had created a new wallow near the edge of the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wallow - to roll about in mud or water, as animals do

Etymology

Old English *wealwian* (to roll), related to rolling or turning about.
