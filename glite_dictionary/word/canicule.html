canicule

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænɪˌkjuːl / (AmE), / ˈkænɪkjuːl / (BrE)
the hottest period of the year, typically from early July to early September, sometimes marked by inactivity
the hottest part of summer

Examples

The oppressive heat during the canicule made outdoor work nearly impossible.
As the canicule dragged on, the city's streets emptied in the afternoon.
Farmers prepared for the canicule by ensuring their crops were well irrigated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dog days - the hottest period of the year, usually in late July and August

Etymology

From French 'canicule', from Latin 'canicula' (little dog), referring to Sirius, the Dog Star, whose rising marked the hottest part of summer.
