copepod

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊpɪˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈkəʊpɪˌpɒd / (BrE)
A minute marine or freshwater crustacean, typically having six pairs of thoracic limbs; found in plankton or as parasites on fish.
small aquatic crustacean

Examples

Many species of copepod drift in ocean currents as important members of plankton.
A single drop of freshwater may contain dozens of copepods swimming rapidly.
Some copepods attach to fish as parasites, feeding on their mucus and skin.

Etymology

From Greek kōpē (oar) + pous, pod- (foot), referring to their oar-like swimming limbs.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊpɪˌpɒd / (AmE), / ˈkəʊpɪpɒd / (BrE)
a minute aquatic crustacean, typically having six pairs of thoracic limbs, found abundantly as plankton in marine and freshwater environments, with some species being parasitic on fish
small aquatic crustacean; often planktonic

Examples

The researchers studied the abundance of copepods in the coastal waters.
Many fish larvae feed on copepods as an important source of nutrition.
Some copepods attach themselves to fish and live as parasites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crustacean - an animal of the large group containing crabs, shrimp, and related species

Etymology

From Greek 'kōpē' (oar) + 'pous, podos' (foot), referring to the oar-like legs.
