aggravation

1. NOUN

/ ˌæɡrəˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌæɡrəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
A feeling of annoyance or exasperation, often caused by something irritating or troublesome.
feeling of annoyance or exasperation

Examples

The delay at the airport was a major source of aggravation for the travelers.
She tried meditation to cope with the daily aggravation at work.
His constant interruptions were an endless aggravation during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritation - feeling of slight anger or discomfort
  • Antonym: relief - feeling of alleviation from distress or annoyance

Etymology

From Latin 'aggravatio', from 'aggravare' (to make heavier, to increase).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæɡ.rəˈveɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæɡ.rəˈveɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
unfriendly or provoking behavior that causes anger or resentment
provocation that causes anger

Examples

His constant aggravation of his classmates eventually got him sent to the principal's office.
The aggravation she received from her neighbor made her move away.
Repeated aggravation from the team manager pushed the players to protest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provocation - an action or statement that angers or irritates
  • Synonym: irritation - the state of being annoyed or provoked
  • Antonym: assuagement - the act of making something less severe or intense

Etymology

From Latin 'aggravatio', from 'aggravare' (to make heavier).

3. NOUN

/ ˌæɡrəˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌæɡrəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making a problem, situation, or disease worse or more severe
worsening of a problem or disease

Examples

Increased stress led to the aggravation of his chronic headaches.
Poor air quality caused the aggravation of her asthma symptoms.
Ignoring the instructions resulted in the aggravation of the technical issue.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
