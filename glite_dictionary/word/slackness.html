slackness

1. NOUN

/ ˈslæknəs / (AmE), / ˈslæknəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being loose rather than tight or taut.
the state of being loose or not tight

Examples

The slackness of the old rope made it unsafe to climb.
After weeks of use, there was noticeable slackness in the elastic band.
He adjusted the tent to remove the slackness in the guy lines.

Synset Examples

he hadn't counted on the slackness of the rope

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tautness - the quality of being pulled tight
  • Synonym: looseness - the state of being loose

Etymology

From slack + -ness, with 'slack' from Middle English 'slak', meaning loose or not tight.

2. NOUN

/ ˈslæknəs / (AmE), / ˈslæknəs / (BrE)
the quality of being careless, negligent, or lacking in strictness or discipline
carelessness or negligence

Examples

The manager was disappointed by the team's slackness in meeting the project deadlines.
His repeated slackness at work eventually led to his dismissal.
Parents were concerned about the general slackness in the school's supervision of students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negligence - failure to take proper care or attention
  • Antonym: diligence - careful and persistent effort or work

Etymology

From slack + -ness; 'slack' from Old English 'slæc' (loose, careless).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈslæknəs / (AmE), / ˈslaknəs / (BrE)
a lack of vitality, energy, or firmness; the state of being weak or lacking in activity or diligence
lack of energy or firmness

Examples

The team's slackness was evident in the way they moved lethargically across the field.
After hours without food or water, slackness overcame his body and mind.
Prolonged slackness can lead to a decline in both morale and productivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laziness - unwillingness to work or use energy
  • Antonym: vigour - physical strength and good health

Etymology

From slack + -ness; 'slack' from Middle English 'slak', of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
