divorce

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈvɔrs / (AmE), / dɪˈvɔːs / (BrE)
The legal ending of a marriage by a court or competent authority.
legal ending of a marriage

Examples

After years of unhappiness, they finally decided to get a divorce.
The divorce was finalized last month in court.
Children may struggle emotionally when their parents go through a divorce.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: marriage - the legally or formally recognized union of two people
  • Degree Lower: separation - a situation in which a married couple stops living together but remains legally married
  • Synonym: annulment - a legal declaration that a marriage is invalid

Etymology

From Latin divortium (separation, dissolution of marriage), from divertere (to turn different ways).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈvɔrs / (AmE), / dɪˈvɔːs / (BrE)
to separate or disassociate oneself completely from something or someone
to separate or disassociate from

Examples

After he learned of their unethical practices, he decided to divorce himself from the company.
She found it hard to divorce her emotions from her professional decisions.
Many artists try to divorce their personal lives from their public personas.

Synset Examples

She disassociated herself from the organization when she found out the identity of the president

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disassociate - to end a connection or association with someone or something
  • Synonym: separate - to stop being together or joined
  • Antonym: to unite - to join together or combine

Etymology

From Old French 'divorcer', from Latin 'divortium' (separation), from 'divortere' (to turn different ways).

3. VERB

/ dɪˈvɔrs / (AmE), / dɪˈvɔːs / (BrE)
to legally end a marriage with one's spouse; to dissolve a marriage by law
legally end a marriage

Examples

After years of unhappiness, they decided to divorce and go their separate ways.
She finally divorced her husband after trying counseling for several months.
Many couples choose to divorce amicably for the sake of their children.

Synset Examples

The couple divorced after only 6 months

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to marry - to become the husband or wife of someone
  • Degree Lower: separate - to stop living together as a couple, often as a preliminary to divorce

Etymology

From Latin divortium (separation), from divertere (to turn aside).
