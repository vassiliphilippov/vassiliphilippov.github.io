clotbur

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɑtˌbɜr / (AmE), / ˈklɒtˌbɜː / (BrE)
a biennial herb native to temperate Eurasia, with a stout taproot and burr-like seed heads that stick to animals or clothing
Eurasian plant with burrs

Examples

The farmer pulled a clotbur from his dog’s fur after their walk through the field.
Clotbur can be difficult to remove from clothing due to its sticky burs.
Several species of clotbur thrive along the riverbanks in temperate regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burdock - a related plant with similar burrs
