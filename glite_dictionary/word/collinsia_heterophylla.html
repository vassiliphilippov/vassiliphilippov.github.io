Collinsia heterophylla

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪnziə ˌhɛtəroʊˈfɪlə / (AmE), / kɒˈlɪnziə ˌhɛtərəʊˈfɪlə / (BrE)
A species of annual flowering plant native to California, noted for its rings of white, lavender, or pale-blue flowers arranged around its stems, resembling a pagoda.
a California wildflower with ringed pale flowers

Examples

Collinsia heterophylla is often found blooming in shaded woodlands throughout California.
The garden's display of Collinsia heterophylla added vibrant rings of color in the spring.
Botanists admire Collinsia heterophylla for its layered floral arrangement resembling a miniature pagoda.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Collinsia', named after American botanist Zaccheus Collins, and Latin 'heterophylla', meaning 'with different leaves'.
