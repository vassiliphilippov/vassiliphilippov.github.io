remake

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːˌmeɪk / (AmE), / ˈriːˌmeɪk / (BrE)
a new version of something, especially a film or song, that has been created again based on an earlier version
new version of a film, song, etc.

Examples

The studio released a remake of the classic 1950s sci-fi movie.
Fans compared the original soundtrack to its recent remake.
Many critics felt the remake failed to capture the magic of the first film.

Synset Examples

it is a remake of an old film

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adaptation - a film or other work created from a previous source, often a book

Etymology

From re- (again) + make, formed in English in the early 20th century.

2. VERB

/ riˈmeɪk / (AmE), / riˈmeɪk / (BrE)
to make something again or differently, especially so as to improve or renew it
make new or different

Examples

The director plans to remake the film for a modern audience.
She decided to remake her wardrobe after moving to a new city.
He is trying to remake his career after a long break.

Synset Examples

She is remaking her image

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revamp - to give new and improved form, structure, or appearance to something
  • Synonym: to restore - to bring something back to its original condition
  • Antonym: to destroy - to put an end to the existence of something by damaging or attacking it

Etymology

From re- (again) + make.

Synset POS: v
