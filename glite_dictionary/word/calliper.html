calliper

1. VERB

/ ˈkælɪpər / (AmE), / ˈkælɪpə / (BrE)
to measure the diameter or thickness of something using calipers.
measure with calipers

Examples

The technician callipered the pipe to check if it met the specified dimensions.
You should calliper the bone before making any further adjustments during surgery.
She carefully callipered each sample to ensure consistent measurements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caliper - to measure with calipers (US spelling)

Etymology

From Middle French *caliper*, of uncertain origin; originally a noun referring to the measuring instrument.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkælɪpər / (AmE), / ˈkælɪpə / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure the distance between two opposite sides of an object, or the thickness or diameter of an object
instrument for measuring thickness or distance

Examples

The engineer used a pair of callipers to measure the thickness of the metal sheet.
Digital callipers are now commonly used in laboratories for precise measurements.
He checked the diameter of the pipe with his calliper before starting the repair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caliper - alternative spelling, primarily American English, for 'calliper'

Etymology

From French *caliper* or variant of Middle French *calibre* (gauge, measuring instrument).
