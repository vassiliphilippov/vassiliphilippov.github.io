alee

1. ADV

/ əˈli / (AmE), / əˈliː / (BrE)
on or toward the side sheltered from the wind (the lee side), especially in a nautical context.
toward the sheltered side; nautical

Examples

The captain ordered the yacht's helm put alee as the winds shifted rapidly.
When the storm approached, the crew stayed alee of the rigging to avoid the spray.
Sailors moved alee to find shelter from the harsh ocean wind.

Synset Examples

put the helm alee

Related Terms

  • Antonym: windward - toward the side from which the wind is blowing; opposite of 'alee'

Etymology

From 'a-' (toward) + 'lee' (sheltered side), used since the 17th century in nautical contexts.
