deformation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːfɔːrˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːfɔːˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of distorting or twisting the shape or form of something, especially in a way that is undesirable or damaging.
the act of distorting a shape or form

Examples

The strong winds caused a noticeable deformation of the metal roof.
Repeated stress can result in the deformation of plastic materials over time.
He was injured in an accident that left a permanent deformation in his right arm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distortion - the act of twisting or altering something out of its true state

Etymology

From Latin deformatio (a deforming), from deformare (to disfigure).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdiːfɔrˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːfɔːˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
a change in the shape or dimensions of something, especially as a result of applied force or stress
change in shape caused by stress

Examples

The intense heat caused a slight deformation of the metal beam.
Repeated pressure can lead to permanent deformation of soft materials.
Engineers studied the deformation patterns after the bridge was loaded with heavy traffic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distortion - the act of twisting or altering something out of its true, natural, or original state
  • Antonym: restoration - the act of returning something to its original condition

Etymology

From Latin deformare (to disfigure, to change shape), from de- (down, away) + forma (form, shape).

3. NOUN

/ ˌdiːfɔːrˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːfɔːˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of changing shape or form, especially in a way that is damaging or makes something worse
change in shape for the worse

Examples

The collapse of the bridge was due to the deformation of its main support beam.
Plastic materials can undergo significant deformation before breaking.
Repeated pressure on the cushion caused deformation of its original shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distortion - the action of giving a misleading or false account or impression; the process of being deformed or twisted out of shape
  • Antonym: improvement - the act of making something better or the state of being improved

Etymology

From Latin 'deformatio' (a disfiguring), from 'deformare' (to disfigure).
