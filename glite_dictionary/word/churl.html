churl

1. NOUN

/ tʃɜrl / (AmE), / tʃɜːl / (BrE)
a rude and selfish person who is unwilling to give or spend
rude, selfish person
archaic

Examples

He was such a churl that he never donated to charity, no matter how desperate the cause.
The guests thought the landlord was a churl for refusing them a glass of water on a hot day.
No one wanted to work with a churl who always put his own interests first.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miser - a person who hoards wealth and spends as little money as possible
  • Antonym: generous - willing to give or share; unselfish

Etymology

Old English *ceorl* (freeman of the lowest rank), later associated with coarseness or rudeness.
