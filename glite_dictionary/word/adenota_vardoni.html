Adenota vardoni

1. NOUN

/ əˈdɛn.oʊ.tə vɑːrˈdoʊni / (AmE), / əˈdɛn.əʊ.tə vɑːˈdəʊni / (BrE)
A species of African antelope closely related to the waterbuck, found in woodlands and grasslands of central and southern Africa.
African antelope species related to waterbuck

Examples

The Adenota vardoni inhabits woodland regions throughout parts of central Africa.
Researchers observed a herd of Adenota vardoni near the riverbank during the dry season.
Unlike some antelopes, the Adenota vardoni is rarely seen far from water sources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waterbuck - large African antelope of the genus Kobus

Etymology

Genus name from Greek, species named after explorer William Vardon.
