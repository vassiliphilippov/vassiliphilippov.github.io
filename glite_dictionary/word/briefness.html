briefness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbriːfnəs / (AmE), / ˈbriːfnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being short in duration or concise in expression
the state of being brief or short

Examples

The briefness of the meeting surprised everyone who expected a long discussion.
She appreciated the briefness of his explanation, which made the instructions easy to follow.
Many poems captivate readers through the briefness of their form and thought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brevity - conciseness or shortness of duration
  • Antonym: lengthiness - the quality of being long or prolonged

Etymology

Formed from 'brief' (from Latin 'brevis', meaning 'short') + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbriːfnəs / (AmE), / ˈbriːfnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being brief or concise; shortness in duration or expression
quality of being brief or concise

Examples

The briefness of his speech impressed everyone in the audience.
I appreciate the briefness with which she explained the complex subject.
The meeting was notable for its briefness and efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brevity - the quality of being brief or concise
  • Antonym: lengthiness - the quality of being lengthy

Etymology

From 'brief' (from Latin *brevis*, short) + suffix '-ness'.
