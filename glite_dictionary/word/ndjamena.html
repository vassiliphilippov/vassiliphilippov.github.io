N'Djamena

1. PROPN

/ əndʒɑːˈmeɪnə / (AmE), / əndʒɑːˈmeɪnə / (BrE)
the capital and largest city of Chad, located in the southwest of the country on the Shari River
capital city of Chad

Examples

She traveled to N'Djamena to attend an international conference last year.
The Chari River flows through N'Djamena, providing water to the city and surrounding areas.
After years abroad, he returned to his hometown, N'Djamena, to start a new business.

Etymology

Renamed from Fort-Lamy to N'Djamena in 1973; from Arabic 'najamina,' meaning 'place of rest.'

2. PROPN

/ ənˌdʒɑːˈmeɪnə / (AmE), / ənˌdʒɑːˈmeɪnə / (BrE)
the capital and largest city of Chad, situated in the southwest of the country on the Shari River
capital city of Chad

Examples

The president delivered a speech in N'Djamena to mark the country's independence day.
International aid organizations have set up bases in N'Djamena for relief efforts.
Flights to N'Djamena are limited, but the city is a growing regional hub.

Etymology

From French N'Djamena, formerly known as Fort-Lamy before 1973.
