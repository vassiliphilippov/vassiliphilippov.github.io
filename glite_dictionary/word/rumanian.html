Rumanian

1. NOUN

/ ruˈmeɪniən / (AmE), / ruːˈmeɪniən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Romania
person from Romania

Examples

There are many Rumanians living in major European cities.
The Rumanian greeted us with a warm smile and traditional food.
She is proud of her Rumanian heritage and speaks the language fluently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Romanian - a native or inhabitant of Romania

Etymology

Variant of 'Romanian', from Romania, the country's name.

3. NOUN

/ ruːˈmeɪniən / (AmE), / ruːˈmeɪniən / (BrE)
the Eastern Romance language spoken mainly in Romania and Moldova, also known as Romanian
the language of Romania and Moldova

Examples

She is fluent in both French and Rumanian.
The novel was translated from Rumanian into English last year.
Many people in Moldova speak Rumanian as their first language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Romanian - the Eastern Romance language spoken mainly in Romania and Moldova

Etymology

From Romanian 'român' (Romanian), formerly spelled 'ruman'; influenced by Latin 'Romanus' (Roman).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
