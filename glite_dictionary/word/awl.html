awl

1. NOUN

/ ɔl / (AmE), / ɔːl / (BrE)
A small pointed tool used for piercing holes, especially in leather, wood, or fabric.
pointed tool for piercing holes

Examples

She used an awl to punch holes in the leather belt.
The carpenter reached for his awl to mark the position of the nails.
A sharp awl is essential for making precise holes in bookbinding.

Etymology

From Old English al, from Proto-Germanic *alō (awl).
