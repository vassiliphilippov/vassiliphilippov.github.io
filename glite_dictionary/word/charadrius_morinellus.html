Charadrius morinellus

1. NOUN

/ kəˈreɪdri.əs mɔːˈrɪnɛləs / (AmE), / kəˈreɪdri.əs məˈrɪnələs / (BrE)
A rare species of plover, commonly known as the Eurasian dotterel, found in upland areas of Eurasia.
rare Eurasian upland plover

Examples

The Charadrius morinellus is highly sought after by birdwatchers due to its rarity and distinctive markings.
During migration, the Charadrius morinellus sometimes appears in unexpected locations across Europe.
Conservationists are working to preserve the habitats essential for the Charadrius morinellus to breed successfully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eurasian dotterel - common English name for Charadrius morinellus
  • Degree Higher: plover - a type of wading bird related to dotterels

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Charadrius' is a bird mentioned in the Vulgate Bible, and 'morinellus' means 'a simpleton' (referring to the bird's tameness).
