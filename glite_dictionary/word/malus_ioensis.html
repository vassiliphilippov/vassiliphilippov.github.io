Malus ioensis

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪləs aɪoʊˈɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˈmaləs aɪəʊˈɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A wild crab apple tree native to the western United States, known for its fragrant pink flowers.
wild crab apple tree of western US

Examples

The Malus ioensis is admired for its beautiful pink blossoms in the spring.
Gardeners often choose Malus ioensis for its ornamental value and fragrance.
Wild Malus ioensis trees can be found growing in prairies across the Midwest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crab apple - a small, sour apple or the tree that bears it

Etymology

From Latin: 'Malus' meaning apple tree, and 'ioensis' indicating origin from Iowa (United States).
