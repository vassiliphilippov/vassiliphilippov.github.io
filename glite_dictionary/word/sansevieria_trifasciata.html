Sansevieria trifasciata

1. PROPN

/ sæn.səˈvɪə.ri.ə traɪ.fæs.iˈɑː.tə / (AmE), / san.səˈvɪə.ri.ə traɪ.fæs.iˈɑː.tə / (BrE)
A stemless tropical plant with long, narrow, rigid leaves, often cultivated indoors as a houseplant; also known as snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue.
tropical houseplant with long, rigid leaves

Examples

The Sansevieria trifasciata is popular in offices due to its low maintenance requirements.
I placed a Sansevieria trifasciata by the window to brighten up the room.
Botanists often recommend Sansevieria trifasciata for improving indoor air quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snake plant - common name for Sansevieria trifasciata
  • Synonym: mother-in-law's tongue - another common name for Sansevieria trifasciata

Etymology

From the genus name Sansevieria, named after Raimondo di Sangro, Prince of San Severo; trifasciata meaning 'three-banded' in Latin, referring to leaf patterns.
