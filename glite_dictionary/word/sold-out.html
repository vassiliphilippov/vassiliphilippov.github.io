sold-out

1. ADJ

/ soʊld aʊt / (AmE), / səʊld aʊt / (BrE)
(of tickets, shows, events, etc.) having all available tickets or items sold; with no more available for sale.
having all available tickets or items sold

Examples

The concert was completely sold-out within hours of ticket sales opening.
We couldn't get seats because the theater was sold-out for the entire weekend.
Every copy of the new book was sold-out on the first day of release.

Synset Examples

had a sold-out house for both performances

Related Terms

  • Antonym: available - able to be bought or obtained
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02267296-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ soʊldˈaʊt / (AmE), / səʊldˈaʊt / (BrE)
having betrayed one's principles, especially by accepting bribes or personal gain
corrupt due to bribe-taking

Examples

The public no longer trusted the sold-out council member to represent their interests.
Her reputation suffered after she was exposed as a sold-out bureaucrat.
Some journalists are accused of being sold-out and publishing stories for money.

Synset Examples

a sold-out politician

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrupt - dishonest or immoral, typically due to bribery
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00625124-s
Synset POS: s
