garrote

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈrɑt / (AmE), / ɡəˈrɒt / (BrE)
An instrument, usually a cord or wire with handles, used for execution or strangulation by tightening around a person's neck.
instrument for strangulation or execution

Examples

The assassin used a garrote to silently eliminate his target.
Historically, the garrote was employed in public executions in Spain.
The victim had marks on his neck consistent with the use of a garrote.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to strangle - to kill by squeezing the throat

Etymology

From Spanish 'garrote' (a stick or cudgel).

2. VERB

/ ɡəˈrɑːt / (AmE), / ɡəˈrɒt / (BrE)
to strangle someone, especially with an iron collar or wire as a method of execution or murder
strangle with an iron collar or wire

Examples

During the Spanish Inquisition, many prisoners were garrotted as a method of execution.
The assassin garroted his target in complete silence, leaving no evidence behind.
In colonial times, criminals were sometimes sentenced to be garroted in public.

Synset Examples

people were garrotted during the Inquisition in Spain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to strangle - to kill by squeezing the throat
  • Synonym: to suffocate - to die or cause to die from lack of air

Etymology

From Spanish 'garrote' (a stick or club, later a strangling device).
