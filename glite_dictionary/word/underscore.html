underscore

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndərˌskɔr / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌskɔː / (BrE)
a line drawn underneath a word, letter, or other written matter, often to emphasize it or indicate where a space is needed
a line drawn under text

Examples

Please write your answers on the line after each underscore.
Her email address uses an underscore instead of a space.
The teacher drew an underscore beneath each important word.

Etymology

Formed from 'under' + 'score' (meaning line or mark).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06814276-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈʌndɚˌskɔɹ / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌskɔː / (BrE)
to draw a line or lines beneath (a word or phrase) for emphasis
to underline for emphasis

Examples

The teacher asked the students to underscore all the important terms in the paragraph.
In the old manuscript, she carefully underscored each unfamiliar word.
Please underscore the main points so they are easy to find later.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: underline - to draw a line under a word or phrase

Etymology

From under- + score, originally meaning to make a line beneath.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01006280-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌʌndərˈskɔr / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈskɔː / (BrE)
to emphasize or give extra importance to something, especially a statement or communication
emphasize; give extra importance to

Examples

The recent events only underscore the need for better security measures.
Her tone seemed to underscore the seriousness of the situation.
His actions underscored his commitment to the project.

Synset Examples

Her gesture emphasized her words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emphasize - to give special importance or prominence to something
  • Antonym: to downplay - to make something seem less important

Etymology

Originally from the practice of drawing a line under words to indicate emphasis. Prefix 'under-' + 'score' (mark with lines).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01016618-v
Synset POS: v
