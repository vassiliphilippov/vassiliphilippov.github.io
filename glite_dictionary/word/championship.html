championship

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæm.pi.ənˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈtʃæm.pi.ən.ʃɪp / (BrE)
a competition to determine the best individual or team in a particular sport, game, or activity
competition to decide a champion

Examples

The team won the championship after an intense final match.
She has competed in the national championship three years in a row.
Qualifying for the world championship is his greatest ambition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tournament - a series of contests in a sport or game

Etymology

From champion + -ship, denoting condition or office.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæm.pi.ənˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈtʃæm.pi.ən.ʃɪp / (BrE)
the status or position of being a champion, especially as recognized by winning a contest or competition
status of being a champion

Examples

He celebrated his championship with his team after winning the tournament.
The championship he earned last year is still recognized worldwide.
Her incredible performance secured her the championship in record time.

Synset Examples

he held the title for two years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: title - the status of being a champion, winner, or holding a championship
  • Antonym: defeat - the state of having been beaten in a competition or contest

Etymology

From 'champion' + '-ship' (denoting state or condition).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæmpiənˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈtʃæmpiənʃɪp / (BrE)
the act of supporting, defending, or arguing for a person, cause, or principle
strong support or advocacy

Examples

Her championship of civil rights earned her widespread respect.
The senator's championship of environmental causes was well known.
He is admired for his tireless championship of free speech.

Synset Examples

his vigorous backing of the conservatives got him in trouble with progressives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advocacy - public support for or recommendation of a cause
  • Antonym: opposition - the act of resisting or going against something

Etymology

From 'champion' (Old French *champion*), meaning a defender or supporter, plus the suffix '-ship' indicating state or condition.
