backstitch

1. VERB

/ ˈbækˌstɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈbæk.stɪtʃ / (BrE)
to sew using a stitch in which the needle is always brought up behind the previous stitch, creating a strong, continuous line
to sew with a strong, overlapping stitch

Examples

The seamstress carefully backstitched the ends of the seam to prevent it from unraveling.
If you backstitch along the edge, the seam will be much stronger.
She backstitching the embroidery to outline the design.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sew - to make or repair something using a needle and thread

Etymology

From 'back' + 'stitch', referring to the backward movement of the needle.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæk.stɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈbæk.stɪtʃ / (BrE)
A sewing stitch in which each new stitch is made by inserting the needle backward into the end of the previous stitch, resulting in a strong, overlapping line.
overlapping sewing stitch

Examples

While embroidering the outline, she used a backstitch to create a solid, continuous line.
It takes some practice to master the backstitch, but it makes the seam much stronger.
He prefers the backstitch over the running stitch for its durability in hand-sewn garments.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: running stitch - a simple sewing stitch with even, separate stitches
  • Degree Higher: overcast stitch - a sewing stitch looping over the edge of fabric

Etymology

From 'back' + 'stitch', indicating the backward motion of the needle.
