lair

1. NOUN

/ lɛr / (AmE), / leə / (BrE)
the place where a wild animal lives and hides; a den or resting place of a wild animal
wild animal's den or resting place

Examples

The fox comfortably settled into its lair beneath the roots of an old tree.
Explorers found the bear’s hidden lair deep within the dense forest.
At dawn, the wolf left its lair in search of food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: den - the home of some kinds of wild animals
  • Synonym: burrow - a hole or tunnel dug by a small animal as a dwelling

Etymology

From Old English 'leger' (bed, couch), related to 'lie'.
