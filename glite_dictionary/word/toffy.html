toffy

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑfi / (AmE), / ˈtɒfi / (BrE)
A sweet made by boiling sugar and butter together, then cooling and hardening the mixture, often forming thin sheets.
caramelized sugar sweet

Examples

She made homemade toffy for the school bake sale.
Children love to chew on sticky toffy during the holidays.
The old-fashioned shop sells toffy in many different flavors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toffee - sweet made from caramelized sugar and butter

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'toffee', likely originating from an English dialect word.
