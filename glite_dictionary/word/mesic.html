mesic

1. ADJ

/ ˈmiːsɪk / (AmE), / ˈmiːsɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving mesons, especially in the context of particle physics.
relating to mesons (physics)

Examples

The scientists studied the mesic properties of the newly discovered particles.
A mesic atom consists of a normal nucleus but an electron replaced by a meson.
Accurately calculating mesic transitions is essential in understanding particle interactions.

Synset Examples

the radii of the mesic orbits

Etymology

From 'meson' (a type of subatomic particle) plus suffix '-ic', forming technical adjectives.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmiː.sɪk / (AmE), / ˈmiː.sɪk / (BrE)
having or characterized by a moderate or well-balanced supply of moisture, especially in ecological contexts.
characterized by moderate moisture

Examples

The forest supports a variety of mesic plant species adapted to moderate soil moisture.
Several amphibian populations thrive in the region's mesic wetlands.
Scientists studied mesic grasslands to understand how plants respond to changes in rainfall.

Synset Examples

mesic habitats

Related Terms

  • Antonym: xeric - characterized by very dry or arid conditions
  • Antonym: hydric - characterized by very wet or saturated conditions

Etymology

From Greek mesos (middle, moderate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02599866-a
Synset POS: a
