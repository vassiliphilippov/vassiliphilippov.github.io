analphabet

1. NOUN

/ ænˈæl.fə.bɛt / (AmE), / ænˈæl.fə.bɛt / (BrE)
a person who is unable to read or write; an illiterate person who does not know the alphabet
an illiterate person; non-reader

Examples

The literacy program aimed to help every analphabet in the region learn to read and write.
Even at an advanced age, she remained an analphabet, never having been taught the alphabet.
Studies show that being an analphabet can significantly limit employment opportunities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illiterate - unable to read or write

Etymology

From Greek 'analphabētos', meaning 'not knowing the alphabet'.
