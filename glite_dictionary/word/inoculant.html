inoculant

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɑkjələnt / (AmE), / ɪˈnɒkjʊlənt / (BrE)
a substance, such as a virus, toxin, or immune serum, introduced into the body to produce or increase immunity to a particular disease
substance used to induce immunity
medical

Examples

The new inoculant helped protect the cattle from disease during the outbreak.
Researchers are testing a more stable inoculant that does not require refrigeration.
Farmers use a soybean inoculant to ensure healthy crop growth and disease resistance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vaccine - a substance used to stimulate the production of antibodies and confer immunity
  • Synonym: antitoxin - an antibody that counteracts a toxin

Etymology

From Latin 'inoculāre' (to graft, implant) + English suffix '-ant'.
