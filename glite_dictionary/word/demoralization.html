demoralization

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌmɔrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌmɒrələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a state of depression or discouragement caused by a loss or undermining of morale
loss of morale; discouragement

Examples

The team's repeated losses led to a sense of demoralization among the players.
There was demoralization in the office after the company announced major layoffs.
Continuous criticism can result in demoralization and reduced productivity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: encouragement - the act of giving support, confidence, or hope

Etymology

From French 'démoralisation', from 'démoraliser' (to demoralize), ultimately from Latin 'moralis' (moral).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˌmɔrələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌmɒrələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of undermining or destroying the moral foundation or justification for a doctrine, policy, or belief.
undermining moral foundation of a belief or policy

Examples

The constant criticism led to the demoralization of the party's ideology.
Revelations of hypocrisy resulted in the demoralization of the religious group's core beliefs.
The policy changes aimed at the demoralization of outdated traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undermining - the act of weakening or subverting something, especially authority or morale
  • Synonym: corruption - the process of becoming morally degraded or dishonest
  • Antonym: reinforcement - the process of strengthening or supporting something

Etymology

From French 'démoralisation', from 'démoraliser' (to demoralize) + '-ation'.

3. NOUN

/ dɪˌmɔrələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌmɒrələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a state of confusion, disorder, or loss of morale, especially within a group or organization
state of confusion and loss of morale

Examples

The team's crushing defeat led to a sense of demoralization among the players.
Continued management failures resulted in the demoralization of the entire department.
The sudden loss of leadership created demoralization and chaos in the organization.

Synset Examples

his inconsistency resulted in the demoralization of his staff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disorganization - a lack of order or structure
  • Antonym: order - a state in which everything is arranged appropriately

Etymology

From French 'démoraliser' (to corrupt morals) + English suffix '-ation'.
