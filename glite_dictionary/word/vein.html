vein

1. NOUN

/ veɪn / (AmE), / veɪn / (BrE)
a distinctive style, manner, or tone in speech or writing
distinctive style or manner

Examples

She began her speech in a humorous vein that charmed the audience.
His second novel continued in a darker vein than his first.
The discussion shifted to a more practical vein as the meeting progressed.

Synset Examples

he continued in this vein for several minutes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: style - a manner of doing or presenting things
  • Synonym: tone - the general character or attitude in speech or writing

2. NOUN

/ veɪn / (AmE), / veɪn / (BrE)
a blood vessel that carries blood towards the heart, as distinct from an artery
blood vessel carrying blood to the heart

Examples

The doctor inserted the needle gently into the vein in her arm.
Oxygen-poor blood returns to the heart through the veins.
Some people have prominent veins visible on their hands.

Synset Examples

all veins except the pulmonary vein carry unaerated blood

Related Terms

  • Antonym: artery - a blood vessel that carries blood away from the heart

Etymology

From Old French 'veine', from Latin 'vena' (a blood vessel, vein).

3. NOUN

/ veɪn / (AmE), / veɪn / (BrE)
a thin layer or deposit of mineral ore, typically running through rock or earth
thin layer of mineral ore in rock

Examples

The miners discovered a rich vein of silver deep within the mountain.
The gold was found in a narrow vein between layers of granite.
After months of searching, geologists finally traced the vein to its source.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lode - a deposit of metal ore in the earth
Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ veɪn / (AmE), / veɪn / (BrE)
any of the vascular bundles or ribs that form the branching framework of conducting and supporting tissues in a leaf or other plant organ
vascular bundle in a plant leaf or organ

Examples

The maple leaf's prominent veins are clearly visible against the sunlight.
Water moves through the veins of the plant, nourishing every part of the leaf.
Some insects feed on the sap flowing inside the veins of leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nerve - a prominent strand of conducting tissue in a leaf; synonym in botanical context

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (blood vessel, watercourse, vein).

5. NOUN

/ veɪn / (AmE), / veɪn / (BrE)
one of the horny ribs that stiffen and support the wing of an insect
rib supporting an insect's wing

Examples

Each transparent wing was crossed by a distinct vein running from base to tip.
Entomologists study the pattern of veins to identify different species of insects.
The central vein gives the dragonfly's wing its characteristic strength and shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nerve - a rib or reinforcing structure in an insect wing

6. VERB

/ veɪn / (AmE), / veɪn / (BrE)
to mark or become marked with lines or streaks resembling veins
to mark with veinlike lines or streaks

Examples

The artist veined the marble sculpture to give it a realistic appearance.
The autumn leaves were beautifully veined with red and gold lines.
Frost veined the windowpane in delicate patterns overnight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: streak - to cover with long, thin lines or marks

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (a vein).
