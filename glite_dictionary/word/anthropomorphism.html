anthropomorphism

1. NOUN

/ ˌænθrəpəˈmɔrˌfɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌænθrəpəˈmɔːfɪzəm / (BrE)
The attribution of human characteristics or behavior to animals, objects, or gods.
attributing human traits to non-humans

Examples

The ancient Greeks often practiced anthropomorphism by giving their gods human emotions and appearances.
Children's stories frequently use anthropomorphism to make animals seem more relatable.
Critics explained the painting's appeal through its clever use of anthropomorphism in depicting the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: personification - the representation of an abstract quality or idea as a person

Etymology

From Greek 'anthrōpos' (human) + 'morphē' (form) + '-ism'.
