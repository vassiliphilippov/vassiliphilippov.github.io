unmyelinated

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈmaɪ.ə.ləˌneɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈmaɪ.lɪ.neɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of neurons or nerve fibers) lacking a myelin sheath.
not having a myelin sheath (nerve fibers)
medical

Examples

Certain pain fibers in the body are unmyelinated and conduct impulses more slowly.
The optic nerve contains both myelinated and unmyelinated axons.
Damage to unmyelinated nerves can result in loss of certain sensory functions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: myelinated - having a myelin sheath (nerve fibers)

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + myelinated, from myelin (the insulating sheath around nerves) + -ated.
