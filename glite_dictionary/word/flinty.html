flinty

1. ADJ

/ ˈflɪnti / (AmE), / ˈflɪnti / (BrE)
(of a person or their expression) very hard and unfeeling; showing a lack of emotion or sympathy.
unfeeling; emotionally hard

Examples

Her flinty eyes betrayed no sign of pity during the interview.
Despite his flinty demeanor, he cared deeply for his family.
The judge remained flinty even as the defendant pleaded for mercy.

Synset Examples

his flinty gaze
the child's misery would move even the most obdurate heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard-hearted - lacking sympathy or compassion
  • Antonym: tender - showing gentleness, kindness, and affection

Etymology

From 'flint', a very hard type of stone, plus the adjectival suffix '-y'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈflɪnti / (AmE), / ˈflɪnti / (BrE)
Containing or resembling flint; very hard or unyielding.
containing or resembling flint

Examples

The soil here is flinty and difficult for most crops to thrive in.
She gave him a flinty stare that conveyed no warmth.
The region is known for its flinty hills and rugged landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard - tough or firm, not easily bent, cut, or broken

Etymology

From flint (a hard, sedimentary rock) + -y (adjective suffix).

3. ADJ

/ ˈflɪnti / (AmE), / ˈflɪnti / (BrE)
(of a person or their expression) very hard and unfeeling; showing no emotion or compassion.
emotionally hard or unfeeling

Examples

Her flinty expression made it clear that she had no sympathy for his excuses.
The judge's flinty demeanor intimidated everyone in the courtroom.
Despite the pleas for help, his flinty heart remained unmoved.

Synset Examples

his flinty gaze
the child's misery would move even the most obdurate heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard-hearted - lacking compassion or kindness; unfeeling
  • Synonym: callous - emotionally insensitive or unfeeling
  • Antonym: tender-hearted - showing compassion, kindness, or sympathy

Etymology

From 'flint', the hard, quartz-like mineral, used figuratively since the 1600s to describe emotional hardness.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01161687-s
Synset POS: s
