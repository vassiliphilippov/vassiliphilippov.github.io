mesencephalon

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛsənˈsɛfəˌlɑn / (AmE), / ˌmɛsɛnˈsɛfəlɒn / (BrE)
the middle portion of the brain, also known as the midbrain, located between the forebrain and hindbrain
middle part of the brain; midbrain
medical

Examples

The mesencephalon plays a crucial role in vision and hearing processing.
Damage to the mesencephalon can affect movement and coordination.
During embryonic development, the mesencephalon gives rise to several important brain structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: midbrain - another term for the mesencephalon, the middle part of the brain

Etymology

From Greek 'mesos' (middle) + 'enkephalos' (brain).
