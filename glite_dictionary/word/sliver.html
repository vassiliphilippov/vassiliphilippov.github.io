sliver

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈslɪvə / (BrE)
a small, thin, sharp piece that has been split or broken off from something, especially wood, glass, or metal
thin sharp fragment (wood/glass/metal)

Examples

She carefully removed the sliver of glass from her finger.
A sliver of wood stuck out from the old fence post.
After the vase shattered, there were slivers of porcelain all over the floor.

Synset Examples

he got a splinter in his finger
it broke into slivers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splinter - a small, sharp fragment of material, especially wood

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'slivere', related to 'sliven' (to split).

5. NOUN

/ ˈslɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈslɪvə / (BrE)
a thin, narrow piece or slice that has been cut or broken off from something, especially wood
thin slice or fragment, especially of wood

Examples

She pulled a sliver of wood out of her finger after handling the old fence.
A thin sliver of cheese was all that was left on the plate.
Sunlight entered the room through a narrow sliver between the curtains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splinter - a small, sharp fragment of wood, glass, or similar material
  • Synonym: chip - a small piece broken off from something, especially wood, stone, or glass

Etymology

From Middle English slivere, from Old English slīefre; related to 'slive' meaning to split or slice.
