anomaly

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɑːməli / (AmE), / əˈnɒməli / (BrE)
something that deviates from what is standard, normal, or expected
deviation from the norm

Examples

Scientists discovered an unexpected anomaly in the experiment's data.
Her calm reaction was an anomaly in an otherwise tense situation.
Astronomers detected a gravitational anomaly that suggested the presence of a hidden planet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abnormality - something abnormal; a deviation from the norm
  • Antonym: normality - the state of being normal; conforming to the standard

Etymology

From Greek anōmalia (unevenness), from an- (not) + homalos (even).

2. NOUN

/ əˈnɑːməli / (AmE), / əˈnɒməli / (BrE)
(Astronomy) The angular distance of a planet, as observed from the sun, from its perihelion.
planet's angular distance from perihelion (astronomy)

Examples

The astronomer calculated the planet's anomaly to predict its position in its orbit.
At that point in its orbit, the planet's anomaly measured nearly forty degrees.
Detailed charts record the anomaly for each planet throughout the year.

Etymology

From Latin anomalia, from Greek anōmalia (irregularity).

3. NOUN

/ əˈnɑː.mə.li / (AmE), / əˈnɒm.ə.li / (BrE)
a person who is very unusual or does not fit what is typical or expected
a highly unusual or atypical person

Examples

The new student proved to be an anomaly in a school known for its strict traditions.
Being left-handed in a family of right-handed people made him feel like an anomaly.
She was considered an anomaly among her peers because of her unique perspective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oddity - a person or thing that is strange or unusual
  • Antonym: normality - a person or thing that is normal or usual

Etymology

From Greek anomalia, from anomalos (uneven, irregular).
