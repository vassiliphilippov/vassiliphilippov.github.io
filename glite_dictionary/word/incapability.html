incapability

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnkəˈpeɪbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəˌpæbɪˈlɪti / (BrE)
The state or quality of not being able to do something, physically, intellectually, or legally.
state of not being capable

Examples

His incapability to perform the necessary tasks led to his dismissal.
The incapability of the system to handle large amounts of data became apparent.
She often doubted her own incapability rather than seeking help.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: capability - the ability to do something
  • Synonym: inability - lack of the ability to do something

Etymology

Formed from 'in-' (not) + 'capability'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnkəˈpæbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəˈpæbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the state of lacking the ability or potential to do or develop something
lack of ability or potential

Examples

The project's failure was due to the team's incapability to adapt to new technologies.
Persistent incapability in leadership can hinder the growth of any organization.
She recognized her incapability in mathematics and sought additional help.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incapacity - the inability to do something; physical or mental lack
  • Antonym: capability - the ability or potential to do something

Etymology

From in- (not) + capability, modeled after 'incapable'.
