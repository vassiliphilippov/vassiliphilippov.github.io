to reiterate

1. VERB

/ riˈɪdəˌreɪt / (AmE), / riˈɪtəreɪt / (BrE)
to say or do something again, often for emphasis or clarity
say or do again; repeat

Examples

The manager had to reiterate the instructions to make sure everyone understood.
She chose to reiterate her apology for any confusion caused.
During the meeting, he reiterated his support for the new policy.

Synset Examples

She kept reiterating her request

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repeat - to say or do something again

Etymology

From Latin 'reiterare' (to repeat), from 're-' (again) + 'iterare' (to do a second time).
