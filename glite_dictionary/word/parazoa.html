Parazoa

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˈzoʊə / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈzəʊə / (BrE)
A subkingdom of multicellular animals characterized by less-specialized cells than the Metazoa, including only the phylum Porifera (sponges).
subkingdom of simple multicellular animals

Examples

The Parazoa represent the simplest multicellular animals, lacking true tissues and organs.
Sponges are classified in the phylum Porifera, which is the only group within the Parazoa.
Researchers study Parazoa to understand early evolutionary developments in animal life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Metazoa - multicellular animals with specialized cells forming tissues and organs
  • Synonym: Porifera - phylum of simple aquatic animals known as sponges

Etymology

From Greek para- (beside, near) + zōion (animal).
