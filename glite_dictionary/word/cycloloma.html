Cycloloma

1. NOUN

/ saɪkəˈloʊmə / (AmE), / sɪkəˈləʊmə / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the family Amaranthaceae, containing a single species, Cycloloma atriplicifolium, known as winged pigweed or plains tumbleweed.
genus of plants (winged pigweed)

Examples

Cycloloma is a monotypic genus, comprising only Cycloloma atriplicifolium as its species.
The dry stems of Cycloloma are commonly seen tumbling across the plains in late summer.
Botanists studied the unique seed dispersal mechanism of Cycloloma in the prairie ecosystem.

Etymology

From Greek 'kyklos' (circle) and 'loma' (border or fringe), referring to features of the plant's fruit.

2. NOUN

/ saɪkəˈloʊmə / (AmE), / sʌɪkəˈləʊmə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae, including the species Cycloloma atriplicifolium, commonly known as tumbleweed.
a plant genus including tumbleweed

Examples

The most widespread species in the Cycloloma genus is commonly found in arid regions of North America.
Botanists recently published a study on the drought resistance of Cycloloma plants.
The distinctive shape of Cycloloma helps it spread its seeds across open plains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tumbleweed - a plant that breaks away from its roots and is driven by the wind

Etymology

From Greek 'kyklos' (circle) + 'loma' (fringe or border).
