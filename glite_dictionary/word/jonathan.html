Jonathan

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑːnəθən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒnəθən / (BrE)
A variety of red, late-ripening apple that is primarily eaten raw.
a cultivar of red apple

Examples

The orchard specializes in growing Jonathan apples, known for their sweet-tart flavor.
She packed a crisp Jonathan in her lunchbox every day during apple season.
Jonathan apples are popular among bakers for their firm texture and bright taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apple - the round fruit of a tree of the rose family, with crisp flesh and red, green, or yellow skin
