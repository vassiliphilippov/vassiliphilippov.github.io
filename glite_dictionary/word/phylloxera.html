phylloxera

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈlɑksərə / (AmE), / fɪˈlɒksərə / (BrE)
A small insect of the genus Phylloxera, especially Phylloxera vitifoliae, which infests and damages the roots and leaves of grapevines.
plant louse harmful to grapevines
medical

Examples

The vineyard suffered severe losses after an outbreak of phylloxera.
Scientists are researching new ways to protect grapevines from phylloxera infestations.
The arrival of phylloxera in Europe devastated the wine industry in the 19th century.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek phyllon (leaf) + xēros (dry).
