Chemakuan

1. NOUN

/ tʃəˈmæk.ju.ən / (AmE), / tʃəˈmæk.ju.ən / (BrE)
a group of Native American languages formerly spoken in the Pacific Northwest region of Washington State, belonging to the Mosan phylum.
a group of indigenous languages of Washington

Examples

The Chemakuan languages are an important part of the linguistic heritage of Washington State.
Linguists have studied Chemakuan to understand its relationship to other Mosan languages.
Only a few speakers of Chemakuan languages remained by the early 20th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chimakum - an extinct Chemakuan language formerly spoken in Washington
  • Synonym: Quileute - a Chemakuan language spoken by the Quileute people
