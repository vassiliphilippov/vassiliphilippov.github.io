neotenic

1. ADJ

/ niˈoʊtənɪk / (AmE), / niːˈəʊtənɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by neoteny, the retention of juvenile features in the adult form of a species
retaining juvenile features in adulthood (biology)

Examples

Axolotls are a classic example of a neotenic species, as they retain larval features throughout life.
Some scientists believe that certain dog breeds show neotenic traits such as large eyes and rounded skulls.
The neotenic development seen in salamanders allows them to remain aquatic throughout adulthood.

Synset Examples

neotenic development

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paedomorphic - retaining juvenile characteristics in the adult form
  • Antonym: mature - fully developed or adult in form

Etymology

From neoteny + -ic. Neoteny from Greek neos (young) + teinein (to stretch, extend).
