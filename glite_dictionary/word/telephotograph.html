telephotograph

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛləˈfoʊtəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˌtɛlɪˈfəʊtəˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
A photograph transmitted and reproduced over a distance by electronic or telegraphic means.
photograph sent over a distance electronically

Examples

The newspaper received a telephotograph of the sports event minutes after it ended.
During the 1930s, it was revolutionary to transmit a telephotograph across continents.
He marveled at the clarity of the telephotograph sent from the remote broadcast station.

Etymology

From 'tele-' (Greek for 'far') + 'photograph'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtɛləˈfoʊtəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˌtɛlɪˈfəʊtəˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
A photograph taken using a telephoto lens, allowing distant objects to be captured as though they are closer.
photograph taken with a telephoto lens

Examples

The wildlife photographer captured a stunning telephotograph of the rare bird from a safe distance.
She hung a telephotograph of the city skyline on her living room wall.
Many news agencies depend on the telephotograph to document events happening far from the action.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photograph - a picture made using a camera

Etymology

Formed from 'telephoto' (from Greek 'tēle' meaning 'far' and 'photo' from 'phōs' meaning 'light') and 'graph' (from Greek 'graphē' meaning 'writing' or 'drawing').
