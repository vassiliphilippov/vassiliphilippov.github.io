cephalopodan

1. ADJ

/ sɛfəˈlɒpədən / (AmE), / sɛfəˈlɒpədən / (BrE)
relating to or belonging to the class Cephalopoda, which includes octopuses, squids, and cuttlefish.
relating to cephalopods

Examples

The octopus is a remarkable example of a cephalopodan creature with advanced intelligence.
Scientists studied the cephalopodan nervous system to understand its unique structure.
Many cephalopodan fossils have been found in ancient marine rocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cephalopod - any marine mollusc of the class Cephalopoda, e.g., octopus, squid, cuttlefish

Etymology

From modern Latin Cephalopoda (class name), from Greek kephalē (head) + pous, pod- (foot), with the adjective suffix -an.
