necromancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛkɹəˌmænsi / (AmE), / ˈnɛk.rə.mæn.si / (BrE)
the practice or belief in communicating with the dead, especially in order to predict the future or influence events by magical means
magic involving communication with the dead

Examples

Legends say the dark wizard practiced necromancy to raise armies of the dead.
Some medieval texts described necromancy as forbidden magic involving spirits.
The villagers feared the old castle was haunted by someone skilled in necromancy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorcery - the use of magic, especially evil magic
  • Synonym: divination - the practice of seeking knowledge of the future by supernatural means
  • Synonym: witchcraft - the practice of magical skills and abilities

Etymology

From Late Latin necromantia, from Ancient Greek nekromanteia (divination by the dead).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɛkrəˌmænsi / (AmE), / ˈnɛkrəˌmænsi / (BrE)
the practice of purportedly communicating with the dead, especially to predict the future
supposed communication with the dead

Examples

Stories about necromancy have fascinated people for centuries.
Some believed the old wizard was skilled in the dark art of necromancy.
The villagers feared anyone accused of necromancy, associating them with evil spirits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorcery - the use of magic, especially that involving the summoning of supernatural powers
  • Synonym: divination - the practice of seeking knowledge of the future or the unknown by supernatural means
  • Synonym: witchcraft - the practice of magical skills

Etymology

From Greek nekromanteia (divination by the dead), from nekros (dead body) + manteia (divination).
