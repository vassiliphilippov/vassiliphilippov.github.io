aviatrix

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪviˈeɪtrɪks / (AmE), / ˌavɪˈeɪtrɪks / (BrE)
a female pilot; a woman who operates an aircraft
female aircraft pilot

Examples

Amelia Earhart remains one of the most famous aviatrixes in history.
The daring aviatrix completed her solo flight across the Atlantic.
In the 1920s, becoming an aviatrix was considered both adventurous and groundbreaking for women.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aviator - a person who operates an aircraft; pilot

Etymology

From Latin 'avis' (bird) and the feminine suffix '-trix', after 'aviator'.
