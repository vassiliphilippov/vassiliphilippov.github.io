Lord

1. NOUN

/ lɔrd / (AmE), / lɔːd / (BrE)
A title used to refer to God, especially in the Judeo-Christian tradition; often used as an expression of reverence or worship.
title for God in Christianity

Examples

Many people prayed to the Lord for guidance during difficult times.
The congregation sang hymns in praise of the Lord.
She thanked the Lord for her good fortune every day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: God - The deity worshipped in monotheistic religions such as Christianity, Judaism, and Islam
  • Degree Lower: Lord - A man of high rank or position, especially in the nobility

Etymology

From Old English 'hlāford', meaning 'master' or 'ruler', originally 'loaf-ward', one who guards the bread.

2. NOUN

/ lɔrd / (AmE), / lɔːd / (BrE)
a man who has the title of a peer in certain systems of nobility, especially in the United Kingdom
nobleman with the title 'Lord'

Examples

The Lord presided over the estate with grace and responsibility for many years.
In the House of Lords, each Lord has a seat by right of their title.
He was made a Lord in recognition of his service to the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baron - a lower rank of nobility, often a synonym for Lord
  • Antonym: lady - the female equivalent of a lord in nobility

Etymology

From Old English 'hlāford', meaning 'master, ruler'.
