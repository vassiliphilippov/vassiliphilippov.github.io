lord

1. VERB

/ lɔrd / (AmE), / lɔːd / (BrE)
to give someone the title or rank of a lord; to make someone a lord
to confer lordship upon

Examples

The king decided to lord his most trusted advisor for years of loyal service.
She was lorded by the monarch after her heroic deeds during the war.
After decades of dedication to the crown, he was finally lorded and given an estate.

Etymology

From Old English 'hlāford' (master), related to general use as a title of rank.

2. NOUN

/ lɔrd / (AmE), / lɔːd / (BrE)
A man who has power and authority over others, especially as a ruler, noble, or powerful figure.
man with power or authority; ruler or noble

Examples

The lord of the manor held great influence over the local villagers.
Local farmers paid taxes to their lord in exchange for protection.
He was regarded as the undisputed lord of the region, commanding respect and authority.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: master - a person in control or in authority over others
  • Antonym: servant - a person who serves another, especially in a subordinate position

Etymology

From Old English 'hlāford' (master, ruler), literally 'bread keeper', from 'hlāf' (loaf) and 'weard' (guardian).
