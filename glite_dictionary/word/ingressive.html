ingressive

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈɡrɛsɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡresɪv / (BrE)
A speech sound made by drawing air inward through the mouth or nose rather than pushing it out.
speech sound made with inward airflow

Examples

Some Scandinavian languages use ingressive sounds in informal communication.
It is rare to find an ingressive in English speech, except in imitations or special effects.
The phonetics professor demonstrated an ingressive by sharply inhaling while speaking.

Synset Examples

An ingressive is a speech sound that is articulated using an inwards moving airstream mechanism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: egressive - a speech sound made by expelling air outward

Etymology

From Latin 'ingressus' (a going in), from 'ingredi' (to enter).
