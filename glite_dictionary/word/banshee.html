banshee

1. NOUN

/ ˈbænʃi / (AmE), / ˈbanʃi / (BrE)
(in Irish folklore) a female spirit whose wailing is believed to foretell the death of a family member.
female spirit who warns of death in Irish folklore

Examples

Local legend holds that the cry of a banshee can be heard before someone in the family dies.
According to myth, a banshee wails near the house of a person who is soon to pass away.
That haunting scream in the night made her think of a banshee from Irish folklore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghost - a spirit of a dead person
  • Synonym: death omen - an event or sign believed to predict death

Etymology

From Irish bean sí, meaning 'woman of the fairy mound'.
