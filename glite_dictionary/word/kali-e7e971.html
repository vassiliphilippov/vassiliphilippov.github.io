kali

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːli / (AmE), / ˈkɑːli / (BrE)
A bushy plant of Old World salt marshes and sea beaches with prickly leaves, historically burned to produce a crude soda ash.
prickly salt marsh plant, source of soda ash

Examples

In the past, kali was burned to extract soda ash for glass making.
Scientists found kali thriving along the Mediterranean coastline.
The prickly leaves of kali make it easily identifiable in the salt marsh.

Etymology

From Arabic 'qali', referring to alkaline ashes of plants.
