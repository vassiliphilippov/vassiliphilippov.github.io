hazelnut

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪzəlˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈheɪzəlˌnʌt / (BrE)
the edible nut of the hazel tree, especially of any tree of the genus Corylus
edible nut from the hazel tree

Examples

She sprinkled chopped hazelnuts over her morning oatmeal for extra crunch.
The rich, buttery flavor of hazelnut is popular in both desserts and coffee.
He bought a bag of roasted hazelnuts at the farmer's market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filbert - another term for the hazelnut, especially in British English

Etymology

From Middle English 'hasel-not', from Old English 'hæselhnutu', literally 'hazel nut'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈheɪzəlˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈheɪzlnʌt / (BrE)
the edible nut of a hazel tree, or the shrub or small tree of the genus Corylus that bears this nut
edible nut from the hazel tree

Examples

She sprinkled chopped hazelnuts on top of the chocolate cake.
Hazelnuts are often used in the production of pralines and spreads like Nutella.
A mature hazelnut shrub can produce several pounds of nuts each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filbert - another name for the hazelnut or a variety of hazelnut

Etymology

From Middle English 'hasel-note', from Old English 'hæselhnutu', combining 'hæsel' (hazel) and 'hnutu' (nut).
