chaser

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪ.sɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪ.sə / (BrE)
A drink taken immediately after another drink, usually to follow a strong or unpleasant-tasting liquor.
drink that follows another drink

Examples

He ordered a whiskey with a chaser of beer to soften the burn.
She always needs a chaser after drinking tequila shots.
A cold soda makes a good chaser for spicy cocktails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shot - a small amount of strong alcoholic drink taken quickly

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪsər / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪsə / (BrE)
a person who pursues or tries to overtake or capture someone or something
person who pursues or tries to capture

Examples

The police officer was a determined chaser who never gave up on a case.
As the thief ran down the alley, his chaser was close behind.
Every hero needs a worthy chaser to keep the story interesting.

Synset Examples

always before he had been able to outwit his pursuers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pursuer - a person who pursues; especially one seeking to capture

Etymology

From Middle English *chasen* (to chase) + -er (agent suffix).
