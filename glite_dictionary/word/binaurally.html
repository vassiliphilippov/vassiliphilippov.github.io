binaurally

1. ADV

/ bɪˈnɔrəli / (AmE), / bɪˈnɔːrəli / (BrE)
In a manner involving or relating to both ears.
involving both ears

Examples

The music was played binaurally to simulate a three-dimensional audio experience.
The test subject heard the tones binaurally through a set of headphones.
Researchers delivered the audio stimulus binaurally to measure the participant's brain response.

Synset Examples

the stimulus was presented binaurally

Related Terms

  • Synonym: binaural - relating to or using both ears
  • Antonym: monaurally - involving only one ear

Etymology

From 'binaural' (relating to both ears), from Latin 'bi-' (two) and 'auris' (ear).
