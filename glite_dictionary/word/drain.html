drain

1. NOUN

/ dreɪn / (AmE), / dreɪn / (BrE)
a tube inserted into a body cavity to remove fluid or other unwanted material, especially during or after surgery
tube for removing fluid from body
medical

Examples

The surgeon inserted a drain to prevent fluid from building up after the operation.
After her procedure, the nurse checked the drain to ensure it was functioning properly.
In some cases, a drain is necessary to aid healing and reduce the risk of infection.

2. NOUN

/ dreɪn / (AmE), / dreɪn / (BrE)
The act or process of emptying something by letting liquid flow out of it.
act of letting liquid drain out

Examples

After the storm, a complete drain of the flooded basement was necessary.
The drain of the oil tank took longer than expected.
He supervised the drain of water from the swimming pool before cleaning it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drainage - the process or means of draining liquid from something

Etymology

From Old French 'dreiner', from Latin 'draganare', related to ‘draw’.
