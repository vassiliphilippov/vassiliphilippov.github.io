Lyndon B. Johnson

1. PROPN

/ ˈlɪn.dən ˈbi ˈdʒɑn.sən / (AmE), / ˈlɪn.dən ˈbi ˈdʒɒn.sən / (BrE)
(1908–1973) the 36th President of the United States, who served from 1963 to 1969 after the assassination of John F. Kennedy.
36th U.S. President (1963–1969)

Examples

Lyndon B. Johnson signed the Civil Rights Act into law in 1964.
Many historians debate Lyndon B. Johnson's legacy regarding the Vietnam War.
Lyndon B. Johnson became president after John F. Kennedy was assassinated.
