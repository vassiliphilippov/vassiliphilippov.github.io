protractible

1. ADJ

/ proʊˈtræktəbəl / (AmE), / prəˈtræktəbl / (BrE)
able to be extended or drawn out, especially used to describe parts of an animal's body or muscle that can be lengthened or protruded.
able to be extended or drawn out

Examples

The lizard's tongue is protractible, allowing it to catch insects from a distance.
The squid has a protractible proboscis it uses to hunt prey.
Certain snails have a protractible proboscis that can be extended to search for food.

Synset Examples

protractile muscle

Related Terms

  • Antonym: retractile - capable of being drawn back or retracted

Etymology

From Latin 'protractus' (stretched forth) + English suffix '-ible' (able to be).
