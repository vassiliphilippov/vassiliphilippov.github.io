prosthodontia

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɑːsθoʊˈdɑːnʃə / (AmE), / ˌprɒsθəˈdɒnʃə / (BrE)
the branch of dentistry concerned with the design, manufacture, and fitting of artificial replacements for teeth and other parts of the mouth
branch of dentistry for artificial tooth replacement
medical

Examples

He visited a specialist in prosthodontia after losing several teeth in an accident.
Advances in prosthodontia have improved options for people needing dentures.
The dental school offers a postgraduate program in prosthodontia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prosthodontics - the dental specialty relating to prosthodontia

Etymology

From Greek 'prosthesis' (addition) and 'odont-' (tooth).
