convictfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːnvɪktˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɒnvɪktˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a species of greenling fish with a whitish body marked by black bands
a striped greenling fish

Examples

The aquarium featured a striking convictfish swimming among rocks and seaweed.
Divers spotted several convictfish near the reef, easily identified by their bold black bands.
A juvenile convictfish was observed hiding in a crevice to avoid predators.

Etymology

Named for its black bands resembling the striped uniforms once worn by convicts.
