dickey

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɪki / (AmE), / ˈdɪki / (BrE)
(British, informal) Not working properly; faulty or unreliable.
not working properly; faulty (British, informal)
informal british

Examples

My dickey knee acts up whenever it rains.
She couldn't make the dickey toaster work this morning.
He was excused from running due to his dickey ankle.

Synset Examples

I've got this dicky heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faulty - not working correctly; having faults
  • Antonym: sound - in good condition; not faulty

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪki / (AmE), / ˈdɪki / (BrE)
a small extra seat in the back of an old-fashioned two-seater car, typically folding and exposed to the elements
small rear seat in older cars
british

Examples

He proudly restored the vintage car, complete with its original dickey at the rear.
The children argued over who would get to ride in the dickey during the parade.
Her grandfather reminisced about riding in the dickey as a boy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rumble seat - an outside seat in the back of a carriage or early automobile

Etymology

From British English 'dickey', possibly related to 'dickey seat', origin uncertain but used from late 19th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɪki / (AmE), / ˈdɪki / (BrE)
a detachable, often starched, front part of a shirt, worn under a jacket to simulate a full shirt
detachable shirt front

Examples

He wore a crisp dickey under his tuxedo for the wedding.
The actor forgot his dickey, so his costume looked incomplete on stage.
In the early 20th century, a dickey was a common accessory for formal occasions.

Etymology

Perhaps from dialectal *dicky* (neckcloth), but origin uncertain.
