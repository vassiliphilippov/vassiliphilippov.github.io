to synchronise

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪŋkrəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋ.krə.naɪz / (BrE)
to cause two or more things, especially clocks or watches, to indicate the same time or operate at the same rate
cause to match in time or rate

Examples

Before starting the experiment, all team members had to synchronise their watches.
The computer system will synchronise data across all devices each night.
We need to synchronise the clocks so that they show the exact same time.

Synset Examples

synchronize your watches

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to desynchronise - cause to become unsynchronized or out of time with each other
  • Synonym: coordinate - bring different elements into a harmonious or efficient relationship
  • Synonym: to align - arrange things in a straight line or in correct relative positions

Etymology

From Greek 'synchronos' (occurring at the same time), via French 'synchroniser'.
