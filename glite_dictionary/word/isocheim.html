isocheim

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪsəˌkaɪm / (AmE), / ˈaɪsəʊˌkiːm / (BrE)
A line drawn on a map connecting places that share the same average winter temperature.
line connecting equal winter temperatures

Examples

The map displayed an isocheim running through several European cities, indicating identical winter climates.
Meteorologists use each isocheim to study changes in seasonal temperatures across regions.
Drawing an isocheim helps visualize areas with similar mean winter temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isotherm - a line connecting points of equal temperature

Etymology

From Greek 'iso-' (equal) and 'kheima' (winter).
