windshield

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪndˌʃild / (AmE), / ˈwɪndʃiːld / (BrE)
a transparent screen made of glass or plastic at the front of a vehicle to protect its occupants from wind and debris
front protective screen of a vehicle
american

Examples

The rain was pouring so hard that I could barely see through the windshield.
He had to replace his cracked windshield after the accident.
She wiped the fog off the windshield before starting the car.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windscreen - front glass protective screen in a vehicle (British English)

Etymology

From 'wind' + 'shield', indicating its function as a shield against wind.
