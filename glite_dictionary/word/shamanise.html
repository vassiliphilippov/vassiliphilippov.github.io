to shamanise

1. VERB

/ ˈʃæməˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈʃɑːmənaɪz / (BrE)
to practise shamanism; to perform rituals or activities typical of a shaman
perform or practise shamanism

Examples

In traditional Siberian cultures, elders would often shamanise to heal members of their tribe.
She traveled to Mongolia to learn how to shamanise from local spiritual leaders.
Many new-age groups shamanise as part of their spiritual retreats, incorporating drumming and chanting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shamanize - American spelling of 'shamanise'

Etymology

Formed from 'shaman' (originally from Russian 'shaman', via Tungusic) + '-ise' (a verb-forming suffix).
