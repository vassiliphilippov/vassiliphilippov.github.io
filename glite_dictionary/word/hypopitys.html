Hypopitys

1. PROPN

/ haɪˈpɑpɪtɪs / (AmE), / hʌɪˈpɒpɪtɪs / (BrE)
A botanical genus formerly used for the pinesaps, typically now included in the genus Monotropa.
former genus of pinesap plants

Examples

The pale yellow stems of Hypopitys can often be seen emerging in shaded pine woods.
Many botanists now classify Hypopitys as part of the genus Monotropa.
During the hike, we spotted a cluster of Hypopitys growing beneath the trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Monotropa - genus of non-photosynthetic plants commonly called Indian pipe and pinesap

Etymology

From Greek hypo- (under) + pitys (pine), referring to the plant’s habit and association with pine forests.
