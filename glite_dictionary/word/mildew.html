mildew

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlˌdu / (AmE), / ˈmɪl.djuː / (BrE)
A fungus that appears as a thin, usually whitish coating on damp organic materials, such as plants, fabrics, or walls.
superficial fungus growth on surfaces

Examples

You should clean the bathroom regularly to prevent mildew from growing on the tiles.
The leaves of the plant were covered with a white, powdery mildew.
The old books in the basement developed a musty smell because of mildew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mold - a fungus that grows in the form of multicellular filaments called hyphae

Etymology

Old English 'meledew', from 'melu' (meal) + 'dēaw' (dew).

2. VERB

/ ˈmɪlˌdu / (AmE), / ˈmɪl.djuː / (BrE)
(of organic matter) to become covered with or affected by mildew, especially as a result of humidity or dampness.
become affected by mildew

Examples

If you leave bread out in a damp kitchen, it may mildew within days.
The old curtains in the attic began to mildew after several rainy seasons.
Books stored in the basement will quickly mildew if the air is too humid.

Synset Examples

The furniture molded in the old house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mold - to become moldy; to grow mold
  • Degree Higher: rot - to decay or decompose due to bacterial or fungal action

Etymology

From Old English *meledew*, related to Proto-Germanic *melþus* (honeydew) and *dēaw* (dew).
