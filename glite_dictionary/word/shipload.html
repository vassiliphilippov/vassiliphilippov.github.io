shipload

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪpˌloʊd / (AmE), / ˈʃɪp.ləʊd / (BrE)
an amount of cargo that can be carried by a single ship
amount a ship can carry

Examples

The company exported a shipload of electronics to Europe last month.
After the storm, they found a shipload of timber washed up on the shore.
A single shipload of grain was enough to feed the entire town for a week.

Synset Examples

he imported wine by the boatload

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boatload - an amount of cargo that can be carried by a single boat

Etymology

Formed from 'ship' + 'load', modeling the pattern of similar words like 'truckload' and 'cartload'.
