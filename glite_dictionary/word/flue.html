flue

1. NOUN

/ fluː / (AmE), / fluː / (BrE)
An organ pipe whose tone is produced by air flowing across a sharp edge or lip, rather than by a vibrating reed.
organ pipe sounded by air over a lip

Examples

The church organ features several flues that create warm, mellow tones.
Each flue in the pipe organ must be carefully tuned to blend with the others.
Unlike reed pipes, a flue produces sound purely through air vibration across its edge.

2. NOUN

/ fluː / (AmE), / fluː / (BrE)
a passage or duct for conveying smoke, fumes, or gases from a fireplace, furnace, or boiler to the outside air
a duct for carrying off smoke or gases

Examples

After the repairs, the flue allowed smoke to exit safely through the chimney.
You should clean the flue regularly to prevent dangerous blockages.
Birds sometimes build nests inside the flue, causing smoke to back up into the house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chimney - a vertical channel or pipe that conducts smoke and gases up from a fire or furnace

Etymology

Possibly from Middle Dutch 'vlo', meaning 'chimney' or 'shaft'.

3. NOUN

/ fluː / (AmE), / fluː / (BrE)
a flat, blade-shaped projection on the arm of an anchor that helps it grip the sea bed
blade-like projection on anchor's arm

Examples

The anchor's flue dug deep into the sandy bottom, securing the vessel in place.
A broken flue can cause the anchor to function improperly, making it difficult to hold the ship steady.
Inspect each flue for damage before lowering the anchor overboard.
