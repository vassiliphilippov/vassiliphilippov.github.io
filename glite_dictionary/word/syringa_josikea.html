Syringa josikea

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈrɪŋɡə joʊˈsɪkiə / (AmE), / sɪˈrɪŋɡə jɒˈsiːkiə / (BrE)
A central European shrub with upright clusters of lilac or deep violet flowers and elliptic leaves, commonly known as the Hungarian lilac.
Hungarian lilac shrub

Examples

The Syringa josikea blooms in early summer, brightening gardens with its vivid violet flowers.
Gardeners value Syringa josikea for its hardiness and elegant upright growth.
We planted a row of Syringa josikea along the fence to create a colorful hedge.

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Syringa' and 'josikea' honoring Baroness Jósika, a Hungarian botanist.
