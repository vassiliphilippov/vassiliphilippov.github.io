paleobotany

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪlioʊˈbɑːtəni / (AmE), / ˌpæliəʊˈbɒtəni / (BrE)
the branch of botany that deals with the study of fossil plants
study of fossil plants

Examples

She specialized in paleobotany during her graduate studies.
Research in paleobotany has revealed ancient plant species once thought extinct.
A background in paleobotany is essential for analyzing fossilized forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paleontology - the scientific study of prehistoric life, including fossils
  • Synonym: botany - the scientific study of plants

Etymology

From Greek 'palaios' (ancient) and 'botany'.
