bibliographical

1. ADJ

/ ˌbɪbliəˈɡræfɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌbɪbliəˈɡræfɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or dealing with bibliography, especially the listing, cataloging, or systematic description of books and written works
relating to bibliography or book lists

Examples

The article included a bibliographical reference to every source used.
She compiled a bibliographical list of all books published on the subject.
His bibliographical research revealed many previously unknown manuscripts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bibliographic - alternative form, also meaning relating to bibliography

Etymology

From Greek 'biblion' (book) + 'graphia' (writing), via French and Medieval Latin.
