headspring

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌsprɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌsprɪŋ / (BrE)
the original source of a stream or river; a spring or place where a stream begins
source of a stream or river

Examples

The hikers followed the river all the way to its headspring in the mountains.
Wildlife gathers near the headspring where the water is pure and cold.
Local legends tell of treasures buried at the ancient headspring of the stream.

Synset Examples

they tracked him back toward the head of the stream

Related Terms

  • Synonym: source - the point where something begins, often used for rivers or streams
  • Antonym: mouth - the place where a river enters a larger body of water

Etymology

From 'head' (source) + 'spring' (a place where water emerges from the ground).
