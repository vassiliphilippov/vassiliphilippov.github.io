compactly

1. ADV

/ kəmˈpæktli / (AmE), / kəmˈpæktli / (BrE)
in a way that takes up as little space as possible; closely or neatly arranged together
in a space-saving way

Examples

The files were compactly arranged on the shelf to save space.
Vegetables were compactly packed into the basket for transport.
The text is compactly written, providing clear information without unnecessary words.

Synset Examples

liquid food compactly stored in a pressurized tank

Related Terms

  • Synonym: densely - closely packed together
  • Antonym: loosely - not closely packed or arranged

Etymology

From 'compact' + '-ly'; 'compact' from Latin 'compactus' (joined together).

2. ADV

/ kəmˈpæktli / (AmE), / kəmˈpæktli / (BrE)
in a way that is closely or neatly packed together; in a compact manner or form
in a compact or closely packed way

Examples

The books were arranged compactly on the shelf to maximize space.
Tourists stood compactly in the small elevator during the busy hours.
The code is written compactly to enhance efficiency and readability.

Synset Examples

The children were packed compactly into the car

Related Terms

  • Synonym: densely - in a closely compacted or crowded manner

Etymology

Formed from 'compact' (from Latin 'compactus', meaning 'joined together') + '-ly' adverbial suffix.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00301256-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ kəmˈpæktli / (AmE), / kəmˈpæktli / (BrE)
in a concise and precise manner; using few words or elements without unnecessary detail
in a concise, precise manner

Examples

The summary was written compactly, covering all the main points in just a few sentences.
She explained the procedure compactly so everyone quickly understood what to do.
The instructions were delivered compactly, without any unnecessary information.

Synset Examples

Please state your case as succinctly as possible
he wrote compactly but clearly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: succinctly - in a brief and clearly expressed manner
  • Synonym: concisely - in a brief way that includes only what is necessary
  • Antonym: wordily - in a verbose or long-winded way

Etymology

From 'compact' (from Latin 'compactus', compressed) + '-ly' adverbial suffix.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00291622-r
Synset POS: r
