DVD

1. NOUN

/ diː viː ˈdiː / (AmE), / diː viː ˈdiː / (BrE)
a digital optical disc used for storing and playing movies, music, or other data, playable on computers or DVD players
digital optical disc for movies or data

Examples

I bought the complete series on DVD last year.
Please return the DVD to the library after watching it.
He prefers to stream movies instead of collecting DVDs.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Blu-ray - a high-definition optical disc format for video and data storage
  • Degree Lower: CD - an older type of optical disc used primarily for audio

Etymology

Acronym of Digital Versatile Disc or Digital Video Disc.
