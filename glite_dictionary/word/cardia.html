cardia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrdiə / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdɪə / (BrE)
the opening of the stomach where it connects to the esophagus, and the adjacent part of the stomach
upper opening of the stomach
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully examined the cardia to check for any abnormalities.
Acid reflux can sometimes damage the tissue near the cardia of the stomach.
Inflammation of the cardia may cause difficulty swallowing and discomfort.

Etymology

From Greek kardia (heart or upper orifice of the stomach).
