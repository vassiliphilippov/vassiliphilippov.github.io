Rabbi Moses Ben Maimon

1. PROPN

/ ˈræbaɪ ˈmoʊzɪz bɛn maɪˈmoʊn / (AmE), / ˈræbaɪ ˈməʊzɪz bɛn maɪˈməʊn / (BrE)
A Spanish-Jewish philosopher and scholar (1135–1204), known as Maimonides, considered the foremost Jewish thinker of the Middle Ages and renowned for codifying Jewish law and writing influential philosophical works.
medieval Spanish-Jewish philosopher and scholar

Examples

The works of Rabbi Moses Ben Maimon greatly influenced Jewish law and philosophy for centuries.
Many regard Rabbi Moses Ben Maimon as the foremost Jewish thinker of the Middle Ages.
The Guide for the Perplexed, written by Rabbi Moses Ben Maimon, is still studied by scholars today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Maimonides - another common name for Rabbi Moses Ben Maimon

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Rabbi' (teacher) and 'Moses ben Maimon' (Moses, son of Maimon); also known by the Greek-influenced name 'Maimonides'.
