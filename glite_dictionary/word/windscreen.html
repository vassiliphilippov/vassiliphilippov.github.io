windscreen

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪn(d)ˌskrin / (AmE), / ˈwɪn(d)ˌskriːn / (BrE)
A transparent screen at the front of a vehicle that protects the occupants from wind and debris.
front glass screen of a vehicle
british

Examples

The mechanic replaced the cracked windscreen on my car.
Raindrops kept hitting the windscreen, making it hard to see the road ahead.
She carefully cleaned the windscreen to improve her visibility while driving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windshield - front glass protecting vehicle occupants (American English)

Etymology

From 'wind' + 'screen', first used in the early 20th century to describe the glass fitted to vehicles.
