platyhelminth

1. NOUN

/ ˌplæt.i.hɛlˈmɪnθ / (AmE), / ˌplæt.ɪ.hɛlˈmɪnθ / (BrE)
A worm of the phylum Platyhelminthes, characterized by a flattened body, which may be parasitic or free-living.
flat-bodied worm of phylum Platyhelminthes
medical

Examples

The planarian is a type of platyhelminth commonly studied in biology classes.
Certain platyhelminths, such as tapeworms, can infect the intestines of animals and humans.
Scientists observed the regeneration abilities of the platyhelminth after it was cut in half.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flatworm - common name for worms with a flattened body of the phylum Platyhelminthes

Etymology

From Greek *platys* (broad, flat) + *helmins* (worm).
