hack

1. NOUN

/ hæk / (AmE), / hæk / (BrE)
A horse, especially one used for ordinary riding or transportation rather than for racing or sport.
riding horse used for transport

Examples

She rode her hack through the village every morning on her way to work.
The old hack was reliable, if not particularly fast or elegant.
Stable hands cared for a variety of horses, but he preferred his faithful hack for long country rides.

Etymology

From Middle English *hacche*, probably shortened from Old French *haque* (a horse for hire), ultimately of Dutch origin.
