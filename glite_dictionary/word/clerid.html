clerid

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɛrɪd / (AmE), / ˈklɛrɪd / (BrE)
a beetle of the family Cleridae, typically predatory on other insects and often brightly colored or metallic in appearance
a predatory beetle of the Cleridae family

Examples

The entomologist discovered a rare clerid among the decaying wood.
Clerids are known to hunt bark beetles, making them useful in pest control.
The bright metallic sheen made the clerid easy to spot on the tree trunk.

Etymology

From New Latin Cleridae (the beetle family name), from Greek klēris (a kind of insect).
