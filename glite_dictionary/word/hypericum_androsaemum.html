Hypericum androsaemum

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɛrɪkəm ændrəˈsiːməm / (AmE), / hɪˈpɛrɪkəm anˈdrɒsiːməm / (BrE)
A deciduous bushy shrub native to Eurasia, with golden yellow flowers and reddish-purple fruits, sometimes used to make a soothing salve.
a bushy Eurasian shrub with yellow flowers and reddish fruits

Examples

The garden featured a Hypericum androsaemum in full bloom, its yellow flowers bright against the greenery.
Traditional healers in Spain sometimes use the fruit of Hypericum androsaemum to prepare herbal ointments.
In autumn, the berries of Hypericum androsaemum turn a deep reddish-purple, adding vivid color to the landscape.

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Hypericum' and species name 'androsaemum', originally used in botanical Latin.
