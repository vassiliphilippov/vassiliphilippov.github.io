Saami

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːmi / (AmE), / ˈsɑːmi / (BrE)
the language of the indigenous Sámi people in northern Scandinavia and the Kola Peninsula
language of the Sámi people

Examples

Many scholars have studied the Saami language and its rich oral tradition.
He is fluent in both Norwegian and Saami after growing up in northern Norway.
Efforts are being made to preserve Saami among younger generations in Finland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lapp - the language of nomadic Lapps

Etymology

From Sámi ‘Sámi people, their languages’, self-designation.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːmi / (AmE), / ˈsɑːmi / (BrE)
A member of an indigenous nomadic people living in the northern parts of Norway, Sweden, Finland, and Russia, traditionally known for reindeer herding.
member of a northern Scandinavian indigenous people

Examples

The Saami have a long tradition of reindeer herding in the Arctic regions.
A Saami elder spoke about the importance of preserving their language and culture.
She learned about the customs of the Saami during her trip to Lapland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lapp - an alternative (now often considered offensive) name for a Saami person

Etymology

From the Saami people's name for themselves, Sámi.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09739429-n
Synset POS: n
