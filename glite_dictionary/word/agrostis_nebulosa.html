Agrostis nebulosa

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡrɑstɪs nɪˈbjuːləsə / (AmE), / əˈɡrɒstɪs nɪˈbjuːləsə / (BrE)
A species of grass with light, feathery flower clusters, often grown for use in dried floral arrangements.
a grass used in dried bouquets

Examples

Agrostis nebulosa is valued for its delicate appearance in dried flower arrangements.
Gardeners sometimes plant Agrostis nebulosa as an ornamental grass in borders.
Florists use Agrostis nebulosa to add a soft, airy texture to bouquets.

Etymology

From Latin: 'agrostis' (a kind of grass) and 'nebulosa' (misty, cloudy).
