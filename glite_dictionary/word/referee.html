referee

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛfəˈri / (AmE), / ˌrɛfəˈriː / (BrE)
a person who reads manuscripts and assesses their suitability for publication, as in academic or scientific journals
person assessing manuscripts for publication

Examples

The journal requires each paper to be evaluated by at least one referee.
As a referee, she provided detailed feedback on the manuscript's methodology and clarity.
The editor thanked the anonymous referee for his constructive comments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reviewer - person who assesses the quality of written work

Etymology

From French *référé* (one to whom something is referred), from Latin *referre* (to refer).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛfəˈri / (AmE), / ˌrɛfəˈriː / (BrE)
an attorney appointed by a court to gather information, investigate, and report on a case or issue
court-appointed attorney to investigate a case
legal

Examples

The judge appointed a referee to review the complex financial records in the lawsuit.
After weeks of investigation, the referee submitted her findings to the court.
The legal team requested a referee be assigned to help clarify the disputes between the parties.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10534236-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌrɛfəˈri / (AmE), / ˌrɛfəˈriː / (BrE)
an official in sports responsible for ensuring fair play and enforcing the rules during a game or match
sports official who enforces rules

Examples

The referee blew the whistle to signal the end of the match.
If you disagree with the referee, you can appeal the decision after the game.
During the heated contest, the referee tried to keep the players calm and focused on the rules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: umpire - an official who watches a game or match to enforce the rules (especially in cricket, tennis, baseball)
  • Antonym: player - person participating as a competitor in a game or sport

Etymology

From French 'référé', past participle of 'référer' (to refer).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10534022-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˌrɛfəˈri / (AmE), / ˌrɛfəˈriː / (BrE)
to act as an official who oversees a sports contest and enforces the rules
act as an official in a sports contest

Examples

He was asked to referee the basketball game because of his experience.
She will referee several matches at the upcoming tournament.
After years of playing, he decided to referee instead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: umpire - to act as an umpire in a game or competition

Etymology

From noun 'referee', derived from 'refer', originally in legal sense; applied to sports from the 19th century.

5. VERB

/ ˌrɛfəˈri / (AmE), / ˌrɛfəˈriː / (BrE)
to assess and provide a formal judgment on the quality or suitability of a colleague's work, especially in academic or professional contexts
professionally evaluate a colleague's work

Examples

Each paper submitted to the journal is refereed by at least two experts in the field.
She agreed to referee several grant proposals for the research council this year.
Before publication, your work will be thoroughly refereed to ensure high standards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: review - to assess or critically examine something, especially for publication or approval
  • Synonym: to assess - to evaluate or estimate the quality, ability, or value of something

Etymology

From the noun 'referee', meaning one who judges, from 'refer'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00857476-v
Synset POS: v
