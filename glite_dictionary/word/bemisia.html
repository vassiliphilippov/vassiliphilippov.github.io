Bemisia

1. NOUN

/ bəˈmɪziə / (AmE), / bɪˈmɪsiə / (BrE)
A genus of small whiteflies, including species known as agricultural pests such as the sweet-potato whitefly.
genus of small whiteflies (insects)

Examples

The infestation of Bemisia caused significant damage to the crops.
Researchers are studying Bemisia to develop better pest control methods.
Farmers reported that Bemisia populations surged after the rainy season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whitefly - small sap-sucking insects, pests of plants

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, used in taxonomy for this group of insects.

2. NOUN

/ bɪˈmɪziə / (AmE), / bɪˈmɪziə / (BrE)
A genus of small whiteflies, including several species known as agricultural pests such as the sweet-potato whitefly.
genus of whiteflies (insects)

Examples

The Bemisia genus contains pests that damage many agricultural crops.
Scientists are researching new methods to control Bemisia populations in greenhouses.
Infestations of Bemisia can lead to significant losses in sweet potato yields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whitefly - small flying insect that feeds on plant sap

Etymology

From New Latin, coined for taxonomic purposes in entomology.
