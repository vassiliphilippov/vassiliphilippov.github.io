ovoid

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.vɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.vɔɪd / (BrE)
An object shaped like an egg; having the outline or form of an egg.
egg-shaped object

Examples

The artist sculpted an ovoid from marble for the centerpiece of the garden.
The fossilized ovoid was believed to be a dinosaur egg.
Each seed is contained within a hard, brown ovoid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ellipsoid - a three-dimensional geometric figure, all plane sections of which are ellipses or circles

Etymology

From Latin ovum (egg) + -oid (having the form of).

2. ADJ

/ ˈoʊ.vɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.vɔɪd / (BrE)
having a rounded, egg-like shape; shaped like an oval or egg.
egg-shaped; oval

Examples

The artist placed an ovoid sculpture in the center of the gallery.
This species lays ovoid eggs with a smooth, glossy shell.
He noticed an ovoid pebble sitting by the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oval - having the shape of an egg or an ellipse
  • Degree Higher: spherical - having the form of a sphere or ball
  • Antonym: rectangular - having the shape of a rectangle

Etymology

From French *ovoïde* or from Modern Latin *ovoides*, from Latin *ovum* (egg) + Greek *-eidos* (form, shape).
