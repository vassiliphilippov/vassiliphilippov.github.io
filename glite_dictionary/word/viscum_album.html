Viscum album

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪskəm ˈæl.bəm / (AmE), / ˈvɪskəm ˈal.bəm / (BrE)
A species of parasitic shrub native to Europe, known as the traditional Christmas mistletoe, with greenish stems, leathery leaves, and white berries.
European mistletoe shrub

Examples

The tradition of kissing under the Viscum album dates back centuries in Europe.
Botanists study the way Viscum album attaches itself to the branches of host trees.
The glistening white berries of Viscum album are easily spotted in winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistletoe - Common name for several plants of the Viscum or Phoradendron genus, traditionally used in Christmas decorations

Etymology

From Latin, where 'viscum' means mistletoe and 'album' means white, referring to the color of its berries.
