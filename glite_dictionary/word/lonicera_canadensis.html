Lonicera canadensis

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈnɪsərə kəˌnædənˈsɪs / (AmE), / ləˈnɪsərə kəˌnædənˈsɪs / (BrE)
A species of erect deciduous shrub native to North America, with yellow-white flowers; also known as American fly honeysuckle.
a North American shrub with yellow-white flowers

Examples

The garden featured several Lonicera canadensis shrubs in bloom.
Botanists observed the distinctive yellow-white flowers of Lonicera canadensis in early spring.
Lonicera canadensis prefers moist, shaded woodlands for optimal growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeysuckle - common name for species in the genus Lonicera

Etymology

From Latin: 'Lonicera' (the genus honeysuckle), named after Adam Lonicer, German botanist; 'canadensis' signifies 'of Canada.'
