Cortinarius semisanguineus

1. NOUN

/ kɔrˌtɪnəri.əs ˌsɛm.i.sæŋˈɡwɪn.i.əs / (AmE), / kɔːˌtɪn.əˌrɪ.əs ˌsɛm.ɪ.sæŋˈɡwɪn.i.əs / (BrE)
A species of fungus with a dry brown cap, rusty red gills, and a yellowish stalk, commonly found in coniferous forests.
species of mushroom with red gills

Examples

The distinctive rusty red gills of the Cortinarius semisanguineus can help identify it in the wild.
Many mushroom enthusiasts avoid collecting Cortinarius semisanguineus due to its similar appearance to toxic species.
In autumn, Cortinarius semisanguineus is often found growing beneath pine and spruce trees.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Cortinarius' (genus name), 'semisanguineus' meaning 'half-blooded' or 'partially blood-colored,' referring to the reddish gills.
