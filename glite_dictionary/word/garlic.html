garlic

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrlɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːlɪk / (BrE)
A plant of the onion family with a strong-smelling, pungent-tasting bulb, used as a flavoring in cooking and sometimes for medicinal purposes.
pungent bulb used as seasoning

Examples

She added a clove of garlic to the soup for extra flavor.
The smell of roasting garlic filled the kitchen.
Many recipes use garlic as a key ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onion - an edible bulb with a pungent taste and smell, related plant in the same family

Etymology

From Old English 'gārlēac', from 'gār' (spear) + 'lēc' (leek); referencing the shape of the cloves.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrlɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːlɪk / (BrE)
a plant of the onion family, with a strong flavor and smell, whose bulb is made up of small sections called cloves and is used in cooking and medicine
a strong-flavored bulbous plant used in cooking

Examples

Chopped garlic adds a rich flavor to many dishes.
He planted some garlic in his backyard garden last spring.
The recipe calls for two cloves of garlic, finely minced.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onion - a bulbous plant closely related to garlic, used as a vegetable

Etymology

From Old English ‘garleac’ (spear leek), from ‘gar’ (spear) + ‘leac’ (leek).
