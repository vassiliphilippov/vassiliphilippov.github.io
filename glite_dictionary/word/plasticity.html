plasticity

1. NOUN

/ plæˈstɪsəti / (AmE), / plæˈstɪsəti / (BrE)
the quality of being easily shaped or molded without breaking; malleability
capacity to be shaped or molded

Examples

The plasticity of clay allows artists to create intricate sculptures.
Metals like gold and silver are valued for their high plasticity.
Exposure to heat can increase the plasticity of certain polymers, making them easier to mold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malleability - the capacity of a material to be shaped without breaking
  • Antonym: brittleness - the quality of breaking easily rather than bending or deforming

Etymology

From Latin 'plasticus', from Greek 'plastikos' (fit for molding), from 'plassein' (to mold).
