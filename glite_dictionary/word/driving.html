driving

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdraɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of hitting a golf ball off a tee with a driver
hitting a golf ball with a driver

Examples

His driving off the first tee set the tone for the match.
She has been practicing her driving to increase her distance.
Consistent driving is key to lowering your golf score.

Synset Examples

he sliced his drive out of bounds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drive - a powerful stroke in golf, especially from the tee

/ ˈdraɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdraɪvɪŋ / (BrE)

Examples

4. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdraɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of controlling and steering the movement of a vehicle or animal
controlling and steering a vehicle or animal

Examples

Driving requires concentration and awareness of your surroundings.
After a long day of driving, she finally reached her destination.
He enjoys driving on country roads early in the morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: driving - the activity of operating and steering a vehicle
  • Synonym: riding - the act of traveling on or controlling a horse, bicycle, or vehicle

Etymology

From the verb 'drive', Old English 'drīfan' (to drive, force, urge).
