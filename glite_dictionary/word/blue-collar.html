blue-collar

1. ADJ

/ bluː ˈkɑlɚ / (AmE), / bluː ˈkɒlə / (BrE)
(of work or workers) relating to manual labor, especially in industry, typically involving physical work and paid by wages rather than salary.
relating to manual or industrial workers

Examples

He grew up in a blue-collar neighborhood where most people worked in factories.
Many blue-collar jobs require physical strength and specialized skills.
The company offers training programs for blue-collar workers to help them advance in their careers.

Synset Examples

party of the propertyless proletariat

Related Terms

  • Antonym: white-collar - relating to office or professional work, as opposed to manual labor

Etymology

From the typical blue shirts worn by manual laborers; popularized in the United States in the early 20th century.

2. ADJ

/ bluː ˈkɑlər / (AmE), / bluː ˈkɒlə / (BrE)
Relating to or designating manual industrial work or workers, especially those who perform physical labor.
manual industrial work or workers

Examples

Many blue-collar workers are employed in manufacturing and construction.
He grew up in a blue-collar neighborhood where most families worked at the nearby factory.
The city has seen a decline in blue-collar jobs over the last decade.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: white-collar - relating to office or professional work

Etymology

From the traditional blue shirts worn by manual laborers.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02567490-a
Synset POS: a
