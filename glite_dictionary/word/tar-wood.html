tar-wood

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑr wʊd / (AmE), / ˈtɑː wʊd / (BrE)
A New Zealand silver pine tree (Manoao colensoi), of conical shape with long, slender, spreading branches, adapted to cold, wet environments and high altitudes.
New Zealand silver pine tree

Examples

The tar-wood thrives in high-altitude regions with cold, wet summers in New Zealand.
Botanists studied the unique adaptation of the tar-wood to severe weather conditions.
The national park is home to several ancient tar-wood trees, notable for their slender branches.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɑr wʊd / (AmE), / ˈtɑː wʊd / (BrE)
A New Zealand shrub with sticky resin, often found in coastal or lowland forests.
New Zealand shrub with sticky resin

Examples

The tar-wood thrives in the damp forests along New Zealand's coastline.
We identified the plant as a tar-wood because of its distinctive sticky leaves.
