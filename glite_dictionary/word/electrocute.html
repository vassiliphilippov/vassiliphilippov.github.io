to electrocute

1. VERB

/ ɪˈlɛk.trə.kjut / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛk.trə.kjuːt / (BrE)
to kill (someone) by passing a powerful electric current through their body, especially as a means of capital punishment
kill with electricity, especially as execution

Examples

After a lengthy trial, the convict was electrocuted in the state penitentiary.
The court decided he should be electrocuted rather than face life imprisonment.
They electrocuted the prisoner using the electric chair as required by the law at that time.

Synset Examples

The serial killer was electrocuted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to execute - to put someone to death as a legal penalty
  • Antonym: spare - to refrain from killing or harming

Etymology

Blend of 'electric' and 'execute'; first used in late 19th century America.

2. VERB

/ ɪˈlɛktrəˌkjut / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrəkjuːt / (BrE)
to kill someone by passing a strong electric current through their body
kill by electric shock

Examples

If you touch that exposed wire, you could easily electrocute yourself.
The old electric fence was powerful enough to electrocute livestock that got too close.
Careless handling of live equipment may electrocute unsuspecting workers.

Synset Examples

She dropped the hair dryer into the bathtub and was instantly electrocuted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shock - sudden and powerful effect produced by electricity

Etymology

Blend of 'electric' and 'execute', coined in the late 19th century after the introduction of the electric chair.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
