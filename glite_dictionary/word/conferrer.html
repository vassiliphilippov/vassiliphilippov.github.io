conferrer

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfɝː.ɚ / (AmE), / kənˈfɜː.rə / (BrE)
a person who confers with others, especially in formal discussions or meetings
person who confers or discusses

Examples

As the lead conferrer, she guided the group toward consensus.
The conferrer asked for input from all team members at the meeting.
Each conferrer brought a unique perspective to the conference table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confer - to discuss something with someone to exchange ideas or seek advice

Etymology

From 'confer' + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈfɜrər / (AmE), / kənˈfɜːrə / (BrE)
a person who grants or bestows something, such as property, an honor, or a right, to another
person who bestows or grants something

Examples

The conferrer of the scholarship attended the ceremony to present the award in person.
As the conferrer of the honorary degree, the university president gave a formal speech.
Property is not transferred until the conferrer signs the necessary legal documents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: donor - a person who gives something, especially to charity
  • Antonym: recipient - a person who receives something

Etymology

From Latin conferre (to bring together, bestow), via the verb 'confer'.
