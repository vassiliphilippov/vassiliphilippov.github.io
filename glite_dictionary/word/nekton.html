nekton

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛk.tɑn / (AmE), / ˈnɛk.tɒn / (BrE)
the aggregate of actively swimming aquatic animals, from tiny organisms to whales, in a body of water
actively swimming aquatic animals

Examples

Marine biologists study nekton to understand how fish and other animals move through the ocean.
Unlike plankton, nekton are able to swim against water currents and actively seek food.
Whales, squid, and many fish species are considered nekton in marine ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plankton - tiny or floating aquatic organisms, especially those that drift with currents

Etymology

From German 'Nekton', based on Greek 'nēktēs' (swimmer), from 'nēchein' (to swim).
