protoceratops

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊtoʊˈsɛrəˌtɑps / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəʊˈsɛrətɒps / (BrE)
a small, herbivorous dinosaur of the genus Protoceratops, with a large frill at the back of the head and found in the Late Cretaceous period.
a small horned dinosaur

Examples

The discovery of a protoceratops fossil shed light on dinosaur parental behavior.
Scientists believe that protoceratops lived in herds for protection from predators.
Children at the museum were fascinated by the skull of a protoceratops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triceratops - large ceratopsian dinosaur with three horns

Etymology

From Greek 'proto-' (first) + 'kerat-' (horn) + '-ops' (face), meaning 'first horned face'.
