Protoceratops

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊtəˈsɛrəˌtɑps / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəˈsɛrətɒps / (BrE)
a genus of small, horned, herbivorous dinosaurs from the Late Cretaceous period, known for their distinctive neck frill and found mainly in Mongolia
a genus of small, horned dinosaurs

Examples

The fossilized skull of a Protoceratops was discovered in the Gobi desert.
Paleontologists study Protoceratops to learn about the evolution of horned dinosaurs.
Unlike its larger relatives, Protoceratops did not have large horns above its eyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Triceratops - a larger horned dinosaur genus related to Protoceratops

Etymology

From Greek 'protos' (first) + 'keratops' (horned face).
