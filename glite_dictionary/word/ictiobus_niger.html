Ictiobus niger

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈtaɪ.oʊ.bəs ˈnaɪ.ɡɚ / (AmE), / ɪkˈtaɪ.ə.bəs ˈnaɪ.ɡə / (BrE)
A species of freshwater fish found in the lower Mississippi River, commonly known as the black buffalo.
species of fish found in the lower Mississippi

Examples

The Ictiobus niger is an important species for commercial fishing in the lower Mississippi River.
Anglers often refer to Ictiobus niger by its common name, the black buffalo.
Scientists study Ictiobus niger to understand river ecosystem health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black buffalo - Common name for Ictiobus niger, a species of freshwater fish

Etymology

From genus 'Ictiobus' (Greek: 'fish' + 'ox') and species 'niger' (Latin: 'black').
