Pongo pygmaeus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɒŋɡoʊ pɪɡˈmiːəs / (AmE), / ˌpɒŋɡəʊ pɪɡˈmiːəs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the Bornean orangutan, a large, long-armed ape native to the rainforests of Borneo and Sumatra
scientific name for the Bornean orangutan

Examples

The Pongo pygmaeus is critically endangered due to deforestation and habitat loss.
Researchers observed several Pongo pygmaeus individuals swinging through the treetops in Borneo.
Unlike other great apes, the Pongo pygmaeus spends most of its time in the forest canopy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orangutan - large ape native to Borneo and Sumatra

Etymology

From Latin; 'Pongo' from Kongo 'mpungu' (ape), 'pygmaeus' meaning 'dwarf'.
