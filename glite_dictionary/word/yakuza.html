yakuza

1. NOUN

/ jɑːˈkuːzə / (AmE), / jæˈkuːzə / (BrE)
a member of a Japanese organized crime syndicate, or the Japanese organized crime syndicate itself
Japanese organized crime syndicate or member

Examples

The police arrested several yakuza members involved in the illegal gambling ring.
He wrote a book about his experiences growing up surrounded by the yakuza in Tokyo.
Many films depict the yakuza as both ruthless criminals and people with their own strict code of honor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mafia - Italian or Sicilian organized crime syndicate
  • Synonym: triad - Chinese organized crime groups

Etymology

From Japanese ヤクザ (yakuza), originally referring to a losing hand in a traditional Japanese card game.

2. NOUN

/ jɑːˈkuːzə / (AmE), / jæˈkuːzə / (BrE)
a member of a Japanese organized crime syndicate; a Japanese gangster
member of Japanese organized crime syndicate

Examples

The police raided the club, suspecting it was operated by a yakuza.
In some films, the yakuza are portrayed with strict codes of honor.
After moving to Tokyo, he became involved with the local yakuza.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gangster - a member of an organized group of criminals

Etymology

From Japanese ヤクザ (yakuza), originally meaning 'good-for-nothing; gangster'.
