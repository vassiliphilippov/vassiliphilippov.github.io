pessimistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɛsɪˈmɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɛsɪˈmɪstɪk / (BrE)
expecting that bad things will happen or that a situation will have a negative outcome
expecting the worst

Examples

Despite the encouraging data, the analyst remained pessimistic about the company's future.
Her pessimistic attitude made it hard for her to see any positives in the situation.
He tends to view the world through a pessimistic lens, always expecting things to go wrong.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: optimistic - expecting the best possible outcome or viewing the future with hope
  • Synonym: gloomy - having a negative or hopeless outlook

Etymology

From French 'pessimiste', from Latin 'pessimus' (worst) + '-ic'.
