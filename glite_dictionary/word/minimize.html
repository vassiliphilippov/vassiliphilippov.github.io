to minimize

1. VERB

/ ˈmɪnɪˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪmaɪz / (BrE)
to make something appear less important or significant than it really is
downplay importance

Examples

She tried to minimize the impact of the scandal during the press conference.
It is wrong to minimize the challenges that people with disabilities face.
The company spokesperson attempted to minimize the environmental damage caused by the new factory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to downplay - to make something seem less important
  • Antonym: to exaggerate - to make something seem more important or significant

Etymology

From Latin 'minimus' (smallest), via 'minimus' + '-ize'.

2. VERB

/ ˈmɪn.ɪ.maɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɪn.ɪ.maɪz / (BrE)
to make something as small or insignificant as possible
make as small as possible

Examples

We need to minimize costs if we want to stay competitive.
She wore sunscreen to minimize the risk of sunburn.
The company tries to minimize its impact on the environment.

Synset Examples

Let's minimize the risk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reduce - to make something smaller or less in amount, degree, or size
  • Antonym: to maximize - to make as large or great as possible

Etymology

From Latin 'minimus' (smallest), via late Latin 'minimizzare'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00428755-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈmɪnɪˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪˌmaɪz / (BrE)
to make something seem less important or serious; to play down the significance of something
play down importance or seriousness

Examples

We should not minimize the challenges that lie ahead.
She tried to minimize the impact of her mistake during the meeting.
The politician sought to minimize the seriousness of the scandal in his speech.

Synset Examples

Don't belittle his influence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to belittle - to make someone or something seem unimportant
  • Antonym: to exaggerate - to make something seem greater than it really is

Etymology

From Latin minimus (smallest) + -ize.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00866139-v
Synset POS: v
