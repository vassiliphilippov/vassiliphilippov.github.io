von Blücher

1. PROPN

/ fɔn ˈbluːkər / (AmE), / fɒn ˈbluːxə / (BrE)
(1742–1819) Prussian general known for his role as a leader in the wars against Napoleon, especially at the Battle of Waterloo.
Prussian general who fought Napoleon

Examples

The arrival of von Blücher and his troops was decisive at the Battle of Waterloo.
Historians often credit von Blücher with turning the tide against Napoleon.
The alliance between Wellington and von Blücher is famous in military history.

Etymology

German noble name; 'von' meaning 'of' or 'from', indicating nobility, plus surname 'Blücher'.
