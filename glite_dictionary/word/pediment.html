pediment

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛdɪmənt / (AmE), / ˈpɛdɪmənt / (BrE)
A triangular section found above the horizontal structure of a building, typically above a portico or entrance, formed by the sloping roof and the entablature.
triangular section above a building's entrance

Examples

The ancient temple's entrance was crowned with a sculpted pediment depicting mythological scenes.
The architect designed the courthouse with a classical pediment supported by marble columns.
Over the doorway, a stone pediment added an elegant touch to the building's façade.

Etymology

From Latin 'pēdimentum', possibly an alteration of 'basimentum' (base or foundation).
