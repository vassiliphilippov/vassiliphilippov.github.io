fetterbush

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛtərˌbʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈfɛtəˌbʊʃ / (BrE)
An ornamental evergreen shrub of the southeastern United States, having small white bell-shaped flowers.
ornamental evergreen shrub of the southeastern US

Examples

The fetterbush is often planted in gardens for its attractive white blossoms and glossy green leaves.
We found a dense thicket of fetterbush near the edge of the swamp.
Because it tolerates wet soil, fetterbush is a popular choice for landscaping in the South.
