to litigate

1. VERB

/ ˈlɪt̬.ɪˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.ɪ.ɡeɪt / (BrE)
to take a dispute to court; to engage in legal proceedings
to engage in legal proceedings
legal

Examples

The company decided to litigate rather than settle the claim out of court.
After negotiations failed, both parties prepared to litigate their dispute.
He has litigated several high-profile cases in federal court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sue - to bring a legal case against someone in a court
  • Antonym: settle - to resolve or reach an agreement in a dispute, especially out of court

Etymology

From Latin litigatus, past participle of litigare (to dispute, to sue), from lis (dispute, lawsuit) + agere (to do).

2. VERB

/ ˈlɪt̬.əˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.ɪ.ɡeɪt / (BrE)
to take a dispute or case to a court of law; to engage in legal proceedings
to take legal action in court

Examples

The company decided to litigate rather than settle out of court.
She hired an attorney to litigate her claims of unfair dismissal.
If the dispute continues, both sides may choose to litigate.

Synset Examples

He was warned that the district attorney would process him
She actioned the company for discrimination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sue - to formally bring a legal action against someone in court

Etymology

From Latin 'litigatus', past participle of 'litigare' (to dispute, quarrel), from 'lis' (lawsuit, dispute) + 'agere' (to drive).
