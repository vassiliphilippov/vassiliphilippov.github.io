Mendelianism

1. NOUN

/ mɛnˈdɛliənɪzəm / (AmE), / mɛnˈdiːlɪənɪzəm / (BrE)
The theory of heredity and inheritance based on the genetic laws discovered by Gregor Mendel.
theory of inheritance from Mendel's laws

Examples

The principles of Mendelianism are fundamental to modern genetics.
Many genetic disorders can be explained through the lens of Mendelianism.
His research challenged traditional views by integrating ideas from Mendelianism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genetics - the study of heredity and the variation of inherited characteristics

Etymology

From the name of Gregor Mendel, Austrian monk and scientist, plus the suffix -ism.
