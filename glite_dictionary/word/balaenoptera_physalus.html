Balaenoptera physalus

1. NOUN

/ bəˌliːnəpˈtɛrə faɪˈseɪləs / (AmE), / bəˌliːnɒpˈtiərə fɪˈseɪləs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the fin whale, a large, flat-headed baleen whale with deep furrows along its throat, found in the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans
scientific name for the fin whale

Examples

The Balaenoptera physalus is the second-largest species of whale in the world.
Researchers tracked the migration patterns of Balaenoptera physalus across the Atlantic Ocean.
Unlike the blue whale, Balaenoptera physalus has distinctive dark and light coloration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fin whale - common name for Balaenoptera physalus, a large baleen whale

Etymology

From Latin: 'balaena' meaning whale and 'pteron' meaning fin; 'physalus' from Greek 'physa' meaning 'blow, bellows'.
