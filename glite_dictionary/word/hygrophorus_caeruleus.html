Hygrophorus caeruleus

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌɡrɑfərəs siːəˈruːliəs / (AmE), / haɪˌɡrɒfərəs siːəˈruːliəs / (BrE)
A species of mushroom distinguished by a broadly convex, cream-colored cap tinged with blue on the margin; bluish green to blue-grey waxy gills; and a short stalk that tapers abruptly at the base.
a species of blue-hued mushroom

Examples

The distinctive blue shades of the Hygrophorus caeruleus make it a favorite among mycologists.
We found several specimens of Hygrophorus caeruleus in the coniferous forest during our mushroom survey.
Because of its unique coloration, Hygrophorus caeruleus is relatively easy to identify in the wild.

Etymology

From Greek 'hygros' (moist) + 'phoros' (bearing) and Latin 'caeruleus' (blue).
