beige

1. ADJ

/ beɪʒ / (AmE), / beɪʒ / (BrE)
of a light greyish-brown color
light greyish-brown color

Examples

The living room was painted a soft beige that made the space look warm and inviting.
She wore a beige dress to the wedding, which complemented her skin tone.
Many office walls are beige, creating a neutral and professional atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tan - pale tone of brown used to describe color
  • Degree Lower: off-white - pale color between white and beige
  • Degree Higher: brown - darker color on the color spectrum

Etymology

From French 'beige', originally denoting natural wool that is neither bleached nor dyed.

2. NOUN

/ beɪʒ / (AmE), / beɪʒ / (BrE)
a pale, light brown color, often with a yellowish or grayish tinge.
a light brown color

Examples

She painted her living room walls a warm shade of beige.
The sofa is upholstered in soft beige fabric, which brightens the room.
I prefer beige over stark white for a more relaxed atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tan - a light yellowish-brown color
  • Degree Higher: brown - a dark color between red and yellow, like that of chocolate or coffee
  • Degree Lower: ivory - a creamy white color

Etymology

From French 'beige', originally meaning 'natural wool color'.

Synset POS: n
