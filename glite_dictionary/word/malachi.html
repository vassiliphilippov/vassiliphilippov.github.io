Malachi

1. PROPN

/ ˈmæləˌkaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmaləˌkaɪ / (BrE)
A book of the Old Testament containing the prophecies of Malachi.
book of the Old Testament

Examples

Many of the sermons referenced passages from Malachi during the service.
The Book of Malachi is the last book of the Old Testament in the Christian Bible.
Scholars have debated the historical context of the prophecies in Malachi.

Etymology

From Hebrew Malʾāḵī, meaning 'my messenger'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈmæləˌkaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmaləkʌɪ / (BrE)
a Hebrew prophet of the 5th century BC, regarded as the author of the last book of the Old Testament in the Hebrew Bible.
Hebrew prophet; author of biblical book

Examples

Malachi is considered the last of the minor prophets in the Hebrew Bible.
Many scholars debate the exact historical period in which Malachi lived and preached.
The Book of Malachi addresses the spiritual and moral state of Israel after the exile.

Etymology

From Hebrew Malʾākhī, meaning 'my messenger'.
