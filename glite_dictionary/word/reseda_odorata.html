Reseda odorata

1. NOUN

/ rəˌsidə oʊˈdɔrətə / (AmE), / rɪˌsiːdə əʊˈdɔːrətə / (BrE)
A Mediterranean plant cultivated for its dense, spikelike clusters of greenish or yellowish white, intensely fragrant flowers; also known as mignonette.
fragrant Mediterranean plant (mignonette)

Examples

Gardeners prize Reseda odorata for its intensely sweet fragrance.
The Reseda odorata blooms throughout the summer, adding a pleasant scent to the garden.
She planted Reseda odorata along the walkway to greet visitors with its spicy aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mignonette - another name for Reseda odorata, a fragrant plant

Etymology

From Latin 'resedare' (to calm, to assuage) and Latin 'odorata' (fragrant), referring to the scent and supposed calming qualities.
