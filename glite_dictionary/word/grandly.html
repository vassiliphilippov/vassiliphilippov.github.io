grandly

1. ADV

/ ˈɡrændli / (AmE), / ˈɡrændli / (BrE)
in a manner that is impressive, magnificent, or dignified
in an impressive or magnificent way

Examples

The palace was decorated grandly for the royal celebration.
She spoke grandly about her future plans, impressing everyone in the room.
The awards ceremony was grandly orchestrated, with elaborate performances and speeches.

Synset Examples

the mansion seemed grandly large by today's standards

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnificently - in an impressive and splendid manner
  • Antonym: modestly - in a humble or unassuming manner

Etymology

From 'grand' + '-ly', with 'grand' from Old French 'grand' (great, large), from Latin 'grandis' (large, great).
