sudorific

1. NOUN

/ suːdəˈrɪfɪk / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəˈrɪfɪk / (BrE)
A medicine or substance that causes or increases sweating.
medicine that induces sweating
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a sudorific to help reduce the patient's fever.
Herbalists sometimes use elderflower as a natural sudorific.
In the eighteenth century, physicians commonly recommended sudorifics for treating colds and flus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diaphoretic - a substance that induces perspiration

Etymology

From Latin 'sudorificus', from 'sudor' (sweat) + '-ficus' (making).

2. ADJ

/ ˌsuːdəˈrɪfɪk / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəˈrɪfɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance or medicine) causing or promoting sweating.
causing sweating
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a sudorific remedy to reduce his fever.
Ginger tea is considered a natural sudorific in herbal medicine.
Applying a sudorific ointment made her sweat profusely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diaphoretic - (of a drug) causing increased sweating

Etymology

From Medieval Latin sudorificus, from Latin sudor (sweat) + -ficus (making).
