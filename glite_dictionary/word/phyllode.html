phyllode

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪl.oʊd / (AmE), / ˈfɪl.əʊd / (BrE)
a flattened leaf stalk (petiole) that resembles and functions as a leaf blade, common in certain plants
flattened leaf stalk acting as a leaf

Examples

In some Australian acacias, the phyllode replaces the traditional leaf structure.
The phyllode enables the plant to minimize water loss in arid environments.
Researchers examined the anatomy of each phyllode to understand its adaptive function.

Etymology

From Greek phyllon (leaf) + -oidēs (form, shape).
