fieldfare

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːldˌfɛr / (AmE), / ˈfiːldˌfɛə / (BrE)
a medium-sized Eurasian thrush (Turdus pilaris) with a grey head and rump, seen chiefly in winter
a Eurasian thrush bird, Turdus pilaris

Examples

A small flock of fieldfare landed in the orchard in early December.
During winter, fieldfare often migrate south in search of food.
The distinctive call of the fieldfare can be heard across the countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrush - a medium-sized songbird of the family Turdidae

Etymology

From Old English 'feldefare', possibly meaning 'traveller through the fields'.
