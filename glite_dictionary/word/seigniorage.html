seigniorage

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːnjərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈseɪnjərɪdʒ / (BrE)
the profit made by a government from issuing currency, especially the difference between the face value of coins or bills and their production costs; formerly, a charge for coining bullion.
profit from issuing currency

Examples

The government benefits from seigniorage when the value of currency exceeds its production costs.
Historically, monarchs relied on seigniorage as a source of revenue.
In modern economies, seigniorage is often discussed in debates about inflation and monetary policy.

Etymology

From Old French 'seigneuriage', from 'seigneur' (lord), reflecting feudal rights over minting.
