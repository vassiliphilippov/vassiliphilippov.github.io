houseroom

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊsˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈhaʊsruːm / (BrE)
space available inside a house for people or things to be accommodated
space in a house for accommodation
british

Examples

We haven't got enough houseroom for a grand piano in the living room.
She wouldn't give that ugly lamp houseroom no matter how valuable it was.
They were grateful for the houseroom provided during the storm.

Synset Examples

I wouldn't give that table houseroom

Etymology

Formed from 'house' + 'room'.
