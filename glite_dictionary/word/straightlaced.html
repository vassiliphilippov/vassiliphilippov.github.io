straightlaced

1. ADJ

/ ˈstreɪtˌleɪst / (AmE), / ˈstreɪtˌleɪst / (BrE)
having very strict moral or social standards; excessively proper or conventional.
excessively proper or strict

Examples

Her parents are so straightlaced that they won't let her go to parties.
Despite his straightlaced reputation, he enjoys the occasional joke.
The school's straightlaced dress code requires all students to wear uniforms.

Synset Examples

my straitlaced Aunt Anna doesn't approve of my miniskirts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: straitlaced - having very strict moral standards; excessively proper
  • Antonym: permissive - allowing or characterized by great freedom of behavior

Etymology

Variant of 'straitlaced', from 'strait' (tight, strict) + 'laced', originally referring to tightly laced clothing as a sign of modesty and propriety.
