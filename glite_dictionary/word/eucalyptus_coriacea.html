Eucalyptus coriacea

1. NOUN

/ juːˌkʌlɪptəs kɔːriˈeɪʃiə / (AmE), / juːˌkælɪptəs kɒriˈeɪsɪə / (BrE)
A small to medium-sized tree native to Australia and Tasmania, known for its smooth white to light-grey bark that sheds in patches or strips.
Australian tree with smooth, shedding bark

Examples

The park is home to the distinctive Eucalyptus coriacea, easily recognized by its smooth, peeling bark.
Many gardeners in Tasmania choose Eucalyptus coriacea for its resilience in windy areas.
The pale trunk of Eucalyptus coriacea contrasts beautifully with the forest's darker foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eucalyptus - Genus of trees native to Australia and nearby islands

Etymology

From Greek *eucalyptos* (‘well-covered’) and the Latinized species name *coriacea*, from Latin ‘leathery’, in reference to the leaves.
