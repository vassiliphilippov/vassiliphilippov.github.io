BMR

1. NOUN

/ bi ɛm ɑr / (AmE), / biː ɛm ɑː / (BrE)
the rate at which energy is expended by the body at rest to maintain basic physiological functions
rate of energy use at rest
medical

Examples

Your BMR can be influenced by your age, gender, and genetics.
Doctors often measure BMR to assess a person's metabolic health.
Improving muscle mass can increase your BMR and help with weight management.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basal metabolic rate - the full term for BMR, rate at which the body uses energy at rest

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'basal metabolic rate'.
