cresol

1. NOUN

/ ˈkriːsɒl, ˈkriːsoʊl / (AmE), / ˈkriːsɒl, ˈkrɛsɒl / (BrE)
any of three poisonous colorless isomeric phenols, usually obtained from coal or wood tar and used as a disinfectant and in chemical manufacturing
a toxic phenol used as a disinfectant
medical

Examples

Exposure to cresol can cause irritation to the skin and respiratory tract.
The laboratory uses cresol as a powerful disinfectant in some procedures.
Wood tar is one of the sources from which cresol is extracted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phenol - a toxic white crystalline compound used in chemical manufacture

Etymology

From cres(yl) + -ol, referring to its relationship with phenol and cresylic acid.
