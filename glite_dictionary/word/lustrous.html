lustrous

1. ADJ

/ ˈlʌstrəs / (AmE), / ˈlʌstrəs / (BrE)
shining with a soft or radiant light; having a smooth, glowing surface that reflects light brilliantly
shining; having a radiant sheen

Examples

Her lustrous hair shimmered in the sunlight as she walked through the park.
The museum displayed ancient pottery with a lustrous glaze that caught everyone's eye.
He admired the lustrous pearls she wore around her neck.

Synset Examples

glistening bodies of swimmers
the horse's glossy coat
lustrous auburn hair
saw the moon like a shiny dime on a deep blue velvet carpet
shining white enamel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shiny - having a glossy or reflective surface
  • Antonym: dull - lacking brightness or sheen; not shiny

Etymology

From Latin 'lustrare' (to illuminate), via 'lustrous' in Middle English.

/ ˈlʌstrəs / (AmE), / ˈlʌstrəs / (BrE)
shining with brilliance or radiance; outstanding or illustrious in a figurative sense.
shining; illustrious or outstanding

Examples

The ceremony was attended by lustrous figures from the world of film and art.
He set a lustrous example of dedication and professionalism in the workplace.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dull - lacking brightness or shine; lacking brilliance or liveliness

having a smooth, shiny surface that reflects light; glowing with a soft shine
shiny or glowing with light

Examples

The museum displayed a lustrous pearl necklace worth thousands of dollars.
After polishing, the old table regained its lustrous finish.

  • Antonym: dull - lacking brightness or sheen
