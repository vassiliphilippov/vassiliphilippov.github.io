czardas

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑːr.dɑːs / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːd.ɑːʃ / (BrE)
a piece of music composed for or in the style of the Hungarian Csárdás dance, typically starting slow and ending fast
music for Hungarian Csárdás dance

Examples

The orchestra performed a lively czardas that brought the audience to their feet.
She learned to play a famous czardas on the violin for her recital.
Many composers have written their own czardas inspired by Hungarian folk music.

Synset Examples

The Csárdás is characterized by a variation in tempo: it starts out slowly (lassú) and ends in a very fast tempo (friss, literally ‘fresh’).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: csárdás - traditional Hungarian folk dance

Etymology

From Hungarian csárdás (a national dance), from csárda (tavern).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑrdɑːs / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːdɑːʃ / (BrE)
a traditional Hungarian dance or a piece of music in the lively rhythm of this dance, often characterized by a slow introduction followed by a fast, spirited section
Hungarian dance and its music

Examples

The orchestra performed a lively czardas to open the concert.
He composed a beautiful czardas inspired by Hungarian folk melodies.
Many violinists include a challenging czardas in their repertoire.

Synset Examples

Classical composers who have used csárdás themes in their works include Emmerich Kálmán, Franz Liszt, Johannes Brahms, Léo Delibes, Johann Strauss, Pablo de Sarasate, Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky and others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waltz - a ballroom dance in triple time

Etymology

From Hungarian 'csárdás', meaning 'pertaining to a tavern' or 'tavern dance'.
