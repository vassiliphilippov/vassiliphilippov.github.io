brig

1. NOUN

/ brɪɡ / (AmE), / brɪɡ / (BrE)
a two-masted sailing ship with square sails on both masts
two-masted square-rigged sailing vessel

Examples

The pirates attacked the merchant brig under cover of darkness.
In the 18th century, a brig was a common sight on the high seas.
The crew quickly raised the sails on the brig as a storm approached.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brigantine - a two-masted sailing ship with different rigging on the foremast and mainmast

Etymology

Shortened form of 'brigantine', from Italian 'brigantino', from 'brigante' (brigand).

2. NOUN

/ brɪɡ / (AmE), / brɪɡ / (BrE)
a prison or jail, especially one located on a ship or at a naval installation
naval prison or jail

Examples

The sailor was thrown into the brig for disobeying orders.
After the fight broke out, the captain confined both men to the brig.
He spent the night in the brig after being caught stealing from the galley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prison - a building for the confinement of criminals
  • Synonym: cell - a small room in a prison
  • Synonym: jail - a place of confinement for people accused or convicted of a crime

Etymology

Probably an abbreviation of 'brigantine', as such ships were sometimes used as prison ships.
