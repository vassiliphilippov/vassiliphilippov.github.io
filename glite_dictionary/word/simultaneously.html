simultaneously

1. ADV

/ ˌsaɪ.məlˈteɪ.ni.əs.li / (AmE), / ˌsɪm.əlˈteɪ.ni.əs.li / (BrE)
at the same time; occurring or done together in the same instant
at the same time

Examples

The two events occurred simultaneously, leaving everyone surprised.
The twins spoke simultaneously during the debate, causing confusion.
The lights flickered simultaneously in every room, signaling a power surge.

Synset Examples

they spoke simultaneously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concurrently - at the same time; in parallel

Etymology

From Latin simul (together) + English -aneous (from Latin -āneus) + -ly.
