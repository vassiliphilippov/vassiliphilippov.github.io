totalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊt̬əˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈtəʊtəlɪzəm / (BrE)
The belief in or support for the complete and unrestricted power of government over all aspects of life.
belief in absolute government control

Examples

Some political scientists argue that totalism can lead to the suppression of individual freedoms.
The novel explores the dangers of totalism in a dystopian society.
Opponents of totalism advocate for checks and balances within the government.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: totalitarianism - the political system advocating absolute state authority and control
  • Antonym: liberalism - a political philosophy emphasizing individual freedoms and limited government

Etymology

Formed from 'total' (from Latin 'totalis', meaning 'entire') + '-ism'.
