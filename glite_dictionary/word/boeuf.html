boeuf

1. NOUN

/ bœf / (AmE), / bœf / (BrE)
meat from an adult domestic bovine, especially as used in French or French-inspired culinary contexts.
meat from an adult cow (culinary, French)

Examples

The chef prepared a classic boeuf bourguignon for the guests.
In French cuisine, boeuf is often cooked slowly in red wine.
He ordered the boeuf dish, intrigued by its rich flavors and tender texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beef - meat from a cow, especially when used as food

Etymology

From French 'bœuf' (beef, ox, cow), ultimately from Latin 'bos'.
