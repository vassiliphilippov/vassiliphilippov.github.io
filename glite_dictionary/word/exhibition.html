exhibition

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛksɪˈbɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛksɪˈbɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of displaying something publicly, especially to demonstrate skill, quality, or ability
the act of displaying or showing something

Examples

Her solo was an impressive exhibition of technical skill.
He gave a brilliant exhibition of chess strategy at the tournament.
The athlete's performance was a true exhibition of strength and endurance.

Synset Examples

a remarkable exhibition of musicianship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: display - the act of putting something in view for others to see
  • Antonym: concealment - the act of hiding something

Etymology

From Latin 'exhibitio' (a showing, display), from 'exhibere' (to display, present).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.səˈbɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɛks.ɪˈbɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a public display of a collection of items such as works of art, goods, or other objects
public display of items or art

Examples

The new art exhibition at the gallery has attracted hundreds of visitors.
We visited a science exhibition featuring interactive experiments for children.
The museum will host a special exhibition of ancient Egyptian artifacts next month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: show - an event at which things, such as goods or works of art, are displayed to the public
  • Synonym: display - an arrangement of items to be seen by the public
  • Synonym: collection - a group of objects displayed together

Etymology

From Latin 'exhibitio' (a showing forth), from 'exhibere' (to display).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
