muscatel

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌskəˈtɛl / (AmE), / ˌmʌskəˈtɛl / (BrE)
A sweet wine made from muscat grapes, often with a strong, fragrant aroma.
sweet wine from muscat grapes

Examples

The dessert was perfectly paired with a glass of muscatel.
He bought a bottle of muscatel for the special occasion.
The fragrance of muscatel filled the room as the wine was poured.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscat - a type of grape used for wine; also, wine made from this grape

Etymology

From Spanish 'moscatel', from Italian 'moscatello', diminutive of 'moscato' (muscat grape).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmʌs.kəˈtɛl / (AmE), / ˌmʌs.kəˈtɛl / (BrE)
A sweet, aromatic variety of grape, often used to make wine or raisins.
sweet aromatic grape for wine/raisins

Examples

The dessert wine was made from muscatel grapes grown in the region.
She added a handful of muscatel raisins to the cake batter.
A glass of chilled muscatel pairs well with fruit-based desserts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscat - A variety of grape known for its sweet, musky flavor, used in wine and raisins

Etymology

From Spanish 'moscatel', from Italian 'moscatello', diminutive of 'moscato' (Muscat grape), from late Latin 'muscatus'.
