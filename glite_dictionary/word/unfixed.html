unfixed

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfɪkst / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɪkst / (BrE)
not defined, settled, or made definite; lacking clear or fixed content or form
not settled or definite

Examples

Her ideas about the future remained unfixed and constantly changed with her mood.
The plan was still unfixed, with many details yet to be decided.
His opinions on the subject were vague and unfixed even after much discussion.

Synset Examples

nebulous reasons
unfixed as were her general notions of what men ought to be

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nebulous - vague or ill-defined; not clear or definite
  • Synonym: indefinite - not clearly expressed or defined; vague
  • Antonym: settled - firmly established or decided; not likely to change

Etymology

From un- (not) + fixed (made firm or definite).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfɪkst / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɪkst / (BrE)
not firmly placed, set, or attached; not fixed in position
not firmly fixed or attached

Examples

The shelves remained unfixed, so they wobbled every time someone brushed past them.
Because the date was still unfixed, attendees struggled to make travel plans.
The painter left the canvas unfixed until she was satisfied with her work.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fixed - firmly placed or attached
  • Synonym: loose - not firmly or tightly fixed in place
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01064484-a
Synset POS: a
