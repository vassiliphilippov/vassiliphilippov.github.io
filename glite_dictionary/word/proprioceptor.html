proprioceptor

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊ.pri.oʊˈsɛp.tər / (AmE), / ˌprəʊ.pri.əˈsɛp.tə / (BrE)
a sensory nerve ending in muscles, tendons, or other organs that responds to stimuli about the position and movement of the body
sensory receptor for body position and movement
medical

Examples

Damage to the ankle's proprioceptor can impair the body's balance and coordination.
Athletes rely on proprioceptors in their muscles to maintain precise control during movement.
Physical therapy often targets exercises that stimulate proprioceptors to aid in recovery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: receptor - a nerve ending that reacts to sensory stimuli
  • Synonym: mechanoreceptor - a sensory receptor that responds to mechanical pressure or distortion

Etymology

From Latin 'proprius' (one's own) + 'ceptor' (taker, receiver).
